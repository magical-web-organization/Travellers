A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Calama, Northern Chile

Calama is a fascinating city located in the Atacama Desert, one of the driest places on earth. Known for its copper mining industry, Calama offers visitors a unique blend of natural marvels, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, local cuisine, and more in Calama.

List of Places to Visit in Calama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calama has a rich history that dates back to pre-Columbian times. From indigenous cultures to the Spanish conquest, the city has been shaped by different influences throughout the years. Here are some of the most interesting places to visit to learn about Calama’s past:

  1. Chuquicamata: This is the biggest open pit copper mine in the world and one of the main attractions of Calama. With guided tours, you can learn about the history and operation of this impressive mine.

  2. Pukará de Lasana: This pre-Columbian fortress is located 75 km east of Calama. It dates back to the 12th century and offers visitors a glimpse into the ancestral culture of the region.

  3. Museum of Calama: This museum showcases the city’s mining history and features exhibits on pre-Hispanic cultures.

Natural Attractions of Calama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calama is surrounded by natural wonders that are a must-visit for nature lovers. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  1. Valley of the Moon: This otherworldly landscape is located 13 km west of Calama. It’s a unique geological formation that resembles the surface of the moon.

  2. Chuquicamata Hike: For those who love hiking, the Chuquicamata mine offers a stunning trail that takes you through the heart of the mine. The hike is a great way to learn about the mining industry and enjoy breathtaking views of the desert.

  3. Puritama Hot Springs: These hot springs are located about 30 km from Calama and offer visitors a relaxing and therapeutic experience in the heart of the desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calama

Calama offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the desert’s unique landscape. Listed below are some of the best routes to try:

  1. Cerro La Cruz: This hike offers incredible panoramic views of Calama and the surrounding desert.

  2. Cerro Chuño: This is a challenging hike that takes you to the top of Cerro Chuño, which offers stunning views of the Atacama Desert.

  3. Atacama Desert Bike Tour: For biking enthusiasts, this tour takes you through the heart of the desert and offers amazing views of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Calama: Activities and Tours

Calama offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Stargazing Tour: With little light pollution, Calama is an ideal location for stargazing. This tour takes you on a stargazing experience that includes transportation and a guide.

  2. Calama City Tour: This tour takes you through the most important sites of the city and provides a great introduction to Calama’s history and culture.

  3. Rainbow Valley and Hierbas Buenas Petroglyphs Tour: This tour takes you to the Rainbow Valley and the Hierbas Buenas Petroglyphs, where you’ll explore the unique geological formations and learn about the indigenous cultures that inhabited the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calama

For those who have a week to spare, here’s an itinerary that will allow you to make the most out of your trip to Calama:

Day 1: Arrive and get settled in your hotel in Calama.

Day 2: Explore the city’s cultural attractions, including the Museum of Calama and the Pukará de Lasana.

Day 3: Take a hiking or biking tour of the desert, such as the Cerro Chuño hike or the Atacama Desert Bike Tour.

Day 4: Go on a day trip to the Rainbow Valley and the Hierbas Buenas Petroglyphs.

Day 5: Relax at the Puritama Hot Springs.

Day 6: Take a stargazing tour of the Atacama Desert.

Day 7: Visit the Chuquicamata mine and learn about the history and operation of this impressive mine.

Food and Drink Scene of Calama

Calama offers a variety of traditional dishes and drinks that reflect the region’s cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Empanadas: These are stuffed pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables. They’re a popular snack or starter in Chile.

  2. Cazuela de Vacuno: This hearty soup is made with beef, potatoes, corn, and other vegetables. It’s a staple of Chilean cuisine and a perfect meal for a cool, desert evening.

  3. Pastel de Choclo: This is a savory pie made with corn and filled with ground beef, chicken, onions, and spices. It’s a popular comfort food in Chile.

Local Beverages

  1. Pisco Sour: This is a cocktail made with Pisco, a grape brandy, lime juice, simple syrup, and egg whites. It’s the national drink of Chile and a must-try when you visit Calama.

  2. Chilean Wine: Chile is famous for its wine, and Calama is no exception. Some of the best wine regions in Chile are located in the north, such as the Elqui Valley and the Limarí Valley.

Where to Stay in Calama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calama offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Park Hotel Calama: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a fitness center. It’s located in the heart of the city, close to main attractions.

  2. Sonesta Hotel Calama: This hotel offers spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the city.

  3. Hostal American: This hostel offers affordable accommodation in the center of Calama. It’s a great option for backpackers or budget travelers.

What to Buy in Calama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Calama is known for its copper mining industry, so it’s no surprise that one of the most popular souvenirs to bring back home is copper products. You can find a variety of items, such as jewelry, pots, and decorative objects, made out of copper. Other local products to try are Algarrobo honey, gourmet salts, and handwoven textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Calama

Calama has a diverse population that includes indigenous peoples, immigrants, and migrants from other regions of Chile. The city’s culture is influenced by its mining industry and its location in the Atacama Desert. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as the use of ponchos and hats to protect against the desert’s harsh sun and wind.

Best Time to Visit Calama

The best time to visit Calama is during the spring and fall months (September-November and March-May), when temperatures are cooler and there’s less chance of rain. Summer months (December-February) can be very hot, with temperatures reaching over 100°F (38°C). Winter months (June-August) can be cooler, with temperatures dropping below freezing at night.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calama

Do:

  • Do try traditional Chilean dishes and beverages, such as empanadas, pastel de choclo, and pisco sour.
  • Do bring sunscreen, a hat, and comfortable shoes for exploring the desert.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural or cultural sites.
  • Don’t underestimate the sun and wind. Wear protective clothing and stay hydrated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calama

Calama is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft or scams. It’s recommended to stay in well-lit areas at night, keep valuables out of sight, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Calama

Visitors to Calama should be aware of the high altitude and dry climate, which can cause altitude sickness and dehydration. It’s recommended to drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol and caffeine, and take it easy during the first few days of your trip.

Getting to Calama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calama is located about 1,050 km north of Santiago, Chile’s capital city. Here are some transportation options to get to Calama:

Airports Near Calama

  • El Loa Airport (CJC): This airport is located in Calama and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Calama

  • Terminal de Buses San Borja: This is the main bus terminal in Santiago, and it offers buses to Calama and other northern cities in Chile.

Other Options

  • Rent a car: There are rental car options available at El Loa Airport and in Calama downtown.
  • Private transfer: You can book a private transfer from the airport to your accommodation in Calama.

How to Get Around in Calama

Calama is a relatively small city, and most of the main attractions can be reached on foot or by public transportation. There are also taxis and rental cars available for those who prefer more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Calama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calama is located in a region that offers many exciting destinations to explore. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • San Pedro de Atacama: This small town is located about 100 km from Calama and is the gateway to the Atacama Desert. It offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, such as sandboarding, stargazing, and hiking.

  • Iquique: This coastal city is located about 550 km from Calama and offers visitors a range of cultural attractions, such as museums, galleries, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Calama is a unique travel destination that offers visitors a glimpse into the mining industry, indigenous cultures, and the unparalleled beauty of the Atacama Desert. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a cultural explorer, Calama has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your itinerary and make the most out of your trip to Northern Chile.
