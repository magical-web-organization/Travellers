A Comprehensive Guide to Vicuña, Chile

Vicuña is a charming small town located in the Elqui Valley of Northern Chile. It’s a perfect destination for those who want to experience both cultural and natural attractions while enjoying the region’s stunning scenery. This guide will provide you with the necessary information to make your trip to Vicuña unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Vicuña

Vicuña has something to offer for every type of traveler. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gabriela Mistral Museum

The Gabriela Mistral Museum is a must-visit attraction for history and literature enthusiasts. The museum is located in Vicuña’s central square and was created to honor the life and work of Gabriela Mistral, the first Latin American woman to win the Nobel Prize in Literature. You’ll learn about her life, work, and how she influenced Chilean and Latin American literature.

Cruz del Tercer Milenio

The Cruz del Tercer Milenio (Third Millennium Cross) stands on a hill overlooking Vicuña. The cross is over 90 meters tall and was erected in 2001 to mark the new millennium. You can take a cable car to the top and enjoy panoramic views of the Elqui Valley.

Natural Attractions of Vicuña: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Elqui River

The Elqui River runs through the valley, providing visitors with beautiful scenery and an opportunity to explore its natural surroundings. You can take a relaxing stroll along the riverbank or go rafting or kayaking on the rapids.

Cerro Mamalluca

Cerro Mamalluca is a mountain that offers stunning views of the Elqui Valley at sunset. You can hike to the summit or take a guided tour to observe the stars at Mamalluca Observatory.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vicuña

The Elqui Valley is a stunning location for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes for hiking or biking:

  • Pisco Route: a 13 km hiking or biking trail that takes you through vineyards and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Cerro Tololo Interamerican Observatory Trail: a 6.5 km hiking trail that takes you through the Tololo Observatory, offering spectacular views of the valley.
  • Horcón Route: a 17 km route for more experienced hikers or bikers, allowing you to explore the hidden corners of the valley.

Best Things to Do in Vicuña: Activities and Tours

The Elqui Valley and Vicuña offer a variety of activities and tours that we recommend adding to your itinerary:

  • Pisco Tasting: visit one of the many distilleries in the valley to try pisco, a type of brandy that’s the national drink of Chile.
  • Stargazing: The Elqui Valley has some of the clearest skies in the world, making it a perfect place for stargazing. You can take a guided tour and observe the stars at one of the observatories in the valley.
  • Horse Riding: Horseback riding is popular in the Elqui Valley, and you can take a guided tour to explore the area.
  • Hot Springs: There are several hot springs in the valley, where you can relax and enjoy the scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Vicuña

No trip to Chile is complete without experiencing its culinary culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Vicuña:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanadas: a pastry filled with beef, chicken, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Pastel de Choclo: a corn and meat pie.
  • Cazuela: a traditional soup, usually made with beef, pumpkin, corn, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco: a type of brandy made from grapes.
  • Piscola: a mixture of pisco and Coca-Cola, the local drink of Chile.
  • Mote con Huesillo: a sweet and refreshing drink made with dried peaches and corn.

Where to Stay in Vicuña: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vicuña has a variety of accommodation options to fit every budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Terral & Spa: a luxury hotel located in the center of Vicuña.
  • Hostal Los Nicholas: a budget-friendly hostel located close to the central square of Vicuña.
  • Elqui Domos: a unique accommodation option offering geodesic domes for stargazers.

What to Buy in Vicuña: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Vicuña, make sure to bring back some local products, including:

  • Pisco: a bottle of pisco to take home makes for a great souvenir.
  • Alpaca Products: blankets, scarves, and clothing made from alpaca wool are popular in the Andes region.
  • Handicrafts: handmade crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles, are popular in the Elqui Valley.

Local Culture and Customs of Vicuña

Chile is a country with deep traditions. Here are some local culture and customs to keep in mind while visiting Vicuña:

  • Mate: a traditional beverage made by steeping yerba mate leaves in hot water, often shared in a group setting.
  • Asado: traditional barbecue, often made with different cuts of meat.
  • Chileans are very friendly and welcoming people.

Best Time to Visit Vicuña

The best time to visit Vicuña is between September and April, during Chile’s summer season. The weather is warm, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vicuña

Do:

  • Try pisco and local dishes.
  • Greet people with a kiss on the cheek.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Take photos without asking permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vicuña

Vicuña is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always recommended to take basic precautions for your safety:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places.
  • Always use official taxis or transportation companies.

Health in Vicuña

Chile is known for having a high-quality healthcare system. Still, it’s recommended to purchase travel insurance before your journey.

Getting to Vicuña: Transportation Options and Logistics

Vicuña is located around 500 km from Santiago, Chile’s capital city. Here are some transportation options to get to Vicuña:

Airports Near Vicuña

The closest airport to Vicuña is La Serena Airport, located around 85 km away. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi to get to Vicuña.

Bus Stations Near Vicuña

You can reach Vicuña by bus from various destinations in Chile, including Santiago, Valparaiso, and La Serena. The bus ride takes around 7 to 8 hours from Santiago.

Other Options

If you prefer a more personalized transportation service, you can hire a private driver or take a shuttle service to get to Vicuña.

How to Get Around in Vicuña

Vicuña is a small town, and the best way to get around and explore the area is on foot. If you prefer other transportation options, you can rent a car or bike or take a taxi or private driver service.

Where to Go Next from Vicuña: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue exploring Chile’s northern region, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Pisco Elqui: a small town located around 20 km from Vicuña, known for its pisco production.
  • La Serena: a colonial city located around 60 km from Vicuña, offering historical attractions and beautiful beaches.
  • Valle del Encanto: a valley located around 30 km from Vicuña, known for its stunning petroglyphs.

Conclusion

Vicuña offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience the Elqui Valley’s stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and culinary traditions. This guide highlights some of the best things to do and see in Vicuña, making it an excellent destination for any type of traveler. Plan your trip today and explore the heart and soul of Northern Chile.
