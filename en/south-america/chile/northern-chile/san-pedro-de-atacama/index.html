Introduction to San Pedro de Atacama

San Pedro de Atacama is a small town in the Atacama Desert, Chile, known for its picturesque landscapes, natural wonders and unique culture. Visitors from all over the world come here to explore the beauty of the area, learn about the local culture and enjoy various outdoor activities.

In this travel guide, we have compiled a list of the best activities, places to visit, and local customs to help you plan your trip to San Pedro de Atacama.

List of Places to Visit in San Pedro de Atacama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Pedro de Atacama has a rich cultural heritage with several historical places to visit:

  • Pukará de Quitor: A 12th-century fortress built by the Atacameño people to defend themselves from enemy tribes.

  • San Pedro Church: A colonial-era church decorated with beautiful frescoes and religious artwork.

  • Gustave Le Paige Archaeological Museum: A research center dedicated to the pre-Columbian culture of the Atacama region.

Natural Attractions of San Pedro de Atacama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural landscape of San Pedro de Atacama is one of the highlights of the town. Here are a few places to visit for breathtaking views and outdoor activities:

  • Valle de la Luna: Named for its resemblance to the moon’s surface, this valley offers a stunning panoramic view of the Atacama Desert.

  • El Tatio Geysers: A natural wonder with geysers that shoot hot water up to 30 meters in the air.

  • Laguna Cejar: A beautiful saltwater lagoon where you can float effortlessly due to high salt concentration.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Pedro de Atacama

San Pedro de Atacama offers many opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking and biking. Here are some recommended routes:

  • Caminos del Inca Route: A two-day hike that follows one of the ancient roads used by the Inca civilization.

  • Guatin Canyon: A scenic bike ride through the canyon that also offers hiking trails and a hot spring.

Best Things to Do in San Pedro de Atacama: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in San Pedro de Atacama. Here are a few options:

  • Stargazing Tour: Experience the clear night sky of the Atacama Desert by taking a guided stargazing tour.

  • Desert Safari: The perfect way to explore the natural wonders of the Atacama Desert, including volcanoes, geysers, and wildlife.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take an early morning hot air balloon ride to experience the sunrise over the Atacama desert.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Pedro de Atacama

If you plan to visit San Pedro de Atacama for a week, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of San Pedro de Atacama

Must-Try Dishes

San Pedro de Atacama has a unique local cuisine that combines traditional Andean ingredients with Spanish and Italian influences. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Empanadas: A meat or cheese-filled pastry that is a staple in Chilean cuisine.

  • Pastel de choclo: A corn and meat pie covered with cheese.

  • Locro: A hearty soup made with corn, pumpkin, and meat.

Local Beverages

San Pedro de Atacama offers a range of local beverages for you to try:

  • Pisco Sour: A popular cocktail made with pisco, lemon juice, and sugar.

  • Chicha: A fermented corn-based drink that has been a staple of Andean culture for centuries.

  • Aguaymanto Sour: A cocktail made with a local fruit called aguaymanto, pisco, and sugar.

Where to Stay in San Pedro de Atacama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Pedro de Atacama has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget:

  • Hostels: There are several hostels in San Pedro de Atacama that offer affordable accommodation with shared or private rooms.

  • Hotels: There are also many hotels in San Pedro de Atacama that offer comfortable rooms, swimming pools, and spa facilities.

  • Guesthouses: For a more authentic experience, consider a guesthouse, where you can stay with locals and learn about the Atacameño culture.

What to Buy in San Pedro de Atacama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Pedro de Atacama is known for its handicrafts and artisanal products. Here are a few items to consider buying:

  • Alpaca wool products: Scarves, hats, and ponchos made from soft alpaca wool.

  • Atacama salt: A natural salt that is harvested from the nearby Atacama salt flats.

  • Llama and cactus wood carvings: Intricately carved wooden sculptures that showcase the local flora and fauna.

Local Culture and Customs of San Pedro de Atacama

The Atacameño people have a rich culture and unique customs that are worth exploring during your stay in San Pedro de Atacama:

  • Pachamama: The Atacameño people believe in a goddess named Pachamama who represents the earth and nature. Respect for nature is an essential part of their culture.

  • Carnivals: San Pedro de Atacama has an annual carnival in February, where locals dance and celebrate with music and colorful costumes.

Best Time to Visit San Pedro de Atacama

The best time to visit San Pedro de Atacama is from March to May and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists. However, San Pedro de Atacama can be visited year-round, depending on your activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Pedro de Atacama

Do:

  • Respect the local culture: The Atacameño people have a unique culture and customs. Be respectful of these traditions and learn about their way of life.

  • Carry cash: Many places in San Pedro de Atacama do not accept credit cards, so always carry cash with you.

  • Stay hydrated: The Atacama desert is a dry and arid place. Make sure to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without permission: Respect people’s privacy and ask permission before taking photos of them.

  • Litter: The desert is a fragile ecosystem, and litter can have a significant impact on the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Pedro de Atacama

San Pedro de Atacama is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take precautions:

  • Stay on marked trails: Some areas can be dangerous due to steep terrain, wildlife, or dangerous weather conditions. Always stick to the marked trails.

  • Use sunscreen: The Atacama desert is hot and sunny. Always use sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Health in San Pedro de Atacama

San Pedro de Atacama is located at high altitude, and visitors can experience altitude sickness. Here are some tips to avoid altitude sickness:

  • Stay hydrated: Drinking lots of water can help prevent altitude sickness.

  • Take it slow: Avoid strenuous activities for the first few days to give your body time to acclimatize.

Getting to San Pedro de Atacama: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Pedro de Atacama is located in Northern Chile, and the nearest airports are Calama or Antofagasta. Here are some transportation options to get to San Pedro de Atacama:

Airports Near San Pedro de Atacama

  • Calama Airport: This is the closest airport to San Pedro de Atacama and is served by several airlines.

  • Antofagasta Airport: This airport is located 5 hours from San Pedro de Atacama and offers flights from several major cities.

Bus Stations Near San Pedro de Atacama

  • San Pedro de Atacama Bus Station: Several regional and long-distance buses arrive at San Pedro de Atacama Bus Station.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car in Calama or Antofagasta and drive to San Pedro de Atacama.

  • Shuttle Service: There are several shuttle services that operate between Calama Airport and San Pedro de Atacama.

How to Get Around in San Pedro de Atacama

San Pedro de Atacama is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you plan to visit the surrounding areas, you will need to use public transportation, rental cars, or tour services.

Where to Go Next from San Pedro de Atacama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Pedro de Atacama is surrounded by several popular destinations that are worth visiting, including:

  • Valle de Elqui: A beautiful valley known for its vineyards, pisco distilleries, and stunning scenery.

  • Salta, Argentina: A colonial-era town in Argentina known for its architecture, museums, and wine.

  • Salar de Uyuni, Bolivia: The largest salt flat in the world, located across the border in Bolivia.

Conclusion

San Pedro de Atacama offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor activities to indulge in. From exploring ancient ruins to hiking through scenic canyons, there’s something for every traveler here. We hope this travel guide helps you plan your next trip to San Pedro de Atacama and makes it an unforgettable experience.
