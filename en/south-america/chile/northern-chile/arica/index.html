Introduction to Arica

Nestled in the heart of Northern Chile, Arica is a hidden gem that deserves recognition for its unique blend of vibrant culture, natural landscapes, and adventurous activities. The city is often overshadowed by its neighboring destinations such as San Pedro de Atacama and Valparaiso, but Arica is a destination in its own right. With its sandy beaches, rich history, and indigenous influences, visitors can expect a truly unforgettable experience in Arica.

List of Places to Visit in Arica

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arica’s rich history dates back to pre-Columbian times and its cultural attractions are a testament to its unique past. The Museo Arqueologico de San Miguel de Azapa (MASMA) is a must-visit for those interested in learning about the region’s ancient cultures. The museum houses a collection of artifacts from the Chinchorro mummies, dating back to over 7,000 years ago. The Cathedral of San Marcos is another historic landmark worth visiting. Built-in 1876, the cathedral’s neo-Gothic architecture stands out among Arica’s more modern buildings.

Natural Attractions of Arica: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arica’s natural beauty extends beyond its beaches. The El Morro de Arica hill provides panoramic views of the city and the Pacific Ocean. The hill is also home to the Museo Historico y de Armas, which showcases Arica’s military history. The nearby Valle de Azapa offers a unique experience for visitors interested in agrotourism. The valley is known for its olive groves, vineyards, and fruit orchards. Additionally, the Lauca National Park is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. The park is located at a higher altitude, making it a perfect destination for a day trip to enjoy scenic landscapes of mountains, lakes, and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arica

Arica’s landscape is ideal for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. The North Coastal Path is a 30-kilometer trail that spans along the coast and provides spectacular views of the Pacific Ocean. For a more challenging experience, the nearby Parque Nacional Lauca offers hiking trails that take visitors through the park’s high altitude landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Arica: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are a variety of other activities and tours to enjoy in Arica. For those interested in water sports, the nearby beaches offer opportunities for surfing, kiteboarding, and paddleboarding. The Surf and Yoga Tour is a unique experience that combines surfing, yoga, and community service. The tour takes visitors to remote areas where they can learn to surf, practice yoga, and engage in volunteer opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arica

A week-long itinerary in Arica can be filled with a variety of cultural and outdoor activities. The first day can be dedicated to exploring the city’s historic landmarks including the Cathedral of San Marcos and the Museo Arqueologico de San Miguel de Azapa. The next day can be spent enjoying a beach day at Playa Chinchorro. The third day can be dedicated to a day trip to the nearby Lauca National Park, followed by a hiking or biking adventure along the coastline on day four. The fifth day can be spent on a cultural tour of the nearby Valle de Azapa, followed by a day of water sports on day six. The week can be concluded with a Surf and Yoga Tour on the last day.

Food and Drink Scene of Arica

Must-Try Dishes

Arica’s cuisine is a reflection of its multicultural influences, incorporating flavors from the Andean highlands, the coast, and even Peru. One must-try dish is the Arroz con Marisco, a seafood dish made with rice, shrimp, mussels, and squid. Another popular dish is the Sopaipilla, a fried dough snack made with pumpkin and flour, usually served with pebre, a Chilean salsa-like condiment.

Local Beverages

Chicha, a fermented corn drink, is a popular beverage in Arica. Another traditional drink is the Mate de Coca, a tea made from coca leaves, which is said to help alleviate altitude sickness.

Where to Stay in Arica: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arica offers a range of accommodation options for all types of travelers, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The Hotel Antay Casino & Spa is a luxurious option located in the heart of the city, while the Hostal Sunny Days provides a more affordable, but still comfortable, option for travelers on a budget. For a unique and immersive experience, the Guesthouse Casa de Campo provides visitors with the opportunity to stay in a traditional Chilean adobe house.

What to Buy in Arica: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional handicrafts are a popular souvenir to bring home from Arica. The city’s markets offer artisanal crafts, including textiles, ceramics, and jewelry, made by local indigenous groups. Olive oil is another popular product to bring home from Arica, as the region is known for its olive groves.

Local Culture and Customs of Arica

Arica’s culture is a mix of indigenous and Spanish influences, resulting in a vibrant and unique community. The city is home to the Aymara indigenous group, who have preserved their traditional way of life and continue to influence the city’s cultural identity. Visitors to Arica should respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Arica

Arica enjoys a desert climate, with hot and dry summers and mild winters. The best time to visit the city is between December and February, when temperatures are warm and ideal for enjoying the beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arica

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Enjoy the beaches and outdoor landscapes
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or traditions
  • Engage in illegal activities
  • Underestimate the strong sun and pack enough sunscreen and protective clothing

Safety and Health

Safety in Arica

Arica is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables such as wallets and phones in secure places.

Health in Arica

Arica is at a relatively high altitude, and visitors may experience altitude sickness. It is recommended to acclimatize to the altitude gradually and drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration. Visitors should also be aware of the strong sun and wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Arica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arica

The Chacalluta International Airport is located 18 kilometers from Arica’s city center. Taxis and rental cars are available at the airport for transportation to the city.

Bus Stations Near Arica

The Terminal de Buses de Arica is the main bus station in the city and offers regular connections to other destinations in Chile and Peru.

Other Options

For those traveling from nearby destinations such as Tacna in Peru, a border crossing is available. Visitors should ensure they have proper travel documents and visas for entry.

How to Get Around in Arica

Taxis and colectivos, shared taxis, are readily available for transportation within Arica. For those interested in cycling, there are bike rental options available. Walking is also a popular option for exploring the city’s sights and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Arica: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations worth visiting from Arica include the Huanchaca Ruins in Antofagasta and the Atacama Desert in Northern Chile. For those interested in exploring other countries, the nearby city of Tacna, Peru, offers a unique cultural experience.

Conclusion

Arica may be a lesser-known destination in Northern Chile, but it is nonetheless a gem that deserves attention. With its unique blend of cultural attractions, natural landscapes, and outdoor adventures, visitors can expect a truly unforgettable experience in Arica. The city’s welcoming community and vibrant energy add to the charm of this hidden treasure and make it a must-visit for any Chilean itinerary.
