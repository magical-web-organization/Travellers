Introduction to Achao

Achao, a small town located on the eastern coast of Chiloé Island, in Southern Chile, is a hidden gem for travelers seeking a mix of cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. This charming town boasts of a rich history, unique architecture, and stunning natural landscapes, making it a perfect destination for a relaxing getaway.

In this travel guide to Achao, we’ll help you plan your trip by highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Achao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Iglesia de Achao - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Iglesia de Achao is a 17th-century wooden church known for its unique architecture and stunning interior design.
  2. Museo de las Tradiciones Chonchinas - This small museum exhibits the traditional Chiloé Island culture, including wood carvings, textiles, and instruments.
  3. Palafitos - These colorful wooden houses built on stilts over the water are a symbol of the typical architecture of the area and can be seen along the waterfront.

Natural Attractions of Achao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lago Huillinco - This serene freshwater lake provides an ideal setting for kayaking, fishing, and swimming.
  2. Parque Nacional Chiloé - This national park features a diverse range of ecosystems such as temperate rainforests, wetlands, and beaches, all home to a variety of wildlife.
  3. Punta Pereira - Take a hike to the top of this hill to enjoy a panoramic view of the surrounding islands and ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Achao

  1. Ruta de los Palafitos - A scenic route along the coast of Achao that passes through the colorful palafitos and offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.
  2. Ruta de las Iglesias - This trail connects the various historical churches of the area and allows travelers to witness the unique architecture and history of each church.

Best Things to Do in Achao: Activities and Tours

  1. Fish Market Tour - Take a tour of the local fish market to observe the daily catch and learn about the traditional methods of fishing in the area.
  2. Traditional Chiloé Cooking Class - Learn to prepare some of the traditional Chiloé dishes like curanto, a seafood stew cooked on hot stones, and enjoy a feast with some local wine.
  3. Whale Watching Tour - From December to March, catch a glimpse of the magnificent humpback whales as they migrate through the Pacific Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Achao

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Curanto - A traditional seafood stew cooked on hot stones, usually served with potatoes, vegetables, and a glass of local wine.
  2. Milcao - A potato dough pancake served as a side dish or breakfast item.
  3. Cazuela de ave - A hearty chicken soup made with vegetables and rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Licor de Oro - A sweet liqueur made from the liquor of fermented golden apples.
  2. Chicha - A fermented alcoholic drink made from corn and flavored with various fruits and spices.

Where to Stay in Achao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hostal de Achao - A cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and stunning views of the sea.
  2. Hotel Don Lucas - A traditional hotel with spacious rooms and a central location.
  3. Camping Parque Hueney - A camping site located near Lago Huillinco, offering beautiful views and various outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Achao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Wool Products - The traditional Chiloé woolen garments, such as ponchos, scarves, and hats, make perfect souvenirs.
  2. Chiloé Baskets - Hand-woven from wild rush, these baskets are a unique and beautiful addition to any home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Achao

The culture of Achao is heavily influenced by its indigenous roots and Catholic traditions. Visitors can witness this rich mix of cultures in the local crafts, architecture, and religious practices. Respect for the environment and community is also an essential cultural aspect of the area.

Best Time to Visit Achao

The best time to visit Achao is during the summer months of December to February when the weather is pleasant, and various outdoor activities are available. However, travelers can also visit during the shoulder seasons of September to November or March to May for fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Achao

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and wildlife.
  • Try the traditional cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the religious practices.
  • Litter or pollute the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the local community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Achao

Achao is a relatively safe place for travelers. However, visitors should be cautious while hiking or exploring the natural surroundings and always follow the safety instructions issued by the tour guides.

Health in Achao

Visitors should be aware of the risks of mosquito-borne diseases in the area and take necessary precautions like using insect repellents and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Achao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Achao

The closest airport to Achao is Mocopulli Airport (MHC), located in the city of Castro, a 45-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Achao

There are frequent bus services from Puerto Montt to Castro, from where visitors can take a local bus or taxi to Achao.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Achao

Achao is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the surroundings or take a local bus to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Achao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Castro - The cultural capital of Chiloé Island, known for its colorful palafitos, traditional crafts, and historical sites.
  2. Ancud - A small coastal town with a strong naval history, featuring stunning beaches, fresh seafood, and wildlife reserves.

Conclusion

Achao, with its unique mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and culinary delights, is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience of Southern Chile. Plan your trip now and discover the beauty of Achao on your next adventure.
