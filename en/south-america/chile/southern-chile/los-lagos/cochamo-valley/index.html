Introduction to Cochamó Valley

Cochamó Valley is one of the best hidden gems in Chile when it comes to natural beauty. Located in Los Lagos in southern Chile, Cochamó Valley offers a unique blend of rich local culture, diverse landscapes, and adventurous activities.

List of Places to Visit in Cochamó Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cochamó Valley is home to several museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into local history and culture. Some popular options include:

  • Las Bandurrias Museum: This museum is dedicated to the indigenous Mapuche culture and is home to some of the oldest artifacts in Chile.
  • Santa Lucia Chapel: Built-in 1892, this chapel is an excellent example of colonial architecture and is a popular pilgrimage site.
  • The Petroglyphs of El Arco: These ancient petroglyphs are believed to date back to the Pre-Columbian era and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • The Andean Integration House: This museum showcases the cultural and historical ties between Chile and its Andean neighbors.

Natural Attractions of Cochamó Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cochamó Valley is known for its beautiful landscapes and diverse ecosystems. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions to add to your itinerary:

  • Cochamó River: This scenic river runs through the heart of Cochamó Valley and offers opportunities for activities like fishing, kayaking, and rafting.
  • La Junta: La Junta is a stunning natural park that is home to stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and several native species of flora and fauna.
  • Cerro Amazón: This mountain peak offers breathtaking views of the Cochamó Valley and is a popular trekking destination.
  • Reloncaví Sound: A fjord located near Cochamó Valley, with impressive Alerce trees growing right up to the water’s edge and dramatic landscapes with incredible views of the Pacific Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cochamó Valley

Cochamó Valley is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with excellent routes to explore for all skill levels. Some popular trails include:

  • Cochamó Valley Circuit: This 5-day hiking trek takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes in the region, including dense forests, trickling streams, and majestic mountains.
  • Paso El León Trek: Another multi-day trek that passes through beatiful mountains and is known for its stunning view of the glacier.
  • Cerro Amazón Trail: A relatively challenging day trek with picturesque views of Cochamó Valley.
  • Alerce Andino National Park: A protected area forty kilometers south of Puerto Varas with beautiful trails and camping sites that features one of the world’s oldest and largest trees.

Best Things to Do in Cochamó Valley: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking trails, you can engage in other activities and tours in Cochamó Valley. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Rafting and kayaking: Cochamó River offers an excellent rafting and kayaking experience for the adventure enthusiast.
  • Horseback riding: The valley’s rolling hills and diverse landscapes make it perfect for a leisurely horseback ride.
  • Zip-line tours: Zip line tours through the lush forests of Cochamó Valley are an ideal way to take in the rugged beauty of this region.
  • Rock climbing: Cochamó Valley is known for its sheer granite walls and offers some of the best rock climbing in Chile.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cochamó Valley

Here’s a suggested itinerary for exploring Cochamó Valley over the course of a week:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the cultural landmarks of Cochamó Valley, including the Santa Lucia Chapel and Las Bandurrias Museum.
  • Day 3-4: Trek along the stunning Cochamó Valley Circuit and explore the magnificent natural wonders of the valley, including serene rivers, rolling hills, and towering peaks.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a day of adventure activities, such as white water rafting or horseback riding.
  • Day 6: Take an exciting zip line tour through the lush forests of Cochamó Valley.
  • Day 7: End your trip by relaxing in one of Cochamó Valley’s many spas and enjoying the delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Cochamó Valley

Cochamó Valley offers many delicious and unique options to travelers in search of a gastronomic adventure.

Must-Try Dishes

Some famous dishes to try in Cochamó Valley include:

  • Curanto: A traditional dish made of meat, seafood, and vegetables cooked in an underground oven.
  • Paila Marina: A seafood soup made with a variety of local seafood, onions, and potatoes.
  • Milcao: A thick, pancake-like dish made from grated potatoes and cooked on a hot stone.
  • Caldillo de Congrio: A hearty soup made from conger eel, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and spices.

Local Beverages

Cochamó Valley produces several locally made alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages, such as:

  • Murtado: A delicious local tea made from rosehips.
  • Chicha: A locally brewed alcoholic beverage made from corn.
  • Bilz y Pap: A beloved non-alcoholic soda that comes in several fruity flavors.

Where to Stay in Cochamó Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cochamó Valley offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Refugio Cochamó: A rustic mountain lodge that offers panoramic views of the surrounding peaks and has all the essential amenities.
  • Cabañas el Bosque Cochamó: A cozy cabin nestled in the heart of the Cochamó forest, ideal for those seeking a peaceful getaway.
  • Hostel Estaquilla: An economical hostel with a great location near Cochamó River and several trailheads for trekking and hiking.

What to Buy in Cochamó Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cochamó Valley is known for its unique handicrafts and locally made products, such as:

  • Textiles: The valley is home to several skilled artisans who produce impressive textiles using traditional techniques.
  • Mapuche Handicrafts: Traditional Mapuche crafts include pottery, weavings, baskets knitted with vegetal fibers, among others.
  • Honey: The valley produces some of the best honey in the region, which is collected from local beehives.

Local Culture and Customs of Cochamó Valley

Cochamó Valley is rich in culture and customs, with several festivals and celebrations throughout the year, highlighting the local Mapuche culture, such as We Tripantu (New Year’s day Mapuche), koyagüe/fintanmapu (authentic cosmovision of Mapuche), Tirana (pilgrimage in honor to Santa Teresa de Los Andes). As a visitor, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Cochamó Valley

The best time to visit Cochamó Valley is during the summer months from December to March when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cochamó Valley

Do:

  • Do pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Don’t underestimate the intensity of some of the valley’s hiking trails.
  • Don’t forget travel essentials such as sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cochamó Valley

Cochamó Valley is generally safe for travelers; however, it’s essential to practice basic safety measures such as keeping your valuables in a safe place, avoiding traveling alone at night, and staying alert in crowded areas. Always check the river’s conditions before participating in any water activities as it can become dangerous during high tide.

Health in Cochamó Valley

Cochamó Valley is located in a remote area; thus, it’s recommended to bring any necessary medication with you. It’s also important to be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking, and ensure you have travel insurance and any required vaccinations.

Getting to Cochamó Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cochamó Valley

The nearest major airport to Cochamó Valley is El Tepual Airport which is approximately 1 hour and 50 minutes away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Cochamó Valley

The nearest bus station to the valley is in Puerto Montt or Puerto Varas. From either of these locations, you can take a local bus or a taxi to the valley.

Other Options

If you’re traveling from Santiago, you can take a flight to El Tepual airport or take a bus. The journey is roughly 1,000 kilometers and takes around 14-16 hours.

How to Get Around in Cochamó Valley

The best way to get around Cochamó Valley is by walking or biking. For longer distances, you can take a local bus or hire a car.

Where to Go Next from Cochamó Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Southern Chile, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Puerto Varas: This charming town on the shore of Lake Llanquihue offers some beautiful views of the Osorno Volcano and is known for its German architecture and cuisine.
  • Chiloé Island: A unique island with a rich cultural heritage and a blend of both modern and traditional farming techniques.
  • Lake District: A region that’s characterized by pristine lakes, beautiful rivers, and stunning mountain scenery.

Conclusion

Cochamó Valley is an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and local culture. With this comprehensive guide, you have all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to the stunning Cochamó Valley.
