Introduction to Futaleufu

Futaleufu is a small town located in the Los Lagos region of Southern Chile, framed by the Andes Mountains and surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes. Its charms lie in its pristine rivers, dense forests, and narrow valleys, making it a popular destination for adventure-seekers and nature-lovers alike.

Futaleufu has become a popular destination for whitewater rafting, hiking, fishing, kayaking, and mountain biking. Visitors can also immerse themselves in local culture, explore museums and witness ancient rock art.

Here is a comprehensive travel guide to Futaleufu, to help plan the perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Futaleufu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Regional de la Carretera Austral: This museum showcases the history and construction of Chile’s Southern Highway.
  • Casa del Escritor: This house-turned-museum celebrates local writers and poets.
  • Piedra el Gato: This attraction features ancient petroglyphs from the Tehuelche peoples, Chile’s indigenous group.

Natural Attractions of Futaleufu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Futaleufu River: This river is a world-renowned destination for whitewater rafting, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Futaleufu National Reserve: This reserve is home to a wide variety of wildlife and birdlife, and is ideal for hiking.
  • Las Escalas Glacier: This glacier is a fascinating sight for visitors interested in natural beauty and geology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Futaleufu

  • Sendero de las 3 Lagunas: This moderate hike offers stunning views of lakes and mountains.
  • La Espuela: A challenging and steep hike through the forest that rewards visitors with scenic views.
  • Mountain Bike Route from Futaleufu to Espolon Lake: A 20-kilometer bike ride that takes visitors through varied landscapes, from forests to open fields.

Best Things to Do in Futaleufu: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Futaleufu River: This is a must-do activity for adventure-seekers with varying levels of difficulty.
  • Fishing in the Futaleufu River: This activity can be done throughout the year, with trout and salmon as the main catch.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can explore the river’s turquoise waters, with a range of journeys suitable for all levels.
  • Horseback Riding: A unique way to explore the countryside, with options for full-day excursions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Futaleufu

Day 1: Arrival in Futaleufu. Day 2: Hike Sendero de las 3 Lagunas. Day 3: Rafting on the Futaleufu River. Day 4: Explore Las Escalas Glacier. Day 5: Visit the Museo Regional de la Carretera Austral. Day 6: Kayak on the Futaleufu River. Day 7: Departure from Futaleufu.

Food and Drink Scene of Futaleufu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanadas: Local savory pastries filled with meat or seafood.
  • Asado: Chilean barbecue, with meat and sausages cooked over open flames.
  • Sopaipillas: Fried dough, served as a snack or side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: A popular mixed drink that combines pisco brandy, lime juice, and egg whites.
  • Cerveza Artesanal: Chilean micro-brew beer, typically featuring local flavors like berries.

Where to Stay in Futaleufu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Espolon: A charming hotel with picturesque lake views.
  • Hostal Al Sur: Comfortable accommodations in the town center.
  • La Gringa: A rustic guesthouse, offering cozy cabins and a tranquil atmosphere.

What to Buy in Futaleufu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woolen Products: From sweaters to mittens, woolen products are made from the region’s sheep and are available in a range of sizes and colors.
  • Honey: Futaleufu is known for its high-quality honey, made from the region’s wildflowers.
  • Leather Goods: Locally crafted bags and purses.

Local Culture and Customs of Futaleufu

Futaleufu is home to a mixture of Mapuche and Patagonian cultures, which influence the town’s cuisine, music, and traditions. Visitors are advised to be respectful of these customs and traditions when exploring the town.

Best Time to Visit Futaleufu

The best time to visit Futaleufu is during the summer months, from December to March. This is the prime season for outdoor adventure activities, and the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Futaleufu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be prepared for outdoor and adventure activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local environments.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in adventure sports.
  • Assume everyone speaks English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Futaleufu

Futaleufu is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions when engaging in adventure sports and follow safety procedures put in place by tour operators.

Health in Futaleufu

Visitors to Futaleufu should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before arriving. Also, they are advised to check with their doctor regarding vaccinations and specific health concerns for the region.

Getting to Futaleufu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Futaleufu

The nearest airports to Futaleufu are Balmaceda Airport, located 200 kilometers away, and El Tepual Airport in Puerto Montt, located 400 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Futaleufu

Futaleufu has a bus terminal that connects to major cities in Chile and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Private transfers and car rentals are also available within Futaleufu.

How to Get Around in Futaleufu

Futaleufu is a small town, and walking is a great option for getting around. Buses and taxis are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Futaleufu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pumalin National Park: This park is home to lush forests, lakes and rivers, and is only a couple of hours away from Futaleufu.
  • Puerto Montt: A bustling city, located 500 kilometers away, that serves as a gateway to the Chilean Patagonia and Chiloe Island.
  • Bariloche: A popular Argentinean destination located across the border, known for its skiing and chocolate.

Conclusion

Futaleufu is a gem in Southern Chile, showcasing the beauty of the Andes Mountains and offering a wide range of adventure activities and cultural experiences. Planning a trip to Futaleufu is easy, and visitors will leave with unforgettable memories of this remarkable town.
