Introduction to Paramaribo

Located in the north-eastern part of South America, Suriname is a small country that’s famous for its pristine rainforests, diverse wildlife, Dutch colonial architecture, and vibrant culture. One of the best places to experience all these is the capital city of Paramaribo. Nestled on the banks of the Suriname River, Paramaribo is a bustling city that’s home to a rich history, cultural diversity, and exciting outdoor adventures.

In this article, we’ll offer a comprehensive guide to Paramaribo, providing you with useful tips, insights, and recommendations to help you plan your trip and make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Paramaribo

Paramaribo is a treasure trove of interesting places to visit, from historical landmarks to natural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places when you’re in Paramaribo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Suriname Museum: Explore Suriname’s rich cultural heritage and history at the Suriname Museum, which showcases fascinating exhibits on the country’s indigenous peoples, colonial past, and more.

  • Fort Zeelandia: One of the most significant historical landmarks in Suriname, Fort Zeelandia is a 17th-century fort that has served various purposes throughout its history. Today, it houses the Suriname Museum of Contemporary Art and is a popular tourist attraction.

  • Saint Peter and Paul Cathedral: A beautiful Catholic cathedral built in the Romanesque Revival style in the 19th century, the Saint Peter and Paul Cathedral is a stunning piece of architecture and a landmark of Paramaribo.

Natural Attractions of Paramaribo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peperpot Nature Park: A former coffee and cocoa plantation, the Peperpot Nature Park is now a nature reserve that boasts a diverse ecosystem of flora and fauna, including monkeys, iguanas, and tropical birds.

  • Brownsberg Nature Park: For stunning views of pristine rainforest and breathtaking waterfalls, head to Brownsberg Nature Park, located just a few hours from Paramaribo. You can take a hike to the top of the mountain for panoramic views or swim in the refreshing waterfalls.

  • Commewijne River: Take a leisurely boat ride along the Commewijne River for a relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Along the way, you’ll pass lush mangroves, historic plantations, and charming fishing villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paramaribo

Paramaribo is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Biking the Waterkant: A leisurely bike ride along the Suriname River, the Waterkant route takes you past the city’s historic buildings, bustling markets, and lively nightlife.

  • Hiking Mount Kasikasima: For a challenging hike that rewards you with stunning views, head to Mount Kasikasima. This trail takes you deep into the rainforest, where you’ll encounter exotic wildlife and enjoy breathtaking views of the Suriname River.

  • Biking the Commewijne Plantation Route: If you’re interested in history and culture, the Commewijne Plantation Route is a must-ride. This trail takes you through the former plantations of Suriname, where you can learn about the country’s colonial past and see the remains of historic buildings.

Best Things to Do in Paramaribo: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, cultural experiences, or leisurely activities, Paramaribo has something to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Paramaribo:

  • Take a walking tour of historic Paramaribo: Stroll through the city’s historic center and discover the unique blend of Dutch and Creole architecture that gives Paramaribo its character.

  • Join a river tour: Explore the Suriname River and its surrounding areas by boat. You can go fishing, bird watching, or just enjoy the scenic views.

  • Visit a local market: Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting one of Paramaribo’s vibrant markets. Try some local treats and pick up some souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paramaribo

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Paramaribo:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel. Explore the city’s historic center on foot.

  • Day 2: Visit Fort Zeelandia and the Suriname Museum to learn more about the country’s history and culture.

  • Day 3: Take a boat tour along the Suriname River and visit a local village.

  • Day 4: Explore the Peperpot Nature Park and spot some exotic wildlife.

  • Day 5: Hike to the top of Mount Kasikasima for stunning views of the rainforest and the Suriname River.

  • Day 6: Head to Brownsberg Nature Park for a refreshing dip in the waterfalls.

  • Day 7: Take it easy and enjoy the local food scene. Try some of the must-try dishes listed below.

Food and Drink Scene of Paramaribo

The food scene in Paramaribo is a mix of Dutch, Creole, and Javanese cuisines, offering a unique blend of flavors and spices. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bami Goreng: A popular Javanese dish, Bami Goreng is fried noodles with meat and vegetables.

  • Roti: A popular dish in Suriname, Roti is a flatbread stuffed with spiced potatoes, vegetables, and meat.

  • Pom: A flavorful dish made with cassava, chicken, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Parbo Beer: A popular local beer in Suriname.

  • Guava Juice: Made from locally grown guavas, guava juice is a refreshing drink that pairs well with spicy foods.

Where to Stay in Paramaribo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Paramaribo has a range of accommodation options to choose from, catering to every budget and preference. Here are some options:

  • Royal Torarica: Located in the heart of Paramaribo, Royal Torarica is a luxurious hotel that offers excellent amenities, including a spa, pool, and restaurant.

  • Eco Resort Inn: A sustainable resort that offers an eco-friendly accommodation experience.

  • Queens Hotel & Casino: A budget-friendly hotel that offers basic amenities and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Paramaribo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Paramaribo, there are several items you can buy as souvenirs or gifts, including:

  • Wooden handicrafts: Suriname is known for its intricate wooden handicrafts, such as masks, totems, and carvings.

  • Maroon crafts: The Maroons are one of the indigenous groups in Suriname, and they produce several handicrafts, such as baskets, bags, and jewelry.

  • Spices and herbs: Suriname is home to several herbs and spices, such as vanilla, pepper, and nutmeg, which make for excellent souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Paramaribo

Paramaribo is a city that celebrates diversity, and locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Greet people with respect: It’s important to greet people with respect and politeness. Handshakes are the standard form of greeting.

  • Dress modestly: Surinamese people are generally conservative, so it’s best to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Observe locals: When in doubt, observe how locals dress, act, and speak and follow their lead.

Best Time to Visit Paramaribo

Paramaribo has a tropical climate, and the best time to visit is during the dry season, from mid-February to mid-August, when rainfall is low, and temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paramaribo

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to help you during your stay in Paramaribo:

Do:

  • Learn some Dutch: Dutch is the official language of Suriname, and while most locals speak English, it’s always appreciated when visitors can speak some Dutch.

  • Try the local cuisine: The local food scene in Paramaribo is vibrant and diverse, so make sure to try some of the must-try dishes listed above.

  • Be respectful of local customs: Surinamese people are friendly and hospitable, but it’s important to respect their cultural customs and way of life.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water: It’s not recommended to drink tap water in Suriname, so stick to bottled water.

  • Don’t wander around at night: While Paramaribo is generally safe, it’s best to avoid walking around at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paramaribo

Paramaribo is considered a relatively safe city, although petty theft can occur. Here are some safety tips:

  • Be alert: As with any city, it’s best to be alert and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

  • Don’t carry too much cash: Keep only the cash you need for the day, and leave the rest in a safe place in your hotel.

  • Avoid walking alone at night: It’s best to avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Paramaribo

Suriname has a few health risks, including mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue fever. It’s essential to take proper precautions such as wearing mosquito repellent and staying indoors during peak mosquito hours.

Getting to Paramaribo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paramaribo

The Johan Adolf Pengel Airport is the main airport serving Paramaribo, with direct flights to and from several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Paramaribo

Paramaribo has several bus stations, including the Central Bus Station and the North Bus Station, that offer bus services to neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

You can also reach Paramaribo by boat, as the city is located on the banks of the Suriname River, or by car, if you’re coming from neighboring countries.

How to Get Around in Paramaribo

The best way to get around Paramaribo is on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available, but it’s essential to negotiate the fare before you get into the cab.

Where to Go Next from Paramaribo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Suriname has several other exciting cities and attractions to explore, including:

  • Galibi: A coastal village famous for its sea turtle conservation center.

  • Nieuw Amsterdam: A historic Dutch fort known for its beautiful views.

  • Cayenne: The capital of French Guiana, Cayenne is a vibrant city that’s only a few hours away from Paramaribo.

Conclusion

Paramaribo, Suriname, is a unique and fascinating destination that offers plenty of cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and opportunities for relaxation. With our comprehensive guide, we hope you have all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this beautiful city.
