Introduction to Colombia

Colombia is a South American country that boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and warm and welcoming locals. From the vibrant cities of Bogota and Medellin, to the lush Amazonian rainforest, to the Caribbean coast, there’s something for every traveler in Colombia. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll share all the essential information you need to plan your trip to this amazing country.

Local Culture and Customs of Colombia

In Colombia, family and community are very important. Colombians are also known for their love of music and dance, especially salsa. It’s important to be respectful of Colombian culture and customs, including greeting locals with a handshake and using formal titles such as “señor” and “señora”.

Best Time to Visit Colombia

The best time to visit Colombia depends on your itinerary and interests. Generally, the dry season from December to March and July to August is the best time to visit, but the rainy season from April to June and September to November can also offer unique experiences such as the Caño Cristales river.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colombia

Do:

  1. Take a salsa class in Cali
  2. Explore the historic district of Cartagena
  3. Try local food and drink
  4. Visit Tayrona National Park
  5. Learn some Spanish phrases

Don’t:

  1. Wear flashy jewelry
  2. Take unmarked taxis
  3. Walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas
  4. Drink tap water
  5. Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health in Colombia

Safety in Colombia

Colombia is generally safe for tourists, but it’s important to take precautions to avoid any potential risks. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoid traveling alone or in remote areas. Keep your valuables out of sight and only use official taxis.

Health in Colombia

It’s important to take proper precautions to stay healthy in Colombia. Drink bottled water, peel all fruits and vegetables, and only eat food that has been thoroughly cooked.
