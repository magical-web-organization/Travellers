Introduction to Huila

Located in the Andino region of Colombia, Huila is a hidden gem that offers cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. The region is known for its coffee production, stunning landscape, and rich history. Travelers will find a mix of colonial architecture, modern amenities, and traditional customs that make this area a unique and unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Huila

Huila has many culturally significant places to explore and natural attractions to experience. Here’s a list of some of the top places to visit in Huila.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Tolima Grande: This museum exhibits how the people lived in the Tolima Grande area from prehistoric times to present day. The museum showcases pottery, natural history, and religious sculptures, providing travelers with an immersive cultural experience.

  • Casa de la Cultura: A cultural center that showcases singing, dancing, music, and literature of Huila. Visitors can learn more about the rich history and culture of Huila through interactive exhibits and workshops.

  • San Juan Bautista Cathedral: Built in 1955, this cathedral is a beautiful example of neo-Gothic architecture. Visitors can admire the stained glass windows and high ceilings while taking in the spiritual atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Huila: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cordillera de los Andes: Huila is located in the Andean mountains, providing travelers with incredible views and hiking opportunities.

  • Desierto de la Tatacoa: This desert is a unique natural attraction that offers a distinct landscape of cacti, red rock formations, and natural observatories.

  • Salto de Bordones: This 590-foot waterfall is a natural wonder that plunges into a crystal-clear pool of water, making it a popular spot for swimming and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huila

Huila is a hiker’s paradise with a variety of trails available. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes available:

  • Caminata al Páramo de las Hermosas: This hiking trail takes visitors to the highest point of the Tolima department. Travelers will witness incredible views of the Andean mountains along the way.

  • Caminata al Farallones de Villalobos: This hiking trail offers travelers a chance to explore stunning waterfalls and natural swimming pools.

  • Cycling Tour of Huila: Visitors can explore the region on a bike tour and take in the scenic views at their own pace.

Best Things to Do in Huila: Activities and Tours

Huila offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the top things to do in Huila:

  • Coffee tours: Huila is known for its coffee production, and travelers can take a tour of local coffee farms and learn the process of coffee production.

  • Whitewater rafting: Travelers looking for an adventure can take a whitewater rafting tour of the Magdalena River, one of the largest rivers in Colombia.

  • Hot springs: Visitors can unwind and relax at one of the many hot springs in the region, including Termales de Rivera and Termales de Santa Rosa.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huila

A week-long itinerary for Huila could look like this:

  • Day 1: Explore the city of Neiva and visit the Casa de la Cultura and San Juan Bautista Cathedral.
  • Day 2: Take a coffee tour and learn about the process of coffee production at a local farm.
  • Day 3: Explore the Desierto de la Tatacoa and witness the unique landscape.
  • Day 4: Take a hiking tour of the Cordillera de los Andes and admire the breathtaking views.
  • Day 5: Visit the Salto de Bordones waterfall and take a swim in the crystal-clear pool.
  • Day 6: Relax at one of the many hot springs in the region.
  • Day 7: Take a cycling tour of Huila and explore the region’s scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Huila

Huila has a rich food and drink scene that showcases the region’s unique flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sopa de mondongo: A traditional soup made with tripe, vegetables, and spices.
  • Lechona: A roasted pork dish stuffed with rice and beans.
  • Arequipe: A caramel-like sauce made from condensed milk.

Local Beverages

  • Tinto: A traditional Colombian coffee made with dark roast beans.
  • Chicha: A fermented corn drink that has a sweet and tangy flavor.
  • Aguardiente: A traditional Colombian liquor that is made from sugarcane.

Where to Stay in Huila: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huila offers a wide variety of accommodations to suit any traveler’s needs. Here are some top choices:

  • Hotel Neiva Plaza: A modern hotel located in the heart of Neiva, offering comfortable rooms and amenities.

  • Hostal Katio: A boutique hostel that offers a unique cultural experience, located in the historic Alamos neighborhood.

  • La Casa del Embajador: A guesthouse located in the countryside, offering a tranquil atmosphere and stunning views.

What to Buy in Huila: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huila offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that travelers can bring home, such as:

  • Coffee: Huila is known for its coffee production, and visitors can bring home high-quality coffee beans to enjoy.
  • Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase handcrafted pottery, textiles, and jewelry that showcase the region’s unique craftsmanship.
  • Panela: A traditional Colombian product made from sugarcane and used as a sweetener or ingredient in cooking.

Local Culture and Customs of Huila

Huila has a rich cultural heritage and traditions that are worth experiencing. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Language: Spanish is the official language in Huila, but locals may also speak indigenous languages.
  • Dress Code: It is recommended to dress conservatively in Huila, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Music and Dance: Huila has a vibrant music and dance scene, and travelers can witness folkloric performances throughout the region.

Best Time to Visit Huila

The best time to visit Huila is from December to April when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the region is temperate throughout the year, so visitors can enjoy outdoor activities year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huila

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn about the cultural history of the region.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drug use.
  • Disrespect religious sites and customs.
  • Leave litter when exploring natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huila

Huila is a safe area for travelers to visit, but it is always important to exercise caution. Here are some tips to stay safe:

  • Keep an eye on personal belongings, such as purses and wallets.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Be aware of the surroundings when using public transportation.

Health in Huila

It is recommended to take the necessary precautions when traveling to Huila, such as:

  • Make sure to drink bottled water or boiled water.
  • Apply insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Seek medical attention if feeling ill.

Getting to Huila: Transportation Options and Logistics

Huila is easily accessible by various means of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Huila

  • Gustavo Artunduaga Paredes Airport: The closest airport to Huila, located in the city of Neiva.

Bus Stations Near Huila

  • Terminal de Transportes de Neiva: The main bus terminal in Huila that serves various cities in Colombia.

Other Options

  • Private Car Service: Visitors can hire a private car service to take them to Huila from nearby cities.
  • Rental Car: Car rental services are available in nearby cities for travelers who wish to drive to Huila.

How to Get Around in Huila

There are various means of transportation available for travelers to explore Huila, including:

  • Taxi: Taxis are available throughout the region, and prices are negotiable.
  • Bus: There are local buses that can take travelers to different parts of the region.
  • Bicycle: Travelers who enjoy cycling can rent bicycles to explore Huila at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Huila: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Huila is surrounded by other fascinating destinations that travelers can explore. Some nearby places to visit include:

  • San Agustín: This town is known for its ancient pre-Columbian statues and tomb sculptures.

  • Tierradentro: An archeological site that offers visitors a glimpse into the historical pre-Columbian culture of the region.

  • Villa de Leyva: A colonial town that offers beautiful architecture and a charming atmosphere.

Conclusion

Huila offers travelers a unique mix of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Visitors can explore historical sites, take in the natural beauty of the Andean mountains, and relax in one of the many hot springs in the region. With a rich food and drink scene, a variety of accommodations, and nearby destinations to explore, Huila is a must-visit destination for any traveler interested in experiencing the diverse culture and landscapes of Colombia.
