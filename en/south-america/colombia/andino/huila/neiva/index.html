Introduction to Neiva

Located in the Huila region of Andino in Colombia, Neiva is a vibrant city with a rich cultural history and natural beauty. It is a gateway to the Tatacoa Desert, famous for its unique reddish landscape and star-filled night skies. Neiva is also a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a variety of hiking and biking trails, scenic views, and adventure activities.

List of Places to Visit in Neiva

Whether you’re interested in exploring Neiva’s cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, or its renowned food and drink scene, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in this lively city. Here’s a comprehensive list of the top places to visit in Neiva:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Arqueológico: This museum houses a large collection of pre-Columbian artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and tools from the indigenous peoples of the Huila region.
  • Catedral de la Inmaculada Concepción: A stunningly beautiful Catholic cathedral with a lovely plaza out front.
  • Casa de la Cultura: The hub of the city’s arts and culture scene, this colonial-era building hosts art exhibitions, music concerts, and theater productions.
  • Parque Santander: A central plaza and park with a variety of street vendors and shops, as well as a historic monument to recognize important moments in the city’s history.

Natural Attractions of Neiva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tatacoa Desert: A surreal, reddish landscape that covers over 300 square kilometers of unique terrain. It’s perfect for star-gazing during the night, hiking and riding ATVs in the day.
  • Río Magdalena: Colombia’s longest river, it boasts stunning views of lush banks, stunning rock formations, and abundant wildlife. It’s an ideal place for fishing, swimming, and river tours.
  • Páramo de las Papas: A hiking trek that takes you up to the altitude of 5,000 meters, allowing for breathtaking views of the Andes, from glaciers to tropical forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neiva

Neiva offers a variety of hiking and biking trails throughout its picturesque landscapes. Here are some of the top routes:

  • La Quebrada: A scenic trail that follows the route of a nearby river, featuring wooded paths and open fields. It’s a popular spot for picnics and family outings.
  • Morro de las Tres Cruces: A challenging hiking trek with stunning panoramic views of the mountains, valleys, and the city of Neiva.
  • La Quincha: This mountain biking trail features varied terrain with steep climbs and descents, providing a thrilling scenic ride.

Best Things to Do in Neiva: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to thrilling adventure activities, there are plenty of things to do in Neiva for travelers of all interests:

  • Desert stargazing: At night, the desert sky above Neiva is one of the clearest views of the stars you will ever see. Guides can take you on tours to teach you more about the stargazing experience and the science behind the sightings.
  • River rafting: Rafting tours in the nearby Río Magdalena offer views of stunning landscapes and an adrenaline-filled experience like no other. Perfect for a group of friends or adventurers!
  • Coffee Farm Tours: Take a tour of one of the many family-owned coffee farms in the region, learn how coffee is grown, harvested, and processed, and indulge in a cup or two of freshly brewed coffee afterwards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neiva

With so much to see and do in Neiva, planning an itinerary can be overwhelming. Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the city:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the city center, museums, and historical landmarks
Day 2 Take a trip to Tatacoa Desert and enjoy the sunset there
Day 3 Go on a coffee farm tour and try some samples
Day 4 Go river rafting or canoeing on the Río Magdalena
Day 5 Take in the stunning views of the Morro de las Tres Cruces, one of the best vantage points in the area
Day 6 Hike in the Páramo de las Papas or go horseback riding
Day 7 Relax at one of Neiva’s many spas or hot springs

Food and Drink Scene of Neiva

Neiva’s food and drink scene is a highlight of any trip to the city, with cuisine that is both diverse and flavorful. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mute: A hearty stew with beef, pork, plantains, cassava, corn and other vegetables, typical of the Huila region.
  • Sudado de Bocachico: a delicious fish dish made with spices and served with rice.
  • Empanadas de pipián y de arroz: savory pastries stuffed with a mix of rice flour, peanuts, corn, bread, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Huila region produces some of the best coffee in Colombia. You can try it everywhere, in coffee shops or in your hotel, and even take a bag home if you like it.
  • Canelazo: a traditional alcoholic drink made with sugar, cinnamon, and aguardiente (or another local clear liquor). An ideal way to warm up if you happen to visit in the cooler months.

Where to Stay in Neiva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Neiva has a variety of accommodations to suit different budget levels, ranging from ultra-luxe to inexpensive guesthouses. It is relatively easy to find something most suitable in the city, depending on your preference and budget level. Here are some options to consider:

  • Luxury hotels: San Pedro Plaza Hotel, GHL Comfort Hotel, and Casa Hotel Tumburagua
  • Mid-range hotels: Hotel Campestre Tierra de Oro, Hotel Geográfico and Hotel Terraza Suite
  • Budget-friendly options: Hostal Neiva, Alunas Hostel and Pension Casa Andalucia.

What to Buy in Neiva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Neiva is known for its unique local products, from handicrafts to agricultural produce. Don’t forget to bring home some lovely souvenirs for your loved ones. Here are some unique products that make excellent gifts:

  • Galan: A locally grown flavorful fruit ideal for juices and cocktails, not often found in the other parts of the world.
  • Pottery and handicrafts : Neiva locals are known for their pottery work, producing beautiful items from plates and jars to vases and mugs.
  • Clothes and Accessories: The city is famous for its traditional weavings and outfits, such as the ruana, a poncho-like shawl ideal for cooler evenings.

Local Culture and Customs of Neiva

Neiva is known for its rich cultural heritage, and visitors can immerse themselves in the city’s traditions and customs. Some important cultural practices to be aware of include:

  • Festival of Bambuco: A traditional dance and music festival that celebrates Huila’s indigenous culture and heritage. It usually takes place in June and July.
  • Indigenous communities: Neiva is home to several indigenous communities, including the Paez and Nasa peoples. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respectfully engage with them.
  • Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta: The stunning mountain range on the north coast of Colombia is considered sacred to many indigenous communities, and visitors to this area should be aware of and respect the cultural significance of the site.

Best Time to Visit Neiva

The best time to visit Neiva is between December and March, during the dry season. July and August are also popular months to visit, especially for the Festival of Bambuco. September and October offer the best temperatures, with warm, dry days and cool evenings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neiva

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts for visitors to Neiva:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish before your trip. Although many locals speak English, a basic knowledge of Spanish will help you navigate the city and immerse yourself in local culture.
  • Respect the local traditions and customs of the indigenous communities.
  • Try some local cuisine, including Mute and empanadas de pipián.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites.
  • Walk alone at night or in isolated areas of the city.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Neiva should take proper precautions to ensure their safety and health during their trip. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Neiva

  • Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid areas known for crime.
  • Use licensed taxis or transportation services to avoid getting scammed or robbed.
  • Keep your valuables safe and out of reach.

Health in Neiva

  • Drink only bottled or purified water.
  • Apply mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect your skin from the sun’s damaging rays.

Getting to Neiva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Neiva is easily accessible by air, land, or water, with a variety of transportation options available. Here are some of the most common options:

  • Airports Near Neiva: The city’s main airport is Benito Salas Airport (NVA), which is serviced by various regional airlines. Other nearby airports include El Dorado International Airport (BOG) in Bogotá, and Perales Airport (IBE) in Ibagué.
  • Bus Stations Near Neiva: Terminal de Transportes de Neiva is the city’s main bus station, with frequent connections to Bogotá, Cali, and other major cities in Colombia.
  • Other Options: Car rentals and private transportation services are also available.

How to Get Around in Neiva

Neiva is a relatively small and walkable city, and most of the main attractions are easily accessible on foot. However, if you prefer to use public transportation, taxis and buses are readily available and inexpensive. For those who prefer more independent means of getting around, renting a car or bike is also a viable option.

Where to Go Next from Neiva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Neiva is an ideal hub for exploring the nearby attractions and destinations in the Huila region and beyond. Here are some of the best places to visit on a day trip or short excursion from Neiva:

  • Villa de Leyva: A charming colonial town with narrow streets, picturesque churches, and a lively central plaza, you can get there in 3 hours by car or bus.
  • San Agustín Archaeological Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park features pre-Columbian sculptures and ruins of ancient civilizations and history. From Neiva, it’s about a 4-hour drive/ bus ride.
  • Popayán: Known as the “White City,” Popayán features colonial architecture, majestic churches, and a vibrant arts and culture scene. It’s a four-hour drive from Neiva.

Conclusion

Neiva is a must-visit for travelers in Colombia, filled with natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. With this detailed guide, you’re ready to plan an unforgettable trip to this vibrant and welcoming city!
