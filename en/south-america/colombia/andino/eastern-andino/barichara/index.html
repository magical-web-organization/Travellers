Introduction to Barichara

Tucked away in the Eastern Andes region of Colombia, Barichara is a colonial town known for its well-preserved architecture, pleasant climate, and breathtaking natural beauty. With its charming streets, picturesque plazas, and stunning views of the surrounding valleys, Barichara is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Colombia. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich history, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply soak in the laid-back atmosphere, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in Barichara.

List of Places to Visit in Barichara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iglesia de la Inmaculada Concepción: The main church of Barichara, this impressive colonial-era church stands at the heart of the town and features exquisite religious artwork.
  • Museo de Arte Religioso: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the city’s religious history, with a fascinating collection of 17th and 18th-century religious art and artifacts.
  • Casa de la Cultura: Housed in a beautifully restored colonial mansion, this cultural center hosts art exhibitions, workshops, and other events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Barichara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Camino Real: This ancient stone path connects Barichara to the nearby town of Guane. It’s a beautiful hike that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Parque Nacional del Chicamocha: Located just a short drive from Barichara, this national park is home to stunning canyons, spectacular views, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, paragliding, and more.
  • Cascada del Salto del Mico: This beautiful waterfall is located just outside of town and is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barichara

  • Camino Real: As mentioned above, this ancient stone path is an excellent choice for hikers looking to explore the region on foot.
  • La Cumbre: This trail offers stunning views of the countryside and is a challenging but rewarding hike.
  • Biking Adventure: Take a guided bike tour through the countryside surrounding Barichara and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Barichara: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the countryside on horseback and experience the local way of life.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn to prepare traditional Colombian dishes with a local chef.
  • Coffee Tour: Visit a local coffee farm and learn about the process of growing and harvesting coffee beans.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barichara

Day 1: Arrive in Barichara and visit the Iglesia de la Inmaculada Concepción and the Parque Central.

Day 2: Hike along the Camino Real to the town of Guane and explore its colonial-era streets and buildings.

Day 3: Take a guided bike tour through the countryside and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Visit the Parque Nacional del Chicamocha and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking or paragliding.

Day 5: Relax in town and enjoy its charming streets and plazas.

Day 6: Take a cooking class and learn to prepare traditional Colombian dishes.

Day 7: Visit a local coffee farm and learn about the process of growing and harvesting coffee beans.

Food and Drink Scene of Barichara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sancocho: A traditional Colombian soup made with yucca, plantain, meat, and vegetables.
  • Arepas: Grilled corn cakes served with a variety of toppings, including cheese, meat, and avocado.
  • Empanadas: Deep-fried or baked pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Aguapanela: A sweet drink made from sugar cane.
  • Canelazo: A hot drink made with aguapanela and cinnamon.
  • Chicha: A fermented drink made from corn or other grains.

Where to Stay in Barichara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Terra Barichara: A beautiful colonial-style hotel located in the heart of town.
  • Casa Barichara Boutique: This charming guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a tranquil setting.
  • Hostal La Nube: A budget-friendly option that offers basic rooms and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Barichara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Barichara is known for its beautiful ceramics, featuring intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  • Santafereños: These handmade dolls are a traditional Colombian craft and make for a unique souvenir.
  • Aguardiente: This traditional Colombian liquor is made from sugarcane and spices and can be found at local markets and liquor stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Barichara

The people of Barichara are known for their traditional customs and laid-back way of life. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including religious holidays and ceremonies. Also, it’s important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, such as churches and cathedrals.

Best Time to Visit Barichara

The best time to visit Barichara is from December to February when the weather is dry and sunny. March to May is also a good time to visit, but the temperatures can be slightly cooler. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (June to September) when the roads can be muddy and impassable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barichara

Do:

  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including religious holidays and ceremonies.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Do explore the countryside and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t dress immodestly when visiting religious sites, such as churches and cathedrals.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t engage in risky behavior, such as hiking alone or going out at night alone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barichara

Barichara is a safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution when traveling alone at night or outside of town. It’s also a good idea to keep an eye on your belongings and not carry large sums of money with you.

Health in Barichara

There are no major health concerns in Barichara. However, visitors should be aware of the high altitude and take the appropriate precautions, such as staying hydrated and taking it slow when hiking or engaging in strenuous activities.

Getting to Barichara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barichara

Barichara doesn’t have its airport. The closest airport is Palonegro International Airport in Bucaramanga, which is approximately a 3-hour drive from Barichara.

Bus Stations Near Barichara

The bus station in San Gil is the closest to Barichara, and there are frequent buses from Bogota, Bucaramanga, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Private car hire can be arranged from Bucaramanga to Barichara.

How to Get Around in Barichara

Walking is the best way to get around Barichara, and most of the town’s attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and motorbike taxis are also available for longer distances or for those with mobility issues.

Where to Go Next from Barichara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Gil: This nearby town is known as the capital of adventure sports in Colombia, and offers a range of outdoor activities, including white water rafting and rock climbing.
  • Villa de Leyva: This colonial town is known for its well-preserved architecture and charming cobblestone streets.
  • Bucaramanga: This bustling city is home to a range of cultural attractions and is the gateway to the Chicamocha Canyon and other natural wonders.

Conclusion

Whether you’re exploring the historic streets of Barichara, hiking through the beautiful countryside, or enjoying the local cuisine and culture, this charming colonial town has something to offer every traveler. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and laid-back atmosphere, Barichara is one of the best destinations to visit in Colombia.
