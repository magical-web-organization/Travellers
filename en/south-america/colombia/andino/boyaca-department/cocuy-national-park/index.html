Cocuy National Park is a breathtaking paradise located in Boyacá, Andino, Colombia, South America. The park is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers as it encompasses over 300,000 hectares of Andean forest, páramo, and snow-capped peaks. Cocuy National Park is also a culturally rich place, with its indigenous communities preserving their customs and traditions for ages.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Cocuy National Park, including its top attractions, the best time to visit, things to do, and much more.

Introduction to Cocuy National Park

Cocuy National Park, also known as Parque Nacional Natural de El Cocuy, was established in 1977 to preserve the Andean ecosystems and the biodiversity of the region. The park is home to Colombia’s second-highest peak, Pico de Cocuy, which stands at an altitude of 5,330 meters.

The park offers visitors a diverse range of landscapes, including forests, glaciers, lakes, and páramo, making it an ideal destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike. The park is also a sacred place of worship for the indigenous communities, who consider the mountains as their gods.

Day 1: Arrive in Cocuy National Park and take a leisurely hike to La Cueva del Humo. Day 2: Embark on a challenging hike to Pico de Cocuy. Day 3: Take a day trip to the neighboring Güicán National Park. Day 4: Go on a horseback riding tour to the stunning Laguna Grande de la Sierra. Day 5: Experience the local culture by touring the indigenous communities in the area. Day 6-7: Relax and spend your remaining days exploring the many trails and breathtaking landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Cocuy National Park

The indigenous communities of Cocuy National Park have a rich culture and traditions that have been passed down from generation to generation. Visitors to the park can experience their way of life by visiting their communities or attending one of their festivals. It’s essential to respect their customs and show appreciation for their culture.

Best Time to Visit Cocuy National Park

The best time to visit Cocuy National Park is from December to March when the weather is dry, and the snow-capped peaks are visible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cocuy National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for the hikes.
  3. Stay on marked trails while hiking.
  4. Carry water and snacks while hiking.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave trash behind.
  2. Pick or damage any plants or wildlife.
  3. Camp outside designated camping areas.
  4. Ignore the park rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cocuy National Park

Cocuy National Park is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid accidents and stay safe while hiking. It’s essential to hike with a guide and avoid hiking alone, especially at night.

Health in Cocuy National Park

Visitors to Cocuy National Park should consult their doctor and be up to date with all necessary vaccinations. It’s also essential to carry a first aid kit, insect repellent, and sunscreen.

Getting to Cocuy National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cocuy National Park

The nearest airports to Cocuy National Park are Palonegro International Airport in Bucaramanga and El Dorado International Airport in Bogotá.

Bus Stations Near Cocuy National Park

The closest bus stations to Cocuy National Park are in El Cocuy and Guican.

Other Options

Private transportation services are also available from major Colombian cities to Cocuy National Park.

How to Get Around in Cocuy National Park

The best way to get around Cocuy National Park is by foot or by horseback. Some areas of the park are only accessible by horseback or on foot, so it’s essential to plan your route.
