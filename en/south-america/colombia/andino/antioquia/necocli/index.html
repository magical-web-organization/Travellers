Introduction to Necoclí

Welcome to Necoclí, a charming port town located in the Antioquia Department of Colombia. The town boasts a rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination for travelers in South America. From stunning beaches and waterfalls to eclectic museums and delicious cuisine, Necoclí has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Necoclí

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Histórico Local: Located in the heart of Necoclí, this museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the town’s rich history and culture. The exhibits feature artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays that showcase the area’s Afro-Colombian heritage.

  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora del Rosario: This stunning 16th-century church is a testament to the town’s Spanish colonial past. The church features elegant architecture, intricate carvings, and stained glass windows that are sure to impress visitors.

Natural Attractions of Necoclí: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa Los Tres Dedos: This beautiful beach is one of the town’s main attractions. With soft white sand and crystal-clear water, it’s a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

  • Cascada de la Caimana: Located just outside of Necoclí, this picturesque waterfall is nestled in a lush forested area. It’s a great spot for hiking, picnicking, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Necoclí

  • La Ruta del Anfibio: This scenic hiking and biking trail winds through the mangroves and dunes of Necoclí. It offers stunning views of the coastline and wildlife sightings along the way.

Best Things to Do in Necoclí: Activities and Tours

  • Dolphin Watching Tour: Take a boat ride out into the ocean to spot playful dolphins in their natural habitat.

  • Local Cuisine Tour: Sample the region’s delicious seafood and traditional dishes on a food tour of Necoclí.

Week-Long Itinerary for Necoclí

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Necoclí and check-in to your accommodation. Spend the day exploring the town center and visiting the Museo Histórico Local.
2 Visit the beautiful Playa Los Tres Dedos and spend the day swimming and sunbathing.
3 Take a guided tour to Cascada de la Caimana to enjoy the stunning waterfall and surrounding area.
4 Hike or bike the La Ruta del Anfibio trail for amazing views of Necoclí’s coastline.
5 Embark on a dolphin-watching tour in the ocean.
6 Take a food tour of Necoclí to sample the local cuisine and try some traditional dishes.
7 Relax on your last day in Necoclí and enjoy the town’s peaceful atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Necoclí

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz con Coco: A popular dish made with rice cooked in coconut milk and served with a variety of seafood, meat or vegetables.

  • Pescado Frito: A classic Colombian dish consisting of fried fish, usually served with patacones (smashed fried plantains).

Local Beverages

  • Agua de Coco: Fresh coconut water is a refreshing and healthy drink that is widely available in Necoclí.

  • Cerveza Costeña: This is a popular beer in the area that’s perfect for enjoying a cold drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Necoclí: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hostal Marlyn: This budget-friendly hostel is located near the town center and offers comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and a patio area for relaxing.

  • Hotel Colonial: This historic hotel boasts beautiful Spanish colonial architecture and is situated in a prime location near the beach.

What to Buy in Necoclí: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handheld Fans: These fans are intricately woven in bright colors and make for a practical and beautiful souvenir.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Necoclí is known for its stunning jewelry made with natural materials such as shells, seeds, and wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Necoclí

Necoclí is known for its Afro-Colombian heritage and cultural traditions, which are celebrated through music, dance, and food. Visitors can experience these customs by attending local festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Necoclí

The best time to visit Necoclí is from December to March, as this is when the weather is typically sunny with little rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Necoclí

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and enjoy the cultural experiences the town has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.

  • Engage in illegal activities such as drug use.

Safety and Health

Safety in Necoclí

Necoclí is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take necessary precautions and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Necoclí

It is recommended that visitors to Necoclí be up to date on routine vaccines and consider getting vaccinated for hepatitis A and typhoid before traveling. It’s also important to practice safe food and water hygiene.

Getting to Necoclí: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Necoclí

The closest airport to Necoclí is El Dorado International Airport in Bogotá, which is approximately a 10-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Necoclí

There is a bus station in Necoclí that offers daily services to and from nearby cities such as Medellín.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Necoclí by taking a taxi or private car rental from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Necoclí

Necoclí is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available for those who prefer a more convenient way of getting around.

Where to Go Next from Necoclí: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Playa Blanca: This stunning beach is located just a short boat ride away from Necoclí and offers beautiful white sand and crystal-clear water.

  • San Antero: This charming town is known for its tranquil beaches, colorful architecture, and traditional fishing villages.

Conclusion

Necoclí is a beautiful and culturally rich destination that is sure to impress travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure. With a variety of natural attractions, delicious cuisine, and welcoming hospitality, Necoclí should be on every traveler’s itinerary when visiting Colombia.
