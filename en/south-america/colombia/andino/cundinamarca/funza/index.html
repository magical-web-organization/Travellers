Introduction to Funza

Funza is a charming town located in Cundinamarca, in the Andino region of Colombia. It is a perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Colombian experience, away from the tourist crowds. The town is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a haven for outdoor adventurers. Funza is located just 30 minutes from Bogotá, the capital city of Colombia, making it easily accessible.

List of Places to Visit in Funza

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Ethnographic Museum of Funza

The Ethnographic Museum of Funza is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in learning about the history and culture of the region. The museum showcases pre-Columbian artifacts, colonial-era artworks, and ethnographic displays.

The Church of Our Lady of Carmen

The Church of Our Lady of Carmen is a beautiful colonial-era church located in the heart of the town. The church dates back to the 18th century and features stunning architectural details.

Natural Attractions of Funza: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salto de Tequendama Waterfall

Located just a short drive from Funza, the Salto de Tequendama waterfall is a natural wonder worth visiting. The waterfall is 132 meters tall and is surrounded by lush rainforest. It is a perfect spot for hiking and birdwatching.

Hummingbird Sanctuary

The Hummingbird Sanctuary is home to over 25 species of hummingbirds and is a perfect spot for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the sanctuary and learn about the different types of hummingbirds found in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Funza

Alto de la Cruz Trail

The Alto de la Cruz Trail is a popular hiking and biking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail is a challenging one, but the stunning scenery makes it worth the effort.

Cerro de Las Tres Cruces Trail

The Cerro de Las Tres Cruces Trail is a moderate-level hiking trail that offers panoramic views of Funza and its surrounding landscapes. The trail is dotted with scenic lookout points and offers a glimpse into the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Funza: Activities and Tours

Coffee Farm Tour

Colombia is known for its excellent coffee, and Funza is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a day trip to a local coffee farm and learn about the coffee-making process.

Paragliding

For adventure seekers, paragliding is an excellent activity to try in Funza. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains while soaring through the sky with a trained professional.

Food and Drink Scene of Funza

Must-Try Dishes

Ajiaco

Ajiaco is a traditional Colombian soup that is perfect for cold weather. The soup includes chicken, corn, potatoes, and avocado and is a flavorful and warming dish.

Arepas

Arepas are a staple food item in Colombia and are made from ground corn. They are often stuffed with cheese, ham, or chicken and make an excellent snack or breakfast option.

Local Beverages

Aguapanela

Aguapanela is a hot drink made from cane sugar and water. It is a perfect beverage option for cold, rainy days in Funza.

Chicha

Chicha is a fermented corn-based drink that is popular in Colombia. It is a refreshing beverage option, and visitors can try it at local restaurants and cafes.

Where to Stay in Funza: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Campestre El Carmelo

Hotel Campestre El Carmelo is a beautiful hotel located just 5 minutes from the Funza town center. The hotel features comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving traditional Colombian dishes.

Hostal Casa Central

Hostal Casa Central is a budget-friendly accommodation option located in the town center. The hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms, making it a perfect option for solo travelers or those traveling on a budget.

What to Buy in Funza: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Funza can bring home a variety of souvenirs and local products, including coffee, handmade crafts, and clothing made from alpaca wool.

Local Culture and Customs of Funza

Funza is a predominantly Catholic town, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches and other cultural sites and avoid wearing revealing clothing.

Best Time to Visit Funza

The best time to visit Funza is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. The scenery is at its most spectacular during this time, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without the worry of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Funza

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Walk alone at night
  • Take photos without asking for permission
  • Leave belongings unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Funza

Funza is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Funza

Visitors to Funza should drink only bottled or filtered water and avoid eating street food to prevent food-borne illnesses. Visitors should also ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations prior to their trip.

Getting to Funza: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Funza

The closest airport to Funza is El Dorado International Airport in Bogotá, which is located just 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Funza

There are several bus stations providing transportation to Funza from other major cities in Colombia, including Bogotá and Medellin.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Funza from major cities in Colombia.

How to Get Around in Funza

The best way to get around Funza is by walking or biking. Visitors can rent bikes from local shops or take guided bike tours of the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Funza: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bogotá

Bogotá is the capital city of Colombia and is just a 30-minute drive from Funza. Visitors can explore the city’s historic district, visit world-class museums, or enjoy the vibrant nightlife.

Zipaquirá

Zipaquirá is a charming town located just an hour’s drive from Funza. It is home to the famous Salt Cathedral, a massive underground cathedral carved out of salt.

Conclusion

Funza is a hidden gem of Colombia, offering visitors the perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan a trip to Funza with ease and get the most out of their experience in this charming town.
