Introduction to Chía

Chía is a picturesque town located in the Cundinamarca region of Colombia, nestled in the Andean Mountains. Known for its scenic views, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, Chía is a popular destination for travelers seeking to explore Colombia beyond its bustling cities. Whether you’re a nature lover or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Chía. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, top things to do, and everything else you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Chía.

List of Places to Visit in Chía

When it comes to places to visit in Chía, there’s no shortage of options. From museums and historical landmarks to stunning natural parks and hiking trails, here are some of the must-visit attractions in Chía:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chía’s Main Square: Featuring a beautiful colonial-era church and surrounded by picturesque streets lined with shops and cafes, Chía’s Main Square is a great place to soak up the town’s charming atmosphere.
  • Museo del Prado: Housed in a colonial mansion, the Museo del Prado showcases a fascinating collection of pre-Columbian art and artifacts.
  • Hacienda Santa Barbara: This restored colonial hacienda features a museum, a restaurant, and beautiful gardens that are perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Natural Attractions of Chía: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Ecologico La Montana: This natural park is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching, with trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Laguna de Guatavita: Located just a short drive from Chía, Laguna de Guatavita is a beautiful, sacred lake that’s steeped in history and legend.
  • Parque Jaime Duque: This theme park features replicas of famous landmarks from around the world, as well as a mini zoo and other fun attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chía

Chía is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery, making it the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Camino de la Laguna: This trail takes you through the stunning countryside around Laguna de Guatavita, offering sweeping vistas and a chance to explore some of the region’s traditional villages.
  • Cerro San Miguel: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Cerro San Miguel, one of the highest peaks in the Andes that offers extraordinary views of the surrounding landscape.
  • La Chorrera Waterfall: This hike will take you to the magnificent La Chorrera Waterfall, one of the tallest in Colombia.

Best Things to Do in Chía: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun and exciting things to do in Chía. Here are just a few ideas:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the thrill of a hot air balloon ride and see the stunning landscapes of Chía from above.
  • Coffee Tour: Learn about the region’s rich coffee culture with a visit to a local coffee farm, where you can see the entire process from bean to cup.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a leisurely horseback ride through the Andean countryside and enjoy the breathtaking scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chía

If you have a week to explore Chía and the surrounding region, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic center of Chía and visit the Museo del Prado and Hacienda Santa Barbara.
  • Day 2: Hike the Camino de la Laguna trail and explore the traditional villages of the region.
  • Day 3: Visit the Parque Jaime Duque and enjoy the theme park’s many attractions.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Laguna de Guatavita and learn about the lake’s fascinating history and significance.
  • Day 5: Go on a coffee tour and learn about Colombia’s world-famous coffee culture.
  • Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning Andean landscapes.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy Chía’s charming atmosphere, or do some shopping for souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Chía

Foodies will find plenty to love in Chía, which boasts a rich culinary scene that draws from traditional Colombian cuisine as well as international influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ajiaco: A hearty soup made with chicken, potatoes, corn, and capers, served with rice and avocado.
  • Lechona: A whole roasted pig stuffed with rice, beans, and spices, typically served with arepas and salad.
  • Empanadas: Fried or baked pastries stuffed with meat, cheese, or vegetables, and served with a spicy salsa.

Local Beverages

  • Aguardiente: Colombia’s national drink, a strong anise-flavored liquor that’s often served as a digestive after meals.
  • Chicha: A fermented corn drink that’s a traditional beverage of indigenous people in the region.
  • Café Tinto: A strong, black coffee that’s a staple of Colombian culture.

Where to Stay in Chía: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Chía has plenty of choices. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Siecha: A luxury hotel with a beautiful outdoor pool and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Hostal Villa Maria Chia: A charming and affordable guesthouse located in the heart of the town.
  • Hotel Campestre La Esmeralda: A quiet and comfortable hotel surrounded by nature, with spacious rooms and a restaurant on-site.

What to Buy in Chía: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some great options:

  • Woolen Rugs: Chía is known for its high-quality woolen rugs, which are made using traditional techniques by local artisans.
  • Handmade Jewelry: You’ll find plenty of beautiful handmade jewelry in the shops and markets of Chía, including intricate filigree pieces made from silver and gold.
  • Coffee: Colombia is known for its excellent coffee, and you can find plenty of high-quality beans to take back home as a delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Chía

Colombia is a country with a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and Chía is no exception. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of while visiting:

  • Respect for Elders: In Colombian culture, showing respect for elders is an important part of social etiquette.
  • Music and Dance: Traditional music and dance are an integral part of Colombian culture, and you’ll likely encounter plenty of both while in Chía.
  • Dress Code: While there are no strict dress codes in Chía, it’s considered polite to dress modestly in public places such as churches and museums.

Best Time to Visit Chía

The best time to visit Chía is during the dry season, which runs from December to March and June to August. During this time, the weather is generally sunny and warm, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. It’s important to note that Chía can also be quite crowded during peak tourist season, so if you’re looking for a more peaceful experience, consider visiting during the shoulder season (April to May and September to November).

Do’s and Don’ts of Chía

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Chía, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish: While many people in Chía speak some English, knowing some basic Spanish phrases will go a long way in helping you communicate and connect with locals.
  • Be respectful: Colombians are generally warm and welcoming people, but it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions while visiting.
  • Keep an open mind: Chía is a place with plenty of surprises, so be open to new experiences and don’t be afraid to explore off the beaten path.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: It’s not advisable to drink tap water in Chía, so be sure to stick to bottled water.
  • Flaunt your wealth: While Chía is generally a safe town, it’s still important to take precautions and avoid attracting unwanted attention by flaunting your wealth or valuables.
  • Be afraid to try new things: Colombia has a reputation for being a bit intimidating to some travelers, but Chía is a friendly and welcoming destination that’s easy to navigate with a little bit of planning.

Safety and Health

While Chía is a generally safe destination for travelers, it’s important to take some basic safety precautions to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Chía

  • Keep your valuables secure: While Chía is a safe town, it’s still a good idea to keep your valuables secure and out of sight to avoid attracting unwanted attention.
  • Take taxis at night: If you’re out and about at night, it’s best to take a taxi rather than walk alone.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Pay attention to your surroundings and be aware of any potentially risky situations.

Health in Chía

  • Get vaccinated: Make sure your routine vaccinations are up to date before traveling to Chía, and consider getting vaccinated for yellow fever and other diseases depending on your travel plans.
  • Drink bottled water: Stick to bottled water rather than tap water to avoid getting sick.
  • Use insect repellent: Mosquitoes can carry diseases such as dengue fever and malaria, so be sure to use insect repellent and take other precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Chía: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chía is located just a short distance from Bogota, Colombia’s capital city, which makes it relatively easy to get to. Here are the transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Chía

  • El Dorado International Airport: Bogota’s main airport is located less than an hour’s drive from Chía, with regular flights to destinations throughout Colombia and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Chía

  • Portal Norte: This bus station in Bogota is one of the main terminals for long-distance buses, with regular service to Chía and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Private Transportation: If you prefer not to take public transportation, you can arrange a private transfer or hire a car and drive to Chía.

How to Get Around in Chía

Getting around Chía is relatively easy, with plenty of transportation options available. Here’s what you need to know:

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout Chía, and are a convenient and affordable way to get around town.
  • Buses: There are plenty of local buses that connect Chía with other towns in the region, making it easy to explore the surrounding countryside.
  • Walking and Biking: Chía is a pedestrian-friendly town with plenty of sidewalks and bike paths, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Chía: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Colombia after visiting Chía, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Bogota: Colombia’s capital city is just a short drive from Chía, and is filled with museums, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions.
  • Zipaquira: This charming colonial town is famous for its underground salt cathedral, which is a must-visit attraction for anyone visiting the region.
  • Villa de Leyva: This picturesque town is located a few hours’ drive from Chía, and is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, and beautiful surrounding countryside.

Conclusion

Chía is a beautiful and fascinating destination that’s perfect for travelers looking to explore another side of Colombia. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, food, or adventure, there’s something for everyone in Chía. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you’re inspired to plan your perfect trip to this charming Andean town!
