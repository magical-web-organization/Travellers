Introduction to La Calera

Located in the heart of the Andino region in Colombia, La Calera is a picturesque town surrounded by the Andes Mountains. Known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and thrilling outdoor activities, La Calera has become a favorite destination for travelers seeking an authentic Colombian experience.

This travel guide provides a comprehensive overview of La Calera, including its best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local customs. Whether you are a cultural enthusiast, nature lover, or thrill-seeker, La Calera has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in La Calera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa-museo Jorge Eliécer Gaitán: A museum dedicated to the life and legacy of Jorge Eliecer Gaitán, an iconic Colombian political leader. The museum features his personal belongings, speeches, and photos.

  • Catedral de Nuestra Señora del Rosario: Built-in the 18th century, this cathedral is an excellent example of colonial architecture.

Natural Attractions of La Calera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural Chicaque: This protected natural reserve is ideal for hiking and camping. It has more than 300 species of birds and 30 species of mammals.

  • Mirador de la Piedra Capira: This stunning viewpoint offers a panoramic view of the entire La Calera Valley. The ideal spot for selfies and Instagram-worthy photos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Calera

La Calera has many exciting hiking and biking routes that offer spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Some popular ones are:

  • La Chorrera: A two-hour moderate hike through dense forests leading to the highest waterfall in Colombia.

  • Camino de la Luz: A four-hour hiking trail that takes you through patches of bamboo forests, Guadua wetlands, and a wide range of bird species.

Best Things to Do in La Calera: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding: La Calera is well-known for its paragliding activities. Experience the thrill of soaring over the Andes Mountains.

  • Horseback riding: A tour of the valleys and forests aboard a majestic horse is an excellent way to connect with nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Calera

Day 1 - Arrival and check-in at a hotel or guesthouse

Day 2 - Visit Casa Museo Jorge Eliécer Gaitán and Parque Natural Chicaque

Day 3 - Hiking to La Chorrera Waterfall

Day 4 - Paragliding and visit to Mirador de la Piedra Capira

Day 5 - Horseback riding and a tour of the surrounding villages

Day 6 - Learn about the local coffee culture and visit a coffee plantation

Day 7 - Departure from La Calera

Food and Drink Scene of La Calera

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ajiaco: A traditional Colombian soup made with chicken, potatoes, and corn.

  • Lechona: Roasted pig stuffed with yellow peas, rice, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Guarapo: A refreshing drink made from sugarcane juice.

  • Tinto: A small, strong coffee shot typically taken in the early morning.

Where to Stay in La Calera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Campestre Paraiso Natural: A peaceful hotel located near all the major tourist attractions.

  • Hostal Bambu: An eco-friendly hostel that offers comfortable and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in La Calera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee: La Calera is renowned for its high-quality coffee. You can buy coffee beans and ground coffee from the local coffee plantations.

  • Woven crafts: La Calera is famous for its beautiful woven baskets and bags made from natural fibers.

Local Culture and Customs of La Calera

La Calera has a rich cultural heritage, and its people are known for their hospitality and warmth towards tourists. Visitors are expected to show respect for local customs, such as greeting people with a warm smile and handshake, giving small gifts to show appreciation, and being mindful of cultural differences.

Best Time to Visit La Calera

The best time to visit La Calera is from December to March, where the weather is dry and pleasant. However, the town is beautiful all year round, so plan accordingly and bring appropriate clothing.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Calera

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the environment by littering.

  • Don’t argue or negotiate with street vendors when shopping.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Calera

La Calera is generally safe for tourists, but like any other city, it’s essential to take precautions. Avoid carrying large sums of money and valuables in public, be aware of your surroundings, and ask for advice from the hotel staff.

Health in La Calera

It’s important to practice safe drinking water habits and use sunscreen to avoid sunburns. Be sure to take any required medications and check with a doctor before traveling.

Getting to La Calera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Calera

The nearest airport to La Calera is El Dorado International Airport in Bogota, which is a one-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near La Calera

The bus station in Bogota offers services to La Calera, with buses running every 15-20 minutes.

Other Options

You can also find private taxis or use ride-sharing apps like Uber to get to La Calera from Bogota.

How to Get Around in La Calera

The easiest and fastest way to get around La Calera is by taxi, which can be hired from the town center. Alternatively, you can also rent a bike or walk to explore the town’s many attractions on foot.

Where to Go Next from La Calera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bogota: The vibrant and bustling capital city of Colombia is only one hour away and has a lot of exciting things to see and do.

  • Zipaquira: A charming colonial town known for its Salt Cathedral, which is just over an hour’s drive from La Calera.

Conclusion

La Calera is a must-visit destination in Colombia, offering a mix of culture, nature, and adventure. With this travel guide, you can plan your trip with ease and explore the town’s many attractions and delights. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready for an unforgettable trip to La Calera.
