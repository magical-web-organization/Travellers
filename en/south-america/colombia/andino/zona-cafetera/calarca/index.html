Introduction to Calarca

Calarca is situated in the Zona Cafetera region of Colombia. It’s an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore the country’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Calarca is a small town with a unique charm that invites visitors to experience the local culture, delicious food, and friendly people.

List of Places to Visit in Calarca

Here’s a list of the top attractions in Calarca that should be on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Oro Quimbaya: This museum is dedicated to the Quimbaya culture and exhibits a collection of gold pieces that represent their artistic expressions.
  • Catedral de Nuestra Senora del Carmen: A beautiful church that is a must-visit for anyone interested in religious and architectural history.
  • Museo Histórico Municipal de Calarcá: This museum houses many exhibits that illustrate the history and cultural traditions of Calarca.

Natural Attractions of Calarca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valle del Cocora: A scenic valley with tall palms that provides a breathtaking panorama of the Andes mountain range.
  • Mirador del Quindio: An observation deck that overlooks Calarca and its surrounding landscape.
  • Parque Nacional Natural Los Nevados: It’s a vast natural park with several peaks, waterfalls, and trails that are ideal for hikers who want to explore Colombia’s natural treasures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calarca

Tourists can discover numerous hiking and biking routes in Calarca that offer ideal experiences for adventure enthusiasts. The top routes include:

  • Palmira to Salento: A challenging route that passes through various valleys and small towns. It’s a perfect location for pro hikers.
  • Calarca to Cocora Valley: A fairly manageable and scenic route for beginners that takes you through beautiful forests and across rivers.

Best Things to Do in Calarca: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other exciting activities and tours that tourists can do in Calarca, such as:

  • Coffee tours and tastings: Calarca is famous for its premium-quality coffee, so taking a coffee tour is a must-do. You can learn about the coffee-making process from start to finish and how the land and climate influence it.
  • Horseback riding: Calarca has a beautiful countryside, and a perfect way to explore it is on horseback. Many tour operators offer horseback riding tours through the surrounding hills and valleys.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calarca

If you’re planning to spend a week in Calarca, we’ve put together an itinerary for you.

Day Plan
1 Visit Museo del Oro Quimbaya and Catedral de Nuestra Senora del Carmen
2 Hike to Valle del Cocora
3 Explore Parque Nacional Natural Los Nevados
4 Visit Salento and Filandia, two of the prettiest and most colorful towns in the area
5 Take a coffee tour and taste the premium-quality coffee that Calarca is famous for
6 Spend the day horseback riding into the countryside
7 Relax and soak in the tranquil surroundings of Calarca. Visit the Mirador del Quindio for a beautiful view of the town

Food and Drink Scene of Calarca

Calarca is renowned for its culinary delights, especially its coffee. Make sure you try the following dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • **Arepas **: Traditional Colombian corn cakes that come stuffed with cheese, butter, or meat.
  • Bandeja Paisa: A huge platter that features grilled meat, beans, rice, plantains, and avocado.
  • Sancocho: A hearty soup made with plantains, yucca, potatoes, and chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Calarca produces some of the world’s best coffee - dark, rich, and bold.
  • Panela: Sugarcane juice boiled and solidified into a block.
  • Aguardiente: An anise-flavored alcoholic beverage that is the national drink of Colombia.

Where to Stay in Calarca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calarca has a range of accommodation options from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay:

  • Hacienda Combia by Sercotel: A beautiful four-star hotel situated within a coffee plantation that offers excellent amenities and stunning views.
  • Hostal Ciudad Bonita Calarcá: A budget-friendly and cozy hostel in the heart of Calarca that’s perfect for solo or group travelers.
  • Hotel Catama Real: A value-for-money hotel with well-furnished rooms and easy access to Calarca’s landmarks.

What to Buy in Calarca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Calarca is home to several markets and shops where you can buy souvenirs and authentic local products. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Coffee Beans: Make sure you bring home some of Calarca’s excellent coffee.
  • Handcrafted Art: Local artisans sell beautiful handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Panela: Don’t leave the town without buying some delicious, organic sugar cane juice in solidified form.

Local Culture and Customs of Calarca

Calarca is a friendly and vibrant town that welcomes visitors with warm hospitality. The town is predominantly Catholic, and visitors should dress modestly in holy places and show respect for religious customs and traditions. The town has a rich cultural heritage that locals take pride in, and tourists should be respectful of these traditions.

Best Time to Visit Calarca

The best time to visit Calarca is during the dry season, which lasts from December to February and July to August. During these months, the climate is mild, and the sky is blue, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calarca

Here are some of the essential do(s) and don’t(s) to keep in mind while visiting Calarca:

Do:

  • Dress modestly for temples and holy places.
  • Take a coffee tour and immerse yourself in the coffee culture of Calarca.
  • Research your itinerary and plan accordingly.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, instead opt for bottled water.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of the town’s culture and traditions.
  • Don’t carry too much cash or valuables.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calarca

While Calarca is generally safe for visitors, it is still advisable to take precautions like any other place. Here are some tips to stay safe:

  • Avoid walking in dark alleys during evenings and late nights.
  • Be careful with your belongings and avoid flashing too much cash or valuables.

Health in Calarca

It’s essential to stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water during your stay in Calarca. Medical facilities are not well-equipped, so it’s best to carry essential medicines with you throughout your trip.

Getting to Calarca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calarca is well-connected via air, road, and rail. It’s easy to reach the town from the main economic centres of Bogota, Medellin, or Pereira. Here are some transportation options to get to Calarca:

Airports Near Calarca

  • El Edén International Airport (AXM) in Armenia, Colombia, is the closest international airport to Calarca.

Bus Stations Near Calarca

  • The Terminal de Transporte de Armenia is the central bus station and the most common location to arrive in Calarca by bus.

Other Options

  • To enjoy the scenic views and privacy, you can hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Calarca.

How to Get Around in Calarca

Here are some transport options to get around the town:

  • Taxis are affordable and easily available in Calarca, making it the most convenient way to travel around the town.
  • Buses are also an affordable and efficient way to travel around Calarca.

Where to Go Next from Calarca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calarca is an excellent base for tourists to explore Zona Cafetera, Andes, and other destinations in Colombia. Here are some locations that you can consider visiting:

  • Salento: A beautiful town known for its bright-colored houses and lush green scenery.
  • Filandia: A small town with winding streets and picturesque cafes, perfect for a relaxing day.
  • Medellín: Known as the city of eternal spring, Medellin is a vibrant city that’s ideal for adventurers.

Conclusion

Calarca is a beautiful small town in Colombia with a rich cultural heritage, panoramic views, and excellent coffee. With its traditional architecture and charming locals, the town is an ideal destination for anyone looking for a peaceful getaway. Follow our comprehensive guide to Calarca to make the best of your trip and discover the hidden gems of the town.
