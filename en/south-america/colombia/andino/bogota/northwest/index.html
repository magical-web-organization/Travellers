Introduction to Northwest

Northwest is a beautiful neighborhood located in the heart of Bogotá. It is known for its charming architecture, rich cultural history, and scenic views. The neighborhood has been home to several artists, writers, and musicians over the years, and this is reflected in its vibrant arts and culture scene. Northwest is a great destination for travelers who want to explore Bogotá’s diverse cultural and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northwest has several museums and historical places that give travelers a glimpse into the area’s rich history and culture. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Museo del Chicó: This is one of Bogotá’s most popular museums, located in the heart of Northwest. It showcases the city’s history, culture, and art through a range of exhibits and displays.
  • Casa Museo Quinta de Bolívar: This is the former home of Simon Bolívar, a key figure in Latin America’s fight for independence. The museum is dedicated to his life and legacy.
  • Museo de Arte Moderno de Bogotá: This museum has an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art, with works by Colombian and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Northwest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northwest is known for its beautiful natural attractions, such as parks and gardens. Here are some places to add to your itinerary:

  • Parque de la 93: This park is a popular hangout spot for locals and visitors alike. It has several restaurants, bars, and cafes, as well as a large green space for picnics and leisurely walks.
  • Jardín Botánico de Bogotá: This botanical garden has an extensive collection of exotic and native plant species, making it a great spot for botany enthusiasts.
  • Mirador de la Calera: This viewpoint offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains, making it a great spot for photographers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest

For travelers looking for some outdoor adventure, Northwest has plenty of hiking and biking routes to choose from. Here are some popular options:

  • Cerro de Monserrate: This mountain is located in the eastern part of Bogotá and offers several hiking trails with different difficulty levels. At the top, you’ll be rewarded with breathtaking views of the city.
  • Parque Nacional Natural Chingaza: This is a national park located about a two-hour drive from Northwest. It has several hiking and biking trails that take you through beautiful forests, rivers, and lakes.
  • Ciclovía de Bogotá: This is a network of trails and bike lanes that spans over 300 kilometers and connects several neighborhoods and parks in the city, including Northwest. It’s a great way to explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Northwest: Activities and Tours

Apart from the attractions mentioned above, Northwest has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some recommendations:

  • Street Art Tour: Northwest is home to some of the most impressive street art in Bogotá. Take a tour to discover the hidden gems and learn about the artists behind them.
  • Coffee Tour: Colombia is famous for its coffee, and Northwest has several cafes and roasteries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to make traditional Colombian dishes like arepas, empanadas, and sancocho in a cooking class with a local chef.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northwest

If you have a week to spare in Northwest, here’s a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest

Must-Try Dishes

Northwest is home to several traditional Colombian dishes, such as:

  • Arepas: These are round, flatbreads made with cornmeal and served with a variety of fillings, such as cheese, meat, and avocado.
  • Sancocho: This is a hearty soup made with beef, pork, or chicken, potatoes, yucca, corn, and other vegetables.
  • Empanadas: These are small, fried or baked pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Apart from coffee, Northwest also has several local beverages to try:

  • Chicha: This is a traditional fermented corn drink that’s slightly sweet and tangy.
  • Aguardiente: This is a licorice-flavored liquor that’s popular throughout Colombia.
  • Lulada: This is a refreshing drink made with lulo fruit, water, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Northwest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwest has several accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Hotel Cité: This is a boutique hotel located in the heart of Northwest. It has modern rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a restaurant.
  • Hostal Casa Quevedo: This is a budget-friendly hostel with dorm rooms and private rooms. It has a communal kitchen and lounge area, as well as a garden.
  • Hotel Bh Bicentenario: This is a mid-range hotel located in a historic building. It has spacious rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Northwest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northwest has several markets and shops that sell unique souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Museo del Chicó Gift Shop: This shop sells souvenirs and products related to the history and culture of Bogotá.
  • Artesanías de Colombia: This shop sells handcrafted products made by Colombian artisans, such as textiles, ceramics, and jewelry.
  • Cerca de Piedra Market: This market sells fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and other local products that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest

Colombians are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and Northwest is no exception. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Greetings: Colombians are very warm and friendly, and it’s customary to greet people with a handshake or a hug.
  • Mealtimes: Lunch is the main meal of the day in Colombia, and it’s typically served between noon and 2 pm.
  • Tipping: Tipping is expected in restaurants and cafes, and the typical amount is 10% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Northwest

Northwest can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is from December to March, which is the dry season. The weather is mild and sunny during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest

Do:

  • Do try the traditional Colombian dishes and local beverages.
  • Do explore the diverse arts and culture scene of Northwest.
  • Do embrace the hospitality and warmth of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothing, as this is considered inappropriate in Colombia.
  • Don’t drink tap water, as it’s not potable.
  • Don’t walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwest

Northwest is generally a safe neighborhood, but it’s important to take the same precautions you would in any unfamiliar area. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night and in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Use only licensed taxis or ride-hailing services like Uber or Beat.

Health in Northwest

Colombia is a tropical country, so it’s important to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and malaria. Here are some tips:

  • Use mosquito repellent and wear long sleeves and pants.
  • Drink only bottled water and avoid street food that may not be prepared in safe conditions.
  • Consider getting vaccinated against yellow fever before traveling to Colombia.

Getting to Northwest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northwest

The closest airport to Northwest is El Dorado International Airport, which is about 40 minutes away by car. From there, you can take a taxi or a private transfer to Northwest.

Bus Stations Near Northwest

The closest bus terminal to Northwest is the Bogotá Central Bus Terminal, which is about 20 minutes away by car. From there, you can take a taxi or a local bus to Northwest.

Other Options

Apart from taxis and local buses, there are several other transportation options to get around Northwest and Bogotá, such as:

  • Metro: Bogotá has a modern metro system, but it currently only serves the southern part of the city.
  • TransMilenio: This is a bus rapid transit system that serves several parts of the city, including Northwest.
  • Ridesharing Services: Uber, Beat, and other ride-hailing services are available in Bogotá.

How to Get Around in Northwest

Northwest is a small neighborhood that can be easily explored on foot. However, if you need to travel further, there are several transportation options, such as:

  • Taxis: Taxis are plentiful in Bogotá and can be hailed on the street or through ride-hailing apps like Uber or Beat.
  • Buses: Local buses are a cheaper transportation option, but they can be crowded and confusing for non-Spanish speakers.
  • Bikes: You can rent bikes from several places in Bogotá, including Cicla Tours, which has a rental depot in Northwest.

Where to Go Next from Northwest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northwest is a great base to explore other parts of Bogotá and nearby destinations. Here are some options:

  • La Candelaria: This is the historic neighborhood in the center of Bogotá, famous for its colorful colonial buildings and museums.
  • Zipaquirá: This is a small town located about an hour outside of Bogotá. It’s known for its stunning salt cathedral, an underground cathedral carved inside a salt mine.
  • Villa de Leyva: This is a charming colonial town located about three hours from Bogotá. It’s known for its cobblestone streets, colonial architecture, and beautiful countryside.

Conclusion

Northwest is a beautiful, culturally rich neighborhood in Bogotá that’s worth exploring. From its museums and historical places to its natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Northwest has something for every type of traveler. It’s a great base to explore other parts of Bogotá and nearby destinations, and its local culture and cuisine are sure to leave a lasting impression.
