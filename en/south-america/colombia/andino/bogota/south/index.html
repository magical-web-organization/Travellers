Introduction to South

South, located in Andino, Bogotá, is a fascinating destination that offers a wealth of cultural experiences, natural attractions, and outdoor activities. As one of the most significant regions of Bogotá, South boasts historical landmarks, museums, and numerous avenues, making it ideal for both sightseeing and exploration.

In this guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive list of all the things South has to offer, including must-visit places, exciting activities, and great food and drink options you cannot miss on your visit.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • ** Gold Museum**: An exceptional museum exhibiting Colombia’s pre-Colombian goldsmith artistry with over 55,000 pieces recovered from numerous cultures during different periods. This museum makes an ideal destination for history lovers.
  • ** Bolivar Square**: Named after Simón Bolívar, the liberator of Colombia, this square carries significant cultural symbolism and is a cultural and historic site worth visiting.
  • ** National Museum of Colombia**: Established in 1823, the National Museum of Colombia is one of Bogotá’s oldest cultural and historical institutions. This museum showcases a wide range of historical collections, from ethnography and archaeology to art and architecture.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • ** Monserrate Hill**: With an elevation of 3,152 meters above sea level, Monserrate Hill offers spectacular panoramic views of Bogotá City, making it an attractive location for tourists and locals alike. This hill also features an incredible cable car ride.
  • ** Bogotá Botanical Garden**: Featuring more than 19,500 species of plants and flowers, the Botanical Garden is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. This garden includes various themed gardens, research centers, and exhibitions regularly updated.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

  • ** Cerros Orientales**: These Eastern ranges provide exhilarating hiking and biking trails with stunning sights of the city. The trails in Cerros Orientales are perfect for people who love adventure and want to explore the natural beauty of the city.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

  • ** Graffiti Tour in La Candelaria**: For art enthusiasts who love walking through streets full of colors and artistic expressions, a graffiti tour in La Candelaria is a must-do. This tour provides an ideal opportunity to learn about graffiti art’s political and social significance in Colombia.
  • ** Bicycle Tour of Bogotá’s southern neighborhoods**: One of the best ways of experiencing and exploring the less-visited parts of Bogotá. These neighborhoods provide a fascinating view of the architectural heritage and the vibrant local culture of Bogotá City.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

  • ** Ajiaco Bogotano**: Bogota’s signature soup that perfectly embodies the city’s flavors and culture made from potato, chicken, corn, avocado, and capers.
  • ** Bandeja Paisa**: This is Colombia’s most famous dish, featuring beans, rice, steak, plantain, avocado and much more.
  • ** Churros with Hot Chocolate**: A warm, sweet, and crispy dessert that Colombians enjoy dipping into hot chocolate to balance the sweetness.

Local Beverages

  • ** Canelazo**: This traditional beverage is a blend of sugar cane alcohol (rum or aguardiente), cinnamon, cloves, and aguapanela. This is a perfect beverage on cold nights.
  • ** Aguardiente**: Famous among Colombian alcohol, this is a drink made from sugar cane with aniseed flavoring.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • ** Hotel Tryp Bogotá Embajada**: This four-star hotel located in Andino offers stylishly decorated rooms infused with modern luxuries. It also has different bars, restaurants, and spa services.
  • ** Casa Legado**: This guesthouse located in Usquen perfectly offers the home-like feeling with luxurious rooms and a personalized experience.
  • ** Republica Hostel Bogotá**: A hostel perfect for backpackers, the Republica Hostel offers free breakfast, comfy beds, and spacious lounges.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • ** Emeralds**: Colombia is the world’s largest producer of emeralds, and South is the perfect place to purchase this precious stone.
  • ** Textiles**: South Market offers an array of handmade bedspreads, rugs, and clothing.
  • ** Handicrafts**: The local artisans create colorful handicrafts made of wood, clay, and fabrics, which make an excellent souvenir for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Colombia’s social structure is evolving, but traditional customs persist in different regions. South is filled with proud communities that continue to preserve their culture despite modernization. Colombians prefer to shake hands and avoid direct body contact when greeting. Dressing up conservatively is also essential for religious and formal events.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is between December and February when the weather is the coolest and the humidity low. This season is Colombia’s high tourist season, so it’s best to book hotels, flights and travel activities well in advance to avoid disappointment.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish.
  • Eat local street food – it’s yummy and cheap.
  • Try Colombian coffee – it’s excellent and rich in taste.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink water from the tap.
  • Don’t wear flashy jewelry when traveling to public areas.
  • Don’t forget to carry your ID, permit and visa if you’re visiting from another country.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

Though Colombia has a history of violence and political instability, the situation has stabilized considerably, and South is generally safe for tourists. But, be aware of pickpocketing and avoid venturing to unknown places alone at night.

Health in South

The standard of medical care in Colombia varies. Tourists must take recommended vaccinations and consider purchasing International Health insurance. In case of any emergency, dial 123 for immediate medical assistance.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

  • El Dorado International Airport: Located only 20 minutes away from South, the El Dorado International Airport is well connected to most cities worldwide.

Bus Stations Near South

  • Terminal del Sur: This bus station is located in South, and many bus services depart from here to various regions of Bogotá City.

Other Options

  • ** Taxi**: Taxis are a convenient option as they provide easy access to many destinations in South, Bogotá.
  • ** Transmilenio**: One of the fastest and most cost-effective ways of getting around the main parts of Bogotá City is through Transmilenio.

How to Get Around in South

South, Bogotá, offers various options for visitors to get around, including taxi, bike rentals and walking. The city is brightly lit and pedestrian-friendly, bringing the best experience to the visitors. Always look for authorized taxis with taximeter or use mobile apps to select a licensed taxi.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • La Candelaria: Located just a few miles from South, Bogotá’s historic Old City is home to many colonial-style buildings strategically situated next to fascinating modern buildings.
  • Zipaquira Salt Cathedral: A popular destination, this cathedral carved inside the salt mines and contains many statues and facades that glitter beautifully when lit up.

Conclusion

South, Bogotá is a fascinating destination with a mix of cultural experiences, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures, making it perfect for sightseeing and exploration. Be sure to use this travel guide to plan the perfect itinerary that highlights South’s best attractions, activities, accommodations, and food and drink options. Enjoy your trip!
