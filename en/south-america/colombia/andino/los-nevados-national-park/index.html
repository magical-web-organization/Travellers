Introduction to Los Nevados National Park

Los Nevados National Park is a natural treasure located in the heart of the Andino region of Colombia. Established in 1974, this sprawling 225,000-acre park is home to numerous snow-capped peaks, pristine lakes, deep valleys, and lush forests. It is a perfect destination for adventure lovers, nature enthusiasts, hikers, bikers, wildlife watchers, and cultural travelers.

Local Culture and Customs of Los Nevados National Park

The locals of Los Nevados National Park are friendly and welcoming. It is essential to respect their culture and customs, such as greeting people in a polite manner, asking before taking photos, and dressing appropriately in religious and cultural venues.

Best Time to Visit Los Nevados National Park

The best time to visit Los Nevados National Park is during the dry season, which lasts from December to February and July to September. It is advisable to avoid the rainy season as the trails can be muddy and slippery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Los Nevados National Park

Do:

  • Respect the nature and wildlife of the park
  • Carry enough water and snacks for the trip
  • Wear proper hiking shoes and clothes
  • Take a guided tour if you’re not an experienced hiker

Don’t:

  • Litter the trails
  • Take plants, rocks, or flowers from the park
  • Approach wildlife too closely
  • Go off-trail

Safety and Health

When visiting Los Nevados National Park, it is essential to take certain safety and health precautions.

Safety in Los Nevados National Park

  • Do not venture into areas that are marked as restricted or off-limits
  • Be aware of the altitude sickness, as some parts of the park are located at high altitudes
  • Follow the park rangers’ instructions and advice

Health in Los Nevados National Park

  • Drink only bottled or purified water
  • Apply sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Carry a basic first-aid kit and any prescription medication you need
