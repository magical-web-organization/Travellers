Introduction to Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna, also known as the Lost City, is an ancient city located in the Sierra Nevada mountains of Colombia. The city was built by the Tairona people around 800 AD, long before the arrival of the Spanish conquistadors. Ciudad Perdida was abandoned during the Spanish conquest and rediscovered only in the 1970s. Today, the site is one of the most important archaeological discoveries in South America and attracts thousands of visitors every year.

List of Places to Visit in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ciudad Perdida Archaeological Park: The main attraction of the city, this archaeological site features the remains of ancient dwellings and terraces.
  • Indigenous Village: The indigenous people of the region still live in traditional villages. Visitors can learn about their way of life and purchase handmade crafts.
  • Zenu Gold Museum: Displays the intricate gold workmanship of the indigenous Zenu people.

Natural Attractions of Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sierra Nevada Mountains: The mountains around Ciudad Perdida offer stunning views and a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, trekking, and bird watching.
  • Río Buritaca: One of the region’s most beautiful rivers, visitors can swim, fish, or go rafting.
  • Pueblito: A smaller archaeological site near Ciudad Perdida that offers similar cultural and natural attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Visitors must hike to reach Ciudad Perdida. The trek lasts four to six days, depending on the route and tour chosen. A moderate level of fitness is required, and guides are mandatory for the trek. Some tour operators offer mountain biking tours as an alternative to hiking.

Best Things to Do in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna: Activities and Tours

  • Hiking/Trekking: Trekking is the main activity in Ciudad Perdida, where visitors will encounter diverse flora and fauna.
  • Bird Watching: Visitors will delight in the colourful birds that can be easily spotted along the hiking trail and in the surrounding mountains.
  • Rafting and Swimming: Visitors may take a dip in the pristine waters of Río Buritaca or go rafting and admire the stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Day 1 - Arrival in Santa Marta
Day 2 - Trek start and hike to Adán camp
Day 3 - Trek to Paraiso camp
Day 4 - Trek to Wiwa camp
Day 5 - Trek to Ciudad Perdida and explore
Day 6 - Return trek to Adán camp
Day 7 - Return trek to Santa Marta

Food and Drink Scene of Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ajiaco: A traditional Colombian soup with chicken, corn, potatoes, and other vegetables.
  • Arroz con coco: A sweet rice dish with coconut milk and raisins.
  • Patacones: Fried plantain slices, often served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A traditional fermented corn drink.
  • Canelazo: A hot alcoholic drink made with aguardiente, sugar, cinnamon, and lime.

Where to Stay in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Since Ciudad Perdida is only accessible by trek, visitors will spend nights in camps along the trail. Most tour operators provide tents, food, and necessary equipment. In Santa Marta, there are many hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to choose from.

What to Buy in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: The indigenous people of the region produce beautiful crafts such as jewellery, woven hammocks, and hand-carved masks.
  • Coffee: Colombia is famous for its high-quality coffee. Tour operators may offer visits to coffee plantations.

Local Culture and Customs of Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

The Tairona people were skilled at agriculture and metalworking. They believed in the spiritual power of nature and were skilled at astronomical observations. Visitors should respect the cultural traditions of the indigenous people who still inhabit the region.

Best Time to Visit Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

The dry season is the best time to visit, from December to February and July to September. During the rainy season, from April to June and October to November, the trail can be muddy and slippery, making it difficult to hike.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Do:

  • Respect the cultural traditions of the indigenous people.
  • Hire a guide from a reputable company.
  • Bring comfortable and appropriate footwear.

Don’t:

  • Remove any artefacts or damage the archaeological site.
  • Hike alone or at night.
  • Litter or disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Visitors should be aware of the risks involved in hiking and take necessary precautions. Theft can also be an issue, so visitors should keep valuables safe and secure. It is recommended to book tours with a reputable company.

Health in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

Visitors should take precautions to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue and zika. It is also recommended to bring a first-aid kit and any necessary medications.

Getting to Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can fly into Santa Marta and take a bus or taxi to Machete Pelao, the starting point for the trek. Some tour operators include round-trip transportation from Santa Marta in their packages.

Airports Near Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

  • Simón Bolívar International Airport (SMR), Santa Marta

Bus Stations Near Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

  • Machete Pelao

Other Options

Some tour operators may offer helicopter tours or private transportation for visitors who are unable to undertake the trek.

How to Get Around in Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna

The only way to reach Ciudad Perdida is by trek. Visitors will hike through the Sierra Nevada mountains and stay in camps along the way.

Where to Go Next from Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tayrona National Park: Visitors can explore this beautiful park with stunning beaches, coral reefs, and rainforest.
  • Santa Marta: This historic city has museums, restaurants, and nightlife.
  • Taganga: A small fishing village with a laid-back vibe and great seafood.

Conclusion

Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna is a unique destination that offers travellers a chance to explore Colombia’s indigenous heritage while also enjoying breathtaking natural beauty. Visitors should prepare themselves for a challenging but rewarding hiking experience and respect the cultural traditions of the indigenous people who still inhabit the region. Plan your trip with this comprehensive tour guide to make the most of your visit to Ciudad Perdida de Teyuna.
