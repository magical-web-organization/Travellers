Introduction to Costa Norte

Nestled on the Caribbean coast of Colombia is Costa Norte, a region teeming with cultural and natural attractions. The area boasts vibrant cities, scenic beaches, and lush landscapes perfect for outdoor adventures.

Costa Norte is made up of the departments of Atlántico, Bolívar, Cesar, Córdoba, La Guajira, Magdalena, San Andrés, Providencia, and Santa Marta. Each department offers unique experiences and attractions, making Costa Norte a must-see destination for any traveler.

Local Culture and Customs of Costa Norte

The local culture of Costa Norte is influenced by the indigenous Wayuu and Kogi communities, as well as Spanish settlers. The region is known for its lively music and dance scenes, with styles like Cumbia and Champeta popular in the area.

Best Time to Visit Costa Norte

The best time to visit Costa Norte is during the dry season from December to March. The region experiences a lot of rainfall during the rest of the year, which can make outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Costa Norte

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Explore the area’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Make negative comments about indigenous culture.
  • Leave valuables unattended in tourist areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Costa Norte

Costa Norte is generally safe for travelers, but it is important to take precautions like avoiding isolated areas and not carrying valuables in public. Petty crime like theft and pickpocketing can occur in tourist areas, so it’s best to stay vigilant.

Health in Costa Norte

Travelers should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before visiting Costa Norte. It’s also recommended to get vaccinated against hepatitis A and typhoid. Mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika are a concern, so it’s best to take precautions like wearing insect repellent and protective clothing.
