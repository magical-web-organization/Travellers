Montería is a hidden gem of Colombia that offers visitors a less touristy and more authentic experience. Located in the Western Costa Norte of Colombia, Montería is a vibrant city that takes pride in its cultural heritage and natural beauty. In this guide, we will take you on a journey to explore all that Montería has to offer and help you plan your trip accordingly.

Introduction to Montería

Montería is the capital of the department of Córdoba, located in the Caribbean region of Colombia. It is known for its lush greenery, diverse wildlife, colonial architecture, and vibrant local culture. The city is also famous for being the birthplace of Porro, a typical Colombian dance that originated in the Caribbean region.

List of Places to Visit in Montería

Below are some of the must-visit places in Montería:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Porro: A museum that showcases the history and evolution of the traditional dance form “Porro”.
  • Palacio de la Gobernación: A historical building that hosts the local government.
  • Catedral San Jerónimo: A stunning cathedral that boasts colonial architecture and tranquil ambiance.
  • Plaza de la Ganadería: A square that pays tribute to the local livestock farming culture.

Natural Attractions of Montería: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Nacional Natural Paramillo: A national park that offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, birding, and camping.
  • Los Colorados Wildlife Sanctuary: A wildlife sanctuary that is home to various species of birds and mammals, including the howler monkey and jaguar.
  • La Mojarra: A river that is popular for water activities such as rafting, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Balneario Hurtado: A natural swimming pool with crystal clear water nestled in the lush jungle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montería

If you love hiking and biking, Montería won’t disappoint you. These are some of the best routes to explore the natural beauty of the region:

  • La Ruana Hiking Trail: A 7-kilometer trail that offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding hills.
  • Las Mujeres del Silencio Hiking Trail: A 5-kilometer trail that takes you through the dense jungle and ends with a beautiful waterfall.
  • Montería-Guacamaya Bike Trail: A 25-kilometer trail that runs along the river and offers stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Montería: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Porro dance lesson: Learn the steps of the traditional Colombian dance form with a certified instructor.
  • Visit a coffee plantation: Discover the process of coffee production and taste some of the finest Colombian coffee.
  • Experience the local nightlife: Go salsa dancing, enjoy live music, or have a few drinks at a local bar.
  • Take a boat tour: Explore the rivers and creeks of the region and witness the local wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montería

Day 1: Visit the Museo del Porro and enjoy a Porro dance show in the evening.

Day 2: Explore the Los Colorados Wildlife Sanctuary and take a boat tour.

Day 3: Go hiking on the La Ruana Hiking Trail and visit the Palacio de la Gobernación.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the Parque Nacional Natural Paramillo and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 5: Visit La Mojarra River and enjoy water activities.

Day 6: Visit Balneario Hurtado and relax on the natural swimming pool.

Day 7: Explore the local markets and sample traditional Colombian food.

Food and Drink Scene of Montería

Food is a crucial part of the local culture of Montería. You must try these local specialties:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carimañola: A deep-fried dough filled with beef and yucca served with hot sauce.
  • Calentao: A rice and bean dish typically eaten for breakfast.
  • Arepa de Huevo: A fried arepa filled with egg and beef.
  • Mute: A soup made with tripe, beans, vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sabajón: A creamy alcoholic drink made with egg yolks, sugar, and rum.
  • Aguapanela: A sweet drink made with sugarcane juice and water.
  • Té de Limón: A refreshing lemon tea.

Where to Stay in Montería: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are multiple options for accommodations in Montería, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Hotel San Jerónimo Plaza: A modern hotel near the city center.
  • Hotel Florida Sinú: A family-friendly hotel with an outdoor pool.
  • Hostal Casa 10: A budget-friendly hostel with a lively atmosphere.
  • Hotel Juan Solito: A boutique hotel that offers a luxurious experience.

What to Buy in Montería: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional handicrafts: Buy handcrafted jewelry, baskets, textiles, and pottery in the local markets.
  • Coffee: Bring home some of the finest Colombian coffee from the nearby plantations.
  • Traditional clothing: Get yourself a colorful “Sombrero vueltiao,” a typical Colombian hat made of palm leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Montería

The locals of Montería take pride in their cultural heritage and are known for their friendly nature. The city hosts various festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Festival Nacional del Porro. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Montería

The best time to visit Montería is between December and February when the weather is dry, and the temperatures are pleasant. However, you can visit the city throughout the year as the weather remains warm and humid due to its proximity to the sea.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montería

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region through hiking and biking routes.
  • Learn and dance the traditional Colombian dance “Porro.”

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites.
  • Take the local wildlife as souvenirs.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montería

Montería is a safe city for visitors, but it is always advised to take necessary precautions while traveling. Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Montería

Make sure to get vaccinated against yellow fever and malaria before visiting the city. It is also advised to use mosquito repellent and drink only bottled water.

Getting to Montería: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Montería

The nearest airport to Montería is Los Garzones Airport, located about 15 minutes away from the city center. The airport offers flights to major cities in Colombia, such as Bogota, Medellin, and Cartagena.

Bus Stations Near Montería

The city is well-connected by bus networks with major cities in Colombia. The main bus station is Terminal de Transportes de Montería and offers both national and local bus services.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available in Montería. It is recommended to book taxis in advance through ride-sharing apps or your hotel.

How to Get Around in Montería

The best way to get around in Montería is by public transport or taxi. The city’s public transport system includes buses and colectivos (shared taxis) that are affordable and reliable. Taxis are also readily available and can be booked through ride-sharing apps or hailed on the street.

Where to Go Next from Montería: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cartagena: A UNESCO world heritage site that showcases colonial architecture and stunning beaches.
  • Santa Marta: A coastal city that offers access to Tayrona National Park and La Ciudad Perdida.
  • Medellin: A vibrant city known for its nightlife and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Montería is a city that offers a unique experience to visitors. From its natural beauty to its rich cultural heritage, there is something for everyone in this Caribbean gem. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your trip to Montería and explore all that the city has to offer.
