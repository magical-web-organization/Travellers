Introduction to Western Costa Norte

Located in the northern part of Colombia, Western Costa Norte is a stunning destination that often flies under the radar of travelers who flock to more famous cities such as Bogotá and Cartagena. But those who venture to this beautiful region will be rewarded with plenty of hidden gems to explore. Western Costa Norte offers a heady mix of culture, natural beauty, adventure and relaxation - all in one place. Keep reading to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and tips for planning your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Western Costa Norte

Here are some of the must-visit places that you should add to your Western Costa Norte itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alto de los Idolos Archaeological Park: Step back in time and explore the ruins of the ancient Tayrona civilization, one of the most fascinating cultures to have inhabited the coastal region of Colombia.
  • Museo del Oro Zenú: Home to more than 5000 gold pieces, this museum showcases the history and art of the indigenous Zenú culture, worth a trip for history and archaeology enthusiasts alike.
  • Sanctuary of the Lord of Miracles of Buga: A Catholic pilgrimage site built in the mid-nineteenth century, considered one of the oldest and most iconic religious monuments in the country.

Natural Attractions of Western Costa Norte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tayrona National Natural Park: this beautiful park is perfect for hiking, camping, swimming, and exploring. It’s home to some of the most breathtaking beaches on the planet, dense tropical forests, abundant wildlife, and archaeological sites.
  • Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta: This imposing mountain range is the highest coastal mountain range in the world and home to a number of indigenous communities. This is an ideal destination for hikers and adventure-seekers as it offers trekking, bird-watching, and waterfalls.
  • Chiribiquete National Park: Home to stunning rock formations, pre-Columbian pictographs and indigenous communities, this biological and archaeological reserve is an unforgettable landscape and a testament to the resilience of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Costa Norte

Western Costa Norte offers a variety of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Cabo de la Vela: A great spot to take in stunning views of the sea and desert, with multicolored cliffs as a backdrop. This is excellent for mountain biking or a steep hike.
  • La Ciudad Perdida: Or the lost city, a challenging but worthwhile hike deep into the jungle that leads to a magnificent abandoned archaeological site dating back to more than a thousand years ago.
  • The Cocora Valley: One of the most beautiful valleys in the world, with a unique ecosystem hosting giant palms - the tallest palms in the world.

Best Things to Do in Western Costa Norte: Activities and Tours

Western Costa Norte offers an endless range of activities to suit every type of traveler. Here are some of the most popular experiences:

  • Whale watching: From July to October, visitors can witness humpback whales as they migrate from the Antarctic to the warm waters of the Pacific, where they mate, play, and give birth.
  • Kayaking and Snorkeling: Immerse yourself in the crystal-clear waters of the Caribbean and uncover a colorful underwater world teeming with marine life.
  • Coffee Tours: Coffee is an important agricultural product in Western Costa Norte. Visitors can participate in guided tours to learn all about the process of coffee production, including visiting the plantations, and trying the famous Colombian coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Western Costa Norte

Here’s an itinerary to make the most of your week in Western Costa Norte:

Day 1: Arrive in Santa Marta and explore the historic center. Day 2-3: Head to Tayrona National Park for some beach time and jungle trekking. Day 4-5: Trek to Ciudad Perdida - a challenging but rewarding hike. Day 6: Head to Cabo de la Vela for some desert scenery and beach time. Day 7: Enjoy some whale watching or kayaking or take a coffee tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Western Costa Norte

The food and drink scene in Western Costa Norte is rich and diverse, influenced by indigenous cultures, African and Spanish heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bandeja Paisa: A hearty plate of beans, rice, beef, and avocado.
  • Sancocho: A rich and flavorful soup made of plantains or yucca, chicken, pork, beef, or fish and cassava root.
  • Arroz con Coco: A delicious side dish made of rice cooked in coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Aguardiente: A strong anise-flavored alcoholic drink that is extremely popular in Colombia.
  • Canelazo: A hot alcoholic drink made of aguardiente, water, and cinnamon, often served during Christmas time.
  • Colombian Coffee: World famous for its quality, fragrance, and taste, Colombia is one of the largest coffee exporters in the world.

Where to Stay in Western Costa Norte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Western Costa Norte offers a range of accommodation options that suit every budget and preference. Here are our top picks:

  • Casa de Leda Boutique Hotel: A charming hotel in the historic center of Santa Marta.
  • Ecohabs Tayrona: Rustic bungalows built with local materials and overlooking the Caribbean Sea at Tayrona National Park.
  • La Brisa Tranquila: A laid-back hostel in the heart of Palomino beach, with affordable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Western Costa Norte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Western Costa Norte:

  • Wayuu Bags: Beautiful hand-woven bags made by the indigenous Wayuu people - a perfect beach accessory.
  • Local Coffee: Colombian coffee is world-famous, and you can buy high-quality beans to take home.
  • Artisanal crafts: Colombia is home to some of the most skillful artisans in the world, and you can find unique handmade crafts such as pottery, textiles, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Western Costa Norte

The culture of Western Costa Norte is a mix of indigenous roots and Spanish influence. People speak Spanish and some still adhere to traditional customs. The region is known for its love of music and dance. Religion plays a significant role, and there are many catholic festivals that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Western Costa Norte

Western Costa Norte experiences two seasons; dry and rainy. The dry season is from December to March, while the wet season is from May to October. The best time to visit is during the dry season when there is much less rainfall, and the weather is moderate, ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western Costa Norte

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.
  • Take reusable water bottle and refuse single-use plastic containers or bags.
  • Be adventurous and try new foods.

Don’t:

  • Do not walk alone at night or in isolated areas.
  • Do not take unlicensed taxis or accept rides from strangers.
  • Do not buy drugs or take part in any illegal activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Western Costa Norte

Western Costa Norte is generally as safe destination for travelers, but you should take normal precautions like any other destination. Keep valuables safe, avoid dark streets or isolated areas, and be aware of scams. If you plan on hiking or trekking, always have a guide with you to avoid any danger.

Health in Western Costa Norte

It is always advisable to keep your vaccinations up to date, especially for travelers to rural areas. Wear appropriate clothing as there are mosquitos, especially during the rainy season. Be mindful of drinking water; always drink bottled water or use your own water filter. Sun protection is also essential.

Getting to Western Costa Norte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Western Costa Norte is an easily accessible destination. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Western Costa Norte

  • Simón Bolivar International Airport (SMR): The main airport in Santa Marta, with flights connecting to Bogota and other major cities in Colombia.

Bus Stations Near Western Costa Norte

  • Santa Marta Terminal: A large bus terminal in Santa Marta, with frequent services to other major cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Cars can be rented from agencies in Santa Marta or at the airport.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available, and ride-hailing apps like Uber also operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Western Costa Norte

There are several options to get around Western Costa Norte, including buses, taxis, and rented cars. But be warned, if you are planning on driving in Western Costa Norte, roads can be difficult to navigate, and heavy traffic can be encountered on the main routes. A better option is to hire a knowledgeable driver or join organized tours.

Where to Go Next from Western Costa Norte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Western Costa Norte is an excellent starting point to explore other captivating destinations in Colombia, including:

  • Medellín: A vibrant city known as the “City of Eternal Spring”, famous for its museums, nightlife, and innovative cuisine.
  • Cartagena: A UNESCO World Heritage City, Cartagena is a treasure trove of history and culture with a stunning colonial center.
  • Barranquilla: Known for being the birthplace of the Carnaval de Barranquilla, one of the biggest festivals in Colombia.

Conclusion

Western Costa Norte is a spectacular region in Colombia that offers an abundance of cultural, outdoor, and natural experiences for visitors. By following this guide, you’ll have an unforgettable trip to Western Costa Norte and bring home memories that will last a lifetime.
