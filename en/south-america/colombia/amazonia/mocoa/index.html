The Ultimate Mocoa Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Mocoa

Pristine waterfalls, verdant forests, and wildlife in abundance, these are what make Mocoa, Colombia, a nature-lover’s paradise. Situated in the heart of Amazonia, Mocoa offers ample opportunities to explore the hidden treasures of the tropical rainforest. This comprehensive Mocoa travel guide will help you get the most out of your trip to this naturally blessed land.

Introduction to Mocoa

Mocoa is a small city in the Putumayo department of southern Colombia, located on the banks of the Mocoa River. The city offers an array of attractions, from breathtaking waterfalls and scenic viewpoints to museums and historical places, making it a perfect travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Mocoa

Here is a list of must-visit places and things to do in Mocoa.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Anthropology and Ethnography: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous people of Putumayo.

  • The Church of Our Lady of Perpetual Help: The church dates back to the early 20th century and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Mocoa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cascada del Fin del Mundo: This waterfall is located at the end of the world, as the locals say. The crystal-clear water falling from 80 meters is an enchanting view.

  • Cascada de Hornoyaco: It is a three-stage waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. The waterfall is a perfect spot for a refreshing swim.

  • Cascada de San Antonio: This is one of the most accessible waterfalls in the region. You can take a refreshing dip in the pool below the waterfall.

  • Pueblito Patojo: Located on the bank of the Mocoa River, Pueblito Patojo is a small village that offers sweeping views of the green mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mocoa

  • Finca el Camino: A 7 km hike in the middle of the jungle that leads to a spectacular viewpoint.

  • Hiking to meet Yaje: You can undertake a guided hike through the depths of Amazonia to learn about the Yaje (Ayahuasca) plant.

  • Los Poços: A 7 km hike through the forest and river, culminating in a beautiful series of waterfalls where you can jump into the refreshing pools.

  • El Encanto: A two-day hike that takes you through thick jungle and boasts more magnificent waterfalls along the trail that culminates in a stunning one at the end.

Best Things to Do in Mocoa: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Mocoa River: An exciting adventure that will make your heart race.

  • Camping: There are several campsites in the area, including the idyllic site of El Crucero.

  • Night Walk Tour: Guided tours of the forest at night with an eco-tourism touch is a unique experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mocoa

If you’re planning to spend a week in Mocoa, here’s a suggested itinerary.

Day Activity
1 Arrival at Mocoa, explore the city centre
2 Tour of Finca El Camino
3 Rafting on the Mocoa River
4 Cascada de San Antonio excursion
5 Day trip to Pueblito Patojo and the Museums
6 Hiking to El Encanto
7 Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Mocoa

Besides its natural beauty, Mocoa is also known for its gastronomic delights.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chontaduro: This fruit is a regional delicacy, it is eaten roasted or boiled.

  • Ayampaco: A traditional dish made with chicken or pork wrapped in banana leaves and cooked in a charcoal oven.

Local Beverages

  • Guarapo: A refreshing drink made from sugarcane juice.

  • Café de la Casa: Delicious coffee made with locally grown beans, it is the perfect start of the day.

Where to Stay in Mocoa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation, Mocoa offers a wide range of options to suit all tastes and budgets.

  • Finca Camino verde: This is located in the heart of the jungle and offers excellent accommodation with spectacular views.

  • La Serrana: This hostel is located a few minutes away from the city centre and is a perfect option for backpackers.

  • Casa del Mono: This guest house is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms with all amenities.

What to Buy in Mocoa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Mocoa, do not forget to buy some of the local products as souvenirs.

  • Traditional woven baskets: These are handmade baskets that are a true work of art.

  • Crafts made by indigenous people: Handmade crafts made by the indigenous people of Putumayo are unique and a perfect souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mocoa

Mocoa is a city that takes great pride in its rich cultural heritage, and it reflects in the people’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Mocoa

The best time to visit Mocoa is between the months of December to March, as the weather is sunny and perfect for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mocoa

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mocoa:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take an experienced guide when venturing deep into the jungle.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and a raincoat.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in the city or the forest.
  • Avoid travelling alone even during the daylight.
  • Do not touch or harm the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mocoa

Mocoa is a relatively safe city, but it is always advisable to exercise caution and care. Take care of your valuables and keep them safe.

Health in Mocoa

The health facilities in Mocoa are limited, so it is advisable to carry your medication and health insurance.

Getting to Mocoa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the transportation options to get to Mocoa:

Airports Near Mocoa

  • The nearest airport is Guamués Airport.

Bus Stations Near Mocoa

  • The main Bus Terminal of Mocoa is located in the city center.

Other Options

  • Taxis or Private car for driving.

How to Get Around in Mocoa

You can take a taxi, moto taxi, or public bus to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Mocoa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations to explore after finishing your visit to Mocoa:

  • Putumayo: A region known for its fantastic culture, and a paradise on earth with Lake Cocha, a breathtaking view and of course nearby Putumayo Amazon jungle.

  • San Agustín Archaeological Park: An ancient pre-Columbian archaeological treasure, a must-see for history buffs.

  • Ipiales and Las Lajas: Home to the breathtaking Las Lajas Sanctuary.

Conclusion

Planning a trip to Mocoa is a great idea if you’re seeking to reconnect with nature, while also learning about the local culture and traditions. From natural wonders to adventure activities, there’s truly something for everyone in this paradise on earth. Follow this comprehensive Mocoa travel guide and let yourself fall into the magic of Amazonia.
