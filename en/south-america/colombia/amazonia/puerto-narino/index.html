Introduction to Puerto Nariño

Located in the heart of the Amazon Rainforest, Puerto Nariño is a charming town in the Colombian region of Amazonia. Known for its lush jungle landscapes and vibrant cultural scene, this hidden gem of South America is a must-visit destination for adventure seekers and culture lovers alike.

Founded in the 1970s as an indigenous settlement, Puerto Nariño is home to a rich and diverse mix of cultures, with indigenous tribes living alongside Colombian settlers and international visitors. Thanks to its unique location in the heart of the Amazon, the town is also home to an incredible array of flora and fauna, making it a great destination for eco-tourism and outdoor adventure.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything Puerto Nariño has to offer, from its top natural and cultural attractions, to its best hiking and biking routes, local food and drink scene, and much more. Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or just a short visit, this guide has you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Puerto Nariño

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Ticuna: Explore the fascinating history and culture of the Ticuna people, one of the largest indigenous tribes in the Amazon region.
  • Isla de los Micos: This monkey sanctuary is home to dozens of different monkey species, and offers a unique opportunity to see these incredible creatures up close in their natural habitats.
  • Isla de los Delfines: Take a boat tour to this picturesque island, which is home to a resident population of friendly pink river dolphins.
  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de la Paz: This beautiful Catholic church is a great place to learn more about the history and religion of this important South American region.
  • La Tarapaca: Climb to the top of this traditional lookout tower for stunning panoramic views of the surrounding jungle and river.

Natural Attractions of Puerto Nariño: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural Amacayacu: Take a guided tour through this incredible national park, which is home to hundreds of species of plants and animals, including jaguars, giant otters, and harpy eagles.
  • Reserva Natural Tanimboca: This verdant nature reserve is a great place for hiking, birdwatching, and exploring the jungle canopy on a zip-line adventure.
  • Lago Tarapoto: Take a relaxing boat ride across this tranquil lake, which is home to a variety of fish species and is a popular spot for swimming and fishing.
  • Mirador de la Piedra del Amor: Climb to the top of this natural lookout point for stunning views of the surrounding rainforest and river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puerto Nariño

  • Camino Ecologico: Take a nature hike along this beautiful ecological trail, which winds through the lush rainforest and offers incredible views of the surrounding jungle landscapes.
  • Ciruito de Las Toninas: This challenging hiking and biking trail takes you through some of the most remote and beautiful parts of the Amazon rainforest, with stunning views of the river and surrounding jungle.
  • Ciruito de Las Mariposas: Follow this scenic hiking trail through the jungle to discover some of the most breathtaking waterfalls and natural swimming pools in the region.

Best Things to Do in Puerto Nariño: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a guided boat tour along the Amazon river to discover hidden beaches, secret estuaries, and some of the most incredible wildlife and plant species in the region.
  • Kayaking and Paddle-Boarding: Rent a kayak or paddle-board and explore the calm waters of the river at your own pace, soaking up the incredible natural beauty of the region.
  • Fishing: Test your fishing skills by hooking up with a local guide and heading out to the river to try your luck catching catfish, piranhas, and other local fish species.
  • Artisan Workshops: Take a class or workshop with local artisans to learn traditional crafts like hammock weaving, pottery, and basket-making.

Food and Drink Scene of Puerto Nariño

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bolón: This traditional Ecuadorian dish is made from mashed green plantains stuffed with cheese and fried until crispy.
  • Caldo de Bolas: A hearty soup made with beef or chicken, potatoes, peanuts, and cilantro.
  • Chontaduro: A unique type of palm fruit often used in salads or juices.
  • Tucupi: A traditional Amazonian soup made with chicken or fish, flavored with local herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A traditional fermented corn drink, often sweetened with local fruits and spices.
  • Cascada: A refreshing fruit smoothie made with local fruits like papaya, pineapple, and mango.
  • Agua de Horchata: A sweet, milky drink made with rice, cinnamon, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Puerto Nariño: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Jangada: This family-run guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere, with easy access to all of the top attractions in the town.
  • Amazonas Bed and Breakfast: This small, eco-friendly hostel offers private rooms and shared dorms, with a focus on sustainability and conservation.
  • Hotel Waira: This upscale hotel offers spacious suites and views of the river, with amenities like a swimming pool and rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Puerto Nariño: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hamacas: These handmade hammocks are a popular local craft, and make great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Pottery: Local artisans create beautiful pottery using traditional techniques and local materials.
  • Jewelry: Look for locally-made pieces featuring bright Amazonian gems like agate and amethyst.

Local Culture and Customs of Puerto Nariño

Visitors to Puerto Nariño should be respectful of the local culture and customs, which include a deep reverence for nature and the local indigenous tribes. It is also important to dress modestly and avoid excessive noise, particularly in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Puerto Nariño

The best time to visit Puerto Nariño is during the dry season, which runs from December to March. This is the best time for outdoor activities like hiking and biking, and also the best time to see wildlife like pink river dolphins and monkeys.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puerto Nariño

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take guided tours to explore the natural attractions of the region.
  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or make excessive noise in residential areas.
  • Interact with wildlife in an inappropriate or dangerous way.
  • Engage in illegal or unethical activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puerto Nariño

While Puerto Nariño is generally a safe destination, visitors should take basic precautions like keeping valuables out of sight and not walking alone at night. It is also recommended to book tours and activities through reputable tour operators.

Health in Puerto Nariño

Visitors to Puerto Nariño should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites and are advised to get vaccinated for yellow fever before visiting the region. It is also important to stay hydrated and to avoid drinking untreated water.

Getting to Puerto Nariño: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puerto Nariño

The closest airport to Puerto Nariño is the Alfredo Vasquez Cobo International Airport in Leticia, which offers daily flights to major cities in Colombia like Bogotá and Medellín.

Bus Stations Near Puerto Nariño

There are no bus stations in Puerto Nariño itself, but visitors can take a bus or taxi from Leticia to reach the town.

Other Options

Visitors to Puerto Nariño can also arrive by boat, with several tour operators offering river boat tours from Leticia and other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Puerto Nariño

The best way to get around Puerto Nariño is on foot or by bicycle, as the town is small and easy to navigate on foot, and there are several bike rental shops in town. Visitors can also take a taxi or moto-taxi for transport within the town or to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Puerto Nariño: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Leticia: This nearby town is a great destination for exploring the Amazonian rainforest, with attractions like the Parque Natural Nacional Amacayacu and the largest zipline in South America.
  • Tabatinga: This Brazilian border town is a great place for shopping and exploring local markets.
  • Iquitos, Peru: Visitors can take a boat or plane to this nearby Peruvian city, which is known for its vibrant local culture and is a great base for exploring the Amazon jungle.

Conclusion

Puerto Nariño is a truly special destination in the heart of the Amazon rainforest. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique mix of indigenous and Colombian traditions, this vibrant town offers visitors an unforgettable travel experience. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, or simply a bit of rest and relaxation, Puerto Nariño has something for everyone.
