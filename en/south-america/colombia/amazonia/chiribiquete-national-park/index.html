Introduction to Chiribiquete National Park

Located in the heart of Amazonia, Chiribiquete National Park in Colombia is a natural wonderland that promises to leave you spellbound. The park is spread over an area of 12,000 square miles and is known for its lush rainforest, spectacular waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.

Local Culture and Customs of Chiribiquete National Park

The indigenous community of Chiribiquete celebrates its cultural roots and traditions. Travelers are expected to respect their customs and follow the guidelines and restrictions.

Best Time to Visit Chiribiquete National Park

The best time to visit Chiribiquete National Park is between December and February when the weather is mild, and the rainy season has ended.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chiribiquete National Park

Do:

  • Follow the guidelines of the park
  • Hire local guides for tours and hikes
  • Respect the local communities

Don’t:

  • Litter the park
  • Damage the flora and fauna
  • Ignore the safety guidelines

Safety and Health

Safety in Chiribiquete National Park

  • Hire authorized guides and tour operators
  • Stay informed about weather conditions
  • Stay alert and cautious about wildlife encounters

Health in Chiribiquete National Park

  • Get vaccinated for yellow fever and Hepatitis A and B
  • Drink bottled water only
  • Wear insect repellent
