Introduction to Amacayacu National Park

Amacayacu National Park is a natural wonderland situated in Amazonia, in Colombia, South America. The park is home to an eclectic mix of flora and fauna, from rarely seen primates and other wildlife, to exotic plants that cannot be found anywhere else on earth. The park lies where the Andes mountains meet the Amazon basin, resulting in diverse geography, featuring soaring mountain peaks, vast forest landscapes, and sprawling river systems. The park has become a favorite destination for nature lovers, ecotourists, and researchers, who come to explore this part of the Amazon region.

List of Places to Visit in Amacayacu National Park

One of the advantages of visiting Amacayacu National Park is that the vast area leaves plenty of places to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places to explore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Indigenous Communities: Visiting the hand-crafted villages within Amazon rainforest to learn about the customs, traditions, and culture of the Tanimuca community.
  • Casa del Marafon: In this museum, visitors can learn about the history of the Amazon through interactive exhibits with local artifacts and objects.
  • Chieftain’s House: Visitors to the Chieftain’s House are given an insight into the political and spiritual significance of the indigenous communities within the park.

Natural Attractions of Amacayacu National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monkey Island: As the name suggests, monkey island is a sanctuary in which to see several species of primates, interacting with each other in their natural habitat.
  • Conchuelas Trail: This moderate hiking trail provides the chance to experience the sights and sounds of the Amazonian jungle with vibrant flora and fauna and incredible views of the Rio Amazonas.
  • Yahuarcaca Lakes: These crystal clear lake views are perfect for swimming, and stand-up paddleboarding, kayaking, and motorboat rides are also available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amacayacu National Park

Amacayacu National Park is an outdoor enthusiast’s dream come true, with numerous hiking and biking trails, each with a unique flavor and varying degrees of difficulty. Some popular routes include:

  • La Jungla Trail: Follow the path of indigenous people who once navigated the forest while learning about medicinal and food plants.
  • Five Senses Trail: This sensory nature trail allows visitors to experience the full beauty of the park with their five senses, while learning about the different floral and faunal composition.
  • Ayu Trail: This moderate hiking route offers the chance to see some of the most beautiful flora and fauna of the park, alongside amazing views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Amacayacu National Park: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking tours, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Amacayacu National Park. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Bird watching: Amacayacu is a bird lover’s paradise with over 450 species of birds, including the famous harpy eagle, the biggest eagle within the Amazon rainforest .
  • Jungle Boat Tours: Embark on an adventure to see the Arapaima gigas, the largest freshwater fish. The tour in the traditional canoes will give you the chance to learn about forest ecology, get closer to the fauna, and learn about the different fluvial systems.
  • Indigenous Cultural Tours: Visit the indigenous communities within the park to get an insight into their culture, see their arts, crafts, and dance performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Amacayacu National Park

Getting to know local food and drink is an essential part of traveling to Amacayacu National Park. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mondongo: Stew made with Tripe, yucca, and potato.
  • Pescado Dorado: Fried fish served with coconut rice.
  • Chontaduro: A unique fruit served as a pulp juice.

Local Beverages

  • Natural juices: Try typical juices made with local fruits mixed with coconut or condensed milk.
  • Café Tinto: A coffee-based beverage extracted from plantations within the Valley area.
  • Viche: It is a homemade alcoholic beverage made from fermented yucca paste juice, essential for Tanimuca rituals.

Where to Stay in Amacayacu National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Within the park’s boundaries are numerous Hostels, Lodges, and campsites to cater to different travelers. Most of these establishments offer a restaurant and bar service, in addition to the accommodation. Here are some recommended accommodations:

  • Waira Selva Hostel: The Waira Selva Hostel is a favorite choice for backpackers located in the Indigenous community and offering cultural exchange experiences.
  • The Omagua Lodge: This eco-friendly lodge is nestled in the heart of the park, surrounded by the forest, and has a floating deck and hammock area for relaxation.
  • The Jungle Palace: A luxury lodge with a swimming pool, hot tub, and private patio rooms that offer opportunities for birdwatching and outdoor dining experiences.

What to Buy in Amacayacu National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can pick up some local souvenirs and products to take back home and always remember their visit to Amacayacu National Park. Here are some recommended products:

  • Handcrafted Beads: Made from locally produced materials, these necklaces, bracelets, and anklets are the perfect souvenir to take back home.
  • A Manaca: Handwoven baskets or hand fans made from natural fibers are great for decoration and look beautiful in any home.
  • Natural Textiles: Hand-woven textiles, bags or covers, are some typical products produced by the indigenous communities within Amacayacu.

Local Culture and Customs of Amacayacu National Park

Amacayacu National Park is home to several indigenous communities, with different cultural practices and customs that are worth experiencing while visiting the park. Visitors should be respectful of local customs by following guidelines on appropriate behavior and dress.

Best Time to Visit Amacayacu National Park

The best time to visit Amacayacu National Park is during the dry season from December to the end of February, when there is less rainfall, fewer mosquitoes, a clearer sky, and a higher concentration of animals gathering in search of clean water. Other good months to visit are June, July, and August, with the most significant chance of rainfall from April to May and September to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amacayacu National Park

Here are some general guidelines on what to do and what not to do when visiting Amacayacu National Park:

Do:

  • Follow the park service’s instructions on safety and regulations.
  • Enjoy the local food and drinks.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions of indigenous communities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the park’s ecosystem.
  • Don’t feed the animals or take part in non-permitted activities.
  • Don’t disrespect the indigenous traditions and culture.

Safety and Health in Amacayacu National Park

Safety in Amacayacu National Park

The park service takes safety and risk management seriously and has policies, and guidelines that visitors must adhere to. Here are some essential safety tips:

  • Always swim in designated areas supervised by park rangers.
  • Do not walk alone at night without a knowledgeable guide.

  • Use sunscreen.

  • Remain cautious of monkeys. They are cute, but some are known to attack tourists when they attempt to take food or belongings.

Health in Amacayacu National Park

Before visiting the park, it’s best to visit a health professional who can provide a recommendation on current shots and vaccines necessary for traveling to the Amazon. Visitors to Amacayacu National Park should practice the following health precautions:

  • Use insect repellent regularly when taking part in outdoor activities.
  • Ensure that water is clean and safe to drink by drinking from certified water sources，or by bringing along a water treatment kit.
  • Wear light-colored comfortable clothing and long sleeves to decrease the chance of mosquito bites.

Getting to Amacayacu National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Amacayacu National Park can be done in a variety of ways, following either a water or land route. Here are some of the most common options:

Airports Near Amacayacu National Park

  • Letica Airport: Located approximately one hour from the park, this airport is serviced by connecting flights from Bogota.

Bus Stations Near Amacayacu National Park

  • Puerto Nariño Bus Terminal: The closest bus station to Amacayacu park is located in Nariño, a small town on the banks of the Amazon River.

Other Options

  • Boat Rides and Ferries: Sailing in one of the small boats from Letica or from Bogotá following the Magdalena River is another option to access the park.

How to Get Around in Amacayacu National Park

Visitors can navigate around the park by foot, bike or hiring a tour guide. Bike rental is readily available at affordable prices. In addition to that, visitors can hire private motorboats to access other remote areas or visit villages.
