Introduction to Cali

Cali is a vibrant city located in Valle del Cauca, in Pacifica, in Colombia, in South America. Known as the salsa capital of the world, Cali offers a rich cultural experience with a mix of modernity and traditional charm. In this guide, you will discover the best things to do, places to visit, food to try, and cultural customs to know to make the most of your trip to Cali.

List of Places to Visit in Cali

Whether you’re interested in culture, history, nature, or adventure, Cali has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Cali:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Tertulia Museum: This modern art museum showcases works from local and international artists.
  • Cali Historical Center: Take a stroll through the charming colonial district of San Antonio and visit some of the historical landmarks, such as the San Antonio Church and the La Merced Archaeological Museum.
  • Museo del Oro Calima: Discover the history of pre-Columbian cultures in the region with a visit to this archaeological museum.
  • Cristo Rey: One of Cali’s most famous landmarks, this statue stands atop a hill and offers stunning views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Cali: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Farallones de Cali National Park: This natural park offers hiking and bird-watching opportunities, as well as stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  • Río Pance: Cool off in the natural pools and waterfalls of this picturesque river, surrounded by lush vegetation.
  • Cerro de las Tres Cruces: Hike up to the three crosses on this hill for panoramic views of the city.
  • Botanical Garden of Cali: With over 2,000 plant species, this garden is a paradise for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cali

Cali offers an abundance of outdoor adventure opportunities, and hiking and biking are two of the most popular activities. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Pico de Loro Trail: This 4.5-mile trail takes you up to the peak of Pico de Loro and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  • Ecoparque Bataclán: This park offers several trails of varying difficulty, including a route to a waterfall.
  • Salsipuedes Trail: This 2-mile trail takes you through lush vegetation to a hidden waterfall.
  • Ciclovia: Cali’s weekly bike ride takes place every Sunday and is a great way to explore the city on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Cali: Activities and Tours

Aside from the cultural and natural attractions, Cali also offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Cali:

  • Salsa Dancing: Cali is known as the salsa capital of the world, so it’s a must-do activity while you’re here. Take a lesson or join a dance party at one of the many salsa clubs in the city.
  • Gastronomic Tour: Discover the flavors of Cali with a food tour that takes you through local markets, street food stalls, and restaurants.
  • Coffee Tour: Colombia is famous for its coffee, and you can learn all about the process of growing, harvesting, and roasting coffee beans on a tour of a local coffee plantation.
  • Paragliding: Admire the stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains from above on a paragliding tour.
  • Cali Zoo: With over 500 species of animals, including many native to Colombia, a visit to Cali Zoo is a fun and educational activity for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cali

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Cali, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Cali Historical Center and visit the La Tertulia Museum
2 Hike to the top of Cerro de las Tres Cruces for panoramic views of the city
3 Take a coffee tour at a local plantation and enjoy a salsa dance lesson in the evening
4 Explore Farallones de Cali National Park and enjoy a picnic lunch
5 Visit the Cristo Rey statue and spend the afternoon at Río Pance
6 Take a gastronomic tour of the city and end the day with a paragliding tour
7 Relax at Cali Zoo in the morning and spend the rest of the day souvenir shopping and trying local food

Food and Drink Scene of Cali

Cali’s food scene is rich with traditional dishes and influences from African, European, and indigenous cultures. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chontaduro: This fruit, also known as the “Babe Ruth,” is typically served with honey or salt.
  • Sancocho: A hearty soup made with chicken, plantains, yucca, and other vegetables.
  • Lulo Juice: A refreshing drink made with the native lulo fruit.
  • Empanadas: Savory turnovers typically filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Ajiaco: A soup made with chicken, potatoes, and corn, topped with avocado, cream, and capers.

Local Beverages

  • Aguapanela: A sweet drink made from panela, a type of unrefined sugar, and water.
  • Canelazo: A hot drink made with aguardiente (an anise-flavored liquor), cinnamon, and panela.
  • Coffee: Colombia is famous for its coffee, and a cup of freshly brewed coffee is a must-try while you’re here.
  • Beer: Local beers such as Club Colombia and Cerveza Aguila are popular options.

Where to Stay in Cali: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cali offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Cali:

  • Hotel Intercontinental Cali: This 5-star hotel offers luxurious rooms and amenities, including a rooftop pool and spa.
  • Casa del Poeta: A charming guesthouse in the heart of the San Antonio district, with beautifully decorated rooms and a rooftop terrace.
  • El Viajero Hostel: A popular hostel chain in South America, El Viajero offers affordable dorms and private rooms, as well as a fun and social atmosphere.
  • NH Cali Royal: This 4-star hotel offers comfortable rooms and a central location near many of Cali’s most popular attractions.

What to Buy in Cali: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cali offers many unique souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • Salsa Music: Cali is known for its salsa music, so a CD or vinyl record of local artists is a great souvenir.
  • Handicrafts: The San Antonio district is a great place to find local handicrafts such as pottery, textiles, and wood carvings.
  • Coffee: Colombia is famous for its coffee, so bring back a bag of high-quality beans to brew at home.
  • Aguardiente: This anise-flavored liquor is a popular drink in Colombia and makes a great gift for friends back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Cali

Colombia is known for its warm and friendly people, and Cali is no exception. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: Colombians are friendly and often greet each other with a kiss on the cheek. Men will often shake hands.
  • Time: Colombians tend to be more relaxed about time and punctuality than some other cultures, so don’t be surprised if meetings and events start late.
  • Tipping: Tipping is appreciated but not always expected. For restaurants, a 10% gratuity is typical.
  • Dress: Colombians tend to dress up for social events, so it’s a good idea to bring some nicer clothes for going out at night.

Best Time to Visit Cali

Cali has a tropical climate with relatively consistent temperatures throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is between December and March, when the weather is driest and the city is lively with festivals and events. June and July are also good months to visit, as they offer slightly cooler temperatures and less chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cali

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Cali:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before you go to help you communicate with locals.
  • Try the local food and drinks, especially salsa music and dance.
  • Take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as Cali is in a region where dengue fever and other illnesses are common.
  • Take a guided tour in order to learn more about the city’s culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Wear expensive jewelry or carry too much cash when walking in the city.
  • Drink tap water, which can cause stomach problems.
  • Take unregulated taxis, as they may be unsafe.

Safety and Health

Like many cities in South America, Cali has some safety concerns to keep in mind. It’s important to take precautions to avoid theft and to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night. In terms of health, it’s important to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and to drink bottled water or other safe fluids. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you as they may not be available or regulated in Cali.

Getting to Cali: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cali is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are the most common transportation options:

Airports Near Cali

  • Alfonso Bonilla Aragón International Airport (CLO): This is the main airport serving Cali and has regular flights to domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Cali

  • Terminal de Transporte de Cali: Located in the northern part of the city, this bus terminal offers regular connections to destinations throughout Colombia.

Other Options

  • Car: If you’re traveling within Colombia, driving a car is a great way to explore the countryside and see beautiful scenery.

How to Get Around in Cali

Cali has a good public transport system, including buses and taxis. You can also rent a car or take a bike or scooter tour to explore the city. In the central area of Cali, it’s easy to walk between many popular attractions.

Where to Go Next from Cali: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the surrounding area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Popayán: This colonial city is located about two hours south of Cali and is known for its white-washed buildings and Easter Processions.
  • San Cipriano: This remote jungle village can only be reached by a unique rail cart system and has natural pools and waterfalls to explore.
  • Pance River Valley: Located just south of Cali, this valley offers beautiful scenery, hiking trails, and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Cali is a vibrant destination with a rich cultural scene, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of activities and attractions to keep visitors entertained. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now equipped with everything you need to plan a memorable trip to Cali, Colombia.
