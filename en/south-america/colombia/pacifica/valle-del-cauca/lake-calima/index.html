Introduction to Lake Calima

Lake Calima is a beautiful body of water located in the Valle del Cauca region of Colombia. This large lake is surrounded by lush green landscape and rolling hills, making it the perfect location for outdoor enthusiasts. The area features a variety of activities, from hiking and biking to water sports and fishing. For travelers looking to experience an authentic Colombian vacation, Lake Calima is an ideal destination.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Calima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Historical Places, and Festivals

  • Museo Arqueológico de Calima: This museum features exhibits that showcase the history of the indigenous Calima culture that once inhabited the area. It’s a great place to learn more about the local culture and history.
  • Chontaduro Festival: This festival is held in March or April, and it celebrates the local fruit known as the “chontaduro.” Visitors can enjoy delicious food and music while learning about the local culture.
  • Basilica de Nuestra Señora de las Mercedes: This beautiful church is a must-visit attraction in the area. It’s a great place to reflect and soak up the peaceful atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Lake Calima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gallineral National Park: This nature reserve is a great location for a relaxing picnic or a scenic hike. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the area and spot native wildlife like monkeys and birds.
  • Cascada del Amor: This waterfall is a hidden gem in Lake Calima. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing swim in the crystal-clear waters of the waterfall and experience the natural beauty of the area.
  • Bosques de la Niebla Reserve: This reserve is known for its unique flora and fauna. Visitors can hike through the dense forests and spot a variety of bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Calima

  • El Penon de Calima: This is a popular hiking destination that offers stunning views of Lake Calima. The trail is challenging, and it takes around 2 hours to complete.
  • Cerro de la Teta: This is another popular hiking destination that offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape. It takes around 2 hours to complete the trail.
  • Biking in the countryside: The rolling hills and scenic landscape make Lake Calima a perfect destination for biking enthusiasts. There are plenty of tracks and trails that cyclists can explore.

Best Things to Do in Lake Calima: Activities and Tours

  • Windsurfing and Kiteboarding: Lake Calima is known for its favorable conditions for wind-based water sports like windsurfing and kiteboarding. Visitors can rent equipment and take lessons from experienced instructors.
  • Fishing: Lake Calima is a great destination for fishing enthusiasts. The lake is known for its abundance of trout, and visitors can rent equipment or hire a guide to show them the best spots.
  • Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of the lake and enjoy the scenic beauty of the area. There are also guided tours of local attractions and cultural landmarks available.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Calima

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sancocho: This is a traditional Colombian soup that is made with meat, potatoes, and plantains. It’s a hearty and delicious dish that visitors must try.
  • Ceviche: This is a popular dish made with raw fish marinated in lime juice. It’s a refreshing and light dish that is perfect for a hot day.
  • Chontaduro: This local fruit is a popular snack in the area. It’s best enjoyed with salt and lime.

Local Beverages

  • Aguardiente: This is a traditional Colombian liquor that is made with anise and sugar. It’s a popular drink that visitors must try.
  • Fresh Fruit Juice: Colombia is known for its wide variety of fresh fruit, and visitors can enjoy a refreshing glass of fruit juice almost anywhere in the area.

Where to Stay in Lake Calima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Tocarema: This is a luxurious hotel located on the shores of Lake Calima.
  • Hostal Carambolo: This is a budget-friendly option for travelers looking for a comfortable stay in the area.
  • Guesthouse Los Cocos: This guesthouse is known for its peaceful and scenic location, making it a popular choice for nature enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Lake Calima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crafts: There are plenty of handmade crafts available for purchase in the area, including traditional pottery and woven textiles.
  • Coffee: Colombia is known for its high-quality coffee, and visitors can purchase locally sourced coffee beans to bring home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Calima

The local culture in Lake Calima is heavily influenced by its indigenous people, the Calima. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, and learn about the history of the area before arriving.

Best Time to Visit Lake Calima

The best time to visit Lake Calima is during the dry season from December to March. This is when the weather is warm and dry, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Calima

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Travel alone in remote areas after dark.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Calima

Lake Calima is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take precautions like avoiding remote areas after dark and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Lake Calima

There are no major health concerns in Lake Calima. Visitors should drink bottled water and be cautious when trying new foods to avoid stomach issues.

Getting to Lake Calima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Calima

The nearest airport to Lake Calima is Alfonso Bonilla Aragón International Airport in Cali, which is around 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Lake Calima

The bus terminal in Cali is the closest major bus station to Lake Calima, and it is around 2 hours away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private transfer to get to Lake Calima.

How to Get Around in Lake Calima

Visitors can get around Lake Calima by renting a car, taking a taxi or shuttle, or hiring a private transfer. Bikes and boats are also available for rent for those looking to explore the area in a more unique way.

Where to Go Next from Lake Calima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cali: This vibrant city is known for its salsa dancing and lively atmosphere.
  • San Cipriano: This small village is known for its unique transportation method - a hand-cranked railway.
  • Buga: This colonial town is home to the Basilica del Señor de los Milagros, a popular pilgrimage site.

Conclusion

Lake Calima is a beautiful destination in Colombia that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. From hiking and biking to fishing and boat tours, there is something for everyone in this stunning area. Visitors can also experience the local culture and enjoy the delicious food and drink scene. Overall, Lake Calima is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Colombian vacation.
