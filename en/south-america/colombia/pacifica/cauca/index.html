Introduction to Cauca

Located in Pacifica, Colombia, Cauca is a beautiful region rich in cultural heritage and natural wonders. Visitors to this area will be able to explore everything from historical landmarks to breathtaking views of the Andes Mountains. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a history buff, Cauca has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cauca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cauca is home to a diverse range of cultural sites, including museums, galleries, and historical landmarks. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Popayan: Known as the “White City”, Popayan is home to a well-preserved colonial architecture. Visitors can explore the city’s beautiful churches, museums, and plazas.

  • The Museum of Natural Sciences: This museum is dedicated to the preservation and study of Cauca’s natural wonders. Visitors can see a wide range of exhibits that showcase the region’s biodiversity and geological history.

  • Torre del Reloj: This clock tower is located in the heart of Popayan’s city center. It’s one of the city’s most recognizable landmarks and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Cauca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cauca boasts some of Colombia’s most beautiful natural scenery, from rolling hills to mountainous landscapes. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and fishing. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Purace National Park: This expansive park is famous for its unique flora and fauna, including Andean bears, spectacled bears, and condors. The park is also home to active volcanoes and hot springs.

  • Los Farallones de Cali National Park: This park is home to some of the best hiking trails in Cauca. Visitors can explore the park’s rugged terrain, stunning waterfalls, and diverse wildlife.

  • Coconuco Hot Springs: These natural hot springs are located in the heart of Cauca’s mountainous region. The warm waters provide a perfect place to relax and unwind after a long day of hiking or exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cauca

Cauca offers a range of challenging and scenic hiking and biking trails for adventurous visitors. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Puente del Humilladero Hiking Trail: This trail is located on the outskirts of Popayan and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is moderately challenging and takes visitors across the Rio Cauca.

  • The Alto de San Antonio Biking Trail: This trail is a popular destination for mountain bikers. The trail is challenging and takes visitors across rocky terrain and steep hills, but the stunning views of the Andes Mountains are well worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Cauca: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Cauca can enjoy a range of activities and tours to explore the region’s cultural and natural wonders. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Popayan Walking Tour: This tour takes visitors through the historic center of Popayan. Visitors will be able to explore the city’s museums, churches, and plazas while learning about the region’s history and culture.

  • Purace National Park Jeep Tour: This tour takes visitors through the rugged terrain of Purace National Park. Visitors will be able to see a range of wildlife, including Andean bears and condors, while enjoying stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cauca

Visitors looking to explore everything that Cauca has to offer can follow this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival in Popayan, explore the city’s historic center.

Day 2: Visit the Torre del Reloj clock tower, followed by a hike on the Puente del Humilladero trail.

Day 3: Take a walking tour of Popayan’s museums and galleries.

Day 4: Day trip to Purace National Park, including a jeep tour of the park’s unique landscapes.

Day 5: Visit the Museum of Natural Sciences in Popayan, followed by a relaxing afternoon at Coconuco Hot Springs.

Day 6: Explore the Los Farallones de Cali National Park on a guided hiking tour.

Day 7: Departure from Cauca.

Food and Drink Scene of Cauca

Must-Try Dishes

Cauca’s culinary scene is a mix of traditional and modern cuisine, with a focus on fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Ajiaco: A hearty soup made with chicken, potatoes, corn, and other vegetables.

  • Empanadas: Fried or baked dough stuffed with meat, vegetables, or cheese.

  • Cholado: A refreshing, sweet dessert made with shaved ice, fruit, and condensed milk.

Local Beverages

Cauca is known for its selection of fresh fruit juices, as well as its locally-brewed beer. Some of the best beverages to try include:

  • Lulada: A sweet, refreshing juice made with lulo fruit.

  • Chicha: A traditional fermented beverage made with corn or maize.

  • Cusqueña Beer: A locally-brewed beer with a light, refreshing flavor.

Where to Stay in Cauca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cauca offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hotel Dann Monasterio: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Popayan. The hotel features an on-site spa, restaurant, and bar.

  • Hostal Caracolí: A budget-friendly hostel located in the historic center of Popayan. The hostel offers private and shared rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge area.

  • Hostal Ciudad Blanca: A charming guesthouse located in the center of Popayan. The guesthouse features colorful, colonial-style decor and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the city.

What to Buy in Cauca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Cauca can find a range of locally-made products to bring home as souvenirs. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Handwoven textiles: Cauca is known for its beautiful, brightly-colored woven fabrics.

  • Coffee: The region is home to some of Colombia’s best coffee plantations.

  • Local crafts: Visitors can find a range of handcrafted products, including pottery, woodcarvings, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Cauca

Cauca is home to a diverse population, with a rich mix of indigenous and colonial cultures. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes and religious practices.

Best Time to Visit Cauca

The best time to visit Cauca is during the dry season, which runs from December to March. Visitors can expect sunny days and comfortable temperatures during this time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cauca

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and cultural landmarks.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take a guided tour of the natural parks to ensure safety and minimize environmental impact.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural landmarks.

  • Don’t try to hike or explore natural areas without a guide or proper gear.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or cultural traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cauca

Cauca is generally a safe region for visitors, with a low incidence of crime. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and not carrying valuables in plain sight.

Health in Cauca

Visitors to Cauca should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue and Zika. It’s also important to drink bottled or purified water and avoid eating street food that may be contaminated.

Getting to Cauca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cauca

The nearest airports to Cauca are the Alfonso Bonilla Aragon International Airport in Cali and the Guillermo Leon Valencia Airport in Popayan.

Bus Stations Near Cauca

Visitors can also reach Cauca via bus from major cities in Colombia, including Bogota and Medellin.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Cauca by car from nearby cities and towns. However, it’s important to note that roads in the region can be narrow and winding, so visitors should exercise caution when driving.

How to Get Around in Cauca

Visitors can travel around Cauca via public transportation, taxis, or by renting a car. However, visitors should be aware that roads can be narrow and winding in the region, so driving can be challenging for inexperienced drivers.

Where to Go Next from Cauca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Cauca can continue their Colombian adventure by visiting nearby destinations such as:

  • Cali: Known as the “Salsa Capital of the World”, Cali is famous for its vibrant nightlife and lively music scene.

  • San Agustin Archaeological Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to a collection of unique statues and carvings dating back to 3300 BCE.

Conclusion

With its abundant natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Cauca is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Colombia. Visitors can explore everything from historical landmarks to stunning natural parks, all while enjoying delicious local cuisine and warm hospitality.
