Introduction to Los Katios National Park

Los Katios National Park is a natural treasure in Pacifica, Colombia, located in the South American region. It is a biodiverse area that boasts an array of natural beauty. This 72,000-hectare park has over 400 bird species, along with exceptional vegetation. Apart from lush forests, the park contains more than twenty significant watercourses, including the Atrato River.

List of Places to Visit in Los Katios National Park

Here is a list of places to visit while in Los Katios National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Panamanian hat makers in the Montelíbano area
  • Indigenous communities like Embera
  • The ruins of the old city of Antioquia

Natural Attractions of Los Katios National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bird watching at the Atrato River trails
  • Kayaking through the park’s waterways
  • Horse riding into the enchanted forest of El Sarare
  • Hiking at Miramar to enjoy the view of the Atrato river basin
  • Jungle trekking to the La Cienaga lagoon

Hiking/Biking Routes of Los Katios National Park

Are you looking for the best hiking and biking routes? Look no further. We’ve got you covered:

  • Manurlango trail
  • Chamiblanco trail
  • San Jorge trail

These trails are perfect for both novice and expert hikers or bikers. You can enjoy hiking and biking through the vast forest of Los Katios National Park and appreciate the richness of flora and fauna that the park offers.

Best Things to Do in Los Katios National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Jungle tours in Cana Kaan Nature Reserve
  • Water activities at Sapo waterslide
  • Visit Mango Village, a local fruit producing community
  • Visit Carballo Island and the Bocas de Atrato
  • Visit the La Ciega lagoon

Week-Long Itinerary for Los Katios National Park

Day 1: Explore the park’s natural attractions, Miramar viewpoint, and Atrato River trails. Day 2: Enjoy jungle trekking at La Cienaga Lagoon. Day 3-4: Kayaking, Cana Kaan Nature Reserve, and visit indigenous communities. Day 5: Water activities at Sapo waterslide. Day 6: Visit Carballo Island and the Bocas de Atrato.
Day 7: Visit Mango Village.

Food and Drink Scene of Los Katios National Park

Colombia is a country with a rich culinary heritage, and Los Katios National Park is not an exception. The following dishes and drinks are a must-try when in the park:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arepa, a cornmeal cake that goes perfectly with cheese or meat.
  • Ajiaco, a soup made of chicken, potatoes, and corn, typically served with avocado and capers.
  • Sancocho, a soup made of beef, chicken, or fish mixed with plantains and yucca.
  • Chicharrón, pork rinds popular throughout the country.

Local Beverages

  • Agua de Panela, a refreshing drink made from sugarcane.
  • Cerveza Aguila, a local beer.
  • Guaro, a sugarcane alcohol generally consumed with lime and salt.

Where to Stay in Los Katios National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The following are some of the most popular places to stay in Los Katios National Park:

  • Hotel Balcones del Mirador - a hotel located in the middle of the forest offering beautiful views and impeccable service.
  • El Morro Eco Adventure Hotel - located in the heart of the park with nature activities and excellent food.
  • Choco Camp Hostel - Affordable prices and hiking tours to the Montelaño forest reserve.

What to Buy in Los Katios National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for something to bring home from your journey to Los Katios National Park, there are many options to choose from. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy:

  • Typical handicrafts handmade by the Embera community.
  • Chocho chocolate, a typical chocolate made from wild cacao.
  • A Panama hat, made from fibers of a palm found in the park.

Local Culture and Customs of Los Katios National Park

Indigenous communities are present throughout the park, and they are an essential part of the area’s cultural heritage. Visitors to Los Katios National Park may encounter locals that have maintained their ancestral traditions of medicinal plant knowledge, music, and artisanal trades.

Best Time to Visit Los Katios National Park

The best months to visit Los Katios National Park are between December and April, the dry season, when the weather is most reliable. During the wet season, from May to November, dirt tracks of the park may be challenging to navigate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Los Katios National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s natural environment and its wildlife.
  • Visit the park with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Buy local souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave any trash behind.
  • Don’t swim in areas where it’s not allowed.
  • Don’t harm or capture any wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Los Katios National Park

Los Katios National Park is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s better to avoid visiting the park alone, particularly in isolated areas like the adjacent forests.

Health in Los Katios National Park

Visitors to Los Katios National Park must use anti-mosquito repellent before entering the park and use mosquito nets to avoid contracting diseases like yellow fever and dengue.

Getting to Los Katios National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Los Katios National Park can travel by air, bus, or taxi from different Colombian cities like Medellin, Quibdó, Cartagena, among others.

Airports Near Los Katios National Park

  • Medellin Airport
  • Quibdó Airport

Bus Stations Near Los Katios National Park

  • Medellin North Transportation Terminal
  • Monteria Terminal
  • Pereira Bus Terminal

Other Options

Visitors can also use private transportation by booking a taxi or getting a rental car.

How to Get Around in Los Katios National Park

The best way to get around Los Katios National Park is by foot. Visitors who want to explore the park’s horizontal dimension may rent a bike or a kayak or ride horses independently if they have previous experience.

Where to Go Next from Los Katios National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby places to visit after Los Katios National Park include:

  • Nuqui, a town with an exotic maritime culture.
  • El Valle, to explore biospheres and waterfalls.
  • Bahía Solano, a remote and rugged area with biodiversity and whale watching tours of migration.

Conclusion

Los Katios National Park is a natural reserve that features jungle treks, water adventures, ancient artifacts, and biodiverse habitats. This travel guide is a comprehensive guide that will provide useful information to make the most of your visit to the park.
