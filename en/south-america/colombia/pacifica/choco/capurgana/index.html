Introduction to Capurganá

Nestled in the natural splendor of Chocó, Capurganá is an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers a relaxing getaway in Colombia. This small town has pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and a vibrant culture that attracts tourists looking for an authentic and unique experience.

List of Places to Visit in Capurganá

If you are planning a trip to Capurganá, here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Capurganá: This museum displays the history and culture of the town, including pre-Columbian artifacts, the process of collecting gold and pearls, and the arrival of the first settlers.
  • San Francisco de Asis Church: This charming church is located in the heart of the town and has an impressive wooden altar piece.
  • Tienda de las Aguas: This colorful store sells medicinal plants, herbs, and traditional remedies.

Natural Attractions of Capurganá: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Cielo Waterfall: A short hike from the town will lead you to this picturesque waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Playa Soledad: This secluded and pristine beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Los Estoraques Natural Park: A UNESCO-recognized site that features incredible rock formations that resemble pillars.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Capurganá

Capurganá is a nature lover’s paradise with numerous hiking, biking, and walking trails. You can visit the main attractions on foot or rent a bike and explore the town and nearby areas at your own pace. Some popular routes include:

  • Capurganá beach to El Cielo waterfall: A 2-kilometer hike along the beach leads to this stunning waterfall.
  • Los Estoraques Natural Park: A 3-kilometer trail that takes you through the park’s incredible rock formations.
  • Punta Soldado Trail: A 6-kilometer round-trip hike to Punta Soldado beach, where you can enjoy some of the best views in Capurganá.

Best Things to Do in Capurganá: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and exploring the natural wonders, here are some other exciting things to do in Capurganá:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Capurganá is surrounded by crystal-clear waters and vibrant coral reefs that offer a perfect opportunity for snorkeling and diving.
  • Fishing: You can experience the traditional fishing practice of “tapon” on the Capurganá and Sapzurro beaches.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the nearby islands to explore the pristine beaches, natural pools, and hidden coves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Capurganá

If you are wondering how to make the most of your week in Capurganá, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Capurganá

Capurganá has a unique food scene that features fresh and local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz con coco: This coconut rice dish is a staple in the local cuisine and goes well with seafood.
  • Cazuela de mariscos: A seafood stew with coconut milk, vegetables, and spices.
  • Sancocho: A flavorful soup with meat, cassava, plantains, and other vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Coco loco: A refreshing cocktail made with coconut water, vodka, and lime.
  • Lulada: A fruit-based drink made with lulo, a sweet and tangy fruit that grows in the region.
  • Guarapo: Freshly squeezed sugarcane juice that is popular in Colombia.

Where to Stay in Capurganá: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Capurganá offers plenty of choices for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to cozy guesthouses and resorts. Some popular options include:

  • El Nido del Condor: A comfortable and eco-friendly lodge with ocean views and a tropical garden.
  • Posada del Cerro: A picturesque guesthouse that is perfect for couples and solo travelers.
  • Hotel Los Robles: A family-friendly hotel with a swimming pool and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Capurganá: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Capurganá has plenty of shops that sell locally-made handicrafts, souvenirs, and traditional herbal remedies. Here are some popular items to buy as a memento of your trip:

  • Handmade jewelry: You can find a variety of handmade jewelry made with local materials like coconut shells, seeds, and gold.
  • Fique bags: These traditional bags are woven by artisans and are often used for carrying groceries or beach supplies.
  • Cocoa butter: Capurganá is known for its high-quality cocoa butter, which is used for making chocolates and cosmetics.

Local Culture and Customs of Capurganá

Capurganá’s culture is heavily influenced by its Afro-Colombian and indigenous heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming, but it is important to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Capurganá

The best time to visit Capurganá is from December to March when the weather is dry, and the sea is calm. This is also the peak season, so expect higher prices and larger crowds. April to September is the rainy season, but you can still enjoy the town’s natural wonders if you don’t mind occasional downpours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Capurganá

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring cash, as there are limited ATMs.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.

Don’t:

  • Play loud music or disturb the wildlife.
  • Throw trash in the beach and natural areas.
  • Swim in areas not designated for swimmers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Capurganá

Capurganá is a safe destination, but it is always recommended to exercise caution and common sense, especially when traveling alone. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking in unlit areas at night.

Health in Capurganá

The town has a small health center, but for serious medical emergencies, it is recommended to go to a larger hospital in Turbo. Visitors are advised to bring insect repellent and sunscreen and to drink bottled water.

Getting to Capurganá: Transportation Options and Logistics

Capurganá is located on the border with Panama, and access is limited to boats and small planes. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Capurganá

  • Capurganá Airport: A small airport that offers flights from Medellin, Cartagena, and Bogota.

Bus Stations Near Capurganá

  • Turbo Bus Station: A large bus station that connects to major cities in Colombia.

Other Options

  • Boat: You can take a boat from Turbo or Necocli to Capurganá.
  • Private charter: You can hire a private plane or boat for a more exclusive experience.

How to Get Around in Capurganá

Capurganá is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, if you want to explore the area outside the town, you can rent a bike or hire a boat.

Where to Go Next from Capurganá: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Capurganá is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Sapzurro: A charming town that is located on the border with Panama and features stunning beaches and impressive cliffs.
  • La Miel: A natural pool located in a remote area of the jungle that can only be accessed by boat.
  • Nuqui: A beach town that offers a more rustic and adventurous experience than Capurganá.

Conclusion

Capurganá is a hidden gem in Colombia that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. Whether you want to relax on the beach, explore the rainforest, or learn about the local traditions, Capurganá has something for everyone. Plan your trip and get ready for an unforgettable experience!
