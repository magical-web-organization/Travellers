A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Chocó, Colombia

Chocó is a region located in the Pacifica department of Colombia, South America. With its beautiful scenery, rich culture, and diverse wildlife, Chocó is a top destination for travelers looking to experience the country’s natural beauty. From stunning beaches to towering mountains, there is no shortage of things to see and do in Chocó. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Chocó, Colombia.

List of Places to Visit in Chocó

Chocó offers a wealth of attractions for visitors. Here are some of the top places to visit in Chocó:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Gold Museum of Chocó - Owing to its rich mining history, Chocó is home to several gold museums. The Gold Museum of Chocó is one of the most prominent and has an impressive collection of pre-Columbian artifacts.

  2. The Quibdó Cathedral - Known as one of the most beautiful colonial churches in Colombia, it is located in the heart of Quibdó.

Natural Attractions of Chocó: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bahía Solano - For beach lovers, Bahía Solano is a must-visit destination. With crystal clear water and fine white sand, it is a paradise for those who prefer a more relaxed pace.

  2. Utría National Park - For adventure seekers, Utria National Park is the perfect destination. It’s home to a wide range of plants and animals, including monkeys, whales, and sea turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chocó

  1. The Pacifico Trail - At roughly 1,200 km (745 miles) long, the Pacifico Trail is a must-visit destination for hikers and bikers. It is the perfect route for those looking to explore Chocó’s diverse natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Chocó: Activities and Tours

  1. Whale Watching - From July to October every year, humpback whales arrive in the waters of the Colombian Pacific Ocean. A visit during this time is a must-see experience.

  2. Surfing - Chocó is home to some of the best surfing spots in Colombia, with some huge waves attracting surfers from around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chocó

Day 1: Explore Bahía Solano Day 2: Visit the Gold Museum of Chocó and the Quibdó Cathedral Day 3: Experience Utria National Park Day 4: Take a surfing lesson Day 5: Go whale watching Day 6: Take a bike ride on the Pacifico Trail Day 7: Relax on the beach

Food and Drink Scene of Chocó

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ceviche de Camarones - A tasty dish featuring shrimp served with lime juice, tomatoes, and onions.

  2. Chontaduro - This fruit, which has a sweet and sour flavor, is used in a wide range of dishes, including soups, salads, and drinks.

Local Beverages

  1. Chicha - A traditional Andean beverage made from boiled maize, sugar, and spices that is fermented and served cold.

  2. Refajo - A refreshing drink consisting of beer and Colombiana, a popular soda.

Where to Stay in Chocó: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Aldea del Mar - Located in Bahía Solano, the Hotel Aldea del Mar is a great option for those looking for a place to stay while exploring the beaches of Chocó.

  2. Tati Hostel - The Tati Hostel is a budget-friendly option in Quibdó. The hostel has a friendly atmosphere, and the staff is always happy to help guests plan their activities and excursions.

What to Buy in Chocó: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tagua Jewelry - Tagua, also known as vegetable ivory, is a sustainable and eco-friendly product that can be made into beautiful jewelry.

  2. Woven Baskets - Made by local artisans, woven baskets are a must-have souvenir from Chocó.

Local Culture and Customs of Chocó

The people of Chocó are known for their vibrant music, food, and dance traditions. The region is home to several Afro-Colombian communities whose unique cultural heritage can be seen in everything from the local cuisine to the traditional music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Chocó

The best time to visit Chocó is between June and November when the weather is dry and sunny, and wildlife sightings are at their peak. The whale-watching season is also during this time, making it an ideal time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chocó

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs

  2. Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  1. Wander off alone at night

  2. Take photos of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Chocó

Violent crime can be a concern in some parts of Chocó, so it is essential to stay vigilant when traveling in the region and to follow basic safety precautions.

Health in Chocó

Visitors to Chocó should be up to date on their vaccinations. They should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses as well as food and waterborne illnesses.

Getting to Chocó: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chocó

  1. José Celestino Mutis Airport in Bahía Solano

Bus Stations Near Chocó

  1. The bus terminal in Quibdó

Other Options

Travelers may also choose to travel via boat or private vehicle.

How to Get Around in Chocó

Transportation in Chocó can be difficult, so travelers should prepare to use a mix of taxis, buses, and boats to get around the region.

Where to Go Next from Chocó: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  2. Cali

Conclusion

Colombia is fast becoming a top travel destination for adventurous travelers, and Chocó is no exception. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and exciting outdoor adventures, Chocó is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience South America’s rugged and spectacular landscapes. With this comprehensive travel guide, travelers can be well-prepared to make the most of their trip to Chocó, Colombia.
