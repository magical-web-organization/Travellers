Introduction to Pasto

Situated in Nariño, in Pacifica, in Colombia, in South America, Pasto is a beautiful city nestled in the Andes Mountains and renowned for its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. It is also famous for hosting the Carnaval de Negros y Blancos (the Blacks and Whites Carnival), one of the largest and most colorful carnivals in the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, best places to stay, what to eat, and much more in Pasto, Colombia.

List of Places to Visit in Pasto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pasto boasts an impressive array of museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the city’s rich cultural heritage. A few must-visit museums include:

  1. Museum of Modern Art Juan José Mayorga
  2. Museum of Natural Sciences
  3. Museo del Oro de Nariño - showcasing indigenous jewelry and other gold artefacts

Historical places worth visiting include:

  1. San Juan de la Cruz Church
  2. Catedral de Nuestra Señora del Rosario - an impressive church that dominates the town square
  3. Palacio Episcopal - the Archbishop’s residency
  4. La Cocha Lagoon - a beautiful lagoon that was declared a natural heritage site.

Natural Attractions of Pasto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an adventure enthusiast, Pasto has a lot to offer. Consider the following experiences:

  1. Volcano Galeras - a must-visit site for hikers and adventure lovers.
  2. Azufral Natural Reserve - a beautiful reserve situated 4,000 meters above sea level with amazing scenic views.
  3. Buesaco Waterfalls - a fantastic sight for nature lovers and adventure seekers who want to hike and admire the surrounding beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pasto

If you enjoy hiking and biking, Pasto has plenty of trails and routes that you can explore. Here are a few that you should consider:

  1. Cocha Waterfall Hike - a day-long hike that will take you through beautiful scenery.
  2. Ecoparque Los Cisnes - a biking trail that leads through the beautiful Cauca River Canyon taking you through caves and underground tunnels.
  3. The route to the Chiles Valley - an 8-hour hike through the beautiful Andean landscape, giving you stunning scenic views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Pasto: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are many other activities you can engage in while in Pasto. Some of them include:

  1. Attend the famous Carnaval de Negros y Blancos - held annually from January 2nd to January 7th, the Carnaval de Negros y Blancos is a festival full of culture, music, and colors;
  2. Walking tour of Pasto - a guided walking tour of the city’s historic sites is a great way to learn more about Pasto’s rich military history.
  3. Take a hot air balloon ride over Pasto - Experience stunning aerial views as you soar over the beautiful Andean range and the city below.

Food and Drink Scene of Pasto

Must-Try Dishes

Pasto is known for its delicious fusion cuisine. Here are a few dishes that are a must-try:

  1. Cuy Asado - roasted and seasoned whole guinea pig.
  2. Hornado Pastuso - oven-roasted pork that is both juicy and flavorful.
  3. Chicha - a cool fermented drink made of maize.

Local Beverages

Apart from the local drinks like chicha, Pasto has fantastic bars and bottle shops where you can enjoy different cocktails and spirits. A must-visit spots include:

  1. Pub La Cantera
  2. La Antigua Estación Gastro Bar
  3. Las Vegas

Where to Stay in Pasto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pasto offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Centro Internacional de Convenciones Casa de la Moneda – a luxurious hotel situated near the city’s historical sites.
  2. Hotel Boulevard Plaza – a modern and comfortable hotel situated near the city center.
  3. Azahar Hostel – a budget-friendly hostel that offers private and dormitory-style accommodation.

What to Buy in Pasto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The city is renowned for its vibrant marketplaces, allowing you to stock up on beautiful souvenirs and local produce. Here are a few things to bring home from Pasto:

  1. Handcrafted woven baskets - look out for brightly colored and intricately woven baskets handmade by indigenous artisans.
  2. Carnaval De Negros y Blancos mementos - buy memorabilia from the festival to remember the trip
  3. Achira pasteles and Carnijaque - these are traditional Pasto pastries and candy.

Local Culture and Customs of Pasto

The people of Pasto are warm and welcoming, and you’ll get to experience their culture as you interact with them. Learning some basic Spanish phrases and learning the local customs is a sign of respect and a great way to show your interest in the culture.

Best Time to Visit Pasto

The best time to visit Pasto is between December and March. This is the Carnival season when the city comes to life with music, colors, and excitement. It is also a great time to enjoy the city’s vibrant culture and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pasto

Do:

  1. Learn a few Spanish phrases
  2. Bring warm clothing - the weather can be quite cold at night.
  3. Attend the Carnival – the highlight of the city’s cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  1. Avoid wearing revealing clothing inside churches
  2. Do not take photographs of people without their permission.
  3. Don’t leave any of your belongings unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pasto

Pasto is a relatively safe city, but it’s always better to take precaution. Always be aware of your surroundings, avoid carrying large amounts of cash or jewelry, and keep your belongings close to you. It’s also important to stay away from any protests or political riots that may occur.

Health in Pasto

It’s vital to have travel insurance when visiting Pasto/Juanco. Be cautious when drinking tap water, avoid street food, and always maintain good hygiene practices, especially when handling food.

Getting to Pasto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pasto

The Antonio Nariño Airport is the main airport in Pasto and is situated around 30 kilometers from the city. Most of the hotels in the city offer shuttle services for airport transfers.

Bus Stations Near Pasto

The bus station, known as “The Terminal,” offers intercity bus services to different cities in Colombia. It’s a 20-minute drive from the city center.

Other Options

Driving from other cities is a possibility too. Just take the Pan-American highway down south.

How to Get Around in Pasto

Getting around in Pasto is easy since there are plenty of taxis and buses available. You can also hire a bike or car and explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Pasto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time on your hands, you can take a day trip to the following nearby destinations:

  1. Popayán - a city renowned for its beautiful white-washed colonial-style buildings.
  2. San Juan de Pasto - a beautiful neighboring city with a rich cultural heritage and scenic views.

Conclusion

Pasto offers an excellent opportunity for travelers to experience a unique and unforgettable vacation. With stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and exciting outdoor activities, Pasto is a must-visit destination in Colombia. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with the necessary information needed to plan your next visit to Pasto.
