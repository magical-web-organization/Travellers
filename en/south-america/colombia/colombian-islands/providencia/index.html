Introduction to Providencia

Providencia is a hidden gem within the Colombian Islands located in the Caribbean sea. It is well known for being a tranquil and exotic setting perfect for a romantic break or a family vacation. Providencia offers a mix of Colombian and Caribbean culture, an intricate natural landscape, turquoise water, coral reefs, and culinary experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Providencia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museums and historical places are a great way to discover and learn about the rich history and culture of Providencia. Here are some must-visit places:

  • The Old Providence McBean Lagoon National Park - A marine protected area that is a paradise for divers and snorkelers.
  • The Morgan’s Head - A rock formation that got its name from the infamous privateer Sir Henry Morgan who used Providencia as a base to raid the Spanish treasures ships passing through.
  • Santa Isabel de La Providencia - A colonial town that is now the center of the island’s government and commerce.

Natural Attractions of Providencia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Providencia is a breathtaking place full of outdoor activities that take visitors on a journey into nature. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions:

  • El Pico Peak - This is the highest point in Providencia. Visitors can hike to the summit for a stunning panoramic view of the island.
  • Cayo Cangrejo - Perfect for snorkeling, this tiny islet is surrounded by coral reefs.
  • South West Bay Beach - This picturesque and secluded beach features white sand and crystal-clear water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Providencia

Hiking and biking routes are a great way to explore the island and get a feel for its natural beauty. These are some of the best routes:

  • The Peak Trail - A challenging hike that takes you to the top of El Pico Peak for a breathtaking view of the island.
  • The Three Bays Trail - This trail takes visitors through forested areas, beaches and rocky coves.
  • The Indian Point Trail - A scenic route through cliffs and outcroppings which ends with an unparalleled view of Morgan’s Head.

Best Things to Do in Providencia: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for relaxation or adventure, Providencia has you covered. These are some popular activities to consider:

  • Diving and Snorkeling - You can scuba dive or snorkel to explore the beautiful coral reefs that surround the island.
  • Kayaking - Paddle out in a kayak and explore the secluded beaches and coves around the island.
  • Fishing - Providencia is well known for its deep-sea fishing and offers a variety of fishing tours.
  • Beach Hopping - Some of Providencia’s beaches are a bit out of the way, which means uncrowded, white-sand beaches with crystal-clear water.

Providencia offers enough activities and sites to keep you occupied for a week or more. This itinerary offers a glimpse of what one might do:

Food and Drink Scene of Providencia

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine of Providencia is a fusion of Colombian and Caribbean flavors. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Rondon - A stew made with fish, plantain, yams, and coconut milk. This is a signature dish of Providencia.
  • Conch Fritters - A tasty appetizer made with conch, flour, and seasoning.
  • Sancocho - A traditional Colombian soup made with a variety of meats, plantain, yucca, potato, and corn.

Local Beverages

  • Coco Loco - A rum-based cocktail that features fresh coconut milk.
  • Guarapo - A sugarcane based drink with a refreshing taste.
  • Mango Juice - Made with juice from fresh mangoes which is a common fruit found in Providencia.

Where to Stay in Providencia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Providencia to fit any budget:

  • Hotel Deep Blue - This luxury hotel features an infinity pool and offers beautiful ocean views.
  • Posada Enilda - A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the center of town.
  • Miss Elma Lodging House - This hostel is located right on the beach and offers beautiful sunsets.

What to Buy in Providencia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are a few local products that are worth taking home with you:

  • Molas - Handmade, brightly colored textiles made by the indigenous people of the San Blas Archipelago.
  • Coffee – is Colombia’s most famous export, so it would only be fair to bring some back as a souvenir.
  • Coconut Candy - Locally made, these small sweets are made with molasses and coconut and are deliciously addictive!

Local Culture and Customs of Providencia

The people of Providencia are known for their hospitality, friendliness, and unique language, Creole English. Clothing is generally casual, and visitors should dress modestly when entering a church.

Best Time to Visit Providencia

The best time to visit Providencia is from December to April when the climate is dry and visitors can enjoy activities like snorkeling and scuba diving.

Do’s and Don’ts of Providencia

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish. Though English and Creole English are commonly spoken, it is always a nice gesture to make an effort to learn the local language.
  • Support local businesses. Purchase souvenirs and goods from local vendors whenever possible.
  • Carry cash. Credit cards are not widely accepted on the island.

Don’t:

  • Litter. Take care to dispose of any trash or belongings responsibly and keep the island as pristine as possible.
  • Underestimate the strength of the sun. Providencia is in the tropics, and the sun can be very strong. Always wear sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Providencia

Providencia is a very safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take the same precautions they would in any foreign country, such as not leaving valuables unattended or walking alone at night.

Health in Providencia

Mosquitoes can be a concern, so it’s recommended that visitors take precautionary measures, such as using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved shirts and pants when outdoors.

Getting to Providencia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Providencia

The closest airport to Providencia is the Gustavo Rojas Pinilla International Airport on San Andres Island, where flights from Bogota and Medellin arrive and depart daily.

Bus Stations Near Providencia

There is no road connection to Providencia, so visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Santa Catalina, followed by a ferry to Providencia.

Other Options

There are several daily flights and ferries that operate between San Andres and Providencia, and these are the most convenient options for most visitors. Visitors can also hire a private boat if they want a more personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Providencia

Renting a bike or a golf cart is the most popular way to get around Providencia. Taxis are also available and offer a great way to explore the island with a knowledgeable local.

Where to Go Next from Providencia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Many visitors choose to continue their Colombian journey in the nearby city of San Andres, another exceptional Caribbean island with a robust tourism industry.

Conclusion

Providencia is a fantastic destination for those looking for a laid-back vacation in a beautiful, exotic location. With its unique culture, fascinating history, and stunning natural beauty, there’s something here for everyone.
