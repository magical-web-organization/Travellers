Introduction to Colombian Islands

Colombian Islands, located off the Caribbean coast of Colombia, offer a perfect combination of sun, sand, and sea. The islands are known for their crystal-clear waters, colorful coral reefs, and pristine beaches. These beautiful islands have something for everyone, including outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and delicious cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colombian Islands

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring Colombian Islands:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit San Felipe Castle and explore Cartagena’s Old Town
Day 2 Visit Bolivar Square and the Gold Museum of Zenu
Day 3 Take a boat trip to Rosario Islands National Park and explore the island
Day 4 Hike through La Cienaga Grande and visit a traditional village in the area
Day 5 Explore Tayrona National Natural Park and relax on the beach
Day 6 Take a city tour of Santa Marta, and visit the Los Nevados National Park
Day 7 Visit the Lost City of Teyuna and return to the mainland

Local Culture and Customs of Colombian Islands

The people of Colombian Islands are warm, welcoming and proud of their unique culture. Visitors to the Colombian Islands are expected to behave respectfully towards local customs, and it’s important to learn about local customs before your visit.

Best Time to Visit Colombian Islands

The best time to visit Colombian Islands is between December and March when the weather is sunny, and there is little chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colombian Islands

Do:

  • Learn a few words in Spanish
  • Embrace the local culture
  • Be respectful towards local customs

Don’t:

  • Overpack
  • Take unnecessary risks
  • Forget to tip your service providers

Safety and Health

Safety in Colombian Islands

Tourists visiting Colombian Islands should take standard precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding walking alone at night and protecting valuables.

Health in Colombian Islands

Tourists are advised to take appropriate measures to prevent mosquito bites, such as using DEET-based repellents and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Colombian Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colombian Islands

  • Rafael Núñez International Airport in Cartagena
  • Simón Bolívar International Airport in Santa Marta

Bus Stations Near Colombian Islands

  • Terminal de Transporte Cartagena
  • Terminal de Transporte Santa Marta

Other Options

Ferries and speedboats are also available for travel between the islands and mainland.

How to Get Around in Colombian Islands

The easiest way to get around Colombian Islands is by taking a taxi, renting a car, hopping on a bus, or bicycles.

Conclusion

The Colombian Islands offer an idyllic escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its natural beauty, unique culture, and plenty of activities to enjoy, the Colombian Islands should be at the top of every traveler’s bucket list.
