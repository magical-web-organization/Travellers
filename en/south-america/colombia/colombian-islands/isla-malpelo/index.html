Introduction to Isla Malpelo

Isla Malpelo, located off the Pacific coast of Colombia, is a small volcanic island that is part of the Colombian Islands. This remote island, which is only accessible by boat, is a hidden gem in Colombia that boasts a unique ecosystem and a rich marine biodiversity. Isla Malpelo is a popular destination for divers and nature lovers, thanks to its underwater volcanic formations, marine wildlife, and stunning beaches.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Isla Malpelo may not have museums or historical sites, but it is rich in cultural diversity, which you can experience through its local cuisine, music, and dances. Make sure to mingle with locals and try the traditional dishes, such as ceviche and fried fish, and to enjoy the local music, such as Currulao, a rhythmic folk music that originated in the Pacific coast of Colombia.

Natural Attractions of Isla Malpelo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Isla Malpelo is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions of Isla Malpelo:

  • Malpelo Fauna and Flora Sanctuary: This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to a diverse range of marine animals and plants. You can take a boat tour or go snorkeling or diving to get up close and personal with the marine wildlife, such as sharks, dolphins, and sea turtles.
  • Punta Soldado Beach: This secluded beach is a great spot for sunbathing and swimming in crystal-clear waters. You can also hike along the nearby cliffs for stunning ocean views.
  • Volcanic formations: Isla Malpelo is a volcanic island and boasts impressive formations, such as the famous La Tortuga rock formation. You can explore the island’s volcanic landscapes by hiking or biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isla Malpelo

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love the hiking and biking trails of Isla Malpelo. Here are some popular routes to explore the island’s natural beauty:

  • La Tortuga hike: This scenic hike takes you to the top of the famous La Tortuga rock formation, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the island and the ocean.
  • Parque Natural hike: This challenging hike takes you through the island’s natural park and offers stunning views of the island’s flora and fauna.
  • Biking trail: You can rent a bike and explore the island’s scenic routes, such as the road that leads to Punta Soldado beach.

Best Things to Do in Isla Malpelo: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Isla Malpelo, including:

  • Diving: Isla Malpelo is one of the best diving destinations in the world, thanks to its rich marine biodiversity and unique volcanic formations.
  • Snorkeling: If you’re not a certified diver, you can still enjoy the island’s marine wildlife by snorkeling in its crystal-clear waters.
  • Boat tours: You can take a boat tour around the island to explore its natural beauty and marine wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Isla Malpelo

Isla Malpelo is not only rich in natural beauty but also in delicious cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: This traditional dish is made with fresh fish, lime juice, onions, and cilantro. It’s a refreshing and flavorful dish that is perfect for a hot day.
  • Fried fish: Isla Malpelo is known for its fresh fish, which can be fried to perfection and served with a side of coconut rice.
  • Arroz con coco: This side dish is made with coconut milk, rice, and onions, and is a popular accompaniment to the island’s seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Lulo juice: Lulo is a tropical fruit that is used to make a refreshing and sweet juice that is perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot day.
  • Cerveza Aguila: This Colombian beer is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.
  • Ron Viejo de Caldas: This traditional Colombian rum is a great way to celebrate your trip to Isla Malpelo.

Where to Stay in Isla Malpelo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isla Malpelo may be a remote island, but it still offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some popular places to stay on the island:

  • El Remanso: This eco-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves fresh seafood and traditional dishes. It’s located in the heart of the town, with easy access to the island’s attractions.
  • Hostal La Casa de Rubi: This cozy hostel offers private and shared rooms and a communal kitchen. It’s located near the beach, and it’s a great option for budget travelers.
  • Casa Turista: This guesthouse offers stunning ocean views, comfortable rooms, and a terrace where you can relax and enjoy the sunset.

Local Culture and Customs of Isla Malpelo

Isla Malpelo is a small island with a rich cultural heritage and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the locals: Isla Malpelo is a small community, and it’s important to be respectful of the locals and their customs.
  • Try the local cuisine: Isla Malpelo’s cuisine is a reflection of its cultural diversity, so make sure to try the traditional dishes and local beverages.
  • Learn some Spanish: While many locals speak some English, it’s always a good idea to learn some Spanish to communicate with them better.

Best Time to Visit Isla Malpelo

The best time to visit Isla Malpelo is from December to March when the weather is dry, and the sea is calm. However, keep in mind that these are also the busiest months, and accommodation and activities can be booked out quickly. If you want to avoid the crowds, consider visiting Isla Malpelo during the shoulder season, from April to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isla Malpelo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Isla Malpelo:

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen and insect repellent: Isla Malpelo is a hot and humid destination, and sunscreen and insect repellent are essential to protect your skin and avoid mosquito bites.
  • Do respect the natural environment: Isla Malpelo is a protected area, and it’s important to respect the natural environment and avoid littering or damaging the reef.
  • Do bring cash: Isla Malpelo is a remote island, and credit cards are not widely accepted, so make sure to bring cash with you.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch the marine wildlife: Isla Malpelo is home to a diverse range of marine animals, and it’s important to avoid touching or harassing them to avoid damaging their habitat.
  • Don’t wander off alone at night: Isla Malpelo is a safe destination, but it’s always a good idea to avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.
  • Don’t forget to take your trash with you: Isla Malpelo is a pristine destination, and it’s important to take your trash with you and dispose of it responsibly.

Safety and Health

Isla Malpelo is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to take some precautions to avoid any risks.

Safety in Isla Malpelo

  • Avoid walking alone at night: Isla Malpelo is generally a safe destination, but it’s always a good idea to avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.
  • Follow the instructions of your guide: If you’re going out on a boat tour or a diving excursion, make sure to follow the instructions of your guide to avoid any accidents.

Health in Isla Malpelo

  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites: Isla Malpelo is a tropical destination, and mosquito-borne diseases such as Dengue and Zika are common in the area. Make sure to bring insect repellent, wear long sleeves and pants, and use mosquito nets when sleeping.
  • Stay hydrated: Isla Malpelo is a humid destination, and it’s important to stay hydrated to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Isla Malpelo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Isla Malpelo is a remote island that is only accessible by boat. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider when planning your trip:

  • Fly to Bogotá or Cali: Most international airlines fly to Bogotá or Cali, which are the main airports in Colombia.
  • Take a domestic flight to Bahía Solano: From Bogotá or Cali, you can take a domestic flight to Bahía Solano, which is the closest town to Isla Malpelo.
  • Take a boat from Bahía Solano: From Bahía Solano, you can take a boat to Isla Malpelo. The boat ride takes around 10 hours, and it’s important to book your transportation in advance.

Airports Near Isla Malpelo

  • Bahía Solano Airport: This is the closest airport to Isla Malpelo, and it’s serviced by local airlines from Bogotá and Cali.
