Introduction to Charity

Charity, located in the Pomeroon-Supenaam region of Guyana, is a charming town with a rich cultural history. The town is known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and friendly locals. In this guide, we will provide all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Charity.

List of Places to Visit in Charity

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Charity Heritage Museum: This museum explores the history of the region and its rich cultural heritage. Learn about the history of the Arawak and Carib tribes and the arrival of the Dutch in the 16th century.

  • Mora Point Nature Park: This nature park is a beautiful attraction that has a historical significance. Visitors can explore the ruins of a fort that was built by the Dutch in the 17th century for the protection of their interests in the region.

  • The Sancho’s Heritage House: This historic house is a valuable testament to Charity’s past. It is over 300 years old and was built by the Dutch in the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Charity: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Channa Nature Trail: Discover the flora and fauna of the region as you hike through the tropical rainforest of the Channa Nature Trail.

  • Essequibo River: Go on a boat ride on the Essequibo River and enjoy the breathtaking views of the rainforest and the surrounding wildlife.

  • Adventure Sports: Try your hand at kayaking, canoeing, and paddleboarding on the Essequibo River, or go on a thrilling zip-lining adventure through the rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charity

There are plenty of hiking and biking trails in and around Charity. The Channa Nature Trail, mentioned earlier, provides visitors with an excellent opportunity to explore the rainforest.

Best Things to Do in Charity: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Local Villages: Take a tour of the nearby indigenous communities to learn about the local culture and traditional practices.

  • Fishing Trips: The Pomeroon River offers some of the best fishing spots in the region. Go on a fishing trip and catch some of the iconic fish species that can be found in the river.

  • Bird Watching: With over 400 species of birds found in the region, bird watching is a popular activity amongst visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Charity

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roti: A popular Indian dish made with flatbread and a variety of fillings like curried vegetables, chicken or fish.

  • Metagee: A vegan stew made with vegetables, cassava, and coconut milk.

  • Pepperpot: A spicy stew made with beef or wild meat, traditionally cooked over a fire for several hours for added flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Mauby: A refreshing drink made from the bark of the Mauby tree, mixed with spices like cinnamon and nutmeg.

  • Pineapple Juice: Freshly squeezed juice made from the tropical pineapples grown in the region.

  • Rum: Guyana is famous for its rum, and the local distilleries produce some of the best in the world.

Where to Stay in Charity: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Charity, ranging from luxury hotels to backpacker hostels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Kamlar Bed & Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse with a restaurant and bar, situated close to the major attractions.

  • Hacienda Horizonte: A luxury hotel with a spa and outdoor pool, set in the heart of the rainforest.

  • Karata Eco Retreat: This eco-friendly resort offers accommodation in rustic huts with stunning views of the forest and river.

What to Buy in Charity: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crafts: Visit the local craft market and pick up some handmade souvenirs like pottery, wood carvings, and woven baskets.

  • Spices: The region is known for its exotic spices, which are used in traditional dishes and sold in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Charity

The locals in Charity are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature. The town has a unique cultural heritage, influenced by the indigenous tribes and the Dutch settlers. It is important to respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting the indigenous communities.

Best Time to Visit Charity

The best time to visit Charity is between December and April, when the weather is dry and sunny, and the river is calm for water activities. The rainy season, which lasts from May to November, can make some outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charity

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional dishes.

  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the region’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting the indigenous communities.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charity

Charity is considered a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should be cautious when exploring the remote areas and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Charity

It is essential to take necessary precautions while traveling to South America. Visitors should ensure that they have received all the required vaccinations before arriving at Charity. It is advisable to carry mosquito repellent and cover exposed skin to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Charity: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Charity

The closest airport to Charity is the Cheddi Jagan International Airport, located approximately 120 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Charity

Buses from Georgetown, the capital of Guyana, run regularly to Charity. The bus journey takes around three hours, and buses are a cost-effective option for those traveling on a budget.

Other Options

Another popular option for visitors is to hire a car and drive to Charity. The drive takes roughly three hours from Georgetown, and visitors can enjoy the stunning scenery along the way.

How to Get Around in Charity

The best way to explore Charity is by foot, as the town is small and walkable. Taxis are also available for hire, and visitors can negotiate the price before they start their journey.

Where to Go Next from Charity: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Georgetown: The capital city of Guyana offers a contrasting experience, with a bustling city atmosphere, historical landmarks, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Kaieteur Falls: One of the largest single-drop waterfalls in the world, Kaieteur Falls is an awe-inspiring natural wonder that is worth the visit.

Conclusion

Charity is a hidden gem in South America, with a unique cultural heritage and beautiful natural scenery. We hope this guide has given you all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to this charming town. Remember to respect the local customs and traditions, try the delicious cuisine, and immerse yourself in the local culture for an authentic experience.
