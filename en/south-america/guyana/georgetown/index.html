Georgetown is the capital city of Guyana, a country in South America. It is known for its colonial architecture, vibrant culture, and incredible natural landscapes. From exploring museums and historical landmarks to basking in the beauty of the outdoors, Georgetown offers a wide range of activities for all types of travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the best things to do, places to stay, and local customs to follow in Georgetown.

Introduction to Georgetown

Georgetown is a city that is rich in culture and history. It was founded in the 18th century and its architecture still reflects the Dutch, British, and French influences that dominated the city’s early days. Today, Georgetown is a bustling city with a population of over 200,000 people. It is located on the north coast of Guyana and is a popular starting point for visitors who want to explore the country’s tropical rainforests and wide-open savannas.

List of Places to Visit in Georgetown

Georgetown offers a wide range of attractions, from cultural landmarks to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. George’s Cathedral: The tallest wooden building in the world, this cathedral is considered to be one of the city’s most iconic landmarks.
  • Guyana National Museum: This museum features exhibits that showcase the country’s fascinating history and culture.
  • Walter Roth Museum of Anthropology: Home to over 17,000 artifacts and specimens, this museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in anthropology and indigenous culture.
  • State House: Formerly the official residence of the Governor-General of British Guiana, this 19th-century building is now used as the residence of the President of Guyana.
  • The Parliament Building: This elegant building, located in the heart of the city, is where Guyana’s government meets to discuss important issues and make decisions.

Natural Attractions of Georgetown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Georgetown Seawall: This 4.5-mile-long sea defense wall is a popular spot for strolling, cycling, and enjoying the sunset.
  • Botanical Gardens: With over 150 species of plants and trees, this botanical garden is a great place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.
  • Kaieteur Falls: This stunning waterfall is one of the tallest single-drop waterfalls in the world and is located deep in the heart of Guyana’s rainforest.
  • Iwokrama Rainforest Reserve: This jungle reserve is a paradise for those who love hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Georgetown

Georgetown offers a number of hiking and biking routes for those who want to explore the city and its surroundings on foot or on two wheels. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • The Canopy Walkway: This suspension bridge is located deep in the heart of the Iwokrama Rainforest Reserve and offers visitors a unique perspective on the forest canopy.
  • The Rupununi Savannah: This vast savannah is home to a wide range of wildlife, including jaguars, giant otters, and anacondas.
  • The Demerara Harbour Bridge: This bridge, which spans the Demerara River, is a popular spot for cycling and jogging.

Best Things to Do in Georgetown: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Georgetown, from exploring museums to trying local cuisine. Here are some of the top activities and tours to enjoy in the city:

  • Food Tour: Discover the flavors of Georgetown with a food tour that will take you to the city’s best markets, restaurants, and street food vendors.
  • Shopping: Whether you want to buy souvenirs or local crafts, Georgetown has plenty of markets and shops to explore.
  • City Tour: Explore the city’s highlights with a guided tour that covers its top landmarks, museums, and cultural sites.
  • Kayaking: Paddle down the majestic Essequibo River and explore the lush rainforest along its banks.

Food and Drink Scene of Georgetown

Guyanese cuisine is a fusion of African, Indian, and Caribbean flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages when visiting Georgetown:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperpot: A spicy stew made with cassareep (a thick sauce made from the cassava root), meat, and vegetables.
  • Roti: A flatbread that is used to wrap curries, stews, and other dishes.
  • Seafood: With a coastline that stretches for over 200 miles, Georgetown offers an abundance of fresh seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Rum: Guyana is famous for its rum, which is made from sugar cane and aged in oak barrels.
  • Mauby: A sweet, bitter, and tangy drink made from the bark of the mauby tree.
  • Banks Beer: This locally brewed beer is a popular drink in Guyana.

Where to Stay in Georgetown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Georgetown has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Here are some of the top places to stay in the city:

  • Pegasus Hotel Guyana: This five-star hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers luxurious amenities and services.
  • Cara Lodge: This colonial-style hotel is located in the historic district of Georgetown and offers comfortable rooms and a charming atmosphere.
  • Aracari Resort: This eco-lodge is located in the rainforest and offers visitors a chance to experience the beauty of nature up close and personal.

What to Buy in Georgetown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs to buy when visiting Georgetown:

  • Cassava Pone: A sweet dessert made from grated cassava, coconut milk, and sugar.
  • Crafts: Georgetown has many markets and shops that sell handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  • Demerara Sugar: This type of sugar is made from sugar cane that is grown in Guyana and has a distinct flavor and texture.

Local Culture and Customs of Georgetown

Guyanese people are friendly and welcoming, and visitors to Georgetown will be greeted with open arms. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting the city:

  • Greetings: It is customary to greet someone with a smile and a handshake.
  • Dress Code: Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and government buildings.
  • Tipping: It is customary to tip at restaurants, bars, and other service establishments.

Best Time to Visit Georgetown

The best time to visit Georgetown is between December and April when the weather is relatively dry. The city experiences a rainy season between May and November, and this is when the humidity is at its highest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Georgetown

Here are some things to do and avoid when visiting Georgetown:

Do:

  • Learn some basic phrases in Creolese, the local language.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wander around alone at night.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Georgetown

Georgetown is generally safe for tourists, but it is always a good idea to take some precautions:

  • Avoid dark alleys and deserted areas at night.
  • Keep your valuables, such as phones and wallets, out of sight.
  • Hire a local guide or take a guided tour if you are unsure about where to go.

Health in Georgetown

Before traveling to Georgetown, it is important to get the necessary vaccinations and medications. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases such as Zika and malaria.
  • Drink bottled water or boil tap water before drinking it.
  • Avoid eating raw or undercooked meat or fish.

Getting to Georgetown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Georgetown is easily accessible by air, land, and water. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Georgetown

  • Cheddi Jagan International Airport: This is the main international airport in Guyana and is located about 25 miles from Georgetown.

Bus Stations Near Georgetown

  • The Georgetown Bus Station: This station is located in the heart of the city and offers bus services to other towns and cities in Guyana.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Georgetown and are a convenient way to get around the city.
  • Boats: River boats are a popular way to explore the rivers and rainforests of Guyana.

How to Get Around in Georgetown

There are several ways to get around Georgetown, including:

  • Taxis and Shared Taxis: Taxis are widely available and are a convenient way to get around the city. Shared taxis are a cheaper option but may take longer to reach your destination.
  • Minibuses: Minibuses are a popular method of transportation in Guyana. They are cheap but can be crowded and uncomfortable.
  • Bicycle and Motorcycle Rentals: Bicycles and motorcycles can be rented from various shops and vendors in the city.

Where to Go Next from Georgetown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guyana has a lot to offer beyond Georgetown. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Iwokrama Rainforest Reserve: This nature reserve offers plenty of outdoor activities, from hiking and birdwatching to fishing and swimming.
  • Lethem: This border town offers access to the incredible Rupununi Savannah, where visitors can see a wide range of wildlife and enjoy outdoor activities like camping and hiking.
  • Essequibo River: This river is one of the longest and widest in South America and offers plenty of opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and other water-based activities.

Conclusion

Georgetown is a fascinating city that offers plenty of things to do and explore. From its rich cultural heritage and history to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, Georgetown has something for everyone to enjoy. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your trip to this incredible city and create unforgettable memories.
