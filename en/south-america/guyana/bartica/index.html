Introduction to Bartica

Bartica, located in the Cuyuni-Mazaruni Region of Guyana in South America, is a town that is quickly becoming a popular destination for tourists. Bartica is situated where the Cuyuni River meets the mighty Essequibo River, making it the ideal location for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and swimming.

Bartica is rich in history and culture, with many museums and monuments celebrating the town’s heritage as a hub for the gold mining and logging industries. Bartica is also a great base camp for those looking to explore the hinterland of Guyana.

List of Places to Visit in Bartica

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or the great outdoors, Bartica has something to offer. Here are the must-visit places in Bartica:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bartica Museum: Learn about the town’s history and culture, including its links to the gold mining industry.

  • St. John the Baptist Anglican Church: A beautiful example of Victorian Gothic architecture built in the 19th century.

  • Baganara resort: A luxurious island resort where you can soak up the sun and explore the beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Bartica: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Potaro River: Take a dip and enjoy the scenic views of Guyana’s longest river.

  • Bartica Islands: Located a few kilometers from Bartica town, this small group of islands offers mesmerizing views of the Essequibo River.

  • Kaieteur Falls: A natural wonder that is a must-visit for anyone exploring Guyana. It is the world’s widest single-drop waterfall and is surrounded by a stunning rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bartica

Bartica is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many trails suitable for all levels. Here are some of the popular trails in Bartica:

  • Baracara Falls trail: A moderate hike to a beautiful waterfall, with mesmerizing views of the surrounding forest.

  • Tapakuma trek: A more challenging hike that takes you through the Tapakuma forest and Mahdia Mountains, with lookout points offering stunning views of the nearby mountains.

  • Bartica biking trail: A relatively flat track that encircles Bartica town, offering scenic views of the river and surrounding forest.

Best Things to Do in Bartica: Activities and Tours

Aside from outdoor adventures, Bartica also has many activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Bartica:

  • Explore the town: Walk around Bartica and explore the history and culture through the buildings and landmarks.

  • Go on a river tour: There are many boat tours available that take you down the rivers to explore the wilderness and experience the impressive flora and fauna of Guyana.

  • Try your hand at fishing: Fishing is a popular pastime in Bartica, with many options available for anglers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bartica

To fully experience Bartica and its surroundings, here is a week-long itinerary to guide your trip:

Day 1: Check into your hotel in Bartica and explore the town, with an emphasis on its history and culture.

Day 2: Take a river tour to explore the town’s surroundings.

Day 3: Hike to Baracara Falls and enjoy a swim.

Day 4: Take a fishing tour and try to catch some of the famous Guyanese fish.

Day 5: Explore the nearby Kaieteur Falls, one of the most stunning natural attractions in Guyana.

Day 6: Relax at Baganara resort and enjoy its amenities.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Linden, a bustling town that is home to many gold and diamond mines.

Food and Drink Scene of Bartica

Bartica is famous for its delicious cuisine based on cassava, coconut, and fish. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Bartica:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepperpot: A spicy stew made from cassareep, meat, and spices.

  • Cook-up Rice: A one-pot meal made from rice, peas, and seasonings.

  • Roti: A flatbread that is used to wrap curried meats and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Mauby: A drink made from the bark of the mauby tree.

  • Rum punch: A sweet and fruity cocktail made from rum, fruit juices, and other ingredients.

  • Coconut water: A refreshing drink straight from the coconut.

Where to Stay in Bartica: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bartica has a range of accommodations for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bartica:

  • Baganara Island Resort: A luxurious resort located on a private island with stunning views of the Essequibo River.

  • Herdmanston Lodge Hotel: A charming hotel with comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.

  • G-View Guest House: A budget-friendly option in the heart of Bartica.

What to Buy in Bartica: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bartica is a great place to buy unique souvenirs and locally made products, including:

  • Handmade crafts: Beautiful textiles, baskets, and pottery made by skilled artisans.

  • Rum: Guyana is well-known for its rum, and Bartica has many varieties available for purchase.

  • Local spices: Guyanese produce many exotic spices that are hard to find elsewhere in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Bartica

Guyana is home to many indigenous cultures, and Bartica is no exception. Visitors should respect local customs and engage with the people to learn more about their heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bartica

The best time to visit Bartica is between December and April when the days are dry, sunny, and warm. The rainy season runs from May to November, and heavy rainfall can cause rivers to flood and roads to close.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bartica

Here are the do’s and don’ts of Bartica:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.

  • Take time to explore the town and its surroundings.

  • Try local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

  • Engage in reckless activities that could harm you or the environment.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Bartica is a relatively safe and healthy town to visit, but visitors should take basic precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Bartica

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

  • Follow safety guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Bartica

  • Make sure to get all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Bartica.

  • Drink bottled water or boiled/filtered water.

  • Watch out for insects and take appropriate precautions as necessary.

Getting to Bartica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bartica is relatively easy to get to, with various transportation options available.

Airports Near Bartica

The closest international airport is Cheddi Jagan International Airport in Georgetown, approximately 60 miles away. From there, visitors can take a connecting flight to Bartica.

Bus Stations Near Bartica

There are no direct bus routes to Bartica, but visitors can take a bus from Georgetown to the nearby town of Cuyuni, where they can arrange for a boat or taxi to take them to Bartica.

Other Options

Visitors can also charter a boat or hire a private taxi to take them to Bartica.

How to Get Around in Bartica

Bartica is a relatively small town and can be explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a bicycle to get around.

Where to Go Next from Bartica: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more of Guyana after visiting Bartica, here are some nearby destinations to explore:

  • Georgetown: The capital city of Guyana, with a rich history and culture.

  • Rupununi: A vast savannah with diverse flora and fauna, offering excellent opportunities for wildlife viewing.

Conclusion

Bartica is a town that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation or an active one, Bartica offers something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip to Bartica and experience the best of Guyana!
