Introduction to New Amsterdam

Nestled in the heart of Guyana, South America, New Amsterdam is a charming city with a culturally rich history and naturally beautiful landscapes. Known for its friendly locals and vibrant atmosphere, New Amsterdam is an up-and-coming destination that offers a unique experience to travelers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, food and drink scene, places to stay, and much more in New Amsterdam.

List of Places to Visit in New Amsterdam

New Amsterdam is filled with exciting places to visit, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Berbice Museum: This museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the history of Berbice, a former Dutch colony. The museum features galleries that display artifacts from the education, transportation, and medical industries.

  • Fort Nassau: Located on the bank of the Berbice River, Fort Nassau is a colonial fort that was built by the Dutch in 1740. The fort is a reminder of Guyana’s colonial history and offers stunning views of the river.

  • Canje River: This beautiful river is a must-visit spot for nature lovers. Take a boat ride down the river to spot a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, birds, and snakes.

Natural Attractions of New Amsterdam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canje Pheasant Island: Located in the Canje Creek, this island is a popular spot for bird-watchers. Visitors can hike around the island, where they’ll be able to view a variety of bird species.

  • Kaieteur Falls: One of the largest single-drop waterfalls in the world, Kaieteur Falls is a spectacle to behold. The falls are located within Kaieteur National Park, which also offers a variety of hiking trails.

  • Iwokrama Forest Reserve: This forest reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can take a guided tour through the reserve and spot wildlife, including jaguars, sloths, and monkeys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Amsterdam

New Amsterdam offers a variety of hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Black Water Trail: This trail takes hikers through the Iwokrama Forest Reserve. The trail is about 3.2 km long and offers stunning views of the forest’s flora and fauna.

  • Nyarai Trail: This is a popular trail for bird-watchers. The trail offers visitors views of a variety of bird species, including the Scarlet Macaw.

  • Bartica-Stelling Fort Island Trail: This trail takes hikers through the heart of Bartica, a small town near New Amsterdam. The trail is about 8 km long and offers visitors views of the town’s rich cultural history.

Best Things to Do in New Amsterdam: Activities and Tours

New Amsterdam offers visitors a variety of activities and tours, including:

  • River Tours: Take a boat ride down the Canje River and spot wildlife such as birds, monkeys, and snakes.

  • Cultural Tours: New Amsterdam is rich in cultural history. Take a guided tour to explore the town’s history and culture.

  • Water Sports: New Amsterdam is surrounded by water, making it the perfect destination for water sports enthusiasts. Visitors can go kayaking, canoeing, or paddle-boarding.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Amsterdam

Day 1: Visit the Berbice Museum and the Fort Nassau.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of New Amsterdam to explore the town’s rich cultural history.

Day 3: Go hiking at Canje Pheasant Island or take a boat ride down the Canje River.

Day 4: Visit the stunning Kaieteur Falls within Kaieteur National Park.

Day 5: Take a forest reserve tour at Iwokrama Forest Reserve.

Day 6: Go on a cultural tour of Bartica-Stelling Fort Island Trail.

Day 7: Enjoy water sports such as kayaking, canoeing, or paddle-boarding.

Food and Drink Scene of New Amsterdam

New Amsterdam’s food and drink scene is as diverse as its cultural heritage. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cook-up Rice: A popular Guyanese rice dish that is typically served with chicken, pork, or fish.

  • Pepperpot: A traditional Guyanese stew made with cassava and a variety of meats. It is often served during Christmas.

  • Roti: An Indian-inspired dish made with flatbread and vegetable or meat filling.

Local Beverages

  • Mauby: A popular sweet drink made with bark, sugar, and spices.

  • Guinness: A staple drink in Guyana, Guinness is particularly popular during Christmas.

  • Pineapple Juice: Made from fresh pineapples, this refreshing juice is a must-try when in New Amsterdam.

Where to Stay in New Amsterdam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Amsterdam offers accommodations for every budget, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • Grand Coastal Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of New Amsterdam.

  • New Amsterdam Inn: A budget guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms at affordable rates.

  • Solid Rock Lodge: A cozy lodge that is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in New Amsterdam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products that you might consider taking back home:

  • Handicrafts: Guyanese handicrafts are made from materials such as wood, leather, and beads. They are often sold in the local markets.

  • Rum: Guyana is known for its premium quality rum, which is distilled from sugar cane.

  • Local Spices: Guyanese cuisine is rich in flavor, and local spices such as cinnamon, tamarind, and allspice are commonly used.

Local Culture and Customs of New Amsterdam

New Amsterdam is home to a diverse population that includes people of Indian, Chinese, and African descent. A majority of the population is bilingual, and English is widely spoken. Locals are friendly and are always willing to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit New Amsterdam

The best time to visit New Amsterdam is during the dry season, from December to March. The weather is warm and pleasant during this time, and the chance of rain is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Amsterdam

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Take a guided tour to explore the history and culture of New Amsterdam.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

  • Photograph locals without their permission.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Amsterdam

New Amsterdam is generally a safe place to visit, but it is advisable to take precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your belongings secure.

Health in New Amsterdam

Visitors should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella. Malaria is prevalent in certain areas of Guyana, so it is important to take appropriate precautions.

Getting to New Amsterdam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Amsterdam

The closest airport to New Amsterdam is the Cheddi Jagan International Airport in Georgetown, which is approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near New Amsterdam

There are several bus stations in Georgetown that offer services to New Amsterdam.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to get to New Amsterdam.

How to Get Around in New Amsterdam

New Amsterdam is a small town that is easily navigable by foot or bicycle. Taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from New Amsterdam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors may want to consider:

  • Georgetown: Guyana’s capital is roughly 100 km away from New Amsterdam and is home to several cultural attractions, including the St. George’s Cathedral.

  • Kanuku Mountains: This mountain range is located in southern Guyana and is known for its beautiful scenery and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

New Amsterdam is a beautiful and culturally rich destination that offers visitors a unique travel experience. From exploring the town’s rich cultural history to hiking through stunning landscapes, there is something for everyone in New Amsterdam. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can make the most of their time in this hidden gem of South America.
