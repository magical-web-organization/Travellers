Are you looking for a unique travel experience in South America? Look no further than Lethem, a vibrant town located on the border of Guyana and Brazil. With its mix of culture, natural beauty, and adventurous activities, Lethem is the perfect destination for those seeking something different. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit in Lethem, the top things to do, and important tips for a successful trip.

Introduction to Lethem

Lethem is situated in the Upper Takutu-Upper Essequibo region of Guyana, and is the capital of the Rupununi district. The town is famous for its location at the junction of the main road connecting the coast and the interior, making it a central hub for transportation. Lethem is known for its cultural diversity, with indigenous tribes such as the Wai-Wai and Macushi living alongside Guyanese, Brazilians, and other South Americans.

List of Places to Visit in Lethem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the history and culture of Lethem and the surrounding region, there are several museums and historical places worth visiting. The Rupununi Museums is a great place to start, with exhibits on the flora and fauna of the region, as well as the local indigenous tribes. You can also visit St. Ignatius Village, which has a fascinating history as a Jesuit mission and indigenous boarding school.

Natural Attractions of Lethem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lethem is located in the heart of the Rupununi savanna, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. The Kanuku Mountains are a must-see, with their scenic hiking trails and beautiful waterfalls. South Rupununi Conservation Society is a wildlife reserve, home to several endangered species such as the Giant Anteater and the Harpy Eagle. You can also explore the nearby Iwokrama Rainforest, which spans over one million acres and is home to a vast array of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lethem

Lethem is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Iwokrama Rainforest offers several trails for hiking, ranging from easy to difficult. If you prefer biking, you can rent a bike and explore the savannas around Lethem.

Best Things to Do in Lethem: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Lethem. You can take a boat tour down the Rupununi River, go birdwatching, or even swim with the friendly Arapaima fish. If you’re interested in local cultures, you can visit the indigenous villages around Lethem and learn about their way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lethem

Day 1: Visit the Rupununi Museums and the nearby St. Ignatius Village. Day 2: Take a boat tour down the Rupununi River and explore the nearby savannas. Day 3: Hike the Kanuku Mountains and see the beautiful waterfalls. Day 4: Visit Iwokrama Rainforest and go birdwatching. Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby indigenous villages and learn about their way of life. Day 6: Swim with the Arapaima fish and go fishing. Day 7: Rest day and visit local cafes and bars.

Food and Drink Scene of Lethem

Must-Try Dishes

Lethem is known for its diverse cuisine, influenced by its indigenous, Guyanese, and Brazilian populations. Some must-try dishes include Pepperpot, a spicy stew made with cassava and meat, and Metemgee, a creamy soup made with cassava, meat, and fish.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Lethem should try the local beer, Banks. There are also a variety of fruit juices and coconut water available in the town.

Where to Stay in Lethem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Lethem, ranging from budget-friendly to upscale. Options include hotels like Takutu Hotel, Hostels like Jungle Paradise and guesthouses like Aranawa Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Lethem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Lethem has plenty of options. You can find handmade crafts like woven baskets, ceramics, and jewelry from the indigenous villages around the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Lethem

Lethem is a diverse town with many cultures and customs. Visitors should respect local customs like dress code and language used and ask for permission before taking photographs of local people.

Best Time to Visit Lethem

The best time to visit Lethem is during the dry season, which runs from September to April. The temperatures are cooler during this time, making it pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lethem

Do:

  • Respect local cultures and customs
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Prepare for outdoor activities with appropriate gear

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of local people without permission
  • Travel to the region alone and without proper guidance
  • Leave litter in natural areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Lethem

Lethem is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still exercise caution and avoid traveling alone at night. Visitors should also take appropriate precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like Malaria and Dengue Fever.

Health in Lethem

Visitors should ensure they are up to date on their vaccines before traveling to Lethem. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as using mosquito nets while sleeping and applying insect repellent regularly.

Getting to Lethem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lethem

The nearest airport to Lethem is the Lethem Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lethem

Visitors can take a bus or taxi from Georgetown, the capital of Guyana, to Lethem.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a boat down the Essequibo River to Bartica, and then take a bus to Lethem.

How to Get Around in Lethem

The easiest way to get around Lethem is by taxi or motorbike taxi, available throughout the town. Bikes can also be rented to explore the nearby savannas.

Where to Go Next from Lethem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more exciting destinations to visit after Lethem, you can explore the nearby town of Georgetown, famous for its colonial architecture and lively nightlife. The Kaieteur Falls, one of the largest single-drop waterfalls in the world, is also accessible from Georgetown.

Conclusion

Lethem is a fantastic destination for travelers who want to explore the natural beauty and cultural diversity of South America. With its stunning natural attractions and unique cultural experiences, it’s sure to be an unforgettable trip. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to Lethem.
