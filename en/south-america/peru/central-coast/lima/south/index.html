Introduction to South

Located on the Central Coast of Peru, South is a bustling region in Lima. It has a unique blend of rich history, diverse culture, and beautiful nature that can cater to any type of traveler. From world-famous museums to outdoor adventures, South has it all. With this travel guide, you’ll discover the best things to do in South, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in South

There are plenty of places to visit in South, Lima. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South is home to many cultural landmarks, museums, and historical places that showcase the region’s rich history and culture. Here are a few places that you must visit:

  • Larco Museum: This is one of the best museums in Lima, featuring ancient ceramics and artifacts of the Moche, Chimu, and Inca cultures.

  • Huaca Pucllana: This pre-Incan archeological site is located in the heart of Lima and has an impressive pyramid structure.

  • Barranco: This bohemian district of Lima is known for its colorful architecture, street art, and lively nightlife.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South is a paradise for nature lovers. It has scenic parks, beaches, and mountains that offer a wide range of outdoor activities. Here are a few natural attractions that you must explore:

  • El Morro Solar: This is a popular hiking spot with a breathtaking 360-degree view of Lima’s coastline and cityscape.

  • Parque Kennedy: This park is home to various species of birds, fountains, and sculptures, and is a great spot for picnicking.

  • Miraflores Boardwalk: This scenic boardwalk offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, and it is perfect for jogging or cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

South has plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer an opportunity to explore the region’s natural beauty and enjoy outdoor activities. Here are a few hiking and biking routes that you can take:

  • El Morro Solar: This is a challenging hiking trail that takes about 2-3 hours to complete, but the view at the top is worth it.

  • Circuito Mágico del Agua: This is a trail that goes around a park that has 13 illuminated fountains and is an excellent spot for nighttime walks.

  • Costa Verde: This is a paved bike route that runs along the coast of Peru and offers scenic views of the Pacific Ocean.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in South, Lima, from visiting museums to outdoor activities. Here are some of the best things to do in South:

  • Try Paragliding: South is one of the best paragliding spots in Lima, and it offers stunning views of the coastline.

  • Learn to Surf: With its warm waters and consistent waves, South is a great place to learn to surf.

  • Visit the Barranco Art Route: Take a walking tour of Barranco’s vibrant street art scene and learn about local artists and their work.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

If you’re planning to spend a week in South, Lima, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Lima and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the city center and visiting attractions such as the Huaca Pucllana archeological site.

Day 2: Visit the Larco Museum and then head to Barranco to explore the bohemian district.

Day 3: Spend the day at El Morro Solar for hiking or biking.

Day 4: Take a paragliding lesson or try surfing.

Day 5: Visit the Circuito Mágico del Agua at night and enjoy the illuminated water fountains.

Day 6: Head to the Miraflores Boardwalk for a scenic bike ride or jog.

Day 7: Visit the Parque Kennedy area to birdwatch, explore gardens and see the street performers before your departure.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers a wide range of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: This is a classic Peruvian dish made of raw fish marinated in lime juice and spices.

  • Anticuchos: These are grilled beef heart skewers that are marinated in spices and served with potatoes.

  • Lomo Saltado: This popular dish features stir-fried beef, onions, tomatoes, and french fries.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: This is Peru’s national drink, made of pisco (grape brandy), lime juice, egg whites, and syrup.

  • Chicha Morada: This is a sweet purple corn drink that is often served with meals.

  • Cusqueña Beer: This is a popular Peruvian beer that comes in various styles, from light lagers to dark ales.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South has many accommodation options to fit any budget, from luxury hotels to backpacker hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in South:

  • Costa Del Sol Ramada Hotel: This is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Miraflores, with an outdoor pool, spa, and gym.

  • Red Psycho Llama Hostel: This is a trendy hostel located in Barranco, known for its colorful decor, rooftop terrace, and social events.

  • Guesthouse Vila: This guesthouse has a cozy and homey atmosphere located in the residential neighborhood of San Isidro.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South is known for its vibrant handicrafts, textiles, and art. Here are some recommendations on souvenirs to bring home:

  • Alpaca Wool Clothing: One of the most popular souvenirs to bring home is alpaca wool clothing such as sweaters, scarves, and blankets.

  • Chullos: These are traditional Peruvian hats made of alpaca wool and feature earflaps and tassels.

  • Pottery and Ceramics: South is home to many talented potters and ceramicists, producing unique pieces of art, from vases to plates.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Peru is a diverse country with many cultural customs and practices. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting South:

  • Language: The official language of Peru is Spanish, but many locals also speak Quechua and Aymara.

  • Respect for Culture: Peru has a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to respect local customs and traditions.

  • Public Displays of Affection: Peruvians generally do not display public affection, such as holding hands, so it is best to avoid this behavior.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South, Lima, is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, the weather is mild and comfortable, perfect for outdoor activities and site-seeing. The rainy season runs from November to April and can disrupt travel plans with heavy rainfall and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting South, Lima:

Do:

  • Try Local Food and Drink: Peru has some of the best cuisine in South America, so be sure to try local dishes and drinks.

  • Learn Some Spanish: Learning some Spanish phrases can help you communicate better with locals and enhance your travel experience.

  • Bargain in Markets: In markets, bargaining is expected, so feel free to negotiate prices with vendors.

Don’t:

  • Flash Valuables: Avoid wearing expensive jewelry, watches and beware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

  • Waste Water: Peru is a country that struggles with water shortages, so it is important to conserve water whenever possible.

  • Take Photos of People Without Permission: It is polite to ask for permission before taking photos of people.

Safety and Health

Before traveling to South, Lima, it is essential to take some safety precautions and stay healthy.

Safety in South

  • Stay Alert: Be vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

  • Avoid Taxi Scams: Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing apps when possible to avoid scams and negotiate a price before the ride.

  • Watch for Altitude Sickness: South Lima is at sea level, but if you plan to visit nearby high altitude destinations, acclimate gradually to avoid altitude sickness.

Health in South

  • Vaccinations: It’s essential to make sure that your vaccines are up-to-date before traveling to Peru, especially for Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and Yellow Fever.

  • Food and Water: Be cautious with consuming salads, fruits, street foods, and drink bottled or boiled water before consumption.

  • Sun Exposure: South Lima has a warm climate, and exposure to the sun can cause sunburns, dehydration, and heat strokes, so bring sunscreen, a hat and stay hydrated.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

South, Lima, is well-connected and easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip:

Airports Near South

  • Jorge Chavez International Airport: Lima’s international airport has direct flights from many major cities worldwide, and it is a 30-minute drive from South.

Bus Stations Near South

  • Lima Bus Terminal: This is Lima’s primary bus terminal, with connections to cities all over Peru and South America, including Cusco and Arequipa.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Lima. It is best to use licensed taxis or ride-sharing apps such as Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in South

Getting around South, Lima is a breeze with various transportation options, from taxis to public buses. Here are some practical options to choose from:

  • Taxis: Taxis are common, but it is essential to use licensed taxis or ride-sharing apps when possible to avoid scams.

  • Public Buses: Public buses are cheap and run throughout Lima, but it requires negotiation and is frequently crowded.

  • Metro: Lima has a recently developed metro system which is a safe and fast way to travel throughout Lima.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from South, Lima. Here are some suggestions:

  • Paracas: Located south of Lima, Paracas is a small coastal town famous for its natural reserve and beautiful beaches.

  • Ballestas Islands: These islands are just a few hours from Lima and are home to sea lions, penguins, and many birds.

  • Ica: This desert city is famous for its sand dunes, wineries and many outdoor activities such as sandboarding, dune buggies, and desert hiking.

Conclusion

South, Lima, is a treasure trove of culture, nature, and adventure. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to South, Lima, and discover the best places to visit, things to do, and more. Don’t forget to try the local food, bargain with vendors, and enjoy your adventure in South, Lima!
