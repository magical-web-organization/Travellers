Introduction to Central Lima

Located in the Central Coast of Peru, Central Lima is the historic center of the capital city of Lima. With its stunning colonial architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and delicious cuisine, Central Lima is a must-visit destination in South America. Whether you’re interested in history, art, or outdoor adventures, Central Lima has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Central Lima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Historic Centre of Lima

Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this area is home to stunning colonial architecture, beautiful parks, and lively markets.

Huaca Pucllana

A pre-Incan archaeological site in the heart of Miraflores, this ancient complex highlights the country’s indigenous roots and features a museum on-site.

National Museum of Archaeology, Anthropology, and History of Peru

Home to over 100,000 artifacts, this museum gives visitors an in-depth look into the country’s rich history.

Plaza Mayor

Located in the heart of Lima, this historic plaza is surrounded by some of the most iconic buildings in the city, including the Presidential Palace and the Cathedral of Lima.

Larco Museum

With over 45,000 pieces on display, the Larco Museum is one of the largest pre-Columbian art collections in the world.

Natural Attractions of Central Lima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parque de las Leyendas

A wildlife reserve and zoological park, this site features over 200 species and includes a botanical garden and a butterfly farm.

Magic Water Circuit

A collection of interactive fountains and light shows, this family-friendly attraction is sure to provide an unforgettable experience.

Cerro San Cristobal

Rising over 400 meters above the city, this hilltop provides an incredible panoramic view of Lima and is accessible by hiking or cable car.

Pantanos de Villa Wildlife Refuge

This wetland oasis is home to over 200 bird species, including flamingos, and offers unique opportunities for birdwatching and nature photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Lima

The Miraflores Coastal Walkway

This 6-mile path hugs the cliffs above the Pacific Ocean and takes visitors through some of the most beautiful neighborhoods in Lima.

Rímac River Path

A unique 17-km bike route that follows the Rímac River, providing scenic views of the city and access to some of Lima’s lesser-known neighborhoods.

Pachacamac Trail

This 25-km trail leads hikers through one of the city’s most important archaeological sites, the Pachacamac complex, and through breathtaking natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Central Lima: Activities and Tours

Bike Tours

A fun and unique way to explore the city, bike tours offer visitors an up-close look at some of Lima’s most iconic landmarks and lesser-known spots.

Food Tours

Lima is known for its incredible cuisine, and food tours are a great way to sample some of the city’s best dishes, from ceviche to anticuchos.

Surfing Lessons

With some of the best waves in South America, Lima is a hotspot for surfers of all levels, and surf lessons are available for those interested in trying this thrilling sport.

Boat Tours

A relaxing and informative way to see Lima from a different angle, boat tours offer visitors the chance to see the city’s skyline from the water and learn about its history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Lima

Must-Try Dishes

Ceviche

Lima’s most famous dish, this zesty and refreshing seafood salad is made with fresh fish marinated in lime juice, red onion, and chili peppers.

Anticuchos

Grilled beef hearts on skewers, this dish is a popular street food in Lima and is often served with potatoes and aji sauce.

Lomo Saltado

A stir-fry dish featuring beef, onions, tomatoes, and French fries, this hearty and savory dish is a staple in Peruvian cuisine.

Local Beverages

Pisco Sour

Peru’s iconic cocktail, this tangy and refreshing drink is made with pisco, lime juice, egg whites, and bitters.

Chicha Morada

A refreshing and sweet drink made from purple corn, pineapple, and spices, this non-alcoholic drink is perfect for hot days in Lima.

Where to Stay in Central Lima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Agustin Riviera

Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and stunning views of Lima’s skyline.

Kokopelli Hostel Lima

A budget-friendly and sociable option, this hostel features clean and cozy dormitories and private rooms, a stunning rooftop bar, and plenty of chances to connect with other travelers.

El Tambo II

Located in the trendy Miraflores neighborhood, this guesthouse offers clean and spacious rooms, a friendly and English-speaking staff, and easy access to some of Lima’s best restaurants and bars.

What to Buy in Central Lima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alpaca Wool Clothing

Lima’s proximity to the Andean highlands means that alpaca wool products are easily available here, from cozy sweaters to warm blankets and scarves.

Peruvian Chocolate

Peru is home to some of the finest chocolate in the world, and visitors can find a variety of delicious and high-quality chocolate products in Lima, from single-origin bars to artisanal truffles.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Lima

Peruvian culture is a unique blend of indigenous, Spanish, African, and Asian influences, and is known for its warm hospitality, vibrant music and dance scene, and delicious cuisine. Some customs to keep in mind include greeting people with a kiss on the cheek, being punctual for appointments, and dressing conservatively when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Central Lima

Lima’s weather is mild and temperate year-round, but the best time to visit is from December to April, when the weather is sunny and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Lima

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including ceviche, lomo saltado, and pisco sour.
  • Explore Lima’s vibrant cultural scene, including its museums, art galleries, and music venues.
  • Take advantage of Lima’s outdoor adventures, including surfing, hiking, and biking.
  • Engage with the locals, who are known for their warmth and hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Drink tap water, which is not safe for consumption.
  • Walk around with valuables or large amounts of cash.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Lima

While Lima is a relatively safe city, visitors should take precautions to avoid petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag-snatching. It’s also important to avoid certain areas of Lima after dark and to only use licensed taxis.

Health in Central Lima

Visitors to Lima should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and should take caution to avoid stomach illnesses, which can be common in Peru. It’s also important to use insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Central Lima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central Lima

Jorge Chavez International Airport is located just 11 km from the city center and offers both international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Central Lima

The main bus station in Lima is located in the neighborhood of La Victoria and offers both long-distance and local bus services.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber are widely available in Lima, and bike rentals offer a fun and eco-friendly way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Central Lima

Lima’s public transportation system includes buses and the Metropolitano, a modern and efficient bus rapid transit system. Taxis are also a convenient way to get around, but visitors should be sure to use only licensed taxis to avoid scam artists and unsafe vehicles.

Where to Go Next from Central Lima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lima’s location on the Central Coast makes it a great jumping-off point for exploring other regions of Peru. Some nearby destinations to consider include:

  • Paracas and the Ballestas Islands, known for their stunning coastal landscapes and diverse wildlife.
  • The Nazca Lines, a series of ancient geoglyphs that can only be seen from the air.
  • The Sacred Valley of the Incas, a region near Cusco that is home to some of Peru’s most iconic archaeological sites.

Conclusion

Central Lima offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. From exploring ancient archaeological sites to trying the city’s delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in this vibrant and dynamic city.
