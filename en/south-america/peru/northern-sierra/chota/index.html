Introduction to Chota

Nestled in the Northern Sierra region of Peru, Chota is a charming destination that offers a glimpse into Peru’s rich culture, history, and natural beauty. This small town is surrounded by picturesque landscapes dominated by the Andes mountains and is home to a vibrant community that preserves its Afro-Peruvian heritage. In this guide, we explore the must-see attractions, best food and drink spots, top activities, and insider tips to help plan your Chota itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Chota

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Afroperuano: This museum showcases the cultural contributions and history of the Afro-Peruvian community in Chota. Visitors can see exhibits and artifacts related to music, dance, religion, and daily life.
  • Iglesia de la Virgen de la Asunción: This beautiful church is located in the center of Chota and is considered one of the town’s most significant historical landmarks.
  • Casa Hacienda San Juan de Algodonal: This former sugar plantation is now a museum that showcases the region’s colonial and agrarian history. Visitors can explore the estate’s chapel, stables, and living quarters.

Natural Attractions of Chota: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chota Valley: This valley offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is a popular spot for hiking and bird-watching.
  • Cascades de Condebamba: The Condebamba waterfalls are a series of cascading falls set against a beautiful forest backdrop. Visitors can hike to the falls and take a refreshing swim in the natural pools.
  • Laguna de Condebamba: This tranquil lagoon is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a great spot for fishing, picnicking, or just relaxing in the natural setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chota

Chota and its surrounding countryside offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Chotano Ecotourism Association offers guided tours of the region’s natural and historical landmarks. Some popular routes include:

  • Camino Inca Trail: This historic trail dates back to the Inca empire and passes through scenic mountain landscapes, small towns, and archeological sites.
  • Mirador de la Cumbre: This vantage point offers panoramic views of the Chota Valley and is an easy hike suitable for all levels of fitness.
  • Cerro de La Culebra: This challenging trek takes hikers to the summit of Mount Culebra and rewards them with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Chota: Activities and Tours

  • Festival de San Juan Bautista: This annual festival celebrates the patron saint of Chota with music, dancing, food, and parades.
  • Cooking classes: Visitors can learn how to prepare traditional Afro-Peruvian dishes like tacu-tacu and arroz con pato from local cooks.
  • Music and dance performances: Chota has a vibrant music and dance scene that reflects its Afro-Peruvian heritage. Visitors can attend live performances or take classes to learn how to dance or play traditional instruments such as the cajon.

Food and Drink Scene of Chota

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacu-tacu: This traditional Afro-Peruvian dish consists of rice and beans shaped into a patty and pan-fried. It is usually served with a side of salsa criolla and fried plantains.
  • Arroz con pato: This flavorful dish is made with duck marinated in beer and cilantro and served with rice and beans.
  • Causa rellena: This popular dish consists of mashed potatoes mixed with lime juice and chili powder, filled with chicken or tuna salad, and garnished with avocado, olives, and hard-boiled eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha de jora: This fermented corn beverage is a popular drink in Chota and is traditionally consumed during festivals and celebrations.
  • Pisco: This grape brandy is Peru’s national drink and is often used to make the traditional cocktail Pisco Sour.

Where to Stay in Chota: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chota has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  • Hospedaje Villa Chota: This guesthouse is located in the historic center of Chota and offers comfortable rooms, a garden, and free Wi-Fi.
  • El Destino Hostal: This charming hostel has a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of the town and mountains. It offers private and shared rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lounge area.
  • Casa Hacienda San Juan de Algodonal: This historic estate also offers accommodation options for visitors who want to experience the colonial charm of the region.

What to Buy in Chota: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cajon: This percussion instrument is an essential part of the Afro-Peruvian musical tradition and is popular worldwide.
  • Textiles: Chota is known for its colorful textiles, including rugs, blankets, and ponchos, made from locally sourced wool.
  • Pottery: Local artisans produce clay pottery in a variety of shapes and sizes, often decorated with intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Chota

Chota has a distinct Afro-Peruvian culture that blends African, Spanish, and Andean influences. The town’s music, dance, and cuisine reflect this rich heritage, and visitors are encouraged to participate in festivals and cultural events to experience it firsthand. The people of Chota are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should always show respect for local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Chota

The best time to visit Chota is between May and October, which is the dry season when the weather is mild and sunny. The festival of San Juan Bautista is held in June and is a highlight of the town’s cultural calendar.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chota

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Participate in cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without asking for permission first.
  • Disrespect religious sites and symbols.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chota

Chota is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings, especially at night. It’s also recommended to use registered taxis and avoid unlit or isolated areas.

Health in Chota

Visitors should have all necessary vaccinations and follow basic hygiene practices when visiting Chota. The town’s altitude (over 2800 meters) may also cause altitude sickness, so it’s recommended to stay hydrated and take it easy during the first few days.

Getting to Chota: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chota is located about 14 kilometers from the city of Cajamarca and can be reached by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Chota

  • Mayor General FAP Armando Revoredo Iglesias Airport: Located in Cajamarca, this airport is the closest one to Chota and offers domestic flights to Lima and other cities in northern Peru.

Bus Stations Near Chota

  • Cajamarca Terminal Terrestre: This bus station in Cajamarca is the main transportation hub for the region and offers daily buses to Chota and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi: Visitors can hire a private car or taxi from Cajamarca to Chota if they prefer a more comfortable and flexible option.

How to Get Around in Chota

Most of Chota’s attractions are located within walking distance of the town center. For longer distances, visitors can take local taxis or join guided tours offered by the Chotano Ecotourism Association.

Where to Go Next from Chota: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chota is surrounded by other interesting destinations that are worth exploring, such as:

  • Cajamarca: This historic city is known for its picturesque colonial architecture, hot springs, and archeological sites.
  • Celendin: This charming town is located in a valley surrounded by mountains and is known both for its colonial architecture and for being the gateway to the Gran Bosque de Pomacocha nature reserve.
  • Marañón Canyon: This impressive canyon is one of the deepest in the world and offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscapes.

Conclusion

Chota is a hidden gem in Northern Peru that offers a unique cultural and natural experience. From its Afro-Peruvian heritage to its scenic views and outdoor adventures, this destination has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their itinerary and make the most of their time in Chota while experiencing the warmth and hospitality of its people.
