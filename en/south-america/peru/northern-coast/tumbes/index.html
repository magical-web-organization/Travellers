Introduction to Tumbes

Tumbes is a small city and region in Northern Coast, Peru, with a rich cultural and natural heritage. It is known for its pre-Columbian cultures, colonial-era architecture, pristine beaches, and protected forests. Visitors to Tumbes can experience a diverse range of activities, from hiking and biking in scenic parks, to exploring museums and historical sites.

This guide will cover everything you need to know about Tumbes, including places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and local culture and customs. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor to Tumbes, this guide will help you plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Tumbes

Tumbes offers a wide range of cultural and natural attractions for visitors to explore. This list includes some of the must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tumbes Regional Museum: This museum features exhibits on the history, art, and culture of Tumbes, including pre-Columbian artifacts, colonial-era paintings, and contemporary art.
  • Pizarro House Museum: This museum is a historic house where the Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro is said to have resided. Visitors can learn about the life and times of Pizarro and the Spanish conquest of Peru.
  • Tumbes Cathedral: This colonial-era cathedral has a stunning baroque facade and ornate interior, with altarpieces, paintings, and sculptures by local and foreign artists.
  • Cabeza de Vaca Archaeological Site: This pre-Columbian archaeological site dates back to the Moche culture and features pyramid-shaped structures, ceremonial plazas, and artifacts of gold, silver, and pottery.

Natural Attractions of Tumbes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amotape Hills National Park: This park is located in the Tumbes-Piura dry forest ecoregion and features diverse wildlife, including jaguars, pumas, monkeys, and birds. Visitors can hike, bike, or drive through the park and enjoy panoramic views of the forest.
  • Manglares de Tumbes National Sanctuary: This sanctuary is located in the Tumbes River delta and features mangrove swamps, lagoons, and beaches. Visitors can take boat tours, observe wildlife, and swim in the warm waters of the Pacific.
  • Cerros de Amotape Reserved Zone: This zone is a protected area of forests, hills, and plains that is home to endangered species such as the white-winged guan and the spectacled bear. Visitors can hike, camp, or study the flora and fauna of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tumbes

Tumbes offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the region. The following routes are recommended for adventurers:

  • Tumbes-Puerto Pizarro Route: This route follows the Tumbes River from the city of Tumbes to the seaside town of Puerto Pizarro, passing through mangroves, wetlands, and fishing villages. It is a great route for birdwatchers and nature lovers.
  • Amotape Hills Route: This route takes visitors through the Amotape Hills National Park, offering breathtaking views of the forest, the mountains, and the sea. It is a challenging route for experienced hikers and bikers.
  • Cabeza de Vaca Route: This route explores the archaeological site of Cabeza de Vaca, offering insights into the pre-Columbian cultures of Tumbes. It is an easy route for beginners and families with children.

Best Things to Do in Tumbes: Activities and Tours

Tumbes offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. The following list includes some of the best things to do in Tumbes:

  • Go birdwatching in the mangroves of Manglares de Tumbes National Sanctuary and observe species such as the black-necked stilt, the snowy egret, and the osprey.
  • Take a boat tour of the Tumbes River and discover the fishing villages, the wetlands, and the flora and fauna of the region.
  • Visit the Cabeza de Vaca Archaeological Site and learn about the Moche culture, the Inca Empire, and the Spanish conquest of Peru.
  • Snorkel or scuba dive in the warm waters of the Pacific and observe marine life such as turtles, dolphins, and sea lions.
  • Surf or kiteboard in the beaches of Punta Sal, Zorritos, and Mancora and experience the adrenaline of the waves.
  • Taste the local cuisine of Tumbes, including dishes such as ceviche, chilcano, and arroz con mariscos, and savor the flavors of the sea and the land.

Food and Drink Scene of Tumbes

Tumbes is known for its delicious seafood, its tropical fruits, and its spicy sauces. The following list includes some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Tumbes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: This dish consists of marinated raw fish or seafood, usually flavored with lime juice, onions, chili peppers, and cilantro. It is a refreshing and light meal that is perfect for hot days.
  • Chilcano: This drink is a cocktail made with pisco, lime juice, ginger ale, and bitters. It is a popular drink in Peru, and it complements seafood dishes very well.
  • Arroz con Mariscos: This dish is a rice-based meal that includes mixed seafood, such as shrimp, squid, scallops, and mussels, usually cooked with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and peppers. It is a hearty and flavorful meal that is often served with fried plantains.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This drink is a fermented corn-based beverage that is popular in the Andean region of Peru. It is mildly alcoholic and has a sweet taste.
  • Inca Kola: This soda is a national favorite in Peru and has a unique flavor that is described as bubblegum-like. It is a bright yellow color and is often served with ice.

Where to Stay in Tumbes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tumbes offers a range of accommodations for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. The following list includes some of the recommended places to stay in Tumbes:

  • Casa Andina Standard Tumbes: This hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, a bar, and a swimming pool.
  • Bungalows Costa del Sol: This beachfront hotel is located in Zorritos and offers spacious bungalows, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Hospedaje Playa Tumbes: This guesthouse is located in the seaside town of Puerto Pizarro and offers affordable rooms, a restaurant, and a boat tour service.
  • Mango B&B: This bed and breakfast is located in the countryside near Tumbes and offers peaceful rooms, a garden, and a pool.

What to Buy in Tumbes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tumbes offers several local products that make great souvenirs or gifts for friends and family. The following list includes some of the recommended items to buy in Tumbes:

  • Artisanal crafts: Tumbes is known for its handcrafted textiles, ceramics, and jewelry, often made by indigenous communities. Visitors can find unique and colorful pieces in the markets and shops of the city.
  • Tropical fruits: Tumbes is famous for its sweet and juicy mangoes, pineapples, and guavas. Visitors can buy fresh or dried fruits in the local markets and enjoy the flavors of the region.
  • Pisco: This grape-based brandy is a national drink of Peru, and Tumbes produces some of the best piscos in the country. Visitors can buy bottles of pisco in the duty-free shops of the airports or the distilleries of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Tumbes

Tumbes has a diverse cultural heritage, influenced by indigenous, colonial, and African traditions. Visitors to Tumbes should respect the local customs and be aware of the following:

  • Language: The official language of Peru is Spanish, but many people in Tumbes speak indigenous languages such as Quechua or Mochica.
  • Dress code: Tumbes is a warm and humid region, but visitors should dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Music and dance: Tumbes is known for its lively music and dance, often performed during festivals or celebrations. Visitors can enjoy the rhythms of cumbia, salsa, and Afro-Peruvian music.
  • Hospitality: The people of Tumbes are known for their warm and friendly hospitality, so visitors should be polite and respectful when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Tumbes

Tumbes has a tropical climate with high temperatures and humidity throughout the year. The best time to visit Tumbes is during the dry season, from May to October, when the weather is cooler and drier. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers and mosquitoes, especially in the forested areas of the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tumbes

To have a safe and enjoyable trip to Tumbes, visitors should follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do take insect repellent and sunscreen when visiting the natural parks and beaches of Tumbes.
  • Do learn some basic Spanish phrases to communicate with locals and to order food and drinks.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions of Tumbes.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages and savor the flavors of the region.
  • Do take a rain jacket and waterproof shoes when hiking or biking in Tumbes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables when going out at night.
  • Don’t swim or surf alone in the beaches of Tumbes, and always follow the instructions of lifeguards and local authorities.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural or cultural sites of Tumbes.
  • Don’t take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Don’t drink tap water in Tumbes, and always drink bottled or purified water.

Safety and Health

Tumbes is a safe and welcoming region for visitors, but travelers should take some precautions to avoid any health or safety issues:

Safety in Tumbes

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables when going out. Use hotel safes and lockers to store your valuable items.
  • Use licensed taxis or public transportation when moving around the city, and avoid walking alone at night in remote areas.
  • Follow the instructions of local authorities and respect the signs and rules of the natural parks and beaches.
  • Be aware of scams or money exchange issues, and avoid changing money with unlicensed individuals or businesses.

Health in Tumbes

  • Drink only bottled or purified water in Tumbes, and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Apply insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothes to avoid mosquito bites, especially in the forested areas of Tumbes.
  • Use sunscreen and protective gear when swimming, surfing, or any outdoor water activities.
  • Keep a basic first aid kit with you when traveling to Tumbes, including band-aids, antiseptic cream, and painkillers.

Getting to Tumbes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tumbes is located in Northern Coast, Peru, and can be easily accessed by air, bus, or car.

Airports Near Tumbes

  • Tumbes Airport (TBP): This is the main airport of Tumbes, and it has direct connections with Lima, Cusco, and other cities in Peru.

Bus Stations Near Tumbes

  • Tumbes Bus Station: This is the main bus station of Tumbes, and it has connections with major cities in Peru, such as Lima, Piura, and Chiclayo.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car or hire a private driver to move around Tumbes and explore the region at their own pace.
  • Shuttle service: Some hotels and tour operators offer shuttle services from the airport or bus station to the accommodation.

How to Get Around in Tumbes

Tumbes has a well-connected transportation system, including buses, taxis, and motorbikes. Visitors can choose the following options to move around Tumbes:

  • Bus: The local buses, called combis or colectivos, run between the city center and the suburbs of Tumbes. They are cheap and frequent but may be crowded and slow.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Tumbes, and they are relatively cheap and safe. Visitors should agree on the fare before getting into the taxi and should take licensed taxis whenever possible.
  • Motorbike: Motorbikes or mototaxis are a popular and fun way to move around Tumbes, especially in the beach towns. Visitors should wear helmets and avoid overloaded or reckless drivers.

Where to Go Next from Tumbes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tumbes is a great starting point for exploring Northern Coast and neighboring regions. The following list includes some of the recommended destinations and excursions from Tumbes:

  • Piura: This region is located south of Tumbes and is known for its pre-Columbian and colonial heritage, as well as its beautiful beaches and deserts.
  • Mancora: This beach town is located north of Tumbes and is famous for its surf, nightlife, and tropical climate.
  • Ecuador: This neighboring country is easily accessible from Tumbes, and visitors can explore its historical and natural sites, such as the city of Guayaquil and the Galapagos Islands.
