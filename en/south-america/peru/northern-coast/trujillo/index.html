Introduction to Trujillo

Located on the northwestern coast of Peru, Trujillo is a city that is rich in history, culture and natural beauty. As one of Peru’s oldest inhabited cities, Trujillo has been the center of extensive archaeological discoveries dating back 5000 years. With an exceptional blend of pre-Columbian, colonial, and republican architecture, Trujillo is a popular destination for travelers in search of a diverse experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Trujillo

Trujillo has a strong cultural history that is visible in its architecture, food, and traditions. Here are some customs and traditions that you should know while visiting:

  • The Marinera, a traditional dance, is a vital element of Trujillo’s cultural identity.
  • The residents of Trujillo are hospitable and friendly
  • Buses and taxis are common modes of transportation in the city. It is important to negotiate the fare as many taxis do not have meters.

Best Time to Visit Trujillo

The best time to visit Trujillo is between May and October. The weather is dry, and the temperature is mild, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities. Additionally, Trujillo hosts many of its colorful festivals during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trujillo

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Trujillo:

Do:

  • Visit Chan Chan, the largest mud city in the world.
  • Try out the food and beverages unique to Trujillo.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat as the sun can be strong in Trujillo.

Don’t:

  • Carry large amounts of cash.
  • Accept fruit or other goods from street vendors.
  • Travel alone at night.

Safety and Health

While Trujillo is considered relatively safe, it’s important to take certain precautions to keep yourself protected.

Safety in Trujillo

  • Avoid carrying valuable items around in public or poorly lit areas.
  • Never leave your bags or phones unattended.
  • Do not walk alone on dark streets or neighborhoods.

Health in Trujillo

  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach problems.
  • Stay up-to-date on your vaccinations before traveling to Peru.
  • Apply insect repellent, use bed nets and wear long sleeves and pants to avoid mosquito bites.
