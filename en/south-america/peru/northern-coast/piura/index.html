Introduction to Piura

Welcome to Piura, a city located in the Northern Coast of Peru, South America. Known as the “City of the Eternal Heat,” Piura is famously hot and dry, but its warm local culture and rich history make it an incredibly interesting and rewarding destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Piura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plaza de Armas: The main square of Piura, surrounded by beautiful colonial-era buildings and the stunning Cathedral of Piura.
  • Museo Regional de Piura: Learn about the history, culture, and art of Piura and Northern Peru at this excellent regional museum.
  • Iglesia de San Francisco: This stunning church dates back to the 16th century and features beautiful Baroque architecture and interior decor.
  • Casa Museo del Almirante Miguel Grau: Explore the life of Peru’s most celebrated war hero, Miguel Grau, at this well-preserved museum in his former home.

Natural Attractions of Piura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa Mancora: One of Peru’s most famous beach destinations, with crystal-clear waters, incredible surfing, and a lively international crowd.
  • Parque Nacional Cerros de Amotape: A stunning national park with diverse ecosystems and wildlife, including dry forest, cloud forest, and mangroves.
  • Catarata de Huancabamba: This gorgeous waterfall is hidden in the hills outside of Piura, accessible via a scenic hiking trail.
  • Reserva Ecológica de Chaparri: A protected natural area home to numerous endangered species, including the Spectacled Bear and White-winged Guan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Piura

  • Bosque de El Algarrobo: A beautiful, shady forest filled with Algarrobo trees, perfect for hiking, birdwatching, and picnicking.
  • Lomas de Lachay: This unique ecosystem features a variety of desert plants and flowers, blooming in the mist created by Pacific Ocean fog.
  • Parque Nacional Cerros de Amotape: In addition to its breathtaking views and diverse flora and fauna, this national park offers numerous hiking trails of varying difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Piura: Activities and Tours

  • Take a surf lesson in Mancora
  • Join a birdwatching tour in Chaparri
  • Visit Piura’s central market for locally-made crafts and goods
  • Take a guided tour of Mural de las Tradiciones, a colorful mural depicting local history and culture
  • Enjoy a swim in one of Piura’s hot thermal springs

Week-Long Itinerary for Piura

Day 1: Explore the colonial architecture of Piura, including the Plaza de Armas and Cathedral of Piura. Day 2: Take a day trip to nearby Huancabamba to visit the stunning Catarata de Huancabamba waterfall. Day 3: Spend a beach day in Mancora, trying out the waves and enjoying the international vibe. Day 4: Visit the Museo Regional de Piura and Casa Museo del Almirante Miguel Grau to learn about the local history and culture. Day 5: Take a day trip to Parque Nacional Cerros de Amotape, exploring its diverse ecosystems and spotting local wildlife. Day 6: Hike or bike through Bosque de El Algarrobo, then cool off with a dip in one of Piura’s hot springs. Day 7: Spend the day shopping for locally-made crafts and souvenirs at Piura’s central market.

Food and Drink Scene of Piura

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seco de Cabrito: A delicious lamb stew, often served with rice and beans.
  • Cebiche de Piura: A regional variation on Peru’s most famous dish, featuring sweet potato and white fish marinated in lime juice.
  • Chifles: Thinly sliced, seasoned plantain chips, often served as a side or snack.
  • Turrón: A sweet, nutty dessert made with honey and almonds.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha de Jora: A fermented corn drink, popular throughout Peru.
  • Pisco Sour: Peru’s national cocktail, made with pisco brandy, lime juice, and egg whites.
  • Cusqueña: A popular Peruvian beer, widely available in Piura and throughout the country.

Where to Stay in Piura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Costa del Sol Wyndham Piura: A luxurious four-star hotel with spacious rooms, pool, and onsite restaurant and bar.
  • Intiotel Piura: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, pool, and gym.
  • Piura Hostel: A budget-friendly option with clean shared rooms, hot showers, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Piura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Alpaca wool products, such as sweaters, hats, and gloves
  • Locally-made ceramics and pottery
  • Hand-woven textiles and fabrics, including traditional textiles from the nearby town of Chulucanas
  • Coffee and chocolate made in the nearby province of Jaen

Local Culture and Customs of Piura

Piura is famous for its vibrant festivals and celebrations, particularly during the months of June and July. These include the Festival de San Juan, the Festival de las Almas, and the Festival de los Negritos de Huánuco, which celebrates the city’s Afro-Peruvian heritage.

Best Time to Visit Piura

The best time to visit Piura is during the dry season, from May to October. While temperatures can be scorching during the day, it’s a great time for outdoor activities and exploring the region’s natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Piura

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including regional dishes and delicacies like ceviche and turrón.
  • Respect the local culture, particularly during religious festivals and celebrations.
  • Take advantage of the dry weather to explore Piura’s outdoor attractions and natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the strength of the sun - bring plenty of sunscreen and stay hydrated.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English - it’s helpful to know a few basic phrases in Spanish.
  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Piura

While Piura is generally a safe and welcoming city, it’s always a good idea to take precautions while traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night or in crowded areas, and keep your valuables secured.

Health in Piura

If you’re traveling to Piura from outside South America, it’s recommended to get vaccinated for yellow fever and typhoid. Drink bottled water and avoid ice or uncooked foods that may have been washed in local water. It’s also a good idea to bring a basic first aid kit and any necessary medications with you on your travels.

Getting to Piura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Piura

  • Capitán FAP Guillermo Concha Iberico International Airport (PIU)

Bus Stations Near Piura

  • Terminal Terrestre de Piura

Other Options

  • Taxi or private car service
  • Public transportation, including buses and mototaxis

How to Get Around in Piura

The easiest way to get around Piura is by taxi or mototaxi, though it’s important to agree on a price before starting your journey. Alternatively, you can explore the city and its surroundings on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Piura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chiclayo: A vibrant city and cultural center, known for its archaeological sites and museums.
  • Trujillo: A city with rich colonial history and stunning architectural sites, such as Chan Chan and the Huaca de la Luna.
  • Tumbes: A beach destination near the border with Ecuador, featuring gorgeous coastline and excellent seafood.

Conclusion

Piura is a unique and fascinating destination that boasts culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring its rich history, hitting the beach, or simply relaxing in a hot spring, Piura has something to offer every kind of traveler. Through its delicious cuisine, vibrant festivals, and welcoming locals, it’s sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors from near and far.
