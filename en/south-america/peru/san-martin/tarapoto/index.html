Introduction to Tarapoto

Nestled deep in the Peruvian jungle, lies the charming town of Tarapoto. Far away from the hustle and bustle of big cities, Tarapoto is a peaceful oasis surrounded by stunning natural beauty. With lush rainforests, majestic rivers, and rugged mountains, this town is the perfect destination for nature-loving travelers seeking adventure.

In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at Tarapoto’s most popular attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Tarapoto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museo Regional de la Universidad Nacional de San Martin - showcasing the region’s art, archeology, and culture.

Casa de la Cultura de Tarapoto - featuring exhibitions, classes, and workshops related to arts and culture.

Iglesia San Antonio de Padua - a stunning colonial-era church worth visiting for its impressive architecture and intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Tarapoto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Catarata de Ahuashiyacu - a stunning waterfall surrounded by lush forest and wildlife.

Lago Lindo - a beautiful lake popular for water activities such as canoeing, kayaking and swimming.

Cerro Escalera - a natural reserve known for its scenic trails, abundant wildlife, and breathtaking views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarapoto

Huayco and Castillo Trail - a 6 km trail that leads to the top of a hill with stunning views of the city and surrounding jungle.

Alto Shilcayo Trail - a 7 km trail that takes you through the lush forest and to a beautiful waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Tarapoto: Activities and Tours

Go River Rafting - experience the thrill of rapids while surrounded by the scenic beauty of the jungle.

Go Bird Watching - with over 300 species of birds, Tarapoto is a haven for bird enthusiasts.

Take a Coffee Tour - visit the coffee plantations to explore the process of coffee making and sample the different varieties of coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarapoto

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Tarapoto, explore the city, and enjoy local cuisine
Day 2 Visit the Museo Regional de la Universidad Nacional de San Martin and the Casa de la Cultura de Tarapoto
Day 3 Explore Cerro Escalera and enjoy hiking
Day 4 Take a coffee tour and learn about coffee making
Day 5 Raft down the Rio Mayo river and enjoy the rapids
Day 6 Visit the Catarata de Ahuashiyacu waterfall and Lago Lindo
Day 7 Depart from Tarapoto

Food and Drink Scene of Tarapoto

Must-Try Dishes

Juane - a dish made of rice, chicken, egg, and spices, wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.

Tacacho con Cecina - roasted plantains with grilled pork and salad.

Inchicapi - tender chicken in a creamy sauce made from peanuts, cilantro, and yucca.

Local Beverages

Masato - a fermented drink made from yucca and maize.

Coca Tea - a traditional tea made from the leaves of the coca plant.

Chicha de Jora - a traditional maize beer.

Where to Stay in Tarapoto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Casa de La Abuela - a charming guesthouse located within walking distance of all the main attractions.

Rio Sol Tarapoto Hotel - a luxurious hotel with stunning views of the Rio Mayo river.

Pumarinri Amazon Jungle Lodge - an eco-friendly lodge located in the heart of the jungle.

What to Buy in Tarapoto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Martin Coffee - Sample the excellent range of coffee grown in the region.

Crafts and textiles - browse through the local markets for handicrafts and textiles made by the indigenous people.

Amazonian fruits and preserved fish - take some of the delicious and exotic fruits and fish home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarapoto

Tarapoto has a rich and diverse cultural heritage and is home to many indigenous communities. Respect for cultural differences and customs is essential when visiting this region. Engage with the locals to learn more about their way of life, language, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Tarapoto

The best time to visit Tarapoto is from April to November, during the dry season. However, even in the rainy season, Tarapoto has great weather and is a perfect destination year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarapoto

Do:

Respect the local customs and traditions.

Carry insect repellent and sunblock.

Try the local cuisine and coffee.

Don’t:

Perform any activity that may harm the environment.

Use drugs.

Disrespect the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarapoto

Tarapoto is a safe town, but it is always important to take basic precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Tarapoto

It is recommended to take necessary vaccinations against yellow fever, rabies, hepatitis A and B before traveling to Tarapoto. Carry a mosquito repellent cream and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Tarapoto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarapoto

Cad. FAP Guillermo del Castillo Paredes Airport - the nearest airport, located just 5km outside the town.

Bus Stations Near Tarapoto

Estación Terrestre de Tarapoto - located near the center of the town for easy access to the main attractions.

Other Options

Taxis, mototaxis and Uber are also available for transportation within the town.

How to Get Around in Tarapoto

Travel around Tarapoto is easy with buses, taxis, and mototaxis readily available.

Where to Go Next from Tarapoto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chachapoyas - Explore the ancient ruins and scenic beauty of the Amazonian region.

Kuelap - A historical fortress located in the Andean mountains.

Yurimaguas - A town located on the bank of the Huallaga River.

Conclusion

Tarapoto is a beautiful and mystical destination in Peru, with an abundance of natural and cultural wonders waiting to be explored. From luxurious hotels to eco-lodges, exciting outdoor activities to delicious food, there is something for every type of traveler here. We hope our comprehensive guide has helped you to plan your Tarapoto adventure.
