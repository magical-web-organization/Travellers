Introduction to Sauce

Sauce, located in the north-central region of Peru, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. While many travelers tend to focus their attention on the more popular and bustling cities like Lima and Cusco, Sauce is a quieter, off-the-beaten-path destination that’s perfect for those who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. With its beautiful natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming locals, Sauce is an ideal destination for anyone looking to experience true Peruvian hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Sauce

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sauce is rich in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that you won’t want to miss. Some of the most notable include:

  • Casa Museo Ricardo Palma: This museum is dedicated to the renowned Peruvian writer and journalist, Ricardo Palma. Visitors can explore the writer’s former home while learning about his life and works.
  • Templo de San Martin de Tours: This 16th-century church is a beautiful example of colonial architecture and features stunning frescoes and elaborate altar pieces.
  • Iglesia Santa Cruz: Another historic church that’s well worth a visit, the Iglesia Santa Cruz dates back to the 18th century and is famous for its intricate wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Sauce: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for adventure and natural beauty, Sauce has plenty to offer. Some of the top outdoor attractions include:

  • Parque Nacional Cordillera Azul: This sprawling national park is a nature lover’s paradise, with rugged mountains, deep canyons, and dense rainforests that are home to a variety of wildlife, including tapirs, jaguars, and monkeys.
  • Rio Huallaga: Known for its crystal-clear waters and scenic beauty, the Rio Huallaga is a popular spot for kayaking, rafting, and other water-based activities.
  • Mirador del Cerro de Carmen: This lookout point offers stunning panoramic views of the city of Sauce and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sauce

For those who love to explore on foot or by bike, Sauce has plenty of options. Some of the best routes include:

  • Cerro Cullicocha Trail: This challenging hiking trail takes you up to the summit of Cerro Cullicocha, where you’ll be rewarded with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Rio Huallaga Trail: This easy bike ride takes you along the banks of the Rio Huallaga, through lush forests and past stunning waterfalls.
  • Pumarinri Trail: This multi-day hiking trail is the perfect adventure for those who want to experience the best of Sauce’s natural beauty. The trail winds through rugged mountains, deep canyons, and dense forests, offering stunning views at every turn.

Best Things to Do in Sauce: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, there’s no shortage of things to do in Sauce. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Coffee Plantation Tour: Peru is known for its high-quality coffee, and a visit to one of Sauce’s coffee plantations is a must for any caffeine lover.
  • Amazon River Cruise: Take a boat tour along the Amazon River and explore the region’s rich biodiversity, including exotic birds, caimans, and pink river dolphins.
  • Tucan Trail Tour: This comprehensive tour takes you through some of the most beautiful and culturally significant places in Peru, including Sauce, Tarapoto, and Machu Picchu.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sauce

If you have a week to spare, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Sauce:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic center of Sauce, including the Templo de San Martin de Tours and the Iglesia Santa Cruz.
  • Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour through the Rio Huallaga Trail.
  • Day 3: Visit the Casa Museo Ricardo Palma and learn about the life and works of the renowned Peruvian writer.
  • Day 4: Take a coffee plantation tour and sample some of Peru’s finest coffee.
  • Day 5: Take a relaxing Amazon River cruise and enjoy the region’s rich biodiversity.
  • Day 6: Visit the Parque Nacional Cordillera Azul and marvel at its stunning natural beauty.
  • Day 7: Take a Tucan Trail tour and explore some of the most culturally significant places in Peru, including Tarapoto and Machu Picchu.

Food and Drink Scene of Sauce

Must-Try Dishes

Peruvian cuisine is world-renowned for its complex and flavorful dishes, and Sauce is no exception. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Causa Rellena: This dish consists of layers of mashed potatoes stuffed with various fillings, including avocado, tuna, and chicken.
  • Anticuchos: These skewered meat dishes are marinated in a spicy sauce and are a popular street food in Peru.
  • Chupe de Camarones: This hearty shrimp soup is a staple of Peruvian cuisine and is made with potatoes, corn, and spices.

Local Beverages

Peru is known for its excellent wines and spirits, and Sauce is no exception. Some of the most popular local beverages include:

  • Chicha de Jora: This fermented corn drink is a staple of Peruvian cuisine and is often served at festivals and celebrations.
  • Pisco Sour: This classic cocktail is made with pisco (a clear grape brandy), lime juice, and egg whites, and is a must-try for any visitor to Peru.
  • Inca Kola: This bright yellow soda is a beloved Peruvian icon and is known for its sweet, bubblegum-like flavor.

Where to Stay in Sauce: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sauce has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget and travel style. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • El Sauce Resort: This luxurious resort offers stunning views of the surrounding jungle and mountains, as well as a range of activities and amenities for guests.
  • La Casa de La Abuela: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Sauce and is known for its warm hospitality and charming decor.
  • Hostal Sauce: This affordable hostel is a great option for budget travelers and offers clean, comfortable accommodations in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Sauce: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Sauce, there are plenty of unique and traditional souvenirs and local products to choose from. Some of the most popular include:

  • Alpaca Wool Clothing: Alpaca wool is a staple of Peruvian textiles and is warm, durable, and soft. You can find a variety of alpaca wool clothing items in Sauce, including sweaters, scarves, and hats.
  • Ceramics: Sauce is known for its traditional ceramics, including colorful bowls, plates, and vases.
  • Coffee: Peru is famous for its high-quality coffee, and a bag of locally roasted beans is the perfect souvenir for any coffee lover.

Local Culture and Customs of Sauce

Peruvian culture is rich and diverse, with its own unique customs and traditions. Some of the key cultural experiences to look out for in Sauce include:

  • Festivals and Celebrations: Peru is home to a wide variety of festivals and celebrations throughout the year, and Sauce is no exception. Some of the most popular festivals include the Feast of San Martin de Tours in November and the Fiesta de la Cosecha in August.
  • Traditional Clothing: In many parts of Peru, including Sauce, traditional clothing is still worn by locals on a daily basis. Look out for brightly colored woven textiles and embroidered details.
  • Music and Dance: Peruvian music and dance are an integral part of the country’s culture, and you’re likely to hear lively music and see dancers in traditional dress throughout Sauce.

Best Time to Visit Sauce

The best time to visit Sauce is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is generally warm and sunny, and there’s less chance of rain. Keep in mind, however, that the high season for tourism in Peru is from June to August, so prices may be higher and popular attractions may be crowded during this time.

Getting to Sauce: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sauce

The nearest airport to Sauce is Cadete FAP Guillermo del Castillo Paredes Airport in Tarapoto, which is located approximately 60 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Sauce.

Bus Stations Near Sauce

Sauce has its own bus terminal, which offers regular services to and from nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

There are also private transfer services available from Tarapoto to Sauce, which can be arranged in advance through most travel agencies.

How to Get Around in Sauce

The best way to get around Sauce is on foot or by bike, as many of the main attractions are located within walking or biking distance of each other. Taxis are also available, but be sure to negotiate the fare in advance to avoid overpaying.
