Introduction to Juanjuí

Welcome to Juanjuí, a city located in the San Martín region of Peru in South America. This city is known for being a commercial center for the Amazon region, and its location at the foothills of the Andes Mountains provides unique landscapes to explore. Whether you are a nature enthusiast, history lover, or adventure-seeker, Juanjuí has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Juanjuí

Here is a list of must-visit places in Juanjuí:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santa Cruz de los Motilones: It is a historical site located in the vicinity of Juanjuí, where the remains of an ancient pre-Inca culture can be found.
  • Museo de Arqueología de Juanjuí: This museum is the perfect place to learn about the rich history of Juanjuí and the surrounding San Martín region.
  • Plaza de Armas de Juanjuí: This is the main square of the city, where you can observe the colonial-style architecture typical of the cities of the Andes.
  • Iglesia Matriz de San Juan Bautista: This church is an architectural jewel that dates back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Juanjuí: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Catarata del Diablo: A majestic 35-meter high waterfall, located less than an hour’s drive from the city, surrounded by lush vegetation.
  • Lomas de Los Caciques: These hills are known for their spectacular views of the Andes Mountains and are an ideal location for hiking and birdwatching.
  • Parque Nacional Cordillera Azul: This park is a massive protected area of ​​deserts, Andes Mountains, and Amazonian rainforests covering over 10,000 square kilometers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Juanjuí

For outdoor enthusiasts, Juanjuí offers many hiking and biking trails that offer some of the most stunning views in Peru.

  • Lomas de Los Caciques: As mentioned before, Lomas de Los Caciques is a perfect place for hiking while enjoying the scenic views of the Andes Mountains.
  • El Mirador de Bello Horizonte: This route offers some of the best views of the city, and although it is a bit steep, it is suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

Best Things to Do in Juanjuí: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Juanjuí:

  • Visit the local markets: Juanjuí is where the locals come to shop for goods; that’s why visiting local markets is a must-do activity.
  • Take a coffee tour: San Martín is known for producing some of the best coffee beans globally, and in Juanjuí, you can experience a complete coffee tour and learn how coffee is made, from bean to cup.
  • Go on a river adventure: With the Huallaga River running through the city, there are a variety of water activities to enjoy, from canoeing and kayaking to fishing and swimming.

Food and Drink Scene of Juanjuí

Juanjuí is known for its traditional cuisine, which features a variety of fresh ingredients from the Amazon region.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacacho con Cecina: This dish consists of mashed green banana and dried pork, and it is one of the most popular dishes in the region.
  • Juanes: A dish made with rice, chicken, and a variety of species wrapped in a banana leaf.
  • Patarashca: A fish dish, seasoned with lemon, cilantro and wrapped in a bijao leaf.

Local Beverages

  • Masato: A traditional alcoholic beverage made with fermented cassava.
  • Coca tea: A tea made from the coca leaves that have long been a part of the Andean culture and history.

Where to Stay in Juanjuí: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some recommended places to stay in Juanjuí:

  • Garden Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Hostal Real J: A budget-friendly option located near the central square of the city.
  • Mirador del Cerro: A guesthouse located in the hills, offering breathtaking views of the city.

What to Buy in Juanjuí: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs to bring home as a reminder of your stay in Juanjuí:

  • Coffee beans: The region is renowned for its coffee production, and bringing home quality coffee beans is a must.
  • Amazonian craftwork: You’ll find unique handicrafts such as woven baskets, wood carvings and ceramics.
  • Traditional garb: Traditional outfits such as ponchos and shawls with unique regional patterns make for a memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Juanjuí

The people of Juanjuí are warm, friendly, and proud of their cultural heritage. Here are some customs and traditions to learn about:

  • Music and dance: Folk music and dance performances are prevalent in Juanjuí, with a perfect mix of indigenous, African, and Spanish cultural influences.
  • Religion: The dominant religion of the region is Roman Catholic, so expect to see several religious celebrations throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Juanjuí

The best time to visit Juanjuí is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. These months offer the most pleasant weather for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Juanjuí

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Juanjuí is known for its culinary tradition, so be sure to sample the local dishes.
  • Respect the local culture and customs: The Peruvian people are proud of their culture and customs, so observe and respect them during your stay.
  • Carry cash: Many businesses in the region do not accept credit cards, so it is best to carry cash with you.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals: Peruvians are welcoming; always be respectful of their traditions, beliefs, and customs.
  • Assume everyone speaks English: Most locals don’t speak English, so it’s a great idea to learn some basic Spanish phrases before traveling to Juanjuí.
  • Travel alone at night: Like many cities, it is safer to travel with a friend or group at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Juanjuí

As with any tourist destination, it’s best to be cautious when traveling to Juanjuí. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of petty crime: Petty theft and pickpocketing occur occasionally in busy tourist areas; stay vigilant and keep valuable items secure.
  • Avoid being too flashy: avoid wearing expensive clothing/jewelry and carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Stick to well-lit areas at night: stick to well-lit and busy areas if you venture out at night.

Health in Juanjuí

The San Martín region is not endemic to malaria or yellow fever, but the mosquito-borne diseases can still be present in certain areas. Therefore, taking proper precautions is recommended.

Getting to Juanjuí: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Juanjuí

The nearest airport to Juanjuí is Tarapoto’s Cadete FAP Guillermo del Castillo Paredes Airport, located around 174 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Juanjuí

Juanjuí has a central bus terminal, where most of the inter-city bus services arrive and depart.

Other Options

There are no railways that directly service Juanjuí. Still, it is possible to take a train to a nearby city and then take a bus to reach Juanjuí.

How to Get Around in Juanjuí

Taxis, buses, and tricycles are the primary modes of public transportation in Juanjuí, with buses being the cheapest option in the city.

Where to Go Next from Juanjuí: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are three recommended destinations near Juanjuí for an extended trip:

  • Tarapoto: This bustling city is known for its excellent coffee and cocoa beans. Its proximity to Juanjuí makes it a perfect escape.
  • Chachapoyas: A city surrounded by stunning natural beauty, historic ruins and located only a short flight away from Juanjuí.
  • Lamas: This city, located on the Andes Mountains, is a beautiful destination known for its colonial architecture, handicrafts, and hot springs.

Conclusion

Juanjuí is a hidden gem in Peru and offers unique outdoor experiences, rich cultural heritage, fantastic cuisine, and warm hospitality. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Juanjuí. Enjoy your travels!
