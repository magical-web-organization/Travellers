Caballococha is a hidden gem located in the Peruvian Amazon. This remote town is known for its picturesque views of the Amazon River and its abundance of outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Caballococha. From the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more, you’ll have everything you need to make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Caballococha

Caballococha is located in the northernmost region of the Peruvian Amazon, near the border of Brazil and Colombia. The town is named after the nearby lagoon, which translates to “horse lake” in Spanish. The town was established as a river port in the early 20th century and has since become a popular tourist destination. Caballococha is known for its stunning natural scenery, diverse wildlife, and vibrant local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Caballococha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Regional de Caballococha: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Peruvian Amazon. Here, you can learn about the indigenous people of the region, the rubber boom, and the first settlers of Caballococha.
  • Casa de la Cultura: This cultural center hosts art exhibits, live performances, and workshops that showcase the local culture.

Natural Attractions of Caballococha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amazon River: Take a boat ride along the Amazon River and take in the stunning views of the surrounding rainforest.
  • Reserva Nacional Pacaya-Samiria: This national reserve is one of the largest protected areas in Peru. Explore the pristine rainforest, observe exotic wildlife and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking and fishing.
  • Lago de Caballococha: This lagoon is the largest freshwater lake in the Peruvian Amazon and is home to an abundance of aquatic fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caballococha

  • Paseo de los Otorongos Trail: This trail takes you through lush rainforest and offers the opportunity to spot exotic wildlife such as the otorongo (jaguar). The trail is approximately 5 km and takes about 2 hours to complete.
  • Mirador Trail: This trail leads to a lookout point with scenic views of the Amazon River and the surrounding rainforest. The trail is approximately 3 km and takes about an hour to complete.

Best Things to Do in Caballococha: Activities and Tours

  • Boat trips: Take a boat trip along the Amazon River and explore the surrounding rainforest. You can choose from a variety of boat tours ranging from a few hours to several days.
  • Fishing: Enjoy a relaxing day of fishing on the Amazon River or Lago de Caballococha. You can catch a variety of fish such as piranhas, catfish and dorado.
  • Kayaking: Explore the waterways of the Peruvian Amazon on a guided kayaking tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Caballococha

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juane: This traditional Amazonian dish consists of rice, chicken, and spices wrapped in a bijao leaf and boiled.
  • Tacacho con cecina: Mashed plantains served with grilled pork and chorizo.
  • Sopa de Motelo: A traditional soup made with fish, yucca, and plantains.

Local Beverages

  • Masato: A fermented drink made from cassava.
  • Chuchuhuasi: A liquor made from the bark of the chuchuhuasi tree.

Where to Stay in Caballococha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Amazonian Lodge: This lodge is located on the banks of the Amazon River and offers comfortable rooms and a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Casa Hotel Caballococha: This hotel is located in the center of town and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • Casa Maravilla: This guesthouse offers private rooms and is located near Lago de Caballococha.

What to Buy in Caballococha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts: Artisans in Caballococha make a variety of handicrafts such as woven baskets, pottery and carvings.
  • Traditional clothing: You can also find traditional clothing such as ponchos and hats made from natural fibers.

Local Culture and Customs of Caballococha

The people of Caballococha are proud of their culture and traditions. They celebrate many local festivals throughout the year, such as the Feast of San Juan, which is celebrated in June. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, but it is important to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Caballococha

The best time to visit Caballococha is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. This is the best time to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caballococha

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take a boat tour along the Amazon River.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Throw trash in the river or on the streets.
  • Ignore the advice of your guide when on outdoor tours.
  • Take photos of the locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caballococha

Caballococha is a safe destination, but it is important to take precautions when traveling in the Amazon rainforest. Always travel with a guide and follow their instructions. Also avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in Caballococha

It is essential to take malaria pills before your trip to Caballococha. Also, it is important to take precautions against mosquito bites, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Caballococha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caballococha

The nearest major airport to Caballococha is the Coronel FAP Alfredo Mendívil Duarte Airport (AYP) in Ayacucho.

Bus Stations Near Caballococha

There are several bus companies that operate routes to Caballococha from cities such as Iquitos, Lima and Pucallpa.

Other Options

You can also reach Caballococha by taking a boat from Iquitos or Pucallpa along the Amazon River.

How to Get Around in Caballococha

The best way to get around Caballococha is on foot or by boat. You can also rent a bicycle to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Caballococha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Iquitos: The city of Iquitos is located approximately 400 km away and is known for its historic buildings, museums and vibrant nightlife.
  • Leticia, Colombia: The Colombian town of Leticia is located approximately 200 km away and offers a variety of outdoor activities such as birdwatching and jungle treks.

Conclusion

Caballococha is a beautiful and unique destination that offers a blend of cultural experiences and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re exploring the rainforest, taking a boat trip along the Amazon River, or trying the local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Caballococha.
