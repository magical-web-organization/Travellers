Introduction to Chachapoyas

Nestled in the Peruvian Amazon, Chachapoyas is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and adventurous outdoor activities, this charming town is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience.

List of Places to Visit in Chachapoyas

If you’re planning a trip to Chachapoyas, here’s a list of the top attractions and must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kuelap Fortress: This ancient fortress dates back to the 6th century and is considered to be one of the most significant pre-Columbian ruins in South America.
  • Revash Mausoleums: These tombs, located in a cliffside, are adorned with colorful geometric designs and are a testament to the Chachapoyas culture.
  • Leymebamba Museum: This museum houses artifacts from the Chachapoyas civilization, including mummies and textiles.

Natural Attractions of Chachapoyas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gocta Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is one of the highest in the world, and you can reach it by hiking through the lush jungle.
  • The Gran Vilaya: This natural reserve is home to diverse wildlife and ancient ruins.
  • Yumbilla Waterfall: This lesser-known waterfall is just as spectacular as Gocta, but it’s a bit of a hidden gem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chachapoyas

Chachapoyas is a paradise for hikers and bikers, thanks to its diverse terrain and stunning views. Some popular routes include:

  • The Gocta Waterfall Trail: This moderate hike takes you through the jungle to the base of the stunning Gocta Waterfall.
  • The Gran Vilaya Circuit: This challenging hike takes you through the Gran Vilaya natural reserve and allows you to explore the ancient ruins of the Chachapoyas civilization.
  • The Quiocta Caverns Route: This easy hike takes you to the Quiocta Caverns, home to stunning rock formations and underground rivers.

Best Things to Do in Chachapoyas: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Chachapoyas. Here are some of our favorite activities:

  • Go on a canopy walk: The tree canopy walk in the Gocta Cataracts Park offers stunning views of the surrounding rainforest.
  • Take a horseback ride: Explore the rugged terrain of Chachapoyas on horseback and experience a unique perspective of the landscape.
  • Visit a coffee plantation: Tour a local coffee plantation and learn how coffee is grown and processed.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chachapoyas

If you have a week to explore Chachapoyas, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Chachapoyas and explore the town’s charming architecture and markets.
  • Day 2: Visit Kuelap Fortress, one of the most important archaeological sites in Peru.
  • Day 3: Hike to Gocta Waterfall and enjoy a picnic lunch overlooking the cascading water.
  • Day 4: Visit the Leymebamba Museum and learn about the Chachapoyas civilization.
  • Day 5: Take a horseback riding tour and explore the nearby countryside.
  • Day 6: Go on a canopy walk and visit a local coffee plantation.
  • Day 7: Depart from Chachapoyas.

Food and Drink Scene of Chachapoyas

Peruvian cuisine is world-renowned, and Chachapoyas offers unique culinary experiences that blend local flavors with Spanish influences.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuy: This traditional delicacy consists of roasted or fried guinea pig and is a common dish in the Chachapoyas region.
  • Juanes de Yuca: These delicious tamales are made with yucca root, rice, meat, and spices.
  • Tacacho con Cecina: This hearty dish features mashed plantains, grilled pork, and spicy salsa.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This fermented corn drink is a traditional Peruvian beverage and is commonly served in the Chachapoyas region.
  • Pisco Sour: Peru’s national drink is a cocktail made with Pisco, lime juice, syrup, and egg whites.

Where to Stay in Chachapoyas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chachapoyas offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel La Casona de Yma Sumac: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Chachapoyas and offers comfortable rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Hostal Tambo Colorado: This budget-friendly hostel is located just a few blocks from the main square and offers basic amenities at an affordable price.
  • Gocta Lodge: This eco-friendly lodge is located near the Gocta Waterfall and features comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Chachapoyas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring home from Chachapoyas, here are some suggestions:

  • Handwoven textiles: The Chachapoya culture is known for its intricate textiles, and you can find beautiful rugs, blankets, and clothing in local markets.
  • Coffee: Chachapoyas is home to many coffee plantations, and you can find locally roasted beans to bring home.
  • Hand-carved gourds: Artisans carve intricate designs into dried gourds, creating unique and beautiful works of art.

Local Culture and Customs of Chachapoyas

The Chachapoya culture has a rich history, and you can experience its traditions and customs during your visit. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for elders: The Chachapoya people place a high value on respecting and caring for their elders.
  • Festivals and celebrations: Chachapoyas is known for its colorful festivals, which often involve parades, music, and traditional clothing.
  • Offerings to Pachamama: The Chachapoya people pay tribute to the earth goddess Pachamama, and you can see offerings and rituals throughout the region.

Best Time to Visit Chachapoyas

The best time to visit Chachapoyas is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, you can enjoy clear skies and pleasant temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chachapoyas

To make the most of your visit to Chachapoyas, here are some important do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions of the region.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious or cultural sites.
  • Use single-use plastic, as it’s not sustainable for the environment.

Safety and Health

While Chachapoyas is generally a safe and welcoming destination, it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of potential risks.

Safety in Chachapoyas

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Stay on designated hiking trails and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Chachapoyas

  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach discomfort.
  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases.
  • Consult your doctor before traveling to Chachapoyas, especially if you have health concerns.

Getting to Chachapoyas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chachapoyas is located about 550 miles north of Lima, and there are several transportation options to reach the town.

Airports Near Chachapoyas

The nearest airport to Chachapoyas is the FAP Padre Jose Aldamiz International Airport in Jaen, about 93 miles from Chachapoyas. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to reach Chachapoyas.

Bus Stations Near Chachapoyas

There are several bus companies that offer routes to Chachapoyas from Lima, Trujillo, and other nearby cities. The journey can take anywhere from 12 to 24 hours, depending on your starting point.

Other Options

You can also hire a driver or rent a car to reach Chachapoyas from nearby cities. However, be aware that the roads can be winding and sometimes challenging to navigate.

How to Get Around in Chachapoyas

Chachapoyas is a small town, and many of the attractions are located within walking distance of the city center. However, you might need transportation to reach some of the more remote natural attractions. Some options include:

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available and affordable in Chachapoyas, and you can negotiate prices with the driver.
  • Guided tours: Some attractions, such as Kuelap and Gocta, require a guided tour to enter, and transportation is often included.

Where to Go Next from Chachapoyas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Peruvian Amazon: Chachapoyas is a gateway to the Amazon rainforest, and you can arrange a tour to explore this incredible ecosystem.
  • Trujillo: This vibrant city is known for its colonial architecture, museums, and beaches, and is about 13 hours by bus from Chachapoyas.
  • Huaraz: This mountain town is a hub for outdoor activities such as trekking and mountaineering and is about 16 hours by bus from Chachapoyas.

Conclusion

Chachapoyas is a hidden gem in the Peruvian Amazon that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. From the stunning natural attractions to the rich cultural heritage and culinary delights, this charming town offers something for everyone.
