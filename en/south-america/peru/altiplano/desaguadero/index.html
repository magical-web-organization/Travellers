Desaguadero Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Desaguadero, Peru

Nestled in the heart of the Altiplano region of Peru, Desaguadero is the gateway to the Bolivian border. It’s a vibrant town with rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. From ancient museums to breathtaking sites and lively markets, Desaguadero offers a unique and authentic Peruvian experience. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Desaguadero, Peru.

List of Places to Visit in Desaguadero

Desaguadero offers something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bolivian-Peruvian Border - A landmark that separates the two countries and offers stunning panoramic views
  • Museum of Pre-Columbian Art and Culture - A museum that showcases ancient artifacts and cultural pieces from the Inca civilization
  • The Church of San Antonio de Padua - A historic church that dates back to the colonial era

Natural Attractions of Desaguadero: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Titicaca - The highest navigable lake in the world that offers stunning views and unique cultural experiences
  • Puno’s Floating Islands - Man-made islands that float on the surface of Lake Titicaca, constructed by the Uros people
  • The Chullpas - Ancient burial towers that offer scenic views of the surrounding area

Hiking/Biking Routes of Desaguadero

Desaguadero is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Huayllata Circuit - A circuit that offers stunning views of the Andes and the lake, with trails that run up to 4800 meters above sea level
  • The Inti Raymi Trail - A scenic trail that reveals the ancient Inca civilization sites and ends in Cusco
  • Colca Canyon - A breathtaking canyon that offers views of the Andes and the Colca River, perfect for adventurous hikers

Best Things to Do in Desaguadero: Activities and Tours

Desguadero offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best:

  • Uros Islands Tour - A boat tour that takes you to the man-made floating islands of the Uros people
  • Chucuito and Pomata Tour - A tour that takes you to the colonial towns of Chucuito and Pomata to see their unique architecture and cultural heritage
  • Inca Trail to Machu Picchu - A guided trek that takes you through the ancient sites of the Inca civilization, ending at the famous Machu Picchu

Week-Long Itinerary for Desaguadero

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival and settling in, exploring Desaguadero town
Day 2 Visit the Museum of Pre-Columbian Art and Culture, and the Church of San Antonio de Padua
Day 3 Uros Islands Tour and Lake Titicaca
Day 4 Chucuito and Pomata Tour

Day 6 Visit The Bolivian-Peruvian Border and the Chullpas
Day 7 Departure and onward travel

Food and Drink Scene of Desaguadero

Desaguadero has a rich food and drink scene, with plenty of local delicacies to try. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuy al Horno - A roasted guinea pig dish, common in the Andean region
  • Pachamanca - A traditional dish made with meat and vegetables, cooked underground with hot stones
  • Chairo Soup - A hearty soup made with potatoes, meat, and spices

Local Beverages

  • Mate de Coca - A tea made from the coca leaf, commonly used for its medicinal properties
  • Chicha Morada - A sweet and refreshing drink made from purple corn
  • Pisco Sour - A classic Peruvian cocktail made with pisco, lime juice, simple syrup, and egg whites

Where to Stay in Desaguadero: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Desaguadero offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some that we recommend:

  • Hotel Hacienda Puno - A luxury hotel with stunning lake views and traditional Peruvian decor
  • Hostal los Uros - A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable rooms and breakfast
  • Casa Andina Premium Puno - A mid-range hotel that offers great amenities and stunning views of Lake Titicaca

What to Buy in Desaguadero: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Desaguadero is known for its markets, where you can find local handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some items to look out for:

  • Alpaca clothing - A soft and warm fabric made from the wool of the alpaca, a South American camelid
  • Textiles and weavings - Colorful textiles and fabrics made by local artisans
  • Pottery and ceramics - Handmade pottery and ceramics that showcase the unique cultural heritage of Desaguadero

Local Culture and Customs of Desaguadero

Desaguadero has a rich cultural heritage that is visible in its architecture, festivals, and traditions. Here are some customs to be aware of:

  • Pachamanca ritual - A traditional pre-Columbian ritual that involves cooking food underground with hot stones
  • Carnival de Tinke - A colorful festival that takes place in February and celebrates the Andean traditions and culture
  • Inti Raymi - An ancient Inca festival that celebrates the winter solstice and marks the beginning of a new year

Best Time to Visit Desaguadero

The best time to visit Desaguadero is during the dry season, which falls between May and October. This is when the weather is mild and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the town. However, the offseason months might offer more affordable lodging and less tourist crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Desaguadero

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Desaguadero:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Learn some Spanish phrases to communicate with locals
  • Try the local cuisine and drink

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their consent
  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural heritage sites
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting the church or other religious places

Safety and Health

Safety and health precautions are important when traveling to Desaguadero. Here are some tips:

Safety in Desaguadero

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure at all times
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night

Health in Desaguadero

  • Altitude sickness is a common health issue in the high altitude regions
  • Drink only bottled or boiled water
  • Protect yourself from sun exposure and wear warm clothing to prevent hypothermia in the cold weather

Getting to Desaguadero: Transportation Options and Logistics

Desaguadero is well-connected by different modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Desaguadero

  • Juliaca International Airport - Located about 45 minutes from Desaguadero and offers regular flights to Lima and other major cities in Peru.

Bus Stations Near Desaguadero

  • Desaguadero Terminal Terrestre - The main bus station in Desaguadero that offers both local and long-distance services.

Other Options

  • Taxis are readily available in Desaguadero town and can be hired for local transportation or airport transfers.

How to Get Around in Desaguadero

Desaguadero is a compact town and can be easily explored on foot. Taxis and public buses are also available for transportation to nearby attractions or destinations.

Where to Go Next from Desaguadero: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Desaguadero serves as a gateway to several nearby destinations, including:

  • Puno - A vibrant city on the shores of Lake Titicaca, known for its rich culture, dances, and festivals.
  • Cusco - The ancient capital of the Inca civilization, known for its historic sites and cultural heritage
  • La Paz - The administrative capital of Bolivia, known for its lively markets, unique architecture, and stunning scenery

Conclusion

Desaguadero is a unique and authentic Peruvian experience that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your visit to this vibrant town, and that you have an unforgettable experience in Desaguadero, Peru.
