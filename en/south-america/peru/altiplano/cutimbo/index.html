Introduction to Cutimbo

Located in the Altiplano region of Peru, Cutimbo is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. The town is known for its archaeological sites, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Cutimbo has something to offer.

In this travel guide, we’ll provide all the information you need to make the most out of your visit to this wonderful destination. From cultural attractions to natural wonders, hiking trails to local cuisine, and everything in between, our guide will take you on a journey through Cutimbo.

List of Places to Visit in Cutimbo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cutimbo Archaeological Complex: Located at an elevation of 4,200 meters above sea level, this complex is home to several pre-Inca ruins. The site includes the famous Chullpas of Cutimbo, which are circular stone towers that were used by the Colla people as burial tombs.

  • Church of San Juan Bautista: Built in the 17th century, this beautiful church features stunning architecture and religious art.

  • Museum of Cutimbo: This small museum showcases the art, history, and culture of the region, including artifacts from the Colla and Inca cultures.

Natural Attractions of Cutimbo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Titicaca: Cutimbo is just a short distance away from the world-famous Lake Titicaca, which is the highest navigable lake in the world. Visitors can take boat tours of the lake, go hiking around its shores or visit the floating islands of Uros.

  • Cutimbo Valley: Surrounded by dramatic mountains and rolling hills, the Cutimbo Valley is an excellent place for hiking or mountain biking.

  • Condoroma Overlook: If you want to take in a breathtaking vista, head to the Condoroma Overlook. This spot offers panoramic views of the Cutimbo Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cutimbo

  • Cutimbo to Iscata Trail: This trail runs through the Cutimbo Valley and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and hillsides. It’s a moderately challenging hike that takes around 5 hours to complete.

  • Cutimbo to Huayllani Trail: This is another great option for hiking enthusiasts. Along the trail, visitors will find stunning landscapes and pre-Inca ruins.

Best Things to Do in Cutimbo: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of Lake Titicaca: Take a boat tour of the world-famous Lake Titicaca and visit the floating islands of Uros.

  • Visit Local Markets: The local markets in Cutimbo offer a wide range of traditional products, from handicrafts to textiles to fresh produce.

  • Historic Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of Cutimbo and learn about the town’s rich history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Cutimbo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chupe de Quinoa: This hearty soup is made with quinoa, potatoes, and pork, seasoned with a variety of herbs and spices.

  • Cuy: Cuy, or guinea pig, is a traditional delicacy in the Andean region. It’s usually roasted or fried and served with potatoes and chili sauce.

  • Aji de Gallina: This creamy chicken dish is made with a spicy sauce of aji peppers, bread, and milk.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: Chicha is a fermented corn drink that has been a staple of Andean culture for centuries.

  • Pisco Sour: Pisco Sour is a cocktail made with pisco, lime juice, sugar, egg white, and Angostura bitters. It’s a classic Peruvian drink that’s a must-try when in Cutimbo.

Where to Stay in Cutimbo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Libertador Lago Titicaca: Located near the shores of Lake Titicaca, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and stunning views.

  • Hostal Margarita: This welcoming guesthouse is located in the heart of Cutimbo and offers clean and affordable rooms.

  • Hotel Mirador del Titicaca: This hotel is located in Puno, just a short drive away from Cutimbo. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and panoramic views of Lake Titicaca.

What to Buy in Cutimbo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Cutimbo is known for its intricate textiles, pottery, and wood carvings.

  • Alpaca Wool Products: The Andean region is famous for its alpaca wool products, such as sweaters, scarves, and blankets.

  • Coffee: The coffee from the nearby region is known for its rich and bold flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Cutimbo

The people of Cutimbo are mainly of Aymara and Quechua descent, and many still practice traditional customs and rituals. Visitors should respect local customs and seek to learn about the culture of the region. Examples of local customs include:

  • Offering coca leaves to Pachamama (Mother Earth) before starting a journey

  • Wearing traditional clothing during festivals and celebrations

Best Time to Visit Cutimbo

The best time to visit Cutimbo is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, the weather is mild and sunny, with clear blue skies. The wet season, from November to April, is characterized by heavy rain and muddy trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cutimbo

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Commit any act of cultural appropriation

  • Take photos of people without asking permission

  • Engage in any activities that harm the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Cutimbo

Cutimbo is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions to avoid petty theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Cutimbo

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude of Cutimbo (4,200 meters above sea level) and take necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness. It’s recommended to drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol and cigarettes, and take it easy for the first few days of your visit.

Getting to Cutimbo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cutimbo

The closest airport to Cutimbo is the Inca Manco Capac International Airport in Cusco, which is about 6 hours by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Cutimbo

The main bus station in Cutimbo is located in the town center, with daily departures to nearby destinations such as Puno, Arequipa, and Cusco.

Other Options

Visitors can hire taxis or private transportation to get to Cutimbo from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Cutimbo

The best way to get around Cutimbo is on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and private transportation are also available for getting around the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Cutimbo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puno: The city of Puno is just a short drive away from Cutimbo and is known for its lively festivals and the famous Lake Titicaca.

  • Arequipa: Known as the “White City”, Arequipa is a charming colonial town with beautiful architecture and delicious cuisine.

  • Colca Canyon: The Colca Canyon is one of the deepest canyons in the world and is home to stunning landscapes and traditional Andean communities.

Conclusion

Cutimbo is a hidden gem in the Altiplano region of Peru, offering a unique and unforgettable travel experience. With its rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and vibrant culture, Cutimbo has something to offer to every kind of traveler.
