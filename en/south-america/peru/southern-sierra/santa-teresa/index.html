Introduction to Santa Teresa

Nestled in the Southern Sierra region of Peru, Santa Teresa is a charming town located on the border of the Cusco and Madre de Dios regions. While it may not be the most famous tourist destination in Peru, Santa Teresa’s natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventurous spirit make it a must-visit destination. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, archaeology, or simply soaking up the local culture, Santa Teresa has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Teresa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Machu Picchu

Of course, no trip to Santa Teresa is complete without a visit to the famous ancient Incan city, Machu Picchu! The iconic ruins are located just a short train or bus ride away.

The Santa Teresa hot springs

Close to Santa Teresa town, the nearby Santa Teresa hot springs is one of the town’s most unusual and relaxing attractions. The natural hot water pools are surrounded by lush vegetation and offer a perfect escape from the bustle of the town.

Natural Attractions of Santa Teresa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Salkantay Trail

The Salkantay Trail is a stunning hike that passes through snow-capped mountains, rugged landscapes, and small Andean villages. The hike is an alternative to the more touristy Inca Trail and offers incredible views and breathtaking scenery.

Santa Teresa Canyon

Located just outside of town, the Santa Teresa Canyon is a beautiful natural attraction that offers breathtaking views and a variety of adventure activities including rafting, ziplining, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Teresa

Salkantay Trek

The five-day Salkantay Trek to Machu Picchu is a demanding but rewarding hiking route. The trek offers amazing scenery, diverse flora and fauna, and the opportunity to explore remote Andean villages.

Mountain Biking

If hiking isn’t your thing and you’re ready for some adventure, mountain biking is an ideal way to explore the Santa Teresa area. The mountains and valleys surrounding the town offer a variety of challenging routes.

Best Things to Do in Santa Teresa: Activities and Tours

Whitewater Rafting

The Santa Teresa Canyon is known for its exciting rafting. The river offers Class III and IV rapids and a memorable experience for adventure seekers.

Coffee Farm Tours

Coffee lovers should not miss a visit to the local coffee farms in the region. Visitors can learn about the coffee cultivation and processing and also enjoy a delicious cup of coffee that has been freshly roasted.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Teresa

Peruvian cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique ingredients. In Santa Teresa, visitors should try local specialties like Cuy (guinea pig) or Alpaca meat.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche
  • Lomo Saltado
  • Cuy (guinea pig)
  • Pachamanca

Local Beverages

  • Chicha Morada
  • Pisco Sour
  • Inca Kola

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Teresa

Peru is a country with a diverse mix of cultures and Santa Teresa is no exception. The town’s population is a mix of Indigenous, Creole, and Andean cultures. Visitors should be aware of the local customs and traditions:

  • Traditional clothing is still common and highly valued in many communities, so it’s important to respect and admire it.
  • Many Peruvians are Catholic, so churches and religious festivals play an important role in society.
  • Tipping is not always expected, but is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Santa Teresa

The best time to visit Santa Teresa is during the dry season, from May to September. During these months, the weather is mild and there is less rainfall. The peak tourist season is from June to August, so visitors should be prepared for crowds at popular attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Teresa

Do:

  • Respect the local people and their customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and coffee.
  • Be prepared for high altitudes, including altitude sickness.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Buy or ingest any illegal substances.
  • Be overly excessive with alcohol consumption.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Teresa

Santa Teresa is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take the usual precautions against pickpocketing and petty theft. Tourists should also be cautious while hiking and biking, and should only take authorized routes with trusted guides.

Health in Santa Teresa

Altitude sickness is a common problem for visitors to Peru, and Santa Teresa is no exception. Visitors should plan to spend a few days acclimating to the altitude before embarking on any strenuous activities. Travelers should also seek medical advice before arriving in Peru.

Getting to Santa Teresa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Teresa

The closest airport to Santa Teresa is Alejandro Velasco Astete International Airport in Cusco, approximately 109 kilometers away. From Cusco, visitors can take a bus to Santa Teresa.

Bus Stations Near Santa Teresa

Major bus companies, including Cruz del Sur, offer regular services to Santa Teresa from Cusco.

Other Options

The most popular way to reach Santa Teresa is by train from the town of Ollantaytambo. The train offers a scenic route through the mountains and passes through Aguas Calientes before arriving in Santa Teresa.

How to Get Around in Santa Teresa

While Santa Teresa is a relatively small town, there are a variety of transportation options available to visitors. Taxis, tuk-tuks, and shared minivans, known as colectivos, are all available for hire. Alternatively, visitors can also explore on foot or by bike if they’re feeling adventurous.

Where to Go Next from Santa Teresa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aguas Calientes

Located just a short train ride away, Aguas Calientes is the gateway to Machu Picchu and a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Santa Teresa.

Cusco

The historic capital of the Inca Empire, Cusco is a vibrant city that is often referred to as the “archaeological capital of the Americas.” Visitors can explore the city’s many churches, museums, and vibrant markets.

Conclusion

Santa Teresa is a hidden gem in the Southern Sierra region of Peru. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and wide variety of activities, it’s the perfect destination for adventure seekers, culture enthusiasts, and nature lovers alike. Whether you’re looking to hike through the mountains, explore local museums, or simply relax in natural hot springs, Santa Teresa is sure to impress.
