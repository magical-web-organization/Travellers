Introduction to Chinchero

Nestled at an altitude of 3,762 meters (12,343 ft) in the Southern Sierra region of Peru, Chinchero is a charming town that offers a blend of natural and cultural attractions. Surrounded by breathtaking mountain views, Chinchero is known for preserving the indigenous culture and traditions of the Andean people. The town is famous for its textile arts and is home to ancient Inca ruins, colonial-era churches, and museums that showcase the local heritage. With its vibrant traditions, beautiful scenery, and exciting activities, Chinchero is a must-visit destination in Peru.

List of Places to Visit in Chinchero

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chinchero boasts several cultural sites that are worth a visit. Here are some of the most notable:

  • Chinchero Archaeological Park: This park is home to Inca ruins, including an ancient Inca palace, agricultural terraces, water channels, and an Inca trail that connects Chinchero to other Inca sites in the Sacred Valley. The park also has a museum that displays artifacts and exhibits related to the Inca civilization.

  • Iglesia Colonial de Chinchero: This colonial-era church was built on top of an Inca temple. It features an ornate altar, religious paintings, and a bell tower with panoramic views of the town and the surrounding mountains.

  • Centro de Textiles Tradicionales del Cusco: This museum showcases the traditional textile arts of the Andean people. Visitors can learn about the techniques used to weave Andean textiles and purchase high-quality handmade textiles directly from the local weavers.

Natural Attractions of Chinchero: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its rich cultural heritage and traditions, Chinchero offers a variety of natural attractions and outdoor activities. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Moray Terraces: Located in the Sacred Valley, a short distance from Chinchero, the Moray Terraces are a series of circular agricultural terraces that were used by the Incas to experiment with different crops and climates. The terraces offer a stunning view of the surrounding mountains.

  • Salineras de Maras: These salt mines have been in use since Inca times and are still operated by local families. Visitors can see the unique salt pools and learn about the salt-production process.

  • Laguna de Piuray: This picturesque lake is located near Chinchero and is a popular spot for water-based activities such as paddleboarding, kayaking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chinchero

Chinchero is surrounded by stunning mountain scenery and offers various hiking and biking trails that offer a chance to explore the natural wonders of the area. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Chinchero to Maras and Moray: This moderate-level hike offers magnificent views of the Moray Terraces and the surrounding mountains. The trail passes through the town of Maras and offers a glimpse of the traditional Andean way of life.

  • Chinchero to Cusco via the Pumamarca ruins: This challenging hike includes a visit to the Pumamarca ruins, which are believed to have been a military fortress. The route also passes through several Andean communities and offers a chance to interact with the locals.

Best Things to Do in Chinchero: Activities and Tours

Aside from its natural and cultural attractions, Chinchero offers several exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Chinchero:

  • Andean Textile Tour: This tour offers a hands-on experience of the traditional textile arts of the Andean people. Visitors can learn how to spin wool, dye yarn using natural pigments, and weave textiles on a backstrap loom.

  • Chinchero Market Tour: Chinchero is famous for its colorful market, where locals sell their fresh produce, handmade textiles, and other handicrafts. The tour offers a chance to experience the vibrant market scene and purchase unique souvenirs.

  • Horseback Riding Tour: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Chinchero, as it allows visitors to explore the area’s natural beauty at a leisurely pace. The tour includes a visit to some of the town’s most notable attractions, such as the Inca ruins and the colonial church.

Food and Drink Scene of Chinchero

Chinchero offers a diverse culinary scene that includes traditional Andean dishes as well as international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuy al horno: This roasted guinea pig dish is a delicacy in the Andean region and is often served during celebrations and festivals.

  • Pachamanca: This traditional Peruvian dish is prepared by cooking meat and vegetables in an underground oven, together with hot rocks.

  • Empanadas: These savory pastries are filled with meat, vegetables, or cheese and are a popular snack in Peru.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This drink is made from fermented maize and is a staple of Andean cuisine. It is often served during festivals and celebrations.

  • Mate de coca: This herbal tea is made from coca leaves and is believed to offer several health benefits, such as alleviating altitude sickness.

Where to Stay in Chinchero: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chinchero offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Chinchero:

  • La Casa de Barro Lodge & Art: This eco-friendly lodge offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the mountains. The lodge also features an art gallery that showcases the works of local artists.

  • Hermoso Compartir: This hostel offers affordable dormitory-style accommodation and is ideally located within walking distance of the town center.

  • Boutique Hotel Lizzy Wasi: This hotel offers luxurious rooms and personalized service. It is located a short distance from the Chinchero Market and other attractions.

What to Buy in Chinchero: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chinchero is famous for its high-quality handmade textiles, which are produced using traditional Andean techniques. Visitors can purchase these textiles directly from the local weavers or at the Chinchero Market. Other souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Alpaca wool products: Alpaca wool is a staple of Andean textiles and is used to make a variety of products, such as sweaters, scarves, hats, and gloves.

  • Local food products: Chinchero is known for its delicious jams, honey, and chocolates, which make for tasty souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Chinchero

Chinchero is a town that takes pride in preserving its cultural heritage and traditional way of life. The town’s inhabitants are predominantly indigenous Andean people who speak Quechua, the language of the Inca Empire. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting churches or ancient ruins, and asking for permission before taking photographs of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Chinchero

The best time to visit Chinchero is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During these months, the weather is mild and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. October and November are also good months to visit, as the weather is still pleasant, and the tourists crowds have thinned out. The rainy season, which runs from December to April, can make travel more challenging due to landslides and flooded roads, but can also be an exciting time to visit for those who enjoy the lush green landscapes and seasonal festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chinchero

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches or ancient ruins.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or their property.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on the ancient ruins or monuments.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Engage in illegal or unethical activities.
  • Ignore safety warnings or advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chinchero

Chinchero is a relatively safe destination for tourists, but visitors should exercise caution and take appropriate measures to safeguard their belongings. Some basic safety tips include:

  • Keep valuable items such as passports, money and cameras in a secure place.
  • Avoid walking alone at night and in less well-lit areas.
  • Follow local advice regarding safety risks and natural hazards.

Health in Chinchero

Visitors to Chinchero should take precautions to avoid altitude sickness, as the town is located at an elevation above 3,500 meters (11,484 feet). Some tips to prevent altitude sickness include:

  • Allow time for acclimatization by avoiding strenuous activities for the first few days of the trip.
  • Drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol and caffeine.
  • Consult a doctor before traveling if you have a pre-existing medical condition or are unsure about your health.

Getting to Chinchero: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chinchero is located about 28 kilometers (17.4 miles) from Cusco, the nearest major airport. Visitors can reach Chinchero by a variety of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Chinchero

  • Alejandro Velasco Astete International Airport: This airport is located in Cusco and offers international and domestic flights to several destinations in Peru and South America.

Bus Stations Near Chinchero

  • Cusco Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the city center of Cusco and offers several daily services to Chinchero.

Other Options

  • Private Taxi or Transfer: Visitors can arrange a private taxi or transfer service from Cusco or other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Chinchero

The town of Chinchero is small and easy to navigate on foot. Visitors can also hire a taxi or take a local bus to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Chinchero: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chinchero is located in the heart of the Sacred Valley, which offers several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth a visit. Some of the most notable ones include:

  • Machu Picchu: This ancient Inca city is one of the most iconic attractions in Peru and is easily accessible from Chinchero.

  • Ollantaytambo: This well-preserved Inca town is located in the Sacred Valley and is famous for its ancient ruins and historic architecture.

  • Pisac: This charming town is home to an Inca fortress, colonial-era houses, and a bustling market that sells traditional handicrafts and fresh produce.

Conclusion

Chinchero is a hidden gem in the Andes Mountains that offers a delightful mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and traditional arts. Whether it is hiking in the mountains, exploring ancient ruins, or shopping for souvenirs, Chinchero has something for everyone. With its friendly locals and charming ambiance, Chinchero is an ideal destination for those looking for an authentic Andean experience.
