Introduction to Tarma

Tarma is a city in Southern Sierra, in Peru, South America. The city’s diverse cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty make it an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Peru. Tarma is known for its mild climate, warm hospitality, and spectacular views of the Andes mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Tarma

Tarma has a lot to offer visitors, from museums and historical attractions to outdoor adventures and scenic natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museo Regional de Junin: This museum features exhibits on the ancient culture of the Junin region, including artifacts from the Inca Empire.
  2. Cathedral of Tarma: Built in the colonial Baroque style, this cathedral is located in the heart of Tarma’s historic center.
  3. Las Capillas de Tarma: These small chapels are located throughout the city and date from the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Tarma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cerro de la Sal: This mountain offers amazing views of the surrounding valley and is a popular destination for hiking.
  2. Huaytapallana Glacier: This glacier is located on the Huaytapallana mountain and is a fantastic place to visit for stunning natural beauty.
  3. La Oroya: This mining town is located near Tarma and offers visitors a glimpse into Peru’s industrial heritage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarma

Tarma is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, who can enjoy hiking and biking on the many trails in the nearby mountains.

  1. Cerro de la Sal: This mountain offers several hiking trails with varying degrees of difficulty.
  2. Huaytapallana: This mountain offers a challenging hiking experience with amazing views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Biking Trails: There are several bike routes in the surrounding mountains that offer stunning views and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Tarma: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Tarma that will keep visitors busy and entertained.

  1. Hot Springs: Visit the local hot springs for a relaxing soak after a day of hiking or biking.
  2. Coffee Plantations: Take a tour of the local coffee plantations and learn about the region’s coffee industry.
  3. Culinary Tours: Sample the local cuisine with a culinary tour of the city’s best restaurants and cafes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarma

Day 1: Explore the historic center of Tarma, including the Cathedral and Las Capillas de Tarma.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Cerro de la Sal for amazing views of the surrounding valley.

Day 3: Visit the Museo Regional de Junin to learn about the ancient culture of the region.

Day 4: Take a bike tour of the surrounding mountains and enjoy the stunning natural beauty.

Day 5: Soak in the local hot springs and relax after a week of outdoor activities.

Day 6: Take a tour of the local coffee plantations and learn about Peru’s coffee industry.

Day 7: Sample the local cuisine with a culinary tour of the city’s best restaurants and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarma

Tarma is known for its delicious cuisine, which features local ingredients and traditional cooking methods.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pachamanca: This dish is made from meat and vegetables cooked in an underground pit with hot stones.
  2. Cuy: A traditional Peruvian dish made from roasted guinea pig.
  3. Trucha Frita: Fried trout, commonly served with rice and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  1. Chicha: A fermented corn drink that is popular throughout Peru.
  2. Mate de Coca: A tea made from the leaves of the coca plant, which is believed to help with altitude sickness.
  3. Pisco: A type of brandy made from fermented grapes.

Where to Stay in Tarma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarma has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  1. Hotel Tarma: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Tarma’s historic center.
  2. Hostal La Posada: A budget-friendly option located just outside the city center.
  3. Casa Blanca Hospedaje: A guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Tarma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tarma is known for its handicrafts, including woven textiles, pottery, and wood carvings.

  1. Alpaca Wool Products: Scarves, hats, and sweaters made from locally-sourced alpaca wool.
  2. Pottery: Handmade ceramics that reflect the region’s rich cultural heritage.
  3. Wood Carvings: Intricate carvings made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarma

Tarma has a diverse cultural heritage, with influences from the Inca Empire, Spanish colonialism, and more.

  1. Music and Dance: Tarma has a rich musical tradition, with festivals and performances throughout the year.
  2. Religious Observances: Tarma is home to several religious festivals, including the Feast of Corpus Christi and the Festival of Saint Sebastian.
  3. Craftsmanship: Handicrafts play an important role in Tarma’s cultural heritage, with many local artisans practicing traditional crafts.

Best Time to Visit Tarma

The best time to visit Tarma is during the dry season, which runs from May through October. Temperatures during this time are mild, and visitors can enjoy the region’s outdoor activities without worrying about rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarma

Do:

  1. Respect Local Customs: Tarma has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Learn a Few Words of Spanish: While many people in Tarma speak English, knowing a few words of Spanish can help you connect with locals and navigate the city more easily.
  3. Try the Local Cuisine: Tarma is known for its delicious food, so make sure to try some of the local dishes.

Don’t:

  1. Wear Revealing Clothing: Tarma is a conservative city, and it’s important to respect local customs by avoiding clothing that is too revealing.
  2. Disrespect Religious Sites: Tarma is home to several important religious sites, and visitors should be respectful when visiting these places.
  3. Ignore Safety Precautions: While Tarma is generally a safe place to visit, visitors should still take precautions such as not traveling alone at night and being aware of their surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarma

Tarma is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid theft and other crimes. It’s also important to be aware of the risks of altitude sickness when traveling to a high-altitude destination like Tarma.

Health in Tarma

Altitude sickness is a common health concern in Tarma, so visitors should take care to acclimate gradually and stay hydrated. It’s also important to use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Tarma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarma is located approximately 250 km east of Lima and is easily accessible by bus or car.

Airports Near Tarma

  1. Jorge Chávez International Airport (LIM): The nearest international airport, located in Lima.

Bus Stations Near Tarma

  1. Terminal Terrestre Plaza Grau: Tarma’s main bus station, with daily connections to Lima and other destinations in Peru.

Other Options

  1. Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car in Lima and drive to Tarma, though it’s important to be aware of the risks of driving in Peru.

How to Get Around in Tarma

Taxis and buses are the most popular forms of transportation in Tarma. Visitors can also rent a bike or hire a guide for outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Tarma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. La Merced: A town located 60 km from Tarma that is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  2. Laguna de Paca: A scenic lake located 20 km from Tarma that is popular for fishing and hiking.
  3. Huancayo: A city located 130 km from Tarma that is known for its museums and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Tarma is a unique destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the best of Peru’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. With so many things to see and do, it’s easy to see why Tarma is quickly becoming a popular destination for travelers from around the world. From its museums and historical attractions to its outdoor adventures and stunning natural beauty, Tarma truly has something for everyone.
