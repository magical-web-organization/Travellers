Huancayo Travel Guide: Discovering the Southern Sierra of Peru

Huancayo is a city located in the Southern Sierra of Peru, South America. It is a fascinating destination that is filled with cultural and natural beauty. Huancayo is surrounded by the Andean mountain range, which provides a stunning backdrop to the city. In this comprehensive travel guide to Huancayo, we’ll take a look at everything this city has to offer.

Introduction to Huancayo

Huancayo is located at an altitude of 3,271 meters above sea level. Despite its high altitude, the weather in Huancayo is temperate and dry throughout the year. The city is known for its vibrant culture and traditions, which are celebrated through various festivals and events throughout the year. The people of Huancayo are warm and friendly, welcoming tourists with open arms.

List of Places to Visit in Huancayo

Huancayo has a plethora of places to visit, ranging from culturally significant museums and historical places to natural attractions that showcase the region’s stunning scenery. Here are some of the top places to visit in Huancayo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Regional de Junín: One of the most famous museums in Junin, which showcases the cultural heritage and history of the region. The museum displays a vast collection of pre-Columbian and colonial art, traditional costumes, and musical instruments.

  • La Merced Church: A beautiful colonial-style church located in the heart of the city. It features stunning Baroque architecture and is home to a variety of religious art.

  • Torre Torre: A geological formation that resembles a stone forest. It is a popular spot for tourists who come to marvel at its unique natural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Huancayo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque de la Identidad Huanca: A beautiful park located on the outskirts of the city, showcasing the region’s biodiversity. It features hiking trails and offers stunning views of the landscape.

  • Cerro de la Libertad: A mountain located on the outskirts of the city, which provides a great outdoor adventure experience for those who enjoy hiking and climbing. From the top of the mountain, visitors can enjoy stunning views of the landscape.

  • Lago de Paca: A stunning lake located on the outskirts of the city, which offers a peaceful and serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and boat rides on the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huancayo

Huancayo has a lot to offer for those who enjoy hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Huancayo:

  • Cordillera Huaytapallana Circuit: A popular hiking trail that takes visitors through stunning Andean landscapes and passes by beautiful lagoons and glaciers.

  • Santa Ines Hill: A steep hike that offers stunning views of the city from the top.

  • Tikas Valley: A beautiful valley located on the outskirts of the city, which offers a great biking experience for those who enjoy cycling.

Best Things to Do in Huancayo: Activities and Tours

Huancayo offers a wide variety of activities and tours that cater to the needs of every type of traveler. Here’s are some of the best things to do in Huancayo:

  • Traditional Market Tour: A tour that takes visitors through the bustling markets of Huancayo, where they can sample traditional foods and learn about the local culture.

  • Huaytapallana Glacier Trek: A challenging trek that takes visitors through stunning Andean landscapes, passing by beautiful lagoons and glaciers.

  • Canyoning Adventure: An adrenaline-filled adventure that takes visitors through the stunning canyons of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huancayo

If you have a week to spare, here is a comprehensive week-long itinerary for Huancayo:

Day Activity
1 Explore the museums and historical places in the city.
2 Hike Santa Ines Hill in the morning and visit La Merced Church in the afternoon.
3 Visit Parque de la Identidad Huanca and enjoy a picnic in the park.
4 Embark on a canyoning adventure in the morning and explore the city’s traditional markets in the afternoon.
5 Go on a Huaytapallana Glacier trek.

Food and Drink Scene of Huancayo

Food and drink are an essential part of the culture of Huancayo. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papa a la Huancaína: A dish made of boiled potatoes covered with a creamy sauce made of cheese, milk, and aji amarillo.

  • Cuy al Horno: A roasted guinea pig, a traditional dish in the Andean region of South America.

  • Caldo de Gallina: A chicken soup made with a variety of herbs and spices, popular during the cold winter months.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha de Jora: A popular fermented drink made from corn, which has been consumed in the region for thousands of years.

  • Pisco: The national drink of Peru, made from a grape-like fruit and is typically consumed as a cocktail.

Where to Stay in Huancayo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huancayo is home to a variety of accommodation options that cater to the needs of every type of traveler. Here are some of the best places to stay in Huancayo:

  • Hotel Presidente Huancayo: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city that offers excellent amenities and stunning views of the Andean mountain range.

  • KARPA Hostel Huancayo: A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable accommodation in the city.

  • Residencial Amaru Hostal: A guesthouse that offers a homey ambiance and excellent services for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Huancayo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huancayo is known for its craftsmanship, and there are many souvenirs and local products that visitors can purchase. Here are some of the best things to buy in Huancayo:

  • Alpaca Wool Clothing and Accessories: Huancayo is home to a vibrant alpaca wool industry, producing high-quality clothing and accessories made from the wool of alpacas.

  • Pottery and Ceramics: The city is home to many skilled artisans who produce stunning pottery and ceramics, often featuring traditional Andean motifs.

  • Quinoa Products: Quinoa is a staple ingredient in the Andean region, and Huancayo is home to many shops and markets selling different quinoa products, including flour, pasta, and snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Huancayo

The people of Huancayo are warm and friendly, and their culture is deeply rooted in tradition. Here are some customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of:

  • Greetings: Traditional greetings in Huancayo include shaking hands, hugging, and giving a small kiss on the cheek.

  • Festivals and Celebrations: The people of Huancayo love to celebrate their culture and traditions through various festivals and events throughout the year, including the annual Inti Raymi festival.

  • Respect for Elders: Huancayo is a culture that values respect for elders, and visitors should always show respect for older people.

Best Time to Visit Huancayo

The best time to visit Huancayo is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is temperate during this time of the year, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the city. However, visitors should be aware that the dry season is also the peak tourist season in Huancayo, and the city can be crowded during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huancayo

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts of Huancayo:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Visitors should always be respectful of the local customs and traditions of the people of Huancayo.

  • Dress appropriately: The region is predominantly Catholic, and visitors should dress appropriately while visiting churches and other religious sites.

  • Try the local food: Huancayo is known for its vibrant food culture, and visitors should try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture: Visitors should never be disrespectful of the local culture and traditions of the people of Huancayo.

  • Take photographs without permission: It is considered impolite to take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Leave valuables unattended: Visitors should not leave valuables unattended, and should keep an eye on their belongings at all times.

Safety and Health

Huancayo is a relatively safe city, and it is generally safe for tourists to explore. However, visitors should take some precautions to ensure their safety and health:

Safety in Huancayo

  • Stay in well-lit areas: Visitors should avoid walking around in poorly lit areas, especially at night.

  • Be cautious of pickpockets: Pickpocketing can be a problem in crowded areas, and visitors should keep their belongings in a safe place at all times.

  • Use licensed taxis: Visitors should only use licensed taxis, which can be easily identified by the yellow and black taxi sign on the roof.

Health in Huancayo

  • Stay Hydrated: The high altitude of Huancayo can cause dehydration, and visitors should drink plenty of water to avoid altitude sickness.

  • Take precautions for food and water safety: Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and eating uncooked or undercooked food to avoid stomach problems.

  • Get vaccinated: Visitors should consult their healthcare provider before traveling to Huancayo and get vaccinated for diseases such as typhoid and hepatitis A.

Getting to Huancayo: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options available to get to Huancayo. Here’s a breakdown of the different options:

Airports Near Huancayo

The closest airport to Huancayo is the Francisco Carle Airport in Jauja, which is located about 30 minutes away from the city. The airport offers domestic flights to Lima and other cities in Peru.

Bus Stations Near Huancayo

Huancayo is well-connected by buses, and there are several bus stations in the city. The most popular bus companies that serve Huancayo include Cruz del Sur, Oltursa, and Movil Tours.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Huancayo or hire a private car to get there.

How to Get Around in Huancayo

Huancayo is a relatively small city, and it is easy to get around on foot. Visitors can also take taxis or colectivos, which are shared taxis that operate on set routes around the city.

Where to Go Next from Huancayo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after visiting Huancayo:

  • Cerro de Pasco: A mining town located in the central highlands of Peru, which offers stunning natural and cultural attractions.

  • Ayacucho: A colonial city located in the Andes Mountains, which features stunning architecture and rich cultural heritage.

  • Pampas Galeras National Reserve: A nature reserve located about 5 hours from Huancayo, which is home to vicunas and other rare animals.

Conclusion

Huancayo is a beautiful city in the Southern Sierra of Peru, which offers a unique blend of cultural and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the city’s museums and historical places, indulge in the local food scene, and embark on outdoor adventures that showcase the stunning Andean landscapes. With its vibrant culture, warm people, and stunning scenery, Huancayo is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
