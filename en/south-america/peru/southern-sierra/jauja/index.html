Jauja Travel Guide: Explore the Best of Southern Sierra, Peru

Located in the heart of Southern Sierra, Peru, Jauja is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant culture, Jauja is the perfect destination for travelers who want to explore something off the beaten path. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to plan your trip to Jauja.

Introduction to Jauja

Jauja is a city that has played an important role throughout Peruvian history. This city was once the capital of Peru during the Spanish conquest and is filled with beautiful architecture from the colonial period. Jauja is also famous for its vibrant culture, delicious food, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Jauja

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Jauja Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Jauja. Visitors can explore exhibitions that cover everything from Jauja’s colonial past to the present day.
  • The Church of San Francisco: This beautiful church was built in the colonial period and is considered one of the most important religious sites in Jauja. Visitors can explore the church’s baroque architecture and artwork.
  • The Palace of the Governor: This historic building was once the residence of Peru’s governor during the colonial period. Today, visitors can explore its architecture and learn about the history of Jauja.

Natural Attractions of Jauja: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Mantaro River: The Mantaro River is the longest river in Peru and is a popular spot for water-based activities like kayaking and rafting.
  • The Tunanmarca Ruins: These ruins are located in the mountains above Jauja and were once used by the Incas. Visitors can explore the ruins and take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • The Laguna de Paca: This beautiful lake is located just outside Jauja and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jauja

  • The Circuit of the Lagoons: This 2-day trek takes visitors through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Jauja. Along the way, hikers will pass several lagoons and can explore natural hot springs.
  • The Valley of the Caves: This bike route takes visitors through stunning valleys and past hidden caves.

Best Things to Do in Jauja: Activities and Tours

  • The Pampa de Antapampa Geoglyphs: These geoglyphs are located just outside Jauja and are an impressive example of pre-Columbian art. Visitors can explore the geoglyphs on a guided tour.
  • The Kañaris Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is located just outside Jauja and is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jauja

Day Activities
1 Explore the Jauja Museum and the Church of San Francisco
2 Take a guided tour of the Pampa de Antapampa Geoglyphs
3 Hike the Circuit of the Lagoons
4 Visit the Tunanmarca Ruins and explore the Valley of the Caves
5 Enjoy the outdoors at the Laguna de Paca
6 Take a bike tour of the Kañaris Waterfall
7 Sample local cuisine and buy souvenirs before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Jauja

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pachamanca: This traditional dish is made by cooking meat, potatoes, and vegetables underground.
  • Chuño Colado: This hearty soup is made with dried potatoes and meat.
  • Lomo Saltado: This dish features marinated beef stir-fried with onions, tomatoes, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This drink is made by fermenting corn and is a popular alcoholic beverage in Peru.
  • Inka Cola: This bright yellow soft drink is extremely popular in Peru and has a unique flavor that is hard to describe.

Where to Stay in Jauja: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jauja Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the heart of Jauja and features clean and comfortable rooms.
  • The Country Club Jauja: This luxurious hotel features a golf course, spa, and restaurant. Rooms are spacious and beautifully decorated.

What to Buy in Jauja: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Textiles: Jauja is famous for its textiles, which include beautiful blankets, scarves, and ponchos.
  • Pottery: The ceramics produced in Jauja are known for their intricate designs and beautiful colors.
  • Jewelry: Local artisans produce beautiful jewelry made from silver and other precious metals.

Local Culture and Customs of Jauja

Jauja has a rich cultural heritage that is still celebrated today. One of the most important cultural events in Jauja is the Feast of Our Lady of Carmen, which takes place every July. During this festival, locals parade through the streets, dance, and sing in honor of the city’s patron saint.

Best Time to Visit Jauja

The best time to visit Jauja is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. During this time, the weather is mild and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for cold temperatures at night, especially if they plan to go hiking in the mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jauja

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Pachamanca and Lomo Saltado
  • Explore the natural beauty of Jauja, including the lakes and mountains
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially during cultural events

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended, especially in crowded areas
  • Disrespect religious sites, such as churches and cathedrals
  • Take photographs without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Jauja

Overall, Jauja is a safe city to visit. However, visitors should take basic precautions to prevent theft and other crimes. It is recommended that visitors travel in groups and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Jauja

Visitors to Jauja should be aware of the high altitude, which can lead to altitude sickness. To prevent altitude sickness, visitors should take time to acclimate and stay hydrated. Visitors should also be mindful of food and water safety to prevent illnesses.

Getting to Jauja: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jauja

The closest airport to Jauja is the Francisco Carle Airport, located in the neighboring city of Ayacucho. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Jauja.

Bus Stations Near Jauja

Jauja has a central bus station that serves many destinations throughout Peru, including Lima and Cusco.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or private car to Jauja from nearby cities or hire a tour operator to organize transportation.

How to Get Around in Jauja

The best way to get around Jauja is by taxi or by renting a bicycle or motorbike. Taxis are cheap and plentiful, and most drivers are familiar with the local area.

Where to Go Next from Jauja: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cusco: Cusco is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Peru, known for its beautiful architecture and proximity to Machu Picchu.
  • Lima: Lima is the capital of Peru and is home to many museums, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Paracas National Reserve: This national park is located near the coast and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Jauja is a hidden gem in Southern Sierra, Peru, that has something for every traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s history and culture, hiking in the mountains, or simply enjoying the local cuisine, Jauja has it all. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to plan your trip to Jauja and experience everything this beautiful city has to offer.
