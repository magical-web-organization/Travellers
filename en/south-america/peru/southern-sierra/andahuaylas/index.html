Introduction to Andahuaylas

Located in Southern Sierra, Peru, Andahuaylas is a humble yet charming town that offers an authentic glimpse of the Peruvian culture and lifestyle. Surrounded by mountains and nature reserves, Andahuaylas boasts natural beauty and is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture seekers. In this travel guide, we will take you on a tour of Andahuaylas, covering everything from the must-visit attractions to the best places to stay, eat, and shop.

List of Places to Visit in Andahuaylas

Andahuaylas is home to many culturally significant attractions and natural wonders. Below are some of the must-visit places in Andahuaylas.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. San Pedro Apostol de Andahuaylas Church

Built in the 17th century, San Pedro Apostol de Andahuaylas Church is a stunning example of baroque architecture in South America. The church houses an impressive collection of religious art and sculptures.

2. Casa de la Cultura de Andahuaylas

Located in the heart of the town, Casa de la Cultura de Andahuaylas is a cultural center that promotes the preservation and showcasing of Peruvian traditions. The center hosts various events such as music and dance performances, art exhibitions, and workshops.

3. Choquechaca Archaeological Site

Choquechaca Archaeological Site is a fascinating pre-Columbian ruin located in the outskirts of Andahuaylas. The site features stone structures and terraced slopes that date back to the Wari culture.

Natural Attractions of Andahuaylas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Laguna de Pacucha

Laguna de Pacucha is a tranquil lake located 20 km from Andahuaylas. The lake is surrounded by mountains and grassy fields, making it a perfect spot for hiking, picnicking, and birdwatching.

2. Apurimac River Canyon

The Apurimac River Canyon is a breathtakingly beautiful natural wonder located near Andahuaylas. The canyon offers a range of exciting outdoor activities such as rafting, hiking, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Andahuaylas

Andahuaylas offers several hiking and biking routes that cater to different skill levels and preferences. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Choquechaca Circuit
  • Huaytara Circuit
  • Pacucha Circuit
  • Lagunillas Circuit

Best Things to Do in Andahuaylas: Activities and Tours

Andahuaylas has a lot to offer in terms of experiences and activities. Below are some of the best things to do in Andahuaylas.

  • Attend a traditional music or dance performance at Casa de la Cultura de Andahuaylas.
  • Visit a local market to buy traditional textiles, ceramics, and other souvenirs.
  • Take a guided tour of Choquechaca Archaeological Site.
  • Go on a scenic drive through the mountains and valleys surrounding Andahuaylas.

For visitors planning to spend a week in Andahuaylas, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and Check-In Day 2: Visit San Pedro Apostol de Andahuaylas Church and Casa de la Cultura de Andahuaylas Day 3: Hike or Bike on Pacucha Circuit Day 4: Explore Choquechaca Archaeological Site Day 5: Rafting on Apurimac River Day 6: Shopping in local markets Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Andahuaylas

Andahuaylas boasts a diverse culinary scene that reflects the unique Peruvian cuisine. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuy (guinea pig) served with potatoes and vegetables
  • Chupe de gallina (a creamy chicken stew)
  • Papas a la huancaína (boiled potatoes with a spicy cheese sauce)
  • Anticuchos (grilled beef heart)

Local Beverages

  • Chicha (fermented corn beverage)
  • Pisco Sour (Peru’s national cocktail made with pisco, lime juice, and egg whites)
  • Mate de coca (coca leaf tea)

What to Buy in Andahuaylas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Andahuaylas is famous for its traditional textiles, ceramics, and wood carvings. Below are some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products.

  • Alpaca wool scarves, sweaters, and hats
  • Handmade pottery and ceramics
  • Wooden statues and figurines
  • Peruvian coffee and chocolate

Local Culture and Customs of Andahuaylas

Andahuaylas is a town steeped in tradition and customs. One of the most significant events in Andahuaylas is the Takanakuy festival, where locals engage in a friendly boxing match to settle conflicts and grievances that have arisen over the past year.

Best Time to Visit Andahuaylas

The best time to visit Andahuaylas is from May to September when the weather is dry and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Andahuaylas

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local Peruvian dishes and beverages.
  • Learn some Spanish phrases before arriving.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Arrogantly haggle with vendors in local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Andahuaylas

Andahuaylas is generally a safe town, but visitors should exercise caution while exploring the natural attractions and hiking trails. It is advisable to hire a guide and carry necessary safety equipment.

Health in Andahuaylas

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and Typhoid before arriving in Andahuaylas. It is also recommended to drink bottled water and avoid eating street food.

Getting to Andahuaylas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Andahuaylas has limited transportation options, but visitors can reach the town by air or bus.

Andahuaylas is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by taxi. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or hire a guide for hiking tours.
