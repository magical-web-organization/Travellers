Introduction to La Merced

Located in Southern Sierra, Peru, La Merced is a beautiful town that attracts travelers from all around the globe. It is situated in a valley in the Andes Mountains, and boasts of picturesque landscapes, mouth-watering cuisine, and a rich cultural heritage.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through some of the top places to visit, the best things to do, and the must-try cuisine of La Merced. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in La Merced

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

La Merced has a rich cultural history, and there are several museums and historical places that you can visit to learn about the town’s past. Some of the top cultural attractions in La Merced are:

  • Museo del Tabaco: This museum showcases the history of tobacco in the region and features exhibitions on the traditional and modern ways of tobacco production.

  • Museo Zacarias Villanueva: This museum is a tribute to Zacarias Villanueva, a famous Andean musician, and features exhibits on the indigenous culture of the region.

  • Casa de la Cultura: This cultural center is located in the town center and hosts exhibitions, workshops, and performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of La Merced: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Merced is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty and offers a plethora of outdoor adventures for nature lovers. Some of the top natural attractions in La Merced are:

  • Parque Nacional Otishi: This national park is located on the eastern side of La Merced and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. It is a great place to go hiking, bird-watching, and camping.

  • Rio Pachitea: This river flows through the La Merced valley and offers several opportunities for adventure activities such as kayaking, canoeing, and rafting.

  • Mirador Yanachaga Chemillén: This viewpoint offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding forests and mountains and is a great place for hiking and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Merced

La Merced is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts, and there are several trails that you can explore. Some of the top hiking/biking routes in La Merced are:

  • Camino Inca: This trail runs through the Andes Mountains and offers a glimpse into the country’s rich Inca heritage.

  • Trek to Laguna Iskobakebo: This trek takes you through the lush tropical forests and leads up to a beautiful lagoon where you can go fishing and swimming.

  • Bike Tour of La Merced: This bike tour takes you through the town’s main attractions, including the town center, the markets, and the cultural centers.

Best Things to Do in La Merced: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s cultural heritage, there are several other things that you can do in La Merced. Some of the best activities and tours in La Merced are:

  • Coffee Plantation Tour: La Merced is known for its high-quality coffee, and you can take a tour of the coffee plantations to learn about the coffee-making process and sample some of the best coffee in the world.

  • Tarapoto Waterfall: This waterfall is located on the outskirts of La Merced and is a great place for a day trip. You can swim in the crystal-clear water and enjoy a picnic in the lush green surroundings.

  • Amazon Jungle Tour: La Merced is a great place to start your Amazon journey. You can take a guided tour of the nearby Amazon jungle and explore the vast flora and fauna of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Merced

If you have a week to spare, you can explore all the major attractions of La Merced. Here is a week-long itinerary that you can follow:

Day 1: Explore the Museums and Historical Places of La Merced. Day 2: Hike to Laguna Iskobakebo. Day 3: Take a bike tour of the town and explore the cultural centers. Day 4: Visit Parque Nacional Otishi and go hiking or camping. Day 5: Take a day trip to Tarapoto Waterfall. Day 6: Take a coffee plantation tour. Day 7: Embark on an Amazon jungle tour.

Food and Drink Scene of La Merced

La Merced is known for its delicious traditional cuisine, and there are several must-try dishes and drinks that you cannot miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: This is a popular Peruvian dish made with raw fish marinated in citrus juices and served with onions, chili peppers, and sweet potato.

  • Lomo Saltado: This dish comprises of stir-fried beef, onions, tomatoes, and french fries, served with rice.

  • Tiradito: A variation of ceviche, it comprises of thin slices of raw fish served with a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: This is a popular Peruvian cocktail made with pisco, a grape brandy, mixed with lime juice, egg whites, and sugar.

  • Chicha Morada: This is a traditional drink made with purple corn, pineapple, cinnamon, and cloves.

Where to Stay in La Merced: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Merced has a range of accommodation options, depending on your budget and preferences. Some of the top places to stay in La Merced are:

  • Hotel Marañón: A 3-star hotel located in the town center, with air-conditioned rooms and excellent amenities.

  • Hostal Casa Andina: A cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms, located close to the town’s main attractions.

  • Hostal Arma del Tiempo: A budget-friendly accommodation option that offers clean and comfortable rooms and a great location.

What to Buy in La Merced: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

La Merced is known for its artisanal products, many of which are made from natural materials and traditional Andean techniques. Some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home from La Merced are:

  • Hand-woven textiles and clothing made from alpaca wool.

  • Traditional Andean musical instruments, such as the charango and the quena.

  • Coffee beans and chocolate made from locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of La Merced

La Merced is a town with a rich cultural heritage, and it is important to be aware of the local customs and etiquette when visiting. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is customary to greet people with a smile and a handshake.

Best Time to Visit La Merced

The best time to visit La Merced is during the dry season, from May to October. The weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Merced

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Respect the local customs and culture.

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases.

Don’t:

  • Worry too much about safety. La Merced is a safe town, but it is advisable to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

  • Take photos without asking for permission.

  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Merced

La Merced is a safe town, but it is advisable to take some precautions to ensure your safety. It is advisable to avoid walking alone late at night, to keep your valuables secure, and to be vigilant when using public transportation.

Health in La Merced

La Merced is located at an altitude of 700 meters above sea level, and it is important to take some precautions to avoid altitude sickness. It is advisable to stay hydrated, avoid alcohol and tobacco, and take some time to acclimatize before undertaking any strenuous activities.

Getting to La Merced: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Merced

The nearest airport to La Merced is Coronel FAP Francisco Secada Vignetta International Airport, located in the town of Iquitos.

Bus Stations Near La Merced

La Merced has a bus station that is well-connected to the neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or take a shared taxi to reach La Merced from other towns in the region.

How to Get Around in La Merced

La Merced is a small town and can be easily explored on foot. Biking and walking are the most popular modes of transportation in the town.

Where to Go Next from La Merced: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Southern Peru, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can undertake. Some of the top places to visit near La Merced are:

  • Machu Picchu: The famous Incan ruins are located a short distance from La Merced and are a must-visit destination for any traveler to Peru.

  • Ayacucho: This colonial town is located in the Andean highlands and is known for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful architecture.

  • Cusco: The ancient capital of the Incan Empire is located a short distance from La Merced and is a great place to explore the country’s rich Incan history.

Conclusion

La Merced is a beautiful town that offers a range of natural, cultural, and adventure attractions to its visitors. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, delicious Peruvian cuisine, or a glimpse into the Andean culture, La Merced has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip to La Merced today and discover the beauty of Southern Peru!
