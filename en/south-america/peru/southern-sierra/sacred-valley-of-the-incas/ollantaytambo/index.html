Introduction to Ollantaytambo

Ollantaytambo, located in the Southern Sierra region of Peru, is a small town in the Sacred Valley of the Incas. It offers visitors a chance to explore the rich history of the Incas, mixed with nature and outdoor activities, making it an exciting destination. This Peruvian town is steeped in history, with some buildings dating back to the 15th century.

Whether you’re here for the history, hiking trails, or to explore the food and drink scene, Ollantaytambo is sure to impress.

List of Places to Visit in Ollantaytambo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Ollantaytambo Ruins

The Ollantaytambo Ruins are a must-visit destination while you’re in Ollantaytambo. The ruins are regarded by most as a significant part of Peru’s Inca history. In the 15th century, the ruins were either a fortress or a temple for the Incas that protected the town’s entrance.

Inca Bridge

If you’re looking for an adventure, Inca Bridge should be on your list. The bridge consists of several logs and cocoyas bound together. It’s hung over the gorge, making it an exciting experience while crossing it.

The Pinkuylluna Mountain Granaries

The Pinkuylluna Mountain Granaries were once used as storehouses for the Inca’s food. The granaries are in various shapes and sizes and sit on a mountain overlooking Ollantaytambo’s town.

Natural Attractions of Ollantaytambo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Patacancha Valley Trek

The Patacancha Valley Trek is perfect if you’re short on time but want to experience the scenic mountain views of the Ollantaytambo area. The hike takes approximately 3 hours and is easy to moderate.

Rainbow Mountain Trek

The Rainbow Mountain Trek is another impressive day trip that can be done from Ollantaytambo, taking approximately 4 hours’ drive. The trek is challenging but offers rewarding views of Ausangate Mountain and the iconic Rainbow Mountains in the Andes mountain range.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ollantaytambo

The Lares Trek

The Lares Trek is suitable for intermediate and advanced hikers. This 3-day trek passes villages, Inca ruins, and spectacular mountain views. The trek also includes visits to hot springs, which is a perfect reward for hikers after the hard work.

Mountain Biking around the Sacred Valley

Biking is a great way to explore the region. Biking in Ollantaytambo offers routes through small towns, open countryside, and spectacular mountain ranges. You can also experience some easy, family-friendly routes with beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Ollantaytambo: Activities and Tours

Machu Picchu Tour

A visit to Machu Picchu is essential when you’re in Peru. The Lost City of the Incas is one of the most iconic and recognizable landmarks in Peru. You can easily arrange a day tour to Machu Picchu from Ollantaytambo.

Maras and Moray Salt Mines Tour

Visit the Maras and Moray Salt Mines from Ollantaytambo to experience a fascinating insight into the indigenous people’s lives. You’ll see how they’ve used the salt mines for over 1000 years.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ollantaytambo

Day 1: Ollantaytambo Ruins & Inca Bridge Day 2: Patacancha Valley Trek Day 3: Maras and Moray Salt Mines Tour Day 4-5: Lares Trek (2-day trek) Day 6: Machu Picchu Tour Day 7: Rainbow Mountain Trek

Food and Drink Scene of Ollantaytambo

Must-Try Dishes

Cuy

Cuy, or guinea pig, is a traditional dish locals affectionately call “Cuy Chactado.” It’s a hearty meal of deep-fried guinea pig served with potatoes, corn, and salad.

Aji de Gallina

Aji de Gallina is a chicken dish cooked with a creamy sauce made from peppers, cream, and bread. It’s a delightful comfort meal perfect after a day of exploring Ollantaytambo.

Local Beverages

Pisco

Pisco is a brandy distilled in Peru. It can be consumed on its own or as a mix. The most famous Pisco-based drink is the Pisco Sour containing Pisco, lime juice, and sugar syrup.

Mate de Coca

Mate de Coca is a traditional and stimulating Andean tea made with coca leaves, boiled water, and sometimes sweetened with honey.

Where to Stay in Ollantaytambo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Sol

The Hotel Sol is a three-star hotel located within the walled Incan town itself and is perfect for those wanting to stay in the middle of the action.

Hostal Iskay

Hostal Iskay offers basic, affordable accommodation in the heart of Ollantaytambo. It’s perfect for those backpacking on a budget.

What to Buy in Ollantaytambo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hand-Woven Alpaca Items

Hand-woven alpaca items are a popular souvenir choice when exploring Ollantaytambo. You can find various styles of hats, sweaters, gloves, and scarves all made from alpaca wool.

Chaskis Chocolate

Chaskis Chocolate, also known as the chocolate of the Incas, uses traditional processing techniques with organic cocoa beans to create delicious chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of Ollantaytambo

Visitors to Ollantaytambo will notice the town’s unique architecture with narrow streets and Inca-style stonework buildings. When visiting historical sites and temples, it’s essential to show respect and dress appropriately. The people of Ollantaytambo are known for being friendly and welcoming.

Best Time to Visit Ollantaytambo

The best time to visit Ollantaytambo is during the dry season from May to September. During this period, the weather is dry, sunny, and relatively warm. Temperatures during this time range from 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F).

Do’s and Don’ts of Ollantaytambo

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting historical sites
  • Respect the locals, their customs, and beliefs
  • Try the local delicacies and sip on Mate de Coca

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Take photos with the locals without asking for permission
  • Disrespect the historical sites or destroy natural habitats

Safety and Health

Safety in Ollantaytambo

Ollantaytambo is generally a safe place for travelers. However, it’s essential always to have your wits about you and watch your belongings. Violent crime is not prevalent, but petty theft and pickpocketing can occur.

Health in Ollantaytambo

It’s recommended that you speak to your doctor before traveling to Peru about inoculations, prescriptions, and the precautions you should take while traveling. Don’t drink water from taps without boiling or purifying it first.

Getting to Ollantaytambo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ollantaytambo

The closest airports to Ollantaytambo are in Cusco and Lima. From Cusco airport, you can arrange a private transfer or taxi to your destination.

Bus Stations Near Ollantaytambo

You can also take a bus to Ollantaytambo from Cusco or other cities like Lima. The bus journey from Cusco takes approximately 2 hours.

Other Options

You can also opt for a train journey to Ollantaytambo from Cusco or other cities like Aguas Calientes. The train journey is considered one of the best and most picturesque in South America.

How to Get Around in Ollantaytambo

Ollantaytambo is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Taxis are also available around the town center.

Where to Go Next from Ollantaytambo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is one of the most famous landmarks in Peru and can easily be reached from Ollantaytambo. The Lost City of the Incas is accessible by daily train services.

Cusco

Cusco was the capital of the Inca Empire and boasts beautiful buildings and museums. Cusco is easily accessible from Ollantaytambo by bus or taxi.

Conclusion

Ollantaytambo is a destination that offers a combination of culture, history, outdoor adventure, and natural beauty. With its rich history, magnificent ruins, and unique architecture, it’s an excellent place to explore the traditional Andean way of life. Whether you’re a solo traveler or with family, Ollantaytambo is sure to impress, making it a must-visit destination for explorers in South America.
