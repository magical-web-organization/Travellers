Introduction to Patacancha

Patacancha is a town located in the Sacred Valley of the Incas, in the Southern Sierra region of Peru, South America. It is a must-visit destination for those who want to experience the traditional and vibrant cultures of rural Peru. Patacancha is known for its stunning landscapes, rich Inca history, and a cultural heritage that has been preserved for generations.

Visitors to Patacancha can expect to be immersed in the colorful traditions and culture of the local Quechua people, who have been living in the area long before the Spanish Conquest. The town is surrounded by imposing mountain peaks, fertile valleys, and ancient ruins that make it a perfect destination for nature lovers and history enthusiasts alike.

Here is a comprehensive guide on everything you need to know before you embark on your journey to Patacancha:

List of Places to Visit in Patacancha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Textiles Andinos: This museum houses a spectacular collection of textiles created by the local Quechua communities. Visitors can learn about the intricate weaving techniques that have been passed down for generations and the significance of each pattern and color in the fabrics.
  • Ollantaytambo Fortress: This ancient Inca stronghold is considered one of the most significant ruins in Peru. The massive walls, astrological sundials, and terraced fields display the architectural and engineering capabilities of the Incas.
  • Moray Archaeological Site: This site is composed of several circular agricultural terraces that served as a laboratory for the Incas to experiment with crop growth in varying temperatures.

Natural Attractions of Patacancha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pumamarca Ruins: This pre-Columbian archaeological complex has well-preserved Inca ruins, including a two-story house and palace, a temple, and a funeral platform. It is situated on a hill that offers beautiful panoramic views of the surrounding valley and mountains.
  • The Salinas de Maras: It is a massive salt mine that has been in use since Inca times. The salt occurs naturally and is a result of evaporation. The mine is still in use, and you can watch the locals mining the salt in the traditional way.
  • Huchuy Qosqo: This ancient Inca site is another must-visit spot. The site occupies an enormous area, and the path leading up to it takes you through several small villages, offering some stunning viewpoints.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Patacancha

  • Inca Trail: One of the most famous hiking routes in the world, the Inca Trail takes you through some breathtakingly beautiful terrain. The trek is challenging, but the stunning views and rewarding experience make it all worth it.
  • Machu Picchu: This is an ancient Inca city and one of the most famous destinations in Peru. Visitors can hike up to the ruins from the town of Aguas Calientes or take a bus up to the main entrance. The site is an excellent place to explore on foot, with hiking trails that take you around the ancient citadel.
  • Sacred Valley: The Sacred Valley is a vast region with several hiking and biking routes that take you through traditional villages, remote mountain ranges, and ancient ruins. The landscapes in this region are breathtaking and provide a perfect cultural and adventure experience.

Best Things to Do in Patacancha: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Quechua Weaving: Learn about the traditional Quechua methods of textile weaving and dyeing from the locals themselves. The weavers will teach you about the significance of each pattern and color and allow you to try your hand at weaving.
  • Coca Leaf Reading: Learn about the ancient Inca practice of coca leaf reading, which was used to tell fortunes and gain insight into the spiritual world. A local guide will show you how to read the leaves and interpret their meanings.
  • Traditional Dancing: Join in on a traditional dance performance by the local Quechua people. The vibrant costumes and music provide a beautiful insight into the culture of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Patacancha

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: A popular dish in Peru, ceviche consists of raw fish marinated in lime juice and chili peppers.
  • Lomo Saltado: A traditional Peruvian dish that consists of stir-fried beef, onions, tomatoes, and french fries, served over rice.
  • Cuy: Cuy is a traditional dish of roasted guinea pig, a delicacy in Peru.

Local Beverages

  • Pisco Sour: The national drink of Peru, Pisco Sour is made from Pisco, a grape brandy, lime juice, syrup, and egg white.
  • Chicha: A popular fermented drink made from maize and is commonly served during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Patacancha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pakaritampu Hotel and Spa: This hotel is located in Ollantaytambo, and it is a perfect base for exploring the town’s historical sites. They have cozy rooms and an excellent spa facility.
  • Apu Lodge: Apu Lodge is a family-run guesthouse that features a picturesque garden. Rooms are cozy and comfortable with scenic views of the surrounding mountains and valley.

What to Buy in Patacancha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Alpaca wool clothing: Alpaca wool is a popular material used by the Quechua people to make clothing. You can buy high-quality knitted hats, gloves, ponchos, and blankets.
  • Coca leaves: Coca leaves are a traditional plant used by the Incas for medicinal and spiritual purposes. The leaves can be purchased in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Patacancha

The Quechua culture is at the core of Patacancha’s identity. Respect for tradition is deeply ingrained, and it is important to follow local customs to engage with the community better. Drinking a traditional cup of coca tea upon arrival is a must-do to show respect to the local culture. It is also essential to dress appropriately, cover your shoulders and knees, and ask before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Patacancha

The weather in the Sacred Valley is temperate throughout the year, but the best time to visit Patacancha is between May and October, as the weather is more clement with sunny days and chilly nights. It is the best time to go for hiking, biking, and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Patacancha

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.
  • Drink coca tea is a must when arriving to show respect.
  • Cover shoulders and knees and dress appropriately.
  • Ask locals before photographing.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the local traditions and culture.
  • Take pictures without asking or being intrusive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Patacancha

Patacancha is a relatively safe destination with low crime rates. However, it is essential to take common sense precautions, such as not carrying large sums of money and keeping valuables in a secure place. It is also essential to make sure that you have travel insurance that covers adventurous activities such as hiking.

Health in Patacancha

The high altitude of Patacancha can pose some health risks to travelers. It is recommended to acclimatize and take things slowly, keeping yourself well hydrated to prevent altitude sickness. You should also ensure you are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to the area.

Getting to Patacancha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Patacancha

The closest international airport to Patacancha is in Lima. From Lima, you can take a short flight to Cusco, which is the nearest domestic airport. From Cusco, you can take a bus or taxi to Patacancha.

Bus Stations Near Patacancha

There are several bus companies that operate in and out of Cusco that can take you to Patacancha. The journey takes around 2-3 hours.

Other Options

If you are feeling more adventurous, you can take a train from Cusco to Machu Picchu and then hike from Aguas Calientes to Patacancha, which takes about 2-3 days.

How to Get Around in Patacancha

The best way to get around Patacancha is by foot. The town is small, and most of the attractions and sites are within walking distance. However, if you are planning to explore the surrounding areas, you can hire a taxi with a local guide or rent a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Patacancha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Machu Picchu: No visit to Patacancha is complete without a visit to Machu Picchu. The ancient site offers a unique insight into the Inca civilization.
  • Cusco: The former capital of the Inca Empire is a beautiful colonial city with a rich cultural heritage and several historic sites.
  • Rainbow Mountain: This colorful mountain in Peru’s Andes is a popular hiking destination that offers one of the most stunning natural vistas in the area.

Conclusion

Patacancha is a breathtakingly beautiful destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and adventure. Plan your trip to Patacancha with this guide, and you are sure to come away with unforgettable memories of one of the most beautiful destinations in Peru.
