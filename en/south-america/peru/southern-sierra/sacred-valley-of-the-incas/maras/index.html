Introduction to Maras

Nestled in the heart of the Sacred Valley of the Incas in Southern Sierra, Peru, Maras is a small town that packs a big punch. Known for its stunning views, natural wonders, and rich cultural history, Maras is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to experience the best that Peru has to offer. Here’s our comprehensive travel guide to Maras, including everything you need to know to plan a trip to this beautiful town.

List of Places to Visit in Maras

From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Maras has something for everyone. Here are the top places to visit in Maras:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moray Archaeological Site At Moray, you can witness one of the most unique Inca sites in existence. Featuring a series of concentric terraces that resemble an amphitheater, these ruins are believed to have been used for agricultural experiments.

  • Maras Salt Mines The Maras salt mines are a complex of nearly 3000 salt pools carved into the side of a mountain. This unique salt production method dates back to Inca times.

  • Chinchero Experience the traditional Andean life in Chinchero, a small mountain village that’s famous for its weavings and textiles. The rugged hills of the Andes surrounding Chinchero offer some of the most stunning views of the region.

Natural Attractions of Maras: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sacred Valley Sacred Valley is the cradle of Inca civilization and is one of the most visited regions in Peru. It’s a must-visit destination for travelers in search of outdoor adventure, scenic views, and ancient Inca ruins.

  • Salineras de Maras Trails Explore the Salineras de Maras Trails which is known for its striking Andean landscapes, Inca sites, and vibrant culture.

  • The Maras Waterfall You can witness the Maras waterfall just a 30-minute walk away from Maras. The beautiful waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a popular destination for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maras

Maras offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that take you through some of the most breathtaking landscapes in Peru.

  • Inca Trail The Inca Trail is a multi-day trek that takes you to Machu Picchu, one of the most iconic Inca ruins in the world. It’s a challenging trek that rewards you with stunning views of the Andes.

  • Maras - Moray Loop The Maras - Moray Loop takes you through the stunning landscapes of the Sacred Valley, including the Moray Archaeological Site and the Maras Salt Mines.

Best Things to Do in Maras: Activities and Tours

  • Maras Salt Mines Tour Take a guided tour of the Maras Salt Mines to learn more about the history and process of salt production in Maras.

  • Mountain Biking Tours The mountain biking tours in Maras are a great way to explore the stunning landscapes of the Sacred Valley and discover the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Maras

Maras is known for its delicious cuisine and unique beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuy One of the most popular dishes in Maras is cuy, which is made from guinea pig meat. It’s typically served roasted and is a must-try for foodies in Maras.

  • Ajiaco Ajiaco is a traditional Peruvian soup that’s made with potatoes, corn, and meats such as chicken or beef. It’s a hearty dish that will keep you warm on chilly days in Maras.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha Chicha is a fermented corn drink that’s a popular local beverage in Maras. It’s often consumed during festivals and celebrations in the region.

  • Pisco Pisco is a type of brandy that’s made from grapes and is a popular drink in Peru. It’s often served as a cocktail or on its own.

Local Culture and Customs of Maras

The people of Maras are proud of their cultural heritage and traditions. Two of the most important customs that visitors should be aware of are:

  • Inti Raymi Inti Raymi is the Inca Festival of the Sun and is celebrated every year in Cusco and the Sacred Valley, including Maras. The festival is a celebration of the Inca heritage and involves traditional dancing, music, and processions.

  • Pacha Mama Pacha Mama is the Inca goddess of the earth and is revered by the people of Maras. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions when visiting Maras.

Best Time to Visit Maras

The best time to visit Maras is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. During this time, you’ll have clear skies and warm temperatures, making it the perfect time for outdoor adventures and sightseeing. The wet season, which runs from November to March, can be chilly and rainy, so it’s not the best time for outdoor activities.

Maras is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash and avoiding dark alleys at night.

Safety in Maras

  • Carry a map
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times

Health in Maras

  • Use sunscreen
