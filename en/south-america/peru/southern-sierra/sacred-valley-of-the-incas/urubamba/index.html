Urubamba, located in the Sacred Valley of the Incas, is a quaint town in Southern Sierra, Peru. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, incredible natural landscapes, and breathtaking outdoor adventures. If you’re planning a trip to Urubamba, our comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of this wonderful town.

Introduction to Urubamba

As one of the best-preserved colonial towns in Peru, Urubamba is steeped in rich cultural history. It is an ideal destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone looking for a peaceful retreat. The climate is mild throughout the year, and the town is bustling with local markets, restaurants, and attractions.

Local Culture and Customs of Urubamba

Urubamba is a town that is deeply rooted in its cultural traditions. You can see that in the way the people dress, the food they eat, and their celebrations. Visitors should respect the local customs and be aware of the dress code in religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Urubamba

The best time to visit Urubamba is during the dry season between May and September. The weather is mild, the skies are clear, and there’s very little rainfall. However, the town is pleasant to visit year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Urubamba

Do:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Take cash with you, as credit cards are not widely accepted.
  • Carry an umbrella or raincoat during the rainy season.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Drink tap water without boiling or purifying it first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Urubamba

Urubamba is relatively safe, but visitors should take the necessary precautions while visiting. Leave valuables at your accommodation, avoid walking alone at night, and carry a copy of your passport with you.

Health in Urubamba

Visitors should prioritize staying hydrated, use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites, and avoid street food to minimize the risk of food poisoning. It is recommended to consult your doctor to ensure that your vaccinations are up to date before traveling to Urubamba.

Getting to Urubamba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Urubamba has good road connections to other major cities in Peru and is easy to reach by bus. The closest airport to Urubamba is Alejandro Velasco Astete International Airport in Cusco, which is just 50 minutes away by car. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Urubamba

  • Alejandro Velasco Astete International Airport (Cusco)

Bus Stations Near Urubamba

  • San Francisco Bus Station (Cusco)
  • Pavitos Bus Station (Cusco)

Other Options

  • Private taxi
  • Shared shuttle
  • Local bus

How to Get Around in Urubamba

The town of Urubamba is best explored on foot. However, if you want to venture further afield, you can find taxis or hire a car. Buses are a cheap option, but they tend to be very crowded.
