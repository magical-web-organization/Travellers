Introduction to Arequipa

Arequipa, also known as the “White City” due to its buildings made of white volcanic rock, is a city located in the Southern Coast of Peru. This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts a rich history, a unique food scene, and breathtaking natural attractions. Arequipa is the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the peruvian culture and experience both the city life and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Arequipa

Make sure you visit these attractions during your stay in Arequipa.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Explore the rich history of Arequipa by visiting its famous museums and historical sites.

  • Santa Catalina Monastery: This historical cloister is one of the most popular and iconic landmarks of the city. The monastery, built in the 16th century, is home to nuns and most of the walls, streets, and buildings are painted in bright colors.
  • Juanita Mummy Museum: Located near the city center, the Juanita Mummy Museum houses the frozen teenager’s body discovered in 1995 on an Andean mountain. You can learn about the Inca sacrifices and customs that once took place in the area.

Natural Attractions of Arequipa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Experience the beauty of the city and its surrounding landscapes by visiting these outdoor attractions.

  • Colca Canyon: The Colca Canyon is one of the deepest canyons in the world and is a popular destination for tourists. You can explore the canyon by hiking and enjoy stunning panoramic views of the valley, see the Andean Condor in action, and visit the hot springs.
  • Misti Volcano: Explore the beautiful landscape by climbing the Misti Volcano, an active volcano located close to downtown Arequipa. The climb can take two days, but the stunning view from the top will make it worthwhile.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arequipa

Arequipa offers several hiking and biking routes for visitors looking for outdoor adventures.

  • Chachani: Chachani is a popular peak that stands tall at 6,075 meters. It’s a four-day trek that involves hiking in cold weather and climbing numerous glaciers.
  • Salinas and Aguada Blanca National Reserve: The Salinas and Aguada Blanca National Reserve is home to various volcanoes and a vast wetland habitat. You can hike and bike through the reserve and experience breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Arequipa: Activities and Tours

Experience the best of Arequipa by participating in these unique activities and tours.

  • Andean Kitchen Cooking Class: Immerse yourself in the Peruvian food culture by taking a cooking class with Andean Kitchen. You’ll learn how to make traditional Peruvian dishes in the cozy setting of a local’s home.
  • Arequipa Free Walking Tour: Take a free walking tour of the city with Arequipa Free Walking Tour. You’ll learn about the history and culture of the city while exploring its most famous landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Arequipa

Arequipa is known for its unique food scene that blends Spanish and Inca culinary traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocoto Relleno: This traditional Peruvian dish consists of a spicy beef or chicken filling with potatoes, onions, and peppers. It’s then topped with cheese and baked in a rocoto pepper.
  • Chupe de Camarones: Chupe de Camarones is a thick and creamy soup made with potatoes, milk, eggs, and prawns. It’s a perfect dish to warm you up on a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: Chicha is a fermented maize drink that is often served at festivals and celebrations. It’s a great way to experience the local culture of Arequipa.
  • Pisco Sour: Pisco Sour is the national drink of Peru and is made with Pisco, a grape brandy, fresh lime juice, simple syrup, an egg white, and Angostura bitters.

Where to Stay in Arequipa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arequipa has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Arequipa.

  • Casa Arequipa: Casa Arequipa is a beautiful boutique hotel located in the heart of the city. It features spacious rooms decorated with local artwork and offers a traditional Peruvian breakfast.
  • Wild Rover Hostel: Wild Rover Hostel is a backpacker’s paradise that offers clean, comfortable dorm rooms, a rooftop bar, and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Arequipa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arequipa is famous for its alpaca wool products, traditional textiles, and artisanal crafts. Here’s what you should buy to bring home a special souvenir.

  • Alpaca Wool Products: Alpaca Wool Products such as sweaters, scarfs, hats, and gloves are a must-buy in Arequipa. They are made from the fleece of the alpaca, a South American camelid valued for its soft and warm wool.
  • Traditional Textiles: Arequipa is known for its traditional textiles, which are made using ancient weaving techniques. You can buy colorful blankets, rugs, and wall hangings.

Local Culture and Customs of Arequipa

Arequipa has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in its history. The locals are friendly and hospitable, but it’s important to respect their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Arequipa

The best time to visit Arequipa is between May and September when the weather is dry and sunny. The winter months from June to August can be chilly, but the skies are clear, making it ideal for hiking and outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arequipa

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Arequipa.

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before you go.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting churches.
  • Explore unsafe or dark areas alone.
  • Take pictures without permission from locals.

Safety and Health

Arequipa is generally a safe city to visit, but like any other destination, it’s important to take precautions to stay healthy and safe during your stay.

Safety in Arequipa

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Be cautious during the night and in secluded areas.
  • Use official taxis when traveling.

Health in Arequipa

  • Pack sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid getting sick from tap water.
  • Use insect repellant to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Arequipa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arequipa is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip.

Airports Near Arequipa

Arequipa is served by the Rodríguez Ballón International Airport (AQP). The airport is located around 8 kilometers from the city center and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Arequipa

Several bus companies operate from the Arequipa bus terminal, offering connections to other Peruvian cities and destinations in Bolivia and Chile.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or book a private transfer from Lima, the capital city of Peru, to Arequipa.

How to Get Around in Arequipa

The best way to get around the city is by walking or using taxis. Taxis are relatively cheap and can be hailed on the street or through an app. You can also rent a bike to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Arequipa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arequipa is located in a strategic location that makes it easy to explore other famous tourist destinations in Peru. Here are some popular places to visit near Arequipa.

  • Cusco: Cusco is the ancient capital city of the Inca Empire and is a popular destination for travelers visiting Peru. You can explore the city’s Inca ruins, shop for souvenirs, and visit the famous Machu Picchu ruins.
  • Nazca Lines: The Nazca Lines are a series of giant geoglyphs, including shapes of animals and plants that can only be seen from above. You can take a flight over the lines from Arequipa.

Conclusion

Arequipa offers a unique and fascinating experience for travelers looking to explore the culture, history, and natural wonders of Peru. From the Colca Canyon to the Misti Volcano, Arequipa has everything to offer for a fulfilling adventure.
