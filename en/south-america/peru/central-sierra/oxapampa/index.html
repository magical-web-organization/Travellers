Introduction to Oxapampa

Located in the Central Sierra region of Peru, Oxapampa is a charming town nestled in the heart of the Andes Mountains. Known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning landscape, this small town is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you’re interested in exploring the great outdoors, getting a taste of local culture, or simply winding down and relaxing, Oxapampa has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Oxapampa

There are countless places to discover in Oxapampa, from culturally important landmarks to breathtaking natural wonders. Here are just a few of the top attractions to add to your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Chontabamba: Learn about the culture and traditions of the Chontabamba people, one of the indigenous groups that call Oxapampa home.
  • Casa de la Cultura de Oxapampa: This cultural center is dedicated to preserving and celebrating the traditions of Oxapampa, offering workshops, exhibitions, and other cultural activities.
  • Pozuzo Main Square: Take a stroll through the town square and admire the unique blend of German and Peruvian architecture.

Natural Attractions of Oxapampa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Perla Waterfall: This impressive waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a refreshing place to take a dip on a hot day.
  • Oxapampa Ashaninka Yanesha Biosphere Reserve: This UNESCO-recognized reserve is home to an incredible array of rare and endangered species, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers.
  • Cerro El Mirador: Climb to the highest point in Oxapampa and take in panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oxapampa

Oxapampa is a paradise for adventurous travelers, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the best options:

  • Ahuashiyacu Waterfall Trail: This 4.5-kilometer trail takes you through lush rainforest to the stunning Ahuashiyacu waterfall.
  • Pozo de las Animas Trail: This challenging trail takes you to the Pozo de las Animas, a beautiful waterfall and natural swimming hole.
  • El Mirador Trail: This steep hike takes you to the top of Cerro El Mirador, where you can enjoy unparalleled views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Oxapampa: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Oxapampa. Here are a few of the best options:

  • Coffee Plantation Tour: Learn about the coffee-making process from start to finish on a tour of a local coffee plantation.
  • Rafting on the Huancabamba River: Get your adrenaline pumping on a thrilling rafting adventure down the Huancabamba River.
  • Visit to a Traditional Farm: Experience life on a traditional Peruvian farm, complete with an ox-drawn cart ride and a delicious lunch of locally grown food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oxapampa

To make the most of your visit to Oxapampa, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Oxapampa, check into your accommodations, and explore the town square and nearby shops and restaurants.
2 Take a coffee plantation tour in the morning and spend the afternoon hiking to La Perla waterfall.
3 Spend the day exploring the Oxapampa Ashaninka Yanesha Biosphere Reserve, including hiking trails and wildlife watching.
4 Join a guided rafting tour down the Huancabamba River.
5 Take a day trip to Pozuzo, a nearby town founded by German settlers that has a unique blend of German and Peruvian culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Oxapampa

Peruvian cuisine is world-renowned for its bold flavors and fresh ingredients, and Oxapampa is no exception. Here are a few must-try dishes and drinks to sample during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chiriuchu: A traditional Peruvian dish made with different meats, boiled eggs, rocoto pepper, potatoes, and other ingredients, usually eaten during cultural festivities.
  • Pachamanca: Meat cooked underground with hot stones and several vegetables, herbs and spices.
  • Cecina: A thinly sliced salted meat than can be cooked various ways, traditionally served with sweet potato and corn.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A fermented drink made from ground corn that is popular throughout Peru.
  • Masato: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from cassava or maize that is popular in the Amazon region of Peru.
  • Coffee: You can’t visit Oxapampa without trying the local coffee, known for its bold flavor and smooth finish.

Where to Stay in Oxapampa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oxapampa offers a range of accommodation options to fit every budget and preference. Here are a few top picks:

  • Hostal Monte Verde: This budget-friendly hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a location close to the town center.
  • Hotel El Grial: A mid-range option that offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.
  • Villa Santa: A luxurious guesthouse set in a beautiful garden, with spacious rooms, hot tubs, and a restaurant serving farm-to-table cuisine.

What to Buy in Oxapampa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oxapampa is known for its traditional crafts and locally produced goods, making it the perfect place to pick up a unique souvenir or gift. Here are a few top picks:

  • Alpaca wool blankets, hats, and scarves: Made from the softest wool in the world, these cozy items make for a warm and stylish addition to any home or wardrobe.
  • Huayruro Seeds: These brightly colored seeds are believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits.
  • Coffee: Take home a bag of locally grown coffee and enjoy the bold and complex flavor long after your trip ends.

Local Culture and Customs of Oxapampa

Oxapampa is a melting pot of different cultures, with indigenous populations, European settlers, and a thriving Afro-Peruvian community. Some customs and traditions unique to Oxapampa include:

  • Chonguinada: An annual festival celebrated in Oxapampa that blends traditional Andean music and dance with Spanish colonial influences.
  • Carnaval de Yaneshas: Another annual festival that celebrates the Yanesha indigenous community, complete with dancing, music, and traditional food and drinks.
  • Traditional dress: Many locals in Oxapampa still wear traditional clothing, including brightly colored ponchos, wide-brimmed hats, and embroidered blouses.

Best Time to Visit Oxapampa

The best time to visit Oxapampa depends on your priorities and preferences. The rainy season runs from November to April, with the heaviest rain occurring in December and January. The dry season runs from May to October, with warm days and cool nights. Some of the best times to visit for specific activities include:

  • Hiking and outdoor activities: May to September, when the weather is mild and dry.
  • Cultural festivals: July and August, when many festivals and celebrations take place.
  • Coffee season: October to December, when the coffee harvest is in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oxapampa

To ensure a safe and enjoyable visit to Oxapampa, here are a few important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local coffee and traditional dishes.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Spanish to help you communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.
  • Consume tap water without boiling or treating it first.

Safety and Health

Like any travel destination, there are a few key safety and health considerations to keep in mind when visiting Oxapampa.

Safety in Oxapampa

  • Practice basic safety precautions like keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.
  • Watch for uneven pavement and steep drops in the mountains.
  • Use a reputable tour guide or tour company for outdoor activities.

Health in Oxapampa

  • Drink bottled water or treated water.
  • Pack a basic first aid kit with essentials like sunscreen, insect repellent, and bandages.
  • Consult with a doctor about any necessary vaccinations or medications before traveling to Peru.

Getting to Oxapampa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oxapampa is easily accessible via several transportation options. Here are a few of the top ways to get there:

Airports Near Oxapampa

The nearest airport to Oxapampa is the Juan Simons Vela Airport (AYP), which is located approximately two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Oxapampa

Several bus companies offer direct routes to Oxapampa from major cities like Lima and Huancayo.

Other Options

If you’re interested in exploring the surrounding region, renting a car or hiring a driver can be a good option.

How to Get Around in Oxapampa

Walking is a great way to explore the town center of Oxapampa, but if you’re interested in exploring the surrounding areas, consider renting a bike or hiring a local driver. Taxis and colectivos (shared taxis) are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Oxapampa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region surrounding Oxapampa, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Tarma: A picturesque town known for its flower-filled parks and beautiful colonial architecture.
  • Huancayo: A bustling city with a thriving arts and culture scene, known for traditional Andean textiles and handicrafts.
  • Chanchamayo: A beautiful valley located north of Oxapampa, known for its colorful orchids and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Oxapampa is a unique and unforgettable destination that offers a little bit of everything, from natural wonders and outdoor adventures to rich cultural traditions and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re interested in exploring the Andes Mountains or immersing yourself in local culture, Oxapampa is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Peru.
