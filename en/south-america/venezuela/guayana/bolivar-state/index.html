Introduction to Bolívar (State)

Bolívar is a state in Guayana, Venezuela, located in the southern region of the country, bordering Brazil and Guyana. The state is named after one of Venezuela’s most renowned leaders, Simón Bolívar – a prominent military and political leader who played a key role in the liberation of South America from Spanish colonialism.

Bolívar State is rich in history and culture, with a diverse landscape of natural wonders, including the Amazon rainforest, the Gran Sabana, waterfalls, and wildlife. Bolívar is an ideal destination for adventure seekers, culture enthusiasts, and anyone looking for an authentic South American experience.

List of Places to Visit in Bolívar (State)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa San Isidro Museum

One of Bolívar’s most significant cultural attractions, the Casa San Isidro Museum, is situated in Ciudad Bolívar, the state’s capital. The museum is a grand colonial-style house that belonged to one of the wealthiest families during the Spanish colonial era, the Sotillo family. The Casa San Isidro Museum displays a vast collection of art, artefacts, and furniture from the colonial period and narrates the story of the city’s history through exhibitions and cultural events.

Angostura Historical Museum

The Angostura Historical Museum is located in Ciudad Bolívar and housed in an old colonial house that dates back to 1664. The museum is dedicated to the life and achievements of Simón Bolívar, Venezuela’s independence hero. It houses a vast collection of historical pieces, including documents and weapons used by Bolívar and his army during the freedom battles against the Spanish colonisers.

Natural Attractions of Bolívar (State): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Angel Falls

Angel Falls is one of the most iconic natural attractions in Venezuela and the world’s highest waterfall, standing at a height of 979m. The falls are located in Canaima National Park and surrounded by lush greenery, mist, and rainbows, which create an unforgettable sight. Angel Falls can only be accessed by boat or by taking a tourist flight from Ciudad Bolivar.

Roraima Tepui

Roraima Tepui is a remarkable mountain with an elevation of 2,810 meters above sea level. The tepui is located in Canaima National Park and is surrounded by deep canyons and cliffs. The climb to the top of Roraima Tepui takes around two to three days and offers hikers stunning views of the Gran Sabana and beyond.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bolívar (State)

The Lost World Trail

The Lost World Trail is a hiking route that takes adventurers through dense jungle and pristine rainforest, offering stunning views along the way. The trail is located in Gran Sabana and stretches for 16km. Visitors can explore hidden caves, waterfalls and swim in freshwater pools. The trail is relatively challenging and is best suited for experienced hikers.

Bike Route in Auyán-tepui

Biking in Auyán-tepui offers a unique and exhilarating experience, exploring the rugged terrain of the mountainous landscape. The biking route begins in the village of Kavac and takes visitors through an old airstrip, pastureland, and indigenous villages. Biking in Auyán-tepui requires a certain level of fitness and experience.

Best Things to Do in Bolívar (State): Activities and Tours

Helicopter Tours

Helicopter tours are a great way to explore Bolívar State’s diverse landscape, including Angel Fall’s aerial view, Roraima Tepui, and Canaima National Park. The tours range from 45 minutes to an hour and offer a unique perspective of the state as a whole.

Kayaking in Caroni River

Kayaking in Caroni River is an exciting way to explore the natural beauty of Bolívar’s forests. The river runs through the heart of the state, providing an unmissable opportunity to experience the wildlife, birds and flora of the region. The kayaking tour can vary from easy to moderately challenging, depending on the length and the intensity of the route.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bolívar (State)

Day 1: Visit the Angostura Historical Museum and explore the historic city center of Ciudad Bolivar. Day 2: Take a boat tour to Angel Falls and spend the day exploring the national park. Day 3: Do some trekking through the rainforest and visit the village of Kavac. Day 4: Take a helicopter tour to Roraima Tepui. Day 5: Explore the Lost World trail and swim in freshwater pools. Day 6: Visit the Casa San Isidro Museum and enjoy some typical food from the region. Day 7: Kayak down the Caroni River.

Food and Drink Scene of Bolívar (State)

Must-Try Dishes

Arepas

Arepas are a traditional Venezuelan dish, made with maize flour, stuffed with meat, cheese, and other fillings. It is a classic staple food, and every region of Venezuela has its take on the popular dish.

Pabellón Criollo

Pabellón Criollo is a typical Venezuelan dish made with rice, black beans, banana, and shredded beef. It is a comfort meal and a must-try dish when visiting Bolívar State.

Local Beverages

Cacao Juice

Cacao juice is a delicious and refreshing local drink that comes from cacao fruit. The drink has a sweet and tangy flavour and is packed with nutrients, including antioxidants, vitamins and minerals.

Cocuy

Cocuy is a native spirit derived from the agave plant and made through artisanal methods. It is popular in the region and has a unique flavour that is both smooth and potent.

Where to Stay in Bolívar (State): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Gran Sabana

Hotel Gran Sabana is a cozy hotel located in Santa Elena de Uairen, offering comfortable rooms and fantastic views of the Gran Sabana. It is an excellent option for those looking for a more luxurious stay in Bolívar State.

Backpackers Hostel

Backpacker’s Hostel is an affordable and laid-back hostel situated in Ciudad Bolivar. It is a perfect option for those travelling on a budget, offering clean and comfortable beds, great location, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Bolívar (State): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Indigenous Crafts

Bolívar State is home to several indigenous communities that produce beautiful crafts and textiles. Visitors can buy colourful hammocks, carved wooden objects, and clothes, made using traditional techniques, that make excellent souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bolívar (State)

Bolívar State is a diverse and vibrant state, known for its rich cultural heritage and unique customs. The state’s population comprises several indigenous people who have resided in the region for centuries. Bolívar festivities and cultural events are celebrations of diversity and unity, showcasing the region’s diverse musical traditions, dance, crafts, and food.

Best Time to Visit Bolívar (State)

The dry season (December to April) is the best time to visit Bolívar State, as the weather is sunny and dry, giving visitors the opportunity to explore with ease. However, the state is beautiful all year round, and it is a perfect place to visit for adventure and cultural experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bolívar (State)

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take precautions to prevent mosquito bites and other insects in the region.
  • Try the traditional foods and drinks, especially typical dishes from the region.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothes in the more traditional areas.
  • Litter or damage the natural landscape during any activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bolívar (State)

Bolívar State is generally safe for visitors, but it is always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as keeping valuable items safe, using trusted transportation, and staying in well-lit areas. Visitors should also avoid travelling at night, especially on foot.

Health in Bolívar (State)

Bolívar State is located in a tropical region with a warm climate throughout the year, so visitors need to take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites and other insect-borne diseases, such as malaria and yellow fever. Visitors are recommended to get vaccination against these diseases before travelling to Bolívar. Drinking bottled water is also advisable.

Getting to Bolívar (State): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bolívar (State)

The nearest airport to Bolívar is Manuel Carlos Piar Guayana Airport, located in Ciudad Guayana.

Bus Stations Near Bolívar (State)

There are several bus terminals in Ciudad Bolívar where travellers can take buses to other cities in the region.

Other Options

Travellers can also take shared taxis or private cars to get around Bolívar State.

How to Get Around in Bolívar (State)

The best way to get around Bolívar State is by private car or taxi, as there are no official public transportation routes in the area. Tourists wishing to take a tour to natural attractions, such as Angel Falls, must book a tour through a licensed tour company.

Where to Go Next from Bolívar (State): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Bolívar State, visitors can head to nearby destinations such as Gran Sabana, one of the country’s most spectacular nature reserves. Alternatively, visitors can head to Caracas, Venezuela’s capital city, and explore its cultural attractions or beautiful beaches on the Caribbean Coast.

Conclusion

Bolívar State is an exciting and enchanting destination in South America, offering visitors a unique blend of adventure, history, and culture. Visitors can explore the region’s unspoiled natural wonders, taste the divine local cuisine, and learn about the indigenous culture and rich history of the region. Bolívar State has something for everyone, from outdoor thrill-seekers to culture enthusiasts, making it an unforgettable destination.
