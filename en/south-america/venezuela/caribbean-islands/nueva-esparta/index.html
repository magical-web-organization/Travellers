Introduction to Nueva Esparta

Nueva Esparta is a state located in the Caribbean Islands of Venezuela, in South America. Comprising three islands – Margarita, Coche, and Cubagua – Nueva Esparta offers visitors a unique blend of colonial and beachside charm. Whether you’re interested in vibrant nightlife, outdoor adventures, historical sites, or simply soaking up the sun on a secluded beach, there’s something for everyone in Nueva Esparta.

List of Places to Visit in Nueva Esparta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Marino de Margarita: This museum showcases the rich marine life of the Caribbean, with exhibits on fish, coral, and other marine creatures.
  • Fortín de la Galera: A colonial-era fort that once served as a defensive stronghold against pirates.
  • Casa Natal & Museo de Luisa Cáceres de Arismendi: Explore the life of Luisa Cáceres de Arismendi, a revolutionary hero who fought for independence from Spain.

Natural Attractions of Nueva Esparta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Nacional Laguna de la Restinga: This national park features extensive mangrove swamps and a stunning lagoon, perfect for kayaking or boat tours.
  • Playa El Agua: One of the most popular beaches in Nueva Esparta, Playa El Agua offers crystal-clear water and soft white sand, perfect for sunbathing or swimming.
  • La Sierra: Go hiking in the hills that make up the mountain range of La Sierra, offering breathtaking views of Nueva Esparta.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nueva Esparta

  • Cerro El Copey: This is the highest peak of Nueva Esparta, and a challenging yet rewarding day hike for those with good physical endurance.
  • Cerro Guayamuri: This is a less strenuous hike, and allows hikers to see a unique perspective of the island and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Nueva Esparta: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkelling at Los Frailes: This protected marine park is home to a wide variety of marine life, including coral, tropical fish, and even sea turtles.
  • Sailing and Boat Tours: Take a leisurely boat tour around the islands of Nueva Esparta, or go on a sunset sailing trip.
  • Shopping at Sambil Margarita: This large mall features a variety of shops, restaurants, and entertainment options.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nueva Esparta

Food and Drink Scene of Nueva Esparta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pabellón Criollo: A traditional dish made of rice, black beans, shredded beef, and fried plantains.
  • Cachapas: A savory pancake made from fresh corn and stuffed with cheese, beef, or chicken.
  • Asado Negro: A slow-cooked beef dish that is rich and flavorful.

Local Beverages

  • Cocuy: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented agave cactus.
  • Papelón con Limón: A refreshing drink made from lemon juice and sugar cane.

Where to Stay in Nueva Esparta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Margarita’s Marriott Hotel Playa Grande: A luxury hotel situated on Playa Grande, offering a variety of amenities, from a spa and fitness center to fine dining options.
  • Posada La Mar: A stylish and comfortable guesthouse in the heart of Porlamar, offering personalized service and proximity to local attractions.
  • Hostal Posada Villa del Sol: A charming and affordable option located in the more secluded areas of Margarita Island.

What to Buy in Nueva Esparta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Rum: Venezuela is known for its rum, and Nueva Esparta offers many options for bringing home this local product.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Look for unique and intricate pieces made by local artisans.
  • Handmade Textiles: Beautifully crafted scarves, blankets and table runners make for colorful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Nueva Esparta

Nueva Esparta is home to a rich blend of cultures, with influences from natives, Spaniards and Africans. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and the social scene is lively and welcoming. Visitors should also be respectful of the local customs and traditions, which include the wearing of hats when entering certain religious buildings, and speaking to locals in a polite and respectful manner.

Best Time to Visit Nueva Esparta

The best time to visit Nueva Esparta is from December to April. During these months, the weather is dry and sunny, and the temperature ranges from 80-90°F. This makes it the ideal time for beach activities and outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nueva Esparta

Do:

  • Try out the local cuisine and drinks, it’s a huge part of the culture and experience
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take a walk around the local markets, they offer unique shopping experiences

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water; avoid getting sick by drinking bottled water and using it to brush your teeth
  • Walk alone late at night, always stick to well-lit areas and stay within tourist spots
  • Engage in any illegal activities, this could lead to significant legal issues

Safety and Health

Safety in Nueva Esparta

While the crime rates are low in Nueva Esparta, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take precautions against theft or scams. It’s important to stay in tourist areas, not walk alone at night, and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Nueva Esparta

Visitors should take precautions against the spread of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus; wear long sleeves and pants, and use insect repellent.

Getting to Nueva Esparta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nueva Esparta

  • Del Caribe Santiago Mariño International Airport (PMV): Located in Porlamar, Margarita Island, it is the main airport serving Nueva Esparta.

Bus Stations Near Nueva Esparta

  • Terminal de Pasajeros de Porlamar: The main bus terminal serving Nueva Esparta, located in Porlamar.

Other Options

  • Ferry Services: There are ferry services available from Puerto La Cruz in the mainland to Margarita Island.

How to Get Around in Nueva Esparta

The most popular way to get around Nueva Esparta is by taxi, particularly for visitors who don’t have their own transportation. There are also car rental services available, as well as motorcycle rentals for those who prefer something more daring.

Where to Go Next from Nueva Esparta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • La Tortuga Island: A nearby uninhabited island, perfect for a day trip, featuring beautiful beaches and great snorkeling spots.
  • Caracas: The capital city of Venezuela features plenty of culture and history, and is just a short flight away.

Conclusion

Nueva Esparta is an enchanting and unique destination that offers something for everyone. Its breathtaking natural beauty, vibrant culture, and rich history make it a must-see destination for travelers to the Caribbean. Whether you’re looking to sunbathe on white sandy beaches, explore colonial fortifications, or hike the rugged mountains, Nueva Esparta is sure to delight and impress.
