Pedro Gonzalez Island, also known as Isla de Pedro González, is a hidden gem located in the Nueva Esparta state of the Caribbean Islands in Venezuela. This beautiful island is home to a plethora of attractions that cater to every taste, from natural wonders to culturally significant landmarks. In this travel guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, recommended outdoor activities, transportation options, and where to stay while in Pedro Gonzalez.

Introduction to Pedro Gonzalez

Considered one of the smallest islands on the southern coast of the Caribbean Sea, Pedro Gonzalez is a pristine island that boasts a population of less than 5,000 people. The island is known for its serene waters, soft-sand beaches, and beautiful coral reefs. The local people are friendly, and visitors can expect warm hospitality from them.

Local Culture and Customs of Pedro Gonzalez

The people of Pedro Gonzalez Island celebrate their local cultur with passion, and they are always happy to share it with visitors. They celebrate several festivals throughout the year, including:

  • Yare Festival
  • La Tiradera de Coco

Best Time to Visit Pedro Gonzalez

The best time to visit Pedro Gonzalez Island is during the dry season, between December to April. During this time, visitors can enjoy activities like snorkeling, diving, and other water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pedro Gonzalez

Do:

  • Respect the local people and their customs.
  • Carry cash, as some areas may not accept credit cards.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local people and their culture.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public areas.
  • Leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pedro Gonzalez

Pedro Gonzalez is a safe island, but visitors should always take precautions such as locking up valuables, being aware of surroundings, and not venturing into unfamiliar areas alone.

Health in Pedro Gonzalez

Pedro Gonzalez is a safe destination regarding diseases such as Zika and Malaria. Still, it is always advisable to use insect repellent and take appropriate precautions to protect against mosquito bites.
