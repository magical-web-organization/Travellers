Introduction to Aruanã

Aruanã is a charming city in the state of Goiás, located in the Central-West region of Brazil. Known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant outdoor sports activities, Aruanã is the perfect destination for adventurers and nature enthusiasts. This city has a population of around 12 thousand residents who speak Portuguese and Goiás accents, and it is a popular tourist spot both for Brazilians and international visitors. Aruanã is situated on the banks of the Araguaia River, and its stunning landscapes, picturesque beaches, and the tropical climate attract thousands of tourists every year. In this guide, we will explore the must-visit places and exciting things to do in Aruanã.

List of Places to Visit in Aruanã

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aruanã is a city rich in cultural heritage and history. There are several museums and historical sites to explore, including:

  1. Museu de arqueologia de Aruanã: This museum exhibits prehistoric artifacts and archaeological finds from the region, including pottery, weapons, and tools.

  2. Igreja Matriz Nossa Senhora da Saúde: This beautiful church was built in the 19th century and is a popular cultural attraction in Aruanã.

Natural Attractions of Aruanã: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aruanã boasts breathtaking natural beauty, offering visitors opportunities to experience several outdoor adventures. From scenic hikes and treks to water sports activities, there is something for everyone. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Aruanã are:

  1. Praia do Poção: This scenic beach is located in a remote area just a few minutes from the city center. Visitors can relax amid the breathtaking views of the Araguaia River and enjoy a dip in its calm waters.

  2. Parque Estadual da Serra dos Pirineus: This park is located nearby and offers an ample opportunity for hiking, mountain climbing, and enjoying the beautiful views of the waterfalls and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aruanã

Aruanã is famous for its scenic trails and biking routes that offer visitors a chance to explore its beautiful landscapes.

  1. Trilha do Sol: This hiking trail leads to the summit of Serra Negra, providing stunning views of the region.

  2. Ciclovia Aruanã: This cycling route is ideal for a leisurely bike ride and offers stunning views of the city and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Aruanã: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Aruanã. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Araguaia River Boat Tour: Take a relaxing boat ride along the scenic Araguaia River, admiring the views of its natural beauty.

  2. Rafting in Araguaia: Enjoy an adventure-filled day rafting through the mighty rapids of Araguaia’s river.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aruanã

A week in Aruanã can be an unforgettable experience. Here is a proposed itinerary of the places to visit and things to do:

  • Day 1: Arrive, settle in and take a guided city tour.
  • Day 2: Visit the Museu de arqueologia de Aruanã and explore the Igreja Matriz Nossa Senhora da Saúde.
  • Day 3: Go on a guided boat tour on the Araguaia River.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Parque Estadual da Serra dos Pirineus.
  • Day 5: Embark on an adventure-filled day of rafting in Araguaia.
  • Day 6: Go for a leisurely bike ride and explore the scenic routes of Aruanã.
  • Day 7: Relax at Praia do Poção and enjoy a sun-soaked day on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Aruanã

The cuisine of Aruanã is rich in flavors and predominantly regional. Some of the must-try dishes when visiting Aruanã are:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pamonha: This tasty dish is made of fresh corn, grated coconut, and milk, traditionally wrapped in corn husks and boiled or grilled to perfection.

  2. Feijão Tropeiro: This dish is a flavorful mix of beans, rice, bacon, eggs, and cassava flour, garnished with spicy sausage, parsley, and spring onions.

Local Beverages

  1. Cachaça: This spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice is a local favorite and is used to make tasty cocktails.

  2. Graviola Juice: This refreshing drink is made from the sweet and tangy fruit of the graviola tree and is perfect to beat the heat.

What to Buy in Aruanã: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find several souvenirs and local products to take home from Aruanã. Some of the popular items to buy are:

  • Handmade pottery and crafts
  • Cachaça and other local spirits
  • Jewellery made with precious stones found in the region

Local Culture and Customs of Aruanã

Aruanã is a city steeped in culture and has several customs that visitors should be aware of, such as:

  • Respect for elders: Age is highly respected in the region, and visitors should address older people with the appropriate terms of respect.
  • Religious observances: The region is predominantly Catholic, and visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Love for nature: The region is known for its love for nature, and visitors should avoid littering and help in environmental conservation efforts.

Best Time to Visit Aruanã

The best time to visit Aruanã is between May and September when the temperatures are pleasant, and the humidity levels are low, making it the ideal time for outdoor activities and exploring the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aruanã

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the region.
  • Carry sun protection and insect repellent when going outdoors.
  • Explore the scenic trails and natural treasures of Aruanã.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Wear offensive clothing or behave disrespectfully in public places.
  • Engage in unsafe or illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Travelers to Aruanã should be aware of the following safety and health concerns:

Safety in Aruanã

  • Travelers should be cautious when swimming in the Araguaia River, as strong currents and underwater obstacles can be dangerous.
  • Travelers should be aware of petty theft and take necessary precautions to avoid thefts during their stay.

Health in Aruanã

  • Visitors should use insect repellents to avoid mosquito bites and protect themselves from mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever or Zika virus.
  • Visitors should carry a first-aid kit and be prepared for any medical emergencies.
