Introduction to Caldas Novas

Located in the heart of Brazil’s Central West region, Caldas Novas is a popular tourist destination famous for its thermal pools and beautiful countryside. This charming city attracts visitors from all over the world who come to enjoy the hot springs, natural scenery, and vibrant cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Caldas Novas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Caldas Novas has a rich history and culture that can be seen in its numerous museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Caldas Novas include:

  • Museum of Sacred Art – This museum is dedicated to the religious art of the region and houses a vast collection of artifacts dating back to the colonial period.
  • José Barroso Cultural Center – This cultural center is named after the famous Brazilian artist and features his works as well as those of other regional artists.
  • Feira do Luar – This night market offers a unique blend of live music, regional crafts, and delicious food – all in an open-air setting.

Natural Attractions of Caldas Novas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Caldas Novas is blessed with a stunning natural landscape that is perfect for outdoor activities and scenic vistas. Some of the best outdoor attractions in Caldas Novas are:

  • Parque Estadual da Serra de Caldas Novas – This state park offers incredible views of the surrounding countryside with hiking trails, waterfalls, and great picnic spots.
  • Lagoa Quente de Pirapitinga – This natural lake is famous for its crystal clear waters and is the perfect spot for swimming, kayaking, and paddle-boarding.
  • Parque Aquático Hot Park – This water park offers an array of adrenaline-pumping slides, wave pools, and lazy rivers surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caldas Novas

For those who love to get out and explore the great outdoors, Caldas Novas has some excellent hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes to try out:

  • Trilha Ecológica – This hiking trail winds through the beautiful scenery of Parque Estadual da Serra de Caldas Novas.
  • Rio Quente Adventure Park – This adventure park offers several biking routes with varying degrees of difficulty.
  • Caldas Novas Bike Tour – This guided bike tour takes visitors on a scenic ride through the city and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Caldas Novas: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities and tours in Caldas Novas that offer something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Thermal Pools – Caldas Novas is famous for its thermal pools, and visitors can choose from a variety of pools and hot springs to relax and unwind.
  • Safari das Emas – This ecological reserve offers visitors a unique guided safari tour to see the native flora and fauna of the region.
  • Balão Panorâmico – Take a hot air balloon ride to enjoy breathtaking views of the city and surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caldas Novas

Here is an example week-long itinerary of what to do and see during your visit to Caldas Novas:

Day 1 - Arrive in Caldas Novas and check into your hotel or guest house. Relax in one of the thermal pools.

Day 2 - Visit the Museum of Sacred Art and have lunch at one of the many delicious restaurants in the city.

Day 3 - Take a bike tour of Caldas Novas and try some local treats at the Feira do Luar night market.

Day 4 - Explore Parque Estadual da Serra de Caldas Novas and have a picnic lunch.

Day 5 - Spend the day at the Hot Park water park and enjoy all the rides.

Day 6 - Take a guided safari tour at the Safari das Emas and have dinner at one of the many local restaurants.

Day 7 - Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning and then spend your afternoon at Lagoa Quente de Pirapitinga.

Food and Drink Scene of Caldas Novas

Caldas Novas has a vibrant food and drink scene that is a must-experience for foodies. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pamonha - A traditional Brazilian dish made with sweet corn that is boiled in the husk and served hot with butter and cheese.
  • Leitão a Pururuca - Slow-roasted suckling pig with crispy skin that is a popular dish in the region.
  • Feijão Tropeiro - A flavorful mixture of beans, bacon, sausage, and dried beef that is served as a side to many meals.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - This popular Brazilian spirit is made from sugarcane juice and is similar to rum.
  • Cerveja de Garrafa - This bottled beer is a favorite in the region, and visitors can try it at many local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Caldas Novas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Caldas Novas has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Caldas Novas include:

  • Rio Quente Resorts and Spa - This high-end resort offers luxurious accommodations, thermal pools, and an array of amenities for guests.
  • Eco Chalé Jardim Acqua - This eco-friendly guesthouse is located in Parque Estadual da Serra de Caldas Novas and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hostel Tribo da Lua - This charming hostel is located in the heart of Caldas Novas and is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Caldas Novas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Caldas Novas is known for its local crafts and products that make great souvenirs to take home. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Caldas Novas Soap - This unique handmade soap is made with local herbs and flowers and is available in many shops and markets.
  • Handpainted Ceramics - The region is famous for its beautiful hand-painted ceramics that come in a variety of styles and colors.
  • Serra de Caldas Novas Honey - This delicious honey is made from the flowers in the region and is available in many local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Caldas Novas

Caldas Novas has a rich culture and customs that visitors should be aware of during their stay.

  • Religion - The region has a strong Catholic tradition, and visitors should be respectful when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Language - Portuguese is the official language of Brazil, but many locals also speak Spanish.
  • Greeting Customs - Brazilians are generally friendly and warm, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake or hug.

Best Time to Visit Caldas Novas

The best time to visit Caldas Novas is from April to October when the weather is cooler, and there is less rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caldas Novas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit:

Do:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated in the heat.
  • Try the local cuisine and crafts to get a taste of the region’s culture.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended, especially in crowded areas.
  • Drive without a valid driver’s license and insurance.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caldas Novas

Caldas Novas is generally considered a safe city, but visitors should take basic safety precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable stay.

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Use a trusted taxi service or Uber when traveling at night.

Health in Caldas Novas

Visitors to Caldas Novas should take basic health precautions to stay healthy during their stay.

  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach illness.
  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases.
  • Take precautions when swimming in natural bodies of water to avoid waterborne illness.

Getting to Caldas Novas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Caldas Novas is easily accessible from nearby cities and airports. Here are some of the best transportation options:

Airports Near Caldas Novas

The nearest major airport to Caldas Novas is Brasília International Airport, which is approximately 200 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Caldas Novas

The city has a major bus terminal with buses arriving from major Brazilian cities like Brasília, São Paulo and Goiânia on a regular basis.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car or taxi or rent a car to reach Caldas Novas.

How to Get Around in Caldas Novas

Taxis and Uber are widely available in Caldas Novas, and visitors can easily hire them to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Caldas Novas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Caldas Novas is also a great jumping-off point to explore other nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some of the best:

  • Pirenópolis - This charming colonial town is located just over an hour away from Caldas Novas and offers beautiful architecture and natural attractions.
  • Chapada dos Veadeiros - This national park is located a few hours’ drive from Caldas Novas and offers stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and wildlife sightings.
  • Brasília - Brazil’s capital city is just a few hours away from Caldas Novas and offers fascinating architecture and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Caldas Novas is a fantastic addition to any Brazilian travel itinerary with its stunning natural scenery, rich cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. Follow this comprehensive guide to explore all the best parts of this vibrant city and its surrounding region.
