Introduction to Corumbá de Goiás

Corumbá de Goiás is a charming colonial town located in the state of Goiás, in the Central West region of Brazil. Known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and cultural attractions, Corumbá de Goiás is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Brazilian experience. The town is located near the Serra dos Pirineus mountains and offers visitors the opportunity to explore the stunning natural landscapes of the Cerrado biome.

List of Places to Visit in Corumbá de Goiás

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nossa Senhora da Penha Church: Built in the 18th century, this church is one of the most important historical landmarks in Corumbá de Goiás. Its baroque architecture and beautiful artwork make it a must-visit attraction for art and history enthusiasts.
  • Museum of Religious Art: Housed in a historic building, this museum showcases a collection of religious art and artifacts from the colonial era.
  • Corumbá de Goiás Cultural Center: Featuring a theater, art gallery, and library, this cultural center is a hub for cultural activities and events in the town.

Natural Attractions of Corumbá de Goiás: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra dos Pirineus National Park: Located just outside of Corumbá de Goiás, this national park offers visitors the chance to explore the stunning mountain landscapes and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and birdwatching.
  • Venda Velha Waterfall: This picturesque waterfall is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.
  • Mirante do Cruzeiro: A lookout point with incredible views of the town and surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corumbá de Goiás

  • Serra dos Pirineus National Park Trails: The park offers several hiking trails of varying difficulty levels that provide visitors with stunning views of the mountain landscapes and Cerrado biome.
  • Cachoeira do Lajeado Trail: A scenic hiking trail that leads visitors to a beautiful waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Corumbá de Goiás: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the town’s historic center: Take a guided walking tour of Corumbá de Goiás to learn about the town’s rich colonial history and see its beautiful historic architecture.
  • Visit a local farm: Take a tour of a local farm to learn about the region’s agricultural traditions and sample some of the locally grown produce.
  • Attend a cultural event: Check the town’s calendar of events to see if there are any cultural festivals or celebrations happening during your visit.

Food and Drink Scene of Corumbá de Goiás

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pequi: A fruit native to the Cerrado biome, pequi is a key ingredient in many local dishes, including the traditional dish pequi rice.
  • Pastel de Gueroba: A savory pastry filled with gueroba (a type of palm heart) and cheese, a popular snack in the region.
  • Cerveja Artesanal: Locally brewed craft beer made with local ingredients such as pequi and guavira.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane juice, cachaça is a popular drink in Brazil and is produced locally in Corumbá de Goiás.
  • Guavira: A fruit native to the Cerrado biome, guavira is used to make a delicious juice that is refreshing and rich in vitamin C.

Where to Stay in Corumbá de Goiás: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada dos Pireneus: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of town, Pousada dos Pireneus offers cozy accommodations and personalized service.
  • Pousada Amarela: A rustic guesthouse located in a beautifully restored colonial building, Pousada Amarela offers a unique and authentic experience for visitors.
  • Hotel Serra das Águas: Located just outside of town, this hotel features comfortable accommodations and stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

What to Buy in Corumbá de Goiás: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cachaça: Bring home a bottle of locally produced cachaça to enjoy and share with friends and family back home.
  • Handicrafts: Corumbá de Goiás is known for its handicrafts made by local artisans, including ceramics, pottery, and woven textiles.
  • Pequi Oil: A specialty product made from pequi fruit, pequi oil is used in cooking and has a distinct flavor that is unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Corumbá de Goiás

Corumbá de Goiás has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the indigenous peoples, Portuguese colonizers, and African slaves who shaped the region’s history. The town is known for its traditional festivals and celebrations, including the Festa do Divino Espírito Santo and the Festa de São Sebastião, which showcase local music, dance, and food. Visitors are encouraged to participate in these events to experience the local culture and customs firsthand.

Best Time to Visit Corumbá de Goiás

The best time to visit Corumbá de Goiás is between May and September when the weather is dry and temperatures are cooler. The town can get busy during the summer months (December-February) and during major holidays, so it’s best to plan ahead if traveling during these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corumbá de Goiás

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take a guided walking tour of the town’s historic center to learn about its rich history.
  • Try the local cuisine, including traditional dishes made with pequi and gueroba.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural landmarks and attractions.
  • Disrespect the local traditions or cultural practices.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Corumbá de Goiás

Corumbá de Goiás is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but like in any other place, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night in unlit or deserted areas and keep valuables out of sight. Be aware of your surroundings and take caution when traveling in remote areas.

Health in Corumbá de Goiás

Visitors to Corumbá de Goiás should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Brazil. It’s also recommended to receive vaccinations for hepatitis A, typhoid fever, and yellow fever. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria by using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing. It’s important to consult with a healthcare provider before traveling to Corumbá de Goiás to ensure that you are taking the necessary health precautions.

Getting to Corumbá de Goiás: Transportation Options and Logistics

Corumbá de Goiás is located approximately 115 kilometers from Brasília, the capital of Brazil.

Airports Near Corumbá de Goiás

The closest airport to Corumbá de Goiás is Brasília International Airport (BSB), located approximately 115 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Corumbá de Goiás

Visitors can travel to Corumbá de Goiás via bus from Brasília. The bus ride takes approximately 2 hours and 30 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Brasília to Corumbá de Goiás.

How to Get Around in Corumbá de Goiás

Corumbá de Goiás is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Corumbá de Goiás: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pirenópolis: Located approximately 50 kilometers from Corumbá de Goiás, Pirenópolis is a charming colonial town known for its historic architecture and natural attractions.
  • Chapada dos Veadeiros: A national park located approximately 200 kilometers from Corumbá de Goiás, Chapada dos Veadeiros is a stunning natural wonderland with waterfalls, hiking trails, and incredible landscapes.

Conclusion

Corumbá de Goiás is a hidden gem in the heart of Brazil, offering visitors a unique and authentic travel experience. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and cultural attractions, Corumbá de Goiás is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Central West region of Brazil.
