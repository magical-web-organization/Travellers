Introduction to Cavalcante

Located in the heart of Brazil, Cavalcante is a small town in the Goiás state. This hidden gem is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking an adventurous and authentic experience.

List of Places to Visit in Cavalcante

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceição: This Catholic church is one of the oldest in the region and has an impressive colonial style architecture.
  • Casa de Cultura Cavalcanti: This cultural center showcases the history, art, and traditions of Cavalcante and the nearby region.
  • Casa da Lua: This artistic and cultural center is dedicated to preserving and promoting the indigenous culture and language of the Kalunga people, who have inhabited the region for generations.

Natural Attractions of Cavalcante: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cachoeira da Capivara: This stunning waterfall is located only 2km from the town center, and its crystal-clear waters and lush surroundings make it an unforgettable experience.
  • Parque Nacional da Chapada dos Veadeiros: This nature reserve is located just a few kilometers from Cavalcante and covers over 200,000 hectares. The park boasts a diverse variety of flora and fauna, as well as breathtaking trails and waterfalls.
  • Canyon do Rio Poti: This natural formation is located in the Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park and is a great destination for hiking, canyoning, and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cavalcante

Cavalcante is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of routes available to explore both the town’s surroundings and the Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Trilha dos Cânions: This trekking route spans approximately 12km and takes you through breathtaking canyons, natural pools, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Trilha do Mirante da Ventania: This hike takes you up to a lookout point that offers a fantastic view of the Serra Geral range and the beautiful surrounding valleys.
  • Trilha das Cachoeiras dos Macaquinhos: This trail is perfect for those who want to explore the area’s stunning waterfalls. The route covers approximately 8km and takes you through a beautiful forest.

Best Things to Do in Cavalcante: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Rafting on Rio dos Couros: The Rio dos Couros is considered one of the most beautiful rivers in Brazil, and kayaking and rafting are perfect ways to explore it.
  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a fantastic way to explore the town’s surroundings and get a glimpse of the local wildlife and flora.
  • Indigenous Cultural Tours: Exploring the indigenous culture and traditions is a must-do experience in Cavalcante, and several tours are available to take you through the Kalunga community and learn about their customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cavalcante

Day 1: Arrive in Cavalcante and explore the town’s historic center. Day 2: Visit the Casa de Cultura Cavalcante and learn about the region’s cultural heritage. Day 3: Trek through the Trilha dos Cânions and admire the breathtaking natural scenery. Day 4: Take a day trip to the Parque Nacional da Chapada dos Veadeiros and explore its trails, waterfalls, and natural formations. Day 5: Indulge in adventure sports such as kayaking, rafting, and canyoning. Day 6: Visit the indigenous community of Kalunga and learn about their traditions and culture. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of Cavalcante before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Cavalcante

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pequi: This traditional dish is made from a small fruit with yellow flesh and a bitter taste, and is typically served with rice and meat.
  • Scraps: This dish of pork scraps, cassava, and cumin is a local favorite and is served on special occasions.
  • Pamonha: A delicious sweet corn cake wrapped in corn husks and cooked in boiling water.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This alcoholic drink is made from fermented sugarcane juice and is the most popular spirit in Brazil.
  • Quentão: A traditional Brazilian drink made with cachaça, sugar, cloves, cinnamon, and ginger. Perfect for cold nights!
  • Guaraná Jesus: A bright pink soft drink that is unique to the region and is made with guaraná fruit.

Where to Stay in Cavalcante: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cavalcante offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Whether you prefer guesthouses with a rustic and authentic charm or hotels with modern amenities, you will find the perfect place to stay. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Pousada dos Candeeiros: This charming guesthouse is located in the historic center of Cavalcante and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Pousada-Camping Raizama: This eco-friendly guesthouse is located in the middle of the Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park and offers a unique opportunity to experience nature in its purest form.
  • Pousada Vale das Araras: This cozy guesthouse is located near the Rio dos Couros and offers a beautiful view of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

What to Buy in Cavalcante: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Trinkets and Souvenirs: The Casa de Cultura Cavalcante and the Casa da Lua offer a variety of handicrafts and souvenirs made by the local indigenous communities, such as necklaces, bracelets, and traditional clothing.
  • Pequi Oil: This natural oil is extracted from the pequi fruit and is a popular ingredient in the local cuisine.
  • Cachaça: You can find artisanal cachaça produced by local distilleries and bring home a taste of Brazil.

Local Culture and Customs of Cavalcante

The town of Cavalcante is home to several indigenous communities, such as Kalunga, and it’s essential to respect their customs and traditions. Visitors should avoid taking pictures of people without their permission, respecting the sacred places, and buying souvenirs only from authorized sellers.

Best Time to Visit Cavalcante

The best time to visit Cavalcante is from May to September when the weather is milder and more comfortable for outdoor activities. The rainy season lasts from December to February, and the dry season from April to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cavalcante

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Bring suitable footwear and clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their consent.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Buy souvenirs from unauthorized sellers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cavalcante

Cavalcante is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of petty theft and take precautions to protect their belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Cavalcante

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria by using insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Cavalcante: Transportation Options, and Logistics

Airports Near Cavalcante

The closest airport to Cavalcante is Brasília International Airport, which is approximately a 4-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Cavalcante

The closest bus station is in Alto Paraíso de Goiás, which is approximately 50km from Cavalcante. Buses from the Brasília Terminal Rodoviário can be taken to Alto Paraíso de Goiás, and a local bus can be taken from there to Cavalcante.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and private transfers are also available options for those who prefer more private transportation.

How to Get Around in Cavalcante

The town of Cavalcante is small and walkable, but to explore the surrounding areas, it’s recommended to rent a car or book a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Cavalcante: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Alto Paraíso de Goiás: This charming town is located only 50km from Cavalcante and is home to beautiful waterfalls, natural pools, and hiking trails.
  • Rio Quente: Known for its thermal waters and hot springs, this popular tourist destination is located approximately 200km from Cavalcante.
  • Brasília: The capital of Brazil, Brasília, is located approximately four hours from Cavalcante, and its futuristic architecture and cultural attractions make it a must-visit destination.

Conclusion

Cavalcante is a hidden gem in the heart of Brazil and offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. From its natural wonders and adventurous outdoor activities to its rich cultural heritage and authentic local cuisine, Cavalcante is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
