Introduction to Passo do Lontra

Passo do Lontra is located in the Mato Grosso do Sul region of Central West in Brazil. The region is called Pantanal, which is known for its wildlife, including jaguars, anacondas, and giant otters, making this a must-visit destination for nature-lovers. Passo do Lontra is a small town in the heart of the Pantanal wetlands and is surrounded by stunning natural beauty with many outdoor activities to take part in.

List of Places to Visit in Passo do Lontra

Passo do Lontra offers a wide array of culturally significant and natural attractions for tourists to explore. Here is a list of must-visit places in Passo do Lontra.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Municipal Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Passo do Lontra, showcasing the region’s heritage. You can learn about the history of cattle farming in the wetlands and the traditional lifestyle of the locals.
  • The Indigenous Village: It is a live museum where you can learn about traditional life in a tribal village. Visitors can see how indigenous people make crafts and learn about their culture.

Natural Attractions of Passo do Lontra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fishing in rivers and wetlands: Passengers can fish for piranha or other fish in the rivers and wetlands. Small boats can take you to the best fishing spots.
  • Hiking trails: Passo do Lontra offers a fantastic network of hiking trails, running through the beautiful villages, rivers, and forests.
  • Horseback Riding: A guided horseback ride with a local guide is a perfect way to explore the surrounding tropical countryside, with the sights and sounds of birds, monkeys, and other animals.
  • Wildlife Safaris: Pantanal is famous for its unique wildlife. Wildlife safaris through the wetlands and savannahs of Pantanal are popular tours in Passo do Lontra.
  • River safaris: Passo do Lontra is known for its breathtaking river safaris through the rivers which intersect the Pantanal wetlands where visitors can spot various wild animals like capybaras, otters, and wolves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Passo do Lontra

Passo do Lontra is a great place to trek and bike through the wilderness of Pantanal. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Passo do Lontra.

  • Rota dos Passaros (Bird Route)
  • Rota do Tamanduá – Mirante da Serra (Anteater Route – Serra Viewpoint)
  • Estrada Parque do Pantanal (Pantanal Highway)
  • Serra do Amolar Trek

Best Things to Do in Passo do Lontra: Activities and Tours

  • Jaguar Safaris: Passo do Lontra is an excellent place to go on a jaguar safari tour to observe this elusive predator in the wild.
  • Piranha Fishing: Fishing in the rivers and wetlands is one of the most popular activities in the region.
  • Wildlife Safaris: Explore the region’s abundant wildlife on a wildlife safari tour.
  • Indigenous Village Tour: A tour to the indigenous village to explore traditional lifestyles.

Food and Drink Scene of Passo do Lontra

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz Carreteiro: Rice, beef, onion, and garlic are cooked together in a single pot.
  • Feijão Tropeiro: Beans and meat fried with manioc flour and served with rice.
  • Churrasco: Barbequed beef skewers are a traditional dish not to be missed.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: The sugar cane-based alcohol is very popular in Brazil and is distilled locally in Passo do Lontra.
  • Tereré: Mate tea is served cold with herbs and water. It is a perfect refresher in the tropical heat.

Where to Stay in Passo do Lontra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors have a variety of options to choose from when it comes to their accommodation in Passo do Lontra. Here are some popular places to stay.

  • Hostel Pantanal - A high-quality hostel with a great location.
  • Pousada Tia Jussara - An eco-friendly guesthouse located near Municipal Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of Passo do Lontra

Passo do Lontra has a rich cultural heritage shaped by indigenous culture and cattle ranching. Tourists are advised to respect the culture and customs of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Passo do Lontra

The best time to visit Passo do Lontra is from June to September as the weather is cooler, and wildlife is easy to spot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Passo do Lontra

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow the wildlife guidelines and regulations strictly.
  • Carry insect repellent while venturing out.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment,
  • Bring or purchase any wildlife products.

Safety and Health

Safety in Passo do Lontra

Passo do Lontra is relatively safe. However, be mindful of petty thefts like pickpocketing in crowded areas. Avoid traveling alone, especially at night.

Health in Passo do Lontra

The region is safe and has a low risk of malaria. However, visitors are advised to carry insect repellent and dress up in full-sleeved clothes at dawn and dusk when mosquitoes are most active.

Getting to Passo do Lontra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Passo do Lontra

  • Campo Grande International Airport (CGR)

Bus Stations Near Passo do Lontra

  • Campo Grande Bus Station
  • Corumbá Bus Station

Other Options

One may rent a car or book a cab service to reach Passo do Lontra.

How to Get Around in Passo do Lontra

Passo do Lontra is a relatively small town with most attractions within walking distances. Visitors can rent bicycles from local shops to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Passo do Lontra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bonito

Bonito is a popular tourist destination located about 300 km from Passo do Lontra. Bonito is famous for its waterfalls and is an excellent place to go snorkeling, rafting, and cavern explorations.

Corumbá

Corumbá is about 140 km north of Passo do Lontra; it is a great place to learn more about the region’s culture and customs. Corumbá is also a gateway to Bolivia.

Conclusion

Passo do Lontra offers a beautiful blend of cultural history and natural beauty. Visitors to Passo do Lontra can explore the local culture, wildlife, and natural beauty of the Pantanal wetlands. From walking tours to hiking trails or river rafting tours and wildlife safaris, there is something for everyone in Passo do Lontra.
