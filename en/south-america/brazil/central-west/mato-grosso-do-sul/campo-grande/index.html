Introduction to Campo Grande

Located in the heart of Brazil’s Central West region, Campo Grande is a vibrant and picturesque city that boasts a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Known locally as the “Capital of the Brazilian Pantanal,” this bustling metropolis offers a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests.

Whether you’re a nature lover looking to explore the lush rainforests and wetlands of the surrounding countryside, a history buff keen to discover the city’s colonial past, or a foodie eager to sample the local cuisine, Campo Grande is the perfect destination for you.

In this comprehensive Campo Grande travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through everything this incredible city has to offer, from must-visit sights and top-rated restaurants to hidden gems and insider tips. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready to experience the very best of Campo Grande!

List of Places to Visit in Campo Grande

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Campo Grande is a city steeped in history and culture, with a rich heritage that dates back centuries. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical landmarks in the city:

  • Museum of Contemporary Art: Showcasing the works of some of Brazil’s most talented contemporary artists, this museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the country’s thriving art scene.

  • Dom Bosco Museum: Dedicated to the life and legacy of Italian missionary Saint Dom Bosco, this fascinating museum offers a unique insight into the history and culture of Campo Grande’s Catholic community.

  • Morada dos Baís: Originally built in the early 20th century as a private residence, this stunning colonial-style building is now a cultural center that hosts a wide range of events and exhibitions throughout the year.

  • Palace of Lions: Constructed in the early 20th century as a seat of government, this magnificent building is now home to the Museum of Image and Sound, which boasts a vast collection of photographs, films, and music recordings that chronicle the history of Campo Grande and the wider Mato Grosso do Sul region.

Natural Attractions of Campo Grande: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, you’ll be spoilt for choice in Campo Grande. Here are some of the best natural attractions and outdoor activities to experience in the city:

  • Parque das Nações Indígenas: Located in the heart of Campo Grande, this sprawling park is a great place to soak up the sun and enjoy a picnic with friends and family. With plenty of walking trails, bike paths, and wide open spaces, it’s the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse yourself in nature.

  • Gruta do Lago Azul: One of the most stunning natural wonders in the region, this deep-blue lake is surrounded by a network of underground caverns that are covered in sparkling stalactites and stalagmites. Guided tours are available for those brave enough to venture into the cave system.

  • Pantanal Adventure Tour: If you’re looking for a truly unforgettable experience, why not take a guided tour of the Pantanal wetlands? This vast network of lagoons, rivers, and flooded grasslands is home to an incredible array of wildlife, including capybaras, jaguars, and anacondas.

  • Parque Estadual do Prosa: This wildlife reserve is home to a wide range of exotic flora and fauna, with plenty of hiking trails and outdoor activities on offer. Whether you’re a keen birdwatcher or an avid naturalist, there’s something here for everyone.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campo Grande

For fitness enthusiasts and outdoor lovers, Campo Grande is an ideal destination. With its network of scenic trails and bike paths, there’s no better way to explore the natural beauty of the region than by foot or bike. Here are some of our favorite hiking and biking routes:

  • Serra de Maracaju: This challenging hike takes you up to the summit of the Serra de Maracaju, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Parque dos Poderes: With its wide, flat trails and spacious parkland, Parque dos Poderes is the perfect destination for a leisurely bike ride or easy hike.

  • Jardim Botânico de Campo Grande: Home to a wide range of exotic plants and flowers, this botanic garden offers a tranquil escape from the busy city streets. With its network of meandering trails and scenic vistas, it’s the perfect place to explore by foot or bike.

Best Things to Do in Campo Grande: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for an adrenaline-pumping adventure or a more laid-back sightseeing tour, Campo Grande has something to offer everyone. Here are some of our top-rated activities and tours:

  • Bike Tour of Campo Grande: This guided bike tour takes you through some of the city’s most scenic neighborhoods, stopping off at key landmarks and attractions along the way.

  • Aerial View of Campo Grande: This thrilling helicopter ride gives you a bird’s-eye view of the city and surrounding countryside, offering spectacular views of the Pantanal wetlands and surrounding mountains.

  • Cachaça Tasting Tour: Brazil is famous for its cachaça, a distilled spirit made from sugarcane. This guided tasting tour takes you to some of the region’s top producers, giving you an insight into the history and culture of this beloved beverage.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Campo Grande, here’s a suggested itinerary that takes in all the city’s top attractions and activities:

Food and Drink Scene of Campo Grande

No visit to Campo Grande would be complete without trying some of the city’s delicious local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz Carreteiro: A hearty rice-based dish that’s made with beef, onions, and garlic. Perfect for a filling lunch or dinner.

  • Pão de Queijo: A popular Brazilian snack that’s made with cheese and tapioca flour. Perfect for a mid-afternoon pick-me-up.

  • Feijão Tropeiro: This hearty bean stew is a staple of Brazilian cuisine, and is usually made with bacon, sausage, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: Brazil’s national spirit is made from sugarcane and is famous for its distinctive taste and smooth finish. Perfect for sipping on its own or mixing into cocktails.

  • Guarana: This refreshing soft drink is made from the guarana berry, which is believed to have energizing properties. Perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot day.

What to Buy in Campo Grande: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a souvenir or gift to remember your trip to Campo Grande, here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Handmade crafts: Campo Grande is known for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, with a range of local artisans producing beautiful ceramics, textiles, and other handmade items.

  • Cachaça: If you’ve developed a taste for Brazil’s favorite spirit, why not bring home a bottle of locally produced cachaça to enjoy with friends and family back home?

  • Brazilian barbecue spices: Brazilian cuisine is famous for its smoky, spicy flavors. Bring home a selection of delicious barbecue spices and condiments to recreate your favorite dishes back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Campo Grande

As with any destination, Campo Grande has its own unique customs and traditions. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture:

  • Greetings: Brazilians are generally very friendly and outgoing, and will often greet one another with a warm hug or kiss on the cheek.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Brazil, but it’s common to leave a small amount of change or round the bill up to the nearest whole number to show your appreciation.

  • Dress code: The dress code in Campo Grande is generally quite casual, but it’s always a good idea to dress modestly and cover your shoulders and knees when visiting religious sites or participating in formal events.

Best Time to Visit Campo Grande

The best time to visit Campo Grande is between May and September, when the weather is cooler and drier, and the natural attractions of the region are at their most spectacular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campo Grande

Before you visit Campo Grande, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region, taking in the lush rainforests and wetlands that make the area so special.

  • Sample the local cuisine, trying some of the delicious dishes and beverages that are unique to the region.

  • Be friendly and open with the locals, who are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the importance of sun safety, with the region experiencing high temperatures for much of the year.

  • Don’t forget to bring your appetite, as the local cuisine is one of the highlights of any trip to Campo Grande.

Safety and Health

As with any destination, it’s important to stay safe and healthy when visiting Campo Grande. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Campo Grande

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you when walking around the city, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

  • Stick to well-lit, populated areas when walking at night.

  • Use official taxi services rather than hailing cabs on the street, and make sure to agree on the fare before beginning your journey.

Health in Campo Grande

  • Remember to drink plenty of water and wear sun protection when exploring the city, as temperatures can get extremely high during the summer months.

  • Be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and Zika virus, and take appropriate precautions to avoid being bitten.

Getting to Campo Grande: Transportation Options and Logistics

Campo Grande is well connected to the rest of Brazil and South America, with a range of transportation options available to visitors. Here are some of the most convenient ways to get to the city:

  • Airports Near Campo Grande: The city is served by Campo Grande International Airport, with regular flights available from major cities throughout Brazil and South America.

  • Bus Stations Near Campo Grande: There are several long-distance bus stations located in and around the city, with regular services available from destinations throughout the region.

  • Other Options: Campo Grande is also well connected by road and rail, with major highways and train lines connecting the city to destinations throughout Brazil and beyond.

How to Get Around in Campo Grande

Getting around Campo Grande is easy and convenient, with a range of transportation options available to visitors. Here are some of the best ways to get around the city:

  • Bus: The local bus network is extensive and affordable, with regular services available to most major attractions and landmarks throughout the city.

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available throughout Campo Grande, and are a convenient way to get around the city if you’re short on time or looking for a more comfortable option.

  • Bike: With its network of scenic trails and bike paths, Campo Grande is a great city for cycling, with several bike rental services available for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Campo Grande: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip to Campo Grande, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Bonito: Located just a few hours south of Campo Grande, Bonito is famous for its crystal-clear rivers and underground caves, making it the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

  • Corumbá: Situated on the border with Bolivia, Corumbá is a historic town that offers a fascinating glimpse into the region’s colonial past.

  • Pantanal Wetlands: For those who didn’t get enough of the Pantanal during their time in Campo Grande, exploring the larger region is an adventure worth exploring!

Conclusion

Campo Grande is a city that has something for everyone, from history and culture to nature and adventure. With its stunning natural beauty, vibrant arts scene, and delicious local cuisine, this Central West metropolis is a destination that’s sure to capture your heart and imagination.
