Introduction to Mato Grosso do Sul

Situated in Central West, Brazil, Mato Grosso do Sul is a state that boasts incredible natural beauty, rich culture, and fascinating history. The region’s name translates to “thick bushes of the south,” highlighting the abundance of lush forests that cover its landmass of over 350,000 square kilometers. Mato Grosso do Sul is also home to the iconic Pantanal, the world’s largest inland wetland, which harbors an incredible range of flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul has a diverse array of attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural wonders. Here are a few must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Image and Sound of Mato Grosso do Sul: located in the capital city of Campo Grande, this museum explores the local cultural and artistic heritage.
  • Corumba Historical Museum: this museum in Corumba showcases the city’s rich past and its importance in colonial trade.

Natural Attractions of Mato Grosso do Sul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Pantanal: a network of wetlands and rivers that spans over 210,000 square kilometers, the Pantanal is an unmatched haven for ecotourism and animal watching.
  • Bonito: famous for its crystal-clear blue rivers and stunning waterfalls, Bonito is a top attraction for nature lovers.
  • Parque das Nações Indígenas: a park in Campo Grande that features an indigenous village, gardens, and numerous walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul’s vast landscapes make it an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few recommended routes:

  • Estrada Parque: a scenic stretch of road that runs through the Pantanal, offering beautiful views of the wetlands and wildlife.
  • Gruta de Sao Miguel: located in Bonito, this trail passes through small caves and narrow passageways, culminating in fantastic views of the surrounding forests and hills.

Best Things to Do in Mato Grosso do Sul: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the region’s natural beauty, there are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy, including:

  • Wildlife watching safaris in the Pantanal.
  • River snorkeling in Bonito, where visitors can witness diverse marine life.
  • Visiting indigenous villages to learn about their way of life and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul’s gastronomy scene is influenced by indigenous, Portuguese, and African cultures. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carne de Sol: a slow-cooked, salted beef dish that pairs well with rice and beans.
  • Arroz Carreteiro: a flavorful rice dish made with beef jerky, onions, and peppers.
  • Chipa: a gluten-free cheese bread made from cassava flour and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Tereré: a traditional drink made from yerba mate served cold, usually shared with friends in a circle.
  • Guavira Liquor: a sweet, fruity liquor made from the guavira fruit, endemic to the region.
  • Cachaça: a distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice, usually served in caipirinhas or other cocktails.

Where to Stay in Mato Grosso do Sul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mato Grosso do Sul has accommodation options for every budget, from luxury hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Jatoba Lodge in the Pantanal: a stunning lodge offering immersive ecotourism experiences and top-notch accommodation.
  • Hostel Bonito: a budget-friendly hostel in Bonito with comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a great location.

What to Buy in Mato Grosso do Sul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mato Grosso do Sul has several unique souvenirs and local products to take back home, including:

  • Handmade crafts and jewelry made by indigenous communities.
  • Leather items, such as belts, wallets, and bags.
  • Jams and jellies made from local fruits like guava and pequi.

Local Culture and Customs of Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul’s culture and customs are influenced by indigenous communities, European colonialism, and African heritage. Visitors should respect and embrace the diversity of the region while making an effort to learn about and appreciate its unique heritage.

Best Time to Visit Mato Grosso do Sul

The best time to visit Mato Grosso do Sul is between May and October, during the dry season. This is a great time to explore the Pantanal and other natural destinations, as the weather is mild and wildlife sightings are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mato Grosso do Sul

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try to learn some Portuguese phrases, as English is not widely spoken.
  • Be respectful to wildlife and follow eco-tourism guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Overpack, as the region is warm throughout the year.
  • Underestimate the power of the sun, as even in the winter months, the region can get quite hot.
  • Disrespect the environment by littering or disturbing natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul is a relatively safe destination, although travelers should always exercise caution and avoid walking alone in the dark, crowded areas, or secluded locations.

Health in Mato Grosso do Sul

Visitors should consult their doctor and obtain proper travel vaccinations before visiting Mato Grosso do Sul. It is also essential to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Mato Grosso do Sul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mato Grosso do Sul

The Campo Grande International Airport (CGR) is the main airport in Mato Grosso do Sul, with frequent flights to and from other major Brazilian cities.

Bus Stations Near Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul has several bus stations serving different regions of the state and other Brazilian destinations.

Other Options

Travelers can also rent cars or hire private drivers to explore Mato Grosso do Sul at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Mato Grosso do Sul

Mato Grosso do Sul has excellent road networks, making it easy to explore the state by car, bike, or motorcycle. Taxis and buses are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Mato Grosso do Sul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mato Grosso do Sul is a gateway to many other Brazilian destinations, such as the neighboring state of Mato Grosso, which features the iconic Chapada dos Guimaraes National Park. The region is also well-connected to other key Brazilian cities, such as Sao Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.

Conclusion

Mato Grosso do Sul is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for a unique and unforgettable experience. With our comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their time in the region, creating memories that will last a lifetime.
