Introduction to Corumbá

Located in the state of Mato Grosso do Sul, in Central West Brazil, Corumbá is a charming city known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Nestled alongside the Paraguay River, Corumbá offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural wonders that are sure to satisfy any traveler’s wanderlust.

List of Places to Visit in Corumbá

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Pantanal - Learn about the history, flora, and fauna of the Pantanal wetlands through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Municipal Museum of Corumbá - Explore the city’s rich cultural heritage through exhibitions showcasing traditional clothing, handicrafts, and photographs.
  • Railway Museum - Discover the longstanding railway history of Corumbá through vintage train exhibits, memorabilia, and more.
  • Church of Our Lady of the Candelária and the Historical Square - Admire the colonial architecture of the church and stroll through the historic square, surrounded by charming shops and cafes.

Natural Attractions of Corumbá: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pantanal Wetlands - Get up close to a diverse range of wildlife, including jaguars, capybaras, and giant otters, while exploring the vast wetlands of the Pantanal.
  • Bonito - Just a short day trip from Corumbá, Bonito is a beautiful natural reserve that offers activities such as snorkeling, hiking, and cave exploration in crystal-clear waters.
  • Serra da Bodoquena National Park - Witness stunning natural views and explore caves, waterfalls, and crystal-clear rivers in this beautiful national park.
  • Paraguay River Cruise - Take a scenic river cruise and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corumbá

  • Chiquito Trail - This scenic hiking trail takes you through the lush forests and wildlife of Av. General Rondon’s land, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Bike Path on the Paraguay River - For those who prefer to bike, this path takes you along the river, with beautiful views and fresh air.

Best Things to Do in Corumbá: Activities and Tours

  • Brazilian Carnival - Experience the vibrant and colorful celebration of Brazilian Carnival, with its lively parade, samba and costumes.
  • Safari photo tour - Take a tour of the Pantanal Wetlands, where you can get the opportunity to see and photograph jaguars, anacondas, and other wildlife.
  • Hidrovia Transparana: Enjoy boat trips to the Penitente Island and enjoy local fauna and flora.
  • Historical and Cultural Tour - Visit the main historical and cultural points of Corumbá in a single tour by car or foot.

Food and Drink Scene of Corumbá

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pirão - A traditional dish made from fish, yucca, and spices.
  • Tucunaré - Grilled freshwater fish, similar to bass or trout, often served with rice and vegetables.
  • Carne de Sol - Dried and salted beef, commonly served with beans, rice, and salad.

Local Beverages

  • Tereré - A refreshing cold tea made with yerba mate, traditionally drunk from a gourd and shared with friends.
  • Caipirinha - Brazil’s national drink, made with cachaca (a distilled spirit) and lime.

Where to Stay in Corumbá: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Fazenda Rio Negro - A charming farmhouse-style hotel featuring comfortable rooms and delicious traditional cuisine.
  • Hotel Nacional Palace - A stylish and affordable hotel in the heart of Corumbá, close to main attractions and restaurants.
  • Pousada em Corumbá - A cozy guesthouse offering clean and comfortable rooms, perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Corumbá: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Ceramics - Unique ceramic art pieces inspired by the Pantanal’s flora and fauna.
  • Leather Goods - High-quality leather products such as belts, wallets, and bags, made by local artisans.
  • Handicrafts - Colorful handcrafted souvenirs made by local indigenous communities.

Local Culture and Customs of Corumbá

Corumbá is a melting pot of indigenous, African, and European cultures, which is reflected in the city’s traditional festivals, music, and cuisine. Brazilians in Mato Grosso do Sul, including Corumbá, tend to be more reserved and formal than those in other regions. It’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in sacred spaces.

Best Time to Visit Corumbá

The best time to visit Corumbá is during the dry season between May and September when the weather is sunny, dry, and mild. This season is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, boating, and wildlife observation. However, be prepared for cooler temperatures in the evenings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corumbá

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and natural attractions
  • Purchase handmade souvenirs and support local artisans

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately
  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Ignore safety recommendations

Safety and Health

Safety in Corumbá

Corumbá is a relatively safe city, but it’s recommended to take precautions like avoiding quiet streets at night, securing valuables, and using common sense.

Health in Corumbá

Vaccinations against yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, rabies, and tetanus are recommended. It’s also advised to bring insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites, which can carry diseases like malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Corumbá: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Corumbá

The nearest airport to Corumbá is the Corumbá International Airport located 4.5 miles (7.2 km) from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Corumbá

The Corumbá Bus Station is located in the city center and offers multiple daily departures to and from other major destinations in Brazil.

Other Options

It’s also possible to arrive in Corumbá by car, boat, or train.

How to Get Around in Corumbá

The best way to get around Corumbá is by foot, bike, or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Corumbá: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bonito - Explore this beautiful natural reserve only a few hours away and indulge in activities such as snorkeling, hiking, and cave exploration.
  • Campo Grande - The capital of Mato Grosso do Sul state is a vibrant city with a well-preserved historic center, cultural attractions, and nightlife.
  • Bolivian Pantanal - A neighboring area in Bolivia with similar landscapes to the Brazilian Pantanal.

Conclusion

Corumbá has something for everyone, whether you’re interested in immersing yourself in history and culture, exploring natural wonders, or indulging in local cuisine and drink. Plan a trip to Corumbá today and discover all the city has to offer!
