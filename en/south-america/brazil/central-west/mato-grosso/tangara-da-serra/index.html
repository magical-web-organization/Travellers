Introduction to Tangará da Serra

Tangará da Serra is a city located in the state of Mato Grosso, Brazil. Situated in the Central West region of the country, Tangará da Serra has become a popular destination for tourists looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience. Surrounded by lush green hills, the city is known for its natural beauty as well as its rich history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Tangará da Serra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tangará Cultural Foundation: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Tangará da Serra and features various exhibits showcasing the region’s art, culture, and traditions.
  • Marechal Rondon Museum: Located in the center of the city, this museum celebrates the life and achievements of Brazilian explorer Marechal Cândido Rondon.
  • São Bento Church: Designed in a colonial style, this church is considered one of the main religious landmarks in the city.

Natural Attractions of Tangará da Serra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra das Águas Park: Known for its stunning waterfalls and hiking trails, this park is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Pioneiros Hill: This hill offers panoramic views of Tangará da Serra and the surrounding countryside.
  • Rio Sepotuba: Enjoy a scenic drive through the countryside and take in the beautiful views of the river and surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tangará da Serra

  • Serra das Águas Trail: This scenic hike takes travelers through the beautiful Serra das Águas Park and provides stunning views of the park’s many waterfalls.
  • Cycling Tour: Rent a bike and explore the city’s many parks and scenic routes.
  • Rio Sepotuba Hike: Experience the natural beauty of Tangará da Serra on this riverside hike.

Best Things to Do in Tangará da Serra: Activities and Tours

  • Brazilian Cowboy Experience: Immerse yourself in local customs and culture with a horseback-riding trip through the Brazilian countryside.
  • Handicrafts Tour: Visit local artisans and learn about their traditional handicrafts, such as pottery-making and weaving.
  • Ecotourism Tour: Explore the natural beauty of Tangará da Serra on a guided hike or boat tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Tangará da Serra

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pacu Assado: A Brazilian fish dish that is typically grilled and served with vegetables.
  • Arroz Carreteiro: This traditional Brazilian dish is made with rice, sausage, and cured beef.
  • Brigadeiro: A popular Brazilian dessert made with condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: A classic Brazilian cocktail made with cachaça, sugar, and lime.
  • Guaraná: A popular Brazilian soda made from the guaraná fruit.

Where to Stay in Tangará da Serra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tangará Palace Hotel: A modern and comfortable hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Pousada Recanto dos Passaros: This charming guesthouse offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Hostel Tangará: Affordable and centrally located, this hostel is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Tangará da Serra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: From pottery to textiles, Tangará da Serra is known for its rich tradition of local handicrafts.
  • Local Specialty Foods: Bring home local treats such as guava paste or Brazilian coffee.
  • Indigenous Artwork: Tangará da Serra is known for its indigenous artwork, featuring bright colors and intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Tangará da Serra

Tangará da Serra has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, food, and traditions. The region is also known for its close ties to agriculture and farming, which are celebrated in local festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Tangará da Serra

The best time to visit Tangará da Serra is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, and visitors can enjoy the region’s many outdoor activities without the threat of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tangará da Serra

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional Brazilian desserts.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting indigenous communities.
  • Take advantage of the region’s many hiking trails and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the importance of sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Wear revealing clothing to religious sites or other important cultural landmarks.
  • Engage in activities that could harm the environment or damage local ecosystems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tangará da Serra

Tangará da Serra is generally a safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution when exploring the city at night. Additionally, it is important to be aware of the local wildlife, especially when hiking or camping in the area.

Health in Tangará da Serra

It is recommended that visitors to Tangará da Serra receive all necessary vaccinations before traveling. Additionally, travelers should be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and chikungunya.

How to Get Around in Tangará da Serra

Tangará da Serra is a walkable city, and many of the main attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Additionally, taxis and public transportation are readily available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Tangará da Serra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chapada dos Guimarães National Park: Located just a few hours from Tangará da Serra, this park is known for its stunning natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Cuiabá: The capital of the state of Mato Grosso, Cuiabá is a vibrant city with a rich colonial history.
  • Bom Jardim: This picturesque town is known for its natural swimming holes and waterfalls.
