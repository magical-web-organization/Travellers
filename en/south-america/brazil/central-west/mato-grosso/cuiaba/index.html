A Complete Travel Guide to Cuiabá, Brazil

Located in the Central West region of Brazil, Cuiabá is the capital city of the state of Mato Grosso. Known for its rich culture, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, Cuiabá is a must-visit destination for any traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Cuiabá.

List of Places to Visit in Cuiabá

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Waters: This museum showcases the history of the Pantanal region and its unique ecosystem.
  • Casa Dom Aquino Museum: A historic house that used to be the residence of the state’s governors, now turned into a museum that displays the local culture and history of Mato Grosso.
  • Cine Teatro Cuiabá: A historic theater that hosts cultural events and performances.
  • Mother Bonifacia Park: A popular city park with walking trails and a playground for children.

Natural Attractions of Cuiabá: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chapada dos Guimarães National Park: This park is known for its stunning landscapes and offers hiking trails and opportunities for rock climbing.
  • Pantanal: The world’s largest wetland ecosystem, which is home to diverse wildlife like jaguars, anacondas, and capybaras.
  • Nobres: A town known for its crystal-clear rivers and waterfalls, where visitors can snorkel, scuba dive, and take boat trips.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cuiabá

  • Chapada dos Guimarães: This region offers several hiking trails with varying levels of difficulty, including the Véu de Noiva waterfall trail, which is around 9 km long.
  • Parque das Águas: This park is ideal for walkers and joggers and offers a 2.2 km-long trail that surrounds one of the largest artificial lakes in the city.

Best Things to Do in Cuiabá: Activities and Tours

  • Pantanal Wildlife Safari Tour: A guided tour that takes visitors through the Pantanal, showcasing its diverse wildlife.
  • Boat Trip in the Rio Cuiabá: Experience the city and the surrounding area from a unique perspective, with calm waters and beautiful landscapes.
  • Soil Fertility Experiment Station: This agricultural center offers tours that explain the innovative techniques used in farming that have made the region prosperous.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cuiabá

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Museum of Waters, then take a walk around the Mother Bonifacia Park
Day 2 Take a tour to Chapada dos Guimarães National Park
Day 3 Visit the Casa Dom Aquino Museum and the Cine Teatro Cuiabá
Day 4 Take a Pantanal Wildlife Safari Tour
Day 5 Walk through Parque das Águas and enjoy the artificial lake
Day 6 Visit Nobres and enjoy freshwater snorkeling and diving
Day 7 Take a boat trip in the Rio Cuiabá

Food and Drink Scene of Cuiabá

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peixada cuiabana: A traditional dish made with fish cooked in a spicy sauce with rice and farofa.
  • Maria Izabel: A delicious cake made with grated coconut, condensed milk, and cloves.
  • **Tigers: A type of small salty cake stuffed with sausages, onions, and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Chopp: A popular ice-cold beer, perfect to enjoy during hot days.
  • Cachaça: A Brazilian spirit distilled from sugarcane juice, often used in cocktails.

Where to Stay in Cuiabá: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Odara Hotel: This luxurious hotel offers excellent facilities, including an outdoor pool and a fitness center.
  • Hostel Safari: A budget-friendly hostel that has a laid-back atmosphere and organized activities throughout the week.
  • Hostel Pantanal Backpacker: This hostel is located in a quiet and safe neighborhood and offers private and shared rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Cuiabá: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Brazilian Cowboy Hat: Also known as the ‘Gaucho hat,’ it has become a symbol traditional to Brazilian rural culture.
  • Handmade local crafts: From handmade blankets to beautiful pottery, take a piece of Cuiabá’s cultural heritage home.

Local Culture and Customs of Cuiabá

Cuiabá is well-known for its traditions, architecture, cuisine, and festivals, many of which have indigenous roots. Its culture is influenced by African, European, and indigenous cultures, making it a unique blend of multiple social, religious, and cultural practices.

Best Time to Visit Cuiabá

The best time to visit Cuiabá is from June to September, as this is the dry season, with mild temperatures, making it the ideal weather to enjoy outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cuiabá

Do:

  • Try traditional dishes to fully imbue yourself in the local culture.
  • Be respectful of the environment and refrain from littering.

Don’t:

  • Refuse to interact with locals: they are friendly and want to share their culture.
  • Wear revealing clothes or beachwear outside resorts and beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cuiabá

While Cuiabá is considered a safe city, it’s always recommended to be cautious, especially when walking around unfamiliar neighborhoods or late at night. Pickpocketing and theft can occur so be careful with your belongings.

Health in Cuiabá

There are no specific vaccine requirements to visit Cuiabá, but it is recommended to get vaccinated against yellow fever, as this is a commonly reported disease in the region. It’s crucial to use mosquito repellant when outdoors to avoid mosquitos that convey infections like Zika, Malaria, and Dengue.

Getting to Cuiabá: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cuiabá

Cuiabá’s international airport, Marechal Rondon International Airport, is located only 8 km from the city’s center. Regular flights from major Brazilian cities like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro, as well as from some international destinations like Bolivia and Argentina, operate daily.

Bus Stations Near Cuiabá

Cuiabá’s bus terminal is located in the city center, with regular buses connecting to major cities in Brazil.

Other Options

One of the best ways to get to Cuiabá is to take a boat trip from Manaus and down the Amazon river, but it’s a multi-day journey and requires planning in advance.

How to Get Around in Cuiabá

The best way to get around Cuiabá is by using taxis, which are cheap and easy to find in the city center. For more extended travel, organized tours, or renting a car or motorcycle is an option.

Where to Go Next from Cuiabá: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Poconé: A historic town with an excellent location to start tours to the Pantanal.
  • Chapada dos Guimarães: A popular destination with impressive landscapes that include mountains, waterfalls, and canyons.

Conclusion

Cuiabá is a city with unique cultural and architectural heritage, natural beauty, and fantastic food. Take the trip to Cuiabá and immerse yourself in this unique Brazilian destination.
