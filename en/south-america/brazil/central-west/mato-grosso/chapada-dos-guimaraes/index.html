Introduction to Chapada dos Guimarães

Chapada dos Guimarães is a natural paradise located in Mato Grosso, Brazil. This beautiful municipality is known for its stunning landscapes, waterfalls, creeks, and rough terrain that is perfect for adventure seekers. Whether you’re a nature lover or an adrenaline junkie, this destination offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we have listed everything you need to know about Chapada dos Guimarães to make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Chapada dos Guimarães

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Geodesic Centre of South America

Located on the top of a beautiful hill, the Geodesic Centre of South America is a historical landmark that marks the spot of the geographical centre of the South American continent. It is also a great place to enjoy a panoramic view of the surrounding landscapes.

2. Casa de Pedra

Casa de Pedra is a remarkable archeological site that takes you back in time. This ancient cave is adorned with rock paintings, and it is a testament to the history and culture of the indigenous people of the region.

3. Forte de Coimbra

Forte de Coimbra is a well-preserved military fortress that dates back to the 18th century. It was built to protect the region from invasion and served as a strategic point in the battle against the Paraguayan army.

Natural Attractions of Chapada dos Guimarães: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Cachoeira Véu de Noiva

Cachoeira Véu de Noiva is the most famous waterfall in Chapada dos Guimarães. It has a 86m drop, and its name translates to “Bride’s Veil,” due to the similarity of the waterfall’s flow to a bride’s veil. The view from the top is breathtaking, and it is definitely worth a visit.

2. Mirante do Centro Geodésico

Mirante do Centro Geodésico is a lookout point from where you can enjoy a panoramic view of Chapada dos Guimarães. You can see the entire region from this point, including the Serra de São Vicente mountain range, which separates the Cuiabá River basin from the Paraná River basin.

3. Parque Nacional da Chapada dos Guimarães

Parque Nacional da Chapada dos Guimarães is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The park is home to many exciting hiking trails, waterfalls, and caves, all surrounded by stunning landscapes. Visitors can explore the park on foot, by bike, or on horseback.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chapada dos Guimarães

##1. Cachoeira da Martinha Trail

The Cachoeira da Martinha Trail is a moderate-level hike that takes you through the dense forests of the Chapada dos Guimarães. The trail leads to the stunning Martinha Waterfall, which drops into a natural pool perfect for a refreshing swim.

##2. Circuito das Cachoeiras Trail

The Circuito das Cachoeiras Trail takes you through the most beautiful waterfalls of the Chapada dos Guimarães. This 6km trail includes a visit to nine waterfalls, each more stunning than the last.

Best Things to Do in Chapada dos Guimarães: Activities and Tours

##1. Horseback Riding

One of the best and most traditional ways to explore the Chapada dos Guimarães is on horseback. The horseback riding tour takes you through stunning landscapes, picturesque towns, and grand plains where you can see the stunning sunsets.

##2. Canoeing

Canoeing is a fantastic way to explore the rivers of Chapada dos Guimarães, offering a unique perspective of the natural beauty of the region. This activity is suitable for both beginners and advanced paddlers and can be done alone or in a group.

Food and Drink Scene of Chapada dos Guimarães

Must-Try Dishes

1. Pacu Fish

Pacu is a famous fish in the region, usually grilled with a side of mandioca or rice. This dish is a traditional staple of the cuisine of Chapada dos Guimarães.

2. Maria Izabel

Maria Izabel is a traditional dessert made with cassava, coconut, and baked pumpkin. This sweet dish is a must-try on your trip to Chapada dos Guimarães.

Local Beverages

1. Tereré

Tereré is a refreshing drink made with mate tea leaves served in a traditional gourd. It is a perfect for hot summer days in the region.

2. Cerveja Artesanal

Chapada dos Guimarães also boasts some of the best artisanal beers in the country. Made using locally-sourced ingredients, these beers offer a range of unique and authentic flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Chapada dos Guimarães

The locals in Chapada dos Guimarães are known for their warm hospitality and rich culture. Visitors to the region should respect the local customs and traditions, and be open to learning about local customs, beliefs, and values.

Best Time to Visit Chapada dos Guimarães

The best time of year to visit Chapada dos Guimarães is during the dry season from May to September. During this time, the weather is cooler and dryer, and it is perfect for outdoor activities. However, visitors should be aware that this is also the busiest tourist season, so it is important to plan well in advance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chapada dos Guimarães

Chapada dos Guimarães is a relatively safe place for tourists to visit. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to safeguard their belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Chapada dos Guimarães

Visitors to Chapada dos Guimarães should ensure that they have received all necessary vaccinations before traveling. It is also important to carry a basic first-aid kit and consult a doctor if needed.

Getting to Chapada dos Guimarães: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chapada dos Guimarães

The nearest airport to Chapada dos Guimarães is the Cuiabá Airport.

Bus Stations Near Chapada dos Guimarães

Visitors can travel by bus from Cuiabá to Chapada dos Guimarães. Buses depart regularly from the main bus station in Cuiabá.

Other Options

Car rentals and transfers are available from the nearby airports of Cuiabá.

How to Get Around in Chapada dos Guimarães

Visitors can rent a car or a bike to explore the region. However, horseback riding and canoeing are both great ways to experience the natural beauty of the region.
