Introduction to Mato Grosso

Mato Grosso is a state located in Central West Brazil, known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse wildlife, and breathtaking natural landscapes. It is one of the largest states in the country, boasting 903,000 square kilometers of lush forests, winding rivers, and sprawling plateaus.

Local Culture and Customs of Mato Grosso

Mato Grosso has a rich cultural heritage shaped by its indigenous, European, and African influences. It is known for its music, dance, and traditional festivals, such as the Festa de São Benedito and the Festival de Siriri. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as covering up when visiting rural areas and avoiding trespassing on indigenous lands without permission.

Best Time to Visit Mato Grosso

The best time to visit Mato Grosso is from June to September, during the dry season, when the weather is sunny and pleasant for outdoor activities. However, if you want to see the Pantanal in its full glory with lots of wildlife, the rainy season between December and February is best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mato Grosso

Do:

  • Try local specialties such as pintado na brasa
  • Respect local customs and traditions when visiting indigenous lands
  • Bring formal wear for attending cultural events and festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter in natural areas
  • Take photos of indigenous people without permission
  • Touch or interact with wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Mato Grosso

Mato Grosso is, in general, a safe destination for visitors. However, it is important to remain vigilant and take precautions to prevent theft and pickpocketing, especially in urban areas. Petty crime can occur, but violent crime is not common. Avoid visiting dangerous areas like favelas, especially at night.

Health in Mato Grosso

Mato Grosso can be a risk for yellow fever outbreak, and it is recommended to get a yellow fever vaccine. It’s also essential to protect yourself against mosquito bites and drink only bottled or filtered water to avoid water-borne diseases.

How to Get Around in Mato Grosso

Renting a car or taking a guided tour are two of the best ways to get around and see the state’s most stunning sites. Public transportation can be spotty, and many attractions require a private vehicle to reach.
