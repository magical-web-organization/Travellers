Introduction to Várzea Grande

Várzea Grande is a city located in the state of Mato Grosso, Brazil, and is known for its rich culture, natural beauty, and diverse culinary scene. Founded in the late 19th century, Várzea Grande has grown to become a major commercial and cultural hub in the region, attracting visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast looking for adventure, a history buff interested in learning about the local culture, or a foodie eager to sample the local cuisine, Várzea Grande has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Várzea Grande

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Várzea Grande is home to several museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the city’s rich culture and history. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Museu de Arte Sacra de Mato Grosso: This museum showcases a vast collection of religious art and artifacts from the colonial period of Brazil.
  • Casa de Bem-Bem: A historic house museum that offers a glimpse into the life of a wealthy Cuiabano family during the early 20th century.
  • Centro Cultural do Pantanal: A cultural center that hosts art exhibitions, concerts, and theater performances.

Natural Attractions of Várzea Grande: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Várzea Grande is surrounded by natural beauty, featuring lush forests, rivers, and breathtaking landscapes. Take some time to explore the great outdoors and discover some of the following wonders:

  • Chapada dos Guimarães National Park: A national park that boasts stunning waterfalls, canyons, and hiking trails that offer breathtaking vistas of the surrounding region.
  • Parque das Águas: A park that features several swimming pools, a small zoo, and a forest for hiking and picnicking.
  • Parque Mãe Bonifácia: A city park with several trails for hiking, jogging, and biking, as well as a lake with paddleboats and a playground for children.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Várzea Grande

Várzea Grande offers several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for nature lovers looking to explore the region. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Trilha da Salgadeira: A 3.5 km trail that passes through the Cerrado vegetation and leads to a stunning 86-meter-high waterfall.
  • Trilha do Mirante: A short hike to a scenic overlook that provides a panoramic view of the city and its surroundings.
  • Ciclovia: A bike path that follows the banks of the Cuiabá River and is perfect for a leisurely ride or jog.

Best Things to Do in Várzea Grande: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Várzea Grande, from cultural tours to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top activities:

  • City Tour: Explore the historic landmarks and cultural attractions of Várzea Grande with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the nearby rivers with professional guides.
  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and enjoy the stunning views of Várzea Grande from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Várzea Grande

To get the most out of your trip to Várzea Grande, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museu de Arte Sacra de Mato Grosso and Casa de Bem-Bem.
  • Day 2: Explore Chapada dos Guimarães National Park and take a dip in one of its many waterfalls.
  • Day 3: Discover the city’s art scene with a visit to the Centro Cultural do Pantanal.
  • Day 4: Enjoy a leisurely bike ride along the Ciclovia.
  • Day 5: Take a paragliding tour of the city and its surroundings.
  • Day 6: Spend the day at Parque Mãe Bonifácia, exploring the walking trails and enjoying a picnic.
  • Day 7: Relax at Parque das Águas, taking a dip in the pools and observing the local wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Várzea Grande

No trip to Várzea Grande is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pacu Assado: A grilled fish dish that is a local favorite.
  • Arroz com pequi: Rice with pequi fruit, a regional specialty.
  • Carne de Sol: A salted and sun-dried beef dish that is popular throughout the region.

Local Beverages

  • Guaraná Antartica: A popular soft drink that is flavored with the guaraná fruit.
  • Cachaça: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane that is a staple in Brazilian culture.

Where to Stay in Várzea Grande: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Várzea Grande offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hotel Mato Grosso: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, offering comfortable rooms and top-notch amenities.
  • Pousada Pantaneira: A charming guesthouse that offers a cozy atmosphere and personalized service.
  • Hostel Safari: A budget-friendly option that provides dormitory-style rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Várzea Grande: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Várzea Grande, be sure to pick up some local souvenirs and products to bring home with you. Some of the top options include:

  • Handmade crafts: Várzea Grande is known for its artisanal crafts, including pottery, woodcarvings, and textiles.
  • Local produce: Sample some of the region’s fresh fruits and vegetables, such as the pequi fruit or the Brazilian nut.
  • Cachaça: Bring home a bottle of authentic Brazilian cachaça as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Várzea Grande

Várzea Grande is a city with a rich cultural heritage, influenced by its indigenous, African, and European roots. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or events.
  • Use etiquette when dining, including using utensils and not talking with your mouth full.
  • Try to learn some basic Portuguese phrases to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Várzea Grande

The best time to visit Várzea Grande is between June and September, during the dry season. This is when the weather is most pleasant and outdoor activities are at their best. However, visitors should be prepared for warm temperatures year-round, as Várzea Grande is located in a tropical climate zone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Várzea Grande

Visitors to Várzea Grande should be aware of their surroundings and take standard precautions to ensure their safety. Some tips include:

  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas after dark.
  • Use licensed taxis or rideshare services for transportation.
  • Keep important documents and valuables in a safe place at all times.

Health in Várzea Grande

Visitors to Várzea Grande should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. It is also important to stay hydrated and avoid consuming tap water, as it may be contaminated.

Getting to Várzea Grande: Transportation Options and Logistics

Várzea Grande is accessible by both air and land and offers several transportation options to get around the city and its surroundings.
