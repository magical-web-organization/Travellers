Cáceres is a hidden gem in Brazil’s Central West region, tucked away in the state of Mato Grosso and surrounded by the lush Pantanal wetlands. This small city draws visitors with its rich history, outdoor adventures, and unique culture. Plan your trip with our comprehensive travel guide to Cáceres, Brazil.

Introduction to Cáceres

Cáceres is a picturesque city with a colonial charm and a rich history that dates back to the 18th century. It was founded by the Portuguese, who came in search of gold, and later became a hub for the cattle trade. Today, Cáceres is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from indigenous, European, and African traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Cáceres

Cáceres has an array of attractions to discover. Here are some must-see places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu de Arqueologia e Paleontologia - The Archaeology and Paleontology Museum features exhibits that showcase the region’s rich history and geology. Visitors can see fossils, artifacts, and learn about the indigenous cultures that once lived in the area.
  • Igreja de Nossa Senhora da Conceição - The Church of Our Lady of the Conception is a beautiful colonial-style church that dates back to the 18th century. It is a symbol of the city’s heritage and is worth a visit.
  • Casa do Artesão - This cultural center showcases local artisans and their crafts. Visitors can buy souvenirs, learn about traditional handicrafts, and even take a workshop.

Natural Attractions of Cáceres: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Nacional do Pantanal Mato-Grossense - The Pantanal National Park is an ecological paradise that boasts a diverse array of flora and fauna. Visitors can take a guided tour, go hiking, bird watching, and enjoy the natural beauty of the wetlands.
  • Rio Paraguay - The Paraguay River is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts. The river is home to various species of fish, including piranhas, and visitors can take a fishing tour or hire a boat to explore the area.
  • Flutuação no Aquário Encantado - The Enchanted Aquarium is a natural pool with crystal-clear waters, where visitors can snorkel and observe the local fishes and flora.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cáceres

Cáceres has various trails for hiking and biking. Here are some options:

  • Trilha do Mato Grosso - This trail is a 32 km round trip that takes visitors through the Pantanal wetlands. The path is flat and easy to navigate, making it suitable for all levels of fitness.
  • Trilha do Parque Ecológico - The Ecological Park Trail is a shorter 6 km circuit that takes visitors through the park’s natural habitat.
  • Trilha da Serra do Ricardo Franco - This more advanced trail is 15 km long, passing through dense forests, waterfalls, and views of the Pantanal.

Best Things to Do in Cáceres: Activities and Tours

Cáceres offers a variety of unique experiences to its visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Safari Fotográfico - A photographic safari takes visitors through the Pantanal wetlands to observe wildlife and capture the beauty of the region.
  • Festa de São Benedito - Every August, Cáceres hosts a religious festival in honor of Saint Benedict, a Catholic saint of African descent. The festival features traditional dances, food, and music.
  • Cavalgada - For those who love horseback riding, this trail is a must-do. Horseback riding is a popular activity in the region, and visitors can enjoy scenic views with a tour guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cáceres

To make the most out of your trip to Cáceres, follow this itinerary:

Day Plan
1 Check in at your hotel and explore the city by walking at Praca da Cathedral
2 Visit Museu de Arqueologia e Paleontologia and Festa de São Benedito
3 Discover the Pantanal, take a photographic safari and sail at Rio Paraguay
4 Enjoy a scenic ride through the mountains, and visit the Flutuação no Aquário Encantado
5 Horseback riding adventure at the nearby park
6 Explore local artisans at Casa do Artesão and Trilha do Parque Ecológico
7 Check out and travel by bus to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Cáceres

Cáceres offers visitors a unique culinary experience. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pintado a Urucum - Grilled freshwater fish seasoned with Urucum spice is a local favorite.
  • Arroz Carretilha - A rice dish served with beef, pork, sausage, and vegetables, is a hearty meal for carnivores.
  • Doce de banana em calda - this sweet banana dish is a delicious dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Tereré - A refreshing drink of cold mate served with herbs and fruit juices
  • Café do Cerrado - Brazilian coffee blends with a unique flavor due to the region’s soil and climate.

Where to Stay in Cáceres: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cáceres has several accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Pouso da Barranca - A beautiful guesthouse with rustic decoration and views of Pantanal.
  • Hotel Nossa Senhora Aparecida - A mid-range hotel near the Cathedral Square.
  • Albergue e Pousada Pantaneira - A budget-friendly hostel with private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in Cáceres: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Cáceres. Here are some ideas:

  • Artesanato de Couro - Handmade leather crafts, such as belts, sandals, and bags, make great souvenirs
  • Doces Caseiros - Homemade sweets and jams made from local fruits are a tasty gift.
  • Pequi Oil - A nutritious and flavorful oil extracted from the Pequi fruit, a native Cerrado tree.

Local Culture and Customs of Cáceres

Cáceres is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from indigenous, European, and African traditions. Always remember to respect the local customs and culture.

Best Time to Visit Cáceres

The best time to visit Cáceres is from May to September when the weather is dry and sunny, making it easier to navigate through the mountains and Pantanal wetlands.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cáceres

Do:

  • Try local food and beverages
  • Respect the environment and the native wildlife
  • Learn about the region’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitat
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs
  • Wear inappropriate clothes in religious ceremonies

Safety and Health

Safety in Cáceres

Cáceres is generally a safe destination, but visitors should always be vigilant and take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables
  • Be aware of pickpockets in tourist areas
  • Always use a guide or ask locals for advice when exploring the mountains or Pantanal wetlands.

Health in Cáceres

Visitors should take precautions to protect themselves against mosquito-borne diseases, such as Dengue Fever and Malaria.

Getting to Cáceres: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cáceres

The nearest international airports are:

  • Cuiabá International Airport (CGB) - about 200 km from Cáceres
  • Campo Grande International Airport (CGR) - about 400 km from Cáceres

Bus Stations Near Cáceres

Cáceres has a bus station that connects the city to other nearby destinations, such as Cuiabá and Campo Grande.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private car service to get to Cáceres.

How to Get Around in Cáceres

Cáceres is a small city that can be easily explored on foot. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Cáceres: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cáceres is a great base for exploring the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Chapada dos Guimarães - A beautiful national park with incredible rock formations, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Serra das Araras - A scenic mountain range with breathtaking views and rock climbing opportunities.
  • Bonito - A charming town with crystal clear rivers and caves to explore.

Conclusion

Cáceres is a picturesque city with an incredible natural setting that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Brazil’s history and culture. With this guide, you’ll be able to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in the Central West.
