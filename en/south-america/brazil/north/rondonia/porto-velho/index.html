Introduction to Porto Velho

Porto Velho is the capital city of Rondônia, a state located in the northern region of Brazil. The city has a rich history dating back to the early 20th century when it was founded during the construction of the Madeira-Mamoré Railroad. Today, Porto Velho is a vibrant destination known for its natural beauty, diverse culture, and warm hospitality.

Food and Drink Scene of Porto Velho

No visit to Porto Velho would be complete without indulging in some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Caldeirada de Tambaqui (fish stew)
  • Carne de Sol (dried and salted meat)
  • Arroz com Pequi (rice cooked with a fragrant fruit)
  • Tacacá (soup made with jambu and tucupi)

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça (sugar cane liquor)
  • Guaraná Antarctica (Brazilian soft drink)
  • Coconut water

Local Culture and Customs of Porto Velho

Porto Velho is a diverse city with a vibrant culture and a rich history. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Brazilians typically greet each other with a hug and kiss on the cheek.
  • Respect the locals and their beliefs, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Avoid wearing revealing clothing in public places.

Best Time to Visit Porto Velho

The best time to visit Porto Velho is during the dry season, which runs from June through September. The weather is pleasant, and the skies are clearer, making it easier to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Porto Velho

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Porto Velho:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Carry large amounts of cash with you

Safety and Health

It’s important to stay safe and healthy when traveling to Porto Velho. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Porto Velho

  • Avoid visiting slums and isolated areas.
  • Use taxis or ride-sharing services instead of public transportation.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and don’t flash valuables in public.

Health in Porto Velho

  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and malaria.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Bring any necessary medications with you.
