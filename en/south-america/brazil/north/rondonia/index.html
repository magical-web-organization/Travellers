Introduction to Rondônia

Rondônia is one of Brazil’s most hidden gems, located in the northwest corner of the country. Its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture make it an intriguing destination for any traveler seeking off-the-beaten-track experiences. This region is renowned for its ecotourism, offering a plethora of outdoor activities including hiking, camping, fishing, kayaking, bird-watching, and more. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, food to try, activities to do, and cultural experiences to have in Rondônia.

List of Places to Visit in Rondônia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

João Batista Teixeira Museum

The João Batista Teixeira Museum is situated in the capital of Rondônia, Porto Velho, and is dedicated to preserving the state’s cultural heritage. The museum exhibits artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the state’s history and culture.

Madeira-Mamore Railroad Museum

The Madeira-Mamore Railroad Museum is another essential stop in Porto Velho. It tells the history of one of the most impressive engineering feats in Brazil –the construction of the Madeira-Mamoré Railway– through exhibitions of old machinery and railway exhibits.

Amazonia Museum of Natural Sciences

The Amazonia Museum of Natural Sciences, located inside the campus of the Federal University of Rondônia, is devoted to the natural history of the Amazonia region. Visitors can admire a diverse collection of animals, including a giant anaconda, a tiger, and many butterflies.

Natural Attractions of Rondônia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Guajará-Mirim River

The Guajará-Mirim River, which separates Brazil from Bolivia, is an excellent destination for a scenic river tour. This river, one of the largest tributaries of the Amazon, boasts breathtaking views, a vibrant ecosystem, and several indigenous communities along its banks.

Pacaas Novos Mountains

The Pacaas Novos Mountains are a perfect spot for trekking, camping, and birdwatching. The mountain range covers an area of 250,000 ha, and it’s one of the most important sites for bird diversity in Brazil.

Mamirauá Sustainable Development Reserve

The Mamirauá Sustainable Development Reserve is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in the southern part of Rondônia. This reserve is a true gateway to the Amazon Rainforest, and it’s home to many endangered species such as giant otters, black caimans, and pink river dolphins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rondônia

If you’re looking for a prime hiking or biking adventure, Rondônia has you covered. Here are our top picks:

Three Falls Trail

This 7km trail inside the Serra dos Parecis features unique natural scenery, including rocky walls, stunning waterfalls, and lush vegetation. The trail is of moderate difficulty, and it’s suitable for hikers of all ages and levels.

Rio Madeira Bike Trail

This 15 km bike trail stretches along the river Madeira and offers panoramic views of the city of Porto Velho. The trail is relatively flat and is perfect for beginners and advanced bikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Rondônia: Activities and Tours

Fishing in Rio Branco

Besides its breathtaking scenic beauty, Rondônia is also renowned for its exceptional fishing opportunities. The best place to try your luck is the Rio Branco, a river that has a wide variety of fish species, including pacu, tambaqui, and piranhas.

Indigenous Villages Tour

Rondônia is home to many indigenous communities, and a tour of these villages will give you a unique insight into their culture and traditions. Indigenous communities are often welcoming to visitors and are happy to explain their way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rondônia

Day 1 - 2: Explore the Peaceful Town of Pimenteiras do Oeste and hike the Chico Mendes Ecological Trail. Day 3: Spend a day in the Guaporé River and visit the indigenous Suruí community. Day 4: Travel to Ji-Paraná and explore the city’s top attractions. Day 5 - 7: Head to the Mamirauá Sustainable Development Reserve and immerse yourself in the Amazon Rainforest.

Food and Drink Scene of Rondônia

Must-Try Dishes

Tacacá

Tacacá is a soup that is a staple food in the northern region of Brazil. It is made with jambu leaves, tucupi (a type of brazil nut milk), and shrimp.

Pato no Tucupi

Pato no tucupi is a traditional dish from the Amazon region made with duck meat and tucupi sauce. This dish is often served with rice and grilled manioc.

Local Beverages

Caldo de Cana

Caldo de cana is a sugarcane juice that is a refreshing beverage option in the hot, humid weather of Rondônia.

Cachaça

Cachaça is a distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice. It is a traditional drink in Brazil and is used as the base for popular cocktails such as caipirinha.

Where to Stay in Rondônia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rondônia offers a variety of accommodations ranging from luxurious to budget-friendly options. Here are a few standouts:

Oscar Hotel Executive

Located in the heart of Porto Velho, Oscar Hotel Executive offers comfortable and well-furnished rooms, a swimming pool, and a sauna, making it a popular choice for tourists and business travelers alike.

Hotel Harbor Inn

Located near the city center of Ji-Parana, Harbor Inn offers comfortable and clean rooms, a restaurant, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Rondônia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Rondônia is known for its exquisite handicrafts, including pottery, wood-carvings, and woven baskets.

Brazil Nuts

Brazil nuts, a product native to the Amazon region, are a perfect souvenir to bring back from Rondônia.

Local Culture and Customs of Rondônia

Festivals

The region of Rondônia has many unique and colorful festivals throughout the year, celebrating religious and cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Rondônia

The best time to visit Rondônia is from June to September when the weather is dry and sunny for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rondônia

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Carry your sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and enough water during outings.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect indigenous customs and traditions.
  • Don’t go hiking or biking alone.

Safety and Health in Rondônia

Safety in Rondônia

When traveling in Rondônia, tourists should take the necessary precautions to stay safe from petty crime such as theft and robbery.

Health in Rondônia

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or purified water and eat thoroughly cooked meat and vegetables to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Rondônia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rondônia

The city of Porto Velho has an international airport that serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Rondônia

The main bus station in Rondônia is located in the city of Porto Velho, with buses running to all major cities in Brazil.

Other Options

While the roads in Rondônia are relatively well-maintained, hiring a car or taking a taxi is a popular option for tourists.

How to Get Around in Rondônia

The best way to get around Rondônia is by car or bus, as it provides travelers with the flexibility to explore the area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Rondônia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Amazon River Cruise

Several cruise operators offer tours on the Amazon River, departing from Manaus in the nearby state of Amazonas. These tours offer visitors an opportunity to explore the remote region of the Amazon.

Bolivia

Rondônia shares a border with Bolivia, and visitors can cross the border and explore the neighboring country’s attractions.

Conclusion

Rondônia is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience Brazil’s unique culture, history, and natural beauty. With this Rondônia travel guide, we hope you have a better understanding of everything this region has to offer, and we’re confident you’ll have a great trip!
