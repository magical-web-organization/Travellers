Introduction to Amapá

Amapá is a state located in the northern part of Brazil and its capital is Macapá. This state is known for its lush greenery, jaw-dropping landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and scrumptious cuisine. It is ideal for nature lovers and adventure seekers as Amapá offers an array of outdoor adventure activities.

List of Places to Visit in Amapá

Here’s a rundown of places you shouldn’t miss on your trip to Amapá.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fortaleza de São José de Macapá: A historical fortress that dates back to the 18th century. It was built to guard the Amazon River from foreign invasions.
  • Museu Sacaca: This museum displays various cultural artifacts of the indigenous tribes of Amapá.
  • Marco Zero: An iconic landmark that marks the Equator line.

Natural Attractions of Amapá: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pedra Branca do Amapari: The highest peak in Amapá where you can witness breathtaking views of the Amazon forest.
  • Ilha de Maracá: A beautiful island surrounded by clear water and white sands. Ideal for a day trip.
  • Rio Araguari: The river offers activities like fishing, kayaking, and boat tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amapá

One of the best ways to explore the beauty of Amapá is by hiking and biking. Here is a list of the most popular routes:

  • Serra do Navio
  • Pedra Branca do Amapari
  • Rio Araguari

Food and Drink Scene of Amapá

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacaca: A soup made of tapioca, shrimp, and jambu herb.
  • Pato no Tucupi: A dish made of duck and manioc broth
  • Vatapá: A dish made of manioc flour, shrimp, and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A traditional Brazilian alcohol
  • Açai: A refreshing energy boost made of frozen, crushed açai berries
  • Cupuaçu: A sweet, yellow, tropical fruit used to make juice and ice cream

Where to Stay in Amapá: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various options for accommodation in Amapá, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular examples are:

  • Hotel Ceta Ecotel
  • Hotel Mais

What to Buy in Amapá: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts made by the indigenous tribes: You can buy colorful handcrafted baskets, hammocks, and pottery in local stores.
  • Açai powder: For a taste of Amapá, take home packages of açai powder and make açai bowls at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Amapá

The people of Amapá are known for their openness and hospitality. They are proud of their indigenous heritage and celebrate their culture with festivals and events throughout the year. The main religion followed is Christianity.

Best Time to Visit Amapá

The best time to visit Amapá is during the dry season, from June to December when it is possible to explore the national parks and waterfalls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amapá

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Follow the dress code while visiting the religious sites

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places
  • Touch or take pictures of the indigenous people without asking for permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Amapá

Amapá is a safe place to travel, but it is always wise to take precautionary measures like keeping your valuables safe. Avoid traveling alone after dark and always use reputable taxis or transportation services.

Health in Amapá

Make sure you carry your prescription and over-the-counter medicines with you while travelling to Amapá. It is advisable to apply mosquito repellent and take pre-emptive measures against Zika virus, dengue fever, and malaria.

Getting to Amapá: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amapá

The Macapa International Airport is the major airport in Amapá and is well-connected to major cities in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near Amapá

Rodoviária de Macapá is the main bus station in Amapá, connecting various cities within the state.

Other Options

There are ferries and speedboats that connect Amapá to other states in Brazil.

How to Get Around in Amapá

The best way to get around Amapá is by car, bus, or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Amapá: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Belém: A neighboring state of Pará that is known for its delicious cuisine and colonial architecture.
  • Oiapoque: A small town near the border of French Guiana that offers stunning beaches and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Amapá is a hidden gem that offers diverse experiences from cultural exploration to outdoor adventures. The state is a great choice for travelers looking for a unique vacation experience, off the beaten track and away from the crowds. With this guide, plan your trip to Amapá and explore the beauty of Brazil’s north.
