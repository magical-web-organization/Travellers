Introduction to Tocantins

Nestled in the north of Brazil, Tocantins is one of the country’s newest states, formed in 1988. It boasts a wealth of natural beauty, including waterfalls, cave systems, and national parks, as well as fascinating cultural landmarks. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and immersing yourself in local traditions, Tocantins has plenty to offer visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Tocantins

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palacinho

Once the governor’s residence, this impressive neoclassical building is now a museum showcasing the state’s history and culture. The exhibits include artwork, photographs, and artifacts from the indigenous tribes that call Tocantins home.

Casa da Cultura de Palmas

This cultural center, located in the capital city of Palmas, is a hub of artistic and musical activity. It features regular concerts, workshops, and exhibitions, and is a great place to experience traditional Brazilian culture.

Natural Attractions of Tocantins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jalapão State Park

Known as the “Brazilian Outback,” this vast protected area of over 34,000 square kilometers is home to towering sand dunes, crystal-clear waterfalls, and otherworldly rock formations. A trip to Jalapão State Park is a must for those seeking adventure and natural beauty.

Praia do Rio Sono

Located in the municipality of Mateiros, Praia do Rio Sono is a stunning beach which features crystal-clear water and pristine sand dunes. It’s the perfect place to relax and soak up the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tocantins

The Tocantins Cycle Route

This cycling route covers over 500 kilometers and takes riders through some of Tocantins’ most beautiful scenery, including national parks and breathtaking viewpoints.

The Jalapão Trek

This challenging trek covers over 200 kilometers and takes hikers through some of the most remote and unspoiled parts of Jalapão State Park. It’s not for the faint of heart, but the rewards are well worth it.

Best Things to Do in Tocantins: Activities and Tours

Rafting

The rivers of Tocantins offer some of the best rafting experiences in Brazil. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a first-time paddler, there’s a rafting adventure for everyone.

Indigenous Village Tour

Tocantins is home to several indigenous tribes, and taking a tour of one of their villages is an excellent way to experience their customs and way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of Tocantins

Must-Try Dishes

Arroz com Pequi

A traditional dish made with rice and the aromatic fruit of the pequi tree. It’s a unique and delicious taste that’s quintessentially Brazilian.

Pamonha

A sweet corn cake, similar to a tamale, which is wrapped in corn husks and steamed. It’s a delicious and filling snack that’s popular throughout Brazil.

Local Beverages

Cachaça

A potent Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane juice. It comes in many different varieties, ranging from mellow to fiery.

Guaraná

A soft drink made from the guaraná plant, which is native to Brazil. It has a unique and refreshing taste and is an excellent way to cool down on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Tocantins: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Girassol Plaza

Located in Palmas, this modern and luxurious hotel is a great base for exploring the city and the state at large.

Hostel 021

This cozy hostel in Mateiros is a great budget option for travelers seeking a more communal experience.

What to Buy in Tocantins: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Crafts and Artwork

Tocantins is home to many talented artisans, and their work makes for perfect souvenirs. Look for intricately woven baskets, handmade pottery, and colorful textiles.

Pequi Oil

A healthy and delicious oil made from the pequi fruit which can be used for cooking or as a salad dressing. It’s a unique and flavorful ingredient that’s hard to find outside of Brazil.

Local Culture and Customs of Tocantins

Tocantins has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, influenced by its indigenous and Afro-Brazilian populations. Visitors should take the time to learn about local customs and traditions, including music, dance, and religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Tocantins

The best time to visit Tocantins is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. This time of year offers the best weather for outdoor activities and exploring the state’s many natural wonders.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tocantins

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Wear sunscreen and bring plenty of water when exploring outdoor areas
  • Try the local cuisine and drink

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended in public places
  • Take photos of indigenous people or their communities without permission
  • Engage in behavior that would be considered disrespectful or rude

Safety and Health

Safety in Tocantins

Tocantins is generally a safe place for travelers, but visitors should be aware of the usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being vigilant in crowded areas.

Health in Tocantins

As with any tropical location, visitors should take steps to protect themselves against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria. It’s also important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Tocantins: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tocantins

Palmas International Airport, located in the capital city, is the largest airport in Tocantins. It offers regular flights to major Brazilian cities such as Brasília and São Paulo.

Bus Stations Near Tocantins

The main bus station in Palmas offers regular connections to other cities in the state as well as major cities throughout Brazil.

Other Options

Tocantins is fairly remote, so driving is usually the best way to get around. Car rentals are available at several locations throughout the state.

How to Get Around in Tocantins

Taxis and buses are available in most urban areas, but for longer trips, it’s best to rent a car or hire a driver.

Where to Go Next from Tocantins: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Goiás

The neighboring state of Goiás is home to stunning natural landmarks, such as the Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park and the Serra da Mesa dam, as well as charming colonial towns.

Brasília

The capital city of Brazil offers a unique blend of modern architecture and political history, including the iconic national congress building and the presidential palace.

Conclusion

Tocantins is a unique and beautiful state that offers something for every type of traveler. From the pristine beaches and lush forests to the vibrant culture and delicious cuisine, Tocantins is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Brazil.
