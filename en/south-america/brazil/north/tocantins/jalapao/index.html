Introduction to Jalapão

Jalapão is one of the most beautiful and unexplored places in Brazil. Located in the Tocantins state in the north of Brazil, Jalapão is a natural paradise that offers visitors a unique travel experience. It’s a land of natural wonders, with vast savannas, refreshing waterfalls, and breathtaking vistas.

The region is mostly composed of the Jalapão State Park, an area of 34,000 hectares that has been preserved since 2000. The park is considered a conservation unit, keeping intact the vegetation, the fauna, and the flora native to the region.

List of Places to Visit in Jalapão

Jalapão has a lot to offer to those who venture into the region. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Naturteca: This museum showcases the local fauna and flora, including fossils, minerals, and taxidermy specimens.
  • Pedra Furada: A rock formation that has holes created by erosion, considered a sacred local site.
  • Capim Dourado Shopping: A shopping area dedicated to local handicrafts made of “golden grass,” a type of grass only found in the Jalapão region.

Natural Attractions of Jalapão: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fervedouro: Natural limestone pools that are so dense in minerals that they create an ethereal atmosphere, making those in it feel like they are floating.
  • Cachoeira da Velha: Majestic waterfall that reaches 100 ft, ideal for a swim.
  • Serra do Espírito Santo: Plateau with a panoramic view of the region, ideal for hikes and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jalapão

Jalapão’s diverse terrain, including savannas, waterfalls, and canyons, makes it the perfect place to hike and bike. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Jalapão Circuit: A 320 Km hiking/biking trail that takes around six to thirteen days to complete. It’s the perfect way to explore the beauty and diversity of Jalapão.
  • Serra do Espírito Santo Trail: A challenging 7 Km trail that rewards adventurous hikers with a breathtaking view of the Jalapão region.

Best Things to Do in Jalapão: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy in Jalapão:

  • Scenic Flights: Take to the skies to get a bird’s eye view of Jalapão’s unique landscape.
  • Off-roading: Pic up a 4x4 vehicle and venture out into the rugged terrain of Jalapão.
  • Rafting and Canoeing: Discover the waterfalls and rivers of Jalapão on a raft or canoe.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jalapão

Here is a suggested one-week itinerary for Jalapão:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Palmas, the capital of Tocantins and transfer to Jalapão
2 Visit the Cachoeira da Velha and enjoy some hiking through the region

5 Go on a hike to Pedra Furada and see the Capim Dourado Shopping
6 Take a scenic flight to see the entire region of Jalapão fromabove

Food and Drink Scene of Jalapão

Jalapão has a rich culinary tradition. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pamonha: A traditional dish made of fresh corn, boiled and wrapped in corn husks.
  • Arroz com Pequi: A rice dish with meat and a local fruit called Pequi that has a unique, strong flavor.
  • Frango Caipira com Guariroba: Delicious stewed chicken with a local type of palm fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A distilled sugar cane drink that is widely produced and consumed in Brazil.
  • Tiquira: A traditional alcoholic drink made from cassava which has a unique aroma and flavor.
  • Cupuaçu Juice: A local fruit juice that has a delicious taste and is incredibly refreshing.

Where to Stay in Jalapão: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jalapão has a limited number of accommodations. Some of the popular options include:

  • Pousada Gira Sol: A cozy guesthouse that offers a pool, restaurant, and comfortable rooms.
  • Estalagem, Cachoeiras do Jalapão: A cozy inn surrounded by waterfalls with comfortable rooms.
  • Jalapão Ecolodge: A eco-friendly lodge that offers guided tours and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Jalapão: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jalapão is famous for its handicrafts that use golden grass, an indigenous plant that grows in the region. Some other popular souvenirs include:

  • Belts, bags, and hats made of golden grass.
  • Handmade hammocks.
  • Ceramic pieces and woodcraft.

Local Culture and Customs of Jalapão

Jalapão is a region of diverse customs and traditions. Some of the customs still practiced in Jalapão include:

  • Capim Dourado Crafting: This is the traditional art of making crafts from golden grass, and the process has been handed down for generations.
  • Music: Jalapão is region is known for its vibrant music scene, with many people playing traditional instruments like the Tamborim and Pífanos.
  • Bumba-meu-boi Festival: A popular festival celebrated in Jalapão with performances, music, and traditional foods.

Best Time to Visit Jalapão

The best time to visit Jalapão is between April to September. During this time, the weather is drier with less rainfall than the rest of the year. Due to the tropical climate, Jalapão experiences high humidity throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jalapão

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while in Jalapão:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Hire a local guide to help explore the region.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in natural areas.
  • Ignore safety precautions when enjoying waterfalls and other natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the local traditions when interacting with the community.

Safety and Health

Jalapão is considered a relatively safe travel destination. However, it’s essential to follow safety guidelines related to natural attractions and wildlife. Be aware of the following:

Safety in Jalapão

  • Do not swim in waterfalls or rivers without supervision.
  • Keep valuables safe.
  • Ensure you have proper gear when going hiking or biking.

Health in Jalapão

  • Bring plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquitos that may carry diseases.
  • Check with a medical professional before traveling to ensure all necessary vaccinations are up to date.

Getting to Jalapão: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jalapão is a remote area that can be challenging to access. However, some transportation options are available:

Airports Near Jalapão

The nearest airport to Jalapão is Palmas International Airport, which is around 198 km away.

Bus Stations Near Jalapão

From Palmas, there are buses to São Félix do Tocantins, which is located near the entrance to Jalapão. Buses operate daily, and the trip takes around nine hours.

Other Options

Renting a car is also an option to reach Jalapão, although the route is quite challenging and requires a 4x4 vehicle.

How to Get Around in Jalapão

One of the best ways to explore Jalapão is by hiring a 4x4 vehicle, which allows you to go off-road and explore some of the region’s more remote areas. Some areas can be challenging to traverse by car, so it’s essential to rent a car with an experienced driver.

Where to Go Next from Jalapão: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue your adventure in Brazil, here are some nearby destinations that are worth visiting:

  • Chapada dos Veadeiros: A National Park with stunning waterfalls, located in the neighbor state of Goiás.
  • Lençóis Maranhenses National Park: A stunning coastal national park with vast, emerald-hued lagoons.
  • Jericoacoara: A coastal town with some of the best beaches and sand dunes in Brazil.

Conclusion

Jalapão is a natural paradise that offers visitors a unique experience in Brazil. It’s a mystical and breathtaking region that deserves more attention from adventurous travelers seeking off-the-beaten-path experiences. From waterfalls, savannas, jungles, and trails, Jalapão has everything for those seeking nature’s awe-inspiring beauty. Make sure to visit Jalapão on your next trip to Brazil, and you won’t regret it.
