Barcelos Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tourist Handbook

Barcelos is a must-see destination in Amazonas, Brazil, and South America. This charming small town is rich in history, culture, and nature, offering a diverse array of activities and attractions for travelers of all ages and interests. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will show you everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Barcelos, including:

Introduction to Barcelos

Barcelos is a hidden gem of Amazonas, located in the heart of the Amazon Forest and surrounded by the Rio Negro and the Anavilhanas Archipelago, the largest river archipelago in the world. Barcelos is famous for its handmade ceramics, colorful crafts, and religious festivals, which attract visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Barcelos

Barcelos offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Centro Cultural de Barcelos: This museum showcases Barcelos’ rich history and culture, with exhibits of ceramics, folklore, and religious artifacts.
  • Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceição: This historical church is a symbol of Barcelos’ religious heritage, with beautiful Baroque and Rococo architecture and richly decorated interior.
  • Casa da Memória: This historical house serves as a cultural center and library, with exhibitions and workshops on local arts and crafts.

Natural Attractions of Barcelos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Municipal do Balneário das Águas Claras: This park is a natural oasis in Barcelos, with crystal-clear cascading pools, lush vegetation, and picnic areas.
  • Anavilhanas Archipelago: This unique ecosystem comprises over 400 islands and islets, surrounded by waters rich in wildlife and vegetation.
  • Cachoeira do El Dorado: This stunning waterfall is a must-see sight for nature lovers, with its 15-meter cascade, crystal-clear waters, and exotic fauna and flora.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barcelos

Barcelos boasts some of the best hiking and biking routes in Amazonas, offering breathtaking views of the forest, rivers, and waterfalls. Some of the top routes to try include:

  • Trilha do Rio Negro: This 10-kilometer trail follows the shores of the Rio Negro, passing through stunning landscapes of beaches, waters, and forests.
  • Trilha do Anavilhanas: This 5-kilometer trail takes you deep into the Anavilhanas Archipelago, through exotic vegetation, wildlife, and riverside communities.
  • Ciclovia de Barcelos: This 3-kilometer bike path winds through the streets and parks of Barcelos, offering a fun and safe way to discover the town’s landmarks and scenery.

Best Things to Do in Barcelos: Activities and Tours

Barcelos offers plenty of fun and exciting things to do for travelers of all ages and preferences, such as:

  • Handmade ceramics workshop: Learn how to make your own pottery like a local artisan, using the traditional techniques and materials of Barcelos.
  • Canoeing and fishing tour: Explore the Rio Negro and its tributaries on a canoe trip, admiring the scenery and catching fish with the guidance of experienced locals.
  • Religious festival: Join Barcelos’ biggest and liveliest festival, the Festa do Divino, which takes place annually in May and celebrates the town’s religious heritage with music, dance, and processions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barcelos

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel or guesthouse. Visit Centro Cultural de Barcelos and Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceição.

Day 2: Hiking in the Anavilhanas Archipelago, with a visit to Cachoeira do El Dorado.

Day 3: Handmade ceramics workshop and visit to Casa da Memória.

Day 4: Canoeing and fishing tour, with lunch on a river beach.

Day 5: Relaxation day, with a visit to Parque Municipal do Balneário das Águas Claras and a picnic lunch.

Day 6: Bike tour of Barcelos’ highlights, including the Mercado Municipal and the town square.

Day 7: Departure day, with a final souvenir shopping and farewell lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Barcelos

Barcelos’ cuisine is a reflection of its diverse cultural influences, combining indigenous, European, and African flavors and ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes and drinks include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacacá: a spicy soup made with cassava flour, shrimps, and jambu.
  • Pirarucu: a giant fish from the Amazon, usually served grilled or in a stew.
  • Tambaqui assado: another fish specialty, typically cooked on a open fire and served with farofa (an accompaniment made with cassava flour and spices).

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail, made with cachaça (sugar cane spirit), lime, sugar, and ice.
  • Guaraná: a soft drink made from the guaraná fruit, which is native to the Amazon and has high caffeine content.
  • Cachaça Barcelos: a locally produced cachaça, made in copper stills and aged in wooden barrels.

Where to Stay in Barcelos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barcelos offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels and resorts.

Budget Options

  • Pousada da Maria: a simple and comfortable guesthouse, located near the center of town.
  • Hotel Grey: a budget hotel with air-conditioned rooms and free breakfast.
  • Hostel da Lili: a friendly and relaxed hostel, with shared dorms and private rooms.

Mid-Range Options

  • Hotel Avenida: a modern and well-equipped hotel, with swimming pool, gym, and restaurant.
  • Barcelos Resort: a secluded and serene resort, with exclusive bungalows, swimming pool, and spa.
  • Park Hotel: a charming hotel, located in a historic building, with elegant rooms and traditional décor.

Luxury Options

  • Anavilhanas Jungle Lodge: a luxurious and eco-friendly lodge, nested in the heart of the Anavilhanas Archipelago, with gourmet dining, spa, and guided tours.
  • Juma Amazon Lodge: a boutique and high-end lodge, located on a private lake and surrounded by pristine nature, with fine dining, pool, and spa.
  • Mirante do Gavião: a five-star and sophisticated hotel, offering stunning views of the Rio Negro and the forest, with elegant suites, spa, and gourmet cuisine.

What to Buy in Barcelos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Barcelos is famous for its handmade ceramics, colorful crafts, and exotic fruits and spices. Some of the best items to bring home as souvenirs include:

  • Ceramics: plates, bowls, vases, and figurines, decorated with bold colors and intricate patterns.
  • Woven baskets and mats: made with vegetable fibers and natural dyes, and featuring unique designs and shapes.
  • Spices and herbs: such as turmeric, chili peppers, and cinnamon, which add flavor and aroma to traditional dishes and drinks.

Local Culture and Customs of Barcelos

Barcelos has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, influenced by indigenous, European, and African traditions. Some of the unique aspects of Barcelos’ culture and customs include:

  • Handmade ceramics: a craft that has been passed down for generations, and that reflects the town’s artistic and creative spirit.
  • Religious festivals: including the Festa do Divino, the Festa de São Francisco, and the Festa de Nossa Senhora do Rosário, which blend music, dance, and food with religious devotion.
  • Hospitality and warmth: Barcelos’ locals are known for their friendly and welcoming attitude towards visitors, making them feel at home in their town.

Best Time to Visit Barcelos

Barcelos’ climate is tropical, with high temperatures and humidity throughout the year. However, the best time to visit Barcelos is during the dry season, from June to November, when the skies are clearer, the waters lower, and the wildlife more visible. The rainy season, from December to May, can be more challenging for outdoor activities, but also offers its own allure, with greener vegetation, higher water levels, and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barcelos

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as avoiding public displays of affection and dressing modestly in religious sites.
  • Embrace the local culture and cuisine, by trying local dishes, taking part in festivals, and buying local products.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, especially when in crowded places or at night.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural heritage of Barcelos, by leaving garbage or vandalizing historical or artistic sites.
  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drug use or wildlife trafficking, which are strongly prohibited and penalized in Brazil.
  • Disrespect local people, by using offensive language or behaving rudely, which can result in a negative and unsafe experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barcelos

Barcelos is generally a safe and peaceful town, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it is always advisable to take some precautions to ensure your safety, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated or poorly lit areas.
  • Keep your valuable items, such as money, passport, and electronics, in a safe place, such as a hotel safe or a hidden pocket.
  • Be aware of common scams or frauds, such as fake currency or overpriced tours, and report any suspicious behavior to the authorities.

Health in Barcelos

Barcelos is located in a tropical region, which can expose travelers to some health risks, such as mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever, malaria, or Zika virus, or water-borne diseases, such as diarrhea or hepatitis. Therefore, it is essential that travelers take some health measures, such as:

  • Consult a doctor or a travel health clinic before your trip, to get informed about the necessary vaccinations or preventive medication.
  • Use mosquito repellents, wear long-sleeved clothes, and use bed nets, to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water, and avoid eating raw or undercooked food, especially seafood.

Getting to Barcelos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Barcelos is accessible by air, boat or road, depending on your departure point and preferences. Some of the top options to consider include:

Airports Near Barcelos

  • Manaus International Airport: the main airport in Amazonas, with direct flights from major cities in Brazil and abroad, and connecting flights to Barcelos.

Boat Stations Near Barcelos

  • Porto de Manaus: the main boat station in Manaus, which offers frequent and affordable boat trips to Barcelos, with a travel time of about 24 hours.

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi: if you prefer a more flexible and personalized travel experience, you can hire a car or a taxi from Manaus, with a travel time of about 7 hours.
  • Charter boat: if you want to experience the Rio Negro and its tributaries, you can charter a private boat from Manaus, with a travel time of about 15 hours.

How to Get Around in Barcelos

Barcelos is a small and walkable town, with many of its attractions and amenities located within walking distance. However, if you want to explore more distant sights or travel in a more comfortable way, you can use some of the following options:

  • Taxi or moto-taxi: available at the main squares and hotels, and offering affordable rates and friendly services.
  • Bicycle rental: several guesthouses and bike shops offer rental bicycles, which are a fun and healthy way to discover the town’s surroundings.
  • Boat tour: several local operators offer boat tours to the nearby islands and waterfalls, which allow you to enjoy the scenery and wildlife of Barcelos from a different perspective.

Where to Go Next from Barcelos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barcelos is located in a privileged region of Amazonas, which offers plenty of other exciting destinations and excursions to choose from, such as:

  • Manaus: the capital of Amazonas, with its famous Teatro Amazonas, its lively Mercado Municipal, and its urban jungle tours.
  • Presidente Figueiredo: a scenic town known for its waterfalls and caves, located about 120 kilometers from Manaus.
  • Novo Airão: a charming town known for its handicrafts and river dolphins, located about 180 kilometers from Manaus.

Conclusion

Barcelos is a fascinating and enchanting town in Amazonas, offering a unique combination of cultural heritage, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Whether you come for a day or a week, you will find plenty to explore and enjoy in Barcelos, from its historical landmarks and artisanal crafts to its exotic wildlife and delicious cuisine. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to pack your bags and discover Barcelos for yourself!
