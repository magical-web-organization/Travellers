Introduction to Acre

Located in North Brazil, Acre is a fascinating destination filled with natural wonders and a rich cultural history. Famous for its rubber exports, the city is now home to a diverse range of people and traditions. If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure, Acre won’t disappoint. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Acre

Here are the top destinations in Acre that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museu da Borracha (Rubber Museum)
  2. Presépio de Xapuri
  3. Complexo da Gameleira

Natural Attractions of Acre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Parque Nacional da Serra do Divisor
  2. Cachoeira do Jaguar
  3. Gruta do Amor

Hiking/Biking Routes of Acre

Acre is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking trails:

  1. Trilha do Sítio Cagado
  2. Trilha do Parque Estadual Chandless
  3. Trilha do Juruá

Best Things to Do in Acre: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the indigenous communities
  2. Take a river cruise down the Juruá River
  3. Witness the art of making rubber at the Rubber Museum

Week-Long Itinerary for Acre

Day 1: Explore the Museu da Borracha and Parque Nacional da Serra do Divisor

Day 2: Visit the Presépio de Xapuri and Cachoeira do Jaguar

Day 3: Take a river cruise down the Juruá River

Day 4: Hike Trilha do Sítio Cagado

Day 5: Visit the Gruta do Amor and Complexo da Gameleira

Day 6: Witness the art of making rubber at the Rubber Museum

Day 7: Visit the indigenous communities

  1. Handmade baskets and bags
  2. Rubber sandals
  3. T-shirts with indigenous prints

Local Culture and Customs of Acre

Acre has a unique cultural heritage, shaped by its history and geography. Here are some things to know about the local culture and customs:

  1. Acre is home to several indigenous communities, who still maintain their traditional way of life.
  2. The rubber industry played a significant role in shaping Acre’s history and economy.
  3. Portuguese is the official language, but many people speak Spanish and indigenous languages.

Best Time to Visit Acre

The best time to visit Acre is during the dry season from June to September. The temperature is cooler, and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Acre

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Be mindful of the environment and wildlife.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the indigenous communities or their way of life.
  2. Litter or leave your trash behind.
  3. Get too close to wild animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Acre

Acre is generally a safe destination. However, it’s always wise to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when travelling alone.

Health in Acre

There’s a risk of malaria in some parts of Acre. Be sure to take appropriate precautions and speak to your doctor before travelling.

Getting to Acre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Acre

  1. Aeroporto Internacional de Rio Branco (RBR)
  2. Aeroporto de Cruzeiro do Sul (CZS)

Bus Stations Near Acre

  1. Terminal Rodoviário de Rio Branco
  2. Terminal Rodoviário de Cruzeiro do Sul

Other Options

You can also take a riverboat from Manaus to reach Acre.

How to Get Around in Acre

The best way to get around Acre is by renting a car or hiring a private driver. Alternatively, you can take a taxi or use public transport.

Where to Go Next from Acre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you might want to check out:

  1. Manaus
  2. Porto Velho
  3. Bogotá, Colombia

Conclusion

Acre is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. From its natural wonders to its rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor adventurer, Acre won’t disappoint. Plan your trip today and get ready for an unforgettable experience!
