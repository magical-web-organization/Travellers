Travel Guide to Alter do Chão, Brazil

Alter do Chão is a charming village located in the heart of the Amazon rainforest in Pará, northern Brazil. It boasts beautiful beaches, lush forests, unique wildlife, and plenty of culture and history. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just relaxation, Alter do Chão has something to offer. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you make the most of your trip to Alter do Chão:

Introduction to Alter do Chão

Alter do Chão, also known as “Caribbean of the Amazon,” is a small village located on the banks of the Tapajós River, a tributary of the Amazon River. Its pristine white-sand beaches, turquoise waters, and lush forests attract visitors from all over the world. The village is home to friendly locals who are proud of their culture and traditions. The best time to visit Alter do Chão is between July and December, when the waters of the Tapajós River are calmer and the weather is drier.

List of Places to Visit in Alter do Chão

There are plenty of places to visit in Alter do Chão, from cultural attractions to natural wonders. Here are some highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu do Tapajós: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Tapajós people, with exhibits on their art, traditional practices, and history.
  • Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Saúde: This church, built in the 18th century, is a beautiful example of Baroque architecture with intricate wood carvings and colorful paintings.
  • Fortaleza de Alter do Chão: This fort, built in the 1600s, is a great place to learn about the region’s colonial past and enjoy stunning views of the Tapajós River.

Natural Attractions of Alter do Chão: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia do Amor (Love Beach): This stunning beach, with its clear waters and soft sand, is a must-visit. You can also rent kayaks or paddleboards to explore the surrounding waters.
  • Praia do Pindobal: This beach is located in a remote area of the rainforest, accessible only by boat. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy nature.
  • Flona do Tapajós: This national forest is home to diverse wildlife, including jaguars, giant otters, and monkeys. You can take a guided tour to explore the forest and learn about its importance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alter do Chão

Alter do Chão is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. Here are some routes to consider:

  • Lago Verde Trail: This trail, located within the Flona do Tapajós, takes you through the rainforest to a beautiful lagoon surrounded by lush vegetation.
  • Serra da Piroca Trail: This challenging trail takes you to the top of Serra da Piroca mountain, with stunning views of the surrounding rainforest.
  • Biking along the Tapajós River: Rent a bike and explore the village and surrounding areas at your own pace, taking in the beautiful views along the Tapajós River.

Best Things to Do in Alter do Chão: Activities and Tours

Here are some top activities and tours to consider in Alter do Chão:

  • Boat tour of the Tapajós River: Take a boat tour to explore the river, visit remote villages, and enjoy breathtaking views of the rainforest.
  • Paddleboarding: Rent a board and explore the Tapajós River at your own pace.
  • Guided canoe trip: Take a guided canoe trip of the Amazon rainforest to see wildlife and learn about the traditions of the local communities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alter do Chão

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Alter do Chão:

Day 1 Arrival in Alter do Chão; explore the village and relax on Praia do Amor

Food and Drink Scene of Alter do Chão

Alter do Chão has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options to sample local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacacá: A traditional soup made with tapioca, jambu leaves, and shrimp broth.
  • Maniçoba: A stew made with tapioca leaves and different types of meat, cooked for several days.
  • Pirarucu: A large fish from the Amazon, often served grilled or in a stew.

Local Beverages

  • Açaí: A popular Amazonian fruit, often served in a smoothie or bowl.
  • Cachaça: A distilled spirit made from sugar cane, often served as a caipirinha cocktail.
  • Guaraná: A soda made from the Guaraná fruit, a popular drink in Brazil.

Where to Stay in Alter do Chão: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Alter do Chão to suit all budgets. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Mirante da Ilha: This hotel boasts stunning views of the Tapajós River and offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • Pousada Alterosa: This guesthouse is located just a few minutes’ walk from Praia do Amor and offers friendly service and comfortable rooms.
  • Hostel Tapajós: This hostel offers budget-friendly dorm rooms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Alter do Chão: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, here are some options to consider:

  • Crafts and art from local artisans: You can find handmade items such as baskets, pottery, and jewelry, made by local artisans using traditional techniques.
  • Cachaça and other local spirits: You can find a variety of local spirits to take home, including cachaça, açaí liqueur, and more.
  • Local delicacies: You can find many local food items to take home, including dried fish, farinha (a type of flour), and fresh fruit.

Local Culture and Customs of Alter do Chão

Alter do Chão is home to a diverse mix of indigenous and non-indigenous communities, each with its own traditions and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs: Be aware of local customs and traditions, and show respect when visiting communities.
  • Support local communities: Consider supporting local communities by buying handmade crafts or booking tours with local guides.
  • Learn about the environment: The Amazon rainforest is a unique and fragile ecosystem, so be mindful of your impact when exploring it.

Best Time to Visit Alter do Chão

The best time to visit Alter do Chão is between July and December, when the weather is drier and the waters of the Tapajós River are calmer. The village can get quite busy during festival season, which takes place in September, but is a great opportunity to experience local culture and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alter do Chão

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Alter do Chão:

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks: Alter do Chão is known for its delicious and unique cuisine, so be sure to experience it.
  • Explore the natural surroundings: Alter do Chão is surrounded by beautiful rainforest and waters, so make the most of it.
  • Respect local culture and customs: Be mindful of local customs and show respect to the communities.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the environment clean and dispose of waste properly.
  • Disrupt wildlife: Be mindful of wildlife and avoid disrupting their habitats.
  • Disrespect local culture: Avoid engaging in any activities that may be disrespectful to local culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Alter do Chão is generally a safe destination, but it’s still important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Alter do Chão

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Use common sense and be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night.
  • Use reliable transportation: Book transportation through reputable sources and avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Protect against mosquitos: Alter do Chão is located in an area with a risk of mosquito-borne illnesses, so use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants when possible.

Health in Alter do Chão

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of fluids, particularly during the hot months.
  • Be mindful of food and water safety: Stick to bottled or purified water, and avoid raw or undercooked food.
  • Seek medical attention if needed: If you experience any health issues during your trip, seek medical attention as soon as possible.

Getting to Alter do Chão: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alter do Chão is accessible by air, road, and river. Here are some options:

Airports Near Alter do Chão

  • Santarém-Maestro Wilson Fonseca International Airport: This is the closest airport, located about 40 km from Alter do Chão. It offers domestic flights from major cities in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near Alter do Chão

  • Santarém Bus Station: This is the closest bus station to Alter do Chão, located in the city of Santarém. Buses run regularly from cities throughout Brazil.

Other Options

  • Private transportation: You can book private transportation from Santarém to Alter do Chão through a tour company or hotel.
  • River transportation: You can also travel to Alter do Chão by boat from Santarém or other nearby cities along the Tapajós River.

How to Get Around in Alter do Chão

Alter do Chão is a small village that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and moto-taxis are also available for longer distances or for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Alter do Chão: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider after your visit to Alter do Chão. Here are some options:

  • Santarém: This city is located about 30 km from Alter do Chão and offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Amazon rainforest lodges: There are many lodges located deep in the Amazon rainforest, where you can experience wildlife and nature up close.
  • Other nearby destinations: Other nearby destinations include Belém, Manaus, and the Brazilian coastline.

Conclusion

Alter do Chão is a beautiful and unique destination in the heart of the Amazon rainforest. With its stunning beaches, lush forests, and rich culture and history, it’s a great place to explore and experience. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to Alter do Chão and make the most of your time there. Enjoy your travels!
