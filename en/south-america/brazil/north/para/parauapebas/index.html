Introduction to Parauapebas

Located in the northern region of Brazil, Parauapebas is a picturesque city that boasts unique cultural and natural attractions. It is famous for its mining industry, and visitors can explore the beautiful landscape and geological formations of the nearby Serra dos Carajás mountains. This guide provides a comprehensive look at what to do and see in Parauapebas, including must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, local culture and customs, and safety considerations.

List of Places to Visit in Parauapebas

Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural or natural attractions, there is something for everyone in Parauapebas. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Parauapebas City Museum: This museum is located in the city center and showcases the history of Parauapebas and the surrounding region. Visitors can see various exhibits about the indigenous cultures that lived here, as well as the growth and development of the city.
  • Igreja do Sagrado Coração de Jesus: This historical church is one of the most beautiful landmarks in Parauapebas. Built in 1974, the church features stunning architecture and design.
  • Casa da Cultura: A cultural center that is home to various art and music events throughout the year. Visitors can explore the local artisan shops and enjoy the exhibitions on display.

Natural Attractions of Parauapebas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra dos Carajás: This national park features beautiful landscapes, waterfalls, and unique geological formations that are perfect for hiking and exploring. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the area’s unique culture and natural history.
  • Lago do Sossego: A serene lake surrounded by nature that is the perfect spot for picnicking, fishing, and taking peaceful walks.
  • Cachoeira do Tingui: A stunning waterfall that is accessible by hiking. Visitors can cool off in the refreshing waters or enjoy the tranquility of the surrounding forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parauapebas

Parauapebas is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in Brazil. Whether you’re looking for scenic views, challenging terrains, or just a leisurely walk, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Cachoeira do Tingui Trail: A moderate hiking trail that takes visitors to the breathtaking Cachoeira do Tingui waterfall. Visitors will enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding forest and landscapes.
  • Serra dos Carajás Trail: This hiking trail offers panoramic views of the Serra dos Carajás mountains and the surrounding landscapes. It’s a challenging route, but definitely worth the effort!
  • Bosque Rodrigues Alves Trail: A popular hiking and biking route that features stunning scenery, including a botanical garden, butterfly house, and a mini zoo.

Best Things to Do in Parauapebas: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the must-see attractions and exploring the natural beauty of Parauapebas, there are many other fun things to do in the city. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Horseback riding: A popular activity in Parauapebas that allows visitors to explore the surrounding countryside in a unique and exciting way.
  • Rafting and kayaking: Visitors can enjoy an adrenaline rush by going rafting or kayaking on nearby rivers and rapids.
  • Paragliding: For those who seek adventure and stunning views, paragliding options are available in the Serra dos Carajás.
  • Explore Local Artisan Shops: Parauapebas is a haven for local artisans, and visitors can find unique souvenirs and gifts in the various shops around the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Parauapebas

No trip to Parauapebas is complete without indulging in the local food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tucupi: A savory soup made with fermented manioc juice and served with shrimps and jambu leaves.
  • Pato no Tucupi: Duck meat cooked in tucupi sauce, served with rice, manioc flour and lime.
  • Moqueca de tambaqui: A delicious fish stew made with tambaqui fish, coconut milk, onion, tomato and coriander.

Local Beverages

  • Cupuaçu juice: An exotic fruit juice, rich in vitamins and minerals, with a refreshing taste.
  • Cachaça: A distilled spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice, that is popular throughout Brazil.

Local Culture and Customs of Parauapebas

Parauapebas has a unique local culture that visitors should be aware of. Some of the customs and traditions of the city include:

  • Celebration of Holy Week: This is one of the most important religious celebrations in Parauapebas, and includes processions and other events throughout the city.
  • Crafts and Artwork: As mentioned, Parauapebas is home to many skilled artisans, and visitors can find unique crafts and artwork around the city.
  • Lifestyle: The local lifestyle in Parauapebas is warm and friendly, and locals are welcoming to visitors from all over the world.

Best Time to Visit Parauapebas

The best time to visit Parauapebas is between June and August, which is the dry season. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and explore the natural beauty of the region without having to worry about heavy rainfall. However, note that the ideal time to visit can vary depending on the type of experience you are looking for.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parauapebas

Overall, Parauapebas is a relatively safe city, and visitors can explore the cultural and natural attractions without having to worry about crime. However, it is recommended to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas after dark and not carrying large amounts of cash or jewelry.

Health in Parauapebas

Parauapebas has good quality medical facilities, and visitors can expect to receive adequate medical attention if needed. However, it is recommended to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and to stay hydrated during hikes and outdoor activities.
