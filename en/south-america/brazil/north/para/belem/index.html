Introduction to Belém

Located in the northern region of Brazil, Belém is the capital city of Pará state. Boasting a rich history, distinct food and drink scene, and diverse natural landscapes, it’s no wonder that Belém is known as “The Brazilian Amazon’s Door.” Ideal for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike, Belém offers plenty of activities, both urban and rural. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with everything you need to know about Belém, including where to stay, what to eat, and the must-visit attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Belém

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Theatro da Paz

Built in the late 19th century, Theatro da Paz is one of the most iconic landmarks in Belém. It is a majestic opera house with Italian-style architecture and has been impeccably preserved. Visitors can take a guided tour of the building and learn about its rich history, as well as attend events such as operas and concerts.

Forte do Presépio (Fort of the Nativity)

Built in the 17th century, Forte do Presépio is a well-preserved fort that was constructed to defend the city from invaders. Located by the riverside, visitors can enjoy a breathtaking view of the city and get a sense of Belém’s past. History enthusiasts will particularly enjoy the fort’s museum, which houses a collection of historical artifacts.

Museu Emílio Goeldi (Emílio Goeldi Museum)

The Emílio Goeldi Museum is a natural history and anthropology museum, one of the most important of its kind in Brazil. It features a variety of exhibits, including the Amazonian flora and fauna, and a replica of a Marajoara vase, which is a pre-Columbian artifact found in the Marajó island.

Natural Attractions of Belém: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ver-o-Peso Market

Located next to the docks in the city center, Ver-o-Peso Market is one of the biggest open-air markets in Latin America, where visitors can buy local foods and handicrafts. Visitors can explore the market’s alleys lined with colorful stalls, and try regional food, such as the açaí bowls and grilled fishes.

Mangal das Garças

Mangal das Garças is a large urban park with a beautiful nature reserve. Visitors can take a guided tour of the park, where they will observe mangroves, wading birds, and other animals. The park has a tower with a stunning view of Belém, as well as a butterfly house.

Ilha do Marajó

Ilha do Marajó is the largest fluvial-maritime island in the world, located around 2.5 hours away from Belém by boat. The island is home to a large population of water buffalos, and visitors can take horseback riding tours or visit the many beaches that the island has to offer. Marajoara pottery and handicrafts are also available on the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belém

Refúgio Ecológico do Charles Darwin

The Charles Darwin Ecological Refuge offers visitors the opportunity to rent bicycles and pedal through a 40-hectare forest reserve while observing and learning about the Amazonian environment. Visitors can spot howler monkeys, sloths, and various bird species.

Parque Ambiental de Belém

The Belém Environmental Park is located in the city center, just a short distance away from the Redemptorist Basilica. Pedestrians and bikers can follow a trail through the park’s lush greenery, find an ecological sanctuary, and see the park’s interesting sculptures.

Best Things to Do in Belém: Activities and Tours

Sunset Sail on the River

One of the most magical experiences in Belém is to see the sunset from a viewpoint that can only be seen from the river. The tour takes place during the late afternoon, and visitors can watch the changing colors of the sky as the sun sets over the city while enjoying drinks and snacks.

Combu Island Beach Day

Combu is a small island located a short boat ride away from Belém. Visitors can relax and bask in the sun on the island’s white sand beaches while enjoying fresh seafood and cocktails. Visitors can also rent kayaks and venture out to explore the island’s crystal clear river waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Belém

Must-Try Dishes

Tacacá

Tacacá is a soup made with cassava broth, dried shrimps, jambu leaves, and regional spices. It is served in a bowl with a wooden spoon, and should be consumed while hot.

Açaí

Açaí is a fruit that is native to the Amazon rainforest. It’s a vital component of Pará’s cuisine and is used in various dishes, such as bowls, smoothies, and ice cream. Visitors can try this antioxidant-rich fruit at any street corner fruit stand or juice bar.

Local Beverages

Cachaça

Cachaça is a distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice. It’s a perfect drink to have on hot days and can be enjoyed straight or used to make caipirinhas and other cocktails.

Cupuaçu Juice

Cupuaçu is a fruit that is also native to the Amazon. Its juice is a favorite among locals and visitors alike, and can be found in most juice bars and restaurants throughout Belém.

Where to Stay in Belém: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Grand Mercure Belém

The Grand Mercure Belém is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Belém, making it an excellent option for those wanting to be near all of the main tourist attractions. It features an outdoor pool, gym, and a restaurant that serves both regional and international dishes.

Hotel Manacá

Hotel Manacá is a charming guest house located in the historic district of Nazaré. The guest house has comfortable rooms with traditional decor and a delicious breakfast provided every morning.

Hostel Amazonia

Hostel Amazonia is a budget-friendly option, perfect for backpackers and solo travelers. It’s located in the city center and offers various dorm rooms and private rooms with shared bathrooms.

What to Buy in Belém: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Marajoara Pottery

Marajoara pottery is a particular type of pottery that dates back to the pre-Columbian era and is named after the Marajó island. Its intricate designs and shapes make it a unique handmade souvenir.

Brazilian Gems and Jewelry

Brazil is home to various precious gems, such as emerald and tourmaline, which are used to make beautiful jewelry. Visitors can buy these gems at any jewelry store in Belém.

Local Culture and Customs of Belém

Belém is a diverse city, influenced by indigenous, European, and African cultures. The locals are known for their cordiality, so visitors can expect to be treated with warmth and friendliness. It is common for the locals to greet each other with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek, depending on the degree of familiarity.

Best Time to Visit Belém

Belém enjoys a tropical climate, with temperatures ranging between 27°C to 32°C throughout the year. While it is warm all year-round, the best time to visit is from late July to early December when rainfall is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belém

Do:

  • Try the regional dishes and drinks.
  • Carry enough cash as some establishments don’t accept debit/credit cards.
  • Be conscious of your belongings while exploring the city.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended in public areas.
  • Wear flashy clothing and expensive jewelry when exploring the city.
  • Drink tap water – always opt for bottled water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belém

While Belém is generally safe, visitors should take extra precautions when traveling to the city. It’s recommended to avoid walking alone at night and to always be aware of your surroundings. Visitors should also avoid carrying large amounts of cash and should be cautious while using ATMs.

Health in Belém

Visitors should take sanitary precautions, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding mosquito bites. Yellow fever is endemic to the Amazon region, so visitors should get vaccinated beforehand.

Getting to Belém: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belém

Belém is served by Val de Cães International Airport (BEL), located approximately 11 kilometers from the city center. The airport is easily accessible by taxis or ride-sharing services.

Bus Stations Near Belém

Belém has various bus terminals to choose from, including Rodoviária de Belém, Rodoviária do Guamá, and Terminal de Passageiros de São Brás. The terminals are close to the city center and offer reliable services toward surrounding cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Belém by boat, as Belém is located along the Amazon River and is a popular stop for river cruises and ferry travels.

How to Get Around in Belém

The city has various public transportation options, including buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services. Taxis tend to be the most expensive option, but it’s also the most comfortable choice.

Where to Go Next from Belém: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salinópolis

Located around four hours away from Belém, Salinópolis is a popular coastal destination with beautiful white sand beaches and clear waters. It’s an ideal destination for those who want to relax and enjoy the beach.

Santarém

Santarém is a city located in the western part of Pará. It has a diverse culture, beautiful beaches, and an abundance of wildlife. Santarém makes a great stop for visitors who wish to continue exploring the Amazon.

Conclusion

Belém is a city that’s full of natural beauty and cultural richness. With a wide selection of attractions, activities, and food, it’s a destination that’s worth visiting for any traveler. Whether it’s hiking, visiting historical landmarks, or exploring the local cuisine, Belém has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Belém and make the most out of your experience.
