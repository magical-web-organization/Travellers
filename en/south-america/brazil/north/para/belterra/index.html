Belterra is a hidden gem in the Brazilian state of Pará, located in the north of the country in South America. This charming town is known for its rich history and abundant natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll find everything you need to know to plan your trip to Belterra, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get here.

Introduction to Belterra

Belterra was founded in 1934 as a planned city to support the rubber industry in the Amazon region. Today, it’s a peaceful town that attracts travelers who want to experience the beauty of the Brazilian Amazon and learn about the history of the region. You’ll find a mix of natural and cultural attractions here, along with friendly people and a laid-back atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Belterra

Belterra has a variety of attractions to explore, from historical sites to stunning natural landscapes. Here are some of the top places to visit in Belterra:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Histórico de Belterra: This museum traces the history of the town and the rubber industry in the Amazon region. You’ll find artifacts and exhibits that give insight into life in the early 1900s and the impact of the rubber boom.
  • Ruínas de Fordlândia: This abandoned town was once a bustling center of the Ford Motor Company’s rubber plantation in the Amazon. Today, you can explore the ruins of buildings and machinery and learn about the rise and fall of the industry.
  • Igreja de São Francisco de Assis: This church features beautiful architecture and religious artifacts that reflect the town’s spiritual traditions.

Natural Attractions of Belterra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Floresta Nacional do Tapajós: This national forest is located near Belterra and offers visitors the chance to hike, fish, and explore the diverse wildlife of the Amazon rainforest.
  • Rio Tapajós: This river is one of the major waterways in the Amazon Basin and offers spectacular views of the surrounding wilderness. You can take a boat tour or go swimming and enjoy the pristine waters.
  • Cachoeira do Aruã: This picturesque waterfall is located near Belterra and is popular among locals and tourists alike for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belterra

Belterra offers several options for hiking and biking in the surrounding areas. The Floresta Nacional do Tapajós offers a range of trails for all skill levels, with opportunities to spot wildlife and learn about the flora and fauna of the Amazon.

Best Things to Do in Belterra: Activities and Tours

Belterra has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the top options:

  • Boat tours of the Rio Tapajós and local nature reserves
  • River swimming and fishing
  • Cultural tours of the town and surrounding areas
  • Outdoor adventures in the Floresta Nacional do Tapajós, including hiking, camping, and wildlife watching

Food and Drink Scene of Belterra

Belterra has a rich culinary tradition that reflects the diverse influences of the Amazon region. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacacá: This iconic dish is made from a broth of tucupi (a yellow broth made from cassava root), dried shrimp, and jambu (an herb that creates a tingling sensation in the mouth). It’s served hot and is often accompanied by a piece of tapioca.
  • Pato no tucupi: This hearty dish features duck cooked in tucupi broth, along with vegetables and seasoning.
  • Pirarucu: This large freshwater fish is a staple of the Amazon region and is often served grilled or sautéed with local herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This distilled spirit is made from sugarcane and is a popular drink in Brazil. It can be enjoyed straight or mixed with fruit juice or soda.
  • Cupuaçu juice: This refreshing juice is made from the fruit of the same name, which is native to the Amazon. It has a sweet and tangy flavor and is high in vitamin C.

Where to Stay in Belterra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belterra has a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  • Pousada Tapajós: This guesthouse is located in the heart of the town and offers comfortable rooms with air conditioning and free Wi-Fi.
  • Fordlândia Palace Hotel: This historic hotel is located near the Ruínas de Fordlândia and features a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.
  • Itaoca Eco Resort: This eco-friendly resort is located on the edge of the Floresta Nacional do Tapajós and offers secluded cabins, hiking trails, and a natural swimming area.

What to Buy in Belterra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belterra is known for its handicrafts and local products, which make great souvenirs. Here are some items to look for:

  • Rubber-made products: These include items like belts, bags, and wallets that are made from natural rubber and are a unique souvenir from the Amazon region.
  • Hand-carved wooden objects: These can include figurines, utensils, and bowls made from local wood.
  • Cachaça: Belterra has several distilleries that produce high-quality cachaça, which makes a great gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Belterra

Belterra is a town with a rich cultural heritage, blending elements from indigenous, European, and African traditions. The people of Belterra are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are often struck by the laid-back pace of life here. It’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes for certain events and the use of local greetings and phrases.

Best Time to Visit Belterra

The best time to visit Belterra is during the dry season, which runs from June to December. This is when the weather is most comfortable and outdoor activities are most enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belterra

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages
  • Be respectful of local customs
  • Pack comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor adventures

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Assume everyone speaks English (Portuguese is the primary language)

Safety and Health

Safety in Belterra

Belterra is a relatively safe town, but it’s important to take precautions to avoid theft or other crimes. Be aware of your surroundings and don’t leave valuables unattended. It’s also a good idea to avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Belterra

Belterra is located in a region known for mosquito-borne illnesses like malaria and dengue fever. Be sure to take appropriate measures to avoid bites, including wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent. It’s also a good idea to talk to your doctor about any necessary vaccinations before traveling to the region.

Getting to Belterra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belterra is located about 80 km (50 miles) from the city of Santarém, which is the closest major transportation hub. Here are some options for getting to Belterra:

Airports Near Belterra

  • Santarém-Maestro Wilson Fonseca International Airport (STM): This airport is located in Santarém and is served by several Brazilian airlines.

Bus Stations Near Belterra

  • Santarém Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Santarém and offers regular service to Belterra.

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi: You can hire a private car or taxi from Santarém to take you to Belterra.

How to Get Around in Belterra

Belterra is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. You can also rent a bike or take a taxi or bus to reach more distant attractions.

Where to Go Next from Belterra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belterra is located in a region with many other interesting destinations to explore. Here are some nearby attractions and excursions:

  • Santarém: This city is known as the “pearl of the Tapajós” and offers a lively cultural scene, natural beauty, and historic landmarks.
  • Alter do Chão: This small town is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear water, and laid-back atmosphere. It’s often referred to as the “Caribbean of the Amazon.”
  • Amazon River cruises: Many companies offer multi-day cruises along the Amazon River, which allow visitors to explore the remote communities and pristine wilderness of the region.

Conclusion

Belterra is a town that’s rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the ruins of Fordlândia, hiking in the Floresta Nacional do Tapajós, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local food and scenery, you’ll find something to love in Belterra. Plan your trip today and discover the hidden gems of the Brazilian Amazon.
