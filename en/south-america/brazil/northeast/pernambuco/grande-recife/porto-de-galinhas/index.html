Introduction to Porto de Galinhas

Porto de Galinhas is a charming beach town located in the Grande Recife region of Pernambuco state in Northeast Brazil. The name, which translates to “Port of Chickens,” is a reminder of its history as a slave trade port where the slaves were smuggled in hidden in boxes filled with chickens.

Today, Porto de Galinhas is a popular tourist destination famous for its crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and stunning natural scenery. The town boasts a rich cultural history and offers visitors a chance to explore and experience traditional Brazilian life.

List of Places to Visit in Porto de Galinhas

Here is a list of some of the top attractions in Porto de Galinhas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Project Hippocampus - a non-profit organization dedicated to the conservation of marine life, especially seahorses.
  • Pontal de Maracaipe - A quiet little village that centers around its mangroves. Visitors can take a boat tour or kayak through the mangroves, they can also see the seahorses and other marine life.
  • Fishermen’s Village - this is the historical center of Porto de Galinhas. Stroll around the charming streets with colorful houses or stop by the restaurants to taste some local dishes.
  • Cupe Beach - a paradise that makes a perfect day trip location.

Natural Attractions of Porto de Galinhas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muro Alto Beach - this popular beach is located just outside of Porto de Galinhas and is famous for its natural reef.
  • Natural pools of Porto de Galinhas - a must-visit for all tourists, with its crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life. One can swim and snorkel around the coral reefs.
  • Hippocampus Project - host educational visits to learn about marine conservation, visitors can also snorkel in the seahorse sanctuary to see the species in their natural environment.
  • Maracaipe Beach - famous for hosting surfing competitions. With a fun and lively atmosphere, Maracaipe Beach is perfect for those who enjoy water sports.
  • Cupe Beach - visitors with an adventurous streak can go on a buggy ride over the dunes of the beach. The ride will allow the passenger to explore the stunning coastal scenery, bug and explore the hill tops and valleys, and also visit the beautiful river and lagoon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Porto de Galinhas

Porto de Galinhas offers visitors multiple hiking and biking trails to explore the local landscape. Following are the most recommended routes:

  • Biking in Ipojuca - Rent or bring your bicycle, explore the tropical sceneries, pass the coconut tree orchards, and the local fishing village.
  • Maracaipe Beach - Travelers can visit the Maracaipe Beach with a bike or go on horseback, or even on camels.

Best Things to Do in Porto de Galinhas: Activities and Tours

Following are some of the best things to do in Porto de Galinhas:

  • Snorkeling and Diving - Enjoy the breathtaking marine life in the crystal-clear waters of Porto de Galinhas.
  • Surfing - With strong waves and ideal surfing conditions, try surfing lessons or join a competition in Maracaipe Beach.
  • Buggy tours - A fun way to see the natural wonders of Porto de Galinhas. Travelers can experience the dunes of Muro Alto and enjoy the beautiful coastal scenery.
  • Horseback riding and camel tours - A unique way to explore the natural landscapes of Maracaipe beach.
  • Day trips to nearby destinations - Book tours to Cabo de Santo Agostinho, Recife, Olinda, Caruaru, and other nearby destinations to get a taste of traditional Brazilian life.

Food and Drink Scene of Porto de Galinhas

The food in Porto de Galinhas is an eclectic mix of traditional Brazilian cuisine and innovative contemporary creations. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca - A traditional Brazilian fish stew cooked in coconut milk and herbs.
  • Feijoada - One of Brazil’s most popular dishes, made with beans, meat, and vegetables.
  • Tapioca - A breakfast food, similar to a crepe or pancake made from cassava flour, filled with shredded coconut or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - A Brazilian distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice, similar to rum.
  • Caipirinha - A cocktail made with cachaça, lime, sugar, and ice.
  • Coconut water - A refreshing drink obtained from young coconuts.

Where to Stay in Porto de Galinhas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Porto de Galinhas offers a wide range of accommodation options, suitable for different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the recommended options:

  • Kembali Hotel Porto de Galinhas - A luxury oceanfront resort that offers swimming pools, a spa, and a wide range of outdoor activities.
  • Pousada Tabajuba - A boutique hotel that offers sea view rooms, a pool, and a restaurant.
  • Hostel Porto de Galinhas - A budget-friendly hostel that offers private and shared rooms, a pool, and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Porto de Galinhas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Brazilian Bikinis - The beachwear culture is a big thing in Porto de Galinhas, visitors can try out different styles and designs that they can make purchases at any of the local shops.
  • Cangas - This tapestry made of cotton can be used at the beach or hung as decoration in homes.
  • Coconut products - visitors can take some of the tropical fruit home, in the form of oil, confections, or even bowls made out of coconut shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Porto de Galinhas

Porto de Galinhas celebrates Carnival with its blocos - small groups of musicians and dancers parading on the streets, following the rhythms of traditional Brazilian music.

The town also holds Feira Nordestina de Artesanato (Northeastern Crafts Fair) annually, where local artisans showcase their products such as jewelry, ceramics, and embroidery.

Best Time to Visit Porto de Galinhas

The best time to visit Porto de Galinhas is between September and March, when the weather is dry and mild temperatures prevail. Though for those travelling on a budget, tourists can travel from April to August to catch off-season rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Porto de Galinhas

Here are some quick do’s and don’ts for visitors to Porto de Galinhas:

Do:

  • Try different flavors of caipirinha.
  • Visit the local beaches early in the morning.
  • Go on a buggy tour to experience the dunes of Muro Alto.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches.
  • Swim in the natural pools during high tide.
  • Leave your valuables on the beach unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Porto de Galinhas

As you travel to Porto de Galinhas, always keep in mind the following:

  • Be aware of pickpockets and keep your valuable items safe.
  • Ensure that your property is secure and locked and do not give strangers keys to your accommodation.
  • Always leave your travel itinerary and passport details with someone at home.

Health in Porto de Galinhas

Tourists traveling to Porto de Galinhas should take precautionary measures for the following:

  • Always keep yourself hydrated, especially on hot and sunny days.
  • Apply sunscreen to avoid sunburn.
  • Visitors should take necessary vaccinations before travel such as mini-yellow fever and COVID-19 vaccination provided by the WHO.

How to Get Around in Porto de Galinhas

Tourists can get around in Porto de Galinhas in a few ways:

  • Rent a bike or scooter - an excellent way of exploring the town.
  • Take a taxi - it is a safer, quicker, and more convenient way of getting around.
  • Hop on a buggy - Tourists can join a guided buggy tour or request a chauffeur to take them from point A to B.

Where to Go Next from Porto de Galinhas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are various exciting day trips to nearby destinations that travelers can embark on:

  • Recife - the capital city of Pernambuco, home of the XII century Olinda city, and the popular museum of modern art.
  • Olinda - is a UNESCO-listed colonial town with interesting cultural events such as the Maracatu, a colorful and lively carnival.
  • Itamaracá Island - an unspoiled island of beauty with an idyllic beach and an environmental museum.
  • Lagoa do Carro and Carpina - for a day trip or an extended stay to explore the regions’ beautiful sites in history.
