South, located in Bahia, is a beautiful destination for travelers seeking cultural experiences and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in South Bahia, Brazil.

Introduction to South

South, also known as Costa do Descobrimento, is a region in Bahia that stretches along the coast of the Atlantic Ocean. It’s a lively location with a rich culture, history, and natural beauty. The region offers a combination of beautiful beaches, lush forests, and diverse wildlife, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all types.

List of Places to Visit in South

Here is the list of places you should consider visiting while in South:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museu Memorial do Descobrimento - a museum that showcases the history of Portugal’s arrival in Bahia and the indigenous people of the region.
  2. Casa da Lenha - a historical building that showcases the African and European influences that shape the region’s culture.
  3. Pataxo Indian Reserve - a cultural park that showcases the indigenous Pataxo culture.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Rio Caravelas - a beautiful river that snakes between the Atlantic Ocean and mangrove forests.
  2. Corumbau Beach - a breathtaking beach surrounded by coral reefs.
  3. Discovery Coast Atlantic Forest Reserve - a preserved area with a range of wildlife, including jaguars, pumas, and ocelots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Hiking and biking are excellent ways to explore South and its stunning landscapes. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking:

  1. Caminho do Descobrimento - a trail that follows the path of the Portuguese explorers.
  2. Parque Nacional do Monte Pascoal - a park with diverse flora and fauna, including wildlife like monkeys and birds.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities and tours in South that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are a few that we think you’ll enjoy:

  1. Beach hopping - South is known for its gorgeous beaches, and you can explore them all on a beach hopping tour.
  2. Whale watching - between July and November, you can witness the migration of humpback whales, an unforgettable experience.
  3. Historic city tour - visit the historic cities of Porto Seguro, Santa Cruz Cabralia, and Belmonte, and learn about their fascinating histories.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Day 1 - Arrival in Porto Seguro and relax on Taperapuan beach.

Day 2 - Take a historic city tour of Porto Seguro and Santa Cruz Cabralia.

Day 3 - Explore Pataxo Indian Reserve and its cultural park.

Day 4 - Visit the Corumbau beach and the surrounding coral reefs.

Day 5 - Take a whale watching tour in the morning and relax on a beach in the afternoon.

Day 6 - Hike along the Caminho do Descobrimento trail.

Day 7 - Visit the Museu Memorial do Descobrimento and the Casa da Lenha before departing from Porto Seguro.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South’s food scene is a reflection of the region’s cultural diversity. From seafood to traditional dishes, here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bobo de camarão - a traditional dish made with shrimp, coconut milk, and cassava.
  2. Acarajé - a popular street food made with black-eyed peas, onions, and shrimp.

Local Beverages

  1. Caipirinha - a refreshing cocktail made with cachaça, sugar, and lime.
  2. Cerveja Fritz - a local beer made in the town of Guriri.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South has a wide range of accommodation options to suit different budget levels. Here are some options that offer comfortable stays and excellent services:

  1. Villas de Trancoso - a luxury hotel set in a beautiful natural setting.
  2. La Cabana - a comfortable hostel located near the beach.
  3. Pousada Porto Escondido - a guesthouse that offers a relaxed environment and excellent service.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re traveling to South, you must grab these souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  1. Cachaça - a distilled spirit unique to Brazil.
  2. Locally made crafts - handcrafted items made with wood and woven fibers.

Local Culture and Customs of South

South’s culture is a reflection of its diverse history and traditions. The region is known for its lively music, dance, and vibrant festivals. Visitors should respect the local customs and dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is between July and November when the weather is pleasant, and the flora and fauna are at their best. Travelers should avoid visiting during the summer months when rainfall and high temperatures are prevalent.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Stay hydrated and wear light clothing during the hot summer months.
  3. Explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the local customs and culture.
  2. Worry about brushing up on your Portuguese - locals are friendly and willing to help even if they don’t speak English.
  3. Ignore the safety advice of locals and authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and stay safe while swimming in the ocean. It’s also advisable to avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.

Health in South

Travelers should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever, Zika virus, and malaria. Visitors should also carry sunscreen, hats, and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travelers can reach South by air or bus. The nearest airport is Porto Seguro Airport, which is well-connected to other Brazilian cities. Buses also connect South with other destinations in Bahia and Brazil.

Airports Near South

  1. Porto Seguro Airport (BPS) - the nearest airport to South.

Bus Stations Near South

  1. Rodoviaria de Porto Seguro - a bus station located in Porto Seguro.
  2. Rodoviaria de Eunapolis - a bus station located in Eunapolis.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and private transfers are available for exploring the region.

How to Get Around in South

Taxis are available for short trips, and rental cars are an excellent option for exploring South. Public transportation, such as buses, is also available but can be unreliable.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of destinations to explore beyond South. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  1. Trancoso - a beautiful coastal town.
  2. Arraial d’Ajuda - a charming historic town.
  3. Itacare - a popular beach destination for surfers.

Conclusion

South, Bahia, is a beautiful destination that offers a combination of natural beauty and cultural experiences. From historic cities to the stunning natural landscapes, there’s something for everyone in South. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty of South for yourself!
