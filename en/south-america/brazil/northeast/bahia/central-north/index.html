Introduction to Central North Brazil

Central North Brazil, located in the Bahia state in Northeast Brazil, is a region filled with natural beauty, fascinating history, and a rich culture. From majestic waterfalls to picturesque beaches and colonial towns, there are plenty of experiences to explore in this region of Brazil.

List of Places to Visit in Central North Brazil

Plan your trip to Central North Brazil with these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pelourinho and Historical Center in Salvador
  • San Francisco Church and Convent of Igarassu in Igarassu
  • Casa do Rio Vermelho Museum in Salvador

Natural Attractions of Central North Brazil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chapada Diamantina National Park
  • Morro de Sao Paulo Beach
  • Lençois and Fumaça Waterfalls

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central North Brazil

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will enjoy these popular routes:

  • Sendero das Bromélias in Chapada Diamantina National Park
  • Praia do Encanto in Morro de Sao Paulo
  • Trilha do Ouro in Praia do Forte

Best Things to Do in Central North Brazil: Activities and Tours

Make the most of your experience in Central North Brazil with these best things to do:

  • Visit Praia do Forte Turtle Sanctuary
  • Take a Capoeira class in Salvador
  • Explore the arts and crafts shops in Lençois

Local Culture and Customs of Central North Brazil

Central North Brazil is a cultural melting pot, with a mix of African, Indigenous, and European culture. Learn more about the culture and customs of this region during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Central North Brazil

The best time to visit Central North Brazil is between March and June, which is the dry season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central North Brazil

Do:

  • Respect cultural and religious traditions
  • Try local dishes and drinks
  • Learn to dance Capoeira

Don’t:

  • Wear expensive jewelry or carry large amounts of cash in public
  • Disrespect cultural and religious traditions
  • Buy souvenirs made from endangered species

Safety and Health

Safety in Central North Brazil

Central North Brazil is generally safe, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Health in Central North Brazil

It is recommended to get vaccinated for yellow fever before visiting Central North Brazil, as the region has reported cases in the past. It is also important to practice safe food and water hygiene to avoid illness.
