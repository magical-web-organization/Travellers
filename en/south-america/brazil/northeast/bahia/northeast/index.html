Introduction to Northeast

Northeast Brazil, located in the state of Bahia, is a culturally rich and diverse region known for its beautiful beaches, colonial architecture, and vibrant music and dance scenes. With a warm and sunny climate year-round, it’s the perfect destination for outdoor activities and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Northeast

Northeast Brazil is home to many stunning attractions that offer a glimpse into the region’s history and culture. Here are some must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pelourinho Historic Center: a UNESCO World Heritage site in the city of Salvador, with colorful colonial architecture and historic churches.

  • Outeiro da Gloria: a beautiful baroque-style church with stunning views of the All Saints Bay in Salvador.

  • Casa do Rio Vermelho: a museum dedicated to the life and work of Brazilian author Jorge Amado, located in Salvador.

Natural Attractions of Northeast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chapada Diamantina National Park: a stunning park with waterfalls, caves, and hiking trails, located in the state of Bahia.

  • Praia do Forte: a beautiful beach with clear waters, located in the city of Cabo Frio.

  • Fernando de Noronha: an archipelago with crystal-clear waters and diverse marine life, located off the coast of the state of Pernambuco.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast

Northeast Brazil offers many scenic hiking and biking routes that allow you to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Some popular routes include:

  • The Serra do Mar trail: a mountainous trail that takes you through the stunning landscape of Chapada Diamantina National Park.

  • The Paulo Souto bike path: a scenic route that takes you along the coast of the state of Bahia, featuring breathtaking views of the ocean.

  • The Ponta dos Mangues trail: a hiking route through the beautiful mangroves of the Piauí coast.

Best Things to Do in Northeast: Activities and Tours

With so much to see and do in Northeast Brazil, it can be hard to narrow down your options. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Capoeira classes: a martial art that is also a type of dance, capoeira is an essential aspect of Brazilian culture. Take a class and learn some moves while immersing yourself in local culture.

  • Beach hopping: with so many beautiful beaches to choose from, a beach-hopping tour is a great way to see them all and find your favorite.

  • Samba dancing: experience the vibrant music and dance scene of Brazil with a samba dance class or a night out at a samba club.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northeast

If you have a week to spend in Northeast Brazil, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Salvador and explore the Pelourinho Historic Center.

  • Day 2: Visit Outeiro da Gloria, explore the city’s beaches, and take a capoeira class.

  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Praia do Forte and enjoy some beach time.

  • Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of Chapada Diamantina National Park on a hiking tour.

  • Day 5: Experience the vibrant music and dance scene of Salvador, with dinner and a samba show.

  • Day 6: Relax on the beaches of Fernando de Noronha.

  • Day 7: Take a boat tour of the All Saints Bay and visit the Casa do Rio Vermelho museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast

Northeast Brazil has a rich culinary tradition that features fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and flavorful spices. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Acarajé: a deep-fried fritter made with black-eyed peas and stuffed with shrimp, onions, and peppers.

  • Caruru: a stew made with okra, shrimp, and spices.

  • Moqueca: a seafood stew made with coconut milk, peppers, and other seasonings.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail, made with cachaça (a sugarcane liquor), sugar, and lime juice.

  • Cajuína: a sweet, non-alcoholic beverage made from the cashew fruit.

  • Cachaça: a potent sugarcane liquor that is often used in cocktails and other drinks.

Where to Stay in Northeast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northeast Brazil offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and travel styles. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Fasano Salvador: a luxury hotel in the heart of Pelourinho, with stunning views of the city.

  • Hostel Che Lagarto: a budget-friendly hostel in Salvador, offering comfortable dormitory and private rooms.

  • Villa Santo Antonio: a charming guesthouse in Praia do Forte, with a swimming pool and gardens.

What to Buy in Northeast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring home from your trip to Northeast Brazil, consider the following:

  • Artisan crafts: the region is known for its beautiful handicrafts, such as pottery, textiles, and woodcarvings.

  • Cachaça: bring home a bottle of Brazil’s favorite liquor, made from sugarcane.

  • Local sweets: try some of the region’s delicious sweets and candies, such as cocada and bolo de rolo.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast

Northeast Brazil has a unique culture and customs that reflect the region’s African, European, and indigenous roots. Some cultural practices to be aware of include:

  • Capoeira: a martial art that is also a type of dance, capoeira is an essential aspect of Brazilian culture.

  • Carnaval: an annual festival that takes place in February or March, featuring music, dance, and elaborate costumes.

  • Candomblé: a religion that blends African and Catholic traditions, often featuring music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Northeast

With a warm and sunny climate year-round, there is no bad time to visit Northeast Brazil. However, the peak tourist season runs from December to February, when the weather is typically at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeast

When traveling to Northeast Brazil, it’s important to be aware of local customs and etiquette. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases before your trip.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Flash expensive jewelry or electronics.

  • Leave valuables unattended.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Northeast Brazil, like any travel destination, has its potential safety and health risks. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Northeast

  • Use caution when traveling alone or at night.

  • Avoid walking alone on deserted streets or beaches.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and use common sense.

Health in Northeast

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites to prevent illnesses such as Zika virus and dengue fever.

  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated in the hot sun.

Getting to Northeast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northeast Brazil is easily accessible by air or bus from major cities in Brazil and beyond. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Northeast

  • Salvador International Airport: the main airport serving Northeast Brazil, located in the city of Salvador.

  • Recife/Guararapes International Airport: located in the state of Pernambuco, offering domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Northeast

  • Salvador Bus Terminal: the main bus station in Salvador, offering connections to major cities in Brazil.

  • Recife Bus Terminal: located in the city of Recife, offering connections to other destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • By car: rent a car and drive to Northeast Brazil, taking advantage of scenic routes along the way.

  • By ferry: take a ferry from a nearby island or coastal city to reach Salvador.

How to Get Around in Northeast

Once you’ve arrived in Northeast Brazil, there are many transportation options to help you get around and explore the region. Consider the following:

  • Taxis and rideshare services: a convenient way to get around Salvador and other cities.

  • Buses: an affordable option for travel between cities and towns.

  • Walking and biking: a great way to explore the local neighborhoods and natural scenery.

Where to Go Next from Northeast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your travels beyond Northeast Brazil, consider the following nearby destinations:

  • Rio de Janeiro: the vibrant city of Rio is just a short flight away, with stunning beaches and iconic landmarks.

  • São Paulo: Brazil’s largest city offers world-class dining, shopping, and culture.

  • Buenos Aires: just a short flight from Brazil, Argentina’s capital city boasts a rich cultural scene and beautiful architecture.

Conclusion

Northeast Brazil is a destination unlike any other, offering a rich blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, or simply a relaxing beach vacation, Northeast Brazil has something for everyone.
