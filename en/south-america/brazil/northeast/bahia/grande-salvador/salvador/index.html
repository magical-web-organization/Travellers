Introduction to Salvador

Salvador, the capital of Bahia state, is one of Brazil’s most vibrant cities. Known for its rich history, eclectic culture, and stunning natural landscapes, Salvador offers a unique travel experience for all types of travelers. The city is divided into two parts: the Cidade Baixa (lower city) and the Cidade Alta (upper city), connected by the Elevador Lacerda, an iconic elevator that gives visitors stunning views of the city.

This comprehensive tour guide for Salvador will help you plan your trip to this beautiful city. Discover the top places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Salvador

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salvador is a city rich in history and culture, with many museums and historical places to visit. Some of the top cultural attractions to add to your itinerary include:

  • Pelourinho: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Pelourinho is a historic neighborhood of Salvador known for its colorful colonial architecture, music, and dance.
  • Museu Afro-Brasileiro: A museum dedicated to showcasing the African culture and heritage in Brazil through art and artifacts.
  • Museu de Arte da Bahia: A museum housing an impressive collection of Brazilian art from the 17th century to the present day.
  • Igreja e Convento de São Francisco: A Baroque-style church dating back to the 17th century, known for its ornate gold-leaf interior.
  • Elevador Lacerda: The iconic elevator connecting the Cidade Baixa and Cidade Alta, offering breathtaking views of the city.

Natural Attractions of Salvador: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salvador is also home to a variety of natural attractions, including stunning beaches, parks, and nature reserves. Here are some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore:

  • Praia do Farol da Barra: A popular beach with crystal-clear waters and a historic lighthouse.
  • Parque Nacional da Chapada Diamantina: A nature reserve with rugged mountains, crystal-clear rivers, and beautiful waterfalls.
  • Ilha dos Frades: A beautiful island located in the Bay of All Saints, known for its clear waters and white sandy beaches.
  • Morro de São Paulo: A picturesque island with idyllic beaches and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • Lagoa do Abaeté: A picturesque lake surrounded by sand dunes and palm trees, offering tranquil views and stunning sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salvador

If you’re feeling adventurous, head out to explore Salvador’s natural landscapes on foot or bike. Some of the top hiking and biking routes to try include:

  • Trilha do Morro de São Paulo: A scenic hike through the jungle to the top of Morro de São Paulo.
  • Caminho da Fé: A pilgrimage route through the countryside, passing by colonial churches, chapels, and small towns.
  • Praia do Forte Bike Tour: A bike tour through the beautiful beaches and nature reserves of Praia do Forte.

Best Things to Do in Salvador: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to enjoy in Salvador, catering to all types of travelers. Some of the best things to do in Salvador include:

  • Capoeira Show: Watch a live performance of capoeira, the Afro-Brazilian martial art form and dance.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Bahian cuisine with a local chef and enjoy a delicious meal.
  • Sunset Cruise: Enjoy stunning views of the city and the Bay of All Saints as you watch the sunset on a cruise.
  • Folklore Show: Experience the vibrant folklore and music of Salvador with a live show.
  • Street Art Tour: Explore the city’s colorful street art scene on a walking tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Salvador

No trip to Salvador would be complete without indulging in the city’s delicious food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca: A traditional seafood stew made with fish, shrimp, coconut milk, and dendê oil.
  • Acarajé: A famous street food made with black-eyed peas, shrimp, and spicy pepper sauce.
  • Feijoada: A hearty stew made with black beans and meat (usually pork or beef).
  • Vatapá: A spicy sauce made from bread, shrimp, and coconut milk, often served with rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail made with cachaça (sugarcane liquor), lime, and sugar.
  • Cerveja: Brazilian beer, often served ice-cold.
  • Cacau: Chocolate drink made with cocoa powder, cinnamon, and sugar.
  • Guaraná: A soft drink made from the guaraná fruit, unique to Brazil.

Hotels

Guesthouses

What to Buy in Salvador: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salvador is known for its handicrafts, music, and art, making it the perfect destination for souvenir shopping. Some of the top items to bring home include:

  • Carnaval costumes and masks: Salvador hosts one of the biggest Carnaval celebrations in Brazil, making it the perfect place to buy colorful costumes and masks.
  • Berimbaus and Capoeira accessories: A berimbau is a percussion instrument used in capoeira, the Afro-Brazilian martial art form and dance. You can also buy capoeira clothing and accessories in Salvador.
  • Art and handicrafts: Salvador is home to many talented artists and artisans, selling their work in markets and boutiques around the city.
  • Cachaça: Brazil’s national liquor, made from fermented sugarcane juice. You can find many different types of cachaça in Salvador, including some flavored with fruit or spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Salvador

Salvador has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions, festivals, and day-to-day life. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Salvador:

  • Religion: Salvador has a strong connection to the Catholic Church, with many churches, chapels, and religious festivals.
  • Music and dance: Music and dance are an integral part of Salvador’s culture, with many different styles and genres to experience.
  • Language: Brazilian Portuguese is the official language of Salvador, but many locals also speak English and other languages.
  • Tipping: It is customary to tip in restaurants and hotels in Salvador, usually around 10%.
  • Social etiquette: Salvadorians are generally friendly and hospitable, so it’s important to be respectful and follow local customs.

Best Time to Visit Salvador

The best time to visit Salvador depends on your preferences and interests. Here’s what you need to know about the city’s climate and seasons:

  • Seasons: Salvador has a tropical climate, with two seasons: wet (April to September) and dry (October to March).
  • Carnaval: Salvador hosts one of the biggest and most popular Carnaval celebrations in Brazil, which takes place in February or March.
  • High season: The high season in Salvador is from December to March, when the weather is dry and warm.
  • Low season: The low season is from April to November, when the weather is wet and cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salvador

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including acarajé and moqueca.
  • Do visit Pelourinho, one of the most iconic neighborhoods in Salvador.
  • Do participate in local festivals and traditions, such as Carnaval or São João.
  • Do keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or public transportation.
  • Do take a sunset cruise on the Bay of All Saints, one of the most beautiful sights in Salvador.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear expensive jewelry or carry large amounts of cash in public.
  • Don’t visit unsafe neighborhoods or areas at night.
  • Don’t drink tap water in Salvador, to avoid any stomach issues.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to apply sunscreen and insect repellent, as the sun and mosquitoes can be intense in Salvador.

Safety and Health

If you’re traveling to Salvador, it’s important to take some precautions to stay safe and healthy. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Salvador

  • Salvador is generally safe for tourists, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to avoid theft or scams.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in unsafe neighborhoods.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-share services and avoid taking public transportation at night.
  • Keep your belongings close and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Salvador

  • Salvador is a tropical city, so it’s important to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Dengue, Zika, and Chikungunya.
  • Drink bottled water and use sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.
  • Avoid eating street food from non-reputable vendors to avoid stomach issues.

Getting to Salvador: Transportation Options and Logistics

Salvador is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here’s what you need to know about getting to the city:

Airports Near Salvador

  • Deputado Luís Eduardo Magalhães International Airport: The main airport serving Salvador, located about 20 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Salvador

  • Terminal Rodoviário de Salvador: The main bus terminal in Salvador, located in the Iguatemi neighborhood.

Other Options

  • Car rental: You can rent a car at the airport or in the city to explore Salvador and the surrounding areas.
  • Taxi or ride-share: Taxi and ride-share services are widely available in Salvador, providing a convenient and safe option for transportation.

How to Get Around in Salvador

Salvador has a well-developed public transportation system, so getting around the city is easy and affordable. Here are some of the best ways to get around in Salvador:

  • Bus: The city has an extensive bus system, with regular routes and schedules.
  • Metro: The metro system connects the Cidade Baixa and Cidade Alta, offering a fast and convenient way to travel between the two parts of the city.
  • Taxi or ride-share: Taxis and ride-share services are affordable and widely available in Salvador.

Where to Go Next from Salvador: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salvador is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas and nearby destinations. Here are some of the top places to visit and excursions to consider:

  • Praia do Forte: A charming beach town located about 80 km from Salvador, known for its stunning beaches and nature reserves.
  • Chapada Diamantina: A beautiful mountain region located about 400 km from Salvador, offering hiking, waterfalls, and stunning landscapes.
  • Itaparica Island: A picturesque island located in the Bay of All Saints, with beautiful beaches and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Lençóis: A charming town located about 430 km from Salvador, serving as the gateway to the stunning Chapada Diamantina National Park.

Conclusion

Salvador is a city full of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Brazil.
