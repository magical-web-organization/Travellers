Introduction to Northern Maranhão

Located in the northwestern part of the state of Maranhão in Brazil, Northern Maranhão is a land of untouched beauty. This hidden gem offers visitors a unique experience with a blend of culture and nature. From the stunning landscape and outdoor activities to vibrant nightlife, there is something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Maranhão

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Casa das Tulhas Museum - This museum is a perfect place to start your exploration of Northern Maranhão’s history. The museum provides insight into the lifestyles of the native population.

  2. Museu Histórico de Alcântara - This museum focuses on the history of Alcântara, which was once an important center for the slave trade.

  3. Igreja de São João Batista - Built-in the 17th century, the Igreja de São João Batista is a stunning example of colonial architecture.

Natural Attractions of Northern Maranhão: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lençóis Maranhenses National Park - This national park is one of Brazil’s most spectacular natural wonders. The park is famous for its rolling sand dunes, which create hundreds of freshwater lagoons during the rainy season.

  2. Delta do Parnaíba - The delta is Brazil’s only delta that flows into the Atlantic Ocean. It is also home to several species of wildlife, including monkeys, crocodiles, and a variety of bird life.

  3. Chapada das Mesas - This region is home to several stunning natural swimming pools, waterfalls, and rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Maranhão

  1. Sete Cidades National Park - This national park offers visitors stunning trails that offer a unique perspective on the landscape of Northern Maranhão.

  2. Pedra Caída - This natural reserve is known for its waterfalls and incredible rock formations. There are also several hiking trails that provide an opportunity to explore the region.

Best Things to Do in Northern Maranhão: Activities and Tours

  1. Kite Surfing - The North Coast of Maranhão is considered one of the best destinations for kite surfing in Brazil. The strong winds and warm water, combined with stunning scenery, make it an ideal place to practice the sport.

  2. Boat Tour - A boat tour is a must-do activity to explore the Delta do Parnaíba. There are several tour operators available who offer guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Maranhão

Day 1 - Explore São Luis and visit Casa das Tulhas Museum

Day 2 - Head to Lençóis Maranhenses National Park and spend the day exploring the sand dunes and lagoons.

Day 3 - Take a boat tour of the Delta do Parnaíba.

Day 4 - Visit Alcântara and explore the Museu Histórico de Alcântara and Igreja de São João Batista.

Day 5 - Hike Pedra Caída and explore the rock formations and waterfalls.

Day 6 - Take a day trip to the stunning Chapada das Mesas.

Day 7 - Relax and enjoy the beaches of Northern Maranhão.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Maranhão

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Arroz de Cuxá - A traditional dish made with rice, nuts, shrimp, and a blend of spices.

  2. Caldo de Mocotó - A hearty soup made with beef and cow’s feet, vegetables, and spices.

  3. Peixada - A fish stew made with a variety of fresh fish, coconut milk, and spices

Local Beverages

  1. Cachaça - A popular Brazilian spirit made from sugar cane that is used to make the famous Caipirinha cocktail.

  2. Guaraná - A sweet carbonated drink made from the South American Guaraná fruit.

Where to Stay in Northern Maranhão: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Grand São Luis Hotel - A modern hotel located in the heart of São Luis, with comfortable rooms and excellent views.

  2. Pousada Boa Vista - A charming guesthouse located in Alcântara, which offers a relaxing atmosphere and friendly staff.

  3. Porto Preguiças Resort - A stunning resort located near Lençóis Maranhenses National Park, which offers a variety of luxurious amenities.

What to Buy in Northern Maranhão: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Artisanal crafts - The region is known for its beautiful embroidery, ceramics, and wood carvings.

  2. Cachaça - A bottle of cachaça makes a great souvenir or gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Maranhão

Northern Maranhão has a rich cultural heritage. The region is home to several ethnic groups, including indigenous people, descendants of African slaves, and colonial Portuguese.

Best Time to Visit Northern Maranhão

The best time to visit Northern Maranhão is between June and September, during the dry season. This is the best time to visit Lençóis Maranhenses National Park and the Delta do Parnaíba.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Maranhão

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions.
  2. Try local cuisine and drinks.
  3. Learn some basic Portuguese phrases.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the environment or wildlife.
  2. Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  3. Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Maranhão

Northern Maranhão is considered a safe region to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Northern Maranhão

Travelers should take precautions against insect bites, particularly during the rainy season when mosquitoes are more prevalent. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of sunburn and heat exhaustion.

Getting to Northern Maranhão: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Maranhão

The closest airport to Northern Maranhão is Marechal Cunha Machado International Airport, located in São Luis.

Bus Stations Near Northern Maranhão

There are several bus stations throughout Northern Maranhão, including in São Luis, Alcântara, and Barreirinhas.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Northern Maranhão by private car or taxi.

How to Get Around in Northern Maranhão

The best way to get around Northern Maranhão is by car or taxi. There are also buses available but they can be unreliable.

Where to Go Next from Northern Maranhão: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Parnaíba River Delta National Park - Located on the border of Piauí and Maranhão states, this national park offers visitors stunning wetlands and wildlife.

  2. Jericoacoara - Located just across the border in the state of Ceará, Jericoacoara is a picturesque fishing village known for its beaches and stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Northern Maranhão is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of culture and nature. With stunning landscapes, outdoor activities, and vibrant nightlife, there is something for everyone. Plan your trip to Northern Maranhão for an unforgettable experience of Brazil.
