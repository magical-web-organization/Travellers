Introduction to Caburé

Caburé is a small, idyllic fishing village located on a sandbar that separates the Preguiças River from the Atlantic Ocean. The sandbar is only accessible by boat, making it a perfect destination for those looking for a more secluded and intimate getaway. Caburé is renowned for its natural beauty, with white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters that are perfect for swimming and sunbathing. The village is also the gateway to the stunning Lençois Maranhenses National Park, a unique ecosystem of sand dunes and freshwater lagoons that is truly a wonder of nature.

List of Places to Visit in Caburé

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Church of São José dos Pericús - A small, white-washed church located in the heart of Caburé that is over 100 years old. The church is a testament to the village’s deep Catholic faith and is still in use today.

  2. Casa de Farinha - A traditional house where visitors can learn about the process of making manioc flour, a staple of the local cuisine. The house also features exhibits on the history and culture of the region.

  3. Casa de Renda - A small shop that sells beautiful lacework made by local artisans. Visitors can watch as the lace is made using traditional techniques and purchase souvenirs to take home.

Natural Attractions of Caburé: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lençóis Maranhenses National Park - A vast expanse of sand dunes and freshwater lagoons that is unlike anything else on earth. Visitors can hike across the dunes and swim in the cool, clear waters of the lagoons.

  2. Preguiças River - A slow-moving river that winds through the lush vegetation of Maranhão. Visitors can take a boat tour down the river, stopping at small towns along the way and marveling at the diverse wildlife.

  3. Atins Beach - A beautiful white sand beach that is perfect for swimming and sunbathing. From here you can see the impressive dunes of the Lençóis Maranhenses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caburé

  1. Trekking at Lençóis Maranhenses - There are several hiking trails through the dunes, ranging from a few hours to several days.

  2. Preguiças River Trail - A beautiful trail that follows the river from Caburé to the town of Atins.

Best Things to Do in Caburé: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat tour down the Preguiças River - A relaxing trip down the river is one of the best ways to experience the diverse ecosystems of Maranhão.

  2. Kitesurfing and windsurfing - Caburé’s location on the coast makes it an ideal spot for these thrilling water sports.

  3. Hiking across Lençóis Maranhenses - The unique landscape of the national park is perfect for adventurous hikers.

Food and Drink Scene of Caburé

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Moqueca de camarão - A flavorful shrimp stew made with coconut milk and palm oil, served with rice and manioc flour.

  2. Arroz de cuxá - A rice dish made with cuxá leaves, a type of local herb, and served with a variety of meats.

  3. Tapioca - A popular local snack made from manioc flour. Tapioca can be filled with a variety of fillings, such as cheese, coconut, and bananas.

Local Beverages

  1. Caipirinha - Brazil’s national cocktail, made with cachaça, sugar, and lime.

  2. Guarana soda - A refreshing soda made from the guarana fruit, which is native to Brazil.

Where to Stay in Caburé: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pousada do Barulho - A family-run guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

  2. Caburé Sol - A small, boutique hotel with spacious rooms and private balconies that overlook the ocean.

  3. Hostel Herivelton - A budget-friendly hostel that offers dorm-style rooms and a communal kitchen for guests to use.

What to Buy in Caburé: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Lacework - Caburé is famous for its beautiful lacework, which is made using traditional techniques by local artisans.

  2. Cachaça - A strong, distilled spirit made from sugarcane that is popular throughout Brazil.

Local Culture and Customs of Caburé

Caburé is a small, close-knit community that values family and tradition above all else. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and beliefs, such as attending mass on Sundays and refraining from loud and disruptive behavior. The people of Caburé are welcoming and friendly, and visitors should take the time to learn about the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Caburé

The best time to visit Caburé is from May to September, when the weather is dry and sunny. During this time, visitors can enjoy the beaches, the Preguiças River, and the national park without being interrupted by rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caburé

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bring plenty of sunscreen and bug repellent

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the national park
  • Disrespect local beliefs or traditions
  • Venture into the national park without a guide

Safety and Health

Safety in Caburé

Caburé is a safe and welcoming destination for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of pickpockets and take the usual precautions to protect their valuables. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the dangers of swimming in the ocean and the river, as currents can be strong.

Health in Caburé

Visitors to Caburé should take precautions to protect themselves from mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Insect repellent and long-sleeved shirts and pants can help prevent mosquito bites. Visitors should also take care to stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water, instead opting for bottled water.

Getting to Caburé: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caburé

The nearest airport to Caburé is the São Luís International Airport (SLZ), which is located about 260 km away. From here, visitors can take a bus or hire a private car to travel to Barreirinhas and then take a small boat to Caburé.

Bus Stations Near Caburé

The closest bus station to Caburé is located in Barreirinhas, which is about 45 minutes away. From here, visitors can take a small boat to Caburé.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars or take guided tours from São Luís or Barreirinhas to reach Caburé.

How to Get Around in Caburé

The village of Caburé is very small and can be easily explored on foot. However, visitors may also rent bicycles or hire local guides to take them on boat tours of the Preguiças River or to explore the national park.

Where to Go Next from Caburé: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Barreirinhas - A nearby town that is the gateway to Lencois Maranhenses National Park.

  2. Jericoacoara - A small town known for its stunning beaches and laid-back vibe, located about 8 hours by bus from Caburé.

Conclusion

A trip to Caburé is a unique and unforgettable experience that offers visitors a chance to connect with nature and immerse themselves in the culture and traditions of Maranhão. From the stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters to the sand dunes and freshwater lagoons of the national park, Caburé is a destination that will stay with visitors long after they leave.
