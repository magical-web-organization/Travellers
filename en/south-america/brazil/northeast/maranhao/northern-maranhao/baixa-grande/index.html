Introduction to Baixa Grande

Baixa Grande is a charming town located in the northern region of Maranhão, in the northeast of Brazil. It is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by travelers seeking off-the-beaten-path destinations. This town is surrounded by natural beauty and offers a glimpse into Brazil’s rich culture and history. In this guide, we will highlight the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Baixa Grande.

List of Places to Visit in Baixa Grande

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa de Cultura de Baixa Grande

This is the cultural hub of Baixa Grande, where visitors can learn about the town’s history, art, and culture. The Casa de Cultura de Baixa Grande hosts art exhibitions, cultural events, and workshops year-round.

Igreja de São João Batista

This is the oldest church in Baixa Grande and is a must-visit for history buffs. The beautiful architecture and design of the church are sure to impress visitors.

Natural Attractions of Baixa Grande: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parque Estadual de Mirador

This is a beautiful nature reserve with hiking trails, natural pools, and stunning waterfalls. Visitors can explore the park on foot, bike, or horseback.

Cachoeira do Macaco

This is a picturesque waterfall located near Baixa Grande. Visitors can swim in the natural pool at the base of the falls and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baixa Grande

Baixa Grande has a vast network of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular routes include the trails in Parque Estadual de Mirador and the Caminho das Águas trail.

Best Things to Do in Baixa Grande: Activities and Tours

Caminho das Águas

This is a guided hike that takes visitors through the waterfalls and swimming holes in the Parque Estadual de Mirador. It is a great way to experience the natural beauty of Baixa Grande.

Horseback Riding Tours

Visitors can take guided horseback riding tours to explore the countryside around Baixa Grande. This is a great way to experience the local culture and see the area’s beautiful natural landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baixa Grande

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Baixa Grande:

Day 1: Arrive in Baixa Grande and explore the town center.

Day 2: Visit Casa de Cultura de Baixa Grande and Igreja de São João Batista.

Day 3: Hike Caminho das Águas in Parque Estadual de Mirador.

Day 4: Take a horseback riding tour.

Day 5: Visit Cachoeira do Macaco and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 6: Rent bikes and explore the countryside around Baixa Grande.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the town before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Baixa Grande

Must-Try Dishes

Tapioca

This is a popular dish in Baixa Grande made from cassava starch and served with various fillings like cheese, coconut, and meat.

Feijoada

This is a Brazilian classic and is made from a stew of black beans, pork, and sausage.

Local Beverages

Cachaça

This is a distilled sugarcane liquor and is a popular drink across Brazil. Visitors can sample local varieties of cachaça at bars and restaurants in Baixa Grande.

Where to Stay in Baixa Grande: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Casa de Campo

This hotel is located near Parque Estadual de Mirador and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape. Rooms are comfortable and spacious, and guests can enjoy the swimming pool and restaurant on-site.

Pousada do Betão

This guesthouse is located in the town center of Baixa Grande, making it a convenient choice for travelers. The rooms are cozy, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

What to Buy in Baixa Grande: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Crafts

Baixa Grande is known for its local artisans and its handmade crafts. Visitors can purchase locally made souvenirs like hammocks, baskets, and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Baixa Grande

Baixa Grande is a friendly town where locals are proud of their culture and customs. Visitors should be aware of traditional customs like eating with your hands and removing your shoes before entering homes and sacred places.

Best Time to Visit Baixa Grande

The best time to visit Baixa Grande is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is mild and comfortable, and the waterfalls and natural pools are at their best during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baixa Grande

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Explore the natural wonders of the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in parks and natural areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Disrespect locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baixa Grande

Baixa Grande is a safe town, but visitors should take normal precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Baixa Grande

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Baixa Grande. It is also recommended to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Baixa Grande: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baixa Grande

The nearest major airport to Baixa Grande is São Luís International Airport, which is approximately 5 hours drive away.

Bus Stations Near Baixa Grande

Visitors can take a bus to Baixa Grande from São Luís, the nearest major city. The journey takes around 6 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or a driver to get to Baixa Grande from São Luís.

How to Get Around in Baixa Grande

The best way to get around Baixa Grande is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car in São Luís or rent a bike in town to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Baixa Grande: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barreirinhas

This is a popular tourist destination and is known for its stunning sand dunes and natural swimming pools. It is approximately 3 hours drive from Baixa Grande.

Lençóis Maranhenses National Park

This is a beautiful national park with sand dunes, lagoons, and unique landscapes. It is approximately 4 hours drive from Baixa Grande.

Conclusion

Baixa Grande is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its beautiful natural attractions, rich culture, delicious food, and friendly locals, it is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure in Brazil. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty of Baixa Grande.
