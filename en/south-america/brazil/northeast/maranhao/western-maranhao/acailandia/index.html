Introduction to Açailândia

Located in Western Maranhão, Açailândia is a vibrant city with a mix of modern and traditional elements. With an extensive transportation network connecting it to major cities in Brazil, Açailândia is a popular destination for tourists looking to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Maranhão.

In this travel guide, we will bring you a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, and local customs of Açailândia. You can use this guide to plan your itinerary and make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Açailândia

From culturally significant museums to breathtaking natural attractions, Açailândia has something to offer to everyone. Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Açailândia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Histórico e Cultural de Açaílandia (Açailândia Historical and Cultural Museum)
  • Catedral Nossa Senhora de Fátima (Our Lady of Fatima Cathedral)
  • Praça do Cinquentenário (50th Anniversary Square)
  • Capela São Francisco de Assis (St. Francis of Assisi Chapel)
  • Festa do Peão de Boiadeiro (Brazilian rodeo festival)

Natural Attractions of Açailândia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Estadual do Bacaba (Bacaba State Park)
  • Cachoeira do Romão (Romão Waterfall)
  • Lajes Canyon (Lajes Canyon)
  • Lago da Pedreira (Quarry Lake)
  • Sabiá Park (Sabiá Park)

Hiking/Biking Routes of Açailândia

Açailândia offers a range of hiking/biking options for the adventurous traveler. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes:

  • Parque Estadual do Bacaba (Bacaba State Park) hiking trail
  • Cachoeira do Romão (Romão Waterfall) hiking trail
  • Lajes Canyon (Lajes Canyon) hiking trail
  • Bike route from Açailândia to Porto Franco

Best Things to Do in Açailândia: Activities and Tours

Apart from the places listed above, Açailândia also offers several other activities and tours:

  • Brazilian rodeo festival (Festa do Peão de Boiadeiro)
  • Local craft market
  • Guided tours of the city
  • Nightlife at local bars and restaurants
  • Experience the local cuisine

Week-Long Itinerary for Açailândia

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Açailândia:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Açailândia and check-in at your hotel. Ease into the city with a guided tour of the city and a visit to Catedral Nossa Senhora de Fátima.
Day 2 Visit the Sabiá Park and Lago da Pedreira in the morning. In the afternoon, explore the local craft market.
Day 3 Take a bike ride to Porto Franco and explore the city.
Day 4 Enjoy a guided tour of the Museu Histórico e Cultural de Açaílandia and a visit to Capela São Francisco de Assis.
Day 5 Hike through Bacaba State Park and enjoy scenic views of the area.
Day 6 Visit the Cachoeira do Romão and enjoy a picnic lunch.
Day 7 Spend the day at the Brazilian rodeo festival (Festa do Peão de Boiadeiro).

Food and Drink Scene of Açailândia

No trip to Açailândia is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz com pequi (rice with pequi fruit)
  • Paçoca de carne seca (dried beef paçoca)
  • Tiquira (local liquor)

Local Beverages

  • Guaraná Jesus (a sweet, pink soda)
  • Cerveja Brasileira (Brazilian beer)

What to Buy in Açailândia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Apart from the local cuisine, Açailândia is known for its handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some popular items to bring back home:

  • Handmade hammocks
  • Wooden sculptures and crafts
  • Local ceramics
  • Handwoven baskets

Local Culture and Customs of Açailândia

Açailândia has a rich cultural heritage that is evident in its music, food, and art. Respect for local customs and traditions is appreciated, and travelers are encouraged to learn about the local culture and engage with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Açailândia

Açailândia can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is from June to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. The Brazilian rodeo festival (Festa do Peão de Boiadeiro) takes place in August, making it a great time to visit the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Açailândia

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their culture
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions
  • Hike or bike without proper equipment and guidance

Safety and Health

Safety in Açailândia

Açailândia is generally safe for tourists, but travelers should take usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Açailândia

Unlike some other parts of Brazil, Açailândia has low incidence rates of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria. Travelers should still take ample precautions and consider using insect repellant.

Getting to Açailândia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Açailândia is accessible from major cities in Brazil via air, bus, and train. Here are some options to get to Açailândia:

Airports Near Açailândia

  • Imperatriz-Prefeito Renato Moreira Airport (IPZ)
  • Belém-Val-de-Cans International Airport (BEL)

Bus Stations Near Açailândia

  • Açailândia Bus Station
  • Imperatriz Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train from Belém to Imperatriz and then take a bus to Açailândia
  • Private car or taxi

How to Get Around in Açailândia

Açailândia is a small city and can be explored by foot or bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire.

Conclusion

Açailândia is a city of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip and make the most of your time in Açailândia. So pack your bags and get ready to immerse yourself in the local culture and natural wonders of this beautiful Brazilian city.
