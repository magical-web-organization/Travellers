Introduction to Barra do Corda

Barra do Corda is a city located in the central region of Maranhão, Brazil. It is known for its beautiful landscapes that include waterfalls, rivers, and hills. The city is a popular destination for tourists who are looking for a unique travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Barra do Corda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa de Cultura Josué Montello: This is a cultural center that showcases the history and culture of Barra do Corda. It is a great place to learn about the local indigenous tribes, the customs and traditions of the region, and the history of the city.
  • Igreja do Rosário: This is a historical church that dates back to the 18th century. It is a great place to admire the beautiful architecture and learn about the religious history of the city.
  • Museu Histórico e Artístico do Maranhão: This museum is located in São Luis, the capital of Maranhão state, and features collections of art, artifacts, and historic documents that showcase the cultural heritage of the region.

Natural Attractions of Barra do Corda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cachoeira do Lajeado: This is a beautiful waterfall that is located on the outskirts of Barra do Corda. It is a great place to swim, hike, and take stunning pictures.
  • Parque Nacional da Chapada das Mesas: This national park is located about 150 km from Barra do Corda and features a wide range of natural landscapes, such as waterfalls, rivers, canyons, and hills. It is a great place for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching.
  • Rio Corda: This river runs through the city and offers several activities such as kayaking, fishing, and boat rides. Its clear waters and stunning landscapes are breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barra do Corda

  • Serra do Carneiro Trail: This trail is a popular hiking spot that offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding landscapes. It is perfect for adventurers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Rio Corda Trail: This trail follows the river and offers a scenic route for bikers and hikers. It is relatively easy, making it a great choice for families and beginners.

Best Things to Do in Barra do Corda: Activities and Tours

  • Rio Corda Boat Tour: Take an amazing boat ride exploring the beautiful river throughout the city and admire the beautiful landscapes.
  • Cultural and Historical City Tour: Learn about the history and culture of Barra do Corda by visiting the Casa de Cultura Josué Montello, Igreja do Rosário, and other historical sites.
  • Parque Nacional da Chapada das Mesas Tour: Take a day trip to explore the amazing landscapes and wildlife of this national park.

Food and Drink Scene of Barra do Corda

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz de cuxá: A traditional Maranhense dish made with rice, shrimp, and herbs.
  • Carne de Sol: A typical dish made with salt-cured beef that is grilled and served with rice and beans.
  • Torta de babaçu: A dessert made with babaçu, a type of palm tree, and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Guaraná Jesus: A soda made from guaraná fruit that is popular in Maranhão.
  • Cachaça: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane juice.

Where to Stay in Barra do Corda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada Beira Rio: A guesthouse located near the river, offering beautiful views and a tranquil atmosphere.
  • Hotel do João: A budget-friendly hotel in the city center with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Pousada do Lago: A cozy guesthouse located near the Cachoeira do Lajeado.

What to Buy in Barra do Corda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Barra do Corda is known for its beautiful handicrafts made by local artisans.
  • Cachaça: Take home a bottle of this local distilled alcoholic beverage as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Barra do Corda

Barra do Corda is known for its rich cultural heritage, which is strongly influenced by its indigenous and African roots. The city celebrates several festivals throughout the year, such as the Festa de São Sebastião and the Festa do Divino. It is also known for its traditional dances, such as the cacuriá and bumba-meu-boi.

Best Time to Visit Barra do Corda

The best time to visit Barra do Corda is between May and September, during the dry season when the weather is pleasant and there is less chance of rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barra do Corda

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the environment.
  • Don’t take pictures of local people without their permission.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barra do Corda

Barra do Corda is generally a safe city to visit, but it is recommended that travelers take basic precautions to avoid theft and other crimes. It is suggested to keep valuable items such as money, passport, and electronics in a safe place, and do not carry too much cash or jewelry on the streets.

Health in Barra do Corda

It is suggested to have a check-up and take general precautions such as staying hydrated and using mosquito repellent. It is also advised to get vaccinated against yellow fever if you are visiting Parque Nacional da Chapada das Mesas or other areas with a high risk of mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Barra do Corda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barra do Corda

The closest airport to Barra do Corda is São Luís International Airport (SLZ), which is located about 400 km away.

Bus Stations Near Barra do Corda

There are several bus stations that connect Barra do Corda to other cities in the region, such as Rodoviaria de Barra do Corda and Rodoviaria de Presidente Dutra. There are regular buses to São Luis that take around 7 hours.

Other Options

Some travelers opt for renting a car or hiring a private driver to get to Barra do Corda from São Luis.

How to Get Around in Barra do Corda

The best way to get around Barra do Corda is by taxi or renting a car. If you wish to explore the city’s natural beauty, a bike or motorcycle rental is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Barra do Corda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Parque Nacional da Chapada das Mesas: This national park offers several hiking trails, scenic views, and waterfalls that are worth exploring.
  • São Luis: The capital of Maranhão state, São Luis is known for its colorful buildings, charming streets, and rich cultural heritage. It is a perfect destination for history and culture lovers.
  • Teresina: Located in the neighboring state of Piauí, Teresina is known for its beautiful riverfront, parks, and museums.

Conclusion

Barra do Corda is a unique and beautiful destination that offers a perfect mix of culture, history, and nature. With its stunning landscapes, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, it is a must-visit destination for travelers who are looking for an authentic Brazilian experience.
