Introduction to Balsas

Balsas is a city in Maranhão, a state in Northeast Brazil. It is an agricultural and livestock center, surrounded by a beautiful countryside characterized by vast fields and hills. Despite its relative remoteness, it is an emerging travel destination, blessed with a rich history, charming natural beauty, cultural attractions, and friendly locals. The city is easily accessible by road and air, making it an accessible stopover for various trips through Maranhão.

List of Places to Visit in Balsas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Balsas History

Located in the heart of the city, this museum chronicles the history of Balsas from its beginnings to the present day. Here, you can see artifacts from colonial times, indigenous populations, and various agricultural and livestock techniques.

Church of São Francisco de Assis

This beautiful church is located in the center of Balsas and is considered one of the most charming and historic churches in Maranhão. It has a beautiful altar, carved from wood, and various images of saints.

Casa da Cultura

This building was once the headquarters of a rural social security service and has since been restored as a cultural center for Balsas. It hosts exhibitions, shows, and cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Balsas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parque Ambiental

This natural park is located on the outskirts of Balsas and is ideal for picnics, walks, and jogging. Its stunning views make it a perfect location for beautiful photos.

Serra da Mesa

This is a well-known destination in Balsas for hiking and trekking activities, as it offers spectacular views and beautiful nature trails.

Corredeiras do Rio Balsas

The rapids of Rio Balsas are perfect for rafting, kayaking, and canoeing and are an exciting adventure for all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balsas

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Balsas offers several well-marked trails, including:

Serra da Mesa Trail

This is a wonderful afternoon hike that takes you through beautiful nature and scenery. It is a relatively easy hike, suitable for hikers of all ages.

Senhor do Bonfim Trail

This scenic hike is located in the rural area of Balsas and offers stunning views of the countryside. It is an excellent trail for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Balsas: Activities and Tours

Fazenda Liberdade

This is a famous coffee farm that offers tours and tastings to visitors. The farm also produces beans, fruits, vegetables, and livestock and offers accommodation for guests.

Agropecuária Pujol

This farm offers tours and experiences of livestock breeding and production, including sheep, goats, and pigs.

Food and Drink Scene of Balsas

Must-Try Dishes

Balsas’ cuisine has a variety of flavors that showcase its agricultural and livestock culture. Popular dishes include:

Arroz Carreteiro

This popular rice dish is made with beef jerky, garlic, onions, and peppers.

Guisado de Galinha

This traditional dish is a hearty chicken stew seasoned with onions, tomatoes, and herbs.

Local Beverages

Cachaça

This traditional Brazilian beverage is distilled from fermented sugarcane juice and is a common local spirit.

Cafezinho

This is a small, black, and strong coffee that is embedded in Brazilian culture. It is usually served after meals.

Where to Stay in Balsas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Balsas offers a variety of accommodations, from luxury hotels to comfortable guesthouses. Some options include:

Balsas Palace Hotel

This elegant hotel is located in the city center, offering clean and spacious rooms, a restaurant, a bar, and a swimming pool.

Pousada Corredeiras do Rio Balsas

This charming guesthouse is located near the rapids of Rio Balsas and offers cozy rooms, a garden, and welcoming hosts.

What to Buy in Balsas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Indigenous Arts and Crafts

Balsas is home to several indigenous communities, and various shops and markets sell their diverse and unique candlesticks, baskets, jewelry, and textiles.

Coffee and Livestock Products

Various local farms and producers sell coffee, beef, goat, and pork products that make excellent souvenirs for food enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Balsas

Hospitality, family values, and a deep appreciation for agriculture and ranching characterize the culture of Balsas. The locals are friendly and helpful to tourists, and visitors are encouraged to learn and participate in traditional celebrations and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Balsas

The best time to visit Balsas is between June and October, during the dry season when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balsas

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Try local cuisine and coffee
  • Explore local markets and shops
  • Carry cash for small businesses
  • Experience local culture and festivals

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or accessories
  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Disrespect local laws and norms
  • Litter or damage natural resources

Safety and Health

Safety in Balsas

Balsas is relatively safe for tourists, but it is still recommended to be mindful of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Balsas

Before traveling to Balsas, it is recommended to have your routine vaccinations updated, including Hepatitis A and B, Malaria, and Yellow Fever. It is best to consult your doctor before departing.

Getting to Balsas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balsas

The nearest airport is Imperatriz Airport, located 222 km away from Balsas. From there, you can take a bus or hire a private car to Balsas.

Bus Stations Near Balsas

Balsas has a bus station with frequent connections to other cities and regions of Maranhão.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired to travel in and around Balsas.

How to Get Around in Balsas

Balsas is a relatively small city, and most of its main attractions are located within easy walking distance. Taxis and moto-taxis are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Balsas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chapada das Mesas

This beautiful national park is located 200 km away from Balsas, offering beautiful waterfalls, canyons, and rock formations, ideal for trekking, rafting, and swimming.

Tocantins State

Tocantins is the eastern neighbor of Maranhão, and it is known for its beautiful lakes, beaches, and water sports.

Conclusion

Balsas is a hidden gem in the heart of Northeast Brazil, characterized by its striking landscape, cultural richness, and friendly locals. It is an ideal destination for travelers looking for adventure, relaxation, and authentic local experiences. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your trip to Balsas and makes your visit a memorable one.
