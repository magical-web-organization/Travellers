Introduction to Ubajara

Located in the Ceará state of Northeast Brazil, Ubajara is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, scenic beauty, and adventure. The city is known for its breathtaking mountain views, lush green forests, cascading waterfalls, and intriguing history. Ubajara is also the gateway to the Ubajara National Park, which is the perfect destination for nature and wildlife enthusiasts. This travel guide provides a comprehensive overview of the best places to visit, things to do, and logistics to consider during your visit to Ubajara.

List of Places to Visit in Ubajara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ubajara Municipal Museum
  • Archaeological Site of São José de Belmonte

Natural Attractions of Ubajara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ubajara National Park
  • Ubajara Mountains
  • Poço da Pedra Branca (White Stone Pool)
  • Cascata do Boi Morto (Dead Bull Waterfall)

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ubajara

  • Trilha da Cachoeira do Boi Morto (Dead Bull Waterfall Trail)
  • Trilha do Morro do Chapéu (Hat Hill Trail)
  • Trilha do Pico da Ibiapaba (Ibiapaba Peak Trail)

Best Things to Do in Ubajara: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Ubajara National Park and take a guided tour to explore its amazing flora and fauna.
  • Climb the Ubajara Mountains and immerse yourself in breathtaking views of the city and natural surroundings.
  • Go on a hiking or biking adventure to discover the hidden waterfalls and scenic trails of Ubajara.
  • Learn about the city’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.
  • Savor the local cuisine and enjoy traditional drinks while immersing yourself in the city’s vibrant nightlife.
  • Shop for unique handicrafts and souvenirs to take back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Ubajara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Baião de Dois (Rice and beans mixed with cheese and bacon)
  • Peixe na Telha (Fish cooked on a roof tile)
  • Carneiro no Buraco (Lamb cooked in a hole in the ground)

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça (Sugarcane Spirit)
  • Caipirinha (Traditional Brazilian Cocktail)

Local Culture and Customs of Ubajara

Ubajara is known for its vibrant cultural traditions, including Forró dancing, Bumba Meu Boi festivals, and Capoeira martial arts. The city’s residents are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ubajara

The best time to visit Ubajara is from July to September, during the dry season when the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear. However, visitors can also enjoy the city’s beauty and adventures throughout the year, even during the rainy season (from January to May).

Do’s and Don’ts of Ubajara

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some Portuguese to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect historic and cultural sites.
  • Wear revealing or skimpy clothing in public.
  • Wander alone in deserted areas after dark.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ubajara

Overall, Ubajara is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions to avoid theft or scams. It is also important to wear sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring outdoors to avoid sunburn and mosquito bites.

Health in Ubajara

Health services and hospitals are available in the city, but visitors should be careful about consuming unfiltered water and eating uncooked food.

Getting to Ubajara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ubajara

  • Pinto Martins-Fortaleza International Airport (FOR), Fortaleza, Ceará (about 315 km from Ubajara)

Bus Stations Near Ubajara

  • Sobral Bus Station, Sobral, Ceará (about 30 km from Ubajara)
  • Tianguá Bus Station, Tianguá, Ceará (about 25 km from Ubajara)

Other Options

  • Private car/taxi
  • Tour buses

How to Get Around in Ubajara

The city is small and easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also use local buses or hire taxis for transportation.
