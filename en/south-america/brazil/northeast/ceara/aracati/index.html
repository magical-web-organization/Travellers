Introduction to Aracati

Aracati is a beautiful city located in the state of Ceará, in Northeast Brazil. Known for its stunning beaches, rich history, delicious cuisine, and warm people, Aracati is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic Brazilian experience. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, food, and drink scene, where to stay and what to buy, safety, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Aracati

Aracati has a rich cultural heritage, with many museums and historical places to visit. But it’s also surrounded by natural beauty, with stunning beaches and lush forests. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Solar dos Nunes: This historic mansion was built in the 18th century and is now a museum. Visitors can learn about the history of the region and see art exhibitions.
  • Aracati City Hall: Built in the early 20th century, the city hall is a beautiful example of colonial architecture.
  • The Church of Saint Peter and Saint Paul: This 18th-century church is a landmark of the city and has beautiful baroque-style decorations.

Natural Attractions of Aracati: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canoa Quebrada: This iconic beach is known for its stunning cliffs and beautiful sunsets. Visitors can go on a buggy tour, parasail, or simply relax on the beach.
  • Parajuru: This fishing village is located 25 km from Aracati and is known for its natural beauty and tranquil beaches.
  • Serra de Aratanha: This is a mountain range located 20 km from the city. It’s ideal for hiking and has many beautiful view points.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aracati

  • Rota Assombrada: This is a 10 km hiking trail located in the Serra de Aratanha. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Route of the Old Railway: This is a 9 km bike trail that follows the path of an old railway. It offers views of the countryside and is a great way to explore the region.

Best Things to Do in Aracati: Activities and Tours

  • Sunset on Canoa Quebrada Beach: This is a must-do activity. Watching the sun set over the cliffs is a magical experience.
  • Buggy Tour of Canoa Quebrada: This tour takes visitors on a thrilling ride along the coast, with stops at various points of interest.
  • Kitesurfing: With strong winds and calm waters, Aracati is an ideal destination for kitesurfing.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can explore the countryside on horseback, with tours available to suit all levels of experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aracati

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors who want to get the most out of their trip to Aracati:

  • Day 1: Explore Aracati’s historic center and visit Solar dos Nunes.
  • Day 2: Visit the Church of Saint Peter and Saint Paul and take a bike ride along the Old Railway route.
  • Day 3: Go on a hiking tour of Serra de Aratanha.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the fishing village of Parajuru.
  • Day 5: Spend the day on Canoa Quebrada Beach and go on a buggy tour.
  • Day 6: Go kitesurfing or horseback riding.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Aracati

Aracati is known for its delicious food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peixada Cearense: A seafood stew made with fish, shrimp, crab, and coconut milk. Served with rice and farofa (toasted manioc flour).
  • Carne de Sol: A type of salt-cured meat that is grilled and served with roasted cassava and salad.
  • Baião de Dois: A traditional rice and bean dish made with bacon, sausage, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: The national drink of Brazil, made from fermented sugarcane juice.
  • Caipirinha: A cocktail made with cachaça, lime, sugar, and ice.
  • Guaraná: A popular soft drink made from the guaraná fruit.

Where to Stay in Aracati: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aracati has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended options:

  • Vila Selvagem Hotel: A luxury hotel located on Praia do Pontal de Maceió. It features a spa, infinity pool, and beachfront restaurant.
  • Pousada Janelas do Mar: A cozy guesthouse with a rooftop terrace and great views of the sea.
  • Hostel Dragão do Mar: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the historic center.

What to Buy in Aracati: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aracati is famous for its handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some recommended items to buy:

  • Lace and Embroidery: Aracati is known for its high-quality lace and embroidery, which is created by local artisans.
  • Hand-painted Ceramics: The region is also known for its beautiful hand-painted ceramics, which make great souvenirs.
  • Cachaça: Visitors can bring home a bottle of locally produced cachaça as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Aracati

Aracati has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from indigenous, African, and European traditions. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as dress codes for visiting churches and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Aracati

The best time to visit Aracati is between July and December when the weather is dry and sunny, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aracati

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases before arriving.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Flash cash or valuables in public.
  • Drink tap water.
  • Swim in unguarded areas of the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aracati

Aracati is generally a safe destination but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Aracati

Visitors should drink bottled water and watch out for mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Vaccinations for yellow fever and hepatitis A and B are recommended.

Getting to Aracati: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aracati is located 150 km from Fortaleza, the capital of Ceará state. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Aracati

  • Fortaleza International Airport: The closest airport, located 170 km from Aracati.

Bus Stations Near Aracati

  • Fortaleza Bus Station: Serviced by several bus companies with regular departures to Aracati.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from Fortaleza and drive to Aracati.

How to Get Around in Aracati

Visitors can use taxis, buses, or rental cars to get around Aracati and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Aracati: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions visitors can consider:

  • Jericoacoara: A beautiful beach town located 290 km from Aracati.
  • Fortaleza: The capital of Ceará state and a big city with lots of things to do and see.

Conclusion

Aracati is a beautiful and unique destination in Brazil, with stunning beaches, rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and warm people. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their trip and experience all that Aracati has to offer.
