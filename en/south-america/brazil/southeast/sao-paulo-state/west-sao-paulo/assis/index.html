Introduction to Assis

Assis is a quaint city located in the west of São Paulo State in Brazil. The city is known for its rich cultural history and beautiful natural scenery. From historical landmarks to picturesque natural attractions, Assis has a lot to offer to all kinds of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Assis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Assis Museum of Art (AMA)
  • Assis Railroad Museum
  • House of Culture and History

Natural Attractions of Assis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horto Canon of Assis
  • Ecological Park
  • Morro Sao Francisco

Hiking/Biking Routes of Assis

  • Horto Canon Route
  • Ecological Park Route
  • Morro Sao Francisco Route

Best Things to Do in Assis: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Assis Museum of Art and explore the works of renowned regional artists.
  • Take a tour of the Assis Railroad Museum and learn about the history of railways in Brazil.
  • Go for a hike or bike ride in the beautiful natural settings of Horto Canon, Ecological Park, and Morro Sao Francisco.
  • Visit House of Culture and History for a glimpse into Assis’ rich cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Assis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pão de Queijo - Brazilian cheese bread
  • Pastel - Brazilian fried pastry with savory or sweet fillings
  • Brigadeiro - traditional Brazilian chocolate dessert

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - Brazilian sugarcane liquor
  • Caipirinha - Brazilian cocktail made with cachaça, lime, and sugar

Local Culture and Customs of Assis

Assis is a lively and welcoming city. Brazilians are known for their joie de vivre, and this is reflected in the local culture. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and to show respect for local customs.

Best Time to Visit Assis

The best time to visit Assis is between May and September. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and there is an opportunity to explore the beautiful natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Assis

Do:

  • Engage with locals and learn about the cultural heritage of Assis.
  • Dress modestly and show respect for local customs.
  • Try local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards the local culture and customs.
  • Use drugs or consume excessive alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Assis

Assis is a relatively safe city. However, visitors are advised to take common precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Assis

There are medical facilities available in Assis, including hospitals and clinics. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions and seek medical help in case of emergencies.

Getting to Assis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Assis

The nearest airport to Assis is Presidente Prudente Airport located 100kms away. Other airports near Assis are Bauru-Arealva Airport and Marília Airport.

Bus Stations Near Assis

Assis Bus Station is the main bus station that connects the city to other parts of Brazil. Regular buses operate from here to major cities, including São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Assis from nearby cities, although it is not always recommended due to road safety concerns.

How to Get Around in Assis

The best way to get around Assis is by car or taxi. Visitors can also use public transport options, such as buses and trains.

Conclusion

Assis is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural settings. From museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and picturesque landscapes, Assis has something to offer for all kinds of travelers. Whether you want to explore the city’s rich culture or enjoy its scenic beauty, Assis is a great destination for a memorable trip.
