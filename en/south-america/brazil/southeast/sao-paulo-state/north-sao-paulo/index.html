Introduction to North São Paulo

Located in the southeastern region of Brazil, North São Paulo is an off-beat travel destination that offers a perfect mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. Divided into several cities, including Sao José dos Campos, Mogi das Cruzes, and Taubate, North São Paulo is an ideal place to spend a long weekend or a week exploring the region’s many attractions.

List of Places to Visit in North São Paulo

Here is a list of must-visit places in North São Paulo for experiencing its cultural and natural beauty:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu do Cafe in Taubate: A museum that showcases the history of coffee production in Brazil.
  • Museu do Folclore in Sao José dos Campos: A museum that exhibits regional folk culture, including handicrafts, traditional costumes, musical instruments, and regional festivals.
  • Solar do Visconde de Guarjá in Mogi das Cruzes: A historic 17th-century mansion that serves as a cultural center and museum.

Natural Attractions of North São Paulo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar: The park covers over 315,000 hectares of Atlantic Forest and offers diverse flora and fauna, including waterfalls, hiking trails, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Pedra do Bau in Sao Bento do Sapucai: A massive granite rock formation that offers stunning panoramic views of the valley below.
  • Serra do Itapeva in Pindamonhangaba: A scenic mountain range known for its winding drives, hiking trails, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North São Paulo

North São Paulo is an ideal destination for adventure seekers. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in North São Paulo are:

  • Pedra do Bau Trail in Sao Bento do Sapucai
  • Serra da Mantiqueira Trail in Campos do Jordao
  • Pico do Itapeva Trail in Pindamonhangaba

Best Things to Do in North São Paulo: Activities and Tours

North São Paulo is a destination that offers a wide range of activities and tours. Some of them include:

  • Brazilian coffee plantations tours in Taubate and Mogi das Cruzes
  • Visiting the cultural center in Sao José dos Campos
  • Balloon rides over the Serra do Mar Park in Caraguatatuba

Food and Drink Scene of North São Paulo

Must-Try Dishes

North São Paulo has a rich culinary culture. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Feijão tropeiro: A traditional dish made with beans, bacon, meat, eggs, and cassava flour.
  • Pão-de-queijo: A cheese-bread that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
  • Brigadeiro: A popular Brazilian sweet made with condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.

Local Beverages

Coffee is the most popular beverage in North São Paulo, known for its quality Arabica coffee. The region also produces several locally made wines and cachaça, a distilled spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice.

Where to Stay in North São Paulo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North São Paulo offers various options for budget and luxury accommodation.

  • Pousada Morada do Luar in Caraguatatuba: A beautiful guesthouse with ocean views and a garden.
  • Hotel Fazenda Vale do Paraíba in Sao José dos Campos: A luxury hotel with a pool and horseback riding facilities.
  • Hostel 71 in Mogi das Cruzes: A comfortable and budget-friendly hostel with a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in North São Paulo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North São Paulo is a great place to purchase locally produced items. Some popular souvenirs include:

  • Handmade goods made from local artisans such as embroidery, lace, pottery, and baskets.
  • Brazilian coffee, produced in the region and sold in specialty coffee shops.
  • Traditional Brazilian sweets, chocolates, and other delicacies available in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of North São Paulo

North São Paulo is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions influenced by Portuguese, Indigenous, and African cultures. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should learn basic Portuguese phrases to communicate more effectively.

Best Time to Visit North São Paulo

The best time to visit North São Paulo is during the dry season, from May to September, when the weather is cooler and drier. The peak season is from December to February, so it is advised to book accommodation and activities in advance during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of North São Paulo

Do:

  • Explore the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Try the local cuisine and coffee.
  • Learn basic Portuguese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the ocean outside designated areas due to strong currents and open ocean.
  • Leave your belongings unattended in crowded areas.
  • Disrespect local customs, traditions, and beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in North São Paulo

North São Paulo is generally considered a safe destination for tourists, but travelers should take precautions in crowded areas, public transportation, and beaches. Pickpocketing and petty theft can occur, so be aware of your belongings at all times and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in North São Paulo

North São Paulo is not a high-risk area for infectious diseases, but it is always advisable to visit your healthcare provider and get vaccines up-to-date before traveling. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus can occur, so it is advisable to take precautions and use mosquito repellent regularly.

Getting to North São Paulo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North São Paulo

São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport is the nearest international airport, located approximately 80 km from North São Paulo. The region also has several domestic airports, including Sao Jose dos Campos Airport and Campos do Jordao Airport.

Bus Stations Near North São Paulo

North São Paulo is well-connected by bus, with regular services from Sao Paulo City and other nearby destinations. The main bus stations are located in Sao José dos Campos and Taubate.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are also available, but they can get expensive compared to other modes of transport in the region.

How to Get Around in North São Paulo

To get around North São Paulo, travelers can use buses, taxis, or rent a car. However, navigating around the region can be challenging, so it is advisable to use a GPS and plan ahead for travel logistics.

Where to Go Next from North São Paulo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore from North São Paulo:

  • Ilhabela: A beautiful island known for its beaches and water sports activities.
  • Paraty: A colonial town with cobbled streets, island beaches, and azure waters.
  • Rio de Janeiro: Brazil’s most iconic city known for its beaches, carnival, and nightlife.

Conclusion
