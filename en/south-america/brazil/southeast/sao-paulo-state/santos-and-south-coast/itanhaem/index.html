Introduction to Itanhaém

Itanhaém is a charming coastal town located in the state of São Paulo, Brazil. With its pristine beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture, Itanhaém is a favored destination among locals and tourists alike. The town’s name, in the Tupi language, means “rock that sings.”

List of Places to Visit in Itanhaém

Here are some of the top-rated attractions in Itanhaém that are definitely worth a visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Convento Nossa Senhora da Conceição - This beautiful 17th-century convent houses an impressive collection of religious art and artifacts.
  • Casa do Olhar - An art gallery and cultural center located in the heart of Itanhaém, showcasing the works of local artists.
  • Casa da Praia - A historical house museum that depicts life in Itanhaém during the colonial era.
  • Igreja Matriz Santos Cosme e Damião - A 16th-century church with a stunning Baroque interior.

Natural Attractions of Itanhaém: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia dos Pescadores - This is the most popular beach in Itanhaém, with various restaurants, kiosks, and water sports facilities.
  • Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar - A protected forest reserve that offers spectacular hikes and trails, surrounded by lush vegetation and waterfalls.
  • Cama de Anchieta - A strip of sandbanks that connect the mainland to a small island. Anchieta used this bed as a shelter during his missionary work in Brazil.
  • Morro do Sapucaitava - A hill with a panoramic view of the town and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Itanhaém

  • Trilha dos Orixás - A 10km trail through the Atlantic Rainforest that leads to the top of a hill, where hikers can admire panoramic views of the ocean and the surrounding vegetation.
  • Trilha do Rio Branco - A 5km trail along the Rio Branco stream, surrounded by the lush of the Atlantic Forest.
  • Rota das Capelas - A 9km route that connects several historical chapels and churches in Itanhaém, showcasing the town’s colonial architecture.

Best Things to Do in Itanhaém: Activities and Tours

  • Stand-up paddleboarding/kayaking - Explore the crystal-clear waters of Itanhaém from a different perspective.
  • Fishing - A popular activity among locals, visitors can arrange a fishing trip with local fishermen.
  • Buggy Tours - A fun and unique way to explore Itanhaém and visit its major attractions.
  • Horseback Riding - Take a romantic ride along the beach and enjoy the stunning views of the coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Itanhaém

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Itanhaém:

Day 1: Visit the historic center of Itanhaém, including the Casa da Praia and the Igreja Matriz. Day 2: Spend the day at Praia dos Pescadores, the most famous beach in Itanhaém. Day 3: Visit the Convento Nossa Senhora da Conceição and go shopping for local souvenirs in the adjacent patio. Day 4: Take a hike on the Trilha dos Orixás trail or explore the Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar. Day 5: Go on a buggy tour and visit the Morro do Sapucaitava. Day 6: Take stand-up paddleboarding/kayaking lessons or go horseback riding along the coastline. Day 7: Relax on the beach and enjoy a seafood barbecue.

Food and Drink Scene of Itanhaém

Itanhaém is known for its seafood, and some of the best places to try local cuisine are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca - A traditional Brazilian seafood stew consisting of fish, onions, tomatoes, peppers, and coconut milk.
  • Caldeirada - A Fish Stew with various types of seafood marinated in lemon and served with rice and beans.
  • Pastel de Camarão - A savory pastry filled with shrimp and other seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - A popular Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane.
  • Caipirinha - A popular Brazilian cocktail made with cachaça, lime juice, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Itanhaém: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Itanhaém has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets. Some of the highly-rated hotels in Itanhaém are:

  • Pousada Solar da Praia - Located right next to the beach, this guesthouse has rooms with ocean views.
  • Ibis Styles Itanhaém Paranapuan - A budget-friendly hotel with modern amenities and a swimming pool.
  • Grand Hotel Itanhaém - A luxury hotel with a spa, outdoor pool, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Itanhaém: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Calunga dolls - These handmade black dolls are a symbol of Afro-Brazilian culture and have become a popular souvenir among tourists.
  • Cachaça - Take a bottle of this delicious Brazilian spirit home as a souvenir or gift for friends and family.
  • Jewelry and crafts - There are numerous shops in Itanhaém selling beautiful handmade crafts and unique jewelry made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Itanhaém

Itanhaém has a rich culture influenced by its indigenous, African, and European roots. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should respect the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Itanhaém

The best time to visit Itanhaém is during the summer months from December to March when the temperatures are warm, and the beaches are packed with tourists. Visitors can also enjoy local festivals and events during this time, such as the Itanhaém Carnival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Itanhaém

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the seafood dishes.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing during the day as temperatures can be high.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the streets.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Itanhaém

Itanhaém is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should keep an eye on their belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Itanhaém

Visitors should ensure they are up-to-date with their routine vaccinations and take necessary precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and zika virus.

Getting to Itanhaém: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Itanhaém

The closest airport to Itanhaém is São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport, which is approximately 110 km away. Visitors can also fly to Santos Airport or Congonhas Airport in São Paulo and take a bus or taxi to Itanhaém.

Bus Stations Near Itanhaém

Itanhaém has a bus terminal that connects the town to other cities in the region, including Santos and São Paulo.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or arrange for a private transfer to Itanhaém from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Itanhaém

Itanhaém is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also use taxis or ride-sharing services to get around.

Where to Go Next from Itanhaém: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore:

  • Santos – A historic port city with a rich cultural heritage, delicious food, and beautiful beaches.
  • Praia Grande – A seaside town known for its large beaches and water parks.
  • Ubatuba - A coastal paradise with over 100 beaches, waterfalls, and hidden coves.

Conclusion

Itanhaém is a beautiful coastal town in Brazil that offers something for every type of traveler. With its stunning beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture, Itanhaém is a destination that should be on every traveller’s bucket list. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply relaxation, Itanhaém has it all. So what are you waiting for?
