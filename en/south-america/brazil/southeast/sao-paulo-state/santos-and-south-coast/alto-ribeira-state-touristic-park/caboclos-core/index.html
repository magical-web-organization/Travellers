Introduction to Caboclos Core

Caboclos Core is a beautiful travel destination located in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park, in Santos and South Coast, in São Paulo, Southeast, Brazil. It is a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The name “Caboclos” comes from the indigenous population that has lived in this region for centuries. Caboclos Core is famous for its lush green forests, pristine waterfalls, spectacular hiking and biking routes, and rich history.

Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, an adventure seeker, or someone looking for a relaxing holiday, Caboclos Core has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and all the information you need to plan your trip to Caboclos Core.

List of Places to Visit in Caboclos Core

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Caboclos Core is steeped in history and has several museums and historical places worth visiting. Museu do Índio, or the Indian Museum, showcases the history and culture of the indigenous population of the region. The museum has an impressive collection of artifacts, including clothing, weapons, and pottery, that offer an insight into the lifestyle and traditions of the natives.

Another attraction that you cannot miss is the Ruins of Sao Miguel. These ruins date back to the 18th century and were once a Jesuit church. The ruins have a haunting beauty that transports you back in time.

Natural Attractions of Caboclos Core: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Caboclos Core is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, including waterfalls, rivers, forests, and mountains. A visit to the Cachoeira do Contraste (Contraste Waterfall) is a must. It is one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the region and is surrounded by lush vegetation. The waterfall is also perfect for swimming, so make sure to bring your swimwear.

Another place that you should add to your list is the Serra da Bocaina National Park. The park is home to a vast expanse of forests, rivers, and mountains, and is a paradise for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts. You can also visit the Parque das Neblinas (Mist Park), which is a conservation area that has several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caboclos Core

Caboclos Core has several hiking and biking routes that cater to every skill level. The Trilha do Ouro (Gold Trail) is a popular hiking trail that follows the path that was once used by the gold miners in the 19th century. The trail is about 10 kilometers long and takes you through dense forests, mountains, and rivers.

For the biking enthusiasts, the Circuito das Águas (Water Circuit) is a must-try. This route takes you through several waterfalls and rivers and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The route is about 30 kilometers long and is suitable for intermediate-level bikers.

Best Things to Do in Caboclos Core: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of Caboclos Core, there are several other activities and tours that you can enjoy. One of the most popular tours is the Canyoning Tour, which takes you through the canyons and waterfalls of Caboclos Core. It is an adventurous and adrenaline-pumping activity that is perfect for thrill-seekers.

You can also take a boat tour along the Juquiá River, which offers a unique perspective of the region’s natural beauty. During the tour, you’ll get to see several waterfalls and rapids and enjoy a relaxing time on the boat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caboclos Core

Here’s a week-long itinerary that covers most of the must-see attractions of Caboclos Core:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museu do Índio and the Ruins of Sao Miguel
  • Day 2: Hike the Trilha do Ouro
  • Day 3: Explore the Cachoeira do Contraste and take a swim
  • Day 4: Enjoy the Canyoning Tour
  • Day 5: Bike the Circuito das Águas
  • Day 6: Take a boat tour along the Juquiá River
  • Day 7: Relax and explore the local culture and customs of Caboclos Core

Food and Drink Scene of Caboclos Core

Must-Try Dishes

Caboclos Core has a diverse cuisine that reflects its cultural heritage. One of the must-try dishes is the Peixe na Telha (Fish in Tile) - a traditional dish made with freshwater fish cooked on a ceramic tile. Another dish that you should not miss is the Virado à Paulista - a hearty dish made with rice, beans, meat, and vegetables, typical of the region.

Local Beverages

Caboclos Core has several local beverages that you can enjoy during your stay. The most famous drink is Cachaça, which is made from fermented sugarcane juice and has a high alcohol content. You can also try the regional beer, which is known for its refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Caboclos Core: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Caboclos Core has several accommodation options that cater to every budget and preference. If you’re looking for a luxurious stay, you can check out the Rancho Silvestre, which offers an eco-lodge experience amidst nature. For those on a budget, there are several hostels and guesthouses that offer affordable yet comfortable stays, such as the Hostel Pedra do Lagarto and the Pousada do Chalé.

What to Buy in Caboclos Core: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Caboclos Core has several local products and souvenirs that you can buy to take back home. One of the most popular souvenirs is the indigenous handicrafts, such as pottery, baskets, and paintings. You can also buy local products such as Cachaça, coffee, and cheese from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Caboclos Core

Caboclos Core has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its music, dance, and lifestyle. The region is known for its vibrant festivities, such as the Festa do Divino (Festival of the Divine), which celebrates the region’s religious roots. The locals are warm and friendly and are always eager to share their customs and traditions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Caboclos Core

The best time to visit Caboclos Core is from June to September when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. The region is also less crowded during this time, so you can enjoy the attractions without the hustle and bustle of tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caboclos Core

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that harm the environment.
  • Swim in prohibited areas.
  • Leave your valuable items unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caboclos Core

Caboclos Core is generally a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it is advisable to take precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash and avoiding remote areas at night. Also, beware of snakes while hiking or walking in the wilderness.

Health in Caboclos Core

Caboclos Core does not have any major health risks, but it is advisable to carry insect repellent and sunscreen to protect yourself from mosquito bites and sunburn. Also, make sure to stay hydrated and carry your medication if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Caboclos Core: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caboclos Core

The nearest airport to Caboclos Core is Sao Paulo Congonhas Airport, located about 176 kilometers away. You can take a bus or rent a car from the airport to reach Caboclos Core.

Bus Stations Near Caboclos Core

The nearest bus stations to Caboclos Core are Santos and Cubatão bus stations. From there, you can take a local bus to reach Caboclos Core.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a taxi to reach Caboclos Core from any major city in the region.

How to Get Around in Caboclos Core

The best way to get around in Caboclos Core is by renting a car or taking a local bus. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Caboclos Core: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, you can explore the nearby destinations from Caboclos Core, such as the historic city of Paraty, the beautiful beach town of Ubatuba, and the bustling city of Sao Paulo.

Conclusion

Caboclos Core is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing holiday, Caboclos Core has something for everyone.
