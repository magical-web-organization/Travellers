Welcome to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park, a must-visit destination for all nature and adventure lovers. This park, located in Santos and South Coast, in São Paulo (state), in Southeast, in Brazil, in South America, is home to breathtaking landscapes, rich biodiversity, and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, or just taking in the scenic views, Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll help you plan your trip by providing all the essential information on the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

Introduction to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

Spread across 35,000 hectares of lush Atlantic Rainforest, Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park is a nature lover’s paradise. Founded in 1958, this park is one of the largest and oldest protected areas in Brazil. The park is home to over 100 species of mammals, 300 species of birds, and 80 species of reptiles and amphibians. In addition to the stunning natural beauty, the park also has a rich cultural heritage. The park is home to indigenous communities, caiçaras, and quilombolas, who have preserved their traditional practices and customs for generations.

List of Places to Visit in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

There’s nothing like a trip to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park to connect with nature and indulge in outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of places you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Casa do Patrimônio - This museum has exhibits on the history and culture of the park.
  2. Pátio dos Escravos - This site is an important historical landmark that showcases the history of slavery.

Natural Attractions of Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cachoeira da Usina - This waterfall is one of the most popular in the park, with clear water cascading down a rocky slope.
  2. Trilha do Rio das Pedras - This hiking trail is a great way to explore the park and see its rich biodiversity.
  3. Mirante da Pedra Grande - This lookout point offers stunning views of the Atlantic Rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

There are several hiking and biking routes in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park that offer breathtaking views of the park’s lush natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  1. Trilha do Ouro - This trail passes through the heart of the park and has plenty of sightings of exotic wildlife along the way.
  2. Jequitibá Trail - This trail is great for cycling and features a beautiful view of the Jequitibá tree, the largest tree species in Brazil.

Best Things to Do in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fun activities to do while in the Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park, consider the following:

  1. Rafting - This adrenaline-pumping activity takes you down the park’s rapids and is suitable for all levels of experience.
  2. Waterfall rappelling - Do as the locals do and try out waterfall rappelling. This thrilling activity involves descending down a waterfall while attached to a rope.

Food and Drink Scene of Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

No trip to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park is complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some dishes and drinks you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pescada Cambucu - This is a fish dish served with rice and beans, a common local staple.
  2. Bolinho de Mandioca - These cassava balls are a popular snack served in the park.

Local Beverages

  1. Cachaça - This local liquor is made from sugarcane and is often served in a caipirinha cocktail.
  2. Guarana Antarctica - This Brazilian soft drink is a refreshing choice during hot days in the park.

Where to Stay in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Pousada Raposa - This charming guesthouse offers a comfortable stay in a traditional setting.
  2. Hotel Fazenda Ribeirão - This hotel features a swimming pool and horseback riding tours.

What to Buy in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t miss out on the opportunity to take home some unique souvenirs and local products from your trip to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park. Here are some ideas:

  1. Honey - This is produced locally from bees living in the park.
  2. Handmade crafts - These beautiful handicrafts include pottery and embroidery, made by locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park is home to indigenous communities, caiçaras, and quilombolas, who have rich cultural traditions and customs that have been preserved over the years. Visitors to the park are encouraged to respect their traditions and learn more about their unique way of life.

Best Time to Visit Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

The best time to visit Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park is between March and May, and between September and November. These are the periods when the weather is mild, and there is less rainfall. However, the park is open year-round, so plan your trip according to your schedule and preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park:

Do:

  1. Respect the park’s animals and wildlife.
  2. Carry plenty of water and sun protection on hikes.
  3. Bring cash, as there may not be many ATMs in the park.

Don’t:

  1. Litter - Keep the park clean and carry out all your trash.
  2. Disturb the wildlife, including by feeding them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

The park is generally safe, but it’s important to take precautions, especially during outdoor activities. Always wear protective gear while engaging in adventure sports and obey all safety guidelines.

Health in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

Be sure to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses such as Zika and dengue fever. There are also medical facilities in the surrounding areas if needed.

Getting to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park is easy, as it is well connected to major cities in the region. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

The closest airports to the park are São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport (GRU) and São Paulo–Congonhas Airport (CGH).

Bus Stations Near Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

There are several bus stations that connect to the park, including São Paulo Bus Station and Santos Bus Station.

Other Options

You can also rent a car and drive to the park, which gives you flexibility in exploring the park at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park

The best way to get around the park is on foot, especially for exploring hiking trails and natural landmarks. You can also hire a guide or rent a bicycle if you prefer.

Where to Go Next from Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region surrounding Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park, here are some destinations to consider:

  1. Santos - This seaside town is known for its beautiful beaches and historical architecture.
  2. São Paulo - Brazil’s largest city and home to a range of cultural attractions and contemporary art.

Conclusion

Alto Ribeira State Touristic Park offers a unique combination of untouched nature, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor activities that is sure to leave a lasting impression. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip with ease and get the most out of your visit to this vibrant destination.
