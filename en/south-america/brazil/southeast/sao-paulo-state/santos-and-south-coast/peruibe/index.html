Introduction to Peruíbe

Peruíbe is a charming coastal town located in the Santos and South Coast region of São Paulo state, Brazil. It is located 138 km from the city of São Paulo, and 85 km from Santos, making it an easy and popular weekend getaway destination for locals and tourists alike. The town offers a great mix of natural and cultural attractions, from beautiful beaches, waterfalls, and ecological reserves to museums, historical buildings, and local artisan markets. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see in Peruíbe, as well as where to stay and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Peruíbe

Peruíbe offers a wide array of attractions for visitors to explore, from culturally important museums and historical buildings to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of must-visit places in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Peruíbe boasts a rich history with deep cultural roots which reflects in the heritage and history of the town. The following are a few cultural and historical places that you should visit:

  • Peruíbe Museum: This museum showcases the history of the town and its cultural and biological diversity, as well as the local customs of indigenous people.
  • Una Park: This is a historic site was the place of Communication Cave, the first communication device in Brazil.
  • Ruins of Abarebebê: These are the ruins of the first church built by Portuguese in Brazil, in 1534. It’s a particularly interesting spot for history lovers.
  • Indian Cemetery Park: From the Uma tribe in Peruíbe, this cemetery has anthropological and historical importance in the region.
  • Guaranis Museum: This project has been led by Guarani people to consider and preserve knowledge and wisdom of the tribe.

Natural Attractions of Peruíbe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Peruíbe has a bounty of natural attractions, including gorgeous beaches and ecological reserves. Here are a few can’t-miss natural attractions of Peruíbe:

  • Prainha Branca Beach: This is one of the most popular beaches in Peruíbe, and it’s known for its clear waters and beautiful views.
  • Deserted Beach: This is a secluded and serene beach located in the Reserva Lagamar, perfect for swimming, hiking or walking.
  • Perequê Beach: It is a popular spot for kitesurfing and other water sports and activities for families and adventurers alike.
  • Guaraú Beach: It is an unspoiled, pristine beach, surrounded by dense vegetation. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Pé da Serra Waterfall: This an ideal destination for people who love hiking and outdoor adventure. The waterfall is located within the Serra do Mar Ecological Reserve and offers stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peruíbe

Peruíbe has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails, which take visitors through lush forests, up towering peaks, and past sparkling waterfalls. Here are a few of the best hiking and biking routes in Peruíbe:

  • Pico Do Serra Do Mar State Park: It is located along the Atlantic coast with beautiful views from the peak. Pico Do Serra is also the highest peak in the park and the state of São Paulo and is worth the climb.
  • Xavier Alves Trail: This is an enjoyable pathway through dense forest and natural vegetation with beautiful views.
  • Andinha Trail: It is a popular route for hikers with several waterfalls and offers beautiful views of the region.
  • Moon, Sun and Stars Trail: tourists can hike in the region at night through the forest to see the stars and sounds of the night animals.

Best Things to Do in Peruíbe: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Peruíbe offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best things to do in Peruíbe are:

  • Fishing: Experience the local fishing culture in Peruíbe, where locals have caught fresh fish for centuries.
  • Helicopter Tour: Get an aerial view of Peruíbe’s stunning coastline, and its natural surroundings on this one-of-a-kind tour.
  • Stand-Up Paddle: Rent a board and hit the water to see Peruíbe’s coastline from a unique perspective.
  • Eco-Tourism: Take a tour of some of Peruíbe’s ecological reserves or enjoy birdwatching in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peruíbe

For travelers who can stick around for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary for Peruíbe:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in with a day to relax/laze on the beach Day 2: Visit the Guaranis Museum and take a tour of the Peruíbe Museum Day 3: Spend the day at Prainha Branca Beach and go for a hike to the nearby Moon, Sun and Stars Trail. Day 4: Discover the Pico do Serra do Mar State Park with a full day trekking tour. Day 5: Explore the beaches of Guaraú and Deserta Beach Day 6: Take an Eco-Tour and go horseback riding. Day 7: Visit the local artisan market to buy souvenirs and relax on the beach before departure

Food and Drink Scene of Peruíbe

Peruíbe offers an eclectic food and beverage scene, known for its fresh seafood, traditional dishes and local fruits. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages in Peruíbe:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca Capixaba: It is a classic Brazilian dish featuring a fish stew with local ingredients such as tomatoes, onions, peppers and coriander.
  • Banana Leaf Crab Cake: Peruíbe is known for locals using local ingredients, and this dish is made with locally caught crab and it&s wrapped in a banana leaf.
  • Mandioca Frita: Locally knowns as Yucca, it is fried and served as snack or side dish for meals.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This Brazilian spirit is made from sugar cane juice and is used in an array of cocktails and drinks.
  • Guaraná Antarctica: It is a delicious soda made from the guarana fruit native to the Amazon rainforest.

Where to Stay in Peruíbe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peruíbe has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some options for where to stay in Peruíbe:

  • Pousada Mirante do Mar - located on the mountainside with stunning coastal views.
  • Guarau Surf House - located near the beach with a surf-ambience.
  • Hotel Bem Te Vi - located beachfront with pool and facilities for children.

What to Buy in Peruíbe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peruíbe has a flavorful artisanal craft scene, where you can buy locally made souvenirs. Here are a few examples of what to buy in Peruíbe:

  • Soap and other skincare products made from local herbs.
  • Coconut candles.
  • Local handicrafts such as clay pots, woven baskets and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Peruíbe

Peruíbe has a rich cultural heritage derived from of the indigenous history of the Guarani ethnic group in the region and Portuguese influences during colonization. During your visit, pay attention to traditional music and customs of the region; and be sure to check the calendar for festivals and events happening during your stay.

Best Time to Visit Peruíbe

The best time to visit Peruíbe is between December and March, as it is the most sunny and dry season in the region. However, if you prefer a cooler and more relaxed visit, consider planning for the off-season month from May until August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peruíbe

Do:

  • Try the local beer and cocktail scene.
  • Experience a traditional Brazilian Rodizio (all-you-can-eat meat/steakhouse).
  • Visit several beaches and explore the natural reserves.

Don’t:

  • Don’t visit during the mid-season holidays if you dislike crowds and noise.
  • Don’t swim alone or after dark, and always heed signs indicating areas closed to public access.
  • Don’t litter or damage the local flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peruíbe

Peruíbe is a safe and peaceful town; however, tourists should exercise general caution as they would in any unfamiliar place but ensure they take all physical safety precautions such as wearing masks and maintaining appropriate social distance.

Health in Peruíbe

Peruíbe has a small regional hospital in case of medical emergencies. It’s important to bring any necessary prescription medications with you during your visit, as pharmacies in the area may have a limited selection of medicines and supplies.

Getting to Peruíbe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peruíbe

  • São Paulo-Congonhas Airport: This is the closest airport to Peruíbe, located roughly 130 km away.
  • Santos Dumont Airport: This is the second closest airport, located roughly 170 km away.

Bus Stations Near Peruíbe

  • Peruíbe Bus Station: Located in the downtown area.
  • São Paulo Bus Station: This is the closest main bus station to Peruíbe, located about 130 km away.

Other Options

You can rent a car from São Paulo and drive yourself to Peruíbe, which is about a 2.5-hour drive. In addition to driving, you can take a private transfer or a shared van from São Paulo.

How to Get Around in Peruíbe

Peruíbe is a small town where most attractions are easily accessible by foot, however renting a car or a bike is a popular choice for those who prefer to explore at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Peruíbe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peruíbe is well-located for exploring the surrounding area, including a host of beaches. Here are a few nearby destinations to explore if you have time:

  • Eimm Ybudui Waterfall: located near Monguaga, this waterfall is a beautiful scenic spot.
  • Guaraú Beach: An idyllic spot to paddle in the lagoon, fish, kayak or just relax on the beach.

Conclusion

Peruíbe is a wonderful coastal town offering tourists a beautiful chance to explore the natural harmony of the ocean and the mountains. From marvellous beaches and hikes, diverse culture and heritage, to local artisanal markets, and great eating and drinking options, Peruíbe has something fascinating and exciting for everyone. Plan your trip today and explore one of Brazil’s most scenic hidden gems.
