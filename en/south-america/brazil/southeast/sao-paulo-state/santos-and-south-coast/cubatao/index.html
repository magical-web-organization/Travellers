Cubatão is a charming town located in the Santos and South Coast region of São Paulo state in Southeast Brazil. It’s known for its captivating natural beauty and rich cultural heritage that makes it a popular destination for both Brazilian and foreign tourists. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan the perfect trip to Cubatão, including all the best places to visit, hike/bike routes, must-try local cuisine, where to stay, how to get around, and more.

Introduction to Cubatão

Cubatão is a small town nestled between the Serra do Mar mountain range and the famous Santos coastline. Despite its small size, it is filled with incredible natural and cultural attractions that make it a must-visit destination in Brazil. The town is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, and the clean waters of the Rio Cubatão that runs through the heart of the town.

Cubatão has a rich cultural heritage, with a long history of colonization and industrial development. The town has a number of museums, historical landmarks, and monuments that provide a glimpse into its past. It’s also a popular destination for adventure seekers thanks to its extensive network of hiking and biking trails that snake through the surrounding hills and forests.

List of Places to Visit in Cubatão

Cubatão has plenty of places to explore, including a range of natural and cultural attractions that cater to all kinds of tourists. Here are our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Igreja Matriz de Cubatão: This historic church dates back to the 17th century and is considered one of the most important religious landmarks in the town.
  • Ecomuseu do Quilombo: This museum showcases the rich African heritage and culture of the local quilombo communities.
  • Cine Roxy: One of the oldest cinemas in Brazil, the Cine Roxy dates back to the early 1930s and has hosted countless film festivals and premieres.

Natural Attractions of Cubatão: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar: A stunning natural park that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare species like the maned sloth and the green-billed toucan.
  • Pico do Paranapiacaba: This mountain peak offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding hills and forests that make it a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.
  • Ambiental Team: A local ecotourism company that offers adventure activities like zip-lining, rappelling, and tree climbing in the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cubatão

Cubatão has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to explore the town’s natural beauty on foot or by bike. Here are some popular routes to try:

  • Trilha do Morro das Pedras: A moderate hike that leads to the top of Morro das Pedras, with stunning views of the surrounding forests and towns.
  • Estrada Velha: A scenic bike route that follows an old railway track through the beautiful Serra do Mar mountains.
  • Trilha do Rio Cubatão: A beginner-level hiking trail that follows the course of the Rio Cubatão through lush green forests and serene waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Cubatão: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural and cultural attractions, Cubatão has plenty of exciting activities and tours that promise to deliver a memorable experience. Here are our picks:

  • Rio Cubatão Kayak Tour: Take a guided kayak tour down the Rio Cubatão, passing through scenic forests and waterfalls.
  • Stand-Up Paddle Boarding: Glide atop the clear waters of Rio Cubatão on a SUP board while enjoying the scenic beauty of the surrounding nature.
  • Quilombo Experience: Experience the rich Afro-Brazilian culture of the local quilombo communities through a guided tour that includes dance performances and traditional food tasting.

Food and Drink Scene of Cubatão

Cubatão has a vibrant culinary scene that showcases both Brazilian and international cuisine, as well as the local Afro-Brazilian heritage and culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijoada: A traditional Brazilian stew made with black beans, pork, and beef that is served with rice, collard greens, and farofa.
  • Pastel de Feira: Fried pastries that are filled with various ingredients like chicken, cheese, and vegetables.
  • Pamonha: A savory corn cake that is stuffed with creamy cheese and served hot.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A high-proof distilled spirit that is made from sugarcane juice and is used in many popular Brazilian cocktails.
  • Caipirinha: A refreshing cocktail made with cachaça, lime, sugar, and ice.

Where to Stay in Cubatão: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cubatão offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and needs. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Pousada Recanto Primavera: A beautifully decorated guesthouse that offers a range of amenities and services, including an outdoor pool and free wifi.
  • Hostel Cubatão: A modern, affordable hostel in the heart of Cubatão that offers free breakfast and has an on-site bar.

What to Buy in Cubatão: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cubatão is famous for its local crafts and products that make for perfect souvenirs and gifts to bring home. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics
  • Basketry and woven goods
  • Locally grown coffee beans and paçoca (Brazilian peanut candy)

Local Culture and Customs of Cubatão

Cubatão has a rich cultural heritage that is influenced by both Brazilian and Afro-Brazilian traditions. Visitors should be respectful of traditional customs and practices, such as the local Candomblé and Quilombo communities’ rituals and ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Cubatão

The best time to visit Cubatão is during the dry season from May to September when the weather is mild and sunny, and the chances of rainfall are low. However, visitors can also enjoy the lush greenery and waterfalls during the wet season from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cubatão

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor adventures and scenic beauty of Cubatão.

Don’t:

  • Litter trash. Cubatão has a beautiful landscape that attracts many tourists, so it’s important to keep it clean and tidy.
  • Disrespect the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cubatão

Cubatão is a safe town to visit. However, visitors should remain cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when visiting remote or isolated areas like hiking trails.

Health in Cubatão

Cubatão has a well-equipped health system that can handle most common medical issues and emergencies. Visitors are advised to ensure that they have adequate travel medical insurance.

Getting to Cubatão: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cubatão is well-connected by both road and air, making it easily accessible to visitors from Brazil and beyond. Here are some transport options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Cubatão

  • The closest airport to Cubatão is São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport, which is approximately 70 km away.

Bus Stations Near Cubatão

  • Bus station closest to Cubatão is the Santos bus station.

Other Options

  • Taxis from nearby towns or Uber are widely available, and visitors can also rent cars from the local car rental agencies.

How to Get Around in Cubatão

The best way to get around Cubatão is by car or bike, both of which can be rented from local rental companies. Alternatively, visitors can also take local buses or hire taxis to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Cubatão: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cubatão is ideally located for visitors who want to explore other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular options:

  • Santos: The nearby coastal city of Santos is famous for its beautiful beaches, museums, and historical landmarks.
  • São Paulo: The state capital, Sao Paulo is home to some of Brazil’s best museums, restaurants, and cultural attractions.
  • Ilha Bela: A stunning island that is known for its breathtaking beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Cubatão is a hidden gem that offers visitors the perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and adventure activities. From hiking and biking to sampling the delicious local cuisine and souvenirs, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in this vibrant little town. Plan your visit and enjoy all that Cubatão has to offer on your next trip to Brazil!
