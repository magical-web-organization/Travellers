Introduction to Bertioga

Bertioga is a small municipality located in the Santos and South Coast region of São Paulo, in Southeast Brazil. It is a popular destination for tourists because of its scenic beauty, historical sites, and outdoor adventures. The city boasts of a rich culture and history dating back to the 16th century, when it was a part of the Portuguese colonization.

List of Places to Visit in Bertioga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The city has a rich history that dates back to the 16th century and has a plethora of museums and historical places that tourists can explore. The following are a few culturally important places to add to your bucket-list:

  • Praça dos Emancipadores: Here, you can witness a symbolic monument commemorating the city’s founding before Portuguese colonization.

  • Casa da Cultura: This historical house museum offers a glimpse into Bertioga’s history, with displays of furnitures, clothing, and culture during the late 19th and early 20th century.

  • Forte São João: Constructed in 1547, Forte São Joao is a well-maintained citadel that has played an essential role in Bertioga’s history. You can visit the fort to explore the history of the Portuguese colonization.

  • Museu Histórico Forte São João: A modern museum located inside the citadel that showcases the history of colonization in Bertioga and the surrounding coastal regions.

Natural Attractions of Bertioga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bertioga is known for its stunning landscapes, beautiful beaches, and outdoor adventures. The following are a few natural attractions that you should visit:

  • Praião: Located near the historic center of Bertioga, Praião is a picturesque, long beach with clear waters that offer plenty of opportunities for beach activities like swimming, sunbathing & fishing.

  • Serra do Mar State Park: This state park is a must-visit destination for hikers and eco-tourists. With an area of over 350,000 hectares, it is home to a diverse range of wildlife and flora, including several waterfalls, caves, and trails.

  • Guaratuba Beach: Guaratuba Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches near Bertioga, ideal for surfers due to the strong waves. Here you can sign up for a surf lesson or board rental.

  • Morro do São João: A popular destination for lovers of the great outdoors, Morro do São João is a hill with excellent views of the area. It has an observation tower that can be reached after a gentle hike and is perfect for sunset photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bertioga

Bertioga is a trekker’s paradise, with a variety of trails, ranging from easy to moderate, in and around the city. The most prominent hiking and biking trails in Bertioga are:

  • Trail of the Three Beaches: A 6km trek starting at Praia da Enseada and crosses Praia de São Lourenço before ending in Praia Preta.

  • Riviera de São Lourenço Bike Trail: This 22km trail meanders through the Riviera de São Lourenço complex and provides stunning views of the coast.

  • Jureia-Itatins Ecological Park: The park is a habitat for over 200 species of birds and is home to impressive fauna and flora. There are several hiking paths in the park that are well-suited for seasoned trekkers.

Best Things to Do in Bertioga: Activities and Tours

Bertioga offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to people of all ages. The following are a few of the best things to do in Bertioga:

  • Boat tours: Explore the beautiful coastline of Bertioga by taking a boat tour that goes around the region’s most famous spots. You can see dolphins, sea turtles, and other local marine life.

  • Stand-up paddleboarding: Try your hand at stand-up paddleboarding, a fun and relatively easy sport that is increasing in popularity.

  • Fishing Tour: Sign up for a recreational fishing tour and learn some of the best techniques used in Bertioga to catch fish.

Food and Drink Scene of Bertioga

Must-Try Dishes

Bertioga’s cuisine is inspired by the region’s vast seafood. You can find several restaurants in Bertioga that serve excellent seafood dishes. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Sardine Pasteis: Thin pastries stuffed with sardines and leeks, a popular snack in Bertioga.

  • Moqueca: Fish stew made with local saltwater fish, onions, tomatoes, garlic, bell peppers, and palm oil.

  • Pastel de Camarão: Fried pies stuffed with shrimp and well-seasoned with a mixture of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A strong liquor made from fermented sugarcane juice, where each producer has its unique recipe and aging process.

  • Caipirinha: A classic Brazilian cocktail of sugar, limes and cachaça

Where to Stay in Bertioga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bertioga is home to many accommodation options, ranging from hotels to guesthouses and hostels. If you’re traveling to Bertioga, here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Riviera de São Lourenço: A posh residential complex with rental apartments and hotels, world-class infrastructure, swimming pools, leisure clubs and excellent beaches.

  • Hotel Costa da Riviera: A fantastic resort that combines luxury and comfort in the heart of Bertioga, with complete services for river and beach access.

  • Pousada Sollar Luna: A romantic pousada nestled among the Atlantic rainforest flora and fauna, with charming rooms, a pool and several trails for nature walks.

What to Buy in Bertioga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bertioga is famous for its handicrafts made from paper, seashells, and clothing. Some of the best places to purchase souvenirs and local products are:

  • Água Viva Patronato: This colorful handicraft market is an excellent place to pick up souvenirs.

  • Praia dos Milionários : This beach is also known as “Fisherman’s Beach” and is a great place to purchase fresh fish from traditional fishermen.

Local Culture and Customs of Bertioga

The local culture of Bertioga is greatly influenced by its colonial roots, coupled with indigenous, African, and Middle-Eastern influences that still reflect in its architecture, music, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Bertioga

Bertioga enjoys a tropical climate throughout the year, with temperatures ranging between 22°C- 33°C. The best time to visit Bertioga is between May and September, during the dry season. During this time, the climate is pleasant, and the water clarity is excellent, making it the perfect time to relax on the beach.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bertioga

Do:

  • Drink Coconut Water: Try a refreshing drink of coconut water, which can be found practically anywhere.

  • Try the Seafood: Bertioga is known for its seafood, so make sure to indulge in some of the amazing seafood dishes available.

  • Be Respectful: Respect the local customs by dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Drink Tap Water: Make sure to always drink bottled water to avoid any health risks.

  • Don’t Leave Your Belongings Unattended: Avoid leaving your belongings unattended or unsecured in open areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bertioga

Bertioga is a safe destination for tourists; however, common sense is always valuable. Be wary of petty theft and avoid walking alone on the beach late at night.

Health in Bertioga

Bertioga is not known for any significant health risks. Still, it is advisable to bring sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and any prescription medications you might require on your trip.

Getting to Bertioga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bertioga

The nearest airport to Bertioga is São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport, located in Guarulhos, about 95km away. From there, you can hire a taxi, use a shuttle service, or rent a car.

Bus Stations Near Bertioga

The Bertioga bus terminal offers many buses that travel to and from Sao Paulo city in about two hours.

Other Options

For a more luxurious mode of transportation, one could hire a helicopter or seaplane from São Paulo to Bertioga.

How to Get Around in Bertioga

The easiest way to get around in Bertioga is by car or taxi. You can also rent a bike or take the bus, which is convenient but less popular.

Where to Go Next from Bertioga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maresias

Located 40km away from Bertioga, Maresias is another beautiful coastal town known for its white sandy beaches and world-class surf breaks.

Guarujá

About 30km from Bertioga, Guarujá is a bustling beach city that has a beautiful coastline and fantastic nightlife, making it one of the most popular destinations for locals and tourists alike.

Conclusion

Bertioga is a fantastic destination for travelers looking to experience a diverse mix of culture, history, and outdoor adventure. With its beautiful coastline, national parks, and diverse cuisine and cultural attractions, Bertioga is a hidden paradise with something for everyone.
