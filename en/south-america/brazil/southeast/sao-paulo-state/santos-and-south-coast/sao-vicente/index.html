Introduction to São Vicente

São Vicente is a charming beachside city located in the state of São Paulo, Southeast Brazil. It is one of the oldest cities in Brazil, founded in 1532 by the Portuguese explorer Martim Afonso de Sousa. São Vicente is known for its stunning beaches, rich history, and natural beauty. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in São Vicente.

List of Places to Visit in São Vicente

Here are the top attractions you shouldn’t miss on your trip to São Vicente:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museu do Mar - This museum showcases the region’s maritime history with fascinating exhibits on fishing, navigation, and shipbuilding.
  2. Memorial Santos Dumont - A homage to the famous Brazilian aviation pioneer, Alberto Santos Dumont. The museum showcases his many aviation inventions, including his famous plane, Demoiselle.
  3. Cidade Historica de Sao Vicente - The historic center of São Vicente is one of the most important landmarks of this coastal city. It represents the first Portuguese settlement in Brazil, with its traditional Portuguese architecture, narrow streets, and historic buildings.

Natural Attractions of São Vicente: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ilha Porchat - This panoramic viewpoint is located on a hill, overlooking the bay of Santos. It offers a spectacular view of the city and the ocean.
  2. Parque Cultural Vila de São Vicente - This park has a vast area of mangroves and trails that lead to a preserved area of the Atlantic Forest. You can enjoy a picnic while taking in the stunning ocean views.
  3. Praia do Itararé - This is one of the most popular beaches in São Vicente. It stretches for 2.5 kilometers and offers excellent conditions for surfing and kiteboarding.
  4. Morro do Itatiaia State Park - This protected area in the Serra do Mar mountain range is home to numerous hiking trails, waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of São Vicente

São Vicente is a city surrounded by natural wonders, making it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular trails to explore:

  • Trilha do Itatins - This 15 km trail begins in the Serra do Mar State Park and ends in Praia dos Itanhaéns. It offers the perfect opportunity to explore the lush Atlantic Forest along the way.
  • Pista Cláudio Coutinho - This trail is located between Praia do Itararé and Praia dos Milionários and is perfect for jogging and cycling. The path offers beautiful views of the sea and green mountains.
  • Ciclovia Guarujá-São Vicente - This bike path stretches for 25 km and is perfect for a long ride, offering views of the sea, the city, and nearby hills. Make sure to stop at Ilha Porchat for a breathtaking view of the bay.

Best Things to Do in São Vicente: Activities and Tours

From exploring historic sites to getting your adrenaline pumping, São Vicente offers plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Take a boat tour: Explore the coast of São Vicente and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Visit Praia dos Milionários: It is known for its calm waters, cosmopolitan atmosphere, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Kiteboarding at Praia do Itararé: With its strong winds and waves, it’s the perfect spot for kiteboarding enthusiasts.
  • Diving at Laje de Santos: This underwater park is home to numerous species of fish, turtles, and sea stars. It’s a must-visit spot for divers.

Week-Long Itinerary for São Vicente

Day 1: Start with a visit to Cidade Historica de São Vicente. Wander its streets and enjoy lunch at one of the many seafood restaurants. End the day with a relaxing sunset view at Ilha Porchat.

Day 2: Take a boat tour along the coast of São Vicente and explore the beautiful beaches of Praia dos Milionários and Praia do Itararé.

Day 3: Hike one of the beautiful trails such as Trilha do Itatins or Pista Cláudio Coutinho. End your day with a refreshing swim at Praia dos Itanhaéns.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the stunning Morro do Itatiaia State Park. Experience the beautiful waterfalls and scenic views of the protected area.

Day 5: Learn about the region’s maritime history at the Museu do Mar. End the day with a delightful dinner at one of the many seafood restaurants in town.

Day 6: Take a day off and relax at Parque Cultural Vila de São Vicente. Enjoy a picnic and the stunning views of the ocean.

Day 7: Take to the skies with a paragliding experience from Morro do Voturuá. Experience the stunning landscape from up high before your journey ends.

Food and Drink Scene of São Vicente

São Vicente is a paradise for seafood lovers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Caldeirada - A hearty fish stew cooked with tomato, onion, and bell pepper. It’s a perfect comfort food.
  • Camarão na Moranga - A dish made of shrimp and pumpkin, seasoned with herbs and spices. It’s a must-try dish when in São Vicente.
  • Moqueca - A traditional Brazilian dish made with seafood and served with rice. It’s a popular dish in São Vicente and worth trying out.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha - Brazil’s national drink and a staple when dining out.
  • Cachaça - A distilled alcohol made from sugarcane that’s used in many cocktails and other drinks in Brazil.
  • Choop – Draft beer.

Where to Stay in São Vicente: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

São Vicente offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Mont Rey: This hotel is located near Praia dos Milionários and has well-equipped rooms and a rooftop pool with stunning views of the ocean.
  • Hostel Casa da Praia: A budget-friendly option located just steps from the beach, this hostel offers shared and private rooms and a common kitchen.
  • Pousada 700 Mares: A charming guesthouse located near the historic center, this accommodation offers comfortable rooms, a garden, and a pool.

What to Buy in São Vicente: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wooden crafts: São Vicente woodworkers create fantastic pieces, such as boxes, jewelry, and the like, that make for unique souvenirs.
  • Cachaca – The Brazilian sugarcane liquor is known worldwide, and São Vicente has several distilleries around that produce unique Cachaca.
  • Handmade Sandals and Clothing: You can find plenty of handmade clothing and footwear to take back home in São Vicente.

Local Culture and Customs of São Vicente

São Vicente has a mix of cultures due to its colonial past and the mix and blend of Portuguese, indigenous, and African descent. Respect is a crucial part of Brazilian culture and visitors should respect local customs and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit São Vicente

São Vicente has a warm, tropical climate all year round. The best time for tourism, when the weather is cooler and less rainy, is from April to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of São Vicente

Do:

  • Try traditional Brazilian dishes.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Visit the Morro do Itatiaia State Park.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended in public places.
  • Swim in unsafe and unguarded beaches.
  • Disrespect religious and cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in São Vicente

Like many Brazilian cities, São Vicente does have high crime rates. Visitors should be cautious and keep an eye on their belongings. Avoid walking alone at night, carrying expensive items, or engaging in any suspicious activities.

Health in São Vicente

Brazil has excellent healthcare facilities, with both public and private hospitals. However, visitors should ensure they have health insurance, and take measures to protect themselves against mosquito-borne illnesses like Dengue and Zika viruses. It is also crucial to drink only bottled or filtered water.

Getting to São Vicente: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near São Vicente

São Paulo International Airport (GRU) is the closest airport to São Vicente. The airport is around 120 km away, and the journey can take around 1 hour and 40 minutes by car.

Bus Stations Near São Vicente

São Vicente has a bus terminal located downtown that connects the city with other nearby cities in the region.

Other Options

Renting a car or using a ride-hailing service like Uber or 99Pop is another option for getting around the city.

How to Get Around in São Vicente

São Vicente is a walkable city, and most attractions are located near each other. Taxis, buses, and ride-hailing services like Uber are available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from São Vicente: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations that visitors to São Vicente can explore, including:

  • Santos: A port city only 12 miles away from São Vicente with a historic city center, several beaches, and a remarkable aquarium.
  • Guarujá: A resort town with beautiful beaches and a famous aquarium.
  • Ilha Bela: A beautiful island with plenty of beaches, trails, and spectacular landscapes.

Conclusion

São Vicente is a fantastic destination in Brazil, from its stunning beaches to its rich history and vibrant culture. With our comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their itineraries, explore the best attractions and activities, and fully enjoy this coastal gem. Come, spend your time, and enjoy yourself in São Vicente, where every day brings new adventures!
