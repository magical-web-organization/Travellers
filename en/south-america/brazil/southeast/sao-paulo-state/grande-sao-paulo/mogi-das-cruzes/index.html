Introduction to Mogi das Cruzes

Mogi das Cruzes is located in Grande São Paulo, in the southeastern region of Brazil. Founded in 1560 by the Jesuit priests, it was initially a small village that grew rapidly over the years. Today, it is a bustling city with a population of over 400,000 people.

The city boasts a mix of modern and traditional architecture, with its colonial buildings and historic sites still intact. Mogi das Cruzes is also known for its pristine forests, mountains, and rivers, making it a nature lover’s paradise.

Food and Drink Scene of Mogi das Cruzes

Brazilian cuisine is world-famous for its bold flavors, unique combinations, and fresh ingredients. Mogi das Cruzes is no exception when it comes to delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your trip.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijão tropeiro (bean and pork stew)
  • Coxinha (fried chicken balls)
  • Pão de queijo (cheese bread)
  • Churrasco (Brazilian barbeque)

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha (national cocktail made with cachaça, lime, and sugar)
  • Cerveja (beer)
  • Guaraná Antarctica (Brazilian soft drink)
  • Café (coffee)
