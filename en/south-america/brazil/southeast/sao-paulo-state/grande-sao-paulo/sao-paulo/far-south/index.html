Introduction to Far South

Far South, nestled in the Grande São Paulo region of Brazil, is an underrated gem. Often overlooked by tourists, this region has much to offer visitors - from its vibrant culture and lively atmosphere to its untouched natural beauty and rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Far South

Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, foodie, or adventurous traveler, Far South has something exciting in store for you. Here are some of the must-visit destinations in this region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Far South is home to many museums and historical sites that offer insight into Brazil’s rich culture and heritage. Some of the top places to visit in this category include:

  • Museu Afro Brasil: This unique museum showcases the history and culture of Afro-Brazilians and their contributions to the nation’s art, literature, and music.

  • Museu do Ipiranga: Considered to be the birthplace of Brazil, this impressive museum features over 125,000 exhibits related to the country’s history.

  • Museu de Arte de São Paulo: Known as MASP, this museum boasts one of the most extensive collections of Western art in Latin America.

Natural Attractions of Far South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Far South is also known for its stunning natural attractions, which attract adventure enthusiasts from all over the world. Some of the top natural spots to include in your itinerary are:

  • Horto Florestal: This lush forest reserve is the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse yourself in nature. It offers hiking trails, picnic spots, and scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Parque Estadual Serra do Mar: This vast national park is a pristine wilderness area that offers a chance to see some of Brazil’s rarest wildlife and flora. Visitors can explore the park’s hiking trails, waterfalls, and breathtaking vistas.

  • Ilha do Cardoso: This tropical paradise is a hidden gem that can only be accessed by boat. It offers white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and untouched rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far South

Far South is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with its numerous hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes to explore in this region include:

  • Rota da Mata Atlântica: This trail runs through the Atlantic Forest and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Pedra Grande: This challenging hike leads to a stunning viewpoint where visitors can see the entire city of São Paulo.

  • Ciclofaixa de Lazer: This popular biking route runs through the streets of São Paulo and is closed off to traffic on Sundays, making it a safe and enjoyable ride for cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Far South: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Far South, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Here are some ideas:

  • Experience the vibrant nightlife of São Paulo and dance the night away at one of the city’s many nightclubs.

  • Take a food tour and sample some of the delicious cuisine that São Paulo is famous for, like feijoada, pão de queijo, and churrasco.

  • Visit a favela and learn about the history of these unique communities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Far South

If you have a week to spare, Far South has plenty to offer. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the museums and historical sites in São Paulo.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Ilhabela and enjoy its beautiful beaches and hiking trails.

Day 3-4: Head to Parque Estadual Serra do Mar and explore its waterfalls and hiking trails.

Day 5: Visit a favela and learn about its unique culture and history.

Day 6: Take a bike ride along Ciclofaixa de Lazer and experience the bustling streets of São Paulo.

Day 7: Relax and recharge at Horto Florestal.

Food and Drink Scene of Far South

São Paulo is a melting pot of cultures, and its cuisine reflects that diversity. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Far South.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijoada: This hearty stew made with beans, meat, and vegetables is considered to be Brazil’s national dish.

  • Pão de queijo: These cheesy bread rolls are a popular breakfast treat in São Paulo.

  • Churrasco: This Brazilian-style barbecue features a variety of meats cooked over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: Made with cachaça (a Brazilian liquor), lime, sugar, and ice, this potent cocktail is the perfect way to cool off on a hot day.

  • Guarana: This popular Brazilian soda is made from the guarana plant and has a slightly sweet, fruity taste.

Where to Stay in Far South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Far South has plenty of options for travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in this region:

  • Hotel Fasano São Paulo: This five-star hotel is located in the heart of São Paulo and offers luxurious amenities and stunning city views.

  • The Hostel Paulista: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and offers a laid-back atmosphere and friendly staff.

  • Hotel Unique: This modern hotel is a favorite among celebrities and boasts an infinity pool and rooftop bar with panoramic views of São Paulo.

What to Buy in Far South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Far South, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to look for:

  • Brazilian coffee: Brazil is one of the largest producers of coffee in the world, and their beans are renowned for their rich flavor.

  • Havaianas flip flops: These iconic sandals are a staple of Brazilian fashion and are available in a variety of colors and styles.

  • Brazilian gemstones: Far South is known for its production of gemstones like amethyst, topaz, and aquamarine.

Local Culture and Customs of Far South

Brazilian culture is diverse and rich, and it is essential to understand some of the local customs and etiquette. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Far South:

  • Brazilians are known for their warmth and friendliness, so be prepared to receive a lot of hugs and kisses on the cheek.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and avoid wearing revealing clothing.

  • It is customary to leave a small amount of money as a tip (around 10%) for services like restaurants and taxis.

Best Time to Visit Far South

The best time to visit Far South is during the dry season (May to September), as the weather is mild and pleasant during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far South

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Far South, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases to communicate with the locals.

  • Embrace the local culture and try new things.

  • Stay vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large amounts of cash.

  • Ignore official warnings or advice about safety.

  • Assume that everyone speaks English.

Safety and Health

While Far South is generally safe for tourists, it is essential to take some precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Far South

  • Avoid walking alone at night or in isolated areas.

  • Use caution when using public transport and keep a close eye on your belongings.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid flashing expensive items like phones or cameras.

Health in Far South

  • Make sure you are up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Brazil.

  • Mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and Zika are prevalent in Brazil, so use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to protect yourself.

  • Avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water or purified water.

Getting to Far South: Transportation Options and Logistics

São Paulo is the main gateway to Far South, and there are several transportation options to reach this region.

Airports Near Far South

  • São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport (GRU): This is the primary international airport in São Paulo, with flights to and from many destinations worldwide.

  • Congonhas-São Paulo Airport (CGH): This airport offers domestic flights to many destinations in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near Far South

  • Rodoviária de São Paulo: This bus station is located in the city center and offers bus services to many destinations in Brazil.

  • Terminal Tietê: This is one of the largest bus stations in São Paulo, with services to many cities in Brazil and neighboring countries.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: If you prefer a more comfortable and convenient option, you can arrange a private transfer from São Paulo to Far South.

How to Get Around in Far South

The best way to get around Far South is by car or taxi, as many of the region’s attractions are spread out and not easily accessible by public transport.

Car Rental

Renting a car is a convenient way to explore Far South at your own pace. Most major car rental companies have branches in São Paulo.

Taxi

Taxis are widely available in Far South, and most drivers are honest and reliable. Be sure to negotiate the fare before getting in.

Where to Go Next from Far South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spare, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Rio de Janeiro: This vibrant city is just a short flight away and is known for its stunning beaches, lively atmosphere, and iconic landmarks like the Christ the Redeemer statue.

  • Paraty: This quaint colonial town is located in the state of Rio de Janeiro and is known for its picturesque streets, historic buildings, and beautiful beaches.

  • Iguazu Falls: This natural wonder is located on the border of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay and is one of the largest waterfalls in the world.

Conclusion

Far South is a beautiful and diverse region that offers something for everyone. Whether you are interested in history, culture, nature, or adventure, this region of Brazil is sure to impress. Plan your visit to Far South today and experience all this stunning region has to offer!
