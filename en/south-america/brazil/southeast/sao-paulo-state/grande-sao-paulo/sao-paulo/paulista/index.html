Introduction to Paulista

Paulista is a small city located in Grande São Paulo, in the southeast of Brazil. It is named after the famous Paulista Avenue in São Paulo City, located just 26km away. Paulista offers several attractions for visitors of all ages, including cultural and natural attractions, outdoor activities, excellent food, and warm hospitality. This travel guide for Paulista will help you to plan your trip and make the most of your time in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Paulista

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa do Olhar Luiz Sacilotto: An art museum featuring modern and contemporary art from local and international artists.
  • Memorial do Imigrante: A historical museum that showcases the Brazilian immigration history and culture.
  • Pinacoteca de Paulista: A gallery featuring local and Brazilian artists.
  • Museu Histórico Municipal de Paulista: A historical museum that documents the city’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Paulista: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Municipal do Paulista: A large city park with several hiking and biking trails, a lake, and picnic areas.
  • Pico do Papagaio: A popular hiking destination with stunning views of the region.
  • Praia da Conceição: A beautiful beach with clear waters and white sand, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Pedra Grande: A natural rock formation that can be reached after a moderate hike, offering panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paulista

  • Ecological Trail of Clovis Paiva: A 6.7 km hiking trail that passes through the Atlantic Forest, offering a glimpse of the local wildlife.
  • Paulista Cycling Route: A 60 km cycling route that explores the city and surrounding areas, passing through beautiful landscapes and interesting towns.

Best Things to Do in Paulista: Activities and Tours

  • Taste local gastronomy: Sample delicious street food, such as pão de queijo (cheese bread) and pastéis (fried pastries filled with meat or cheese).
  • Attend local festivals: Join the lively celebrations of Sao Joao, one of the most popular cultural festivals in Brazil.
  • Take a boat tour: Take a boat tour along the river, admiring the local fauna and flora.
  • Visit the weekly markets: Experience the local culture by visiting the weekly markets that sell traditional crafts, food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Paulista

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijoada: A popular Brazilian dish made with black beans and various pork cuts, served with rice and farofa (toasted manioc flour).
  • Brigadeiro: A traditional Brazilian dessert made with chocolate, condensed milk, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail made with cachaça (sugar cane spirit), lime, and sugar.
  • Guarana: A soft drink made from the guarana berry, popular in Brazil for its unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Paulista: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada Villa Boa Vista: A cozy guesthouse located near Parque Municipal do Paulista.
  • Ami Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the city center.
  • Pousada Tabajubá: A comfortable guesthouse with a swimming pool, located close to the beach.

What to Buy in Paulista: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts: Visit the local markets to buy traditional crafts, such as woven baskets, jewelry, and pottery.
  • Cachaça: Bring home a bottle of the local sugar cane spirit, available in several flavors and brands.

Local Culture and Customs of Paulista

  • Brazilians are generally warm and friendly, so expect a warm welcome from locals.
  • Learn a few Portuguese phrases before your trip to communicate with locals and get the most out of your experience.

Best Time to Visit Paulista

The best time to visit Paulista is from February to May or from September to November, during the shoulder seasons when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer (December to March) is the peak season, with high temperatures and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paulista

Do:

  • Eat at local restaurants and try the regional gastronomy.
  • Learn some Portuguese phrases to communicate with locals and take advantage of the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large sums of money on you.
  • Be too loud or disrespectful in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paulista

Paulista is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding unsafe areas and walking alone at night.

Health in Paulista

Visitors to Brazil should be up to date on standard vaccinations, including the yellow fever vaccine, as Brazil has areas with risk for yellow fever transmission. Tap water may not be safe to drink, so visitors should stick to bottled water, and use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Paulista: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paulista

The closest airport to Paulista is São Paulo/Guarulhos–Governador André Franco Montoro International Airport, located 35 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Paulista

The main bus station in the city is Terminal Rodoviário de Paulista, located in the downtown area.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Paulista by car, as the city is well connected with the major highways in the region.

How to Get Around in Paulista

Getting around Paulista is easy, with several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and rental cars. Biking is also a popular option.

Where to Go Next from Paulista: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • São Paulo City: Just 26 km away from Paulista, Brazil’s largest city is a must-visit destination, offering several attractions, including museums, parks, and nightlife.
  • Guarujá: A beautiful beach destination, located just 100 km away from Paulista, featuring numerous beaches, water sports, and a lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Paulista is a hidden gem in the Sao Paulo region, offering a variety of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the region’s culture, natural beauty, and enjoy the warm hospitality of its inhabitants. Don’t miss out on this unique destination in South America, plan your trip to Paulista today!
