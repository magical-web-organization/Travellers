Introduction to Far East

Located in the southeastern part of Brazil, Far East is a region of São Paulo that is well-known for its distinctive features such as diverse culture, breathtaking landscapes, and an abundance of outdoor activities. This region boasts of numerous attractions to suit different interests. For instance, history lovers can explore various museums and historical sites, whereas adventure enthusiasts can revel in the vibrant natural settings.

List of Places to Visit in Far East

Here are the top places to visit in Far East:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Immigration: This museum provides insight into the lives of the immigrants who came to Brazil, with an exhibition of photos, documents, and letters.
  • Ipiranga Museum: This museum is one of the most significant historical landmarks for Brazilians as it houses the country’s independence symbol.
  • São Paulo Museum of Art: This museum features a vast collection of works by Brazilian and international artists, including paintings, sculptures, and drawings.

Natural Attractions of Far East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra do Mar State Park: This park is known for its stunning waterfalls, dense vegetation, and hiking trails for all skill levels.
  • São Lourenço da Serra Reservoir: This man-made lake is a popular leisure spot that offers fishing, swimming, and boat rides.
  • Mount Fuji: This mountain is home to a Buddhist temple, hiking trails, and a lookout point that offers spectacular views of the surrounding scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far East

Far East is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking for hiking and biking activities. Here are some of the most beautiful trails in the region:

  • Cachoeira do Marsilac Trail: A 14 km round trip hike through dense forest that takes visitors to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Represa do Rio Grande Trail: A relatively flat 15 km round trip bike ride around the stunning Rio Grande Reservoir.
  • Riacho Grande Trail: A 10 km round trip hike through lush forest with views of stunning waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Far East: Activities and Tours

Far East offers visitors plenty of fun and exciting activities. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Visit the Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar: A must-visit destination for nature and outdoor enthusiasts, this park features panoramic views, waterfalls, and various hiking trails.
  • Go Island Hopping: Take a boat ride around the islands or rent a boat and experience the freedom of exploring the waterways.
  • Explore the Local Cuisine: Far East is known for its cuisine, and visitors should try the famous dishes such as feijoada, tropeiro, and pastel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Far East

Day 1: Visit the Museum of Immigration and explore the city center. Day 2: Hike to Cachoeira do Marsilac Trail. Day 3: Visit the Ipiranga Museum and the São Paulo Museum of Art. Day 4: Take a boat tour around the islands in the Rio Grande Reservoir. Day 5: Visit the Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar and hike one of the various trails. Day 6: Take a trip to the top of Mount Fuji. Day 7: Visit the Serra do Mar State Park and swimming in the stunning waterfalls.

Food and Drink Scene of Far East

Brazilian cuisine is a melting pot of cultures, and Far East is known for its unique blend of dishes. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tropeiro: This dish is made with beans, rice, eggs, and dried meat.
  • Feijoada: A stew of black beans and various meats, including pork, beef, and sausage, served with rice and farofa.
  • Pastel: A savory pastry filled with cheese, meat, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A Brazilian spirit made from distilled sugar cane juice.
  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail made with cachaça, lime, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Far East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Far East offers visitors a range of accommodation options. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Hostel São Paulo: A budget-friendly accommodation option that offers shared and private rooms.
  • Riviera Hotel: A comfortable hotel near the beach with beautiful views.
  • Pousada Ilha do Sol: A charming guesthouse with a swimming pool and large garden.

What to Buy in Far East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Crafts: The region is known for its handicrafts, such as wood carvings, pottery, and weaving.
  • Cachaça: Brazil’s national drink makes for a perfect souvenir or gift.
  • Coffee: Brazil is one of the world’s largest producers of coffee, and Far East has some of the best beans in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Far East

Brazil has a rich culture that is a fusion of African, European, and Indigenous traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Far East:

  • Brazilians are friendly and hospitable people who appreciate visitors.
  • Portuguese is the official language, although English is spoken in larger tourist areas.
  • Tipping in restaurants is customary, and it’s around 10% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Far East

The best time to visit Far East is from May to September, which is the region’s dry season and perfect for outdoor activities. For those interested in experiencing the local culture, visiting during carnival season (February or March) is a must.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far East

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Learn a few Portuguese phrases.
  • Respect the culture and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear expensive jewelry or flashy accessories.
  • Flaunt large amounts of cash in public areas.
  • Trust strangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Far East

Far East is relatively safe; however, visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. It’s best to stay in well-lit and populated areas at night and avoid walking alone.

Health in Far East

Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations such as flu, measles, and chickenpox. Water-borne illnesses are common in Brazil, and visitors are advised to drink bottled or filtered water. It is also essential to protect yourself from mosquito-borne illnesses by wearing insect repellent and covering exposed skin.

Getting to Far East: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport
  • Congonhas-São Paulo Airport

Bus Stations Near Far East

  • Terminal Rodoviário Tietê
  • Terminal de Ônibus Jabaquara

Other Options

  • Car rental - this is a popular option for visitors who want to explore the region independently.
  • Taxis - taxis are widely available but can be expensive depending on the destination.

How to Get Around in Far East

The best way to get around Far East is by car or taxi; however, public transport is also available, including buses and trains. Visitors can purchase a rechargeable transport card that is valid for most of the public transport systems in São Paulo.

Where to Go Next from Far East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santos - A coastal city known for its picturesque beaches and historic center.
  • Paranapiacaba - A small, charming village that is a popular hiking and ecotourism destination.
  • São Paulo - The largest city in Brazil and home to various attractions such as museums, restaurants, and clubs.

Conclusion

Far East is a fantastic destination that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, nature, and adventure. This comprehensive travel guide has provided an overview of the top attractions, activities, and logistics visitors need to know when planning a trip to Far East. Explore the stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and local cuisine, and experience the vibrant culture of this beautiful region of São Paulo, Brazil.
