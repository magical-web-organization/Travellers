Introduction to Guarulhos

The city of Guarulhos is located in the state of São Paulo, Brazil. Despite being one of the largest cities in the country, it is often overlooked by tourists in favour of other destinations in the region. However, those who decide to visit Guarulhos are rewarded with a rich cultural heritage, sprawling natural attractions and a bustling culinary scene.

List of Places to Visit in Guarulhos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most important historical landmarks to visit in Guarulhos is the Adamastor Pimentas Fort, a military fort dating back to the 18th century. The Fort is set amongst idyllic grounds, perfect for a picnic or stroll.

The Padre Bento Theatre, located in the centre of Guarulhos, is a historical and cultural landmark. This impressive theatre boasts a Neo-Classical façade and hosts various productions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Guarulhos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Serra da Cantareira State Park, located to the North of Guarulhos, is a sprawling natural reserve home to over 300 species of birds and numerous waterfalls. The park offers hiking and biking trails, making it an ideal spot for outdoor adventurers.

Another must-visit spot for nature-lovers is the Cabuçu Municipal Park. This park is set within an area of over 100 hectares and features a variety of trails and paths, perfect for hiking or cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guarulhos

One of the best hiking trails in Guarulhos is the Guaru-Parque Ecological Park. This park has over 16,000m² of green space, including areas of Atlantic Rainforest. Visitors can follow the trails through the forest and admire the views from the highest point in the park.

Cyclists should make their way to the Ciclovia Recreational Path, which winds its way through the city centre and into the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Guarulhos: Activities and Tours

For visitors looking for an immersive cultural experience, a visit to the Guarulhos Arts Centre is highly recommended. The centre hosts a range of exhibitions and performances throughout the year, showcasing local and international talent.

The Guarulhos Zoo is a must-visit for families. The zoo is home to over 1000 animals, both native and from around the globe. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore the exhibits on their own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Guarulhos

Day 1: Start your cultural immersion with a trip to the Adamastor Pimentas Fort.

Day 2: Spend a day exploring the natural beauty of the Serra da Cantareira State Park.

Day 3: Visit the Guarulhos Arts Centre for an afternoon of cultural entertainment.

Day 4: Discover the wonders of the Cabuçu Municipal Park.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Guarulhos Zoo.

Day 6: Spend a day exploring the city centre, including the Padre Bento Theatre.

Day 7: Explore the Ciclovia Recreational Path and take in the stunning natural surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Guarulhos

Must-Try Dishes

One of the most iconic dishes in Guarulhos is the traditional Brazilian dish, feijoada. This hearty dish is made with black beans, meat, and spices and is often served with rice.

For breakfast, try acai bowls, a local favourite. These bowls are made with frozen acai and topped with fresh fruits and granola.

Local Beverages

The most popular drink in Guarulhos is cachaça, a Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane. Visitors can also try a range of local craft beers or sample some of the delicious fruit juices on offer.

Where to Stay in Guarulhos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For visitors looking for luxury, the Slavieiro Suites Guarulhos is an excellent choice. This hotel boasts spacious suites and a range of amenities including a rooftop pool.

For those looking for budget-friendly options, the Hostel na Casa dos Amigos is a great choice. This cosy hostel offers a range of shared and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and living areas.

What to Buy in Guarulhos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

One of the most popular souvenirs to bring home from Guarulhos is locally-made handicrafts. These can be found at the Guarulhos Artisan Market, where a range of textiles, pottery and woodwork can be purchased.

Visitors can also purchase a range of locally-sourced coffee and chocolates, perfect for bringing home as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Guarulhos

The people of Guarulhos are known for their warmth and hospitality. Visitors can expect to be greeted with a smile and a friendly welcome wherever they go.

In terms of customs, Brazilians tend to dress informally but conservatively. Visitors should dress modestly and avoid overt displays of wealth.

Best Time to Visit Guarulhos

The best time to visit Guarulhos is between March and May or between September and November. During these months, the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guarulhos

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Learn some Portuguese phrases
  • Be respectful of local customs

Don’t:

  • Carry large amounts of cash
  • Wear expensive jewellery or watches
  • Ignore basic safety precautions

Safety and Health

Safety in Guarulhos

Guarulhos is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables and avoiding dark or unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Guarulhos

Visitors should ensure that they are up to date with routine vaccinations before travelling to Guarulhos. Dengue fever and Zika virus are present in some areas of Brazil, including Guarulhos. Visitors should take measures to avoid mosquito bites, such as using insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to Guarulhos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Guarulhos

The São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport is the largest airport in Brazil and is located approximately 25km from Guarulhos city centre.

Bus Stations Near Guarulhos

There are several bus stations in Guarulhos, including the Guarulhos Bus Terminal and the Cumbica Bus Station. Buses from these stations connect to destinations throughout Brazil.

Other Options

Visitors can take a taxi or hire a private car to travel to or from Guarulhos.

How to Get Around in Guarulhos

The most convenient way to get around Guarulhos is by taxi or rideshare service. Alternatively, visitors can use the city’s bus system or hire a bike to explore independently.

Where to Go Next from Guarulhos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

São Paulo, the largest city in Brazil, is located just 30 minutes from Guarulhos by car or train and is an obvious choice for a day trip.

Alternatively, visitors can head to the coast to visit the beautiful beaches of Santos or explore the charming colonial city of Paraty.

Conclusion

Guarulhos is a vibrant and culturally-rich destination that offers visitors an authentic Brazilian experience. From natural wonders to historical landmarks, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this dynamic city. By following this travel guide, visitors are sure to have a memorable and unforgettable experience in Guarulhos.
