Introduction to São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo is a city located in the southeast of Brazil, in the state of São Paulo, and is part of the Greater São Paulo metropolitan area. Founded in 1812, the city has a rich history, and today it is known for its strong economy, beautiful parks, and vibrant cultural scene.

List of Places to Visit in São Bernardo do Campo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of São Bernardo do Campo is its vast cultural heritage, which can be seen in its excellent museums and historic sites. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Pinacoteca de São Bernardo do Campo: This museum houses a collection of contemporary art from local and international artists.
  • Parque Estoril: This park combines history, art, and nature in a beautiful setting. It features a historic house that has been converted into a museum, gardens, and a lake.
  • Casa do Olhar Luiz Sacilotto: This museum is dedicated to the work of the artist Luiz Sacilotto, a pioneer of concrete art in Brazil.

Natural Attractions of São Bernardo do Campo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy the many parks and natural attractions in and around São Bernardo do Campo. Here are some of the best:

  • Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar: This park is situated on the Atlantic coast and has an impressive biodiversity, with many rare and endangered species. The park also offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Parque Natural Municipal Estoril: Located in the heart of the city, this park has well-manicured gardens, lakes, and trails. It’s the perfect place for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.
  • Represa Billings: This large reservoir offers boating, fishing, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo has many hiking and biking trails for those who enjoy outdoor activities. Here are some popular routes:

  • Caminho do Mar: This historic trail was once the main road linking the port of Santos to São Paulo. Today, it’s a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the coast and the surrounding mountains.
  • Parque da Juventude: This park has a cycling path that winds through its lush greenery, making it perfect for a scenic bike ride.
  • Trilha do Manancial: This hiking trail takes you through the lush forests of the Billings Reservoir, one of the largest reservoirs in the world, where you can observe a diversity of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in São Bernardo do Campo: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do and see in São Bernardo do Campo, from cultural tours to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best:

  • Brewery Tour: São Bernardo do Campo is home to many craft breweries, and a tour of one of them is a great way to relax and taste some locally made beer.
  • City Tour: This guided tour takes you through the historic center of São Bernardo do Campo, where you can see some of the city’s most important landmarks, including the Municipal Palace and the Church of Nossa Senhora da Boa Viagem.
  • Voo Livre: For those who are daring enough, a tandem paragliding flight over the city provides breathtaking views and an adrenaline rush.

Week-Long Itinerary for São Bernardo do Campo

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to São Bernardo do Campo:

Day 1: Visit the Pinacoteca de São Bernardo do Campo and enjoy lunch at one of the city’s many restaurants.

Day 2: Take a guided city tour and explore the historic center of São Bernardo do Campo.

Day 3: Spend a day in Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar, exploring the park and its many trails.

Day 4: Visit Casa do Olhar Luiz Sacilotto to see the work of this celebrated artist, and then go for a relaxing picnic in Parque Natural Municipal Estoril.

Day 5: Go for a brewery tour, tasting some of the best locally made beers.

Day 6: Experience the thrills of a tandem paragliding flight over the city.

Day 7: Head to the Billings Reservoir for a day of boating, fishing, and relaxation.

Food and Drink Scene of São Bernardo do Campo

Must-Try Dishes

São Bernardo do Campo has a diverse food scene, with many traditional Brazilian dishes and international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Feijoada: A classic Brazilian dish made with black beans, pork, and beef.
  • Churrasco: Brazilian-style grilled meat served with rice, beans, and farofa.
  • Pão de Queijo: A type of cheese bread that is a staple of Brazilian cuisine.

Local Beverages

In terms of beverages, Brazil is known for its coffee and caipirinha, a cocktail made with cachaca, sugar, and lime. São Bernardo do Campo is home to many breweries, where you can sample locally made beers.

Where to Stay in São Bernardo do Campo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

São Bernardo do Campo offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some notable options include:

  • Hotel Pampas: A centrally located hotel with modern amenities and great views of the city.
  • Comfort Hotel São Bernardo do Campo: Another centrally located hotel with a fitness center and an outdoor pool.
  • Hostel Casa Branca: A budget-friendly option located in a charming historic house in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in São Bernardo do Campo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

São Bernardo do Campo is known for its craftsmanship, and there are many local products that make great souvenirs. Some popular options include:

  • Wood carvings: The city has many artisans who specialize in wooden toys, tools, and decorations.
  • Ceramics: The local ceramics are of high quality and come in a wide variety of styles.
  • Clothing and accessories: São Bernardo do Campo is known for its leather goods, such as boots, belts, and bags.

Local Culture and Customs of São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo is a welcoming and friendly city, with a strong cultural heritage. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Greetings: Brazilians are known for their warm and friendly greetings, often with a kiss on the cheek.
  • Dress code: While there is no specific dress code, it is considered impolite to wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Brazil, but it is customary to leave a small amount for good service.

Best Time to Visit São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo has a tropical climate, with warm temperatures year-round. The best time to visit is between May and September, during the dry season, when temperatures are cooler and there is less rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of São Bernardo do Campo

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Learn a few phrases in Portuguese to better communicate with the locals.
  • Practice sun safety and use sunscreen when outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Display valuables, such as cameras, phones, or jewelry, in public.
  • Drink tap water, instead opt to drink bottled water.

Safety and Health

Safety in São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo is a safe city to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in São Bernardo do Campo

Health care facilities in São Bernardo do Campo are of high quality, with many private hospitals and clinics. However, it’s always best to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to São Bernardo do Campo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near São Bernardo do Campo

The closest airport to São Bernardo do Campo is São Paulo–Congonhas Airport (CGH), located about 20 km away. Another option is São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport (GRU), which is about 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo has two bus terminals, Terminal de Ônibus Ferrazópolis and Terminal de Ônibus São Bernardo, both of which offer connections to other cities in Brazil.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services are widely available in São Bernardo do Campo and are a convenient way to get around the city. Car rentals are also available at the airport.

How to Get Around in São Bernardo do Campo

São Bernardo do Campo has an efficient public transportation system, with buses serving most parts of the city. There are also plenty of taxis and ride-sharing options available.

Where to Go Next from São Bernardo do Campo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

São Bernardo do Campo is well-connected to many other interesting destinations in Brazil, such as:

  • São Paulo: The largest city in Brazil, known for its vibrant culture and nightlife.
  • Guarujá: A beach town with a relaxed atmosphere, located about 50 km from São Bernardo do Campo.
  • Santos: A historic coastal city that is home to the largest coffee export port in the world.

Conclusion

São Bernardo do Campo is a hidden gem in Brazil, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoors enthusiast, or a foodie, São Bernardo do Campo is a destination not to be missed.
