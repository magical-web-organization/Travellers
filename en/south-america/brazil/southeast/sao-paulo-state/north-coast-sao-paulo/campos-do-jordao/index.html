Introduction to Campos do Jordão

Nestled in the North Coast of São Paulo, the charming town of Campos do Jordão is one of Brazil’s most breathtaking destinations. Known as the “Swiss Alps of Brazil” for its Alpine-style architecture, stunning mountain views, and tranquil surroundings, Campos do Jordão is a top choice for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Campos do Jordão

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Campos do Jordão boasts a rich cultural heritage, and visitors will find a variety of fascinating museums and historical landmarks throughout the town. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Felicia Leirner Museum: this open-air museum sits on a sprawling park and showcases the works of Brazilian sculptor Felicia Leirner.
  • Casa da Xilogravura: a unique museum that features the art of woodcutting, a popular craft in Brazil.
  • Boa Vista Palace: this stunning palace-turned-museum offers a glimpse into the region’s history and was once the summer residence of the governor of São Paulo.

Natural Attractions of Campos do Jordão: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will be in awe of the natural beauty found in and around Campos do Jordão. From scenic hikes to breathtaking views, there’s something for everyone. Some of the top outdoor attractions include:

  • Horto Florestal: a beautiful park that is perfect for hiking and picnicking.
  • Pedra do Baú: this iconic rock formation is a favorite among climbers and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Ducha de Prata: a gorgeous waterfall that is a popular spot for visitors to relax and take in the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campos do Jordão

Campos do Jordão is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with miles of scenic trails and quiet roads to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Pedra do Baú Trail: this challenging hike takes you up to the top of Pedra do Baú and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Estrada do Horto Trail: a scenic route that winds through the stunning countryside around Campos do Jordão. Bikers will love the quiet roads and picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in Campos do Jordão: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Campos do Jordão. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Chocolate tasting tours
  • Horseback riding through the countryside
  • Wine tasting at local vineyards
  • Hot air balloon rides

Week-Long Itinerary for Campos do Jordão

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Campos do Jordão, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore the charming town center and visit the Casa da Xilogravura museum.
2 Take a scenic hike through the Horto Florestal park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

5 Take a hot air balloon ride over the stunning mountains of Campos do Jordão.
6 Embark on a challenging hike up to the top of Pedra do Baú for breathtaking views of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Campos do Jordão

Campos do Jordão may be a small town, but it’s home to a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trutas (trout): a local fish dish that is typically served with a side of vegetables and rice.
  • Pinhão (pine nut): a traditional Brazilian snack that is made from roasted pine nuts.
  • Fondue: a Swiss-style dish that is popular in Campos do Jordão, particularly during the colder months.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: a popular Brazilian liquor that is made from sugarcane.
  • Vinho Gourd: a delicious wine that is produced locally and is a must-try for any wine connoisseur.

Where to Stay in Campos do Jordão: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Campos do Jordão has a variety of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget. Some popular choices include:

  • Hotel Toriba: a luxurious five-star hotel that offers stunning mountain views and exceptional service.
  • Pousada Villa Tambo: a charming guesthouse that is located in a tranquil spot in the countryside.
  • Hostel Alto da Boa Vista: a budget-friendly option that is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Campos do Jordão: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from your trip to Campos do Jordão, here are some top choices:

  • Handcrafted wood carvings and sculptures
  • Locally produced wine and cachaça
  • Traditional Brazilian clothing and accessories

Local Culture and Customs of Campos do Jordão

Campos do Jordão is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors should feel free to engage with locals and learn more about their way of life. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Brazilians are generally very warm and hospitable, and visitors should reciprocate this kindness.
  • Portuguese is the official language spoken in Brazil, though English is spoken in many tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit Campos do Jordão

The best time to visit Campos do Jordão is during the winter months (June-August), when the town is at its busiest and there are plenty of winter activities to enjoy. However, visitors can enjoy the town’s beautiful scenery and outdoor activities year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campos do Jordão

Do:

  • Tip restaurant servers around 10%.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable shoes, especially if you plan on hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Use your phone while driving.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Campos do Jordão is a safe destination for tourists, though visitors should exercise caution and take basic safety measures to avoid problems. Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Campos do Jordão:

Safety in Campos do Jordão

  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas after dark.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings when in public places.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services to get around town.

Health in Campos do Jordão

  • The town has several hospitals and medical clinics in case of emergency.
  • Avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water or filtered water.
  • Protect yourself against mosquito bites, as the region is known to have instances of dengue fever.

Getting to Campos do Jordão: Transportation Options and Logistics

Campos do Jordão is located roughly 2.5 hours away from São Paulo by car, making it an easy day trip or weekend getaway. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Campos do Jordão

  • São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport (GRU) is the closest major airport to Campos do Jordão, located around 2.5 hours away by car.
  • Viracopos International Airport (VCP) is an alternative option, located around 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Campos do Jordão

  • The town has a bus station located in the center of town, with regular connections to São Paulo and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a popular option for tourists and gives you more flexibility in terms of exploring the surrounding countryside and nearby attractions.

How to Get Around Campos do Jordão

Campos do Jordão is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Taxis are also readily available for longer distances, and ride-sharing services like Uber are also an option.

Where to Go Next from Campos do Jordão: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more of Brazil after your visit to Campos do Jordão, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • São Paulo: Brazil’s largest city is just a few hours away and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, nightlife, and dining options.
  • Ilhabela: a stunning island located off the coast of São Paulo that is known for its gorgeous beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Parque Nacional da Serra da Canastra: a breathtaking nature reserve that is home to waterfalls, rock formations, and a variety of unique wildlife.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a bit of both, Campos do Jordão is the perfect destination to escape and unwind. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, it’s no wonder that this town has become one of Brazil’s premiere travel destinations.
