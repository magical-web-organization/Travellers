Introduction to Ubatuba

Tucked away in the northern coast of São Paulo, Ubatuba is a hidden gem of Brazil that offers a unique mix of culture, nature, and outdoor adventures. Located in the heart of the Atlantic forest, Ubatuba is blessed with over 100 beaches, stunning waterfalls, and breathtaking trekking trails that will leave you awestruck.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore all the amazing things to do and see in Ubatuba, from its culturally important attractions to its outdoor adventures and scenic views. We’ll also provide tips on how to get around, where to stay, what to eat, and more. So sit back, relax, and get ready to plan your perfect journey to Ubatuba.

List of Places to Visit in Ubatuba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a culture buff, Ubatuba has plenty of options for you to explore:

  • Sobrados e Barcos Museum: This museum is ideal for visitors who want to learn more about the history of Ubatuba and its people. Located in the Downtown area, Sobrados e Barcos Museum has an impressive collection of photographs, documents, and artifacts related to the city’s past.

  • Anchieta Museum: Dedicated to the life and work of Saint Anchieta, this museum offers a glimpse of the life of one of Brazil’s most revered saints. The museum is located in the city center and includes exhibitions of artwork, historic objects, and manuscripts in honor of Saint Anchieta.

  • Taubaté-Ubatuba Railway Station Museum: Formerly a railway station, this museum showcases the history of Ubatuba’s train line. It’s definitely a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Ubatuba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, Ubatuba won’t disappoint:

  • Praia da Fazenda: This beach is a perfect spot for surfing and hiking. Surrounded by nature, it offers white sands, crystal-clear waters, and stunning views of the Atlantic forest.

  • Ilha Anchieta State Park: This park covers an area of 828 hectares and has an abundance of hiking trails, waterfalls, beaches, and a range of wildlife. Wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers should not miss this attraction.

  • Tamar Project: This project aims to protect the endangered Loggerhead turtle species. Visitors can learn about the lifecycle of turtles and even see baby turtles making their way to the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ubatuba

For those who love hiking and biking, Ubatuba won’t disappoint:

  • Pico do Corcovado: This 1,180-meter high mountain offers one of the most beautiful views of Ubatuba. Trekking up to the summit is a must-do activity.

  • Saco das Bananas Trail: This 9 km trail offers stunning views of the sea and the banana plantations. It’s a great combination of hiking and history as it passes through the ruins of the old gold mines and colonial towns.

  • Caminhos de Ouro Trail: This trail allows you to travel back in time and discover Ubatuba’s colonial past. From the Atlantic forest to the waterfalls, this adventurous trail will take you through the gold-mining region of Serra do Mar.

Best Things to Do in Ubatuba: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Ubatuba:

  • Beach hopping: Ubatuba has over 100 beaches to explore. Hire a car or motorbike and discover the charms of each one.

  • Snorkeling: The crystal clear waters of Ubatuba offer a great opportunity to snorkel and observe various species of marine life.

  • Boat Tours: Hop on a boat and explore the hidden gem of Ilha Anchieta, Praia do Bonete and other places.

  • Try your hand at surfing: With plenty of surf schools and calm waters, Ubatuba is an ideal place for surfing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ubatuba

Day 1: Explore Downtown and visit Sobrados e Barcos Museum and Tamoios indigenous village. Day 2: Hike to Pico do Corcovado and enjoy the panoramic views from its summit. Day 3: Visit Ilha Anchieta to see the ruins of an old prison and explore the trails, beaches and the beautiful underwater world. Day 4: Relax on the stunning Praia Vermelha do Sul. Go snorkeling and kayaking at this gorgeous beach. Day 5: Take a boat trip to Praia do Bonete, one of the most beautiful beaches in Ubatuba, and hike to the waterfall there. Day 6: Visit Taubaté-Ubatuba Railway Station Museum and enjoy mountain biking on the trails around it. Day 7: Spend the day on Praia da Fazenda and enjoy surfboarding.

Food and Drink Scene of Ubatuba

Must-Try Dishes

Ubatuba boasts a unique cuisine that blends both seafood and traditional Brazilian dishes. Here are some dishes you must try while in Ubatuba:

  • Moqueca de Peixe: A traditional Brazilian fish stew with coconut milk, tomatoes, peppers, and cilantro.

  • Caiçara Fish Stew: A fish stew of indigenous origin, made by the natives of the region and flavored with local herbs.

  • Banana Pie Ubatuba-style: A typical dessert made with locally harvested bananas.

Local Beverages

Ubatuba is popular for its “cachaça” (sugar cane liquor) and beer:

  • Cachaça Havana: This is the most popular cachaça in Ubatuba, made from fermented sugarcane juice.

  • Ubatuba Craft Beer: A unique beer with local flavors like coffee and acai berry. Brewery Mistura Clássica has a great selection of local beers to try.

Where to Stay in Ubatuba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ubatuba has a variety of accommodation options to choose from:

  • Hotel Porto do Eixo: A beachfront hotel that offers stunning views of the sea and a range of amenities like a pool, bar, and a 24-hour reception desk.

  • Pousada Villa Tenorio: A guesthouse that offers both privacy and tranquility, located near Praia do Tenório.

  • Hostel Refuge da Praia: A budget-friendly hostel that caters to all types of travelers and is centrally located.

What to Buy in Ubatuba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cachaca Havana: Take a bottle of the local cachaça back home with you.

  • Handicrafts: The local artisans of Ubatuba create beautiful handicrafts using traditional techniques and natural materials. You can find great souvenirs in the shops, kiosks, and markets throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Ubatuba

Ubatuba’s history, traditions, and customs are deeply rooted in its indigenous and colonial past. To respect the local culture, try to follow these customs:

  • Avoid wearing revealing clothes while in the city center.

  • Follow the customs and rituals of the indigenous village.

  • Take off his shoes when entering an indigenous house.

Best Time to Visit Ubatuba

The best time to visit Ubatuba is from April to September when the weather is dry and pleasant. The sea is calm during these months, so it’s ideal for outdoor activities and water sports like surfing, snorkeling, and paddleboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ubatuba

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Do explore the various beaches and nature trails that Ubatuba offers.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter the beaches or waters.

  • Don’t leave valuables in your car or visible in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ubatuba

Like any other tourist destination, Ubatuba also has its fair share of petty crime. However, it’s relatively safe to travel if you follow some basic safety precautions:

  • Avoid wandering around the city center late at night.

  • Stay away from deserted areas and keep an eye out for your valuables.

Health in Ubatuba

  • Drink bottled water.

  • Be prepared for mosquito bites.

Bus Stations Near Ubatuba

  • São Paulo

Other Options

  • Take a taxi or private transfer.

How to Get Around in Ubatuba

The best way to get around Ubatuba is by car or motorbike. Public transportation is limited so hiring a car or motorbike is highly recommended.

Where to Go Next from Ubatuba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations from Ubatuba that are worth visiting:

  • Paraty: A charming colonial town that’s only a short drive away from Ubatuba.

  • Ilhabela: An island filled with unspoiled natural beauty, just a ferry ride away from Ubatuba.

Conclusion

Ubatuba is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those looking for a cultural experience. With its pristine beaches, breathtaking trails, and rich history, Ubatuba is a must-visit destination in Brazil. We hope this travel guide helps you plan your perfect journey to Ubatuba. Don’t forget to pack your sunscreen, hiking boots, and adventurous spirit for your trip to this paradise on earth.
