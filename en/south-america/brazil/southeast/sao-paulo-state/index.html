Introduction to São Paulo (State)

São Paulo (State) is a vibrant and bustling region filled with cultural landmarks, natural beauty, and lively cities. The state is located in Southeast Brazil and is the most populous area of the country. São Paulo is known for its diversified economy, cultural richness, hospitality, and food. With so much to explore and learn, it’s no wonder millions of travelers visit São Paulo (State) every year.

If you’re planning a trip to this incredible region, read our comprehensive guide for tips on exploring the culture, attractions, food, and logistics of São Paulo (State).

Week-Long Itinerary for São Paulo (State)

Here’s a suggested itinerary that covers some of the best places to visit in São Paulo (State) in a week:

  • Day 1: Explore the São Paulo Museum of Art, Banespa Building, and Municipal Theatre.
  • Day 2: Take a wine tour in São Roque.
  • Day 3: Go trekking in Pedra Grande, Serra do Mar State Park, or Campos do Jordão.
  • Day 4: Visit Pinacoteca de São Paulo, São Paulo Independence Park, and Museum of the Portuguese Language.
  • Day 5: Go shopping in Jardins neighborhood and explore the traditional Brazilian markets.
  • Day 6: Visit Ilhabela or Serra da Mantiqueira for hiking, biking, or simply relax at the beach.
  • Day 7: Attend a football game or a local music event to understand local culture and customs.

Local Culture and Customs of São Paulo (State)

São Paulo (State) is a melting pot of cultures with Portuguese, Italian, and African influences being one of the most notable ones. The area also has a large number of Japanese people and stores, due to migration waves during the past century. Brazilians are generally welcoming and friendly, so practice basic manners, including greeting the people you meet and saying thank you.

Best Time to Visit São Paulo (State)

The best time to visit São Paulo (State) is between March and May or August and November when the weather conditions are pleasant and there is plenty of sunshine. The summertime (December to February) is usually humid and has frequent rain showers.

Do:

Safety and Health

Safety in São Paulo (State)

São Paulo (State) is a relatively safe place for travelers, but some areas can be unsafe, especially at night. Avoid isolated areas at night and take care of your belongings. It’s also a good idea to use official taxis or transportation apps instead of hailing a taxi on the street.

Health in São Paulo (State)

São Paulo (State) has excellent hospitals and doctors, and travelers should take basic precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It’s also advisable to drink bottled water, and visitors should take precautions against the Zika virus if they are of childbearing age or pregnant.

Getting to São Paulo (State): Transportation Options and Logistics

São Paulo (State) has two major airports: Congonhas-São Paulo Airport (domestic) and São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport. The region has excellent public transportation links, including buses and trains, making it easy to get around the cities and towns in the state.

Airports Near São Paulo (State)

  • São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport (GRU)
  • Congonhas-São Paulo Airport (CGH)

Bus Stations Near São Paulo (State)

  • Tietê Bus Terminal
  • Barra Funda Bus Terminal
  • Jabaquara Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Car rental
  • Private driving services

How to Get Around in São Paulo (State)

São Paulo (State) has a well-developed public transportation network, including metro lines, buses, and trains. Renting a car may be a good idea for exploring the rural areas of the state, but navigating the busy city roads can be difficult during peak traffic hours. Taxis and ride-sharing apps are also widely available.
