Introduction to Aracruz

Aracruz is a charming city in the North Coast of Espírito Santo, in southeastern Brazil. This hidden gem is surrounded by lush forests, pristine beaches, and crystal-clear rivers, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. In addition to its natural attractions, Aracruz is also rich in history and culture, with several museums and historical monuments that showcase its past. Whether you want to hike in the jungle, surf in the ocean, or explore the city’s cultural heritage, Aracruz has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Aracruz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu do Artesanato: This museum showcases the traditional crafts of Aracruz, including weaving, pottery, and wood-carving. Visitors can watch artisans at work, learn about the history of local crafts, and purchase unique souvenirs to take home.
  • Igreja Matriz São João Batista: The main church of Aracruz dates back to the 17th century and is one of the most beautiful examples of colonial architecture in the region. Its Baroque-style façade and richly decorated interior are a must-see for history buffs and architecture lovers alike.
  • Casa da Cultura: This cultural center hosts exhibitions, workshops, and concerts that showcase the art and culture of Aracruz. Visitors can admire local artwork, participate in traditional dance and music performances, and learn about the city’s cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Aracruz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia dos Padres: This secluded beach is surrounded by cliffs and jungle, offering a breathtaking view of the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can swim, hike, and relax on the pristine white sand, far from the crowds and noise of the city.
  • Parque Natural Municipal do Aricanga: This park is a paradise for nature lovers, with trails that lead through lush forests, rivers, and waterfalls. Visitors can hike, bike, and bird-watch, and enjoy the fresh air and tranquility of the wilderness.
  • Rio Piraquê-Açu: This river is one of the most beautiful and pristine in Brazil, with crystal-clear water and dense vegetation. Visitors can kayak, canoe, and swim, and enjoy the stunning scenery and wildlife of the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aracruz

Aracruz offers several hiking and biking trails that lead through its forests, parks, and beaches. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Trilha dos Escravos: This trail leads through the jungle and the ruins of an old sugar mill, providing a glimpse into the history of slavery in the region.
  • Trilha do Chocolate: This trail leads through a cocoa plantation and includes a visit to a chocolate factory, where visitors can learn about the production process and sample the delicious local chocolate.
  • Trilha da Costa: This trail leads along the coast, offering stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the surrounding cliffs and beaches.

Best Things to Do in Aracruz: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: Aracruz is known for its excellent surfing conditions, with waves that are suitable for both beginners and experienced surfers. Visitors can take surfing lessons from local experts and enjoy the thrill of riding the waves.
  • Whale Watching: Aracruz is one of the best places in Brazil to observe humpback whales in their natural habitat. From June to October, visitors can take boat tours that will take them close to these magnificent creatures.
  • Kayak Tours: Aracruz’s rivers and lagoons offer a perfect opportunity for kayaking and exploring the surrounding natural beauty. Visitors can take guided kayak tours and discover the hidden gems of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aracruz

Day 1: Arrive in Aracruz and check into your accommodation. Visit the Museu do Artesanato and buy some local crafts.

Day 2: Hike the Trilha dos Escravos and learn about the history of slavery in the region.

Day 3: Take a surfing lesson and catch some waves at Praia de Santa Cruz.

Day 4: Explore the Parque Natural Municipal do Aricanga and hike its trails.

Day 5: Take a boat tour to observe humpback whales in their natural habitat.

Day 6: Visit the Casa da Cultura and attend a traditional music and dance performance.

Day 7: Kayak on the Rio Piraquê-Açu and enjoy the peaceful beauty of the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Aracruz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca Capixaba: This traditional seafood stew is a specialty of Espírito Santo and a must-try for foodies. Made with fish, onion, tomatoes, cilantro, and palm oil, it is rich and flavorful.
  • Feijão Tropeiro: This hearty dish is made with beans, pork, sausage, eggs, and farofa (toasted cassava flour) and is a favorite among locals.
  • Queijo de Minas: This soft, fresh cheese is a staple of Brazilian cuisine and is a perfect accompaniment to bread, crackers, or fruits.

Local Beverages

  • Cerveja Artesanal: Aracruz is home to several microbreweries that produce delicious craft beers, using local ingredients and traditional brewing methods.
  • Cachaça: This distilled spirit is made from sugarcane juice and is a popular drink in Brazil. Aracruz is known for its high-quality cachaça, which is often flavored with ginger, cinnamon, or fruits.
  • Coco Verde: This refreshing drink is made from the juice of young green coconuts and is a perfect way to beat the heat in Aracruz.

Where to Stay in Aracruz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada Sonho Meu: This charming guesthouse is located near Praia dos Padres and offers cozy rooms, a pool, and a restaurant that serves delicious homemade food.
  • Hotel Fazenda Flamboyant: This hotel is nestled in the heart of a natural reserve and offers rustic yet comfortable rooms, a restaurant that serves organic food, and several outdoor activities, such as horseback riding and hiking.
  • Pousada Chez Domaine: This guesthouse is located in the countryside of Aracruz and offers spacious rooms, a pool, and a restaurant that serves gourmet cuisine.

What to Buy in Aracruz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artesanato: The Museu do Artesanato is a perfect place to shop for local crafts, such as woven baskets, pottery, and wood-carvings.
  • Cachaça: Aracruz is known for its high-quality cachaça, which is often flavored with ginger, cinnamon, or fruits. Bring home a bottle of this delicious rum as a souvenir.
  • Cocoa and Chocolate: Aracruz is famous for its cocoa plantations and chocolate production. Visit the Trilha do Chocolate and buy some local chocolate to savor at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Aracruz

Aracruz is a friendly and welcoming city that values its cultural heritage and natural resources. The locals are proud of their traditions and are happy to share their heritage with visitors. Respect for nature, family, and community are core values in Aracruz, and visitors are expected to follow these principles as well.

Best Time to Visit Aracruz

The best time to visit Aracruz is from June to September, when the weather is dry and the temperatures are milder. However, Aracruz is a year-round destination, and each season has its own charm and attractions. Just be sure to bring sunscreen and insect repellent, as the city is located in a tropical region with high humidity and abundant wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aracruz

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the region and protect the environment.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the ecosystem.
  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs.
  • Engage in illegal or harmful activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aracruz

Aracruz is a safe city for travelers, but it is always wise to take basic precautions, such as avoiding empty streets at night, carrying valuables discreetly, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Aracruz

There are no major health risks in Aracruz, but visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Use insect repellent, wear long sleeves and pants, and avoid stagnant water. Tap water is safe to drink in Aracruz, but bottled water is also widely available.

Getting to Aracruz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aracruz

The nearest major airports to Aracruz are Vitória Airport (VIX) and São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport (GRU). From there, visitors can take a connection flight or a bus to Aracruz.

Bus Stations Near Aracruz

Aracruz has a bus station that connects it with major cities in Espírito Santo and neighboring states. The bus station is located in the city center and is easily accessible.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Aracruz from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Aracruz

Aracruz is a small city that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer trips or for those who prefer not to walk or ride a bike. Rental cars are also available for those who want to explore the surrounding region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Aracruz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vitória: This charming colonial city is the capital of Espírito Santo and offers beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and excellent restaurants and bars.
  • Praia da Costa: This lively beach town is known for its vibrant nightlife, excellent seafood, and stunning sunsets.
  • Pedra Azul: This mountain village is known for its scenic views, fresh air, and outdoor adventures such as hiking, horseback riding, and rock climbing.

Conclusion

Aracruz is a charming and underrated destination in southeastern Brazil, with a rich history, vibrant culture, and breathtaking natural beauty. Whether you want to swim in pristine beaches, hike in lush forests, or explore the city’s cultural heritage, Aracruz has something for everyone. Visit Aracruz and discover a hidden gem that will leave you with unforgettable memories and experiences.
