Introduction to São Mateus

Located in the North Coast region of the state of Espírito Santo, São Mateus is a hidden gem known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and tranquil vibe. The city is home to a diverse array of bird and animal species, as well as a fascinating history that dates back to the colonial period. With its stunning beaches, lush forests, and vibrant festivals, São Mateus is the perfect destination for those who want to explore the best of Brazil’s Southeast region.

List of Places to Visit in São Mateus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

São Mateus is home to a number of fascinating museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s past. Some of the must-visit attractions in this category include:

  • Sao Mateus City Museum - This museum showcases the history of São Mateus, from its early beginnings to its present day.
  • Igreja De São João Batista - Built in the 18th century, this church is an impressive example of colonial architecture.
  • Museu do Tropeiro - This museum tells the story of the “tropeiros”, or mule drivers, who were an important part of São Mateus’ economy in the past.

Natural Attractions of São Mateus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, then São Mateus won’t disappoint. The region is home to a number of beautiful natural attractions that are sure to take your breath away. Some of the highlights include:

  • Praia de Guriri - This stunning beach is the perfect place to soak up the sun and enjoy the crystal-clear waters.
  • Monte Aghá - This mountain offers incredible views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching.
  • Parque Natural Municipal Mata da Siriaco - This nature reserve is home to a variety of species, including monkeys and toucans, and offers a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of São Mateus

São Mateus is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the best trails in the area include:

  • Trilha do Mirante - This trail takes you through the Mata da Siriaco nature reserve to a lookout point with stunning views of the mountain range.
  • Trilha do Monte Aghá - This challenging hike takes you to the top of Monte Aghá for some breathtaking views.
  • Trilha do Caeté - This trail follows the coastline, offering views of the ocean and the surrounding vegetation.

Best Things to Do in São Mateus: Activities and Tours

São Mateus has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours, so you’ll never be bored. Some of the best things to do in the area include:

  • Go on a jeep tour - This is a great way to explore the surrounding areas and get off the beaten path.
  • Take a boat tour - Explore the coast and see the marine life up close on a boat tour.
  • Attend a festival - São Mateus is known for its vibrant festivals, including the Festa do Padroeiro and the Festa da Cidade.

## Week-Long Itinerary for São Mateus

If you’re planning to spend a week in São Mateus, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1 - Explore the city center and visit the São Mateus City Museum.
  • Day 2 - Take a jeep tour of the surrounding areas and visit some of the natural attractions.
  • Day 3 - Spend the day at Praia de Guriri, soaking up the sun and swimming in the ocean.
  • Day 4 - Take a boat tour and explore the coast.
  • Day 5 - Hike the Trilha do Mirante and take in the stunning views.
  • Day 6 - Attend a festival and experience the local culture.
  • Day 7 - Relax and enjoy your last day in São Mateus.

Food and Drink Scene of São Mateus

Must-Try Dishes

São Mateus is known for its delicious seafood dishes and traditional Brazilian cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include:

  • Moqueca capixaba - This is a traditional seafood stew that’s made with fish, peppers, onions, and tomatoes.
  • Feijão tropeiro - This is a hearty dish made with beans, bacon, sausage, and manioc flour.
  • Brigadeiros - These chocolate truffles are a popular Brazilian dessert.

Local Beverages

Don’t forget to try some of the local beverages while you’re in São Mateus. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Cachaça - This is a traditional Brazilian spirit that’s made from sugarcane juice.
  • Caipirinha - This cocktail is made with cachaça, lime juice, and sugar.
  • Guarana - This is a popular soft drink in Brazil that’s made from the guarana plant.

Where to Stay in São Mateus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

São Mateus has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to stay in the area include:

  • Rio Cricaré Palace Hotel - This hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Pousada Vila dos Corais - This guesthouse is located near Praia de Guriri and offers stunning views and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Hostel Itapemirim - This hostel is a great budget option that’s located close to the city center.

What to Buy in São Mateus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home something special from São Mateus, here are some great souvenir ideas:

  • Handwoven baskets - These baskets are made by local artisans and are perfect for storing fruits and veggies.
  • Cachaça - Bring home a bottle of this traditional Brazilian spirit to share with friends and family.
  • Handmade jewelry - São Mateus is known for its jewelry made from natural gemstones like amethyst and topaz.

Local Culture and Customs of São Mateus

São Mateus has a unique culture that’s influenced by its history and geography. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind while you’re in the area:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local wildlife and avoid littering.
  • Try to learn a few words of Portuguese to communicate with the locals.

Best Time to Visit São Mateus

The best time to visit São Mateus is from May to September, when the weather is dry and sunny. The city can get crowded during the summer months, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of São Mateus

Do:

  • Explore the natural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Attend a festival.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect religious sites or customs.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in São Mateus

São Mateus is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s important to take certain precautions. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables out of sight.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in São Mateus

There are no major health concerns in São Mateus, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Here are some tips to stay healthy while you’re in the area:

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Check with your doctor to see if any vaccinations are necessary.

Getting to São Mateus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near São Mateus

The closest airport to São Mateus is in Vitória, which is about 200 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to São Mateus.

Bus Stations Near São Mateus

São Mateus has a bus station that connects the city to other destinations in the region.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, São Mateus is accessible via the BR-101 highway.

How to Get Around in São Mateus

The best way to get around São Mateus is by car or taxi. There’s also a local bus system, but it can be unreliable.

Where to Go Next from São Mateus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations worth checking out:

  • Vitória - This coastal city is the capital of Espírito Santo and is known for its beaches and architecture.
  • Domingos Martins - This charming town is located in the mountainous region of Espírito Santo and is a popular destination for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Conceição da Barra - This beach town is located to the north of São Mateus and is known for its stunning beaches.

Conclusion

São Mateus may not be as well-known as some of Brazil’s other destinations, but it’s definitely worth a visit. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures, history and culture, or great food and drinks, you’ll find something to love in São Mateus. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this amazing destination.
