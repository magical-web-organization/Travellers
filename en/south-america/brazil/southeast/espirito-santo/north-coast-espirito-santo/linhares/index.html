Introduction to Linhares

Linhares is a charming city situated in North Coast Espírito Santo, in Southeast Brazil, South America. The city is known for its rich culture, historic architecture, and natural beauty. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Brazil, especially among nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Linhares

No trip to Linhares is complete without exploring the following top-rated attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Municipal de Historia e Tesouros Alberto Lamego: A fantastic museum showcasing the city’s rich history and treasures.
  • Igreja Matriz de São José: A beautiful 18th-century church with a stunning façade and elaborate interiors.
  • Caixa d Água: A unique water reservoir made of stone and brick, built in the 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Linhares: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural Municipal de Setiba: A vast natural park covering over 100 hectares, offering several hiking trails, cascading waterfalls, and scenic views of the Atlantic Coastal Forest.
  • Reserva Biológica de Duas Bocas: An ecologically rich reserve featuring numerous trails and natural pools for swimming.
  • Cachoeira do Rio Palmital: A picturesque waterfall nestled amid the lush greenery, perfect for picnics and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Linhares

Linhares is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering a range of trails suitable for all skill levels. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Trilha do Parque Setiba: A moderate level 4km trail through the Atlantic Forest with stunning views of the park’s lush vegetation, flora, and fauna.
  • Trilha da Pedra do Elefante: A challenging 6.2km trek to a massive stone formation resembling an elephant, offering breathtaking vistas of the forest.
  • Caminho do Vale: A 13km stretch of the river valley trail offering a mix of pristine waterfalls, ancient ruins, and views of the surrounding scenic hills.

Best Things to Do in Linhares: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Corrego Grande: A small village famous for traditional handicrafts, especially the hand-carved wooden furniture and decorative items.
  • Explore Setiba Park: Take a guided tour of the park and witness the beauty of nature up close. There are options for bird-watching, tree trekking for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Enjoy Beaches: Head to the Lagoa Nova beach, located about 35 km from Linhares, to relax and soak up the sun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Linhares

Day 1-2: Explore the historical sites in the city, including Museu Municipal de Historia e Tesouros Alberto Lamego, Igreja Matriz de São José and Caixa d Água.

Day 3-4: Venture out on a hike or bike ride in Parque Natural Municipal de Setiba or Reserva Biológica de Duas Bocas.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the surrounding areas of Linhares, including Corrego Grande and the nearby beaches.

Day 6-7: Try some local delicacies and relax, explore the town at your own pace before beginning your journey to nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Linhares

Linhares is best known for its seafood, cheese, and traditional cuisine influenced by African and indigenous cultures. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca Capixaba: A popular dish made with fresh fish stew cooked in coconut milk, tomatoes and onions.
  • Torta Capixaba: A delicious pie made with local fish and shellfish.
  • ​Queijo de Linhares: Popular cheese from the region that is incredibly soft and creamy.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice.
  • Café Capixaba: A unique blend of coffee beans grown in the nearby hills.

Where to Stay in Linhares: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Linhares offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget hostels to luxurious resorts. Some of the top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city include:

  • Bristol Easy Hotel - Linhares: A chic, budget-friendly hotel in the heart of the city.
  • Pousada Cidade Sol: A cozy, family-owned guesthouse situated near the beach.
  • Hotel Concha Palace: A luxurious, beachfront hotel offering top-notch amenities and breathtaking ocean views.

What to Buy in Linhares: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Furniture: Linhares is known for its exceptional wooden furniture crafted by local artisans.
  • Local cheeses: Bring back some of the famous Queijo de Linhares.
  • Artisanal Crafts: Look for the locally made handicrafts, especially the wooden artefacts and decorative items from Corrego Grande.

Local Culture and Customs of Linhares

Linhares is a culturally rich city, and visitors can witness its unique blend of African and indigenous cultures. It is essential to be respectful of the traditions and customs of the locals and dress appropriately while visiting religious or cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Linhares

The best time to visit Linhares is from March to December when the weather is pleasant and rainfall is scarce. However, during the summer months, the city can get crowded, so plan your itinerary accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Linhares

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and cheese.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Stay hydrated and take precautions against the sun.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch the wildlife or feed the animals.
  • Avoid littering and respect the environment.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal or harmful activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Linhares

Linhares is a relatively safe city to visit, but it’s essential to be cautious as petty thefts can occur. Avoid carrying large sums of cash and valuable items while exploring the city.

Health in Linhares

Linhares is a malaria-risk area, so it is essential to take necessary precautions and consult a doctor before your trip. Carry mosquito repellent and cover skin as mosquitoes in this area can carry diseases.

Getting to Linhares: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Linhares

The nearest airport to Linhares is the Eurico de Aguiar Salles International Airport in Vitória, located about 140 km away. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use public transport to reach Linhares.

Bus Stations Near Linhares

There are several bus stations in Linhares that connect the city to nearby towns and cities. The central bus station is Terminal Rodoviario de Linhares.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for rental cars, taxis, or ride-sharing services like Uber to reach and travel around Linhares.

How to Get Around in Linhares

Getting around in Linhares can be done by public transport, taxis, and rental cars, among other options. Public transport in the city comprises buses and vans. Taxis are readily available, but it is recommended to book them in advance. Visitors can also use ride-sharing services like Uber or rent a car to travel around the city and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Linhares: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Linhares is ideally located as a base for exploring other nearby tourist destinations like:

  • Vitória: The capital of Espírito Santo, famous for its beaches, historic architecture, and cultural heritage.
  • Pedra Azul: A mountain range famous for its blue rock formations and scenic trails, ideal for outdoor pursuits.
  • Guriri: A small beach town located about 60 km from Linhares, known for its vibrant nightlife, seafood, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Linhares, with its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious cuisine, is an excellent choice for travellers planning a refreshing escape from city life. Visit the city to enjoy the best in outdoor adventures, explore historical sites and indulge in some mouthwatering seafood. Start planning your trip to Linhares today!
