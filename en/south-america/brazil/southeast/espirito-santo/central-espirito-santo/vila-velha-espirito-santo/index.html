Introduction to Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Vila Velha Espírito Santo, located in Central Espírito Santo, Brazil, is a land of scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage. With its charming beaches, waterfalls, and hiking trails along with historical landmarks, it attracts various tourists looking for a peaceful retreat. Offering a blend of natural and cultural activities, Vila Velha is a must-visit destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Here is a list of all the must-visit places in Vila Velha Espírito Santo.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Convento da Penha: A 16th-century monastery set on a hilltop, surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Igreja Nossa Senhora do Rosário: A church which is a combination of baroque and colonial architectural styles.
  • Praça Duque de Caxias: The central square of Vila Velha, featuring historic landmarks, fountains, and benches.
  • Forte de São Francisco Xavier: A 16th-century fort, offering a glimpse into the past military architecture and scenic views of the ocean.

Natural Attractions of Vila Velha Espírito Santo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia da Costa: The perfect spot for sunbathing and swimming, featuring crystal-clear blue water.
  • Morro do Moreno: A hill that offers panoramic views of the city.
  • Pedra dos Dois Olhos: A rock formation that looks like two eyes and a hideout for the adventurous visitors.
  • Parque Estadual Paulo Cesar Vinha: The largest conservation area in Vila Velha, home to several wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vila Velha Espírito Santo

For adventure enthusiasts, Vila Velha has several trails for hiking and biking.

  • Trilha do Morro do Moreno: A hiking trail leading to the Morro do Moreno, offering scenic views of the city and wildlife spotting.
  • Parque da Prainha: A park with hiking and biking trails, and a natural pool for a refreshing swim.
  • Trilha do Jerônimo Monteiro: A trekking trail through dense jungle with waterfalls and natural pools.

Best Things to Do in Vila Velha Espírito Santo: Activities and Tours

When in Vila Velha Espírito Santo, here are some must-do activities and tours.

  • Visit to Fábrica de Chocolates Garoto: A tour to the Garoto Chocolate Factory, to learn about the rich Brazilian chocolate-making tradition.
  • Sunset at Convento da Penha: A breathtaking sight to behold, watching the orange sun setting over the ocean from the hilltop.
  • Ecotourism: A guided tour to explore the flora and fauna in Parque Estadual Paulo Cesar Vinha.
  • Surfing: Praia da Costa is a popular spot for surfing enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Here is a 7-day itinerary for you to explore Vila Velha Espírito Santo at its best.

Day Plan
1 Visit Convento da Penha, Mirante do Convento, and Praça Duque de Caxias
2 Go on a guided tour to Parque Estadual Paulo Cesar Vinha, followed by a visit to Forte de São Francisco Xavier
3 Visit the Igreja Nossa Senhora do Rosário and relax on Praia da Costa
4 Go trekking to Trilha Jerônimo Monteiro and explore the dense jungle
5 Visit Morro do Moreno, followed by surfing lessons at Praia da Costa
6 Take a tour of Fábrica de Chocolates Garoto and try different chocolate flavors
7 Spend a day relaxing at Pedra dos Dois Olhos followed by a sunset at Convento da Penha

Food and Drink Scene of Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Vila Velha Espírito Santo is a foodie’s paradise, offering a unique blend of the coastal and Brazilian culinary heritage.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moqueca Capixaba: A fish stew prepared with tomatoes, onions, coriander, garlic, oil, and Urucum seeds. It is served with rice and pirão.
  • Espiríto Santo’s Shrimp Bobó: A creamy shrimp curry served with cassava flour.
  • Caranguejo Crab: Fresh crab, cooked in clay pots and served with garlic, spices, and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A Brazilian distilled spirit made from sugarcane. The popular ones are Seleta, Reserva do Gerente, and Itinga.
  • Caipirinha: A cocktail made of cachaça, sugar, and lime.

Where to Stay in Vila Velha Espírito Santo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vila Velha has several options for accommodation, catering to all budgets and preferences.

  • Hotel Praia Sol: A beachfront hotel with rooms featuring sea views and a pool.
  • Pousada Praia do Ribeiro: A comfortable guesthouse with a fully-equipped kitchen.
  • Hostel Vale Verde: A budget-friendly hostel located in the central district of Vila Velha.

What to Buy in Vila Velha Espírito Santo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chocolates from Garoto: A variety of Brazilian chocolates, including milk and dark, or assorted boxes.
  • Crocheted Lacework: A handmade lacework with intricate designs, available in several shops.
  • Panelas de Barro: Handcrafted clay pots that are traditionally used to cook Brazilian dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Vila Velha Espírito Santo is a culture-rich destination.

  • Capixaba Traditions: The cultural and historical heritage of Espírito Santo, including music, dance, cuisine, and handicrafts.
  • Brazilian Festivities: The famous Brazilian festivals like carnival, Semana Santa, Festa Junina, and Natal are all celebrated with much enthusiasm in Vila Velha.

Best Time to Visit Vila Velha Espírito Santo

The best time to visit Vila Velha Espírito Santo is between April and June or August and October when there is less rainfall and pleasant temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to have a safe and enjoyable trip to Vila Velha Espírito Santo.

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to avoid any discomfort.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes, especially when in places of worship.
  • Photograph or film people without their consent.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vila Velha Espírito Santo

Overall, Vila Velha Espírito Santo is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is advisable to be cautious about petty crimes like pickpocketing, especially in crowded tourist spots.

Health in Vila Velha Espírito Santo

It is recommended to check your vaccination status before travelling to Vila Velha Espírito Santo. Make sure to carry a first aid kit and insect repellents to avoid any health complications.

Getting to Vila Velha Espírito Santo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vila Velha Espírito Santo

The closest airport to Vila Velha Espírito Santo is the Eurico de Aguiar Salles International Airport (VIX) in Vitória, which is around 16 km away.

Bus Stations Near Vila Velha Espírito Santo

The only bus station in Vila Velha Espírito Santo is the Terminal Rodoviário de Vila Velha. It is well-connected with other major cities in Brazil.

Other Options

You can also hire a taxi or rent a car to get around Vila Velha Espírito Santo.

How to Get Around in Vila Velha Espírito Santo

The best way to explore Vila Velha Espírito Santo is by private car or bike, as there are not many public transport options available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Vila Velha Espírito Santo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vitória: A vibrant coastal city, offering a wide range of local experiences, like cultural landmarks, scenic beaches, and recreational activities.
  • Guarapari: A beach town located 45 km south of Vila Velha, offering white-sand beaches, hot springs, and outdoor activities.
  • Domingos Martins: A mountainous region known for its scenic beauty and local farmers’ markets.

Conclusion

Vila Velha Espírito Santo is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a break from the city’s hustle. With its serene beaches, cultural landmarks, and unique cuisine, one can have a magical experience that will be hard to forget. Planning a trip to Vila Velha Espírito Santo is worth the effort, as it has something delightful to offer for everyone.
