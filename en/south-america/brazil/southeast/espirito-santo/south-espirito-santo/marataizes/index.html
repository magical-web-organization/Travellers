Introduction to Marataízes

Nestled in the southern region of Espírito Santo, Marataízes is a small coastal town that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural diversity. With its stunning beaches, abundant marine life, and hospitable locals, Marataízes is quickly becoming one of the most sought-after destinations in Brazil. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, go hiking in the mountains, or explore the local history and culture, Marataízes has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Marataízes

Here are some of the top places to visit in Marataízes:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Fishing: This museum showcases the fishing culture of the region and houses artifacts related to fishing tools and equipment.

  2. Church of Nossa Senhora da Penha: This historic church is a popular pilgrimage site and boasts a stunning view of the ocean.

  3. Casa da Cultura: This cultural center hosts exhibitions and events related to local art, music, and dance.

Natural Attractions of Marataízes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Praia dos Cações: This pristine beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports like surfing and stand-up paddleboarding.

  2. Montanhas Capixabas: These mountains offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and are a popular hiking and camping destination.

  3. Parque Municipal de Marataízes: This municipal park has trails for hiking and biking, as well as picnic areas and playgrounds for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marataízes

Marataízes offers many hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty, including:

  1. Trilha do Vale do Sol: This hiking trail winds through the mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Rota da Lagoinha: This easy bike trail passes through beautiful beaches and lagoons.

  3. Rota das Águas: This hiking trail follows a series of waterfalls and natural pools, making for a refreshing day trip.

Best Things to Do in Marataízes: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing tours: Take a guided boat tour to catch your own seafood dinner and learn about the local fishing culture.

  2. Scuba diving: Explore the underwater world of Marataízes and see colorful fish, coral reefs, and marine life.

  3. Stand-up paddleboarding: Paddle along the coast and enjoy the scenic views while getting a great workout.

Food and Drink Scene of Marataízes

Marataízes offers a variety of seafood dishes and local drinks to try during your visit. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Moqueca Capixaba: A traditional seafood stew made with fish, tomatoes, peppers, onions, and cilantro.

  2. Casquinha de Siri: A baked crab shell filled with creamy crab meat and bread crumbs.

  3. Frutos do Mar: A seafood platter that includes grilled shrimp, octopus, lobster, and fish.

Local Beverages

  1. Cachaça: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane, often used in cocktails.

  2. Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail made with cachaça, lime, sugar, and ice.

  3. Guarana Antarctica: A Brazilian soft drink made with guarana berries, which are native to the Amazon rainforest.

Where to Stay in Marataízes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marataízes has options for every budget and interest - from beachside resorts to cozy guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  1. Pousada Dois Irmãos: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Marataízes.

  2. Hotel Espadarte: A beachfront hotel that offers spacious rooms and a pool.

  3. Pousada da Barra: A charming guesthouse with a garden and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Marataízes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade crafts: Marataízes is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and weaving.

  2. Local seafood: Take home some fresh fish and shrimp to recreate the flavors of Marataízes at home.

  3. Cachaça: Bring home a bottle of local cachaça to enjoy or to give as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Marataízes

Marataízes is known for its friendly, laid-back culture, which is deeply rooted in its fishing heritage. It is important to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly in public places and being mindful of the environment when exploring natural areas.

Best Time to Visit Marataízes

The best time to visit Marataízes is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. Temperatures are mild, and rain is less frequent during this time. However, offseason travel can offer lower prices, fewer crowds, and a more authentic experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marataízes

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and cultural traditions.

  2. Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  3. Take care of the environment and be mindful of the impact of your actions.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public places.

  2. Disturb wildlife or marine life when exploring natural areas.

  3. Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marataízes

Marataízes is a safe destination, but it is important to take precautions to avoid petty theft and pickpocketing. Be sure to lock your valuables in a safe place and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Marataízes

Marataízes has a few medical facilities, but it is recommended to bring any necessary medications with you. Mosquitos are prevalent, so it is important to pack insect repellent and long-sleeved clothing to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Marataízes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marataízes

The nearest airport is Vitória-Eurico de Aguiar Salles Airport (VIX), which is approximately 120km from Marataízes. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Marataízes.

Bus Stations Near Marataízes

The closest bus station is Cachoeiro de Itapemirim Bus Station, which is approximately 60km from Marataízes. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a local bus to Marataízes.

Other Options

Car rental is a popular option for visitors who prefer to explore Marataízes and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Marataízes

The best way to get around Marataízes is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited. Taxis are also available, but prices can be high, especially for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Marataízes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Marataízes, including:

  1. Vitória: The capital city of Espírito Santo, known for its historic center, beaches, and cultural events.

  2. Guarapari: A popular beach town known for its hot springs and natural pools.

  3. Alegre: A small town located in the mountains, known for its coffee plantations and natural beauty.

Conclusion

From pristine beaches to stunning mountain views, Marataízes has something for every traveler. With its friendly locals, rich cultural heritage, and diverse activities, this charming coastal town is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Brazil.
