Introduction to Nova Venécia

Nova Venécia is a charming municipality located in the northwest part of the state of Espírito Santo, Brazil. Known for its agricultural production, particularly coffee, it is also home to several cultural and natural attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with insights on the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and accommodations to stay in for your trip to Nova Venécia.

List of Places to Visit in Nova Venécia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu do Imigrante Italiano: This museum explores the history of Italian immigrants who settled in Nova Venécia. Visitors can learn about their traditions, cuisine, and way of life through photographs, artifacts, and documents.

  • Igreja Matriz de São Marcos: This beautiful church is the main religious monument in the city. Built in the early 1940s, it has a unique architecture and is a must-visit for its beautiful stained glass windows and religious artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Nova Venécia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horta Comunitária de Nova Venécia: The community gardens of Nova Venécia is owed by a group known as the Company. It’s an excellent place to relax and enjoy nature with friends and family.

  • Represa de Santa Maria do Doce: The Santa Maria do Doce Dam is a popular spot for picnics, fishing, hiking, and camping, surrounded by lush vegetation and scenic views of the lake’s crystal clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nova Venécia

  • Eco Valley Park: Eco Valley Park offers bike and hiking trails with stunning views, waterfalls, and hot springs. Adventurers can opt for solo explorations or join guided tours.

Best Things to Do in Nova Venécia: Activities and Tours

  • Coffee tours: One of the best things to do while in Nova Venécia is to take a coffee tour. You can witness the entire process of coffee production, from harvesting to roasting, and end your tour sipping a freshly brewed cup of coffee.

  • Hot air balloon rides: Nova Venécia’s beautiful landscapes and peaceful atmosphere is a perfect fit for an early morning ride above the clouds in a hot air balloon ride. One can see stunning views of the city and vast coffee plantations.

Food and Drink Scene of Nova Venécia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sushi: Nova Venécia offers one of the best sushi restaurants in Brazil known as Samauma Sushi. Its chef serves an array of sushi delicacies made with local seafood, rice, and vegetables.

  • Coxinha: Another must-try snack in Nova Venécia is coxinha. It’s a deep-fried dough made of shredded chicken and spices and is served with a side of hot sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Nova Venécia is known for its coffee production, so tasting a freshly brewed cup of coffee is a must.

  • Cachaça: Cachaça is a Brazilian alcoholic beverage made from fermented sugarcane. It’s popular in Nova Venécia, where you can find exotic flavors such as coconut, berry, and ginger.

Where to Stay in Nova Venécia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grande Hotel São Marcos: This hotel is located in central Nova Venécia, offering comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisines.

  • Pousada Paradiso: A beautiful and cozy guest house located in Nova Venécia’s rural area, 20 minutes from the city’s center, offers a perfect retreat setting, enveloped in trees and shrubs.

What to Buy in Nova Venécia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Coffee: Nova Venécia is known for its coffee production, and you can find fantastic coffee beans to take home as souvenirs.

  • Handmade Crafts: Nova Venécia’s downtown has several shops selling handmade crafts such as baskets, clothing, and ornaments.

Local Culture and Customs of Nova Venécia

The locals in Nova Venécia are known for being friendly and hospitable to visitors, and many celebrate religious festivals such as Corpus Christi and Saint Mark’s Day with lively parades, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Nova Venécia

The optimal time to visit Nova Venécia is during the dry season from May to October, where the weather is usually stable, and rainfall is minimal, giving visitors the opportunity to explore the town’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nova Venécia

Do:

  • Carry a comfortable shoe for walking.
  • Keep valuables safe.
  • Plan your visit with tourism agencies.

Don’t:

  • Overstay without permission.
  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Wandering solo at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nova Venécia

Nova Venécia is a safe town to visit. Still, visitors should keep in mind the general safety tips such as locking doors and windows, especially when staying overnight.

Health in Nova Venécia

Nova Venécia has several hospitals and medical centers that provide excellent medical care in case of an emergency.

Getting to Nova Venécia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nova Venécia

The nearest airports to Nova Venécia are Eurico de Aguiar Salles Airport (VIX) in Vitória, which is around 177 km away. Another option is Val de Cães International Airport in Belém, which is approximately 1266 km from Nova Venécia.

Bus Stations Near Nova Venécia

The primary bus terminal in Nova Venécia is the Terminal Rodoviário de Nova Venécia, which connects the city with several other destinations in Brazil.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi to travel around Nova Venécia and its neighboring villages.

How to Get Around in Nova Venécia

Nova Venécia is a small town, and you can quickly get around by foot, taxi or rental bike.

Where to Go Next from Nova Venécia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some locations to visit near Nova Venécia:

  • Guarapari: A coastal town known for its beautiful white sandy beaches and excellent seafood cuisine.

  • São Mateus: A historical town that is home to several colonial-style churches and museums.

Conclusion

Nova Venécia is a perfect destination for travelers looking for an exotic experience, great food, and discovering tranquil rural areas. With its friendly locals, vibrant traditions, excellent attractions, and outdoor adventures, it’s hard not to fall in love with this beautiful gem in the northwest part of Espírito Santo, Brazil.
