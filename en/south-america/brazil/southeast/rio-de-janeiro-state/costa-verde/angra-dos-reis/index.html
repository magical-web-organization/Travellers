Angra dos Reis Travel Guide

Angra dos Reis is a picturesque and vibrant town located in the Costa Verde region of Southeast Brazil. Nestled among the lush green mountains and beautiful coastline, it offers visitors a perfect mix of tropical scenery, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and some of the best food and drink in the region. If you’re planning a trip to Brazil and want to explore beyond the typical tourist destinations, this comprehensive guide will help you discover all the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Angra dos Reis.

List of Places to Visit in Angra dos Reis

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or just in search of some relaxation, Angra dos Reis has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Angra dos Reis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa da Cultura: This charming historical building was built in the 18th century and has been restored as a cultural center, showcasing the history and culture of the town and its people.
  • Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceicao: The stunning church, dating back to 1750, is a prime example of colonial architecture and features intricate Baroque details.
  • Museu do Centro Historico: Located in a beautifully restored colonial house, the museum displays artifacts and documents related to the town’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Angra dos Reis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ilha Grande: With crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and lush vegetation, Ilha Grande is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Visit the Lopes Mendes beach for stunning views.
  • Serra da Bocaina National Park: The park offers a myriad of breathtaking hiking trails, waterfalls, and panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and coastline.
  • Lagoa Azul: Surrounded by rocky cliffs and crystal-clear waters, the lagoon is a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Angra dos Reis

Angra dos Reis is a popular destination for hikers and cyclists alike. Here are some of the best routes to explore the town’s beautiful landscapes:

  • Aventureiro Trail: This challenging hike takes you through the heart of lush Atlantic rainforest, with scenic views of waterfalls, beaches, and local wildlife.
  • Pereque-Acu Trail: This well-marked trail is perfect for beginners looking to explore the local flora and fauna, and the stunning views of Angra Bay.
  • Paraty-Mirim to Sono Beach Trail: This bike ride along the coast takes you through beautiful beaches, rivers, and quiet fishing villages.

Best Things to Do in Angra dos Reis: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, Angra dos Reis offers an array of thrilling activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Boat Tours: Explore the beautiful coastline of Angra dos Reis by boat and discover hidden beaches, coves and snorkeling spots.
  • Parasailing: Get a bird’s eye view of the beautiful landscape of Angra dos Reis while soaring above the waters with parasailing.
  • Kayaking: Experience the beauty of Angra dos Reis at your own pace, exploring hidden coves, serene lagoons and remote beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Angra dos Reis

Day 1: Explore the historical center of Angra dos Reis, visit the Casa da Cultura and Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceicao.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Ilha Grande and visit the Lopes Mendes beach.

Day 3: Visit the stunning Serra da Bocaina National Park and take a few hikes through the scenic trails and waterfalls.

Day 4: Experience the waters of Angra dos Reis with a kayaking tour.

Day 5: Enjoy a relaxing day on the beaches of Lagoa Azul and do some snorkeling.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Paraty, known for its charming cobblestone streets and beautifully preserved colonial architecture.

Day 7: Take the Paraty-Mirim to Sono Beach bike tour and explore the beautiful coast of Angra dos Reis.

Food and Drink Scene of Angra dos Reis

Brazil boasts one of the most diverse and flavorful cuisines in the world, and it’s no different in Angra dos Reis. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijoada: This hearty stew made with black beans, pork, and beef is considered the national dish of Brazil.
  • Moqueca: Hailing from the coastal states, this fragrant seafood stew is flavored with coconut milk, dende oil, onions, and tomatoes.
  • Coxinha: A popular street food, coxinha is a deep-fried dough filled with shredded chicken or ground beef.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: The traditional Brazilian cocktail is made with cachaça (a sugarcane alcohol), sugar, and lime juice.
  • Chimarrão: A traditional tea made with yerba mate leaves, passed around and shared among friends in communal gourds.

Where to Stay in Angra dos Reis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Angra dos Reis offers a variety of lodging options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some top recommendations for places to stay:

  • Vila Galé Eco Resort: A luxury beachfront resort with multiple swimming pools, spa treatments, and gourmet dining options.
  • Pousada Arcobaleno: A cozy and affordable guest house located in the historical center of Angra dos Reis.
  • Che Lagarto Hostel Angra: A popular hostel located near the town’s center, offering comfortable and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Angra dos Reis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cachaça: Bring back a bottle of the locally produced cachaça to savor the flavors of Angra dos Reis at home.
  • Handicrafts: From woven baskets to ceramic plates and pottery, there’s an array of beautiful handicrafts to choose from made by the locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Angra dos Reis

Angra dos Reis has a rich history and culture that is still alive and thriving today. Visitors can experience the local culture by attending the traditional festivals, such as the Festa de Sao Benedito and Festa de Nossa Senhora da Conceicao, both celebrated with vibrant parades, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Angra dos Reis

The best time to visit Angra dos Reis is during the summer months from December to March when the weather is mild and sunny, and the sea is calm for swimming and other watersports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Angra dos Reis

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks, including the cachaça and feijoada.
  • Respect the local people and their customs.
  • Take care of the environment and follow the rule of “leave no trace” when hiking and exploring.

Don’t:

  • Go swimming on the beaches after dark.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beaches.
  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large sums of cash while exploring the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Angra dos Reis

Angra dos Reis is generally a safe place to visit, but travelers should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings from theft. It’s also recommended to avoid walking alone at night on the beaches.

Health in Angra dos Reis

Visitors to Angra dos Reis should check with their healthcare provider before traveling to ensure they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus have been reported in Brazil, so it’s important to use insect repellent and wear protective clothing.

Getting to Angra dos Reis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Angra dos Reis can be reached by both air and ground transportation. Here are some options for getting there:

Airports Near Angra dos Reis

The closest international airport to Angra dos Reis is the Galeao International Airport in Rio de Janeiro, which is approximately a three-hour drive from the town. From the airport, visitors can either rent a car or take a taxi to Angra dos Reis. Another nearby airport is the Aeroclube de Angra dos Reis, which serves private flights and charter planes.

Bus Stations Near Angra dos Reis

Angra dos Reis has a bus terminal located in the town’s center, which serves several different bus companies that connect the town to other major destinations in Brazil.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Angra dos Reis is to take a private shuttle or hire a car and driver, which can be arranged through many travel agencies and hotels in the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Angra dos Reis

The best way to get around Angra dos Reis is by car or taxi. Visitors can rent a car at the Rio de Janeiro airport or hire a taxi at the Angra dos Reis bus terminal. Those who prefer to explore the town on foot can do so in the historical center, which is small enough to walk around.

Where to Go Next from Angra dos Reis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Angra dos Reis’ location on the coast makes it a perfect starting point for exploring other destinations in the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Angra dos Reis:

  • Paraty: A charming colonial town located about an hour’s drive south of Angra, famous for its preserved colonial architecture and picturesque cobblestone streets.
  • Rio de Janeiro: Brazil’s iconic cultural capital is a three-hour drive from Angra dos Reis and offers endless opportunities for cultural and natural exploration.
  • Ilha Grande: Visitors can take a boat or ferry from Angra dos Reis to Ilha Grande, a beautiful and secluded island that is perfect for hiking, swimming, and relaxing.

Conclusion

Angra dos Reis is a hidden gem of Brazil, offering visitors a chance to explore the best of Brazilian culture, history, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their perfect vacation and experience all that Angra dos Reis has to offer, from scenic hiking trails to delicious local food and drink to luxurious beachfront accommodations.
