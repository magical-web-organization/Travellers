Introduction to Varre-Sai

Located in the northwest region of Rio de Janeiro, Varre-Sai is a small and charming town that is surrounded by beautiful mountains and valleys. Despite being a lesser-known destination, this town has a lot to offer for travelers seeking a quiet and authentic experience. Known for its stunning natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and traditional cuisine, Varre-Sai is a must-visit destination in Rio de Janeiro state. This guide provides you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Varre-Sai.

List of Places to Visit in Varre-Sai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Varre-Sai Museum

The Varre-Sai Museum is the perfect place for you to explore the history and culture of this small town. The museum features a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the local history and traditions. Visitors can learn about the indigenous culture that inhabited the region before the arrival of the Portuguese, as well as the colonial times and the development of the town.

Saint Elizabeth Chapel

This beautiful colonial-style chapel is one of the most important landmarks in Varre-Sai. It was built in the 19th century and features a stunning Baroque altar and beautiful paintings on the ceiling. Visitors can admire the architecture and learn about the religious traditions of the locals.

Natural Attractions of Varre-Sai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Itaporanga Waterfall

The Itaporanga waterfall is a spectacular natural attraction that is located just a few minutes from the town center. This waterfall has several cascading pools where visitors can swim and enjoy the refreshing water. Surrounded by lush vegetation and bird songs, this is the perfect spot to relax and connect with nature.

Pico da Pedra Selada

The Pico da Pedra Selada is a must-visit destination for those seeking adventure and breathtaking views. This 1,755 meters high mountain is located in the nearby town of Resende and offers a challenging hike to the summit. Upon reaching the top, visitors are rewarded with a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varre-Sai

The region around Varre-Sai is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, making it the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several trails and routes that visitors can explore, such as:

  • Pedra da Onça trail: a 6-kilometer trail that leads to a beautiful panoramic view of the region.
  • Itaporanga-Pedrinhas trail: a 7-kilometer trail that passes through the Itaporanga waterfall and ends at Pedrinhas, a small village with colonial-style houses and traditional crafts.
  • Parque do Tinguá: a beautiful park located in the nearby town of Nova Friburgo, which offers several trails and routes for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Varre-Sai: Activities and Tours

Participate in a Traditional Festival

Varre-Sai is known for its lively and colorful traditional festivals, such as the Festa de São João and the Cavalhada. These festivals feature live music, dances, and cultural performances that showcase the local traditions and folklore. Visitors can participate in these festivals and learn about the local culture.

Visit a Cachaça Distillery

Cachaça is a traditional Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane juice, and Varre-Sai is home to several cachaça distilleries. Visitors can tour the distilleries, learn about the process of making cachaça, and taste different flavors of this drink.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varre-Sai

Day 1: Visit the Varre-Sai Museum and Saint Elizabeth Chapel Day 2: Explore the Itaporanga waterfall and nearby trails Day 3: Participate in a traditional festival or visit a cachaça distillery Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby towns, such as Resende or Penedo Day 5: Hike the Pedra da Onça trail Day 6: Visit the nearby town of Nova Friburgo and explore the Parque do Tinguá Day 7: Relax and enjoy the town’s local cuisine and traditional crafts

Food and Drink Scene of Varre-Sai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijão Tropeiro: a traditional Brazilian dish made with beans, bacon, and cassava flour.
  • Chicken with Pequi: a dish made with chicken and a local fruit called pequi.
  • Arroz com Galinha: a rice dish made with chicken, onions, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: a traditional Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane juice.
  • Guaraná: a soft drink made from a local fruit called guaraná.

Where to Stay in Varre-Sai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Varre-Sai has several accommodation options for visitors. Some of the most recommended ones are:

  • Pousada do Gilson: a cozy and comfortable guesthouse located in the town center.
  • Hotel Fazenda Pedras Brancas: a beautiful hotel located in a rural area, which offers a pool, hiking trails, and other activities.
  • Pousada Água Fresca: a charming guesthouse located near the Itaporanga waterfall, which offers a peaceful and relaxing environment.

What to Buy in Varre-Sai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find several traditional crafts and souvenirs in Varre-Sai, such as:

  • Woven baskets and mats made from bamboo and straw.
  • Cachaça bottles and accessories.
  • Locally-grown coffee and organic honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Varre-Sai

Varre-Sai is a community that values its traditions and heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and interact with the locals, who are known for their hospitality and kindness. It is common to hear music and dance in the streets during festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Varre-Sai

The best time to visit Varre-Sai is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the region’s natural attractions. Visitors should avoid the rainy season, which runs from December to March and can sometimes make travel difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varre-Sai

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Interact with the locals and learn about their culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Varre-Sai

Varre-Sai is a peaceful and safe destination. However, visitors should take some precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in public places.
  • If driving, be careful on the narrow and winding roads.

Health in Varre-Sai

Visitors should take some precautions regarding health, such as:

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.
  • If hiking or biking, bring appropriate gear and take breaks when needed.

Where to Go Next from Varre-Sai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other destinations in Rio de Janeiro state, such as:

Conclusion
