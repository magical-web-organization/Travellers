Introduction to Resende

Located in the southern part of Rio de Janeiro, Resende is a charming city that boasts of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant festivities. The city is surrounded by picturesque mountains, valleys, and rivers while being home to world-famous industries and universities.

List of Places to Visit in Resende

No matter your interests, Resende has plenty of attractions that cater to people of all ages and backgrounds. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Câmara Municipal de Resende
  • Museu de Arte Moderna de Resende
  • Palacete Visconde de Mauá
  • The Resende Observatory
  • The Santuário Diocesano Nossa Senhora do Amor Divino

Natural Attractions of Resende: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cachoeira do Escorrega
  • Cachoeira da Fumaça
  • Parque Nacional do Itatiaia
  • Parque das Águas de Caxambu
  • Vista Chinesa de Resende

Hiking/Biking Routes of Resende

Resende offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Rio de Janeiro. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Trilha da Mina
  • Trilha da Pedra Selada
  • Trilha para a Cachoeira das Três Quedas
  • Trilha para a Cachoeira da Conquista
  • Trilha para a Pedra do Peito do Pombo

Best Things to Do in Resende: Activities and Tours

Apart from the attractions and scenic views, Resende offers a broad range of activities and tours. Here are some of the must-try activities:

  • Rafting in Rio Paraíba do Sul
  • Paragliding at Belvedere Vista Chinesa
  • Visit the Vinho dos Pássaros Vineyard
  • Horseback Riding in the Atlantic Rainforest
  • Take a Waterfall Walking Tour

Day 1:

  • Go Rafting in Rio Paraíba do Sul

Day 5:

Day 6:

  • Go Paragliding at Belvedere Vista Chinesa

Local Culture and Customs of Resende

Resende has a rich cultural heritage that’s deeply rooted in its history and traditions. The locals are known for their traditional music and dance, folk festivals, and arts and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Resende

The best time to visit Resende is from June to September, when the weather is mild and dry, making it more convenient for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Resende

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Visit the natural reserves and national parks
  • Be mindful of the environment

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in the natural areas
  • Disrespect the local traditions and customs
  • Overpack for your visit

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety and well-being while staying here.

Safety in Resende

Resende is a safe place to visit, but it’s always a good idea to keep your possessions safe and ensure the safety of yourself as well as others.

Health in Resende

It’s recommended to take the usual precautions for your health, such as carrying mosquito repellents, rehydrating regularly, and ensuring proper food hygiene.

Getting to Resende: Transportation Options and Logistics

Resende is accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some of the most popular one’s:

Airports Near Resende

  • Belo Horizonte - Tancredo Neves/Confins International Airport

Bus Stations Near Resende

Other Options

How to Get Around in Resende

Resende is a relatively small town; hence, it’s easy to navigate around on foot. However, buses and taxis are available and are cheap modes of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Resende: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Whether you’re into natural beauty, outdoor adventures, rich culture, or vibrant city life, Resende has something to offer for everyone. From the stunning waterfalls to the delicious local cuisine, this charming city is an experience you don’t want to miss.
