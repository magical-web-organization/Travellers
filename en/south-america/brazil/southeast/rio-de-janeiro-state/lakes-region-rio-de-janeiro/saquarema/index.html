Introduction to Saquarema

Nestled in the Lakes Region of the Rio de Janeiro State, Saquarema is a small beach town with a population of approximately 89,000. With its paradise beaches, stunning natural beauty, and laid-back lifestyle, Saquarema is a popular destination among Brazilian tourists and recently has been gaining popularity among international travelers as well. Whether you’re looking for an adventure-packed holiday or a peaceful and relaxing getaway, Saquarema offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Saquarema

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saquarema Church

The Saquarema Church is one of the main attractions of the city. Built in the 17th century, it is a symbol of the city’s culture and history. With a baroque-style façade, the church houses a remarkable collection of paintings and sculptures.

Sea Museum

Located in the heart of Saquarema, the Sea Museum is a must-visit for all visitors. The museum is home to an impressive collection of artifacts and displays that detail the history and culture of the local fishing community.

Natural Attractions of Saquarema: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Itaúna Beach

Famous for its excellent surfing conditions, Itaúna Beach is one of Saquarema’s most popular tourist destinations. Known locally as the “Brazilian Pipeline,” Itaúna Beach is a paradise for surfers from around the world.

Jaconé Beach

With its crystal-clear waters, Jaconé Beach is a great place for relaxing and soaking up the sun. The beach is surrounded by natural vegetation and offers amazing views of the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saquarema

Pedra do Telégrafo Trail

The Pedra do Telégrafo trail offers travelers the opportunity to hike up to Pedra do Telégrafo viewpoint and enjoy breathtaking views of Saquarema from above. The trek is moderate to difficult, but the reward is definitely worth the effort.

Ciclovia Niemeyer

Ciclovia Niemeyer is a bike path that winds its way through the stunning landscapes of Saquarema. The trail offers visitors the opportunity to explore Saquarema’s natural beauty at their own pace, with plenty of opportunities to stop and take in the scenery.

Best Things to Do in Saquarema: Activities and Tours

Surfing Lessons

With its world-renowned surfing conditions, Saquarema is the perfect place to learn how to surf. Several companies in the area offer lessons for beginners and intermediate surfers.

Boat Tours

Explore the beautiful coastline of Saquarema and discover hidden gems that are only accessible by boat. From diving into pristine waters to watching the sunset from the deck of a boat, boat tours are a must-do activity during your stay.

Food and Drink Scene of Saquarema

Must-Try Dishes

Pão de Queijo

This traditional Brazilian snack is a baked cheese roll that originated in the state of Minas Gerais. It’s a great option for a breakfast on-the-go or a mid-afternoon snack.

Brigadeiro

A traditional Brazilian dessert, Brigadeiro is made of chocolate, condensed milk and butter. It’s a must-try when in Saquarema and can be found in almost every bakery in town.

Local Beverages

Caipirinha

Brazil’s national cocktail, the Caipirinha, is a popular choice among locals and tourists alike. Made with cachaça (Brazilian sugar cane rum), lime, and sugar, this refreshing cocktail is perfect for a hot day on the beach.

Cerveja

Brazil’s beer scene is growing rapidly, and Saquarema has several great options for beer lovers. With locally brewed beers available in many bars and restaurants, make sure to try one of Saquarema’s craft beers while visiting.

Where to Stay in Saquarema: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Fazenda Itaúna

Located in Itaúna Beach, Hotel Fazenda Itaúna is a luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the ocean. Rooms are beautifully decorated and equipped with modern amenities, and the hotel has several on-site facilities such as a pool and a sauna.

Pousada Maison Aires Malcher

Located in the heart of Saquarema, Pousada Maison Aires Malcher is a cozy guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a peaceful atmosphere. The guesthouse has a beautiful garden and provides daily breakfast for its guests.

What to Buy in Saquarema: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Saquarema is known for its handmade crafts made of natural materials such as wood, clay, and feathers. Visitors can find unique and authentic items such as jewelry, home decor, and souvenirs at local stores and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Saquarema

Saquarema is a city with a strong local culture. Visitors can experience this culture firsthand through the food, music, festivals, and art that are an integral part of the community. Respect for the environment and people is also important in Saquarema, as the town relies heavily on tourism and local commerce.

Best Time to Visit Saquarema

The best time to visit Saquarema is during the summer months from December to February when temperatures are warm and rainfall is at its lowest. However, for those seeking a quieter and more relaxed visit, the shoulder seasons of March to May and September to November offer great weather and smaller crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saquarema

Do:

  • Respect the environment and keep beaches and natural areas clean.
  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Wear sunscreen and bring hats and sunglasses on beach days.

Don’t:

  • Litter on beaches, streets or natural areas.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Leave anything of value unattended on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saquarema

Saquarema is a safe place for travelers. However, as with any destination, visitors should take general safety precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Saquarema

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Zika virus and dengue fever, by wearing insect repellent and appropriate clothing. Always consult with a doctor before traveling to Brazil, and make sure all necessary vaccinations are up-to-date.

Getting to Saquarema: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saquarema

The nearest airport to Saquarema is Rio de Janeiro International Airport, approximately 120 km away. From there, visitors can opt for a private transfer or rent a car to reach Saquarema. Alternatively, visitors can take a bus from the airport to Saquarema.

Bus Stations Near Saquarema

Visitors can reach Saquarema via a bus line, with daily services running from Rio de Janeiro and other nearby cities. The main bus station in the city center also offers additional connections to other destinations within Brazil.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a private transfer or rent a car with a driver to reach Saquarema. This allows for more flexibility and can be arranged in advance through a travel agency or hotel.

How to Get Around in Saquarema

While walking is the easiest way to get around the town center, visitors may want to rent a car or take a taxi to explore the area around Saquarema. Bicycles are also available for rent and are a great way to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Saquarema: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Búzios

Famous for its beaches and nightlife, Búzios is a popular destination among Brazilian and international tourists. Búzios is approximately 110 km away from Saquarema.

Rio de Janeiro

One of the world’s most famous cities, Rio de Janeiro is a must-visit when in Brazil. Its beaches, culture, and music are world-renowned, making it an unforgettable destination. Rio de Janeiro is approximately 120 km away from Saquarema.

Conclusion

A hidden gem in Rio de Janeiro’s Lakes Region, Saquarema offers a unique and unforgettable experience for travelers. From its stunning natural beauty to its rich culture and history, Saquarema has something for everyone. With its welcoming atmosphere, Saquarema is a must-visit destination for any traveler’s itinerary of Brazil.
