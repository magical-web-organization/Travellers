Introduction to Capitólio

Located in the southern region of the state of Minas Gerais in Southeast Brazil, Capitólio is a charming town that is quickly gaining popularity for its stunning natural beauty. The town is nestled amidst a landscape of emerald-green waters, rugged cliffs, and lush green forests that make it an idyllic escape for outdoor enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Capitólio

Capitólio is an excellent destination that is home to a variety of attractions that cater to different interests and preferences. Here is a list of places you must visit in Capitólio:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Capitólio is a relatively small town, and in that regard, there are not many historical sites or museums. However, the town’s deep-rooted cultural heritage is reflected in the stunning colonial-style architecture that can be seen in some of its oldest buildings.

One such building is the Capitólio Church (Igreja Matriz de Santo Antônio), which dates back to the 1700s, and features Baroque-style architecture with intricately carved wooden facades and stunning stained-glass windows. Another historic site is the Museum of Capitólio (Museu de Capitólio), which features exhibits on the town’s history and culture, including its indigenous heritage, mining past, and fishing traditions.

Natural Attractions of Capitólio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Capitólio’s main attraction is its stunning natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to experience. One of the most popular activities is visiting the Furnas Lake, a vast man-made lake that covers over 1,400 hectares and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and greenery. You can explore the lake’s numerous waterfalls, caves, and islands by taking a boat tour or renting a kayak or paddleboard.

Capitólio is also home to several natural parks and reserves, including the Serra da Canastra National Park, which is renowned for its diverse wildlife and stunning waterfalls, including Casca D’Anta. Another remarkable natural attraction is the Canyons of Furnas, a series of narrow canyons that were formed by the damming of the river. Visitors can hike through the canyons or take a jeep tour for an exciting adventure to discover stunning views of the cliffs and the water flowing between them.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Capitólio

There are several hiking and biking routes in Capitólio that offer excellent opportunities to explore and discover the town’s natural beauty.

One of the most popular hiking routes is the Trilha do Sol, which translates to “Sun Trail,” is a moderate hike covering four kilometers with about six viewpoints overlooking the Furnas Lake. It takes approximately 1-2 hours and offers stunning views of the lake from above.

For cycling enthusiasts, the Circuito do Lago is a 40 km ride that will take you around Furnas Lake over the paved roads surrounding it. It’s a picturesque ride that will allow you to appreciate the lake’s vastness and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Capitólio: Activities and Tours

Besides the natural beauty, Capitólio is home to several exciting activities and tours that cater to different preferences and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Capitólio:

  • Take a boat tour of the Furnas Lake and explore its hidden caves, waterfalls, and islands
  • Rent a kayak or paddleboard and glide through the lake’s calm waters
  • Enjoy a scenic drive through the Canyons of Furnas
  • Take a Jeep tour and explore the beauty of Canastra National Park
  • Go fishing and catch some of the delicious local fish species, including pintado and dourado
  • Go horseback riding and explore the surrounding countryside

Food and Drink Scene of Capitólio

Capitólio has a wide range of dining options, from high-end restaurants to local street food vendors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frango ao Molho Pardo: a chicken dish served in a rich, dark sauce made from blood and broth
  • Arroz com Pequi: a dish made with rice and the fruit of a local tree, which has a tangy and slightly sour flavor.
  • Pão de Queijo: a classic Brazilian snack made from cheese and tapioca flour
  • Pastel: A deep-fried pastry stuffed with either beef or cheese, but you can find other fillings as well

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: a popular Brazilian spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice
  • Doce de Leite: a sweet, creamy milk-based dessert that is popular throughout Brazil
  • Coffee: Brazil is the world’s largest coffee producer, so you must try a locally brewed cup of coffee!

Where to Stay in Capitólio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Capitólio provides plenty of accommodation options to suit various budgets and preferences. There are luxurious hotels, guest houses, and hostels for budget-conscious travelers. Here are some recommended places to stay in Capitólio:

  • Hotel Escarpas do Lago: A well-known resort that is located on the shore of the Furnas Lake
  • Pousada Mar de Minas: A cozy guest house that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and lake
  • Hostel Caminho do Sol: A budget-friendly hostel that offers private and shared rooms options

What to Buy in Capitólio: Souvenirs and Local Products to bring home

Capitólio offers an array of unique, locally made products that would make perfect souvenirs to bring back home. Here are some of the best souvenirs to look for in Capitólio:

  • Cachaça: Capitólio is home to several cachaça producers, so you must try and bring home a bottle or two.
  • Embroidery: The town’s indigenous heritage is reflected in its embroidery work, which is handmade by local artisans.
  • Cheese: Capitólio is famous for its dairy products, especially its cheese. You should consider bringing some home.

Local Culture and Customs of Capitólio

Capitólio is a relatively small town, and the locals are very hospitable and welcoming to visitors. Its deep-rooted cultural heritage is reflected in its architecture, embroidery work, and unique culinary offerings. It is vital to respect the locals’ culture and customs while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Capitólio

The best time to visit Capitólio is during the dry season, which runs from May to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. Temperatures range between 20-28ºC (68-83ºF) during the day, and the water levels at the Furnas Lake are low, allowing for better access to the caves and waterfalls.

Safety and Health

Safety in Capitólio

Capitólio is a generally safe town, with low crime rates. However, it is advised to practice common sense and take necessary precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. Some of the tips are:

  • Avoid carrying around large amounts of cash or valuables
  • Keep your belongings close to you in crowded places
  • Avoid walking alone in deserted areas.

Health in Capitólio

Visitors to Capitólio should take any necessary precautions when it comes to their health. Bring along insect repellents, wear protective clothing, and use sunscreen. You should also make sure to practice safe food and water precautions by drinking bottled water and avoiding street food vendors.

Getting to Capitólio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Capitólio is located about 280 km from the capital city of Belo Horizonte. There are several ways to get to Capitólio, depending on your preferences and budget.

Airports Near Capitólio

The nearest major international airport is Tancredo Neves/Confins International Airport in Belo Horizonte. From there, you can take a bus or hire a taxi or private car for the four-hour transfer to Capitólio.

Bus Stations Near Capitólio

Another option is taking a bus from Belo Horizonte to Capitólio. The bus ride takes around five to six hours, depending on the route.

Other Options

You can rent a car in Belo Horizonte and drive to Capitólio, which will take around four hours.

How to Get Around in Capitólio

One of the best ways to get around Capitólio is by car rental or hiring a private car service. Scooters and bikes are also available for rent for those looking to explore the town on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Capitólio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, check out some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • The historic city of Ouro Preto, which is about 200 km away from Capitólio
  • The beautiful city of São Thomé das Letras, which is about 100 km away, is renowned for its natural beauty and unique architecture.
  • The Sanctuary of Our Lady of Aparecida in Aparecida do Norte, which is about 250 km away and is considered one of the holiest sites in Brazil.

Conclusion

Capitólio is a hidden gem that is worth visiting for its unique natural beauty, exciting outdoor adventures, and cultural heritage. This comprehensive travel guide has everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town in Southern Brazil.
