Introduction to Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas, located in the southern region of Minas Gerais in Brazil, is a charming town known for its picturesque scenery, hot springs, and outdoor adventures. The town was founded in 1872 and has since become a popular tourist destination for both domestic and international travelers who seek a laid-back atmosphere and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Poços de Caldas

Here’s a comprehensive list of places to visit in Poços de Caldas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palace of Arts: A cultural center that houses various exhibitions, shows, and events throughout the year.
  • Urca Bernardino de Souza: A park with stunning views of the town and a historical train ride.
  • Museum of Geography and History of Poços de Caldas: A museum that showcases the history and culture of the town with various exhibitions, artifacts, and documents.

Natural Attractions of Poços de Caldas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cristo Redentor: A statue of Jesus Christ located on top of a hill that offers panoramic views of the town.
  • Thermas Antonio Carlos: A hot spring park that offers therapeutic and relaxing baths with mineral-rich water.
  • Recanto Japonês: A Japanese-style garden that features a waterfall, a small lake, and exotic plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas offers various hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts, including:

  • Serra de São Domingos trail: A scenic trail that takes you through lush green forests and offers panoramic views of the town.
  • Pedro David Trail: A challenging trail that takes you through rugged terrain with stunning views of waterfalls and natural pools.
  • Circuito das Águas Trail: A moderate-level trail that takes you to various hot springs, lakes, and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Poços de Caldas: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Poços de Caldas:

  • Hot spring baths and spas: Relax in the therapeutic and mineral-rich hot springs of Poços de Caldas.
  • Outdoor adventures: Go hiking, biking, horseback riding, or paragliding in the beautiful natural surroundings of the town.
  • Visiting cultural attractions: Explore the rich history and culture of Poços de Caldas by visiting museums, parks, and historical sites.
  • Food and drink scene: Savor the delicious local cuisine and beverages of Poços de Caldas by visiting local restaurants, cafes, and bars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poços de Caldas

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Poços de Caldas:

  • Day 1-3: Explore the town’s cultural and historical attractions, such as the Palace of Arts, Urca Bernardino de Souza, and the Museum of Geography and History of Poços de Caldas.
  • Day 4-5: Relax and rejuvenate in the hot springs and spas of Poços de Caldas, such as Thermas Antonio Carlos and Fonte dos Amores.
  • Day 6-7: Engage in outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, or horseback riding, in the scenic natural surroundings of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas offers some delicious local cuisine and beverages that you must try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pão de queijo: A famous Brazilian cheese bread made with cassava flour and cheese.
  • Feijão tropeiro: A traditional Brazilian dish made with beans, pork, sausage, and farofa (toasted flour).
  • Churrasco: Brazilian barbecue that features various cuts of meat served on skewers.

Local Beverages

  • Pingá: A traditional Brazilian liquor made from fermented sugar cane juice.
  • Caipirinha: A popular Brazilian cocktail made with cachaça (distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice), lime, and sugar.
  • Café Mineiro: A strong and aromatic coffee brewed with beans grown in Minas Gerais.

Where to Stay in Poços de Caldas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Poços de Caldas offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Thermas Resort Walter World: A family-friendly resort that features hot springs, waterparks, and various leisure activities.
  • Hotel Fazenda Poços de Caldas: A peaceful hotel surrounded by natural beauty that offers horseback riding, fishing, and hiking.
  • Hotel Lisboa: A well-located hotel in the town’s center that offers comfortable rooms and breakfast.

What to Buy in Poços de Caldas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you should buy during your visit to Poços de Caldas:

  • Crystals and stones: Poços de Caldas is famous for its natural crystals and stones, which make for beautiful souvenirs or gifts.
  • Arte na Praça: A fair that takes place every weekend in the town center, featuring various artisanal products, including ceramics, jewelry, and paintings.
  • Cassava flour and cheese: Buy some authentic cassava flour and cheese, used to make the famous pão de queijo, to bring home and recreate the flavor of Poços de Caldas.

Local Culture and Customs of Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, food, music, and customs. The town is known for its hospitality and warmth towards visitors, and it’s customary to greet people with a smile and a friendly “bom dia” (good morning) or “boa tarde” (good afternoon).

Best Time to Visit Poços de Caldas

The best time to visit Poços de Caldas is between April and September when the weather is dry and mild, with temperatures ranging between 50 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit. December and January are the peak tourism months due to school holidays, but the town may be crowded during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poços de Caldas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Poços de Caldas:

  • Protect the environment and wildlife by not littering or disturbing the natural surroundings.

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or traditions by being rude or insensitive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas is generally a safe town to visit, with a low crime rate. However, like any other tourist destination, petty theft may occur, particularly in crowded areas. It’s recommended to be cautious and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in busy public areas.

Health in Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas is located in a high-altitude area, and some visitors may experience altitude sickness. It’s recommended to drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol and caffeine, and rest as much as possible to alleviate symptoms. Additionally, visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue and Zika, by wearing protective clothing and using repellent.

Getting to Poços de Caldas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the transportation options for getting to Poços de Caldas:

Airports Near Poços de Caldas

The nearest airports to Poços de Caldas are:

  • Viracopos International Airport (Campinas): located 97 miles from Poços de Caldas, with domestic and international flights.
  • Guarulhos International Airport (São Paulo): located 130 miles from Poços de Caldas, with domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas Bus Station, located in the town center, offers daily buses to and from major cities in Brazil, such as São Paulo, Belo Horizonte, and Rio de Janeiro.

Other Options

Some visitors may enjoy driving to Poços de Caldas from nearby towns or cities. The town is well-connected by highways, and the scenic road trip offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

How to Get Around in Poços de Caldas

Poços de Caldas is a walkable town, and most attractions are within walking distance from each other. Alternatively, visitors can rent bicycles or take taxis to navigate the town. Additionally, there are bus routes in the town, and buses run frequently between the town center and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Poços de Caldas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
