Introduction to Caxambu

Caxambu is a small enchanting town located in the south of Minas Gerais, in Southeast Brazil. Known for its charming architecture and therapeutic waters, the town is famous for its mineral springs which are said to have healing properties. Caxambu is a popular destination for tourists looking to awe-inspiring scenery, outdoor activities, rich cultural heritage, and relaxing hot springs.

List of Places to Visit in Caxambu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Caxambu has a rich cultural heritage, and you can explore the town’s rich history by visiting the following museums and historical sites:

  • Aguas Park - a 45-hectare park that is home to several mineral water springs and was once a fashionable spa resort.

  • Palace of the Waters - a neo-classical building that was the summer residence of the Royal Family and is currently home to several museums, including the Museum of Mineral Water and the Regional Historical Museum.

  • Railroad Museum of Caxambu - a small museum that celebrates the town’s railway station with a collection of trains, wagons, and rail tracks.

Natural Attractions of Caxambu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Caxambu’s natural beauty is breathtaking. Enjoy moments of peace while being surrounded by nature by visiting these places:

  • Parque das Águas - The park’s lush green lawns and a beautiful collection of mineral water fountains are a highlight of the trip.

  • Cachoeira dos Henriques - The cascading waterfall is perfect for a refreshing dip on those hot summer days.

  • Serra de São Lourenço - A range of hills that offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Malacara Canyon - A picturesque canyon located just outside the town of Baependi.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caxambu

For nature enthusiasts, Caxambu offers a range of scenic hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails are:

  • Trilha do Urubu - A trail that takes you up to the Urubu peak, where you can get an incredible view of the city.

  • Trilha dos Macacos - A trail that takes you through the woods and offers various points where you can see the city of Caxambu.

  • Rota Real Trail - An ancient road that connects Diamantina and Parati and offers beautiful views of the Brazilian countryside.

Best Things to Do in Caxambu: Activities and Tours

Caxambu has a lot to offer in terms of outdoor activities and tours. Some of the popular things to do in Caxambu include:

  • Taking a relaxing swim in the park’s mineral springs and fountains.

  • Renting a bike and taking a relaxing ride around town.

  • Taking a guided tour of the museums and historical sites.

  • Enjoying a scenic horseback ride through the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caxambu

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Caxambu, here is a rough itinerary you can use:

Day Activity
1 Explore Aguas Park, take a stroll, have a picnic, and enjoy the mineral water fountains.
2 Take a guided tour of the Palace of the Waters, the Railroad Museum of Caxambu, and the Regional Historical Museum.
3 Take a hike through Trilha do Urubu, visit Serra de São Lourenço, and take in the incredible views of the town.
4 Rent a bike and explore downtown Caxambu. Afterward, enjoy a relaxing afternoon in Cachoeira dos Henriques.
5 Go on a guided horseback ride through the countryside, take in the beautiful scenery, and enjoy the fresh air.
6 Take a trip to nearby Malacara Canyon and explore the canyon’s unique geological formations.
7 Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Caxambu

Must-Try Dishes

Caxambu has a unique food culture, and some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Pão de queijo - A savory roll made from cassava starch, cheese, and eggs.

  • Feijão Tropeiro - A delicious bean dish made with pork, sausage, bacon, and seasonings.

  • Tutu de feijão - A traditional Brazilian dish made with mashed beans, sausage, and rice.

Local Beverages

Caxambu is especially famous for its mineral water, which is said to have healing powers. You can find a vast array of mineral water types, and one of the most famous brands is Cantareira.

Where to Stay in Caxambu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Caxambu offers various accommodation options to meet the needs of every traveler. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Grand Hotel Caxambu - A historic hotel that dates back to 1875 and is the most luxurious accommodation in town.

  • Pousada Caxambu - A charming guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodation and great service.

  • Caxambu Parque Hotel - A mid-range hotel with a beautiful garden, swimming pool, and clean rooms.

What to Buy in Caxambu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Caxambu is famous for its local handicrafts, which include pottery and woodwork. You can find many unique souvenirs to bring home from your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Caxambu

Caxambu has a rich cultural heritage and is renowned for its warm hospitality. When you visit, you’ll be greeted with a smile, and you’ll find friendly locals who are always happy to help.

Best Time to Visit Caxambu

The best time to visit Caxambu is from March to May or from September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are thin. The summer months can be very hot, and the winter months can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caxambu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and norms.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Enjoy peaceful moments in the parks and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals, culture, and customs.

  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caxambu

Caxambu is generally a safe destination, but it is always important to take precautions like keeping your belongings secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Caxambu

Caxambu is famous for its healing mineral waters, and many visitors come to the town for its therapeutic properties. However, it is always essential to ensure that you drink clean water and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like Dengue fever and yellow fever.

Getting to Caxambu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caxambu

The nearest airport to Caxambu is the São Paulo International Airport (GRU), which is approximately 225 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Caxambu

Caxambu has a bus station that is serviced by several bus companies from major cities in Brazil.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a taxi from the nearest cities like Itajubá, Pouso Alegre.

How to Get Around in Caxambu

Caxambu is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot. You can also rent a bike or take a taxi to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Caxambu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other beautiful destinations after your trip to Caxambu, some of the options include:

  • Ouro Preto - Known for its colonial architecture and baroque churches, Ouro Preto is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Tiradentes - A beautiful little town known for its cobblestone streets, colonial architecture, and charming cafes.

  • Paraty - A colonial town with beautiful beaches, islands, and a historic town center.

Conclusion

Caxambu is a hidden gem in Southeast Brazil that is full of rich culture, natural beauty, and healing waters. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide and get ready to explore everything this enchanting town has to offer.
