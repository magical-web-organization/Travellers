Introduction to Diamantina

Located in the state of Minas Gerais in Southeast Brazil, Diamantina is a small colonial town that dates back to the 18th century. Best known for its diamond mines, Diamantina has a rich history that is reflected in its well-preserved architecture, cobblestone streets, and numerous museums and cultural attractions. Visitors to Diamantina can also explore the natural beauty of the surrounding mountains and forests, enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking, and sample delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Diamantina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museu do Diamante

This museum is located inside the former diamond commissioner’s house and features a collection of mining tools, artifacts, and natural history exhibits related to the diamond mining industry in Minas Gerais.

Casa de Juscelino

The birthplace of former Brazilian president Juscelino Kubitschek, Casa de Juscelino is now a museum that showcases his life and political career.

Igreja de São Francisco de Assis

This baroque-style church was built in the 18th century and features intricate artwork and architecture, including a ceiling painted by renowned artist Manuel da Costa Ataíde.

Natural Attractions of Diamantina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Serra do Espinhaço

This mountain range encompasses the region around Diamantina and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike or bike along the trails or simply enjoy the scenery from one of the many lookout points.

Cachoeira do Tabuleiro

Located just outside of Diamantina, this waterfall is one of the tallest in Brazil, with a drop of over 800 feet. Visitors can hike to the top of the falls or swim in the natural pool at the bottom.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Diamantina

Caminho dos Escravos

This historic trail was used by enslaved people in the 18th century to transport goods between diamond mines and the coast. Today, visitors can hike the trail and learn about its history and significance.

Parque Estadual do Biribiri

This state park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that wind through the forest, past waterfalls, and past historic ruins.

Best Things to Do in Diamantina: Activities and Tours

Diamond Mining Tour

Visitors can take a guided tour of one of the old mines around Diamantina to learn about the history of diamond mining in the region.

Carnaval de Diamantina

This four-day festival held in February or March is one of the biggest tourist draws in Diamantina. Visitors can enjoy parades, music, dancing, and cultural events.

Food and Drink Scene of Diamantina

Must-Try Dishes

Tutu de Feijão

A traditional dish made with beans, rice, and pork.

Pão de Queijo

A popular Brazilian snack made with cheese and served warm.

Local Beverages

Cerveja Artesanal

Craft beer is becoming increasingly popular in Diamantina, with several local breweries producing unique and flavorful beers.

Cachaça

This Brazilian liquor made from sugar cane is a staple of the local bar scene.

Where to Stay in Diamantina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hospedaria Vila do Imperador

This guesthouse is located in the heart of Diamantina and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

Pousada do Garimpo

This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building and features a garden, pool, and beautiful views of the mountains.

What to Buy in Diamantina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pedras Sabão

These soapstone carvings are a popular souvenir and can be found in shops throughout Diamantina.

Queijo Minas

This cheese made from cow’s milk is a staple of Minas Gerais cuisine and can be found in many local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Diamantina

Diamantina has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, art, and cuisine. Visitors to Diamantina should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as covering their shoulders and knees when visiting religious sites and observing local customs during Carnaval.

Best Time to Visit Diamantina

The best time to visit Diamantina is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, visitors can enjoy sunny weather and comfortable temperatures. The wet season, from October to April, can be rainy and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Diamantina

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Learn about the history and culture of Diamantina

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage historic sites
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or biking in the mountains

Safety and Health

Safety in Diamantina

Diamantina is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution when traveling alone or at night. Pickpocketing and petty theft can occur in busy tourist areas, so it’s important to keep valuables secure.

Health in Diamantina

Visitors to Diamantina should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and may also need additional vaccinations depending on the length and purpose of their visit. It’s also important to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus if visiting during the rainy season.

Getting to Diamantina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Diamantina

The nearest airports to Diamantina are Confins International Airport in Belo Horizonte, approximately 180 miles away, and Tancredo Neves International Airport in São Paulo, approximately 310 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Diamantina

Diamantina has a bus station with connections to nearby towns and cities, including Belo Horizonte and São Paulo.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to travel to Diamantina.

How to Get Around in Diamantina

Diamantina is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Taxis and local buses are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Diamantina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ouro Preto

This historic town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its baroque architecture and rich cultural heritage.

Belo Horizonte

The capital city of Minas Gerais, Belo Horizonte is a bustling metropolis with a thriving food and arts scene.

Conclusion

Diamantina offers an immersive glimpse into Brazil’s colonial past, combined with stunning natural scenery and a vibrant cultural scene. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip to Diamantina that includes the best attractions, activities, and local experiences.
