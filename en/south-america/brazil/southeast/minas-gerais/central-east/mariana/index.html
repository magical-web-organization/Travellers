Introduction to Mariana

Mariana is a beautiful colonial town in the state of Minas Gerais in Brazil. Nestled among the rolling hills, it is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. It was founded in 1696 and is famous for its baroque architecture, delicious food, and breathtaking landscapes. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a food lover, Mariana has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mariana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mariana Cathedral of the Assumption: This cathedral is one of the most famous landmarks in Mariana. The cathedral was built in the 18th century and is a great example of baroque architecture.

  • Museum of Sacred Art: The museum is located in the Chapel of Our Lady of Mercy and displays a collection of sacred art from the colonial period.

  • House of Culture: This museum is dedicated to the history of Mariana and displays artifacts and documents related to the town.

  • Santa Clara Convent: The convent was founded in the early 18th century and is a great example of colonial architecture. It has a beautiful courtyard and a chapel with frescoes painted by Jose Soares de Araujo.

Natural Attractions of Mariana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Passagem de Mariana: This is an old railway line that runs through the mountains. It is a great place to enjoy nature and go for a hike or a bike ride.

  • Pico do Itacolomi State Park: This park has a beautiful mountain and offers a stunning view of Mariana and the surrounding area.

  • Cachoeira do Brumado: This is a beautiful waterfall and a great place to swim and relax.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mariana

Mariana has a lot of hiking and biking routes that you can explore. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Passagem de Mariana: This is an old railway line that runs through the mountains. It is a great place to go for a bike ride or a hike.

  • Pico do Itacolomi: This is a popular hiking route that takes you to the top of the mountain. The trail is well marked and offers beautiful views of the surrounding area.

  • Cachoeira do Brumado: This is a short hiking trail that takes you to a beautiful waterfall. It is a great place to swim and relax.

Best Things to Do in Mariana: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Mariana. Here are some of the best things:

  • Take a walking tour of the town: Mariana has many historic buildings and streets that you can explore on foot. You can hire a guide or do it on your own.

  • Visit a mine: Mariana is famous for its gold mines. You can take a tour of a mine and learn about the history of mining in Mariana.

  • Try the local food: Mariana has many great restaurants that serve local food. You can try traditional dishes like feijão tropeiro, tutu de feijão, and pão de queijo.

If you are planning to spend a week in Mariana, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Mariana

Must-Try Dishes

Mariana has many great restaurants that serve local food. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Feijão Tropeiro: This is a traditional dish made with beans, bacon, sausage, and manioc flour.

  • Tutu de Feijão: This is a dish made with black beans, bacon, and manioc flour. It is usually served with rice and pork ribs.

  • Pão de Queijo: This is a small cheese bread made with cassava flour and cheese. It is a typical snack in the region.

Local Beverages

Mariana is famous for its coffee. You can try a cup of coffee in one of the many coffee shops in the town. Cachaça, a distilled spirit made from sugarcane, is also popular in the region.

Where to Stay in Mariana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mariana has many options for accommodation. Here are some of the best:

  • Pousada do Chafariz: This is a guesthouse located in the historic center of Mariana. It has a beautiful garden and offers comfortable rooms.

  • Pousada Villa Alferes: This is a guesthouse located in a colonial mansion. It has a swimming pool and a restaurant that serves local food.

  • Hotel Providência: This is a hotel located in the center of Mariana. It has comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves Brazilian cuisine.

What to Buy in Mariana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mariana is famous for its handicrafts and local products. Here are some of the things you can buy:

  • Gold and Silver Jewelry: Mariana has a long tradition of gold and silver mining. You can buy beautiful jewelry made by local artisans.

  • Handicrafts: Mariana has many shops that sell handcrafted items like pottery, baskets, and woodcarvings.

  • Cheese: The region is famous for its cheese. You can buy cheese made from cow’s milk or goat’s milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Mariana

Mariana has a rich culture and many traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Some of the customs and traditions include:

  • Religious Festivals: Mariana has many religious festivals throughout the year. The most famous one is the Feast of Our Lady of the Assumption, which takes place in August.

  • Arts and Crafts: Mariana has a long tradition of arts and crafts. You can see many handicrafts being made in the town.

Best Time to Visit Mariana

The best time to visit Mariana is from May to September, during the dry season. The weather is mild and there are many festivals and events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mariana

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Buy souvenirs and local products.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive.

  • Disrespect religious sites and festivals.

  • Leave trash in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mariana

Mariana is a safe town for tourists. However, as with any place, you should take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Mariana

Mariana has a good healthcare system. However, you should take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and Zika.

Getting to Mariana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mariana is located about 120 km from Belo Horizonte, the capital of Minas Gerais. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Mariana

  • Tancredo Neves International Airport: This is the closest airport to Mariana. It is located about 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mariana

  • Belo Horizonte Bus Station: This is the closest bus station to Mariana. There are regular bus services from Belo Horizonte to Mariana.

Other Options

  • You can rent a car and drive to Mariana.

  • You can take a taxi or a private transfer from the airport or the bus station to Mariana.

How to Get Around in Mariana

Mariana is a small town and you can explore most of it on foot. However, if you want to go further, you can take a taxi or rent a bike.

Where to Go Next from Mariana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mariana is located in a beautiful region with many nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best:

  • Ouro Preto: This is another colonial town located about 10 km from Mariana. It is also famous for its baroque architecture.

  • Tiradentes: This is a beautiful town located about 200 km from Mariana. It has a well-preserved colonial center and is famous for its festivals.

  • Inhotim: This is a contemporary art museum located about 70 km from Mariana. It has a large collection of modern art and beautiful gardens.

Conclusion

Mariana is a beautiful colonial town with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a food lover, Mariana has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Mariana with ease and make the most of your visit.
