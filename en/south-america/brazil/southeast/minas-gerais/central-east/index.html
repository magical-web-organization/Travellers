Introduction to Central East

Located in the southeastern state of Minas Gerais in Brazil, Central East offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and adventure for travelers of all interests. The region is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, delicious local cuisine, and rich colonial history.

List of Places to Visit in Central East

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu de Arqueologia e Paleontologia de Montes Claros - This museum features exhibits that showcase the region’s rich archaeological history.
  • Museu Regional de Diamantina - A former municipal jail transformed into a museum displaying diverse collections.
  • São Francisco de Paula Church in Serro - This magnificent church is regarded as one of the most beautiful rococo-style temples in the region.

Natural Attractions of Central East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Estadual Serra do Brigadeiro - A popular spot for hiking, offering scenic hiking trails and breathtaking waterfalls.
  • Mirante do Cruzeiro in Serro - A panoramic viewpoint surrounded by an immense natural landscape.
  • Gruta do Janelão - An impressive cave system filled with stunning rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central East

  • Estrada Real - A historic route cascading through the mountains of Minas Gerais in Brazil. The trail offers spectacular views and showcases the region’s rich colonial history.
  • Parque Nacional da Serra do Cipó - Featuring picturesque cascading waterfalls and rugged hiking trails, this park is a must-visit destination for adventure enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Central East: Activities and Tours

  • Explore historic churches and colonial architecture in Diamantina, Serro, and Montes Claros.
  • Take a scenic drive through the Estrada Real, stopping to sample local delicacies and unique products along the way.
  • Take a guided tour to visit local cachaça distilleries and learn about the production of Brazil’s famous distilled beverage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central East

Day 1: Arrive in Montes Claros and visit the Museu de Arqueologia e Paleontologia.

Day 2: Spend the day in nearby Januária for a boat ride on the Sao Francisco River and enjoy fresh fish for lunch.

Day 3: Take a road trip to Serro and hike to Mirante do Cruzeiro for panoramic views.

Day 4: Explore the historic downtown of Diamantina and its many colonial-era buildings.

Day 5: Sample local cachaça on a tour of the region’s distilleries.

Day 6: Head to Parque Estadual Serra do Brigadeiro for a day of outdoor adventure.

Day 7: Depart Montes Claros.

Food and Drink Scene of Central East

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pão de Queijo - A cheesy, chewy bread made with cassava flour and cheese that is an essential part of the Minas Gerais cuisine.
  • Frango Com Quiabo - a hearty stew made with chicken, okra, tomatoes, and other veggies.
  • Doce de Leite - A caramel-like sweet that is simply divine.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - A distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice that is widely consumed in Brazil.
  • Guaraná - A highly caffeinated soda made from the guaraná plant native to Brazil.

Where to Stay in Central East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada Casa das Abelhas, Diamantina - An idyllic guesthouse with a stunning garden and first-class service.
  • Hotel Estrela do Norte, Montes Claros - With its central location and comfortable rooms, this hotel is the perfect choice for travelers who want to be close to the action.
  • Pousada Solar de Maria, Serro - This charming and romantic inn features a cozy atmosphere and plenty of character.

What to Buy in Central East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cachaça - Be sure to pick up a bottle of local cachaça at one of the region’s many distilleries.
  • Handicrafts - From woven baskets to pottery, the region is known for its rich tradition of handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Central East

The people of Central East are friendly and welcoming, with a rich cultural heritage. Traditional dress and music also make up a part of the region’s customs. Visitors should respect local customs and be mindful of their language and dress.

Best Time to Visit Central East

The best time to visit Central East is between March and October, when the weather is pleasant and there is less chance of rainfall. Peak tourist season is between December and January.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central East

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and sightseeing opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs or religious sites.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central East

Overall, Central East is a safe place for travelers. However, it’s always important to keep your guard up, be aware of your surroundings, and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and security.

Health in Central East

Travelers should be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. It’s important to take necessary precautions, such as using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing when outside.

Getting to Central East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central East

  • Montes Claros Airport - The main airport in the region, with daily flights to and from major cities in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near Central East

  • Montes Claros Bus Station - The main bus station for the region, with buses connecting to major cities throughout Brazil.

Other Options

  • Rental car: Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Central East

Public transportation, including buses, is the most convenient option for getting around Central East. Taxis and rental cars are also available but can be relatively expensive.

Where to Go Next from Central East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ouro Preto - A historic colonial town located just a few hours away by car.
  • Parque Nacional da Serra do Cipó - A beautiful national park featuring incredible waterfalls and scenic landscapes.
  • Belmonte - A charming seaside town known for its beautiful beaches and delicious local cuisine.

Conclusion

Central East, Minas Gerais, offers a wide variety of cultural, natural, and adventure attractions for travelers seeking a unique experience in Brazil. With an abundance of historic landmarks, outdoor experiences, and delicious local cuisine and drinks, this region should be on your must-visit list.
