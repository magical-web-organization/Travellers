Introduction to Serro

Serro is a charming town located in the Central East region of Minas Gerais state, in Southeast Brazil. It is known for its colonial-era architecture, cultural heritage, and natural wonders. Serro’s historic center is listed as a National Heritage Site, and it hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Festival of Baroque Music and the Holy Week Celebrations.

List of Places to Visit in Serro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Serro is a town with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore its museums and historical sites to learn more about its history and traditions. The main attractions include:

  • Serro Museum: This museum is located in a historic mansion and displays collections of furniture, ceramics, textiles, and religious artifacts from the 18th and 19th centuries.

  • Nossa Senhora do Rosário Church: This Baroque-style church dates back to the 18th century and features beautiful paintings and sculptures by local artists.

  • Casa de Guignard Museum: This museum is dedicated to Alberto da Veiga Guignard, one of the most renowned Brazilian painters of the 20th century. Visitors can see his paintings, drawings, and personal belongings.

Natural Attractions of Serro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Serro has a diverse landscape that includes mountains, rivers, waterfalls, and trails. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and admire the stunning natural scenery. The top natural attractions are:

  • Serra do Espinhaço: This mountain range is part of the Atlantic Forest and offers hiking and biking trails, panoramic views, and wildlife watching.

  • Cachoeira do Funil: This waterfall is located in a beautiful valley and has a natural pool ideal for swimming and picnicking.

  • Parque Estadual do Rio Preto: This protected area has several trails, waterfalls, and caves, and it is home to endangered species such as the maned wolf and the giant otter.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Serro

Serro has several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore its natural and cultural attractions. The most popular routes are:

  • Caminho dos Diamantes: This trail follows the old route used by the Portuguese to transport diamonds from Minas Gerais to Rio de Janeiro. It passes through picturesque towns such as Serro and Diamantina and offers stunning views of the mountains.

  • Estrada Real: This historic route connects several towns in Minas Gerais and was used to transport gold, diamonds, and other precious minerals during the colonial era. Visitors can hike or bike along the trail and visit charming villages and cultural sites.

Best Things to Do in Serro: Activities and Tours

Serro has many activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Visit the local markets: Serro has several markets where visitors can buy handicrafts, food, and souvenirs. The most famous are the Cheese Market and the Farmer’s Market.

  • Attend a cultural event: Serro hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Festival of Baroque Music, the Holy Week Celebrations, and the Cultural Week. These events offer music, dance, theater, and other cultural activities.

  • Take a cooking class: Serro is famous for its cuisine, which includes dishes such as chicken with okra, tutu de feijão, and canjiquinha. Visitors can learn how to prepare these dishes and other local specialties through cooking classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Serro

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Serro:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Visit the Serro Museum and the Nossa Senhora do Rosário Church.

Day 2: Explore the Serra do Espinhaço and hike to the Pico do Itambé.

Day 3: Visit the Casa de Guignard Museum and attend a cultural event in the evening.

Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare local dishes.

Day 5: Visit the Parque Estadual do Rio Preto and swim in the waterfalls.

Day 6: Bike along the Estrada Real and visit the charming towns of Diamantina and Milho Verde.

Day 7: Check-out and departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Serro

Must-Try Dishes

Serro is known for its delicious cuisine, which combines African, European, and Indigenous influences. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Queijo de Serro: This cheese is made with raw cow’s milk and aged for at least 40 days. It has a creamy texture and a strong flavor.

  • Frango com Quiabo: This dish consists of chicken cooked with okra, tomatoes, onions, and spices. It is usually served with rice and beans.

  • Canjiquinha: This dish is made with ground corn, pork ribs, and vegetables. It is a typical winter food and is very filling.

Local Beverages

Serro has several local beverages that reflect its cultural heritage. Some of the most popular are:

  • Cachaça: This distilled spirit is made from sugarcane and has a strong flavor. Visitors can try different varieties and learn how it is produced.

  • Café de Minas: This coffee is grown in the mountains of Minas Gerais and has a unique flavor and aroma.

  • Chá de Cana de Milho: This tea is made from maize leaves and is believed to have medicinal properties. It is a popular drink in the region.

Where to Stay in Serro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Serro has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Pousada do Ouro: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building and offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Hotel Serra Negra: This hotel is situated in a mountainous region and has a spa, tennis court, and outdoor pool.
  • Hostel Serrote: This hostel is a great option for budget travelers and offers shared and private rooms, a kitchen, and a lounge area.

What to Buy in Serro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Serro is known for its handicrafts, food, and souvenirs. Visitors can buy items such as:

  • Queijo de Serro: This cheese is a popular souvenir and can be found in several markets and shops.

  • Handicrafts: Serro has several artisans who produce ceramics, textiles, and woodcarvings. Visitors can buy unique and handmade items.

  • Café de Minas: This coffee is a great gift for coffee lovers and can be found in several shops and supermarkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Serro

Serro has a rich cultural heritage and traditions that are deeply intertwined with its history and landscape. Visitors can experience the local culture through festivals, music, art, and cuisine. Some of the customs and beliefs include:

  • Folia de Reis: This tradition is a form of popular devotion that involves singing, dancing, and praying. It is usually performed during Christmas time and is a way to celebrate the birth of Jesus.

  • Congado: This tradition is a mix of African and Catholic elements and consists of music, dance, and colorful costumes. It is performed during religious festivals and represents the struggle of the slaves for freedom.

  • Baroque Architecture: Serro has several Baroque-style buildings that represent the legacy of the Portuguese colonial era. Visitors can admire the ornate facades and interiors of churches, mansions, and public buildings.

Best Time to Visit Serro

The best time to visit Serro is from April to September when the weather is dry and mild. The town hosts several festivals and events during this period, such as the Festival of Baroque Music and the Cultural Week. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and explore the natural and cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Serro

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Attend festivals and cultural events
  • Learn some Portuguese expressions and phrases
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect religious sites and beliefs
  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public places
  • Wander alone at night in unfamiliar areas
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Serro

Serro is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take common sense precautions, such as avoiding dark and deserted streets at night, keeping valuables out of sight, and not carrying large amounts of cash. It is recommended to use official taxis or public transportation and to be aware of pickpocketing in crowded places.

Health in Serro

Serro has several health facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. It is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and to bring any necessary medications. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and Zika virus, by using insect repellent, wearing long-sleeved clothes, and avoiding stagnant water.

Getting to Serro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Serro is located about 280 km from Belo Horizonte, the capital of Minas Gerais state, and can be reached by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Serro

The nearest airport to Serro is Tancredo Neves International Airport (CNF), located in Belo Horizonte. From there, visitors can take a bus or rent a car to Serro (about 3-4 hours).

Bus Stations Near Serro

Serro has a bus station that connects it to other towns in Minas Gerais and neighboring states. Some of the bus companies that operate in the region are Gontijo, Saritur, and Santa Cruz.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi from Belo Horizonte or other nearby cities. Another option is to join a tour or excursion that includes transportation and activities in Serro and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Serro

Serro is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances or to visit more remote attractions.

Where to Go Next from Serro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Serro is located in a region with many other attractions and destinations. Visitors can explore some of them on day trips or longer excursions. Some of the options are:

  • Diamantina: This town is located about 60 km from Serro and is known for its colonial architecture, festivals, and museums.

  • Serra do Cipó: This mountain range is located about 120 km from Serro and offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and camping sites.

  • Ouro Preto: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located about 180 km from Serro and is famous for its Baroque-style architecture, historical sites, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Serro is a hidden gem in the heart of Minas Gerais, Brazil, that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and local traditions. Visitors can explore its museums, churches, and markets, hike or bike along its trails, taste its delicious cuisine and beverages, and learn about its customs and beliefs. Serro is a perfect destination for those who want to escape the city and connect with nature and culture.
