Introduction to Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata is a region located in the Southeastern state of Minas Gerais in Brazil, dotted with charming cities and towns, beautiful natural landscapes, and historical and cultural attractions. This region has a diverse range of things to offer, ranging from the sprawling mountain ranges, picturesque countryside, and vibrant cultural escapades. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through all the must-do highlights of Zona da Mata so that you can make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Zona da Mata

If you’re planning a trip to Zona da Mata, here are some of the must-visit destinations that you should include on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zona da Mata is home to several museums and historical attractions that give us a glimpse into Brazilian culture and heritage.

  • Imperial Museum in Petropolis: The Imperial Museum is a must-visit destination in Petropolis and one of the region’s most popular attractions. The museum is housed in the former Summer Palace of Emperor Dom Pedro II and showcases various collections, including furniture, documents, and artwork.

  • Tiradentes Square in Ouro Preto: The charming city of Ouro Preto is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture and incredible history, with Tiradentes Square being the heart of this UNESCO World Heritage city. The square is home to several colonial-era buildings and the beautiful Church of St.Francis of Assisi.

  • Museu Casa Alphonsus de Guimaraens in Mariana: If you’re interested in Brazilian literature, you will love visiting the birthplace of Alphonsus de Guimaraens, a famous Brazilian author. The museum features books, letters, manuscripts of his work, and other cultural and historical documents.

Natural Attractions of Zona da Mata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zona da Mata is home to plenty of natural wonders, including lush forests and mountains, waterfalls, and natural parks.

  • Parque Estadual do Ibitipoca in Lima Duarte: Enjoy a day of hiking and immerse yourself in nature at Parque Estadual do Ibitipoca, an expansive natural park known for its lush flora, fauna, waterfalls, and the limestone caves.

  • Serra da Mantiqueira Mountain Range: Serra da Mantiqueira is a mountain range that stretches across several Brazilian states and is one of the most beautiful natural attractions in Zona da Mata. It is adorned with lush forests, waterfalls, and hiking trails boasting scenic views that will take your breath away.

  • Mata do Krambeck Natural Park: One of the most beautiful natural wonders in Zona da Mata, Mata do Krambeck Natural Park, will amaze you with its greenery, birdlife, waterfalls, and spring waterfalls. It offers a tranquil and rejuvenating escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Some popular routes include:

  • Pico do Papagaio in Paraty: For those who love trekking through the mountains, Pico do Papagaio in Paraty is a must-visit destination. The trail stretches for about 5km and takes about 3 to 4 hours to complete. The trek offers stunning views of the entire Paraty bay and the lush forest surrounding the mountain.

  • Caminho do Imperador in Petropolis: The Caminho do Imperador is a hiking trail that follows the path taken by Emperor Don Pedro II when he traveled to Petropolis in the 19th century. The trail offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and valley.

Best Things to Do in Zona da Mata: Activities and Tours

Zona da Mata offers visitors a range of activities and tours, which give travelers an opportunity to explore the region’s rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Zona da Mata:

  • Visit the Cachoeira do Tabuleiro waterfall in Conceicao do Mato Dentro: Cachoeira do Tabuleiro is one of Brazil’s highest waterfalls, and it offers a beautiful backdrop for a day of hiking, swimming, and cycling.

  • Explore the historic center of Ouro Preto: Ouro Preto’s historic center is one of Brazil’s best-preserved colonial-era cities and boasts magnificent churches, cobbled streets, and beautiful architecture.

  • Take a food tour in Tiradentes: Tiradentes is known for its excellent food scene, and taking a food tour is the best way to experience the city’s best culinary offerings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zona da Mata

To make the most of your trip to Zona da Mata, we have prepared a week-long itinerary to showcase some of the region’s highlights:

Day 1: Explore Parque Estadual do Ibitipoca and hike to the Lookout of the Three Peaks. Day 2: Visit Mariana and explore the city’s historical landmarks, including the Museu Casa Alphonsus de Guimaraens. Day 3: Spend a day in Ouro Preto, exploring the city’s well-preserved colonial architecture and vibrant culture. Day 4: Hike in Serra da Mantiqueira mountain range and enjoy the incredible scenic views. Day 5: Visit the charming city of Tiradentes and indulge in a food tour. Day 6: Travel to Petropolis and explore the Imperial Museum and Caminho do Imperador trail. Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate in Mata do Krambeck Natural Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata is home to some of the most delicious and authentic Brazilian cuisines. The food in this region is characterized by its richness, variety, and unique blend of regional ingredients. Here is some of the most popular food and drink to try in Zona da Mata:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Canastra Cheese: Canastra Cheese is a type of artisanal cheese made from cow’s milk that is aged for up to 90 days. It is a delicious and popular dish in the region.

  • Feijao Tropeiro: Feijao Tropeiro is a traditional Brazilian dish made from beans, bacon, sausage, and cassava flour.

  • ** Brigadeiro:** Brigadeiro is a chocolate truffle made from condensed milk, cocoa powder, and chocolate sprinkles, and is a popular dessert in Brazil.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: Cachaça is a type of spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice and is the main ingredient in Brazil’s national cocktail, caipirinha.

Where to Stay in Zona da Mata: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zona da Mata offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Zona da Mata:

  • Solar do Rosario in Ouro Preto: This hotel is located in the heart of Ouro Preto’s historic center, and it offers stunning views of the city. The hotel boasts modern and comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.

  • Pousada do Douro in Petropolis: Located in the beautiful city of Petropolis, Pousada do Douro is a charming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.

What to Buy in Zona da Mata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Zona da Mata has an abundance to offer. Here are some of the best products to buy in Zona da Mata:

  • Canastra Cheese: Canastra Cheese is a must-buy for cheese lovers.

  • Cachaça: Cachaça is the perfect souvenir for anyone who loves cocktails or wants to experience Brazil’s national spirit.

Local Culture and Customs of Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata has a rich cultural history, and a visit to the region will allow you to experience and appreciate Brazilian culture up close. Brazilians have a strong sense of community and friendliness, so expect to be greeted with a smile and warmth.

Best Time to Visit Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata is a destination that can be visited year-round. It has a humid subtropical climate, which is characterized by warm summers and mild winters. The best time to visit the region is during the dry season, which runs from May to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zona da Mata

Do:

  • Learn a few Portuguese phrases before your trip.

  • Try local foods and drinks.

  • Tip your tour guide or waitstaff.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in or near religious or cultural landmarks.

  • Drink tap water.

  • Carry large amounts of valuable items around with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings, especially in busy areas, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Zona da Mata

There are no major health concerns for visitors traveling to Zona da Mata. However, it is recommended to get vaccinated against yellow fever and to take precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Zona da Mata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zona da Mata is easily accessible by air or bus.

Airports Near Zona da Mata

  • Presidente Juscelino Kubitschek International Airport in Brasilia: This is the nearest major airport to Zona da Mata, located about 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Zona da Mata

  • Central Station in Belo Horizonte: This is the busiest bus station in Zona da Mata, with connections to several cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Train services: Travelling by train is an excellent option for those visiting Zona da Mata, with regular services connecting Petropolis and other regions to major cities in the area.

How to Get Around in Zona da Mata

Zona da Mata is best explored by car or by guided tours. Car rentals are available at airports in the region.

Where to Go Next from Zona da Mata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zona da Mata is bordered by several other Brazilian states, making it convenient to visit nearby destinations like:

  • Rio de Janeiro: Known for its beautiful coastline and vibrant culture, Rio de Janeiro is just a few hours drive from Zona da Mata.

  • Sao Paulo: Often considered as Brazil’s cultural capital, Sao Paulo is a popular tourist destination owing to its beautiful museums, galleries, and architecture.

Conclusion

Zona da Mata is an incredible destination for travelers looking to experience Brazil’s rich culture, history, and natural beauty. We hope that our comprehensive tour guide has provided you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Zona da Mata successfully.
