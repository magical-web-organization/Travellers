Introduction to Sananduva

Nestled on the Northwest region of Rio Grande do Sul, Sananduva is a beautiful and culturally rich city that attracts both Brazilian and international travelers. This hidden gem offers a wide range of natural attractions, including scenic views, outdoor adventures, and hiking routes, as well as unique cultural experiences with its museums and historical places. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a deep dive into everything that Sananduva has to offer, including the best places to visit, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Sananduva

Sananduva is one of the most noteworthy cities of Rio Grande do Sul, and there are many places worth seeing and discovering. Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Sananduva:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The House of Culture and Art: The House of Culture and Art is an important center for artists and artisans alike. It features art exhibits, art workshops, and other cultural events throughout the year.

  • Museum of Sananduva: The museum of Sananduva is a great place to learn about the history of this city. Its exhibitions showcase artifacts and documents from the time of the city’s founding and its evolution throughout the years.

  • The Church of Our Lady Aparecida: This iconic religious building is a must-visit for anyone interested in local traditions. The church hosts masses, weddings, and other religious events throughout the year, making it a central landmark of the city.

Natural Attractions of Sananduva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cascata do Salto: The Cascata do Salto is a breathtaking waterfall located just outside of Sananduva. It is an excellent spot for those who enjoy swimming, hiking, kayaking, and other outdoor activities.

  • Passo da Ilha State Park: The Passo da Ilha State Park is a lush area of preserved forest and beautiful views of Route RS-126. It’s a great spot for hiking, birdwatching, and exploring nature in its most raw form.

  • Belvedere Pedro Cecconello: Located on the top of a hill, the Belvedere Pedro Cecconello offers panoramic views of the city of Sananduva. It’s a great place to watch the sunset or to take beautiful photos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sananduva

For nature lovers, the hiking and biking routes in and around Sananduva are a must-visit. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Trilha do Pinhalão: A scenic trail that starts in Passo da Ilha State Park and takes you through lush forests and waterfalls.

  • Trilha da Cruz: A tough, uphill hike through the mountains that yields stunning panoramic views from the top.

  • Pedal da Sul Carolina: This 50 km bike path is a great way to explore the countryside of Sananduva at your own pace. It offers beautiful views of the landscape and typical southern Brazilian farms as you cycle through scenic rural routes.

Best Things to Do in Sananduva: Activities and Tours

Sananduva offers a variety of activities that cater to travelers of all ages and interests. Here are our recommendations:

  • EcoTrekking: EcoTrekking offers guided hiking tours to some of Sananduva’s most scenic locations such as Belvedere Pedro Cecconello, Passo da Ilha State Park, and Cascata do Salto.

  • Horseback Riding: Tourists can go horseback riding across Sananduva’s countryside on trails specially designed for horses.

  • Artisan Shopping: Sananduva offers the opportunity to support local artisans by purchasing diverse types of handicrafts, such as pottery, lacework, and leather articles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sananduva

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Sananduva.

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Sananduva’s city center, including the House of Culture and Art and Museum of Sananduva.
Day 2 Visit the Church of Our Lady Aparecida and enjoy a traditional Brazilian barbecue in a local churrascaria.
Day 3 Take a hike at Passo da Ilha State Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.
Day 4 Visit Cascata do Salto and enjoy a day of kayaking, swimming, and other outdoor activities.
Day 5 Go horseback riding in the countryside and explore Sananduva’s artisanal shops.
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby cities, such as Tapejara or Lagoa Vermelha.
Day 7 End your trip by watching the sunset at Belvedere Pedro Cecconello.

Food and Drink Scene of Sananduva

Local cuisine in Sananduva is characterized by the mix of flavors from Italian, German, and Portuguese immigrants who moved to the region between the late 1800s and early 1900s. Here are some delicious dishes and local beverages that visitors must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Churrasco: Brazilian barbecue is a must-try when traveling to Sananduva. Be sure to try the “picanha” cut.

  • Polenta: Homemade “polenta” is a traditional dish made of cornmeal with a range of different condiments.

  • Cachaça Cake: Cachaça (sugar cane alcohol) cake is a typical dessert with a filling made from cachaça.

Local Beverages

  • Cerveja Artesanal: Sananduva has several local breweries that produce delicious craft beers, such as Cervejaria Donno.

  • Agua Mineral Sananduva: Sananduva has mineral water springs that are used to produce bottled water, Sananduva’s most famous product.

Where to Stay in Sananduva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sananduva offers a range of accommodations for all types of travelers and budgets.

  • Pousada Santa Terezinha: Located in the city center, this guesthouse provides a comfortable stay with all the amenities necessary for a relaxing vacation.

  • Hotel Sananduva Palace: This hotel offers comfortable options for larger groups and families with a great value for the price. It is located in the heart of the city.

  • Hostel EcoSan: For budget-minded travelers, Hostel EcoSan offers dormitory-style accommodation with a shared kitchen and living space. Perfect for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Sananduva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sananduva is known for its artisanal crafts and local food production, making it an ideal place to buy souvenirs and gifts.

  • Lacework: Handmade lacework is a popular artisanal craft in Sananduva, and its lace makers have been perfecting the art for over 50 years.

  • Cheese: Sananduva is also known for producing delicious cheeses such as “serra do mar,” “serrano,” “colonial,” and “pecorino.”

Local Culture and Customs of Sananduva

The culture of Sananduva is strongly influenced by the traditions of the immigrant communities that exist in the region, including Italian, German, and Portuguese. The city celebrates its heritage with festivals, parades, and other cultural events throughout the year. Visitors should expect a warm welcome from the local people, who are known for their kindness and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Sananduva

The best time to visit Sananduva is between October and April, when the weather is mild and dry. During these months, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, such as trekking and outdoor sightseeing, without the risks of heavy rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sananduva

Here are some important tips to keep in mind while visiting Sananduva:

Do:

  • Learn some Portuguese before your trip to better communicate with locals.

  • Try the local cuisine and drink.

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the people of Sananduva.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind in natural attractions.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Be loud and disrespectful in public places.

Safety and Health

Sananduva is a safe city, but travelers should take measures to ensure their safety and well-being.

Safety in Sananduva

  • Keep valuables safe and in lockers in your accommodation.

  • Avoid flashing money or other valuables in public places.

  • Don’t walk alone late at night.

Health in Sananduva

  • Drink bottled or filtered water.

  • Bring insect repellent and sunscreen.

  • Wear sturdy and comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.

Getting to Sananduva: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest major airport to Sananduva is the Salgado Filho International Airport, located in Porto Alegre, about 240 km away. From there, travelers can take a bus or hire a taxi to the city of Sananduva.

Airports Near Sananduva

  • Salgado Filho International Airport: Located in Porto Alegre, this is the closest major airport to Sananduva.

  • Hercílio Luz International Airport: Located in Florianópolis, about 460 km from Sananduva.

Bus Stations Near Sananduva

  • Terminal Rodoviário de Sananduva: The central bus station of the city.

  • Terminal Rodoviário Limentão: A nearby bus station located in Lagoa Vermelha.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the city and its surroundings.

  • Taxi: Taxis are available to transport travelers to different destinations in the city.

How to Get Around in Sananduva

Sananduva is a compact city, which makes it easy to get around on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Sananduva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sananduva is located in a region of Rio Grande do Sul with many attractions. Here are some suggested destinations and excursions:

  • Lagoa Vermelha: A nearby city with a range of natural attractions and festivals throughout the year.

  • Valley of Vineyards: A perfect destination for wine lovers, located about 80 km away from Sananduva.

  • São José dos Ausentes: A mountainous city located about 150 km away that has natural landmarks such as canyons and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Sananduva is a hidden gem that offers a unique experience to travelers looking for adventure, culture, and relaxation. From outdoor activities to tasty local cuisine, Sananduva has something for everyone. Remember to take the necessary precautions, stay safe, and enjoy your trip to this amazing Brazilian city.
