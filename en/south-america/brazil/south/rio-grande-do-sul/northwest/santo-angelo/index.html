Introduction to Santo Ângelo

Located in the Northwest region of Rio Grande do Sul, Santo Ângelo is a charming city known for its cultural and historical attractions, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. The city is home to important museums and historical sites, as well as stunning landscapes perfect for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in learning about the local culture, trying new dishes, or immersing yourself in nature, Santo Ângelo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Santo Ângelo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu das Missões: This museum showcases the history and art of the Guarani-Jesuit Missions, declared a World Heritage site by UNESCO. Here you can see archaeological finds, sculptures, and other artifacts from the 17th and 18th centuries.
  • Catedral Angelopolitana: This gorgeous cathedral is one of the most iconic landmarks of Santo Ângelo. Built in neoclassical style, it has an impressive altar adorned with paintings and sculptures.
  • Casa de Cultura: This cultural center hosts art exhibitions, concerts, and theater performances. It also houses a library and a collection of antique furniture.
  • Ruínas de São Miguel Arcanjo: These ruins are the remains of the São Miguel Arcanjo church, part of the Guarani-Jesuit Missions. The site was partially restored and is now an open-air museum.

Natural Attractions of Santo Ângelo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque das Tuias: This park has over 70 hectares of green space, with trails, picnic areas, and a small lake. It’s a great place to hike, bike, or just relax and enjoy the nature.
  • Lago Azul: This blue lagoon is located about 20 km from Santo Ângelo and is a popular spot for swimming, kayaking, and camping. Surrounding by forested hills, it offers a peaceful and beautiful setting.
  • Cerro do Botão: This hilltop offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding hills. It’s an easy trek, with a trail that leads to the top and a small chapel dedicated to Our Lady of Lourdes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santo Ângelo

  • Circuito dos Lagos: This 34-km circuit connects the three main lakes of Santo Ângelo - Tuiuti, Madeira, and Dourado. It’s a great option for a long bike ride, with scenic views and some hills.
  • Trilha do Poção: This 3-km trail leads to a natural pool, where you can swim and enjoy the waterfall. It’s a moderate hike, with some steep sections and rocky terrain.
  • Circuito das Cachoeiras: This 5-km circuit passes by three waterfalls - Salto do Gritador, Salto do André, and Salto do Yucumã. It’s a challenging hike, with some river crossings and slippery rocks.

Best Things to Do in Santo Ângelo: Activities and Tours

  • City Tour: Join a guided tour to explore the main attractions of Santo Ângelo, including the museums, cathedrals, and cultural centers.
  • Canoeing: Rent a canoe or kayak and paddle around the lakes of Santo Ângelo, enjoying the nature and spotting birds and fish.
  • Day Trip to São Miguel das Missões: Visit the nearby town of São Miguel das Missões to see more of the Guarani-Jesuit Missions. The town also has a beautiful church and a cultural center.

Food and Drink Scene of Santo Ângelo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Churrasco: This Brazilian barbecue consists of grilled meat - usually beef, pork, or chicken - served with sides such as rice, beans, and salad. The meat is typically seasoned with salt and served medium-rare.
  • Arroz de Carreteiro: This hearty dish is made with rice, beef, onions, and peppers, cooked together until the rice is tender and the meat is flavorful.
  • Espetinho: These skewers are popular street food in Brazil, and Santo Ângelo has a variety of delicious options. Look for espetinho de frango (chicken), espetinho de queijo (cheese), or espetinho misto (mixed meats).

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: This classic Brazilian cocktail combines cachaça (a distilled spirit made from sugarcane), lime, and sugar. It’s a refreshing and potent drink that goes well with spicy food.
  • Mate: This herbal tea made from yerba mate leaves is a popular beverage in southern Brazil. It can be served hot or iced, and is often sweetened with sugar or honey.
  • Cerveja Artesanal: Many small breweries have emerged in the region, offering a range of unique and tasty beers. Look for options like IPA, wheat, or stout.

Where to Stay in Santo Ângelo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Maerkli: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a restaurant serving local cuisine. It’s located in the city center, close to the main attractions.
  • Pousada do Bosque: This guesthouse has a rustic and cozy atmosphere, with rooms surrounded by nature. It has a swimming pool, a sauna, and a bar, and is located near Parque das Tuias.
  • Hostel Santo Ângelo: This hostel is a budget-friendly option for travelers who prefer a communal setting. It has dorm rooms and private rooms, a kitchen, and a lounge area. It’s located in the city center, close to bars and restaurants.

What to Buy in Santo Ângelo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artesanato: Santo Ângelo has a thriving handicraft scene, with many artists producing unique and beautiful pieces. Look for pottery, woodcarvings, textiles, and jewelry.
  • Erva-Mate: If you enjoy mate tea, consider buying some high-quality yerba mate leaves or a gourd and a bombilla (straw). These traditional utensils are easy to find in local shops.
  • Chimarrão: This other popular drink in the region is prepared with hot water and yerba mate, but is sipped through a special metal straw, called bomba. You can buy bombas and containers for preparing the drink in specialty stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Santo Ângelo

  • Guarani-Jesuit Missions: The region of Santo Ângelo has a rich history of Jesuit missions that converted the Guarani indigenous people to Christianity. Visitors can learn about this unique cultural exchange by visiting the museums and historical sites.
  • Language: Portuguese is the official language of Brazil, but Spanish also widely spoken in the border areas. English is not very common, so it’s a good idea to learn some basic Portuguese phrases.
  • Hospitality: Brazilians are known for being friendly and welcoming, so don’t be surprised if locals strike up a conversation or offer you a coffee.

Best Time to Visit Santo Ângelo

The best time to visit Santo Ângelo is from September to April, when the weather is warmer and drier. However, the city can get crowded during holidays and summer vacation, so it’s important to book accommodations in advance. If you’re interested in attending cultural events, check the calendar of Casa de Cultura, which hosts concerts, festivals, and theater productions throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santo Ângelo

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including churrasco and mate tea.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Explore the nature and outdoor activities, such as hiking and canoeing.

Don’t:

  • Worry about mosquitoes, which are not a huge problem in Santo Ângelo.
  • Walk alone at night, especially in less crowded areas.
  • Leave without trying a caipirinha or a cold beer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santo Ângelo

Overall, Santo Ângelo is a safe city for travelers. However, it’s always important to take basic precautions, such as using common sense, avoiding dark alleys, and keeping an eye on your belongings. Street crime, such as pickpocketing and robbery, can happen, particularly in touristy areas. The most important thing is to stay alert and avoid risky situations.

Health in Santo Ângelo

No specific vaccines are required to visit Santo Ângelo, but it’s recommended to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations. Mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue and Zika, are not very common in the region, but it’s advisable to use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants during dawn and dusk. Tap water is safe to drink in Santo Ângelo, but if you prefer, you can buy bottled water in supermarkets and stores.

Getting to Santo Ângelo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santo Ângelo

  • Aeroporto de Santo Ângelo: This small domestic airport operates flights to Porto Alegre and other cities in Rio Grande do Sul.

Bus Stations Near Santo Ângelo

  • Rodoviária de Santo Ângelo: This is the main bus station of the city, with regular services to Porto Alegre and other destinations in Brazil and Argentina.

Other Options

  • Car rental: There are several car rental companies in Santo Ângelo, which is a good option if you want to explore the surroundings at your own pace.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and Uber are available in Santo Ângelo, but make sure to negotiate the fare beforehand.

How to Get Around in Santo Ângelo

Most of the attractions in Santo Ângelo are located in the city center, so it’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to visit the outskirts or go to a nearby town, it’s advisable to rent a car or use public transportation. Buses are the main form of public transportation and run regularly between Santo Ângelo and other cities in the region. Taxis and Uber are also available, but they can be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Santo Ângelo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • São Miguel das Missões: This town is home to some of the best-preserved ruins of the Guarani-Jesuit Missions and is only 40 km from Santo Ângelo.
  • Santa Rosa: This city is located 95 km from Santo Ângelo and is known for its beautiful parks and festivals.
  • Santo Cristo: This small town is located about 25 km from Santo Ângelo and is famous for its dairy products, such as cheese and butter.

Conclusion

Santo Ângelo is a hidden gem in Rio Grande do Sul, with a rich history, stunning landscapes, and delicious food. Whether you’re interested in visiting museums, hiking in the woods, or trying local delicacies, this charming city has plenty to offer. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be ready to explore Santo Ângelo and make the most of your stay.
