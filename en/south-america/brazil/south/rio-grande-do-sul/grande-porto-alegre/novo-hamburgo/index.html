A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Novo Hamburgo, Brazil

Novo Hamburgo is a vibrant city in the Rio Grande do Sul state of Brazil. It is known for its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in exploring museums or trying out some adventure sports, Novo Hamburgo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Novo Hamburgo

Here is a list of must-visit attractions in Novo Hamburgo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National Shoe Museum
  2. Historic Center
  3. Fenac
  4. Novo Hamburgo Municipal Museum
  5. Igreja Sao Luis Gonzaga

Natural Attractions of Novo Hamburgo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lomba Grande Park
  2. Moda Litoral Park
  3. Morro Ferrabraz
  4. Guarani Aquatic Park
  5. Schmitt-Presser Botanic Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novo Hamburgo

  1. Morro Ferrabraz: This mountain is the highest point of the region and provides a scenic view of Novo Hamburgo and neighboring cities. You can hike up the mountain and enjoy the view from the top.
  2. Lomba Grande Park: This park has a beautiful trail that goes through lush greenery and provides a perfect spot for a hike or bike ride.
  3. Schmitt-Presser Botanic Park: The park has several hiking trails with varying difficulty levels, making it suitable for everyone.

Best Things to Do in Novo Hamburgo: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the National Shoe Museum to learn about the history of shoe making in Novo Hamburgo.
  2. Head down to Fenac to experience the local culture and attend an event or exhibition.
  3. Visit Morro Ferrabraz for a breathtaking view of the city and enjoy outdoor sports like paragliding and hang gliding.
  4. Go on a scenic drive through the Lomba Grande Park and admire the natural beauty around you.
  5. Explore the Schmitt-Presser Botanic Park and its beautiful hiking trails.

Week-Long Itinerary for Novo Hamburgo

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip in Novo Hamburgo:

Day Activities
1 Explore the National Shoe Museum and learn about the history of shoe making in Novo Hamburgo.
2 Visit the Historic Center and walk through the streets to admire the unique architecture.
3 Spend the day at Lomba Grande Park and enjoy a hike through the lush greenery.
4 Check out the Fenac and attend an exhibition or event.
5 Head out to Morro Ferrabraz and enjoy outdoor sports like paragliding and hang gliding.
6 Visit the Schmitt-Presser Botanic Park and explore its beautiful hiking trails.
7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine and drinks while admiring the city’s beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Novo Hamburgo

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Novo Hamburgo:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Churrasco: Brazilian BBQ with different types of meat cooked over an open flame.
  2. Bolo de Aipim: A sweet cake made from cassava, coconut milk and sugar.
  3. Feijoada: A traditional Brazilian stew made with black beans, pork and beef.

Local Beverages

  1. Chimarrão: A tea-like beverage made with hot water and yerba mate leaves, a popular drink in Brazil and the southern cone.
  2. Caipirinha: A cocktail made with cachaça, lime, sugar, and ice.

Where to Stay in Novo Hamburgo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Ibis Novo Hamburgo - Budget hotel in the city center.
  2. Swan Tower Novo Hamburgo - Mid-range hotel with excellent amenities.
  3. Locanda Hotel - Luxury boutique hotel with spa and restaurant.

What to Buy in Novo Hamburgo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Leather Shoes: As Novo Hamburgo is known for its shoe industry, it’s a great place to buy high-quality leather shoes.
  2. Handicrafts: Explore the local markets to find handmade crafts and souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Novo Hamburgo

Portuguese is the official language of Brazil. Novo Hamburgo has a rich cultural history and customs, such as the famous ‘churrasco’ or Brazilian BBQ. It is important to dress appropriately when visiting churches, and politeness is highly valued in Brazilian society.

Best Time to Visit Novo Hamburgo

The best time to visit Novo Hamburgo is from March to June or from September to November when the weather is mild, and accommodation is affordable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novo Hamburgo

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  2. Learn about the local culture and history by visiting museums and landmarks.
  3. Dress comfortably and appropriately for the weather and activities.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water, always drink bottled water.
  2. Be disrespectful towards the local culture or customs.
  3. Wander alone in unfamiliar and unfamiliar areas at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Novo Hamburgo

As with any city, it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night, particularly in isolated areas.

Health in Novo Hamburgo

It is important to drink bottled water and seek medical attention if you experience any health problems during your stay in Novo Hamburgo.

Getting to Novo Hamburgo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Novo Hamburgo

  1. Salgado Filho International Airport (POA): The closest airport to Novo Hamburgo, approximately 35 km away.

Bus Stations Near Novo Hamburgo

  1. Novo Hamburgo Bus Station: Located in the city center, operates buses to various destinations.

Other Options

There are options to rent a car or hire a private taxi to get to Novo Hamburgo.

How to Get Around in Novo Hamburgo

Public transportation in Novo Hamburgo is provided by buses and taxis. Alternatively, renting a car is also an option for those who prefer to drive themselves.

Where to Go Next from Novo Hamburgo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Porto Alegre: The capital of Rio Grande do Sul, Porto Alegre has many historic and cultural landmarks to explore.
  2. Gramado: A charming mountain town known for its European architecture, stunning gardens, and Christmas markets.
  3. Canela: A neighboring town to Gramado known for its stunning natural beauty and adventure sports.

Conclusion

Novo Hamburgo is a perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the history, culture, and natural beauty of Brazil. With its rich cultural landmarks, hiking trails, lovely parks, and scenic views, it’s a place that you will surely fall in love with.
