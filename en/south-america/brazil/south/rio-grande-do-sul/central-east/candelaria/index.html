Candelária is a small town in Rio Grande do Sul, one of the southernmost states of Brazil. The town boasts of rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Candelária has something to offer to everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, activities, and experiences that Candelária has to offer.

Introduction to Candelária

Candelária is a town known for its rich cultural heritage and diverse natural landscapes. The town was settled in the early 19th century and has since grown into a bustling hub of activity. The main attraction of the town is its beautiful churches, which dot the landscape and serve as reminders of its long and storied history. The town also offers a wide range of outdoor activities, from hiking to biking, which allows visitors to explore and experience the beauty of the surrounding countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Candelária

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, then there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore in Candelária. The first place to start is the Candelária Museum, which offers a fascinating insight into the town’s rich cultural heritage. The museum displays a wide range of historical artifacts and exhibits, including traditional costumes, weapons, and instruments.

Another must-visit attraction is the Nossa Senhora da Conceição Church, which is one of the town’s main landmarks. The church is known for its elegant facade and stunning architecture, which blends Gothic and Baroque styles. It is also home to the image of Nossa Senhora da Conceição, which is a revered religious symbol in the town.

Natural Attractions of Candelária: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Candelária is also known for its stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities. One of the must-visit attractions is the Rincão dos Kroeff Nature Reserve, which is located just outside the town. The reserve offers a wide range of hiking trails and scenic views, which allow visitors to explore the surrounding countryside and experience its beauty and diversity.

Another popular outdoor activity in Candelária is bird watching. The town is surrounded by a rich diversity of flora and fauna, and is home to a wide range of bird species. Visitors can take guided bird watching tours, which allow them to explore the surrounding countryside and discover its hidden treasures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Candelária

Candelária offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails, which allows visitors to explore the town and its surrounding countryside. One of the most popular trails in the area is the Rincão dos Kroeff Trail, which takes visitors through the nature reserve and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Another good option is the Candelária - Santa Cruz Trail, which offers a challenging but rewarding experience for hikers and bikers alike. The trail takes visitors through some of the area’s most beautiful natural scenery, and offers a chance to explore the surrounding countryside and discover its hidden treasures.

Best Things to Do in Candelária: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions, Candelária also offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. One of the most popular options is the river rafting tours, which takes visitors on an exciting journey down the River Taquari. The tour offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and allows visitors to experience the thrill of river rafting.

Another good option is the wine tasting tours, which take visitors on a journey through the town’s wineries and vineyards. The region is known for its excellent wines and offers a chance to taste some of the finest local produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Candelária

Day 1: Explore the Candelária Museum and learn about the town’s rich cultural heritage. Day 2: Take a guided bird watching tour and explore the town’s diverse natural scenery. Day 3: Go hiking on the Rincão dos Kroeff Trail and discover the hidden treasures of the nature reserve. Day 4: Visit the Nossa Senhora da Conceição Church, which is one of the town’s main landmarks. Day 5: Go river rafting on the River Taquari and experience the thrill of adventure. Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and discover the region’s excellent wines and vineyards. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the town’s laid-back vibe.

Food and Drink Scene of Candelária

Must-Try Dishes

Candelária is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, which is a reflection of its rich cultural heritage. Some of the must-try dishes include the traditional feijoada, which is a stew of beans, meat, and vegetables, and the churrasco, which is a Brazilian barbecue.

Another popular dish is the xixo, which is a skewer of meat and vegetables grilled over an open flame. It is usually served with a side of rice and beans and is a staple of Brazilian cuisine.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Candelária offers a wide range of options to visitors. One of the most popular drinks in the region is chimarrão, which is a traditional herbal tea made from a local plant called yerba mate. The drink is usually sipped from a gourd using a metal straw, and is a staple of the local culture.

Another popular drink is the caipirinha, which is a cocktail made from cachaca, lime, and sugar. It is a refreshing and delicious drink that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Candelária: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Candelária offers a wide range of accommodation options to visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Some of the best options include the Hotel Poente, which is a comfortable and well-appointed hotel located in the heart of the town, and the Pousada do Alemão, which is a charming guesthouse located on the outskirts of the town.

What to Buy in Candelária: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Candelária offers a wide range of options for visitors. One of the most popular options is the local handicrafts, which are made by the skilled artisans in the region. These include hand-woven rugs, pottery, and wood carvings, among others.

Another good option is the local wine, which is a reflection of the region’s rich wine-making tradition. Visitors can purchase bottles of the local wine from the town’s wineries and vineyards, and bring home a taste of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Candelária

Candelária is known for its laid-back and relaxed culture, which is a reflection of its rural lifestyle. The town has a strong sense of community and is known for its friendly and welcoming people. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs, and experience the unique charm of the town for themselves.

Best Time to Visit Candelária

The best time to visit Candelária is during the spring and autumn seasons, which offer comfortable temperatures and mild weather conditions. The town can get quite hot and humid during the summer months, and can be quite chilly during the winter season.

Getting to Candelária: Transportation Options and Logistics

Candelária is well-connected to other parts of Brazil, and visitors can easily reach the town via air, road, and rail. The town is located about 150km from Porto Alegre, the capital of Rio Grande do Sul, and is easily accessible by bus or car.

The closest airport to Candelária is the Salgado Filho International Airport in Porto Alegre, which offers regular flights to major cities across Brazil and South America.
