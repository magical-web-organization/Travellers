Introduction to Campo Largo

Located in the state of Paraná, in the southern region of Brazil, Campo Largo is a charming city known for its natural beauty and rich history. The region is home to vast green areas, waterfalls, and hills, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

But Campo Largo isn’t just about nature – the city also has a fascinating cultural heritage, with museums, churches, and historical landmarks that showcase the region’s past. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Campo Largo has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Campo Largo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Campo Largo has a rich history that is visible in its many museums and historical landmarks:

  • Museu Histórico de Campo Largo: This museum is housed in a beautiful colonial-style building and showcases the history and culture of the region. Visitors can see artifacts from the indigenous population, as well as items from the colonial era.

  • Igreja Matriz São Pedro: This beautiful church is one of the oldest in Campo Largo and is a must-visit for anyone interested in architecture or religious history.

  • Parque Histórico de Carambeí: While not in Campo Largo itself, this open-air museum is located nearby and is well worth a visit. It showcases the history of Dutch immigration to Brazil and features a replica of a traditional Dutch village.

Natural Attractions of Campo Largo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Campo Largo is blessed with stunning natural scenery, from rolling hills to crystal-clear waterfalls. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in the region:

  • Cachoeira da Mariquinha: This stunning waterfall is located just outside Campo Largo and is easily accessible via a short hike. The views are breathtaking and the water is perfect for swimming on a hot day.

  • Parque Estadual de Vila Velha: This state park is located about an hour from Campo Largo and features unique rock formations that date back millions of years. Visitors can hike the trails that wind through the park, taking in the sights and sounds of this natural wonder.

  • Represa do Passaúna: This large reservoir is a popular destination for boating and fishing, and the surrounding area features several hiking trails and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campo Largo

For those who love to explore the great outdoors on foot or by bike, Campo Largo offers plenty of options. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Trilha dos Pioneiros: This trail takes visitors through the beautiful countryside around Campo Largo, passing small farms and rolling hills. It’s an easy hike that’s perfect for families with young children.

  • Ciclovia Passaúna: This 16 km bike path follows the shores of Represa do Passaúna and offers stunning views of the water and the surrounding hills.

  • Trilha do Arroio Tigre: This moderate hiking trail takes visitors through lush forests and past several waterfalls. It’s a great way to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and connect with nature.

Best Things to Do in Campo Largo: Activities and Tours

Whether you prefer adrenaline-fueled adventures or something more laid-back, Campo Largo has something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in the region:

  • Rafting on Rio Iguaçu: This thrilling activity takes visitors down the rapids of one of Brazil’s most famous rivers. It’s an unforgettable experience that’s sure to get your heart racing.

  • Horseback Riding in the Countryside: For a more relaxed outdoor experience, take a horseback ride through the stunning countryside around Campo Largo. It’s a great way to unwind and take in the scenery.

  • City Tour of Campo Largo: This guided tour takes visitors through the city’s historic center, showing off landmarks like Igreja Matriz São Pedro and the Museu Histórico de Campo Largo. It’s a great way to learn about the region’s rich history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Campo Largo

If you have a week to spend in Campo Largo, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrival/Explore Downtown Campo Largo
  • Day 2: Trek to Cachoeira da Mariquinha/Visit Museu Histórico de Campo Largo
  • Day 3: Visit Parque Histórico de Carambeí/Relax in the Countryside
  • Day 4: Hike Trilha do Arroio Tigre/Rafting on Rio Iguaçu
  • Day 5: Bike the Ciclovia Passaúna/Visit Igreja Matriz São Pedro
  • Day 6: Explore Parque Estadual de Vila Velha
  • Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Campo Largo

One of the best things about traveling is trying new foods and drinks, and Campo Largo won’t disappoint. Here are some foods and drinks you won’t want to miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado: This hearty stew is made with beef, bacon, and a variety of spices. It’s slow-cooked for hours until the meat is tender and served with rice or cassava flour.

  • Pão de Queijo: This cheesy bread is a staple in Brazil and is especially popular in the southern region. It’s perfect for breakfast or as a snack.

  • Pastel: This fried pastry is filled with a variety of savory fillings like ground beef, cheese, or chicken. It’s a popular street food in Brazil.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This distilled spirit is made from sugarcane and is the main ingredient in Brazil’s national cocktail, the Caipirinha.

  • Chimarrão: This traditional tea is made from yerba mate leaves and is a popular social drink in Brazil’s southern region.

  • Cerveja: Brazil is known for its beer, and in Campo Largo you can find a variety of local and national brands.

Where to Stay in Campo Largo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Campo Largo has a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hospedaria Refugio: This charming guesthouse is located in the countryside just outside Campo Largo and features comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.

  • Hotel Campo Largo: This modern hotel is located in the heart of the city and features spacious rooms, a gym, and a rooftop pool.

  • Hostel Campo Largo: For budget travelers, this hostel offers shared dorm rooms and private rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Campo Largo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Campo Largo, shop for these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Handmade Crafts: Campo Largo is known for its artisanal crafts, including pottery, lacework, and woodcarving.

  • Cachaça: Pick up a bottle of this traditional Brazilian spirit to take home with you.

  • Yerba Mate Tea: The southern region of Brazil is known for its yerba mate tea, and there are several local brands that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Campo Largo

When visiting Campo Largo, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: Brazil is a fairly conservative country, and visitors should avoid wearing revealing or overly casual clothing.

  • Greeting Etiquette: Brazilians are known for being friendly and outgoing, and a handshake or kiss on the cheek is a common way to greet someone.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Brazil, but leaving a small amount for good service is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Campo Largo

The best time to visit Campo Largo is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is cooler and there is less rain, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campo Largo

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Campo Largo has a vibrant food scene, and trying the local dishes should be at the top of your list.

  • Explore the countryside: The natural beauty of the region is one of its main attractions, so be sure to spend some time exploring the hills, waterfalls, and forests.

  • Learn about the local culture: Campo Largo has a rich history and culture, and taking some time to learn about it will enhance your travel experience.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful: Brazilians are generally friendly and welcoming, but being disrespectful to their culture or customs is not acceptable.

  • Ignore safety precautions: While Campo Largo is generally a safe destination, visitors should still take basic safety precautions like avoiding dimly lit areas at night and not flashing valuables.

  • Litter: Respect the natural beauty of the region by not littering or damaging the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Campo Largo

Campo Largo is generally a safe destination, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions like:

  • Avoiding dimly lit areas at night
  • Not flashing valuables
  • Being aware of your surroundings

Health in Campo Largo

Visitors to Campo Largo should be up to date on routine vaccinations like measles, mumps, and rubella. It’s also a good idea to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and yellow fever.

Getting to Campo Largo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Campo Largo

The closest airport to Campo Largo is the Afonso Pena International Airport (CWB), which is located about an hour’s drive away. Visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Campo Largo.

Bus Stations Near Campo Largo

Campo Largo has a bus station that serves several regional and national destinations. Visitors can take buses to and from cities like Curitiba, São Paulo, and Florianópolis.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Campo Largo by renting a car or taking a private shuttle.

How to Get Around in Campo Largo

The best way to get around Campo Largo is by car or taxi. The city has a limited public transportation system, and many of the natural attractions and hiking trails are located outside the city center.

Where to Go Next from Campo Largo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spend in the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Curitiba: This vibrant city is located just a short drive from Campo Largo and is known for its art museums, botanical gardens, and lively cultural scene.

  • Ilha do Mel: This beautiful island off the coast of Paraná is a popular destination for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts.

  • Foz do Iguaçu: This world-famous destination is home to the stunning Iguaçu Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in the world.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Campo Largo has something to offer. From its stunning natural beauty to its rich history and culture, this charming city is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.
