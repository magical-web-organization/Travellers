Curitiba, located in the southern region of Brazil, is a vibrant city that attracts visitors with its cultural and natural riches. With its world-renowned architecture, ecological parks, and rich cultural heritage, it is no wonder that Curitiba is often referred to as Brazil’s cultural and natural gem. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip, so let’s get started!

Introduction to Curitiba

Curitiba, the capital of Paraná state, is known for its well-planned urban infrastructure, public transportation, parks, and cultural events. The city has a rich history dating back to the early 17th century when it was founded as a small gold-mining village. Today, it is a metropolitan city that houses over 1.8 million people and offers plenty of cultural and natural attractions for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Curitiba

Here is a list of top places to visit in Curitiba:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Oscar Niemeyer Museum - dedicated to the works of the famous Brazilian architect, this museum features several exhibitions and stunning art installations.
  2. Museu Paranaense - showcasing the cultural and historical heritage of Paraná state through various artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  3. Metropolitan Cathedral - a beautiful church with an impressive stained-glass façade depicting the history of Christianity in Brazil.
  4. Largo da Ordem - a historical district that houses several museums, monuments, and beautiful colonial-style buildings.

Natural Attractions of Curitiba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Botanical Garden - a lush green space with over 50,000 plant species, including a French-style greenhouse and a serene Japanese garden.
  2. Tanguá Park - a stunning park that overlooks a man-made lake, with hiking trails, a waterfall, and a lookout tower offering panoramic views of the city.
  3. Parque Barigui - a popular park with a beautiful lake that attracts locals and tourists alike for jogging, picnicking, and cycling.
  4. Serra Verde Express - a scenic train ride through the lush Atlantic Forest that offers a stunning view of Paraná state’s countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Curitiba

If you love hiking or biking, Curitiba has plenty of trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes to consider:

  1. Parque Tanguá Hiking Trail - a 5km trail through the Tanguá Park with stunning views of the lake and the city.
  2. Passeio Público Hiking Trail - a short 1km trail through the Passeio Público park ideal for a quick morning hike.
  3. Pico Paraná Hiking Trail - This difficult trail leads to the highest peak in southern Brazil with a height of 1,877 meters.
  4. Ciclofaixa de Lazer Biking Trail - Every Sunday from 7 a.m. to 2 p.m., a designated roadway for bikes-only is provided and winds through inviting city streets.

Best Things to Do in Curitiba: Activities and Tours

Curitiba offers a range of activities and tours to suit different interests and budgets. Here are some of the best things to do in Curitiba:

  1. Curitiba City Tour - a guided tour of the city that covers important landmarks and attractions.
  2. Curitiba by Night Tour - a tour that showcases the city’s nightlife, including bars, restaurants, and cultural events.
  3. Brazilian Coffee Experience - a tour that introduces visitors to the history and culture of coffee in Brazil.
  4. Paranaguá Bay Cruise - a day trip to Paranaguá Bay, a lively seaport village with a rich history and culture dating back to the 16th century.

Food and Drink Scene of Curitiba

Curitiba’s food scene is a mix of traditional Brazilian cuisine with a modern twist. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Barreado - a slow-cooked beef dish, seasoned with onion, garlic, and herbs, and served with cassava flour.
  2. Pão de queijo - a savory cheese bread made with cassava flour and cheese.
  3. Coxinha - a fried dough stuffed with cheese, chicken, and spices.
  4. Brigadeiro - Brazil’s famous chocolate truffle made with condensed milk.

Local Beverages

  1. Caipirinha - Brazil’s national cocktail made with cachaça, lime juice, and sugar.
  2. Chimarrão - a traditional tea-like beverage made with yerba mate and hot water.
  3. Cerveja - Curitiba is known for its craft beer scene with several breweries offering unique artisanal brews.
  4. Guaraná - a popular Brazilian soda made with the guaraná berry.

Where to Stay in Curitiba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Curitiba has several accommodation options ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some popular options:

  1. Four Points by Sheraton Curitiba - A centrally located hotel offering comfortable rooms and amenities such as a fitness center and a rooftop bar.
  2. Nomaa Hotel - An elegant boutique hotel with spacious rooms and a gourmet restaurant.
  3. Curitiba Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel located close to the city center with clean rooms and free breakfast.
  4. Pousada Serra Verde - A charming guesthouse located in scenic Serra Verde with beautiful views and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Curitiba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Curitiba offers several unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for family and friends. Here are some popular options:

  1. Craft Beer - Curitiba’s breweries offer unique artisanal brews that make great gifts.
  2. Handicrafts - Largo da Ordem and Feira do Largo da Ordem are popular spots for purchasing handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  3. Coffee - Brazil is known for its coffee, and Curitiba has several gourmet coffee shops and roasters that offer high-quality beans.
  4. Artisanal Cheese - Curitiba and Paraná state are known for their artisanal cheese production, featuring different varieties and flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Curitiba

Curitiba is a multicultural city with influences from European, African, and indigenous cultures. The local culture is a mix of traditional and modern, with a strong appreciation for art, music, and nature. Brazilian Portuguese is the primary language spoken in the city, and English is also spoken in some tourist areas. Tipping in restaurants is customary but not required.

Best Time to Visit Curitiba

The best time to visit Curitiba is during the spring and fall, from September to November and March to May, respectively. The weather is mild and pleasant during these seasons, with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Curitiba

Do:

  1. Try local food and drinks, including the famous Brazilian coffee and caipirinha.
  2. Visit the Botanical Garden and Oscar Niemeyer Museum.
  3. Get familiar with the history and culture of Paraná state through the museums and historical landmarks.
  4. Enjoy the parks and outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious or historical sites.
  2. Leave your belongings unattended in crowded areas or public transport.
  3. Drink tap water, always preferring bottled water.
  4. Hike in a new place without a guide or an experienced person.

Safety and Health

Safety in Curitiba

Like any other metropolitan area, Curitiba has its share of crime, so it’s essential to take precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables, avoiding unlit areas and deserted streets at night, and always locking your hotel room door. It is advisable to travel with trusted guide or group tours in case you are not familiar with the areas and customs of Curitiba.

Health in Curitiba

The tap water in Curitiba is generally safe to drink, however, it is recommended to go to the supermarket or grocery store nearby to purchase bottled water. It is also advisable to bring mosquito repellent, especially during the rainy season, to avoid dengue and other mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Curitiba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Curitiba is accessible by several modes of transportation, including air, bus, and car. Here are some of the best options:

Airports Near Curitiba

Afonso Pena International Airport (CWB) is located about 17 km from Curitiba’s city center. It is serviced by several major airlines, including LATAM, Gol, and Azul.

Bus Stations Near Curitiba

Curitiba’s main bus station is the Rodoferroviária, where several bus companies operate regular services to and from other Brazilian cities. It is also possible to enter Curitiba by bus from other South American destinations like Uruguay or Argentina.

Other Options

Driving to Curitiba is also an option if you are coming from nearby cities. However, during peak hours, the traffic can be heavy. Ridesharing services such as Uber and Lyft are available for those who want to explore the city without having to navigate public transport.

How to Get Around in Curitiba

Curitiba has an excellent public transportation system, including buses and trams, that are interconnected and affordable. It is also possible to navigate the city’s neighborhoods by bike, and several bike trails are strategically placed throughout the city center.

Where to Go Next from Curitiba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Curitiba is an excellent base for exploring Paraná state and the surrounding region. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  1. Morretes - a charming colonial town famous for its barreado.
  2. Foz do Iguaçu - home to the world-famous Iguazu Falls and a natural wonder of the world.
  3. Ilha do Mel - a scenic island located off the coast of Paraná state with stunning beaches and eco-trails.
  4. Florianópolis - an island city located in Santa Catarina state known for its stunning beaches and scenic views.

Conclusion

Curitiba is a city with much to offer. Its blend of natural beauty and cultural richness has made it one of Brazil’s most popular tourist destinations. With its well-connected public transportation, stunning parks, museums, historical sites, natural attractions, and delicious cuisine, Curitiba leaves a lasting impression on anyone who visits. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to Curitiba and enables you to enjoy everything that this beautiful Brazilian city has to offer.
