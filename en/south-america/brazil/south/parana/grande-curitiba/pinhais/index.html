Introduction to Pinhais

Located in the Grande Curitiba region of Paraná, Pinhais is a vibrant city known for its rich culture, breathtaking natural beauty, and various outdoor activities. Its strategic location in the heart of Brazil’s south region makes it an ideal destination for tourists looking to explore the many wonders of the country.

From museums and historical sites to hiking and biking trails, Pinhais has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide introduces you to the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and everything else you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Pinhais.

List of Places to Visit in Pinhais

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museu da Porcelana Schmidt

The Museu da Porcelana Schmidt is dedicated to the history of one of the most important porcelain manufacturers in Brazil, the Porcelain Schmidt. The museum offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the creative process behind the production of porcelain and to discover the history of the company.

Igreja de Nosso Senhor do Bonfim

Built in colonial style in 1944, the Igreja de Nosso Senhor do Bonfim is a beautiful church to visit in Pinhais. The church has a unique altar made of Brazilian hardwood, and the entrance is adorned by a bell tower that can be seen from far away.

Jardim Botânico de Pinhais

The Jardim Botânico de Pinhais has a vast collection of plants and several greenhouses with rare and exotic plants. The garden is an ideal place to relax, take a stroll, and also to understand the importance of the conservation of nature.

Natural Attractions of Pinhais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rio Iguaçu

Rio Iguaçu is a must-visit for any nature lover. It is one of the most important rivers in Paraná, flowing into the Paraná River. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing or kayaking and the beautiful view of the valley surrounding it.

Parque das Águas

Parque das Águas is a park perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park has hiking trails, beautiful picnic spots, and many water springs. Visitors can also enjoy activities such as rafting, kayaking, and mountain biking.

Pedreira Paulo Leminski

Pedreira Paulo Leminski is an open-air amphitheater used for cultural events featuring a pleasant forest and natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pinhais

Trilha do Ribeirão

The Trilha do Ribeirão is a 2.5km hiking trail that winds along the Ribeirão river. Visitors can appreciate the breathtaking views of the river, witness the exotic flora and fauna, and take a refreshing dip in the river at the end of the trail.

Ciclovias

Pinhais has a vast network of cycling lanes, providing an eco-friendly way of exploring the city and surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Pinhais: Activities and Tours

Rota do Vinho

The Rota do Vinho is a wine tour through the countryside of Curitiba, one of the nearby towns from Pinhais. Visitors can explore the vineyards, taste the wines, and learn about the wine-making process. The tour also includes a visit to a famous cheese-making factory.

Pesque Pague São José

Pesque Pague São José is a great place to relax and enjoy a day of fishing, surrounded by the beautiful vegetation of the Parana State Forest, visitor can catch various fishes and enjoy them afterward.

Food and Drink Scene of Pinhais

Must-Try Dishes

Barreado

Barreado is a typical dish from Paraná that consists of slow-cooked beef or pork with cassava flour and various spices.

Pastel

Pastel is a popular Brazilian appetizer that consists of fried pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Cachaça

Cachaça is a traditional Brazilian distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane juice. It is popular in the country and often used to make delicious cocktails.

Cafezinho

Brazil is famous for its coffee production. Cafezinho is a small and strong coffee popular in Paraná. It is a must-try for all coffee lovers.

Where to Stay in Pinhais: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Pinhais

Hotel Pinhais offers comfortable accommodation, including breakfast, parking, Wi-Fi, and is located in downtown Pinhais.

Hostel Vitória

Hostel Vitória is a budget-friendly option with shared and private rooms, including breakfast and free Wi-Fi. Lage Homestay, and Pinhais Guest House both provide well-equipped self-catering accommodation with excellent facilities.

What to Buy in Pinhais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pine nuts and handicrafts made with pine needles are popular specialties in Pinhais. Visitors can purchase these and other local products from the street vendors and souvenir shops located throughout the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Pinhais

During the annual Festa da Montanha, Pinhais celebrates its heritage with music, dancing, and traditional foods. The festival attracts visitors from all over the region.

Best Time to Visit Pinhais

Pinhais enjoys a temperate climate, with hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit is from October to February when the weather is cooler, and precipitation is lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pinhais

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect the local customs and culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Get too close to the wild animals

Safety and Health

Safety in Pinhais

Pinhais is considered to be a safe destination in Brazil. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.

Health in Pinhais

Brazil requires visitors to have certain vaccinations before entering the country. Visitors should ensure they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations and consider vaccination against yellow fever.

Getting to Pinhais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pinhais

The nearest airport to Pinhais is Aeroporto Internacional Afonso Pena is located in Curitiba, has both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Pinhais

Pinhais has two main bus stations: Terminal Rodoviário de Pinhais and Terminal Central de Transporte.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a taxi provides visitors more flexibility when arriving in Pinhais.

How to Get Around in Pinhais

Pinhais has a well-organized public transport system that includes buses and taxis. Visitors might also opt to rent a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Pinhais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Curitiba

Curitiba is the capital city of Paraná and has many cultural attractions, including the Oscar Niemeyer Museum, the Botanical Garden of Curitiba, and much more.

Morretes and Paranaguá

Morretes and Paranaguá are coastal towns known for their historic architecture and delicious seafood.

Conclusion

With its beautiful natural sceneries and fascinating cultural attractions, Pinhais is an ideal destination for anyone planning a trip to Brazil. This comprehensive guide provides all the necessary information and tips to make the most out of your visit to Pinhais.
