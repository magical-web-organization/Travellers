Introduction to North

Located in the southern part of Brazil, North Paraná is a dazzling region that has much to offer visitors. Surrounded by picturesque mountains and lush forests, this region is home to several national parks, charming towns, and cities, rich in history and culture. Famed for its world-renowned artisanal crafts, delicious cuisine, and beautiful natural landscapes, North Paraná is a popular tourist destination. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, North Paraná is a fantastic place to explore.

List of Places to Visit in North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to cultural attractions, North Paraná has no shortage of places to visit. The region is home to several iconic museums and historical sites that showcase the fascinating history and culture of the area. Here are some of the top sites:

  • Museu Histórico de Londrina – A remarkable museum that showcases the history of Londrina, one of the largest cities in the region. Visitors can learn about the history of the city through various artifacts, photographs, and exhibitions.
  • Memorial de Londrina – An architectural masterpiece that honors the memory of the pioneers who founded Londrina. The memorial features a beautiful garden, sculpture collection, and a series of panels that depict the city’s growth.
  • Museu de Arte de Londrina – A beautiful museum located in a historic building that features an impressive collection of artworks from renowned Brazilian artists.
  • Museu Histórico de Maringá – A fascinating museum that showcases the history of Maringá, another major city in North Paraná. The museum features several artifacts, photographs, and exhibitions that depict the region’s rich history.
  • Museu da Lingua Portuguesa – A unique museum that celebrates the Portuguese language and its history. The museum features interactive exhibits, audiovisual displays, and other educational materials.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Paraná is renowned for its breathtaking natural landscapes, from towering mountains to lush forests, rivers, and waterfalls. There are also several national parks and reserves that offer visitors a chance to explore and experience nature up close. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Parque Estadual do Guartelá – A stunning natural reserve that features a unique geological formation called Vila Velha, which was formed millions of years ago. The park also features several hiking trails, waterfalls, and other natural wonders.
  • Parque Nacional do Iguaçu – One of the most famous national parks in Brazil, the Iguaçu National Park is home to the awe-inspiring Iguaçu Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in the world. The park also features several hiking trails, wildlife observation areas, and other natural attractions.
  • Parque Estadual Mata dos Godoy – A beautiful natural reserve that features several hiking trails, waterfalls, and other natural wonders. The park is home to many species of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for nature lovers.
  • Parque Nacional do Superagui – A picturesque national park that boasts an unspoiled stretch of the Atlantic Rainforest. The park features several hiking trails, stunning beaches, and other natural attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

North Paraná is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several scenic trails and routes that offer breathtaking views of the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • PR 538 Trail – A challenging seven-kilometer hike that offers spectacular views of the Paraná River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Caminho do Itupava – A historic trail that dates back to the 18th century. The trail takes visitors through stunning natural landscapes and historical landmarks.
  • Cicloturismo Paineiras – A popular biking route that takes visitors through several towns and villages in North Paraná. The route features several scenic stops, including sugar cane fields, nature reserves, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

Aside from the cultural and natural attractions, there are several fun activities and tours visitors can enjoy in North Paraná. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Rafting on the Paranapanema River – A thrilling adventure that takes visitors on a rafting trip down the Paranapanema River, offering them stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Craft Fair – A popular fair that offers visitors the chance to purchase authentic handmade crafts made by local artisans.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride – A mesmerizing experience that takes visitors on a scenic flight over the beautiful landscapes of North Paraná.
  • Boat Cruise on the Iguaçu River – A scenic cruise that takes visitors on a journey down the Iguaçu River, offering them stunning views of the Iguaçu Falls and the surrounding landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of North

Must-Try Dishes

North Paraná is famous for its delicious cuisine, which is a blend of indigenous and European influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Barreado – A traditional dish made with beef, bacon, onion, garlic, and cassava flour, cooked slowly in a clay pot until it becomes tender and flavorful.
  • Carneiro no Buraco – A unique dish made with lamb cooked in a hole dug in the ground, covered with leaves, and cooked over hot coals.
  • Pão de Queijo – A popular cheese bread made with cassava flour and cheese, often served as a breakfast item or snack.

Local Beverages

North Paraná is also renowned for its local beverages, such as:

  • Cachaça – A distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice that is the primary ingredient in Caipirinha, Brazil’s national cocktail.
  • Cerveja Artesanal – A craft beer that is a popular drink in the region.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in North Paraná, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Londrina Hostel – A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable dormitories and private rooms.
  • Bourbon Londrina Hotel – A four-star hotel that offers spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Golden Blue Hotel Express Maringá – A three-star hotel that offers modern rooms, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Paraná is well-known for its artisanal crafts, which are made by local artisans using traditional techniques. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Pottery – Handmade pottery items such as vases and plates are available in several stores across the region.
  • Embroidered Textiles – Intricately embroidered textiles such as table runners, pillowcases, and towels are available in several markets and stores.
  • Wood Carvings – Beautiful wood carvings made by local artisans can be found in several shops in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of North

North Paraná’s culture is a unique blend of indigenous and European influences. The region is renowned for its vibrant music scene, lively festivals, and colorful dances. The people of North Paraná are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to explore the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North Paraná is between March and May, and September and October, as these months offer mild weather and fewer crowds. The summer months (December to February) are the busiest and the hottest, while the winter months (June to August) can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Portuguese phrases to communicate with the locals.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Travel with a map, guidebook, or a local guide.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling or filtering it first.
  • Wear expensive jewelry or carry large sums of cash in public areas.
  • Hike or camp alone in remote areas, always travel with a group or a guide.
  • Touch wildlife or remove any plants or rocks from natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in North

North Paraná is generally safe for visitors, although it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash or wear flashy jewelry in public areas.
  • Keep your belongings close and secure.
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas or deserted streets at night.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and travel with a group whenever possible.

Health in North

It’s essential to take necessary health precautions when traveling to North Paraná. Here are some tips:

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses when spending time outdoors.
  • Bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus.
  • Make sure you are up to date on routine vaccinations.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

  • Londrina Airport (LDB) – The main airport serving the region. It offers domestic flights from major cities in Brazil.
  • Maringá Airport (MGF) – A small airport offering domestic flights from major cities in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near North

  • Rodoviária de Londrina – The main bus station in Londrina, with frequent bus services to several cities in North Paraná and other regions in Brazil.
  • Terminal Rodoviário de Maringá – The main bus station in Maringá, with frequent bus services to several cities in North Paraná and other regions in Brazil.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies in North Paraná that offer convenient transportation for visitors who prefer to drive themselves around the region. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available in the major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in North

The best way to get around North Paraná is by car or organized tours. Several car rental companies operate in the region, and most hotels and tour operators offer transportation services to nearby attractions. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the major cities.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Paraná is a beautiful region with plenty of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some suggestions:

  • São Paulo – A bustling metropolis that is the largest city in Brazil and a cultural hub of the country.
  • Ilha do Mel – A picturesque island off the coast of Paraná that features stunning beaches, hiking trails and beautiful landscapes.
  • Foz do Iguaçu – A popular tourist destination that features the awe-inspiring and world-famous Iguaçu Falls.

Conclusion

North Paraná is a wonderful destination in Brazil that offers a wide range of cultural, natural, and adventurous attractions for visitors. With its friendly people, unique cuisine, and beautiful landscapes, North Paraná is a must-visit destination for anyone wanting to explore the best of what Brazil has to offer.
