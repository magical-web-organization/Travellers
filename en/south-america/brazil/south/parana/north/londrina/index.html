Londrina is a bustling city located in the state of Paraná, Brazil. With beautiful natural and cultural attractions, it’s no wonder that this city is quickly becoming a must-see destination for travelers. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a look at everything you need to know to plan your trip to Londrina, including where to stay, what to see, and what to do while you’re there.

Introduction to Londrina

Londrina is a city rich with history, nature, and culture. Founded in 1931, the area is known for its coffee plantations and its impressive modernist architecture. Today, the city is home to over half a million people, making it one of the largest cities in the state of Paraná.

List of Places to Visit in Londrina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Londrina has plenty of opportunities for visitors to explore its rich cultural heritage. Some of the best museums and historical sites in the city include:

  • Museu Histórico de Londrina Padre Carlos Weiss: This historic museum features exhibits on the history and culture of Londrina and the surrounding area.
  • Memorial da Cultura e Cidadania: This cultural center offers visitors a glimpse into the culture of Paraná, featuring art exhibits, concerts, and more.
  • Catedral Metropolitana de Londrina: This beautiful cathedral is a must-see for anyone interested in religious architecture.
  • Cemitério São Pedro: This historic cemetery is the final resting place of many of the city’s early settlers and is a fascinating place to explore.

Natural Attractions of Londrina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Londrina is also home to many breathtaking natural wonders, including:

  • Parque Estadual Mata dos Godoy: This beautiful park is the perfect place to explore the native flora and fauna of Paraná.
  • Represa do Lago Igapó: This man-made lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Pico do Jacutinga: This peak offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Londrina

Londrina has many hiking and biking trails that provide great opportunities to explore the city’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Parque Ecológico Municipal: This park offers several trails through its lush forests, providing a beautiful spot for outdoor recreation.
  • Lago Norte Trail: A scenic path around the Lago Norte lake, this trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Londrina: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Londrina also has many activities and tours available. Some of the best things to do in the city include:

  • City Tour: A great way to learn about the history and culture of Londrina, a city tour is a must for first-time visitors.
  • Coffee Tours: Visit local coffee plantations and learn about the history of coffee production in Paraná.
  • Shopping: Londrina has many shopping centers and markets, offering visitors the chance to purchase souvenirs and local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Londrina

If you’re planning a trip to Londrina, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Parque Estadual Mata dos Godoy and take a city tour in the afternoon.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Catedral Metropolitana de Londrina and the Cemitério São Pedro.

Day 3: Take a coffee tour and visit local coffee plantations.

Day 4: Visit the Memorial da Cultura e Cidadania and enjoy a concert or art exhibit in the evening.

Day 5: Explore the scenic Pico do Jacutinga and enjoy some mountain biking or hiking.

Day 6: Visit the Represa do Lago Igapó for swimming, fishing, and boating.

Day 7: Visit local shopping centers and markets for souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Londrina

No trip to Londrina is complete without trying some of the local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijão tropeiro: A hearty bean dish made with sausage and various spices, this is a staple in Paraná cuisine.
  • Churrasco: A traditional Brazilian barbecue, churrasco is a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Arroz carreteiro: A delicious rice dish made with beef jerky and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Café: As a city with a long history of coffee production, Londrina offers some of the best coffee in Brazil.
  • Cachaça: A popular Brazilian liquor made from sugarcane, cachaça is the perfect drink to enjoy on a night out in Londrina.

Where to Stay in Londrina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Londrina has a variety of lodging options to fit any budget. Here are some of the best options:

  • Bristol Londrina Residence Hotel: A luxury hotel in the center of the city, this hotel offers guests a pool, sauna, and fitness center.
  • Pousada Vale das Águas: A charming guesthouse outside the city, this lodging option features a natural swimming pool and trails for hiking.
  • Hostel Lira: A great budget option for travelers, this hostel offers clean and comfortable accommodations in a central location.

What to Buy in Londrina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Londrina has plenty of options. Some great choices include:

  • Coffee: With a long history in coffee production, Londrina is the perfect place to purchase high-quality coffee beans.
  • Artisanal Crafts: Local artisans in Londrina offer a variety of handmade crafts, including blankets, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Londrina

Londrina has a fascinating culture that blends indigenous, Afro-Brazilian, and European traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as always greeting people with a handshake and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Londrina

The best time to visit Londrina is between June and September when the weather is dry and sunny. During this time, temperatures can range from 60-80 degrees Fahrenheit, making it the perfect weather for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Londrina

Do:

  • Try the local coffee and cuisine.
  • Be respectful of local customs when visiting religious sites.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the hotter months.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.
  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Londrina

Overall, Londrina is a safe city for travelers. However, visitors should take the same precautions they would in any large city, such as avoiding dark alleyways at night and keeping an eye on their possessions in crowded areas.

Health in Londrina

Visitors should take care to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors in Londrina. It’s also a good idea to bring bug spray, as the city is home to some mosquitoes that can carry diseases like dengue fever.

Getting to Londrina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Londrina

The closest airport to Londrina is the Londrina Airport (LDB), which offers flights to and from major Brazilian cities.

Bus Stations Near Londrina

Londrina has several bus stations that offer service to nearby cities and destinations. The most popular bus station in the city is the Terminal Rodoviário de Londrina.

Other Options

In addition to air and bus travel, visitors to Londrina can also travel by car or bike. The city has several bike rental shops, making it easy to explore on two wheels.

How to Get Around in Londrina

Londrina has a variety of public transportation options, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent a car or bike to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Londrina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels after visiting Londrina, there are several nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Some great options include:

  • Iguazu Falls: Located a few hours from Londrina, these breathtaking waterfalls are a must-see for nature lovers.
  • Curitiba: The capital of Paraná, Curitiba is home to world-class museums, parks, and cultural attractions.
  • Ilha do Mel: This beautiful island off the coast of Paraná offers visitors a chance to relax on pristine beaches and explore untouched nature.

Conclusion

Londrina is a city with something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene, this city is a must-see destination for any traveler to Brazil. Using this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to Londrina, ensuring that you don’t miss a thing while you explore this exciting Brazilian city.
