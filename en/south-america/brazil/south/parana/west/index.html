Introduction to West, Paraná

Located in South Brazil, West is a destination that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. The region is home to some of the most stunning landscapes in Brazil, with a picturesque countryside and enchanting waterfalls. West is also well-known for its vibrant cultural heritage, rooted in the history of the state of Paraná. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see in West, including its historical landmarks, natural wonders, and cultural hotspots.

List of Places to Visit in West

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The West Railway Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the transportation history of West. It features historical locomotives, railcars, and other railway-related equipment that played a crucial role in the development of Paraná.

  • Church of Saint Anthony: Considered one of the most beautiful churches in Paraná, the Church of Saint Anthony stands out for its stunning architecture, beautiful stained glasses, and intricate artwork.

  • Juscelino Kubitschek Memorial: This memorial is dedicated to the former Brazilian president and it showcases his life and legacy through photographs, documents, and personal items.

Natural Attractions of West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canyon Guartelá: Located in the municipality of Tibagi, Canyon Guartelá is a breathtaking attraction that offers awe-inspiring views of the Paraná River and the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can hike, bike, and even go rafting on the river.

  • Véu de Noiva Waterfall: This stunning waterfall, located in the Vila Rica do Espírito Santo municipality, is a must-visit attraction in West. The waterfall drops nearly 80 meters into a pristine pool, creating a mesmerizing sight.

  • Simon’s Valley: This valley is a great spot for nature lovers. Situated in the municipality of Campo Largo, it features beautiful hiking trails, scenic views, and a large diversity of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West

  • Rota do Corvo: This trail is a great option for hiking or biking enthusiasts as it offers beautiful views of Paraná countryside and historical landmarks.

  • Parque Barigui Trail: This trail is located in Curitiba’s most popular park. It is a well-maintained and flat cycling and jogging path that runs along a stunning lake.

Best Things to Do in West: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Parque das Aves: Parque das Aves is a bird park located in Foz do Iguaçu, approximately 300km from West. This park features a wide variety of birds, including exotic species of macaws, toucans and parrots in a beautiful tropical forest.

  • Try Adventure Sports in Tibagi: Adventure Sports enthusiasts should definitely try out Tibagi, where you can go rafting, rock climbing, trekking, and rappelling.

Week-Long Itinerary for West

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit the Guartelá Canyon and enjoy a picnic by the river bank.
Day 2 Head to Tibagi and explore its waterfalls. Try out an adventure sport, like rafting or trekking.
Day 3 Visit Curitiba and explore its attractions, like the historic railway and the Botanical Garden.
Day 4 Visit Foz do Iguaçu and explore the National Park, including the iconic Iguazu Falls.
Day 5 Tour Lapa do Rato, an archaeology site located on the city of Castelo Branco, or Bela Vista do Paraíso, and visit a dairy farm.
Day 6 Go shopping and try local cuisine in the city of Cascavel.
Day 7 Relax at the Praia de Leste Beach, located in Pontal do Parana, and had an unforgettable time in the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of West

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado: A traditional dish of Paraná, Barreado is a slow-cooked beef stew, ideally served with rice and minced cassava.

  • Pão com bolinho: A famous snack in the city of Curitiba, it consists of bread filled with ground beef.

Local Beverages

  • Quentão: A popular drink during the winter months, quentão is a hot drink made with cachaça and spices, such as cinnamon and clove.

  • Mate: This traditional drink of South Brazil is made by brewing yerba mate with hot water and is usually served cold.

Where to Stay in West: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of options for where to stay when visiting West including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Hotel Lancaster: A historic hotel located in downtown Curitiba, the Lancaster offers comfortable rooms, helpful staff and a charming atmosphere.

  • Fazenda Theodoro: Located in Campo Largo, Fazenda Theodoro is a beautiful, working farm that allows visitors to experience days in the country.

What to Buy in West: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: If you are looking for unique and locally made souvenirs, then handicrafts are a great option. From embroidered items to woodcarvings, there are numerous stores sell locally made items.

  • Wine: West is home to several vineyards, and as such, wine is a great option for those who want to take a bit of the region home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of West

West culture is heavily influenced by the region’s German and Italian heritage, with European influences evident throughout the area. The people are known for being welcoming and hospitable and it’s considered good manners to bring a small gift if you are invited to someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit West

The best time to visit West is during the Southern Hemisphere winter (June-August) when temperatures are cooler and rain less frequent. October to April is a great time for those who want to hike and visit waterfalls but expect more frequent rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of West

Do:

  • Bring warm clothing if you are visiting in the winter months.
  • Practice proper etiquette when visiting local religious or cultural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Littering is not well-tolerated in Brazil. Be sure to dispose of your trash properly, or carry it with you if there is no nearby trash bin.
  • Avoid political discussions to avoid making anyone uncomfortable.

Safety and Health

Safety in West

West is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings, especially after dark or in more rural areas. It’s also recommended to avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuable items.

Health in West

Travelers should consult a doctor before their trip and ensure that vaccinations are up-to-date. West does not have any major health concerns.

Getting to West: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West

  • Aeroporto Internacional Afonso Pena, located approximately 100 km southeast of Curitiba can be considered the formal gateway to the region.

Bus Stations Near West

  • Terminal Rodoviário de Curitiba, located north of Curitiba, is one of the largest transportation hubs in South Brazil. Additionally, there are several bus stations that connect West to cities like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available to those who want to explore the region on their own terms.

How to Get Around in West

Public transportation in West consists of buses, and there are also several taxi services available in larger urban centers. Renting a car is also a popular option for those who want to explore the region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from West: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Foz do Iguaçu: Located approximately 300 km from West, Foz do Iguaçu is home to one of the most stunning natural wonders in Brazil, the Iguazu Falls.

  • Curitiba: This city is considered one of the most well-planned and organized cities in Brazil. It’s home to several cultural attractions, including numerous art museums, and botanical gardens.

Conclusion

West is a destination that offers a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural landscapes. Whether you are interested in the region’s history and cultural landmarks or eager to explore its stunning vistas and natural wonders, West is a must-visit destination in South Brazil. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you are sure to have an unforgettable trip to this enchanting region.
