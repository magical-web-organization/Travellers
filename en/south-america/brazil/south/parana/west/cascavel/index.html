Introduction to Cascavel

Located in Paraná, Brazil, Cascavel is a vibrant city known for its cultural landmarks, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. Home to over 300,000 residents, Cascavel offers visitors a unique blend of history, adventure, and relaxation. Whether you are a first-time visitor or returning traveller, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your time in Cascavel.

List of Places to Visit in Cascavel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in learning about Cascavel’s rich history and culture, a visit to the city’s museums and historical landmarks is a must-do experience. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Museu Histórico de Cascavel: This museum showcases the city’s history through artefacts, photographs, and exhibits.

  • Catedral Nossa Senhora Aparecida: A beautiful cathedral, the Catedral Nossa Senhora Aparecida is an impressive architectural feat with intricate stained glass windows.

  • Museu de Arte de Cascavel: This art museum houses an impressive collection of Brazilian artwork and is a hub for local art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Cascavel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are a nature lover, Cascavel has no shortage of natural attractions, parks, and reserves. Here are some of the must-visit outdoor destinations in Cascavel:

  • Parque Municipal Danilo Antonio Berta: This park is famous for its crystal-clear lake, a perfect spot for a picnic or boating.

  • Parque Ambiental Leocádio Francisco de Oliveira: Explore the native flora, fauna, and the scenic countryside in this nature park.

  • Serra do Mico Ecological Park: This park offers stunning views of the Serra do Mico Mountain range. You can enjoy hiking trails, bicycle paths and even zip-line adventures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cascavel

If you are an adventure enthusiast, Cascavel has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. Put on your hiking boots and prepare to go through the natural and scenic beauty of Cascavel. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Trilha do Morro do Cal Serrao: A challenging hike, Trilha do Morro do Cal Serrão takes you through lush vegetation, and at the end, you will experience breathtaking views of the entire region.

  • Ciclovia Marginal do Rio Cascavel: This bike path runs alongside the river and is surrounded by lush trees and green landscapes.

  • Trilha do Cânion: A hike in the canyon is a must-do for outdoor enthusiasts. This trail takes you through waterfalls, rocky gorges and provides awe-inspiring views of the natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Cascavel: Activities and Tours

Whether you prefer exploring the city’s history or having an adrenaline-rushing adventure, Cascavel has several activities and tours options to keep you engaged. Here are some of the top things to do in Cascavel:

  • Paragliding Adventure: Soar above the city and enjoy panoramic views of the green surroundings in a tandem paragliding adventure.

  • Museu de Arte de Cascavel Tour: As art lovers, Museu de Arte de Cascavel Museum is the perfect option to know more about Brazilian art, culture, and history.

  • Wine Country Tour: Discover the region’s wineries, learn about wine-making and taste some of the best local wines of Cascavel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cascavel

If you have a week to explore Cascavel, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 - Admire the beauty of the Cathedral Nossa Senhora Aparecida and visit the Museu Histórico de Cascavel Day 2 - Explore the trails at Parque Municipal Danilo Antonio Berta and Parque Ambiental Leocádio Francisco de Oliveira. Day 3 - Discover nature’s beauty in Serra do Mico Ecological Park and take part in the zip-line adventure. Day 4 - Cycle the Ciclovia Marginal do Rio Cascavel and discover the green landscapes of the city. Day 5 - Take a tour of the many vineyards and enjoy the wine country experience. Day 6 - Experience the thrill of paragliding adventure over the city. Day 7 - Spend the day shopping in the open market, taste local delicacies and buy souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Cascavel

No trip to Cascavel is complete without sampling the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the top culinary experiences to enjoy in Cascavel:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Galinhada: A Brazilian stew made with rice, chicken, and spices.

  • Dumplings: These fried or baked dumplings are filled with beef, chicken, or cheese.

  • Barreado: A stew with beef, cassava flour and cooked in a clay pot, it’s a popular dish in Cascavel.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A Brazilian rum widely used in cocktails, or used to prepare typical Brazilian drinks such as “Caipirinha.”

  • Vinho: Cascavel is renowned for producing high-quality wines. Don’t miss the opportunity to taste them.

Where to Stay in Cascavel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cascavel offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Wish Hotel Cascavel: A modern and comfortable 5-star hotel with an outdoor pool located close to the city’s main attractions.

  • Ibis Cascavel: A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and a strategic location.

  • Hostel Way2go: A cozy and friendly hostel perfect for budget travellers.

What to Buy in Cascavel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you are looking for a small souvenir or a unique and local product for your home, Cascavel has plenty of options. Some popular places to shop in Cascavel are:

  • Open Market (Mercado Municipal): Shop for fresh fruits, vegetables, local delicacies and artisanal crafts.

  • SIECI - Fair of Handicrafts and Culture of the Interior of Paraná: This annual event offers products and crafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Cascavel

Cascavel is proud of its rich history and cultural tradition, which can be seen in its festivals, art, music, and dance. One of the most significant cultural events is the Expovel-Expo do Oeste Paranaense, an agricultural and commercial fair that takes place annually, showcasing local products and talents.

Best Time to Visit Cascavel

The best time to visit Cascavel is from April to September when it is less hot. However, keep in mind it can get chilly during this time. Between December to March, it’s also a peak season as it’s summer vacation, and the weather is warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cascavel

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

  • Learn some Portuguese phrases to communicate with the locals.

  • Explore nature and outdoor activities in Cascavel.

Don’t:

  • Don’t carry too much cash or valuables when exploring the city.

  • Don’t ignore the safety regulations when participating in adventure activities.

  • Don’t litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cascavel

Cascavel is considered a safe city for tourists; however, it’s always essential to take basic precautions as you explore. Be mindful of your surroundings, keep your valuables close, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Cascavel

It’s always recommended to carry travel insurance when travelling. The public healthcare system is great in Brazil; however, visitors may be charged depending on the service needed. Malaria and yellow fever are also prevalent in some parts of the country, so check the latest updates on vaccines and always wear insect repellent.

Getting to Cascavel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cascavel

Cascavel is served by the Adalberto Mendes Da Silva Airport (CAC), which has connections to some major airports in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near Cascavel

The city has a modern and reliable bus terminal with connections to various cities in Paraná and other states of Brazil.

Other Options

If you are arriving from other parts of Paraná, you can also take a taxi or hire a car.

How to Get Around in Cascavel

Getting around Cascavel is easy as it’s a small city. You can use public transport, including buses and taxis, or ride-hail services. If you prefer to explore the natural wonder that Cascavel, hiking and biking trails are available.

Where to Go Next from Cascavel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to experience:

  • Foz do Iguaçu: Visit the spectacular Iguazu Falls on the Brazil-Argentina border.

  • Curitiba: A cultural and artistic hub, Curitiba is known for its vibrant art scene, museums, and festivals.

  • Guarapuava: A charming and peaceful town in the mountains, perfect for a relaxing and nature-filled excursion.

Conclusion

Cascavel is a fascinating city, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and adventure. From exploring the museums to hiking and cycling around beautiful vistas, there’s no shortage of activities to enjoy. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has helped you plan a memorable trip to Cascavel.
