Introduction to Matinhos

Matinhos is a coastal city located in the state of Paraná, in the south of Brazil. The city is known for its stunning sandy beaches, diverse wildlife and biodiversity, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Matinhos can enjoy a wide range of activities, from lounging on the beach to hiking through lush forests, exploring historic sites and museums, and indulging in delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Matinhos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Matinhos can delve into the city’s rich history and culture by visiting some of its many museums and historical sites. The most notable attractions in this category include:

  • Museu Arqueológico de São Miguel: This museum houses a diverse collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the area’s indigenous history and culture. Visitors can learn about the traditions and customs of the Guarani people, who have inhabited the region for over 2,000 years.

  • Forte de Nossa Senhora dos Prazeres: This 18th-century colonial fort was built by the Portuguese to protect the region from pirates and other threats. Today, visitors can explore the fort’s historic walls and learn about its fascinating history.

  • Santuário Nossa Senhora do Rocio: This iconic religious sanctuary is dedicated to Our Lady of Rocio, the patron saint of Paraná. The sanctuary holds a special place in the hearts of many locals and visitors alike and hosts many religious festivals and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Matinhos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Matinhos is home to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Brazil. Whether you’re looking to hike through lush forests, take a dip in pristine waters, or simply soak up the stunning scenery, there’s plenty to see and do in Matinhos. Some of the most popular natural attractions include:

  • Parque Nacional do Superagui: This national park boasts miles of unspoiled beaches, towering sand dunes, and lush Atlantic Forest. Visitors can explore the park’s many trails, take a dip in the crystal-clear waters, or simply relax on one of the secluded beaches.

  • Ilha do Mel: This picturesque island is home to some of the most beautiful beaches and landscapes in Brazil. Visitors can hike through lush forests, explore historic lighthouses, and take a swim in calm waters.

  • Gruta do Bacaetava: This impressive cave system features stunning rock formations and underground lakes. Visitors can explore the cave’s many chambers and learn about the local geology and ecology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matinhos

Matinhos is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Brazil. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a casual cyclist, there’s something for everyone in Matinhos. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Parque Nacional do Superagui Trail: This trail takes hikers through the heart of the national park, offering stunning views of the surrounding forest and coastline.

  • Ilha do Mel Trail: This scenic trail takes hikers through the island’s lush forests and along its pristine beaches. Visitors can explore historic lighthouses, take a swim in crystal-clear waters, and enjoy stunning views of the Atlantic coastline.

  • Rio São João Trail: This trail follows the scenic Rio São João, offering stunning views of the surrounding forest and wildlife. Visitors can hike, bike, or even kayak along the river, taking in the sights and sounds of this beautiful natural landscape.

Best Things to Do in Matinhos: Activities and Tours

Matinhos offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours to choose from, including:

  • Beach Hopping: Visit the city’s many stunning beaches, from Prainha Beach to Caiobá Beach, and enjoy the sun and sand.

  • Water Sports: Take a surf lesson or try your hand at stand-up paddleboarding, kayaking, or windsurfing.

  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through lush forests and along pristine beaches.

  • Boat Tour: Take a boat tour of the surrounding coast and islands, and enjoy stunning views of Brazil’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matinhos

For visitors looking to make the most of their time in Matinhos, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the historic sites and museums of Matinhos, including the Forte de Nossa Senhora dos Prazeres and the Museu Arqueológico de São Miguel.

Day 2: Venture out to the Parque Nacional do Superagui and hike the park’s many trails.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Ilha do Mel and explore the island’s many beaches and historic landmarks.

Day 4: Relax on one of Matinhos’ many beautiful beaches, such as Praia do Cristo or Prainha Beach.

Day 5: Take a scenic boat tour of the surrounding coast and islands.

Day 6: Enjoy water sports such as surfing, windsurfing, or kayaking.

Day 7: Indulge in some of Matinhos’ delicious local cuisine, including fresh seafood and traditional Guarani dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Matinhos

While Matinhos may be best known for its stunning natural beauty, the city also boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with many local dishes and traditional Guarani specialties to try. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado: A traditional beef stew simmered for over 24 hours and served with rice, cassava flour, and banana.

  • Sororoca: A freshwater fish dish served with tomato sauce and onions.

  • Churrasco: Brazilian-style barbecue, often served with rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A distilled spirit made from sugar cane, often served in caipirinhas or other cocktails.

  • Guaraná: A popular non-alcoholic soft drink made from the Guarana fruit.

  • Mate: A traditional South American tea, often served with herbs and sweeteners.

Where to Stay in Matinhos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matinhos offers visitors a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to cozy hostels and guesthouses. Some of the most popular choices include:

  • Pousada Solar da Praia: a cozy and welcoming guesthouse located just minutes from Matinhos’ stunning beaches.

  • Hotel VillaReal: A luxurious beachfront hotel with stunning views of the surrounding coastline.

  • Pousada Dona Laura: A comfortable and affordable guesthouse with easy access to Matinhos’ many attractions.

What to Buy in Matinhos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to bring home a piece of Matinhos, there are many souvenirs and local products to choose from, including:

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Many local artisans sell beautiful handcrafted jewelry, often incorporating local materials.

  • Cachaça: Bring home a bottle of this popular Brazilian spirit, often flavored with local fruits.

  • Handmade Pottery: Visit local markets to find beautiful handmade pottery and other ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Matinhos

Like much of Brazil, Matinhos has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, with a blend of indigenous, African, and European traditions. Visitors to Matinhos may want to keep the following customs and traditions in mind:

  • Respect for Elders: In Guarani culture, respect for elders is highly valued, and visitors should strive to show respect for older members of the community.

  • Religious Festivals: Matinhos is home to many religious festivals throughout the year, and visitors may want to take part in these celebrations to experience the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Matinhos

The best time to visit Matinhos is from December to March, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their most beautiful. Visitors may also want to consider visiting during the city’s many festivals and events, such as Carnival in February or the Festa da Tainha (Mullet Festival) in July.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matinhos

To make the most of your visit to Matinhos, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Matinhos boasts a diverse and delicious food scene, so be sure to sample some of the local specialties.

  • Explore the Surrounding Landscape: Matinhos is home to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Brazil, so don’t miss out on the chance to explore the area’s many beaches, forests, and islands.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Local Customs: Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, particularly during religious festivals and other cultural events.

  • Litter: Like many coastal cities, Matinhos’ beaches can suffer from litter and pollution, so visitors should do their part to keep the area clean and beautiful.

Safety and Health

Matinhos is a relatively safe city with low rates of violent crime. However, visitors should take some basic precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure. Visitors should also consult with a healthcare professional before travelling to ensure they are up to date on any necessary vaccinations or precautions.

Safety in Matinhos

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: To reduce the risk of theft or other crimes, visitors should avoid walking alone on the streets of Matinhos after dark.

  • Keep Valuables Secure: Visitors should take care to keep their wallets, phones, and other valuables secure when out in public.

Health in Matinhos

  • Consult with a Healthcare Professional: Visitors should consult with a healthcare professional before travelling to Matinhos to ensure they are up to date on necessary vaccinations or precautions.

  • Stay Hydrated: The hot and humid climate of Matinhos can be tough on visitors, so be sure to stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

Getting to Matinhos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matinhos is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Visitors can fly into the nearest airport, which is located in Curitiba, and then take a bus or taxi to Matinhos. There are also many bus routes that connect Matinhos to nearby cities and towns.

Airports Near Matinhos

  • Afonso Pena International Airport: Located in Curitiba, approximately 90 minutes from Matinhos.

Bus Stations Near Matinhos

  • Matinhos Bus Station: Located in the city center, this bus station offers connections to many nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors may also want to consider renting a car to explore Matinhos and the surrounding area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Matinhos

Matinhos is a relatively small city, so getting around is easy and convenient. Visitors can get from place to place on foot, by bicycle, or by taking a taxi or car service.

Where to Go Next from Matinhos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors looking to explore more of the region, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to choose from, including:

  • Curitiba: The capital city of Paraná, Curitiba is known for its stunning parks and museums, as well as its vibrant cultural scene and nightlife.

  • Ilha do Superagui: This nearby island is part of the Parque Nacional do Superagui and is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including many rare and endangered species.

  • Serra do Mar: This beautiful mountain range is located just a short distance from Matinhos and offers stunning views, hiking trails, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Matinhos offers visitors a rich and diverse experience, with stunning natural landscapes, historic sites and museums, delicious cuisine, and a vibrant cultural scene. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore new adventures, Matinhos has something for everyone, making it an ideal destination for your next trip to Brazil.
