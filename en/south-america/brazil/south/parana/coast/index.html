Introduction to Coast

Located in Paraná, in the southern region of Brazil, Coast is a charming coastal town with a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking nature, and an exciting food scene. This hidden gem of South America offers visitors a unique experience of exploring Brazil’s natural wonders while enjoying the local hospitality and warm weather.

List of Places to Visit in Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Coast has a rich cultural heritage, and you can visit iconic landmarks and museums in the town. The museums and historical places that you should visit include:

  • Museum of Antonina: The museum has a fantastic collection of art, artifacts, and photographs showcasing the town’s history and culture. The museum was established in 1981 and is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and art.

  • Municipal Market: The market has a vast array of fresh produce, artisanal crafts, and souvenirs. This market has been an iconic place for locals and visitors alike since 1922.

  • Igreja Matriz: The church is a historical landmark that dates back to the sixteenth century. It has beautiful architecture, and the inside is adorned with intricate paintings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Coast is best known for its stunning natural beauty, and it is home to many natural attractions that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. The natural attractions that you must visit include:

  • Serra do Mar: The Serra do Mar is a part of the Atlantic Forest, and it is home to many hiking trails with stunning views of the coastline and the lush greenery.

  • Pico do Paraná: Pico do Paraná is the highest mountain in Southern Brazil and attracts many avid hikers and climbers looking for a challenging adventure.

  • São João da Graciosa Road: This road offers stunning views of the coastline, with hairpin turns, and a glimpse of the indigenous flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coast

Coast has some of the best hiking and biking routes in South America. From challenging mountain trails to easy coastal routes, there’s something for everyone. The best hiking/biking routes in Coast include:

  • Morro do Rocha: This trail is a moderate hike that offers stunning views of the coastline and the Atlantic Forest. The hike usually takes around three hours, and it is best to hire a guide who can provide insights into the local flora and fauna.

  • Serra do Marechal: This trail is considered challenging, and it usually takes around five hours to complete. However, it offers breathtaking views of the Serra do Mar, and on clear days, you can see as far as Sao Paulo.

Best Things to Do in Coast: Activities and Tours

Coast has a plethora of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. The best things to do in Coast include:

  • Boat Tours: Boat tours are an excellent way to explore the coastline and nearby islands. You can go on dolphin-watching tours, eco-tours, and sunset tours that showcase the stunning beauty of the town.

  • Beach Hopping: Coast’s beaches are well-known for their clear waters and white sand. You can spend a day beach hopping at Praia de Leste, Praia do Leste, and Praia de Graciosa, which are the most popular beaches in the town.

  • Fishing Tours: The fishing tours offer an authentic Brazilian experience, and you can catch various fish species like snapper, grouper, and salmon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coast

If you are planning to visit Coast for a week, we have put together an itinerary that highlights the best of this charming town in Brazil:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel. Spend the day exploring the historic center of the town, and visit the Municipal Market and Museum of Antonina.

Day 2: Start your day early and hike up Morro do Rocha. Spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach.

Day 3: Take a boat tour and explore the nearby islands and coastline.

Day 4: Spend the day visiting the historical landmarks and churches in the town.

Day 5: Visit the Serra do Mar and go on a challenging hike on the Serra do Marechal trail.

Day 6: Spend the day fishing and soaking in the local culture and cuisine.

Day 7: Departure from Coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Coast

Must-Try Dishes

The food scene in Coast is dominated by fresh seafood, local fruits, and vegetables. Some of the must-try dishes in Coast include:

  • Barreado: Barreado is a traditional dish that originated in the nearby town of Morretes. It consists of slow-cooked beef in a clay pot and served with rice and manioc flour.

  • Shrimp Soup: The shrimp soup is a popular dish in Coast, and it is made with fresh shrimp, vegetables, and spices.

  • Cachaça: Cachaça is a Brazilian distilled spirit made from sugar cane, and it is a popular local drink.

Local Beverages

The local beverage scene in Coast is vibrant, and you can try many drinks made from exotic fruits and herbs. Some of the local beverages that you can try include:

  • Cajuína: Cajuína is a non-alcoholic drink made from cashew juice, and it is a popular drink in the town.

  • Mate: Mate is a South American tea that is similar to green tea, and it is a popular beverage in Brazil.

Where to Stay in Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Coast has a range of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. The best places to stay in Coast include:

  • Pousada do Serra: Pousada do Serra is a boutique hotel located in the heart of the town. The hotel has comfortable rooms and a fantastic restaurant that serves traditional Brazilian cuisine.

  • Ilha do Sol: Ilha do Sol is a tropical paradise that offers beachfront villas with stunning views of the coastline and the Atlantic Ocean.

What to Buy in Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Coast has a vibrant artisanal scene, and you can find many unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Some of the best souvenirs and local products that you can buy in Coast include:

  • Artisanal Crafts: The market in the town has many artisanal crafts like pottery, woodwork, and textiles.

  • Cachaça: Cachaça is a popular local drink in the town, and you can buy bottles of the distilled spirit to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Coast

Coast has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their traditions and customs. The town celebrates many festivals and events throughout the year, and you can experience the local culture and customs firsthand.

Best Time to Visit Coast

The best time to visit Coast is during the summer months between December and February when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also beautiful during the off-season when the crowds are thin.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coast

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural attractions and landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities like drug use.
  • Disrespect the locals or the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coast

Coast is a safe town, but you should always take precautions to ensure your safety. Keep your valuables safe, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Coast

The town has a good healthcare system, but you should always carry your medical insurance and any necessary medication.

Getting to Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Coast is well-connected to other parts of Brazil, and there are many transportation options available. The best ways to get to Coast include:

Airports Near Coast

  • Afonso Pena International Airport: The airport is located in Curitiba, which is around 70 kilometers from Coast.

Bus Stations Near Coast

  • Antonina Bus Station: The bus station is located in the town and has regular buses that connect to other parts of Brazil.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: You can hire a private transfer to take you from Curitiba to the town.

How to Get Around in Coast

Coast is a small town, and most places are within walking distance. You can also rent bicycles or motorbikes to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coast is surrounded by many beautiful destinations that you can explore. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Coast include:

  • Ilha do Mel: Ilha do Mel is a beautiful island located a few kilometers from the town, and it is accessible by boat.

  • Morretes: Morretes is a nearby town that is famous for its Barreado dish, and it has a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Coast is a hidden gem in Brazil, offering visitors a unique experience of exploring the country’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to visit Coast and explore everything this beautiful town has to offer.
