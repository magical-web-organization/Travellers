Antonina is a small coastal town located in the state of Paraná in southern Brazil. It is a hidden gem in South America that boasts natural beauty, rich history, and cultural diversity. Whether you are an adventure seeker, history buff, or foodie, Antonina offers the perfect mix of activities and attractions to explore. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best things to do, places to visit, outdoor adventures, food scene, and cultural attractions of Antonina, Brazil.

Introduction to Antonina

Antonina is a picturesque town that dates back to the 17th century. It was originally founded as a Portuguese settlement and later became an important port for exporting Brazilian coffee to Europe. Today, Antonina is a popular tourist destination known for its charming colonial architecture, vibrant culture, and natural wonders. The town is situated at the foot of the Serra do Mar mountain range and is surrounded by lush forests, sparkling rivers, and pristine beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Antonina

Antonina has a variety of attractions that cater to diverse interests. Here is a list of places you shouldn’t miss during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Antonina Municipal Museum: This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s rich history, including its Portuguese colonial past and coffee export industry.
  • Saint Benedict Monastery: This historical landmark was built in the 18th century and features stunning Baroque architecture. The monastery is open for tours and masses.
  • Nossa Senhora do Pilar Church: This church was built in the 18th century and features picturesque blue and white tiles. It is a great example of colonial architecture and is open for visitors.
  • Casa de Cultura de Antonina: This cultural center offers exhibitions, workshops, and cultural events that showcase the town’s artistic expression and ancestral traditions.

Natural Attractions of Antonina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Graciosa Road: This scenic road winds through the Atlantic Rainforest and offers breathtaking views of the Serra do Mar mountain range. It is perfect for a leisurely drive or a bike ride.
  • Marumbi State Park: This park is a nature lover’s paradise and offers hiking trails, camping, and birdwatching opportunities. It is home to some of the most stunning vistas of the region.
  • Morretes Waterfalls: These cascading falls are located in the outskirts of Antonina and are surrounded by beautiful forest scenery. There are plenty of hiking trails and photo opportunities in this area.
  • Parque Nacional do Superagui: This national park is located on the coast of Paraná and boasts diverse ecosystems, including mangroves, sand dunes, and the Atlantic Rainforest. Visitors can take guided boat tours to explore this secluded paradise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Antonina

Antonina is home to many scenic hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the Atlantic Rainforest and the sea. Here are some of the best:

  • Morro da Caixa d’Água Trail: This trail is located near the Antonina dam and offers a steep ascent through the forest. At the top, you’ll be rewarded with stunning views of the bay and the town.
  • Graciosa Road Bike Ride: This bike ride starts in Antonina and follows the scenic Graciosa Road to the town of Morretes. The road is paved and relatively flat, making it accessible even for beginners.
  • Pico Paraná Hike: This challenging hike is not for the faint of heart. It is the highest peak in Southern Brazil and offers stunning views of the sea and the Serra do Mar mountain range. The hike takes about 10 hours to complete round trip.

Best Things to Do in Antonina: Activities and Tours

Antonina offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best:

  • Coffee Tour: Antonina has a rich coffee culture, and visitors can take a tour of local coffee plantations to learn about the process of growing and harvesting coffee beans. Try some of the local brews and learn about the history of Brazilian coffee exports.
  • Kayak Tour: Take a guided kayak tour of the Bay of Antonina to explore the mangroves and spot wildlife such as capuchin monkeys, egrets, and cormorants.
  • Paragliding: Soar above the town and the bay with a tandem paragliding tour. The views are simply stunning and will make for a memorable experience.
  • Historical Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of the town to explore its colonial architecture, historical landmarks, and culturally significant sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Antonina

If you have a week to spend in Antonina, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive and explore the historic town center, visiting landmarks such as Nossa Senhora do Pilar Church and Antonina Municipal Museum.
Day 2 Take a guided coffee plantation tour and sample some of the local brews. In the afternoon, take a hike to Morro da Caixa d’Água for stunning views of the bay.
Day 3 Spend the day exploring Marumbi State Park, hiking to the famous Olimpo Peak and camping overnight.
Day 4 Take a tandem paragliding tour for an unforgettable experience, followed by a kayak tour of the Bay of Antonina to explore the mangroves.
Day 5 Explore the Graciosa Road on a bike ride to Morretes and stop for lunch in one of the local restaurants.
Day 6 Take a boat tour of the secluded Parque Nacional do Superagui and explore its diverse ecosystems.
Day 7 Relax on one of the local beaches or explore the town on foot.

Food and Drink Scene of Antonina

Antonina has a diverse culinary scene that caters to a range of tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado: A traditional meal made from a delicious stew of beef, bacon, onions, and cassava flour, cooked slowly for hours in a clay pot. Served with rice, farofa, and banana.
  • Pastels: These are small pockets of dough filled with various ingredients such as cheese, beef, chicken, or vegetables. They can be found in many street vendors throughout the town.
  • Banana Cake: A dessert made from mashed bananas, flour, sugar, eggs, and cinnamon. It is usually served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A distilled spirit made from sugarcane, similar to rum. It is used in many Brazilian cocktails such as Caipirinha and Batida.
  • Coffee: Brazilian coffee is known for its rich flavor and aroma. Try some of the local blends and learn about the coffee export history of the region.
  • Mate: A traditional tea made from yerba mate leaves, a popular beverage in southern Brazil.

Where to Stay in Antonina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Antonina offers a variety of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some of the best:

  • Pousada Solar da Beira: This colonial-style guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, a beautiful garden, and a swimming pool. It is located in the historic town center.
  • Pousada Vila dos Prazeres: This charming guesthouse is located in a quiet neighborhood and offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast. It is perfect for couples and families.
  • Hotel Camboa: This hotel is located on the shore of the Bay of Antonina and offers stunning views, comfortable rooms, and a swimming pool. It is great for those looking for a beachfront getaway.

What to Buy in Antonina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Antonina offers a variety of local products that make for great souvenirs or gifts. Here are some of the best:

  • Artisanal Crafts: Antonina has a vibrant artisanal scene, and visitors can find beautiful handcrafted items such as ceramics, textiles, and woodcarvings.
  • Cachaça: Bring home a bottle of local cachaça to enjoy or to give as a gift. There are various brands and flavors to choose from, and many are available for tasting.
  • Coffee: Bring home some of the local coffee blends to savor the taste of Brazilian coffee long after your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Antonina

Antonina is a culturally diverse town that enjoys a rich heritage of Portuguese, indigenous, and African roots. Visitors can experience this unique blend of culture through various festivals, culinary traditions, music, and dance. The town has a laid-back pace of life, and locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Antonina

The best time to visit Antonina is during the dry season, which typically runs from May to September. The weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists, making it the perfect time to explore the town and its natural wonders.

Do’s and Don’ts of Antonina

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially in religious sites.
  • Try local food and beverages, and support small businesses and artisans.
  • Explore the natural wonders of Antonina, such as the Marumbi State Park and the mangroves.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the history and cultural significance of the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites or in public spaces.
  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Antonina

Antonina is a safe town, and violent crime rates are low. However, visitors should take precautions against petty crime such as pickpocketing and theft. It is also important to follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking.

Health in Antonina

There are no major health risks in Antonina, and the town has a hospital and medical facilities to provide basic healthcare services. Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue fever, especially during the rainy season.

Getting to Antonina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Antonina is easily accessible by car or bus from Curitiba, the capital of Paraná. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Antonina

  • Afonso Pena International Airport: This is the closest airport to Antonina, located in Curitiba.
  • São Paulo-Guarulhos International Airport: This is a major international airport located in São Paulo, approximately 6 hours away from Antonina.

Bus Stations Near Antonina

  • Curitiba Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Curitiba, and buses depart regularly to Antonina.
  • Morretes Bus Terminal: This is a bus terminal in the nearby town of Morretes, which is about 20 minutes away from Antonina.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Curitiba and drive to Antonina, which takes about 1 hour and 30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Antonina

Antonina is a small town that can be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who want to venture out to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Antonina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Antonina is surrounded by many picturesque towns and natural wonders that are worth exploring. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Morretes: This charming town is located 20 minutes away from Antonina and is famous for its historic train ride through the mountains and its traditional barreado meal.
  • Ilha do Mel: This secluded island is located off the coast of Paraná and offers pristine beaches, hiking trails, and a tranquil atmosphere.
  • Curitiba: The capital of Paraná offers a vibrant cultural scene, delicious cuisine, and stunning public parks and gardens.

Conclusion

Antonina is a small town that offers a wealth of natural, cultural, and culinary experiences for visitors. Whether you want to hike through the Atlantic Rainforest, sample local coffee blends, or relax on the beach, Antonina has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan your next adventure in this hidden gem of South America.
