Introduction to Morretes

Morretes is a charming coastal town in Paraná, Brazil, situated in the lush green mountains of the Serra do Mar. The town is known for its rich cultural history, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Morretes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Center of Morretes: The center of Morretes is a preserved colonial-era town, with charming cobblestone streets and historic buildings.
  • Morretes Railroad Station: This historical station is no longer in operation, but it is now a museum that showcases the town’s transportation history.
  • Church of Nossa Senhora do Porto: This baroque-style church is one of the oldest buildings in Morretes, dating back to the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Morretes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra do Mar State Park: This park is home to lush rainforest, breathtaking waterfalls, and a diversity of wildlife.
  • Estrada da Graciosa: This scenic road winds its way through the Serra do Mar, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Rio Nhundiaquara: This river runs through Morretes, offering opportunities for kayaking and river tubing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morretes

  • Trilha do Pico do Marumbi: This hiking trail is a challenging but rewarding trek that offers stunning views of the Serra do Mar.
  • Parque Estadual Pico do Marumbi: This state park offers a range of hiking trails for all levels, as well as camping facilities.
  • Caminho do Itupava: This historic hiking trail was once used to transport goods between Morretes and Curitiba, and now offers a scenic and challenging trek through the forest.

Best Things to Do in Morretes: Activities and Tours

  • Train Ride from Curitiba to Morretes: This scenic train ride winds its way through the Serra do Mar, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Rafting on the Rio Nhundiaquara: This river adventure takes you through Morretes, offering a unique perspective on the town’s natural beauty.
  • Cachoeira Salto da Fortuna: This breathtaking waterfall is located just outside of Morretes and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Food and Drink Scene of Morretes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado: This dish is a slow-cooked beef stew that is served with cassava flour and banana.
  • Peixe na Telha: This dish is a grilled fish served on a ceramic tile, and it is a staple of the coastal cuisine in Morretes.
  • Banana Flambé: This dessert is a tasty combination of bananas, sugar, cinnamon, and cachaça.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This distilled spirit is made from sugarcane and is a staple of Brazilian culture. In Morretes, it is often used to make cocktails or served as a digestive after a meal.
  • Cerveja Artesanal: This craft beer scene in Morretes is growing, and it offers a refreshing way to cool off after a day of hiking or exploring.

Where to Stay in Morretes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada Casa do Sossego: This charming guesthouse is located in the Historic Center of Morretes and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Pousada Itupava: This cozy guesthouse is located near the Caminho do Itupava hiking trail and offers beautiful views of the surrounding forest.
  • Pousada Bella Morretes: This elegant guesthouse is located near the river and offers a swimming pool and a sauna.

What to Buy in Morretes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cassava Flour: This staple ingredient in Brazilian cuisine is often used as a side dish or in desserts. It is readily available in Morretes and makes a great souvenir.
  • Cachaça: This distilled spirit is a popular souvenir in Morretes and comes in a variety of flavors and styles.
  • Handcrafted Souvenirs: Morretes has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and visitors can find a variety of handmade souvenirs such as ceramics, textiles, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Morretes

The culture of Morretes is heavily influenced by its colonial history, as well as its location on the coast and in the mountains. Visitors can expect to find a mix of Portuguese, African, and indigenous cultures in the town’s food, music, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Morretes

The best time to visit Morretes is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and the trails and outdoor activities are more accessible. However, Morretes can be visited year-round, and each season offers its own unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morretes

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the barreado and peixe na telha.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.
  • Do learn a few words of Portuguese, as it will help you communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural areas or on the streets of Morretes.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellant for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t assume that everyone speaks English, as Portuguese is the official language of Brazil.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morretes

Morretes is a relatively safe town, but visitors should always take precautions to protect their belongings and themselves. It is not recommended to walk alone at night, and it is advisable to keep valuables secure when in crowded areas.

Health in Morretes

Morretes has good medical facilities, but visitors are advised to bring any prescription medications with them. It is also recommended to have up-to-date vaccinations for typhoid and hepatitis A, especially if you plan to eat the local cuisine.

Getting to Morretes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morretes

The nearest major airports to Morretes are Curitiba International Airport (CWB) and São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport (GRU). From either airport, visitors can take a bus or hire a car to travel to Morretes.

Bus Stations Near Morretes

Morretes has a bus station that offers regular services to cities such as Curitiba and Paranaguá.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the scenic train ride from Curitiba to Morretes, which is a popular way to travel to the town.

How to Get Around in Morretes

Morretes is a relatively small town and is easy to navigate on foot. However, for longer distances or to explore the surrounding areas, it is recommended to hire a car or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Morretes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Paranaguá: This coastal town is located just north of Morretes and offers beautiful beaches and a bustling port.
  • Ilha do Mel: This stunning island off the coast of Paraná is known for its pristine beaches and stunning natural beauty.
  • Curitiba: The capital of Paraná is located just a few hours’ drive from Morretes and offers a range of cultural attractions and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Morretes is a hidden gem in Brazil, offering a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its charming colonial center, lush rainforest, and delicious cuisine, it’s no wonder that Morretes is a popular destination for visitors from around the world.
