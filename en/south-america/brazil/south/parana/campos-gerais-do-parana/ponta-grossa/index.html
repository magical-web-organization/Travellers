Introduction to Ponta Grossa

Ponta Grossa is a hidden gem in southern Brazil, boasting rich cultural heritage and awe-inspiring natural beauty. Located in the Campos Gerais region of Paraná, Ponta Grossa is a bustling city, surrounded by picturesque green fields and hills. From its stunning museums and historical places to its beautiful outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone to be found in Ponta Grossa.

List of Places to Visit in Ponta Grossa

Here is a list of must-visit places in Ponta Grossa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Campos Gerais: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the history and culture of the Campos Gerais region, with its collection of artifacts, documents, and interactive exhibits.

  • Catedral de Nossa Senhora da Conceição: Built-in neo-gothic style, this beautiful cathedral is a must-visit spot for anyone interested in history or architecture.

  • Memorial da Imigração Holandesa: Discover the incredible story of Dutch immigrants in Brazil at this well-curated museum, which displays artifacts, documents, and photographs in a beautifully restored farmhouse.

Natural Attractions of Ponta Grossa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Estadual de Vila Velha: The park is home to three geological formations known as “the Golden” that are worth exploring. The breathtaking views, along with the park’s other attractions, such as the Furnas Lake and Lagoa Dourada, make it a must-visit spot.

  • Buraco do Padre: One of the most popular natural spots in the region is the Buraco do Padre, a beautiful waterfall with a natural rock formation that looks like a bathtub.

  • Parque das Andorinhas: This park is a nature lover’s paradise. It’s home to many species of birds, as well as waterfalls, hiking trails, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ponta Grossa

Ponta Grossa offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

  • Cachoeira da Mariquinha: Not very far from the city, this trail has a moderate difficulty level, and leads to a beautiful waterfall, Mariquinha, where hikers swim in the natural pool.

  • Trilha do Pinheiro: A hike through the trails of Pinheiro is highly recommended as you get to observe the fascinating flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Ponta Grossa: Activities and Tours

Here is a list of some of the best things to do in Ponta Grossa:

  • Take the “Trem da Vida” (Train of Life) Tour: A mesmerizing experience for all the senses. This tour takes you through the mountains of Campos Gerais, through tunnels and the Vila Velha Park.

  • Explore Vila Velha Park: With its surreal rock formations shaped like chimneys and forts, the Vila Velha Park opens up a world of possibilities for exploration and adventure. You can enjoy many activities such as hiking trails, horseback riding, and even gliding through the clouds in a paraglider.

  • Take a day trip to Foz do Iguaçu: It is one of the most incredible natural wonders in the world, with breathtaking waterfalls and a unique ecosystem.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ponta Grossa

Day 1: Visit Museu Campos Gerais and Catedral de Nossa Senhora da Conceição
Day 2: Explore Parque Estadual de Vila Velha or Buraco do Padre Day 3: Take a bike ride on the Trilha do Pinheiro Day 4: Relax at Parque das Andorinhas Day 5: Take the “Trem da Vida” (Train of Life) Tour Day 6: Visit the Memorial da Imigração Holandesa Day 7: Day trip to the Foz do Iguaçu.

Food and Drink Scene of Ponta Grossa

Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ponta Grossa:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado - a slow-cooked beef stew
  • Arroz de carreteiro - a hearty rice dish with beef, beans, and bacon
  • Pierogi - a Polish dish with dumplings filled with meat or cheese and served with sour cream

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - a distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice
  • Mate - a traditional South American beverage made from the yerba mate plant

Where to Stay in Ponta Grossa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best options for accommodation in Ponta Grossa:

  • Bourbon Ponta Grossa Convetion Hotel: A luxurious option with a swimming pool, steam sauna, and restaurant.

  • Hostel Casa Verde: A reasonably priced option located in a central location where you can mingle with other travelers.

  • Pousada Villa Fontana: A guesthouse with a lovely garden and boutique decor near the city’s main square.

What to Buy in Ponta Grossa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Do not miss the sweet or salted version of the original “Joconde Wafer” on which you can spread a variety of toppings.

Local Culture and Customs of Ponta Grossa

The people of Ponta Grossa are proud of their local heritage and culture. They celebrate the region’s cultural diversity through events, festivals, and local cuisines.

Best Time to Visit Ponta Grossa

The best time to visit Ponta Grossa is between March and May or September and November when the temperature is cooler, and tourists are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ponta Grossa

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine such as Barreado
  • Take your umbrella as weather can be unpredictable
  • Learn some Portuguese phrases to communicate better with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Visit during the Christmas holiday season, as the city is overcrowded.
  • Wear excessive jewelry and carry large amounts of cash
  • Be disrespectful of the local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ponta Grossa

Ponta Grossa is generally a safe place for tourists. However, it’s advisable to avoid walking alone at night and taking proper precautions against petty crimes such as pickpocketing and purse snatching.

Health in Ponta Grossa

Ponta Grossa does not pose any significant health risks to tourists. One should be careful about mosquitoes to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as Dengue fever.

Getting to Ponta Grossa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ponta Grossa

Afonso Pena International Airport (CWB) is the nearest airport to Ponta Grossa, located around 131 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ponta Grossa

Rodoviária de Ponta Grossa is the main bus station, connecting Ponta Grossa to nearby towns and cities in Brazil.

Other Options

One can take a rental car or a taxi from the airport to Ponta Grossa or hire a car from the bus station.

How to Get Around in Ponta Grossa

The easiest way to get around in Ponta Grossa is by taxi, car rental, or public transportation. Public buses, which are cheaper, run frequently throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Ponta Grossa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting after Ponta Grossa:

  • Curitiba: Visiting Curitiba is an excellent option for a day trip from Ponta Grossa, where you can explore the beautiful Botanical Garden, vibrant nightlife, and the local culture.

  • Morretes: Located about 120 km from Ponta Grossa, Morretes is a picturesque town known for its delicious cuisine, colonial architecture, and its historic train ride.

Conclusion

Ponta Grossa is the perfect destination for those looking to explore the history, culture, and natural beauty of Brazil. Its stunning museums, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures will captivate your senses and leave an unforgettable experience in your memory.
