Introduction to Maringá

Maringá is a city located in the south of Brazil, in the state of Paraná. It is known for its vibrant cultural scene, beautiful parks and natural attractions, as well as its impressive architecture. Whether you’re a nature lover, an architecture aficionado or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Maringá has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Maringá

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cathedral of Maringá - With its 124-metre-high tower, this cathedral is one of the tallest in the world, and a true architectural wonder. Make sure to climb up to the top of the tower for breath-taking views of the city.
  • Museum of the University of Maringá - This museum is located inside the university campus and houses a wide range of exhibits, including archeological objects, paintings, and historical artifacts.
  • Maringá Municipal Museum - Housed in a beautiful historical building, this museum displays the art and culture of Maringá and its surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Maringá: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque do Ingá - This park offers a beautiful natural escape from the city and is a favorite among locals. It has several trails for walking and jogging, a lake, and a picnic area.
  • Parque Nacional do Superagui - This national park is a biodiverse treasure trove, located just a short drive from Maringá. Here, you can find a variety of ecosystems, including wetlands, forests, and coastal areas.
  • Parque do Japão - As the name suggests, this park celebrates Japanese culture and tradition. It features a beautiful Japanese garden, a koi pond, and a temple.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maringá

Maringá has a variety of trails and routes that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Trilha Pico Paraná - A challenging but beautiful hike that takes you up to the top of the Paraná peak, the highest mountain in the region.
  • Ciclovia da Mandacaru - A 22-kilometer-long cycling path that takes you through some of the most scenic areas of Maringá.

Best Things to Do in Maringá: Activities and Tours

  • Helicopter tour of the city - See Maringá from a bird’s eye view by taking a helicopter tour of the city.
  • City tour by bike - Rent a bike and explore Maringá’s cultural and historical landmarks at your own pace.
  • Food tour - Take a food tour of Maringá to sample local specialties and learn about the city’s culinary culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Maringá

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz Carreteiro - A traditional rice dish that is popular in the region, made with beef, onions, and garlic.
  • Barreado - A beef stew that is slow-cooked for hours and served with rice and farofa.
  • Paçoca de Amendoim - A sweet dessert made with peanuts and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - A distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane juice.
  • Cerveja Artesanal - Craft beer is becoming increasingly popular in Maringá and the surrounding regions.

Where to Stay in Maringá: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Deville Maringá - A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, with a rooftop pool and panoramic views of Maringá.
  • Hostel Cândido Portinari - A budget-friendly hostel located in a charming historical building, with comfortable beds and clean bathrooms.
  • Pousada das Águias Guesthouse - A peaceful guesthouse surrounded by greenery, with cozy rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Maringá: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts - Maringá is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and woodcarvings.
  • Artisanal cheese - The region is famous for its cheese production, and you can find a variety of local cheeses at markets and shops.
  • Coffee beans - Paraná is one of the largest coffee producers in Brazil, and you can find high-quality beans in Maringá.

Local Culture and Customs of Maringá

Maringá has a diverse population and a rich cultural history. Some customs and traditions that are important to the locals include:

  • Festivals - Maringá hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, including a Japanese Festival, a Folklore Festival, and a Christmas Festival.
  • Religious celebrations - The city has a strong Catholic presence, and religious celebrations such as Easter and Christmas are important to many of its residents.
  • Respect for nature - Maringá is surrounded by lush forests and parks, and residents take pride in preserving the region’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Maringá

The best time to visit Maringá is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is cooler and less humid during this time, making it more pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maringá

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially traditional dishes like barreado and arroz carreteiro.
  • Do take advantage of Maringá’s natural beauty by visiting parks and hiking trails.
  • Do dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter - Maringá prides itself on its clean and well-maintained parks and streets.
  • Don’t disrespect religious traditions or customs.
  • Don’t forget to carry cash - many small businesses do not accept credit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maringá

Maringá is generally a safe city, but like any urban area, it’s important to take basic safety precautions. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in areas that are not well-lit.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services.

Health in Maringá

Maringá has a good healthcare system and several hospitals and clinics in the city, as well as pharmacies located throughout the area. However, it’s important to take appropriate health precautions such as using insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Maringá: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maringá

Maringá has its own airport, the Regional de Maringá-Silvio Name Junior Airport, which is served by several airlines that operate daily domestic flights to and from major cities in Brazil.

Bus Stations Near Maringá

Maringá is connected to other cities in Brazil by a reliable bus system, and there are several bus stations located throughout the city. Some of the major companies that operate in Maringá include Viação Garcia, Viação Umuarama, and Viação Águia Branca.

Other Options

Maringá is located on the interstate BR-376 highway, which connects it to other major cities such as Curitiba and São Paulo. Additionally, there are several car rental companies located in the city for those who prefer to rent a car for the duration of their stay.

How to Get Around in Maringá

Maringá has a reliable and affordable public transportation system, with buses that run regularly throughout the city. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer a more convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Maringá: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maringá is located in a beautiful region of Brazil, with several other cities and towns nearby that are worth visiting. Some popular destinations include:

  • Londrina - Located about 90 kilometers from Maringá, this city is known for its vibrant cultural scene and beautiful parks.
  • Curitiba - The capital of Paraná and one of the greenest cities in the world, known for its impressive urban planning and architecture.
  • Foz do Iguaçu - Located about 480 kilometers from Maringá, this city is home to the world-famous Iguaçu Falls, as well as several other natural and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Maringá is a hidden gem in the south of Brazil, with a rich cultural history, impressive architecture, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a nature lover, a foodie, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Maringá has something to offer everyone. With this guide in hand, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Maringá.
