Introduction to Paraná

Located in Brazil’s southern region, Paraná is a state that has it all – vibrant cities, stunning natural landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage. The state is known for its diverse geography, ranging from the Atlantic coastline in the east to the Iguaçu Falls, one of the world’s natural wonders, in the west. The countryside is dotted with verdant hills, lush forests, and winding rivers that provide a backdrop for a range of outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and white-water rafting.

Paraná is also home to several historic towns and villages that have preserved their traditional architecture and cultural practices. The capital city, Curitiba, is known for its innovative urban planning, efficient public transportation system, and a thriving arts scene.

With so much to explore in Paraná, it can be overwhelming to plan a trip. This guide will help you discover the state’s top destinations, activities, and hidden gems.

Food and Drink Scene of Paraná

Paraná’s cuisine is a blend of indigenous, European, and African influences, making it a culinary delight. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Barreado - a slow-cooked beef stew served with rice and cassava flour
  2. Pierogi - a Polish dumpling filled with meat, cheese, or potato
  3. Paraná Fish and Seafood dishes - barreado, grilled fish, and shrimp stew
  4. Churrasco - grilled meat served with a variety of side dishes
  5. Corn and cassava based dishes - polenta, pamonha, and farofa

Local Beverages

  1. Cachaça - traditional Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane juice
  2. Mate tea - a traditional South American drink made from steeped yerba mate leaves
  3. Microbrewed Beer - several craft breweries in Curitiba and other cities of Paraná offer unique flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Paraná

The people of Paraná are friendly, and their culture reflects the diverse heritage of the region. Brazilians in Paraná are fond of samba music, and the state has a vibrant art scene that is reflected in several museums and galleries. Greeting people with a kiss on the cheek rather than a handshake is a common Brazilian custom.

Best Time to Visit Paraná

The best time to visit Paraná depends on what you want to do and see. For outdoor activities and sightseeing, the ideal time would be during the dry season from May to September. For those who wish to experience the local culture, the state’s numerous festivals and events take place throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paraná

Do:

  1. Learn a few basic Portuguese phrases.
  2. Dress comfortably for outdoor activities.
  3. Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  4. Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  1. Display your wealth or valuable items in public.
  2. Leave your belongings unattended.
  3. Wear expensive jewelry or watches in crowded areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paraná

Paraná is generally considered a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, as in any other destination, it’s best to take precautions such as avoiding isolated areas, not displaying valuables and being alert when using public transportation.

Health in Paraná

Visitors to Paraná should be up to date on routine vaccinations and consider getting vaccinated before traveling. The state has good health care facilities, especially in the major cities. Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and buy bottled water instead.
