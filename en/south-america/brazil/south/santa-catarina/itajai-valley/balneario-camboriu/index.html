Introduction to Balneário Camboriú

When it comes to Brazilian beaches, Balneário Camboriú stands out among the rest with its unique combination of scenic beauty and entertainment options. Located in the Itajaí Valley of Santa Catarina in southern Brazil, this coastal gem has it all: long stretches of golden sand, crystal-clear waters, green forests, thrilling activities, vibrant nightlife, and a rich culture.

But Balneário Camboriú is not just about soaking up the sun and partying. It also offers an array of attractions, from historical landmarks to natural wonders, making it a versatile destination for all types of travelers. If you’re planning to visit this Brazilian paradise, keep reading to learn about the must-visit places, exciting tours and activities, local cuisine and culture, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Balneário Camboriú

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Balneário Camboriú is brimming with museums and historical landmarks that showcase its fascinating history and culture. Here are some of the most popular ones that you should not miss:

  • Museum of Contemporary Art: This modern art museum located on the hills of Balneário Camboriú offers a vast collection of Brazilian contemporary artworks.

  • Unipraias Park: This panoramic cable car ride connects three stations and provides breathtaking views of Balneário Camboriú. Each stop is an attraction in itself, with various activities such as zip-lining, trekking, and walking on a suspended bridge available.

  • Naval Memorial: This museum highlights the naval history of Santa Catarina. It is located on the banks of the Itajaí-Açu River and features marine artifacts and historic vessels on display.

  • Cristo Luz monument: An iconic statue of Christ that rises 103 meters high, this monument offers a stunning view of the cityscape and coastline.

  • Atlântico Shopping Mall: With over 200 shops, restaurants and entertainment areas, the mall is a must-visit for those looking to indulge in some retail therapy.

Natural Attractions of Balneário Camboriú: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surrounded by the Atlantic Forest, Balneário Camboriú has several natural attractions that offer endless opportunities for outdoor activities and scenic wonders. Here are some top picks:

  • Parque Natural Municipal do Morro do Careca: This natural park on a hill is home to an abundance of wildlife and vegetation. The panoramic view from the top offers a stunning vista of the city.

  • Praia das Laranjeiras: This beach is surrounded by hills on both sides and competes with others for being one of the most beautiful beaches in the area.

  • Praia Central: This is the main beach of Balneário Camboriú and is perfect for sunbathing and swimming in the turquoise water.

  • Parque Cyro Gevaerd: Also known as Balneário Camboriú Zoo, it features a variety of animals from the Amazon jungle and replicas of dinosaurs.

  • Pico da Pedra, Camboriú: A hill located in the nearby town of Camboriú, which offers a challenging hike to the top, but the reward is a panoramic view of Balneário Camboriú and the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balneário Camboriú

Apart from the well-known ones like Parque Natural Municipal do Morro do Careca and Pico da Pedra, Balneário Camboriú has several other hiking and biking trails that offer stunning vistas and a chance to explore the beauty of Santa Catarina. Some favorite routes are:

  • Trilha Praia do Estaleirinho: An easy trek that leads to a secluded beach, one of the lesser-known beaches in Balneário Camboriú.

  • Caminho do Santuário: A historical trail that passes through a series of old churches and chapels in the region.

  • Praia de Taquaras: A beautiful beach with relatively calm waters and a picturesque lighthouse at the end of the trail.

Best Things to Do in Balneário Camboriú: Activities and Tours

Besides the stunning beaches and amazing parks, Balneário Camboriú has a plethora of thrilling activities that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. Here are some of the best tours and activities:

  • Oceanic Aquarium: A great place to observe marine creatures. It exhibits over 1500 species in over 25 tanks and also organizes snorkeling tours.

  • Surf Lessons: Balneário Camboriú is a surfer’s paradise, and there are plenty of surf schools offering classes for beginners and experts alike.

  • Jet Ski and Boat Tours: If you want to explore beaches and coves inaccessible by foot, book a jet-ski or boat tour.

  • Balneário Camboriú Cable Car Ride: An exciting rope way ride that gives breathtaking views of the whole city and landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Balneário Camboriú

Day 1: Visit the Cristo Luz monument and enjoy a panoramic view of the city. Head over to Atlântico Shopping Mall for some retail therapy.

Day 2: Explore Parque Cyro Gevaerd and admire the animals and enjoy the replica dinosaurs. Follow this up with a visit to the Naval Memorial Museum.

Day 3: Take the cable car to enjoy panoramic views of Balneário Camboriú. Take part in adventure activities like zip-lining and treks at the various stops.

Day 4: Head to Praia das Laranjeiras to enjoy a day on the beach and light water sports activities

Day 5: Go for a hike on Trilha Praia do Estaleirinho and visit the hidden beach at the trail’s end.

Day 6: Rent a boat or a jet-ski tour to explore Balneário Camboriú’s hidden coves and bays in the Atlantic.

Day 7: Departure day: Head to Praia Central for a final beach day before leaving.

Food and Drink Scene of Balneário Camboriú

Must-Try Dishes

Balneário Camboriú is famous for its seafood, and visitors can find fresh seafood dishes in every part of the city. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Moqueca: A typical Brazilian seafood stew that is made with fish, crab, shrimp, onions, and tomatoes and infused with coconut milk and peppers.

  • Camarão ao Bafo: Literally translated as ‘steam shrimp,’ the dish includes shrimp cooked in garlic butter, salt, and lemon juice.

  • Pastel de Camarão: A light pastry stuffed with shrimp, cheese, and spices.

  • Feijoada: A hearty traditional Brazilian stew made from black beans, pork, beef, and chorizo.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: The signature Brazilian cocktail made with cachaca (a distilled spirit made of sugarcane), lime, and sugar.

  • Chopp: Brazilian-style draft beer, generally brewed and delivered locally.

  • Suco de Abacaxi: Pineapple juice, a refreshing drink that is popular, especially in summer.

Where to Stay in Balneário Camboriú: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Balneário Camboriú has no shortage of accommodation options for travelers, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular lodgings are:

  • Infinity Blue Resort & Spa: A luxury hotel located in the hills, offering panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Sibara Flat Hotel & Convenções: A modern hotel located close to both Praia Central and Atlântico Shopping Mall.

  • Reserva Praia Hotel: A relaxed boutique hotel with modern amenities and a great view of the beach.

  • Green Valley Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Balneário Camboriú, close to all major attractions.

What to Buy in Balneário Camboriú: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home are:

  • Handmade Crafts: Brazilian crafts made of wicker, ceramics and colored fibers, commonly found in the markets.

  • Brazilian Cheese: A must-try when you’re there. This cheese is similar to mozzarella but with a different texture and flavor.

  • Bikinis and Swimwear: Brazil is famous for its swimwear collection, and many beach shops sell beautiful bikinis and swimsuits.

  • Cachaça: A distilled alcoholic drink made from sugarcane, and is the star ingredient in caipirinhas.

Local Culture and Customs of Balneário Camboriú

Balneário Camboriú has a lively and diverse culture, influenced by indigenous tribes, European settlers, and African cultures. The languages spoken are Portuguese, Spanish, and English. Religion in the area is predominantly Catholicism, but nonetheless, the city is a multicultural hub, and so respect for cultural nuances is essential.

Best Time to Visit Balneário Camboriú

The best time to visit Balneário Camboriú is between October and March when the weather is warmer, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balneário Camboriú

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and bring sun protection gear like hats, sunglasses, and beachwear.

  • Respect local customs and culture.

  • Speak a little Portuguese if possible, and be courteous to locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be too flashy with your belongings. Be sure to keep an eye on valuable items.

  • Don’t litter, be mindful of your trash to avoid not polluting the natural environment.

  • Don’t visit dangerous areas of the town alone to avoid incidents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Balneário Camboriú

Balneário Camboriú is generally safe, but be wise with your belongings when enjoying the nightlife. Avoid isolated places if traveling alone and ensure to lock your doors and windows of your accommodations. If you encounter any problems, you can visit the Polícia Civil de Santa Catarina station.

Health in Balneário Camboriú

According to the WHO, the government has been improving the health care system in Brazil, and Balneário Camboriú has many high-quality clinics and hospitals. It’s worth noting, however, that the public healthcare system has limitations and long wait times. Ensure adequate travel insurance coverage for a hassle-free trip.

Getting to Balneário Camboriú: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balneário Camboriú

The nearest airport is the Navegantes Airport (NVT), which is only a 30-minute drive away from Balneário Camboriú. Connecting flights from major airports and Brazilian cities arrive and depart from here.

Bus Stations Near Balneário Camboriú

Balneário Camboriú has two bus stations: The central station can travel to all Brazilian cities, while the second nearby station serves the local and neighboring townships.

Other Options

For groups or individual transport, ride-hailing apps like Uber, Didi, and others, operate in and around Balneário Camboriú. Car rental services are also readily available for visitors who want to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in Balneário Camboriú

The best way to navigate Balneário Camboriú is via a combination of walking, cycling, and cab rides as the city is well connected with extensive pedestrian, bike lanes, and public transport.

Where to Go Next from Balneário Camboriú: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

A few popular excursions nearby to visit from Balneário Camboriú:

  • Florianópolis: A tranquil beach town located about a few hours’ drive from Balneário Camboriú.

  • Penha: A small town located to the north of Balneário Camboriú that’s home to the famous Beto Carrero World theme park.

  • Blumenau: A German-colonized city located about two hours’ drive from Balneário Camboriú, rich in local architecture, history, and traditions.

Conclusion

Balneário Camboriú is a one-of-a-kind beach destination that offers a unique blend of natural, historical, and cultural attractions. Visitors can enjoy anything from hiking and exploring natural parks to experiencing vibrant nightlife and dining on fantastic local cuisine. Add the town’s friendly locals and relaxed culture, and you have a beautiful periphery of Brazil that is worth exploring.
