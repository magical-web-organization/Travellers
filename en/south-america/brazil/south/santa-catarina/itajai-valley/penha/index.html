Introduction to Penha

Penha is a charming Brazilian municipality located in the Itajaí Valley in the southern state of Santa Catarina. It is known for its breathtaking beaches, exciting theme parks, and rich cultural heritage. This coastal town is famous for its unique attractions that attract visitors from every corner of the world.

List of Places to Visit in Penha

If you’re looking for things to do in Penha, you’re in luck. This city features a variety of both natural and cultural attractions. Here are some of the best places to visit in Penha:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beto Carrero World: The largest theme park in Latin America, this attraction has over 100 rides, entertainment shows and country-themed villages.
  • Projeto Tamar: This sea turtle conservation center welcomes visitors with a series of aquariums and large tanks with sharks and rays.
  • Museu Oceanográfico Univali: A unique museum run by Univali University that offers students and visitors a glimpse of the marine life in the surrounding sea.

Natural Attractions of Penha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural Municipal Da Serra do Itajaí: For nature lovers, the park is home to natural beauty and around 100 km of trails.
  • Praia do Quilombo: A stunning white sandy beach that offers several opportunities for outdoor activities like surfing and kite-surfing.
  • Ilha Feia: For those seeking solitude, this secluded island offers stunning views and is perfect for bird watching, camping, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Penha

Penha offers several hiking and biking trails, taking visitors through lush forests and pristine coastline. Some of the most popular routes are:

  • Trilha da Figueira: A moderate level hiking trail that leads to the 500-year-old Figueira tree at the entrance of Beto Carrero World.
  • Ciclovia: A scenic six km biking trail along the Praia do Grande beach.

Best Things to Do in Penha: Activities and Tours

  • Gozetto Tirolesa: A thrilling zipline ride that spans more than 300 meters across the Penha Jungle.
  • Rafting in Penha: For adventurous visitors, rafting down the Itajaí-Açu River is a thrilling experience.
  • San Francisco de Assis Sanctuary: A tour of this historic church, dating back to the mid-18th century, is a must-visit.

Food and Drink Scene in Penha

Food and drink in Penha are inspired by the city’s local culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp Bobo: a dish, stew served on pureed cassava blend with coconut milk and shrimp, is one of the most traditional ones in Penha.
  • Churrasco: a Brazilian classic recipe of barbecued tender meat seasoned to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail served with lime, sugar, and cachaça, a distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice.
  • Cerveja Artesanal: Penha has several micro-breweries producing their craft beers, such as Cervejaria Das Enguias and Cervejaria Barra Sul.

Where to Stay in Penha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Penha offers a wide variety of accommodations for all budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Pousada Quinta da Baleeira: A luxurious guesthouse with a relaxing pool and access to Praia da Paciencia, one of Penha’s most beautiful beaches.
  • Pousada Recanto das Flores: A cozy bed and breakfast located in the center of Penha, with easy access to local shops and restaurants.
  • Grand Mercure Hotel: A luxury hotel with access to Beto Carrero World and a private water park.

What to Buy in Penha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Penha, you might want to bring home unique souvenirs or local products from the city. Some popular options include:

  • Artisan cachaça: Produced in the surrounding towns, these are sold at local markets and distilleries.
  • Handicrafts: Made by local artisans and sold on the streets and beaches.
  • Coffee: Penha’s artisanal coffee is highly rated, and you can find it in local shops.

Local Cultures and Customs of Penha

Penha is a melting pot of different cultures and customs. It is a welcoming and inclusive city, which fiercely values its traditions. One of the key local customs to remember is the emphasis on respect for family and elders and the importance of religion in daily life.

Best Time to Visit Penha

The best time to visit Penha is from March to November, which is the dry season. The weather during these months is perfect for outdoor activities, and the town’s festivals and events happen mainly during the offseason, providing a more cultural experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Penha

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Penha:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear sunblock and insect repellent for outdoor activities.
  • Bring cash, as some small stores and markets may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when entering religious sites.
  • Engage in any illegal activities, including drug use.

Safety and Health

Safety in Penha

Penha is a relatively safe town, but visitors should be cautious around large crowds, beaches, and parks and avoid walking alone at night. It is also recommended to keep your valuables in safety deposit boxes or securely locked in your accommodations.

Health in Penha

Visitors should ensure that their vaccines are up to date and be vigilant against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever. It is also essential to stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Penha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Penha

The nearest airport is Navegantes Minister Victor Konder International Airport (NVT), which is located 12 kilometers away from the center of Penha. The airport has several domestic and international flights serving tourists and locals.

Bus Stations Near Penha

The Penha bus station is located at Rua Do Sossego, 453, in the city center. It has an extensive network of bus routes connecting the town with nearby cities like Itajaí, Balneário Camboriú, and Florianópolis.

Other Options

The best way to travel to Penha is by private vehicle or rental car, which provides flexibility and convenience while exploring the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Penha

Penha is a small city with easy accessibility for visitors. You can use local buses, taxis, or rideshare services like Uber, or rent a bike or car.

Where to Go Next from Penha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Penha has some fantastic nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best:

  • Florianópolis: With world-renowned beaches like Praia Mole and Joaquina, it is a must-visit destination that attracts surfers and beachgoers from around the globe.
  • Balneário Camboriú: Known as the “Brazilian Dubai,” this city’s luxury high rises and city attractions offer a unique blend of urban, beach, and mountain landscapes.
  • Itapema: A charming coastal town to lay back, relax, and enjoy the local cuisine and beaches.

Conclusion

Penha is a town with something for everyone. From nature lovers and thrill-seekers to culturally curious travelers, Penha’s diversity provides a memorable trip for anyone. Ensure you take the time to explore the city’s beauty, immerse yourself in the local culture, and enjoy with your family and friends.
