Introduction to Tijucas

Tijucas is a charming city located in the Grande Florianópolis region of Santa Catarina, Brazil. With a population of over 35 thousand residents, this bustling city is known for its stunning natural landscapes, vibrant culture, and rich history. From exploring ancient ruins and traditional museums to embarking on adventurous outdoor excursions, there is no shortage of fun and unique things to do in Tijucas.

Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Tijucas has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will explore the many fascinating attractions and activities that make Tijucas a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Tijucas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tijucas is a city rich in history and culture. Visitors can explore many ancient ruins and museums that showcase the artistic and cultural heritage of the region - including:

  • Tijucas Archeological Museum
  • São José Fort
  • São Francisco de Paula Church
  • Casa Cultural de Tijucas

Each of these attractions offers a unique glimpse into the lives and traditions of the people who have called this region home.

Natural Attractions of Tijucas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tijucas is well-known for its stunning natural landscapes, pristine beaches, and adventurous outdoor excursions. Many visitors flock to the region to enjoy activities such as:

  • Surfing at Praia Brava
  • Trekking through Parque Estadual da Serra do Tabuleiro
  • Catching a glimpse of rare wildlife at Reserva Volta Velha

Whether you’re looking for a thrilling adventure or a peaceful escape, Tijucas has a wealth of natural wonders to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tijucas

For those who love to hike or bike, Tijucas is a paradise filled with scenic routes and challenging trails. The region’s natural beauty is highlighted on these routes, which offer breathtaking views of mountains, forests, and coastline.

Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Trilha do Macaco;
  • Trilha da Cachoeira do Ferreira;
  • Trilha do Encantado.

These trails range in difficulty, making them accessible to visitors of all ages and skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Tijucas: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of exciting activities and tours offered in Tijucas. From exploring ancient ruins to embarking on thrilling outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Some of the most popular activities and tours include:

  • Jeep tours of the countryside
  • Walking tours of the historic center
  • Horseback riding tours on the beach
  • Adventure sports (surfing, trekking, ziplining)

There’s no better way to experience the rich culture and natural landscapes of Tijucas than through a guided tour or activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tijucas

If you’re planning a trip to Tijucas, you may be wondering how to make the most of your time in the region. Here is a sample week-long itinerary that showcases the best the region has to offer:

Day 1: Explore the archeological museum and visit São José Fort.

Day 2: Hike through the Parque Estadual da Serra do Tabuleiro and catch a glimpse of rare wildlife in its natural habitat.

Day 3: Embark on a guided jeep tour of the countryside, exploring ancient trails and scenic vistas off the beaten path.

Day 4: Take a walking tour of the historic center, visiting churches, museums, food markets, and other cultural attractions.

Day 5: Spend a day lounging on the pristine beaches of Tijucas, with opportunities for surfing, paddleboarding, and beach volleyball.

Day 6: Explore the beautiful scenery of Reserva Volta Velha, catch a glimpse of rare flora and fauna, and visit the Mangrove Restoration Project.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local bar or restaurant, enjoying local cuisine, live music, and the vibrant nightlife of Tijucas.

Food and Drink Scene of Tijucas

Must-Try Dishes

Tijucas is renowned for its unique culinary traditions, blending indigenous, African, and European ingredients and flavors. Some of the must-try dishes in Tijucas include:

  • Moqueca de Peixe (a traditional seafood stew with coconut milk and spices)
  • Pirão (a thick porridge made from cassava/manioc flour)
  • Feijoada (a hearty bean and meat stew)
  • Tainha na Grelha (grilled mullet fish)

Many of these traditional dishes can be enjoyed at local restaurants and markets throughout the city.

Local Beverages

Tijucas is also famous for its local beverages, including:

  • Cachaça (a distilled spirit made from sugarcane juice)
  • Chopp (draft beer often served in a frosted glass)
  • Caipirinha (a cocktail made from cachaça, lime, sugar, and ice)

Visitors to Tijucas can also enjoy a variety of locally produced wines and artisanal craft beers.

What to Buy in Tijucas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those traveling to Tijucas, there are many unique and locally produced items to bring home as souvenirs or gifts. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics

Local Culture and Customs of Tijucas

Tijucas is a city with a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the blending of indigenous, African, and European traditions. Visitors to the city will find a friendly and welcoming community that celebrates diversity and hospitality.

One of the most unique customs of Tijucas is the celebration of the Festa do Boi Tamboara, a traditional festival that honors the indigenous musicians who played music for the cattle during the colonial era.

Visitors to the city should also be aware of the tradition of chimarrão, a tea made from yerba mate leaves that is shared among friends and family as a sign of hospitality and friendship.

Best Time to Visit Tijucas

The best time to visit Tijucas is between September and December, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the tourist crowds are smaller. Visitors should be aware that the region can experience occasional rainstorms, particularly during the summer months (January through March).

When traveling to Tijucas, visitors should keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore the natural landscape of the region
  • Visit the museums and historical sites to learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beaches
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Tijucas is generally a safe and healthy place to visit, with no major health risks or safety concerns for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from the sun, stay hydrated, and practice basic safety measures to prevent theft and other crimes.

Safety in Tijucas

Visitors to Tijucas should take the following safety measures to ensure a safe and enjoyable visit:

  • Only use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services when traveling around the city
  • Always keep valuable items secured and out of sight

Health in Tijucas

Visitors to Tijucas should take precautions to protect themselves from the sun and stay hydrated in the hot and humid climate of the region. It is also recommended to get up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Brazil and to take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Tijucas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Tijucas have several transportation options for getting to the region, including:

Airports Near Tijucas

The nearest airport to Tijucas is Hercílio Luz International Airport in Florianópolis, approximately 45 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Tijucas

Visitors can take a bus to Tijucas from nearby cities such as Florianópolis or São Paulo.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to travel to Tijucas.

How to Get Around in Tijucas

There are several transportation options for getting around Tijucas, including:

  • Taxis
  • Ride-sharing services (Uber, 99)
  • Buses

Visitors can also rent a car or bicycle to explore the region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Tijucas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore more of Santa Catarina and neighboring regions, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider, including:

  • Florianópolis: Explore the charming capital city of Santa Catarina, with its bustling waterfront, historic center, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Balneário Camboriú: Visit the bustling beach town of Balneário Camboriú, known for its modern architecture, fine dining, and amusement parks.
  • São Paulo: Take a road trip to the vibrant city of São Paulo, Brazil’s largest city and cultural capital.

Tijucas is a hidden gem in the heart of Santa Catarina, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality.
