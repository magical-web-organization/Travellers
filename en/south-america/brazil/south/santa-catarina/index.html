Introduction to Santa Catarina

Santa Catarina is a state located in the southern region of Brazil. It is known for its stunning beaches, rugged mountains, and vibrant culture. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, Santa Catarina is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a diverse and exciting trip. In this comprehensive guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Santa Catarina, including the best places to visit, where to eat and stay, and important safety and health considerations.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Catarina

There are countless places to visit in Santa Catarina, each offering its own unique charm and beauty. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Histórico de Santa Catarina: This museum houses a collection of historical artifacts and exhibits that chronicle the state’s past.
  • Fortaleza de Santa Cruz: This fort is a 16th-century military complex that served as a defense against pirates and other potential invaders.
  • Casa das Máquinas: This museum is located in an old power station and showcases the history of industrialization in Santa Catarina.

Natural Attractions of Santa Catarina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia do Rosa: This breathtaking beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and soft sand.
  • Serra do Rio do Rastro: This is a stunning winding road that offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Florianópolis: Known as the “Island of Magic,” this is a popular destination that boasts beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Catarina

Santa Catarina offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes:

  • Trilha do Morro da Coroa: This trail offers stunning views of the beach and is considered moderate in difficulty.
  • Trilha das Ninfas: This route is located in the Parque Estadual da Serra do Tabuleiro and features stunning waterfalls and lush vegetation.
  • Passeio de Bike em Jurerê: This is a great bike ride that takes you through some of the most beautiful neighborhoods in Florianópolis.

Best Things to Do in Santa Catarina: Activities and Tours

There are many fun and exciting things to do in Santa Catarina. Here are some of the best:

  • Surfing lessons at Praia Mole: This is a popular spot for surfers of all levels.
  • Stand-up paddleboarding on Lagoa da Conceição: This is a great way to explore the lake and surrounding nature.
  • Boat tour of Baía dos Golfinhos: This tour takes you to an area where you can see dolphins and sea turtles up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Catarina

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Santa Catarina:

  • Day 1-2: Spend the first two days exploring Florianópolis, including a visit to Praia Mole and a hike on the Trilha da Galheta.
  • Day 3-4: Visit the quaint town of Garopaba and relax on the beautiful beach of Praia do Rosa.
  • Day 5-6: Explore the stunning scenery and hiking trails of Serra do Rio do Rastro and Parque Estadual da Serra do Tabuleiro.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a boat tour of Baía dos Golfinhos and watch for dolphins and sea turtles.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Catarina

Santa Catarina’s food and drink scene is known for its diversity and unique flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barreado: This is a beef dish made with spices and manioc flour.
  • Sequilho: These are small biscuits made with cornstarch and coconut.
  • Salpicão: This is a chicken salad made with mayonnaise, apple, and raisins.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This is a traditional Brazilian spirit made from sugarcane juice.
  • Mate: This is a popular drink made from dried leaves of the yerba mate plant.
  • Chopp: This is a type of beer that is served cold and is often enjoyed with friends.

Where to Stay in Santa Catarina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Catarina offers a variety of accommodations for travelers of all budgets. Here are some top options:

  • Majestic Palace Hotel: This is a luxury hotel located in Florianópolis that offers stunning views of the bay and a rooftop pool.
  • Eco Hostel Jardim da Barra: This is a budget-friendly hostel located in Barra da Lagoa with a garden and a shared kitchen.
  • Pousada Vila do Coral: This is a charming guesthouse located in Bombinhas, offering rustic, yet comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Santa Catarina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Santa Catarina offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some top options:

  • Handmade ceramics: Santa Catarina is known for its beautiful ceramics, which are often handcrafted by local artisans.
  • Traditional clothing: Items such as ponchos, woolen hats, and leather boots are popular souvenirs.
  • Cheese and wine: The state produces a variety of delicious cheeses and wines, which make great gifts for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Catarina

Santa Catarina is known for its rich cultural traditions. Here are some customs and practices to be aware of:

  • Family is highly valued: Family is an important part of life in Santa Catarina, and gatherings and meals often revolve around spending time with loved ones.
  • Respect for elders: Elders are highly respected in Santa Catarina, and it is customary to show deference to older individuals.
  • Festivals and celebrations: Santa Catarina is home to a variety of festivals and celebrations, including Carnival and Oktoberfest.

Best Time to Visit Santa Catarina

The best time to visit Santa Catarina is during the spring and fall months, from September to November and March to May, respectively. During these times, the weather is mild, and crowds are smaller. However, if you plan to visit during the summer months, from December to February, be prepared for high temperatures and large crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Catarina

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Santa Catarina:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Plan ahead for hikes and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, as it is not always safe for consumption.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Ignore safety warnings related to weather conditions.

Safety and Health

When visiting Santa Catarina, it is important to take proper safety and health precautions. Here are some important considerations:

Safety in Santa Catarina

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables with you.
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean, as strong currents can be dangerous.

Health in Santa Catarina

  • Use insect repellant to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Zika, Dengue fever, and Chikungunya.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid gastrointestinal issues.
  • Consult with a healthcare professional before traveling to ensure that you have all necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Santa Catarina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Santa Catarina is accessible by several transportation modes, including air, bus, and car. Here are some options:

Airports Near Santa Catarina

  • Hercílio Luz International Airport (FLN) in Florianópolis.
  • Navegantes Airport (NVT) in Navegantes.

Bus Stations Near Santa Catarina

  • Terminal Rodoviário Rita Maria in Florianópolis.
  • Terminal Rodoviário de Joinville in Joinville.

Other Options

  • Trains: There are no passenger trains in Santa Catarina.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services: These are widely available in larger cities like Florianópolis and Joinville.

How to Get Around in Santa Catarina

The best way to get around Santa Catarina is by car, as it gives you the most flexibility and allows you to explore the state’s many beautiful destinations. However, if you don’t want to drive, there are also several public transportation options, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Santa Catarina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Santa Catarina is a great base for exploring other parts of Brazil. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Rio Grande do Sul: This neighboring state boasts beautiful landscapes, such as hills, canyons, and beaches.
  • Paraná: This state is home to the famous waterfalls of Foz do Iguaçu and the historic city of Curitiba.
  • São Paulo: Brazil’s largest city offers a plethora of cultural and entertainment options, from museums to vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Santa Catarina offers the perfect mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, delicious food, and comfortable accommodations. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast or a city dweller, there’s something for everyone in this beautiful Brazilian state. By following our comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well on your way to planning the ultimate trip to Santa Catarina.
