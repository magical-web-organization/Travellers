Introduction to Tubarão

Nestled in the southern region of Santa Catarina, Brazil, Tubarão offers visitors a unique blend of rich heritage, natural beauty, and exciting outdoor adventures. With its beautiful beaches, charming historic sites, and vibrant local culture, Tubarão is a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic Brazilian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Tubarão

Here are the top places to visit and things to do during your trip to Tubarão:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tubarão boasts a rich history and is home to several museums and historical sites that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best:

  • Museu Ferroviário de Tubarão: The Tubarão Railway Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and train enthusiasts. It showcases the history of the railway system in Tubarão and highlights the importance of the railway in the region’s economic development.

  • Catedral Diocesana São José: The St. Joseph’s Cathedral is an architectural masterpiece located in the heart of Tubarão. It was built in the Neo-Gothic style and features stunning stained glass windows and intricate carvings.

  • Praça Walter Zumblick: This public square is a popular gathering place for locals and visitors alike. It has a beautiful fountain in the center and is surrounded by historical buildings and shops.

Natural Attractions of Tubarão: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tubarão is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, including mountains, rivers, and beaches. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Serra do Rio do Rastro: The Rio do Rastro Mountain Range offers breathtaking scenery and outdoor adventures. Visitors can explore the mountain range on foot or by car and enjoy views of lush forests, waterfalls, and stunning rock formations.

  • Praia do Gi: Located just a short drive from Tubarão, Praia do Gi is a beautiful beach that boasts crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, or simply relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

  • Lagoa de Santo Antônio: This natural lagoon is a paradise for nature lovers. It offers visitors the opportunity to spot a wide variety of bird species and other wildlife, as well as enjoy fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tubarão

Tubarão has several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. These include:

  • Trilha do Galo: The Galo Trail is a challenging hike that takes visitors through lush forests and up to stunning viewpoints. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Rota dos Engenhos: This biking route takes visitors through scenic countryside and past several old sugarcane mills. It is an excellent way to experience the local culture and history of the region.

Best Things to Do in Tubarão: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums, historical sites, and natural attractions listed above, Tubarão has several other exciting things to do and tours to take part in. These include:

  • Tasting local wine and cheese: Visitors can explore the region’s wine and cheese making traditions by taking a tour of local wineries and cheese factories.

  • Exploring local markets: Tubarão has several vibrant markets where visitors can buy fresh produce, local crafts, and other unique gifts.

  • Taking a river tour: Visitors can go on a guided tour of the region’s rivers and waterways, which offer a stunning view of the surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tubarão

Here is a week-long itinerary for Tubarão that covers all the main attractions:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic center of Tubarão, including the St. Joseph’s Cathedral and Praça Walter Zumblick.

  • Day 2: Visit the Tubarão Railway Museum and take a tour of the train station.

  • Day 3: Go on a guided river tour and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 4: Explore the stunning scenery of the Rio do Rastro Mountain Range and take a guided hike.

  • Day 5: Spend the day at Praia do Gi, swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the peaceful surroundings.

  • Day 6: Take a tour of local wineries and cheese factories and sample some of the region’s best-known products.

  • Day 7: Visit the Lagoa de Santo Antônio and enjoy fishing or boating on the lagoon.

Food and Drink Scene of Tubarão

Tubarão has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a wide variety of local dishes, wines, and beverages to try. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Feijão Tropeiro: This traditional dish is a hearty stew made with beans, sausage, and bacon.

  • Moqueca de Peixe: This fish stew is a popular dish in the region and is typically made with coconut milk, peppers, and tomatoes.

  • Churrasco: Brazilian barbecue is famous worldwide, and visitors to Tubarão should try this delicious dish.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A potent spirit made from sugar cane, cachaça is a popular local drink.

  • Vinho de Jabuticaba: This sweet wine is made from the Jabuticaba fruit and is a must-try for wine lovers.

  • Chimarrão: This traditional drink is a type of yerba mate tea that is popular in the region.

Where to Stay in Tubarão: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tubarão has several excellent options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to affordable guesthouses and hostels. Some of the top options include:

  • Hotel Sandrini: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Tubarão and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Pousada Verde Farol: This charming guesthouse is located near Praia do Gi and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.

  • Hostel Tubarão: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style accommodation and is perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Tubarão: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tubarão has several local products and souvenirs that make excellent gifts and mementos. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Local crafts: Visitors can buy handmade crafts like wooden figurines, ceramic pottery, and beautiful hand-woven textiles.

  • Cheeses: Tubarão is known for its excellent cheeses, which make great gifts or snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Tubarão

Tubarão has a unique local culture that reflects its history and geography. Visitors should make sure to experience some of the local customs and traditions, such as:

  • The Festa do Divino: This traditional festival is celebrated in June and includes parades, music, and traditional food and drink.

  • Horseback riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in the region and is a great way to experience the local countryside.

Best Time to Visit Tubarão

The best time to visit Tubarão is from September to November when the weather is dry and comfortable. However, visitors can also enjoy the destination year-round as the temperature stays mild most of the time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tubarão

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Tubarão:

Do:

  • Try the local wine and cheese.

  • Explore the beautiful natural scenery around Tubarão.

  • Experience the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches and other religious sites.

  • Be disrespectful to locals or ignore their customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tubarão

Tubarão is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should always exercise caution when traveling and take the usual precautions such as keeping an eye on belongings, using licensed taxis, and avoiding secluded areas at night.

Health in Tubarão

Tubarão offers excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where visitors can receive high-quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Tubarão: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tubarão is easily accessible by air, bus, and car.

Airports Near Tubarão

The nearest airport to Tubarão is the Florianópolis-Hercílio Luz International Airport, which is approximately 137 kilometers or 85 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Tubarão

Tubarão has a bus station that connects to several major cities in Brazil. Visitors can arrive in Tubarão by bus from other cities like Florianópolis and Porto Alegre.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Tubarão by car, taking the BR-101 highway in Santa Catarina.

How to Get Around in Tubarão

Getting around in Tubarão is easy, with several transportation options available. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use public transportation to get around the city and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Tubarão: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tubarão is surrounded by several other destinations in Santa Catarina that are worth visiting. These include:

  • Florianópolis: The state capital has stunning beaches and a charming historic center.

  • Balneário Camboriú: This seaside resort town is known for its beaches and nightlife.

  • Garopaba: A charming fishing village that boasts beautiful beaches, water sports, and unique culinary traditions.

Conclusion

Tubarão is a unique destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the local culture, history, and natural beauty of southern Brazil. With its stunning natural attractions, exciting outdoor adventures, and vibrant food and drink scene, Tubarão is a must-visit destination for any traveler seeking an authentic Brazilian experience.
