Imbituba is one of the hidden gems of Santa Catarina, with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural attractions. This charming city in southern Brazil is known for its gorgeous beaches, crystal-clear waters, rugged cliffs, and wildlife sanctuaries. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Imbituba has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, and much more. So pack your bags, grab your sunscreen, and get ready to explore Imbituba.

Introduction to Imbituba

Imbituba is a coastal city located in the southern state of Santa Catarina, Brazil. The city has a rich history dating back to the 18th century when it was a fishing village. Today, it is a thriving tourist destination known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and wildlife. Imbituba is located around 36 km south of the city of Laguna and around 90 km south of Florianópolis, the capital of Santa Catarina.

List of Places to Visit in Imbituba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Imbituba has a rich cultural heritage which can be explored in various museums and historical sites throughout the city. The following are some of the top cultural attractions in Imbituba:

  • Museu de Imbituba - a small but informative museum showcasing the history of Imbituba, from its indigenous roots to its colonial past and the present day. It is located in the heart of the city, close to other attractions, making it a great starting point for exploring Imbituba.

  • Casa da Alfândega - an 18th-century building that once served as a customs house and that now houses a cultural center with regular exhibitions, musical performances, and theater shows.

  • Praia do Rosa Lighthouse - a historic lighthouse built in 1885 and is located on-top of a hill providing panoramic views of the sea and surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Imbituba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Imbituba is a nature lover’s paradise, with a variety of outdoor adventures and scenic views for visitors to enjoy. From whale watching to hiking, surfing to lounging on the beach, here are some of the best natural attractions in Imbituba:

  • Praia do Rosa - one of the most famous beaches in Brazil and the ultimate destination for surfers. It is known for its rugged beauty, crystal-clear waters, and consistent waves.

  • Imbituba Whale Museum - dedicated to the marine giants that visit the region each year from July to November. Visitors can learn about the biology and behavior of these magnificent creatures and observe them from observation decks at various points along the coast.

  • Serra do Tabuleiro State Park - a vast natural reserve that spans over 84,130 hectares of unspoiled wilderness, varied wildlife, and scenic trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Imbituba

Imbituba offers plenty of options for hiking and biking enthusiasts who want to explore the natural beauty of the region. Some of the best routes include:

  • Caminho dos Pescadores - a 10 km long trail that runs along the coast, passing through Praia do Rosa, Ibiraquera, Ribanceira, and Itapirubá beaches. The trail offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding hills.

  • Trilha do Gravatá - a short but challenging trail that starts at Praia do Rosa and meanders through dense forests and steep hillsides, leading to a stunning panoramic view of the coast.

Best Things to Do in Imbituba: Activities and Tours

Imbituba has a diverse range of activities and tours on offer for visitors, from surfing to horseback riding, gastronomic tours to cultural excursions. Here are some of the best things to do in Imbituba:

  • Surfing - Imbituba’s famous beaches offer some of the best surfing conditions in Brazil, and several surfing schools and rental shops can get you out on the waves.

  • Praia do Rosa Horse Riding - explore Imbituba’s rolling hills and tranquil forests on horseback. The beachfront is the perfect backdrop for a relaxing or adventurous ride.

  • Whale Watching - from July to November, Imbituba is one of the best places to observe Southern Right Whales, as these magnificent creatures visit the region’s warm waters to mate, give birth, and nurse their young.

Week-Long Itinerary for Imbituba

Imbituba’s wealth of attractions makes it the perfect location for a week-long itinerary. Here’s how you could structure your seven-day visit to the city:

Day 1: Arrive in Imbituba, settle into your accommodation and spend the afternoon exploring the city center Day 2: Visit the Museu de Imbituba and other historical sites. Day 3: Go surfing or horseback riding on Praia do Rosa Day 4: Go whale watching and visit the Imbituba Whale Museum. Day 5: Take a tour to the Serra do Tabuleiro State Park or spend the day hiking on one of Imbituba’s trails. Day 6: Visit the Casa da Alfândega cultural center and catch a performance or exhibition. Day 7: Enjoy a gastronomic tour or relaxing day at the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Imbituba

Imbituba offers a unique gastronomic experience combining traditional Brazilian cuisine with local seafood specialties. Here are some must-try dishes in Imbituba:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sequência de Camarão - a platter of prawns served in various ways, pan-fried, baked, grilled, and sautéed.
  • Barreado - a slow-cooked beef stew that originated in Paraná with a rich and creamy flavor.
  • Moqueca de Peixe - a fish stew with coconut milk, palm oil, onions, garlic, cilantro, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - the national spirit of Brazil, distilled from fermented sugarcane juice.
  • Chopp - a draft beer served in local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Imbituba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Imbituba has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Imbituba:

  • Praia do Rosa Hostel - a charming and affordable option located close to Praia do Rosa.
  • Capitão n’Areia - a cozy bed and breakfast with a spectacular view of the sea.
  • Pousada Vistacalma - a luxury guesthouse with a swimming pool and beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Imbituba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Imbituba is known for its handicrafts, unique decorations, and clothing. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back from Imbituba:

  • Handmade pottery, particularly dishes and vases decorated with traditional Brazilian designs.
  • Hand-knitted woolen crafts and sweaters made from locally sourced wool.
  • Wooden carved statues and art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Imbituba

Imbituba is a melting pot of cultures, with a rich mixture of European, African, and Indigenous heritage. The locals are friendly, hospitable, and value family, tradition, and community above all else. Music and dance are an essential part of the culture, with samba, bossa nova, and forró being popular genres. The city has several annual music and dance festivals, including the Imbituba Jazz Festival, which takes place in August.

Best Time to Visit Imbituba

The best time to visit Imbituba is during the summer months (December to March), when the weather is sunny, warm, and conducive to outdoor activities. However, the whale-watching season is from July to November, so if you want to see these majestic creatures, plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Imbituba

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases before you go
  • Use sunscreen and a hat when exploring Imbituba’s outdoor attractions
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural sites and places of worship

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in the city
  • Don’t wear expensive jewelry or carry large sums of cash when exploring the city
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Imbituba

Imbituba is a generally safe destination, but like any other tourist destination, it’s important to take precautions. To stay safe, avoid isolated areas after dark, and always keep an eye on your belongings. If you’re planning to rent a car, park in secure and well-lit areas and avoid leaving valuables inside the car.

Health in Imbituba

Imbituba is a generally healthy destination, with no major health risks. However, it’s important to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and Zika. Use mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved shirts and pants when exploring the natural attractions of the city.

Getting to Imbituba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Imbituba is well-connected by air, road, and sea.

Airports Near Imbituba

The nearest airport to Imbituba is Hercílio Luz International Airport, which is located 90 km to the north in Florianópolis.

Bus Stations Near Imbituba

The Imbituba bus station is located in the city center, with regular services connecting the city to major cities in the region, including Florianópolis, Porto Alegre, and Curitiba.

Other Options

For those arriving by car, Imbituba is easily accessible via the BR-101 highway, which runs along the coast of Santa Catarina.

How to Get Around in Imbituba

The best way to get around Imbituba is by car, bike, or on foot. Most of the city’s attractions are within easy reach of each other, with several scenic routes connecting the various towns, beaches, and trails.

Where to Go Next from Imbituba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Imbituba is located in the heart of Santa Catarina’s stunning coastal region, making it an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the top options:

  • Florianópolis - the state’s vibrant capital city with a rich history, culture, and shopping scene.
  • Garopaba - a charming coastal town known for its beautiful beaches, excellent surf breaks, and laid-back vibe.

Conclusion

Imbituba is a hidden gem of Southern Brazil, with a rich history, natural beauty, and cultural charm. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural delights, or simply a relaxing beach retreat, Imbituba has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now ready to explore Imbituba like a local and make the most of your visit.
