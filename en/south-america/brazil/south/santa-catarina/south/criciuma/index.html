Introduction to Criciúma

Criciúma is a picturesque city located in southern Santa Catarina, Brazil, in South America. Despite being a relatively small city, it is known for its rich cultural heritage, gorgeous natural landscapes, and exciting outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we’ll take a deep dive into all the amazing things to see, do, and experience in Criciúma. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural attractions, conquering its hiking and biking routes, or indulging in its delicious food and drink scene, you’ll find everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Criciúma

Criciúma boasts a wealth of attractions that offer something for everyone, from history buffs to nature lovers to adventure seekers. Let’s take a closer look at some of the city’s top sights and experiences.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu de Zoologia da Unesc: This natural history museum is renowned for its comprehensive collection of South American animals, including birds, insects, and mammals, as well as fossils and rocks.
  • Casa da Cultura Neusa Nunes Vieira: Located in the heart of Criciúma, this cultural center showcases regional artworks, sculptures, and handicrafts, and frequently hosts concerts, dance shows, and other events.
  • Museu ao Ar Livre Princesa Isabel: Visitors can take a trip back in time with a visit to this outdoor museum, which features a replica of a 19th-century rural village, complete with typical houses, a church, and a traditional mill.

Natural Attractions of Criciúma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque das Nações Cincinato Naspolini: This expansive park is perfect for getting some fresh air and exercise, with plenty of sports facilities, playgrounds, and walking trails, plus a stunning lake that’s ideal for boating or fishing.
  • Morro da Cruz: For panoramic views of Criciúma and the surrounding countryside, take a hike up this hill, which is also home to a beautiful chapel.
  • Parque Municipal dos Imigrantes: This nature reserve boasts acres of untouched forest and grassland, as well as a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, birds, and reptiles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Criciúma

Criciúma is a paradise for enthusiasts of outdoor activities, with a plethora of hiking and biking trails that showcase the city’s stunning natural scenery. Here are some popular routes worth exploring:

  • Trilha do Rio Maina: This 5 km trail winds along the banks of the Maina River, offering gorgeous views of the surrounding forests and meadows. It’s suitable for hikers of all skill levels, and there are plenty of spots to stop and rest along the way.
  • Ciclovia do Carvão: This 25 km bike path follows the route of a historic railway that once transported coal all the way to the coast. It takes cyclists through several beautiful parks and green spaces, as well as scenic neighborhoods and industrial sites.

Best Things to Do in Criciúma: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with a group, or as a family, Criciúma has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the city’s most exciting activities and tours:

  • Parque das Águas: This water park features thrilling slides and pools for all ages, as well as a large picnic area and a variety of food stands. It’s a great choice for a fun day out with friends or family.
  • Passeio de Maria Fumaça: Take a leisurely train ride through the beautiful countryside surrounding Criciúma, complete with charming historical settings and live music performances.
  • City Tour: If you’re short on time or want to get an overview of the city’s main landmarks, consider booking a guided city tour. You’ll be able to see all the main sights and attractions while learning about the city’s fascinating history and culture.

If you’re lucky enough to spend a whole week in Criciúma, there’s plenty to keep you entertained. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Criciúma

No trip to Criciúma would be complete without experiencing the local food and drink scene, which is known for its fusion of Italian, German, and Brazilian flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Escondidinho de Carne Seca: This savory dish features shredded beef and mashed potatoes, baked to perfection with cheese and herbs.
  • Bolo de Aipim: A classic Brazilian dish, this cake is made with cassava flour and coconut milk, and is often served with a side of meat or fish.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: This popular Brazilian spirit is made from sugar cane and can be enjoyed straight or mixed into cocktails.
  • Chimarrão: A traditional South American drink, this herbal tea is often consumed through a metal straw and shared among friends.

What to Buy in Criciúma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring back from your trip? Criciúma has no shortage of unique and locally made products, including:

  • Wine: Criciúma is a rising star in the Brazilian wine industry, with many local vineyards producing high-quality red and white wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Criciúma

To fully appreciate your visit to Criciúma, it’s important to understand and respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some key cultural insights to be aware of:

  • Religion: The predominant religion in Criciúma is Catholicism, and many of the city’s landmarks and festivals have religious significance.
  • Music and Dance: Traditional Brazilian music and dance, such as samba and forró, are an important part of local culture, and visitors should try to experience them firsthand.
  • Hospitality: Hospitality is highly valued in Criciúma, and visitors can expect to be treated with warmth and generosity.

Best Time to Visit Criciúma

Criciúma enjoys a mild climate year-round, with warm summers and cool winters. However, the best time to visit is during the spring (September to November) or fall (March to May), when the weather is most pleasant and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Criciúma

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese: Although many people in Criciúma speak English, it’s always appreciated when visitors make an effort to communicate in the local language.
  • Respect local customs and traditions: Whether it’s dressing appropriately for religious sites or leaving a small tip at restaurants, taking the time to learn and observe local customs will show respect and appreciation for the local culture.
  • Stay safe: While Criciúma is generally a safe city for visitors, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone or in dark or deserted areas at night.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: Stick to bottled water to avoid any potential health risks.
  • Be disrespectful: Avoid making negative comments about the local culture or customs, as this can be seen as rude or offensive.
  • Flash valuable items: To avoid drawing unnecessary attention or becoming a target for theft, keep expensive jewelry or electronics out of sight.

Safety and Health

While Criciúma is considered a safe destination for visitors, it’s always important to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and well-being. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Criciúma

  • Keep an eye on personal belongings: Pickpocketing and theft can occur in crowded areas, so keep a close watch on your valuables and avoid carrying large sums of cash or expensive jewelry.
  • Observe traffic laws: When walking or driving, be aware of traffic patterns and road conditions, as accidents can occur.
  • Stay alert at night: While there are many safe areas for walking or dining at night, it’s always wise to exercise caution and avoid dark or deserted areas.

Health in Criciúma

  • Drink bottled water: To avoid stomach illness or other health risks, it’s best to stick to bottled water for drinking and brushing teeth.
  • Get vaccinated: Prior to your trip, be sure to consult with your healthcare provider about any necessary vaccinations or precautions to take.
