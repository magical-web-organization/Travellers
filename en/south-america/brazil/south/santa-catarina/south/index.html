Introduction to South

Nestled in the heart of Santa Catarina, in Brazil, South is a charming region that offers a plethora of tourist attractions, ranging from rich cultural heritage to breathtaking natural scenery. This region holds a special place in the hearts of Brazilian natives due to its vibrant history and picturesque landscapes.

From idyllic beaches to towering mountains, South has something for every type of traveler. Visitors can gaze in awe at the stunning views, go on thrilling hikes, and bike through the picturesque countryside. The region is also known for its vibrant culture and rich history, making it an ideal destination for history buffs, art lovers, and culture enthusiasts.

This travel guide will help you explore the best of South so that you can make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in South

One of the main reasons why South is such a popular tourist destination is its diverse range of attractions. Here is a comprehensive list of places you should visit when you’re in South:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Woven with a rich history and vibrant culture, South offers several museums and historical places that are worth visiting. Some must-visit places include the Nereu Ramos Museum, the Casa da Fazenda do Palácio Cruz e Sousa, and the Mirante do Morro da Cruz.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South’s natural beauty is one of the region’s main attractions. Visitors will be awestruck by the stunning landscapes, idyllic beaches, and towering mountains. Some of the most breathtaking natural attractions in South include Praia Mole, Lagoa do Peri, and Trilha Parque das Dunas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

South is also a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several trails and routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include the Morro da Cruz Trail, the Trilha Parque das Dunas, and the Lagoa do Peri Trail.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

From thrilling adventures to cultural expeditions, South offers plenty of things to see and do. Exploring the vibrant culture of this region, going on adventurous hikes, and relaxing on idyllic beaches are some of the best activities to enjoy while in South.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

For those interested in experiencing everything South has to offer, a week-long itinerary is a great way to see the best of the region. Start by exploring the regional culture, then move on to hiking and biking adventures, then finally unwind on one of South’s picturesque beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South is also known for its vibrant food and drink scene, which offers delicious local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular dishes in South include bolinhos de aipim, caranguejada, and lagosta. Additionally, the region is famous for its seafood, which can be sampled in dishes like camarão na moranga, peixe na telha, and arroz de polvo.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, South is famous for its local brews, such as chimarrão, caipirinha, and brahma. These drinks are an integral part of the region’s culture and are a must-try for any visitor.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular hotels in the region include Pousada Pedra da Ilha, Mar de Canasvieiras Hotel & Eventos, and Costão do Santinho Resort.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that represent South’s rich culture and history. Some popular items include handmade pottery, traditional lacework, and local handcrafted jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of South

South has a vibrant culture that is a blend of Portuguese and African influence. Visitors can explore this unique culture by attending traditional dances and music events, dining on local cuisine, and visiting historically significant landmarks.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is during the summer months, between December and February, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, it’s worth noting that South can be visited throughout the year, and each season offers its own unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases
  • Try traditional local cuisine
  • Respect the local customs and culture

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas
  • Take unmarked taxis
  • Leave valuables visible in parked cars

Safety and Health

Safety in South

In general, South is a safe region to visit. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash, staying in well-lit areas at night, and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in South

Visitors to South should also take some health precautions, such as dressing appropriately for the weather, staying hydrated, and getting vaccinated before traveling (if necessary). It’s also best to drink bottled water and avoid street food to minimize the risk of food poisoning.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

South is easily accessible by plane, bus, and car. Visitors can reach South by flying into Florianópolis International Airport, which is located just a short distance from South. Guests can also take a bus from nearby cities such as Curitiba and Porto Alegre.

Airports Near South

  • Florianópolis International Airport

Bus Stations Near South

  • Terminal São Carlos

Other Options

  • Car rental
  • Private shuttle service

How to Get Around in South

While in South, visitors can get around by car, rent a scooter, or bike. Taxis and bus services are also available, although schedules may vary.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South’s location also makes it an ideal starting point for exploring other nearby destinations. Some popular excursion options include Ilha do Campeche, Parque Unipraias, and Blumenau.

Conclusion

South is a dream destination for anyone who is looking to explore the best of Brazil’s stunning landscapes and vibrant culture. This comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your visit, from exploring the region’s rich cultural heritage to hiking its picturesque trails and tasting its delicious local cuisine. So get ready to pack your bags, and experience the beauty and charm of South, Santa Catarina.
