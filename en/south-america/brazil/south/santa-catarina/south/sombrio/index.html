Introduction to Sombrio

Located in the southern region of Santa Catarina, Brazil, Sombrio is a hidden gem with stunning beaches and breathtaking landscapes. Despite being less recognized amidst other coastal cities in Brazil, this charming town has plenty to offer its visitors. With miles of pristine sandy beaches, beautiful natural scenery, rich history and authentic local culture, it’s the perfect destination for those seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of busy tourist destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Sombrio

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu Histórico de Sombrio: Explore the rich cultural heritage of Sombrio with a visit to this museum, which houses an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits chronicling the town’s history.
  • Igreja Matriz São Pedro Apóstolo: Built in 1922, this historic church is a towering example of classic Brazilian architecture. Take in the intricate details of its design, and marvel at the beautiful stained-glass windows within.
  • Marco Geodésico: Climb the hill to the east of Sombrio beach and admire the panoramic view from the top. Here you’ll find a geodesic landmark, a stone structure that once served as a reference point for the region.

Natural Attractions of Sombrio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia da Gaivota: This tranquil and less crowded beach is perfect for those who want to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature. It’s a good spot for surfing, kitesurfing and windsurfing too.
  • Morro dos Conventos: A unique hill with a high cliff facing the sea, Morro dos Conventos is a popular spot for paragliding and hang gliding. It also offers a beautiful view of the beaches.
  • Refúgio da Vida Silvestre Costão da Ararauna: A protected wildlife reserve covering an area of 1,723 hectares, this park is home to endangered species such as the red-tailed parrot, the jaguarondi, and the ocelot. Take a guided tour to explore its beauty, or hike the trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sombrio

  • Trilha do Morro da Cruz: A popular trail that can be done on foot or by bike, the Morro da Cruz trail stretches for about 6 kilometers and features steep ramps and challenging terrain. But at the top, the view of Sombrio and the surrounding areas is extraordinary.
  • Trilha do Costão da Ararauna: This trail is a moderate and easy hike that leads to the Costão da Ararauna, the beautiful coastal reserve. The trail is approximately 4 kilometers long and takes around 1.5 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Sombrio: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: Sombrio has some of the best waves for beginner to intermediate surfers. Local surf schools offer lessons and equipment rental.
  • Hang Gliding and Paragliding: Take a tandem flight or sign up for lessons and soar over the beautiful landscape of Sombrio.
  • Beach Hopping: Sombrio boasts of several stunning beaches, and each has its unique charm. Rent a bike or car and go on a beach-hopping adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Sombrio

Sombrio is a paradise for seafood lovers and offers delicious local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Camarão na Moranga: A delightful dish where prawns are cooked in a creamy pumpkin sauce.
  • Moqueca de Peixe: A classic fish stew with spices and vegetables.
  • Barreado: A traditional dish made with beef, cooked slowly for several hours.

Local Beverages

  • Chimarrão: A traditional hot beverage made from an infusion of yerba mate.
  • Caipirinha: Brazil’s national cocktail made with cachaca, sugar, and lime.

Where to Stay in Sombrio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Sombrio, catering to a range of budgets and preferences.

  • Pousada dos Pireneus - Located near Gaivota beach, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a fantastic breakfast buffet.
  • Hotel Maré - A budget friendly option, this hotel is located near the city center and within walking distance of the beach.
  • Vila Eco Park - A great choice if you’re looking for some R&R, Vila Eco Park is a stunning eco-friendly resort that’s surrounded by nature and perfect for those seeking a peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Sombrio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cachaça: A distilled spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice, it’s the most famous alcoholic beverage in Brazil.
  • Handicrafts: The local artisans make several handicrafts, including pottery, wood carvings, and woven baskets.
  • Souvenir beach towels and t-shirts: A great way to remember your time in Sombrio.

Local Culture and Customs of Sombrio

The people of Sombrio are friendly and welcoming. The culture of Sombrio is heavily influenced by its rich history and the customs of its indigenous people. Some must-know cultural customs include:

  • Respect for elders and authority figures.
  • Dining customs: Always use utensils and observe basic table manners.
  • Appropriate Dress: When visiting churches and other cultural sites, it’s advisable to dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Sombrio

The best time to visit Sombrio is between December to March when the weather is warm and sunny, and the possibility of rain is lower. Avoid visiting during the winter months (June-August) as the weather can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sombrio

Do:

  • Try local food and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs.
  • Learn some Portuguese expressions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear flashy jewelry or belongings.
  • Disturb the wildlife and natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sombrio

Sombrio is generally a safe town, but it’s advisable to take precautions when exploring natural attractions.

  • Carry small bills and avoid showing large amounts of cash.
  • Stay in well-lit and well-traveled areas.
  • Keep your valuables safe at all times.

Health in Sombrio

  • Hydrate often and use sunscreen.
  • Mosquitoes can be a problem in some areas, so use mosquito repellent.
  • Visitors from outside Brazil must get vaccinated for yellow fever and bring their vaccination card.

Getting to Sombrio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sombrio

  • Hercílio Luz International Airport in Florianopolis, the capital city of Santa Catarina, is the closest international airport. It’s approximately 150 km away from Sombrio.

Bus Stations Near Sombrio

  • There are several options for bus services from Florianopolis and other nearby cities. It’s recommended to book tickets online in advance.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a popular option for those who want to explore the area at their pace.

How to Get Around in Sombrio

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the city center. It’s recommended to agree on the fare before getting into the taxi.

  • Bicycle: Rent a bike and explore the beaches and trails.

  • Car Rental: Car rental options are available, and it’s convenient when exploring the region.

Where to Go Next from Sombrio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Praia do Rosa: A beautiful beach town located 70 km from Sombrio. It’s popular for its surfing and beach bars.
  • Garopaba: About one and a half hours from Sombrio, this charming town is a popular tourist destination for its beaches and nightlife.
  • Florianopolis: The capital city of Santa Catarina is a three-hour drive from Sombrio. It’s one of the best beach destinations in Brazil, known for its relaxed vibe, beautiful beaches, and lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Sombrio is a hidden gem located in the southern region of Brazil, and there’s so much to explore and experience in this charming town. From its beautiful beaches to rich cultural heritage, Sombrio has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can now plan your trip, stay safe, and make the most of your time in this beautiful destination.
