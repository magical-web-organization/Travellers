Introduction to Lages

Nestled in the heart of Serrana, in the state of Santa Catarina, Lages is a charming city that is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. The city is situated amidst the stunning Serra do Rio do Rastro mountain range, offering visitors plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. It’s a great place to visit for those looking to experience the best of Brazil’s stunning landscapes and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Lages

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museu Thiago de Castro - This impressive museum is a must-visit destination in Lages, showcasing the history and culture of the city and the region of Serrana. The museum’s exhibits include a range of artifacts, artworks, and historical documents that tell the story of Lages and its people.
  2. Catedral Diocesana - Built in the mid-20th century, this beautiful cathedral is a stunning example of modernist architecture. The cathedral’s soaring ceiling and unique design make it a popular spot for visitors to Lages.
  3. Palácio da Cruz e Sousa - This historical building was once the home of the governor of Santa Catarina and has now been converted into a museum showcasing the region’s history and culture. Visitors can explore the museum’s exhibits and learn about the area’s unique architecture and art.

Natural Attractions of Lages: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Morro da Cruz - This popular hiking spot offers visitors stunning views of Lages and the surrounding area. It’s a great place to go to catch the sunset or just to enjoy a scenic walk.
  2. Serra do Rio do Rastro - This mountain range is an iconic landmark in the region and is home to some of Brazil’s most stunning scenery. Visitors can explore the range by hiking, biking, or taking a scenic drive through the mountains.
  3. Parque Jonas Ramos - This park is a popular destination for families and outdoor enthusiasts. It features an impressive playground, sports fields, and picnic areas, as well as trails for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lages

  1. Rota das Águas - This hiking trail takes visitors through the beautiful Serra do Rio do Rastro mountain range, passing by stunning waterfalls and rivers along the way.
  2. Caminho dos Ciclistas - This biking route takes visitors through the heart of Lages, passing by the city’s most popular landmarks and cultural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Lages: Activities and Tours

  1. Horseback riding - This is a popular activity for visitors to Lages, with several local companies offering guided tours through the city’s stunning countryside.
  2. Cultural tours - Visitors interested in learning more about the city’s history and culture can sign up for a cultural tour that takes them to the city’s most important landmarks and museums.
  3. Scenic drives - One of the best ways to see the stunning scenery of the Serra do Rio do Rastro is by taking a scenic drive through the mountain range.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lages

Day 1: Explore the city’s historical landmarks and museums. Day 2: Take a scenic drive through the Serra do Rio do Rastro and explore the area’s stunning natural beauty. Day 3: Visit Parque Jonas Ramos and enjoy a day of outdoor activities and a picnic lunch. Day 4: Go horseback riding through the stunning countryside surrounding Lages. Day 5: Take a guided cultural tour and learn more about the area’s unique history and culture. Day 6: Hike along the Rota das Águas trail, passing by stunning waterfalls and rivers along the way. Day 7: Relax and soak up the local atmosphere at one of Lages’ charming cafes or restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Lages

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Churrasco - This traditional Brazilian barbecue is a must-try for meat lovers. It typically includes a variety of grilled meats, such as beef, pork, and chicken, and is served with a range of sides, including rice, beans, and salad.
  2. Brigadeiro - This popular Brazilian dessert is a sweet and gooey chocolate treat that is perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  1. Caipirinha - This classic Brazilian cocktail is made with cachaça, lime, and sugar, and is a refreshing drink that is perfect for sipping on a hot day.
  2. Chimarrão - This traditional tea is a popular drink in the region and is made with yerba mate leaves, hot water, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Lages: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Planalto Hotel - This centrally located hotel offers comfortable rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and a range of amenities, including a fitness center and a pool.
  2. Estalagem Villa da Montanha - This charming guesthouse is situated in the stunning countryside surrounding Lages, offering visitors a peaceful place to relax and unwind.
  3. Hostel Bojuru - This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers on a budget, offering shared and private rooms in a cozy and welcoming setting.

What to Buy in Lages: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade crafts - Visitors to Lages will find a range of handmade crafts, including beautiful ceramics, textiles, and hand-carved wooden items.
  2. Local food products - Visitors can also bring home a taste of Lages by picking up some of the region’s delicious food products, such as locally produced honey and artisanal cheeses.

Local Culture and Customs of Lages

Visitors to Lages should be aware of the region’s proud cultural heritage and should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Many of the city’s residents are passionate about the area’s history and culture and will be happy to share their knowledge and insights with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Lages

The best time to visit Lages is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most stunning. Visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers, especially during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lages

Do:

  1. Do explore the stunning natural beauty of the Serra do Rio do Rastro.
  2. Do try the regional dishes, such as churrasco.
  3. Do be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  2. Don’t disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  3. Don’t leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lages

Lages is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take the usual precautions to keep their belongings safe and should be aware of their surroundings when exploring the city.

Health in Lages

Visitors to Lages should be up-to-date on all their routine vaccines and should take precautions to protect themselves from mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Zika and Dengue fever.

Getting to Lages: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lages

The nearest airport to Lages is the Hercílio Luz International Airport in Florianópolis, which is located about 250 kilometers from Lages.

Bus Stations Near Lages

Lages has a modern and well-connected bus station that serves routes to a variety of destinations throughout the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to explore Lages and the surrounding area on their own.

How to Get Around in Lages

Visitors to Lages can get around the city by taxi, bus, or rental car.

Where to Go Next from Lages: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Florianópolis - This vibrant seaside city is home to stunning beaches, delicious seafood restaurants, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  2. Balneário Camboriú - This popular seaside resort town is a great place to go for sun, sand, and surf, as well as top-notch shopping and dining.

Conclusion

Lages is a charming and fascinating city that is well worth a visit for those looking to experience the best of Brazil’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery. With a range of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local charm, Lages is a destination that is sure to delight visitors of all ages and interests.
