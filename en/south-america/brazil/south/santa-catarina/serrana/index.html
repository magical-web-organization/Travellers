Introduction to Serrana

Located in the southern region of Brazil, in the state of Santa Catarina, Serrana is a charming city that draws visitors from all over the world. Known for its lush green surroundings, rich history, and vibrant culture, Serrana offers a plethora of opportunities to explore and enjoy.

This comprehensive guide will take you on a journey through all the must-see attractions and activities that Serrana has to offer. From natural wonders to cultural landmarks, we give you the lowdown on where to go, what to see and do, where to eat and stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Serrana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Serrana is has a rich history that can be explored through its museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Museu Histórico e Cultural Jordão - This museum, located in the heart of Serrana, is dedicated to the preservation and promotion of the city’s cultural heritage. It houses a vast collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs that showcase the region’s history and traditions.
  • Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceição - This beautiful church built in the 18th century, is a stunning example of Baroque architecture. It is one of the oldest and most iconic landmarks of the city.
  • Casa da Cultura Estácio de Sá - This cultural centre features exhibitions, performances, and workshops that celebrate the city’s artistic and musical heritage. It is a great place to learn about the local culture and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Serrana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Serrana is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, with mountains, forests, lakes, and rivers providing endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Serra do Rio do Rastro - This stunning mountain range offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking and trekking. It is also home to the famous Serra do Rio do Rastro Road, which is considered one of the top scenic drives in the world.
  • Parque Aquático Cascata Véu de Noiva - This water park is a family-friendly destination that offers fun-filled activities such as water slides, wave pool, and paddleboats, set against the backdrop of a beautiful waterfall.
  • Rio Novo Adventure - This adventure tour service provides exciting activities like zip-lining, rapelling, and tree climbing, giving visitors an adrenaline rush while enjoying the gorgeous natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Serrana

Serrana has many hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the beautiful natural surroundings of the city. Here are some of the best options:

  • Trilha do Morro da Igreja - This hiking trail takes you through the stunning Serra do Rio do Rastro mountain range, offering spectacular views of the surrounding landscape and the opportunity to observe rare birds and other wildlife.
  • Trilha do Mirante - This relatively easy hiking trail takes you to a scenic lookout point from where you can enjoy panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Caminho das Águas - This cycling route takes you through the countryside, passing by swimming holes, waterfalls, and stunning landscapes. It’s a great way to enjoy the natural beauty of Serrana while getting some exercise.

Best Things to Do in Serrana: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top-rated activities and tours you should not miss while in Serrana:

  • Helicopter Tour of Serrana - See the city from a bird’s eye view on a thrilling helicopter ride that takes you over the mountains, forests, and waterfalls of Serrana.
  • Lagoa da Cidade Tour - Enjoy stunning views of Lagoa da Cidade, one of the region’s most beautiful lakes, and explore the surrounding forest on a thrilling hike.
  • Serra do Rio do Rastro Tour - Take a scenic drive on the famous Serra do Rio do Rastro Road, stopping at various viewpoints to soak in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Serrana

Here’s a week-long itinerary that covers some of the best things to see and do in Serrana:

  • Day 1: Visit Museu Histórico e Cultural Jordão, and nearby Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora da Conceição.
  • Day 2: Explore Serra do Rio do Rastro by car, stop at various viewpoints, and trek the Trilha do Morro da Igreja.
  • Day 3: Visit Parque Aquático Cascata Véu de Noiva, and enjoy the water slides and wave pools.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of Lagoa da Cidade and explore the nearby forest on a hike.
  • Day 5: Hire a bike and cycle along the Caminho das Águas trail.
  • Day 6: Take a helicopter tour of the city, soak in its beauty from above.
  • Day 7: Complete your trip with a Rio Novo Adventure tour. Take on some intense adventure activities like zip-lining, rapelling, and tree climbing.

Food and Drink Scene of Serrana

Must-Try Dishes

Serrana has an array of culinary treats that are worth trying. Here are some dishes you cannot miss:

  • Bife a Parmegiana - This is a popular dish consisting of breaded steak, tomato sauce, and melted cheese served with rice and fries.
  • Pastel de Palmito - A savoury pastry filled with palm hearts and cheese, this dish is a popular snack in Serrana and a must-try for all visitors.
  • Churrasco - A Brazilian barbecue style featuring juicy cuts of meat that are cooked on a skewer, served with black beans, rice, and farofa.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça - This distilled spirit is made from sugar cane and is the national drink of Brazil.
  • Caipirinha - A refreshing cocktail made with cachaça, lime, and sugar.
  • Guaraná - A popular soft drink in Brazil, made from the guarana fruit.

Where to Stay in Serrana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Serrana has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, catering to all types of travellers and budgets. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Pousada Vale Do Sol - A charming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms surrounded by lush green landscapes.
  • Hotel Internacional Serrana - Located in the heart of the city, this hotel provides easy access to all major tourist attractions, shops, and restaurants.
  • Hostel Vila Nova - A budget-friendly hostel located near Parque Aquático Cascata Véu de Noiva. It offers dorm-style and private rooms and has a shared kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Serrana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the local products and souvenirs you can bring back home from Serrana:

  • Cachaça - A bottle of the famous Brazilian distilled spirit is a great gift to bring back home and savour whenever you want.
  • Artifact Crafts - Handmade tiles, and plates, as well as other ceramic and wooden crafts, make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Jewelry And Accessories - The city has many shops selling unique jewelry and accessories, made using local materials like feathers and shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Serrana

Serrana has a rich cultural heritage that can be seen in its museums, historical sites, and vibrant festivals. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are always welcomed with warm smiles and open arms. The city’s culture is a blend of indigenous, African, and European customs, which are reflected in its music, dance, food, and lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Serrana

The best time to visit Serrana is from May to September when the weather is dry and mild, and the skies are clear. The summer months from December to February can be hot and humid, while the winters from June to August can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Serrana

Do:

  • Explore the Natural Wonders - Serrana has much to offer in terms of natural beauty, and it’s worth exploring to the fullest.
  • Try the Local Cuisine - Brazilian food is a must-try, and Serrana has some local dishes that are unique and delicious.
  • Respect Local Customs - The locals have a rich heritage and customs that must be respected.

Don’t:

  • Litter or Pollute - The city takes pride in its natural beauty, and visitors are expected to help keep it clean.
  • Disregard Safety Measures - Tourists should always be cautious when travelling in unfamiliar places, and Serrana is no exception.
  • Forget to Bargain - Many street vendors and small shops offer fair prices, and tourists will be expected to bargain to arrive at a fair price.

Safety and Health

Safety in Serrana

Serrana is a relatively safe city, but tourists should always exercise caution and take necessary safety measures such as not carrying large amounts of cash, being vigilant in crowded areas, and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Serrana

Serrana is considered a low-risk area for infectious diseases. Still, visitors should take necessary precautions such as using mosquito repellent and using sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Serrana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Serrana

Serrana is located approximately 98 kilometres from Hercílio Luz International Airport in Florianópolis, which is the main international airport in the region.

Bus Stations Near Serrana

The city has a well-connected network of bus stations that cater to various major cities in the region, including Florianópolis, and São Paulo.

Other Options

Taxis and restricted-area vehicles can be used to travel around the city. It is also possible to rent cars for longer trips around the area.

How to Get Around in Serrana

The best ways to get around in Serrana are hiking, biking, or driving. Many attractions are a few hours’ drive from the city centre, and public transport is limited. Local buses, taxis, and rented cars are a feasible option.

Where to Go Next from Serrana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some popular nearby destinations to visit:

  • Florianópolis - A lively beach town, Florianópolis is just a few hours from Serrana and provides the perfect beach vacation.
  • Gramado - A charming mountain town known for its quaint architecture, stunning landscapes, and European charm that is perfect for a romantic holiday.

Conclusion

Serrana is a beautiful city that offers a unique mix of culture, history, natural beauty, and adventure. It’s a place where travellers can escape from the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in the natural beauty and friendly atmosphere. We hope that this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in Brazil.
