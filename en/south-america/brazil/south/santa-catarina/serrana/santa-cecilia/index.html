Introduction to Santa Cecília

Located in the picturesque southern state of Santa Catarina, Santa Cecília is a charming town in Brazil that boasts of natural wonders and beautiful landscapes. The town is nestled in the Serrana region, and it is an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure, culture, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Cecília

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for fun experiences, Santa Cecília has plenty of attractions to offer. Here’s our shortlist of the must-visit places in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Saint Cecilia Museum: Discover the history and culture of Santa Cecília through this museum, which is dedicated to Saint Cecilia - the town’s patron saint.

  • Our Lady of Lourdes Chapel: A charming religious landmark that features breathtaking architecture and impressive stained-glass windows.

  • The Fontana Palace: A magnificent mansion that has been renovated to serve as the City Hall of Santa Cecília. The palace features peculiar architecture, with a facade that resembles a castle.

Natural Attractions of Santa Cecília: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Morro do Escoteiro: A breathtaking hilltop that offers panoramic views of Santa Cecília and the surrounding countryside. It is an ideal spot for hiking, picnics, and birdwatching.

  • Ferrovia do Trigo: A hike through the old railway track provides an opportunity to appreciate the beauty of the Serrana region, with stunning views of the mountains and forests along the way.

  • Cachoeira do Ouro: A picturesque waterfall that cascades into a refreshing pool, perfect for swimming on a hot day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Cecília

Santa Cecília has a wealth of hiking and biking trails that offer a perfect way to soak up the stunning scenery and enjoy the fresh air. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Ferrovia do Trigo: This old railway track offers a unique hiking experience with spectacular views and terrain.

  • Caminho das Águas: A 5 km walk from Santa Cecília to the Ouro Waterfall, passing through the beautiful landscapes of Santa Catarina.

  • Rota das Cachoeiras: A popular trail that takes hikers and bikers through seven waterfalls that are spread over 4 km round trip from Santa Cecília.

Best Things to Do in Santa Cecília: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking routes, Santa Cecília offers various activities and tours that are suitable for solo travelers, families, and groups. Some of the must-try activities to do are:

  • Jeep Tours: A fun-filled adventure through the rugged terrains of Serrana. The tours take you through lush forests, mountains, and picturesque rural villages.

  • Zipline Adventure: Get your adrenaline pumping by zip-lining Santa Cecília’s beautiful natural scenery.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the tranquil countryside of Santa Cecília in a unique way, on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Cecília

If you’re looking for an immersive experience to explore Santa Cecília in a week, here’s a recommended itinerary to make the most of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Cecília

In Santa Cecília, food is an essential part of the culture and daily life. The town is renowned for its flavorful dishes and tasty local beverages. Here are some of the foods and drinks you should try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Churrasco: A Brazilian barbecue, Churrasco is a must-try dish in Santa Cecília. The town is famous for its various cuts of meats, including beef, pork, and chicken, authentically barbecued to perfection.

  • Sopa de Capeletti: A traditional Italian soup is a popular dish in Santa Cecília. It’s a warm and tasty soup with meat-filled dumplings.

  • Barreado: Another traditional dish of Paraná state, made of beef, bacon, onions, tomatoes, and manioc flour, which is slowly cooked for up to 24 hours.

Local Beverages

  • Cachaça: A popular Brazilian spirit made from fermented sugarcane juice is produced regionally and is often used in cocktails.

  • Mate Tea: Mate tea is a traditional drink in Brazil, typically served in a gourd and sipped through a straw called a bombilla.

  • Coffee: Brazilian coffee is world-renowned for its bold flavor and is a staple of daily life in Santa Cecília.

Where to Stay in Santa Cecília: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Cecília has a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some suggestions:

  • Pousada A Estação: A cozy guesthouse located at the historical railway station, with comfortable rooms and a lovely garden.

  • Hotel Santa Cecília: A charming hotel with modern amenities, located near the town center, offering excellent service, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

  • Camping Santa Cecília: If you prefer a unique outdoor experience, Camping Santa Cecília provides tents with all needed equipment and facilities.

What to Buy in Santa Cecília: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Santa Cecília is complete without taking a piece of the town back home. Here are some of the unique souvenirs and local products you should buy during your visit:

  • Cachaça: A perfect gift for anyone who loves spirits and cocktails, and a chance to experience a piece of Santa Cecília at home.

  • Crafts: The best place to purchase local crafts is at the craft fair, where artisans from the region gather to sell unique and beautiful handicrafts made of wood, leather, and other local materials.

  • Mate Tea sets: Mate tea sets make for a unique and practical souvenir, and you’ll be able to enjoy this Brazilian beverage at home just like the locals do.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Cecília

Santa Cecília’s culture is a blend of Brazilian, German, and Italian customs due to the European influence on the region. The town’s residents celebrate annual religious festivals such as the Festa de Santa Cecília and the Semana Santa.

Best Time to Visit Santa Cecília

The best time to visit Santa Cecília is between April and June and September to November when the weather is mild and the tourist season is not at its peak. Summer months can be hot and crowded, and the winter months can be chilly and drizzly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Cecília

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take part in outdoor activities and embrace the natural beauty of the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities
  • Disrespect religious monuments and festivals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Cecília

Santa Cecília is a generally safe place for tourists. However, it’s always a good idea to be careful of your belongings and keep an eye on your surroundings.

Health in Santa Cecília

Before embarking on your trip to Santa Cecília, ensure that you have the necessary vaccinations as per recommendations by your health provider. The town has standard medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies.

Getting to Santa Cecília: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Cecília

The closest airports to Santa Cecília are Lages Airport, which is 70 km from the town, and Hercílio Luz International Airport, located 198 km away in Florianópolis

Bus Stations Near Santa Cecília

Santa Cecília is also accessible by bus, with a few bus companies providing regular services to/from other cities in the region.

Other Options

The town is also reachable by car. A rented car is the most flexible option for traveling around Santa Cecília.

How to Get Around in Santa Cecília

The best way to explore Santa Cecília is on foot, and the town is compact, making it easy to navigate. You might consider renting a car, bike, or motorcycle if you want to venture further out of town.

Where to Go Next from Santa Cecília: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have the time, and want to explore more of the surrounding areas near Santa Cecília, here are some recommendations:

  • São Joaquim: A charming town located in the region of Serrana, São Joaquim is famous for its wine and apple production.

  • Urubici: A scenic town with stunning landscapes and adventurous activities, including rock climbing, rapel, and trekking.

  • Serra do Rio do Rastro: A beautiful scenic drive through the mountains, with breathtaking views of the surrounding valley.

Conclusion

With its blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities, Santa Cecília is an excellent destination to explore and experience the beauty and simplicity of life in southern Brazil. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to plan a trip to Santa Cecília soon.
