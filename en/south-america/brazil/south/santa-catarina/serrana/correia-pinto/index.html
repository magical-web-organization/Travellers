Introduction to Correia Pinto

Correia Pinto is a charming town situated in the Serrana region of Santa Catarina, Brazil. This peaceful and welcoming town is surrounded by breathtaking landscapes that comprise mountains and green valleys. The town is named after Manoel Antonio Correia Pinto, a Brazilian politician, and it has a population of nearly 15,000 people.

Correia Pinto boasts a rich culture and history that is reflected in its museums, historical sites, and traditional festivals. The town is a popular destination for adventurers and nature lovers as well. Tourists who visit Correia Pinto are amazed by its natural beauty and the warm hospitality of the locals.

List of Places to Visit in Correia Pinto

Correia Pinto offers a wide range of attractions that cater to all interests and tastes. From historical landmarks to outdoor adventures, here is a list of places to visit in Correia Pinto:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Paróquia São Sebastião: This beautiful Catholic church, which stands in the center of Correia Pinto, is well-known for its remarkable architecture and stunning stained glass windows.
  • Matriz Sonora Museum: The Matriz Sonora Museum is a cultural center in the town that displays the rich and diverse musical heritage of Brazil and Santa Catarina region. It’s a perfect spot to learn about the local music culture.
  • Municipal Museum: Municipal Museum is a small museum where you can learn about the history of Correia Pinto town. It is home to many historical artifacts, antiques, and photographs that depict the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Correia Pinto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Morro das Antenas: This is one of the highest peaks in the region, which offers spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. It’s a great spot for hiking, mountain biking, and paragliding.
  • Salto dos Marianos Park: Located near the center of the town, this park is a great place to go for a quick picnic and a swim in the refreshing waterfall.
  • Mirante do Lajeado Park: This park offers stunning views of the town and the nearby Lajeado mountains. It’s a perfect spot for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Correia Pinto

Correia Pinto is a fantastic destination for hikers, bikers, and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Correia Pinto:

  • Cânion da Ronda Trail: This is a challenging hike that offers breathtaking views of the Correia Pinto Valley.
  • Antenas Trail: This trail takes you through the beautiful Morro das Antenas hills and offers gorgeous panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Lavandário Trail: This trail takes you to the famous Lavandário farm, where you can see fields of beautiful lavender flowers.

Best Things to Do in Correia Pinto: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the natural attractions of the town, Correia Pinto offers a range of exciting activities to its visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Correia Pinto:

  • Paragliding: The town offers a stunning landscape and perfect weather conditions for paragliding. If you wish to have an unforgettable view of Correia Pinto, consider going paragliding.
  • Horseback riding: Horseback riding is another popular activity in Correia Pinto, where you can explore the town’s beautiful landscapes while riding a horse.
  • Jeep tours: If you’d like an off-road adventure, you can take a jeep tour through the nearby mountains and valleys.

Week-Long Itinerary for Correia Pinto

If you are planning to visit Correia Pinto and want to experience the best of what the town has to offer, here is a week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Municipal Museum and Paróquia São Sebastião Church.

Day 2: Take a hike to Cânion da Ronda Trail and see the beautiful Correia Pinto Valley.

Day 3: Visit Salto dos Marianos Park and enjoy a refreshing swim in the waterfall.

Day 4: Visit the Matriz Sonora Museum and learn about the local music culture.

Day 5: Go horseback riding through the beautiful landscapes.

Day 6: Take a jeep tour through the nearby mountains and valleys.

Day 7: Visit Mirante do Lajeado Park and explore the stunning views of Correia Pinto town.

Food and Drink Scene of Correia Pinto

Correia Pinto has a rich cuisine that reflects Brazilian and Portuguese flavors. The town offers some of the best restaurants and cafes that serve delicious and traditional dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages of Correia Pinto:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frango com polenta (chicken with polenta)
  • Arroz carreteiro (rice with beef)
  • Feijão preto com farofa (black beans with farofa)

Local Beverages

  • Chopp (draught beer)
  • Caipirinha (a popular Brazilian cocktail made with cachaca, sugar, and lime)

What to Buy in Correia Pinto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some popular things to buy in Correia Pinto:

  • Handmade crafts: The town boasts many talented artists who make handmade crafts, such as pottery, embroidery, and paintings.
  • Local honey: Correia Pinto produces delicious honey, which you can buy directly from the local farmers.

Local Culture and Customs of Correia Pinto

Correia Pinto has a rich cultural heritage that reflects in its traditional festivals, music, and cuisine. The town hosts several festivals throughout the year, such as the Lavender Festival, Festa do Divino Espírito Santo, and Correia Pinto Giants Parade. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and they take pride in their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Correia Pinto

The best time to visit Correia Pinto is from March to November when the weather is mild and dry. The town experiences heavy rainfall from December to February, which may affect outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Correia Pinto

Don’t:

Getting to Correia Pinto: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you are planning to visit Correia Pinto, here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Correia Pinto

The nearest airport to Correia Pinto is Hercílio Luz International Airport in Florianópolis, which is approximately a 2.5-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Correia Pinto

The nearest bus station is located in Lages, which is approximately 20 km from Correia Pinto. From there, you can take a taxi or a local bus to reach the town.

Other Options

You can also drive to Correia Pinto or hire a private car from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Correia Pinto

Correia Pinto town is small and easy to navigate. You can explore the town on foot or rent a bike to go around.

Where to Go Next from Correia Pinto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
