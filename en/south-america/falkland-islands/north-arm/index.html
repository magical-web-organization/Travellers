Introduction to North Arm

North Arm is a hidden gem in the Falkland Islands, a British Overseas Territory situated in South America. It is a small settlement of less than 20 inhabitants located on the southeastern coast of East Falkland. North Arm is known for its tranquility, beautiful landscapes, and rich history. It is an ideal destination for travelers who want to get off the beaten path and immerse themselves in nature and local culture.

List of Places to Visit in North Arm

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North Arm is home to fascinating historical sites and museums that tell the story of the Falkland Islands’ rich cultural heritage. The major tourist attractions in this category includes:

  • Green Hill Fort: This historical site is a five-minute drive from North Arm. It was built in 1861 and played a crucial role in the Falklands War. Today, it is a popular tourist spot that offers panoramic views of the coastline.

  • Falklands Islands Museum and National Trust: This museum is located in Stanley, the capital of Falkland Islands. It provides a comprehensive overview of the islands’ history and culture, with exhibits that range from photographs and artifacts to documents and maps.

Natural Attractions of North Arm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North Arm is blessed with breathtaking natural beauty that makes it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. Whether you’re interested in hiking or scenic drives, you’ll find plenty of options in this category.

  • Bertha’s Beach: This beautiful white sand beach is a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. It offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding hills.

  • Kidney Island: This small uninhabited island is a natural reserve, and it is located a short distance from North Arm. Visitors can observe a variety of sea birds and marine mammals, including penguins, seals, and dolphins.

  • Mount Tumbledown: This is a popular hiking trail that leads to the summit of Mount Tumbledown, which is the site of a crucial engagement of the Falklands War. The hike offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Arm

North Arm is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails that offer stunning views of the landscape. Some of the popular routes in this category include:

  • Ocean View Circuit: This is a moderate four-hour hike that offers panoramic views of the coastline and the rolling hills. The trail starts and ends in North Arm, and it is suitable for hikers of all levels.

  • North Arm Loop: This is a scenic eight-mile bike ride that takes you through the countryside and past several lakes. It is a great way to experience the beauty of the Falkland Islands and get some exercise at the same time.

Best Things to Do in North Arm: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours available in North Arm that cater to all interests and budgets. Some of our top recommendations within this category includes:

  • Fishing: The coastal waters around North Arm offer great fishing opportunities for sea trout, salmon, and other species.

  • Birdwatching: North Arm is home to a variety of bird species, including penguins, albatross, and many more.

  • City Tour: Explore the local area of this small settlement with a city tour, you can absorb its unique history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Arm

A week-long trip to North Arm can be a good way to see the best of what the settlement has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in North Arm; Relax after the journey. Day 2: Hike to the summit of Mount Tumbledown. Day 3: Visit the Falkland Islands Museum and National Trust in Stanley. Day 4: Enjoy a day of fishing or birdwatching. Day 5: Explore Kidney Island. Day 6: Take a scenic drive to Bertha’s Beach. Day 7: Depart North Arm

Food and Drink Scene of North Arm

North Arm’s food and drink scene is relatively small, with only a few places to eat and drink. However, travelers can enjoy some delicious and unique local dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sheep Meat: Falkland Islanders are known for their love of mutton, and it is a staple of the local cuisine.
  • Fish and Chips: This classic British dish is a favorite of many people in North Arm.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Tea is the most popular beverage in North Arm and throughout the Falkland Islands.

Where to Stay in North Arm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Arm is a small settlement with limited accommodation options, but visitors can still find comfortable and affordable places to stay. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Sea View Cottages: These self-contained cottages offer stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding hills. They are located near Bertha’s Beach and the hiking trails, and they are ideal for families or groups of friends.

  • New Haven: This guesthouse offers a warm and friendly welcome to travelers. It is located in Stanley, and it provides comfortable and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in North Arm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products available in North Arm that travelers can bring home as a reminder of their trip. Some of the popular items include:

  • Wool Products: Sheep are the main source of income in Falkland Islands, so wool products are abundant.
  • Handmade Crafts: Locals are famous for crafting beautiful items made of different materials, making it one of the best souvenir choices

Local Culture and Customs of North Arm

North Arm’s culture is heavily influenced by British traditions, but it also reflects the unique history and heritage of the Falkland Islands. Visitors can learn about the strong maritime and farming traditions that permeate the local culture and interact with the friendly locals to learn more.

Best Time to Visit North Arm

The Falkland Islands experience a temperate climate year-round. However, the best time to visit North Arm is during the austral summer (November to March), when the weather is milder so travelers can comfortably explore the beautiful outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Arm

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a camera to capture the stunning landscapes and wildlife.
  • Bring warm clothing, as the weather can change quickly.

Don’t:

  • Cut flowers or disturb the wildlife while exploring the natural habitats.
  • Litter and damage any environment.
  • Leave without experiencing the local culture.

Safety and Health

Falkland Islands is considered a safe travel destination, but visitors should still exercise normal precautions. Medical facilities are limited in North Arm, and travelers are advised to bring their medications. Take extra precautions against hypothermia during the cold Falklandic weather,

Getting to North Arm: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Arm is a remote settlement, and getting there requires some preparation. Here are some options for transportation:

Airports Near North Arm

  • Mount Pleasant Airport: This airport is located about 50 kilometers from North Arm. It has daily flights from Santiago, Chile, and Punta Arenas operated by LATAM Airlines.

Bus Stations Near North Arm

There are no bus stations in North Arm, the closest stations are located in Stanley, which is approximately 35 km away.

Other Options

  • Taxis and Shuttles: Visitors may use rental taxis and shuttles available in Stanley to get to North Arm.

How to Get Around in North Arm

North Arm can be explored comfortably on foot, but taxis and shuttles are available for persons who prefer private transportation. Be sure to carry comfortable and warm clothing during hiking or walking tours as the weather can be unpredictable.

Where to Go Next from North Arm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Falkland Islands is an archipelago of islands with plenty of exciting destinations to explore. Some of the popular day trips and excursions from North Arm includes:

  • Stanley: This is the capital of the Falkland Islands, and it is located only 35 kilometers away from North Arm.

  • Carcass Island: This beautiful island is a birdwatchers paradise, with a rich variety of birdlife.

Conclusion

North Arm is an amazing destination that offers travelers a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage and tranquility with a hint of adventure. By following our travel guide, visitors can explore the best attractions, learn about local culture and customs, and have a once-in-a-lifetime experience. So get ready to immerse yourself in the beauty of North Arm, Falkland Islands.
