Port Howard is a hidden gem in the Falkland Islands that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural wonders. With its stunning landscapes, fascinating museums, and outdoor adventures, it’s no wonder why Port Howard is becoming a popular destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore everything that Port Howard has to offer.

Introduction to Port Howard

Located in the West Falklands, Port Howard is a small settlement with a population of around 40 people. The settlement was established in the late 19th century and has a rich history that reflects the cultural diversity of the Falklands. Today, Port Howard is known for its beautiful landscapes, including sandy beaches, rocky cliffs, and lush grasslands. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, Port Howard has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Port Howard

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Port Howard has a fascinating history that is reflected in its cultural attractions. The Port Howard Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of the Falklands. The museum features exhibits on the early settlers, the Falklands War, and the island’s wildlife. Other cultural attractions include the St. Michael’s Chapel, the Port Howard Lodge, and the Argentine Cemetery, a reminder of the historic conflict over the islands.

Natural Attractions of Port Howard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Port Howard’s natural beauty is breathtaking, with stunning landscapes and fantastic wildlife. The Daisy Pool is a popular natural swimming hole that is perfect for a refreshing dip on a hot day. Visitors can take guided wildlife tours to see the islands’ wildlife, including sea lions, dolphins, and penguins. For those seeking adventure, the hills and mountains surrounding Port Howard offer fantastic hiking and biking opportunities. The views from the top are worth the effort.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Howard

Port Howard’s hills and mountains offer endless opportunities for hiking and biking. The most popular route is the Mount Maria hike, which offers stunning views of the Port Howard settlement and the surrounding mountains. Other popular hikes include the Lively Peak hike, the Hooker’s Mountain hike, and the Cape Dolphin hike. Biking is also a popular activity, with plenty of dirt tracks and roads to explore around the settlement.

Best Things to Do in Port Howard: Activities and Tours

Port Howard is packed with activities and tours that cater to all interests. For wildlife enthusiasts, the Falkland Islands Wildlife Adventure Tour offers a chance to see the islands’ unique wildlife up close. The Port Howard Tour takes visitors on a journey through the settlement’s history and culture. For adventure seekers, Mount Usborne offers fantastic opportunities for climbing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Howard

Day 1: Port Howard Museum and St. Michael’s Chapel Day 2: Daisy Pool and Wildlife Tour Day 3: Mount Maria Hike Day 4: Port Howard Lodge and Argentine Cemetery Day 5: Mount Usborne Climbing Adventure Day 6: Hooker’s Mountain Hike Day 7: Cape Dolphin Hike and Beach Day

Food and Drink Scene of Port Howard

The food scene in Port Howard may be limited, but it offers quality over quantity. The Port Howard Lodge Restaurant is the best place to enjoy a meal. The restaurant serves traditional Falklands cuisine, including lamb and seafood dishes. The lodge also offers a cozy bar stocked with local beer and spirits.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Falklands Lamb Chops
  • Seafood Chowder
  • Rockhopper IPA

Local Beverages

  • Falkland Islands Distillery Gin
  • Falkland Islands Brewery Beer
  • Yerba Mate Tea

Where to Stay in Port Howard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are limited accommodation options in Port Howard, but visitors can still find comfortable and affordable lodging.

  • Port Howard Lodge: The lodge offers comfortable accommodations with stunning views of the settlement.
  • The Shearing Shed: The guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a communal kitchen.
  • Corral Farm Camping: Visitors can set up their tents in the farm’s beautiful pasture.

What to Buy in Port Howard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Port Howard Museum gift shop is the best place to get souvenirs and local products. Visitors can buy items such as Falklands woolen products, sheepskin rugs, and handmade crafts made by local artisans. The gift shop also sells books and maps on the history and culture of the Falklands.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Howard

The people of Port Howard are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including showing respect for the island’s military heritage and wildlife. Visitors are also expected to take care of the environment by following the Leave No Trace principles and not leaving waste behind.

Best Time to Visit Port Howard

The best time to visit Port Howard is from November to February, which is the summer season. During this time, the weather is milder, and the days are longer, offering more time for outdoor activities and exploration. Visitors should also be aware that the weather in the Falklands can be unpredictable, and it’s always best to come prepared with proper clothing and equipment.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Howard

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment by following the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Explore the countryside on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harm the wildlife in the area.
  • Leave waste behind.
  • Ignore the signs or warnings regarding safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Howard

Port Howard is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of accidents while hiking or biking in rough terrain. Visitors are advised to take safety precautions such as carrying a first-aid kit and informing someone of their itinerary before heading out.

Health in Port Howard

There are no major health risks in Port Howard, but visitors should come prepared with any necessary medication and follow basic hygiene practices such as washing hands frequently. Visitors should also be aware of the presence of ticks and take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Port Howard: Transportation Options and Logistics

The only way to get to Port Howard is by car. Visitors can rent a car in Stanley or take a taxi. The drive from Stanley takes around 3 hours and offers stunning views of the Falklands countryside.

Airports Near Port Howard

  • Stanley Airport: 125 km away

Bus Stations Near Port Howard

  • None

Other Options

  • Taxis: Visitors can take a taxi from Stanley to Port Howard.

How to Get Around in Port Howard

The best way to get around Port Howard is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bikes at the Port Howard Lodge or bring their own. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Port Howard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stanley: The capital city of the Falkland Islands is 125 km away from Port Howard and is home to a range of cultural attractions, including the Government House, Christ Church Cathedral, and the Historic Dockyard Museum.
  • Pebble Island: Located to the north of West Falkland, Pebble Island is a nature reserve with stunning landscapes and wildlife.
  • Carcass Island: Known for its abundant birdlife and sandy beaches, Carcass Island is a perfect destination for beach lovers and birdwatchers.

Conclusion

Port Howard is truly a hidden gem in the Falkland Islands and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural wonders. From its stunning landscapes to fascinating museums and outdoor adventures, Port Howard has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and enjoy this off-the-beaten-path destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression.
