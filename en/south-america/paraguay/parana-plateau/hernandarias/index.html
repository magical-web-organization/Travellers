Introduction to Hernandarias

Located in the Paraná Plateau region of Paraguay, Hernandarias offers visitors a mix of cultural and natural attractions. The city is named after Hernando Arias de Saavedra, one of Paraguay’s founders, and is known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, Hernandarias has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Hernandarias

Here is a breakdown of the top attractions in Hernandarias:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Histórico Daniel Cordovés Reyes: A museum dedicated to the history of the city, featuring artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s past.
  • Iglesia San Roque: A beautiful church known for its unique architecture and well-preserved interior design.
  • Casa de la Cultura: A cultural center that features events, exhibits, and workshops related to music, dance, and art.

Natural Attractions of Hernandarias: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cerro Koi: A beautiful hill that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, perfect for hiking or camping.
  • Lago de la República: A large lake known for its clear waters and great fishing opportunities.
  • Parque Ambiental Niterói: A park that offers hiking and biking trails, wildlife watching, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hernandarias

  • Sendero Cerro Koi: A challenging hiking trail that takes you to the top of Cerro Koi.
  • Ruta 2: A scenic biking route that takes you through some of the most picturesque parts of Hernandarias.
  • Sendero Lago de la Republica: A hiking trail that takes you around the shores of Lago de la República.

Best Things to Do in Hernandarias: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding: For the adrenaline junkies, Hernandarias offers paragliding tours that give you a bird’s eye view of the city.
  • Fishing: The lakes and rivers around Hernandarias are home to a variety of fish, making it an excellent spot for fishing enthusiasts.
  • City Tours: Join a guided tour of the city to get an in-depth look at its history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hernandarias

Day 1: Visit Museo Hístorico Daniel Cordovés Reyes, Iglesia San Roque, and Casa de la Cultura. Day 2: Go hiking or camping on Cerro Koi. Day 3: Explore the Parque Ambiental Niterói. Day 4: Enjoy paragliding or visit Lago de la República for some fishing. Day 5: Bike along Ruta 2 and take in the scenic views. Day 6: Take a guided city tour and try some local cuisine. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products to bring home, and relax in one of Hernandarias’ hotels or guesthouses.

Food and Drink Scene of Hernandarias

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chipa: A traditional Paraguayan bread made from cassava starch and cheese.
  • Sopa Paraguaya: A savory cornbread made with onions, cheese, and milk.
  • Bori Bori: A hearty soup made with meatballs and cornmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: A popular herbal tea made from the leaves of the yerba mate plant.
  • Tereré: A refreshing cold drink made from yerba mate, cold water, and herbs.

Where to Stay in Hernandarias: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Casino Acaray: A luxurious hotel with a casino and spa.
  • Posada Bella Vista: A cozy guesthouse with a scenic view of the city.
  • Hostal Gardenia: An affordable hostel located in the city center.

What to Buy in Hernandarias: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ñanduti Lace: A traditional Paraguayan lace made from fine cotton thread.
  • Tereré mug: A cup used for drinking tereré and popular among locals.
  • Chaco sandals: Traditional sandals made from leather and worn by the Guarani people.

Local Culture and Customs of Hernandarias

  • Paraguayans are generally warm and hospitable people.
  • Spanish and Guarani are the most commonly spoken languages.
  • Greetings are an important part of the culture, and it is customary to greet people with a kiss on the cheek.

Best Time to Visit Hernandarias

The best time to visit Hernandarias is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hernandarias

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any natural attractions.
  • Take photos of locals without asking for permission.
  • Make political or religious statements in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hernandarias

Overall, Hernandarias is a safe city for travelers. However, it is still important to take precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Hernandarias

It is recommended to get vaccinated for yellow fever before traveling to Paraguay. It is also important to take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid contracting diseases like dengue fever or Zika virus.

Getting to Hernandarias: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hernandarias

The nearest airport to Hernandarias is Silvio Pettirossi International Airport in Asunción, which is approximately 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Hernandarias

Hernandarias is easily accessible by bus from Asunción and other major cities in Paraguay.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer more flexibility in their transportation options.

How to Get Around in Hernandarias

Taxis and buses are the most common modes of public transportation in Hernandarias. Bikes and scooters can also be rented at certain locations.

Where to Go Next from Hernandarias: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ciudad del Este: A bustling city known for its shopping and proximity to the Itaipu Dam.
  • Iguazu Falls: One of the world’s largest waterfalls, located on the border of Brazil and Argentina.

Conclusion

Hernandarias offers an enticing mix of cultural and natural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Paraguay. From its scenic views and outdoor adventures to its delicious cuisine and warm hospitality, Hernandarias has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of this charming city.
