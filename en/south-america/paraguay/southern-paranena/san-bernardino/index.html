Introduction to San Bernardino

Located in the Southern Paraneña region of Paraguay, San Bernardino is a charming small town known for its refreshing climate, scenic natural beauty, and interesting cultural offerings.

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a peaceful and relaxing getaway, San Bernardino is the perfect destination for you.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you a detailed rundown of the best things to see and do in San Bernardino, as well as provide you with plenty of practical advice and tips for planning your trip.

So let’s dive in and explore all that this fascinating Paraguayan town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in San Bernardino

San Bernardino is a small town, but there are plenty of attractions and places to visit that will keep you entertained during your stay. Here are some of the must-see places in San Bernardino:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Bernardino is rich in history and culture, and its museums and historical sites are a testament to its fascinating past. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit:

  • Centro Cultural Juan de Salazar: This cultural center hosts regular exhibitions showcasing the best of Paraguayan art and culture. It also houses a library and a theater.

  • Casa de la Cultura: This colonial-style building was the first residence of the Jesuits in the area. It now serves as a museum where you can learn about the region’s history and culture.

  • Iglesia de San Bernardino: This beautiful neoclassical church dates back to the 19th century and is a must-visit attraction in San Bernardino.

Natural Attractions of San Bernardino: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

San Bernardino is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and biking to birdwatching and kayaking, there’s no shortage of outdoor activities to enjoy in San Bernardino.

Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Lake Ypacarai: This large, freshwater lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, boating, and picnicking.

  • Cerro Kuñí: This hill offers breathtaking views of San Bernardino and the surrounding areas. It’s a great spot for a hike or bike ride.

  • Parque Nacional Ybycuí: This national park is home to a variety of plant and animal species and offers stunning views of waterfalls and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Bernardino

San Bernardino is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and routes to explore. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, there’s a route for everyone.

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in San Bernardino:

  • Cerro Kuñí Trail: This 3.5-kilometer trail leads to the top of Cerro Kuñí and offers panoramic views of San Bernardino and the surrounding areas.

  • Parque Nacional Ybycuí Trails: The national park has several hiking trails that wind through forests and lead to stunning waterfalls and scenic viewpoints.

  • Cycling Route to Lake Ypacarai: This 20-kilometer bike route takes you through the countryside, passing small towns and farms, and ends at the beautiful Lake Ypacarai.

Best Things to Do in San Bernardino: Activities and Tours

San Bernardino offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the top things to do in San Bernardino:

  • Visit the Beach: The beach at Lake Ypacarai is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.

  • Enjoy Water Sports: Rent a kayak or paddleboat and explore the lake, or go fishing and try your luck at catching some local fish species.

  • Take a Cultural Tour: Explore the town’s history and culture by taking a guided tour of its museums, historical sites, and churches.

If you’re planning to stay in San Bernardino for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time in this beautiful town:

Food and Drink Scene of San Bernardino

San Bernardino offers a variety of delicious food and drink options that reflect the region’s rich gastronomic heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sopa Paraguaya: A savory cake made with cornmeal, cheese, and onions.

  • Chipa: A type of bread made with cheese and cassava starch.

  • Asado: A traditional barbecue dish made with grilled meats and sausages.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: A traditional Paraguayan tea made with yerba mate leaves and hot water. It’s a popular social drink and is often shared among friends and family.

  • Tereré: A cold infusion of yerba mate leaves, often mixed with fruit juice or herbs.

Where to Stay in San Bernardino: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Bernardino offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, there’s something for everyone.

Here are some of the top places to stay in San Bernardino:

  • Hotel Del Lago: A luxurious hotel overlooking Lake Ypacarai, offering rooms with lake views, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant.

  • Posada Del Sauce: A charming guesthouse that offers cozy rooms, a swimming pool, and a beautiful garden.

  • Hostal Primavera: A budget-friendly hostel located in the center of town, offering dormitory-style rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in San Bernardino: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Bernardino offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or mementos. Here are some of the best things to buy in San Bernardino:

  • Cheese: San Bernardino is known for its delicious cheese, made from cow or sheep milk.

  • Ñandutí Lace: A traditional Paraguayan craft, Ñandutí is a spiderweb-like lace that is used to make clothing, tablecloths, and other decorative items.

  • Tereré Set: Bring home a traditional tereré set, which includes a gourd, a metal straw, and a thermos flask for carrying hot water.

Local Culture and Customs of San Bernardino

San Bernardino’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in Paraguayan tradition and history. One of the most important cultural events in San Bernardino is the Feast of the Virgen de la Candelaria, which is celebrated in early February with colorful processions, live music, and traditional dances.

Another important aspect of San Bernardino’s culture is its traditional music and dance. The polka and guarania are two of the most popular types of music in Paraguay, and you can often hear them playing in San Bernardino’s cafes and restaurants.

Best Time to Visit San Bernardino

The best time to visit San Bernardino is between June and September, during the dry season when temperatures are mild and pleasant. However, if you don’t mind some rain, you can visit any time of year, as the town’s natural beauty and cultural offerings are worth seeing regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Bernardino

Here are some important Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting San Bernardino:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before you arrive, as many locals don’t speak English.

  • Tip your servers in restaurants and cafes, as this is a common practice in Paraguay.

  • Take the time to explore the town’s history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, as it may not be safe for consumption. Stick to bottled water and other sealed beverages.

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or other religious sites, as this may be considered disrespectful.

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions, such as not participating in the March of the Candelaria or taking photos without permission.

Safety and Health

San Bernardino is a safe town with low levels of crime. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables hidden and avoiding areas that are known to be unsafe at night.

In terms of health, San Bernardino has medical facilities that can provide basic care. However, if you need specialized medical attention, you may need to travel to a larger city nearby.

Getting to San Bernardino: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Bernardino is accessible by several means of transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near San Bernardino

The nearest airport to San Bernardino is Silvio Pettirossi International Airport in Asunción, Paraguay’s capital city. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to San Bernardino.

Bus Stations Near San Bernardino

The town has its own bus station, where you can catch buses to nearby cities and towns. Some of the top bus companies that operate in the area include La Santaniana, Flota Guaraní, and La Yutyrá.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a private driver to take you to San Bernardino.

How to Get Around in San Bernardino

The best way to get around San Bernardino is on foot or by bicycle. The town is small and easy to navigate, and there are plenty of bike rental shops if you want to explore on two wheels.

Taxis and buses are also available, but they may be less convenient for short trips around town.

Where to Go Next from San Bernardino: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Paraguay, there are several other destinations and excursions you can take from San Bernardino. Here are some ideas:

  • Asunción: Paraguay’s capital city is just a short bus or car ride away and offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions.

  • Areguá: This colonial-style town is known for its pottery and handicrafts and makes for a great day trip from San Bernardino.

  • Itaipu Dam: This impressive hydroelectric dam is located on the border between Paraguay and Brazil and is one of the largest dams in the world.

Conclusion

San Bernardino is a hidden gem in Paraguay, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re seeking a relaxing getaway or an action-packed trip, San Bernardino has something for every type of traveler.

With this comprehensive travel guide, you now have all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to San Bernardino. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready to explore this fascinating Paraguayan town.
