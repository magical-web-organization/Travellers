Introduction to Belén

Belén is a small town located in the Northern Paraneña region of Paraguay, South America. Situated along the Jacaré river, Belén is known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage. The town is home to friendly locals who are proud of their history and traditions. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in the charming town of Belén.

List of Places to Visit in Belén

Here are the top places to visit in Belén:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of Nuestra Señora de la Asunción: A beautiful church with a rich history dating back to the 18th century.
  • Museum of Indigenous Arts: A museum showcasing the art and culture of the indigenous people of Paraguay.
  • Historical Museum of Belén: A museum that tells the story of Belén’s history and culture through exhibits and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Belén: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salto Suizo: A stunning waterfall located in a lush forest, perfect for hiking and picnicking.
  • Cerro Guaity: A mountain that offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding areas.
  • Jacaré River: A beautiful river that runs through the town and is perfect for fishing and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belén

Belén has several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the town and surrounding areas. Popular routes include:

  • Cerro Guaity Trail: A challenging hike that takes you to the summit of the mountain.
  • Salto Suizo Trail: A moderate hike that takes you through a lush forest to the waterfall.
  • Jacaré River Trail: A leisurely walk that follows the river and offers scenic views of the town.

Best Things to Do in Belén: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Belén:

  • Kayaking on the Jacaré River: Rent a kayak and explore the beautiful river.
  • Fishing on the Jacaré River: Spend a relaxing afternoon fishing for the local fish species.
  • Indigenous Arts and Crafts Tour: Learn about the art and culture of the local indigenous people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belén

Day 1: Arrive in Belén and take a leisurely walk along the Jacaré River. Day 2: Hike to Salto Suizo waterfall and have a picnic in the forest. Day 3: Visit the Church of Nuestra Señora de la Asunción and explore the town center. Day 4: Take a kayaking tour on the Jacaré River or go fishing. Day 5: Go on a tour of the indigenous arts and crafts and visit the Museum of Indigenous Arts. Day 6: Hike to the summit of Cerro Guaity and enjoy the panoramic views of the town. Day 7: Visit the Historical Museum of Belén and learn about the town’s rich history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Belén

Belén has a variety of local dishes and beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chipa: A type of cheese bread made with manioc flour and anise seeds.
  • Sopa paraguaya: A dense cornbread made with cheese and onions.
  • Locro: A hearty stew made with corn, beef, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: A traditional herbal tea made with yerba mate leaves.
  • Tereré: A cold version of mate that is popular in the hot summer months.
  • Caña: A sugarcane spirit that is a popular local drink.

Where to Stay in Belén: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belén has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  • Hotel Jacaré: A comfortable hotel in the town center with views of the river.
  • Hostal Cerro Guaity: A budget-friendly hostel located at the base of Cerro Guaity mountain.
  • Posada del Museo: A guesthouse located near the Historical Museum of Belén.

What to Buy in Belén: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belén has several local products and souvenirs to bring home, including:

  • Handwoven textiles: The local indigenous people create beautiful textiles with intricate designs.
  • Ceramic pottery: The town is known for its traditional pottery, which is available in many stores.
  • Artisanal food products: Local vendors sell homemade jams, honey, and other food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Belén

Belén’s culture is influenced by the indigenous people of the region and the history of Paraguay. The locals are proud of their customs and traditions, and visitors can learn more about them by attending local festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Belén

The best time to visit Belén is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is mild, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belén

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Belén:

Do:

  • Learn about the local culture and customs.
  • Respect the environment and be mindful of waste.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be overly loud or disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Belén is a safe town to visit, but visitors should take precautions, such as:

Safety in Belén

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach issues.

Health in Belén

  • Get vaccinated before visiting.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid dengue fever.
  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated to avoid heatstroke.

Getting to Belén: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the transportation options to get to Belén:

Airports Near Belén

  • Silvio Pettirossi International Airport: The nearest international airport is located in Asunción, around 270 km from Belén.

Bus Stations Near Belén

  • Encarnacion Bus Station: This is the nearest bus station to Belén, located approximately 100 km away.

Other Options

  • Private transfers or rental cars are available from the airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Belén

Belén is a small town, and most places are within walking distance. However, visitors can also ride a bicycle or rent a motorbike to explore the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Belén: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belén is situated in a region with many nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Encarnación: A nearby city with beautiful beaches and cultural landmarks.
  • Jesuit Missions of Paraguay: A series of historical ruins dating back to the 17th and 18th century.
  • Ybycuí National Park: A beautiful national park with trails, waterfalls, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Belén is a charming town with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural attractions. Visitors to Belén can enjoy outdoor adventures, learn about the local culture and customs, and relax in a tranquil and welcoming environment. With this comprehensive tour guide, you now have everything you need to plan your trip to Belén.
