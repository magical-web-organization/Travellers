Introduction to Pedro Juan Caballero

Located in Northern Paraneña, in the northeastern region of Paraguay, is the charming city of Pedro Juan Caballero. Known for its stunning natural attractions, rich local culture, and history, it offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences to visitors. With this comprehensive tour guide, you will discover the best places to visit, best things to do such as hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, local customs, and shops in Pedro Juan Caballero.

List of Places to Visit in Pedro Juan Caballero

There are numerous culturally and naturally rich places to visit in Pedro Juan Caballero. Here is a list of the must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Jesuitic Missions: It tells the history of the Jesuit missions and the indigenous cultures of the surrounding area.

  • Museum of South American Army: History lovers will find this military museum fascinating.

  • Casa de la Cultura: It is a building constructed in the colonial era that acted as the seat of the Governmental Headquarters of the Department of Amambay.

  • Cathedral San Juan Batista: The beautiful Cathedral with Gothic Architecture constructed in the year 1922.

Natural Attractions of Pedro Juan Caballero: Outdoor Adventures, and Scenic Views

  • Ñú Guazu Reserve: Home to the Ñú Guazu Birdlife and wildlife found in the area. It is perfect for nature lovers.

  • Saltos del Monday: A beautiful waterfall adorned with natural vegetation and a breathtaking view that will leave you Spellbound.

  • Estancia Ghalieno: It is a ranch that offers a unique rural experience and horse riding in the beautiful countryside of Paraguay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pedro Juan Caballero

  • Yrenruguazu Bike path: Located in the midst of beautiful landscapes, Yrenruguazu Bike path takes riders through the countryside of Paraguay.

  • Estancia Ghalieno Hiking trail: It is an exciting trail that leads to the hills where hikers will witness the beautiful scenery of the Paraguayan countryside.

  • Salto Suizo trail: It is a hiking trail that ends at Salto Suiza, a beautiful waterfall surrounded by stunning natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Pedro Juan Caballero: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding at Estancia Ghalieno: Horseback riding in the countryside to witness the real Paraguay is a must-have experience.

  • Visit Saltos del Laja: A calming sunset at Saltos de Laja is an excellent plan to end your day in Pedro Juan Caballero.

  • Take a jungle tour: Explore the tropical flora and Fauna of Paraguay’s jungles with a guided tour amidst the pristine natural surroundings of Pedro Juan Caballero.

Food and Drink Scene of Pedro Juan Caballero

Enjoy the best of Paraguayan cuisine and traditional beverages while in Pedro Juan Caballero. Here are the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sopa Paraguaya: A traditional Paraguayan dish made of cornmeal and cheese.

  • Parrillada: A mixed grill of meats, including beef, pork, and chicken.

  • Chipa: A baked, cheese and cornmeal-based snack.

Local Beverages

  • Terere: It is a tea made of yerba mate leaves and cold water.

  • Caña: A sugarcane-based alcoholic drink that is a favorite amongst locals

Where to Stay in Pedro Juan Caballero:

There are several accommodations options in Pedro Juan Caballero to suit guests with varying preferences. From hotels to guesthouses:

  • Hotel Salto Suizo: A stunning hotel with all the modern amenities, located in the rustic hills of Pedro Juan Caballero.

  • Hotel Villa Real: A centrally located hotel with an incredibly relaxed atmosphere for a quiet stay.

  • Estancia Ghalieno: A remote ranch that provides visitors a chance to experience rural life in Latin America.

What to Buy in Pedro Juan Caballero: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring back some local and traditional products as souvenirs from your trip to Pedro Juan Caballero:

  • Chipa: It’s a snack that Paraguayan’s just love and eat all the time.

  • Pottery: Paraguay celebrates its native colors, and hand-painted ceramics make for a wonderful decor at homes.

  • Nanduti Lace: A form of lace making that is popular in the surrounding areas.

Local Culture and Customs of Pedro Juan Caballero

The locals of Pedro Juan Caballero are very welcoming to visitors, and ancient customs and traditions are still prevalent in their daily lives. They celebrate joie de vivre, with a vibrant music and dance scene.

Best Time to Visit Pedro Juan Caballero

The best time of year to visit Pedro Juan Caballero is between September and November. During these months, the weather is relatively mild compared to the summer months, and rainfall is at its lowest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pedro Juan Caballero

Here are the do’s and don’ts to follow while visiting Pedro Juan Caballero:

Do:

  • Dress modestly.

  • Learn simple Spanish phrases to converse with the locals.

  • Accept invitations into local homes or establishments

Don’t:

  • Do not tip in restaurants.

  • Do not haggle in markets and shops.

  • Do not explore remote areas alone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pedro Juan Caballero

Pedro Juan Caballero is a relatively safe city. However, visitors must remain aware of their surroundings and take care of their belongings while in public spaces.

Health in Pedro Juan Caballero

Health risks in Pedro Juan Caballero are minimal in comparison to other parts of South America. Mosquitoes can be a problem in the region, and visitors are advised to use repellent while outdoors.

Getting to Pedro Juan Caballero: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pedro Juan Caballero can be reached by air or land, as it is located near the border of Brazil. The best transportation options and logistics are:

Airports Near Pedro Juan Caballero

The nearest international airport to Pedro Juan Caballero is Guarani International Airport of Asunción, which is around a 5-hour drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Pedro Juan Caballero

Several bus companies operate services that connect the city with neighboring regions and countries. Bus routes range from shorter trips to Asunción to more extended routes to Bolivia and Brazil.

Other Options

Visitors may opt to hire a car or motorbike to explore the surrounding regions of Pedro Juan Caballero.

How to Get Around in Pedro Juan Caballero

Hiring a taxi or motorbike is the best way to get around Pedro Juan Caballero. Public buses are limited, so it’s recommended to confirm the schedule before planning on this option.

Where to Go Next from Pedro Juan Caballero: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few destinations to consider after your visit to Pedro Juan Caballero:

  • Saltos de Monday: A perfect after-stop destination with the magestic waterfall on the Brazilian side of the border.

  • Ciudad del Este: A major city that serves as a gateway to the Iguazu Falls and Itaipu Dam.

  • Pocitos: A small town with an enchanting landscape and freshwater springs.
