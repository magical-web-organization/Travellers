Introduction to San Borja

San Borja is a charming town located in the Beni region in Bolivia, South America. With a population of around 18,000 people, San Borja offers a unique blend of fascinating culture, breathtaking natural beauty, and thrilling outdoor adventures.

Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient museums and historical landmarks, hiking scenic trails and biking routes, trying new food and drink, or simply immersing yourself in the local culture, San Borja has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the top places to visit, best things to do, and insider tips for experiencing the very best of San Borja. From outdoor adventures to cultural insights, we’ve got everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in Bolivia.

List of Places to Visit in San Borja

San Borja is a town full of fascinating places to visit, from culturally important museums and historical landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here are the top places to visit in San Borja:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Borja has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its many museums and historical landmarks. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • Museo Moxos: This fascinating museum showcases the history and culture of the Moxos people, one of the indigenous groups that inhabit the Beni region.
  • Iglesia de San Francisco: This 18th-century church is a beautiful example of colonial architecture and a great place to learn about the history of San Borja and the Catholic Church in Bolivia.
  • Casa de la Cultura: This cultural center hosts a variety of events and exhibitions throughout the year, including traditional dances, music, and art.

Natural Attractions of San Borja: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

San Borja is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Parque Nacional Madidi: This vast national park covers over 7,000 square miles and is home to a diverse range of ecosystems, including rainforest, cloud forest, and grasslands. It’s a great place to go hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife spotting.
  • Cerro San Pablo: This scenic hill offers panoramic views of San Borja and the surrounding countryside. It’s a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Rio Maniqui: This beautiful river is a great place to go kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Borja

San Borja is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of scenic trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in San Borja:

  • Cerro San Pablo Trail: This scenic trail leads to the top of Cerro San Pablo and offers stunning views of San Borja and the surrounding countryside. It’s a moderate hike that takes around 2-3 hours to complete.
  • Rio Maniqui Trail: This beautiful trail follows the banks of the Rio Maniqui and offers great opportunities for wildlife spotting and birdwatching. It’s an easy hike that takes around 1-2 hours to complete.
  • Parque Nacional Madidi Trails: The national park offers a variety of hiking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. The most popular trails include the Chalalan trail, which leads to the Chalalan Ecolodge, and the Bala trail, which follows the Bala River and offers great views of the rainforest.

Best Things to Do in San Borja: Activities and Tours

San Borja offers a plethora of activities and tours for visitors of all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in San Borja:

  • Visit a Moxos Community: Take a tour of a local Moxos community and learn about their culture and way of life. You can also try traditional foods and participate in cultural activities.
  • Go Wildlife Spotting: Take a guided tour of Parque Nacional Madidi and spot exotic wildlife such as jaguars, monkeys, and macaws.
  • Try Local Foods: Sample traditional Bolivian dishes, such as salteñas (baked empanadas) and pique a lo macho (spicy beef dish), at local restaurants and street vendors.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Borja

If you’re planning a week-long trip to San Borja, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museo Moxos and Iglesia de San Francisco, followed by a traditional lunch at a local restaurant.
  • Day 2: Go hiking on the Cerro San Pablo Trail, followed by a picnic lunch.
  • Day 3: Take a guided tour of Parque Nacional Madidi and stay overnight at the Chalalan Ecolodge.
  • Day 4: Go kayaking on the Rio Maniqui and then try some local coffee at a nearby café.
  • Day 5: Visit a Moxos community and participate in cultural activities and traditional food tasting.
  • Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Casa de la Cultura and then spend the afternoon shopping for souvenirs at local markets.
  • Day 7: Go on a wildlife spotting tour in Parque Nacional Madidi and then enjoy a farewell dinner at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of San Borja

Bolivian cuisine is a blend of indigenous and European influences, with a range of unique dishes and flavors. Here’s a guide to the food and drink scene of San Borja:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salteñas: These baked empanadas are a Bolivian staple and come filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Pique a lo Macho: This spicy beef dish is made with boiled potatoes, onions, and chili peppers.
  • Mondongo: This hearty soup is made with beef tripe and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Api: This sweet drink is made from purple corn and spices and is traditionally served with fried pastries called buñuelos.
  • Chicha: This fermented corn drink is a popular beverage throughout Bolivia.
  • Café de Altura: This high-altitude coffee is grown in the Andes and has a rich, bold flavor.

Where to Stay in San Borja: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Borja has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Chalalan Ecolodge: This eco-friendly lodge is located inside Parque Nacional Madidi and offers comfortable rooms, delicious food, and guided tours of the rainforest.
  • Hotel Real Santa Cruz: This modern hotel is located in the heart of San Borja and offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Hostal Tio Luis: This budget-friendly hostel offers simple rooms with shared bathrooms and a communal kitchen for guests to use.

What to Buy in San Borja: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Borja is known for its handicrafts and traditional products, which make great souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in San Borja:

  • Weavings: San Borja is known for its colorful weavings, which come in a variety of patterns and designs.
  • Wood Carvings: Local craftsmen create intricate wood carvings of animals, birds, and other objects.
  • Coffee: Bolivian coffee is known for its high quality and rich flavor. Look for locally-grown coffee beans to bring home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of San Borja

San Borja is a town with a strong cultural identity, where traditional customs and festivals are an important part of daily life. Here are some of the customs and traditions you might encounter:

  • Moxos Culture: The Moxos people are one of the indigenous groups that inhabit the Beni region and have their own language, customs, and traditions.
  • Festivals: San Borja hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, including the Fiesta de San Francisco in September and the Carnaval de San Borja in February.
  • Religion: The Catholic Church plays an important role in the lives of many people in San Borja and is reflected in the town’s religious architecture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit San Borja

The best time to visit San Borja is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is sunny and dry, with temperatures ranging from 20-30°C (68-86°F). The wet season, which runs from November to March, can be hot and humid, with frequent rainfall and muddy trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Borja

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting San Borja:

Do:

  • Respect the Local Customs: San Borja has a strong cultural identity and it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the Local Food: Bolivian cuisine is delicious and unique, so don’t be afraid to try new dishes and flavors.
  • Pack Appropriately: Bring comfortable clothes and shoes for outdoor activities, and don’t forget bug spray and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Environment: San Borja is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, so be sure to dispose of trash properly and avoid damaging the environment.
  • Overpay for Tours: Compare prices and quality before booking tours or guides, as prices can vary widely.
  • Assume English is Spoken Everywhere: While some people in San Borja may speak English, it’s always a good idea to learn some basic Spanish phrases and practice your language skills.

Safety and Health

San Borja is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions to stay healthy and secure. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in San Borja

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: Like any town, San Borja has some areas that can be unsafe at night, so avoid walking alone in dark or isolated areas.
  • Secure Valuables: Keep valuables such as passports, cash, and electronics in a secure place, such as a hotel safe.
  • Be Aware of Scams: Like any tourist destination, San Borja has some scams to watch out for. Be wary of strangers offering unsolicited help or tours.

Health in San Borja

  • Drink Bottled Water: Tap water in San Borja may not be safe for drinking, so stick to bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.
  • Get Vaccinated: Consult your doctor before traveling to San Borja and make sure you’re up-to-date on any necessary vaccinations.
  • Bring Insect Repellent: The rainforest environment can attract mosquitoes and other insects, so be sure to pack insect repellent and long-sleeved clothing.

Getting to San Borja: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Borja is located about 400 km from La Paz and 600 km from Santa Cruz, making it somewhat remote. Here are the best ways to get to San Borja:

Airports Near San Borja

  • San Borja Airport (SRJ): This small airport offers flights to and from La Paz and Rurrenabaque.

Bus Stations Near San Borja

  • Terminal de Buses San Borja: This bus station offers frequent buses to and from La Paz, Santa Cruz, and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Private Transportation: Some tour companies offer private transportation to and from San Borja.

How to Get Around in San Borja

San Borja is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and mototaxis are also available for transportation within the town or to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from San Borja: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Borja is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular options:

  • Rurrenabaque: This charming town is located about 3 hours from San Borja and is a popular gateway to the Amazon Rainforest.
  • Parque Nacional Noel Kempff: This vast national park is located about 500 km from San Borja and is home to an array of wildlife and ecosystems.
  • La Paz: Bolivia’s capital city is located about 400 km from San Borja and offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions.

Conclusion

San Borja is a hidden gem in Bolivia, offering a unique blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re interested in visiting museums, hiking scenic trails, trying new foods, or simply immersing yourself in local culture, San Borja has something for everyone.

We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan a trip to San Borja, and that it has provided you with all the information you need to make the most of your visit. Happy travels!
