Introduction to San Ignacio de Moxos

San Ignacio de Moxos is a small town located in the Beni region of Bolivia. Known for its unique blend of indigenous and colonial heritage, San Ignacio de Moxos offers visitors an opportunity to experience a rich culture set against the backdrop of stunning natural beauty. The town is home to a number of heritage buildings, unique customs, and traditions. Whether you’re interested in exploring the local culture or taking on an outdoor adventure, you’ll find plenty to love about San Ignacio de Moxos.

List of Places to Visit in San Ignacio de Moxos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Municipal Museum: This museum is the best place to learn about the town’s history, art, and culture. It features exhibits on local indigenous art and traditions, as well as colonial-era artifacts.
  • Main Square: San Ignacio de Moxos’ main square is one of the town’s most important historical sites. It is dominated by the impressive colonial-era church, and is also home to other important buildings including the municipal hall and the former Jesuit College.
  • Moxeño Art Museum: This museum offers a unique look at the local Moxeño art style. While the displays are limited, visitors will enjoy the opportunity to learn about this distinctive style that has been passed down for generations.

Natural Attractions of San Ignacio de Moxos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Llanos de Moxos: The surrounding area of San Ignacio de Moxos is famous for its Llanos, or plain, which are a vast, flat, tropical lowlands. Here visitors can see an array of birdlife and other wild animals in the savannah landscape.
  • Cotoca Quenual: A short trip from town is Cotoca Quenual, an expansive forest preserve. Visitors can explore the park on foot or bike, and will discover an array of fauna and flora, including several mammal and bird species listed as threatened or vulnerable.
  • San Ignacio de Moxos River: This river is a place to go for fishing, kayaking, or an easy afternoon paddle in a beautiful setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Ignacio de Moxos

Hiking Routes

  • Lomas de Arena: This is a protected area containing a series of massive sand dunes and isolated streams, roughly half an hour from San Ignacio de Moxos. Varying levels of difficulty make this route ideal for the beginner and advanced hiking enthusiasts alike.
  • Moxos Hill: An easy route starting in San Ignacio town will lead you to Moxos Hill. From the top of Moxos Hill, you will get a panoramic view of the surrounding terrain and savannah landscape for as far as the eye can see.

Biking Routes

  • Gran Chaco: This route follows the main road around San Ignacio and heads out to the Gran Chaco wilderness. The route is strenuous and is best tackled by experienced riders. This route features beautiful landscapes, fresh air and an opportunity to experience nature from a unique perspective.

Best Things to Do in San Ignacio de Moxos: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Town Center: San Ignacio de Moxos’ town center offers a glimpse into the area’s unique colonial and indigenous history. Wander around the main square, visit the local church and interact with locals.
  • Attend the Corso Festival: An annual carnival not to be missed. This carnival is held in honor of the Virgin of Carmen and features colorfully dressed dancers parading through the streets of town. Come if you love a good party.
  • Go Fishing: Fishing is an activity which has become very popular in this region because of the many rivers, lakes, and lagoons found here. Join the locals to catch your dinner or just sit on the banks enjoying the views.
  • Take a Kayaking Tour: A kayaking tour is an excellent way to take in the natural beauty of the San Ignacio de Moxos River.

Food and Drink Scene of San Ignacio de Moxos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pacumanca: A traditional dish featuring meat and vegetables cooked underground for a delicious smoky flavor.
  • Fanesca: A soup made with a variety of local Andean grains, nuts, and dairy. Usually served during Holy Week.
  • Charque: Beef jerky-style meat that is seasoned and sun-dried. It is also used as an ingredient in many stews and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Mate de Coca: A tea made from the leaves of the coca plant. It is a popular drink across Bolivia and has long been known for its medicinal properties.
  • Singani: A Bolivian brandy often served as an aperitif that has a subtle grape flavor.
  • Chicha: A fermented drink made from maize, which is common throughout South America.

Where to Stay in San Ignacio de Moxos: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Los Jardines de San Ignacio: A beautiful eco-hotel which offers an intimate and unique experience, including its own butterfly farm, and is located in the heart of San Ignacio de Moxos.
  • Hotel Anaconda: A popular budget option located in a prime location next to the main square, and offers free bike rental.
  • Hostal El Pueblo: A centrally located guesthouse which offers single rooms for budget-friendly travelers.

What to Buy in San Ignacio de Moxos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Moxeño Art: An array of paintings and crafts can be found throughout the town’s markets, the perfect souvenir to remember your trip.
  • Cocoa and Coffee: San Ignacio de Moxos’ coffee and cocoa are world-famous, and coffee beans are sold by many of the local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of San Ignacio de Moxos

San Ignacio de Moxos is home to a unique blend of indigenous and colonial culture. Visitors will find a friendly and welcoming community which takes pride in its history and traditions. It’s common to encounter traditional celebrations and special events in town throughout the year, dress appropriately, and always be respectful and ask permission before taking anyone’s photo. Join in the celebrations to get the best experience.

Best Time to Visit San Ignacio de Moxos

The best time to visit San Ignacio de Moxos is between December to May. This period falls within Bolivia’s dry season and provides the best weather conditions reflected in the blue skies, no clouds, and temperature averages of 30° Celsius daytime. Crowds get dense during the carnival month of February, so plan according to avoid the busiest time. June to September is the wet season, which means the risk of Interruptions and flooding which makes transportation uncertain and treks out of the cities very difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Ignacio de Moxos

Do:

  • Learn a few Spanish words and phrases. The majority of people in San Ignacio de Moxos speak Spanish or Quechua, the local indigenous language.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take waterproof gear as the weather can change quickly, even during the dry season.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water. Drinking bottled water is recommended.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment. Keep the Llanos de Moxos clean.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Ignacio de Moxos

San Ignacio de Moxos is generally considered to be a safe place to visit. That said, like anywhere else in the world, travelers should always be aware of their surroundings and take precautions. Pickpocketing can be a problem, so be mindful of your belongings. It’s best to avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas.

Health in San Ignacio de Moxos

It is important to take appropriate precautionary measures when visiting San Ignacio de Moxos. At present, there are no vaccination requirements for entering Bolivia, however, travelers should consult their travel physician for their personal requirements. Always take sun protection, and mosquito repellent, as mosquitos are present in large numbers. Drinking bottled water and ensuring that food is well-cooked is highly recommended.

Getting to San Ignacio de Moxos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Ignacio de Moxos

  • Jorge Wilstermann Airport: The closest international airport is Jorge Wilstermann, located 270 miles away in Cochabamba.

Bus Stations Near San Ignacio de Moxos

  • El Alto La Paz: The closest bus terminal is El Alto La Paz which is 430 miles away from San Ignacio.

Other Options

  • Driving: You can rent a car in nearby cities and drive to San Ignacio de Moxos. The road from La Paz runs down from the Alto de La Paz through the rugged high plains and eventually drops down into the Amazon Basin.
  • Taxis and Private Shuttles: If you prefer not to drive yourself, taxis and private shuttles are available from nearby cities

How to Get Around in San Ignacio de Moxos

The majority of travelers prefer to walk, hitch rides or rent bikes to get around the town. The town is small and the streets are easy to navigate. Taxis are available if you need to go to areas outside town.

Where to Go Next from San Ignacio de Moxos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Ignacio de Moxos is a great starting point for exploring other regions of Bolivia. Some nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Rurrenabaque: Head down to Rurrenabaque, a nearby town considered as the gateway to Bolivia’s Amazon basin, with flora and fauna diversity and amazing views.
  • La Paz: Bolivia’s capital city is a great place to visit for a couple of days and takes just under 14 hours of driving to arrive there from San Ignacio.
  • Lake Titicaca: Explore the highest navigable lake in the world, which takes about 16 hours of driving to get there from San Ignacio.

Conclusion

San Ignacio de Moxos offers a unique combination of indigenous heritage and natural beauty, making it a great destination for travelers interested in experiencing Bolivia’s rich culture and history. Whether you’re exploring the town center, taking in the natural beauty, or simply relaxing with some local cuisine, you’re sure to find plenty to love about San Ignacio de Moxos. Be sure to take the necessary precautions and immerse yourself in the culture to enjoy a unique experience in this charming, small Bolivian town.
