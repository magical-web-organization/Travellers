Oruro in Bolivia is a culturally rich city with a unique blend of indigenous and Spanish influences. Known for its annual Carnival celebration, the city offers a variety of attractions and experiences that are perfect for those seeking adventure, history, and culture. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve outlined everything you need to know to plan your trip to Oruro, including the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, safety tips, and more.

Introduction to Oruro

Nestled in the Altiplano region of Bolivia, Oruro is a city with a rich history and culture. Founded by the Spanish in the 16th century, the city was later inhabited by the Aymara and Quechua indigenous groups. Today, Oruro is known for its vibrant Carnival celebration, which showcases the rich cultural heritage of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Oruro

Are you wondering where to start your exploration of Oruro? Check out this list of must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Nacional Antropológico y Arqueológico: This museum is dedicated to preserving Bolivia’s rich cultural heritage, with exhibits on indigenous culture, archeology, and anthropology.

  • Church of Our Lady of La Candelaria: Built in the 18th century, this stunning church is a popular pilgrimage site for Catholics and a great example of colonial architecture.

  • Carnival Museum: Learn about the history of the city’s Carnival celebration and see colorful costumes and floats on display at this museum.

Natural Attractions of Oruro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sajama National Park: This national park is home to the highest peak in Bolivia and includes stunning landscapes, hot springs, and wildlife.

  • Lake Poopó: This large saline lake is a great spot for bird watching and fishing, with over 90 species of birds and several fish species.

  • Eduardo Avaroa Andean Fauna National Reserve: This reserve is known for its stunning landscapes, geysers, and hot springs, as well as its diverse wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oruro

Oruro has plenty of options for hiking and biking enthusiasts:

  • Tunupa Volcano: This mountain is a popular hiking spot, with trails that lead through stunning landscapes and to ancient ruins.

  • Cañón del Inca: This canyon offers breathtaking views and is perfect for hiking or mountain biking.

  • Triathlon Route: Oruro hosts an annual triathlon that includes running, cycling, and swimming and offers a challenging route through the city and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Oruro: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for exciting things to do in Oruro, try some of these activities and tours:

  • Take a walking tour of the city to learn more about its history and culture.

  • Take part in the annual Carnival celebration, which takes place in February or March and is a colorful and lively celebration of the city’s culture.

  • Visit an indigenous community to learn about their way of life and participate in traditional activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oruro

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Oruro, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Oruro

Oruro’s food and drink scene is full of traditional dishes, drinks, and snacks. Here are some must-try options:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cuñape: These are savory bites made from cassava starch, cheese, and eggs and are a popular snack throughout Bolivia.

  • Silpancho: A traditional Bolivian dish made from fried rice, beef, and potatoes topped with a fried egg and served with a salad.

  • Pique Macho: This hearty dish is a mix of beef, sausage, and potatoes topped with a variety of sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Singani: A grape-based liquor that is produced in Bolivia and is often mixed with soda or other mixers.

  • Chicha: A traditional fermented drink made from maize and often flavored with fruits or spices.

  • Api: A warm, sweetened drink made from purple corn and often served with cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Oruro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oruro offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget:

  • Hotel Eden: This family-run hotel is conveniently located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  • Hostal Santa Monica: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and is a great option for backpackers and solo travelers.

  • Hotel Edelweiss: This mid-range hotel offers spacious rooms and suites and is a great option for families and groups.

What to Buy in Oruro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for authentic souvenirs and gifts to bring home, try some of these local products:

  • Alpaca wool clothing: Oruro is known for its high-quality alpaca wool products, including sweaters, shawls, and hats.

  • Musical instruments: The city is famous for its brass bands and you can buy traditional instruments like charangos and zampoñas.

  • Handmade crafts: You can find a variety of handmade crafts like textiles, pottery, and jewelry in the city’s markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Oruro

Oruro’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in its indigenous and Spanish heritage. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Respectful behavior: Bolivians are known for their polite and respectful behavior, so it’s important to be courteous and polite in your interactions with locals.

  • Dress code: Traditional dress is often worn during special events and celebrations, so it’s a good idea to dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing.

  • Religious practices: Religion plays an important role in Oruro’s culture and customs, so it’s important to be respectful when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Oruro

The best time to visit Oruro is during the annual Carnival celebration, which takes place in February or March. However, if you’re looking to avoid crowds and high prices, visit the city during the dry season from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oruro

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Oruro:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Carry small bills and change for purchases.

  • Be cautious when using public transportation.

Don’t:

  • Engage in political discussions with strangers.

  • Take photos without permission.

  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Oruro is generally a safe and healthy city to visit, but it’s important to take precautions like the following:

Safety in Oruro

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables close and be aware of your surroundings.

  • Use licensed taxi services.

Health in Oruro

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

  • Be aware of altitude sickness, which can occur due to the city’s high elevation.

  • Pack sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.

Getting to Oruro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oruro is accessible by road and air, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Oruro

  • Juan Mendoza Airport: This airport is located in Oruro and offers domestic flights to and from other Bolivian cities.

Bus Stations Near Oruro

  • Terminal de Buses: This large bus station offers daily services to cities throughout Bolivia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

  • Train: Oruro is a major railway hub, with connections to La Paz and other cities in Bolivia.

How to Get Around in Oruro

Oruro is a relatively small city, so it’s easy to get around on foot or by taxi. If you’re planning to explore the surrounding area, consider renting a car or taking a tour.

Where to Go Next from Oruro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations to consider visiting:

  • La Paz: Bolivia’s capital city is just a few hours away from Oruro and is known for its stunning landscape, rich cultural heritage, and bustling markets.

  • Uyuni: This small town is the gateway to the famous Uyuni Salt Flats, which are a must-visit destination in Bolivia.

  • Potosí: This historic city is known for its silver mines and for being one of the highest cities in the world.

Conclusion

Oruro is a city full of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Bolivia. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to plan your trip, explore the must-visit places, and experience the city’s vibrant culture. Don’t forget to try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in Oruro’s unique charm.
