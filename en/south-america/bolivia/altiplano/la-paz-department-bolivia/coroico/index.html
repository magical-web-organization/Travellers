A Comprehensive Guide to Coroico, Bolivia

Nestled in the Altiplano region of Bolivia’s La Paz department, Coroico is a charming town that serves as a gateway to the Yungas region. Known for its stunning natural beauty, lush vegetation, and pleasant climate, Coroico offers visitors a taste of the Andean culture and outdoor adventures. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Coroico, Bolivia.

List of Places to Visit in Coroico

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Pedro Church: Located in the main square of Coroico, this beautiful 18th-century church features a distinctive bell tower and ornate interior.
  • Coroico Cemetery: A visit to the cemetery offers a glimpse into the fascinating Andean culture and its unique burial rituals.
  • Museum of Coroico: The museum houses an extensive collection of pre-Columbian artifacts, colonial art pieces, photographs, and more, showcasing the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Coroico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Las Cascadas Waterfalls: A popular spot for swimming and picnicking, the waterfalls are a series of cascading pools and stunning natural landscapes located within the Zongo Valley.
  • El Camino de la Muerte: Known as the “world’s most dangerous road,” this 40-mile stretch features awe-inspiring views of the Bolivian Andes and is a popular destination for mountain biking and hiking.
  • Jungle Hikes: Explore the lush vegetation and wildlife of the Yungas region by taking guided hikes through the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coroico

  • Choro Trail: This 3-day hike takes visitors from high mountain passes down to the lush vegetation of the Yungas region, passing through remote Andean villages along the way.
  • Downhill Biking: Descend the winding El Camino de la Muerte on a bicycle and experience some of the most spectacular scenery in the Bolivia.

Best Things to Do in Coroico: Activities and Tours

  • Coffee Plantation Tours: Take a tour of the surrounding coffee plantations to learn about the processes involved in growing and harvesting coffee.
  • Rafting on the Coroico River: Join a guided rafting trip down the Coroico River and experience the thrill of adventure surrounded by stunning scenery.
  • Zip Line Tour: Enjoy bird’s eye views of the Yungas region and feel the adrenaline rush on a thrilling zip line tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coroico

Food and Drink Scene of Coroico

Must-Try Dishes

  • Charque de llama: A traditional Andean dish made from dried llama meat, commonly served with rice and potatoes.
  • Picante de pollo: A spicy chicken dish typically served with rice or potatoes.
  • Humintas: A popular street food made from cornmeal and cheese, often served for breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Singani: A grape-based liquor that is used to make Bolivia’s national drink, the chuflay.
  • Chuflay: Made from Singani, ginger ale, and lime juice, this refreshing cocktail is a must-try while in Coroico.

Where to Stay in Coroico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Esmeralda: A charming mountain lodge located in the heart of Coroico town, featuring a swimming pool and a beautiful garden.
  • Senda Verde Wildlife Sanctuary: Located just outside of Coroico, this animal sanctuary offers visitors the opportunity to stay in cabins surrounded by lush jungle.
  • La Senda Verde Hostel: Also located within the wildlife sanctuary, this hostel offers a budget-friendly option for travelers looking to immerse themselves in nature.

What to Buy in Coroico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee: Take home a bag of locally grown and roasted coffee beans.
  • Artisanal Crafts: From traditional Andean textiles to pottery and jewelry, Coroico has plenty of artisanal crafts for visitors to browse and purchase as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Coroico

The people of Coroico speak a unique dialect of Spanish called “yungueño” and are known for their traditional Andean customs, music, and dance. It is common to see women wearing brightly colored shawls and men wearing straw hats while playing sorrowful music using traditional instruments such as the quena or charango.

Best Time to Visit Coroico

The best time to visit Coroico is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is mild and comfortable, and outdoor activities such as hiking and biking are best enjoyed during this time. However, visitors can also enjoy the lush greenery and wildflowers in the rainy season, which runs from October to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coroico

Do:

  • Learn basic Spanish phrases before visiting to improve communication with locals.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Stay hydrated and use plenty of sunscreen when exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or engage in cultural appropriation.
  • Engage in activities that harm the local environment or wildlife.
  • Leave valuables in plain sight or unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coroico

Coroico is generally a safe destination for travelers, although it is recommended to take standard safety precautions such as avoiding dark or secluded areas at night. Petty theft and pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas, so it’s important to keep valuables such as cellphones and wallets secure.

Health in Coroico

Altitude sickness can be a concern for visitors traveling to Coroico, as the town sits at an elevation of almost 5,000 feet above sea level. Visitors are encouraged to take it easy and drink plenty of fluids when first arriving, as well as avoid alcohol and tobacco to help mitigate symptoms.

Getting to Coroico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coroico

The nearest airport to Coroico is El Alto International Airport in La Paz, which is about a 2.5-hour drive from Coroico. Visitors can arrange for a shuttle or taxi service from the airport into town.

Bus Stations Near Coroico

There are several bus companies that offer service to Coroico from La Paz and surrounding towns. Buses typically depart from La Paz’s Bus Terminal and take about 3 hours to reach Coroico.

Other Options

Private car services and guided tours are also available for visitors who prefer not to navigate the winding mountain roads on their own.

How to Get Around in Coroico

While the town of Coroico is small enough to navigate on foot, visitors can use moto-taxis to reach nearby attractions or take guided tours to explore the surrounding landscape.

Where to Go Next from Coroico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • La Paz: Just a short drive away, Bolivia’s capital city offers a wealth of historical and cultural attractions, as well as opportunities for adventure sports and outdoor activities.
  • Lake Titicaca: Located about 5 hours from Coroico, Lake Titicaca is the highest navigable lake in the world and offers stunning views and a unique cultural experience.
  • Uyuni Salt Flats: A 12-hour drive from Coroico, the Uyuni Salt Flats are a breathtaking natural wonder and a must-see destination in Bolivia.

Conclusion

Coroico is a charming town that offers visitors a unique fusion of Andean culture and stunning natural landscapes. From hiking and biking adventures to cultural and historical attractions, there is something for every traveler in this vibrant Bolivian destination. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your visit to Coroico with ease, knowing that you won’t miss a thing.
