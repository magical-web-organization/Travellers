Introduction to La Paz

La Paz, located in the Altiplano region of Bolivia, is known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. The city’s unique location - nestled in a valley surrounded by the Andes Mountains - provides visitors with breathtaking views and a range of outdoor activities. La Paz is also home to numerous museums and historical sites, showcasing its indigenous cultures and colonial past.

List of Places to Visit in La Paz

There are plenty of things to see and do in La Paz! Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Nacional de Arqueología: This national museum offers insight into Bolivia’s pre-Columbian civilizations.
  • Museo Nacional de Arte: Located in a former palace, this museum displays a collection of Bolivian art from the colonial era to the present day.
  • Plaza Murillo: This large central square is surrounded by important buildings such as the Presidential Palace and the National Congress.

Natural Attractions of La Paz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valle de la Luna: These otherworldly rock formations located just outside the city provide a unique hiking experience.
  • Chacaltaya and Huayna Potosí: These two mountains located within an hour’s drive from La Paz offer challenging hikes for experienced trekkers.
  • Teleférico: Take a cable car ride across the city to enjoy stunning aerial views of La Paz and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Paz

  • La Cumbre to Coroico: This route starts in the Andean plains and descends to the tropical Yungas region, offering beautiful scenery and challenging terrain for experienced hikers or bikers.
  • Illimani Base Camp: Hike to this base camp located at over 15,000 feet in the shadow of Bolivia’s second-highest peak.
  • Chacaltaya and Huayna Potosí: These mountains also offer challenging hikes for experienced trekkers.

Best Things to Do in La Paz: Activities and Tours

  • City Walking Tour: Explore the city’s historic center with a knowledgeable guide, learning about its architecture and culture.
  • Mountain Biking: Take a thrilling ride down the ‘Death Road’ - a narrow mountain road that attracts adrenaline junkies from all over the world.
  • Taste of Bolivia Food Tour: Sample local street food and traditional Bolivian dishes on this delicious tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Paz

Day 1-2: Explore the city’s historical center, visiting Plaza Murillo, the Presidential Palace, and the National Congress.

Day 3: Take a cable car ride for amazing aerial views of the city and surrounding landscape.

Day 4-5: Head out of the city to Chacaltaya or Huayna Potosí for a challenging trek while enjoying stunning mountain scenery.

Day 6-7: Journey down to the tropical Yungas region to go on a hike or bike ride from La Cumbre to Coroico.

Food and Drink Scene of La Paz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicharrón: Deep-fried pork served with potatoes, corn, and salad.
  • Salteñas: Bolivian-style empanadas filled with meat or chicken, potatoes, and a hint of sweetness.
  • Pique a la Macho: A hearty dish consisting of beef, potatoes, onions, peppers, and boiled eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Singani: A grape-based spirit that is Bolivia’s national liquor and used in cocktails such as Chuflay and Yungueñito.
  • Chicha: A traditional fermented drink made from corn or quinoa.

Where to Stay in La Paz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Rosario: This boutique hotel is located in the heart of the historic center and has a rooftop terrace with views of the city.
  • Wild Rover Hostel: A popular hostel with a range of room types, a bar, and organized activities.
  • Casa de Piedra: A cozy guesthouse with charming rooms and a beautiful courtyard.

What to Buy in La Paz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Alpaca wool products: Sweaters, scarves, and blankets made from soft and warm alpaca wool are popular souvenirs.
  • Jewelry: Silver and gold jewelry with traditional indigenous designs can be found in many markets.
  • Bolivian textiles: Colorful tapestries, wall hangings, and tablecloths are also popular handmade souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of La Paz

Bolivia is a predominantly Catholic country, and religion plays an important role in daily life. In La Paz, many indigenous traditions and customs continue to thrive alongside Spanish colonial influences.

Best Time to Visit La Paz

The best time to visit La Paz is during the dry season between May and September. October and November can also be good months to visit as the crowds have dispersed and the weather is still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Paz

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Take a cable car ride for amazing views
  • Learn about indigenous cultures and history

Don’t:

  • Show excessive displays of wealth
  • Discuss politics without caution
  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas

Safety and Health

Safety in La Paz

La Paz can be a safe destination for tourists if they take basic precautions such as avoiding wearing expensive jewelry, carrying valuable items discreetly, and not walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in La Paz

Travelers to La Paz should be aware of the high altitude and take the necessary precautions to avoid altitude sickness. This includes drinking plenty of water and taking it easy for the first few days after arrival.

Getting to La Paz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Paz

  • El Alto International Airport (LPB): Located just outside La Paz, this airport serves numerous domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near La Paz

  • Terminal de Buses La Paz: This modern bus terminal is located in the city center and serves destinations throughout Bolivia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

  • Taxis and shared vans are also available for transportation within the city and to nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in La Paz

The city of La Paz can be easily explored on foot or by taking taxis or public transportation. The cable car system is also a popular and scenic way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from La Paz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many destinations to explore near La Paz. Here are some ideas:

  • Lake Titicaca: This high-altitude lake is located several hours from La Paz and offers tours to its many islands, including the famous Isla del Sol.
  • Uyuni Salt Flats: This otherworldly landscape is located several hours from La Paz and can be reached by taking a tour from the city.
  • Copacabana: A charming and historic town located on the shore of Lake Titicaca, just a few hours drive from La Paz.

Conclusion

La Paz is a captivating city with a unique blend of indigenous and colonial cultures, beautiful natural scenery, and plenty of activities and attractions to keep visitors busy. Whether you want to explore museums and historic sites, go on an adventure hike or bike ride, or simply relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene, La Paz has something for everyone.
