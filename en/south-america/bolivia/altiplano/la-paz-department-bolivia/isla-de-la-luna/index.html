Isla de La Luna Travel Guide - Discover Bolivia’s Island in the Sky

Located in the heart of Bolivia, Isla de La Luna (Moon Island) is a natural wonder that lies in Lake Titicaca’s northern basin. It is a place of enchanting beauty, rich history, and cultural significance. At an elevation of 3,810 meters above sea level, it is higher than many major peaks in the United States.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a deep dive into what Isla de La Luna has to offer. We’ll cover the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay, and other important information to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Isla de La Luna

Isla de La Luna has a lot to offer tourists. Below we’ve listed some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

• Temple of the Virgins of the Sun – This ancient temple is believed to be more than 1,500 years old. It was dedicated to the sun goddess and was a place of worship for the Chimu and Inca civilizations.

• Inca Pachamama Monument - A monument dedicated to Pachamama, the Inca goddess of the Earth. It’s believed to have protective powers and offers stunning views of Lake Titicaca.

• Kallawaya Museum – A museum that showcases the history and culture of the Kallawayas, an ancient group of healers that were renowned for their medicinal skills.

Natural Attractions of Isla de La Luna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

• Cerro Kesanani – One of the highest peaks on the island where you can take in sweeping views of the surrounding landscape.

• Lake Titicaca – The highest navigable lake in the world with over 500 km of shoreline.

• Yumani Stairway - A beautiful set of stone steps that span from the shore of Lake Titicaca to the top of the island, offering panoramic views of the lake and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isla de La Luna

The island’s rugged terrain and scenic beauty make it a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts seeking outdoor adventures. Here are some impressive routes:

• Cerro Kesanani trail – A moderate to challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Cerro Kesanani.

• Yumani Stairway trail – A strenuous hike starting from the shore of Lake Titicaca to the top of the island’s highest peak.

• Circuito de las Perlas bike route – An easy trail that circles the island and offers breathtaking views of Lake Titicaca.

Best Things to Do in Isla de La Luna: Activities, and Tours

There are plenty of activities to choose from on Isla de La Luna. Here are some of the best:

• Explore the ancient ruins – Discover the mysterious Chimu and Inca ruins on the island.

• Take a boat tour – Rent a boat and explore the stunning Lake Titicaca and its surroundings, stopping at scenic spots along the way.

• Attend a traditional dance performance – Experience the rich cultural heritage of the region through traditional dance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isla de La Luna

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Isla de La Luna, here’s an itinerary to consider:

Day 1: Arrival and relaxation

Day 2: Explore the Temple of the Virgins of the Sun and Inca Pachamama Monument

Day 3: Hike to the top of Cerro Kesanani

Day 4: Take a boat tour of Lake Titicaca

Day 5: Visit the Kallawaya museum and enjoy a traditional dance performance

Day 6: Explore the Yumani Stairway trail

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Isla de La Luna

Bolivian cuisine is known for its rich flavors and unique ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

• Salteñas - Savory pastries filled with beef, chicken, or cheese, served with a spicy sauce.

• Silpancho - A dish consisting of thin slices of beef that are heavily breaded and fried, served with rice, potatoes, and eggs.

• Chuflay - A refreshing Bolivian cocktail made with Singani (a Bolivian spirit), ginger ale, and lemon.

Local Beverages

• Chicha - A fermented drink made from maize. It can be either alcoholic or non-alcoholic.

• Mocochinchi - A cold, sweet tea made with dehydrated peaches and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Isla de La Luna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While there are few traditional hotels on the island, there are plenty of budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels to choose from. Here are some options:

• Hostal del Sol – A cozy guesthouse with stunning views of Lake Titicaca

• Hostal Pacha – A lovely guesthouse close to the Yumani Stairway trail.

• Xtreme Everest – A rustic hostel that offers affordable lodging and extraordinary views.

What to Buy in Isla de La Luna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Isla de la Luna. Here are some options:

• Alpaca Wool Products – Beautiful handcrafted clothing made from soft, warm alpaca wool.

• Textiles and Weavings – Find hand-woven textiles and traditional weavings made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Isla de La Luna

The local culture and customs of Isla de La Luna are fascinating and deeply rooted in ancient traditions. One of the most important festivals is the Alasitas festival, held annually in the nearby city of La Paz. It is a festival of abundance where locals offer miniature replicas of everything they want in their lives to Ekeko, the god of abundance.

Best Time to Visit Isla de La Luna

The best time to visit Isla de La Luna is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, the weather is mild, and the skies are clear, making it the perfect time to hike or bike the island trails and take in the stunning lake views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isla de La Luna

Do:

• Respect local customs and traditions.

• Wear sunscreen and bring a hat to protect yourself from the sun’s strong UV rays.

Don’t:

• Litter the island – It is important to keep the island clean and leave no trace.

• Take anything from the island – This includes rocks, plants, and other natural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isla de La Luna

Isla de La Luna is a generally safe destination. However, it is always important to take precautions like locking up your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Isla de La Luna

It is important to acclimatize slowly when visiting Isla de La Luna. As the island is situated at a high altitude, visitors may experience altitude sickness. It is recommended to drink plenty of water and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Isla de La Luna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Isla de La Luna can be a bit tricky. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Isla de La Luna

The nearest airport is El Alto International Airport, located in La Paz, Bolivia.

Bus Stations Near Isla de La Luna

From La Paz, take a bus to Copacabana, and from there, board a boat to the island.

Other Options

Another way to get to Isla de La Luna is to hire a private taxi or a guided tour.

How to Get Around in Isla de La Luna

Isla de La Luna is a small island, and the best way to explore it is on foot or on a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Isla de La Luna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Isla de La Luna, there are many nearby destinations to explore, including:

• Copacabana – A charming town located on the shores of Lake Titicaca, famous for its cathedral and beach.

• Isla del Sol – Another island on Lake Titicaca with many historic sites and great hiking trails.

Conclusion

Isla de La Luna is a magical place that is sure to enchant any traveler. It’s a destination that offers stunning natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventure.
