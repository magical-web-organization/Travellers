Sorata, located in the La Paz department of Bolivia, sits in the Altiplano region of the country. It is a small and charming town surrounded by stunning natural scenery. From high Andean peaks to lush green valleys, Sorata offers visitors a wide range of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Sorata.

Introduction to Sorata

Sorata is a small town in Bolivia’s La Paz department that boasts beautiful natural surroundings and rich cultural heritage. The town is located at an altitude of 2,700 meters above sea level, which makes it a perfect destination for hiking and trekking enthusiasts.

Sorata is home to a vibrant mix of indigenous cultures, and visitors can experience their unique customs and traditions through food, music, and dance. The town itself has picturesque cobblestone streets, colonial-era buildings, and vibrant marketplaces selling handmade crafts and woven textiles.

List of Places to Visit in Sorata

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Max Portugal: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Max Portugal, the famous Bolivian artist. Visitors can see a collection of his paintings, sculptures, and other artworks.
  • Iglesia de la Asunción: This sixteenth-century church is a great example of colonial-era architecture. Visitors can admire the intricate details of the church’s façade and its ornate interior.

Natural Attractions of Sorata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cerro Illampu: This towering mountain peak is a popular destination for hikers and trekkers. It is also considered a sacred site by the local indigenous communities, and visitors can learn about their spiritual beliefs and practices.
  • Parque Nacional Sajama: This national park is located in the Altiplano region and features stunning landscapes, including high Andean peaks, glaciers, hot springs, and more. Visitors can explore the park on hiking and trekking trails, and they may see a variety of wildlife, including llamas, vicuñas, and flamingos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sorata

Sorata offers visitors a range of hiking and biking routes, from easy walks to challenging treks. Some of the most popular include:

  • El Camino del Oro: This trail follows the ancient Inca route that was used to transport gold from the Amazon. Visitors can see the ruins of Inca settlements and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Loma Redonda: This short hike takes visitors to the top of a hill overlooking Sorata. It is a great spot to watch the sunset and take in the stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Sorata: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking classes: Visitors can learn how to cook traditional Bolivian dishes and enjoy a delicious meal.
  • Market tours: Visitors can explore the vibrant marketplaces of Sorata and buy handmade crafts and woven textiles.
  • Cycling tours: Visitors can rent bicycles and explore the surrounding countryside on their own or with a guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Sorata

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sopa de Maní: A traditional Bolivian dish made with peanuts, vegetables, and chicken.
  • Chuño: Freeze-dried potatoes that are a staple in the Andean diet.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A fermented corn drink that is popular among the indigenous communities.
  • Singuani: A traditional Bolivian liquor made from grapes.

Where to Stay in Sorata: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Sorata, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the best options are:

  • Hostal San Francisco: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the town center.
  • Hotel Perla del Illampu: A mid-range hotel with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Hotel Gloria Sorata: A luxury hotel with spa facilities, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Sorata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Sorata can purchase a variety of handmade crafts and traditional textiles, including:

  • Alpaca wool clothing: Warm, cozy clothing made from the soft wool of alpacas.
  • Masks and handicrafts: Colorful masks and handicrafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Sorata

Sorata is home to a vibrant mix of indigenous cultures, and visitors can experience their unique customs and traditions through food, music, and dance. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions and take care not to offend or disrupt local ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Sorata

The best time to visit Sorata is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. This is the best time to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and trekking and to soak in the hot springs. Visitors should be prepared for cold temperatures, especially at higher elevations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sorata

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Explore the surrounding countryside on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt local ceremonies or rituals.
  • Leave trash or litter in the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sorata

Sorata is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is always a good idea to take precautions against theft and crime. Visitors should take care not to leave valuables unattended and to keep their passports and other important documents in a secure location.

Health in Sorata

Visitors to Sorata should take precautions against altitude sickness, which can occur at high elevations. It is recommended that visitors spend at least a day acclimatizing to the altitude before embarking on any strenuous activities.

Getting to Sorata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sorata

The nearest airport to Sorata is the El Alto International Airport in La Paz, which is about a four-hour drive away. Visitors can arrange for taxis or private transportation services to take them to Sorata.

Bus Stations Near Sorata

Visitors can take buses from La Paz to Sorata, which takes about 6 hours. Buses leave from the Terminal de Buses in La Paz.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange for private transportation services or hire a taxi to take them to Sorata.

How to Get Around in Sorata

Sorata is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire a taxi or private transportation service to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Sorata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Titicaca: This high-altitude lake is a popular destination for tourists, as it is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and has a rich cultural heritage.
  • La Paz: Bolivia’s capital city is a vibrant metropolis with beautiful architecture, rich cultural heritage, and a thriving food and drink scene.
  • Copacabana: This charming town is located on the shores of Lake Titicaca and is a popular destination for its beautiful beaches and laid-back vibe.

Conclusion

Sorata is a small town with a big heart. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities and cultural experiences, from hiking and trekking to trying traditional Bolivian dishes. With its stunning natural surroundings and rich cultural heritage, Sorata is a destination that should not be missed.
