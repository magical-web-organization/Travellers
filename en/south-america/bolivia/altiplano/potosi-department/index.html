Introduction to Potosí (department)

Potosí (department) is one of the nine departments of Bolivia, located in the southwestern region of the country. It is named after its capital city, Potosí, which was once considered the wealthiest and largest city in the world due to its vast silver mines. Potosí’s rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and historical sites make it an ideal destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Potosí (department)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa Nacional de la Moneda

The Casa Nacional de la Moneda is a museum and historical site that has served as the National Mint since the 16th century. Visitors can explore the coin-making process and see silver bars, coins, and other historical artifacts.

Convento de Santa Teresa

The Convento de Santa Teresa is a 17th-century baroque convent that now serves as a museum. Visitors can see intricate architecture, religious art, and learn about the lives of the nuns who once lived there.

Cerro Rico Mine

The Cerro Rico Mine is an operating mine and UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can take a guided tour through the tunnels and learn about the mining process and Potosí’s rich history of silver mining.

Natural Attractions of Potosí (department): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Laguna Colorada

Laguna Colorada is a stunning red-colored lake located in the Eduardo Avaroa Andean Fauna National Reserve. Visitors can take a scenic hike around the lake and see pink flamingos in their natural habitat.

Salar de Uyuni

Salar de Uyuni is the largest salt flat in the world and a must-see destination in Potosí. Visitors can take a guided tour and explore the otherworldly landscape of salt mounds, mineral pools, and unusual rock formations.

Torreón

Torreón is a mountain peak located in the Toro Toro National Park. Visitors can take a guided hike to the summit and enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Potosí (department)

Potosí has several scenic hiking and biking trails for adventurers. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Toro Toro National Park Circuit
  • Cerro Rico Mining Tour
  • Salkantay Trek to Machu Picchu
  • La Cumbre to Chulumani Mountain Bike Tour

Best Things to Do in Potosí (department): Activities and Tours

Mountain Biking

Mountain biking is a popular activity in Potosí due to its scenic trails and breathtaking landscapes. Visitors can rent a bike or go on guided tours to explore the surrounding areas of the city.

Hot Springs

Potosí has several natural hot springs, including Termas de Polques and Termas de Chalviri, which provide a perfect escape and relaxation for travelers.

Salt Flats Tour

A guided tour of the Salar de Uyuni salt flats is one of the most popular activities in Potosí. Visitors can witness the unique landscapes of the salt flats and get some amazing photographs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Potosí (department)

Day 1: Visit Casa Nacional de la Moneda and Convento de Santa Teresa

Day 2: Take a guided tour of Cerro Rico Mine and learn about Potosí’s rich mining history

Day 3-4: Enjoy a two-day trip to Salar de Uyuni with a scenic hike around Laguna Colorada

Day 5: Take a guided hike to the summit of Torreón and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains

Day 6: Relax and unwind in the natural hot springs of Termas de Polques or Termas de Chalviri

Day 7: Explore the city on a bike and visit local markets and restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Potosí (department)

Must-Try Dishes

Silpancho

Silpancho is Bolivia’s national dish and a must-try when in Potosí. It consists of rice, potatoes, beef, and egg all stacked together and served with a side of salsa.

Chicha

Chicha is a fermented corn drink that is a staple in Bolivian cuisine. It is often consumed during festivities and is a must-try when in Potosí.

Local Beverages

Singani

Singani is a local alcoholic beverage made from white Muscat grapes. It is often served in cocktails or enjoyed straight up.

Coca Tea

Coca tea is a herbal tea made from coca leaves and is commonly consumed in Bolivia. It is believed to alleviate altitude sickness and provide a boost of energy.

Where to Stay in Potosí (department): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Coloso Potosí

Hotel Coloso Potosí is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Potosí. It offers spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, and stunning views of the city.

Hostal Tukos La Casa Real

Hostal Tukos La Casa Real is a budget-friendly hostel located in the historic center of Potosí. It offers comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of the city.

Casa Cultural Aryakara

Casa Cultural Aryakara is a guesthouse located in the picturesque village of Betanzos, just a short drive from Potosí. It offers cozy rooms, traditional Andean meals, and a peaceful environment surrounded by stunning mountain views.

What to Buy in Potosí (department): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Textiles

Potosí is known for its traditional textiles, including woven blankets and ponchos. Visitors can purchase these items at local markets and shops.

Silver Jewelry

Due to Potosí’s rich history in silver mining, visitors can find beautiful silver jewelry in the city’s shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Potosí (department)

The culture of Potosí is heavily influenced by its indigenous communities, who maintain traditional customs and dress. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and avoid cultural appropriation.

Best Time to Visit Potosí (department)

The best time to visit Potosí is during the dry season which runs from May to October. The weather is mostly sunny, and there are fewer chances of rainfall, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Potosí (department)

Do:

  • Try traditional Bolivian cuisine
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry bottled water and sun protection while exploring outdoors

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Take photographs without permission in religious places
  • Participate in illegal mining activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Potosí (department)

Potosí is generally a safe city for travelers. Visitors should be cautious with their belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Potosí (department)

Altitude sickness is a common concern for travelers visiting Potosí, which lies at an elevation of around 4,000 meters. It is advisable to drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol, and take frequent breaks while hiking or exploring outdoors.

Getting to Potosí (department): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Potosí (department)

The Capitán Nicolas Rojas Airport is the closest airport to Potosí, located about 5 km from the city center. It offers international and domestic flights to major destinations in Bolivia and South America.

Bus Stations Near Potosí (department)

The Potosí Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers regular bus services to major cities in Bolivia, including Sucre and La Paz.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire from major cities in Bolivia to Potosí.

How to Get Around in Potosí (department)

The city center of Potosí is small and easy to navigate on foot. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Potosí (department): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sucre

The colonial city of Sucre is just a few hours away from Potosí and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its historic buildings, colorful streets, and artisan markets.

La Paz

La Paz is the administrative and political capital of Bolivia, located about 9 hours away from Potosí. It is a vibrant city with stunning mountain views, traditional markets, and a unique culture.

Conclusion

Potosí (department) is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore Bolivia’s rich culture and scenic beauty. From historical sites to stunning landscapes, Potosí has something to offer for everyone.
