Introduction to Sucre

Welcome to Sucre, the constitutional capital of Bolivia and a charming city full of historical, cultural, and natural wonders. Located in the Chuquisaca department of Bolivia, Sucre (also called La Ciudad Blanca – The White City) is a UNESCO World Heritage site that boasts colonial-era architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and breathtaking landscapes, making it a sought-after destination for travelers from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Sucre, we have compiled a list of places to visit, things to do, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, and practical information to help you plan your trip to this fascinating city.

List of Places to Visit in Sucre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sucre is a city steeped in history and culture, with a variety of museums and historical places that showcase its rich past. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

  1. Casa de la Libertad Museum: A museum that chronicles Bolivia’s struggle for independence, located in the heart of Sucre.
  2. Recoleta Monastery: A colonial monastery featuring beautiful architecture and art, with a stunning view of the city from the rooftop.
  3. Santa Clara Convent: An impressive 17th-century convent that houses a museum and an art gallery.
  4. Textile Museum: A museum that showcases the traditional textile arts of Bolivia, including weaving and embroidery.
  5. San Francisco Church and Convent: A beautiful colonial-era church and convent complex, known for its intricate facade.

Natural Attractions of Sucre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore in Sucre and its surrounding areas. Here are some of the notable natural attractions:

  1. Maragua Crater: A unique geological formation with stunning landscapes and hiking trails.
  2. Parque Cretácico: A dinosaur-themed park with life-sized replicas of dinosaurs.
  3. Yotala and Chataquila: Two scenic viewpoints near Sucre that offer panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  4. Tarabuco Market: A colorful market located in a small village near Sucre, famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and local food.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sucre

Sucre is a perfect destination for those who love hiking and biking. Two of the best routes to explore in Sucre are:

  1. la Ruta de las Siete Cascadas: A scenic hike that takes you through seven beautiful waterfalls.
  2. la Ruta del Valle: A bike ride that takes you through a picturesque valley, surrounded by the Andean mountains.

Best Things to Do in Sucre: Activities and Tours

Sucre offers a variety of exciting activities and tours for all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Sucre:

  1. Take a walking tour of the historic center of Sucre with a local guide.
  2. Attend a traditional dance performance at the Centro de Artisanías de Tarija.
  3. Take a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Bolivian dishes.
  4. Visit the Potosí mines, a UNESCO World Heritage site located near Sucre, and learn about the mining traditions of Bolivia.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sucre

If you’re planning to spend a week in Sucre, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore the historic center of Sucre – visit the Casa de la Libertad Museum, Recoleta Monastery, and La Glorieta Castle. Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour of the Ruta de las Siete Cascadas or Ruta del Valle. Day 3: Visit the Tarabuco Market and explore the traditional handicrafts and textiles. Day 4: Take a day trip to the Potosí mines and learn about Bolivia’s mining traditions. Day 5: Visit the Textile Museum and the San Francisco Church and Convent. Day 6: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Bolivian dishes. Day 7: Visit the Parque Cretácico and discover Bolivia’s dinosaur heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Sucre

No trip to Sucre would be complete without trying the local food and drinks. Here’s a list of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Salteñas: savory empanadas filled with meat, potatoes, eggs, and vegetables.
  2. Chicha: a traditional Bolivian drink made from fermented maize.
  3. Pique Macho: a hearty dish made with beef, sausages, onions, and chili peppers.
  4. Majadito: a rice and beef stew, flavored with cumin and other spices.
  5. Api con Pastel: a dessert made with cornmeal and cinnamon, served with a pastry.

Local Beverages

  1. Singani: a grape-based spirit that’s Bolivia’s national distilled drink.
  2. Mocochinchi: a refreshing drink made with dried peaches and cinnamon.
  3. Yungueño: a beer brewed locally in Sucre.

Where to Stay in Sucre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sucre offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to affordable guesthouses and hostels. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Parador Santa Maria la Real: a luxurious hotel in a colonial-style building, located in the historic center of Sucre.
  2. KulturBerlin: a cozy and affordable hostel in a central location, with a social atmosphere and cultural activities.
  3. Villa Antigua: a charming bed and breakfast, located in a historic house with a lush garden.
  4. El Hostal de Su Merced: a traditional guesthouse with colonial-style architecture and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Sucre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sucre is a great place to find souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Sucre:

  1. Textiles and Handicrafts: Sucre is famous for its traditional textile arts, including weaving and embroidery. You can find beautiful textiles and handicrafts in the Tarabuco Market and the Centro de Artisanías de Tarija.
  2. Singani: Bolivia’s national distilled drink, Singani, is a unique and flavorful gift to bring home.
  3. Chocolate: Sucre is home to some of the finest chocolate shops in Bolivia, where you can find delicious treats made from local cocoa.
  4. Silver Jewelry: Bolivian silver jewelry is known for its unique designs and high quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Sucre

Bolivia is a country with a rich cultural heritage, and Sucre is no exception. Here are some customs and cultural traits to keep in mind during your stay:

  1. Greetings: Bolivians are generally friendly and hospitable, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake or a hug.
  2. Language: The official language of Bolivia is Spanish, but many indigenous languages are also spoken in Sucre.
  3. Dress: Bolivians take pride in their traditional dress, which varies by region and ethnicity. In Sucre, you may see women wearing colorful skirts and shawls, while men wear ponchos and hats.
  4. Religion: Catholicism is the dominant religion in Bolivia, and many religious festivals and events are celebrated throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Sucre

The best time to visit Sucre is during the dry season, from May to September, when the weather is mild and sunny. However, Sucre has a pleasant climate year-round, and even the rainy season (December to March) has its charms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sucre

Do:

  1. Try the local food and drinks.
  2. Respect local customs and traditions.
  3. Explore the historic center of Sucre on foot.
  4. Learn about Bolivia’s rich cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public.
  2. Bargain aggressively in markets and shops.
  3. Disrespect religious or cultural sites.
  4. Drink tap water without boiling or purifying it first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sucre

Sucre is a safe city, but like any place, it’s important to take some precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  1. Avoid walking alone in dark or isolated areas.
  2. Keep your valuables out of sight and use a money belt when traveling.
  3. Be cautious with taxis – use licensed and reputable companies.
  4. Stay informed about local events and news.

Health in Sucre

Before traveling to Sucre or any other destination in Bolivia, it’s important to visit your doctor or travel health clinic to get the necessary vaccinations and medication. Here are some general health tips:

  1. Avoid tap water and only drink bottled or purified water.
  2. Use insect repellent and protective clothing to prevent mosquito bites.
  3. Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  4. Be cautious with street food and always make sure it’s cooked and served hot.

Getting to Sucre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sucre

The Alcantari International Airport (SRE) is the closest airport to Sucre, located about 9 km from the city center. There are daily flights to and from Sucre from major cities in Bolivia and neighboring countries.

Bus Stations Near Sucre

Sucre’s main bus terminal is located about 2 km from the city center, and buses to and from other cities in Bolivia run regularly. It’s also possible to take a bus from La Paz or Cochabamba to Sucre, which takes about 12-14 hours.

Other Options

There are several options for getting to Sucre from other cities in Bolivia, including private car rental, shared taxis, and organized tours.

How to Get Around in Sucre

The best way to explore Sucre is on foot, as most of the major attractions are located in the historic center of the city. However, taxis and buses are also available, and bike and car rentals are also an option.

Where to Go Next from Sucre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sucre is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and historic towns and cities, making it a perfect starting point for exhilarating excursions:

  1. Potosí: home to some of the richest silver mines in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  2. Samaipata: a small town located in the foothills of the Andes, known for its pre-Columbian ruins.
  3. Santa Cruz de la Sierra: Bolivia’s largest city and a center of commerce and industry.

Conclusion

Sucre is a fascinating and vibrant city that offers plenty of attractions for all kinds of travelers. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Sucre and discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. Enjoy your trip!
