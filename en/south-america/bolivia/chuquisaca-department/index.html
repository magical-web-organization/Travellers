Introduction to Chuquisaca (Department)

Chuquisaca, also known as Sucre, is a department of Bolivia located in the southern part of the country. It is home to the constitutional capital of Bolivia, Sucre, which is a UNESCO world heritage site and known as the “White City” due to its colonial architecture. The department is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic Bolivian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Chuquisaca (Department)

Chuquisaca offers a wide range of attractions ranging from culturally important museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the places that you should visit during your trip to Chuquisaca.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Recoleta: A church and convent founded in 1601, which offers stunning views of the city of Sucre.
  • Museo del Tesoro: A museum that houses a collection of colonial art, jewelry, and silverware.
  • Museo Casa de la Libertad: A museum that showcases the history of Bolivia’s struggle for independence.
  • Dinosaur Park: A fossil park that features life-sized dinosaur models and a collection of fossils from the region.

Natural Attractions of Chuquisaca (Department): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Cretácico: A park that features footprints and fossils of dinosaurs that lived in the region millions of years ago.
  • Tarabuco Market: An Indigenous market that offers local products, including textiles, pottery, and food.
  • Maragua Crater: A crater that offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Cabildo: An archaeological site that features ruins of an Inca fortification.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chuquisaca (Department)

Chuquisaca offers numerous hiking and biking routes for those seeking an adventure in the region. The most popular routes include:

  • Inca Trail: A 30 km trek that takes you through the Andes and features stunning views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Chataquila Trek: A 15 km trek that takes you through rural communities and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Maragua Trek: A 3-day trek that takes you through the Maragua Crater and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Mountain Biking: Sucre offers numerous companies that offer mountain biking tours in the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Chuquisaca (Department): Activities and Tours

Apart from the attractions mentioned above, Chuquisaca offers numerous things to do and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best activities:

  • Spanish Language Course: Sucre is known as Bolivia’s cultural and intellectual capital and offers numerous language schools.
  • Paragliding: Sucre and the surrounding area offer stunning landscapes and perfect conditions for paragliding.
  • Cave Exploration: Chuquisaca offers numerous caves that can be explored for those seeking an underground adventure.
  • Chocolate Factory Tour: Sucre offers numerous chocolate factories that offer tours and tastings.

Food and Drink Scene of Chuquisaca (Department)

Chuquisaca’s food and drink scene offers a unique taste of Bolivian cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicharrón: A dish made from fried pork or chicken served with potatoes and salad.
  • Pique Macho: A dish made from beef, onions, peppers, and potatoes served with boiled egg and olives.
  • Salteñas: A type of empanada filled with meat, potatoes, peas, and other ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A traditional Andean corn beer.
  • Singani: A grape brandy that is the national liquor of Bolivia.
  • Coca Tea: A tea made from coca leaves that is said to help with altitude sickness and is widely used in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Chuquisaca (Department)

Chuquisaca has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some of the things to keep in mind when visiting the region:

  • Language: Spanish is the official language of Bolivia, but many Indigenous communities speak Quechua or Aymara.
  • Religion: Bolivia is a predominantly Catholic country, and many festivals and events are celebrated with religious significance.
  • Etiquette: Bolivians are polite and friendly people, and it is important to show respect for their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Chuquisaca (Department)

The best time to visit Chuquisaca is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, the weather is cool and dry, and there are fewer crowds. However, the wet season from November to April can also be a good time to visit as the landscape is lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chuquisaca (Department)

Here are some things to keep in mind to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to Chuquisaca:

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish: Knowing some basic Spanish phrases can be helpful when interacting with local people.
  • Try local cuisine: Chuquisaca offers a range of unique and flavorful dishes that should be tried during your trip.
  • Respect local customs and traditions: Bolivian culture is rich and diverse, and it is important to show respect for local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Overlook safety precautions: While Chuquisaca is a relatively safe region, it is important to take precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes.
  • Ignore altitude sickness: Chuquisaca is located at high altitude, and it is important to take time to acclimatize and to drink plenty of fluids to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Be disrespectful to Indigenous cultures: Bolivia has a large Indigenous population, and it is important to be respectful and mindful of their customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Chuquisaca is a relatively safe region, but travelers should take precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes. It is also important to be aware of the high altitude and to take precautions to avoid altitude sickness. When it comes to health, travelers are advised to drink bottled water and to avoid street food to avoid stomach problems.

Getting to Chuquisaca (Department): Transportation Options and Logistics

Chuquisaca is easily accessible by air and road. The city of Sucre has a small airport that offers domestic flights to other cities in Bolivia. Alternatively, travelers can take a bus from La Paz or other major cities in Bolivia to Sucre.

Airports Near Chuquisaca (Department)

  • Juana Azurduy de Padilla International Airport (SRE): Located in Sucre, this is the primary airport for the department.

Bus Stations Near Chuquisaca (Department)

  • Sucre Bus Terminal: Located in the city of Sucre, this is the primary bus station for the department.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a private car or arrange for a tour to visit Chuquisaca and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Chuquisaca (Department)

The best way to get around Chuquisaca is by taxi or on foot. Taxis are affordable and readily available in the city of Sucre, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Alternatively, travelers can arrange for a private car or tour to visit attractions further afield.

Where to Go Next from Chuquisaca (Department): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chuquisaca is located in a region of Bolivia that offers numerous nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Salar de Uyuni: located approximately 300 km south of Chuquisaca, Salar de Uyuni is the world’s largest salt flat and a popular tourist destination.
  • La Paz: located approximately 600 km north of Chuquisaca, La Paz is Bolivia’s largest city and a hub for culture and entertainment.
  • Titicaca Lake: located approximately 400 km northwest of Chuquisaca, Titicaca Lake is the largest lake in South America and a popular tourist destination.
