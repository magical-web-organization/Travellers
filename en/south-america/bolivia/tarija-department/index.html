Introduction to Tarija (department)

Tarija is a department located in the southern part of Bolivia. It is known for its wineries, natural beauty, and friendly people. The climate is warm and dry, which makes it a perfect destination year-round. Visitors can explore the city’s cultural heritage by visiting museums, historical sites, and religious landmarks. The department is also a great starting point for adventurers, with its stunning outdoor landscapes. Whether you are looking to relax, explore or have an adventure - Tarija has something to offer for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarija (department)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Choripán - Sausage sandwich served on crusty bread with chimichurri sauce
  • Empanadas - Traditional Bolivian pastries stuffed with meat or cheese
  • Silpancho - Rice and crispy potatoes with a thin beef escalope and a fried egg on top
  • Pique Macho - Beef, french fries, cheese, and spicy sauce

Local Beverages

  • Singani - A pisco-like clear spirit, distilled from grapes grown in high-altitude vineyards.
  • Chicha - A fermented drink made from corn
  • Api - A hot drink made from purple corn, cinnamon, and cloves

Local Culture and Customs of Tarija (department)

Tarija has a rich cultural heritage with many customs and traditions, manifested through festivities, music, dances, and religious celebrations. Bolivian patriotism, the cultural richness of indigenous communities, and Spanish colonial influence are all visible in Tarija. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and show appreciation for the unique local culture.

Best Time to Visit Tarija (department)

The weather in Tarija is warm and dry throughout the year. However, spring (September-November) and autumn (February-May) are the best seasons to visit Tarija. During these months, you can experience comfortable temperatures, sunny skies, and less rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarija (department)

Do:

  • Respect local customs
  • Learn basic Spanish phrases
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Forget to bargain when shopping in markets
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarija (department)

Tarija is generally safe for visitors. However, it is recommended that you take precautions when walking or traveling alone at night. Also, be careful with pickpockets and robberies in crowded places and markets.

Health in Tarija (department)

The quality of medical care in Tarija is adequate but can be limited in certain rural areas. Visitors are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria by using insect repellent and protective clothing. Also, make sure to drink only bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach problems.
