Introduction to Tarija

Nestled in the southern region of Bolivia, Tarija is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. This charming town is known for its rich cultural heritage, delicious food, and stunning natural beauty. Often overlooked by tourists who flock to bigger cities like La Paz and Sucre, Tarija is perfect for those looking for a more intimate and laid-back travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Tarija

Whether you’re a culture buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Tarija has something to offer everyone. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tarija boasts a rich cultural heritage, and its museums and historical sites are a testament to that. Here are a few places that shouldn’t be missed:

  • Casa Dorada - a colonial house that has been converted into a museum showcasing the history of Tarija and its people.
  • Museo Paleontológico - a museum dedicated to the region’s prehistoric past, with displays of fossils, dinosaur bones, and more.
  • Iglesia de San Francisco - a 16th-century church that features beautiful architecture and artwork.

Natural Attractions of Tarija: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tarija is blessed with stunning natural beauty, from rolling vineyards to towering mountains. Here are some of the best places to soak in the natural beauty of Tarija:

  • El Samaipata - a natural rock formation that offers panoramic views of the valley below.
  • Parque Nacional Aguarague - a national park that’s home to diverse wildlife including jaguars, pumas, and Andean condors.
  • Valle de la Concepción - a fertile valley that’s known for its vineyards, and is perfect for wine tasting and scenic walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarija

For outdoor enthusiasts, Tarija offers some excellent hiking and biking routes. Here are our top picks:

  • Circuito La Angostura - a scenic 13 km bike ride that takes you through vineyards and small towns.
  • Mirador de los Pinos - a 4 km hiking trail that leads to a stunning lookout point with panoramic views of Tarija.
  • Volcán Sajama - a challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Bolivia’s highest volcano.

Best Things to Do in Tarija: Activities and Tours

From wine tasting to paragliding, Tarija has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Wine tasting tour - visit the region’s many vineyards to sample some of Bolivia’s best wines.
  • Paragliding - soar over the valleys and mountains for a unique perspective of Tarija.
  • Thermal bath - relax in the natural hot springs of Calao, and soak in the mineral-rich waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarija

If you’re planning to spend a week in Tarija, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Tarija, explore the city center
2 Visit Casa Dorada, Museo Paleontológico, and Iglesia San Francisco
3 Hike to Mirador de los Pinos
4 Wine tasting tour in Valle de la Concepción

6 Soak in the thermal baths of Calao
7 Depart from Tarija

Food and Drink Scene of Tarija

Tarija is known for its delicious food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chairo - a hearty soup made with beef, potatoes, and chuño (freeze-dried potatoes).
  • Patasca - a spicy soup made with wheat and lamb.
  • Humintas - a sweet corn cake that’s steamed and served with cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Singani - a Bolivian brandy that’s made from white Muscat grapes, and is often used in cocktails.
  • Tarija wine - the region produces high-quality wines that are worth sampling.

Where to Stay in Tarija: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Tarija offers a range of accommodations options. Here are our top picks:

  • Los Parrales Resort Hotel - a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Valle de la Concepción.
  • El Hostal de Su Merced - a charming hostel with an excellent location in the city center.
  • La Posada del Sol - a family-run guesthouse with a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Tarija: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some suggestions:

  • Bolivian textiles - colorful woven fabrics can be found in markets throughout Tarija.
  • Alpaca wool products - warm blankets, hats, and gloves are a popular souvenir.
  • Singani - bring back a bottle or two of Bolivia’s national spirit.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarija

Tarija’s culture is a blend of indigenous, colonial, and modern influences. Here are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Greetings - it’s customary to greet people with a kiss on the cheek.
  • Religious festivals - Tarija celebrates many religious festivals throughout the year, and visitors are welcome to join in the festivities.
  • Siesta - many businesses close during midday for a siesta, so plan accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Tarija

The best time to visit Tarija is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is cooler and drier, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarija

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish - while there are some English speakers in Tarija, knowing a few basic phrases will go a long way.
  • Respect the locals - Tarija is a conservative town, so dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Tip - it’s customary to tip 10% at restaurants and cafes.

Don’t:

  • Be a party animal - Tarija is a laid-back town, and excessive partying is frowned upon.
  • Haggle too much - while bargaining is common in markets, don’t haggle too aggressively.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarija

Tarija is a safe town, but as with any destination, it’s important to take basic precautions. Here are a few tips:

  • Be aware of pickpockets - keep your valuables secure, especially in busy markets and tourist areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night - stick to well-lit areas and travel in groups.

Health in Tarija

Tarija is a relatively healthy destination, but travelers should take precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some tips:

  • Drink bottled water - tap water may not be safe to drink, so stick to bottled water.
  • Avoid street food - while tempting, street food may not be prepared in a sanitary manner.

Getting to Tarija: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarija

The closest airport to Tarija is Captain Oriel Lea Plaza Airport, which is served by several airlines including Boliviana De Aviacion and Amaszonas.

Bus Stations Near Tarija

Tarija has several bus stations that connect it to other parts of Bolivia, including the main bus station and Terminal Terrestre de Tarija.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Tarija, but be prepared for chaotic traffic and narrow streets.

How to Get Around in Tarija

Tarija is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Taxis and buses are also available, although buses can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Tarija: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other parts of Bolivia, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Sucre - Bolivia’s constitutional capital, and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Potosí - a city that’s famous for its silver mines, and has a well-preserved colonial center.
  • Uyuni salt flats - one of Bolivia’s most famous attractions, and the world’s largest salt flat.

Conclusion

Tarija may not be as well-known as some of Bolivia’s bigger cities, but that’s part of its charm. This hidden gem is a cultural, culinary, and natural wonder that’s just waiting to be discovered. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore the best of Tarija!
