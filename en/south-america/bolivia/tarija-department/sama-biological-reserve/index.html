Introduction to Sama Biological Reserve

Located in Tarija, Bolivia, Sama Biological Reserve boasts over 100,000 hectares of pristine wilderness. The reserve is home to an incredible array of flora and fauna, including Andean condors, pumas, llamas, and vicuñas. Visitors can explore beautiful landscapes, such as the Cordillera de Sama and the Valle de los Cardos. Additionally, the region is steeped in history and culture; indigenous communities continue to maintain traditional ways of life, and visitors can sample delicious local cuisine and shop for unique handicrafts.

List of Places to Visit in Sama Biological Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Paleontology and Archaeology: Located in Tarija, this museum includes exhibits of dinosaur fossils and displays on the region’s history and culture.
  • Casa Dorada: A restored colonial mansion and popular event venue, Casa Dorada boasts stunning architecture and a rich history.
  • Chaguaya Chapel: A 17th-century chapel located in the middle of the desert, Chaguaya is a popular pilgrimage site for Catholics.

Natural Attractions of Sama Biological Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Cordillera de Sama: This mountain range boasts incredible views of Bolivia’s southern highlands, as well as opportunities for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.
  • Valle de los Cardos: Known for its stunning rock formations and diverse wildlife, this valley is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Laguna de San Martin: A high-altitude lake popular for its fishing and trekking possibilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sama Biological Reserve

  • Mirador Los Condores: A short hike from the reserve’s entrance, this trail leads to a lookout point where visitors can see Andean condors in flight.
  • Cardos Circuit: This moderate hike takes visitors through the Valle de los Cardos and offers stunning views of the region’s unique landscape.
  • Cordillera de Sama Trek: For more experienced hikers, this multi-day trek takes visitors through the heart of the Cordillera de Sama, featuring breathtaking views and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Sama Biological Reserve: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding Tours: Visitors can take guided tours on horseback through the reserve’s vast wilderness areas.
  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can learn about the region’s culture and traditions by visiting local communities and attending indigenous ceremonies.
  • Wildlife Watching Tours: From birdwatching to searching for Andean condors, guides can help visitors spot some of the region’s most fascinating wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sama Biological Reserve

Day 1: Arrival in Tarija and visit to the Museum of Paleontology and Archaeology
Day 2: Hike to Mirador Los Condores and horseback riding tour
Day 3: Cultural tour to a local community and indigenous ceremonies
Day 4: Trek through the Cordillera de Sama
Day 5: Visit to Casa Dorada and Chaguaya Chapel
Day 6: Wildlife watching tour and visit to the Laguna de San Martin
Day 7: Departure from Tarija

Food and Drink Scene of Sama Biological Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

  • Picana: A traditional Bolivian stew made with beef, pork, and a variety of vegetables.
  • Anticuchos: Grilled skewers of marinated beef heart, a popular street food in Bolivia.
  • Empanadas Salteñas: Savory pastries filled with meat, potatoes, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Singani: A grape brandy made in Bolivia that can be enjoyed straight or in a cocktail.
  • Api: A hot, sweet drink made with purple corn, cinnamon, and cloves.
  • Chicha: A fermented corn beverage often served during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Sama Biological Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Los Parrales: A family-friendly hotel located in the outskirts of Tarija with comfortable rooms and a pool.
  • La Posada del Sol: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Tarija’s historic district.
  • Refugio Los Volcanes: A basic eco-lodge located in the Cordillera de Sama with stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

What to Buy in Sama Biological Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Local artisans create beautiful textiles, ceramics, and woodcarvings that make for unique souvenirs.
  • Singani: A bottle of singani makes for a great gift or souvenir to bring back home.
  • Traditional Clothing: Visitors can purchase clothing worn by locals, such as ponchos, hats, and woven belts.

Local Culture and Customs of Sama Biological Reserve

Visitors should be respectful of indigenous customs and traditions, such as the use of coca leaves and the practice of offering thanks to Pachamama (the Earth Mother). Additionally, visitors should dress modestly and ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Sama Biological Reserve

The best time to visit Sama Biological Reserve is from May to September, during Bolivia’s dry season. The weather is mild and allows for comfortable outdoor activities and sightseeing. December to February is the wet season, so visitors should be prepared for rain and muddy trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sama Biological Reserve

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately and modestly
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals
  • Take your time and fully immerse yourself in the region’s natural and cultural wonders

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local communities or their beliefs
  • Ignore safety guidelines when hiking or participating in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Sama Biological Reserve

Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and horseback riding. Visitors should also take precautions to ensure their safety when traveling in remote areas.

Health in Sama Biological Reserve

Altitude sickness can be a concern for visitors to the region, so it is recommended that visitors take time to acclimate to the altitude and stay hydrated. Visitors should also be prepared for basic medical care and bring any necessary medications or first aid supplies.

Getting to Sama Biological Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sama Biological Reserve

  • Capitan Oriel Lea Plaza Airport (TJA): Located in Tarija, this airport offers domestic flights to and from La Paz, Cochabamba, and Santa Cruz.

Bus Stations Near Sama Biological Reserve

  • Tarija Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Tarija where visitors can catch buses to other cities in Bolivia.

Other Options

  • Private Transportation: Visitors can arrange for private drivers to transport them to and from the reserve.

How to Get Around in Sama Biological Reserve

Visitors can hire guides or join tours to explore the region on foot, horseback, or by vehicle. It is recommended that visitors hire a guide or take a tour to ensure they are able to fully experience the region’s natural and cultural wonders.

Where to Go Next from Sama Biological Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Villamontes: A small city located 6 hours south of Tarija known for its natural gas industry.
  • El Chaco: A region located to the east of Sama Biological Reserve known for its cattle ranches and traditional culture.
  • Tupiza: A town located 5 hours south of Tarija known for its stunning red rock formations and cowboy culture.

Conclusion

Sama Biological Reserve offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience Bolivia’s natural and cultural wonders. From hiking and horseback riding to learning about local traditions and savoring delicious cuisine, the region has something for everyone. By planning ahead and taking time to fully immerse themselves in the region’s beauty, visitors can create unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.
