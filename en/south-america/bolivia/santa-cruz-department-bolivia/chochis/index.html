Introduction to Chochís

Chochís is a small town located in Bolivia’s Santa Cruz department. This hidden gem is nestled in the foothills of the Andes and is often overlooked by tourists, making it a perfect off-the-beaten-path destination for those looking for an authentic Bolivian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Chochís

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chochís is rich in history and culture. The town’s main attraction is the Jesuit Mission of Chochí, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 17th century, this beautiful church is a perfect example of the baroque architecture. Visitors can also visit the local museum, which features artifacts and exhibitions on the history and culture of the area.

Natural Attractions of Chochís: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The landscapes of Chochís are stunning, with rolling hills, lush forests, and plenty of outdoor adventure opportunities. Nature lovers can explore the nearby Cloud Forest, which is home to butterflies, monkeys, and a wide variety of bird species. Other must-see natural attractions include the Chochís Lagoon and the Pinturas Rupestres de Santa Ana, where you can see prehistoric rock paintings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chochís

Chochís is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The best trails can be found within the Cloud Forest, featuring paths surrounded by the rich flora and fauna, and leading to scenic views of the area.

Best Things to Do in Chochís: Activities and Tours

To take in the stunning views, visitors can embark on a hot air balloon ride or paragliding tour. Or, visitors can experience the town’s local culture by attending a traditional dance show or trying their hand at Bolivian cuisine through a cooking class.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chochís

  • Day 1: Visit the Jesuit Mission of Chochí and explore the town center.
  • Day 2: Hike the trails of Cloud Forest and visit Pinturas Rupestres de Santa Ana.
  • Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride or paragliding tour.
  • Day 4: Learn about the history and culture of Chochís at the local museum.
  • Day 5: Attend a traditional dance show, or visit a local farm.
  • Day 6: Try your hand at Bolivian cuisine through a cooking class.
  • Day 7: Relax at the Chochís Lagoon before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Chochís

Must-Try Dishes

Bolivian cuisine is often underrated, but there are unique and diverse dishes to try in Chochís. The most popular Bolivian dishes on the menu include Silpancho, Pique Macho, and Chicharrón. Don’t forget to try the local street food specialties, such as Salteñas and Empanadas.

Local Beverages

Bolivians love their beer and the local productions of Taquiña and Paceña Ales can be found in bars all around the town. For a unique drink experience, try the traditional Api, a hot, sweet drink made from purple maize flour, cinnamon and sugar.

Where to Stay in Chochís: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chochís has a variety of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets. For those looking for a luxury stay, the Hotel de Piedra and the Kaa Iya Eco-Lodge are both excellent options. If you prefer a more budget-friendly stay, consider the Hostal Casa Real or the Chochís Hostel.

What to Buy in Chochís: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home unique souvenirs from Chochís to remind them of their trip. Some popular items include woven textiles, jewelry made from locally sourced materials, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Chochís

Chochís is a town that values tradition and the local culture is still alive and well. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting cultural landmarks and be respectful of local customs.

Best Time to Visit Chochís

The best time to visit Chochís is from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry. This is also the time of year that the landscapes of the Cloud Forest are lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chochís

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take in the stunning views of the Cloud Forest
  • Try the local cuisine and traditional drinks

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural landmarks
  • Disrespect the environment by littering or damaging wildlife
  • Engage in unethical tourism practices

Safety and Health

Safety in Chochís

Chochís is a safe destination, but like any other town, visitors should take standard safety precautions. Do not leave valuables unattended and be aware of your surroundings when traveling alone at night.

Health in Chochís

The health situation in Chochís is improving, but visitors should take care when drinking water. It is also advisable to bring insect repellent to prevent bites from mosquitos or other insects.

Getting to Chochís: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chochís

The nearest airport to Chochís is the Viru Viru International Airport in Santa Cruz, which is approximately a 4-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Chochís

Buses to Chochís can be taken from the Santa Cruz Bus Terminal, and the journey takes approximately 6 hours.

Other Options

Some visitors prefer to rent a car to explore the surrounding areas at their own pace. This option can be expensive but offers plenty of flexibility and freedom.

How to Get Around in Chochís

Chochís is a small town that is easy to explore on foot. However, to reach the nearby attractions in the Cloud Forest and other scenic spots, visitors may want to consider renting a car or hiring a local guide.

Where to Go Next from Chochís: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The town of Samaipata is a nearby destination worth visiting, with a selection of cultural attractions that feature the region’s history. Other nearby destinations include El Fuerte de Samaipata, Amboro National Park, and the Valles Cruceños region.

Conclusion

Chochís is a destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the hidden beauty of Bolivia. With stunning natural landscapes, rich history and culture, and plenty of outdoor adventure opportunities, it’s the perfect destination for those looking for an authentic Bolivian experience. Planning a trip to Chochís is easy, with plenty of accommodation options and transportation available. A visit to Chochís is an adventure worth taking, and you’re sure to leave with unforgettable memories of your time here.
