Your Comprehensive Guide to San Rafael de Velasco, Bolivia

Nestled in the Santa Cruz department of Bolivia, San Rafael de Velasco is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. It is a small town with an incredible array of cultural and natural attractions that can satisfy any traveler. In this travel guide, we have curated a list of the best places to visit, activities to do, places to stay, and other important information to help plan your journey to San Rafael de Velasco.

Introduction to San Rafael de Velasco

San Rafael de Velasco is located in the eastern lowlands of Bolivia. It is one of UNESCO’s World Heritage Sites, known for its rich culture, architecture, and history. The town was founded in 1696 and was originally inhabited by the Chiquitano people. Today, the town is home to a diverse mix of cultures and traditions, including the Chiquitano, Mestizo, and Criollo.

List of Places to Visit in San Rafael de Velasco

San Rafael de Velasco has a plethora of cultural and natural attractions that cater to every visitor’s interests. We have compiled a list of some of the must-visit places in San Rafael de Velasco:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Rafael de Velasco Church: This church is one of the most important colonial churches in Bolivia, featuring baroque and Chiquitano architecture. It is considered to be the best-preserved Jesuit mission church in the country.
  • Museum of San Rafael de Velasco: The museum showcases the history and culture of San Rafael de Velasco. It has an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits, including religious icons, paintings, and sculptures.
  • Chiquitano Forest Reserve: This natural reserve houses 15 colonial mission churches from the 17th and 18th centuries, which have been beautifully restored. Each church has its unique architecture, artwork, and history that reflects the fusion of European and indigenous cultures.

Natural Attractions of San Rafael de Velasco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serranía Volcanes: The Serranía Volcanes is a range of extinct volcanoes that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching.
  • Laguna de San Miguel: This scenic lagoon is located near the town and is a great place to relax and enjoy nature. Visitors can take a boat ride or go fishing.
  • Mataracú Reserve: This natural reserve has an impressive variety of flora and fauna, including the jaguar, puma, and anteater. The reserve often conducts guided tours for visitors to explore the trails and get close to nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Rafael de Velasco

San Rafael de Velasco is known for its beautiful landscape and has several hiking and biking routes that are worth exploring. Some popular trails are:

  • Serranía Volcanes Trail: This trail takes you through the Serranía Volcanes range, passing through forests and presenting stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is a great place for birdwatching.
  • Mataracú Reserve Trail: This trail passes through the Mataracú Reserve, offering visitors the chance to witness the wide variety of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in San Rafael de Velasco: Activities and Tours

San Rafael de Velasco offers a range of activities and tours to its visitors, giving them the opportunity to experience its rich culture and natural beauty.

  • Attend Festivals: San Rafael de Velasco has several festivals throughout the year, such as the Chiquitano Culture Week and the Feast of San Rafael. These festivals are an excellent way to learn about the traditions and customs of the town.
  • Take a Historical Tour: Take a guided tour of the colonial churches and museums, which will give you a deeper understanding of San Rafael de Velasco’s rich history.
  • Explore Nature: Go hiking in the Serranía Volcanes or visit the Laguna de San Miguel for a relaxing day out.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Rafael de Velasco

A week-long itinerary is an excellent way to explore the town, and we have curated one that covers the most popular attractions and activities in San Rafael de Velasco.

Day 1: Visit the San Rafael de Velasco Church and Museum of San Rafael de Velasco.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Chiquitano Forest Reserve.

Day 3-4: Go hiking in the Serranía Volcanes and explore the Mataracú Reserve.

Day 5: Experience the local culture and traditions by attending a festival or event.

Day 6: Relax at the Laguna de San Miguel and go fishing.

Day 7: Explore the town on foot, taking in the architecture and local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of San Rafael de Velasco

San Rafael de Velasco has a rich culinary scene that blends indigenous, Spanish, and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Locro: This hearty stew is made with corn, potatoes, and meat.
  • Picana: This traditional stew is made with beef, chicken, rice, and vegetables.
  • Salteña: This is a popular pastry filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: A traditional maize-based fermented drink.
  • Singani: A brandy-like liquor made with grapes and consumed with soda or fruit juice.

Where to Stay in San Rafael de Velasco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Rafael de Velasco has several accommodation options ranging from guesthouses to hotels. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Posada del Sol: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities like free Wi-Fi, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Puerto Quijarro Hostel: This hostel is a great budget option for backpackers and travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in San Rafael de Velasco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Rafael de Velasco is known for its religious artifacts and handicrafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products you can buy:

  • Pottery and ceramics: These are well-known handicrafts in San Rafael de Velasco, and you can buy them from local shops or markets.
  • Religious souvenirs: You can find a range of religious souvenirs, including icons, paintings, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of San Rafael de Velasco

San Rafael de Velasco has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its indigenous and colonial past. It is essential to respect the local customs, traditions, and religions while visiting the town. Some cultural practices to keep in mind include:

  • Respect the traditions and customs of the indigenous people.
  • Dress conservatively while visiting religious sites.
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of people or places.

Best Time to Visit San Rafael de Velasco

The best time to visit San Rafael de Velasco is from May to September, as the weather is pleasant and dry. The town experiences heavy rainfall from December to February, which can make it difficult to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Rafael de Velasco

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases to communicate with locals effectively.
  • Stay hydrated, as the climate can be hot and dry.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute the environment.
  • Don’t disrespect religious sites or traditions.
  • Don’t wander off alone in remote areas.

Safety and Health

San Rafael de Velasco is generally a safe place to visit, but tourists should exercise caution and take some basic precautions:

Safety in San Rafael de Velasco

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid displaying wealth or valuables.
  • Use licensed taxis and agree on the fare before getting in.
  • Keep your important documents in a secure location and make copies.

Health in San Rafael de Velasco

  • Drink bottled or filtered water and avoid ice in drinks.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Consider buying travel insurance before you leave.

Getting to San Rafael de Velasco: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Rafael de Velasco is connected by roads and airways to the rest of Bolivia. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near San Rafael de Velasco

  • El Trompillo International Airport (SRZ) is the nearest airport and is about 280 km away from the town.

Bus Stations Near San Rafael de Velasco

  • San Rafael de Velasco does not have a bus station of its own.
  • Buses from Santa Cruz and other nearby towns operate from the closest terminal.

Other Options

  • Private transportation or car rentals are also available for those who prefer to travel independently.

How to Get Around in San Rafael de Velasco

San Rafael de Velasco is a small town, so walking is the best way to explore. Taxis are readily available for longer distances.
