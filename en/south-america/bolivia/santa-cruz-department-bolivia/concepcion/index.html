Introduction to Concepción

Located in Bolivia’s Department of Santa Cruz, Concepción is a small town with rich cultural and natural attractions. Nestled amidst lush greenery and surrounded by mountains, this little town is a paradise for nature lovers, history buffs, culture seekers, and adventure enthusiasts.

Concepción’s history dates back to the colonial era, and its architecture, museums, and cultural events reflect the town’s glorious past. The natural attractions, including waterfalls, rivers, and forests, make Concepción a perfect place to explore the great outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Concepción

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iglesia San Rafael de Velasco: This impressive church is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is famous for its exquisite baroque architecture and colorful frescoes.

  • Museo de la Mision: This museum showcases the history of Concepción’s Jesuit Missions and their impact on the region’s culture.

  • Casa de la Cultura: A center for art exhibitions, local craft workshops, and cultural events, Casa de la Cultura is a must-visit spot for anyone interested in the town’s vibrant cultural scene.

Natural Attractions of Concepción: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cuevas de los Parajos: This limestone cave system is a popular destination for thrill-seekers and spelunkers.

  • Cascada del Angel: A beautiful waterfall surrounded by lush greenery, this spot is perfect for picnics, hiking, or just soaking up the natural beauty of Concepción.

  • Reserva Biológica Cordillera Santa Elena: This nature reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including howler monkeys, jaguars, and tapirs. A guided tour through the reserve is highly recommended.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Concepción

Concepción is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with plenty of trails and routes offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and natural beauty.

  • Cerro Paucibamba: A challenging hike up the mountain rewards you with sweeping views of the valley and the town.

  • Quebrada San Isidro: This scenic hike takes you through the lush forest to a clear river and has several natural pools for swimming.

  • La Sima de Huasi: A beginner-friendly hike that takes you to a jaw-droppingly beautiful waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Concepción: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Jesuit Missions of Chiquitos: A guided tour of the famous Jesuit Mission holds significant cultural and religious importance and is a must-visit attraction in Concepción.

  • Horseback Riding: A perfect way to explore the lush green forests and the rugged terrain of Concepción.

  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn the recipe for Bolivia’s famous dish, Pique Macho from the locals themselves.

Food and Drink Scene of Concepción

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pique Macho: A hearty dish made with beef, sausage, and spicy sauce, topped with fries and onions.

  • Sopa de Maní: A creamy peanut soup that is a local favorite.

  • Bolivian Empanadas: A delicious snack stuffed with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Singani: A distilled Bolivian liquor made from grapes and is a popular cocktail ingredient.

  • Api: A warm, sweet drink made from maize flour, cinnamon, and cloves, and is perfect for chilly evenings.

  • Chicha: A fermented corn drink that is a staple in Bolivia’s traditional cuisine.

Where to Stay in Concepción: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Los Alamos: A centrally located hotel with a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar, offering affordable accommodation options.

  • Hostal Plaza Concepción: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of town with friendly staff and comfortable rooms.

  • Casa de la Cultura Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with rustic decor and excellent amenities, including a garden and a terrace.

What to Buy in Concepción: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally-made Crafts: Find handmade pottery, woven baskets, and colorful textiles at local markets and craft shops.

  • Singani: A bottle of authentic Bolivian Singani makes for an excellent gift for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Concepción

The town’s cultural practices and traditions are deeply influenced by the Jesuit missionaries, and their legacy is reflected in the town’s architecture, museums, and festivals. The locals celebrate indigenous festivals, and music and dance play a significant role in their day-to-day lives.

Best Time to Visit Concepción

The best time to visit Concepción is from May to October, when the weather is pleasant, and rainfall is minimal. The town’s climate is subtropical, with frequent rain showers throughout the year, especially during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Concepción

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.

  • Plan your travel route and tours in advance, especially during peak tourist season.

  • Carry essential items like sunscreen, insect repellent, and an umbrella.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities or purchase illegal substances.

  • Wear revealing or offensive clothing in public places.

  • Disrespect the rules and regulations at cultural and natural heritage sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Concepción

Concepción is relatively safe for tourists, but it’s always wise to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and leave valuable items in the hotel safe.

Health in Concepción

It’s advisable to get vaccinated for yellow fever before traveling to Concepción. Additionally, carry your mosquito repellent and take preventive medications to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Concepción: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Concepción

  • Viru Viru International Airport: The nearest international airport is in Santa Cruz de la Sierra, about 320 km from Concepción.

  • Teniente Jorge Henrich Arauz Airport: A small airport located in the town of Trinidad, about 200 km from Concepción.

Bus Stations Near Concepción

  • Ormeno Terminal de Buses: Located in Santa Cruz de la Sierra, from where you can get a bus or taxi to Concepción.

  • Terminal Terrestre de Trinidad: Located in the town of Trinidad, from where you can get a bus or taxi to Concepción.

Other Options

You can rent a car or hire a taxi from Santa Cruz de la Sierra to Concepción, but the roads can be narrow and winding, so it’s better to hire a professional driver.

How to Get Around in Concepción

The best way to get around Concepción is by foot, as the town is relatively small and easy to navigate. For longer trips, you can hire a taxi or a private car.

Where to Go Next from Concepción: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Concepción is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding region’s natural and cultural attractions. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Trinidad: A charming town known for its colonial architecture and Jesuit Missions. Located about 200 km from Concepción.

  • Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: This national park is one of Bolivia’s largest and is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. Located about 500 km from Concepción.

Conclusion

Concepción offers a unique experience for all kinds of travelers, with its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Make sure you plan your trip well in advance so that you can make the most of your time in this hidden Bolivian gem.
