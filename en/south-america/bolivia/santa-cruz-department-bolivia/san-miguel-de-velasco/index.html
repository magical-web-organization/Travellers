Introduction to San Miguel de Velasco

San Miguel de Velasco is a small town located in the Santa Cruz department of Bolivia. It is a hidden gem that is worth exploring for its rich history and culture. The town features well-preserved colonial architecture that dates back to the 16th century, which earned it a spot on the list of Bolivia’s World Heritage Sites.

List of Places to Visit in San Miguel de Velasco

If you’re planning a trip to San Miguel de Velasco, make sure to visit the following:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Miguel de Velasco Church: This beautiful church was built in the 18th century and features Baroque-style architecture. Its interior is adorned with stunning paintings and sculptures.
  • San Rafael de Velasco: This nearby town features colonial architecture, including a 17th-century church that is considered one of the best-preserved in Bolivia.

Natural Attractions of San Miguel de Velasco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amboro National Park: This park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, such as jaguars, tapirs, and various bird species. It offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and camping.
  • Lomas de Arena: This nature reserve features towering sand dunes, which offer a unique landscape and opportunities for sandboarding and dune buggy rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Miguel de Velasco

San Miguel de Velasco is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • San Rafael to San Miguel: This 34-kilometer trail takes you through lush forests and past several colonial churches.
  • Amboro National Park: The park offers a variety of hiking trails that range in difficulty level. The most popular is the Escalera del Diablo, or Devil’s Staircase, a steep trail that leads to a stunning waterfall.

Best Things to Do in San Miguel de Velasco: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, here are some of the best things to do in San Miguel de Velasco:

  • Cultural Tour: A guided tour of the San Miguel de Velasco Church and the town’s colonial architecture is a must-do.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through the surrounding forest and countryside.
  • Traditional Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Bolivian dishes using fresh, local ingredients.

Food and Drink Scene of San Miguel de Velasco

The food scene in San Miguel de Velasco is heavily influenced by the country’s indigenous and Spanish roots. Here are some dishes and beverages you can’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Silpancho: Thinly pounded beef served with potatoes, rice, and fried egg.
  • Sopa de Mani: A creamy peanut soup that is typically served as an appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Singani: A distilled grape spirit that is often enjoyed on its own or mixed into cocktails.
  • Mate de Coca: A tea made from coca leaves that is believed to have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in San Miguel de Velasco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Miguel de Velasco offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious lodges. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hostal San Luis: A basic, no-frills hostel that is centrally located and offers affordable rates.
  • Hacienda El Cafetal: A beautiful lodge that is set in the foothills of the Andes. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in San Miguel de Velasco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some of the best options:

  • Handwoven textiles: San Miguel de Velasco is known for its beautiful handwoven textiles, including blankets, rugs, and clothing.
  • Locally made crafts: You can find a range of crafts made by local artisans, such as pottery, jewelry, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of San Miguel de Velasco

San Miguel de Velasco is a predominantly Catholic town, and many of its customs and traditions reflect this religion’s influence. One of the most significant events in the town is the Fiesta de San Miguel, which takes place in late September. During the festival, there are parades, dances, and traditional food and drink.

Best Time to Visit San Miguel de Velasco

The best time to visit San Miguel de Velasco is from May to October when the weather is dry and sunny. It’s also the time of year when the town celebrates its festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Miguel de Velasco

To avoid any cultural mishaps, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn a few Spanish phrases to help communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public, as it’s considered disrespectful.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Drink the tap water, as it may cause stomach problems.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Miguel de Velasco

Overall, San Miguel de Velasco is a safe town to visit. However, visitors should always exercise caution when traveling, such as securing valuables and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in San Miguel de Velasco

Visitors should be up-to-date on their vaccinations and take precautions to prevent illnesses such as mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever. It’s also important to drink only bottled water and wash fruits and vegetables before eating them.

Getting to San Miguel de Velasco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Miguel de Velasco

The nearest airport to San Miguel de Velasco is the Viru Viru International Airport in Santa Cruz. From there, you can take a bus or hire a car to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near San Miguel de Velasco

The town has a small bus station that offers direct connections to nearby destinations such as Santa Cruz and Sucre.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car from the airport or city center in Santa Cruz.

How to Get Around in San Miguel de Velasco

As the town is relatively small, the best way to get around is on foot. Taxis are also available, but negotiate the fare before getting in.

Where to Go Next from San Miguel de Velasco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Sucre: The constitutional capital of Bolivia, known for its colonial architecture and museums.
  • Samaipata: A small village that is known for its ancient ruins and natural beauty.

Conclusion

San Miguel de Velasco offers a unique blend of colonial architecture, natural beauty, and cultural significance. We hope this travel guide has given you all the necessary information to plan your trip to this hidden gem in Bolivia.
