Introduction to San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos is a charming town located in the Santa Cruz department of Bolivia. It is situated on the eastern side of the country and is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful architecture, and natural beauty. The town is also an important pilgrimage site as it is home to the famous Jesuit Missions of Chiquitos, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, hike and bike routes, activities to do, local food and drink, where to stay, the local culture and customs, safety and health. This guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to San José de Chiquitos.

List of Places to Visit in San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos is a town that has a lot to offer for visitors, from visiting culturally important museums to witnessing breathtaking natural beauty. Here are some of the locations that you should not miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jesuit Missions of Chiquitos - This UNESCO World Heritage site was established by Spanish explorers in the 16th century. It comprises a group of six baroque churches located in Chiquitos, which is a town established by the Jesuits.

  • Santa Cruz de la Sierra Cathedral - This cathedral is located in the heart of the city and is considered a work of art. It has a beautiful colonial architecture and is a perfect place to experience some tranquility.

  • San José de Chiquitos Church - This colonial-style church is the most important religious site in the town and is also famous for its beautiful bell tower.

Natural Attractions of San José de Chiquitos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Noel Kempff Mercado National Park - This National Park offers breathtaking scenery, including waterfalls, mountains, and forests. It is home to more than 4,000 species of plants, 300 bird species, and 100 mammal species, making it a brilliant place for nature lovers.

  • Agua Caliente - This hot spring is a great place to relax and unwind after a day of adventure. It is a peaceful place surrounded by nature and is known to have therapeutic qualities.

  • Serranía de Chiquitos - This region provides visitors with an opportunity to experience the tropical dry forest ecosystem of Bolivia. It has a vast area full of biodiversity in its flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos is a town surrounded by beautiful landscapes, making it an ideal place for hiking and biking lovers. Here are some of the suggested trails and routes.

  • San José to Chochís Trek - This hike is 120 kilometers long and takes around five days to complete. You will be able to experience the natural beauty of the Noel Kempff Mercado National Park.

  • San José Mountain Biking Trail - This trail is ideal for mountain bikers and is located near the town. It is around 35 kilometers long and takes around 4 hours to complete and offers stunning views.

Best Things to Do in San José de Chiquitos: Activities and Tours

San José de Chiquitos is a location full of history and natural beauty, which offers various activities that any tourist can enjoy.

  • Birdwatching - San José de Chiquitos offers a variety of bird species and is an ideal location for birdwatching. You can enjoy watching species like the white-backed stilt, spotted sandpiper and black-necked stilt.

  • Explore Jesuit Missions - You can visit the original six Jesuit Missions dating from 1691 to 1760. They are located near Chiquitos and are a must-visit for history lovers.

  • Horse Riding - San José de Chiquitos is also famous for horse riding tours, where you can explore the surrounding areas on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for San José de Chiquitos

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring the best that San José de Chiquitos has to offer.

Day 1: Arrive, check-in at your hotel, and rest. Day 2: Visit Santa Cruz de la Sierra Cathedral and explore the town. Day 3: Visit Serranía de Chiquitos and go hiking. Day 4: Visit Jesuit Missions and explore Chiquitos. Day 5: Explore the Noel Kempff Mercado National Park. Day 6: Go horse riding and visit Agua Caliente hot springs. Day 7: Relax in the town and explore local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos has a unique cuisine known for its delicious flavors and unique spices. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Majao - A rice dish mixed with beef or chicken broth and spices. It is typically served with fried plantains, egg, and salad.

  • Crunchy pork - Pork chops marinated and served crispy.

  • Charque - It is a dehydrated meat pork or beef preserved with salt.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha - A traditional Bolivian fermented corn drink.

  • Singani - A distilled Bolivian spirit made from white Muscat of Alexandria grapes.

Where to Stay in San José de Chiquitos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San José de Chiquitos has a range of accommodation options to choose from, catering for different budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay.

  • Hotel de Misiones - A charming hotel located in the heart of the town.

  • Los Tucanes Hotel - A reasonable option that provides great services and comfortable rooms.

  • Posada de la Abuela - A budget guesthouse ideal for travelers who want to experience the local culture and enjoy a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in San José de Chiquitos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San José de Chiquitos offers a range of unique souvenirs, which are perfect for bringing home.

  • Handicrafts - The town specializes in making different handicrafts made of wood, cane, and leather.

  • Singani - You can bring home a bottle of this traditional Bolivian liquor and enjoy it with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos is a religious town, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions. It is home to a variety of indigenous communities, and visitors should take note of their customs, beliefs, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit San José de Chiquitos

The best time to visit San José de Chiquitos is during the dry season, from May to October, as the weather is warm, and there is less rainfall. However, it is also worth noting that this is the peak season, and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of San José de Chiquitos

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious places.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that may harm the environment or wildlife.

  • Disrespect the local culture, traditions, and beliefs.

  • Litter – always dispose of waste responsibly.

Safety and Health

Safety in San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos is a safe town for tourists, but it is advisable to take the necessary precautions, just as you would anywhere else. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and do not leave valuables unattended.

Health in San José de Chiquitos

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to stay healthy while traveling to San José de Chiquitos. It is recommended to drink bottled water, and to use sunscreen to protect against the sun. It is also advisable to get vaccinated against Yellow Fever, Hepatitis A and B, and Typhoid fever.

Getting to San José de Chiquitos: Transportation Options and Logistics

San José de Chiquitos is accessible via various means of transport. Here is a guide on how to get there.

Airports Near San José de Chiquitos

The closest airport to San José de Chiquitos is Viru Viru International Airport in Santa Cruz, which is around 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near San José de Chiquitos

The major terminal in San José de Chiquitos is the Jodanga Bus Terminal, which connects to major towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or use a rental car to travel from Santa Cruz to San José de Chiquitos.

How to Get Around in San José de Chiquitos

San José de Chiquitos is a small town, and visitors can navigate the town by foot. Alternatively, you can rent a bicycle, which is a popular option and is available for hire in many places.

Where to Go Next from San José de Chiquitos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San José de Chiquitos is surrounded by beautiful destinations worth exploring. Here are some of the nearby destinations:

  • Santa Cruz - The largest city in Bolivia offers visitors a range of activities such as shopping, eating in great restaurants, and visiting famous landmarks.

  • Samaipata - Visit this historical town and explore its ancient ruins, such as El Fuerte, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Conclusion

San José de Chiquitos is a beautiful and charming town that offers visitors a unique experience. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, it is a must-visit location while traveling in Bolivia. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has been helpful in planning your trip to San José de Chiquitos. Bon voyage!
