Introduction to Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Located in the Santa Cruz department of Bolivia in South America, Noel Kempff Mercado National Park is a vast reserve spanning approximately 15,000 square miles at the eastern edge of the Andes Mountains. The park is internationally recognized for its biodiversity and is home to over 4,000 species of plants, 139 species of mammals, and 620 species of birds.

Named after Noel Kempff Mercado, a Bolivian biologist who dedicated his life to preserving the country’s natural resources, the park features a stunning array of landscapes, including vast savannas, pristine rivers, towering waterfalls, and dense forests. Exploring this natural wonderland promises to be an unforgettable adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. San Ignacio de Velasco

Founded in 1748, San Ignacio de Velasco is a small colonial town located on the eastern edge of the park. The town is home to several historic buildings, including the San Ignacio de Loyola church, which is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

2. Fortin Mborore

Fortin Mborore is an archaeological site where you can find the remains of a Jesuit mission that was established in the 18th century. The site is located on the shores of the Iténez River and offers a glimpse into Bolivia’s rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Angel Falls

Angel Falls is a stunning waterfall that cascades over 250 feet into the Churún River. The falls are located in the southern part of the park and can only be reached by boat or on foot.

2. Kaa-Iya del Gran Chaco National Park and Integrated Management Natural Area

Kaa-Iya del Gran Chaco National Park and Integrated Management Natural Area is a neighboring natural reserve that is also worth exploring. The reserve is home to jaguars, giant anteaters, and other impressive wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Pampas del Heath Trail

This route is a six-day trek that takes visitors through a pristine grassland ecosystem. Along the way, you’ll have the opportunity to see capybaras, collared peccaries, and giant anteaters.

Ciruelo Trail

The Ciruelo Trail takes hikers through the park’s dense forest and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. The trail is approximately 20 miles long and takes around four days to complete.

Best Things to Do in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: Activities and Tours

Birdwatching

Noel Kempff Mercado National Park is a birdwatcher’s paradise. With over 620 species of birds, including the elusive Harpy Eagle, finding unique species is a breeze.

Boat Tours

Exploring the park’s rivers is a must-do activity. Boat tours can take visitors deep into the heart of the park’s wildest areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Day 1: Arrive in the park’s gateway city of Santa Cruz

Day 2-3: Explore San Ignacio de Velasco and Fortin Mborore

Day 4-5: Embark on a multi-day hike along the Ciruelo Trail

Day 6-7: Take a guided boat tour along the Churún River to see Angel Falls and other natural wonders

Food and Drink Scene of Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Must-Try Dishes

1. Chuño A traditional Andean dish made from potatoes that have been freeze-dried in the sun.

2. Charque A dish made from Andean beef that has been sun-dried and then cooked.

Local Beverages

1. Singani A grape-based distilled spirit that is Bolivia’s national liquor.

2. Yerba Mate A traditional South American drink made from dried leaves of the yerba mate plant.

Where to Stay in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Camping is a popular lodging option for visitors to Noel Kempff Mercado National Park. However, the nearby cities of Santa Cruz and San Ignacio de Velasco offer a range of hotels and lodges for those seeking more comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Noel Kempff Mercado National Park can purchase locally-made handicrafts, such as woven hats and bags, and traditional Bolivian textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

The park is home to several indigenous communities, including the Ayoreo and Guarani people. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the traditions and customs of these communities.

Best Time to Visit Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

The park is open year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season from May to October when the weather is cooler and dryer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural habitats.

  • Follow Leave No Trace principles when camping or hiking in the park.

  • Learn about and respect the customs of the indigenous communities in the park.

Don’t:

  • Leave any trash behind.

  • Harm or disturb any wildlife in the park.

  • Take any souvenirs from the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Visitors to the park should be aware of the potential dangers of traveling through remote areas, such as exposure to the elements and wildlife encounters.

Health in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Visitors should take steps to prevent mosquito bites and be aware of the risk of diseases such as malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

The closest airport is the Viru Viru International Airport in Santa Cruz, which is approximately 700 miles from the park.

Bus Stations Near Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

Buses to San Ignacio de Velasco, the gateway to the park, depart from Santa Cruz.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a private car or take a guided tour to explore the park.

How to Get Around in Noel Kempff Mercado National Park

The park’s vast size and rugged terrain make it best explored on foot or by boat. Private vehicles are not permitted in the park.

Where to Go Next from Noel Kempff Mercado National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby Kaa-Iya del Gran Chaco National Park and Integrated Management Natural Area is a natural reserve that is also worth exploring.

Conclusion

Noel Kempff Mercado National Park is a true gem of Bolivia’s natural heritage. Whether you’re a nature lover or an adventure seeker, exploring this park promises to be a once-in-a-lifetime experience. Make sure to respect the park’s wildlife and traditions, and leave nothing behind but memories.
