Santa Ana de Velasco Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Santa Ana de Velasco is a charming town located in the Santa Cruz department of Bolivia, South America. This hidden gem is known for its unique blend of indigenous and Spanish colonial architecture, which appears in the grandeur of the church, homes, and other buildings. In addition to its rich cultural heritage, the town’s natural beauty often leaves visitors in awe. With so much to offer, this article will provide an ultimate guide to Santa Ana de Velasco to help you make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Santa Ana de Velasco

Santa Ana de Velasco is filled with historical significance, which has earned it a place as a World Heritage Site. Its history dates back to the Jesuit Missionaries, who founded it in the early 1700s. The Jesuits’ primary objective was to Christianize the indigenous population by drawing them into the mission community. Santa Ana de Velasco was one of the eight Jesuit missions in Bolivia; it was a hub of religious and cultural activity, and it still maintains this status today.

Visitors wishing to experience a unique blend of history and culture can explore the town’s landmark buildings — the church, the convent, and sugar mills. There are also several cultural festivals throughout the year that offer an excellent opportunity to engage with the local community.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Ana de Velasco

Below is a comprehensive list of places to visit in Santa Ana de Velasco, categorized into two sections:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santa Ana de Velasco Church: As the town’s centerpiece, this church boasts a breathtaking altar piece, which is a must-see.
  • Convent of Santa Ana de Velasco: This convent offers a glimpse of religious life during the era of the Jesuit Missions.
  • The Sugar Mills: The sugar mills were a vital part of the Jesuits’ agricultural system. They are still operational and provide a captivating insight into the town’s agricultural heritage.
  • Werner Guttentag Museum: The museum houses numerous items of historical and cultural significance, including indigenous art, clothing, and antiques.

Natural Attractions of Santa Ana de Velasco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mirador: This hilltop lookout offers spectacular panoramic views of Santa Ana de Velasco and its surroundings.
  • Casa Grande: The Casa Grande property is an exceptional place to experience natural beauty and tranquillity. It features ornamental gardens, a freshwater spring, and a serene lake. Visitors can also join a guided birdwatching tour here.
  • Las Trincheras Forest: This subtropical forest is home to various wildlife species, including tucans, parrots, and howler monkeys. Guided tours are available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Ana de Velasco

Cycling and hiking are fantastic ways to explore Santa Ana de Velasco’s natural surroundings. A few recommended routes include:

  • Casa Grande Trails: The Casa Grande property offers several bike trails for all skill levels.
  • Mirador Loop: This is a moderate hike that takes approximately 2-3 hours to complete. It starts at the town center, circles around the hills, and ends at the Mirador lookout.
  • Grutas de Laura Trail: This trail takes hikers to the nearby Grutas de Laura cave system, a natural wonder in the Santa Cruz region.

Best Things to Do in Santa Ana de Velasco: Activities and Tours

There is a range of activities to choose from in Santa Ana de Velasco. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Tours: Several tour companies offer guided tours, including cultural, hiking, and wildlife tours.
  • Biking: Biking is a popular activity in Santa Ana de Velasco. Bikes are available for rent.
  • Bird Watching: Santa Ana de Velasco has over 270 bird species, making it a hub for bird watchers.
  • Festivals: Santa Ana de Velasco has a variety of cultural festivals. Most of them are religiously oriented and can be a fascinating attraction if you’re interested in learning about local customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Ana de Velasco

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Santa Ana de Velasco:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive and spend the day exploring the town center and local museums.
Day 2 Visit the sugar mills and take a guided tour of the Casa Grande property.
Day 3 Go on a bird-watching tour and then relax at the lake within the Casa Grande property.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Ana de Velasco

Apart from the cultural attractions and activities, Santa Ana de Velasco boasts a unique food and drink scene. The town’s diverse culinary delights encompass a blend of European, indigenous flavours making them unique.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ají de fideo: This soup’s key ingredient is noodles in a spicy and flavourful sauce that is typical Bolivian cuisine.
  • Picante de arroz: Seasoned rice with chicken, chili, and other spices.
  • Locro: A thick, hearty soup made with beans, corn, and beef.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This drink is a traditional Bolivian fermented drink made with corn or quinoa.
  • Singani: Another traditional Bolivian drink with a grape brandy base that is often mixed in with other beverages.
  • Api: A hot drink made from purple maize and sweetened with cinnamon or sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Ana de Velasco

Santa Ana de Velasco is a town steeped in culture, and Bolivian custom blossoms from it. There are several customs that visitors should take note of.

  • Greeting: Always greet people with a handshake and a smile to engage in common humanness.
  • Language: Most residents speak Spanish, but the local indigenous language, Guarani, is also prevalent.
  • Attire: Dress modestly, preferably in long pants and a blouse or collared shirt.
  • Religion: Santa Ana de Velasco is firmly Catholic, making religion a significant part of everyday life.

Best Time to Visit Santa Ana de Velasco

The best time to visit Santa Ana de Velasco is during the dry season, from April to October. The weather is milder, and outdoor activities and tours are easier to enjoy. It’s essential to keep in mind that temperatures can vary from day to night, so warm clothing is necessary, especially if you plan to spend a lot of time outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Ana de Velasco

Do:

  • Respect the town’s cultural heritage: Many buildings in Santa Ana de Velasco are historic, so be respectful and mindful of your surroundings while touring.
  • Engage with the local community: Be open to meeting new people and experiencing new traditions.
  • Learn Spanish: Knowing at least the basic Spanish words will go a long way and improve your travel experience.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission: When taking photos of sacred places or people, seek permission first.
  • Ignore the town’s customs and traditions: Bolivians take pride in their customs and traditions. Always be respectful to avoid offending the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Ana de Velasco

Santa Ana de Velasco is generally a safe town, but it is still essential to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Small instances of crime, such as pickpocketing or bag-snatching, are not uncommon.

Health in Santa Ana de Velasco

Before visiting, it is crucial for international visitors to check-in with their healthcare provider and review the current recommended vaccinations for Santa Ana de Velasco. Yellow fever and Hepatitis A are two common vaccine recommendations.

Getting to Santa Ana de Velasco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Ana de Velasco

Santa Ana de Velasco is not currently served by any airport. The nearest airport is Viru Viru International Airport in Santa Cruz, which is about 170 miles northeast of the town.

Bus Stations Near Santa Ana de Velasco

Visitors to Santa Ana de Velasco can take public transport in an express or local bus. The town is situated near the new highway that connects San Ignacio de Velasco to Santa Cruz.

Other Options

Taxis are also available in Santa Cruz, or you can rent a car to explore Santa Ana de Velasco at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Santa Ana de Velasco

While walking is an excellent way to explore Santa Ana de Velasco’s town center, the town’s outskirts and nearby attractions require motorized transport. Taxis and rental cars are readily available and affordable.

Where to Go Next from Santa Ana de Velasco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can experience after exploring Santa Ana de Velasco. A few options include:

  • San Ignacio de Velasco: This well-preserved colonial town is located just a few miles from Santa Ana de Velasco.
  • Amboro National Park: Located approximately 50 miles from Santa Ana de Velasco, this park offers fantastic opportunities to explore Amazonian flora and fauna.
  • Santa Cruz: Bolivia’s largest city is situated about 170 miles northeast of Santa Ana de Velasco and is worth a visit for its lively atmosphere and urban attractions.

Conclusion

Santa Ana de Velasco maintains a rich cultural heritage that is worth exploring. With this tour guide, you have an excellent starting point for planning your trip to the town, including what places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and more. Remember to be cautious, respectful, and above all, to enjoy the beautiful historic town of Santa Ana de Velasco to the fullest!
