Introduction to Salta

Salta, located in the Andean Northwest region of Argentina, is a haven for travelers seeking adventure, natural beauty, and cultural immersion. The city is known for its colonial architecture, lively culture, and friendly locals. Salta’s stunning landscapes, including the Andes Mountains, Salt Flats, and lush forests, provide the perfect backdrop for outdoor adventures. Moreover, the city offers a variety of museums, art galleries, and historical sites that offer a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Salta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salta is a treasure trove of historical and cultural attractions. The following are some of the must-visit places:

  • Museo de Arqueología de Alta Montaña (MAAM): This museum exhibits the mummified remains of three Inca children, discovered near the summit of Llullaillaco Volcano. The exhibition is a window into the ancient Inca civilization.

  • Catedral de Salta: Built in the 19th century, this cathedral is an excellent example of the neoclassical architectural style. The cathedral is located in the central square of the city and features beautiful stained-glass windows.

  • Cerro San Bernardo: Located on the outskirts of the city, the hill is accessible by cable car and offers a breathtaking view of Salta and the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Salta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salta is home to some of the most scenic and awe-inspiring natural attractions in Argentina. The following are some of the must-visit places:

  • Salinas Grandes: The Salt Flats, located between Salta and Jujuy provinces, is a vast expanse of blinding white salt, interrupted only by the blue sky and distant mountains.

  • Quebrada de Humahuaca: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a narrow mountain valley with multi-colored mountains and rock formations. The area is popular for its trekking trails and traditional indigenous villages.

  • Parque Nacional Los Cardones: This national park is home to the giant cactus, one of the largest cactus species in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salta

Salta offers a variety of outdoor activities for adventure enthusiasts. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Travesía de los Cerros: This 3-day trek, which starts in Salta, passes through dense forests, lush green valleys, and high-altitude meadows.

  • Road to the Clouds: The road passes through the Andes Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Quebrada de Escoipe: This trek starts at the bottom of Quebrada del Toro and goes through the Escoipe canyon, passing through stunning views of the canyon and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Salta: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Salta offers a variety of activities and tours. The following are some of the best things to do:

  • Wine Tours: Salta’s wine region is known for its high-altitude vineyards, which produce some of Argentina’s best wines.

  • Horseback Riding: There are several horseback riding tours available in Salta, offering a unique view of the surrounding landscape,

  • Mountain Biking: Salta’s rugged terrain and challenging trails make it an ideal location for mountain biking enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salta

The following is a suggested week-long itinerary for Salta:

Day 1-2: Explore the city of Salta, visit local museums and historical sites, and taste local cuisine.

Day 3: Take a tour of Quebrada de Humahuaca.

Day 4-5: Visit the town of Cafayate, known for its vineyards and wine production.

Day 6-7: Take a trek to the Salinas Grandes Salt Flats.

Food and Drink Scene of Salta

Must-Try Dishes

Salta is known for its hearty cuisine, with flavors influenced by indigenous cultures and European settlers. The following are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Locro: A traditional stew made with corn, beans, meat, and a variety of vegetables.

  • Empanadas: Baked or fried stuffed pastry, filled with a variety of meats, vegetables, or cheese.

  • Humita: A savory dish made of fresh corn husks filled with a mixture of creamed corn, garlic, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Torrontés: A white wine variety unique to Argentina, with a floral aroma and crisp acidity.

  • Fernet con coca: A popular herbal liqueur served with Coca-Cola.

Where to Stay in Salta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salta offers a variety of accommodation options to fit all budgets and preferences. The following are some of the best places to stay:

  • House of Jasmines: A luxurious country house located in the middle of a private estate in El Encón.

  • La Candela: A charming boutique hotel, located in the historic city center of Salta.

  • Backpackers Hostel: Offers affordable accommodation and a great location near the city center.

What to Buy in Salta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salta offers a variety of artisanal products, from textiles and pottery to handcrafted instruments and carved wooden souvenirs. The following are some of the best things to buy in Salta:

  • Ponchos: A traditional wool garment with vibrant colors and patterns.

  • Mate: A traditional herbal tea consumed in Argentina and other South American countries.

Local Culture and Customs of Salta

Salta has a rich cultural heritage, heavily influenced by its indigenous roots and European settlers. The locals are warm and welcoming, and a visit to Salta is an opportunity to immerse yourself in their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Salta

The best time to visit Salta is from May to September when the weather is dry and sunny. October and November are also good months to visit, as the flowers are in bloom, and the weather is still favorable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salta

Do:

  • Sample local cuisine and beverages.

  • Learn about the local culture and customs.

  • Take adequate precautions before engaging in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Touch or remove artifacts from archaeological sites/artifacts

  • Take pictures of people without their consent.

  • Leave valuables in the open and unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salta

Salta is generally safe for tourists. However, precautionary measures should be taken when traveling alone or at night. It is always advisable to take a registered taxi or use a transportation service to travel around the city.

Health in Salta

Travelers should be up-to-date with their routine vaccinations before traveling to Salta. It is also recommended to get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever, depending on the length and nature of your trip.

Getting to Salta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salta

The city is served by Martín Miguel de Güemes International Airport, which is located 6 miles from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Salta

The long-distance bus terminal is located on Avenida Hipólito Yrigoyen, near the city center.

Other Options

Private transportation services and rental cars are available from the airport or city center.

How to Get Around in Salta

Salta’s city center is small and walkable, and exploring the city by foot is easy. For longer distances, you can use the local bus service or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Salta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The following are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can take from Salta:

  • Cachi: A small, picturesque town located in the Calchaquí Valley, known for its colonial architecture and beautiful landscapes.

  • Jujuy: The provincial capital of Jujuy, known for its Quebrada de Humahuaca, indigenous towns, and stunning natural beauty.

  • Iruya: A charming town located in the heart of the Andes Mountains.

Conclusion

Salta is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking adventure, natural beauty, and cultural immersion. The city’s stunning landscapes, historical sites, and delicious cuisine make it an unforgettable experience. Plan your trip to Salta now and explore the best that Argentina has to offer!
