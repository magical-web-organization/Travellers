El Rey National Park Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

El Rey National Park is a breathtaking oasis in the heart of Argentina’s Andean Northwest. It is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Hike amongst the mountains, forests, and rivers. Take in the scenic views of the landscape, which is rich in flora and fauna. The park is also home to significant cultural and historical landmarks that showcase the area’s cultural heritage. If you’re planning to visit El Rey National Park, this comprehensive guide will help you chart your visit.

Introduction to El Rey National Park

El Rey National Park is located in the Salta province of Argentina. It is a 100 sq km park filled with spectacular scenery, including rugged mountains, lush forests, stunning rivers, and waterfalls. Within the park, visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities and the chance to experience the rich history and culture of the area.

List of Places to Visit in El Rey National Park

The following are some of the best places to visit in El Rey National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The El Rey Archaeological Site - The site includes Inca ruins, many of which date back before the fifteenth century. It was once a key place for the Incas, who used it as a stronghold.
  2. The Museum of Contemporary Art of Salta - An interesting museum that showcases the work of contemporary Argentinian artists.
  3. The Museum of High Mountain Archaeology - The museum features exhibits about the ancient cultural heritage of the Andean highlands, with a particular focus on the Inca Empire.

Natural Attractions of El Rey National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Condor viewpoint - This hilltop viewpoint offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and the chance to spot the majestic Andean Condor.
  2. Yaguareté trail - A hiking trail through the forests with many beautiful scenic spots for picnicking, pitching a tent, and crossing streams.
  3. Luna circle - A hike on a path that circles the hill of Maldonado offering panoramic views of the valley and the river that runs about it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Rey National Park

The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in El Rey National Park:

  1. Rio de Los Patos - A challenging but rewarding hike up the river through mountain valleys and passes. Suitable for experienced hikers.
  2. Los Tres Frailes - The hike offers fantastic views of the surrounding landscape, including the three peaks of Los Tres Frailes.
  3. Garganta del diablo - A multi-day trek through a deep canyon with breathtaking scenery and the chance to spot wildlife.

Best Things to Do in El Rey National Park: Activities and Tours

There are so many things to do in El Rey National Park. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  1. Bird watching - The park is home to over 200 species of birds, including the Andean Condor, the majestic bird with a wingspan of up to 3 meters and the colorful Collared Inca Hummingbird.
  2. Rafting - The park has several rivers, with Rio de Los Patos being the most popular for rafting enthusiasts.
  3. Horseback riding - Visitors can explore the park by following ancient Inca trails on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Rey National Park

Day 1: Visit the El Rey Archaeological Site and the Museum of High Mountain Archaeology. Day 2: Hike the Rio de Los Patos trail. Day 3: Tour the Yaguareté trail. Day 4: Visit Condor Viewpoint and have a picnic. Day 5: Explore the Luna circle. Day 6: Go on a horseback riding trip and visit local villages that showcase the area’s rich tradition. Day 7: Go rafting on one of the rivers of the National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of El Rey National Park

El Rey National Park has a vibrant and diverse food and drink scene, with local cuisine and traditional dishes meeting international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Empanadas - A classic dish from Argentina, which is a pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  2. Humita - A sweet or savory corn dish which can be wrapped in the leaves of the corn plant before being cooked.
  3. Tamales - Another classic South American dish, Tamales is made from steamed corn dough wrapped around a filling.

Local Beverages

  1. Argentinian Malbec - Malbec is a popular wine variety produced in Argentina.
  2. Mate - The national drink of Argentina, is a caffeine-rich tea made from the leaves of the yerba mate plant.

Where to Stay in El Rey National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options available for visitors looking to stay in and around El Rey National Park. The choices include hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Some of the most popular options include:

  1. El Rey National Park Lodges - A collection of lodges and campsites located throughout the park.
  2. Cabañas Del Rey - A stylish and comfortable hotel located near to the park that offers a range of amenities.
  3. Hostel del Valle - A budget-friendly hostel located near the Salta city center, with easy access to the park.

What to Buy in El Rey National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to El Rey National Park can take home a slice of the area’s rich cultural heritage by investing in souvenirs or local products.

Some of the most popular items to buy include:

  1. Handicrafts - The region is home to many talented artists and craftsmen, known for producing unique and striking textiles, ceramics, and woodwork.
  2. Traditional Artifacts - Visitors can buy traditional and handmade items such as antiques, clothing, and textiles at the local markets.
  3. Local Products - Argentina is famous for producing quality wines, cheese, and olive oils, all of which make great souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of El Rey National Park

The local culture of El Rey National Park is a blend of Spanish, Indigenous, and Andean influences. The area has a rich tradition of music, dance, and arts & crafts.

Best Time to Visit El Rey National Park

The best time to visit El Rey National Park is between November and April, which is the dry season and perfect for outdoor activities. During the wet season, from May to October, some of the areas may not be accessible due to overflowing rivers and floods. Temperatures are generally mild throughout the year, and visitors need to be prepared for cold nights throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Rey National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Take care of the environment.
  3. Dress appropriately and carry necessary gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or cause damage to nature.
  2. Engage in any activities that may be harmful to wildlife.
  3. Ignore park rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Rey National Park

While El Rey National Park is generally a safe destination, visitors should be aware of the following:

  1. Take care while hiking or biking - Some of the trails can be challenging and may require experience.
  2. Carry essential safety equipment - Visitors should carry first-aid kits, maps, and communication devices.
  3. Avoid traveling alone - Visitors should avoid traveling alone and remain in groups when possible.

Health in El Rey National Park

Visitors should take care of their health while visiting El Rey National Park. Here are some tips:

  1. Drink plenty of water - Argentina is known for its high altitude and dry climate. Visitors should stay hydrated at all times.
  2. Protect yourself against bug bites - Visitors should wear long-sleeved shirts and trousers to cover their skin and use insect repellent.
  3. Use adequate sunscreen lotion - sunscreen lotion with an SPF of at least 30 is highly recommended because of no cloud cover in some parts of the year.

Getting to El Rey National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

El Rey National Park can be reached by different modes of transport, including air, bus, and private vehicle.

Airports Near El Rey National Park

The closest airports to El Rey National Park are the Salta and Jujuy airports.

Bus Stations Near El Rey National Park

The most convenient bus stations serving the park are Salta and Jujuy bus stations.

Other Options

Tourists rentals are available in nearby Villages, smaller towns with guesthouses.

How to Get Around in El Rey National Park

The best ways to get around El Rey National Park are by hiking, biking, and horseback riding. There are also various tour operators in the area that offer guided tours and transportation services.

Where to Go Next from El Rey National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

You can explore the nearby attractions and destinations after visiting El Rey National Park. Visit the historic mining towns of Mendoza and Cachi or go sightseeing in the picturesque Salta City.

Conclusion

El Rey National Park in Argentina is the perfect destination for those who love the great outdoors, culture, and heritage. With a wide range of activities, museums, and natural attractions, visitors can enjoy the experience of a lifetime. Plan your visit with our guide above to get the best of your trip.
