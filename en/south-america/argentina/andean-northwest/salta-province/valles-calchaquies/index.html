Introduction to Valles Calchaquíes

Nestled in the Andean Northwest of Argentina, Valles Calchaquíes is a picturesque valley that offers travelers a unique blend of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. The valley spans across the provinces of Salta and Tucumán, with a total area of approximately 20,000 square kilometers.

List of Places to Visit in Valles Calchaquíes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The cultural heritage of Valles Calchaquíes is rich and diverse, with a blend of ancient indigenous communities, colonial history and modern-day influences. Some of the top places to visit for history and culture buffs include:

  • Pucará de Tilcara: An archaeological site that dates back to the pre-Inca period, featuring the remains of an impressive fortified structure.
  • Museum of High Altitude Archaeology: Showcasing the mummified remains of three Inca children found on Mount Llullaillaco.
  • Cafayate Museum: Housing a collection of pre-Columbian artifacts, fossils, and paintings.
  • Quebrada de las Conchas: A scenic drive that takes you through a series of stunning rock formations, including the famous Garganta del Diablo (Devil’s Throat).

Natural Attractions of Valles Calchaquíes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Valles Calchaquíes boasts an impressive array of natural wonders, including towering mountains, lush valleys, and glistening lakes. Outdoor enthusiasts will be spoilt for choice with the range of activities on offer such as:

  • Hiking to the summit of Mount San Bernardo: A challenging but rewarding climb that offers panoramic views of the valley below.
  • Rafting on the Juramento River: Navigate through the rapids of this picturesque river while taking in the stunning scenery.
  • Parque Nacional Los Cardones: Experience the otherworldly landscapes of this national park, dotted with towering cactus species and unique geological formations.
  • Valle de la Luna (Moon Valley): Located just outside of San Juan, this geological formation is reminiscent of the moon’s surface.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valles Calchaquíes

Valles Calchaquíes is a hiker’s and mountain biker’s paradise. Some of the top routes include:

  • Quebrada de las Flechas: A scenic drive that can also be explored by bike or foot, offering breathtaking views of jagged rock formations.
  • Cuesta del Obispo: A winding road that reaches an altitude of over 3,000 meters, with scenic vistas of the valley below.
  • Parque Nacional El Rey: With over 36,000 hectares of wilderness, this park offers a range of hiking trails suitable for all skill levels.
  • Laguna de Brealito: A picturesque lake that can be reached by a short hike, offering a tranquil setting for a picnic or a swim.

Best Things to Do in Valles Calchaquíes: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities to keep visitors entertained in Valles Calchaquíes. Some of the best experiences include:

  • Wine Tasting in Cafayate: The region is known for its world-class wines, with vineyards dotted throughout the valley offering tours and tastings.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the valley on horseback and take in the breathtaking scenery at a leisurely pace.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the valley from a unique perspective with a hot air balloon tour, offering stunning panoramic views.
  • Stargazing: With minimal light pollution, Valles Calchaquíes is the perfect destination for astronomy enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valles Calchaquíes

For those looking to make the most of their visit to Valles Calchaquíes, here is a sample week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Salta City, visit the MAAM museum and enjoy traditional Argentine cuisine.
  • Day 2: Hike to the summit of Cerro San Bernardo for panoramic views of the city.
  • Day 3: Drive to Cafayate and tour the local wineries.
  • Day 4: Visit the Quebrada de las Conchas for a scenic drive and hike.
  • Day 5: Explore the towns of Cachi and Molinos for a taste of colonial history.
  • Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the views of the valley from above.
  • Day 7: Relax and take a leisurely horseback ride through the valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Valles Calchaquíes

Argentina is known for its world-renowned cuisine, and Valles Calchaquíes is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanadas Salteñas: A traditional Argentine pastry filled with meat, potatoes, and spices.
  • Locro: A hearty stew made with corn, beans, meat, and vegetables.
  • Asado: A traditional barbecue featuring cuts of beef, sausages, and other grilled meats.
  • Humitas: A savory corn cake wrapped in a corn husk.

Local Beverages

  • Torrontés: A white wine grape variety grown exclusively in Argentina, known for its floral and fruity notes.
  • Fernet: An aromatic liqueur commonly mixed with Coca-Cola in Argentina.
  • Mate: A popular Argentine herbal drink brewed from the leaves of the yerba mate plant.

Where to Stay in Valles Calchaquíes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valles Calchaquíes offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top choices include:

  • Grace Cafayate: A luxury hotel and spa set amidst the vineyards of Cafayate.
  • Hostel Don Benito: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Salta City.
  • Hacienda de Molinos: A historic guesthouse in the town of Molinos, featuring colonial architecture and charming courtyards.
  • La Merced del Alto: A boutique hotel and spa located in a stunning natural setting in Cachi.

What to Buy in Valles Calchaquíes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Valles Calchaquíes is known for its unique handicrafts and local artisan products. Some of the top items to bring home include:

  • Ponchos: A traditional Argentine garment made from wool or alpaca.
  • Handmade Pottery: Featuring intricate designs and patterns, these make for beautiful decorative pieces.
  • Alpaca Wool Products: Soft and warm, alpaca products are a popular souvenir for those visiting the region.
  • Andean Musical Instruments: Including traditional pan flutes and charangos (a small guitar-like instrument).

Local Culture and Customs of Valles Calchaquíes

Valles Calchaquíes has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of indigenous communities, colonial history, and modern-day influences. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Some of the local customs include:

  • Greeting with a kiss on the cheek: It is common to greet acquaintances and friends with a kiss on the cheek.
  • Siesta: The culture in Valles Calchaquíes is leisurely, and it is not uncommon to take a mid-day nap.
  • Pachamama: The indigenous communities in the region pay tribute to Mother Earth by performing rituals and offerings to Pachamama.

Best Time to Visit Valles Calchaquíes

The best time to visit Valles Calchaquíes is during the dry season, from May to October. The weather is mild, and the skies are typically clear, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valles Calchaquíes

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few words of Spanish to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone speaks English.
  • Litter or damage natural landmarks.
  • Overpack – pack light and be prepared for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valles Calchaquíes

Argentina is overall a safe country for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Avoiding unlicensed taxi services.
  • Keeping valuables secure and not leaving them unattended.
  • Staying in well-lit and populated areas.

Health in Valles Calchaquíes

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Valles Calchaquíes, but visitors may want to stick to bottled water to be on the safe side. It is also recommended to bring insect repellent, as there is a risk of mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Valles Calchaquíes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Valles Calchaquíes

The closest airport to Valles Calchaquíes is Salta International Airport. From the airport, visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use public transportation to reach Valles Calchaquíes.

Bus Stations Near Valles Calchaquíes

Salta City has several bus stations connecting the region to other destinations in Argentina, including Buenos Aires and Mendoza.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private drivers or take guided tours to explore Valles Calchaquíes and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Valles Calchaquíes

The best way to get around Valles Calchaquíes is by car, taxi, or guided tour. Visitors should note that some of the roads in the region may be unpaved, so it is recommended to rent a 4x4 vehicle if planning to explore off the beaten path.

Where to Go Next from Valles Calchaquíes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Valles Calchaquíes include:

  • Purmamarca: A quaint town located at the base of the famous Seven Colored Hill, with a vibrant artisan market.
  • Tafí del Valle: A scenic town located in the province of Tucumán, offering hiking, horseback riding, and an artisan market.
  • Salinas Grandes: A stunning salt flat located on the border of Salta and Jujuy provinces, offering otherworldly views.

Conclusion

Valles Calchaquíes is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Argentina. With its unique blend of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, there is truly something for everyone. From scenic drives to breathtaking hikes, wine tasting to hot air balloon rides, Valles Calchaquíes has it all.
