Introduction to Humahuaca

Located in the Andean Northwest of Argentina, in the province of Jujuy, Humahuaca is a quaint town that is known for its rich culture, stunning views, and natural beauty. The town is nestled between two mountain ranges, and is surrounded by stunning scenery that includes colorful rock formations and expansive high-altitude plains. Because of its unique geography, Humahuaca is a popular destination for travelers who want to experience the rugged beauty of the Andes.

The town itself is home to many cultural attractions, including museums, festivals, and historical sites. It is also a great base for exploring the surrounding countryside, which is crisscrossed by hiking and biking trails that lead to secluded valleys, hidden waterfalls, and stunning vistas. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at Humahuaca and the many attractions and activities it has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Humahuaca

When it comes to sightseeing in Humahuaca, there are many places to choose from. Here is a list of some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Arqueología de Alta Montaña (MAAM)
  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de la Candelaria
  • Pucará de Tilcara
  • Hornocal Mountains
  • Palacio de Uquía

These places reflect the rich history and cultural heritage of the Andean region, and are must-see for anyone interested in learning more about the area’s unique traditions and customs.

Natural Attractions of Humahuaca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laguna de los Pozuelos Nature Reserve
  • Quebrada de Humahuaca
  • Salinas Grandes
  • Parque Nacional Calilegua
  • Serranía de Zapla

These natural wonders offer a glimpse into the rugged beauty of the Andes, with stunning views of rocky formations, expansive high-altitude plains, and hidden waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Humahuaca

Humahuaca is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Sendero del Altar
  • Trekking up the Hornocal Mountain
  • Circuit Paleta del Pintor - Maimará
  • Trekking to Laguna de los Pozuelos
  • Trekking Quebrada de las Señoritas

These routes offer a good mix of physical challenge and stunning views, allowing visitors to experience the rugged beauty of the Andes up close.

Best Things to Do in Humahuaca: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Humahuaca. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Hot springs in Termas de Reyes
  • Horseback riding in Tilcara
  • Wine tasting in Cafayate
  • Exploring the salt flats in Salinas Grandes
  • Attending the colorful Carnaval de Humahuaca

These activities offer a glimpse into the many facets of life in the Andean region, from the local cuisine and wine to the vibrant festivals and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Humahuaca

For those planning to spend a week in Humahuaca, here is a recommended itinerary:

Local Culture and Customs of Humahuaca

Humahuaca has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its festivals, customs, and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of people or places
  • It is customary to greet people with a “buen día” (good day) or “buenas tardes” (good afternoon)
  • Be respectful of local traditions and customs
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites, such as churches
  • Follow any cultural and social customs when attending festivals or other events

Best Time to Visit Humahuaca

The best time to visit Humahuaca is between May and September, when the weather is dry and sunny, and the landscape is at its most stunning. During this time, the daytime temperatures are warm and comfortable, with cool evenings. However, visitors should be prepared for low temperatures and chilly winds, especially at higher elevations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Humahuaca

When visiting Humahuaca, there are some things to keep in mind to ensure an enjoyable and respectful trip:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and cultural events
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people or places

Safety and Health

Humahuaca is a safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should still exercise caution when visiting, and take basic precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Humahuaca

  • Follow local safety guidelines and rules
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dangerous areas or situations
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure, especially in crowded areas
  • Stay alert and aware when traveling alone

Health in Humahuaca

  • Be aware of the effects of high altitude on your body, and take measures to prevent altitude sickness
  • Bring plenty of water and sunscreen to protect against dehydration and sunburn
  • Seek medical attention promptly if you feel unwell or experience any symptoms of illness
