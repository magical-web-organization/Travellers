Introduction to Uquía

Nestled in Argentina’s Andean Northwest, the small town of Uquía offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. With its rich history and stunning natural landscapes, Uquía is the perfect destination for travelers looking to immerse themselves in traditional Argentinean culture while experiencing the beauty of the Andes.

List of Places to Visit in Uquía

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of Uquía is its rich cultural heritage, which is best experienced through its museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Iglesia de Uquía (Church of Uquía): Built in the 17th century, this historic church features stunning colonial-era architecture and beautiful Baroque-style artwork.
  • Museum of Sacred Art: Housed within the Iglesia de Uquía, this museum showcases a wide range of religious art and artifacts from the 16th to the 18th century, including paintings, sculptures, and textiles.
  • Chapel of Santa Bárbara: Located just outside of Uquía, this charming chapel dates back to the 18th century and features well-preserved frescoes from that era.
  • San Francisco de Paula Church: Another historic church, San Francisco de Paula features stunning Baroque-style architecture and beautiful paintings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Uquía: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its cultural attractions, Uquía is also home to a number of natural wonders that are sure to delight outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top outdoor attractions in the area include:

  • Quebrada de las Señoritas: This scenic canyon offers stunning views of the Andes and is a popular destination for hiking and horseback riding.
  • Laguna de los Pozuelos: Located just outside of Uquía, this large saltwater lagoon is home to a variety of bird species and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Paso de Jama: This stunning mountain pass is located on the border between Argentina and Chile and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uquía

With its stunning natural landscapes and rugged terrain, Uquía is the perfect destination for hikers and bikers. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Quebrada de las Señoritas: This scenic canyon offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy walks to more challenging routes for experienced hikers.
  • Laguna de los Pozuelos: The area around the lagoon is home to several great hiking trails, including a popular 12-kilometer loop that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Paso de Jama: While not technically a hiking or biking route, this stunning mountain pass offers a breathtaking drive that is not to be missed.

Best Things to Do in Uquía: Activities and Tours

In addition to its cultural and natural attractions, Uquía is also home to a variety of fun activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Uquía include:

  • Horseback riding: Explore the rugged landscape surrounding Uquía on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Birdwatching: With its diverse range of bird species, Uquía is a great destination for birdwatching enthusiasts.
  • Traditional cooking classes: Learn how to cook traditional Argentinean dishes with a local chef.
  • Wine tasting tours: Uquía is located in the heart of Argentina’s wine country, making it the perfect destination for a wine tasting tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Uquía

For travelers looking to spend more time in Uquía, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival in Uquía

Take some time to explore the town and get settled in.

Day 2-3: Cultural attractions

Visit Iglesia de Uquía, the Museum of Sacred Art, the Chapel of Santa Bárbara, and San Francisco de Paula Church.

Day 4-5: Outdoor adventures

Explore Quebrada de las Señoritas and Laguna de los Pozuelos with a guided tour or on your own.

Day 6: Cooking class and wine tasting

Take a traditional cooking class and enjoy some of Argentina’s best wines on a wine tasting tour.

Day 7: Departure

Spend your last day in Uquía relaxing before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Uquía

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Argentina is complete without trying some of the country’s famous dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in Uquía include:

  • Empanadas: These savory pastries are filled with a variety of ingredients, such as meat, cheese, and vegetables.
  • Asado: A traditional Argentinean barbecue, asado typically features a variety of meats cooked over an open flame.
  • Locro: A hearty stew made with corn, beans, and meat, locro is a popular dish in Argentina.

Local Beverages

Argentina is known for its wine, but there are a few other local beverages worth trying as well:

  • Mate: A traditional Argentinean drink made from yerba mate, hot water, and sometimes sugar or milk.
  • Fernet: A bitter herbal liqueur that is often mixed with Coca-Cola.

Where to Stay in Uquía: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite its small size, Uquía offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area include:

  • Hospedaje Uquía: Located in the heart of town, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Posada de Los Poetas: This charming hotel is located on the outskirts of Uquía and offers beautiful views of the mountains.
  • Hostería La Ciénaga: Located just outside of Uquía, this peaceful hostel is surrounded by natural beauty and offers a variety of activities and tours.

What to Buy in Uquía: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Uquía is complete without bringing home a few souvenirs and local products. Some of the best things to buy in Uquía include:

  • Hand-woven textiles: Uquía is known for its beautiful textiles, which are often made with traditional techniques and natural dyes.
  • Traditional crafts: From pottery to leather goods, there are a variety of traditional crafts made by local artisans in Uquía.
  • Wine: Don’t forget to bring home a bottle or two of the delicious wine produced in the surrounding region.

Local Culture and Customs of Uquía

Uquía is a town with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take care to respect local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Religion: As a predominantly Catholic town, visitors should dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Language: Spanish is the official language of Argentina, and while some residents of Uquía may speak English, visitors should be prepared to communicate in Spanish.
  • Etiquette: It is customary to greet others with a kiss on the cheek in Argentina, and visitors should be prepared for active social forums guiding mealtime conversation.

Best Time to Visit Uquía

The best time to visit Uquía is during the summer months, between December and February, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for some rain during this time. The spring and fall months, between March and November, are also a good time to visit, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uquía

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Visit religious sites in inappropriate attire.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uquía

Uquía is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and not walking alone at night.

Health in Uquía

There are no major health concerns in Uquía, but visitors should be aware of the high altitude and take precautions to avoid altitude sickness, such as staying hydrated and allowing time to acclimate to the altitude.

Getting to Uquía: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uquía

The closest airports to Uquía are located in Jujuy (90 kilometers away) and Salta (150 kilometers away).

Bus Stations Near Uquía

Uquía has its own bus station, which is served by a variety of local and regional bus companies.

Other Options

Some visitors may prefer to rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Uquía.

How to Get Around in Uquía

While Uquía is a small town, there are several options for getting around, including walking, biking, and hiring a taxi or private driver.

Where to Go Next from Uquía: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations worth exploring after visiting Uquía, including:

  • Humahuaca: This charming colonial town is located just 30 kilometers north of Uquía and is known for its beautiful architecture and stunning natural landscapes.
  • Purmamarca: Located 60 kilometers south of Uquía, this small town is famous for its colorful mountains and beautiful handicrafts.
  • Salta: Argentina’s northwest capital city, Salta is a vibrant destination known for its beautiful colonial architecture and lively cultural scene.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and friendly locals, Uquía is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Argentina’s Andean Northwest. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich history, indulging in traditional Argentinean cuisine, or embarking on an outdoor adventure, Uquía has something for everyone.
