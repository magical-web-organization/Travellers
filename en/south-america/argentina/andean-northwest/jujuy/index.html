Introduction to Jujuy

Jujuy is a province in the Andean Northwest region of Argentina, located in South America. It is known for its diverse landscapes that range from the colorful Quebrada de Humahuaca and Puna regions to the Yungas jungle. In this travel guide, we will explore the various attractions and activities that make Jujuy a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Jujuy

If you’re planning a trip to Jujuy, these are the top places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Colonial City of Humahuaca: A picturesque colonial city in the Quebrada de Humahuaca region, famous for its colorful buildings and stunning Andean landscapes.
  • The Tropic of Capricorn: A monument that marks the line that separates the Southern and Northern Hemisphere.
  • Museum of Archaeology of High Altitude (MAAM): A museum in Salta that houses mummies and artifacts from the Incan Empire.
  • Pucara de Tilcara: This pre-Columbian fortress ruins is a testimony of the ancient culture of the region.
  • San Salvador de Jujuy Cathedral: A religious and architectural landmark in the heart of Jujuy.

Natural Attractions of Jujuy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salinas Grandes: A vast salt desert in the Puna region that offers a unique landscape and fun activities like salt walks, photography, and stargazing.
  • The Hill of Seven Colors: A series of colorful rock formations in Purmamarca that make for a great hike and photo opportunity.
  • Laguna de Yala: A picturesque lagoon nestled in the Yungas jungle region, surrounded by lush vegetation and mountain views.
  • Hornocal: A stunning mountain range located near Humahuaca, famous for its unique colors and panoramic views.
  • Cuesta del Obispo: A mountain road in Salta that offers breathtaking views of the valleys and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jujuy

Jujuy is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The region offers various hiking and biking routes, ranging from easy to challenging. These are some of the must-visit trails:

  • The Inca Trail: A 3-day trek that takes you through the ruins of the Incan Empire, including ruins such as the Citadel of Incahuasi, Pucara de Tilcara, La Cienaga, and the Fort of Abra de Punta Corral.
  • The Quebrada de Humahuaca: A 155-kilometer route that takes you through the Andes mountain range, dotted with small villages and historical sites such as the Hill of Seven Colors and the Cathedral of Humahuaca.
  • The Salinas Grandes Trek: A guided tour that takes you through the vast salt desert of Salinas Grandes, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Jujuy: Activities and Tours

These are the best activities and tours you should do in Jujuy:

  • Take part in a local carnival: A vibrant and colorful celebration held in February, featuring costumes, dances, and music unique to the region.
  • Visit a traditional indigenous community: Learn about the culture and traditions of the indigenous people of the region by visiting one of the many indigenous communities in Jujuy.
  • Take a culinary tour: Jujuy is known for its unique cuisine, which blends indigenous and Spanish influences. A culinary tour will give you a chance to taste local dishes like empanadas, tamales, and locro.
  • Go horseback riding: Explore the stunning landscapes of Jujuy on horseback, with tours ranging from a few hours to a full-day ride.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Admire the stunning landscapes of Jujuy from above on a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jujuy

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Jujuy, this is a comprehensive itinerary that covers the highlights of the region:

Food and Drink Scene of Jujuy

Jujuy has a unique cuisine that blends indigenous and Spanish influences. These are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Empanadas: A traditional pastry filled with meat, onions, potatoes, and other ingredients.
  • Locro: A hearty stew made with corn, beans, meat, and vegetables.
  • Tamales: A dish made from corn dough stuffed with meat, vegetables, or cheese and steamed in corn husks.
  • Humita: A dish of fresh corn dough with cheese and other ingredients, wrapped in corn husks and steamed.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: A traditional hot drink made from yerba mate leaves, served in a calabash gourd with a metal straw.
  • Chicha: A fermented corn drink that is often served at festivals and celebrations.
  • Andean Wine: A regional wine made from grapes grown at high altitudes.

Where to Stay in Jujuy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jujuy offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. These are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Munay: A beautiful hotel in the heart of Jujuy City, offering comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace with stunning views.
  • La Comarca: A luxury hotel in Purmamarca with a spa, restaurant, and outdoor pool.
  • Hostel La Albahaca: A budget-friendly hostel in Tilcara, offering dorms and private rooms.
  • Solar De La Plaza: A historic hotel in Salta, offering elegant rooms and a central location.

What to Buy in Jujuy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts, Jujuy has plenty to offer. These are some of the top options:

  • Handwoven Textiles: Indigenous communities in Jujuy are known for their beautiful, colorful textiles made from llama and sheep wool.
  • Ceramics: The region has a long tradition of pottery, with pieces ranging from functional to decorative.
  • Quinoa Products: A staple food in the region, quinoa is used to make a variety of products like flour, snacks, and pasta.
  • Alpaca Clothing: Soft, warm, and durable, alpaca wool is used to make a variety of clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Jujuy

Jujuy has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique blend of indigenous and Spanish influences. These are some of the customs and traditions you may encounter:

  • Carnival: A lively and colorful celebration held in February, with music, dance, and elaborate costumes.
  • Indigenous customs: Many indigenous communities in Jujuy still practice their traditional customs and beliefs, including ceremonies and rituals.
  • Food and drink: Food is an essential part of the local culture, with traditional dishes like empanadas and locro often served at parties and celebrations.
  • Music and dance: Folk music and dance are widespread in Jujuy, with instruments like the charango and the sikuri (panpipes) commonly played.

Best Time to Visit Jujuy

Jujuy has a semi-arid to arid climate, with mild to hot temperatures throughout the year. The best time to visit depends on your preferences and plans:

  • High season (June to August): The winter months are high season in Jujuy, with cooler temperatures and clear skies. It’s a great time for outdoor activities and sightseeing, but it’s also the busiest time of the year.
  • Shoulder season (April to May and September to November): These months offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds, making it a great time to visit if you’re looking for a quieter experience.
  • Low season (December to March): The summer months can be hot and humid, but they also offer unique events like the Carnival and grape harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jujuy

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and beverages, including mate and empanadas.
  • Do learn about local customs and traditions, including those of indigenous communities.
  • Do explore the region’s stunning landscapes, including the Quebrada de Humahuaca and Salinas Grandes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or beliefs.
  • Don’t travel alone to remote areas without proper preparation and guidance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jujuy

Jujuy is generally a safe place for travelers, but it’s important to take some precautions:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in urban areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Take care when driving, as roads can be narrow and winding.

Health in Jujuy

There are no specific health concerns for Jujuy, but it’s always a good idea to take some precautions:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Be prepared for altitude sickness if you’re traveling to higher elevations.

Getting to Jujuy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jujuy

The closest airport to Jujuy is Gobernador Horacio Guzmán International Airport, located in the city of San Salvador de Jujuy.

Bus Stations Near Jujuy

Jujuy has several bus stations that connect it to major cities in Argentina and neighboring countries:

  • Terminal de Ómnibus de San Salvador de Jujuy: The main bus terminal in Jujuy, offering connections to cities like Salta, Buenos Aires, and La Paz (Bolivia).
  • Terminal de Ómnibus de Tilcara: A smaller terminal in Tilcara that offers connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Some travelers may prefer to rent a car or take a private transfer to explore Jujuy at their own pace. However, it’s important to note that some roads can be narrow and winding, and driving may require some experience and caution.

How to Get Around in Jujuy

Jujuy offers several transportation options for getting around:

  • Bus: Local buses are a cheap and convenient way to get around the city and nearby towns. Fares are usually paid in cash on the bus.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Jujuy, with fares usually paid in cash or by credit card.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the region at your own pace. Several rental companies operate in Jujuy.

Where to Go Next from Jujuy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jujuy is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in the Andean Northwest region:

  • Salta: A lively city known for its colonial architecture, museums, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Tucumán: A historic city with a rich cultural heritage and a booming culinary scene.
  • Purmamarca: A charming village in the Quebrada de Humahuaca region, famous for its Hill of Seven Colors and artisanal crafts.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or adventure, Jujuy has something for everyone. With stunning landscapes, unique cuisine, and a rich cultural heritage, it’s a must-visit destination in Argentina.
