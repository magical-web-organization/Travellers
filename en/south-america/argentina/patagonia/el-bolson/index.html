Introduction to El Bolsón

Nestled in the foothills of the Andes, El Bolsón is a charming town situated in Rio Negro province of Patagonia, Argentina. Surrounded by stunning mountain landscapes with pristine clear water, hidden natural wonders, and multicultural heritage, El Bolsón is a feast for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in El Bolsón

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa de Piedra: A cultural center located on the outskirts of El Bolsón, exhibiting the art and customs of the native Puelche people.
  • Ferrocarril Turístico: A train ride from El Bolsón to Esquel through the Andean forest, with a stop at circular Cumelen Ranch.
  • Museo Regional: A small museum that displays regional history and artwork. The materials used for the house are limestone and natural cedar.

Natural Attractions of El Bolsón: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cascada Azul: A stunning waterfall located on the outskirts of El Bolsón, surrounded by lush greenery and peaceful tranquility.
  • La Trochita: A narrow-gauge railway, popularly known as the Old Patagonian Express. The train takes you through the breathtaking beauty of the Patagonian plateau, lush green forests, and quaint little villages.
  • Cerro Piltriquitrón: A must-visit place for adventurous travelers. The hike to the top is challenging but rewarding, offering panoramic views of the Andes Mountains and the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Bolsón

El Bolsón is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many trails of various levels and types. Here are a few must-visit hiking/biking routes:

  • Sendero del Cerro Amigo: A short and easy trail leading to the top of Cerro Amigo, offering great views of the valley and the town.
  • Refuge Hielo Azul: A moderate 5-hour hike amidst glaciers and scenic valleys, a leading point of the Rio Negro river.
  • Cerro Lindo: An easy trail that leads to the top of a hill, providing stunning panoramic views of the Andes Mountains and the valley.

Best Things to Do in El Bolsón: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting and Kayaking: The Rio Azul and the Rio Manso in El Bolsón are perfect for rafting and kayaking. Rafting services are available, and it’s an outstanding way to explore the valley.
  • Horseback Riding: A fantastic activity for nature lovers, it offers an extensive tour of the region’s stunning natural beauty, led by a local guide and riding a horse.
  • Paragliding: El Bolsón surrounded by the Andes Mountains is the perfect place to experience paragliding. The activity is available all year round and does not require any previous experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Bolsón

Day 1: Arrive at El Bolsón and take an evening stroll around the town Day 2: Hike the Sendero del Cerro Amigo trail, take a swim in the river, eat lunch by the river Day 3: Visit Ferrocarril Turístico and take a train ride to Esquel through mountains. Day 4: Visit Casa de Piedra and Museum Regional and try local food in the city square. Day 5: Kayak day tour for a wild adventure through the exotic tropical forest along the river. Day 6: Go horseback riding followed by a picnic near the Cerro Piltriquitrón. Day 7: Take a day trip to Puerto Montt or Bariloche.

Food and Drink Scene of El Bolsón

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: an Argentine national dish of barbequed meat.
  • Curanto: a dish made of clams and shellfish, baked over hot stones.
  • Choclo con queso: a traditional El Bolsón snack, made of grilled corn with melted cheese on top.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: a traditional Argentine drink prepared by steeping dried yerba mate leaves in hot water.
  • Cerveza artesanal: artisanal beer made from locally grown hops and barley.

Where to Stay in El Bolsón: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Casa del Árbol: A unique bed and breakfast in El Bolsón, located amidst a lush green forest.
  • Pura Vida Hostel: A hostel near the town center and bus station, with a lovely garden and shared kitchen.
  • Hostel Mandala: A cozy hostel with a garden and barbeque facilities, located near the river.

What to Buy in El Bolsón: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted woolen items: El Bolsón is famous for its woolen products. Visitors can find comfortable and stylish woolen hats, scarves, mittens, and sweaters.
  • Local chocolate: is another popular souvenir that is sure to delight visitors to El Bolsón.

Local Culture and Customs of El Bolsón

The culture of El Bolsón is a blend of many cultures, including indigenous Mapuche people. The town is renowned for its artisanal products, local crafts, and festivals like the National Craft Beer Festival, held every year in January.

Best Time to Visit El Bolsón

The best time to visit El Bolsón is from November to April. During this period, the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. However, take note that the weather can change rapidly, so it is important to come prepared.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Bolsón

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and drinks
  • Do respect the local culture
  • Do bring warm clothes and rain gear

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t vandalize
  • Don’t wander alone in isolated areas at night

Safety and Health

Safety in El Bolsón

El Bolsón is quite a safe town, but visitors should always take necessary precautions in crowded or isolated areas. Make sure to lock your accommodation and valuables when leaving, especially when going out at night.

Health in El Bolsón

Visitors are advised to drink purified water to avoid any health complications. The sun can be quite strong in Patagonia, so it’s recommended to use sunscreen and wear protective clothing.

Getting to El Bolsón: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Bolsón

The closest airport to El Bolsón is San Carlos de Bariloche airport, which is approximately 120 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near El Bolsón

  • The El Bolsón Bus Terminal is the most important one, handling long-distance and regional buses.
  • Rio Negro Transport is convenient for people traveling from Carmen de Patagones, Viedma, and Bariloche.

Other Options

For travelers who enjoy driving, the route to El Bolsón by car or motorbike is a perfect way to explore the breathtaking scenery of the Andes Mountains.

How to Get Around in El Bolsón

El Bolsón is a small town, so it’s best to get around on foot. Taxis and buses are also available to take visitors around the town and its outskirts.

Where to Go Next from El Bolsón: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bariloche: An iconic city that lies 130 kilometers to the west of El Bolsón, offering some of Patagonia’s most stunning natural beauty.
  • Puerto Montt: A picturesque fishing town located in the Chilean Patagonia, only a 5-hour drive from El Bolsón.

Conclusion

El Bolsón is a hidden gem in Patagonia, providing an authentic Argentine experience rich in natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and cultural heritage. Make sure you plan your trip well and enjoy the fantastic activities, mouth-watering cuisine, and breathtaking landscapes only El Bolsón can offer!
