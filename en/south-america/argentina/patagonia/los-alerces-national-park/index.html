Introduction to Los Alerces National Park

Los Alerces National Park is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Argentina. It’s located in the heart of Patagonia and boasts stunning landscapes, crystal clear lakes, and diverse wildlife. The park covers an area of 2,630 km² and is home to ancient trees, including the gigantic Alerce tree, as well as indigenous cultures. Los Alerces National Park is considered a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which makes it a must-visit destination for those who love nature, history, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Los Alerces National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

In Los Alerces National Park, you can learn about the region’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums and historical places. Here are some must-see cultural attractions:

  • Museo de Los Alerces: This museum is located near the park entrance and showcases the history and culture of the indigenous Tehuelche people, along with the park’s flora and fauna.
  • Cascada Nant y Fall: This picturesque waterfall is a sacred place for the Mapuche people, who consider it a meeting place of the spirits. Visitors can learn about their traditions and beliefs through interpretive signage on site.

Natural Attractions of Los Alerces National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Los Alerces National Park offers visitors a range of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery. Here are some top natural attractions:

  • Futalaufquen Lake: This is the park’s largest lake and home to the endangered Argentine trout. Visitors can hire a boat or go fishing here.
  • Torrecillas Glacier: This impressive glacier is the park’s main attraction and can be seen from the viewpoint near the town of Cholila.
  • Mount Torrecillas: This 2500-meter mountain is a popular spot for hiking and provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Los Alerces National Park

The park offers a range of hiking and biking trails suitable for different skill levels. Here are some suggestions:

  • Lago Verde Trail: This trail features a moderate level of difficulty and takes you through stunning forests and along the shores of Lago Verde.
  • Alto El Maqui Route: This route is for more experienced hikers and provides a challenging trek to Mount Alto El Maqui’s summit, with spectacular views of the park’s glaciers and Andean peaks.
  • El Bolsón Trail: This trail is perfect for beginners and families with children. It’s a gentle walk that takes you around the charming town of El Bolsón while exploring its great craft market.

Best Things to Do in Los Alerces National Park: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Los Alerces National Park beyond hiking and biking, from kayaking to horseback riding. Some of the most popular activities include:

  • Kayaking: This is a great way to explore the park’s many lakes and rivers from the water, and there are plenty of guided tours available.
  • Fishing: Anglers should not miss the chance to fish the endangered Argentine trout at Futalaufquen Lake.
  • Horseback Riding: This is a fun activity for those who want to experience the park’s striking landscapes from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Los Alerces National Park

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in Los Alerces National Park:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in, visit the museum and Cascada Nant y Fall.

Day 2: Hike in Mount Torrecillas area or do a full-day tour to Torrecillas Glacier.

Day 3: Visit Futalaufquen Lake and take a boat ride or do some fishing.

Day 4: Kayaking tour in Lake Menéndez.

Day 5: Do the “Alto El Maqui” trek or participate in a horseback ride.

Day 6: Take a free day and explore the nearby town of Esquel or enjoy the hotel’s facilities.

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Los Alerces National Park

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re looking to sample local cuisine while exploring the park, here are some must-try dishes:

  • Asado: A classic Argentine meal featuring a range of grilled meats and sausages.
  • Locro: A hearty stew made from corn, beans, meat, and other vegetables.
  • Empanadas: A savory pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

To quench your thirst, try these local drinks:

  • Mate: Argentina’s national drink, made from dried yerba mate leaves brewed in hot water; it has a distinct taste and plenty of caffeine.
  • Malbec Wine: Enjoy a glass of this red wine while a relaxing sunset overlooking the lakes and forests.

Where to Stay in Los Alerces National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The park offers several accommodation options, ranging from rustic lodges to more luxurious hotels. Here are some suggestions:

  • Lago Puelo Lodge: A charming lodge located by Lago Puelo, with comfortable rooms and a private beach.
  • Hostel Tierra Gaucha: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Esquel, with dorms and private rooms.
  • Bahia Bustamante Lodge: A luxurious lodge with splendid views over the bay, perfect for those looking for a unique experience in Patagonia.

What to Buy in Los Alerces National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When exploring the park, be sure to take home some of its unique products. Here are some things you can buy as souvenirs:

  • Handcrafted silver jewelry: Stunning made by locals with traditional techniques.
  • Woolen Items: Warm and rustic clothing, including ponchos and scarves, made by the regional communities.

Local Culture and Customs of Los Alerces National Park

The indigenous Mapuche people have a strong presence in the area surrounding the park and have preserved their cultural traditions, including weaving and ceremonies. Visitors can learn more about their ways of life and beliefs by attending local events and exhibitions.

Best Time to Visit Los Alerces National Park

The best time to visit Los Alerces National Park is from November to March, during the summer months, when the weather is mild and nature is at its most vibrant. It’s also the ideal time for outdoor activities. July and August are the busiest months, with many people coming for winter sports and sightseeing of the snow-covered peaks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Los Alerces National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s rules and regulations, including leaving no trace behind and not disturbing the wildlife.
  • Pack and dress appropriately for the weather and activities you plan to enjoy in the park.
  • Explore different areas of the park and make time for cultural and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach the park’s animals, including birds and mammals.
  • Take plants or animals out of the park, even as souvenirs.
  • Smoke or light fires in undesignated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Los Alerces National Park

While Los Alerces National Park is generally safe, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and follow precautions while engaging in outdoor activities. Here are some safety tips:

  • Hike with a companion or group, and inform someone of your intended route and return time.
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes, including rain, wind, and sun rays.
  • Stay on designated trails and paths to avoid getting lost.

Health in Los Alerces National Park

Visitors who plan on staying in the park for prolonged periods should take adequate measures to stay healthy. Here are some health tips:

  • Protect yourself from sunburn, especially if you plan on being outdoors for most of the day.
  • Bring insect repellent, as mosquitoes and other biting pests are common in the park’s forest areas.
  • Stay hydrated and carry enough water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Los Alerces National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Los Alerces National Park

The Esquel airport is the closest airport to Los Alerces National Park, providing daily flights from Buenos Aires. It takes approximately 40 minutes to drive to the park from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Los Alerces National Park

The town of Esquel has a bus station that is well-connected to other parts of the region, including Bariloche, Puerto Madryn, and Comodoro Rivadavia.

Other Options

For visitors who prefer to drive, taking Route 40 to Esquel will take you straight to the park’s entrance. There are car rental services in the nearby cities of Trelew and Bariloche for those who want to explore the park on their terms.

How to Get Around in Los Alerces National Park

The park’s main roads are paved, which makes getting around simple. Visitors can rent a car or take a guided tour. During the summer months, there is also a shuttle bus service operating around the park.

Where to Go Next from Los Alerces National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Los Alerces National Park, visitors can choose from several nearby destinations worth visiting. Here are some suggestions:

  • Bariloche: This charming town is a popular destination in the Lake District region famous for its skiing, hiking, and outdoor adventures.
  • Puerto Madryn: This coastal town is one of the best places in the world for whale watching and is a gateway to the Valdes Peninsula’s natural wonders.
  • Torres del Paine National Park: Located in Chile, this park is a must-visit destination for lovers of nature and hiking.

Conclusion

Los Alerces National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and those seeking to explore Patagonia’s history and culture. From its striking landscapes to its cultural and natural attractions, the park has something for everyone. With this guide, you can plan the perfect trip to this stunning UNESCO World Heritage Site.
