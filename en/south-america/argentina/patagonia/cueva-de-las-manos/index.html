Introduction to Cueva de las Manos

Cueva de las Manos is an archaeological site in Patagonia, Argentina, that features a series of prehistoric rock artworks dating back to 13,000-9,500 BCE. The cave complex is located in the deep canyon of the Pinturas River, within the province of Santa Cruz, and was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999.

Visitors come to Cueva de las Manos to see the vivid stenciled images of human hands, animals, and hunting scenes painted on the cave walls. There are over 800 different handprints at the site, highlighting the importance of the place in the cultural and artistic development of early human societies.

List of Places to Visit in Cueva de las Manos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The most important cultural attraction in Cueva de las Manos is the cave itself, which can be reached via a guided tour. Visitors can admire the prehistoric paintings, mostly handprints in various colors, depicting hunting scenes, animals, and geometric designs.

Additionally, there are museums and historical places in the region that visitors can explore:

  • Museum of Paleontology Egidio Feruglio: This museum in Trelew features an extensive collection of fossils and dinosaur replicas, including a complete skeleton of a Patagotitan mayorum, one of the largest dinosaurs ever discovered.

  • Museum of Patagonia Francisco P. Moreno: This museum in Bariloche showcases the natural and cultural heritage of Patagonia, including geology, paleontology, archaeology, and indigenous art.

Natural Attractions of Cueva de las Manos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Patagonia is one of the main draws for visitors to Cueva de las Manos. Here are some outdoor adventures and scenic views to enjoy:

  • Piedra Parada: This geological formation near Esquel is a popular spot for rock climbing, hiking, and camping. The tall pillar of volcanic rock stands over 200 meters high and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Lago Puelo National Park: This protected area near El Bolsón features crystal-clear lakes, lush forests, and snow-capped mountains. Visitors can go hiking, kayaking, or fishing, or simply relax in the tranquil scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cueva de las Manos

The Cueva de las Manos area has several hiking and biking routes that offer panoramic views and closer encounters with nature. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Cerro Torre: This challenging hike takes visitors to the base of one of the most iconic peaks in Patagonia, Cerro Torre. The trail leads through forests, glaciers, and alpine lakes, offering breathtaking scenery along the way.

  • La Trochita: This historic railway line runs from Esquel to El Maitén, offering a scenic journey through the Andean foothills and the Patagonian steppe. Visitors can ride the vintage steam train and stop at various stations to explore local attractions.

Best Things to Do in Cueva de las Manos: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the cave and exploring the natural wonders of Patagonia, there are many other things to do in Cueva de las Manos, from cultural activities to adventure tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Hunting and Fishing: Patagonia is a paradise for hunting and fishing enthusiasts, with abundant wildlife and pristine rivers and lakes. Visitors can join guided tours to hunt for deer, wild boar, or trout, or to go fly fishing in some of the world’s best waters.

  • Horseback Riding: Riding through the Patagonian wilderness is an unforgettable experience, and many tour operators offer horseback rides through the forests and mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cueva de las Manos

A one-week trip to Cueva de las Manos and the surrounding region could look something like this:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Buenos Aires, spend the night.

  • Day 2: Fly to Comodoro Rivadavia, rent a car, and drive to Camarones. Stay overnight.

  • Day 3: Visit the Museum of Paleontology Egidio Feruglio in Trelew, drive to Puerto Madryn. Stay overnight.

  • Day 4: Take a whale watching tour in Puerto Madryn, drive to Rio Mayo. Stay overnight.

  • Day 5: Visit the cave of Cueva de las Manos, go hiking around the canyon. Stay overnight in Perito Moreno.

  • Day 6: Drive to El Chaltén, go hiking in Cerro Torre. Stay overnight.

  • Day 7: Visit Lago Puelo National Park, drive back to Comodoro Rivadavia. Return the rental car, fly back to Buenos Aires.

Food and Drink Scene of Cueva de las Manos

The cuisine of Patagonia is characterized by its simple yet flavorful dishes, based on local ingredients such as lamb, seafood, and wild berries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: Argentine barbecue or asado is a staple of the local cuisine, usually consisting of different cuts of beef or lamb grilled over wood or charcoal.

  • Cordero Patagónico: Roasted Patagonian lamb, seasoned with garlic, rosemary, and olive oil, is a delicacy that can be found in most local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: Argentina’s national drink, mate is a traditional infusion made of dried yerba mate leaves, drunk through a metal straw from a gourd or a cup. It is a social ritual as much as a beverage, and locals will often share mate with friends and family.

  • Malbec: Argentinian wine, especially the Malbec variety, has gained international fame for its rich and robust flavor. Patagonia produces some excellent Malbec wines, which can be savored in local wineries and restaurants.

  • Estancia Lazo: A charming guesthouse in El Calafate, on the shores of Lake Argentino, with cozy rooms, a homey atmosphere, and magnificent views.

What to Buy in Cueva de las Manos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Patagonia is famous for its leather products, woolen garments, and artisanal handicrafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Handmade ponchos and sweaters: Patagonian wool is renowned for its softness and warmth, and many artisans produce beautiful ponchos and sweaters with intricate designs.

  • Leather goods: From belts to shoes to bags, Argentine leather is top-quality and affordable. Look out for fine leather products made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Cueva de las Manos

Patagonia is a region with a rich cultural history, shaped by the indigenous peoples who inhabited the area for thousands of years. Visitors should respect the following customs and etiquette:

  • Greeting: When meeting someone new, a handshake or a nod of the head is appropriate. Kisses on the cheek are reserved for close friends or family members.

  • Dress Code: Casual dress is generally accepted in most situations. However, dress modestly when visiting religious or formal sites.

Best Time to Visit Cueva de las Manos

The best time to visit Cueva de las Manos is in the Patagonian spring and summer, from October to March, when the weather is mild and daylight hours are longer. However, the cave can be visited year-round, and each season has its appeal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cueva de las Manos

Do:

  • Follow the rules and regulations at the archaeological site, including not touching or damaging the cave paintings.

  • Respect the local culture and customs, and be mindful of your behavior and language.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind, and try to minimize your impact on the environment.

  • Don’t overexert yourself when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, and follow the safety procedures provided by the tour operators.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cueva de las Manos

Cueva de las Manos is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always wise to take basic precautions:

  • Store your valuables in a safe place and keep an eye on them when in crowded or touristy areas.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid unlit or isolated places at night.

Health in Cueva de las Manos

Travelers should take preventive measures against common health risks in Patagonia:

  • Use insect repellent and protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites and the risk of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever or Zika virus.

  • Drink bottled water or boiled water to avoid gastrointestinal infections.

Getting to Cueva de las Manos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cueva de las Manos

The closest airport to Cueva de las Manos is Comodoro Rivadavia, which is served by domestic airlines such as Aerolíneas Argentinas and LATAM. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Perito Moreno or other nearby towns.

Bus Stations Near Cueva de las Manos

Several bus companies operate in the region, connecting Cueva de las Manos with major cities and towns in Patagonia and Argentina. The most important bus stations are:

  • El Calafate Bus Station
  • Rio Gallegos Bus Station
  • Bariloche Bus Station

Other Options

Driving or renting a car is a popular option for exploring the region, as it allows more independence and flexibility. However, visitors should be aware of the long driving distances, the rough terrain, and the limited gas stations and services in some areas.

How to Get Around in Cueva de las Manos

Most tour operators and hotels offer transportation to and from Cueva de las Manos and other local attractions. Visitors can also rent cars or hire taxis to get around on their own.

Where to Go Next from Cueva de las Manos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After visiting Cueva de las Manos, visitors can explore other fascinating places in Patagonia, such as:

  • Torres del Paine National Park: This stunning park in Chile features rugged mountain peaks, glaciers, turquoise lakes, and abundant wildlife. Visitors can go trekking, kayaking, or horseback riding, or simply enjoy the beautiful landscapes.

  • Tierra del Fuego: The southernmost region of the American continent, Tierra del Fuego is a vast wilderness of fjords, glaciers, and sub-Antarctic forests. Visitors can take cruises to see penguins and sea lions, or hike in the stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Cueva de las Manos is a unique and unforgettable destination that offers a window into the prehistoric world of Patagonia, as well as a chance to explore the natural wonders and cultural richness of the region. Whether you come for the cave paintings, the outdoor adventures, or the local cuisine, you are sure to leave with memories and experiences that will last a lifetime.
