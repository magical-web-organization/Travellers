El Calafate is a picturesque town located in Patagonia, a vast region in Argentina, South America. Home to spectacular glaciers, national parks, and nature reserves, this is a must-visit destination for travelers who are seeking adventure or a peaceful getaway. In this comprehensive tour guide for El Calafate, we’ve covered everything you need to know to plan your trip, whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or simply enjoying the local cuisine.

Introduction to El Calafate

El Calafate is a small town that has become a popular tourist destination in recent years. It’s located in the southwestern part of Santa Cruz Province, near the Chilean border. The town is named after the calafate berry, which is native to the region. There are several theories about the origin of the name, but the most popular one is that it comes from the Tehuelche word “calafate,” which means “blueberry.” The town is situated on the shore of Lake Argentino, the largest freshwater lake in Argentina.

The town’s main attraction is the Perito Moreno Glacier, a 250 km2 glacier that is one of the only glaciers in the world that is still growing. It’s located in Los Glaciares National Park, which was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1981. The national park is part of the Southern Patagonian Ice Field, which is the third-largest icefield in the world.

List of Places to Visit in El Calafate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

El Calafate has several interesting cultural attractions that visitors should not miss. The Glaciarium is a museum dedicated to the study and dissemination of glaciers. The museum has interactive exhibits, a panoramic theater, and a sensorial room that allows visitors to experience the sensations of being in a glacier. Another museum worth visiting is the Historical Interpretation Center, which displays the history of the region and the impact of the indigenous people.

Natural Attractions of El Calafate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There are several fantastic natural attractions to explore in El Calafate, and the best way to experience them is by hiking or trekking. The Perito Moreno Glacier is undoubtedly the main attraction, and it can be observed from several viewpoints or by walking on the glacier. The Upsala Glacier is also a great place to visit, offering boating and hiking opportunities.

For nature lovers, the Torres del Paine National Park is a stunning destination to explore. The park is home to soaring peaks, glaciers, rivers, and lakes. It’s also one of the best places in the world to spot wildlife, including guanacos, foxes, and condors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Calafate

The area around El Calafate is a hiker’s paradise, with several well-marked trails offering breathtaking views of the mountains, lakes, and glaciers. The Huemul Circuit is a popular multi-day hike that takes visitors through forests, glaciers, and mountains. Those looking for an easier trek should check out the Laguna Nimez Reserve, which is a bird-watching paradise.

Best Things to Do in El Calafate: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in El Calafate. Adventure enthusiasts can opt for ice trekking, rock climbing, mountain biking, and kayaking. There are also several guided tours available that allow visitors to learn more about the local culture and history of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Calafate

If you have a week to spare in El Calafate, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in El Calafate and explore the town center

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Perito Moreno Glacier and hike on the glacier

Day 3: Go on a day hike to Laguna de Los Tres in El Chaltén

Day 4: Visit the Upsala Glacier and Estancia Cristina

Day 5: Go horseback riding in Patagonia

Day 6: Explore the Torres del Paine National Park

Day 7: Depart El Calafate

Food and Drink Scene of El Calafate

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food in El Calafate, one cannot miss tasting Patagonian lamb, the region’s specialty. Another traditional staple is the mate, a South American drink that is traditionally consumed in a round and shared. Local restaurants also offer trout, deer, and king crab.

Local Beverages

The Patagonian beer is a must-try drink in El Calafate. Some of the local microbreweries offer refreshing and flavorful beers. Visitors can also sample Patagonian wines, made with Malbec, Merlot and Cabernet Sauvignon grapes.

Where to Stay in El Calafate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in El Calafate, ranging from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Those looking for luxury can consider hotels like Hotel Xelena, Mirador del Lago Hotel, and Eolo Patagonia. Budget travelers can opt for hostels like Hostel Lago argentino and Hostel del Glaciar Pioneros.

What to Buy in El Calafate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking for souvenirs, local artisans sell handmade crafts like handmade leather boots, ponchos, and knitwear. Other popular souvenirs include mate gourds and bombillas, boina hats, and handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of El Calafate

Patagonia was inhabited by indigenous people for thousands of years before the arrival of Europeans. Tehuelches were the largest group roaming the steppe, and traces of their culture still exist in the region. Visitors to El Calafate should respect the customs of locals, including the tradition of mate-sharing.

Best Time to Visit El Calafate

The best time to visit El Calafate is from November to March, which is summertime in Patagonia. Visitors can expect long days, up to 16 hours of sunlight, ideal for exploring the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Calafate

Do:

  • Dress in layers, as weather conditions can change suddenly, even in summer.
  • Use sunscreen.
  • Respect the environment and fauna.
  • Bring cash, as some places do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any trash in natural areas.
  • Approach or feed local animals.
  • Ignore safety instructions when on a guided tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Calafate

El Calafate is considered a safe place for tourists. Visitors should take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded areas or on public transport.

Health in El Calafate

El Calafate is generally considered a low-risk area for infectious diseases. Visitors should be up to date with their routine vaccinations. It’s worth noting that the town is at a relatively high elevation, which may cause altitude sickness, especially for those with underlying health conditions.

Getting to El Calafate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Calafate

The Comandante Armando Tola International Airport (FTE) is the main airport in El Calafate. Several airlines, including Aerolineas Argentinas, LATAM, and Sky Airlines serve the airport.

Bus Stations Near El Calafate

For those traveling by bus, the town has a bus terminal. Several bus companies, including Andesmar, Don Otto, and Taqsa operate routes from major towns and cities in Argentina.

Other Options

One can also drive to El Calafate from other nearby towns and cities, such as El Chaltén and Puerto Natales in Chile. Rental cars are available in El Calafate, but it’s worth noting that some roads in the area can be challenging to drive on, especially in winter.

How to Get Around El Calafate

The town center of El Calafate is small and easily walkable. Those looking to travel outside of the town can use taxis, buses, or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from El Calafate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

El Calafate is an excellent place to start exploring Patagonia, with several nearby destinations worth visiting. El Chaltén, located about three hours from El Calafate, offers several hiking and trekking opportunities, including the famous Fitz Roy Trek. Puerto Natales in Chile is also a popular destination for adventure travelers and offers the opportunity to explore part of the Torres del Paine National Park.

Conclusion

El Calafate is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience Patagonia’s stunning landscapes, wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. With plenty of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine, it’s a great place to spend a week or longer. By following the tips in this tour guide, you can ensure that your visit to El Calafate is a memorable experience.
