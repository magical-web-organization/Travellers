Introduction to Chaco

Chaco is a fascinating province in Argentina known for its diverse landscapes, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Located in the north-eastern region of the country, bordering Bolivia and Paraguay, Chaco has a rich history and culture deeply intertwined with the indigenous people who have inhabited the region for centuries. With its lush green forests, expansive wetlands, and unique wildlife, Chaco offers an ideal setting for those seeking to explore nature at its finest.

List of Places to Visit in Chaco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. El Palmar National Park - a UNESCO world heritage site celebrated for its palm trees and diverse flora and fauna.
  2. Toba Indigenous Museum - an interactive museum that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Toba indigenous people in Chaco.
  3. Resistencia Historical Museum - an informative museum that traces the history of Chaco from the pre-Columbian era to the present.
  4. Nuestra Señora de las Mercedes Church - a beautiful 18th-century church located in the city of Resistencia.
  5. The San Francisco Solano Chapel - A Jesuit mission chapel built-in 1911.

Natural Attractions of Chaco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Paseo Costanero - A popular urban waterfront park in Resistencia with sports and cultural activities.
  2. The Impenetrable Forest - One of the largest native forests in Argentina and a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.
  3. Iberá National Park - A wetland sanctuary that houses a diverse range of wildlife such as giant otters, capybaras, and anacondas.
  4. Cuevas de las Manos - A cave system featuring ancient rock art spanning from 10,000 to 2,000 years ago.
  5. The Córdoba Hill - A beautiful hill station surrounded by lush vegetation and natural beauty. It’s famous for its trekking routes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chaco

Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Chaco are:

  • Roque Sáenz Peña to Las Breñas - A scenic route that takes you through expansive landscapes, small towns, and picturesque countryside.
  • The Pilcomayo River - An ideal location for canoeing and kayaking.
  • Laguna Limpia - A crystal clear lagoon located in the heart of the Impenetrable forest ideal for hiking and picnics.
  • El Palmar National Park – A great spot for hiking and walking trails.

Best Things to Do in Chaco: Activities and Tours

  1. Nature Safari Tour - A guided tour of the Iberá National Park, where you can spot a wide range of bird and animal species.
  2. Paragliding - An exhilarating activity that offers breathtaking views of the landscapes of Chaco.
  3. River Rafting - A thrilling experience that takes you through the turbulent waters of the Pilcomayo River.
  4. Wildlife Watching - Head over to the Impenetrable forest for guided tours that take you up close and personal with the rich wildlife of Chaco.
  5. Visit Local Markets – Explore the local markets in resistencia for buying indigenous crafts and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Chaco

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Locro - a stew made with corn, potato, and meat.
  2. Empanadas - A traditional Argentinian pastry stuffed with meat, vegetables or cheese.
  3. Tamales - A steamed corn-based dish stuffed with meat, peppers, and spices.
  4. Asado - An Argentinian barbecue tradition of grilling beef slowly over charcoal.
  5. Chipá - A gluten-free bread made from cassava starch and cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Mate - A traditional South American drink made by infusing tea leaves in hot water.
  2. Terere - A refreshing drink made by steeping yerba mate in cold water and served with ice.
  3. Juices – Try the local natural juices made from fruits like oranges and grapefruits.
  4. Chicha – A traditional South American beverage made from fermented maize.

Where to Stay in Chaco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Amerian - A modern and comfortable hotel located in Resistencia offering first-class amenities and services.
  2. Hotel Samritan - Centrally located in Resistencia, this hotel is a great option for budget travelers looking for a comfortable stay.
  3. Villa Río Bermejito El Descanso del Guerrero – An inexpensive yet welcoming guesthouse that offers a cozy and comfortable stay amidst the natural scenic beauty of Chaco.
  4. Eco Lodge Iberá - a cozy eco-lodge located within Iberá National Park offering a unique lodging experience.

What to Buy in Chaco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Indigenous Crafts– The local markets in resistencia offer a wide range of indigenous crafts made by the local indigenous people.
  2. Textiles – Invest in beautiful and colorful textiles, shawls and ponchos crafted by skilled artisans.
  3. Handmade Jewellery - Take home exquisite and handcrafted jewelry made from silver and semiprecious stones.
  4. Leather Products – Take home fine quality leather products such as belts, bags, and shoes from the local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Chaco

The population of Chaco is multicultural, shaped by the influence of the indigenous people and European immigrants like the Spaniards and Italians. The music, art, and cuisine of Chaco showcases this diversity, and the people are known for their hospitality and warmth towards tourists.

Best Time to Visit Chaco

The best time to visit Chaco is between May and September when the weather is pleasant and dry, making it ideal for exploring the region’s stunning landscapes and wildlife. During summer, the temperatures can soar, and the rains can be unpredictable, causing flooding in some areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chaco

Do:

  1. Use insect repellent regularly to prevent mosquito and other insect bites.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages to experience the authentic taste of Chaco.
  3. Learn about the indigenous culture and their way of life for a more enriching experience.
  4. Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.
  5. Tipping is not mandatory in Chaco, but it’s a common practice to tip service staff in restaurants and cafes.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter in Chaco’s pristine natural landscapes.
  2. Do not intrude on the privacy of the indigenous people or their customs.
  3. Do not try to touch or feed the wildlife in the national parks and reserves.
  4. Do not flaunt expensive jewelry or gadgets, as it can make you vulnerable to theft or robbery.
  5. Do not neglect safety precautions while hiking or participating in adventure activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chaco

Chaco is generally a safe place for travelers, but it’s advisable to exercise caution and stay alert in crowded areas and at night. Avoid carrying a lot of valuables, use only licensed taxis, and avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas.

Health in Chaco

Travelers to Chaco should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever, yellow fever, and malaria. Stay updated with vaccinations and carry mosquito repellents, long-sleeved clothing, and use a mosquito net while sleeping.

Getting to Chaco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chaco

Chaco has two airports: Resistencia International Airport and Formosa International Airport. Both airports receive domestic flights from major cities in Argentina.

Bus Stations Near Chaco

Resistencia and Formosa have bus stations that receive regular long-distance bus services from Buenos Aires and other major cities in Argentina.

Other Options

Other options to get to Chaco include driving or hiring private cars. Note that while driving in Chaco is relatively safe, it’s advisable to be familiar with the local traffic rules and regulations.

How to Get Around in Chaco

Traveling by public transport in Chaco is affordable and relatively convenient. Taxis and car rental services are readily available in major cities like Resistencia. Biking is also a great way to explore the region’s stunning natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Chaco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The region’s proximity to neighboring countries like Brazil and Paraguay makes it an ideal gateway for exploring other South American destinations. Other popular destinations close to Chaco include the Salta Province, the Misiones Province, and the Esteros del Iberá.

Conclusion

Chaco is a hidden gem in Argentina offering a diverse range of cultural, natural, and adventure experiences. From the lush green forests to the expansive wetlands, the province offers a stunning backdrop for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Explore the indigenous culture, indulge in the local cuisine, and experience the warmth of the locals as you travel through the breathtaking landscapes of Chaco.
