Introduction to Capilla del Monte

Capilla del Monte is a small town located in the Pampas region of Argentina, known for its breathtaking mountains, perfect for hiking and outdoor activities. It is also a hub for spiritual seekers as it is home to several mystical sites.

With a population of just over 10,000, Capilla del Monte is a peaceful destination, with a laid-back vibe. For travelers looking to experience a unique combination of natural beauty, spiritualism, and adventure, a visit to Capilla del Monte is highly recommended.

In this travel guide, we will provide you with an in-depth understanding of the best things to do in Capilla del Monte, where to stay, what to eat, and insider tips to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Capilla del Monte

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Uritorco Hill

Uritorco Hill is perhaps the most famous attraction in Capilla del Monte. With an altitude of 1949 meters, it is the highest mountain in the region. Located at the base of the Sierras Chicas, Uritorco is considered a spiritual energy center by many ancient cultures. You can embark on a trek to the peak, which takes approximately 5 hours. The summit offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

El Zapato

El Zapato (The Shoe) is a pair of mountain formations that look like a shoe when viewed from a distance. According to the locals, it represents the struggle of the working-class people. The hike to the top of El Zapato takes about 2 hours and is relatively easy.

La Techada

La Techada (The Roof) is a natural rock shelter located on the slope of Uritorco. The hike to the shelter takes about an hour and a half, and it is one of the most scenic hikes in Capilla del Monte. It is believed that the shelter was built by indigenous tribes for religious ceremonies.

Archaeological Museum

The Archaeological Museum of Capilla del Monte showcases the history of the town and the region. It exhibits pre-Columbian artifacts, fossils, and other cultural items that help visitors understand the area’s history and different cultures that have contributed to it.

Natural Attractions of Capilla del Monte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Los Terrones

Los Terrones is a beautiful natural formation of granite rocks, formed by the erosion of wind and water. The rock formations offer visitors an unforgettable landscape to behold. The hike to the top of Los Terrones offers an impressive view of the valley, and it’s worth the effort.

Ongamira

Ongamira is a natural reserve of diverse landscapes, flora, and fauna, located about 40 km southwest of Capilla del Monte. There are several trekking trails that lead to breathtaking waterfalls, ruins of pre-Columbian cities, and spectacular scenic views.

Cerro Overo

Cerro Overo is a hill located eight kilometers north of Capilla del Monte. The hill has a unique reddish hue due to the presence of iron oxide. The trek to the hill offers stunning views of the surrounding area and is a must-visit destination for photography enthusiasts.

Dique El Cajón

Located on the edge of the Punilla Valley, Dique el Cajón is a beautiful dam that offers stunning scenic views and is an excellent spot for fishing and water sports. Visitors can also enjoy horseback riding and hiking in the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Capilla del Monte

Capilla del Monte has a wide range of hiking trails suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers. The trails offer spectacular views of the mountains and valleys and are a great way to explore the area’s natural beauty.

Hiking Routes

Cerro Uritorco Trek

The trek to the summit of Cerro Uritorco takes around 5 hours and is suitable for experienced hikers. It’s the highest peak in the region and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking to Los Terrones

The hike to Los Terrones takes around 2 hours and is relatively easy. It offers impressive views of the valley and is perfect for nature lovers and casual hikers.

Trekking to Dique El Cajón

The trek to Dique El Cajón takes around 3 hours and is moderate in difficulty. It offers views of the dam’s blue waters and the surrounding natural landscapes.

Biking Routes

Ruta 38

Route 38 is a popular cycling route in the area, running 370 km long, starting in Rosario de la Frontera in Salta and ending in Capilla del Monte. It passes through the picturesque Punilla Valley and is suitable for experienced bikers.

La Cumbrecita Circuit

The La Cumbrecita Circuit is a mountain bike trail that starts in Capilla del Monte and ends in La Cumbrecita. The circuit is around 50 km long and offers scenic views of the mountains and valleys along the way. It is an excellent option for intermediate bikers.

Ascochinga Circuit

The Ascochinga Circuit is a popular cycling route for beginners, running 60 km long. It starts in Capilla del Monte and passes through the stunning Ascochinga Valley. The route offers views of the Sierras Chicas and the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Capilla del Monte: Activities and Tours

Esoteric Tours

The town of Capilla del Monte is famously known as a spiritual center. Esoteric tours are the most popular tours in the area and cater to spiritual seekers. The tours take visitors to different energy centers, sacred places, and vortexes that are believed to be spiritually significant.

Horseback Riding

Exploring the region on horseback is a popular activity in Capilla del Monte. It offers visitors a unique perspective on the natural beauty of the area. Horseback riding tours usually take visitors through the beautiful landscapes of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Paragliding

Paragliding is a thrilling and exciting activity to try in Capilla del Monte. The activity offers amazing views of the region and is perfect for adrenaline seekers. The best time to enjoy paragliding in Capilla del Monte is during spring and summer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Capilla del Monte

If you’re planning to spend a week in Capilla del Monte, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and City Tour

On the first day of your trip, take a city tour to familiarize yourself with the town and its history. Head to the Archaeological Museum to learn about the region’s pre-Columbian history.

Day 2: Hike to Uritorco

On the second day, hike to the summit of Uritorco Hill. It is a challenging hike, but the view from the top is worth the effort.

Day 3: Visit Los Terrones

Take a trip to Los Terrones and explore the natural rock formations. The hike to the top offers stunning views of the valley.

Day 4: Esoteric Tour

Take an esoteric tour and explore the spiritual side of Capilla del Monte. Visit various energy centers and vortexes that are believed to have spiritual significance.

Day 5: Horseback Riding

On the fifth day, take a horseback riding tour through the mountainous terrain. It offers a unique perspective on the natural beauty of the region and is a must-try activity.

Day 6: Relaxation Day

Spend the day relaxing and enjoying the natural beauty of the region. Head to Dique El Cajón and indulge in water sports or fishing.

Day 7: Departure

On your last day, take a paragliding tour and say goodbye to Capilla del Monte with a bird’s eye view of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Capilla del Monte

Must-Try Dishes

Empanadas

Empanadas are a popular dish in Argentina, and Capilla del Monte serves some of the best. The traditional empanadas are made with ground meat, onions, and spices, baked until golden brown.

Locro

Locro is a traditional Argentine stew made with corn, beans, meat, and vegetables. It’s a hearty dish that is perfect for cold winter days.

Tacos de Trigo

Tacos de Trigo is a vegetarian dish made with wheat tortillas, soy meat, and vegetables. It’s a healthy and delicious option for those looking for a meat-free meal.

Local Beverages

Fernet with Coca Cola

Fernet is a popular Argentine drink made from herbs and spices. When mixed with Coca Cola, it becomes a refreshing and potent cocktail that is enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Mate

Mate is a traditional Argentine tea made with yerba mate leaves. It has a refreshing taste and is usually served with sugar or honey.

Wine

Argentina is known for its excellent wine, and Capilla del Monte is no exception. The region produces high-quality wines, and visitors should try a glass or two during their stay.

Where to Stay in Capilla del Monte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget Accommodations

Hostel Tangoinn

Hostel Tangoinn is a popular hostel located in the city center, offering excellent facilities at an affordable price. The hostel has a swimming pool, a terrace, and a bar.

Los Tres Gomez Hostel

Los Tres Gomez Hostel is located in a quiet residential area, offering tranquil accommodations. The hostel has a garden and a communal kitchen, making it perfect for budget travelers.

Mid-Range Accommodations

Hostería La Guarida

Hostería La Guarida is a charming guesthouse located in the heart of Capilla del Monte. The rooms are comfortable and spacious, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

Posada de Las Águilas

Posada de Las Águilas is a beautiful hotel located on the slope of Uritorco Hill, offering magnificent views of the surrounding mountains. The rooms are cozy and well-equipped, and the hotel has a spa and an outdoor pool.

Luxury Accommodations

Terrazas del Uritorco

Terrazas del Uritorco is a luxurious resort located on the outskirts of Capilla del Monte, offering stunning views of Uritorco Hill. The suites are spacious and elegantly decorated, and the hotel has an outdoor pool and a spa.

Huerta Grande Cabanas

Huerta Grande Cabanas is a beautiful resort located in a tranquil area, offering a unique blend of luxury and nature. The cabins are spacious and well-equipped, and the resort has two swimming pools and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Capilla del Monte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gemstones

Capilla del Monte is famous for its gemstones, and visitors can find a wide range of crystals, quartz, and other stones in the local shops. The gemstones make perfect souvenirs or gifts for loved ones.

Handicrafts

The region is also known for its handicrafts, including pottery and woven textiles. Visit the local markets to find unique and beautiful pieces.

Traditional Argentine Wine

Capilla del Monte is near the wine-growing regions of Mendoza and San Juan, making it an ideal place to buy traditional Argentine wine. Visitors can find high-quality wine at local wine shops or wineries.

Local Culture and Customs of Capilla del Monte

Capilla del Monte is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. The region has a strong indigenous culture, and visitors can find traces of it in local customs, food, and art. The people are warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Capilla del Monte

The best time to visit Capilla del Monte is from September to April when the weather is warm and dry. The summer months of December to February can be hot, but it’s also a popular time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Capilla del Monte

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take an esoteric tour and explore the spiritual side of Capilla del Monte.
  • Visit the region’s natural attractions, such as Uritorco hill, Los Terrones, and Ongamira.
  • Try the local cuisine, including empanadas and locro.
  • Explore the region on horseback or paragliding.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect the natural beauty of the region.
  • Do not engage in activities that may harm the environment.
  • Do not litter or harm the wildlife.
  • Do not ignore the warnings of your tour guide or local authorities.
  • Do not disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Capilla del Monte

Capilla del Monte is a safe place for travelers, with low crime rates. Visitors should take usual precautions and avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.

Health in Capilla del Monte

The region has good healthcare facilities, but visitors are advised to drink bottled water to avoid getting sick. Check with your doctor before traveling to Capilla del Monte to ensure that you have the necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Capilla del Monte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Capilla del Monte

The nearest airport to Capilla del Monte is Ingeniero Ambrosio Taravella International Airport, located in Cordoba City, about 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Capilla del Monte

The bus station in Capilla del Monte has regular services to cities such as Cordoba City and Carlos Paz.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to travel to Capilla del Monte.

How to Get Around in Capilla del Monte

The best way to get around in Capilla del Monte is by car or motorcycle. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Capilla del Monte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cordoba City

Cordoba City is the second-largest city in Argentina and is known for its rich colonial heritage and vibrant culture. It’s a popular tourist destination, with several museums, galleries, and landmarks.

Santa Rosa de Calamuchita

Santa Rosa de Calamuchita is a charming town located about 75 km from Capilla del Monte. It has several attractions, including La Cascada del Rincon, a stunning waterfall, and the region’s many hot springs.

Capilla del Monte is a unique destination, offering a blend of natural beauty, spirituality, and adventure. With a wide range of activities to choose from, it’s a perfect spot for outdoor enthusiasts and spiritual seekers. The town’s rich culture, warm hospitality, and excellent food scene make it a great destination for all.
