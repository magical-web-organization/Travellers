Introduction to Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Located in the heart of Argentina’s Pampas region, Córdoba is the second-largest province in the country after Buenos Aires. Blessed with a pleasant climate, stunning landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage, Córdoba makes for an excellent travel destination for history buffs, adventure-seekers, and foodies alike. With our comprehensive travel guide to Córdoba, you’ll discover the province’s many treasures and hidden gems that make it a must-visit destination in South America.

List of Places to Visit in Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Córdoba is a hub of Argentina’s history, culture, and architecture. From ancient sites to colonial-era buildings, the province has a plethora of museums and historical places to explore. A few notable attractions include:

  • Jesuit Block and Estancias of Córdoba: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that includes five estancias and a campus that was established by Jesuits in the 17th century.
  • Córdoba Cathedral: A magnificent colonial-era cathedral that dates back to the 17th century.
  • Paseo del Buen Pastor: A cultural center that hosts art exhibitions, concerts, and other cultural events.
  • Emilio E. Caraffa Fine Arts Museum: A splendid museum that houses works of art by Argentinean artists.

Natural Attractions of Córdoba (Province, Argentina): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Córdoba is also home to some of the most breathtaking natural wonders in Argentina. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or just looking to unwind amidst scenic beauty, Córdoba has something for all. Some of the popular attractions include:

  • Sierra de Córdoba: Home to various mountains with stunning views, hiking trails, and waterfalls.
  • Quebrada del Condorito National Park: A nature reserve located at the foothills of the Andean mountain range that features spectacular landscapes, rare plants, and rare bird species.
  • La Cumbrecita: A picturesque village situated amidst the forest-covered mountains, teeming with charming cabins, rivers, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Avid hikers and cyclists would find Córdoba to be an excellent destination to explore on foot or a bike. The province boasts of several exciting biking and hiking routes that offer breathtaking views of the mountains and surroundings. Some of the popular options include:

  • Cerro Uritorco: A challenging trek through the mountains covering two days.
  • Valle de los Lisos: A scenic trek along the Quebrada del Condorito National Park.
  • Sierras Chicas: A trail covered with stunning views of the mountain ranges and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Córdoba (Province, Argentina): Activities and Tours

Córdoba is a province that offers a diverse range of activities and tours that cater to all travelers’ interests. From cultural walks to adventure sports, the region has it all. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit the Jesuit Block and Estancias of Córdoba.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic landscapes.
  • Explore the ancient underground city of Alta Gracia.
  • Go rock climbing or white water rafting on the rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Must-Try Dishes

Argentinian cuisine is known for its diverse and mouth-watering dishes, and Córdoba is no exception. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Asado: A popular barbeque dish that features various meats cooked over an open flame.
  • Choripan: A sandwich made with grilled sausage and chimichurri sauce.
  • Empanadas: Tasty baked or fried dough stuffed with meats, vegetables, or cheese.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Córdoba has plenty of options to offer. Some of the best local beverages include:

  • Fernet con Coca: A traditional drink made with Fernet, Coke, and ice.
  • Mate: An herbal tea-like beverage that’s a popular drink among Argentinians.
  • Wine: Córdoba boasts of some of Argentina’s best wine regions, and a glass of wine is a must-try while in the province.

Where to Stay in Córdoba (Province, Argentina): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Córdoba has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in the province include:

  • InterContinental Córdoba Hotel: A five-star hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Amérian Carlos Paz & Convention Center: A luxury hotel situated near the Lake San Roque.
  • Backpackers Cordoba: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Córdoba (Province, Argentina): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Córdoba is a province that’s rich in culture and traditions, and there’s plenty of local products to buy as souvenirs. Some of the best products to bring home include:

  • Leather goods: Córdoba is known for its high-quality leather products, including jackets, bags, and belts.
  • Handwoven blankets and rugs: Made by the indigenous populations living in the region’s remote areas.
  • Traditional pottery: Handcrafted and adorned by the local potters, these pottery pieces are unique and make for excellent souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Córdoba is a province with a rich cultural heritage. The locals take pride in their traditions, which are a mix of indigenous, colonial-era, and modern influences. Some of the customs and traditions worth knowing include:

  • The Carnaval de la Quebrada: A festival that takes place every February and is marked by colorful costumes, dance, and music.
  • Mate drinking: A staple daily ritual that’s deeply ingrained in the local culture.
  • Gaucho traditions: Córdoba is home to several ranches and farms, and the gauchos’ traditions are a vital part of the province’s identity.

Best Time to Visit Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Córdoba has a moderate climate throughout the year, but the best time to visit the province is between March and May, and between September and November. The weather is pleasant, and there’s a lower risk of rain compared to other months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including asado, empanadas, and choripan.
  • Explore the natural wonders of the province, including the Sierras de Córdoba and Quebrada del Condorito National Park.
  • Take part in the local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the altitude. The province’s high-altitude landscapes can be challenging, especially for those not accustomed to it.
  • Don’t disrespect the locals’ traditions and customs.
  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and water, especially when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Córdoba is a relatively safe province, but like every place, there are a few things to bear in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone in dimly lit areas at night.
  • Don’t carry valuable items unnecessarily while travelling.

Health in Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Córdoba has excellent medical facilities, but it’s always a good idea to take necessary precautions before travelling. Here are a few health tips:

  • Get vaccinated before you travel.
  • Wash your hands frequently and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Bring necessary medications and first-aid kits.

Getting to Córdoba (Province, Argentina): Transportation Options and Logistics

Córdoba can be reached via air, land, and sea. The province has several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

  • Ingeniero Aeronáutico Ambrosio L.V. Taravella International Airport, located in the city of Córdoba, is the province’s main airport.

Bus Stations Near Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

  • The Córdoba Bus Terminal, located in the city of Córdoba, is the main bus station in the province.

Other Options

  • You can also travel to Córdoba via train, with several long-distance trains running through the province.

How to Get Around in Córdoba (Province, Argentina)

Córdoba provides several options for getting around, including public transportation, taxis, and rental cars. Most cities in the province have reliable bus services that are inexpensive and easy to use. Taxis are available in towns and cities, but it’s essential to negotiate the fare before boarding. Rental cars are another option, and several rental companies operate in the province.

Where to Go Next from Córdoba (Province, Argentina): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other places around Córdoba, several destinations can be visited from the province. A few noteworthy options include:

  • Villa Carlos Paz: A popular lakeside resort town located near the City of Córdoba.
  • Mendoza: A province with beautiful wine regions and stunning landscapes, located south of Córdoba.
  • San Juan: A province with rich history and stunning mountain landscapes, accessible via car or plane from Córdoba.

Conclusion

Córdoba is a rich and diverse province that has something for everyone. With its culture, scenic landscapes, and outdoor activities, Córdoba provides a unique travel experience that’s both enjoyable and educational. Whether you’re looking for adventure, history, or a simple getaway, Córdoba is a destination worthy of your visit.
