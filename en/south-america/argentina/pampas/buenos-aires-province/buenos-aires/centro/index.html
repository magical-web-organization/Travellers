Introduction to Centro

Centro is the heart of Buenos Aires, the capital city of Argentina. Located in the Buenos Aires Province, in the Pampas region, this vibrant neighborhood offers a mix of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and modern amenities. Centro is known for its iconic avenues, such as Avenida de Mayo and 9 de Julio, as well as for its famous landmarks like the Obelisk and the Casa Rosada.

This comprehensive travel guide includes insider tips and recommendations on the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Centro, Buenos Aires. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, Centro has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Centro

Centro is home to many cultural and natural attractions. Here’s a list of must-visit places in the neighborhood:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Rosada: This iconic pink palace is the official residence of the President of Argentina. Visitors can take guided tours of the building and learn more about the country’s political history.

  • Teatro Colón: This world-renowned opera house is not only a stunning architectural masterpiece, but it’s also home to some of the best performances in Buenos Aires.

  • The National Museum of Fine Arts: One of the largest fine arts museums in Latin America, this museum has an extensive collection of works from Argentine and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Centro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plaza de Mayo: This public square is the central hub of Buenos Aires, surrounded by landmark buildings like the Casa Rosada and the Metropolitan Cathedral. It’s a great place to people-watch and enjoy a cup of coffee.

  • Reserva Ecológica Costanera Sur: This ecological reserve is a green oasis in the middle of the city, with trails for hiking, bird-watching, and biking. It’s a popular spot for picnics and outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Centro

There are many great hiking and biking routes in and around Centro, including:

  • The Costanera Sur Ecological Reserve: With 350 hectares of natural wetlands and wildlife, there are endless trails for hiking and biking.

  • The Buenos Aires Ecological Reserve: This 865-acre nature reserve is located on the banks of the Rio de la Plata, and offers beautiful views of the river and the city.

Best Things to Do in Centro: Activities and Tours

Centro offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors, including:

  • Tango shows: Buenos Aires is the birthplace of tango, and Centro has many venues where you can watch live performances and take tango lessons.

  • Street art tours: Centro is home to some of the best street art in Buenos Aires, and there are many guided tours that showcase the vibrant murals and graffiti around the neighborhood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Centro

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring Centro:

Day 1: Start your trip by visiting the Casa Rosada and taking a tour of the historic building.

Day 2: Spend a day exploring the National Museum of Fine Arts and the nearby parks and plazas.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Teatro Colón, and end the day with a tango lesson and show at one of the nearby dance halls.

Day 4: Explore the Costanera Sur Ecological Reserve by bike or on foot, and enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.

Day 5: Take a street art tour of Centro and nearby neighborhoods.

Day 6: Visit the Obelisk and the nearby museums and landmarks, such as the Museo de Arte Contemporáneo de Buenos Aires.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene in Centro, trying some of the traditional Argentinean dishes and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Centro

Centro has a thriving food and drink scene, with a mix of traditional Argentinean cuisine and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: This is Argentina’s national dish, and it’s a must-try for meat lovers. Asado is a barbecue-style meal that includes grilled meats, such as beef, pork, and chicken, and Argentinean side dishes like chimichurri and empanadas.

  • Milanesa: This is a breaded and fried beef or chicken cutlet, typically served with mashed potatoes or French fries.

Local Beverages

  • Malbec wine: Argentina is known for its wine, and Malbec is the most popular variety. It’s a red wine that pairs well with meats and other Argentinean dishes.

  • Mate: This is a traditional Argentinean drink that’s made from dried leaves of the yerba mate plant and hot water. Mate is often shared among friends and family, and it’s a big part of Argentinean culture.

Where to Stay in Centro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Centro offers a variety of options for accommodations, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hilton Buenos Aires: This luxury hotel is located in Puerto Madero, just a short walk from Centro. It offers stunning views of the city and the river.

  • Milhouse Hostel: Located in the heart of Centro, this popular hostel offers budget-friendly accommodations and a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in Centro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Centro has many souvenir shops and markets where you can find unique gifts and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Tango shoes and clothing: Centro is the birthplace of tango, and there are many shops that sell tango shoes and clothing.

  • Leather goods: Argentina is known for its leather products, such as jackets, bags, and belts, which are often sold in Centro.

Local Culture and Customs of Centro

Centro is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood, with a rich history and culture. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture and customs:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases: While many people in Buenos Aires speak English, it’s always helpful to know some basic Spanish phrases.

  • Be respectful of Argentinean customs: For example, it’s common to greet people with a kiss on the cheek, and to eat dinner late in the evening.

Best Time to Visit Centro

The best time to visit Centro is during the fall (March to May) or spring (September to November) when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months (December to February) can be hot and humid, while the winters (June to August) are cool and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centro

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Centro:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the steak and wine.

  • Do take advantage of the many free walking tours available in Centro.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water – always stick to bottled water.

  • Don’t wear flashy jewelry or carry large amounts of cash, as pickpocketing can be a problem in some areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Centro

Centro is generally a safe neighborhood, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.

  • Keep your valuables out of sight and be aware of your surroundings in crowded public spaces.

Health in Centro

Argentina has a good healthcare system, but it’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses in case of an emergency. It’s also recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to Argentina, especially for hepatitis A and B, and typhoid.

Getting to Centro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Centro

The closest airport to Centro is Aeropuerto Internacional Ministro Pistarini, also known as Ezeiza International Airport, which is located about 20 miles south of Buenos Aires. From there, you can take a taxi or a shuttle bus to Centro.

Bus Stations Near Centro

The main bus station in Buenos Aires is the Retiro Bus Station, located about 2 miles northeast of Centro. From there, you can take a taxi or public transportation to Centro.

Other Options

There are also several train stations in Buenos Aires, as well as ferry services to neighboring Uruguay.

How to Get Around in Centro

Centro is a walkable neighborhood, but there are also many public transportation options, including buses, subways, and taxis. The subway is a good option for getting around quickly and efficiently, while taxis are ideal for longer trips or traveling at night.

Where to Go Next from Centro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Centro is a great starting point for exploring Buenos Aires and the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Recoleta: This elegant neighborhood is known for its historic cemetery, art museums, and upscale shops and restaurants.

  • Tigre: Located about 30 minutes north of Buenos Aires, Tigre is a popular day trip destination for its scenic delta and riverfront attractions.

Conclusion

Centro is a vibrant and exciting neighborhood in the heart of Buenos Aires, offering a mix of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in history, food, or nature, there’s something for everyone in Centro. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this dynamic neighborhood.
