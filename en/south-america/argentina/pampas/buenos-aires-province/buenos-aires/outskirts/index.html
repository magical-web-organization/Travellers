Introduction to Outskirts

Welcome to Outskirts, a charming town located in the outskirts of Buenos Aires, Argentina. Nestled in the heart of Pampas, Outskirts offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to explore the best places to visit, things to do, hiking and biking routes, local food, where to stay, and travel tips to make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Outskirts

Whether you’re interested in museums, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply looking to relax and enjoy scenic views, Outskirts has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the best places to visit in Outskirts:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Outskirts Museum: Explore the rich history and culture of Outskirts at this museum, featuring exhibits on local festivals, traditional crafts, and regional cuisine.
  • San Juan Bautista Church: Admire the stunning architecture of this 17th-century church, which is a perfect example of colonial style.
  • La Pampilla: Discover the legacy of gaucho culture at this historical site, featuring a monument to the legendary cowboy, Martín Fierro.

Natural Attractions of Outskirts: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural de la Lagunas: Immerse yourself in nature at this scenic park, which features several lagoons, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  • El Jagüel Nature Reserve: Explore the diverse ecosystem of this nature reserve, which is home to several species of birds, butterflies, and other wildlife.
  • Mirador del Azul: Enjoy breathtaking views of Pampas from this lookout point, which offers panoramic vistas of the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outskirts

The stunning nature trails in Outskirts offer the perfect opportunity for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Lagunas Trail: This hiking trail takes you through Parque Natural de la Lagunas, offering stunning views of the lagoons and surrounding countryside.
  • Jagüel Trail: Biking enthusiasts will enjoy this scenic route through El Jagüel Nature Reserve, which features diverse terrain and plenty of wildlife.
  • Sierra del Tigre Trail: This challenging hiking trail takes you through the Sierra del Tigre mountain range, offering stunning views and a rewarding sense of accomplishment.

Best Things to Do in Outskirts: Activities and Tours

Outskirts has tons of activities and tours to offer for all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do during your stay:

  • Horseback Riding Tours: Experience the beauty of Pampas from horseback with tours that take you through the countryside and let you experience the local lifestyle.
  • Wine and Cheese Tours: Savor the flavors of the region with tours that take you to local wineries and dairy farms, giving you an authentic taste of Outskirts.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Soar above the stunning landscapes of Outskirts with hot air balloon rides that offer breathtaking views and a thrilling adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Outskirts

Outskirts offers a delicious array of local food and drinks that are sure to satisfy your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: No trip to Argentina is complete without trying the famous barbecue dish known as asado, which is a variety of grilled meats served with chimichurri sauce.
  • Empanadas: These baked or fried pastries are filled with a variety of meats, cheese, and vegetables, and are a popular snack or meal in Argentina.
  • Locro: This hearty stew is a traditional dish made with corn, meat, and vegetables, and is often served during festivals and special occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: This traditional Argentine drink is similar to tea, but is made with yerba mate leaves and hot water, and is often shared among friends or family.
  • Malbec Wine: Argentina is famous for its Malbec wine, which is a full-bodied red wine that pairs well with grilled meats and hearty stews.
  • Fernet con Coca: This herbal liqueur is often mixed with Coca-Cola and is a popular drink among locals.

Where to Stay in Outskirts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Outskirts offers a variety of accommodation options to suit your budget and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Outskirts:

  • Posada del Jagüel: This charming guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a tranquil atmosphere, perfect for relaxing after a day of exploring.
  • Hotel Del Pilar: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location, close to many popular attractions and restaurants.
  • Hostel La Estación: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style accommodations and a communal kitchen, perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Outskirts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some souvenirs or local products from Outskirts, here are some items to consider:

  • Leather Goods: Argentina is known for its high-quality leather products, including shoes, belts, and jackets.
  • Mate Gourds and Bombillas: Bring home a traditional mate set, which includes a gourd and a metal straw called a bombilla, to continue enjoying the popular Argentine drink at home.
  • Horsehair Crafts: Explore the local craft markets for unique souvenirs made of horsehair, including bracelets, necklaces, and keychains.

Local Culture and Customs of Outskirts

Argentine culture is characterized by a strong sense of national identity, passion for soccer, and a love of food and drink. In Outskirts, you’ll also find a deep appreciation for traditional gauchos and their lifestyle, including horseback riding, cattle farming, and mate-drinking.

As with any culture, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions and to learn about the local way of life. Greetings such as a kiss on the cheek are common, and punctuality is not as important as in some other cultures.

Best Time to Visit Outskirts

The climate in Outskirts is mild and temperate year-round, making it a great destination to visit anytime. However, the best time to visit is during the spring and fall months of September to November and March to May, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller than during peak summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outskirts

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Outskirts:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks, including asado, empanadas, mate, and Malbec wine.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region through hiking trails and outdoor adventures.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs, including the tradition of gauchos and their lifestyles.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip at restaurants and for services. Tipping is expected in Argentina, especially for waitstaff and hotel staff.
  • Assume everyone speaks English. While some locals may speak English, it’s always appreciated to attempt to speak Spanish or bring a translator app with you.
  • Discard or disrespect the mate gourd. It is considered rude to knock on the mate or remove the bombilla without being offered to do so. Respect the ritual of drinking mate and wait until it is offered to you.

Safety and Health

While Outskirts is generally a safe destination for travelers, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health during your trip.

Safety in Outskirts

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night or in less crowded areas.
  • Take care of your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items.
  • Consult with your accommodation provider or a reputable travel guide before entering isolated or unknown areas.

Health in Outskirts

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid the risk of stomach illness.
  • Apply insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases such as dengue fever.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect your skin from the sun, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Outskirts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Outskirts is located approximately 100 kilometers from the center of Buenos Aires. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Outskirts

  • Ezeiza International Airport (EZE): Located approximately 60 kilometers from Outskirts.

Bus Stations Near Outskirts

  • Retiro Bus Terminal: Located in the center of Buenos Aires and offering connections to Outskirts.

Other Options

  • Private Transportation: You can also arrange for private transportation from Buenos Aires to Outskirts through a cab, shuttle or car rental.

How to Get Around in Outskirts

The best way to get around the town is by car or motorbike. Public transportation is available but can be less frequent and more time consuming.

Where to Go Next from Outskirts: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Antonio de Areco: Known as the birthplace of gaucho culture, this historic town is renowned for its traditional crafts, festivals, and folkloric music.
  • Sierra de la Ventana: Located in the province of Buenos Aires, this region offers stunning landscapes, outdoor activities, and charming rural villages.
