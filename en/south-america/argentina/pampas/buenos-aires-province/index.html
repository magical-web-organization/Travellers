Introduction to Buenos Aires (Province)

Buenos Aires (Province) is one of the most vibrant and populous provinces of Argentina, located in the Pampas region of South America. It is home to the country’s capital, Buenos Aires City, and boasts a rich cultural heritage, lush greenery, scenic views, high-end restaurants, and bustling nightlife.

List of Places to Visit in Buenos Aires (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Some of the most culturally significant attractions in Buenos Aires Province include:

  • The National Museum of Fine Arts: A stunning collection of Argentine and international art, located in Buenos Aires City.
  • La Plata Museum of Natural Sciences: A must-visit museum for nature enthusiasts, located in La Plata, which features over two million scientific specimens.
  • The Eva Perón Museum: Offering a glimpse into the life and legend of one of Argentina’s most famous political icons.
  • The San Isidro Cathedral: A breathtaking architectural wonder that serves as the seat of the Archbishop of Buenos Aires.

Natural Attractions of Buenos Aires (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, be sure to visit the following natural attractions in Buenos Aires Province:

  • Laguna de Rocha: This serene lagoon situated in Mar Chiquita offers stunning views of flamingos, herons, and other waterbirds.
  • Mar del Plata Beach: A popular beachfront destination, offering many water-based activities, such as swimming, surfing, and parasailing.
  • The Pampas Grasslands: A vast and undulating expanse of grassy plains and rolling hills, the Pampas is known for its cattle farming and breathtaking sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buenos Aires (Province)

Buenos Aires Province offers some great hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Sierra de la Ventana: A range of low-lying mountains and hills with numerous hiking and biking trails of various difficulty levels.
  • The Ecological Reserve of Vicente López: A nature reserve on the banks of the Rio de la Plata that offers excellent trails for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.
  • The Costanera Sur Reserve: Another stunning nature reserve located in Buenos Aires City, offering over 9 kilometers of trails for hikers, bikers, and birdwatchers.

Best Things to Do in Buenos Aires (Province): Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Buenos Aires Province include:

  • Tango shows and dance lessons: Buenos Aires is the birthplace of the tango, so why not experience a traditional show or take a lesson?
  • Wine tasting and vineyard tours: Argentina is famous for its Malbec wines, and there are many vineyards and wineries to explore in Buenos Aires Province.
  • Gastronomic tours: Buenos Aires Province is famous for its food scene, so be sure to try local delicacies such as empanadas, asado (grilled meat), and alfajores (a traditional sweet pastry).

Week-Long Itinerary for Buenos Aires (Province)

Here’s a suggested itinerary for spending a week in Buenos Aires Province:

  • Day 1 - Arrive in Buenos Aires City, check into your hotel, tour the various neighborhoods, and enjoy some local cuisine.
  • Day 2 - Visit the National Museum of Fine Arts, the Recoleta Cemetery, and go shopping for souvenirs.
  • Day 3 - Take a day trip to the Delta de Tigre or the San Antonio de Areco, two beautiful destinations in Buenos Aires Province.
  • Day 4 - Visit the Buenos Aires Botanical Garden and take a tour of the city’s famous street art.
  • Day 5 - Take a wine tasting tour of the Maipú wineries, and explore the town of Chacras de Coria.
  • Day 6 - Head out to Mar del Plata Beach for a day of sun and sand.
  • Day 7 - Visit the Ecological Reserve of Vicente López or the Costanera Sur Reserve before departing Buenos Aires.

Food and Drink Scene of Buenos Aires (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

Must-try dishes when visiting Buenos Aires Province include:

  • Asado: Grilled meat is a staple in Argentina, and no visit would be complete without trying it.
  • Empanadas: Delicious pastry pockets filled with meat, vegetables, or cheese - a perfect snack.
  • Milanesa: Thinly sliced beef, breaded and fried, served with a variety of sauces.
  • Alfajores: Sweet pastries filled with dulce de leche and covered in chocolate or powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Malbec wine: One of the most famous exports of Argentina, this full-bodied red can be found across Buenos Aires Province.
  • Mate: An herbal tea that is served in a traditional gourd and sipped through a metal straw, a staple of Argentine culture.
  • Fernet and Coca Cola: A popular combination of Argentine bitter herbal liqueur and Coca Cola, mixed together over ice.

Where to Stay in Buenos Aires (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buenos Aires Province offers a wide range of accommodations to choose from, including luxurious hotels, affordable hostels, and cozy guesthouses that cater to all budgets and preferences. Some recommendations include:

  • For a luxurious stay: Palacio Duhau Park Hyatt Buenos Aires
  • For a budget-friendly stay: Hostel Suites Obelisco
  • For a unique experience: Art Factory Soho

What to Buy in Buenos Aires (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products you can bring back home from Buenos Aires Province:

  • Leather goods: Argentina is known for its high-quality leather products, such as bags, jackets, belts, and shoes.
  • Locally made wine and beer: Malbec wine and artisanal beer are two great beverages to take back home.
  • Mate paraphernalia: If you’ve become a fan of mate while visiting Argentina, you can easily find gourds, metal straws, and tea blends to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Buenos Aires (Province)

Argentina has a rich and vibrant culture, with a strong European influence. Some customs and practices visitors should be aware of include:

  • The importance of family: Family is highly valued in Argentina, so it is not uncommon for people to gather regularly for meals and celebrations.
  • The pace of life: Argentina is known for its relaxed pace of life, so do not be surprised if things do not move as quickly as you’re used to.
  • Respect for personal space: Argentines are generally friendly and welcoming, but they also value their personal space and may not be as tactile as people from other cultures.

Best Time to Visit Buenos Aires (Province)

The best time to visit Buenos Aires Province is during the spring months (September to November) or the fall months (March to May). The weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists, making it a more enjoyable and affordable trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buenos Aires (Province)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Argentina is famous for its cuisine, so make sure to indulge in some of the best foods and drinks while you’re there.
  • Learn the tango: Buenos Aires is the birthplace of tango, so it’s worth taking a lesson or watching a performance while you’re there.
  • Explore beyond Buenos Aires City: While the capital is a great place to start, be sure to venture outside of the city to see more of Buenos Aires Province.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: Stick to bottled water to avoid any stomach issues.
  • Ignore safety precautions: While Argentina is relatively safe, it’s always best to be cautious and aware, particularly at night.
  • Assume everyone speaks English: Although English is spoken in the main tourist areas, it is always best to learn some basic Spanish before visiting.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buenos Aires (Province)

Buenos Aires Province is generally safe, but it’s worth taking some safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, particularly in poorly lit areas, and keep an eye on your belongings in public areas. As in any big city, be conscious of your surroundings, follow tourist advice, and keep important documents and large sums of money in a safe place.

Health in Buenos Aires (Province)

No specific health concerns are associated with travel to Buenos Aires Province. Tap water is not recommended, so stick to bottled water. And it is good to consult with a travel clinic or a medical professional ahead of the trip for any vaccinations and travel health advice.

Getting to Buenos Aires (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buenos Aires (Province)

Buenos Aires Province has two major airports: the Ezeiza International Airport and the Aeroparque Jorge Newbery Airport. Both airports have regular flights to and from major cities in South America, Europe, and North America.

Bus Stations Near Buenos Aires (Province)

Argentine long-distance bus services are fast, efficient, and run by many reputable operators. Buenos Aires City has three major bus terminals that serve all nearby destinations: Retiro, Liniers, and Once.

Other Options

A private transfer or car rental can also be an option to explore Buenos Aires Province independently.

How to Get Around in Buenos Aires (Province)

Buenos Aires Province can be explored by rental car, taxi, or local transportation options, including subways and buses. The fastest and most convenient method is use an app-based ride-hailing service, such as Uber or Cabify, which can be ordered from your phone.

Where to Go Next from Buenos Aires (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montevideo, Uruguay

A short boat ride from Buenos Aires City, Montevideo is the capital of neighboring Uruguay, known for its historic landmarks, beaches, and museums.

Colonia del Sacramento, Uruguay

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this charming, cobblestoned city in southwestern Uruguay features colonial-era architecture, museums, and lovely beaches.

Iguazu Falls

Located in northeastern Argentina, Iguazu Falls is one of the most spectacular natural wonders of the world, with 275 separate waterfalls and an array of wildlife.

Conclusion

Buenos Aires Province is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Argentina. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, delicious food and wine, and welcoming locals, it is sure to captivate and satisfy any traveler.
