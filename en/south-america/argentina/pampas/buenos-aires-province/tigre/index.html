Introduction to Tigre

Tigre, located in the Buenos Aires province of Argentina, is a charming town surrounded by the Parana Delta. Known for its beautiful landscapes, nature reserves, and waterside trails, Tigre is a popular tourist destination, attracting thousands of visitors each year. It’s an ideal place for those looking for a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Tigre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Naval Museum of Tigre: A must-visit museum for history buffs, showcasing the history of the Argentine Navy.
  • Museo de Arte Tigre: A beautiful museum exhibiting Argentine and European art from the late 19th century to the present day.
  • Tigre Art Museum: Another stunning museum featuring works of South American artists from the late 19th to the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Tigre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque de la Costa: A thrilling amusement park featuring roller coasters, water rides, and plenty of entertainment options for the whole family.
  • Delta del Parana: Explore the Parana Delta through boat tours and soak in the beauty of the region’s nature.
  • Puerto de Frutos: A vibrant market that offers everything from souvenirs to local food products.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tigre

  • Trail of the Seven Cascades: A steep hike leading to stunning views of the seven cascades waterfall.
  • Sarmiento Bike Trail: A beautiful bike route that extends over 53 kilometers, providing breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Tigre: Activities and Tours

  • Tango Show: Experience the essence of Argentinean culture with captivating Tango shows.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore Tigre’s waterways by gliding through them on a kayak or canoe.
  • Fishing Tours: Cast your line and catch some of the region’s most popular fish species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tigre

Day 1: Explore the Parana Delta and visit the Puerto de Frutos market. Day 2: Visit the Naval Museum of Tigre and enjoy a tango show in the evening. Day 3: Go kayaking or boating on the delta. Day 4: Visit the Tigre Art Museum and take a biking tour on the Sarmiento Bike Trail. Day 5: Go fishing or enjoy water sports on the delta Day 6: Visit the Museo de Arte Tigre and enjoy the thrilling rides at Parque de la Costa. Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Tigre’s spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Tigre

Must-Try Dishes

  • Choripán: Classic Argentinean street food comprising sausages, bread, and chimichurri sauce.
  • Empanadas: Savory pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables that are perfect for a quick snack.
  • Asado: A traditional Argentinean barbecue with different cuts of meat, sausages, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Malbec: A full-bodied red wine locally produced in the Mendoza region.
  • Fernet con Coca: The classic Argentinean drink made of Fernet and Coca-Cola.

Where to Stay in Tigre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Puerto Delta Apart Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the city centre, offering comfortable suites and excellent amenities.
  • Puerto Tigre Apart Hotel: Another beautiful hotel located by the river that offers stunning views and top-notch facilities.

What to Buy in Tigre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mate Gourds: A popular traditional drink, served in a gourd, which is a perfect souvenir.
  • Leather Goods: Argentina is known for producing exceptional quality leather items, and you can find a wide variety of leather goods here.
  • Handicrafts: Visit the Puerto de Frutos market to buy handmade souvenirs such as pottery and art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Tigre

Tigre has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by European traditions. Tango is a significant cultural element, and you must experience it while visiting Tigre. Leatherwork and handicrafts are also a big part of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Tigre

The best time to visit Tigre is from March to May and September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and you can participate in outdoor activities without any hassle. However, it’s important to note that the rainy season starts in October and lasts until April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tigre

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of yourself while participating in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Dispose of trash in open spaces.
  • Ignore security warnings or advice.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tigre

Tigre is pretty safe, but you should still exercise caution, especially in the evenings. When out exploring, avoid carrying too much cash and leave your valuables in a secured place.

Health in Tigre

While visiting Tigre, you shouldn’t have any significant health concerns. However, it’s always advisable to pack medicines for stomach issues and allergies, as a precautionary measure.

Getting to Tigre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tigre

The closest airport to Tigre is the Ezeiza International Airport in Buenos Aires. From there, you can take a taxi, a bus, or hire a rental car, to reach your destination.

Bus Stations Near Tigre

The Retiro Bus Station in Buenos Aires is the nearest bus station to Tigre. From there, you can take a bus or a taxi to reach Tigre.

Other Options

You can also reach Tigre via train from Buenos Aires, which offers a scenic view during the journey.

How to Get Around in Tigre

The best way to get around in Tigre is to walk or bike. You can rent a bike from Tigre Bike Rental, which is located at the Tigre station.

Where to Go Next from Tigre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Buenos Aires: The bustling Argentinean capital is just a short distance away from Tigre and an excellent place to explore.
  • San Martin de los Andes: Located 1000 km away from Tigre, this mesmerizing town is surrounded by the Andes mountains and is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise.

Conclusion

Tigre is a beautiful destination that offers an abundance of natural beauty, local culture, and thrilling adventures. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just want to get away from it all, Tigre has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and get ready for an unforgettable experience!
