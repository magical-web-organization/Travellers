Corrientes is a province located in the Mesopotamia region of Argentina, in South America. It is a land of contrasts, where the culture of the indigenous Guarani peoples mixes with Spanish heritage, and where the mighty Paraná River meets the lush Iberá Wetlands. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure-seeker, or a foodie, Corrientes has something to offer. This detailed travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this unique part of Argentina.

Introduction to Corrientes

Corrientes is a diverse province with a rich history and culture. The capital city, also called Corrientes, is a charming place where colonial architecture meets modern amenities. The province is known for its incredible nature, highlighted by the Iberá Wetlands, one of the largest freshwater wetlands in the world, as well as the Paraná River, which borders the province and offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming.

List of Places to Visit in Corrientes

When planning your trip to Corrientes, make sure to include the following places on your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Casa de Gobierno: The Casa de Gobierno is the government house and is known for its grand architecture.
  2. San Francisco Convent and Church: The San Francisco Convent and Church is an 18th-century structure with beautiful architecture and religious significance.
  3. Teatro Vera: The Teatro Vera is a magnificent theater that showcases various cultural events.
  4. Museum of Fine Arts: The Museum of Fine Arts is home to a collection of artworks from renowned Argentine artists and European masters.
  5. Antonio “Yapeyú” Felipoff Museum: This museum highlights the history and culture of Guarani people.

Natural Attractions of Corrientes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Iberá Wetlands: The Iberá Wetlands is a vast and biodiverse ecosystem that houses numerous species of animals and plants.
  2. Parque Nacional Mburucuyá: It is a national park with natural pools, trails, and abundant wildlife for birdwatching and animal watching.
  3. Estero del Iberá Natural Reserve: This reserve is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve that attains conservation, research, and ecotourism motive.
  4. Parque Provincial Tres Cerros: Tres Cerros is a place of beautiful hiking trails and scenery along with animal sightings and comfortable camping spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corrientes

Corrientes is an excellent place for biking and hiking lovers, with numerous trails available for both. The following are some of the best routes available in the province:

  1. Parque Nacional Mburucuyá trails: The park has various hiking trails taking from an hour to several hours, the canopy tree path is one to highlight.
  2. San Miguel hiking trail: A two-kilometer trail that enables visitors to explore the woods and encounter diverse wildlife.
  3. Pellegrini village cycling trail: This route passes through Pellegrini’s outstanding natural reserves, wetlands, landscapes, and ranches.

Best Things to Do in Corrientes: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Corrientes:

  1. Iberá Wetlands Safari: A boat trip through the wetlands is a great way to explore the varied landscapes and wildlife of the wetlands.
  2. Paraná Riverboat Tour: The Paraná River is one of the largest rivers in South America. Take a boat tour to see the impressive waterscape.
  3. Birdwatching Experience: The province’s nature reserves offer a chance to enjoy bird watching to observe numerous species of birdlife.
  4. Jangada Rafting: The Jangada is one of the region’s sports activities, enjoying its fast waters and vast scenery while surfing through them.

Week-Long Itinerary for Corrientes

Here’s an example of a week-long itinerary for Corrientes:

  1. Day 1: Arrival in Corrientes City and explore some historical sites such as the Casa de Gobierno, the San Francisco Convent, and Church.
  2. Day 2: Visit the Iberá Wetlands and explore the diverse ecological system and wildlife through various activities such as hiking, birding, or a boat tour.
  3. Day 3: Visit Parque Nacional Mburucuyá and hike the different trails it offers, go birdwatching, and discover the sanctuary’s beautiful waterfalls.
  4. Day 4: Take a break and spend the day relaxing at the Paraná River beaches.
  5. Day 5: Visit the village of Paso de la Patria and take a riverboat tour to see the waterscape.
  6. Day 6: Take a day trip to Santa Ana de los Guácaras and visit the Antonio “Yapeyú” Felipoff Museum, which highlights the history and culture of the Guarani People.
  7. Day 7: Visit the beautiful Pellegrini and its natural amenities and participate in adventures like jangada rafting, horseback riding, among others.

Food and Drink Scene of Corrientes

Corrientes offers unique flavors and dishes unique to the region. Here are some must-try food and drink in the province:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chipá: it is a traditional dry cake made with yucca starch, eggs, milk, fat, and cheese.
  2. Surubí: this is a river fish known for its soft, savory taste and delicious white flesh.
  3. Ñandipá: it is a native andean dish made with cornmeal, spaghetti, cheese, eggs, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Tereré: a cold drink similar to mate, but it is made with cold water and added fresh fruits for a refreshing taste.
  2. Caña: a sugar cane alcoholic drink similar to rum, native to the area and has a subtle sweet aroma.

Where to Stay in Corrientes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Corrientes offers a range of lodging options for travelers with different budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options:

  1. Hotel Juanita: A colonial-style hotel with great comfort and a view of Paraná River.
  2. Hotel Guaraní: A central hotel close to the museum district.
  3. Hostel el Bambi: This hostel offers central services and breakfast with low rates.
  4. Posada del Sauce: A treehouse-like hostel with beautiful landscapes and excursions service.

What to buy in Corrientes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Corrientes include:

  1. Handcrafted art: You can find beautiful and unique native art pieces throughout the province. From cow horn mate cups to woven baskets to beautifully carved wood with Guarani motifs.
  2. Authentic leather goods: Leather jackets, shoes, boots, and belts are widely available throughout the markets and boutique shops.
  3. Musical instruments: Corrientes is famous for its Chamamé music, and instruments such as accordions and guitars are unique to this part of Argentina.

Local culture and customs of Corrientes

The people of Corrientes are friendly and hospitable. The region has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of indigenous Guarani and Spanish influences.

Some customs that are typical of Corrientes include:

  1. Drinking mate: Mate is a hot beverage made from the yerba mate plant that is shared among friends and family. It’s a significant part of the social fabric in Corrientes, and it’s customary to share a cup with visitors.
  2. Dancing Chamamé: Chamamé is a popular dance genre in Corrientes that has its origins in Guarani and Spanish folklore traditions. It’s regularly performed at festivals and cultural shows throughout the province.

Best Time to Visit Corrientes

The best time to visit Corrientes is during the summer months (December to March), when the weather is warm and dry, which makes outdoor activities more comfortable. Visiting in March guarantees visitors with colorful celebrations on the Carnival, with dancing, music, and typical costumes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corrientes

Do:

  1. Try the culinary flavors of the region, and don’t hesitate to ask locals about their favorites dishes.
  2. Learn about the customs and culture of the Guarani People, the indigenous population.
  3. Explore the Iberá Wetlands, it is a unique and biodiverse ecosystem.
  4. Bring insect repellent for excursions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t underestimate the hot weather in summer and carry sun protection gears along with water.
  2. Don’t litter or pollute the environment.
  3. Don’t touch the wildlife when exploring the Iberá Wetlands.
  4. Avoiding swimming in unfamiliar waters, and always take necessary safety measures while partaking in adventure sports activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Corrientes

Corrientes is considered a safe place for tourists. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and stay vigilant, especially when visiting isolated areas or beaches. Don’t leave your belongings unattended to avoid theft.

Health in Corrientes

It is advisable to have travel medical insurance before traveling to Corrientes. Additionally, it is essential to be aware of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and take preventative measures, including the use of mosquito repellent.

Getting to Corrientes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Corrientes

Corrientes has an international airport, Doctor Fernando Piragine Niveyro International Airport, which operates daily flights from Buenos Aires. From there, buses and taxis can be taken to other parts of the province.

Bus Stations Near Corrientes

Corrientes has a bus station that connects to provinces throughout Argentina, including Buenos Aires, Rosario, and Córdoba, among others.

Other Options

There are regular ferries operating along the Paraná River, connecting Corrientes with other neighboring destinations and countries like Brazil and Paraguay.

How to Get Around in Corrientes

Renting a vehicle is the easiest and most convenient way to get around Corrientes, and you can pick up a rental car at the airport. Taxis and buses are also available, with more flexibility and generally affordable prices.

Where to Go Next from Corrientes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Corrientes offers plenty of opportunities for further exploration. Here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  1. Resistencia: The capital city of the neighboring province of Chaco state is a vibrant place with museums, exhibitions, and festivals.
  2. Posadas: Posada is the capital of Misiones province, known as the gateway to the stunning Iguazu falls.
  3. Uruguayana: A southern Brazilian city located on the border with Argentina and Uruguay where it is famous for its various cultural landmarks and festivities.

Conclusion

Corrientes is a beautiful and diverse destination that offers plenty of experiences for all types of travelers. With its unique nature, rich cultural heritage, and culinary delights, it’s sure to captivate visitors. Plan your trip accordingly and immerse yourself in the beauty that is Corrientes.
