The Ultimate Travel Guide to Esquina, Argentina

Esquina is a hidden gem located in the Corrientes province of Argentina, in the region known as Mesopotamia. The town is situated on the banks of Corriente River and is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to do, places to stay, best time to visit, and more in Esquina.

Introduction to Esquina

Esquina’s location on the Corriente River provides a unique charm which attracts not just locals, but visitors from all over Argentina and beyond. The town is located around 340 km from Corrientes and offers a mix of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and warm hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Esquina

Esquina offers a range of attractions for visitors, from natural beauty to historical sites. Below are some of the top places to visit in Esquina:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Esquina is rich in history and culture, and there are many museums and historical sites to explore. Some of these include:

  • Municipal Museum of Esquina
  • San Martin Square
  • Chapel of San Roque

Natural Attractions of Esquina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find solace in Esquina, which is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Corriente River
  • Isla Apipé
  • Iberá Wetlands

Hiking/Biking Routes of Esquina

Esquina offers some fantastic hiking and biking routes with picturesque views. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Calle del Arbolito
  • Estancia La Rosita
  • Ribera de Esquina

Best Things to Do in Esquina: Activities and Tours

Esquina provides numerous experiences for visitors, here are some of the top things to do:

  • Fishing trips along the Corriente River
  • Boat Rides on Isla Apipé
  • Horseback Riding in the Iberá Wetlands

Week-Long Itinerary for Esquina

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Esquina:

Day 1: Arrival, check into accommodation, and a walking tour of the town. Day 2: Visit the Municipal Museum of Esquina and Chapel of San Roque. Day 3: Explore the Iberá Wetlands, go horse riding or hiking. Day 4: Take a boat ride on Isla Apipé, another island in the Corriente River. Day 5: Rent bikes and cycle around the surrounding countryside and villages. Day 6: Book a fishing trip with local guides. Day 7: Day trip to the city of Corrientes, visit the historical sites, museums, and markets.

Food and Drink Scene in Esquina

Esquina has a range of dining experiences, with delectable gastronomy delights and local beverages. Below is a list of must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado (Argentine BBQ)
  • Empanadas (traditional meat pies)
  • Pacú (a type of fish found in the Corriente River)

Local Beverages

  • Mate (traditional Argentine tea)
  • Malbec (Argentine red wine)
  • Cerveza (local beer)

Where to Stay in Esquina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Esquina provides various types of accommodation suitable for all budget ranges. Below are some options for your stay:

  • Hotel Río Grande
  • Hotel Esquina
  • El Remanso del Paraná Lodge

What to Buy in Esquina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy local souvenirs handcrafted by artisans and enjoy authentic Argentine delicacies to bring home. Some of these include:

  • Leather Goods
  • Traditional Argentine Mate Set
  • Dried herbs such as Yerba Mate and Quinoa

Local Culture and Customs of Esquina

Esquina is known for its friendly community and warm hospitality. The culture of the town is deeply rooted in the Argentine way of life, where family and friends are an integral part of daily life, and festivities are celebrated with gusto.

Best Time to Visit Esquina

The best times to visit Esquina are from April to September when there is lesser rainfall and cooler temperatures. Visitors can find fewer crowds and enjoy the natural attractions and cultural experiences in peace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Esquina

Do:

  • Greet people with a handshake and engage in small talk.
  • Try learning a few Spanish phrases for easy communication.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Don’t ignore the traffic rules.
  • Don’t be duped by street vendors selling counterfeit goods

Safety and Health

Safety in Esquina

Esquina is considered a relatively safe town. Visitors should take the necessary precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended, and should always be aware of their belongings while travelling.

Health in Esquina

Travellers are advised to have a travel health insurance and must take all the required vaccinations, such as Tetanus, Diphtheria, Hepatitis A and B, and Rabies, before travelling to Argentina.

Getting to Esquina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Esquina can be accessed through various modes of transportation, to suit your preference and budget.

Airports Near Esquina

  • Resistencia International Airport ( approximately 230 km away)
  • Posadas International Airport (approximately 450 km away)

Bus Stations Near Esquina

  • Retiro Bus Terminal (Buenos Aires)
  • Mariano Moreno Bus Terminal (Corrientes)

Other Options

Travellers can also hire taxis, private cars or motorbikes, as per their requirements, and explore the town at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Esquina

The best way to get around Esquina is by car or taxi. There are also local buses available, which offer a cheaper but slower means of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Esquina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Below are some nearby towns and destinations that travellers can explore:

  • Paso de la Patria
  • Goya
  • Mercedes

Conclusion

Esquina offers a unique blend of natural and cultural experiences that make it an ideal destination to relax, unwind and explore. Visitors can enjoy the warm hospitality, exquisite cuisine, and local culture while basking in the beauty of the surrounding landscapes. Whether you are looking for adventure, solace, or historical exploration, Esquina offers something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an adventure of a lifetime!
