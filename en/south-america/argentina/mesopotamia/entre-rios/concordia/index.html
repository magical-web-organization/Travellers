Concordia is a city located in the province of Entre Rios, in the northeastern region of Argentina, known as Mesopotamia. It’s a city with a rich history, culture, and breathtaking natural beauty that makes it a popular destination among tourists.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will introduce you to the best attractions and things to do in Concordia to help you plan your trip. From museums to scenic views, outdoor adventures to local cuisine, we’ve got you covered.

Introduction to Concordia

Concordia is a charming city with a population of over 160,000 people, and is located in the eastern region of the Entre Rios province. It is situated on the banks of the Uruguay River, which divides Argentina and Uruguay, and is surrounded by beautiful hills and lush forests.

The city is known for its stunning architecture, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming locals. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities in Concordia, from exploring historical landmarks to relaxing in hot springs.

List of Places to Visit in Concordia

When it comes to places to visit in Concordia, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions and destinations to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Colonización: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the European immigrants who settled in the Entre Rios province. It features exhibits on the daily life of the colonizers, their tools and utensils, and how they developed the region.
  • Casa Ronco: This historic building is a national monument that dates back to the 19th century. It was the residence of the Ronco family, a prominent family of Italian immigrants who played a significant role in the development of the city.
  • Salto Grande Dam: This impressive engineering feat is located on the Uruguay River, between Argentina and Uruguay. Visitors can take a guided tour of the dam and learn about the history and significance of this project.

Natural Attractions of Concordia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Termas del Ayuí: These thermal springs are located in a beautiful natural setting and are known for their therapeutic properties. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, from soaking in the warm waters to hiking in the surrounding forests.
  • Parque San Carlos: This park is a popular destination for families and nature lovers. It features several trails for hiking and biking, a zoo, and a beautiful lake for fishing and boating.
  • Reserva Natural Salto Berrondo: This nature reserve boasts an impressive waterfall and several hiking trails. It’s a great spot for birdwatching, as the area is home to a range of bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Concordia

For outdoor enthusiasts, Concordia has several hiking and biking routes to choose from. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Paseo de la Costa: This trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk. It’s located along the Uruguay River and offers stunning views of the water and the surrounding hills.
  • Parque San Carlos: As mentioned earlier, this park has several trails for hiking and biking. The trails range from easy to moderate, and offer beautiful views of the park’s lake and surrounding forests.
  • Reserva Natural Salto Berrondo: This nature reserve features several trails of varying difficulty levels. The trails take visitors through lush forests, over streams, and to the spectacular waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Concordia: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours available in Concordia that cater to a range of interests. Some of the best things to do in Concordia include:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: The Entre Rios province is home to several wineries that offer guided tours and wine tastings. This is a must-do activity for wine lovers.
  • River Rafting: The Uruguay River offers exciting rapids and stunning scenery, making it a popular spot for river rafting.
  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding tours are a great way to explore the countryside around Concordia. Many tours take visitors through the lush forests and hills that surround the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Concordia

To make the most of your trip to Concordia, we recommend spending at least a week exploring the city and its surroundings. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you get started:

Food and Drink Scene of Concordia

No trip to Concordia is complete without indulging in the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Locro: This traditional Argentine dish is a hearty stew made with corn, meat, and vegetables.
  • Asado: This is Argentina’s national dish, which consists of grilled meat and vegetables.
  • Chipás: These are small cheese breads that are popular in the Entre Rios province.

Local Beverages

  • Yerba Mate: This is a popular drink in Argentina and Uruguay, and is made from the dried leaves of the yerba mate plant.
  • Wine: Argentina is known for its excellent wine, and the Entre Rios province has several wineries that offer tastings.
  • Beer: The craft beer scene is booming in Argentina, and Concordia has several breweries that offer a range of beers.

Where to Stay in Concordia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Concordia to suit every budget and preference. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Salto Grande: This hotel is located near the Salto Grande Dam and offers stunning views of the river. It’s a great option for those who want to be close to nature.
  • Hostel del Rio: This hostel is located in the city center and offers affordable dormitory and private rooms. It’s a great option for budget travelers.
  • Casa Ronco Guesthouse: This historic guesthouse is located in the Casa Ronco building and offers comfortable rooms in a charming setting.

What to Buy in Concordia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Concordia is known for its local handicrafts and products. Some of the best souvenirs to bring back home include:

  • Handmade leather goods, such as belts and wallets.
  • Local wine from the Entre Rios wineries.
  • Artisanal cheeses, such as queso de campo, which is made from milk from cows that graze in the countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Concordia

Concordia is a friendly and welcoming city, and its people are known for their hospitality. Some local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting include:

  • Greeting people with a kiss on the cheek is a common custom in Argentina.
  • Tipping is expected in restaurants and for other services, such as taxi rides.
  • Spanish is the official language, but many people also speak English or Portuguese.

Best Time to Visit Concordia

The best time to visit Concordia is between October and March, as this is when the weather is warm and sunny. The peak tourist season is during the summer months (December to February), so expect larger crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Concordia

To make your trip to Concordia as enjoyable as possible, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before you go.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for walking and hiking.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Forget to pack insect repellent.
  • Drink tap water, as it’s not recommended for visitors.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Concordia, it’s important to take certain safety and health precautions to ensure a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Concordia

  • As with any destination, be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Use a reputable taxi service rather than hailing a taxi on the street.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, and stick to well-lit areas.

Health in Concordia

  • It’s important to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Be sure to pack sunscreen and insect repellent, as the sun can be strong and mosquitoes can be prevalent.
  • Check with your doctor before traveling to make sure you are up to date on any necessary vaccinations or medications.

Getting to Concordia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Concordia is easily accessible from several major cities in Argentina. Here are the most common transportation options:

Airports Near Concordia

  • Concordia Airport (COC): This airport is located approximately 20 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Concordia

  • Concordia Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and offers several daily bus routes to and from major cities in Argentina.

Other Options

  • Driving: If you are driving to Concordia, the most common route is via Route 14, which connects the city to the rest of the country.
  • Train: There are no train routes that currently connect Concordia to other cities in Argentina.

How to Get Around in Concordia

The best way to get around Concordia is by bus, taxi, or rental car. Buses are the most affordable option, and the city has several bus routes that connect different areas of the city. Taxis are a convenient option but can be more expensive. Rental cars are a good option for those who want to explore the surrounding countryside at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Concordia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more of the Entre Rios province, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Gualeguaychú: This city is known for its carnival celebrations and thermal springs.
  • Federacion: This town is home to the impressive Federacion Dam and offers several outdoor activities.
  • Colon: This city is known for its historic architecture and hot springs.

Conclusion

Concordia is a city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, you’re sure to find plenty to keep you busy in this charming city.
