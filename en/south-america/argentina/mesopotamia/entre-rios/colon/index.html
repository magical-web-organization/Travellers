Introduction to Colon

Located in the Mesopotamian region of Argentina, Colon is a quaint and charming city in the province of Entre Rios. The city is known for its natural beauty, as well as its rich culture and history. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore local museums, or indulge in traditional Argentine cuisine, Colon has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Colon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Colonia: Learn about the history and culture of Colon at this museum, housed in a beautiful colonial building.
  • Iglesia Nuestra Senora del Carmen: This stunning church is known for its unique architecture and beautiful stained-glass windows.
  • Palacio San Jose: Visit this historic palace, once home to Argentine president Urquiza, and explore its sprawling gardens and impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Colon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Termas de San Jose: Relax at this natural hot springs complex, complete with pools, waterfalls, and stunning views of the Uruguayan countryside.
  • Parque Nacional El Palmar: Explore this national park, home to a unique type of palm tree and a wide variety of flora and fauna.
  • Rio Uruguay: Take a scenic boat ride down the Uruguay River, which borders Colon and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colon

  • Sendero La Calera: This hiking trail offers scenic views of the river and the city, as well as a glimpse into the local flora and fauna.
  • Ciclovia de la Costa: This biking path follows the river and offers stunning views of the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Colon: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Explore the river and its surroundings on a guided kayaking tour.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a leisurely horseback ride through the countryside and enjoy the scenery.
  • Carnival: Join in the fun during Colon’s annual carnival celebration, featuring parades, music, and dancing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colon

Day Activity
1 Visit Museo de la Colonia and Iglesia Nuestra Senora del Carmen.
2 Relax at Termas de San Jose.
3 Explore Parque Nacional El Palmar.
4 Take a scenic boat ride down the Uruguay River.
5 Hike or bike along Sendero La Calera or Ciclovia de la Costa.
6 Participate in Colon’s annual carnival celebration.

Food and Drink Scene of Colon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: This traditional Argentine barbecue features various cuts of meat, grilled to perfection over an open flame.
  • Empanadas: These savory pastries come in a variety of flavors and are a popular snack or meal option in Argentina.
  • Locro: This hearty stew is made with corn, beans, and beef, and is a staple of Argentine cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: This traditional Argentine beverage is made by steeping yerba mate leaves in hot water and drinking from a gourd with a metal straw.
  • Malbec Wine: Argentina is known for its delicious red wines, particularly the rich and bold Malbec variety.

Where to Stay in Colon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Termas de San Jose Hotel & Spa: This luxury hotel is located on the banks of the Uruguay River and offers stunning views, as well as access to the nearby hot springs.
  • Los Naranjos Resort & Spa: This family-friendly resort is located in the heart of Colon and offers a variety of amenities and activities.
  • Hostería Del Puerto: This charming guesthouse is located on the river and offers cozy rooms and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Colon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery: Colon is known for its talented artisans, who create beautiful pottery using traditional techniques.
  • Yerba Mate: Bring home a bag of high-quality yerba mate leaves to enjoy at home.
  • Leather Goods: Argentina is famous for its high-quality leather, and Colon has several shops selling bags, shoes, and other leather products.

Local Culture and Customs of Colon

Argentines are known for their warmth and friendliness, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals! It’s also common to greet someone with a kiss on the cheek (even if you’ve just met), so be prepared for this gesture.

Best Time to Visit Colon

The best time to visit Colon is during the summer months (December to February), when temperatures are warm and the beaches are open. However, keep in mind that this is also peak tourist season, so crowds may be larger and prices higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colon

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Spanish phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Try traditional Argentine dishes like asado and empanadas.
  • Explore the city by bike or on foot.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in other public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colon

Overall, Colon is a safe and peaceful city with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your doors and windows at night and avoiding poorly lit areas after dark.

Health in Colon

To enter Argentina, you’ll need proof of vaccination for yellow fever. Additionally, it’s a good idea to pack any necessary prescription medications and to drink bottled water to avoid gastrointestinal issues.

Getting to Colon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colon

The nearest major airport to Colon is the General Urquiza Airport (PRA), located in Paraná, about 90 miles away. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to Colon.

Bus Stations Near Colon

Colon has a small bus station with regular service to other cities in the region, including Buenos Aires and Rosario.

Other Options

If you’re traveling from Uruguay, you can cross the border on foot or by car. The closest border crossing is located in Paysandú, about 30 miles away.

How to Get Around in Colon

The best way to get around in Colon is on foot or by bike. The city is small and easily navigable, and there are several bike rental shops and guided biking tours available.

Where to Go Next from Colon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Buenos Aires: Argentina’s capital city is located about 200 miles southwest of Colon and is known for its history, culture, and nightlife.
  • Montevideo, Uruguay: This charming coastal city is located across the Uruguay River and offers beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and delicious local cuisine.
  • Iguazu Falls: These stunning waterfalls are located about 300 miles north of Colon and are a must-see natural wonder.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind or explore the rich culture and history of Argentina, Colon is a wonderful destination to visit. With a variety of natural attractions, cultural sites, and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this charming Mesopotamian city.
