Introduction to Entre Rios

Entre Rios is a province in the Mesopotamia region of Argentina, located in South America. It is known for its beautiful natural reserves, hot springs, museums, and historical landmarks. With a warm and humid climate, Entre Rios is perfect for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and history buffs. There are many things to see and do in Entre Rios, so plan on spending at least a week to fully explore this region.

List of Places to Visit in Entre Rios

Here are some of the must-see attractions in Entre Rios:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Entre Rios is home to some incredibly significant historical landmarks, museums, and churches. Visitors can learn about Argentina’s history and culture through these attractions.

  • Parana Cathedral: The Parana Cathedral is a significant part of Entre Rios’s history and heritage. This gothic cathedral has a beautiful architecture that is a must-visit attraction.
  • History Museum: The Entre Rios History Museum takes visitors on a journey through the history of Entre Rios. It features various exhibits that showcase the province’s involvement in the Argentine War of Independence.
  • Urquiza Museum: The Urquiza Museum is a small museum dedicated to the life and accomplishments of the first constitutional governor of Entre Rios, Justo Jose de Urquiza.
  • Palacio San Jose: Palacio San Jose is a historical residence located in the heart of Colonia San Jose. It has been renovated and now serves as a museum that showcases the life and legacy of Justo Jose de Urquiza.

Natural Attractions of Entre Rios: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Entre Rios is blessed with a diverse natural landscape. From hot springs to national parks, there are so many natural landmarks to explore in this region.

  • Parque Nacional El Palmar: This national park is known for its scenic beauty and natural preserves. It offers some amazing hiking trails, camping, and wildlife spotting opportunities.
  • Termas Villa Elisa: The Termas Villa Elisa is a hot springs facility that offers natural mineral waters, saunas, relaxation pools, and therapeutic massages.
  • Laguna de los Pajaros: The Laguna de los Pajaros is a bird-watching paradise. Visitors can take a boat tour or rent kayaks to explore the beautiful lagoon.
  • Parana River delta: The Parana River delta is located in the south of Entre Rios and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Entre Rios

Entre Rios has several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Parque Nacional El Palmar: The park has several hiking trails and is perfect for nature lovers.
  • El Encanto Tourist Circuit: This is a biking trail that takes visitors through several villages and historical landmarks.
  • Camino a los Tres Cerros: A hiking trail that leads to three different peaks with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Entre Rios: Activities and Tours

Entre Rios has a lot of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Kayaking in Delta del Parana: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the beautiful delta region.
  • Horseback riding in Concordia: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Concordia, and visitors can take guided tours through the countryside.
  • Wine tours in Gualeguaychu: Wine enthusiasts can take a tour of the local vineyards and learn about the wine-making process.
  • Tango Shows in Parana: For those wanting to experience the Argentine Tango, Parana has plenty of restaurants that showcase live tango shows.

Week-Long Itinerary for Entre Rios

Here is a week-long itinerary for Entre Rios:

  • Day 1-2: Visit Parana and explore the city’s museums and historical landmarks.
  • Day 3-4: Explore the Parque Nacional El Palmar, camp, hike or simply relax.
  • Day 5: Take a hot spring bath at Termas Villa Elisa.
  • Day 6: Go bird-watching at Laguna de los Pajaros
  • Day 7: Visit Concordia and go horseback riding.

Food and Drink Scene of Entre Rios

Entre Rios has a rich culinary culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: Argentina is known for its grilled meats, and Entre Rios is no exception.
  • Empanadas: Savory pastries filled with meat, vegetables, or cheese.
  • Provoleta: Grilled provolone cheese, often served as an appetizer.
  • Milanesa: Thinly sliced breaded and fried meat, served with french fries.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: A traditional Argentine drink, similar to tea, served in a gourd and sipped through a metal straw.
  • Wine: Argentina is known for its wine, and Entre Rios has a few vineyards that offer wine tastings.
  • Beer: Entre Rios has a growing craft beer culture, and visitors can try some of the local brews.

Where to Stay in Entre Rios: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Entre Rios offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to backpacker hostels. Here are some of the most popular places to stay:

  • Gran Hotel Parana: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Parana. It offers stunning views of the city and the Parana River.
  • Estancia Los Chañares: A beautiful countryside guesthouse that offers horseback riding and outdoor activities.
  • Guayra Hostel: A budget hostel located in Gualeguaychu that offers dorms and private rooms.
  • Hotel Colon: A family-friendly hotel located in the center of Concordia.

What to Buy in Entre Rios: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Entre Rios has several local products and souvenirs that visitors can take home. Here are some ideas:

  • Mate Gourds and Bombillas: Take home a traditional Argentine mate gourd and metal straw.
  • Leather goods: Entre Rios produces some of the best leather products in Argentina.
  • Handmade pottery: The region is known for its beautiful handmade pottery.
  • Craft Beer: If you enjoy beer, take home some of the local craft brews.

Local Culture and Customs of Entre Rios

Entre Rios is proud of its heritage and culture. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Argentina has a strong Catholic influence, and visitors should avoid offensive clothing in religious spaces.
  • People in Argentina are generally friendly and hospitable, so don’t be shy to ask for help or directions.
  • Tipping is customary in Argentina, so be prepared to tip in restaurants, hotels, and for other services.

Best Time to Visit Entre Rios

The best time to visit Entre Rios is during the spring and fall months, from September to November and March to May, respectively. During these times, the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Entre Rios

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish before you visit Entre Rios.
  • Try some of the local cuisine, including meat dishes, empanadas, and mate.
  • Explore the local villages and countryside.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and for other services.
  • Don’t be overly noisy or boisterous in public spaces.
  • Don’t take pictures in religious spaces without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Entre Rios

Entre Rios is a safe region to visit, but visitors should take basic safety precautions:

  • Don’t carry a lot of cash around with you.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended in public spaces.

Health in Entre Rios

Argentina has a good healthcare system, but visitors should take basic health precautions:

  • Make sure you have travel insurance before you visit Argentina.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated, as the sun can be intense.
  • Avoid drinking tap water and only drink bottled water.

Getting to Entre Rios: Transportation Options and Logistics

Entre Rios is well connected to the rest of Argentina, and visitors can get there by air or bus.

Airports Near Entre Rios

The closest airports to Entre Rios are:

  • General Urquiza Airport in Parana
  • Rosario Airport in Rosario

Bus Stations Near Entre Rios

Entre Rios has several bus stations, and visitors can take a bus from Buenos Aires or other major cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Entre Rios. However, driving in Argentina can be challenging due to traffic and road conditions.

How to Get Around in Entre Rios

There are several options for getting around Entre Rios, including buses, taxis, and rented cars. Visitors can also rent bicycles in some towns and explore the countryside on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Entre Rios: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Entre Rios is located near several other popular destinations in Argentina, including:

  • Buenos Aires: The capital of Argentina, known for its vibrant culture and beautiful architecture.
  • Cordoba: A city with a rich colonial history and surrounded by the beautiful Sierras de Cordoba mountain range.
  • Mendoza: Located in the heart of Argentina’s wine country, Mendoza is a popular destination for wine enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Entre Rios is a beautiful and historic region in Argentina that has so much to offer visitors. With natural reserves, hot springs, and museums, it’s the perfect place for outdoor adventures and cultural exploration. Plan your trip to Entre Rios today, and experience everything this incredible region has to offer.
