Introduction to Posadas

Posadas is a city located in the Misiones province of Argentina’s Mesopotamia region. The city is situated on the left bank of the Parana River and is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. With a population of around 320,000 people, Posadas is the largest city in Misiones and a popular tourist destination.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a look at the best things to see, do, and eat in Posadas, including a list of must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, popular activities and tours, and more. We’ll also cover useful travel information such as where to stay, what to buy, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Posadas

If you’re planning a trip to Posadas, here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Posadas is a city with a rich cultural heritage, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • Museo Provincial de Bellas Artes: The Provincial Museum of Fine Arts is a must-visit for art lovers. The museum showcases works by local and national artists and hosts regular exhibitions and events.
  • Casa de Horacio Quiroga: This museum is dedicated to the life and works of one of Argentina’s most famous writers, Horacio Quiroga. The museum is housed in a beautiful colonial-style building and offers guided tours and special events.
  • Ruinas de San Ignacio: The San Ignacio ruins are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular attractions in Misiones. The ruins are the remnants of a Jesuit mission that was established in the 17th century and abandoned in the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Posadas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Posadas is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, and there are plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Parque Nacional Iguazú: The Iguazú National Park, located just a short drive from Posadas, is home to the famous Iguazú Falls. The falls are one of the most impressive natural wonders in the world and are a must-see when visiting the region.
  • Costanera de Posadas: This waterfront promenade offers stunning views of the Parana River and is a popular spot for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.
  • Estancia Santa Inés: This beautiful ranch is located just outside of Posadas and offers visitors the opportunity to experience traditional Argentine rural life. Visitors can go horseback riding, enjoy a traditional asado, and learn about gaucho culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Posadas

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure in Posadas, there are plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the top options:

  • Green Route: This hiking and biking trail is located just outside of town and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is well-marked and relatively easy, making it a great option for beginners.
  • Parque Provincial Salto Encantado: This provincial park is located just a short drive from Posadas and features several hiking trails that lead to stunning waterfalls and scenic viewpoints.
  • Costanera Sur: This waterfront trail stretches for several kilometers and is a popular spot for jogging, walking, and biking. The trail offers great views of the Parana River and the city skyline.

Best Things to Do in Posadas: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun things to do in Posadas, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the top options:

  • Iguazú Falls Tour: Take a day trip to the Iguazú Falls and experience one of the world’s most breathtaking natural wonders.
  • Kayak Expeditions: Explore the Parana River and its tributaries on a guided kayak tour.
  • Bird Watching Tours: Misiones is home to a wide variety of bird species, and there are several guided bird watching tours available in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Posadas

If you’re planning on spending a week in Posadas, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Museo Provincial de Bellas Artes and Casa de Horacio Quiroga
  • Day 2: Parque Nacional Iguazú
  • Day 3: Costanera de Posadas and Estancia Santa Inés
  • Day 4: Green Route
  • Day 5: Kayak Expedition
  • Day 6: Ruinas de San Ignacio
  • Day 7: Bird Watching Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Posadas

No trip to Posadas is complete without trying some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: Argentine barbecue is a must-try when visiting Posadas.
  • Chipa: This traditional snack is made from manioc flour and cheese and is a popular street food in Misiones.
  • Milanesa: A thin-sliced meat fillet that is breaded and fried, Milanesa is a popular dish in Argentina.

Local Beverages

  • Mate: The national drink of Argentina, mate is a bitter tea that is traditionally shared among friends and family.
  • Beer: Misiones is home to several local breweries, including Cervecería Berlina and La Cruz Brewery.
  • Wines: Argentina is known for its wine, and there are several local wineries in the Misiones region.

Where to Stay in Posadas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Posadas has a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the top choices:

  • La Aldea de la Selva Lodge: This eco-friendly lodge is located just outside of Posadas and offers comfortable rooms and bungalows surrounded by lush tropical vegetation.
  • Hotel Urbano Posadas: Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers modern rooms with all the amenities a traveler could need.
  • Posadas Hotel & Casino: This hotel is located on the waterfront and offers stunning views of the Parana River. The hotel also features a casino and several dining options.

What to Buy in Posadas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir from your trip to Posadas? Here are some of the top products to bring home:

  • Yerba Mate: Along with being the national drink of Argentina, yerba mate is a popular product to bring back home. Look for locally produced yerba mate in specialty shops and markets.
  • Guaraní Crafts: The indigenous Guaraní people are known for their beautiful handicrafts, including woven baskets and pottery.
  • Dulce de Leche: This sweet and creamy caramel sauce is a staple in Argentine cuisine and makes for a delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Posadas

When visiting Posadas, it’s important to be aware of local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: In Argentina, it is customary to shake hands when greeting someone.
  • Eating: Asado and other traditional meals are often eaten with the hands, but it’s also acceptable to use utensils.
  • Siesta: Many businesses and shops close during the afternoon for a siesta, or naptime, so plan your sightseeing accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Posadas

The best time to visit Posadas depends on your preferences. If you’re looking for pleasant weather and smaller crowds, consider visiting during the spring (September to November) or fall (March to May). If you’re looking to experience the vibrant culture and festivities of Argentina, consider visiting during the summer months (December to February).

Do’s and Don’ts of Posadas

When visiting Posadas, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash, as many small businesses do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large sums of cash.
  • Smoke in public places, as it is illegal in Argentina.
  • Leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Posadas is a relatively safe city, but travelers should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

Safety in Posadas

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Posadas

  • Drink bottled water, as tap water is not potable.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites and the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever.

Getting to Posadas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Posadas is relatively easy to reach by a variety of transportation methods. Here are some options:

Airports Near Posadas

The nearest airport to Posadas is Libertador General José de San Martín International Airport (PSS), which receives flights from Buenos Aires and other Argentine cities.

Bus Stations Near Posadas

Posadas is well-connected to other parts of Argentina and nearby countries by bus. The city has several bus terminals, including Terminal de Ómnibus de Posadas, which receives buses from Buenos Aires and other major cities.

Other Options

For those traveling from other parts of the region, Posadas can also be reached by car or boat.

How to Get Around in Posadas

The best way to get around in Posadas is by taxi or ride-sharing services. There are also several public transportation options, including buses and trolleys.

Where to Go Next from Posadas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Argentina’s Mesopotamia region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Corrientes: This city, located just a few hours from Posadas, is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture.
  • Misiones Jungle: This vast area of jungle is home to many bird and wildlife species, and there are several guided tours available to explore the area.
  • Jesuit Missions of the Guaranis: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located in nearby Paraguay and features several well-preserved Jesuit missions.

Conclusion

Posadas is a city that truly has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, you’re sure to find something to enjoy in this vibrant and welcoming city. So pack your bags, grab your passport, and get ready to explore all that Posadas has to offer.
