Introduction to Aconcagua

Aconcagua is a mountain peak located in the Mendoza province of Cuyo, Argentina, in South America. It is the highest mountain peak outside of Asia, standing at an elevation of 6,960.8 meters or 22,837 feet above sea level. Aconcagua is a popular destination for mountain climbers and nature enthusiasts from around the world. In addition to the mountain itself, the area around Aconcagua offers many cultural and natural attractions. In this travel guide, we will explore some of the best places to visit, activities to do, hiking routes, cultural attractions, and more in Aconcagua.

List of Places to Visit in Aconcagua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Historia Natural de San Rafael: This museum houses a vast collection of fossils, rocks, minerals, and other natural wonders found in the Mendoza region.
  • Museo del Area Fundacional: The museum showcases the history of Mendoza’s foundation and the city’s evolution during the colonial and post-colonial periods.
  • Bodega La Rural: This winery offers wine tastings, tours, and workshops for those who are passionate about wine production.

Natural Attractions of Aconcagua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Puente del Inca: This natural bridge formed by the mineral-rich hot springs flowing into the Vacas River is a spectacular sight and one of the most popular tourist attractions in Aconcagua.
  • Laguna de Horcones: The lagoon is located at the base of Aconcagua and is a beautiful spot for a picnic or a relaxing walk.
  • Las Cuevas: This charming mountain village is located near the Chilean border and is a great place for hiking, mountain biking, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aconcagua

Hiking Routes

  • Route A: The Normal Route: This is the most popular hiking route to the summit of Aconcagua, and it takes around 15 to 20 days to complete. The route is strenuous and requires a high level of fitness and acclimatization.
  • Route B: The Polish Glacier: This route is less popular than the Normal Route but offers a more challenging and technical climb. The climb takes around 20 to 25 days, and it is not recommended for inexperienced climbers.
  • Route C: The Vacas Valley: This route is perfect for hikers who are looking for a less crowded and more peaceful hiking experience. The trail offers stunning panoramic views of the mountain and the surrounding landscape.

Biking Routes

  • Route 40: Known as Argentina’s Route 66, this scenic road offers breathtaking views of the Andean Mountain Range and is perfect for biking enthusiasts who enjoy a challenging climb.
  • Circuit of Los Penitentes: This 70km route starts at the mountain resort of Los Penitentes and takes you through some of the most stunning and remote landscapes in Aconcagua.

Best Things to Do in Aconcagua: Activities and Tours

  • Mountain Climbing: If you are an experienced climber, Aconcagua offers a challenging and rewarding experience.
  • Wine Tasting: Aconcagua is famous for its Malbec wines, and many vineyards offer wine tastings and tours.
  • Hot Springs: The hot springs of Puente del Inca offers a relaxing and rejuvenating soak after a long hike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aconcagua

Food and Drink Scene of Aconcagua

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: A traditional Argentine style BBQ, includes beef, lamb, and sausages.
  • Empanadas: Deep-dish pastry stuffed with meat or cheese.
  • Locro: Traditional Argentine stew made with meat, corn, and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Malbec Wine: A deep-purple grape variety widely grown in Argentina and produces full-bodied wines with high tannins.
  • Mate: A traditional Argentine drink made from an herb called Yerba mate, served in a gourd with a metal straw and shared among friends.

Where to Stay in Aconcagua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Aconcagua: A luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Mendoza city.
  • Hostel Cruz del Sur: A comfortable hostel located near the bus terminal in Mendoza city.
  • Refugio de montaña Grajales: A mountain refuge located on the Normal Route to Aconcagua Basecamp, provides a basic and affordable accommodation option for hikers.

What to Buy in Aconcagua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Malbec Wine: Buy a bottle or two of your favorite Malbec wine to enjoy back home.
  • Handwoven Ponchos: Traditional Argentine Ponchos made by local artisans that are great for colder weather.
  • Leather Products: Argentine leather products, including shoes, bags, and belts, are very popular and of good quality.

Local Culture and Customs of Aconcagua

  • Spanish is the official language spoken in Aconcagua.
  • Argentineans are known for their hospitality and warmth, and it is customary to greet people with a kiss on the cheek.
  • Tipping is expected in restaurants and bars, typically ten percent of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Aconcagua

The best time to visit Aconcagua is between November and March, during the summer season, when the weather is mild. However, if you are planning to climb the mountain, December to February is the peak season, with the highest number of climbers on the mountain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aconcagua

Do:

  • Hire a professional guide to climb Aconcagua.
  • Stay hydrated at high altitudes to prevent altitude sickness.
  • Respect the local culture, customs, and people.

Don’t:

  • Don’t climb Aconcagua if you are inexperienced or not physically fit.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions in the mountain range.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aconcagua

  • Always hike with a partner or in a group and inform someone about your planned route.
  • Avoid hiking at night or in bad weather conditions.
  • Stay on the marked trails and avoid shortcuts.

Health in Aconcagua

  • Altitude sickness can occur at high elevations. Be aware of the symptoms and communicate them to your guide immediately.
  • Bring a first aid kit with basic medication, including painkillers, antihistamines, and antibacterial ointment.

Getting to Aconcagua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aconcagua

  • Mendoza International Airport (MDZ): The nearest airport to Aconcagua, is located in Mendoza city, 170 km away from the base camp.

Bus Stations Near Aconcagua

  • Terminal del Sol: The bus station located in Mendoza city, offers bus services to Los Penitentes and Puente del Inca.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: Transfer services from Mendoza city to Aconcagua are readily available and can be arranged through your hotel or tour operator.

How to Get Around in Aconcagua

The best way to get around Aconcagua is by car or private transfer. However, if you are on a budget, bus services are available from Mendoza city to other towns in the region.

Where to Go Next from Aconcagua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santiago, Chile: A beautiful and cosmopolitan city with many historical and cultural attractions.
  • Valle de Uco: A scenic wine region located south of Mendoza city.
  • San Juan: A historical colonial town with many museums and landmarks.

Conclusion

Aconcagua is a remarkable destination that offers travelers the perfect balance of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural attractions. From hiking the trails to the summit of Aconcagua to wine tasting at a local vineyard, Aconcagua has something for everyone. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Aconcagua, Argentina. Get ready to be amazed by the breathtaking landscapes, stunning views, and warm hospitality of the Argentinean people.
