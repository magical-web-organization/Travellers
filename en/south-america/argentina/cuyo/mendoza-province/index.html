Introduction to Mendoza (Province)

Mendoza (Province) is a beautiful region located in the Cuyo region of Argentina, in South America. Known for its extensive vineyards, Mendoza is the largest wine producer in Argentina. However, there is much more to this region than just wine. Mendoza (Province) boasts of stunning natural landscapes, ranging from snow-capped mountains to desert terrain and lush green valleys. It is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts offering a variety of adventure sports. One can also learn about the local culture and history of Mendoza by visiting museums and historical sites.

In this comprehensive travel guide for Mendoza (Province), we have compiled all the necessary information that you need to plan your trip to this beautiful region of Argentina.

List of Places to Visit in Mendoza (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Área Fundacional: a museum that provides an insight into the founding of Mendoza.
  • Museo Municipal de Arte Moderno: displays contemporary art pieces by local artists.
  • Basilica de San Francisco: a beautiful church that was built in the 19th century.
  • Casa de Fader: a museum that was once home to Fernando Fader, a famous painter from Argentina.
  • Parque San Martin: a public park that is home to a zoo, a lake, and several historical monuments.

Natural Attractions of Mendoza (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aconcagua Provincial Park: located in the Andes, this park is home to the highest peak in the Americas.
  • Potrerillos Dam: a man-made lake that is a popular spot for water sports such as kayaking and windsurfing.
  • Uspallata: a picturesque town surrounded by the Andes offering opportunities for hiking and horse riding.
  • Puente del Inca: a natural rock formation which forms an arch over a river.
  • Villavicencio Natural Reserve: home to a variety of wildlife and a natural spring called Caracoles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mendoza (Province)

  • Cerro Aconcagua: the highest peak in the Americas is a popular climbing destination, with both beginner and experienced routes.
  • Laguna de los Horcones: this hiking trail takes you through stunning landscapes to a glacial lagoon.
  • Mendoza River Path: an easy bike ride along the Mendoza river that offers scenic views of the surrounding valley.
  • Quebrada del Condor: a challenging hike through the Andes that rewards you with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Mendoza (Province): Activities and Tours

  • Winery Tours: Mendoza is known for its vast vineyards and wineries. A wine tour is an ideal way to explore the region.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: experience a bird’s eye view of the region by taking a hot air balloon ride.
  • Rafting: take a thrilling rafting trip down the Mendoza River.
  • Horse Riding: explore the Andes in a unique way by taking a horseback ride.
  • Skiing: Mendoza is home to several ski resorts, including Las Leñas, which is known for its advanced ski terrain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mendoza (Province)

Day 1: Arrive in Mendoza and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit the wineries and enjoy wine tasting. Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride over Mendoza (Province). Day 4: Hike through the beautiful scenery of Aconcagua Provincial Park. Day 5: Visit the Potrerillos Dam and participate in watersports. Day 6: Explore Uspallata and take a horseback ride. Day 7: Visit the Villavicencio Natural Reserve and the Puente del Inca.

Food and Drink Scene of Mendoza (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado: an Argentinean barbecue, which is considered a national dish.
  • Empanadas: meat-filled pastries that are a staple in Argentinean cuisine.
  • Locro: a stew made with beans, corn, and meat, which is traditionally served during national holidays.

Local Beverages

  • Malbec: Mendoza is famous for its Malbec wine as it is the largest producer of Malbec in the world.
  • Torrontés: a white wine that is grown in the Cafayate region of Salta and is unique to Argentina.

Where to Stay in Mendoza (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cavas Wine Lodge: a luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding vineyards.
  • Park Hyatt Mendoza: a five-star hotel in the heart of Mendoza.
  • Hostel Lao: budget-friendly hostel located in the city center.
  • Casa Pueblo Hostel: a cozy hostel located in the trendy neighborhood of Chacras de Coria.

What to Buy in Mendoza (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine: Mendoza is known for its vast vineyards and high-quality wines, which make an ideal souvenir.
  • Leather Goods: Argentina is famous for its leather products, including bags, shoes, and belts.
  • Ceramic Crafts: pottery and ceramic crafts are popular souvenirs that are found in Mendoza.

Local Culture and Customs of Mendoza (Province)

Mendoza has a vibrant local culture that is heavily influenced by its indigenous past. The people of Mendoza are friendly, hospitable, and warm. Tango and folklore dancing are popular forms of entertainment in Mendoza. The region also has a rich history, which is reflected in its museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Mendoza (Province)

The best time to visit Mendoza (Province) is during the months of March to May or September to November, as the weather is mild and pleasant. However, if you are a skier, then the winter months from June to August are the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mendoza (Province)

Do:

  • Try the local wine and cuisine.
  • Learn about the local culture and history by visiting museums and historical sites.
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in public spaces.
  • Do not disrespect the locals or their traditions.
  • Do not hike without a professional guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mendoza (Province)

Mendoza (Province) is a relatively safe destination, but it is recommended to take general precautions. Always keep your belongings safe and locked, and avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.

Health in Mendoza (Province)

Argentina has a good healthcare system, and hospitals in Mendoza are well-equipped. However, it is recommended to carry medical insurance when traveling to Mendoza (Province).

Getting to Mendoza (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mendoza (Province)

Mendoza International Airport is the main airport near Mendoza (Province).

Bus Stations Near Mendoza (Province)

Mendoza Bus Terminal is the most important long-distance bus terminal, which connects Mendoza with other major cities in Argentina.

Other Options

If you are arriving from Santiago, Chile, the best way to reach Mendoza (Province) is by taking a bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Mendoza (Province)

The best way to get around Mendoza (Province) is by renting a car or taking taxis. Buses and bikes are also good options for exploring the region.

Where to Go Next from Mendoza (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations that you can visit:

  • Santiago, Chile: the capital of Chile is just a few hours’ drive from Mendoza (Province) and is known for its lively culture and history.
  • Valparaiso: located on the Chilean coast, this city is known for its colorful houses and beautiful beaches.
  • Salta: located in the north of Argentina, Salta is known for its stunning natural landscapes and colonial architecture.

Conclusion

Mendoza (Province) is a beautiful and unique destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you enjoy wine, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, Mendoza (Province) has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this vibrant and fascinating destination.
