A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Saint-Georges, French Guiana

Located in the heart of South America is the small town of Saint-Georges in French Guiana. This charming town may be small in size, but it is packed with cultural and natural attractions. With a blend of French and Creole cultures, Saint-Georges is home to numerous museums, historical sites, and fantastic food. Additionally, the town offers various outdoor activities like hiking, biking and boating, and scenic views. This comprehensive guide to Saint-Georgues will help travelers navigate this town and make the most of their time in this off the beaten track destination.

Introduction to Saint-Georges

Founded in the 17th century by the French, Saint-Georges has a rich history and culture that is influenced by a blend of French, Creole, and Amerindian traditions. The town is nestled between Suriname and Brazil, and the majority of its population is of African and Amerindian descent. The residents here are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature. Saint-Georges is a small town that can be covered on foot within a day but has so much to offer that it’s worth spending a few days here.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Georges

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical places

  • Fort Cépérou - A historical fort built by the French in 1693 that is now an art gallery and cultural center.

  • Musée des Cultures Guyanaises - This is a museum dedicated to preserving and promoting the region’s rich cultural heritage. The exhibitions include everything from Amerindian history to contemporary art.

  • La Maison du Patrimoine de Saint-Georges - This museum exhibits a collection of Amerindian pottery and objects representing the town’s colonial era.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Georges: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salonique Beach - This secluded beach is perfect for swimming and sunbathing. It has white sand and is surrounded by lush greenery.

  • La Crique Sark - This is a picturesque river in the middle of the rainforest, where tourists can take a boat ride amidst the dense foliage and observe the flora and fauna.

  • Montagne des Pères - This is a popular hiking spot among locals and tourists for its breathtaking views of the town from its summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Georges

  • Montagne des Pères - This is a popular hiking trail in Saint-Georges town that offers panoramic views of the city. You can also opt for a guided hike here.

  • Biking the Kourou - Saint-Georges trail- This trail takes you between the towns of Kourou and Saint-Georges and offers scenic views, wildlife sightings, and glimpses of French Guiana’s unique culture.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Georges: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Rainforest - Take a boat tour to the Amazonian rainforest from Saint-Georges and experience the beauty of the dense foliage and waterways.

  • Visit the Amerindian Villages - Saint-Georges has two Amerindian villages, ‘Terre Rouge and Crique Balata,’ that tourists can visit to learn about their lifestyle and customs.

  • Birdwatching Tour - Grab your binoculars and take a guided birdwatching tour to observe the rich collection of exotic birds in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Georges

Day 1-2: Visit the historical sites in town. Musée des Cultures Guyanaises, La Maison du Patrimoine de Saint-Georges, and Fort Cépérou.

Day 3-4: Hike to Montagne des Pères, followed by a trip to the Salonique beach.

Day 5-6: Take a boat ride on La Criques Sark to experience the rainforest up-close. Follow this up with a visit to the nearby Amerindian village.

Day 7: Take a birdwatching tour to observe the region’s exotic birdlife

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Georges

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cassava cake - Made from what is known as breadfruit, this cake is a popular snack. When eaten with a cup of coffee, you can truly experience the local flavors.

  • Stew Chicken – This is a dish made using chicken and potatoes. It is seasoned with traditional French Guianese herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Piman Bouk - This highly popular drink is an infusion of rum with spices, passion fruit juice, and hot pepper.

  • Colombo Saba - A beer locally produced in French Guiana, it is brewed using cassava and is distinctively French.

Where to Stay in Saint-Georges: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Opoya - A luxury hotel that offers panoramic views of the town and has a spa and swimming pool.

  • Le Mayouri - A budget hotel option that is located in the heart of the town.

  • La Baboukera - A family-run Guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices and a view of French Guiana’s lush landscape.

What to Buy in Saint-Georges: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hammocks - The region’s traditional hammocks are comfortable, colorful, and make for an excellent souvenir.

  • Tropical Fruits - Mangoes, papayas, avocados, and soursop are abundant in Saint-Georges and make for a great gift to bring back.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Georges

The local people of Saint-Georges have a mix of African, Amerindian, and French culture that influences their lifestyle. They are known for their hospitality, religious beliefs, and hospitality. French is the official language, but Creole is widely spoken.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Georges

The best time to visit Saint-Georges is during the dry season between June and August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Georges

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.

  • Interact with the local people to learn about their traditions and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the surrounding areas around historic sites and parks.

  • Use recreational drugs while visiting.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Georges

Saint-Georges is a safe small town with very low crime rates. However, it is still advisable to take precautions like avoiding dark alleys and keeping important belongings with you at all times.

Health in Saint-Georges

French Guiana is prone to mosquito-borne illnesses, especially between September and February. It is advisable to take malaria prophylactics before entering the region.

Getting to Saint-Georges: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Georges

  • Cayenne - Félix Eboué Airport - This is the closest international airport to Saint-Georges, which is around five hours away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Georges

Saint-Georges has its bus station that connects to other towns of French Guiana, including Cayenne and Kourou.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a boat ride on the Maroni river to reach Saint-Georges from Suriname.

How to Get Around in Saint-Georges

The best way to explore Saint-Georges is on foot. The town is small enough that it can be covered by walking in a day. Taxi services are also available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Georges: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cayenne - This is the capital city of French Guiana, and it’s located approximately four hours away from Saint-Georges.

  • Kourou - This town is known for its Space Center, and it’s located approximately three hours away from Saint-Georges.

Conclusion

Saint-Georges is a small town in French Guiana, but it is packed with cultural and natural attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-track experience. From historical sites to scenic views and outdoor activities, Saint-Georges has something for everyone. Visitors can spend a few days exploring this picturesque town and visit nearby destinations like Cayenne and Kourou.
