Introduction to Newmarket

Located in York Region, in Greater Toronto Area, in Ontario, Canada, Newmarket is a bustling town that attracts both locals and tourists. It has an interesting mix of history, culture, nature, and modern amenities. Whether you are into outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or just good food, Newmarket has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your visit to Newmarket by highlighting the must-visit places, local dishes to try, where to stay, how to get around, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Newmarket

Newmarket has a plethora of attractions that cater to all interests. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Elman W. Campbell Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the history of Newmarket and its people through exhibits, artifacts, and events.

  • Old Town Hall: Built-in 1883, this historic building now serves as a community and cultural center, housing art exhibits, music shows, and theater performances.

  • Newmarket Cemetery: This historic cemetery dates back to the early 1800s and is the final resting place of some of Newmarket’s prominent families and figures.

Natural Attractions of Newmarket: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fairy Lake Park: This picturesque park boasts a beautiful lake, walking trails, and green spaces. It’s perfect for a picnic, a stroll, or a bike ride.

  • York Regional Forest: With over 2,300 hectares of forested land, York Regional Forest offers excellent hiking, cross-country skiing, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

  • Rogers Reservoir Conservation Area: This conservation area features a peaceful reservoir with stunning views, birdwatching, and fishing spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newmarket

Newmarket has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore, each offering unique views and challenges. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Tom Taylor Trail: This paved trail connects Aurora and Newmarket for a total of 6.3 km. It’s perfect for walking, running, or cycling.

  • Nokiidaa Trail: The Nokiidaa Trail follows the East Holland River for 19 km, passing through forests, fields, and wetlands.

  • Murray Trail: This trail is perfect for mountain biking, featuring rugged terrain and stunning views of York Region’s countryside.

Best Things to Do in Newmarket: Activities and Tours

Newmarket’s vibrant community offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Newmarket:

  • Newmarket Farmers’ Market: Held every Saturday from May to October, this market offers a great opportunity to taste fresh produce, handmade crafts, and local delicacies.

  • Theatre Newmarket: This professional theatre company showcases high-quality productions, including musicals, comedies, and dramas year-round.

  • Discovery Walks: Take a self-guided tour of Newmarket’s downtown core and learn about the history and architecture of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Newmarket

Newmarket has a thriving food and drink scene, highlighting local flavors, seasonal ingredients, and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Butter Tart: This quintessential Canadian dessert consists of a pastry shell filled with butter, sugar, syrup, and sometimes raisins or nuts.

  • Poutine: A dish of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy, this comfort food is a cult favorite in Canada.

  • Maple Syrup: Canada is known for its maple syrup, and Newmarket has several farms and stores where you can taste and buy this sweet liquid gold.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Newmarket has several breweries, serving up a variety of handcrafted beers, from pale ales to stouts; highlights include Market Brewing Company and Second Wedge Brewing Company.

  • Wine: Newmarket is located in the heart of Ontario’s wine country. Several wineries offer tastings, tours, and picturesque vineyard views. Rosewood Estates Winery and Magnotta Winery are among the most popular.

What to Buy in Newmarket: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Newmarket, make sure to bring home some souvenirs and local products that represent the town’s culture and heritage. Here are some ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts: Main Street has several shops offering unique, handmade crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

  • Antiques and Collectibles: Newmarket has several antique shops and markets, selling vintage items, furniture, and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Newmarket

Newmarket is a friendly and welcoming town, where residents take pride in their heritage and traditions. Here are some of the customs and practices to keep in mind:

  • Respect private property: It’s essential to respect people’s privacy and not trespass on private property without permission.

  • Queuing: Canadians value order and respect in public places, including lines, where cutting or jumping ahead is frowned upon.

Best Time to Visit Newmarket

Newmarket has four distinct seasons, each offering something different to visitors. Spring and summer are the best times to explore the town’s parks, trails, and outdoor events, while fall and winter are perfect for indoor activities and winter sports. The weather can be unpredictable, so make sure to check the forecast before planning your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newmarket

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable visit to Newmarket, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Speak politely and respectfully to locals. Canadians are known for their politeness and friendliness, and Newmarket is no exception.

  • Tip generously. Tipping is expected in Canada’s service industry, and the average tip is 15-20% of the total bill.

Don’t:

  • Litter or smoke in public places. Newmarket is a clean and green town, and littering or smoking in public areas is not allowed.

  • Not carry or consume alcohol in public areas or parks. Newmarket has strict rules about consuming alcohol in public places, including parks and trails.

Safety and Health

Newmarket is a safe and healthy town, with negligible crime rates and excellent healthcare facilities. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Newmarket

  • Be aware of your surroundings. Although Newmarket is a safe town, it’s always wise to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

  • Lock your car and secure valuables. Car theft is not uncommon in Canada, so make sure to lock your car and not leave valuables visible.

Health in Newmarket

  • Check your medical insurance coverage. If you’re visiting from outside Canada, make sure to check your medical insurance coverage and carry necessary documents and prescriptions.

  • Practice good hygiene. Like most places, Newmarket has standard hygiene protocols to prevent the spread of infections and illnesses.

Getting to Newmarket: Transportation Options and Logistics

Newmarket is well-connected to other parts of the GTA and Ontario, making it easy to get to and from the town. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Newmarket

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport: Located about 45 minutes south of Newmarket, Toronto Pearson International Airport is the nearest international airport.

  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport: Located in downtown Toronto, Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport is a smaller airport ideal for domestic and short-haul flights.

Bus Stations Near Newmarket

  • Newmarket Bus Terminal: This terminal is located in the heart of Newmarket, and serves bus transit systems that connect to other towns in York Region and Toronto.

Other Options

  • Railway Stations: The Aurora GO Station and Newmarket GO Station are the nearest train stations, with direct trains connecting to downtown Toronto.

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Newmarket, making it easy to rent a car and drive around the town and surroundings.

How to Get Around in Newmarket

Newmarket has an excellent public transportation system, but the best way to explore the town is by car or bike. Here are some ways to get around:

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Newmarket, making it easy to rent a car and drive around the town and surroundings.

  • Biking: Newmarket has several bike trails and bike lanes, making it a bike-friendly town.

  • Public Transportation: York Region Transit operates several bus routes in and around Newmarket, making it easy to get to major tourist spots and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Newmarket: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newmarket is located in the heart of Ontario, making it easy to explore other nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some recommendations:

  • Toronto: The vibrant and multicultural city of Toronto is just a short drive or train ride away, offering iconic landmarks, world-class museums, and diverse neighborhoods.

  • Niagara Falls: This natural wonder and popular tourist destination is about a two-hour drive from Newmarket, showcasing majestic waterfalls, thrilling attractions, and scenic views.

  • Lake Simcoe: This lake is about a 30-minute drive from Newmarket, offering fishing, boating, and outdoor activities.
