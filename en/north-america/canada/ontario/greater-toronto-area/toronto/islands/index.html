Islands Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Are you planning a trip to Toronto and looking for an offbeat experience? Then look no further than the Islands, a group of small islands located in Lake Ontario, just a short ferry ride from the city. The islands are a popular summer destination for Torontonians and tourists alike, offering a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Here’s a comprehensive tour guide to help you make the most of your visit to the Islands.

Introduction to Islands

The Islands consist of 15 interconnected islands and islets, all a part of the Toronto Islands Park. The islands have a rich history, dating back to the indigenous people of the area, and have been used for various purposes over the years. Today, the islands are home to a vibrant community of residents and visitors who come to enjoy the natural beauty and recreational activities of the area.

List of Places to Visit in Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Centreville Amusement Park: A family-friendly amusement park with rides, games, and attractions.
  • Gibraltar Point Lighthouse: The oldest existing lighthouse on the Great Lakes, built-in 1808.
  • Rectory Café: A historic café located in a 19th-century church building.
  • Hockey Hall of Fame: A museum dedicated to the history of ice hockey and its players.

Natural Attractions of Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Toronto Island Park: A scenic park with beaches, gardens, and recreational facilities.
  • Ward’s Island Beach: A popular beach known for its shallow waters and sandy shores.
  • Hanlan’s Point Beach: A clothing-optional beach with a great view of the Toronto skyline.
  • Franklin Children’s Garden: A garden designed for children that encourages exploration and discovery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Islands

The Islands offer numerous hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the various parts of the islands. Some of the popular trails are:

  • The Martin Goodman Trail: A scenic trail that runs along the waterfront of the islands and connects to the mainland.
  • The Loop Trail: A 5 km trail that goes around Ward’s Island, offering stunning views of the city skyline.
  • The Far Enough Farm Trail: A short trail that takes visitors past the Far Enough Farm, a petting zoo that is a popular attraction on the islands.

Best Things to Do in Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Paddle Boating: Rent a paddleboat and explore the waters around the islands.
  • Kayaking: Take a guided kayaking tour of the islands to explore the hidden coves and bays.
  • Bicycle Rentals: Rent a bike and explore the islands at your own pace.
  • Fishing: Try your hand at fishing in the waters surrounding the islands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Islands

If you’re planning to spend a week on the Islands, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip.

Day Activity
1 Explore Centreville Amusement Park
2 Visit Gibraltar Point Lighthouse and take a guided tour.
3 Relax on Ward’s Island Beach and enjoy the scenic views.
4 Take a guided kayaking tour of the islands.
5 Spend the day at Hanlan’s Point Beach and enjoy the clothing-optional experience.
6 Rent a bike and explore the islands at your own pace.
7 Visit the Hockey Hall of Fame and learn about the history of ice hockey.

Food and Drink Scene of Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Island Fish & Chips: A local favorite, made with fresh fish and fries.
  • BeaverTails: A Canadian treat, a fried dough pastry with various toppings.
  • Toronto Island BBQ & Beer Co.: Delicious BBQ and craft beer while enjoying a beautiful lake view.

Local Beverages

  • Steam Whistle Brewery: A local brewery that offers tours and tastings of their popular pilsner.
  • Toronto Island Winery: A winery that produces unique wines made from island-grown fruit.

Where to Stay in Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are no hotels on the islands, but there are several options on the mainland, including:

  • Westin Harbour Castle: A luxury hotel with beautiful lake views.
  • HI Toronto Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in downtown Toronto.
  • Island Cottage: A private guesthouse rental on the islands.

What to Buy in Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Soaps: Made from local ingredients, such as lavender and honey.
  • Toronto Island T-Shirts and Hats: A souvenir to remember your visit to the islands.
  • Local Art and Crafts: Unique and handmade items from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Islands

The Islands have a unique culture that is influenced by their history and geography. The community on the islands is tight-knit and friendly, and visitors are welcomed with open arms. The islands are also known for their commitment to sustainability and preserving the natural beauty of the area.

Best Time to Visit Islands

The best time to visit the Islands is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and all the attractions and recreational facilities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Islands

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife of the islands.
  • Follow the guidelines for trash disposal and recycling.
  • Take advantage of the numerous recreational activities available on the islands.

Don’t:

  • Bring pets to the islands.
  • Damage or remove any plants or wildlife.
  • Litter on the islands.

Safety and Health

Safety in Islands

The islands are generally safe for visitors, but it’s always good to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your bike and keeping an eye on your belongings. Enjoy the beautiful scenery of the islands, but be cautious when swimming in the lake, as there are no lifeguards on duty.

Health in Islands

There are no pharmacies on the islands, so be sure to bring any necessary medications with you. And while the islands are generally safe, be sure to take precautions against insect bites and sunburn.

Getting to Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access the islands via a short ferry ride from the city. Ferries leave from Jack Layton Ferry Terminal, located at the foot of Bay Street on Queens Quay, and run regularly throughout the day. There is also a water taxi service available for private hire.

Airports Near Islands

  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport
  • Toronto Pearson International Airport

Bus Stations Near Islands

  • Toronto Coach Terminal
  • Union Station Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Bike: Visitors can bring their bikes on the ferry and explore the islands on two wheels.
  • Water Taxi: A private water taxi service is available for hire.

How to Get Around in Islands

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around the islands, but visitors can also rent a paddleboat or take a kayak tour to explore the islands from the water.

Where to Go Next from Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While the Islands have plenty to see and do, visitors can also explore other nearby attractions, such as:

  • CN Tower: A 553-meter-high tower and iconic landmark of Toronto.
  • Ripley’s Aquarium: A state-of-the-art aquarium with over 20,000 marine animals.
  • Art Gallery of Ontario: A museum with an extensive collection of Canadian and international art.

Conclusion

The Islands offer a unique and offbeat experience for visitors to Toronto. Whether you’re looking for natural beauty, cultural attractions, or outdoor adventures, the Islands have something for everyone. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to the Toronto Islands.
