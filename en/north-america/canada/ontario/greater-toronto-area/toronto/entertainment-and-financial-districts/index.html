Introduction to Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto’s Entertainment and Financial Districts are located in the heart of the city, with towering skyscrapers, historic landmarks, and a vibrant cultural scene. The Entertainment District is known for its theaters, music venues, and sporting arenas, while the Financial District is home to major financial institutions, corporate headquarters, and luxury shopping. Together, these districts offer visitors an exciting mix of entertainment, culture, and business.

List of Places to Visit in Entertainment and Financial Districts

There are several must-visit attractions in the Entertainment and Financial Districts, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Ontario Museum: One of Canada’s largest museums, with over six million objects and artifacts spanning art, culture, and natural history.
  • CN Tower: A must-visit Toronto landmark offering panoramic views of the city from its observation deck or EdgeWalk at 1168 feet above the ground.
  • Hockey Hall of Fame: A shrine to Canada’s national sport and the world’s greatest players, featuring interactive exhibits and memorabilia.
  • Fort York National Historic Site: An important military fortification from the War of 1812, with restored barracks, cannons, and exhibits on early Canadian history.
  • St. Lawrence Market: A bustling public market with over 120 vendors offering fresh produce, meats, cheeses, and handcrafted goods.

Natural Attractions of Entertainment and Financial Districts: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Toronto Islands: Take a ferry to the Island and enjoy a scenic bike ride, swim at the beach, or visit the amusement park.
  • Harbourfront Centre: A bustling cultural hub with festivals, live music, and outdoor concerts on the waterfront.
  • High Park: A large urban park with hiking trails, gardens, a zoo, and a splash pad perfect for families.
  • Nathan Phillips Square: A public square known for its iconic Toronto sign, outdoor concerts, winter ice skating, and food trucks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto is a perfect destination for biking and hiking enthusiasts. The city offers several scenic routes to explore the Entertainment and Financial Districts:

  • Waterfront Trail: Follow the scenic waterfront from Marie Curtis Park in the west-end to Rouge Park in the east-end of Toronto.
  • Martin Goodman Trail: Popular for biking and rollerblading, this trail runs along the waterfront from east to west Toronto.
  • Don River Trail: A scenic trail that runs through the Don Valley, with access from downtown and midtown Toronto.
  • Queen’s Park Circle: A popular biking route that loops around the University of Toronto and Queen’s Park.

Best Things to Do in Entertainment and Financial Districts: Activities and Tours

There are several fun activities and tours to explore the Entertainment and Financial Districts:

  • Toronto International Film Festival: A must-visit for film buffs, with over 300 films from more than 50 countries screened at various venues in the Entertainment District.
  • EdgeWalk at the CN Tower: For adrenaline junkies, this thrilling experience involves walking around the CN Tower’s EdgeWalk platform, 1168 feet above ground.
  • Toronto Raptors Game: Cheer on Toronto’s professional basketball team at the Scotiabank Arena in the Entertainment District.
  • Hop-On Hop-Off Bus Tour: The perfect way to explore the city’s top attractions, with 20 stops throughout Toronto.

Week-Long Itinerary for Entertainment and Financial Districts

If you’re planning to spend a week in Toronto’s Entertainment and Financial Districts, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Royal Ontario Museum, Casa Loma, and eat at St. Lawrence Market.
  • Day 2: Take a ferry to the Toronto Islands, explore Centreville Amusement Park, and eat at Amsterdam BrewHouse.
  • Day 3: Visit the Hockey Hall of Fame, walk around the Financial District, and eat at Canoe Restaurant & Bar.
  • Day 4: Take a guided walking tour of Graffiti Alley, visit the Art Gallery of Ontario, and eat at The One Eighty.
  • Day 5: Visit Casa Loma, go hiking at High Park, and eat at Saffron Spice Kitchen.
  • Day 6: Take a Hop-On Hop-Off Bus Tour of the city, explore the Distillery District, and eat at El Catrin Destilleria.
  • Day 7: Visit the CN Tower, go to a Toronto Raptors game, and eat at Biff’s Bistro.

Food and Drink Scene of Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto is known for its diverse and vibrant food scene, with options ranging from street food to fine dining. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A quintessential Canadian dish made with fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Peameal Bacon Sandwich: A Toronto classic, featuring Canadian bacon on a Kaiser roll.
  • Butter Chicken: A popular Indian dish made with chicken and a creamy tomato-based curry.
  • Smoked Meat Sandwich: A deli-style sandwich with smoked beef, mustard, and rye bread.
  • Shawarma: A Middle Eastern staple, made with spiced meat, vegetables, and hummus.

Local Beverages

  • Canadian Beer: Try local breweries like Steam Whistle, Mill St. Brewery, and Amsterdam Brewery.
  • Ice-Wine: A sweet dessert wine made from grapes harvested in the winter.
  • Caesar: A Canadian twist on the Bloody Mary, made with clam juice and garnished with celery, bacon, or a pickled bean.
  • Craft Cocktails: Visit The Drake Hotel, BarChef, or BYOB Cocktail Emporium for creative and unique cocktails.

Where to Stay in Entertainment and Financial Districts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in the Entertainment and Financial Districts, including:

  • The Fairmont Royal York: A luxury hotel in the heart of the Financial District.
  • The Drake Hotel: A boutique hotel with live music and a popular rooftop bar.
  • HI Toronto: A budget-friendly hostel with a central location.
  • Airbnb: Explore local neighborhoods and rent a private apartment or house.

What to Buy in Entertainment and Financial Districts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Toronto, be sure to pick up some of these local products:

  • Maple Syrup: The quintessential Canadian souvenir, available at souvenir shops and markets throughout the city.
  • Peameal Bacon: Bring home some of Toronto’s famous bacon from St. Lawrence Market or local butcher shops.
  • Hockey Jerseys: Pick up a jersey or other souvenirs at the Hockey Hall of Fame gift shop.
  • Local Art: Visit galleries and boutiques in the Entertainment District to find unique and handmade art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto is a multicultural and accepting city, known for its diversity and friendliness. It’s important to be respectful of other cultures and customs while visiting, and to try to learn and appreciate different ways of life. Tipping in restaurants and bars is customary, with 15-20% being the standard rate.

Best Time to Visit Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto is a year-round destination, with each season offering unique experiences. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with festivals, outdoor concerts, and warm weather. Fall is known for its beautiful foliage, while winter offers ice skating and holiday festivities. Spring is a quieter time to visit, with blooming cherry blossoms and mild weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Entertainment and Financial Districts

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Explore the city by bike or foot.
  • Be respectful of other cultures and customs.
  • Visit local markets and boutiques.
  • Attend festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your car or hotel room.
  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Smoke in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto is a relatively safe city, but like any urban area, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in less populated areas, and keep valuables hidden.

Health in Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto has a high standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and clinics throughout the city. It’s important to have travel insurance in case of emergencies or unexpected health issues.

Getting to Entertainment and Financial Districts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Toronto is easily accessible by car, bus, train, or plane. Once in the city, there are several transportation options to explore the Entertainment and Financial Districts:

Airports Near Entertainment and Financial Districts

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport: Located 30 minutes west of downtown Toronto.
  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport: Located on Toronto Island, with a ferry connecting to the mainland.

Bus Stations Near Entertainment and Financial Districts

  • Toronto Coach Terminal: Located in the heart of downtown Toronto, with connections throughout Canada and the United States.
  • Union Station: A major transportation hub for trains and buses, located in the Financial District.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are several rental companies located throughout the city, with major car rental locations at Toronto Pearson International Airport.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber are readily available throughout the city.
  • Bike Rental: Rent a bike from local companies like Bike Share Toronto or Rent-a-Bike and explore the city on two wheels.

How to Get Around in Entertainment and Financial Districts

Toronto is a walkable city, with several transportation options to get around the Entertainment and Financial Districts:

  • Toronto Transit Commission (TTC): The city’s reliable and accessible public transit system, with subways, streetcars, and buses.
  • Bike: Rent a bike and explore the city on two wheels.
  • Taxi/Uber: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available throughout the city.
  • Walking: The best way to explore the city and take in the sights and sounds of Toronto.

Where to Go Next from Entertainment and Financial Districts: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Toronto and the surrounding area, consider these nearby destinations:

  • Niagara Falls: A popular day trip from Toronto, with stunning views of the falls and nearby attractions like wine tasting and Niagara-on-the-Lake.
  • Algonquin Provincial Park: A nature lover’s paradise, with hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and camping.
  • Kingston: A charming waterfront town with historic sites, museums, and scenic views.
  • Prince Edward County: A popular wine region located two hours east of Toronto, with picturesque vineyards and countryside.

Conclusion

Toronto’s Entertainment and Financial Districts offer visitors a vibrant and dynamic mix of culture, entertainment, and business. With plenty of attractions, food and drink options, and transportation choices, it’s easy to explore the city and discover Toronto’s unique charm and character.
