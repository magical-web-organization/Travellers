Introduction to Downtown East

Downtown East is one of the most culturally rich, historic, and vibrant neighbourhoods in Toronto, Canada. It is located in the eastern end of the city, with Queen Street East on one side and Gerrard Street East on the other.

With its diverse population, art galleries, museums, outdoor adventures, and exotic cuisine, Downtown East offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, this neighbourhood has something for everyone.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through the best places to visit, stay, eat, and more, in Downtown East, so let’s dive right in!

List of Places to Visit in Downtown East

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Toronto’s Downtown East is a treasure trove of culturally significant attractions which offer a glimpse into the rich history and heritage of the city. Here are a few must-visit places:

  • The Distillery District is a pedestrian-only area that is home to over 40 heritage buildings which date back to the 19th century. You can take a self-guided walking tour or book a guided tour to learn about the history of the site.

  • St. Lawrence Market is one of the oldest markets in Toronto. It offers a fantastic culinary experience with a wide variety of eateries and shops.

  • The Royal Ontario Museum is the largest museum in Canada and houses artefacts from all over the world, including dinosaur bones and other fossils.

  • The Art Gallery of Ontario is home to over 90,000 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs, and offers a strong focus on Canadian art.

Natural Attractions of Downtown East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast or looking for some breathtaking views, Downtown East has you covered:

  • The Beaches is a lovely scenic area that runs from Ashbridges Bay Park in the west to Balmy Beach in the east. It has a boardwalk, beach volleyball courts, and stunning views of Lake Ontario.

  • Toronto Islands are a group of 15 small islands located in Lake Ontario, just a short ferry ride away from Downtown East. Visitors can enjoy activities such as biking, kayaking, swimming, and kiteboarding.

  • Riverdale Park is one of Toronto’s largest parks, and offers amazing views of the city skyline. It’s also a popular spot for picnics, sports, and outdoor events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown East

Downtown East has plenty of hiking and biking trails, some of which are perfect for nature lovers and others for those seeking adventure. Here are some routes to explore:

  • The Lower Don Trail is a 6.7km trail that runs along the Don River. It’s great for cycling, jogging, or even dog-walking.

  • The Tommy Thompson Park Trail is a 5km trail that runs along the Leslie Street Spit. It has stunning views of Lake Ontario and is a great spot for birdwatching.

  • The Martin Goodman Trail is a 56km trail that runs along the city’s waterfront, including Downtown East. It’s the perfect option for cycling enthusiasts wanting to explore Toronto city.

Best Things to Do in Downtown East: Activities and Tours

Downtown East is not only steeped in history and home to numerous natural attractions but also offers plenty of fun and exciting activities for visitors. Check out our recommended activities and tours below:

  • Underground Cinema is a unique experience located in The Village theatre, where they screen cult classic movies in a secret underground location with themed activities before the films start.

  • Casa Loma is a stunning castle in the heart of the city, offering guided tours, exhibits, and activities. It’s a must-visit for history buffs, or anyone with an interest in the fantastic architecture of the site.

  • The Second City is a comedy club that’s been running for over 50 years. Catch a show here and experience the best comedic talents in Toronto.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown East

Toronto is known for its vibrant food and drink scene, and Downtown East is no exception. From classic Canadian poutine to exotic cuisines from around the world, this neighbourhood offers plenty of options to satisfy your tastebuds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine is a Canadian classic consisting of fries, gravy, and cheese curds. Patrons of Toronto in search for the perfect poutine must try those at the Poutinerie in St. Lawrence Market or Lab Cab Sandwich Shop.

  • Hakka Cuisine is a fusion of Indian and Chinese cuisine. Try the delicious Hakka Noodles and Chili Chicken from Lahore Tikka House.

  • Banh Mi is a Vietnamese baguette sandwich filled with meat and vegetables. Try the Banh Mi from Bánh Mì Ba Le or Saigon Lotus at The Beaches.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer is a must-try in Downtown East. Check out Left Field Brewery or Indianapolis Ale House for unique local beer options.

  • Coffee is ubiquitous in Toronto and easily available at almost every corner. For something unique try out Fahrenheit Coffee at the Distillery District or Rooster Coffee House at Riverdale Park.

Where to Stay in Downtown East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown East has plenty of accommodation options ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The Broadview Hotel is a chic and quirky hotel located on Queen Street. It has a rooftop bar, restaurant, and offers stunning skyline views since it is one of the oldest landmarks existing from 1891.

  • The Planet Traveler Hostel is an eco-friendly hostel that offers affordable options for solo-travelers, groups, and even families. It’s located in one of the city’s most vibrant neighbourhoods – Kensington Market – and offers easy access to the rest of Toronto.

  • The Ivy at Verity is a cozy boutique hotel that offers pampering spa treatments, intimate accommodation options and can be considered for couples or solo travelers looking for a unique and memorable stay in Toronto.

What to Buy in Downtown East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The shopping scene in Downtown East is as rich and diverse as the neighbourhood itself. Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to help you remember this fantastic travel experience:

  • The Spice Trader at St. Lawrence Market offers a variety of fragrant loose-leaf teas, spices, and exotic seasonings that are sure to add a unique flavour to your recipes when you take them back home.

  • Blue Banana Market at Kensington Market is a fantastic place to buy vintage and unique clothing, accessories and jewellery.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown East

Toronto is known for its multiculturalism, and Downtown East portrays it the best. Canadians are very friendly and welcoming to tourists and visitors. There are no particular cultural customs or traditions specific to Downtown East. Still, it’s a good idea to familiarize yourself with the greetings and basic manners so you can communicate with locals effectively.

Best Time to Visit Downtown East

Toronto’s Downtown East has something to offer all year round, and the best time to visit will depend on the type of experience you’re looking to have:

  • Summer (June to August) is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and exploring the beaches and islands.

  • Fall (September to November) offers a beautiful autumn foliage and cooler weather, which is perfect for city walks and exploring the historic sites.

  • Winter (December to February) has colder temperatures, with occasional snowfall. However, it offers unique experiences such as skating at Nathan Philips Square or Toronto’s Island Winter Light Exhibition.

  • Spring (March to May) offers pleasant temperatures and blooming gardens.

The best time to visit the city overall is usually between April and May or September and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown East

Knowing a few tips and tricks can help visitors to stay safe and make the most of their trip. Here are a few do’s and don’ts to help you do just that:

Do:

  • Plan ahead and book tickets early to avoid crowds and save money.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the history and culture of the neighbourhood.
  • Explore the city by foot, especially if staying in close proximity to notable attractions.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring warm clothing if travelling during Fall and Winter.
  • Carry substantial amounts of cash, instead use debit or credit cards for transactions.
  • Leave bags or valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety is not a significant concern when visiting Downtown East, as Toronto is generally a very safe place. However, it is still advisable to be cautious and aware of your surroundings when walking around at night.

Safety in Downtown East

  • Stay aware of pickpockets in crowded areas and keep your valuables out of sight.
  • Avoid walking alone in alleys or vacant side streets.
  • Be cautious when taking cabs at night and make sure they are legitimate.

Health in Downtown East

  • For medical emergencies, dial 911 or visit the nearest hospital. Toronto General Hospital, Michael Garron Hospital, and Women’s College Hospital are the three most prominent hospitals closest to Downtown East.
  • For non-urgent health issues or concerns, visit walk-in clinics or pharmacy nearby.

Getting to Downtown East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Downtown East is easily accessible via public transportations and other options.

Airports Near Downtown East

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport is approximately 30 minutes’ drive away from Downtown East.
  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport is located near the district’s western end, and visitors can use the ferry service to reach Downtown East.

Bus Stations Near Downtown East

  • Toronto Coach Terminal is the city’s main bus terminal located at Bay and Dundas, and Greyhound buses offer intercity transportation.

Other Options

  • Taxi and ridesharing apps are widely available and convenient for getting around in Downtown East.

How to Get Around in Downtown East

The best way to explore Downtown East is on foot or by bike. Pedestrian-friendly streets, cycling lanes, and bike rental services are plenty and can be very affordable options.

TTC (Toronto Transit Commission) offers an excellent public transportation system with subway lines, streetcars, and buses connecting every quarter of the city. It’s an affordable, comfortable, and eco-friendly option for exploring Toronto.

Where to Go Next from Downtown East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Toronto has plenty of destinations around Downtown East that are worth exploring. Here are a few ideas:

  • Canada’s Wonderland: A theme park located in the city of Vaughan featuring some of the world’s tallest and fastest rides.

  • Niagara Falls: Iconic waterfalls located 130km away from Toronto that offers unforgettable natural experience

  • Thousand Islands: Located between Kingston and Brockville, and a part of the Canadian conservation area. Visitors can go on boat tours, fish, hike, and much more.

Conclusion

We hope this guide gives you a good sense of what Downtown East has to offer. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or fantastic food, Downtown East will not disappoint. So, start planning now and get ready to fall in love with this vibrant city neighbourhood!
