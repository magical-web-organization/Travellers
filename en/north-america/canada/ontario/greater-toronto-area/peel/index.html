Introduction to Peel

Peel, located in the Greater Toronto Area in Ontario, Canada has something to offer everyone. From culturally significant attractions to natural scenic views and heart-pumping outdoor adventures, Peel is the perfect destination. With an abundance of hiking trails and biking routes, Peel is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Additionally, its diverse culinary scene and exceptional shopping options make it a great destination for foodies and shopaholics alike.

List of Places to Visit in Peel

Peel has several culturally significant attractions and natural wonders that attract tourists from around the world. Here is a list of places to visit in Peel:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Peel houses several museums and historical sites that offer visitors an insight into the region’s rich history and culture. The following are some of the most notable cultural attractions in Peel.

  • Bradley Museum: Bradley Museum is located in the heart of Mississauga and features three historic buildings that depict life in Ontario during the early 19th century.
  • Black Creek Pioneer Village: Black Creek Pioneer Village, located in northwest Toronto, is a living history museum that offers visitors an insight into the life of early settlers in Canada.
  • Benares Historic House: Benares Historic House, located in Mississauga, is a historic home built in 1837. Visitors can explore the house and gardens while learning about the history of the Peel region.

Natural Attractions of Peel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Peel is home to several natural wonders that offer visitors an opportunity to connect with nature and explore the great outdoors. The following are some of the most notable natural attractions in Peel.

  • Heart Lake Conservation Area: Heart Lake Conservation Area is a 169-hectare park located in Brampton. The park offers several hiking trails, picnic areas, and a man-made lake.
  • Credit River: Credit River is a 90-kilometer river located in southern Ontario. The river offers excellent fishing opportunities and is an excellent destination for canoeing and kayaking.
  • Rattray Marsh Conservation Area: Rattray Marsh Conservation Area is a 90-hectare park located in Mississauga. The park offers several hiking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and windsurfing in Lake Ontario.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peel

Peel is renowned for its hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the region’s natural beauty. The following are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Peel.

  • Great Lakes Waterfront Trail: The Great Lakes Waterfront Trail is a multi-use path that runs through Peel and offers spectacular views of Lake Ontario.
  • Chinguacousy Trail: The Chinguacousy Trail is a 9-kilometer trail in Brampton that passes through several parks and natural areas.
  • Etobicoke Creek Trail: The Etobicoke Creek Trail is a 40-kilometer trail that runs through Brampton and Mississauga and offers several hiking and biking opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Peel: Activities and Tours

Peel offers several activities and tours that cater to various interests and age groups. The following are some of the best things to do in Peel.

  • Sheridan Nurseries: Sheridan Nurseries, located in Brampton, offers several gardening and landscaping workshops throughout the year.
  • Canadian Golf Hall of Fame and Museum: Canadian Golf Hall of Fame and Museum, located in Oakville, is a museum that pays tribute to the game of golf and its history.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visitors can take a wine tasting tour of several wineries located in the Niagara region, which is only a short drive from Peel.

Peel has several attractions and natural wonders that require more than a day to explore fully. The following is a week-long itinerary for Peel.

Food and Drink Scene of Peel

Peel is home to a diverse culinary scene that offers visitors a taste of the local cuisine. The following are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Peel.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Butter Chicken: Butter chicken is one of the most popular dishes in Peel. It is a creamy chicken dish that is served with rice or naan bread.
  • Poutine: Poutine is a Canadian dish that consists of fries, cheese curds, and gravy. It is a popular fast food option in Peel.
  • Peameal Bacon: Peameal bacon is a type of back bacon that is brined and rolled in cornmeal. It is a popular breakfast item in Peel.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Peel is home to several craft breweries that offer visitors an opportunity to taste the local beer.
  • Ice Wine: Ice wine is a dessert wine that is made by freezing grapes before pressing them. Niagara region, located near Peel, is one of the most popular regions for ice wine in Canada.

What to Buy in Peel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peel offers several local products and souvenirs that make for great gifts or keepsakes. The following are some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Peel.
