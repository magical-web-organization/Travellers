Whitby, a charming town in the Durham region of the Greater Toronto Area, holds a rich history and natural beauty that attracts visitors from all over the world. This comprehensive tour guide covers everything needed to plan the perfect trip to Whitby, including the must-see attractions, activities, hiking/biking routes, the best time to visit, cultural customs, and transportation options.

Introduction to Whitby

Whitby is a town located in southern Ontario, Canada, situated on the north shore of Lake Ontario. The town bears a rich history, dating back over 2,000 years to the Indigenous peoples. The region has passed through French, English, and American hands, leading to an incredibly diverse and unique cultural background that still exists today. Whitby’s beauty is characterized by a rare combination of natural wonder and striking architecture, making it an ideal destination for anyone in search of a peaceful and remarkable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Whitby

Whether you are drawn to learning about history, exploring natural beauty, or enjoying outdoor activities, Whitby has something to offer everyone. Here is a list of the top must-visit places in Whitby:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Whitby Centennial Building – Considered the cultural hub for the town, this site hosts many events, including art exhibits, concerts, and theatrical performances.
  • Station Gallery – This gallery showcases the work of local artists and offers workshops for all ages.
  • Lynde Shores Conservation Area – Head to this area to witness natural conservation and the annual migration of birds. It also houses an educational center.
  • Camp X – Intrepid Park – A secret spy training school of WW II designed by British Security Coordination. You can take a tour here and learn exciting spy techniques.

Natural Attractions of Whitby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cullen Central Park Trails – These trails offer a mix of natural scenery, hiking, and biking trails, making it a perfect option for families, couples, and solo travelers.
  • Heber Down Conservation Area – This is an ideal place for picnicking, hiking, and enjoying scenic views.
  • Whitby Harbour – Take a stroll along the harbor and catch a stunning view of Lake Ontario, take a boat tour, or get some fresh seafood from the many restaurants.
  • Lynde Shores Marshes Wildlife Sanctuary – It provides unique hiking trails while helping visitors discover the natural beauty of the area, including a beaver pond!

Hiking/Biking Routes of Whitby

Whitby offers extensive biking and hiking trails, known for their natural beauty and exceptional views. Here are some of the top trails in the town:

  • The Brooklin Kinsmen Trail – Known for its gardens and natural beauty, this trail is ideal for family activities such as walking, cycling, and rollerblading.
  • The Trans Canada Trail – This is a national trail that will take you through several cities, making it a unique experience connecting the local Whitby biking/hiking routes with other places.
  • East Cross Forest Trail – This trail will take you through several ecosystems, including forests, wetlands, and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Whitby: Activities and Tours

Whitby has several activities designed to entertain and educate visitors. They include the following:

  • Golf – Known as home to the first golf course in Canada, Whitby now boasts several top-rated courses in the area.
  • Tour by bike – Hire a bike and a guide, and explore the town’s main attractions, natural wonders, and architecture.
  • Ghost walks – Learn about the haunted history of Whitby while enjoying a night walk.
  • Whitby Farmer’s Market – Every Saturday, you can experience the unique and exciting culture of Whitby by visiting the farmer’s market.

Food and Drink Scene of Whitby

Whitby’s food and drink scene is a mix of local and international cuisine. Below is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Butter Tart – A popular dessert made from butter, sugar, and maple syrup.
  • Poutine – A famous Canadian dish made with French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Tourtière – A meat pie widely served in French Canadian homes during special occasions.
  • Fresh Whitby Seafood – Whitby has a thriving fishing industry, so make sure to try local seafood specialities.

Local Beverages

  • Whitby Brewery – This brewery is known for its craft beer made with locally sourced high-quality ingredients.
  • Ice Wine – A dessert wine made from grapes that have been frozen while still on the vine.
  • Canadian Maple Syrup – Canada is known worldwide for its high-quality maple syrup, and Whitby offers its visitors an opportunity to purchase some of the best.

  • Deluxe Whitby Cosy Home – A budget-friendly option that comes with a fully functional kitchen.
  • Epic Stay – The ideal vacation apartment in the heart of the town, close to all the significant attractions.

What to Buy in Whitby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Whitby, there are many souvenirs you can bring back home. Here are some popular products to consider:

  • Maple Syrup and Maple Candies – Authentic Canadian maple syrup is a must-buy.
  • Locally Made Crafts – Consider buying souvenirs made by local artists or crafters.
  • Stained Glass Ornamentation – Whitby has a significant stained glass art culture, and visitors can find beautiful ornaments to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Whitby

Visitors to Whitby are usually fascinated by the diverse cultural backgrounds found in the region. The area has a rich history, reflected in the local customs and traditions. One such example is the remembrance of soldiers who sacrificed their lives during the World War I & II on the Remembrance Day held annually on November 11. It is also common to greet people courteously in Whitby, so make sure you’re courteous too!

Best Time to Visit Whitby

The best time to visit Whitby depends on your preferred activities. For outdoor enthusiasts, summer is the best time to explore the hiking and biking trails. Those looking for winter sports, late December to early March is perfect for skiing and snowboarding. Spring and fall are considered the best time to visit the museums and art galleries.

Do’s and Don’ts of Whitby

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Whitby:

Do:

  • Try the local seafood fresh from the harbor.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle for the extended outdoor activities.
  • Explore the local antique stores and unique boutiques.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the public parks or hiking trails.
  • Inappropriate dressing is strictly prohibited inside the museums, art galleries, and churches in Whitby.
  • Over-speeding and public intoxication can lead to heavy fines and penalties in Whitby.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips to ensure you have a safe and healthy trip to Whitby:

Safety in Whitby

  • Use your seatbelt when driving or in a vehicle.
  • Do not leave your valuables in the car.
  • Follow local driving laws and signals.

Health in Whitby

  • Check the weather forecast before heading out on outdoor adventures.
  • Always carry sunscreen and apply it generously.
  • Pack insect repellent for hikes that will protect you from ticks and mosquitoes.

## Getting to Whitby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whitby is easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Whitby

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport – This airport is located around an hour away from Whitby by car.
  • Oshawa Executive Airport - This airport is significantly smaller but is a private airport used for transportation by personal jets and helicopters.

Bus Stations Near Whitby

  • GO Transit – A network of public transportation in Southern Ontario. You can access Whitby directly from Union Station in Toronto, Toronto Pearson International Airport, and other destinations.
  • Durham Region Transit – A local bus service with routes within the Durham region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental Services – Whitby offers several car rental services options, including Uber, Lyft, and Zipcar, among others.

How to Get Around in Whitby

Whitby has an extensive network of transportation that includes local buses, taxis, and more popularly, bike rentals. Car rental services are also readily available, making it easy to navigate on your own.

Where to Go Next from Whitby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whitby’s strategic location makes it easy to explore nearby locations. Here are some of the places you can visit:

  • Toronto – A vibrant city famous for museums, art galleries, shopping malls, and more.
  • Ajax Waterfront – Ideal for activities such as picnicking, kayaking, paddleboarding, and swimming.
  • Scugog Island Cruises – Experience boat tours and cruises through the picturesque expanse of Scugog Island
