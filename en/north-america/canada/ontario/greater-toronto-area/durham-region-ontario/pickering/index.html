A Comprehensive Guide to Pickering, Ontario

Introduction to Pickering

Located in Durham, the region of Ontario, Pickering is a stunning city in the Greater Toronto Area of Ontario, Canada. The city is bordered by the Pickering Nuclear Generating Station to the west, Rouge River to the east, Lake Ontario to the south, and the city of Ajax to the north. With a population of around 88,000, Pickering offers a perfect blend of urban and rural lifestyles.

List of Places to Visit in Pickering

When it comes to the places to visit in Pickering, the city has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the most popular sites that you must visit during your trip to Pickering:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pickering Museum Village: A living history museum with more than 20 buildings spread across 40 acres of land that showcases the history of the 19th century Pickering village.
  • The Nautical Village: A historic part of town featuring specialty shops, restaurants, cafes, and more.
  • The Artistic Hub (PAMA): A gallery of visual arts, digital media, painting, and craftsmanship that presents and sells creative artwork of local artists.
  • The Pickering War Memorial: A memorial dedicated to Canadian soldiers who died during both world wars.

Natural Attractions of Pickering: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rouge National Urban Park: A picturesque national park where you can go for hiking, birdwatching, fishing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Altona Forest: A sustainable forest venture with hiking trails and recreational areas.
  • Frenchman’s Bay: A stunning bay area where you can watch the sunset or take a sailboat ride.
  • Pickering waterfront Trail: A trail that spans five kilometers along with Pickering’s waterfront and connects Ajax and Whitby.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pickering

Pickering is also known for its excellent trails for biking and hiking. These are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in Pickering:

  • Seaton Trail: A 12.8 kilometers long trail that follows the West Duffins Creek and passes through beautiful ravines, wetlands, and forests.
  • Waterfront Trail: A smooth, 42 km paved trail where you can bike or walk and enjoy the scenic views of Lake Ontario.
  • Greenwood Conservation Area: A forested park with three hiking trails, and plentiful opportunities to hill climb and run mountain bikes.

Best Things to Do in Pickering: Activities and Tours

If you are an adventure lover, you’ll be delighted to know that Pickering offers numerous activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Pickering:

  • Pirate Adventure Cruises: Take a pirate-themed boat ride along the scenic Frenchman’s Bay.
  • Sky Zone Trampoline Park: A perfect place to enjoy trampoline jumping with friends and family.
  • Herongate Barn Theatre: A unique Canadian experience providing dinner and theatrical performances while enjoying the scenic views of the farm.
  • Archery Circuit: Take your outdoor activity to the next level with archery and dodgeball games.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pickering

A week-long itinerary can be a good way to experience the best of Pickering. Here’s how you could spend your days:

Day Activity
Day 1-2 Explore the museums and historical sites of Pickering Museum Village and the colourful Nautical Village
Day 3-4 Discover the outdoor recreation and scenic views at Rouge National Urban Park and Altona Forest
Day 5-6 Take an archery class or go trampoline jumping at Skyzone and then enjoy a theatre performance at Herongate Barn Theatre
Day 7 Enjoy shopping, dining and nightlife in the lively city.

Food and Drink Scene of Pickering

The food scene of Pickering is diverse and exquisite, with restaurants and cafes catering to every taste bud. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Freshly caught fish with fries, served hot and crispy.
  • Butter Chicken: A creamy chicken dish cooked in spiced tomato-based sauce.
  • All-Day Breakfasts: From hearty omelettes and eggs benny to delicious french toast- Pickering’s cafes are the perfect place for your first meal of the day.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Try locally brewed beers from Brock Street Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Try an award-winning bottle of wine from the Napa Valley of Canada, located in Pickering - Pingle’s Farm Market.
  • Coffee: Pickering’s cafes serve some excellent coffee options, from latte and cappuccino to flavoured espresso shots.

Where to Stay in Pickering: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re wondering where to stay in Pickering, you can find something that suits any budget.

  • Comfort Inn Pickering: A budget-friendly accommodation located close to the city’s key attractions, featuring comfortable and clean guest rooms.
  • Hilton Garden Inn Toronto/Pickering: A contemporary hotel that includes a fitness centre, an indoor pool, restaurant, and a bar.
  • Abode Bed and Breakfast: A charming family-owned property that provides a traditional bed-and-breakfast experience to guests.

What to Buy in Pickering: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pickering has an interesting collection of souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home as a memory of your trip to the city. Here are some of the best places to buy souvenirs in Pickering:

  • Pickering Markets: A vibrant flea, farmers’ weekend market where you can buy fresh produce, local crafts, and souvenirs.
  • Seaton Gifts: A retail store that offers unique and beautifully crafted gifts and souvenirs.
  • Atria at Pickering: A shopping mall that houses well-known Canadian retailers like Winners, Best Buy, and other designer stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Pickering

Pickering is a home to diverse cultural activities, events and communities such as Pickering Afro-Caribbean Association, Pickering Agricultural Society, Ontario Philharmonic Orchestra, Pickering Museum, Pickering Public Library, and Pickering Recreation Complex.

Best Time to Visit Pickering

The best time to visit Pickering is between May and September when the weather is mild and pleasant. Though, the Autumn season is also lovely, as the fall colours create a stunning scene. Winter is a non-touristic season as the temperature may go down to minus 20 degrees Celsius, and snowstorms can occur.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pickering

Do:

  • explore the beautiful outdoors and enjoy the scenic views.
  • attend local cultural events and festivals.
  • try local cuisine.
  • indulge in local shopping.

Don’t:

  • litter or damage the natural environment.
  • disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • engage in illegal activities.
  • miss visiting the Pickering Museum Village.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pickering

Pickering is a safe city in Ontario, but like with any place it’s important to prioritize your safety while travelling. Take standard safety precautions, stay away from rough areas at night, and guard personal belongings while in public.

Health in Pickering

Healthcare services in Pickering are excellent, including hospitals and clinics that provide quality health care to locals and visitors alike.

Getting to Pickering: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pickering is a well-connected city, and transportation options are abundant. Here are some of the ways to get to Pickering from major cities:

Airports Near Pickering

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport: The closest international airport to Pickering, situated around 60 kilometers from the city.
  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport: A smaller airport that’s convenient for arriving from nearby locations including other parts of Ontario and Quebec.

Bus Stations Near Pickering

  • Pickering Go Station: A station situated near the city center provides bus connections between all major suburbs and the Greater Toronto area.
  • Greyhound Bus Lines: A national bus company that connects Pickering to nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a prevalent option in Pickering; car rentals are available at Pearson Airport and across the city.
  • Ride-share Apps: Like Uber or Lyft. However, it’s worth checking with the local taxi companies for cheaper options or those who prefer the human touch.

How to Get Around in Pickering

Getting around in Pickering is comfortable, with several public transportation options available. Go Transit, Durham Regional Transit, and taxis are the most convenient and popular modes of transportation in the city.

Where to Go Next from Pickering: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore nearby destinations from Pickering, there are plenty of options available:

  • Toronto: A vibrant, multicultural metropolis, located 30 minutes west of Pickering.
  • Niagara Falls: Enjoy one of the world’s famous waterfalls, located two hours away from Pickering.
  • Prince Edward County: A famous wine region and stunning sand dunes, around 1.5 hours drive away. It’s also possible to visit smaller destinations like Ajax, Whitby, Markham and Oshawa, all within a 30-minute drive to an hour drive.

Conclusion

Pickering, Ontario, is a charming city that offers a range of activities and attractions. From outdoor recreational activities to museums and historical sites, the city has something to offer for everyone. If you’re planning a trip to Pickering, use our comprehensive guide to explore the best places to visit, stay in, eat, and have an unforgettable trip.
