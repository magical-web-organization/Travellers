Introduction to Uxbridge

Located in Durham, Ontario, Uxbridge is a charming town that attracts people who are looking for a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of the city life. With a population of around 22,500 people, the town has a lot to offer to its visitors. Uxbridge is a part of the Greater Toronto Area and is located only an hour’s drive away from Toronto. The town is known for its lush green spaces, deep-rooted history, and vibrant local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Uxbridge

Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or someone who wants to explore local culture, there’s something for everyone in Uxbridge. Below are some of the best places to visit in Uxbridge:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Uxbridge Historical Centre: It is an open-air museum that gives visitors a glimpse into the history and culture of the town. The museum has several historic buildings, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • Thomas Foster Memorial: It is a beautiful and historic mansion that was built in 1866 and is now a museum that offers tours.
  • Leaskdale Manse National Historic Site: This is the former home of Canadian author Lucy Maud Montgomery, who wrote the famous novel “Anne of Green Gables.” It is now a museum and a beautiful place to visit.

Natural Attractions of Uxbridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glen Major Forest and Walker Woods: It is a popular destination for hikers, birdwatchers, and cyclists. The forest has several trails that offer scenic views of the town’s countryside.
  • Elgin Park: This is a beautiful park that offers a picturesque view of the Uxbridge Brook. The park is perfect for picnics, nature walks, and family outings.
  • York-Durham Heritage Railway: It is a vintage train that provides visitors with a scenic and historic tour of the town’s countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uxbridge

Uxbridge is known for its beautiful natural spaces that offer hiking and biking trails. Below are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Uxbridge:

  • Trans Canada Trail/Uxbridge Grand Valley Trail: This is a 10.5 km trail that offers scenic views of the town’s countryside and forests. The trail is perfect for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Glen Major Forest and Walker Woods Trail: It is a 17 km trail that offers a challenging hike and is perfect for experienced hikers and mountain bikers.
  • Wooden Sticks Golf Course Trail: It is a 6.5 km trail that offers a scenic view of the beautiful Wooden Sticks Golf Course.

Best Things to Do in Uxbridge: Activities and Tours

Uxbridge has several activities and tours that are perfect for tourists and visitors. Below are some of the best things to do in Uxbridge:

  • Take a Tour of the Thomas Foster Memorial: It is a beautiful historic mansion, and a tour of the mansion will give you a glimpse of the town’s history and culture.
  • Watch a Play at the Uxbridge Music Hall: The Music Hall is a historic building that hosts several plays, concerts, and events throughout the year.
  • Pick Your Own Fruit at Spademan Tree Farm: It is a fun family activity where visitors can pick their fruits.
