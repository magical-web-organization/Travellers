Orillia is a charming city located in Simcoe County, Central Ontario, Canada. This small but charming city is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a popular destination for travelers from all around the world. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, there are plenty of things to see and do in Orillia. In this travel guide, we will give you a comprehensive tour of the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Orillia, Ontario.

Introduction to Orillia

Located just 135 kilometers north of Toronto, Orillia is a city known for its small-town charm and friendly locals. This city is situated between two lakes, Lake Simcoe and Lake Couchiching, making it a popular destination for water sports, fishing, and boating enthusiasts. Orillia is also home to several cultural attractions, historic sites, and natural wonders, making it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a family vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Orillia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Stephen Leacock Museum: The historic home of the famous Canadian author, Stephen Leacock, has been converted into a museum dedicated to his life and work. The museum features several exhibits showcasing his writing, personal belongings, and other artifacts.

  2. The Orillia Museum of Art and History: This museum showcases the history and culture of Orillia and the surrounding area. The exhibits feature local art, artifacts, and displays that highlight the history of the area.

  3. The Huronia Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the First Nations people in the region. The exhibits focus on the culture, traditions, and art of the Huron people who once inhabited the area.

Natural Attractions of Orillia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Couchiching: This lake is a popular destination for boating, swimming, and fishing. There are several parks and beaches along the shoreline, making it a perfect spot for a picnic or a day of relaxation.

  2. The Orillia Waterfront Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and walking, offering stunning views of Lake Simcoe and Lake Couchiching.

  3. The Scout Valley Loop Trail: This trail offers over 10 kilometers of hiking and biking paths through beautiful forests and scenic views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Orillia

  1. The Oro-Medonte Rail Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and walking. It covers over 19 kilometers of scenic paths through lush forests and breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

  2. The Uhthoff Trail: This trail covers over 13.5 kilometers of scenic paths through forests, fields, and wetlands. It is perfect for hiking, biking, and wildlife watching.

Best Things to Do in Orillia: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat cruises on Lake Couchiching: Take a relaxing boat cruise along the scenic shoreline of Lake Couchiching and enjoy stunning views of the city and the surrounding area.

  2. Mariposa Folk Festival: This annual festival held in Orillia is a celebration of folk music, dance, and theater. It features performances by local and international artists, workshops, and food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene in Orillia

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Butter tart: This sweet pastry is a Canadian classic and is a must-try when visiting Orillia. There are several bakeries in the area that specialize in butter tarts, including the iconic Mariposa Market.

  2. Pickerel: This freshwater fish is a local favorite and is often served in restaurants around the city. It is usually pan-fried and served with a side of fries or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Orillia is home to several craft breweries, including the Couchiching Craft Brewing Company and the Barnstormer Brewing Company, offering a selection of locally brewed beer.

  2. Wine: The region surrounding Orillia is known for its vineyards and wineries, producing a variety of locally produced wines.

Where to Stay in Orillia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Best Western Plus Orillia Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, fitness center, and free parking. It is located near the downtown area, making it a convenient base for exploring the city.

  2. Lakehead University Residence and Conference Centre: This budget-friendly hostel-style accommodation is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers. It offers comfortable rooms, free parking, and is located near several attractions in the city.

What to Buy in Orillia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Craft Beer: Take home a selection of locally brewed beer from Couchiching Craft Brewing Company or the Barnstormer Brewing Company, making for a unique and tasty souvenir.

  2. Artwork: Visit the Orillia Museum of Art and History or one of the several galleries in the downtown area to find unique pieces of art made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Orillia

Orillia is a friendly and welcoming community that values its history, traditions, and natural surroundings. The locals take pride in their city and enjoy sharing it with visitors, making it a perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Canadian experience.

Best Time to Visit Orillia

The best time to visit Orillia is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also when most of the outdoor activities and festivals take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orillia

Do:

  1. Respect the natural surroundings and follow Leave No Trace Principles when hiking, biking and exploring the wilderness areas.

  2. Support local businesses and artisans when shopping and dining in the downtown area.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or engage in any activities that may harm the environment or the natural surroundings.

  2. Disrespect the history and culture of the First Nations people who once inhabited the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orillia

Orillia is generally a safe city, but, like any other city, there are certain precautions that visitors should take to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas and always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Orillia

Orillia is home to several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, offering a range of medical services to both tourists and locals. Visitors are advised to have travel health insurance before visiting the region to ensure they are covered for any injuries or illnesses.

Getting to Orillia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Orillia

  1. Toronto Pearson International Airport: This is the closest international airport to Orillia, located approximately 130 kilometers south of the city.

  2. Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport: This airport is located in downtown Toronto and is served by several domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Orillia

  1. Orillia Bus Station: This station is located in downtown Orillia and is served by several bus companies, including Greyhound and Ontario Northland.

  2. Barrie Bus Terminal: This terminal is located approximately 35 kilometers south of Orillia and is served by several bus companies.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding area and allows visitors to travel at their own pace. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the city.

How to Get Around in Orillia

The best way to get around Orillia is by car, bike, or walking. There are several bike-friendly routes in the city, and most of the attractions in the downtown area are within walking distance from each other.

Where to Go Next from Orillia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Muskoka: This region is known for its picturesque lakes and natural beauty, offering visitors several outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating.

  2. Georgian Bay: This stunning bay is located on the eastern side of Lake Huron and offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including swimming, kayaking, and hiking.

Conclusion

Orillia is a hidden gem of Ontario, offering visitors a wide range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. With its charming small-town atmosphere and friendly locals, it’s easy to see why this city is becoming a popular destination for travelers from all over the world. We hope this travel guide has given you the inspiration and information you need to plan your visit to Orillia and make the most out of your trip to this beautiful part of Canada.
