Introduction to Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene, a town located in Simcoe County, Ontario, is a charming destination for those seeking cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and a relaxed atmosphere. The town is located on the southern tip of Georgian Bay, which makes it an ideal destination for many water-related activities. It also has a rich history and culture, which is evident in its numerous historical sites and cultural attractions. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Penetanguishene, providing you with all the information you need to make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Penetanguishene

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Discovery Harbour

Discovery Harbour is a living history museum that offers visitors a glimpse into life during the 19th century. The museum is located on the shores of Penetanguishene Bay and features reenactments of naval battles, guided tours of recreated barracks and historical vessels, and interactive exhibits.

Sainte-Marie among the Hurons

Sainte-Marie among the Hurons is a reconstructed French Jesuit mission that offers visitors a historical look into the interactions between the Huron-Wendat people and the French Jesuits during the 17th century. The site features several reconstructed buildings, interactive exhibits, and guided tours.

Huronia Museum & Ouendat Village

The Huronia Museum & Ouendat Village offers visitors a comprehensive look into the history and culture of the Huron-Wendat people. The museum features exhibits on indigenous history and culture, as well as a reconstructed village that provides visitors with a hands-on experience of traditional indigenous practices.

Natural Attractions of Penetanguishene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Awenda Provincial Park

Awenda Provincial Park is a 3,300-hectare park that features over 30 kilometers of hiking trails, numerous beaches, and stunning views of Georgian Bay. The park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including black bears, wolves, and bald eagles.

Penetanguishene Bay

Penetanguishene Bay is a picturesque bay that offers visitors a wide range of water-based activities, including swimming, boating, and fishing. The bay is also home to several marinas and boat launches, making it easy for visitors to venture out onto the water.

Champlain Wendat Trail

The Champlain Wendat Trail is a 43-kilometer trail that winds through forests, wetlands, and fields, offering visitors stunning views of Georgian Bay and the surrounding area. The trail is open year-round and is suitable for both hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene offers visitors numerous hiking and biking routes that cater to all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Tay Shore Trail
  • Simcoe County Loop Trail
  • Tiny Marsh Provincial Wildlife Area
  • Ghost Road Trail

Best Things to Do in Penetanguishene: Activities and Tours

Bayview Wildwood Resort

Bayview Wildwood Resort is a year-round resort that offers a wide range of activities for visitors, including kayaking, fishing, and snowmobiling. The resort also offers guided tours of the area, providing visitors with unique insights into the local culture and environment.

Penetanguishene Bike Tours

Penetanguishene Bike Tours offers visitors the opportunity to explore the town and surrounding areas on two wheels. The tours are led by knowledgeable guides who provide visitors with insights into the local culture and history.

Local Culture and Customs of Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene has a rich history and culture that is evident in its museums, historical sites, and local events. The area has a strong indigenous heritage, and visitors can learn about traditional practices and beliefs at the Huronia Museum & Ouendat Village.

Best Time to Visit Penetanguishene

The best time to visit Penetanguishene is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and most of the outdoor attractions are open. However, visiting during the fall months also offers stunning views of colorful foliage and is a great time to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

  • Try local cuisine and beverages

  • Ignore posted signage or safety warnings

Safety and Health

Safety in Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene is a safe and welcoming destination for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or swimming. Visitors should also be vigilant of their surroundings and belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene has several medical clinics and pharmacies, and visitors should have health insurance coverage when traveling. Visitors should also take precautions such as using insect repellent and staying hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Penetanguishene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Penetanguishene

The nearest airport to Penetanguishene is Toronto Pearson International Airport, which is approximately 140 kilometers away. From Toronto, visitors can rent a car or take a bus or train to Penetanguishene.

Bus Stations Near Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene has a bus station located in the downtown area, which provides daily service to Toronto and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Penetanguishene from other parts of Ontario or take a train to nearby stations such as Barrie or Midland, which are approximately 30 minutes away.

How to Get Around in Penetanguishene

Penetanguishene is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Penetanguishene: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Midland

Midland is a charming town located just 10 minutes from Penetanguishene, with a rich history and cultural sites such as the Wye Marsh Wildlife Centre and the Huronia Museum.

Wasaga Beach

Wasaga Beach, located approximately 45 minutes from Penetanguishene, is the longest freshwater beach in the world and offers visitors a wide range of water-based activities and water parks.

Conclusion

Penetanguishene is a charming destination that offers visitors a wide range of cultural and outdoor experiences. From visiting historical sites to hiking through beautiful nature trails, there’s something for everyone in this town. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now equipped with all the information you need to plan your visit and make the most out of your stay.
