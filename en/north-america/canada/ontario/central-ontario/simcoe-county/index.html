Introduction to Simcoe County

Simcoe County is a picturesque region located in Central Ontario, Canada. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Simcoe County is home to rolling hills, pristine lakes, dense forests, and a wide range of wildlife. The region is also rich in history and culture, as evidenced by its many museums, galleries, and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Simcoe County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Huronia Museum

Located in the historic town of Midland, the Huronia Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning about the region’s history and culture. The museum’s extensive collection includes artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays that showcase the area’s Indigenous and pioneer heritage.

2. Barrie Heritage Park

Barrie Heritage Park is a 6-acre historic site that features a collection of restored heritage buildings, including a blacksmith shop, a schoolhouse, and a log cabin. The park also offers guided tours that provide visitors with an insight into the area’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Simcoe County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Wasaga Beach

Wasaga Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Ontario, attracting millions of visitors each year. The beach spans over 14km and offers a wide range of activities, including swimming, sunbathing, and watersports. Visitors can also enjoy a picnic or a walk along the boardwalk.

2. Horseshoe Valley

Horseshoe Valley is a year-round destination that offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including skiing, snowboarding, hiking, and mountain biking. The valley is surrounded by a stunning natural landscape, which makes it a great place for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simcoe County

Simcoe County is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with a range of trails that cater to both beginners and experienced adventurers. Some of the best routes include:

  • Ganaraska Hiking Trail
  • Simcoe County Loop Trail
  • Georgian Trail
  • Oro-Medonte Rail Trail
  • Tiny Marsh Provincial Wildlife Area Trails

Each of these routes offers a unique perspective on the region’s natural beauty and provides an excellent way to explore the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Simcoe County: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Simcoe County, including:

  • Kayaking and canoeing on the Mad River
  • Visiting local wineries and breweries
  • Touring the Sainte-Marie among the Hurons
  • Witnessing the thrill of stock car racing at Sunset Speedway
  • Horseback riding in the Horseshoe Valley

Week-Long Itinerary for Simcoe County

Day 1-2: Explore the Barrie Heritage Park and discover the history of the region. Spend the afternoon at Wasaga Beach and enjoy the beach activities.

Day 3: Hike the Ganaraska Hiking Trail or the Simcoe County Loop Trail.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Sainte-Marie among the Hurons, which is a historical site that showcases the region’s Indigenous heritage.

Day 5: Go horseback riding in the Horseshoe Valley.

Day 6-7: Visit local wineries and breweries, and explore the charming town of Creemore.

Food and Drink Scene of Simcoe County

Must-Try Dishes

Simcoe County is known for its excellent cuisine, which includes:

  • Butter tarts
  • Poutine
  • Kawartha ice cream
  • Fresh local produce

Local Beverages

Simcoe County is home to a range of local breweries and wineries, which offer a variety of craft beers and fine wines. Some of the best places to visit include:

  • Muskoka Brewery
  • Georgian Hills Vineyards
  • Creemore Springs Brewery

Local Culture and Customs of Simcoe County

Simcoe County is a welcoming and hospitable region that values its Indigenous culture and heritage. The region is also known for its friendly locals, who are always happy to help visitors navigate the area.

Best Time to Visit Simcoe County

The best time to visit Simcoe County is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the fall season, from September to November, is also a great time to visit, as it offers stunning fall foliage and harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simcoe County

Do:

  • Respect Indigenous culture and heritage
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Follow the local rules and regulations

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural landscape
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Simcoe County

Overall, Simcoe County is a very safe region, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should still take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Simcoe County

Simcoe County has a range of healthcare facilities, including hospitals and urgent care clinics. Visitors should have appropriate insurance coverage and be aware of any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Simcoe County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Simcoe County

The closest major airport to Simcoe County is Toronto Pearson International Airport, which is approximately 110km away.

Bus Stations Near Simcoe County

Greyhound Bus Lines has several stations throughout Simcoe County, including in Barrie and Orillia.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or use ride-sharing services like Uber to get around the region.

How to Get Around in Simcoe County

The best way to get around Simcoe County is by car, as it provides the most flexibility and allows visitors to explore the region at their own pace. However, visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Simcoe County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Simcoe County include:

  • Georgian Bay Islands National Park

  • Elora Gorge Conservation Area

Conclusion

Simcoe County is a stunning region that offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty, history, and culture of Central Ontario. With a range of activities, attractions, and accommodation options, Simcoe County is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Canada.
