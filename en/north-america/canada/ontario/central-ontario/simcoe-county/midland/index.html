Introduction to Midland

Midland is located in the picturesque Simcoe County, in Central Ontario, Canada. This small town is situated on the southern end of Georgian Bay and bordered by the Wye River, bringing along with it scenic waterfronts, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and much more. Midland is a popular destination for tourists, thanks to its rich history, vibrant culture, and diverse outdoor activities. If you’re planning a trip to Midland, this travel guide will help you make the most of your experience.

List of Places to Visit in Midland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Huronia Museum & Ouendat Village

The Huronia Museum is a must-visit for any history buff. Here, you’ll get to explore the Ouendat Village replica, which features a collection of Native artifacts and historical displays. You can also learn about the history of the area, from the first inhabitants to the present day.

Sainte-Marie among the Hurons

This historical site is a reconstruction of the original French Jesuit Mission that was established in the 17th century. The mission now serves as a living museum, where you can see the daily life of the Jesuit missionaries and the First Nations people in the area.

Discovery Harbour

Discovery Harbour is an open-air museum featuring a recreated 19th-century British naval and military base. Here, you’ll get to see the War of 1812 and the Battle of Georgian Bay come to life.

Natural Attractions of Midland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Martyrs’ Shrine

The Martyrs’ Shrine is a national shrine to the Canadian Martyrs, who were the first European missionaries in the area. This site features beautiful gardens, walking trails, and historical displays.

Little Lake Park

Little Lake Park is a beautiful park located in the heart of Midland. Here, you can enjoy a picnic, go fishing, or just take a relaxing walk.

Awenda Provincial Park

Awenda Provincial Park is a nature lover’s paradise, featuring over 2,900 hectares of wilderness to explore. Here, you can take a hike, go birdwatching, or just enjoy the scenic beauty of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Midland

The Tay Shore Trail

This 20-kilometer trail is a must-do for any outdoor enthusiast. The trail follows the shores of Georgian Bay, offering stunning views of the water and the nearby islands. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking.

Midland Rotary Waterfront Trail

The Rotary Waterfront Trail is a scenic trail that runs for over 9 km along the shores of Georgian Bay. Here, you can enjoy a leisurely walk or bike ride while taking in the beautiful scenery.

Wye Marsh Wildlife Centre

The Wye Marsh Wildlife Centre has a boardwalk that takes you on a scenic journey through the marsh. Here, you can spot various wildlife species, including beavers, turtles, and birds.

Best Things to Do in Midland: Activities and Tours

Georgian Bay and Thirty Thousand Islands Boat Tour

This boat tour offers breathtaking views of the Georgian Bay and the Thirty Thousand Islands. The tour takes you to some of the most beautiful and secluded areas of the bay, giving you the chance to spot wildlife along the way.

King’s Wharf Theatre

The King’s Wharf Theatre is a must-visit for any theater lover. Here, you can catch a live show or play in a beautiful waterfront setting.

Midland Cultural Centre

The Midland Cultural Centre is a hub of art, culture, and entertainment. Here, you can enjoy live performances, art exhibits, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Midland

Must-Try Dishes

Traditional Fish and Chips

Midland is a town known for its delicious seafood in multiple preparations. To top the list, traditional Fish & Chips is something that everyone must try. You can try it at Explorer’s Cafe and Restaurant, which is famous for its seafood preparations.

Classic Canadian Poutine

Poutine is a popular Canadian dish and is made with french fries, cheese curds, and gravy. You can try the classic poutine at The Arch Steak House and Tavern, which offers excellent food and service.

Local Beverages

Beer from the Midland Brewing Company

The Midland Brewing Company offers a variety of beers, including the popular Huronia Honey Blonde beer. You can sample their brews at their brewery, which has a patio overlooking the bay.

Cider from Heritage Estate Winery and Cidery

Heritage Estate Winery and Cidery is a family-owned business that produces a variety of ciders using locally sourced apples. You can visit their tasting room and try their delicious ciders.

Where to Stay in Midland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kings Inn Midland

Kings Inn Midland is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Midland. The hotel features clean and comfortable rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and friendly staff.

Best Western Plus Highland Inn & Conference Centre

The Best Western Plus Highland Inn & Conference Centre is a comfortable and modern hotel that is conveniently located near all major attractions in Midland. The hotel features an indoor pool, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

Silverstar Motel

The Silverstar Motel is a charming and affordable motel located just outside of Midland. The motel features clean and comfortable rooms, a picnic area, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Midland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Midland Honey

Midland is home to a few apiaries that produce local honey. You can bring home some of this honey as a sweet memento of your trip.

Cedar Walking Stick

The Wye Marsh Wildlife Centre sells handmade cedar walking sticks made by local artisans. These make a useful souvenir, and you can use it for hikes to come.

Local Culture and Customs of Midland

In Midland, Canada’s indigenous culture is respected and valued. Many local businesses offer indigenous tours and walks that highlight the history and traditions of the First Nations in Canada.

Best Time to Visit Midland

Midland is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from late spring to early fall (May to October) when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season sees the colors of the trees changing, making it a beautiful time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Midland

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Try the local cuisine

  • Take a boat tour to explore the best of Midland

  • Take advantage of the hiking trails

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or the waterfront

  • Disturb the local wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Midland

Midland is a generally safe place, but it’s always advisable to take precautions while traveling. When exploring the parks, trails, and waterfronts, stay aware of your surroundings and valuables.

Health in Midland

Hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies are available in Midland. Ensure you have travel insurance before visiting Midland.

Getting to Midland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Midland

The nearest airport to Midland is Toronto Pearson International Airport, which is a two-hour drive away. Midland also has a small airport that offers charter flights.

Bus Stations Near Midland

Midland is connected to major cities in Ontario via local bus services. The closest Greyhound bus station is located in Barrie.

Other Options

You can also drive to Midland via highways 400 or 93. There’s no public transit in the town.

How to Get Around in Midland

The best way to get around Midland is by car. You can rent a vehicle from the airport or in the town. Taxis are available, but the fares are relatively high.

Where to Go Next from Midland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blue Mountain

Blue Mountain is a popular ski resort located a short drive from Midland. Here, you can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities.

Georgian Bay Islands National Park

Georgian Bay Islands National Park is accessible via a boat tour from Midland. Here, you can explore the beautiful islands and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Midland is a beautiful town in central Ontario that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, natural attractions, or outdoor activities, Midland has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Midland, Canada.
