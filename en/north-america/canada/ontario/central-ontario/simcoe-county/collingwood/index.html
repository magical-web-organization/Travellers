Collingwood is a serene and picturesque town located in the heart of Simcoe County, Ontario, Canada. It is known for its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance. Whether you are planning a long weekend getaway or a week-long trip, Collingwood has something for everyone. This ultimate travel guide will help you discover all the must-see attractions, the vibrant food and drink scene, and the best things to do in Collingwood.

Introduction to Collingwood

Collingwood’s rich history dates back to the late 1830s when it was first settled by European immigrants. Today, it is a growing town with a population of around 22,000 people. Collingwood is well-known for its natural beauty, surrounded by Georgian Bay, Blue Mountain, and the Nottawasaga Bay. The town also has several attractions that offer visitors a glimpse into its past and present.

List of Places to Visit in Collingwood

Collingwood has a wide variety of attractions, including museums, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and local delicacies that are worth exploring. Here’s a list of places to visit in Collingwood, no matter when you plan to travel.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Collingwood Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich history of Collingwood. It has excellent exhibits featuring the town’s early shipbuilding history, its role in the Underground Railroad, and the development of skiing in the region.
  • Craigleith Heritage Depot: This train station turned museum is home to an impressive archive on the area’s railway history and features exhibits on how the railroad transformed the town.
  • Huron Kinloss Ice Cream Trail: A self-guided tour that takes you through some of the best ice cream shops in Huron-Kinloss, a few kilometers from Collingwood, showcasing handmade ice cream made by locals in different flavors and styles.

Natural Attractions of Collingwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Mountain Resort: In winter, this premier resort offers snowboarding and skiing opportunities to enthusiasts while providing excellent hiking paths during the summer months.
  • Scenic Caves Nature Adventures: An outdoor adventure park with natural caves, a high suspension bridge, and several hiking trails. This place offers visitors a chance to explore some of Canada’s natural beauty.
  • Collingwood Arboretum & Trails: This place has an extensive network of trails for hikers and bikers, covering almost 10 kilometers and offering breathtaking views of Collingwood’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Collingwood

Collingwood and its surroundings have some of the best hiking and biking trails, which appeal to active vacationers and nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try in Collingwood:

  • Georgian Trail: A 34 km trail that stretches from Meaford to Collingwood, with an asphalt surface that caters to cyclists, walkers, and runners. It is highly recommendable for visitors who love biking and gentle walks.
  • Osler Bluff Ski Club Trails: With a network of over ten trails nestled around the ski club, offers a variety of trails for beginner to advanced riders.
  • Collingwood Arboretum & Trails: A well-groomed trail of almost 10 kilometers for hikers and bikers with options of easy, medium, and difficult.

Best Things to Do in Collingwood: Activities and Tours

Collingwood offers an extensive range of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained throughout their trips. Here are some of the best things to do in Collingwood:

  • Blue Mountain Village Adventure Park: One of Ontario’s largest outdoor Adventure Park that offers a range of activities from zip-lining through the trees, climbing ropes, or testing your nerves on the high wire.
  • Boarder Pass Snow Park: A premier snowboarding and skiing center that caters to kids and adults seeking an adrenaline rush. This place offers thrilling winter activities that will leave you spell-bound.
  • Collingwood Sunset Cruise: Take a sunset cruise on the Bay or experience the thrill of a private yacht with a tour guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Collingwood

Collingwood’s food and drink scene is particularly diverse, highlighting their rich food culture. Local food options include different local Canadian dishes with a fusion of world cuisine. Notably, the town is renowned for its seafood, locally brewed beer, and artisanal ice cream shops.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Georgian Hills Vineyards: Award-winning vineyard producing excellent wine selections to pair with their cheese and charcuterie boards.
  • The Tremont Cafe: A local favorite, offering a farm to table menu marked with local and organic produce. Serving brunch, lunch, and dinner.
  • Harbour Street Fish Bar: A top-rated seafood restaurant with a cozy atmosphere, featuring fresh-caught seafood, a raw bar, and craft beer.

Local Beverages

  • Thornbury Village Craft Brewery: A craft brewery with a passion for quality and premium beer. Try our selection of beers featuring the award-winning Lighthaus Lager.
  • Side Launch Brewing Company: A favorite stop for locals and visitors, featuring quality craft beer, live music, and patio ambiance.
  • Georgian Hills Vineyards: Award-winning vineyard producing excellent wine selections to pair with their cheese and charcuterie boards.

Where to Stay in Collingwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Collingwood has a wide range of accommodation options to choose from, so whether you are traveling solo or with your family, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the best accommodations that will offer you a memorable stay during your visit:

  • Living Water Resort: A premier waterfront hotel and spa, with a great pool and amenities facing the Georgian Bay.
  • Cranberry Golf Resort: A golf resort that offers a mix of outdoor sports like hiking, beach volleyball, and golf. The resort also houses several restaurants and an upscale spa.
  • Luxury waterfront home: A luxurious vacation rental situated in the heart of Collingwood, with stunning views over the bay

What to Buy in Collingwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Collingwood offers visitors a range of interesting local products to take home as a reminder of their trip. Some of the best things to buy in Collingwood include:

  • Artisanal chocolates from the Chocolate Factory.
  • Locally crafted pottery by Collingwood Potters.
  • Handmade items and unique crafts at The Collingwood Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Collingwood

Collingwood is very friendly and welcoming to visitors, which mirrors the culture and ethos of the local residents. Visitors should respect local customs and social norms, including dressing modestly in religious places and taking off your shoes in people’s homes.

Best Time to Visit Collingwood

Summer (June to September) is the best time to visit Collingwood, where visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and uncover the area’s much-celebrated natural beauty. Winter brings skiing and snowboarding opportunities and winter-related events like the Crystal Blizzard Fest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Collingwood

Do:

  • Feel free to start conversations with locals, it’s just part of the culture.
  • Try the artisanal ice cream.
  • Always have a camera, as every corner’s view is stunning.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter- keep the town clean.
  • Don’t forget to carry enough cash, as some local shops do not accept cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Collingwood

Collingwood is a peaceful and tranquil town with very low crime rates. Travelers should be cautious when hiking or biking, wear helmets, carry a water bottle, and wear appropriate clothing, especially during the winter season.

Health in Collingwood

The town’s health care facilities are stellar and convenient for tourists. The Collingwood General & Marine Hospital offers quality health services within the town. However, visitors should carry enough medication if they have pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Collingwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Collingwood is accessible by car, bus, or plane:

Airports Near Collingwood

  • Pearson International (YYZ) in Mississauga, Ontario, 124 km away.
  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport (YTZ) in Toronto, Ontario, 148 km away.

Bus Stations Near Collingwood

  • Collingwood Bus Terminal: Located in town, buses to and from Toronto and some other destinations make a stop here.
  • Greyhound Bus Terminal in Barrie, Ontario, 38km away.

Other Options

  • Collingwood Airport: Located four kilometers east of the town, offers private plane services and charter flights.

How To Get Around in Collingwood

Collingwood is a pedestrian-friendly town, with most of its attractions within walking distance. Visitors can also use the Colltrans public transport system for transportation around the town.

Where to Go Next from Collingwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Collingwood is just the beginning of discovering the beauty of Central Ontario. Explore places like:

  • Blue Mountain Ski Resort: A winter paradise featuring skiing and snowboarding and other adventure activities.
  • Bruce Peninsula National Park: Marvel at the glistening turquoise waters, towering cliffs, and deep limestone crevices.
  • Owen Sound: This charming town is the ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts, boasting several walking, biking, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Collingwood’s charm lies in its ability to offer visitors a taste of natural beauty, culture, and history. Whether you are looking for an adventure-packed or relaxed vacation, Collingwood is the place to be. Plan your visit today to discover all the hidden gems that this town has to offer.
