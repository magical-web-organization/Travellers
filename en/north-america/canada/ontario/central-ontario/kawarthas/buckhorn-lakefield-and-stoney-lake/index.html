Introduction to Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Located in the heart of the Kawarthas in Central Ontario, Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake offer unique experiences for travelers looking to explore Canada’s diverse landscape.

Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake boast a wealth of cultural and natural attractions, ranging from historic museums and heritage sites to lush parks and scenic viewpoints. The region is especially famous for its picturesque waterways and abundance of outdoor recreational activities, including hiking, cycling, and canoeing.

This Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake travel guide highlights the best things to see, do, and experience in the area, as well as offer useful tips on where to stay, eat, and what to buy.

List of Places to Visit in Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Canadian Canoe Museum

The Canadian Canoe Museum is a must-see for anyone interested in the history, culture, and art of canoeing. The museum houses over 600 canoes and kayaks from around the world, dating back to over 150 years ago.

2. Lang Pioneer Village Museum

Lang Pioneer Village Museum is an award-winning living history museum that offers visitors the opportunity to experience life in a 19th-century village. The museum has over 30 restored buildings, including a schoolhouse, general store, and blacksmith shop.

3. Petroglyphs Provincial Park

Petroglyphs Provincial Park is home to over 900 petroglyphs, some of which are up to 1,200 years old. The petroglyphs represent the teachings and spirituality of the First Nations people and are considered a sacred site.

Natural Attractions of Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Warsaw Caves Conservation Area

Warsaw Caves Conservation Area is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts, featuring a range of activities such as hiking, caving, and rock climbing. The park’s unique geological formations, including seven limestone caves, are a true natural wonder.

2. Kawartha Highlands Provincial Park

Kawartha Highlands Provincial Park comprises of over 375-square kilometers of rugged, untouched wilderness offering a range of hiking and paddling opportunities.

3. Lockside Trading Company

Lockside Trading Company is both a shopping destination and a cultural attraction, offering visitors a chance to browse home decor, gifts, and outdoor gear amongst a backdrop of the locks of the Trent-Severn Waterway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

1. Kawartha Trans Canada Trail

This trail is part of the world’s longest recreational trail and stretches 26 km throughout the city of Peterborough, passing through the forests and wetlands of the Kawarthas.

2. Stoney Lake Trails

Stoney Lake Trails offer three separate routes: the John Earle Chase Memorial Trail, Fairbairn Trail, and Katchiwano Trail, ranging in distance from 1.5 to 5 km.

Best Things to Do in Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake: Activities and Tours

1. Canoeing and Kayaking

No visit to the Kawarthas is complete without a canoeing or kayaking adventure. With so many lakes and waterways, from quiet backwaters to rapids, there is something for every skill level.

2. Fishing

Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake all offer excellent fishing opportunities, with a wide range of species such as bass, pickerel, and trout.

3. Zipline and Aerial Adventure Parks

If you’re looking for an adrenaline rush, try ziplining and tackling aerial courses at Treetop Trekking Ganaraska.

Food and Drink Scene of Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Must-Try Dishes

1. Butter Tarts

Butter Tarts, a classic Canadian dish, are small pastries made of butter, sugar, and syrup and are a must-try when in the Kawarthas.

2. Kawartha Dairy Ice Cream

Kawartha Dairy Ice Cream is made of rich, natural ingredients and is a popular treat for visitors and locals alike.

Local Beverages

1. Kawartha Country Winery

The Kawartha Country Winery is a local favourite for wine lovers, offering a selection of fruit wines made from locally sourced fruits and berries.

2. Publican House Brewery

The Publican House Brewery is a popular spot for beer fans, with several options on tap including a honey blonde ale and a milk stout.

Where to Stay in Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Beachwood Resort

Beachwood Resort is a sprawling, family-friendly resort with a private beach, water activities, and a spa.

2. Westwind Inn on the Lake

The Westwind Inn on the Lake offers cozy rooms and suites, gourmet meals, and a relaxing atmosphere set against the backdrop of Stoney Lake.

What to Buy in Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Handmade Pottery

Buckhorn and the Kawarthas are known for their beautiful handmade pottery, created by talented local artists.

2. Local Maple Syrup

Maple syrup is a quintessential Canadian souvenir and can be picked up at several local shops and farmers’ markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

The Kawarthas are situated on the traditional lands of the Williams Treaties First Nations. Visitors can take part in various cultural events celebrating the region’s First Nations heritage, such as the Curve Lake First Nation Pow Wow.

Best Time to Visit Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

The best time to visit Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake is during the summer months when the days are long, and the weather is warm, making it perfect for outdoor activities. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the autumn colours in full effect, and winter offers a range of winter activities like snowmobiling, cross-country skiing and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and the animals that inhabit it
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities available
  • Visit the local museums and heritage sites

Don’t:

  • Venture off hiking trails or attempt to explore caves alone
  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats
  • Leave litter behind

Safety and Health

Safety in Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake have a low crime rate and are generally safe. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables in plain sight in their cars.

Health in Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Tick populations in the area may pose a risk of Lyme disease, so it is recommended to dress appropriately and use insect repellent when spending time outdoors in the region.

Getting to Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

The closest airports to Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake are Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ) and Peterborough Airport (YPQ), both around two hours’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Megabus, provide service to the Peterborough Bus terminal, which is 30 minutes away by car from Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to explore Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake, giving visitors the freedom to travel at their leisure and explore the region’s natural beauty.

How to Get Around in Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake

Having a car is the best way to get around the region, but bike rentals are also available during the summer months.

Where to Go Next from Buckhorn, Lakefield and Stoney Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Algonquin Provincial Park

Algonquin Provincial Park, located about two hours north of Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake, is one of Canada’s most famous national parks, boasting vast forests, wildlife, and lakes.

2. Prince Edward County

Prince Edward County, about an hour’s drive from Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake, is a quaint island community featuring wineries, art galleries, and stunning beaches.

Conclusion

Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake offer a unique and exciting experience for those looking to explore central Ontario’s natural and cultural beauty. From hiking in the Kawarthas Highlands Provincial Park to visiting the petroglyphs at Petroglyphs Provincial Park, and trying the local maple syrup, Buckhorn, Lakefield, and Stoney Lake offer a variety of experiences for travelers of all ages and interests.
