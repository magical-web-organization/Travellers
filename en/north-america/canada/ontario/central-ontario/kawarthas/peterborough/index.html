Introduction to Peterborough

Located in the heart of Kawarthas in Central Ontario, Peterborough is a charming city known for its abundance of natural beauty, rich history, and exciting recreational activities. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and galleries, hiking, biking, or paddling, or simply taking in the stunning scenery, Peterborough provides something unique for every type of traveler.

In this ultimate guide to Peterborough, we have curated a comprehensive list of everything you need to know to make the most out of your trip to this beautiful Canadian city.

List of Places to Visit in Peterborough

Peterborough offers a diverse range of attractions and activities that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here is a list of some of the unmissable things to see and do in Peterborough:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Peterborough is full of fascinating museums and historical landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Canadian Canoe Museum: The Canadian Canoe Museum houses the world’s largest collection of canoes, kayaks, and paddled watercrafts and is ideal for those interested in Canada’s rich watercraft heritage and incredible canoeing displays.
  • Peterborough Museum and Archives: This museum provides a detailed insight into the history and culture of the region and features exhibitions dedicated to the city’s industrial heritage, notable figures in Peterborough’s history, and various rotating exhibitions from around the globe.
  • Lang Pioneer Village Museum: This restored 19th-century Pioneer Village offers visitors in-depth knowledge about the lifestyles and customs of the early settlers of the region and provides a super educational and informative experience.

Natural Attractions of Peterborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will fall in love with the natural beauty of Peterborough. There are plenty of awe-inspiring natural attractions to choose from, including:

  • Riverview Park and Zoo: home to over 27 animal exhibits and over 45 acres of walking trails, the park and zoo make a great family friendly outing.
  • The Peterborough Lift Locks: This feat of engineering is an impressive and historic attraction that continues to awe visitors with its hydraulic lifts that raise boats from the Trent Canal to the highest freshwater point in the world, over 65 feet high.
  • Lakefield Trail: This 9.5 km trail starts at the white bridge in downtown Lakefield and is great for joggers, walkers, and bikers, looking to explore the trails and scenic vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peterborough

Peterborough is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of outstanding hiking and biking trails, here are some popular routes:

  • The Great Trail: This trail is part of the world’s biggest network of recreational trails, stretching more than 24,000 kilometers across Canada. The Peterborough section of the trail spans 36 km, following the Otonabee River through the city towards Lakefield.
  • Rotary Greenway Trail: Follows the old railway corridor and extends for about 21 km, linking Peterborough East to Lakefield. The trail is suitable for cycling as well as walking or running.
  • Warsaw Caves Conservation Area: Though it’s famous for its caves, the conservation area of Warsaw Caves is also perfect for hikers, with over 15 km of nature trails that provide visitors with an unwinding journey through forests, marsh, and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Peterborough: Activities and Tours

Peterborough is home to a wide range of entertaining activities that cater to just about any kind of traveler. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Canoeing: Paddle the mellow waters of the Otonabee River with your peers and experience the rich wildlife and greenery that Peterborough has to offer
  • Peterborough Pedal n Paddle: Embark on a fun and unique adventure that lets you paddle for an hour, cycle for an hour, and paddle back for another hour through some of the area’s most picturesque waterways.
  • Horseback Riding: Take an adventurous yet peaceful ride around the stunning countryside and woodland trails of Peterborough with local stables like Horizon Riding Inc.
  • Peterborough Lift Lock Cruise: Enjoy a journey through the Trent Severn Waterway system and learn about the importance of the Peterborough Lift Lock in shaping this waterway. Actors bring the history to life on the entertaining guided boat tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peterborough

Whether you have limited time to explore or are looking to spend an entire week in Peterborough, we have created an itinerary that will help you cover everything essential to the region:

Day 1: Arrive in Peterborough and get settled in your accommodation and roam around the downtown Peterborough region, maybe grab a bite at the trendy restaurants or explore the local shopping boutiques.

Day 2: Tour the local Peterborough Museum and the incredible Canadian Canoe Museum to explore Canada’s historic watercraft history.

Day 3: Explore the stunning natural beauty of Peterborough by hiking the 1.2-mile-long Jackson’s Park Trail and visiting the scenic Paradise park on the river.

Day 4: Rent a canoe or kayak and take a leisurely paddle down the Stillwater Creek or the Trent River or take a leisurely cruise on the lift locks.

Day 5: Pedal and paddle across the picturesque Otonabee River with the local company Peterborough Pedal n Paddle and take a cycling tour around the best parks and landmarks of Peterborough.

Day 6: Take a trip to the Warsaw Caves Conservation area and explore the scenic trails and stunning wildlife of this part of the region.

Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day exploring the incredible local food and drink scene of Peterborough before preparing for your next adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Peterborough

Peterborough’s local food and drink scene is a foodie’s dream come true with a diverse range of dishes and beverages to taste. Here are a few essential food and drink options to try in the city:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kawartha Dairy Ice Cream: Possibly one of the most popular ice cream brands in all of Canada, Kawartha Dairy’s ice cream boasts Canadian pride and diverse and original flavors.
  • Peameal Bacon on a Bun: This Canadian classic is a simple yet delicious sandwich made with juicy slices of bacon and comes with an option of toppings that is perfect for a quick lunchtime meal.
  • Butter Tarts: This regional dessert is a small, tartlet-like pastry that contains a sweet filling of syrup, butter, and eggs, making it a must-try treat for all sweet-toothed visitors.

Local Beverages

  • Kawartha Country Wines: Try some locally produced, unique fruit wines, and celebrate Canadian flavors. Of your choice ranging from raspberry wine to sweet strawberry, and some interesting blueberry lavender wines as well.
  • Publican House Brewery: Publican Brewery serves up locally brewed craft beers, such as Harlot Belgian Pale Ale and Square Nail Pale Ale, among others.

Where to Stay in Peterborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peterborough offers accommodation to suit all budgets and preferences, including:

Best Hotels in Peterborough

  • The Park Place: A modern, four-star hotel located in downtown Peterborough and is known for its stylish rooms and cozy plush furnishings. It is equipped with all the amenities that you might need for a comfortable stay.

  • Best Western Plus Otonabee Inn: Offering comfortable lodgings and easy access to the Peterborough area’s top attractions, including The Canadian Canoe Museum, this hotel is perfect for families looking for quality accommodation that’s easy on the wallet.

Best Hostels in Peterborough

  • Twin Pines Orchards and Cottages: Offering the perfect budget-friendly getaway and ultimate cottage escape, these locally run cottages are the perfect way to get accustomed to the local lifestyle and culture of Peterborough.

  • The Wallflower: A restored building downtown that has been converted into a hostel, dormitory-style accommodation is perfect for travelers who want to make friends and save money while exploring all that Peterborough has to offer.

What to Buy in Peterborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peterborough is known for its locally made products ranging from artisan soaps to meat pies and even old fashion hand-crafted jewelry. Explore local shops and boutiques to discover unique souvenirs like:

  • Chemong Lodge Gift Shop: Take home a piece of the iconic Chemong Lodge from the gift shop’s unique collection of souvenirs

  • Hempire Trading Co: A retail store that specializes in local and handcrafted gifts, like artisan soaps, natural skincare products, and herbal remedies, etc.

Local Culture and Customs of Peterborough

Peterborough is primarily an English-speaking city with a thriving arts and cultural scene. The region is rich in Indigenous history, and visitors have the opportunity to learn and experience local Indigenous culture in-depth. The best way to experience and connect to the local culture is by attending the region’s myriad cultural festivals, galleries, and cultural performances scattered around town.

Best Time to Visit Peterborough

The best time to visit Peterborough is between late spring and fall, which is perfect for outdoor activities, sightseeing, and exploring natural wonders. Summer is the busiest time of year and is ideal for water activities. Winter months in Peterborough are relatively quiet, with far fewer visitors and activities other than skiing and ice skating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peterborough

Here are a few guidelines for what to do and not to do while visiting Peterborough:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Indulge in outdoor and adventure activities as Peterborough offers an experience like no other.
  • Attend local outdoor cultural events like the Festival of Lights, Peterborough Pride, and Canada Day celebrations.
  • Wear comfortable, layered clothing, and walking shoes, depending on the season.

Don’t:

  • Litter around the natural areas and avoid disturbing wildlife habitats.
  • Be loud and inconsiderate during the night and especially in a residential area.
  • Over work yourself on a summer sunny day, always ensure you are hydrated and protected from the harsh sun rays.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peterborough

Peterborough is reasonably safe and connected via multiple modes of transport ensuring easy travel to and fro. In general, Peterborough is safe to walk around at any time of day or night, but it’s always best to keep your belongings safe when in crowded areas and public transport.

Health in Peterborough

Peterborough is a safe and healthy destination providing several medical outlets, hospitals, and clinics. Guests are urged to maintain good health practices, stay hydrated, and wear sunscreen when visiting.

Getting to Peterborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you are traveling from a nearby city or a foreign country, getting to Peterborough is easy thanks to a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Peterborough

The nearest airports to Peterborough are Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport (YTZ) and Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ), which both offer regular flights to several international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Peterborough

The Greyhound Bus Station, located in downtown Peterborough, is serviced by inter-city buses, connecting the city with several major cities across Canada, including Toronto, Ottawa, and Montreal.

Peterborough by Road

Peterborough can be accessed via the 401 East, and the 115/35 highways connect the city to other major cities in Canada and the US.

Other Options

Travelers can also get to Peterborough by train, via the local VIA Rail Station, which offers daily services to Toronto and Ottawa.

How to Get Around in Peterborough

Getting around Peterborough is hassle-free and easy to manage. Public transportation is readily available and affordable, and the city can be explored on foot or bike. For those looking for more privacy, taxis, and ride-sharing options are also in abundance.

Where to Go Next from Peterborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peterborough makes for an ideal starting point for visitors wanting to explore wider areas of Ontario. Popular destinations within a few hours’ drive from Peterborough include:

  • Algonquin Provincial Park
  • Kawartha Lakes
  • Prince Edward County
  • The Blue Mountains

Conclusion

Peterborough is a city that is truly unique in its approach to the environment, culture, and adventure. Its landscape is a wonder to behold, with numerous parks, reserves and gardens for visitors to enjoy. Whether you want to soak up the sun, hike the trails, or dine in one of the many fantastic restaurants in the city, Peterborough is a must-visit on your next trip to Canada. We hope our guide has been useful and that you have an unforgettable experience in Peterborough!
