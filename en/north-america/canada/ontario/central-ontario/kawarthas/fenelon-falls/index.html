Introduction to Fenelon Falls

Fenelon Falls, located in the Kawarthas in Central Ontario, Canada, is a picturesque destination surrounded by stunning natural beauty. The charming town is known for its serene Kawartha Lakes and tranquil canals. Visitors to Fenelon Falls can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, local food and beverages, and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Fenelon Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Explore Fenelon Falls’ rich history and cultural significance by visiting its museums and historical landmarks. Must-see attractions include the Fenelon Museum, where visitors can learn about the town’s past, the Maryboro Lodge, where you can get a glimpse of what life was like in the 1800s, and the Fenelon Falls Lock 34, an impressive engineering feat that has been in operation since 1871.

Natural Attractions of Fenelon Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fenelon Falls boasts an abundance of natural beauty, with its pristine lakes, serene canals, and lush forests. Visitors can take a scenic boat tour of the Trent-Severn Waterway, hike the beautiful trails of the Kawartha Highlands Provincial Park, go fishing for bass, walleye, and panfish in Balsam Lake, or simply relax and enjoy the stunning views of the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fenelon Falls

Fenelon Falls offers a range of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to soak in the natural beauty of the area. The Kawartha Trans Canada Trail, for example, is a 44-kilometre trail that takes hikers and bikers through forests, farmland, and wetlands. Another popular route is the Victoria Rail Trail, an 85-kilometre trail that follows a former railway line and offers stunning views of the Kawartha Lakes.

Best Things to Do in Fenelon Falls: Activities and Tours

Fenelon Falls offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups. Visitors can take a boat tour of the Trent-Severn Waterway, enjoy a round of golf at the Fenelon Falls Golf Club, go horseback riding, or take a guided fishing tour on Balsam Lake. For a unique experience, visitors can join the Fenelon Ghost Walk and explore the town’s haunted past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fenelon Falls

For a comprehensive experience of all that Fenelon Falls has to offer, we recommend a week-long itinerary that includes exploring the town’s museums and historical sites, enjoying outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing, taking scenic boat tours, and enjoying the local food and drink scene. Contact local tourism agencies to customize an itinerary to suit your interests and needs.

Food and Drink Scene of Fenelon Falls

Must-Try Dishes

Fenelon Falls offers a range of dining options that cater to different tastes and preferences. Must-try dishes include poutine from the Cow and Sow Eatery, butter tart squares from Sweet Bottoms Coffee, and artisanal pizzas from the Fenelon Station Gallery.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Fenelon Falls can enjoy a range of local beverages, including craft beers from the Fenelon Falls Brewing Co., award-winning wines from the Fenelon Falls Winery, and artisanal ciders from the Kawartha Country Wines.

Where to Stay in Fenelon Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fenelon Falls offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Options include hotels like the Fenelon Inn and Country Living Suites, hostels like the Sturgeon Lake Cottage and the Canal Pub &, and guesthouses like the Red Umbrella Inn and the Water’s Edge B&B.

What to Buy in Fenelon Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Fenelon Falls can take home a range of unique souvenirs and local products. Recommendations include handmade pottery and ceramics from the Purple Door Studio, artisanal soaps and body products from the Pur Spa, and locally-made maple syrup and honey from the Fenelon Fall Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Fenelon Falls

Fenelon Falls is known for its friendly locals, close-knit community, and rich history. Visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture by attending community events like the Fenelon Falls Country Living Show, shopping at local farmers markets, and engaging with local artisans and craftsmen.

Best Time to Visit Fenelon Falls

The best time to visit Fenelon Falls is during the summer months, between June and September, when the weather is warm and the town comes alive with festivals and events. Visitors can also enjoy the beauty of the fall foliage in October, and winter activities like ice fishing and skating from December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fenelon Falls

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife
  • Support the local economy by shopping at local markets and businesses
  • Embrace the town’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums and landmarks

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment
  • Vandalize or damage historical sites and landmarks
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Fenelon Falls

Fenelon Falls is a safe and peaceful town with low crime rates. Visitors are encouraged to practice common sense safety measures like locking their doors and windows and being aware of their surroundings. For emergencies, call 911.

Health in Fenelon Falls

Fenelon Falls is home to a range of health care providers, including doctors, dentists, and pharmacies. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them and to have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Fenelon Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fenelon Falls

The closest airport to Fenelon Falls is the Peterborough Airport, which is 50 kilometers away. The Toronto Pearson International Airport is a larger airport option, located about 2.5 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Fenelon Falls

The closest bus station to Fenelon Falls is the Greyhound Bus Station in Lindsay, which is about 25 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to get to Fenelon Falls from there.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Fenelon Falls by car, taking Highway 35 North from Lindsay or Highway 121 from Kinmount.

How to Get Around in Fenelon Falls

The best way to get around Fenelon Falls is on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or use local taxis and ride-sharing services.

Where to Go Next from Fenelon Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fenelon Falls is located in the Kawarthas, which is home to a range of nearby destinations and attractions. Recommendations include visiting the nearby towns of Bobcaygeon and Lindsay, exploring the pristine wilderness of the Kawartha Highlands Provincial Park, or taking a scenic drive along the Oak Ridges Moraine.

Conclusion

Fenelon Falls offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and exciting activities and experiences. With a range of options for outdoor adventures, historical exploration, and local food and beverages, Fenelon Falls is a must-visit destination in Ontario, Canada.
