Introduction to Central Ontario

Central Ontario, located in Canada’s most populous province, is a region that offers a range of exciting travel experiences. From the stunning natural landscapes of Algonquin Provincial Park to the vibrant cities of Toronto and Ottawa, there’s something for everyone in Central Ontario.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and where and what to eat in Central Ontario. We’ll also provide insights into the region’s cultural heritage, and offer practical tips on how to maximize your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Central Ontario

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Central Ontario is home to some of Canada’s most significant cultural sites. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • The Canadian Museum of History, located in Gatineau just across the river from Ottawa, is a must-visit for those interested in Canadian history.

  • National Gallery of Canada, situated in Ottawa, is an important art institution with a vast collection of Canadian and international art.

  • Canadian War Museum is a national museum that documents Canada’s military history from the first colonial conflicts to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Central Ontario: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Central Ontario boasts exceptional natural beauty with its forests, lakes, and rivers. Here are some of the most breathtaking outdoor destinations:

  • Algonquin Provincial Park is one of Canada’s most scenic wilderness destinations and is known for its hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and canoeing.

  • Georgian Bay Islands National Park sprawls over several islands in Georgian Bay and is an ideal location fora relaxing getaway.

  • Elora Gorge Conservation Area is a park known for its beautiful hiking trails, swimming, and tubing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Ontario

Central Ontario offers an abundance of hiking and biking trails where you can explore the region’s stunning landscapes and wildlife. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Central Ontario:

  • Bruce Trail stretches for 900 km along the Niagara Escarpment and offers endless views of Central Ontario’s natural beauty.

  • Trans-Canada Trail covers 24,000 km across Canada, with 550 km running through Central Ontario. The trail provides incredible hiking and biking opportunities.

  • Algonquin Park Interpretive Trail is a 1.5 km walking trail that provides an informative overview of the park’s ecology.

Best Things to Do in Central Ontario: Activities and Tours

Central Ontario prides itself on offering a wide range of entertaining and exciting activities for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • CN Tower EdgeWalk is a must-do activity for thrill-seekers. Buckle up and walk around the edge of the CN tower’s main pod for an unforgettable experience.

  • Winery Tours of Niagara offer visitors a chance to sample wines and learn the art of winemaking.

  • Ottawa Tulip Festival is a must-attend event when flowers cover Canada’s capital city’s streets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Ontario

Central Ontario is a vast area that requires careful planning to get the most out of your visit. Here is a comprehensive itinerary that will help you explore the region’s top destinations:

Day 1: Toronto and surroundings

Day 2: Niagara Falls

Day 3: Ottawa and Gatineau Park

Day 4: Muskoka

Day 5: Algonquin Provincial Park

Day 6: Georgian Bay Islands National Park

Day 7: Return to Toronto

Food and Drink Scene of Central Ontario

Must-Try Dishes

Central Ontario offers a diverse culinary scene that reflects the region’s cultural heritage. Some of the must-try dishes in Central Ontario include:

  • Butter Tarts, a sweet pastry with a rich filling of butter and syrup, are a local specialty in Central Ontario.

  • Barbecue at the Ribfest, which takes place in several cities, including Oshawa, Orillia, and Toronto.

  • Poutine, a Canadian staple, is a dish of fries, gravy, and cheese curds that originated in Quebec, but can be found all over Central Ontario.

Local Beverages

Central Ontario is renowned for its rich and diverse array of drinks. Here are some of the must-try local beverages in the region:

  • Craft Beer - The region is home to several microbreweries and craft beer establishments.

  • Ice Wine - Niagara is famous for its ice wines. It is a sweet dessert wine made by freezing the grapes before pressing, creating a concentrated juice that is rich in flavor.

Where to Stay in Central Ontario: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central Ontario offers a vast array of accommodation options. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Fairmont Royal York - a landmark hotel located in the heart of Toronto, offers luxury accommodation with a rich history.

  • Sleep Cheap Hostel, located in Orillia, is a budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable and secure accommodation.

  • The Black Horse Inn in Lion’s Head, offers classic and charming, quintessentially Canadian guesthouse accommodation with a hearty breakfast.

What to Buy in Central Ontario: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Central Ontario offers an array of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home as a memory of your visit. Here are some of the best things to buy in Central Ontario:

  • Lumberjack shirts - as a nod to the Canadian lumberjack history and heritage.

  • Maple syrup - a Canadian favorite made from tree sap, and produced in the region.

  • Handicrafts - such as souvenirs from the native artisan’s arts and crafts at the Woodland Cultural Centre.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Ontario

Central Ontario prides itself on its diverse culture rooted in the indigenous and colonial history. Visitors should respect the culture by:

  • Learning about and visiting cultural attractions and landmarks in the region.

  • Supporting local indigenous festivals and businesses.

Best Time to Visit Central Ontario

Central Ontario has something to offer all year round. For winter sports enthusiasts, the best time to visit is from December to February. For those who want to explore the outdoors, the best time to visit is from May to October. And for those who appreciate the beautiful fall colors, the best time is from September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Ontario

Do:

  • Respect nature and the environment by following the Leave No Trace principles.

  • Try to learn the French language to communicate better, especially if you’re traveling in the Quebec area in Gatineau.

  • Take advantage of the free activities offered by cities and events.

Don’t:

  • Litter and damage nature.

  • Use drones in parks without permits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Ontario

Central Ontario is a safe region to visit, but as with any travel destination, visitors should take necessary safety precautions, such as:

  • Using common sense when traveling alone and at night.

  • Being careful with personal belongings in public places.

  • Getting travel insurance in case of an emergency.

Health in Central Ontario

Visitors should consult their healthcare professional before traveling to Central Ontario. They should also be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations, as well as receiving the COVID-19 vaccine when available.

Getting to Central Ontario: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central Ontario is accessible by various means of transportation. Here are some of the best options:

  • By Air - Toronto Pearson International Airport is the main airport serving the region, followed by Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport.

  • By Bus - Greyhound and Ontario Northland are two main bus companies that serve the region.

  • By Car - Rental cars are available at major airports and cities.

How to Get Around in Central Ontario

Central Ontario has a well-developed transportation system that includes buses, trains, and ferries. Visitors can also take advantage of bike-sharing programs and rental cars to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Central Ontario: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Central Ontario opens parts of Canada to visitors who want to visit other nearby destinations. Here are some recommended places you can visit after Central Ontario:

  • Quebec City - is a historic French city famed for its stunning architecture and outstanding cuisine.

  • Montreal is perfect for its French influences, vibrant culture, and excellent restaurants and nightlife.

  • The Thousand Islands in the St. Lawrence River, offer scenic natural beauty and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Central Ontario offers an escape to its breathtaking natural beauty, a rich cultural heritage, exciting cities. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your visit and experience all that Central Ontario has to offer!
