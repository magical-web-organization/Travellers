Introduction to Huntsville

Located in Muskoka, Central Ontario, Huntsville is a charming town surrounded by scenic lakes and forests. Known as the Gateway to Algonquin Park, the town attracts outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers from all over the world. Huntsville offers a wonderful blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and a thriving local food and drink scene. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will discuss the best places to visit, things to do, local food and drink scene, and cultural customs of Huntsville, along with travel logistics and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Huntsville

Huntsville offers a plethora of tourist attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Huntsville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Muskoka Heritage Place: A living museum that showcases the history of pioneer life in Muskoka.
  • Algonquin Theatre: A performing arts centre that features concerts, plays, and other cultural events.
  • Group of Seven Outdoor Gallery: An open-air art exhibit that features murals inspired by the Group of Seven, Canadian landscape artists.
  • Muskoka Lakes Museum: A museum that displays the history and culture of Muskoka.

Natural Attractions of Huntsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Algonquin Provincial Park: A world-renowned wilderness park that offers hiking, camping, canoeing, and wildlife watching opportunities.
  • Arrowhead Provincial Park: A scenic park that boasts hiking trails, canoeing, a skating trail, and other outdoor activities.
  • Muskoka Lakes: A chain of lakes that offer numerous water activities like boating, fishing, swimming, and kayaking.
  • Huntsville Summit Centre: A recreational facility that offers ice-skating, hockey, curling, and other indoor sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntsville

Huntsville boasts many trails that offer excellent hiking and biking opportunities. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Rainbow Trail: A 3.6 km loop trail that offers scenic views of Hunter’s Bay.
  • Fairy Vista Trail: A 2.2 km trail that features stunning views of Fairy Lake and Muskoka River.
  • Limberlost Forest and Wildlife Reserve: A 70 km network of trails that offers hiking, biking, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing opportunities.
  • Algonquin Provincial Park: A vast wilderness park that offers over 2,000 km of hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Huntsville: Activities and Tours

In Huntsville, there is no shortage of activities and tours for travelers of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Huntsville:

  • Canoeing or kayaking in Algonquin Provincial Park
  • Golfing at one of Huntsville’s many golf courses
  • Enjoying a scenic boat tour of Lake Muskoka
  • Horseback riding through the forest and over scenic trails
  • Visiting one of Huntsville’s many farmers’ markets

Week-Long Itinerary for Huntsville

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week in Huntsville:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore downtown Huntsville, visit the Group of Seven Outdoor Gallery, and have lunch at one of the local restaurants.
Day 2 Hike the Rainbow Trail, have a picnic lunch at Arrowhead Provincial Park, and explore the Muskoka Heritage Place.
Day 3 Kayak or canoe in Algonquin Provincial Park, have lunch on one of the park’s beaches, and visit the Algonquin Visitor Centre.
Day 4 Take a scenic drive around Muskoka’s lakes, see the Bala Falls, and enjoy a sunset boat tour.
Day 5 Visit the Muskoka Lakes Museum, go fishing on one of the many lakes, and have lunch in Port Carling.
Day 6 Take a horseback riding tour through the forest, enjoy a picnic lunch, and visit one of the local golf courses.
Day 7 Visit the Huntsville Summit Centre, go ice-skating or play hockey, and have dinner at one of the local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Huntsville

Huntsville’s food and drink scene is a true reflection of the town’s culture and history. From fine dining restaurants to local food trucks, there is something for everyone in Huntsville.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Muskoka Cranberry Chutney: A sweet and savory relish made with cranberries, maple syrup, and herbs.
  • Pickerel: A freshwater fish that’s popular in Huntsville. It’s often served grilled with a side of vegetables.
  • Butter Tarts: A quintessential Canadian dessert that’s made with pastry and butter filling. Some variations include ingredients like raisins or pecans.

Local Beverages

  • Muskoka Brewery: A local brewery that produces a range of craft beers, including cream ales, stouts, and IPAs.
  • Wine: Huntsville is home to several local vineyards that produce a range of wines.

Where to Stay in Huntsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huntsville offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the best places to stay in Huntsville:

  • Delta Hotels by Marriott Huntsville: A premium hotel located in downtown Huntsville.
  • Hidden Valley Resort: A four-season resort that offers a range of outdoor activities and comfortable lodging.
  • Huntsville Inn: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and convenient access to downtown Huntsville.

What to Buy in Huntsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huntsville offers many unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family back home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Huntsville:

  • Muskoka Chair: A chair that’s known for its sturdy design and comfort. It’s a popular outdoor furniture item and a must-buy for visitors to Huntsville.
  • Maple Syrup: A classic Canadian export that’s made from the sap of maple trees. It’s a popular souvenir and can be found in most of Huntsville’s gift shops.
  • Local Artwork: Huntsville is home to many talented artists and galleries that offer a range of paintings, sculptures, and other artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntsville

Huntsville is a welcoming town that values community, environmental conservation, and outdoor pursuits. Local customs include respecting the natural environment, supporting local businesses, and engaging with the town’s rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors to Huntsville should also be aware of the town’s seasonal events, including the annual Winter Carnival, which showcases local arts and crafts, food, and activities.

Best Time to Visit Huntsville

The best time to visit Huntsville is during the summer months (June to September) when the weather is warm and all the outdoor activities and events are in full swing. However, Huntsville is a year-round destination and offers visitors a range of activities and events in all seasons. The fall foliage is spectacular, and the winter months offer skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huntsville

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and engage in sustainable tourism practices
  • Support local businesses and buy locally made products
  • Participate in local events and festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local customs or cultural heritage
  • Ignore safety advice and warnings from local authorities

Safety and Health

While Huntsville is a safe town, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Huntsville

  • When hiking or camping in Algonquin Provincial Park, always carry bear spray, and follow all safety guidelines.
  • Practice water safety while swimming, boating, or kayaking in the lakes and rivers.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in isolated areas after dark.

Health in Huntsville

  • Always bring a first-aid kit when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and sun protection when exploring outdoors.

Getting to Huntsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Huntsville is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Huntsville

  • Muskoka Airport: Located in Bracebridge, about 36 km south of Huntsville.

Bus Stations Near Huntsville

  • Huntsville Transit terminal: Located in downtown Huntsville at 7 John Street.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in Huntsville and surrounding areas.
  • Train: The nearest train station is in Washago, about 40 km south of Huntsville.

How to Get Around in Huntsville

Huntsville is a walkable town, and many of the downtown attractions and restaurants are within walking distance. Visitors can also bike or drive around the town.

Where to Go Next from Huntsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Huntsville’s location makes it an ideal basecamp for exploring nearby destinations like:

  • Parry Sound: A charming town located on Georgian Bay that offers stunning views, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.
  • Toronto: Canada’s largest city is only a 2-hour drive from Huntsville and offers a range of cultural, culinary, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Huntsville is a hidden gem in the heart of Ontario’s cottage country. With its combination of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local food scene, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers of all kinds. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Huntsville and explore all that the town has to offer.
