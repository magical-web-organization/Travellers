Introduction to Gravenhurst

Located in the heart of Muskoka in Central Ontario, Gravenhurst is a charming town with a rich history and natural beauty. Known as the “Gateway to Muskoka,” Gravenhurst is home to numerous cultural attractions, stunning outdoor adventures, and some of the best food and drink in Ontario. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or an action-packed vacation, Gravenhurst has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Gravenhurst

Here are some of the top attractions to see and explore in Gravenhurst:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Muskoka Steamships & Discovery Centre: A waterfront museum dedicated to the history of steamships in Muskoka, with antique boats and exhibits on steamship technology.
  • Bethune Memorial House National Historic Site: The birthplace of Dr. Norman Bethune, a Canadian physician who pioneered medical supplies and practices across the world.
  • Gravenhurst Opera House: An iconic Victorian-style theatre, built in 1901 that hosts plays, concerts, and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Gravenhurst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muskoka Bay: A scenic bay that offers beautiful views and a chance to kayak, paddleboard or relax with a picnic.
  • Muskoka Wharf: A bustling waterfront entertainment space with a boardwalk, beach, and multiple restaurants and shops.
  • Rotary Centennial Trail: A 7.5 km multi-use trail for hiking, cycling, and running that passes through some of the most scenic areas of Gravenhurst.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gravenhurst

Gravenhurst is an outdoor lover’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the must-visit trails:

  • Devil’s Gap Trail: This scenic trail covers 5 km and winds through a mature forest to a lookout with stunning views of Lake Muskoka.
  • Bracebridge Resource Management Centre: This easy trail system located 12 minutes south of Gravenhurst offers a range of trails suitable for kids, families or casual hikers.
  • Muskoka Bay Trail: This easy trail provides stunning views of Muskoka Bay with gentle boardwalks and benches along the way.

Best Things to Do in Gravenhurst: Activities and Tours

From water sports to cultural experiences, Gravenhurst offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Gravenhurst:

  • Boat Tours on Muskoka Lake: Embark on a cruise around Lake Muskoka with a guide, and enjoy sensational views of the area’s sparkling waters, towering cliffs, and magnificent cottages.
  • Segway Tours at Muskoka Bay Resort: Take a Segway tour of Muskoka Bay Resort’s beautiful trails and golf course.
  • Muskoka Brewery Tour: Enjoy a guided tour of Muskoka Brewery, Canada’s leading craft brewer, and learn about the fascinating history of beer-making.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gravenhurst

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long vacation in Gravenhurst:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Muskoka Bay and enjoy canoeing and paddle boarding.
Day 2 Spend a day at Muskoka Wharf exploring the shops and sites.
Day 3 Go hiking on Bracebridge Resource Management Centre trails.
Day 4 Board a steamship tour at Muskoka Steamships and Discovery Centre.
Day 5 Visit Rabbit’s Bay Beach to enjoy sun, sand, and surf.
Day 6 Enjoy a round of gold at Muskoka Bay Club.
Day 7 Relax at the Scandinave Spa for a rejuvenating spa experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Gravenhurst

Gravenhurst is a foodie’s paradise, offering a wide range of local and international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beer Battered Fish & Chips: A classic Ontario dish made with local Muskoka beer batter and cod.
  • Poutine: This Quebec-based dish is a staple in most Canadian towns with fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Butter Chicken: A flavorful Indian dish made with marinated chicken and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Muskoka Brewery: Try the brewery’s flagship beer - the Muskoka Cream Ale - along with other local beers.
  • Lake of Bays Brewing Company: Enjoy the area’s refreshing beer with a unique taste of hops and grains.

Where to Stay in Gravenhurst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gravenhurst offers various accommodation options suitable for different budgets and preferences. Here are some top choices:

  • Residence Inn by Marriott Gravenhurst Muskoka Wharf: This contemporary hotel offers guests well-equipped studio suites, an indoor pool, gym, and other excellent facilities.
  • Comfort Inn Gravenhurst: This affordable hotel offers clean, comfortable rooms in a central location, and is perfect for visitors on a budget.
  • The Wane Sheppard Memorial Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers simple yet well-maintained dormitory-style rooms in the heart of downtown Gravenhurst.

What to Buy in Gravenhurst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take back home? Here are some top picks:

  • Gravenhurst Farmers’ Market: The Farmer’s Market is open every Wednesday, selling handcrafted gifts, locally-grown fruits, vegetables, and delicious artisanal bread.
  • Muskoka Bay Clothing Company: Shop for stylish and comfortable clothing options made with locally sourced, high-quality materials.
  • Hundred Proof Muskoka: This distillery offers an exclusive range of premium spirits made from organic and natural ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Gravenhurst

Gravenhurst has a rich history and a friendly, welcoming culture. People here appreciate visitors, and it is customary to greet people warmly and politely. The community is very passionate about celebrating the area’s people and places, so don’t miss special events such as the Muskoka Music Festival in July.

Best Time to Visit Gravenhurst

Gravenhurst has something to offer throughout the year. However, the best time to visit the city is during the summer months, from June to August, when outdoor activities and events are in full swing. During the fall season, there are also many outdoor activities, including the beautiful fall colors festivals. Winter activities in Gravenhurst can include snowmobiling, skiing, and snowshoeing, and the Muskoka Ont & Family Fishing Day held annually in February is well worth attending.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gravenhurst

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts while visiting Gravenhurst:

Do:

  • Embrace the nature and get outdoor activities including fishing, hiking, and boat tours.
  • Explore the quaint shops and restaurants downtown.
  • Greet the locals with a smile and respect their customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas like beaches and walking trails.
  • Overlook the natural beauty of the area by staying indoors.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gravenhurst

Gravenhurst is generally a safe place, but it’s best to avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas at night. Lock your doors and windows if you’re staying in a secluded location, and take care while driving on narrow and winding roads. If you’re venturing into nature, ensure that you are well prepared and have enough supplies such as first aid kit, water, and snacks.

Health in Gravenhurst

There are several medical clinics and hospitals in Gravenhurst, in case of any medical emergencies. Ensure that you have adequate travel insurance and consult a doctor if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Gravenhurst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gravenhurst is easily accessible, located two hours north of Toronto. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Gravenhurst

  • Muskoka Airport, which is located 10 minutes from Gravenhurst, offers direct flights to various Canadian cities, including Toronto.

Bus Stations Near Gravenhurst

  • Ontario Northland Bus Services offers daily bus services from various cities, including Toronto, North Bay, and Sudbury, into Gravenhurst.

Other Options

  • Driving: Gravenhurst is easy to navigate by car or motorcycle. Take the Highway 400 or 11 and exit on the relevant exit at Gravenhurst.
  • Boat: Arrive in it style by docking your boat at Muskoka Wharf within the town.

How to Get Around Gravenhurst

Gravenhurst is a small town and is easily walkable. However, if you prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies available in the area. Public transportation options are limited to the Ontario Northland bus services, which runs throughout the town and surrounding cities.

Where to Go Next from Gravenhurst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Don’t miss out on exploring the other amazing nearby destinations around Gravenhurst:

  • Bracebridge: This neighboring town is known for its beautiful waterfalls, shopping, and restaurants located a few minutes south of GravenHurst.
  • Lake of Bays: A lake blessed with stunning natural beauty, surrounded by cottages and delicious restaurants, just a few miles away from Gravenhurst.
  • Algonquin Provincial Park: A paradise for hikers and nature lovers with abundant wildlife and pristine lakes located about an hour’s drive away.

Conclusion

Gravenhurst is an unforgettable destination with a rich culture and exquisite natural features. It’s a perfect vacation spot if you’re looking for adventure, a peaceful retreat, or a romantic getaway. Don’t forget to experience the must-try foods, meet the hospitable locals, and have the time of your life in this hidden gem of Central Ontario.
