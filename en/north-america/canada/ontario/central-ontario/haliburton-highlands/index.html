Introduction to Haliburton Highlands

Haliburton Highlands is a natural playground, located just two and a half hours north of Toronto. It is a region of stunning landscapes, with over 600 pristine lakes and countless trails to explore. From the quaint towns to the rolling hills, there is something for everyone here. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Haliburton Highlands is the perfect destination.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haliburton Highlands

Day 1: Arrival in Haliburton Highlands and check into your cozy cabin or resort. Day 2: Visit the Dorset Heritage Museum and enjoy a picnic lunch on the shores of Kawagama Lake. Day 3: Explore the Haliburton Sculpture Forest and Haliburton Highlands Museum. Day 4: Head to Algonquin Provincial Park for a scenic hike. Day 5: Take a day trip to the Minden Wild Water Preserve and enjoy a day of kayaking or canoeing. Day 6: Go on a brewery tour and tasting at Haliburton Highlands Brewing. Day 7: Relax and unwind at your accommodation or visit the Highlands Nordic Centre for some outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Haliburton Highlands

The best time to visit Haliburton Highlands is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and the lakes are perfect for swimming, kayaking, and canoeing. Fall (September-November) is also a beautiful time to visit when the leaves change color.
