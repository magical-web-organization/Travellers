Introduction to Windsor

Located in Essex County, Southwestern Ontario, Windsor is a city rich in history, culture, and scenic beauty. With its close proximity to Detroit, Michigan, the city is a melting pot of Canadian and American influences that make it a unique destination for travelers. From its many museums and historical sites to its natural outdoor attractions, Windsor offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Canadian Club Brand Centre: Famous for producing the premium Canadian Club whiskey, the Canadian Club Brand Centre showcases the brand’s heritage, process, and global reach through interactive exhibits and tastings.
  • Dieppe Gardens: Located along Windsor’s riverfront, Dieppe Gardens is a place of historical significance that commemorates the Canadian soldiers who lost their lives in the 1942 Dieppe Raid during World War II.
  • Old Walkerville Neighborhood: The first distillery in Canada, Walkerville is a charming and distinct neighborhood that is home to many landmarks, including Willistead Manor, Hiram Walker & Sons Distillery, and St. Mary’s Anglican Church.
  • Windsor’s Community Museum: Housed in a French Renaissance-style building, Windsor’s Community Museum has exhibits that showcase the city’s diverse history, including its French-Canadian roots and the Underground Railroad that helped American slaves escape to freedom in Canada.

Natural Attractions of Windsor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pelee Island: Situated 20 km off the southern coast of Windsor, Pelee Island is a beautiful oasis in the middle of Lake Erie that offers stunning views, wildlife, and nature trails.
  • Ojibway Nature Centre: Home to over 350 rare or endangered species, Ojibway Nature Centre is a unique nature preserve that spans 160 hectares and features diverse habitats such as forests, wetlands, and prairies.
  • Point Pelee National Park: Known as the southernmost point of mainland Canada, Point Pelee National Park is a nature lover’s paradise with its expansive marshes, beaches, and walking trails.
  • Little River Golf Course: Set against a pristine natural backdrop, Little River Golf Course is an 18-hole championship course that offers a challenging and scenic golfing experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor

Windsor has several hiking and biking trails that showcase the city’s beauty and offer a fun way to explore the city’s outdoor attractions. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Windsor River Trail: Following along Windsor’s scenic riverfront, the Windsor River Trail is a paved walkway that spans 5 km and offers stunning views of Detroit’s skyline and the Detroit River.
  • Little River Corridor Trail: The Little River Corridor Trail is a 5 km trail surrounded by natural beauty and features a mix of woodlands, streams, and prairies.
  • Ganatchio Trail: The Ganatchio Trail is a 4 km trail that runs through the Ganatchio Park and Kissinger Park. It follows the Little River and offers scenic views of Windsor’s natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Windsor: Activities and Tours

  • Windsor River Cruises: A great way to experience Windsor’s scenic riverfront, Windsor River Cruises offer guided tours of the Detroit River and its many attractions, including the Alexander Mackenzie Cairn and Fort Malden.
  • Windsor Symphony Orchestra: The Windsor Symphony Orchestra is an award-winning orchestra that offers a diverse range of performances, including classical, pop, and family-friendly concerts.
  • Caesars Windsor: As the largest casino resort in Canada, Caesars Windsor is a premium destination for gaming, entertainment, dining, and shopping.
  • Adventure Bay Family Water Park: This indoor water park is a popular attraction for families and offers a range of thrilling water slides, wave pools, and on-site dining options.

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish, Poutine is made with fries, cheese curds, and gravy. Windsor’s version of Poutine is unique and features toppings like pulled pork, onions, and mushrooms.
  • Tourtiere: A French-Canadian meat pie that is a staple of the Windsor cuisine. It is made with ground pork, beef, and potatoes and is a delicious comfort food.
  • Peameal Bacon: A type of Canadian bacon that is a staple of breakfast and brunch menus in Windsor.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: Windsor’s proximity to the US-Mexico border makes it a tequila hotspot with great places like Mezzo Ristorante Lounge and Ciboulette et Cie.
  • Craft Beer: Walkerville Brewery and Motor Craft Ales are two of Windsor’s top craft breweries, offering unique and flavorful beers that showcase Windsor’s history and culture.

Where to Stay in Windsor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Caesars Windsor: As Windsor’s premier casino resort, Caesars Windsor offers luxury accomodations, fine dining, and entertainment options.
  • Holiday Inn Hotel & Suites: Conveniently located in the heart of Windsor’s downtown district, the Holiday Inn Hotel & Suites is a comfortable and affordable hotel that offers modern amenities and easy access to Windsor’s top attractions.
  • Benmiller Inn & Spa: Located in the nearby town of Goderich, Ontario, Benmiller Inn & Spa is a charming retreat that offers cozy guest rooms, fine dining, and spa treatments.

What to Buy in Windsor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Canadian Club Whiskey: One of the top whiskey brands in Canada, Canadian Club is produced in Walker Distillery in Windsor, and their unique blend is available for purchase at the Canadian Club Brand Centre.
  • Local Produce: Windsor is known for its exceptional produce, especially their greenhouse-grown vegetables like tomatoes, peppers, and cucumbers.
  • Caesar Mix: Originated in Windsor, the Caesar is Canada’s favorite cocktail, and you can find the same mix that’s being used in the city at any local grocery store.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor

Windsor is a city that celebrates its multicultural heritage and prides itself on its friendly and welcoming nature. Being a border town, Windsor has a mix of both Canadian and American customs, and tipping is an accepted practice in restaurants, bars, and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Windsor

The best time to visit Windsor is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is pleasant, and many outdoor festivals and events are happening. However, most of Windsor’s top attractions are open year-round, so any time of the year is a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windsor

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Windsor is known for its unique and delicious cuisine that can’t be found anywhere else.
  • Plan your trip around the weather: Windsor is known for its unpredictable weather, so it’s important to check the forecast and pack accordingly.
  • Observe the Niagara River’s whirlpool phenomena: Windsor is in close proximity to Niagara Falls, one of the world’s top natural wonders, and witnessing Niagara River’s swirling waters is a beautiful and awe-inspiring sight.

Don’t:

  • Speeding: Windsor has strict laws against speeding, and violators face high penalties.
  • Jaywalking: Crossing the street anywhere other than at a designated intersection is considered jaywalking and is punishable by law.
  • Assuming the currency: Although Windsor is in Canada, many stores and businesses in Windsor accept US dollars, so it’s important to clarify the payment method before making a purchase.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windsor

Windsor is a safe city with a low crime rate and a strong police presence. However, travelers should still take basic precautions such as not leaving valuables in their cars and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Windsor

Windsor has excellent healthcare facilities and services. In case of a medical emergency, travelers can visit Windsor Regional Hospital’s two campuses in Windsor or Hotel-Dieu Grace Healthcare.

Getting to Windsor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Windsor

  • Windsor International Airport: Windsor’s primary airport is located 6 km southeast of downtown Windsor and offers flights to and from Toronto and seasonal destinations.

Bus Stations Near Windsor

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Windsor’s downtown core, the Greyhound Bus Station offers daily bus service to many Canadian and American destinations.

Other Options

  • Via Rail: Via Rail provides passenger rail services to Windsor from Toronto and other nearby cities.
  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available at all major nearby airports and throughout Windsor.

How to Get Around in Windsor

Windsor’s public transportation system consists of buses, and several taxi and car rental services are available for getting around the city. Many of Windsor’s top attractions are within walking distance of each other, making walking a popular option for travelers.

Where to Go Next from Windsor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Toronto: Canada’s largest city is a 3-hour drive from Windsor and offers endless possibilities for entertainment, shopping, dining, and culture.
  • Detroit: Just across the river from Windsor, Detroit is known for its rich history, unique architecture, and world-class museums and is only a 20-minute drive from Windsor.
  • Niagara Falls: One of the world’s top natural wonders, Niagara Falls is located a 3-hour drive from Windsor and offers breathtaking views, thrilling activities, and a bustling nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Windsor is a welcoming and diverse city that offers a unique blend of Canadian and American culture, breathtaking natural beauty, and rich history and heritage. Whether you’re looking to unwind with a round of golf or explore museums and historical sites, Windsor has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that Windsor has to offer.
