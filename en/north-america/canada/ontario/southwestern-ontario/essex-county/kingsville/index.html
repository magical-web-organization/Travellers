Introduction to Kingsville

Located in Essex County, in Southwestern Ontario, Kingsville is a charming town that offers a wide array of experiences. Known for its rich agricultural heritage, Kingsville is home to sprawling vineyards that produce some of the best wines in Ontario. The town is also filled with cultural and historical landmarks, picturesque nature trails and scenic views, and a diverse food scene that attracts visitors from around the world. In this comprehensive tour guide for Kingsville, we’ll be exploring everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Kingsville

Make the most of your trip to Kingsville by exploring these must-see attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for new culinary experiences, Kingsville has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kingsville is rich in history and culture, and there are several landmarks and museums where you can learn more about the town’s past. Here are some of the most popular cultural attractions in Kingsville:

  • The Kingsville Historical Park: This park is home to several buildings that offer a glimpse into the region’s agricultural and maritime past. The park features a variety of exhibits and artifacts that showcase the town’s history, including a log cabin, a one-room schoolhouse, and a vintage fire truck.
  • The Carnegie Arts & Visitor Centre: This beautiful building was originally built in 1903 as a library and has since been converted into an arts centre and visitor information centre. Here you can admire the building’s stunning architecture, learn about the town’s art community, and attend cultural events throughout the year.
  • The Colasanti’s Tropical Gardens: This family-owned attraction is more than just a garden. It features animal exhibits, mini-golf, an arcade, and a restaurant. Stroll through the tropical gardens and learn about exotic plants, rare animals, and the history of the Colasanti family.

Natural Attractions of Kingsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, you’ll be spoiled for choice in Kingsville. Here are some natural attractions that you won’t want to miss:

  • Point Pelee National Park: Located just outside of Kingsville, Point Pelee is a paradise for nature lovers. This national park boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna, and is a major stopover for migratory birds. Enjoy hiking, biking, canoeing, and other outdoor activities in this stunning natural enclave.
  • Cedar Beach Conservation Area: This area is a popular spot for fishing, bird-watching, and even stargazing. Relax on the beach, explore the natural wetlands, or hike through the woods for an unforgettable day amid nature.
  • Hillman Marsh Conservation Area: This wetland park is perfect for bird-watching, fishing, boating, and paddle-boarding. Take a stroll on the boardwalks and enjoy the serenity that comes with being surrounded by nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingsville

Kingsville is home to several scenic hiking and biking trails, which offer the perfect opportunity to explore the stunning natural surroundings. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Kingsville:

  • Chrysler Canada Greenway Trail: This 50-kilometre trail was once a rail line but has since been converted into a multi-use trail that is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail offers beautiful views of the region’s natural beauty and is accessible from several points in Kingsville.
  • Hillman Marsh Trail: This trail is located in the Hillman Marsh Conservation Area and surrounds the stunning wetland area. The trail is approximately 3.5 kilometres long and is perfect for hikers and bird watchers.
  • Cedar Beach Trail: This trail is a combination of asphalt and boardwalk. It offers a mix of landscapes, including the beach, the woods, and wetlands. This trail is roughly 3 kilometres long and is perfect for family outings.

Best Things to Do in Kingsville: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or as a family, Kingsville has plenty of activities to keep you busy. From wine tours to water sports, here are some of the best things to do in Kingsville:

  • Wine and Brewery Tours: Kingsville is famous for its wineries and breweries, and there are several winery and brewery tours that you can take to learn more about the local alcohol industry. Popular wineries and breweries include Pelee Island Winery, Sprucewood Shores Estate Winery, Banded Goose Brewing, and Grove Brew House.
  • Water Sports: Located on the shore of Lake Erie, Kingsville is the perfect destination for water sports enthusiasts. You can rent kayaks, canoes, paddle boards, and even jet-skis at several locations throughout town.
  • Fishing and Boating: Kingsville is a paradise for anglers, and there are several opportunities for year-round fishing in the area. Rent a boat, and spend the day on the water catching some of the region’s famous fishspecies including perch, bass, trout, weedless pike, and channel catfish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kingsville

With so much to see and do in Kingsville, planning your itinerary can be overwhelming. Here’s a week-long itinerary that will guarantee you get the most out of your visit to Kingsville:

  • Day 1: Explore Kingsville’s downtown area and get a taste of the local food scene. Check out Jack’s Gastropub, Mettawas Station, and Family Kitchen for the best local eats.
  • Day 2: Take a wine or brewery tour and bask in the area’s viticulture bounty.
  • Day 3: Visit the Kingsville Historical Park and enjoy the area’s architecture
  • Day 4: Explore the nature reserves and parks in and around Kingsville including Point Pelee National Park and Cedar Beach Conservation Area.
  • Day 5: Take a hiking or biking tour and experience Kingsville’s stunning natural beauty.
  • Day 6: Go fishing and boating or participate in a water sport like paddleboarding on Lake Erie
  • Day 7: Spend the day at Colasanti’s Tropical Gardens or Cedar Island Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Kingsville

Kingsville offers a diverse food scene that ranges from quaint cafés to upscale restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogis: These Eastern European dumplings are a local favourite. You can find them at several locations throughout town, but Smashed Apple Gastropub is a must-visit spot for pierogi lovers.
  • Perch Sandwich: Kingsville is famous for its perch sandwiches. Made with local White Perch, this sandwich promises an unforgettable combination of flaky fish, buttery bread, and a secret sauce that locals rave about. Try them at the Lakeside Park Pavilion or at Pelee Island Winery.
  • Cherry Cheesecake: Cherry cheesecake is a dessert that Kingsville residents are very fond of. Visit Lakeside Bakery and Chorizo Fresh Mex for an indulging experience.

Local Beverages

  • Sprucewood Shores Estate Winery: This winery specializes in red wines and offers a stunning backdrop for afternoon tastings. Visit their tasting room or sit on their outdoor patio and enjoy the sunsets with your glass of wine.
  • Grove Brew House: Enjoy a pint of craft beer at this popular brewery. Highly recommended brews include the Hab & Deb’s IPA and Grover’s Cream Ale.
  • Cooper’s Hawk Vineyards: This winery offers an expansive tasting room that overlooks a sprawling vineyard. They specialize in ice wines and have a wide range of appetizers and cheeseboards to pair with their wines.

Where to Stay in Kingsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kingsville offers a decent range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the town’s best places to stay:

  • Grove Hotel: Located in the heart of downtown Kingsville, Grove Hotel offers comfortable and affordable accommodations. The hotel is steps away from several local eateries and attractions.
  • Inn31: This unique hotel provides a cozy and comfortable atmosphere in downtown Kingsville, with themed rooms featuring six romantic themes, ranging from Parisian to Nature. -Sears B&B: This bed and breakfast is located in a stunning heritage home with all the amenities needed for a wholesome stay.

What to Buy in Kingsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kingsville offers several shops and stores where you can pick up souvenirs and locally made products. Check out these places during your visit:

  • Jack’s Everything Store: Located in the heart of downtown Kingsville, Jack’s Everything Store offers a collection of unusual and unique artisan products, ranging from handcrafted jewelry to locally made soaps and lotions.
  • Kingsville Farmers Market: The Kingsville Farmers Market is a great place to buy fresh, local produce and artisanal products. You can find everything from savory cheeses to fresh fruits, and locally made pastries.
  • Joycorn Popcorn: Located in nearby Leamington district, Joycorn Popcorn has been producing flavors like Bacon Cheddar, Peanut Butter, and Cheesy Jalapeno (to name a few) for over 50 years!

Local Culture and Customs of Kingsville

Kingsville is a welcoming and friendly town, and visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your visit:

  • Be respectful of the town’s heritage and culture when visiting public places of worship and cultural landmarks.
  • Always say ‘hello’ and ‘thank you’ to locals for a friendly reception.
  • Politeness and friendliness towards other visitors is highly appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Kingsville

The best time to visit Kingsville is during the summer months when the climate is warmer and several outdoor activities like boating, picnicking, and biking, are at their prime. The town can become busy during summer, so a good alternative is visiting during fall or spring when the crowds dissipate, and lodging prices are more affordable. Plus, it will still be as picturesque and charming beyond the summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingsville

Here are some general guidelines to help you make the most of your visit to Kingsville:

Do:

  • Explore the town’s natural and cultural landmarks
  • Try the local food and wines
  • Rent a kayak or paddleboard to enjoy Lake Erie
  • Book your accommodation in advance

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitats or landmarks
  • Underestimate the power of the sun during summer; always carry sunscreen and stay hydrated
  • Sit around in your hotel room all day when there are so many attractions, activities, and sights awaiting.

Safety and Health

Kingsville is a relatively safe town regarding violent crimes, but as with all outdoor destinations, there are some general guidelines that visitors should follow to ensure they have a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Kingsville

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring at night
  • Don’t leave your valuables and other items unattended
  • Steer clear of any suspicious activity or unsavoury characters

Health in Kingsville

  • Drinking water from taps in Kingsville is safe. Read the latest updates regarding the water safety from the city website before leaving.
  • Carrying an EpiPen while visiting Kingsville or any outdoor destination is always recommended for individuals with a history of allergic reactions.

Getting to Kingsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Kingsville is easy with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Kingsville

The Windsor International Airport is the closest international airport and is located approximately 45 minutes away from Kingsville.

Bus Stations Near Kingsville

Greyhound Canada has bus terminals in several nearby cities, including Windsor and Leamington, which are both about 35 minutes away.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Kingsville can be reached via several highways accessible from Detroit, London, and Toronto, and several other transit services.

How to Get Around in Kingsville

Kingsville can easily be explored on foot due to its compact size, but if you want to travel farther, car rentals and cabs are your best options.

Where to Go Next from Kingsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kingsville is located in the heart of Southwestern Ontario, putting several other towns and cities within driving distance. Here are some destinations that you may want to consider visiting after your Kingsville trip:

  • Detroit: Take a quick trip across the border and experience Motor City food, art, culture and sporting events.
  • Leamington: Known for bird watching and its natural reserve, Leamington is approximately 30 minutes away from Kingsville and is perfect for a day trip.
  • Windsor-Essex County: Spend the day wine-tasting, visiting galleries and discovering cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Kingsville is a hidden gem in Southwestern Ontario that boasts a charming downtown area, a natural beauty, rich cultural experiences, and a diverse food scene. Take your time to explore the region’s history and culture as well as indulge in the natural environment. The above guide will be a great beginning to help you plan your itinerary and have a memorable trip.
