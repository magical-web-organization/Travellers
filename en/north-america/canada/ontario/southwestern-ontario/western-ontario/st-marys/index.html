Introduction to St. Marys

St. Marys is a small town located in southwestern Ontario, Canada. Nestled in the heart of Perth County, the town is known for its rich history, picturesque landscapes, and friendly locals. St. Marys is best explored on foot, with plenty of scenic walking trails and hiking routes that allow visitors to fully appreciate the town’s beauty.

List of Places to Visit in St. Marys

Whether you’re a history buff, art lover, foodie, or nature enthusiast, St. Marys offers an impressive range of attractions that are sure to captivate visitors. Here are some of the town’s must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Marys Museum: Housed in a restored 1860s stone building, this museum tells the story of St. Marys, from its First Nations roots to the present day.
  • Canadian Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum: This museum celebrates Canada’s rich baseball history, including its contributions to the sport at an international level.
  • Blenheim House: This stunning Gothic Revival mansion offers guided tours that take visitors through its rich history.

Natural Attractions of St. Marys: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wildwood Conservation Area: Spanning over 400 hectares, this conservation area includes hiking and biking trails, swimming beaches, and picnic areas.
  • Lindenow Glen: This beautiful glen provides a tranquil natural oasis, complete with limestone cliffs, streams, and diverse wildlife.
  • St. Marys Quarry: This former limestone quarry has been converted into a recreational area, with swimming, scuba diving, and other water activities available in the clear blue waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Marys

  • Maitland Trail: This 49-kilometer trail extends from Goderich to Auburn, with sections in St. Marys providing stunning views of the Thames River and surrounding countryside.
  • Ski Hill Park: This park offers hiking trails that lead through the St. Marys Quarry and into the town’s wilderness, offering a mix of natural beauty and historical landmarks.
  • Iron Horse Trail: Named after the railway that once ran through the area, this trail passes through St. Marys, connecting several trails and waterways.

Best Things to Do in St. Marys: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Walks: Get a spooky tour of St. Marys’ historic buildings and landmarks, while learning about the town’s haunted history.
  • Paddle the Thames: Kayak or canoe down the Thames River, passing through stunning natural landscapes and historic sites.
  • Chocolate Trail: Experience St. Marys’ sweet side with a tour of the town’s chocolate shops, including sampling locally-made treats.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Marys

Day 1: Explore the St. Marys Museum, followed by a stroll along the Thames River. Day 2: Hike the Maitland Trail or Iron Horse Trail. Day 3: Discover St. Marys’ historic Blenheim House and Lindenow Glen. Day 4: Experience the Canadian Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum, followed by a visit to the St. Marys Farmers’ Market. Day 5: Paddle the Thames River or take a ghost walk through St. Marys. Day 6: Visit the Wildwood Conservation Area for kayaking, hiking, or swimming. Day 7: Take a tour of St. Marys’ chocolate shops, followed by a relaxing picnic in the park.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Marys

No trip to St. Marys is complete without sampling the town’s delicious cuisine. Here are some local dishes and beverages that you can’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Perth County Pork: This locally-raised pork is famous throughout the region for its tender meat and hearty flavor.
  • St. Marys Althletic Club Burger: A classic cheeseburger that’s a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Butter Tarts: Small, sweet, and buttery, these tarts are a Canadian classic and are widely available in St. Marys.

Local Beverages

  • Stonetown Artisan Cheese: This local cheesemaker offers a range of award-winning cheeses, including some made with beer.
  • St. Marys Brewery: A small craft brewery that produces a range of beers, including the popular Holy Molé Stout.
  • Brick Brewing Co.: While not located in St. Marys itself, this brewery in nearby Kitchener offers a range of iconic local beers, including the Waterloo Dark.

Where to Stay in St. Marys: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Marys offers a range of accommodations for visitors, no matter what their budget or preference. Here are some recommended options:

  • The Westover Inn: A historic inn located in nearby St. Marys, offering luxurious accommodations and stunning grounds.
  • Stone Willow Inn: This cozy inn offers spacious, comfortable rooms and a charming, welcoming atmosphere.
  • Trout Hollow Lodge: This rustic lodge is perfect for those seeking a more secluded, natural retreat, with easy access to hiking trails and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in St. Marys: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Products: From maple syrup to maple candy, St. Marys is home to a range of delicious maple treats.
  • Handmade Crafts: St. Marys has a vibrant crafts scene, with local artisans producing everything from pottery to jewelry and more.
  • St. Marys Cement: While not exactly a “souvenir”, St. Marys Cement is known worldwide for its high quality and is a unique local product you won’t find anywhere else.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Marys

St. Marys is known for its friendly, welcoming locals who are always happy to share their town with visitors. The town also has a strong sense of community spirit, which is reflected in its vibrant local events, ranging from farmers’ markets to art festivals and more.

Best Time to Visit St. Marys

The best time to visit St. Marys depends on your personal preferences. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm temperatures ideal for outdoor activities and plenty of local events. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the changing colors of the leaves creating a stunning backdrop. Winter is less popular but can be a great time to experience the town’s cozy, festive atmosphere, with holiday events and warm drinks to keep you feeling merry.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Marys

Do:

  • Do explore the town on foot, taking advantage of the many scenic walking trails and paths.
  • Do respect the town’s history and culture, including its First Nations heritage.
  • Do take time to chat with the locals, who are known for their hospitality and friendliness.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural landscapes, which are a precious resource for the community.
  • Don’t make assumptions about the town’s residents based on stereotypes or preconceptions.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor adventures, which can range from hiking to kayaking and beyond.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Marys

St. Marys is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always take standard precautions, such as locking doors and securing valuables.

Health in St. Marys

St. Marys offers a range of healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should also pay attention to the town’s environmental risks, such as ticks during the summer months.

Getting to St. Marys: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Marys is easily accessible by car from nearby cities such as Toronto, London, and Kitchener. For visitors coming from farther afield, there are several transportation options to consider.

Airports Near St. Marys

  • London International Airport: The closest major airport to St. Marys, located approximately 50 kilometers away.
  • Toronto Pearson International Airport: Located approximately 150 kilometers away, Toronto’s international airport provides access to a range of domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near St. Marys

  • Greyhound: Greyhound provides bus services to nearby London and Kitchener.
  • VIA Rail: VIA Rail provides train services to nearby Stratford, which is a short drive or taxi ride from St. Marys.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are widely available in nearby cities, offering visitors the flexibility to explore St. Marys and its surrounding areas at their own pace.
  • Taxi or Ride Sharing: Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber are also available in the area, offering convenient transportation options for visitors.

How to Get Around in St. Marys

As mentioned earlier, St. Marys is best explored on foot, with many natural attractions and cultural landmarks within walking distance of the town center. Some attractions may require a car or other transportation, such as the Wildwood Conservation Area or Blenheim House. Visitors may also consider renting bikes or taking taxis for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from St. Marys: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stratford: This charming town is home to the famous Stratford Festival, as well as plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • London: A bustling city with a rich history and vibrant arts scene, including the Museum London and London Fringe Theatre Festival.
  • Grand Bend: A popular summer getaway spot, known for its sandy beaches, boating, and nightlife.

Conclusion

St. Marys is a hidden gem in southwestern Ontario, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, St. Marys is a town that is sure to capture your heart. With so much to see and do, you’ll want to extend your trip to fully appreciate all this charming Canadian town has to offer.
