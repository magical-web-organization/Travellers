Introduction to Bruce County

Nestled in southwestern Ontario, Bruce County is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and welcoming local culture, it’s no surprise that it’s becoming one of the most popular destinations for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Bruce County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bruce County Museum and Cultural Centre – Explore the history of Bruce County with interactive exhibits and engaging displays.
  • Southampton Art School and Gallery – Discover the works of local artists and participate in workshops and programs.
  • Chantry Island – Take a guided tour of this National Historic Site and the Chantry Island Lighthouse.

Natural Attractions of Bruce County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bruce Peninsula National Park – Hike the famous Bruce Trail, explore the historic cave systems or swim in the crystal clear waters of Georgian Bay.
  • Sauble Beach (South Bruce Peninsula) – Relax on this sandy beach, which is one of the longest freshwater beaches in the world.
  • MacGregor Point Provincial Park – Enjoy stunning views of Lake Huron and explore the park’s extensive trail system.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bruce County

Bruce County is a hiker’s paradise with its extensive trail system, including the renowned Bruce Trail. With over 900 km of trails, there’s something for every skill level. Some popular routes include:

  • Malcolm Bluff Trail
  • Skinner’s Bluff Trail
  • Lion’s Head Loop
  • The Grotto Trail

Best Things to Do in Bruce County: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there is no shortage of things to do in Bruce County. Some must-try activities and tours include:

  • Bruce County Brews Cruise – Sample local beer, cider, and wine, while learning about the history and culture of Bruce County.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing on the Saugeen River – Experience the beauty of Bruce County from a new perspective.
  • ATV Adventure Tours – Explore the backcountry of Bruce County on an adrenaline-pumping ATV.
  • Bruce Trail Waterfall Tour – Discover the stunning waterfalls located along the Bruce Trail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bruce County

Day 1: Arrive in Bruce County and explore nearby attractions such as the Bruce County Museum and Cultural Centre.

Day 2-4: Hiking excursions along famous trails such as Malcolm Bluff Trail, Skinner’s Bluff Trail and The Grotto Trail.

Day 5: Relax on Sauble Beach, one of the longest freshwater beaches in the world, or enjoy kayaking and canoeing on the Saugeen River.

Day 6: Take a tour of Chantry Island or the Bruce County Brews Cruise.

Day 7: Visit MacGregor Point Provincial Park and enjoy the park’s extensive trail system.

Food and Drink Scene of Bruce County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pickerel – Try this fresh and delicious fish, which is a local specialty.
  • Butter Tart – A classic Canadian dessert you can’t miss.
  • Perch – Another tasty fish that is caught fresh from the waters of Lake Huron.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer – Check out the many craft breweries in the area, such as Arran Brewery or MacLean’s Ales.
  • Cider – Explore the local cider houses such as Coffin Ridge Winery and Spirit Tree Estate Cidery.

Where to Stay in Bruce County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great places to stay in Bruce County, ranging from luxurious waterfront resorts to cozy guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Cobble Beach Resort
  • Windspire Inn
  • Sunset Motel

What to Buy in Bruce County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery – Southampton Art School and Gallery has a wide selection of beautiful handmade pottery.
  • Craft Beer and Cider – Take home some of the best local brews from the area’s many craft breweries and cider houses.
  • Art – Visit local artists in their studios to purchase one-of-a-kind works of art.

Local Culture and Customs of Bruce County

Bruce County is known for its friendly and welcoming community, with a rich history dating back to the 1800s. There is a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of the area, and visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and the local way of life.

Best Time to Visit Bruce County

The best time to visit Bruce County is between May and October when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, each season has its unique beauty and attractions, such as skiing and snowboarding in the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bruce County

Do:

  • Respect the local way of life and take time to learn about the rich history of the area.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Bruce County by hiking, biking, or kayaking.
  • Support local businesses and artisans by purchasing handmade pottery and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage or litter in natural areas.
  • Disturb wildlife or the ecosystem.
  • Ignore safety regulations when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bruce County

Bruce County is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling in unfamiliar areas. Always lock your car and store valuables in a safe place.

Health in Bruce County

Medical facilities are available in major towns and cities in Bruce County. It is recommended that visitors purchase travel insurance before arriving in the area.

Getting to Bruce County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bruce County

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport is approximately 2-3 hours from Bruce County.
  • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport is approximately 2-3 hours from Bruce County.

Bus Stations Near Bruce County

  • Greyhound Canada offers service to Owen Sound, which is located in Bruce County.

Other Options

  • Driving – Bruce County is easily accessible by car, with the famous Highway 6 running directly through the area.

How to Get Around in Bruce County

The best way to get around Bruce County is by car, as many of the natural attractions and hiking trails are located outside of major towns. However, there are also local transit options and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Bruce County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manitoulin Island – Explore the world’s largest freshwater island, located just off the east coast of Bruce County.
  • Georgian Bay Islands National Park – Head north to this stunning national park, located on the eastern coast of Georgian Bay.

Conclusion

Bruce County is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered, offering a diversity of natural beauty and cultural experiences. With this guide, you’re well-equipped to plan the perfect trip to this stunning part of Ontario.
