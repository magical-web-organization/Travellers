Introduction to Aylmer

Situated in the Lake Erie Shore region of Southwestern Ontario, Aylmer is a captivating small town in the heart of Elgin County. With an area of 2.1 km², Aylmer’s population of 7,492 has been growing steadily over the years, making it a popular destination for tourists from across Canada and beyond.

Settled in the early 1800s, Aylmer boasts a rich history and culture that appeals to visitors looking for an authentic Canadian experience. The town is known for its scenic parks, picturesque hiking and biking trails, and unique cultural events. Here is a comprehensive tour guide to Aylmer, highlighting the top things to do, where to eat, play, and stay.

List of Places to Visit in Aylmer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aylmer-Malahide Museum & Archives: This museum features historical exhibits and artifacts, including early pioneer memorabilia, antique farm tools, and military history items.
  • Clovermead Adventure Farm: A unique family-friendly destination that offers animal interactions, nature trails, a giant jumping pillow, and honey bee demonstrations.

Natural Attractions of Aylmer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spicer Trail and Arboretum: The Spicer Trail is one of the town’s most popular natural attractions, featuring acres of trees, plants, and wildlife, making it the perfect place to go bird-watching and exploring the outdoors.
  • Catfish Creek Conservation Area: This conservation area features a natural amphitheater and stunning views of Catfish Creek, making it an ideal location for picnics or just taking in the incredible scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aylmer

Hiking Trails:

  • Catfish Creek Trail: This 45 km trail features many scenic views, wildlife sightings, and historic landmarks, such as the Old Mill and limestone buildings.
  • Backus-Page House Trail: Perfect for nature lovers, this 3 km trail leads you through the lush forests and gently rolling hills around the historic Backus-Page House.

Biking Trails:

  • The Gatner-Talbot Trail: This 50 km trail runs from St. Thomas to Port Stanley along an abandoned railroad line, making it the perfect location for a day or afternoon ride.
  • The Millennium Trail: This 22 km trail offers breathtaking scenery, making it a popular destination for biking enthusiasts of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Aylmer: Activities and Tours

  • Aylmer Farmers’ Market: Experience Aylmer’s agriculture scene and discover locally sourced produce, artisanal goods, and homemade treats every Saturday morning from 9 am - 1 pm.
  • Port Stanley Terminal Rail: Take a charming train ride along the historic CPR tracks between Port Stanley and St. Thomas. The scenic journey is perfect for sightseeing and soaking up the sights and sounds of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aylmer

Day 1: Arrive in Aylmer and explore the town’s historical landmarks and museums.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour of Aylmer’s natural trails and conservation areas.

Day 3: Head to the Aylmer Farmers’ Market for a taste of local produce and craft goods, then visit a nearby winery for a wine tasting experience.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby St. Thomas and visit the St. Thomas Elevated Park, a former railway turned green space.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of adventure at Clovermead Adventure Farm, featuring a petting zoo, beehive demonstrations, and nature trails.

Day 6: Visit Port Stanley and take the Port Stanley Terminal Rail train ride along the scenic countryside.

Day 7: Explore Aylmer’s local stores and farmers’ markets before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Aylmer

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Buttertarts: A sweet and flaky dessert tart filled with buttery goodness.
  • Perogies: A traditional Ukrainian dish made with boiled dough, stuffed with potato or meat filling.

Local Beverages:

  • Elgin County Wine: Discover locally produced wine in the town’s vineyards.
  • Aylmer brewed Beers: Enjoy locally brewed beer at any of the town’s restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Aylmer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • De Vere House: A beautiful bed and breakfast situated in the heart of Elgin County, featuring comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • The Windmill Country Inn: A stunning country property offering cozy rooms and a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Aylmer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Discover local products like beeswax candles, maple syrup, handmade soaps, and artisanal jams at any of Aylmer’s farmers’ markets and local stores, including Aylmer Artisans and Gailer’s Fine Clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Aylmer

Aylmer has a rich cultural history, including a large Mennonite population and a thriving farming industry. Visitors can immerse themselves in local festivals and traditions, such as the Yuletide Tour of Homes, summer agricultural fairs, and the Elgin County Railway Museum.

Best Time to Visit Aylmer

The best time to visit Aylmer is between May and September, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aylmer

Do:

  • Do bring comfortable shoes for walking tours and outdoor excursions.
  • Do respect the town’s Mennonite culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in parks and public spaces.
  • Don’t access private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aylmer

Aylmer is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Aylmer

Healthcare facilities in Aylmer are readily available - including ER, general practice representatives, and outpatient centers. Visitors must ensure that they have adequate health coverage before visiting the town, as medical bills can be high.

Getting to Aylmer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aylmer

Aylmer is within driving distance of the following airports:

  • London International Airport (37 km)
  • Windsor International Airport (159 km)
  • Toronto Pearson International Airport (193 km)

Bus Stations Near Aylmer

Greyhound provides bus service to Aylmer from London, ON.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient transportation option when traveling to Aylmer.

How to Get Around in Aylmer

Aylmer is a small town that is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Aylmer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations that visitors can explore include:

  • London, ON: The city is known for its bustling nightlife, arts scene, and historical landmarks. It is a half hour drive from Aylmer.
  • Port Stanley: The charming village is known for its sandy beaches along Lake Erie’s shoreline. It is a 20-minute drive from Aylmer.

Conclusion

Aylmer is a charming small town in Southwestern Ontario that has something to offer everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to foodies and history buffs. With its scenic hiking trails, historic landmarks and museums, and farm-to-table culinary scene, Aylmer is an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.
