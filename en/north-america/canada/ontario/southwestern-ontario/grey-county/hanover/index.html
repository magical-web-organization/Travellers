Introduction to Hanover

Hanover is a charming town located in Grey County, Southwestern Ontario, Canada. It is a small town with a population of approximately 7000, known for its rich history, friendly folks, and scenic beauty. Whether you want to embrace outdoor adventures, explore history, or just relax, Hanover has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Hanover

Discover the top places to visit in Hanover and take in the town’s rich history and natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Hanover Heritage Square

Visiting Hanover Heritage Square is like taking a step back in time, as it features historical buildings and architecture. The Square is home to the Hanover Civic Theatre, which offers stage shows and movies throughout the year. Other buildings to check out include the Old Railway Station, which has been restored and houses a museum featuring artifacts from Hanover’s railway era, and the Clock Tower, which is a symbol of the town.

2. Hanover Public Library

The Hanover Public Library is a hub of information for residents and visitors alike. It’s home to over 30,000 resources, including books, magazines, DVDs, and more. The library also hosts events and activities throughout the year, such as guest speakers, book clubs, and children’s storytime.

Natural Attractions of Hanover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Saugeen River

The Saugeen River flows through Hanover and is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can canoe, kayak, or fish on the river, or simply take a leisurely stroll along its banks. The river is also home to a variety of wildlife, including bald eagles, otters, and beavers.

2. Hanover Trails

Hanover has several trails for hiking and biking, including the Hanover-Walkerton Rail Trail and the Heritage Walking Tour. The Hanover-Walkerton Rail Trail is a 25-kilometre trail that passes through beautiful countryside, small towns, and historic sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanover

Hanover boasts some fantastic hiking and biking routes. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced hiker, you’ll find trails that are perfect for you.

1. Hanover-Walkerton Rail Trail

The Hanover-Walkerton Rail Trail is a 25-kilometre trail that follows the old CN railway line through the countryside. The trail has a gravel surface and is ideal for walking, cycling, and horseback riding. It’s easily accessible and well-maintained, making it perfect for families and beginners.

2. Heritage Walking Tour

The Heritage Walking Tour is a self-guided tour that takes visitors through Hanover’s historic downtown. Along the way, visitors can admire the town’s unique architecture and learn about its rich history.

Best Things to Do in Hanover: Activities and Tours

Experience the best of what Hanover has to offer by trying any of these things to do, activities, and tours.

1. Hanover Drive-In Theatre

The Hanover Drive-In Theatre is a must-visit attraction for movie lovers. The theatre is open from May to October and features a double-feature on most nights. Visitors can relax under the stars while watching a movie on the big screen.

2. Hanover Raceway

Hanover Raceway is a harness racing track that hosts races throughout the season. Visitors can enjoy a day at the races and place bets on their favourite horses.

Food and Drink Scene of Hanover

Hanover has a diverse food and drink scene. Whether you want to try local delicacies or international cuisine, you’ll find plenty of options in Hanover.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Butter Tarts

Butter tarts are a Canadian classic, and you’ll find them in most bakeries in Hanover. These sweet treats have a flaky pastry shell filled with a creamy mixture of sugar, butter, and eggs.

2. Perogies

Perogies are a traditional Eastern European dish that’s popular in Hanover. These dumplings are filled with potatoes, cheese, and other fillings and served with sour cream and onions.

Local Beverages

1. Beer

Hanover is home to several microbreweries that produce high-quality beer. Some of the top breweries to visit include MacLean’s Ales, Neustadt Springs Brewery, and the Hanover Brewing Company.

2. Cider

Cider lovers will enjoy visiting Duxbury Cider. This family-owned business produces small-batch cider using local apples and traditional methods.

Where to Stay in Hanover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hanover has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

1. Spa Bed & Breakfast

The Spa Bed & Breakfast is a cosy guesthouse that offers a relaxing stay. The rooms are well-equipped, and there’s a sauna and Jacuzzi on-site. Guests can enjoy a healthy breakfast each morning.

2. Best Western Plus Walkerton Hotel & Conference Centre

The Best Western Plus Walkerton Hotel & Conference Centre is a modern hotel that offers comfortable and well-appointed rooms. The hotel has an indoor pool, fitness centre, and restaurant, making it a great choice for families.

What to Buy in Hanover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Hanover home with you by purchasing these unique souvenirs and local products:

1. Apple Cider Vinegar

Duxbury Cider produces a high-quality apple cider vinegar that’s perfect for salad dressings and marinades. It’s made using local apples and traditional methods.

2. Handmade Soap

Huron Ridge Acres produces handmade soap using natural ingredients. These soaps are gentle on the skin and come in a variety of scents.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanover

Hanover is a friendly town with a strong sense of community. Locals are proud of their town’s history and natural beauty and are always happy to share their knowledge with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Hanover

The best time to visit Hanover is during the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanover

Do:

  • Say hello to locals and strike up friendly conversations.
  • Respect the town’s history and cultural heritage.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the town.
  • Disrespect locals or their customs.
  • Take photographs without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanover

Hanover is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Hanover

Hanover has a hospital and several doctors’ offices. Visitors should have travel insurance and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Hanover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hanover

The closest airport to Hanover is Toronto Pearson International Airport, which is approximately 150 kilometres away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take public transportation to Hanover.

Bus Stations Near Hanover

Hanover is serviced by Greyhound bus, with bus stops in nearby towns such as Owen Sound and Walkerton.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Hanover using Highway 10, which runs through the town. Car rental is available in surrounding cities.

How to Get Around Hanover

Hanover is a small town that’s easily navigable on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Hanover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hanover is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

1. Owen Sound

Owen Sound is a beautiful waterfront city with stunning views of Georgian Bay. It’s home to several historic sites, cultural attractions, and natural wonders.

2. Sauble Beach

Sauble Beach is a popular tourist destination with a stunning seven-kilometre-long beach. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, or take part in water sports.

Conclusion

Hanover is a charming town with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, natural beauty, or simply relaxing, Hanover has something for you. Use this guide to plan your perfect trip and experience all that this delightful town has to offer.
