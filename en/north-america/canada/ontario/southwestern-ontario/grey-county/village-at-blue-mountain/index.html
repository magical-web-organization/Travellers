Introduction to Village at Blue Mountain

Located in Grey County, in Southwestern Ontario, Village at Blue Mountain is a year-round resort town that boasts beautiful natural scenery, a rich history, and a bustling tourist industry. A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Village at Blue Mountain offers a wide range of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. Whether you are looking to embark on a scenic hiking/biking adventure or explore the area’s rich history and culture, Village at Blue Mountain is the perfect place for your next vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Village at Blue Mountain

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Blue Mountain Village Conference Centre: home to a wide range of events and conferences throughout the year.
  • Craigleith Heritage Depot: a local history museum that features exhibits on the area’s rich heritage and culture.
  • South Georgian Bay Visitors’ Centre: a great place to get information on local attractions and events.

Natural Attractions of Village at Blue Mountain: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Mountain Resort: a popular ski resort that offers a wide range of winter sports activities.
  • Georgian Trail: a scenic trail that runs along the coast of Georgian Bay and offers stunning views of the bay.
  • Scenic Caves Nature Adventures: a park with many outdoor activities including a zip-line, suspension bridge, and plenty of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Village at Blue Mountain

Village at Blue Mountain is home to a wide range of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Georgian Trail
  • Bayview Escarpment Provincial Nature Reserve
  • Beaver River Wetland Conservation Area

Best Things to Do in Village at Blue Mountain: Activities and Tours

  • Blue Mountain Resort activities: skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports activities, summer activities include golfing, zip-lining, mountain biking, and hiking.
  • Scenic Caves Nature Adventures: zip-lining, suspension bridge, and hiking trails.
  • Blue Mountain Village: shopping, dining, and live events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Village at Blue Mountain

Day 1: Arrival and visit the South Georgian Bay Visitors’ Center
Day 2: Visit the Craigleith Heritage Depot and explore the Georgian Trail
Day 3: Skiing or snowboarding at Blue Mountain Resort
Day 4: Scenic Caves Nature Adventures
Day 5: Blue Mountain Village shopping and dining
Day 6: Hike the Bayview Escarpment Provincial Nature Reserve
Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Village at Blue Mountain

Must-Try Dishes

  • BeaverTails: a popular Canadian pastry that is topped with your choice of toppings such as chocolate, cinnamon, and sugar.
  • Poutine: A famous Canadian dish that’s fries drenched in gravy and cheese curds.
  • Apple Pie: The warm apple pie, with a crumbly crust and fresh apples, the ultimate comfort food.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer from the nearby Collingwood Brewery
  • Georgian Bay Spirit Co. - a distillery located in the nearby town of Meaford known for its vodka, gin, and whisky.

Local Culture and Customs of Village at Blue Mountain

Village at Blue Mountain is a welcoming destination with a laid-back atmosphere. It is customary to say “hello” or “good morning” to strangers while passing by. English is the language preference, and tipping rates fall between 15-20%. When dining in restaurants, large tables of six or more individuals should tip accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Village at Blue Mountain

The best time to visit Village at Blue Mountain is during the winter season (December through March) for skiing and other winter sports activities. Summer’s season is May through October where the temperature is mild, and visitors can indulge in outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and go-karting.
