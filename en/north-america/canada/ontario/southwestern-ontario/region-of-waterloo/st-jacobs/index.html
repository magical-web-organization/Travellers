Introduction to St. Jacobs

Located in the Region of Waterloo, in Southwestern Ontario, St. Jacobs is a charming rural community that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Known for its Mennonite heritage, St. Jacobs is a popular destination for tourists looking to immerse themselves in Canadian culture and traditions.

With a wide range of attractions, activities, and events, St. Jacobs is an ideal destination for families, couples, solo travelers, and adventure seekers. From museums and historical landmarks to hiking trails and scenic views, St. Jacobs has something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, food to try, and activities to do in St. Jacobs, and help you plan your dream vacation in this beautiful part of Ontario, Canada.

List of Places to Visit in St. Jacobs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Jacobs is home to several museums and historical landmarks that provide visitors with a glimpse into the region’s rich history and cultural heritage.

St. Jacobs & Aberfoyle Model Railway

Located in the village of St. Jacobs, the St. Jacobs & Aberfoyle Model Railway is a must-visit attraction for families with kids and train enthusiasts. The museum features 25,000 sq. ft. of model trains, railroads, miniature cities, and landscapes, and offers a fascinating insight into the world of rail transportation.

St. Jacobs Mennonite Heritage Village

The St. Jacobs Mennonite Heritage Village is a living museum that showcases the life and traditions of the Mennonite community. Visitors can explore authentic Mennonite homes, barns, and buildings, and learn about the history, culture, and values of this peaceful and self-sufficient community.

Natural Attractions of St. Jacobs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St. Jacobs is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, including lakes, rivers, forests, and wetlands. The region offers a wide range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, canoeing, and birdwatching, and boasts spectacular views and photo opportunities.

St. Jacobs Country

St. Jacobs Country is a sprawling agricultural region that covers over 100,000 acres of farmland, forests, and rivers. Visitors can explore the country by car, bike, or on foot, and discover charming towns, quaint cottages, and scenic vistas along the way.

Conestogo River Horseback Adventures

Conestogo River Horseback Adventures is a family-owned and operated business that offers guided horseback rides along the Conestogo River. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque scenery, spot wildlife, and learn about the history and culture of the region while riding through the woods and fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Jacobs

St. Jacobs is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and routes that cater to all skill levels and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

The Trans Canada Trail

The Trans Canada Trail is a multi-use trail that stretches over 24,000 km across Canada, connecting communities and natural wonders along the way. In St. Jacobs, the trail follows the Grand River, offering stunning views of the water and the surrounding landscapes.

Elmira to St. Jacobs Trail

The Elmira to St. Jacobs Trail is a 4.2 km trail that connects the towns of Elmira and St. Jacobs, passing through fields, forests, and wetlands. The trail is suitable for walking, jogging, and cycling, and offers an easy and scenic way to explore the countryside.

Best Things to Do in St. Jacobs: Activities and Tours

St. Jacobs offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and budgets. From shopping and dining to sightseeing and adventure, here are some of the best things to do in St. Jacobs:

Shopping at St. Jacobs Farmers’ Market

The St. Jacobs Farmers’ Market is a year-round market that features over 600 vendors selling fresh produce, meats, cheese, baked goods, crafts, and more. Visitors can sample local delicacies, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy live music and entertainment.

St. Jacobs Horse Drawn Tours

St. Jacobs Horse Drawn Tours offers scenic and historical tours of St. Jacobs and the surrounding countryside, using horse-drawn carriages and sleighs. Visitors can choose from various tours, including wine tours, farm tours, and evening lantern tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Jacobs

If you’re planning a week-long trip to St. Jacobs, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore St. Jacobs Village and visit the St. Jacobs & Aberfoyle Model Railway and the St. Jacobs Mennonite Heritage Village.

Day 2: Go hiking or biking along the Trans Canada Trail, and enjoy the scenic views of the Grand River.

Day 3: Visit the St. Jacobs Farmers’ Market and enjoy some local delicacies, and then take a guided horse-drawn tour of the countryside.

Day 4: Go canoeing or kayaking on the Conestogo River, and spot wildlife and birds along the way.

Day 5: Visit the Region of Waterloo Museum and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Day 6: Relax at a local spa or take a hot air balloon ride over the countryside.

Day 7: Visit nearby attractions, such as the Waterloo Park and the University of Waterloo, before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Jacobs

St. Jacobs is known for its farm-to-table cuisine, craft beer, and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Butter Tarts

Butter tarts are a Canadian dessert that consists of a flaky pastry shell filled with a sweet and creamy mixture of butter, sugar, and eggs. St. Jacobs is famous for its butter tarts, which are made with fresh local ingredients and have a unique taste and texture.

Schnitzel

Schnitzel is a German dish that consists of breaded and fried meat, usually veal or pork. St. Jacobs has a strong German heritage, and many local restaurants serve delicious schnitzel dishes that are crispy, juicy, and flavorful.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

St. Jacobs is home to several craft breweries that produce unique and flavorful beers, using local hops and malt. Visitors can sample a variety of beers, such as IPAs, stouts, and lagers, at local breweries and pubs.

Wine

St. Jacobs is also home to several wineries that produce high-quality wines, using local grapes and fruits. Visitors can taste red, white, and sparkling wines, and learn about the wine-making process at local wine cellars and tasting rooms.

Where to Stay in St. Jacobs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Jacobs offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all preferences and budgets. From luxury hotels and cozy B&Bs to rustic cabins and campsites, here are some of the best choices:

The Old Rectory Bed and Breakfast

The Old Rectory Bed and Breakfast is a charming B&B that offers comfortable rooms, a delicious breakfast, and personalized service. The B&B is located in a beautiful historic building, and is within walking distance of St. Jacobs Village and other attractions.

Green Acre Park RV Resort

Green Acre Park RV Resort is a family-friendly campground that offers spacious RV sites, tent sites, and cabins, as well as a pool, a playground, and a mini-golf course. The park is located in a peaceful and scenic area, and is close to many outdoor activities and attractions.

What to Buy in St. Jacobs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Jacobs is a shopper’s paradise, with a variety of stores, boutiques, and markets that sell unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some items you should consider bringing home:

Quilts and Crafts

The Mennonite community is known for their exquisite quilts and handicrafts, which are made with skill and precision. Visitors can buy handmade quilts, pottery, woodwork, and other crafts at local markets and galleries.

Maple Syrup

Maple syrup is a Canadian delicacy that is made from the sap of maple trees. St. Jacobs is home to several maple syrup producers that sell pure and authentic maple syrup, as well as maple candies, cookies, and other treats.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Jacobs

St. Jacobs has a unique cultural heritage that is influenced by the Mennonite community, as well as German, Irish, and Scottish immigrants. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, such as dress modestly in places of worship, and avoid taking photos of Amish or Mennonite people without permission.

Best Time to Visit St. Jacobs

The best time to visit St. Jacobs is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor events and festivals take place. However, St. Jacobs is beautiful in all seasons, and each season offers unique activities and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Jacobs

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: St. Jacobs has many parks, trails, and rivers that offer stunning views and outdoor adventures.
  • Try local food and drink: St. Jacobs is known for its farm-to-table cuisine, craft beer, and wine, so make sure to sample some of the local flavors.
  • Learn about the local culture: St. Jacobs has a rich history and heritage, so take some time to visit the museums and landmarks that showcase the region’s past.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs: St. Jacobs has a strong Mennonite community, so be respectful of their beliefs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and avoiding alcohol and tobacco in their presence.
  • Litter or damage the environment: St. Jacobs is a pristine and beautiful region, so make sure to dispose of your garbage properly and avoid damaging the natural habitats.
  • Ignore safety guidelines: St. Jacobs offers many outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and canoeing, which can be fun and exciting, but also pose risks. Follow safety guidelines and regulations, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Jacobs

St. Jacobs is a safe and peaceful region, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables, especially in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Health in St. Jacobs

St. Jacobs has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, that offer a range of services and treatments. Visitors should have valid health insurance and carry necessary medications and prescriptions with them.

Getting to St. Jacobs: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Jacobs is located about 105 km west of Toronto, and is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near St. Jacobs

The nearest airports to St. Jacobs are Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ) and London International Airport (YXU), which are both about 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near St. Jacobs

Greyhound Canada and Ontario Northland operate buses that stop at the Kitchener Bus Terminal, which is about 20 minutes away from St. Jacobs by car.

Other Options

St. Jacobs is also accessible by VIA Rail trains, which operate daily from Toronto and other major cities. However, visitors will need to take a taxi or shuttle to get to St. Jacobs from the train station.

How to Get Around in St. Jacobs

St. Jacobs is a small and walkable community, with many attractions, shops, and restaurants located within walking distance of each other. However, visitors may need to rent a car, bike, or take a taxi to get to some of the outdoor attractions and sights outside of the village.

Where to Go Next from St. Jacobs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Jacobs is located in a beautiful region of Ontario, with many interesting and exciting destinations within a short drive or train ride away. Here are some ideas for your next adventure:

  • Niagara Falls: One of Canada’s most famous and iconic natural wonders, Niagara Falls is a must-see destination for visitors to Ontario.
  • Stratford: A charming town known for its theater festival, Stratford offers a rich cultural experience, with plays, concerts, and art shows happening year-round.
  • Grand Bend: A popular beach destination on the shores of Lake Huron, Grand Bend is a great place to relax, swim, and sunbathe, and also offers water sports and festivals.

Conclusion

St. Jacobs is a hidden gem in Ontario, Canada, that offers a unique mix of culture, history, and nature. Whether you’re a foodie, a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a culture enthusiast, you’ll find plenty to do and see in St. Jacobs.

From hiking and biking to shopping and dining, St. Jacobs offers something for everyone, and is an ideal destination for families, couples, and solo travelers. So pack your bags, plan your itinerary, and get ready for an unforgettable vacation in St. Jacobs!
