Introduction to Lincoln

Nestled in beautiful Niagara Peninsula, Lincoln is a picturesque town in Ontario, Canada. Known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery, this region offers countless opportunities for outdoor adventures, cultural exploration, and relaxation. Whether you are planning a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Lincoln has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Lincoln and Welland - The museum offers a unique insight into the history of the region, featuring exhibits and artifacts that showcase the local heritage and culture.
  2. St. Catharines Museum and Welland Canal Centre - This museum provides a fascinating glimpse into the history of the Welland Canal, a vital transportation link that connects the Great Lakes to the St. Lawrence Seaway.
  3. Jordan Historical Museum - Situated in a beautifully restored Victorian home, this museum celebrates the culture and heritage of early settlers in the region.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ball’s Falls Conservation Area - A beautiful park with hiking trails, waterfalls, and a historic grist mill dating back to the 1800s.
  2. Twenty Valley Trail - This scenic trail offers breathtaking views of Niagara Escarpment, with opportunities for hiking, biking, and nature photography.
  3. Cave Springs Conservation Area - A stunning conservation area home to a rare ecosystem of old-growth forests, wetlands, and meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln

  1. Bruce Trail - A 900-kilometer trail that runs across Niagara Escarpment, offering spectacular views of the scenic beauty of the region.
  2. Lincoln Greenway - A 7-kilometer trail perfect for hiking, biking, and nature watching. It follows the old rail line that once ran through the town, offering glimpses of the region’s history and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln: Activities and Tours

  1. Wine Tasting Tours - Lincoln is renowned for its excellent wineries, with over 50 wineries in the region. Tour the wineries and enjoy sampling some of the best wine in the country.
  2. Hot Air Balloon Rides - Take in the breathtaking views of the Niagara Escarpment and the vineyards from high above in a hot air balloon ride.
  3. Golfing - With world-class golf courses located amidst stunning landscapes, Lincoln provides an exceptional golfing experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lincoln

Day Activities
1 Visit the Museum of Lincoln and Welland, explore the Jordan Art Gallery, and enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.
2 Hike the Twenty Valley Trail, enjoy a picnic, and visit the ball’s falls conservation area.
3 Take a wine tasting tour of the region and visit the local wineries.
4 Spend the day golfing at one of the world-class golf courses in the region.
5 Visit the St. Catharines Museum and Welland Canal Centre and take a boat tour of the Welland Canal.
6 Spend a relaxing day at the spa and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
7 Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the stunning views of Niagara Escarpment.

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beef on Weck - A traditional sandwich made with roast beef, horseradish, and kummelweck roll.
  2. Peameal Bacon - A Canadian specialty made with boneless pork loin trimmed, rolled, and then coated with dried and powdered yellow peas.
  3. Butter Tarts - A pastry filled with butter, syrup, sugar, and eggs that originated in the region in the early 1900s.

Local Beverages

  1. Icewine - A sweet dessert wine made from grapes that have been frozen while still on the vine.
  2. Craft Beer - Lincoln is home to several award-winning craft breweries, including Bench Brewing Company and Oast House Brewery.

Where to Stay in Lincoln: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Vintage Hotels - A series of historic hotels located throughout the Niagara region, offering luxury accommodations and exceptional service.
  2. Jordan House Hotel - A beautifully restored historic hotel located in the heart of Lincoln, offering comfortable rooms and exceptional dining.

What to Buy in Lincoln: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Niagara Wine - Bring home a bottle or two of world-class wine produced in the region.
  2. Artisanal Cheese - Niagara is home to several award-winning specialty cheese makers, including Upper Canada Cheese Company and Black River Cheese Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln

  1. Friendly People - The locals in Lincoln are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors can expect a warm and hospitable welcome.
  2. Small-Town Charm - Lincoln is a small town with a big heart, offering visitors the opportunity to experience the intimate and friendly charm of a small community.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln

  1. Spring and Fall - The best time to visit Lincoln is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln

Do:

  1. Enjoy the local cuisine and wine.
  2. Visit the local museums and galleries.
  3. Take a wine tasting tour of the region.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke in public areas.
  2. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  3. Ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln

Lincoln is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables in plain sight and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lincoln

Lincoln has several medical centers and hospitals for any medical emergencies. Travelers should take care to stay hydrated during the hot summer months and protect themselves from sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Lincoln: Transportation Options and Logistics

  1. By Car - Lincoln can be easily accessed by car via the Queen Elizabeth Way (QEW) or Highway 400.
  2. By Bus - Greyhound and Megabus operate regular bus services to Niagara Falls, which is a short drive from Lincoln.
  3. By Air - The nearest major airport is Toronto Pearson International Airport, which is located approximately 100 kilometers from Lincoln.

Airports Near Lincoln

  1. Toronto Pearson International Airport

Bus Stations Near Lincoln

  1. Niagara Falls Bus Terminal

Other Options

  1. Taxis and rental cars are available for travel within the town.

How to Get Around in Lincoln

  1. Visitors can get around Lincoln either by car, bike, or on foot. There are also several local taxi companies and rental car services available.

Where to Go Next from Lincoln: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Niagara Falls - A breathtaking natural wonder, just a short drive from Lincoln.
  2. Toronto - A bustling metropolis located just a few hours’ drive from Lincoln, offering world-class dining, shopping, and cultural attractions.
  3. Niagara-on-the-Lake - A charming town located on the banks of the Niagara River, known for its historic sites, wineries, and theater festivals.

Conclusion

Lincoln, Ontario, Canada offers visitors a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and small-town charm. With a plethora of attractions and activities, visitors are sure to find something to enjoy in this stunning location.
