Introduction to Fort Erie

Located in the Niagara Peninsula, Fort Erie is a picturesque city in Ontario, Canada. It borders the United States and is home to various landmarks and cultural attractions. With its stunning natural beauty and rich history, Fort Erie is a favorite destination for many tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Erie

Fort Erie has a lot to offer, from natural attractions and historical sites to outdoor adventures and local culture. Here’s a list of places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Erie Historical Museum: Learn about the history of the city, from its role in the War of 1812 to its growth as a border town.
  • Ridgeway Battlefield Site: Commemorating the Battle of Ridgeway, one of the most significant battles of the Fenian Raids.
  • Old Fort Erie: Experience life in a 19th-century fort and learn about soldiers’ daily routines and responsibilities.

Natural Attractions of Fort Erie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crystal Beach: A beautiful beach with a crystal-clear lake and breathtaking sunset views.
  • Friendship Trail: Perfect for bike rides and walks, the Friendship Trail offers scenic views adorned with a variety of wildlife.
  • Point Abino Lighthouse: A historic lighthouse that’s a sight for sore eyes.
  • Safari Niagara: A thrilling wildlife park with exotic animals from around the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Erie

Fort Erie is full of nature trails, ideal for hiking, biking, and exploring wildlife. Here are some routes to consider:

  • Niagara River Recreation Trail: A 56 km-long trail, following the Niagara River, with views of waterfalls and historical sites.
  • Peace Bridge Trail: A 4.5 km-long trail leading you to international vistas and scenic landscapes.
  • Steve Bauer Trail: A beautiful trail that starts in Fort Erie and joins with the Niagara River Recreation Trail, passing by the wine country and Lake Erie.

Best Things to Do in Fort Erie: Activities and Tours

  • Fort Erie Race Track: A hub for thrill-seekers and horse racing fans, Fort Erie Race Track hosts various racing events throughout the year.
  • Safari Niagara: A wildlife park famous for its exotic species and interactive animal exhibits.
  • Whisky Run Golf Club: A stunning golf course renowned for its distinctive natural beauty and serene atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Erie

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visiting Fort Erie:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Fort Erie Historical Museum and Old Fort Erie
Day 2 Explore Crystal Beach and take a stroll along the Friendship Trail
Day 3 Take a tour of the Point Abino Lighthouse and relax at Safari Niagara
Day 4 Hike the Niagara River Recreation Trail and see the waterfalls
Day 5 Enjoy a day of horse racing at Fort Erie Race Track
Day 6 Play golf at Whisky Run Golf Club
Day 7 Shop at local markets and bring home some souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Erie

Fort Erie offers a wide range of culinary delicacies that will tantalize your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Butter tarts: A flaky pastry filled with butter, sugar, and egg. A Canadian classic!
  • Poutine: A delicious snack featuring french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Smoked meat: A tender smoked beef sandwich originating from Montreal.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Fort Erie has a thriving craft beer scene, with various breweries around the city.
  • Niagara wine: The city is located in the Niagara wine region, famous for its ice wine and other varieties.

Where to Stay in Fort Erie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Erie has various lodging options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top picks:

  • The Buffalo Marriott Niagara: A luxurious and modern hotel with fantastic views of the Buffalo skyline.
  • The Haven Guesthouse: A charming and cozy guesthouse that provides a relaxing stay with modern amenities.
  • Hostelling International Niagara Falls: A budget-friendly hostel with various amenities and a perfect location in downtown Niagara Falls.

What to Buy in Fort Erie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fort Erie is full of local markets and souvenir shops where you can shop for gifts and local products. Here are some items to consider buying:

  • Niagara wine: The city is famous for its exquisite wine, and you can bring home a bottle or two from the various vineyards.
  • Fort Erie souvenirs: You can find many souvenir shops around the city selling Fort Erie branded products such as t-shirts, hats, and bags.
  • Maple Syrup: Canada is famous for its Maple Syrup production, and you can get a Canadian bottle to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Erie

Fort Erie’s culture is a blend of Canadian and American influences, with a great emphasis on the community. The city has a rich history, and the locals are proud of their heritage. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Canadians are polite and respect personal space.
  • The official languages are English and French.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants, bars, and other service locations.

Best Time to Visit Fort Erie

The best time to visit Fort Erie is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild, and the natural beauty is at its peak. Summer is the busiest season, with plenty of events in full swing. However, visiting in the fall can also be a treat with stunning autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Erie

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Fort Erie:

Do:

  • Learn about the city’s history and landmarks.
  • Visit Niagara Falls, which is about a 20-minute drive from Fort Erie.
  • Use courteous language and be polite with locals and fellow tourists.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Litter or vandalize public or private properties.
  • Speed on roads and highways.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Erie

Fort Erie is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, it is wise to be cautious and take safety precautions as you would in any foreign city.

Health in Fort Erie

Fort Erie has modern healthcare facilities and offers quality medical services. However, it is always advisable to have travel insurance when visiting foreign countries.

Getting to Fort Erie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Erie

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport- 30 min drive
  • Toronto Pearson International Airport- 1 hour 45 min drive

Bus Stations Near Fort Erie

  • Greyhound Terminal- Buffalo, NY, 20 min drive
  • Niagara Falls Transit Terminal- Niagara Falls, Ontario, 30 min drive

Other Options

  • VIA Rail Canada trains stop at Niagara Falls station. Fort Erie is approx. 30 min by car from there.
  • Car rentals and taxis are readily available throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Fort Erie

Fort Erie is a walkable city with several bike rental places available for short-term rentals. Taxis, buses, and rental cars are also options to consider.

Where to Go Next from Fort Erie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Other notable destinations and excursions around Fort Erie include:

  • Niagara Falls, Ontario: Known internationally for its stunning waterfalls and impressive views.
  • Buffalo, NY: A bustling city with excellent shopping, dining, and architecture.
  • Toronto, Ontario: A vibrant and diverse city known for its arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Fort Erie has something to offer everyone, from its natural beauty and wildlife to its historical sites and cultural attractions. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and explore this beautiful city in Ontario, Canada.
