Introduction to Belleville

Belleville is a charming city situated in the Quinte-Northumberland region of Eastern Ontario. This lively city is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a great destination for tourists looking for a mix of adventure and relaxation. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know before visiting.

List of Places to Visit in Belleville

Belleville offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors. Here are some must-visit places in Belleville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Glanmore National Historic Site: This beautifully restored 1883 mansion showcases the lifestyle of a wealthy family in the late 19th century.
  • The Hastings County Museum of Agricultural Heritage: This museum tells the story of agriculture and rural life in the Quinte area.
  • The Belleville Heritage Centre: This museum features exhibits on the history of Belleville from the 18th century until the present day.

Natural Attractions of Belleville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Quinte Conservation Area: This 420-hectare nature reserve offers hiking, birdwatching, and fishing opportunities.
  • The Zwick’s Park Beach: This beautiful sandy beach is perfect for swimming, picnicking, and sunbathing.
  • The Bay of Quinte: This stunning bay offers some of the best fishing and boating opportunities in Ontario.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belleville

Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a novice, Belleville has plenty of hiking and biking routes that cater to every level of fitness. Some popular trails include the Waterfront Trail, the Belleville-Napanee Trail, and the Harold Town Conservation Area Trail.

Best Things to Do in Belleville: Activities and Tours

  • Brewery Tours: Take a tour of the Signal Brewery and learn about the process of brewing beer.
  • Ghost Walks: Explore the haunted history of Belleville with a ghost walk through the city.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit the Prince Edward County wineries and indulge in some of the best wine Ontario has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belleville

Day 1: Visit the downtown core and discover the historic architecture of the city. Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of the Quinte Conservation Area and enjoy a picnic by the lake. Day 3: Take a brewery tour and sample some of Belleville’s finest craft beer. Day 4: Experience the haunted history of Belleville on a ghost walk. Day 5: Visit the Zwick’s Park Beach and take a stroll along the boardwalk. Day 6: Go on a wine tasting tour in Prince Edward County. Day 7: Visit the Glanmore National Historic Site and learn about the rich history of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Belleville

Belleville is home to a diverse range of restaurants, cafes, and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic consisting of crispy fries topped with gravy and cheese curds.
  • Butter Tarts: A small pastry filled with buttery sugar mixture and raisins.
  • Maple Syrup: Ontario’s famous syrup is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Belleville is home to some great micro-breweries, such as Signal Brewery and Wild Card Brewing Company.
  • Ice Wine: This sweet wine is produced from grapes that have been frozen on the vine.

Where to Stay in Belleville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belleville has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some recommendations:

  • Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Belleville: A modern hotel with great amenities.
  • The Park Place Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast in the heart of the city.
  • Couchsurfing: A budget-friendly option that allows you to stay with locals and experience the city like a local.

What to Buy in Belleville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: A classic Canadian souvenir.
  • Artisanal Cheeses: Prince Edward County is known for its artisanal cheeses, available at local markets.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: The Prince Edward County Potters Guild is a great place to find unique pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Belleville

The people of Belleville are known for their warmth and friendliness. Visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Tipping in restaurants and bars is customary, usually 15-20% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Belleville

The best time to visit Belleville is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belleville

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Use offensive language or behavior.
  • Ignore local regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belleville

Belleville is a safe city for visitors. However, common sense precautions like not leaving valuables in plain sight are advised.

Health in Belleville

Belleville has excellent health care facilities. In case of an emergency, dial 911.

Getting to Belleville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belleville

  • Toronto Pearson International Airport: 201 km
  • Ottawa Macdonald-Cartier International Airport: 167 km

Bus Stations Near Belleville

  • Belleville Bus Terminal
  • Trenton Transit Centre

Other Options

  • By Car: Belleville is easily accessible via Highway 401.
  • By Train: VIA Rail connects Belleville to Toronto, Ottawa, and Montreal.

How to Get Around in Belleville

Belleville has a good public transportation system, with bus services connecting all major tourist attractions. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Belleville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Prince Edward County: Visit the picturesque towns of Picton and Wellington and explore the countryside.
  • Sandbanks Provincial Park: Enjoy the beautiful sand dunes and the turquoise waters of Lake Ontario.
  • Kingston: A historic city with beautiful architecture and great waterfront views.

Conclusion

Belleville is a hidden gem in the heart of Eastern Ontario. With its natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking for an authentic Canadian experience. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your next trip to Belleville.
