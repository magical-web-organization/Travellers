Introduction to Napanee

Napanee is a small town located in the Quinte-Northumberland region of Eastern Ontario, Canada. Though it’s small, it is a big name in Canadian history as it is the birthplace of Sir John A. Macdonald, the first Prime Minister of Canada. Napanee has a rich history and beautiful natural scenery which continues to attract visitors from all around the world. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll explore Napanee’s best attractions and learn some of its unique customs and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Napanee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Macpherson House: It’s the birthplace of Sir John A. Macdonald which is now a museum
  • Lennox & Addington County Museum and Archives: It showcases the county’s culture and history from the late 1700s to the present day.
  • Napanee Beer Company: It brews small-batch craft beers inspired by the history of Napanee, which enjoys massive popularity in the area.

Natural Attractions of Napanee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Napanee River Promenade: A scenic path that winds around the river and is perfect for strolling and cycling.
  • Millennium Trail: This 13-mile trail extends from Newburgh to Napanee and is popular for hiking and biking.
  • Provincial Parks: Bon Echo Provincial Park and Lake on the Mountain Provincial Park offer unique natural experiences while at the same time preserving the natural environment of Eastern Ontario.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Napanee

Here are some of the hiking and biking routes you ought to consider while in Napanee.

  • Millennium Trail stretches for more than 13 miles through the scenic countryside and is perfect for biking, running, or hiking. It connects various communities and offers scenic views of swamps, rivers, and streams.

Best Things to Do in Napanee: Activities and Tours

Napanee has many things to do, from outdoor adventures to cultural events. Here are some of the best activities and tours to explore while in Napanee.

  • Glenora Ferry: The ferry service connects Prince Edward County with Lennox and Addington County and provides beautiful views of the Bay of Quinte
  • Hike to Bon Echo Provincial Park: Napanee’s Bon Echo Provincial Park is a prime destination for adventure lovers, boaters, hikers, swimmers, and climbers.
  • Explore the Town: Napanee boasts of some of the best local businesses. Visit stores here to buy souvenirs or experience the area’s unique products and flavors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Napanee

If you have a week to spend in Napanee, you’re in for a treat. Here’s one suggested itinerary that can help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 Explore Downtown Napanee
Day 2 Visit Provincial Parks and go fishing and boating
Day 3 Hiking in Bon Echo Provincial Park
Day 4 Cycling on the Millennium Trail
Day 5 Visit Local Attractions
Day 6 Enjoy Glenora Ferry and Scenic Views
Day 7 Relax and Shop Local

Food and Drink Scene of Napanee

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mac Attack: A burger with all the usual toppings, but with processed macaroni and cheese smashed right on top of the patty.
  • Butter Tarts: A flaky pastry filled with a sweet mixture of syrup, sugar, eggs, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Napanee Beer Company: A local brewery that offers a vast selection of ales, IPAs, Lagers, and seasonal brews.
  • Bergeron Estate Winery: This winery produces fine wines using local fruit.

Where to Stay in Napanee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Napanee offers a wide selection of accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels, cozy cottages, and budget-friendly motels.

  • Smugglers Cove Inn: A family-friendly, waterfront boutique located on the banks of the Napanee River.
  • Camelot Inn: A cozy and affordable motel featuring modern amenities and great views of the Napanee River.

What to Buy in Napanee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Napanee is the perfect destination for travelers looking to buy unique souvenirs and products.

  • Macdonald’s Originals: A souvenirs store with a wide variety of mementos, crafts, and maple syrup named after Sir John A. Macdonald.
  • Healing Hollow Store: A locally-owned shop dedicated to aromatherapy, organic teas, and essential oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Napanee

Napanee locals are known for preserving and honoring their country’s roots and customs. The town’s rich heritage is evident in its museums, historic sites, and local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Napanee

The best time to visit Napanee is from June to September when the temperatures are warm and humidity is low, making outdoor activities more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Napanee

Do:

  • Do taste the local dishes
  • Do explore the town’s shops for genuine Canadian souvenirs
  • Do bring a first-aid kit
  • Do respect the local residents and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended
  • Don’t disturb the natural environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Napanee

Napanee has a low crime rate, and it is considered safe. However, visitors need to be careful to avoid accidents, especially when exploring the outdoor attractions.

Health in Napanee

Visitors are advised to have updated travel vaccinations, health insurance, and sanitize their hands regularly to prevent the outbreak of COVID-19 and other communicable diseases.

Getting to Napanee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Napanee

  • Kingston International Airport is the closest airport to Napanee, approximately 28 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Napanee

  • Coach Canada operates a bus service that connects Napanee to other cities in Ontario.

Other Options

  • Hire a car from Kingston
  • Hiring a taxi or a limousine

How to Get Around in Napanee

  • You can rent a car from a local vendor or hire a taxi service.
  • Walk on the Millennium trail to explore the town.
  • Cycling or hiring an e-bike can be an enjoyable way to explore the town more closely.

Where to Go Next from Napanee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Belleville: A waterfront town that An ideal outing for freshwater fishing enthusiasts, with nearby lakes and rivers.
  • Prince Edward County: A perfect destination for oenophiles, with over 35 local wineries, and a focal point for those who appreciate local arts and crafts.

Conclusion

Napanee is an exceptional historic town that offers a blend of outdoor scenery, cultural attractions, unique heritage and culinary experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan an unforgettable trip that’s tailor-made to suit your taste and preferences. Get packing!
