Addington Highlands is a charming township located in Quinte-Northumberland, in Eastern Ontario, Canada. This picturesque region offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and thrilling outdoor adventures. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, Addington Highlands has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Addington Highlands

Addington Highlands covers an area of 1,202 square kilometers and is home to approximately 2,700 residents. The township is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes that include pristine lakes, rolling hills, and dense forests. Several rivers and streams flow through the region, making it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The region is also steeped in history, with several museums and historical sites that showcase the rich cultural heritage of the area.

List of Places to Visit in Addington Highlands

Here are some of the top places to visit in Addington Highlands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Flinton Pioneer Museum - This museum showcases the history and heritage of the Addington Highlands area.
  • Cloyne Pioneer Museum & Archives - This museum features exhibits on local agriculture, heritage homes, and a one-room schoolhouse.
  • Wesleyan Methodist Church - This historic church was built-in 1864 and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Addington Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bon Echo Provincial Park - This park is known for Mazinaw Rock, North America’s second-largest monolith. Hiking trails, campsite, and crystal-clear lakes await.
  • Foy Provincial Park - This beautiful park offers excellent hiking trails and stunning views of the water.
  • Kaladar Pine Barrens - An ecologically significant site that is home to many rare and endangered species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Addington Highlands

For hikers and bikers, there are several exciting trails to explore in Addington Highlands:

  • Bon Echo Provincial Park Trails - offers the Abes and Essens Trail, Beach Trail, and the Cliff Top Trail. The views of the park from these trails are a must-see.
  • Foy Provincial Park Trails - offers quiet trails that pass through meadows, forests and along the water’s edge.
  • K&A Trail - stretches for 33 kilometers across Addington Highlands. A great trail to bike.

Best Things to Do in Addington Highlands: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the must-try activities and tours in Addington Highlands:

  • Fishing - The region’s clear and pristine lakes are perfect for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Kayaking/Canoeing - The waterways of Addington Highlands offer some of the best kayaking and canoeing experiences in Ontario.
  • Horseback Riding - Guided rides offer fantastic views of the countryside and surrounding forests.
  • Dark Sky Viewing - This area is a Dark Sky Preserve and offers excellent views of the stars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Addington Highlands

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Addington Highlands:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Bon Echo Provincial Park, hike the Abes and Essens Trail, and Beach Trail, camp overnight.
Day 2 Take the Cliff Top Trail hike in the am and then paddle a canoe or kayak to Mazinaw Lake rock.
Day 3 Visit Flinton Pioneer Museum in the morning and then head to Foy Provincial Park, hike the various paths, and camp overnight.
Day 4 Go fish in one of the several nearby lakes.
Day 5 Learn of the region’s history by visiting the Cloyne Pioneer Museum & Archives, then go horseback riding in the evening.
Day 6 Head to the Kaladar Pine Barrens for a day of hiking and exploring.
Day 7 Enjoy a local beer at one of the nearby breweries, then stargaze on a clear night.

Food and Drink Scene of Addington Highlands

Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Addington Highlands:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Walleye Supper - One of the most popular dishes in the region, these freshwater fish are a delicacy, served in local restaurants.
  • Flint Corn Porridge - A traditional dish made of stone-ground cornmeal and served for breakfast or lunch.
  • Fiddleheads - These young ferns, that taste like a mix between asparagus and green beans, are picked in early spring.

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider - Addington Highlands is known for its delicious apple cider that can be found in several local producers.
  • Craft Beer - Several breweries produce local beer, using water from the area’s pristine lakes and streams.

Where to Stay in Addington Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the top places to stay in Addington Highlands:

  • Bon Echo Provincial Park Campground - Offering both tent sites as well as yurts, fully furnished tent structures.
  • Provincial Park Foy - Offers comfortable campsites all year round, including facilities for RVs too.
  • Gemmill’s B&B - Located in Cloyne, this cozy B&B provides a peaceful retreat close to popular attractions.

What to Buy in Addington Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best local products, souvenirs, and crafts that you can bring home from Addington Highlands:

  • Maple Syrup - Addington Highlands is known for its local maple syrup, which can be found at several roadside stands.
  • Handmade Crafts - Many local artisans create beautiful handmade crafts with materials found in the area.
  • Locally Produced Honey - Honey producers in Addington Highlands produce some of the purest and tastiest honey in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Addington Highlands

Addington Highlands is a friendly, welcoming community. The region is known for its rural charm, rich cultural heritage, and love of the outdoors. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Addington Highlands

The best time to visit Addington Highlands is during the summer months from May to September when the weather is pleasant, and most attractions and activities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Addington Highlands

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts of visiting Addington Highlands:

Do:

  • Respect the wilderness and natural environment.
  • Take advantage of local knowledge and experience.

Don’t:

  • Litter, harm, or mistreat local fauna or flora.
  • Ignore weather and safety precautions when engaging in outdoors activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Addington Highlands

Addington Highlands is generally a safe area. Visitors should take basic safety precautions and follow safety rules when engaging in outdoor activities. Visitors should also be mindful of wildlife, especially bears, and should take appropriate precautions.

Health in Addington Highlands

There are a few hospitals and medical clinics in the surrounding areas which can offer medical treatments in case of emergencies and there is also a pharmacy and a dental clinic available to access in Cloyne.

Getting to Addington Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Addington Highlands by bus, car, or plane. The nearest airport is Kingston International Airport, which is approximately 107 km away. Toronto is around 232 km away, and Ottawa is 195 km.

Airports Near Addington Highlands

  • Kingston International Airport (YGK) - 107 km

Bus Stations Near Addington Highlands

  • Land O’Lakes Tourist Services - 14701 Hwy #41, Cloyne, ON K0H 1K0

Other Options

  • Renting a car may be the most practical option.

How to Get Around in Addington Highlands

The best way to get around in Addington Highlands is by car, as public transportation is limited. Hiking and biking are excellent options for exploring the region.

Where to Go Next from Addington Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions that you can add to your itinerary:

  • Kingston - This historic city is home to charming historic sites and a vibrant entertainment scene. Only one hour away!
  • Algonquin Provincial Park - Explore the iconic Canadian wilderness at Algonquin Park. Located approx. 4 hours away.
  • Ottawa - The Canadian capital is famous for its stunning architecture, world-class museums, and vibrant food scene. Approx. 2 hours away.

Conclusion

Addington Highlands is a hidden gem located in the heart of Eastern Ontario. This beautiful region offers a unique blend of natural beauty, thrilling outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage. With so many things to see and do, it’s no wonder why Addington Highlands is quickly becoming a popular travel destination. Follow this comprehensive travel guide and discover everything that this stunning region has to offer.
