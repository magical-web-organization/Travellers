Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Quinte-Northumberland has a rich history, and there are many museums and historical places to explore:

The National Air Force Museum of Canada

The National Air Force Museum of Canada is a must-visit attraction in Quinte-Northumberland. The museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Royal Canadian Air Force, with a focus on the role played by Canadian airmen and women in both World War I and II.

Macaulay Heritage Park

Macaulay Heritage Park is a living history museum that allows you to step back in time and experience life in 19th-century Canada. You can tour the historic buildings, explore the gardens, and learn about the Macaulay family, who were one of the founding families of Picton.

Trent-Severn Waterway

The Trent-Severn Waterway is a 386-kilometer canal system that connects Lake Ontario to Georgian Bay. The waterway is a marvel of engineering, and it’s a beautiful spot to hike, bike, boat, or kayak.

Signal Brewery

Signal Brewery is a craft brewery located in Corbyville. The brewery is housed in a historic building that dates back to the 1800s and produces a variety of beers that are available for tasting or purchase.

Natural Attractions of Quinte-Northumberland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Quinte-Northumberland is home to many natural attractions, from provincial parks to lavender farms:

Sandbanks Provincial Park

Sandbanks Provincial Park is a must-visit attraction in the region. The park boasts three beautiful beaches, dunes, and hiking trails. Visitors can also go swimming, kayaking, windsurfing, and paddleboarding.

Prince Edward County Lavender Farm

Prince Edward County Lavender Farm is a beautiful spot to visit in the summer months. The farm grows over 10,000 lavender plants, which are used to create essential oils, soaps, and other lavender products. Visitors can take a tour of the farm, wander through the fields, and enjoy the tranquility of the surroundings.

Northumberland Provincial Park

Northumberland Provincial Park is a rugged wilderness park that offers visitors the chance to go hiking, camping, fishing, and paddling. The park is home to beautiful lakes and forests, and there are plenty of hiking trails to explore.

Mount Pelion Lookout Trail

Mount Pelion Lookout Trail is a beautiful hiking trail that offers stunning views of the region. The trail is rated as moderate and is approximately 4.2 kilometers long.

Presqu’ile Provincial Park

Presqu’ile Provincial Park is a beautiful park located on the shores of Lake Ontario. The park boasts beautiful beaches and offers visitors the chance to go hiking, bird watching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quinte-Northumberland

Quinte-Northumberland is a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • Macaulay Mountain Conservation Area: This beautiful conservation area offers visitors the chance to go hiking and enjoy beautiful views of Prince Edward County.
  • Shorelines Trail: This 140-kilometer trail runs along the north shore of Lake Ontario and offers stunning views of the water and the surrounding countryside.
  • Millennium Trail: This 49-kilometer trail runs from Carrying Place to Picton and is perfect for biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Quinte-Northumberland: Activities and Tours

There are many activities to enjoy while in Quinte-Northumberland, including:

  • Wine Tasting: The region is home to many wineries, and there are plenty of opportunities to taste local wines.
  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of the Trent-Severn Waterway or explore the region’s beautiful lakes.
  • Golfing: There are many beautiful golf courses in the region, including Bay of Quinte Golf and Country Club and Timber Ridge Golf Course.
  • Festivals and Events: Quinte-Northumberland hosts many festivals and events throughout the year, including the Sandbanks Music Festival, the Trenton Scottish Irish Festival, and the Belleville Waterfront Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Quinte-Northumberland

Must-Try Dishes

Quinte-Northumberland is home to many delicious local dishes, including:

  • Butter Tarts: This Canadian classic can be found at many bakeries in the region, including the popular Honey Pie Hives and Herbals in Roslin.
  • Poutine: This Canadian dish of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy is a must-try while in Quinte-Northumberland. Try it at local restaurants like The Port Bistro Pub in Trenton.
  • Apple Pie: The region is known for its delicious apple pies, which can be found at bakeries like Vicki’s Veggies in Milford.

Local Beverages

Quinte-Northumberland is also home to many great local beverages, including:

  • Wine: The region is known for its great wines, and there are many wineries to choose from. Try the Pinot Noir at the Grange of Prince Edward Vineyards and Estate Winery in Hillier.
  • Craft Beer: With breweries like Signal Brewery and Wild Card Brewery, Quinte-Northumberland is a great place to taste delicious craft beer.

Where to Stay in Quinte-Northumberland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great accommodations options in Quinte-Northumberland:

  • Drake Devonshire Inn: This beautiful inn is located in Wellington and offers stunning views of Lake Ontario.
  • The Waring House: This historic inn is located in Picton and offers comfortable rooms and delicious food.
  • The Fidler’s House Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in Brighton and offers a cozy place to stay.

What to Buy in Quinte-Northumberland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs and local products to bring home from Quinte-Northumberland include:

  • Lavender Products: Purchase essential oils, soaps, and other products made with locally grown lavender from the Prince Edward County Lavender Farm.
  • Cheese: The region is known for its delicious cheese, which can be purchased at many local stores, including Brighton Fine Foods in Brighton.
  • Wine: Take home a bottle of local wine as a souvenir from your trip to Quinte-Northumberland.

Local Culture and Customs of Quinte-Northumberland

Quinte-Northumberland has a rich culture, and locals take pride in their history and traditions. One tradition in the region is the annual maple syrup festival, which takes place in the early spring and celebrates the region’s maple syrup production. Visitors can enjoy maple taffy on snow, maple syrup pancakes, and other maple-flavored treats.

Best Time to Visit Quinte-Northumberland

The best time to visit Quinte-Northumberland is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the lakes and beaches are perfect for swimming and other outdoor activities. However, the region is also beautiful in the fall when the leaves change color, and the wineries and local apple orchards are in full swing.
