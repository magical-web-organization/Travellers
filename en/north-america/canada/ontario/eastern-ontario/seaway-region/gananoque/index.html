Located in the Seaway Region of Eastern Ontario, Gananoque is a small, picturesque town surrounded by majestic waterfront scenery. Its name means “town on two rivers” in the First Nations language, and this is a reflection of the town’s unique position between the St. Lawrence River and the Gananoque River. The town is a popular destination for both Canadians and international visitors, thanks to its natural attractions, historic landmarks, and vibrant cultural scene.

Introduction to Gananoque

Gananoque is a town that caters to travelers of all tastes. Whether you’re interested in exploring the outdoors, learning about history and culture, trying local food, or simply relaxing by the water, you’ll find plenty of things to do here. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best attractions, activities, and food options in town, as well as the local customs and culture.

Best Things to Do in Gananoque: Activities and Tours

Gananoque has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Take a boat tour of the Thousand Islands
  • Go kayaking or canoeing on the St. Lawrence River
  • Rent a bike and explore the town and surrounding countryside
  • Embark on a fishing trip
  • Go ziplining at the Skywood Eco Adventure Park
  • Try your luck at the Shorelines Casino Thousand Islands

Week-Long Itinerary for Gananoque

A week-long trip to Gananoque allows for plenty of time to see the town and surrounding area. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Explore Gananoque’s downtown and waterfront area. Take a stroll along the Gananoque River and visit the Arthur Child Heritage Museum.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Thousand Islands. You can choose from a variety of excursions ranging from short one-hour trips to full-day adventures.

Day 3: Visit St. Lawrence Islands National Park and hike the trails. You can also camp in the park overnight.

Day 4: Rent a bike and explore the Thousand Islands Parkway Trail. Stop at Landon Bay Centre along the way.

Day 5: Experience the Skywood Eco Adventure Park. Try the ziplines, aerial courses, and treetop walkways.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Kingston, just a short drive away. Explore the historic downtown area and visit Kingston’s museums and galleries.

Day 7: Relax on Joel Stone Beach and take a swim in the waters of the St. Lawrence River.

Local Culture and Customs of Gananoque

Gananoque’s rich First Nations history is an important part of its local culture. Visitors to the town will find a welcoming community that celebrates its heritage while embracing diverse cultures from around the world. In Gananoque, it’s customary to be respectful of the town’s natural surroundings and to take part in outdoor activities like hiking and canoeing.

Best Time to Visit Gananoque

The best time to visit Gananoque is during the summer months, from June through August. This is when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town’s outdoor attractions are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gananoque

Safety and Health

Gananoque is a safe and clean destination for travelers. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Gananoque

  • Always wear a life vest when boating or kayaking
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen while outdoors
  • Watch for wildlife like bears and snakes
  • Respect private property and follow trail markers

Health in Gananoque

  • Bring insect repellent to avoid tick bites
  • Drink bottled water if you have a sensitive stomach
  • Wear sturdy shoes while hiking to avoid injuries

How to Get Around in Gananoque

Gananoque is a walkable town, with most attractions within easy walking distance of each other. Biking is also a popular way to get around the town, and many local companies offer bike rentals. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Gananoque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spend in Ontario, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some options:

  • Kingston: Just a short drive from Gananoque, Kingston is a historic city with a vibrant arts and culture scene.

  • Ottawa: The capital city of Canada is located about 200 km from Gananoque and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.

  • Prince Edward County: This wine country region located on Lake Ontario offers beautiful countryside scenery and delicious locally produced wines.

Conclusion

Gananoque is a beautiful town located in Ontario’s Seaway Region. Its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and vibrant cultural scene make it a popular destination for travelers from around the world.
