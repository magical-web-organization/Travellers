Introduction to Brockville

Nestled in Seaway Region in Eastern Ontario, Canada, Brockville is a picturesque destination where stunning natural scenery meets cultural heritage. Known as the “City of the 1000 Islands,” Brockville is home to a wide array of outdoor activities, fascinating museums, and historic landmarks that are sure to delight visitors of all ages. In this travel guide, we’ve compiled a list of the best places to visit, dine, and stay in Brockville - ensuring that your trip to this charming city is as enjoyable and memorable as possible.

List of Places to Visit in Brockville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brockville is a city rich in history and cultural significance. Be sure to visit the following museums and historical places to fully immerse yourself in its fascinating past:

  • The Brockville Museum: Learn about the city’s early history, including its role as a transportation hub during the 19th century, at this fascinating museum. The collections feature over 12,000 artifacts, including photographs, documents, and household items.

  • Fulford Place: This grand estate was constructed during the Gilded Age and features opulent interiors and magnificent gardens. Explore the home of the wealthy Fulford family and witness the extravagant lifestyle of this era.

  • Brockville Tunnel: This railroad tunnel was built in the 1860s and was recently restored into a unique attraction that you can walk through. Discover the history of the tunnel and view the fascinating artwork located inside.

Natural Attractions of Brockville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brockville’s natural beauty is awe-inspiring and one of a kind. Make sure you explore the following natural attractions during your visit:

  • 1000 Islands: Take a boat tour around the 1000 Islands to view its stunning natural beauty. The islands feature a variety of state parks, beaches, and historic sites.

  • Brockville Railway Tunnel: The tunnel is an adventure destination that you can explore on foot. Indulge in the experience of walking through the tunnel.

  • Mac Johnson Wildlife Area: This 532-hectare park is a nature enthusiast’s paradise with marshlands, meadows, and forests. It features activities such as hiking, cycling, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brockville

Brockville is a paradise for those who love exploring nature through hiking and biking. Here are some suggested routes:

  • Brock Trail: This is a 5.6 km multi-use trail that connects historic downtown Brockville to the scenic waterfront. It is also wheelchair accessible.

  • Jones Creek Trail: This trail is a 3 km scenic loop trail that wanders through limestone cliffs and lush forest. Travelers may encounter a variety of birds and wildlife along the way.

  • Charleston Lake Provincial Park: This park offers four hiking circuits, ranging from 1.4 km to 21 km in length. The trails offer scenic views of the lake, diverse wildlife, and geological formations.

Best Things to Do in Brockville: Activities and Tours

Brockville has a plethora of activities and tours that are sure to add excitement to your trip. Don’t miss out on the following:

  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat tour to explore the 1000 Islands and learn about its rich history. Different tours are available for half-day, full day, or even overnight trips.

  • Skywood Eco Adventure Park: An adventure park for those who crave excitement, the Skywood Eco Adventure Park features zip lines, aerial games, and more.

  • Brockville Arts Center: This historic theater hosts a variety of live performances that cater to all genres.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brockville

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for your stay in Brockville that will give you a taste of the city’s rich history and natural beauty:

Day 1: Explore historic downtown Brockville and visit the Brockville Museum.

Day 2: Take a boat tour around the 1000 Islands and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Day 3: Visit the Fulford Place estate and spend some time strolling through their beautiful gardens.

Day 4: Bike the Brock Trail to the scenic waterfront and enjoy the views along the way.

Day 5: Head to Jones Creek Trail and hike the serene loop trail to witness the natural beauty.

Day 6: Visit the Skywood Eco Adventure Park and zip line through the trees.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at Charleston Lake Provincial Park, exploring the scenic trails and wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Brockville

Brockville has an exciting food and drink scene that reflects its diverse culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic, this dish is made with crispy fries topped with gravy and cheese curds.

  • Butter Tarts: A beloved Canadian dessert, this tart is made with a buttery pastry shell filled with a sweet mixture of butter, sugar, and eggs.

  • Fish and Chips: Freshly caught fish dipped in a beer batter then deep-fried until crispy.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Syrup: A quintessential Canadian product that is spread on pancakes, waffles, and other breakfast dishes.

  • Canadian Beer: Brockville has a variety of local breweries that offer a selection of delicious beers. Try the Railway City Brewing Company and pick from their impressive selection of Canadian beers.

Where to Stay in Brockville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brockville has accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some options that you can consider:

  • The Noble Suites: This charming guesthouse is located in the historic downtown district and offers luxurious accommodations.

  • Pine Street Inn B&B: A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Brockville, only a short walk away from the stunning waterfront.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: A modern hotel that offers guests luxurious amenities at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Brockville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking to bring home a keepsake that will remind you of your trip to Brockville? Check out the following local products and souvenirs:

  • Maple Syrup: A Canadian classic product that you can bring home for your loved ones.

  • Handmade Jewellery: There are numerous jewellery makers who create one-of-a-kind handmade jewellery, making it a unique souvenir to bring home.

  • Local Crafts: Whether it’s handmade soaps or artisan candles, you will find several local boutiques selling handmade wares for you to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Brockville

Canada is a land of diverse cultures, and Brockville is no exception. Here are some cultural customs that you may encounter:

  • Politeness: Canadians are known for their politeness and will often apologize even if they are not at fault.

  • Multiculturalism: Brockville is a melting pot of different cultures, and you will see this diversity reflected in the city’s food, festivals, and people.

  • National Holidays: Canada celebrates several national holidays throughout the year, including Labour Day, Thanksgiving, and Canada Day (July 1st).

Best Time to Visit Brockville

The best time to visit Brockville is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warm and the city is alive with festivals and events. However, be aware of the crowded streets during this time. If you prefer cooler weather, consider visiting during the spring or fall when the foliage is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brockville

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Brockville:

Do:

  • Bring comfortable shoes: Brockville is a city that’s perfect for exploring on foot, so bring a pair of sturdy and comfortable shoes.

  • Try Canadian cuisine: From poutine to butter tarts, Brockville has a variety of delicious Canadian dishes that you must try during your visit.

  • Respect the local culture: Canadians are known for their politeness, and it’s important to show respect and appreciation for local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty: Brockville is a city that boasts some of the most stunning natural beauty, and it’s important not to ignore it. Make sure to take it all in and enjoy the views.

  • Forget your manners: Always remember to say “please” and “thank you” - polite manners are highly appreciated in Canadian culture.

  • Assume everyone speaks English: Canada is a bilingual country, so it’s important not to assume that everyone speaks English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brockville

Brockville is a safe city to visit with low crime rates. However, it’s important to take necessary precautions, such as:

  • Locking valuables: Ensure that you lock up your valuables while exploring the city.

  • Observe traffic laws: Pedestrians must obey traffic rules to avoid any accidents.

  • Carry cash securely: When carrying cash, always ensure that it is stored securely.

Health in Brockville

Brockville has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Make sure you have health insurance that covers any medical expenses during your stay. Don’t forget to carry necessary prescriptions and medical documents with you.

Getting to Brockville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brockville is easily accessible by air, road, or rail. Here are some options for transportation to Brockville:

  • Kingston Airport: This is the most convenient airport to Brockville and is well-served by several airlines.

  • Driving: Brockville is well-connected to highways, making it an easy drive from major Canadian cities.

  • Via Rail: Via Rail offers train service that connects Brockville to its surrounding cities.

How to Get Around in Brockville

Brockville is a city that’s perfect for exploring on foot or bike. There are also several car rental agencies available in the city, and public transportation such as buses and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Brockville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brockville is located in a region that’s full of amazing attractions and destinations. Here are some places you can visit next:

  • Thousand Islands: This is a stunning region located a short drive from Brockville, and it offers a variety of natural attractions and outdoor activities.

  • Kingston: This historic city is a popular destination with tourists and is located only a short drive from Brockville.

  • Ottawa: Canada’s capital is located 90 minutes away from Brockville and boasts a rich cultural heritage, outdoor activities, and year-round festivals and events.

Conclusion

Brockville is a city that’s full of surprises and offers something for everyone. From its stunning natural beauty to its rich cultural heritage, there’s never a dull moment in this charming city. So pack your bags, and head to Brockville to experience everything this amazing destination has to offer.
