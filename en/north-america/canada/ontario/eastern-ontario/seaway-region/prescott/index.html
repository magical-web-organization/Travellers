Introduction to Prescott

Perched on the banks of the mighty St. Lawrence River, Prescott is a picturesque town in Eastern Ontario, Canada. Once a bustling trading hub, this charming town is now a popular tourist destination known for its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality.

Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a history buff, or a leisure traveler, there is something for everyone in Prescott. This travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your stay in this charming town. Let’s explore the must-see attractions, activities, local culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Prescott

Here are the top-rated places to visit in Prescott:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Wellington National Historic Site: This well-preserved fort served as a military stronghold during the War of 1812, and it’s now a fascinating museum showcasing the lives of soldiers and civilians during that time period.

  • The Ogdensburg-Prescott International Bridge: This scenic bridge connects the US to Canada and offers stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.

  • The Sandra S. Lawn Harbour and Marina: This bustling marina is a great place to watch the boats sail by while enjoying a meal at one of the nearby restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Prescott: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. Lawrence River: Take a stroll along the shoreline and take in the stunning views of the river and across to the United States.

  • Mac Johnson Wildlife Area: This conservation area is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and beavers. Hike the trails, go birdwatching, or go fishing at one of the ponds.

  • Thousand Islands National Park: Just a short drive from Prescott, this national park has over 80 islands with breathtaking views and amazing recreational opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prescott

Prescott is home to several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the St. Lawrence River and the surrounding countryside. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • St. Lawrence Recreational Trail: This 58 km trail runs from Brockville to Cornwall, following the St. Lawrence River. It’s perfect for bike rides or afternoon hikes.

  • Mac Johnson Wildlife Area Trails: Located on the outskirts of town, this conservation area has several hiking and mountain biking trails suitable for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Prescott: Activities and Tours

From historical tours to scenic cruises, Prescott has a wide range of activities to suit every traveler’s taste:

  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat ride along the St. Lawrence River and explore the Thousand Islands National Park.

  • Ghost Walks: Learn about Prescott’s paranormal past on a guided ghost walk. These walks are available in the evenings and are a must-do for history buffs and ghost enthusiasts.

  • Aquatarium: Located in nearby Brockville, the Aquatarium is an interactive museum showcasing the wonders of the St. Lawrence River and the marine life that calls it home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prescott

If you’re planning to spend a week in Prescott, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Fort Wellington National Historic Site and explore the town center
Day 2 Go on a boat tour of the Thousand Islands or cruise the St. Lawrence River
Day 3 Go on a hiking or biking trail at Mac Johnson Wildlife Area
Day 4 Take a ghost walk and learn about Prescott’s haunted history
Day 5 Visit the Aquatarium in Brockville
Day 6 Explore the nearby town of Gananoque and the Thousand Islands
Day 7 Relax by the St. Lawrence River and enjoy the town’s charm

Food and Drink Scene of Prescott

Prescott’s food and drink scene is a testament to the town’s rich agricultural heritage and cultural diversity. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fries and Gravy: A quintessentially Canadian dish, fries with gravy and cheese curds is a local favourite.

  • Butter Tarts: These sweet, flaky pastries are made with butter, sugar, and eggs, and are a perfect snack or dessert.

  • Fresh Fish: Being located adjacent to the St. Lawrence River provides plenty opportunities for having fresh fish. This local delicacy is available in many of the restaurants in town.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Syrup: Canada is famous for its maple syrup, and Prescott is no exception. Try a delicious pancake breakfast or some maple syrup flavored coffee.

  • Canadian Beer: Sample some of Canada’s best beers, including Moosehead and Labatt’s in town pubs and bars.

Where to Stay in Prescott: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prescott has a variety of accommodation options to suit all travelers’ needs and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in town:

  • The Colonel’s Inn: This elegant 19th-century inn offers comfortable rooms, a hearty breakfast, and warm hospitality.

  • The Blue Heron: This cozy bed and breakfast offer spacious rooms, a picturesque garden, and delicious breakfast options.

  • The Provincial Inn: This family-owned hotel offers clean and spacious rooms and is located within the town’s center.

What to Buy in Prescott: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Prescott is home to several boutiques and gift shops selling unique and handmade souvenirs and local products. Some must-buy items include:

  • Maple Syrup Products
  • Prescott T-Shirts and Memorabilia
  • Local Art and Crafts

Local Culture and Customs of Prescott

Canadians are polite and friendly, and Prescott is no exception. Here are some local customs and culture to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Tipping: Tipping approx 15-20% is the standard in restaurants, salons, and spas.

  • Language: English is the dominant language in Prescott, but many locals also speak French.

  • Dress Code: Prescott is an informal town, so dress according to the weather and the activity you have planned for the day.

Best Time to Visit Prescott

The best time to visit Prescott is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and there are many outdoor events and festivals. Spring and fall also offer pleasant temperatures, breathtaking foliage, and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prescott

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Prescott:

Do:

  • Visit the Fort-Wellington: This national historic site is a must-see and offers a glimpse into the town’s history.

  • Try the Local Cuisine and Beverages: From maple syrup to fresh fish to Canadian beer, Prescott has a vibrant food and drink scene to explore.

  • Respect the Local Environment: Always clean up after yourself and try to be mindful of the impact of your visit on the town and its surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Forget to Tip: Tipping is standard, so make sure to tip your servers and service providers.

  • Break the Law: Be aware of local ordinances and regulations, and observe traffic and parking laws.

  • Be Rude: Canadians are known for their politeness, so it’s important to treat everyone with respect and kindness.

Safety and Health

Prescott is a safe and healthy town to visit, with friendly people and welcoming hospitality. However, travelers should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety and health during their stay.

Safety in Prescott

As with any town, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and to take the necessary precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

  • Avoid Dark and Quiet Streets: Stick to well-lit and populated areas, especially at night.

  • Watch Your Belongings: Don’t leave your belongings unattended and always keep an eye on your valuables.

  • Guard Your Personal Information: Be cautious when sharing personal information with strangers or online.

Health in Prescott

Prescott offers medical facilities and emergency services that cater to travelers’ health needs. It’s always important to have travel insurance that covers any unexpected medical expenses.

Make sure to pack personal medications and wear sunscreen to protect against sunburn.

Getting to Prescott: Transportation Options and Logistics

To get to Prescott, there are several transportation options available:

Airports Near Prescott

  • Ottawa International Airport: Approx 120km away from Prescott.

  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport: Approx 220km away from Prescott.

Bus Stations Near Prescott

  • Greyhound Bus: This long-distance bus service has a stop in town.

  • Megabus: This is another bus service that travels to and from Prescott.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: This is a popular option for those who want the freedom to explore town and its surroundings.

  • Taxi and Car Services: There are taxi and car services available in town for those who do not rent a car.

How to Get Around in Prescott

Prescott is a walkable town with many attractions located within walking distance of each other. There are also taxis and car services available for those who prefer not to walk or ride a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Prescott: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Prescott is surrounded by several scenic and vibrant towns and cities, making it an excellent base for exploring the Eastern Ontario region. Some recommended destinations include:

  • Brockville: A charming town with a rich history, galleries, and museums.

  • Gananoque: This picturesque town is known as the gateway to the Thousand Islands and has plenty of outdoor activities to explore.

  • Kingston: This lively city has a vibrant arts and culture scene, waterfront parks, and historic architecture that mesmerizes its visitors.

Conclusion

Prescott is a charming and culturally rich town that is worth the visit. With its natural beauty, history, and outdoor adventures, there is something for every traveler. Whether you’re seeking tranquility or adventure, Prescott has what it takes to make your visit unique and unforgettable.
