Introduction to Alexandria

Nestled in Eastern Ontario, Alexandria is a small town with a rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. The town’s friendly locals, exceptional food and drink scene, and fantastic outdoor activities make it an ideal destination for travellers.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture in Alexandria, Ontario.

List of Places to Visit in Alexandria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alexandria is home to several significant museums and historical sites that offer an insightful glimpse into the town’s past. The Glengarry Pioneer Museum showcases artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the early settlers in the area, while the Sir John Johnson House National Historic Site provides a fascinating look into the region’s colonial history. If you’re interested in military history, make sure to visit the Canadian War Museum in Ottawa.

Natural Attractions of Alexandria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alexandria isn’t just a destination for history buffs – it’s also a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The town’s natural surroundings are ideal for hiking, cycling, fishing, and kayaking. The Raisin River Conservation Area, the Glengarry Trails, and the Long Sault Parkway are all popular destinations for hiking and biking. Don’t miss the opportunity to take in the stunning panoramic views from the top of the Alexandria Lookout Trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alexandria

One of the best ways to experience Alexandria’s natural beauty is by taking a hike or bike ride through its scenic trails. The Glen Robertson Trail is a popular destination for beginner and intermediate-level bikers, while the 10-kilometre Glengarry Trails offer more challenging terrain for experienced riders. The Alexandria Lookout Trail offers a short but steep hike that rewards visitors with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Alexandria: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for unique experiences in Alexandria, there’s plenty to choose from. History buffs can go on a guided tour of the historic sites or take a ghost walk, while outdoor enthusiasts can go kayaking, fishing, or bird-watching. The town also hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Glengarry Highland Games, the Alexandria Celtic Music Festival, and the Alexandria Food and Wine Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Alexandria

Must-Try Dishes

Alexandria offers a diverse selection of local dishes that are sure to whet your appetite. Make sure to try the poutine, a classic Canadian dish made of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy, at the Alexandria Pizzeria. For a unique culinary experience, head to the Quirky Carrot, a vegan cafe that serves delicious plant-based fried potatoes.

Local Beverages

Ontario is known for its world-class wines, and Alexandria is no exception. Take a tour of the local wineries, or sample the craft beer at the nearby Rurban Brewery. For something stronger, try the locally distilled high-proof spirit, Loon Vodka.

Where to Stay in Alexandria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alexandria has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious. The Bonnie Glen Pavilion is a popular destination for group events, while the Brigadoon Country Inn provides a more upscale experience. For those on a budget, the Alexandria Inn and Suites and the Quail Hollow Motor Inn both offer comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Alexandria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Alexandria, make sure to bring home some unique souvenirs and local products. The Alexandria Farmers’ Market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal crafts, while the Queen’s Bakery is famous for its traditional Scottish shortbread. For a more unique gift, head to the Glengarry Encore Store, which sells handmade quilts and crafts produced by local seniors.

Local Culture and Customs of Alexandria

The town of Alexandria is proud of its Scottish heritage, which is reflected in the town’s architecture, events, and culture. Visitors can experience this culture firsthand at the Glengarry Highland Games or the Alexandria Celtic Music Festival. It’s important to note that Canada is a multicultural country that values diversity and inclusivity.

Best Time to Visit Alexandria

The best time to visit Alexandria is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and the town’s many outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re a fan of winter sports, you’ll want to visit between December and March, when the nearby ski resorts are open for business.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alexandria

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture
  • Bring appropriate outdoor gear if you plan to hike, bike, or kayak
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious or cultural landmarks
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs

Safety and Health

Safety in Alexandria

Alexandria is a generally safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid theft or other types of petty crime. Keep your valuables in a secure location, and be mindful of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Alexandria

It’s always a good idea to travel with medical insurance when visiting a foreign country. There are several medical facilities in Alexandria, including the Glengarry Memorial Hospital and the Alexandria Family Health Team.

Getting to Alexandria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alexandria

The closest major airport to Alexandria is Ottawa International Airport, which is about an hour and a half drive away. Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport is also within driving distance.

Bus Stations Near Alexandria

The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Cornwall, about 40 minutes southwest of Alexandria.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Alexandria or take a train to neighboring towns, such as Lancaster or Maxville.

How to Get Around in Alexandria

For visitors without a car, there are several taxi services available in Alexandria. However, due to the town’s size and pedestrian-friendly layout, many visitors choose to walk or bike to their destinations.

Where to Go Next from Alexandria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Eastern Ontario, there are several nearby towns and attractions worth visiting. Take a day trip to the Thousand Islands, a series of islands located in the St. Lawrence River, or explore the beautiful city of Kingston. Ottawa, the capital of Canada, is also within driving distance and offers plenty of cultural attractions, such as the National Gallery of Canada and the Canadian Museum of History.

Conclusion

Alexandria is a hidden gem in Eastern Ontario, with plenty of attractions and activities to keep visitors busy. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or delicious food and drink, Alexandria has something for everyone. Come and experience the town’s warmth and hospitality for yourself!
