Introduction to Perth

Perth is a charming town located in the Ottawa-Rideau region of Eastern Ontario, Canada. Known for its rich history, beautiful parks, and thriving arts and culture scene, Perth is a popular destination for tourists from Canada and beyond. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking to relax and unwind, Perth has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Perth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perth has a rich and storied history that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. Some top attractions include:

  • Perth Museum: This excellent museum provides an in-depth look at the history of Perth and features exhibits on the town’s early settlers, its growth during the 19th century, and its thriving arts and culture scene.
  • Matheson House Museum: Located in a beautifully restored 1840s home, this museum offers a glimpse into the lives of Perth’s early settlers. Visitors can tour the historic home and learn about the period furnishings and artifacts on display.
  • Stewart Park: This lovely park is home to the Perth and District Union Public Library, which houses a collection of over 70,000 books and other materials. Visitors can also explore the park’s beautiful gardens, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

Natural Attractions of Perth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perth is surrounded by some of the most beautiful natural areas in Ontario, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions in Perth:

  • Rideau Trail: This 387-kilometer trail stretches from Kingston to Ottawa and passes through Perth. Hikers can enjoy stunning views of the Rideau Canal, old-growth forests, and abundant wildlife.
  • Murphy’s Point Provincial Park: Located just outside of Perth, Murphy’s Point Provincial Park offers over 1,200 hectares of beautiful wilderness to explore. Visitors can hike, fish, camp, and swim in the park’s crystal-clear lakes.
  • Balderson Village Cheese: This local cheese factory offers tours and tastings of their delicious artisanal cheeses. Visitors can also browse the factory’s store, which sells locally made jams, jellies, and honey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perth

Perth and the surrounding area offer some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ontario. Here are some of the top trails:

  • Rideau Trail: As mentioned earlier, the Rideau Trail is over 387 kilometers long and passes through Perth. It is an excellent option for hikers and bikers looking to explore the area’s natural beauty.
  • The Great Trail: This trail is the longest recreational trail in the world, stretching over 24,000 kilometers. It passes through Perth and the surrounding area and offers stunning views of the Rideau Canal and the town’s historic buildings.
  • Perth Wildlife Reserve Trail: This easy, 2-kilometer trail offers stunning views of the surrounding wetlands and is a great place for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Perth: Activities and Tours

Perth is a great destination for visitors looking for fun and engaging activities. Here are some of the top things to do in Perth:

  • Experience the Rideau Canal: The Rideau Canal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a must-see attraction for visitors to Perth. Take a boat tour, rent a kayak or stand-up paddleboard, or simply relax by the water and take in the stunning views.
  • Explore the Local Arts Scene: Perth is home to a thriving arts community, with galleries, theaters, and performance spaces throughout the town. Be sure to check out the Foster Museum, which showcases the work of local and regional artists.
  • Take a Golfing Tour: Perth is home to some of the best golf courses in Ontario, making it the perfect destination for golf enthusiasts. Take a tour of the area’s top courses, including the Perth Golf Course and the Timber Run Golf Club.

Food and Drink Scene of Perth

Must-Try Dishes

Perth is known for its delicious food, with local chefs and restaurateurs using the freshest ingredients to create innovative and flavorful dishes. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Maple Syrup Pie: This delicious dessert is a Canadian staple and is made with pure maple syrup, cream, and butter. Be sure to try it at one of Perth’s many bakeries and cafes.
  • Tourtière: This savory meat pie is made with beef, pork, and spices and is a traditional Quebecois dish. Try it at the Stone Cellar Restaurant or any of the town’s other French-inspired eateries.
  • Poutine: This classic Canadian dish consists of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy. Perth has no shortage of poutine joints, so be sure to try it at least once while you’re in town.

Local Beverages

Perth is also known for its delicious craft beers, ciders, and wines. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Perth Brewery: Located in the heart of the town, this brewery offers a range of craft beers made on-site. Try their flagship beer, the Bonfire Black IPA, or any of their seasonal offerings.
  • Top Shelf Distillers: This local distillery creates handcrafted spirits using locally sourced ingredients. Try their award-winning moonshine or their signature gin and tonic.
  • Fieldwork Brewing Company: Located just outside of Perth, this brewery specializes in small-batch craft beers made using traditional brewing techniques. Be sure to try their popular Belgian Dubbel or their smooth and creamy Chocolate Milk Stout.

Where to Stay in Perth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perth has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Best Western Plus Perth Parkside Inn & Spa: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of Perth and offers a range of rooms and suites, many with stunning views of the town and surrounding area. Guests can also take advantage of the hotel’s full-service spa and restaurant.
  • Fiddlehead Resort: This cozy, family-friendly resort is located just outside of Perth and offers a range of rooms, cottages, and cabins. Guests can enjoy the resort’s private beach, walking trails, and fire pits.
  • Perth Manor Boutique Hotel: This historic hotel is located in a beautifully restored 1840s mansion and features elegant rooms and suites with period furnishings and modern amenities. Guests can also enjoy the hotel’s on-site restaurant and intimate bar.

What to Buy in Perth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perth is home to a range of specialty shops, boutiques, and farmers’ markets, making it the perfect destination for souvenir shopping. Here are some great local products to bring home:

  • Maple Syrup: Canadian maple syrup is world-famous, and Perth has no shortage of places to buy it. Look for local, organic products at the Perth Farmers’ Market or at Stewart Park.
  • Local Crafts and Artwork: Perth is home to a thriving arts and crafts community, and visitors can find unique handmade items at specialty shops and galleries throughout the town.
  • Antiques and Vintage Finds: Perth is a historic town, and visitors can find a range of antiques and vintage items at shops like Vintage 7 and The Book Nook and Other Treasures.

Local Culture and Customs of Perth

Perth is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community and pride in its heritage. Visitors should respect local customs and take care to preserve the town’s historic buildings and natural areas.

Best Time to Visit Perth

The best time to visit Perth is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perth

Do:

  • Do explore downtown Perth and take in the town’s historic architecture and beautiful parks.
  • Do take a boat tour of the Rideau Canal and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • Do try local specialties like maple syrup pie, tourtière, and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage local natural areas or historic buildings.
  • Don’t park in prohibited areas or ignore local parking regulations.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing for outdoor activities and changing weather conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perth

Perth is generally a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take care to follow common sense safety precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, lock your doors and windows at night, and avoid walking alone after dark.

Health in Perth

Perth has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Perth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perth is located approximately 80 kilometers southwest of Ottawa and can be reached by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Perth

The nearest international airports to Perth are Ottawa Macdonald–Cartier International Airport (YOW) and Kingston/Norman Rogers Airport (YGK).

Bus Stations Near Perth

The nearest bus stations to Perth are located in Ottawa and Kingston and are serviced by Greyhound and other major bus companies.

Other Options

Perth is also easily accessible by car, with major highways and scenic routes connecting it to nearby cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Perth

Perth is a small town that is easily walkable and bikeable. Visitors can also take advantage of the town’s public transit system or rent a car or bike to explore the area at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Perth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perth is located in the beautiful Ottawa-Rideau region of Eastern Ontario, making it the perfect gateway to explore some of the area’s other top destinations. Here are some nearby excursions to consider:

  • Ottawa: Canada’s capital city is just a short drive from Perth and offers a range of museums, galleries, and attractions, including the Parliament Buildings, the National Gallery of Canada, and the ByWard Market.
  • Kingston: Located on the shores of Lake Ontario, Kingston is known for its historic downtown, beautiful parks, and thriving arts scene.
  • Thousand Islands: Take a scenic boat tour of this beautiful archipelago, which is home to over 1,800 islands and boasts stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.

Conclusion

Perth, Canada is a charming and historic town that is the perfect destination for visitors looking to explore the natural beauty and rich culture of Eastern Ontario. With its stunning outdoor attractions, delicious food and drink scene, and range of accommodation options, Perth has something for everyone.
