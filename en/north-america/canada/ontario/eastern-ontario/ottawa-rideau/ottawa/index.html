Introduction to Ottawa

As the capital city of Canada, Ottawa is a vibrant destination that offers an array of activities, attractions, and experiences for visitors to enjoy. From outdoor adventures and scenic vistas to world-class museums and cultural events, there’s something for everyone in this exciting Canadian city.

List of Places to Visit in Ottawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ottawa has a rich cultural and historical heritage, and there are many landmarks and attractions that reflect this. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Ottawa include:

  • Canadian Museum of History: This award-winning museum located in Gatineau, just across the river from Ottawa, is the most-visited museum in all of Canada. Among its exhibits is the world’s largest indoor collection of totem poles, as well as a permanent exhibit on Canadian history.
  • Canadian War Museum: This museum tells the story of Canada’s military history, from the early battles fought by Indigenous peoples and French and English settlers to modern-day peacekeeping missions.
  • Parliament Hill: This iconic attraction offers informative tours of Canada’s parliament buildings and stunning views of the Ottawa River and Gatineau Hills.

Natural Attractions of Ottawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re the type of traveller who loves outdoor adventures and scenic vistas, Ottawa will not disappoint. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the city:

  • Rideau Canal: In winter, the Rideau Canal becomes the world’s largest skating rink, while in summer you can take a scenic boat tour along its 47 locks, many of which are still operated by hand.
  • Gatineau Park: Just across the river from Ottawa, Gatineau Park offers beautiful hiking and biking trails, scenic vistas, and opportunities for swimming, boating, and picnicking.
  • Mackenzie King Estate: This estate, once owned by former Canadian Prime Minister William Lyon Mackenzie King, offers beautiful manicured gardens, hiking trails, and a unique look into the life of one of Canada’s most influential leaders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ottawa

Ottawa is among the best cities in Canada for outdoor enthusiasts, with a wide range of trails and parks that are suitable for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • Greenbelt Trail: This 31-kilometre trail starts at the Ottawa River and takes you through the city’s beautiful green spaces and natural areas.
  • Rideau River Eastern Pathway: This 10-kilometre path runs along the Rideau River and offers scenic views of the waterfalls, bridges, and greenery that dot the landscape.
  • Gatineau Park Trails: With over 165 kilometres of trails, Gatineau Park offers something for hikers and bikers of all levels. Take a leisurely stroll along the Pink Lake Trail or challenge yourself with the strenuous King Mountain Trail.

Best Things to Do in Ottawa: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun and exciting things to do in Ottawa, no matter what your interests may be. Here are some of the top activities and tours to check out:

  • ByWard Market: This bustling farmer’s market is the perfect place to sample local food and drink, shop for unique souvenirs, and take in some live music.
  • Ottawa River Cruise: Take a scenic boat tour along the Ottawa River and enjoy stunning views of the city from the water.
  • National Gallery of Canada: This must-visit museum houses a vast collection of Canadian and international art, including works by the Group of Seven, Emily Carr, and other famous Canadian artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ottawa

If you’re planning to spend a week in Ottawa, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Ottawa and explore the downtown area, including Parliament Hill, ByWard Market, and the National Gallery of Canada.
  • Day 2: Take a leisurely walk or bike ride along the Rideau Canal and visit the Canadian Museum of History in Gatineau.
  • Day 3: Spend the day hiking or biking in Gatineau Park.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of the Canadian War Museum and spend the afternoon exploring some of the lesser-known museums and historical sites in Ottawa.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Merrickville, a charming village that offers quaint shops, restaurants, and a picturesque canal.
  • Day 6: Explore the local food and drink scene, visiting some of the city’s best restaurants and microbreweries.
  • Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a scenic boat tour along the Ottawa River, taking in the city’s stunning scenery from the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Ottawa

No trip to Ottawa is complete without sampling some of the city’s delicious, locally sourced food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BeaverTails: These unique pastries are shaped like a beaver’s tail and coated in cinnamon and sugar.
  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish made with french fries, gravy, and cheese curds.
  • Maple-syrup flavoured everything: Ottawa’s food scene is obsessed with maple syrup, and you’ll find it in everything from cocktails to desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Ottawa is home to a thriving craft beer scene, with over two dozen microbreweries and taprooms that offer unique and delicious brews.
  • Apple cider: The Ottawa Valley is famous for its apple orchards, and many local producers offer delicious and refreshing apple ciders that are perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Ottawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ottawa is a cosmopolitan city with a wide range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city:

  • Fairmont Chateau Laurier: This iconic hotel, located just steps from Parliament Hill, offers luxurious rooms, a world-class spa, and stunning views of the Ottawa River.
  • HI Ottawa Jail Hostel: This unique hostel, located in a converted 19th-century jailhouse, offers a budget-friendly option for travellers who want to stay in the heart of downtown Ottawa.
  • Swiss Hotel: This charming guesthouse located in the trendy ByWard Market neighbourhood offers comfortable rooms, a cosy lounge, and friendly service.

What to Buy in Ottawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and locally made products to bring home from your trip to Ottawa, here are some of the top options:

  • Maple syrup: Ottawa’s obsession with maple syrup means you’ll find it in everything from candy to barbecue sauce.
  • Handmade crafts: The ByWard Market is a great place to find locally made jewellery, pottery, and other crafts.
  • Canadian whisky: Ottawa and the surrounding area is home to several world-class whisky distilleries, including Crown Royal and J.P. Wiser’s.

Local Culture and Customs of Ottawa

Ottawa is a cosmopolitan and diverse city, and visitors are sure to notice the influence of Canada’s Indigenous peoples, French and English settlers, and other cultural groups. Some important customs and etiquette to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for Canada’s Indigenous peoples: Whether you’re taking part in a cultural activity or simply visiting a museum, it’s important to show respect for Canada’s Indigenous peoples and their rich cultural heritage.
  • Language: Ottawa is a bilingual city, with both English and French widely spoken. It’s always a good idea to learn a few basic phrases in both languages before your trip.
  • Tipping: As with most of Canada, tipping is common in Ottawa’s restaurants, cafes, and bars. A 15-20% tip is standard.

Best Time to Visit Ottawa

The best time to visit Ottawa depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is the most popular time, with warm weather and a busy schedule of festivals and events. Fall is a great time to visit if you love foliage and outdoor adventures, while winter offers a magical holiday atmosphere and the opportunity to skate on the Rideau Canal or hit the slopes in Gatineau Park. Spring is a quieter time, with fewer crowds and milder weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ottawa

To make the most of your visit to Ottawa, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Ottawa is famous for its natural beauty, so take advantage of the many parks, hiking trails, and outdoor adventures available.
  • Try the local cuisine: From poutine to craft beer, Ottawa offers some unique and delicious food and drink options that you won’t find anywhere else.
  • Respect the culture and heritage: From Canada’s Indigenous peoples to its French and English settlers, Ottawa has a rich cultural heritage that is worth exploring and respecting.

Don’t:

  • Forget your coins: Many of Ottawa’s public amenities, including public washrooms, require a small fee in coins.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English: While English is widely spoken in Ottawa, it’s always a good idea to learn a few basic French phrases and show respect for the city’s bilingual culture.
  • Underestimate the weather: Ottawa is known for its cold and snowy winters, so make sure to pack appropriately if you’re visiting during this time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ottawa

Ottawa is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe, especially at night and in less populated areas. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Stay alert and aware of your surroundings at all times, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night: Whenever possible, travel with a companion or group and avoid walking alone after dark.
  • Take precautions against pickpocketing: As with any city, pickpocketing can be a problem, so keep your valuables secure and never leave them unattended.

Health in Ottawa

Ottawa is a healthy city with plenty of medical facilities and healthcare services. Here are some important health tips for visitors:

  • Stay hydrated: Ottawa’s weather can be hot and dry during the summer months, so make sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  • Watch out for ticks: If you’re spending time in Ottawa’s parks and natural areas, be aware of the risk of tick bites and take precautions to avoid them.
  • Be prepared for the cold: Ottawa’s winters can be harsh and cold, so make sure to dress appropriately and be prepared for the weather.

Getting to Ottawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ottawa

There are several airports near Ottawa, including:

  • Ottawa International Airport: Located just south of downtown Ottawa, this is the city’s main airport and offers flights to destinations across Canada and the world.
  • Montréal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport: Located less than two hours from Ottawa by car, Montreal is the largest airport in Quebec and offers flights to destinations across North America and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Ottawa

Ottawa is well-served by intercity bus services, with several stations located throughout the city. Some of the most popular stations include:

  • Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Ottawa, this station offers service to destinations across Canada and the United States.
  • Ottawa Central Station: This station located near the city centre offers services to cities across Ontario and Quebec.

Other Options

If you’re traveling to Ottawa by car, the city is well-connected to several major highways, including Highway 417, which runs through the heart of downtown Ottawa.

How to Get Around in Ottawa

Ottawa is a very walkable city, and many of the top attractions are located within easy walking distance of each other. If you prefer to use public transportation, Ottawa’s bus and light rail system is clean, safe, and efficient, with fares starting at just $3.55 for a single ride. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also widely available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Ottawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Ontario or Eastern Canada, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to check out. Some of the top options include:

  • Montreal: Just a two-hour drive from Ottawa, Montreal is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city with an array of cultural attractions, world-class cuisine, and exciting nightlife.
  • Toronto: Located about four hours from Ottawa by car, Toronto is Canada’s largest city and offers everything from world-class museums and galleries to iconic landmarks like the CN Tower and the Toronto Islands.
  • Thousand Islands: This beautiful region on the border of Ontario and New York State is known for its scenic beauty, crystal-clear waters, and charming small towns and villages.

Conclusion

Ottawa is an exciting and diverse city that offers an array of activities, attractions, and experiences for visitors to enjoy. With its incredible natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and lively food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in this amazing Canadian city. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, or just a relaxing getaway, Ottawa should definitely be on your travel bucket list.
