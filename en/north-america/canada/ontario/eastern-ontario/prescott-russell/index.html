Introduction to Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell, located in Eastern Ontario, is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by tourists. It is home to a vast array of cultural and natural attractions that are worth exploring. This region is known for its charming small towns, scenic countryside, and friendly locals. In this guide, we will dive into the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Prescott-Russell.

List of Places to Visit in Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell has something for everyone, whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to visit in this region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Champlain Trail Museum and Pioneer Village - located in Vankleek Hill, this museum tells the story of the pioneers who settled in this region.

  • Famous Five Monument - located in Crysler, this monument honors the five women who fought for women’s rights in the early 20th century.

  • L’Orignal Old Jail - located in L’Orignal, this historic jail has been converted into a museum and is a great place to learn about the region’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Prescott-Russell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Larose Forest - located in Bourget, this forest boasts over 10,000 acres of hiking and biking trails, as well as cross-country skiing and snowshoeing in the winter.

  • Alfred Bog Boardwalk - located in Alfred, this boardwalk takes visitors through a unique wetland ecosystem that is home to rare plant and animal species.

  • Calypso Water Park - located in Limoges, this water park is perfect for families and offers a variety of water slides, pools, and attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell has an abundance of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Prescott-Russell Recreational Trail - this 72-kilometer trail stretches from St. Eugene to Hammond and is perfect for cycling, running, or walking.

  • Pine Grove Trail - located in L’Orignal, this trail is a 2.5-kilometer loop that takes visitors through a peaceful forest setting.

  • Ski Heritage East - located in Plantagenet, this trail network offers over 30 kilometers of groomed cross-country skiing trails in the winter.

Best Things to Do in Prescott-Russell: Activities and Tours

From art galleries to vineyards, there is no shortage of things to do in Prescott-Russell. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Domaine Perrault Vineyard - located in Navan, this winery offers tastings, tours, and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Art Gallery - located in Vankleek Hill, this gallery showcases artwork by local and regional artists.

  • Tactical Tours - located in St. Isidore, this tour company offers ATV tours of the region’s beautiful countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prescott-Russell

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Prescott-Russell, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Champlain Trail Museum and Pioneer Village
2 Hike the Pine Grove Trail and enjoy lunch in L’Orignal
3 Spend the day at Calypso Water Park

5 Take a Tactical Tours ATV tour

Food and Drink Scene of Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell has a thriving food and drink scene that showcases the region’s agricultural and culinary offerings. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine - a Canadian classic, this dish consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

  • Tourtière - a meat pie that is a staple of Quebec cuisine.

  • Maple Syrup - a sweet and delicious syrup that is harvested from maple trees in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Beau’s Beer - a craft brewery located in Vankleek Hill that produces a variety of award-winning beers.

  • Vignoble Clos du Vully - a vineyard located in Cheney that produces excellent wines using grapes grown in the region.

  • Artisanal Cider - several cideries in the region produce delicious ciders using local apples.

Where to Stay in Prescott-Russell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prescott-Russell has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top choices:

  • Russell Manor Bed and Breakfast - located in Russell, this charming bed and breakfast has beautifully appointed rooms and a delicious breakfast.

  • Microtel Inn and Suites by Wyndham Casselman - located in Casselman, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a fitness center.

  • Auberge des Gallant - located in Ste. Marthe, this luxurious guesthouse offers spa services and gourmet dining.

What to Buy in Prescott-Russell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Prescott-Russell is known for its agricultural products, so be sure to bring home some local food and drink as souvenirs. Some other options include:

  • Maple Syrup Products - from candy to pancake mix, there are plenty of maple syrup-themed products to choose from.

  • Pottery - several local artists create beautiful pottery that makes for a unique souvenir.

  • Honey - there are several apiaries in the region that produce delicious honey and honey products.

Local Culture and Customs of Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell has a rich Franco-Ontarian culture that is reflected in the region’s language, music, and cuisine. Many local festivals celebrate this unique culture, including the Hawkesbury Winter Carnival and the St. Isidore Carnival.

Best Time to Visit Prescott-Russell

The best time to visit Prescott-Russell is during the summer, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The fall is also a beautiful time to visit, as the foliage changes color and farmers’ markets are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prescott-Russell

To make the most of your visit to Prescott-Russell, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Take advantage of the regional hiking and biking trails.

  • Attend a local festival or event.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the importance of insect repellent during the summer months.

  • Assume that everyone in the region speaks English.

  • Overlook the small towns in the region, as they often have the most charm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell is a safe destination, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions when exploring the region’s natural areas, such as hiking with a partner and carrying a map and compass.

Health in Prescott-Russell

Prescott-Russell has several medical clinics and hospitals, as well as pharmacies in most towns. It’s a good idea to bring any necessary medications and health insurance information with you when traveling to the region.

Getting to Prescott-Russell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prescott-Russell

The closest airports to Prescott-Russell are Ottawa International Airport and Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Prescott-Russell

Greyhound Canada and Ontario Northland offer bus services to several towns in the region.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option for exploring the region at your own pace. Taxis are also available in most towns. Additionally, several cycling tours operate in the region for those interested in exploring by bike.

How to Get Around in Prescott-Russell

Public transportation is limited in Prescott-Russell, so renting a car or bringing your own vehicle is the best option for getting around. Several bike rental companies also operate in the region for those interested in cycling.

Where to Go Next from Prescott-Russell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Prescott-Russell is located within driving distance of several other attractions in Ontario, Quebec, and the United States. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Ottawa - the capital of Canada, located only an hour’s drive west of Prescott-Russell.

  • Montreal - a vibrant city with a rich history and culture, located only a couple of hours’ drive east of Prescott-Russell.

  • Adirondack Mountains - located a few hours’ drive south of Prescott-Russell, this mountain range is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Prescott-Russell is a wonderful destination for those looking to explore the natural beauty and unique culture of Eastern Ontario. With its range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine, this region has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover all that Prescott-Russell has to offer.
