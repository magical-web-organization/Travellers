Introduction to Pembroke

Pembroke is a charming city located in Ottawa Valley, in Eastern Ontario, Canada. Known for its picturesque landscapes, outdoor adventures, and rich culture, Pembroke is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Pembroke

Pembroke has a diverse range of attractions that cater to every type of traveler. Here are some of the must-visit places in Pembroke:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pembroke Heritage Murals - Explore the city’s history through its street art and murals. This outdoor exhibit showcases more than 30 murals depicting the city’s culture and heritage.

  • Champlain Trail Museum and Pioneer Village - This museum takes visitors on a journey through Canada’s history, featuring exhibits that showcase the region’s pioneer life and First Nations culture.

  • Pembroke Shire Hall and Courthouse - This historic building was once the seat of county government and has now been converted into a museum showcasing the area’s judicial history.

Natural Attractions of Pembroke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Algonquin Provincial Park - Just a short drive away, this park offers visitors a chance to explore the Canadian wilderness, with hiking, biking, camping, and canoeing opportunities.

  • Petawawa Terrace Provincial Park - This park is famous for its scenic views and hiking trails, offering visitors a chance to experience the region’s natural beauty.

  • Whitewater Adventures - This adventure company offers rafting and kayaking experiences through the beautiful waters of the Ottawa River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pembroke

Pembroke offers visitors a chance to explore the region’s natural beauty through its countless hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  • High Falls Trail - This trail features stunning views of the surrounding landscape, culminating in a magnificent waterfall.

  • Ottawa Valley Rail Trail - This trail runs along the Bonnechere River, offering visitors a chance to explore the region’s beautiful landscape.

  • Cave Lake Trail - This hiking trail features breathtaking views of the area’s lakes and valleys, making it a must-do for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Pembroke: Activities and Tours

Besides the above-mentioned places, there are many other exciting things to do in Pembroke. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Zip Line Adventure - Experience the thrill of zip lining through the forest canopy with Wilderness Tours.

  • Ottawa River Tours - Take a scenic boat tour through the beautiful Ottawa River with OWL Rafting Adventures.

  • Horseback Riding - Explore the region’s natural beauty on horseback with Pembroke Equestrian Center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pembroke

For those planning a week-long trip to Pembroke, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Visit the Champlain Trail Museum and Pioneer Village

Food and Drink Scene of Pembroke

Pembroke offers visitors a chance to experience its unique cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine - A classic Canadian dish, it consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

  • BeaverTails - A pastry that is shaped like a beaver’s tail, covered in cinnamon sugar, and served warm.

  • Freshwater Fish - Pembroke is famous for its fresh fish, including trout and bass.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Explore the local breweries and sample some of their unique craft beers.

  • Maple Syrup - Made from locally-sourced maple trees, this syrup is a Pembroke specialty and is used in a variety of dishes.

Where to Stay in Pembroke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pembroke offers a wide range of accommodation options to cater to every type of traveler. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Best Western Pembroke Inn & Conference Center - This hotel offers guests comfortable rooms and modern amenities, including a fitness center and indoor pool.

  • Days Inn & Conference Center by Wyndham Pembroke - This hotel offers guests comfortable rooms with free Wi-Fi and an onsite restaurant.

  • Bob’s Lake Cottages - Located on the shores of Bob’s Lake, this guesthouse offers visitors a chance to experience the region’s natural beauty while enjoying comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Pembroke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pembroke offers visitors a chance to take home some unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Maple Syrup - Made from locally-sourced maple trees, this syrup is a Pembroke specialty and is the perfect gift for foodies.

  • Pembroke Heritage Murals Merchandise - Bring home a piece of the Pembroke Heritage Murals exhibit with some souvenirs and memorabilia.

  • Local Crafts and Artwork - Pembroke is home to a thriving art and craft scene, and visitors can find unique handmade items at the local markets and stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Pembroke

Pembroke is known for its friendly locals and rich culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the Outdoors - Pembroke is famous for its natural beauty, and visitors are encouraged to respect the environment by not littering and leaving no trace behind.

  • Learn about the Culture - Pembroke has a rich First Nations culture and history, and visitors are encouraged to learn more about it by visiting the museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Pembroke

Pembroke is a year-round destination, with each season offering a unique experience. The summer months of June to August are popular among tourists, but the fall months of September and October offer stunning foliage views. Winter sports enthusiasts can enjoy skiing and snowboarding from December to March, and spring brings wildflowers and hiking opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pembroke

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts of visiting Pembroke:

  • Explore the Outdoors - Pembroke is known for its natural beauty, so make sure to explore it by hiking or biking on the trails.

Don’t:

Getting to Pembroke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pembroke is well-connected by air, rail, and road. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Pembroke

  • Ottawa International Airport - Located about 150 km away, this airport offers direct flights to several cities in Canada and the USA.

  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport - Located about 305 km away, this airport offers international flights to several destinations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Pembroke

  • Greyhound Pembroke - This bus station is located in the heart of the city and offers several routes to major cities in Canada.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals - Several car rental companies operate in Pembroke, offering visitors a chance to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in Pembroke

Pembroke is a small city, and most of the popular attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors can also use local taxis or car rentals to get around.

Where to Go Next from Pembroke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pembroke is located in a beautiful region of Canada, with several nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Ottawa - Located about 150 km away, Canada’s capital city is filled with historic sites, museums, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Montreal - Located about 305 km away, Montreal is a city known for its European charm, cultural events, and iconic landmarks.

Conclusion

Pembroke has something for every type of traveler, from its natural beauty and outdoor adventures to its rich culture and history. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Pembroke, and we look forward to welcoming you to our city.
