Renfrew Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tourist Guide to Renfrew, Ontario

Renfrew is a small town located in the Ottawa Valley, in Eastern Ontario, Canada. It’s well known for being a hub for outdoor adventure, with a variety of both natural and man-made attractions to explore. From hiking trails and scenic drives to museums and historical sites, Renfrew has something for everyone.

Introduction to Renfrew

Located just over an hour’s drive from Ottawa, Renfrew is a charming town known for its outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. With a population of just over 8,000 people, Renfrew still manages to offer visitors a wide range of things to see and do.

List of Places to Visit in Renfrew

Renfrew boasts plenty of tourist attractions, from culturally important museums and historical sites to natural wonders. Here are a few:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The O’Brien Theatre: This restored 1930s theatre is a must-visit for movie-lovers and history buffs.

  • Renfrew Museum: This museum chronicles the history of the town, with exhibits on the lumber industry, local businesses, and more.

  • Swinging Bridge: This pedestrian bridge links Renfrew with McDougall Mill Museum and is a popular spot for taking pictures and viewing the river.

Natural Attractions of Renfrew: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bonnie View Inn: For a taste of natural scenery, take in the view of one of the area’s more popular lakes from the comfortable confines of the Bonnie View Inn.

  • Finnegan’s Quietly Resting Place Trail: This 1-kilometer trail offers visitors a peaceful, nature-filled stroll with lovely views.

  • Logos Land Resort: In the summer, families flock to this fun-filled water park for sun, splash, and family adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Renfrew

Hikers and bikers, rejoice! Renfrew offers some of the best trails in the Ottawa Valley for getting out and enjoying the great outdoors. Whether you’re looking for a quick afternoon trek or an epic, multi-day adventure, there’s something for everyone.

  • Ardslignish Loop Trail: This 3.2-kilometer trail offers stunning views of the Bonnechere River, with an easy hike perfect for beginners.

  • Bonnechere River Waterway Provincial Park: With over 46 kilometers of trails winding through pristine forests, this park is a favorite among adventurers.

Best Things to Do in Renfrew: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more to do in Renfrew beyond its attractions and natural landmarks, there are plenty of activities and tours suitable for all ages.

  • Renfrew Million Dollar Adventure Challenge: This scavenger hunt-style challenge takes visitors on a self-guided tour of the town’s attractions and landmarks.

  • RiverRun Whitewater Rafting: For an adrenaline-pumping experience, try white water rafting down the Ottawa River.

Food and Drink Scene of Renfrew

Renfrew is known for its local cuisine and food scene, offering visitors a unique taste of Ontario’s culinary culture.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This classic Canadian dish consists of French fries, gravy, and cheese curds, and is a staple of the Renfrew area.

  • Maple Syrup: Ontario is the largest producer of maple syrup in the world, and Renfrew is home to many maple syrup producers.

Local Beverages

  • Whitewater Brewing Company: This local brewery has gained national recognition for producing some of the best craft beers in Ontario.

Where to Stay in Renfrew: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast style dwelling or a larger hotel, there are plenty of options in and around Renfrew.

  • Renfrew Inn: Located in the heart of the town, the Renfrew Inn is a cozy spot offering easy access to Renfrew’s attractions.

  • Bonnechere Lodge: For a more upscale experience, try the Bonnechere Lodge. This renovated historical lodge is located just a short drive from the town centre.

What to Buy in Renfrew: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Renfrew would be complete without taking home a few mementoes to remember your visit.

  • Maple Syrup: As mentioned earlier, Renfrew is known for its maple syrup production, so be sure to bring some home with you.

  • Handmade Crafts: There are plenty of artists and artisans living and working in the Renfrew area, so keep an eye out for local handmade crafts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Renfrew

Renfrew is a small but vibrant town, with a strong sense of community and history. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the area’s past and appreciate its natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Renfrew

Renfrew is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August). During this time, the weather is mild and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Renfrew

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the town’s rich history and cultural attractions.

  • Take photos on the Swinging Bridge.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any natural or historical landmarks.

  • Ignore basic safety precautions while enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Renfrew is generally a safe and welcoming place, but visitors should take care to follow basic safety precautions when exploring the area.

Safety in Renfrew

  • Always carry a map or GPS device when hiking or biking on Renfrew’s trails.

  • Take care when swimming or boating in local rivers and lakes.

Health in Renfrew

  • Tap water is safe to drink in Renfrew, but it’s always a good idea to bring bottled water or a refillable water bottle with a filter.

Getting to Renfrew: Transportation Options and Logistics

Renfrew is located just over an hour’s drive from Ottawa, with a variety of transportation options available to visitors.

Airports Near Renfrew

  • Ottawa International Airport

Bus Stations Near Renfrew

  • Renfrew Bus Station

Other Options

  • Car rental is available at the airport and in nearby towns.

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Renfrew

Visitors can get around Renfrew by car, bike, or on foot. Biking and walking trails are plentiful, and there are several car rental agencies in the area.

Where to Go Next from Renfrew: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Renfrew, visitors may want to check out nearby destinations such as:

  • Ottawa: Canada’s capital city is just over an hour’s drive from Renfrew, with plenty of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy.

  • Algonquin Provincial Park: This breathtaking park is home to waterfalls, lakes, and over 1,200 kilometers of hiking trails.

Conclusion

Renfrew is a hidden gem in the Ottawa Valley, offering visitors a wide range of attractions and outdoor activities. From hiking and biking to museums and historical landmarks, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
