Introduction to Calabogie

Calabogie is a picturesque community located in Ottawa Valley, Eastern Ontario, Canada. This charming town is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts who want to experience rugged natural beauty, thrilling adventures, and world-class recreation.

Nestled among rolling hills and crystal-clear lakes, Calabogie is the perfect place for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation. Its breathtaking landscapes, cultural attractions, and gastronomic scene draws tourists from all over the world.

This comprehensive travel guide to Calabogie will give you all the information you need to plan the perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Calabogie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calabogie is steeped in history, and there are many museums and historical sites worth visiting. Here are a few of the highlights:

  • Victoria Hall Museum: This museum, housed in the former town hall, showcases the history of Calabogie and its surrounding area.
  • Renfrew County ATV Club Museum: For off-road enthusiasts, this museum is dedicated to sharing the history and culture of ATVing in Eastern Ontario.
  • The Calabogie Peaks Resort: The resort hosts various events throughout the year, including weddings, corporate events, and holiday parties.

Natural Attractions of Calabogie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calabogie is home to many natural attractions that will leave you breathless. Below are some of its most popular outdoor attractions:

  • Calabogie Motorsports Park: This motor sports park hosts yearly racing events and offers experiences such as karting, drifting, and snowmobiling.
  • Highland Golf Club: This stunning golf course located in the heart of Calabogie’s natural landscape boasts of panoramic vistas and rolling greens.
  • Calabogie Peaks Ski Resort: With 22 runs, this ski resort is perfect for all levels of skiers and snowboarders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calabogie

Calabogie is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with multiple scenic routes suitable for both levels. Here are a few possible routes:

  • Calabogie Peaks Loop: This 3.9-kilometer trail is perfect for hiking or mountain biking.
  • The Fire Tower Trail: The 5.3-kilometer stretch trail is dotted with great views of lakes, forests, and charming cottages.
  • Burnstown and Pucker Street Loop: This gorgeous 21.2-kilometer route offers a challenging and scenic mountain biking adventure.

Best Things to Do in Calabogie: Activities and Tours

Calabogie has a plethora of things to do throughout the year. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Calabogie Brewing Company: A brewery/restaurant that offers visitors a great experience of local craft beers and people.
  • Calabogie Peaks Resort: A popular destination for winter tourist that offers skiing and snowboarding on the peaks.
  • Madawaska River Rafting: For adventure lovers, the experience of rafting is worth having.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calabogie

Day 1: Explore Calabogie’s local attractions - Victoria Hall Museum, Renfrew County ATV Club Museum and Calabogie Peaks Resort

Day 2: Take in the scenic views of Calabogie on a hike or bike ride - Calabogie Peaks Loop or Burnstown and Pucker Street Loop.

Day 3: Get adventurous with an ATV tour or a zip line through the treetops in Calabogie.

Day 4: Explore the town’s gastronomic scene and savour the local dishes, such as the famous Calabogie Chili, at one of the many restaurants near the lake.

Day 5: Visit nearby lakes and rivers, such as Round Lake, Madawaska River and Burnstown Beach.

Day 6: Relax and rejuvenate at a nearby spa or enjoy swimming and other water sports at the lake.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Pembroke, Ottawa or Mont Tremblant.

Food and Drink Scene of Calabogie

Must-Try Dishes

Calabogie is a gastronome’s delight and there are delicacies you just can’t miss out on. Below are must-try dishes:

  • Calabogie Chili: A classic meaty chili made with exotic hot vegetables, this dish is an all-time favourite of Calabogie locals.
  • Township 7 Brewing Company: This brewery serves the best beer options which is a must for a beer lover.
  • Blackbird Cafe: This charming café serves a variety of mouth-watering dishes that are a must-try.

Local Beverages

There are multiple places in Calabogie where you can get your fill of local beverages. Here are a few spots worth checking out:

  • Township 7 Brewing Company: This artisanal beer brewery is a must-visit for beer aficionados.
  • Calabogie Craft Nature: Visitors can indulge in locally-produced honey and honey products.
  • Calabogie Brewing Company: A microbrewery that brews beer onsite and serves up delicious pub grub to accompany the libations.

Where to Stay in Calabogie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There a variety of options when it comes to accommodation in Calabogie. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Calabogie Lodge Resort: The resort is located on Calabogie Lake and has large cabins, swimming pools, tennis courts and many other amenities.
  • Black Donald Tent and Trailer Park: If budget-friendly options are what you are looking for in Calabogie try this campsite located at the easternmost end of Black Donald Lake which offers guests a range of camping experiences.

What to Buy in Calabogie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some not-to-be-missed products you must take home as souvenirs:

  • Grand Pine Cabinetry & Small Accents: Visitors can pick up handmade wooden crafts and locally sourced antiques to bring back home.
  • Calabogie Brewing Co.: Bring back some local craft beers from this brewery home to share with your friends.
  • Calabogie Peaks Resort: The resort’s European-style ski village is a prime shopping destination, where visitors can purchase boutique clothing, jewellery, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Calabogie

Calabogie’s traditions are firmly rooted in the community’s love of nature, local sports, and cultural activities. Visitors can experience local events such as Summerfest or snowmobiling events.

Best Time to Visit Calabogie

Calabogie is a four-season destination with events round the clock. The best time to visit depends on your interests.

For nature lovers, the spring and fall are great months to visit when the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy great views of the changing colours of the leaves. For skiing/snowboarding enthusiasts, winter is the best season to visit Calabogie Peak Resort.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calabogie

Do:

  • Do hike or bike through the nature trails in Calabogie.
  • Do bring your outdoor gear, such as hiking boots, camping gear, and skiing/snowboarding equipment.
  • Do check out the quaint local shops and boutiques in the village.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Don’t take part in any outdoor activities without proper guidance or equipment.
  • Don’t disrespect the cultural or social norms of Calabogie.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calabogie

Calabogie is generally a safe place; however, visitors should be mindful of the risks associated with outdoor activities and ski resort adventures, and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Calabogie

Visitors are advised to be aware of their personal health and are recommended to carry medication and supplies for personal use.

Getting to Calabogie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calabogie can be accessed by car, plane or bus:

  • Airports Near Calabogie: The closest airport is Ottawa International Airport which is around 90 km from Calabogie.
  • Bus Stations Near Calabogie: Greyhound operates a daily bus which connects Ottawa to Renfrew and from there visitors can hire a taxi to Calabogie.
  • Other Options: Visitors can rent a car from the airport or a nearby location in Ottawa.

How to Get Around in Calabogie

To really explore Calabogie’s scenic routes and get the most out of what it has to offer, visitors are advised to rent a car or motorbike. However, taxi services are offered too.

Where to Go Next from Calabogie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations that you may want to visit after enjoying Calabogie:

  • Pembroke: The County Seat of Renfrew County, Pembroke is rich in art and history and offers visitors a range of unique experiences.
  • Ottawa: Canada’s capital is a must-visit destination, with many attractions, museums, parks, and restaurants worth exploring.
  • Mont-Tremblant: This popular resort town is located in Quebec and is known for being a fantastic destination for winter sports and village life.

Conclusion

Calabogie offers endless possibilities for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, gastronomic delights and much more. No matter the season, tourists from around the world will find something exciting to experience in this scenic retreat. Be sure to plan ahead and make the most of your trip to Calabogie.
