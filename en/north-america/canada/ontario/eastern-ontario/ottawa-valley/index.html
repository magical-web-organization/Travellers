Introduction to Ottawa Valley

Located in Eastern Ontario, Ottawa Valley is a picturesque region renowned for its rugged landscape, rich culture, and outdoor activities. The valley covers a vast area and is home to charming towns, towering forests, glistening lakes, and rivers. Apart from its natural beauty, the Ottawa Valley boasts of diverse cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and historic places that provide a glimpse into its fascinating past.

List of Places to Visit in Ottawa Valley

When it comes to attractions and things to do, Ottawa Valley has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bonnechere Caves: Embark on an underground adventure tour through ancient limestone caves.
  • Canadian Museum of History: Discover Canada’s diverse human history and cultural heritage in this world-renowned museum.
  • The Logos Land Resort: Explore a vast water park filled with thrilling water slides, pools, and water trails.
  • The Pembroke City Hall: Marvel at the stunning architecture of a heritage building, it was built in 1914.

Natural Attractions of Ottawa Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Algonquin Provincial Park: A vast nature reserve that offers year-round recreational opportunities including hiking, camping, canoeing, and kayaking.
  • The Ottawa River: Both the upper and lower parts of the river offer exciting water sports, such as whitewater rafting, tubing, and kayaking.
  • The Hogs Back Falls: A scenic waterfall situated in Confederation Park, perfect for picnics and sightseeing.
  • The Bonnechere River: Known for its excellent fishing opportunities, take a lazy river paddle down the river or hop on a jet boat for a thrilling ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ottawa Valley

For adventure enthusiasts, Ottawa Valley is a haven for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Cataraqui Trail: A 104 km multi-use recreational trail for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. It’s also a must-see for leaf-peepers each fall.
  • The Millennium Trail: A 49-kilometer trail perfect for hiking, walking, running, or cycling, that connects Carleton Place and Smiths Falls.
  • The Ottawa River Pathway: A scenic, paved pathway that stretches 21-kilometers along the river, offering stunning views and easy biking and walking.
  • The Petawawa Heritage Trail: A 3.8 km trail, perfect for hiking or walking, that highlights the history and natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Ottawa Valley: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking routes, here are some best things to do in Ottawa Valley:

  • Whitewater Rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Ottawa River’s rapids.
  • Fishing at Bonnechere River The Bonnechere River is known for its excellent fishing opportunities and is worth exploring.
  • The Ottawa Valley Farm Show: An annual event that takes place in March celebrating the farming industry and its history.
  • The Ottawa Valley Harvest Festival: This festival takes place annually in September. Attend this event and indulge in farm-fresh produce and local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ottawa Valley

If you have a week to explore Ottawa Valley, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Bonnechere Caves, then head to Renfrew and visit McDougall Mill Museum and the O’Brien Theatre
2 Take a day trip to Algonquin Provincial Park and enjoy the beautiful scenery
3 Participate in a whitewater rafting tour on the Ottawa River
4 Go hiking in the Petawawa Heritage Trail
5 Check out the Canadian Museum of History in Ottawa, head to Gatineau Park for scenic hikes and picnic
6 Explore the town of Pembroke, visit the historic Champlain Trail Museum which features exhibits from Pembroke’s past
7 Spend time on The Logos Land Resort and in the evening consider a visit to Pembroke’s drive-in movie theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of Ottawa Valley

The Ottawa Valley has a diverse food and drink scene with plenty of local cuisine. These are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A delicious Quebecois dish consisting of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Maple Syrup: Ontario is the largest producer of maple syrup in the world, so make sure to try it when you’re there.
  • BeaverTails: A sweet pastry dough stretched into the shape of a beaver’s tail, topped with various sweet toppings such as Nutella, or cinnamon sugar.
  • Perth Pie Co. pie: These family recipe pies are famous all around Ottawa Valley for their delicious, fresh-made fillings.

Local Beverages

  • Whitewater Brewing Company: This popular local brewery offers craft beers and a delicious selection of food.
  • Boshkung Brewing Co: Try out their selection of beers on the patio on McKechnie Road, especially in the summer.
  • Equator coffee roasters: A local favorite organic and fairly traded coffee roasters in Almonte, Ontario.

Where to Stay in Ottawa Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options when visiting Ottawa Valley:

  • Algonquin’s Edge Resort: This resort is located at the gates of Algonquin Provincial Park and offers comfortable cabins and suites, with fabulous local dining.
  • The Sands on Golden Lake: This resort offers cottages on the beach of Golden Lake.
  • Bonnechere Lodge Resort: A perfect spot for couples who want cozy lakeside cottages.

What to Buy in Ottawa Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you are done exploring Ottawa Valley, here are some locally-made products you could take home:

  • Maple Syrup: The world-famous sweet condiment, produced in large quantities in Ottawa Valley and can be bought in any store or farmer’s market in the region.
  • Cheddar Cheese: Try the local cheddar cheese, which is renowned for its quality and flavor.
  • Handmade Ceramics: The town of Perth is known for its rich pottery history, and its local artisans produce beautiful ceramics that you can take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ottawa Valley

Before you head to Ottawa Valley, it is good to know about the local culture and customs. The people of Ottawa Valley are warm and welcoming, and they have preserved their customs and traditions, such as:

  • Maple Syrup production: Ottawa Valley has a tradition of maple syrup production, and it’s known as the Maple Syrup Capital of Canada.
  • Ice Sports: Ottowa Valley is famous for winter sports, especially ice hockey and curling.
  • Music: There is a wide variety of traditional music genres in the valley, including Celtic, fiddling, and bluegrass.

Best Time to Visit Ottawa Valley

The best time to visit Ottawa Valley is during the fall season, primarily from September to November. The fall foliage lights up the region, and you will see vibrant colors everywhere you look.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ottawa Valley

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Ottawa Valley:

Do:

  • Hike, cycle, canoe, or kayak: Ottawa Valley has various recreational opportunities that will make your visit more enjoyable.
  • Try the local cuisine: Ottawa Valley boasts diverse homegrown dishes that are truly delicious.
  • Engage with locals: Ottowa Valley people are kind and welcoming towards tourists, so make sure to ask for any help you may require

Don’t:

  • Litter: Ensure you leave no waste while hiking or visiting parks. Use the provided bins.
  • Disrespect the local culture: Ottawa Valley has a vast history worth exploring. Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Not expecting cold weather: Be ready for cold weather during winter and fall seasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ottawa Valley

Ottawa Valley is mostly a safe place to visit, but tourists should take precautions like

  • Do not leave your property in your car as break-ins often occur.
  • Stay hydrated while engaging in outdoor activities and wear proper protective gear.
  • Stay on designated trails if hiking or biking.

Health in Ottawa Valley

There are Medical Centres, Pharmacies and a hospital in Ottowa Valley to attend to emergency medical needs for locals and tourists alike.

Ottawa Valley can be accessed through several transport options:

Airports Near Ottawa Valley

Bus Stations Near Ottawa Valley

Other Options

  • By car as the town is accessible through highways and scenic roads.

How to Get Around in Ottawa Valley

The best way to explore Ottawa Valley’s rural and remote areas is to drive. Renting a car will provide for an easy commute, plus there are several shuttle and tour services within the area.

Where to Go Next from Ottawa Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re done with exploring the Ottowa Valley, here are some nearby destinations to check out:

  • Kingston: a popular city with a rich history and beautiful waterfront, also known as the “Limestone City” characterized by its limestone buildings.
  • Prince Edward Island: Beautiful sandy beaches, charming lighthouse, and sandy dunes that make for a perfect day out.
  • Montreal: Located close enough, explore Old Montreal, sample delicious food and learn about the fascinating French culture.

Conclusion

Ottawa Valley offers travelers a combination of natural beauty, cultural attractions, distinct cuisines, and countless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re hiking the numerous trails, discovering the history and heritage of the region, or relaxing at a cottage, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Come explore Ottawa Valley and take home lasting memories.
