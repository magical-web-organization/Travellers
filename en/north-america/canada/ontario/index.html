Introduction to Ontario

Ontario is the most populous province in Canada and is located in North America. It is home to stunning natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Ontario’s capital city is Toronto, which is known for its iconic landmarks such as the CN Tower and the Royal Ontario Museum. Other major cities include Ottawa, the country’s capital, and Niagara Falls, home to one of the most famous waterfalls in the world.

List of Places to Visit in Ontario

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ontario is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the province’s rich history. The Royal Ontario Museum is one of the must-visit museums in Ontario. It provides a comprehensive look at the natural world and human civilizations throughout the ages. Another popular museum in the province is the Canadian Museum of History, which is located in Ottawa. It showcases Canada’s history and culture from the first Indigenous people to present-day.

If you are interested in history, be sure to also visit Fort York National Historic Site in Toronto. It is a historic military fort that played a crucial role in the Upper Canada Rebellion of 1837. Another important site to visit is the Canadian War Museum in Ottawa, which showcases Canada’s military history.

Natural Attractions of Ontario: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ontario is home to some of the most stunning natural attractions in Canada. Algonquin Provincial Park is one of the go-to places for outdoor adventurers. It is located in the heart of Ontario and offers visitors the opportunity to canoe, fish, hike, and camp in a pristine wilderness setting. Another natural wonder to explore is the Bruce Peninsula National Park, which has scenic coastlines and crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming.

If you are looking for more adrenaline-pumping activities, head to Niagara Falls, where you can experience the thrill of ziplining or take a breathtaking helicopter tour over the Falls. You can also go on a thrilling boat ride on the Maid of the Mist to get up close and personal with the Falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ontario

Ontario has several trails and routes for cycling and hiking enthusiasts. One of the most scenic routes is the Waterfront Trail, which extends along the shores of Lake Ontario and Lake Erie. The ride provides stunning views and is a great way to explore the charming towns and villages along the way. Another great route to explore is the Rideau Canal Trail, which provides an immersive nature experience and showcases the stunning landscapes of Eastern Ontario.

Best Things to Do in Ontario: Activities and Tours

From boat tours to food tours, Ontario offers an exciting variety of activities and tours for visitors. One of the most unique tours in the province is the Haunted Walk of Toronto, which offers a spooky exploration of some of Toronto’s most haunted sites. The Toronto Islands are also a popular destination, where you can rent bicycles and kayaks to explore the beautiful landscape.

If you are a foodie, be sure to take a culinary tour of Ontario’s food scene. Stop by St. Lawrence Market in Toronto to sample some fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products, or take a wine tour of the Niagara Wine Region to experience the best of Ontario’s wine scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ontario

A week-long itinerary in Ontario could include exploring Toronto, visiting Niagara Falls, and outdoor adventures in Algonquin Provincial Park or the Bruce Peninsula National Park. Other noteworthy destinations to explore include Ottawa, Thousand Islands, Prince Edward County, and Manitoulin Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Ontario

Must-Try Dishes

Ontario has a diverse food scene, and you can find a variety of dishes that range from global cuisine to classic Canadian dishes. Poutine, a dish of fries topped with gravy and cheese curds, is a must-try Canadian classic. Other popular dishes in the province include the famous butter tart, smoked meat sandwich, and peameal bacon.

Local Beverages

Ontario boasts a thriving craft beer and wine scene. Local craft breweries like Steam Whistle and Mill Street Brewery offer some of the best beer in the country, while the Niagara wine region produces some of the most delicious wines in Ontario.

Where to Stay in Ontario: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ontario has a vast range of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. If you are looking for a luxurious stay, the Fairmont Royal York Hotel in Toronto is known for its stunning architecture and top-notch service. For a more budget-friendly stay, HI Toronto Hostel offers affordable yet comfortable accommodations in the heart of Toronto.

What to Buy in Ontario: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home various souvenirs and local products from Ontario. The Niagara region is famous for producing delicious wines and ice wines, which make for great gifts. Another popular souvenir is the iconic Canadian maple syrup, which is also widely available throughout the province. If you are looking for something unique, head to the Distillery District in Toronto to purchase handmade artisanal products.

Local Culture and Customs of Ontario

Ontario has a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to respect its customs and traditions. Politeness and courtesy are highly valued, and it is customary to greet people with a friendly “hello” or “hi.” Canadians are also known for their love of hockey, and it is not uncommon to strike up a conversation about the sport.

Best Time to Visit Ontario

The best time to visit Ontario is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities. Autumn (September to November) is also a great time to visit when the leaves change colors and the landscapes are breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ontario

Do:

  • Try local food like poutine and maple syrup.
  • Explore the stunning natural landscapes of Ontario.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect the environment.
  • Underestimate the importance of sunscreen and bug spray during outdoor activities.
  • Forget to bring your passport – it is essential when crossing the United States border.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ontario

Ontario is a relatively safe place to visit. However, visitors must remain vigilant in large cities like Toronto, especially at night. It is also essential to keep belongings safe and secure when out and about.

Health in Ontario

Ontario has an excellent healthcare system with public hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Ontario: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ontario

The main airport in Ontario is the Toronto Pearson International Airport, which receives flights from major cities around the world. The Ottawa International Airport is also a popular option for travelers visiting the capital city.

Bus Stations Near Ontario

Ontario is well-connected through the Greyhound bus service, which operates stops in most major cities and towns in the province. The Toronto Bus Terminal is the main transportation hub for travelers in Toronto and also provides connections to other cities in Canada and the United States.

Other Options

Other transportation options include rental cars, trains, and ferries. VIA Rail operates trains across the province, with the Via Rail Station in Toronto being the hub for all rail lines. Ferries operate between the mainland and the various islands and ports across the province.

How to Get Around in Ontario

Ontario is a large province, and it is recommended to rent a car to explore the various destinations. However, if you are staying in Toronto, the public transportation system includes buses, subways, and streetcars.

Where to Go Next from Ontario: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ontario is surrounded by several nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Head to Montreal, the second-largest city in Canada, for an authentic French-Canadian experience. Alternatively, head south across the border to visit the iconic American city of New York.

Conclusion

Ontario is a beautiful and diverse province that offers something for everyone. From stunning natural landscapes to rich cultural history and a thriving food and drink scene, there is never a dull moment in Ontario. Whether you are looking for an outdoor adventure, a relaxing getaway, or a cultural outing, Ontario has it all.
