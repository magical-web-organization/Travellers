Introduction to Manitouwadge

Manitouwadge is a picturesque town located in Lake Superior North Shore, in Northern Ontario, Canada. Despite its remote location, Manitouwadge is a hidden gem that offers an array of exciting activities, cultural attractions, and unforgettable experiences. The town is surrounded by pristine wilderness and scenic vistas, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to Manitouwadge that will help you plan your next adventure in this Canadian wonderland.

List of Places to Visit in Manitouwadge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manitouwadge Heritage Museum: This museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s rich history and heritage. The museum also has a gift shop where you can buy souvenirs and local products.

Natural Attractions of Manitouwadge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ouimet Canyon Provincial Park: This park is known for its stunning views of a deep gorge and soaring cliffs. It’s popular among hikers and nature lovers who come to explore the park’s trails and unique geological formations.
  • Magpie High Falls: These magnificent waterfalls are a must-visit destination in Manitouwadge. The falls are surrounded by lush greenery, making it a perfect spot for a picnic or a photo-op.
  • Wabakimi Provincial Park: This massive wilderness area is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here you can enjoy canoeing, fishing, hiking, and wildlife watching.
  • Hiking and Biking Trails: Manitouwadge offers a network of well-marked hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness and experience. Some of the popular trails include the Casque Isles Trail, the Lake Superior Coastal Trail, and the Magpie Relay Trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manitouwadge

If you’re a hiking or biking enthusiast, then Manitouwadge is the place for you. The town has a vast network of well-marked trails that cater to all levels of fitness and experience. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Magpie Relay Trail: This 80-kilometer trail is a multi-use route that runs from Manitouwadge to Marathon. It’s perfect for hikers, bikers, and cross-country skiers who want to explore the scenic beauty of the area.
  • Casque Isles Trail: This 53-kilometer trail runs along the northern shore of Lake Superior, offering breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding wilderness.
  • Lake Superior Coastal Trail: This challenging trail runs along the southern shore of Lake Superior and offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Manitouwadge: Activities and Tours

Manitouwadge offers an array of exciting activities and tours that cater to all interests and budgets.

  • Fishing: Manitouwadge is a popular destination for anglers, thanks to its abundance of lakes and rivers that are teeming with fish. You can enjoy a day of fishing for trout, pike, and walleye.
  • Hunting: The town is also a prime destination for hunting enthusiasts who come to pursue game such as moose, bear, and deer.
  • Canoeing: Manitouwadge offers an excellent opportunity for canoeing enthusiasts to explore the town’s many waterways and lakes.
  • Wildlife Watching: The town is home to an array of wildlife, including black bears, moose, wolves, and birds of prey.
  • Cultural Tours: Manitouwadge offers an array of cultural tours that allow visitors to learn more about the town’s history and heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Manitouwadge

Must-Try Dishes

Manitouwadge offers an array of unique and delicious dishes that you must try on your visit:

  • Bannock: This traditional indigenous dish is a type of bread that’s made from flour, water, and baking powder. It’s often served with jam or honey.
  • Pickerel: This fish is a popular dish among locals, thanks to its delicate and flavorful flesh. It’s often served grilled or fried.
  • Wild Rice: This dish is a staple of indigenous cuisine in the region. It’s often served as a side dish or used in soups and stews.
