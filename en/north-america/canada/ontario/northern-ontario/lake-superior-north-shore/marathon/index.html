Introduction to Marathon

Nestled in the pristine wilderness of Northern Ontario, Marathon is a small town on the north shore of Lake Superior. It’s a hidden gem, known for its stunning lake views, peaceful atmosphere, and abundance of outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a quiet retreat or an adrenaline-pumping experience amidst nature, Marathon has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Marathon

Here are the must-visit places in Marathon that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marathon Heritage Museum: Discover the town’s rich history and culture at this local museum.
  • Pukaskwa National Park: Learn about the region’s Indigenous heritage and natural wonders at this national park.

Natural Attractions of Marathon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pebble Beach: Spend a relaxing day at this serene beach, where you can enjoy sunbathing, swimming, and beachcombing.
  • Pukaskwa River: Raft down the river and admire the stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Pukaskwa Coastal Hiking Trail: Hike along this spectacular trail that runs through rugged coastlines, scenic fjords, and lush forests.
  • Aguasabon Falls & Gorge: Witness the power of nature at this breathtaking waterfall and gorge that plummets into the Lake Superior.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marathon

Marathon is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness.

  • Pukaskwa Coastal Hiking Trail: As discussed above, this trail is a must-do for any hiker.
  • Casque Isles Trail: This 52-kilometer trail spans undulating hills, old-growth forests, rocky outcrops, and sandy beaches.

Best Things to Do in Marathon: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Cast your line at the Lake Superior and you might catch some of the region’s famous fish such as brook trout, lake trout, and salmon.
  • Boating: Take a boat tour of the Lake Superior and explore the stunning coastline dotted with lighthouses, islands, and wildlife.
  • Kayaking/Canoeing: Paddle down the Pukaskwa River and savor the serene beauty of the region.
  • Golfing: Play some rounds of golf at the scenic Peninsula Golf Course.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marathon

For an optimal Marathon experience, we suggest this itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive at Marathon, check-in at your accommodation, and unwind at Pebble Beach. Day 2: Hike the Pukaskwa Coastal Hiking trail. Day 3: Enjoy a kayaking/canoeing trip on the Pukaskwa River. Day 4: Visit the Marathon Heritage Museum and learn about the town’s history. Day 5: Take a boat tour of the Lake Superior and explore the stunning scenery. Day 6: Play some rounds of golf at Peninsula Golf Course. Day 7: Depart Marathon.

Food and Drink Scene of Marathon

Savor the local cuisine and beverages of Marathon at these must-try food and drink spots:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lake Superior Fish Fry: Taste some of the region’s freshest fish at Drifters.
  • Wild Rice and Bison Stew: Try this indigenous delicacy at Scenic High Cafe.
  • Maple Syrup Pie: Enjoy this sweet delicacy at Rose’s Restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Lake of the Woods Brewing Company: Quench your thirst with some locally brewed beer and cider.

Where to Stay in Marathon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Superior Shores Resort: Enjoy the tranquil lake views and comfortable amenities at this resort.
  • Peninsula Motel: Stay in the cozy and affordable rooms of the Peninsula Motel.
  • Marathon Inn: Experience the warm hospitality of the town at this guesthouse.

What to Buy in Marathon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring back a piece of Marathon home for your loved ones with these souvenirs and local products:

  • Indigenous crafts like dreamcatchers, jewelry, and artwork
  • Wild Rice harvested from the local fields
  • Locally made maple syrup and other maple products

Local Culture and Customs of Marathon

Marathon’s culture is deeply rooted in its Indigenous heritage. It’s important to respect the traditions and beliefs of the local Anishinaabe communities, who have lived in these lands for thousands of years. Also, the locals are friendly and welcoming, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation or ask for their recommendations.

Best Time to Visit Marathon

The best time to visit Marathon is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The autumn months of September and October are also popular for admiring the beautiful fall foliage. However, if you’re a winter sports enthusiast, then March to May is the ideal time for skiing and other snow activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marathon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Marathon:

Do:

  • Respect the local Indigenous communities and their traditions.
  • Try the delicious local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Pack appropriate outdoor gear for your activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the sensitive ecosystems of the national parks or nature reserves.
  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the outdoor activities or tours you take.
  • Trespass into private property or disturb the locals.

Safety and Health

Marathon is a safe and peaceful town. However, it’s important to take some safety precautions while indulging in outdoor activities to ensure you have a pleasant vacation.

Safety in Marathon

  • Carry a map and a reliable GPS device while hiking or exploring the wilderness.
  • Dress appropriately for the activities and weather.
  • Be cautious while hiking near waterfalls, cliffs, or rough terrains, and stick to the designated paths.
  • Avoid swimming in the lake or river without a guide.

Health in Marathon

  • Bring your prescription medications and essentials like sunscreen, insect repellent, and allergy medicines.
  • Have a medical insurance plan and emergency contacts handy.
  • Keep yourself hydrated and nourished while indulging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Marathon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marathon is well-connected to the rest of Ontario and Canada with multiple transportation options.

Airports Near Marathon

Thunder Bay International Airport is the nearest airport to Marathon, located around 4 hours north of the town.

Bus Stations Near Marathon

Marathon is connected to the major cities in Canada via Ontario Northland buses, with stops in Thunder Bay and Sault Ste. Marie.

Other Options

If you have your vehicle, you can drive from Thunder Bay to Marathon via the Trans-Canada Highway 17. Alternatively, you can take a train from Toronto to White River and then drive to Marathon.

How to Get Around in Marathon

The best way to get around Marathon is by renting a car, as public transportation is limited. Additionally, using a bike or walking is also recommended, especially if you’re exploring the town’s surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Marathon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Thunder Bay: Explore the urban delights of this vibrant city and its attractions such as Sleeping Giant Provincial Park and the Thunder Bay Marina.
  • Wawa: Discover the Northern Lights and scenic beauty of this cute little town home to the Wawa Goose statue and Lake Superior Provincial Park.

Conclusion

Marathon is a hidden paradise in Northern Ontario with stunning natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. From hiking the trails to fishing at the Lake Superior, this town offers a perfect getaway for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts. With our comprehensive guide, you can plan your dream vacation to Marathon and enjoy everything it has to offer. So, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable experience amidst the wilderness of Northern Ontario.
