Introduction to Thunder Bay

Located in Lake Superior North Shore, in Northern Ontario, Canada, Thunder Bay is a vibrant city that offers a plethora of cultural and natural attractions to explore. With stunning views of the Sleeping Giant, the largest of the Sibley Peninsula’s islands, and crystal-clear waters of the bay, Thunder Bay is a year-round destination that attracts visitors from across the world.

In this comprehensive guide, we take a closer look at the top places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat and drink in Thunder Bay. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Thunder Bay has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Thunder Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thunder Bay is home to a rich history and heritage that is depicted in its many cultural attractions. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Thunder Bay include:

  • Thunder Bay Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Thunder Bay and the surrounding areas, showcasing exhibits on the region’s Aboriginal and European settler history, as well as its industrial and transportation developments.

  • Fort William Historical Park: This living history museum is Canada’s largest and most comprehensive fur trade site, offering visitors an immersive experience of the 19th-century fur trade era.

  • Terry Fox Monument: This monument honors the legacy of Canadian icon Terry Fox, who ran across Canada to raise funds for cancer research, stopping in Thunder Bay in his monumental Marathon of Hope.

Natural Attractions of Thunder Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thunder Bay’s natural beauty is a major draw for visitors, with its pristine lakes and forests offering abundant wildlife, hiking and biking trails, and scenic views. Some of the top outdoor attractions in Thunder Bay include:

  • Kakabeka Falls: This stunning waterfall is Thunder Bay’s most visited attraction, dropping over 40 meters into the Kaministiquia River valley below. The surrounding park offers excellent hiking and picnicking opportunities.

  • Sleeping Giant Provincial Park: This vast park covers over 250 square kilometers, featuring a rugged coastline, towering cliffs, and numerous hiking trails with breathtaking views of Lake Superior.

  • Amethyst Mine Panorama: This operating mine and visitor center offers a unique opportunity to discover Thunder Bay’s rare and beautiful amethyst deposits, the largest in North America.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Northern Ontario, with hundreds of kilometers of trails winding through forests, along lakeshores, and up hills. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Top of the Giant Trail: This 22 km trail is a challenging but rewarding hike up to the Top of the Giant, an iconic rock formation and the highest point on the Sleeping Giant peninsula, with stunning views of Thunder Bay and Lake Superior.

  • Trowbridge Falls Loop: This 6 km loop trail runs along the Kaministiquia River and passes through beautiful rapids and waterfalls, offering abundant wildlife viewing opportunities.

  • Thunder Bay Cycling Route: This 70 km route takes cyclists through the heart of Thunder Bay, past many of the city’s key attractions, along the water’s edge, and through parks and natural areas.

Best Things to Do in Thunder Bay: Activities and Tours

Thunder Bay is a hub of activities and tours, catering to all ages and interests. Some of the top things to do in Thunder Bay include:

  • Wilderness Helicopter Tours: Take to the skies and enjoy a birds-eye view of Thunder Bay’s natural beauty on a helicopter tour of the region.

  • Thunder Bay Symphony Orchestra: This professional orchestra performs a wide range of classical and contemporary music throughout the year, featuring both local and international artists.

  • Kayak Adventure Tours: Paddle through the serene waters of Lake Superior or the nearby rivers and lakes, and explore the coastlines, islands, and hidden beaches of Thunder Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay’s food and drink scene showcases the region’s unique culinary traditions and locally sourced ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Persian Rolls: These iconic Thunder Bay treats are a must-try for visitors, featuring a sweet, cinnamon-flavored dough with pink icing.

  • Fish Fry: Thunder Bay’s proximity to Lake Superior means seafood is a staple in the city’s restaurants, with the Fish Fry being a popular dish featuring battered and fried fish and chips.

  • Wild Rice: This Northern Ontario delicacy is often found in dishes across Thunder Bay, featuring nutty, earthy flavors and a unique texture.

Local Beverages

  • Sleeping Giant Brewing Company: This local microbrewery offers a range of craft beers, including seasonal brews and limited edition releases.

  • Northern Delight Cocktail: This cocktail features locally sourced wild blueberries and maple syrup, served with a shot of Crown Royal whiskey.

  • Finnish Bread Cheese: This specialty cheese is a Thunder Bay classic, made from fresh cow’s milk and baked to form a cheesy bread-like texture.

Where to Stay in Thunder Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thunder Bay offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Thunder Bay:

  • Valhalla Inn: This hotel is located in the heart of Thunder Bay, offering luxurious rooms, a restaurant and lounge, and conference facilities.

  • Prince Arthur Waterfront Hotel: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Thunder Bay’s waterfront district, featuring elegant rooms and a restaurant and bar with stunning views of Lake Superior.

  • Thunder Bay Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the downtown area, offering comfortable dorm and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a cozy living room.

What to Buy in Thunder Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thunder Bay offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or mementos of your trip. Some of the top items to buy in Thunder Bay include:

  • Amethyst Jewelry: Thunder Bay has the largest amethyst deposits in North America, making it the perfect destination to purchase amethyst jewelry or other amethyst souvenirs.

  • Nanabijou Art Gallery: This gallery showcases local Indigenous art, jewelry, and other cultural products.

  • Preserved Moose Antlers: Moose antlers are a staple of Thunder Bay’s art and décor scene, and visitors can purchase antlers in various states of preservation, from fresh and raw to polished and finished products.

Local Culture and Customs of Thunder Bay

The Indigenous culture of Thunder Bay is an important aspect of the region’s heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the customs and traditions of the local community. Thunder Bay is also a hospitable and welcoming city, known for its friendly locals and laid-back vibe.

Best Time to Visit Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay is a year-round destination, with each season offering unique experiences and activities. Summer is the busiest time of the year, with warm weather and a variety of outdoor adventure options available. Winter is also a popular time for visitors, with snow sports, ice fishing, and Northern Lights viewing opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thunder Bay

Do:

  • Do respect Indigenous customs and traditions, and learn about the traditions and cultural heritage of the local community.

  • Do bring comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and kayaking.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, and explore the region’s unique culinary traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave garbage behind in natural areas or public places.

  • Don’t swim in unfamiliar waters without supervision or knowledge of the water conditions.

  • Don’t underestimate the weather conditions, especially in the winter months, and come prepared with appropriate clothing and gear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay is generally a safe destination for visitors, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, as with any destination, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Thunder Bay

The health care system in Thunder Bay is comprehensive and efficient, with excellent medical facilities and services available. Travelers should carry adequate health insurance and take basic precautions, such as avoiding drinking tap water and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Thunder Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay International Airport is located approximately 5 kilometers from the city center and offers regular flights to and from major Canadian cities.

Bus Stations Near Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay Bus Terminal is located in the city center, with regular bus services connecting Thunder Bay to various destinations across Northern Ontario.

Other Options

Thunder Bay is also accessible via car, with major highways connecting the city to other parts of Ontario and Canada, as well as via train, with VIA Rail offering regular services to and from Thunder Bay.

How to Get Around in Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay has an efficient public transit system, with buses running throughout the city and surrounding areas. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those looking for more flexibility and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Thunder Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thunder Bay is surrounded by a wealth of natural beauty and diverse destinations, offering visitors the opportunity to explore Northern Ontario’s vast and varied landscape. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Lake Nipigon: This pristine lake is located approximately 180 kilometers northwest of Thunder Bay, offering excellent fishing, camping, and hiking opportunities.

  • Pukaskwa National Park: This stunning park is located approximately 260 kilometers to the east of Thunder Bay, featuring rugged coastlines, towering cliffs, and pristine lakes and forests.

Conclusion

Thunder Bay is a city that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty, providing visitors with a one-of-a-kind travel experience. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s museums and historical sites, take to the trails on foot or bike, or indulge in the local cuisine and beverages, Thunder Bay has something for everyone. Come and discover the gems of Northern Ontario today!
