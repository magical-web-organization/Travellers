Introduction to Magnetawan

Magnetawan is a quaint village situated in the Near North region of Northern Ontario, Canada. Formerly a forestry town and a vital hub of the fur trade, Magnetawan has retained its rustic charm while evolving into a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and nature lovers. With its pristine lakes, dense forests, and stunning vistas, Magnetawan is a paradise for fishing, boating, camping, hiking, and sightseeing. It also offers a glimpse into Canada’s past, with well-preserved museums, historic buildings, and cultural events celebrating the heritage of the Ojibwe and early settlers.

List of Places to Visit in Magnetawan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Magnetawan First Nation Cultural Centre: Learn about the rich history and traditions of the Magnetawan First Nation through artifacts, exhibits, and workshops.
  • Magnetawan Heritage Centre: Discover the town’s past from the late 1800s to the present, including the role of Magnetawan in the fur trade, settlement, and forestry.
  • Magnetawan River Walk: Take a self-guided tour along the Magnetawan River and see the landmarks and plaques that commemorate the town’s legacy.

Natural Attractions of Magnetawan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ahmic Lake Conservation Area: Enjoy swimming, picnicking, hiking, and wildlife spotting in this spacious park that also features a playground, a boat launch, and camping facilities.
  • Magnetawan River Provincial Park: Paddle along the Magnetawan River and experience its rapids, waterfalls, and calm stretches. The park also offers portage, camping, and hiking opportunities.
  • Magnetawan Beach: Soak up the sun and water activities on Magnetawan Beach, which is lifeguarded from late June to Labour Day weekend.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Magnetawan

  • Magnetawan River Trail: This 7.4 km loop trail follows the Magnetawan River and offers scenic views, interpretive signs, and access to the beach and the provincial park.
  • Waters Trail: This 5.6 km trail takes you through a forested area dotted with wetlands and a beaver pond.
  • Georgian to Magnetawan Trail: This 38 km trail is shared by hikers, cyclists, and snowmobilers and connects Georgian Bay to Magnetawan.

Best Things to Do in Magnetawan: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Magnetawan is known for its abundance of bass, pike, walleye, and trout. Charter a boat, rent equipment, or join a guided tour to explore the best fishing spots.
  • Boating: Rent a canoe, kayak, or motorboat and explore the Magnetawan River, Ahmic Lake, and other scenic bodies of water in the area.
  • Golfing: Tee off at Magnetawan Golf Club, a challenging 9-hole course that also has a clubhouse and a pro shop.
  • Snowmobiling: The magnetawan Trail Blazers maintain over 300 km of snowmobile trails in the area, providing access to scenic vistas and quaint towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Magnetawan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips: Enjoy freshly caught fish battered and fried to perfection, accompanied by crispy fries and tangy tartar sauce.
  • Wild game: Try venison, elk, or moose prepared in various styles, such as grilled, stewed, or jerked.
  • Butter tarts: Indulge in this quintessential Canadian dessert, which features a flaky pastry shell filled with a rich mixture of butter, sugar, and egg.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Quench your thirst with a cold pint of locally brewed beer, such as the Magnetawan Ale or the Ahmic Lake Lager.
  • Maple syrup: Enjoy the sweet, amber liquid that symbolizes Canadian identity and heritage, either as a topping for pancakes or as a flavouring for coffee or cocktails.

Where to Stay in Magnetawan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ahmic Lake Resort: This family-friendly resort offers waterfront cottages, RV sites, and amenities such as a pool, a hot tub, and a marina.
  • Magnetawan Inn and Restaurant: This historic inn dating back to 1879 has nine rooms furnished with antiques, as well as a restaurant serving seasonal dishes.
  • Crooked Lake Wilderness Lodge: This secluded lodge overlooks Crooked Lake and offers all-inclusive packages for fishing, hiking, and relaxation.
  • Camping grounds: Magnetawan River Provincial Park and Ahmic Lake Conservation Area both offer campsites for tents and RVs, with access to hiking trails, beaches, and other attractions.

What to Buy in Magnetawan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Art and crafts: Browse the wares of local artists and artisans, including pottery, paintings, woodwork, and jewellery.
  • Maple products: Take home a bottle of maple syrup, maple candies, or maple butter as a tasty and authentic memento of Canada.
  • Fishing gear: Upgrade your fishing gear and tackle by visiting the local bait and tackle shops, which offer a wide range of equipment and advice.

Local Culture and Customs of Magnetawan

Magnetawan has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the traditions of its indigenous and settler communities. Visitors are invited to respect the local customs and values, appreciate the natural beauty of the area, and participate in community events such as powwows, festivals, and markets. Indigenous culture is particularly celebrated in Magnetawan, with the Magnetawan First Nation Cultural Centre offering workshops and exhibits on language, arts, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Magnetawan

Magnetawan’s climate is characterized by cold winters, warm summers, and moderate precipitation throughout the year. The peak tourist season is from June to August, when the weather is mild, the lakes are warm, and most attractions and services have optimal operating hours. Fall and spring are also good times to visit Magnetawan, as they offer lower prices, fewer crowds, and the opportunity to witness the changing colours of the leaves or the emergence of wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Magnetawan

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and their habitat by keeping your distance and not feeding them.
  • Follow the local fishing and hunting regulations and obtain the necessary licenses and permits.
  • Carry insect repellent, sunscreen, and appropriate clothing and gear for the season and the activities.
  • Support the local businesses and farmers by buying their products and services.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the peace and quiet of the town by making unnecessary noise, littering, or vandalizing public property.
  • Approach or touch wild animals, especially bears and moose, which are dangerous and unpredictable.
  • Use drugs or alcohol in public places or while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Trespass on private or indigenous lands without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Magnetawan

Magnetawan is a safe and friendly place, but visitors should be aware of some common risks and take precautions accordingly. These include:

  • Water safety: Always wear a life jacket when boating or swimming, follow the signs and buoys for navigation, and avoid drinking or diving in cold water.
  • Wildlife safety: Keep a safe distance from bears, moose, and other animals, make noise to avoid surprising them, and store food and garbage securely to deter them from approaching.
  • Road safety: Drive carefully and follow the speed limits and traffic signs, especially on the winding and wooded roads.
  • Emergency services: Know the location and phone number of the nearest hospital, police station, fire department, and search and rescue team, and carry a charged cell phone at all times.

Health in Magnetawan

Magnetawan has limited healthcare facilities, so visitors are advised to bring their own medications and first aid kits, as well as to obtain travel health insurance. The nearest hospitals are located in Parry Sound, Huntsville, and North Bay, which are all over an hour’s drive from Magnetawan. Visitors should also protect themselves against ticks, mosquitoes, and other bugs that can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease or West Nile virus.

Getting to Magnetawan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Magnetawan is accessible by car, plane, or bus. The nearest major city is Toronto, which is about 300 km south of Magnetawan and has an international airport, a train station, and a bus terminal. From Toronto, visitors can either drive north on Highway 11 or take a bus to the nearby towns of Sundridge or Burk’s Falls, and then transfer to a local taxi or shuttle service. The closest airports to Magnetawan are in North Bay, Sudbury, or Toronto, which offer domestic and international flights. There is no public transportation system in Magnetawan, so visitors are advised to rent a car or bring their own vehicle for maximum mobility.

Airports Near Magnetawan

  • North Bay Airport: 110 km southwest of Magnetawan, served by Air Canada, Porter, and WestJet.
  • Sudbury Airport: 150 km northwest of Magnetawan, served by Air Canada, Porter, and WestJet.
  • Toronto Pearson Airport: 300 km south of Magnetawan, served by all major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Magnetawan

  • Ontario Northland Bus Depot in Sundridge: 20 km south of Magnetawan, offers daily connections to Toronto and North Bay.
  • Ontario Northland Bus Depot in Burk’s Falls: 25 km east of Magnetawan, offers daily connections to Sudbury and Ottawa.

Other Options

  • Car rental companies: Several car rental companies operate in Toronto, North Bay, Sudbury, and other major cities in Ontario, offering various models and prices.
  • Shuttle services: Local shuttle services, such as Magnetawan Shuttle and Airport Taxi, offer door-to-door transportation within the Magnetawan area and to neighbouring towns.
  • Bicycle rental: Magnetawan Outfitters and other local businesses offer bicycle rental for exploring the trails and roads in the area.

How to Get Around in Magnetawan

The best way to get around in Magnetawan is by car, as most attractions and accommodations are located outside the town centre and public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car from a local or a major car rental company, or bring their own vehicle. Bicycle and ATV rental are also available for exploring the trails and roads in the area. Walking is a pleasant option for short distances within the town or along the river.

Where to Go Next from Magnetawan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Parry Sound: This charming town on Georgian Bay offers cultural attractions, shopping, dining, and outdoor activities.
  • Algonquin Provincial Park: This iconic wilderness area is renowned for its rugged landscapes, lakes, trails, and wildlife.
  • Killarney Provincial Park: This pristine park on the shores of Georgian Bay features stunning cliffs, beaches, and hiking trails.
  • Sault Ste. Marie: This historic city on the border with the US offers heritage sites, museums, casinos, and cruises on the St. Marys River.

Conclusion

Magnetawan is a hidden gem in Northern Ontario that offers a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. Whether you are seeking adventure, relaxation, or education, this town has something to offer to everyone.
