Introduction to Mattawa

Mattawa is a picturesque town located in the Near North region of Northern Ontario, Canada. Nestled between the Ottawa River and the Laurentian Mountains, the town offers stunning natural scenery, a rich history, and a wealth of outdoor activities. Known as the “Meeting Place of Two Rivers,” Mattawa is where the Mattawa and Ottawa Rivers converge. The town has a population of approximately 2,000 people and is a popular tourist destination for those seeking adventure, culture, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Mattawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mattawa Museum: Learn about the town’s rich history and culture at the Mattawa Museum. The museum features displays and exhibits showcasing the town’s fascinating past, including the forestry industry and the fur trade.

  • Explorer’s Point: This scenic lookout offers panoramic views of the Mattawa and Ottawa Rivers and is an excellent location to watch the sunrise or sunset.

Natural Attractions of Mattawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Antoine Mountain Ski Resort: This ski resort offers a variety of skiing and snowboarding trails and is perfect for winter sports enthusiasts.

  • Mattawa River Provincial Park: A nature lover’s paradise, the Mattawa River Provincial Park offers picture-perfect scenery, with winding rivers, stunning forests, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mattawa

  • Voyageur Multi-Use Trail System: This trail system contains over 300 kilometers of trails that are perfect for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling. The trails take visitors through beautiful forests and offer stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Mattawa-Bonfield Rail-Trail: This trail is an old railway that has been converted into a hiking and biking trail. The trail takes visitors through picturesque wooded areas and is home to a variety of wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Mattawa: Activities and Tours

  • Whitewater Rafting: The Ottawa River offers some of the best whitewater rafting in North America. Visitors can take a guided tour and experience the thrilling rapids of the river.

  • Fishing: Mattawa is a premier destination for fishing enthusiasts. The Mattawa River is home to a variety of fish species, including trout, walleye, and pike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mattawa

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Mattawa Museum and learn about the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.
Day 2 Hit the slopes at Antoine Mountain Ski Resort and enjoy the winter sports activities.
Day 3 Hike the Voyageur Multi-Use Trail System and enjoy the stunning scenery along the way.
Day 4 Take a guided tour and go whitewater rafting on the Ottawa River.
Day 5 Visit the Mattawa River Provincial Park for a day of hiking and wildlife watching.
Day 6 Head to the Mattawa-Bonfield Rail-Trail for a day of biking and outdoor adventure.
Day 7 Relax and unwind at one of the local spas or enjoy some of the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Mattawa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mattawa Poutine: This unique twist on traditional poutine features French fries, cheese curds, and gravy, topped with bacon bits and sour cream.

  • Wild Boar Sausage: This local delicacy is made from wild boar meat and features a rich, savory taste that is sure to delight any food lover.

Local Beverages

  • Mattawa Craft Beer: Local breweries offer a variety of craft beers with unique flavors that are a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Mattawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Valois’ Motel & Restaurant: This family-owned motel offers comfortable rooms, delicious food, and a welcoming atmosphere.

  • Mattawa River Resort: This resort offers a range of accommodations, including cabins and campsites, and provides access to a variety of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Mattawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native Crafts: Mattawa is home to a thriving indigenous population, and visitors can find authentic, handmade crafts that make perfect souvenirs.

  • Maple Syrup: Ontario is known for its delicious maple syrup, and Mattawa is no exception. Visitors can purchase locally made maple syrup and other maple-based products.

Local Culture and Customs of Mattawa

  • The indigenous population in Mattawa is an essential part of the town’s cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect their customs and traditions.

  • Mattawa is a bilingual town, with both English and French widely spoken.

Best Time to Visit Mattawa

The best time to visit Mattawa is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mattawa

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the area, including the Mattawa and Ottawa Rivers, Mattawa River Provincial Park, and Antoine Mountain Ski Resort.

  • Do learn about the town’s rich history and culture by visiting the Mattawa Museum and other historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturb the local wildlife.

  • Don’t litter or pollute the environment. Mattawa is committed to preserving its natural beauty, and visitors are encouraged to do the same.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mattawa

Mattawa is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take the usual safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Mattawa

Visitors to Mattawa should ensure that they have up-to-date medical insurance and that they are aware of any potential health risks, such as tick-borne diseases.

Getting to Mattawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mattawa

  • North Bay/Jack Garland Airport (YYB): This airport is located approximately 75 kilometers from Mattawa and offers daily flights to and from major Canadian cities.

Bus Stations Near Mattawa

  • Ontario Northland: This bus service provides daily service to and from Mattawa, with connections to other major cities in Ontario.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in North Bay and drive to Mattawa.

How to Get Around in Mattawa

Visitors to Mattawa can get around on foot, by bike, or by car. The town is small and easy to navigate, and many of the top sights and attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Mattawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Algonquin Provincial Park: Located approximately 100 kilometers from Mattawa, Algonquin Provincial Park is a stunning nature reserve that offers outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and canoeing.

  • Ottawa: Canada’s capital city is located approximately 200 kilometers from Mattawa and offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, including the Parliament Buildings and the Canadian Museum of History.

Conclusion

Mattawa is a hidden gem in Northern Ontario, Canada, and offers a wealth of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and scenic views. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Mattawa. Plan your visit today and discover the beauty of this unique town.
