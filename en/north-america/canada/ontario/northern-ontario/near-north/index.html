Introduction to Near North

Nestled in Northern Ontario, Near North is a breathtaking region with abundant natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage. With beautiful lakes, rolling hills, and picturesque forests, the region is a popular tourist destination for visitors looking to enjoy outdoor activities, scenic views, and unique cultural experiences.

This guide covers everything you need to know to plan the ultimate trip to Near North, from the must-visit attractions to the best places to stay and eat. So let’s begin!

List of Places to Visit in Near North

There are plenty of attractions in Near North for visitors of every age and interest. Whether you’re looking for cultural enrichment, outdoor adventures, or a mix of both, Near North has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Near North is home to a range of museums and historic sites that offer fascinating insights into the region’s past. The following are some of the most popular cultural attractions in the area:

  • Heritage Village and Museum: This open-air museum showcases life in Northern Ontario in the 19th and early 20th centuries, complete with authentic pioneer homes, a general store, and a blacksmith’s shop.
  • Callander Bay Heritage Museum: This museum explores the region’s history, from its geological past to its First Nations heritage to the early days of European settlement.
  • Discovery North Bay Museum: Located in the heart of North Bay, this museum celebrates the city’s rich cultural heritage and features a range of exhibits on local history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Near North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Near North is blessed with an array of natural wonders, including rugged forests, pristine lakes, and rolling hills. The following are some of the most popular natural attractions in the area:

  • Algonquin Provincial Park: This vast park encompasses over 7,000 square kilometers of pristine wilderness, including forests, lakes, and rivers. Here, visitors can hike, bike, fish, camp, and enjoy a range of outdoor activities.
  • North Bay Waterfront: This picturesque waterfront area is perfect for a leisurely stroll, with stunning views of Lake Nipissing and a range of shops, restaurants, and cafes.
  • Chief Commanda II: Take a cruise on Chief Commanda II and discover the beauty of Lake Nipissing. Enjoy scenic views of the lake and its surroundings, as well as a chance to see local wildlife and learn about the region’s history and culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Near North

For outdoor enthusiasts, Near North offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes in the region:

  • Kate Pace Way: This 12-kilometer paved trail offers stunning views of North Bay and is perfect for biking, walking, or jogging.
  • Algonquin Provincial Park: With over 2,400 lakes and 1,200 kilometers of streams and rivers, Algonquin Park is a hiker’s paradise. Trails range from easy strolls to challenging multi-day hikes, with opportunities to see local wildlife and breathtaking views along the way.
  • Laurier Woods Conservation Area: Located in North Bay, this 245-hectare nature reserve boasts over 17 kilometers of trails, with opportunities to see a range of birds and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Near North: Activities and Tours

Near North offers a range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Fishing: With over 400 lakes and rivers in the region, Near North is a fishing haven. Visitors can enjoy a range of fishing experiences, from fly-fishing for trout to trolling for walleye.
  • Canoeing and kayaking: Near North is home to some of the most beautiful waterways in Ontario, and there’s no better way to explore them than by canoe or kayak. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful paddle on one of the region’s many lakes or venture down one of its meandering rivers.
  • ATVing: For an adrenaline-filled adventure, visitors can explore the region’s rugged terrain on an ATV tour. With over 2,000 kilometers of ATV trails in the area, there’s plenty to explore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Near North

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Near North, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrival and Orientation

Arrive in Near North and settle into your preferred accommodation, whether it be a hotel, hostel, or guesthouse. Spend the day exploring North Bay and getting acquainted with the area.

Day 2: Heritage Village and Museum

Discover life in Northern Ontario in the 19th and early 20th centuries with a visit to Heritage Village and Museum.

Day 3: Algonquin Provincial Park

Spend the day exploring the stunning wilderness of Algonquin Provincial Park, hiking, fishing, or canoeing along the way.

Day 4: North Bay Waterfront

Take a leisurely stroll along the North Bay Waterfront and enjoy the stunning views of Lake Nipissing.

Day 5: Chief Commanda II

Discover the beauty of Lake Nipissing with a cruise on Chief Commanda II.

Day 6: Laurier Woods Conservation Area

Explore the natural wonders of Laurier Woods Conservation Area with a hike along its many trails.

Day 7: Departure

Spend your last day in Near North exploring the local shops, restaurants, and galleries before departing for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Near North

Near North’s food and drink scene is a reflection of the region’s diverse cultural heritage. Visitors can expect to find a range of delicious dishes and local beverages to sample during their stay.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular local dishes in Near North include:

  • Butter tarts: A sweet pastry made with butter, sugar, and eggs. This delicious treat is a Canadian classic and a must-try in Near North.
  • Poutine: A traditional Canadian dish made with french fries, cheese curds, and gravy. This hearty dish is perfect for a cold winter day.
  • Pickerel: A local fish that’s a popular choice for dinner. Visitors can enjoy pickerel cooked in a variety of ways, from pan-fried to grilled to deep-fried.

Local Beverages

Near North offers a range of local beverages to sample, including:

  • Ice wine: This sweet dessert wine is made from grapes that have been frozen on the vine. Near North is home to several wineries that produce high-quality ice wine.
  • Craft beer: Local breweries in Near North offer a range of craft beers, from light lagers to rich stouts.
  • Wild blueberry juice: Blueberries are a staple of the Near North landscape, and visitors can sample the region’s delicious wild blueberry juice during their stay.

Where to Stay in Near North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Near North offers plenty of options for accommodation, from luxurious hotels to rustic cabins. Visitors can choose from a range of properties to suit their needs and budget.

Luxury Hotels

Near North is home to several luxury hotels that offer top-notch amenities and stunning views, including:

  • Deerhurst Resort: This resort boasts two golf courses, several restaurants, and a spa, as well as access to beautiful Peninsula Lake.
  • JW Marriott The Rosseau Muskoka Resort & Spa: Perched on the shore of Lake Rosseau, this luxury resort offers beautiful views and top-notch amenities, including a spa and several restaurants.

Budget Accommodation

For travelers on a budget, there are several affordable options available, including:

  • Algonquin Eco-Lodge: This rustic lodge is located in the heart of Algonquin Park and offers affordable accommodation in a stunning natural setting.
  • Canadore College Residence and Conference Centre: This modern hostel-style accommodation is located in North Bay and offers affordable rooms in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Near North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Near North offers plenty of opportunities to pick up unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Maple syrup: Canada is known for its maple syrup, and Near North is no exception. Visitors can pick up a bottle of locally produced maple syrup to take home.
  • Handmade crafts: Near North is home to many talented artisans, and visitors can pick up one-of-a-kind handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Local honey: Near North is home to several beekeepers who produce high-quality honey. Visitors can pick up a jar of local honey to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Near North

Near North’s cultural heritage is a blend of First Nations traditions, European settlement, and modern Canadian culture. Visitors can expect to encounter a diverse range of customs and traditions during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Near North

Near North is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charms. The best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences.

Summer

Summer is a popular time to visit Near North, with warm weather and plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. This season is perfect for hiking, canoeing, and fishing, as well as enjoying the region’s beautiful lakes and beaches.

Fall

Fall is a beautiful time to visit Near North, with the region’s forests turning vibrant shades of red, orange, and gold. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and leisurely drives through the countryside to appreciate the stunning fall colors.

Winter

Winter is a magical time in Near North, with snow-covered landscapes and plenty of opportunities for winter activities such as skiing, snowshoeing, and ice-fishing.

Spring

Spring is a great time to visit Near North, with milder temperatures and fewer crowds than in the summer. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and exploring the region’s many lakes and rivers before the busy summer season arrives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Near North

As with any travel destination, there are certain do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Near North.

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment: Near North’s wilderness is a precious resource and should be treated with care. Visitors should avoid littering, stay on designated trails, and follow the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Try the local cuisine: Near North’s food culture is a reflection of the region’s diverse heritage. Visitors should sample local dishes such as butter tarts and pickerel to fully appreciate the region’s culinary traditions.
  • Get to know the locals: Near North is home to friendly and welcoming people. Visitors should take the time to talk to locals and learn about the region’s unique customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines: Near North’s natural environment can be dangerous if not treated with respect. Visitors should follow safety guidelines, particularly when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking or canoeing.
  • Disrespect cultural traditions: Near North has a rich cultural heritage that should be respected by all visitors. Visitors should avoid making derogatory comments or stereotypes about any cultural groups they encounter during their stay.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Near North should be aware of the region’s safety and health considerations.

Safety in Near North

Near North’s natural environment can be dangerous if not treated with respect. Visitors should follow basic safety guidelines, such as:

  • Staying on designated trails when hiking or biking.
  • Dressing appropriately for the weather and terrain.
  • Carrying plenty of water and snacks, especially during long hikes or outdoor activities.
  • Following all safety guidelines when participating in water-based activities such as canoeing or boating.

Health in Near North

Visitors to Near North should take basic health precautions to protect themselves from illness and injury, including:

  • Bringing any necessary medications with them on their trip.
  • Protecting themselves from insect bites, particularly during the summer months when mosquitoes and ticks are active.
  • Following all safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking or camping.

Getting to Near North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Near North is located in Northern Ontario, and getting there requires some planning and preparation.

Airports Near Near North

The following airports are located near Near North:

  • North Bay/Jack Garland Airport: This airport is located in North Bay and offers domestic flights to several Canadian cities.
  • Toronto Pearson International Airport: This major airport is located in Toronto and offers domestic and international flights to destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Near North

Several bus companies offer service to Near North, including:

  • Ontario Northland: This bus company offers service to several cities in Northern Ontario, with connections to Toronto and Ottawa.
  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers service to North Bay from several cities in Canada and the United States.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Near North by car or train.

How to Get Around in Near North

Visitors to Near North will need their own vehicle to explore the region, as public transportation is limited. Rental cars are available in North Bay and other major cities in the region.

Where to Go Next from Near North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Near North is the perfect starting point for exploring the natural beauty and cultural heritage of Northern Ontario. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Sudbury: This city is known for its mining history and is home to several museums and cultural attractions.
  • Killarney Provincial Park: This park is located south of Sudbury and offers stunning views of the Canadian Shield and Georgian Bay.
  • Temagami: This small town is known for its stunning natural beauty and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

Near North is a truly unique travel destination, offering a beautiful combination of natural beauty and cultural heritage.
