A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Manitoulin Island

Manitoulin Island is the world’s largest freshwater island, lying in the waters of Lake Huron in Ontario, Canada. The island is a haven for nature lovers, with breathtaking scenery, crystal-clear lakes, and abundant wildlife. Manitoulin Island also boasts a rich indigenous history, making it a unique and culturally significant destination.

Introduction to Manitoulin Island

Covering an area of about 2,766 km2, Manitoulin Island is blessed with a mild climate, making it ideal for outdoor activities all year round. The island is home to more than 100 inland lakes, perfectly suited for fishing or swimming. Manitoulin Island also has thriving cities, quaint towns, and villages that offer a unique blend of customs, traditions, and tranquillity.

List of Places to Visit in Manitoulin Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Aundeck Omni Kaning Cultural Centre: A museum dedicated to preserving and sharing the history of the indigenous people of Manitoulin Island.
  2. St. Joseph’s Mission Residential School: A place of deep sorrow and great cultural significance in the history of the Anishinaabe people of Manitoulin Island.

Natural Attractions of Manitoulin Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bridal Veil Falls: This scenic spot features a 35-foot waterfall that is mesmerizing to behold year-round.
  2. Cup and Saucer Trail: A challenging 12km hike with breathtaking views of Manitoulin Island from the top of the cliffs, 275m above sea level.
  3. Misery Bay Provincial Park: A must-visit for hikers and bird-watchers, this park features a unique ecosystem with a pristine nature reserve, and beautiful sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manitoulin Island

  1. The Great Spirit Circle Trail: A network of trails that covers most of Manitoulin Island and is perfect for hiking, cycling, and horse-riding.
  2. Chi-Cheemaun Bike Tour: A 75-km tour that takes you around the island, allowing you to experience the breathtaking landscape up close.

Best Things to Do in Manitoulin Island: Activities and Tours

  1. Star-gazing: Manitoulin Island is a designated dark sky area and offers a unique astronomical experience that is perfect for night-sky enthusiasts.
  2. Kayaking: Explore the crystal-clear waters of Manitoulin Island’s lakes by kayak or canoeing.
  3. Farm Tours: Experience local farming life while exploring the eastern part of the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Manitoulin Island

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Freshly caught whitefish: This local delicacy is a must-try when visiting Manitoulin Island.
  2. Pickerel Cheeks: A lesser-known, but equally delicious local dish found in the island’s restaurants.

Local Beverages

  1. Manitoulin Brewing Company: A local artisanal brewery that offers a wide range of unique beers.
  2. Beckwith’s Farm: A local winery and cidery that produces a variety of specialty wines and ciders.

Where to Stay in Manitoulin Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Manitoulin Hotel & Conference Centre: A full-service hotel located in Little Current that offers modern amenities and is centrally located on the island.
  2. Shelter Lodge: A unique wilderness retreat located near Bridal Veil Falls, offering a serene ambiance and customized experiences.
  3. The Manitoulin Inn: A cozy and historic 19th-century inn located in Mindemoya with 23 rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Manitoulin Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Dream-catchers: A traditional Anishinaabe item, often made from natural materials like willow branches, feathers, and beads.
  2. Laird Fairgrounds Maple Syrup: Pure maple syrup that is produced locally, extracted from the trees on this very island.

Local Culture and Customs of Manitoulin Island

The indigenous community of Manitoulin Island has a deep and rich cultural heritage that is evident in its lifestyles, ceremonies, and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and laws and learn as much as they can about their history and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Manitoulin Island

Manitoulin Island can be visited any time of year, depending on the visitor’s preference. Summers are warm and offer the best weather for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and swimming. Fall, on the other hand, offers tourists the chance to experience the breathtaking fall foliage colors. Winter is perfect for snowmobiling, ice-skating, and ice-fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manitoulin Island

Do:

  1. Bring insect repellent during the summer months.
  2. Respect the customs and culture of the indigenous people on the island.

Don’t:

  1. Litter as trash can be challenging to dispose of on the Island.
  2. Attempt to fish, hunt, or forage without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manitoulin Island

Manitoulin Island is a very safe place for visitors. However, there are some safety tips to keep in mind, such as following the speed limits on the many winding roads, dressing appropriately for winter outdoor activities, and knowing beforehand where you can and can’t fish or hunt.

Health in Manitoulin Island

Visitors are advised to ensure they have the appropriate vaccinations before visiting Manitoulin Island. Visitors are also encouraged to bring along any necessary medication and to have appropriate health insurance.

Getting to Manitoulin Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manitoulin Island

The two major airports in the region are Greater Sudbury Airport and Sault Ste. Marie Airport.

Bus Stations Near Manitoulin Island

The Greyhound Bus Line serves the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also access the island by private vessel or by taking the Manitoulin Island ferry from Tobermory.

How to Get Around in Manitoulin Island

Visitors can rent cars, bicycles, and canoes or kayaks on Manitoulin Island. Visitors can also join one of the many cycling tours offered on the island.

Where to Go Next from Manitoulin Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. North Bay: Located in Northern Ontario, North Bay offers visitors an opportunity to explore natural attractions like the Manitou Islands and Duchesnay Falls.
  2. Sault Ste. Marie: Located south of Manitoulin Island, Sault Ste. Marie offers tourists an opportunity to explore its history, museums, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Manitoulin Island is a place of exceptional beauty, rich indigenous culture, and an abundance of exciting activities and stunning scenery. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking to escape the hustle and bustle of modern life, Manitoulin Island promises a unique and unforgettable experience.
