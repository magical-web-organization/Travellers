Introduction to Parry Sound

Nestled in the heart of Near North in Northern Ontario, Canada, Parry Sound is a charming town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences. The town is situated on the shore of the stunning Georgian Bay, surrounded by pristine forests, rolling hills, and sparkling lakes.

In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll take a deep dive into the must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, food and drink scene, accommodations, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions of Parry Sound. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Parry Sound

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, don’t miss these exciting attractions in Parry Sound:

  • Charles W. Stockey Centre for the Performing Arts: This stunning performing arts center hosts concerts, theater performances, and exhibits.

  • Museum on Tower Hill: This museum showcases the history and culture of Parry Sound and the surrounding area through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.

Natural Attractions of Parry Sound: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parry sound offers some of the most breathtaking views in Canada, so be sure to check out these outdoor activities during your visit:

  • Georgian Bay Islands National Park: Explore the beautiful islands of the Georgian Bay, from wildflower meadows to rocky coves, by kayaking or hiking.

  • Killbear Provincial Park: Take a hike on one of several trails in the park, take a dip in the cool waters of Georgian Bay, or soak up the sun on its sandy beaches.

  • Parry Sound Waterfront Trail: A scenic 3.75km walking, biking, and rollerblade trail that meanders through the quiet residential neighbourhoods and the waterfront of Parry Sound.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parry Sound

Exploring Parry Sound on foot or by bike is a great way to get up close and personal with its natural beauty. Here are a few popular trails to choose from:

  • Huckleberry Rock Lookout Trail: A 3.5km trail that leads to a stunning view of the surrounding landscape from the top of Huckleberry Rock.

  • Rose Point Trail: This 2.5km trail winds through beautiful forests and is perfect for hiking and birdwatching.

  • Frying Pan Island Lookout Trail: A 2.7 km hike to a lookout where you’ll be rewarded with a stunning view of Georgian Bay.

Best Things to Do in Parry Sound: Activities and Tours

Parry Sound has a wide range of activities to choose from, whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Island Queen Cruise: This boat tour offers a scenic view of the Bay and a chance to see the area’s wildlife, including eagles, ospreys, and loons.

  • Ontario Northland Train: Ride this train and enjoy stunning views of the Canadian wilderness and landscapes.

  • Parry Sound Bikes: Rent a bike and explore Parry Sound on your own. They also offer guided tours of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Parry Sound

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Parry Sound:

  • Day 1: Hiking at Georgian Bay Islands National Park.

  • Day 2: Kayaking at the Thousand Islands & Discovery Centre.

  • Day 3: Visit the Museum on Tower Hill and learn about the local culture and history.

  • Day 4: Biking on Rose Point Trail.

  • Day 5: Take a boat tour with the Island Queen Cruise.

  • Day 6: Visit the Charles W. Stockey Centre for the Performing Arts and catch a concert or a play.

  • Day 7: Relax on the sandy beaches at Killbear Provincial Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Parry Sound

Must-Try Dishes

Parry Sound has some unique dishes to try:

  • Butter Tarts: A sweet treat that originated in Ontario and can be found at the many bakeries in the town.

  • Pickerel: A local favourite. This fish is found in the clear waters of Georgian Bay and a must-try dish for any seafood lover.

Local Beverages

  • Georgian Bay Spirit Co.: This small batch distillery features gin, vodka, and whisky that showcases local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Parry Sound: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Parry Sound has a wide range of cozy and affordable accommodations. Here are a few great options:

  • Bayside Inn: A comfortable family-friendly inn near the waterfront has stunning views of Georgian Bay.

  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Parry Sound: Offers some of the most comfortable accommodations in the centre of the town with an indoor pool.

  • Travellers Hotel: A budget option in the centre of the town with basic necessities.

What to Buy in Parry Sound: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for something special to bring home? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Handmade Crafts: Visit the local boutiques for handmade, one-of-a-kind clothing, accessories, and art created by local artists.

  • Maple Syrup: Ontario is known for its delicious maple syrup. Stop by a local shop to pick up your own bottle.

Local Culture and Customs of Parry Sound

Parry Sound is a friendly and welcoming town. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect Wildlife and Nature: The town is home to many protected species. Ensure you practice their rightful behaviour in their natural habitats.

  • Tip for Good Service: It is customary to tip 10-20% for any good service.

Best Time to Visit Parry Sound

The best time to visit Parry Sound is from May to October, this is a great time for outdoor activities and the town has plenty of events and festivals to enjoy during the warmer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parry Sound

Do:

  • Explore Nature: Parry Sound is home to stunning landscapes and outdoor activities that are a must-try!

  • Talk to Locals: Parry Sound locals are friendly and always happy to share their knowledge of the area and best tips.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Parry Sound takes pride in its natural beauty, so ensure you keep the space clean and tidy.

  • Wildlife Contamination: Ensure that you respect wildlife and keep them safe by not feeding them. Do not leave any open foods around.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parry Sound

Parry Sound is a relatively safe place to visit with a low crime rate. Ensure you use common sense and take the necessary precautions while venturing through the natural landscape.

Health in Parry Sound

The healthcare system across Canada is world-class, Newfoundland is no different. Ensure that you have travel health insurance that covers all possible emergency medical issues.

Getting to Parry Sound: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parry Sound

The closest airport is Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ), which is 2 hours and 46 minutes from Parry Sound via car. The Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport (YTZ) is another option that is approximately 2 hours and 42 minutes from Parry Sound via car.

Bus Stations Near Parry Sound

Ontario Northland offers daily bus service to/from Parry Sound with connections to other major cities in Ontario.

How to Get Around in Parry Sound

Parry Sound is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis, bikes, and rental cars are also available, in case you want to venture further.

Where to Go Next from Parry Sound: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations that are worth exploring:

  • Algonquin Provincial Park: One of Canada’s largest and most visited provincial parks, is a must-visit destination, only 2 hours and 48 minutes from Parry Sound.

  • Sudbury: A vibrant city that has a thriving art scene, is 2 hours and 29 minutes from Parry Sound.

  • Toronto: The largest city in Canada, approximately 2 hours and 39 minutes drive from Parry Sound.

Conclusion

There you have it: the ultimate guide to exploring Parry Sound. With its rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, Parry Sound is an excellent destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and exciting vacation. Don’t forget to pack your camera, ready to capture the beautiful scenery and unique experiences on offer in Parry Sound.
