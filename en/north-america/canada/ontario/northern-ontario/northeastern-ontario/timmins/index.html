Introduction to Timmins

Timmins is a small city located in Northeastern Ontario, Canada, and is considered the heart of Northern Ontario. With a population of just over 43,000, it is a vibrant and culturally rich city that is home to many activities and adventures.

Timmins offers breathtaking scenery and natural attractions, but it also has a rich history with many landmarks and museums to explore. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventure or discovering the region’s cultural heritage, Timmins has something to offer. Let’s dive into the best places to visit and things to do in Timmins.

List of Places to Visit in Timmins

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Timmins Museum National Exhibition Centre

The Timmins Museum is one of the most important cultural landmarks of the city. The museum’s main exhibition hall showcases the history of Timmins and its people, while several smaller rooms host temporary exhibits on various themes.

Hollinger Park

Hollinger Park is a large public park that’s home to many festivals and events throughout the year. It’s also home to the famous McIntyre headframe, one of the last remaining structures from Timmins’ mining past.

Porcupine Mining District

Known as the “City with a Heart of Gold”, Timmins owes much of its heritage and development to its mining industry. The Porcupine Mining District is the site of several mining companies and properties, including Hoyle Pond Mine, the largest gold mine in Canada.

Natural Attractions of Timmins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cedar Meadows Resort & Spa

Cedar Meadows Resort and Spa is a nature lover’s paradise. The resort is located on a 175-acre wildlife sanctuary, where guests can enjoy guided tours and observe a variety of animals, including deer, elk, and bison.

Kettle Lakes Provincial Park

Kettle Lakes Provincial Park is home to 22 small and secluded lakes, which are popular among locals and visitors for fishing, swimming, and kayaking. The park is also home to several hiking trails, including the picturesque lookout trail.

Kamiskotia Snow Resort

Kamiskotia Snow Resort is Timmins’ premier winter sports destination. It offers skiing, snowboarding, and tubing opportunities for visitors of all ages and skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Timmins

Timmins offers several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Many of the trails are accessible all year round, regardless of the season.

Gillies Lake Trail

Gillies Lake Trail is a 5 km hiking and biking trail that wraps around Gillies Lake. The trail offers stunning views of the lake and is a great spot for birdwatching.

Hersey Lake Conservation Area

The Hersey Lake Conservation Area features a 4 km trail through a variety of terrain, including forests, meadows, and wetlands. The area is home to several species of plants and animals and is a popular spot for picnics and camping.

Best Things to Do in Timmins: Activities and Tours

Timmins Adventure Tours

Timmins Adventure Tours offers a variety of guided tours and activities, including snowshoeing, ice fishing, and dog sledding. The tours are led by experienced guides who are knowledgeable about the region’s history and wildlife.

Timmins Symphony Orchestra

The Timmins Symphony Orchestra is a group of skilled musicians who perform classical and contemporary music in venues throughout the city. The orchestra performs throughout the year and is a must-see for music lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Timmins

Day 1: Arrive in Timmins, visit the Timmins Museum National Exhibition Centre, and take a walking tour of downtown Timmins.

Day 2: Visit Cedar Meadows Resort & Spa and go on a guided wildlife tour.

Day 3: Explore the Porcupine Mining District and visit Hoyle Pond Mine.

Day 4: Go skiing or snowboarding at Kamiskotia Snow Resort.

Day 5: Visit Kettle Lakes Provincial Park and hike the lookout trail.

Day 6: Take a guided tour with Timmins Adventure Tours and go snowshoeing or ice fishing.

Day 7: Attend a performance by the Timmins Symphony Orchestra.

Food and Drink Scene of Timmins

Timmins has a diverse and unique food scene, which is a product of its rich cultural heritage. Visitors can find everything from traditional Canadian cuisine to Italian, Indian and Chinese dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic dish consisting of french fries and cheese curds smothered in gravy.
  • Tourtière: A traditional meat pie that’s popular in Quebec and other parts of Canada.
  • Butter tarts: A sweet pastry filled with sugar, eggs, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Labatt Blue: A Canadian beer brewed in London, Ontario.
  • Northern Ontario Craft Beer: Locally brewed beer featuring unique flavors and ingredients from the region.

What to Buy in Timmins: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Indigenous artwork and crafts

Local Culture and Customs of Timmins

Northern Ontario has a unique and vibrant culture, which is a mix of Indigenous, French-Canadian, and Anglophone influences. Residents of Timmins are known for their outdoor activities, such as hunting, fishing, and snowmobiling.

Best Time to Visit Timmins

The best time to visit Timmins is during the summer months (May to September) when the weather is warm and many outdoor activities are available. However, visitors looking to ski or snowboard should plan their trip during the winter months (December to March).

Do’s and Don’ts of Timmins

Do:

  • Be respectful of the Indigenous culture and traditions.
  • Visit the Porcupine Mining District to learn about Timmins’ mining history.
  • Try local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave the environment in disarray.
  • Trespass on private property or vandalize historical landmarks.
  • Ignore safety measures in place while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Timmins

Timmins is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and exercise caution while participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Timmins

Timmins has several medical clinics and a hospital that can provide necessary medical care. Visitors should have proper health insurance coverage and consider bringing their medication and medical equipment.

Getting to Timmins: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Timmins by car, bus, or plane. The city also has several rental car companies and taxi services to help visitors get around.

Airports Near Timmins

  • Timmins Victor M. Power Airport
  • Sudbury Airport

Bus Stations Near Timmins

  • Ontario Northland Bus Station

Other Options

  • Northern Airport Services, which offers charter flights across Northern Ontario.

How to Get Around in Timmins

The best way to get around Timmins is by car or taxi. The city also has a public transit system, but visitors should check the schedules and plan their trip accordingly.

Where to Go Next from Timmins: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cochrane

Cochrane is a small town located just west of Timmins. It’s known for its beautiful scenery and is home to the Polar Bear Habitat, a facility that protects and studies polar bears.

Temiskaming Shores

Temiskaming Shores is a small city located on the border of Ontario and Quebec. It’s known for its picturesque lakes and rivers and is a popular destination for fishing and camping.

Conclusion

Timmins is a beautiful and unique destination that offers many exciting adventures and cultural experiences. Visitors can explore its rich history, enjoy outdoor activities, or simply relax and enjoy the stunning scenery. No matter your interests, Timmins has something to offer.
