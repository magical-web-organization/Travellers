Introduction to Cochrane

Cochrane is an idyllic town situated in Northeastern Ontario, Canada. It is a charming community with a wealth of attractions to offer. The town is known for its exceptional outdoor adventures, scenic views, rich history, and hospitable locals. Cochrane is a great place to explore and discover the beauty of Northern Ontario.

List of Places to Visit in Cochrane

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Canadian Polar Bear Habitat

The Canadian Polar Bear Habitat is one of Cochrane’s top-rated attractions. This unique sanctuary is home to polar bears and other animals from around the world. Visitors can observe these magnificent creatures up close through the facility’s state-of-the-art indoor and outdoor exhibit areas.

Polar Bear Express

The Polar Bear Express is a train ride that offers unforgettable views of the surrounding landscapes. This scenic route passes through the rugged terrain of the Canadian Shield. This trip is a must-do for anyone visiting Cochrane.

Cochrane Railway Heritage Museum

The Cochrane Railway Heritage Museum is a testament to Cochrane’s rich railway history. The museum displays an impressive collection of rail cars and memorabilia. Visitors can explore the museum’s interactive exhibits and learn about the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Cochrane: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Commando

Lake Commando is a beautiful and serene lake located within the town of Cochrane. The lake is known for its crystal-clear waters and verdant surroundings. Visitors can enjoy a multitude of water activities such as swimming, fishing, kayaking, and boating.

Abitibi River

The Abitibi River is another natural attraction in Cochrane that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. It is a popular spot for fishing, canoeing, and kayaking. It is also possible to go on a guided river tour and see some of the local wildlife.

Cochrane Polar Bear Habitat Boardwalk

The Cochrane Polar Bear Habitat Boardwalk is a scenic trail that offers stunning views of the surroundings. It is an excellent spot for a relaxing stroll or a jog. Visitors can also observe the local wildlife, including beavers, otters, and a variety of bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cochrane

Driftwood Provincial Park Trails

Driftwood Provincial Park is a nature reserve located 45 minutes from Cochrane. It has a network of trails that take hikers through forests of white pine and birch, over ridges with panoramic views, and along rocky shorelines.

Cochrane Bike Trails

Cochrane has a network of over 50 km of bike trails that wind through town and beyond. The trails are well-maintained and take riders through beautiful scenery, including forests, hills, and lakes.

Best Things to Do in Cochrane: Activities and Tours

Train Tours

Cochrane is home to the Polar Bear Express, one of the most scenic and atmospheric train journeys in North America. A trip on the Polar Bear Express takes you through the wilderness of Northern Ontario, offering up-close views of the local wildlife.

Ice Fishing

Cochrane is the perfect destination for ice fishing enthusiasts. The nearby lakes and rivers provide plenty of opportunities to catch walleye, trout, pike, and more. Visitors can hire a guide to take them to the best ice fishing spots.

Food and Drink Scene of Cochrane

Must-Try Dishes

Cochrane has plenty of delicious local specialties to try, including:

  • Poutine: a dish made of french fries, gravy, and cheese curds.
  • Fish and chips: a deep-fried battered fish served with fries.
  • Tourtière: a meat pie traditionally served during the holiday season.

Local Beverages

Cochrane has a microbrewery that offers locally crafted beer. Visitors can also try a selection of local wines and spirits.

Where to Stay in Cochrane: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Western Swan Castle Inn

The Best Western Swan Castle Inn is a great place to stay in Cochrane. This cozy hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

Cochrane Polar Bear Habitat & Heritage Village

The Cochrane Polar Bear Habitat & Heritage Village is another option for visitors looking for a unique accommodation experience. This facility offers overnight stays in comfortable lodges onsite.

What to Buy in Cochrane: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cochrane Polar Bear Habitat Gift Shop

The Cochrane Polar Bear Habitat Gift Shop is an excellent place to buy souvenirs. Visitors can purchase souvenirs such as polar bear plush toys, mugs, and t-shirts.

Cochrane Farmers’ Market

The Cochrane Farmers’ Market is a great place to buy local produce and artisanal products. Visitors can buy fresh fruits and vegetables, homemade jams, and handcrafted items.

Local Culture and Customs of Cochrane

Cochrane prides itself on its friendly and welcoming locals. Visitors can expect to encounter the unique and vibrant local culture when they visit.

Best Time to Visit Cochrane

The best time to visit Cochrane is during the summer months from June to August. These months offer the best weather for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cochrane

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Wear appropriate clothing for the season and outdoor activities.
  • Follow the rules and regulations when hiking, fishing, and hunting.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.
  • Ignore the weather forecast.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cochrane

Cochrane is a very safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should exercise standard safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables in plain view and locking their vehicles.

Health in Cochrane

Visitors should have up-to-date medical insurance when visiting Cochrane. They should also bring along any necessary medication, especially if they have allergies or pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Cochrane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cochrane

The closest airport to Cochrane is the Timmins Airport, which is approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Cochrane

The Ontario Northland Bus Service offers regular shuttle service from Cochrane to other destinations in Northern Ontario.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cochrane via the Highway 11, which runs through the town.

How to Get Around in Cochrane

The best way to get around Cochrane is by car. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Cochrane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sudbury

Sudbury is situated approximately 300 km from Cochrane. This city is known for its beautiful surroundings, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

North Bay

North Bay is approximately 230 km from Cochrane. This city is surrounded by natural beauty and offers plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and canoeing.

Conclusion

Cochrane is a unique and exciting destination that offers visitors an abundance of attractions and activities. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a place to relax and unwind, Cochrane has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the beauty of this charming town in Northeastern Ontario.
