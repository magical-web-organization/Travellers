Introduction to Kapuskasing

Kapuskasing is a small town located in Northeastern Ontario, in Northern Ontario, in Ontario, in Canada, in North America. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and friendly people. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a peaceful getaway, Kapuskasing will not disappoint you. In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Kapuskasing.

List of Places to Visit in Kapuskasing

There is plenty to see and do in Kapuskasing. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Riverside Murals - A collection of murals depicting the history, culture, and nature of Kapuskasing. It is a great way to learn about the town’s heritage and appreciate the local artists’ work.
  • Heritage Walking Tour - A self-guided tour that takes visitors through the historic downtown area, highlighting some of the town’s most important buildings, public art, and landmarks.
  • Kapuskasing Riverfront Park and Statue - A beautiful park located by the Kapuskasing River, featuring a statue of early settlers to the area and a boardwalk where you can enjoy scenic views of the water.
  • Kapuskasing Lumberjack Heritage Festival - An annual event held in July that celebrates the town’s lumberjack history with competitions, demonstrations, and entertainment.

Natural Attractions of Kapuskasing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kapuskasing River Waterfront Trail - A scenic trail that follows the Kapuskasing River and provides views of the town’s industrial and natural landscapes.
  • Polar Bear Habitat - One of the only places in Canada where you can see polar bears up close in a natural habitat. The habitat also provides educational opportunities about the arctic ecosystem and conservation.
  • Moonbeam Nature Trail - A beautiful hike through a forested area with lots of wildlife sightings opportunities.
  • Kap-Vo-George Reservoir - A peaceful spot located outside of the town and a great place for fishing, boating, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapuskasing

Kapuskasing has many beautiful trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Riverside Murals Walking Tour (1.8km) - A self-guided tour that takes visitors through the historic downtown area, highlighting some of the town’s most important buildings, public art, and landmarks.
  • Kapuskasing River Waterfront Trail (2.4km) - A scenic trail that follows the Kapuskasing River and provides views of the town’s industrial and natural landscapes.
  • Moonbeam Nature Trail (2km) - A beautiful hike through a forested area with lots of wildlife sightings opportunities.
  • Kapuskasing Demonstration Forest (15km) - A series of trails and roads that take hikers and cyclists through a managed forest area with lots of education about the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Kapuskasing: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours you can do in Kapuskasing:

  • Polar Bear Habitat Tour - A guided tour of the habitat where you can learn about polar bears and their conservation, and see them up close.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking - The Kapuskasing River is a great place for paddling and exploring the surrounding wildlife and landscapes.
  • Fishing - Kapuskasing has many rivers and reservoirs that are great for fishing. Book a guide or rent equipment at one of the local outfitters.
  • ATV Tours - Rent an ATV and explore the surrounding forests and trails with a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kapuskasing

Day 1: Riverside Murals Walking Tour and Heritage Walking Tour; Lunch at The Ridge Roadhouse; Kapuskasing River Waterfront Trail.

Day 2: Kapuskasing Lumberjack Heritage Festival; Dinner at The Hitching Post.

Day 3: Visit the Polar Bear Habitat in the morning; Lunch at Little Joe’s; Afternoon canoe or kayak rental along the Kapuskasing River.

Day 4: Moonbeam Nature Trail hike in the morning; Lunch at Dunn Place Inn; Afternoon fishing at Kap-Vo-George Reservoir.

Day 5: ATV Tour in the morning; Lunch at Al’s Pizzeria; Afternoon shopping for local souvenirs.

Day 6: Kapuskasing Demonstration Forest hike in the morning; Lunch at Cedar Meadows Resort; Afternoon massage, spa or a relaxing swim at the Cedar Meadows Resort.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapuskasing

Kapuskasing has a variety of restaurants that offer delicious food, from traditional Canadian cuisine to international specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine - A Canadian classic made with French fries, gravy, and cheese curds.
  • Wild Rice Pilaf - A local dish that features wild rice mixed with vegetables and seasonings.
  • Burger with Perogies - A unique twist by adding perogies on top of the patty for that savory yet starchy bite.

Local Beverages

  • Crow’s Black IPA - A local beer made by Crow’s Nest Bar and Grill that features a deep and rich flavor that appeals to those who love a good IPA.
  • Northern Delight Cocktail - A cocktail that incorporates local cranberry juice with vodka and simple syrup to produce this refreshing Northern delight drink.

  • The Hillcrest Motel - A convenient stay with good quality service at a reasonable price.
  • Northgate Inn & Suites - A cozy stay with a great breakfast included.

What to Buy in Kapuskasing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kapuskasing has many locally made products that would be perfect souvenirs to take home:

  • Birch Bark Baskets - Made by local artisans, these baskets are unique to the area, and are not only beautiful but also practical.
  • Wild Rice - A locally grown crop that is harvested in the fall, and can be used in a variety of dishes at home.
  • Bear Claws - Made of soapstone, these iconic Canadian souvenirs make great decorations or paperweights.

Local Culture and Customs of Kapuskasing

Kapuskasing has a unique cultural mix that blends the traditions of the indigenous peoples with the early settlers’ heritage. The town is proud of its history and has many events and festivals that celebrate its cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Kapuskasing

The best time to visit Kapuskasing is during the summer months of May to September when you can enjoy the outdoors and participate in many of the town’s festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kapuskasing

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and local cuisine.
  • Visit during the Lumberjack Heritage Festival in July if possible.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural attractions.
  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Disrespect the local wildlife.

Where to Go Next from Kapuskasing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some recommended destinations and excursions to check out while you’re in the area:

  • Timmins - A larger city located about two hours away from Kapuskasing, known for its mining history and cultural attractions.
  • Pukaskwa National Park - A scenic national park located on the shore of Lake Superior and home to many hiking trails and wildlife sightings.
  • Cochrane - A small town located about an hour away from Kapuskasing with beautiful rugged landscape and featuring the Northern Ontario Railroad Museum and Heritage Center.
