Introduction to Temiskaming Shores

Temiskaming Shores is a charming city situated in Northeastern Ontario, Canada. Located near the Quebec border, the city is situated on the shores of Lake Temiskaming, which acts as a natural boundary between Ontario and Quebec. The area is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it an excellent destination for a relaxing vacation. In this guide, we’ll explore some of the top attractions and activities that you can enjoy in Temiskaming Shores to help you plan your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Temiskaming Shores

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Liskeard and Area Historical Society: This museum is a must-visit for history buffs and features exhibits on the area’s past, including a replica of a 1920’s general store and a blacksmith shop.
  • Haileybury Heritage Museum: Located in Haileybury, this museum showcases the history of the town, including exhibits on mining and the famous “Lady Evelyn” steamboat.
  • Cobalt Mining Museum: Learn about the history of Cobalt, a town known for its silver mining industry, at this museum. It has exhibits on mining equipment, geological history, and the town’s role in shaping Canada’s history.
  • Little Claybelt Homesteaders Museum: Housed in the historic 1905 Harvey Township Hall, the museum provides insight into the early homesteading days of the area.
  • Devil’s Rock National Historic Site: Located in nearby Temagami, Devil’s Rock is a historic landmark that offers breathtaking views of the Northern Ontario wilderness and Lake Temiskaming.

Natural Attractions of Temiskaming Shores: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kipawa River Waterfall Trail: Enjoy hiking, fishing, and scenic views along this trail. The trail is named after the Kipawa River, which flows through the region and is known for its waterfalls and rapids.
  • Famous Haileybury Golf Club: Tee off at this 9-hole golf course with stunning views of Lake Temiskaming and Cobalt hills
  • Lake Temiskaming Beach Park: Relax and soak up the sun at this park which has a sandy beach, playgrounds, and water sports activities.
  • Poitras Park: Take a peaceful walk or enjoy a picnic in this park, which also acts as a viewing point for the lake and the town skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Temiskaming Shores

  • Earl’s Trail: This 16 km trail runs alongside the Kipawa River and is perfect for hiking, mountain biking, and bird-watching.
  • Temiskaming Nordic Ski Club Trails: Make your way through the beautiful northern Ontario wilderness on these groomed trails, which are open year-round and perfect for hiking or snowshoeing in winter.

Best Things to Do in Temiskaming Shores: Activities and Tours

  • Lake Temiskaming Tour Boat: Take a tour around the lake on this 47-passenger boat. The tour includes a visit to Devil’s Rock and historical commentary about the region.
  • Temiskaming Art Gallery: This gallery features exhibitions from local artists and a collection of over 3000 artworks.
  • Temiskaming Shores Public Library: If you’re looking for something to do indoors, head to the public library which has reading programs, book clubs, and a selection of books and magazines for adults and kids.

Week-Long Itinerary for Temiskaming Shores

Day Activity
1. Start with Lake Tour Boat and visit Devil’s Rock
2. Go Museum Hopping in Haileybury to learn about Cobalt mining history
3. Stop at the New Liskeard farmer’s market, featuring local produce, baked goods and artisanal products.

Food and Drink Scene of Temiskaming Shores

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtiere: This savory meat pie is a Quebecois classic and can be found at various restaurants, including Rooster’s Bar & Grill.
  • Pickerel (Walleye): Fresh from Lake Temiskaming, grilled or fried pickerel is a must-try dish at The Waterfront Inn.
  • Poutine: This Canadian favorite features French fries, gravy, and cheese curds and can be found at local eateries like The Station.
  • Maple Syrup: Ontario is famous for its maple syrup, and you can enjoy this local product in various forms, including maple butter, syrup, and candy.

Local Beverages

  • Northern Ontario Wines: Try some red or white wines made from grapes grown in the area at Thornloe Cheese and Wine Store.
  • Beer: Try some locally brewed beer at Rooster’s Bar & Grill or The Waterfront Inn.
  • Hot Beverages: Warm up with a cup of coffee or hot chocolate made with locally produced milk at the Java Connection Coffee Shop.

Where to Stay in Temiskaming Shores: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Waterfront Inn: This hotel is located on the shore of Lake Temiskaming and offers mesmerizing views to its visitors. It features 30 rooms with all necessary amenities for a comfortable stay.
  • Cedar Gables Lodge and Residence: This is a charming B&B located in Cobalt. The historic and stylishly decorated property has five spacious rooms with an inviting atmosphere where visitors can sit back, relax and enjoy.
  • Haileybury Beach Motel: Offering affordable stays, Haileybury Beach Motel is only a minute’s walk from Lake Temiskaming Beach Park. The motel has twelve clean, tidy rooms with the essential amenities needed for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Temiskaming Shores: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thornloe Cheese: Thornloe Cheese Factory produces award-winning artisan cheese with the milk from Northern Ontario Dairy farms located near the region. Try cheese curds, cheddar or brie!
  • Paintings and Art: Temiskaming Art Gallery store features locally-produced artisan works including paintings, pottery, and jewelry, to bring back home as souvenirs.
  • Basketry and Woodworking: Visit Pioneer Crafts/Restaurant in New Liskeard to buy handcrafted wooden baskets, toys, and trinkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Temiskaming Shores

Temiskaming Shores is a multicultural city that celebrates diversity. English, French, and Indigenous languages are spoken in the region. Visitors should respect the culture of the region and be courteous to the locals. The area also has a thriving art and music scene, so make sure to include some cultural events in your itinerary.

Best Time to Visit Temiskaming Shores

The best time to visit Temiskaming Shores is from May to October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season in Temiskaming Shores is particularly stunning, with the changing colors of the leaves and crisp weather providing a picturesque setting for hiking or cycling. Winter, with its snowy landscape, is perfect for snowmobiling and other winter activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Temiskaming Shores

Temiskaming Shores is a generally safe city for visitors with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take standard safety precautions such as keeping valuable items secure and not leaving bags unattended in public areas.

Health in Temiskaming Shores

The city has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical services at the Temiskaming Hospital. Moreover, make sure to carry enough medication and obtain adequate travel health insurance before arriving, especially if planning to engage in adventure activities.

Getting to Temiskaming Shores: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Temiskaming Shores

The nearest airport is Earlton-Timiskaming Regional Airport, located around 22 kilometers from Temiskaming Shores. Another option is North Bay Jack Garland Airport, located around 155 kilometers from the city.

Bus Stations Near Temiskaming Shores

Ontario Northland Limited operates bus services that connect Temiskaming Shores with nearby areas, including North Bay and Toronto. The bus station is located at 45 Government Rd W, Kirkland Lake, ON.

Other Options

Renting a car is another viable option to reach Temiskaming Shores, with several car rental agencies operating in the area.

How to Get Around in Temiskaming Shores

The best way to get around the city is by car. However, visitors can also choose to explore the region via bicycle or bike rentals available in the town. Taxis and public transit systems also operate in the area, providing another option for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Temiskaming Shores: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rouyn-Noranda, Quebec: Only a two-hour drive from Temiskaming Shores, it is a multicultural city with excellent food and drink scene and recreational activities.
  • North Bay, Ontario: A 2.5-hour drive from Temiskaming Shores, North Bay offers numerous outdoor and cultural activities worth exploring.
  • Mont-Tremblant, Quebec: Located around 190 kilometers from the city, it is an exciting winter destination for skiing and snowboarding.

Conclusion

Temiskaming Shores is a hidden gem of Northern Ontario, Manitoba, where visitors can enjoy a mix of relaxing and adventurous activities. From exploring the rich history to taking in the stunning natural beauty, the city has something for everyone. With this travel guide, you can plan your trip to Temiskaming Shores with ease and make the most of your visit to this beautiful town.
