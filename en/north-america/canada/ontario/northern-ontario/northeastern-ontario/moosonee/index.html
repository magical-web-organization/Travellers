Introduction to Moosonee

Moosonee is a town situated on the Moose River, about 19 miles east of James Bay. The town is known for its lush natural beauty, historical significance, and unique culture. Moosonee is one of the oldest settlements in Ontario, with a population of just over 3500. This comprehensive tour guide will explore the Best Places to Visit, Activities, Must-Try Dishes, Culture, Safety, and Transportation Options in Moosonee.

List of Places to Visit in Moosonee

A visit to Moosonee is not complete without exploring its cultural and natural attractions. Here is a list of the must-visit places in Moosonee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Canadian National Railway Station: Explore the historic train station that was once a hub for transportation of lumber and other goods within the region.
  • Moose Factory Island: This island is just across the river from Moosonee and is home to the Cree Nation’s Museum, an exhibition of First Nations’ art and culture, and the historic Hudson Bay Company post.
  • St. Thomas’ Anglican Church: A unique church built in 1904 and designed to resemble a teepee, a tribute to local indigenous culture.

Natural Attractions of Moosonee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • James Bay: With its rich biodiversity of wildlife, James Bay is perfect for kayaking, canoeing, fishing, and observing wildlife.
  • Polar Bear Provincial Park: This breathtaking park provides visitors with a chance to see polar bears, beluga whales, and other local wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Moose River: Hiking and fishing can be enjoyed along the banks of this stunning river. Explore the peaceful surroundings and breathe in the fresh air.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moosonee

Moosonee provides plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are some hiking and biking routes that are worth exploring:

  • Polar Bear Habitat Nature Trail: A 1.5-mile trek through the wilderness of the Polar Bear Provincial Park.
  • Owl River Lookout Trail: A 5.6-mile loop with stunning views of the Moose and James Rivers, Moose Factory Island, and Cochrane Bay.
  • Moose River Heritage Trail: Follow the beautiful Moose River by foot or bike, a trail that leads you to Moose Factory Island and Hudson Bay after 4 hours of walking.

Best Things to Do in Moosonee: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring the natural and cultural attractions, here are some other activities you can do while in Moosonee:

  • Northern Lights Tour: Witnessing the northern lights is a unique experience which is possible nearly year-round in Moosonee.
  • Fishing Tour: Experience the thrill of fishing in Northern Ontario. Local fishing tour operators provide the gear, boats, and expertise.
  • Moose and Wildlife Tour: Take a guided trip to observe moose, bears, caribou, and other native wildlife in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moosonee

For those interested in spending a week exploring Moosonee, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a local hotel
  • Day 2-4: Natural Attractions: kayak the James Bay, explore the Polar Bear Provincial Park.
  • Day 5: Moose Factory Island tour, explore the Cree Nation’s Museum and Hudson Bay Company post.
  • Day 6: Visit the Canadian National Railway, experience life in the station, and learn about its history.
  • Day 7: Shopping and exploring the historic downtown area of Moosonee.

Food and Drink Scene of Moosonee

Moosonee has a unique dining scene, with plenty of options to satisfy your appetite. The town is famous for its fish and game dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock: A pastry-style bread native to indigenous cuisine and available in many local eateries.
  • Roast Duck: This dish originated in the local Cree culinary tradition and prepared roasted in a clay oven or slow-cooked with potatoes and vegetables.
  • Smoked Trout: A specialty dish made from local trout, which has been smoked in traditional methods, and available in many restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Berry Juice: The most popular local beverage made of local berries, especially blueberries, raspberries, strawberries, and blackcurrants.
  • Cree Tea: A locally produced herbal tea that is rich in aroma and beneficial medicinal qualities.
  • Beer: Some of the local breweries like the Moose River Brewing Company make small-batch beers from locally sourced barley, hops, and water.

Where to Stay in Moosonee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Moosonee is small, it offers a range of accommodation options from hotels, guesthouses, and hostels to meet any budget. You could book the Nelson River Bed and Breakfast, the Hudson Bay Lodge, or the Polar Bear Inn guesthouse in Moosonee.

What to Buy in Moosonee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the keepsakes to bring back home that will remind you of your adventures in Moosonee:

  • Cree Artworks: Look out for the handmade Cree art by the local indigenous people, including woodcarvings, beadwork, pottery, and other traditional crafts.
  • Fur Products: Purchase handmade fur products from local artisanal shops, including hats, mittens, and clothing made from beaver, fox, and wolf furs.
  • Local Food Products: Bring home a jar of locally made blueberry jam, birch syrup, bannock mix, or moose jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Moosonee

Moosonee’s rich culture and customs are reflected in its food, art, and festivals. The Moose Cree First Nation people have a deep connection with their heritage and offer visitors a rare chance to learn about local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Moosonee

The best time to visit Moosonee is during the mild months from May to September. The weather is pleasant for outdoor activities, and there is plenty of daylight for exploring the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moosonee

When visiting Moosonee, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the cultural traditions and customs of Moosonee’s people.
  • Pack appropriately for outdoor activities and bring insect repellent and sunscreen.
  • Learn about the wildlife you may encounter before you hike or venture out into nature.

Don’t:

  • Approach wildlife or interfere with their habitats.
  • Leave trash or litter in Moosonee’s natural areas.
  • Wear clothes or accessories that disrespect local customs.

Safety and Health

Before visiting Moosonee, there are some safety and health concerns to be aware of:

Safety in Moosonee

  • While crime is low, visitors should be cautious of their belongings in public places.
  • Visitors should also be wary of hazards from wild animals and rough terrain.
  • Do not go off-trail or approach wildlife without an experienced guide.

Health in Moosonee

  • Visitors should be prepared for insects and take measures to prevent mosquito and tick bites.
  • Visitors should also bring and use insect repellents and sunscreen regularly.
  • Health care facilities are available in Moosonee, but visitors should plan to bring their basic medical needs supplies due to limited availability and high costs in Northern Ontario.

Getting to Moosonee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Although Moosonee is remote, there are several ways to reach it from other parts of Canada.

Airports Near Moosonee

The nearest airport to Moosonee is the Moosonee Airport (YMO), which offers direct flights to Timmins and is the gateway to other far-reaching Northern Ontario communities.

Bus Stations Near Moosonee

Ontario Northland Bus Line services several Northern Ontario communities, including Moosonee, and offers visitors an affordable way to reach the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to take the Ontario Northland Polar Bear Express train from Cochrane to Moosonee, a scenic and unique way to reach the town.

How to Get Around in Moosonee

The most recommended way of getting around Moosonee is on foot or by bike. Taxi services are limited and expensive, but visitors can rent kayaks or canoes to explore the rivers and waterways. Small boat tours and fishing charters are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Moosonee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Moosonee’s remote location makes it an excellent starting point for exploring the surrounding regions, including:

  • Pukaskwa National Park: An excellent destination from Moosonee, Pukaskwa offers stunning vistas and hiking opportunities along the shores of Lake Superior.
  • Timmins: Visitors could take a short flight from Moosonee to Timmins to see the Shania Twain exhibit, a feature of the National Mining Museum.
  • Sudbury: Another city worth visiting is Sudbury, where visitors can go underground to see the Sudbury mine - the world’s deepest nickel mine.

Conclusion

Moosonee is a stunning town that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Northern Ontario’s history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring the natural wonders of James Bay, hiking the Polar Bear Provincial Park trails to experiencing the indigenous culture and tasting the local cuisine, there is something spectacular for everyone in Moosonee. Plan a trip to Moosonee and discover why it is one of Canada’s hidden gems.
