Introduction to Cobalt

Welcome to Cobalt, a charming town located in Northeastern Ontario amid stunning natural surroundings, making it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Cobalt is a former silver mining town with a rich history and diverse tourist attractions that showcase its natural beauty. In this article, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive guide to the best things to do in Cobalt, Ontario.

List of Places to Visit in Cobalt

Here’s a list of top-rated attractions you must visit while exploring this charming town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cobalt Mining Museum: This museum showcases the town’s history of silver mining with an extensive collection of historical mining artifacts, rocks, minerals, and fossils.

  • Cobalt Heritage Silver Trail: A historical walking tour that highlights the town’s mining history, including its industrial heritage, architectural landmarks, and scenic beauty.

  • Historic Train Station: A heritage building that provides visitors with a glimpse of the town’s golden era. The station has a museum that displays artifacts from railway history.

  • Shatford Memorial Museum: A history museum that showcases the town’s rich history and natural surroundings. The museum displays beauty in regional plants, animals, and rocks.

Natural Attractions of Cobalt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Temiskaming: A stunning natural feature with crystal-clear water that offers an array of watersports and other seasonal activities such as kayaking, fishing, and swimming.

  • Cobalt Hill: A natural landmark that offers scenic overlooks of the town and the surrounding countryside. Hiking and wildlife viewing are popular activities here.

  • Cobalt Military Plane Crash Site: A unique and quiet outdoorsy attraction that provides visitors with an opportunity to explore the wreckage of an RCAF aircraft.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cobalt

Cobalt is the ultimate outdoor destination, offering a range of hiking and biking trails that showcase the town’s pristine natural surroundings. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Cobalt Brook Trail: A scenic trail with serene views of the brook amidst a peaceful and calm environment.

  • Mississippi River Trail: A biking route that meanders alongside the Mississippi River, offering breathtaking views of the countryside.

  • Temiskaming Abitibi Trail: A hiking and cycling trail that connects the towns of New Liskeard and Haileybury, showcasing the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Cobalt: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do while in Cobalt:

  • Cobalt Mining Tour: This tour provides visitors with a fascinating glimpse into the town’s mining history and the processing of silver ore.

  • Laurentian Escarpment Tour: A tour that explores the town’s scenic beauty, including its geological features, natural resources, and biodiversity.

  • Silverman Tour: A unique and exciting tour that showcases the town’s mining history in an immersive experience that involves underground exploration.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cobalt

Cobalt is an adventurous and charming town with many attractions that require an extended stay to explore fully. Below is a week-long itinerary for Cobalt that highlights the town’s top attractions:

Food and Drink Scene of Cobalt

Experience Cobalt’s cuisine by trying out the following dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A local favorite that features Cod battered and deep-fried to golden perfection.

  • Tourtiere: A delicious meat pie that is a traditional dish of French Canadian culture.

  • Poutine: A classic, mouth-watering Canadian meal that consists of French fries, cheese curds and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Syrup Ale: A unique beer made using local maple syrup, giving it a sweet taste with a hint of maple flavour.

  • Boreal Forest Tea: A local herbal tea made from native herbs found in the boreal forest, including Labrador tea, mint, and chamomile.

Where to Stay in Cobalt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cobalt has a range of accommodation options, including:

  • Cobalt Heritage Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Cobalt, offering an elegant and atmospheric atmosphere.

  • Hilltop Motel: A budget-friendly motel located on Highway 11, a short distance from Cobalt Hill and the Mississippi River Trail.

  • Reid’s Guest House: A cozy bed and breakfast that offers a personalized experience with a homey touch.

What to Buy in Cobalt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cobalt has a range of unique souvenirs to bring back home, including:

  • Silver Jewelry: Cobalt is famous for its silver mining history, and visitors can find beautifully crafted silver jewelry that showcases the town’s heritage.

  • Maple Syrup and Maple Products: Maple Syrup is a famous Canadian delicacy, and in Cobalt, visitors can purchase a wide variety of maple syrup and maple products, including candies, syrups, and other maple-based products.

Local Culture and Customs of Cobalt

Cobalt’s culture is deeply intertwined with its mining history, and there are several festivals and events that reflect the town’s unique cultural customs. Visitors should try to attend the Cobalt Mining Heritage Festival and enjoy the unique culture of Cobalt.

Best Time to Visit Cobalt

Cobalt is a destination that is perfect to visit year-round, although the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cobalt

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and be mindful of your actions’ impact on the local ecosystem.

  • Tip your restaurant servers and housekeeping staff.

Don’t:

  • Dispose of trash haphazardly or break any environmental laws.

  • Ignore the town’s mining heritage or local customs, as it’s an essential part of the town’s identity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cobalt

Cobalt is a safe town to visit, and visitors should still take reasonable precautions, such as keeping valuables close to them and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Cobalt

Cobalt has a health centre that provides medical and emergency services to visitors and residents.

Getting to Cobalt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cobalt

The closest airport to Cobalt is North Bay Airport, which is located about 102 km south of Cobalt. The airport has several car rental services available, and visitors can easily reach Cobalt by car.

Bus Stations Near Cobalt

Greyhound Buses operate from North Bay to Temiskaming Shores. Visitors can take these buses and then rent a car to reach Cobalt.

Other Options

Driving remains the most convenient mode of transportation due to Cobalt’s location, but visitors can also access Cobalt through VIA Rail and Ontario Northland Railways.

How to Get Around in Cobalt

Cobalt has a considerable network of roads that are well maintained and accessible to visitors. Visitors can rent a car or use taxis to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Cobalt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations nearby that visitors should explore after visiting Cobalt, such as:

  • Temiskaming Shores: A city located about 20 km away from Cobalt, with an aesthetic view of Lake Temiskaming.

  • Haileybury: A scenic town located about 10 km away from Cobalt, with several cultural attractions, including the Haileybury Heritage Museum.

  • New Liskeard: A beautiful town located about 30 km away from Cobalt, which is a hub for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking trails.

Conclusion

Cobalt is a must-visit travel destination, with a rich history, natural beauty, and a thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re exploring the town’s mining history, hiking through its scenic landscapes, or enjoying its food and drink scene, Cobalt is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories.
