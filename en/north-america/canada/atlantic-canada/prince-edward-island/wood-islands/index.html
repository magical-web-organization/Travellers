Introduction to Wood Islands

Located in Prince Edward Island, Canada, Wood Islands is known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural history, and delicious local cuisine. The island is home to numerous lighthouses, museums, and historic sites that offer visitors a glimpse into its fascinating past. Additionally, the area boasts an extensive network of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for beachcombing, kayaking, and fishing.

In this Wood Islands Travel Guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, the best things to do, and the local culture and customs of this stunning destination.

List of Places to Visit in Wood Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wood Island Lighthouse
  2. Northumberland Ferries Limited
  3. The Rossignol Estate Winery
  4. Sir Andrew MacPhail Homestead
  5. St. Peter’s Cathedral

Wood Islands is home to numerous cultural landmarks that provide visitors with insights into the island’s history and traditions. The Wood Island Lighthouse is a must-visit attraction that offers sweeping views of the Northumberland Strait, while the Rossignol Estate Winery is perfect for wine lovers looking to discover new flavors. Additionally, history buffs can visit the Sir Andrew MacPhail Homestead and St. Peter’s Cathedral to immerse themselves in the island’s past.

Natural Attractions of Wood Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Greenwich Beach
  2. Confederation Trail
  3. Basin Head Provincial Park
  4. Orwell Corner Historic Village
  5. Green Gables

The natural beauty of Wood Islands is unparalleled, and there are a plethora of outdoor activities that visitors can enjoy. Hiking enthusiasts can explore the stunning landscapes along the Confederation Trail, while beach lovers can bask in the sun at Greenwich Beach or Basin Head Provincial Park. Additionally, history buffs can take a trip back in time at Orwell Corner Historic Village, or explore the world of Anne of Green Gables at the Green Gables Heritage Place.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wood Islands

If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure in Wood Islands, hiking and biking are great ways to explore the picturesque landscapes. The Confederation Trail is a 449 km trail that passes through forests, farmland, and coastal regions and is perfect for cyclists, hikers, and cross-country skiers. The trail is well-maintained and offers stunning views of the island’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Wood Islands: Activities and Tours

  1. Kayaking and Paddle Boarding
  2. Whale Watching
  3. Fishing Tours
  4. ATV Tours
  5. Island Helicopter Tours

Wood Islands has something to offer for every type of traveler, and there are a plethora of activities and tours to explore. Kayaking and paddleboarding are popular options for water enthusiasts, while whale watching and fishing tours are perfect for those who want to experience marine life up close. Additionally, visitors can explore the island’s rugged terrain with ATV tours or take a bird’s eye view of the natural beauty with island helicopter tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wood Islands

If you’re planning to spend a week in Wood Islands, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Wood Island Lighthouse and take a scenic walk along the shoreline
2 Explore the Greenwich Beach and its protected dune system
3 Take an eco-tour of the Northumberland Strait and watch for seals, porpoises and other marine life
4 Visit the Sir Andrew MacPhail Homestead and learn about PEI’s rural life
5 Take a day trip to Prince Edward Island National Park
6 Take an ATV tour to explore the island’s rugged terrain
7 Say goodbye to the island with a sunset boat tour

Food and Drink Scene of Wood Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  1. PEI Lobster
  2. Malpeque Oysters
  3. Island Farmhouse Cheddar
  4. Blueberry Pie
  5. PEI Potato

The food scene in Wood Island is a treat for foodies, and there are a plethora of local delicacies to explore. PEI lobster and Malpeque oysters are a must-try for seafood lovers, while Island Farmhouse Cheddar and Blueberry Pie are flavors unique to the island. Additionally, the PEI potato is an important agricultural product for the region and is used in numerous dishes.

Local Beverages

  1. PEI Brewing Company
  2. Upstreet Craft Brewing
  3. Rossignol Estate Winery
  4. Matos Winery
  5. Deep Roots Distillery

To complement its culinary scene, Wood Islands has a thriving drinks scene, which offers visitors a range of options to indulge in local beverages. The PEI Brewing Company and Upstreet Craft Brewing are popular breweries that serve up locally crafted beer, while the Rossignol Estate Winery and Matos Winery offer visitors a chance to discover new wines. Additionally, the Deep Roots Distillery is a perfect spot for those looking to taste locally created spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Wood Islands

Wood Islands has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its numerous museums and historic landmarks. Visitors can learn about the island’s traditions and customs by exploring its cultural sites. Additionally, Islanders are known for their friendliness and hospitality, and visitors can experience this firsthand by interacting with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Wood Islands

The best time to visit Wood Islands is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild, and the island is bustling with activity. Additionally, fall is a great time for visitors who want to avoid the crowds and enjoy the fall foliage.

  3. Explore the island’s natural beauty by hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wood Islands

Wood Islands is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take standard safety precautions. Lock your car doors, avoid unlit areas at night, and always keep an eye on your belongings. Additionally, if you’re engaging in any outdoor activities, make sure to check weather conditions before venturing out.

Health in Wood Islands

There are no significant health risks in Wood Islands, but visitors should take standard health precautions such as staying hydrated and wearing sunscreen when spending time outdoors in the sun.

Getting to Wood Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wood Islands

The nearest airport to Wood Islands is Charlottetown Airport, which is approximately 60 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle service to reach Wood Islands.

Bus Stations Near Wood Islands

The nearest bus station to Wood Islands is in Charlottetown, which is approximately 60 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Wood Islands from there.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Wood Islands by ferry from Caribou, Nova Scotia. The Northumberland Ferries Limited operates a ferry service between the two destinations and is an excellent way to reach the island.

How to Get Around in Wood Islands

The best way to explore Wood Islands is by car. Visitors can rent a vehicle from Charlottetown Airport or from various rental companies on the island. Additionally, there are tour operators that offer guided tours of the island, which is another option.

Where to Go Next from Wood Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Charlottetown
  2. Fort Amherst
  3. Cavendish Beach
  4. Green Gables Heritage Place

Wood Islands is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Charlottetown, the capital of Prince Edward Island, is a must-visit destination with its historic landmarks and world-class restaurants. Additionally, visitors can explore Fort Amherst, a national historic site located in nearby eastern PEI. Finally, don’t miss the chance to visit Cavendish Beach and Green Gables Heritage Place.

Conclusion

Wood Islands is a perfect destination for those looking to explore Canada’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and delicious culinary scene, there’s something for every type of traveler. Plan your trip today and get ready to experience the warmth and hospitality of this beautiful island.
