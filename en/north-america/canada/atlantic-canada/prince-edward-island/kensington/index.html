Introduction to Kensington

Kensington is a charming town located in Prince Edward Island, Atlantic Canada. Known for its picturesque landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Kensington is a destination that blends historic charm with modern amenities. Whether you’re a nature lover, history enthusiast, or foodie, Kensington has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kensington

If you’re planning a trip to Kensington, here is a list of places that you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kensington Heritage Museum - Showcasing the town’s agricultural, industrial, and social heritage.
  • Canadian Potato Museum - Explore the history of the potato industry in Canada.
  • Kensington Railway Station - A designated heritage railway station used to showcase the town’s rich railway heritage.

Natural Attractions of Kensington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Darnley Greens Provincial Park - A 9-hole golf course, campsite, and picnic area along the coast of Darnley Basin.
  • Malpeque Bay - Known for its red-sand beaches, oyster beds, and stunning sunsets.
  • Confederation Trail - A former railway line turned into a scenic trail running through picturesque landscapes, charming communities, and stunning seascapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kensington

Hiking and biking are popular outdoor activities in Kensington, and there are numerous trails and routes to explore:

  • Kensington Intermediate Senior Hiking Trail - A 2.5-kilometre loop trail through woodland terrain with interpretive signs and benches.
  • Side Road Trail - A 12-kilometre trail with breathtaking views of the countryside and Gulf of St. Lawrence.
  • Confederation Trail - A 449-kilometre multi-use trail for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling.

Best Things to Do in Kensington: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Kensington:

  • Garden of the Gulf Museum - A guided tour of the historic town hall that showcases the town’s agricultural heritage.
  • Darnley Greens Provincial Park - Golfing, camping, and hiking.
  • Malpeque Bay Kayak Tours - Guided tours exploring the stunning coastline of Malpeque Bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kensington

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kensington, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Kensington Heritage Museum and the town’s historic district.
2 Play a round of golf at Darnley Greens Provincial Park and enjoy a picnic by the coast.
3 Take a guided tour of the Canadian Potato Museum and enjoy a potato-themed lunch.
4 Hike the Side Road Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the countryside.
5 Rent a kayak and explore Malpeque Bay, followed by a seafood dinner.
6 Bike the Confederation Trail and visit some of the charming communities along the way.
7 Relax and shop for souvenirs at the local craft markets and boutiques.

Food and Drink Scene of Kensington

Kensington has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects its agricultural heritage and coastal location:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Potato dishes, such as poutine, baked potatoes, and potato soup.
  • Fresh seafood, such as lobster, mussels, and oysters.
  • Local cheeses, such as cheddar and gouda.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beers from local breweries, such as Upstreet Craft Brewing.
  • Local wines from wineries, such as Island Hill Farm.
  • Hot apple cider from local orchards, such as Van Kampen’s Greenhouses.

Where to Stay in Kensington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kensington has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference:

  • The Anchor Motel - A family-friendly motel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • The Lookout Inn - A historic inn with breathtaking views of Malpeque Bay.
  • The Pineapple Inn - A charming Victorian-style guesthouse with a garden and patio.

What to Buy in Kensington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kensington has plenty of artisanal products and souvenirs to bring home:

  • Local cheeses from Prince Edward Island Cheese Company.
  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics from Island Made Giftware.
  • Woolen products and yarns from MacAusland’s Woolen Mills.

Local Culture and Customs of Kensington

Kensington has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its agricultural, industrial, and social history. Islanders are known for their hospitality, and visitors are welcomed with warmth and friendliness.

Best Time to Visit Kensington

The best time to visit Kensington is from May to October when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kensington

Do:

  • Learn about the town’s history and cultural heritage.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take care of the natural environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local traffic rules and regulations.
  • Litter or leave trash behind in natural areas.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kensington

Kensington is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take precautions while enjoying outdoor activities and natural areas. Be aware of changing weather conditions, stay on designated trails, and follow safety guidelines for water sports.

Health in Kensington

Kensington has modern medical facilities and emergency services. Visitors should have proper health insurance coverage and take necessary precautions while traveling.

Getting to Kensington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kensington

  • Charlottetown Airport - About 45 minutes’ drive from Kensington.

Bus Stations Near Kensington

  • Maritime Bus - Serves Kensington with daily bus service to other cities in Atlantic Canada.

Other Options

  • Car rentals - Available at Charlottetown Airport and in nearby towns.
  • Private shuttle services and taxis - Available for hire in Charlottetown and Kensington.

How to Get Around in Kensington

Kensington is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, car rentals and taxis are available for longer trips and transport to nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Kensington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kensington is a great base for exploring other attractions and destinations in Prince Edward Island:

  • Charlottetown - The provincial capital, about 30 minutes’ drive from Kensington.
  • Cavendish - Home of the famous Anne of Green Gables heritage site, about 20 minutes’ drive from Kensington.
  • North Cape - The westernmost point of Prince Edward Island, about 1.5 hours’ drive from Kensington.

Conclusion

Kensington is a hidden gem in Prince Edward Island, with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historic sites, or charming communities, Kensington has something to offer for all types of travelers. Plan your visit today and discover the beauty of Kensington!
