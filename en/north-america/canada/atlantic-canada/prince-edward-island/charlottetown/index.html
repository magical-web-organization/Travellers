Introduction to Charlottetown

Charlottetown is a small city located in Prince Edward Island, on the eastern coast of Canada. It is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and rich history. Visitors to Charlottetown can expect to be captivated by the charming streets lined with brightly colored houses, stunning parks, and friendly locals. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Charlottetown has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Charlottetown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Charlottetown is steeped in history and culture, and there are many museums and historical sites to explore. Some of the must-visit historical attractions include:

  • Province House National Historic Site: a restored neoclassical building that served as the birthplace of Canada’s confederation.

  • The Beaconsfield Historic House: a stunning Victorian mansion that offers a glimpse into the lives of Charlottetown’s wealthy residents in the 19th century.

  • The Prince Edward Island Regiment Museum: a war museum that explores the military history of PEI, including its role in both world wars.

Natural Attractions of Charlottetown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Charlottetown is surrounded by natural beauty, and visitors can explore the great outdoors in many ways. The city has many parks and beaches, making it an ideal destination for hiking, biking, or just taking a leisurely stroll. Some of the best natural attractions in Charlottetown include:

  • Victoria Park: a 40-acre park that features walking trails, a swimming pool, tennis courts, and stunning views of the Charlottetown Harbour.

  • Confederation Trail: a 449 km long trail that runs the length of PEI, offering visitors a chance to explore the stunning rural landscapes of the island.

  • Brudenell River Provincial Park: a beautiful park that offers visitors a chance to explore the lush forests, marshes, and beaches of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charlottetown

Charlottetown is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many trails and routes to explore. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Charlottetown include:

  • Confederation Trail: a 449 km long trail that offers visitors a chance to explore the stunning rural landscapes of the island.

  • Charlottetown City Century Ride: a 100 km long bike route that takes you through Charlottetown’s historic neighborhoods and scenic countryside.

  • Victoria Park Trail: a 3 km walking trail that offers stunning views of the Charlottetown Harbour.

Best Things to Do in Charlottetown: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Charlottetown. Some of the must-try experiences include:

  • Anne of Green Gables: The Musical: a world-renowned musical that is based on the beloved book by L.M Montgomery.

  • Charlottetown Walking Tour: an informative and entertaining walking tour that takes visitors through the city’s historic neighborhoods and landmarks.

  • Sea Kayaking Tour: a unique way to explore the stunning coastline and waterways around Charlottetown.

Week-Long Itinerary for Charlottetown

If you are planning a week-long trip to Charlottetown, then here is a day-by-day itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore Charlottetown’s downtown, including the historic Province House and Beaconsfield Historic House. In the evening, catch a show at the Confederation Centre of the Arts.

  • Day 2: Take a sea kayaking tour or enjoy a leisurely bike ride along the Confederation Trail. In the evening, catch a sunset at Victoria Park.

  • Day 3: Visit the Charlottetown Farmers’ Market, where you can sample the best local produce and snacks. Later, take a scenic drive out to the north shore beaches, including Cavendish Beach.

  • Day 4: Take a day trip to explore the stunning rural landscapes of PEI. You can choose to visit Prince Edward Island National Park or Brudenell River Provincial Park.

  • Day 5: Visit the Greenwich Interpretation Centre, which features a unique floating boardwalk that takes visitors on a journey through a stunning coastal landscape.

  • Day 6: Take a trip to Green Gables Heritage Site, a historic landmark that inspired the famous book.

  • Day 7: Wrap up your trip by exploring downtown Charlottetown and enjoying some of the best food and drink in the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Charlottetown

Charlottetown has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants and pubs to choose from. Some must-try local dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: a classic Atlantic Canadian dish that features fresh lobster meat served on a soft bun.

  • PEI Mussels: PEI is known for its high-quality mussels, which are served in many restaurants around Charlottetown.

  • Poutine: a Canadian classic that features french fries topped with gravy and cheese curds.

Local Beverages

  • Cows Creamery Ice Cream: a local creamery that produces some of the best ice cream in the region.

  • Red Island Cider: a local cider company that produces a range of delicious apple ciders.

  • Upstreet Craft Brewing: a local brewery that produces a range of unique and delicious craft beers.

Where to Stay in Charlottetown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charlottetown has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Charlottetown include:

  • The Great George Hotel: a historic boutique hotel that offers luxurious accommodations in the heart of downtown Charlottetown.

  • Delta Hotels by Marriott Prince Edward: a modern hotel that offers stunning views of the Charlottetown Harbour.

  • HI Charlottetown Backpackers Inn: a budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable accommodations in a central location.

What to Buy in Charlottetown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Charlottetown is known for its unique and high-quality souvenirs and local products. Some must-buy items include:

  • Anne of Green Gables memorabilia: there are plenty of shops in Charlottetown that offer souvenirs and gifts based on the beloved character Anne of Green Gables.

  • Local artwork: Charlottetown is home to many talented artists, and there are plenty of galleries and shops where you can purchase unique and beautiful pieces.

  • Local handicrafts: PEI is known for its high-quality handmade crafts, including pottery, textiles, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Charlottetown

Charlottetown is known for its friendly locals and warm hospitality. Visitors to Charlottetown should expect to be greeted with a smile and a warm welcome. The local culture is heavily influenced by the sea, and many of the city’s traditions and festivals are centered around maritime culture.

Best Time to Visit Charlottetown

The best time to visit Charlottetown is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the shoulder season (May and October) can also be a great time to visit, as the crowds are smaller and the prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charlottetown

Do:

  • Try the local seafood: Charlottetown is known for its fresh and delicious seafood, and there are plenty of restaurants and markets where you can try the best the city has to offer.

  • Take a walking tour: Charlottetown’s historic neighborhoods and landmarks are best explored on foot.

Don’t:

  • Neglect sunscreen: Charlottetown can be very sunny, so it’s important to wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun’s rays.

  • Litter: Charlottetown is a beautiful city, and it’s important to keep it that way by disposing of your litter properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charlottetown

Charlottetown is a very safe city, and visitors can feel comfortable walking around at night. However, as with any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions.

Health in Charlottetown

Charlottetown has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. It’s important for visitors to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise.

Getting to Charlottetown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Charlottetown is easily accessible by air, bus, and car. Some of the main transportation options for getting to Charlottetown include:

Airports Near Charlottetown

  • Charlottetown Airport: the main airport that serves Charlottetown and the surrounding areas.

Bus Stations Near Charlottetown

  • Maritime Bus: the main bus company that offers service to Charlottetown and other destinations throughout the Maritimes.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: many car rental companies have offices at Charlottetown Airport, making it easy to rent a car and explore the island on your own.

  • Ferries: visitors can take a ferry from Nova Scotia to Charlottetown during the summer months.

How to Get Around in Charlottetown

Charlottetown is a very walkable city, and many of the city’s attractions are located within walking distance of downtown. Visitors can also use taxis, buses, or rental cars to get around. For those who enjoy biking, the Confederation Trail is a great way to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Charlottetown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Charlottetown is a great jumping-off point for exploring the rest of Prince Edward Island. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Prince Edward Island National Park: a stunning natural park that features hiking trails, beaches, and campgrounds.

  • Brudenell River Provincial Park: a beautiful park that offers visitors a chance to explore the lush forests, marshes, and beaches of the island.

  • Cavendish Beach: a must-visit destination for beach lovers, featuring stunning sand dunes and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Charlottetown is a charming and vibrant city that offers visitors a chance to explore the stunning natural beauty and rich culture of Prince Edward Island. Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, or delicious food, Charlottetown has something to offer. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well equipped to plan your perfect trip to Charlottetown.
