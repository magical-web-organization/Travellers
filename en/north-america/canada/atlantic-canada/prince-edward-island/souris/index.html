Introduction to Souris

Souris, located in Prince Edward Island, Atlantic Canada, is a charming town that boasts a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. The town is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures that attract visitors from different parts of the world. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family with kids, Souris has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover everything that you need to know to plan your trip to Souris.

List of Places to Visit in Souris

Souris offers a plethora of attractions that should definitely be on your itinerary. From culturally important museums to outdoor adventures, there is no shortage of things to do in Souris.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll love Souris. Here are some of the culturally important attractions that you should definitely visit:

  • Souris Lighthouse: The iconic red and white Souris Lighthouse is a must-see attraction in the town. You can take a tour to the top of the lighthouse and enjoy the stunning views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence.
  • St. Mary’s Church: Located in the heart of Souris, St. Mary’s Church is a 100-year-old heritage building that has played an important role in the town’s history.
  • Souris Railway Museum: If you’re interested in trains and railways, the Souris Railway Museum is the place to be. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits on the history of the railway in Souris.

Natural Attractions of Souris: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Souris is known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Basin Head Provincial Park: This park is home to the famous “Singing Sands” beach, which is known for its stunning white sand and unique sound when you walk on it.
  • East Point Lighthouse: The East Point Lighthouse offers breathtaking views of the ocean and the surrounding countryside. It’s a great place to go for a walk and take in the beautiful scenery.
  • Red Point Provincial Park: This park is home to a beautiful beach, hiking trails, and a campground. It’s a great place for a family picnic or a day trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Souris

Souris offers a plethora of hiking and biking routes that allow you to explore the town’s natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Some of the top routes include:

  • Confederation Trail: This 279-kilometer trail runs through the heart of Prince Edward Island and offers a scenic route for hiking and biking enthusiasts.
  • Souris Glen Nature Trail: This trail is located in the Souris Wildlife Sanctuary and offers a beautiful and peaceful hike through the forest.
  • Basin Head Trail: This 1.2 kilometer trail offers stunning views of the Singing Sands beach and the surrounding area. It’s a relatively easy hike that is perfect for families with kids.

Best Things to Do in Souris: Activities and Tours

Apart from the top attractions in Souris, there are many other things to do in the town. Here are some of the best activities and tours that you can enjoy:

  • Deep-Sea Fishing: Souris is known for its deep-sea fishing tours. You can charter a boat and head out into the Gulf of St. Lawrence for a day of fishing and relaxation.
  • Kayaking: If you’re interested in kayaking, Souris has many kayaking tours that allow you to explore the coastline and the nearby islands.
  • Whale Watching: Souris is home to many different whale species, including humpback whales, minke whales, and North Atlantic right whales. You can take a whale watching tour and get up close and personal with these majestic creatures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Souris

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Souris, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Check in to your hotel and explore the town’s cultural attractions, such as the Souris Lighthouse and St. Mary’s Church.
  • Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking one of the town’s many trails, such as the Confederation Trail or the Souris Glen Nature Trail.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as the East Point Lighthouse or the Basin Head Provincial Park.
  • Day 4: Spend the day on one of Souris’ beautiful beaches, such as the Singing Sands beach or the Red Point Provincial Park.
  • Day 5: Go deep-sea fishing or kayaking and enjoy the natural beauty of the Gulf of St. Lawrence.
  • Day 6: Take a whale watching tour and get up close and personal with some of the ocean’s most magnificent creatures.
  • Day 7: Spend the day relaxing and enjoying the town’s local food and drink scene before checking out of your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Souris

Souris boasts an impressive food and drink scene that is sure to delight your taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder: The seafood chowder in Souris is a must-try dish. Made with local seafood, such as lobster, shrimp, and scallops, it’s a delicious and hearty meal.
  • Fresh Lobster: Souris is known for its fresh lobster, and you can’t leave the town without trying it at least once. It’s served in many local restaurants and is a true delicacy.
  • Island Beef: If you’re a meat-lover, you’ll love the Island Beef in Souris. It’s grass-fed and is known for its flavor and tenderness.

Local Beverages

  • Island Beer: Prince Edward Island is known for its craft beer scene, and Souris is no exception. There are many local breweries that offer unique and delicious beers.
  • Blueberry Wine: Souris is located in the heart of blueberry country, so it’s no surprise that the town is known for its blueberry wine. It’s a sweet and delicious drink that is perfect for a warm summer day.
  • Honey Wine: If you’re looking for something a little different, try the honey wine in Souris. It’s made with local honey and is a unique and delicious drink.

Where to Stay in Souris: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Souris offers a range of accommodation options, from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Inn at Bay Fortune: This charming inn is located just outside of Souris and offers beautiful rooms, delicious food, and stunning views of the countryside.
  • The Breakwater Inn: This waterfront inn is located in the heart of Souris and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s top attractions.
  • Souris Beach House: This guesthouse is located just steps from the beach and offers comfortable and affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Souris: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souris is known for its locally made products, including crafts, art, and food. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Blueberry Products: Souris is located in the heart of blueberry country, so it’s no surprise that there are many local products made with blueberries, including jams, jellies, and pies.
  • Pottery: There are many local potters in Souris who create unique and beautiful pottery. It’s a great souvenir to bring home.
  • Honey: Prince Edward Island is known for its delicious honey, and Souris is home to many local beekeepers who sell their honey at the farmers’ market.

Local Culture and Customs of Souris

Souris is known for its friendly and welcoming culture. The town is proud of its heritage and is always happy to share it with visitors. Here are some local customs and traditions:

  • Islanders are known for their hospitality and will often go out of their way to make visitors feel welcome.
  • Souris is an English-speaking town, but many locals also speak French.
  • The summers in Souris are short but sweet, and locals take full advantage of the warm weather by spending time outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Souris

The best time to visit Souris is during the summer months, from June to September. This is when the weather is at its warmest, and many of the top attractions, such as the beaches and outdoor adventures, are open. The fall is also a beautiful time to visit, as the leaves change color and the town becomes quieter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Souris

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Souris:

Do:

  • Do visit the local attractions, such as the Souris Lighthouse and St. Mary’s Church.
  • Do try the local seafood and beer.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor adventures, such as hiking, biking, and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or trails.
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to the locals.
  • Don’t swim in areas that are marked as dangerous or off-limits.

Safety and Health

Souris is a safe and healthy town to visit. Here are some tips to ensure that you have a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in Souris

  • Always follow the rules and regulations of the local attractions, such as the beaches and hiking trails.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid areas that are marked as dangerous or off-limits.
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean and always obey the lifeguard’s instructions.

Health in Souris

  • Make sure to bring any necessary medications with you on your trip.
  • Protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen and a hat.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, especially if you’re spending time outdoors.

Getting to Souris: Transportation Options and Logistics

Souris is located approximately 80 kilometers east of Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island’s capital city. Here are some transportation options to get to Souris:

Airports Near Souris

The closest airport to Souris is the Charlottetown Airport, which is approximately 80 kilometers away. There are several domestic and international flights that connect to the Charlottetown airport.

Bus Stations Near Souris

There are several bus stations near Souris that connect to other parts of Prince Edward Island and Canada. The closest bus station is in Charlottetown.

Other Options

You can also rent a car and drive to Souris, or take a taxi or shuttle from the airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Souris

There are several transportation options to get around Souris, including:

  • Walking: Many of Souris’ top attractions are within walking distance of each other, making it easy to explore the town on foot.
  • Biking: There are many bike rental shops in Souris that allow you to explore the town’s trails and countryside.
  • Driving: If you’re planning to explore nearby areas, renting a car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Souris: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other areas of Prince Edward Island or Atlantic Canada, there are many nearby destinations and excursions that you can enjoy. Here are some suggestions:

  • Charlottetown: Prince Edward Island’s capital city is a must-visit destination and is located just 80 kilometers west of Souris.
  • Nova Scotia: Just a short ferry ride from Prince Edward Island, Nova Scotia offers stunning coastal views, historic towns, and beautiful parks and beaches.
  • New Brunswick: Home to the famous Bay of Fundy, New Brunswick offers a range of outdoor adventures and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Souris is a charming town that is full of surprises. From its natural beauty to its rich history, there is something for everyone in Souris. Whether you’re a foodie, a history buff, or an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll find plenty to keep you entertained. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide helps you plan your trip to Souris and that you make memories that last a lifetime.
