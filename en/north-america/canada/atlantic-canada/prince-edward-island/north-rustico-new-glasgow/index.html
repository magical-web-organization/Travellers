Introduction to North Rustico-New Glasgow

North Rustico-New Glasgow is located in the heart of Prince Edward Island, a scenic province in Atlantic Canada. This charming region boasts breathtaking pastoral landscapes, a vibrant cultural scene, and a wealth of outdoor activities and attractions.

Whether you’re planning a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a solo adventure, North Rustico-New Glasgow has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you an insider’s look into the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs that make this region so unique.

List of Places to Visit in North Rustico-New Glasgow

Here’s a quick rundown of recommended places to visit in North Rustico-New Glasgow:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Anne of Green Gables Museum, which is dedicated to the beloved novel by Lucy Maud Montgomery
  • North Rustico Lighthouse, a picturesque lighthouse that overlooks the Gulf of St. Lawrence

Natural Attractions of North Rustico-New Glasgow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prince Edward Island National Park, which spans over 60 km of beautiful sandy beaches, dunes, salt marshes, and forests
  • New Glasgow Highlands, a scenic hiking trail that runs through rolling hills, meadows, and woodlands

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Rustico-New Glasgow

North Rustico-New Glasgow is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Here are some recommended routes:

  • The Cavendish Beach Multi-Use Trail is a 7.2 km trail that runs from Cavendish Beach to North Rustico, passing through dense forests, scenic meadows, and tranquil wetlands.
  • The New London Trail runs for 11.7 km along the Confederation Trail, offering stunning views of rolling hills, quaint villages, and lush farmland.

Best Things to Do in North Rustico-New Glasgow: Activities and Tours

North Rustico-New Glasgow offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours, from kayaking and whale watching to culinary adventures and cultural festivals. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Kayak Tours, which offer guided tours of the region’s picturesque bays, coves, and estuaries.
  • Whale Watching Tours, which let you watch majestic whales and other marine wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Farm-to-Table Culinary Experiences, where you can sample the region’s delicious local produce and traditional cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of North Rustico-New Glasgow

North Rustico-New Glasgow is renowned for its delicious seafood, fresh produce, and traditional cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: Whether steamed, boiled, or grilled, lobster is a staple of the local cuisine and a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • PEI Potatoes: Prince Edward Island is famous for its sweet, creamy potatoes that are perfect for frying, mashing, or baking.
  • Clam Chowder: This creamy soup is made with fresh clams, potatoes, and cream, and is a warm and delicious comfort food.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Prince Edward Island is home to several artisanal cider breweries, and their crisp, refreshing ciders are perfect for summertime sipping.
  • Craft Beer: The region is also home to several microbreweries that produce unique and flavorful craft beers, including the popular Gahan Brewery.

Where to Stay in North Rustico-New Glasgow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Rustico-New Glasgow offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every taste and budget, from cozy B&Bs to luxurious resorts. Some recommended options include:

  • Dalvay by the Sea, a historic inn located on the shores of Dalvay Lake that was once a summer residence of the Canadian Royal Family.
  • Kindred Spirits Country Inn, a charming B&B that offers cozy rooms and suites, delicious breakfasts, and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Greenwich Moonlight Bay Cottages, a family-friendly resort that features spacious cottages, a pool, a playground, and easy access to the nearby beach and trails.

What to Buy in North Rustico-New Glasgow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Rustico-New Glasgow is home to a variety of artisan shops, boutiques, and markets that sell a wide range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Handcrafted pottery and ceramics: The region is famous for its pottery and ceramics, which are inspired by the natural beauty of the area.
  • Locally-made jams, jellies, and preserves: The area is known for its delicious fruit preserves, made from locally grown berries and fruits.
  • Artisanal chocolates and sweets: From handcrafted chocolates to maple candies, there’s no shortage of sweet treats to indulge in.

Local Culture and Customs of North Rustico-New Glasgow

North Rustico-New Glasgow is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are proud of their community’s rich history and cultural heritage. Here are some notable customs and traditions:

  • Festival culture: There are several festivals and events throughout the year that celebrate the area’s heritage, including the North Rustico Street Festival and the PEI Fall Flavours Culinary Festival.
  • Island hospitality: Islanders are known for their warm and welcoming nature, and visitors can expect to be treated with kindness and respect.
  • Creative arts: The region is home to several artists, musicians, and writers, who draw inspiration from the landscape and culture of the area.

Best Time to Visit North Rustico-New Glasgow

The best time to visit North Rustico-New Glasgow is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches, trails, and festivals are in full swing. However, travelers can also enjoy the region’s picturesque landscapes and cozy atmosphere during the off-season, from late September to May.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Rustico-New Glasgow

North Rustico-New Glasgow is a safe and welcoming destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always important to take common-sense precautions, such as locking your doors and windows at night, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in North Rustico-New Glasgow

Prince Edward Island has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located throughout the province. Travelers are advised to have comprehensive medical insurance before visiting the region, and to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with them.

Getting to North Rustico-New Glasgow: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Rustico-New Glasgow is located on Prince Edward Island, which is accessible by air, car, and ferry. Here are some options:

Airports Near North Rustico-New Glasgow

The nearest airport to North Rustico-New Glasgow is Charlottetown Airport, which is located about 30 km away.

Bus Stations Near North Rustico-New Glasgow

There are several bus stations located throughout Prince Edward Island, including in Charlottetown and Summerside.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can travel to Prince Edward Island by ferry from Nova Scotia, with several ferry routes available throughout the year.

How to Get Around in North Rustico-New Glasgow

The best way to get around North Rustico-New Glasgow is by car, as the region is quite spread out and public transportation can be limited. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Charlottetown, or bring their own vehicle over on the ferry.

Where to Go Next from North Rustico-New Glasgow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the region, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out, including:

  • Charlottetown, the capital city of Prince Edward Island, which offers a vibrant arts and culture scene, historic landmarks, and great dining and shopping.
  • Cape Breton Island, an island destination in Nova Scotia that boasts stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a variety of outdoor adventures and attractions.

Conclusion

North Rustico-New Glasgow is a must-visit destination in Prince Edward Island, offering breathtaking scenery, exciting outdoor adventures, and a rich cultural scene. Start planning your trip today and discover the beauty and charm of this hidden gem in Atlantic Canada.
