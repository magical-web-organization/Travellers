Introduction to Cavendish

Cavendish is a charming town in Prince Edward Island, located in the eastern part of Canada’s Atlantic coast. Known for its lush greenery, spectacular beaches, and vibrant cultural scene, Cavendish is a popular destination for tourists looking to experience the natural beauty of Canada’s maritime provinces. In this article, we will guide you through the must-visit attractions, outdoor adventures, and cultural landmarks of Cavendish, making sure that you have a fun and enriching travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Cavendish

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cavendish boasts a rich history that is closely intertwined with the founding of Canada. The town is home to several museums and historic sites that celebrate this heritage:

  • Green Gables Heritage Place: The famous Green Gables house, located within the Prince Edward Island National Park, is a must-visit for literary buffs and fans of the Anne of Green Gables series.
  • Avonlea Village: This living museum brings to life the setting of Lucy Maud Montgomery’s beloved novel and offers visitors a glimpse into life in the early 19th century.
  • Historic Post Office: The historic post office is a testament to Cavendish’s role in the development of postal services in Canada. The building now serves as an exhibition space for local artists.

Natural Attractions of Cavendish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cavendish’s stunning scenery and diverse natural landscape make it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and biking trails to beachcombing and kayaking, there is no shortage of adventure to be had in Cavendish’s great outdoors:

  • Cavendish Beach: The town’s most famous attraction is Cavendish Beach, with its white sandy shores and crystal-clear waters. Enjoy a picnic, build sandcastles, or take a dip!
  • Prince Edward Island National Park: This park, which encompasses much of the town, offers a variety of outdoor activities to visitors, including hiking trails, birdwatching, camping, and more.
  • Cavendish Boardwalk: Stroll along the boardwalk, take in the sights and sounds of the ocean, and explore the many shops and restaurants that line the boardwalk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cavendish

Cavendish is known for its outstanding hiking and biking trails, with options for all skill levels and interests:

  • Homestead Trail: A leisurely 2.8 km trail that takes you through fields and forests, showcasing the natural beauty of Cavendish.
  • Gulf Shore Way: A challenging 7.8 km trail that runs along the north shore of the park, offering stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding countryside.
  • Robinsons Island Trail: An easy 2.4 km trail that takes you through forested areas and along the beach, providing a unique perspective of the island.

Best Things to Do in Cavendish: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions listed above, there are plenty of other activities and tours to partake in while in Cavendish:

  • Anne of Green Gables Musical: Watch a live performance by the world-renowned Anne of Green Gables Musical, held at the Confederation Centre of the Arts.
  • Deep-Sea Fishing: Charter a boat and explore the waters around Cavendish, catching fish like mackerel and cod in the company of experienced guides.
  • Golfing: Cavendish offers several world-class golf courses that cater to both novice and experienced golfers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cavendish

Day 1: Visit Green Gables Heritage Place and take a stroll through the Haunted Woods Trail.

Day 2: Spend the day at Cavendish Beach and enjoy the various water sports activities available.

Day 3: Explore Avonlea Village and take in the historic sites.

Day 4: Grab a bike and explore the Gulf Shore Way trail.

Day 5: Deep-sea fishing trip or whale watching excursion.

Day 6: Visit the Cavendish Boardwalk and enjoy the shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Cavendish’s many spas or enjoy a round of golf at one of the town’s world-class golf courses.

Food and Drink Scene of Cavendish

Cavendish is home to a variety of restaurants that serve up delicious local cuisine, as well as international dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: A local delicacy that is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • PEI Potato: A unique side dish that showcases the high-quality potatoes that thrive in the island’s soil.
  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty and delicious soup featuring an assortment of fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Million Dollar Cocktail: A fruity and refreshing cocktail made with rum, orange juice, lemon juice, and grenadine.
  • Beach Chair Lager: A light and crisp beer brewed in Prince Edward Island.

Where to Stay in Cavendish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cavendish offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels and resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels:

  • Cavendish Bosom Buddies Motel & Suites: A family-friendly motel that offers cozy rooms and suites, as well as a variety of outdoor activities for guests.
  • The Cavendish Motel: Located in the heart of the town, this motel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the area’s attractions.
  • Green Gables Bungalow Court: A charming guesthouse that offers cute, cottage-style bungalows in the heart of Cavendish.

What to Buy in Cavendish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Anne of Green Gables Memorabilia: From t-shirts and hats to keychains and mugs, there are plenty of souvenirs for fans of the famous redhead.
  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Local artisans often create beautiful jewelry and ornaments made from the colorful sea glass found on the beach.
  • Local Jams and Preserves: Prince Edward Island is famous for its high-quality produce, so be sure to stock up on local jams and preserves to bring home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Cavendish

Cavendish is known for its relaxed and friendly atmosphere, a reflection of the island’s maritime culture. Visitors are welcomed with open arms, and locals are proud to share the town’s history and culture with them. Respect for the environment and the island’s natural resources is also a core value in Cavendish, and visitors are encouraged to do their part in preserving this beautiful town and its surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Cavendish

Peak season in Cavendish is during the summer months, from late June to early September. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s attractions during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cavendish

Do:

  • Take advantage of the great outdoors and enjoy all that Prince Edward Island has to offer.
  • Visit the local museums and historic sites to learn more about the town’s fascinating history.
  • Respect the environment and do your part in keeping Cavendish clean and beautiful.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings or rules, particularly any relating to beach safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cavendish

Cavendish is generally a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take common-sense precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and locking car doors when parked.

Health in Cavendish

Prince Edward Island has a high-quality healthcare system, with several hospitals and medical clinics located throughout the island.

Getting to Cavendish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cavendish

Charlottetown Airport is located approximately 30 minutes from Cavendish and is the easiest and most convenient option for visitors arriving by air. Several car rental agencies are located at the airport, making it easy to get to Cavendish by car.

Bus Stations Near Cavendish

There are no bus stations in Cavendish, but visitors can take a bus to Charlottetown and then rent a car or take a taxi to the town.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get to Cavendish, as it allows visitors to explore the island at their own pace. Ferries from Nova Scotia also offer another option for those who prefer to travel by boat.

How to Get Around in Cavendish

Visitors can get around Cavendish easily by car or bike. Rental cars are available at Charlottetown Airport, and several bike rental shops can be found throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Cavendish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Prince Edward Island offers a multitude of attractions and destinations that are well worth exploring. A few popular options include:

  • Charlottetown: The capital city of Prince Edward Island is a charming and historic town with many cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters.
  • North Rustico: A picturesque fishing village located just 15 minutes from Cavendish, North Rustico is famous for its fresh seafood and unique coastal scenery.
  • Victoria-by-the-Sea: A quaint and charming village located on the southern shore of Prince Edward Island, Victoria-by-the-Sea boasts scenic beauty, excellent restaurants, and vibrant cultural activities.

Conclusion

Cavendish is a charming and welcoming town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. Whether you are visiting for a weekend or an extended stay, we hope that this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your perfect trip to this beautiful corner of Canada!
