Introduction to Montague

Nestled in the southern part of Prince Edward Island, Montague is a quaint town that boasts of scenic natural beauty, rich history, and a unique local culture. With its charming streets, historic sites, and welcoming locals, Montague presents a perfect escape for travelers looking for an intimate experience. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a peaceful retreat, Montague has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Montague

Montague has a wide range of places to visit that offer a rich and varied experience for travelers. Some of the must-visit places include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Montague is steeped in history and preserves its rich heritage through the numerous museums and historical sites that it has. A visit to the following places will provide you with an insight into the town’s culture and history:

  • Montague PEI Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Montague and its surrounding areas. It has a range of exhibits, artifacts, and historical records that showcase the town’s rich past.

  • Orwell Corner Historic Village: Located a few miles away from Montague, this historic village is a living museum that presents visitors with a slice of life from the 19th century. It has an old schoolhouse, a church, and a blacksmith shop, among other things.

Natural Attractions of Montague: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montague is surrounded by natural beauty that provides ample opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic drives. Here are some of the natural attractions that you should visit in Montague:

  • Panmure Island Provincial Park: This park is a popular destination for beachgoers, offering beautiful sandy beaches, picnic areas, and camping facilities.

  • Montague Harbour: The harbor offers stunning views of the town and is an excellent place for a stroll or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montague

Montague has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer beautiful views of the town and its surrounding areas. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The Confederation Trail: This trail is a popular route for both hikers and bikers. It is a 470km trail that runs through the province, with a section that passes through Montague. The trail offers beautiful scenery and is suitable for all fitness levels.

  • Panmure Island Walking Trail: This trail leads through a woodland area and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Montague: Activities and Tours

Montague offers an array of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Some of the best things to do in the town include:

  • Kayaking in the Montague River: This is a popular activity that gives you the opportunity to explore the river and its surroundings.

  • Golf at Brudenell River Golf Course: This golf course is a championship course and is considered one of the best in the country.

Week-Long Itinerary for Montague

A week in Montague can give you enough time to explore the town and its surroundings fully. Here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Montague PEI Museum, followed by a stroll along the Montague River and a visit to the nearby Brudenell River Golf Course.
  • Day 2: Spend the day at Panmure Island Provincial Park, and enjoy the beach and the hiking trails.
  • Day 3: Take a tour of the Orwell Corner Historic Village.
  • Day 4: Enjoy a kayaking tour of the Montague River.
  • Day 5: Spend the day exploring the town and its shops, cafes, and restaurants.
  • Day 6: Take a scenic drive along the coastal areas of Montague.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at Montague Harbour.

Food and Drink Scene of Montague

Montague has a vibrant food and drink scene that caters to all palates. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: Montague is known for its fresh seafood, and the lobster roll is a must-try dish. It is a traditional sandwich that features fresh lobster meat and is usually served with fries.

  • PEI Potato Pie: This dish features locally grown potatoes and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • PEI Brewing Company: This brewery is located in Charlottetown, a short drive from Montague, and produces some of the most exceptional craft beers on the island.

  • Rossignol Estate Winery: This winery is located near Murray Harbour, a short drive from Montague, and offers tastings of its award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Montague: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montague has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Montague:

  • The Inn at Spry Point: This inn is located on a secluded peninsula overlooking the sea and offers a luxurious and peaceful stay.

  • The Montague Rose B&B: This cozy B&B is located in the heart of Montague and offers comfortable rooms and warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Montague: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montague offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Montague:

  • Pottery and Handicrafts: Montague has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and there are several studios and workshops that offer pottery and handmade crafts.

  • PEI Preserves: These preserves are made with locally grown fruits and make excellent gifts or souvenirs to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Montague

Montague has a unique local culture that is deeply rooted in its history and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming and take pride in their town’s heritage and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Montague

The best time to visit Montague is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm, and the town is bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montague

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Explore the town and its beautiful natural surroundings.

  • Try the local seafood.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural areas.

  • Disturb the wildlife or harm the environment.

  • Ignore the local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montague

Montague is a safe town, but it is essential to take necessary precautions to stay safe. Be aware of your surroundings, lock your doors and windows, and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Montague

Montague has a hospital and several clinics, so medical care is readily available. It is advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Montague: Transportation Options and Logistics

Montague can be reached by air, road, or sea. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Montague

The Charlottetown airport is the closest airport, located about 30 minutes’ drive from Montague.

Bus Stations Near Montague

Bus services from Charlottetown and other parts of Prince Edward Island are available to Montague

Other Options

Ferry services are also available from Nova Scotia and New Brunswick.

How to Get Around in Montague

Montague is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Car rentals are also available if you prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Montague: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montague is located in a beautiful part of Prince Edward Island, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore.

  • Charlottetown: The island’s capital, located about 30 minutes away from Montague, is a charming town with lots of history and cultural attractions.

  • Green Gables: Located about an hour’s drive from Montague, this is the famous site that inspired the Anne of Green Gables series of novels.

Conclusion

Montague is a hidden gem among the many destinations in Prince Edward Island, Canada. With its beautiful landscapes, cultural attractions, and rich history, it is a perfect destination for travelers looking for an intimate and authentic experience.
