Introduction to Borden-Carleton

Borden-Carleton is a charming village located on the southern shore of Prince Edward Island, Canada’s smallest province. The town is known for its iconic red soil and stunning views of the Northumberland Strait. Borden-Carleton is also the site of the Confederation Bridge, which connects Prince Edward Island to the mainland.

List of Places to Visit in Borden-Carleton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Borden-Carleton Railway Station: Built in 1905, this historic train station now serves as a museum, featuring exhibits on the history of the railway on the island.

  • Gateway Village: This complex features a variety of local shops, restaurants, and attractions, offering a glimpse into the island’s cultural and historical heritage.

  • Victoria Playhouse: This charming theater showcases local productions and live music performances.

Natural Attractions of Borden-Carleton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cedar Dunes Provincial Park: Explore the beautiful sand dunes, stroll along the beach, or enjoy a picnic in the park.

  • Confederation Bridge: Take a drive or walk across this impressive engineering feat and enjoy breathtaking views of the sea.

  • Northumberland Strait: Go swimming, kayaking, or fishing in the beautiful waters of the strait.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borden-Carleton

  • Confederation Trail: This 449 km trail stretches across Prince Edward Island, and offers a scenic route for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling.

Best Things to Do in Borden-Carleton: Activities and Tours

  • Deep Sea Fishing Tours: Book a tour and reel in some fresh seafood while enjoying stunning views of the Northumberland Strait.

  • Island Helicopter Tours: Take to the skies and see the island from a different perspective on a helicopter tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Borden-Carleton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: Prince Edward Island is known for its delicious lobster, so be sure to try it while you’re in town.

  • PEI Potato Fries: Made from the island’s famous potatoes, these fries are a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • PEI Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers.

  • Rossignol Winery: Visit this winery for a taste of their unique fruit wines.

Where to Stay in Borden-Carleton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Carleton Motel & Coffee Shop: This cozy motel offers comfortable rooms and a lovely on-site coffee shop.

  • Johnson Shore Inn: This waterfront inn offers beautiful views of the Northumberland Strait and a delicious breakfast in the morning.

What to Buy in Borden-Carleton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Island-made Crafts: Gateway Village offers a range of locally-made crafts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Borden-Carleton

Prince Edward Island has a rich cultural heritage, and its residents are known for their hospitality and friendly nature. Islanders are proud of their history, and many events and festivals throughout the year celebrate the island’s culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Borden-Carleton

The best time to visit Borden-Carleton is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the town is bustling with tourists. However, be aware that peak season also means higher prices and bigger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Borden-Carleton

Do:

  • Do try the seafood, it’s delicious!

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in natural areas.

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities, such as sunscreen and bug spray.

Safety and Health

Safety in Borden-Carleton

Borden-Carleton is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, as with any travel destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in Borden-Carleton

Health services are readily available in Borden-Carleton, and the town has a medical clinic and pharmacy.

Getting to Borden-Carleton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Borden-Carleton

  • Charlottetown Airport: Located approximately 45 minutes from Borden-Carleton, Charlottetown Airport offers daily flights from major cities in Canada and the United States.

Bus Stations Near Borden-Carleton

  • Maritime Bus: This bus company offers service to several cities on Prince Edward Island, including Borden-Carleton.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option for visitors to the island, and several major rental companies have locations in Charlottetown.

How to Get Around in Borden-Carleton

The town is small and easily walkable, but if you want to explore further afield, renting a bike or car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Borden-Carleton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Charlottetown: The capital city of Prince Edward Island, Charlottetown is known for its charming streets, historic buildings, and world-class cuisine.

  • Cavendish: Home to the famous Green Gables House and beautiful beaches, Cavendish is a must-visit destination on Prince Edward Island.

Conclusion

Borden-Carleton is a picturesque and peaceful town, offering visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty and warm hospitality of Prince Edward Island. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or just relaxing by the sea, Borden-Carleton has something for everyone.
