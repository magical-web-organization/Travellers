Introduction to Ramea

Ramea is a small island town located in Southern Newfoundland, in Newfoundland and Labrador, in Atlantic Canada, in Canada, in North America. Despite its small size, Ramea is a popular tourist destination due to its stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and unique cultural heritage. The town is home to fewer than 500 people, giving it a quaint charm that is hard to resist. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking to relax in a beautiful setting, Ramea has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ramea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ramea Heritage Centre: This museum showcases the town’s rich cultural heritage, including exhibits on fishing, boat-building, and local history.
  • St. Boniface Anglican Church: Built in the late 1800s, this historic church has stunning stained-glass windows and a quaint cemetery.
  • Ramea Lighthouse: This picturesque lighthouse has been guiding ships to Ramea’s shores for more than a century.

Natural Attractions of Ramea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grand Bay West Beach: This beautiful sandy beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and building sandcastles.
  • Ramea Ecological Reserve: This reserve is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, including seabirds, moose, and rare orchids.
  • Ramea Islands: Take a boat tour to explore the stunning natural beauty of the Ramea Islands, where you’ll see rugged coastlines, hidden coves, and abundant marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramea

  • Ramea Puffin Trail: This easy, 5km trail takes you along the rugged coastline past nesting puffins.
  • Ramea Lighthouse Trail: This moderate, 8km trail takes you to the top of Ramea’s historic lighthouse, with stunning views of the surrounding islands.
  • Ramea Lookout Trail: This challenging, 14km trail takes you to the top of a scenic lookout, with panoramic views of the entire region.

Best Things to Do in Ramea: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Paddle your way around the Ramea Islands to see stunning scenery and abundant marine life.
  • Fishing: Go fishing with a local guide to catch cod, mackerel, and other local fish.
  • Boat Tours: Take a guided boat tour to explore the rugged coastline and beautiful islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramea

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Ramea is known for its fresh seafood, and the local fish and chips is a must-try dish.
  • Toutons: These traditional Newfoundland doughnuts are made with dough fried in pork fat, and are typically served with molasses.
  • Jiggs Dinner: This hearty boiled dinner is made with salt beef, vegetables, and other local ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Iceberg Beer: This locally brewed beer is made with water from icebergs, giving it a unique flavor.
  • Screech: This Newfoundland rum is famous for its strong, distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Ramea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ramea Cozy Suites: These modern, comfortable suites are located in the heart of town, with easy access to all of Ramea’s attractions.
  • Ramea Island Inn: This charming inn is located on a quiet street, with beautiful views of the ocean and surrounding hills.
  • Ramea B&B: This cozy bed and breakfast is located just a short walk from the town’s main attractions, with comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Ramea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ramea Crafts: These handmade crafts include pottery, textiles, and other unique items made by local artisans.
  • Newfoundland Screech: Bring home a bottle of this famous Newfoundland rum as a souvenir.
  • Local Jams and Jellies: Ramea is known for its delicious homemade jams and jellies, made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramea

Ramea is a town with a rich cultural heritage, focused on fishing and boat-building traditions. The people of Ramea are friendly and welcoming, and proud of their unique culture and traditions. Some customs to be aware of include:

  • Friendliness: The people of Ramea are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and visitors can expect to be greeted warmly.
  • Fishing Traditions: Fishing is an important part of Ramea’s economy and culture, and visitors may be able to see local fishermen at work.
  • Community Spirit: Ramea is a close-knit community, where residents look out for each other and value their shared traditions and culture.

Best Time to Visit Ramea

The best time to visit Ramea is during the summer months, between June and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the days are long. This is also the best time to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramea

Do:

  • Respect Local Traditions: Ramea is a town with a rich cultural heritage, so be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Take Advantage of Outdoor Activities: Ramea is known for its outdoor adventures, so make sure to take advantage of hiking, kayaking, and other activities.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Ramea is known for its fresh seafood and unique dishes, so be sure to try some of the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Ramea is a pristine natural environment, so be sure to dispose of your garbage properly and respect the environment.
  • Ignore Safety Guidelines: Always follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Disrespect Local Residents: Ramea is a small town with a close-knit community, so be respectful of local residents and their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramea

Ramea is a safe town with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should always take basic safety precautions, such as locking their doors and windows at night, and being aware of their surroundings when walking alone.

Health in Ramea

There is a small medical clinic in Ramea, but visitors should bring their own medications and medical supplies, as the town may not have specific items available. It’s also a good idea to bring insect repellent, especially during the summer months when mosquitoes can be a nuisance.

Getting to Ramea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ramea

The closest airports to Ramea are St. John’s International Airport and Stephenville International Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Ramea.

Bus Stations Near Ramea

There are several bus stations near Ramea, including the Port aux Basques bus station and the Stephenville bus station. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Ramea.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry to Ramea from either Burgeo or Francois, both located on the west coast of Newfoundland.

How to Get Around in Ramea

Ramea is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside and natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Ramea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Burgeo: This nearby town is known for its beautiful coastal scenery and outdoor adventures, including kayaking and hiking.
  • Gros Morne National Park: This stunning national park is home to some of the most beautiful scenery in Canada, including fjords, mountains, and forests.
  • St. John’s: Newfoundland’s lively capital city has a rich history and a vibrant cultural scene, with plenty of museums, galleries, and restaurants to explore.

Conclusion

Ramea is a small town with a big heart, offering visitors the chance to experience Newfoundland’s stunning natural beauty, unique culture, and friendly hospitality. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or just some peaceful relaxation, Ramea is the perfect destination for your next trip.
