Francois, Newfoundland and Labrador Travel Guide

Located in Southern Newfoundland, Francois is a picturesque fishing village that offers a wide range of outdoor adventures, scenic vistas, and cultural attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the must-visit places, things to do, and local customs of Francois, Newfoundland and Labrador.

Introduction to Francois

Francois is a small town located in the remote wilderness of Southern Newfoundland, in Atlantic Canada. The town has a population of just over 100 people, making it the perfect destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Francois is known for its rugged coastline, beautiful hiking trails, and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Francois

When visiting Francois, there are several places that you simply cannot miss. Here are the top cultural and natural attractions that you need to put on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Francois Historical Society Museum showcases the history and culture of the town and its people. You can learn about the fishing industry, the impact of World War II, and the town’s unique way of life.
  • The Gun Hill Trail showcases a series of historic gun emplacements that were used during WWII. This trail is perfect for history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Francois: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Francois Lake Trail is a scenic hiking trail that takes you through beautiful forested areas, across rivers and streams, and offers stunning views of both Francois Lake and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • The Francois Glacier is a must-visit attraction for outdoor enthusiasts. This beautiful glacier offers a great opportunity for adventure and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lighthouse Point Park provides a great view of the ocean, with picnic tables and barbeque facilities where visitors can enjoy a bite to eat and relax.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Francois

Francois has excellent hiking and biking routes that take you through scenic landscapes and beautiful trails. The trails are well marked, making it easy for visitors to explore on foot or on two wheels.

  • The Francois Lake Trail is a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding wilderness. The trail is approximately 5 km (3.1 miles) long and takes about an hour to complete.
  • The Gun Hill Trail is another popular hiking trail that takes visitors through historic WWII gun emplacements. The trail is approximately 2.5 km (1.6 miles) long and takes about an hour to complete.

Best Things to Do in Francois: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing tours: Francois is well known for its excellent fishing opportunities, with a range of fish species like Atlantic salmon, sea trout, and more.
  • Kayaking tours: The sea kayaking tour is a great outdoor activity in Francois with spectacular views of the ocean & marine wildlife.
  • Whale watching tours: Several tour operators in Francois offer whale watching tours where you can catch a glimpse of humpback whales, dolphins, and seals in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Francois

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Francois, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and orientation.

Day 2: Francois Lake Trail hike and visit to the Francois Glacier.

Day 3: Explore the historic attractions of Francois, including the Francois Historical Society Museum and Gun Hill Trail.

Day 4: Day trip to the scenic Francois Lighthouse Park

Day 5: Fishing tour and visit to the local fish market.

Day 6: Sea Kayaking Tour

Day 7: Departure and travel to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Francois

The food scene in Francois is heavily influenced by the local fishing industry, meaning that seafood is the star on most menus. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages in Francois:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cod tongues, a local specialty that is often served fried
  • Fish chowder, a hearty soup that is made with fresh seafood
  • Fish and chips, a staple of the local food scene

Local Beverages

  • The Screech-In Ceremony, a traditional Newfoundland welcome drink
  • Quidi Vidi Beer, a locally brewed craft beer that is served in most bars.

Where to Stay in Francois: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Francois has a few hotels, hostels, guesthouses, and bed and breakfast options, providing different types of accommodation for different budgets and preferences. Here are a few popular options:

  • Francois Narrows Outpost Lodge is an excellent option for visitors who want to explore the great outdoors, with stunning views and outdoor amenities.
  • Francois Fishing Lodge is a comfortable lodging option situated on the shores of Francois Lake.
  • Mistaken Point B&B is a cozy guesthouse that offers comfortable and affordable lodging options for visitors.

What to Buy in Francois: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local handicrafts such as knitted hats, mitts, and scarves are excellent souvenirs to bring back from Francois.
  • Dried fish and seafood products are also great gifts to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Francois

Francois has a unique and vibrant local culture and customs. Some of the customs include traditional fishing methods that have been passed down from generation to generation, and residents speaking a unique dialect of English called Newfoundland English.

Best Time to Visit Francois

The best time to visit Francois is during the summer months, from June to August, as the weather is warm and pleasant, and most of the outdoor attractions and activities are open during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Francois

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be prepared for outdoor adventures and bring the appropriate equipment and clothing.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Ignore local wildlife and marine life regulations.
  • Bring pets into local restaurants and attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Francois

Francois is generally a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the potential dangers associated with outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Make sure to follow all safety instructions and be prepared for emergencies.

Health in Francois

Medical facilities in Francois are limited, and visitors should be aware of any pre-existing medical conditions before traveling. It is recommended that visitors have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Francois: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Francois

The nearest airport is St. John’s International Airport, located about 360 km (224 miles) away from Francois. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, shuttle bus, or rent a car to get to Francois.

Bus Stations Near Francois

There are no bus stations in Francois, but visitors can take a bus from St. John’s to Burgeo, which is the nearest town. From Burgeo, visitors can take a ferry or private boat to Francois.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to drive to Francois from St. John’s. The drive takes about 6 hours and is a beautiful scenic route that passes through several towns and attractions.

How to Get Around in Francois

The best way to get around Francois is on foot or by bicycle. Most of the attractions and activities are located within walking distance of each other, and the town is small enough to easily navigate.

Where to Go Next from Francois: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Burgeo is a nearby town that offers a range of outdoor activities and attractions.
  • The Gros Morne National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site located a few hours’ drive from Francois.

Conclusion

Francois, Newfoundland and Labrador, is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to enjoy the great outdoors, scenic vistas, and unique local culture. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip to this beautiful destination.
