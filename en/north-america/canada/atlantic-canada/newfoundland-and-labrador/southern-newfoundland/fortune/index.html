Introduction to Fortune

Located in the southern tip of Newfoundland, Fortune is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse into the maritime culture of Atlantic Canada. Known for its picturesque setting and rich history, Fortune is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Fortune.

List of Places to Visit in Fortune

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fortune has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s history. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Fortune include:

  • Fortune Head Interpretation Centre - This center provides visitors with insight into the Fortune Head Ecological Reserve, an area of natural beauty where one can witness the unique geological and topographical features of Fortune Head.

  • Grand Bank Regional Theatre - This is a newly renovated theatre that was originally an old church. The theatre has played host to several musical and theatrical performances, making it a must-visit spot for any art lover visiting Fortune.

  • Museum of the Bank of Nova Scotia - This museum offers visitors a chance to view original artifacts and documents related to the banking industry in Newfoundland.

  • The Point House Museum - A beautifully restored 1800s sea captain’s home which provides a fantastic peek into the past of Fortune.

Natural Attractions of Fortune: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fortune is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, which makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the natural attractions that you should not miss when in Fortune:

  • Fortune Head Ecological Reserve - This reserve occupies a large area of shoreline and offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscapes. It is a nature lover’s paradise, with hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities.

  • Mineral Spring Park - This park is known for its mineral-rich water that once had a reputation for its medicinal properties. The park is perfect for picnicking, hiking, and relaxation.

  • Cape St. Mary’s Ecological Reserve - This ecological reserve is located on the coast of the Avalon Peninsula, just a short drive from Fortune. Visitors can see a plethora of seabirds such as gannets and murres.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fortune

Fortune is home to scenic hiking and biking trails that provide visitors with a chance to experience the town’s beauty and tranquility up close. Here are some of the popular hiking/biking routes in Fortune:

  • Fortune Head Trail - This short loop trail is an easy hike that offers stunning views of Fortune Bay, its rugged coastline and the adjoining windswept barrens.

  • Butterpot Provincial Park - This park is known for its network of hiking trails that are suitable for beginners to experienced hikers. It is also an ideal destination for bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Fortune: Activities and Tours

Fortune offers visitors an array of activities and tours that cater to different preferences and interests. Here are some of the best things to do when in Fortune:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing - Fortune offers several opportunities for kayaking or canoeing in its scenic bay where you can glide past breathtaking scenery and explore hidden coves and inlets.

  • Fishing Trips - Fortune Bay is a famous fishing destination that is renowned for its variety of fish species. Visitors can take a fishing trip to experience the thriving fishing industry of Fortune.

  • Scenic Tours - Visitors can take a scenic tour around Fortune to see its picturesque landscapes, colorful houses and to learn about its economic and cultural history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fortune

Here’s a suggested itinerary for visitors who are planning to spend a week in Fortune:

Monday: Visit the Fortune Head Interpretation Centre and take the Fortune Head Trail.

Tuesday: Visit the Point House Museum and Grand Bank Regional Theatre.

Wednesday: Take a scenic tour around Fortune and its surrounding areas.

Thursday: Take a day trip to Cape St. Mary’s Ecological Reserve.

Friday: Do some kayaking or canoeing in Fortune Bay.

Saturday: Visit the Mineral Spring Park and take a leisurely walk around.

Sunday: Take a day trip to the Burin Peninsula and explore some fishing villages.

Food and Drink Scene of Fortune

Must-Try Dishes

Fortune is known for its seafood dishes, and visitors should not miss the chance to try the following:

  • Fish and Chips - A classic dish made with fresh codfish and crispy batter.

  • Battered Clams - Another seafood dish that is popular in Fortune.

Local Beverages

  • Screech Rum - A Newfoundland rum that is made from molasses, alcohol distilled from sugar beet and imported Jamaican rum. It is often seen as the “Official” rum of the province.

  • Iceberg Beer - A beer that is brewed with water from the icebergs that float by the Newfoundland coast.

Where to Stay in Fortune: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fortune has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Fortune:

  • Fortune Harbourview Bed and Breakfast - Located in the heart of Fortune, this bed and breakfast offers stunning views of Fortune Bay.

  • The Fortune Hotel - This hotel provides visitors with comfy rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Fortune: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fortune has many locally made products that visitors can purchase as a souvenir. Here are some of the things you should buy in Fortune:

  • Traditional Newfoundland Crafts - Fortune is known for its locally made handicrafts such as knit ware, woven baskets, and pottery, amongst others.

  • Local Seafood- Fortune is known for its thriving seafood industry, so buying some fresh seafood is an excellent idea.

Local Culture and Customs of Fortune

Fortune has a unique culture and customs that visitors should respect. Here are some things you should know:

  • Respect for the Local Language - Many people in Fortune speak Newfoundland English, a distinct dialect that has been influenced by Irish, Scottish and English language.

  • Friendliness of the Locals - Newfoundlander’s are often considered to be some of the friendliest people in Canada.

Best Time to Visit Fortune

The best time to visit Fortune is during the summer, from June to August, when the days are longer, and the weather is warmer. However, visitors who enjoy winter activities such as snowmobiling or ice fishing can visit Fortune during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fortune

Do:

  • Do Explore Fortune’s Natural Beauty - Fortune is known for its natural beauty, and visitors should take the time to explore its scenic landscapes.

  • Do Respect the Local Culture - Visitors should respect the local customs and be friendly to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Litter - Visitors should respect the environment and ensure that they dispose of their trash properly.

  • Don’t Disturb Wildlife - Fortune has a variety of wildlife species, and visitors should avoid disturbing their habitat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fortune

Fortune is a safe destination for visitors, and they should take the usual precautions when travelling, such as locking their doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Fortune

Fortune has a hospital and health clinic that can provide medical care to visitors if needed.

Getting to Fortune: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fortune can be reached by road, air or sea. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Fortune

The closest airport to Fortune is in St. John’s, which is approximately a 2-3 hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Fortune

Bus services are available from St. John’s to nearby Burin peninsula.

Other Options

Visitors can take a ferry from Nova Scotia to Port aux Basques, which is a three-hour drive from Fortune.

How to Get Around in Fortune

Getting around Fortune is easy and visitors can walk, bike, or drive through the town. Car rental or taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Fortune: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Fortune. Some of them include:

  • Burin Peninsula - A neighboring region that offers visitors a chance to explore more of Newfoundland’s coastal beauty and fishing villages.

  • St. John’s - The capital of Newfoundland and Labrador offers visitors a chance to explore its rich history and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Fortune is a beautiful destination that has something for everyone. The town’s natural beauty, rich culture, and exciting activities make it an ideal place for visitors to explore. With this travel guide, visitors can experience the very best of Fortune, Newfoundland and Labrador.
