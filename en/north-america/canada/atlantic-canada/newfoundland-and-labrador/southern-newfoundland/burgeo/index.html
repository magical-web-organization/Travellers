Introduction to Burgeo

Nestled in Southern Newfoundland, Burgeo is a charming fishing town that boasts stunning natural scenery, a rich history, and warm hospitality. This hidden gem is the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Newfoundland experience. In this guide, we’ll show you the top things to do in Burgeo, how to get there, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Burgeo

When it comes to attractions, Burgeo has something for everyone. Here are the must-visit places in Burgeo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Burgeo Heritage House Museum: This museum features exhibits on the town’s history, from its early fishing industry to its role in World War II.
  • Burgeo Marine Interpretation Centre: Learn about the local marine life and the Burgeo fishing industry.
  • St. John the Evangelist Anglican Church: This picturesque church dates back to the 1800s and is a beautiful example of Gothic Revival architecture.

Natural Attractions of Burgeo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandbanks Provincial Park: This stunning park has a sand beach that stretches for miles.
  • Mealy Mountains National Park Reserve: A vast wilderness area that is great for hiking, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Wapmagoostui-Kuujjuarapik Ice Cap Expedition: This tour takes you on a multi-day trek across the ice cap.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burgeo

Burgeo is a hiker’s paradise, with many trails to choose from. Here are the top hiking and biking routes:

  • Ramea-Burgeo Ferry Trail: This 15 km trail is perfect for biking or walking and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Wreckhouse Trail: This shorter trail is only 3 km long but features spectacular scenery and unique geological formations.
  • Barachois Pond Provincial Park Trail: This moderate trail is 6.5 km long and leads through a beautiful forest to a scenic pond.

Best Things to Do in Burgeo: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Burgeo has plenty of activities to offer. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Whale Watching Tour: Take a boat ride and spot humpback whales, killer whales, and dolphins.
  • Sea Kayaking Tour: Paddle it out on the ocean and explore the stunning coastline.
  • Fishing Trip: Go on a fishing trip and catch some fresh seafood to cook for dinner.
  • Beach Day Trip: Spend a day at Sandbanks Provincial Park and soak up the sun on the sandy beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burgeo

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Burgeo, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Burgeo Heritage House Museum and walk around town
Day 2 Go on a whale watching tour
Day 3 Hike the Ramea-Burgeo Ferry Trail
Day 4 Relax on the beach at Sandbanks Provincial Park
Day 5 Visit the Burgeo Marine Interpretation Centre and St. John the Evangelist Anglican Church
Day 6 Take a sea kayaking tour
Day 7 Go on a fishing trip and cook your catch for dinner

Food and Drink Scene of Burgeo

When it comes to food and drink, Burgeo has a unique cuisine that reflects the town’s fishing culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A classic Newfoundland dish made with fresh cod and locally grown potatoes.
  • Lobster Rolls: A delicious sandwich made with fresh lobster meat and mayonnaise.
  • Jiggs Dinner: A hearty meal made with boiled salt beef, cabbage, potatoes, and carrots.

Local Beverages

  • Newfoundland Screech: A rum-based drink that is often served during traditional Newfoundland parties.
  • Moosehead Beer: A popular beer brand that is brewed in Newfoundland and known for its smooth taste.

Where to Stay in Burgeo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Burgeo has a variety of options to suit any budget. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Captain’s Inn: A cozy and comfortable bed and breakfast that offers stunning views of the ocean.
  • Harbour Lights Inn: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Burgeo that features unique decor and modern amenities.
  • Burgeo Haven Bed and Breakfast: A charming and welcoming guesthouse with comfortable rooms and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Burgeo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products, Burgeo has plenty of options. Here are some ideas:

  • Hooked Rugs: Handmade rugs that are created using traditional Newfoundland techniques.
  • Antler Carvings: Intricately carved antlers that are made by local artisans.
  • Fisherman’s Sweaters: Thick wool sweaters that are perfect for keeping warm during Newfoundland’s chilly winters.

Local Culture and Customs of Burgeo

Newfoundlanders are known for their warm hospitality and laid-back lifestyle. When visiting Burgeo, it’s important to be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Here are a few cultural tips:

  • Chat with the Locals: Newfoundlanders love to chat and get to know visitors, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.
  • Take Part in Kitchen Parties: Kitchen parties are a staple in Newfoundland culture, and often involve music, food, and drinks.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Newfoundland cuisine is unique and delicious, so make sure to try some of the local dishes and beverages.

Best Time to Visit Burgeo

The best time to visit Burgeo is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burgeo

When visiting Burgeo, there are a few things to keep in mind. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Pack Warm Clothes: Newfoundland weather can be unpredictable, so it’s always a good idea to pack warm clothes and rain gear.
  • Be Prepared for Black Flies: Black flies can be a nuisance during the summer months, so make sure to bring bug spray.
  • Respect the Wildlife: Newfoundland is home to many wild animals, so make sure to observe them from a safe distance.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep Burgeo beautiful by disposing of your trash properly.
  • Trespass on Private Property: Respect the privacy of local residents by staying on public land.
  • Overfish the Waters: Leave the fish for the locals and respect the fishing quotas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burgeo

Burgeo is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to take basic precautions, such as locking your doors and not leaving valuables in your car.

Health in Burgeo

Burgeo has a medical clinic that is open during the week and a hospital in nearby Stephenville. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Burgeo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burgeo

The closest airport to Burgeo is Stephenville International Airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive away. Alternatively, visitors can fly into St. John’s International Airport and take a scenic six-hour drive to Burgeo.

Bus Stations Near Burgeo

There are no bus stations in Burgeo, but visitors can take a bus to nearby Port aux Basques and then rent a car or take a taxi to Burgeo.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Burgeo, as it offers the flexibility to explore the surrounding area. Rental cars are available at the airports and in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Burgeo

The best way to explore Burgeo is on foot, as it’s a small town with many attractions within walking distance. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Burgeo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Newfoundland adventure, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Gros Morne National Park: This stunning park features fjords, mountains, and hiking trails.
  • Corner Brook: A charming city located on the west coast of Newfoundland that offers shopping, dining, and outdoor activities.
  • Bay of Islands: A picturesque coastal region that is great for fishing, hiking, and kayaking.

Conclusion

Burgeo is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. With its breathtaking scenery, rich history, and warm hospitality, it’s the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Newfoundland experience. We hope this guide has inspired you to visit Burgeo and explore all that it has to offer.
