Introduction to St. Lawrence

Nestled in the southern region of Newfoundland, St. Lawrence is a hidden gem that promises to surprise and delight travelers. This small town, lively with friendly locals and fascinating history, is a perfect destination for those seeking a serene and laid-back atmosphere to enjoy their holidays.

List of Places to Visit in St. Lawrence

Before you pack your bags and head to St. Lawrence, there are a few places you should definitely add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cobblers Island Trail
  • St. Lawrence Miner’s Memorial Museum
  • Little Salmonier Nature Park
  • Grandy’s Point Heritage Square

Natural Attractions of St. Lawrence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Beach Fossil Site
  • Fortune Head Ecological Reserve
  • Point D’Argent Beach
  • Samson’s Island Walking Trail

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Lawrence

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will fall in love with St. Lawrence thanks to the breathtaking landscapes dotted with beautiful trails perfect for both adventurous and laid-back pursuits.

  • Berkeley Forest Trail
  • Lawrence Pond Trail
  • St. Lawrence Bay Trail
  • Blue Crest Trail

Best Things to Do in St. Lawrence: Activities and Tours

From hikes and bike rides to exciting tours, St. Lawrence offers a range of activities that will keep you amazed throughout your stay.

  • Visit the St. Lawrence Miner’s Memorial Museum
  • Enjoy the Cobblers Island Trail
  • Go on a kayak adventure to Samson’s Island
  • Experience historical fishing at the Codroy Valley

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Lawrence

Day 1: Visit the St. Lawrence Miner’s Memorial Museum and Grandy’s Point Heritage Square

Day 2: Hike the Berkeley Forest Trail and explore Little Salmonier Nature Park

Day 3: Take a kayak tour of Samson’s Island Walk and enjoy a seafood lunch at the Blue Water Inn

Day 4: Visit the Point D’Argent Beach and Fortune Head Ecological Reserve

Day 5: Explore the historical fishing Codroy Valley and enjoy a delicious dinner at Julie’s Fusion Kitchen

Day 6: Enjoy the Blue Beach Fossil Site and Lawrence Pond Trail

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day on the Cobblers Island Trail and visit the Blue Crest Trail for a sunset view

Where to Stay in St. Lawrence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in St. Lawrence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re a souvenir enthusiast or someone who loves taking home unique local products, St. Lawrence will impress you with its range of interesting and authentic finds, such as:

  • Hand-knitted woolen socks
  • Locally produced honey, jams, and jellies
  • Salt fish and other seafood delicacies

Local Culture and Customs of St. Lawrence

The locals of St. Lawrence are proud of their rich history and heritage, and visitors can experience it through various traditions, such as mummering and storytelling sessions.

Best Time to Visit St. Lawrence

The best time to visit St. Lawrence is from May to September with moderate temperatures and clear skies to enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Lawrence

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dispose of garbage properly
  • Carry bug spray and sunscreen

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats
  • Litter in public places
  • Indulge in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Lawrence

St. Lawrence is a safe destination, but visitors should always take general precautions and stay aware of their surroundings to avoid any mishap. It’s wise to carry a map, keep your belongings safe, and only carry essentials when exploring hiking trails or backwoods.

Health in St. Lawrence

The region is equipped with basic medical facilities, and visitors should carry their prescribed medication for existing health conditions.

How to Get Around in St. Lawrence

The best way to explore St. Lawrence is through cycling, hiking, or driving, but visitors can hire a taxi service for comfortable and timely travel.

Where to Go Next from St. Lawrence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

St. Lawrence is a great destination for those wanting to immerse themselves in natural beauty, indulge in authentic local cuisine and culture, and escape from the crowds of more popular destinations. By following this comprehensive guide, you can plan the perfect trip, making the most of your time in St. Lawrence.
