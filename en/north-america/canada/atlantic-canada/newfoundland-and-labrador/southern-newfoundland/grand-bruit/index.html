Introduction to Grand Bruit

Grand Bruit is a small village located in Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. It is situated on the south coast of the island, and is part of the larger Fortune Bay region. The residents of Grand Bruit are primarily of English and Irish descent, and the village was once a hub for the cod fishing industry. Today, Grand Bruit is known for its unspoiled nature, vibrant local culture, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Grand Bruit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Grand Bruit Heritage and Craft Centre: This community-run museum offers visitors a chance to learn about local history and customs, as well as view handmade crafts made by local artisans.
  • Grand Bruit Whaler’s Memorial: This memorial site is dedicated to the lives lost in the whaling industry off the coast of Southern Newfoundland.
  • St. Michael’s Church: This charming church dates back to the early 1900s, and is a testament to the strong faith of the Grand Bruit community.

Natural Attractions of Grand Bruit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grand Bruit Mountain Hike: This hike offers stunning views of the village and surrounding coastline.
  • Fortune Bay North Trail: This hiking trail is an excellent option for those seeking a longer and more challenging trek, with breathtaking views of the bay and surrounding landscape.
  • Grand Bruit Beach: This secluded and peaceful beach is a hidden gem, perfect for relaxing and enjoying the tranquil surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand Bruit

  • Grand Bruit Mountain Hike: This hike takes 2-3 hours round trip and offers stunning views of the village and surrounding coastline.
  • Fortune Bay North Trail: This hiking trail takes 6-7 hours round trip, and is an excellent option for those seeking a longer and more challenging trek, with breathtaking views of the bay and surrounding landscape.
  • Carrigain Beach Hike: This hike takes 2-3 hours round trip and leads visitors to the secluded and peaceful Carrigain Beach.

Best Things to Do in Grand Bruit: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tour: This tour offers visitors a chance to witness the majesty of the whales that call the Atlantic waters home.
  • Kayaking Tour: This tour is a great way to explore the coastline and nearby bays while getting exercise and fresh air.
  • Fishing Excursion: This excursion offers visitors a chance to try their hand at catching some of the many fish species found in the area’s waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Grand Bruit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cod and Brewis: A traditional Newfoundland dish made with ‘hard tack’ biscuits and salt cod.
  • Jiggs Dinner: A classic Newfoundland dish that includes boiled cabbage, turnip, potatoes, carrots, and salt beef.
  • Toutons: Fried dough that is served with molasses for breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Iceberg Beer: A locally brewed beer made using water from icebergs that float along the coastline.
  • Screech Rum: A Newfoundland rum that has become a symbol of the province’s local culture and hospitality.
  • Newfoundland Tonic: A locally produced tonic water that is perfect for mixing with local spirits.

Where to Stay in Grand Bruit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Seaview Cottage: A private cottage overlooking the bay, perfect for those seeking a secluded and tranquil retreat.
  • Fortune Haven Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse that offers visitors a chance to experience local hospitality and culture.

What to Buy in Grand Bruit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: The Grand Bruit Heritage and Craft Centre offers visitors a chance to purchase handmade crafts made by local artisans.
  • Newfoundland Screech Rum: A bottle of Screech Rum is the perfect memento of your visit to Newfoundland and Labrador.
  • Local Jams and Preserves: Made from locally sourced berries and fruits, these jams and preserves make a delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand Bruit

Grand Bruit is known for its friendly and hospitable locals, as well as its strong ties to local history and culture. Visitors should be prepared to respect local customs and traditions, such as sharing a drink of Screech Rum and participating in traditional Newfoundland music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Grand Bruit

The best time to visit Grand Bruit is during the summer months, from June to September. This is when the weather is warmest and the majority of outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grand Bruit

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as sharing a drink of Screech Rum and participating in traditional Newfoundland music and dance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grand Bruit

Grand Bruit is a safe and welcoming village, but visitors should be aware of potential hazards when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking. Always follow safety guidelines and advice from local guides and tour operators.

Health in Grand Bruit

Grand Bruit is a remote village with limited healthcare facilities. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and come prepared with any necessary medications or medical supplies.

Getting to Grand Bruit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grand Bruit

The nearest airport to Grand Bruit is St. John’s International Airport, located approximately 370 km east of Grand Bruit. From there, visitors can take a rental car or bus to reach the village.

Bus Stations Near Grand Bruit

The nearest bus station to Grand Bruit is in the town of Fortune, approximately 20 km northeast of the village. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rental car to reach Grand Bruit.

Other Options

There are also ferry services available from nearby towns and cities, such as Port aux Basques and Burgeo.

How to Get Around in Grand Bruit

Grand Bruit is a small village with limited options for transportation. Visitors should come prepared with a rental car or be prepared to walk or bike to get around.
