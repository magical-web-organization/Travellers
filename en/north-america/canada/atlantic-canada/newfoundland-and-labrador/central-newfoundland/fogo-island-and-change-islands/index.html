Introduction to Fogo Island and Change Islands

Fogo Island and Change Islands are two remote, but stunningly beautiful, islands in Central Newfoundland, Canada. Both islands are known for their breathtaking scenery, friendly locals, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors to these islands can enjoy a range of activities, from hiking and biking to fishing and whale watching. Additionally, Fogo Island and Change Islands offer a unique opportunity for travelers to experience authentic Newfoundland culture, cuisine, and hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Fogo Island and Change Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fogo Island and Change Islands have a rich cultural history, with museums, galleries, and heritage sites showcasing the area’s traditions and history. One must-visit cultural site is the Beothuk Interpretation Centre on Fogo Island, which tells the story of the Beothuk people, the original inhabitants of Newfoundland. Another culturally significant attraction is the Change Islands Historic District, a walking tour of the island’s preserved heritage buildings, including traditional saltbox houses and fishing stages.

Natural Attractions of Fogo Island and Change Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fogo Island and Change Islands offer spectacular natural scenery, with rugged coastlines, densely forested trails, and pristine beaches. Visitors can explore the islands on foot or bike, with popular trails including the Lion’s Den Trail on Fogo Island and the Change Islands Trails Network on Change Islands. Additionally, water-based activities like kayaking and whale watching provide visitors with up-close experiences to the islands’ marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fogo Island and Change Islands

For those who enjoy hiking and biking, Fogo Island and Change Islands offer an array of trails that take visitors through stunning landscapes and offer breathtaking ocean views. The Fogo Island and Change Islands Trails Networks provide multiple marked trails of varying difficulty levels that showcase the area’s natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Best Things to Do in Fogo Island and Change Islands: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours available in Fogo Island and Change Islands. Visitors can explore the traditional fishing villages, book guided tours of the islands’ rugged coasts and pristine beaches, go whale watching, or try their hand at fishing or crabbing. Additionally, attending local festivals, such as the Fish, Fun, and Folk Festival in Change Islands, provides a glimpse into the heart of Newfoundland’s culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fogo Island and Change Islands

A week-long itinerary for Fogo Island and Change Islands might include exploring the heritage sites, hiking or biking the area’s trails, and participating in guided tours and activities. Visitors can also dedicate time for relaxing on the beach, enjoying local cuisine, and experiencing traditional Newfoundland music and storytelling.

Food and Drink Scene of Fogo Island and Change Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Newfoundland cuisine features local seafood and traditional dishes using locally sourced ingredients. For visitors to Fogo Island and Change Islands, local delicacies include fresh cod or cod tongue, seafood chowder, toutons (traditional Newfoundland fried dough), and Jigg’s Dinner (a traditional Newfoundland boiled dinner with cabbage, turnip, potatoes, and salt beef).

Local Beverages

Among Newfoundland’s beverages, the most popular are craft beer, iceberg water (as a non-alcoholic alternative), and the locally made Screech Rum. Additionally, local wineries and distilleries offer tasting tours and opportunities to sample unique, Newfoundland-inspired drinks.

Where to Stay in Fogo Island and Change Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fogo Island and Change Islands offer an array of lodging options, including cozy bed and breakfasts, boutique hotels, and luxurious resorts. Visitors can also opt for unique accommodation experiences, such as staying in one of Fogo Island’s striking modern architectural designs or in a traditional saltbox house in Change Islands.

What to Buy in Fogo Island and Change Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors have the opportunity to bring home a piece of Newfoundland’s culture and heritage by purchasing locally made artisan crafts and artwork, woolen goods, and traditional Newfoundland music. Additionally, local shops offer products made using locally sourced ingredients, such as jams and baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Fogo Island and Change Islands

Newfoundland culture is steeped in traditions and customs influenced by local folklore and the area’s unique natural surroundings. Visitors will find locals friendly and welcoming, and traditional Newfoundland music and storytelling are an essential part of the culture. Newfoundlanders also hold a unique ceremony known as “Newfoundland Screech-In,” which welcomes visitors to the island and involves drinking local Screech Rum and kissing a cod.

Best Time to Visit Fogo Island and Change Islands

The best time to visit Fogo Island and Change Islands is during the summer months, from late June to September, when the weather is warmest, and many local festivals and events occur. Additionally, visitors can experience the stunning fall foliage during the months of October and November or continuously enjoy the winter season’s beauty and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fogo Island and Change Islands

Do:

  • Respect the islands’ natural environments and take care of local wildlife.
  • Try the local seafood and traditional dishes.
  • Attend local festivals and cultural events.
  • Take the time to explore the area’s unique cultural heritage.
  • Be friendly and receptive to locals.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the opportunity to connect with the area’s friendly locals.
  • Miss the opportunity to sample the area’s unique cuisine and beverages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fogo Island and Change Islands

Fogo Island and Change Islands are generally safe, with little crime and a welcoming community. Visitors should take care when hiking or participating in outdoor activities, as the terrain can be rugged.

Health in Fogo Island and Change Islands

The level of health care available in Fogo Island and Change Islands is excellent, with clinics and hospitals available. Visitors should carry their necessary medications, though, as it’s a remote area, certain medications might not be readily available.

Getting to Fogo Island and Change Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fogo Island and Change Islands are remote, but visitors can access the islands via ferry or plane. Ferries operate year-round from Farewell to Fogo Island and Change Islands. Visitors can also fly to Gander International Airport or Deer Lake Airport, which is a 2-3 hour drive from Fogo Island and Change Islands. Transport options available on arrival include taxis, car rental, or bike rental.

Airports Near Fogo Island and Change Islands

The closest airports to Fogo Island and Change Islands are Gander International Airport and Deer Lake Airport.

Bus Stations Near Fogo Island and Change Islands

There are no bus stations available on Fogo Island and Change Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can access Fogo Island and Change Islands via water taxi, helicopter, or private boat.

How to Get Around in Fogo Island and Change Islands

Visitors can access Fogo Island and Change Islands by foot, bicycle, rental car, or taxi. Additionally, local tour operators offer guided tours of the area’s spectacular landscapes, cultural sites, and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Fogo Island and Change Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Fogo Island and Change Islands, visitors can continue their adventure by exploring nearby areas such as Twilingate, Trinity, and Bonavista. Additionally, visitors can extend their Newfoundland experience by exploring the province’s diverse and beautiful landscapes, including Western Newfoundland’s Gros Morne National Park and the East Coast Trail near St. John’s.

Conclusion

Fogo Island and Change Islands offer a unique experience for travelers seeking to explore an unspoiled landscape, rich cultural heritage, and unique cuisine. With an array of natural and cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and lodging options, Fogo Island and Change Islands are a must-visit destination for adventurous travelers looking to experience authentic Newfoundland culture and hospitality.
