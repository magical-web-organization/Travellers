A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Grand Falls-Windsor, Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada

Grand Falls-Windsor is a charming town located in Central Newfoundland, in the province of Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. It is a perfect destination for those who enjoy outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and scenic views. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best things to see and do in Grand Falls-Windsor, including hiking and biking routes, must-visit cultural and historical landmarks, delicious local food and drink scene, as well as local customs and safety tips.

Introduction to Grand Falls-Windsor

Grand Falls-Windsor is a vibrant town that boasts a scenic location surrounded by the mighty Exploits River and rolling hills. It is home to a diverse population of about 13,000 residents, whose historical and cultural diversity are reflected in the many attractions and landmarks that dot the town. The town has a rich history and cultural heritage with fascinating stories dating back to the early 1900s.

List of Places to Visit in Grand Falls-Windsor

There are several places to visit when exploring Grand Falls-Windsor that suit all tastes and interests, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you enjoy learning about history and culture, there are several museums and historical landmarks that you should add to your itinerary, including:

  • Mary March Provincial Museum: Discover the rich history, customs, and cultural heritage of Newfoundland and Labrador at the Mary March Provincial Museum. This museum showcases an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits from early European settlements, ancient Aboriginal cultures, and modern-day Newfoundland and Labrador.

  • Harmsworth House: Built-in the early 1900s as a place of residence for members of the Harmsworth family, Harmsworth House is one of the most important cultural landmarks in Grand Falls-Windsor. Today, it is open to visitors who come to see its beautiful architecture, interior decor, and rich history.

  • The Salmonid Interpretation Center: Located on the banks of the Exploits River, the Salmonid Interpretation Center is a fascinating place to learn about the Atlantic salmon’s life cycle and see the fish up close. The center also offers guided tours of the impressive Salmon ladder, which helps the fish bypass the nearby dam.

Natural Attractions of Grand Falls-Windsor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grand Falls-Windsor is known for its gorgeous natural landscape, which offers visitors numerous opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Exploits River: The Exploits River is the largest river in Newfoundland and Labrador, and it boasts some of the best fishing, hiking, and kayaking opportunities in the region. The river is also an excellent spot for birdwatching.

  • Corduroy Brook Trail: If you enjoy hiking or biking, the Corduroy Brook Trail is an excellent choice. This 6-kilometer trail follows the path of an old railway track and offers breathtaking views of the river and surrounding hills.

  • Salmonier Nature Park: Located just outside Grand Falls-Windsor, Salmonier Nature Park is a wildlife and nature reserve that is home to several indigenous plants and animals such as Moose, Lynx, and Black Bear. Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the reserve and learn about local wildlife and nature conservation efforts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand Falls-Windsor

Grand Falls-Windsor is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many scenic routes to choose from. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the town include:

  • Corduroy Brook Trail: An easy hike that follows the path of an old railway track. The trail takes you through beautiful forests and offers views of the river and surrounding hills.

  • Beothuk Interpretation Trail: This moderate to the difficult trail offers hikers stunning views of the Exploits River’s gorge. It is an excellent option for seasoned hikers.

  • The Willows Hiking Trail: This easy to moderate trail takes hikers through a forested area with a diverse range of flora and fauna. It is an excellent option for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Grand Falls-Windsor: Activities and Tours

Grand Falls-Windsor offers visitors a wide selection of things to do, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Below are some of the best activities and tours to do in Grand Falls-Windsor:

  • Salmon Fishing: Grand Falls-Windsor is home to some of the best salmon fishing spots in Canada. Visitors can enjoy guided tours and rentals of fishing equipment.

  • Exploring the Town: The town’s unique architecture, historical landmarks, and cultural attractions make it an excellent place for walking and bike tours, allowing visitors to soak up the town’s charm.

  • T’Railway Provincial Park: This park stretches over 900 kilometers, making it Canada’s second-longest park. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, or snowmobiling on one of the well-manicured trails while also taking in the beautiful scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grand Falls-Windsor

Grand Falls-Windsor is diverse enough to keep visitors entertained for more than a week. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in the morning and explore the town’s historical landmarks, such as Harmsworth House and the Mary March Provincial Museum.

Day 2: Take a hiking tour of the Corduroy Brook Trail and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 3: Visit the Salmonid Interpretation Center and take a tour of the Salmon ladder.

Day 4: Take a bike tour of the town and enjoy the local architecture and cultural landmarks.

Day 5: Visit the T’Railway Provincial Park and enjoy hiking, biking, or snowmobiling the trails.

Day 6: Take a salmon fishing tour of the Exploits River.

Day 7: Depart for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Grand Falls-Windsor

Grand Falls-Windsor has a vibrant food and drink scene that is a must-experience for any visitor. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiggs Dinner: A traditional Newfoundland dish made of boiled vegetables, salt beef, and potatoes.

  • Toutons: A delicious local treat made of fried dough drizzled with molasses.

  • Fish and Chips: Fresh, locally caught fish served with crispy chips.

Local Beverages

  • Newfoundland Screech Rum: A flavorful dark rum with a long history of being served in Newfoundland and Labrador.

  • Iceberg Beer: A crisp and refreshing beer made with water harvested from nearby icebergs.

Where to Stay in Grand Falls-Windsor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grand Falls-Windsor has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here some top choices are:

  • Riverfront Chalets: These cozy cabins are set on the banks of the Exploits River and offer scenic views and comfortable accommodations.

  • Mount Peyton Hotel: This modern hotel offers guests excellent amenities, including a restaurant, fitness center, and indoor pool.

  • Exploits Hostel: An affordable option for backpackers and budget travelers that provides comfortable beds and a great atmosphere.

What to Buy in Grand Falls-Windsor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grand Falls-Windsor has several local products and souvenirs that make great gifts, including:

  • Local Crafts: There are several local craft shops in town that sell handmade jewelry, pottery, and clothing.

  • Local Food and Drink: Bring home a taste of Newfoundland and Labrador by purchasing locally produced jams, teas, and rum.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand Falls-Windsor

Grand Falls-Windsor has a rich and diverse culture that is worth experiencing. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions, including the traditional music and dance of Newfoundland and Labrador.

Best Time to Visit Grand Falls-Windsor

The best time to visit Grand Falls-Windsor is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is pleasant and many outdoor activities are available. However, fall and spring are also excellent times to visit as the scenery is beautiful, and the town is less crowded.

Getting to Grand Falls-Windsor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grand Falls-Windsor

The nearest airport is the Gander International Airport, which is about 70 kilometers from Grand Falls-Windsor.

Bus Stations Near Grand Falls-Windsor

Several bus companies, including DRL Coachlines and TNL Express, operate in Newfoundland and Labrador. Bus routes connect Grand Falls-Windsor to other towns and cities in the province.

Other Options

Renting a car or bike is also an excellent option for visitors who wish to explore Grand Falls-Windsor and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Grand Falls-Windsor

Grand Falls-Windsor is a small town that can be conveniently explored on foot. However, visitors can also rent cars, bicycles or take taxis and buses to get around.

Where to Go Next from Grand Falls-Windsor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several exciting places to visit near Grand Falls-Windsor, including:
