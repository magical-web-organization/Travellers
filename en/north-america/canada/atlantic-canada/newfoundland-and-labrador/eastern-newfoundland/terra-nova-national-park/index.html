Introduction to Terra Nova National Park

Terra Nova National Park is one of Canada’s most beautiful and untouched natural treasures. Located in Eastern Newfoundland, in Newfoundland and Labrador, in Atlantic Canada, it is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. The park covers over 400 km² of pristine boreal forest, inland ponds, and coastal bays. With its mix of rugged terrain and breathtaking scenery, Terra Nova National Park is truly a wonder of nature.

List of Places to Visit in Terra Nova National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most significant historical sites in the park is the Newman Wine Vaults, which used to hold wines and spirits shipped to Newfoundland from the Old World. It’s now a national historic site originally built in the early 1700s and has been well-preserved. Next is The Salvation Army Citadel, which served as a gathering place for the community. Visitors can see the centre’s original documents, photographs, and clothing worn by the past cadets. Finally, there’s Rattling Brook Falls, which is a popular spot for hikers, especially for tourists who love a breathtaking view of a waterfall chopping down an impressive 160 feet.

Natural Attractions of Terra Nova National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Terra Nova National Park’s remarkable scenery includes several rivers and streams, over 100 km of hiking and biking trails, and miles of unspoiled coastline. The park is home to many wildlife species such as moose, caribou, and foxes; many of whom roam free in their natural habitats. For visitors who want to get up close and personal with these creatures, wildlife tours are available. The park is also famous for its cabin/camping facilities, where visitors can sleep overnight and enjoy the beauty of a star-filled sky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Terra Nova National Park

There are over 100km of hiking and mountain biking trails in Terra Nova National Park. The most popular route is the Coastal trail, which stretches for over 30km and provides visitors with some of the park’s most breathtaking views. The Ochre Hill Trail, the South Broad Cove Trail, and the Malady Head Trail are also excellent treks for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Terra Nova National Park: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and mountain biking, there are several other activities and tours visitors can take part in. Among others, visitors enjoy kayaking, canoeing, and boating. The park also offers golf, guided hiking tours, and whale watching. In the winter months, snowshoeing and cross-country skiing are available. Terra Nova National Park is an ideal destination for families and solo travellers looking for an adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Terra Nova National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Newfoundland and Labrador are famous for their unique cuisine, including dishes like Toutons (fried dough), Baked Salmon, and Fish and Brewis (made with salt cod). Seafood is abundant in Terra Nova National Park, and visitors can enjoy an array of fresh seafood dishes like crab cakes, lobster, and mussels for an authentic Newfoundland experience.

Local Beverages

Craft beer and whiskey are experiencing a renaissance in Newfoundland and Labrador, so visitors should try the locally brewed Yellowbelly beer or the St. John’s Iceberg Rum. Newfoundland and Labrador is known for producing excellent varieties of wine, such as the non-sweet red wine “Demure.”

Where to Stay in Terra Nova National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for staying in Terra Nova National Park, ranging from hotels to campsites. For those who love living in a cabin style, Terra Nova Resort and Country Inn is the best option. Their guests boast of spacious and comfortable rooms, and they are located conveniently, just few kilometres away from many attractions like the Burin Peninsula and Gros Morne National Park, among others. Another alternative is the Terra Nova Golf Resort, which provides excellent facilities including Golf, Minigolf, and Tennis courts.

What to Buy in Terra Nova National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase several local products, including freshly caught seafood from the park’s fishermen. Visitors can also indulge in local handicrafts and purchase traditional Newfoundland salt fish. Several shops sell sealskin shoes, capotes, and other clothing items made from sealskin or other Newfoundland leather. Visitors can also purchase paintings and handicrafts made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Terra Nova National Park

Newfoundland and Labrador has a rich cultural heritage. Local customs vary and include the famous Newfoundland music, dance, and storytelling tradition from its Irish and English settlers’ culture. Visitors should also be familiar with Newfoundland English, the dialect spoken by locals before going.

Best Time to Visit Terra Nova National Park

The best time to visit Terra Nova National Park is from late spring to early autumn when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer is the peak season, and visitors must book accommodations and tours well in advance. Fall is the second-best time of year to visit, with fewer crowds and beautiful fall foliage. Winter, especially the Christmas season has its charm with outdoor activities such as snowmobiling, skiing, and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Terra Nova National Park

Do:

  • Dress in layers as the weather can change quickly.
  • Respect wildlife and stay at a safe distance.
  • Stay on designated trails, and do not venture off into the wilderness.
  • Bring reusable water bottles to refill with fresh water from the park.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park or leave any garbage behind.
  • Approach wildlife too closely or feed them.
  • Carry any firearms or weapons in the park.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas or leave smoking materials behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Terra Nova National Park

Terra Nova National Park is considered a safe place to visit, but visitors must be aware of the risks posed by wildlife and rough terrain. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquitoes, which can be abundant in the summer months.

Health in Terra Nova National Park

Visitors should take precautions to prevent tick bites, which can be common in the park during the summer. The park has limited health services available, and visitors should bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Terra Nova National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Terra Nova National Park through several transportation options. The closest airport, Gander International Airport, is approximately 90 km away from the park. Visitors can also use bus services from Newfoundland’s major cities to the park’s entrance.

Airports Near Terra Nova National Park

  1. Gander International Airport - approximately 90 km away from the park.

Bus Stations Near Terra Nova National Park

  1. Avalon Peninsula - approximately 90 km away from the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars at the airport or taxi services to the park.

How to Get Around in Terra Nova National Park

Visitors can rent cars, bicycles, or walk to get around the park. Rental cars can be readily available at the airport or from within the park. Visitors can also go on guided tours and hikes inside the park.

## Where to Go Next from Terra Nova National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore Newfoundland’s Burin Peninsula and Gros Morne National Park, which are popular destinations located nearby and have outstanding features like the Berry Head Lighthouse, Green Gardens hiking trail.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Terra Nova National Park is undoubtedly a nature lover’s paradise, complete with scenic views, historic sites, beautiful trails and paths, and incredible local culture to indulge in. The park is an amazing destination with so many things to do and see. Visitors must plan well and follow safety guidelines while exploring Terra Nova National Park.
