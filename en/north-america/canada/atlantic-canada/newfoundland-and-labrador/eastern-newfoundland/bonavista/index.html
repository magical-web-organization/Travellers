Introduction to Bonavista

Nestled on the eastern coast of Newfoundland in Atlantic Canada, Bonavista offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals. From the rugged coastline to the historic lighthouse, there’s plenty to explore in Bonavista. In this guide, we’ll take you through the must-see places, unique experiences, and practical tips to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Bonavista.

List of Places to Visit in Bonavista

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ryan Premises National Historic Site: Discover the rich history and culture of Bonavista at this former fish merchant’s complex.
  • Mockbeggar Plantation: Explore the reconstructed 18th-century village and learn about the area’s traditional way of life.
  • The Matthew Legacy: Visit the replica of John Cabot’s ship, The Matthew, and learn about the historical voyages that shaped the area.

Natural Attractions of Bonavista: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Bonavista Lighthouse: Admire the panoramic views and learn about Newfoundland’s seafaring history at this iconic lighthouse.
  • Elliston’s Puffin Viewing Site: Witness the adorable Atlantic puffins in their natural habitat at one of the continent’s largest puffin colonies.
  • Bonavista Adventure Tours: Experience a wide range of outdoor activities, from sea kayaking to hiking, and explore the scenic beauty of Bonavista.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bonavista

  • The Cape Shore Trail: Enjoy the stunning coastal views and explore the rugged shoreline of Cape Bonavista on foot or by bike.
  • The Dungeon Provincial Park: Hike through the forest and rugged coastline, and explore the unique sea caves and rock formations at the Dungeon.
  • The Keystone Loop Trail: Bike or hike through the beautiful coastal meadows and forests, and keep an eye out for local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Bonavista: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local art galleries and studios: Explore the unique craftsmanship and artistry of Newfoundland’s creative community.
  • Take a scenic drive on the Baccalieu Trail: Drive along the coast and explore some of Newfoundland’s charming coastal towns.
  • Go on a whale-watching tour: Experience the beauty of the ocean and witness the majestic whales that frequent the surrounding waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Bonavista

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salt fish and Brewis: This traditional Newfoundland dish of salt fish and boiled potatoes is a must-try for any visitor to Bonavista.
  • Fish and Chips: Indulge in some classic fish and chips at one of Bonavista’s seafood restaurants.
  • Bakeapple Liquor: Savour the unique taste of bakeapple, a wild Newfoundland berry, in the form of a dessert liquor.

Local Beverages

  • Iceberg Beer: Try the locally brewed beer, made with pure iceberg water from the surrounding North Atlantic.
  • Newfoundland Screech: Experience the traditional Newfoundland rum and follow the Screech-In ceremony to become an honorary Newfoundlander.

Where to Stay in Bonavista: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bonavista B&B: Stay in this cozy and comfortable bed and breakfast that offers stunning ocean views and friendly hosts.
  • The Garrick Hotel: This newly renovated hotel offers modern amenities and a central location in the heart of Bonavista.
  • The Loftus Heritage Guest House: Experience the heritage charm and vintage decor of this historic guest house that overlooks the picturesque Bonavista harbour.

What to Buy in Bonavista: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sea Salt: Bring home some locally harvested sea salt to add flavour to your dishes at home.
  • Knitted clothing and accessories: Take home some warm and cozy knitted clothing and accessories, such as hats, mittens, and scarves, made by local artisans.
  • Local art and crafts: Buy some unique Newfoundland art and crafts, such as hand-carved wooden sculptures or hand-painted glassware.

Local Culture and Customs of Bonavista

Music and dance are an essential part of Newfoundland’s cultural heritage. The lively and spirited performances that feature traditional instruments - such as the accordion, fiddle, and tin whistle - are common in Bonavista. The locals are also known for their friendly and hospitable nature, and they enjoy sharing their culture and customs with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Bonavista

The best time to visit Bonavista is from May to August when the weather is mild, and the scenic sites are in full bloom. However, the fall season (September to October) offers beautiful foliage and fewer crowds, making it an ideal time for hiking and exploring. Winters in Bonavista are quite cold, but they offer a chance to experience the unique winter festivals and cultural celebrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bonavista

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty and scenic landscapes of Bonavista
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Visit the local art galleries and cultural sites
  • Learn about Newfoundland’s rich seafaring history

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions of Bonavista
  • Disturb the wildlife, including the Atlantic puffins
  • Ignore the safety and health warnings while exploring the outdoor attractions

Safety and Health

Safety in Bonavista

While exploring the natural attractions of Bonavista, it is essential to take precautions and respect the local safety guidelines. Watch out for slippery or unstable terrain, wear sturdy shoes, and stay on the designated trails. If you’re hiking or biking alone, inform someone about your plans and expected return time.

Health in Bonavista

Bonavista has a modern and reliable healthcare system, and visitors can visit any of the local clinics or hospitals if needed. It is also important to carry enough medication and first aid supplies if required.

Getting to Bonavista: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bonavista

The closest airport to Bonavista is St. John’s International Airport, located about 3 hours away by car. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to reach Bonavista.

Bus Stations Near Bonavista

The local bus service, DRL Coachlines, connects Bonavista to other cities and towns in Newfoundland.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a guided tour that includes transportation and accommodation, or rent a car to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Bonavista

The best way to explore Bonavista and its surrounding areas is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can rent a car from the local rental agencies, or take a taxi to travel within the town.

Where to Go Next from Bonavista: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Terra Nova National Park: Explore the diverse wildlife, hiking trails, and beautiful beaches of this stunning national park, located about 2 hours away from Bonavista.
  • The Irish Loop: Drive along the scenic Irish Loop to explore the charming coastal towns and stunning landscapes of the Avalon Peninsula, located south of Bonavista.

Conclusion

Bonavista offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. From historic museums to scenic hiking trails, there’s something for everyone in this charming coastal town. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can easily plan a memorable trip to Bonavista and make the most of your time exploring this hidden gem of Newfoundland and Labrador.
