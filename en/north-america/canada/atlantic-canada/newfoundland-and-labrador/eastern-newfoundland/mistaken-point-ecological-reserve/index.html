Introduction to Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve is a UNESCO World Heritage site that covers a stretch of rugged coastline on the Avalon Peninsula in Eastern Newfoundland. The 17 km² reserve features fossilized remains of the oldest known complex life forms on Earth, dating back 580 to 560 million years. Guided interpretive tours are offered for visitors to view these remarkable geological treasures.

Aside from its unique geological significance, Mistaken Point is also an excellent destination for outdoor adventures. The reserve’s diverse ecosystem includes barren, windswept hills, coastal bogs, and heathlands that are home to a variety of wildlife species. Visitors can hike, bike, camp, or take boat tours to discover the reserve’s natural beauty.

This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve.

List of Places to Visit in Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Colony of Avalon - A reconstructed 17th-century colony that interprets the early European colonization of Newfoundland. Guided tours, exhibits, and archaeological sites tell the story of the settlement and its inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mistaken Point Fossils - Guided interpretive tours are offered for visitors to view the fossilized remains of the oldest known complex life forms on Earth on the explore the coastline of the reserve.
  • Hiking Trails - Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve has several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the reserve’s unique ecosystem. The trails range in difficulty and length, so visitors can choose one that suits their fitness level.
  • Boat Tours - Visitors can take boat tours from Portugal Cove South to explore the rugged coastline and view the reserve’s fossil sites from the water.
  • Camping - Camping is available at two locations within the reserve - Bay Bulls Big Pond and Tors Cove Pond. Both offer basic campsites for tents and RVs in a wilderness setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve offers several hiking and biking trails that vary in length and difficulty. The Reserve is open year-round, but some trails may be inaccessible during the winter months.

Trail descriptions and difficulty levels:

  • Mistaken Point Trail - 8 km return, moderate. This trail takes hikers to the Mistaken Point Fossil Site and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Gaskiers Point Trail - 6 km return, easy. This trail winds through a picturesque fishing village and offers views of the surrounding hills and coastline.
  • Big Hill Trail - 16 km return, difficult. This challenging trail climbs up to the highest point in the reserve and offers sweeping views of the coast and interior.

Food and Drink Scene of Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cod Tongues - A Newfoundland delicacy made from the tongue of the Atlantic cod, fried until crispy and golden-brown.
  • Moose Stew - A hearty stew made from local moose meat, potatoes, carrots, and other vegetables.
  • Fish and Chips - Fresh, locally caught fish battered and fried to crispy perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Screech - A Newfoundland rum with a high alcohol content, traditionally enjoyed as part of a ‘screech-in’ ceremony for visitors to the province.
  • Iceberg Beer - Produced by Quidi Vidi Brewery, this beer is made with water sourced from 20,000-year-old iceberg that float off the coast of Newfoundland.

Local Culture and Customs of Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Newfoundland and Labrador have a distinct culture and identity that reflects their long history as a seafaring nation. Residents of the area are known for their hospitality, good humor, and love of music and story-telling. Visitors should make time to attend traditional music and dance events like the Newfoundland Kitchen Party and the St. John’s Folk Festival.

Best Time to Visit Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve is open year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences.

For outdoor enthusiasts, the spring and summer months (May to August) are the best time to visit, when the weather is mild and the reserve’s flora and fauna are in full bloom. The fall months are also beautiful, with changing colors that make for great photographic opportunities.

Winter is also a great time to visit for those interested in winter sports like skiing or snowshoeing, but be prepared for cold and snowy conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Do:

  • Follow all the rules and regulations of the reserve to minimize your impact on the ecosystem.
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife and do not feed them.
  • Pack out all garbage and leave no trace to keep the reserve pristine for future visitors.
  • Respect the culture and customs of the local communities and seek out opportunities to learn about their traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take fossils or other natural objects from the reserve.
  • Camp or build fires outside of designated areas.
  • Leave the trails or kayak routes in the reserve to ensure the protection of the ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve is a safe destination for visitors, but it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety while enjoying the reserve’s natural beauty.

  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions and dress in layers.
  • Stay on designated trails and stay aware of your surroundings.
  • Watch your footing on rocky terrain and be careful near the water’s edge.
  • Bring insect repellent, as mosquitoes and other biting insects can be active in the summer months.

Health in Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve is located in a rural area, so it’s important to take steps to ensure your health during your stay.

  • Bring any necessary medications with you.
  • Drink plenty of water and avoid dehydration.
  • Wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun’s UV rays.
  • Stay aware of the potential risks of tick-borne illnesses and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing long-sleeved shirts and using insect repellent with DEET.

Getting to Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve is located on the southern tip of Newfoundland’s Avalon Peninsula, approximately 150 kilometers south of St. John’s.

Airports Near Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

  • St. John’s International Airport - Located approximately 107 kilometers north of Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve.

Bus Stations Near Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

  • Bus Service - Bus services are available between St. John’s and the nearby town of Trepassey, approximately 15 kilometers from the reserve.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from St. John’s to Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve.

How to Get Around in Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

The best way to get around Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve is by foot or bike. Several hiking and biking trails provide access to some of the reserve’s most scenic areas. There is also a road that runs through the reserve for visitors with vehicles.

Where to Go Next from Mistaken Point Ecological Reserve

  • St. John’s - Newfoundland’s capital city is a vibrant cultural hub known for its colorful architecture, charming streets, and distinctive cuisine.
  • Cape Race Lighthouse - The site of the first distress signal from the Titanic is just a short drive from Mistaken Point.
  • Cape Spear - Located just 60 kilometers north of Mistaken Point, Cape Spear is the easternmost point in North America, with stunning coastal views and a historic lighthouse.
