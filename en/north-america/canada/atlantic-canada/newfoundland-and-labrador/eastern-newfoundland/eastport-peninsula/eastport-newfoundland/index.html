Introduction to Eastport (Newfoundland)

Eastport is a picturesque town located on the Eastport Peninsula in the eastern part of Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, historical sites, and friendly locals. The town has a population of just over 700 people, making it a quiet and peaceful destination for travelers looking to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Eastport (Newfoundland)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eastport Peninsula Heritage Society

The Eastport Peninsula Heritage Society is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the Eastport Peninsula. Visitors can browse through the exhibits and artifacts that showcase the town’s rich history, including the role it played in the fishing and logging industries. The society is located on Main Street in Eastport.

The Eastport Art Gallery

The Eastport Art Gallery features the works of local artists, including paintings, sculptures, and photography. The gallery is located on Main Street and often hosts special events and workshops throughout the year.

The Beaches Heritage Centre

The Beaches Heritage Centre is a great place to learn more about the history and culture of the surrounding area. The center offers exhibits on the fishing and logging industries, as well as the cultural heritage of the region. It is located on Beaches Road and is open year-round.

Natural Attractions of Eastport (Newfoundland): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Terra Nova National Park

Terra Nova National Park is a stunning wilderness park that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the region. The park features hiking trails, camping facilities, and breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding landscape. It is located just a short drive from Eastport and is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts.

Sandy Cove Beach

Sandy Cove Beach is a beautiful sandy beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. Visitors can take in the stunning views of the ocean and surrounding cliffs while enjoying the clear waters and relaxing atmosphere.

The Mattie Mitchell Trail

The Mattie Mitchell Trail is a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape. The trail is 10 km long and is rated as moderate, making it a great option for hikers of all skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastport (Newfoundland)

The East Coast Trail

The East Coast Trail is a 336 km-long hiking trail that winds its way along the coast of Newfoundland. It offers stunning views of the ocean and several smaller trails that can be completed in a day. The trail passes through Eastport and is a great option for hikers looking for a challenging and scenic adventure.

Best Things to Do in Eastport (Newfoundland): Activities and Tours

Kayaking Tours

Kayaking tours are a great way to explore the natural beauty of the region and get up close and personal with the wildlife. There are several companies that offer guided tours, including Cozy Corner Kayak and Seakisses Kayaking.

Fishing Charters

Fishing charters are popular in Eastport thanks to the town’s rich fishing heritage. Visitors can book a charter with one of several companies and try their luck at catching cod, salmon, or trout.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastport (Newfoundland)

Must-Try Dishes

Toutons

Toutons are a traditional Newfoundland breakfast food made from fried dough that is served with molasses or syrup. They are a delicious and filling way to start the day.

Fish and Chips

Fish and chips are a popular dish in Eastport thanks to the town’s rich fishing heritage. Visitors can enjoy fresh, locally-caught fish that is breaded and fried to perfection.

Local Beverages

Screech

Screech is a type of rum that is produced in Newfoundland. It has a high alcohol content and is often served as a shot or mixed with Coke.

Iceberg Beer

Iceberg Beer is a unique beer that is brewed with water sourced from icebergs that float off the coast of Newfoundland. It has a crisp and refreshing taste and is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Eastport (Newfoundland): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at Happy Adventure

The Inn at Happy Adventure is a charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy rooms and stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is located just a short drive from Eastport and is the perfect option for travelers looking for a quiet and relaxing stay.

The Hillside Suites

The Hillside Suites offer stylish and modern accommodations that are perfect for couples or families. The suites are located on a hillside overlooking the town of Eastport and offer stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Eastport (Newfoundland): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Crafts

Eastport is known for its rich craft culture, and visitors can browse through several local shops and markets to find unique souvenirs and gifts. Some popular options include pottery, jewelry, and wood carvings.

Locally Made Food Products

Visitors can also bring home locally made food products, including jams, jellies, and baked goods. These items are the perfect way to savor the flavors of Eastport long after your trip has ended.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastport (Newfoundland)

Eastport has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can immerse themselves in the local customs and traditions during their visit. Some popular cultural attractions include the Great Eastern Music Festival, which takes place annually in August, and the town’s annual Fisherman’s Festival, which celebrates the town’s history and heritage as a fishing community.

Best Time to Visit Eastport (Newfoundland)

The best time to visit Eastport is during the summer months, between June and September. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, and visitors can take advantage of the many outdoor activities and events that take place across the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastport (Newfoundland)

Do:

  • Get to know the locals and their culture
  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and events

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Be disrespectful or rude to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastport (Newfoundland)

Eastport is a safe and welcoming community, and visitors can feel confident exploring the town and its surrounding areas. However, it is always important to exercise common sense and caution, particularly when engaging in outdoor activities or exploring unfamiliar areas.

Health in Eastport (Newfoundland)

Medical facilities are available in Eastport, and visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance before traveling to the region.

Getting to Eastport (Newfoundland): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastport (Newfoundland)

The nearest airport to Eastport is Gander International Airport, which is located approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Eastport (Newfoundland)

There are several bus stations located near Eastport, including ones in Gander and Clarenville. However, it is recommended that visitors rent a car to have more flexibility and freedom when exploring the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the ferry from Nova Scotia to Newfoundland and drive to Eastport from there.

How to Get Around in Eastport (Newfoundland)

The most convenient way to get around Eastport is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in nearby towns like Gander or Clarenville.

Where to Go Next from Eastport (Newfoundland): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bonavista

Bonavista is a charming town located just a short drive from Eastport. Visitors can explore the historic lighthouse and browse through several local shops and restaurants.

St. John’s

St. John’s is the capital city of Newfoundland and Labrador and is located approximately 3 hours away by car. It is a vibrant and bustling city that offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy.

Conclusion

Eastport is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the natural landscape and rich culture of Newfoundland and Labrador. With its charming small-town atmosphere and stunning natural beauty, Eastport is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a unique and memorable experience.
