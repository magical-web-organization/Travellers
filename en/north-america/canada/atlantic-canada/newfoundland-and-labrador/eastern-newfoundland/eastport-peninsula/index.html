Introduction to Eastport Peninsula

Located in Eastern Newfoundland, in Atlantic Canada, Eastport Peninsula is an idyllic destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Its breathtaking natural scenery, rich culture, and warm hospitality will captivate your heart and invite you to explore its many wonders.

The peninsula is accessible by car or bus, and some areas can also be explored on foot or bike. From stunning coastal views to charming fishing villages, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to experience the unique culture and natural beauty of Eastport.

List of Places to Visit in Eastport Peninsula

Eastport Peninsula has a wealth of attractions for travelers, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Beaches Arts and Heritage Centre: This museum and cultural center showcases the history and culture of Eastport Peninsula, including exhibits on local history, art, and traditional craft-making.
  • Terra Nova National Park: This park features historic sites dating back to the early 17th century, including a reconstructed 19th-century salmon fishing site and a lighthouse that has been in operation since 1903.
  • Norton’s Cove Studio: This studio features the work of local artist Janet Davis and is a great place to browse local crafts, pottery, and artwork.

Natural Attractions of Eastport Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Skerwink Trail: This 5.3km (3.3 miles) coastal trail has spectacular views of Trinity Bay and features frequent stops with interpretive signs and stunning panoramic views.
  • Sandy Cove Beach: Located in Terra Nova National Park, this sandy beach is a great spot for swimming and beachcombing.
  • Tickle Cove Sea Arch: A geological wonder, this sea arch is an amazing place to view the ocean and watch seabirds, and is located in Tickle Cove, a quaint coastal village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastport Peninsula

Eastport Peninsula has many great hiking and biking trails that allow you to experience the natural beauty of the region up close. Some popular routes to explore include:

  • Skerwink Trail: As mentioned above, this trail is a must-visit, with stunning coastal views and opportunities to spot seabirds and whales.
  • Eastport Peninsula Coastal Trail: This 20km (12.4 miles) trail winds through many of the area’s coastal communities, including Salvage and Sandy Cove.
  • Terra Nova National Park’s Coastal Trails: The park offers several hikes and biking routes, including the Coastal Trail and the Boardwalk Trail, that allow visitors to explore the park’s natural beauty and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Eastport Peninsula: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Eastport Peninsula, including:

  • Whale watching tours: Eastport Peninsula is a great place to spot whales, including humpback, minke, and fin whales, and there are many tours available that take you out on the ocean for a chance to see these majestic creatures up close.
  • Kayaking tours: Kayaking is a great way to explore the coastlines of Eastport Peninsula and to see seabirds and other local wildlife.
  • Fishing tours: The area is famous for its salmon and trout fishing, and there are many opportunities for guided fishing expeditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastport Peninsula

If you’re planning to spend a week in Eastport Peninsula, consider this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Salvage and visit the Beaches Arts and Heritage Centre.
  • Day 2: Hike the Skerwink Trail and visit the Tickle Cove Sea Arch.
  • Day 3: Take a fishing or kayaking tour, or relax on one of the area’s beautiful beaches.
  • Day 4: Visit Norton’s Cove Studio and Terra Nova National Park’s historic sites and hiking trails.
  • Day 5: Indulge in the local food and drink scene by visiting local restaurants and breweries.
  • Day 6: Take a whale watching tour and explore more of the area’s natural beauty.
  • Day 7: Depart Eastport Peninsula, but not before picking up some local souvenirs to bring home as a reminder of your trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastport Peninsula

Eastport Peninsula is known for its fresh seafood, craft beer, and locally produced jams and preserves. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Traditional fish and chips: This classic Newfoundland dish is a must-try when visiting Eastport Peninsula, and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Cod tongues: Another local delicacy, cod tongues are a unique and delicious treat that can be found at many seafood restaurants in the area.
  • Toutons: A Newfoundland staple, toutons are similar to doughnuts or pancakes and are often served with molasses or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Quidi Vidi Brewery: This local brewery produces a range of craft beers, including the popular Iceberg Lager.
  • Newfoundland Distillery Company: This distillery produces a range of spirits using locally sourced ingredients, including blueberry liqueur and seaweed gin.

Where to Stay in Eastport Peninsula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to luxury hotels, there are many accommodation options in Eastport Peninsula to suit every budget. Some top choices include:

  • Campbell House: This charming bed and breakfast in Terra Nova National Park is surrounded by natural beauty and is a great spot to relax and unwind.
  • Eastport Beach Cottages: Located right on the beach, these cottages offer stunning ocean views and a peaceful retreat.
  • Governor’s Pub & Inn: This historic inn offers comfortable accommodations, delicious food, and live music on weekends.

What to Buy in Eastport Peninsula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs and local products to bring home from Eastport Peninsula include:

  • Local jams and preserves: Made with locally grown berries and fruits, these jams and preserves are a delicious reminder of your trip.
  • Handmade crafts: From pottery to woven baskets, there are many local crafts to choose from that make great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Newfoundland chocolate: Made with local ingredients, such as blueberries and sea salt, Newfoundland chocolate is a unique and tasty treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastport Peninsula

Eastport Peninsula has a rich culture steeped in tradition and history. Visitors can experience this through the local arts and crafts, traditional music, and storytelling. It’s also worth noting that the area has a strong connection to the fishing industry, and many locals still make their living from the sea.

Best Time to Visit Eastport Peninsula

The best time to visit Eastport Peninsula is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the area is alive with activity. The fall months are also a great time to visit, as the foliage turns to vibrant hues of orange and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastport Peninsula

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty by exploring the area’s hiking and biking trails.
  • Try the local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local traditions or customs.
  • Leave valuables unattended in your vehicle or accommodations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastport Peninsula

Eastport Peninsula is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it is important to be aware of the natural hazards of the area, including ocean currents and wildlife. Visitors should also take precautions against ticks, which can be present in wooded areas.

Health in Eastport Peninsula

There are no major health concerns in Eastport Peninsula, but it’s always a good idea to pack a basic first-aid kit and to be prepared for any minor illnesses or injuries.

Getting to Eastport Peninsula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastport Peninsula is accessible by car or bus, and there are several airports within driving distance. Some transportation options to consider include:

Airports Near Eastport Peninsula

  • St. John’s International Airport: Located about 3 hours from Eastport Peninsula, this is the closest major airport.
  • Gander International Airport: Located about 2 hours from Eastport Peninsula, this airport offers flights to several Canadian cities.

Bus Stations Near Eastport Peninsula

  • DRL Bus: This bus company provides service from St. John’s to Terra Nova National Park and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the area at your own pace.
  • Taxi service: Taxis are available in some areas of Eastport Peninsula for short trips.

How to Get Around in Eastport Peninsula

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Eastport Peninsula, as public transportation is limited. Biking and walking are also popular options for exploring some areas, such as the Skerwink Trail and Sandy Cove Beach.

Where to Go Next from Eastport Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your East Coast adventure, consider these nearby destinations:

  • St. John’s: The capital of Newfoundland is a vibrant, historic city with plenty of attractions and cultural events.
  • Gros Morne National Park: This stunning park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is known for its fjords, mountains, and unique geology.

Conclusion

Eastport Peninsula is a hidden gem on the East Coast of Canada, offering visitors a perfect combination of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re hiking the Skerwink Trail, trying local seafood and craft beer, or simply relaxing on the beach, Eastport is a destination you won’t soon forget.
