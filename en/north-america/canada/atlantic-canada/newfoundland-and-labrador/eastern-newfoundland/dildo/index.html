Introduction to Dildo

Located in Eastern Newfoundland, Dildo is a picturesque town in Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. Nestled on the shores of Trinity Bay, Dildo boasts stunning views of the ocean and offers a unique experience to visitors.

This charming town is popular among outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone who enjoys a relaxing vacation. With a population of just over 1,200, Dildo is a quiet and peaceful place that is perfect for a weekend getaway or a longer holiday.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and more in Dildo, Newfoundland and Labrador.

List of Places to Visit in Dildo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dildo has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the past.

  • Dildo Area Interpretation Centre: Located on Dildo’s waterfront, the Dildo Area Interpretation Centre is a great place to start your visit. This centre provides an overview of the town’s history and culture and features interactive exhibits.
  • South Dildo Cemetery: The South Dildo Cemetery is one of the oldest cemeteries in Newfoundland and Labrador, and its headstones offer a glimpse into the town’s past.
  • Dildo Island: A short boat ride from the mainland, Dildo Island is a beautiful and secluded spot that is perfect for a picnic or a hike.
  • Georgetown Historic District: A short drive from Dildo, Georgetown is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features well-preserved historic buildings from the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Dildo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dildo is home to several natural attractions that offer breathtaking views and opportunities for outdoor activities.

  • Dildo Run Provincial Park: This park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful views of Dildo Bay.
  • Deep Cove Lookout: A scenic lookout that offers panoramic views of the ocean and the town.
  • The Old Man in the Mountain: A rock formation that resembles an old man’s face, this is a popular spot for hiking and enjoying the scenery.
  • Whale watching tours: Dildo is a popular spot for whale watching, and several local companies offer tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dildo

Dildo offers several hiking and biking routes that provide an opportunity to explore the town’s beautiful natural scenery.

  • Dildo Run Provincial Park Trails: This park has several hiking trails that range from easy to difficult and provide stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.
  • The T’Railway: This former railway line has been converted into a multi-use trail that is perfect for biking and hiking.
  • The Old Man in the Mountain Trail: This hiking trail leads to the Old Man in the Mountain rock formation and offers beautiful views of Dildo Bay.

Best Things to Do in Dildo: Activities and Tours

Dildo offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Dildo:

  • Whale Watching Tours: These tours provide an opportunity to see humpback and minke whales up close.
  • Zodiac Tours: Zodiac tours are a unique way to explore the coastline and see wildlife.
  • Kayaking: Dildo Bay is a popular spot for kayaking, and several local companies offer rental services.
  • Fishing Tours: Several local companies offer fishing tours that provide an opportunity to catch cod, mackerel, and other fish species.
  • Golfing: The South River Golf Course is a beautiful and challenging course that is popular among golf enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dildo

For those who want to explore Dildo and the surrounding area in depth, here is a week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Dildo Area Interpretation Centre and learn about the town’s history and culture. Take a stroll along the waterfront and enjoy the views.
  • Day 2: Take a whale watching tour and see humpback and minke whales up close. In the afternoon, visit Dildo Run Provincial Park and hike one of the park’s trails.
  • Day 3: Visit Georgetown and explore its well-preserved historic district. Stop for lunch at one of the town’s cafes or restaurants.
  • Day 4: Rent a kayak and explore Dildo Bay. In the afternoon, visit Deep Cove Lookout and enjoy the beautiful views.
  • Day 5: Take a fishing tour and try to catch cod, mackerel, or other fish species. In the evening, have dinner at one of the town’s seafood restaurants.
  • Day 6: Play a round of golf at the South River Golf Course. In the afternoon, hike the Old Man in the Mountain Trail and see the rock formation up close.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Trinity and explore its historic district. Stop for lunch at one of the town’s restaurants. In the evening, have a farewell dinner in Dildo.

Food and Drink Scene of Dildo

Dildo has a vibrant food and drink scene that features fresh seafood and traditional Newfoundland and Labrador cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cod Tongues: These deep-fried cod tongues are a Newfoundland and Labrador delicacy.
  • Jiggs Dinner: This traditional Newfoundland and Labrador meal consists of boiled salt beef, vegetables, and dumplings.
  • Fish and Chips: Dildo’s seafood restaurants serve some of the best fish and chips in the province.
  • Bakeapple Pie: This sweet pie is made with bakeapples, a local berry that grows in the boggy areas of the province.

Local Beverages

  • Iceberg Beer: This beer is brewed using icebergs that float off the coast of Newfoundland and Labrador.
  • Newfoundland Screech: This rum is a Newfoundland and Labrador icon and is often served as a shot.
  • Moosehead Beer: This Canadian beer is brewed in nearby Saint John’s, Newfoundland and Labrador.

What to Buy in Dildo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Dildo

Newfoundland and Labrador has a unique culture and heritage, and Dildo is no exception. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Dildo:

  • Friendliness: Newfoundland and Labrador is known for its friendly people, and Dildo is no exception. Don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you.
  • Mummering: Mummering is a tradition in Newfoundland and Labrador where people dress up in disguises and visit their neighbours during the Christmas season. While it is not an everyday occurrence in Dildo, it is a cultural tradition in the province.
  • Language: Newfoundland and Labrador has a distinctive accent and vocabulary. Some common Newfoundland and Labrador phrases include “Yes b’y” (yes, boy) and “What’s dis?” (what is this?).

Best Time to Visit Dildo

The best time to visit Dildo is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Dildo is a year-round destination, and each season has its own charms and attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dildo

Dildo is a safe town, but it is always important to remain vigilant and aware of your surroundings when travelling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind when visiting Dildo:

  • Be aware of wildlife: Newfoundland and Labrador is home to wildlife such as moose and bears. Be cautious when hiking or walking in areas where wildlife may be present.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear: Dildo has a rugged coastline and uneven terrain, so it is important to wear appropriate clothing and footwear when exploring the town’s outdoor attractions.
  • Practice water safety: Dildo Bay can be subject to strong tides and currents, so it is important to practice water safety when swimming or boating.

Health in Dildo

Dildo has a small medical clinic that provides basic health services. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel health insurance coverage.

Getting to Dildo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dildo does not have a bus station, but there are several bus companies that operate in the area, including DRL Coachlines and Newhook’s Transportation.

How to Get Around in Dildo

Dildo is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, it is also possible to rent a bike or car to explore the town and its surrounding area.
