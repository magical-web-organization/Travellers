Introduction to Great Northern Peninsula

Located in Western Newfoundland, Great Northern Peninsula is a scenic and culturally rich region in Atlantic Canada. With a vast coastline dotted with quaint fishing villages, dramatic cliffs, and pristine beaches, this region is a paradise for nature lovers. The Great Northern Peninsula is also home to a rich cultural heritage, with a history that dates back thousands of years. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing vacation, Great Northern Peninsula has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Great Northern Peninsula.

List of Places to Visit in Great Northern Peninsula

Here are the top places to visit in Great Northern Peninsula:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. L’Anse aux Meadows National Historic Site: Step back in time to this Viking settlement, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Learn about Viking history and explore the reconstructed sod buildings.
  2. Grenfell Historic Properties: Visit the historic home and medical station of Sir Wilfred Grenfell, a British medical missionary who transformed the health and well-being of Newfoundland and Labrador.
  3. Port au Choix National Historic Site: Discover 5,000 years of history in this archaeological site, featuring the remains of four distinct ancient cultures.

Natural Attractions of Great Northern Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Gros Morne National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site is known for its spectacular landscapes, including fjords, mountains, and waterfalls. Go hiking, fishing, or kayaking while taking in the breathtaking natural beauty.
  2. Lobster Cove Head Lighthouse: A picturesque lighthouse on the coastline, offering panoramic views that showcase the natural beauty of the region.
  3. St. Anthony Whale Watching Tours: Experience the thrill of spotting the magnificent whales in their natural habitat as they swim along the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Northern Peninsula

  1. Long Range Traverse: Take a multi-day backpacking trip through the spectacular Long Range Mountains.
  2. Tablelands Trail: A relatively easy hike that takes you through the unique geology of the Tablelands, where the Earth’s mantle is exposed.
  3. Marble Mountain Trail: This beautiful trail offers stunning views of Marble Mountain, the Steady Brook Falls, and the Humber Valley.

Best Things to Do in Great Northern Peninsula: Activities and Tours

  1. Zodiac boat tour: Take a Zodiac boat tour to explore the majestic coastlines, tiny coves, and scenic bays. You’ll have a good chance of spotting whales, dolphins, and other wildlife.
  2. Iceberg tours: Take a cruise to spot pristine icebergs in Twillingate, one of the best places in the world to see icebergs.
  3. Berry picking: Berries are abundant in the region, making for a fun family activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Northern Peninsula

Day 1: Explore the Viking history and reconstructed sod buildings at L’Anse aux Meadows National Historic Site.

Day 2: Visit the Grenfell Historic Properties and learn about healthcare in Newfoundland and Labrador.

Day 3: Go hiking in Gros Morne National Park.

Day 4: Take a whale watching tour in St. Anthony.

Day 5: Enjoy a Zodiac boat tour and explore the coastlines of Great Northern Peninsula.

Day 6: Take a cruise to see icebergs in Twillingate

Day 7: Learn about local culture and history at the Port au Choix National Historic Site.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Northern Peninsula

The Great Northern Peninsula has a diverse food and drink scene, offering something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Jiggs Dinner: A traditional Newfoundland dish made with salted beef, cabbage, potatoes, turnips, and carrots.
  2. Fish and Brewis: A classic dish made with salt cod, hardtack, onions, and butter.
  3. Bakeapple Pie: A dessert made with the tart and sweet bakeapple berry.

Local Beverages

  1. Iceberg Beer: Made with real iceberg water, this beer has a refreshing taste.
  2. Screech Rum: A Newfoundland staple, this rum is said to be so strong it can “screech” (i.e., make you scream) when you drink it.

Where to Stay in Great Northern Peninsula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sherwood Suites: A cozy boutique hotel located in the heart of St. Anthony, offering modern amenities and comfortable accommodations.
  2. Tuckamore Lodge: A nature lover’s paradise located just outside of town, offering rustic cabins and guided tours.
  3. Haven Inn: A charming guesthouse located in the scenic community of Rocky Harbour, offering stunning views of Gros Morne National Park.

What to Buy in Great Northern Peninsula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bakeapple Jam: Made from the wild bakeapple berries, this jam is a sweet and tangy treat.
  2. Local Artisans and Crafts: Check out the local artisans and craft stores, where you can find handmade pottery, knitwear, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Northern Peninsula

The Great Northern Peninsula has a rich cultural heritage, including Indigenous, Viking, and European influences. The region is known for its hospitality, friendliness, and strong community spirit. It’s common to hear local dialects and phrases, such as “scoff” (meal) and “jawns” (things). Residents are proud of their heritage and traditions, and visitors can experience it through the local events, festivals, and music scene.

Best Time to Visit Great Northern Peninsula

The best time to visit Great Northern Peninsula is between June and September, when the weather is warm, and the days are longer. This is also the peak tourism season, with many festivals and events happening during this time. However, if you’re looking for a more peaceful experience, consider visiting during the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) when there are fewer crowds, and the scenery is still stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Northern Peninsula

Do:

  1. Respect the locals and their customs.
  2. Explore the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  3. Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Disrespect the wildlife and their habitats.
  3. Ignore safety warnings and advisories.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Northern Peninsula

The Great Northern Peninsula is a safe destination; however, like any other place, visitors should take precautions and stay aware of their surroundings. It’s recommended to stay on marked trails, carry bear spray, and have a means of communication. If you’re traveling in winter, be aware of the freezing temperatures and potential hazards.

Health in Great Northern Peninsula

There are some health risks to be aware of if you’re visiting Great Northern Peninsula. Ticks are prevalent in some areas, so it’s important to take precautions against tick bites. The region also has a high UV index, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Great Northern Peninsula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Northern Peninsula

The closest major airports to Great Northern Peninsula are:

  1. St. John’s International Airport (YYT)
  2. Gander International Airport (YQX)

Bus Stations Near Great Northern Peninsula

There are no bus stations in Great Northern Peninsula. However, there are some bus services that operate in the surrounding areas, such as DRL Coachlines.

Other Options

Car rental and taxis are also available in the region. Keep in mind that some areas may have limited cell service, so be prepared with a paper map or GPS device.

How to Get Around in Great Northern Peninsula

The best way to get around Great Northern Peninsula is by car. There are several car rental companies in the area. Alternatively, taxis and private tour companies are available for hire. Biking is also a great option for exploring some of the region’s trails and scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Great Northern Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Twillingate: Known as the “Iceberg Capital of the World,” this community is a great place to see pristine icebergs up close.
  2. Corner Brook: A bustling city located on the west coast of Newfoundland, offering a wide range of attractions and activities.
  3. St. John’s: The capital of Newfoundland and Labrador, known for its colorful architecture, vibrant nightlife, and rich culture.

Conclusion

Great Northern Peninsula is a must-visit destination if you’re looking for a unique blend of stunning natural beauty, cultural heritage, and friendly locals. Curate your own itinerary using our comprehensive travel guide and immerse yourself in the magic of this region.
