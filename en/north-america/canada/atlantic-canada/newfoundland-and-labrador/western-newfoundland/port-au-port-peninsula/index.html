Introduction to Port au Port Peninsula

Port au Port Peninsula in Western Newfoundland is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and rich culture of Atlantic Canada. This remote region is home to picturesque fishing villages, rugged coastlines, and pristine forests, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Port au Port Peninsula

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Port au Port Heritage House showcases the history and culture of the area with interactive exhibits and guided tours.
  • Our Lady of Mercy Museum is a restored Roman Catholic church that now functions as a museum highlighting the influence of religion on the region.

Natural Attractions of Port au Port Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape St. George Lighthouse offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding wilderness and is a great spot for whale watching.
  • Southwest Coast Trail is a scenic hiking trail that runs through the wilderness of the peninsula, offering breathtaking views of the coastline and forests.
  • Gros Morne National Park offers over 1,800 km² of rugged coastal terrain, including fjords, waterfalls and distinctive rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port au Port Peninsula

  • Leif Erikson Trail is a 7 km hiking trail that offers stunning views of the coastline and the Gulf of St. Lawrence.
  • The Copper Mine is another popular hiking route, which leads to abandoned copper mines in the region.

Best Things to Do in Port au Port Peninsula: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing through the bays and coves of Port au Port Peninsula.
  • Boat Tours are a popular way to explore the rugged coastlines of the area.
  • Whale Watching in spring and summer, when the humpback whales are in their migration routes.
  • Snowshoeing and Cross-country skiing in winter time through the forests and panoramas.

Food and Drink Scene of Port au Port Peninsula

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiggs Dinner is a traditional Newfoundland dish that consists of salt beef, potatoes, vegetables, and a boiled pudding.
  • Fish and Chips made from freshly caught fish from the local waters.
  • Screech is a local rum or whiskey with a high alcohol content that is popular among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • Moosehead beer is a locally brewed beer that is popular in the province.
  • Iceberg Vodka is a premium vodka made with water from icebergs that float off the coast of Newfoundland and Labrador.

Where to Stay in Port au Port Peninsula: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Port au Port is located in the heart of the peninsula and offers comfortable rooms with stunning views of the ocean.
  • White Bear Inn is a cozy and inviting guesthouse that offers guests a chance to experience the warmth and hospitality of local families.

What to Buy in Port au Port Peninsula: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Knitted Sweaters made from locally sourced wool are a perfect souvenir that represents the local culture.
  • Wild Blueberry Jam is a local delicacy and is an excellent gift for food lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Port au Port Peninsula

The region is influenced by Irish, Scottish, and French culture, which is reflected in the traditions, music, and arts of the area. Visitors can experience this rich heritage through festivals, music, and local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Port au Port Peninsula

The best time to visit Port au Port Peninsula is from May to September when the weather is mild, and the region is bustling with activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port au Port Peninsula

Do:

  • Respect the local culture, traditions, and customs.
  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty and scenic views of the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.
  • Ignore safety warnings while engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port au Port Peninsula

  • Be aware of weather conditions and plan accordingly.
  • Follow safety guidelines while engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and boating.
  • Take precautions against insect bites, particularly in the summer months.

Health in Port au Port Peninsula

  • Visitors should be vigilant about food and water hygiene to avoid foodborne illnesses.
  • The nearest hospital is located in the town of Stephenville.

Getting to Port au Port Peninsula: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port au Port Peninsula

  • Stephenville Airport is the nearest airport, located around 42 km from the region.

Bus Stations Near Port au Port Peninsula

  • There is no bus service to Port au Port Peninsula.

Other Options

  • Visitors can rent a car to explore the region independently.

How to Get Around in Port au Port Peninsula

The best way to get around Port au Port Peninsula is by car, as it gives you the freedom to explore the region at your own pace. Bike rentals and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Port au Port Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Corner Brook is a coastal city located around 150 km from Port au Port Peninsula, known for its picturesque landscapes and outdoor activities.
  • L’Anse aux Meadows National Historic Site is a UNESCO World Heritage site located around 500 km away from Port au Port Peninsula that showcases the Viking settlement that dates back to the 11th century.

Conclusion

Port au Port Peninsula is a charming and inviting destination that offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty, rich culture, and hospitable people of western Newfoundland. With its picturesque landscapes, outdoor activities, and local delicacies, it is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic Canadian experience.
