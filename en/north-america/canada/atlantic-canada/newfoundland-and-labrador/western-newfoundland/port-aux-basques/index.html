Introduction to Port aux Basques

Port aux Basques is a vibrant town located in Western Newfoundland, Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. It is the main gateway to the island of Newfoundland and a popular destination for tourists visiting the area. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture, there is something for everyone in Port aux Basques.

List of Places to Visit in Port aux Basques

As you plan your trip to Port aux Basques, be sure to include the following places on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Port aux Basques Heritage Museum: This museum is housed in a former courthouse and jail and offers visitors a glimpse into the history of the town and the region.

  • Rose Blanche Lighthouse: This lighthouse is one of the oldest in Newfoundland and Labrador and has been designated a National Historic Site of Canada.

  • Grand Bay West Beach: This beach is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike, offering stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Port aux Basques: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Ray Lighthouse: This lighthouse is a must-see for anyone visiting Port aux Basques and offers panoramic views of the coastline and ocean.

  • Barachois Pond Provincial Park: This park is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike and offers a range of activities, including hiking, fishing, and swimming.

  • The Newfoundland Trailway: This trailway spans over 900km and offers visitors the opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Newfoundland and Labrador.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port aux Basques

Port aux Basques is a hiker and biker’s paradise with a variety of routes to suit all skill levels. Some popular trails include:

  • The Grand Bay West Trail: This trail offers easy hiking and stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

  • The Codroy Valley International Wetlands Trail: This trail spans over 90km and is a great spot for bird watching and wildlife viewing.

  • The Port aux Basques Harbour Authority Walkway: This walkway is a popular spot for walkers and runners and offers stunning views of the harbour and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Port aux Basques: Activities and Tours

  • Take a whale watching tour: Visitors can enjoy a boat tour and get up close and personal with whales, dolphins, and other marine wildlife.

  • Visit the Port aux Basques Farmers’ Market: This market is a must-visit for foodies, offering a wide range of locally-made products and fresh produce.

  • Explore the town on foot: Port aux Basques is a walkable town with stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Port aux Basques

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiggs Dinner: A traditional Newfoundland and Labrador dish consisting of salt beef, cabbage, turnips, potatoes, and peas pudding.

  • Fish and Brewis: A classic Newfoundland and Labrador dish consisting of salt cod and hard bread.

  • Moose Meat: A game meat that is a popular dish in Newfoundland and Labrador.

Local Beverages

  • Screech Rum: A rum that is a Newfoundland and Labrador favorite.

  • Iceberg Beer: A beer that is made with water from icebergs and is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Port aux Basques: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Port aux Basques has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Port aux Basques: A modern hotel located in the heart of the town.

  • St. Christopher’s Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the town center.

  • The Inn at the Cape: A cozy guesthouse located near the Cape Ray Lighthouse.

What to Buy in Port aux Basques: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Knitwear: Newfoundland and Labrador is famous for its locally-made knitwear, including sweaters, hats, and mittens.

  • Local Artwork: There are many talented artists in the area who create beautiful paintings, sculptures, and other works of art.

Local Culture and Customs of Port aux Basques

Port aux Basques has a strong cultural heritage, shaped by its history and unique location. Some customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Mummering: A traditional Newfoundland and Labrador custom where residents dress up in costumes and go door-to-door singing and dancing.

  • Kitchen Parties: A popular social gathering where friends and family come together to play music, dance, and enjoy each other’s company.

Best Time to Visit Port aux Basques

The best time to visit Port aux Basques is in the summer months (June to September), when the weather is mild and there are lots of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port aux Basques

Port aux Basques is generally a safe town, but it’s important to take normal precautions when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, keep your valuables secure, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Port aux Basques

The town has a hospital and medical clinic, but it’s important to have travel insurance in case of unexpected medical emergencies. Be sure to pack any medications you need and speak with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Port aux Basques: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Port aux Basques is in Stephenville, Newfoundland and Labrador, which is about 130km away.

Bus Stations Near Port aux Basques

Other Options

Ferry services are available from North Sydney, Nova Scotia to Port aux Basques, Newfoundland and Labrador.

How to Get Around in Port aux Basques

Port aux Basques is a small town and is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.
