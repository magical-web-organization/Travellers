Introduction to Gros Morne National Park

Gros Morne National Park, located in Western Newfoundland, Canada, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its natural beauty and unique geology. This stunning park spans over 1,800 square kilometers and features vast mountain ranges, idyllic fjords, and incredible marine life. Apart from its breathtaking scenery, Gros Morne National Park also boasts a rich cultural heritage that includes the Maritime Archaic, Paleo-Eskimo, and Recent Indigenous peoples.

List of Places to Visit in Gros Morne National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Discovery Centre: The perfect place to begin your visit to Gros Morne National Park, this museum showcases the area’s unique geology and cultural heritage.
  • L’Anse aux Meadows National Historic Site: Located just north of the park, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to Viking ruins that date back more than a thousand years.
  • Gros Morne Heritage Museum: Explore the story of Newfoundland and Labrador through fascinating exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Green Point Geological Site: Discover the park’s astonishing geology at this scenic coastal location.

Natural Attractions of Gros Morne National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Western Brook Pond: This 16-kilometer fjord lake is one of the park’s most iconic landmarks and is best explored by boat tour.
  • Gros Morne Mountain: The second-highest mountain in Newfoundland and Labrador offers incredible panoramic views of the surrounding mountain landscape.
  • Tablelands: This unique mountain range is composed of peridotite rock, a rare geological formation that is only found in a few locations around the world.
  • Sandy Cove Beach: Relax on this serene sandy beach overlooking the Gulf of St. Lawrence.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gros Morne National Park

  • The Long Range Traverse: A challenging 35-kilometer backcountry hike across Gros Morne Mountain’s rugged terrain.
  • Green Gardens Trail: A moderate 9-kilometer hike that leads to a secluded beach in the park’s southern headland.
  • Berry Hill: A 4-kilometer trail through bog and taiga that provides a stunning view of Bonne Bay.
  • Bicycle Tour of the Park: Experience Gros Morne’s stunning scenery on two wheels by following the park’s scenic coastal roads.

Best Things to Do in Gros Morne National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a scenic boat tour to explore Western Brook Pond, a picturesque fjord lake nestled deep in the Long Range Mountains.
  • Fishing: Gros Morne National Park offers excellent fishing opportunities, including salmon, trout, and Arctic char.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the park’s coastal waters and pristine lakes by kayak or canoe.
  • Wildlife Watching: Keep your eyes peeled for moose, black bears, and caribou, among many other species of wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gros Morne National Park

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Gros Morne National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiggs Dinner: A traditional Newfoundland meal that features salt beef, cabbage, turnip, carrot, potato, and pease pudding.
  • Fish and Brewis: A dish made of salt cod and hardtack crackers boiled with potatoes and onions.
  • Moose Stew: A hearty stew made with tender moose meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Screech Rum: A Newfoundland favorite, this rum is traditionally used for the iconic Screech-In ceremony, where non-locals are welcomed to the island.
  • Iceberg Beer: This light and refreshing beer is brewed using water from ancient icebergs that float off the coast of Newfoundland.

Where to Stay in Gros Morne National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gros Morne Cabins: These cozy log cabins are the perfect retreat for those looking for a peaceful escape.
  • Rocky Harbour Motel: Located in the heart of Rocky Harbour, this motel offers clean and comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.
  • Gros Morne Hostel: Backpackers will love this budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable dorms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Gros Morne National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Birch Bark Canoe: A hand-crafted miniature replica of a traditional Newfoundland birch bark canoe.
  • Bottle of Screech Rum: Take home a bottle of Newfoundland’s favorite rum to remember your visit.
  • Moose Antler Jewelry: Unique jewelry made from local moose antlers.

Local Culture and Customs of Gros Morne National Park

Newfoundland and Labrador have a rich cultural heritage that is evident throughout Gros Morne National Park. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly locals, traditional music, delicious cuisine, and a strong sense of community. It is important to respect the locals and their traditions, but don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with those you meet during your travels.

Best Time to Visit Gros Morne National Park

The best time to visit Gros Morne National Park is during the warmer months between June and September. This is when the park’s trails are the most accessible, and the temperatures are comfortable for outdoor activities. However, if you’re looking to experience the park’s winter wonderland, then plan a trip between December and February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gros Morne National Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife by leaving no trace and following park rules.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for the activities you plan to do.
  • Take your time and enjoy the scenery.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Damage or remove natural features or artifacts.
  • Smoke, light fires, or dispose of waste in undesignated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gros Morne National Park

Gros Morne National Park is generally a safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution and use common sense when exploring the wilderness. Be aware of weather conditions and dress appropriately, carry a map and a compass, and inform someone of your itinerary before heading out on a hike.

Health in Gros Morne National Park

The park has only limited medical facilities, so it’s essential to bring necessary medication and first-aid supplies. Those with pre-existing medical conditions should consult their doctor before traveling to Gros Morne National Park.

Getting to Gros Morne National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gros Morne National Park

The closest airport to Gros Morne National Park is Deer Lake Regional Airport, located about 50 kilometers southeast of the park. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to the park.

Bus Stations Near Gros Morne National Park

Bus service to Gros Morne National Park is limited, with most visitors arriving by car or shuttle service.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the park by ferry from mainland Canada or other parts of Newfoundland and Labrador.

How to Get Around in Gros Morne National Park

A personal vehicle is the most convenient way to get around Gros Morne National Park. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or utilize shuttle services to explore the park.

Where to Go Next from Gros Morne National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Terra Nova National Park: This beautiful national park is located on the eastern side of Newfoundland and offers visitors excellent hiking, fishing, and kayaking opportunities.
  • St. John’s, Newfoundland: A vibrant city with a fascinating history, St. John’s is the perfect place to explore the local culture, vibrant nightlife, and delicious cuisine.
  • Fogo Island: A remote island located off the coast of Newfoundland, Fogo Island is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and unique arts scene.

Conclusion

Gros Morne National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and anyone looking to experience the beauty of Newfoundland and Labrador. With its stunning landscape, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor activities, Gros Morne National Park is an unforgettable experience. Plan your trip, pack your bags, and prepare for an adventure of a lifetime.
