Introduction to Wabush

Nestled in the heart of Labrador, Wabush is a small town in Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. Surrounded by stunning natural beauty, Wabush offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its rich cultural heritage, diverse wildlife, and a plethora of hiking trails, Wabush has something for everyone.

In this ultimate guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Wabush

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wabush has a deep history, and you can explore it at the Aurora Labrador Historical Society museum. The museum features several exhibits about the town’s industrial and mining past.

Natural Attractions of Wabush: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wabush is a nature lover’s paradise. The town is situated close to the pristine Lake Wabush, which provides ample opportunities for water sports like canoeing, kayaking, and fishing. You can also go hiking in the nearby Wabush Lake mountains and take in the breathtaking views of the town from the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wabush

Wabush has several spectacular hiking and biking trails. The Wabush Lake Trail is a 7-kilometer trail that winds around the town and is perfect for a moderate hike. The Wabush Mountain Trail offers a more challenging ascent and rewards with stunning views of Lake Wabush from the peak.

Best Things to Do in Wabush: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and water sports, Wabush offers several exciting activities. You can take a guided snowmobile tour, go wildlife watching for caribou, moose, and black bears, or book a scenic flight over the town and surrounding areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wabush

Day 1: Aurora Labrador Historical Society Museum

Day 2: Hiking on Wabush Lake Trail

Day 3: Kayaking/Fishing on Lake Wabush

Day 4: Wildlife Watching Tour

Day 5: Scenic Flight over the town

Day 7: Relax and unwind at Mineral Spa

Food and Drink Scene of Wabush

Must-Try Dishes

Wabush is known for its delicious seafood, especially the fresh salmon and cod caught in the nearby waters. You should also try the traditional Labrador tea, a herbal tea made from the leaves of the rhododendron groenlandicum plant, which is a specialty of the region.

Local Beverages

Labrador’s famous spirits are Birch Beer and Cloudberry Wine, which you can enjoy at the local bars and restaurants. For a non-alcoholic option, try the locally brewed apple cider, made from handpicked Labrador crab apples.

Where to Stay in Wabush: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wabush has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the popular choices include Hotel Wabush, Wabush Hotel and Suites, and North Star guesthouse.

What to Buy in Wabush: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can bring home some unique souvenirs from Wabush, such as Labradorite jewelry, handmade traditional Inuit crafts, and maple syrup products. You can also get your hands on some locally caught salmon and cod, or buy some of the region’s famous spirits as a gift for loved ones back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Wabush

Labrador’s indigenous Innu culture has a strong presence in Wabush. You can learn about their traditional way of life, language, and customs by visiting the Innu Tea House, or by attending one of their cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Wabush

The best time to visit Wabush is during the warmer months of May to October, when the temperatures are mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, if you’re a fan of winter sports and adventure, you can visit during the colder months and try out some activities like snowmobiling and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wabush

Do:

  • Respect the region’s indigenous culture and customs.
  • Be cautious when hiking in the wilderness and stick to designated trails.
  • Follow the local fishing regulations.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Ignore the weather warnings, especially during the winter months.
  • Take any souvenirs that are prohibited.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wabush

Wabush is a safe town, but as with any other place, you should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities and not venture into the wilderness or unfamiliar areas alone.

Health in Wabush

Wabush has a hospital and several medical clinics where you can receive medical attention if required. You should also carry any necessary medication and insect repellent if visiting during the summer months.

Getting to Wabush: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wabush

Wabush has a regional airport, Wabush Airport, which is served by several commercial airlines.

Bus Stations Near Wabush

Wabush is connected by bus, and you can take a bus from nearby towns and cities like Labrador City and Corner Brook.

Other Options

You can also drive or rent a car to get to Wabush, and several car rental agencies operate in the town.

How to Get Around in Wabush

The town is small, and you can easily navigate it on foot or by renting a car. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Wabush: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Newfoundland and Labrador, you can head to other towns like Labrador City, Happy Valley-Goose Bay, and Corner Brook. You can also visit the stunning Gros Morne National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Conclusion

Wabush is a hidden gem in the heart of Newfoundland and Labrador, and offers a perfect balance of cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and scenic beauty. Plan your trip to Wabush using our ultimate guide, and experience the best that this unique town has to offer.
