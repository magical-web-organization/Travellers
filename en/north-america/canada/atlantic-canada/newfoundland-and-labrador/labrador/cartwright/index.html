Located in the beautiful province of Newfoundland and Labrador, Cartwright is an idyllic fishing town that offers a wide range of attractions for all kinds of travelers. From picturesque vistas to adrenaline-pumping outdoor adventures, Cartwright has something special for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Cartwright.

Introduction to Cartwright

Cartwright is a town located on the southern coast of Labrador, Newfoundland and Labrador, in Atlantic Canada. It is named after British explorer John Cartwright and is home to a population of around 500 people. Once a bustling settlement for hunting and fishing, Cartwright now attracts tourists from around the world with its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Cartwright

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cartwright Heritage Centre - This museum houses a fascinating collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents from Cartwright’s history. It’s a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in the town’s past and culture.
  • St. George’s Anglican Church - Built in the early 20th century, this church is a fine example of Gothic Revival architecture. Its stained-glass windows and intricate woodwork make it a beautiful attraction to explore.
  • Battle Harbour Historic Village - Take a ferry ride to this nearby island, which was once a thriving fishing community. Now a preserved historic village, Battle Harbour offers visitors a glimpse into the life of early settlers in Cartwright.

Natural Attractions of Cartwright: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coastal Trail - This scenic trail offers magnificent views of the ocean, mountains, and forests. It’s perfect for hiking, biking, or simply taking a leisurely stroll while taking in the surrounding beauty.
  • Sandbanks Provincial Park - This picturesque park is situated on a sandy beach that stretches for miles. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking in the park’s tranquil surroundings.
  • Pinware River Provincial Park - Spread over 12,000 hectares, this park is a sanctuary for wildlife. Visitors can go hiking, fishing, boating, or birdwatching in this unspoiled wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cartwright

  • Cartwright Bay Trail - This easy trail passes through forests and meadows before descending to the beautiful Cartwright Bay. The trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a quick bike ride.
  • Coastal Trail - As mentioned earlier, this trail offers stunning views of the ocean and is an excellent hiking or biking route.
  • Tuckamore Wilderness Trail - This challenging trail winds its way through the dense forest and requires a bit of experience and stamina.

Best Things to Do in Cartwright: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tours - Take a boat ride to see humpback, minke, and fin whales playfully swimming in the ocean.
  • Fishing Tours - Experience the traditional way of fishing with a guided tour that takes you to the best spots for trout, salmon, and cod fishing.
  • Kayaking Tours - Explore the hidden coves and scenic bays around Cartwright on a guided kayaking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cartwright

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Cartwright and check into a hotel or guesthouse. Spend the rest of the day exploring the town.
2 Visit the Cartwright Heritage Centre and learn about the town’s history and culture.
3 Take a day trip to the Battle Harbour Historic Village and experience life as an early settler.
4 Spend the day hiking or biking along the Coastal Trail.
5 Take a guided fishing tour and try your luck at catching cod or salmon.
6 Go kayaking and explore the hidden coves and bays around Cartwright.
7 Spend the last day relaxing on the beach or taking a leisurely stroll around the town before departing from Cartwright.

Food and Drink Scene of Cartwright

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips - Freshly caught cod served with fries and tartar sauce is a local favorite.
  • Baked Goods - Try the traditional Newfoundland Toutons, a type of fried doughnut served with molasses.
  • Seafood Chowder - Made with a variety of seafood, vegetables, and cream, this hearty soup is a must-try in Cartwright.

Local Beverages

  • Iceberg Beer - Brewed with water harvested from icebergs, this beer has a unique taste that’s worth a try.
  • Partridgeberry Wine - Made from a local berry, partridgeberry wine is a popular local drink that’s sweet yet refreshing.

Where to Stay in Cartwright: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cartwright Hotel - This cozy hotel offers comfortable rooms and a friendly staff. It’s the perfect base for exploring the town and its surroundings.
  • Cartwright Inn - This guesthouse offers private rooms with beautiful views of the coastal landscape. It’s an excellent choice for travelers looking for a more personalized experience.
  • Cartwright Hostel - This affordable option is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers. It offers dormitory-style rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Cartwright: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Jewelry - Local artisans create beautiful silver jewelry in traditional designs.
  • Labradorite Products - This unique stone is found only in Labrador and is used to create a variety of souvenirs such as necklaces, keychains, and earrings.
  • Seal Skin Products - Seal skin mittens, hats, and boots are a popular souvenir among visitors to Cartwright.

Local Culture and Customs of Cartwright

Cartwright has a rich cultural heritage that’s influenced by its unique location and history. The locals are friendly and value tradition, and visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and beliefs. English is the primary language in Cartwright.

Best Time to Visit Cartwright

The best time to visit Cartwright is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s off-season charm during the fall and winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cartwright

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Try the local seafood and delicacies.
  • Be mindful of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Litter or disturb the natural surroundings.
  • Use drones or other equipment that may disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cartwright

Cartwright is a safe place for visitors, but it’s always prudent to take standard precautions, such as locking your doors and keeping your valuables with you while out exploring.

Health in Cartwright

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Cartwright. It’s also a good idea to bring mosquito repellent as mosquito-borne illnesses like West Nile Virus can be a risk in the area.

Getting to Cartwright: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cartwright

  • Cartwright Airport - A small regional airport that services Cartwright and its surroundings.

Bus Stations Near Cartwright

There are no bus services directly to Cartwright. However, visitors can take a bus to nearby towns such as Goose Bay, and then either take a local taxi or rent a car to travel to Cartwright.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get to Cartwright, but visitors can also take a ferry ride or a chartered boat to reach the town.

How to Get Around in Cartwright

Walking or biking around town is the most convenient way to get around in Cartwright. Taxis and car rentals are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Cartwright: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Red Bay - This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a historically significant whaling village that’s located just across the border in Labrador.
  • St. John’s - Newfoundland’s capital city, St. John’s, is one of the oldest cities in North America and offers visitors a perfect mix of history and modern attractions.
  • Gros Morne National Park - This stunning park features towering mountains, roaring waterfalls, and pristine lakes. It’s a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Conclusion

Cartwright is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be discovered by travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality, it’s an excellent destination for anyone looking to experience Newfoundland and Labrador’s unique culture. We hope this guide helps you plan your perfect Cartwright vacation.
