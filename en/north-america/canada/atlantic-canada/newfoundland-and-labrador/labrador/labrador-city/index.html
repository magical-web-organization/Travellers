Introduction to Labrador City

Located in the province of Newfoundland and Labrador, in Atlantic Canada, Labrador City is a small remote town that often gets overlooked by many tourists. However, this hidden gem is a great travel destination for those who appreciate natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich culture.

Labrador City is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, outdoor recreational activities, and friendly locals. It is also a prime location for mining and fishing activities, which contribute to its fascinating history and industrial character.

Whether you’re a nature lover, culture enthusiast, or thrill-seeker, there’s something for you in Labrador City. This comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, and experiences to have in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Labrador City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Labrador West Mining and Geological Interpretation Centre

The Labrador West Mining and Geological Interpretation Centre is an educational museum that showcases the history of mining in Labrador. The museum features various interactive exhibits that explore the geological and cultural significance of mining in the region. Here, you can learn about the extraction of iron ore and its impact on the local economy.

Wabush Mines

Wabush Mines is a significant Canadian historic mining site that currently offers guided tours and exhibits. Located in Wabush, Labrador, this historic mine explains the life and work of miners, and the role this site played in the region’s development.

Gateway Labrador

Gateway Labrador is a unique cultural centre that is dedicated to showcasing the indigenous culture of Labrador. The museum features native arts and crafts, music, and cuisine.

Natural Attractions of Labrador City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Menihek Nordic Ski Club

The Menihek Nordic Ski Club offers over 30 trails for cross-country skiing and snowshoeing. The trails range from easy to advanced levels, making it suitable for skiers of all abilities. In the summer months, the area is used for hiking and mountain biking, with several trails available.

Smokey Mountain

Smokey mountain is a popular hiking destination, providing visitors with scenic views of the surrounding area. It is a moderate to difficult hike because of its steep terrain.

Jean Lake

Jean Lake is a serene and picturesque lake set amidst natural surroundings. It is an ideal location for fishing, swimming, and kayaking, especially in the summer months. The lake has several rental cabins available for visitors to stay in.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Labrador City

Menihek Nordic Ski Club Trails

The Menihek Nordic Ski Club has over 30 trails suitable for hiking and biking in the summer months. These trails range in distance and difficulty, providing options for every level of hiker and biker.

Smokey Mountain Trail

The Smokey Mountain trail is a 5 km hiking and mountain biking trail located close to Labrador City. The trail rises more than 260 meters in height, providing excellent scenic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Labrador City: Activities and Tours

Snowmobiling

The rugged terrain surrounding Labrador City makes it an ideal location for snowmobiling. Several local tour companies offer guided snowmobile tours, giving visitors a chance to experience the beauty of the area in the winter months.

Fishing

With several lakes, rivers, and streams nearby, fishing is a popular activity among tourists in Labrador City. Trout, salmon, and arctic char are the most common fish found in the area. Many tour companies offer guided fishing trips for visitors.

Kayaking

Jean Lake is the perfect location for kayaking enthusiasts, providing a peaceful and serene setting for paddling. Several local tour companies offer kayaking trips and rentals of kayaks.

Food and Drink Scene of Labrador City

Must-Try Dishes

Jiggs Dinner

Jiggs dinner is a traditional Newfoundland and Labrador meal consisting of boiled meats, potatoes, carrots, turnips, peas, and cabbage.

Toutons

Toutons is a traditional breakfast food enjoyed in Newfoundland and Labrador. It is a type of deep-fried dough that is often topped with molasses.

Local Beverages

Screech

Screech is a type of rum that has been distilled in Newfoundland and Labrador since the 1800s. It is typically enjoyed as a shot or mixed into cocktails.

Quidi Vidi Beer

The Quidi Vidi brewery in St. John’s, Newfoundland and Labrador, produces a variety of craft beers. Their ice beer is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Labrador City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel North Two

Hotel North Two is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Labrador City. It offers guests various amenities, including an indoor pool, on-site restaurant, and fitness center.

Scenic View Motel

The Scenic View Motel is a budget-friendly option located on the outskirts of the city. It offers a variety of rooms and suites, all equipped with a private bathroom and cable TV.

Menihek Nordic Ski Club Lodge

The Menihek Nordic Ski Club Lodge is an excellent option for budget-conscious travelers. It is located within the Menihek Nordic Ski Club and offers family-style accommodations for up to 22 guests.

What to Buy in Labrador City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Labrador Tea

Labrador tea is a traditional tea made from the leaves of the Ledum groenlandicum plant common in Labrador and Northern Quebec.

Handmade Quilts

Several local artisans in Labrador City create handmade quilts using traditional patterns and techniques. These make unique, practical souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Labrador City

Labrador City is a unique blend of First Nations, Mi’kmaq and Metis cultures, and the country’s French, British, and Irish roots. The Labrador culture is essential in this region and has significantly influenced the town’s way of life.

Best Time to Visit Labrador City

June to August is the best time to visit Labrador City. During this time, the weather is favorable, and the city hosts several outdoor events and festivals. In the winter months, the temperature can drop significantly, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Labrador City

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the vast outdoor recreational opportunities available.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do visit the mining and cultural museums

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife or natural surroundings
  • Don’t forget to dress properly for the weather conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Labrador City

Labrador City is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, it is always good to exercise regular travel precautions, such as not walking alone at night or leaving valuables in cars.

Health in Labrador City

Labrador City has a modern health care system and several medical clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage when travelling.

Getting to Labrador City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Labrador City

Wabush Airport is the main airport that serves Labrador City, with daily flights to and from St.John’s and Montreal.

Bus Stations Near Labrador City

Labrador City has regular bus services that connect it to the neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

Car rentals are also available at the airport or in town for those who prefer to drive themselves while exploring Labrador City.

How to Get Around in Labrador City

There are several rental car companies in the area that visitors can use to get around. Additionally, the town’s public transportation system is reliable and affordable, servicing most of the city’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Labrador City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Churchill Falls

Churchill Falls is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Located 200 kilometers from Labrador City, it offers stunning scenery, including the second-largest waterfall in eastern Canada.

Western Brook Pond

Western Brook Pond is situated in Gros Morne National Park and offers scenic boating trips. It is approximately a five-hour drive from Labrador City.

Conclusion

Labrador City is a hidden gem in Atlantic Canada that offers visitors an abundance of outdoor recreational opportunities and rich culture. This comprehensive tour guide provides a detailed overview of the best attractions, activities, and experiences that make Labrador City a must-visit destination for anyone who loves nature, history, and adventure. Plan your trip today and discover all that Labrador City has to offer.
