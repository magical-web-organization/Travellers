Introduction to North West River

Located in the eastern part of Labrador, North West River is a small community that is home to a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a host of outdoor adventures and activities. From hiking and biking to fishing and snowmobiling, there is no shortage of things to do and see in and around North West River.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take a closer look at what makes North West River a unique and must-visit destination in Atlantic Canada.

List of Places to Visit in North West River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Labrador Heritage Museum: Located in the heart of North West River, the Labrador Heritage Museum showcases the unique history and culture of the Labrador region. Here, visitors can learn about the indigenous people of Labrador and their traditional way of life, as well as the European traders and settlers who eventually arrived in the area.

  • Mission Chapel: Dating back to the 19th century, the Mission Chapel is one of the oldest wooden chapels in North America. This tiny church is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history of Christianity in Labrador.

Natural Attractions of North West River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Melville: This massive body of water is actually an estuary, where the Churchill River meets the Atlantic Ocean. The lake is a hub for outdoor activities, including fishing, kayaking, and boating. Visitors can also catch a glimpse of the local wildlife, including bald eagles and moose, as well as the annual migration of the Atlantic salmon.

  • Ramea Island: Located off the coast of Labrador, Ramea Island is a nature lover’s paradise. Visitors can hike along the stunning coastline, watch for whales and seabirds, and explore the charming fishing villages that dot the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North West River

  • Mealy Mountains: This vast mountain range is one of the most spectacular natural attractions in the region. Visitors can embark on a multi-day hiking trip through the Mealy Mountains National Park Reserve, which offers stunning views of glaciers, lakes, and waterfalls.

  • Trans-Labrador Highway: For cyclists looking for a long-distance adventure, the Trans-Labrador Highway stretches over 1,000 kilometers across the province. The route spans rugged terrain, picturesque coastline, and charming small towns.

Best Things to Do in North West River: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tour: A whale watching tour is one of the best ways to experience the natural beauty and wildlife of Labrador. Visitors can witness humpback whales breaching and diving just off the coast, as well as spotting other marine life such as dolphins and seals.

  • Fishing Trip: With Lake Melville on its doorstep, North West River is an ideal place for fishing enthusiasts. Visitors can take a guided fishing trip to catch native species such as salmon and trout, or try their luck at ice fishing during the winter months.

Week-Long Itinerary for North West River

Day 1-2: Explore the town of North West River, including a visit to the Labrador Heritage Museum and Mission Chapel.

Day 3-4: Embark on a hiking trip through the Mealy Mountains National Park Reserve, overnighting in backcountry campsites or lodges.

Day 5-6: Take a whale watching tour or go on a fishing trip on Lake Melville, and enjoy the stunning scenery that surrounds North West River.

Day 7: Spend the day exploring the nearby Ramea Island, hiking along the scenic coastline and visiting its charming fishing villages.

Food and Drink Scene of North West River

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiggs Dinner: This traditional Newfoundland and Labrador dish features boiled vegetables, salt beef, and split pea soup, served with a dollop of savoury pudding.

Local Beverages

  • Screech: This potent rum-based liquor is a Newfoundland and Labrador favourite. Visitors can “kiss the cod” (part of a traditional Screech-In ceremony) to become an honorary Newfoundlander and receive a shot of the drink.

Where to Stay in North West River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Northern Lights Motel and Chalets: This locally-owned motel and chalet complex offers comfortable accommodations and stunning views of Lake Melville.

  • Largerow Lodges: Located just outside North West River, Largerow Lodges offers cozy cabins and chalets for a secluded getaway in the wilderness.

What to Buy in North West River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Labradorite: This unique mineral is found only in Labrador and is known for its iridescent colours. Visitors can purchase Labradorite jewelry or other souvenirs made from the stone.

Local Culture and Customs of North West River

Labrador has a rich cultural heritage that dates back thousands of years. The indigenous people of the region, including the Innu and Inuit, have a deep respect for the land and the animals that live on it. Visitors should take the time to learn about these cultures and traditions, and show respect for the natural beauty of the region.

Best Time to Visit North West River

The best time to visit North West River is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and the days are long. However, visitors should be aware that this is also the busiest time of year for tourism in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of North West River

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Learn about the indigenous cultures and traditions of the region.

Don’t:

  • Go off-trail while hiking or camping, as this can be dangerous and cause damage to the environment.

  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.

  • Ignore weather warnings or advisories.

Safety and Health

Safety in North West River

North West River is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, such as bringing appropriate gear and clothing, and following safety guidelines.

Health in North West River

Visitors to North West River should pack any necessary medication or medical supplies, as there are limited healthcare facilities in the area. It is also important to stay hydrated and protected against the sun when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to North West River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North West River

  • Goose Bay Airport (YYR)

Bus Stations Near North West River

  • There are no bus stations in North West River.

Other Options

  • Driving: North West River is accessible via the Trans-Labrador Highway.

How to Get Around in North West River

  • Walking and biking are the best options for getting around North West River. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi for longer journeys outside of the town.

Where to Go Next from North West River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Happy Valley-Goose Bay: This larger town is located just a short drive from North West River and offers a range of amenities and attractions, such as the Labrador Interpretation Centre and Terra Nova National Park.

  • Torngat Mountains National Park Reserve: This remote park is located in northern Labrador and is accessible only by boat or charter plane. Visitors can go on guided hikes, watch for polar bears, and learn about the indigenous cultures of the region.

Conclusion

Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply immersing yourself in a unique culture, North West River in Labrador is the perfect destination for your next trip. With stunning natural beauty, a rich cultural heritage, and a range of activities and attractions to suit every interest, North West River is a must-visit destination for any traveller to Atlantic Canada.
