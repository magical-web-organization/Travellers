Introduction to Forteau

Forteau is a charming town located in Labrador, Newfoundland and Labrador, Atlantic Canada. It is situated near the southern coast of Labrador, making it a fantastic destination for anyone who enjoys stunning natural scenery. Forteau is home to several historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and natural wonders, making it a popular tourist destination. This travel guide will provide visitors with a comprehensive guide to Forteau, including the best things to do, places to stay, and where to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Forteau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Forteau has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can learn more about this history by visiting several cultural landmarks and museums. Some of the most popular historical places and museums in Forteau include:

  • Point Amour Lighthouse: The Point Amour Lighthouse is a National Historic Site of Canada, and it is the tallest lighthouse in Atlantic Canada. The lighthouse provides panoramic views of the coastal landscape and is open to visitors from June to September.
  • Labrador Straits Museum: The Labrador Straits Museum is a small museum in the town of L’Anse-au-Loup, located just a short drive from Forteau. The museum showcases the history and culture of Labrador, including exhibits on traditional crafts, fishing, and hunting practices.
  • Battle Harbour: Battle Harbour is a restored fishing village that dates back to the 18th century. Visitors can take a guided tour of the village to learn more about life in a fishing community, and they can also explore the surrounding nature trails.

Natural Attractions of Forteau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Forteau is renowned for its natural beauty, and visitors can enjoy several outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the most popular natural attractions in Forteau include:

  • Pinware River Provincial Park: Pinware River Provincial Park is located just a short drive from Forteau, and it is a popular destination for camping, fishing, and hiking. Visitors can take a scenic hike along the Pinware River or rent a canoe to explore the park’s waterways.
  • Long Point Lighthouse: The Long Point Lighthouse is a National Historic Site of Canada, and it provides visitors with stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike along the surrounding trails or take a guided tour of the lighthouse.
  • Red Bay National Historic Site: Red Bay National Historic Site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit destination in Forteau. The site contains several 16th-century Basque whaling vessels and exhibits on the history of the Basque whaling industry in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forteau

Forteau is a fantastic destination for outdoor activities, and visitors can enjoy several hiking and biking trails in the area. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Forteau include:

  • Forteau River Trail: The Forteau River Trail is a 5-kilometer trail that follows the scenic Forteau River. The trail is suitable for hikers of all skill levels, and it provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Trans-Labrador Highway: The Trans-Labrador Highway is a popular route for bikers looking to explore the natural beauty of Labrador. The highway stretches across 1,200 kilometers and provides stunning views of the rugged Labrador landscape.
  • Eagle River Nature Trail: The Eagle River Nature Trail is a 3-kilometer hiking trail that follows the scenic Eagle River. The trail provides visitors with stunning views of the nearby mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Forteau: Activities and Tours

Forteau has several exciting activities and tours for visitors to explore during their stay. Some of the best things to do in Forteau include:

  • Whale Watching: Visitors can take a guided boat tour to see the whales that migrate along the coast of Labrador. The tours provide visitors with stunning views of the surrounding landscape and an up-close look at the stunning marine life of the area.
  • Salmon Fishing: Labrador is also known for its fantastic salmon fishing, and visitors can try their hand at fishing in one of the area’s many rivers.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the scenic waterways surrounding Forteau. Kayakers can explore the rugged coastline and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Forteau

Day 1: Arrive in Forteau and visit the Point Amour Lighthouse. Day 2: Explore the Long Point Lighthouse and go hiking in Pinware River Provincial Park. Day 3: Take a guided tour of Battle Harbour and visit the Labrador Straits Museum. Day 4: Take a whale watching tour and try your hand at salmon fishing. Day 5: Rent a kayak and explore the stunning coastline around Forteau. Day 6: Visit Red Bay National Historic Site and enjoy the scenery along the Trans-Labrador Highway. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene of Forteau.

Food and Drink Scene of Forteau

Must-Try Dishes

Forteau is home to several local restaurants and food establishments that serve up delicious traditional dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in Forteau include:

  • Cod Tongues: Cod tongues are a local delicacy in Forteau. They are usually served in a batter and fried to perfection.
  • Toutons: Toutons are a traditional Newfoundland and Labrador dish made of fried bread dough and served with molasses.
  • Jiggs Dinner: Jiggs Dinner is a traditional Sunday dinner in Newfoundland and Labrador, consisting of boiled meat (usually beef or salted pork), turnips, cabbage, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

Forteau is home to several local breweries and distilleries that produce fantastic craft beer and spirits. Some of the best local drinks to try in Forteau include:

  • Iceberg Beer: Iceberg beer is brewed using water harvested from icebergs that float along the coast of Labrador. The beer has a unique flavor and is a must-try for anyone visiting Forteau.
  • Screech: Screech is a traditional Newfoundland and Labrador rum that is made by fermenting molasses. It is a popular drink among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Forteau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Forteau has several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Forteau include:

  • Forteau Bay Lodge: Forteau Bay Lodge is a beautiful guesthouse located on the shores of Forteau Bay. The lodge provides visitors with stunning views of the surrounding landscape and comfortable accommodations.
  • Alexis Hotel: The Alexis Hotel is a popular hotel in Forteau that provides visitors with comfortable accommodations and excellent amenities.
  • Many Rooms: Many Rooms is a unique hostel located in the town of L’Anse-au-Loup, just a short drive from Forteau. The hostel provides visitors with budget-friendly accommodations and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Forteau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Forteau is home to several local artisans and crafters that produce fantastic hand-crafted products. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Forteau include:

  • Labradorite Jewelry: Labradorite is a gemstone that is found in Labrador, and several local jewelry makers use the stone to create beautiful, one-of-a-kind pieces.
  • Traditional Crafts: Several local crafters produce handmade baskets, quilts, and other traditional crafts that make fantastic souvenirs.
  • Iceberg Vodka: Iceberg vodka is a locally produced spirit that is made using water harvested from icebergs that float along the coast of Labrador. It is a unique and delicious souvenir to bring home from Forteau.

Local Culture and Customs of Forteau

Forteau has a unique culture that is heavily influenced by its natural surroundings. Visitors can experience the local culture by attending traditional events and festivals, such as the Forteau Crab Festival. The local language in Forteau is English, but French is also spoken in some areas. Local customs include fishing and hunting, and visitors should respect the traditions and practices of the local community.

Best Time to Visit Forteau

The best time to visit Forteau is during the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. However, visitors should be prepared for cool temperatures and occasional rainfall, even in the summer months.

Getting to Forteau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Forteau is located in a remote part of Labrador, making it challenging to access for visitors. However, there are several transportation options available.

Airports Near Forteau

The nearest airports to Forteau are Deer Lake Regional Airport and Happy Valley-Goose Bay Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Forteau.

Bus Stations Near Forteau

There are several bus stations located near Forteau, including the Port aux Basques Ferry Terminal, the Corner Brook Bus Terminal, and the Happy Valley-Goose Bay Bus Terminal. From there, visitors can take a bus to Forteau.

Other Options
