Introduction to Port Hope Simpson

Port Hope Simpson is a small town located in Labrador, part of the Canadian province of Newfoundland and Labrador. The town is known for its rich history and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for those looking for a unique and authentic Canadian experience. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Port Hope Simpson.

List of Places to Visit in Port Hope Simpson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Labrador Heritage Museum: This museum showcases artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of the people and the land of Labrador. Visitors can learn about the region’s indigenous cultures, the fishing and fur trades, and early European settlers.

  • Masonic Lodge: This historic building was once used as a meeting place for the Freemasons. Today, visitors can explore the building and learn about its history.

  • Sir Richard Squires Memorial Park: This park is named after a former Newfoundland premier and features a statue and plaque in his honor. It’s a great spot for picnics and relaxation.

Natural Attractions of Port Hope Simpson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mealy Mountains National Park Reserve: This park is located just west of Port Hope Simpson and offers hiking trails, fishing spots, and incredible views of Labrador’s rugged landscape.

  • Pinware River Provincial Park: This park is located to the east of Port Hope Simpson and is a popular spot for canoeing and kayaking. Visitors can also hike the trails or enjoy a picnic in the park.

  • Battle Harbour National Historic District: This island community was once a major center for the cod fishing industry. Today, visitors can explore the restored buildings and learn about the region’s history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Hope Simpson

  • Traverspine Trail: This 4.5 km trail offers stunning views of Musgrave Harbour and the surrounding area.

  • Mealy Mountain Trail: This 8 km trail takes hikers through the heart of Mealy Mountains National Park Reserve, offering breathtaking views of the landscape.

  • Wonderstrand Trail: This 9 km trail winds through beautiful forests and along the Wonderstrand River, offering a peaceful and relaxing hike.

Best Things to Do in Port Hope Simpson: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the Labrador coast to see whales, seals, and other marine wildlife up close.

  • Fishing: Port Hope Simpson and the surrounding area offer some of the best salmon and trout fishing in Atlantic Canada. You can hire a guide or go on your own.

  • Cultural Tours: Learn about the indigenous cultures of Labrador through guided tours, cultural centers, and local events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Hope Simpson

Day 1: Arrive in Port Hope Simpson and explore the town’s museums and historic sites.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour of Mealy Mountains National Park Reserve.

Day 3: Go fishing in one of the area’s many rivers or lakes.

Day 4: Take a cultural tour of the local indigenous communities.

Day 5: Relax on the beach or explore Pinware River Provincial Park.

Day 6: Take a boat tour of the coast to see whales and other marine wildlife.

Day 7: Depart Port Hope Simpson.

Food and Drink Scene of Port Hope Simpson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jiggs Dinner: This traditional Newfoundland dish is made with salt beef, turnips, cabbage, and potatoes.

  • Fish and Chips: Fresh fish is battered and fried, then served with fries and tartar sauce.

  • Bakeapples: These small, tart berries are a local delicacy and can be found in jams, sauces, and desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Screech: Newfoundland’s famous rum is a must-try for any visitor.

  • Iceberg Beer: Made with water from nearby icebergs, this beer is a unique taste of Labrador.

Where to Stay in Port Hope Simpson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Northern Lights Motel: This family-owned motel offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

  • Labrador Inn: This historic hotel dates back to 1926 and offers a unique blend of history and comfort.

  • Port Hope Simpson Guest House: This cozy guest house offers affordable accommodations and a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Port Hope Simpson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Labradorite: This semi-precious stone is found only in Labrador and is known for its unique iridescence.

  • Crafts: The indigenous communities in the area produce a variety of handmade crafts, including birch bark baskets and moose hide moccasins.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Hope Simpson

The indigenous cultures of Labrador, including the Innu and the Inuit, have a rich history and tradition that is still alive today. Visitors are encouraged to learn about these cultures through guided tours, cultural centers, and local events.

Best Time to Visit Port Hope Simpson

The best time to visit Port Hope Simpson is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is mild and sunny, and the area’s natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Hope Simpson

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural beauty of the area.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Disrespect the local wildlife.

  • Underestimate the rugged terrain and weather conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Hope Simpson

Port Hope Simpson is a relatively safe town, but visitors are encouraged to take common sense precautions, such as locking doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Port Hope Simpson

Visitors should be aware that medical services in Port Hope Simpson are limited. It’s a good idea to bring any necessary medications with you and to take care when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Port Hope Simpson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port Hope Simpson

The closest airport to Port Hope Simpson is Goose Bay Airport, which is served by several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Port Hope Simpson

There are no bus stations in Port Hope Simpson, but there are several companies that offer bus service to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Port Hope Simpson, although the roads can be challenging and rugged.

How to Get Around in Port Hope Simpson

Most of Port Hope Simpson is easily accessible by foot or bicycle, although visitors may want to use a car or hire a taxi for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Port Hope Simpson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • L’Anse aux Meadows National Historic Site: This Viking site is located on the northern tip of Newfoundland and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Cartwright: This small town is located on the coast of Labrador and offers beautiful views and a glimpse into the region’s fishing history.

  • Terra Nova National Park: This park is located on the east coast of Newfoundland and offers hiking, camping, and beaches.

Conclusion

Port Hope Simpson is a hidden gem in Atlantic Canada, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, fishing, or learning about local culture, there’s something here for everyone. Come explore this beautiful region and create memories that will last a lifetime.
