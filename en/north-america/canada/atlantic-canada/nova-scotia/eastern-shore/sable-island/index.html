Introduction to Sable Island

Sable Island, located off the coast of Nova Scotia in Eastern Shore, Atlantic Canada, is one of the most stunning islands in North America. This crescent-shaped, sandy island stretches for about 42 kilometers and is home to more than 400 wild horses, over 20 bird species, and various other marine creatures. Sable Island is a protected site, which means visitors must obtain permission before visiting. Here is everything you need to know for planning your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Sable Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sable Island Museum: The museum is a great place to learn about the history of the island and its inhabitants, including the wild horses. It has several exhibitions showcasing the island’s maritime artifacts and stories.

Natural Attractions of Sable Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • East Light: The East Light is a lighthouse on the eastern tip of the island, offering panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean and the island.

  • West Light: The West Light is a historic lighthouse on the western end of the island. It is an excellent spot for watching sunsets.

  • Beaches: Sable Island is famous for its pristine beaches, including the main beach, West Spit, and South Beach. Visitors can walk along the shore, collect seashells, or relax on the sand.

  • Dune Fields: Sable Island’s dune fields are remarkable; they shift and change with the wind and offer a scenic landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sable Island

Sable Island is a great spot for hiking or biking. Visitors can explore the island’s trails, including the Graveyard Trail, the Gully Lake Trail, the Backcountry Trail, and the Sandy Trail.

Best Things to Do in Sable Island: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Watching: One of the main reasons people visit Sable Island is the wild horses. Visitors can watch and photograph them from a distance, respecting their space.

  • Bird Watching: Sable Island is a hub for more than 20 bird species, including the Ipswich Sparrow.

  • Kayaking: Kayaking around the island’s coast and waterways is a popular activity, offering a unique perspective on the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Sable Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: Sable Island is home to some of the freshest lobster in the world, and visitors should definitely try it.

  • Blueberries: The island is also known for its wild blueberries. Visitors can find them fresh or incorporated into various dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Alexander Keith’s Beer: Alexander Keith’s beer is a Nova Scotia classic, and visitors can try it in Sable Island.

Where to Stay in Sable Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Since Sable Island is a protected site and has limited facilities, visitors must camp on the island to stay the night. There are several designated camping sites.

What to Buy in Sable Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Horsehair products: Visitors can find horsehair bracelets and keychains as souvenirs.

  • Maritime Artifacts: There are several locally-made maritime artifacts available for purchase, including pottery and sculptural pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Sable Island

Visitors are expected to respect the island’s natural environment, follow the Leave No Trace principles, and ensure they do not disturb the local wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Sable Island

The best time to visit Sable Island is between June and October; the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy the outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sable Island

Do:

  • Respect the wildlife and their habitats.

  • Bring proper camping equipment and food.

  • Secure permission before visiting.

Don’t:

  • Remove anything from the island.

  • Approach the wild horses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sable Island

Visitors should be cautious of the potential dangers of the island, including hidden sinkholes, quicksand, and strong winds.

Health in Sable Island

Visitors should bring prescription medications and adequate first aid supplies.

Getting to Sable Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sable Island is accessible by air or sea. Visitors can fly from Halifax Stanfield International Airport or take a boat from Halifax or mainland Nova Scotia.

Airports Near Sable Island

  • Halifax Stanfield International Airport

Bus Stations Near Sable Island

N/A

Other Options

Visitors can take a chartered helicopter service from Halifax or mainland Nova Scotia.

How to Get Around in Sable Island

Visitors can walk or bike around the island since there are no motor vehicles allowed.

Where to Go Next from Sable Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Halifax: Visitors can explore the capital of Nova Scotia and its rich history and culture.

  • Cape Breton Highlands National Park: Visitors can experience one of the most breathtaking natural areas in the world, located in Nova Scotia.

Conclusion

Sable Island is an ideal destination for nature lovers seeking unique experiences. Visitors can explore the island’s natural beauty, watch the wild horses and birds, and enjoy the local food and culture. Be respectful of the island and its natural inhabitants, and plan your trip today!
