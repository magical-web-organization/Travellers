Canso is a small fishing town located on the Eastern Shore of Nova Scotia, in Atlantic Canada. Known for its rugged coastline, historic landmarks, and charming locals, Canso makes for an ideal off-the-beaten-path destination. In this travel guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Canso, Nova Scotia.

Introduction to Canso

Canso was once a booming fishing port and hub of commerce during the 18th and 19th centuries. Today, it offers visitors a glimpse into its rich history and quaint way of life. Due to its location at the mouth of the Canso Strait, the town has been witness to numerous shipwrecks and its lighthouse serves as a symbol of safety for those at sea. With a small population of just over 800 people, Canso is a peaceful retreat for those seeking a quieter pace of life.

List of Places to Visit in Canso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the biggest draws to Canso is its history. Visitors can explore many of the town’s most significant landmarks and museums. These are:

  • The Canso Islands National Historic Site - a collection of small islands that were once a thriving hub of commerce between France and England
  • The Whitman House Museum - a restored Greek Revival-style house that serves as a museum showcasing local history and artifacts
  • St. Paul’s Anglican Church - one of the oldest continuously used wooden churches in Canada, built in 1827

Natural Attractions of Canso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The town’s location on the Eastern Shore of Nova Scotia makes it an idyllic destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach day or an adventurous hike through the woods, Canso has plenty to offer.

Some of the natural attractions and outdoor adventures in Canso are:

  • Chedabucto Bay Beach - a stunning and secluded beach for a relaxing day by the water
  • The Coastal Walking Trail - a scenic route that offers beautiful views of the coastline of Canso and the surrounding area, ideal for a leisurely walk or bike ride
  • Grassy Island National Historic Site - a small island with plenty of wildlife and scenic views of the Canso Strait.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canso

For those who want to experience more of the natural beauty of Canso, there are several hiking and biking routes to explore.

Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Canso are:

  • The Chedabucto Bay Trail - a 6.5 km trail that follows the coastline from Canso to Hazel Hill, offering scenic views of the bay and the nearby islands
  • The Lighthouse Trail - a 10.5 km route that takes hikers and bikers past several lighthouses, including the famous Canso Lighthouse
  • The Grassy Island Trail - a 2 km trail that takes visitors around the small island, offering stunning views of the wildlife and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Canso: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the historic landmarks and natural attractions, there are several other things to do in Canso.

Some of the best activities and tours in Canso are:

  • Fishing Charters - Canso is known for its abundant fishing opportunities, and visitors can take a guided charter to catch some fish and learn about the town’s fishing history
  • Whale Watching Tours - visitors can go on a guided tour to see humpback whales and other marine life in the nearby waters
  • Local Festivals - the town hosts several festivals throughout the year, such as the Stan Rogers Folk Festival and the Canso Regatta.

Week-Long Itinerary for Canso

To make the most of your time in Canso, consider this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Canso Islands and learn about their history at the Canso Islands National Historic Site.
  • Day 2: Take a scenic walk along the Coastal Walking Trail and explore the Grassy Island National Historic Site.
  • Day 3: Spend the day fishing on a guided charter and learn about the town’s fishing history.
  • Day 4: Take a whale watching tour and see humpback whales and other marine life in their natural habitat.
  • Day 5: Visit the Whitman House Museum and learn about local history and artifacts.
  • Day 6: Explore the Lighthouse Trail and see several lighthouses, including the famous Canso Lighthouse.
  • Day 7: Attend one of the town’s many festivals or events.

Food and Drink Scene of Canso

The food and drink scene in Canso is characterized by its fresh seafood and locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Canso are:

  • Lobster - a staple in Nova Scotia, visitors can enjoy fresh lobster at several restaurants in Canso.
  • Digby Scallops - another seafood dish that is a must-try in Nova Scotia.
  • Fish and Chips - made with locally caught fish and served with fresh tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

Visitors can try a variety of local beverages in Canso, including:

  • Nova Scotia Wine - Nova Scotia is home to several vineyards that produce award-winning wines.
  • Garrison Brewery - visitors can try locally brewed craft beer at the Garrison Brewery.
  • Nova Scotia Cider - Nova Scotia is known for its delicious cider, made from locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Canso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Canso offers several lodging options for visitors depending on their budget and preference.

Some of the best accommodations in Canso include:

  • Seawind Landing Country Inn - a charming inn overlooking Chedabucto Bay.
  • Harbourview Lodge - a cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of the town.
  • Canso Lodge Motel - a budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and convenient location.

What to Buy in Canso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home several souvenirs and local products from Canso, including:

  • Handmade Pottery - visitors can find beautiful handmade pottery at the Osprey Arts Centre.
  • Local Honey - visitors can enjoy sweet and delicious honey from local bees.
  • Nova Scotia Tartan - a unique souvenir, visitors can buy the Nova Scotia Tartan at several shops in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Canso

Canso’s local culture is characterized by its rich history, traditional fishing practices, and vibrant arts community. Visitors can immerse themselves in the town’s culture by attending one of the many festivals and events, such as the Canso Regatta and Stan Rogers Folk Festival.

Best Time to Visit Canso

The best time to visit Canso is during the summer months (May-September) when the weather is mild and several festivals and events take place. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s off-season charm during the fall and winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canso

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and natural beauty by following all rules and regulations.
  • Engage in local culture and attend the town’s festivals and events.
  • Be respectful to the locals and their traditional fishing practices.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the town’s historic landmarks.
  • Ignore local customs or traditions.
  • Overfish or disrupt the town’s fishing industry.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canso

Canso is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. Visitors should always take necessary precautions and practice general safety measures.

Health in Canso

The town has several medical facilities, including a hospital and a pharmacy. Visitors should always carry the necessary medication and take precautions against tick bites during the summer months.

Getting to Canso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Canso is easily accessible by car, bus, and airplane.

Airports Near Canso

The nearest airports to Canso are Halifax Stanfield International Airport (approximately 3 hours away) and Sydney/J.A. Douglas McCurdy Airport (approximately 3.5 hours away).

Bus Stations Near Canso

The nearest bus stations are located in Halifax and Antigonish, both approximately 2 hours away from the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a scenic drive along the Eastern Shore of Nova Scotia or take a ferry from Newfoundland.

How to Get Around in Canso

Visitors can easily get around by car, bike, or by foot. Several car rentals and bike rental shops are available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Canso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby destinations and excursions from Canso, including:

  • Antigonish - a charming university town with plenty of outdoor activities and cultural events.
  • Cape Breton Island - one of Canada’s most spectacular scenic attractions and a popular destination for hiking, fishing, and whale watching.
  • Halifax - the capital city of Nova Scotia, with plenty of history, culture, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Canso is a hidden gem in Eastern Shore, Nova Scotia, that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Canada’s fishing industry, history, and natural beauty. With plenty of outdoor adventures, historic landmarks, and charming locals, Canso is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience.
