Introduction to Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is a wonderland of natural beauty and stunning landscapes located on Cape Breton Island, Nova Scotia. Covering an area of approximately 948 km², the park is known for its spectacular scenery, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. With over 26 scenic trails, you can find endless opportunities for hiking, camping, biking, or simply soaking up the magnificent scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cape Breton Highlands National Park has a wealth of culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places that are a must-visit for history and culture lovers:

1. Acadian Cultural Interpretive Centre

The Acadian Cultural Interpretive Centre serves as a gateway to the rich history and vibrant culture of Acadian people in Nova Scotia. This museum provides a glimpse into the Acadian lifestyle, traditions and history through artifacts, exhibits and multimedia presentations.

2. Alexander Graham Bell National Historic Site

The Alexander Graham Bell National Historic Site is a must-visit for those interested in the life and legacy of the famous inventor and innovator, Alexander Graham Bell. This museum showcases Bell’s life through informative exhibits and interactive displays.

3. Highland Village Museum

If you’re interested in learning more about the Scottish heritage of Nova Scotia, then the Highland Village Museum is the place to be. This open-air museum is a replica of a 19th-century Scottish village, complete with costumed interpreters, blacksmiths, weavers and craftspeople.

Natural Attractions of Cape Breton Highlands National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is a nature lover’s paradise, with a range of breathtaking natural attractions and outdoor adventures to experience:

1. Cabot Trail

The Cabot Trail is a scenic drive that winds its way through the park, offering stunning views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence and the Atlantic Ocean. It’s one of the most popular tourist attractions in the region, with numerous lookout points along the route.

2. Skyline Trail

The Skyline Trail is a popular hiking trail that offers panoramic views of the park’s rugged coastline. The trail is 8.5 km long and takes about 2-3 hours to complete, passing through boreal forests and open meadows.

3. Fishing

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is a popular spot for fishing enthusiasts, with plenty of lakes, rivers, and streams to explore. Brook trout and Atlantic salmon are just a few of the fish species found in the park’s waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cape Breton Highlands National Park

The park has over 26 scenic hiking trails, each offering a unique vista of the natural beauty of Cape Breton Highlands National Park. Here are our top three favorites:

1. Skyline Trail

As mentioned above, the Skyline Trail is one of the most popular hiking trails in the park, offering breathtaking views of the coastline.

2. Franey Trail

The Franey Trail is a challenging 7.4 km hike that winds through Acadian forest, over rocky outcrops, and provides panoramic views over both the park and the Atlantic Ocean.

3. Jack Pine Trail

The Jack Pine Trail is a moderate 2.3 km hike, with stunning views of the park’s plateau mountaintops. The trail runs through a mixed boreal forest and is a great place to spot wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Cape Breton Highlands National Park: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Cape Breton Highlands National Park. Here are some of our favorites:

1. Whale Watching

Take a boat tour to see some of the park’s marine life, including Humpback Whales, Minke Whales, and Atlantic White-Sided Dolphins.

2. Guided Tours

Many tour companies offer guided tours of the park, providing insights into the region’s flora, fauna, and history.

3. Beaches

Cape Breton Highlands National Park has several beautiful beaches, where visitors can relax and soak up the sun, including Ingonish Beach and Black Brook Beach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Day 1-2: Discover the parks most significant sights and attractions, including the L’Acadien trail, Alexander Graham Bell National Historic Site, and the Acadian Cultural Interpretive Centre.

Day 3-4: Take on some of the park’s more challenging hikes and trails, such as the Franey Trail and the Skyline Trail.

Day 5-6: Immerse yourself in the park’s history and culture, with a visit to the Highland Village Museum and a guided tour of the Acadian region.

Day 7: Relax and unwind on the shores of Cape Breton Highlands National Park’s beautiful beaches, before embarking on your next adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is well-known for its traditional cuisine, influenced by the French, Scottish, and Indigenous cultures that have shaped the region. Here are some must-try dishes:

1. Lobster

Nova Scotia is famous for its lobster, and Cape Breton Highlands National Park is no exception. Whether cooked traditionally or in a modern fusion dish, lobster is a must-try ingredient.

2. Fish and Chips

Freshly caught fish, battered and fried to perfection, is a popular dish in the region and is a must-try when visiting.

3. Blueberry Grunt

A traditional Nova Scotian dessert, the Blueberry Grunt is a delicious combination of blueberries and dumplings, served with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

Nova Scotia is home to some of Canada’s oldest and most historic breweries and wineries. Visitors to Cape Breton Highland National Park should try a range of local alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages, including:

1. Apple Cider

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is known for its delicious, locally-made apple cider, a refreshing non-alcoholic drink.

2. Wine and Beer

From the light, fruity vintages of Benjamin Bridge Winery to the crisp, hoppy brews of Breton Brewing Company, Cape Breton Highland National Park has something to suit every palate.

Where to Stay in Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Cape Breton Highlands National Park provides visitors with a range of accommodation options, including:

1. Ingonish Beach Campground

This popular campground is located on the east side of the park, providing visitors with easy access to Ingonish Beach.

2. Glenview Cottages and Campground

Glenview Cottages and Campground are situated on the Cabot Trail, offering stunning views of both the park and the Gulf of St. Lawrence.

3. Keltic Lodge Resort and Spa

If you’re looking for something a little more luxurious, the Keltic Lodge Resort and Spa is an excellent option. The hotel overlooks the Atlantic Ocean, providing visitors with stunning panoramic views.

What to Buy in Cape Breton Highlands National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Cape Breton Highlands National Park, be sure to buy some souvenirs and locally made products to bring home. Here are some items to consider:

1. Tartan Products

Tartan is an important part of Cape Breton’s Scottish heritage, and tartan products are sold throughout the region, including kilts, scarves, and blankets.

2. Handicrafts

Handicrafts produced by local artisans, such as pottery, woodcarvings, and textiles, make great souvenirs of your visit.

3. Lobster

Bring home some fresh lobster from one of Cape Breton’s many seafood markets and prepare a delicious meal when you return home.

Local Culture and Customs of Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is proud of its unique cultural heritage, influenced by the Indigenous, Scottish, and Acadian communities that have called the region home throughout its history. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Dress modestly when visiting places of worship or other sacred sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, including those related to food and dress.
  • Always ask permission before taking photographs of people, particularly Indigenous people.

Best Time to Visit Cape Breton Highlands National Park

The best time to visit Cape Breton Highlands National Park is between June and September when the weather is mild, and the park is at its busiest. However, if you prefer quieter periods, consider visiting outside of these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape Breton Highlands National Park

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and follow the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Make sure to bring warm clothes, as the temperature can drop in the evenings.
  • Plan your trip and make reservations for camping and accommodations in advance.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the park’s natural features.
  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Light fires in unapproved areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape Breton Highlands National Park

  • Hike only on the designated trails and carry a map or GPS device.
  • Stay on the lookout for wildlife, including moose, black bears and coyotes.
  • Stay hydrated and apply insect repellent to avoid tick bites in the summer months.

Health in Cape Breton Highlands National Park

  • Check for ticks after hiking or other outdoor activities, as they can carry Lyme disease.
  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants in areas where ticks are present.
  • Drink only bottled water or boiled water, as some areas in the park have untreated water.

Getting to Cape Breton Highlands National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cape Breton Highlands National Park

The closest airport to Cape Breton Highlands National Park is Sydney/J.A. Douglas McCurdy Airport, located about an hour’s drive from the park.

Bus Stations Near Cape Breton Highlands National Park

There are several bus companies that service Cape Breton Island, including Greyhound and Maritime Bus. The closest station to the park is located in Ingonish.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cape Breton Highlands National Park via the scenic Cabot Trail.

How to Get Around in Cape Breton Highlands National Park

The best way to get around Cape Breton Highlands National Park is by car or bike. There are several car rental companies in the region, and bike rentals are available at the park’s entrance.

Where to Go Next from Cape Breton Highlands National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cape Breton Island has much to offer visitors, with several nearby destinations and excursions, including:

1. Louisbourg

The Fortress of Louisbourg National Historic Site is located on the south-east coast of Cape Breton Island and is a must-visit for history buffs.

2. Baddeck

The village of Baddeck is situated on the shores of Bras d’or Lake, offering visitors a chance to relax and enjoy the scenery, with plenty of hiking trails and museums to explore.

3. Halifax

Nova Scotia’s capital city of Halifax is located on the mainland, around a four-hour drive from Cape Breton Island. The city is known for its historic attractions, including the Halifax Citadel and Maritime Museum of the Atlantic.

Conclusion

Cape Breton Highlands National Park is a must-visit destination in Atlantic Canada, offering stunning panoramas and a range of exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in hiking, kayaking or simply lounging on the beach, there’s something for everyone in this incredible park. Enjoy the local cuisine, appreciate the local customs, and immerse yourself in the region’s rich cultural history.
