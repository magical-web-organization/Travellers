Introduction to Windsor

Windsor is a charming town in the heart of Nova Scotia’s Annapolis Valley. It is also known as the birthplace of hockey and is home to the oldest continuously operating hockey rink in Canada. Windsor has a rich history, natural beauty, and a cultural vibe that attracts thousands of tourists every year. It is a perfect destination for those who want to explore a quintessential Canadian town with plenty of things to do and see.

List of Places to Visit in Windsor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hockey Heritage Museum: It is the birthplace of hockey, and you can witness the history of this popular winter sport at the Hockey Heritage Museum. You can see artifacts, photos, and videos that highlight the evolution of hockey from its humble beginnings to the modern-day game.

  • Fort Edward National Historic Site: Fort Edward is a historical fort that played a significant role during the 18th-century war between the French and the British. Visitors can explore the fort’s grounds and learn about the conflicts that took place.

  • Haliburton House Museum: Haliburton House is a well-preserved historic home that once belonged to Thomas Chandler Haliburton, a renowned Canadian author. Visitors can see the house’s original decor, architecture, and furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Windsor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blomidon Provincial Park: Blomidon Provincial Park is a natural park that offers picturesque views of the Bay of Fundy. It is an ideal spot for hiking, picnicking, and camping.

  • Avon River: Avon River is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, who can enjoy kayaking, canoeing, and fishing. It is a peaceful spot for picnicking and bird-watching.

  • Mermaid Theatre of Nova Scotia: The Mermaid Theatre of Nova Scotia is a world-renowned puppet theater that presents imaginative and entertaining shows for adults and kids. The theater has a puppet museum where you can see various types of puppets and learn about theater history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Windsor

Windsor has several hiking and biking trails that are suitable for all levels of adventurers. Some of the popular routes are:

  • The Windsor Waterfront Trail: The trail is a leisurely stroll along the Avon River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

  • The Fundy Shore and Annapolis Valley Trails: The trail is a 300-kilometer network of hiking and biking trails that runs from Halifax to Annapolis Royal.

Best Things to Do in Windsor: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the wineries: The Annapolis Valley is known for its wineries, and Windsor has several vineyards that offer wine tastings, tours, and seasonal events.

  • Take the Ghost Walk Tour: The Ghost Walk Tour is a popular evening tour that takes visitors to the town’s haunted places and eerie spots.

  • Attend the Pumpkin Regatta: The Pumpkin Regatta is an annual event that takes place in October and involves racing giant pumpkins across Lake Pisiquid.

Food and Drink Scene of Windsor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Meat Poutine: A Canadian classic with fries, gravy, cheese curds, and smoked meat.

  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty and delicious soup made with fresh seafood.

  • Donair: A Halifax specialty, Donair is a delicious sandwich with spiced meat, tomato, onion, and a sweet garlic sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Annapolis Valley is home to some of the best wineries in Canada, and visitors can try different types of wine.

  • Craft Beer: Windsor has several craft breweries that offer unique and flavorful beers.

Where to Stay in Windsor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Clockmakers Inn: A historic inn with luxurious rooms and modern amenities.

  • Tara Inn: A cozy and affordable bed and breakfast in the heart of Windsor.

  • Old Orchard Inn & Spa: A beautiful spa resort with stunning views of the Annapolis Valley.

What to Buy in Windsor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Nova Scotia is famous for its maple syrup, and visitors can buy different varieties.

  • Handmade Crafts: Windsor has several local artisans who sell handmade crafts, jewelry, and artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Windsor

Windsor is a friendly and welcoming community that values its history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors should show respect for local customs, including courtesy, politeness, and good manners. It is customary to tip 15-20% at restaurants and for taxi drivers.

Best Time to Visit Windsor

The best time to visit Windsor is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy outdoor adventures, festivals, and events. Autumn is also a great time to visit, with colorful foliage and seasonal activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Windsor

Do:

  • Respect local customs and manners.

  • Visit the local wineries and breweries.

  • Take part in seasonal festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.

  • Ignore safety guidelines and signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Windsor

Windsor is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings and belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist spots.

Health in Windsor

Windsor has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Windsor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Windsor

The nearest international airport is the Halifax Stanfield International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from Windsor. Several airlines offer daily flights from major Canadian and US cities.

Bus Stations Near Windsor

Windsor is served by several regional and intercity bus services, including Acadian Lines, Maritime Bus, and Greyhound Canada.

Other Options

Visitors can explore Windsor on foot, by bike, or by car. Rental cars and bicycles are available from several local agencies.

How to Get Around in Windsor

Windsor is a walkable town with several bike-friendly paths and sidewalks. Taxis, buses, and rental cars are also available for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Windsor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Halifax: Halifax is the capital city of Nova Scotia and offers a vibrant culture, historic landmarks, and scenic waterfront.

  • Annapolis Royal: Annapolis Royal is a charming town in the Annapolis Valley, famous for its historic fort and gardens.

  • Bay of Fundy: Bay of Fundy is a natural wonder with the world’s highest tides, rugged cliffs, and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Windsor is a fascinating town that has something to offer for everyone. Visitors can explore its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural heritage and enjoy activities and events throughout the year. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can experience the best of Windsor and create unforgettable memories.
