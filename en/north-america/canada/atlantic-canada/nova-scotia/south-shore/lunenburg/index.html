Lunenburg is a charming town located on the South Shore of Nova Scotia, in Atlantic Canada. This UNESCO World Heritage site is known for its colourful architecture, rich history, and thriving seafood industry. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, Lunenburg has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Lunenburg.

Introduction to Lunenburg

Founded in 1753, Lunenburg is one of the best-preserved examples of British colonial settlement in North America. The town’s historic district boasts over 200 buildings from the 18th and 19th centuries, many of which have been restored to their original colours and architectural styles. Lunenburg is also home to a vibrant fishing industry, which has been the backbone of the local economy for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Lunenburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lunenburg is a town steeped in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical sites that visitors should not miss.

  • Fisheries Museum of the Atlantic: This museum chronicles the history and culture of Atlantic Canada’s fishing industry, with exhibits on everything from boat-building to lobster fishing.

  • Lunenburg Academy: Originally built as a school in 1895, this National Historic Site now houses a community cultural centre and art gallery.

  • St. John’s Anglican Church: This beautiful church, built in 1754, is the oldest surviving Anglican church in Canada.

Natural Attractions of Lunenburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lunenburg is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, from rugged coastlines to lush forests.

  • Ovens Natural Park: This park features sea caves, hiking trails, and stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Blue Rocks: This picturesque fishing village is a popular spot for kayaking and bird-watching.

  • Kejimkujik National Park: This park is a wilderness oasis featuring hiking trails, lakes, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes in and around Lunenburg

Lunenburg is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking.

  • Rum Runners Trail: This 119-kilometre trail runs from Halifax to Lunenburg and is popular among both hikers and cyclists.

  • The Bluenose Trail: This scenic 10-kilometre trail meanders along the coast from Lunenburg to Blue Rocks.

  • The LaHave River Trail: This 6-kilometre trail follows the LaHave River and is a scenic spot for hiking and bird-watching.

Best Things to Do in Lunenburg: Activities and Tours

From historical tours to seafood cruises, there are plenty of fun and unique things to do in Lunenburg.

  • Lunenburg Walking Tours: These guided tours take visitors through the historic district, offering a glimpse into the town’s fascinating history.

  • Fisheries Museum of the Atlantic Seafood Tours: These tours take visitors onboard a fishing vessel to learn about the local lobster and scallop fishing industry.

  • Lighthouse Route: This scenic drive takes visitors along the coast, passing by picturesque fishing villages and lighthouses.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lunenburg

Day 1: Visit the Fisheries Museum of the Atlantic and explore the historic district.

Day 2: Take a seafood tour and enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

Day 3: Hike the Rum Runners Trail or the LaHave River Trail.

Day 4: Explore nearby Mahone Bay and Chester, two charming coastal towns.

Day 5: Visit Kejimkujik National Park for a day of hiking and nature exploration.

Day 6: Enjoy a scenic drive along the Lighthouse Route.

Day 7: Take a leisurely stroll through the town’s beautiful parks and gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Lunenburg

Lunenburg is known for its fresh seafood and thriving culinary scene. Here are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: A classic Maritime dish, lobster rolls are a must-try when visiting Lunenburg.

  • Fish and Chips: Fresh, locally caught fish served with chips is a popular dish in many local restaurants.

  • Digby Scallops: These succulent and sweet scallops are renowned for their quality and are a must-try when visiting Lunenburg.

Local Beverages

  • Ironworks Distillery: This local distillery produces award-winning spirits, including vodka, gin, and rum.

  • Hell Bay Brewing Company: This microbrewery produces a variety of craft beers, including the popular English Ale.

Where to Stay in Lunenburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lunenburg offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style.

  • The Lunenburg Inn: This elegant inn offers luxurious rooms and is located in the heart of the historic district.

  • The Bluenose Lodge: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a lovely garden, just a short walk from the waterfront.

  • Smugglers Cove Inn: This waterfront hotel boasts stunning views of Lunenburg Harbour and offers comfortable rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Lunenburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Lunenburg, be sure to pick up some unique souvenirs or local products to bring home.

  • Lunenburg’s iconic Dory: These beautiful and functional handmade wooden dories are made in the town.

  • Ironworks Distillery Products: Bring home a bottle of vodka, gin, or rum from this local distillery.

  • Folk Art: Lunenburg is known for its folk art and is home to several galleries showcasing local works.

Local Culture and Customs of Lunenburg

Lunenburg is a welcoming and friendly community, known for its hospitality and warmth. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and cultural traditions, such as the town’s strong fishing heritage.

Best Time to Visit Lunenburg

The best time to visit Lunenburg is between May and October when the weather is mild, and many of the town’s attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lunenburg

Do:

  • Explore the town’s beautiful historic district.

  • Try the local seafood, especially the lobster rolls and Digby scallops.

  • Go on a scenic drive along the Lighthouse Route.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on beaches or hiking trails.

  • Disturb the town’s wildlife or ecosystems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lunenburg

Lunenburg is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking their vehicles.

Health in Lunenburg

Visitors to Lunenburg should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and any necessary vaccinations. The town has a hospital and several medical clinics in the event of an emergency.

Getting to Lunenburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lunenburg

The closest major airport to Lunenburg is Halifax Stanfield International Airport, which is approximately an hour and a half away by car.

Bus Stations Near Lunenburg

There is no direct bus service to Lunenburg, but several bus companies offer service to nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lunenburg by car, bike, or ferry.

How to Get Around in Lunenburg

Lunenburg is a compact town and can be easily explored on foot. Bikes are also a popular mode of transportation, and several local companies offer rentals.

Where to Go Next from Lunenburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lunenburg is surrounded by several charming coastal towns and natural attractions worth visiting.

  • Mahone Bay: This quaint town is known for its picturesque harbour and charming shops and restaurants.

  • Peggy’s Cove: This iconic lighthouse and fishing village is a popular day trip destination from Lunenburg.

  • The Annapolis Valley: This fertile valley is home to several wineries, fruit orchards, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Lunenburg is a town full of history, culture, and natural beauty, and is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Nova Scotia. From exploring the town’s charming historic district to indulging in its world-class seafood, Lunenburg offers something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you some insight into the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Lunenburg.
