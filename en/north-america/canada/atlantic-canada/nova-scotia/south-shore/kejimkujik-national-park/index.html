Introduction to Kejimkujik National Park

Kejimkujik National Park is a natural and cultural gem located in the South Shore of Nova Scotia, Atlantic Canada. It boasts a unique blend of forest, lakes, rivers and diverse wildlife that make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers and cultural explorers. Whether you are looking for hiking, biking, paddling, camping, stargazing, fishing, or cultural immersion, Kejimkujik National Park has something special for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kejimkujik National Park

Here are some of the top places you should not miss when visiting Kejimkujik National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kejimkujik National Park Seaside Adjunct: This lesser known adjunct of Kejimkujik National Park is located on the South Shore near Port Joli, allowing visitors to view the rugged and beautiful coastline.
  • Mi’kmaw Petroglyphs: Discover ancient Mi’kmaw rock art, one of the most important archaeological discoveries in Canada.
  • Kejimkujik National Park Visitor Centre: Explore interactive exhibits on the park’s cultural and natural history.
  • Kejimkujik National Historic Site: Discover the traditional life of the Mi’kmaq people, who were the first inhabitants of the area, and more about Canada’s history.

Natural Attractions of Kejimkujik National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Mersey River: This winding river offers great paddling, swimming, fishing and scenic hiking trails.
  • The Kejimkujik Seaside Trail: Located on the South Shore, an easy trail that winds through the coastal landscape and offers glimpses of seals and shorebirds.
  • The Eastern Shore Islands: Hire a sea-kayak and explore these untouched islands to spot seabirds and seals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kejimkujik National Park

Kejimkujik National Park offers multiple hiking and biking trails that traverse its diverse landscapes. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes:

  • Hemlocks and Hardwoods Trail
  • Mill Falls Trail
  • Kejimkujik Seaside Trail
  • Peskowesk Trail
  • Grafton Woods Trail

Best Things to Do in Kejimkujik National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Paddling on the tranquil lakes and rivers of Kejimkujik.
  • Star-gazing at the Dark Sky preserve, one of the best in North America.
  • Fishing for trout and Atlantic salmon in crystal-clear streams.
  • Camping under starry skies at the numerous campsites in the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kejimkujik National Park

For a week-long trip to Kejimkujik National Park, here’s an extraordinary itinerary that ensures you get the best of both worlds – nature and culture:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Kejimkujik National Park, check into one of the park’s modern campsites and explore the nearby trails.
  • Day 2: Rent a canoe to paddle across the Mersey River and pick up fishing tips from expert fishing guides.
  • Day 3: Spend the day exploring the cultural heritage sites of the park, such as the Mi’kmaw Petroglyphs and Kejimkujik National Historic Site.
  • Day 4: Visit the Kejimkujik Seaside Adjunct for a scenic hike along the shoreline, and catch a glimpse of marine wildlife.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the park to learn more about the natural landscape and the diverse wildlife that call it home.
  • Day 6: Hike along one of the many trails, such as the Hemlocks and Hardwoods Trail or the Mill Falls Trail.
  • Day 7: Wrap up your trip by enjoying a star-gazing experience at the Kejimkujik Dark Sky Preserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Kejimkujik National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood chowder - filled with chunks of local Atlantic fish.
  • Wild blueberry pie - made with native berries grown in the region.
  • Lobster rolls - filled with chunks of succulent lobster, bound with mayo, butter, and served on a freshly baked roll.

Local Beverages

  • Nova Scotian Lager – a refreshing beer that is brewed using local ingredients and has a uniquely Nova Scotian taste.

Where to Stay in Kejimkujik National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kejimkujik National Park has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget. Some popular options are:

  • Backcountry Camping: Explore the wilderness of the park by camping on one of the many campsites. The park has more than 100 tent sites, as well as 17 yurts available for rent.
  • Guesthouse Accommodation: Stay in a cozy guesthouse located within the park, such as the Trout Point Lodge.
  • Nearby Hotels: Stay at one of the multiple hotels located in nearby towns, such as Liverpool, Bridgewater, or Shelburne.

What to Buy in Kejimkujik National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aboriginal crafts and arts representing the local Mi’kmaq culture.
  • Literature highlighting the park’s natural and cultural heritage.
  • Wild blueberry jam, a local delicacy that perfectly captures the sweet and sour flavors of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Kejimkujik National Park

The Mi’kmaq people are the first inhabitants of Kejimkujik National Park and continue to maintain their cultural heritage through their ongoing stewardship of the land. Visitors are encouraged to enjoy and respect their customs, and learn more about their rich history and traditions while visiting the park.

Best Time to Visit Kejimkujik National Park

The best time to visit Kejimkujik National Park is from May to October when the weather is mild and the park is open in full glory. However, the fall season (September-October) is particularly worth visiting because it offers a splendid display of changing colors on the foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kejimkujik National Park

Do:

  • Respect the natural and cultural heritage of the park.
  • Practice leave-no-trace principles when camping and hiking.
  • Adhere to park rules and regulations.
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Pick or disturb plants or wildlife.
  • Litter or leave waste behind.
  • Start a campfire or use stoves outside designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kejimkujik National Park

Kejimkujik National Park is a safe place to visit as long as visitors stay safe while hiking, paddling and camping. Visitors are encouraged to check with park staff for any safety warnings regarding wildlife, water conditions, and weather advisories.

Health in Kejimkujik National Park

Visitors should come with all the necessary supplies for their camping or hiking trips within the park. It is advisable to carry first-aid kits, sunscreen, insect repellent, and other essential medications or prescriptions as required.

Getting to Kejimkujik National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kejimkujik National Park

The closest airport to Kejimkujik National Park is Halifax Stanfield International Airport (YHZ), approximately 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kejimkujik National Park

Kejimkujik National Park is not accessible by public transportation.

Other Options

The best way to get to Kejimkujik National Park is by car.

How to Get Around in Kejimkujik National Park

Visitors can hire and use canoes, kayaks and bikes to navigate around Kejimkujik National Park.

Where to Go Next from Kejimkujik National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yarmouth: Explore the bustling port city known for seafood, folk art, and stunning architecture.
  • Annapolis Royal: Discover the historic town with a vibrant community and a rich cultural heritage.
  • Peggy’s Cove: Visit the iconic lighthouse and picturesque fishing village.
  • Lunenburg: Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Site, a charming town with a rich seafaring history.

Conclusion

Kejimkujik National Park of Canada is a magical blend of culture, history, and nature. It provides visitors with a glimpse of the rich heritage and natural beauty of the region. From hiking, paddling, fishing, and star-gazing, Kejimkujik National park has a lot to offer. Plan a visit and immerse yourself in the beauty and mystery of this Canadian treasure.
