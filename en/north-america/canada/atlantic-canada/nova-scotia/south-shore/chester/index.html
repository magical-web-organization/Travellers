Chester is a small town situated in the South Shore region of Nova Scotia, Atlantic Canada. The town is home to a unique maritime heritage, stunning landscapes, and a rich cultural legacy. Chester is a perfect destination to relax, unwind and explore the natural beauty of North America’s east coast.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Chester, we will take you on an unforgettable journey through the hidden treasures of this charming town.

Introduction to Chester

Chester is a picturesque coastal town nestled on the shoreline of Mahone Bay, approximately 90 minutes’ drive southwest of Halifax. The town boasts a rich maritime history, with a thriving fishing industry and a bustling summer tourism trade. The town’s exceptional setting, with its rocky coastline, pristine beaches, and tranquil waters, makes it an ideal destination for tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Chester

Chester offers a range of experiences for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best places to visit in Chester.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chester Heritage Society and Museum: The museum holds a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcases the town’s maritime heritage and the cultural legacy of its early settlers.

  • St. Stephen’s Anglican Church: The church is an iconic landmark of the town and has been a place of worship for over a century. The intricate stained-glass windows and the beautiful architecture of the building make it a must-visit place in Chester.

Natural Attractions of Chester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chester Golf Club: The golf course is located on a scenic hilltop within the town, offering stunning views of the surrounding coastline. Chester Golf Club is a perfect place to enjoy a relaxing day out, surrounded by nature.

  • Hiking and Biking Trails: Chester boasts a variety of hiking and biking trails for nature and adventure enthusiasts. The trails provide an opportunity to witness the stunning beauty of the town’s natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chester

One of the best ways to explore Chester is by taking a hike or bike ride through its beautiful surroundings. Here are the top routes to explore in Chester:

  • Aspotogan Trail: The trail spans for over 30km of Nova Scotia’s coastline and offers stunning sea views and secluded beaches.

  • Chester Connection Trail: The trail is an 11km-long walk through the countryside that provides stunning views of the town’s natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Chester: Activities and Tours

Chester offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy, embracing the town’s culture and history. Here are some of the best things to do in Chester:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the harbour and surrounding islands for a chance to witness the local marine life, such as dolphins, porpoises, and seals.

  • Whale Watching: Experience the magnificent beauty of the humpback and minke whales in their natural habitats on a whale watching tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chester

Here is a week-long itinerary for Chester to help you make the most of your visit to this beautiful town:

Food and Drink Scene of Chester

Chester’s food and drink scene offer an array of dining options for visitors, with most restaurants and cafes serving up locally-sourced seafood, craft beers and wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Chester:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder: A two-time winner of the Nova Scotia Chowder Cook-off, Chester’s seafood chowder is a must-try for any seafood lover.

  • Lobster Roll: The famous Nova Scotia Lobster Roll is a staple of the town’s dining scene. Made from fresh-caught lobster, it is a delicacy that should not be missed.

Local Beverages

  • Nova Scotia Craft Beer: Chester’s local craft beer scene offers a range of locally brewed beer flavors, including sea salt and caramel to wild blueberry ale.

  • Ice Wine: Chester’s unique climate provides suitable grape-growing conditions, producing high-quality ice wines with a sweet and fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Chester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chester offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  • Atlantica Oak Island Resort & Conference Centre: A grand resort overlooking Mahone Bay, with stunning views and a plethora of onsite amenities.

  • Oceanstone Seaside Resort: The resort offers a variety of accommodation options, including private cottages and luxurious suites, all along the scenic coast.

What to Buy in Chester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chester offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that can be brought home to serve as memories of your trip. Here are some of the things you can buy in Chester:

  • Local Art: From pottery to paintings, Chester boasts a thriving art scene, making it an ideal place to find unique pieces of art to bring home.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Chester’s jewelry makers specialize in handcrafting intricate and unique pieces that are perfect for a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Chester

Chester’s culture is greatly influenced by its maritime heritage and its close relationship with the sea. The town is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who are always ready to share their stories and experiences with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Chester

Chester is a year-round destination, with something to offer in every season. However, the best time to visit is during the summer months between June to August, when the weather is milder, and the town is alive with events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chester

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts of Chester to keep in mind while visiting:

Do:

  • Take a boat tour of the harbour and surrounding islands for a chance to witness the local marine life.

  • Try the locally sourced seafood and wine at one of the many excellent restaurants in the town.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash outside after a picnic, being careful in keeping the environment clean.

  • Take home any souvenirs made from endangered species.

Safety and Health

Chester is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate and a friendly community. However, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Chester

  • Visitors should avoid walking alone in isolated areas, mostly at night.

  • Visitors should take caution while swimming, and follow guidelines to keep safe.

Health in Chester

  • Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before traveling to Chester.

  • Visitors should bring insect repellent, especially during the summer months when the risk of mosquito-borne diseases is high.

Getting to Chester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chester is well connected to major cities in the North East of the United States and the rest of Canada, making it easy for visitors to access. Here are some transportation options to get to Chester:

Airports Near Chester

  • Halifax Stanfield International Airport (YHZ): The airport is located approximately 75 kilometers from Chester, and it provides a range of domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Chester

  • Chester Transit: A local bus company that provides services to towns and cities around Chester.

Other Options

  • Car: Visitors can rent a car from Halifax or another major city, and drive along the scenic coastal roads to reach Chester.

  • Taxi: Visitors can take a taxi from the airport or a nearby town to reach their accommodation in Chester.

How To Get Around in Chester

Chester is a small town, and visitors can easily navigate the town on foot or by bike. Bicycles can be rented at local bike shops.

Where to Go Next from Chester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chester’s location makes it an ideal base to explore the surrounding area and nearby towns. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Peggy’s Cove: A small, beautiful fishing village famous for its iconic lighthouse and rugged coastline.

  • Mahone Bay: A picturesque town with over 350 years of history, known for its bustling markets, art galleries and lively festivals.

Conclusion

Chester is a hidden gem located in the heart of Atlantic Canada, encompassing everything from historic landmarks, outdoor adventures, and a bustling food and drink scene. From the stunning views along the coastal trails to the cultural experiences in its museums, Chester is a unique and unforgettable destination for visitors to explore.
