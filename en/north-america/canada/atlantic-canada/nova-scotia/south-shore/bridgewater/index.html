Introduction to Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a small town located in South Shore, Nova Scotia, Canada. The town lies on the banks of both the LaHave River and Cornwallis River, making it a scenic destination for tourists. Visitors can experience the charm of a small town with a rich historical background that dates back to 1812. Bridgewater is a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life, and offers plenty of attractions and activities for tourists to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Bridgewater

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bridgewater is a town with a rich history that dates back to 1812. Visitors can explore the fascinating historical landmarks of Bridgewater such as:

  • The DesBrisay Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, this museum features exhibits of the region’s history showcasing the lives of the Indigenous People, Acadians, and African-Nova Scotians who have lived in the area.
  • Wile Carding Mill Museum: This historic building, located in the heart of the town, gives visitors a glimpse into the local industry of wool carding and textile production.
  • South Shore Genealogical Society: Visitors that are interested in genealogy should visit the South Shore Genealogical Society and research their family’s history.

Natural Attractions of Bridgewater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bridgewater is a scenic destination, offering visitors an authentic outdoor experience like no other. Some of the top natural attractions of Bridgewater include:

  • LaHave River: Visitors can explore the beautiful LaHave River by renting a kayak, canoe or take a boat tour.
  • Oak Island: Located just a few kilometers from Bridgewater, Oak Island is home to one of the world’s longest running treasure hunts. Take a boat tour or a guided walking tour of the island to learn about its fascinating history.
  • Osprey Ridge Golf Club: With breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, visitors can enjoy a relaxing day at this beautiful golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridgewater

Bridgewater has several trails ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts to explore:

  • Centennial Trail: This 3.1 kilometer trail weaves through the heart of Bridgewater and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Rails to Trails: Starting in Bridgewater and going east, this trail is 124 kilometers long and is ideal for cycling or hiking.

Best Things to Do in Bridgewater: Activities and Tours

  • Bridgewater Farmers’ Market: An outdoor market that runs on Saturdays from May – October. Visitors can buy local produce, crafts, and food.
  • Theatre Center: A 70-seat theatre that presents live theater year-round with an eclectic mix of professional and community productions.
  • Riverfest: A family-friendly event held annually in August that celebrates the LaHave River and the community of Bridgewater.

Food and Drink Scene of Bridgewater

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Freshly caught seafood served with crispy, golden fries is a staple dish in Bridgewater.
  • Lobster Roll: A lobster roll is a quintessential maritime dish, and visitors must try it while in Bridgewater.
  • Blueberry Grunt: This is a local dessert consisting of blueberries topped with delicious dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Ironworks Distillery: This artisan distillery produces award-winning gin, rum, and vodka, and offers tastings on-site.
  • Roadside Willies Smokehouse and Bar: This local bar is famous for the “Willie Classic,” a cocktail made from their own pepper sauce.

What to Buy in Bridgewater: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • LaHave Bakery: Visitors can buy freshly baked bread, cakes, and pastries that are perfect for picnics and snacks on the go.
  • The Mariner’s Daughter: This shop features unique and locally-made clothing, jewelry and decor items.
  • LaHave Craft Co-operative Limited: This co-operative features local art and crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridgewater

The local culture of Bridgewater is an amalgamation of Indigenous People, Acadians, and African-Nova Scotians who have lived in the area. Visitors will notice that Bridgewater is proud of its heritage and is eager to share it with visitors. The town has several community events that celebrate local traditions and culture such as Riverfest and Oak Island Day.

Best Time to Visit Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a year-round destination and every season brings something unique to explore. The summer months are the busiest when the weather is warm, and tourists can explore the natural sights of the town. Winters can be cold but beautiful, and the town transforms into a winter wonderland, perfect for skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bridgewater

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the town.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Take part in the community events.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Carry firearms without proper documentation and permits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bridgewater

Bridgewater is a peaceful town with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking their vehicles and accommodation, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Bridgewater

The local healthcare system in Bridgewater is adequate, with the South Shore Regional Hospital located nearby. Visitors should carry health insurance during their stay in Bridgewater.

Getting to Bridgewater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bridgewater

  • Halifax Stanfield International Airport: 90 kilometers away from Bridgewater.

Bus Stations Near Bridgewater

  • Maritime Bus: Operates daily bus service to Bridgewater with multiple stops along the way.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from Halifax Stanfield International Airport or in Bridgewater to explore the town and its surroundings.
  • Taxi Services: Local taxi services are available in Bridgewater.

How to Get Around in Bridgewater

Bridgewater has a relatively small town center that can be easily navigated by walking. Visitors can rent a bicycle to explore the town’s cycling routes or rent a car to explore the places further away from the town center.

Where to Go Next from Bridgewater: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mahone Bay: A picturesque town located a short drive from Bridgewater, known for its unique architecture and craft shops.
  • Kejimkujik National Park: A national park and nature reserve located approximately 50 kilometers from Bridgewater, perfect for hiking and wildlife spotting.

Conclusion

Bridgewater is a small town on the banks of two rivers, offering stunning natural beauty and rich history. Visitors can explore the town’s museums, enjoy outdoor activities, taste delicious local cuisine, and relax at one of the town’s beaches. With its warm hospitality and welcoming locals, Bridgewater is a great destination for a peaceful getaway.
