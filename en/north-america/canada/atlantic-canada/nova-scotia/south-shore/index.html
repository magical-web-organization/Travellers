Introduction to South Shore

South Shore is a stunningly beautiful region located in Nova Scotia, Canada. Its blend of scenic countryside and picturesque coastal towns make it a popular destination for anyone looking to escape from city life and experience the beauty of nature. The region is known for its rich history, unique culture and traditions, and delicious local cuisine. In this guide, we’ll take you through some of the best places to visit, top hiking and biking routes, local food and drink scene, and more to help you plan your trip to South Shore.

List of Places to Visit in South Shore

Whether you’re interested in exploring the rich cultural history of Nova Scotia, taking in the natural wonder of Atlantic Canada, or simply indulging in the local cuisine, South Shore has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in South Shore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fisheries Museum of the Atlantic
  • Black Loyalist Heritage Centre
  • Fort Point Museum
  • Thomas Raddall Provincial Park
  • Ross Farm Museum
  • Desbrisay Museum
  • St. John’s Church

Natural Attractions of South Shore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peggy’s Cove
  • Kejimkujik National Park
  • Rissers Beach Provincial Park
  • Harbour Rocks Trail
  • The Ovens Natural Park
  • Gaff Point Trail
  • LaHave Islands Marine Museum

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Shore

The South Shore region boasts an impressive collection of hiking and biking routes for all levels. Some of the top routes to consider include:

  • Rum Runners Trail
  • LaHave River Trail
  • Miles Lake Loop
  • Blue Rocks-Seaside Trail
  • Coastal Heritage Trail
  • Port Medway Loop
  • Mahone Bay-Chester Trail

Best Things to Do in South Shore: Activities and Tours

There are endless activities and tours to enjoy in South Shore. Here are some of the best:

  • Whale Watching Tours
  • Kayaking
  • Deep-Sea Fishing
  • Wine Tasting
  • Photography Tours
  • Golf Courses
  • Zipline Tours
  • ATV Trails

Week-Long Itinerary for South Shore

Here is an example week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to South Shore:

Day 1: Visit the historic town of Lunenburg and enjoy a meal at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Explore the Peggy’s Cove fishing village and lighthouse.

Day 3: Take a whale watching tour in the morning, then go for a hike in Kejimkujik National Park.

Day 4: Head to the Black Loyalist Heritage Centre and learn about the region’s African Nova Scotian community.

Day 5: Take a kayaking tour of the LaHave River and explore the stunning scenery.

Day 6: Indulge in some wine tasting at one of the many vineyards in the area.

Day 7: Relax on the beach at Rissers Beach Provincial Park.

Food and Drink Scene of South Shore

The South Shore region has a rich culinary heritage that includes fresh seafood, locally sourced ingredients, and traditional recipes that have been passed down for generations. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll
  • Digby Scallops
  • Hodge Podge (vegetable soup)
  • Fish Chowder
  • Blueberry Grunt (dessert)

Local Beverages

  • Nova Scotia Wine
  • Alexander Keith’s Beer
  • Ironworks Gin and Vodka
  • Nova Scotia Cider

What to Buy in South Shore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to take home from South Shore? Here are some local products you won’t want to miss:

  • Handcrafted pottery from the waterfront shops in Mahone Bay
  • Wooden toys and crafts made on-site at the Ross Farm Museum
  • Locally made jams and preserves
  • Traditional Nova Scotia tartans and kilts from the Made in Nova Scotia Store
  • Nova Scotia wines and beers to share with family and friends

Local Culture and Customs of South Shore

South Shore has a unique and rich cultural history that is worth exploring. Visitors can learn about the region’s historic relationship with the fishing industry, as well as the traditions of its Indigenous and African Nova Scotian communities. The region is also known for its art festivals and music scene.

Best Time to Visit South Shore

The best time to visit South Shore is during the summer months (June-August) when temperatures are mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, autumn is also a beautiful time to visit as the leaves change color and the local festivals begin. Winter can also be a wonderful time to see the unique beauty of South Shore in the snow and enjoy winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Shore

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the small coastal towns and enjoy the lovely people and local culture.
  • Try the seafood dishes and local beverages.
  • Be respectful of the cultural heritage and customs of the region.
  • Dress in layers for the changing weather.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying lobster roll and Digby scallops.
  • Wear skimpy clothing in the small towns and villages.
  • Be rude or dismissive of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Shore

South Shore is a generally safe region to visit, but it’s always important to exercise caution and common sense when exploring new places. Watch out for slippery rocks near the shore and be mindful of the changing weather.

Health in South Shore

Basic medical care is easily available in South Shore and there are several hospitals and clinics in the area. It’s important to have travel health insurance in case of any accidents or illnesses.

Getting to South Shore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Shore

Halifax Stanfield International Airport is the main airport servicing South Shore. From here, you can rent a car or take a bus to get to your destination.

Bus Stations Near South Shore

The South Shore region is serviced by several bus companies including Acadian Lines and Maritime Bus.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get around South Shore.

How to Get Around in South Shore

The best way to get around South Shore is by car. However, there are several bike rental companies and plenty of hiking trails for those who prefer to get around on foot.

Where to Go Next from South Shore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve exhausted the sights and sounds of South Shore, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Halifax, Nova Scotia’s capital city
  • Annapolis Valley
  • Prince Edward Island
  • Cape Breton Island
  • Fundy National Park

Conclusion

South Shore is a beautiful and culturally rich region with something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the history and traditions of the region, enjoying outdoor activities, or simply indulging in the local cuisine, South Shore has got you covered. Use this guide to help plan your trip and make the most of your time in this stunning part of Atlantic Canada.
