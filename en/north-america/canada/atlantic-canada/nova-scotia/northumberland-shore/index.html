Introduction to Northumberland Shore

Northumberland Shore is a region in Nova Scotia that consists of picturesque small towns and villages, historic sites, and stunning landscapes. It is located on the northern shore of Nova Scotia, overlooking the Northumberland Strait. The region is well known for its warm waters, sandy beaches, and rugged coastlines. With its rich history, unique culture, and natural beauty, Northumberland Shore is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a peaceful and offbeat vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Northumberland Shore

Northumberland Shore offers a range of attractions, including museums, historic sites, scenic routes, and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Presbytery Art Centre – The Presbytery Art Centre is a historic building that was renovated into an art gallery and studio. It showcases contemporary and traditional artwork from local artists.
  • McCulloch House Museum – The McCulloch House, built-in 1770, is a National Historic Site that offers visitors a glimpse into the life of one of Nova Scotia’s greatest politicians.
  • Northumberland Fisheries Museum – The Northumberland Fisheries Museum showcases the history of fishing in the region and the evolution of the fishing industry.

Natural Attractions of Northumberland Shore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Caribou/Munroes Island Provincial Park – Caribou/Munroes Island Provincial Park is a great place to experience the beauty of the Northumberland Strait. The park has a picnic area, hiking trails, and a bird-watching area.
  • Tidal Bore Park – The Tidal Bore Park is a unique natural attraction that occurs when the tidal bore forces its way up the Salmon River. The park has a viewing platform, walking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Powell’s Point Provincial Park – Powell’s Point Provincial Park is an ideal location to explore the north shore’s scenic coastline. The park has benches, picnic tables, and a boardwalk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northumberland Shore

Northumberland Shore has several hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to experience the region’s natural beauty up close. Here are some of the top trails in the region:

  • Tatamagouche Bay Trail – The Tatamagouche Bay Trail is a 10.5 km long trail that winds through forests, fields, and along the shore of the bay.
  • MacDonald Trail – The MacDonald Trail is a 6 km long trail that follows an abandoned railway line and offers stunning views of the Northumberland Strait.
  • Feltzen South Shore Hiking Trail – The Feltzen South Shore Hiking Trail is a 7 km long trail that runs along the coast and offers breathtaking views of the ocean and nearby islands.

Best Things to Do in Northumberland Shore: Activities and Tours

Northumberland Shore offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit travelers of all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Northumberland Shore:

  • Kayaking Tours – Northumberland Shore’s calm waters are ideal for kayaking. Several tour operators offer guided tours of the region’s waterways, including the Northumberland Strait and Pictou Harbour.
  • Golfing – Northumberland Shore has several beautiful golf courses that offer visitors stunning views of the surrounding landscape while they enjoy a leisurely game.
  • Wine Tasting Tours – Northumberland Shore is home to several vineyards and wineries. Visitors can take a guided tour of the vineyards, learn about the wine-making process, and sample some of the best wines in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Northumberland Shore

One of the best ways to explore the local culture of Northumberland Shore is through its food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder – Northumberland Shore is home to some of the best seafood in the world. A bowl of seafood chowder is a must-try dish when visiting the region.
  • Hodge Podge – Hodge Podge is a traditional Acadian dish made with fresh vegetables and cream.
  • Blueberry Grunt – Made with fresh blueberries and a sweet biscuit dough, the blueberry grunt is a delicious dessert that’s a staple in Northumberland Shore.

Local Beverages

  • Tidal Bay Wine – Tidal Bay Wine is a crisp, dry white wine that’s made with grapes grown exclusively in Nova Scotia.
  • Glenora Whiskey – Glenora Whiskey is an award-winning whiskey that’s distilled at Glenora Inn & Distillery, located in Cape Breton.

Where to Stay in Northumberland Shore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northumberland Shore has several accommodation options for visitors, from luxury resorts to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the top places to stay in Northumberland Shore:

  • Fox Harb’r Resort – Fox Harb’r Resort is a luxury resort that offers visitors a range of amenities, including a golf course, spa, and fine dining restaurant.
  • Pictou Lodge Beachfront Resort – Pictou Lodge Beachfront Resort is a charming resort that’s located right on the beach. It offers visitors stunning views of the Northumberland Strait.
  • The Train Station Inn – The Train Station Inn is a unique accommodation option that’s located in a historic train station. It offers visitors cozy rooms, a restaurant, and a museum.

What to Buy in Northumberland Shore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northumberland Shore has several unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. Here are some of the top products to buy in Northumberland Shore:

  • Handmade Crafts – Northumberland Shore is home to several artists and crafters who produce handmade items such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Local Wines – Nova Scotia is known for its local wines, and Northumberland Shore has several wineries that offer a range of delicious wines.
  • Maple Syrup – Nova Scotia is home to several maple syrup producers, and Northumberland Shore has several stores that sell high-quality maple syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of Northumberland Shore

Northumberland Shore has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The region is home to several festivals and events that celebrate its unique culture, including the Tatamagouche Oyster Festival and the Pictou Lobster Carnival. Visitors to the region should also be aware of the local customs, such as the importance of seafood in the diet and the significance of the Acadian heritage.

Best Time to Visit Northumberland Shore

The best time to visit Northumberland Shore is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is mild, and the beaches are perfect for swimming and sunbathing. The region is also home to several festivals and events during the summer, including the Lobster Carnival and the Celtic Colours International Festival.

Safety and Health

Northumberland Shore is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of the following:

Safety in Northumberland Shore

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings, particularly when visiting crowded areas.
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean, as currents and tides can be unpredictable.
  • Be aware of wildlife when hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Health in Northumberland Shore

Northumberland Shore has several medical facilities and emergency services available to visitors. Visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Bring any necessary medications with you.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.
  • Stay hydrated, particularly when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Northumberland Shore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northumberland Shore is located in Nova Scotia and is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Northumberland Shore:

Airports Near Northumberland Shore

  • Halifax International Airport is the closest airport to Northumberland Shore, located approximately 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Northumberland Shore

  • Maritime Bus provides service to several cities, including New Glasgow and Truro.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the airport or bus station to Northumberland Shore.

How to Get Around in Northumberland Shore

Northumberland Shore is a relatively small region, and visitors can easily explore the area by car or bike. Several rental car and bike rental companies are available in the region. Several taxi and shuttle services can also provide transportation to specific locations.

Where to Go Next from Northumberland Shore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northumberland Shore is located in Nova Scotia, which is home to several other charming towns and cities. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Cape Breton Island – Cape Breton Island is a stunning destination that’s located approximately 4 hours away from Northumberland Shore and has several scenic routes and outdoor attractions.
  • Halifax – Halifax is the capital city of Nova Scotia and is home to several historic sites, museums, and cultural events. It is located approximately 2 hours away from Northumberland Shore.

Conclusion

Northumberland Shore is a hidden gem in Nova Scotia that offers visitors a chance to experience the region’s unique history, natural beauty, and cultural significance. With its warm waters, breathtaking landscapes, and charming small towns, Northumberland Shore is a perfect destination for travelers looking for an offbeat vacation. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Northumberland Shore.
