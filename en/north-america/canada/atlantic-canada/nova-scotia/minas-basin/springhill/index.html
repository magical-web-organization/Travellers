Introduction to Springhill

Springhill is a small town in Nova Scotia, Canada, that emerged in the late 19th century as a coal mining town. It has a population of over 3,500 residents and is known for its abundant coal reserves. The town is situated on the border of Cumberland and Westmorland Counties, and it provides easy access to the Bay of Fundy. With peaceful surroundings, lush green fields, and scenic views, Springhill is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat.

List of Places to Visit in Springhill

Springhill offers several places to visit, which cater to different interests and preferences, such as:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Springhill Coal Mining National Historic Site: This is a must-visit site for anyone interested in coal mining history. This museum provides a comprehensive view of the town’s coal mining heritage dating back to the early 19th century. Visitors can explore the underground mine and learn about the lives of the miners who worked there.
  • Driftwood Park Retreat Centre: Built-in 1880, this historic building is situated on the outskirts of Springhill. It has a rich history, and visitors can stroll through the gardens or engage in guided tours to learn more about its history.
  • Allison Boomhower Monument: A historical monument erected in honor of Allison Boomhower, a victim of the Springhill mining disaster of 1956. This monument is a reminder of the courageous spirit of Springhill residents.

Natural Attractions of Springhill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amherst Point Bird Sanctuary: A perfect place for bird lovers, it is situated on a peninsula that dominates the Cumberland Basin. It has scenic trails, picnic facilities, and a birdwatching tower that provides excellent views of migrating waterfowl.
  • The Springhill Fen: This virgin peat bog is a unique natural attraction that boasts an ecosystem that is rare in North America. Visitors can discover the exotic plant species that thrive in this wetland.
  • Folly Lake: It is a perfect spot for swimming, kayaking, and fishing. The lush green surroundings provide an ideal environment for picnics and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Springhill

Springhill offers several hiking and biking routes that provide an opportunity for visitors to explore the town’s natural beauty and rugged terrain:

  • Coal Miner’s Marsh Boardwalk: A family-friendly hiking trail that winds around the marshy area in the heart of town. The boardwalk provides excellent opportunities to spot wildlife and enjoy panoramic views of the area.
  • Springhill Trailway: A hiking and biking trail that runs along an abandoned railway line. The trail spans over 8 km and offers scenic views of the town and its surroundings.
  • Springhill to Oxford Connector: This trail connects Springhill to Oxford and offers scenic views of the Minas Basin. It is a part of the Trans Canada Trail, and visitors can explore the areas in between.

Best Things to Do in Springhill: Activities and Tours

Springhill offers several activities that cater to different interests and preferences:

  • Biking and Hiking: Visitors can explore the town’s natural beauty by biking or hiking. The trails offer panoramic views of the area and an opportunity to enjoy its natural beauty.
  • Coal Mining National Historic Site Tour: Visitors can explore the underground mine and learn about the daily life of miners who worked there. Guided tours are available and provide an in-depth insight into the coal mining heritage of the town.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Visitors can explore the Folly lake by kayaking or canoeing, enjoying the natural beauty of the surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Springhill

Springhill offers several culinary treats that represent the town’s culture and tradition. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder: A classic seafood dish with a creamy texture and a hint of spice.
  • Lobster Rolls: A lobster meat sandwich traditionally served in a hotdog bun with mayo, butter and fresh celery.
  • Donairs: A spicy meat dish with a sweet and tangy sauce well-known among the locals.

Local Beverages

  • Ironworks Distillery: An award-winning craft distillery that offers a selection of spirits that use local ingredients.
  • Tatabrew Beer: A popular craft beer that is brewed locally in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Springhill

Springhill is home to a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its arts, festivals, and customs. Best Time to Visit Springhill

Getting to Springhill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Springhill is accessible via various modes of transportation.

The nearest airport is Halifax International Airport, about two hours away from Springhill.

Bus Stations Near Springhill

Other Options
