Introduction to Amherst

Welcome to Amherst, a charming town located in the Minas Basin of Nova Scotia, Atlantic Canada. Amherst is known for its diverse array of tourist attractions that cater to the interests of people of all ages.

Amherst serves as an excellent base for visitors to explore the Bay of Fundy, Cape Chignecto Park, and other local attractions. With natural beauty and cultural attractions galore, it is an ideal place to visit for a vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Amherst

Here are some of the top places to visit in Amherst:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amherst County Museum: Explore the rich heritage of Amherst County through this museum’s collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents. Admission is free of charge.

  • Fort Beausejour and Fort Cumberland: These historic fortresses served as significant strategic points in the defense of Acadia during the 18th century.

  • The Nova Scotia Highland Museum: Learn more about the unique culture and traditions of the Scottish who settled here and their ties to the area.

Natural Attractions of Amherst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parrsboro Rock and Mineral Shop and Museum: This unique museum houses an extensive collection of rocks and minerals from around the world, including some of the oldest rocks in the world.

  • Amherst Point Bird Sanctuary: Immerse yourself in the beauty of nature with a stroll through the trails of this bird sanctuary. You may spot over 100 bird species, including warblers, flycatchers, and shorebirds.

  • Sackville Waterfowl Park: Located 15 minutes from Amherst in Sackville, New Brunswick, the Waterfowl Park is home to over 160 species of birds in a tranquil setting that’s perfect for a walk or picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amherst

  • Dobson Trail: This 58-kilometer hiking trail connects the Fundy National Park to Riverview, New Brunswick, and is known for its stunning fall foliage displays, wildlife, and panoramic views.

  • Folly Lake Trail: Located only 20 minutes from Amherst, this 3-kilometer trail features two waterfalls and is ideal for hiking and biking.

  • Tantramar Trail: This 35-kilometer trail passes through the wilderness along the Bay of Fundy, through the forests of Cumberland County, and a local marsh with plenty of wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Amherst: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the nearby Oxford Riverside Gallery, which showcases works from local artists.

  • Explore the Bay of Fundy and its famous tides on a sea kayaking tour.

  • Savor a decadent lobster feast at one of Amherst’s many seafood restaurants.

Where to Stay in Amherst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Regent Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is located in the heart of downtown Amherst and provides a cozy and comfortable stay.

Local Culture and Customs of Amherst

The Scottish Celts played a significant role in shaping the culture of Amherst. Visitors can experience this by attending the events and festivals that showcase Scottish heritage and customs.

Best Time to Visit Amherst

The best time to visit Amherst is between May and September, when temperatures are mild, and there is lots of sunshine. The fall season is also an excellent time to explore the region, with gorgeous foliage displays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amherst

Do:

  • Do explore the fantastic natural beauty of the area.
  • Do attend local festivals to get a taste of the region’s culture.
  • Do try the seafood, maple syrup, and local craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, and always dispose of waste properly.
  • Don’t swim in unfamiliar waters without proper precautions and equipment.
  • Don’t forget to pack for the weather, as it can change quickly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amherst

Amherst is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions like locking their vehicles and securing valuables. It’s always a good idea to carry a cell phone and an emergency contact number while traveling.

Health in Amherst

It’s important to note that the tap water in Amherst is safe to drink. There are also hospitals and medical facilities in the area that provide medical assistance that visitors may need.

Getting to Amherst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amherst

The closest international airport to Amherst is the Halifax Stanfield International Airport, located 90 minutes away. From there, you may rent a car, take the bus, or arrange for a shuttle to take you to Amherst.

Bus Stations Near Amherst

There are several bus stations near Amherst, with the nearest one being the Tatamagouche Bus Station, which is located around 45 minutes away from Amherst. From there, you may take the bus to Amherst.

Other Options

Other popular ways to reach Amherst include driving, taking the train, and cycling.

How to Get Around in Amherst

The easiest way to get around Amherst is by car or bike. Visitors may rent a car, motorbike, or bicycle in town.

Where to Go Next from Amherst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the notable nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Fundy National Park – located two hours east of Amherst, with stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and a beach.
  • Moncton, New Brunswick – a vibrant and colorful city located only an hour from Amherst.
