Introduction to Bedford

Located in Halifax Region, in Nova Scotia, Bedford is a charming coastal community that boasts a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant cultural scene. This town is the perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful getaway with plenty of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and gastronomic delights.

List of Places to Visit in Bedford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bedford is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase the region’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • Bedford Institute of Oceanography: This world-renowned research facility offers a fascinating insight into the underwater world of Nova Scotia.
  • Scott Manor House: Step back in time and discover the traditional way of life in Nova Scotia’s early settlements.
  • Bedford Heritage Trail: Take a leisurely stroll along this scenic trail and discover the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Bedford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

With its stunning landscapes and breathtaking natural beauty, Bedford is the ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions:

  • DeWolf Park: This park offers a picturesque waterfront view and is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and picnicking.
  • Papermill Lake: With its crystal-clear waters and beautiful surroundings, Papermill Lake is a great place for kayaking, canoeing, and paddleboarding.
  • Bedford-Sackville Greenway: This extensive trail system provides opportunities for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bedford

With over 30 kilometers of hiking and biking trails, Bedford is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Nine Mile River Trail: This scenic trail takes you along the banks of the Nine Mile River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Bedford Waterfront Walkway: This beautiful trail winds along the shore of Bedford Basin and provides sweeping views of the ocean.
  • The Shoreline Trail: This trail takes you along the coastline of Bedford Basin and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk.

Best Things to Do in Bedford: Activities and Tours

From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, Bedford has plenty of activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Bedford Farmers’ Market: Experience the best of local food, crafts, and entertainment at the Bedford Farmers’ Market, open every Saturday from May to October.
  • Bedford Days: This annual festival held in July offers a week-long celebration of music, food, and fun.
  • Sunnyside Mall: Located in the heart of Bedford, this popular shopping destination features over 40 stores, restaurants, and services.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bedford

If you’re planning to spend a week in Bedford, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time in this beautiful town:

Day Activity
1 Explore the historic Scott Manor House and the local parks
2 Take a stroll along the Bedford Waterfront Walkway and enjoy a seafood dinner
3 Enjoy an outdoor adventure in the beautiful landscapes of DeWolf Park and Papermill Lake
4 Discover the cultural scene of Bedford by visiting the Bedford Institute of Oceanography and the Bedford Heritage Trail
5 Take a scenic drive to Peggy’s Cove and explore this picturesque fishing village

Food and Drink Scene of Bedford

Must-Try Dishes

Nova Scotia is famous for its fresh seafood, and Bedford is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Bedford:

  • Lobster Rolls: Made with fresh Nova Scotia lobster and served on a warm toasted bun, the Lobster Roll is a local specialty.
  • Fish and Chips: Enjoy the classic British dish made with fresh Atlantic cod.
  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty soup made with a blend of fresh seafood and local vegetables.

Local Beverages

Nova Scotia is famous for its craft beer and wine. Here are some of the local beverages you should try when visiting Bedford:

  • Benjamin Bridge: This award-winning winery produces some of the best sparkling wines in Canada.
  • Garrison Brewery: This local brewery is known for its craft beer, including the popular “Irish Red” and “Tall Ship” ale.
  • Luckett Vineyards: Sample some of the local wines and enjoy stunning views at this beautiful vineyard located just a short drive from Bedford.

Where to Stay in Bedford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bedford offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Oceanstone Resort: This luxury beachfront resort features comfortable rooms, stunning ocean views, and a range of amenities, including a spa and a restaurant.
  • Lakefront Cottage: Located on the shores of Papermill Lake, this cozy cottage is the perfect place to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of Bedford.
  • Comfort Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a convenient location just a short drive from the town center.

What to Buy in Bedford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bedford offers a range of unique and locally-made products that make the perfect souvenir. Here are some of the best items to bring home from your trip:

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Nova Scotia is famous for its handcrafted jewelry made with local gems and metals.
  • Maple Syrup: The region’s cool climate and rich soil make it the perfect place for producing high-quality maple syrup.
  • Tartan Blanket: The Nova Scotia Tartan is an iconic symbol of the region, and a tartan blanket makes a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Bedford

Nova Scotia has a strong Celtic heritage, and this is reflected in the region’s music, dance, and cultural traditions. When visiting Bedford, it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions, including

  • The Nova Scotia tartan is a symbol of the region, and many locals wear it with pride.
  • The Gaelic language is still spoken in some parts of Nova Scotia, and it’s a important part of the region’s cultural heritage.
  • The region is known for its friendly and welcoming people, and visitors are encouraged to engage with locals and experience the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Bedford

The best time to visit Bedford is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town comes alive with festivals and events. However, visitors can also enjoy year-round activities and attractions, including hiking, biking, and skiing in the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bedford

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Engage with locals and experience the local culture
  • Explore the town’s stunning natural landscapes and outdoor attractions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Take photos of people without their permission
  • Disrespect other visitors or locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Bedford

Bedford is a safe and welcoming community, and visitors can enjoy the town without any major safety concerns. However, it’s always important to take precautions when traveling, including:

  • Staying aware of your surroundings
  • Keeping your valuables safe
  • Following local laws and regulations

Health in Bedford

Nova Scotia offers high-quality health care, and visitors can access medical care in case of any emergencies. It’s always important to carry your medical insurance details and travel documents with you.

Getting to Bedford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bedford

The closest major airport to Bedford is the Halifax Stanfield International Airport, located about 20 kilometers from the town center. From here, visitors can take a taxi or public transportation to reach Bedford.

Bus Stations Near Bedford

The Halifax Transit bus system offers frequent services to Bedford from the Halifax city center. Visitors can also take a taxi or rental car to reach the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bedford by car, with easy access from the Trans-Canada Highway and other major routes in the region.

How to Get Around Bedford

Bedford is a small town, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or use the local bus system to get around.

Where to Go Next from Bedford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bedford is a great base for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Halifax: Explore this vibrant coastal city and discover its historic landmarks, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Peggy’s Cove: Visit the iconic lighthouse and enjoy the rugged coastal scenery of this picturesque fishing village.
  • Annapolis Valley: Discover the vineyards, orchards, and charming towns of this beautiful region.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant food and drink scene, Bedford is the perfect destination for those looking for an authentic and unforgettable Nova Scotia experience. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, this charming town has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the best of Bedford!
