Introduction to Hubley

Hubley is a charming village located in the Halifax Region of Nova Scotia, Canada. Surrounded by beautiful nature and a friendly community, it is a perfect destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and peaceful getaway. With a rich cultural heritage and a wealth of natural wonders, Hubley has something for everyone. Take a stroll through the lush forests, visit historical sites, or feast on tasty local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Hubley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Saint Margaret of Scotland Anglican Church: this beautiful 19th-century church is a designated heritage site and draws many visitors for its stunning architecture.
  2. Hubley Centre for Craft Food and Art: this community center showcases the work of local artists and craftspeople, offering unique souvenirs and cultural experiences.
  3. Peggys Point Lighthouse: located just a short drive from Hubley, this iconic 100-year-old lighthouse is a must-visit attraction for its picturesque views of the Atlantic coastline.
  4. Maritime Museum of the Atlantic: located in Halifax, this museum offers a fascinating look at Nova Scotia’s seafaring history and is a great stop for anyone interested in learning more about the region’s cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Hubley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Rails to Trails: this scenic trail runs through the heart of Hubley and offers a beautiful route for hiking, cycling, and admiring the local wildlife.
  2. Five Island Lake Provincial Park: located just outside of Hubley, this park offers a tranquil setting for boating, swimming, and hiking.
  3. The Bluff Wilderness Hiking Trail: this popular trail is perfect for anyone looking for a challenging hike through stunning scenic vistas and a variety of terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Hubley

Hubley is a hiker’s paradise, with scenic trails winding through forests and along picturesque lakes. The Rails to Trails system is a popular option for leisurely walks or casual cycling, while more experienced hikers can explore the challenging terrain of The Bluff Wilderness Hiking Trail. The Secret Garden Trail is also a local favorite, with its picturesque flower beds and peaceful ambiance.

Best Things to Do in Hubley: Activities and Tours

With its vast natural scenery and cultural heritage, Hubley offers a wealth of activities and experiences for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top activities to take part in:

  1. Kayaking or canoeing along the tranquil waters of Five Island Lake
  2. Local food tours to experience the region’s delicious culinary delights
  3. Visiting nearby attractions such as the picturesque Peggy’s Cove and historic Halifax
  4. Participating in outdoor adventures like zip-lining at Atlantica Oak Island Resort

Week-Long Itinerary for Hubley

Day 1: Arrival and checking into your accommodation

Day 2: Exploring the Rails to Trails and Five Island Lake Provincial Park

Day 3: Visiting historical sites such as Saint Margaret of Scotland Church and the Hubley Centre for Craft Food and Art

Day 4: Kayaking or canoeing in Five Island Lake

Day 5: Taking a day trip to Peggy’s Cove and Halifax

Day 6: Hiking the Bluff Wilderness Trail

Day 7: Participating in outdoor adventure activities like zip-lining at Atlantica Oak Island Resort

Food and Drink Scene of Hubley

The local food scene in Hubley is a must-try experience, with fresh seafood, locally sourced produce, and unique twists on traditional dishes. Here are some of the best dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster Rolls: a local classic, made with the freshest lobster caught from nearby shores.
  2. Blueberry Grunt: a Nova Scotian dessert made with juicy, plump blueberries and fluffy dumplings in a sweet sauce.
  3. Garlic Cheese Fingers: a regional specialty, made with pizza dough and slathered with a garlic butter and cheese and served at most local pizzerias.

Local Beverages

  1. Traditional Nova Scotia cider: a refreshing beverage made from locally grown apples.
  2. Nova Scotia wine: this region boasts a growing and thriving wine industry and produces some of the country’s best wines.

Where to Stay in Hubley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Atlantica Oak Island Resort: a luxury resort located just a short drive from Hubley. This resort features stunning ocean views, a spa, and numerous outdoor activities.
  2. Red Maple Inn: a cozy bed and breakfast nestled in the heart of Hubley that exudes comfort and tranquility.

What to Buy in Hubley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a unique keepsake, visitors can take home handmade crafts and local artwork found at Hubley Centre for Craft Food and Art. Local bakeries and markets also offer delicious local goods such as fresh berries and homemade preserves.

Local Culture and Customs of Hubley

Hubley is a welcoming community with strong ties to its Scottish heritage and roots. Visitors should take care to respect the local customs and traditions, and taking the time to learn more about these fascinating cultural factors can help visitors gain a deeper appreciation of this quaint village.

Best Time to Visit Hubley

The best time to visit Hubley is in the summer, from June to August. This time of year offers the warmest weather conditions and plenty of outdoor activities and festivals. The fall months of September to November are also an excellent time to visit, with milder temperatures and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hubley

Do:

  1. Take time to explore the local natural wonders and scenery
  2. Respect the local culture and customs
  3. Try the delicious local cuisine

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave behind any trash when visiting natural sites
  2. Disrespect the local traditions and heritage
  3. Leave your valuables or belongings unattended in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Hubley

Hubley is a relatively safe location with very low crime rates. Visitors should take the necessary precautions when outdoors or in public places, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding traveling alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Hubley

Health care is readily accessible in Hubley with many medical centers and hospitals located in nearby Halifax. Visitors should ensure they have sufficient travel insurance before arrival and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Hubley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hubley

  1. Halifax Stanfield International Airport is the closest major airport, located approximately 50 km from Hubley.

Bus Stations Near Hubley

  1. Halifax Bus Terminal is located in the heart of Halifax and connects visitors with several major bus routes to various cities across the province.

Other Options

  1. Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the area and surrounding regions.

How to Get Around in Hubley

Although most of Hubley’s attractions and sites are walking distance, visitors can use a car or bike to travel around more easily. Car rentals are available at Halifax airport, or visitors can use local taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Hubley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Peggy’s Cove: located just a short drive from Hubley, Peggy’s Cove offers stunning views of the coastline and picturesque lighthouse
  • Halifax: the capital of Nova Scotia, Halifax offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, from museums and galleries to the iconic Citadel Hill fortress.
  • Chester: a quaint coastal town known for its boutique stores and seaside charm.

Conclusion

Hubley is a hidden treasure in Halifax Region and a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a serene, outdoorsy getaway. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and warm hospitality, visitors are sure to fall in love with Hubley’s charm and allure. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, cultural buff, or foodie, Hubley has something to offer for everyone.
