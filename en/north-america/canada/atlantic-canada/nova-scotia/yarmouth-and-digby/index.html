Yarmouth and Digby Travel Guide

Located in Nova Scotia, Atlantic Canada, Yarmouth and Digby are charming, coastal towns that offer a unique mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re looking to explore museums, hike scenic trails, or indulge in local cuisine, Yarmouth and Digby have something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to these picturesque towns.

List of Places to Visit in Yarmouth and Digby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Firefighters’ Museum of Nova Scotia

Located in Yarmouth, the Firefighters’ Museum of Nova Scotia showcases the history of firefighting in the province. With a collection of vintage firefighting equipment, interactive exhibits, and educational programs, the museum is a must-visit for families and history enthusiasts.

Western Counties Military Museum

Also located in Yarmouth, the Western Counties Military Museum features exhibits and artifacts that highlight the military history of the region, from the early colonial period to modern times. The museum is a great place to learn about the contributions of Nova Scotians to various conflicts around the world.

Admiral Digby Museum

In Digby, the Admiral Digby Museum is a treasure trove of local history and culture. From displays of Acadian artifacts to exhibitions on the town’s maritime heritage, the museum offers a fascinating look at this charming community.

Natural Attractions of Yarmouth and Digby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cape Forchu Lighthouse

Located just outside Yarmouth, Cape Forchu Lighthouse is one of the most iconic landmarks in the region. With stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and opportunities for hiking and picnicking, the lighthouse is a popular destination for visitors and locals alike.

Kejimkujik National Park

A short drive from Yarmouth and Digby, Kejimkujik National Park offers a variety of outdoor activities, from hiking and camping to kayaking and fishing. With pristine forests, sparkling lakes, and abundant wildlife, the park is a great place to connect with nature and recharge your batteries.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yarmouth and Digby

The Tusket River Trail

Running through the heart of Yarmouth County, the Tusket River Trail is a beautifully maintained 22-kilometre trail that’s perfect for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing in the winter months. With stunning views of the Tusket River and a variety of wildlife along the way, the trail is a must-do for outdoor enthusiasts.

Harper’s Trail

Located in Digby County, Harper’s Trail is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with breathtaking views of the Bay of Fundy. With rugged terrain and steep inclines, the trail is recommended for experienced hikers only.

Best Things to Do in Yarmouth and Digby: Activities and Tours

Whale Watching

One of the most popular activities in Yarmouth and Digby is whale watching. With several tour operators offering trips into the Bay of Fundy, visitors can see humpback, minke, and fin whales up close and personal.

Bay Ferries

For a unique way to explore the region, hop on a Bay Ferry from Digby to Saint John, New Brunswick. With stunning views of the Bay of Fundy and easy access to Canada’s Maritime provinces, the ferry is a great way to experience the beauty of the region from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yarmouth and Digby

Check out this week-long itinerary for Yarmouth and Digby:

Day 1: Arrive in Yarmouth and explore the Firefighters’ Museum of Nova Scotia and the Western Counties Military Museum. Day 2: Visit Cape Forchu Lighthouse and explore the Tusket River Trail. Day 3: Take a whale watching tour and enjoy a seafood dinner in Digby. Day 4: Explore the Admiral Digby Museum and catch a Bay Ferry to Saint John. Day 5: Explore the city of Saint John and take in the sights and sounds of this vibrant community. Day 6: Return to Yarmouth and check out the local microbreweries for some craft beer tasting. Day 7: Relax and unwind with a day trip to Kejimkujik National Park, where you can hike, paddle, and soak up the natural beauty of this region.

Food and Drink Scene of Yarmouth and Digby

Must-Try Dishes

With a strong fishing tradition, Yarmouth and Digby are known for their delicious seafood. From lobster rolls to scallop chowder, visitors won’t be disappointed with the local cuisine.

Local Beverages

In addition to its seafood, Yarmouth and Digby are also known for their craft beer scene. With several microbreweries in the area, visitors can enjoy locally brewed beer while taking in the sights and sounds of this charming region.

Where to Stay in Yarmouth and Digby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to sleek hotels, Yarmouth and Digby offer a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Some of our top recommendations include the Rodd Grand Yarmouth Hotel, the Seawind Landing Country Inn, and the Harbourview Inn in Digby.

What to Buy in Yarmouth and Digby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Yarmouth and Digby can take home a piece of the region’s maritime heritage by purchasing locally made crafts and souvenirs. From handcrafted pottery to maritime-themed clothing, there is something for everyone in this coastal town.

Local Culture and Customs of Yarmouth and Digby

The people of Yarmouth and Digby are known for their warmth and hospitality, and visitors to the area can expect to be greeted with a friendly smile and a warm welcome. The region has a strong fishing tradition, and visitors can often see fishermen at work on the docks or hauling in their catch of the day.

Best Time to Visit Yarmouth and Digby

The best time to visit Yarmouth and Digby is from May to October, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy. Visitors should be prepared for occasional rain and fog, as the region is known for its unpredictable weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yarmouth and Digby

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood and beer scene.
  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the region.
  • Do immerse yourself in the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to locals or their traditions.
  • Don’t ignore safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yarmouth and Digby

Yarmouth and Digby are generally safe towns, but visitors should still exercise common sense and caution, especially when engaging in outdoor activities. Be aware of any wildlife or hazards in the area, and follow safety guidelines provided by tour operators.

Health in Yarmouth and Digby

While Nova Scotia has a universal healthcare system for residents, visitors to the area are recommended to purchase medical insurance prior to their trip. Facilities in Yarmouth and Digby are generally of a high standard, but visitors should still take precautions to prevent illness and injury.

Getting to Yarmouth and Digby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Yarmouth and Digby can arrive by car, bus, or air. The nearest airport is Halifax International Airport, which is approximately a three-hour drive from Yarmouth. Visitors can also take a bus to Yarmouth from Halifax, or rent a car and drive the scenic route along the coast.

How to Get Around in Yarmouth and Digby

Taxis and rental cars are available for visitors who want to explore the towns on their own. Bicycles and mopeds can also be rented for those looking for a more active way to see the sights.

Where to Go Next from Yarmouth and Digby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Yarmouth and Digby, visitors can explore the rest of Nova Scotia, or head across the Bay of Fundy to New Brunswick. Some nearby destinations worth visiting include Halifax, Lunenburg, and Annapolis Royal.

Conclusion

Yarmouth and Digby are two of the most charming towns in Nova Scotia and offer visitors a unique mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re looking to hike scenic trails, indulge in local cuisine, or explore the region’s maritime heritage, Yarmouth and Digby have something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to this beautiful part of Atlantic Canada!
