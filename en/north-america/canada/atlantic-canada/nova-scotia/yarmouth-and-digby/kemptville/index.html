Introduction to Kemptville

Kemptville is a small, serene town located in Yarmouth and Digby counties of Nova Scotia, Canada. It is situated between the Bay of Fundy and the Atlantic Ocean, making it a popular destination for those who seek serene landscapes, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The town has a rich history and culture that reflects in its museums and historical sites. Visitors flock to Kemptville to experience its local food and drink scene, shop for souvenirs, and explore the surrounding natural wonders.

List of Places to Visit in Kemptville

Kemptville has several places to visit that cater to different interests. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or outdoor enthusiast, there is something for everyone in Kemptville.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kemptville has a rich history that dates back to the early 19th century. It is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Kemptville United Baptist Church: A magnificent church built-in 1853 that reflects the town’s strong Baptist heritage.
  • Clam Harbour Beach Provincial Park: A beautiful provincial park with stunning views. It is home to the annual Clam Harbour Sandcastle Competition.
  • Yarmouth County Museum: A museum that showcases Yarmouth’s history from the earliest times to the present day.
  • Argyle Township Court House and Gaol: A historic courthouse and jail built-in 1805, that has witnessed several notable trials in Canadian history.

Natural Attractions of Kemptville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kemptville is known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventure opportunities. The town and its surrounding areas feature several natural attractions, including:

  • Kejimkujik National Park: A vast park that is perfect for hiking, camping, and kayaking. It is home to several unique flora and fauna species.
  • Cape Forchu Lighthouse: A historic lighthouse that is a popular spot for tourists. It offers stunning views of the nearby islands.
  • Smugglers Cove Provincial Park: A scenic park with a beautiful beach that lies on the shore of the Bay of Fundy.
  • Keene’s Mountain: A serene hike with beautiful views of the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kemptville

Kemptville is home to several scenic hiking and biking routes that cater to all skill levels. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or a beginner cyclist, you can find a trail that suits your needs. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails in Kemptville include:

  • The Kejimkujik Seaside Adjunct: A serene trail that runs along the coastal areas of Kejimkujuk National Park.
  • Kemptville Creek Trail: A popular biking trail that runs from the town of Kemptville to the nearby town of Denmark.
  • The Yarmouth Trail: A scenic hike that runs along the old railway lines. It offers stunning views of the surrounding areas.
  • Tusket River Trail: A picturesque hiking trail that runs along the banks of the Tusket River. It is perfect for families with kids.

Best Things to Do in Kemptville: Activities and Tours

Kemptville is a hub of outdoor and cultural activities that cater to all ages and interests. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, kayaking, fishing, and other outdoor activities. The town also hosts several festivals and concerts throughout the year. Some of the best things to do in Kemptville include:

  • Kayaking in Kejimkujik National Park
  • Attending the Annual Clam Harbour Sandcastle Competition
  • Exploring the historic museums and courtrooms in the town
  • Visiting the Yarmouth County Museum
  • Taking a scenic driving tour of the town
  • Attending the Yarmouth Seafest
  • Strolling around the local farmers market

Week-Long Itinerary for Kemptville

Visitors can spend a week in Kemptville by exploring the town’s surrounding areas and natural attractions. The following is a week-long itinerary for Kemptville:

Day 1: Arrive in Kemptville and explore the town’s museums and historical sites. Day 2: Take a scenic driving tour around Kejimkujuk National Park. Day 3: Hike the Kejimkujik Seaside Adjunct and kayak in the nearby lakes and rivers. Day 4: Biking on the Yarmouth Trail, followed by a picnic at the Tusket River Trail. Day 5: Visit the Cape Forchu Lighthouse and Clam Harbour Beach Provincial Park. Day 6: Explore the local food and drink scene and try the local dishes. Day 7: Departure from Kemptville.
