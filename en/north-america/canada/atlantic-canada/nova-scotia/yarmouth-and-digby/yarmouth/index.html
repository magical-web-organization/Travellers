Introduction to Yarmouth

Located in the southwestern tip of Nova Scotia, Yarmouth is a vibrant coastal town that offers a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its charming streets lined with colorful houses to its stunning landscapes and scenic views, Yarmouth has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide covers everything you need to know about planning a trip to Yarmouth, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat and buy.

List of Places to Visit in Yarmouth

Whether you are interested in history, culture or the great outdoors, Yarmouth has plenty to offer. From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and scenic views, here are some of the must-visit places in Yarmouth:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yarmouth is steeped in history and culture, with several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into its rich past. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Yarmouth:

  • Yarmouth County Museum and Archives. This museum is a great place to learn about Yarmouth’s fascinating history and culture. It features exhibits on shipbuilding, fishing, and other local traditions, as well as artifacts and photographs from the area’s past.

  • Pelton-Fuller House Museum. This beautifully restored historic house museum takes visitors back in time to the 19th century, with period furnishings and artifacts on display.

  • Cape Forchu Lightstation. This working lighthouse offers stunning views of the coastline and is a great place to learn about the area’s maritime history.

Natural Attractions of Yarmouth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yarmouth’s natural beauty is truly breathtaking, with rugged coastlines, pristine beaches, and lush forests to explore. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Yarmouth:

  • The Cat Ferry Terminal. This is the primary gateway into Yarmouth, and the ferry crossing from Maine, US, provides scenic views of the coastline.

  • Ellenwood Lake Provincial Park. This park features miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as a beautiful lake for swimming and fishing.

  • Leif Erikson Trail. The trail follows the coastline and offers breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yarmouth

Yarmouth has no shortage of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Great Trail. This trail network spans the entire country and runs right through Yarmouth, offering plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking.

  • Rails to Trails. This former rail line has been converted into a scenic trail that runs through Yarmouth and is perfect for biking or hiking.

  • Yarmouth County Rail Trail. This trail offers stunning views of the countryside and is a great place for hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Yarmouth: Activities and Tours

Yarmouth has something for everyone, from exciting outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and more. Here are some of the best things to do in Yarmouth:

  • Whale Watching Tours. Several tour operators offer whale watching tours along the coast, where you can see humpback whales, pilot whales, and even dolphins.

  • Kayaking Tours. Discover the rugged coastline of Yarmouth by sea kayak, with tours available for all experience levels.

  • Guided Hiking Tours. Join a guided hiking tour to explore Yarmouth’s beautiful natural surroundings while learning about the area’s history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Yarmouth

Yarmouth is home to several fantastic restaurants and breweries, serving up delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Yarmouth:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Digby Scallops. These locally caught scallops are a must-try in Yarmouth, served up in many dishes in local restaurants.

  • Lobster Roll. Another must-try dish, the lobster rolls in Yarmouth are packed full of delicious, fresh lobster meat.

  • Wild Blueberry Pie. Nova Scotia is known for its wild blueberries, and the pie is a must-try dessert in Yarmouth.

Local Beverages

  • Boxing Rock Brewing Company. This popular brewery in Yarmouth offers a range of craft beers, all brewed on-site.

  • Winegarden Estate. This winery offers a range of wines made from locally grown grapes, as well as tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Yarmouth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yarmouth has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best places to stay in Yarmouth:

  • Rodd Grand Yarmouth Hotel. This historic hotel is located in the heart of Yarmouth and features comfortable rooms, on-site dining, and a fitness center.

  • The Harbourview Inn. This charming inn is located near the waterfront and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving up delicious local cuisine.

  • The Carleton Motel and Coffee Shop. This budget-friendly motel features clean, comfortable rooms and an on-site coffee shop serving up delicious breakfast and lunch options.

What to Buy in Yarmouth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yarmouth is home to a range of shops and boutiques selling locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best buys in Yarmouth:

  • Handmade Quilts. Yarmouth is known for its beautiful handmade quilts, with several shops selling them in a range of styles and patterns.

  • Nova Scotia Sea Salt. This locally made sea salt is harvested from the ocean surrounding Yarmouth and is a must-have ingredient for any foodie’s kitchen.

  • Handcrafted Pottery. Many local artisans make beautiful, unique pottery that makes for a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Yarmouth

Yarmouth is a friendly, welcoming town with a strong sense of community and history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and take the time to learn about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Yarmouth

The best time to visit Yarmouth is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yarmouth

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Yarmouth, here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Be Respectful. Be respectful of the town’s history and culture, and be kind to locals and fellow travelers.

  • Visit Local Shops and Restaurants. Support local businesses by visiting independent shops and restaurants in town.

  • Enjoy the Outdoors. Take advantage of Yarmouth’s beautiful natural surroundings by exploring its hiking and biking trails and scenic drives.

Don’t:

  • Litter. Always dispose of trash in the proper receptacles and leave the area better than you found it.

  • Disturb Wildlife. Be respectful of the local wildlife and do not disturb their habitat.

  • Break any Laws. Always obey local laws and regulations, and do not engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Yarmouth is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should always take common sense precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Yarmouth

  • Be Aware of Your Surroundings. Always be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts if you feel unsafe.

  • Lock Your Doors. Always lock your car and hotel room doors, and keep valuables out of sight.

  • Stay on Designated Trails. When hiking or exploring the outdoors, always stay on designated trails and obey posted signs and warnings.

Health in Yarmouth

  • Stay Hydrated. Drink plenty of water, especially during the summer months when temperatures can get hot.

  • Protect Yourself from the Sun. Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.

  • Bring Insect Repellent. Mosquitoes and black flies can be a nuisance in Yarmouth during the summer months, so bring insect repellent to keep them at bay.

Getting to Yarmouth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yarmouth is easily accessible by car, ferry, and bus.

Airports Near Yarmouth

The closest airport to Yarmouth is Halifax Stanfield International Airport, located about 3.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Yarmouth

Several bus companies operate in Yarmouth, including Maritime Bus, which provides regular service to Halifax and other destinations in Nova Scotia.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Yarmouth is by ferry, with several crossings available from Maine, US.

How to Get Around in Yarmouth

Yarmouth is a walkable town, and most of the must-visit attractions are located within easy walking distance of each other. However, visitors may also choose to rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Yarmouth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Yarmouth, visitors can explore the stunning coastline of Nova Scotia or take a trip to nearby Cape Breton Island. Here are some great places to visit after Yarmouth:

  • South Shore. Explore the picturesque towns and beautiful beaches of the South Shore, including Mahone Bay and Lunenburg.

  • Cape Breton Island. Take a road trip along the Cabot Trail, a scenic drive that circles the entire island and offers stunning views of the coastline and mountains.

Conclusion

Whether you are into history, culture, the great outdoors, or simply relaxing by the sea, Yarmouth has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide covers everything you need to know about planning a trip to Yarmouth, from the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and what to eat and buy. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to Yarmouth!
