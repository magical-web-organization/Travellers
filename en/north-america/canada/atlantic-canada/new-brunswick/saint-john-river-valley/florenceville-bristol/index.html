Introduction to Florenceville-Bristol

Florenceville-Bristol is a picturesque town located in Saint John River Valley, in the province of New Brunswick, Atlantic Canada. Known as the “French Fry Capital of the World,” this charming town is home to the head office of McCain Foods Limited, one of the world’s largest producers of French fries.

With its scenic beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor activities, Florenceville-Bristol is a popular destination for tourists. Whether you prefer hiking trails, museums, or mouth-watering delicacies, there is something for everyone in Florenceville-Bristol.

List of Places to Visit in Florenceville-Bristol

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

McCain Heritage Center

The McCain Heritage Center is a must-visit place for history buffs. It offers an insight into the world of business and innovation through the eyes of the McCain family. Visitors can explore the history of McCain Foods Limited and learn how the company grew from a small French fry plant to a global enterprise.

Florenceville-Bristol Potato World

Potato World is an interactive museum dedicated to the humble potato. It showcases the history of the potato industry and the impact of McCain Foods Limited on the global market. Visitors can learn the importance of potatoes in the economy and culture of Florenceville-Bristol. Potato lovers can also indulge in a variety of potato-based delicacies at its café.

Natural Attractions of Florenceville-Bristol: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Florenceville Nature Park

Florenceville Nature Park is a serene retreat in the heart of the town. The park offers various hiking trails that take visitors through forested areas and along the scenic Saint John River. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching, fishing, or a picnic beside the river.

Hartland Covered Bridge

The Hartland Covered Bridge is the longest covered bridge in the world, a national historic site, and only a 20-minute drive from Florenceville-Bristol. It spans the width of the Saint John River and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florenceville-Bristol

Florenceville is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Florenceville Nature Park Trail: A 4.2 km easy trail that offers scenic views of the Saint John River.
  • Tobique Valley Trail: A 19.5 km trail that offers a challenging biking experience through dense forests and rolling hills.
  • Hartland to Perth-Andover Trail: A 44 km scenic route that connects two towns along the Saint John River.

Best Things to Do in Florenceville-Bristol: Activities and Tours

McCain® Plant Tour

Visitors can take a guided tour of the McCain food processing plant and learn how the company produces its famous French fries and other potato products.

McCain® Culinary Institute

The McCain Culinary Institute is an immersive culinary experience that offers guests a chance to learn cooking techniques from expert chefs. The institute offers different programs throughout the year, ranging from a one-day cooking class to a week-long culinary experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Florenceville-Bristol

Day 1: Visit Florenceville-Bristol Potato World and hike the Florenceville Nature Park Trail.

Day 2: Experience the McCain Culinary Institute.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the McCain plant.

Day 4: Visit the Hartland Covered Bridge and enjoy a picnic.

Day 5: Explore Tobique Valley Trail.

Day 6-7: Spend a weekend in Perth-Andover, visit the Perth-Andover Market and shop for local crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Florenceville-Bristol

Must-Try Dishes

Poutine

Poutine is a Canadian delicacy that originated in Quebec but quickly made its way across the country. The dish features French fries, cheese curds, and gravy and is a staple in most fast-food restaurants and mom-and-pop diners in Florenceville-Bristol.

Tartes and Tartelettes

Tartes and tartelettes are small, buttery pastries filled with fruit and custard. They are a popular dessert in Florenceville-Bristol and are available in many bakeries and cafes around town.

Local Beverages

Moosehead Breweries

Moosehead Breweries are Canada’s oldest independent brewery and are located in Saint John, only an hour’s drive from Florenceville-Bristol. Visitors can take a guided tour of the brewery and indulge in a tasting of their finest lagers and ales.

Where to Stay in Florenceville-Bristol: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hill Top Motel

The Hill Top Motel is a family-owned hotel that offers affordable accommodation in the heart of Florenceville-Bristol. Its rooms are clean and comfortable, and it offers easy access to all the town’s attractions.

The Bristol House Bed and Breakfast

The Bristol House Bed and Breakfast is a charming Victorian house that offers overnight accommodation with a full breakfast. It has four beautifully appointed rooms and is just a short walk from downtown Florenceville-Bristol.

What to Buy in Florenceville-Bristol: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Honey

Florenceville-Bristol is famous for its honey production, and visitors can buy jars of locally produced honey from roadside stands and farmers’ markets.

Crafts

Local artisans sell unique crafts inspired by the town’s agricultural heritage. Visitors can buy handmade quilts, rugs, and clay pottery at the Bristol Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Florenceville-Bristol

The people of Florenceville-Bristol are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Visitors can expect to be treated like family when visiting the town. The town’s agricultural traditions and strong ties to the potato industry are an essential part of its cultural identity.

Best Time to Visit Florenceville-Bristol

The best time to visit Florenceville-Bristol is during the warmer months, from May to October. The weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its best during this time of year. Visitors can attend the Potato Blossom Festival, which takes place in July, and celebrates the town’s agricultural heritage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florenceville-Bristol

Do:

  • Visit the Potato World Interactive Museum.
  • Try local dishes like poutine and tartes.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the Saint John River Valley.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor adventures.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and etiquette.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florenceville-Bristol

Florenceville-Bristol is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as locking doors and windows when leaving their rooms.

Health in Florenceville-Bristol

Visitors to Florenceville-Bristol should be aware of bugs and insects that can cause Lyme disease. It’s important to use insect repellents, wear long-sleeved shirts and pants, and perform tick checks during and after outdoor activities.

Getting to Florenceville-Bristol: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Florenceville-Bristol

There are two airports near Florenceville-Bristol. Fredericton International Airport is 1.5 hours away, while Saint John Airport is about an hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Florenceville-Bristol

Greyhound Bus Lines and Maritime Bus stop in Florenceville-Bristol.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent a car or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft to get around town.

How to Get Around in Florenceville-Bristol

Florenceville-Bristol is a small town that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi for longer trips outside of town.

Where to Go Next from Florenceville-Bristol: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint John

Saint John is the largest city in New Brunswick province and is only an hour’s drive from Florenceville-Bristol. The city features historic buildings, museums, and a bustling port.

Fundy National Park

Fundy National Park is a scenic nature reserve located two hours from Florenceville-Bristol. It features the highest tides in the world, dense forests, and majestic waterfalls.

Conclusion

Florenceville-Bristol is a town that is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors can experience the town’s unique potato heritage, explore its scenic trails, and savor its local delicacies. With well-maintained accommodations and a welcoming community, Florenceville-Bristol is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Atlantic Canada.
