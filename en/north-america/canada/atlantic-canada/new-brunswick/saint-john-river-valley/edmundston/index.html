Introduction to Edmundston

Edmundston is a charming town located in Saint John River Valley, in New Brunswick, Atlantic Canada. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and friendly locals, Edmundston offers plenty of exciting experiences to visitors. From exploring museums and historical landmarks to enjoying outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Edmundston

If you’re wondering what to see and do in Edmundston, here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Brunswick Botanical Garden - Featuring over 80,000 flowers, shrubs, and trees, this botanical garden is a paradise for nature lovers. With various themed gardens, walking trails, and a gift shop, it’s a great place to spend a day with family and friends.
  • Madawaska Historical Museum - Showcasing the rich history of Madawaska County, this museum has exhibits on the Acadian culture, logging industry, and local military history. Don’t miss the model train display and antique car exhibit.
  • Fraser Edmunston Golf Club - If you’re a golf enthusiast, you’ll love this 18-hole golf course. With beautiful scenery, lush greens, and challenging holes, it’s a fun place to tee off.
  • Jean-Daigle Cultural Center - This cultural center houses a theater, art galleries, and a library. Check their schedule for upcoming cultural events and performances.

Natural Attractions of Edmundston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Carleton Provincial Park - Named after the highest peak in the Maritime provinces, this park is perfect for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching. With over 11 trails of various difficulty levels and stunning views, it’s a hiker’s paradise.
  • Sugarloaf Provincial Park - This year-round recreational destination boasts over 22 km of cross-country ski trails, 16 km of snowshoe trails, and 13 downhill ski runs. During the summer, it’s a popular spot for mountain biking and hiking.
  • River Valley Scenic Drive - This scenic drive offers breathtaking views of Saint John River Valley, with stops at quaint villages and lookouts. Pack a picnic and enjoy the stunning scenery along the way.
  • Riviere-Verte Eco-Center - This eco-center is home to various wildlife species, including deer, bears, and coyotes. Take a guided tour to learn more about the local ecosystem and conservation efforts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edmundston

For outdoor enthusiasts, Edmundston has plenty of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Tabusintac River Trail - This 11km trail is perfect for hiking, cycling, and cross-country skiing. With stunning views of the river and various lookout points, it’s a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Edmundston Treasure Hunt Trail - This fun trail takes you on a scavenger hunt through the town, with clues and puzzles to solve along the way. It’s a great way to explore the town and learn about its history.
  • Aroostook Valley Trail - This 21km trail runs between Maine and Edmundston, passing through scenic countryside and farmlands. It’s ideal for cycling and walking, with plenty of picnic areas and rest stops along the way.

Best Things to Do in Edmundston: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Edmundston:

  • Photography Tour - Join a local photographer for a guided tour of Edmundston’s best photo spots. Learn photography tips, techniques, and editing skills while capturing stunning scenery.
  • Snowmobile Adventure - If you’re visiting in the winter, don’t miss a snowmobile tour of the town’s snow-covered trails and countryside. It’s a thrilling way to explore the winter wonderland of Edmundston.
  • River Rafting - For a fun and exhilarating adventure, try river rafting on the Saint John River. With rapids ranging from beginner to expert level, it’s an exciting experience for adrenaline junkies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edmundston

If you’re planning a one-week trip to Edmundston, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Edmundston and check into your hotel. Explore the town and its local shops and restaurants.
Day 2 Visit the Madawaska Historical Museum, Jean-Daigle Cultural Center, and New Brunswick Botanical Garden.
Day 3 Spend the day hiking in Mount Carleton Provincial Park or Sugarloaf Provincial Park.
Day 4 Join a photography tour of Edmundston or go snowmobiling in the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Edmundston

Edmundston has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many local favorites. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine Râpée - A traditional Acadian dish made by boiling grated potatoes and stuffing them with salt pork or chicken.
  • Seafood Chowder - A rich and creamy soup made with clams, scallops, and other seafood.
  • Fiddleheads - A local delicacy, fiddleheads are young ferns harvested before they unfurl and served as a side dish.
  • Maple Syrup - Canada’s iconic export, maple syrup can be found in local stores and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Gin Thuya - Made by a local distillery, this gin features a unique blend of northern herbs and botanicals.
  • Picaroons Traditional Ales - This award-winning brewery produces a range of craft ales with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Wild Blueberry Wine - This delicious wine is made from wild blueberries harvested in the region.

Where to Stay in Edmundston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to luxury hotels, Edmundston has a range of accommodation options. Here are some recommendations:

  • Four Points by Sheraton Edmundston - This upscale hotel is located in the heart of downtown Edmundston, with modern amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Auberge Les Jardins Inn - This charming inn features cozy rooms and suites, a gourmet restaurant, and beautiful gardens.
  • Bluebird Motel - A budget-friendly option, this motel offers clean and comfortable rooms with all basic amenities.

What to Buy in Edmundston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some recommendations:

  • Maple Syrup - Canada is famous for its maple syrup, and you can find locally made bottles in many stores and markets.
  • Acadian Crafts - Acadian culture is an integral part of Edmundston’s history, and you can find wood carvings, pottery, and other handmade crafts in local shops.
  • Blueberry Products - Wild blueberries are a popular crop in the region, and you can find jams, syrups, and other products made from this delicious fruit.

Local Culture and Customs of Edmundston

Edmundston is a town with a strong Acadian heritage, reflected in its culture, traditions, and festivals. The locals are known for their warmth and hospitality, and visitors can expect a friendly and welcoming atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Edmundston

The best time to visit Edmundston depends on your preferences and interests. If you’re into winter sports, visiting during the winter months is ideal. For hiking and outdoor activities, summer and fall are the best seasons. Fall is particularly beautiful, with stunning foliage and mild temperatures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edmundston

Edmundston is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take general safety precautions while walking or hiking in remote areas, such as telling someone their planned route and carrying a cellphone.

Health in Edmundston

Edmundston has a modern healthcare system, with a hospital and several clinics in the town. If you have any health concerns, it’s best to see a doctor or a healthcare professional.

Getting to Edmundston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edmundston

The closest airport to Edmundston is the Greater Fredericton International Airport, located 165 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to reach Edmundston.

Bus Stations Near Edmundston

The town has a bus station, with daily services to major cities and towns in New Brunswick and Quebec.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Edmundston by car or train.

How to Get Around in Edmundston

The town is small and easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Edmundston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to continue your trip from Edmundston, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Fredericton - New Brunswick’s capital city offers a range of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Quebec City - This beautiful city, located across the Canadian border, is known for its historic landmarks, charming streets, and delicious cuisine.
  • Halifax - The capital of Nova Scotia, Halifax is a vibrant city with a rich maritime history, lively arts scene, and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Edmundston is a hidden gem in Atlantic Canada, with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, this charming town is worth a visit. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip and enjoy all that Edmundston has to offer.
