Introduction to Hartland

Located in the Saint John River Valley of New Brunswick, Hartland is a small yet charming town known for its world-famous covered bridge. The town’s scenic location along the banks of the Saint John River, coupled with its rich history and vibrant culture, make it a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Hartland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hartland Covered Bridge

Hartland’s most famous attraction is undoubtedly the Hartland Covered Bridge, which is the longest covered bridge in the world. The bridge spans across the Saint John River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding scenery. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the history of the bridge and the engineering that went into building it.

Hartland Heritage Center

The Hartland Heritage Center is a must-visit museum for anyone interested in the town’s history. The museum showcases artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Hartland and its people over the years. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the center to learn more about the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Hartland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bell Forest

The Bell Forest is a beautiful natural area located just outside of Hartland. Visitors can explore the forest’s hiking trails, take a picnic, or go bird watching. The forest is also home to a variety of wildlife and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Albert County Museum

The Albert County Museum is another must-visit attraction for nature lovers. The museum is home to a vast collection of fossils and artifacts that showcase the area’s geological history. The museum also offers guided tours of the surrounding rock formations and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hartland

Hartland offers a range of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The Hartland Trail, a 33km former railway line, is perfect for those who want to explore the town’s scenic beauty. The trail passes through forests, over streams, and along the Saint John River, offering incredible views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Hartland: Activities and Tours

Hartland Covered Bridge Tours

Tourists can take a guided tour of the Hartland Covered Bridge for an in-depth understanding of the history and engineering that went into building it. This guided tour will provide tourists with unparalleled views of the surrounding landscapes, offering excellent photo opportunities.

Ice Fishing

Winter in Hartland is a time of ice fishing, and many tourists take this opportunity to try their luck in the frozen waters of the Saint John River. The fish caught during this time include salmon, trout, and smelt. Fishing enthusiasts are provided with the necessary equipment and gear through companies offering this service.

Food and Drink Scene of Hartland

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors to Hartland must try the Town’s famous lobster roll, among other outstanding local cuisines. ‘The Pork Shop’ in neighboring Florenceville is known for its smoked meat, while The River Valley Coffee House and the Hartland Farmers’ Market offer locally grown and organic food.

Local Beverages

The ‘The Tipsy Muse Café’ and ‘Van Ardenne’s’ are popular spots for people who want to delight in a cozy atmosphere, scrumptious coffee, fresh food, and homemade beers.

Where to Stay in Hartland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Hartland have a variety of options when it comes to accommodation. Some of the best hotels in the area include the Covered Bridge Inn, the Hartland Hotel, and the Hartland Motel, which are all recognized for their excellent hospitality.

What to Buy in Hartland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists should take home authentic handmade crafts produced by the town’s artists, such as pottery and wood carvings. The Hartland Farmers’ Market is also an excellent place to buy fresh locally grown produce and other foods.

Local Culture and Customs of Hartland

Hartland has a rich cultural heritage that is visible in its art, music, language, and food. The town is known for its friendly and welcoming people who embrace tourists and share the town’s history and traditions with them.

Best Time to Visit Hartland

Tourists should visit Hartland during the summer or fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. The peak season is between June and September with lots of activities and events. Winter in Hartland is also a great time for ice fishing and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hartland

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try out the local dishes and beverages.
  • Visit the museums and historical places.
  • Explore the natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause damage to the environment.
  • Take pictures of the locals against their will.
  • Behave in a disruptive or disrespectful manner.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hartland

Hartland is a safe and secure town with low crime rates. However, tourists should be cautious and take necessary measures to protect themselves and their belongings while out and about.

Health in Hartland

The town has several hospitals, healthcare centers, and pharmacies, with well-trained practitioners, to cater to any health issues that may arise.

Getting to Hartland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hartland

The closest airport to Hartland is the Fredericton International Airport, which is about 87 Km (54 miles) away. Other airports include Saint John Airport and Aroostook Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Hartland

The town has several bus stations, with regular connections to other towns and cities in Canada.

Other Options

Hartland is easily accessible by road, making it an ideal destination for road travelers. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi from the airport or bus station to reach Hartland.

How to Get Around in Hartland

The best way to explore Hartland is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent a bike from the local rental service providers or join the popular cycling tours. Taxis and Public transport buses are also an option.

Where to Go Next from Hartland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Florenceville

Located just a few miles from Hartland, Florenceville is a charming town known for its beautiful countryside and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and fishing.

Fredericton

Fredericton is the capital city of New Brunswick and a popular tourist destination. The city is home to several museums, galleries, and parks, making it a must-visit destination for culture enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Hartland is a hidden gem in New Brunswick that is waiting to be discovered. Its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming locals make it a must-visit destination for any traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or delicious food, Hartland has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
