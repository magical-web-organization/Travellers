Introduction to Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock is a picturesque village situated in the Saint John River Valley in New Brunswick, Atlantic Canada. The village is known for its natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and charming culture. Plaster Rock is an ideal destination for both adventure seekers and those who seek peaceful destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Plaster Rock

Here is a compiled list of the top places to visit in Plaster Rock:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Plaster Rock is steeped in a rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-visit historical landmarks and museums in the village:

  • Plaster Rock Heritage Railway Station: This beautifully restored railway station houses a museum that houses exhibits that showcase the rich railway history of the region. You can also enjoy train rides that provide stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Historic St. Thomas Anglican Church: Built-in 1863, this church has been serving the community for more than a century. The wood-carved interior, stained-glass windows, and carefully curated exhibits provide an insight into the vibrant history of the village.
  • Pebble Creek Mine and Museum: This museum is dedicated to the region’s mining history. Exhibits include a geology exhibit, a model mine, and artifacts used in mining.

Natural Attractions of Plaster Rock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Plaster Rock is blessed with a diverse natural landscape. Here are the top outdoor adventures and scenic views that you must explore while visiting the village:

  • Tobique River: This river is a popular destination for salmon fishing and recreational activities like canoeing, kayaking, and rafting.
  • Mount Carleton Provincial Park: A perfect place for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching. It boasts the highest peak in the Maritime provinces and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Plaster Rock Golf Course: This challenging 9-hole golf course is located in a pristine woodland area, providing a spectacular backdrop to play golf.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock has many outstanding hiking and biking trails where you can explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Tobique Trail: This 28 km long trail, located in the Mount Carleton Provincial Park, takes you through lush forests and rolling hills in the heart of Tobique River Valley.
  • Plaster Rock ATV Club Trail: This trail is perfect for those who want to explore the region by an ATV. Located in the rolling hills of the village, the trail offers riders stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and a chance to see wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Isabella Lake Trail: This trail is popular among hikers, and it offers a scenic view of the Isabella Lake. The trail is easy to traverse, and it is suitable for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Plaster Rock: Activities and Tours

Plaster Rock offers a variety of activities and tours to suit everyone’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Heritage Railway Station Train tour: Take a scenic ride on the railway train and learn about the rich railway history of the region.
  • Salmon Fishing: The Tobique River is famous for its salmon fishing. Hire a fishing guide and enjoy this exciting activity.
  • ATV Touring: Experience the pristine beauty of the Tobique Valley by taking an ATV tour. Explore the beautiful trails and take in the magnificent sights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plaster Rock

Here’s a week-long itinerary that you can follow to experience the best of Plaster Rock:

Day Activities
1 Visit Plaster Rock Heritage Railway Station, St. Thomas Church, and Pebble Creek Mine and Museum.
2 Hike the Tobique Trail in Mount Carleton Provincial Park
3 Enjoy recreational activities along the Tobique River, like kayaking or fishing.
4 Play golf at the Plaster Rock Golf Course.
5 Take an ATV tour and explore the scenic beauty of the Tobique Valley.
6 Visit Isabella Lake Trail and go for a scenic hike.
7 Relax and soak in the natural beauty of the village before your departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock has a unique food and drink scene that reflects the region’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon: Plaster Rock is famous for its salmon, and it is a must-try dish while you’re here. There are plenty of restaurants that serve fresh salmon, cooked to perfection.
  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish, poutine is a must-try while you’re in Plaster Rock. Fries, gravy, and cheese curds are served together to create a savoury, indulgent combination that’s impossible to resist.
  • Lobster: The nearby Gulf of St. Lawrence provides fresh lobster that is cooked in many of the village’s restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The village has several microbreweries that produce unique, excellent craft beer.
  • Cider: The Cider scene in Plaster Rock is emerging. The sweet and hard cider made from the fresh apples grown in the valley is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Plaster Rock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Plaster Rock offers various accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options to stay:

  • Motel Leo: This budget-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms that are perfect for travellers on a budget.
  • Tobique Log Home: This beautiful log home is situated in a tranquil location and offers guests fantastic countryside views.
  • Riverside Resort: This luxurious resort offers guests excellent amenities, including indoor and outdoor pools, hot tubs, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Plaster Rock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique and locally produced souvenirs and products that you can take home as a memory of your trip to Plaster Rock:

  • Handmade Crafts: The village is home to many craft shops that offer unique handmade items like pottery, woodwork, and paintings.
  • Maple Syrup: Maple Syrup is a staple product of Canada, and the locally produced premium quality Maple Syrup makes for a perfect gift.
  • Wild Blueberries: Wild Blueberries grown in the nearby fields are a delicious treat that can be taken home as a reminder of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock has a rich culture that revolves around fishing, hunting, and outdoor activities. The village also has a strong musical heritage that dates back many years. The ‘Fiddlehead Festival’ and ‘Tobique Valley Bluegrass’ festival are held annually and showcase local and regional talents.

Best Time to Visit Plaster Rock

The best time to visit Plaster Rock is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The autumn months (September to November) offer stunning fall foliage and mild weather. Winter months (December to February) are ideal for winter sports like skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plaster Rock

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Be mindful of the locals and their customs.
  • Explore the surrounding area responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment in any way.
  • Ignore safety warnings while doing outdoor activities.
  • Enter private property or restricted areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock is a safe community, and crime rates are low. However, visitors are advised to follow basic safety measures like keeping their valuables safe and not leaving their belongings unattended.

Health in Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock has medical facilities and pharmacies to take care of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Plaster Rock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plaster Rock

The Fredericton International Airport is the closest airport to Plaster Rock, located about 3 hours away. The airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Plaster Rock

The nearest bus station is located in Fredericton, which is about a 3-hour drive away from Plaster Rock.

Other Options

Plaster Rock is a remote village, and travelling by car is the best option. There are car rental services available at the Fredericton International airport.

How to Get Around in Plaster Rock

Plaster Rock is a small village, and walking is a great way to explore the area. You can also rent a bike or take an ATV tour to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Plaster Rock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore while visiting Plaster Rock:

  • Grand Falls: A 45-minute drive from Plaster Rock, Grand Falls is a stunning natural wonder with a 75-feet high waterfall.
  • Fredericton: The Capital city of New Brunswick, Fredericton is located about 1.5 hours away from Plaster Rock and offers visitors various historic and cultural attractions.
  • Kouchibouguac National Park: Located on the east coast of New Brunswick, this national park is famous for its sandy beaches, seals, and bird-watching opportunities.

Conclusion

Plaster Rock is a charming village with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. There’s something to suit every traveler’s interests, from outdoor adventures to exploring the region’s history and culture.
