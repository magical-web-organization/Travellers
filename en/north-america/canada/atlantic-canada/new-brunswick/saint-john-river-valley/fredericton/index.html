Introduction to Fredericton

Welcome to Fredericton, the charming capital city of New Brunswick! Located in the heart of Saint John River Valley, Fredericton is known for its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and a vibrant arts scene. The city invites visitors to explore its cultural attractions, natural wonders, hiking trails, and food scene. In this comprehensive guide, we will highlight the best things to do in Fredericton, places to stay, local customs and traditions, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Fredericton

Fredericton has something for everyone, from history buffs to nature lovers to foodies. Here are the top places to visit in Fredericton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beaverbrook Art Gallery: A world-renowned art museum featuring over 3,000 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs.

  • Fredericton Region Museum: This museum showcases the history of Fredericton and the surrounding area, from indigenous peoples to modern times.

  • Old Government House: This Georgian-style mansion is the former residence of the Lieutenant Governor of New Brunswick and is now open to the public for tours.

Natural Attractions of Fredericton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Odell Park: This 400-acre park features walking trails, picnic areas, and a duck pond. It’s a popular spot for hiking, birdwatching, and relaxing.

  • Killarney Lake: A beautiful lake surrounded by forests with several hiking trails around the shoreline.

  • Mactaquac Provincial Park: A vast park with over 5,000 acres, offering camping, hiking, fishing, golfing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fredericton

Fredericton has many excellent walking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the city. Some of the best routes are:

  • Nashwaak Trail: A 7.5-kilometer trail that follows the Nashwaak River, offering scenic views and opportunities for birdwatching.

  • Riverfront Trail: A 9-kilometer trail that runs along the Saint John River and offers stunning views of the city and the river. It’s perfect for walking, running, and cycling.

  • O’Dell Park: A 10-kilometer trail network that includes various loops for walkers, hikers, and bikers. The trails cross several streams and lakes and pass through a variety of forest environments.

Best Things to Do in Fredericton: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Fredericton, such as:

  • Explore the Historic Garrison District: Visit the Historic Garrison District, a national historic site, which includes the Old Officer’s Quarters, the Guard House, the Carleton Martello Tower, and the Fredericton Region Museum.

  • Take a Boat Tour: There are several boat tours available in Fredericton, such as Mactaquac Boat Tours, which take visitors to scenic spots on the river.

  • Attend Fredericton’s Many Festivals: Fredericton has an active arts and culture scene, with many events and festivals taking place throughout the year. Some of the major events include the Harvest Jazz & Blues Festival and the Fredericton Craft Beer Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Fredericton

Fredericton has a thriving food and drink scene, with many local specialties and craft beers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fiddleheads: A type of fern that grows in the forest and is a seasonal delicacy in Fredericton. Fiddleheads are often served steamed or in a salad.

  • Lobster Rolls: Fresh lobster is a staple food item in New Brunswick. Enjoy a lobster roll made with locally sourced ingredients in one of Fredericton’s seaside restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Picaroons Traditional Ales: Fredericton is home to Picaroons, one of the most famous craft breweries in the region. Their beer is made with locally sourced ingredients and has won many awards.

  • Cider: Cider is a popular drink in Fredericton and the surrounding area. Many local orchards produce high-quality ciders that are available in restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Fredericton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fredericton offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Fredericton are:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Fredericton Downtown: Located in the heart of downtown Fredericton, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

  • HI Fredericton Hostel: A budget-friendly option offering clean and comfortable dorm rooms, private rooms, and shared facilities.

  • Carriage House Inn: A charming guesthouse located in a historic building with cozy rooms, a garden, and a breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Fredericton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fredericton has many unique products and souvenirs to bring home, such as:

  • Maple Syrup: Maple syrup is a local specialty in Fredericton and can be found in many local shops and markets.

  • Handmade Crafts: Fredericton has an active arts and crafts scene, and many local artists sell their handmade products, including pottery, jewellery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Fredericton

New Brunswick has a rich culture and history, with influences from the French, British, and First Nations peoples. Fredericton is known for its friendly and welcoming people, and visitors to the city should respect local customs and traditions. Some common customs include greeting people with a handshake or a nod, respecting personal space, and avoiding sensitive topics in conversation.

Best Time to Visit Fredericton

The best time to visit Fredericton is between May and September when the weather is mild and sunny. In the fall, the colorful foliage of the trees is a sight to see. Winter can be cold and snowy, but also offers unique outdoor activities like ice skating and skiing. Spring can be rainy, but the city is blooming with flowers and growth.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fredericton

Do:

  • Dress in layers, as the weather can be unpredictable

  • Try local specialties like fiddleheads and lobster rolls

  • Visit Fredericton’s farmers markets and craft fairs

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas

  • Litter or damage the nature trails or parks

  • Ignorantly assume First Nation’s cultural practices

Safety and Health

Safety in Fredericton

Fredericton is generally a safe city, but visitors should take standard precautions such as locking their cars and not leaving valuables unattended. Fredericton also has many outdoor recreational activities, and visitors should be aware of the potential for accidents like falls or encounters with wild animals.

Health in Fredericton

Medical facilities are available in Fredericton, and visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before arrival. Ticks carrying Lyme disease are common in New Brunswick, so visitors should protect themselves with appropriate clothing and insect repellent.

Getting to Fredericton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fredericton

The Greater Fredericton International Airport is located about 14 kilometers from the city center and offers direct flights to Toronto, Montreal, and Ottawa.

Bus Stations Near Fredericton

Several bus companies serve Fredericton, including Maritime Bus, Acadian Lines, and Greyhound Canada. The main bus station is located in the downtown area.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Fredericton; the city is located on the Trans-Canada Highway. Several rental car companies operate in the city, and there are parking options available.

How to Get Around in Fredericton

Fredericton has a well-developed public transportation system with buses running throughout the city. Taxis and Uber are also available. Visitors can also rent bicycles and explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Fredericton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many exciting destinations and excursions within driving distance of Fredericton, such as:

  • St. Andrews: A quaint seaside town known for its historical charm, challenging golf courses, and whale watching tours.

  • Fundy National Park: A rugged coastal wilderness park with hiking trails, camping, and ocean views.

  • Hopewell Rocks: A unique attraction where the highest tides in the world reveal fascinating geological formations like flowerpot rocks and caverns.

Conclusion

Fredericton is a beautiful and welcoming city that offers a fantastic mix of cultural attractions, natural scenery, and recreational activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, foodie, or adventurer, Fredericton has something for you. Plan your next trip to Fredericton and discover the magic of Saint John River Valley!
