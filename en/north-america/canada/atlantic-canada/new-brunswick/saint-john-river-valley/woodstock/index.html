Introduction to Woodstock

Nestled between the Appalachian Mountains and the Bay of Fundy, Woodstock is a charming town in the heart of Saint John River Valley, in New Brunswick, Atlantic Canada. With its rich history, natural beauty, and welcoming atmosphere, it’s no wonder that Woodstock is a popular destination for travelers from around the world.

In this comprehensive guide to Woodstock, we’ll explore the best things to do in the area, including natural and cultural attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink, accommodation options, and much more.

So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Woodstock

First things first, let’s take a look at some of the must-visit places in Woodstock:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Carleton County Historical Society Museum: Located in the heart of Woodstock, this museum showcases the history and culture of the region, including displays of artifacts, photographs, and artwork.
  • The L.P. Fisher Public Library: This beautiful building, built in 1904, is a local landmark and an important hub of community culture.
  • The Old Carleton County Court House: Built in 1832, this historic courthouse is a stunning example of Georgian architecture and is now home to a museum showcasing the region’s legal history.

Natural Attractions of Woodstock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hartland Covered Bridge: The longest covered bridge in the world, spanning 1,282 feet across the Saint John River, is a must-visit attraction in Woodstock.
  • The Meduxnekeag River Nature Preserve: This beautiful nature reserve is home to diverse wildlife, hiking trails, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Woodstock Farm and Craft Market: This weekly market is a great place to shop for local produce and crafts, while taking in the gorgeous views of the Saint John River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Woodstock

For outdoor enthusiasts, Woodstock is a paradise of hiking and biking routes. Some of the best trails to explore in the area include:

  • The Meduxnekeag River Trail: This trail follows the river for 7.2 miles, offering stunning views of the nature reserve and opportunities for bird-watching and fishing.
  • The NB Trail: The New Brunswick Trail is a 56-mile trail that passes through Woodstock, offering opportunities for hiking, cycling, and cross-country skiing in the winter.
  • The Sentier NB Trail: This beautiful trail follows the Saint John River, offering stunning views and opportunities for fishing and canoeing.

Best Things to Do in Woodstock: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Woodstock. Some of our top picks include:

  • Grande Caribe Zip Line Adventure: This thrilling zip line adventure takes you through the forest canopy, offering breathtaking views of the Saint John River Valley.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking Tours: The Saint John River is the perfect place to explore by canoe or kayak, with its calm waters and stunning views.
  • Tasting Tours: Woodstock is home to many local breweries, wineries, and distilleries, offering tours and tastings of their delicious beverages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Woodstock

If you’re planning to spend a week in Woodstock, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Woodstock

When it comes to dining and drinking in Woodstock, there is no shortage of delicious options to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the most popular local dishes to try in Woodstock include:

  • Lobster Rolls: A classic maritime dish, lobster rolls are a must-try in Woodstock, particularly when paired with crispy fries and a cold beer.
  • Fiddleheads: A local delicacy, fiddleheads are the curled shoots of ferns that grow in the springtime and are often served sautéed with butter and garlic.
  • Butter Tarts: A beloved Canadian dessert, butter tarts are a sweet and buttery pastry filled with a mixture of sugar, butter, and eggs.

Local Beverages

Woodstock is also known for its delicious local beverages, including:

  • Maple Syrup: Famous for its maple trees, New Brunswick is a major producer of sweet and delicious maple syrup, which can be enjoyed on its own or used to sweeten other dishes.
  • Craft Beer: Woodstock is home to several local breweries, such as the Big Axe Brewery and the Hammond River Brewing Company, which offer a variety of delicious and unique craft beers.
  • Ice Wine: Made from grapes that have been frozen on the vine, ice wine is a sweet and dessert-like wine that is a must-try when visiting Woodstock.

Where to Stay in Woodstock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation options in Woodstock, there is something to suit every taste and budget, from cozy B&Bs to luxurious hotels and resorts.

Some of the best places to stay in Woodstock include:

  • The Shiretown Inn: This charming B&B is located in a historic building in the heart of Woodstock, offering a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.
  • The Best Western Plus: This modern and comfortable hotel offers all the amenities you need for a comfortable and enjoyable stay in Woodstock.
  • The Castle Inn: This luxurious inn features beautifully appointed rooms and suites, along with a spa and a restaurant serving delicious cuisine.

What to Buy in Woodstock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Woodstock, there are many souvenirs and local products to choose from, including:

  • Handmade Crafts: Woodstock is home to many talented artisans, who create beautiful pottery, textiles, jewelry, and woodwork.
  • Maple Syrup: As mentioned earlier, maple syrup is a major product of New Brunswick, and makes for a delicious and unique souvenir.
  • Fiddlehead Cookbooks: If you’ve fallen in love with fiddleheads, consider picking up a cookbook featuring recipes for this local delicacy.

Local Culture and Customs of Woodstock

Woodstock is a welcoming and friendly town, with a rich cultural heritage and a variety of customs and traditions.

Some of the most important cultural events and traditions to look out for in Woodstock include:

  • The Carleton County Annual Agricultural Exhibition: This event, held every fall, celebrates the area’s agricultural heritage with a variety of exhibits, competitions, and entertainment.
  • Christmas in Woodstock: This annual holiday celebration features a variety of events and activities, including a parade, a craft fair, and a tree-lighting ceremony.
  • The Potato Festival: This festival, held every August, celebrates the region’s potato industry with a variety of potato-themed events and activities.

Best Time to Visit Woodstock

The best time to visit Woodstock depends on your interests and preferences, but there is something to see and do in the area year-round.

If you enjoy hiking and outdoor activities, the best time to visit is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the trees are changing colors. For fans of winter sports, the winter months offer opportunities for skiing and snowmobiling.

If you’re interested in cultural events and festivals, plan your visit around one of Woodstock’s annual celebrations, such as the Potato Festival or Christmas in Woodstock.

Do’s and Don’ts of Woodstock

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable visit to Woodstock, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take advantage of the area’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Be open to learning about the history and culture of the region.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or damage natural environments.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Ignore safety regulations or warnings.
  • Pass up on trying local foods and drinks!

Safety and Health

Woodstock is a safe and welcoming town, but it’s still important to take some basic precautions to ensure your safety and health while visiting.

Safety in Woodstock

  • Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Follow all safety regulations while engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Woodstock

  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot weather.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and insect bites.
  • Be aware of the local wildlife and take precautions to avoid common hazards.

Getting to Woodstock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Woodstock is easily accessible by car, with major highways passing through the town. If you don’t have access to a car, there are also several transportation options available:

Airports Near Woodstock

The closest airport to Woodstock is Fredericton International Airport, which is approximately 75 miles away. Alternatively, you can also fly into Saint John Airport, which is approximately 95 miles from Woodstock.

Bus Stations Near Woodstock

Acadian Lines and Greyhound both operate bus services to Woodstock from several major cities in the region, including Fredericton and Saint John.

Other Options

If you prefer to explore the area at your own pace, you may also consider renting a car or joining a tour group.

How to Get Around in Woodstock

Woodstock is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer to drive, there are several car rental services available in the town.

Alternatively, you may also consider biking or taking public transportation, such as the local bus or taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Woodstock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of New Brunswick and Atlantic Canada after your visit to Woodstock, there are many destinations and excursions to choose from, such as:

  • Fundy National Park: This beautiful national park is located approximately 2 hours from Woodstock and offers stunning coastal views, hiking trails, and camping opportunities.
  • Saint John: The largest city in New Brunswick, Saint John is home to a variety of cultural attractions, including the Saint John City Market and the Reversing Falls.
  • Halifax: Located approximately 4.5 hours from Woodstock, Halifax is the largest city in Atlantic Canada and is known for its historic architecture, stunning waterfront, and vibrant arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Atlantic Canada or immerse yourself in its rich cultural heritage, Woodstock is the perfect destination for your next trip. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to pack your bags and experience everything this charming town has to offer.
