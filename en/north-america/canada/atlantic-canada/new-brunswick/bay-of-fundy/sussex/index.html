Introduction to Sussex

Nestled in Bay of Fundy in the Atlantic Canada region of North America, Sussex is a charming town that offers visitors an array of cultural, historical, and outdoor experiences. From hiking and biking trails to museums and local food, there is no shortage of things to do in Sussex. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the towns’ top attractions, activities, and local insights to help you plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Sussex

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Sussex has something for everyone. Here are some must-visit attractions in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apohaqui Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Apohaqui region and features exhibits on local industries and the community’s early settlers.
  • Agricultural Museum of New Brunswick: This museum showcases the history of agriculture in New Brunswick and features exhibits on farming techniques, tools, and equipment.
  • Kings County Museum: Located in a former courthouse, the museum features displays on local history and culture, including the role of the judiciary in the town.

Natural Attractions of Sussex: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fundy Trail Parkway: A scenic 16km drive along Fundy Bay with access to several lookout points and hiking trails.
  • Poley Mountain: This ski resort features skiing and snowboarding in the winter and hiking and biking trails in the summer.
  • Irving Nature Park: A nature reserve with hiking trails, beaches, and bird watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sussex

With its beautiful landscapes, Sussex offers numerous hiking and biking opportunities for visitors. Here are some popular trails to explore:

  • Dobson Trail: This 58km trail through the Caledonia Highlands is ideal for backpackers and experienced hikers.
  • Fundy Footpath: This rugged 41km trail runs along the Bay of Fundy coast and offers some of the best views in the region.
  • Sussex Bluffs Trail: A shorter option, this 10km trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sussex: Activities and Tours

From visiting museums to taking part in outdoor activities, there are plenty of things to do in Sussex. Here are some top-rated activities to add to your itinerary:

  • Agritourism: Explore the town’s agricultural roots with a visit to a local farm or farmer’s market.
  • Skywalking: Experience the thrill of walking across a suspension bridge 230 feet above the Salmon River.
  • Geocaching: Try your hand at treasure hunting with a GPS-enabled scavenger hunt throughout Sussex.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sussex

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Sussex:

Day 1: Explore the town’s museums and historical sites, including Apohaqui Museum and Kings County Museum.

Day 2: Hit the trails and hike or bike your way through Dobson Trail or Sussex Bluffs Trail.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Fundy National Park and explore the park’s hiking trails and beaches.

Day 4: Spend the day on the water with a kayak tour of the region’s many waterways.

Day 5: Try your hand at geocaching or take a guided wildlife tour to see Sussex’s local creatures up close.

Day 6: Head to Poley Mountain for some downhill skiing or snowboarding in the winter months.

Day 7: Indulge in some local cuisine with a food tour of Sussex’s many restaurants and cafés.

Food and Drink Scene of Sussex

No trip to Sussex is complete without sampling the town’s unique cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Atlantic seafood: Sample some fresh seafood dishes made with local lobsters, scallops, and mussels.
  • Fiddleheads: These ferns are a local delicacy and are often found in dishes throughout the region.
  • Maple products: Enjoy some local syrup or try a maple-glazed treat.

Local Beverages

  • Pump House Brewery: This local brewery offers a wide range of craft beers, including seasonal and limited editions.
  • Wine Tours: Take a tour of Sussex’s nearby wineries and sample local vintages.

Where to Stay in Sussex: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotels, Sussex has a variety of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Amsterdam Inn & Suites Sussex: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Jubilee Cottage Inn: This charming bed-and-breakfast is located in a historic Victorian home and features beautifully designed rooms and a hearty breakfast.
  • Backpacker’s Bunk: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms and a friendly, community atmosphere.

What to Buy in Sussex: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Sussex. Here are some popular options:

  • Maple syrup: This local staple makes a great gift for friends and family back home.
  • Handcrafted art: Check out the local art scene and find a unique piece to add to your collection.
  • Fleece blankets: Cozy up with a warm fleece blanket made by artisans in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Sussex

The people of Sussex are proud of their heritage and celebrate it in their local festivals and events throughout the year. Be sure to check the town’s events calendar and join in on the festivities.

Best Time to Visit Sussex

The best time to visit Sussex is during the summer months when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are plenty. The fall months also offer stunning foliage and a chance to sample some local seasonal treats.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sussex

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind during your visit to Sussex:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the town’s many outdoor activities and explore the beautiful landscapes.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages for a true taste of Sussex.
  • Attend local festivals and events to experience the town’s culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave without exploring the town’s museums and historical sites to learn about its rich history.
  • Miss out on trying some local products, such as maple syrup or handcrafted art.
  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.

Safety and Health

Here are some essential safety and health tips to keep in mind during your trip to Sussex:

Safety in Sussex

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking and visiting remote areas.
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife if you encounter any during your outdoor adventures.
  • Take precautions when participating in water sports and be aware of tides and currents in Bay of Fundy.

Health in Sussex

  • Ensure you are up-to-date on routine vaccinations before your trip.
  • Protect yourself from ticks when hiking in wooded areas.
  • Drink bottled water or boiled water to avoid waterborne illnesses.

Getting to Sussex: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider when planning your trip to Sussex:

Airports Near Sussex

  • Saint John Airport (YSJ)
  • Moncton Airport (YQM)

Bus Stations Near Sussex

  • Maritime Bus (Saint John Bus Terminal)

Other Options

  • Car rental: A car rental is a popular and convenient option for exploring Sussex and the surrounding area.
  • Train: VIA Rail offers passenger rail service from Montreal and Halifax, with a stop in Moncton.

How to Get Around in Sussex

The best way to get around Sussex is by car, as it offers the most flexibility for exploring the region. However, there are also biking and hiking options for those looking to experience the outdoors on foot or two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Sussex: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out during your visit to Sussex:

  • Saint John: Located just 45 minutes away, Saint John offers visitors a vibrant arts scene, historic sites, and world-class seafood.
  • Moncton: A quick hour’s drive from Sussex, Moncton is known for its natural wonders, such as the tidal bore on the Petitcodiac River.
  • Hopewell Rocks: Visit the iconic Hopewell Rocks, located approximately 90 minutes from Sussex, and marvel at the dramatic tides and rock formations on the Bay of Fundy.

Conclusion

Sussex is a town full of natural beauty, rich history, and unique cultural experiences. Whether you’re interested in hiking through the wilderness, exploring local museums, or sampling the region’s delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Sussex. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this vibrant community.
