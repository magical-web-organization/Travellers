Introduction to Saint Andrews

Saint Andrews, located in the Bay of Fundy of New Brunswick, is a charming coastal town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and Scottish heritage. Initially settled by the Mi’kmaq people, Saint Andrews was later inhabited by European explorers in the 17th and 18th centuries. Today, it is a popular vacation destination, offering visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the relaxing and tranquil lifestyle that is characteristic of Atlantic Canada.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Andrews

Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life, Saint Andrews has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of must-visit places in Saint Andrews:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ross Memorial Museum - This museum, housed in a Georgian-style mansion, showcases the history and culture of Saint Andrews.
  • The St. Andrews Blockhouse - Built in 1842, this fort was constructed to defend against American invaders during the Aroostook War.
  • The Charlotte County Museum - This museum features exhibitions on the natural and cultural history of Charlotte County.

Natural Attractions of Saint Andrews: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Ministers Island - Accessible by a gravel road at low tide, this island features the summer home of Sir William Van Horne, former president of Canadian Pacific Railway.
  • The Huntsman Marine Science Centre - This aquarium boasts a variety of marine life and offers educational programs, including whale watching and touch tank experiences.
  • The St. Andrews Biological Station - This research facility is open to the public for tours and features a variety of marine organisms and ecosystems.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Andrews

Saint Andrews is home to many beautiful hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. Whether you enjoy a leisurely stroll along the coastline or a more strenuous trek through the forest, there is a trail for every level of adventurer. Here are a few of the best routes to explore:

  • The Caves Trail - This popular trail takes hikers along the rocky coast of Saint Andrews and offers fantastic views of the ocean and nearby lighthouses.
  • The Fundy Coastal Trail - This bike trail takes riders through the charming village of St. Stephen and along the Bay of Fundy shoreline.
  • Sugar Island Trail - This trail features a diverse range of flora and fauna, as well as stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Saint Andrews: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for fun and unique ways to experience Saint Andrews, here are a few activities and tours to consider:

  • Whale Watching - Saint Andrews is one of the best places in Canada for whale watching. Several companies offer tours, providing visitors a chance to witness humpback, fin, and minke whales in their natural habitat.
  • Golfing - Saint Andrews boasts several world-class golf courses, including the famous Algonquin Resort Golf Course.
  • Pepperpot Lighthouse Tour - This tour offers visitors a chance to learn about the history of Saint Andrews and explore the inside of the iconic Pepperpot Lighthouse.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint Andrews

If you want to experience the best of Saint Andrews over the course of a week, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore downtown Saint Andrews and visit the Ross Memorial Museum.

5 Spend the day at nearby Fundy National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Andrews

Saint Andrews has a variety of dining options and local specialties that are not to be missed. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll - A classic Atlantic Canada dish made with fresh, locally caught lobster and served on a toasted bun.
  • Fish and Chips - Saint Andrews has several restaurants that serve delicious fish and chips, made with crispy batter and fresh, flaky fish.
  • Potato Scallops - A Saint Andrews specialty made with sliced potatoes, lightly battered and fried until crispy.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Saint Andrews has several breweries that offer local craft beers, including the award-winning Trailway Brewing Company.
  • Whisky - The town’s Scottish heritage is reflected in its selection of whisky, with local favorites including the Glen Breton Rare and Glenora Distillery.

Where to Stay in Saint Andrews: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint Andrews has a variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly options. Here are a few suggestions for where to stay:

  • The Algonquin Resort - This historic hotel, built-in 1889, is located in the heart of downtown Saint Andrews and offers a range of amenities, including a spa, restaurants, and an indoor pool.
  • The Picket Fence Motel - This budget-friendly motel is just a short walk from downtown Saint Andrews and features comfortable rooms and a scenic back yard.
  • The Europa Inn - This guesthouse is located in a charming Victorian-era home and features cozy rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a variety of amenities.

What to Buy in Saint Andrews: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Saint Andrews, here are a few suggestions:

  • Sea Glass Jewelry - Saint Andrews is known for its beautiful sea glass, which can be found on the shoreline and turned into unique jewelry pieces.
  • Tartan Accessories - The town’s Scottish heritage is reflected in its selection of tartan accessories, including scarves, ties, and skirts.
  • Handmade Pottery - Saint Andrews has several local artisans that create beautiful, handmade pottery pieces, inspired by the natural beauty of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Andrews

Saint Andrews has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, highlighted by its Scottish roots and proximity to indigenous communities. Visitors to the town can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending festivals and events, such as the annual Scottish Games or the Wabanaki Artist Market.

Best Time to Visit Saint Andrews

Saint Andrews can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is mild, and the town is alive with festivals and events. September and October are also popular months, as the fall foliage is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint Andrews

When visiting Saint Andrews, here are a few things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try local seafood, especially lobster and seafood chowder.
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Do dress in layers, as the weather can change quickly.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Saint Andrews is a safe and healthy destination, with several medical clinics and hospitals located in the nearby city of Saint John. However, visitors should always take precautions when exploring the natural environment, such as wearing appropriate footwear and following safety guidelines when hiking or biking.

Safety in Saint Andrews

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.
  • Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be cautious when hiking or biking, as the terrain can be challenging.

Health in Saint Andrews

  • Be prepared for fluctuating weather conditions by dressing appropriately.
  • Bring any necessary medications and keep them with you at all times.
  • Be aware of any allergies or medical conditions that may require special attention.

Getting to Saint Andrews: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saint Andrews is located approximately 170 km from the nearest airport (Saint John Airport) and is accessible by car, bus, or ferry.

Airports Near Saint Andrews

  • Saint John Airport - Located 90 km from Saint Andrews, this airport offers regular flights to Toronto, Halifax, and Montreal.

Bus Stations Near Saint Andrews

  • Maritime Bus - This bus company offers service to Saint Andrews from various locations in New Brunswick and Nova Scotia.

Other Options

  • Ferry - Visitors can take a ferry from several locations in Maine to nearby Digby, Nova Scotia, and then drive the remaining distance to Saint Andrews.

How to Get Around in Saint Andrews

Visitors to Saint Andrews can get around the town easily on foot, although rental cars and bikes are also available for those who wish to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Saint Andrews: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint Andrews is located near many other beautiful destinations in Atlantic Canada. Here are a few nearby options to consider:

  • Fundy National Park - Located approximately 80 km from Saint Andrews, Fundy National Park features over 100 km of hiking trails and stunning natural beauty.
  • Saint John - Located approximately 100 km from Saint Andrews, Saint John is a historic city with a vibrant arts and culture scene.
  • St. Andrews by-the-Sea - This charming village is located just a few kilometers from Saint Andrews and features beautiful architecture and stunning coastal views.

Conclusion

Saint Andrews is a wonderful destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Atlantic Canada. With its rich history, natural beauty, and Scottish heritage, Saint Andrews has plenty to offer visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you are looking to explore the outdoors, immerse yourself in local culture, or simply relax and unwind, Saint Andrews is the perfect vacation destination.
