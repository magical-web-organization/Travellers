Introduction to Campobello Island

Located in Bay of Fundy, off the coast of New Brunswick, Campobello Island is a hidden gem in Atlantic Canada. It is a quiet and peaceful island with breathtaking natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant culture. The island is famously known as the summer home of President Franklin D. Roosevelt, who loved the island so much he created an international park here. Campobello Island is a perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy a relaxing vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Campobello Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Campobello Island has a rich history that is well-preserved in its museums and historical sites. The top culturally important attractions on the island include:

  • Roosevelt Campobello International Park: This is the most popular attraction on the island and for a good reason. It is a unique international park that honors the legacy of President Franklin Roosevelt and his family. You can tour Roosevelt Cottage, the family’s summer home, as well as a museum showcasing artifacts from their personal collections.

  • The Mulholland Point Lighthouse: Built in 1885, the iconic lighthouse offers a fantastic view of the island’s natural beauty. It is a perfect spot for photo opportunities, picnics, and coastal walks.

  • East Quoddy Lighthouse & Museum: Dubbed as the most photographed lighthouse in Canada, East Quoddy is a picturesque site to visit. The museum offers a perfect opportunity to learn more about traditional lobster fishing and the history of local lighthouses.

Natural Attractions of Campobello Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts will feel right at home on Campobello Island. The island has many natural attractions, including:

  • Herring Cove Provincial Park: Located on the western shore, the park offers visitors a chance to hike, picnic, swim, and even camp. It has a beautiful white sand beach and warm waters that are perfect for swimming or exploring the surrounding Islands.

  • Roosevelt-Campobello International Park: This incredible park offers visitors a chance to walk among the towering spruce, picnic on the lawn of Roosevelt’s summer cottage, and explore the rugged coastline.

  • Liberty Point: Liberty Point provides a spectacular view of the Bay of Fundy, and it is a perfect place to watch whales.
  • Welshpool Provincial Park: A popular destination for boaters, kayakers, and canoers. The park provides a perfect chance to explore the coastline, beaches and islands in the Passamaquoddy bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campobello Island

Campobello Island has a unique trail system perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the island’s natural beauty by following these stunning trails:

  • Roosevelt Campobello International Park Hike: The Eagle Hill trail is an easy trail that allows hikers to explore the park’s natural beauty and Roosevelt Cottage.

  • Welshpool Trails: These trails offer a more varied terrain along the coastline and inland with a 8-kilometer walking and cycling loop.

Best Things to Do in Campobello Island: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour and see whales, sea lions, and other marine wildlife as they swim in their natural habitats. You can also try kayaking, paddleboarding or simply explore the rugged coast on foot.

  • Fishing: Campobello Island has a rich fishing tradition. You can try your luck at catching Atlantic Salmon, Rainbow Trout, and Trout in Lake Glenburn and other waterways.

  • Photography: The island has stunning natural beauty that is perfect for photographers who want to capture memorable moments.

  • Airplane Tours: Take an aerial tour of the island and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the majestic landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Campobello Island

To make the most out of your stay, here is a great itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrival, check-in to your lodging, and explore the city center.

  • Day 2: Visit Roosevelt Campobello International Park in the morning and the Mulholland Point Lighthouse in the afternoon.

  • Day 3: Hike some trails and beaches such as Eagle Hill Trail, Herring Cove Provincial Park, or Liberty Point.

  • Day 4: Take a whale watching tour and enjoy the scenery from the water.

  • Day 5: Take an airplane tour and get a scenic view of the whole island.

  • Day 6: Visit some local shops and try out some of the foods and drinks that Campobello Island has to offer.

  • Day 7: Departure, say goodbye to the island and head back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Campobello Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster: Campobello Island is known for its delicious and fresh lobsters. You can taste them in one of the local restaurants or even catch them yourself and cook fresh with family and friends.

  • Herring: A delicacy for the island, a smoked herring is a must-try for visitors who want to explore the local cuisine on the island.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Ale: Try a bottle of maple-flavored beer or cider, which is unique to the island.

Where to Stay in Campobello Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Campobello Island has many lodging options that cater to different budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Roosevelt Campobello International Park: The park has multiple lodging options that range from campsites to hotel-like rooms.

  • The Oceanfront Oasis Resort: A family-friendly resort with ocean views and a variety of rooms available.

  • Historic Welshpool Hall: A rustic guesthouse built in 1879 that features simple, self-catering rooms with a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Campobello Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery: Purchase one-of-a-kind pottery made by local artists.

  • Homemade Jams: Try some delicious homemade jams made with locally-sourced fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Campobello Island

The people on Campobello Island are friendly and hospitable. It is advisable to show respect to the local customs and culture. Always ask permission before taking pictures of locals and respect their privacy.

Best Time to Visit Campobello Island

The best time to visit Campobello Island is between June to August when the weather is beautiful and perfect for outdoor activities. The island has a temperate climate, with mild summers and winters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campobello Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment of the island

  • Observe the local customs and respect cultural practices

  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellents, and comfortable clothing to protect yourself while outdoors

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or waste

  • Smoke or litter on the beaches, parks, or public areas

  • Disrespect local traditions, cultures, or customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Campobello Island

Campobello Island is generally safe for visitors. However, it is recommended to take extra precautions while near the water, hiking, or doing any outdoor activities. It is also advisable to bring or rent a car on the island as public transport is limited.

Health in Campobello Island

Healthcare on the island is limited, and it is advisable to carry medical insurance or cash to cover any medical expenses.

Where to Go Next from Campobello Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Charlotte County: Known for its lush scenery, the county boasts various natural attractions such as the Bay of Fundy and the Roosevelt Campobello International Park.

Conclusion

Campobello Island is an excellent destination for those who want to explore nature and learn about traditional fishing communities. It has a rich history and a vibrant culture that is reflected in its museums and local food scene.
