Introduction to St. Stephen

St. Stephen is a picturesque town located in Bay of Fundy, New Brunswick, Atlantic Canada. The town is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. St. Stephen is the perfect destination for travelers who want to explore the beauty of rural Canada, experience local culture, and indulge in delicious food and beverages. This comprehensive guide will give you a complete picture of St. Stephen, including the best places to visit, exciting activities, and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in St. Stephen

St. Stephen is home to many culturally significant attractions, natural beauty spots, and exciting adventures. Here are some must-visit places in St. Stephen.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Croix Island International Historic Site: This site marks the point where French explorers founded a settlement in 1604. The site has a museum, hiking trail, and stunning views of the Bay of Fundy.
  • Chocolate Museum: Experience the mouth-watering history of chocolate production at Canada’s first chocolate museum. The museum showcases the history of chocolate making and offers delicious chocolate samples.
  • Ganong Chocolate Factory: Visit the oldest chocolate company in Canada and learn about the history of chocolate production. The factory offers guided tours and chocolate making workshops.
  • Charlotte County Courthouse: The courthouse dates back to the 19th century and is a beautiful example of Victorian-era architecture. The building now serves as a museum and showcases the history of Charlotte County and the court system.

Natural Attractions of St. Stephen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. Croix River: Take a scenic walk alongside St. Croix River, which runs through the town and offers stunning views of the Bay of Fundy.
  • Minister’s Island: Accessible only during low tide, this unique island offers a glimpse into the life of Sir William Van Horne, one of Canada’s most prominent railway tycoons.
  • Spednic Lake Provincial Park: Enjoy hiking, swimming, and canoeing in the pristine Spednic Lake Provincial Park, located just a few miles from St. Stephen.
  • Jarea Art Studio and Gallery: Experience the natural beauty of St. Stephen through art and painting at the Jarea art studio and gallery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Stephen

St. Stephen has many excellent trails and routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes.

  • St. Croix Island Trail: This trail is a beautiful 1.2-mile loop trail that offers stunning views of the Bay of Fundy.
  • St. Croix River Trail: This trail is a 3-mile scenic route that follows the St. Croix River and offers breathtaking views.
  • Charlotte County Trail: This 39-mile multi-use trail is ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trail passes through several small towns and offers stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in St. Stephen: Activities and Tours

From kayaking to whale watching, there are many exciting activities and tours to explore in St. Stephen. Here are some of the top things to do in St Stephen:

  • Whale watching tour: Get up close and personal with humpback whales and other marine life on the Bay of Fundy.
  • Kayaking tour: Paddle through the breathtaking St. Croix River and surrounding waters.
  • Tidal Bore Rafting: Experience the thrill of rafting on the rapids created by the world’s highest tides in Bay of Fundy.
  • Guided Chocolate Tour: Take a guided tour of Canada’s oldest chocolate factory and learn everything there is to know about chocolate making in St. Stephen.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Stephen

Day 1: Visit St. Croix Island International Historic Site and hike the trail.

Day 2: Explore the Charlotte County Courthouse Museum and learn about the history of the court system.

Day 3: Go on a whale watching tour and experience the beauty of Bay of Fundy.

Day 4: Visit the Ganong Chocolate Factory and learn about the history of chocolate making in Canada.

Day 5: Go on a kayaking tour of the St. Croix River and explore the stunning scenery.

Day 6: Take a trip to Minister’s Island and explore the historic island during low tide.

Day 7: Relax and explore the town, try local cuisine, and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Stephen

St. Stephen’s food and drink scene is a treat for any foodie and beverage lover. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in St. Stephen.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty and flavorful chowder made with fresh seafood caught in the Bay of Fundy.
  • Lobster Rolls: The classic Canadian dish made with fresh lobster meat served in a toasted roll.
  • Blueberry Grunt: A delicious dessert made with fresh blueberries topped with a biscuit or dumpling.

Local Beverages

  • Moosehead Beer: This Canadian beer has been brewed in St. John, New Brunswick, since 1867.
  • Appie Juice: A refreshing and locally produced sparkling apple juice.

Where to Stay in St. Stephen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Stephen has several accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in St. Stephen.

  • Winsome Inn: A charming inn located in the heart of the town offering comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Coastal Inn: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms and great views of the Bay of Fundy.
  • The Chocolate Museum B&B: A unique accommodation option located above Canada’s first chocolate museum.

What to Buy in St. Stephen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Stephen offers a range of souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home to loved ones or as a keepsake. Here are some of the best options.

  • Ganong chocolates: Buy some of Canada’s oldest and most delicious chocolates to bring back home.
  • Local art: St. Stephen has a thriving art scene, and you can find beautiful paintings and sculptures at local galleries.
  • Handmade crafts: Artisans in St. Stephen make many unique crafts, including pottery and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Stephen

St. Stephen has a rich local culture, and the people are known for their hospitality and warmth. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind.

  • English is the most common language in St. Stephen, although some locals may speak French.
  • Canadians are friendly and polite, so treat everyone with respect and kindness.
  • St. Stephen has festivals and events that celebrate the local culture and heritage, so make sure to check if there are any events happening during your visit.

Best Time to Visit St. Stephen

The best time to visit St. Stephen is from May to October when the weather is pleasant, and there are many outdoor activities to explore. Summers are warm and sunny, making it a great time for hiking, biking, and kayaking. Fall is the perfect time to enjoy the changing colors of the leaves and attend the many festivals and events in the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Stephen

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of St. Stephen.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in natural sites or on the streets.
  • Be impatient or rude to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Stephen

St. Stephen is a safe and quiet town, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions while traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind.

  • Lock your valuables in a safe place.
  • Do not leave your belongings unattended in public places.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.

Health in St. Stephen

St. Stephen has a good healthcare system, and there are clinics and hospitals available in case of emergency. Make sure to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to St. Stephen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Stephen

The nearest airport to St. Stephen is the Saint John Airport, located about an hour’s drive from the town. The airport has regular flights from Toronto and other Canadian cities.

Bus Stations Near St. Stephen

St. Stephen has a bus station that connects it to other towns and cities in New Brunswick and Canada.

Other Options

There are several car rental options in Saint John and Fredericton, which are the nearest big cities to St. Stephen. You can also take a taxi or hire a private car to get to St. Stephen from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in St. Stephen

St. Stephen is a small town, and you can visit most attractions on foot. Bicycles and cars are also available for rent, and there are buses available to get around town.

Where to Go Next from St. Stephen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Stephen is a great starting point for exploring nearby towns, cities, and natural attractions. Here are some popular destinations to visit.

  • Saint John, New Brunswick: A charming port city located about an hour’s drive from St. Stephen. Saint John is known for its historic architecture and vibrant arts scene.
  • Fundy National Park: A beautiful national park located about two hours from St. Stephen. The park offers hiking trails, scenic drives, and majestic waterfalls.
  • Bay of Fundy: Explore the stunning coastline and witness the world’s highest tides in action.

Conclusion

St. Stephen is a hidden gem located in Bay of Fundy, New Brunswick. This charming town offers a unique combination of natural beauty, local culture, and exciting activities. From museums to hiking trails, from whale watching to chocolate making, St. Stephen has something for everyone. Plan your trip to this picturesque town and explore the wonders of rural Canada.
