Introduction to Blackville

Located in the picturesque Miramichi River Valley, Blackville is a charming village in New Brunswick, Canada. With its breathtaking natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals, Blackville is the perfect destination for anyone seeking a relaxing getaway from the hustle and bustle of daily life. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply want to unwind and soak up the serene surroundings, Blackville has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Blackville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Blackville is steeped in history, and visitors can explore the village’s past by visiting the following museums and historical sites:

  • Blackville Heritage Bridge & Riverbank Trail: Take a stroll across the wooden walking bridge that overlooks the Miramichi River and enjoy the scenic views from the riverbank trail.

  • Blackville Municipal Park: A great spot for picnics, fishing, and swimming, this park is an ideal spot for families and nature lovers.

  • Blackville Railway Station Museum: The former railway station features exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of the area’s railroad history and its impact on the local community.

  • Miramichi Salmon Conservation Centre: Learn about the Atlantic salmon and its remarkable journey from ocean to river at this fascinating conservation center.

Natural Attractions of Blackville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blackville is blessed with natural beauty, and visitors can take part in the following activities, and enjoy the sights and scenery:

  • Wilson’s Point Provincial Park: Set on the banks of the Miramichi River, this park is a popular spot for birdwatching, hiking, and picnics.

  • Upper Oxbow Outdoor Adventures: Take a guided fly-fishing trip and try your luck catching Atlantic salmon, trout, or smallmouth bass.

  • Bicentennial Park: This park has several walking trails that lead to spectacular waterfalls, such as the Salmon Shoals Falls, Big Hole Salmon Brook falls, and Broadleafs Falls.

  • The Giant Axe: This 11-meter-tall sculpture of an axe was created by artist Peter Mitchell. The sculpture pays homage to the village’s logging industry and the settlers who cleared the land.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blackville

Blackville offers several hiking and biking trails that provide an excellent opportunity to explore the region’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Blackville Community Trail
  • Blackville Heritage Bridge & Riverbank Trail
  • Wilson’s Point Trails
  • Central New Brunswick Woodmen’s Museum Trail
  • Renous Trail and Boardwalk

Best Things to Do in Blackville: Activities and Tours

Blackville has plenty of attractions and activities that visitors can enjoy throughout the year. Some of the popular things to do include:

  • Salmon Fishing: With several rivers and streams teeming with Atlantic salmon, Blackville is a top fishing destination. Visitors can try their hand at fly-fishing or hire a local guide to take them on a fishing trip.

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The Miramichi River is great for paddling, and visitors can rent canoes and kayaks or take a guided tour.

  • Golf: Blackville has two golf courses, Blackville Golf and Country Club, and Miramichi Golf and Country Club.

  • Snowmobiling and Snowshoeing: During the winter months, visitors can enjoy snowmobiling on the groomed trails or take a scenic snowshoe hike in the woods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blackville

Day 1: Visit Blackville Railway Station Museum, Miramichi Salmon Conservation Centre, and Wilson’s Point Provincial Park.

Day 2: Go fishing or take a canoe/kayak ride on the Miramichi River.

Day 3: Explore the Broadleafs Falls, Salmon Shoals Falls, and Big Hole Salmon Brook falls in Bicentennial Park.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Upper Oxbow Outdoor Adventures.

Day 5: Visit the Central New Brunswick Woodmen’s Museum Trail and try snowshoeing in the winter.

Day 6: Play a round of golf at Blackville Golf and Country Club or Miramichi Golf and Country Club.

Day 7: Enjoy a picnic at Blackville Municipal Park and take a leisurely stroll across the Blackville Heritage Bridge & Riverbank Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Blackville

Must-Try Dishes

Blackville is known for its seafood dishes, such as the Atlantic salmon, lobster, and scallops. Other local delicacies include blueberry and apple pies, maple syrup, and fiddleheads. Some of the best places to try these dishes include:

  • Seaside Seafoods: Known for its fresh seafood, this restaurant is a great place to sample the area’s most popular dishes.

  • Northern Restaurant: This family-owned diner serves hearty breakfasts and comfort food classics.

  • Wild Blueberry Cafe: This charming cafe serves homemade blueberry pies, jams, and other blueberry-themed snacks and drinks.

Local Beverages

When visiting Blackville, don’t miss the opportunity to try some of the local drinks and brews. Some of the popular beverages in the area include:

  • Maple syrup: Blackville produces Canada’s finest maple syrup, which is used to sweeten everything from pancakes to cocktails.

  • Moosehead Breweries: Take a tour of the Moosehead Breweries and sample some of their famous brews.

Where to Stay in Blackville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blackville offers various accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, including:

  • Country Haven Lodge and Cottages: This luxurious lodge offers accommodation in cottages or guest rooms, and amenities such as a hot tub, a small gym, and a game room.

  • Lakeview Lodge and Cottages: Set on the shores of Lake O’Donnell, this family-owned lodge offers cozy cabins and fantastic lake views.

  • Blackville Motel: A budget-friendly motel offering comfortable rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Blackville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Blackville with you by purchasing some of the locally made products and souvenirs. Some of the items to consider include:

  • Maple syrup: Made locally, Blackville’s maple syrup is a must-buy souvenir.

  • Handmade crafts: Many local artisans create unique and beautiful crafts, such as quilts, pottery, and woodcarvings.

  • Blackville T-shirts and souvenirs: The village has several gift shops selling Blackville-themed clothing and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Blackville

Blackville residents are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors are often greeted with a smile and a warm welcome. The village also has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through a range of events and festivals throughout the year, such as the Blackville Mango Festival, the Harvest Festival, and the Christmas Tree Lighting Ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Blackville

Blackville has something to offer visitors all year round, but the best time to visit depends on what you want to do and see. Summer is the busiest season, and the village is filled with outdoor enthusiasts, anglers, and nature lovers. Fall is a beautiful time to visit, with the changing leaves creating a stunning backdrop for hiking and exploring. Winter provides the opportunity to take part in winter sports and activities, such as snowmobiling and snowshoeing. Spring is a great time to visit for birdwatching and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blackville

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take part in local events and celebrations.

  • Try the seafood and local beverages.

  • Explore the beautiful hiking trails and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blackville

Blackville is a safe and welcoming place for visitors, but it’s always essential to take precautions when traveling. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Always lock your vehicle and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.

  • Wear insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

  • Keep an eye on the weather forecast and take appropriate precautions during extreme weather conditions.

Health in Blackville

Blackville has excellent medical facilities, including several hospitals and clinics in the nearby cities of Miramichi and Fredericton. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Blackville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blackville

The closest airport to Blackville is the Greater Moncton International Airport, which is about a 2-hour drive away. Other nearby airports include Fredericton International Airport and Saint John Airport.

Bus Stations Near Blackville

Maritime Bus provides regular bus services to Blackville from cities such as Fredericton, Moncton, and Saint John.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car from the nearby cities or take a taxi or private car service to Blackville.

How to Get Around in Blackville

Blackville is a small village, and many of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also hire a car or bike or take a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Blackville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blackville is situated in a picturesque part of New Brunswick, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions for visitors to explore. Some of the popular places that visitors can visit include:

  • Miramichi River: Take a scenic drive along the Miramichi River and explore the charming towns and villages along the way.

  • Kouchibouguac National Park: This beautiful national park offers hiking trails, beaches, and several other outdoor activities.

  • Fredericton: The capital city of New Brunswick is known for its historical sites, museums, and galleries.

Conclusion

Blackville is a hidden gem in New Brunswick, Canada, that offers visitors an opportunity to experience the beauty of the Miramichi River Valley and the rich culture of the local community.
