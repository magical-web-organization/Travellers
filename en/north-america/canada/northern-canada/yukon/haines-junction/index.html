Introduction to Haines Junction

Nestled in the southwestern part of the Yukon, Haines Junction is a small community with a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. Surrounded by stunning mountain ranges, and overlooking the stunning Kluane National Park, this hidden gem has so much to offer for those looking for an adventure.

Whether you’re seeking outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Haines Junction should be on your travel list. This guide will take you through the best places to visit, activities to try, and everything else you need to know to plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Haines Junction

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Kluane Museum of History

Discover the history of the local First Nations cultures and the pioneering spirit of the early European explorers who settled the area. Explore interactive exhibits and displays that showcase the history of the region and its inhabitants.

The Alaska Highway Interpretive Centre

Learn about the construction of the Alaska Highway and how it impacted the area’s history and culture. The centre has exhibits that showcase the hardships, struggles, and triumphs of those involved in building the highway.

Natural Attractions of Haines Junction: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kluane National Park and Reserve

Kluane National Park and Reserve is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a nature lover’s paradise. The park is home to some of North America’s most stunning scenery, including glaciers, mountains, and abundant wildlife. Take a hiking or camping trip and experience the rugged beauty of this pristine wilderness.

Kluane Lake

Kluane Lake is the largest lake in the Yukon and a popular spot for fishing, boating, and camping. Take a dip in the crystal clear waters or explore the shoreline on a hike. The lake is also a great spot for a relaxing picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haines Junction

The Cottonwood Trail

This hiking trail is a great option for those looking for a relatively easy hike with stunning views. The trail winds through a peaceful forest and offers amazing views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

The King’s Throne Trail

For the more adventurous hikers, the King’s Throne Trail is a challenging but highly rewarding hike. The trail leads to a stunning vista overlooking the Kluane National Park, and the breathtaking scenery makes it well worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Haines Junction: Activities and Tours

Flightseeing Tours

Take to the skies and witness the stunning beauty of the area from above. A flightseeing tour of Haines Junction gives you a unique and unforgettable perspective of the mountain ranges, glaciers, and wildlife below.

Rafting and Kayaking

Experience the rush of adventure with a white water rafting or kayaking trip in one of the nearby rivers. With options for both beginners and experienced adventurers, there’s something for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of Haines Junction

Must-Try Dishes

Bannock

Bannock is a traditional First Nations bread that’s similar to a biscuit. It’s a staple food in Haines Junction, and you’ll find it served at many local restaurants and cafes.

Arctic Char

Arctic char is a fish that’s commonly found in local rivers and lakes. It’s a delicious and healthy option that’s often served grilled or smoked.

Local Beverages

Yukon Brewing

Yukon Brewing is a local brewery that produces a range of high-quality beers. The brewery offers tours and tastings for visitors looking to sample some of the best beers in the region.

Where to Stay in Haines Junction: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

The Pine Tree Motel

The Pine Tree Motel is a family-owned and operated motel that offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices. The motel is conveniently located in the heart of Haines Junction and is within walking distance of many local attractions.

Hostels

The Raven’s Nest Hostel

The Raven’s Nest Hostel is a popular choice for budget-conscious travellers. It offers dorm-style accommodations and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge area.

Guesthouses

Kluane Green Sprout

The Kluane Green Sprout is a unique and eco-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodations in a beautiful natural setting. The guesthouse is powered by solar panels and is surrounded by gardens and wildlife.

What to Buy in Haines Junction: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

First Nations Artwork

Haines Junction is home to many talented First Nations artists and craftspeople. You’ll find a wide selection of handmade jewellery, pottery, and artwork at local shops and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Haines Junction

The local culture of Haines Junction is heavily influenced by the traditional First Nations cultures that have inhabited the area for centuries. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the history and customs of the local First Nations communities and to respect their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Haines Junction

Summer months (from June to August) are the best time to visit Haines Junction. The weather is mild, and the days are long, giving visitors plenty of time to explore the local attractions and natural wonders.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haines Junction

Do:

  • Respect the local First Nations cultures and customs
  • Take advantage of local adventure activities and tours
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local wildlife or environment
  • Ignore trail warnings or safety guidelines
  • Forget to carry bear spray when hiking or camping in the area

Safety and Health

Safety in Haines Junction

Haines Junction is a generally safe community, but visitors should take normal precautions such as locking their valuables and cars when parking overnight. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and camping, and should take necessary safety precautions.

Health in Haines Junction

The community has essential health services, pharmacy, and dental services. Visitors should always carry their health insurance card and contact their primary care provider before travelling. Personal protective gear such as mosquito repellant and sunscreen may be necessary when hiking and camping.

Getting to Haines Junction: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haines Junction

The nearest airport is the Whitehorse International Airport, which is about a two-hour drive from Haines Junction. Car rentals and shuttle services are available at the airport for those looking to explore the area.

Bus Stations Near Haines Junction

Greyhound bus service goes through Whitehorse, and visitors can hire cab service or shuttle from Whitehorse to Haines Junction. Bear in mind that bus travel to Haines Junction requires strict adherence to schedules.

Other Options

For visitors looking for a unique experience, the White Pass and Yukon Route railroad offers a scenic train ride through the Alaskan and Canadian wilderness. The train’s southern line goes between Skagway, Alaska, and Carcross, just north of Whitehorse.

How to Get Around in Haines Junction

The best way to get around Haines Junction is by car as this offers visitors maximum flexibility. Rental cars are available at the Whitehorse International Airport, or visitors can opt for local taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Haines Junction: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kluane Lake

Take a scenic drive to Kluane Lake and explore its stunning scenery. The lake is home to many outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking.

Haines, Alaska

Take a short road trip to Haines, Alaska, and explore its rich history and culture. The city is home to many museums, galleries, and shops, and the surrounding countryside offers a wealth of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Haines Junction is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re seeking outdoor activities, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, this small Canadian community has something for everyone. Plan your visit today, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
