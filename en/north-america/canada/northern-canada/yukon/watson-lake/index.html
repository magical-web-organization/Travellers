Introduction to Watson Lake

Watson Lake is a small town located in the southeastern part of Yukon, Canada. It has a population of around 800 people and is known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and interesting tourist attractions. There are plenty of things to do and places to see in Watson Lake, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts and culture lovers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Watson Lake

Here’s a list of some of the most popular tourist attractions in Watson Lake:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sign Post Forest - This is a unique attraction where visitors can see thousands of signs from all over the world. The Sign Post Forest started in 1942 when a soldier posted a sign pointing to his hometown on a wayward post. Since then, it has become a tradition for visitors to add their own signs to the forest.
  • Northern Lights Centre - This is a planetarium-style theatre that showcases the beauty and science of the Northern Lights. It’s a great place to learn about the Aurora Borealis and the history of Yukon.
  • Watson Lake Historical Society Museum - This museum exhibits the history of Watson Lake and the surrounding area. It has an impressive collection of artifacts, photos, and documents that tell the story of the town’s development.

Natural Attractions of Watson Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Watson Lake - This lake is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and swimming. It’s surrounded by beautiful forests and is a great place to relax and enjoy the scenery.
  • Mount Maichen - This mountain offers stunning views of Watson Lake and the surrounding area. It’s a great hiking spot, and visitors can choose from a variety of trails that range in difficulty.
  • Yukon Beringia Interpretive Centre - This centre offers visitors a chance to learn about the unique land and animals that once roamed the Beringia area. It has interactive exhibits, a movie theatre, and walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watson Lake

Watson Lake has many hiking and biking trails that range in difficulty, length, and terrain. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Mount Decoli Trail - This is a challenging 4.4 km trail that takes hikers to the summit of Mount Decoli. The views from the top are breathtaking.
  • Watson Lake Loop Trail - This is an easy 4.3 km trail that follows the shore of Watson Lake. It’s a great place to go for a picnic or a bike ride.
  • Carcross Desert Trail - This is a moderate 8.6 km trail that takes hikers through the Carcross Desert, which is the smallest desert in the world. It’s a unique and beautiful experience.

Best Things to Do in Watson Lake: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Watson Lake, no matter what your interests are. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Fishing - Watson Lake is known for its excellent fishing opportunities. Visitors can catch lake trout, Northern Pike, and Whitefish.
  • Northern Lights Viewing - In the winter months, visitors can witness the mesmerizing beauty of the Northern Lights. There are many tour companies that offer night-time viewing adventures.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking - Watson Lake is a great place to go canoeing and kayaking. Visitors can explore the lake and its many islands and coves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watson Lake

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Watson Lake:

Day 1: Visit the Watson Lake Sign Post Forest and the Northern Lights Centre. Day 2: Hike the Mount Decoli Trail and explore the town. Day 3: Go fishing on Watson Lake and relax at the beach. Day 4: Take a tour of the Yukon Beringia Interpretive Centre and go for a bike ride on the Watson Lake Loop Trail. Day 5: Visit the Watson Lake Historical Society Museum and go for a scenic drive. Day 6: Canoe or kayak on Watson Lake and enjoy the natural beauty of the area. Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations like Whitehorse or Skagway.

Food and Drink Scene of Watson Lake

When it comes to food and drink, Watson Lake has a small but vibrant scene, with a few local favorites worth trying.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock - This is a traditional indigenous bread, which can be sweet or savory and is often served with a variety of toppings.
  • Fish and Chips - Watson Lake is known for its excellent fishing, so fish and chips are always a popular dish.
  • Caribou Stew - This is a warming and hearty stew made with caribou meat, potatoes, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Yukon Brewing - This brewery uses local ingredients to create unique and flavorful beers, ranging from pale ales to stouts.
  • Northern Lights Chaga Tea - This tea is made from the Chaga mushroom, which is found in the forests of Yukon. It’s rich in antioxidants and has a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Watson Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Watson Lake has a variety of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the most popular choices:

  • Airbnb - Visitors can rent cozy and affordable apartments or rooms from local hosts.
  • Air Force Lodge - This lodge offers comfortable and affordable rooms, with amenities like a restaurant, bar, and hot tub.
  • Watson Lake Wilderness Village - This is a unique eco-friendly resort that offers a range of accommodation options, from canvas tents to yurts and cabins.

What to Buy in Watson Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Watson Lake:

  • Signs from the Sign Post Forest - Visitors can purchase their own sign from the forest, or choose from a selection of signs for sale at the gift shop.
  • Artisanal Crafts - Yukon has a thriving artisanal scene, with many local makers creating beautiful and unique products like pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Yukon Gold - These potatoes are famous for their rich flavor and smooth texture. Visitors can buy bags of Yukon Gold potatoes to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Watson Lake

Watson Lake is home to several distinct indigenous cultures, including the Liard First Nation, the Kaska Nation, and the Tlingit Nation. Visitors should be respectful of these cultures, and take time to learn about their history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Watson Lake

The best time to visit Watson Lake is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the hours of daylight are long. However, visitors can also enjoy the beauty of Watson Lake during the winter months, when the Northern Lights are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Watson Lake

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Watson Lake:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Bring warm clothes, even during the summer months.
  • Upgrade your vehicle to handle the rugged terrain.

Don’t:

  • Damage the natural environment.
  • Dispose of waste improperly.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting Watson Lake:

Safety in Watson Lake

  • Be aware of wildlife, including bears and moose, and take appropriate precautions.
  • Bring a reliable GPS or map, as cellular service can be limited.
  • Carry bear spray and know how to use it.

Health in Watson Lake

  • Ensure your routine vaccinations are up to date.
  • Practice safe food and water precautions, especially when camping or hiking.
  • Take insect precautions during the summer months.

Getting to Watson Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Watson Lake is located on the Alaska Highway, which is accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Watson Lake

  • Watson Lake Airport - This is a small airport that serves the town of Watson Lake. It has flights to and from Whitehorse, Yukon, and other regional airports.

Bus Stations Near Watson Lake

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Greyhound offers service to Watson Lake from Whitehorse and other regional cities.

Other Options

  • Driving - Watson Lake is accessible by car via the Alaska Highway.
  • Cycling - The Alaska Highway is a popular cycling route, and Watson Lake offers several services for cyclists.

How to Get Around in Watson Lake

Visitors can explore Watson Lake by car, bike, or foot. The town is small and walkable, but a car is recommended for longer trips and visits to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Watson Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Watson Lake can explore many nearby attractions and destinations, including:

  • Whitehorse - The capital city of Yukon is just a few hours’ drive from Watson Lake. It offers many attractions, including the Yukon Wildlife Preserve and the SS Klondike National Historic Site.
  • Skagway - This historic city in Alaska is just a short drive from Watson Lake. It offers many attractions, including the Klondike Gold Rush National Historical Park and the White Pass and Yukon Route Railway.
  • Liard River Hot Springs - These natural hot springs are located just a few hours’ drive from Watson Lake and are a perfect place to relax and unwind.

Conclusion

Watson Lake is a unique and beautiful destination that offers plenty of things to do and see. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or just a relaxing getaway, Watson Lake has something for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be well-prepared for a memorable trip to this stunning part of Canada.
