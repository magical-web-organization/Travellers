Introduction to Dawson City

Nestled in the heart of Canada’s Yukon region, Dawson City is a destination with a rich history and natural beauty. Founded in 1896 during the Klondike Gold Rush, it quickly grew into one of the largest cities in Canada’s Yukon Territory, with a population of over 30,000. Today, it is a vibrant hub for tourism, with plenty to see and do for visitors of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Dawson City

Dawson City has plenty of cultural and natural attractions to visit, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dawson City has a rich history, and visitors can explore it through its many museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Dawson City Museum: Located downtown, this museum has displays on the Klondike Gold Rush, First Nations culture, and more.

  • Robert Service Cabin: The cabin where famous poet Robert Service lived is now a museum. Visitors can learn about his life and works.

  • Dawson City Cemetery: This historic cemetery dates back to the time of the gold rush and has graves of many famous people.

Natural Attractions of Dawson City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dawson City is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, and visitors can explore it through hiking, boating, and other outdoor activities. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Klondike River: This winding river is ideal for boating and fishing. Visitors can rent a boat or join a river tour.

  • Midnight Dome: This mountaintop lookout provides stunning views of the city and surrounding landscape.

  • Tombstone Territorial Park: Located just outside Dawson City, this park has hiking trails that offer views of mountains and glaciers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dawson City

Dawson City has several hiking and biking trails that visitors can explore. Some of the best routes include:

  • Klondike National Historic Site: This is a short hike with a lot of history. The site includes the part of the original Chilkoot Trail where stampeders climbed the dreaded Golden Stairs.

  • Dempster Highway: This 736 km road is perfect for bikers who want to challenge themselves. It is one of the world’s most beautiful highways, with views of Mackenzie Delta, Richardson Mountains, Eagle Plains, and Blackstone Uplands.

  • Klondike River Trail: This is a moderate-level hiking trail that follows the historic Klondike River and provides stunning views of surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Dawson City: Activities and Tours

Dawson City offers plenty to see and do for visitors, including:

  • Gold Panning: One of the most popular activities in Dawson City, visitors can try their luck at this historic gold panning.

  • Sourtoe Cocktail Club: Visitors can join this iconic club by taking a shot of whisky with a preserved human toe in it.

  • Diamond Tooth Gerties: This historic casino offers nightly shows, gambling, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dawson City

For visitors who want to make the most of their visit, here is a week-long itinerary for Dawson City:

Day 1: Arrival and Downtown Exploration

  • Arrive in Dawson City and explore the historic downtown.

Day 2: Cultural Attractions and Hiking

  • Visit the Dawson City Museum and Robert Service Cabin.
  • Take a short hike on the Klondike National Historic Site.

Day 3: Gold Rush History and Scenic Views

  • Visit the Gold Rush Cemetery.
  • Go up to Midnight Dome for panoramic views.

Day 4: Natural Attractions and Outdoor Activities

  • Visit Tombstone Territorial Park and hike the trails.
  • Take a river tour on Klondike River.

Day 5: Shopping and Dining

  • Shop for souvenirs and local products.
  • Enjoy some local cuisine at one of the many restaurants or food trucks.

Day 6: Adventure and Exploration

  • Go gold panning or join a guided tour.
  • Take the Diamond Tooth Gerties’ show and spend the night out on the town.

Day 7: Departure and Nearby Destinations

  • Depart from Dawson City or visit one of the nearby destinations like Whitehorse or Juneau.

Food and Drink Scene of Dawson City

Dawson City has a diverse food and drink scene, with many unique experiences to try. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock: A traditional First Nations food, it is a type of bread made with flour, salt, baking powder, and water cooked over an open fire.
  • Arctic Char: A fish native to the North, it is often grilled or fried and served with local vegetables.
  • Klondike Corn Dogs: These popular snacks are hot dogs coated in cornmeal batter and fried.

Local Beverages

  • Yukon Gold Beer: A locally brewed beer made with Yukon barley.
  • Wine from the Northern Vineyard: A unique vineyard in Dawson City, which grows grapes despite the cold climate.

Where to Stay in Dawson City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dawson City has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style. Some of the popular options include:

  • Aurora Inn: A cozy, centrally-located inn with rooms and suites.
  • Bombay Peggy’s: A boutique hotel with themed rooms and a lively bar.
  • Triple J Hotel: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Dawson City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dawson City is known for its arts and crafts, and visitors can find plenty of unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the best things to buy in Dawson City include:

  • Mukluks: Traditional footwear made of animal hides and fur, perfect for the cold Canadian winters.

  • Klondike gold: Visitors can purchase gold nuggets and gold dust found by local miners.

  • First Nations Art: Visitors can buy art and crafts made by the local First Nations people, including jewelry, carvings, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Dawson City

Dawson City has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the Klondike Gold Rush, the First Nations people, and more. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices and learn about the local culture through museums, events, and conversations.

Best Time to Visit Dawson City

The best time to visit Dawson City is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, visitors can also enjoy the winter activities from December to March, including dog sledding and Northern Lights viewing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dawson City

Visitors should keep in mind the following do’s and don’ts during their visit to Dawson City:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress in layers to prepare for the changing weather.
  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Touch or disturb wildlife.
  • Leave food or garbage outside, as it can attract bears.

Safety and Health

Dawson City is a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take normal safety precautions, including keeping an eye on their belongings and following traffic rules. As for health, visitors should be prepared for the weather and terrain, carry necessary medications and first aid kit, and take precautions against the mosquitoes.

Getting to Dawson City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dawson City is located in the Yukon Territory of Canada, and visitors can reach it by air, road, or water. Here are some of the transportation options and logistics to plan:

Airports Near Dawson City

The closest airport to Dawson City is the Dawson City Airport (YDA), which offers regular flights to and from Whitehorse and Inuvik.

Bus Stations Near Dawson City

There are no direct bus services to Dawson City. However, visitors can take the Yukon Express Bus from Whitehorse to Dawson City, or from Dawson City to Inuvik.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Dawson City by road, taking the Dempster Highway that links Inuvik and Dawson City. It is a gravel road that is only open during the summer months. Another option is to take a riverboat tour from Whitehorse to Dawson City along the Yukon River.

How to Get Around in Dawson City

Dawson City is a small, walkable city, with most attractions located within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars to explore further afield. There are no public transportation options within the city.

Where to Go Next from Dawson City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After visiting Dawson City, visitors can explore some of the nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Whitehorse: The capital city of the Yukon Territory, Whitehorse, is a short distance from Dawson City and offers many more attractions.

  • Kluane National Park: Known for its towering mountains, glaciers, and wildlife, the park is located to the west of Dawson City.

  • Juneau: Visitors can take a ferry from Skagway, Alaska, to Juneau, the capital of Alaska, for historical attractions, whale watching, and more.

Conclusion

Dawson City is a unique and memorable destination that offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking to soak up the local culture, Dawson City has something to offer.
