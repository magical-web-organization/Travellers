Introduction to Vuntut National Park

Located in the heart of the Arctic Refuge, Vuntut National Park is a hidden gem on the northernmost border of Canada. With more than 4,345 square kilometers of pristine arctic wilderness, the park is home to breathtaking landscapes, unique northern flora, and ancient traditions of the Vuntut Gwitchin people. Come and discover the hidden treasures of the Canadian Arctic in Vuntut National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Vuntut National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Crow - The administrative center and home to the Vuntut Gwitchin people, visitors can learn about the community, culture, and customs by speaking with locals and participating in community activities.

Natural Attractions of Vuntut National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fishing Adventures - With several rivers running through the park, fishing is a popular activity for catching arctic char and grayling.
  • Vuntut Gwitchin Cultural Centre - The cultural center exhibits artifacts, artworks, and interactive displays reflecting the culture, history, and values of Vuntut Gwitchin people.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vuntut National Park

  • Crowberry Trail - A 5km easy trail, this route is dotted with opportunities to see wildlife
  • Aklavik Crosstrail - A stunning route which takes bikers through a wide range of Arctic ecosystems.

Best Things to Do in Vuntut National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Arctic Villages Tour - Learn about Inuit culture and life at the most northerly Inuit communities of the world
  • Northern Lights Tour - Observe the stunning display of colorful Northern Lights and marvel at the grandeur of the Canadian Arctic.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vuntut National Park

Day 1: Explore historic Old Crow Day 2: Take a wildlife spotting hike on the Crowberry Trail Day 3: Experience the Aklavik Crosstrail by bike Day 4: Embark on a cultural tour of Inuit Villages Day 5: Witness Nature’s beauty with the Northern Lights Tour Day 6: Observe the Grayling migration Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Vuntut National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic Char – with a unique taste and white texture, Arctic char is caught in the Yukon waters and is a must-try
  • Nanook Soup – a hearty soup, which is made from caribou meat and vegetables, is loved by locals.

Local Beverages

  • Alaska Ice Wine – A local specialty, a glass of Alaska ice Wine will have you wanting more.

Where to Stay in Vuntut National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Crow’s Nest Cabins - A cozy and comfortable place to stay, Crow’s Nest Cabins offer ideal accommodation options complete with fully-equipped kitchens, laundry facilities, and internet access
  • Old Crow Bed and Breakfast - A local B&B with offers a peaceful atmosphere and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Vuntut National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Arctic Qiviut – made from musk ox wool, it’s one of the warmest, most luxurious fibers found on the planet.
  • Northern Lights Inspired Art – a unique work of art that captures the beauty of the Northern Lights in a variety of mediums.

Local Culture and Customs of Vuntut National Park

  • The Vuntut Gwitchin People - The Vuntut Gwitchin people, part of the larger Gwich’in Nation, have lived in the region for thousands of years. The people have a strong connection to the land and a rich culture that seamlessly blends with the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Vuntut National Park

The best time to visit Vuntut National Park is from June to September, where the weather is the mildest, and wildlife is abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vuntut National Park

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the Vuntut Gwitchin people.
  • Always obey the park regulations and travel in designated areas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t get too close to the wildlife.
  • Don’t litter in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vuntut National Park

  • Always check the weather before embarking on outdoor activities as the temperature can fluctuate.
  • Be cautious when hiking as there are grizzly bears and wolves in the park.

Health in Vuntut National Park

  • Always drink purified water
  • Be prepared to deal with symptoms of altitude sickness caused by higher altitudes above sea level.

Getting to Vuntut National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vuntut National Park

  • Old Crow Airport - The closest airport to Vuntut National Park.

Bus Stations Near Vuntut National Park

  • There are no bus stations near Vuntut National Park

Other Options

  • Private Transportation - Private vehicles and taxis are available for hire from local transportation companies.

How to Get Around in Vuntut National Park

  • Car Rentals -It is recommended to rent a car from outside Vuntut National Park and drive to your destinations within the park.

Where to Go Next from Vuntut National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ivvavik National Park - A remote and wild park where visitors can explore the Northern Arctic Tundra.
  • Kluane National Park and Reserve -A UNESCO World Heritage site, Kluane National Park and Reserve offers beautiful landscapes, incredible wildlife, and outdoor recreation opportunities.

Conclusion

The vastness and stunning vistas of Vuntut National Park are waiting to be explored. With unlimited outdoor recreational opportunities, rich cultural heritage, and unforgettable experiences, a visit to Vuntut National Park is a must for anyone seeking to discover the beauty of Canada’s northernmost regions.
