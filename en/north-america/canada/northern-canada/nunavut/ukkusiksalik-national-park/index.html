Introduction to Ukkusiksalik National Park

Ukkusiksalik National Park, located in Nunavut, Canada, is a vast and remote wilderness area that offers visitors a chance to experience the unspoiled beauty of the Arctic with its rugged terrain, towering cliffs, and pristine river valleys. The park covers an area of more than 20,500 square kilometers and has a rich cultural history, including numerous archaeological sites that reveal the history of the Inuit people who have lived in the area for thousands of years.

List of Places to Visit in Ukkusiksalik National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iqalugaarjuup Nunanga Territorial Park: A historic Inuit campsite that was active for thousands of years can be found in the park, and its well-preserved artifacts and tools provide insight into traditional Inuit life and culture.
  • Qaummaarviit Territorial Historic Park: This park is home to ancient Thule archaeological sites, which date back more than 1,000 years and contain a wealth of artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Ukkusiksalik National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wager Bay: This glacial valley is home to towering cliffs, sweeping vistas, and a variety of wildlife, including caribou, wolves, and polar bears.
  • Marine Excursion: Visitors can rent boats or join guided tours to visit the nearby rivers and fjords, offering stunning views of icebergs, glaciers, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ukkusiksalik National Park

  • Sandhill Glacier Hike: This challenging hike leads visitors up to the top of a nearby glacier and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valley.
  • Kuugaaruk River Hike: This scenic hike takes visitors through a variety of landscapes, including rivers, forested areas, and open tundra.

Best Things to Do in Ukkusiksalik National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Polar Bear Viewing Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour to view polar bears in their natural habitat in the park.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The park’s rivers and fjords provide opportunities for kayaking and canoeing, offering stunning views of glaciers and icebergs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ukkusiksalik National Park

Day 1: Arrive and check into accommodations in Rankin Inlet. Day 2: Take a guided wildlife viewing tour in the park. Day 3: Explore the park’s hiking trails and enjoy the stunning scenery. Day 4: Visit the Iqalugaarjuup Nunanga Territorial Park and Qaummaarviit Territorial Historic Park. Day 5: Take a cultural tour to learn more about the Inuit people who have lived in the area for generations. Day 6: Go kayaking or canoeing in the park’s rivers and fjords. Day 7: Departures from Rankin Inlet.

Food and Drink Scene of Ukkusiksalik National Park

Local Cuisine

  • Arctic Char: A freshwater fish that is found in the area and prepared in a variety of ways.
  • Caribou: The quintessential Arctic meat that is often served in stews or smoked.

Local Beverages

  • Kabloona Beer: A locally brewed beer made with Arctic berries.
  • Arctic Ice Wine: A rare and delicious dessert wine made with grapes that have been frozen on the vine.

Where to Stay in Ukkusiksalik National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rankin Inlet Hotels: There are several hotels located in Rankin Inlet, which is the closest town to the park and offers easy access to the park’s attractions.
  • Camping: There are several campgrounds located throughout the park that offer primitive camping facilities for visitors.

What to Buy in Ukkusiksalik National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Inuit Artwork: The park’s surrounding communities are home to numerous Inuit artists who produce stunning carvings, sculptures, and prints.
  • Arctic Wildlife Photography: Numerous guides and outfitters in the area offer guided wildlife viewing tours and photography workshops.

Local Culture and Customs of Ukkusiksalik National Park

The Inuit people have lived in the area for thousands of years and have developed a rich and unique culture that is closely tied to the area’s natural environment. Visitors are encouraged to learn more about the Inuit culture and traditions while visiting the park, including their hunting and fishing techniques, traditional clothing, and arts and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Ukkusiksalik National Park

The best time to visit Ukkusiksalik National Park is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is mild, and the days are long. However, visitors should be aware that the area experiences 24-hour daylight during this time, which can affect sleep patterns.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ukkusiksalik National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural environment.
  • Bring warm clothing and gear suitable for the Arctic climate.
  • Take guided tours to ensure safety and learn more about the area’s attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt or remove artifacts from the park’s archaeological sites.
  • Approach or feed wildlife.
  • Venture out into the park without appropriate gear or preparation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ukkusiksalik National Park

Visitors to Ukkusiksalik National Park should be aware of the area’s rugged and remote terrain, as well as the potential dangers posed by polar bears and other wildlife. Visitors are also advised to take appropriate precautions against hypothermia and other cold-weather-related injuries.

Health in Ukkusiksalik National Park

Visitors to Ukkusiksalik National Park should be aware of the potential risks of drinking untreated water from area streams and rivers. Visitors should also be aware of the potential danger of tick bites and should take appropriate precautions against Lyme disease and other tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Ukkusiksalik National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ukkusiksalik National Park

The closest airport to Ukkusiksalik National Park is in Rankin Inlet, which is located approximately 160 kilometers from the park’s main entrance.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the park via chartered bush or floatplane service, which can be arranged through local outfitters or tour operators.

How to Get Around in Ukkusiksalik National Park

Visitors can hike along the park’s established trails or take guided tour to visit the park’s major attractions.

Where to Go Next from Ukkusiksalik National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Auyuittuq National Park: This stunning national park features numerous hiking trails, picturesque glaciers, and abundant wildlife.
  • Pond Inlet: This charming town offers visitors a chance to explore Inuit culture and history, including traditional crafts, food, and music.

Conclusion

Ukkusiksalik National Park is a remote and beautiful wilderness area that offers visitors a chance to experience the stunning natural beauty of the Arctic, as well as learn more about the culture and history of the Inuit people who have called the area home for thousands of years. With numerous hiking trails, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences, Ukkusiksalik National Park is an ideal destination for anyone seeking a truly unique and unforgettable travel experience.
