Introduction to Qikertaaluk Island

Qikertaaluk Island is a remote, sparsely populated island in Nunavut, Canada. It is located in northern Canada, in Canada, and in North America. The island is known for its breathtaking and untouched natural landscapes, rich Inuit culture, and unique heritage sites. Whether you’re an adventurer seeking thrilling outdoor activities or a traveler looking to immerse yourself in local culture, Qikertaaluk Island has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you through the must-see attractions on the island, where to eat, where to stay, and how to travel safely.

List of Places to Visit in Qikertaaluk Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Qikertaaluk Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the ancient Inuit culture and history of Qikertaaluk. It contains exhibits that include artifacts, traditional clothing, and tools used in daily life.
  • Arctic Bay Anglican Parish: The church was built in the early 1900s and still offers traditional Anglican services to the community of Arctic Bay.
  • Iqalugaaqtiit Health Centre: An important healthcare facility for the local Inuit communities.

Natural Attractions of Qikertaaluk Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Thule: The highest mountain peak in the region offers a challenging, multi-day hike with stunning panoramic views of Qikertaaluk Island.
  • Floe Edge: A unique and breathtaking phenomenon where the sea ice meets the open ocean. Visitors can witness the abundance of marine life in the area.
  • Auyuittuq National Park: A pristine national park, home to rugged mountain peaks, pristine rivers, and glaciers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qikertaaluk Island

  • Mount Thule Trek: A multi-day hiking adventure up the highest mountain peak on Qikertaaluk Island.
  • Narwhal Coast Trail: A scenic coastal hike that takes visitors through beautiful fjords.
  • Pond Inlet Heritage Trail: A moderate hike, starting from the small town of Pond Inlet, that takes visitors through historic Inuit landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Qikertaaluk Island: Activities and Tours

  • Northern Lights Viewing: The island offers an exceptional opportunity to witness the beauty of aurora borealis, known as the northern lights.
  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour to witness marine wildlife such as narwhals, beluga whales, and seals.
  • Dog Sledding: Experience traditional transportation in the Arctic region and learn about the Inuit way of traveling by dog sledding.

Food and Drink Scene of Qikertaaluk Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic Char: A delicious fish caught in the cold waters around the island. Commonly served smoked or grilled.
  • Muskox Stew: A hearty stew made from locally hunted muskox.
  • Bannock: A type of bread that is fried, baked, or boiled and is a staple food in the Inuit diet.

Local Beverages

  • Arctic Berry Juice: Made from locally grown berries, this drink is high in antioxidants and vitamin C.
  • Cloudberry Tea: A traditional tea made from the leaves of the cloudberry plant.

Where to Stay in Qikertaaluk Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Panikpak Camp: A popular camping site for visitors with amenities such as washrooms, fire pits, and running water.
  • Northern Lodge: A comfortable lodge where visitors can enjoy modern amenities such as heating, comfortable beds, and Wi-Fi.
  • Guesthouses: An affordable way to experience the local way of life, some of them offering a chance to learn about traditional Inuit crafts and activities.

What to Buy in Qikertaaluk Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Inuit Carvings: Masterfully crafted sculptures depicting various animals and mythological stories.
  • Arctic Char products: Locally made products, including smoked and canned food items, from the Arctic Char fish.
  • Inuit clothing: Beautiful hand-crafted garments made from caribou and seal fur by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Qikertaaluk Island

  • Inuit Culture: The Inuit culture plays a significant role in the local community, with daily routines and traditions deeply rooted in their heritage.
  • Respect for Elders: Traditional values give the elderly people the utmost respect and honor.
  • Nature and Environment: The Inuit have a deep connection with nature and the environment, and they prioritize the protection of their land.

Best Time to Visit Qikertaaluk Island

The best time to visit Qikertaaluk Island is between June and August, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the flora and fauna are at their peak. However, visitors must always prepare for the harsh conditions of the Arctic region, even during the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qikertaaluk Island

Do:

  • Research: Plan your trip well in advance and be aware of weather and travel conditions.
  • Respect: Show respect for the local culture and traditions.
  • Learn the Language: Inuit have their own language, and learning common phrases could go a long way in communicating and making connections with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Overlook Safety: Qikertaaluk Island can be unforgiving, always ensure you have safety equipment when venturing out.
  • Disrespect Religion and Culture: The Inuit religion and traditions are deeply ingrained in their lifestyle and should be respected.
  • Litter: Nature is sacred in the Arctic, and visitors should protect it at all times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qikertaaluk Island

Visitors must be mindful of the harsh weather conditions from late fall to early spring, as it can turn dangerous in minutes. It is always advisable to wear appropriate clothing and to check weather updates before traveling.

Health in Qikertaaluk Island

Visitors must come prepared with first aid kits and medication, as the access to healthcare facilities outside medical centers is limited. It is also recommend getting vaccinations up to date before visiting.

Getting to Qikertaaluk Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qikertaaluk Island

The nearest airport is Iqaluit Airport, with regular flights from Ottawa, Montreal, and other Canadian cities. From Iqaluit, visitors can fly to Arctic Bay, the closest airport to Qikertaaluk Island.

Bus Stations Near Qikertaaluk Island

The remote location of Qikertaaluk Island means that there are no bus stations nearby.

Other Options

Visitors can take a charter or private flight to access the land or water transportation services to reach areas not served by air travel.

How to Get Around in Qikertaaluk Island

Visitors can explore the island on foot, hire local guides, or rent snowmobiles or ATVs to traverse the rugged terrain.

Where to Go Next from Qikertaaluk Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can embark on a multi-day expedition to the Ellesmere Island to explore the largest national park in the country, Quttinirpaaq National Park.

Conclusion

Qikertaaluk Island is a unique and unspoiled destination perfect for adventurers and travelers looking to connect with nature and immerse themselves in Inuit culture. Visitors can witness the magic of the northern lights, embark on multi-day hiking adventures, or visit heritage sites that showcase the rich history of the Inuit people. When planning your trip to Qikertaaluk Island, remember to respect the local culture, prepare for the harsh conditions, and prioritize safety at all times.
