Introduction to Ellesmere Island

Ellesmere Island is a remote island located in the Canadian Arctic Archipelago, part of Nunavut Territory. It is the northernmost island in Canada and has one of the lowest population densities in the world. Despite its harsh climate and rugged landscape, it is a fascinating destination for adventurous travelers looking to explore the pristine nature and cultural heritage of the Canadian Arctic.

List of Places to Visit in Ellesmere Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Polar Continental Shelf Program: This research station in Resolute Bay offers educational tours about the Arctic research and history of the area.
  • Grise Fiord Hamlet Office and Museum: Learn more about the Inuit culture and history of the northernmost civilian settlement in Canada.
  • Aulavik National Park: Explore the archaeological remains of the Thule culture in this national park located on the northwest coast of the island.

Natural Attractions of Ellesmere Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quttinirpaaq National Park: This is the northernmost national park in Canada and offers unique opportunities for wildlife viewing, hiking, and kayaking.
  • Lake Hazen: Discover the largest lake in the Canadian high Arctic, located near the center of Ellesmere Island. It is surrounded by stunning mountain landscapes and is a popular destination for scientists and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Barbeau Peak: Climb the highest mountain in eastern North America, with an elevation of 2,616 meters

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellesmere Island

  • Alexandra Fiord: This is a popular spot for hiking tours, and it’s a great way to admire the stunning scenery of the island. Hikers can climb glaciers, scale cliffs and look across winding valley vistas.
  • Thule Site by the Baillarge Bay Trail: Walk through history and experience the dwellings of the ancient Thule culture.

Best Things to Do in Ellesmere Island: Activities and Tours

  • Arctic Wildlife Tours: Experience the Arctic’s majestic natural beauty and come across some of its inhabitants, like polar bears, caribou, wolves and Arctic foxes.
  • Night Enthusiasts Cold Weather Tours: Witness the Northern Lights from a cozy igloo, along with sipping hot drinks provided from camp out post and exploring the Arctic night sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ellesmere Island

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Resolute Bay and take a city tour.
2 Visit the research station of the Polar Continental Shelf Program.
3 Take a scenic plane ride to Quttinirpaaq National Park and go on a hiking tour.
4 Visit the Grise Fiord Hamlet Office and Museum to learn about Inuit culture.
5 Visit Lake Hazen for a day trip and enjoy the stunning scenery.
6 Climb Barbeau Peak to get a panoramic view of Ellesmere Island.
7 Take part in wildlife tours and explore the island’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellesmere Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic Char: Enjoy this delicious fish that is a staple of the Inuit cuisine.
  • Caribou Stew: Try this hearty stew that is made with caribou meat and vegetables.
  • Nanook Soup: This traditional Inuit soup is made with caribou meat or whale blubber, vegetables and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Arctic Rose Tea: This tea is made from wild rose hips, which grow abundantly in the Arctic.
  • Iced Berry Juice: Enjoy this refreshing drink made from Arctic berries like blueberries, cranberries, and cloudberries.

Where to Stay in Ellesmere Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Polar Continental Shelf Program Guest House: Offers comfortable quarters near Resolute Bay with easy access to the city’s amenities.
  • Inns North: Offers rooms at both the northern and southern ends of Ellesmere Island. These comfortable and functional inns provide access to the island’s beautiful scenery.

What to Buy in Ellesmere Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Inuit Art: Discover and purchase unique Inuit art pieces and crafts. Soapstone and animal furs are commonly used for sculpting.
  • Arctic Char: Bring home a tasty souvenir by purchasing some smoked Arctic char.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellesmere Island

The Inuit cultures have inhabited the region for more than 4,000 years, and their customs and traditions continue to influence everyday life on Ellesmere Island. Visitors are encouraged to learn about their culture, heritage and values with tributes such as Inuit art and traditional food.

Best Time to Visit Ellesmere Island

The best time to visit Ellesmere Island is during the summer months (June to August); this is the only practical time of year when the island is accessible as water and ice alike can make travel impossible. During this time, the temperature ranges from 5 to 20°C, and the days are long with up to 24 hours of daylight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellesmere Island

Do:

  • Dress warmly in layers.
  • Respect the Inuit culture and customs.
  • Book tours with authorized local operators.

Don’t:

  • Approach or feed wild animals.
  • Wear strong fragrances that may attract animals or insects.
  • Leave trash or waste in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellesmere Island

  • Bring the required gear and clothes
  • Hiring a licensed local guide wherever possible.
  • Respect the Arctic environment and weather conditions

Health in Ellesmere Island

  • Check with your doctor before traveling.
  • Carry all necessary medication and first aid kits with you as basic health facilities may not be available.
  • Be prepared for remoteness or possibly any emergency situations.

Getting to Ellesmere Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ellesmere Island

  • Resolute Bay Airport (YRB) is the easiest way to get to Ellesmere Island from major Canadian cities.

Bus Stations Near Ellesmere Island

  • There are no bus stations because of the remote location.

Other Options

  • Private charter planes and small tour operators offer customizable charter tours during the summer months.

How to Get Around in Ellesmere Island

  • Charter planes and helicopter tours provide access to the remote areas of the island and offer stunning views of the arctic landscapes.
  • Snowmobiles, ski-doo, and dogsleds are available to hire as well for touring in winter seasons.

Where to Go Next from Ellesmere Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Devon Island: Located to the south of Ellesmere Island, this island is known for its impressive geological features, unique wildlife, and breathtaking beauty.
  • Baffin Island: Visit Canada’s largest island located east of Ellesmere Island, which is home to stunning natural wonders like the Auyuittuq National Park.

Conclusion

Ellesmere Island is a must-see destination for adventurous travelers seeking a unique experience in the Canadian Arctic. From the stunning landscapes to the rich Inuit culture, the island offers a wealth of attractions and activities. Whether taking a scenic plane ride, exploring the wildlife, or discovering the cultural heritage, Ellesmere Island promises a once-in-a-lifetime adventure. Get ready to cross it off your bucket list!
