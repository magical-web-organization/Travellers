Introduction to Cambridge Bay

Cambridge Bay is a small yet culturally rich community in Nunavut, Canada’s northernmost territory. The town overlooks the Arctic Ocean and is surrounded by stunning landscapes, ample wildlife, and unique cultural experiences. Visitors to Cambridge Bay will be rewarded with a glimpse into the Inuit way of life while enjoying the beauty and adventure of this remote region.

List of Places to Visit in Cambridge Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arctic Coast Visitors Centre

Located in the heart of Downtown Cambridge Bay, the Arctic Coast Visitors Centre is a great starting point for any visitor to the town. The centre showcases the area’s unique ecology, cultural history, and information on the current social landscape.

Canadian High Arctic Research Station

This research center brings together scientists and researchers from across Canada and around the globe to conduct research on topics ranging from climate change to Arctic wildlife. It also serves as a center for community engagement and educational programming.

Natural Attractions of Cambridge Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ovayok Territorial Park

Ovayok Territorial Park is located on the northwestern edge of Cambridge Bay and offers a variety of activities. Activities include hiking, fishing, and camping. The park is also home to Ovayok Mountain, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Queen Maud Gulf Wildlife Sanctuary

The Queen Maud Gulf Wildlife Sanctuary is a vast protected area where visitors can spot an array of wildlife such as polar bears, foxes, and land mammals. Boat trips often available during the summer months, allow visitors to view Arctic wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambridge Bay

Mount Pelly

Mount Pelly provides an exhilarating yet adventurous hike offering stunning panoramic views at the peak. Visitors are required to undertake the hike with a local guide.

Best Things to Do in Cambridge Bay: Activities and Tours

Dog Sledding

Dog sledding is among the popular things to do in Cambridge Bay. Visitors can take a tour through the Inuvialuit lands behind the sled of these remarkable creatures.

Hunting and Fishing

Visitors can join an Inuit guide and hunt for Thule seals, caribou, wolves, muskoxen, and foxes. Fishing Guides are available to help guests fish for trout and Arctic char during their stay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cambridge Bay

Sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and check-in; visit to Arctic Coast Visitors Centre.

Day 2: Explore the scenic town and its history, visit the historical Hudson’s Bay Trading Post.

Day 3: Take a guided boat tour for wildlife viewing and sightseeing.

Day 4: Hike the Mount Pelly Trail with a local guide.

Day 5: Join a guided hunting and fishing excursion into the wilderness.

Day 6: Explore Ovayok Territorial Park.

Day 7: Spend the day at leisure before departing for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Cambridge Bay

Must-Try Dishes

Arctic Char

A local favorite in the region, Arctic Char is usually smoked or grilled over an open flame, often served with wild berries or a delicious raspberry vinaigrette.

Local Beverages

Kugluktuk Icewine

Kugluktuk Icewine describes the result of aromatic berries that are fermented slowly, resulting in an intense and flavorful wine.

Where to Stay in Cambridge Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Umingmak Lodge

The Umingmak Lodge is an affordable option located in the town that caters to visitors with a range of comfortable rooms and amenities.

Kanayok Lodge

Kanayok Lodge is a higher-end option located in the town and can be booked to cater for visitors seeking a mid-range option complete with amenities such as conference rooms and an in-house restaurant.

What to Buy in Cambridge Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Inuit Art Pieces

Inuit art pieces are the most popular souvenirs in Cambridge Bay. Visitors can find carved bone and stone pieces or beautiful jewellery crafted out of different natural elements.

Local Culture and Customs of Cambridge Bay

The unique culture and customs of the Inuit people remain strong in Cambridge Bay. Visitors to the area respect and participate in such customs.

Best Time to Visit Cambridge Bay

The best time to visit Cambridge Bay is between July and September when the daylight hours are long, providing ample time for exploring outdoors and indulging in local activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambridge Bay

Do:

  • Respect the local cultural customs, traditions and beliefs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather conditions.
  • Plan for transportation and tours in advance.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or their natural habitats.
  • Take photos without the permission of the subject or without consulting the tourist guide.
  • Ignore the advice of the locals on weather conditions, landscape and transportation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cambridge Bay

Cambridge Bay is a safe and remote community where visitors can enjoy a peaceful and worry-free vacation. Still, visitors are encouraged to take proper safety measures such as proper clothing and footwear when exploring outdoor and remote locales.

Health in Cambridge Bay

Visitors are advised to undergo vaccination against tuberculosis and Hepatitis B. The town’s hospital provides basic medical services, but anyone with pre-existing medical conditions is advised to take precautionary measures and consult a doctor before planning a visit.

Getting to Cambridge Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Cambridge Bay via air transport by travelling through Edmonton to Cambridge Bay. Flight service is available from major airports.

Airports Near Cambridge Bay

The Cambridge Bay Airport is the main airport servicing the region. Visitors can connect to Edmonton, where they can opt for flights to other parts of the world.

Bus Stations Near Cambridge Bay

No Bus Service is available within Cambridge Bay.

Other Options

Water vessels from other parts of Canada service Cambridge ports during summer months, offering an adventurous maritime option.

How to Get Around in Cambridge Bay

The town is small enough to explore on foot, while visitors can rent bicycles and cars from some of the town’s hotels or lodges.

Where to Go Next from Cambridge Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bathurst Inlet

Bathurst Inlet, located roughly 100 km south of Cambridge Bay, is the perfect location to learn about Inuit culture and history.

Victoria Island

Victoria Island is an isolated yet beautiful destination, lying in the Arctic Archipelago of Canada.

Conclusion

A small yet remarkable Arctic destination, Cambridge Bay offers visitors a breathtaking Arctic experience in a culturally rich community. Whether exploring the historic architecture, experiencing regional cuisine, or immersing oneself in native Inuit culture, Cambridge Bay is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a distinct travel adventure.
