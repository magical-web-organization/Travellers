Introduction to Qausuittuq National Park

Located on Bathurst Island in the Arctic Archipelago, Qausuittuq National Park is a remote natural wonderland that offers visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the breathtaking beauty of the Canadian Arctic. The park is accessible only by air, and its rugged terrain, permafrost landscapes, and pristine tundra make it a unique destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Qausuittuq National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The 17th-century Thule archaeological site, where visitors can see the ruins of an ancient Inuit settlement.
  • The Nunavut Research Institute, where researchers study the fauna, flora, and ice conditions of the Arctic and provide insight into Inuit knowledge and traditions.
  • The community of Resolute Bay, which serves as a gateway to the park and offers visitors a chance to learn about Inuit culture and local customs.

Natural Attractions of Qausuittuq National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The polar bear denning areas, where visitors can observe mother polar bears and their cubs in their natural habitat.
  • The hiking trails that wind through the park, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • The birdwatching opportunities, which allow visitors to spot a variety of Arctic bird species, including the snowy owl, the arctic tern, and the peregrine falcon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qausuittuq National Park

Qausuittuq National Park offers a range of hiking and biking routes for visitors of all levels of experience. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • The North Arm Circuit, a 33-kilometer route that takes hikers and bikers through the heart of the park.
  • The Polar Bear Pass Trail, a 7-kilometer trail that offers stunning views of the Arctic landscape.
  • The Eureka Mountain Trail, a challenging 10-kilometer trail that rewards hikers with breathtaking views of Bathurst Inlet.

Best Things to Do in Qausuittuq National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking through the park’s pristine waters, where visitors can spot seals, whales, and other marine life.
  • Taking a guided tour of the Thule archaeological site, where visitors can learn about the ancient Inuit culture and their way of life.
  • Going on a polar bear viewing excursion, which allows visitors to observe these majestic animals from a safe distance.

Food and Drink Scene of Qausuittuq National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic char, a cold-water fish that is a staple of the Inuit diet.
  • Caribou stew, a hearty stew made with caribou meat and vegetables.
  • Bannock, a traditional bread that is often served with meals.

Local Beverages

  • Iqaluit beer, a locally brewed beer made with Arctic ingredients.
  • Arctic berry teas, which are made with local berries and herbs and have a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Qausuittuq National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodations in Qausuittuq National Park are limited, but visitors have a few options to choose from.

  • The Qausuittuq Lodge, which offers comfortable accommodations in Resolute Bay.
  • The park’s campsites, which allow visitors to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the Arctic.

What to Buy in Qausuittuq National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Inuit art, including carvings, sculpture, and prints.
  • Arctic clothing and accessories, such as parkas and mittens.
  • Arctic jewelry, made from local materials such as stones, bone, and ivory.

Local Culture and Customs of Qausuittuq National Park

The Inuit culture is an integral part of life in Qausuittuq National Park. Visitors should be respectful of Inuit traditions and customs, including:

  • Asking permission before taking photographs of Inuit people, their homes, or their belongings.
  • Refraining from touching or handling Inuit artwork, which may be considered taboo.
  • Dressing appropriately for the Arctic climate and being mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Qausuittuq National Park

The best time to visit Qausuittuq National Park is during the summer months, when the weather is milder and the park is accessible. The park is typically open from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qausuittuq National Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment and leave no trace.
  • Observe wildlife from a safe distance.
  • Carry bear spray and be aware of polar bear safety measures.

Don’t:

  • Approach wildlife or feed them.
  • Damage or disturb cultural or historical sites.
  • Ignore the advice of local guides or park staff.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qausuittuq National Park

  • Carry bear spray and be aware of polar bear safety measures.
  • Dress appropriately for the Arctic climate and be prepared for changing weather conditions.
  • Be cautious when hiking or biking in the park, as the terrain can be rugged and challenging.

Health in Qausuittuq National Park

  • Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with traveling to a remote location and should carry any necessary medications or medical equipment.
  • The park has limited medical facilities, and visitors should take precautions to prevent injury or illness.

Getting to Qausuittuq National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The only way to access Qausuittuq National Park is by air. Visitors can fly into Resolute Bay, which is the closest community to the park. From there, transportation options include:

Airports Near Qausuittuq National Park

  • Resolute Bay Airport, which is the closest airport to the park.

Bus Stations Near Qausuittuq National Park

  • There are no bus stations near Qausuittuq National Park.

Other Options

  • Visitors can charter a private plane or helicopter to access the park.

How to Get Around in Qausuittuq National Park

The best way to get around Qausuittuq National Park is on foot, as the park’s rugged terrain is not suitable for cars or other vehicles. Visitors can also rent bicycles or kayaks to explore the park’s trails and waterways.

Where to Go Next from Qausuittuq National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The city of Iqaluit, the capital of Nunavut, which offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.
  • A trip to the nearby Arctic communities, where visitors can experience the traditional Inuit way of life.

Conclusion

Qausuittuq National Park is a unique and remote destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the breathtaking beauty of the Canadian Arctic. From hiking and biking to polar bear viewing and kayaking, the park has something for every adventurer. Visitors should be respectful of Inuit traditions and customs, dress appropriately for the climate, and take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being.
