Introduction to Resolute

Resolute is a small hamlet located in the Nunavut territory of Canada. This picturesque village located at the bottom of Cornwallis Island is a charming destination for any traveler seeking an authentic northern experience. It is the ideal location to explore the exceptional Arctic weather, natural beauty, and cultural attractions of the region, as well as engage in outdoor pursuits. Here is a comprehensive guide to help you make the most of your trip to Resolute.

List of Places to Visit in Resolute

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The cultural attractions of Resolute help visitors understand its fascinating history and the way of life of the Inuit people. Visit the Nanilavut exhibition to witness artifacts and learn about the traditional ways of the Inuit. The local Anglican church - St. Peter’s Church - established in 1947 by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), provides insight into the early days of development in the area. The Resolute Bay Airport also features artifacts, historical displays, and cultural vignettes reflecting the life, culture, and history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Resolute: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hiking, wildlife observation, and birdwatching are all experiences in their own right in Resolute, which has a variety of natural attractions. Spend the day exploring the Qausuittuq National Park, which is home to numerous migratory birds like Snow Geese and Canada Geese. Observe local wildlife, such as polar bears, arctic hares, and wolves. You can also take a boat tour to explore the breathtaking sea ice formations that characterize the area during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Resolute

The vast expanse of Resolute Bay is a sight to behold, with its towering mountains, deep valleys, and open skies. The hiking and bike trails in the area are perfect for exploring this scenic landscape. The Quasuittuq National Park provides many hiking trails, including the Devon Island Trail, which takes hikers into the wilderness of the park. The area also features several popular biking routes, such as the Sugarloaf Mountain Trail, which provides scenic panoramic views of the area and the Arctic Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Resolute: Activities and Tours

Resolute offers many unique experiences and attractions that are not available elsewhere in the world. Tourists can take advantage of popular activities such as dog sledding, snowmobiling, ice fishing, and many other winter pursuits that take advantage of the unique winter climate. Summer activities, such as kayaking, paddle boarding, and boat tours, allow visitors to take in the beautiful coastal landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Resolute

Day 1: Hike Sugarloaf Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the area. Day 2: Visit the Nanilavut exhibition and learn about the traditional Inuit lifestyle in the area. Day 3: Go dog sledding or snowmobiling in the winter months or kayaking and paddleboarding in the summer. Day 4: Visit the Resolute Bay Airport and learn about the local history and culture of the region. Day 5: Explore the Qausuittuq National Park and witness the wildlife, migratory birds, and the stunning sea ice formations. Day 6: Visit St. Peter’s Church and learn about the early days of development in the area. Day 7: Enjoy a day of relaxation and unwind in the natural beauty of the reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Resolute

Must-Try Dishes

Explore the local culinary scene and try the dishes that Resolute is best known for. Arctic Char, caught in the pristine Arctic waters, is a local favorite and is often cooked with traditional Inuit techniques. Caribou stew, featuring local ingredients such as carrots, onions, and potatoes, is another must-try dish in the area.

Local Beverages

Resolute is known for its selection of locally brewed beer and spirits made with local ingredients. Try the Nunavut Brewing Company’s beer, which is made in Iqaluit and features unique flavors such as wildflower honey.

Where to Stay in Resolute: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The accommodations in Resolute range from cozy lodges to luxurious resorts, offering visitors a variety of options to suit their needs. The North 45 Adventures lodge is an ideal location for visitors looking for comfortable accommodations and a beautiful northern experience. The lodging features many amenities, including stunning views of the surrounding area.

What to Buy in Resolute: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some various items that encapsulate your northern adventure. Bring home items like the Inuit-made carvings and sculptures, fur and wool garments, and narwhal-ivory carving which Residents make.

Local Culture and Customs of Resolute

Resolute is a welcoming community with a proud tradition and a unique way of life. Respect the Inuit way of life and follow the “leave no trace” principle. Resolute’s approach to living off the land is a way of life and economy for locals, and guests should be mindful and respect indigenous customs.

Best Time to Visit Resolute

Visitors can enjoy Resolute year-round with unique attractions in every season. Summer permits warm-weather activities such as boat tours and hiking, and winter visitors have a wide range of unique winter adventures that take full advantage of the area’s climate and natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Resolute

Do:

  • Respect the beautiful Arctic wilderness and wildlife.
  • Learn about the Inuit culture and their heritage while in Resolute.
  • Visit the Nanilavut exhibit to learn more about the region and its history.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local wildlife and natural surroundings.
  • Overcrowd or litter while in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Resolute

Resolute is a safe place, but it’s always best to take precautions when in the wilderness to avoid getting lost in the vast, unpopulated areas. Visitors should stay on designated trails, avoid walking on the sea ice, and never approach wildlife.

Health in Resolute

Northern climates can be harsh, and travelers should prepare for colder temperatures and take precautions to avoid hypothermia. It is recommended to pack several layers of clothing and have a first aid kit. Visitors should take good care of their skin and stay hydrated to avoid the effects of the dry, cold air.

Getting to Resolute: Transportation Options and Logistics

Resolute is easily accessible by plane, charter, or courier flights from major Canadian cities like Ottawa, Toronto, and Montreal.

Airports Near Resolute

The Resolute Bay Airport is the only airport in the region and operates year-round.

Other Options

Marine transportation is available for travel in the summer, but restrictions apply.

How to Get Around in Resolute

Local transport options include snowmobiles, which provide an adventurous and exhilarating way to explore the area, or guided tours. There are also a variety of boat tours available during the summer season.

Where to Go Next from Resolute: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The local Inuit communities in Pond Inlet and Clyde River make for worthwhile excursions. You can connect with them during your stay, perhaps with an organized tour or a local guide.

Conclusion

Resolute offers visitors a unique chance to immerse themselves in the natural beauty, unique cultural attributes, and outdoor adventure experiences of Canada’s North. By exploring these unforgettable destinations, you will make memories that will last a lifetime.
