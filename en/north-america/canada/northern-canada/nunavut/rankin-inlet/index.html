Discover Rankin Inlet: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Rankin Inlet is a small town in Nunavut, Canada, located on the western coast of the Hudson Bay. Although it may seem like a small, isolated town, it is rich in history and culture, and offers a range of outdoor activities and natural wonders to explore. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best things to do in Rankin Inlet, including attractions, food, accommodations, culture, safety, transportation, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Rankin Inlet

Rankin Inlet is located in the northernmost part of Canada, on the western coast of Hudson Bay. It is a small town of approximately 3,000 people, most of whom are Inuit. The town was established in the 1950s as a trading center and has since grown into a thriving community, rich in history and culture. The town is surrounded by tundra and dotted with lakes and rivers.

List of Places to Visit in Rankin Inlet

Rankin Inlet may be a small town, but it is packed with attractions that offer insight into the region’s culture, history, and natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Nunavut Arts and Crafts Association: This gallery showcases traditional Inuit artwork, from sculptures and prints to jewelry and textiles. Visitors can also purchase pieces to bring home.
  • The Rankin Inlet Centennial Library and Museum: This museum has a collection of artifacts that tell the history of the region, from prehistoric times to the present day. Visitors can learn about traditional Inuit hunting and fishing practices, as well as contemporary life in the town.

Natural Attractions of Rankin Inlet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Meliadine River: This scenic river is a great spot for fishing and canoeing, and offers stunning views of the tundra landscape.
  • Rankin Inlet Beach: The town’s beach is a great spot for a walk, picnic, or to watch the sunset.
  • Owl Ridge: This scenic lookout provides a stunning view of the tundra and surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rankin Inlet

Rankin Inlet is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are many hiking and biking routes to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Inukshuk Trail: This scenic trail leads up to the iconic Rankin Inlet inukshuk, a stone structure that represents the Inuit culture’s navigation markers.
  • Caribou Ridge Trail: This hike leads up to a scenic lookout point with picturesque views of the surrounding tundra.

Best Things to Do in Rankin Inlet: Activities and Tours

  • Dog Sledding: Rankin Inlet is known for its dog sledding tours, where visitors can experience the thrill of being pulled across the tundra by a team of sled dogs.
  • Arctic Char Fishing: The Meliadine River is a popular spot for fishing, and visitors can try their hand at catching Arctic char, a type of salmon found in the region.
  • Wildlife Watching: The surrounding tundra is home to a variety of wildlife, including caribou, muskox, and polar bears. Visitors can go on guided tours to spot these animals in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rankin Inlet

  • Day 1: Arrive in Rankin Inlet, settle in, and visit the Nunavut Arts and Crafts Association.
  • Day 2: Explore the Rankin Inlet Centennial Library and Museum, and go on a hiking or biking tour.
  • Day 3: Take a dog sledding tour and go ice fishing on one of the nearby lakes.
  • Day 4: Visit Owl Ridge and hike the Caribou Ridge Trail.
  • Day 5: Take a wildlife watching tour and spot caribou, muskox, and hopefully polar bears.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at the beach and explore the local shops for souvenirs.
  • Day 7: Depart from Rankin Inlet.

Food and Drink Scene of Rankin Inlet

In addition to exploring the natural beauty of Rankin Inlet, visitors can also explore the local food scene. Traditional Inuit cuisine is rich in seafood, game meat, and berries, and there are many dishes to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic Char: This type of fish is found in the pristine waters of the Arctic and has a delicate, mild flavor.
  • Caribou Stew: This hearty stew is made with tender chunks of caribou meat and root vegetables.
  • Bannock: This traditional bread is a staple of the Inuit diet and is made with flour, water, and fat.

Local Beverages

  • Arctic Berry Juices: These juices are made from locally sourced berries, such as cloudberry, cranberry, and blueberry.
  • Coffee: Coffee is a popular drink in Rankin Inlet, and there are several local cafes and coffee shops to try.

Where to Stay in Rankin Inlet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rankin Inlet has a variety of accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Siniktarvik Hotel: The town’s only hotel, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Inuit cuisine.
  • Louisette Bed and Breakfast: A cozy bed and breakfast with beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Wash’s Lodge and Hostel: A budget-friendly option with dormitory and private rooms.

What to Buy in Rankin Inlet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Rankin Inlet can bring home a piece of Inuit culture with them, from traditional artwork to natural beauty products.

  • Inuit Artwork: The Nunavut Arts and Crafts Association and local shops have a range of traditional Inuit art, including sculptures, prints, and textiles.
  • Natural Beauty Products: There are several local shops that sell natural beauty products made from local ingredients, such as berries and herbs.

Local Culture and Customs of Rankin Inlet

Rankin Inlet is a predominantly Inuit community, with a rich culture and strong traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices, including:

  • Dressing appropriately for the weather and for cultural events.
  • Greeting people with a friendly “hello” or “bonjour”.
  • Asking permission before taking photographs of people or their belongings.

Best Time to Visit Rankin Inlet

The best time to visit Rankin Inlet is from June to August, when the weather is mild and there are long hours of daylight. However, visitors should be prepared for the possibility of sudden weather changes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rankin Inlet

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and for cultural events.
  • Ask permission before taking photographs of people or their belongings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local culture and customs.
  • Leave trash or litter in public areas.
  • Approach wildlife, such as polar bears, without a trained guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rankin Inlet

Rankin Inlet is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the outdoors. This includes:

  • Staying on marked trails and roads when hiking or biking.
  • Carrying bear spray when hiking in bear country.

Health in Rankin Inlet

Visitors to Rankin Inlet should be prepared for the possibility of cold, windy weather and should dress appropriately. There are no major health concerns in the town, but visitors should bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Rankin Inlet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rankin Inlet

  • Rankin Inlet Airport: The town’s airport is served by several airlines, including Canadian North and First Air.

Bus Stations Near Rankin Inlet

  • There are no bus stations near Rankin Inlet.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Rankin Inlet

Visitors to Rankin Inlet can explore the town on foot or by bike. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Rankin Inlet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rankin Inlet is a small town, but there are several nearby destinations and excursions to explore.

  • Whale Cove: This nearby town is known for its traditional Inuit artwork and stunning views of the Hudson Bay.
  • Churchill: This historic town is known for its polar bear viewing tours and is located several hours south of Rankin Inlet by plane.

Conclusion

Rankin Inlet may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors to explore. From traditional Inuit artwork to outdoor adventures and natural wonders, this northern Canadian town has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to learn about local culture, explore the beauty of the tundra, or simply relax on the beach, Rankin Inlet is a must-visit destination.
