Introduction to Hay River

Nestled in Northwest Territories, Hay River is a picturesque town brimming with natural beauty, authentic culture, and a warm community of friendly locals. The town is situated at the edge of the Great Slave Lake, which is the deepest lake in North America, making for stunning views and outdoor adventures. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, Hay River has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will list all the must-visit attractions, best things to do, where to stay and eat, cultural practices, and everything you need to know before visiting Hay River.

List of Places to Visit in Hay River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Northern Life Museum and Cultural Centre: Provides insights into the history and culture of Hay River and the surrounding areas through exhibits such as the Dene and Metis Gallery, Fur Trade Gallery, and Pioneer Gallery.

  • The Hay River Heritage Centre: A museum and art gallery that preserves the history and culture of Hay River and features several works of art by local artists.

Natural Attractions of Hay River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alexandra Falls Territorial Park: A serene park located just outside of Hay River where you can enjoy magnificent views of the Alexandra Falls and the Hay River Canyon.

  • Great Slave Lake: The deepest lake in North America offers endless possibilities for outdoor activities such as fishing, kayaking, canoeing, and swimming. You can also explore the small islands and secluded bays by renting a boat.

  • Twin Falls Gorge Territorial Park: This park is a natural wonder that features two magnificent waterfalls, hiking trails, and stunning views of the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hay River

  • Hay River Heritage Trail: This scenic trail follows the famous Hay River and offers spectacular views of the town and the surrounding wilderness.

  • Vale Island Trail: A gentle hike that weaves through the peaceful Vale Island, where you can watch migratory birds, wildlife, and the beautiful sunset.

Best Things to Do in Hay River: Activities and Tours

  • Aurora viewing: Hay River is one of the best places to witness the magical Northern Lights, and several tour operators offer guided tours for an unforgettable experience.

  • Fishing: Great Slave Lake is famous for its abundance of fish species such as trout, pike, and whitefish. You can fish on your own or hire a guide for a better experience.

  • Cultural experiences: You can immerse yourself in the local culture by participating in traditional practices and events such as Dene games, drumming, and dance performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Hay River

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison meat: A local delicacy that is lean, tender, and full of flavor.

  • Fresh-caught fish: Great Slave Lake offers ample opportunities for fishing, and you can taste delicious trout, pike, and whitefish at local restaurants.

  • Wild berries: The town is surrounded by lush wilderness, and you can find abundant wild berries such as raspberries, blueberries, and cranberries.

Local Beverages

  • Blachford Lake Lodge & Wilderness Resort’s Blueberry Martini: A signature drink made with local wild blueberries.

  • Northern Cocktail: A delicious mix of vodka, cranberry juice, and a hint of lime.

Where to Stay in Hay River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ptarmigan Inn: A comfortable hotel located in the heart of the town, with all basic amenities and easy access to local attractions.

  • Hay River Suites: Fully furnished apartments with all amenities and stunning views of Great Slave Lake.

  • The Moose’s B & B: A cozy guesthouse located in a serene location, offering comfortable stay and local hospitality.

What to Buy in Hay River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Dene crafts: The Dene people are skilled artisans, and you can buy beautiful traditional crafts such as moccasins, birch-bark baskets, and dreamcatchers.

  • Fish meat and jerky: Local shops sell fish meat and jerky that are perfect for snacking and make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hay River

The Dene people have lived in Hay River and the surrounding areas for thousands of years and have a rich culture and heritage. Respect and honor their traditions, practices, and beliefs. Take permission before clicking photographs or filming. Learn some Dene words and phrases as a gesture of goodwill.

Best Time to Visit Hay River

The best time to visit Hay River is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is pleasant, and the town hosts several festivals and cultural events. You can witness the Northern Lights from September to April, which is an awe-inspiring natural wonder to witness.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hay River

Do:

  • Take part in local events and cultural practices.

  • Respect nature and wildlife.

  • Purchase traditional crafts from local artisans.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.

  • Smoke or drink in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hay River

Hay River is a safe and friendly town, but you should take basic precautions such as locking doors and windows, keeping valuables safe, and staying alert at night.

Health in Hay River

Canadian healthcare facilities are among the best in the world, but you should carry medical insurance before traveling to Hay River. Take adequate precautions against insect bites, drink bottled water, and follow food safety measures.

Getting to Hay River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hay River

  • Norman Wells Airport (YVQ): The closest airport, located about 270 km from Hay River.

  • Yellowknife International Airport (YZF): Located about 460 km from the town, with daily flights to major cities in Canada and the USA.

Bus Stations Near Hay River

There is no bus station in Hay River, but you can take a Greyhound or a local bus from nearby locations such as Fort Smith and Yellowknife.

Other Options

You can rent a car and drive to Hay River from Yellowknife or other nearby towns. Several tour operators offer guided tours from major cities in Canada, such as Edmonton and Calgary.

How to Get Around in Hay River

The town is small and easy to explore on foot or by renting a bike or car. There are also local taxis and buses available.

Where to Go Next from Hay River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fort Smith: A historic town located about 299 km from Hay River, known for its abundant wildlife, waterfalls, and hiking trails.

  • Yellowknife: The capital city of Northwest Territories, located about 460 km from Hay River, offering cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and a thriving food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Hay River is a hidden gem in Northwest Territories, Canada, offering a blend of natural beauty, authentic culture, and warm hospitality. From visiting museums to witnessing the magical Northern Lights, there is something for every traveler in Hay River. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your visit and experience the town’s charm and beauty.
