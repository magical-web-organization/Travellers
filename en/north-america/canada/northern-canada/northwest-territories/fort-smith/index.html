Introduction to Fort Smith

Fort Smith is a picturesque town located in the Northwest Territories, Canada. It’s situated on the Slave River, near the border with Alberta, and serves as a gateway to Wood Buffalo National Park. Fort Smith features a range of natural attractions, hiking trails, and historical landmarks. The local culture and customs, along with the food and drink scene, are unique and worth exploring. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Fort Smith.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Smith

Here are some of the top places to visit in Fort Smith:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Fort Smith National Historic Site: This former Hudson’s Bay Company trading post is now a museum that showcases the history of the town and the fur trade.

  • Wood Buffalo National Park Visitor Centre: Learn about the UNESCO World Heritage Site, Wood Buffalo National Park, which is home to free-roaming Plains and Wood Bison, wolves, and the endangered Whooping Crane.

  • Northern Life Museum and Cultural Centre: This museum is dedicated to the local lifestyle, customs, and traditions of the First Nations, Metis, and Inuit communities in the region. You can see traditional arts, crafts, and clothing, and many vintage items from earlier times.

Natural Attractions of Fort Smith: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wood Buffalo National Park: Take a day trip or overnight camping trip to see the world’s largest bison herd and the pristine landscapes of the Canadian Shield. The park also includes the unique natural phenomenon of the Pingos, conical hills unique to permafrost formations.

  • Midnight Sun Xperience: Enjoy a guided boat tour through the rapids of the Slave River and into the wilderness of the boreal forest. Discover the eternal daylight of the subarctic summer, as the sun barely dips below the horizon.

  • Pelican Rapids Inn: The inn offers cabin rentals, a campground, and an excellent restaurant, but the real draw is its access to the Slave River. Rent a canoe or kayak, fishing rod, or even a stand-up paddleboard, to explore the tranquil waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Smith

Fort Smith offers several hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Sanguez Point Trail: A 10 km moderate-level hike that features scenic views of the Slave River, with interpretive panels that detail the area’s history and flora.

  • Abbot Ridge Trail: A challenging hike of approximately 20 km that leads to an impressive vista of Wood Buffalo National Park and the south shore of Great Slave Lake.

  • Pine Lake Trail: A local favorite that offers an easy 2 km loop hike through boreal forest, perfect for families or those with limited time.

Best Things to Do in Fort Smith: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities and tours available in Fort Smith, including:

  • Guided Fishing Tours: Fish the Slave River with an experienced guide, and try your luck at catching Northern Pike or Walleye.
  • Northern Lights Viewing: See the Aurora Borealis, one of the world’s most spectacular natural phenomenons, during the winter months. Several tour operators offer guided Northern Lights experiences in the Fort Smith region.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: Take a leisurely paddle on the Slave River with a rental canoe or kayak. Or, book a multi-day guided canoe trip to Wood Buffalo National Park for the ultimate outdoor adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Smith

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Fort Smith:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Fort Smith National Historic Site and the Northern Life Museum and Cultural Centre
2 Go on a guided fishing tour or rent a canoe/kayak for a leisurely paddle on the Slave River
3 Hike the Sanguez Point Trail and have a picnic lunch by the river.
4 Take a guided boat tour with Midnight Sun Xperience to see the boreal forest
5 Visit the Wood Buffalo National Park Visitor Centre, take a break at Pine Lake Trail
6 Hike the challenging Abbot Ridge Trail or bike around the town
7 Explore the local culture and customs, grab some souvenirs, or enjoy the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Smith

The food and drink scene in Fort Smith is small but diverse. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burgers: Enjoy locally raised bison meat cooked to perfection. The burgers are juicy, flavorful, and often served with homemade fries.

  • Fried Whitefish: Fresh-caught whitefish is a local specialty, typically served with a side of seasonal vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Northern Lights Ale: A crisp amber ale with a fruity hop finish, brewed with locally sourced barley and crystal malts.

  • Canadian Whiskey: The town has a rich heritage of whiskey distilling, so take the opportunity to try some of Canada’s best whiskey.

Where to Stay in Fort Smith: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Smith has a limited selection of accommodations, but here are a few options to consider:

  • Pelican Rapids Inn: The inn is conveniently located near the Slave River, offering cabin rentals, a campground, and a small hotel with several well-appointed rooms.

  • Super 8 by Wyndham Fort Smith: The chain hotel offers comfortable accommodations with free breakfast, Wi-Fi, and a gym.

  • Northern Lights Motel and Chalets: Unique and cozy, this accommodation provides a more intimate experience in chalet-style accommodations closer to the town center.

What to Buy in Fort Smith: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Fort Smith’s unique culture and craftsmanship by shopping at these stores:

  • Northern Life Museum and Cultural Centre Gift Shop: The museum gift shop offers a range of handmade jewelry, clothing, and art from local artisans.

  • Pelican Narrows Trading Post: This general store sources locally made moccasins, maple syrup, and handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Smith

Fort Smith is home to a vibrant and diverse community of First Nations, Inuit, and Métis peoples. Respect for the people and their customs is essential to experiencing life in Fort Smith fully. There are many opportunities to learn about the area’s unique cultural heritage through visiting cultural landmarks and museums, attending festivals and events, and conversing with local peoples.

Best Time to Visit Fort Smith

The best time to visit Fort Smith is during the summer months, from June to August. The warmer weather and longer daylight hours make it ideal for outdoor adventures. In contrast, winter months bring the Northern Lights and the chance to enjoy winter-specific activities from snowshoeing to ice fishing. Remember that during mid-June, visitors need to be aware of high water levels that can persist in some areas of the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Smith

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Fort Smith:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs by learning about them and following the guidance from the local communities.
  • Take nothing but photographs, leave nothing but footprints when you visit parks and natural areas.
  • Leave wildlife alone. Avoid feeding or harassing any animals, and practice bear safety measures when hiking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass on private property, even if it looks inviting or photograph-worthy.
  • Don’t leave your campfire unattended. Make sure to follow the local and park rules about fires.
  • Don’t litter, please. This includes cigarette butts and food scraps.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Smith

Fort Smith is generally a safe place to travel, but visitors should take basic precautions to stay safe:

  • Let someone know where you’re going and when you intend to return before you engage in any outdoor activities
  • Bring a map or take a GPS-equipped device when hiking unfamiliar areas
  • Stay aware of the local weather and check for any alerts or warnings

Health in Fort Smith

Fort Smith has a medical clinic for emergencies and minor health issues, but it’s best to bring any prescription medications and first-aid supplies. Remember that Canada’s medical system is vastly different from other countries, and travel insurance for healthcare emergencies is recommended for all visitors.

Getting to Fort Smith: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort Smith is remote and only accessible by land, air, or on the river. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Fort Smith

There is a small airport in Fort Smith- Smith & Russel Regional Airport- only offers flights through Northwestern Aviation, so visitors can arrive from Yellowknife or Edmonton. The local airport code is YSM. Edmonton has more extensive flights, but you’ll still need to take a connection from there.

Bus Stations Near Fort Smith

There is no regular bus service between major Canadian cities and Fort Smith.

Other Options

One can consider driving from Edmonton, which often takes around 12 hours. Another option is to take a road trip separately to Fort Chipewyan, then take a ferry for 4.5 hours to Fort Smith. Visitors can book on Northern Ferry Services throughout the year to cross the four kilometres wide Slave River.

How to Get Around in Fort Smith

Fort Smith is compact and easy to navigate on foot or by bike, making it a perfect way to explore the town. For rental cars, guests must make a reservation for a vehicle in advance and then pick it up at the airport.

Where to Go Next from Fort Smith: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yellowknife: Adventure capital of the Northwest Territories and a great place to see the Northern Lights!
  • Edmonton: The largest city in Alberta and a metropolitan hub for food, music, and culture in the region.
  • Hay River: A scenic 2-hour drive from Fort Smith, with beautiful views of the Pine Point and Hay River’s Great Slave Lake.

Conclusion

Fort Smith is a hidden gem in the vast wilderness of northern Canada, with a rich history and natural beauty. From exploring the local cuisine to hiking the trails to simply enjoying the scenic vistas, Fort Smith is a trip that promises lifelong memories and unforgettable experiences.
