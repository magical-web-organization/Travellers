Introduction to Inuvik

Welcome to Inuvik, a small yet vibrant town located in the Northwest Territories of Northern Canada. It is a remote wilderness destination that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and a scenic getaway from bustling cities. Inuvik is a perfect destination for those seeking to explore the pristine wilderness, Northern Lights, and experience the rich Inuit culture. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your perfect getaway to Inuvik.

List of Places to Visit in Inuvik

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Inuvik Community Greenhouse: A captivating greenhouse project that features a wide variety of vegetables and herbs grown year-round in this subarctic region of Canada. It is a unique place to visit, showcasing an innovative approach to community farming.
  • Igloo Church: A landmark of Inuvik, a unique and stunning structure built in the shape of an igloo by local Inuit craftspeople in the 1970s.
  • Midnight Sun Complex: A cultural center and museum that showcases the art and culture of the Inuvialuit people. It also features a great library and a pool which offers public swim times.

Natural Attractions of Inuvik: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tuktoyaktuk: Located just north of Inuvik is Tuktoyaktuk, an Arctic Ocean community only accessible by boat or ice road during the winter months. It is a unique community with a unique landscape, Windy Bay, locally known as “pingos,” ice house-shaped frozen mounds in the ground, are a must-visit attraction here.
  • Herschel Island-Qikiqtaruk Territorial Park: A beautiful northern park located in the Beaufort Sea providing tours, hiking, and camping activities offering a getaway campsite for nature lovers.
  • Aurora Borealis & Midnight Sun: You can experience the unique phenomenon of Aurora Borealis from the comfort of Inuvik. During the winter months, the town provides many activities such as a Aurora Tour to experience one of nature’s most stunning visuals.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Inuvik

  • East 3 Loop: A 2-kilometer (1.24 miles) trail that offers beautiful views of the Inuvik townsite.
  • Boot Lake Trail: A 20-kilometer (12.43 miles) route located south of Inuvik, features an upstream route offering a scenic views of Glacial Lake Agassiz.
  • Cameron Hills Trail: A multi-day looped trail crossing two mountain ranges and providing panoramic views, featuring one of the best hike routes of the area.

Best Things to Do in Inuvik: Activities and Tours

  • Cultural Tours: The local community offers various cultural tours, ranging from dog sledding, snowmobiling, to traditional cooking, to provide one the unique experiences of the Inuit community.
  • Ice Fishing: During winter months, you can try your hand at ice fishing on the frozen rivers or lakes of the Inuvik region.
  • Floatplane Tours: Get a bird’s eye view of the splendor of the Arctic Ocean, before experiencing the unforgettable tranquility of a water landing back in Inuvik.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inuvik

Day 1: Arrive in Inuvik, explore the town’s landmarks such as the Igloo Church or explore the town History by visiting the Midnight Sun Complex.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Tuktoyaktuk to experience the stunning Windy Bay and other places of attraction.

Day 3: Explore the outdoors with a hike on the panoramic lookout point of Mount Pelly or enjoy your day fishing the nearby Richardson Mountains.

Day 4: Experience the thrill of Snowmobiling or Dog sledding, take your adrenaline levels sky-high.

Day 5: Travel to the Herschel Island-Qikiqtaruk Territorial Park, enjoy camping on the grounds or take a walking tour to the Heritage sites, learn the customs and traditions of the Inuit.

Day 6: Take a floatplane tour across the Arctic ocean to get a bird’s eye view of the beauty of Inuvik.

Day 7: A final day to explore the culinary delights at one of the local restaurants such as Happy Valley Hunter or Alestine’s.

Food and Drink Scene of Inuvik

Must-Try Dishes

Inuvik offers a wide range of culinary delights, that brings the cultural diversity in this town to life:

  • Bannock bread: A simple flatbread that can be enjoyed anytime and is a staple of the local diet.
  • Arctic Char: A delicate, flavorful fish served pan-fried or baked in the oven with a buttery sauce is a must-try dish for any seafood lover.
  • Caribou Stew: A hearty stew, slow-cooked to perfection with tender chunks of caribou meat, and served in a unique style using a caribou antler as a bowl.

Local Beverages

  • Iced Tea- Juniper Berries: A refreshing drink that is prevalent in Inuvik, using Juniper Berries.
  • Traditional Inuit Tea: A locally brewed tea using common herbs found only in Arctic regions.

Where to Stay in Inuvik: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Inuvik offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and requirement, which includes:

  • Nova Inn: A modern premium hotel, providing excellent facilities such as a hot breakfast buffet, fitness center, and meeting facilities.
  • Arctic chalet: A spacious and affordable option, offering self-catering and comfortable stay facilities with basic amenities.
  • Inuvik Guest House: A hostel-style accommodation with shared kitchen and bathroom which suits budget travelers.

What to Buy in Inuvik: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Inuvik has a rich local culture and history, you will find a range of local products and gifts to take back home, including:

  • Inuvialuit Art: You can find beautifully crafted traditional Inuit art such as Soapstone carvings, or take a piece of Inuvik history home from the local gift stores.
  • Traditional Clothing: You can also find unique traditional clothing such as mukluks, parkas, or mittens made from real fur.

Local Culture and Customs of Inuvik

Inuvik is a community that celebrates its unique culture and traditions owing to the Inuit community who have also helped preserve the traditions of the Gwich’in people. Northern Canada’s official languages are English, French, and Indigenous languages, making Inuvik authentic and culturally diverse where you can experience the unique way of living.

Best Time to Visit Inuvik

The best time to visit Inuvik is June to September when the weather is warm and the sun is up 24/7. During these months, visitors can enjoy a range of activities such as camping, hiking, or taking a tour of Inuvik while also not being limited by snowy weather and winter road closures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inuvik

Do:

  • Do respect the culture and traditions of the Inuit community.
  • Do be prepared for colder weather and pack layers of clothing to keep warm.

Don’t:

  • Don’t try to touch or pet roaming wildlife like caribou or bears, they can be dangerous.
  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural surroundings of Inuvik.

Safety and Health

Safety in Inuvik

Inuvik is generally considered a very safe town to visit. However, visitors should still take precautions when exploring the local area, such as staying alert when walking alone in the dark or traveling on less-traveled roads.

Health in Inuvik

Visitors should ensure they have the necessary vaccinations and medical insurance before traveling to Inuvik. Since it is a remote area, it is always good to carry the individual medical kit to face any emergency without any delay.

Getting to Inuvik: Transportation Options and Logistics

Inuvik is a remote destination, hence reaching there could sound difficult but there are various available options:

Airports Near Inuvik

The closest airport to Inuvik is Inuvik Mike Zubko Airport (YEV). It offers direct flights from Edmonton, Yellowknife, and Tuktoyaktuk.

Bus Stations Near Inuvik

Inuvik does not have a bus system; however, there is a Greyhound bus station available in Yellowknife.

Other Options

During the winter months, an ice road is built on the frozen river which connects Inuvik to Tuktoyaktuk, a one-of-a-kind experience to be added to the travel itinerary.

How to Get Around in Inuvik

People visiting Inuvik can simply walk around the town, but for the rest of the places, the best way to get around Inuvik is by renting a car, hiring a taxi or going on a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Inuvik: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dempster Highway: A 736 km road that connects Yukon to Inuvik showcasing natural beauty.
  • Aulavik National Park: A National Park located in the Northwest Territories showcasing natural beauty at its finest including Mountains, forests, and rivers.
  • Northern Lights: Embark on a cruise or visit one of the nearby villages to experience the Northern Lights, a unique and spectacular phenomenon that can only be seen in the Arctic region.

Conclusion

Inuvik is a beautiful and unique town filled with rich culture, breathtaking scenery, and endless opportunities for adventure seekers. Visitors can experience the unique natural and cultural attractions of Inuvik during the summer months, encompassing a unique experience that no other place can offer. Make sure to add Inuvik to your travel list and experience the wild side of Northern Canada.
