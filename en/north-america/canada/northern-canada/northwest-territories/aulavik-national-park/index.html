Aulavik National Park Travel Guide

Nestled in the Northwest Territories, in Northern Canada, lies the stunning Aulavik National Park. Known for its remote location, pristine wilderness, and unique cultural heritage, this park offers visitors a chance to experience Canada’s true north. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore all the best things to do and see in Aulavik National Park, from natural attractions to outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and more. Whether you’re a seasoned explorer or a first-time visitor, Aulavik National Park has something for everyone.

Introduction to Aulavik National Park

Located on the Arctic Ocean’s Banks Island and spread over 12,274 km2, Aulavik National Park is known for its diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural history. This remote wilderness is home to many indigenous people, including the Inuvialuit, who have lived here for thousands of years. The park is also known for the Western Arctic Caribou herd, which migrates through the area every year. Visitors can expect to be impressed by the rugged beauty of this true northern gem.

List of Places to Visit in Aulavik National Park

When visiting Aulavik National Park, there are plenty of places to see. From museums and historical sites highlighting the area’s cultural heritage to scenic outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aulavik National Park has many historically significant sites that offer visitors insight into the area’s rich cultural heritage. A few must-see sites include:

  • Ulukhaktok Cultural Centre: The Ulukhaktok Cultural Centre is the perfect place to learn about the traditional Inuvialuit culture. This center hosts regular cultural events and houses traditional artifacts, carvings, and artwork.
  • Kittigazuit Archaeological Site: The Kittigazuit Archaeological Site is an important archaeological site that sheds light on the area’s ancient history. This site contains remains of ancient Inuit dwellings and artifacts from over 2,000 years ago.

Natural Attractions of Aulavik National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aulavik National Park is known for its rugged wilderness and stunning natural beauty, making it an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. A few natural attractions to visit include:

  • Arctic Ocean: The Arctic Ocean is a must-visit when exploring Aulavik National Park. Not only can visitors take a dip, but they can also take a boat tour and witness the stunning natural beauty of the Arctic landscape.
  • Thomsen River: The Thomsen River is a great place to go canoeing or kayaking. Visitors can witness magnificent wildlife, including caribou and polar bears, in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aulavik National Park

One of the best ways to explore Aulavik National Park is through its many hiking and biking trails, which offer visitors stunning views of the surrounding wilderness. A few popular routes include:

  • The Aulavik Rim Trail: This trail offers a 30km hike across the rim of the Aulavik River valley, providing visitors with breathtaking views of the park’s stunning scenery.
  • The Pingo Trail: This 6km hike offers visitors the opportunity to see several pingos, which are ice-cored hills unique to the area.

Best Things to Do in Aulavik National Park: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Aulavik National Park, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. A few popular activities and tours include:

  • Boat Tours: A boat tour of Aulavik National Park is a great way to explore the park’s stunning Arctic Ocean shoreline.
  • Dog Sledding: Dog sledding is a great way to explore the park’s breathtaking wilderness while getting a unique cultural experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aulavik National Park

For visitors who want to get the most out of their Aulavik National Park experience, a week-long itinerary is the perfect way to explore all the park has to offer. A few must-see sites include:

Food and Drink Scene of Aulavik National Park

Aulavik National Park is known for its delicious local cuisine, which features fresh, seasonal ingredients. A few must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arctic Char: Arctic Char is a local fish that is often served smoked with a side of berries.
  • Bannock: Bannock is a traditional Inuit bread that is often served with butter or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Arctic Cold Brew: Arctic Cold Brew is a refreshing coffee beverage made with purified Arctic water and steeped overnight.

Where to Stay in Aulavik National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options available to visitors in Aulavik National Park, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. A few popular options include:

  • Arctic Chalet: The Arctic Chalet is a guesthouse located just outside the park, offering visitors a cozy stay and stunning Arctic views.
  • Tuktoyaktuk Motel: The Tuktoyaktuk Motel is located in the nearby town of Tuktoyaktuk and offers visitors comfortable accommodations and stunning Arctic scenery.

What to Buy in Aulavik National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many local products and souvenirs to bring home from Aulavik National Park, including:

  • Muskox Yarn: Muskox Yarn is a locally produced yarn made from the fur of the endangered muskox.
  • Inuit Carvings: Inuit carvings are a stunning representation of the area’s rich cultural heritage and make for a beautiful souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Aulavik National Park

Visitors to Aulavik National Park should be aware of the area’s rich cultural heritage and the customs of its indigenous people. It is important to be respectful when visiting traditional sites and to learn about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Aulavik National Park

The best time to visit Aulavik National Park is during the summer months, from June to September. At this time, visitors can enjoy the warm weather and witness the beautiful migratory patterns of the Western Arctic Caribou herd.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aulavik National Park

Do:

  • Respect the area’s rich cultural heritage and learn about its significance.
  • Bring appropriate outdoor gear, including bear spray and mosquito repellent.

Don’t:

  • Approach wildlife, including caribou and polar bears.
  • Damage local cultural heritage sites or artifacts.

Safety and Health

When visiting Aulavik National Park, visitors should be aware of the area’s rugged terrain and cold temperatures. A few safety and health tips to follow include:

Safety in Aulavik National Park

  • Always carry bear spray and know how to use it.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and dress accordingly.

Health in Aulavik National Park

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.
  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites to avoid contracting insect-borne illnesses.

Getting to Aulavik National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aulavik National Park is a remote wilderness area, and transport options can be limited. A few transportation options to consider include:

Airports Near Aulavik National Park

  • Inuvik Airport: Inuvik Airport is the closest airport to Aulavik National Park, located approximately 200km away.

Other Options

  • Boat Tours: Boat tours are a great way to access Aulavik National Park’s stunning Arctic coastline.

How to Get Around in Aulavik National Park

When exploring Aulavik National Park, visitors are encouraged to use hiking or biking to get around. However, professional tour guides can also provide transportation.

Where to Go Next from Aulavik National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors looking to explore the surrounding area, a few nearby destinations to visit include:

  • Tuktoyaktuk: Tuktoyaktuk is a culturally significant town located nearby, offering visitors a chance to explore the area’s history and culture.
  • Inuvik: Inuvik is a bustling town just a short distance from Aulavik National Park, offering visitors a chance to experience modern amenities and services.

Conclusion

Aulavik National Park is a true northern gem, offering visitors stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their experience and create lifelong memories in this remote wilderness.
