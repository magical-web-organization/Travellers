Introduction to Quill Lakes

Quill Lakes is a group of interconnected lakes located near the town of Wadena, Saskatchewan. The three main lakes in the Quill Lakes system are Big Quill, Little Quill, and Mud Lake. The lakes are surrounded by wetlands and grasslands, making it a haven for a variety of wildlife. The region is also steeped in history, with numerous museums and historical attractions showcasing the area’s rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Quill Lakes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Quill Lakes is home to several museums and historical attractions that offer a glimpse into the region’s past.

  • The Wadena Heritage Museum: This museum features exhibits on the history of Wadena and the surrounding area, including displays on agriculture, the fur trade, and the railway.
  • The Foam Lake Heritage Interpretive Centre: This museum showcases the history and culture of Foam Lake and the surrounding area. Highlights include exhibits on pioneer life, wildlife, and geology.
  • The Kelvington and District Heritage Park: This open-air museum features historic buildings, including a schoolhouse, blacksmith shop, and a church. Visitors can also explore a restored train station and a working grain elevator.

Natural Attractions of Quill Lakes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Quill Lakes is known for its stunning natural beauty, offering numerous outdoor adventures and breathtaking views.

  • Fishing: The Quill Lakes system is a popular spot for fishing, with numerous species of fish including Northern Pike, Walleye, and Yellow Perch.
  • Bird Watching: The wetlands surrounding the lakes are a haven for birds, offering the opportunity to spot a wide variety of species including Sandhill Cranes, White Pelicans, and Tundra Swans.
  • Hiking and Biking: The region offers several trails for hiking and biking, including the Foxtail Trail and the Quill Lakes Birding Trail. Both trails offer scenic views of the surrounding wetlands and grasslands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quill Lakes

The Foxtail Trail is a 10-kilometer trail that winds through the Quill Lakes Wildlife Management Area. This trail is perfect for hiking and biking, offering stunning views of the wetlands and grasslands. The Quill Lakes Birding Trail is a 13-kilometer trail that offers bird watchers the opportunity to spot a wide variety of species in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Quill Lakes: Activities and Tours

Quill Lakes offers an array of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy.

  • Kayaking: Explore the lakes and wetlands up close with a guided kayaking tour.
  • ATV Tours: Take an ATV tour of the region’s rugged terrain, offering a unique perspective on the area’s natural beauty.
  • Museum Tours: Learn about Quill Lakes’ cultural heritage with a guided tour of one of the region’s many museums.

Week-Long Itinerary for Quill Lakes

Day 1: Arrive in Wadena, Saskatchewan, and explore the town’s shops and restaurants. Day 2: Visit the Wadena Heritage Museum and the Foam Lake Heritage Interpretive Centre. Day 3: Take a guided kayaking tour on Big Quill Lake. Day 4: Explore the Quill Lakes Wildlife Management Area on foot or by bike. Day 5: Take an ATV tour of the region’s rugged terrain. Day 6: Visit the Kelvington and District Heritage Park and the Quill Lakes Birding Trail. Day 7: Spend the day fishing on Little Quill Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Quill Lakes

Must-Try Dishes

Quill Lakes offers a range of culinary delights, including local specialties that visitors won’t want to miss.

  • Perogies: This traditional Ukrainian dish is a local favorite, with several restaurants in the region offering their own unique spin on the classic recipe.
  • Bison Burgers: Bison is a staple of the region’s agricultural industry, and visitors can try this lean and flavorful meat in the form of a delicious burger.

Local Beverages

Quill Lakes is known for its craft breweries and wineries.

  • Saskatchewan Craft Beer: This brewery offers a range of craft beers made with locally sourced hops and barley.
