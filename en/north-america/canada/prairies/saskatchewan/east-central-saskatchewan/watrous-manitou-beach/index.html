Watrous-Manitou Beach is a picturesque resort village located in East Central Saskatchewan, Canada. It is nestled in the heart of Prairies, offering an ideal getaway for tourists and locals alike. The village is renowned for its natural mineral waters, diverse attractions and outdoor activities, making it a must-visit destination.

Introduction to Watrous-Manitou Beach

Watrous-Manitou Beach is a gem in Saskatchewan’s tourist industry crown. The village is popular for the renowned Manitou Springs Resort & Mineral Spa and its mineral-rich lake. The waters have been recognized for their therapeutic benefits and attract tourists from around the world. The region’s rich history, rural environment, and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for travellers seeking a tranquil vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Watrous-Manitou Beach

Visitors to Watrous-Manitou Beach will find plenty of attractions to suit their needs. The village has numerous natural and cultural attractions, adventurous tours, and outdoor activities for guests. Here are the top places to visit in Watrous-Manitou Beach:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Watrous-Manitou Beach is home to many historical sites, museums, and art galleries. The Saskatchewan Railway Museum is a must-visit tourist attraction for those who love trains. The museum has a collection of restored vintage steam, electric and diesel locomotives, as well as cabooses and cars. Another notable destination is the Watrous Heritage Centre and Museum. The museum showcases the town’s rich history, hosting rotating exhibits throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Watrous-Manitou Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Watrous-Manitou Beach’s beautiful scenery and natural wonders provide visitors with plenty of outdoor activities. The resort village’s mineral-rich lake is its biggest natural attraction. The lake has beautiful beaches, hiking trails, fishing spots, and a marina where one can rent watercraft for an adventure on the water. Visitors can also visit Little Manitou Lake, located a few kilometres from Watrous-Manitou Beach, which also has therapeutic waters. The Pike Lake Provincial Park is an ideal location for picnics, swimming, boating, and hiking, with well-marked trails offering scenic walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watrous-Manitou Beach

Watrous-Manitou Beach has a variety of hiking, biking, and walking trails that offer beautiful views of nature. The Manitou Springs Resort & Mineral Spa offers beautiful trails with guided walks offered consistently. For adventurists looking to hit the trails, the Karen Shipley Trail is a long biking trail offering several routes depending on difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Watrous-Manitou Beach: Activities and Tours

Planning is essential when visiting Watrous-Manitou Beach as there are numerous activities and tours to explore. Whether it’s experiencing the mineral-rich waters, delving into history, or embarking on an adventure, any vacationer can find something to do. Here are some activities to look into:

  • Mineral Spa and Bathing
  • Horseback Riding Adventures
  • Fieldstone Ventures Tours
  • Fishing tours and Charters
  • Hiking Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Watrous-Manitou Beach

The village of Watrous-Manitou Beach’s food and drink scene is vibrant, with an array of options for various palates. From locally-sourced produce and food, visitors can sample traditional Saskatchewan fare in dining establishments or buy to cook in their accommodation. Here are some options that every foodie should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine (french fries topped with gravy and cheese curds)
  • Saskatoon Berry Pie (made with Saskatoon Berries, a local fruit)
  • Perogies (dumplings traditionally stuffed with potato, onion and cheese)
  • Bannock (a traditional bread with smoky flavours)

Local Beverages

  • Saskatoon Berry Wines and Ciders
  • Local Brewers like Nokomis Craft Ales and Bushwakker Brewing Company

Where to Stay in Watrous-Manitou Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Watrous-Manitou Beach has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget, from luxurious hotels to rustic campsites. Some of the top accommodation choices include Manitou Springs Resort & Mineral Spa, Wagon Wheel Motel, and The Guest House.

What to Buy in Watrous-Manitou Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Watrous-Manitou Beach

Watrous-Manitou Beach is a small community town that values its heritage and history. Engage with locals and find out more about the history of the region, their arts and crafts, and the Saskatchewan culture that is present everywhere. Visitors can also attend cultural festivals held throughout the year, including the Watrous Fun Day Festival, which offers a fun-filled day of live music, food, vendor booths, and fireworks.

Best Time to Visit Watrous-Manitou Beach

The best time to visit Watrous-Manitou Beach is in the summer months, with July and August being peak tourist season, offering warm weather and clear skies for outdoor activities. The shoulder seasons of spring and fall offer cooler temperatures and are ideal for those looking for a quieter vacation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Watrous-Manitou Beach

Watrous-Manitou Beach is a safe and secure destination, with low crime rates. However, it is advisable to secure valuable possessions and follow local laws and regulations. It is also critical to follow safety protocols while engaging in outdoor activities, such as wearing appropriate clothing.

Health in Watrous-Manitou Beach

The waters at Manitou Springs Resort & Mineral Spa are therapeutic and may help with skin conditions and joint pain. The destination is also safe from any pandemic occurrences. Nonetheless, visitors should take standard precautions, such as drinking and using water bottles properly stored throughout the day and staying up to date on any current pandemic protocols.
