Melfort, located in East Central Saskatchewan, is a vibrant city filled with rich history, breathtaking natural wonders and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in this picturesque Canadian city. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, what to buy, and much more to help you plan your trip to Melfort.

Introduction to Melfort

Melfort is a small city situated in East Central Saskatchewan, the Prairies region of Canada. It is renowned for its abundant natural beauty, its hospitable locals, and its rich cultural heritage. The city is also home to a thriving arts community, top-rated restaurants, and a lively nightlife scene, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking to immerse themselves in the true Canadian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Melfort

Melfort is a city that is bursting at the seams with attractions and activities for the whole family. Here are just a few of the top places to visit while in Melfort.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Melfort & District Museum - A museum that celebrates the rich history of Melfort, Saskatchewan, and surrounding communities.
  2. Star City Heritage Park - A community-maintained park that features a historic homestead, a logging museum, and a wildlife exhibit.
  3. Wapiti Valley Regional Park - A park featuring a restored log farmhouse, a pioneer museum, and a variety of outdoor recreational activities.

Natural Attractions of Melfort: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Melfort Bike Park - An exciting bike terrain park for all ages.
  2. Greenwater Provincial Park - A vast, sprawling park that offers breathtaking views of nature, including forests of birch, aspen, and pine.
  3. Melfort & District Trail System - A network of groomed winter trails for cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and winter hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Melfort

Melfort offers an array of hiking and biking opportunities for travelers, whether you’re looking for a leisurely walk or a challenging trek. Here are three of the best:

  1. Boreal Forest Nature Trail - A self-guided trail that winds through the Saskatchewan boreal forest and offers stunning views of nature.
  2. The Trans-Canada Trail - A world-renowned trail that stretches over 20,000 km, with a section that runs through Melfort.
  3. Melfort Multi-Use Pathway - An eight-kilometer trail that is perfect for biking or walking.

Best Things to Do in Melfort: Activities and Tours

Melfort offers a plethora of activities and tours for travelers. Here are just a few of the best that Melfort has to offer:

  1. Fishing - The many lakes surrounding Melfort offer excellent opportunities for fishing.
  2. Golfing - Melfort Golf & Country Club showcases the beautiful views of the Saskatchewan prairie while challenging your abilities.
  3. Bird Watching – Discover a wide variety of bird species in the Melfort area in nesting and feeding grounds including Seed Lake, St. Brieux Lake, and Wapiti Valley Regional Park.
  4. Horseback Riding – The wilderness trails of Melfort and the surrounding area provide stunning views on a guided trail rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Melfort

To help you plan your trip to Melfort, follow this itinerary for a week-long stay:

  • Day 1: Explore the Melfort & District Museum and Melfort Bike Park.
  • Day 2: Take a walking tour of the city’s historic district and spend the afternoon at Greenwater Provincial Park.
  • Day 3: Try your luck fishing in one of the many lakes around Melfort or take a horseback ride at the local stables.
  • Day 4: Go golfing at Melfort Golf & Country Club.
  • Day 5: Spend the day hiking or biking on the Trans-Canada Trail.
  • Day 6: Visit Star City Heritage Park and Wapiti Valley Regional Park.
  • Day 7: Spend your final day in Melfort souvenir shopping in the downtown areas like Spruce Haven or A&W Gifts Galore.

Food and Drink Scene of Melfort

Melfort’s food and drink scene are burgeoning, making it a great place to try some local culinary delights. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Poutine - A French-Canadian dish that is served with cheese curds, gravy, and fries.
  2. Perogies - A traditional Eastern European dish that is filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and/or cabbage.
  3. Saskatoon Berry Pie - A pie that has been a favorite among locals for generations.

Local Beverages

  1. Prairie Brew Co. - A delicious line of beer produced right in Melfort.
  2. Saskatchewan-made wine - Try some of the best-made wines locally produced.

Where to Stay in Melfort: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Melfort has no shortage of accommodation options. Here are three of the best places to stay while in Melfort:

  1. Best Western Plus Melfort Hotel - A beautiful hotel located in the heart of Melfort.
  2. Nipawin Bridgeview Inn - This hotel is west of Melfort is very popular and it has a great view of the river.
  3. Guesthouses & B&Bs - There are many beautiful guesthouses and B&Bs nestled throughout Melfort.

What to Buy in Melfort: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home.

Melfort has many quaint downtown gift shops to browse in that specialize in local products. Here are a few top picks:

  1. North East Trading Company Inc - A store selling Canadian Aboriginal jewellery and artwork.
  2. Melfort Farmer’s Market - Offers a variety of fresh and locally produced products.
  3. A&W Gifts Galore - This store provides a variety of souvenirs and unique gifts that will remind you of your trip to Melfort.

Local Culture and Customs of Melfort

Melfort is a welcoming and friendly place, with a deep appreciation for the environment and rich cultural heritage. Locals are happy to share their customs and culture, so be sure to engage them in small talk whenever you get the chance.

Best Time to Visit Melfort

Melfort can be enjoyed all year round. Summer months (June – August) offer the most comfortable weather accompanied by outdoor activities and the perfect ice fishing experience during the winter months (January – April).

Do’s and Don’ts of Melfort

Do:

  1. Respect the environment.
  2. Embrace the community’s kindness.
  3. Take time to discover the beautiful landscape and rich cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  1. Litter - Melfort has a zero-tolerance policy for littering.
  2. Disturb Wildlife - Melfort’s wildlife is very sensitive, please observe and take photos only without causing disturbances.
  3. Fail to greet – Melfortians are friendly and have a culture of greeting one another so feel free to greet anyone you come across!

Safety and Health

Safety in Melfort

Melfort is a very safe city and almost hassle-free for travelers. Melfort RCMP detachment ensures maximum safety for visitors and residents alike.

Health in Melfort

Melfort has primary healthcare services available within the city with multiple well-equipped medical clinics and hospitals.

Getting to Melfort: Transportation Options and Logistics

Melfort is easily accessible by roadways, airways, or railways.

Airports Near Melfort

  1. Prince Albert Airport - Located 108km south of Melfort.
  2. Saskatoon John G. Diefenbaker International Airport - Located 219km south of Melfort.
  3. Regina International Airport - Located 339km south of Melfort.

Bus Stations Near Melfort

Greyhound provides transportation services from Saskatoon and Prince Albert with stops in Melfort.

Other Options

Driving or renting a car is always recommended for a seamless travel experience in Saskatchewan.

How to Get Around in Melfort

Melfort is a very walkable city. However, there are Taxis and Car Rentals available for tourists who need a ride to their destination.

Where to Go Next from Melfort: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the most notable nearby destinations and excursion ideas near Melfort are:

  • Prince Albert National Park – An endless range of camping, hiking and water-based activities.
  • Crooked Bush - A small forest filled with hundreds of twisted trees.
  • East Central Saskatchewan - Explore the many towns and see the outstanding landscape of this part of Saskatchewan.

Conclusion

With so much to see and do in Melfort, it’s no wonder this gem of East Central Saskatchewan is quickly becoming one of the most sought-after travel destinations in Canada. From its breathtaking natural beauty to its rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone in this idyllic city. So pack your bags and come to experience all that Melfort has to offer; it is truly an unforgettable adventure!
