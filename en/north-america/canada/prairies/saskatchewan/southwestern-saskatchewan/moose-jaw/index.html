Introduction to Moose Jaw

Moose Jaw, located in the southwestern part of Saskatchewan, is a charming city filled with cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and natural wonders. Known as the “Friendly City,” Moose Jaw offers something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Moose Jaw

Here are some of the top places to visit in Moose Jaw:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moose Jaw Tunnels: Take a tour through the underground tunnels where mobsters and bootleggers operated during Prohibition.
  • Temple Gardens: Visit the historic mineral spa resort that has been welcoming guests since 1927.
  • Western Development Museum: Explore the history of Saskatchewan through exhibits and displays of vintage equipment and vehicles.
  • Sukanen Ship Pioneer Village and Museum: Step back in time and experience life on the prairies in the early 1900s.
  • Moose Jaw Museum and Art Gallery: Discover the rich history and art of Moose Jaw and its surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Moose Jaw: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buffalo Pound Provincial Park: Hike, bike, or paddle through the picturesque park filled with rolling hills and sparkling lakes.
  • Wakamow Valley: Relax in the natural beauty of this river valley that offers scenic walking and biking paths.
  • Murals of Moose Jaw: Stroll through downtown Moose Jaw and admire the colorful murals that depict the city’s history and culture.
  • Moose Jaw River: Take a boat tour along the river or cast a line and try your luck at fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moose Jaw

Moose Jaw offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the natural beauty of the area. Some of the top routes include:

  • Buffalo Lookout Trail: Enjoy a moderate hike up to a lookout point that offers stunning views of Buffalo Pound Lake.
  • Wakamow Valley Trail: This 5 km paved trail is perfect for walking, running, or biking through the beautiful river valley.
  • Murals of Moose Jaw Tour: Take a self-guided walking tour of downtown Moose Jaw and enjoy the colorful murals that tell the story of the city.

Best Things to Do in Moose Jaw: Activities and Tours

Moose Jaw offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few top picks:

  • Moose Jaw Spa Experience: Relax and rejuvenate at the Temple Gardens Mineral Spa Resort.
  • Tunnels of Moose Jaw Tour: Explore the underground tunnels that once served as a secret hideout for bootleggers.
  • Ghost Tours of Moose Jaw: Hear spooky tales of Moose Jaw’s past on a guided ghost tour.
  • Sukanen Ship Pioneer Village and Museum Tour: Step back in time and experience life on the prairies in the early 1900s.
  • Boat Tours on the Moose Jaw River: Take a relaxing boat tour and enjoy the natural beauty of the Moose Jaw River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moose Jaw

Day 1: Arrive in Moose Jaw and take a tour of the historical Moose Jaw Tunnels.

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Buffalo Pound Provincial Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Take a relaxing boat tour along the Moose Jaw River and enjoy the scenery.

Day 4: Visit the Sukanen Ship Pioneer Village and Museum and learn about the early settlers in the area.

Day 5: Take a dip in the hot mineral springs at Temple Gardens Mineral Spa Resort.

Day 6: Explore downtown Moose Jaw and admire the colorful murals that depict the city’s history and culture.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at Wakamow Valley, walking the trails or renting a bike.

Food and Drink Scene of Moose Jaw

Moose Jaw has a wide variety of dining and drinking options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local specialties:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic, Moose Jaw has several spots with gourmet twists on this indulgent dish.
  • Saskatoon Berry Pie: Try this local specialty made with tender Saskatoon berries.
  • Bison Burger: Sample a local taste of the Canadian prairies with a Bison burger.

Local Beverages

  • Moosehead Lager: Try the local craft beer, available on tap at many of Moose Jaw’s bars and restaurants.
  • Saskatoon Berry Wine: Sample local fruit wines unique to the area.

Where to Stay in Moose Jaw: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moose Jaw has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some top picks:

  • Temple Gardens Mineral Spa Resort: Luxurious resort hotel with a mineral spa and lush gardens.
  • Grant Hall Hotel: Historic hotel in the heart of downtown Moose Jaw.
  • Motel 6 - Moose Jaw: Affordable option with comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Moose Jaw: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Moose Jaw has several specialty shops that offer unique gifts and local products. Here are some top picks:

  • Tunnels of Moose Jaw Gift Shop: Bring home a piece of Moose Jaw’s unique history with a souvenir from the Tunnels Gift Shop.
  • Mac the Moose Souvenirs: Take home a souvenir from Canada’s largest moose sculpture, located in Moose Jaw.
  • SaskMade Marketplace Moose Jaw: Stock up on local specialty food and gift items at this artisanal market.

Local Culture and Customs of Moose Jaw

Moose Jaw is known for its friendly and welcoming community spirit. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and learn more about the area’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Moose Jaw

The best time to visit Moose Jaw is from May to September when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moose Jaw

Do:

  • Do try the local specialty dishes and beverages.
  • Do take a guided tour of the Moose Jaw Tunnels.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to learn more about the local history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moose Jaw

Moose Jaw is a safe city for visitors. However, it is always important to take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors, being aware of your surroundings and belongings, and following posted safety signs at outdoor attractions.

Health in Moose Jaw

Moose Jaw has several hospitals and medical facilities in case of emergency. It is always important to have travel insurance and bring any necessary medication.

Conclusion
