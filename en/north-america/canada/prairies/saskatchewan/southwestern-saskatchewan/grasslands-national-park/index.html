Introduction to Grasslands National Park

Grasslands National Park is a beautiful and serene destination located in the Southwestern region of Saskatchewan, Canada. The park comprises 907 square kilometers of mixed-grass prairie, including several hills, valleys, and canyons that provide magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.

The park is home to various wildlife species, including bison, pronghorns, coyotes, and black-tailed prairie dogs. The park also features several natural and cultural attractions, making it a must-visit location in Saskatchewan.

List of Places to Visit in Grasslands National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Visitor Centre: Grasslands National Park Visitor Centre is the perfect starting point for your exploration of the park. It features various exhibits, films, and interactive displays that showcase the park’s history, wildlife, and geology.

  2. Frenchman River Valley Campground: This campground is a historic site that was once home to the Last Mountain Métis. Visitors can explore the restored historic structures and learn about Métis culture and history.

Natural Attractions of Grasslands National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Two Trees Hiking Trail: This 6.5-km round-trip trail takes visitors through the heart of the park’s mixed-grass prairie ecosystem. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys as they walk along the trail.

  2. Rock Creek Badlands: This unique area offers some of the most impressive geological formations in the park. Visitors can walk along the trail to see intricate rock formations, hoodoos, and cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grasslands National Park

Grasslands National Park offers various hiking and biking routes, suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers.

  1. Eagle Butte Hike: This 3.8-km round-trip hike takes visitors to the top of Eagle Butte, providing panoramic views of the park.

  2. Paririe Vista Hike: This moderate-level hike will take visitors to one of the highest prairie ridges in the park, offering sweeping views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Grasslands National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Bison Drives: The park offers guided bison drives where visitors can get up close and personal with these magnificent animals while learning about their history and conservation.

  2. Stargazing: Grasslands National Park is a designated Dark Sky Preserve, making it an ideal location for stargazing. Visitors can enjoy a guided night-sky tour to see the stars and constellations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grasslands National Park

Day 1: Explore the Visitor Centre and learn about the park’s history and wildlife. Day 2: Hike the Two Trees Hiking Trail. Day 3: Take a guided bison drive and learn about the park’s conservation efforts. Day 4: Stargaze in designated Dark Sky Preserve. Day 5: Hike the Prairie Vista Hike. Day 6: Visit Rock Creek Badlands and explore the unique geological formations. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings of the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Grasslands National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bison Burger: Many restaurants in the area serve the signature bison burger, made with locally sourced bison meat.

  2. Saskatoon Berry Pie: This tasty dessert is made from Saskatoon berries, a native plant that grows in the area.

Local Beverages

  1. Saskatchewan Craft Beer: The area features several breweries and wineries that produce locally sourced craft beer and wine.

  2. Coffee: The area has several coffee shops that serve locally roasted coffee.

Where to Stay in Grasslands National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Crossing Inn and Suites: This hotel features comfortable rooms and convenient amenities, including an on-site restaurant and bar.

  2. Frenchman Valley Campground: This rustic campground offers campsites, cabins, and tipis for overnight stays.

What to Buy in Grasslands National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bison Meat: Locally sourced bison meat is a popular souvenir, available in various forms such as jerky, sausages, and steaks.

  2. Saskatoon Berry Products: Visitors can also take home locally made Saskatoon berry jams, syrup, and other berry-infused products.

Local Culture and Customs of Grasslands National Park

The area is home to various indigenous communities, including the Métis and First Nations people. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs and learn about the area’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Grasslands National Park

The best time to visit Grasslands National Park is from May to October, when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. The park is closed during the winter months from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grasslands National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles and pack out all waste.
  • Plan and prepare for your visit, including checking park regulations and weather forecasts.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Remove or disturb artifacts or natural features in the park.
  • Venture off-trail in protected areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grasslands National Park

Visitors should be aware of potential safety hazards, including sudden weather changes, steep cliffs, and wildlife encounters. Visitors should take precautions and follow all safety guidelines while in the park.

Health in Grasslands National Park

Visitors should bring adequate supplies and medical equipment, including sunscreen, insect repellent, and first aid kits, to ensure optimal health during their visit.

Getting to Grasslands National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grasslands National Park

The nearest airports to Grasslands National Park are Regina International Airport and Saskatoon John G. Diefenbaker International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Grasslands National Park

The Greyhound Bus Station in Regina serves the area and connects to other cities in Canada.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or use a ridesharing service to get to the park.

How to Get Around in Grasslands National Park

Visitors can explore the park on foot, by bike, or by car. The park offers various hiking and biking trails, and the roads are well-maintained for driving.

Where to Go Next from Grasslands National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cypress Hills Interprovincial Park: Located just a few hours from Grasslands National Park, Cypress Hills Interprovincial Park is a beautiful destination that features hiking trails, lakes, and unique geological formations.

  2. Regina: The capital city of Saskatchewan, Regina, is just a few hours away from the park and offers various cultural attractions and activities.

Conclusion

Grasslands National Park is a beautiful and unique destination that offers diverse natural and cultural attractions. Visitors can explore the park’s mixed-grass prairie ecosystem, learn about indigenous cultures and history, and enjoy various outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, Grasslands National Park is a must-visit location in Saskatchewan.
