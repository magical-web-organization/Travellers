Introduction to Great Sandhills

Discover the unique landscape of Great Sandhills, a natural wonder in Southwestern Saskatchewan, Canada. This 1,900 square kilometer area is known for its rolling sand dunes and prairie grasslands, creating a striking contrast that attracts visitors from around the world. Here, you can explore outdoor adventures, learn about local culture and history, and immerse yourself in stunning scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Great Sandhills

Check out our list of must-visit attractions in Great Sandhills, categorized by cultural and natural highlights.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sceptre Heritage Site: Learn about the history of this former town, which was established in 1913 and abandoned in 1959, through a self-guided tour of the remaining buildings and artifacts.
  • Maple Creek Heritage District: Stroll through the streets of Maple Creek and admire the well-preserved Victorian and Edwardian architecture, including the old courthouse and the former post office.
  • Fossil Discovery Centre: Discover the prehistoric past of the region, through displays of dinosaur skeletons and fossils found locally.

Natural Attractions of Great Sandhills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Sandhills Ecological Reserve: Explore the sand dunes that make Great Sandhills unique by hiking, birdwatching, or even sandboarding.
  • Sphinx Trail: Enjoy a scenic drive along the Sphinx Trail, which goes through the heart of Great Sandhills and offers panoramic views of the landscape.
  • Cypress Hills Interprovincial Park: Venture out of Great Sandhills to Cypress Hills, an interprovincial park shared by Saskatchewan and Alberta, where you can hike, camp, fish, and ski.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Sandhills

Discover the best trails for hiking and biking in Great Sandhills and enjoy the stunning nature that surrounds you.

  • Sand Dunes Trail: This 5.6 km loop trail takes you through the heart of Great Sandhills and offers stunning views of the sand dunes and prairie grasslands.
  • Birch Creek Trail: This 2.5 km trail follows the Birch Creek and offers excellent birdwatching opportunities.
  • The Great Trail: Join a part of the longest recreational trail in the world, which passes through Great Sandhills and connects all provinces and territories of Canada.

Best Things to Do in Great Sandhills: Activities and Tours

To experience Great Sandhills to the fullest, check out our list of recommended activities and tours:

  • Sandboarding: Experience the thrill of sliding down the sand dunes on a sandboard, a unique sport that is becoming increasingly popular in Great Sandhills.
  • Stargazing: With little light pollution, Great Sandhills offers exceptional stargazing opportunities. Join a guided tour or find a quiet spot and enjoy the view.
  • Scenic Drives: Take a scenic drive along the many gravel roads in Great Sandhills and discover the hidden gems of this unique landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Sandhills

Discover the local cuisine and beverages of Great Sandhills and indulge in unique flavors and tastes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saskatoon Berry Pie: This local specialty uses the Saskatoon berry, a small purple fruit that grows abundantly in the area and has a sweet and tart flavor.
  • Perogies: These Eastern European dumplings are a common dish in Saskatchewan and have various fillings, such as potato and cheese or meat.
  • Bison Burger: Taste the local meat of the prairie by trying a bison burger, a healthier and leaner alternative to beef.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Saskatchewan has a growing craft beer scene, with breweries in Saskatoon, Regina, and other cities. Try the local brews and support the small businesses.
  • Canadian Whisky: Canada is well-known for its whisky, and Saskatchewan has a few distilleries that offer tastings and tours, such as the Last Mountain Distillery.

Where to Stay in Great Sandhills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Find the best accommodation options for your stay in Great Sandhills, whether you prefer luxury hotels, budget hostels, or cozy guesthouses.

  • Prairie Wind Bed & Breakfast: This charming B&B offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere, as well as a delicious breakfast.
  • Great Sandhills Guest House: This guesthouse is located in the heart of the sand dunes and offers stunning views and peaceful surroundings.
  • Cypress Hills Lodge: If you’re looking for a more luxurious option, check out the Cypress Hills Lodge, which offers spacious rooms, a spa, and an indoor pool.

What to Buy in Great Sandhills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Great Sandhills with you by purchasing souvenirs and local products from the area. Here are some ideas:

  • Sand art: Buy a unique piece of art made with sand from Great Sandhills, created by local artists and artisans.
  • Saskatoon berry jam: Bring back a taste of Saskatchewan by getting some Saskatoon berry jam, a delicious and healthy spread for your toast or pastries.
  • Bison jerky: Try some bison jerky, a high-protein snack made with local meat. It’s a perfect souvenir for foodies and fitness enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Sandhills

Learn about the local culture and customs of Great Sandhills, and discover the pride that comes from living in the heart of the prairies.

Best Time to Visit Great Sandhills

The best time to visit Great Sandhills is between May and October, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, keep in mind that the sand dunes can get very hot during the summer months, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Sandhills

Here are some tips on how to make the most of your trip to Great Sandhills:

Do:

  • Do respect the local nature and wildlife by staying on designated trails and not disturbing the animals.
  • Do pack sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses, as the sun can be strong in the prairies.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks, and support the small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave garbage behind, as it can harm the environment and the wildlife.
  • Don’t go off-trail or climb the sand dunes, as it can damage the fragile ecosystem and put yourself at risk.
  • Don’t underestimate the changeable weather in Saskatchewan, and pack layers and waterproof clothing.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips on how to stay safe and healthy during your trip to Great Sandhills:

Safety in Great Sandhills

  • Be aware of the potential hazards of the sand dunes, such as steep drops and shifting sands. Stay on designated trails and don’t climb the hills.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and other insect-related health issues.
  • When driving on gravel roads, slow down and watch out for other vehicles and wildlife. Don’t drive too fast for the conditions.

Health in Great Sandhills

  • Be prepared for the sun and heat by staying hydrated, wearing sun protection, and taking breaks in the shade.
  • Respect the wildlife and don’t approach or touch them, as some species can carry diseases or be dangerous.
  • If you have any health concerns, seek medical attention promptly and don’t hesitate to call 911 in case of emergency.

Getting to Great Sandhills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Discover the best ways to get to Great Sandhills and navigate the local transportation options.

  • By car: Rent a car in Saskatoon or another nearby city and drive to Great Sandhills. Most of the roads are gravel, so check the conditions and plan accordingly.
  • By air: The nearest airport is in Saskatoon, which offers connections to other major Canadian cities. From there, rent a car or take a bus to Great Sandhills.
  • By bus: Greyhound Canada operates a bus service to Swift Current and Maple Creek, which are the closest towns to Great Sandhills. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi.

How to Get Around in Great Sandhills

The best way to get around Great Sandhills is by car, as most of the attractions are spread out and not easily accessible by public transportation. If you don’t have your own car, consider renting one or joining a guided tour that includes transportation.

Where to Go Next from Great Sandhills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Saskatchewan, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Saskatoon: Discover the largest city in Saskatchewan, known for its vibrant arts scene, riverfront park, and culinary offerings.
  • Prince Albert National Park: Venture further north to this stunning national park, which offers camping, hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Grasslands National Park: Head south to this remote and beautiful park, which protects the last remaining mixed-grass prairie ecosystem in Canada.

Conclusion

Great Sandhills offers a unique and unforgettable experience for nature lovers and adventurers. With its stunning sand dunes, diverse wildlife, and rich history and culture, this destination has something for everyone. Plan your trip carefully, respect the local nature and customs, and enjoy the beauty of Great Sandhills.
