Introduction to Carlyle

Carlyle is a charming town in the southeastern region of Saskatchewan, Canada. It is situated on the Moose Mountain Creek and near Moose Mountain Provincial Park. Carlyle is a popular tourist destination for outdoor enthusiasts as it offers an array of hiking and biking trails, natural attractions, and cultural landmarks. Visitors can explore the rich history of the region, participate in various recreational activities, and discover the local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Carlyle

When planning a trip to Carlyle, consider visiting these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carlyle Heritage Museum: Home to a vast collection of artifacts that encompass the town’s history. The museum is set in a charming, old-fashioned building and displays galleries that showcase the area’s agricultural history and famous women.

  • Coulter House: This historic house is one of Carlyle’s oldest buildings and a designated heritage site. Visitors can enjoy guided tours and learn about early settlement life.

  • Southern Prairie Railway: This old-fashioned railway offers scenic tours of the countryside around Carlyle. Passengers can travel on an authentic railcar and enjoy the breathtaking views of the prairies.

Natural Attractions of Carlyle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moose Mountain Provincial Park: This park’s rugged terrain includes forests trails, lakes and ponds, and several excellent camping locations. Visitors can explore over 35km of bike trails and relax in the outdoor pools and hot tubs.

  • Kenosee Lake: This picturesque lake is the perfect place to cool off on a hot summer day with a sandy beach, swimming, and fishing. The park surrounding the lake offers hiking and camping trails and a variety of seasonal activities.

  • Windover Wildlife Reserve: This wildlife sanctuary is home to 15km of hiking trails, and visitors can spot various species of animals such as elk, deer, coyotes, and foxes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carlyle

Carlyle offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that suit different skill levels. Some of the best trails include:

  • Wilson Trail: A 5km trail located at the Moose Mountain Provincial Park that features beautiful landscapes and views of the surrounding hills.

  • Cooking Lake Blackfoot Trail Network: An extensive system of trails that cater to different levels of difficulty and suitable for hiking or mountain biking.

  • Kenosee Lake Trail: This 9km trail around Kenosee Lake is perfect for easy hiking or biking and offers beautiful views of the lake during the summer.

Best Things to Do in Carlyle: Activities and Tours

There are various activities and tours visitors can participate in while in Carlyle, including:

  • Fishing trips on nearby lakes
  • Airplane and helicopter sightseeing tours
  • Horseback riding through the Saskatchewan prairies
  • Kayaking excursions on Moose Mountain Creek

Week-Long Itinerary for Carlyle

Day 1: Visit the Carlyle Heritage Museum, Coulter House and explore the town’s historic downtown area.

Day 2: Enjoy water activities at Kenosee Lake.

Day 3: Hike the Wilson Trail at Moose Mountain Provincial Park and visit the Lookout Point.

Day 4: Visit Windover Wildlife Reserve to spot local wildlife and enjoy hiking trails.

Day 5: Take a scenic train ride on the Southern Prairie Railway.

Day 6: Participate in a kayaking excursion on Moose Mountain Creek.

Day 7: Relax and unwind with a spa treatment at one of the resorts at Kenosee Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Carlyle

Carlyle has a unique food scene with a mix of Canadian cuisine and local specialties. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish that consists of French fries, gravy, and cheese.
  • Saskatoon berry pie: A delicious dessert that features the local Saskatoon berry.
  • Pickerel: Fresh-caught local fish, often served fried or grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie berry wine: Made from Saskatoon berries, this wine is a famous Saskatchewan specialty.
  • Micro-brewed beer: Carlyle has several local breweries that produce excellent craft beer.

Where to Stay in Carlyle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carlyle has several accommodation options to suit different budgets and tastes, including hotels, motels, and lodges. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Bear Claw Casino and Hotel: A luxurious hotel with a spa, casino, and indoor waterpark.

  • Kenosee Inn and Cabins: This rustic resort offers cabins, campsites, and tent pads, in addition to a golf course and several restaurant options.

  • Moosomin Lodge Motel: A budget-friendly option that features clean and comfortable rooms with excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Carlyle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saskatoon berry jam: A jar of this delicious jam makes the perfect local souvenir.

  • Handmade crafts: Visitors can find small, unique locally hand made souvenirs in different stores in Carlyle downtown.

Local Culture and Customs of Carlyle

Carlyle has a rich history and culture. Visitors are recommended to show respect for the local customs and traditions while touring the area.

Best Time to Visit Carlyle

The best time to visit Carlyle is during summer between May to August, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without being hindered by cold weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carlyle

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and culture
  • Attend local events and festivals
  • Try local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural parks and outdoor areas
  • Smoke in public areas where smoking in not allowed

Safety and Health

Safety in Carlyle

Carlyle is a safe town but as with any neighborhood, visitors should take care of their belongings and be mindful of their surroundings at night.

Health in Carlyle

Carlyle has adequate healthcare facilities, including pharmacies and a medical clinic that serves local residents and tourists.

Getting to Carlyle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carlyle is accessible by road, air, and bus.

Airports Near Carlyle

  • Regina International Airport, approximately a 2-hour drive from Carlyle
  • Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport, 3 hours and 30 minutes drive.

Bus Stations Near Carlyle

  • STC Bus Depot, 2 hours and 15 minutes away in Estevan, Saskatchewan.
  • Greyhound Canada, 1 hour and 45 minutes away in Moosomin, Saskatchewan.

Other Options

  • Visitors can rent a car and drive to Carlyle from nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Carlyle

Carlyle is a relatively small town, and visitors can easily explore most of the town’s attractions on foot. However, renting a car is recommended for visitors who wish to explore the surrounding natural areas.

Where to Go Next from Carlyle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Regina, the provincial capital of Saskatchewan, located about 2 hours away from Carlyle.
  • Winnipeg, Manitoba, is located roughly 3 hours and 30 minutes away from Carlyle and is known for its vibrant arts scene and iconic locations.

Conclusion

Carlyle has a charm of its own with ample natural scenic beauty, historic landmarks, outdoor activities, and exciting cuisine. Visitors can enjoy the town’s friendly community, rich culture, and beautiful scenery. Plan a trip to Carlyle today and experience Saskatchewan at its finest.
