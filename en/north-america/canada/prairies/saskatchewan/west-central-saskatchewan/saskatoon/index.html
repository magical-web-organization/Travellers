Introduction to Saskatoon

Located in West Central Saskatchewan, Saskatoon is a vibrant city brimming with cultural and natural attractions. Known for its picturesque riverbanks, sprawling parks and trails, and a thriving food and drink scene, Saskatoon is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore Canada’s Prairies.

List of Places to Visit in Saskatoon

Saskatoon is a city with a rich history and diverse culture, boasting numerous museums, galleries, and historical attractions. Here’s a list of places worth visiting when in Saskatoon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Western Development Museum
  • Wanuskewin Heritage Park
  • Ukrainian Museum of Canada
  • Remai Modern Art Gallery
  • Museum of Natural Sciences

Natural Attractions of Saskatoon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Meewasin Valley Trail System
  • Beaver Creek Conservation Area
  • Pike Lake Provincial Park
  • Cranberry Flats Conservation Area
  • Saskatoon Forestry Farm Park & Zoo

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saskatoon

Best Things to Do in Saskatoon: Activities and Tours

Saskatoon offers a wide variety of activities and tours for tourists of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Saskatoon:

  • Ride the Prairie Lily riverboat
  • Go on a walking tour of Saskatoon’s street art murals
  • Visit the Enchanted Forest and Holiday Light Tour
  • Join a cooking class to learn how to make local cuisine
  • Visit a local farmer’s market

Local Culture and Customs of Saskatoon

Saskatoon has a rich history and heritage, with an amalgamation of Indigenous, European, and Métis cultures. Saskatoon’s local customs include:

  • Emphasis on outdoor activities and appreciation for nature
  • Respectful treatment of Indigenous culture and heritage
  • Friendliness and hospitality towards tourists
  • Love for local cuisine and products

Best Time to Visit Saskatoon

The best time to visit Saskatoon is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city is bustling with outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saskatoon

Do:

  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and hiking trails
  • Try local cuisine and visit farmer’s markets
  • Respect the city’s diverse culture and heritage
  • Visit museums and historical attractions

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the city’s natural areas
  • Disrespect Indigenous culture and heritage
  • Ignore traffic laws and pedestrian crossings

Safety and Health

Safety in Saskatoon

Saskatoon is a safe city to visit, but as in any city, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. These include:

  • Avoiding unsafe areas of the city
  • Locking doors and windows when leaving accommodations or vehicles
  • Using caution when using public transportation or walking at night
  • Avoiding confrontations or arguments with strangers

Health in Saskatoon

Saskatoon has a high standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and medical facilities located throughout the city. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before traveling to Saskatoon.
