Introduction to Eston

Located in West Central Saskatchewan, Estonia is a charming prairie town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. With a population of around 1,100 people, Eston offers a unique blend of city convenience and small-town charm.

List of Places to Visit in Eston

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food lover, you won’t run out of things to do in Eston. Here’s a list of the top attractions and activities to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eston Heritage Park Museum: Explore the town’s history through interactive exhibits, vintage machinery, and antique vehicles. You can also visit the Kinchee Cemetery, a historic site that tells the story of early Eston settlers.
  • Eston Main Street: Take a stroll down Main Street to check out the town’s charming architecture, unique shops, and local businesses.
  • Eston Opera House: Catch a show at this historic venue, which has been a local entertainment hub since 1912.

Natural Attractions of Eston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Eston Riverside Regional Park: This beautiful park offers hiking trails, fishing, camping, and stunning views of the surrounding prairie landscape.
  • School Hill: Take a short hike up this hill to enjoy panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • South Saskatchewan River: Take a peaceful canoe or kayak trip down the river to enjoy the serenity of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eston

Eston is a great spot for outdoor enthusiasts, with many scenic biking and hiking trails to explore. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Eston Riverside Regional Park Trail: This trail takes you through the park’s scenic woods and along the river.
  • School Hill Trail: Hike to the top of School Hill for beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Prairie Sky Trail: This scenic trail takes you through open prairies and by several small lakes.

Best Things to Do in Eston: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Eston, including:

  • Fishing: Cast your line in one of the many local bodies of water, such as the South Saskatchewan River or Waseca Lake.
  • Golfing: Hit the links at the Eston Golf Course, a beautiful 9-hole course with stunning prairie views.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided horseback riding tour to explore the beautiful countryside and enjoy the fresh air.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eston

Looking to spend a week in Eston? Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Eston

Eston may be a small town, but it boasts a diverse and delicious culinary scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock: This traditional Indigenous fried bread is a staple in Eston and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Perogies: These Eastern European dumplings filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and bacon are a local favorite.
  • Paska: This sweet bread is a traditional Ukrainian Easter treat that can be found in Eston year-round.

Local Beverages

  • Eston Oasis: This popular drink is a refreshing blend of grapefruit and orange juices, carbonated water, and grenadine.
  • Eston IPA: This locally crafted beer is a hoppy and flavorful brew that pairs well with hearty meals.
  • Saskatchewan Berry Wine: Made from locally grown Saskatoon berries, this wine is a delicious and unique taste of the region.

Where to Stay in Eston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eston offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some top options include:

  • Eston Hotel: This charming historic hotel features comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant and bar, and easy access to local attractions.
  • Eston Motor Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a convenient location.
  • Local Guesthouses: For a more authentic and personalized experience, consider staying in one of the many guesthouses and vacation rentals available in Eston.

What to Buy in Eston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique and locally made souvenirs to bring home from your trip to Eston? Here are some top options:

  • Handmade Crafts: Check out the local markets and craft fairs to find handmade quilts, pottery, and other unique crafts made by local artisans.
  • Honey: Saskatchewan is known for its delicious honey, and Eston is no exception. Look for jars of honey made by local beekeepers.
  • Indigenous Artwork: Visit the local Indigenous-owned galleries to find beautiful and meaningful artwork, jewelry, and crafts made by Indigenous artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Eston

Eston is a diverse and welcoming community with a rich history and culture. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for Indigenous Culture: Eston is located on Treaty 6 territory, and the local Indigenous community plays an important role in the town’s history and culture. Be sure to show respect for Indigenous traditions and customs.
  • Hospitality: Estonians are known for their warm and friendly hospitality. Don’t be surprised if locals strike up a conversation with you or offer to show you around town.
  • Agriculture: Saskatchewan is known for its agricultural industry, and many people in Eston have deep ties to farming and ranching. Show appreciation for the local agriculture industry and consider visiting a local farm or ranch.

Best Time to Visit Eston

Eston is a year-round destination, with something to offer in every season. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. Spring and fall offer beautiful scenery and fewer crowds, while winter provides opportunities for ice fishing, snowmobiling, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eston

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Eston:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Show respect for local traditions and customs, including Indigenous culture and hospitality.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Take advantage of the many hiking, biking, and outdoor activities available in Eston.
  • Try Local Food and Drink: Sample Eston’s diverse and delicious culinary scene, including traditional dishes and craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep Eston’s beautiful parks and waterways clean by properly disposing of trash.
  • Disrespect Wildlife: Saskatchewan is home to many different animal species, including large mammals like bears and moose. Be respectful and mindful of wildlife during your outdoor adventures.
  • Ignore Safety Precautions: Always follow safety guidelines and instructions when participating in outdoor activities, like hiking and canoeing.

Safety and Health

Eston is a safe and welcoming community, but it’s still important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health during your trip.

Safety in Eston

  • Weather: Saskatchewan weather can be unpredictable, so always check the forecast and dress appropriately for the season.
  • Wildlife: As mentioned, be aware of wildlife during your outdoor adventures and follow safety guidelines to avoid encounters.
  • Emergency Services: Know the location of the nearest hospital and emergency services in case of an emergency.

Health in Eston

  • Water: The tap water in Eston is safe to drink, but if you prefer bottled water, it’s widely available at local stores and restaurants.
  • Health Services: The local clinic offers basic medical services, and there are several hospitals and medical centers in nearby cities.

Getting to Eston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eston is easily accessible by car or bus, located just off the Highway 44 and Highway 30 junction. The nearest major airport is the Saskatoon John G. Diefenbaker International Airport, located about 150 km from Eston.

Airports Near Eston

  • Saskatoon John G. Diefenbaker International Airport (150 km)

Bus Stations Near Eston

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Located in nearby Kindersley, 50 km from Eston)

Other Options

  • Car Rental: If you’re flying into Saskatoon, consider renting a car at the airport for convenient access to Eston and surrounding areas.
  • Private Transportation: Many private transportation companies offer car and shuttle services to and from the airport.

How to Get Around in Eston

Eston is a small town and most attractions and activities are easily accessible on foot or by bike. If you need transportation, there are taxis and car rental services available in town.

Where to Go Next from Eston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Saskatchewan and the surrounding areas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Saskatoon: Located about 150 km from Eston, Saskatoon is a vibrant city with a bustling downtown, beautiful riverfront, and many cultural attractions.
  • Cypress Hills: Head south to explore this beautiful and unique natural area, known for its rolling hills, diverse wildlife, and historical sites.
  • Banff National Park: If you’re interested in a longer road trip, head west to Banff National Park for stunning mountain views and endless outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Eston is a hidden gem in Saskatchewan, offering a rich history, beautiful landscapes, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming prairie town. Plan your perfect trip to Eston today!
