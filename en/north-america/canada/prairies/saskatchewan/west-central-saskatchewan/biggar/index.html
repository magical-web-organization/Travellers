Introduction to Biggar

Welcome to Biggar, a charming town in West Central Saskatchewan that offers visitors a true Saskatchewan experience. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Biggar is a must-visit destination for those looking to explore Saskatchewan’s Prairies.

List of Places to Visit in Biggar

Here are some of the top sights and attractions to visit in Biggar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Biggar Museum: This museum showcases the history of the community and features exhibits on the town’s agricultural heritage and Indigenous history.

  • The Majestic Theatre: This historic theatre has been a hub of entertainment in the community since 1911 and still hosts shows and events throughout the year.

  • The CN Station: Once a bustling transportation hub, this historic station now serves as a museum that highlights the town’s railroad history.

Natural Attractions of Biggar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Biggar Dino Park: This park features several dinosaur statues and a replica of a T-Rex skull.

  • Biggar and District Regional Park: Located just outside of town, this park offers visitors the opportunity to hike, swim, camp, fish, and more.

  • Mons Lake: This scenic lake is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, boating, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biggar

Biggar and District Regional Park offers several hiking and biking trails that wind through forested areas and around Mons Lake. One of the most popular routes is the Nature Trail, a 2.5 km loop that takes you through a mixed forest and offers stunning views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Biggar: Activities and Tours

  • Take a historical walking tour of Biggar to learn about the town’s past and see some of its most interesting landmarks.

  • Visit the Biggar Golf Course to test your skills on its challenging greens.

  • Attend a live performance or movie screening at the Majestic Theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biggar

Day 1 - Begin your trip by checking out the Biggar Museum and learning about the history of the town.

Day 2 - Take a hike through Biggar and District Regional Park and try your hand at fishing or swimming in Mons Lake.

Day 3 - Explore the town’s historic architecture and landmarks, including the CN Station and the Majestic Theatre.

Day 4 - Visit the Biggar Dino Park and snap a few photos with the impressive dinosaur statues.

Day 5 - Play a round of golf at the Biggar Golf Course and take in the stunning Prairie views.

Day 6 - Take a day trip to nearby Saskatoon to explore the city’s shopping, dining, and cultural scene.

Day 7 - Spend your final day in Biggar relaxing at the regional park or exploring some of the town’s lesser-known attractions, such as the local farmers market or art gallery.

Food and Drink Scene of Biggar

No trip to Biggar would be complete without sampling some of the town’s delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Perogies: These boiled or fried dumplings are often stuffed with potatoes, cheese, or meat.

  • Saskatoon berry pie: This classic prairie dessert features a sweet filling made from Saskatoon berries, a native fruit of the region.

Local Beverages

  • Great Western Brewing Company: This Saskatoon-based brewery produces a range of beer styles that can be found in bars and restaurants throughout the province.

Where to Stay in Biggar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Canalta Hotel: This modern hotel offers comfortable accommodations and convenient amenities like an indoor pool, fitness center, and free breakfast.

  • Biggar Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and simple rooms located just a short walk from many of the town’s top attractions.

  • Apartment Suites by Gallery on Main: These spacious and well-appointed suites are perfect for families or groups looking to spread out and relax.

What to Buy in Biggar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saskatoon berry jam: Made from locally grown berries, this sweet and tangy spread is a delicious souvenir to bring home.

  • Handmade knitwear: Many local artisans sell cozy and stylish knitwear, perfect for staying warm during Saskatchewan’s chilly winters.

Local Culture and Customs of Biggar

Biggar is known for its friendly locals and strong sense of community. Saskatchewan culture values hard work, hospitality, and a connection to nature, all of which are reflected in the town’s traditions and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Biggar

The best time to visit Biggar is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities like hiking and swimming are at their best. The fall and spring can also be beautiful times to visit, with vibrant foliage and milder temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biggar

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the Prairies by taking a hike, swim, or bike ride through the region’s scenic parks and trails.

  • Embrace the town’s small-town charm by attending local events, such as festivals, fairs, and farmers markets.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the power of the sun. Be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors, as the Prairie sun can be intense.

  • Assume that tipping is not necessary. While Canadian service industry workers are paid a fair wage, tipping is still customary for good service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biggar

Biggar is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as locking their cars and securing valuables in their hotel rooms. Be aware of your surroundings when hiking or biking, and follow all park rules and regulations.

Health in Biggar

Saskatchewan has a universal public healthcare system that provides basic medical services to all residents and visitors. There are several medical clinics and hospitals in the area that are equipped to handle a variety of health issues.

Getting to Biggar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Biggar is located approximately 100 km west of Saskatoon, the nearest major city, and is easily accessible by car or bus.

Airports Near Biggar

The nearest airport is the Saskatoon John G. Diefenbaker International Airport, which offers daily flights to major Canadian and US cities.

Bus Stations Near Biggar

Greyhound offers daily bus service from Saskatoon to Biggar.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a convenient way to get to and explore Biggar. The town is located off of highway 14, which is well-maintained and easy to navigate.

How to Get Around Biggar

The town is small and walkable, making it easy to explore on foot. Biking is also a popular option, with several bike rental shops available in town. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Biggar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saskatoon: Saskatchewan’s largest city is just a short drive away and offers plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

  • The Great Sandhills: Located south of Biggar, this natural wonder features vast expanses of sand dunes and unusual rock formations.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore Saskatchewan’s Prairies or simply want to enjoy a relaxing getaway, Biggar offers something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and friendly locals, it’s easy to see why this charming town is quickly becoming a must-visit destination for travelers from around the world.
