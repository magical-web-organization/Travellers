Introduction to Kindersley

Located in west central Saskatchewan, Kindersley is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of small-town charm and natural beauty. With a population of just over 4,300 residents, Kindersley offers visitors a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. This guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and tips for a safe and enjoyable trip to Kindersley, Saskatchewan.

List of Places to Visit in Kindersley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kindersley and District Plains Museum - Explore the history of Kindersley and its surrounding areas through exhibits featuring artifacts and photographs.
  • Kindersley’s 1913 Heritage Home Museum - Step back in time and experience life in Kindersley during the early 1900s at this living history museum.
  • Teichman Heritage Village - Located just north of Kindersley, this recreated 1920s village features a schoolhouse, church, and several other buildings from the era.

Natural Attractions of Kindersley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Floral Park - This picturesque park is home to a beautiful flower garden, a duck pond, walking trail, and playground.
  • Goose Lake - Enjoy boating, fishing, and picnic facilities at this scenic lake located just west of Kindersley.
  • Kindersley Sandhills Ecological Reserve - Explore the unique sand dunes and diverse wildlife of this ecological reserve located just south of Kindersley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kindersley

  • Kindersley and District Plains Museum Walking Trail - This short walking trail takes visitors through the outdoor exhibits of the Kindersley and District Plains Museum.
  • Floral Park Nature Trail - This paved trail winds through the beautiful flower garden of Floral Park.
  • Kindersley and District Aquatic Centre Trail - Enjoy a leisurely bike ride or walk along this scenic trail that runs parallel to the Kindersley and District Aquatic Centre.

Best Things to Do in Kindersley: Activities and Tours

  • Kindersley Community Golf Course - Enjoy a round of golf on this challenging 18-hole course that features scenic views of the surrounding prairies.
  • Kindersley Kinsmen Water Park - Cool off on a hot summer day at this family-friendly water park that features a wave pool, water slide, and splash pad.
  • Kindersley Street Market and Trade Show - Held annually in June, this popular event features local vendors, live music, and a variety of food options.

Food and Drink Scene of Kindersley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Perogies from The Rusty Owl Restaurant and Pub - This local hotspot is known for their delicious homemade perogies.
  • Steak from The Keg Steakhouse and Bar - Treat yourself to a juicy steak and a glass of wine at this elegant restaurant.
  • Fried Rice from Asian Garden Restaurant - If you’re in the mood for Asian cuisine, this restaurant serves up tasty fried rice and other classic dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer from Barn Hammer Brewing Company - This popular Winnipeg-based brewery has a taproom in Kindersley where you can sample their delicious beers.
  • Wine from Sage Hill Winery - Take a short drive to the nearby town of Webb and visit this award-winning winery that produces a variety of red, white, and dessert wines.

Where to Stay in Kindersley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kindersley Inn - This hotel offers affordable rates and comfortable accommodations in the heart of Kindersley.
  • Kinderhostel - Traveling on a budget? This hostel offers affordable dormitory-style lodging in a cozy rural setting.
  • Kindersley Sunset Cottage - This charming guesthouse on the outskirts of Kindersley offers guests a peaceful retreat with all the comforts of home.

What to Buy in Kindersley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kindersley and District Plains Museum gift shop - Shop for unique souvenirs and gifts at the museum’s gift shop.
  • Local honey from Holmestead Honey - This local honey producer offers a variety of artisanal honey products made with honey harvested from the nearby prairies.
  • Handmade pottery from Kathleen Potter Pottery - Purchase beautiful handmade pottery made by local potter Kathleen Potter.

Local Culture and Customs of Kindersley

Kindersley is known for its friendly locals and hospitable nature. Visitors are encouraged to say hello and strike up a conversation with locals, as they are always happy to share their stories and tales about life in Kindersley.

Best Time to Visit Kindersley

The best time to visit Kindersley is during the summer months (June to August) when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the natural beauty of the town during the fall months (September to November) when the leaves change colors and the air is brisk and refreshing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kindersley

Do:

  • Say hello and strike up a conversation with locals.
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of Kindersley and the surrounding area.
  • Check out local events and festivals, such as the Kindersley Street Market and Trade Show.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in public spaces.
  • Ignore safety guidelines while exploring outdoor areas.
  • Be disrespectful towards local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kindersley

Kindersley is a generally safe and peaceful town, but visitors should still exercise common sense and take precautions to ensure their safety. Keep valuables out of sight and lock your car when parking in public areas. Don’t venture too far off the beaten path when exploring natural areas and always be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kindersley

Visitors to Kindersley should take precautions against insect bites, particularly during the summer months when mosquitoes are prevalent. Carry insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to prevent bites. Visitors should also ensure they have adequate medical coverage while in Kindersley, as medical expenses can be quite high in Canada.

Getting to Kindersley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kindersley

The nearest airport to Kindersley is the Kindersley Regional Airport which offers daily flights to and from Calgary.

Bus Stations Near Kindersley

Greyhound buses service the town of Kindersley.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Kindersley from major cities like Regina or Saskatoon.

How to Get Around in Kindersley

The best way to get around Kindersley is by car. Visitors can rent a car at Kindersley Regional Airport or in nearby cities like Regina and Saskatoon. Bicycles are also a popular way to explore the town, and can be rented from local bike shops.

Where to Go Next from Kindersley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saskatoon - Located just a three-hour drive from Kindersley, Saskatoon is a vibrant city with a thriving arts scene and numerous cultural attractions.
  • Prince Albert National Park - Located three hours northeast of Kindersley, this national park offers visitors a chance to explore the beautiful Boreal Forest and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Grasslands National Park - Located four hours south of Kindersley, this national park is home to some of the last remaining grasslands in North America and is a great place to spot bison and other wildlife.

Conclusion

Kindersley, Saskatchewan offers visitors a chance to relax and immerse themselves in the natural beauty and small-town charm of the region. Whether you’re interested in exploring history and culture, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying good food and drink, Kindersley has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip and discover all the best things to see and do in Kindersley.
