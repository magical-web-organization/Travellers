Introduction to Beausejour

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Manitoba, Beausejour is a charming town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. Known for its friendly locals, quaint shops, and picturesque parks, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical sites, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a peaceful setting, there’s something for everyone in Beausejour.

List of Places to Visit in Beausejour

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, make sure to check out the town’s many cultural attractions. The Beausejour Heritage Society operates three museums including The Beausejour District Museum, The Tyndall Stone Lodge Museum and The Kudryk Museum. Here you will learn about the history of Beausejour dating back to the fur trading era and the settlement of the area.

Natural Attractions of Beausejour: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

What makes Beausejour truly special are the stunning natural attractions that surround it. Perfectly situated for Manitoba’s provincial park system, Beausejour is a launching pad to the boreal forest and a myriad of lakes perfect for fishing and canoeing in our summer months. Bird watchers and photographers will delight in the abundance and diversity of species to be found in the nearby ecologically diverse Marsh and Wetlands Interpretive Centre while enjoying our tranquil prairies and the wildlife that call it home.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beausejour

Beausejour offers a variety of hiking and biking routes to explore the town and its natural surroundings. The Trans-Canada Trail, which is a 4,300 km trail system that winds throughout Canada, passes through Beausejour. Other outdoor activities include exploring the Bird Tail Valley and its diverse trail system, Fresh Air Trail which follows an old rail bed, and, of course, a nice stroll through the town’s colourful parks.

Best Things to Do in Beausejour: Activities and Tours

Beausejour offers a range of activities for all interests. Visit the Brokenhead Wetlands Interpretive Trail and experience the rich wildlife and ecology of Manitoba. Take a guided tour of some of the oldest buildings in the area and learn about the history of Beausejour, which dates back to the early 1800s. For adrenaline junkies, try out the “Frog Jump”, a towering 40 feet cable-climbing tower in the heart of town.

Food and Drink Scene of Beausejour

Must-Try Dishes

Beausejour is a small town with a big appetite. Satisfy any hunger with the town’s delicious cuisine, served from diversive cultures. Sample freshly baked bread from our local, Baker Tom’s, delicious burgers from Riley’s, perogies and pies entrees at the local Golf Course or fill up on our generous portions of Chinese cuisine with the Dragon Boat Restaurant.

Local Beverages

Thirsty travelers will not be disappointed with the locally made beer at the Back Forty Brewing Company. This local brewery uses only the freshest ingredients to produce a range of flavours that are sure to satisfy even the most discerning palates. The town also has several fully licensed establishments to gather for beverages and good conversation with the locals.

Where to Stay in Beausejour: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beausejour has a range of accommodations for any traveler. Choose from cozy guesthouses, family-owned hotels, and well-known chain hotels. Hints of culture will found in our trendy boutique inn or support local entrepreneurs with a stay at the country-side Krazy J’s Bed and Breakfast.

What to Buy in Beausejour: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Beausejour with you and support local vendors by purchasing souvenirs and locally produced items. Check out the weekly Farmers Market for freshly grown produce, local honey and colorful hand-painted art. A trip to the Beausejour Heritage Society may lead you to our “Nosey Nellie” store which carries a variety of unique and interesting souvenirs, ranging from Manitoba and Beausejour-themed, to products regarding many cultures and customs.

Local Culture and Customs of Beausejour

Beausejour is a melting pot of rich, diverse culture and customs. Whether it’s the Northern Canadian furs, or the traditions passed on from settlers who landed from across the sea, Beausejour welcomes all to experience our culture and customs during weekly events such as the Farmers Market, seasonal fairs and annual car and music show.

Best Time to Visit Beausejour

Beausejour is a year-round destination that offers a wide range of activities regardless of the season. The warm summer months are ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and boating, while winter is perfect for skiing, snowmobiling, and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beausejour

Do:

  • Make sure to take part in the town’s many cultural events.
  • Take a hike through our natural trails and take in all Manitoba’s wildlife has to offer.
  • Stop by local vendors and artisans to get a taste of true Beausejour style.

Don’t:

  • Forget your sunscreen and bug spray for outdoor excursions.
  • Litter. Beausejour prides itself on being a pristine town.
  • Forget to embrace the culture and friendly locals, who make their way of life unique to Beausejour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beausejour

Be sure to comply with Beausejour’s common sense safety tips while visiting the area. Never leave valuables unattended and avoid dangerous back-country trails without a proper guide. In case of any emergencies, Beausejour has its own health centre and RCMP detachment.

Health in Beausejour

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Beausejour and there are several local pharmacies and a hospital to get medical attention if needed.

Getting to Beausejour: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beausejour

The Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport is the nearest airport, located 70.6 km away.

Bus Stations Near Beausejour

Beausejour has a bus depot that runs through Manitoba’s own Greyhound Bus Line.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to get to Beausejour.

How to Get Around in Beausejour

Beausejour is a small town that is easily explored on foot. However, travel by car can be preferable if you are exploring the surrounding smaller towns and parks.

Where to Go Next from Beausejour: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Several destinations are within reach of Beausejour, perfect for day trips. Continue your hiking adventure in Whiteshell Provincial Park or visit Hecla, where you since Victorian-era can indulge in the pleasures of fine dining and full-service spas.

Conclusion

Beausejour is a fantastic destination that offers a wide range of attractions for travelers of all ages and interests. With its charming downtown, natural beauty, welcoming locals, and vibrant culture, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Manitoba. Get planning and book your next adventure to Beausejour today!
