Introduction to Sprague

Sprague is a small town located in Eastern Manitoba, in the Prairies of Canada. It is known for its picturesque natural beauty, friendly people, and diverse range of outdoor recreational opportunities. While it may not be a well-known tourist destination, Sprague is definitely worth a visit for those seeking an authentic Canadian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Sprague

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sprague & District Historical Museum

Sprague & District Historical Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. Housed in a former school building, the museum showcases the region’s rich cultural heritage through an extensive collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents. Its exhibits cover various themes, such as agriculture, transportation, and First Nations culture, and are presented in an engaging and informative manner. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the museum to learn more about Sprague’s past.

St. Michael’s Ukrainian Orthodox Church

St. Michael’s Ukrainian Orthodox Church is a magnificent landmark that reflects the town’s vibrant Ukrainian heritage. A designated Manitoba Heritage Site, the church is renowned for its stunning architecture, ornate interior, and beautiful stained glass windows. Visitors can attend a Sunday service or take a guided tour of the church to appreciate its beauty and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Sprague: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sprague River Trail

Sprague River Trail is a scenic hiking trail that runs along the Sprague River. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests, wetlands, and grasslands, and opportunities to spot local wildlife, such as beavers, birds, and moose. The trail is well-maintained, easily accessible, and suitable for all levels of hikers. Visitors can also go fishing or canoeing in the river, or have a picnic in one of its scenic spots.

Sandilands Provincial Forest

Sandilands Provincial Forest is a vast wilderness area ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. It features over 2,000 square kilometers of forests, lakes, rivers, and trails, and offers a range of activities, such as hiking, camping, fishing, hunting, and snowmobiling. Visitors can explore its diverse natural habitats, including boreal forests, wetlands, and grasslands, which are home to numerous species of flora and fauna. Some of the popular spots in the forest include Falcon Lake, Pine Point Rapids, and Crowduck Lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sprague

Sprague River Trail

Sprague River Trail is a 9 km long hiking trail that follows the Sprague River from Sprague to Manigotagan. It is a moderate-level hike that takes about 3 hours round trip. The trail is well-marked, has several scenic viewpoints, and offers opportunities for fishing and canoeing. It is also a popular spot for bird watching, with over 70 species of birds recorded in the area.

Sandilands Provincial Forest Trails

Sandilands Provincial Forest has a network of trails suitable for hiking and biking. The trails range from easy to difficult, and cover various terrains, such as forests, hills, and wetlands. Some of the popular trails include the Pine Point Rapids Trail, the Crowduck Lake Trail, and the Falcon Creek Trail. Visitors can also go off-trail and explore the forest’s remote regions, but they are advised to bring a map and be prepared for rugged conditions.

Best Things to Do in Sprague: Activities and Tours

Fishing Tours

Sprague and the surrounding areas offer excellent fishing opportunities for anglers of all levels. Visitors can go on fishing tours with local guides who know the best spots and techniques for catching fish, such as walleye, northern pike, and bass. Some of the recommended tours include the Sprague River Fishing Tour and the Sandilands Provincial Forest Fishing Tour.

ATV Tours

ATV tours are a thrilling way to explore the rugged terrain of Eastern Manitoba. Visitors can rent ATVs or join guided tours that take them to the most scenic and remote spots in the region, such as the Sandilands Provincial Forest, the Winnipeg River, and the Manigotagan River. The tours are suitable for riders of all experience levels, and provide a unique perspective on the area’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Sprague

Must-Try Dishes

Perogies

Perogies are a traditional Ukrainian dish that is popular in Sprague and other Canadian prairie towns. These delicious dumplings are made of unleavened dough and filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, or meat. They are usually boiled or fried and served with sour cream or gravy. Visitors can try homemade perogies at local restaurants and cafes, such as the Sprague Hotel.

Pickerel

Pickerel, also known as Walleye, is a popular freshwater fish that is abundant in the lakes and rivers of Eastern Manitoba. It is a staple of the local cuisine and can be prepared in various ways, such as pan-fried, grilled, or smoked. Visitors can taste pickerel at local eateries, such as the Sandilands Restaurant.

Local Beverages

Fort Garry Brewing Company

Fort Garry Brewing Company is a craft brewery located in Winnipeg that produces a range of high-quality beers using locally sourced ingredients. Visitors can sample their beers at local bars and restaurants, or take a guided tour of their brewery to learn about the brewing process and history of beer in Manitoba.

Where to Stay in Sprague: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sprague Hotel

Sprague Hotel is a historic hotel located in the heart of Sprague. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, a bar, and a lounge, as well as free Wi-Fi and parking. It is a great choice for visitors who want to experience the town’s traditional hospitality and charm.

Sandilands Motel & Restaurant

Sandilands Motel & Restaurant is a family-friendly motel located in the Sandilands Provincial Forest. It offers cozy rooms, a restaurant, a playground, and a picnic area, as well as easy access to hiking trails and fishing spots. It is a perfect choice for visitors who love nature and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Sprague: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ukrainian Crafts and Souvenirs

Sprague has a strong Ukrainian heritage, and visitors can find a variety of traditional crafts and souvenirs that reflect this culture. Some of the recommended items include embroidered clothes, pottery, wood carvings, and pysanky (Ukrainian Easter eggs). Visitors can buy these items at local markets and gift shops, such as the Sprague & District Historical Museum gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Sprague

Sprague has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its music, dance, food, and architecture. The town is proud of its Ukrainian roots, and celebrates it through various festivals and events, such as the Ukrainian Festival and the Vyshyvanka Day (Ukrainian embroidery day). Visitors are encouraged to participate in these events and learn more about the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sprague

The best time to visit Sprague is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural scenery is at its best. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and boating, and attend local festivals and events. However, Sprague is also charming during the winter months (December to February), when it is covered in snow and offers opportunities for snowmobiling and ice fishing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sprague

Sprague is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. However, visitors should take precautions, such as locking their vehicles, avoiding dark and isolated areas at night, and being aware of their surroundings. They should also follow safety guidelines for outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and boating, and carry a first-aid kit and a cellphone with them.

Health in Sprague

Sprague has a medical clinic and a pharmacy that offer basic health services. Visitors who require urgent medical attention can go to the nearest hospital, which is located in Pine Falls, about 40 km away from Sprague. Visitors should also bring their necessary medication with them, and take precautions for insect bites and ticks, which are common in the area.

Getting to Sprague: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sprague

The nearest airport to Sprague is Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport, which is about 150 km away. Visitors can hire a car or take a bus or taxi to Sprague from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Sprague

Sprague is served by Greyhound Canada bus service, which provides connections to Winnipeg and other towns in Manitoba. The nearest Greyhound bus station to Sprague is located in Beausejour, about 57 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Sprague from the bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Sprague from Winnipeg or other nearby towns, using the Trans-Canada Highway or the Highway 12. It is advisable to have a car or a 4x4 vehicle for exploring the natural areas around Sprague.

How to Get Around in Sprague

Visitors can explore Sprague on foot or by bike, as it is a small town with well-maintained streets and sidewalks. They can also hire a car or a 4x4 vehicle to explore the surrounding areas, such as the Sandilands Provincial Forest and the Winnipeg River. ATV and snowmobile rentals are also available for those who want to venture off-road.

Where to Go Next from Sprague: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pine Falls

Pine Falls is a scenic town located about 40 km away from Sprague. It is famous for its hydro-electric dam, which provides power to the region, and offers a range of recreational activities, such as fishing, swimming, and boating. Visitors can also explore the town’s historic architecture, such as the St. James Anglican Church and the Pine Falls Manitoba Hydro Dam.

Winnipeg

Winnipeg is the capital city of Manitoba, located about 150 km away from Sprague. It is a vibrant and multicultural city that offers a range of attractions, such as museums, art galleries, restaurants, and shopping centers. Some of the recommended places to visit in Winnipeg include the Canadian Museum for Human Rights, the Forks Market, and the Assiniboine Park and Zoo.

Conclusion

Sprague is a hidden gem in the heart of Eastern Manitoba, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Visitors can explore its scenic hiking trails, go fishing or canoeing in its rivers and lakes, taste its local cuisine, and learn about its Ukrainian customs and traditions. They can also venture to nearby destinations, such as Pine Falls and Winnipeg, for more adventures. A visit to Sprague is a great way to experience the authentic Canadian prairie life.
