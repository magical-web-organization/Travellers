Introduction to Pinawa

Located in Eastern Manitoba, Canada, Pinawa is a beautiful town known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic views, and natural beauty. Nestled on the banks of the Winnipeg River, Pinawa offers a wide range of year-round outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, boating, skiing, and snowshoeing.

This travel guide will help you explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, and where to stay in Pinawa. From historical museums to outdoor adventures, we’ll take you on a journey through the hidden gems and must-see destinations of this picturesque town.

List of Places to Visit in Pinawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pinawa Heritage Sundial - A unique sundial featuring a mosaic design made up of over 5,000 ceramic tiles made by local residents.
  • Pinawa Dam Provincial Heritage Park - A hydroelectric dam built in the 1900s, now a park with a walking trail, picnic area, and museum.
  • Pinawa Art Gallery - Showcases the work of local artists and provides workshops and classes throughout the year.
  • Pinawa Historic Walking Tour - A self-guided tour of the historic buildings and landmarks of Pinawa.

Natural Attractions of Pinawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Seven Sisters Falls - A series of picturesque waterfalls located on the Winnipeg River.
  • Pinawa Suspension Bridge - A suspension bridge that spans the Pinawa Channel, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding nature.
  • Edgar Experimental Forest - A research forest dedicated to the study of sustainable forestry practices.
  • Pinawa Beach - A sandy beach on the shores of the Winnipeg River, perfect for a picnic or a swim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pinawa

  • Ironwood Trail - A challenging 9 km hiking trail through the forest, with stunning views of the Winnipeg River.
  • Centennial Trail - A 4.5 km easy-to-moderate trail perfect for hiking, biking, or snowshoeing.
  • Pinawa Channel Trail - A 7 km loop trail along the Pinawa Channel, with excellent opportunities for birdwatching.
  • Whiteshell River Trail - A 7 km easy-to-moderate hiking trail located in the nearby Whiteshell Provincial Park.

Best Things to Do in Pinawa: Activities and Tours

  • Pinawa Golf Course - A beautiful 9-hole golf course located in the heart of Pinawa.
  • Pinawa Marina - Offers boat rentals, fishing gear rentals, and guided fishing tours on the Winnipeg River.
  • Pinawa Motocross Track - A popular dirt bike track for enthusiasts of all levels.
  • Pinawa Horseback Adventure - Provides guided horseback riding tours through the stunning natural beauty of Pinawa.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pinawa

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Pinawa Art Gallery and enjoy a leisurely stroll along the Pinawa Suspension Bridge.
Day 2 Hike the Ironwood Trail and enjoy a picnic at the Seven Sisters Falls.
Day 3 Explore the Pinawa Dam Provincial Heritage Park and learn about the history of hydroelectric power.
Day 4 Take a guided fishing tour on the Winnipeg River with Pinawa Marina.
Day 5 Try your hand at golfing at the Pinawa Golf Course.
Day 6 Take a day trip to the nearby Whiteshell Provincial Park and hike the Whiteshell River Trail.
Day 7 Relax on the Pinawa Beach and enjoy a swim in the Winnipeg River.

Food and Drink Scene of Pinawa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger - A juicy burger made with locally sourced bison meat.
  • Pickerel Cheeks - A local delicacy made with the succulent cheeks of freshly caught pickerel fish.
  • Berries and Cream - A refreshing dessert made with locally grown berries and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Farm Fresh Apple Cider - Made with fresh apples grown in the nearby farms.
  • Saskatoon Berry Wine - A delicious fruit wine made with local Saskatoon berries.
  • Maple Syrup - a staple souvenirs of Canada, Pancakes and waffles will never be the same with this sweetness.

Where to Stay in Pinawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Whitemouth River Campground - A beautiful riverside campground with both RV and tent camping sites.
  • Pinewood Lodge - A cozy lodge located in the heart of Pinawa, featuring an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Pinawa Motel and Park - A family-friendly motel and campground with an indoor pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Pinawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery - Unique and beautiful pottery handmade by local artists.
  • Local Honey - Delicious honey made from the hives of bees that pollinate the nearby flowers and forests.
  • Dried Wild Berries and Jam - A tasty snack and a great souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Pinawa

  • Manitoba Pioneer Festival - A celebration of the pioneering spirit of Manitoba with live music, crafts, and artisanal food.
  • St. George’s Ukrainian Festival - A festival showcasing Ukrainian food, dance, and culture.

Best Time to Visit Pinawa

The best time to visit Pinawa is in the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities. However, Pinawa offers year-round recreational activities, with winter sports such as skiing and snowshoeing being popular in the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pinawa

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Take your trash with you and dispose of it properly.
  • Explore the local culture and customs.
  • Bring a camera to capture the stunning beauty of Pinawa.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Leave food and garbage unattended.
  • Ignore signs and warnings about wildlife and safety hazards.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pinawa

Pinawa is a safe town, but it’s always best to exercise caution and take safety precautions, especially when exploring the outdoors. Be aware of wildlife, weather conditions, and follow all safety guidelines to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Health in Pinawa

Pinawa has a local health center and a pharmacy for minor medical issues. However, for more serious health needs, Winnipeg is the nearest city with a hospital.

Getting to Pinawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pinawa

The Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport is the nearest airport to Pinawa, located approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Pinawa

Greyhound and Evans Transportation offer bus services from Winnipeg to Pinawa.

Other Options

Car rentals are available from the Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport and Winnipeg city. Taxi services are also available in Pinawa.

How to Get Around in Pinawa

The best way to explore Pinawa is by car or bike. However, many of the town’s top attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Pinawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Whiteshell Provincial Park - A stunning park with rocky shores, clear lakes, and scenic hiking trails in the rugged Canadian Shield.
  • Kenora, Ontario - A charming lakeside town with an abundance of recreational activities, including fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Falcon Lake - A popular recreational area with great swimming, fishing, and hiking opportunities.

Conclusion

Pinawa is a hidden gem in Eastern Manitoba, offering a diverse range of year-round outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and natural beauty. From hiking trails to historical sites, there’s something for everyone in Pinawa. Be sure to plan your trip ahead and explore all that this charming town has to offer.
