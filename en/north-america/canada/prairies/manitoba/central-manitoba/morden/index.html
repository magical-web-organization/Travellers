Introduction to Morden

Morden is a charming city located in Central Manitoba, Canada. Home to just over 8,000 people, this friendly community is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery, including colourful prairies, crystal-clear lakes, and stunning rolling hills. Whether you’re looking for a fun outdoor adventure or a relaxing weekend getaway, Morden has something for everyone. This travel guide will help you explore the best attractions, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, and nearby destinations in Morden.

List of Places to Visit in Morden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Canadian Fossil Discovery Centre: If you’re a history buff, don’t miss the Canadian Fossil Discovery Centre. This museum houses one of the largest collections of marine reptile fossils in the world, including the complete skeleton of Bruce, a 43-foot-long marine reptile.
  • Back 40 Walking Trail: This trail is a must-visit for anyone interested in local history. The Back 40 Walking Trail takes you on a journey through the city’s agricultural past, showcasing historic buildings and artifacts from the early 1900s.
  • Morden Heritage Village: Step back in time at the Morden Heritage Village. This living museum features over a dozen historic buildings, including a schoolhouse, church, and blacksmith shop, all of which have been restored to their original 1800s charm.

Natural Attractions of Morden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Minnewasta: Found just south of Morden, Lake Minnewasta is a beautiful natural landmark that’s perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating. The lake is also surrounded by a network of hiking and cycling trails that offer some of the best views of the countryside.
  • Stephenfield Provincial Park: This 88-acre park is situated just west of Morden and offers spectacular views of the surrounding prairies. Summer activities include hiking, birdwatching, and relaxing on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morden

  • Pembina Threshermen’s Museum Trail: This trail offers over 3km of scenic walking through Morden’s beautiful countryside. The trail takes you around the museum grounds and through prairies and forested areas.
  • Minnewasta Trail: This 8km loop is perfect for hiking, cycling, or jogging. Open year-round, the trail takes you through forests, fields, and beside Lake Minnewasta.

Best Things to Do in Morden: Activities and Tours

  • Morden Corn and Apple Festival: This annual event takes place every August and is a great way to experience the city’s local culture and cuisine. Enjoy live music, a parade, and delicious food made from local ingredients.
  • Morden Drive-In Theatre: If you’re looking for a unique movie watching experience, head to the Morden Drive-In. This locally owned theatre screens the latest Hollywood hits under the stars.

Food and Drink Scene of Morden

Must-Try Dishes

  • Prairie Poutine: This Canadian classic gets a local twist, with a hearty helping of Morden’s famous potato wedges topped with local cheese curds and homemade gravy.
  • Lemon Meringue Pie: This tangy dessert is a must-try in Morden, made with fresh local lemons.
  • Perogies: This Ukrainian specialty is a favourite in Morden, filled with potato and cheese and served with sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Farmhouse Brewing Co.: This local brewery is known for its small-batch, handcrafted beers that showcase the best of Manitoba’s agricultural heritage.
  • **Morden’s Own Coffee: ** This coffee roasting company is a local favourite, offering a range of unique blends and roast levels that are perfect for coffee connoisseurs.

Where to Stay in Morden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Canad Inns Destination Centre Garden City: This hotel features an on-site restaurant, indoor pool, and fitness center, and is centrally located in the heart of Morden.
  • Prairie Heart Inn & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable and affordable accommodation, with free Wi-Fi, breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Shur-Gro Farm Services Guesthouse: Located just outside of Morden, this guesthouse offers a peaceful and rustic accommodation experience, with modern amenities and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Morden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Potatoes: Morden is famous for its delicious and fresh potatoes, which you can buy at local farmers markets and shops.
  • Handmade Quilts: The Morden Heritage Village gift shop features a variety of handmade quilts and other local artisanal goods that make for perfect souvenirs.
  • Canadian Fossil Discovery Centre Store: Don’t miss the chance to take home a unique fossil or other local souvenir from the Canadian Fossil Discovery Centre store.

Local Culture and Customs of Morden

Morden is a friendly and welcoming community that prides itself on its strong local history and agricultural heritage. Visitors are sure to be charmed by the city’s laid-back pace of life, delicious local cuisine, and unique cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Morden

The best time to visit Morden is from May to September when the weather is warm, and the outdoor attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morden

Do:

  • Explore the city’s local history and heritage by visiting the Canadian Fossil Discovery Centre and Morden Heritage Village.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor attractions and scenic hiking and biking trails.
  • Try the local cuisine, including the city’s famous potatoes and homemade perogies.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the power of the sun - always wear sunscreen and stay hydrated during the summer months.
  • Ignore the city’s rich cultural heritage and local traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morden

Morden is a very safe city, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should take normal precautions while exploring the city, such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Morden

Morden has excellent medical facilities and access to quality healthcare services. However, visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before arriving in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Morden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morden

  • Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport: Located approximately 116km east of Morden, this airport offers regular flights to major Canadian and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Morden

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers regular bus service to Morden from major cities in Manitoba and across Canada.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available from major international car rental companies at Winnipeg International Airport or at downtown Winnipeg locations.
  • Morden is also easily accessible via car, with good road links to the Trans-Canada Highway.

How to Get Around in Morden

Morden is a small city that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Car rentals are available for visitors who prefer to drive, and taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Morden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Morden is perfectly located for exploring the beauty of Southwestern Manitoba. Nearby destinations include:

  • Winkler: This friendly community is located just east of Morden and offers many of the same quaint small-town charm and agricultural heritage as Morden.
  • Pembina Valley Provincial Park: This park is located near Winkler and is known for its stunning forested trails and beautiful waterfalls.
  • Riding Mountain National Park: Located approximately 4 hours north of Morden, this national park features over 3,000 square kilometres of pristine wilderness, including forests, lakes, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Morden is a hidden gem that’s just waiting to be discovered by travellers from around the world. With its breathtaking natural beauty, charming small-town vibe, and unique cultural heritage, Morden offers something for every type of traveller. Use this travel guide to plan your perfect Morden getaway and start exploring all that this amazing city has to offer!
