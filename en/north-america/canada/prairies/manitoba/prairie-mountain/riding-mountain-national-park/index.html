Introduction to Riding Mountain National Park

Nestled in the heart of the Prairies, Riding Mountain National Park is a nature lover’s paradise. With stunning rolling hills, sprawling forests, and abundant wildlife, this park is a must-visit for those seeking adventure and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Riding Mountain National Park

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Riding Mountain National Park. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lakehouse Museum: Learn about the history of the park and its surrounding area at this fascinating museum.
  • The William Lake Métis Cultural Centre: Explore the rich cultural heritage of the Métis people and their connection to the land.
  • The Onanole Visitor Centre: Stop by this centre to learn about the flora and fauna of the park, and to get tips on the best trails and activities to try.

Natural Attractions of Riding Mountain National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clear Lake: This stunning body of water is perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Bald Hill: Take in panoramic views of the park from this lookout point.
  • Wasagaming Campground: This cozy campground is the perfect base for exploring the park’s many trails and waterways.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Riding Mountain National Park

Riding Mountain National Park boasts some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the country. Here are a few routes to try:

  • The Central Trail: A 21-kilometre loop that takes you through some of the park’s most stunning scenery.
  • The Whirlpool Lake Trail: A challenging hike that rewards you with gorgeous views of Whirlpool Lake.
  • The Bald Hill Trail: A moderate hike that offers unparalleled views of the park from atop Bald Hill.

Best Things to Do in Riding Mountain National Park: Activities and Tours

From kayaking to wildlife watching, there’s no shortage of things to do in Riding Mountain National Park. Here are a few of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the park’s trails on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Wildlife Watching: Keep an eye out for bears, moose, and other animals while exploring the park’s forests and meadows.
  • Kayaking: Take a leisurely paddle along the shores of Clear Lake and enjoy stunning views of the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Riding Mountain National Park

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Riding Mountain National Park, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in the park, set up camp, and explore the Lakehouse Museum
2 Hike the Central Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch
3 Take a guided horseback tour through the park
4 Spend the day on Clear Lake, swimming, boating, and fishing
5 Explore the park’s many scenic lookouts, including Bald Hill
6 Spend the day kayaking along the shores of Clear Lake
7 Depart the park, stopping at the William Lake Métis Cultural Centre on your way out

Food and Drink Scene of Riding Mountain National Park

Riding Mountain National Park is home to a diverse range of culinary experiences, from local delicacies to international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Perogies: These delicious dumplings are a Ukrainian favourite and can be found in many local restaurants.
  • Pickerel: A freshwater fish that’s often served grilled or baked, pickerel is a local delicacy that’s not to be missed.
  • Bison Burgers: These juicy burgers are made from locally-raised bison and are a tasty alternative to traditional beef burgers.

Local Beverages

  • Farm Fresh Milk: Stop by the local dairy farm to pick up fresh milk. It’s a refreshing and healthy alternative to store-bought milk.
  • Manitoba Beer: Sample a locally-brewed beer at one of the many pubs and microbreweries in the area.
  • Saskatoon Berry Wine: This sweet and fruity wine is made from Saskatoon berries, a local specialty.

Where to Stay in Riding Mountain National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Riding Mountain National Park offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy cabins to fully-equipped hotels. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Elkhorn Resort: This luxurious resort features a range of amenities, including a spa and golf course.
  • Wasagaming Campground: This popular campground offers both tent and RV camping options.
  • Moon Lake Resort: Located just outside the park, this resort offers cozy cabins and scenic views.

What to Buy in Riding Mountain National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Be sure to pick up some souvenirs and local products to bring home from Riding Mountain National Park. Here are a few ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts: Stop by the local artisan markets to pick up unique handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  • Manitobah Mukluks: These stylish and cozy boots are made in Manitoba and are perfect for cold winter days.
  • Local Honey: Pick up a jar of local honey as a delicious and healthy souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Riding Mountain National Park

Manitoba’s Indigenous culture is an integral part of the park’s history and identity. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect this important culture.

Best Time to Visit Riding Mountain National Park

The best time to visit Riding Mountain National Park is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny and the park is in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Riding Mountain National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and keep a safe distance from any animals you may encounter.
  • Stay on designated trails to help preserve the park’s delicate ecosystem.
  • Pick up after yourself and leave no trace of your visit.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach wildlife, which can be dangerous to both humans and animals.
  • Litter or leave trash in the park, as this can harm wildlife and damage the environment.
  • Ignore posted signs or warnings, as they are intended to keep you safe.

Safety and Health

Safety in Riding Mountain National Park

The park is generally safe, but visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  • Wildlife: Keep a safe distance from animals and avoid approaching or feeding them.
  • Weather: Be prepared for sudden changes in weather, including thunderstorms and cold snaps.
  • Driving: Be cautious when driving in the park, as wildlife may cross the road unexpectedly.

Health in Riding Mountain National Park

Visitors should be aware of the following health concerns:

  • Ticks: Wear long pants and use tick repellent to avoid tick bites, which can transmit Lyme disease.
  • Sunburn: Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Dehydration: Bring plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during hot weather or strenuous activities.

Getting to Riding Mountain National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The park is located approximately three hours from Winnipeg, Manitoba, and can be reached by car or bus.

Airports Near Riding Mountain National Park

  • Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport (YWG)

Bus Stations Near Riding Mountain National Park

  • Brandon Bus Depot

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available in Winnipeg and Brandon.

How to Get Around in Riding Mountain National Park

The best way to get around the park is by car, although bicycles and on-foot exploration are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from Riding Mountain National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Winnipeg: Manitoba’s capital city is just a few hours’ drive from the park and offers a range of cultural attractions and entertainment options.
  • Brandon: This nearby city offers a range of dining and shopping options, as well as cultural attractions like the Art Gallery of Southwestern Manitoba.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, swim, or just soak up some natural beauty, Riding Mountain National Park has something for everyone. Make sure to plan your trip carefully, and don’t forget to explore all that this stunning park has to offer.
