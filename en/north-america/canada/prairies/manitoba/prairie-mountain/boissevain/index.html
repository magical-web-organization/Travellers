Boissevain, nestled amidst the rolling hills of the Manitoba Prairies, is a town full of charms and surprises. From the rich cultural heritage to the scenic natural landscapes, Boissevain offers a plethora of intriguing sites and activities for travelers of all kinds. This travel guide is here to help you explore the town and get the most out of your trip to Boissevain.

Introduction to Boissevain

Founded in 1884, Boissevain is a town with a rich history. Surrounded by farms and prairies, Boissevain typifies what is best about the rural communities in Manitoba. Its friendly folks, charming downtown, and historic buildings give visitors a glimpse into the past and the perfect place to rest and recharge in a peaceful environment.

List of Places to Visit in Boissevain

There are many exciting places to visit while touring Boissevain, from natural wonders to culturally important museums. Here are the top places to visit while in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boissevain & Morton Heritage Park: This open-air museum offers a glimpse of what life was like for early pioneers in the Prairies. With 18 buildings spread over 12 acres, visitors can experience historic homes, a one-room schoolhouse, a general store, and even a blacksmith shop.
  • International Peace Garden: Located on the US and Canadian border, this garden is one of the most popular attractions in the region. The garden spreads over 2,300 acres and features various attractions, including the 911 Memorial and the Canadian Flag Garden.

Natural Attractions of Boissevain: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boissevain Reservoir: A perfect spot for kayaking, fishing, and picnicking, one cannot miss the charming reservoir that sits just outside the town of Boissevain. With many different species of fish that thrive here, anglers and nature enthusiasts alike will be excited to check this spot out.
  • Optimist Park: A small park with hilltop views of the prairie and the town of Boissevain. Sports enthusiasts can enjoy baseball, soccer, basketball and frisbee golf while taking in the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boissevain

The best thing about Boissevain is its location. The town is surrounded by picturesque prairies, rolling hills, and lush forested areas, making it perfect for hiking or biking. Here are the two most scenic hiking/biking trails:

  • Boissevain Walking Trail: A trail that leads through forests, grasslands, and wetlands with a range of wildflowers and plant species. The trail is often enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.
  • International Peace Garden Trail: The trail is on the US and Canadian border. This gentle trek passes through idyllic prairies, beautiful gardens, and woodlands, making it perfect for cycling or a leisurely hike.

Best Things to Do in Boissevain: Activities and Tours

For visitors seeking unique experiences, Boissevain has a variety of activities to offer. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Agricultural Tours: Manitoba is known for agriculture, and Boissevain is no exception. Take a tour of local farms and producers and see what makes the region so unique.
  • Annual Turtle Fest: Held every year in June, Turtle Fest is a family-friendly celebration that features turtle races, sporting events, live music, and other exciting entertainment.
  • Explore the Local Art Scene: Boissevain has a thriving arts community, and visitors can explore galleries, pottery shops, and even take part in art workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boissevain

Boissevain is a small town, but there is plenty to do for a weeklong trip. Here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Boissevain & Morton Heritage Park.
Day 2 See the International Peace Garden.
Day 3 Go kayaking at Boissevain Reservoir.
Day 4 Take a tour to see the local farms and producers in the area
Day 5 Attend the annual Turtle Fest in June.
Day 6 Explore the local art scene.
Day 7 Take a hike in the Boissevain Walking Trail or explore the International Peace Garden Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Boissevain

Boissevain has a great food and drink scene, perfect for travelers who love to explore local cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes while in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Perogies: Boissevain has a rich Ukrainian heritage, and perogies are a must-try dish.
  • Giant Chocolate Chip Cookies: A favorite of the International Peace Garden gift shop.

Local Beverages

  • Honey: Manitoba is known for producing high-quality honey, and many local farmers sell it fresh from their hives.
  • Manitoba-made beer: With local microbreweries, visitors can enjoy beer brewed right here in the Prairies.

Where to Stay in Boissevain: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boissevain has a variety of lodging options, suitable for any budget. Here are some recommended places to stay while in Boissevain:

  • Barkley Bridge Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is located only a few steps from the center of town and offers free breakfast and plenty of amenities.
  • Camping: In addition to the many hotels in the area, visitors can enjoy a more rustic experience by camping in the local parks and gardens.

What to Buy in Boissevain: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Boissevain is the perfect place to buy locally made items. Here are some items to consider purchasing before heading home:

  • Honey: Manitoba is known for producing high-quality honey, and many local farmers sell it fresh from their hives.
  • Pottery: From coffee mugs to vases, the local pottery scene is thriving in Boissevain.
  • Perogies and Cabbage Rolls: Fresh or frozen, these traditional Ukrainian dishes make great souvenirs and travel snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Boissevain

As with anywhere new, in Boissevain, it is important to respect local customs and culture. Here are some general etiquette practices to keep in mind:

  • Greet locals with a smile: People in Manitoba are generally friendly and happy to chat with visitors.
  • Respect religious customs: Some residents of Boissevain may have customs and practices that differ from your own. It is important to remain open-minded and respectful.

Best Time to Visit Boissevain

The best time to visit Boissevain is between May and September when the weather is at its warmest, and most of the events and festivals are taking place. Keep in mind, the weather in the prairies can be unpredictable, so always dress in layers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boissevain

Do:

  • Do visit local farms and markets: Boissevain and the surrounding area are known for their agriculture roots. Make sure to explore and meet the locals.
  • Do take advantage of outdoor activities: Boissevain has many outdoor activities, like hiking, biking, and kayaking.
  • Do attend the International Peace Garden: The gardens alone are breathtaking, but the 911 Memorial is a must-see.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: It is important to respect the nature and beauty of Boissevain. Make sure to properly dispose of trash.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of the prairie sun: It can get very hot in the summer; make sure to bring plenty of water and sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun’s UV rays.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boissevain

Boissevain is a safe town with a low crime rate. If hiking or doing outdoor activities, make sure to bring a whistle, a fully charged cellphone, and always let someone know where you are going.

Health in Boissevain

Boissevain has a pharmacy and a medical clinic for any minor health issues that may arise.

Getting to Boissevain: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Boissevain is easy. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Boissevain

  • Brandon, Manitoba airport – located approximately 45 minutes east of Boissevain. They offer a range of different airlines and most travel options to and from the airport.
  • Minot International Airport, North Dakota – located approximately 90 minutes south of the International Peace Garden and 2 hours south of Boissevain.

Bus Stations Near Boissevain

  • Greyhound Canada – Boissevain is a stopping point and connection point for buses to/from Winnipeg, Western Canada, and the United States of America.

Other Options

  • Driving: Boissevain is on the Trans-Canada Highway and is an easy drive from either direction. With rental cars and ride-hailing services available, driving is a solid option for many travelers.

How to Get Around in Boissevain

Boissevain is a small town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. For those who prefer to drive, there are car rental and ride-hailing services available.

Where to Go Next from Boissevain: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boissevain is located in Manitoba, a province with many other amazing places to explore. Here are some notable areas to consider visiting after Boissevain:

  • Winnipeg: A little over 2 hours east is Winnipeg, the largest city in Manitoba. The city is known for its diverse cultural heritage and excellent dining scene.
  • Riding Mountain National Park: A three hours’ drive from Boissevain, Riding Mountain National Park is known for its prairie wetlands, boreal forests, and grasslands. The park is a haven for wildlife and a favorite destination for nature lovers.
  • Souris: Located just 15 minutes south of Boissevain, Souris is a small town famous for its swinging bridge and excellent golf courses.

Conclusion

Boissevain is a hidden gem, rich with culture and creativity, surrounded by beautiful scenery, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals making it a perfect destination for travelers who prefer less crowds and busy tourist spots. Whether your interest is in learning about cultural heritage, exploring the outdoors, or just enjoying good food and wine, Boissevain has something to offer everyone. We hope that this travel guide will help you have the most enjoyable and memorable vacation.
