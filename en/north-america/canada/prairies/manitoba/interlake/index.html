Food and Drink Scene of Interlake

Image by Wes Hicks from Unsplash

Must-Try Dishes

Interlake’s food scene is influenced by its diverse cultural heritage, including Ukrainian, German, and Icelandic cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes:

1. Ukrainian Perogies

Perogies are a traditional Ukrainian dumpling filled with potatoes, cheese, and onions, served with sour cream or fried onions.

2. Icelandic Meat Soup

Kjötsúpa, Icelandic meat soup, is a hearty soup consisting of lamb or beef, potatoes, carrots, and onions, simmered for hours.

Local Beverages

1. Ice Wine

Ice wine is a sweet wine made from grapes that have been left on the vine until the first frost. The freezing process concentrates the grapes’ sugars, resulting in a dessert wine with a refreshing acidity.

2. Craft Beer

Interlake is home to several craft breweries, producing artisanal beer made with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Interlake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Interlake offers various types of accommodation, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels and resorts. Here are some of the top options:

Victoria Beach Guest House

The Victoria Beach Guest House is located on Lake Winnipeg, offering stunning views and easy access to the beach. The guest house has several private suites and rooms.

Lakeview Resort and Conference Centre

Lakeview Resort and Conference Centre is a luxurious accommodation with waterfront views. The resort offers a variety of outdoor activities and amenities to keep visitors entertained and relaxed.

What to Buy in Interlake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Image by Kobu Agency from Unsplash

Interlake is known for its artisanal products and locally made crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles. Visitors can also buy locally sourced products such as honey, jams, and maple syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of Interlake

Interlake’s cultural heritage is a blend of the Icelandic, Ukrainian, and German cultures that shaped the region. Visitors can learn about the region’s history and cultural practices through its museums, historic sites, and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Interlake

The best time to visit Interlake is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, the fall months offer beautiful autumn foliage, and the winter months are perfect for winter sports, such as skiing and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Interlake

Do:

  • Respect the region’s natural beauty and wildlife
  • Try the regional delicacies and local products
  • Take part in cultural festivals and activities

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage behind while hiking or camping
  • Disturb the natural habitats and wildlife
  • Disrespect cultural sites and practices

Safety and Health

Safety in Interlake

Interlake is a safe region for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take necessary precautions while hiking or camping in the wilderness.

Health in Interlake

Visitors should bring adequate supplies and medication, especially if they have existing medical conditions. The region has a few hospitals and medical centers, but visitors should have travel insurance.

Getting to Interlake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Interlake

The nearest airport to Interlake is Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport, with flights to and from major Canadian and US cities.

Bus Stations Near Interlake

Greyhound Canada has bus service to Winnipeg, which has a central bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Interlake from Winnipeg.

How to Get Around in Interlake

The best way to get around Interlake is by car, especially if you plan to explore the region’s outdoor destinations. However, taxis are available in cities and towns, and some tour companies offer shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Interlake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Interlake is a perfect starting point for exploring Manitoba’s other attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Churchill

Churchill, located in northern Manitoba, is a remote town known as the “Polar Bear Capital of the World.”

Winnipeg

Winnipeg, the capital of Manitoba, is a vibrant city with cultural attractions, delicious food scene, and plenty of shopping options.

Conclusion

Interlake offers an exciting mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and scenic beauty. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a returning traveler, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see in this spectacular region of Canada’s Prairies.
