A Comprehensive Travel Guide to West Interlake, Manitoba

Located in the heart of Interlake in Manitoba, Canada, West Interlake is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its charming small towns, vast lakes, and ample opportunities for outdoor adventures, West Interlake is a perfect destination for anyone looking for a relaxing yet exciting getaway.

Introduction to West Interlake

West Interlake is situated in the heart of Manitoba’s Interlake region, which is part of the Prairies in Canada’s North America. This region includes several rural communities, each with its distinct charm and offerings. It is surrounded by Lake Winnipeg, which is the sixth-largest freshwater lake in Canada, and is home to an array of wildlife and vegetation. West Interlake is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, scenic lakes, meandering rivers, and rolling hills.

List of Places to Visit in West Interlake

There are several places to visit in West Interlake, each with its own unique offerings. Here are some of the must-visit attractions and destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. New Iceland Heritage Museum: This museum is a great place to learn about the history of the Icelandic settlement in Manitoba and its arduous journey.
  2. Lower Fort Garry National Historic Site: Located in Selkirk, this fort dates back to 1830 and played a significant role in Canada’s fur trade and the Red River settlement.
  3. Grosse Isle Heritage Site: This site offers a glimpse into the lives of early Scottish settlers, who arrived in Canada during the nineteenth century.

Natural Attractions of West Interlake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hecla Island Provincial Park: This park boasts over 40 km of hiking and biking trails, sandy beaches, and stunning lakeside views.
  2. Oak Hammock Marsh Interpretive Centre: Home to over 300 species of birds and other wildlife, this wetland is an ideal spot for nature lovers and birdwatchers.
  3. Netley-Libau Marsh Provincial Park: This park offers stunning views of Lake Winnipeg and is known for its excellent fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Interlake

West Interlake is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Hecla Island Trails: The trails in Hecla Island Provincial Park offer some of the best hiking and biking opportunities in the region. The scenic views of vast lakes, boreal forests and wildflowers are exquisite.
  2. Gimli Walk and Bike Trail: This 5.5 km trail runs along the beachside of Lake Winnipeg and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.
  3. Lake Winnipeg Ice Trail: During the winter, the frozen lake offers a unique opportunity for hikers and bikers to explore the snow-covered beauty of the park.

Best Things to Do in West Interlake: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing: West Interlake is home to some of the best fishing spots in Manitoba. Anglers can catch walleye, northern pike, and whitefish in the region’s numerous lakes and rivers.
  2. Golfing: West Interlake has several golf courses, including the popular Hecla Golf Course, which boasts a beautiful view of the lake.
  3. Horseback Riding: Embark on a horseback trail ride at one of West Interlake’s ranches, including the Gypsumville Giddy-Up Ranch or Wilderness Spirit.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Interlake

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your West Interlake vacation:

Day Activities

7 Take a boat tour of Hecla Island and its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of West Interlake

West Interlake has foodie delights for every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish Fry: Freshly caught fish served with fries, a salad, and tartar sauce—a popular dish in the region.
  2. Pancake Breakfast: West Interlake is known for its homemade pancakes served with farm-fresh butter and pure maple syrup.
  3. Wild Rice Soup: A traditional soup made in the region using locally sourced wild rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Farm-Fresh Milk: The region is home to several dairies that sell farm-fresh milk from their cows, perfect for a cold glass of milk or the bases of a cheese-making adventure.
  2. Craft Beer: With the rise of craft breweries in the area, indulge yourself and try some beer brews from the several microbreweries found in the region.
  3. Wine tasting: Manitoba’s wine industry is thriving, and West Interlake is increasingly becoming popular for wine tasting.

Where to Stay in West Interlake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Interlake has a range of lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  1. Lakeview Gimli Resort & Conference: Overlooking the beach of Lake Winnipeg, the resort has several amenities, including a spa, indoor pools, restaurants, and a conference centre.
  2. Montcalm Heritage Estate B & B: This charming bed and breakfast is situated in a hundred-year-old estate, offering guests a luxurious and homely stay.
  3. Bear Country Cottages: A cozy and comfortable stay that offers picturesque views of the surrounding wilderness and Lake.

What to Buy in West Interlake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

West Interlake has unique local products that make perfect souvenirs, including:

  1. Wild Rice: Manitoba Wild Rice is a popular local product, which is produced in West Interlake. You can use it to prepare delicious dishes or as a gift to family and friends.
  2. Handmade Crafts: From moccasins to carvings, the region offers incredible handmade products made by Indigenous peoples, perfect for decorating your home or gifting friends and family.
  3. Natural Beauty Products: The region has several artisans that make skincare and beauty products from natural ingredients available in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of West Interlake

West Interlake has a rich cultural heritage, which is celebrated throughout the year. Some popular celebrations in the region include the Gimli Film Festival, which is held in July, and The Icelandic Festival of Manitoba, which is held in August. Both festivals celebrate the region’s diversity while providing a platform for artists, filmmakers, and performers to showcase their talent.

Best Time to Visit West Interlake

The best time to visit West Interlake is during the Summer season, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and water sports are in full swing. However, Fall and Winter have unique events and attractions, including the Northern Owl Fest, a bird festival held in November, and snowmobiling adventures during the Winter season.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Interlake

Do:

  1. Respect the Indigenous Peoples and their traditions.
  2. Prepare your fishing and hunting licenses before venturing into the region.
  3. Take advantage of the local food and beverage scene, including fish fry, craft beer, and handcrafted wine.

Don’t:

  1. Leave your trash behind.
  2. Intrude or disturb the wildlife.
  3. Ignore the weather forecasts; the region can experience a sudden change in weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Interlake

West Interlake is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always essential to be vigilant and take precautions against health and safety risks. Follow the safety guidelines and signs in the parks, hiking trails, and fishing areas.

Health in West Interlake

Manitoba is known for its health care system, ensuring that you are in good hands when traveling in the province. However, always carry your prescription medication with you and bring a first aid kit while venturing outdoors.

Getting to West Interlake: Transportation Options and Logistics

West Interlake is accessible via road, air, and rail.

Airports Near West Interlake

The closest airport to West Interlake is Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport, which is approximately 83 km away.

Bus Stations Near West Interlake

Major bus companies operate regular services to Selkirk, which is the closest bus stop. From there, travelers can arrange for taxis or car rentals.

Other Options

Driving to West Interlake is the best and most comfortable option, with ample spaces for parking. Renting a car is recommended as it allows for more flexibility and easier access to the region.

How to Get Around in West Interlake

The best way to explore West Interlake is by car as public transport services are minimal. There are several rental car companies in Winnipeg; alternatively, you can rent a car or bike from a local agency in West Interlake.

Where to Go Next from West Interlake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West Interlake is surrounded by several destinations worth exploring, including:

  1. Winnipeg: The capital of Manitoba offers a rich cultural and culinary heritage, museums, and art galleries.
  2. The Whiteshell Provincial Park: A 2-hour drive from West Interlake, the park boasts breath-taking landscapes and several hiking trails.
  3. The Northern Manitoba: A vast wilderness area that offers out-of-this-world adventures like polar bear spotting and viewing the Northern Lights.

Conclusion

West Interlake is a hidden gem that offers a vibrant culture, stunning natural beauty, and tasty food and drink scene. With its rich heritage and numerous attractions, West Interlake is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a relaxing yet exciting getaway. Plan your trip now to explore the region’s significant historical landmarks, enjoy outdoor activities, and indulge in delicious local cuisine.
