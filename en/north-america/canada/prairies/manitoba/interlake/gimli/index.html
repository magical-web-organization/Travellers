Introduction to Gimli

Gimli is a picturesque town located in Interlake, Manitoba, on the shores of Lake Winnipeg. It is a place of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and friendly people. There are many fun things to do and see in Gimli, from exploring its museums and historical sites to hiking in its scenic forests and biking along its peaceful coastline. Visitors can also enjoy the local food, beverages, and festivals that showcase the vibrant culture of Gimli. In this guide, we will give you a comprehensive overview of Gimli’s attractions, activities, accommodations, and more to help you plan your trip and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Gimli

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Iceland Heritage Museum: This museum is a must-see for anyone interested in the history and culture of Gimli and its Icelandic roots. The exhibits cover the early settlement days, the fishing industry, and the contributions of the Icelandic people to the development of the region. It also features a genealogy center where visitors can research their family history.
  • Gimli Glider Exhibit: This exhibit is dedicated to the famous Air Canada Flight 143 that made an emergency landing in Gimli in 1983. It tells the story of the event and displays artifacts and memorabilia related to it.
  • Lady of the Lake: This historic lighthouse was built in 1910 and is now a popular landmark and visitor attraction in Gimli. Visitors can climb to the top for panoramic views of Lake Winnipeg.

Natural Attractions of Gimli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaches: Gimli has several beautiful beaches where visitors can swim, sunbathe, and enjoy water sports such as kayaking and paddleboarding. They include Gimli Beach, Sandy Hook Beach, and Winnipeg Beach.
  • Hecla Provincial Park: This park is located just a short drive from Gimli and offers an array of outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, fishing, and wildlife watching. It also has a golf course and a spa for those who prefer a more relaxing experience.
  • Gimli Wildlife Management Area: This area is a bird-watching paradise, with over 200 bird species recorded here. Visitors can also hike, bike, and horseback ride through the scenic wetlands and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gimli

  • Gimli Hiking Trail: This trail is a 3 km loop along the lake and through the woods, offering stunning views of the lake and the town. It is an easy hike suitable for all ages and skill levels.
  • Lake Winnipeg Shoreline Cycling trail: This trail is a 46 km route that runs along the coastline of Lake Winnipeg, from Selkirk to Gimli. It is a scenic and challenging ride that takes you through beaches, forests, and small towns.

Best Things to Do in Gimli: Activities and Tours

  • Viking Village: This attraction is a replica of a Viking-age settlement that showcases the life and culture of the Vikings who settled in the area. Visitors can learn about Viking crafts, customs, and food, and even dress up in Viking costumes for photos.
  • Fisherman’s Festival: This annual festival in July celebrates Gimli’s fishing heritage with live music, food, and fun activities such as fishing derbies and boat races.
  • Gimli Film Festival: This popular film festival in July features Canadian and international films, workshops, and special events.
  • Grindstone Provincial Park: This park is located on the eastern shore of Lake Winnipeg and features sandy beaches, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Gimli

Must-Try Dishes

  • Icelandic Fish and Chips: This classic dish features lightly battered fish and crispy fries, a Gimli local favourite.
  • Lefse: A thin, soft Norwegian flatbread, perfect for a quick snack on-the-go.
  • Perogies: These dumplings stuffed with mashed potatoes, cheese, and bacon are a popular Ukrainian dish that is enjoyed in Gimli as well.
  • Smoked Goldeye: A Manitoba delicacy - small, smoked fish, usually served with cream cheese and crackers.

Local Beverages

  • Souris River Brewing: A locally made craft beer and cider made with Manitoba hops and apples.
  • Forty Creek Whisky: A Canadian whisky brand produced in Gimli, available for tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Gimli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lakeview Resort and Conference Centre: A full-service resort with a pool, spa, and restaurant, located on the lake shore.
  • Gimli Beach Club: A beachfront hotel with modern amenities that is perfect for families or couples.
  • Evergreen Gate Bed & Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast located in a 100-year-old house within walking distance of downtown.

What to Buy in Gimli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Icelandic Wool Sweaters: Keep warm with a hand-knitted wool sweater.
  • Fisherman’s Tote Bags: Made from recycled fishing nets, these eco-friendly tote bags are practical and stylish.
  • Local Honey: Honey produced by local beekeepers, with unique flavours such as clover and wildflower.

Local Culture and Customs of Gimli

Gimli has a rich cultural heritage influenced by its early settlers, who came from Iceland, Ukraine, and other European countries. The town is proud of its heritage and celebrates it through festivals, museums, and cultural events. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in the natural beauty of Gimli and its surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Gimli

The best time to visit Gimli is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is buzzing with festivals and activities. However, Gimli is also a popular winter destination, with its frozen lake and snow sports such as ice fishing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gimli

Do:

  • Take a stroll along the lake shore and watch the beautiful sunset.
  • Visit the local farmers’ market on Saturdays and buy fresh produce and crafts.
  • Respect the wildlife and natural surroundings, and do not litter or disturb the animals.

Don’t:

  • Swim in areas designated as unsafe or unguarded.
  • Touch or feed wild animals such as bears and moose.
  • Park your car on the roadside or in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gimli

Gimli is a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should take usual precautions to ensure their safety. Lock your car and store valuables out of sight, especially when parking in remote areas. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in dimly lit areas.

Health in Gimli

The town has a clinic and pharmacy, but visitors with serious medical conditions should seek medical care in larger cities nearby. It is essential to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you when traveling.

Getting to Gimli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gimli

  • Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport: Located about 100 km south of Gimli, it is the closest major airport, with domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Gimli

  • Greyhound Canada: A bus station located in Selkirk, 30 km south of Gimli, with connections to major Canadian cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Gimli is accessible via Highway 9 from Winnipeg, about a 1-hour drive.
  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at the Winnipeg airport and in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Gimli

Gimli is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Rental cars are also available for those who prefer more flexibility and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Gimli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Winnipeg: Manitoba’s capital city is located about an hour’s drive south of Gimli and offers a wide range of attractions such as museums, galleries, parks, and restaurants.
  • Hecla Island: A beautiful island located about 20 km north of Gimli, known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and luxury spa.
  • Lake Manitoba: A large lake located about 100 km west of Gimli, offering fishing, boating, and wildlife watching opportunities.

Conclusion

Gimli is a hidden gem of Manitoba, with its unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and friendly people. Whether you enjoy outdoor adventures, historical sites, or local cuisine, Gimli has something for everyone. Plan your trip and explore this charming town and its surrounding areas. You will surely have an unforgettable experience in Gimli.
