St. Laurent is a small community located in the Interlake region of Manitoba, in the heart of the Canadian Prairies. Known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals, St. Laurent is a must-see destination for anyone traveling through the region. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in St. Laurent.

Introduction to St. Laurent

St. Laurent is a small, picturesque community located on the western shore of Lake Manitoba. The town was established in the early 1900s and quickly became a hub for agriculture and fishing in the region. Today, St. Laurent is known for its charming small-town feel, rich history, and scenic natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in St. Laurent

St. Laurent is home to a wide variety of attractions and activities. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Laurent Heritage Centre - This museum showcases the history of St. Laurent and the surrounding region, with exhibits on everything from local agriculture to the town’s founding.
  • Saints-Martyrs-Canadiens Catholic Church - This beautiful church was built in the early 1900s and has become a local landmark thanks to its stunning stained glass windows and ornate architecture.

Natural Attractions of St. Laurent: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Manitoba - St. Laurent is located on the western shore of Lake Manitoba, which offers stunning views and a variety of outdoor activities, including fishing and boating.
  • Manipogo Provincial Park - This park is located just outside of St. Laurent and offers visitors access to hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic viewpoints.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Laurent

St. Laurent offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking routes in Manitoba. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Manipogo Provincial Park Trails - This park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes.
  • St. Laurent River Trail - This trail follows the scenic St. Laurent River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in St. Laurent: Activities and Tours

St. Laurent offers visitors plenty of things to see and do. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Fishing - St. Laurent is known for its excellent fishing, especially for walleye and northern pike.
  • Boating - Lake Manitoba offers visitors plenty of opportunities for boating, from motorized boats to kayaks and canoes.
  • Guided Tours - The St. Laurent Heritage Centre offers guided tours of the town and surrounding area, providing visitors with a deeper insight into the history and culture of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Laurent

If you’re planning to spend a week in St. Laurent, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

1 Arrive in St. Laurent, visit the St. Laurent Heritage Centre

Food and Drink Scene of St. Laurent

St. Laurent is home to a variety of local foods and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye Fillet - Freshly caught walleye is a staple of the local cuisine, and can be found in many restaurants around town.
  • Perogies - These delicious dumplings filled with potatoes and cheese are a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Farm-Fresh Milk - St. Laurent and the surrounding region are home to many dairy farms, which produce some of the freshest and most flavorful milk in the region.
  • Craft Beer - Manitoba has a thriving craft beer scene, and St. Laurent is home to several local breweries.

Where to Stay in St. Laurent: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Laurent offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • St. Laurent Motel - This motel offers comfortable and affordable accommodations, as well as easy access to all the top attractions in St. Laurent.
  • Lakeview Resort and Conference Centre - This luxury hotel is located just outside of St. Laurent and offers stunning views of Lake Manitoba and the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in St. Laurent: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Laurent is home to a variety of local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Handmade Crafts - St. Laurent and the surrounding regions are home to many talented artisans, who produce a wide variety of handmade crafts.
  • Local Honey - Manitoba is home to many beekeepers, who produce some of the finest honey in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Laurent

St. Laurent has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of French, Indigenous, and European influences. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to learn more about the region’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit St. Laurent

The best time to visit St. Laurent is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Laurent

When visiting St. Laurent, there are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the local hiking and biking trails.
  • Try some of the local food and drinks.
  • Learn more about the history and culture of the region.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disturb the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

St. Laurent is a safe and welcoming community, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take basic precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in St. Laurent

  • Keep valuables out of sight and locked in your accommodations.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.
  • Always wear a lifejacket when boating on the lake.

Health in St. Laurent

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from the sun and bugs.
  • Check for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to St. Laurent: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Laurent is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near St. Laurent

  • Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport - This airport is located approximately 2 hours south of St. Laurent and offers connections to major cities across North America.

Bus Stations Near St. Laurent

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - This company offers daily bus service to and from Winnipeg, with connections to other major Canadian cities.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Winnipeg to St. Laurent.

How to Get Around in St. Laurent

St. Laurent is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. However, visitors who plan to explore the surrounding area will likely need a car. Taxis and rental cars are available in town.

Where to Go Next from St. Laurent: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Laurent is located near many other popular destinations in Manitoba. Here are some nearby places to consider visiting:

  • St. Ambroise Beach Provincial Park - This park is located just a short drive from St. Laurent and offers a beautiful sandy beach and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Dauphin - This charming town is located approximately 1 hour north of St. Laurent and is known for its beautiful parks, historic architecture, and friendly locals.

Conclusion

St. Laurent is a hidden gem in the heart of Manitoba. With its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals, it’s a must-see destination for anyone traveling through the Prairies. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in St. Laurent.
