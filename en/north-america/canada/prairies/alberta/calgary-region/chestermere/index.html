Chestermere is a small but beautiful city in the Calgary Region of Alberta, Canada. It is known for its breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains, outdoor recreational activities, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, Chestermere has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through some of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay and eat in Chestermere.

Introduction to Chestermere

Chestermere is a city in the Prairies of Canada, located just 20 minutes east of Calgary. It is known for its beautiful lake, which is a popular destination for watersports and fishing. The city is also home to many parks and trails, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city has a rich history, with evidence of indigenous occupancy dating back over 8000 years, and was settled in the late 1800s by European immigrants.

List of Places to Visit in Chestermere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the rich history of Chestermere, there are many museums and historical places to visit. The Chestermere Historical Foundation operates the Chestermere Museum, which houses exhibits about the history of the city and the surrounding area. You can also visit the Nan Boothby Memorial Library, which has a collection of historical books and documents.

Natural Attractions of Chestermere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chestermere is blessed with natural beauty, and there are many outdoor adventures and scenic views to enjoy. The Chestermere Lake is a popular destination for water activities, including swimming, boating, and fishing. The city has many parks, including Anniversary Park, which has a beach, picnic facilities, and a water park. You can also take a hike or bike ride on the many trails throughout the city, such as the Rotary/Mattamy Greenway trail system.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chestermere

Chestermere has many hiking and biking routes that offer visitors beautiful scenery and a chance to escape from the bustle of the city. One of the best places to hike is the Kananaskis Country, which offers a variety of trails for all skill levels. If you’re an experienced mountain biker, you’ll love Moose Mountain, which has over 60 km of trails.

Best Things to Do in Chestermere: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to do in Chestermere, whether you’re looking for a thrill or a relaxing experience. The Blue Devil Golf Club is a beautiful 18-hole golf course that offers stunning views of the lake and the Rocky Mountains. If you’re looking for an adrenaline rush, you can go skydiving or paragliding. For a more relaxing experience, you can take a guided tour of the city’s historical sites or go on a scenic boat ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chestermere

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Chestermere:

Day 1: Visit the Chestermere Museum and explore the city’s historical sites. Day 2: Go hiking or mountain biking on the Kananaskis Country. Day 3: Play a round of golf at the Blue Devil Golf Club. Day 4: Take a scenic boat ride on the Chestermere Lake. Day 5: Explore the city’s parks and trails, such as the Rotary/Mattamy Greenway trail system. Day 6: Go skydiving or paragliding for an adrenaline-filled day. Day 7: Visit a local brewery or winery to sample the city’s beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Chestermere

Must-Try Dishes

Chestermere has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many restaurants and cafes that offer a wide variety of local and international cuisine. Some must-try dishes include Alberta beef, poutine, Bison burger, and maple bacon donut.

Local Beverages

Chestermere is known for its craft beer and wine. Some of the best places to try local beverages are Blindman Brewing and Troubled Monk Brewery. You should also try local ice wine from Symphony in the Park Winery.

Where to Stay in Chestermere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chestermere has many options for accommodation, ranging from hotels to guesthouses. Some of the best hotels include Best Western Premier Calgary Plaza Hotel & Conference Centre and Holiday Inn Express & Suites Calgary South - Medical Area. For a more local experience, you could stay in a guesthouse or bed and breakfast, such as Jim and Penny’s Farm.

What to Buy in Chestermere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Chestermere, you can find many options in the local shops and markets. Some popular items include Alberta beef jerky, maple syrup, and handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Chestermere

Chestermere has a diverse and welcoming community that values its cultural heritage. The local culture is influenced by the area’s indigenous tribes, European settlers, and modern-day immigrants. English and French are the official languages of Chestermere.

Best Time to Visit Chestermere

The best time to visit Chestermere is during the summer (June to August), when the weather is warm, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the city is also beautiful during the fall (September to November), when the leaves change colors, and the air is crisp.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chestermere

Chestermere is a safe city, with low crime rates. However, you should still exercise caution and common sense, especially when in crowded areas or at night.

Health in Chestermere

Chestermere has excellent healthcare facilities, including the Chestermere Community Health Centre. It is also recommended that you have travel insurance when visiting Canada.

Getting to Chestermere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chestermere is located just 20 minutes east of Calgary, making it easily accessible by car. There are also several airports and bus stations near Chestermere that offer transportation options to and from the city.

Airports Near Chestermere

The nearest airport to Chestermere is Calgary International Airport, which is approximately 30 minutes away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Chestermere

The nearest bus station to Chestermere is the Calgary Greyhound Station, which is approximately 20 minutes away by car or bus.

Other Options

You can also rent a car, take a taxi, or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft to get to and from Chestermere.

How to Get Around in Chestermere

The best way to get around Chestermere is by car or bike. The city also has a public transportation system, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Chestermere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for nearby destinations and excursions from Chestermere, you can visit the Rocky Mountains, Banff National Park, or the city of Calgary.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or local food and drink, Chestermere has something for everyone.
