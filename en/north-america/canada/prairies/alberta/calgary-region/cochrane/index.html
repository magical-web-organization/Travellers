Introduction to Cochrane

Cochrane is a charming town located in the Calgary Region of Alberta, Canada. Known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and outdoor recreation opportunities, Cochrane is a must-visit destination for travelers of all types. The town is surrounded by breathtaking mountains and valleys, and its historic downtown area boasts a unique blend of shops, restaurants, and services.

List of Places to Visit in Cochrane

Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying the local food and drink scene, Cochrane has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Cochrane:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cochrane Historical Museum - Learn about the town’s fascinating history through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • Mackay’s Ice Cream - A local institution since 1948 and the oldest remaining ice cream shop in Alberta.
  • Glenbow Ranch Provincial Park - A sprawling park with over 1,300 hectares of protected land, featuring hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic vistas.

Natural Attractions of Cochrane: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bow River - A popular spot for fishing, rafting, and canoeing, the Bow River offers stunning views of the Rocky Mountains.
  • Cochrane Ranche Historic Park - A former working ranch, the park now boasts lush green spaces, picnic areas, and trails for hiking and biking.
  • Big Hill Springs Provincial Park - A scenic park with natural springs, hiking trails, and dense forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cochrane

Cochrane has numerous hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. From beginner to advanced trails, the town offers something for everyone. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Mitford Park Loop - A scenic loop trail that takes you through dense forest and offers sweeping views of the Bow River.
  • Cochrane Ranche Trail System - A network of trails that winds its way through the Cochrane Ranche Historic Park.
  • Bow Valley Provincial Park - A sprawling park with over 13 hiking and biking trails, ranging in difficulty from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Cochrane: Activities and Tours

Cochrane offers a wide variety of activities and tours for all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Cochrane:

  • Cochrane Farmers’ Market - A bustling market featuring local produce, crafts, and food items.
  • Cochrane Polar Dip - An annual event in which brave souls take a dip in the frigid Bow River to raise money for charity.
  • Cochrane Ranchehouse - A large event space that hosts a variety of concerts, festivals, and other cultural events throughout the year.

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to Cochrane:

Food and Drink Scene of Cochrane

Cochrane boasts a diverse and delicious food and drink scene. From locally sourced cuisine to craft beer and wine, visitors are sure to find something to satisfy their taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta beef - Famous for its quality and flavor, Alberta beef is a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Poutine - A classic Canadian dish made with french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Butter tarts - A sweet and flaky pastry filled with a buttery mixture of sugar, eggs, and syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Big Hill Ciderworks - A local cidery producing refreshing hard apple ciders.
  • Half Hitch Brewing Company - A locally owned brewery with a range of craft beers, including their popular Bulldogger Brown Ale.
  • Tannery & Company - A boutique winery crafting unique blends of handpicked fruit wines, mead, and cider.

Where to Stay in Cochrane: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cochrane has a variety of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Cochrane:

  • Days Inn by Wyndham Cochrane - A budget-friendly hotel featuring free breakfast and a fitness center.
  • Ramada by Wyndham Cochrane - A pet-friendly hotel with an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Cochrane Guest House - A cozy bed and breakfast with personalized service and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Cochrane: Souvenirs, and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Cochrane will find a variety of unique souvenirs and locally made products to bring home. Here are some of the top items to buy in Cochrane:

  • Cochrane Ranchehouse Cookbook - A collection of recipes from local chefs using fresh, locally sourced ingredients.
  • Cochrane Farmers’ Market Products - Locally grown produce, artisanal cheeses, homemade preserves, and more.
  • Artisanal Gifts - Handcrafted jewelry, pottery, and textiles made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Cochrane

Cochrane has a unique and vibrant local culture. The town is proud of its western heritage and hosts many cultural events throughout the year, including rodeos and music festivals. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dressing appropriately for cultural events and being mindful of local religious beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Cochrane

The best time to visit Cochrane depends on your interests. For outdoor enthusiasts, the summer months (June-August) offer the best weather for hiking and other outdoor activities. Fall (September-November) is a great time to visit for the colorful autumn foliage and harvest festivals. Winters (December-February) are cold but offer opportunities for skiing and other winter sports. Spring (March-May) is an ideal time to visit for mild weather, wildflowers, and shoulder-season prices.

  • Do explore the local food and drink scene.
  • Do attend local cultural events and festivals.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t engage in loud or disruptive behavior in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cochrane

Cochrane is a safe destination for visitors. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows, being aware of their surroundings, and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Cochrane

Cochrane has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and have adequate travel insurance to cover medical emergencies.

Getting to Cochrane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cochrane

The nearest airport to Cochrane is the Calgary International Airport (YYC), located approximately 35 km southeast of the town. Visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle service to Cochrane.

Bus Stations Near Cochrane

Greyhound Canada operates a bus service that stops in Calgary, approximately 35 km southeast of Cochrane. Visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle service to Cochrane.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cochrane from other destinations. Highway 22 runs north-south through the town, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Cochrane

Visitors can get around Cochrane by walking, biking, or driving. The town is compact and easy to navigate, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Biking is a great way to explore the town’s many trails and parks. Visitors can also rent a car or use a taxi service for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Cochrane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cochrane is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in Alberta. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Banff National Park - A stunning park with hiking trails, lakes, and mountain views.
  • Calgary - Alberta’s largest city, known for its museums, galleries, and cultural events.
  • Canmore - A charming mountain town known for its outdoor recreation opportunities and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Cochrane is a hidden gem in Alberta, offering something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich history, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying the food and drink scene, Cochrane is a must-visit destination. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your Cochrane itinerary and make the most of your visit.
