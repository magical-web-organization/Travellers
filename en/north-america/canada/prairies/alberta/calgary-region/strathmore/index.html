Introduction to Strathmore

Nestled in the Calgary Region of Alberta, in the Prairies of Canada, Strathmore is a picturesque town that has emerged as a popular tourist destination in recent years. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, unique cultural experiences, or simply a relaxed vacation, Strathmore has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Strathmore

Below are some of the top tourist destinations in Strathmore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Strathmore Station Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the town’s rich history, including its agricultural roots and railway heritage.
  • Downtown Mural Tour: A self-guided tour that takes you through the town’s vibrant murals, showcasing the local art scene.
  • The Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village: A living museum that showcases the contributions of Ukrainian settlers to the region’s cultural diversity.

Natural Attractions of Strathmore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kinsmen Lake: A popular spot for swimming, fishing, and picnicking, surrounded by scenic views.
  • The Strathmore Golf Club: A beautiful golf course surrounded by the rolling hills of Strathmore.
  • The Wheatland Trail: A 25 km scenic drive that passes through picturesque farmland and offers spectacular views of the prairie landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strathmore

Strathmore offers several hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels. The most popular ones are:

  • The Strathmore to Calgary Trail: A relatively easy trail that spans 38 km and offers scenic views of the prairie landscape.
  • The Canal Path: An easy hike that follows the canal and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Monument Ridge Trail: A 4 km hike with stunning views of the Canadian Rockies, located just a short drive from Strathmore.

Best Things to Do in Strathmore: Activities and Tours

Unleash your inner adventurer with these fun activities in Strathmore:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the stunning views of Strathmore and its surrounding countryside from above.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the rolling hills of Strathmore on horseback.
  • ATV Tours: Take a guided ATV tour through the scenic backcountry of Strathmore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strathmore

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Strathmore, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1 Arrive in Strathmore & spend the day exploring downtown, including the murals and local shops
Day 2 Visit the Strathmore Station Museum & take a hike on the Monument Ridge Trail
Day 3 Spend the day horseback riding & exploring the town’s scenic countryside
Day 4 Take a hot air balloon ride & enjoy a relaxing day at Kinsmen Lake
Day 5 Go on an ATV tour through the backcountry & stop for a picnic at a scenic spot
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby Calgary, including a visit to the Calgary Zoo and the Glenbow Museum
Day 7 Depart from Strathmore

Food and Drink Scene of Strathmore

From local favorites to delicious international cuisine, Strathmore has a diverse food scene that is sure to satisfy all tastes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta Beef: A must-try delicacy that is synonymous with the region.
  • Perogies: An iconic Ukrainian dish that is popular in Strathmore.
  • Saskatoon Berry Pie: A delicious local dessert made with Saskatoon berries, a staple of the prairie region.

Local Beverages

  • Microbreweries: Strathmore has several microbreweries where you can sample locally brewed beers.
  • Alberta Vodka: Made from locally grown wheat, Alberta vodka is a must-try for spirit enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Strathmore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Strathmore offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hotels to luxurious guesthouses. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Travelodge Strathmore: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms and excellent service.
  • Strathmore Motor Inn: A comfortable and affordable option with a variety of room types to choose from.
  • Executive Suites Hotel & Conference Centre: A luxurious hotel with elegant suites, a restaurant, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Strathmore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Strathmore offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts and keepsakes. Some popular options include:

  • Handmade pottery from local artists.
  • Local honey and preserves made from locally grown produce.
  • Quilts and textiles made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Strathmore

Strathmore is a town that prides itself on its strong community spirit. Visitors are often struck by the warm hospitality and friendly nature of the locals. The town also has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong Ukrainian influence from the early settlers who helped shape the area’s history.

Best Time to Visit Strathmore

The best time to visit Strathmore is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the town’s outdoor attractions are at their best. However, if you’re looking for a quieter experience, consider visiting during the shoulder season (April-May, September-October), when the crowds are thinner, and the prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strathmore

Do:

  • Take a hike or bike ride on one of the town’s scenic trails.
  • Try some of the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride to get a bird’s-eye view of the town.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s cultural heritage.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Trespass on private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Strathmore

Strathmore is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Strathmore

Strathmore has a reliable healthcare system, with several medical facilities and hospitals in the area. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage before their trip.

Getting to Strathmore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Strathmore

  • Calgary International Airport: Located approximately 45 minutes’ drive from Strathmore.

Bus Stations Near Strathmore

  • Strathmore Greyhound: Located in the heart of downtown Strathmore.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in the area, making it easy to explore surrounding destinations.

How to Get Around in Strathmore

The most convenient way to get around Strathmore is by car. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or take a taxi to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Strathmore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding area, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Calgary: A vibrant city known for its cultural attractions, shopping, and dining.
  • Drumheller: A town famous for its dinosaur fossils and natural wonders.
  • Banff National Park: A stunning national park with some of Canada’s most breathtaking scenery.

Conclusion

Strathmore is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the charm of rural Canada. From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural scenery and outdoor opportunities, this picturesque town has something for everyone.
