Introduction to Southwest

Southwest is located in Calgary, in Calgary Region, Alberta, situated in the scenic Prairies region of Canada. Known for its vibrant culture, astounding natural beauty, and warm-hearted locals, Southwest is the perfect destination for any traveler seeking a unique experience.

List of Places to Visit in Southwest

Do you want to discover what to see and do during your visit to Southwest, Canada? These are some of the must-visit places in the region.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Glenbow Museum: Learn about the history, peoples, and art of Western Canada through the Glenbow Museum’s extensive collection of artifacts, archives, and artworks.

  • Heritage Park Historical Village: Explore and experience life in Western Canada’s early days in this living museum, featuring over 180 historical exhibits, recreated buildings, and costumed staff.

Natural Attractions of Southwest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Banff National Park: Take in the breathtaking alpine scenery, wildlife, and pristine lakes in one of Canada’s most beloved parks.

  • Fish Creek Provincial Park: A perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, this park boasts over 80 kilometers of hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and opportunities for wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southwest

If you are an adventurer looking for exciting trails to hike or bike to, Southwest has numerous routes for you to explore. Here is a list of some of the best:

  • Sulphur Mountain Trail, Banff National Park: Take a four-kilometer hike to the top of Sulphur Mountain, and enjoy panoramic views of Banff and the surrounding mountains.

  • Elbow Valley Trail, Fish Creek Provincial Park: This 20-kilometer trail follows the Elbow River through the park’s stunning landscapes, perfect for hikers and bikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Southwest: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with your family, or with a group of friends, Southwest offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit your interests. Some of the best experiences include:

  • Calgary Food Tour: This guided tour takes you through the best eats in Calgary, sampling delicious food from local restaurants, cafes, and bars.

  • Canmore Caving Adventure: Explore the depths of Rat’s Nest Cave in Canmore and marvel at underground waterfalls, natural rock formations, and limestone passages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southwest

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Southwest:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Calgary’s downtown area and visit the Calgary Tower, Heritage Park Historical Village and Glenbow Museum.
Day 2 Take a day trip to Banff National Park and hike the Sulphur Mountain Trail.
Day 3 Rent a bike and enjoy the Elbow Valley Trail through Fish Creek Provincial Park and end the day at Spruce Meadows.
Day 4 Take a Canmore Caving Adventure and visit local art galleries in Calgary.
Day 5 Experience Indigenous culture and art at Blackfoot Crossing Historical Park and unwind at the Eau Claire Distillery.
Day 6 Go on a wildlife safari in Kananaskis Country and enjoy a relaxing spa day at the Willow Stream Spa.
Day 7 Visit Calgary Zoo before you leave.

Food and Drink Scene of Southwest

Southwest offers a wide range of local foodie experiences, from farm-to-table restaurants to craft breweries and distilleries.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calgary’s Steak: Savour a juicy steak dinner at one of the city’s renowned steak houses, known for their locally sourced beef.

  • Alberta Beef and Bison: Try a classic Alberta dish with locally raised beef or bison meat, often served in burgers, stews, and roasts.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Sample some of the best beers from local breweries such as Wild Rose Brewery and Village Brewery.

  • Craft Spirits: Sip on locally distilled spirits like gin, vodka, and single-malt whiskey at distilleries such as Park Distillery and Eau Claire Distillery.

Where to Stay in Southwest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southwest has some of the best accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels that cater to every need. Here are some of the top options:

  • Fairmont Banff Springs: This legendary hotel is a Canadian icon, offering stunning views of the Rocky Mountains along with top-of-the-line amenities and services.

  • ** HI Calgary City Centre:** A budget-friendly option for travelers seeking comfort and convenience in the heart of Calgary’s downtown area.

What to Buy in Southwest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and locally-made products to bring back with you? Southwest has plenty of options to choose from, including:

  • Stetson hats: A classic Western accessory that’s both stylish and practical, you can find these iconic hats at local shops such as Lammle’s Western Wear.

  • Maple Syrup: Take home a taste of Canada with locally made maple syrup, found at specialty stores such as the Maple Leaf.

Local Culture and Customs of Southwest

Southwest has a rich and diverse culture influenced by its indigenous population, European settlers, and recent immigrants from around the world. Some important customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Respect for Indigenous culture: The region’s Blackfoot and Tsuu T’ina nations are critical to the region’s early history and must always be respected in words and actions.

  • Warm Hospitality: Southern Alberta is know for its friendly people and welcoming atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Southwest

The best time to visit Southwest is from June to September with warm weather, plenty of daylight hours, and numerous events and festivals to enjoy. The region’s winter months can be quite cold and barren.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southwest

Do:

  • Hike and explore: Take advantage of the Southwest’s natural beauty by hiking, biking, or exploring the local parks and trails.

  • Try local cuisine: Embrace the unique flavors of Southern Alberta and enjoy some of the regions local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore indigenous history: Remember the region’s rich indigenous history, culture, and traditions, and don’t partake in any practices that disrespect their heritage.

  • Litter or damage nature: Respect the natural environment and take care of it by not littering and not leaving any damage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southwest

Southwest is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is still important to take necessary precautions. Always be aware of your surroundings and exercise caution when traveling alone at night.

Health in Southwest

Soutwest experiences a dry climate, so it is important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen when outdoors. Additionally, it’s always a good idea to consult a healthcare professional before traveling to the area.

Getting to Southwest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southwest

The region’s major international airport is Calgary International Airport, which handles flights from major cities around the US and Canada.

Bus Stations Near Southwest

Calgary Greyhound Station is a major hub from which to catch a bus to other parts of Canada and the United States.

Other Options

Southwest can also be reached via car, and local car rental companies offer excellent options for travelers looking for flexibility and freedom.

How to Get Around in Southwest

The best way to get around Southwest is by car or public transportation, such as buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Southwest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southwest’s nearby destinations offer more opportunities to explore and experience Canadian culture and nature. Some options include:

  • Edmonton: Explore this vibrant city and its many attractions, such as West Edmonton Mall, the largest shopping mall in North America.

  • Waterton Lakes National Park: A must-visit for nature lovers, Waterton Lakes boasts stunning glacier-formed lakes, mountain vistas, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Southwest, Canada is an amazing travel destination that offers something for everyone. Packed with natural beauty, exciting activities, rich culture, and warm hospitality, Southwest is sure to create unforgettable memories for any traveler.
