Introduction to Southeast

Situated in the Calgary region of the Alberta province in Canada, Southeast offers a unique blend of magnificent natural beauty, historical charm, and thrilling recreational opportunities. Whether you are a solo traveler or with your family, Southeast has plenty to offer. From its breathtaking mountain ranges and serene crystal-clear lakes to its buzzing city life and cultural landmarks, Southeast is a destination that caters to various interests, making it a perfect starting point to explore other parts of Alberta.

List of Places to Visit in Southeast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southeast is a place that pays homage to its history and traditions. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit:

  • Heritage Park Historical Village - This living history museum showcases life in Western Canada from the 1860s to the 1950s. The park features a vintage amusement park, antique cars, steam trains, and more.
  • Gasoline Alley Museum - This museum displays a collection of vintage cars and trucks, gas pumps, and other automobile memorabilia from the 1930s to the 1970s.
  • Fort Calgary - This historic site was once a police barracks but is now a museum where visitors can learn about Calgary’s founding.

Natural Attractions of Southeast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southeast’s natural landscape is awe-inspiring, with a variety of outdoor recreational opportunities. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Fish Creek Provincial Park - This massive park features tons of hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and a vast variety of flora and fauna.
  • Elbow Falls - A beautiful waterfall located in the scenic Elbow River Valley, with a picnic area and plenty of hiking trails.
  • Sikome Lake - A man-made lake with sandy beaches, paddle boats, and swimming opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southeast

With an abundance of parks and natural attractions, Southeast is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes that you should add to your itinerary:

  • The Douglas Fir Trail - A moderate 2.5 km hike in Griffith Woods, a pristine urban forest.
  • The Rotary/Mattamy Greenway - This bike path is 138 km long, and a significant portion of it follows the Bow River and Fish Creek.

Best Things to Do in Southeast: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top adventures and tours in Southeast that will keep you busy:

  • Zip-lining in Weaselhead Natural Area - A thrilling and unforgettable adventure that lets you soar above the treetops on a zipline.
  • Calgary Zoo - A wonderful place to explore a variety of exotic animals and learn about their habitats.
  • Explore the Street Art Scene - Southeast is home to some fascinating street art murals that are worth checking out.

Food and Drink Scene of Southeast

Southeast has a food scene that caters to all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta Beef - This delicious beef is famous for its high-quality and juicy texture.
  • Butter Tart - A quintessential Canadian dessert made with butter, sugar, eggs, and raisins.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Southeast has a thriving craft beer scene, with breweries like Annex Ale Project and Banded Peak Brewery.
  • Ice Wine - This sweet wine is produced in the colder climates of Alberta and is a favorite among wine-lovers.

Where to Stay in Southeast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southeast has a mix of accommodations ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Arts - A luxurious hotel located in the heart of downtown Calgary.
  • Wicked Hostels - A budget-friendly option with clean, comfortable beds and communal spaces.
  • Carriage House Inn - A hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a cozy restaurant, and a beautiful courtyard.

What to Buy in Southeast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southeast has a variety of local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the top items to buy:

  • Maple Syrup - A sweet treat that is a staple of Canadian cuisine.
  • Wildflower Honey - Produced by local beekeepers, this honey is a great souvenir to take home.
  • Canadian-Made Souvenirs - From t-shirts to mugs to keychains, there are plenty of souvenirs that celebrate Canadian culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Southeast

The culture of Southeast is a blend of Western and Indigenous traditions. Here are some customs and traditions that you should be aware of when visiting:

  • Respect for Indigenous Culture - The region is home to the Treaty 7 nations, and visitors should be mindful of their culture and traditions.
  • Politeness and Friendliness - Canadians are known for being polite and friendly, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you.

Best Time to Visit Southeast

The best time to visit Southeast is in the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is pleasant, and the recreational opportunities are abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southeast

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Southeast:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife in Southeast.
  • Be prepared for the weather changes, even during the summer months.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural parks or wildlife habitats.
  • Disturb or harm the wildlife in Southeast.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southeast

Southeast is a safe region with low crime rates. But visitors should still take precautions to stay safe, including being aware of their surroundings and keeping their valuables secure.

Health in Southeast

It’s always recommended to obtain medical insurance while traveling to a foreign country. Visitors should also check with their doctor regarding any vaccinations that they may require for their trip.

How to Get Around in Southeast

Southeast is best explored by car, but visitors also have options for public transportation, including the Calgary Transit System, which operates a bus and train network throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Southeast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southeast is an excellent starting point for exploring other parts of Alberta, including:

  • Banff National Park - Located just an hour and a half from Calgary, Banff National Park is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Drumheller - This small town is home to the world-famous Royal Tyrrell Museum of Paleontology.

Conclusion

Southeast is a destination that offers a great mix of natural beauty, historical significance, and urban culture. With a wide variety of activities and attractions, Southeast is an excellent starting point for exploring the region and discovering the best things that Alberta has to offer. Whether you’re looking to go hiking, explore museums, or just relax by the lake, Southeast has something for everyone.
