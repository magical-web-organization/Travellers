Introduction to Northeast

Nestled in the Calgary Region of Alberta, Northeast is a diverse and dynamic hub of culture, nature, and adventure waiting to be explored. From the serene natural landscapes to the vibrant cityscape, Northeast is a traveler’s paradise. Whether you want to indulge in outdoor activities, educate yourself on the local culture, or enjoy some delicious local food, Northeast has it all. In this travel guide, we will provide you with the ultimate list of attractions and activities, as well as tips on where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Northeast

Here is a list of must-visit places in Northeast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Hangar Flight Museum: This museum features a variety of aircraft and exhibits about aviation history.
  2. Heritage Park Historical Village: A historic park showcasing western Canadian history from the 1860s to the 1950s.
  3. Fort Calgary: A national historic site that tells the story of the city’s original settlement and founding.

Natural Attractions of Northeast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Nose Hill Park: A vast natural park with beautiful walking and biking trails.
  2. Calgary Zoo: Canada’s second most visited zoo featuring more than 900 animals.
  3. Prince’s Island Park: A lush urban park on an island in the Bow River with plenty of picnic areas, gardens and paths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast

If you’re looking for some scenic and challenging hikes in Northeast, don’t miss these routes:

  1. Rotary/Mattamy Greenway: A 138 km pathway that circles around Calgary, perfect for cycling and hiking.
  2. Nose Hill Park: Several trails with amazing views of downtown Calgary and the Rocky Mountains.
  3. Fish Creek Provincial Park: Beautiful trails through forests, prairie, and riverside that provide opportunities for hiking, cross-country skiing, and bird-watching.

Best Things to Do in Northeast: Activities and Tours

  1. Calgary Tower: A one-of-a-kind observation tower that provides panoramic views of the city, and an award-winning restaurant.
  2. Calgary Stampede: A world-renowned rodeo, exhibition, and festival in July.
  3. CrossIron Mills: A large shopping mall in Rocky View County that features over 200 shops, restaurants, and movie theatres.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northeast

If you have a week to explore Northeast, check out this itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Calgary Zoo
2 Hike in Nose Hill Park
3 Explore Fort Calgary
4 Visit Heritage Park Historical Village and enjoy a live theatre show
5 Go shopping at CrossIron Mills
6 Attend a Calgary Stampede event
7 Visit Prince’s Island Park and kayak in the Bow River

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast

Experience Northeast’s culinary scene with these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Alberta Beef: A must-try for meat lovers. Alberta beef is known worldwide for its quality and delicious taste.
  2. Ginger Beef: A sweet and spicy take on Asian-style beef stir-fry that originated from Calgary.
  3. Perogies: A traditional Eastern European dish filled with savory potatoes and cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Village Brewery: A local craft brewery with an emphasis on high-quality ingredients and innovative brewing techniques.
  2. Eau Claire Distillery: A locally-owned distillery that produces a variety of spirits such as gin, vodka, and whisky.
  3. Caffe Beano: A cozy neighbourhood coffee shop that serves the best coffee in the northeast.

Where to Stay in Northeast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Northeast:

  1. Delta Hotels by Marriott Calgary Airport In-Terminal: A luxurious airport hotel with sophisticated design and amenities.
  2. Wyndham Garden Calgary Airport: A budget-friendly hotel with an indoor pool and fitness centre.
  3. Hi-Calgary City Centre Hostel: A centrally located hostel that offers affordable and clean accommodations for budget travellers.
  4. Sandman Hotel & Suites Calgary Airport: A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a spa, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Northeast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Northeast:

  1. Calgary Stampede souvenirs such as cowboy hats, rodeo shirts, and leather boots.
  2. Canmore handmade soaps, lotions and locally made honey.
  3. Pies and baked goods from the Little Known Bakery

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast

Northeast is home to a diverse community that has its own unique customs and traditions. Some of the most significant cultural celebrations in Northeast include:

  1. Calgary Stampede: A massive rodeo, exhibition, and festival, held annually in July.
  2. GlobalFest: A multi-cultural festival showcasing fireworks and cultural performances, held annually in August.
  3. Diwali: The Hindu festival of lights, held annually in November.

Best Time to Visit Northeast

The best time to visit Northeast is from June to September, but if you love winter sports, winter is also a great time to visit for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeast

Do:

  1. Dress in layers as temperatures can vary throughout the day.
  2. Visit Nose Hill Park for a scenic walking trail.
  3. Try some locally brewed beer.

Don’t:

  1. Litter the parks or the streets.
  2. Leave your valuables unattended in the car.
  3. Skip the Canadian Rockies if you visit Northeast.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northeast

Northeast is considered a safe area to visit, but it is always important to practice common sense and safety measures.

Health in Northeast

Northeast has adequate medical facilities, including several hospitals, clinics, and emergency services.

Getting to Northeast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northeast

The Calgary International Airport (YYC) is the closest airport that serves Northeast.

Bus Stations Near Northeast

Greyhound has a bus station in Northeast, located near the airport.

Other Options

It’s easy to get around Northeast as it has an extensive network of public transport, including buses and trains.

How to Get Around in Northeast

The Calgary Transit operator provides efficient transportation services throughout Northeast. It is easy to use and affordable.

Where to Go Next from Northeast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular excursions and destinations located near Northeast include:

  1. Banff National Park: With majestic mountains, turquoise lakes, and abundant wildlife, Banff is a nature lover’s paradise.
  2. Drumheller: Known as the “Dinosaur Capital of the World,” Drumheller is a beautiful town that is home to the Royal Tyrrell Museum and the Badlands.
  3. Canmore: A picturesque town nestled in the Bow Valley, famous for its stunning scenery, outdoor activities and quaint shops.

Conclusion

Northeast Calgary is a treasure trove of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and exciting activities. From pristine parks to museums, eclectic dining, and shopping options, Northeast has it all. Our comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Northeast with ease, and be sure to check out all the must-see attractions and things to do.
