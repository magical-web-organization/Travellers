Introduction to St. Albert

St. Albert is a picturesque city located in the Edmonton Capital Region, in Alberta, Canada. With a population of over 65,000, the city is known for its beautiful parks, recreational facilities, cultural attractions, and vibrant food scene. St. Albert is a perfect destination for those looking for a relaxing getaway or outdoor adventure. In this guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive list of things to do and see in St. Albert, as well as tips on local culture, food, and transportation.

List of Places to Visit in St. Albert

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Albert Grain Elevator Park: A popular attraction for history enthusiasts, this park features a fully restored grain elevator and train station.
  • Musée Héritage Museum: Located in downtown St. Albert, this museum showcases the region’s fascinating history, art, and culture.
  • Father Lacombe Chapel: This historic chapel dates back to 1861 and is one of the oldest buildings in Alberta.
  • St. Albert Botanic Park: This beautiful park features various gardens, including a rose garden, Japanese garden, and herb garden.

Natural Attractions of St. Albert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lois Hole Centennial Provincial Park: This park covers over 140 hectares of land and offers various hiking trails and scenic viewpoints.
  • Red Willow Trail System: The trail is perfect for hiking, cycling, jogging, or picnicking. It is a 17 kilometers trail that runs through the city and offers beautiful views of the Sturgeon River.
  • Big Lake: This lake is a bird watcher’s paradise, with over 240 different species of birds that can be spotted in the area.
  • Lacombe Lake Park: A beautiful park that offers a variety of outdoor activities, including a beach, picnic areas, hiking and biking trails, a playground and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Albert

St. Albert has an extensive network of trails suitable for hiking, biking, and winter sports. The Red Willow Trail system is one of the most popular in the city. Other popular trails include the St. Albert Botanic Park Trail, Lacombe Lake Park Trail, and Big Lake Trail.

Best Things to Do in St. Albert: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Arden Theatre: A performing arts venue located in the heart of downtown St. Albert, showcasing various concerts, theatre productions and family-friendly events throughout the year.
  • A trip to the city’s farmers’ market: The market offers a wide selection of locally grown produce, baked goods, handmade crafts and more.
  • Go shopping: Check out St. Albert Centre or Perron District for unique shopping opportunities.
  • Take a cooking class: Enjoy a cooking class at Taste of the Mediterranean, where you’ll learn to cook delicious and flavorful dishes from the Mediterranean cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Albert

  • Day 1: Explore the Musée Héritage Museum and Father Lacombe Chapel, followed by a picnic at Lacombe Lake Park.
  • Day 2: Hike the Red Willow Trail, and then enjoy a show at the Arden Theatre.
  • Day 3: Visit the St. Albert Botanic Park and then head to the farmers’ market.
  • Day 4: Spend a day at Big Lake, go bird watching and enjoy a picnic.
  • Day 5: Visit the St. Albert Grain Elevator Park and Perron District for some shopping.
  • Day 6: Take a cooking class at Taste of the Mediterranean or enjoy a wine and dine experience at one of the city’s fine dining restaurants.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Lois Hole Centennial Provincial Park.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Albert

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This classic Canadian dish consists of French fries, gravy, and cheese curds and is served at many restaurants in St. Albert.
  • Steak Sandwich: A hearty and delicious sandwich made with beef and served on a fresh bun.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Visit Apex Predator or Hog’s Head Brewing Company for a taste of St. Albert’s best craft beers.
  • Wine: Head to Glasshouse Bistro and Café for a glass of wine in a warm, cozy atmosphere.

Where to Stay in St. Albert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Albert has various accommodation options available, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the most popular hotels include the Hampton Inn & Suites by Hilton, St. Albert Inn & Suites, and Sleep Inn Motel.

What to Buy in St. Albert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • A bottle of craft beer from a local brewery.
  • A piece of art from the St. Albert Art Gallery.
  • Handmade crafts and souvenirs from the farmers’ market.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Albert

The people of St. Albert take pride in their city and appreciate visitors who take the time to learn about their history and culture. Make sure to show respect for local customs and traditions, such as wearing modest clothing in religious sites.

Best Time to Visit St. Albert

The best time to visit St. Albert is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, winter is also an excellent time to visit for snow sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Albert

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take time to explore the city’s natural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Disrespect local wildlife or damage nature areas.
  • Do not litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Albert

St. Albert is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard precautions against pickpocketing and minor crime, especially in crowded areas. Emergency services can be reached by calling 911 in case of an emergency.

Health in St. Albert

St. Albert has excellent medical facilities, including Sturgeon Community Hospital, which provides a full range of medical services. Visitors are encouraged to purchase travel health insurance to avoid any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to St. Albert: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Albert is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. The nearest airport is Edmonton International Airport, which is approximately 50 kilometers away from the city. Visitors can also take a Greyhound bus or VIA Rail train to Edmonton and then transfer to a local bus service to St. Albert.

Airports Near St. Albert

  • Edmonton International Airport
  • Edmonton Municipal Airport

Bus Stations Near St. Albert

  • Edmonton Greyhound Station
  • St. Albert Transit Station

Other Options

  • Rent a car
  • Take a taxi
  • Hire a private transportation service

How to Get Around in St. Albert

St. Albert is a relatively small city, and most attractions can be reached by walking or cycling. The city also has an excellent public transportation system, which includes buses and light rail transit (LRT). Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from St. Albert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Edmonton: The capital city of Alberta is known for its excellent shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Jasper National Park: A stunning park known for its crystal-clear lakes and breathtaking views.
  • Banff National Park: Another breathtaking national park known for its hiking trails and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

St. Albert is an incredible destination, with various attractions, natural beauty, and cultural offerings. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to this beautiful city in Alberta, Canada. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s history, enjoying outdoor adventures, or indulging in delicious food, St. Albert has something for everyone. So, pack your bags and get ready to experience a memorable trip to one of the most beautiful destinations in Canada.
