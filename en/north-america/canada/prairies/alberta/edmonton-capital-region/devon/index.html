Introduction to Devon

Devon is a charming village located in Edmonton Capital Region of Alberta, Canada. Surrounded by scenic landscapes, Devon is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the outdoors, explore the local history and culture, and indulge in authentic Canadian cuisine. This ultimate travel guide will help you plan your visit to Devon by providing an overview of the top attractions, activities, hotels, food scene, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Devon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Devon has a rich history that is reflected in its museums and historical places that provide a glimpse into its past. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Devonian Botanic Garden: A scenic garden that showcases a wide variety of plants and trees from Alberta.
  • Oil Workers Monument: A sculpture that pays tribute to the oil workers who have contributed to Alberta’s oil industry.
  • Devon Pioneer Cemetery: A historic cemetery that dates back to the late 1800s and holds the remains of some of Devon’s earliest settlers.
  • Devon Museum and Archives: A museum that houses a collection of artifacts that depict the social and cultural history of Devon.

Natural Attractions of Devon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Devon offers an abundance of outdoor adventures and breathtaking views that attract travelers from around the world. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Devon:

  • Devonian Trail: A scenic trail that takes you through lush forests, rolling hills, and along the banks of the North Saskatchewan River.
  • Lions Park: A serene park with picnic areas, playgrounds, and walking trails that provide stunning views of the river.
  • Devon Boat Launch: A popular spot for fishing, boating, and kayaking, with access to the North Saskatchewan River.
  • Devon Golf and Conference Centre: An 18-hole golf course surrounded by lush greenery and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Devon

Devon has a variety of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for travelers who love the outdoors. Some popular routes include:

  • River Valley Adventure Co. Trail: A scenic trail that runs along the North Saskatchewan River and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lions Park Trail: A 5 km loop that offers a chance to witness the natural beauty of Devon and its surroundings.
  • Bird Sanctuary Trail: A 4 km trail that winds through a natural habitat for birds and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Devon: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions and hiking/biking trails, Devon offers a plethora of activities and tours that are perfect for travelers of all ages. Here’s a list of must-do activities and tours in Devon:

  • Devonian Botanic Garden Guided Tour: A guided tour of the stunning Devonian Botanic Garden, led by a local expert.
  • Alberta Railway Museum Tour: A guided tour of the historic railway museum that boasts a collection of vintage locomotives, rail cars, and equipment.
  • Downtown Devon Art Crawl: A self-guided tour of downtown Devon’s best art galleries and studios.

Food and Drink Scene of Devon

Devon’s food and drink scene is a delightful mix of international cuisine and local flavors. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian staple dish, made from crispy French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Maple Syrup Dishes: From pancakes to waffles and even ice cream, Devon offers a delicious selection of dishes infused with maple syrup, Canada’s signature flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Alberta Craft Beer: Alberta’s beer is world-famous, and Devon is no different. Some local favorites include Blindman Brewing and Troubled Monk Brewing.
  • Koch Beverages: A local beverage that is available in many flavors and is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Devon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Devon offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top options:

  • Best Western Plus Edmonton Airport Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near the Edmonton International Airport with modern amenities and excellent services.
  • Devonian Hotel and Suites: A budget-friendly hotel situated in the heart of Devon, providing easy access to local attractions.
  • Black Gold Guesthouse and Suite: A cozy guesthouse offering excellent services and amenities and is perfect for families and couples.

What to Buy in Devon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Devon is famous for its maple syrup, craft beer, and other local products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some top local products to bring home:

  • Maple Syrup Products: From maple syrup to maple candies and pastries, Devon offers a wide range of maple syrup products.
  • Alberta Craft Beer: Make sure to bring back a few bottles of the famous Alberta craft beer.
  • Koch Beverages: Koch Beverages make for a refreshing, healthy, local drink that you can take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Devon

Devon has a diverse cultural heritage, and it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the Outdoors: Devonians love the outdoors, and it’s crucial to respect the natural beauty and preserve it for future generations.
  • Manners Matter: Canadian culture strongly emphasizes the importance of good manners, such as saying please and thank you.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Canada, and it’s generally expected to tip 15-20% of the total bill for good service.

Best Time to Visit Devon

The best time to visit Devon is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Spring and fall are also a great time to visit when the crowds are thinner, and the weather is still mild. However, if you’re interested in winter sports, then winter is the ideal time to visit Devon.

Do’s and Don’ts of Devon

Do:

  • Try the Local Food: Devon has a diverse culinary scene, and it’s essential to sample the local flavors.
  • Respect Wildlife: Devon is home to many species of wildlife, and it’s crucial to respect their natural habitat.
  • Explore the Outdoors: Devon’s natural beauty is breathtaking, and it’s essential to spend time outdoors and explore.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Littering is illegal in Canada, and it’s essential to avoid littering and dispose of waste properly.
  • Use Your Phone While Driving: Using your phone while driving is illegal in Canada, and it’s crucial to avoid it.
  • Over-tip: Tipping is customary in Canada, but over-tipping can come across as disrespectful. Tipping 15-20% of the total bill is generally considered appropriate.

Safety and Health

Safety in Devon

Devon is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, tourists are advised to exercise caution and take necessary safety precautions, such as:

  • Keep an Eye on Your Belongings: Petty theft can occur in busy tourist areas, and it’s essential to keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Follow Traffic Rules: Follow traffic rules while driving or walking and always wear a helmet if biking.
  • Be Aware of Wildlife: Devon is home to many species of wildlife, and it’s essential to keep a safe distance and never feed wild animals.

Health in Devon

Devon has a modern healthcare system with excellent medical facilities. However, it’s essential to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Devon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Devon

  • Edmonton International Airport: Located 10 km south of Devon, it’s the closest international airport, and many airlines offer flights to various destinations.

Bus Stations Near Devon

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Runs from Edmonton to Devon, and other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental is available at Edmonton International Airport and in the city of Devon.

How to Get Around in Devon

Devon is a compact village, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot or bike. Buses and taxis are also available, and car rental is a great option if you plan to explore nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Devon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Devon is close to many other exciting destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Edmonton: The provincial capital of Alberta is just 30 minutes away from Devon and offers a variety of attractions, events, and entertainment options.
  • Ukrainian Cultural Village: A living museum that showcases the history and culture of Ukrainian Canadians and is just a 30-minute drive from Devon.
  • Elk Island National Park: A nature reserve located just 30 minutes from Devon, offering a chance to see bison, wolf, and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

Conclusion

Devon offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures that make it a perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Canadian experience. Use this ultimate travel guide to plan your trip to Devon and make the most of your visit.
