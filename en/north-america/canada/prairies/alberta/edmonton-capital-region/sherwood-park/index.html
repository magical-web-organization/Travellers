Introduction to Sherwood Park

Located in the Edmonton Capital Region of Alberta, Canada, Sherwood Park is a vibrant community that attracts visitors from all parts of the world. Despite its small size, Sherwood Park boasts a rich cultural heritage and diverse natural landscape, making it a popular destination for tourists seeking outdoor adventures and scenic beauty.

Flanked by the prairies to the east and the Rockies to the west, Sherwood Park offers a unique blend of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply relax in the great outdoors, Sherwood Park is the perfect place to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Sherwood Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sherwood Park is a treasure trove of cultural attractions, with a variety of museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into its past. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to visit in Sherwood Park:

  • Strathcona County Museum: Located in the heart of Sherwood Park, the Strathcona County Museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that celebrate the region’s history and heritage.
  • Festival Place: Festival Place is a premier performing arts center that hosts world-class concerts, plays, and other cultural events throughout the year.
  • Elk Island National Park: Just a short drive from Sherwood Park, Elk Island National Park is a sprawling wilderness area that is home to a variety of wildlife, including bison, elk, and moose.

Natural Attractions of Sherwood Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking and biking to fishing and birdwatching, Sherwood Park offers a wealth of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Sherwood Park Natural Area: The Sherwood Park Natural Area is a 14-acre park that features a variety of hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic vistas.
  • Broadmoor Lake Park: Broadmoor Lake Park is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and birdwatching, and also boasts picnic areas and a playground for children.
  • Miquelon Lake Provincial Park: Just a short drive from Sherwood Park, Miquelon Lake Provincial Park is a pristine wilderness area that offers hiking, birdwatching, and camping opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sherwood Park

Sherwood Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • Centennial Park Trails: The Centennial Park trails are a network of interconnected walkways and hiking trails that wind their way through the heart of Sherwood Park. Perfect for a scenic stroll or a leisurely bike ride.
  • Heritage Trail: The Heritage Trail is a scenic pathway that connects the Strathcona County Museum to Broadmoor Lake Park, offering beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Heartland Trails: The Heartland Trails are a network of multi-use pathways that crisscross the countryside around Sherwood Park, offering a range of hiking and biking opportunities for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Sherwood Park: Activities and Tours

Aside from its natural beauty and cultural attractions, Sherwood Park also offers a range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do in Sherwood Park:

  • Strathcona Wilderness Centre: The Strathcona Wilderness Centre offers a range of outdoor programs, including guided nature hikes, snowshoeing, and cross-country skiing.
  • Ride the Rails: Ride the Rails is a scenic train tour that departs from the Strathcona County Museum and takes visitors on a guided tour of the surrounding countryside.
  • Sherwood Park Farmers’ Market: The Sherwood Park Farmers’ Market is a must-visit destination for foodies, featuring fresh local produce, artisanal cheeses, and a range of baked goods and other treats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sherwood Park

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Sherwood Park, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Explore the Strathcona County Museum and Festival Place, then head to Broadmoor Lake Park for some fishing and kayaking.
  • Day 2: Hike the Centennial Park Trails in the morning, then spend the afternoon touring Elk Island National Park.
  • Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Strathcona Wilderness Centre, then head to Miquelon Lake Provincial Park for a day of hiking and birdwatching.
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and explore the Heartland Trails, then stop by the Sherwood Park Farmers’ Market for some fresh local food.
  • Day 5: Relax at the Summer Fun Park Water Park or play a round at Raven Crest Golf Course.
  • Day 6: Take the Ride the Rails tour, then head to the community centre to enjoy some indoor activities, such as swimming, skating, and rock climbing.
  • Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the WOW! Water Outfitters store to shop for souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Sherwood Park

Must-Try Dishes

Sherwood Park is home to a thriving food and drink scene, featuring a variety of local specialties and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Sherwood Park:

  • Poutine: A Quebec delicacy that has spread across Canada, poutine consists of French fries smothered in gravy and cheese curds. The best poutine in Sherwood Park can be found at the Jack’s Drive-In restaurant.
  • Smoked Meat Sandwich: A Montreal-style sandwich featuring smoked beef, mustard, and pickles. The best smoked meat sandwich in Sherwood Park can be found at Joe’s European Deli.
  • Alberta Beef: Alberta is famous for its high-quality beef, and there are plenty of local restaurants that serve up delicious steaks and burgers.

Local Beverages

No trip to Sherwood Park is complete without trying some of the local brews and libations. Here are some of the top local beverages to try:

  • Alley Kat Brewery: Alley Kat Brewery is a local craft brewery that produces a variety of beers, including its popular Full Moon Pale Ale and Aprikat Apricot Wheat Ale.
  • Lamb Brewery: Lamb Brewery is another local craft brewery that is known for its delicious beers, including its signature Stiff Upper Lip IPA and Lamb’s Weiss Wheat Ale.
  • Lucky 97 Liquor Store: If you’re looking for a unique selection of local and international wines and spirits, head to the Lucky 97 Liquor Store.

Where to Stay in Sherwood Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sherwood Park offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels and resorts. Here are some of the top places to stay in Sherwood Park:

  • Four Points by Sheraton: Located in the heart of Sherwood Park, the Four Points by Sheraton is a stylish and modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.
  • Sherwood Park Lodge & Conference Center: The Sherwood Park Lodge & Conference Center is a cozy and welcoming hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Foxy’s Retreat: Foxy’s Retreat is a delightful bed and breakfast that offers charming rooms and personalized service in a tranquil countryside setting.

What to Buy in Sherwood Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While in Sherwood Park, don’t forget to pick up some souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the top things to buy in Sherwood Park:

  • Maple Syrup: Canada is famous for its maple syrup, and there are plenty of local shops that sell this sweet treat.
  • Hockey Gear: Hockey is Canada’s national sport, and Sherwood Park is home to many local teams and players. Pick up some hockey gear to show off your Canadian pride.
  • Local Art: Sherwood Park is home to many talented artists and artisans. Check out the local art galleries and shops to find unique and handmade souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sherwood Park

Sherwood Park is a friendly and welcoming community that values its cultural heritage and traditions. While visiting Sherwood Park, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Saying “please” and “thank you”: Canadians are known for their politeness and friendliness. Always say “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Canada, with a standard tip of 15-20% of the total bill at restaurants and cafes.
  • Respecting the natural environment: Sherwood Park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. It’s important to respect the natural environment and follow all posted signs and rules when hiking, biking, or exploring the outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Sherwood Park

Sherwood Park has a continental climate, with hot summers and cold winters. The best time to visit Sherwood Park is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sherwood Park

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Sherwood Park is home to some of the most beautiful natural areas in Canada. Don’t miss the opportunity to explore the local parks and trails.
  • Try the local cuisine: Sherwood Park is known for its delicious food and drink scene. Be sure to try some of the local specialties.
  • Respect the locals: Canadians are known for being polite and friendly. Always be respectful and courteous when interacting with locals.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car: While Sherwood Park is generally a safe community, it’s always important to take precautions. Make sure to lock your car and avoid leaving valuables visible.
  • Ignore posted signs and rules: Sherwood Park’s natural areas are subject to specific rules and regulations. Always follow posted signs and rules to protect the environment and ensure everyone’s safety.
  • Litter: Sherwood Park takes pride in its natural beauty and cleanliness. Always dispose of your trash properly, and avoid littering.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sherwood Park

Overall, Sherwood Park is a safe community. However, visitors should always take basic safety precautions, including:

  • Locking your car: Car theft can be a problem in any community. Always lock your car and avoid leaving valuables visible.
  • Staying aware of your surroundings: Always be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring the outskirts of town.
  • Avoiding dangerous wildlife: Elk Island National Park is home to a variety of large mammals, including bears, moose, and bison. Always stay at a safe distance and follow all posted signs and rules.

Health in Sherwood Park

Canada has a world-class healthcare system, and visitors to Sherwood Park can feel confident in the quality of care they’ll receive if needed. However, it’s always a good idea to purchase travel health insurance before your trip, just in case.

Getting to Sherwood Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sherwood Park

The closest airport to Sherwood Park is Edmonton International Airport, which is located about 30 minutes south of Sherwood Park. From the airport, you can take a rental car, taxi, or shuttle bus to Sherwood Park.

Bus Stations Near Sherwood Park

Greyhound Canada operates a bus station in nearby Edmonton, which is located about 20 minutes north of Sherwood Park. From the bus station, you can take a taxi or rental car to Sherwood Park.

Other Options

If you’re planning on exploring the surrounding countryside, renting a car is the most convenient option. There are several car rental agencies located in Sherwood Park and at Edmonton International Airport.

How to Get Around in Sherwood Park

Sherwood Park is a relatively small community, and most of the attractions and amenities are located within a few miles of each other. If you’re exploring the downtown area, walking or biking is a great option. There is also a local bus service that operates throughout Sherwood Park.

Where to Go Next from Sherwood Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Alberta’s Capital Region, there are plenty of nearby destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some of the top places to go from Sherwood Park:

  • Edmonton: Alberta’s capital city is located just 20 minutes north of Sherwood Park. Explore the city’s museums, cultural attractions, and shopping destinations.
  • Jasper National Park: Located about three hours west of Sherwood Park, Jasper National Park is a wilderness paradise that offers hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities.
  • Banff National Park: Located about four hours south of Sherwood Park, Banff National Park is a world-famous wilderness area that offers stunning alpine vistas, hot springs, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Sherwood Park is more than just a small town in Alberta’s Capital Region - it’s a destination that offers something for everyone. From its natural beauty to its rich cultural history and vibrant food and drink scene, Sherwood Park is the perfect place to explore and discover all that Canada has to offer.
