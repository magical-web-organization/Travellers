Introduction to Gibbons

Located in the heart of the Edmonton Capital Region of Alberta, Canada lies the small town of Gibbons. With a population of just over 3,000 people, Gibbons may seem small, but it boasts some of the most beautiful landscapes in the Prairies. Along with its stunning natural scenery, Gibbons is also home to a rich cultural heritage, preserved through historical sites, museums, and events.

List of Places to Visit in Gibbons

Visitors to Gibbons will find plenty of attractions to explore, from pristine parks to cultural landmarks. Here are some must-visit places in Gibbons:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gibbons Museum - Dedicated to preserving the town’s cultural heritage, the Gibbons Museum is a fascinating place to visit. Fully equipped with informative displays and exhibits, it is a great place to get a glimpse of the town’s history and culture.
  • St. Columba Church - Built in 1927, the St. Columba Church is a designated historical site that offers a peek into the town’s religious heritage and architectural history.
  • Gibbons Community Cultural Centre - The Gibbons Community Cultural Centre hosts a range of cultural activities and events throughout the year, from theater performances to art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Gibbons: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Goose Hummock Golf Resort - Golf enthusiasts will relish the opportunity to swing their clubs at Goose Hummock Golf Resort, a picturesque and challenging 18-hole course in Gibbons.
  • Jurassic Forest - Take a step back in time and explore the Jurassic Forest, a prehistoric park with life-size dinosaurs, winding trails, and lush greens all around.
  • Sturgeon River - Gibbons is situated in the heart of the Sturgeon River valley, which is renowned for its breathtaking scenery, flora, and fauna. Hiking, fishing, and kayaking are popular activities in this area of outstanding natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gibbons

Gibbons has a wealth of hiking and biking trails, which are perfect for those who are looking to explore the town’s natural scenery. Here are some of the most popular hiking/biking routes in Gibbons:

  • Sturgeon River Trail - This trail follows the Sturgeon River and offers stunning views of the surrounding valley. The trail is suitable for hikers of all skill levels.
  • Goose Hummock Trail - This trail weaves through wooded areas and provides hikers and bikers with a challenging and scenic experience.
  • Jurassic Forest Trail - This 1.2 km trail takes visitors through the Jurassic Forest, past life-size dinosaur replicas, and other prehistoric exhibits.

Best Things to Do in Gibbons: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Tours - Take a guided tour of Gibbons and enjoy a personalized experience that highlights the town’s rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes.
  • Agriculture Tours - Visitors can take a tour of Gibbons’s farms and learn about the area’s agricultural heritage while enjoying delicious local produce.
  • Fishing - Sturgeon River is a popular fishing spot, particularly for its salmon run. Visitors can cast their lines and enjoy a peaceful day of fishing with the picturesque setting as a backdrop.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gibbons

Day 1: Arrive in Gibbons and explore the town’s museums and historical landmarks.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the town or the local farms, followed by a round of golf.

Day 3: Spend the day outdoors on the Sturgeon River Trail or the Goose Hummock Trail.

Day 4: Enjoy a day of fishing on the Sturgeon River or the nearby Joe Lake.

Day 5: Embark on a self-guided tour to nearby attractions like Elk Island National Park, Fort Saskatchewan, and Jurassic Forest.

Day 6: Explore the local food and drink scene with a visit to Gibbons’s best restaurants and pubs.

Day 7: Departure day - reflect on your Gibbons experience and say goodbye to this quaint and charming town.

Food and Drink Scene of Gibbons

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pie - With a long history of farming, Gibbons is known for its fresh and delicious pies. Try the peach and blackberry pies at Mrs. Banks Bakery.
  • Alberta Beef - Alberta beef is some of the best in the world, and you can get a taste of it at the popular local restaurant, The Dhaba’s.

Local Beverages

  • Local Brews - Check out Troubled Monk Brewery in nearby Red Deer for some of the best local beers on tap.
  • Cocktails - Sip on handcrafted cocktails at the Gibbons Hotel, a local landmark that has been serving drinks for over 100 years.

Where to Stay in Gibbons: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Gibbons and the surrounding areas can choose from a range of budget-friendly to luxury lodgings. Here are some top choices:

  • Sunrise Motel - This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms to visitors with a tight budget.
  • Paragon Motel - Another budget-friendly option, the Paragon Motel is situated right in the heart of Gibbons.
  • The Westin Edmonton - For those seeking luxury and amenities, The Westin Edmonton offers easy access to not only Gibbons but many other nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Gibbons: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take a little bit of Gibbons home with them by picking up some unique souvenirs or local produce. Some must-buy items in Gibbons are:

  • Homemade pies - Pick up a pie or two (or more) from Mrs. Banks Bakery.
  • Local honey - Gibbons has a thriving beekeeping community, and visitors can take home some delicious honey from local producers.

Local Culture and Customs of Gibbons

Gibbons has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the year with various festivals, events, and markets. Be sure to check out the Gibbons Farmers’ Market and the various concerts and performances at the Gibbons Community Cultural Centre. Respect for the natural landscapes, local traditions, and open-mindedness are also encouraged.

Best Time to Visit Gibbons

The best time to visit Gibbons is between May and October when the weather is warm and dry. Visitors can enjoy all the outdoor activities that Gibbons has to offer. Winter is also a great time to visit, particularly for those who enjoy winter sports and activities like snowmobiling, skiing or snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gibbons

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Take advantage of Gibbons’s great outdoor activities.
  • Observe wildlife from a safe distance.
  • Visit local attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gibbons

Gibbons is a generally safe place to visit, but tourists should always take extra care with their belongings and valuables. Visitors should also be careful when engaging in outdoor activities, particularly when hiking alone, or in areas with wildlife.

Health in Gibbons

Visitors to Gibbons and the surrounding areas should always carry appropriate medical coverage and take precautions when it comes to their health. Ensure that you have adequate travel insurance and know the location of nearby hospitals and medical facilities in case of an emergency.

Getting to Gibbons: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gibbons

  • Edmonton International Airport - The closest airport to Gibbons is the Edmonton International Airport (YEG), located about 65 km away.

Bus Stations Near Gibbons

  • Red Arrow Bus Lines - Visitors can take a bus from Red Arrow Bus Lines, which operates daily buses from Edmonton to Gibbons.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Visitors can also rent a car at the airport and drive to Gibbons.

How to Get Around in Gibbons

Gibbons is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. For visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas or get around town more quickly, renting a car may be the best option.

Where to Go Next from Gibbons: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Elk Island National Park - A short drive from Gibbons lies the Elk Island National Park, a beautiful nature reserve that is home to free-roaming bison, elk, and moose.
  • Fort Saskatchewan - About 15 km from Gibbons, Fort Saskatchewan is a charming little town that is packed with history and culture.
  • Jurassic Forest - A quick drive from Gibbons, the Jurassic Forest is a prehistoric park with life-size dinosaurs, winding trails, and lush greens all around.

Conclusion

Gibbons, located in the heart of the Edmonton Capital Region, is a charming little town with a rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes. Visitors can explore the many museums and cultural landmarks, enjoy the great outdoors, and indulge in the local food scene. From hiking and biking to guided tours and local festivals, Gibbons has something for everyone. Don’t miss out on this hidden gem in the Prairies.
