Introduction to Beaver Hills

Nestled in the Edmonton Capital Region, in Alberta province of Canada, Beaver Hills is a serene enclave that offers an abundance of nature, history, cultural attractions and outdoor adventures. This picturesque area located in the Prairies of North America is dotted with wetlands, lakes, valleys, and forests, making it a perfect weekend getaway for nature enthusiasts, fitness freaks, and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Beaver Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Strathcona County Museum and Archives: Get a glimpse of the region’s history through interactive exhibits and guided tours at this museum.
  • Fort Saskatchewan Heritage Precinct: Discover the city’s heritage and its role in the fur trade through storytelling and displays in this unique open-air museum.
  • Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village: Step back in time and explore the early Ukrainian settlement that shaped the region’s identity through interactive exhibits and costumed characters.

Natural Attractions of Beaver Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elk Island National Park: Home to bison, elk, moose, and over 250 bird species, this pristine park offers a myriad of activities such as hiking, biking, camping, and paddling.
  • Miquelon Provincial Park: Go fishing, picnicking, or kayaking in this tranquil park that boasts a beautiful lake and wetlands.
  • Astotin Lake Recreation Area: Relax on the sandy beach, take a dip in the clear waters, or enjoy boating in this serene lake surrounded by lush forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaver Hills

  • Beaver Hills Trails: Explore 19 kilometers of trails, through scenic landscapes filled with forests, wetlands, and diverse wildlife at Elk Island National Park.
  • Miquelon Lake Provincial Park Trail: This easy 3-kilometre loop trail takes you through the heart of the park’s wetlands and Aspen forest.
  • Gold Bar Park Trail: Located in Edmonton, this park offers a winding trail along the North Saskatchewan River and through serene forests.

Best Things to Do in Beaver Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Bison Conservation Tour: Learn about bison conservation efforts and observe the majestic creatures in their natural habitat at Elk Island National Park.
  • Star-gazing: Embrace the natural beauty of Beaver Hills and experience the incomparable views of the night sky during a guided star-gazing tour.
  • Horseback Riding: Take in the picturesque scenery on horseback on one of the serene trails offered in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Beaver Hills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: French fries covered in gravy and cheese curd, a staple dish on many Canadian menus.
  • Alberta beef: Alberta beef is known for its superior quality and taste, making it a must-try when in the area.
  • Wild Berry Pie: Enjoy locally sourced wild berries in a freshly baked pie that you will not soon forget.

Local Beverages

  • Alberta Craft Beer: Alberta is fast establishing itself as a hub for craft beer and has a range of breweries producing unique and flavorful beers.
  • Wild Tea Kombucha: This fermented drink uses ingredients such as wild berries and herbs, creating a distinct and refreshing taste from the prairies.
  • Alberta Spirits: The province of Alberta offers a variety of locally made spirits, including gin, vodka, and whisky.

Where to Stay in Beaver Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Elk Island Retreat: Tucked away on the outskirts of Elk Island National Park, this cozy retreat offers private cabins, perfect for a comfortable and private stay.
  • Ford Motel: This motel in Fort Saskatchewan is perfect for a budget stay while still providing the amenities and comfort you expect.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites by Hilton: A modern hotel situated close to Elk Island National Park and with easy access to highways, this hotel is a great option for a comfortable and easy stay.

What to Buy in Beaver Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bison Jerky: Made from the leanest and freshest bison meat, this jerky is a delicious and healthy snack full of North American flavor.
  • Wild Berry Preserves: Made with local berries and fruits, these jams and preserves are ideal for adding some regional flavors to your breakfast table at home.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: This region is home to talented potters producing unique pieces inspired by prairies’ natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Beaver Hills

The area has a rich and diverse culture, shaped by the region’s many indigenous communities and settlers. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s cultural heritage and traditions, notably the history of the local indigenous communities, and ensure cultural sensitivity during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Beaver Hills

The best time to visit Beaver Hills is in the summer between June and August. The weather is pleasant, and the nature and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a great time to visit when the leaves change colors, adding to the picturesque scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beaver Hills

Do:

  • Respect the local environment and its wildlife.
  • Embrace the local culture and enjoy the area’s traditions respectfully.
  • Try out the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas and attractions.
  • Disrespect the local culture or history.
  • Disturb the region’s wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beaver Hills

Beaver Hills is a safe region to visit, but visitors should still take standard safety precautions, including locking cars and securing valuables. When hiking or exploring off the beaten path, visitors should be extra cautious of the area’s wildlife encounters.

Health in Beaver Hills

Visitors should be aware of potential hazards when heading outdoors, including ticks and other insects that carry diseases, and exposure to the sun. Visitors should ensure that all vaccines are up to date as required by Canadian travel guidelines.

Getting to Beaver Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beaver Hills

The closest major airport to Beaver Hills is Edmonton International Airport, located approximately a 35-minute drive away. Visitors can rent a car or book a shuttle from the airport to get to Beaver Hills.

Bus Stations Near Beaver Hills

The Greyhound Bus Lines service the Edmonton area, including Fort Saskatchewan and Sherwood Park, providing bus access to Beaver Hills and its surrounding regions.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent way to explore Beaver Hills and its neighboring destinations. Visitors can also book a ride-share or take a taxi to travel in and around the area.

How to Get Around in Beaver Hills

Beaver Hills is best explored on foot, bike, or car. Visitors can rent bikes, e-bikes and cars at several locations at Elk Island National Park and the surrounding area. Taxis and rideshares are also available.

Where to Go Next from Beaver Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Edmonton: Explore Alberta’s capital city with its thriving arts and culture scenes, culinary delights, and opportunities for urban adventures.
  • Jasper National Park: A stunning gem in the Rocky Mountains known for its majestic peaks, glaciers, and glacial-fed turquoise lakes.
  • Banff National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site situated in the Rocky Mountains with stunning landscapes and a range of adventure and activities.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural landscapes, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Beaver Hills, Alberta, has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, this picturesque enclave will leave you with unforgettable memories. Plan your trip by following this comprehensive travel guide and make your visit to Beaver Hills one you won’t forget.
