Introduction to Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove is a charming city located in Edmonton Capital Region of the province of Alberta, in the Prairies region of Canada. With a population of approximately 35,000, Spruce Grove offers a small-town feel with the amenities of a larger city, and is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove has a lot to offer tourists, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top places to visit when exploring this lovely city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Spruce Grove is home to a variety of museums and historical places that offer insight into the city’s rich history and cultural heritage. Some of the top attractions in this category include:

  • Spruce Grove Grain Elevator Museum: a historical museum that tells the story of the grain elevator in Spruce Grove and its role in the community’s development.
  • Pioneer Museum: a museum that showcases the history of Spruce Grove and the surrounding area, including exhibits on agriculture, forestry, and transportation.
  • Stony Plain & Parkland Pioneer Museum: located just outside Spruce Grove, this museum features historical buildings and artifacts, including a replica of an early 20th century homestead.

Natural Attractions of Spruce Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spruce Grove is surrounded by natural beauty, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Jubilee Park: a beautiful park that offers trails for hiking and biking, picnic areas, a playground, and a spray park.
  • Chickakoo Lake Recreation Area: a beautiful nature reserve that offers hiking trails, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Heritage Grove Park: a park that features a natural area with walking trails, picnic areas, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove has a variety of trails and routes that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Spruce Grove Heritage Trail: a 10 km trail that winds through the city and features historical landmarks and points of interest.
  • Chickakoo Wapiti Trail: a 12 km trail that travels through the Chickakoo Lake Recreation Area, offering scenic views of the lake and surrounding forest.
  • Stony Plain Multicultural Heritage Trail: a 9 km trail that passes by historical landmarks and cultural sites in Stony Plain, just outside of Spruce Grove.

Best Things to Do in Spruce Grove: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re visiting Spruce Grove alone, with family or friends, there’s a wide range of activities and tours to enjoy in the city. Here are some of the top things to do in Spruce Grove:

  • Take a brewery tour at Dog Island Brewing.
  • Attend a Spruce Grove Saints hockey game at the city’s Grant Fuhr Arena.
  • Play a round of golf at one of the city’s beautiful golf courses, like the Links at Spruce Grove or the Whitetail Crossing Golf Club.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spruce Grove

If you’re planning to spend a week in Spruce Grove, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Spruce Grove Grain Elevator Museum and Pioneer Museum.

Day 2: Explore Jubilee Park and have a picnic.

Day 3: Hike the Chickakoo Wapiti Trail and have a BBQ by the lake.

Day 4: Take a tour of Dog Island Brewing and sample some local craft beers.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Whitetail Crossing Golf Club.

Day 6: Visit the Heritage Grove Park and take a leisurely walk through the natural area.

Day 7: Attend a Spruce Grove Saints hockey game at the Grant Fuhr Arena.

Food and Drink Scene of Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove offers a diverse culinary scene for food enthusiasts. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to indulge in while in the city:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta beef: try a steak or burger made from locally sourced beef.
  • Poutine: a classic Canadian dish made from french fries, gravy, and cheese curds.
  • Perogies: a Polish dish consisting of dough pockets filled with mashed potatoes and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beers from Dog Island Brewing in Spruce Grove.
  • Alberta wines from the nearby wineries like the Barr Estate Winery in Sherwood Park.
  • Whiskies from the nearby Hansen Distillery in Edmonton.

Where to Stay in Spruce Grove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spruce Grove offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences, ranging from hotels and motels to guesthouses and B&Bs. Here are some recommendations to consider:

  • Best Western Sunrise Inn & Suites: a modern hotel located in the heart of Spruce Grove with great amenities like a pool, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Horizon Motel: a cozy and affordable motel located close to Jubilee Park.
  • Three Point Inn: a charming guesthouse located just outside of Spruce Grove, offering a more peaceful and rural setting.

What to Buy in Spruce Grove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spruce Grove offers a variety of local products and souvenirs that you can bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • Local arts and crafts: visit the Spruce Grove Farmers’ Market to find unique handmade items like pottery, jewelry, and artwork.
  • Alberta honey and jams: Alberta is known for its high-quality honey and delicious jams - check out the Spruce Grove Honey and Berry Farm for some of the best local products.
  • Spruce Grove Saints merchandise: support the city’s junior hockey team by purchasing some official merchandise at the arena or local sportswear stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove, like all Canadian cities, has its own unique cultural traditions and customs. One of the most prominent cultural events is Spruce Grove’s annual Hay Maker Festival, which celebrates the city’s agricultural heritage with live music, food, and farming demonstrations.

Best Time to Visit Spruce Grove

The best time to visit Spruce Grove is between May and September when the weather is warmest and the days are longest. During this time, you can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and picnicking without worrying about the chilly winter temperatures. In the winter, Spruce Grove offers festive lights displays and a thriving local hockey scene.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spruce Grove

Here are some helpful tips and recommendations to keep in mind when visiting Spruce Grove:

Do:

  • Do try local foods and craft beers.
  • Do visit the local farmers’ market for unique souvenirs.
  • Do explore the city’s natural landmarks and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to carry extra layers for sudden weather changes.
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove is generally a safe city, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s recommended to take the usual precautions like locking your car and not walking alone after dark.

Health in Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove has good medical facilities and pharmacies, but it’s still advisable to bring any necessary prescription medications with you.

Getting to Spruce Grove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spruce Grove

The nearest airport to Spruce Grove is the Edmonton International Airport, which is located around 50 km away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or shuttle service to reach Spruce Grove.

Bus Stations Near Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove is well-connected by bus to other cities in the region, with regular services from major bus stations in Edmonton.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or use ride-sharing services like Uber to get to and around Spruce Grove once you arrive.

How to Get Around in Spruce Grove

Spruce Grove is a walkable city with a range of public transportation options available, including buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services.

Where to Go Next from Spruce Grove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region around Spruce Grove, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Edmonton: Alberta’s capital city is located just 20 minutes from Spruce Grove and offers a range of cultural attractions and activities.
  • Jasper National Park: a stunning national park located in the Rocky Mountains, approximately a 4-hour drive from Spruce Grove.
  • Elk Island National Park: a beautiful nature reserve that’s home to bison and elk herds, located around 30 minutes from Spruce Grove.

Conclusion

Spruce Grove is a charming and friendly city with plenty to offer visitors, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures and local culinary delights. By following this guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this lovely Canadian city.
