Introduction to North

North, located in the Edmonton Capital Region of Alberta, is known for its exceptional outdoor adventures, stunning scenic views, rich culture, and vibrant nightlife.

The region of North covers a vast area, making it an ideal destination for those who love to explore natural landscapes. From hiking and biking trails to wildlife sanctuaries, there is something for everyone. There is no shortage of cultural attractions, including museums and historical sites, in North.

In this guide, we will show you the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to get around in North.

List of Places to Visit in North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Fort Edmonton Park

Fort Edmonton Park is a living history museum that takes visitors back in time to explore the city’s history. The park is home to over 80 historic buildings and structures, including an 1846 Hudson’s Bay Fort, a 1905 Streetcar, and a 1920s midway.

2. Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village

The Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village is an open-air museum that showcases the Ukrainian settlement in east-central Alberta from 1892 to 1930. Visitors can explore over 40 restored buildings, including a grain elevator, a blacksmith shop, and a school.

3. Royal Alberta Museum

The Royal Alberta Museum is the largest museum in western Canada and home to over 2 million artifacts and specimens. The museum’s exhibits explore the natural history, human history, and cultural diversity of Alberta.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Elk Island National Park

Elk Island National Park is a wildlife sanctuary that is home to over 250 species of birds, 45 species of mammals, and 7 species of amphibians. Visitors can explore over 80km of hiking and biking trails, camp, and stargaze in one of Canada’s remaining dark sky preserves.

2. Miquelon Lake Provincial Park

Miquelon Lake Provincial Park is a serene oasis of wilderness and wetlands. Visitors can paddle its waters in a kayak, take a walk along the shores, or hike the park’s trail system.

3. North Saskatchewan River Valley

The North Saskatchewan River Valley is an oasis in the city of Edmonton. Follow the winding river trails through the heart of the city or hop on a bike and explore the river valley from above.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

1. The Blackfoot Area

The Blackfoot Area is a network of multi-use trails that stretches over 50km through the Blackfoot Recreational Area. The trails are accessible to hikers, bikers, and equestrians, and offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

2. Beaver Hills Trail Network

The Beaver Hills Trail Network offers over 19km of hiking and biking trails that wind through the grass and mixed-wood forests of the Cooking Lake-Blackfoot Provincial Recreation Area.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

1. Edmonton Riverboat Tours

Edmonton Riverboat Tours offers sightseeing, dinner, and event cruises on the North Saskatchewan River. Get a new perspective on the city and watch the sun set over the river.

2. Elk Island Guided Hike

Join a guided hike in Elk Island National Park to learn about the park’s history, wildlife, and ecosystem. Discover hidden gems and go off the beaten path.

Food and Drink Scene of North

Must-Try Dishes

1. Alberta Beef

Alberta beef is famous for its quality and flavour, thanks to the province’s grass-fed cattle industry. Try a steak or burger from one of the many local restaurants.

2. Poutine

Poutine is a Canadian classic, and many local restaurants serve up their own unique versions. Try poutine topped with smoked meat or pulled pork for a delicious twist on the classic dish.

Local Beverages

1. Craft Beer

Edmonton is home to an impressive craft beer scene, with dozens of microbreweries and taprooms to choose from. Try some of the local favourites, like Alley Kat Brewing Company or Blind Enthusiasm Brewing Company.

2. Alberta Wine

Alberta wine is a hidden gem, produced in the province’s wine region just south of Edmonton. Try a glass of wine from one of the many vineyards in the area, like Barr Estate Winery or Field Stone Fruit Wines.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget Option: HI Edmonton Hostel

The HI Edmonton Hostel offers affordable private rooms and dorms in a historic building in the heart of downtown. The hostel also provides bike rentals, a communal kitchen, and free breakfast.

Mid-Range Option: Matrix Hotel

The Matrix Hotel is a stylish and modern hotel located in the heart of downtown Edmonton. Enjoy complimentary breakfast, a fitness center, and a rooftop hot tub.

Luxury Option: Fairmont Hotel Macdonald

The Fairmont Hotel Macdonald is an iconic landmark in the city, with stunning views of the river valley and the city. Enjoy luxurious amenities and exceptional service at this historic hotel.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Canada is famous for its maple syrup, and many local producers in North offer their own unique varieties. Bring home a bottle of pure, locally sourced maple syrup to enjoy on your pancakes or waffles.

Local Artisan Crafts

North is home to many talented artisans and crafters who create unique and beautiful works of art. Browse local galleries and markets for one-of-a-kind souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of North

The people of North are proud of their heritage and culture. Respectful behaviour is always appreciated, and visitors are encouraged to learn more about the local history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the region’s winter activities, like skiing and snowshoeing, from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take time to explore the region’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in nature areas
  • Ignore warning signs in wildlife areas
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in North

North is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions like locking their vehicles and being aware of their surroundings. Wildlife encounters are possible in areas like Elk Island National Park, so visitors should follow park guidelines for staying safe.

Health in North

Visitors to North should ensure that they have appropriate medical coverage for their trip. Bring any necessary medications or prescriptions, and be aware of the nearest medical facilities in case of an emergency.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

The Edmonton International Airport is the closest major international airport, located about 45 minutes south of North. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use public transportation to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near North

The Greyhound Bus Station in Edmonton is the closest bus station to North. Visitors can take a bus or catch a ride with a shuttle service to reach North.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to North, as the region is accessible via major highways. Car rentals are available at the airport and in downtown Edmonton.

How to Get Around in North

The best way to get around North is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in downtown Edmonton.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jasper National Park

Jasper National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site located about 3.5 hours west of North. The park is home to pristine wilderness, stunning glaciers, and crystal-clear lakes.

Banff National Park

Banff National Park is another UNESCO World Heritage site that is located about 4 hours south of North. The park is known for its stunning landscapes and world-famous hot springs.

Conclusion

North is a must-see destination for anyone who loves natural beauty, outdoor adventure, and vibrant culture. With so much to see and do, you’ll want to come back again and again. Plan your trip today and discover all that North has to offer.
