Introduction to South

Located in Edmonton Capital Region, South is a vibrant and diverse destination in Alberta, Canada, with a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and modern amenities. This region is known for its stunning natural landscapes, such as hills, parks, and forests, as well as its rich cultural heritage, which can be seen in its museums, historical sites, and local festivals.

Here, you’ll find plenty of things to do, whether you’re into outdoor adventures, art and culture, or food and drink. From hiking and biking trails to historic landmarks and local markets, South has something to offer everyone. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this beautiful region of Canada.

List of Places to Visit in South

If you’re planning a trip to South, you’ll want to make the most of your time here by visiting some of the region’s must-see sights. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alberta Railway Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of rail transportation in Alberta and features a collection of historic trains and railway equipment. Visitors can take a ride on a vintage train and learn about the importance of rail transportation in Canada’s history.

  • Fort Edmonton Park: This living history museum is set in four different periods of Edmonton’s past and features interactive exhibits, historic buildings, and costumed interpreters. It’s a great way to learn about the city’s history and experience life in the past.

  • John Walter Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of John Walter, who was a prominent Edmonton businessman and entrepreneur in the early 1900s. Visitors can tour his historic home, which features original furnishing and artifacts from the time period.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elk Island National Park: This park is a great place to go for a day trip or overnight camping. It’s home to a variety of wildlife, including bison, elk, and beavers, and features hiking trails, lakes, and picnic areas.

  • Blackfoot Grazing Reserve: This area is known for its rolling hills, grasslands, and wildlife, including deer, moose, and coyotes. Visitors can hike or bike the trails to explore the beautiful scenery and catch a glimpse of the local fauna.

  • Mill Creek Ravine Park: This park is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and picnics. It features a scenic creek, wooded areas, and stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

If you’re a fan of outdoor adventures, South has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • River Valley Adventure Co.: This company offers bike rentals, guided bike tours, and bike repairs. They also offer shuttle service to and from the trails, so you can focus on enjoying the ride.

  • Mill Creek Ravine Trail: This trail is a 9-kilometre loop that takes you through some of South’s most scenic areas, including Mill Creek Ravine Park and the North Saskatchewan River Valley. It’s a great way to explore the natural beauty of the region.

  • Waskahegan Trail: This trail is a 300-kilometre loop that takes you through some of the most beautiful wilderness areas in Alberta, including South. It’s a challenging hike, but well worth the effort for the incredible views and chance to see local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for something adventurous, educational, or just plain fun, South has plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best options:

  • Segway Tours Edmonton: This company offers guided Segway tours of Edmonton’s river valley and other scenic areas. Visitors can ride through the park, past historic sites, and enjoy stunning views of the city.

  • High Level Bridge Streetcar: This historic streetcar offers a unique way to see the city and learn about its history. It’s a great activity for families and history buffs alike.

  • Culinary Bike Tour: This tour combines two of South’s best features - cycling and food. Participants bike to several different local restaurants and food markets, where they can sample some of the best local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

If you’re planning to spend a week in South, you’ll want to make sure you have enough time to see everything this region has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Fort Edmonton Park and John Walter Museum

Day 2: Mill Creek Ravine Trail and local food markets

Day 3: Blackfoot Grazing Reserve and outdoor activities in Elk Island National Park

Day 4: Museum of Natural History and the Royal Alberta Museum

Day 5: Segway tour of the river valley and local breweries

Day 6: Waskahegan Trail hike and picnic lunch

Day 7: Local festivals and events, such as Edmonton Folk Music Festival or Canadian Finals Rodeo

Food and Drink Scene of South

South is known for its diverse culinary scene, with everything from local specialties and food markets to international cuisine and craft breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This iconic Canadian dish consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy. You can find it at many local restaurants and food trucks in South.

  • Bannock: This traditional Indigenous bread is a staple in many local kitchens and restaurants. It’s made with flour, water, and salt and can be eaten on its own or served with jam or butter.

  • Wild game: Elk, bison, and other game meats are common in South and can be found on the menus of many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: South is home to several craft breweries, including Situation Brewing Company and Blind Enthusiasm Brewing Company, where you can sample unique and delicious beers.

  • Wine: Several local wineries, such as the Barr Estate Winery and vUrban Vineyard, offer tastings and tours and feature wines made from regional fruits and grapes.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, South has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the top places to stay in the area:

  • Metterra Hotel on Whyte: This upscale hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a rooftop hot tub, as well as a great location near shopping and dining in Old Strathcona.

  • Crash Hotel: This hip, boutique hotel offers stylish rooms, an on-site bar, and a great location near the river valley and local attractions.

  • HI Edmonton Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorms and private rooms, as well as a shared kitchen, lounge, and laundry facilities. It’s a great option for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, South has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • Old Strathcona Farmers’ Market: This popular weekend market features over 130 vendors selling everything from fresh produce and baked goods to handmade crafts and local artwork.

  • High Street: This trendy shopping district features local boutiques, galleries, and specialty shops where you can find unique gifts and souvenirs.

  • Edmonton Downtown Farmers’ Market: This year-round market features local vendors selling fresh produce, meats, baked goods, and crafts. It’s a great place to find unique souvenirs and sample some of the best local cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of South

South is a diverse region with a rich cultural heritage. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Indigenous culture: South is home to several Indigenous communities, including the Cree, the Blackfoot, and the Métis. Visitors can learn about their traditions and history at local museums and cultural centers.

  • Multicultural community: South is a diverse region with a mix of cultures and languages. Visitors can experience this diversity through the local food, festivals, and community events.

  • LGBTQ+ community: Edmonton is known for its inclusive and welcoming LGBTQ+ community. Visitors can find a variety of LGBTQ+ friendly establishments and events in the area.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South depends on your personal interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the different seasons:

  • Summer (June-August): This season is the most popular time to visit South, thanks to the pleasant weather and abundance of outdoor festivals and activities.

  • Fall (September-November): This season is known for its beautiful foliage and mild weather, as well as its harvest festivals and Halloween events.

  • Winter (December-February): This season is cold and snowy, but also offers opportunities for winter sports and festive holiday events.

  • Spring (March-May): This season is known for its mild weather and blooming wildflowers, as well as local events such as the Canadian Birkebeiner Ski Festival and the Edmonton Poetry Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

If you’re visiting South, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

  • Do explore the outdoors: South is known for its stunning natural landscapes, so make sure to spend plenty of time hiking, biking, and exploring the parks.
