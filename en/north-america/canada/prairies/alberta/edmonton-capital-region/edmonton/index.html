Introduction to Edmonton

Edmonton is the capital city of the Canadian province of Alberta and is located in the Prairies region of North America. Known as the “Gateway to the North,” Edmonton is the fifth largest municipality in Canada and is a hub for education, culture, and politics. The city is home to several natural and man-made attractions and parks, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Edmonton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Alberta Museum: This museum houses exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Alberta and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Alberta Legislature Building: The imposing building is home to the Legislative Assembly of Alberta and is a popular spot to witness the changing of the guard ceremony.
  • Art Gallery of Alberta: The art gallery displays a vast collection of contemporary and historical art and is a great place to learn about Alberta’s artistic heritage.
  • Fort Edmonton Park: This living history museum portrays the city’s development from a trading post to a modern metropolis. Visitors can explore the four distinct eras of Edmonton - 1846, 1885, 1905, and 1920.

Natural Attractions of Edmonton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elk Island National Park: Just a short drive from Edmonton, Elk Island National Park offers an escape into nature with stunning views, picnic areas, hiking, and wildlife watching.
  • Muttart Conservatory: This botanical garden features four glass pyramids with plants from the tropical and arid regions.
  • Rundle Park: This park is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy golfing, cross-country skiing, and beautiful trails along the North Saskatchewan River.
  • High Level Bridge Streetcar: This historic streetcar takes visitors across the Saskatchewan River and offers stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edmonton

  • River Valley Parks: The River Valley in Edmonton is a green belt spanning over 7400 hectares with a network of trails perfect for hiking and biking.
  • Mill Creek Ravine: A natural oasis located in the heart of the city perfect for exploring on foot or by bike.
  • Terwillegar Park: This park features over 186 hectares of forest and ravines with a network of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Edmonton: Activities and Tours

  • West Edmonton Mall: One of the biggest shopping malls in the world with an indoor amusement park, ice rink, and waterpark.
  • Edmonton Downtown Farmers Market: This market is an excellent place to find fresh produce, handmade crafts, artisanal foods, and live entertainment.
  • Edmonton Riverboat: Cruise along the North Saskatchewan to view the city skyline or enjoy a sunset dinner aboard the Riverboat.
  • Segway Tours: Take a guided tour of the city while riding a Segway around the downtown core.

Food and Drink Scene of Edmonton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta beef: Alberta is known for its high-quality, grass-fed beef. Try it at Rge Rd, a farm-to-table restaurant, or The Butternut Tree, which showcases regional ingredients in its dishes.
  • Poutine: A Quebec specialty that has made its way to Edmonton, poutine is a dish of crispy fries smothered in gravy and cheese curds. Try it at La Poutine.
  • Maple-glazed bacon donut: A unique breakfast must-try at the Moonshine Doughnuts.
  • Craft beer: Edmonton is home to several local breweries such as Alley Kat Brewing Company, Situation Brewing, and Blind Enthusiasm Brewing Company.

Local Beverages

  • Alberta Craft Distilleries: Try the locally-made vodka, gin, and rum at the Rig Hand Distillery.
  • Coffee: Edmonton is known for its vibrant coffee scene, with several independent cafes serving organic, locally roasted coffee, such as The Colombian Coffee & Roastery.
  • Wine: Enjoy a glass of local wine at the Chateau Lacombe Hotel, which has a vast selection of Canadian wines.

Where to Stay in Edmonton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Fairmont Hotel Macdonald: This grand hotel offers luxurious accommodations with stunning views of the river valley and the city skyline.
  • Varscona Hotel on Whyte: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Old Strathcona with unique and comfortable rooms.
  • HI Edmonton Hostel: A budget-friendly option with shared and private rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Glenora Bed and Breakfast Inn: A charming guesthouse in the tree-lined Glenora neighborhood with cozy rooms and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Edmonton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local honey: Pick up some honey from Beary Berry Honey, a family-run business that uses honey from bees who pollinate herbs such as lavender, cilantro, and basil.
  • Indigenous art: Edmonton is home to a vibrant Indigenous community, and local galleries such as Bearclaw Gallery and Artifacts Gallery showcase the rich history and culture of the indigenous people.
  • Local beer and spirits: Take home a bottle of locally made craft beer or spirits from one of the several local breweries and distilleries.
  • Handmade crafts: Visit the Old Strathcona Farmers’ Market for handmade crafts, artisanal soaps, and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Edmonton

Edmonton has a diverse and welcoming community. It is essential to be respectful of the city’s Indigenous heritage and culture. Visitors should also be aware of the extreme weather conditions during the winter months and prepare accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Edmonton

The best time to visit Edmonton is between May to September when the weather is pleasant, and most of the outdoor activities are open. The city also hosts several events and festivals during the summer, including the Edmonton Fringe Festival, Heritage Days, and the Edmonton International Street Performers Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edmonton

Do:

  • Respect the Indigenous heritage and culture of Edmonton.
  • Explore the River Valley parks.
  • Enjoy the vibrant food scene of Edmonton.
  • Support the local arts scene.
  • Visit Elk Island National Park and Fort Edmonton Park.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect natural spaces.
  • Don’t visit during winter if you are not prepared for extreme cold weather.
  • Don’t be rude to locals or visitors from other cultures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edmonton

Edmonton is generally a safe city, but visitors should be cautious and avoid walking alone late at night. Always lock your car and your accommodation, and avoid keeping valuables in places that are easily accessible.

Health in Edmonton

Edmonton has a top-quality healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care, including emergency services, at any time. It is essential to have travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies or accidents.

Getting to Edmonton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edmonton

  • Edmonton International Airport: Located 30 km south of the city center, the airport handles international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Edmonton

  • Red Arrow Motorcoach: Offers direct bus service to Edmonton from several cities in Alberta, such as Calgary, Fort McMurray, and Grande Prairie.

Other Options

  • Via Rail Canada: Trains offer service to Edmonton from several Canadian cities, including Toronto, Vancouver, and Winnipeg.
  • Greyhound Canada: Offers bus service to Edmonton from several Canadian cities.

How to Get Around in Edmonton

  • Public transportation: Edmonton has an efficient public transportation system that includes buses, light rail transit, and the High Level Bridge Streetcar.
  • Biking: Edmonton has an extensive network of bike paths and bike lanes, making biking a popular option for getting around the city.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services: Taxis and ride-sharing services are available throughout the city.
  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car or drive their vehicle in Edmonton, but it is essential to be cautious of winter road conditions.

Where to Go Next from Edmonton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jasper National Park: Explore the stunning glaciers, lakes, and mountains of the Rocky Mountains in Jasper National Park, located just a few hours’ drive northwest of Edmonton.
  • Banff National Park: Another must-visit destination near Edmonton is Banff National Park, known for its stunning scenery, wildlife, and hot springs.
  • Prince Albert National Park: Enjoy camping, hiking, and wildlife watching in Prince Albert National Park, located a few hours’ drive northeast of Edmonton.

Conclusion

Edmonton is a vibrant and exciting city that offers something for everyone. From natural attractions and outdoor activities to cultural landmarks, food scene, and convenient transportation options, Edmonton is a perfect destination for travelers seeking an adventure.
