Introduction to Nanton

Nanton is a small town in Southern Alberta, located in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains. It is a perfect place to visit for nature lovers, history buffs, and culture enthusiasts. Nanton offers a range of activities and attractions, including museums, hiking and biking trails, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. Discover the best things to do in Nanton and plan your trip now!

List of Places to Visit in Nanton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bomber Command Museum of Canada

The Bomber Command Museum of Canada is a great place to learn about Canadian Aviation History. The museum exhibits one of the largest collections of military aircraft in Canada, including a Lancaster Bomber and a rare B-25 Mitchell bomber.

Canadian Grain Elevator Discovery Centre

The Canadian Grain Elevator Discovery Centre is a historic site with two elevators that have been restored to their original state. Visitors can learn about the role of grain elevators in the development of Canada’s prairies.

Nanton Lancaster Society Air Museum

The Nanton Lancaster Society Air Museum is dedicated to the preservation of a Lancaster bomber aircraft and the history of Bomber Command. Visitors can also check out the authentic British wartime vehicle collection, including fire engines, ambulances, and staff cars.

Natural Attractions of Nanton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chain Lakes Provincial Park

Chain Lakes Provincial Park is a stunning natural area that features several lakes and wetlands. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including camping, hiking, fishing, canoeing, and picnicking.

Coutts Centre for Western Canadian Heritage

The Coutts Centre for Western Canadian Heritage is a beautiful park with gardens, walking trails, and historic buildings. Visitors can take a guided tour of the centre to learn about the Coutts family and their contribution to the development of Western Canada.

Mosquito Creek Hiking Trail

The Mosquito Creek Hiking Trail is a challenging trail that offers great views of the Rocky Mountains. The trailhead is located just outside of Nanton, and the hike takes around 3-4 hours to complete.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nanton

Nanton has a range of hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best:

Rotary Park Trail

The Rotary Park Trail is a beautiful walking trail that winds through a park in the heart of Nanton. The trail features a playground, picnic area, and benches for visitors to relax and enjoy the scenery.

Misty Ridge Trail

Misty Ridge Trail is a moderate hiking trail that offers stunning views of the valley and foothills. The trailhead is located just south of Nanton, and it takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

Crowsnest Pass (Biking Route)

For experienced cyclists, the Crowsnest Pass is a challenging biking route that takes you through scenic mountain views and valleys. The route is approximately 80 kilometers long and takes around 4-5 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Nanton: Activities and Tours

Bomber Command Museum of Canada Tour

Take a tour of the Bomber Command Museum of Canada and immerse yourself in Canada’s aviation history. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the Lancaster bomber aircraft and learn about the lives of the crew members.

Dog Creek Trail Rides

Experience the beauty of the Rocky Mountains on horseback with Dog Creek Trail Rides. Visitors can enjoy a trail ride through the foothills and Rocky Mountains, and will be accompanied by an experienced guide.

Coutts Centre for Western Canadian Heritage Guided Tour

Take a guided tour of the Coutts Centre for Western Canadian Heritage and learn about the history of Western Canada. Visitors can also stroll through the beautiful gardens and enjoy the stunning views of the prairies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nanton

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Nanton:

Day 1 - Explore downtown Nanton and visit local shops and restaurants.

Day 2 - Visit the Bomber Command Museum of Canada and take a guided tour of the Lancaster bomber aircraft.

Day 3 - Go hiking on the Mosquito Creek Hiking Trail and enjoy scenic views of the Rocky Mountains.

Day 4 - Take a tour of the Canadian Grain Elevator Discovery Centre and learn about the role of grain elevators in the development of Western Canada.

Day 5 - Enjoy a trail ride with Dog Creek Trail Rides and experience the beauty of the Rocky Mountains.

Day 6 - Visit the Coutts Centre for Western Canadian Heritage and take a guided tour of the centre.

Day 7 - Spend the day at Chain Lakes Provincial Park and enjoy a range of outdoor activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Nanton

Must-Try Dishes

Nanton is known for its local cuisine and offers a range of dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

Beef Jerky

Nanton is famous for its beef jerky, available in different flavors, including teriyaki, hot, and honey garlic.

Butter Tarts

Butter tarts are a classic Canadian dessert, and the ones from Nanton are some of the best. They are made with flaky pastry crusts and filled with a delicious mixture of butter, sugar, and eggs.

Honey

Nanton is also known for its delicious honey. Visitors can buy honey from local beekeepers in the area.

Local Beverages

Visitors can enjoy local beverages in Nanton, including craft beer and spirits.

Bomber Brewing Company

Bomber Brewing Company produces craft beer with unique flavors, including IPA, stout, and lager.

Trailhead Spirits Distillery

Trailhead Spirits Distillery produces premium craft spirits, including vodka, gin, and whiskey.

Where to Stay in Nanton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nanton Inn

Nanton Inn is a comfortable hotel with spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a restaurant. The rooms are equipped with all necessary amenities, including cable TV, a mini-fridge, and a microwave.

Lancaster Inn

Lancaster Inn is a historic hotel with charming rooms and a restaurant. The hotel is located in the heart of Nanton, and visitors can easily access local shops and restaurants.

Nanton Camping

Visitors can also camp at Chain Lakes Provincial Park, which offers campsites with beautiful views of the lakes and wetlands. The park has all necessary amenities, including showers, toilets, and fire pits.

What to Buy in Nanton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Antique Stores

Nanton is known for its antique stores, which offer a range of vintage items, including furniture, art, and collectibles.

Sweetgrass and Sage Brush Products

Visitors can also purchase sweetgrass and sage brush products, which are used in indigenous ceremonies.

Local Culture and Customs of Nanton

The local culture of Nanton is influenced by its history and geography. The town is surrounded by prairies and is home to several historic sites. The culture is also influenced by the indigenous people who have lived in the area for centuries.

Best Time to Visit Nanton

The best time to visit Nanton is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and camping.

Getting to Nanton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nanton is located approximately 1.5 hours south of Calgary, and visitors can reach the town by car or bus.

Other Options

Where to Go Next from Nanton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waterton Lakes National Park

Waterton Lakes National Park is a stunning natural area with several lakes, mountains, and wildlife. It is located approximately 2 hours south of Nanton.

Banff National Park

Banff National Park is a popular tourist destination with stunning mountain views, wildlife, and several outdoor activities. It is located approximately 2.5 hours west of Nanton.
