Introduction to Brooks

Located in Southern Alberta, Brooks is a small city with a population of around 15,000 people. Though it is often overshadowed by larger cities like Calgary and Edmonton, Brooks has plenty to offer visitors in terms of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, there’s something for everyone in Brooks.

List of Places to Visit in Brooks

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to get a sense of Brooks’s rich history and culture is by visiting some of its most important museums and historical sites.

  • Brooks and District Museum: Explore artifacts and exhibits that showcase the area’s heritage and evolution over time.
  • Heritage Park: Enjoy a guided tour of this living museum, which includes 16 buildings, artifacts, and exhibits from Brooks’s settlement period.
  • Duchess Historical Walkway: Take a self-guided tour and learn about the history of nearby Duchess.

Natural Attractions of Brooks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to experience the stunning beauty of Southern Alberta’s natural landscapes, Brooks boasts plenty of outdoor activities and scenic vistas.

  • Dinosaur Provincial Park: Discover the world-renowned badlands and UNESCO World Heritage site, with fascinating fossils and interpretive exhibits.
  • Tillebrook Provincial Park: Hike, bike, or boat around this peaceful park’s 120 hectares of rolling hills, riverbanks, and natural beauty.
  • Brooks Aqueduct: Walk the only remaining aqueduct of its kind in Canada and take in the stunning views of the surrounding prairie.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brooks

Given the region’s abundance of natural beauty, hiking and biking are popular activities in and around Brooks. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Brooks & District Bike Park: This bike park offers multiple routes that are suitable for beginner to advanced riders and includes both dirt jumps and a pump track.
  • Brooks Aqueduct Trail: This easy, scenic trail follows the historic Brooks Aqueduct, offering panoramic views of the area’s prairies and badlands.
  • Dinosaur Provincial Park Trails: The park offers multiple hiking and mountain biking trails, allowing visitors to get up close and personal with the area’s dinosaur fossils.

Best Things to Do in Brooks: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in local culture or simply have some fun, Brooks offers a wide range of activities and tours.

  • Crooked Creek Fun Park: This family-friendly park features bumper boats, go-karts, mini-golf, and more, making it a great option for a day of fun.
  • Brooks Corn Maze: Get lost (and found) in Canada’s largest corn maze, which also offers wagon rides, mini-golf, and other activities.
  • Skydive Big Sky: Take in the breathtaking views from above with a tandem skydive, suitable for both first-timers and experienced jumpers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brooks

If you’re planning to spend a full week in Brooks, consider the following itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Brooks

Must-Try Dishes

From classic Canadian comfort food to global culinary influences, Brooks has plenty of delicious dishes to offer.

  • Beef dip at Bailey’s Pub: This classic sandwich uses Brooks’s famous beef for a hearty and delicious lunch option.
  • Greek salad at Bernie & The Boys: This fresh and flavorful salad is a great option for a light but satisfying meal.
  • Indian cuisine at Spice Hut: Enjoy authentic Indian food, including a variety of curry dishes and tandoori specialties.

Local Beverages

No trip to Southern Alberta would be complete without sampling some of its local beverages.

  • Brooks Brewing: Sample a variety of local brews at this popular microbrewery.
  • Origin Malting & Brewing Co.: Try some craft beer made with locally sourced malts at this popular brewery.
  • Ebel’s Ace Brewing Co.: Enjoy some excellent craft beer options, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

Where to Stay in Brooks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodations or a luxury getaway, Brooks has plenty of options to choose from.

  • Heritage Inn & Suites: This modern hotel offers comfortable and spacious guest rooms and a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Ramada by Wyndham: Located in the heart of Brooks, this hotel offers easy access to the city’s attractions and offers an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Canalta Hotel: This hotel offers spacious suites with kitchenettes, offering guests more flexibility during their stay.

What to Buy in Brooks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Brooks’s unique culture and history with these locally made products:

  • Brooks beef: Bring home some of the area’s famous beef to cook up at home.
  • Dinosaur fossils: Take home a piece of history with a souvenir fossil from Dinosaur Provincial Park.
  • Local art: Browse and purchase works from local artists at the Brooks and District Museum gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Brooks

Southern Alberta is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, and Brooks is no exception. Locals take pride in their community and are always happy to share their tips and recommendations with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Brooks

The best time to visit Brooks is during the summer season, from June to August. This is the ideal time to enjoy all of the outdoor activities and attractions that the city has to offer, including hiking, biking, and river rafting. The weather is warm and sunny, with average temperatures ranging from 20 to 25 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brooks

Do:

  • Do bring comfortable shoes for exploring the city and the surrounding parks and attractions.
  • Do visit the local breweries to sample some of the area’s best beer and meet some friendly locals.
  • Do take advantage of the area’s natural beauty by hiking, biking, or taking a scenic drive.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the sunshine - be sure to wear sunscreen and bring a hat to protect yourself from the strong UV rays.
  • Don’t miss out on the chance to try some of Brooks’s famous beef dishes.
  • Don’t forget to check hours of operation for attractions and restaurants during holidays and special events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brooks

Brooks is a generally safe and friendly community, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against theft and other crimes. Be sure to lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodations and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight. If you’re traveling alone, try to avoid walking in unlit areas at night.

Health in Brooks

One of the most important things to keep in mind when visiting Brooks is the area’s strong sunshine. Be sure to wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburns and skin damage. If you’re hiking or spending time outdoors, be sure to carry plenty of water and snacks to stay hydrated.

Getting to Brooks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brooks

The closest airport to Brooks is the Medicine Hat Regional Airport, which is located about 70 kilometers to the east. The Calgary International Airport is another option and is located about 200 kilometers to the northwest.

Bus Stations Near Brooks

Greyhound offers daily bus service to Brooks from Calgary and Medicine Hat.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Brooks is located along the Trans-Canada Highway, making it easily accessible by car. You can also take the train to the nearby town of Tilley, which is about a 30-minute drive from Brooks.

How to Get Around in Brooks

While some attractions and parks are located outside of the city and require a car, most of the downtown area is easily walkable. If you need to travel farther, taxi services are available in Brooks, or you can rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Brooks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re ready to explore beyond Brooks, consider one of these nearby destinations:

  • Medicine Hat: This city is known for its vibrant arts scene and outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • Calgary: Just a two-hour drive away, Calgary is known for its lively food and drink scene, museums, and shopping.
  • Banff National Park: This stunning national park is about a 4-hour drive away and offers gorgeous scenery, hiking, and winter sports activities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply unwinding in a friendly, welcoming community, Brooks is a must-visit destination in Southern Alberta. With its beautiful parks, rich history, and delicious food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this hidden gem of a city.
