Introduction to Hanna

Nestled in the stunning landscapes of Southern Alberta, Hanna is a charming town that offers an ideal escape from the hustle and bustle of big cities. With its pristine wilderness, rich culture, and warm hospitality, Hanna has a lot to offer for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor adventurer or a foodie, you’ll find plenty to explore and enjoy in this hidden gem of Alberta.

List of Places to Visit in Hanna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hanna Pioneer Museum: Located in the heart of Hanna, this museum takes you back in time to the early 20th century and showcases the town’s pioneer heritage. You’ll find antique farm machinery, wagons, and a variety of exhibits that depict the life and times of the early settlers in the area.
  • Hanna Roundhouse Society: This historical site is home to the last remaining Canadian Northern Railway Roundhouse in Western Canada. Built-in 1913, this national historic place is a must-visit for railway enthusiasts.
  • The Hanna Water Tower: Standing tall at 33 meters high, the Hanna Water Tower is a prominent landmark that has been part of the town’s skyline for over a century. Take a tour of the tower and learn about its history and significance in the development of the town.

Natural Attractions of Hanna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elkwater Lake: Located just outside of town, Elkwater Lake is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and fishing. The area is surrounded by forests, making it an excellent place for hiking and picnicking.
  • Dyck’s Lake: This picturesque lake is surrounded by trees and is an ideal spot for a lazy afternoon of fishing or a tranquil paddle boat ride.
  • Pembina River Provincial Park: Just a short drive from Hanna, Pembina River Provincial Park is a serene park with clear waters, sandy beaches, and amazing natural scenery. Enjoy hiking, canoeing, and picnicking amidst the beauty of the Canadian Prairies.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanna

  • Hanna Trail System: Explore the town’s surroundings through its scenic walking and biking trail. The Hanna trail system offers over 20 kilometers of well-maintained paths that take visitors out into the stunning countryside, ideal for those who love to explore the outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Hanna: Activities and Tours

  • Teepee Creek Stampede: This annual rodeo event held in nearby Teepee Creek draws thousands of tourists each year for four days of action-packed entertainment.
  • Fishing: With an abundance of small lakes and streams, Hanna is an angler’s paradise, perfect for catching walleye, northern pike, and rainbow trout.
  • Farm Tours: If you’re interested in agriculture, then book a farm tour and learn about the local ranching and farming lifestyles.
  • Star Gazing: With its location away from big cities, Hanna provides incredible stargazing opportunities. Join a tour and learn about the constellations and Northern Lights.

Food and Drink Scene of Hanna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Local Beef: Hanna is in the heart of Alberta’s beef country, making locally raised beef a must-try. Enjoy a juicy steak or mouth-watering Alberta beef burger at one of the town’s many dining spots.
  • Perogies: A traditional Eastern European dish, Hanna’s perogies are deliciously doughy and stuffed with savory fillings such as cheese, potatoes, and sauerkraut.

Local Beverages

  • Alberta Craft Beers: Hanna is home to a few microbreweries which produce unique and distinctive craft beers that are well worth a try.
  • Prairie Berry Wines: Indulge in some local Prairie Berry Wines, made from the berries that grow in the surrounding prairies, providing a taste of the region’s rich cultures.

Where to Stay in Hanna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Canalta Hanna: A hotel conveniently located in the heart of the town, Canalta offers excellent amenities coupled with modern rooms and friendly staff.
  • Hanna Hotel: A budget-friendly guesthouse located close to Main Street. The Hanna Hotel offers basic accommodation within walking distance to restaurants and attractions.

What to Buy in Hanna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hanna Honey: The town is famous for its honey, perfect for taking home a sweet and sticky memento.
  • Western Apparel: You’ll find plenty of western-style clothing and accessories at the local shops, perfect for embodying the spirit of the Canadian Prairies.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanna

Hanna is a friendly and welcoming place, reflective of its authentic Western hospitality and vibrant ranching history. The town celebrates its local customs and cultural heritage, offering visitors a truly unique experience of rural life on Canada’s prairies

Best Time to Visit Hanna

The best time to visit Hanna is summer, between June and September, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are at their peak. Autumn offers a beautiful display of fall colors, perfect for those who enjoy hiking and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanna

Do:

  • Do take a farm tour to learn about the surrounding area’s agricultural industry and local cultures.
  • Do bring your swimsuit to take advantage of the stunning natural lakes and waterways in the area.
  • Do sample the local cuisine, including the famous Hanna honey and Alberta beef.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing during winters where temperatures tend to drop below -20 °C
  • Don’t be too harsh with the locals, as decency and kindness are part of the town’s customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanna

Hanna is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals always willing to lend a helping hand. However, it is advisable to exercise caution when venturing into the wilderness, always dress appropriately for the weather, and let someone know where you’re going and when you plan to return.

Health in Hanna

Hanna has a hospital that is open 24/7 to cater to any medical emergencies. However, it is always advisable to carry essential medications and inform your doctor before embarking on your journey.

Getting to Hanna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hanna

The closest international airport to Hanna is Calgary International Airport, which is 220 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Hanna

There are Greyhound Bus services from Calgary to Hanna.

Other Options

Renting a car or driving is the most convenient way to get to Hanna. There are car rental options at the Calgary International Airport and in other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Hanna

The easiest way to get around in Hanna is by car. Walking and biking are also ideal options and provide an opportunity to take in the stunning scenery of the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Hanna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Drumheller: Located just a 1.5-hour drive from Hanna, Drumheller boasts stunning landscapes and is home to the Royal Tyrrell Museum, a must-visit for dinosaur enthusiasts.
  • Calgary: Cosmopolitan Calgary is only a 2-hour drive away and offers a wide range of cultural and historic attractions such as Calgary Tower and the Glenbow Museum.

Conclusion

Hanna’s unparalleled outdoor activities, rich culture, and welcoming hospitality are enough reasons for any adventurous traveler to add the town to their itinerary. Its beautiful wilderness and unique heritage make it a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Canadian Prairie experience. From its historical sites to its local cuisine, Hanna offers something exciting for every type of traveler.
