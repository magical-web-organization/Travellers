Introduction to Milk River

Milk River, a charming town located in Southern Alberta, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by avid travellers. Nestled in the middle of the Prairies, the town is surrounded by natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse outdoor adventures.

Visitors come to Milk River to immerse themselves in stunning scenery, engage in outdoor activities, learn about history and culture, and experience the authentic hospitality of the locals.

List of Places to Visit in Milk River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Milk River has a wealth of history and cultural significance to explore, with its many museums and historical places offering a glimpse into the past.

  • Writing-On-Stone Provincial Park: Known as one of Alberta’s most significant Indigenous archaeological sites, it is a must-visit historical attraction. The park is rich in history with many petroglyph sites and over 140 archeological sites that date back over 3,500 years.
  • Milk River and District Museum: A Canadian Register of Historic Places designated museum, which aims to preserve the cultural heritage of Milk River and surround regions. It exhibits the historical items and stories of the region’s past.
  • St. Mary’s Irrigation Canal: This historic site, built-in 1905, was the first major irrigation project in Southern Alberta. Take a tour and appreciate the architecture and engineering of the canal.

Natural Attractions of Milk River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Milk River is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of outdoor activities and impressive scenery.

  • Milk River Natural Area: Spanning over 4000 hectares, it is an excellent spot for scenic drives, mountain biking, hiking, and wildlife watching.
  • Writing-On-Stone Provincial Park: Apart from its historical significance, the park offers breathtaking views of the hoodoos (unique rock formations) and Milk River valley.
  • Warrior’s Path Provincial Park: A popular choice for those who love to cycle and hike while enjoying the natural surroundings and wildlife sightings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milk River

Milk River and its surroundings offer plenty of trails for hiking and mountain biking, catering to all skill levels. Here are some of the trails that one must explore:

  • Kodiak trail and Rattler Trail: A 6 km hiking trail route through hills, forest, and a creek.
  • Wild Horse Trail: Approximately a 7 km trail offering bikeriders a stunning view of the prairies, greenery, and natural surroundings.
  • Pine Coulee Trail: A 16 km moderately challenging trail running around the coulee reservoir, offers scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Milk River: Activities and Tours

Milk River delights visitors with a plethora of activities, giving them a chance to experience the local culture, cuisine, and outdoor activities. Here are some top things to do:

  • Horseback Riding at Rafter Six Ranch: Explore the natural surroundings of Milk River on horseback, witnessing the stunning scenery up close.
  • River Tubing with Lazy-r: Float down the Milk River and take in the scenic views while lounging on a tube.
  • Fossil Hunting with Dinosaur Valley Studios: Embark on a thrilling fossil hunting adventure tour with the experts and discover the remnants of the prehistoric era.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milk River

Suppose you’re planning to spend a week or more in Milk River and want to make sure you don’t miss out on anything exciting. In that case, this itinerary guarantees you an exciting week filled with history, culture, and outdoor adventures.

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Writing-On-Stone Provincial Park and explore the history and stunning views of this area.
Day 2 Stroll around Milk River and the District Museum to learn about the town’s heritage.
Day 3 Engage in outdoor activities like hiking or mountain biking on the Wild Horse trail.
Day 4 Visit the St. Mary’s Irrigation Canal and learn about southern Alberta’s first successful irrigation project.
Day 5 Go for a river tubing trip with Lazy-r and spend the day soaking up the sun and taking in the beautiful scenery.
Day 6 Take a day trip to the Head-Smashed-In Buffalo Jump Provincial Park and explore its rich history and natural surroundings.
Day 7 Spend the day exploring the various hiking and biking trails in the milk river natural area.

Food and Drink Scene of Milk River

When it comes to the food scene, Milk River does not disappoint. The town boasts several cafes and restaurants that offer unique local dishes and beverages that you must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cowboy Cookies: A cookie with chocolate chips, oats, and coconut that is an excellent snack for adventurers.
  • Barbecued Beef Sandwiches: A delicious sandwich made with fresh roast beef and a BBQ sauce, perfect for lunch.
  • Hutterite Plate: A popular local dish comprising chicken or pork, mashed potatoes, and apple sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Milk River Brewery: A microbrewery offering unique brews, whiskey, and scotch whiskeys made with locally sourced grains.
  • Coffee Canteen: An excellent spot for coffee lovers, serving freshly roasted coffee, and homemade pastries.

Where to Stay in Milk River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milk River has a range of accommodation options, including spacious hotels, cozy guesthouses, and backpackers’ hostels. We recommend the following:

  • Milk River Motor Inn: A budget-friendly motel offering comfortable and spacious rooms.
  • Scranton’s Motel: Located downtown, it offers comfortable rooms, friendly staff, and a restaurant serving delicious meals.
  • Prairie Rose Bed & Breakfast: A beautiful guesthouse offering cozy rooms and breakfast with a stunning view.

What to Buy in Milk River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milk River is renowned for its local arts and crafts, and you can purchase several locally sourced products to take home with you.

  • Western Wear: Pick up your cowboy hat, boots, and other western wear at the local shops in downtown Milk River.
  • Milk River Pottery: Hand-crafted pottery and ceramics are available at local boutiques.
  • Art and Crafts: Milk River has several stores and studios that sell locally sourced art and crafts items.

Local Culture and Customs of Milk River

Milk River’s culture is highly influenced by local heritage and Western cowboy culture. The residents are known to be warm and welcoming to tourists. During your stay, you’ll notice that:

  • Locals do not wear shoes inside their homes.
  • It is customary to greet the locals you meet on the street and make eye contact with a friendly smile.

Best Time to Visit Milk River

The best time to visit Milk River is between late spring and early fall when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are abundant. The town holds several exciting festivals and events during this time, including the Milk River Days, a celebration of the town’s culture and heritage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milk River

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry cash as some local shops do not accept cards.
  • Take part in the outdoor activities available.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside homes.
  • Litter in public areas or natural surroundings.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milk River

Milk River is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, we advise visitors to take precautions such as:

  • Follow safety guidelines while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Secure valuables and lock hotel rooms when going out.

Health in Milk River

Milk River has a range of medical facilities and pharmacies. The nearest hospital is the Chinook Regional Hospital in Lethbridge, approximately an hour away. We advise visitors to carry their medication and take precautions against the sun and dehydration during outdoor activities.

Getting to Milk River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Milk River

The closest airport to Milk River is Calgary International Airport, approximately a four-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Milk River

Milk River has no direct bus service. However, visitors can take a bus to neighbouring cities such as Lethbridge, from where they can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Milk River.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car from airports or neighbouring cities to reach Milk River.

How to Get Around in Milk River

Milk River is a small town, and visitors can stroll and explore the main attractions on foot. Still, having a car would be beneficial when planning to take day trips or engage in outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Milk River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Head-Smashed-In Buffalo Jump: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located approximately an hour away from Milk River.
  • Lethbridge: A vibrant city known for its culture, restaurants, and nightlife, an hour and a half drive away from Milk River.
  • Glacier National Park: A stunning national park located in Montana, just a two-hour drive south from Milk River.

Conclusion

Milk River is a picturesque town that offers visitors the chance to explore its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. The town’s friendly locals, diverse outdoor activities, and scenic views make it an excellent destination for travellers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Plan your visit today and experience the authentic charm of Milk River.
