Introduction to Drumheller

Nestled in the heart of Southern Alberta, Drumheller is a small town that offers big adventures. Known as the “Dinosaur Capital of the World,” Drumheller boasts a rich history steeped in culture and boasts a vibrant food and drink scene. With an abundance of outdoor activities, natural wonders, and exciting attractions, this charming town is a must-visit for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Drumheller

Looking for things to do in Drumheller? Here are the top attractions you can’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Tyrrell Museum: This renowned museum is home to over 130,000 fossils and is widely considered one of the best dinosaur museums in the world.

  • Atlas Coal Mine National Historic Site: Take an underground tour of this historic coal mine and learn about the life of miners in the early 20th century.

  • Homestead Antique Museum: A fascinating collection of vintage agricultural machinery, homesteading equipment, and other historic artifacts.

  • World’s Largest Dinosaur: Climb up inside this giant T-Rex for a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding Badlands.

Natural Attractions of Drumheller: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseshoe Canyon: Take a scenic hike and marvel at the stunning natural beauty of this unique geological formation.

  • Hoodoos: These towering rock formations are a natural wonder and will leave you in awe of Mother Nature’s power.

  • Badlands: Explore this unique landscape and discover hidden canyons and stunning rock formations.

  • Drumheller Suspension Bridge: Hang 117 feet above the flowing Red Deer River under this stunning suspension bridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Drumheller

Drumheller boasts a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • Hoodoo Trail: A moderate 6.2 km hike that offers stunning views of the badlands and hoodoos.

  • Badlands Trail: A 7.3 km hike that takes you through twisting canyons and past stunning rock formations.

  • Dinosaur Trail: An easy 4.1 km trail that winds through a stunning desert valley.

  • Rosebud River Trail: A 9.7 km biking trail that features stunning views of the Red Deer River and the surrounding badlands.

Best Things to Do in Drumheller: Activities and Tours

Looking for things to do in Drumheller? Here are some must-try activities and tours:

  • Royal Tyrrell Museum Tour: Dive into the world of dinosaurs with a guided tour of the Royal Tyrrell Museum.

  • Helicopter Tour: Take in the stunning views of the badlands from above with a thrilling helicopter tour.

  • ATV Excursions: Brave the backcountry on an ATV and explore Drumheller’s wilderness.

  • Star Gazing Tour: With minimal light pollution, Drumheller is the perfect spot for star gazing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Drumheller

Day 1: Visit the Royal Tyrrell Museum and take a guided tour to learn about the history of dinosaurs.

Day 2: Explore the Atlas Coal Mine and take an underground tour of the historic coal mine.

Day 3: Hike or bike one of Drumheller’s scenic trails, such as the Hoodoo Trail or the Badlands Trail.

Day 4: Take a helicopter tour to see the stunning beauty of the badlands from above.

Day 5: Go on an ATV excursion to explore the backcountry and experience the wilderness of Drumheller.

Day 6: Visit the Homestead Antique Museum and check out the vintage agricultural machinery and homesteading equipment.

Day 7: Take a relaxing stroll on the Rosebud River Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the Red Deer River.

Food and Drink Scene of Drumheller

Drumheller is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with a variety of culinary experiences on offer:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Taste Alberta’s famous bison in a juicy burger with all the fixings.

  • Prairie Oyster Shooter: Try this unique cocktail made with whisky and a raw egg yolk.

  • Perogies: A classic Canadian dish, perogies are best enjoyed with sour cream and onion,

  • Butter Tarts: These delicious, sweet tarts are filled with buttery, sugary goodness.

Local Beverages

  • Alberta Craft Beer: Drumheller boasts a vibrant craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering unique beers.

  • Prairie Vodka: Made from locally grown wheat, prairie vodka is the perfect drink for any cocktail lover.

  • Prairie Berry Wine: Sample some of Alberta’s delicious homemade wine, which is made from locally grown berries.

Where to Stay in Drumheller: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Looking for the perfect place to stay in Drumheller? Here are some options:

  • Canalta Jurassic: A family-friendly hotel that offers great amenities and comfort.

  • Travellers Inn: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean rooms and great service.

  • Badlands Motel: A charming motel that is conveniently located near the town’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Drumheller: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and local products to take home after your trip to Drumheller? Here are some great options:

  • Dinosaur fossils: Drumheller is famous for its fossils, so why not take home some of these ancient treasures?

  • Prairie Beef Jerky: Alberta is famous for its beef, so grab a bag of delicious, locally made beef jerky.

  • Handmade crafts: Drumheller is home to many talented artisans, so pick up some handmade pottery, jewelry, or other crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Drumheller

Alberta has a rich cultural heritage, and Drumheller is no exception. The town is home to many Indigenous communities, so be sure to respect their culture and customs when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Drumheller

Drumheller is best visited between May and September when the weather is warm and the town’s many outdoor attractions are open for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Drumheller

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the local Indigenous communities.

  • Take precautions when hiking or biking, as the terrain can be challenging.

  • Visit the Royal Tyrrell Museum and Atlas Coal Mine for an educational and insightful experience.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or remove fossils from the badlands or any other natural areas.

  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring Drumheller’s outdoor attractions.

  • Forget to try the local food and drink scene.

Safety and Health

Safety in Drumheller

Drumheller is a safe and welcoming town. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring the backcountry and rough terrain.

Health in Drumheller

Drumheller has excellent medical facilities, including a hospital and numerous clinics. It is always recommended to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Drumheller: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Drumheller

  • Calgary International Airport: Located about 1.5 hours away from Drumheller, this is the closest major airport.

  • Edmonton International Airport: Located about 2.5 hours away from Drumheller, this is another option for visitors.

Bus Stations Near Drumheller

  • Greyhound Canada: The station is located in the downtown area.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from either Calgary or Edmonton and drive to Drumheller.

How to Get Around in Drumheller

Renting a car is the best way to get around Drumheller, especially if you plan on exploring the backcountry and natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Drumheller: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Banff National Park: Located about 2.5 hours away, Banff is one of Canada’s most popular national parks and is a must-visit for any traveler.

  • Calgary: Alberta’s largest city is just a little over an hour’s drive from Drumheller.

  • Edmonton: The provincial capital is located about 2.5 hours away from Drumheller and offers numerous attractions for visitors.

Conclusion

Drumheller is a hidden gem in Southern Alberta, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the town’s rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene. With so many must-visit attractions and experiences on offer, Drumheller should be on every traveler’s itinerary.
