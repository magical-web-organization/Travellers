Introduction to Taber

Located in Southern Alberta, Taber is a small but vibrant town known for its agricultural roots, breathtaking scenery, and rich cultural heritage. With a population of just over 8,000 people, Taber is an ideal destination for those seeking a relaxing getaway in a picturesque rural setting. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical sites, embarking on outdoor adventures, or savoring local cuisine, Taber has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Taber

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taber Irrigation Impact Museum: This museum explores the history of irrigation in the Taber area and its impact on the local economy and environment.

  • Taber Heritage Inn: This elegant bed and breakfast is housed in a beautifully restored historic mansion and offers a glimpse into Taber’s past.

  • Chinese Cemetery: This historic site is the final resting place of Chinese immigrants who played a vital role in the development of the Taber area.

Natural Attractions of Taber: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Confederation Park: This spacious park boasts stunning gardens, winding walking paths, and a serene fish pond.

  • Taber Corn Maze: This seasonal attraction features a challenging maze made entirely of corn stalks.

  • Writing-on-Stone Provincial Park: This protected area features stunning geological formations, hiking trails, and ancient rock art.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taber

  • Taber Bike Path: This 7-mile paved trail is ideal for a leisurely bike ride or walking adventure and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • St. Mary’s River: This scenic river offers excellent fishing opportunities and is an ideal spot for a relaxing picnic or leisurely hike.

Best Things to Do in Taber: Activities and Tours

  • Taber Corn Festival: This annual summer festival celebrates the community’s agricultural roots with live music, corn-inspired cuisine, and a festive parade.

  • Taber Pheasant Festival: This fall festival showcases the area’s abundant wildlife and offers hunting opportunities, live music, and delicious local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Taber

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taber Corn: This local delicacy is available fresh throughout the summer and can be enjoyed boiled, grilled, or roasted.

  • Alberta Beef: Taber is surrounded by fertile farmland, making it an ideal destination for meat lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Taber Corn Whiskey: This unique spirit is distilled from locally grown corn and offers a smooth and flavorful taste.

  • Taber Cherry Wine: This fruity and refreshing wine is made from locally grown cherries and is best enjoyed on a warm summer day.

Where to Stay in Taber: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Heritage Inn Hotel & Convention Centre: This modern hotel offers comfortable accommodations, a full-service restaurant, and a relaxing indoor pool.

  • Taber Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms and is located just a short walk from downtown Taber.

  • Westwinds Motor Hotel: This cozy motel features spacious guest rooms, free Wi-Fi, and convenient access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Taber: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Taber Corn: This local staple makes for an excellent souvenir and can be purchased fresh from local farmers during the summer months.

  • Taber Cherry Jelly: This sweet and tangy jelly is made from locally grown cherries and can be found in many of Taber’s specialty shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Taber

Taber has a rich cultural heritage shaped by the contributions of its diverse immigrant communities. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s historic sites and learn more about the area’s many cultural traditions, including the annual Corn Festival and Pheasant Festival.

Best Time to Visit Taber

The best time to visit Taber is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the local farmer’s markets are in full swing. However, outdoor enthusiasts may prefer to visit during the spring or fall, when hiking and biking conditions are optimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taber

Do:

  • Take the time to explore Taber’s historic downtown and its many unique shops and restaurants.

  • Sample local cuisine, such as Taber Corn and Alberta Beef.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s rich cultural heritage and historic sites.

  • Miss the opportunity to explore the area’s many outdoor attractions and natural wonders.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taber

Taber is a safe and welcoming community that welcomes visitors from around the world. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Taber

Taber offers excellent healthcare services, including a full-service hospital and a variety of primary care clinics. Visitors are encouraged to purchase travel insurance and familiarize themselves with local emergency services before embarking on their trip.

Getting to Taber: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taber

The nearest major airport to Taber is the Calgary International Airport, which is approximately a three-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Taber

Greyhound has a station in Taber that serves the southern Alberta area.

How to Get Around in Taber

The best way to get around Taber is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car or bike from several local vendors or opt for a guided tour that includes transportation.

Where to Go Next from Taber: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Waterton Lakes National Park: This stunning national park boasts pristine lakes, rugged mountains, and abundant wildlife.

  • Lethbridge: Nicknamed “The Windy City,” this charming university town is home to a thriving arts scene and a wealth of cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, or just a relaxing getaway, Taber offers something for everyone. With its breathtaking scenery, vibrant cultural scene, and unique culinary heritage, Taber is truly a hidden gem in the heart of Southern Alberta. Plan your visit today and discover all that this wonderful town has to offer!
