Introduction to Big Valley

Big Valley is a small village located in Central Alberta, Canada. It’s known for its historical significance and natural beauty, making it an interesting destination for travelers looking for a unique experience. The village’s history dates back to the late 1800s, when it was founded as a railway town. Today, it’s home to just under 400 residents and attracts visitors throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Big Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main attractions in Big Valley is the Canadian Northern Railway Station, which was built in 1912. The station has been restored to its original state and serves as a museum that displays artifacts and information about the town’s history. Other historical places to visit in Big Valley include:

  • Big Valley Heritage Trail
  • Big Valley School Museum
  • The Prairies Endurance: Red Deer River Crossing Interpretive Centre

Natural Attractions of Big Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Big Valley is home to a variety of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. One of the most popular activities is hiking the nearby trails. The Big Valley Heritage Trail, for instance, is a 19-kilometer trail that takes visitors through scenic views of the prairies and valleys. Other natural attractions include:

  • Jubilee Park, which features a playground, picnic area, and walking trails
  • Badlands Viewpoint, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding badlands
  • Red Deer River, which runs through the village and is perfect for fishing and kayaking

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Valley

Big Valley offers a diverse range of hiking and biking routes, with options for beginners and experienced individuals. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Big Valley Heritage Trail
  • Hemaruka Lake Trail
  • Badlands Trail

Best Things to Do in Big Valley: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the village’s various attractions, there are other activities and tours available that visitors can enjoy. For instance:

  • Railway Excursions: Big Valley’s historic locomotive takes visitors on a scenic tour of the prairies
  • Fishing: Red Deer River offers a great opportunity for catching trout and other fish
  • Golfing: There are several golf courses in the area, such as the Big Valley Golf Course and Coronation Golf Course

Week-Long Itinerary for Big Valley

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Big Valley:

  • Day 1: Visit the Canadian Northern Railway Station and learn about the town’s history at the museum
  • Day 2: Hike the Big Valley Heritage Trail
  • Day 3: Take a railway excursion and explore the surrounded areas
  • Day 4: Go fishing at Red Deer River
  • Day 5: Go golfing at the nearby golf courses
  • Day 6: Explore the natural attractions, such as the Badlands Viewpoint and Jubilee Park
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks

Food and Drink Scene of Big Valley

Must-Try Dishes

Big Valley’s food scene is heavily influenced by its rural location, with plenty of comfort food and local ingredients. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Alberta Beef: Alberta is famous for its beef, so be sure to try a hearty steak
  • Saskatoon Berry Pie: Saskatoon berries are a local delicacy that frequently make an appearance in pies and other pastries
  • Bannock: A classic Indigenous Canadian bread that can be enjoyed as a savory or sweet dish

Local Beverages

Craft beer and local wines are prevalent in Big Valley. Visitors can enjoy a wide variety of handcrafted beers from Two Sergeants Brewing, which features a taproom in the village. They can also check out local wineries such as Field Stone Fruit Wines and Door 53 Winery, which offers a diverse selection of wines made from local fruit.

Where to Stay in Big Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Big Valley offers a few lodging options for visitors, such as:

  • Night Owl Guest House: A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of the village
  • Big Valley Hotel: A historic hotel that features elegant rooms and modern amenities
  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: A chain hotel that offers comfortable accommodations

What to Buy in Big Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring a piece of Big Valley home with them by purchasing local products and souvenirs. Some popular items include:

  • Craft Beer: Visitors can bring back a taste of Big Valley with them by purchasing local craft beer from Two Sergeants Brewing
  • Saskatoon Berry Products: Local shops sell various saskatoon berry jams, syrups, and pies
  • Arts and Crafts: Visitors can find unique handcrafted items at the Big Valley Craft Market

Local Culture and Customs of Big Valley

Big Valley has a rich history and culture that can be explored through its local museums and historical sites. Visitors are encouraged to respect and learn about the local Indigenous culture and the role it has played in the area’s development.

Best Time to Visit Big Valley

The best time to visit Big Valley is during the summer months, from June to August. This time of year offers the best weather for outdoor activities, with average temperatures ranging from 20 to 25 degrees Celsius. Visitors can also enjoy popular events such as the Big Valley Jamboree, which takes place every August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Valley

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the village’s natural and historical attractions
  • Try the local cuisine, particularly Alberta Beef and Saskatoon berries

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings
  • Ignore the village’s history and heritage
  • Disrespect fellow travelers or residents

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Valley

Big Valley is a relatively safe place to visit, with low rates of crime and violence. However, visitors are encouraged to take the usual precautions, such as locking their cars and securing valuables.

Health in Big Valley

Health services in Big Valley are limited, with the nearest hospital located in Stettler, 30 minutes away by car. Visitors are advised to bring any medications or medical supplies they may need during their stay.

Getting to Big Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Big Valley

The closest airport to Big Valley is Edmonton International Airport, located 180 kilometers northeast of the village. From there, visitors can rent a car to reach Big Valley or take a bus to Stettler and then take a taxi or shuttle to the village.

Bus Stations Near Big Valley

Greyhound Canada serves the nearby town of Stettler, which is a 30-minute drive from Big Valley. Visitors can take a taxi or shuttle from Stettler to reach the village.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to reach Big Valley, as it offers the flexibility to explore the surrounding areas at one’s own pace. Visitors can rent a car from Edmonton International Airport or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Big Valley

Big Valley is a small village that can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a car or bicycle to get around.

Where to Go Next from Big Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Big Valley is located in a region that offers various opportunities for travel. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Drumheller: A short day trip away, Drumheller offers unique attractions such as the Royal Tyrrell Museum and the Hoodoos.
  • Banff National Park: One of Canada’s most popular tourist destinations, Banff is a four-hour drive from Big Valley and features stunning views of the Rocky Mountains.
  • Calgary: A bustling metropolitan city a two-and-a-half-hour drive away, Calgary offers a variety of attractions such as the Calgary Zoo and Heritage Park Historical Village.

Conclusion

Big Valley is a charming village that offers a unique glimpse into the history and culture of Central Alberta. Whether visitors are seeking outdoor adventures, exploration of historical sites or indulging in local cuisine, Big Valley has something to offer for every type of traveler.
