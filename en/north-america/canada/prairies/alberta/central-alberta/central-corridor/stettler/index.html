Stettler Travel Guide: Discovering the Central Alberta Gem

Located in Central Corridor, in Central Alberta, Stettler is a charming town that attracts visitors with its rich history and beautiful natural scenery. From hiking and biking trails to museums and cultural landmarks, Stettler offers something for everyone. If you’re planning a trip to this part of Alberta, we’ve got you covered with this comprehensive travel guide to Stettler.

List of Places to Visit in Stettler

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stettler is home to several museums and historical landmarks that give an insight into the town’s heritage and culture. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Stettler:

  • Alberta Prairie Steam Tours: Take a ride on a steam train through the scenic countryside of Alberta.
  • Stettler Town and County Museum: This museum showcases the history of Stettler and the surrounding area through exhibits of artifacts, photos, and documents.
  • The Stettler Train Station: A beautifully restored railway station that now houses a museum showcasing the town’s connection to the railway industry.
  • Westwoods Pioneer Museum: A collection of over 8000 artifacts, including antique cars and farming equipment.

Natural Attractions of Stettler: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are a nature lover, you’ll be amazed by the breathtaking natural scenery that surrounds Stettler. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Buffalo Lake: A popular spot for boating, fishing, and swimming that also offers stunning views.
  • Red Deer River: This river is perfect for kayaking, canoeing, or just relaxing with its scenic landscapes.
  • Paradise Shores RV Resort: A great place to enjoy the outdoors with plenty of amenities such as a beach and playground.
  • Big Valley Creation Science Museum: Learn about the area’s geology and natural history with interactive displays.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stettler

Stettler is a paradise for hikers and bikers alike, with a variety of trails that cater to all skill levels. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • The Great Trail: This 24,000 km trail runs through Stettler and offers stunning views of the natural beauty of the area.
  • Stettler Town and Country Trails: With over 50-km of trails, everyone can find a perfect route for themselves.
  • Buffalo Lake Recreation Area: A great place to explore with hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of wildlife and nature.

Best Things to Do in Stettler: Activities and Tours

Looking for something fun to do in Stettler? Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  • Alberta Prairie Steam Tours: Take a train tour of the area.
  • The Stettler Art Walk: Visit the art galleries and studios throughout the town.
  • The Stettler Variety Showcase: A live performance series that showcases local talent and performers from around the world.
  • Paradise Shores RV Resort: Rent a paddleboat or kayak, go fishing, or take a dip in the lake on a hot day.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stettler

Day 1: Arrive and explore downtown Stettler’s historic sites, restaurants, and shops. Day 2: Hike or bike the Stettler Town and Country Trails or enjoy a day at the Buffalo Lake Recreation Area. Day 3: Visit the Stettler Town and County Museum and The Stettler Train Station. Day 4: Take a tour on the Alberta Prairie Steam Train, a trip through scenic countryside. Day 5: Explore the Red Deer River or visit the Westwoods Pioneer Museum. Day 6: Enjoy a day at Paradise Shores RV Resort, renting a paddleboat or kayak, going fishing, or swimming in the lake. Day 7: Spend the day at the Big Valley Creation Science Museum and finish the day off with a stroll through the art galleries on Stettler Art Walk.

Food and Drink Scene of Stettler

Stettler has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for visitors to taste the local cuisine or relax with a drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Stettler:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta Beef: Alberta is known for its beef, and Stettler is no exception with several restaurants serving a perfectly cooked steak.
  • Wild Game: Sample the wild game which includes moose, elk, and bison that are abundant in the area.
  • Prairie Oysters: Also known as Rocky Mountain Oysters, a traditional delicacy in which the testicles of bulls are breaded and fried.

Local Beverages

  • Grain Bin Brewery: A new brewery featuring rotating beer selection that includes American Pale Ale, Red Ale and Lager.
  • Heartland Cafe: A popular spot for coffee lovers with all kinds of delicious drinks, from lattes to cappuccinos.

Where to Stay in Stettler: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From rustic guesthouses to luxurious hotels, Stettler offers a wide range of accommodations that suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top options for all budgets:

  • Canalta Stettler: A comfortable and affordable hotel featuring an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Paradise Shores RV Resort: An “Oasis” of recreational grandeur with a beach, playground, and water area.
  • Waverly Hotel: A historic and unique hotel located in the heart of downtown Stettler.

What to Buy in Stettler: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some perfect souvenirs or local products to bring home after your visit to Stettler? Here’s what you should look for:

  • Quilts and Handmade Goods made by Amish Communities
  • Local honey made by bees of the area, available at the numerous agricultural antique shops.
  • Original Art Pieces by Local Artists from Stettler Art Walk.

Local Culture and Customs of Stettler

Stettler is a town that cherishes its history and community values. The Western-themed history combined with the natural beauty of the area makes for a must-visit destination for anyone who loves a good story. Visitors to Stettler are greeted with warm, friendly locals who take pride in their small-town values and community spirit.

Best Time to Visit Stettler

The best time to visit Stettler is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny and festivals are in full swing. Fall and spring can be equally enjoyable, with fewer crowds, and the magnificent colors of a Canadian autumn.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stettler

Do:

  • Respect the local community and their values
  • Take care of the natural environment and wildlife
  • Experience Western culture by attending rodeos or visiting nearby ranches.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural ecosystem in any way
  • engage in activities that may disturb the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Disrespect any other culture or belief around.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stettler

Stettler is a relatively safe town and crime rates are low. Still, it is advisable to take standard safety precautions, lock your car and don’t leave valuable things visible. Avoid hitchhiking and wandering alone in isolated places at night.

Health in Stettler

Stettler does not have a hospital or medical center within its limits; the nearest hospital is located in Red Deer, 72 kilometers away. Visitors are encouraged to carry their medicine and first aid kits in case of an emergency.

Getting to Stettler: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Stettler by land or air transportation, options include:

  • By Car: Stettler is approximately 75 minutes East of Red Deer, with most of the route served by highways.
  • By bus: Red Arrow buses offer regular services direct to Stettler from Calgary and Edmonton.
  • By Air: The nearest commercial airports are located in Edmonton (190 km) and Calgary (250 km).

How to Get Around in Stettler

Stettler is a small town, and you can easily walk or cycle to most attractions. A car is quite handy if you plan on traveling to more remote areas. Local taxis and bike rentals are available if you prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Stettler: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stettler is a perfect destination for day trips or as a base for exploring surrounding areas such as:

  • Banff National Park: This must-visit park is located around 300 km away from Stettler and offers stunning natural beauty with hiking trails, skiing, and hot springs.
  • Jasper National Park: Located around 530 km away, Jasper National Park is an excellent destination for anyone looking to explore the Canadian Rockies and sightsee natural wonders such as glaciers and canyons.
  • Calgary: Calgary is one of the main cities in Alberta, located approximately 190 km from Stettler, offering a vibrant cultural scene, attractions, and sports events year-round.

Conclusion

Stettler is a hidden gem located in the heart of Central Alberta, with its Western culture, rich history, and picturesque scenery. With something for everyone, Stettler is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Alberta. Remember to respect and enjoy the hospitality of the locals and immerse yourself in Western culture by visiting museums, attending rodeos, and exploring the beautiful natural surroundings. We hope this travel guide has been helpful to plan your trip to Stettler.
