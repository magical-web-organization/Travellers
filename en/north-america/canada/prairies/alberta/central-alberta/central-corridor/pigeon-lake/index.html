Introduction to Pigeon Lake

Pigeon Lake is a beautiful lake located in Central Corridor, in Central Alberta, Canada. Known for its crystal clear waters and lush greenery, Pigeon Lake attracts visitors from all over the world. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Pigeon Lake, with information on the best things to do, places to visit, and local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Pigeon Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pigeon Lake Museum

The Pigeon Lake Museum is a must-visit attraction in Pigeon Lake for history buffs. The museum features exhibits on local history, including the lives of early settlers in the area. Visitors can learn about the development of the lake and how it became an important part of the community.

Battle River Railway

The Battle River Railway is a historic railway station that has been restored to its former glory. Visitors can take a tour of the station and learn about the railway’s rich history. The station also hosts events and activities throughout the year, including train rides and concerts.

Natural Attractions of Pigeon Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pigeon Lake Provincial Park

Pigeon Lake Provincial Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park features hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and picnic areas. Visitors can also rent boats and explore the lake’s many islands and coves.

Pigeon Lake Escarpment

The Pigeon Lake Escarpment is a natural wonder that stretches along the eastern shore of the lake. The escarpment features rocky cliffs and stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscapes. Visitors can hike along the escarpment and take in the breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pigeon Lake

Pigeon Lake Trail

The Pigeon Lake Trail is a 10-kilometer trail that circles the entire lake. The trail is suitable for hiking and biking, and offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscapes. There are several picnic areas along the trail where visitors can stop and enjoy the scenery.

Kiskayo Trail

The Kiskayo Trail is a 12-kilometer trail that winds through Pigeon Lake Provincial Park. The trail is suitable for hiking and biking, and features beautiful scenery, including forests, wetlands, and grasslands. Visitors can also see wildlife along the way, such as moose, deer, and beavers.

Best Things to Do in Pigeon Lake: Activities and Tours

Lake Tours

One of the best ways to experience Pigeon Lake is by taking a tour of the lake. There are several companies that offer boat tours of the lake, including sightseeing tours and fishing charters. Visitors can also rent boats and explore the lake on their own.

Golfing

Pigeon Lake Golf Course is a beautiful 27-hole golf course located near the lake. The course features stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscapes, and is suitable for golfers of all skill levels. Visitors can also enjoy the course’s clubhouse, which offers food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Pigeon Lake

Must-Try Dishes

Pickerel

Pickerel is a popular local dish in Pigeon Lake, made from the lake’s freshwater fish. Visitors can enjoy pickerel at local restaurants and cafes, prepared in a variety of ways, including fried and grilled.

Saskatoon Berry Pie

Saskatoon berry pie is a delicious local dessert, made from the berries that grow in the area. Visitors can enjoy a slice of pie at local bakeries and cafes.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

Pigeon Lake is home to several craft breweries, producing a variety of delicious beers. Visitors can enjoy a cold beer at local pubs and breweries.

Berry Wine

Berry wine is a local specialty in Pigeon Lake, made from the region’s saskatoon berries. Visitors can sample berry wine at local wineries and vineyards.

Where to Stay in Pigeon Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pigeon Lake Resort

Pigeon Lake Resort is a beautiful lakeside resort that offers a wide range of accommodations, including cabins, RV sites, and tent sites. The resort also features a restaurant, lounge, and boat rentals.

Village Creek Country Inn

Village Creek Country Inn is a charming guesthouse located near Pigeon Lake. The Inn features cozy rooms and a beautiful garden, and offers a delicious breakfast in the morning.

What to Buy in Pigeon Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saskatoon Berry Jam

Saskatoon berry jam is a delicious local product that makes a perfect souvenir or gift. Visitors can find saskatoon berry jam at local stores and markets.

Handmade Crafts

Pigeon Lake is home to many talented artisans, producing handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings. Visitors can find these unique items at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Pigeon Lake

Pigeon Lake is home to a vibrant local culture, with strong ties to the land and water. Visitors can experience this culture through local events and activities, such as powwows, farmers markets, and fishing tournaments.

Best Time to Visit Pigeon Lake

The best time to visit Pigeon Lake is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is warmest and the lake is busiest. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities during this time, including hiking, biking, and boating.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pigeon Lake

Pigeon Lake is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and good infrastructure. However, visitors should still take basic precautions, such as locking their vehicles and securing their belongings.

Health in Pigeon Lake

Pigeon Lake is a healthy destination, with clean drinking water and good medical facilities nearby. Visitors should take basic precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, and staying hydrated during outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Pigeon Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Edmonton is a vibrant city located about 100 kilometers north of Pigeon Lake. The city features a variety of attractions, including museums, restaurants, and shopping.
