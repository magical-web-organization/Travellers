Welcome to Bentley, a charming little town located in the heart of Central Alberta, Canada. Whether you’re looking to explore the area’s rich cultural heritage, take on some outdoor adventures, or simply soak up the small-town ambiance, Bentley has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the top places to visit, dine, stay, and get active in Bentley. From must-see cultural attractions to the best hiking and biking routes, we’ll give you all the insider tips and tricks to make the most of your time in this lovely Alberta gem.

Introduction to Bentley

Bentley is a small town with a population of around 1,000 people. Founded in the early 20th century, it was initially a farming and ranching community, but has since grown to become a popular tourist destination.

Despite its small size, Bentley boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and plenty of outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, an adrenaline junkie, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Bentley has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bentley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bentley Museum - Explore the town’s history and heritage at this charming local museum. You’ll find exhibits on agriculture, local businesses, and early pioneer life.
  • Lacombe Blacksmith Shop Museum - Located just a short drive from Bentley, this museum showcases the art of blacksmithing, a trade that was once essential to the area’s early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Bentley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gull Lake - This picturesque lake is a favorite spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. Take a stroll along the shoreline and enjoy the stunning sunset views.
  • Bentley Ski Hill - Enjoy some winter fun at this local ski hill, which features several runs for skiers of all levels. Equipment rentals and lessons are available.
  • Kristiansand Regional Park - Located just a few kilometers from town, this park offers hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bentley

  • Pigeon Lake Provincial Park Trail - This beautiful trail extends over 7 km and offers stunning views of Pigeon Lake. It’s an easy hike that’s suitable for all skill levels.
  • Chickadee Trail - This 2.8 km trail winds through the forest and is a popular spot for birdwatching. You may even spot a few chickadees, which often eat from visitors’ hands!
  • Wolf Creek Trail - This challenging 22 km trail is perfect for experienced hikers looking for a workout. It takes you through forests, meadows, and wetlands, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Bentley: Activities and Tours

  • Bentley Farmer’s Market - Visit this thriving local market and sample fresh produce, baked goods, and artisan products.
  • Bentley Corn Maze - Get lost in this family-friendly corn maze, which offers fun activities for kids of all ages.
  • Historic Walking Tour - Take a self-guided tour of Bentley and explore the town’s historic buildings and landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bentley

Day 1: Arrive in Bentley and explore the town’s charming downtown area.

Day 2: Take a hike at Kristiansand Regional Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Visit the Lacombe Blacksmith Shop Museum and learn about the art of blacksmithing.

Day 4: Fish or boat on Gull Lake, or simply relax on the beach.

Day 5: Rent a bike and take a ride on the Chickadee Trail.

Day 6: Explore the local food scene and sample some of Bentley’s best dishes.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Red Deer and check out some of the city’s most popular attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Bentley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta beef - Alberta is known for its high-quality beef, and you’ll find plenty of delicious steak dishes in Bentley.
  • Wild game - If you’re feeling adventurous, try some of the local wild game, such as elk or bison.
  • Pierogies - This classic Ukrainian dish is a popular staple in many local restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Alberta craft beer - Alberta has a thriving craft beer scene, and you’ll find plenty of great local breweries in Bentley and the surrounding area.
  • Bentley Berry Juice - This sweet and refreshing juice is made from local raspberries and is a must-try for anyone visiting Bentley.
  • Canadian whisky - If you’re a whisky lover, be sure to try some of Canada’s famous whiskies, such as Crown Royal or Canadian Club.

Where to Stay in Bentley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bentley Hotel - This charming historic hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a lounge.
  • Bentley Motel - This affordable motel features clean, basic rooms and is located just steps from the town’s main attractions.
  • Bentley Bed and Breakfast - This cozy B&B offers comfortable rooms and a delicious homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Bentley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bentley Berry Jam - Made from local raspberries, this sweet and tangy jam makes a great souvenir or gift.
  • Handcrafted pottery - Many local artists create beautiful pottery pieces that make unique and stunning gifts.
  • Alberta beef jerky - If you’re a jerky lover, you can’t leave Alberta without trying some of the local beef jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Bentley

  • Farming and ranching - Agriculture has always been a significant part of Bentley’s local culture, and many locals still work in farming and ranching today.
  • Small-town hospitality - Visitors to Bentley often note that locals are incredibly friendly and welcoming, making it easy to feel at home in this charming town.

Best Time to Visit Bentley

The best time to visit Bentley depends on your interests. If you’re a winter sports enthusiast, visiting during the winter months is ideal when you can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and ice fishing.

For those who prefer warmer weather, visiting between June and August is best for enjoying the outdoors, swimming at Gull Lake, and attending local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bentley

Do:

  • Do immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions.
  • Do be respectful of the local wildlife.
  • Do try some of the local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in natural areas.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t feed wildlife, as it can be harmful to their health and safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bentley

Bentley is a safe and friendly town, but it’s always essential to take regular safety precautions, such as locking up valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Bentley

Bentley has standard healthcare facilities. If you require urgent medical attention, please dial 911.

Getting to Bentley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bentley

The nearest major airport to Bentley is Edmonton International Airport, approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Bentley

Greyhound no longer operates in Western Canada. But there are local shuttle services out of Red Deer.

Other Options

Car rentals are available out of Red Deer.

How To Get Around in Bentley

The best way to get around Bentley is by car, although cycling is also a great option for exploring the town and surrounding area. Walking and hiking trails are abundant in Bentley.

Where to Go Next from Bentley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Red Deer - The nearest city, located approximately 40 km to the east of Bentley. Many travelers head here to enjoy the shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Sylvan Lake - This nearby lake is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and other water-based activities.
  • Banff National Park - This world-famous national park is located approximately three hours away from Bentley and is worth the journey for its stunning natural beauty and abundant wildlife.

Conclusion

Bentley is a charming and inviting town that offers a little something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to get active in the great outdoors, immerse yourself in the local culture, or simply relax and unwind, Bentley has everything you need to enjoy a memorable and rewarding trip to Central Alberta.
