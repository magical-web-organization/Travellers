Introduction to Wainwright

Wainwright is a town in east-central Alberta, known for its rich history and stunning natural scenery. Established in the late 1800s as a settlement along the Canadian Pacific Railway, the town has grown into a thriving community with a population of over 6,000.

Visitors to Wainwright can explore a range of cultural and natural attractions, including museums, parks, and hiking trails. The town also boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with local restaurants and breweries serving up delicious fare.

List of Places to Visit in Wainwright

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wainwright Rail Park - This community park features a restored Canadian Pacific Railway caboose, as well as a playground, picnic area, and walking trails.
  • Wainwright Museum - Learn about the history of Wainwright and the surrounding area at this fascinating local museum, which features exhibits on everything from early settlers to the oil and gas industry.
  • Battle River Railway Trestle - This historic railway trestle spans the scenic Battle River, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Wainwright: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bison Viewing Area - Get up close with these majestic creatures at this unique attraction, which features a herd of bison roaming across a natural prairie landscape.
  • Wainwright Wildlife Reserve - This beautiful park offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and fishing.
  • Saskatchewan River Breaks - Just a short drive from Wainwright, this stunning natural area features dramatic rock formations and breathtaking views of the Saskatchewan River valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wainwright

  • Iron Horse Trail - This 300-km trail runs through Wainwright and offers a range of terrain for both hiking and biking, including flat prairie landscapes and forested areas.
  • Wainwright Rail Trail - This easy, scenic trail runs along the former Canadian Pacific Railway line, offering views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Wainwright: Activities and Tours

  • Wainwright Stampede - This annual rodeo and exhibition is a highlight of the Wainwright calendar, featuring exciting events like bull riding, barrel racing, and chuckwagon races.
  • Wainwright Farmers’ Market - Held every Saturday during the summer, this bustling market features fresh local produce, handmade crafts, and live music.
  • Painted Warriors - Get a taste of Indigenous culture with a range of guided tours and outdoor activities, including horseback riding and archery.

Food and Drink Scene of Wainwright

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison burgers - Try this local specialty at restaurants like The Stables Grill or Daddy’z Kitchen.
  • Perogies - Enjoy a hearty Ukrainian meal at Baba’s Kitchen, featuring homemade perogies and other authentic dishes.
  • Alberta beef - Savor the flavor of premium Alberta beef at spots like the Wainwright Hotel and Grill.

Local Beverages

  • Ribstone Creek Brewery - Sample a range of locally-brewed beers at this popular brewery, known for its award-winning offerings like Old Man Winter Porter and Lonely Wolf IPA.
  • Joker’s Den - This cozy pub features live music and a wide range of beers, along with a menu of classic pub fare.

Where to Stay in Wainwright: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ramada by Wyndham Wainwright - This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, along with amenities like an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Jinglepot Guest House - This charming bed and breakfast features cozy rooms, a beautiful garden, and a delicious homemade breakfast each morning.
  • Wainwright Hotel and Grill - This historic hotel has been a Wainwright landmark since 1913, offering comfortable rooms and a popular restaurant and bar.

What to Buy in Wainwright: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wainwright Railway souvenirs - Pick up a t-shirt, hat, or other souvenir featuring the town’s historic rail roots.
  • Saskatchewan River Breaks postcards - Send a scenic reminder of your trip to family and friends back home.
  • Local honey - Taste the sweetness of Wainwright with a jar of delicious honey from a local apiary.

Local Culture and Customs of Wainwright

Wainwright has a strong sense of community pride, with residents often participating in events like the Wainwright Stampede or volunteering at local museums and parks. Visitors should be respectful of this community spirit when exploring the town and its attractions.

Best Time to Visit Wainwright

The best time to visit Wainwright is during the summer months (June-August), when temperatures are mild and outdoor activities like hiking and wildlife viewing are at their peak. The Wainwright Stampede, held in late June, is a particularly lively time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wainwright

Do:

  • Take a hike - Wainwright offers some of Alberta’s most picturesque hiking trails, so take advantage of the opportunity to explore nature.
  • Try local cuisine - Wainwright’s food and drink scene is a delicious reflection of the town’s history and culture, so indulge in some Alberta beef or bison burgers.
  • Be respectful of local customs - Wainwright residents take pride in their town and its history, so be respectful when exploring museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places - Wainwright has strict smoking regulations, so be aware of designated smoking areas.
  • Litter - Keep Wainwright’s natural areas beautiful by properly disposing of trash and recycling.
  • Ignore local wildlife regulations - Wainwright’s wildlife areas are carefully managed to ensure the safety of both animals and visitors, so follow all posted regulations and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wainwright

Wainwright is a generally safe town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions like locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight. In the event of an emergency, dial 911.

Health in Wainwright

There are several medical and dental clinics throughout Wainwright, as well as a hospital with emergency services. Visitors should ensure they have proper health insurance coverage before traveling to Wainwright.

Getting to Wainwright: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • By car - Wainwright is located along Highway 14, approximately 2.5 hours east of Edmonton and 3 hours southeast of Lloydminster.
  • By bus - Greyhound Canada offers bus service to Wainwright from several locations throughout Alberta and Saskatchewan.
  • By air - The nearest airports to Wainwright are located in Edmonton (approximately 2.5 hours away by car) and Lloydminster (approximately 1 hour away by car).

How to Get Around in Wainwright

Wainwright’s compact size makes it easy to explore on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car from a local rental agency to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Wainwright: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Edmonton - Alberta’s capital city is home to a range of cultural attractions and outdoor activities, including the West Edmonton Mall and the North Saskatchewan River Valley.
  • Lloydminster - This unique city straddles the border between Alberta and Saskatchewan, offering a range of attractions like the Lloydminster Cultural and Science Centre and the Barr Colony Heritage Cultural Centre.
  • Saskatoon - This vibrant city in neighboring Saskatchewan offers a range of year-round festivals and events, as well as outdoor adventures like hiking and canoeing along the South Saskatchewan River.

Conclusion

Wainwright is a hidden gem in Alberta, offering a range of cultural and natural attractions for visitors to explore. From hiking and wildlife viewing to local cuisine and historic sites, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. So pack your bags and start planning your trip to Wainwright today!
