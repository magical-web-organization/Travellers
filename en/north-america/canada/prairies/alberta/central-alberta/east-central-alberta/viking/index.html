Introduction to Viking

Nestled in the heart of East Central Alberta lies the quaint town of Viking. Known for its rich history, outdoor recreational opportunities, and unique local culture, Viking is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Viking has something to offer everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Viking.

List of Places to Visit in Viking

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit places in Viking is the Viking Historical Museum. This small museum has a vast collection of artifacts and displays that showcase the history of the town. The exhibits include vintage cars, farm machinery, traditional clothing, and household items that give a glimpse of Viking’s past.

Another cultural attraction worth visiting is the Viking Carena Complex. This facility offers a variety of activities, including ice skating, curling, and ice hockey. They have a well-maintained ice rink that hosts local games and tournaments throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Viking: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor lovers should visit the Coal Lake Recreation Area, a popular spot for fishing, boating, and water sports. Situated just a short drive from Viking, the lake boasts abundant fish species such as walleye, pike, and yellow perch. Besides fishing, you can explore the beautiful trails that surround the lake, such as the Trans-Canada Trail, which extends through the town.

Additionally, take a scenic drive along the Highway 619, which connects the town to the neighboring communities. It is a picturesque route that passes through miles of rolling hills, open prairies, and natural reserves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Viking

The Trans-Canada Trail, Viking’s most popular hiking and biking trail, stretches over 24 km and follows the old railroad bed. The trail takes you through the heart of Viking, offering stunning views of the town, farms, and rolling hills. Moreover, the trail is suitable for all ages and fitness levels with many stopping points along the way.

Best Things to Do in Viking: Activities and Tours

Viking hosts several annual events that are popular among locals and tourists. One of such events is the Viking Air Show, held every July. This show features aerobatic performances, vintage aircraft displays, and exciting skydiving performances.

For those interested in farming, visit the U of A Augustana Farm. This working farm is actively involved in providing education to farmers and the community about sustainable agriculture practices.

Week-Long Itinerary for Viking

Day 1: Arrive in Viking and explore the town’s cultural attractions, such as the Viking Historical Museum and the Viking Carena Complex. Day 2: Spend the day at Coal Lake Recreation Area, fishing, and hiking on the Trans-Canada Trail. Day 3: Have fun at U of A Augustana Farm, learning about farming techniques and sustainable practices. Day 4: Go golfing at the Viking Golf Course, which has a challenging 9-hole course. Day 5: Take a day trip to the charming neighboring towns of Killam and Forestburg. Day 6: Visit a winery in the Camrose area, try some local wines and enjoy the beautiful countryside scenery. Day 7: Depart Viking.

Food and Drink Scene of Viking

Must-Try Dishes

When in Viking, try the town’s specialties such as beef and pork dishes. The Farmers’ Pantry is a local favorite and is well known for their homemade sausages, gravies, and pies.

Local Beverages

For a refreshing drink, visit the Viking Farmer’s Market where you can buy freshly squeezed juices made with local produce. Besides, you can sample different brews at the Hammerhead Brew Pub, which has an extensive collection of locally crafted beers.

Where to Stay in Viking: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Viking has a limited number of places to stay, but visitors can still choose from a selection of comfortable accommodations. Some of the popular places to stay include the Viking Lamplighter Motel, B&B lodge, or the Viking Hotel.

What to Buy in Viking: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Viking, make sure to get some homemade jams, honey, and syrups. These products are available at most local markets and provide the perfect edible souvenir for your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Viking

The people of Viking are warm and welcoming, and like any other small town, everyone knows everyone else. It’s essential to respect the locals’ way of life and follow their customs.

Best Time to Visit Viking

The best time to visit Viking is in the summer months, from June to August. The weather is mild, and you can explore the town’s plenty of outdoor recreational activities without the interruption of cold weather or snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Viking

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take part in the annual Viking Air Show, which takes place every July.
  • Explore the town’s history at the Viking Historical Museum.
  • Try the local beef and pork dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the hiking trails and outdoor spaces.
  • Ignore the speed limits in and around town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Viking

Viking is generally a safe place to visit and has low crime rates. However, it’s crucial to take standard safety precautions, such as not leaving your belongings unattended and locking your car doors.

Health in Viking

Visitors to Viking should ensure they carry necessary medication for allergies and other health conditions. Medical facilities in Viking include the Lamont Health Care Centre and the Daysland Health Centre.

Getting to Viking: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Viking

The closest major airport to Viking is Edmonton International Airport, which is approximately 1 hour and 45 minutes from the town.

Bus Stations Near Viking

Greyhound Canada has multiple bus lines that stop in Camrose, which is about a 35-minute drive from Viking. Buses connect Camrose to other significant cities in the province, such as Edmonton and Calgary.

Other Options

Car rentals are an option if you prefer to drive to Viking. The town is located along Highway 14, which connects Edmonton to east-central Alberta.

How to Get Around in Viking

Most destinations in the town are within walking or biking distance, and several companies offer car rental services.

Where to Go Next from Viking: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Viking’s proximity to several towns and cities makes it an ideal starting point for visiting other destinations. Lake Louise, Jasper National Park, and Calgary are popular destinations among visitors who make Viking their base during their travels.

Conclusion

Viking is a charming town that has something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring local history and culture, outdoor recreation, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Viking has it all. Follow our comprehensive travel guide to Viking, and you won’t be disappointed.
