Introduction to Foothills

Nestled in the heart of Central Alberta, Foothills is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Characterized by rolling hills, vast prairies, and snow-capped mountain peaks, Foothills attracts visitors year-round with its scenic beauty and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adrenaline-fueled vacation, Foothills has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Foothills

Foothills is home to several culturally important attractions, natural landmarks, and excellent hiking/biking trails. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Foothills:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Leighton Art Center: The Leighton Art Center is a unique place where visitors can learn about local art and culture. They offer various art programs, workshops and exhibitions throughout the year.

  • Bar U Ranch National Historic Site: Bar U Ranch is a preserved 19th-century cattle ranch that offers a glimpse into the region’s ranching history. Visitors can explore the ranch house, stables, and various outbuildings.

  • Heritage Park Historical Village: Visitors can experience what life was like in the past at the Heritage Park Historical Village. The park features over 180 exhibits, including antique vehicles, steamboats, and historical buildings.

Natural Attractions of Foothills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kananaskis Country: Kananaskis Country is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts. The region offers hiking, camping, fishing, and mountain biking. Visitors can also take scenic drives through the mountains.

  • Fish Creek Provincial Park: Fish Creek Provincial Park is a beautiful park that offers a break from the hustle and bustle of the city. The park has over 80 km of hiking and biking trails and is home to a diverse range of wildlife.

  • Sheep River Provincial Park: Sheep River Provincial Park is a picturesque wilderness area with several boat launches, picnic sites, and campgrounds. Visitors can hike the park’s many trails, fish in the river, or simply relax by the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Foothills

Foothills is a haven for nature lovers, and there are several hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Elbow Valley Trail: This trail follows the scenic Elbow River and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Powderface Trail: Powderface Trail is a popular mountain biking trail that winds through dense forests and rocky terrain.

  • Mist Mountain Trail: Mist Mountain Trail is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with panoramic views of the mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Foothills: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities in Foothills. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • White Water Rafting: Take a thrilling ride down the Bow River’s rapids with a white water rafting tour.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the stunning landscapes of Foothills on horseback and take in the beauty of the region at a leisurely pace.

  • Helicopter Tours: Experience Foothills from above with a helicopter tour that takes you to the top of the mountains for breathtaking views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Foothills

If you plan on spending a week in Foothills, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Leighton Art Center and explore the region’s art and culture.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive through Kananaskis Country and stop at the various viewpoints along the way.

Day 3: Spend the day hiking the Mist Mountain Trail and taking in the spectacular views.

Day 4: Go white-water rafting down the Bow River.

Day 5: Explore Fish Creek Provincial Park and enjoy a relaxing picnic.

Day 6: Go horseback riding and take in the beautiful landscapes of Foothills.

Day 7: Visit Bar U Ranch National Historic Site and learn about the region’s ranching history.

Food and Drink Scene of Foothills

Foothills has a rich food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alberta Beef: Alberta is known for its high-quality beef, and Foothills offers plenty of opportunities to try some of the best cuts.

  • Saskatoon Berry Pie: Saskatoon berries are a local specialty and make for a delicious pie.

  • Wild Mushroom Risotto: Foothills is home to an array of wild mushrooms, which make for a tasty risotto.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Foothills has several craft breweries that offer a variety of beers, from light lagers to hoppy IPAs.

  • Alberta Wines: The region’s vineyards produce several award-winning wines made from local grapes.

Where to Stay in Foothills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Foothills has numerous accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Delta Hotels by Marriott Kananaskis Lodge: This luxurious lodge is set in the stunning Kananaskis Valley and is perfect for those looking to relax and unwind.

  • HI-Kananaskis Wilderness Hostel: This hostel is a budget-friendly option for those who want to experience the wilderness of Foothills.

  • Cochrane Rockyview Hotel: The Cochrane Rockyview Hotel is a cozy and comfortable hotel in the heart of Cochrane.

What to Buy in Foothills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Foothills is home to several local artisans and craftspeople who create unique handmade goods. Here are some suggestions for souvenirs:

  • Pottery from the Leighton Art Center: The Leighton Art Center is home to several talented potters who create beautiful and functional pieces.

  • Alberta Beef Jerky: Take home a taste of Foothills with some locally sourced beef jerky.

  • Handmade Soaps: Foothills has several soap makers who create all-natural, handmade soaps using local ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Foothills

Foothills is a region with a rich cultural heritage. It is home to several Indigenous communities, and their customs and traditions are an important part of the region’s history. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the region’s culture and customs and show respect and appreciation for the local communities.

Best Time to Visit Foothills

The best time to visit Foothills is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the region’s beauty is year-round, and visitors can enjoy winter activities such as skiing and snowshoeing in the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Foothills

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Foothills:

Do:

  • Respect the local communities and their customs.

  • Take care of the natural environment and leave no trace.

  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.

  • Damage or disturb the local flora and fauna.

  • Leave litter on the trails or in the parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Foothills

Foothills is generally a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of possible hazards such as wildlife encounters, flash floods, and adverse weather conditions. Always follow safety guidelines and take appropriate precautions when exploring the region’s natural areas.

Health in Foothills

Foothills has several medical facilities and hospitals that offer basic medical care. It is recommended that visitors bring any necessary medications and travel health insurance.

Getting to Foothills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transport options and logistics to consider when traveling to Foothills:

Airports Near Foothills

  • Calgary International Airport: The Calgary International Airport is the closest major airport to Foothills.

Bus Stations Near Foothills

  • Greyhound Canada: Greyhound Canada operates bus services to various locations in Foothills.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Car rentals are available from the Calgary International Airport and other locations in the region.

How to Get Around in Foothills

The best way to get around Foothills is by car. Several car rental companies operate in the region. Alternatively, visitors can explore the region on foot or by bike using the many trails and pathways.

Where to Go Next from Foothills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Foothills is located in close proximity to several other exciting destinations and attractions. Here are some suggestions:

  • Banff National Park: Banff National Park is located just west of Foothills and offers some of the most stunning mountain scenery in Canada.

  • Calgary: Calgary is located just east of Foothills and is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city with plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Foothills is a breathtaking region that offers visitors a mix of natural beauty and cultural history. Visitors can spend their days hiking, biking, and exploring amidst stunning surroundings, and their nights enjoying local food and drink and relaxing in cozy accommodation. Whether you have a week or just a weekend, Foothills is a must-visit destination for all nature lovers and adventure seekers.
