Rocky Mountain House is a hidden gem in Foothills, in central Alberta, in the Prairies of Canada. Surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, this town is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. From hiking and biking routes to scenic drives and natural attractions, Rocky Mountain House has something for everyone.

Introduction to Rocky Mountain House

Rocky Mountain House is a small town in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains. The town is located in central Alberta and is home to a population of just over 6,000 people. The town is named after the nearby Rocky Mountains and has a rich history of exploration and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Rocky Mountain House

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rocky Mountain House has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places that you can visit to learn more about the town’s past. The Rocky Mountain House National Historic Site is a must-visit for history buffs, as it was once a vital trade route for First Nations, fur traders, and explorers.

Another popular attraction is the Rocky Mountain House Museum, which showcases the town’s history through exhibits and artifacts. You can also visit the David Thompson Memorial, which pays tribute to the famous explorer who helped map Western Canada.

Natural Attractions of Rocky Mountain House: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rocky Mountain House is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to discover. Head to the Crimson Lake Provincial Park, where you can hike, bike, paddle, fish, or simply soak in the stunning views. If you’re a wildlife enthusiast, you can spot the local beavers, ospreys, and bears in their natural habitat.

The Bighorn Backcountry is another popular destination among hikers, backpackers, and nature lovers. You can explore the vast wilderness via trails that range from easy to advanced difficulty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rocky Mountain House

Rocky Mountain House is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. The town has several trails of varying difficulty levels that offer stunning views of the surrounding natural beauty.

Some of the popular trails include Coliseum Mountain Trail, Prairie Creek Trail, and Siffleur Falls Trail. While hiking, make sure to follow the safety guidelines, take plenty of water, and wear appropriate shoes and clothing.

Best Things to Do in Rocky Mountain House: Activities and Tours

Rocky Mountain House offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to different interests and age groups. For thrill-seekers, there’s the White Water Rafting on the Red Deer River, where you can experience rapids ranging from Class II to Class IV.

For a more laid-back activity, embark on a scenic horseback ride at the Circle Square Ranch. You can also take an ATV tour, go fishing, or try your hand at geocaching.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rocky Mountain House

If you’re planning to spend a week in Rocky Mountain House, here’s an itinerary that includes the best places to visit and things to do:

Day 1: Visit the Rocky Mountain House National Historic Site and the Rocky Mountain House Museum.

Day 2: Go hiking on the Siffleur Falls Trail, followed by a horseback ride at the Circle Square Ranch.

Day 3: Head to Crimson Lake Provincial Park and enjoy outdoor activities like paddling, fishing, and hiking.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Banff National Park and explore the picturesque town of Banff.

Day 5: Go white water rafting on the Red Deer River.

Day 6: Explore the Bighorn Backcountry, go backpacking, and camp overnight.

Day 7: Go on an ATV tour or try your hand at geocaching.

Food and Drink Scene of Rocky Mountain House

Rocky Mountain House has several restaurants and cafes that serve locally-sourced and fresh ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison burger: Made with bison patty, caramelized onions, and cheddar cheese, this burger is a local favorite.
  • Alberta beef steak: Alberta is known for its high-quality beef, and you can try it in various cuts and styles at local restaurants.
  • Pickerel fish and chips: You can’t visit Rocky Mountain House without trying the local pickerel, which is a freshwater fish native to Canada.

Local Beverages

  • Traditional root beer: Made with natural ingredients like sassafras and molasses, this beverage is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Alberta craft beer: Alberta has a thriving craft beer scene, and you can try different styles and flavors at local breweries.

Where to Stay in Rocky Mountain House: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rocky Mountain House has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options are:

  • Rocky Inn Express: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and Wi-Fi.
  • Walking Eagle Inn: This hotel is located near the town center and offers amenities like an on-site restaurant, fitness center, and hot tub.
  • Prairie Creek Inn: If you’re looking for a luxurious and romantic stay, this inn is perfect for you. Set amidst the natural beauty, this inn offers beautifully decorated rooms, a hot tub, and a gourmet breakfast.

What to Buy in Rocky Mountain House: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rocky Mountain House has several shops and markets where you can buy souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the items you should consider buying:

  • Handmade pottery: The town has several artisans who specialize in creating beautiful and unique pottery.
  • Maple syrup: Alberta is known for its pure and delicious maple syrup.
  • Locally made crafts: You can browse through local markets to find unique and handcrafted items like wooden toys, jewelry, and leather goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Rocky Mountain House

Rocky Mountain House has a unique culture and customs that are shaped by its history and natural surroundings. The town has a strong First Nations influence, and you can see it through local traditions, festivals, and ceremonies.

The town also has a deep love for outdoor activities and adventure, and you’ll find people hiking, biking, climbing, and fishing all year round.

Best Time to Visit Rocky Mountain House

The best time to visit Rocky Mountain House is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re a winter sports enthusiast, winter (December-March) is also a great time to visit, as you can go skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing in the nearby ski resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rocky Mountain House

Do:

  • Respect local culture, customs, and traditions.
  • Stay on designated trails while hiking and biking.
  • Take plenty of water and snacks while exploring outdoor areas.
  • Wear appropriate shoes and clothing depending on the activity.
  • Take pictures and capture the stunning beauty of nature.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Approach wildlife or feed them.
  • Smoke in designated non-smoking areas.
  • Use loud or disturbing activities that may disturb wildlife or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rocky Mountain House

Rocky Mountain House is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always recommended to take basic safety precautions like locking your doors and not leaving valuable items unattended.

If you’re exploring the wilderness, make sure to bring a first aid kit, a map, and a compass. Also, take note of the weather forecast, and avoid hiking during bad weather.

Health in Rocky Mountain House

Rocky Mountain House does not have any major health risks, but it’s always recommended to bring a first aid kit, insect repellent, and sunblock.

Getting to Rocky Mountain House: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rocky Mountain House is well-connected to other major cities in Alberta, and there are several transportation options to get here.

Airports Near Rocky Mountain House

The nearest airport is the Edmonton International Airport, which is about a 2.5-hour drive from Rocky Mountain House. Calgary International Airport is also another option and is about a 3-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Rocky Mountain House

Red Arrow has a bus service that connects Edmonton and Calgary to Rocky Mountain House. You can also use Greyhound Canada to get here from nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Rocky Mountain House is located on Highway 11, and it’s easy to access from Calgary or Edmonton.

How to Get Around in Rocky Mountain House

The best way to get around in Rocky Mountain House is by car or bike. You can rent a car from nearby rental agencies, or you can bring your bike if you’re a cycling enthusiast.

Where to Go Next from Rocky Mountain House: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to explore other nearby destinations, here are some suggestions:

  • Banff National Park: Just a few hours’ drive away, Banff is known for its stunning natural beauty and adventurous activities.
  • Jasper National Park: Jasper is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
  • Red Deer: Red Deer is a thriving city that offers cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.

Conclusion

Rocky Mountain House is a hidden gem that offers stunning natural beauty, thrilling adventures, and a unique local culture. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip and enjoy all that this town has to offer.
