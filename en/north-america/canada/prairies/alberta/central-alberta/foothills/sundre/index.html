Introduction to Sundre

Located in the heart of Foothills in Central Alberta, Sundre is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage. If you’re planning to explore the hidden gems of Central Alberta, then Sundre should definitely be on your list. This town is known for its breathtaking landscapes, stunning waterfalls, hiking and biking trails, local food and drink scene, and friendly locals. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker, or simply seeking some relaxation time in the great outdoors – Sundre is the perfect place for you.

List of Places to Visit in Sundre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or enjoy exploring local culture and heritage, Sundre has plenty to offer. Here are some of the must-see historical places and museums in the town:

  • Sundre and District Pioneer Village Museum: Explore the rich history of Sundre at this fantastic open-air museum that preserves the town’s heritage. The museum features various exhibits showcasing the artifacts, buildings, and agricultural machinery used by the early settlers in the region.

  • Greenwood Campground and Trail Centre: This site is home to the remains of a trading post and a stagecoach stop that used to be on the old Calgary-Edmonton Trail. You can take a relaxing walk on the trails or enjoy a picnic while learning about the history of this site.

  • Sundre Roundup Arena: This arena has been hosting some of the finest rodeo events in Canada since 1927, and it’s an excellent place to experience the western culture and heritage of Sundre.

Natural Attractions of Sundre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, then Sundre has plenty to offer. The town is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, and here are some of the best natural attractions to explore in Sundre:

  • Bearberry Creek Waterfall: This stunning waterfall can be found just off the beaten path near Sundre. The 27-meter high waterfall flows down into a clear pool, making it an excellent spot for nature photography or relaxing in the great outdoors.

  • James River: This river has fantastic fishing opportunities, and you can enjoy canoeing, rafting, or kayaking on its waters. There are also hiking trails along the river that offer some stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Sundre Golf Club: This golf course is situated amidst the vast forests and rolling hills of Sundre and offers a challenging yet enjoyable golfing experience. The course is a must-visit for golf enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sundre

Sundre is surrounded by amazing trails that are perfect for hikers, walkers, and cyclists. Enjoy the beautiful scenery while hiking or biking in one of these picturesque routes:

  • Ya-Ha-Tinda Nature Reserve: This area offers a great opportunity for hikers and cyclists to explore the idyllic views of the Rocky Mountains. You can also spot some wildlife on your adventure, including moose, elk, and deer.

  • Sundre Red Deer Loop: This 65 km loop is great for cyclists of all abilities. It takes you through gorgeous scenery, across creeks, and past panoramic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Sundre: Activities and Tours

If you’re wondering what to do in Sundre, then there are plenty of exciting activities and tours to keep you busy. Here are some of the best things to do in Sundre:

  • Horseback Riding: Sundre has some excellent riding trails for beginners and experienced riders alike. You can opt for guided trail rides or lessons at one of the local stables.

  • Fishing: With access to several streams, lakes, and rivers, Sundre is a wonderful place to go fishing. Trout and walleye are the most common fish species in the area.

  • Sundre Roundup: This iconic event features world-class rodeo competitions, concerts, and a midway. The Sundre Roundup is usually held in June and draws thousands of visitors to the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sundre

If you’re planning to stay in Sundre for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Start your day off with a horseback ride followed by a tour of the Sundre and District Pioneer Village Museum.

Day 2: Take a hike in Bearberry Creek and relax with a picnic by the falls.

Day 3: Spend a day fishing at James River and enjoy a scenic drive through the countryside.

Day 4: Go on a guided trail ride or take a golf lesson at Sundre Golf Club.

Day 5: Get a taste of the local cuisine at one of the town’s many restaurants and cafes.

Day 6: Browse for souvenirs at the local shops and markets in town.

Day 7: Attend the Sundre Roundup and take in the rodeo competitions, concerts, and other activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Sundre

Must-Try Dishes

Sundre’s food scene is a delight for foodies, and the town boasts several restaurants serving fresh, delicious foods. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Bison Burger: This dish is a local favorite and features a juicy bison patty with all the fixings.

  • Pickerel: Sundre is a great place to sample freshwater fish. Order the pickerel at one of the local restaurants for a taste of true local cuisine.

  • Saskatoon Berry Pie: No meal is complete without dessert, and this pie is a true Alberta favorite. Made with locally sourced Saskatoon berries, this dish is a must-try.

Local Beverages

Sundre’s thriving drink scene is not to be missed. Here are some popular local beverages you must try:

  • Craft Beer: Alberta is famous for its craft beer, and Sundre has several breweries where you can sample a cold one.

  • Wine: Alberta is home to several vineyards, and you can sample some of the region’s best at one of the local wineries.

Where to Stay in Sundre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sundre offers several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sundre:

  • Mountain View Inn and Suites: This hotel is located in the heart of Sundre and offers well-appointed rooms and suites. The hotel also features an indoor pool, hot tub, and fitness center.

  • Sundre Motor Inn: This motel offers affordable and comfortable rooms with modern amenities. The motel is in a quiet location just off the main road.

  • Nordegg Lodge: Located on a working cattle ranch 45 minutes from town, this unique accommodation is perfect for those who want a true western experience. The lodge offers rooms, cabins, and a bunkhouse.

What to Buy in Sundre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sundre has a wealth of local products and souvenirs that make excellent gifts for loved ones back home. Some of the best things to buy in Sundre include:

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Sundre is home to several artisan potters who create unique and beautiful pieces.

  • Local Honey: Alberta is famous for its honey, and Sundre has some excellent local varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Sundre

Sundre is a friendly and welcoming town, and the locals are known for their hospitality. When visiting Sundre, it’s important to respect local customs and be mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Sundre

The best time to visit Sundre is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. This time of year is perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. If you’re interested in attending the Sundre Roundup, plan your trip for June.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sundre

Do:

  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the region.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and drink scene, you won’t regret it.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Hunt or disturb wildlife.

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sundre

Sundre is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should be cautious when exploring natural areas and follow basic safety guidelines when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Sundre

Healthcare services are available in Sundre, and there is a local hospital and several clinics in town. Visitors should have adequate travel insurance before beginning their trip.

Getting to Sundre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sundre

The closest major airport to Sundre is Calgary International Airport, which is approximately an hour and a half drive away. There are rental car services available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Sundre

Greyhound Canada services Sundre, and the bus station is located in town.

Other Options

Driving to Sundre is the easiest way to get to the town, with several major highways providing easy access.

How to Get Around in Sundre

The best way to get around Sundre is by car, bike or foot. Rental car services are available in the town, and several bike rental shops offer bikes for hire.

Where to Go Next from Sundre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sundre is a great base for exploring some of the other attractions in Central Alberta. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Banff National Park: This park is a world-renowned natural wonder, located an hour and a half from Sundre. The park features stunning scenery, hiking trails, and plenty of outdoor activities.

  • Kananaskis Country: Kananaskis is an hour’s drive from Sundre and is home to picturesque scenic drives, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

  • Calgary: The vibrant city of Calgary is just over an hour’s drive from Sundre and offers plenty of entertainment, shopping, and dining options.

Conclusion

With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, Sundre is a must-visit destination in Central Alberta. From hiking and biking to exploring local history and culture, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
