Introduction to Kananaskis Country

Kananaskis Country is a breathtaking region located in the heart of the Alberta Rockies, in Alberta, Canada. The area spans over 4,000 square kilometers, offering a plethora of natural beauty, rich cultural attractions, and top-notch outdoor activities. Nestled on the eastern slopes of the Canadian Rockies, Kananaskis Country is composed of five provincial parks, including Peter Lougheed Provincial Park, Spray Valley Provincial Park, and Elbow-Sheep Wildland Provincial Park.

List of Places to Visit in Kananaskis Country

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

History enthusiasts will find Kananaskis Country a true treasure trove. There are several museums and historical places that showcase the region’s rich heritage and culture, including:

  • Kananaskis Nordic Spa: Relax in the midst of nature and take a dip in the outdoor hot tubs and saunas at the Kananaskis Nordic Spa. Located in the heart of the Kananaskis Village, this contemporary spa offers a variety of treatments and therapies to rejuvenate the mind and body.
  • Eau Claire Distillery: Take a tour of the Eau Claire Distillery, one of the most popular distilleries in the region that produces small-batch spirits using locally sourced grains and botanicals. The guided tour will let you sample the different varieties of gin and whisky available.
  • Canmore Museum and Geoscience Centre: Located just a short drive from Kananaskis Village, the Canmore Museum and Geoscience Centre is the perfect destination for history buffs and geology enthusiasts. Discover the rich history of the region and learn about the geology and fossils of the Canadian Rockies.

Natural Attractions of Kananaskis Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kananaskis Country is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, from hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding. There are several natural attractions in the region that offer scenic views and unforgettable experiences, including:

  • Ha Ling Peak: Climb the iconic Ha Ling Peak, the most popular hike in Kananaskis Country. Enjoy spectacular views of the Canadian Rockies and the Bow Valley.
  • Kananaskis Valley: Hike or bike along the picturesque Kananaskis Valley, where several trails wind through stunning forests and meadows.
  • Spray Lakes Reservoir: Enjoy a scenic drive along the Spray Lakes Reservoir, a stunning body of water surrounded by towering mountains. It’s also a great spot for fishing and camping.
  • Elk Island National Park: Take a day trip to the Elk Island National Park, a wildlife reserve that’s home to bison, bears, and several species of birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kananaskis Country

Kananaskis Country is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with several trails that cater to all skill levels. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region:

  • Elbow Lake Trail: This 16km trail is perfect for intermediate hikers, offering stunning views of Elbow Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Jewell Pass Trail: This 11km trail is perfect for hikers and mountain bikers, offering breathtaking views of the Canadian Rockies and Kananaskis Valley.
  • Mt. Allan Trail: This 17km trail is perfect for advanced hikers looking for a challenging ascent. The trail ends at the summit of Mt. Allan, providing awe-inspiring views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Kananaskis Country: Activities and Tours

With so much natural beauty and adventure, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Kananaskis Country. Here are some of the best activities and tours that the region has to offer:

  • Whitewater Rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Kananaskis River, which offers exciting Class 3 rapids that are perfect for adrenaline junkies and beginners alike.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided horseback ride through the stunning backcountry of Kananaskis Country, providing breathtaking views and an unforgettable experience.
  • Helicopter Tours: See Kananaskis Country from a bird’s eye view with a helicopter tour, offering panoramic views of the majestic Canadian Rockies.
  • Ice Fishing: Enjoy a unique winter experience by trying your hand at ice fishing on one of Kananaskis Country’s frozen lakes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kananaskis Country

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your week in Kananaskis Country:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the natural wonders of the region with a hike along Kananaskis Valley and a visit to Ha Ling Peak.
  • Day 3-4: Take a break from outdoor activities and explore the region’s cultural attractions, including a visit to the Canmore Museum and Geoscience Centre and Kananaskis Nordic Spa.
  • Day 5-6: Embark on outdoor adventures with whitewater rafting and horseback riding tours.
  • Day 7: Wrap up your stay with a helicopter tour and ice fishing excursion.

Food and Drink Scene of Kananaskis Country

Must-Try Dishes

Kananaskis Country’s food scene is a blend of traditional Canadian fare and contemporary gourmet cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes in the region:

  • Alberta Beef: Enjoy a juicy steak made from locally raised Alberta beef, which is known for its exceptional quality and taste.
  • Bison Burger: Bite into a delicious bison burger, a lean and healthy alternative to traditional hamburgers.
  • Maple Candies: Indulge in the sweet and sticky treat of maple candies, a Canadian delicacy made from pure maple syrup.

Local Beverages

Kananaskis Country also boasts a vibrant and growing craft beer and wine scene, with several local breweries and wineries producing top-notch beverages. Here are some of the best local drinks to try:

  • Canmore Brewing Company: Sample the beers produced at Canmore Brewing Company, a popular local brewery that offers a variety of craft beer flavors, including a double IPA and a classic lager.
  • Highlander Wine & Spirits: Savor the crisp and fruity flavors of the wines produced at Highlander Wine & Spirits, a local winery that uses only the best handpicked grapes.

Where to Stay in Kananaskis Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kananaskis Country has several cozy and comfortable accommodations to suit all budgets and travel styles. Here are some options to consider:

  • Kananaskis Mountain Lodge: This luxurious full-service property is ideal for those looking for an upscale experience with mountain-inspired decor, on-site dining, and a world-class spa.
  • Crosswaters Resort: This eco-friendly resort is perfect for those who want to be surrounded by nature while still enjoying modern amenities, including an on-site golf course and full-service spa.
  • Mount Engadine Lodge: This cozy and intimate lodge is perfect for those who want a secluded wilderness experience, with cozy accommodations and access to outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Kananaskis Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kananaskis Country is an excellent destination to buy souvenirs that showcase the region’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Here are some local products to bring home:

  • Handmade Pottery: Kananaskis Country is home to several local pottery studios that produce beautiful handmade pottery, including mugs, vases, and plates.
  • Rocky Mountain Soap Company Products: Take home some eco-friendly soaps and bath products made from all-natural and plant-based ingredients from the Rocky Mountain Soap Company, a local skincare company.
  • Canadian Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle or two of the region’s famous maple syrup, a signature Canadian product that’s both sweet and savory.

Local Culture and Customs of Kananaskis Country

Kananaskis Country is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, with indigenous and European heritage shaping the region’s customs and practices. Here’s a glimpse into the local culture:

  • First Nations Culture: The indigenous people of the region have a long and storied history, and their culture is reflected in several festivals and events held throughout the year.
  • Outdoor Adventure Culture: Kananaskis Country is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and the locals take their love for nature seriously. Respect for the outdoors, including leaving no trace and obeying wildlife safety protocols is a big part of local culture.

Best Time to Visit Kananaskis Country

Kananaskis Country is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm. The best time to visit depends on your preferred activities, but generally, the best seasons to visit are spring, summer, and fall when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in abundance. Winter is also a great time for skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kananaskis Country

Do:

  • Respect wildlife safety protocols and give animals their space.
  • Carry a map and take adequate safety precautions before heading out on a hike or bike ride.
  • Check current weather and trail conditions before embarking on an outdoor activity.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in natural areas.
  • Disturb the natural environment or remove plants or rocks.
  • Attempt difficult outdoor activities without adequate preparation or a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kananaskis Country

Kananaskis Country is a safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  • Wildlife Encounters: Visitors should carry bear spray while hiking or camping in the region’s backcountry and follow wildlife safety precautions to avoid dangerous encounters.
  • Weather: The weather in Kananaskis Country can be unpredictable and can change quickly. Visitors should be prepared for changing conditions and pack appropriate gear.

Health in Kananaskis Country

Kananaskis Country has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, to cater to any health concerns that visitors may have. Visitors should carry adequate health insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Kananaskis Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Kananaskis Country is relatively easy thanks to several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Kananaskis Country

The nearest major airport to Kananaskis Country is the Calgary International Airport, which is located approximately an hour’s drive from Kananaskis Village.

Bus Stations Near Kananaskis Country

Several bus companies offer regular services to and from Kananaskis Country, including Greyhound and Sundog Tours.

Other Options

Kananaskis Country is also accessible by car, with several rental car companies operating out of Calgary and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around Kananaskis Country

The best way to get around Kananaskis Country is by car, as the region is vast and spread out. Visitors can also rent bikes from several shops in Kananaskis Village or around the area.

Where to Go Next from Kananaskis Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kananaskis Country is perfectly situated for those who want to explore other destinations in the Rockies. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Banff: Visit the charming town of Banff, just over an hour away, to explore the famous Banff National Park and enjoy a range of outdoor activities.
  • Jasper: Further up the Rockies is Jasper National Park, a stunning area with dazzling lakes, snow-capped peaks, glaciers, and hot springs.
  • Helicopter Tours: Take a helicopter tour to explore the surrounding area from an aerial view, including the Badlands, Drumheller, and more.

Conclusion

Kananaskis Country is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and culture seekers alike. With its breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural attractions, and top-notch outdoor activities, there’s something for everyone in this wonderful region. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Kananaskis Country and experience the best that Canada has to offer.
