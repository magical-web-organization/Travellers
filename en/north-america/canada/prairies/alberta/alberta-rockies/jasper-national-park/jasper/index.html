Introduction to Jasper

Welcome to Jasper, a beautiful town nestled in the heart of Jasper National Park, in Alberta Rockies, in Alberta! Jasper is a nature lover’s paradise, with stunning natural landscapes, pristine lakes, breathtaking views, and an abundance of wildlife. This guide will help you plan your trip to Jasper with all the essential information you need to know before visiting this charming Canadian town.

List of Places to Visit in Jasper

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jasper-Yellowhead Museum and Archives: Explore the history and culture of Jasper at this museum and archives.
  • Jasper Information Centre: Stop by the center to get information about the town and the national park, and learn about the Indigenous culture and history of the area.
  • Jasper Art Gallery: Admire contemporary and traditional art pieces by local artists and artists from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Jasper: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maligne Lake: Take a boat tour or kayak through the crystal clear waters of Maligne Lake and enjoy the majestic views of the surrounding peaks and glaciers.
  • Athabasca Glacier: Hike on the glacier, or take a guided tour on an Ice Explorer vehicle and experience the beauty of the icefields.
  • Jasper SkyTram: Take a ride on the Jasper SkyTram, the longest and highest aerial tramway in Canada, and enjoy panoramic views of the town and the surrounding mountain range.
  • Jasper National Park: This is the largest national park in the Canadian Rocky Mountains. It boasts serene lakes, waterfalls, vibrant forests, glaciers, and abundant wildlife, including bears, elk, moose, and bighorn sheep.
  • Marmot Basin: Ski through the Rocky Mountains at Marmot Basin, a stunning ski resort with over 1,600 acres of terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jasper

  • Jasper Discovery Trail: This scenic trail runs through the Jasper townsite and through beautiful forests, lakes, and valleys.
  • Sulphur Skyline Trail: Hike to the Sulphur Skyline and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Canadian Rockies from the summit.
  • Old Fort Point Trail: This easy hike takes you through the forests and up to the Old Fort Point, where you can enjoy the panoramic views of the Athabasca River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Jasper: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting: Raft down the Athabasca River and enjoy the stunning views of the Canadian Rockies.
  • Wildlife Tours: Take a custom wildlife tour and experience the natural beauty of Jasper and its wild inhabitants.
  • Golfing: Play a round of golf at the renowned Jasper Park Golf Course, a meticulously groomed course boasting stunning views of the Rockies.
  • Jasper Dark Sky Preserve: Stargaze in the world’s second-largest Dark Sky Preserve and experience the beauty of the cosmos.

Food and Drink Scene of Jasper

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: A juicy burger made from locally sourced bison meat.
  • Elk Sausage: A delicious sausage made from Alberta elk meat and local spices.
  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish made with French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Jasper Brewing Company: Enjoy locally crafted beer at Jasper’s famous brewery. Try their signature IPA, Crisp Pilsner, or other seasonal beers.
  • Tea Leaf: Savor a variety of organic teas, locally roasted coffee, and handcrafted cold beverages.

Where to Stay in Jasper: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge: A luxurious hotel known for its stunning views and world-class amenities.
  • HI Hostel: A budget-friendly and eco-friendly hostel located in the heart of Jasper town.
  • Pocahontas Cabins: Rustic cabins perfect for a cozy getaway in the woods.

What to Buy in Jasper: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jasper Gems and Minerals: A shop that sells various minerals and gems from Jasper and other parts of the world.
  • Jasper Local Foods: A store that sells locally made jams, jellies, and other foods.
  • On-Line Sport and Tackle: A sporting goods store that sells gear for fishing, hunting, hiking, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Jasper

Jasper has a rich history and culture, and the Indigenous community has played an essential role in shaping the town’s identity. Visitors are encouraged to respect the land, people, and culture by following the principles of “Leave No Trace” and learning about the history and culture of the Indigenous community.

Best Time to Visit Jasper

The best time to visit Jasper is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warm, and the wildlife is abundant. However, Jasper is open year-round and offers unique experiences in each season. Visit during the winter months for skiing and other cold-weather activities, or travel during the fall and enjoy the stunning autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jasper

Do:

  • Follow the principles of “Leave No Trace.”
  • Respect the wildlife and keep a safe distance.
  • Learn about the history and culture of the Indigenous community.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Approach or feed the wildlife.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jasper

While Jasper is a relatively safe town, visitors should take precautions when exploring the wilderness and hiking trails. Make sure to carry a bear spray while hiking and stay aware of your surroundings. It’s also essential to follow the guidelines and safety measures provided by tour guides and park officials.

Health in Jasper

Jasper has medical facilities and pharmacies, but visitors should be aware that medical care may be limited in remote areas. It’s recommended to carry a first-aid kit with basic medical supplies, including insect repellent and sun protection.

Getting to Jasper: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jasper

The nearest airport to Jasper is the Edmonton International Airport, located approximately 350 kilometers away. Visitors can rent a car or take a bus or shuttle to reach Jasper from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Jasper

The major bus station near Jasper is the Jasper Bus Station, located in the town center. Buses from Edmonton and Calgary stop here regularly.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Jasper by taking the Icefields Parkway from Lake Louise or Banff, one of the world’s most scenic drives.

How to Get Around in Jasper

Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use public transportation to explore Jasper. The town is small enough to walk or bike around, but renting a car is recommended to access remote areas and hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Jasper: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Banff National Park: Explore the natural beauty and outdoor activities of Banff National Park, located approximately three hours from Jasper.
  • Yoho National Park: Visit Yoho National Park, located approximately five hours from Jasper, and explore stunning waterfalls, lakes, and wildlife.
  • Lake Louise: Admire the emerald waters of Lake Louise and explore the nearby hiking trails and ski resorts.

Conclusion

Jasper is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those looking for a peaceful getaway. Plan your trip to Jasper, and experience the stunning natural landscapes, wildlife, history, and culture of this charming Canadian town.
