Introduction to Columbia Icefield

Columbia Icefield is a breathtaking sight located in Jasper National Park, in the Alberta Rockies, in Alberta, in Prairies, in Canada, in North America. It is a 325-square kilometer remnant from the past ice age and features eight major glaciers, stunning waterfalls, and serene blue lakes. Visiting Columbia Icefield provides a chance to experience a natural wonder and connect with the outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Columbia Icefield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Columbia Icefield Glacier Discovery Centre - The starting point for your adventure. It’s an interpretive center featuring interactive exhibits about the history and ecology of the area.
  • The Athabasca Glacier - One of the most popular attractions at Columbia Icefield. You can walk directly onto the glacier or take a snow coach tour.
  • The Banff-Jasper Highway - A scenic highway that takes you from Banff to Jasper and offers stunning views of the Canadian Rockies.
  • The Sunwapta Falls - A picturesque waterfall that should not be missed.

Natural Attractions of Columbia Icefield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Athabasca River - Enjoy a scenic boat tour of the Athabasca River and experience the beauty of the river valley.
  • Wilcox Pass - A strenuous day hike that offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • The Skywalk - An observation platform suspended above the Sunwapta Valley that offers a panoramic view of the surrounding area.
  • The Glacier Skywalk - A glass-bottomed platform that offers breathtaking views of the glacier and the surrounding valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbia Icefield

Columbia Icefield is a hiker’s paradise. With miles of trails ranging from easy to difficult, there’s something for everyone.

  • Wilcox Pass Trail - A moderate 8.0 km hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Toe of the Athabasca Glacier - Experience walking on the glacier itself by taking a guided tour.
  • Icefields Parkway - A scenic drive that passes by Columbia Icefield and other natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Columbia Icefield: Activities and Tours

There are lots of things to do during your visit to Columbia Icefield.

  • Athabasca Glacier Adventure - Take a guided tour of the Athabasca Glacier via a specially designed Snowcoach vehicle.
  • Skywalk and Glacier Helicopter Tour - An exciting helicopter tour over the glaciers followed by a visit to the skywalk observation platform.
  • Boat Tours - Explore the Athabasca River on a boat tour and take in the stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbia Icefield

Day 1: Visit the Columbia Icefield Glacier Discovery Centre and enjoy the interactive exhibits that showcase the area’s history and ecology.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Athabasca Glacier via a Snowcoach vehicle.

Day 3: Hike Wilcox Pass Trail and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Day 4: Take a helicopter tour over the glaciers followed by a visit to the skywalk observation platform.

Day 5: Relax with a scenic boat tour of the Athabasca River.

Day 6: Explore the nearby areas, such as Banff or Jasper, by taking a scenic drive along the Banff-Jasper Highway.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby waterfalls, such as Sunwapta Falls and Athabasca Falls.

Food and Drink Scene of Columbia Icefield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Bison Burger - A local specialty made from free-range bison.
  • Alberta Beef - A must-try for meat lovers. Alberta beef is known for its quality and taste.
  • Poutine - A classic Canadian dish that consists of fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Ice Wine - A sweet dessert wine made from grapes frozen on the vine.
  • Beer - Alberta is known for its local craft beer scene, and there are lots of breweries to choose from.

Where to Stay in Columbia Icefield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Glacier View Lodge - A hotel located at the Columbia Icefield Glacier Discovery Centre with stunning views of the glaciers.
  • HI-Rampart Creek Hostel - A budget-friendly option that provides easy access to the surrounding hiking trails.
  • Becker’s Chalets - Located in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, these chalets offer stunning views of the surrounding peaks.

What to Buy in Columbia Icefield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup - A classic Canadian souvenir that is perfect for sweetening up your pancakes or waffles.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry - Local artisans create unique jewelry featuring stones and metals from the surrounding area.
  • Photography Prints - Bring home a piece of natural beauty with stunning prints of the glaciers and surrounding landscape.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbia Icefield

  • Indigenous Culture - The area surrounding Columbia Icefield has a rich indigenous history and culture. Take time to learn about the local First Nations’ history and traditions.
  • Leave No Trace - The glaciers are a fragile ecosystem, so it’s important to practice Leave No Trace principles while exploring.

Best Time to Visit Columbia Icefield

The best time to visit Columbia Icefield is during the summer, from June to August when the weather is warm and the hiking trails are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbia Icefield

Do:

  • Respect the environment and practice Leave No Trace principles.
  • Be prepared for sudden changes in weather, bring warm clothes and rain gear.
  • Take guided tours with professional guides, especially when walking on glaciers.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines when hiking or exploring around the glacier.
  • Leave trash or litter behind on the hiking trails or picnic areas.
  • Ignore the instructions of guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbia Icefield

While exploring Columbia Icefield, it’s important to take measures to stay safe.

  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes and bring appropriate clothing and gear.
  • Follow the safety guidelines and instructions of guides, especially when walking on glaciers.

Health in Columbia Icefield

  • Always bring adequate water and food while exploring.
  • Be aware of the elevation and potential effects on breathing.

Getting to Columbia Icefield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Columbia Icefield

  • Calgary International Airport - The nearest major airport, located 290 kilometers away.
  • Edmonton International Airport - Located 365 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Columbia Icefield

  • Greyhound - Provides service to nearby areas such as Banff or Jasper.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a popular and convenient way to explore the area.
  • Private Shuttle - Private shuttle services are available from the major airports.

How to Get Around in Columbia Icefield

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Columbia Icefield. However, guided tours are available for those who prefer to be chauffeured around.

Where to Go Next from Columbia Icefield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Banff National Park - Explore one of Canada’s most famous national parks, located just a short drive from Columbia Icefield.
  • Jasper National Park - A stunning wilderness area with scenic drives, hiking trails, and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Columbia Icefield is a natural wonder that offers endless opportunities for adventure and exploration. Whether you’re a keen hiker, a foodie, a photographer, or a history buff, there’s something for everyone.
