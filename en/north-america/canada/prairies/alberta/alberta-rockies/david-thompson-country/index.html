Introduction to David Thompson Country

Nestled within the Alberta Rockies, David Thompson Country is an outdoor playground offering breathtaking scenery, fascinating history, and endless adventure opportunities. The region is named after fur trader and explorer David Thompson, who first traversed the area in the late 1700s.

From hiking and fishing to skiing and snowmobiling, David Thompson Country has something for every outdoor enthusiast. The region covers a vast expanse of land, encompassing numerous small towns and communities, each with its unique charm and offerings.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, see, eat, and stay in David Thompson Country.

List of Places to Visit in David Thompson Country

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rocky Mountain House National Historic Site

Discover the fascinating history of the fur trade at Rocky Mountain House National Historic Site. This site was an essential trading post in the 18th and 19th centuries and offers guided tours, interactive exhibits, and seasonal events.

Norwegian Laft Hus Museum

Immerse yourself in the history of the Norwegian settlers who first homesteaded the region with a visit to the Norwegian Laft Hus Museum. This museum features a traditional log house built in the Norwegian style and is open for tours.

Natural Attractions of David Thompson Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Abraham Lake

Abraham Lake is a stunning man-made lake located in the heart of David Thompson Country. The lake is famous for its unique ice bubbles that form in the winter, making it a popular destination for photographers and nature enthusiasts.

Crimson Lake Provincial Park

Crimson Lake Provincial Park is a beautiful park located on the shores of Crimson Lake. The park features hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and picnic areas, making it an excellent destination for a day trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of David Thompson Country

Sentinel Pass Trail

The Sentinel Pass Trail is a challenging hike that rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The trail is approximately 11 kilometers and takes approximately 6-8 hours to complete.

Iron Horse Trail

The Iron Horse Trail is a popular multi-use trail that spans over 300 kilometers and connects many of the small towns in the region. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, and offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in David Thompson Country: Activities and Tours

Snowmobiling

Experience the thrill of the Canadian wilderness with a guided snowmobile tour. Many operators offer guided tours of the region’s snowmobile-friendly trails, allowing visitors to explore the backcountry in a unique way.

Fishing

David Thompson Country is renowned for its excellent fishing opportunities. The region is home to numerous lakes, rivers, and streams, teeming with fish, including trout, pike, and walleye.

Week-Long Itinerary for David Thompson Country

For those looking to experience everything that David Thompson Country has to offer, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary to help plan your trip.

Day 1: Start your trip in Rocky Mountain House. Visit the Rocky Mountain House National Historic Site and explore the local shops and restaurants.

Day 2: Head south to the town of Nordegg and explore the area’s hiking and mountain biking trails.

Day 3: Spend a day on the water at Abraham Lake, enjoying fishing, kayaking, and the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Take a snowmobile tour of the region’s snowy backcountry.

Day 5: Explore the Iron Horse Trail and visit the small towns along the way.

Day 6: Visit the Norwegian Laft Hus Museum in nearby Red Deer and learn about the area’s Norwegian heritage.

Day 7: End your trip with a relaxing day at Crimson Lake Provincial Park.

Food and Drink Scene of David Thompson Country

Must-Try Dishes

Bison Burger

No trip to Alberta is complete without trying a bison burger. The meat is lean, tender, and full of flavor, making for a delicious meal.

Alberta Beef

Alberta is renowned for its excellent beef, thanks to the region’s high-quality grazing lands. Many restaurants in the area serve up the best cuts of meat, including ribeye and tenderloin.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

Craft beer is thriving in Alberta, and the David Thompson Country region is no exception. Many local breweries offer tours and tastings, allowing visitors to sample the region’s best beers.

Canadian Whiskey

Canada is renowned for its whiskey, and the region is home to numerous distilleries producing high-quality spirits. Visitors can try a dram or two and learn more about the whiskey-making process.

Where to Stay in David Thompson Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glacier View Lodge

Located in the town of Jasper, Glacier View Lodge offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and the Athabasca Glacier. The lodge features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and easy access to hiking and other outdoor activities.

Ramada by Wyndham Hinton

Conveniently located in the town of Hinton, the Ramada offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and hot tub, making it an excellent choice for families.

What to Buy in David Thompson Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Canada is famous for its maple syrup, and David Thompson Country is no exception. Visitors can purchase high-quality maple syrup from local producers to take home and enjoy.

Handmade Crafts

The region is home to numerous artisans producing high-quality handmade crafts, including pottery, woodworking, and textile arts. Visitors can purchase these unique items as souvenirs or gifts for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of David Thompson Country

David Thompson Country is a multicultural region, home to numerous indigenous communities and settlers from around the world. Visitors should be respectful of these cultures and customs, including following proper etiquette when visiting indigenous communities.

Best Time to Visit David Thompson Country

The best time to visit David Thompson Country depends on your interests. Summer is the busiest time of year, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities. Winter is perfect for snowsports and outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of David Thompson Country

Do:

  • Respect the region’s natural beauty by leaving no trace and respecting the environment.
  • Take the time to learn about the region’s diverse cultures and traditions.
  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions and rough terrain.

Don’t:

  • Approach wildlife or disturb their natural habitat.
  • Leave your garbage and waste behind.
  • Ignore safety guidelines and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in David Thompson Country

David Thompson Country is a beautiful and wild region, but visitors should take the necessary precautions to stay safe. This includes being aware of your surroundings, staying on designated trails and routes, and carrying proper equipment, including bear spray.

Health in David Thompson Country

Visitors to the region should be aware of the potential risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hypothermia, dehydration, and injuries from falls or wildlife encounters. It’s essential to come prepared with proper clothing, gear, and first aid supplies.

Getting to David Thompson Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near David Thompson Country

The nearest major airport is Edmonton International Airport, located approximately three hours north of David Thompson Country.

Bus Stations Near David Thompson Country

Greyhound Canada operates bus services to the town of Rocky Mountain House.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to rent a car to explore the region independently or book a guided tour.

How to Get Around in David Thompson Country

The best way to get around David Thompson Country is by car. Alternatively, visitors can explore the region’s many hiking and biking trails, as well as take guided tours.

Where to Go Next from David Thompson Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Banff National Park

Just a few hours’ drive west of David Thompson Country lies Banff National Park, one of the world’s most iconic national parks. Visitors can explore stunning scenery, take a dip in natural hot springs, and spot local wildlife.

Conclusion

David Thompson Country is a hidden gem in the Canadian Rockies, offering a wealth of outdoor adventures, fascinating history, and stunning natural beauty. We hope this guide has inspired you to plan your own adventure to this incredible region.
