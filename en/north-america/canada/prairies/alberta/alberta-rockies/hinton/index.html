Introduction to Hinton

Hinton, located in the Alberta Rockies of Alberta, Canada, is a small town with a big personality. Known for its natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities, Hinton also boasts a rich cultural history with important museums and historical sites. Whether you’re a nature lover or history buff, Hinton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hinton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hinton and District Chamber of Commerce Visitor Information Centre: Start your trip with a visit to the Visitor Information Centre to learn about the town’s history and current events.

  2. Hinton Institute: A former school turned community hub, the Hinton Institute houses art exhibits, cultural events, and more.

  3. Hinton Pioneer Museum: Step back in time at the Hinton Pioneer Museum, which features artifacts and exhibits from the town’s early days as a mining community.

  4. Hinton Train Station: The Hinton Train Station, built in 1911, is an important piece of the town’s history and a popular spot for photos.

Natural Attractions of Hinton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. William A. Switzer Provincial Park: This park offers hiking, biking, and camping opportunities, as well as beautiful views of the mountains and nearby lakes.

  2. Brule Sand Dunes: The Brule Sand Dunes are a unique geological feature formed thousands of years ago by glaciers. Visitors can hike or picnic in the area.

  3. Beaver Boardwalk: Take a stroll on the Beaver Boardwalk, a wooden walkway through a wetland area where visitors can spot beavers and other wildlife.

  4. Athabasca Riverfront Park: Located along the Athabasca River, this park has picnic areas, a playground, and a beach for swimming and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hinton

Hinton offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels.

  1. Windy Point Ridge: This 12-kilometer hike offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is best tackled in the summer or fall.

  2. Hardisty Trail: This 8-kilometer trail is perfect for mountain biking enthusiasts, with challenging terrain and beautiful views.

  3. Maligne Pass: This 14-kilometer hike is a challenging but rewarding trek through Jasper National Park, with panoramic views of the mountains and glaciers.

  4. Overlander Trail: This 6-kilometer trail runs through scenic forest and is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Hinton: Activities and Tours

  1. Whitewater rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Athabasca River, with tours available for all skill levels.

  2. Horseback riding: Saddle up and explore the beautiful backcountry of Hinton on horseback.

  3. Sightseeing tours: Take a guided tour of the town and nearby attractions, such as Jasper National Park and the Columbia Icefield.

  4. Caving: Explore underground caves and see unique geological formations with a guided caving tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Hinton

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Poutine: A Canadian classic, poutine is a dish of French fries topped with cheese curds and gravy.

  2. Bison burger: Hinton is known for its high-quality bison meat, which is often used to make delicious burgers.

  3. Maple syrup: Canada is famous for its maple syrup, which is used in a variety of dishes and desserts.

  4. Butter tarts: A sweet and buttery pastry filled with raisins or pecans, butter tarts are a Canadian specialty.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft beer: Hinton has a thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries producing unique and delicious beers.

  2. Canadian whiskey: Canada is known for its whiskey, with brands like Crown Royal and Canadian Club being popular choices.

Where to Stay in Hinton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Holiday Inn Express: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like an indoor pool and hot tub.

  2. Pillows B&B: This cozy bed and breakfast offers a homey atmosphere and personalized service.

  3. Hinton Lodge: This affordable hotel offers clean rooms and a convenient location close to downtown.

What to Buy in Hinton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bison meat: Take home a taste of Hinton with bison meat, which is available at local markets and butcher shops.

  2. Maple syrup: Another popular souvenir, maple syrup can be found at local markets and gift shops.

  3. Handmade crafts: Hinton is home to several talented artists and craftsmen, who create unique and beautiful pieces like pottery and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Hinton

Hinton’s culture is a blend of indigenous traditions and Western influences. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s history and traditions, and strive to learn more about the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Hinton

The best time to visit Hinton is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also beautiful in the fall, when the leaves change colors and the air is crisp and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hinton

Do:

  1. Respect the area’s natural beauty and wildlife.

  2. Learn about the history and culture of the town.

  3. Take advantage of outdoor recreation opportunities.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.

  2. Disrespect local customs or traditions.

  3. Take unnecessary risks while hiking or exploring.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hinton

Hinton is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Keep valuables in a secure location and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Hinton

Hinton has medical facilities and pharmacies for any health needs. Be sure to bring any necessary medications with you, and take steps to prevent illness and injury while exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Hinton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hinton

The closest major airport to Hinton is Edmonton International Airport, which is located approximately 3 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Hinton

Greyhound Canada offers bus service to Hinton from cities like Edmonton and Calgary.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Hinton or rent a car from a nearby city.

How to Get Around Hinton

A rental car is recommended for exploring the area, but visitors can also take taxis or use public transportation like buses and shuttles.

Where to Go Next from Hinton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Jasper National Park: Just an hour’s drive from Hinton, Jasper National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

  2. Banff National Park: Canada’s most famous national park is also within driving distance of Hinton, and offers stunning mountain scenery and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Hinton offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural history, and outdoor recreation opportunities. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply relax in the great outdoors, Hinton has something for everyone.
