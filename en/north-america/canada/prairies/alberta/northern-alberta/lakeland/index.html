Introduction to Lakeland

Lakeland, located in Northern Alberta, is a charming destination known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and vibrant cultural heritage. This region offers a unique blend of prairie landscapes, shimmering lakes, forests, and rolling hills, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you discover the best of Lakeland, from its must-visit attractions and hiking routes to its local cuisine and cultural customs.

List of Places to Visit in Lakeland

There are numerous places to visit in Lakeland that cater to a wide variety of interests. Here are some of the highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lakeland is home to many cultural and historical sites that pay tribute to the region’s rich heritage. Visit the following to get a taste of Lakeland’s past:

  • Ukrainian Cultural Heritage Village: Explore more than 30 restored buildings depicting Ukrainian pioneer life in Canada.
  • Cold Lake Museums: Discover local history at the Air Force Museum, the Heritage Museum, and the Marine Museum.
  • Lac La Biche Mission Historical Site: Immerse yourself in the history of the Lac La Biche Mission, established in 1853 by Catholic missionaries.

Natural Attractions of Lakeland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lakeland boasts an array of natural attractions that’s sure to satisfy adventurers and nature lovers alike. Here are some of the top spots to explore:

  • Elk Island National Park: Experience the wild beauty of the Canadian prairies and see bison, elk, moose, and other wildlife up close.
  • Sir Winston Churchill Provincial Park: Enjoy outdoor recreation such as camping, fishing, swimming, and hiking in this picturesque park.
  • Beaver River Trestle: Take a walk across this historic railway trestle, which offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakeland

Lakeland has many hiking and biking trails that reveal the stunning beauty of the region’s natural landscapes. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  • Cold Lake Provincial Park Hiking Trails: Choose from several trails that offer access to lakeside views, forests, and wetlands.
  • Iron Horse Trail: This multi-use trail stretches over 300 km and passes through several charming communities.
  • Beaver River Trestle Trail: Enjoy a leisurely walk or bike ride across this historic trestle and take in the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Lakeland: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Lakeland, from outdoor activities to cultural experiences. Here are some of the must-try activities and tours:

  • Fishing: Lakeland is famous for its fishing opportunities, with numerous lakes and rivers offering a chance to catch freshwater species like walleye and pike.
  • Boat tours: Board a boat tour for a leisurely cruise on Cold Lake or Lac La Biche.
  • Star-gazing: Take in the clear, starry skies at one of Lakeland’s designated dark sky sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakeland

If you have a week to spare in Lakeland, here’s a sample itinerary that will give you a taste of what this region has to offer:

Food and Drink Scene of Lakeland

Lakeland’s food and drink scene is a vibrant glimpse into the region’s cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Perogies: These Ukrainian stuffed dumplings can be found at many local restaurants and cafes.
  • Bison burgers: Try a juicy burger made with locally sourced bison meat.
  • Bannock: This traditional Indigenous bread is a staple of Lakeland cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Blueberry mead: This sweet, fruity wine is made from honey and blueberries and is a popular local drink.
  • Craft beer: Lakeland has a growing number of microbreweries producing delicious and unique craft beers.
  • Wild berry smoothies: Made with locally foraged berries, these smoothies are a tasty and healthy option.

Where to Stay in Lakeland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Lakeland, whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Lakefront Cottage: This beautiful cabin nestled on the shores of Cold Lake is perfect for a romantic getaway or family vacation.
  • Canalta Hotel: This hotel in Lac La Biche offers comfortable rooms and all the amenities you need for a relaxing stay.
  • HI-Athabasca Falls Wilderness Hostel: This wilderness hostel is located near the magnificent Athabasca Falls and is a great base for outdoor adventures.

What to Buy in Lakeland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Lakeland, consider the following:

  • Birch bark baskets: These beautiful handmade baskets are a traditional Indigenous craft and make for a unique home decor item.
  • Wild berry jams and jellies: Made with locally foraged berries, these spreads are a great way to bring a taste of Lakeland home with you.
  • Indigenous artwork: Browse local galleries and shops for stunning artwork by Indigenous artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakeland

Lakeland’s cultural heritage is shaped by the Indigenous people who have lived on these lands for thousands of years and the Ukrainian settlers who arrived in the late 1800s. By learning about these cultures, you’ll gain a deeper understanding and appreciation for Lakeland. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for the land: Lakeland’s natural landscapes are sacred to the Indigenous people who have lived here for generations. Make sure to treat the land with care and respect.
  • Ukrainian traditions: Many Lakeland residents are of Ukrainian descent, and you can learn about this cultural heritage through music, dance, and food.

Best Time to Visit Lakeland

Lakeland is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique appeal. The summer months (June to August) are the busiest and offer warm temperatures and long days. Fall (September to November) is a great time to experience the changing colors of the leaves and cooler temperatures. Winter (December to March) brings snow and opportunities for winter sports and activities, while spring (April to May) brings the return of wildflowers and migratory birds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakeland

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Lakeland:

Do:

  • Respect the land and cultural heritage of Indigenous people and Ukrainian settlers.
  • Pack for all types of activities and weather.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage or litter behind when visiting natural areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Lakeland is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to take certain precautions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Lakeland

  • Watch for wildlife when hiking and camping.
  • Use caution when participating in water activities, and always wear a life vest.
  • Observe fire bans and respect fire safety restrictions in natural areas.

Health in Lakeland

  • Bring insect repellent to ward off mosquitoes and ticks.
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun when spending time outdoors.
  • Check for ticks after hiking or spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Lakeland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lakeland is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options for getting to the region:

Airports Near Lakeland

  • Edmonton International Airport (YEG)
  • Fort McMurray International Airport (YMM)

Bus Stations Near Lakeland

  • Cold Lake Bus Station
  • Lac La Biche Bus Station

Other Options

  • Many visitors choose to rent a car to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Lakeland

The best way to get around Lakeland is by car, as many of the top attractions are spread out over a large area. There are also some public transit options, including buses and taxi services, in larger towns like Cold Lake and Lac La Biche.

Where to Go Next from Lakeland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue your exploration of Alberta after visiting Lakeland, consider the following destinations:

  • Jasper National Park: Head west from Lakeland to explore the stunning beauty of Jasper National Park, which is home to glacier-fed lakes, mountain peaks, and abundant wildlife.
  • Elk Island National Park: Located just south of Lakeland, Elk Island National Park offers lakeside beaches, hiking trails, and bison viewing opportunities.
  • Edmonton: The capital of Alberta, Edmonton is a vibrant city with a thriving arts and culture scene, top-notch restaurants, and plenty of outdoor recreation opportunities.

Conclusion

Lakeland is a unique destination that’s perfect for those seeking natural beauty, outdoor adventure, and cultural experiences. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to explore all the best that this region has to offer. Enjoy your trip to Lakeland!
