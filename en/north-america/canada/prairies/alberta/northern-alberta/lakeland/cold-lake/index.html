Introduction to Cold Lake

Cold Lake is a beautiful city located in the Lakeland area of Northern Alberta, Canada. The city is situated on the shores of the fourth-largest lake in Alberta, Cold Lake, which spans over 350 km and covers an area of 373.8 square km. The stunning landscape of Cold Lake and surrounding areas offers a range of year-round outdoor activities, incredible natural beauty, and fascinating history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Cold Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cold Lake Museums

One of the must-visit attractions in Cold Lake is the Cold Lake Museums. The museum complex comprises two separate museums, The Heritage Museum and The Air Force Museum. You will find exhibits featuring local history, military memorabilia, artifacts, and photographs that will provide insight into the city’s past.

Cold Lake Marina

Cold Lake Marina is not only an essential stop for boating enthusiasts, but it also offers a fascinating look into the region’s history and culture. The marina features a scenic boardwalk overlooking the lake, and a bronze sculpture “The Beaver” that pays tribute to the region’s fur-trading past.

Natural Attractions of Cold Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kinosoo Ridge Adventure Park

Kinosoo Ridge Adventure Park is a hub of year-round outdoor activities, located near Cold Lake and nearby Bonnie Lake. In winter, you can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, or tubing on the slopes, while in summer you can enjoy hiking, mountain biking, or scenic chairlift rides that showcase the area’s stunning natural beauty.

Cold Lake Provincial Park

Cold Lake Provincial Park is a popular destination among visitors who adore freshwater lakes. The park is located on the southern shore of Cold Lake and offers sandy beaches, natural hiking trails, excellent fishing, and boating opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cold Lake

The Cold Lake area serves as an excellent hiking and biking destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are several hiking and biking trails you can explore that showcase the region’s beauty, including:

Iron Horse Trail

Iron Horse Trail is a 300 km long trail that runs from Waskatenau in the south to Cold Lake in the north. The trail passes through communities, forests, parks, wildlife habitats, and farmlands, displaying the best of Alberta’s natural beauty.

Brady’s Lookout Trail

Brady’s Lookout Trail is a 14 km long hiking trail that offers stunning views of Cold Lake. The trail is moderate in difficulty and suitable for most hikers, and you can enjoy scenic views of the lake and natural landmarks that include the Cold Lake Air Force Bombing Range.

Best Things to Do in Cold Lake: Activities and Tours

Art Walk and Culture Fest

Art Walk and Culture Fest are two annual events in Cold Lake that provide visitors with a vibrant showcase of the city’s culture and arts. The events feature live music, art shows, street vendors, and a range of food trucks, giving visitors the perfect opportunity to experience the city’s art scene while enjoying a truly entertaining day out.

Cold Lake Beach Bash

Every year, the city of Cold Lake hosts the Cold Lake Beach Bash, an event that takes place at Kinosoo Beach. The event features live music, food, beverages, and activities like wakeboarding, tubing, and volleyball for visitors to enjoy.

Food and Drink Scene of Cold Lake

Must-Try Dishes

Kugel

Kugel is a traditional Jewish dish that is quite popular in Cold Lake. It is a baked pudding made from egg noodles, cream of mushroom soup, and cottage cheese, served topped with brown sugar and cinnamon.

Perogies

Perogies are a type of dumpling popular in East European cuisine and a staple in Cold Lake. You can enjoy perogies with various fillings, including cheese and potato, bacon, and sauerkraut, served with sour cream and onion.

Local Beverages

Cold Lake Brewing and Distilling Co.

Cold Lake is home to the popular Cold Lake Brewing and Distilling Co., where you can enjoy locally brewed beers and sample a range of whiskey and vodka spirits. Their sleek taproom offers scenic views of Cold Lake and a full-service restaurant service that serves an array of delicious meals and bar bites.

Where to Stay in Cold Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

With numerous hotels, hostels, and guesthouses, Cold Lake has plenty of accommodations for visitors and tourists. Some popular options include:

Lakeland Inn

Lakeland Inn is a hotel located in downtown Cold Lake, offering spacious and comfortable rooms, fantastic amenities and services, and proximity to local restaurants, shops, and markets.

East Courte Motel

East Courte Motel is a budget-friendly motel located off Highway 28, providing comfortable and clean rooms with complimentary Wifi and parking for visitors on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Cold Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can buy various souvenirs and local products in Cold Lake, showcasing the region’s rich culture and history. Some popular items that you can buy include:

History Books

The Cold Lake Museums store offers an excellent selection of history books that highlight Alberta’s fascinating history, culture, and heritage.

Handcrafted Goods

There are several local shops and markets in Cold Lake that offer handcrafted goods such as jewelry, pottery, and knitted clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Cold Lake

The Cold Lake region is home to cultural diversity, and the community treasures and respects its members and their traditions. The region’s indigenous population has a rich history and culture, and visitors can experience the traditional drumming, dancing, and storytelling of the Cree, Dene and Metis people in different cultural events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Cold Lake

The best time to visit Cold Lake is in the summer months when the weather is mild and sunny, allowing visitors to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, camping, fishing and water sports. The late fall season is also an excellent time to visit as visitors can witness the stunning fall foliage and enjoy various fall-related events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cold Lake

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the region.
  • Keep the area clean and preserve its cleanliness.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Trespass on private property without permission.
  • Cause any disturbance in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cold Lake

Cold Lake is a very safe city, but visitors and tourists should take standard precautions and avoid any risks by staying aware of their surroundings, locking their cars, and taking standard safety measures.

Health in Cold Lake

Cold Lake has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care through several medical clinics, hospitals, and pharmacies in the city.

Getting to Cold Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cold Lake

Cold Lake has its airport, Cold Lake Regional Airport, providing direct commercial flights from major Canadian cities, including Edmonton, Calgary, and Vancouver.

Bus Stations Near Cold Lake

The city of Cold Lake offers Greyhound Bus services that connect the city to other major destinations within Alberta and Canada.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cold Lake from nearby Canadian cities, including Edmonton or Calgary, via highways 28 and 55.

How to Get Around in Cold Lake

Visitors can get around Cold Lake by car, bike, bus, or taxi, and the area also offers several rental car services for tourists who prefer to explore on their own.

Where to Go Next from Cold Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Several nearby destinations and excursions are worth exploring after visiting Cold Lake, including:

Bonnyville

Bonnyville is a town located approximately 20 minutes north of Cold Lake, offering perfect getaways for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy various outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, camping, and fishing.

Lac La Biche

Lac La Biche is a beautiful destination located approximately 90 km south of Cold Lake, known for its stunning scenery, excellent fishing, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Cold Lake Alberta is an excellent destination for visitors seeking to explore Canada’s natural beauty, experience its diverse culture and traditions, and enjoy a range of outdoor activities throughout the year. Use this guide to plan your visit to Cold Lake, and experience its rich history, culture, and natural beauty for yourself.
