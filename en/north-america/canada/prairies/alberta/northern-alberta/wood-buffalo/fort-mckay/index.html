Introduction to Fort McKay

Fort McKay is a rural hamlet in the Regional Municipality of Wood Buffalo in Northern Alberta, Canada. This scenic destination boasts an extensive history dating back to the early 1800s when the fur trade was predominant in the region. Today, Fort McKay is a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions that draw visitors from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve compiled all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Fort McKay. From top attractions and activities to dining and lodging options, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Fort McKay

Fort McKay offers a wide range of attractions for visitors to enjoy. We’ve divided these attractions into two categories - Culturally Important Attractions and Natural Attractions of Fort McKay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Fort McKay can experience the rich history and culture of the region through its various museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Fort McKay Museum: A great place to learn about the history of Fort McKay and the surrounding community. This museum showcases the culture and lifestyle of the indigenous people of the region.

  • Fort McKay Trading Post: This historic trading post dates back to the early 1800s and provides visitors with a glimpse of what life was like during the fur trade era.

  • St. Kateri Tekakwitha Catholic Church: This beautiful church is one of the oldest buildings in Fort McKay and is an important religious site for the local population.

Natural Attractions of Fort McKay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fort McKay is blessed with stunning natural beauty and diverse outdoor activities. Below are some top natural attractions to explore:

  • Birchwood Trails: A popular destination for hiking enthusiasts, the Birchwood Trails offer stunning views of the surrounding nature.

  • Birchwood Golf & Country Club: Located just minutes from Fort McKay, this golf course offers challenging holes in a beautiful natural setting.

  • Gregoire Lake Provincial Park: A beautiful provincial park that offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort McKay

Fort McKay is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking and biking are great ways to explore the region’s natural beauty. Here are some popular trails:

  • Birchwood Trails: This trail system comprises over 130 kilometers of hiking and biking trails through the boreal forest.

  • Gregoire Lake Provincial Park: The park offers several scenic trails that range from easy to challenging.

  • Moose Mountain Trail: A moderate hiking trail that offers amazing views of the Athabasca River.

Best Things to Do in Fort McKay: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to outdoor adventures, Fort McKay offers plenty of things to do for visitors of all ages. Here are some top activities:

  • Indigenous cultural tours: Learn about the rich history and culture of the indigenous people of the region through guided tours of cultural sites.

  • Fishing: The region is known for its abundance of fish, including walleye, northern pike, and lake trout.

  • ATV tours: Explore the rugged terrain around Fort McKay on an ATV tour. Several tour operators in the region offer guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort McKay

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Fort McKay:

Day 1:

  • Learn about the history of the region at the Fort McKay Museum and the Fort McKay Trading Post.

Day 2:

  • Experience the natural beauty of the region by hiking one of the scenic trails in the Birchwood Trails system or Gregoire Lake Provincial Park.

Day 3:

  • Go fishing in one of the many lakes and rivers around Fort McKay.

Day 4:

  • Take an indigenous cultural tour and learn about the traditions and customs of the local people.

Day 5:

  • Play a round of golf at the Birchwood Golf & Country Club.

Day 6:

  • Explore the rugged terrain on an ATV tour.

Day 7:

  • Relax and unwind at one of the local spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort McKay

The food and drink scene in Fort McKay is a unique blend of indigenous and western cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock: A traditional indigenous bread that is often served with soup or stew.

  • Wild berry jams and jellies: Made from locally sourced wild berries, these jams and jellies are a popular souvenir item.

  • Steak and potatoes: A classic western dish that is served in many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Berry tea: Made from local berries, this tea is a popular traditional beverage in Fort McKay.

  • Fort McKay brewery beer: This local microbrewery offers a range of handcrafted beers that showcase the region’s unique flavors.

Where to Stay in Fort McKay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort McKay offers a range of lodging options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Best Western Plus Sawridge Suites: A luxurious hotel that offers comfortable rooms and great amenities, including an on-site restaurant and bar.

  • Birchwood Hotels: Featuring two locations, these hotels offer comfortable rooms, excellent amenities, and friendly service.

  • Fort McKay Guest House: A family-run guest house that offers budget-friendly accommodations and personalized service.

What to Buy in Fort McKay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fort McKay is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some top items to bring home:

  • Indigenous artisan crafts: Local artisans produce a range of traditional crafts, including beadwork, quillwork, and hand-carved wooden items.

  • Wild berry jams and jellies: Made from locally sourced wild berries, these jams and jellies are a popular souvenir item.

  • Fort McKay brewery beer: This local microbrewery offers a range of handcrafted beers that showcase the region’s unique flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort McKay

The indigenous culture is an essential part of Fort McKay’s identity. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the traditions and customs of the local people and to show respect for their culture.

Best Time to Visit Fort McKay

The best time to visit Fort McKay is from May to October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Winter months in Fort McKay can be particularly harsh, with temperatures dipping well below freezing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort McKay

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Bring appropriate clothing for outdoor activities, including hiking boots, warm jackets, and rain gear.

  • Try the local cuisine, including traditional indigenous dishes.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or engage in non-consensual photography or recording.

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Ignore the advice of local guides or park rangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort McKay

Fort McKay is a relatively safe destination, but it is important to take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always carry a map and compass when hiking or exploring remote areas.

  • Stay aware of wildlife in the area and take appropriate precautions, including carrying bear spray.

  • Tell someone your travel itinerary before heading out, including the planned route and expected return time.

Health in Fort McKay

No special health precautions are necessary for visitors to Fort McKay. However, it is always advisable to drink bottled water and carry a basic first aid kit when traveling.

Getting to Fort McKay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort McKay is located approximately 65 km north of Fort McMurray. Here are some transportation options to get there:

Airports Near Fort McKay

  • Fort McMurray International Airport: The closest international airport to Fort McKay, located approximately 70 km south.

  • Edmonton International Airport: Located approximately 425 km south of Fort McKay, this airport offers flights to many major Canadian and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Fort McKay

  • Fort McMurray Greyhound Bus Terminal: Located in downtown Fort McMurray, about 70 km south of Fort McKay.

  • Cold Lake Greyhound Bus Terminal: Located approximately 200 km northeast of Fort McKay.

Other Options

  • Renting a car: Several car rental companies operate out of Fort McMurray and Edmonton, making it easy to rent a car and drive to Fort McKay.

How to Get Around in Fort McKay

The best way to get around Fort McKay is by car. Taxis and rideshare services are also available for short trips within the local region.

Where to Go Next from Fort McKay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort McKay is located near several other popular destinations in Alberta. Here are some nearby excursions and destinations:

  • Fort McMurray: Known for its rich history, this city is a must-visit for any tourist traveling through the area.

  • Wood Buffalo National Park: Just south of Fort McKay, this park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the largest national park in Canada.

  • Jasper National Park: Located approximately 600 km northwest of Fort McKay, this national park is known for its stunning mountain scenery and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Fort McKay is a unique and charming destination that offers visitors an authentic and unforgettable experience. Whether you’re interested in exploring the region’s rich history or experiencing its stunning natural beauty, Fort McKay has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be ready to plan your trip to Fort McKay and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Northern Alberta.
