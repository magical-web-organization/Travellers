Fort Chipewyan is a small hamlet located in the northernmost tip of Alberta, surrounded by pristine wilderness that offers a unique perspective on Canada’s natural beauty. This remote town is nestled on the banks of Lake Athabasca, in Wood Buffalo, Northern Alberta - perfect for an off-the-beaten-track adventure. Plan your trip around Fort Chipewyan with this comprehensive travel guide that covers all the must-see attractions, top-rated activities, and distinctive cultural traits for a wonderful trip.

Introduction to Fort Chipewyan

Fort Chipewyan is a small community known for its natural reserves, scenic beauty, and rich history. At the heart of the town lies the Chipewyan Prairie Dene First Nation, whose culture and traditions are still practiced. Being close to the Athabasca River and the Lake Athabasca, Fort Chipewyan is an ideal spot for outdoor activities. You can also learn about the history of the area and its indigenous people in the town’s museums and cultural centers.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Chipewyan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Chipewyan Museum: This museum houses artifacts and exhibits that explains the history of the town, providing an immersive experience of the folklore, history, and ecosystem of the Athabasca region.

  • John Franklin Museum: Named after the famous explorer, it chronicles the voyages of Franklin, who explored the area between 1816 and 1827. You can learn about the town’s early fur trade routes, history, and culture.

Natural Attractions of Fort Chipewyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wood Buffalo National Park: The largest national park in Canada and the second-largest in the world, Wood Buffalo is home to the only natural nesting ground of the whooping crane in the world and the largest population of free-ranging buffalo in North America, among other animals like muskox and wolves.

  • Lake Athabasca: This lake is known for its serene blue-green waters surrounded by pristine natural forests and wetlands where you can see incredible wildlife like eagles, moose, and beavers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Chipewyan

  • Asphalt Trail: This 2.5-kilometer-long trail follows the Athabasca River, taking in sweeping vistas of the water and lush forests.

  • Birchwood Trail: It is a five-kilometer loop around the town and offers stunning views of Lake Athabasca. You may also encounter wildlife like foxes, lynxes, and deer.

Best Things to Do in Fort Chipewyan: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: You explore Fort Chipewyan’s shoreline, bays, and creeks while spotting bald eagles, beavers, waterfowl and more.

  • Boat Cruise on Lake Athabasca: You can hop on a boat and take a lovely cruise on Lake Athabasca, which not only offers scenic beauty but also the chance to witness otters, beavers, and a variety of waterbirds.

  • Fishing: Fort Chipewyan offers excellent fishing opportunities, with Walleye and Lake Trout being the prominent catches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Chipewyan

Day 1. Arrival day.

Day 2. Go on a hike in the Wood Buffalo National park and take in the breath-taking scenic beauty.

Day 3. Rent a kayak and explore the Athabasca river.

Day 4. Take a boat cruise on Lake Athabasca and enjoy its serene blue-green waters.

Day 5. Visit the John Franklin Museum to learn about the town’s early fur trade routes, history, and culture.

Day 6. Enjoy Fishing in one of the lakes surrounding Fort Chipewyan.

Day 7. Make Your way back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Chipewyan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Traditional bannock: This bread is a staple of indigenous cuisine made from flour, baking powder, lard, and salt.

  • Wild game: Leaning into the hunting culture of Fort Chipewyan, the deer, and game meat dishes are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Dandelion Wine: Made from locally grown dandelions and fermented for 2 months, it’s a unique beverage to try while local.

  • Birch Syrup: Made from the sap of the paper birch trees, it’s a sweet and flavorful traditional local syrup.

Where to Stay in Fort Chipewyan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grosbeak Motel: The motel located on the waterfront of Lake Athabasca provides a beautiful view of the lake and comfortable rooms.

  • Birchwood Guesthouse: This guesthouse provides an excellent stay to experience a secluded, outdoor adventure without compromising on comfort.

What to Buy in Fort Chipewyan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Indigenous Crafts: You can find a unique collection of indigenous art, jewelry, and traditional souvenirs at the John Franklin Museum and Fort Chipewyan Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Chipewyan

The culture of Fort Chipewyan is dominated by the indigenous population located at the heart of the town. The town is close to nature, and the locals are incredibly knowledgeable about the surrounding flora and fauna. They bring the stories of the culture that has existed in the region since time immemorial, so be sure to respect their customs and traditions on your trip.

Best Time to Visit Fort Chipewyan

The best time to visit Fort Chipewyan is during the warmer months (June to August), when the region’s lakes and rivers thaw, and the wildlife is abundant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Chipewyan

Do:

  • Respect the indigenous culture.

  • Pack appropriate outdoor gear.

Don’t:

  • Litter the area during your stay.

  • Hunt without an appropriate license.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Chipewyan

Fort Chipewyan is a safe town, but it’s better to be cautious while out hiking or canoeing. Watch out for wild animals and dress appropriately when heading outdoors.

Health in Fort Chipewyan

Make sure to bring insect repellent, sunscreen, and any medication you may need, like an Epi-pen. Medical aid is limited in the town, so be prepared for an emergency beforehand.

Getting to Fort Chipewyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Chipewyan

The closest airport to Fort Chipewyan is the Fort Chipewyan Airport (YPY).

Bus Stations Near Fort Chipewyan

The town is not connected by road; however, bus services are available from strategic positions to other parts of Alberta.

Other Options

You can get to Fort Chipewyan by air charter or by riverboat (for the adventurous souls).

How to Get Around in Fort Chipewyan

Fort Chipewyan is a small town, and the best way to move around is by walking or cycling.

Where to Go Next from Fort Chipewyan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Athabasca Sand Dunes: These are the largest active sand dunes in the world, covered in white sand that looks like snow. You can take an excursion from Fort Chipewyan to explore them.

Conclusion

Fort Chipewyan is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, especially those interested in learning about indigenous culture. Its remoteness and serenity add to its charm, making it the perfect getaway for a refreshing escape.
