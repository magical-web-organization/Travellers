A Comprehensive Tour Guide to High Level, Alberta

High Level, a small town in Northern Alberta, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Surrounded by stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, High Level offers an unforgettable travel experience for adventure seekers and history buffs alike. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the must-see attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink recommendations, and more.

Introduction to High Level

High Level is a small town located in the Northern part of Alberta. It is home to approximately 3,000 people and is known for its untouched natural beauty. High Level is situated in the middle of the boreal forest and is a gateway to some of the wildest and most beautiful regions in all of Alberta. Its close proximity to numerous outdoor adventures and opportunities to see the Northern Lights make it an ideal destination for those looking for an adventure.

List of Places to Visit in High Level

Here are some of the top places to visit in High Level.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • High Level Heritage Centre: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of High Level and the surrounding areas. It features exhibits on the town’s early settlers, the fur trade, and the oil and gas industry.

  • St. Mark’s Anglican Church: Built in 1958, this church is a testament to the town’s history and heritage.

Natural Attractions of High Level: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sucker Creek: Located just south of High Level, Sucker Creek is a beautiful spot for fishing and hiking. The area is known for its scenic views and abundance of wildlife.

  • High Level Golf Course: This 18-hole golf course is surrounded by stunning natural beauty and is a great opportunity to get some exercise and enjoy the scenery.

  • Paddle Prairie Provincial Park: This park is known for its stunning scenery and incredible opportunities for hiking, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of High Level

High Level is an ideal destination for those interested in hiking or biking. Here are some of our top hiking and biking routes:

  • Sucker Creek Trail: This 18-kilometer trail runs along the Sucker Creek Valley and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and wildlife.

  • Twin Lakes Trail: This 10-kilometer trail is an easy hike and takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the area.

Best Things to Do in High Level: Activities and Tours

High Level offers a variety of activities for adventure seekers. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Northern Lights Tour: High Level is one of the best places in Alberta to see the Northern Lights. Book a tour with a local guide to get the best experience.

  • Canoeing/Kayaking: The region surrounding High Level is known for its beautiful lakes and rivers, making it an ideal destination for canoeing and kayaking.

  • ATV Tour: For those looking for a bit more excitement, an ATV tour is a great way to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for High Level

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to High Level:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore High Level Heritage Centre and St. Mark’s Anglican Church
Day 2 Hike or bike the Sucker Creek Trail
Day 3 Take a Northern Lights tour
Day 4 Go canoeing or kayaking on one of the nearby lakes
Day 5 Visit Paddle Prairie Provincial Park and Twin Lakes Trail
Day 6 Go on an ATV tour
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the scenery

Food and Drink Scene of High Level

High Level is home to a variety of delicious restaurants and local breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your itinerary.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bear Paw Cafe: This local diner is known for its delicious homemade pies and hearty breakfast dishes.

  • Kap’s Kitchen: For a taste of Alberta’s signature dishes, head to Kap’s Kitchen. Their menu features dishes made with locally sourced ingredients, including Alberta beef and bison.

Local Beverages

  • High Level Brewing Co.: This local brewery is known for its craft beers, brewed right in the heart of High Level.

Where to Stay in High Level: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

High Level offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Days Inn by Wyndham High Level: This hotel features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a hot tub.

  • Riverview Lodge: For a more rustic option, stay at the Riverview Lodge, located on the banks of the Peace River.

What to Buy in High Level: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a souvenir to remember your trip to High Level? Here are some options for local products to bring home:

  • Northern Woods and Wildlife: This local gift shop offers a variety of artisanal gifts, including hand-carved wooden figurines and native art.

  • High Level Honey: For a sweet taste of Northern Alberta, pick up a jar of locally sourced honey from High Level Honey.

Local Culture and Customs of High Level

High Level is home to a rich cultural heritage, including the Cree and Dene First Nations. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, including the importance of respecting the land and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit High Level

The best time to visit High Level is between May and October, when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. High Level is also one of the best places in the world to see the Northern Lights, so plan your trip accordingly if you’re interested in this natural wonder.

Do’s and Don’ts of High Level

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting High Level:

Do:

  • Respect the land and wildlife.

  • Follow all park and trail rules and regulations.

  • Hire a local guide for the best Northern Lights experience.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

  • Touch or disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in High Level

High Level is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions, especially when hiking or exploring the outdoors. Always carry a first aid kit and a map, and let someone know where you’re going in case of an emergency.

Health in High Level

High Level does not have a hospital, but it does have a health center to treat minor injuries and illnesses. Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications with them.

Getting to High Level: Transportation Options and Logistics

High Level is accessible by air and car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near High Level

  • High Level Airport (YOJ): Located just outside of town, the High Level Airport offers daily flights to Calgary and Edmonton.

Bus Stations Near High Level

  • Greyhound Bus: The Greyhound Bus station is located in High Prairie, a neighboring town of High Level.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at any of the major airports or in nearby towns like Fort Vermilion or Peace River.

How to Get Around in High Level

The best way to get around High Level is by car, as the area is quite spread out. Visitors can also rent bikes for easy access to hiking and biking trails.

Where to Go Next from High Level: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

High Level is located near some popular destinations in Northern Alberta. Here are some of our top recommendations for nearby excursions:

  • Manning Provincial Park: Located just two hours south of High Level, Manning Provincial Park is known for its beautiful landscapes and abundant wildlife.

  • Wood Buffalo National Park: This park is located just a few hours east of High Level and is home to the largest buffalo herd in North America.

Conclusion

High Level, Alberta, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and abundance of outdoor activities, it’s an ideal destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike. Follow our comprehensive tour guide for the ultimate High Level experience.
