A Comprehensive Guide to Peace Country, Alberta, Canada

Welcome to Peace Country, a beautiful region located in Northern Alberta, in Prairies, in Canada, in North America. This travel guide will take you on a journey to discover the top tourist spots, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, food and drink scene, accommodations, and much more. Let’s dive in.

Introduction to Peace Country

Peace Country is a region located in northern Alberta, and it’s home to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in Canada. Rolling hills, sparkling lakes, and winding rivers are just some of the features you’ll find here, along with charming communities and friendly locals.

The region takes its name from the Peace River, which is one of the longest rivers in Canada. The river is a major waterway for the region, providing opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and other water-based activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dawson Creek Art Gallery

Best Things to Do in Peace Country: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for adventure, then Peace Country has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some top picks:

  • Whitewater Rafting on the Peace River
  • Fishing for Northern Pike and Trout
  • Horseback Riding through the Countryside
  • Golfing at the Dunes Golf and Winter Club

Local Beverages

  • Peace Country Coffee from the Mighty Peace Coffee Co.

Where to Stay in Peace Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peace Country has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some options to consider:

  • Stonebridge Hotel - A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and amenities in Grand Prairie.
  • Northern Lights Hostel - A cozy hostel located in the heart of Dawson Creek.
  • Bear Creek Lodge - A rustic cabin-style lodge with stunning views of Bear Creek Provincial Park.

What to Buy in Peace Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Being in Peace Country, there are a few things you should bring home with you. Here are some local products to consider:

  • Handmade Pottery from the Peace River Pottery
  • Bison or Elk Jerky from local artisans
  • Peace Country Honey, produced by local beekeepers
  • Organic Tea from the Gindara Tea Company

Local Culture and Customs of Peace Country

Peace Country is home to a diverse population, and communities maintain unique cultures and traditions. Some of the most notable customs include:

  • The Powwow: A cultural celebration for Indigenous people celebrating dance, music, and traditional dress.
  • The Rodeo: A Western-style competition featuring events such as bull riding and barrel racing.
  • Winter Festivals: Celebrations that showcase the region’s winter sports and traditions, such as ice fishing and snowmobiling.

Best Time to Visit Peace Country

The best time to visit Peace Country is in the summer (June to August) when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, winter (December to February) also has its charms, such as skiing, ice fishing, and winter festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peace Country

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Peace Country:

Do:

  • Respect local cultures and traditions.
  • Bring appropriate clothing for the weather.
  • Take your time and explore the region’s hidden gems.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage sensitive environments.
  • Ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect wildlife or engage in dangerous encounters.

Safety and Health

Peace Country is generally a safe region to visit, but there are some health and safety considerations to keep in mind.

Safety in Peace Country

  • When engaging in outdoor activities, always check the weather forecast and be prepared for changing conditions.
  • Follow the safety guidelines of each activity, such as wearing a lifejacket when rafting or carrying bear spray when hiking.
  • Be aware of wildlife and take precautions to avoid dangerous encounters.

Health in Peace Country

  • It’s always a good idea to bring a first-aid kit when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Make sure to stay hydrated and protect your skin with sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any unusual symptoms or injuries.

Getting to Peace Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Peace Country region is well-connected with several options available to get there.

Airports Near Peace Country

  • Grande Prairie Airport (YQU)
  • Dawson Creek Airport (YDQ)
  • Fort St. John Airport (YXJ)

Bus Stations Near Peace Country

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal - Grande Prairie
  • Dawson Creek Bus Depot

Other Options

  • Drive: Peace Country is well connected with major roads and highways.
  • Train: VIA Rail has several train stations around the region

How to Get Around in Peace Country

The best way to explore Peace Country is by car as the region is spread out and there are many hidden gems to discover. However, there are also shuttle bus services available for major tourist sites like Kakwa Wildland Provincial Park.

Where to Go Next from Peace Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Peace Country is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the natural beauty and cultural richness of northern Alberta. From hiking trails and outdoor adventures to museums and historical sites, this region has it all.
