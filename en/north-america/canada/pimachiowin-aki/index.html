Introduction to Pimachiowin Aki

Pimachiowin Aki is a vast forest landscape located in the eastern region of Manitoba and Ontario in Canada. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that boasts of naturally diverse boreal forests that are home to rare animal species such as woodland caribou, wolverines, and bald eagles. In addition to its natural beauty, Pimachiowin Aki’s rich cultural heritage comprises of several Indigenous communities that have lived in the area for thousands of years.

If you plan on visiting Pimachiowin Aki, then this comprehensive guide will show you some of the best things to do and see in this beautiful Canadian landscape.

List of Places to Visit in Pimachiowin Aki

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manitoba Museum - This museum is home to different exhibits that showcase the culture, history, and natural features of Manitoba and the surrounding area.
  • Gimli Heritage Park Museum - This museum is located in the town of Gimli and showcases the history of the Icelandic settlers who first arrived in the area.
  • Whiteshell Provincial Park - This popular destination features several unique attractions such as ancient aboriginal rock paintings, the Bannock Point Petroforms, and several hiking and camping sites.

Natural Attractions of Pimachiowin Aki: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Atikaki Provincial Park - This park is situated in the boreal forests north of Pimachiowin Aki and offers a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, canoeing, fishing, and wildlife tours.
  • Boreal Forest Trail - This trail meanders through the heart of Pimachiowin Aki and displays the natural beauty of the boreal forests and the wildlife that call it home.
  • Rushing River Provincial Park - This park is situated on the shores of Dogtooth Lake and boasts of several beautiful scenic views and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pimachiowin Aki

Hiking Trails

  • Mantario Trail - This 60-kilometer trail is one of the most popular hiking trails in Manitoba and passes through rugged terrain, countless lakes, and streams.
  • Boreal Trail - This 120-kilometer trail is a backcountry hiking trail that provides an immersive experience of the boreal forest and its unique wildlife.

Biking Routes

  • Great Trail - This trail is one of the world’s longest trails and runs through Pimachiowin Aki, offering an opportunity for bikers to explore the beautiful scenery of the boreal forests.
  • Trans-Canada Trail - This trail is Canada’s national multi-use trail and passes through different scenic routes in Pimachiowin Aki, giving bikers an unforgettable experience.

Best Things to Do in Pimachiowin Aki: Activities and Tours
