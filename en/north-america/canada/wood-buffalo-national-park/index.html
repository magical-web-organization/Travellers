Introduction to Wood Buffalo National Park

Wood Buffalo National Park, located in Canada’s Northwest Territories, is the largest national park in the country and one of the largest in the world, covering an area of 17,300 square miles. The park was established in 1922 to protect the last remaining herd of wood bison and the park is the only place in the world where you can see and experience these magnificent animals in their natural habitat. In addition to the wood bison, the park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including black bears, grizzlies, wolves, and moose.

The park has a rich indigenous history, with the Dene, Cree, and Métis people having lived and hunted in the region for thousands of years. Visitors to the park can learn about the indigenous culture and history at various cultural attractions and events.

List of Places to Visit in Wood Buffalo National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Fort Smith Mission Historic Park - A historic site featuring a museum and interpretive center that tells the history of the indigenous peoples, missionaries, and fur traders of the region.
  • The Heritage Village - An open-air museum that showcases the traditional lifestyle and culture of the region’s inhabitants, including the Dene, Cree, and Métis. Visitors can see traditional crafts, historic buildings, and artifacts.
  • Peace Park - A monument created by the indigenous people and settlers of Fort Smith to celebrate peace and reconciliation.

Natural Attractions of Wood Buffalo National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salt Plains - A unique geological formation that creates a landscape unlike any other in the world. Visitors can explore the salt flats, which are home to a variety of wildlife.
  • Pine Lake - A beautiful lake with crystal clear water that is perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Buffalo Paddling Trail - A scenic canoe route that takes visitors through the heart of the park’s wilderness.
  • Caribou Mountains - A mountain range that provides some of the best hiking and backpacking opportunities in the park. Visitors can see stunning views, crystal-clear lakes, and glaciers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wood Buffalo National Park

  • Salt Plains Trail - A 10-kilometer hiking trail that takes visitors through the park’s unique salt flats.
  • Centennial Trail - A 3.7-kilometer hiking trail that starts at Pine Lake and takes visitors through a beautiful forested area.
  • Pine Lake Trail - A 6-kilometer hiking trail that loops around Pine Lake.
  • Buffalo Paddling Trail - A scenic 170-kilometer canoe route that takes visitors through the heart of the park’s wilderness.
  • Caribou Mountains Trail - A 60-kilometer backcountry hiking trail that takes visitors through the beautiful Caribou Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Wood Buffalo National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Viewing - The Wood Buffalo National Park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, with the wood bison being the main attraction. Visitors can see these magnificent animals in their natural habitat, as well as black bears, grizzlies, wolves, and moose.
  • Boating and Fishing - The park has several lakes that are perfect for boating and fishing, including Pine Lake and the Slave River.
  • Camping - The park has several campgrounds, ranging from primitive to fully-equipped.
  • Backpacking - The park offers several backcountry hiking trails, including the Caribou Mountains Trail.
  • Canoeing/Kayaking - The park has several canoe routes, including the Buffalo Paddling Trail.
  • Northern Lights Viewing - The park is one of the best places in the world to see the Northern Lights, with auroras visible from late August to early May.

Food and Drink Scene of Wood Buffalo National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock - A traditional indigenous bread that is often served with soup or stew.
  • Saskatoon berries - A sweet and tart berry that is indigenous to the region.
  • Wild game - The wood bison, elk, and caribou are popular meats in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Arctic Berry Juice - A refreshing juice made from locally-grown arctic berries.
  • Northern Frontier Coffee - A coffee roaster based in Fort Smith that specializes in high-quality, sustainably sourced coffee.

Where to Stay in Wood Buffalo National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Wood Buffalo Inn - A comfortable hotel located in Fort Smith that offers a range of room types and on-site dining options.
  • The Backpackers’ Hostel - A cozy backpacker’s hostel located in Fort Smith that offers affordable accommodation and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Guesthouses - There are several guesthouses located throughout the park, offering affordable, comfortable accommodation.

What to Buy in Wood Buffalo National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Birch Bark Baskets - A traditional indigenous craft that is unique to the region.
  • Fur and Leather Goods - The fur trade played a significant role in the history of the region, and today, visitors can purchase high-quality fur and leather goods.
  • Indigenous Artwork - The park features several galleries and shops that sell traditional indigenous artwork, including paintings, carvings, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Wood Buffalo National Park

The indigenous culture has a significant influence on the region, and visitors to the park are encouraged to learn about their traditions and customs. Visitors should also respect the land and wildlife, follow leave-no-trace principles, and adhere to any cultural protocols when visiting indigenous areas or cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Wood Buffalo National Park

The best time to visit Wood Buffalo National Park is in the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and the wildlife is more active. However, visitors can also see the Northern Lights, and enjoy cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and ice fishing in the winter months from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wood Buffalo National Park

Do:

  • Respect the land, wildlife, and indigenous culture
  • Follow leave-no-trace principles
  • Observe park rules and regulations
  • Visit cultural attractions and learn about indigenous culture
  • Enjoy outdoor activities and wildlife viewing opportunities

Don’t:

  • Feed wildlife
  • Leave litter or damage the environment
  • Trespass into indigenous territories
  • Disturb or disrupt any cultural events or ceremonies

Safety and Health

Safety in Wood Buffalo National Park

  • Follow all park safety regulations
  • Be aware of wildlife and keep a safe distance
  • Come prepared for any weather conditions

Health in Wood Buffalo National Park

  • Bring all necessary medication and medical supplies
  • Stay hydrated and well-rested
  • Be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Wood Buffalo National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wood Buffalo National Park

  • Fort Smith Airport - The closest airport to the park, located in the town of Fort Smith.

Bus Stations Near Wood Buffalo National Park

  • Greyhound Canada - Provides service to Fort Smith, which is the closest town to the park.

Other Options

  • Driving - The park is accessible via Highway 5, which runs from the town of Hay River to Fort Smith.
  • Charter Plane - Visitors can also charter a plane from nearby cities to the Fort Smith airport.

How to Get Around in Wood Buffalo National Park

The best way to get around the park is by car, as many of the attractions are spread out throughout the park. However, visitors can also access some areas of the park by canoe, kayak, or hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Wood Buffalo National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nahanni National Park Reserve - Another beautiful national park located in the Northwest Territories, known for its stunning waterfalls and river canyon.
  • Yellowknife - The largest city in the Northwest Territories, known for its rich indigenous culture, Northern Lights, and aurora viewing tours.

Conclusion

Wood Buffalo National Park is a truly unique destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the beauty of Canada’s wild landscape, indigenous culture, and wildlife. Whether you are interested in hiking, wildlife viewing, or learning about the region’s history and culture, Wood Buffalo National Park has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip with ease and make the most out of their time in this magnificent park.
