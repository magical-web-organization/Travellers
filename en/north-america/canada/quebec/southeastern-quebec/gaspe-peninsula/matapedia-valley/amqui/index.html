Amqui is a charming town located in the heart of the Matapédia Valley in Gaspé Peninsula, Southeastern Quebec, Canada. With a population of just over 6,000, Amqui offers visitors a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Whether you want to explore the great outdoors, learn about local history and culture, or simply unwind and relax, Amqui has something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide to Amqui, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to this hidden gem of Quebec.

List of Places to Visit in Amqui

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amqui is home to several museums and historical sites that offer insights into the town’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Amqui:

  1. Musée régional de la Matapédia - This museum showcases the history and culture of the Matapédia Valley. Visitors can learn about the area’s First Nations heritage, the arrival of European settlers, and the development of the local economy.
  2. Église Sainte-Jeanne-d’Arc - This beautiful church is one of the most important landmarks in Amqui. Built in the early 20th century, it features stunning stained-glass windows and impressive Gothic architecture.
  3. Musée de la Gare d’Amqui - Housed in a restored train station, this museum tells the story of the railway in the Matapédia Valley. Visitors can see vintage trains and learn about the impact of the railway on the region.

Natural Attractions of Amqui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Amqui is surrounded by natural beauty, with lush forests, sparkling lakes, and the Matapédia River in the nearby area. Here are some natural attractions that you won’t want to miss:

  1. Parc national de la Gaspésie - Located just a short drive from Amqui, this national park is a hiker’s paradise. With over 140 km of trails of varying difficulty, visitors can explore stunning peaks, valleys, and waterfalls and get an opportunity to spot moose, caribou, and other wildlife.
  2. Mont Blanc - This nearby mountain offers breathtaking views of the Matapédia Valley and surrounding foothills and is an excellent place for a scenic hike or bike ride.
  3. Rivière Matapédia - The river is ideal for fishing and kayaking, with its calm waters and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amqui

Amqui is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, with various hiking and biking routes to enjoy the scenic beauty of the region. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in Amqui are:

  1. Sentier pédestre de la Coulée douce - This 9-km trail is perfect for hiking and biking, with its lush forest and scenic views of the surrounding hills.
  2. Parc des Mille Lieux de la colline - This park offers various trails of varying lengths and difficulty levels for hiking and biking, with stunning views of the Matapédia Valley.
  3. Montagne Blanche Trail - This trail is a challenging 18-km hike that leads to the peak of Mont Blanc, with breathtaking views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Amqui: Activities and Tours

Amqui offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Amqui:

  1. Canoe/Kayak Tour - Take a river tour and see the beauty of Rivière Matapédia up close.
  2. Snowmobiling/ATV Tour - Enjoy the winter or summer in Amqui with outdoor activities like snowmobiling and ATV tours.
  3. Cultural Tours - Book tours for a chance to explore the town’s unique local culture, attend concerts and exhibitions, and immerse yourself in the rich history of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Amqui

Amqui offers visitors a chance to savor traditional dishes with the freshest local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks while you’re in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Torteau des Rois - A traditional dessert of the Gaspé Peninsula, made of pastry cream and a flaky crust, filled with almond paste.
  2. Pâté à la viande - A meat pie that is a staple dish in Quebec cuisine.
  3. Cipaille - A hearty meat and vegetable dish that originated in the Gaspé region.

Local Beverages

  1. Micro-brasserie La Captive - A local brewery that offers various types of beer, using only locally grown barley and hops.
  2. Boisson énergisante Matapédi - A local energy drink made from the North American ginseng root.

Where to Stay in Amqui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amqui offers various accommodation options for visitors of all budgets. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  1. Motel Le Gaspesiana - This motel features an outdoor pool, onsite restaurant, and spacious, comfortable rooms.
  2. Auberge Beauséjour - An intimate, family-run inn that offers cozy rooms and homemade breakfast.
  3. Gîte de la rivière Matapédia - A charming bed and breakfast located on the banks of the river Matapédia.

What to Buy in Amqui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home various souvenirs and local products from Amqui that are unique to the region. Here are some recommended souvenirs to bring home:

  1. Maple Syrup - Quebec is famous for its pure and organic maple syrup, and Amqui has its own producers.
  2. Hand-Knitted Sweaters - The Gaspé region is known for its high-quality wool, and you can find excellent hand-knitted wool sweaters and other woolen products in town.
  3. Matapédia Trout - The region is well known for its high-quality trout, and the local markets offer fresh and smoked trout to take back with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Amqui

The town has a rich cultural heritage, derived from its history of First Nations peoples and settlers from France and elsewhere. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s cultural customs, such as learning a few words of French before arrival and taking off your shoes before entering someone’s home. Additionally, locals are known for their warm hospitality and kindness to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Amqui

The best time to visit Amqui is in the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures are mild and the town hosts various cultural events and celebrations. However, winter is also an excellent time to visit if you enjoy snow sports and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amqui

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region and take the time to enjoy the great outdoors.
  • Learn about the town’s cultural heritage and visit the local museums and landmarks.
  • Try traditional Gaspé cuisine and local beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places and natural environments.
  • Do not disturb local wildlife and respect their natural habitats.
  • Do not take souvenirs from protected areas and cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amqui

Canada is generally considered a safe country, and Amqui is no exception. Visitors should take the same precautions they would take in any unfamiliar area.

Health in Amqui

The Canadian health care system is known to be one of the best in the world. However, visitors are encouraged to ensure they have proper health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Amqui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amqui

The nearest airport to Amqui is the Mont-Joli Airport, located approximately 52 km from town. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to reach their accommodations.

Bus Stations Near Amqui

There is no bus service that goes directly to Amqui, but visitors can take coach service to nearby towns of Rimouski, Matane, and Mont-Joli and rent a car or take a taxi from there.

Other Options

Visitors can drive themselves to Amqui, as the town is accessible by car via Quebec Route 132.

How to Get Around in Amqui

The best way to get around in Amqui is by car, as public transport service is limited. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available, but they can be costly.

Where to Go Next from Amqui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of the Gaspé Peninsula, nearby destinations include the towns of Matane, Rimouski, and Carleton-sur-Mer. Other popular excursions include visiting the nearby Percé Rock in the Gaspésie National Park.

Conclusion

Amqui is a hidden gem in Quebec, offering visitors a chance to discover the natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality of this lesser-known town. We hope this comprehensive travel guide to Amqui helps you plan your trip and make memories that will last a lifetime.
