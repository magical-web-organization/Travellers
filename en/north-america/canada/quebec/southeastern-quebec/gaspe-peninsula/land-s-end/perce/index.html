Introduction to Percé

Percé is a small town located in the Gaspé Peninsula in southeastern Quebec, Canada. It is known for its incredible natural beauty, including the famous Percé Rock, a massive monolith rising out of the Gulf of Saint Lawrence that has become an iconic symbol of Quebec. Visitors to Percé can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Percé

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Percé boasts a number of museums and historical sites that are worth visiting to learn more about the region’s rich past. The Musée Le Chafaud offers a fascinating look at the history of fishing in the region, while the Maison du Pêcheur, a restored fisherman’s house, provides an up-close look at traditional fishing methods. The nearby Forillon National Park is home to the Fort Peninsula, where visitors can explore an historic military fort and learn about the area’s role in World War II.

Natural Attractions of Percé: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Percé is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities. The iconic Percé Rock is perhaps the most famous attraction, and visitors can take boat tours to see it up close. The nearby Bonaventure Island is home to a large colony of Northern Gannets, providing an unforgettable wildlife viewing experience. Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore the many trails of Forillon National Park, while sea kayakers can paddle along the coastline for a different perspective on the area’s beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Percé

Forillon National Park is home to many hiking and biking trails of varying difficulty levels and lengths. The Les Crêtes Trail offers stunning panoramic views of the coastline, while the Cap-Bon-Ami Trail leads hikers to a beautiful lookout overlooking the Gulf of St. Lawrence. The Mont Saint-Alban Trail is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with breathtaking vistas of the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Percé: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Percé have a range of activities and tours to choose from. Scuba diving enthusiasts can explore the underwater world of the Gulf of St. Lawrence, while whale-watching tours provide unforgettable encounters with the majestic creatures of the sea. A visit to the nearby Parc National de la Gaspésie offers opportunities for wildlife viewing, camping, and hiking in spectacular mountain terrain. For those looking for a more leisurely experience, Percé’s beaches offer sunbathing, swimming, and picnicking opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Percé

Day 1: Arrive in Percé and explore the town’s many shops and restaurants

Day 2: Take a boat tour to see the iconic Percé Rock up close

Day 3: Hike or bike through the many trails of Forillon National Park

Day 4: Visit Bonaventure Island to see the Northern Gannet colony and explore its trails

Day 5: Discover the local food scene with a culinary tour of Percé

Day 6: Go whale-watching to see these majestic creatures in their natural habitat

Day 7: Relax on one of Percé’s beaches and enjoy the stunning views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence

Food and Drink Scene of Percé

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors to Percé should definitely try some of the local seafood specialties, including Atlantic salmon, lobster, crab, and shrimp. The region is also known for its delicious smoked fish and locally made cheeses. For those with a sweet tooth, maple syrup is a popular ingredient in many desserts and pastries.

Local Beverages

The Gaspé Peninsula boasts several microbreweries that produce some excellent craft beers. Visitors can also enjoy delicious wines made from locally grown grapes, as well as a variety of fruit wines and ciders.

Where to Stay in Percé: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Percé have a wide variety of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Many of the hotels in the area offer stunning views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence, while guesthouses provide a more intimate and authentic experience of local life.

What to Buy in Percé: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Percé can bring home a range of souvenirs and locally made products, including artisanal cheeses, maple syrup, and smoked fish. Local artists and craftsmen also produce beautiful handcrafted items, such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Percé

Percé’s rich cultural heritage is reflected in its many festivals and events, including the International Writers’ and Artists’ Festival, the Percé Photo Festival, and the Traditional Music Festival. Visitors to the area should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as expressing gratitude with a “merci beaucoup” and removing shoes when entering a home or temple.

Best Time to Visit Percé

The best time to visit Percé is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the many outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors should be prepared for large crowds and higher prices during this peak season. Fall foliage is also a beautiful time to visit, with the leaves turning vibrant shades of red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Percé

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and tours available
  • Try the local seafood specialties

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in public spaces
  • Ignore safety warnings when engaging in outdoor activities
  • Be disrespectful to wildlife or natural habitats

Safety and Health

Safety in Percé

Percé is generally a safe place to visit, although visitors should exercise caution when engaging in outdoor activities and be aware of local wildlife. It is also important to follow safety guidelines when using public transportation or walking alone at night.

Health in Percé

Visitors to Percé should ensure that they have adequate medical insurance before arriving in Canada, as healthcare costs can be expensive. It is also important to take precautions against tick bites when hiking in the forests and to ensure that food and water are safe to consume.

Getting to Percé: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Percé

The closest airport to Percé is the Gaspé Airport, which offers daily flights to Montreal. Visitors can also fly into Quebec City or Halifax and make the approximately six-hour drive to Percé.

Bus Stations Near Percé

Greyhound Canada operates buses to the nearby city of Gaspé, where visitors can take a local shuttle or taxi to Percé.

Other Options

Driving is also a popular option for visitors to Gaspé, as it provides greater flexibility in exploring the region. Car rentals are available at the airport, in Montreal, and in Quebec City.

How to Get Around in Percé

Percé is a small town that is easily explored on foot. Visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas can rent bicycles, cars, or participate in guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Percé: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Percé can use the town as a base for exploring the other beautiful areas of the Gaspé Peninsula. Nearby destinations include the Parc National de la Gaspésie, the Magdalen Islands, and the charming town of Carleton-sur-Mer.

Conclusion

A trip to Percé offers visitors the chance to explore the region’s rich cultural heritage and unparalleled natural beauty. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and delicious local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming Quebec town.
