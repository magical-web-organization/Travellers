Discovering Gaspé: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Gaspé, located in the Land’s End region of Quebec’s Gaspé Peninsula, is a hidden gem that offers a wide range of activities for all types of travelers. With its rich history, breathtaking scenery, and charming culture, Gaspé has become a popular vacation destination for those looking for an escape from the city. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs and culture of Gaspé.

List of Places to Visit in Gaspé

Gaspé is a beautiful destination with plenty of attractions to explore. Here are some of the best places to visit in Gaspé:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of Gaspe – This museum showcases the history of Gaspé and its surrounding areas, featuring exhibits on the history of fishing, agriculture, and shipbuilding.
  2. Fort Peninsula – A National Historic Site of Canada, Fort Peninsula is a military installation that played a significant role in the history of Gaspé during the colonial period.
  3. The Church of Saint Michael – Built in the 19th century, this historic church is an architectural gem that showcases the local history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Gaspé: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Forillon National Park – This park is located at the tip of the Gaspé Peninsula and offers breathtaking scenery and outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and kayaking.
  2. Percé Rock – This natural wonder, located offshore from the town of Percé, is a massive limestone formation that provides stunning views and photo opportunities.
  3. Bonaventure Island – This beautiful island is a popular birdwatching destination, with thousands of gannets and puffins nesting on its shores during the summer months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gaspé

Exploring Gaspé on foot or by bicycle is a fantastic way to experience its natural beauty. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Gaspé include:

  1. La Route Verte – Quebec’s premier cycling network boasts over 5,000 kilometers of bike paths, and the Gaspé leg is considered to be among the most scenic.
  2. International Appalachian Trail – Part of the world-renowned Appalachian Trail, this section boasts rugged terrain and stunning views of the nearby coastline.
  3. Mount Albert Trail – A challenging hike that rewards those who make it to the top with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Gaspé: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Gaspé, and these activities are sure to satisfy any travelers’ interests:

  1. Whale Watching – Gaspé Bay is an excellent place to spot whales. Companies like Croisières Baie de Gaspé offer whale-watching tours for visitors to get up close and personal with these majestic creatures.
  2. Fishing – Gaspé is well known for its Atlantic salmon fishing, and a guided fishing trip on the York River or Grande River is a must for fishing enthusiasts.
  3. Sea Kayaking – With its stunning coastline, Gaspé is the perfect destination for exploring the sea from a kayak. Many local companies offer guided tours along the coast.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gaspé

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Gaspé, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in the area:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Gaspé, spend the day exploring the town, and enjoy a relaxing dinner at one of the local restaurants.
Day 2 Take a whale-watching tour in the morning, followed by a visit to the Museum of Gaspé in the afternoon.
Day 3 Spend the day hiking in Forillon National Park, taking in the views from some of the park’s most scenic outlooks.
Day 4 Take a guided fishing trip on the York River or Grande River and enjoy a fresh-caught fish dinner in the evening.
Day 5 Explore the picturesque town of Percé, take a boat tour to see Percé Rock up close, and enjoy a delicious seafood lunch.
Day 6 Kayak along the coast and explore some of the hidden coves and beaches that can only be reached by water.
Day 7 Take it easy, enjoy a leisurely breakfast, and pack up for your departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Gaspé

Gaspé’s cuisine is known for its fresh seafood and local products. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Smoked Salmon – Gaspé is known for its delicious smoked salmon that can be found in many local restaurants and markets.
  2. Gaspé Lobster – Freshly caught lobster is a delicacy in Gaspé and can be enjoyed in many local restaurants.
  3. Grilled Trout – Trout is a popular local fish that is often cooked on a grill or over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  1. Microbrasserie Le Malbord – Located in Gaspé’s historic district, this craft brewery offers a variety of delicious beers made with local products.
  2. Cidrerie et Vergers Pedneault – This apple orchard is famous for producing high-quality cider and apple-based products that can be enjoyed in their on-site café.
  3. Café des Artistes – This quaint café is a popular spot for coffee-lovers and is known for its fair-trade, organic coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Gaspé: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gaspé offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all travellers’ budgets, including:

  1. Hotel Plante – Located in the heart of Gaspé, this hotel offers modern amenities and a full-service restaurant.
  2. Auberge de La Grande Vallée - This charming guesthouse is located in the Percé region and offers an unforgettable experience with a mix of quiet rural scenery and bustling coastal towns.
  3. Gaspé Peninsula Hostel – Located in Gaspé’s historic district, this hostel offers affordable accommodations, communal spaces, and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Gaspé: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gaspé is known for its local products and unique souvenirs. Here are a few ideas of what to bring home:

  1. Maple Syrup - Bring home some of Gaspé’s most flavorful, sustainably harvested, and organic maple syrup to keep a taste of Gaspé with you for a while.
  2. Gaspesian Products - Support the local artisans by purchasing handcrafted pottery from near the Gulf or woven textiles made by a local artisan.
  3. Smoked Salmon - Don’t forget to bring some of Gaspé’s locally prepared smoked salmon back home with you - your family will thank you!

Local Culture and Customs of Gaspé

Gaspé is known for its friendly and welcoming community. Locals are proud of their heritage and culture, often celebrating it by participating in annual events throughout the year. Some notable customs and cultural traditions include:

  1. Fiddling Music: Fiddlers are common at local community events, and traditional music can be heard throughout the town.
  2. Indigenous Culture: The region has a rich Indigenous history, and traditional practices are still celebrated today through ceremonies and festivals.
  3. Craftsmanship: From woodworking and textiles to jewelry-making and pottery, Gaspé’s artisans produce high-quality, handcrafted products that showcase the region’s cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Gaspé

The best time to visit Gaspé is from early June to mid-October. During the summer months, the weather is pleasant, and the hiking trails, beaches, and other outdoor attractions are open for visitors to enjoy. Fall in Gaspé is also a beautiful time of year, with the changing leaves providing a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Winter in the region can be harsh making it less optimal to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gaspé

When visiting Gaspé, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  1. Learn some French: Although many locals speak English, knowing some basic French phrases will go a long way in showing your respect for the local culture.
  2. Respect traditions and customs: Be mindful and respectful of Gaspé’s culture and customs, such as Indigenous practices or traditional fiddle music events.
  3. Get out in nature: Gaspé’s beautiful scenery and outdoor activities are not to be missed!

Don’t:

  1. Litter: Gaspé is known for its natural beauty; please help preserve it by properly disposing of your trash.
  2. Ignore local rules: Make sure to follow the regulations at local parks and attractions, such as park hours, hiking rules and fishing regulations.
  3. Overfill your itinerary: Allow yourself some leisurely time to enjoy the atmosphere and beauty of the region.

Safety and Health

Gaspé is a relatively safe destination for travellers. However, as with any travel, it is important to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Gaspé

  1. Avoid isolated areas after dark.
  2. Always lock your car doors, and keep your valuables out of sight.
  3. Know the weather conditions and listen to warnings by local officials regarding changing weather and changing trail conditions.

Health in Gaspé

  1. Apply bug spray when necessary, especially during the summer months.
  2. Take care when hiking, as challenging terrain is common. Wear hiking boots to avoid injury and take plenty of water on your hike.
  3. Bring sunscreen to protect against the sun’s strong rays.

Getting to Gaspé: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located at the tip of the Gaspé Peninsula, getting to Gaspé requires some planning. Here are some transportation options for getting to Gaspé:

Airports Near Gaspé

The closest airport to Gaspé is the Michel-Pouliot Gaspé Airport, located directly in town. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi to get around Gaspé and its surrounding areas.

Bus Stations Near Gaspé

Gaspé has a bus station that is serviced by Intercar regional transportation company. Intercar offers connections to Montreal, Quebec City, and other cities in the region.

Other options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to explore the region’s beautiful scenery, particularly if you want to go hiking in Forillon Park or visit towns that are farther away. Alternatively, driving from Montreal or other major Canadian cities to the region is an option.

How to Get Around in Gaspé

Getting around Gaspé is relatively easy, and there are a number of transportation options:

  1. Car Rentals – Renting a car is the most convenient way to travel around Gaspé and explore its surrounding areas.
  2. Bicycle Rentals – Bicycles are a popular choice for exploring the region’s trails and roads, and many local shops offer rentals.
  3. Public Transit – The Réseau Interurbain de Gaspésie (RIG), offers a regional bus service from town to town within the peninsula for an affordable price.

Where to Go Next from Gaspé: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gaspé is well-positioned for exploring the surrounding areas. If you’re interested in exploring the nearby destinations, here are a few options:

  1. Matane - This small town in Quebec is about a 2-hour drive south and is known for its scenic beauty, historic sites, and outdoor activities.
  2. Charlevoix - A short 3-hour drive, in the opposite direction, Charlevoix offers mountain and coastal scenery that easily rivals Gaspé’s.
  3. Sept-Îles - If you’re looking for a remote northwest coast experience, a night’s drive from Gaspé, Sept-Îles offers scenic coastal views and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Gaspé is a vacation destination that offers a little bit of everything. With its rich history, beautiful scenery, and friendly locals, it’s no surprise why it has become a favorite of so many travelers. By following our comprehensive guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan your perfect visit to Gaspé.
