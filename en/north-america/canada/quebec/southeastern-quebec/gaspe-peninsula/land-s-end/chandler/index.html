Introduction to Chandler

Chandler is a charming coastal town located in the Gaspé Peninsula, in Southeastern Quebec. Surrounded by dramatic landscapes and dotted with picturesque villages, Chandler is a fantastic destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and unique local experiences. Whether you’re visiting as a family, solo traveler, or with friends, Chandler promises to offer you a memorable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Chandler

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chandler boasts a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore the town’s history by visiting its various museums and historical landmarks:

  • Musée de la Gaspésie: This fascinating museum showcases the history and culture of the Gaspé Peninsula. Visitors can explore exhibits on the region’s natural history, first nations culture, local arts and crafts, and much more.
  • Parc National de l’Île-Bonaventure-et-du-Rocher-Percé: Located nearby, this park is home to a stunning natural reserve where thousands of seabirds nest each year. Visitors can take guided tours to discover the island’s history and wildlife.
  • Le Phare de Cap-des-Rosiers: This imposing lighthouse is the tallest in Canada and offers stunning views of the surrounding coastline. Visitors can explore the tower’s history and learn about the lives of lighthouse keepers.

Natural Attractions of Chandler: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chandler is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, offering plenty of opportunity for outdoor adventures:

  • Hiking and Biking Trails: The Gaspé Peninsula is home to numerous hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy strolls to challenging mountain expeditions. Some of the most popular trails include Parc national de la Gaspésie and Forillon National Park.
  • Beaches and Water Activities: With over 800 km of coastline, Chandler offers plenty of beaches and water activities for visitors. Water sports enthusiasts can enjoy kayaking, paddleboarding, fishing, whale watching tours, and much more.
  • Géoparc de Percé: This UNESCO world heritage site is an exceptional destination for geology enthusiasts. Visitors can discover the park’s unique geological formations, including the world-famous Percé Rock.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chandler

Chandler and the surrounding area are home to numerous hiking and biking trails. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Sentiers Agnès-Lefort: This 10 km trail is an excellent option for hikers and bikers of all levels. The trail winds through a beautiful forested area and provides stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains.
  • Le Parc du Bourg de Pabos: This park offers numerous hiking and biking trails, ranging from 1 to 12 km in length. Perhaps the most popular trail in the park is La Vallée du Percy, which offers stunning views of the Percé Rock.
  • Parc national du Canada de la Gaspésie: This vast park offers over 140 km of hiking trails, ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes. Visitors can take guided hikes to some of the most scenic views in Quebec, including the peaks of Mont Jacques-Cartier and Mont Albert.

Best Things to Do in Chandler: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for leisure or adventure, Chandler offers plenty of exciting things to do. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Explore the Town’s Markets: Chandler’s farmers’ markets offer visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the local culture and sample fresh, regional products. Some of the most popular markets include the Marché de Chandler and the Marché public de Paspébiac.
  • Go Whale Watching: The waters surrounding Chandler are home to numerous whale species, including the humpback, minke, and beluga whales. Visitors can take guided whale watching tours and observe these majestic creatures in their natural habitat.
  • Take a Scenic Drive: The Gaspé Peninsula is known for its breathtaking scenery, and one of the best ways to experience it is to take a scenic drive. The Route des Phares and the Route des Bâtisseurs are two of the most popular drives in the area.
  • Experience Local Cuisine: Chandler offers visitors a chance to sample some of the region’s most delicious and distinctive dishes. Popular local specialties include poutine, tourtière, and seafood dishes like lobster, scallops, and snow crab.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chandler

For visitors planning a week-long excursion in Chandler, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the town’s museums and historical landmarks, including Musée de la Gaspésie and Le Phare de Cap-des-Rosiers.
Day 2 Take a whale watching tour and explore the nearby town of Percé.
Day 3 Spend the day hiking or biking on Sentiers Agnès-Lefort or the Parc du Bourg de Pabos.
Day 4 Visit the Géoparc de Percé and take a guided tour of the park’s unique geological formations.

Food and Drink Scene of Chandler

Chandler offers a unique and diverse culinary scene, with many local specialties and unique dining experiences.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This classic Quebecois dish consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy, and it’s a must-try for visitors to the area.
  • Tourtière: This meat pie is a Gaspé Peninsula specialty, made with pork or beef, onions, potatoes, and spices.
  • Lobster and Seafood Dishes: The coastal town of Chandler is known for its fresh seafood, including lobster, scallops, and snow crab.

Local Beverages

  • Microbrasserie Pit Caribou: This local brewery offers an extensive selection of craft beers with unique, regional flavors. Visitors can take guided tours and sample their products.
  • Cidrerie le Somnambule: This local cidery produces an assortment of ciders made with locally sourced apples and fruit.

Where to Stay in Chandler: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chandler offers visitors a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

  • Gîte des Oiseaux Migrateurs: This charming guesthouse offers visitors a comfortable and welcoming stay with a scenic view of the town and the Bay of Gaspé.
  • Hôtel Le Francis: Located in the heart of the town, this hotel offers comfortable and modern rooms with fantastic views of the area.
  • Auberge de Douglastown: This historic inn offers a unique accommodation experience for visitors seeking something with a bit of character and charm.

What to Buy in Chandler: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Chandler can bring home a range of locally sourced products and souvenirs:

  • Gaspésie Gourmande: This regional organization promotes local culinary products and offers gift baskets with a selection of the best local products.
  • Local Crafts: The Gaspé Peninsula is renowned for its local crafts and arts, including pottery, weaving, and woodworking.
  • Local Wines and Ciders: Visitors can bring home unique and flavorful wines and ciders from local producers such as Cidrerie le Somnambule.

Local Culture and Customs of Chandler

The Gaspé Peninsula has a rich cultural history, and visitors to Chandler can immerse themselves in the area’s unique customs and traditions. Local customs include:

  • Celebrating St Jean Baptiste: Every year on June 24th, Chandler celebrates the St Jean Baptiste holiday with a range of festivities including parades, fireworks, and live music.
  • First Nations Culture: The Gaspé Peninsula has a rich First Nations history, and visitors can explore the region’s indigenous customs and traditions through educational programs and guided tours.

Best Time to Visit Chandler

The best time to visit Chandler is during the summer months, from June to September. During this time, visitors can enjoy the town’s outdoor activities, cultural events, and warm weather. The fall season is another excellent time to visit, as the foliage transforms the surrounding landscapes into a colorful wonderland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chandler

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Chandler and its surrounding areas.
  • Explore the history and culture of the Gaspé Peninsula.
  • Sample local cuisine and craft brews.
  • Bring home locally sourced souvenirs and products.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the local environment in any way.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore safety regulations for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chandler

Chandler is a relatively safe destination, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should be cautious when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking, and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Chandler

Chandler has a well-developed medical system, with several hospitals and clinics located in the region. Visitors should ensure they have medical insurance, and it’s always wise to bring along any necessary medications in case of an emergency.

Getting to Chandler: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chandler

Chandler is serviced by several airports, including Quebec City, Montreal, and Halifax.

Bus Stations Near Chandler

Chandler has a bus station, which is serviced by several bus companies.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Chandler by car or by ferry.

How to Get Around in Chandler

Chandler is a relatively small town, and visitors can easily explore the area on foot or by bike. For visitors planning to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car is an excellent option.

Where to Go Next from Chandler: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chandler is ideally situated for visitors looking to explore the nearby towns and villages of the Gaspé Peninsula. Popular destinations within driving distance include Percé, Gaspé, and Bonaventure.

Conclusion

Chandler is a fantastic destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and unique local experiences. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine, Chandler is sure to offer visitors a truly memorable vacation.
