Introduction to Miguasha National Park

Miguasha National Park is a stunning natural landscape in the Chaleur Bay region of Quebec, Canada. Established in 1985, this park is famous for the rare fossil record of some of the earliest known fish, dating back over 380 million years. In addition to its rich geological history, Miguasha offers a range of outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Miguasha National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Miguasha National Park Museum: Discover the history of the park’s fascinating fossil collection, as well as the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Chaleur Bay Museum: Explore the cultural heritage and maritime history of the region, with exhibitions on shipwrecks, fishing, and local legends.
  • Clyde River Interpretive Centre: Learn about the traditional lifestyle and culture of the Mi’gmaq people, who have lived in the area for thousands of years.

Natural Attractions of Miguasha National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Miguasha Beach: Walk along the stunning shoreline and admire the beautiful view of Chaleur Bay, or go for a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear water.
  • The Fossil Cliffs: Explore the fossil-rich cliffs and learn about the ancient fish species, as well as the geological processes that formed this unique landscape.
  • Mount Saint-Joseph: Hike up to the top of this scenic mountain for breathtaking views of the surrounding forests, lakes, and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miguasha National Park

  • Le Point de Vue Trail: A short and easy hike that offers stunning views of the park and Chaleur Bay.
  • La Tourbière Trail: Walk through the wetlands and observe the local flora and fauna, including beavers, ducks, and rare orchids.
  • Le Sommet Trail: A challenging trek to the top of Mount Saint-Joseph, with rewarding views of the park and the surrounding wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Miguasha National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Fossil Exploration Tour: Join a guided tour to learn about the park’s fossil history and discover rare specimens with a trained guide.
  • Camping and Picnicking: Set up camp on one of the park’s scenic campsites, or enjoy a picnic in one of the lush meadows or riverside areas.
  • Whale-watching: Book a boat tour and observe the magnificent whales, dolphins, and other marine life that inhabit Chaleur Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Miguasha National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Quebecois dish of fries, gravy, and cheese curds, available at many local diners and restaurants.
  • Seafood: Freshly caught fish and shellfish are abundant in the region, with local specialties including lobster, scallops, and mussels.
  • Maple Syrup: Quebec is famous for its maple syrup, and visitors can sample this sweet treat on pancakes, waffles, and other dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Microbrews: Quebec has a vibrant craft beer scene, with many local microbreweries offering unique and flavorful brews.
  • Ice Cider: This sweet dessert drink is made from hard cider that has been frozen and concentrated, resulting in a rich and flavorful beverage.
  • Spruce Beer: Made from the needles and bark of the local spruce tree, this traditional Quebecois beverage has a crisp and refreshing taste.

  • Hotel Motel Belle Plage: Located just steps from Miguasha Beach, this cozy motel offers simple and affordable accommodations, with a range of rooms and suites to choose from.
  • Auberge du Parc: This rustic and welcoming hostel is nestled in the heart of the park, and offers comfortable dormitory-style accommodations and shared kitchen facilities.

What to Buy in Miguasha National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fossils: Take home a piece of the park’s rich geological history with a souvenir fossil, available for purchase at the Miguasha National Park Museum and other local shops.
  • Maple Syrup: Bring home a bottle of Quebec’s famous maple syrup, available in many local gift shops and markets.
  • Handicrafts: Support local artisans and craftspeople by purchasing handmade pottery, jewelry, and other souvenirs at the Chaleur Bay Museum and other cultural landmarks.

Local Culture and Customs of Miguasha National Park

Miguasha National Park is located in a rich and diverse cultural region, with a long history of Mi’gmaq culture and French-Canadian heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and take the time to learn about the cultural importance of the local landmarks and attractions.

Best Time to Visit Miguasha National Park

The best time to visit Miguasha National Park is in the spring and summer months, when the weather is mild and the park is in full bloom. The park is open year-round, but visitors should be prepared for colder temperatures and winter weather during the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miguasha National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s natural environment and wildlife, and take care to properly dispose of all trash and waste.
  • Follow all park rules and regulations, including restrictions on camping, bonfires, and other activities.
  • Support local businesses and artisans by purchasing souvenirs and eating at local restaurants and cafes.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harm any of the park’s wildlife or plant life, and avoid feeding animals or disturbing their habitats.
  • Bring any illegal substances or engage in unauthorized activities within the park boundaries.
  • Leave any garbage or litter behind, and be respectful of the natural environment.

Safety and Health
