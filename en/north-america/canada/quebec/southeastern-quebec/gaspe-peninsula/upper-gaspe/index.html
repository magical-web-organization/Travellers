Introduction to Upper Gaspé

Upper Gaspé is a beautiful region situated in the Gaspé Peninsula in Southeastern Quebec, Canada. This region is a hidden gem offering breathtaking natural landscapes, outdoor adventures, culturally important attractions, and delicious food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history enthusiast, outdoor adventurer or a foodie seeking unique culinary experiences, Upper Gaspé has something to offer.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper Gaspé

Upper Gaspé has a rich cultural heritage that manifests in various aspects of life, including art, music, and food. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their unique heritage. Learn about the local culture, customs, and traditions from the locals themselves.

Best Time to Visit Upper Gaspé

The best time to visit Upper Gaspé is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is mild and perfect for exploring the outdoors. Moreover, the region hosts several festivals and events during this period, such as the Festival Musique du Bout du Monde.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper Gaspé

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Upper Gaspé:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture
  • Carry cash in remote areas
  • Take extra precautions during wildlife encounters

Don’t:

  • Leave your garbage in public areas
  • Litter on beaches and trails
  • Disturb local flora and fauna

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper Gaspé

Upper Gaspé is a safe region to travel to, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, do keep in mind that the region has rugged terrain, and you should take extra care during outdoor activities.

Health in Upper Gaspé

Upper Gaspé has modern healthcare facilities equipped to handle medical emergencies. Nevertheless, bring your prescribed medication and first aid kit as a precaution.
