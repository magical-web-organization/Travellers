Introduction to Matane

Travelers looking to explore the rugged beauty of Gaspesian Coast should definitely consider visiting the scenic town of Matane in Quebec, Canada. Located in the southeastern part of the Gaspé Peninsula, Matane has a rich history dating back to the 17th century. The town sits on the banks of the Matane River and overlooks the gorgeous St. Lawrence River. With its beautiful natural landscapes, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities, Matane offers something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Matane

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning more about the history and cultural heritage of Matane, you’ll find many museums and historical places to explore. Some of the popular attractions are:

  • Matane Lighthouse: Built in 1906, the lighthouse is an iconic symbol of the town and is now a national historical site.
  • Matane Museum: The museum displays the history and culture of the Matane region, including exhibits on the fur trade, the maritime industry, and the First Nations people.
  • Matane Heritage Chapel: This historic chapel has been restored to its original 19th-century appearance and provides insight into religious life in Quebec.

Natural Attractions of Matane: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Matane has some of the most stunning natural beauty in Quebec, from the rugged mountains of the Chic-Choc range to the pristine beaches and wildlife reserves. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Matane:

  • Matane Wildlife Reserve: Spanning over 1,265 square kilometers, the wildlife reserve is a nature lover’s paradise with a vast array of flora and fauna, including moose, black bears, and beavers.
  • Jardin de Doris: This beautiful garden offers a variety of plants and flowers that bloom throughout the summer months.
  • Parc des îles: This park features walking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Matane

If you’re an adventure seeker looking to explore the natural beauty of Matane, you’ll find a plethora of hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Sentier du lac Matane: This hiking trail offers a beautiful view of the Matane River and the surrounding forests.
  • Parc national de la Gaspésie: This national park has over 140km of hiking trails and is popular for its stunning views of the Chic-Choc Mountains and wildlife.
  • Liaison vélo Matane-Baie-Comeau: This cycling trail offers an adventurous ride through the rugged landscape of northern Quebec.

Best Things to Do in Matane: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do in Matane, from exploring the scenic landscapes to experiencing the local culture. Here are some of the must-do activities and tours in Matane:

  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour to spot the majestic whales that pass through the St. Lawrence River during the summer months.
  • Kayaking: Paddle through the Matane River and explore the stunning wetlands that are home to a variety of waterfowl.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic landscapes of Matane on horseback and experience the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Matane

If you’re planning to explore Matane for a week, here is a suggested itinerary that includes many of the top attractions and activities in the area:

Food and Drink Scene of Matane

No trip to Matane is complete without indulging in the local cuisine. From seafood to meat dishes, local restaurants offer a variety of options.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Matane include:

  • Matane Shrimps: Fresh seafood is a specialty of the region, and Matane shrimps are a must-try dish for any food lover.
  • Poutine Gaspésienne: This regional twist on the classic poutine is made with local cheese curds, gravy, and smoked meat.
  • Tartare de Saumon: Raw salmon served with diced onions, capers, and herbs is a popular dish in Matane.

Local Beverages

Quebec has a vibrant beer and wine scene, and Matane offers several options for local drinks. Some popular options include:

  • Microbrasserie Le Malbord: This microbrewery offers a variety of local beers, including their signature IPA.
  • Coteau Rougemont Vineyard: Tour the vineyard and enjoy a tasting of their award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Matane: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Matane has a variety of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top options are:

  • Riôtel Matane: Located on the banks of the St. Lawrence River, this hotel offers stunning views and comfortable rooms.
  • Auberge La Seigneurie des Monts: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Matane and offers a cozy atmosphere and personal touch.
  • HI-Matane: For budget travelers, this hostel offers affordable accommodations and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Matane: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matane offers several options for souvenirs and local products to take home. Some popular options include:

  • Matane Shrimps: Take home some of the freshest shrimps from the region to prepare your own dishes or gift to friends.
  • Artisanal Soaps: Made with natural ingredients from the region, artisanal soaps are a perfect gift for anyone who loves natural beauty products.
  • Maple Products: Quebec is famous for its maple syrup, and Matane offers a variety of local maple products, including syrup, candy, and mustard.

Local Culture and Customs of Matane

The people of Matane are proud of their local culture and enjoy sharing it with visitors. Some common customs and traditions in the region include:

  • Fête des chants de marins: This festival is held every year in Matane and celebrates the maritime heritage of the region with singing and dancing.
  • Traversée de la Gaspésie: This annual ski and snowshoe race traverses the stunning landscapes of the Gaspé Peninsula.

Best Time to Visit Matane

The best time to visit Matane depends on the type of traveler you are. The summer months from June to August offer warm weather and many outdoor activities, while the winter months offer skiing and snowshoeing. For those looking to avoid crowds and enjoy the fall foliage, September to October is a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matane

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region: Matane has some of the most stunning landscapes in Quebec, and exploring the region is a must-do activity.
  • Try the local cuisine: From seafood to cheese curds, Matane offers a variety of local dishes that are worth trying.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs: Take some time to learn about the history and culture of Matane and its people.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment: Matane has some of the most pristine nature in Quebec, and it is important to respect and protect the environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions: Matane has a rich heritage, and it is important to respect and honor the local customs and traditions of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matane

Matane is generally a safe town to visit, and violent crime is rare. However, it is important to take standard precautions such as locking your doors and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Matane

Matane has several hospitals and medical clinics in case of any emergencies. It is also important to take standard precautions such as using sunscreen and staying hydrated when exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Matane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Matane

The nearest airport to Matane is the Mont-Joli Airport, which is approximately 55km away. From there, travelers can rent a car or take a shuttle bus to Matane.

Bus Stations Near Matane

Matane has a bus station where travelers can take a bus from Quebec City, Montreal, or other nearby towns.

Other Options

Travelers can also rent a car and drive to Matane from major cities such as Quebec City or Montreal.

How to Get Around in Matane

Matane is a walkable town, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, for those looking to explore further, renting a car or taking a taxi is the best option.

Where to Go Next from Matane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring after visiting Matane. Some popular options include:

  • Parc national du Bic: This national park offers stunning landscapes and a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking and kayaking.
  • Baie-Comeau: This bustling town is located on the north shore of the St. Lawrence River and offers a variety of cultural attractions and outdoor activities.
  • Rimouski: This historic town offers a variety of museums and cultural attractions, as well as stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.

Conclusion

Matane is a hidden gem in Quebec, offering stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and a variety of outdoor activities for travelers. With its welcoming people and charming atmosphere, Matane is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty of Gaspesian Coast.
