Introduction to Gaspesian Coast

Located in the southeastern area of Quebec’s Gaspé Peninsula, the Gaspé Coast boasts stunning landscapes, charming villages, and rich history and culture. The region has long been popular with travelers seeking outdoor adventures, as well as those interested in the cultural and historical aspects of the area. The Gaspesian Coast is a perfect location to explore the outdoors, sample local food, and immerse yourself in Quebecois culture.

List of Places to Visit in Gaspesian Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Musée de la Gaspésie

The Musée de la Gaspésie is located in Gaspé city and is dedicated to showcasing the rich history and culture of the region, from prehistoric times to present-day. The museum features exhibits that focus on the natural history, culture, and prominent residents of the Gaspésie region. Don’t miss the stunning panoramic views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence from the museum’s observation deck.

Fort Prével

A National Historic Site, Fort Prével, was built by the French in the early 1700s. The fort was a strategic location for the French colonial powers and was also a stopover point for their fishing and trading industries. Visitors can walk around the remains of the fort and see the cannons and ramparts that were once used to repel British and American invaders.

Natural Attractions of Gaspesian Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Forillon National Park

Forillon National Park, located at the northeastern end of the Gaspé Peninsula, is a must-visit destination for any nature lover. The park boasts some of the most breathtaking scenery in Quebec, including coastline cliffs, dense forests, and calm bays. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, camping, canoeing, kayaking, and wildlife watching. The park is also home to a diversity of animals, including moose, black bear, and whales.

Percé Rock

One of Quebec’s most iconic landmarks, Percé Rock, is a sheer rock formation that rises out of the waters of the Gulf of St. Lawrence. The rock is best viewed from a boat tour that will allow you to get up close to this impressive natural wonder. Visitors can also take a hike to the lookout point overlooking the rock.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gaspesian Coast

The Gaspesian Coast is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

Le Petit-Gaspé Trail

Located in Forillon National Park, Le Petit-Gaspé trail is a 12-kilometer hike that offers stunning coastal views and wildlife sightings.

Route Verte

The Route Verte is a 5,000-kilometer bike route that spans across Quebec. The Gaspesian Coast section of the Route Verte offers breathtaking views of the coastline and the Appalachian Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Gaspesian Coast: Activities and Tours

Kayaking Tours

Explore the region’s coastline and wildlife up close with a guided kayak tour. Kayak tours are available in various locations, including the Percé Rock, Bonaventure Island, and Forillon National Park.

Whale-watching

The waters around the Gaspesian Coast are home to a variety of whales, including minke, fin, and humpback whales. Whale-watching tours are available from several locations, including Gaspé city and Percé.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gaspesian Coast

Day 1 – Gaspé City

Begin your trip in Gaspé and explore the town’s rich history and culture. Visit the Musée de la Gaspésie and explore the town center.

Day 2 and 3 – Forillon National Park

Spend the next two days exploring the diverse landscapes and outdoor activities of Forillon National Park. Hike along the coast, go whale-watching, or enjoy a scenic drive along the park’s roads.

Day 4 – Percé

Head to Percé and explore the rugged Cahill-Desjardins Trail. Be sure to visit the iconic Percé Rock and go on a whale watching tour.

Day 5 – Bonaventure Island

Take a ferry to Bonaventure Island and explore the island’s stunning landscapes and wildlife. Keep an eye out for gannets, the island’s most famous residents.

Day 6 – Historic Sites

Spend your last day exploring the region’s rich history by visiting historic sites like Fort Prével and Cap-Des-Rosiers Lighthouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Gaspesian Coast

Must-Try Dishes

Seafood Platter

The Gaspesian Coast is known for its fresh seafood. A seafood platter is one of the most popular dishes in the region, featuring a variety of seafood, including lobster, shrimp, and scallops.

Tourtière Gaspésienne

A meat pie made with beef and pork, potatoes, onions, and spices is a staple of traditional Quebecois cuisine. The Gaspésienne version often includes rabbit meat.

Local Beverages

Les Brasseurs du Petit-Sault

Located in Edmundston, New Brunswick, Les Brasseurs du Petit-Sault produces a range of craft beers that are popular throughout Quebec.

Gaspesian Heritage Whisky

This locally produced whiskey is aged in oak barrels, resulting in a smooth and flavorful drink.

Where to Stay in Gaspesian Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Cap-Blanc

A hotel with stunning views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence and easy access to the Gaspé National Park.

Auberge de la Pointe

A gorgeous beach-side Inn in the town of Rivière-du-Loup, Auberge-de-la-Pointe offers luxury and beautiful location.

What to Buy in Gaspesian Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fleur de Sel

This premium, hand-harvested sea salt is renowned for its unique taste and is a popular souvenir among tourists.

Jam and Preserves

The Gaspesian Coast is known for its berries, and the region’s artisanal jams and preserves make for great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Gaspesian Coast

The region has a deep-rooted French culture, with Quebecois customs and traditions - and the local dialect is influenced by both English and French languages. People of the region are known for their hospitality and the cultural festivities that form a part of their daily life.

Best Time to Visit Gaspesian Coast

The best time to visit Gaspesian Coast is during the summer months when the weather is mild, and the region’s outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gaspesian Coast

Do:

  • Try the local seafood and traditional dishes.
  • Respect the region’s natural beauty by picking up trash.
  • Visit the region’s historic sites and museums.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets or beaches.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gaspesian Coast

The region is very safe for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard safety measures, such as keeping your belongings secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Gaspesian Coast

Medical facilities are available in the region, and travelers should always carry travel insurance.

Getting to Gaspesian Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gaspesian Coast

Gaspé Airport serves the region with domestic flights, while international travelers can fly to Montreal or Quebec City.

Bus Stations Near Gaspesian Coast

Intercar and Orleans Express offer bus transportation to Gaspesian Coast from Montreal and Quebec City.

Other Options

A rental car is a popular option among travelers to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Gaspesian Coast

The best way to get around the Gaspesian Coast is by car. Several rental car companies are available in the region. Alternatively, public transportation is available through bus services such as Intercar and Orleans Express.

Where to Go Next from Gaspesian Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saguenay Fjord

Located a few hours’ drive from the Gaspesian Coast, the Saguenay Fjord is a breathtaking destination offering stunning views and diverse outdoor activities, such as hiking, camping, and kayaking.

Conclusion

The Gaspesian Coast is a must-visit destination for any traveler in Quebec. From outdoor adventures to rich cultural experiences and delicious local food, the region offers something for everyone. We hope our travel guide has helped you plan an unforgettable trip to Gaspesian Coast.
