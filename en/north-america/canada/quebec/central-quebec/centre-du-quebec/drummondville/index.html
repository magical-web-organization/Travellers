Introduction to Drummondville

Drummondville is a city located in the Centre-du-Québec region of Quebec, Canada. It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural landscapes. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to make the most of your visit to Drummondville.

List of Places to Visit in Drummondville

Here is a list of must-visit attractions in Drummondville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maison des arts Desjardins Drummondville: This cultural center is home to various exhibitions, performances, and events showcasing local and international artists.

  • Musée Populaire de la Photographie: This museum features a collection of over 200,000 historic and contemporary photographs, showcasing the history of Quebec and Canada.

  • Village Québécois d’Antan: Step back in time and experience the 19th century life in Quebec at this authentic village museum with over 70 both restored and reconstructed historical buildings.

  • Le Monument national de la Bataille de la Châteauguay: This national historic site commemorates the battle fought between British and American forces in 1813.

Natural Attractions of Drummondville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc de l’Île Saint-Quentin: This park is perfect for outdoor activities, including water sports, picnic, and hiking trails, it is an urban park that highlight the Saint-Francois river.

  • Parc du Mont Arthabaska: Located at the top of a hill, this park offers stunning panoramic views of Drummondville and the surrounding area, as well as hiking trails and a ski resort in winter.

  • D’Arbre en Arbre Drummondville: This adventure park is perfect for thrill-seekers of all ages, featuring different challenges and obstacle courses among the trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Drummondville

Drummondville offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for nature enthusiasts:

  • Parc Terre des jeunes is a great park with trails that wind up and down beside the river. The scenery of the natural beauty of the park is sure to captivate its visitors.

  • Parc du Chalet des Lilas: This park covers over 47 hectares of forest reserve and offers great hiking trails through maple groves and wildflowers, beautiful forest landscapes worth to be noticed.

Best Things to Do in Drummondville: Activities and Tours

  • Le Village québécois d’antan: You’ll feel the nostalgia once you take a walk through the old town Quebec, with beautifully restored 19th-century buildings where live interactive demonstrations take place.

  • Spa Bolton: Enjoy a day of relaxation with a variety of eco-friendly services, saunas, and hot tubs.

  • Cinéma RGFM Drummondville: Catch the latest blockbuster while enjoying a bag of popcorn at this movie theatre. It is located in the city center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Drummondville

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring what Drummondville has to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Musée Populaire de la Photographie and the Maison des arts Desjardins Drummondville
Day 2 Explore the D’Arbre en Arbre adventure park and go for a hike in the Parc de l’Île Saint-Quentin
Day 3 Discover Drummondville’s historical side by touring the Village Québécois d’Antan and le Monument national de la Bataille de la Châteauguay
Day 4 Relax at the Spa Bolton and enjoy the surrounding nature
Day 5 Take a bike ride along the trails of Parc Terre des jeunes or Parc du Chalet des Lilas
Day 6 Watch a movie at the Cinéma RGFM Drummondville and have dinner at a local restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Drummondville

Drummondville’s food scene offers a great mix of casual to upscale dining with emphasis on local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This classic Quebec dish consists of French fries topped with cheese curds and gravy, a perfect snack anytime.

  • Tourtière: This savory pie features spiced meat filling and is traditionally served during the holiday season.

Local Beverages

  • Unibroue Beer: This beer is brewed locally in Quebec and is famous for its distinct flavor and quality. A must try when in Quebec.

  • Ice Cider: This sweet dessert wine is made using frozen apples and is a perfect accompaniment to cheese or dessert.

Where to Stay in Drummondville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodations to stay in Drummondville:

  • Maison des arts Desjardins Drummondville: This cultural center also offers comfortable and spacious rooms.

  • Manoir des Sables: Located on the outskirts of town, this hotel features stunning views of Mounts Orford and Bromont.

  • Le Dauphin: This hotel is conveniently located in the heart of the city and offers a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Drummondville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Drummondville offers a myriad of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some recommended items to buy:

  • Maple Syrup: Being in the Quebec region its the perfect souvenir to take home, delicious and locally sourced.

  • Artwork: Visit the Maison des arts Desjardins Drummondville gift shop to find beautiful pieces of folk and contemporary art.

Local Culture and Customs of Drummondville

Drummondville’s culture is a mix of French and British influences, and is reflected in its language, cuisine, and traditions. It’s important, when travelling, to respect the city community customs and norms.

Best Time to Visit Drummondville

The best time to visit Drummondville is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you want to experience Drummondville’s winter activities, it’s recommended to visit between December and March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Drummondville

Do:

  • Try the classic Quebec dish, Poutine.

  • Take a bike tour or hike to explore the nature around Drummondville.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas, as it is banned.

  • Disrespect Quebec’s official language, which is French.

Safety and Health

Safety in Drummondville

Drummondville is regarded as a safe city to visit. Exercise regular safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belonging in busy areas and taking necessary precautions while taking part in outdoor adventure.

Health in Drummondville

If you require medical attention, Drummondville’s is well equipped with hospitals and clinics. Be sure to have adequate travel insurance before departure.

Getting to Drummondville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Drummondville has several transportation options and logistics to choose from. Here are some options:

Airports Near Drummondville

  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport: Located at 120 km from Drummondville, it’s the 2nd largest airport in Canada.

  • Québec City Jean Lesage International Airport: Located at 246 km from Drummondville.

Bus Stations Near Drummondville

  • Drummondville Bus Station: Located in the city center, provides transportation options both regional and international.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are plenty of car rental services available in Drummondville, with competitive rates and offers.

  • Taxis and Ubers: These are available all around the city.

How to Get Around in Drummondville

The best option for getting around Drummondville is driving. However, there are also public transportation options such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Drummondville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Drummondville is located in the Centre-du-Québec region, offering access to numerous destinations nearby. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Montreal: Explore Canada’s second-largest city, known for its vibrant culture, historical landmarks, and beautiful architecture, located 120 km from Drummondville.

  • Quebec City: Discover this UNESCO World Heritage Site, with its upper and lower towns, fortifications, and museums rich with history. It is located 2 and a half hours from Drummondville.

Conclusion

Drummondville is a gem in the Centre-du-Québec region of Quebec, packed with rich history, culture, and scenic landscapes that will leave you captivated. Plan your trip, pack your bags and don’t forget to check out some of the amazing places and activities we’ve recommended above when visiting Drummondville!
