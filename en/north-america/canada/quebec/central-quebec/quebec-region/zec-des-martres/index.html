Introduction to ZEC des Martres

ZEC des Martres is a stunning natural reserve in the heart of Quebec, offering an endless array of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and scenic views. Located in central Quebec, ZEC des Martres spans over 100 square kilometers of preserved wilderness, featuring a diverse range of natural habitats, from forests and lakes to rivers and mountains. Whether you’re an avid hiker, a wildlife enthusiast, or a culture buff, ZEC des Martres has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, hiking/biking routes, must-try foods, and other essential information for planning your trip to ZEC des Martres.

List of Places to Visit in ZEC des Martres

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saint-Gabriel-de-Valcartier Church: This historic church in the heart of Saint-Gabriel-de-Valcartier dates back to 1893 and features stunning stained-glass windows and intricate wooden carvings.
  • PHARE Mémoire et Racines: This unique museum in Saint-Antoine-de-Tilly showcases local history and culture through exhibits on farming, craftsmanship, and traditional life in Quebec.
  • Musée Maritime du Québec: Explore Quebec’s rich maritime history at this fascinating museum in L’Islet, featuring an extensive collection of boats, artifacts, and exhibits on fishing, shipbuilding, and navigation.

Natural Attractions of ZEC des Martres: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Sept Chutes Trail: This challenging hiking trail takes you deep into the heart of the Martres River valley, offering stunning views of seven cascading waterfalls along the way.
  • Parc Régional du Massif du Sud: This scenic park features over 160 kilometers of hiking trails, as well as biking, skiing, and snowshoeing trails, and breathtaking panoramic views from the top of Montagne Blanche.
  • Lac St-Joseph: Enjoy a dip in the crystal-clear waters of this serene lake, surrounded by forested mountains and abundant wildlife. Perfect for swimming, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of ZEC des Martres

  • Les Sentiers des Caps: This 140-kilometer network of hiking and mountain biking trails winds through the stunning mountain landscapes of Charlevoix, offering breathtaking views of the St. Lawrence River and surrounding forests.
  • Sentier de la Rivière-Malbaie: Follow the magnificent Malbaie River through this picturesque hiking trail, featuring scenic waterfalls, deep gorges, and abundant wildlife.
  • Mont-Sainte-Anne: This iconic mountain range features over 200 kilometers of hiking, mountain biking, and skiing trails, offering stunning panoramic views of the St. Lawrence River and the surrounding mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in ZEC des Martres: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Watching: ZEC des Martres is home to a diverse range of wildlife, from moose and black bears to coyotes and lynx. Join a licensed guide for a wildlife watching tour.
  • Fishing: ZEC des Martres is a popular destination for anglers, with abundant populations of trout, walleye, and pike in its many lakes and rivers.
  • Skiing: The nearby Mont-Sainte-Anne ski resort offers some of the best downhill skiing and snowboarding in eastern Canada, with over 70 trails and spectacular views of the St. Lawrence River.

Food and Drink Scene of ZEC des Martres

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: Quebec’s iconic dish of fries, gravy, and cheese curds can be found at many local restaurants and food trucks.
  • Tourtière: A traditional meat pie made with pork, beef, or game meat and savory spices.
  • Maple Syrup: Quebec is the largest producer of maple syrup in the world, so don’t miss a chance to try this sweet liquid gold.

Local Beverages

  • Unibroue Beer: Produced in nearby Chambly, Unibroue is a world-renowned brewery famous for its Belgian-style beers.
  • Ice Cider: Made from frozen apples, this sweet dessert wine is a local specialty in Quebec.
  • Caribou: A warming cocktail made with red wine, whiskey, maple syrup, and spices. Perfect for a cold winter night.

Where to Stay in ZEC des Martres: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hôtel Québec Inn: This charming hotel is located in Quebec City, just a short drive from ZEC des Martres, and features comfortable rooms and a restaurant with a view.
  • Auberge La Rocaille: This cozy guesthouse in Saint-Antoine-de-Tilly offers affordable rooms and French breakfast in a charming historic building.
  • Chalets Alpins: These rustic, log cabin-style chalets offer a quiet retreat in the heart of the peaceful Martres River valley.

What to Buy in ZEC des Martres: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup Products: Take home a bottle of pure Quebec maple syrup, or try maple leaf-shaped candy or cookies.
  • Local Art and Crafts: Visit one of the many artisan workshops in ZEC des Martres to find unique handcrafted items made from wood, glass, and other natural materials.
  • Unibroue Beer: Bring home a variety of Unibroue beers to share with friends and family or to enjoy yourself as a reminder of your trip to Quebec.

Local Culture and Customs of ZEC des Martres

Quebec has a unique Francophone culture, with its own language, cuisine, and traditions. It’s important to respect the local customs and language when interacting with locals, and to be open to trying new foods and experiences.

Best Time to Visit ZEC des Martres

ZEC des Martres is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique beauty and activities. Summer is the most popular time for outdoor activities, while winter is perfect for skiing and snowshoeing. Spring and fall offer fewer crowds and a chance to see the region’s stunning foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of ZEC des Martres

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and guided tours.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or feed the wildlife.
  • Litter or damage natural habitats.
  • Ignore warnings and safety regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in ZEC des Martres

ZEC des Martres is a safe and welcoming destination, but it’s important to take precautions when traveling in the wilderness. Stay on marked trails, observe wildlife from a safe distance, and follow all safety guidelines for outdoor activities.

Health in ZEC des Martres

Quebec has a high standard of healthcare, but it’s still recommended to have travel insurance when visiting ZEC des Martres. Be aware of ticks and other insect-borne diseases when hiking or camping, and take necessary precautions to avoid infection.

How to Get Around in ZEC des Martres

  • Most of the natural attractions and outdoor activities in ZEC des Martres are accessible by foot or bike.
  • Guided tours are the best way to explore the region if you’re not comfortable hiking or biking.

Where to Go Next from ZEC des Martres: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Quebec City: Explore the historic Old Town, visit the famous Château Frontenac, and take a boat tour of the St. Lawrence River.
  • Montmorency Falls: Marvel at the power and beauty of this towering waterfall, just a short drive from ZEC des Martres.
  • Île d’Orléans: Discover French colonial architecture and rural charm on this picturesque island in the St. Lawrence River.

Whether you’re seeking a peaceful retreat in the wilderness or an action-packed vacation with the family, ZEC des Martres has something for everyone.
