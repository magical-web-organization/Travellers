Introduction to ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

Nestled in the Quebec Region, in Central Quebec, ZEC du Lac-au-Sable is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. The area is known for its pristine wilderness, abundant wildlife, and breathtaking scenery. ZEC stands for Zone d’exploitation contrôlée (controlled harvesting zone), which means that the land is managed and conserved sustainably while also allowing visitors to enjoy recreational activities like fishing, camping, and hiking.

List of Places to Visit in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée des Abénakis: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Abenaki people, one of the region’s indigenous communities.

Natural Attractions of ZEC du Lac-au-Sable: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lac au Sable: This crystal-clear lake is perfect for swimming, canoeing, kayaking, and fishing. It’s surrounded by forests of pine, spruce, and birch, offering a tranquil and serene atmosphere.

  • Falls of the Cabonga Reservoir: These magnificent waterfalls are a popular spot for adventure seekers. Visitors can hike to the falls, swim in the natural pools, and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

ZEC du Lac-au-Sable is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Some of the popular trails and routes are:

  • Sentier du Barrage: This 12 km trail follows the shores of the Cabonga Reservoir and offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Le Mont Observation: This moderate trail leads to an observation tower that offers panoramic views of the region. The trail is approximately 5 km and takes about an hour to complete.

Best Things to Do in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: ZEC du Lac-au-Sable is a popular destination for anglers, offering some of the best fishing in Quebec. The region is home to numerous lakes, rivers, and streams teeming with trout, perch, and walleye.

  • Camping: The area has several campgrounds, ranging from primitive sites to fully equipped facilities. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing camping experience under the stars while surrounded by nature’s beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

Day 1: Arrive in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable and explore the Musée des Abénakis.

Day 2: Explore the Lac au Sable and enjoy outdoor activities like swimming, canoeing, and fishing.

Day 3: Hike the Sentier du Barrage and enjoy scenic views of the Cabonga Reservoir.

Day 4: Visit the Falls of the Cabonga Reservoir and go for a swim in the natural pools.

Day 5: Go on a guided fishing tour and catch some trout, perch, or walleye.

Day 6: Camp at one of the ZEC’s campgrounds and enjoy a bonfire under the stars.

Day 7: Depart from ZEC du Lac-au-Sable.

Food and Drink Scene of ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtière: This traditional meat pie is a staple in Quebec cuisine and is a must-try for visitors.

  • Poutine: This iconic dish consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • Maple syrup: Quebec is the largest producer of maple syrup in the world, and visitors can taste the local variety during their stay.

  • Microbreweries: The region has several microbreweries that offer a unique selection of craft beers.

Where to Stay in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

ZEC du Lac-au-Sable has several accommodation options, including:

  • Auberge du Lac Taureau: This 4-star hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites overlooking Lake Taureau.

  • Camping ZEC du Lac-au-Sable: The region has several campgrounds, ranging from primitive sites to fully equipped facilities.

What to Buy in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup: Quebec’s maple syrup is famous worldwide, and visitors can bring back a bottle or two as a souvenir.

  • Handcrafted items: The region has several artisan shops that sell handcrafted items like wood carvings, pottery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

  • Language: The official language of Quebec is French, and visitors may encounter some language barriers when communicating with locals.

  • Etiquette: Visitors are expected to be polite and respectful towards locals and follow the customs and traditions of the region.

Best Time to Visit ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

The best time to visit ZEC du Lac-au-Sable is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

  • Support local businesses and buy souvenirs.

  • Follow the rules and regulations of the ZEC.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

ZEC du Lac-au-Sable is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions like:

  • Hiking and camping with a companion.

  • Bringing adequate supplies and equipment.

  • Checking weather conditions before heading out.

Health in ZEC du Lac-au-Sable

Visitors to ZEC du Lac-au-Sable should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. They should also pack a basic first-aid kit.
