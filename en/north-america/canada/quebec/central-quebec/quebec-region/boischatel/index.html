Boischatel Travel Guide

Boischatel, located in Central Quebec, Quebec Region, Canada, welcomes visitors with its French-influenced architecture, stunning natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage. This charming destination offers plenty of things to see and do, making it a great choice for a solo trip, couples retreat, or family vacation. This travel guide will help you plan your Boischatel vacation with detailed information on places to visit, activities, accommodation options, local cuisine, and more.

Introduction to Boischatel

Boischatel is a picturesque town situated on the northern shores of the St. Lawrence River, about a 15-minute drive from downtown Quebec City. This community boasts a population of approximately 7,000 residents, and it’s renowned for its peacefulness and extraordinary views of the river.

List of Places to Visit in Boischatel

Here are some of the must-visit places in Boischatel:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Place Royale: This site is a National Historic Site of Canada, which dates back to the early 1600s, and it served as the first permanent French settlement in North America.

  • Musée de la Civilisation: This museum features exhibits related to the history, culture, and art of Quebec City, including temporary exhibits that showcase contemporary art.

  • Musée de la Place Royale: This site is dedicated to the history of Place Royale, and it provides an opportunity to experience the era of New France.

Natural Attractions of Boischatel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Montmorency Falls Park: Located close to Boischatel, this park offers a stunning view of the 272-foot-high waterfall, hiking trails, ziplines, and suspension bridges.

  • Île d’Orléans: A beautiful island that’s just off the shore of Quebec City, it’s famous for its lush fields, apple orchards, and vineyards. It’s a great place to go on a scenic drive, explore charming villages or enjoy locally produced wine and cider.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boischatel

Boischatel is blessed with several picturesque trails that visitors can explore during their visit. The most famous are:

  • Sentier de la Forêt: A 3.5 km forest trail that takes you through woods and leads to a lookout point with a stunning view of Quebec City.

  • Le Corridor des Cheminots: A converted railway track that’s now a bike path stretching over 70 km, connecting residents and visitors to surrounding towns and cities.

Best Things to Do in Boischatel: Activities and Tours

  • Take a scenic drive: The scenic roads in Boischatel offer impressive views of the St. Lawrence River and the Quebec countryside, making it a perfect destination to explore on the wheels.

  • Shop at the artisans’ market: This market, located inside a converted train station, offers locally made arts and crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and handmade soaps.

  • Experience the Quebecois cuisine: With access to fresh produce, Boischatel has a strong food culture that visitors can indulge in at local eateries and markets. Don’t forget to try “Poutine,” a Quebecois specialty consisting of fries, gravy, and cheese curds.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boischatel

Food and Drink Scene of Boischatel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Quebecois dish consisting of golden fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

  • Tourtière: A meat pie prepared in the traditional Quebec style with potatoes, onions, and pork or beef.

  • Fèves au Lard: A sweet and savory baked bean dish that’s often served with maple syrup and crusty bread.

Local Beverages

  • Cidre de glace: Or Ice cider, an apple-based alcoholic drink that’s harvested and fermented in sub-zero temperatures.

  • Caribou: A potent wine-based cocktail that’s perfect for warming up on chilly winter evenings.

Where to Stay in Boischatel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Several accommodation options cater to various preferences and budgets. Some of the most highly recommended include:

  • Le Royal Champêtre: A quaint bed and breakfast that features comfortable rooms, a garden, and a hot tub.

  • Au Gîte de la Chute: This charming inn is situated within walking distance of Montmorency Falls Park and features cozy rooms and a lovely garden.

  • Hôtel Cofortel: This mid-range hotel offers comfortable rooms, free parking, a fitness center, and a convenient location close to Quebec City.

What to Buy in Boischatel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Canada produces more than 80% of the world’s maple syrup, and visitors should definitely sample this golden delight while in Boischatel.

  • Handicrafts: Local artisans specialize in a range of crafts, including pottery, weaving, and glassblowing. Bring home a unique piece of Quebecois craftsmanship as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Boischatel

Boischatel enjoys a rich cultural heritage, and its residents proudly incorporate their heritage into the town’s daily life. Visitors should be prepared to encounter charming customs and a fascinating history incorporated into everyday life.

Best Time to Visit Boischatel

The best time to visit Boischatel is between May and October, when the weather is mild, and most of the outdoor activities and attractions are open for visitors. Summer is the peak season and is the best time to experience the town’s festivities and enjoy the warm weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boischatel

Do:

  • Try the Quebecois cuisine and drinks.

  • Speak some basic French words to the locals.

  • Take a scenic drive or hike.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Assume that everyone speaks English.

  • Take pictures or videos of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boischatel

Boischatel is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, as with any destination, visitors should take precautions like not leaving valuables visible in cars or apartments and avoiding walking in unlit areas at night.

Health in Boischatel

Boischatel has a well-established healthcare system, with various clinics and hospitals serving its residents and visitors. Visitors should be sure to have adequate travel insurance to avoid incurring medical expenses during their stay.

Getting to Boischatel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Boischatel

  • Jean Lesage International Airport: Located about 27 kilometers southwest of Boischatel, this is the closest airport that serves domestic and international passengers.

Bus Stations Near Boischatel

  • Réseau de Transport de la Capitale: This bus network is the primary form of public transportation, serving Quebec City and surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Several car rental companies, including Avis, Hertz, and Budget, operate in Quebec City.

How to Get Around in Boischatel

Most visitors explore Boischatel by car, bike, or on foot. Public transportation in the form of buses and taxis is also available, with frequent services connecting Boischatel to Quebec City and surrounding towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from Boischatel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boischatel is ideally located to explore neighboring towns and cities. Some of the recommended destinations for excursions include:

  • Quebec City: A vibrant and historic city, it’s famous for its European-style streetscapes, lively music scene, and fantastic food.

  • Charlevoix: A beautiful region known for its unique natural landscapes featuring cliffs, mountains, and the famous Saint Lawrence River.

Conclusion

Boischatel is a charming destination that offers both a peaceful retreat and adventure for travelers. From hiking and cycling to wine tasting and exploring the town’s rich history and culture, this hidden gem of Quebec is sure to impress visitors of all ages.
