Introduction to Beaupré

Beaupré is a small town located in Quebec, Canada. This charming town is considered a gateway to Quebec City, with plenty of natural attractions and cultural landmarks to explore. Sprawling mountains and lush forests surround Beaupré, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, places to visit, local customs, and transportation options to help you plan your trip to Beaupré.

List of Places to Visit in Beaupré

Beaupré offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. There’s a lot to discover in this charming town. Here are some of the best places to visit in Beaupré:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Basilica of Sainte-Anne-de-Beaupré

The Basilica of Sainte-Anne-de-Beaupré is one of the most popular attractions in the town. It is a significant religious site in Canada, known for its architecture. This Basilica is a beautiful place with unique architecture and beautiful artwork.

2. Royal Snowmobile Museum

Located on the Boulevard Ste-Anne, this museum presents 40 historic snowmobiles used in competitions from the early 1960s. Skim through the exhibits displaying snowmobile development over the years with the help of original photos, videos, TV clips ad other objects.

3. Saint Anne Canyon

The Saint Anne Canyon is another must-visit attraction in Beaupré. The canyon offers scenic views of rushing waterfalls, deep gorges, and natural rock formations. You can take an exhilarating zip-line ride for a bird’s eye view of the canyon.

Natural Attractions of Beaupré: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Mont-Sainte-Anne

For skiing enthusiasts, Mont-Sainte-Anne is a must-visit destination in Beaupré. The resort is popular among tourists and locals alike. In the summer months, you can also enjoy mountain biking and hiking expeditions.

2. Canyon Sainte-Anne

Embrace the stunning natural beauty of the Canyon Sainte-Anne. With over three suspension bridges, the canyon offers visitors a thrilling experience while providing them with their scenic backdrop.

3. La Seigneurie-des-Aulnaies Gardens

La Seigneurie-des-Aulnaies Gardens boasts more than 15 gardens and is located just outside of Beaupré. Tourists can enjoy an enchanting stroll through these immaculate gardens and enjoy the fresh air in the tree-lined corridors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaupré

Hiking and biking in Beaupré is one of the most popular activities for visitors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails that you can check out in Beaupré:

  • Sentier Mestachibo
  • Trails in Parc du Cap Tourmente
  • Sentier Le Cabouron
  • Vallée Bras-du-Nord

Best Things to Do in Beaupré: Activities and Tours

1. Parc national de la Jacques-Cartier

Parc national de la Jacques-Cartier is a popular destination for a guided tour featuring beautiful natural scenery and activities for all ages. Tourists can enjoy a guided tour with forest rangers to gain insight into the park’s history and natural elements.

2. Spa Nordique Le Germain

Located at the foot of the Laurentian Mountains, Spa Nordique Le Germain provides tourists with tailored spa experiences that cater to your needs. Take a swim in the heated outdoor pool, indulge in memorable body massages while enjoying the wonderful view of the surrounding mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Beaupré

Must-Try Dishes

Quebec’s cuisine is famous across Canada, and Beaupré is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes in the area:

  • Poutine- A dish that consists of crispy french fries, gravy, and cheese curds. A classic Canadian comfort food.
  • Tourtiere- A meat pie made with ground pork, potatoes, and spices.
  • Creton- A pork spread eaten for breakfast with slices of bread or toast.

Local Beverages

  • Cidrerie Verger Bilodeau produces award-winning quality cider hand-crafted using traditional techniques and local ingredients.

What to Buy in Beaupré: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Le Centre d’Art et d’Histoire de Sainte-Anne De Beaupré is a great place to buy souvenirs. They have a range of artisanal products such as jewelry, clothing, and Canadian-made artisanal crafts to preserve and promote Quebec’s heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Beaupré

The most widely spoken language in Beaupré is French, so it’s nice to know a few basic French phrases before your visit. Also, tipping is based on the standard percentage of about 15% in North America.

Best Time to Visit Beaupré

The peak season in Beaupré is during the winter months, but the town is equally beautiful during summer and autumn. Summers are perfect for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities

Do’s and Don’ts of Beaupré

Do:

  • Always bring warm clothing and reliable winter gear during the colder months of the year.
  • Practice outdoor safety.
  • Be polite and say “Bonjour” as a greeting.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks and natural trails.
  • Smoke in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Beaupré

Beaupré is generally safe for visitors and residents alike, but it’s always wise to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Beaupré

The town has several medical clinics and hospitals to provide any medical needs. However, it’s still ideal to bring important medication with you.

Getting to Beaupré: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beaupré

  • Quebec City Jean Lesage International Airport(41.6km)

Bus Stations Near Beaupré

  • Gare Sainte-Anne-de-Beaupré (3.7km)

Other Options

Driving to Beaupré is the most convenient way of getting to town. Several car rental companies are available at the airport.

How to Get Around in Beaupré

Most of the town’s attractions are near each other, so walking is the most convenient way of getting around. The town also offers taxi services when needed.

Where to Go Next from Beaupré: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beaupré is the gateway to Quebec City and the Saguenay Fjords. Other nearby destinations worth exploring are the National Park of Canada Forillon and the Chic-Choc Mountains.

Conclusion

Beaupré is a magnificent destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and history buffs. This travel guide has provided you with a comprehensive list of things to do, places to visit, and local customs to experience during your visit. With proper planning and this guide’s tips, you’ll be able to make the most of your trip to Beaupré, Quebec.
