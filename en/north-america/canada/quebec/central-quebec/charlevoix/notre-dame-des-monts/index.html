Introduction to Notre-Dame-des-Monts

Nestled in the heart of Charlevoix, Quebec, Notre-Dame-des-Monts is a quaint village that offers an authentic Quebecois experience. The town is surrounded by the breathtaking Laurentian Mountains, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors to Notre-Dame-des-Monts can expect to find a charming village with friendly locals, a unique culture, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Notre-Dame-des-Monts

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Charlevoix: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning more about the region’s cultural, historical, and natural heritage. Its collection includes exhibits on the early history of Charlevoix, the local wildlife, and the region’s traditional crafts.

  • Historic Church of Notre-Dame-des-Monts: Built in the 1800s, this church is an excellent example of Quebecois religious architecture and a testament to the region’s rich cultural heritage. Visitors can attend mass or explore the church’s intricate design and artwork inside and out.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc national des Grands-Jardins: This park offers a vast range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and skiing, with breathtaking views of the Laurentian Mountains. In summer, visitors can hike one of the numerous trails, enjoy a picnic, or go fishing in one of the park’s pristine lakes. In winter, skiing and snowshoeing are popular activities, with plenty of groomed trails.

  • Le Massif de Charlevoix: This ski resort boasts some of the most incredible views in Quebec, with over 50 trails for skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing in winter. In summer, it transforms into a unique Quebecois-style theme park offering zipline courses, tubing, and hiking on Mount Liguori.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Notre-Dame-des-Monts

  • Le Sentier des Caps de Charlevoix: This 67-km trail offers stunning panoramic views of the St. Lawrence River and the surrounding mountains. The trail is a challenge for advanced hikers but offers plenty of opportunities for those looking for a more leisurely pace, with campsites and rest areas along the way.

  • Le Circuit des traditions: This 100 km cycling route takes in some of the most picturesque views of Charlevoix. It is perfect for those who want to experience the region’s cultural landmarks and countryside spread over two days of cycling.

Best Things to Do in Notre-Dame-des-Monts: Activities and Tours

  • Whale-watching tours: Take a boat tour from nearby Baie-Sainte-Catherine to see the region’s breathtaking whales up-close. Visitors can see finbacks, belugas, minke whales, and even humpback whales during their migration.

  • Horseback riding: Head to Centre Équestre Le Cabré, where they offer guided tours of the Charlevoix countryside on horseback. It is an excellent way to experience the region’s natural beauty with a unique perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Notre-Dame-des-Monts

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtiere: This French Canadian meat pie is a traditional Quebecois dish that is usually served during the holidays. The pie is filled with a mixture of ground meat, herbs, and spices and is a delicious, hearty meal.

  • Poutine: It is a beloved Quebecois dish made up of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy. It is perfect for a late-night snack or as a meal with a cold beer.

Local Beverages

  • Mulled wine: Red wine served warm with spices is a perfect way to warm up on a chilly evening.

  • Maple syrup: Quebec is known for its maple syrup, and you can buy maple syrup candy, maple pie, or flavored syrups, making it a perfect gift to bring back home.

Where to Stay in Notre-Dame-des-Monts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge Beausejour: This cozy bed and breakfast offer guests charming accommodation with breakfast included. It is within walking distance of the church and the town center.

  • Le Domaine Belle Plage: This beautiful lakeside resort offers guests a range of accommodation options, including cabins and chalets. You can enjoy the resort’s amenities, including water sports, tennis, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Notre-Dame-des-Monts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Charlevoix art and crafts: The region is known for its artisans, and you can buy unique items such as pottery, woodwork, or natural soaps.

  • Maple products: Bring back a taste of Quebec with maple syrup, candy, or flavored syrups, which are great gifts to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Notre-Dame-des-Monts

Notre-Dame-des-Monts has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through the town’s architecture, art, and historical landmarks. The locals are friendly, welcoming, and take pride in their unique Quebecois identity. Visitors can participate in local festivals, such as the winter carnival, a celebration of winter sports, and the International Symposium of Contemporary Art every summer.

Best Time to Visit Notre-Dame-des-Monts

The best time to visit Notre-Dame-des-Monts depends on your preferences. In winter, visitors can go skiing, snowboarding, or snowshoeing. In summer, visitors can hike, bike, or enjoy the local cuisine at one of the many restaurants in town. Spring and fall offer a pleasant climate for outdoor activities such as fishing, camping, and scenic drives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Notre-Dame-des-Monts

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including tourtiere, poutine, and maple products.

  • Bring your camera to capture the stunning views of the Laurentian Mountains and the St. Lawrence River.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the hiking trails or parks.

  • Ignore the instructions of your tour guides while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Notre-Dame-des-Monts

Notre-Dame-des-Monts is generally a safe destination for travelers. In winter, be cautious of icy roads and sidewalks and follow the instructions of your skiing or snowboarding instructors. In summer, be aware of ticks and mosquitoes and take measures to avoid bites.

Health in Notre-Dame-des-Monts

The local hospital is located in nearby La Malbaie, which is around 20 minutes away from Notre-Dame-des-Monts. It is recommended to have travel health insurance for emergencies.

Getting to Notre-Dame-des-Monts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Notre-Dame-des-Monts

The nearest airport is Quebec City Jean Lesage International Airport, which is around three hours drive from Notre-Dame-des-Monts.

Bus Stations Near Notre-Dame-des-Monts

The best option for public transportation is the Societe de transport de Charlevoix, which offers bus connections between multiple destinations in the region.

Other Options

A rental car offers more flexibility to explore the region’s many attractions at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Notre-Dame-des-Monts

The best way to get around Notre-Dame-des-Monts is by car or bike. Most attractions in the area are within driving distance, and a bike is a great way to explore the countryside at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Notre-Dame-des-Monts: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Baie-Saint-Paul: A charming village known for its cultural attractions, including art galleries, museums, and festivals.

  • Tadoussac: A historic town known for whale watching and its beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Notre-Dame-des-Monts offers a unique Quebecois experience with breathtaking scenery and outdoor adventures. With plenty of cultural landmarks, local cuisine, and outdoor activities, it is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a laid-back, authentic experience in Canada.
