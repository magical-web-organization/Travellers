Introduction to Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park

Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie, also known as the High Gorges of the Malbaie River, is a stunning national park located in Charlevoix, Quebec, Canada. It is known for its steep cliffs and deep valleys, making it a beautiful destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park is also listed as a UNESCO World Biosphere Reserve, due to its unique flora and fauna, including black bears, beavers, and bald eagles.

List of Places to Visit in Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park is primarily a destination for nature lovers, there are a few cultural attractions worth visiting. The Interpretation Centre is a great place to start, as it offers information on the park’s history, ecology, and geology. There are also a few historic sites within the park, including the Saint-Siméon church, which dates back to the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main draws of Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park is its stunning nature. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, canoeing, and kayaking in the park. The park’s most famous trail is the Acropole des Draveurs, which is a challenging 8.5 km hike that offers beautiful views of the Malbaie River and surrounding mountains. Other popular trails include Les Érables and Les Hautes-Gorges, which are both shorter and less challenging.

For those who prefer to take in the scenery from the water, the park offers canoe and kayak rentals. The Malbaie River is a popular spot for paddling, as it offers calm waters and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park. The park has over 30 km of hiking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Acropole des Draveurs
  • Les Érables
  • Les Hautes-Gorges
  • La Chute
  • Les Loups

Biking is also a great way to explore the park, with several scenic routes available. The park’s most popular biking trail is the Le Vallon, which is a 10 km trail that follows the Malbaie River.

Best Things to Do in Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Camping: The park offers several campgrounds, including two that are accessible only by canoe or kayak.
  • Wildlife Watching: The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including black bears, beavers, and bald eagles.
  • Stargazing: The park has minimal light pollution, making it a great spot for stargazing.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The Malbaie River is a popular spot for paddling, with calm waters and stunning views.
  • Guided Tours: The park offers several guided tours, including a wildlife watching tour and a guided hike of the Acropole des Draveurs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie National Park

Day 1: Arrive in the park and set up camp at one of the campgrounds. Day 2: Hike the Acropole des Draveurs trail. Day 3: Explore the park’s waterways by canoe or kayak. Day 4: Take a guided wildlife watching tour. Day 5: Bike the Le Vallon trail. Day 6: Relax at the campsite and stargaze at night. Day 7: Depart from the park.
