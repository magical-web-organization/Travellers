A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Saint-Séverin, Quebec

Located in the Mauricie region of Central Quebec, Saint-Séverin is a hidden gem. This charming village offers visitors an opportunity to experience Quebec’s rich history and natural beauty. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historical sites, there is something for everyone in Saint-Séverin. If you’re planning a trip to this lovely area, read on for our comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Séverin

Saint-Séverin offers both natural and cultural attractions. Here is a list of places you should consider visiting during your stay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Saint-Séverin Church - This picturesque church, built in 1853, is a must-see for those interested in Quebec’s religious history.
  2. Maison Félix-Leclerc - This museum dedicated to the famous Quebecois poet and songwriter is a treat for culture enthusiasts.
  3. Musée du Bûcheron - This museum pays tribute to the history of the logging industry in the region. You’ll find historical tools, machines, and artifacts on display.
  4. Moulin Michel de Gentilly - A windmill dating back to the 19th century, that has now been converted to a museum.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Séverin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Réserve naturelle du Marais-Léon-Provancher - This nature reserve offers visitors great views of both flora and fauna in the region.
  2. Parc National de la Mauricie - This national park has over 536 square kilometers of preserved forests, rivers, lakes, and hiking trails.
  3. Chutes à Magnan - These waterfalls are located in Saint-Léon-le-Grand and can be accessed from Saint-Séverin.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Séverin

Saint-Séverin is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best biking and hiking trails in the area:

  1. Le Baluchon Eco-resort - This resort offers a 9-kilometer trail for both hikers and bikers that passes through vast forests, meadows, and lakes all around.
  2. Sentier des Coteaux - This beautiful trail in Saint-Séverin is perfect for hiking. It provides scenic views of the village and the valley’s deep forests.
  3. Parc National de la Mauricie - This park offers over 536 square kilometers of hiking trails and picturesque views of Quebec’s untouched nature for all levels.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Séverin: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, there are plenty of other activities and tours that you can take in Saint-Séverin. Here’s a list:

  1. Horseback Riding - Equitation St-Pierre offers horseback riding services and tours for adults and children.
  2. Fishing - With numerous lakes and rivers in the region, Saint-Séverin is perfect for fishing enthusiasts.
  3. Camping - Whether you want to camp in a tent or an RV, there are plenty of campsites in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Séverin

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Saint-Séverin:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Saint-Séverin, visit the Saint-Séverin Church
2 Take a guided tour of Maison Félix-Leclerc

4 Hike or bike on the Sentier des Coteaux
5 Visit the windmill at Moulin Michel de Gentilly

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Séverin

Saint-Séverin has a small but diverse food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tarte au sucre - A traditional Quebecois sugar pie
  2. Poutine - Quebec’s most famous dish, consisting of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  3. Tourtière - A meat pie with origins in Quebecois cuisine and popular across Canada.

Local Beverages

  1. Wine from Domaine Smalling Winery: Wine lovers can take a tour of this local winery and sample some of their award-winning wines.
  2. Maple Syrup- can be found all over the village.

Where to Stay in Saint-Séverin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Séverin has a variety of accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels to guesthouses. Here are some options:

  1. Le Baluchon Eco-resort - A luxurious eco-resort that offers rooms and cottages.
  2. La Maison Félix-Leclerc - This former residence of the famous Quebecois poet has been converted into a guesthouse.
  3. Auberge du Trappeur - A cozy inn located in Saint-Léon-le-Grand.

What to Buy in Saint-Séverin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to take home some Quebecois souvenirs from Saint-Séverin! Here are some local products to consider:

  1. Maple Syrup - A classic Quebecois souvenir found in every region of Quebec.
  2. Wood products - You can buy wooden bowls or spoons, as well as cutting boards, furniture, and décor at Village du Bûcheron.
  3. Local Art and Pottery - Visiting one of Saint-Séverin’s art galleries enough for you to see high-quality art-forms and ceramics, as well as several chances to purchase them for your personal collections.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Séverin

Quebec has a distinct culture, and Saint-Séverin is no exception. It’s best to brush up on Quebecois customs and etiquette before your trip. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. ** French is the official language**: Quebecois primarily speak French, so learning a few phrases will come in very handy.
  2. Tipping: Tipping in Quebec is customary. It’s customary to tip waiters 15-20% of the bill.
  3. Religious Customs and Holidays: The Catholic Church has a significant influence on Quebec culture, so be prepared for celebrations on their holy days.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Séverin

The best time to visit Saint-Séverin is in the summer months, from June to August. The temperatures during these months are warm and sunny, and the hiking trails are open. However, fall foliage makes a beautiful backdrop for photos, and winter brings skiing and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Séverin

As a cultural hub, Saint-Séverin has some customs you should keep in mind while visiting. Here are a few:

Do:

  1. Learn French - Quebecois appreciate when visitors at least try to speak French, even in basic phrases.
  2. Respect Religious Customs - Most people in Quebec are devout Catholics, so be respectful of their customs and traditions.
  3. Hike and Bike Safely - Always bring necessary equipment such as water, map, and a first-aid kit.

Don’t:

  1. Not Speak French - Quebecois expect visitors to at least speak some French, particularly in areas with less tourism.
  2. Smoke in Public Places - Quebec has strict anti-smoking laws and smoking isn’t acceptable in public places, including restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Saint-Séverin is a very safe place to visit, with low levels of crime. However, it’s always essential to take necessary safety precautions while traveling.

Safety in Saint-Séverin

  1. Keep your valuables safe: Ensure to keep your valuables secure when traveling or exploring.
  2. Traveling Solo: Inform the locals about your itinerary and call them when you are about to leave the place.

Health in Saint-Séverin

  1. Stay Hydrated: During a hike, make sure to carry sufficient water and remain hydrated.
  2. Wear Proper Hiking gear: Proper hiking shoes and clothes will save you from any unexpected falls or injuries.

Getting to Saint-Séverin: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Saint-Séverin from Montreal. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Saint-Séverin

The nearest international airport to Saint-Séverin is Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Séverin

There are no bus stations in Saint-Séverin. However, you can rent a car from Montreal and drive to the village.

Other Options

You can also take a private taxi or an Uber from Montreal to Saint-Séverin.

How to Get Around in Saint-Séverin

Saint-Séverin is a small village making it easily walkable. However, Hiring a car, cab or a bike rental would be convenient, especially if visiting the sights outside the village.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Séverin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations to visit if you have additional days to spare:

  1. Trois-Rivières: A charming city known for its historical district, Old Trois-Rivières, is just an hour drive away.
  2. Shawinigan: A city on a picturesque promontory on the shores of Saint-Maurice River.
  3. Mauricie National Park: There are plenty of activities to indulge in, making it worth the hour drive to reach the park.

Conclusion

Saint-Séverin is a little-known gem in the Mauricie region. Its incredible nature reserves, museums, and historical architecture make it worth the trip. While visiting Saint-Séverin, take some time to stroll in beautiful nature and admire local culture. Remember to speak some French, explore local markets, and bring home some souvenirs! Happy Travelling!
