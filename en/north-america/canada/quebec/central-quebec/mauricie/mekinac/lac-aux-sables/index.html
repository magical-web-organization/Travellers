Lac-aux-Sables is a hidden gem in the heart of central Quebec, Canada. It is a picturesque town that attracts travelers from around the world with its rich historical and cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure opportunities. If you are planning to visit Lac-aux-Sables, this ultimate travel guide will help you discover the top attractions, best time to visit, how to get there, and more.

Introduction to Lac-aux-Sables

Lac-aux-Sables is located in the Mékinac region of Mauricie in central Quebec, Canada. The town is situated on the shores of Lac aux Sables, a beautiful and serene lake that is the main attraction of the town. With a population of around 1400, Lac-aux-Sables is a small town that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Lac-aux-Sables

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lac-aux-Sables is rich in history, culture, and traditions. There are several museums and historical places that you can visit to learn about the town’s past. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Saint-Marc-des-Carrières church
  • The Église de Saint-Théophile church
  • The Château Duquet
  • The Maison Grand-Mère
  • The Vieux Presbytère de Saint-Ubalde

Natural Attractions of Lac-aux-Sables: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lac-aux-Sables is known for its natural beauty, and there are many outdoor adventure activities that visitors can enjoy. Some of the popular natural attractions include:

  • Lac aux Sables beach
  • Parc national de la Mauricie
  • Réserve faunique Mastigouche
  • Sentier national (Québec) (National Trail)
  • Canoeing and kayaking on the Saint-Maurice river

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lac-aux-Sables

Lac-aux-Sables is a hiker’s paradise with many hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the top trails in the area include:

  • Sentier national (Québec) (National Trail)
  • The “Chemin du Roi” bike trail
  • The “Route Verte” bike trail
  • Parc national de la Mauricie hiking trails

Best Things to Do in Lac-aux-Sables: Activities and Tours

Lac-aux-Sables offers a wide range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, there is something for everyone. Top activities include:

  • Whale watching tours
  • Kayaking and canoeing
  • Fishing
  • Hiking and biking
  • Horseback riding
  • Cultural tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Lac-aux-Sables

If you are planning to visit Lac-aux-Sables for a week, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the town’s history and culture by visiting the museums and historical places.
  • Day 3: Enjoy the natural beauty of the area by hiking or biking one of the local trails.
  • Day 4-5: Experience the outdoor adventures and activities that Lac-aux-Sables has to offer.
  • Day 6-7: Relax on Lac aux Sables beach or take a scenic drive through the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Lac-aux-Sables

Lac-aux-Sables offers a diverse culinary scene with many local specialties that visitors must try. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtière (meat pie)
  • Poutine
  • Sugar pie
  • Cretons (pork spread)

Local Beverages

  • Maple syrup
  • Apple cider
  • Quebec microbrews

Where to Stay in Lac-aux-Sables: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lac-aux-Sables offers a range of options for accommodations, from hotels, hostels to guesthouses, and camping sites. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Auberge Lac‑à‑L’Eau‑Claire
  • Gîte Aux Hirondelles
  • Camping du lac aux sables

What to Buy in Lac-aux-Sables: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lac-aux-Sables is known for its local products, including maple syrup, cider, and craft beers. Visitors can also bring home souvenirs from the local boutiques and craft shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Lac-aux-Sables

The culture of Lac-aux-Sables is heavily influenced by its French-Canadian heritage and its position as a seasonal lake resort and fishing town. Visitors can experience this uniquely Quebecois culture through the town’s food, music, and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Lac-aux-Sables

The best time to visit Lac-aux-Sables is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are available. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the vibrant colors of the changing leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lac-aux-Sables

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Take advantage of the outdoor adventures and activities

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect the local people, culture, or traditions
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Lac-aux-Sables

Lac-aux-Sables is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Lac-aux-Sables

There are medical facilities in Lac-aux-Sables for any medical emergencies. Visitors should have health insurance and take basic precautions against illnesses like ticks, mosquitoes, and Lyme disease.

Getting to Lac-aux-Sables: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lac-aux-Sables is most easily accessible by car, but there are also public transportation options available.

Airports Near Lac-aux-Sables

  • Québec City Jean Lesage International Airport
  • Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport

Bus Stations Near Lac-aux-Sables

  • Trois-Rivières Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Canoeing and kayaking on the Saint-Maurice river
  • Biking on the “Route Verte” bike trail
  • Taking a scenic drive through the countryside

How to Get Around in Lac-aux-Sables

The best way to get around Lac-aux-Sables is by car, but visitors can also use bicycles, or even by foot.

Where to Go Next from Lac-aux-Sables: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions from Lac-aux-Sables that visitors can explore. Some of the top destinations include:

  • Parc de la Rivière-Batiscan

Conclusion

Lac-aux-Sables is a hidden gem in central Quebec, with rich historical and cultural heritage, scenic views, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Whether you are looking to relax on the beach, hike or bike one of the trails, or explore the local culture, Lac-aux-Sables has it all. Plan your visit today and discover this unexplored paradise for yourself.
