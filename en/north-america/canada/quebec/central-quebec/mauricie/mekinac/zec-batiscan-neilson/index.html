Introduction to ZEC Batiscan-Neilson

ZEC Batiscan-Neilson is situated in the heart of Quebec, Canada, in the region of Mauricie. It offers travelers an outstanding natural experience with various outdoor activities. ZEC stands for “Zone d’Exploitation Contrôlée,” which translates to “Controlled Harvesting Zone,” and indeed, ZEC Batiscan-Neilson is a fantastic area for hunting and fishing, attracting visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in ZEC Batiscan-Neilson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Le Village Quebecois d’Antan

Le Village Quebecois d’Antan is an open-air museum displaying Quebec’s traditional architecture and lifestyle from the 19th and 20th centuries. You can see the various old buildings and learn about their stories from the knowledgeable guides at the museum.

Old Prison of Trois Rivieres

The Old Prison of Trois Rivieres is a historical prison located in Trois-Rivieres, Quebec, constructed in 1822. The prison had been in use for more than 160 years and is now a museum open to the public. Visitors can see the prison cells and learn about prisoners’ lives in the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of ZEC Batiscan-Neilson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lac-a-la-Tortue

Lac-a-la-Tortue is a lake located in the Mauricie region, offering breathtaking views and crystal clear water for visitors to enjoy swimming, fishing, and other water activities. You can also rent a boat for a scenic ride around the lake.

Batiscan River

The Batiscan River is a freshwater river stretching 196 km long, offering thrilling rapids for kayaking and rafting activities. It also has some of the best salmon fishing spots in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of ZEC Batiscan-Neilson

Le Sentier National

Le Sentier National is a network of hiking trails in Canada that covers more than 1000 km, with a trail running through ZEC Batiscan-Neilson. Visitors can take on a day or multi-day hike, passing through different terrains and experiencing the region’s beautiful scenery.

Le Massif de Charlevoix

Le Massif de Charlevoix is a famous cycling route starting from Charlevoix and passing through ZEC Batiscan-Neilson. It is known for its scenic views of the Saguenay Fjords and the St. Lawrence River.

Best Things to Do in ZEC Batiscan-Neilson: Activities and Tours

Fishing

ZEC Batiscan-Neilson is known for its excellent fishing, with many species like trout, salmon, walleye, and pike available to catch in the region’s various lakes and rivers. There are many fishing tour companies, which provide guides and equipment in the area.

Hunting

Hunting is also a popular activity in ZEC Batiscan-Neilson, where visitors can go for a big game hunt or upland game bird hunting. The most common animals to hunt are deer, moose, and bear.

Food and Drink Scene of ZEC Batiscan-Neilson

Must-Try Dishes

Poutine

Poutine is a Canadian specialty dish authentic to Quebec, consisting of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy. It is a popular dish found all over Canada with slight variations.

Tourtiere

Tourtiere is a traditional meat pie dish originating from Quebec, containing cubed pork meat, veal, and beef served with starchy vegetables like potatoes and rutabagas.

Local Beverages

Ice Cider

Ice cider is a dessert cider made from frozen apple juice extracts, resulting in a sweet and syrupy texture with fruity and floral notes.

Unibroue Beer

Unibroue is a Quebec-based brewing company that produces premium Belgian-style ales, known for its strong flavors and aromas and its distinct color.

Where to Stay in ZEC Batiscan-Neilson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auberge Godefroy

Auberge Godefroy is a luxurious hotel offering customers a serene environment, restaurant services, spa services, and a golf course. It is a perfect choice for a comfortable stay.

Les Chalets de la Rivière

Les Chalets de la Rivière is a peaceful lodging option, offering visitors comfort and relaxation in a natural, serene environment. It is a few meters away from the river and offers some of the best views of the surrounding areas.

What to Buy in ZEC Batiscan-Neilson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Maple syrup is an iconic Canadian product originating from Quebec, and it is a must-buy when visiting ZEC Batiscan-Neilson. The region’s maple trees produce some of the best quality syrups in the world.

Indigenous Arts and Crafts
