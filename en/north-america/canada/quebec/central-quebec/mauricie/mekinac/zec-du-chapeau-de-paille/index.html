Introduction to ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille

ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille is located in Mékinac, in the heart of the beautiful Mauricie region of Central Quebec, Canada. This area is characterized by its diverse landscapes, including forests, lakes, and rivers, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors come from around the world to explore the natural beauty of ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille, and to experience the unique culture and history of this scenic region.

List of Places to Visit in ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée de la Résistance et de la Déportation de Mékinac: This museum showcases the history of the French Resistance movement during World War II and the stories of those who were deported.
  • Église de Saint-Tite: A beautiful church located in the nearby town of Saint-Tite. It features unique architecture and a rich history dating back to the 1800s.
  • Site patrimonial et écologique de la Pointe-aux-Orignaux: A heritage and ecological site along the Saint-Maurice River that includes historical artifacts, natural exhibits, and walking trails.

Natural Attractions of ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lac au Sorcier (Wizard Lake): One of the most popular lakes in the region for fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • Rivière du Loup: A scenic river ideal for canoeing, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Parc Régional de la rivière Batiscan: A provincial park that offers hiking trails, camping sites, and river activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille

  • Sentier de la Traverse: A 17 km trail that crosses the northern part of the ZEC. It offers spectacular views and is a popular spot for hikers and mountain bikers.
  • Sentier de l’Érablière: A 3 km trail that leads to a sugar shack and provides an easy hike or bike ride for individuals or families.

Best Things to Do in ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: ZEC du Chapeau-de-Paille is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, especially for trout and walleye.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The region is home to many rivers and lakes that are ideal for canoeing and kayaking trips.
  • ATV Tours: Visitors can enjoy ATV tours through the forested areas of the ZEC.
  • Hunting: The region is known for its hunting opportunities, especially for large game such as deer and moose.
