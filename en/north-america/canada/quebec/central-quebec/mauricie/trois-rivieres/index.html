Introduction to Trois-Rivières

Nestled in the heart of the Mauricie region, in central Quebec, lies Trois-Rivières, a charming city with deep historical roots and a vibrant culture. Despite being the second oldest city in Quebec, Trois-Rivières is often overlooked by travelers, but those who make the journey are in for a treat. With a population of just over 130,000 people, Trois-Rivières offers a perfect balance of big-city amenities and small-town charm.

List of Places to Visit in Trois-Rivières

From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Trois-Rivières offers something for everyone. Here is a list of the must-visit attractions in Trois-Rivières.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boréalis Museum: A beautifully restored historic mill that showcases the history of the pulp and paper industry in Trois-Rivières.

  • Musée québécois de culture populaire: A museum dedicated to local popular culture, displaying exhibits on traditional Quebecois life, music, and art.

  • Old Prison of Trois-Rivières: A former prison that has been transformed into a museum showcasing the history of the prison system in Quebec.

  • Cathédrale de l’Assomption: A beautiful cathedral built in 1854, which features Gothic Revival architecture and stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Trois-Rivières: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Laviolette: A waterfront park with many paths for walking and biking, as well as a beach and picnic areas.

  • Mondial des cultures de Drummondville: A yearly festival dedicated to world cultures featuring dance performances, music, and food.

  • Parc National de la Mauricie: An outdoor wonderland with over 536 square km of scenic views, hiking trails, swimming, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trois-Rivières

Trois-Rivières has several excellent hiking and biking routes that offer fantastic views of the city and surrounding natural landscapes. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Parc Laviolette Loop: A 9 km bike trail that circles Trois-Rivières on the St. Lawrence River.

  • La Coulée verte: A paved bike path along the Saint-Maurice River.

  • Parc National de la Mauricie: This park has dozens of hiking trails ranging from easy 1 km walks to multi-day backpacking trips.

Best Things to Do in Trois-Rivières: Activities and Tours

Trois-Rivières offers a wide range of activities and tours that showcase the best of the city and surrounding areas. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Kayak or canoe on the Saint-Maurice River: Paddle down this scenic river and take in the beauty of the natural surroundings.

  • Treetop Trekking: Located in the nearby forest, this activity is perfect for those who love nature and adventure.

  • Whale watching: Take a guided tour to see these majestic creatures in the nearby Saint Lawrence River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trois-Rivières

With so much to see and do, it’s easy to spend a full week exploring Trois-Rivières and surrounding areas. Here is a suggested itinerary that covers the highlights:

Day 1 - Walk around downtown Trois-Rivières, visit the Boréalis Museum, and dine at a local restaurant.

Day 2 - Visit the area around the Saint-Maurice River, including La Coulée verte and the Old Prison Museum.

Day 3 - Take a guided whale watching tour and explore the coastline.

Day 4 - Visit Parc National de la Mauricie and hike one of the many trails.

Day 5 - Discover the local culture and try traditional Quebecois dishes at a local restaurant.

Day 6 - Take a day trip to the nearby city of Drummondville to visit the Mondial des cultures festival.

Day 7 - Spend a relaxing day at Parc Laviolette, swimming, biking, and taking in the beautiful views.

Food and Drink Scene of Trois-Rivières

When it comes to food, Trois-Rivières has a lot to offer, from traditional Quebecois dishes to global cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Quebecois dish made with french fries, gravy, and cheese curds.

  • Tourtière: A savory meat pie that is a staple in Quebecois cuisine.

  • Maple syrup: Quebec is known for producing some of the world’s best maple syrup, which can be found in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Unibroue Beer: Trois-Rivières is home to one of the world’s best breweries, which produces award-winning beers.

  • Cider: Local cideries produce some of the freshest and most delicious cider in Quebec, a must-try for any cider lover.

Where to Stay in Trois-Rivières: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trois-Rivières has many accommodation options for all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Delta Hotels by Marriott Trois-Rivières Conference Centre: A modern and comfortable hotel with great views of the city.

  • Auberge Internationale de Trois-Rivières: A cozy and affordable hostel that offers a great location and friendly staff.

  • Gîte Saint-Laurent: A charming bed and breakfast with a beautiful garden and a great location near downtown Trois-Rivières.

What to Buy in Trois-Rivières: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trois-Rivières has a range of unique and locally made souvenirs that are perfect for taking home as gifts or keepsakes. Here are some of the top items:

  • Maple syrup: A classic Quebecois product that is a must-buy for anyone visiting the province.

  • Local artwork: Many local artists sell their beautiful paintings, sculptures, and crafts in Trois-Rivières

Local Culture and Customs of Trois-Rivières

The culture of Trois-Rivières is deeply tied to Quebecois history and tradition. The city values its French-speaking heritage and is known for its love of music, art, and food. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and try to learn some basic French phrases if possible.

Best Time to Visit Trois-Rivières

The best time to visit Trois-Rivières is from June to September when the weather is warm and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trois-Rivières

Do:

  • Try traditional Quebecois cuisine.

  • Visit local museums and historical sites.

  • Take part in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Be rude to locals or disregard the local customs.

  • Litter or damage the beautiful natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trois-Rivières

Trois-Rivières is a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling in an unfamiliar area, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables hidden from view.

Health in Trois-Rivières

The healthcare system in Quebec is of a high standard, and travelers should have no issues finding a pharmacy or doctor if needed.

Getting to Trois-Rivières: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trois-Rivières is easily accessible by car, bus, or train. Here are some of the most convenient ways to get there:

Airports Near Trois-Rivières

  • Montreal-Trudeau International Airport: This airport is approximately 150 km from Trois-Rivières.

  • Quebec City Jean Lesage International Airport: This airport is approximately 120 km from Trois-Rivières.

Bus Stations Near Trois-Rivières

  • Trois-Rivières bus station: Located in downtown Trois-Rivières and offering service to several key destinations in Quebec.

Other Options

  • Via Rail Canada: Train service is available from several major cities in Canada to Trois-Rivières.

How to Get Around in Trois-Rivières

The best way to get around Trois-Rivières is by car, as many attractions are spread out. However, there are also many biking and walking paths available, and public transportation is an option for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Trois-Rivières: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trois-Rivières is located in the heart of Quebec, making it an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Quebec City: Just a two-hour drive from Trois-Rivières, Quebec City is a beautiful and historic city with cobblestone streets, castle-like architecture, and great restaurants.

  • Montreal: About a two-and-a-half-hour drive from Trois-Rivières, Montreal is the largest city in Quebec and offers a vibrant arts scene, delicious dining options, and historic architecture.

Conclusion

Trois-Rivières is an underrated destination in Quebec that offers a perfect balance of history, outdoor adventure, and great food. Whether you are interested in hiking, biking, or trying traditional Quebecois cuisine, Trois-Rivières has something to offer every type of traveler. So why not plan a trip to this charming city and discover its many delights for yourself?
