Introduction to ZEC de la Croche

ZEC de la Croche is a nature reserve located in the La Tuque Region, in Mauricie, Central Quebec, Canada. It covers an area of 352 km2 and is home to a variety of wildlife, including moose, deer, black bear, and beaver. This nature reserve is known for its beautiful lakes, rivers, and forests, and offers visitors the opportunity to enjoy outdoor activities such as fishing, hiking, and kayaking.

List of Places to Visit in ZEC de la Croche

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Doré archaeological site
  • Chemin de Croix, a religious path with 14 wooden crosses
  • Musee du Lac Saint-Jean, located in Alma, Quebec, showcasing the history and culture of the region

Natural Attractions of ZEC de la Croche: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lac Croche, a glacial lake with crystal-clear water that is ideal for fishing and kayaking
  • La Croche River, a popular spot for whitewater kayaking and canoeing
  • Mont du lac des cygnes, a small mountain that offers stunning views of the surrounding area

Hiking/Biking Routes of ZEC de la Croche

  • La Croche Mountains Trail, a 7 km trail that takes visitors through the mountains and offers stunning views of the reserve
  • Sentier de la Savane, a 2 km trail that takes visitors through a savanna ecosystem
  • La Décharge Loop Trail, a 8 km trail that leads to a waterfall and a beaver pond

Best Things to Do in ZEC de la Croche: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing, with the possibility of catching brook trout, pike, and walleye
  • Kayaking and canoeing
  • Wildlife watching, with the possibility of seeing moose, deer, and beavers
  • Snowmobiling and cross-country skiing in the winter

Food and Drink Scene of ZEC de la Croche

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine, a Canadian dish made of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy
  • Tourtière, a meat pie that is a specialty of the region
  • Cipaille, also known as “six-penny pie,” a traditional meat pie made with game meat

Local Beverages

  • Unibroue beer, a brewery located in Chambly, Quebec, known for its Belgian-style beers
  • Ice cider, a sweet dessert wine made from frozen apples

Where to Stay in ZEC de la Croche: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge l’Ambassadeur, a hotel located in La Tuque with comfortable rooms and a restaurant
  • Gîte La Jeunesse, a charming guesthouse with a garden and a terrace
  • La Ferme du Péché Mignon, a farm stay with animals and activities for children

What to Buy in ZEC de la Croche: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup, a traditional Canadian product that is made from the sap of maple trees
  • Blueberry products, including jam, syrup, and fresh berries, which are grown abundantly in the region

Local Culture and Customs of ZEC de la Croche

  • French is the primary language spoken in the region, but English is also widely spoken
  • The region has a significant Indigenous population, and visitors should be respectful of their culture and traditions

Best Time to Visit ZEC de la Croche

The best time to visit ZEC de la Croche is from May to October. The weather is warm and sunny during these months, making it ideal for outdoor activities. In the winter, the reserve is covered in snow, and visitors can enjoy snowmobiling and cross-country skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of ZEC de la Croche

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife by not littering or disturbing animals
  • Learn about the region’s Indigenous culture and traditions
  • Bring appropriate gear and clothing for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Pick flowers or disturb vegetation
  • Feed or approach wild animals, as this can be dangerous for both visitors and the animals

Safety and Health

Safety in ZEC de la Croche

  • Visitors should be aware of the risk of tick bites and should take precautions to avoid them
  • It is recommended to avoid hiking alone and to inform someone of your planned route and estimated return time

Health in ZEC de la Croche

  • There are no major health concerns in the region, but visitors should bring any necessary medication with them
  • It is recommended to bring mosquito repellent during the summer months to avoid mosquito bites
