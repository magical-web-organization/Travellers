A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé

Nestled in the heart of the Mauricie region in Quebec, Canada, Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé is a charming city with natural beauty, historical sites, and outdoor adventures. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your itinerary when visiting Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé.

Introduction to Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé

Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé is a small city that was named after the parish of St. Edward the Confessor and the Maskinongé River, which runs through the city. With a population of just over 2,000 residents, Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé is a peaceful place to visit and escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Maison Louis-Fréchette

Maison Louis-Fréchette is a museum dedicated to the life and work of Louis Fréchette, a Quebec poet. The museum is located in Louis Fréchette’s house, and visitors can learn about his life, his work, and his contributions to Quebec literature.

Musée des Religions du Monde

Musée des Religions du Monde is a museum dedicated to the study of religions from around the world. The museum has a large collection of religious art, artifacts, and documents, and visitors can learn about the beliefs and practices of various religions.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parc des Chutes de Sainte-Ursule

Parc des Chutes de Sainte-Ursule is a beautiful park with waterfalls, hiking trails, and picnic areas. Visitors can enjoy a scenic hike through the park, relax by the waterfalls, or have a picnic with their family or friends.

L’Île-d’Entrée

L’Île-d’Entrée is an island located in the Maskinongé River, where visitors can enjoy swimming, kayaking, and fishing. The island is also home to a campground, where visitors can spend the night surrounded by nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé

Le Grand R

Le Grand R is a hiking trail that runs for 100 km through the Maskinongé region. The trail is divided into nine sections, and visitors can choose to hike the entire trail or just a section of it. The trail offers spectacular views of the region’s lakes, rivers, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé: Activities and Tours

Canoeing and Kayaking on the Maskinongé River

The Maskinongé River is a popular destination for canoeing and kayaking. Visitors can rent a canoe or kayak at one of the many rental locations in the city and paddle down the river, enjoying the beautiful scenery.

Poutine

Poutine is a popular Quebecois dish made with french fries, gravy, and cheese curds. Visitors to Saint-Édouard-de-Maskinongé can try this delicious dish at many local restaurants.
