Introduction to Maskinongé (municipality)

Maskinongé (municipality) is a small town located in the Maskinongé Region of Mauricie in Central Quebec. The town is known for its vibrant culture and stunning natural beauty. Visitors to Maskinongé (municipality) will find themselves surrounded by picturesque landscapes, including forests, lakes, and rolling hills.

The town is also known for its rich history, dating back to the 17th century. Visitors to Maskinongé (municipality) can explore the town’s historic sites and museums to learn more about the town’s heritage and cultural significance. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventure or a cultural getaway, Maskinongé (municipality) has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Maskinongé (municipality)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Village Historique de Val-Jalbert: This living history museum takes visitors back to the early 20th century and showcases the life of a small industrial village.
  • Musée des Religions du Monde: This museum houses a collection of more than 1,000 religious artifacts from around the world, offering a fascinating glimpse into various cultures and beliefs.
  • Musée des Ursulines: This museum is dedicated to the history of the Ursuline nuns, who played a significant role in the development of the region.

Natural Attractions of Maskinongé (municipality): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc National de la Mauricie: This national park covers 536 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including moose, beavers, and black bears. Visitors can enjoy hiking, skiing, and snowshoeing with stunning views of the park’s lakes and forests.
  • Lac Maskinongé: This beautiful lake is the perfect destination for water sports enthusiasts, offering opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Sentier de la Vallée du Bras-du-Nord: This breathtaking hiking trail is located just outside of the town and offers stunning views of the valley and river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maskinongé (municipality)

  • Parc de la Rivière Batiscan: This park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, including a 12-kilometer trail that loops around the Batiscan River.
  • Parc de la Rivière Yamaska: This park offers more than 30 kilometers of hiking and biking trails through forests and along the river, providing stunning views throughout.

Best Things to Do in Maskinongé (municipality): Activities and Tours

  • Le Baluchon: This eco-resort offers a variety of activities, including horseback riding, canoeing, and snowshoeing.
  • Camping Domain du Lac St-Pierre: This campsite offers a range of activities, including canoeing, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Le Village Enchanteur: This holiday village offers a range of activities throughout the year, including a pumpkin festival in the fall and a Christmas market in the winter.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maskinongé (municipality)

  • Day 1: Visit the Village Historique de Val-Jalbert and explore the town’s history.
  • Day 2: Explore the Musée des Religions du Monde and the Musée des Ursulines.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a day of outdoor adventure at the Parc National de la Mauricie.
  • Day 4: Take a hike or bike ride on one of the many trails in the Parc de la Rivière Batiscan.
  • Day 5: Spend the day at Lac Maskinongé, swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Day 6: Visit Le Baluchon eco-resort for horseback riding, canoeing, and snowshoeing.
  • Day 7: Head to Le Village Enchanteur for a day of fun seasonal activities.
