Introduction to Saint-Maurice

Nestled in the heart of Central Quebec’s Les Chenaux region, Saint-Maurice is a charming village known for its rich history, picturesque landscapes, and outdoor recreational opportunities. The town of Saint-Maurice is located approximately 2 hours northeast of Montreal and is accessible by car or public transportation. Visitors to Saint-Maurice can expect to experience a unique blend of French and Canadian cultures, a laid-back lifestyle, and unparalleled natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Maurice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Seigneurial Mill: One of the oldest industrial buildings in Canada, now a museum that showcases the history of milling and its importance to the region’s economy.

  • The Ursulines Museum: A 300-year-old convent-turned-museum that highlights the role the Ursulines played in educating girls in Quebec and North America.

  • The Mauricie National Park: A vast wilderness of forests, lakes, rivers, and mountains that offers visitors a wealth of outdoor activities such as hiking, canoeing, fishing, and camping.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Maurice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saint-Maurice River: A picturesque river that flows through the village and provides visitors with opportunities for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing.

  • Saint-Maurice Wildlife Reserve: A vast nature reserve that offers nature enthusiasts the chance to observe wildlife in its natural habitat.

  • La Baie du Febvre: A popular spot for bird watching, biking, and hiking that offers incredible views of the Saint-Maurice River and surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Maurice

  • Sentier national: A 1,100-kilometer trail that crosses Quebec connecting southern Quebec’s Frontenac National Park with the northern reaches of the province’s La Mauricie National Park. This trail passes by Saint-Maurice and offers visitors the opportunity to experience the diverse landscapes of Quebec.

  • The Devil Jump Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail that follows a historic logging route and leads visitors to an impressive waterfall and scenic viewpoints.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Maurice: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: The Saint-Maurice River is an ideal spot for water-based adventures, with options for guided tours or rentals available.

  • Fishing: The Saint-Maurice River is recognized for its trophy-sized Atlantic salmon, as well as its bass and trout populations.

  • Village Tours: Take a self-guided or guided walking tour through the village to learn about its history and unique architecture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Maurice

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Seigneurial Mill and take a guided tour of the building and a demonstration of its old machinery.
Day 2 Explore the Mauricie National Park, with hiking, paddling, and wildlife watching opportunities.
Day 3 Take a biking tour along the Devil Jump Trail and enjoy the scenic views along the way.
Day 4 Visit the Ursulines Museum and learn about the history of the convent and the role of the Ursulines in the region.
Day 5 Spend the day fishing in the Saint-Maurice River.
Day 6 Take a kayaking or canoeing tour on the Saint-Maurice River.
Day 7 Souvenir shopping before departing Saint-Maurice.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Maurice

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A popular Canadian dish that consists of fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

  • Tourtière: A meat pie traditionally made with pork and spices.

  • Maple Syrup Taffy: A sticky treat made from heated maple syrup that’s poured onto snow and then rolled onto sticks.

Local Beverages

  • Microbreweries: Saint-Maurice has a thriving microbrewery scene so be sure to try the local craft beers.

  • Ice Cider: A popular beverage made by freezing apples before pressing them, resulting in a sweet, cider-like drink ideal for cold winter days.

Where to Stay in Saint-Maurice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge Saint-Maurice: A charming and cozy guesthouse ideal for couples or solo travelers.

  • The Wood Cabin: A rustic cabin ideal for families or groups looking for a peaceful retreat.

  • Hotel Marineau: A luxurious hotel with a spa and in-house restaurant.

What to Buy in Saint-Maurice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Quebec is famous for its maple syrup, and Saint-Maurice has several local producers.

  • Handicrafts: Local artisans produce beautiful items such as pottery, hand-knit items, and jewelry.

  • Beer and Cider: Bring home some locally brewed beer or ice cider as a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Maurice

Saint-Maurice’s culture is deeply rooted in Quebec and French traditions, with a reverence for natural beauty and outdoor activities. The locals are welcoming and friendly, so make sure to engage them in conversation and learn more about the village and the surrounding region.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Maurice

The best time to visit Saint-Maurice is from late May through September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in their prime. However, for those who enjoy winter sports and activities, the winter months are also an option.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Maurice

Do:

  • Be respectful towards local customs and traditions.

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor recreational opportunities.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore public safety warnings when engaging in outdoor activities.

  • Litter or damage natural environments.

  • Disrespect local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Maurice

Saint-Maurice is a safe village with low crime rates. However, visitors are always advised to take appropriate precautions when engaging in any outdoor recreational activities.

Health in Saint-Maurice

Health services are readily available in Saint-Maurice, with several clinics and hospitals nearby. Visitors coming from outside Canada should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Saint-Maurice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Maurice

Quebec City Jean Lesage International Airport (YQB) is the closest airport to Saint-Maurice, located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes by car.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Maurice

Saint-Maurice is accessible by bus, with several stations close by, including the Trois-Rivières bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can choose to drive to Saint-Maurice, with several car rental agencies available in Quebec City and Montreal.

How to Get Around in Saint-Maurice

The best way to get around Saint-Maurice is by car, as the village is spread out. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or take advantage of the public transportation options.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Maurice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Quebec City: A short drive southwest of Saint-Maurice, Quebec City is a charming and historic city that’s a must-visit for those interested in Canadian history and culture.

  • Shawinigan: Located on the western shore of the Saint-Maurice River, Shawinigan is known for its manufacturing history and now boasts several popular tourist destinations.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and laid-back lifestyle, Saint-Maurice is the perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Quebec experience. From outdoor recreational opportunities to cultural and historical landmarks and unique food and drink experiences, Saint-Maurice has something for everyone. Plan your trip today to discover all that this charming village has to offer.
