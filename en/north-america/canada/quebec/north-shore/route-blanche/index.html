Introduction to Route Blanche

Route Blanche is a scenic road that runs along the North Shore of the St. Lawrence River in Quebec, Canada. Known for its stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains, Route Blanche is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and travelers looking to experience the beauty of Canada’s natural landscape.

List of Places to Visit in Route Blanche

Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions or outdoor adventures, Route Blanche has something to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places on this scenic road trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manoir Fraser: A historic mansion built in the early 19th century that now serves as a museum and cultural center.
  • Musee Regional de la Cote-Nord: This museum showcases the history and culture of the North Shore region of Quebec.
  • Site Historique Maritime de la Pointe-au-Pere: This historic site features a museum, lighthouse, and shipwreck exhibit.

Natural Attractions of Route Blanche: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc national du Bic: This national park is known for its stunning vistas of the St. Lawrence River, as well as its hiking and biking trails.
  • Tadoussac: This charming village is known for its whale-watching tours and stunning natural scenery.
  • Saguenay Fjord National Park: This park features a 100 km long fjord that is home to several species of marine mammals and a popular spot for kayaking and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Route Blanche

If you’re looking to explore the outdoors on foot or bike, Route Blanche has plenty of options. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area include:

  • Sentier Les Caps: A 22 km hiking trail that runs along the Cap-Chat River and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Parc national du Bic: This national park has over 25 km of hiking trails that offer scenic views of the St. Lawrence River and surrounding landscape.
  • Piste cyclable du littoral: A 256 km bike path that runs along the St. Lawrence River and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Route Blanche: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for unique and exciting activities to do in Route Blanche, here are some ideas:

  • Whale-watching tours: Take a guided boat tour and spot humpback and beluga whales.
  • Zip-lining: Soar over the treetops and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Fishing: Cast your line and try your luck at catching salmon or trout in the rivers and lakes of Route Blanche.

Food and Drink Scene of Route Blanche

Quebec is known for its delicious cuisine, and Route Blanche is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Quebecois dish made with french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Tourtiere: A savory meat pie that is a staple of Quebec cuisine.
  • Sugar pie: This dessert is made with maple syrup and has a sweet, gooey texture.

Local Beverages

  • Ice cider: This sweet, dessert-like cider is made with apples that have been frozen on the tree.
  • Caribou: A warm alcoholic drink made with red wine, whiskey, and maple syrup.
  • Craft beer: Route Blanche is home to several local breweries that offer a variety of craft beers.

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Route Blanche has plenty of options for places to stay. Here are some recommendations:

  • Auberge Saint-Antoine: This boutique hotel in Quebec City offers luxurious rooms and a prime location in the heart of the city.
  • Auberge Beausejour: This charming inn is located in the village of Saint-Simeon and offers cozy rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

Local Culture and Customs of Route Blanche

Quebec has a rich history and unique culture that is well worth exploring during your visit to Route Blanche. Here are some customs and cultural practices to be aware of:

  • Language: French is the primary language spoken in Quebec.
  • Dress: The culture in Quebec tends to be more formal than in other parts of North America, and visitors may want to dress accordingly.
  • Festivals: Quebec is known for its festivals, and there are several throughout the year that offer a chance to experience local culture and traditions.
