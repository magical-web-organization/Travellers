Introduction to Havre-Saint-Pierre

Havre-Saint-Pierre is a charming coastal town located in the Côte-Nord region of Quebec, Canada. With stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy, Havre-Saint-Pierre is a must-visit destination for travelers.

Whether you want to explore the town’s historic landmarks, hike or bike through its beautiful countryside, or simply kick back and relax on the beach, there’s something for everyone in Havre-Saint-Pierre. In this guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Quebec.

List of Places to Visit in Havre-Saint-Pierre

Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Havre-Saint-Pierre:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Le Vieux Poste de Havre-Saint-Pierre: This historic site was originally a trading post and fort that served as a hub for the fur trade in the 1800s. Today, it houses a museum that showcases the region’s history and culture.
  • Eglise Saint-Pierre-aux-Liens: This beautiful 19th-century church is a prominent landmark in the town and is well worth a visit.
  • Centre d’interpretation de l’histoire de la Cote-Nord: This museum provides fascinating insights into the region’s history and culture, including its First Nations heritage.

Natural Attractions of Havre-Saint-Pierre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc National d’Anticosti: This stunning national park is located on Anticosti Island, just a short boat ride from Havre-Saint-Pierre. It’s known for its rugged coastline, pristine beaches, and diverse wildlife, including caribou, moose, and seals.
  • Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve: Another nearby national park, Mingan Archipelago is a collection of islands and islets that feature stunning rock formations, hiking trails, and views of the Gulf of St. Lawrence.
  • Cap desrosiers lighthouse: Located at the tip of the Gaspe Peninsula, this lighthouse is one of the tallest in Canada and offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding countryside.
  • Baie-Johan-Beetz: A charming fishing village located a short distance from Havre-Saint-Pierre, offering visitors a glimpse of authentic Quebecois culture and traditions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Havre-Saint-Pierre

Havre-Saint-Pierre is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the top routes to check out include:

  • Sentier des Deux Montagnes: This 9-km trail takes you through the heart of Havre-Saint-Pierre and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Mont-Sainte-Anne: This scenic mountain offers a range of hiking and biking trails, including the challenging 8-km hike to the summit, which rewards visitors with sweeping panoramas of the Gulf of St. Lawrence.

Best Things to Do in Havre-Saint-Pierre: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with others, there are plenty of things to do in Havre-Saint-Pierre to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Whale watching: Havre-Saint-Pierre is one of the top places in Quebec to spot whales, including humpbacks, finbacks, and minke whales. Tours depart regularly from the town’s marina and offer the chance to see these majestic creatures up close.
  • Sea kayaking: Paddle your way through the crystal-clear waters of the Gulf of St. Lawrence and explore hidden coves and beaches along the coast.
  • Fishing: If you’re a fan of fishing, Havre-Saint-Pierre offers the perfect opportunity to cast your line and catch some of the region’s famous Atlantic salmon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Havre-Saint-Pierre

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Havre-Saint-Pierre, here’s an example itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Havre-Saint-Pierre and explore the town’s historic landmarks, including Le Vieux Poste and Eglise Saint-Pierre-aux-Liens.

Day 2: Take a whale watching tour and spot some of the region’s famous marine life.

Day 3: Hike or bike along the Sentier des Deux Montagnes trail and enjoy the beautiful natural scenery.

Day 4: Visit the Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve and explore its stunning rock formations and beaches.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Baie-Johan-Beetz and immerse yourself in Quebecois culture and traditions.

Day 6: Go sea kayaking or fishing and experience the region’s outdoor adventures firsthand.

Day 7: Visit Parc National d’Anticosti and explore its rugged coastline and diverse wildlife before heading back to Havre-Saint-Pierre.

Food and Drink Scene of Havre-Saint-Pierre

No trip to Havre-Saint-Pierre would be complete without sampling some of the region’s delicious food and drink. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtiere: A savory meat pie that’s a staple of Quebecois cuisine.
  • Poutine: A dish of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy that’s become a Canadian classic.
  • Cipaille: A layered meat pie that’s traditionally baked for hours in a covered pot.

Local Beverages

  • La Chouape: This local brewery produces a range of delicious craft beers that are perfect for sipping after a long day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Havre-Saint-Pierre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Havre-Saint-Pierre offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Hotel Tadoussac: This historic hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, along with a range of amenities including a spa, restaurant, and pool.
  • Gite du Havre: This cozy bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms and a warm welcome from the friendly hosts.
  • Auberge La Salicorne: This unique property features eco-friendly accommodations in the form of yurts and cabins, along with a range of activities including sea kayaking and whale watching tours.

What to Buy in Havre-Saint-Pierre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Havre-Saint-Pierre, here are a few ideas:

  • Handmade crafts: There are plenty of local artisans in Havre-Saint-Pierre selling handmade crafts such as pottery, woodworking, and jewelry.
  • Local food products: Stock up on local specialties like smoked salmon, maple syrup, and Quebecois cheese to bring home and share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Havre-Saint-Pierre

Like many parts of Quebecois culture, Havre-Saint-Pierre has its own unique customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind when visiting include:

  • French is the primary language spoken in Havre-Saint-Pierre, although many locals also speak English.
  • Quebecois food and drink, particularly poutine and craft beer, are an important part of the local culture and should be experienced firsthand.

Best Time to Visit Havre-Saint-Pierre

The best time to visit Havre-Saint-Pierre depends on your interests and preferences. Summer (June to August) is the peak tourist season and offers warm weather, plenty of outdoor activities, and long hours of daylight. Fall (September to November) is a great time to visit if you enjoy seeing the fall foliage and want to avoid the crowds. Winter (December to February) is the least popular time to visit, but offers the chance to experience winter sports and activities like snowmobiling and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Havre-Saint-Pierre

When visiting Havre-Saint-Pierre, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the region’s outdoor activities and natural attractions.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Havre-Saint-Pierre

Havre-Saint-Pierre is generally a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe. Some tips to follow include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Lock your car doors and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities, such as wearing appropriate gear and avoiding dangerous or risky behaviors.

Health in Havre-Saint-Pierre

There are no major health concerns or risks associated specifically with Havre-Saint-Pierre. However, as with any travel destination, it’s always a good idea to be up-to-date on your routine vaccinations and to take appropriate precautions to avoid common illnesses like colds and flu.

Getting to Havre-Saint-Pierre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Havre-Saint-Pierre is located on the northeastern side of Quebec and is somewhat isolated from other major cities and population centers. However, there are several transportation options available for getting there.

Airports Near Havre-Saint-Pierre

The closest airport to Havre-Saint-Pierre is Sept-Iles Airport, which is approximately a three-hour drive away. Alternately, visitors can fly into Quebec City or Montreal and then drive or take a bus to Havre-Saint-Pierre.

Bus Stations Near Havre-Saint-Pierre

There are several bus companies that offer service to Havre-Saint-Pierre, including Orleans Express and Intercar. Buses depart regularly from Quebec City, Montreal, and other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Havre-Saint-Pierre by car, either by driving themselves or by renting a vehicle. However, it’s important to note that the town is somewhat isolated and driving conditions can be challenging in certain seasons.

How to Get Around in Havre-Saint-Pierre

The best way to get around in Havre-Saint-Pierre is by car or bike, as many of the town’s attractions and activities are spread out across a wide area. However, there is also a local taxi service available for visitors who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Havre-Saint-Pierre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip and explore more of the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Tadoussac: This picturesque village is located at the confluence of the Saguenay and St. Lawrence Rivers and is known for its stunning natural beauty, whale watching tours, and historic landmarks.
  • Gaspe Peninsula: Located to the east of Havre-Saint-Pierre, the Gaspe Peninsula is a scenic and rugged region known for its beautiful beaches, historic lighthouses, and vibrant Francophone culture.

Conclusion

Havre-Saint-Pierre is a destination that truly has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing and taking in the natural beauty of the region, this charming town has it all. With this guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your trip, make the most of your time in Havre-Saint-Pierre, and leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
