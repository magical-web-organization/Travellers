Introduction to Forestville

Nestled along the North Shore of Quebec lies the bustling yet quaint town of Forestville. It is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. This small town with a population of just over 4,000 people is a hidden gem known for its natural beauty, exceptional food and drink scene, and rich history.

Despite its size, Forestville has a lot to offer visitors, from outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore everything that Forestville has to offer, and help you plan the perfect trip to this charming town in Quebec, Canada.

List of Places to Visit in Forestville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maison Paul-Provencher: This historical house-museum is one of the most important cultural attractions in Forestville. It was the birthplace of Father Paul Provencher, who played a significant role in the development of Quebec and Canada.
  • Forestville Railway Station: The town of Forestville was originally a railway town, and the Forestville Railway Station is a testament to this history. The station has been restored and now houses a museum that showcases the town’s railway history.
  • Forestville Lighthouse: Located at the mouth of the Forestville River, this lighthouse has been guiding sailors since the 19th century. The lighthouse was rebuilt in 1958 and remains a significant landmark in the town.

Natural Attractions of Forestville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Nature de Pointe-aux-Outardes: This nature park is a must-visit attraction in Forestville. The park has trails that offer scenic views of the St. Lawrence River, and you can spot many species of birds and wildlife.
  • Parc des Deux Rivières: This park has hiking and biking trails that overlook the Forestville and the Outardes Rivers. It’s rated among the best places in Canada to view the fall foliage.
  • Montagne à Liguori: This mountain peak, located just south of the town, offers breathtaking views of the St. Lawrence River and the surrounding landscapes. The trails, covering almost 32 kilometers, are perfect for those seeking a challenging trek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forestville

Forestville has an extensive trail network that is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The trails are fitted with signboards for easy navigation and are mostly rated as moderate to difficult.

Here are a few of the top trails:

  • Sentier Center-de-Ski-de-Fond: This 13-kilometer trail is marked and groomed for cross-country skiing in winters and is perfect for hiking in summers.
  • Sentier de la Piste-aux-Pins: Located in the Parc Nature de Pointe-aux-Outardes, this trail is designed for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing. It’s a 3.5-kilometer trail and is suitable for beginners.
  • Sentier de la Rivière-Outardes: Located in the Parc des Deux Rivières, this trail is a 6.5-kilometer loop that offers beautiful views of the Outardes River. The trail is rated as moderate.

Best Things to Do in Forestville: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tour: The area around Forestville is abundant in marine life, and whale-watching tours are popular in the region. You can try a tour to see humpback, beluga, or grey whales.
  • River Rafting: Forestville’s rivers make it an excellent destination for river rafting enthusiasts. You can take a guided tour and experience the adrenaline rush while enjoying the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • ATV Tours: Forestville’s terrain is perfect for an all-terrain vehicle (ATV) tour. You can rent an ATV or join a guided tour and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Forestville

When it comes to food, Forestville has a lot to offer. The town is known for its local delicacies and fusion cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Quebecois dish that consists of fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Tourtière: A savory meat pie that originated in Quebec.
  • Gâteau aux bleuets: A blueberry cake that is a popular dessert in Forestville.

Local Beverages

  • La Fin du Monde: A beer brewed by the Unibroue brewery in nearby Chambly, Quebec.
  • Ice Cider: Made from apples that are left to freeze during late autumn, this cider is unique to Quebec.
  • Caribou: A traditional Quebecois alcoholic drink that is usually served during the winter.

  • Auberge de la Rivière Saguenay: A 4-star hotel located near the Saguenay Fjord. This classy hotel has upscale amenities such as an outdoor pool and a full-service restaurant.
  • Gîte La Maison du Jardinier: A charming 3-star bed and breakfast located in a peaceful countryside setting, a few kilometers north of Forestville.

What to Buy in Forestville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are a few unique souvenirs and local products that you can take home as a memento from your trip to Forestville:

  • Maple Syrup: Quebec is renowned for its maple syrup, and Forestville is no exception. You can buy maple syrup from local shops in town.
  • Local handicrafts: You can find handcrafted jewelry, woodwork, and knitwear in the local shops of Forestville.
  • Whale Bone Carvings:Forestville is known for its whale watching tours, and you can get whale bone carvings as a perfect souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Forestville

Forestville’s local culture and customs are influenced by its history as a railroad and forestry town. The town has a unique blend of French-Canadian and Indigenous cultures. In Forestville, it’s customary to greet people with a friendly “Bonjour” or “Salut.”

Best Time to Visit Forestville

The best time to visit Forestville is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and pleasant. The fall (September to November) is also a great season to visit, as the leaves change colors, and the forests become a beautiful shade of red and yellow. The winter months (December to February) can be cold, but if you’re a fan of winter sports, then this is the perfect time to visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forestville

Forestville is generally a safe town, but it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety. Ensure you lock your hotel room and vehicles.

Health in Forestville

Forestville has excellent healthcare facilities. However, it’s always advisable to have travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Forestville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Forestville is located about an hour’s drive east of Baie-Comeau. Here are a few transportation options to get to Forestville:

Airports Near Forestville

  • Baie-Comeau Airport: Located approximately 45 kilometers west of Forestville.

Bus Stations Near Forestville

  • Autobus Galland: This bus service operates services from Montreal, Quebec City, and Saguenay to Forestville.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car from nearby towns such as Baie-Comeau or Quebec City.
  • Taxis: Taxi services are available in Forestville.

How to Get Around in Forestville

Walking or biking around Forestville is the best way to explore the town’s attractions and natural beauty. You can also rent a car or an ATV to explore the nearby countryside.

Where to Go Next from Forestville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations to consider for your next trip:

  • Saguenay: A 2.5-hour drive north of Forestville, this town is a popular destination for outdoor activities, such as kayaking and hiking.
