Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Blanc-Sablon has something to offer for everyone. Introduction to Blanc-Sablon

Blanc-Sablon is a small coastal town located in eastern Quebec, Canada. The town is situated on the Blanc-Sablon Bay, which separates Labrador from Quebec. Blanc-Sablon is a gateway to the Lower North Shore region of Quebec and is surrounded by rugged landscapes, pristine lakes, and historic sites. The town has a rich history and is home to several interesting museums and historical places that showcase the local culture and heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Blanc-Sablon

When planning your trip to Blanc-Sablon, make sure to include the following must-visit attractions in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Blanc-Sablon has a rich history that is waiting to be explored. Here are some top museums and historical places you shouldn’t miss:

  • Blanc-Sablon Lighthouse: This lighthouse was built in 1880 and offers stunning views of the coastline and the sea. You can climb the stairs to the top and enjoy the panoramic views.
  • The Old School House Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Blanc-Sablon through artifacts, photographs, and other exhibits.
  • Cote Bleue Historical Site: This site is a former trading post dating back to the 17th century and offers a glimpse into the region’s past.

Natural Attractions of Blanc-Sablon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blanc-Sablon is blessed with natural beauty and offers several stunning outdoor attractions for nature lovers. Here are some top natural attractions:

  • Brador Bay Beach: This beautiful sandy beach is located in Brador Bay and is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Mont Babel Lookout offers breathtaking views of the coastline and the sea horizon. The lookout can be reached by hiking or driving.
  • Fishing at Blanc-Sablon Marina: Fishing enthusiasts shouldn’t miss the opportunity to try their luck at Blanc-Sablon Marina, where the waters are filled with trout, salmon, and other fish species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blanc-Sablon

Blanc-Sablon is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering several hiking and biking trails that wind through stunning natural scenery. Here are some of the top routes to try:

  • Brador trail is a 3.5 km long route that takes you through the forest and along the coast, offering stunning views of the sea.
  • Le Petit Boule hiking trail is a challenging and rewarding trail that takes you to the top of Mount Le Heurteloup for panoramic views of the area.
  • Brador to Lourdes-De-Blanc-Sablon bike route is a scenic bike route that takes you along the coastline and offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Blanc-Sablon: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there are plenty of things to do in Blanc-Sablon for travelers of all interests. Here are some top suggestions:

  • Whale Watching Tour: Take a boat tour to see the whales and puffins that inhabit the waters around Blanc-Sablon. This is an unforgettable experience that you will cherish forever.
  • Visit the Local Markets: Visit the local markets and galleries to discover the local crafts, foods, and art. You can meet the artisans and learn about their techniques and traditions.
  • Go Snowmobiling: In winter, snowmobiling is a popular activity in Blanc-Sablon. The snowy trails offer an exciting and unique experience for adventure seekers.

Food and Drink Scene of Blanc-Sablon

Blanc-Sablon has a rich culinary scene that is marked by seafood, game meat, and local produce. Here are some dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtière - meat pie made with game meat and spices
  • Poutine - French fries and cheese curds topped with gravy
  • Lobster and Crab - freshly caught from the nearby waters and served with butter
  • Seal meat - a traditional Inuit dish often served in stews or sausages

Local Beverages

  • Spruce beer - a non-alcoholic beverage made from young spruce needles and sugar
  • Ice Cider - an alcoholic apple cider that is produced in the cold winters

Where to Stay in Blanc-Sablon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blanc-Sablon has plenty of accommodation options that cater to every budget and taste. Here are some recommendations:

  • L’Escale L’Anse aux Meadows: This hotel is located on the waterfront and offers stunning views of the bay. The hotel has modern amenities, including a restaurant and bar.
  • Auberge de Blanc-Sablon: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves local dishes.
  • Camping Paradis Plein Air: This campground is located near the beach and offers amenities such as showers, laundry, and a convenience store.

What to Buy in Blanc-Sablon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Blanc-Sablon is home to several local artisans and craftsmen who produce unique and high-quality products. Here are some souvenirs and local products that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Handmade knitwear - hats, mittens, and scarves made from wool or sealskin
  • Local jams and preserves - made from locally sourced fruits and berries
  • Inuit carvings and sculptures - intricate carvings in soapstone and bone that depict traditional Inuit legends and stories

Local Culture and Customs of Blanc-Sablon

Blanc-Sablon is a culturally rich town that values its traditions and heritage. As a visitor, it is important to respect local customs and practices. Here are some customs and practices to keep in mind:

  • Learn a few French phrases - although most locals speak English, they appreciate visitors who try to speak French
  • Dress appropriately - avoid wearing revealing clothes or beachwear in public places
  • Follow the local customs - Inuit culture is an integral part of Blanc-Sablon, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the local customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Blanc-Sablon

The best time to visit Blanc-Sablon depends on the type of experience you are seeking. Summer (June-August) is the peak season and offers beautiful weather, long daylight hours, and plenty of outdoor activities. Winter (December-March) is ideal for sports such as snowmobiling, skiing, and ice-fishing. If you prefer a quieter and more affordable time to visit, you can consider visiting in the shoulder season (May, September, and October).

Do:

  • Speak French if possible

Safety and Health

Blanc-Sablon is a safe place to visit, but it is important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Blanc-Sablon

  • Beware of bears and other wildlife when hiking or camping
  • Follow the weather warnings and take necessary precautions when activities involve water.
  • Take precautions while driving on the snowy roads in winter

Health in Blanc-Sablon

  • Ensure that you are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling.
  • Pack any prescription medications that you need
  • Use insect repellents to protect against insect-borne diseases

Getting to Blanc-Sablon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Blanc-Sablon is a remote town, and transportation options are limited, but it is worth the effort of getting there. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Blanc-Sablon

  • Blanc-Sablon Airport (YBX) - This is the nearest airport that serves the town.

Bus Stations Near Blanc-Sablon

  • There is no direct bus service to Blanc-Sablon. However, you may connect through nearby cities like Havre-Saint-Pierre.

Other Options

  • Driving - You can reach Blanc-Sablon by driving from Quebec City, a distance of about 1200 km.
  • Ferries – you can take a ferry to Blanc-Sablon that departs from Newfoundland and Labrador.

How to Get Around in Blanc-Sablon

Blanc-Sablon is a compact town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, if you want to venture outside the town, you can consider renting a car or a snowmobile.

Where to Go Next from Blanc-Sablon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the beauty and the culture of Blanc-Sablon, you can consider visiting nearby towns such as Lourdes-de-Blanc-Sablon, or you can embark on an excursion to the stunning Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve. You can also take a ferry or drive to the nearby provinces or territories, such as Newfoundland and Labrador.

Conclusion

Blanc-Sablon is a truly unique travel destination that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventure. Whether you plan to explore the history of the town, hike the winding trails, indulge in the local cuisine or relax on the beach, Blanc-Sablon is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories that you will cherish for years to come.
