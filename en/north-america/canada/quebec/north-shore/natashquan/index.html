Introduction to Natashquan

Located on the eastern coast of Quebec’s North Shore, Natashquan is a small but charming village rich in natural beauty, history, and cultural traditions. Its name comes from the Innu language and means “where there is plenty of wood for making paddles”. Indeed, Natashquan has been known for its thriving fishing and forestry industries, which have sustained its local economy and communities over the centuries.

Today, Natashquan is an ideal destination for eco-tourism, outdoor activities, and cultural tourism. It offers scenic views of the St. Lawrence River, the surrounding forests, and a diverse range of flora and fauna. It is also home to several museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks that celebrate its Indigenous and French-Canadian heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all the top attractions and experiences in Natashquan, plan your trip, and travel confidently to this hidden gem of Quebec.

List of Places to Visit in Natashquan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maison René-Richard: This house museum showcases the life and works of René Richard, a renowned artist who lived in Natashquan for several decades and depicted the local landscapes and people in his paintings.
  • Musée de la maison Genre: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the local fishing community, including its tools, clothing, and daily life.
  • L’Échouerie: This cultural center hosts exhibitions, concerts, and workshops that focus on the Indigenous and Acadian heritage of Natashquan and the North Shore.
  • Notre-Dame-de-Lorette Church: This historic church dates back to the mid-19th century and features a beautiful altar, stained-glass windows, and a peaceful garden.

Natural Attractions of Natashquan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pointe-Parent: This rocky promontory offers breathtaking views of the St. Lawrence River, the surrounding islands, and the Gaspé Peninsula. It is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Cap-de-la-Madeleine: This rocky cape is a prime location for birdwatching, with many species of gulls, terns, and eagles nesting there. It is also a great spot for hunting and fishing.
  • Oasis de l’Île Verte: This island nature reserve is accessible by boat and features many trails, observation towers, and picnic areas. It is home to a variety of birds, mammals, and plants.
  • Rivière Natashquan: This river is a popular destination for canoeing, kayaking, and fishing. It flows through rocky canyons, lush forests, and calm meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Natashquan

Natashquan has many hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of its nature and culture. Some of the top routes are:

  • Sentier René-Richard: This 8-km trail starts at the Maison René-Richard and leads through the forest, over the hills, and along the riverbank.
  • Sentier des Pionniers: This 10-km trail starts at the Musée de la maison Genre and leads through the countryside, past old homesteads and historic landmarks.
  • Sentier de la Coulée d’Or: This 5-km trail starts at the Oasis de l’Île Verte and leads through the wetland, over the boardwalk, and past the observation tower.

Best Things to Do in Natashquan: Activities and Tours

Some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Natashquan are:

  • Whale Watching Tour: Take a boat tour to see the whales and seals that frequent the St. Lawrence River, including the impressive beluga whale.
  • Kayaking Tour: Explore the scenic river and coastline of Natashquan by kayak.
  • Fishing Tour: Catch some of the delicious fish that are abundant in the local waters, including salmon, trout, and eel.
  • Mushroom Picking Tour: Learn about the native mushrooms that grow in the forests and fields of Natashquan and how to identify and cook them.
  • Photography Tour: Capture the beautiful landscapes, wildlife, and cultural landmarks of Natashquan with the guidance of a professional photographer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Natashquan

Day 1: Explore the cultural heritage of Natashquan by visiting the Maison René-Richard, the Musée de la maison Genre, and the L’Échouerie cultural center.
Day 2: Take a whale watching tour and explore the Pointe-Parent and Cap-de-la-Madeleine scenic viewpoints.
Day 3: Go canoeing or kayaking on the Rivière Natashquan and have a picnic on its banks.
Day 4: Hike the Sentier René-Richard or the Sentier des Pionniers and have lunch at a local restaurant.
Day 5: Visit the Notre-Dame-de-Lorette Church, the Oasis de l’Île Verte, and the Sentier de la Coulée d’Or boardwalk.
Day 6: Go fishing or mushroom picking with a local guide and have a cooking lesson.
Day 7: Take a photography tour to capture the best shots of Natashquan and its surroundings, and enjoy a farewell dinner at a local tavern.

Food and Drink Scene of Natashquan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine à la truite: This local spin on the classic poutine features fresh trout instead of the traditional gravy and cheese curds. It is a tasty and healthy option for seafood lovers.
  • Tarte aux bleuets: This blueberry pie is a classic Quebec dessert that is made with wild blueberries and a flaky crust. It is best enjoyed with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or some whipped cream.
  • Saumon fumé: This smoked salmon is one of the main specialties of Natashquan, thanks to its abundant salmon stocks. It is typically served with crackers, cream cheese, and fresh herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Labrador tea: This herbal tea is made from the leaves of the Labrador tea plant, which grows abundantly in the area. It has a pleasant and refreshing taste, as well as some medicinal properties.
  • Caribou: This alcoholic drink is a combination of red wine, whisky, and maple syrup. It is a warming and festive beverage that is especially popular during the winter season.

Where to Stay in Natashquan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge de l’Anse: This charming inn offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a terrace with views of the St. Lawrence River.
  • Gîte chez Gilles: This cozy guesthouse features a shared kitchen, a living room, and a garden with a barbecue.
  • Camp de Base: This eco-lodge offers rustic cabins and tents, as well as guided tours and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Natashquan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • René Richard Prints: Take home a beautiful print or reproduction of one of René Richard’s famous paintings, depicting the landscapes and people of Natashquan.
  • Smoked Salmon: Bring back some of Natashquan’s famous smoked salmon, which is sold fresh or vacuum-sealed at local shops and markets.
  • Labrador Tea: Buy some dried or fresh Labrador tea leaves to make a healthy and tasty tea at home.
  • Artisanal Crafts: Choose from a variety of handmade crafts, such as knitting, weaving, and carving, that showcase the skills and traditions of the local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Natashquan

Natashquan has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that reflects the influences of Indigenous, French-Canadian, and Acadian traditions. Some of the key cultural practices and customs include:

  • Music and Dance: Natashquan has a lively folk music scene, which features fiddles, accordions, and guitars, as well as traditional dances such as the jig and the reel.
  • Religion: The Catholic Church has been a major influence on the cultural and social life of Natashquan, and many of its landmarks and festivals are linked to religious practices and beliefs.
  • Fishing and Hunting: Fishing and hunting have been a way of life for the people of Natashquan for generations, and they continue to play a significant role in the local economy and culture.
  • Community Spirit: Natashquan is known for its strong sense of community, where people help and support each other in times of need or celebration.

Best Time to Visit Natashquan

The best time to visit Natashquan depends on your preferences and interests. Here are some general tips:

  • Summer (June to August): This is the high season for tourism in Natashquan, thanks to the warmer weather, longer days, and many outdoor activities and festivals. However, it can also be crowded and expensive, so plan ahead and book early.
  • Fall (September to November): This is a quieter and more colorful time to visit Natashquan, as the leaves change into vibrant hues of gold and red.
  • Winter (December to March): This is a magical and quiet time to visit Natashquan, as the snow blankets the landscape and many winter sports and activities are available. However, the weather can be harsh and the days are shorter, so dress warmly and plan accordingly.
  • Spring (April to May): This is a transitional time to visit Natashquan, as the weather becomes milder and the nature awakens from winter sleep. It is also a good time to see the migration of birds and whales in the St. Lawrence River.

Do’s and Don’ts of Natashquan

Do:

  • Respect the local nature and wildlife: Natashquan is a precious ecosystem that needs to be preserved and respected, so avoid littering, disturbing animals, or damaging plants.
  • Learn some basic French: French is the official language of Quebec, and while most people in Natashquan are bilingual, it is appreciated when visitors make an effort to speak some French.
  • Try the local seafood: Natashquan is famous for its fresh and delicious seafood, so don’t miss a chance to taste some of the local specialties, such as smoked salmon, trout, or crab.
  • Listen to some traditional music: Natashquan has a vibrant and unique music scene, which reflects its heritage and culture. Attend a concert or a jam session and enjoy the melodies and rhythms of the local musicians.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property: Some of the natural and cultural attractions in Natashquan are located on private land, so be respectful and ask for permission before entering.
  • Ignore safety rules: Many of the outdoor activities in Natashquan, such as boating, hiking, or fishing, involve some risks and precautions. Follow the safety rules and guidelines provided by the local authorities or guides.
  • Be insensitive to local customs: Natashquan is a diverse and welcoming community, but it has its own customs and sensibilities. Avoid making insensitive or offensive remarks or gestures, and learn about the local culture and history before making assumptions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Natashquan

Natashquan is generally a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly people. However, some safety tips to keep in mind are:

  • Be aware of natural hazards: Natashquan is located in a remote and rugged area, and some outdoor activities can involve risks such as falling, drowning, or getting lost. Follow the safety rules and guidelines provided by the local authorities or guides, and bring appropriate gear and supplies.
  • Respect the wildlife: Natashquan is home to many wild animals, such as bears, moose, and wolves, which can be dangerous if provoked or surprised. Keep a safe distance and avoid feeding, approaching, or harassing them.

Health in Natashquan

Natashquan has a small medical clinic and pharmacy, but for emergencies or more serious health issues, it is advisable to seek medical attention in larger towns such as Havre-Saint-Pierre or Sept-Îles. Some health tips to keep in mind are:

  • Take precautions against ticks: Natashquan and the surrounding areas are known to have ticks that can transmit Lyme disease. Wear long-sleeved clothes, use tick repellent, and check your body for ticks after spending time in the outdoors.
  • Respect the local water quality: The water in the rivers and lakes of Natashquan is generally safe to drink, but it is advisable to bring a water filter or purifier, especially in the more remote areas.
  • Practice safe sex: Natashquan has a low prevalence of sexually transmitted diseases, but it is always recommended to practice safe sex and carry contraceptives.

Getting to Natashquan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Natashquan

The nearest airports to Natashquan are:

  • Sept-Îles Airport: This regional airport is located about 200 km south of Natashquan and offers daily flights to Quebec City and Montreal, as well as several other destinations in Quebec and Labrador.
  • Havre-Saint-Pierre Airport: This smaller airport is located about 100 km south of Natashquan and offers daily flights to Montreal and other regional destinations.

From the airports, you can rent a car, take a taxi, or book a shuttle to Natashquan.

Bus Stations Near Natashquan

There are no direct bus services to Natashquan, but you can take a bus to Havre-Saint-Pierre or Sept-Îles and then a taxi or shuttle to Natashquan. Some bus companies that serve these areas are:

  • Intercar: This bus company offers daily services from Quebec City and Montreal to Sept-Îles and Havre-Saint-Pierre.
  • Orléans Express: This bus company offers daily services from Quebec City and Montreal to Sept-Îles.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car from the airports or from major cities in Quebec and drive to Natashquan on Highway 138. The trip takes about 8 hours from Quebec City and 12 hours from Montreal.
  • Ferry: You can take a ferry from Rimouski to Blanc-Sablon and then drive to Natashquan. The ferry operates once a week during the summer season and takes about 10 hours.
  • Charter Flights: You can also book a charter flight from Quebec City or Montreal to Natashquan, especially if you have a large group or need more flexibility in your schedule.

How to Get Around in Natashquan

Natashquan is a small village that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel to nearby attractions or villages, you can rent a car, take a taxi, or book a shuttle. Some local taxi companies and shuttle services are:

  • Taxi l’Empreinte: This taxi company offers transportation services to and from Natashquan and other nearby communities, as well as airport transfers and tours.
  • Camp de Base: This eco-lodge also offers shuttle services to its guests and can arrange custom tours and excursions.

Where to Go Next from Natashquan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time and want to explore the surrounding areas, some of the nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Île d’Anticosti: This island nature reserve is located about 120 km west of Natashquan and is famous for its spectacular landscapes, wildlife, and fossil cliffs. Accessible by boat or plane.
  • Côte-Nord: This scenic region of Quebec stretches along the St. Lawrence River and offers many attractions, such as the Mingan Archipelago, the Manicouagan Crater, and the Saguenay Fjord. Accessible by car or bus.
  • Labrador: This vast and sparsely populated region of Canada is located to the east of Natashquan and offers rugged natural landscapes, Indigenous culture, and historic landmarks. Accessible by boat or plane.

Conclusion

Natashquan is a hidden gem of Quebec that offers a unique blend of nature and culture. It is a perfect destination for eco-tourism, outdoor activities, and cultural tourism, with its scenic views, rich history, and welcoming community. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Natashquan confidently and fully enjoy its natural and cultural marvels.
