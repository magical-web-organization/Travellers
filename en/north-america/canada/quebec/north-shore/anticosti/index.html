Anticosti Island, located in the Gulf of Saint Lawrence, is the world’s 20th largest island and a hidden gem of Quebec, Canada. The island is a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventure that awaits travelers from all over the world. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Anticosti Island with essential information about the best places to visit, the most exciting things to do, and plenty of tips and advice that will make your trip unforgettable.

Introduction to Anticosti

Anticosti Island is a haven for nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. The island boasts dramatic landscapes that are known for their rugged beauty, pristine forests, breathtaking waterfalls, and spectacular coastal views. The island is also home to several significant cultural sites and museums that showcase the history of the island. Visitors can explore the island’s unique flora and fauna, go on scenic hikes or bike rides, or embark on whale-watching tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anticosti

Day 1: Arrival in Anticosti Island Explore Port Menier, the principle port of the island. Walk along the wharf or spend time at a local café and bask in the picturesque views.

Day 2: Sightseeing and Museum Hopping Visit attractions of Anticosti and delve into the island’s past. Discover the history of the island at Beaumont Cove, the Pointe-Ouest Lighthouse, or the Port-Menier Wildlife Reserve.

Day 3: Outdoor Adventures Anticosti Island is an adventurer’s paradise. Go on a guided hiking tour or take a scenic boat trip and catch a glimpse of the local wildlife.

Day 4: Beach Day Take a tour of the beautiful beaches of Anticosti like the Chouinard Cove or the Chicotte Bay Cove. Take a swim in the warm waters and relax on the soft sand.

Day 5: Scenic Views and Photography Capture all the beauty of Anticosti Island with views of cliffs and fabulous sunsets. Head up to Jupiter’s Mud Lake or the Grassy Bay Cliffs to capture some of the island’s breathtaking views.

Day 6: Local Cuisine and Culture Spend the day exploring local cafes and trying out authentic island dishes. In the evening, head down to the Manoir Miguasha for some entertainment or catch a concert at the Chateau Madelinot.

Day 7: Departure from Anticosti Island Spend the day finishing up last-minute shopping and souvenirs. Leave Anticosti with amazing memories and return home with the stories of an exciting adventure.

Local Culture and Customs of Anticosti

The people of Anticosti Island have a rich culture and local customs that originate from their Acadian heritage. Visitors can expect to see that mirrored in the local cuisine and architecture.

Best Time to Visit Anticosti

The best season to visit Anticosti is during the summer months from June to August. During this time, the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities, and most attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anticosti

Do:

  • Respect the Island, wildlife, and other visitors
  • Dress appropriately; pack warm clothing for winter months
  • Follow all the sign’s instructions while hiking and biking

Don’t:

  • Take prohibited items like drugs, firearms, and fireworks.
  • Trespass or disturb the wildlife.
  • Litter or violate the island’s conservation protocols.

Safety and Health

Anticosti Island is generally safe, and there are no particular health threats of visiting. Visitors need to exercise safety precautions while exploring the island’s different attractions and outdoor activities.

Safety in Anticosti

  • Stay on the designated paths or trails while hiking
  • Wear appropriate safety gear while biking and on ATV tours.
  • Be mindful of the wildlife and follow park protocols while watching them.

Health in Anticosti

  • Bring mosquito repellent during the summer months.
  • Pack any necessary medications.
  • Ensure to drink only bottled water.

Getting to Anticosti: Transportation Options and Logistics

The only way to reach Anticosti Island is by taking a ferry ride from Havre-Saint-Pierre. Arriving at the port of entry in Anticosti Island, local transportation includes buses, taxis, rental cars, and bicycles.

Airports Near Anticosti

The nearest airport to Anticosti Island is in Baie-Comeau, Quebec, about 188 km away.

Bus Stations Near Anticosti

There are no regular bus connections to Anticosti, and the best way to get here is by taking a ferry ride from Havre-Saint-Pierre.

Other Options

  • The Ferry ride from Havre-Saint-Pierre
  • Helicopter from Quebec City or Montreal

How to Get Around in Anticosti

Anticosti is small enough to be explored via cycling or walking. Bicycles and cars can be rented from Port-Menier.

Anticosti Island is an escape for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and culture lovers.
