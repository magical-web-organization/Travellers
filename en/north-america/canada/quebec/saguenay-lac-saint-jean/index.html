Introduction to Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is a region in Quebec, Canada that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. The region is rich in traditions, with French-Canadian roots, and is home to plenty of outdoor adventures, hikes, and scenic views.

Located about two hours from Quebec City, Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is an ideal travel destination for those looking to escape into nature, unplug and explore a hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Pulperie de Chicoutimi: a museum near the Saguenay River, housed in a former pulp mill and dedicated to showcasing the region’s history and culture.
  • Site de la Nouvelle-France: a historical village where visitors can experience life as it was in 17th-century New France.
  • Musée du Fjord: a museum located on the banks of the Saguenay Fjord that showcases the area’s natural history.
  • The Petite Maison Blanche: this small white house in the heart of Chicoutimi is a symbol of hope and resilience in the wake of the devastating 1996 flood.

Natural Attractions of Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Route du Fjord: a scenic drive that follows the Saguenay Fjord for 100 km, offering stunning views.
  • Parc National du Saguenay: a beautiful national park with hiking trails, canoeing, kayaking, and whale watching tours.
  • Zoo Sauvage de Saint-Félicien: a unique zoo where visitors can see animals in their natural habitat.
  • Caverne Trou de la Fée: a natural cave in Saint-Fulgence that visitors can explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean has many options for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few popular routes to consider:

  • Fjord Route Bike Trail: an 85 km bike trail that runs along the Saguenay Fjord, taking you through four municipalities and some of the region’s most beautiful scenery.
  • Mont Lac-Vert: a ski resort in winter, but offers a range of outdoor activities during the summer, including a chairlift ride to the top of Mount Lac-Vert.
  • Parc national de la Pointe-Taillon: this park, located on a peninsula on Lac Saint-Jean, has 45 km of bike trails that offer stunning views of the lake and surrounding area.
  • Sentier National: Côte-Nord au Saguenay: a 50 km hiking trail that takes visitors through the heart of the Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean region.

Best Things to Do in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean: Activities and Tours

  • Whale watching: The Saguenay-St. Lawrence Marine Park is home to a diverse range of marine wildlife, including whales, porpoises, and seals. A guided tour out onto the fjord is a fantastic way to see these animals in their natural habitat.
  • Beaux Préserves: Learn how to preserve jams, pickles, and other foods through hands-on classes at this local business.
  • Saguenay craft brewery tour: Beer enthusiasts will enjoy learning about the local beer scene on a guided tour of some of the region’s breweries, such as Microbrasserie du Lac Saint-Jean and Le Coureur des Bois.
  • Parc Aventures Cap-Jaseux: This adventure park offers zip-lining, kayaking, via ferrata, and other outdoor activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Here’s a week-long itinerary we recommend:

Day 1: Start your trip with a scenic drive along the fjord route, taking in stunning views and stopping at some of the small towns along the way.

Day 2: Hike one of the many trails available in Parc national du Saguenay, and then take a whale-watching cruise on the Saguenay River.

Day 3: Visit the Pointe-Taillon National Park and try one of the park’s biking trails or rent a kayak to explore the lake.

Day 4: Explore the region’s history by visiting the Musée du Fjord and the Site de la Nouvelle-France.

Day 5: Take a day trip to experience some of the adventures at Parc Aventures Cap-Jaseaux.

Day 6: Visit the charming village of Saint-Félicien and discover its history at the Musée amérindien de Mashteuiatsh.

Day 7: End your trip by visiting one of the region’s craft breweries or distilleries, such as Microbrasserie du Lac Saint-Jean.

Food and Drink Scene of Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtière: a traditional meat pie that is a staple of Quebecois cuisine.
  • Fricot: a hearty stew made with potatoes, chicken, and other vegetables.
  • Poutine: a combination of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy – a classic Canadian dish.

Local Beverages

  • Blueberry wine: A popular local wine produced with berries grown in the region. It is known for its sweet and fruity flavor.
  • Saguenay beer: Served at many local breweries and restaurants, this beer has a crisp, refreshing flavor and is perfect to enjoy after a day of outdoor activities.

Where to Stay in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is no shortage of accommodations in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean. Some popular options include:

  • Hôtel Chicoutimi: With a central location and great amenities, this hotel is perfect for those looking for a comfortable stay.
  • Auberge des Battures: An eco-friendly hotel offering easy access to the fjord area, this charming option is ideal for those looking for something a little different.
  • Auberge de Nos Aieux: This picturesque B&B offers a cozy and relaxing stay while also providing access to outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some local products and souvenirs to consider:

  • Blueberry jam: As one of the area’s signature foods, blueberry jam is a great gift or keepsake.
  • Traditional crafts: Many local artisans create traditional crafts such as hand-knit mittens and wool sweaters.
  • Local spirits: Microdistilleries in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean offer locally made gin and whiskey that make great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is a region steeped in French-Canadian culture. Visitors will find that the locals are friendly and proud of their heritage. It is common to hear French being spoken, and visitors should make an effort to learn a few phrases to engage with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

The best time to visit Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean depends on what you’re looking for. Summer, from June to August, is the most popular time to visit due to the mild weather, numerous outdoor activities, and festivals. Fall is a great time to visit for the stunning autumn foliage and cooler temperatures, while winter offers the possibility of snowmobiling, skiing, and other winter activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Do:

  • Embrace the outdoor activities: Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean has an abundance of outdoor activities, from kayaking to hiking to skiing.
  • Make an effort to learn some French: The region is proud of its French-Canadian culture and language, so it’s always appreciated when visitors make an effort to speak the language.
  • Respect the nature: Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is home to diverse wildlife, so visitors should respect their environment and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Forget your bug spray: The region is known for its mosquitoes in the summer months, so to avoid getting bitten, pack bug spray and long sleeves/pants.
  • Overpack: The weather can be unpredictable, so it’s better to bring a few layers rather than too much stuff.
  • Be too noisy: Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is a tranquil destination, so visitors should try to keep noise levels low in residential areas to respect the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is a relatively safe destination, and tourists should not encounter many problems. As with any trip, it’s always important to stay aware of your surroundings and keep your personal belongings secure.

Health in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

As in the rest of Canada, healthcare in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is of a high standard and readily available. Medical care can be accessed both at clinics and hospitals.

Getting to Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean can be reached by car or public transportation. Here are some logistical details:

Airports Near Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

The nearest airport is the Bagotville Airport, located in La Baie, approximately 15 km from Chicoutimi. It offers flights to Montreal and Quebec City.

Bus Stations Near Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

Buses run by Orléans Express connect the region with Montreal and Quebec City.

Other Options

Driving your own car or renting one is a great way to get around the region, allowing you to explore the various attractions at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean

While a car is useful for getting around, biking and walking are also good options, especially in the summer months. Public transportation is available, but it can be limited in some areas.

Where to Go Next from Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After visiting Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean, these other destinations in Quebec are worth exploring:

  • Quebec City: Canada’s only walled city, Quebec City is a UNESCO World Heritage site with charming architecture and a vibrant cultural scene.
  • Tadoussac: A picturesque town located on the north bank of the St. Lawrence River, this is an excellent place for whale watching.
  • Charlevoix: A region known for its stunning natural beauty, Charlevoix offers outdoor activities, charming towns, and plenty of relaxation.

Conclusion

Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is an inspiring travel destination that showcases the beauty of Quebec. The region has something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to foodies to culture lovers. Whether you’re interested in hiking in national parks, shopping for local crafts, or eating traditional Québécois cuisine, Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean is a must-visit destination for people looking for an authentic and immersive travel experience.
