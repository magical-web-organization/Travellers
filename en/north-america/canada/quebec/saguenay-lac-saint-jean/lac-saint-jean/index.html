Introduction to Lac-Saint-Jean

Lac-Saint-Jean, also known as Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean, is a scenic region located in the heart of Quebec, Canada. Famous for its vast forests, pristine lakes, and numerous outdoor activities, Lac-Saint-Jean is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and history buffs. The region is also known for its rich cultural heritage, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine. This comprehensive travel guide will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and where to stay in Lac-Saint-Jean. So, buckle up and get ready to explore this magical region!

List of Places to Visit in Lac-Saint-Jean

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Village Historique de Val-Jalbert – a beautifully preserved village that takes you back to the 1920s, complete with period buildings, actors in traditional costumes, and activities like boat rides and a zip line.
  • The Musée du Fjord – a fascinating museum that showcases the geology, wildlife, and human history of the Saguenay Fjord and its surroundings.
  • The Musée Louis-Hémon – a small museum dedicated to Louis Hémon, a French writer who immortalized the region in his novel “Maria Chapdelaine”.

Natural Attractions of Lac-Saint-Jean: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Parc national de la Pointe-Taillon – a stunning park that offers 45 km of sandy beaches, bike paths, hiking trails, and wildlife watching opportunities, including moose, beavers, and over 220 bird species.
  • The Canyon Sainte-Anne – a 74-meter deep canyon that features three suspension bridges, waterfalls, and a via ferrata route for adrenaline junkies.
  • The Zoo sauvage de Saint-Félicien – an immersive zoo that showcases over 100 species of animals from boreal forests, including wolves, bears, lynx, and caribou.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lac-Saint-Jean

  • The Véloroute des Bleuets – a 256 km bike path that circles the Lac-Saint-Jean and takes you through picturesque villages, forests, and farmlands.
  • The Sentier de la Statue – a 2.5 km hike that leads to a giant statue of the Virgin Mary overlooking the lake from a hilltop.
  • The Mont Lac-Vert – a ski resort in the winter that transforms into a mountain bike park in the summer, featuring 25 km of trails for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Lac-Saint-Jean: Activities and Tours

  • Take a cruise on the Lac-Saint-Jean or the Saguenay Fjord, and admire the breathtaking scenery from the water.
  • Go dog sledding in the winter, and experience the thrill of gliding through the snow with a team of huskies.
  • Visit a local farm, and learn about the production of cheese, cider, or blueberries, which are all specialties of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Lac-Saint-Jean

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtière – a savory meat pie that usually contains ground pork, beef, or game meat, and is served with ketchup or cranberry sauce.
  • Poutine – a classic Quebec dish made of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy, which can be customized with various toppings like smoked meat, bacon, or vegetables.
  • Cipâte – a type of meat and potato pie that is layered with a thin pastry crust and is often served as a main course or a snack.

Local Beverages

  • Bière Boréale – a craft beer brewery that specializes in using local and organic ingredients, such as wild berries and boreal herbs, in their beers.
  • Cidrerie du Domaine Lafrance – an award-winning cider producer that offers tastings, tours, and events, as well as a restaurant that serves local foods and desserts.
  • Café de la Brûlerie du Quai – a cozy coffee shop that roasts its own beans and offers a wide selection of drinks and baked goods.

Where to Stay in Lac-Saint-Jean: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge des Battures – a charming country-style hotel that offers rooms and suites with river or garden views, a restaurant, and a spa.
  • Auberge les Berges – a cozy inn that features rooms with private balconies, a restaurant that serves French and Quebecois cuisine, and a terrace with a stunning view of the Saguenay Fjord.
  • Auberge La Diligence – a historic guesthouse that dates back to 1851 and offers rooms with antique furniture and modern amenities, a garden, and a library.

What to Buy in Lac-Saint-Jean: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blueberry products – from jams and pies to wine and beer, blueberries are one of the region’s specialties, and you can find them in many forms and flavors.
  • Local crafts – artisans in Lac-Saint-Jean produce beautiful and unique items, such as pottery, wood carvings, and hand-knit sweaters, that make great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Maple products – Quebec is famous for its maple syrup, and you can find it in many forms, including candies, butter, and creams, as well as maple-flavored donuts and pancakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Lac-Saint-Jean

The locals in Lac-Saint-Jean are proud of their central Quebecois culture, which includes traditional music, dances, and foods. They are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and often have a strong connection to nature and the land. It is customary to say “bonjour” or “hi” when greeting someone, and to wait for the host to say “bon appétit” before starting a meal. Dressing appropriately for the weather and the occasion is also important, as is respecting the environment and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Lac-Saint-Jean

Lac-Saint-Jean is a year-round destination, with each season offering unique activities and experiences. The high season is from June to September, when the weather is warm and the lakes are perfect for swimming and boating. The fall is a beautiful time to visit, as the leaves turn into vibrant colors and the apples and blueberries are ripe for picking. The winter is perfect for outdoor activities like skiing, snowmobiling, and ice fishing, and the region hosts many festivals and carnivals, such as the famous Carnaval de Saint-Félicien.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lac-Saint-Jean

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife by following the Leave No Trace principles and not feeding or approaching wild animals.
  • Try the local cuisine and products, and support small businesses and artisans.
  • Learn some French phrases and greetings to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas, and always dispose of your trash properly.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places or accommodations.
  • Underestimate the weather and the terrain, and always prepare for outdoor activities accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lac-Saint-Jean

Lac-Saint-Jean is generally a safe and peaceful region, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, as with any destination, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night or in secluded areas, and to not leave valuables unattended. Public transportation and roads are well-maintained and accessible, but be cautious while driving or biking on unfamiliar routes, especially during winter or snowy weather.

Health in Lac-Saint-Jean

Lac-Saint-Jean has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies available in the major towns and cities. It is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, as well as to bring any necessary medications and vaccinations before your trip.

Getting to Lac-Saint-Jean: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lac-Saint-Jean is located about 2.5 hours north of Quebec City, and can be accessed by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Lac-Saint-Jean

  • Saguenay-Bagotville Airport (YBG) – located about 30 minutes from Saguenay, this airport serves several Canadian cities and offers car rental and shuttle services.
  • Quebec City Jean Lesage International Airport (YQB) – located about 2.5 hours from Lac-Saint-Jean, this airport serves many domestic and international destinations and offers car rental and bus services.

Bus Stations Near Lac-Saint-Jean

  • Intercar – a bus company that operates daily routes between major cities in Quebec, including Saguenay, Roberval, and Alma.
  • Orléans Express – another bus company that serves several destinations in Quebec and offers connections to Ottawa and Montreal.

Other Options

  • Car rental – many major car rental companies have offices in Quebec City and Saguenay, and offer various car models and rental rates.
  • Train – although there is no direct train service to Lac-Saint-Jean, you can take VIA Rail to the nearby cities of Jonquière or Chambord and then transfer to a regional bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Lac-Saint-Jean

While having a car is recommended for exploring Lac-Saint-Jean, there are alternative modes of transportation available for those who prefer not to drive or bike.

  • Public transportation – several local bus companies operate in Lac-Saint-Jean, offering routes between towns, cities, and attractions.
  • Taxis – taxis are available in the major urban areas and can be booked in advance or on-demand.
  • Bike rentals – many accommodation providers offer bike rentals, and there are also several bike shops and rental services in the region.

Where to Go Next from Lac-Saint-Jean: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lac-Saint-Jean is ideally situated for day trips or extended stays in the nearby regions of Quebec.

  • Saguenay – located about an hour from Lac-Saint-Jean, this city offers a vibrant culture, outdoor activities, and shopping and dining options.
  • Tadoussac – located about 2 hours from Lac-Saint-Jean, this charming village is famous for its whale watching cruises and historic sites.
  • Quebec City – located about 2.5 hours from Lac-Saint-Jean, this UNESCO World Heritage site is a beautiful and historic city that offers a plethora of cultural, culinary, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Lac-Saint-Jean is a true gem in Quebec’s crown, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and friendly hospitality. Whether you’re seeking adventure, immersion in local culture, or relaxation in cozy accommodations, there is something for everyone in this magical region.
