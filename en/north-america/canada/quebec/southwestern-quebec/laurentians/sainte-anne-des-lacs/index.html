Introduction to Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs is a small town located in the heart of Laurentians, Southwest Quebec, Canada. The town is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, charming small-town vibes, and rich cultural heritage. In this travel guide, we will explore the many attractions and activities that Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs has to offer, from the natural wonders of the region, to cultural landmarks and local customs, to hiking and biking routes, to food and drink scene and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

Get ready to be amazed by what Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs has to offer. Here are some highlights of the must-visit places in the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Musée du Ski des Laurentides: This museum is a tribute to the rich skiing heritage of the Laurentians. It showcases the history of skiing in the region, from humble beginnings to world-class ski resorts.
  • Church of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs: This beautiful church is a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts. The church was built in 1955, following the destruction of the previous church during World War II.
  • La Boutique de Noël et Cie: This unique shop is full of Christmas decorations and gifts all year round. It is a favorite stop for locals and visitors alike, and a perfect place to find a unique souvenir from your trip.

Natural Attractions of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc des Falaises: This park is a natural wonder, full of lush forests, amazing cliff faces, and stunning views of the surrounding area. It is a popular destination for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife watching.
  • Lac Marois: This serene lake is an ideal retreat from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Its clear waters are perfect for swimming, fishing, and relaxing on the beach.
  • La Rivière du Nord: This beautiful river offers a perfect setting for kayaking and canoeing. The calm waters and peaceful scenery make it a great place for a leisurely paddle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

For those looking for outdoor adventure, Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs has a number of hiking and biking trails to explore. These include:

  • Les Sentiers de la Chute du Diable: This trail leads visitors through a stunning forest to the top of a beautiful and powerful waterfall. The hike is challenging but rewarding, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Le Sentier de la Montagne Verte: This trail takes visitors to the top of a nearby mountain, offering panoramic views of the Laurentians. The hike is moderate, featuring both ascent and descent.
  • Le Petit Train du Nord: This trail follows an old railway line, offering a scenic and easy day of biking or hiking. It is a great way to experience the beautiful countryside of the Laurentians.

Best Things to Do in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Here are some favorites:

  • Aerial Adventures: This tour offers a unique perspective on the region, as visitors soar through the air on a zip line or ropes course.
  • Local Brewery Tour: This tour takes visitors behind the scenes of a local brewery, offering insights into the beer-making process and a chance to sample some of the best brews in the region.
  • Horseback Riding: This activity is perfect for those looking to experience the natural beauty of the Laurentians at a leisurely pace. Visitors can take a guided ride through the countryside, taking in the stunning scenery and fresh air.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

For those looking to make the most of their trip to Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs, here is a tentative itinerary for a perfect week in the town:

Day Activity
1 Take a leisurely hike on Les Sentiers de la Chute du Diable and enjoy the stunning views of the waterfall and surrounding forest.
2 Explore the cultural attractions of the town, including Musée du Ski des Laurentides, Church of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs and La Boutique de Noël et Cie.
3 Enjoy a day of relaxation on Lac Marois, swimming, fishing, and lounging on the beach.
4 Take the Local Brewery Tour for a unique insight into local beer-making and a chance to sample some of the best brews in the region.
5 Go horseback riding through the beautiful countryside of the Laurentians, taking in the stunning scenery and fresh air.
6 Spend the day exploring the natural wonders of Parc des Falaises, picnicking, and wildlife watching.
7 Take a morning bike ride on Le Petit Train du Nord, followed by an afternoon kayak trip down La Rivière du Nord.

Food and Drink Scene of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs has a thriving food and drink scene that is perfect for visitors looking to sample the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tourtière: This traditional French-Canadian meat pie is a regional favorite in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs.
  • Maple Syrup: Canada is known for its maple syrup, and visitors to Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs will have ample opportunity to try this sweet treat in its many forms, from syrup drizzled on pancakes to maple-flavored candies.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The town is home to a thriving craft brewery scene, with several local breweries producing award-winning beers.
  • Ice Cider: This local specialty is made by fermenting apple juice in sub-zero temperatures, producing a sweet and crisp cider that is perfect for sipping on a warm summer day.

Where to Stay in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some options:

  • Gîte Le Roupillon: This charming bed and breakfast is perfect for those looking for a cozy and intimate experience in the heart of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs.
  • Chalet de la Montagne: This spacious and modern chalet offers all the amenities of home, perfect for families or groups of friends.
  • Hôtel Le Chantecler: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the Laurentians, as well as world-class amenities such as a spa and gourmet restaurant.

What to Buy in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking for unique souvenirs and local products, Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs has plenty to offer. Here are some options:

  • Maple Syrup Products: From maple candies to syrup, visitors can bring home the taste of the region.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans create beautiful jewelry inspired by the natural beauty of the Laurentians.
  • Christmas Decorations: La Boutique de Noël et Cie is the perfect place to find unique Christmas ornaments and decorations all year round.

Local Culture and Customs of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs is a town proud of its cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this local culture and customs in many ways. Here are some highlights:

  • Fêtes de la Saint-Jean-Baptiste: This annual festival celebrates the patron saint of Quebec with parades, music, and fireworks.
  • French-Canadian Cuisine: Visitors can sample the rich culinary heritage of the region, from tourtière to maple syrup sweets.
  • Winter Sports: The town has a strong skiing and snowboarding culture, with world-class ski resorts and plenty of winter sports enthusiasts.

Best Time to Visit Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

The best time to visit Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs depends on what visitors are looking for. Summer is the most popular time for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports, while winter is perfect for skiing and snowboarding. Spring and fall offer a more relaxed pace, with beautiful scenery and charming small town vibes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

Here are some do’s and don’ts for those planning a trip to Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs:

Do:

  • Do bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including tourtière and maple syrup.
  • Do visit the natural wonders of the region, including Parc des Falaises and Lac Marois.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget insect repellent during the summer months, as mosquitoes and ticks can be prevalent in the region.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take common sense precautions such as:

  • Lock cars and valuables when parked.
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night.
  • Practice caution during outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking.

Health in Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs

The town has a good healthcare system, with clinics and hospitals located in nearby cities such as Montreal and Saint-Jérôme. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before embarking on their trip.

Where to Go Next from Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors looking to continue their adventure beyond Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Mont-Tremblant: This world-class ski resort is located just a short drive from Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs and offers plenty of winter sports and entertainment.
  • Montreal: The vibrant city of Montreal is just an hour’s drive away, offering endless cultural attractions, restaurants, and nightlife.
  • Quebec City: This historic city is around a three-hour drive from Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs and is home to historic landmarks and a thriving arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs is a hidden gem in the Laurentians, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and small-town charm. With plenty of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and culinary delights to discover, visitors to Sainte-Anne-des-Lacs are sure to be enchanted by this special place.
