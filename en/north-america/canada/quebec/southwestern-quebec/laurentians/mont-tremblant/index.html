Introduction to Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant is a beautiful village located in the Laurentians region of Quebec, Canada. The picturesque town is surrounded by a vast expanse of nature, consisting of forests, mountains, lakes, and rivers. Mont-Tremblant is a year-round destination, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to experience its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Mont-Tremblant

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mont-Tremblant has a rich history and cultural heritage, which can be explored through its museums and historical attractions.

  • Mont-Tremblant Activity Centre: A great place to start your visit to Mont-Tremblant, the activity center offers visitors access to hiking trails, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Domaine Saint-Bernard: This nature reserve is located just five minutes from the village and features over 20 km of trails for hiking and cross-country skiing. It also has a bird sanctuary, an arboretum, and a butterfly garden.
  • Scandinave Spa: The Scandinavian-inspired spa offers luxurious spa treatments, hot and cold baths, saunas, and relaxation areas.
  • Le P’tit Train du Nord: This 232 km long trail was once a railway track and now serves as a biking and hiking trail connecting Mont-Tremblant to other towns in the Laurentian region.
  • Mont-Tremblant National Park: The park is home to over 400 lakes and rivers and offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, canoeing, biking, and camping.
  • Centre d’Exposition de Mont-Tremblant: This gallery showcases the work of local artists and hosts exhibitions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Mont-Tremblant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mont-Tremblant’s natural beauty can be experienced by exploring the great outdoors.

  • Tremblant Resort: Known for its skiing and snowboarding in the winter, Tremblant Resort offers a variety of outdoor activities year-round, including ziplining, bungee jumping, rock climbing, and more.
  • Mont-Tremblant Pedestrian Village: The pedestrian village is the heart of the town and offers a variety of shops, restaurants, and entertainment options.
  • La Diable River: This river is ideal for canoeing or kayaking, and it has several spots for picnicking and camping along its banks.
  • Tremblant Beach and Tennis Club: The Beach and Tennis Club is a great spot to relax and soak up some sun. It also offers tennis and beach volleyball courts, pedal boats, kayaks, and canoe rentals.
  • Mont-Tremblant Casino: The casino is a great place to try your luck and has a variety of slot machines, table games, and poker. It also features a bar and a restaurant.
  • Golf Courses: Mont-Tremblant is home to several world-class golf courses, including the Le Diable Golf Course and Le Geant Golf Course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant has some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Quebec.

  • Le P’tit Train du Nord: This 232 km long trail was once a railway track and now serves as a biking and hiking trail connecting Mont-Tremblant to other towns in the Laurentians region.
  • Tremblant Resort: The resort has over 50 km of hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and lakes.
  • Mont-Tremblant National Park: The park has over 82 km of hiking trails that wind through forests, rivers, and lakes.
  • La Belle-Cordière: This hiking trail is a 10 km loop that offers stunning views of Tremblant Resort and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Mont-Tremblant: Activities and Tours

Mont-Tremblant has an abundance of activities and tours to keep visitors busy year-round.

  • Parcours Aventure: This adventure park offers zip-lining, tree-top adventure courses, and other outdoor activities.
  • Canopy Zipline: This zipline tour zips through the gorgeous forests of the Laurentian Mountains.
  • Helicopter Tours: Take a helicopter tour of Mont-Tremblant and enjoy breathtaking views from above.
  • Dogsledding Tours: Experience the thrill of dogsledding through the snowy trails of Mont-Tremblant.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the beauty of the Laurentians on horseback.
  • Snowmobiling Adventures: Snowmobile through the winter wonderland of Mont-Tremblant.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant has so much to offer that it can be difficult to choose what to do in just a couple of days. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to make the most out of your visit:

  • Day 1: Explore the pedestrian village. Stroll through the shops, grab some lunch, and take in the sights and sounds.
  • Day 2: Bike or hike along the Le P’tit Train du Nord trail and explore the surrounding countryside.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Mont-Tremblant National Park and hike one of the many trails. Have a picnic lunch and spend the afternoon canoeing or kayaking on one of the park’s many lakes.
  • Day 4: Go dogsledding or snowmobiling, depending on the season, then relax in the afternoon with a spa treatment at Scandinave Spa.
  • Day 5: Take the gondola up to the top of Mont-Tremblant and enjoy the spectacular views of the surrounding area.
  • Day 6: Tee off at one of the many world-class golf courses in Mont-Tremblant.
  • Day 7: Spend the last day taking in any sights you’ve missed and enjoying the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant is known for its delicious local cuisine, which includes a range of traditional Quebec dishes with a modern twist.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This Quebec classic is a dish made up of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Tourtière: A traditional meat pie filled with beef, pork, and vegetables.
  • Maple Syrup: Quebec produces a significant amount of the world’s maple syrup, making it a must-try in Mont-Tremblant.

Local Beverages

  • Ice Cider: A sweet and crisp cider made from frozen apples.
  • Quebec Microbrews: The province has a thriving microbrew scene, so be sure to sample some of the local beers while you’re in town.

Where to Stay in Mont-Tremblant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mont-Tremblant offers plenty of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

  • Fairmont Tremblant: A luxurious hotel located on the slopes of Tremblant Resort with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Le Westin Resort & Spa Tremblant: This upscale resort has a spa and is located just a few minutes from the pedestrian village.
  • Le Lodge de la Montagne: A cozy ski-in/ski-out lodge located in Tremblant Resort’s upper village.
  • Auberge La Porte Rouge: A quaint guesthouse located just outside of the pedestrian village.

What to Buy in Mont-Tremblant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mont-Tremblant has several souvenir shops that sell a variety of locally-made products, including:

  • Maple Syrup: Quebec produces a significant amount of the world’s maple syrup, making it a must-buy in Mont-Tremblant.
  • Handmade Soaps: Made with natural ingredients, these artisanal soaps are perfect for a spa day at home.
  • Local Artwork: Local artists create unique and beautiful pieces inspired by Mont-Tremblant’s natural beauty.
  • Gourmet Food Products: Local food co-ops and markets offer a range of delicious gourmet foods, such as homemade jams and jellies.

Local Culture and Customs of Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant has a charming and laid-back culture that is heavily influenced by Quebecois and First Nations traditions. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and the town is known for its vibrant music and arts scene.

Best Time to Visit Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant is a year-round destination, and the best time to visit depends on what you want to do while you’re in town.

  • Winter (December to March): The winter months are perfect for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities.
  • Spring (April to May): Spring is ideal for hiking and biking as the snow melts and the weather starts to warm up.
  • Summer (June to August): Summer is perfect for outdoor activities such as camping, canoeing, and golfing.
  • Fall (September to November): The fall colors in Mont-Tremblant are breathtaking, making it a popular time for hiking and leaf-peeping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mont-Tremblant

  • Spend time outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty.
  • Be respectful of the local culture.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant is a very safe destination, but visitors should take precautions when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking, kayaking, or snowboarding.

  • Wear appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.
  • Follow the safety guidelines provided by activity providers.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when outdoors.

Health in Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant has excellent medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics.

  • Ensure you have medical insurance before traveling to Mont-Tremblant.
  • Don’t forget to pack any necessary prescription medications.
  • Be aware of any food allergies and ensure you have any required medications on hand.

Getting to Mont-Tremblant: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mont-Tremblant is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Mont-Tremblant

  • Montreal-Trudeau International Airport: Approximately 140 km from Mont-Tremblant.
  • Mont-Tremblant International Airport: Approximately 40 km from Mont-Tremblant.

Bus Stations Near Mont-Tremblant

  • Gare d’autocars de Mont-Tremblant: Located in the pedestrian village, this is the main bus station in Mont-Tremblant.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental is available at both Montreal-Trudeau International Airport and Mont-Tremblant International Airport.
  • Taxi Services: Several taxi services are available in Mont-Tremblant for local transportation.
  • Uber: Uber operates in Mont-Tremblant.

How to Get Around in Mont-Tremblant

Mont-Tremblant is a pedestrian-friendly town, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. The town also offers public transportation options, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Mont-Tremblant: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mont-Tremblant is surrounded by several charming small towns and natural attractions worth visiting, including:

  • St-Jovite: A nearby town that offers a wide range of shops and restaurants.
  • Laurentian Mountains: Explore the beauty of the Laurentian Mountains by visiting nearby parks and lakes.
  • Rouge-Matawin Wildlife Reserve: A nature reserve located just an hour’s drive from Mont-Tremblant that is perfect for hiking and canoeing.

Conclusion

Mont-Tremblant is a fantastic destination for visitors who love outdoor adventures, stunning natural scenery, and a vibrant local culture. With so much to see and do, it’s easy to see why it’s a year-round favorite with visitors from all over the world. Plan your trip to Mont-Tremblant today and experience all that this beautiful town has to offer.
