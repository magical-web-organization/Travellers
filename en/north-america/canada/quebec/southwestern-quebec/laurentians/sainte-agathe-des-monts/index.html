Introduction to Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

Located in the beautiful Laurentians, in Southwestern Quebec, Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts is a picturesque town that’s perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Throughout the year, it attracts visitors with its natural beauty and activities, such as hiking, skiing, fishing, and cycling. It’s widely considered one of the most beautiful tourist destinations in Canada, in terms of its many natural attractions and local customs and culture. This guide will help you plan your trip to Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts by highlighting the best places to visit, things to see and do, and where to stay and eat.

List of Places to Visit in Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The House of Everlasting, a former convent with a rich history that has been transformed into an art center and museum.
  • Musée du Ski des Laurentides, a skiing museum with cultural and historical artifacts from the Laurentians, including ski equipment and clothing.
  • Le Patriote, a local theatre with a rich cultural history that offers live performances and shows from local artists and groups.

Natural Attractions of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Parc des Campeurs, located in the heart of the Laurentian Mountains, is a stunning park with a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, fishing, and swimming.
  • Montagne d’Argent, a gorgeous mountain with stunning views and a range of hikes and bike trails for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Lac des Sables, a picturesque lake that’s perfect for swimming, boating, and other water-related activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

  • Laurentian Peak Trail, a scenic hike that offers stunning views of the mountains, forests, and lakes of the region.
  • The Trans-Canada Trail, a bike trail that passes through Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts, offers a scenic route through the Laurentians and is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts looking for adventure.
  • Sentier des Trotteurs, Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts’ scenic hiking trail, is perfect for those looking for something less strenuous.

Best Things to Do in Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts: Activities and Tours

  • Go skiing at the Mont Avalanche Ski Station, which is located just 15 minutes away from the town center.
  • Go fishing at Trout Lake, one of the most popular fishing spots in the region.
  • Book a horseback riding tour, which is perfect for those looking to take in the beautiful landscapes of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

  • Day 1: Visit Lac des Sables and hike around the lake.
  • Day 2: Spend the day skiing or snowboarding at Mont Avalanche Ski Station.
  • Day 3: Take a bike tour of the town and explore its beautiful surroundings.
  • Day 4: Visit the House of Everlasting and immerse yourself in the culture and history of the region.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the region and learn more about the local customs and traditions of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts.
  • Day 6: Take a horseback ride tour through the stunning landscapes of the Laurentian Mountains.
  • Day 7: Enjoy the day at Parc des Campeurs and explore its many outdoor activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine, a classic Quebecois dish made with French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Tourtière, a savory meat pie that’s popular in Quebec.
  • Sugar pie, a sweet and delicious dessert that’s made with maple syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Unibroue beer, a popular local craft beer that’s made in nearby Chambly.
  • Ice cider, a sweet and refreshing apple cider that’s made with apples that are left to freeze on the tree.
  • Local wine, which is grown locally and is known for its unique and distinctive flavors.

Where to Stay in Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge Villa Bellerive, a popular guesthouse with stunning views of the mountains and lake.
  • The Hotel Le Fleur de Lys, located in the heart of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts, offers a range of rooms and suites for all budgets.
  • The Domaine de la Rivière is a popular hostel that’s perfect for budget travelers and outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup and maple candy, which are some of Quebec’s most famous exports.
  • Handcrafted soap, which is made with locally sourced ingredients and is perfect for those looking for natural products.
  • Local artwork and crafts, which are perfect for those looking for a unique and memorable souvenir of their visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts is home to a rich and diverse culture that’s deeply rooted in its history and traditions. The town’s residents are friendly, welcoming, and always willing to share their customs with visitors. Some of the most important customs and traditions in Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts include:

  • La tire sur la neige, a popular wintertime tradition that involves pouring hot maple syrup onto the snow to make candy.
  • The Fête Nationale du Québec, a celebration of Quebecois culture and heritage that takes place annually in June.

Best Time to Visit Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

The best time to visit Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts depends on your preferences and interests. Those who love winter sports will enjoy visiting in the winter, when the town’s ski resorts are open and the local landscape is transformed by snow and ice. Those who prefer outdoor activities in warmer weather should visit in the summer and fall months, when the weather is milder and the town’s natural beauty is at its peak.

Getting to Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts is located approximately 100 km north of Montreal. There are several transportation options for getting to the town:

  • Mont-Tremblant International Airport (YTM), which is approximately 38 km north of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts.

Bus Stations Near Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

  • Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts Bus Station, which is located in the heart of the town and has connections to nearby towns and cities.
  • Montreal Bus Station, which is approximately 100 km south of Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts and offers connections to major cities throughout Canada.
