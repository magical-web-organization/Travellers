Introduction to Laval

Located in the beautiful region of Laurentians, Laval is a charming and captivating city known for its rich history, stunning outdoors, and vibrant cultural scene. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Laval has something to offer for everyone.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Laval, including the must-see attractions, best activities, top accommodations, local food, customs, and safety tips. So, let’s explore this hidden gem and make the most of your visit to Laval!

List of Places to Visit in Laval

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Maison Andre-Benjamin-Papineau

Built in the 18th century, the Maison Andre-Benjamin-Papineau is a national historic site that offers a glimpse into the life and times of the Papineau family, one of the most influential families in Canada’s history.

Musee Armand-Frappier

A world-renowned research centre and museum, Musee Armand-Frappier is dedicated to promoting public health and science education. Here, you can learn about the latest scientific discoveries, explore the interactive exhibits, and take an immersive journey into the molecular level of life.

Natural Attractions of Laval: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parc de la Riviere-des-Mille-Îles

Parc de la Riviere-des-Mille-Îles is a breathtaking natural park that spans across 44 islands and more than 42 kilometers of shoreline. Here, you can kayak, canoe, fish, hike, and bike along the river, and enjoy the serene and peaceful environment of Laval’s outdoors.

Mount Royal Park

If you’re looking for stunning views of the city, Mount Royal Park is the place to go. From the top of the mountain, you can enjoy panoramic views of Montreal, Laval, and the surrounding area. You can also hike, bike, or take a guided tour of the park and learn about its history and ecological significance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laval

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Laval has plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Sentier du Bocage: An 8.7 km trail that takes you through the lush green fields and forests of the Bois de l’Equerre park.
  • Parc de la Montagne-des-Roches: A 12 km trail that offers breathtaking views of the city and takes you through the rugged and beautiful landscape of Laval’s mountain.
  • Parc des Prairies: A 6 km trail that takes you along the scenic banks of the Prairies River and offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Best Things to Do in Laval: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling alone or with your family, Laval has no shortage of fun and engaging activities to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Laval:

  • Aventures Plein Air: This adventure park offers a range of exhilarating outdoor activities, including zip-lining, tree-top courses, and obstacle courses.
  • Cosmodome: This unique facility is dedicated to space exploration and offers a range of interactive exhibits, simulations, and displays.
  • SkyVenture: If you’re a thrill-seeker, you’ll love skydiving in a wind tunnel at SkyVenture.
  • Centropolis: This popular shopping and entertainment complex offers a range of activities, including outdoor concerts, food festivals, and movie nights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laval

Day 1: Explore Old Laval and visit the Maison Andre-Benjamin-Papineau. Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Musee Armand-Frappier and learn about the latest scientific discoveries. Day 3: Go kayaking or canoeing in Parc de la Riviere-des-Mille-Îles. Day 4: Hike or bike along the Sentier du Bocage trail. Day 5: Spend a day at Cosmodome and learn about the wonders of space. Day 6: Visit Mount Royal Park and enjoy panoramic views of Montreal and Laval. Day 7: Shop and dine at Centropolis and enjoy the lively and vibrant atmosphere of Laval.

Food and Drink Scene of Laval

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish made with French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Tourtiere: A traditional meat pie made with pork, beef, and spices.
  • Bagels: Montreal-style bagels are famous for their doughy, slightly sweet taste and bagels are perfect at any time of day; breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Local Beverages

  • Labatt Blue: A popular Canadian beer that pairs well with barbecue and sports games.
  • Ice Wine: A refreshing wine made from grapes that have been frozen on the vine and harvested in winter.

Where to Stay in Laval: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Laval offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Laval include:

  • Sheraton Laval Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Laval’s shopping and entertainment district.
  • Le Fabreville Motel & Suites: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms and suites.
  • Chez William Guesthouse: A cozy and welcoming guesthouse that offers a range of amenities and personalized service.

What to Buy in Laval: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup: Quebec is known for producing some of the best maple syrup in the world, and Laval is no exception.
  • Cheese: Laval is home to some of the best cheese producers in the country, so be sure to stock up on some delicious, fresh cheese.
  • Local crafts and artwork: You can find a range of locally-made souvenirs and artwork that reflect Laval’s rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Laval

  • French is the predominant language in Laval, so it’s a good idea to brush up on some basic French phrases.
  • Laval is a multicultural city, so be sure to respect and appreciate the diverse cultures and customs of the locals.
  • Tipping is customary in Laval, so be sure to tip your servers and hotel staff.

Best Time to Visit Laval

The best time to visit Laval is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing. However, if you’re looking for a quieter and more budget-friendly trip, you can also consider visiting during the off-season, which runs from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laval

Do:

  • Explore the beautiful outdoors of Laval.
  • Respect the local customs and cultures.
  • Sample some of the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or make excessive noise in public places.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore the traffic rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laval

Laval is generally a safe and welcoming city, but it’s always a good idea to take some basic safety measures, such as carrying a copy of your passport, keeping your valuables safe, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Laval

Laval has a range of medical facilities and clinics to help you with any health concerns you may have. You can also purchase travel insurance beforehand to give yourself some peace of mind.

Getting to Laval: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Laval

The nearest airport to Laval is Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport (YUL), which is located about 30-45 minutes from Laval by car.

Bus Stations Near Laval

Laval has several bus stations, including the Société de transport de Laval, which offers frequent and reliable bus services to Montreal and other cities in Quebec.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Laval is easily accessible via the Trans-Canada Highway and other major highways. You can also take a train or rental car from downtown Montreal, which is about 20-30 minutes away.

How to Get Around in Laval

Laval has a range of transportation options to help you get around the city, including buses, taxis, and car rentals. You can also explore the city by bike or on foot, which is a great way to discover Laval’s hidden gems.

Where to Go Next from Laval: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Quebec’s beauty and charm, there are several destinations near Laval that are worth a visit:

  • Montreal: Quebec’s largest and most vibrant city, Montreal is known for its rich culture, history, and gastronomy.
  • Mont-Tremblant: A picturesque mountain town that offers a range of outdoor activities, such as skiing, snowboarding, and hiking.
  • Gatineau: Home to Gatineau Park, one of Canada’s most beautiful natural reserves, Gatineau is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Laval may be a hidden gem, but its rich history, stunning outdoors, and vibrant culture make it a truly magical and captivating destination. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip to Laval with ease, and make the most of your visit by exploring all the top attractions, activities, and local cuisine. So what are you waiting for? Pack your bags, and come discover the charm of Laval!
