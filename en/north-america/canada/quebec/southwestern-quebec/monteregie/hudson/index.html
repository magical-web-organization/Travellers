Introduction to Hudson

Hudson is a charming small town located in the heart of Montérégie, Quebec. It is known for its historic heritage, captivating natural beauty, and vibrant cultural scene. This town is a perfect getaway for those looking for a serene environment that combines old-world charm with modern amenities. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best attractions, hiking routes, activities, food, accommodation, and more to ensure that your visit to Hudson is nothing short of a remarkable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Hudson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Greenwood Centre for Living History: A museum dedicated to preserving the history of the region’s early settlers.
  • Hudson Village Theatre: A historic landmark that features a range of musical and theatrical performances throughout the year.
  • Hudson Historical Society: A non-profit organization aiming to conserve and share the history of Hudson and its surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Hudson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandy Beach: A peaceful spot on the shore of Lake of Two Mountains. It is an ideal place for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.
  • Hudson Bay Wetlands: A fantastic destination for nature lovers featuring hiking trails, bird watching, and wildlife spotting.
  • Hudson Farmers’ Market: An outdoor market held every Saturday throughout the summer and early fall, where you can find locally grown produce, artisanal products, and handcrafted souvenirs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudson

  • Jack Layton Park Trail: A scenic hike along the waterfront that offers breathtaking views across the water.
  • Sandy Beach Trail: A moderate hike that meanders through the forest and ends up on the lakeshore
  • Lakeview Trail: A difficult hike that rewards with picturesque views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Hudson: Activities and Tours

  • Hudson Village Theatre: A cultural landmark and performance venue that offers a range of live entertainment options.
  • Boat Tours: Take in the scenery along the lake edge.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore Hudson on horseback with an experienced guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hudson

Day Activities
Day 1 Get around on foot and explore the historic village center, visit the Greenwood Centre for Living History and check a concert at the Hudson Village Theatre.
Day 2 Hike the Lakeview Trail in the morning, enjoy lunch at the Hudson Farmer’s Market, and spend the afternoon relaxing on the shore of Sandy Beach.
Day 3 Go on a shopping spree and explore the local galleries.
Day 4 Try some outdoor activities such as kayaking, fishing, or horseback riding.
Day 5 Hit the golf greens, head north to Leduc or Hautes Plaines Golf courses.
Day 6 Visit local vineyards & wineries where you can sample some of Hudson’s exceptional wines.
Day 7 Take a leisurely stroll along the waterfront to watch the sunset and enjoy dinner at one of Hudson’s exceptional restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Hudson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Quebecois delicacy made up of french fries and gravy topped with cheese.
  • Sugar Pie: A traditional dessert made from a filling of brown sugar, flour, and cream in a shortcrust pastry

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made locally with local apples.
  • Ice Cider: Made by cold-pressing and then fermenting the juice of frozen apples.

Where to Stay in Hudson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Auberge Willow Inn: A luxurious, elegant hotel with beautiful gardens and stunning river views.
  • Wyndham Garden Pierrefonds: Located close to the Waterfront, this hotel offers comfortable, affordable accommodation for visitors.
  • Le Petit Chateau: A charming bed and breakfast with spacious rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Hudson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hudson Made: Collection of handcrafted perfume and body care products that embrace Hudson’s unique scents
  • Hudson Village Shops: Unique clothing stores and artisan markets with one-of-a-kind souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudson

  • Hudson is an English-speaking community with strong ties to French-Canadian Culture.

Best Time to Visit Hudson

The best time to visit Hudson is during the warm summer months from May to September when you can take advantage of all the outdoor activities and enjoy the beautiful weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudson

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of all the natural and cultural attractions that Hudson has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the trails and in public places.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hudson

Hudson is a safe place to visit. However, it is always important to take standard precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Hudson

Make sure to bring along any necessary medications for your trip. The nearest medical center is the Hudson Medical & Sports Therapy Center where you can receive medical care if necessary.

Getting to Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hudson

The nearest airport to Hudson is Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport (YUL), located about 40 minutes away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi, rent a car or take the shuttle service to Hudson.

Bus Stations Near Hudson

The nearest bus stop is located in Vaudreuil-Dorion and departures to Hudson are frequent. From the bus stop in Hudson, you will have access to all of Hudson’s attractions, shopping, dining, and accommodation choices.

Other Options

You can drive to Hudson or hire a car if you prefer.

How to Get Around in Hudson

Hudson is a small town, which is easy to navigate on foot, bike, or rental car. There are also taxis and a public bus system that will take you to most of the attractions in and around the town.

Where to Go Next from Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Montreal: Montreal is one hour away from Hudson and is an excellent destination for those looking for a bigger city experience.
  • Oka National Park: It is situated to the north of Hudson offers plenty of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Hudson is a beautiful place to visit, with a lot of attractions, a rich cultural heritage, and outdoor activities to keep you engaged. This travel guide will help you make the most out of your trip by highlighting all of the best things to see and do while you’re in town. Enjoy your trip to Hudson!
