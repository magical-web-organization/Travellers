Introduction to Roussillon (Region)

Roussillon is a beautiful region in Montérégie, Southwestern Quebec, Canada. Known for its stunning natural scenery, rich culture and history, and warm hospitality, Roussillon is a popular destination for tourists seeking a relaxing and rejuvenating break from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Whether you are a solo traveler, a couple, or traveling with family and friends, Roussillon has something for everyone. From hiking and biking routes to museums and historical sites, from local cuisine to cultural events, Roussillon offers a wide range of experiences that are sure to delight and awe visitors.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best things to do, places to visit, and tips for making the most of your trip to Roussillon (Region).

List of Places to Visit in Roussillon (Region)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roussillon boasts of a rich history, and there are many museums and historical sites that you can visit to learn more about the region’s past. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Roussillon include:

  • The Château De Ramezay: A historic château that now functions as a museum dedicated to the local history of Montreal and Quebec.
  • The Musée de la défense aérienne: Explore the history of defense aviation in Canada at this museum.
  • The Canadian Railway Museum: Learn about the history of railways in Canada through exhibits and interactive displays.

Natural Attractions of Roussillon (Region): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Roussillon is unparalleled. Here are some of the natural attractions that you must visit when in the region:

  • Mont Saint-Bruno Provincial Park: A beautiful park featuring five lakes, an orchard, and numerous hiking, skiing, and snowshoeing trails.
  • Les Coteaux Missisquoi Vineyard: Tasting tours amidst breathtaking views of the vineyards.
  • The Saint Lawrence River: Take a boat tour on this iconic river and witness the stunning landscapes that the region has to offer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roussillon (Region)

Roussillon is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in Canada. The region is known for its beautiful and diverse landscapes, from mountain ranges to picturesque lakes, making it the perfect destination for adventure seekers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes around:

  • Sentiers de L’Escapade: A 6km cycling and hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Saint Lawrence River.
  • Saint-Jacques-le-Majeur-de-Wolfestown: A 12km hiking trail with rocky terrains and magnificent views of the local flora and fauna.
  • The Mont Saint-Bruno Provincial Park hike: This park offers numerous hiking trails of different levels with overhanging trees, thick forested pathways, and incredible views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Roussillon (Region): Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do when you are in Roussillon:

  • Take a river cruise: What better way to relax and enjoy the beauty of Roussillon than by taking a leisurely cruise on the Saint Lawrence river?
  • Visit the vineyards: The region is famous for its vineyards and wineries - take a tour and indulge in some delicious wine tasting.
  • Try ice fishing: Experience a unique Canadian tradition by going ice fishing in the many lakes and rivers that dot the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Roussillon (Region)

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you explore Roussillon:

Day 1: Visit the Château De Ramezay and the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Mont Saint-Bruno and soak in the stunning views of the landscape.

Day 3: Go for a food and drink tasting tour at Les Coteaux Missisquoi Vineyard.

Day 4: Take a boat cruise on the Saint Lawrence River.

Day 5: Explore the Canadian Railway Museum.

Day 6: Rent a bike, and head out for the Sentiers de L’Escapade cycling trail.

Day 7: Try ice fishing on one of Roussillon’s many lakes and rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of Roussillon (Region)

Roussillon is known for its diverse and delicious cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: Quebec’s most famous dish, consisting of french fries with cheese curds and gravy.
  • Tourtière: A savory meat pie that originated in Quebec and is a traditional holiday dish in the region.
  • Pouding Chômeur: A classic Quebec dessert made of maple syrup and cake.

Local Beverages

  • Ice Cider: A sweet, syrupy wine made from apples.
  • Labatt Blue: Canada’s best-known beer, brewed in Quebec.

Where to Stay in Roussillon (Region): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Roussillon offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Auberge Harris: A comfortable and affordable option located in the heart of the region.
  • Le Saint-Martin Château Bromont: A luxurious hotel with an excellent spa and restaurant.
  • Bonneville Motel: An affordable and charming option for travelers looking for a more traditional experience.

What to Buy in Roussillon (Region): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Roussillon:

  • Maple Syrup: Quebec is famous for its maple syrup, and you will find it in many stores and souvenir shops in Roussillon.
  • Quebecois Artwork: From paintings to sculpture, Roussillon has a vibrant arts scene that you can explore.
  • Ice Wine: A quintessential Canadian product that is sure to impress your friends back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Roussillon (Region)

Roussillon has a unique culture that is shaped by its history, geography, and people. Here are a few things that you should keep in mind while exploring the region:

  • Social etiquette is essential in Quebec, so it is essential to know some French phrases to show respect and politeness.
  • The region has a diverse population, including many English, Spanish, and Portuguese speakers.
  • Quebec cuisine has a strong French influence, but you will also find many dishes that originated in other parts of the world.

Best Time to Visit Roussillon (Region)

The best time to visit Roussillon is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roussillon (Region)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some French phrases to show respect and politeness.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the locals.
  • Assume everyone speaks English.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roussillon (Region)

Roussillon is generally a safe place for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it is always essential to keep your belongings safe and practice common sense when in public places.

Health in Roussillon (Region)

Roussillon has excellent health facilities, and visitors can easily access medical care if needed. It is always essential to have travel insurance before visiting the region.

Getting to Roussillon (Region): Transportation Options and Logistics

Roussillon is easily accessible via different transportation modes:

Airports Near Roussillon (Region)

The closest airport to Roussillon is the Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, which is located around 20 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Roussillon (Region)

The nearest bus station is the Montreal Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also access the region via train or car.

How to Get Around in Roussillon (Region)

Renting a car or a bike is the best way to get around Roussillon, as it gives you the flexibility to explore the region at your own pace. Alternatively, there are numerous public transportation options, including buses, trains, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Roussillon (Region): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, there are many exciting destinations to explore near Roussillon, including:

  • Ottawa: The capital city of Canada, located just a few hours away from Roussillon.
  • Quebec City: The charming provincial capital, full of history, culture, and delicious food.

Conclusion

Roussillon is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking natural beauty, rich history, and a vibrant culture. With its stunning landscapes, delicious food, and warm hospitality, Roussillon offers an unforgettable experience that you will cherish for years to come.
