Introduction to Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours, a charming town in Montérégie, Southwestern Quebec, offers the perfect escape for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts. Located in the heart of the Richelieu Valley, Saint-Ours is known for its picturesque landscapes, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours has a plethora of indoor and outdoor attractions to visit. The town has something to offer to everyone, from history buffs and art lovers to adventure enthusiasts and foodies.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saint-Ours has a rich history that is reflected in its museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Saint-Ours are:

  • Chambly Canal: A part of the Parks Canada network, the Chambly Canal is a 20-kilometer waterway that connects the Saint Lawrence River with Lake Champlain. It is a National Historic Site and is perfect for cycling, hiking, picnicking, and boat tours.
  • Fort Chambly: A historical fort that dates back to the 17th century, Fort Chambly is a National Historic Site that offers tours, exhibits, and reenactments.
  • Maison LePailleur: This historic house and museum is a must-visit for art lovers. The museum features works of local artists and has exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Centre d’Exposition Louise-Carrier: This exhibition center showcases the works of local artists and hosts cultural events and concerts.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Ours: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Saint-Ours is a paradise for nature lovers. The town is surrounded by the Saint Lawrence River and Lake Champlain, offering stunning views and plenty of outdoor activities. Some of the natural attractions in Saint-Ours are:

  • Mont Saint-Bruno: This beautiful regional park is a 30-minute drive from Saint-Ours, offering hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and cross-country skiing in the winter.
  • Îles-de-Saint-Timothée Regional Park: A nature paradise, this park offers kayaking, canoeing, fishing, hiking, and camping.
  • Mont Yamaska: This mountain offers panoramic views of the Richelieu Valley and has hiking trails for all levels of fitness.
  • Richelieu River: The Richelieu River offers plenty of opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours has plenty of hiking and biking trails that cater to all fitness levels. Some of the most popular trails are:

  • Le Sentier des Douaniers: This 30-kilometer trail starts at Fort Chambly and ends at Mont-Saint-Grégoire, offering stunning views of the Richelieu Valley.
  • Le Parc Des Champs-De-Bataille: This park has hiking and biking trails, as well as picnic areas and playgrounds.
  • Le P’tit Train Du Nord: The trail connects many towns and cities in Quebec, including Saint-Ours, and offers a scenic route for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Ours: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and visiting cultural attractions, Saint-Ours has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Some of the best things to do in Saint-Ours are:

  • Wine Tours: The town is surrounded by vineyards, offering plenty of wine-tasting tours and experiences.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the beautiful countryside by horseback riding.
  • Golf: The Saint-Ours Golf Club offers a challenging course and beautiful views of the town.
  • Fishing: The Richelieu River is renowned for walleye and bass fishing.
  • Winter Sports: During the winter, Saint-Ours offers cross-country skiing, snowmobiling, and snowshoeing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Ours

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Saint-Ours:

Day 1: Visit Fort Chambly and explore the Chambly Canal.

Day 2: Take a wine-tasting tour of the surrounding vineyards and visit Maison LePailleur.

Day 3: Hike Mont Saint-Bruno and have a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Go horseback riding and golfing at the Saint-Ours Golf Club.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Montreal, which is only an hour’s drive from Saint-Ours.

Day 6: Go fishing on the Richelieu River and have a picnic lunch by the river.

Day 7: Explore Îles-de-Saint-Timothée Regional Park and go kayaking.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours offers a unique culinary experience, with its fusion of French and Canadian cuisine. The town is surrounded by vineyards, orchards, and farms, offering fresh and delicious produce.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Saint-Ours are:

  • Poutine: A Quebec specialty, poutine is a dish of French fries topped with cheese curds and gravy.
  • Tourtière: A savory meat pie, tourtière is a Quebecois classic.
  • Sugar Pie: A sweet and delicious dessert, sugar pie is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

Saint-Ours is renowned for its vineyards, producing some of the best wines in Quebec. Some of the other local beverages to try are:

  • Ice Cider: Made from frozen apple juice, ice cider is a popular local drink.
  • Unibroue: A brewery in Chambly that produces some of the best craft beer in Quebec.

Where to Stay in Saint-Ours: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Ours has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from upscale hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Saint-Ours are:

  • Auberge Handfield: A five-star hotel that offers luxurious rooms, a spa, and a gourmet restaurant.
  • Gîte La Maison Sainte-Catherine: This guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Saint-Ours: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saint-Ours has plenty of souvenir shops and local markets that offer unique and handmade products. Some of the best things to buy in Saint-Ours are:

  • Wine: Bring home a bottle of wine from the surrounding vineyards.
  • Maple Products: Quebec is known for its maple syrup, and Saint-Ours has plenty of local producers.
  • Cheese: Quebec is renowned for its cheese, and Saint-Ours has some of the best cheesemakers in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours has a rich and diverse culture, reflecting its history and geography. The town has a mix of French and Canadian influences, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. Some of the local customs and traditions in Saint-Ours are:

  • La tire d’érable: A tradition of making maple taffy on snow during the winter.
  • Fêtes de la Nouvelle-France: An annual festival that celebrates the history and culture of Quebec.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Ours

The best time to visit Saint-Ours is during the summer, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the colorful foliage and the harvest season. Winter offers a unique experience, with winter sports and holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Ours

Do:

  • Explore the town’s history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and kayaking.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, such as poutine, tourtière, and ice cider.

Don’t:

  • Litter in natural areas or historical sites.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours is a safe and peaceful town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Saint-Ours

Saint-Ours has a good healthcare system, with clinics and hospitals in the surrounding towns. Make sure to have travel insurance and carry any necessary medication.

Getting to Saint-Ours: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saint-Ours is easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Saint-Ours

The nearest airport to Saint-Ours is Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, which is approximately 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Ours

The main bus station near Saint-Ours is Chambly Bus Station, which is 10 kilometers away.

Other Options

The nearest train station to Saint-Ours is Mont-Saint-Hilaire Train Station, which is 15 kilometers away.

How to Get Around in Saint-Ours

The best way to get around Saint-Ours is by car or bike. There is limited public transportation in the town, and most of the attractions are spread out.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Ours: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint-Ours is a great base for exploring the surrounding towns and cities in Quebec. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Montreal: A vibrant and multicultural city, Montreal is only an hour’s drive from Saint-Ours.
  • Magog: A beautiful town on the shores of Lake Memphremagog, Magog offers stunning views and outdoor activities.
  • Orford: A popular ski resort and national park, Orford is a great destination for winter sports and nature lovers.

Conclusion

Saint-Ours offers a unique and memorable travel experience, with its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and diverse culture. From hiking in the mountains to wine-tasting and exploring the town’s history, there is something for everyone in Saint-Ours.
