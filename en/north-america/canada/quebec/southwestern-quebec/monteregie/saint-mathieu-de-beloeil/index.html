Introduction to Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil

Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil is a charming municipality located in Montérégie, in Southwestern Quebec, Canada. The town is surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and pristine lakes, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness.

Named after Saint Matthew, the Evangelist, Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil has a population of approximately 6,000 people. The town’s rich history can be traced back to the mid-17th century, when the first French settlers arrived in the area.

With its picturesque landscapes and historical landmarks, Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Le Chenail Cultural Center

Le Chenail Cultural Center is a must-visit destination for those interested in arts and culture. The center features an art gallery, a museum, and a performance hall, showcasing local talent and historical artifacts.

Église Saint-Mathieu

Église Saint-Mathieu is a stunning Roman Catholic church dating back to the early 20th century. The church’s Gothic Revival architecture and beautiful stained-glass windows make it a popular spot for tourists and locals alike.

Moulin Seigneurial de Beloeil

Once a flour mill, Moulin Seigneurial de Beloeil is now a museum showcasing the rich history of the region’s milling industry. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore the mill at their own pace.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mont Saint-Hilaire

Mont Saint-Hilaire is a popular hiking destination, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The mountain features over 25 kilometers of hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult.

Duquette Park

Duquette Park is a beautiful natural park located on the shores of the Richelieu River. The park features hiking and biking trails, as well as picnic areas and a playground.

Lac Hertel

Lac Hertel is a picturesque lake located in the heart of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil. The lake is surrounded by lush forests and is a popular spot for boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil

Mont Saint-Bruno National Park

Mont Saint-Bruno National Park is located just a short drive from Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil and features over 30 kilometers of hiking and biking trails. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Les Sentiers du Mont-Beloeil

Les Sentiers du Mont-Beloeil offers over 14 kilometers of well-maintained hiking trails, showcasing the region’s natural beauty. The trails range from easy to difficult and offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil: Activities and Tours

Richelieu River Cruise

Embark on a scenic boat tour along the Richelieu River, taking in the picturesque scenery and historic landmarks of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil.

Horseback Riding at Centre Équestre des Hautes Terres

Experience the beauty of the region on horseback with a guided tour at Centre Équestre des Hautes Terres. The tour takes visitors through lush forests and rolling hills, offering a unique perspective of the town’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil

Must-Try Dishes

Poutine

A classic Canadian dish, poutine is a must-try when visiting Quebec. Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil offers many local restaurants serving up this delicious dish.

Tourtière

Tourtière is a popular meat pie that originated in Quebec. The filling typically includes ground pork, beef, and veal, as well as warm spices such as cinnamon and cloves.

Local Beverages

Ice Cider

A specialty of Quebec, ice cider is made by freezing apples on the tree, resulting in a concentrated juice that is then fermented.

Craft Beer

Quebec is known for its craft beer scene, and Saint-Mathieu-de-Beloeil is no exception. Be sure to sample the local brews at one of the town’s many breweries.
