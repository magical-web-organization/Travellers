Introduction to Marguerite-D’Youville

Located in the Montérégie region of southwestern Quebec, Marguerite-D’Youville is a small borough in the city of Varennes. The region is named after Marguerite d’Youville, the founder of the Grey Nuns of Montreal, and is known for its historical significance and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Marguerite-D’Youville

Here are some must-visit places in Marguerite-D’Youville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Marguerite-D’Youville Museum

Located in the heart of the old village of Varennes, the Marguerite-D’Youville Museum is dedicated to the life and works of Marguerite d’Youville. The museum features exhibits showcasing the history of the region and the legacy of the Grey Nuns.

2. Église Sainte-Anne-de-Varennes

Built in 1713, Église Sainte-Anne-de-Varennes is the oldest church in the region and a testament to the historical significance of Marguerite-D’Youville.

3. Maison LePailleur

Maison LePailleur is a historic house museum that pays tribute to the rich cultural heritage of the region. Visitors can explore the house and its surrounding gardens, as well as several rotating exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Marguerite-D’Youville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Îles-de-Boucherville National Park

Located just a short drive from Marguerite-D’Youville, Îles-de-Boucherville National Park is a true paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The park features several hiking and biking trails, as well as kayaking and canoeing opportunities. Visitors can also enjoy the park’s diverse flora and fauna, including beavers, herons, and more.

2. Les Petits-Fruits du Rang

Les Petits-Fruits du Rang is a family-owned berry farm that offers visitors a chance to pick their own berries in the summer months. The farm also has a cafe and boutique selling locally made jams, syrups, and other products.

3. Varennes Islands

Located in the Richelieu River, the Varennes Islands are a series of small, uninhabited islands that offer stunning views of the surrounding river and countryside. Visitors can take a boat tour of the islands or rent their own boat for a more intimate experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Marguerite-D’Youville

The region has a rich cultural heritage and is deeply tied to the history of the Grey Nuns of Montreal. Visitors should make sure to learn more about their legacy and contributions to the region.

Best Time to Visit Marguerite-D’Youville

The best time to visit Marguerite-D’Youville is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are plentiful.

Getting to Marguerite-D’Youville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marguerite-D’Youville

The nearest major airport is Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, which is approximately a 30-minute drive from Marguerite-D’Youville.

Bus Stations Near Marguerite-D’Youville

The closest bus station is the Varennes station, which is served by several regional bus routes.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available in the region.

How to Get Around in Marguerite-D’Youville

The easiest way to get around Marguerite-D’Youville is by car, bike, or foot. Several bike rental shops are available near the most popular tourist destinations.

Marguerite-D’Youville is a hidden gem in the heart of southwestern Quebec, offering a unique mixture of natural beauty and cultural history. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking or exploring the rich heritage of the region, Marguerite-D’Youville has something for everyone.
