Introduction to Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel is a charming town located in the Montérégie region of Southwestern Quebec, Canada. It is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and scenic natural beauty. With its close proximity to Montreal, it offers an ideal weekend getaway or day trip for tourists looking to explore a small town in Quebec. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have compiled an extensive list of the top attractions, activities, and places to stay to help plan your trip to Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel is rich in cultural history and has many museums and historical attractions that reflect the town’s past. One such attraction is the Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel Maritime Museum, which traces the town’s history from its early settlement days to its shipbuilding industry in the 1800s. Other cultural attractions include the Ursuline Convent, which has a fascinating history dating back to the 1700s, and the Drouin House Historical Society Museum, which displays artifacts from the 1800s and early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel has no shortage of outdoor activities for nature enthusiasts. The town is situated along the Richelieu River, which offers opportunities for boating and fishing. The Parc Régional des Grèves offers a fantastic view of the river and also has hiking and biking trails. Another natural attraction to visit is the Chambly Canal National Historic Site, which is a historic navigation route that connected New France with the rest of France. The canal is now used for recreational activities such as boating, canoeing, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel has several trails that offer scenic views of the town and the surrounding nature. The Parc Régional des Grèves is an ideal spot for hiking and biking and has trails for all levels, including the easy 4.5-km L’Estuaire and the more challenging 15.6-km Parcours des 5 points. The trails pass by forests, wetlands, and the Richelieu River, making them perfect for nature lovers and photographers.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy. The Richelieu River provides opportunities for boating and fishing, while several tour companies offer guided boat tours and kayak rentals. The town is also home to several festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Festival de la Gourgane, which celebrates the Quebec delicacy of the same name.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel

For those planning a longer trip to Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel Maritime Museum and explore the town’s historical landmarks.
  • Day 2: Go boating or fishing on the Richelieu River and enjoy a picnic lunch at the Parc Régional des Grèves.
  • Day 3: Rent a kayak or take a guided boat tour to explore the Richelieu River from a different perspective.
  • Day 4: Visit the Ursuline Convent and the Drouin House Historical Society Museum to learn about the town’s history.
  • Day 5: Go hiking or biking at the Parc Régional des Grèves and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 6: Attend a festival or event happening in the town, such as the Festival de la Gourgane.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as the Chambly Canal National Historic Site or Montreal.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel

Must-Try Dishes

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel is known for its unique delicacy called “la gourgane,” which is made from beans that grow along the Richelieu River. This dish is typically served as soup, stew, or casserole and is a must-try for foodies visiting the town. Other local specialties include cretons, a type of pork spread popular in Quebec, and maple syrup products.

Local Beverages

Quebec is famous for its microbreweries, and Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel has several local breweries that offer unique craft beers. Some popular breweries to visit include Brasserie Dépareillée and Brasserie La Confrérie. The town is also known for its ice cider, which is a sweet and crisp beverage that is made from apples and served as a dessert wine.

Where to Stay in Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel has several accommodation options for visitors to choose from. Some popular hotels include the Hotel Richelieu and the Motel des Deux Lacs, which both offer comfortable rooms and amenities such as free Wi-Fi and parking. For a more budget-friendly option, visitors can stay at the Auberge de la Rive, which has affordable rates and a convenient location near the river.

What to Buy in Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel can bring home several local products and souvenirs to remember their trip. Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel

Saint-Joseph-de-Sorel’s population is predominantly Francophone, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and language.
