Introduction to Mile-End

Mile-End is a neighborhood in Montreal, Quebec, in Canada, that has its own unique charm and allure. It is known for its vibrant arts and music scenes, diverse cultural offerings, and a plethora of boutiques, cafes, and restaurants. The neighborhood combines the best of the old and the new, with its historic architecture and modern-day establishments. In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, food to try, and things to do in Mile-End, Montreal.

List of Places to Visit in Mile-End

Mile-End has plenty of attractions that cater to all interests and ages. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Mile-End:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maison Saint-Gabriel Museum and Historic Site: This mi-century farmhouse, located on a beautiful piece of land, is a renowned historical site in Montreal. It features exhibits on the daily life of farmers in the 17th century.
  • St. Michael’s Mission: This Catholic mission, established in the 19th century, is a great place to learn about the history of the mission and the involvement of the Jesuits in the fur trade.
  • The Mordecai Richler Library: Dedicated to the life and works of one of Montreal’s most famous authors, this library features an impressive collection of books and memorabilia related to Richler’s life.

Natural Attractions of Mile-End: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Royal Park: With over 200 hectares of land, Mount Royal is the largest park in Montreal. It offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and breathtaking views of Montreal’s skyline and the St. Lawrence River.
  • Outremont Park: This park is famous for its natural beauty and is ideal for picnics, bike riding, or merely relaxing in its green spaces.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mile-End

  • Lachine Canal: This canal offers a scenic bike path and hiking trails that pass through Mile-End, and it’s a great way to experience the beauty of the area from a different perspective.

Best Things to Do in Mile-End: Activities and Tours

  • Montreal Quebec Tours: Explore Mile-End and its surrounding neighborhoods on a bike tour that covers the city’s food, culture, and history.
  • Mile-End Food Tour: Taste the flavors of the neighborhood with a walking food tour that takes you through Mile-End’s best culinary offerings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mile-End

Day 1: Explore the neighborhood’s artistic community by visiting the Plateau-Mont-Royal and Outremont neighborhoods.

Day 2: Visit the various museums and historical sites in Mile-End.

Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Lachine Canal, and end the day at the Atwater Market.

Day 4: Visit one of Montreal’s top attractions, the Montreal Biodome, and the Montreal Botanical Garden.

Day 5: Shop at Mile-End’s unique boutiques, followed by a relaxing afternoon at Westmount’s spa.

Day 6: Take a day trip from Mile-End to explore nearby towns like Laval, Sainte-Catherine, and Brossard.

Day 7: Enjoy a picnic and take in the views at Mount Royal Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Mile-End

Mile-End is known for its diverse culinary offerings, from traditional Montreal smoked meat to modern-day vegan cafes. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bagels from St. Viateur Bagel & Café: Freshly baked Montreal-style bagels that are crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside are a delicacy.
  • Smoked Meat from Schwartz’s: This iconic deli is famous for its smoked meat sandwiches, served on rye bread with mustard.
  • Viande Fumee from Wilensky’s: A hot dog bun stuffed with bologna, salami, and Swiss cheese. It’s a Mile-End institution.

Local Beverages

  • Microbrasserie Dieu du Ciel: This temple of beer offers a range of locally crafted beers that are a must-try.
  • Café Olimpico: This bustling Italian café serves excellent coffee and espresso drinks, perfect for a quick break from exploring the neighborhood.

Where to Stay in Mile-End: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mile-End offers various accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some that we recommend:

  • The Ritz-Carlton Montreal: A luxurious option, located at the heart of Mile-End and offers elegant guest rooms, a spa, and a restaurant.
  • Auberge Bishop Bed & Breakfast: A charming guesthouse located near Mile-End that offers affordable rates and unique artwork-themed rooms.

What to Buy in Mile-End: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mile-End is famous for its vintage stores and independent shops that offer unique and locally made goods. Here are some items to consider:

  • Antiques from the Marché aux Puces St-Michel: This massive flea market offers an eclectic mix of antiques, gadgets, and other unique finds.
  • Clothing from General 54: This clothing store offers a curated selection of locally made items that are both stylish and sustainable.

Local Culture and Customs of Mile-End

Mile-End prides itself on its multiculturalism, with various ethnic backgrounds living in the area. The neighborhood is known for its vibrant arts and music scenes, with many artists and musicians who call Mile-End home. It’s not uncommon to see street art and musicians performing on the sidewalks.

Best Time to Visit Mile-End

The best time to visit Mile-End is during the summer months of June to August, where the weather is warm and welcoming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mile-End

Do:

  • Explore the streets and side alleys: You’ll find many hidden gems in Mile-End.
  • Try out the local cuisine: Mile-End offers an array of culinary delights that are unique to the neighborhood.
  • Go to the festivals: Mile-End is known for its festivals and cultural events that take place throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the neighborhood clean and dispose of waste properly.
  • Be noisy: Remember that Mile-End is a residential area, and residents value their peace and quiet.
  • Be disrespectful: Be mindful of the different cultures that coexist in the area and respect their customs and values.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mile-End

Mile-End is generally considered a safe neighborhood to walk around, but it’s always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Mile-End

There are several hospitals and clinics in the area, including the Jewish General Hospital and the CLSC Metro.

Getting to Mile-End: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mile-End

Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 20 minutes away from Mile-End by car.

Bus Stations Near Mile-End

The nearest bus station is Station Outremont, which is on the blue line of the Montreal Metro.

Other Options

Mile-End is easily accessible by car, taxi, or bike.

How to Get Around in Mile-End

Mile-End is a walkable neighborhood, but for those who prefer not to walk, there are several transportation options, including:

  • The Montreal Metro: Station Laurier and Station Mont-Royal are the two stations that service Mile-End.
  • Biking: Bixi is a bike-sharing system that is available in Mile-End.
  • Taxis: Taxis and Ubers are readily available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Mile-End: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

In addition to Mile-End’s attractions, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can take:

  • The Montreal Biodome and Botanical Garden: two of Montreal’s most famous attractions, located nearby in the Hochelaga-Maisonneuve neighborhood.
  • Old Montreal: This historic neighborhood is located just a few kilometers from Mile-End and is known for its impressive architecture and cobblestone streets.
  • Mount Tremblant: A popular destination for outdoor adventures, Mount Tremblant offers activities like skiing, snowshoeing, and hiking.

Conclusion

Mile-End is a neighborhood that boasts a unique blend of history, culture, and modern-day amenities. With its diverse culinary scene, vibrant arts and music scenes, and stunning natural beauty, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler to Montreal. From the museums and historical sites to the outdoor adventures and scenic views, Mile-End has something to offer everyone.
