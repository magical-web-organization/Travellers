Introduction to Old Montreal

Old Montreal, also known as Vieux-Montreal, is a neighborhood situated in the south of Montreal, Quebec, Canada. With its cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and a charming ambiance, Old Montreal is a visitor’s paradise. The neighborhood features numerous attractions, outdoor spaces, restaurants, and cultural events, making it a hotspot for tourists visiting Montreal.

List of Places to Visit in Old Montreal

Whether you’re a history enthusiast, outdoor adventurer, or foodie, Old Montreal has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in the neighborhood.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Montreal is home to some of the oldest buildings and museums in Canada. These culturally significant attractions include:

  • Notre-Dame Basilica: A stunning example of Gothic Revival architecture, Notre-Dame Basilica is a must-visit attraction in Old Montreal.
  • Montreal Museum of Archaeology and History: Located in the Pointe-à-Callière National Historic Site, the museum chronicles the history of Montreal from the 14th century to the present.
  • Chateau Ramezay: Built-in 1705, Chateau Ramezay is the oldest building in Montreal and serves as a museum of local history.
  • Bonsecours Market: Built in 1847, Bonsecours Market once served as a public market and is now home to various events and exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Old Montreal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Old Montreal is not just about history and culture; it’s also a haven for nature lovers. Here are some of the natural attractions you should explore:

  • Old Port of Montreal: The Old Port of Montreal offers breathtaking views of the St. Lawrence River, and visitors can enjoy bike rides, boat tours, and other outdoor activities.
  • Montreal Botanical Garden: Located about a 15-minute drive from Old Montreal, the Botanical Garden offers a peaceful escape from the city. The garden features over 22,000 plant species in themed gardens.
  • Mount Royal Park: A 30-minute walk or short bus ride from Old Montreal, Mount Royal Park offers panoramic views of the city and outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Montreal

Exploring Old Montreal on foot or by bike is a great way to experience the neighborhood’s unique charm. The city has several hiking and biking routes that take you through the historic streets, picturesque parks, and scenic viewpoints. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Lachine Canal: This 14 km (9 miles) trail takes you along the historic Lachine Canal, where you’ll find plenty of green space, restaurants, and shops.
  • The Underground City: A system of tunnels and passageways in downtown Montreal, the Underground City is an interesting place to explore during the colder months.
  • Saint-Lawrence River: Rent a bike and take a ride along the riverfront, where you’ll enjoy views of the city, historic buildings, and public art installations.

Best Things to Do in Old Montreal: Activities and Tours

Old Montreal offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in the neighborhood:

  • Ghost Walks: Join a guided tour of Old Montreal’s haunted sites and learn about the spooky history of the neighborhood.
  • Food Tours: Indulge in Montreal’s culinary scene with a food tour of Old Montreal, where you’ll get to taste local food and drinks.
  • IMAX Theatre: Catch a movie at the IMAX Theatre in the Old Port of Montreal, where you can watch films in 3D and enjoy an immersive experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old Montreal

Here’s an itinerary to help you make the most out of your week in Old Montreal:

  • Day 1: Visit Notre-Dame Basilica, explore the Old Port, and climb up to the Clock Tower.
  • Day 2: Learn about Montreal’s history at the Montreal Museum of Archaeology and History.
  • Day 3: Go on a hike in Mount Royal Park and enjoy a picnic.
  • Day 4: Take a food tour of Old Montreal and taste some local delicacies.
  • Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the city’s many bike paths.
  • Day 6: Visit the Montreal Botanical Garden and enjoy the beautiful flora.
  • Day 7: Relax and catch a movie at the IMAX Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of Old Montreal

Montreal is known for its diverse and vibrant food scene, and Old Montreal is home to some of the best restaurants and bars. Here’s a guide to the neighborhood’s food and drink scene:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Quebec dish made of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Montreal-style bagels: Smaller, sweeter, and denser than New York’s, Montreal-style bagels are boiled in honey water before baked to perfection.
  • Smoked meat sandwich: A popular meat sandwich made of salt-cured and smoked beef brisket.

Local Beverages

  • Maple whiskey: Made with pure maple syrup, this whiskey is a Canadian staple.
  • Ice cider: A dessert wine made from frozen apples, which creates a unique sweetness.
  • Montreal craft beer: Montreal has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries in the city.

Where to Stay in Old Montreal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old Montreal has a range of accommodation options for all budgets, from luxurious hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in the neighborhood:

  • Hotel William Gray: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Old Montreal.
  • Auberge du Vieux-Port: A charming hotel with stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.
  • HI Montreal Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers.

What to Buy in Old Montreal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Old Montreal boasts many unique souvenir shops and local boutiques where you can find one-of-a-kind items to bring home. Here are some gift ideas:

  • Maple syrup and maple-flavored products
  • Local artwork and crafts
  • Montreal-style bagels

Local Culture and Customs of Old Montreal

Montreal is a multicultural city, and Old Montreal is no exception. Visitors will find a mix of French, English, and Indigenous influences in the area’s art, food, and architecture. The neighborhood also values its heritage and traditions, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Old Montreal

The best time to visit Old Montreal is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the neighborhood is also beautiful in the spring and fall, when the streets are lined with changing leaves and colorful flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Montreal

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Old Montreal:

Do:

  • Attend a cultural event, such as the Montreal International Jazz Festival or Just for Laughs.
  • Try some local cuisine, such as poutine or bagels.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of the neighborhood.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the neighborhood clean and dispose of trash properly.
  • Speak too loudly in public spaces.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Montreal

Old Montreal is a safe neighborhood to visit, but like any other city, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables in a secure place.
  • Be cautious when withdrawing money from ATMs.

Health in Old Montreal

Montreal is well-known for its excellent healthcare system, and there are many hospitals and clinics located throughout the city. Visitors who need medical attention can visit the Montreal General Hospital or the McGill University Health Centre.

Getting to Old Montreal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Old Montreal is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including air, train, bus, and car.

Airports Near Old Montreal

  • Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, located about a 30-minute drive from Old Montreal.

Bus Stations Near Old Montreal

  • The Gare d’autocars de Montréal (Montreal Coach Terminal) is located in downtown Montreal and offers many bus routes to and from Old Montreal.

Other Options

  • The VIA Rail Canada train station is located in downtown Montreal and offers train routes throughout Canada.
  • Taxis and rideshare services are also available throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Old Montreal

Old Montreal is a compact neighborhood, and many of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or take public transportation to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Old Montreal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Montreal is located in a beautiful region of Quebec, and there are many places to explore outside of Old Montreal. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Quebec City: A picturesque city located about a three-hour drive from Montreal.
  • Mont-Tremblant: A popular ski resort located about a two-hour drive from Montreal.
  • Ottawa: Canada’s capital city, located about a two-hour drive from Montreal.

Conclusion

Old Montreal is a charming neighborhood that offers something for everyone, from history and culture to outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine. Make sure to incorporate some of these attractions and experiences into your itinerary to make the most out of your trip to the neighborhood.
