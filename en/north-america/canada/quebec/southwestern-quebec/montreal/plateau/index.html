Introduction to Plateau

Plateau, located in Montreal, is a vibrant and eclectic neighborhood known for its hip and trendy atmosphere. It is a melting pot of cultures with a French and English influence, offering a diversity of attractions that cater to all types of travelers. Here, you can find an array of galleries, restaurants, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Plateau is a must-visit destination in Montreal, offering a unique travel experience for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Plateau

Grab your camera and head to Plateau to start exploring the following must-visit attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Plateau boasts a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical attractions that showcase the area’s past. Some of the highly recommended attractions in this category are:

  • Musée des Beaux-Arts de Montréal: This museum is home to an extensive collection of artwork spanning many genres and periods, including works from Canadian artists.

  • The Montreal Holocaust Museum: Housed in a beautifully restored building, this museum contains exhibits and artifacts that tell the stories of Holocaust victims and survivors to keep their stories alive.

  • Château Ramezay: This historic building showcases the history of Montreal with exhibitions and historical artifacts from the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Plateau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Plateau is known for its picturesque streets and parks, providing travelers a unique natural experience. Famous attractions in this category include:

  • Lafontaine Park: This park is the perfect place for a relaxing afternoon, with its expansive green spaces, scenic views, and walking paths that add to its charm.

  • Parc du Mont-Royal: The highest point of Montreal, this park has stunning views of the city. It is the perfect place to hike, bike, or have a picnic.

  • Mount Royal Lookout: Located next to Chalet Mont Royal, this lookout has a 180-degree view of Montreal’s downtown skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plateau

Plateau is an excellent place for outdoor enthusiasts to enjoy hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Whether you want to stretch your legs or enjoy nature’s scenic beauty, some of the best routes include:

  • Montreal Bike Trail: The trail runs parallel to the Lachine Canal and is a perfect spot for cycling. You can go on guided tours or explore on your own.

  • Parc de la Montagne: This park is located near Mont-Royal and is perfect for hiking. You can experience the vibrant foliage and stunning panoramic views of Montreal.

  • Promenade Fleuve-Montagne: A pedestrian and bike path that links the St. Lawrence River to Mount Royal, this route has many lookouts, gardens, and natural discoveries to explore.

Best Things to Do in Plateau: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Plateau, including trying out some of the best activities and tours of the area. Here are some of the activities and tours that should be on your list:

  • Cooking Classes: Several cooking classes around Plateau teach visitors how to make classic Montreal dishes.

  • Montreal Craft Beer Tour: This tour takes you around some of the best craft beer breweries in Montreal, sampling their beer, and learning about the brewing process.

  • Plateau Street Art Tour: A walking tour that focuses on Plateau’s street art scene, providing insight into Montreal’s culture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Plateau

Montreal is known for its food scene, and Plateau has some of the best options around. Here are some of the dishes and beverages that you must try when in Plateau.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: French fries topped with cheese curds and gravy, a Canadian classic.

  • Bagels: Montreal-style bagels are slightly sweeter, denser, and smaller than the New York variety.

  • Smoked Meat: The Montreal Smoked Meat is a delicious Jewish-Canadian beef sandwich that is world-renowned.

Local Beverages

  • Unibroue Beer: Local beer from one of the oldest breweries in Canada

  • Maple Taffy: Canadian candy made by boiling maple syrup and then pouring it on fresh snow or ice to create a syrupy, sweet drink.

  • Ice Cider: Cider fortified with the concentrated fermentation juice of frozen apples.

Where to Stay in Plateau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Plateau has several accommodation options that cater to different travel budgets. Some of the best places to stay when exploring Plateau include:

  • The Plateau Royal: Mid-range hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Pied-A-Terre Mont Royal: A beautiful guesthouse at the center of Plateau.

  • M Montreal Hostel: Budget-friendly hostel located in Plateau.

What to Buy in Plateau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Plateau without taking something with you as a souvenir. Here are some things to buy in Plateau:

  • Maple Syrup: A quintessential Canadian souvenir.

  • Montreal Bagels: Take home some Montreal bagels with a variety of flavors.

  • Artwork: Browse through Plateau’s many galleries and purchase original artwork made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Plateau

Plateau has a vibrant and diverse culture, with a mix of French and English influences. Respect for the French language and speaking culture is vital in Plateau, and most locals are proud of their language and customs. It’s crucial to know about the culture and customs of the area before visiting.

Best Time to Visit Plateau

The best time to visit Plateau is during the summer months from June to August, where the weather is ideal for outdoor exploration. The city is always busy during the summer, with many events and festivals happening in Plateau.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plateau

Here are some things you should do and shouldn’t do when visiting Plateau:

Do:

  • Respect the French language and customs.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

  • Research public transport options before your trip.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their customs.

  • Litter.

  • Speak loudly in public transport or museums.

Safety and Health

Safety is not a prevalent issue in Plateau. However, it’s important to take care of your health and wellbeing by following these tips:

Safety in Plateau

  • Keep your valuables safe and hidden.

  • Avoid alleys and dimly lit areas at night.

  • Keep an eye on your surroundings when walking around at night.

Health in Plateau

  • Bring necessary medication and prescriptions.

  • Stay hydrated and get proper rest.

  • Use sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Plateau: Transportation Options and Logistics

The following are various ways to reach Plateau, depending on your budget and preferences:

Airports Near Plateau

The closest airport to Plateau is Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, which is approximately 16 miles away. You can take the airport bus service or a taxi to reach Plateau

Bus Stations Near Plateau

Plateau is well-connected by bus services, making it easy to access surrounding areas as well. The main bus terminal in Montreal is the Gare d’autocars de Montreal, with connecting services to Plateau.

Other Options

You can also reach Plateau via train. The Montreal Central Train Station serves as the central hub of Canada’s Via Rail network, making it easily accessible from other major cities.

How to Get Around in Plateau

Plateau is a walkable area, and you can easily explore its streets and alleys by foot. You can also opt for public transportation, bike rentals, or private taxis to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from Plateau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Plateau is a central hub that connects you to several nearby attractions, including:

  • Old Montreal: Explore the historic district of Montreal that dates back to the city’s founding in 1642.

  • Quebec City: A charming city with a European feel and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • The Laurentians: A beautiful area of lakes and mountains north of Montreal with stunning scenery and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

As you have seen in this travel guide, Plateau is an incredible destination that offers a unique cultural experience, beautiful views, and delicious food. With this comprehensive guide, you now have all the information needed to make the most of your visit to Plateau, Montreal.
