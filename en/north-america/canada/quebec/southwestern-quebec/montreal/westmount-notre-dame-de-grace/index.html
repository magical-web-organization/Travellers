Introduction to Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce

Located in the heart of Montreal, Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce is an affluent and culturally rich borough. Its geographic location and topography offer numerous opportunities for outdoor activities, scenic beauty, and cultural immersion. The neighborhood comprises several pristine parks, museums, historic sites, and eateries, making it the perfect destination for all kinds of travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the beautiful borough of Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce, providing you with information on places to visit, hiking/biking trails, best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Montreal Museum of Fine Arts: The Montreal Museum of Fine Arts is one of the top-rated museums in Canada and is located in the heart of Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce. It was founded in 1860 and features over 43,000 art pieces, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative objects from around the world.

  • Musée des Hospitalières de l’Hôtel-Dieu de Montréal: The Musée des Hospitalières de l’Hôtel-Dieu de Montréal is a must-visit for history enthusiasts. It displays the history and evolvement of medical practices in Quebec and has numerous exhibits highlighting the life and work of nuns and doctors who served the patients in the Hospital.

Natural Attractions of Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Westmount Summit: It’s a beautiful walking trail and the perfect spot for a morning or evening walk. The lookout at the summit offers beautiful panoramic views of the city, making it one of the top attractions of Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce.

  • Notre-Dame-de-Grâce Park: It is one of the largest parks in Montreal and has a diverse population of plants and flora. The vast park offers numerous picnic areas, walking trails, and a picturesque fountain that looks gorgeous during summers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce

  • Mount-Royal Park: Mount-Royal Park is one of the most beautiful natural reserves in Montreal and is a perfect spot for hiking and biking. It features numerous hiking trails, picnic tables, playgrounds, and sports areas, making it an ideal spot for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Lachine Canal: The Lachine Canal connects Lake Saint-Louis to the Old Port of Montreal, and its scenic views attract many tourists throughout the year. The canal features a 13.5 km long hiking and biking trail that runs alongside the canal, providing breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce: Activities and Tours

Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce is full of exciting and adventurous activities that you can experience solo or with a group. Some of the must-do things when in Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce include:

  • Food Tours: Get a glimpse of the local culinary scene through guided food tours and try some of the best local cuisines.

  • Theatres & Music Venues: Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce is home to several theaters and music venues that host numerous cultural events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce

For the ultimate Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce experience, consider staying for at least a week. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1-2: Visit the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts, explore Westmount Park, and indulge in a guided food tour.

Day 3-4: Trek up to the summits of Westmount and Mount-Royal parks. Have a picnic in Notre-Dame-de-Grâce Park.

Day 5-6: Take a stroll around the historic Old Montreal and learn about the city’s rich heritage. Attend a music performance in one of the city’s music venues.

Day 7: Wind up your week-long trip with a river cruise along the Lachine Canal before you depart.

Food and Drink Scene of Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: Poutine is a quintessential Canadian dish that originated from Quebec; it’s a bowl of fries, gravy, and cheese curds that take comfort food to another level.

  • Smoked Meat Sandwiches: Smoked meat is a staple in Montreal, and Schwartz’s Deli serves some of the best smoked meat sandwiches in the city.

Local Beverages

  • Caesars: The Caesar is a classic Canadian cocktail made using vodka, Clamato juice, and various spices and seasonings.

  • Montreal Craft Beer: Montreal is known for its vibrant craft beer scene, and there are several breweries and pubs that you can visit and enjoy a pint or two.

What to Buy in Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Quebec Cheese: Quebec produces several award-winning cheeses that are famous globally. You can find numerous cheese shops and dairies that offer free tasting before you make your purchase.

Getting to Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce is the Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, located just 20 minutes away from the borough.

Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce is well-connected to the rest of the city through bus services, and there are several bus stations located throughout the borough that can take you to various parts of Montreal.

Where to Go Next from Westmount–Notre-Dame-de-Grâce: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
