Introduction to Outremont

Outremont is a charming and affluent borough located in the heart of Montreal, Quebec. It is known for its stunning architecture, picturesque streets, and vibrant cultural scene. Outremont is mostly residential, but it boasts a wide range of attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you are interested in history, art, nature, or food, Outremont is the perfect destination for an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Outremont

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Musée des Beaux-Arts de Montréal

The Musée des Beaux-Arts de Montréal is Quebec’s largest museum and one of the most important art museums in Canada. It boasts an impressive collection of over 40,000 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs. The museum includes a broad range of collections, such as native art, international contemporary art, European art, and decorative arts.

Maison Outremont

Maison Outremont is an elegant 19th-century mansion that has been renovated to become a cultural center. It hosts a variety of events and exhibits, featuring art, music, and theater. Maison Outremont also has a beautiful garden, which is a perfect spot for a picnic or a relaxing walk.

Natural Attractions of Outremont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Summit Woods

Summit Woods is a beautiful natural park located at the summit of Mount Royal. The park offers a variety of walking paths, and it is an excellent spot for a hike. Summit Woods is also an excellent place to enjoy a picnic while taking in the breathtaking views of the city and surrounding mountains.

Parc Sir-Wilfrid-Laurier

Parc Sir-Wilfrid-Laurier is a lovely park that offers a range of outdoor activities, including tennis, soccer, basketball, and picnicking. The park has a playground for children, a public pool, and a skate park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outremont

Outremont has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. The Outremont Summit Park offers challenging trails that take you through the woods and up to the summit of Mount Royal. The Outremont Bike Route is another popular route that takes you through the borough’s most beautiful streets, parks, and gardens.

Best Things to Do in Outremont: Activities and Tours

Guided Walking Tour

A guided walking tour is an excellent way to discover the borough’s rich history and culture. Walk Tours Montreal offers a great walking tour that takes you to the most significant landmarks and attractions of Outremont.

Cooking Class

If you are a foodie, you will enjoy taking a cooking class at Atelier & Saveurs. They offer a variety of cooking classes, from French cuisine to sushi making.

Food and Drink Scene of Outremont

Must-Try Dishes

Poutine

Poutine is a traditional Quebec dish that’s popular in Outremont. It consists of fries topped with cheese curds and gravy.

Bagels

Montreal-style bagels are world-famous and a must-try when visiting the city. Fairmount Bagel and St-Viateur Bagel are two of the best-known bagel shops in Outremont.

Local Beverages

Microbreweries

Outremont is home to several microbreweries that offer a wide range of delicious beers. Dieu du Ciel!, Brasserie Dunham, and Trou du diable are three excellent breweries to try while in Outremont.

Where to Stay in Outremont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Outremont has a range of accommodations to choose from, whether you’re interested in a luxury hotel, a budget hostel, or a charming guesthouse. The Hotel Ruby Foo’s, The Ritz-Carlton Montréal, and Manoir Ambrose are three places to consider for luxurious accommodations.

What to Buy in Outremont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Outremont, make sure to bring home some traditional Quebecois food products, such as maple syrup, apple cider, and locally-crafted cheeses. Additionally, some of Quebec’s most famous artisans and artists have studios in Outremont. Taking home a unique piece of Quebecois art is a must.

Local Culture and Customs of Outremont

Outremont is known for its European influence, and it has a unique arts and culture scene that reflects this heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming of tourists, and you will find the borough to be safe and clean.

Best Time to Visit Outremont

The best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, spring and fall are also excellent times to visit, as temperatures are more moderate, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outremont

Do:

  • Explore the borough’s unique architecture
  • Indulge in the local food and drink scene
  • Take a guided walking tour to learn about the borough’s history and culture
  • Be respectful of the locals and their customs

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the streets
  • Be too noisy or disruptive after hours
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Outremont

Outremont is generally a safe borough, and visitors should feel comfortable walking around during the day or at night. It is always a good idea to stay aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close.

Health in Outremont

Outremont has several hospitals and clinics, and the healthcare system in Quebec is generally of high quality. Make sure to have adequate health insurance coverage while traveling.

Getting to Outremont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Outremont

The closest major airport to Outremont is the Montreal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, which is about 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Outremont

Outremont is well-connected by local buses, and the Gare d’Autocars de Montréal (Montreal’s central bus station) is located about 15 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Outremont is easily accessible by car or taxi, and there are also several car rental services available in the city.

How to Get Around in Outremont

Outremont is a pedestrian-friendly borough, and walking is a great way to experience the area. Bikes and scooters are also popular ways to get around and can be rented from various locations in the city.

Where to Go Next from Outremont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Old Montreal

Old Montreal is a beautiful historic district located just a few minutes from Outremont. It is home to many historical sites and attractions, including the Notre-Dame Basilica and the Old Port.

Plateau Mont-Royal

Plateau Mont-Royal is another trendy and artistic borough that is worth visiting. It has a great nightlife and a range of hip shops and restaurants.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking for natural beauty, cultural attractions, delicious food, or outdoor adventures, Outremont has something for everyone. Its charm and elegance make it one of the most beautiful boroughs in Montreal, and a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Quebec.
