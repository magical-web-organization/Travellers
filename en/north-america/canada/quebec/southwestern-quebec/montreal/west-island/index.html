Introduction to West Island

West Island is a charming borough of Montreal, Quebec, located in southwestern Quebec, Canada. It is bounded to the north by Roxboro, to the east by Rivière des Prairies, to the west by Sainte-Anne-de-Bellevue, and to the south by Lake Saint-Louis. West Island has become a popular tourist destination due to its beautiful parks, cultural attractions, natural wonders, and lively food scene.

In this travel guide, we’ll showcase the top attractions and things to do in West Island, as well as provide you with a list of must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, suggested itineraries, local food specialties, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in West Island

Here are some of the top-rated attractions that West Island offers:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stewart Museum: A gem of history, Stewart Museum is home to various collections of artifacts that speak to the history of Canada - from its French and British colonial past to the First Nations’ history.
  • Dorval Museum of Local History and Heritage: This small museum gives you a glimpse of the local history and heritage of the area, including the story of the Fur Trade and the City’s growth over time.
  • Lachine Fur Trade Museum: The Lachine Fur Trade Museum showcases exhibits highlighting the fur trade era in Lachine and its pivotal role in the growth and development of Canada.

Natural Attractions of West Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cap-Saint-Jacques Nature Park: A vast park with plenty of opportunities for hiking, cycling, fishing, and picnics. The beach area is a must-visit for some relaxing time in the sun, and the quiet trails are perfect for bird watching.
  • Bois-de-Liesse Nature Park: A natural forest trail full of beautiful views, wildlife sightings, bird watching, and more. The park offers picnic facilities, and there are benches along the path.
  • Fairview Pointe Claire Beach: This artificial white sand beach beside the mall is great for summer, and offers plenty of water activities and relaxation zones.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Island

West Island is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails and routes that you shouldn’t miss while you’re here:

  • L’Anse-à-l’Orme Nature Park: A vast terrain for hiking and biking with a beautiful view of the Saint-Lawrence River.
  • Pierrefonds-Roxboro Nature Park: This nature park offers several loops of varying lengths, complexity, and flora to discover on foot or on a bike.
  • Le Parc des Rapides: Stretched along the Saint Lawrence River, Le Parc des Rapides offers a few trails for walking or biking. The Lachine Rapids, one of the main attractions, gives an inspiring view of the race between humanity and nature over time.

Best Things to Do in West Island: Activities and Tours

West Island offers a wide variety of things to do, with something for everyone. Here are our top picks:

  • Montreal Aviation Museum: A must-visit for aviation enthusiasts and history buffs. The collection includes vintage aircraft, air force artifacts, and interactive exhibits.
  • Kayak Safari: This adventure-tour company provides a thrilling kayaking experience, where you get to explore the coastline, witness the diverse wildlife, and even participate in games.
  • Ile-Bizard Farmers Market: A treasure trove of natural delights, the farmers market is an excellent place to buy some locally grown and produced food.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Island

Are you planning to spend a week in West Island? Here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Begin with a visit to the Stewart Museum followed by the Dorval Museum of Local History and Heritage.
  • Day 2: Next, go for a hike or bike ride at Cap-Saint-Jacques Nature Park in the morning, followed by an afternoon on the beach at Fairview Pointe Claire.
  • Day 3: Tour the Montreal Aviation Museum in the morning, then take a break from the heat at the indoor SkyVenture Montreal wind tunnel, where you can try indoor skydiving.
  • Day 4: Take a leisurely stroll along the Saint-Lawrence River on the Lachine Canal and stop to visit the Lachine Fur Trade Museum.
  • Day 5: Take a kayak safari to experience the coast line and wildlife.
  • Day 6: Go to Ile-Bizard Farmers Market and spend the afternoon outdoors at Bois-de-Liesse Nature Park.
  • Day 7: Visit Le Parc des Rapides and enjoy the beautiful scenery, before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of West Island

West Island’s food scene is vast and varied, with something to offer everyone. Here’s a list of food and drink specialties to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Meat Sandwich: A staple of the Montreal food scene, this smoked meat sandwich from Schwartz’s Deli is a must-try.
  • Poutine: This classic Quebec dish consists of crispy fries topped with cheese curds and gravy. Try a delicious poutine at La Belle Province.
  • Bagels: Montreal is known for its delicious bagels, which are smaller and denser than traditional New York bagels. St-Viateur Bagel Shop is a local popular spot.

Local Beverages

  • Ice Cider:This refreshing beverage is created by freezing apples and then pressing them. Try it at the Cidrerie Du Minotaure.
  • Local Beers: Micro-brasserie Les 3 Brasseurs and La Succursale breweries are known for their wide selection of beer on tap.

Where to Stay in West Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Island offers a wide variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Sheraton Montreal Airport Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near the airport offering well-furnished rooms, on-site dining options, and amenities such as a fitness center and indoor pool.
  • Auberge St-Jacques: A charming guesthouse located in Dorval, providing guests with a comfortable stay, free WiFi, and a continental breakfast.
  • Holiday Inn Hotel & Suites Pointe-Claire: A family-friendly hotel located near the center of West Island, featuring an indoor pool, a fitness center, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in West Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you’re in West Island, don’t forget to pick up some souvenirs to take home. Here are some recommendations:

  • Montreal Canadiens Merchandise: The Montreal Canadiens are a famous ice hockey team, and you can buy all sorts of merchandise to support them, like jerseys and hats.
  • Maple Syrup: Quebec is famous for its maple syrup, so why not take some home with you? You can find it at the Ile-Bizard Farmers Market.
  • Art and Crafts: Visit the local artisan shops in Sainte-Anne-de-Bellevue or Pointe-Claire Village to find unique pieces that will remind you of your trip to West Island.

Local Culture and Customs of West Island

West Island is a mix of cultures and customs. Montreal is predominantly French-speaking, with English being its second language. However, West Island is a multicultural community that includes many other languages and cultures. The official currency is the Canadian dollar, and the exchange rate is relatively favorable to many foreign visitors. Shake hands when you meet, avoid talking while eating, and be sure to greet everyone when entering a store or a restaurant.

Best Time to Visit West Island

The best time to visit West Island is during the warmer months between May and October, where the average temperature is between 15-30°C, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities, and festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Island

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring West Island.

Do:

  • Greet people with a “Bonjour/Hello”: Montreal is a predominantly French city, so remember to greet people in both French and English when you meet them.
  • Try the local food: Be sure to try the local delicacies and specialties that West Island offers.
  • Respect the environment: West Island is known for its natural beauty and outdoor attractions, so be sure to respect the environment and maintain cleanliness.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places: Smoking is banned in most public places in Quebec, including bars, nightclubs, restaurants, and parks.
  • Forget to tip: Waiters, bartenders, and other service staff rely on tips as a significant part of their income. A 15% tip is considered standard in West Island.

Safety and Health

West Island is generally a safe place to visit. Like any other city, you should be careful and take necessary precautions.

Safety in West Island

  • Keep an eye on your belongings: As in any other busy city, be sure to keep a watch on your belongings in crowded areas to avoid pickpocketing incidents.
  • Use common sense: Like everywhere else, use your common sense, stay in the safe areas of the city, and be vigilant.

Health in West Island

Visitors to West Island do not require any vaccinations, as medical facilities, hospitals, and pharmacies are readily available. Remember to purchase travel insurance before embarking on your trip.

Getting to West Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

West Island is well-connected and accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near West Island

  • Montréal-Trudeau International Airport (YUL): Located about 10 minutes away from West Island, it is the most convenient airport for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near West Island

  • Gare d’autocars de Montréal: The bus terminal is located in downtown Montreal and is easily accessible by metro or taxi.

Other Options

  • Taxis and Shuttles: You can also hire a taxi or shuttle service to get around the city.

How to Get Around in West Island

The best and most convenient way to get around West Island is by car. You can rent a car at the airport, or you can use a ride-hailing service like Uber or Lyft. Public transportation is also available, but it is not as convenient for tourists, as the bus routes can be complex to navigate and may take longer.

Where to Go Next from West Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring West Island, there are other places to visit nearby, including:

  • Montreal: Montreal is a vibrant and multicultural city, located just a short drive away from West Island, with plenty of attractions, shopping, and dining options.
  • Oka National Park: This nearby national park offers beautiful landscapes, hiking trails, and campsites.
  • Saint-Joseph’s Oratory of Mount-Royal: A majestic basilica and national shrine, located in Montreal, where you can witness stunning views of the city.

Conclusion

West Island is undoubtedly a hidden gem that offers visitors a mix of scenic views, cultural experiences, outdoor activities, and fantastic food. It’s no wonder that it has become a popular tourist destination in Canada. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can customize your trip to your liking and make the most of your visit to West Island by experiencing its natural beauty and cultural richness.
