Introduction to Terrebonne

Terrebonne is a charming city located in the Laplaine region of Lanaudière, Southwestern Quebec, Canada. With its rich history, breathtaking nature, and vibrant culture, Terrebonne is a must-visit destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Terrebonne

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, Terrebonne has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Terrebonne:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Île-des-Moulins: A historical site that showcases the architectural and industrial heritage of Terrebonne. The island features several waterfalls, bridges, and old mills, making it a perfect spot for a leisurely stroll.
  • Le Vieux-Terrebonne: A picturesque quarter full of historic buildings, restaurants, and shops. The area is pedestrian-friendly and is perfect for a leisurely walk.
  • La Maison Bélisle: A 19th-century house museum that provides visitors with a glimpse into the daily life of Terrebonne’s affluent families during the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Terrebonne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc du Grand-Coteau: A park with several walking trails, picnic areas, and a playground for children. The park offers scenic views of the city and is a popular spot for birdwatching.
  • Parc des Pépinières: A peaceful park that features a variety of plant species and a small pond. The park is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.
  • Mont Rigaud: A nearby mountain that offers some of the best hiking trails in the region. The trails wind through forests, streams, and hills, providing visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Terrebonne

Terrebonne is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. The following are some of the top routes in the area:

  • Le P’tit Train du Nord: A 232 km-long trail that spans the entire province of Quebec. The trail starts in Bois-des-Filion, passes through Terrebonne, and ends in Mont-Laurier.
  • Parc du Grand-Coteau: The park features several walking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Parc des Prairies: A 26-hectare park that offers walking and cycling trails, a skatepark, and several sports fields.

Best Things to Do in Terrebonne: Activities and Tours

Terrebonne offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Terrebonne:

  • Helicopter Tours: An exciting way to see Terrebonne and its surroundings from the air. The tour takes visitors on a 20-minute flight over the city and its landmarks.
  • Kayaking on the Mascouche River: A relaxing way to explore the Mascouche River and its surroundings. The tour takes visitors on a 2-hour kayaking adventure through calm waters and scenic views.
  • Food Tours: A delicious way to discover Terrebonne’s culinary scene. The tour takes visitors on a 3-hour walking tour that stops at different restaurants and shops, sampling some of the city’s best food and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Terrebonne

Must-Try Dishes

Terrebonne’s food scene is a blend of French and Canadian cuisine, making it unique and appetizing. Here are some must-try dishes in Terrebonne:

  • Tourtière: A savory meat pie that is a traditional French-Canadian dish.
  • Poutine: A dish made with fries, cheese curds, and gravy, which is a Canadian favorite.
  • Maple Taffy: A candy made from maple syrup, which is a popular treat in Quebec.

Local Beverages

In Terrebonne, you’ll find a range of alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages to quench your thirst after a long day of exploring. Here are some local beverages you can try:

  • Unibroue Beer: An award-winning microbrewery that produces some of the best beer in Quebec.
  • Ice Cider: A sweet, dessert wine made from frozen apples, which is unique to Quebec.

Where to Stay in Terrebonne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Terrebonne offers several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Holiday Inn Express Terrebonne: A comfortable and modern hotel that offers a fitness center and a free breakfast buffet.
  • Auberge de la Fontaine: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Terrebonne’s Old Town. The guesthouse features cozy rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • HI Montreal Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that is perfect for travelers on a tight budget. The hostel offers dormitory rooms and private rooms at an affordable rate.

What to Buy in Terrebonne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Terrebonne, you’ll find several unique souvenirs and local products to take home. Here are some of the best options:

  • Maple Syrup: Quebec produces some of the best maple syrup in the world, and Terrebonne is no exception. You can find maple syrup and maple-based products in most shops around the city.
  • Artisanal Crafts: Local artisans create a range of handmade products, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles. You can find these products in Terrebonne’s boutiques and markets.
  • Unibroue Beer: You can take home some of Terrebonne’s best beer as a souvenir, but make sure to check for any regulations when flying with alcoholic beverages.

Local Culture and Customs of Terrebonne

Terrebonne is a multicultural society where you’ll find a blend of French and Canadian traditions. It’s important to respect the local culture and customs when visiting Terrebonne. Here are some essential things to know:

  • Language: The official language of Terrebonne is French, but English is also spoken widely.
  • Greeting etiquette: It’s typical to greet people with a handshake and to use “vous” instead of “tu” for formal conversation.
  • Tipping etiquette: It is customary to leave a 15-20% tip at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Terrebonne

Terrebonne experiences four seasons with varying temperatures and weather conditions. The best time to visit Terrebonne is during the summer months (June to August), where you’ll find warm weather and a range of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Terrebonne

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including maple syrup, tourtière, and poutine.
  • Do take a scenic hike or bike ride in the local parks.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places, and always dispose of waste properly.
  • Don’t neglect your personal safety and security, and always be aware of your surroundings.
  • Don’t forget to respect the wildlife and natural environment when exploring the parks and hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Terrebonne

Terrebonne is a safe city for travelers, but it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Always be aware of your surroundings, avoid walking alone at night, and keep your valuables safe and secure.

Health in Terrebonne

Terrebonne has a high standard of healthcare facilities and services, and emergency services are available 24/7. It’s always a good idea to purchase health insurance before traveling to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Terrebonne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Terrebonne is easily accessible by various modes of transport.

Airports Near Terrebonne

If you’re flying into Terrebonne, you’ll most likely land at Montreal’s Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport, which is located 40 km away from Terrebonne.

Bus Stations Near Terrebonne

Terrebonne has a bus station located in the city center, which is served by local and regional buses.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or car rental from Montreal to Terrebonne, which is a 30-minute drive.

How to Get Around in Terrebonne

Terrebonne is a walkable city, but there are also several transportation options available:

  • Bus: The local bus service is reliable and affordable and connects the city’s different neighborhoods.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available throughout the city and are a convenient way to get around.
  • Car Rental: If you plan on exploring the surrounding area, renting a car is a good option for added convenience.

Where to Go Next from Terrebonne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Terrebonne is surrounded by several destinations that are worth exploring:

  • Montreal: A vibrant city full of culture, history, and attractions. Montreal is only a 30-minute drive away from Terrebonne.
  • Laval: A nearby city with several parks, nature reserves, and cultural attractions.
  • Laurentian Mountains: A beautiful mountain range located just north of Montreal, which is perfect for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Conclusion

Terrebonne is a charming city that offers something for everyone, whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now ready to plan your perfect trip to Terrebonne. Bon voyage!
