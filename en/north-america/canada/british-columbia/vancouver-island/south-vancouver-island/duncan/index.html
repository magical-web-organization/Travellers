Introduction to Duncan

Located in South Vancouver Island, Duncan is a small but charming city in British Columbia, Canada. Known as the “City of Totems,” Duncan is proud of its rich Indigenous heritage and celebrates it through its numerous totem poles scattered throughout the town. Not only does the city have a rich cultural history, but it also boasts an abundance of natural attractions, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight some of the best things to do in Duncan and offer some tips and insights to help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Duncan

Duncan has plenty of attractions to visit including culturally important museums and historical places, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Duncan can explore the cultural heritage of the region through a range of attractions, including:

  • The Quw’utsun’ Cultural and Conference Centre: Home to many Indigenous exhibits, traditional dance shows, and displays.
  • The Cowichan Valley Museum and Archives: It’s inside the Vancity Credit Union building and is perfect to learn more about the history of Cowichan Valley.
  • The Railway Forest: Here you’ll find the history of the Canadian Railway and learn how the forest helped contribute to the Duncan’s history.

Natural Attractions of Duncan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Duncan also offers plenty of outdoor activities with scenic views and picturesque attractions to explore, such as:

  • The Cowichan River Trail: One of the most famous trails in the area, perfect for hiking and biking with a winding river to add to the beauty.
  • Mount Tzouhalem: Another popular hiking trail that offers an excellent view of the valley.
  • The Kinsol Trestle: A 614ft-long bridge along the Trans-Canada Trail with scenic views of the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Duncan

Duncan has many fantastic trails that hikers and bikers can explore. Some of the popular ones are:

  • The Cowichan Valley Trail: A trail that runs from Victoria to Duncan and provides scenic views of the valley and nearby forests.
  • Mount Prevost Trail: A challenging trail that is perfect for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Duncan: Activities and Tours

Besides exploring Duncan’s natural and historical attractions, visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities and tours like:

  • Whale Watching Tours: Nothing beats seeing whales and other marine animals in their natural habitat.
  • Golfing: With three courses nearby, visitors can indulge in a round of golf in the beautiful Cowichan Valley.
  • Wine Tours: Visitors can enjoy a day trip in Cowichan Valley visiting multiple wineries and enjoy tasting the locally made wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Duncan

For a comprehensive exploration of Duncan, we suggest the following itinerary:

Day 1 Quw’utsun’ Cultural and Conference Centre
Day 2 Cowichan River Trail
Day 3 The Cowichan Valley Museum and Archives
Day 4 Mount Prevost Trail
Day 5 The Kinsol Trestle
Day 6 Whale Watching Tours
Day 7 Cowichan Valley Wine Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Duncan

Duncan has a great food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants and bars serving excellent cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock: a traditional Indigenous bread.
  • Bulldog Rolls: a spicy, cheesy roll filled with chicken, served hot.
  • Fish and Chips: A popular Canadian dish, Duncan does it best with their locally caught seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Cowichan Bay Sea Cider: Hand-crafted ciders made on Vancouver Island.
  • Phillips Brewing Company: A local microbrewery offering plenty of unique beer flavors to try.
  • Vancouver Island Wineries: Offering locally made wine for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Duncan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Duncan has plenty of options for accommodations. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Best Western Cowichan Valley Inn: Perfect for families and couples looking for a comfortable stay with luxurious amenities.
  • Travelodge by Wyndham: Ideal for budget travelers and short stays with all the necessary amenities you need.
  • Inn at the Quay’utsun Conference and Cultural Centre: A luxurious way to immerse oneself in local culture with various heritage exhibits in the building.

What to Buy in Duncan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Duncan has plenty of shops that sell souvenirs and local products, including:

  • The Quw’utsun’ Cultural and Conference Centre Gift Shop: Here, visitors can purchase Indigenous artwork and locally made souvenirs and pottery.
  • Cowichan Valley Artisans: A shop selling locally made arts and crafts, such as pottery, glasswork, and jewelry.
  • Nancy’s Fashion: A boutique for fashion enthusiasts with unique styles of clothing and accessories made by local designers.

Local Culture and Customs of Duncan

As mentioned earlier, Duncan has a rich Indigenous heritage that is celebrated. Visitors are encouraged to learn more about the heritage and also respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Duncan

The best time to visit Duncan is during the months of June-September as the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities one can indulge in.

Do’s and Don’ts of Duncan

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit the Indigenous cultural centers and learn more about their heritage.
  • Indulge in outdoor activities and hikes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the town or on the hiking trails.
  • Refrain from taking home Indigenous artifacts and totem poles from public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Duncan

Duncan is generally a safe place; visitors need to exercise regular caution as they would in any other town or city.

Health in Duncan

Duncan has medical facilities and sufficient pharmacies for visitors who wish to be vaccinated or need medication.

Getting to Duncan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Duncan is easily accessible from several airports and bus stations. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Duncan

  • Victoria International Airport: Located about 45 kilometers away from Duncan, which is an hour’s drive.
  • Nanaimo Airport: Located 76 kilometers from Duncan, which is around an hour and a half drive.

Bus Stations Near Duncan

  • Greyhound Station: Located in Victoria downtown and provides a direct bus service to Duncan.

Other Options

  • Driving from Vancouver: Duncan is around a 4-hour drive from Vancouver.
  • Taking a ferry: Visitors can take a ferry from Vancouver (Tsawwassen Terminal) to Victoria and then drive to Duncan.

How to Get Around in Duncan

Duncan is a small town and can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by car. Visitors can rent bikes or cars from several rental services in the town.

Where to Go Next from Duncan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Duncan is located near several beautiful and famous destinations that visitors can explore, such as:

  • Nanaimo: Located only an hour’s drive from Duncan, known for its beaches and fishing.
  • Victoria: The capital city of British Columbia and only 50 kilometers away from Duncan, offering heritage sites, museums, and beautiful restaurants.

Conclusion

Duncan is a small but charming town located in British Columbia. It offers a unique combination of Indigenous culture, history, natural beauty, outdoor activities, and delicious cuisine. Plan your trip to this beautiful city and explore everything it has to offer.
