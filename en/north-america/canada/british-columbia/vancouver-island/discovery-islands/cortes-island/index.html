Introduction to Cortes Island

Cortes Island, located in the Discovery Islands region of British Columbia, Canada, is a hidden gem that attracts visitors seeking a peaceful, natural escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its dense forests, pristine beaches, and abundance of wildlife, Cortes Island is ideal for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and anyone looking for a relaxing vacation. Accessible only by ferry or private boat, Cortes Island offers visitors a chance to truly disconnect and unwind.

List of Places to Visit in Cortes Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cortes Island Museum and Archives: This museum, located in the heart of Cortes Island, offers visitors a chance to learn about the history of the island, including its First Nations heritage.

Natural Attractions of Cortes Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kw’as Park: This park, located on the south end of Cortes Island, offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding ocean and mountains, as well as access to various hiking trails.
  • Hague Lake: This freshwater lake, located in the middle of Cortes Island, is ideal for swimming, fishing, canoeing, and paddleboarding.
  • Carrington Bay: With its white sandy beach, crystal-clear waters, and lush forests, Carrington Bay is one of the most picturesque spots on Cortes Island. Visitors can hike to the bay or take a water taxi from nearby Manson’s Landing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cortes Island

  • Carrington Bay Trail: This easy, 2.5 km trail leads visitors through dense forests to the beautiful Carrington Bay.
  • Gorge Harbour Trail: This moderate, 5 km trail offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding ocean and mountains, as well as access to various beaches and swimming spots.
  • Siskin Lane Trail: This challenging, 14 km trail takes visitors through rugged terrain to various viewpoints and secluded beaches.

Best Things to Do in Cortes Island: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Watching: Cortes Island is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including orcas, humpback whales, seals, sea lions, bald eagles, and black bears. Visitors can go on guided tours to see these animals up-close in their natural habitats.
  • Kayaking: With its calm waters and stunning scenery, Cortes Island is a kayaker’s paradise. Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the island’s coastline on their own or join a guided tour.
  • Yoga Retreats: Cortes Island is known for its spiritual and wellness-focused retreats. Visitors can participate in yoga classes, meditation sessions, and other wellness activities while surrounded by the island’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cortes Island

Day 1: Arrival and settling into accommodation Day 2: Hike to Carrington Bay and enjoy a picnic on the beach Day 3: Kayak tour of the surrounding waters Day 4: Visit the Cortes Island Museum and Archives Day 5: Explore Hague Lake and enjoy a swim or paddle Day 6: Join a guided wildlife watching tour Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Cortes Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood: Cortes Island is known for its fresh and delicious seafood, including salmon, halibut, and shellfish.
  • Organic produce: Many local farms on Cortes Island offer fresh, organic produce that visitors can purchase at farmers’ markets or in local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Cortes Island Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers that incorporate local ingredients.
  • Hollyhock: This wellness retreat center offers various teas and tonics made from locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Cortes Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cortes Island Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms in the heart of Cortes Island.
  • Hollyhock: This wellness retreat center offers accommodations for those seeking a more holistic and spiritual experience.
  • Sea Parrot Ocean View Manor: This luxury bed and breakfast offers stunning ocean views and personalized service.

What to Buy in Cortes Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally-Made Crafts: Many local residents on Cortes Island are artisans who create unique and beautiful crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Local Honey: Cortes Island produces delicious honey that visitors can purchase at local farmers’ markets or in local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Cortes Island

The culture of Cortes Island is heavily influenced by the First Nations people who have lived on the land for thousands of years. Visitors to Cortes Island should be respectful of this cultural heritage and seek to learn more about it through visits to museums and other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Cortes Island

The best time to visit Cortes Island is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and dry and the island is alive with outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cortes Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace while exploring the island
  • Learn about the First Nations culture and heritage of the island
  • Support local businesses and artisans while shopping and dining on the island

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harm the wildlife on the island
  • Take shells or other natural treasures from the beaches
  • Leave trash or garbage around the island

Safety and Health

Safety in Cortes Island

Cortes Island is generally a safe and peaceful place to visit, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the island’s natural areas. Be aware of the danger of wild animals and check with local guides or authorities before embarking on any outdoor expeditions.

Health in Cortes Island

Cortes Island has one medical clinic that is open Monday to Friday. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them and take steps to prevent illnesses such as Lyme disease and West Nile virus.

Getting to Cortes Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cortes Island

The closest airport to Cortes Island is located in Campbell River, which is accessed via a 10-minute flight from Vancouver.

Bus Stations Near Cortes Island

There are no direct bus routes to Cortes Island, but visitors can take buses from nearby towns to Campbell River or Quadra Island and then take a ferry to Cortes Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private boat or water taxi to Cortes Island from nearby towns or cities.

How to Get Around in Cortes Island

The best way to get around Cortes Island is by foot, bike, or car. Visitors can rent bikes or cars on the island or bring their own. There is limited public transportation on the island, and taxis are expensive.

Where to Go Next from Cortes Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Quadra Island: This neighboring island is a popular destination for outdoor activities and offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding ocean and wilderness areas.
  • Desolation Sound: This nearby wilderness area is known for its rugged beauty and opportunities for outdoor adventures, including kayaking, hiking, and wildlife watching.

Conclusion

Cortes Island is an idyllic destination that offers visitors a chance to connect with nature, learn about local culture and history, and enjoy a peaceful, relaxing vacation. With its abundance of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine, Cortes Island is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Vancouver Island region of British Columbia, Canada.
