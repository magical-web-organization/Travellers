Introduction to Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Nestled in the heart of Vancouver Island, British Columbia, Canada, Mount Washington Alpine Resort is a popular winter sport and summer recreation destination. Located in a sub-alpine region, Mount Washington features natural beauty, hiking trails, and stunning vistas all year round.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alpine Lodge: A historic wooden lodge that offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Raven Lodge: A modern facility that houses a restaurant, conference rooms, and other amenities.
  • Vancouver Island Mountain Center: A hub for outdoor education, research, and adventure.
  • Comox Valley Visitor Centre: A great starting point for visitors to gather information about the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Mount Washington Alpine Resort: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paradise Meadows: A scenic alpine meadow known for its diverse plant and animal life.
  • Glacier Viewpoint Trail: A moderate hike that offers breathtaking views of the Comox Glacier.
  • Battleship Lake Trail: A beginner’s hike around a stunning forested lake.
  • Mount Albert Edward Loop Trail: A challenging hike that covers 17.8 km and reaches an elevation of 2,150 meters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Washington Alpine Resort

  • Mount Becher: A moderate 8 km hike that offers panoramic views of the Discovery Islands and beyond.
  • Mount Washington: A challenging 5 km hike that reaches the top of Vancouver Island’s highest peak.
  • Forbidden Plateau: A stunning 13 km hike that takes you to the ancient lava flow and Karmutzen Lake.
  • Moon Saddle: A 7 km hike that offers beautiful waterfall views and stunning sub-alpine vistas.

Best Things to Do in Mount Washington Alpine Resort: Activities and Tours

  • Snowboarding and skiing at the resort’s top-rated slopes.
  • Snowshoeing, ice skating, and tobogganing for winter enthusiasts.
  • Snowcat or snowmobile tours for those looking for a different adventure.
  • Summer activities including hiking, mountain biking, and zipline tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at Raven Lodge Day 2: Snowboarding at the resort’s slopes Day 3: Snowshoeing in the Paradise Meadows Day 4: Lava Flow and Karmutzen Lake Hike Day 5: Moon Saddle Hike Day 6: Becher Mountain Hike Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: Canada’s traditional dish of crispy fries topped with cheese curds and gravy.
  • Nanaimo Bars: Rich and creamy dessert bars made of chocolate, custard, and coconut.
  • Smoked Salmon: A local dish featuring Vancouver Island’s fresh smoked salmon.

Local Beverages

  • Vancouver Island Brewing Company: A craft brewery known for their wide selection of beers.
  • Crown Isle Resort: A golf resort featuring a bar that serves locally crafted cocktails and other popular drinks.

Where to Stay in Mount Washington Alpine Resort: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Raven Lodge: A modern facility that offers rooms and suites.
  • Bear and Deer Lodges: Hotel-style properties offering comfortable rooms that are within walking distance of the resort’s amenities.
  • Guesthouses: A great choice for budget-conscious travelers, with several options available in the nearby Comox Valley.

What to Buy in Mount Washington Alpine Resort: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade ceramic mugs featuring alpine-inspired artwork
  • Wood-carved figures depicting local wildlife
  • Locally made chocolates and preserves
  • Vancouver Island honey

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Locals appreciate the area’s natural beauty and the importance of preserving the delicate ecosystem surrounding it. Visitors to the area are encouraged to respect nature, to pack out any trash they generate, and to stay on marked trails to prevent any damage to the flora and fauna.

Best Time to Visit Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Summer and winter seasons both offer unique and diverse opportunities to experience the resort. However, the peak season for visitors is usually December to February for winter sports, and June to September for summer recreation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Do:

  • Plan your trip well in advance of travel dates.
  • Bring appropriate outdoor gear.
  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Stay on marked trails.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or waste on trails or anywhere else in the park.
  • Disturb or harass any wildlife.
  • Light fires outside designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Washington Alpine Resort

  • Observe physical distancing protocols and wear masks when indoors.
  • Stay away from the boundary of the area and don’t cut switch-backs
  • Read and respect the park’s safety and trail signs.

Health in Mount Washington Alpine Resort

Visitors should ensure they have necessary health and travel insurance before visiting the resort.

Getting to Mount Washington Alpine Resort: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Driving: There are numerous highways and roads that lead to the resort, making it a great option for those who plan to drive.
  • Bus: Local and regional bus service is available to the resort - however, the timetable can be limited.
  • Flights: Regional airports are located nearby in Comox, Nanaimo, and Campbell River.

How to Get Around in Mount Washington Alpine Resort

The resort has free shuttles that run at several designated stops around the vicinity to transport you to the different areas of the property. Alternatively, visitors may choose to rent a car or take part in tours that include transportation.

Where to Go Next from Mount Washington Alpine Resort: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Denman and Hornby Islands: Take a short ferry ride to visit these stunning islands for island tours, bike rides, and beach activities.
  • Campbell River and Strathcona Park: A day trip perfect for those who love outdoor adventures including kayaking, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
