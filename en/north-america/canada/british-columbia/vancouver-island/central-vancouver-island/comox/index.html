Introduction to Comox

Comox is a quaint and picturesque town located in central Vancouver Island, in British Columbia, Canada. It is a popular tourist destination known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage.

Comox is situated on the east coast of Vancouver Island, overlooking the scenic Comox Bay and surrounded by the Beaufort Range mountains. The town boasts a diverse range of attractions, from museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a detailed look at the best things to see and do in Comox, where to stay, how to get around, and much more. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Comox

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Comox has a rich cultural heritage, and there are several museums and historical places that reflect its history and traditions. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Comox:

  • Comox Air Force Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Royal Canadian Air Force and the role played by the Comox airbase during World War II.
  • Filberg Heritage Lodge and Park: This heritage park features a beautiful lodge, gardens, and art galleries showcasing the works of local artists.
  • I-Hos Gallery: This First Nations gallery showcases the works of Indigenous artists from the region and offers a glimpse into their history and culture.
  • Comox Valley Art Gallery: This contemporary art gallery features works by local and regional artists and hosts regular exhibitions and events.

Natural Attractions of Comox: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Comox is blessed with beautiful natural scenery, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views for visitors to explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Comox:

  • Mount Washington Alpine Resort: This popular ski resort offers a range of outdoor activities, including skiing, snowboarding, tubing, snowshoeing, hiking, and mountain biking.
  • Comox Valley Farmers’ Market: This weekly farmers’ market offers visitors the chance to sample fresh local produce, crafts, and artisanal foods.
  • Nymph Falls Nature Park: This scenic park features a beautiful waterfall, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Seal Bay Nature Park: This park offers visitors the chance to explore a beautiful forest, beaches, and tidal lagoons, home to an abundance of wildlife, including seals and bald eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Comox

Comox has a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness and expertise. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Comox:

  • Comox Lake Bluffs Ecological Reserve Trail: This short but scenic trail offers stunning views of the Comox Valley and is a great spot for birdwatching.
  • Comox Valley Land Trust Trail: This extensive trail network covers over 40 kilometers of meadows, woods, and streams and is a great spot for hiking, biking or horseback riding, and discovering the local wildlife.
  • Goose Spit Park Trail: This easy trail offers visitors a scenic stroll along the beach and through a beautiful forest of Douglas fir trees.

Best Things to Do in Comox: Activities and Tours

Comox offers some of the best outdoor activities and tours, so let’s explore some of the must-try experiences in Comox:

  • Whale watching tours: Take a tour to see the amazing humpback whales that migrate through Comox from June to November.
  • Fishing charters: Comox is an angler’s paradise, and visitors can experience fishing for salmon, halibut, and other species in the rich waters of the Strait of Georgia.
  • Kayaking tours: Explore Comox Bay’s coastline and wildlife on a kayak tour led by expert guides.
  • Golfing: Comox has some of the best golf courses in Canada, including the Crown Isle Resort and Golf Community and the Comox Golf Club.

Week-Long Itinerary for Comox

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Comox:

  • Day 1: Visit Filberg Heritage Lodge and Park, I-Hos Gallery, and Comox Valley Art Gallery.
  • Day 2: Go skiing or snowboarding at Mount Washington Alpine Resort.
  • Day 3: Explore Nymph Falls Nature Park and Seal Bay Nature Park.
  • Day 4: Take a whale watching tour or go fishing in the Strait of Georgia.
  • Day 5: Go kayaking and explore Comox Bay’s coastline and wildlife.
  • Day 6: Play golf at one of Comox’s excellent golf courses.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Comox Valley Farmers’ Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Comox

Comox has a vibrant food and drink scene, offering a range of local and international cuisine, artisanal products, and craft beverages. Here’s a look at some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips: Comox’s proximity to the ocean means visitors can enjoy some of the freshest seafood, including fish and chips, a local favorite.
  • Seafood chowder: This hearty soup made with fresh seafood, vegetables, and cream is a comforting and delicious dish that is perfect for chilly days.
  • Nanaimo bars: This Canadian dessert is a delicious combination of a crumbly chocolate and graham cracker base, creamy custard filling, and a chocolate topping.

Local Beverages

  • Comox Valley Brewery Trail: This beer trail features several local breweries that offer craft beers and ciders to suit all tastes, including Gladstone Brewing, Land and Sea Brewing Company, and Forbidden Brewing Co.
  • Coastal Black Estate Winery: This family-owned winery offers a range of award-winning fruit wines made from locally sourced fruits.

Where to Stay in Comox: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Comox has many accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, ranging from luxurious resorts and hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts and affordable hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Comox:

  • Crown Isle Resort and Golf Community: This luxurious resort offers spacious rooms, a golf course, a spa, and delicious dining options.
  • Kye Bay B&B: This cozy bed and breakfast offer comfortable rooms, a delicious breakfast, and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Comox Valley Inn and Suites: This affordable hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Comox: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Comox offers visitors the chance to take home unique and authentic souvenirs and locally produced products, such as:

  • Arts and crafts from the Filberg Heritage Lodge and Park
  • Fruit wines from Coastal Black Estate Winery
  • Local honey from Wayward Distillation House
  • Handmade chocolates from Hot Chocolates

Local Culture and Customs of Comox

Comox and the Vancouver Island region have a rich First Nations history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the local customs and traditions. Some of the cultural events and festivals in Comox include:

  • Kw’aksistala: A Celebration of Indigenous Culture: This annual event showcases the traditions, art, music, and dance of the K’ómoks First Nation.
  • Comox Valley Exhibition: This annual agricultural fair features livestock exhibits, competitions, and entertainment for the whole family.

Best Time to Visit Comox

The best time to visit Comox is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor events and activities to enjoy. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the foliage turns vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it an ideal time for hikes and scenic drives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Comox

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the First Nations communities.
  • Take time to explore Comox’s natural beauty and landmarks.
  • Try the local seafood and produce.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Comox

Comox is a safe destination for visitors, but it’s always important to exercise basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows, using designated crosswalks while walking, and being aware of your surroundings while out exploring the city.

Health in Comox

Comox has a well-established healthcare system, with several medical clinics and hospitals available to visitors in case of emergency or illness. It’s advisable to purchase travel insurance before visiting Comox to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Comox: Transportation Options and Logistics

Comox is easily accessible by air, road, or bus. Here are some of the transportation options available to visitors:

Airports Near Comox

  • Comox Valley Airport: This airport offers regional flights to cities in British Columbia and Alberta and national and international connecting flights through Vancouver or Calgary.

Bus Stations Near Comox

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus company offers service to Comox from Vancouver, Victoria, and other cities in British Columbia.

Other Options

  • BC Ferries: This ferry service connects Vancouver Island to the mainland of British Columbia and offers service to and from Comox via Powell River and other nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Comox

Comox is a small town, and most of the attractions and landmarks are within walking distance. Visitors can also use public transportation, taxis, or rental cars to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Comox: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Comox is located in a beautiful part of British Columbia, surrounded by many nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the top nearby destinations to visit:

  • Victoria: This beautiful city is located on Vancouver Island’s southeast coast and is known for its colonial architecture, gardens, and cultural attractions.
  • Tofino: This popular surfing town is located on the west coast of Vancouver Island and is known for its stunning beaches, rainforests, and wildlife.
  • Campbell River: This beautiful town is located to the north of Comox and is a great spot for outdoor adventures, including fishing, hiking, and wildlife watching.

Conclusion

Comox is a beautiful and charming town that offers visitors plenty of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Comox has something to offer everyone. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to Comox, and you’ll experience the best the city has to offer, from its delicious local cuisine to its rich cultural heritage.
