Introduction to Saturna Island

Saturna Island is one of the least populated and the most unspoiled of the Southern Gulf Islands, situated in the Salish Sea between Vancouver Island and the mainland of British Columbia. Saturna Island is an excellent destination for visitors who want to enjoy the natural wonders of the Pacific Northwest, hike and bike in pristine forests, sunbathe at secluded beaches, explore cultural treasures, meet friendly locals, and taste local delicacies. Plan your stay on Saturna Island, Canada, to discover what you have been missing.

List of Places to Visit in Saturna Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saturna Island Museum

The Saturna Island Museum presents the history and culture of Saturna Island and its inhabitants, including the First Nations, early settlers, naturalists, and visitors. The museum has unique exhibits, including a reproduction of a Depression-era kitchen, a collection of fossils and archaeological artifacts, and a tribute to the Saturna Island whale-seeking industry.

East Point

East Point, also known as the Lighthouse Park, is a unique and spectacular place that offers a glimpse into Saturna Island’s maritime history and natural beauty. East Point is home to a first-order Fresnel lens lighthouse, two observation decks, a restored fog alarm building, and a visitor center that hosts interactive displays, educational programs, and historical accounts. East Point is also an excellent spot to watch whales, sea lions, porpoises, eagles, and other seabirds.

Gulf Islands National Park Reserve

Gulf Islands National Park Reserve features some of the most spectacular natural scenery in Canada, including rugged coastline, old-growth forests, meadowlands, wetlands, and marine environments. The park covers several Gulf Islands, including Saturna Island, and offers endless opportunities for hiking, kayaking, boating, wildlife watching, and camping. Some of the popular sites in the park include the Cabbage Island Marine Reserve, the Winter Cove Marine Provincial Park, the Narvaez Bay, and the Lyall Creek Trail.

Natural Attractions of Saturna Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Warburton Pike

Mount Warburton Pike, also known as Mount Saturna, is the highest peak on Saturna Island and offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the Gulf Islands, San Juan Islands, and the Olympic Mountains. The hike to the summit is relatively challenging, but the reward is worth the effort.

Narvaez Bay

Narvaez Bay is a relatively small, sheltered, and picturesque bay located on the southeast side of Saturna Island. Narvaez Bay is an excellent spot for kayaking, canoeing, swimming, and wildlife watching as it is home to a diverse range of marine life, including seals, sea lions, otters, and many species of fish and birds.

Winter Cove

Winter Cove is a pristine estuary and nature reserve located in the southwest corner of Saturna Island and accessible by foot or by boat. Winter Cove is a unique ecosystem that features a saltwater lagoon, a sandbar, a sandy beach, and a tidal zone, and attracts many migratory birds, such as sandpipers, eagles, ospreys, and herons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saturna Island

Saturna Island offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails suitable for all skill levels and interests, from short day hikes to multi-day backcountry adventures. Some of the most popular trails on the island include:

Mount Warburton Pike Trail

The Mount Warburton Pike Trail is a strenuous, 6.2 miles, out-and-back trail that starts from the Mount Warburton Pike trailhead, near the Saturna Island Vineyards. The trail rises gradually through thick forests before reaching the summit, offering panoramic views of the surrounding islands.

Narvaez Bay Trail

The Narvaez Bay Trail is an easy, 2.4 miles, out-and-back trail that starts from the Narvaez Bay parking lot and leads to the Narvaez Bay beach. The trail offers stunning views of forested hills, rocky shores, and peaceful waters, and is an excellent spot for birdwatching, swimming, and picnicking.

Winter Cove Trail

The Winter Cove Trail is a moderate, 2.5 miles, out-and-back trail that starts from the Winter Cove parking lot and leads to the Georgina Point Lighthouse. The trail passes through a scenic coastal forest and offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean and surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Saturna Island: Activities and Tours

Saturna Island offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all interests, including:

Whale Watching

Saturna Island is one of the best places to watch whales, as it is located on the migratory path of several species, such as Orcas, Humpbacks, and Grey whales. Several local companies offer whale watching tours that run from May to October and provide a chance to witness these magnificent creatures up close.

Kayaking

Kayaking is a popular activity on Saturna Island, as the island’s coastline is rugged, remote, and scenic. Several local companies offer guided kayaking tours that explore the island’s natural wonders, including the caves, coves, and waterfalls.

Wine Tasting

Saturna Island is home to several award-winning wineries, such as Saturna Island Vineyards and Vineyard 28, that offer guided tours and tastings of their locally grown wine, cider, and mead. Visitors can learn about the island’s unique climate, soil, and grape varieties while enjoying the stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saturna Island

Day 1: Arrive at Saturna Island and explore the East Point Lighthouse Park, the Saturna Island Museum, and the Cabbage Island Marine Reserve.

Day 2: Hike the Mount Warburton Pike Trail and enjoy the panoramic views of the Gulf Islands and the Olympic Mountains.

Day 3: Take a guided whale watching tour and witness the Orcas, Humpbacks, and Grey whales in their natural habitat.

Day 4: Explore the Gulf Islands National Park Reserve and visit the Winter Cove Nature Reserve and the Narvaez Bay.

Day 5: Visit the Saturna Island Vineyards or Vineyard 28 and enjoy a guided wine tasting tour.

Day 6: Kayak the rugged coastline of Saturna Island and explore the remote caves, coves, and waterfalls.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of Saturna Island or take a day trip to nearby Vancouver Island or Salt Spring Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Saturna Island

Saturna Island offers a diverse selection of local food and drinks that reflect the island’s unique environment and culture. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages on the island include:

Must-Try Dishes

Fresh Seafood

Saturna Island is renowned for its fresh seafood, including salmon, crab, shellfish, and halibut. Visitors can enjoy these delicacies at local restaurants, such as the Saturna Café or the Wild Thyme Café.

Saternation Pizza

The Saternation Pizza is a local specialty that features a thin crust, homemade tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and unique toppings, such as smoked salmon, goat cheese, and wild mushrooms. Visitors can try this mouth-watering pizza at the Saturna Island General Store.

Local Beverages

Saturna Island Wines

Saturna Island is home to several award-winning wineries that produce unique and delicious wines, such as Pinot Noir, Ortega, and Siegerrebe, using locally grown grapes. Visitors can enjoy these wines at the Saturna Island Vineyards or Vineyard 28.

Saturna Island Beer

Saturna Island is also home to the Saturna Island Brewing Company, which produces refreshing and innovative craft beers, such as the Dark Island Ale and the East Point Pale Ale, using local spring water and hops.

Where to Stay in Saturna Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saturna Island offers several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the popular choices include:

Saturna Island Lodge

The Saturna Island Lodge is a luxurious and eco-friendly hotel that features comfortable rooms, breathtaking views of the ocean, and amenities such as a fitness center, a hot tub, and a library. The Lodge also offers guided hiking and kayaking tours.

Saturna Island Campground

The Saturna Island Campground is a rustic and scenic campground that offers tent and RV sites, shower facilities, and picnic tables. The campground also features direct beach access, fire pits, and a playground.

Breezy Bay Bed and Breakfast

The Breezy Bay Bed and Breakfast is a cozy and peaceful guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms, private decks, and stunning views of the ocean. The guesthouse also serves homemade breakfast and provides access to hiking and biking trails.

What to Buy in Saturna Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saturna Island offers several unique souvenirs and local products that reflect the island’s culture and traditions, including:

Pottery

Saturna Island is home to several talented potters who create beautiful and functional pottery using local clays and glazes. Visitors can find an excellent selection of pottery pieces, such as mugs, bowls, and vases, at local galleries, such as the Saturna Island Pottery.

Artwork

Saturna Island is also home to several talented artists who create stunning works of art, such as paintings, sculptures, and jewelry, using locally sourced materials. Visitors can find an array of artwork at local galleries, such as the Saturna Island Art Gallery.

Local Culture and Customs of Saturna Island

Saturna Island is home to a rich cultural and historical heritage that reflects its diverse and unique environment and population. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

First Nations History

Saturna Island is the traditional territory of the Tsawout First Nation, who continue to maintain their cultural traditions and way of life in the area. Visitors can learn about the First Nations’ history, culture, and art at local cultural centers, such as the First Nations Cultural Center in the nearby Pender Island.

Lighthouse History

Saturna Island is also home to several historical lighthouses that date back to the early 20th century and played a crucial role in the island’s maritime history. Visitors can learn about the lighthouse keepers’ lives and understand the importance of lighthouses at local museums, such as the East Point Lighthouse Park.

Best Time to Visit Saturna Island

The best time to visit Saturna Island is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island is buzzing with events and activities. The fall season, from September to November, is also a great time to visit, as the crowds thin out, the foliage turns colorful, and the whales return for their winter migration.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saturna Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife and follow the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Learn about the local culture and history and show respect for the First Nations people and their traditions.
  • Visit the local wineries and breweries and enjoy the locally produced food and drinks.
  • Take part in activities and tours that support the local economy and promote sustainable tourism.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or feed the wildlife, such as the seals, sea lions, orca, and eagles.
  • Remove or damage natural or cultural features, such as rocks, shells, or artifacts.
  • Litter or leave garbage in the wilderness or on the beaches.
  • Trespass on private property or restricted areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saturna Island

Saturna Island is a relatively safe and peaceful destination, but visitors should take its remoteness and natural hazards into consideration, including:

  • Sudden weather changes, such as strong winds, rain, or fog.
  • Undertaking outdoor activities without proper training, gear, or guidance.
  • Swimming or kayaking in strong currents or cold water.
  • Contacting wildlife without proper precautions.

Visitors are advised to carry a first-aid kit, a communication device, spare clothing, and water while venturing into remote areas.

Health in Saturna Island

Saturna Island has adequate medical facilities, including a health clinic and an ambulance service, but visitors should carry their prescription medications and health insurance information, as well as take necessary precautions to prevent illnesses, such as:

  • Drinking potable water and avoiding untreated water sources.
  • Avoiding contact with sick or injured animals.
  • Using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing to avoid tick-borne diseases.

Getting to Saturna Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saturna Island is accessible by several transportation modes, including:

Airports Near Saturna Island

The nearest international airport is the Vancouver International Airport (YVR), which serves several domestic and international flights. Visitors can also use the Victoria International Airport (YYJ), which serves several domestic and international flights and has connections to the Vancouver Island region.

Bus Stations Near Saturna Island

Visitors can take a bus from Vancouver to Tsawwassen Ferry Terminal or Victoria Bus Terminal and then board a BC Ferries vessel to Saturna Island. The BC Ferries operate several daily sailings from the terminals and take around 2-3 hours to reach Saturna Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also use private or chartered boats, seaplanes or car rentals to reach Saturna Island. The island has several docking facilities, marinas, and boat launches that welcome visitors and provide necessary amenities, such as fuel, water, and waste disposal.

How to Get Around in Saturna Island

Saturna Island is a relatively small island, and visitors can explore most of its attractions by foot, bike, or car. The island has several car rental and bike rental options, as well as a public transit system that operates several times a day and covers the island’s main roads and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Saturna Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saturna Island is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding islands and regions, such as:

Vancouver Island

Vancouver Island is a large island located just north of Saturna Island and offers endless opportunities for adventure, including hiking, kayaking, surfing, and whale watching. The island also features several historical towns, such as Victoria and Tofino, and natural attractions, such as the Pacific Rim National Park.

Gulf Islands

The Gulf Islands offer a unique set of experiences that blend natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor recreation. The islands are connected by BC Ferries and offer endless opportunities for hiking, biking, paddling, and wildlife watching.

Victoria

Victoria is the capital city of British Columbia and is located on the southern tip of Vancouver Island. Victoria offers a mix of urban sophistication, historical charm, and natural beauty, including attractions such as the Butchart Gardens, the Royal BC Museum, and the Fisherman’s Wharf.

Conclusion

Saturna Island offers a unique and authentic Canadian travel experience that blends natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. Visitors can explore the island’s natural wonders, such as Mount Warburton Pike and the Gulf Islands National Park Reserve, visit the cultural landmarks, such as the East Point Lighthouse Park and the Saturna Island Museum, taste the local food and drinks, such as the Saternation Pizza and the Saturna Island wines, and meet the friendly locals who call Saturna Island home. Plan your stay on Saturna Island, Canada, and immerse yourself in the Pacific Northwest’s natural wonders.
