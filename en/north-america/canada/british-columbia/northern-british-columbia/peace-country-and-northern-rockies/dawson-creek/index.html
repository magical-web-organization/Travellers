Introduction to Dawson Creek

Nestled in the heart of Peace Country and Northern Rockies, in Northern British Columbia, Canada, lies the welcoming town of Dawson Creek. Also known as the “Mile Zero City”, it is the starting point of the Alaska Highway, which connects British Columbia to Alaska. Though Dawson Creek is a small town, it is packed with picturesque landscapes and numerous outdoor activities that attract visitors from all over the world.

If you are planning on visiting British Columbia, Canada, don’t miss out on exploring the beautiful town of Dawson Creek. Our comprehensive travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Dawson Creek

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dawson Creek is steeped in history and has numerous museums and historical sites that offer visitors a glimpse into the town’s intriguing past. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Alaska Highway House Museum: One of the most popular museums in Dawson Creek, this interactive site features exhibits that depict the struggles and triumphs of the construction of the Alaska Highway.
  • Walter Wright Pioneer Village: A living museum, this attraction provides an experience of how early settlers lived in the Dawson Creek area. Visitors can learn about traditional farming practices, pioneer life and even participate in special events and fairs.
  • NAR Park: This park is the site where the first wooden bridge in Dawson Creek was built over the Dawson Creek River. It also contains preserved railway equipment, including a steam locomotive and caboose.

Natural Attractions of Dawson Creek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dawson Creek is undoubtedly a nature lover’s paradise, with many outdoor activities and scenic views to explore. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions of Dawson Creek:

  • Bear Mountain Wind Park: Located approximately 10 kilometers south of Dawson Creek, the wind park offers a fantastic panoramic view of the town and the surrounding hills.
  • Kiskatinaw River Provincial Park: This picturesque park features the longest wooden curved bridge in North America, a breathtaking view of the Kiskatinaw River, and excellent hiking trails.
  • Dawson Creek Rotary Trail: Perfect for an early morning stroll or an evening walk, this trail offers a scenic view of the town’s landscapes and natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dawson Creek

The town of Dawson Creek is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with miles of hiking and biking trails throughout the area. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Pouce Coupe Tumbler Ridge Trail: A challenging route that will take you on a 64 km trail between the towns of Pouce Coupe and Tumbler Ridge. The trail is suitable for experienced hikers and bikers; it is not recommended for beginners.
  • Bear Mountain: Located south of Dawson Creek, this trail offers both a pleasant hiking experience and a panoramic view of the town. It is ideal for families with kids of all ages.
  • Kiskatinaw River Provincial Park Trail: One of the most scenic trails in the area, this route is perfect for various activities such as hiking, biking, and running. It’s an excellent option for family outings.

Best Things to Do in Dawson Creek: Activities and Tours

The town of Dawson Creek is home to several exciting adventures and tours that will keep visitors entertained throughout their stay. Here are some top things to do in Dawson Creek:

  • Dawson Creek Golf and Country Club: Located in the picturesque Peace River valley, the golf club offers an excellent golfing experience. It is the perfect place to spend a leisurely day, practicing your game.
  • Dawson Creek Farmer’s Market: Taking place every Saturday, this farmer’s market connects visitors with the town’s vibrant cultural community and showcases locally grown produce and handmade artisanal products.
  • High-Adventure ATV Tours: Explore the surrounding nature on a thrilling ATV ride with High-Adventure ATV Tours. Their knowledgeable guides will take you through winding trails and steep hills offering a unique and thrilling experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dawson Creek

Day 1: Explore the city’s rich history by visiting the Alaska Highway House Museum and the Walter Wright Pioneer Village.

Day 2: Head to Bear Mountain Wind Park to enjoy a panoramic view of the town and the surrounding hills.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring the magnificent Kiskatinaw River Provincial Park.

Day 4: Take a scenic tour of the town by bike or hike on the Kiskatinaw River Provincial Park Trail.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Dawson Creek Golf and Country Club.

Day 6: Enjoy a thrilling ATV ride with High-Adventure ATV Tours.

Day 7: Take in the local culture at the Dawson Creek Farmer’s market.

Food and Drink Scene of Dawson Creek

Whether you are a foodie or not, you must try the local dishes and beverages in Dawson Creek. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bannock: A traditional Indigenous dish made of flour, salt, and baking powder, and deep-fried in oil.
  • Poutine: A Canadian dish consisting of fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Smoked Salmon: Freshly caught Pacific salmon that is smoked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Dawson Creek Ale: A locally crafted beer that bears the name of the town, it is brewed at the Bear Mountain Brewing Company.
  • Moosemeat Wine: A unique locally produced wine made from fermented moose meat.

Where to Stay in Dawson Creek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dawson Creek offers several options for accommodation, catering to every budget and preference. From luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses, here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Stonebridge Hotel: A luxurious hotel with amenities such as a fitness center, pool, and restaurant.
  • Bear Mountain Motel: A budget-friendly motel with comfortable rooms and picturesque views of Bear Mountain.
  • Pomeroy Inn and Suites: A trendy hotel, perfect for families, couples, or business travelers, with high-speed internet and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Dawson Creek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Dawson Creek, don’t forget to pick up some locally made souvenirs and products as a memento of your trip. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Birch syrup: A sweet and flavorful syrup extracted from the birch tree; it’s a classic Canadian product.
  • Indigenous Artwork: A range of beautiful handmade pieces, from pottery to carvings, showcasing the local indigenous culture.
  • Bear Mountain Hot Sauce: A spicy and tangy locally made hot sauce that’s a perfect pairing with any meal.

Local Culture and Customs of Dawson Creek

The town prides itself on celebrating and showcasing its unique cultural heritage. The local indigenous communities have significantly influenced the town’s culture and customs, and many events and festivals celebrate their tradition and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Dawson Creek

The perfect time to visit Dawson Creek is between May and October, as the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dawson Creek

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately with suitable clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.
  • Take advantage of the local farmer’s markets and locally sourced cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural surroundings, and respect the environment.
  • Ignore the wildlife. Dawson Creek is home to many animals, such as bears and cougars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dawson Creek

Dawson Creek is generally a safe town for tourists and visitors. However, it is essential to take precautions and follow safety measures during outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

Health in Dawson Creek

Dawson Creek has several medical facilities that provide quality medical care to residents and visitors alike. Visitors can find medical assistance at the Dawson Creek and District Hospital, which has a 24-hour emergency department.

Getting to Dawson Creek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Dawson Creek is easy, with several transportation options available. Here are some ways to reach Dawson Creek:

Airports Near Dawson Creek

  • Dawson Creek Airport: Located within the town, the airport is suitable for private planes and small jets.
  • Fort St. John Airport: Located approximately 50 km away from Dawson Creek, the airport is the nearest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Dawson Creek

  • Greyhound Canada: The bus service connects Dawson Creek to other cities in British Columbia and Alberta.

Other Options

  • Driving: Dawson Creek is located on the junction of highways 2 and 97 and is accessible from major cities in British Columbia and Alberta.

How to Get Around in Dawson Creek

Getting around Dawson Creek is hassle-free, with several transportation options available. Here are some ways to get around the town:

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the town at your pace.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout the town.
  • Buses: The town has a robust bus transportation system that connects all parts of the town.

Where to Go Next from Dawson Creek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dawson Creek is a fantastic starting point for exploring the Peace Country and Northern Rockies, with many nearby destinations that make for great day trips. Here are some of the top destinations to visit:

  • Tumbler Ridge: Located approximately two hours from Dawson Creek, Tumbler Ridge is a picturesque town known for its hiking trails and waterfalls.
  • Fort St. John: Fort St. John is the nearest city, approximately one hour from Dawson Creek. It is a vibrant hub that offers visitors a variety of attractions, including a museum and art galleries.
  • Hudson’s Hope: A small town approximately two hours from Dawson Creek, Hudson’s Hope is famous for its hydroelectric dam and its beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Dawson Creek is a hidden gem in Northern British Columbia, offering visitors stunning natural landscapes, outdoor adventures, rich history, and friendly locals. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan the perfect trip to Dawson Creek, discover its hidden treasures, and have an unforgettable experience.
