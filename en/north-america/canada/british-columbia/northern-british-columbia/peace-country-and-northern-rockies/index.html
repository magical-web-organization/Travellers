Peace Country and Northern Rockies Travel Guide

If you’re looking for a unique and memorable travel experience in Canada, then Peace Country and Northern Rockies are the perfect destinations. Located in Northern British Columbia, these regions offer spectacular scenery, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Peace Country and Northern Rockies.

Introduction to Peace Country and Northern Rockies

Peace Country and Northern Rockies are located in Northern British Columbia, a vast and sparsely populated region known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty. The area is home to a diverse range of communities, including Indigenous peoples and settlers of European, Asian, and Scandinavian descent. The region is characterized by its rugged terrain, pristine lakes, rushing rivers, and unspoiled wilderness areas, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Peace Country and Northern Rockies

There are many attractions to visit in Peace Country and Northern Rockies. Here are some of the must-see destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hudson’s Hope Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Hudson’s Hope and the surrounding Peace River area.
  • Moberly Lake Provincial Park: This park features an interpretive center highlighting the area’s Indigenous history and culture.
  • Kiskatinaw Bridge: This historic wooden bridge is one of the last of its kind in North America and is a National Historic Site.
  • Dawson Creek Art Gallery: This gallery features works from local artists as well as rotating exhibits from across Canada.

Natural Attractions of Peace Country and Northern Rockies: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muncho Lake: Known for its clear blue waters, this lake is a popular spot for fishing, canoeing, and hiking.
  • W.A.C. Bennett Dam: This impressive dam is over 183 meters high and provides hydroelectric power to the region. Visitors can take guided tours of the facility.
  • Stone Mountain Provincial Park: This park offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain ranges, as well as hiking and camping opportunities.
  • Tumbler Ridge Geopark: This geopark is home to unique rock formations, waterfalls, and fossils dating back millions of years.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peace Country and Northern Rockies

Peace Country and Northern Rockies offer endless opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Beatton River: This scenic trail follows the Beatton River and offers excellent opportunities for bird-watching.
  • Monkman Cascades: This challenging hike takes you through a rugged and remote area with stunning views of waterfalls and canyons.
  • Willow River Trails: These easy trails are perfect for families, offering beautiful views of the river and surrounding nature.
  • Hudson’s Hope Town Trails: These trails offer a unique look at the history of Hudson’s Hope and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Peace Country and Northern Rockies: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Peace Country and Northern Rockies. Here are some of the best options:

  • Wildlife Tours: Take a guided tour to see local wildlife, including bears, moose, and elk.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: Rent a canoe or kayak to explore the area’s lakes and rivers.
  • Fishing: Peace Country and Northern Rockies offer excellent opportunities for fishing, including trout, salmon, and sturgeon.
  • Helicopter Tours: Take a helicopter tour for a unique and thrilling perspective on the area’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Peace Country and Northern Rockies

Peace Country and Northern Rockies offer a unique culinary scene, with a focus on local and regional ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison burgers: Bison is a popular local meat and is often served in burger form.
  • Wild berry desserts: The area is home to a variety of wild berries, which are often used in desserts and jams.
  • Salmon: Fresh salmon is a local favorite and is often served grilled or smoked.

Local Beverages

  • British Columbia wine: The region is home to many wineries that produce high-quality wines.
  • Craft beer: The area has several microbreweries that produce unique and flavorful beers.
  • Berry teas: Local berries are often used to make herbal teas, which are a refreshing and healthy beverage option.

Where to Stay in Peace Country and Northern Rockies: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Peace Country and Northern Rockies, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel. Here are some options:

  • Pomeroy Inn & Suites: This hotel offers modern and comfortable rooms and is located in Fort St. John.
  • Mighty Peace Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in Clear Hills and offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Canadian Motor Inn: This budget-friendly option is located in Dawson Creek and offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Peace Country and Northern Rockies: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, there are many unique local products to choose from. Here are some ideas:

  • Handmade crafts: Local artisans produce a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Wild berry jams: Wild berries are a popular local ingredient and are often used to make delicious jams and preserves.
  • Local wine and beer: Take home a bottle of the region’s best wine or beer as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Peace Country and Northern Rockies

The area’s culture is a unique blend of Indigenous and settler heritage, with a focus on preserving and celebrating local history and traditions. Some things to keep in mind when visiting include:

  • Respect for Indigenous culture: Many Indigenous communities live in the area, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect their culture and customs.
  • Conservation and sustainability: The region’s natural beauty and resources are highly valued, so visitors are encouraged to leave a minimal impact on the environment.

Best Time to Visit Peace Country and Northern Rockies

The best time to visit Peace Country and Northern Rockies is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the area is beautiful year-round, and each season offers its own unique attractions and experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peace Country and Northern Rockies

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Peace Country and Northern Rockies:

Do:

  • Explore the area’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Learn about local history and culture.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Indigenous culture or traditional lands.
  • Leave a negative impact on the environment.
  • Engage in unsafe outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peace Country and Northern Rockies

While the region is generally safe, visitors should be aware of the following safety concerns:

  • Wildlife encounters: Visitors should take precautions to avoid encounters with bears and other wildlife, such as carrying bear spray and making noise while hiking.
  • River and lake safety: Visitors should be cautious when swimming, boating, or kayaking in the area’s rivers and lakes, as they can be cold and fast-moving.
  • Remote areas: Many of the area’s attractions are located in remote or wilderness areas, so visitors should take precautions and be prepared for emergencies.

Health in Peace Country and Northern Rockies

There are no specific health concerns for visitors to Peace Country and Northern Rockies. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as wearing sunscreen, staying hydrated, and avoiding ticks.

Getting to Peace Country and Northern Rockies: Transportation Options and Logistics

The region is primarily accessed by car or bus, as there are no major airports in the area. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Peace Country and Northern Rockies

  • Fort St. John Airport: Located in Fort St. John, this airport offers domestic flights within Canada.

Bus Stations Near Peace Country and Northern Rockies

  • Dawson Creek Bus Depot: This bus station offers bus service to several communities in the area.

Other Options

  • Driving: Many visitors choose to rent a car and self-drive to Peace Country and Northern Rockies.
  • Tours: Several tour companies offer guided tours of the region.

How to Get Around in Peace Country and Northern Rockies

The best way to get around Peace Country and Northern Rockies is by car. However, there are also several taxi companies and bus services available in the larger communities.

Where to Go Next from Peace Country and Northern Rockies: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Alaska Highway: This historic highway connects British Columbia and Alaska and offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Northern Rockies Lodge: This lodge is located on Muncho Lake and offers comfortable rooms, as well as fishing and boating opportunities.
  • Banff National Park: Located in neighboring Alberta, Banff National Park is a must-see destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Peace Country and Northern Rockies offer a unique and memorable travel experience, with stunning scenery, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for hiking, fishing, or cultural exploration, this region has something for everyone. So start planning your trip today and get ready to experience the natural beauty and warm hospitality of Peace Country and Northern Rockies.
