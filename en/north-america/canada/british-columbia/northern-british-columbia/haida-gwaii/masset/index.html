Introduction to Masset

Nestled in the heart of Haida Gwaii, Masset is a charming town in Northern British Columbia, Canada. With its stunning natural beauty and fascinating culture, Masset is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Canadian experience.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Masset, highlighting the top attractions, best places to stay and eat, and offering insider tips for making the most of your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Masset

Whether you’re interested in immersing yourself in the local culture, or exploring the natural beauty of the region, Masset has something to offer. Here are the top places to visit in Masset:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haida Heritage Centre: This must-visit attraction offers visitors a glimpse into the rich culture and heritage of the Haida people. The center features a museum, carving shed, gift shop, and various outdoor exhibits.
  • Masset Museum: This small but fascinating museum showcases the history and culture of Masset and its surrounding area, with exhibits on local industry, marine life, and First Nations culture.
  • Old Massett Village: This quaint village is home to several totem poles and longhouses, highlighting the art and culture of the Haida people.

Natural Attractions of Masset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Naikoon Provincial Park: This massive park offers visitors a diverse mix of outdoor recreation, from wilderness camping and hiking to beachcombing and surfing. Famous for its long sandy beaches and impressive sand dunes, Naikoon is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Tow Hill: This towering volcanic rock formation offers visitors unparalleled views of the surrounding landscape, including the nearby ocean and Haida Gwaii’s lush green forests.
  • Balance Rock: Another incredible natural site, Balance Rock is a massive boulder balanced precariously on a small rock pedestal, shaped by the elements over thousands of years.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Masset

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Masset is on foot or by bike. Here are our top hiking and biking routes in Masset:

  • Pesuta Shipwreck Trail: This scenic hiking trail takes you to the remains of a 90-year-old shipwreck stranded on the beach, offering a unique glimpse into the region’s maritime history.
  • North Beach Trail: This 14 km scenic trail takes you through lush forests and along the beautiful beaches of Naikoon Provincial Park.
  • Golden Spruce Trail: This moderate hike leads through the lush forests of Yakoun River Valley to the site of the famous Golden Spruce tree, which was tragically cut down in 1997.

Best Things to Do in Masset: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Masset has something for everyone. Here are the best things to do in Masset:

  • Fishing: Masset is renowned for its thriving fishing industry, and visitors can join local guides for a day out on the water to catch salmon, halibut, and other fish.
  • Surfing: With its long, wide beaches and consistent waves, Masset is one of the best surfing destinations in Canada, especially for beginners.
  • Kayaking: Take to the waters of Masset Inlet and explore the region’s diverse wildlife, including eagles, whales, bear, and seals, all from the safety of a kayak.
  • Beachcombing: With miles of pristine beaches to explore, beachcombing is a popular pastime in Masset, with treasures such as driftwood and shells to be found.

Week-Long Itinerary for Masset

To experience the best of Masset and its surrounding area, we recommend spending at least a week here. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Haida Heritage Centre and Masset Museum
2 Hike the Golden Spruce Trail
3 Spend the day fishing or surfing
4 Visit Old Massett Village and Balance Rock
5 Kayak tour of Masset Inlet
6 Hike and picnic at Tow Hill
7 Beach day at Agate Beach and North Beach Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Masset

Masset may be small, but it has a surprisingly diverse dining scene, with options ranging from seafood shacks to cafes and breweries. Here are our top recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked salmon: A Masset specialty, smoked salmon is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Haida bannock: A traditional First Nations bread made with flour, water, and yeast, served warm with butter or jam.
  • Fish and chips: Whether enjoyed at a local seafood shack or fine restaurant, you can’t go wrong with this classic dish in Masset.

Local Beverages

  • Haida Wild Beer: Brewed with local ingredients by the Haida Gwaii Brewing Company, this refreshing beer is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Island Spirit Distillery: Sample the handcrafted gins and vodkas produced by this local distillery, located just outside of Masset.

Where to Stay in Masset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Masset offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Northern Shores Lodge: Located in the heart of Masset, this rustic lodge is the perfect base for exploring the region. Featuring comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving locally sourced seafood, it’s a great option for couples or families.
  • Haida House at Tllaal: Nestled in the heart of the forest, Haida House at Tllaal offers guests a chance to experience the traditional Haida longhouse style of accommodation, featuring beautiful cedar architecture and hand-carved totem poles.
  • RV and Tenting: Naikoon Provincial Park offers visitors the chance to camp in the great outdoors, with a range of campsites suitable for tents and RVs.

What to Buy in Masset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Masset offers a range of unique souvenirs and locally crafted products, many of which feature the distinctive artwork of the Haida people. Here are some of the best things to buy in Masset:

  • Haida artwork: From intricate carvings to stunning paintings and prints, Haida artwork makes for a beautiful and meaningful souvenir to bring home.
  • Wild salmon: Visitors can purchase smoked salmon to bring home with them, a mouth-watering reminder of their time in Masset.
  • Handmade crafts: Look for locally crafted jewelry, pottery, and textiles, all made using traditional methods and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Masset

Masset is located in the heart of Haida Gwaii, a region rich in the culture and heritage of the Haida people. Visitors to Masset should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, including the importance of totem poles and longhouses in the Haida culture.

Best Time to Visit Masset

The best time to visit Masset is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and the region’s natural beauty is at its most spectacular. You can also visit in the shoulder seasons, when you’ll benefit from fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Masset

Here are our top do’s and don’ts of visiting Masset:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture: Take the time to learn about the culture and customs of the Haida people, and be respectful of their heritage.
  • Pack for all weather: The weather in Masset can be unpredictable, so be sure to bring warm clothing and rain gear.
  • Try the seafood: Masset is renowned for its seafood, so be sure to try some fresh local fish and shellfish while you’re here.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife: Masset is home to a diverse range of wildlife, from bear and deer to whales and seals. Always keep a safe distance and avoid disturbing the animals.
  • Litter: Help keep Masset clean and beautiful by disposing of trash properly and respecting the environment.

Safety and Health

Masset is a safe and welcoming destination for travelers, but it’s always best to take precautions to stay healthy and safe while on your trip.

Safety in Masset

  • Wildlife safety: Be bear aware and follow park regulations when in areas known to have bears or other wildlife.
  • Water safety: Always be aware of the tides and currents when swimming or kayaking. If in doubt, consult with local guides and outfitters.

Health in Masset

  • Medical services: Masset has a small hospital and medical clinic, but visitors should bring any necessary medical supplies and medication with them.

Getting to Masset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Masset is located on Haida Gwaii, which is accessible by air or sea. Here are the different transportation options for getting to Masset:

  • By air: Daily flights connect Masset to Vancouver International Airport, with a flight time of around two hours.
  • By ferry: BC Ferries operates a ferry service between Prince Rupert and Skidegate Landing, with a journey time of around seven hours.

Airports Near Masset

  • Masset Regional Airport: Located just outside of town, this airport offers daily connections to Vancouver International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Masset

There are no bus stations in Masset, but several companies operate shuttle services from the airport to the town.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Visitors can rent a car from the airport or one of the rental agencies in town to explore the region at their own pace.
  • Bicycle rentals: For those looking to explore Masset by bike, rentals are available from several local shops and outfitters.

How to Get Around in Masset

Masset and its surrounding area are easily explored on foot, by bike, or by car. In town, a rental car or bicycle offers the most flexibility, while hiking trails and beaches can be accessed on foot.

Where to Go Next from Masset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Masset is just one of many highlights to be discovered on Haida Gwaii, known as the Galapagos of the North. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions for visitors:

  • Queen Charlotte: A charming coastal village, Queen Charlotte is home to several art galleries and boutiques, and offers easy access to several hiking trails and nearby islands.
  • Gwaii Haanas National Park: A remote wilderness park accessible only by boat or plane, this park is home to diverse plant and animal life, ancient Haida villages, and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Masset offers travelers a unique and authentic Canadian experience, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and diverse range of activities. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip to Masset and discover all that this Northern British Columbia gem has to offer.
