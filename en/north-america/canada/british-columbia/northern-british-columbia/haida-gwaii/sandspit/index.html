Introduction to Sandspit

Sandspit, located on the eastern shore of Moresby Island in the Queen Charlotte Islands of British Columbia, Canada, is a hidden gem off the beaten path. The town is surrounded by the lush green forest and the Pacific Ocean, making it a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts.

Sandspit is part of Haida Gwaii, an archipelago of more than 150 islands resident to the Haida people. Filled with cultural landmarks, amazing wildlife, textiles, and stunning natural beauty, Sandspit, and Haida Gwaii in general, is a destination that every traveler should experience at least once in their life.

List of Places to Visit in Sandspit

Sandspit has a lot to offer, from cultural landmarks to stunning natural beauty. Below is a list of places to visit in Sandspit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haida Heritage Centre at Ḵay Llnagaay - Experience the rich culture of Haida Gwaii from its people and the environment at the Haida Heritage Centre at Ḵay Llnagaay. The heritage centre features contemporary Haida art, traditional weaving, art studios, and much more.

Natural Attractions of Sandspit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Naikoon Provincial Park - Experience the rugged and untamed beauty of Haida Gwaii at Naikoon Provincial Park. The park, which covers an area of 72,500 hectares, features stunning beaches, sand dunes, and forests. Take in the stunning views of the North Beach, explore the blowhole, and spot the local wildlife.

  • Delkatla Nature Sanctuary - Located near Masset, Delkatla Nature Sanctuary is a must-visit for birdwatchers. The sanctuary, which covers an area of 130 hectares, features a mix of wetlands, forests, and freshwater ponds. The sanctuary is home to over 140 species of birds, including trumpeter swans, great blue herons, bald eagles, and northern harriers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandspit

Sandspit is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking opportunities to explore. Below are some of the most popular routes:

  • Tlell River Trail - Located near Tlell, the Tlell River Trail is a 2-kilometer trail that offers stunning views of the forest and the river. The trail is perfect for hiking and biking, and it is accessible year-round.

  • Pesuta Shipwreck Trail - Located in Naikoon Provincial Park, the Pesuta Shipwreck Trail is a 9-kilometer trail that leads to the wreckage of the Pesuta, a 1918 steamship that was abandoned in 1928. The trail features stunning views of the forest and the ocean, and it is accessible from May to September.

Best Things to Do in Sandspit: Activities and Tours

Sandspit is a destination that has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Sandspit:

  • Fishing - Sandspit is home to some of the best salmon and halibut fishing spots in the world. Book a guided fishing tour and experience the thrill of catching your dinner.

  • Whale Watching - Haida Gwaii is home to a vast array of marine life, including humpback whales, orcas, porpoises, and sea lions. Book a guided whale watching tour and experience the thrill of seeing these majestic animals up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sandspit

If you plan to spend a week or more in Sandspit, here is a sample itinerary:

Day 1-2 Visit the Haida Heritage Centre at Ḵay Llnagaay, explore Masset, hike Tlell River Trail
Day 3-4 Visit Naikoon Provincial Park, hike/beachwalk Agate and North Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Sandspit

Sandspit has a modest culinary scene, focusing mainly on seafood. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Freshly Caught Salmon - Salmon is a staple food in Sandspit, and there is no better way to experience it than freshly cooked. You can catch your own salmon or enjoy it at one of the local restaurants.

  • Pacific Halibut - Another local seafood delicacy that you can enjoy is Pacific halibut. Freshly cooked halibut is often served with rice or potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Haida Gwaii Brewery and Tasting Room - Haida Gwaii Brewery and Tasting Room is the only brewery on Haida Gwaii. The brewery produces a variety of craft beer and cider made with local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Sandspit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sandspit offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sandspit:

  • Sandspit Inn - Sandspit Inn offers comfortable and affordable accommodation in the heart of Sandspit. The Inn features clean rooms with free Wi-Fi, cable TV, and continental breakfast.

  • Northern Shores Lodge - Northern Shores Lodge is a luxury wilderness resort located on the shores of the Naikoon National Park. The lodge offers cozy cabins with ocean views, gourmet meals, and access to outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Sandspit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sandspit is a great place to buy unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Haida Art - Haida art is renowned worldwide for its intricate designs and use of natural materials. You can buy Haida art from local galleries and shops.

  • Locally Made Jams and Honey - Sandspit is home to many small-scale agricultural producers who make delicious jams and honey. You can buy these products at local farmers’ markets or specialty stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Sandspit

Sandspit is part of Haida Gwaii, which has a rich cultural heritage. The Haida people have long-standing traditions and customs that should be respected by visitors, such as asking permission before taking photographs or entering private land.

Best Time to Visit Sandspit

The best time to visit Sandspit is from May to September when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Visitors can also experience the Haida culture and attend festivals and events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandspit

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Sandspit:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Take part in outdoor activities, but be mindful of wildlife and their habitat.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Take home any flora or fauna without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sandspit

Sandspit is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of the potential risks of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and whale watching. Visitors should take necessary precautions such as wearing appropriate clothing, carrying bear spray, and being mindful of their surroundings.

Health in Sandspit

Sandspit has limited medical facilities, so visitors should pack a first aid kit and any necessary medications. Visitors should also be aware of potential foodborne illnesses and practice good hygiene practices when handling food.

Getting to Sandspit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sandspit is located on Moresby Island in Haida Gwaii, which is accessible by air or sea.

Airports Near Sandspit

  • Sandspit Airport - Sandspit has its own airport, which is served by regional airlines such as Pacific Coastal Airlines and Air Canada.

  • Masset Airport - Masset Airport is located 39 kilometers north of Sandspit and is also served by Pacific Coastal Airlines.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Sandspit by ferry or by driving from the mainland. However, the journey can be longer and more complicated.

How to Get Around in Sandspit

Sandspit is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas can rent a car or book a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Sandspit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sandspit is a great base for exploring the other islands of Haida Gwaii. Visitors can take a ferry or a flight to explore nearby towns like Masset or Queen Charlotte City or take a boat trip to remote islands like Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve.

Conclusion

Sandspit is a hidden gem of Northern British Columbia. With its stunning natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities, Sandspit offers something for everyone. Visitors to Sandspit are sure to leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
