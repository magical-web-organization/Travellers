Introduction to Haida Gwaii

Located off the coast of Northern British Columbia, Haida Gwaii is a remote and stunning group of islands known for its natural beauty, wildlife, and rich cultural heritage of the Haida people. The islands are home to ancient forests, pristine beaches, and diverse wildlife such as whales, bears, eagles, and salmon. Visitors to Haida Gwaii can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Haida Gwaii

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haida Gwaii is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the Haida culture and history. Some of the top culturally important attractions to visit include:

  • Haida Heritage Centre & Museum: This museum features exhibitions and artifacts that showcase the history and cultural practices of the Haida people.
  • St. John Church: This historical church is a testament to the strong Christian influence on the Haida people, and it is a popular spot for weddings and other events.
  • Ninstints: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to ancient Haida totem poles and other artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Haida Gwaii: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Haida Gwaii is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. With so many beautiful natural attractions, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Gwaii Haanas National Park: This stunning park located at the southern tip of the archipelago offers everything from ancient rainforests to hot springs and tidal pools.
  • Naikoon Provincial Park: Visitors to this park can enjoy sandy beaches, towering sand dunes, and hiking trails through old-growth forests.
  • Tow Hill: This volcanic plug offers incredible panoramic views of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haida Gwaii

Hiking and biking are popular activities on Haida Gwaii. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Pesuta Shipwreck Trail: This hike takes visitors to the abandoned wreckage of a 347-foot logging schooner that ran aground in 1928.
  • Spirit Lake Trail: This 18-kilometer hike takes visitors through old-growth cedar forests and offers stunning views of Spirit Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Queen Charlotte Islands Trail: This multi-day cycling route takes visitors through six different communities on the islands, each with their unique charm and attractions.

Best Things to Do in Haida Gwaii: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other fun things to do in Haida Gwaii. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Whale watching: Visitors can embark on whale watching tours and see orcas, humpback whales, and minke whales up close.
  • Fishing: Haida Gwaii is known for its fantastic fishing opportunities, such as salmon and halibut fishing.
  • Art tours: Visitors can take guided tours of Haida Gwaii’s many art galleries and studios, which showcase the work of local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haida Gwaii

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Haida Gwaii:

  • Day 1: Explore Queen Charlotte, the largest community on the islands. Visit the Haida Heritage Centre & Museum and enjoy a delicious dinner at one of the local restaurants.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Gwaii Haanas National Park and explore the ancient rainforests, hot springs, and tidal pools.
  • Day 3: Go whale watching or fishing for the day.
  • Day 4: Take a guided art tour and visit local galleries and studios.
  • Day 5: Go hiking on the Spirit Lake Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch in the forest.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Ninstints to see the ancient totem poles and explore the beach.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beach and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of Haida Gwaii before departing on your journey home.

Food and Drink Scene of Haida Gwaii

Haida Gwaii is known for its delicious and fresh cuisine, with a strong focus on locally sourced ingredients. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haida-style salmon: A traditional dish featuring marinated and smoked salmon, served with a variety of sides.
  • Bannock: A fluffy and delicious bread that has been a staple of Indigenous diets for hundreds of years.
  • Dungeness crab: A popular seafood dish that is abundant in the waters surrounding Haida Gwaii.

Local Beverages

  • Haida Gwaii Brewing Company: This local brewery produces a variety of unique and delicious beers, including the popular Spruce Tip Ale.
  • North Beach Wines: This local winery produces a range of wines made with grapes grown in the northern climate of Haida Gwaii. Try the Pinot Noir or Gewurztraminer.

Where to Stay in Haida Gwaii: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haida Gwaii has a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Haida House: This luxurious lodge offers ocean views, cultural experiences, and delicious food.
  • Tlell River House: This cozy guesthouse is a great budget-friendly option and is located near many natural attractions.
  • HI-Masset hostel: This hostel offers affordable accommodations in the heart of the town of Masset.

What to Buy in Haida Gwaii: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Haida Gwaii include:

  • Haida art: The intricate and beautiful Haida art can be found in many galleries and shops on the islands.
  • Local honey: Haida Gwaii’s honey is known for its unique and delicious flavour.
  • Smoked salmon: Fresh and locally caught salmon is smoked and sold in many shops and markets on the islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Haida Gwaii

The Haida people have a rich and unique culture that is deeply connected to the natural world. Visitors to Haida Gwaii should respect and learn about the traditional customs and practices of the Haida people, such as the potlatch ceremony, which is a traditional feast and gift-giving ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Haida Gwaii

The best time to visit Haida Gwaii is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should also consider the spring and fall months, which offer unique experiences such as wildlife viewing and storm watching.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haida Gwaii

Do:

  • Respect the Haida culture and customs.
  • Take only photos and leave only footprints in natural areas.
  • Purchase locally made products and support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • litter or leave trash in natural areas.
  • Take artifacts or souvenirs from cultural or historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Haida Gwaii

Visitors to Haida Gwaii should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking, boating, and fishing. It is important to follow safety guidelines and regulations to ensure a safe trip.

Health in Haida Gwaii

There are no major health concerns in Haida Gwaii, but it is important to take precautions such as wearing sunscreen, staying hydrated, and avoiding contact with wildlife.

Getting to Haida Gwaii: Transportation Options and Logistics

Haida Gwaii is a remote destination, but it is still accessible by air and sea. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Haida Gwaii

  • Vancouver International Airport is the main airport for connections to Haida Gwaii.
  • Sandspit Airport and Masset Airport both offer regional flights to Haida Gwaii.

Bus Stations Near Haida Gwaii

  • The BC Transit system offers bus service from Prince Rupert and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • BC Ferries provides ferry service from Prince Rupert to Skidegate Landing.

How to Get Around in Haida Gwaii

The best way to get around Haida Gwaii is by car or bicycle, as public transportation is limited. Many natural attractions are located off the main roads, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Where to Go Next from Haida Gwaii: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Haida Gwaii can continue their adventure by exploring nearby destinations such as:

  • Prince Rupert: This coastal town is known for its fishing industry and is a gateway to other destinations in Northern British Columbia.
  • Vancouver: This vibrant city is located on the mainland and offers a wide range of attractions, from museums to shopping and dining.

Conclusion

Haida Gwaii is a unique and beautiful destination that offers something for everyone. With a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, this remote archipelago is definitely worth a visit.
