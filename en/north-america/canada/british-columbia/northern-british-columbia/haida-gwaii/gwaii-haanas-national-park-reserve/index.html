Introduction to Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve is a protected area located in Haida Gwaii, Northern British Columbia, Canada. It is a remote wilderness destination that boasts diverse flora and fauna, stunning landscapes, and a rich cultural history. The park is jointly managed by the Haida Nation and the Government of Canada, and is accessible by boat or floatplane only.

List of Places to Visit in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Haida people have a rich cultural history with well-preserved artwork, historic sites, and landmarks throughout the park. Some of the top culturally significant attractions include:

  1. The Haida Heritage Centre at Ḵay Llnagaay: A museum that showcases the Haida culture through its exhibits, programming, and stories.
  2. Sgang Gwaay (Ninstints): A UNESCO World Heritage Site that features the remains of a Haida village, including tall standing totem poles and longhouses.
  3. Tanu (T’aanuu Ḵingaay): Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring a village site that dates back to 1300 AD.

Natural Attractions of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a wide range of activities including:

  1. Kayaking: Paddle through the calm waters of the park and explore hidden coves and inlets.
  2. Hiking: Embark on one of the park’s several hiking trails, such as the Yatza, or the rugged route to the highest peak, SGang Gwaay.
  3. Wildlife Watching: Keep an eye out for the diverse range of wildlife, including bald eagles, black bears, otters, and whales.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

The park offers several hiking and biking trails that range in difficulty, offering something for everyone. Some popular routes are:

  1. Yatza: A challenging trail that takes hikers to a stunning alpine lake.
  2. Gwaii Haanas Trail: A four-day trail that takes trekkers through varied terrain and an opportunity to see cascading waterfalls and spectacular wildlife.
  3. Rose Harbour to Echo Bay: A relatively gentle hike through the forest and marsh of Haida Gwaii, passing by Haida village sites and narrow channels along the way.

Best Things to Do in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve: Activities and Tours

Take advantage of the many activities and tours that Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve has to offer, including:

  1. Kayaking Tours: Explore the coastal waters and tidal lagoons that surround Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve.
  2. Cultural Tours: Learn about the rich history of the Haida people through guided tours and interactive exhibits.
  3. Wildlife Tours: Spot the diverse wildlife that calls Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

Day 1: Arrive in Sandspit, BC, and take a floatplane to the Moresby Camp in the park.

Day 2: Explore Jasper Cove, a serene inlet with stunning views and nesting birds.

Day 3: Visit the historic village site of Tanu and walk through the forest to the shores of Burnaby Narrows.

Day 4-5: Paddle or hike further inland to Hotspring Island, home to one of the hottest natural sulfuric springs in BC, and spend the night at the rustic hot spring campsites.

Day 6: Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Site of SGang Gwaay, with its towering totem poles and longhouses.

Day 7: Take a boat ride back to Sandspit to connect with a flight to Vancouver.

Food and Drink Scene of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

Try some of these delicious local dishes when in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve:

  1. Smoked Salmon: This staple of west coast cuisine is typically smoked and served with bread and cream cheese.
  2. Halibut: Another popular fish, halibut is often pan-seared or grilled and served with a side of seasonal vegetables.
  3. Haida Wild Berries: A mix of berries including blueberries and cranberries is served as a refreshing dessert or breakfast.

Local Beverages

When in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve, be sure to try some of these locally brewed beverages:

  1. Haida Wild Tea: A mix of dried berries, herbs, and spruce tips, this tea has a sweet and earthy flavour.
  2. Haida Gwaii Ale: A refreshing beer made with wildflower honey, it’s the perfect drink for a warm summer evening.

Where to Stay in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is no shortage of places to stay in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve. Some of the most popular options include:

  1. Floating Lodges: A unique and sustainable option, floating lodges at Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve offer a comfortable stay with stunning waterfront views.
  2. Camping: Several campsites throughout the park offer visitors the chance to truly immerse themselves in the wilderness and soak up the natural beauty of the park.
  3. Hostels and Guesthouses: For those who prefer their creature comforts, hostels and guesthouses in nearby villages offer a variety of affordable accommodation options.

What to Buy in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve – here are some great souvenir options:

  1. Haida Art: From intricate carvings to woven baskets, handcrafted Haida art is a must-buy souvenir.
  2. Local Seafood: Bring home some of the freshest seafood you’ll ever taste, including smoked salmon and canned tuna.
  3. Books on Haida Culture and Heritage: Learn more about the rich history of the Haida people and their connection to the land and sea in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve.

Local Culture and Customs of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

Respect the Haida culture and heritage by following these local customs and traditions:

  1. Dress Appropriately: As a sign of respect, it’s best to dress modestly when visiting Haida heritage sites.
  2. Respect Wildlife: Follow park guidelines and regulations to ensure you don’t disturb the wildlife or their natural habitat.
  3. Take Nothing but Pictures, Leave Nothing but Footprints: Take only photographs and memories with you when you leave Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve and ensure you dispose of any waste responsibly.

Best Time to Visit Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

The best time to visit Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve is during the summer months of June to September, when the weather is warmest and driest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

Do:

  1. Follow Park Rules and Regulations: Respect the environment, wildlife, and culture of the park.
  2. Pack for All Weather: Bring clothing layers, rain gear, and appropriate footwear as weather conditions can change quickly.
  3. Hire a Guide: Hiking and kayaking through the park can be challenging – consider hiring a guide for a safe and educational experience.

Don’t:

  1. Take Unnecessary Risks: Stay on designated trails and don’t approach wildlife to ensure your safety.
  2. Remove Artifacts: Leave all artifacts where you find them to avoid damaging Haida cultural heritage.
  3. Bring Non-Native Species: To avoid introducing non-native species that could damage the park’s delicate ecosystems, avoid bringing plants, animals, or insects from outside the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

  1. Be Prepared for Emergencies: Have a basic first aid kit with you and know your location in case of emergency.
  2. Watch for Weather Changes: Weather can change quickly in the region, so be sure to check local forecasts before heading out.
  3. Keep Wildlife at a Distance: Maintain a safe distance from wildlife to avoid possible attacks.

Health in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

  1. Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during warmer months to avoid dehydration.
  2. Wear Appropriate Clothing: Dress in layers to protect yourself from unpredictable weather conditions.
  3. Apply Sunscreen: To avoid sunburn, apply sunscreen regularly, especially when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

  1. Vancouver: Vancouver International Airport is the main entry point to British Columbia and can be reached via several airlines.
  2. Sandspit: Sandspit Airport is the closest airport to Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve and is served by several regional airlines.

Bus Stations Near Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

There are no bus stations in the immediate vicinity of Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve. However, there are several car rental companies in Sandspit that can provide transportation.

Other Options

Boat travel is also an option to reach Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve. Several tour companies provide boat tours from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve

The park is not accessible by car, so visitors must explore the area by foot, kayak, or boat. Guided tours are also available.

Where to Go Next from Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Haida Heritage Centre at Ḵay Llnagaay: Located on Haida Gwaii, this cultural center features exhibits, art, and programming that honors the Haida people.
  2. Naikoon Provincial Park: A vast wilderness park featuring long, sandy beaches, dramatic dunes, and hiking trails through pristine rainforest.
  3. Prince Rupert: This port city on the mainland offers visitors the chance to explore the Skeena River estuary, fish for pacific salmon, or visit the North Coast Museum.

Conclusion

Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve is a UNESCO World Heritage site that offers visitors the chance to immerse themselves in the rich culture and natural beauty of the region. Whether it’s exploring the rugged coastline by kayak or hiking through ancient forests, the park has something for everyone. Be sure to pack your sense of adventure and discover all that Gwaii Haanas National Park Reserve has to offer.
