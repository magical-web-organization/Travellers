Daajing Giids-Skidegate is a small Haida community located on the west side of the Queen Charlotte Islands, also known as Haida Gwaii. The island is located on the northern coast of British Columbia, Canada, and is known for its natural beauty, unique culture, and rich history. Daajing Giids-Skidegate, which is the ancestral home of the Haida people, is one of the best places to experience the authentic Haida culture.

Introduction to Daajing Giids-Skidegate

Daajing Giids-Skidegate is a picturesque community located on the edge of the Pacific Ocean. It is a place where visitors can experience the Haida culture, art, and traditional cuisine. The community is home to several museums and art galleries that showcase the history and culture of the Haida people. Visitors can also explore the beautiful wilderness and enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, kayaking, and fishing.

List of Places to Visit in Daajing Giids-Skidegate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haida Heritage Centre and Museum: a cultural center that showcases the art and history of the Haida people, featuring a totem pole village, longhouse, carving shed, and galleries that highlight the Haida Gwaii.
  • Balance Rock: a cultural monument that has been standing for over 200 years, and is said to be balancing on a stone beneath the surface of the hill it stands on. An important place for the Haida people, who used it as a marker for navigation.
  • K ay Llnagaay, Gaay Museum: a museum that features traditional Haida artefacts, contemporary art, and temporary exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Daajing Giids-Skidegate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • S G ang Gwaii: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and National Historic Site that is home to historic Haida village ruins and totems.
  • Skidegate Inlet: a stunning body of water that offers visitors the chance to go kayaking and spot various aquatic wildlife, including whales and sea lions.
  • Yakoun Lake: a serene lake where visitors can do some fishing and take in the surrounding natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Daajing Giids-Skidegate

  • Tow Hill Trail: a moderate hiking trail that offers scenic views of the coast and mountains, and takes visitors to the top of Tow Hill.
  • Pesuta Shipwreck Trail: a hiking trail that leads to the site of a historic shipwreck, offering visitors the chance to learn about Haida culture and history.
  • Spirit Lake Trail: a hiking trail that takes visitors through lush forests and over wooden bridges, offering stunning views of Spirit Lake and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Daajing Giids-Skidegate: Activities and Tours

  • Cultural Tours: visitors can take guided tours that showcase the Haida art, history, and culture.
  • Fishing: Visitors can explore the surrounding waters and do some fishing, with the chance to catch salmon, halibut, and more.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can go kayaking and explore the stunning coastline of Haida Gwaii.
  • Whale Watching: Visitors can take tours that take them to the areas where orcas, humpbacks, and other marine wildlife can be seen.

Week-Long Itinerary for Daajing Giids-Skidegate

Day 1: Haida Heritage Centre and Museum Day 2: Skidegate Inlet kayaking tour Day 3: S G ang Gwaii (Haida village ruins and totems) Day 4: Balance Rock Day 5: Fishing Trip Day 6: Pesuta Shipwreck Trail hike and nearby beach visit Day 7: Yakoun Lake and Tow Hill Trail hike

Food and Drink Scene of Daajing Giids-Skidegate

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Bannock bread: Bannock bread, a traditional Indigenous bread, is fried and often served with jam or honey.
  • Smoked salmon: Haida people rely on salmon as a traditional food source, and it is often smoked and served as a snack or meal.
  • Fiddlehead ferns: a traditional Haida food that is often pickled, sautéed, or roasted.

Local Beverages

  • Spruce-tip tea: tea made from the fresh tips of spruce branches, often paired with honey.
  • Devil’s club tea: tea made from the bark and roots of the Devil’s Club plant, known for its medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Daajing Giids-Skidegate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Haida House at Tllaal: a lodge-style accommodation with a spacious deck that offers scenic views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Queen Charlotte Lodge: a cozy lodge that offers guided fishing trips, as well as heli-adventures.

What to Buy in Daajing Giids-Skidegate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Haida art and crafts: From traditional Haida jewelry and carvings to contemporary art pieces.
  • Abalone shells: Known for their intricate designs, these shells are often hand-painted and adorned with traditional Haida designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Daajing Giids-Skidegate

The Haida people have a rich culture that dates back over 10,000 years. Visitors to Daajing Giids-Skidegate can witness the cultural practices of the Haida people through art, music, and dance. They have a strong connection to the natural world and place great importance on sustainability and environmental stewardship.

Best Time to Visit Daajing Giids-Skidegate

The best time to visit Daajing Giids-Skidegate is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warmer and drier. The fall months, from September to November, are also a good time to visit, as the temperatures are cooler and the fall colours are in full effect.

Do’s and Don’ts of Daajing Giids-Skidegate

  • Ask for permission before taking photos of the Haida people or their art.
  • Try to purchase Haida art directly from local artists.

Don’t:
