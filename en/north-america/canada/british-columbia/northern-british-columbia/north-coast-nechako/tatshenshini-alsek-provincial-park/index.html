Introduction to Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park is a protected wilderness area located in Northern British Columbia, Canada. It covers an area of over 935,000 hectares and is part of a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The park is named after two major rivers, the Tatshenshini and the Alsek, which run through its remote and rugged landscape. The park is home to a vast array of wildlife such as grizzly bears, moose, wolves, and mountain goats.

List of Places to Visit in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most culturally significant attractions in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park is the First Nations Community of Haines Junction. Here, visitors can learn about the traditions and customs of the Champagne and Aishihik First Nations people. The Kluane Museum of Natural History is also located in Haines Junction and features exhibits on the park’s natural history and wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park offers a wealth of outdoor activities and scenic views that are sure to delight visitors. The park is home to the Kaskawulsh Glacier, which is one of the largest non-polar ice caps in the world. Visitors can also hike the Cottonwood Trail, an easy and scenic trail that offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains.

The park is also home to many waterways, including the Tatshenshini and Alsek Rivers, which offer world-class recreational opportunities such as whitewater rafting and kayaking. The park also has a number of hiking trails that range from easy day hikes to more challenging multi-day backpacking trips.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

King’s Throne Trail

The King’s Throne Trail is a popular hiking trail that offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains. The trail is about 7 km long and is best hiked from June to September.

Alsek Trail

The Alsek Trail is a more challenging multi-day backpacking trip that offers incredible views of the Alsek River and the park’s surrounding glaciers. The trail is about 97 km long and involves crossing several rivers and steep mountain passes.

Best Things to Do in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park: Activities and Tours

Rafting and Kayaking

Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park is a popular destination for whitewater rafting and kayaking. Several outfitters offer guided tours that cater to different skill levels.

Wildlife Watching

The park is home to a diverse array of wildlife such as grizzly bears, moose, wolves, and caribou. Visitors can experience the park’s remarkable wildlife by taking a guided wildlife tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

Day 1-2: Explore Haines Junction

Spend your first few days exploring the First Nations Community of Haines Junction and the Kluane Museum. Visit local restaurants and shops and learn about the traditions and customs of the Champagne and Aishihik First Nations people.

Day 3-4: Hiking and Backpacking

Take a multi-day backpacking trip on the Alsek Trail or the Cottonwood Trail and experience the park’s incredible scenery and wildlife.

Day 5-6: Whitewater Rafting and Kayaking

Spend a day or two on the water, taking in the park’s breathtaking views of the Tatshenshini and Alsek Rivers while engaging in some adrenaline-pumping activities like whitewater rafting or kayaking.

Day 7: Departure

Wrap up your visit to Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park with a day of relaxation before departing back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

Must-Try Dishes

Visitors must try the bannock, a traditional bread that originated from the Indigenous people of North America. The bread is shaped like a pancake and is often served with butter or jam.

Local Beverages

The Yukon region is known for its microbreweries that offer delicious beer. Visitors can stop at one of the many local breweries for a cold pint.

Where to Stay in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dalton Trail Lodge

Set on the shores of Dezadeash Lake, Dalton Trail Lodge is a fishing and hunting lodge offering several guest rooms and cabins.

Alsek Lodge

Located on the banks of the Tatshenshini River, Alsek Lodge offers several cabins and guest rooms and is a great base for exploring the park’s natural beauty.

What to Buy in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase Indigenous art and crafts, including ceramic pottery and handwoven baskets at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

The Champagne and Aishihik First Nations people have lived in the area for thousands of years and have a deep connection to the land. Visitors should respect and appreciate the history and traditions of the local Indigenous people.

Best Time to Visit Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

The best time to visit Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park is from June to September when the weather is milder. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather, and it is always advisable to check weather conditions before arriving.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

Do:

  • Respect and appreciate the park’s natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for your activities.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Hire a licensed guide for activities like whitewater rafting and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Litter or pollute the park’s natural beauty.
  • Ignore park regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

Visitors should be aware of potential hazards, such as steep terrain and swift water. It is always advisable to follow safety guidelines and regulations and to hire experienced, licensed guides for activities like whitewater rafting and kayaking.

Health in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

There are no major health risks in the park, but visitors should always be prepared for sudden weather changes, including cold temperatures and rain. Visitors should bring appropriate medication and supplies and consult with a healthcare professional before traveling.

Getting to Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The park is located in a remote region of Northern British Columbia, and visitors must either fly to Whitehorse and drive to the park or take a charter flight directly to the park.

Airports Near Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

The nearest airport is the Whitehorse International Airport in Yukon, Canada.

Bus Stations Near Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

There are no major bus stations near the park.

Other Options

Charter flights are available from Whitehorse to the park.

How to Get Around in Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park

The park is mostly accessible by foot, and visitors should be prepared for challenging terrain. Visitors can also hire licensed guides and outfitters for activities like whitewater rafting or kayaking.

Where to Go Next from Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kluane National Park and Reserve

Kluane National Park and Reserve is located adjacent to Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park and is renowned for its natural beauty and pristine wilderness.

Skagway, Alaska

Skagway, Alaska, is located just a few hours from the park and offers visitors the opportunity to explore the town’s rich Gold Rush history and scenic coastal surroundings.

Conclusion

Tatshenshini-Alsek Provincial Park is a breathtaking destination that offers visitors a wealth of outdoor activities and scenic views. From hiking and backpacking to whitewater rafting and kayaking, visitors can experience the park’s incredible natural beauty and wildlife. Visitors should also take the time to appreciate the region’s Indigenous history and traditions and respect the park’s regulations and safety guidelines.
