Introduction to Kitimat

Located in Northern British Columbia, Kitimat is a hidden gem nestled amidst stunning natural beauty. With an area of over 3200 hectares, it is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, offering numerous trails and opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

Kitimat is a culturally diverse community, with a population of 8,335, known for its friendly locals, rich indigenous cultures, and diverse culinary scene.

Whether you are here to experience its natural beauty or learn about its culture, there is something for everyone in Kitimat.

List of Places to Visit in Kitimat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Haisla Nation - The Haisla Nation cultural center is a fascinating place to explore and learn about indigenous cultures. The center features exhibits of traditional Haisla art, including carvings, weavings, and more.

  2. Kitimat Museum & Archives - The Kitimat Museum & Archives is a must-visit, featuring exhibits on the history of Kitimat, its people, and the surrounding areas. It also features collections of art and artifacts from various cultural groups.

Natural Attractions of Kitimat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kitimat River - The Kitimat River is a world-class salmon fishing destination and is perfect for a peaceful walk along its banks. The river is home to many wildlife species, including eagles and bears.

  2. Giant Spruce Trail - The Giant Spruce Trail offers a scenic hike through old-growth forests to view one of the largest spruce trees in the region. The trail is suitable for all hiking levels and is perfect for nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kitimat

  1. Kitimat Canyon - The Kitimat Canyon trail is a 10-kilometer-long hiking trail and is perfect for those looking for a challenging hike. You’ll trek through rugged terrain and witness stunning views of the Kitimat River and its surrounding area.

  2. Hirsch Creek Park Trail - The Hirsch Creek Park Trail is a 10-kilometer-long hiking trail that winds through beautiful Pacific coastal rainforest. The trail features waterfalls, crystal-clear creeks, and diverse wildlife habitats.

Best Things to Do in Kitimat: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing - Kitimat is legendary for sport fishing enthusiasts. Fishing tours and charters are readily available, taking visitors to some of the best salmon fishing locations in the world.

  2. Outdoor Adventures - Kitimat is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, from hiking to kayaking to wildlife viewing, there are endless ways to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Kitimat

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish & Chips - Kitimat is famous for its fresh salmon, and there’s no better way to enjoy it than as classic fish and chips.

  2. Indigenous Cuisine - Visitors can get a taste of Haisla traditions and culture through traditional dishes of seafood, wild berries, and delicacies such as smoked salmon and bannock bread.

Local Beverages

  1. Snow Valley Honey Ale - Made from locally sourced honey, this unique beer is a must-try for beer lovers.

  2. White Raven IPA - An award-winning IPA brewed using local ingredients that perfectly captures the distinct flavor of the region.

Where to Stay in Kitimat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Kitimat Hotel - Located downtown, the Kitimat Hotel is an excellent option for those looking for a comfortable stay at an affordable price.

  2. Moon Sockeye Inn - Located within walking distance of many attractions, the Moon Sockeye Inn offers comfortable and clean accommodations with a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Kitimat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Haisla Art - Visitors can purchase traditional Haisla art, including totem poles, carvings, and jewelry.

  2. Local Honey - The honey produced in Kitimat is some of the best in the world, and it’s a perfect souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kitimat

The Haisla Nation has always been the custodians of the land, and their traditions continue to thrive today. Visitors can learn about their customs, art, and stories through the cultural center and museum.

Haisla Nation emphasizes respect for the environment through activities such as eco-tourism and sustainable living.

Best Time to Visit Kitimat

The best time to visit Kitimat is in the summer and fall. During these seasons, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and the region’s stunning natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kitimat

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow the leave-no-trace ethos and minimize your impact on the environment.
  • Bring appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Wildlife viewing should be done with the utmost care and respect for the animals. Do not disturb their habitats.
  • Avoid littering and dispose of your waste properly.
  • Do not disturb or remove any Haisla Nation artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kitimat

Kitimat is safe for visitors, but they should take the normal safety precautions like anywhere else.

Health in Kitimat

Visitors should have a health insurance plan that covers medical emergencies. For emergencies, the nearest hospital is the Kitimat General Hospital.

Getting to Kitimat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kitimat

  1. Terrace-Kitimat Airport
  2. Northwest Regional Airport Terrace-Kitimat

Bus Stations Near Kitimat

  1. BC Transit - Kitimat

Other Options

  1. Drive - Kitimat can be accessed via Highway 16, also known as the Yellowhead Highway.

How to Get Around in Kitimat

Visitors can get around Kitimat via bus using BC Transit or by renting a car. There are also walking and biking trails throughout the town that offer relaxing ways to explore the picturesque locale.

Where to Go Next from Kitimat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Prince Rupert - Located 142 kilometers away from Kitimat, Prince Rupert is a beautiful seaside town with stunning views of the surrounding areas.

  2. Terrace - Located only 63 kilometers from Kitimat, Terrace is known for its stunning outdoor beauty, including deep river valleys and glaciated mountains.

Conclusion

Kitimat is a unique destination with something to offer for every kind of traveler. From outdoor activities to immersing yourself in Indigenous culture, this charming town is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Northern British Columbia. With this comprehensive guide, we hope you have all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Kitimat.
