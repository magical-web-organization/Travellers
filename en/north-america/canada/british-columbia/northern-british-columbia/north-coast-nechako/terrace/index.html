Introduction to Terrace

Nestled in the picturesque Northern British Columbia region of Canada, Terrace is a charming town that’s steeped in natural beauty and rich cultural history. Surrounded by towering mountains, lush forests, and pristine lakes and rivers, Terrace offers a range of outdoor activities and adventures for travelers of all ages and interests.

Whether you’re looking for hiking and biking trails, scenic drives, fishing spots, or attractions that offer a glimpse into the local culture, Terrace has something for everyone. In this tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the top places to visit, things to do, and must-try dishes and beverages in Terrace. We’ll also share some helpful tips on where to stay, how to get around, and what to buy as souvenirs. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this beautiful region!

List of Places to Visit in Terrace

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or looking to learn more about the local culture, Terrace has a range of museums and historical places that are worth a visit. Here are some of the top attractions in this category:

Heritage Park Museum

The Heritage Park Museum is a must-visit destination for history lovers. Here, you can explore a range of exhibits that showcase the region’s natural, cultural, and industrial history. From learning about the life of the First Nations people to understanding how the region’s sawmills contributed to the local economy, there’s so much to see and discover at this fascinating museum.

Nisga’a Museum

The Nisga’a Museum is another top attraction for those interested in Indigenous culture. Located just a short drive from Terrace, this museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts, artworks, and historical objects that tell the story of the Nisga’a people. Don’t forget to explore the beautiful architecture and design of the building itself, which was inspired by traditional Nisga’a longhouses.

Kwinitsa Railway Station

For a glimpse into the region’s railway history, head to the Kwinitsa Railway Station. This beautifully restored station dates back to the early 20th century and is a great place to learn about the role of the railway in shaping the region. Be sure to take a guided tour and explore the exhibits to fully appreciate the station’s history and significance.

Natural Attractions of Terrace: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main draws of Terrace is its stunning natural beauty. From crystal-clear lakes to soaring mountains, there are countless opportunities to explore the great outdoors here. Here are some must-visit natural attractions in and around the town:

Terrace Mountain

For breathtaking views of the region, hike to the top of Terrace Mountain. This trailhead is just a few minutes’ drive from town and is a moderate hike that takes around three hours round trip. From the summit, you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of the Skeena River, Terrace, and the surrounding mountains.

Lakelse Lake Provincial Park

If you’re looking for a spot to swim, fish, or paddle, head to Lakelse Lake Provincial Park. This beautiful park is located just a short drive from Terrace and features a range of outdoor activities for all ages. From swimming in the calm, clear waters of the lake to hiking the nearby trails, there’s something for everyone at Lakelse Lake.

Kleanza Creek Provincial Park

For a scenic drive and a peaceful place to picnic, check out Kleanza Creek Provincial Park. This park features a refreshing swimming hole, several walking trails, and breathtaking views of the Kleanza Mountain range. Take a stroll along the river or pack a picnic and enjoy the serenity of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Terrace

Terrace is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with countless hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

Terrace Mountain

As mentioned earlier, the hike to the top of Terrace Mountain is not to be missed. This hike is a few hours long and takes you through lush forests and alpine fields before reaching the summit with stunning 360-degree views.

Fisherman’s Trail

Fisherman’s Trail is a popular biking and hiking route that takes you on a scenic journey along the banks of the Skeena River. This easy, flat trail is perfect for families or those looking for a leisurely ride or walk. Take in the beautiful views of the river and watch for wildlife, including bald eagles and salmon.

Sleeping Beauty Trail

For a more challenging hike, check out the Sleeping Beauty Trail. This eight-kilometer hike takes you through dense forests and steep inclines before reaching a stunning lookout point with sweeping views of the surrounding valleys. Be prepared for a strenuous but rewarding hike.

Best Things to Do in Terrace: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the natural beauty of the region, there are plenty of other things to do in Terrace. Here are some top activities and tours to consider:

Fishing Tours

Terrace is a world-renowned destination for fishing, with some of the best salmon and steelhead rivers in the world. If you’re an avid angler or looking to try your hand at fishing for the first time, consider booking a guided fishing tour. You’ll have the opportunity to learn about the region’s fish species and ecosystems while enjoying a day on the water.

Helicopter Tours

For a bird’s-eye view of the region, book a helicopter tour. You’ll be able to take in the stunning views of Terrace, the Skeena River, and the surrounding mountains from above. This is an experience you won’t soon forget.

Caving Adventures

Explore the depths of the earth with a caving adventure. Terrace has several underground caves that are accessible to experienced spelunkers. You’ll have the chance to see underground waterfalls, sparkling crystals, and ancient rock formations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Terrace

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Terrace, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Terrace Mountain and take in the stunning views.

Day 2: Visit the Heritage Park Museum and learn about the region’s natural and cultural history.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Lakelse Lake Provincial Park for swimming, fishing, and hiking.

Day 4: Visit the Nisga’a Museum and learn about the culture and traditions of the Nisga’a people.

Day 5: Go on a guided fishing tour and try your hand at catching salmon or steelhead.

Day 6: Take a helicopter tour to see the region from a bird’s-eye view.

Day 7: Relax and unwind with a caving adventure in one of Terrace’s underground caves.

Food and Drink Scene of Terrace

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Terrace is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Smoked salmon

As a world-renowned fishing destination, it’s no surprise that Terrace is known for its delicious smoked salmon. Whether you try it on its own or in a meal, this local delicacy is a must-try.

Bannock

Bannock is a traditional Indigenous flatbread that’s a staple in the local cuisine. It’s often accompanied by honey or jam and is a satisfying snack or meal.

Local Beverages

Don’t forget to sample some of the local beverages during your stay in Terrace. Here are some to try:

Skeena Brewing Company

If you’re a beer lover, visit the Skeena Brewing Company. This local brewery offers a range of craft beers inspired by the region’s landscape and culture. Take a tour and enjoy a pint or two of their delicious brews.

Shames Mountain Coffee

For a warming drink, visit Shames Mountain Coffee. This local café offers a variety of hot beverages made with locally roasted coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Terrace: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Terrace offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. From cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels, here are some of the top places to stay:

Northern Motor Inn

The Northern Motor Inn is a comfortable and affordable option for travelers. The hotel offers clean and spacious rooms and is conveniently located in the heart of town.

The Lodge at Skeena Landing

For a more luxurious stay, check out The Lodge at Skeena Landing. This beautiful hotel offers stunning views of the Skeena River and is a short drive from town.

Tralee Indigo Guesthouse

For a cozy and welcoming stay, consider Tralee Indigo Guesthouse. This charming guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Terrace: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Terrace has plenty of options. Here are some top picks:

Indigenous Artwork

Terrace is home to several Indigenous artists and galleries that offer stunning artwork inspired by the local culture and landscape. Consider purchasing a piece of art as a unique and meaningful souvenir.

Salmon Jerky

As mentioned earlier, smoked salmon is a local delicacy in Terrace. Take some home with you in the form of salmon jerky, a portable and delicious snack that’s perfect for the road.

Local Culture and Customs of Terrace

The region surrounding Terrace has a rich history and culture, largely influenced by the Indigenous peoples of the region. Visitors should be respectful of this history and culture and learn as much as they can about the traditions and customs of the local people.

Best Time to Visit Terrace

The best time to visit Terrace is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities and adventures. If you’re interested in fishing, plan your visit for early fall, when the salmon and steelhead runs are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Terrace

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Pack for all types of weather.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and adventures.

Don’t:

  • Stray from designated hiking trails
  • Leave litter or destroy natural habitats
  • Disturb wildlife or endanger your safety

Safety and Health

Safety in Terrace

Terrace is a safe and welcoming destination for travelers. However, visitors should take caution when hiking, biking, or exploring the natural areas surrounding the town, always ensuring they are prepared and aware of the inherent risks associated with outdoor activities.

Health in Terrace

The healthcare facilities in Terrace are comprehensive, and visitors can rest assured knowing they are in good hands should they require medical attention.

Getting to Terrace: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Terrace

The Northwest Regional Airport is located in Terrace and serves as the primary airport for travelers arriving by air.

Bus Stations Near Terrace

Greyhound Canada offers daily bus service to Terrace from Vancouver and other major cities in British Columbia.

Other Options

Rental cars are also available at the airport and in town, providing a convenient option for travelers who want to explore independently.

How to Get Around in Terrace

Renting a car is a popular option for travelers who want to explore the surrounding areas independently. However, there are also several taxi and shuttle services available in town.

Where to Go Next from Terrace: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Terrace, travelers can check out several nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some top picks:

Prince Rupert

Located just a few hours’ drive from Terrace, Prince Rupert is a charming coastal town that offers beautiful scenery and a range of outdoor activities and adventures.

Nass Valley

The Nass Valley is a remote and wild region that’s home to several Indigenous communities and stunning natural beauty. Visitors can explore the valley on guided tours and learn about the history and culture of the Nisga’a people.

Conclusion

Terrace, British Columbia, is a beautiful and welcoming destination that offers plenty of opportunities to explore the natural beauty and rich cultural heritage of the region. From hiking and fishing to exploring museums and galleries, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your itinerary and make the most of your visit to Terrace.
