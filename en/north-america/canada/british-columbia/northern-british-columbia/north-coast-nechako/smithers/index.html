Introduction to Smithers

Nestled in the heart of Northern British Columbia, Smithers is a charming small town surrounded by stunning natural beauty. The town is home to a vibrant arts community, diverse cultural attractions, and a wealth of outdoor adventures.

With welcoming locals, a relaxed pace of life, and breathtaking scenery, Smithers is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Smithers

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Smithers offers a rich cultural scene, with several museums and historical sites that offer insight into the area’s past and present.

Bulkley Valley Museum

The Bulkley Valley Museum is housed in an iconic red-brick building in the heart of downtown Smithers. The museum showcases the history and heritage of the Bulkley Valley through a variety of exhibits and artifacts.

Smithers Art Gallery

The Smithers Art Gallery hosts a range of exhibits throughout the year, featuring local and regional artists. The gallery also hosts workshops and events, making it a hub for the local arts community.

St. Joseph’s Mission Residential School Building

The St. Joseph’s Mission Residential School Building is a poignant reminder of the residential school system that impacted Indigenous communities across Canada. The building is now a National Historic Site and can be visited to learn about this dark chapter in Canadian history.

Natural Attractions of Smithers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Smithers is surrounded by incredible natural beauty, with abundant opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic exploration.

Hudson Bay Mountain

Hudson Bay Mountain is a year-round playground for outdoor enthusiasts. In the winter months, the mountain offers excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities. In the summer, the mountain transforms into a hiking and mountain biking paradise, with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Babine Mountains Provincial Park

Babine Mountains Provincial Park is a wilderness area located just north of Smithers. The park offers miles of hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and spectacular scenery, including alpine meadows and pristine lakes.

Telkwa Mountains

The Telkwa Mountains are a stunning range of peaks located just outside of Smithers. The range offers a variety of hiking trails, including the increasingly popular Crater Lake trail. The area is also rich in wildlife, with the potential to spot mountain goats, grizzly bears, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smithers

Smithers is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of the most popular:

  • Crater Lake Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views of the Telkwa Mountains and Crater Lake.

  • Perimeter Trail: This 15-kilometre trail circles the town of Smithers, offering a great way to explore the area on foot or by bike.

  • Hudson Bay Mountain Trails: Hudson Bay Mountain offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging backcountry adventures.

Best Things to Do in Smithers: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural beauty and cultural attractions, Smithers offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy.

  • Smithers Paragliding: Soar above the mountains and valleys with a tandem paragliding flight.

  • Northern Lights Wildlife Wolf Centre: Learn about the natural history of wolves with a tour of this wildlife centre.

  • Bulkley Valley Brewery Tour: Enjoy a tour and tasting at one of Smithers’ local breweries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Smithers

Here’s an idea for a week-long itinerary to experience the best of Smithers:

Day 1: Explore downtown Smithers, including the Bulkley Valley Museum and St. Joseph’s Mission Residential School Building.

Day 2: Take a guided hiking tour of the Telkwa Mountains, with a picnic lunch overlooking the stunning scenery.

Day 3: Visit the Northern Lights Wildlife Wolf Centre and enjoy a scenic drive through the Babine Mountains Provincial Park.

Day 4: Cycle the Perimeter Trail and enjoy a leisurely picnic lunch in Riverside Park.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Hazelton to experience the rich Indigenous history of the area, including the ‘Ksan Historical Village.

Day 6: Go paragliding over the Telkwa Mountains and experience the breathtaking views from above.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day in Smithers, with a visit to the local farmers’ market and a tour of the Bulkley Valley Brewery.

Food and Drink Scene of Smithers

Smithers offers a diverse and delicious food and drink scene, with something to suit every taste and budget.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: Try this Canadian classic at one of Smithers’ local cafes or diners.

  • Wild Salmon: Enjoy fresh, locally caught salmon at one of Smithers’ seafood restaurants.

  • Bannock: A traditional Indigenous bread that can be found at the farmers’ market and local bakeries.

Local Beverages

  • Bulkley Valley Brewery: Enjoy locally crafted beers and ciders at this popular brewery.

  • Two Sisters Vineyards: Sample award-winning wines at this picturesque vineyard outside of Smithers.

Where to Stay in Smithers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Smithers offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to high-end hotels.

  • Stork Nest Inn: A charming bed and breakfast located in downtown Smithers.

  • Hudson Bay Lodge: A well-appointed hotel with a range of amenities, including a restaurant, pool, and sauna.

  • Babine Lodge: A rustic lodge located in the heart of Babine Mountains Provincial Park.

What to Buy in Smithers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Smithers is known for its locally crafted products and unique souvenirs.

  • Smithers Farmers’ Market: Visit the market on Saturdays to sample and purchase locally grown produce, crafts, and more.

  • Cornerstone Crafts: This local shop offers a variety of handmade goods, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Smithers

Smithers has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply intertwined with the natural surroundings. The area is home to several Indigenous communities, and their traditions and customs are an integral part of the local culture.

Visitors should be respectful of the natural environment and local customs, and should take the time to learn about the history and traditions of the area.

Best Time to Visit Smithers

The best time to visit Smithers depends on your interests and preferences.

  • Summer: The summer months offer excellent opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities.

  • Winter: The winter months are perfect for skiing and snowboarding on Hudson Bay Mountain.

  • Spring/Fall: The shoulder seasons offer mild weather and fewer crowds, making it a great time to explore the town and its surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smithers

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the area.

  • Learn about the local Indigenous culture and traditions.

  • Support the local economy by shopping at the farmers’ market and local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the natural environment by littering or damaging plants.

  • Disrespect the traditions and customs of local Indigenous communities.

  • Take unnecessary risks while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Smithers

Smithers is a safe and welcoming community. However, visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking doors and windows and avoiding dark alleys at night.

Health in Smithers

Smithers has a fully equipped hospital, as well as several medical clinics and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance, as medical care in Canada can be expensive.

Getting to Smithers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Smithers

The Smithers Regional Airport offers regular flights to several major cities in British Columbia, including Vancouver and Prince George.

Bus Stations Near Smithers

Greyhound Canada operates a bus service that stops in Smithers.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Smithers from other parts of British Columbia or neighbouring provinces.

How to Get Around in Smithers

Smithers is a small town that is easily walkable. Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding area more easily.

Where to Go Next from Smithers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hazelton: A nearby town that is rich in Indigenous history and culture.

  • Prince George: A larger city located to the east of Smithers, with a range of cultural attractions and amenities.

  • Skeena River: A beautiful river that is popular for fishing and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Smithers is a hidden gem in Northern British Columbia, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. With a relaxed pace of life and welcoming atmosphere, Smithers is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the stresses of modern life and immerse themselves in nature and culture.
