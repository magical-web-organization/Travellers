Introduction to Prince Rupert

Nestled in the stunning scenic beauty of Northern British Colombia, Prince Rupert is a charming coastal city that deserves to be explored by every traveler. Sitting at the mouth of the Skeena River, the city is known for its friendly locals, rich culture, and unrivaled hospitality.

Home to outstanding natural wonders, Prince Rupert offers a tranquil and refreshing retreat for nature lovers. From scenic views of the Pacific Ocean to hiking trails in the nearby mountains, this city caters to every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Prince Rupert

If you are planning a trip to Prince Rupert, here is a list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Northern British Columbia: Discover the rich history and culture of Northern British Colombia through an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  • Totem Park: A tribute to the indigenous culture of the area, this park boasts of a magnificent collection of totem poles.
  • Sunken Gardens Park: A beautiful park in downtown Prince Rupert, this garden features a waterfall, a pond, a stream, and a floral arrangement.

Natural Attractions of Prince Rupert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Butze Rapids Park: A scenic park ideal for hiking, kayaking, and observing wildlife.
  • Khutzeymateen Grizzly Bear Sanctuary: Home to a large population of grizzly bears, this sanctuary offers a rare opportunity to watch these majestic animals in their natural habitat.
  • Cow Bay: A charming coastal boardwalk that takes visitors through Prince Rupert’s downtown core, featuring local shops, fishing boats, and restaurants.
  • Salmon fishing: From saltwater fishing to freshwater salmon runs, Prince Rupert is a paradise for anglers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prince Rupert

Prince Rupert has numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to every level of adventurer. Whether you prefer a leisurely stroll through the forest or a challenging hike to a mountain peak, this city has it all.

Some popular trails include Mount Hays, the Butze Rapids trail, the Rushbrook Trail, and the Harbourview Trail.

Best Things to Do in Prince Rupert: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do and tours to take in Prince Rupert:

  • Whale watching tour: Get up close and personal with humpback whales and orcas in their natural habitat.
  • Fishing tour: Experience the thrill of deep sea fishing or freshwater salmon runs.
  • Helicopter tour: Get a bird’s eye view of the stunning scenery of the city.
  • Harbour cruise: Enjoy a leisurely boat ride, soaking in the mesmerizing scenic beauty of Prince Rupert’s harbour.
  • Local brewery tour: Enjoy a pint of local beer while experiencing the unique flavor of Prince Rupert’s beer culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prince Rupert

Day 1: Arrive in Prince Rupert and explore the town’s charming coastal boardwalk, Cow Bay.

Day 2: Take a whale watching tour to see the majestic orca and humpback whales. Spend the evening exploring the Museum of Northern British Columbia.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Mount Hays and enjoy panoramic views of Prince Rupert and its surrounding areas.

Day 4: Take a fishing tour and catch salmon or halibut in the nearby waters.

Day 5: Relax on a harbour cruise and explore the local breweries in the afternoon.

Day 6: Visit the Khutzeymateen Grizzly Bear Sanctuary and marvel at the beauty of these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

Day 7: Spend the day exploring various hiking trails around Prince Rupert.

Food and Drink Scene of Prince Rupert

Prince Rupert boasts of a unique culinary scene. The city is known for its fresh seafood, unique craft beers, and locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips: A classic seafood dish that is a must-try in Prince Rupert
  • Wild salmon: Cooked fresh with a dash of lemon and herbs, this is a local delicacy
  • Dungeness crab: Often served with a side of garlic butter, this dish is a favourite among locals and visitors alike

Local Beverages

  • North Pacific Cannery Brewery: Known for its unique and refreshing craft beer, this brewery is a popular tourist spot.
  • Cowpuccino’s Cafe: A local coffee shop that serves up perfectly brewed coffee and delicious baked goods.

Where to Stay in Prince Rupert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options in Prince Rupert, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Crest Hotel
  • Pioneer Hostel
  • Tall Trees Bed and Breakfast

What to Buy in Prince Rupert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products you should consider bringing home from Prince Rupert:

  • Native art: The indigenous culture of the area is centered around totem poles, masks, and carvings, all of which make for a fantastic souvenir.
  • Local fish: Fresh fish can be purchased at the local market and taken home to be cooked.
  • North Coast Gift Gallery: A great place to find souvenirs and locally made products.

Local Culture and Customs of Prince Rupert

The indigenous culture of the area is an integral part of Prince Rupert’s cultural landscape. The Tsimshian First Nation people have lived in this area for thousands of years, and their influence can be seen in many of the city’s attractions and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Prince Rupert

The best time to visit Prince Rupert is between May and September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prince Rupert

Do:

  • Respect the Tsimshian First Nation people and their culture.
  • Try the local seafood.
  • Explore the hiking and biking trails.
  • Take a tour of the local breweries.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment.
  • Trespass on private property.
  • Disturb or feed wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prince Rupert

Prince Rupert is a safe place to visit, with a noticeable police presence throughout the city. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and exercise caution when walking alone or at night.

Health in Prince Rupert

There are various medical facilities in Prince Rupert, and visitors can access medical assistance if needed. It’s always a good idea to purchase travel insurance before embarking on any trip.

Getting to Prince Rupert: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prince Rupert

The Prince Rupert Airport is serviced by a few regional airlines and charter flights.

Bus Stations Near Prince Rupert

Greyhound Bus operates a station in Prince Rupert, servicing regional locations.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Prince Rupert, take a train, or arrive by boat.

How to Get Around in Prince Rupert

Walking, biking, and taxis are popular ways to get around Prince Rupert. Visitors can also rent a car or take a bus.

Where to Go Next from Prince Rupert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Haida Gwaii

Known for their stunning natural beauty, the Haida Gwaii islands are a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Terrace

Located just over 50 miles east of Prince Rupert, Terrace is a hub for outdoor adventures and exploration.

Conclusion

Prince Rupert is a hidden gem that is waiting to be explored by travelers from around the world. This charming coastal city offers magnificent natural wonders, rich culture, and a welcoming hospitality that is second to none. We hope that this guide has given you a glimpse into the endless possibilities that Prince Rupert has to offer.
