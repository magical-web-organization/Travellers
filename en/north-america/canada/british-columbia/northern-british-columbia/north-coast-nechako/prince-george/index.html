Introduction to Prince George

Nestled in Northern British Columbia, Prince George is a city renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or just looking to explore a new destination, Prince George has something to offer for everyone. From hiking and biking trails to museums and art galleries, from local cuisine to unique souvenirs, this comprehensive tour guide will help you discover all the best places to visit and things to do in Prince George.

List of Places to Visit in Prince George

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Prince George is home to a variety of museums and historical sites that give a glimpse into the city’s fascinating past and rich cultural heritage. Some of the top attractions include:

  • The Exploration Place Museum and Science Centre: Located in the heart of Prince George, this museum offers a fascinating look at the natural and cultural history of the region, with interactive exhibits, live demonstrations, and hands-on activities for visitors of all ages.
  • The Two Rivers Gallery: This contemporary art gallery showcases the work of local and regional artists, with a focus on contemporary Canadian art. From painting and sculpture to photography and mixed media, the gallery has something to offer for every art lover.
  • Huble Homestead Historic Site: This well-preserved site is a window into the history of the early pioneers who settled in the area. Visitors can tour the historic buildings, including a general store, post office, and blacksmith shop, and learn about the daily life of early settlers in the region.
  • The Railway and Forestry Museum: This museum celebrates the contributions of the railway and forestry industries to the development of Northern British Columbia, with an impressive collection of vintage locomotives and forestry equipment.

Natural Attractions of Prince George: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Prince George is surrounded by pristine wilderness, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, fish, or just soak in the natural beauty of the region, there are plenty of places to explore. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Lheidli T’enneh Memorial Park: Located in the heart of the city, this park offers a beautiful green space for picnicking, walking, and enjoying the scenic views. It also features a water park, outdoor pool, and skateboarding facility, making it a great destination for families with kids.
  • UNBC Greenway Trail System: This network of hiking and biking trails spans over 15 kilometers of lush forest and scenic views, with options for all skill levels.
  • Eskers Provincial Park: This unique park features a series of ancient sand dunes, offering a glimpse into the geological history of the region. Visitors can hike or bike the trails, take a guided tour, or just enjoy the scenery.
  • Purden Lake Provincial Park: This picturesque lake is a popular spot for fishing, swimming, and boating, with beautiful mountain views and plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prince George

If you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Prince George on foot or by bike, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Cranbrook Hill Greenway Trail: This 17-kilometer trail system includes a variety of hiking and biking trails, with options for all skill levels and beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Otway Ski Centre: In the winter months, this ski center offers a network of groomed cross-country skiing trails, but in the summer, the trails are open for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Walper Lake Trail: This easy, 2-kilometer hike takes visitors through a beautiful forested area with views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Tabor Mountain Recreation Area: With over 30 kilometers of trails for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing, this area is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Prince George: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural attractions and cultural sites, Prince George offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do in the area:

  • Northern Lights Estate Winery: This local winery offers tastings and tours of its vineyard and winery, with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • PG Climb: This indoor rock climbing gym is a great way to stay active and challenge yourself, even on rainy days.
  • Prince George Farmers’ Market: This weekly farmers’ market features a variety of local vendors, selling fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal crafts.
  • Ghost Tours: Explore the city’s spooky side with a guided ghost tour of some of its most haunted locations.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Prince George, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the area:

Food and Drink Scene of Prince George

No trip to Prince George would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to check out:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic, this dish consists of french fries, cheese curds, and gravy, and is a popular comfort food in Prince George.
  • Wild Salmon: With its proximity to the Pacific Ocean, Prince George is known for its fresh and flavorful wild salmon dishes.
  • Bannock: This traditional Indigenous food is a type of flatbread that is often served with soup or stew, and is a staple in the local cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Northern Lights Estate Winery: This winery offers a variety of award-winning wines, made from locally grown grapes.
  • Trench Brewing and Distilling: This local brewery and distillery offers a variety of craft beers and spirits, with a focus on using locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Prince George: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prince George offers a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top options to consider:

  • Ramada by Wyndham Prince George: This hotel is located in the heart of the city, and offers comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a fitness center.
  • The Courtyard by Marriott: This hotel is a great option for business travelers, with its convenient location and modern amenities.
  • North Country Lodge: This cozy guesthouse is a great budget-friendly option, with comfortable rooms and a quaint, rustic atmosphere.

What to Buy in Prince George: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to remember your trip to Prince George? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Pine Beetle Wood Products: This company creates beautiful, one-of-a-kind wood products from beetle-damaged pine trees, such as cutting boards, coasters, and bowls.
  • Northern Hardware and Furniture: This local hardware store sells a variety of unique and handmade items, from woodworking tools to kitchen gadgets.
  • Salt Boutique: This local shop sells a variety of handcrafted bath and body products, made from locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Prince George

Prince George has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Indigenous, European, and Asian influences. Here are some unique customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • First Nations Culture: The Lheidli T’enneh First Nation has a strong presence in the region, with a rich history and culture to explore.
  • Hockey: Like many Canadian cities, Prince George is crazy about hockey, with a passionate fan base and a local team, the Prince George Cougars.
  • Northern Lights: Due to its location in northern British Columbia, Prince George is a great destination to see the Aurora Borealis, or northern lights.

Best Time to Visit Prince George

The best time to visit Prince George depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a breakdown of the seasons and what to expect:

  • Summer (June-August): This is the peak tourist season, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy.
  • Fall (September-November): This is a great time to visit if you’re interested in fall foliage, as the trees around Prince George turn brilliant shades of red, yellow, and orange.
  • Winter (December-February): This is a great time to visit if you’re interested in skiing, snowboarding, or snowmobiling, as there are several nearby ski resorts and trails to explore.
  • Spring (March-May): This is a quieter time to visit, with milder temperatures and fewer crowds, making it a great time to enjoy the city’s museums and cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prince George

To make the most of your visit to Prince George, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of the region.
  • Do try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do respect the cultural heritage of the First Nations communities in the area.
  • Do pack for diverse weather conditions, depending on the season.
  • Do be open to new experiences and meeting new people.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of the sun – even on cloudy days, sun protection is important.
  • Don’t forget to tip at restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t ignore safety warnings or trail closures.
  • Don’t assume everyone speaks English – many locals in the area are bilingual or multilingual.

Safety and Health

Prince George is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to take precautions and be aware of potential risks. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Prince George

  • Be aware of wildlife hazards, such as bears, and take appropriate precautions, such as carrying bear spray and making noise while hiking.
  • Be aware of potential weather hazards, such as snowstorms or icy conditions on the roads or trails.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or biking alone.
  • Lock your car and don’t leave valuables visible.
  • Keep emergency numbers handy, such as the local police, ambulance, and fire department.

Health in Prince George

  • Be aware of potential hazards while exploring the natural environment, such as poison ivy or insect bites.
  • Take precautions against sun exposure, especially during the summer months.
  • Stay hydrated while hiking or exploring the outdoors.
  • Bring any necessary medications with you, and be aware of any local health concerns, such as tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Prince George: Transportation Options and Logistics

Prince George is easily accessible by road, air, and rail. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • By Air: The Prince George Airport serves several major airlines, including Air Canada, WestJet, and Central Mountain Air, with flights to major Canadian cities such as Vancouver, Calgary, and Edmonton.
  • By Road: Prince George is accessible from major highways, including the Yellowhead Highway (Highway 16) and the Cariboo Highway (Highway 97), making it easy to reach by car or bus.
  • By Rail: Via Rail Canada offers train service to Prince George, with stops in major Canadian cities such as Vancouver and Edmonton.

How to Get Around in Prince George

While having a car is the most convenient way to get around Prince George and the surrounding areas, there are other transportation options, including:

  • Public Transit: The Prince George Transit system offers bus service throughout the city, with fares starting at $2.50 CAD per ride.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available throughout the city, with fares starting at $3.50 CAD plus $1.90 CAD per kilometer.
  • Bike: The city has a growing network of bike lanes and trails, making it easy to get around on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Prince George: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Northern British Columbia, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Barkerville Historic Town: This well-preserved gold rush-era town is now a designated National Historic Site, and offers a fascinating glimpse into the history of the region.
  • Nechako River: This scenic river offers opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and rafting, with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Smithers: This charming town is known for its arts and culture scene, with a variety of galleries, museums, and festivals to explore.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of the region, learn about the local history and culture, or just enjoy some delicious cuisine and beverages, Prince George has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your trip to this beautiful Northern British Columbia city.
