Introduction to Kitwanga

Nestled in the picturesque valley of the Skeena River, Kitwanga offers a splendid and authentic experience of Northern British Columbia’s natural beauty and rich culture. It is a small, historic community situated on Highway 16, about 140 km east of Prince Rupert.

Kitwanga is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts as it boasts some of the best hiking/biking trails, camping spots, and fishing opportunities in BC. Besides, it is home to the Gitxsan First Nations, whose culture is deeply intertwined with Kitwanga’s history. Visitors can explore the First Nations’ museums, totems, villages, and witness traditional dances and ceremonies.

Visiting Kitwanga is undoubtedly a unique and enriching experience. This guide will help you plan your trip and discover all that Kitwanga has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kitwanga

To make the most of your trip to Kitwanga, check out this list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

‘Ksan Historical Village and Museum

Located in nearby Hazelton, ‘Ksan Historical Village and Museum is a living museum showcasing the Gitxsan First Nations’ lifestyle, traditions, and history. It features authentic totem poles, longhouses, art, and artifacts, and knowledgeable guides provide insight into the First Nations culture.

Gitwangak Battle Hill National Historic Site

Gitwangak Battle Hill National Historic Site is a place of significant cultural and historical value. The site commemorates the Gitxsan victory over the advancing Haida First Nations in the late 18th century. Visitors can explore the trails and enjoy panoramic views of the Gitwangak (Kitwanga) valley.

Natural Attractions of Kitwanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kitwanga Mountain Provincial Park

Kitwanga Mountain Provincial Park is a wilderness paradise for hikers, bikers, campers, and outdoor enthusiasts. The park boasts numerous trails that offer stunning views of the Skeena River valley, including the popular Vetter Falls Trail, which leads to a majestic waterfall.

Gitwangak Battle Hill Trail

If you are into hiking, the Gitwangak Battle Hill Trail is a must-try. The trail leads through pristine forests and meadows to the national historic site. It offers a challenging but rewarding experience, with panoramic views of the valley and the surrounding peaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kitwanga

Vetter Falls Trail

The Vetter Falls Trail is a moderate-level hiking trail that leads to a spectacular waterfall. The trail is 2.5 km long and offers scenic views of the Kitwanga River and the Skeena River valley.

Gitwangak Battle Hill Trail

As mentioned earlier, the Gitwangak Battle Hill Trail offers a challenging but breathtaking hiking experience. It is a 7.5 km round-trip that leads to the historic site and offers stunning views of the valley.

Best Things to Do in Kitwanga: Activities and Tours

Fishing

Kitwanga is known for its excellent fishing opportunities. The Kitwanga River boasts healthy populations of salmon, steelhead, and trout, among others. There are several fishing lodges and outfitters in the area that offer guided tours.

Cultural Tours and Workshops

Visitors to Kitwanga can participate in various cultural tours and workshops that offer insight into the First Nations’ history, traditions, arts, and crafts. The Gitxsan First Nations offers guided tours of their totems, museums, and cultural centers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kitwanga

If you have a week to spend in Kitwanga, here is an itinerary that covers most of the must-visit places and activities:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Kitwanga, check into your hotel, and explore the town.
  • Day 2: Visit ‘Ksan Historical Village and Museum.
  • Day 3: Hike the Gitwangak Battle Hill Trail.
  • Day 4: Go fishing on the Kitwanga River.
  • Day 5: Take a cultural tour of the Gitxsan villages and museums.
  • Day 6: Visit Kitwanga Mountain Provincial Park and hike the Vetter Falls Trail.
  • Day 7: Depart Kitwanga.

Food and Drink Scene of Kitwanga

The food and drink scene of Kitwanga is influenced by the First Nations’ cuisine and local produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Bannock

Bannock is a traditional First Nations’ bread that is fried or baked. It is typically served with salmon or other meats.

Sockeye Salmon

Sockeye salmon is a local delicacy and a significant part of the First Nations’ food culture. It can be served grilled, smoked, or baked.

Local Beverages

Spruce Beer

Spruce beer is a refreshing and unique drink made from spruce tree needles. It is a traditional First Nations’ beverage.

Haskap Berry Juice

Haskap berry is a native fruit that grows abundantly in the Kitwanga valley. Its juice is a healthy and delicious drink that is rich in antioxidants and vitamins.

What to Buy in Kitwanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kitwanga offers several unique and authentic souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts or reminders of your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • First Nations’ art and crafts, such as totem poles, carvings, and baskets.
  • Local produce, such as berries, jams, and syrups.
  • Outdoor gear, such as hiking and fishing equipment.

Local Culture and Customs of Kitwanga

The Kitwanga valley is home to the Gitxsan First Nations, who have inhabited the area for thousands of years. Their culture is deeply ingrained in the valley’s history and traditions. Visitors to Kitwanga should respect the First Nations’ customs and traditions and seek to learn more about their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Kitwanga

The best time to visit Kitwanga is from June to September when the weather is warm and dry, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall (September to October) offers stunning colors of the changing leaves, and the winter (November to March) offers excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kitwanga

To make your visit to Kitwanga a pleasant and respectful one, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the First Nations’ culture and traditions.
  • Seek permission before taking photos of the First Nations’ art and totems.
  • Explore the outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the First Nations’ customs and traditions.
  • Disturb wildlife or damage the environment.
  • Stray off the trails and into private property without permission.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kitwanga

Kitwanga is a safe and welcoming community. Still, visitors should take usual precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables. Hiking and camping in the backcountry should be done in pairs or groups, and visitors should carry bear spray and know how to use it.

Health in Kitwanga

Kitwanga has a small medical clinic and pharmacy for basic medical needs. Visitors with specific medical conditions should bring their medication with them. It is also advisable to carry insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Kitwanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kitwanga is located on Highway 16, also known as the Yellowhead Highway. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Kitwanga

The closest international airport to Kitwanga is the Prince George Airport, which is about 319 km away. The Smithers Regional Airport is about 73 km away and offers regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Kitwanga

Greyhound has a bus stop in Kitwanga that connects to other cities in British Columbia and Alberta.

Other Options

Kitwanga is located on the Canadian National Railway’s mainline, connecting to Prince Rupert and Prince George. The Skeena train runs through Kitwanga and offers a spectacular scenic route.

How to Get Around in Kitwanga

The best way to get around Kitwanga is by car or bike. There is no public transportation in the town, and most attractions and activities require transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kitwanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kitwanga is conveniently located near several other towns and attractions that are worth exploring. Here are some recommendations:

  • Prince Rupert: The port city of Prince Rupert offers stunning coastal views and a vibrant art scene. It is about a 2-hour drive from Kitwanga.
  • Terrace: Terrace is a hub for outdoor activities and offers excellent fishing, hiking, and skiing opportunities. It is about a 45-minute drive from Kitwanga.
  • Stewart: The charming town of Stewart is known for its historic buildings, museums, and proximity to the stunning Bear Glacier. It is about a 3-hour drive from Kitwanga.

Conclusion

Kitwanga is a unique and authentic destination that offers the best of Northern British Columbia’s natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a culture lover, Kitwanga has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this little slice of paradise in Northern BC.
