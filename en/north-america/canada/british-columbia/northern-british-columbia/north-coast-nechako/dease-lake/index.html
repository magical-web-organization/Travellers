Introduction to Dease Lake

Dease Lake is a small community located in North Coast-Nechako, Northern British Columbia, Canada. Surrounded by the stunning beauty of the province, this remote town offers a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. Dease Lake is situated on the shore of its namesake lake, which is one of the largest natural lakes in British Columbia.

With its unspoiled wilderness, Dease Lake is an outdoor lover’s paradise. Whether you enjoy hiking, fishing, or just taking in stunning scenic views, this remote town has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Dease Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dease Lake Museum - Located downtown near the lake, the museum contains historical artifacts of Dease Lake and the region, including displays of early pioneer and First Nations items.

  2. Ktunaxa Nation Historical Centre - Learn about the history, traditions and culture of the Ktunaxa Nation People at the interpretive centre in Cranbrook, about 3 hours drive away from Dease Lake.

Natural Attractions of Dease Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dease River Hot Springs - Soak in the natural hot springs alongside the serene Dease River. These remote hot springs are only accessible by foot or by boat, but the journey is well worth it.

  2. Mount Edziza Provincial Park - This stunning provincial park is home to Mount Edziza, an active volcano. The park also features a surreal landscape, including lava cones, hot springs, and glacial lakes. Hiking and camping are popular activities.

  3. Tahltan Bear Sanctuary - This bear sanctuary is located along the scenic Stewart-Cassiar Highway. It provides a unique opportunity to witness these majestic animals in their natural habitat. Be sure to book a guided tour in advance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dease Lake

  1. Mount Edziza Provincial Park Hiking Routes - Mount Edziza Provincial Park offers a range of hiking routes, from easy day hikes to multi-day backcountry trips. The park is also a popular destination for mountain biking.

  2. Dease Lake to Telegraph Creek Trail - This 125-kilometre trail follows the Stikine River and offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness. The trail can be completed in three to four days.

Best Things to Do in Dease Lake: Activities and Tours

  1. Fishing - The lakes and rivers around Dease Lake offer excellent fishing opportunities. Expect to catch northern pike, rainbow trout, and arctic grayling.

  2. Wildlife Watching - The remote wilderness surrounding Dease Lake is home to a range of animals, including moose, grizzly bears, and caribou. Book a guided wildlife tour to increase your chances of spotting these majestic creatures.

Food and Drink Scene of Dease Lake

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bannock Bread - This traditional Indigenous bread is a local favourite. It’s made with flour, baking powder, salt, and water, and can be served sweet or savoury.

  2. Wild-Caught Fish - Freshly caught fish from the surrounding lakes and rivers is a must-try when in Dease Lake. Expect to find northern pike and rainbow trout on the menu.

Local Beverages

  1. BC Breweries - Many local breweries are popping up in the region, offering a range of craft beers. Be sure to try the offerings from Three Ranges Brewing Company in Valemount, or the Wheelhouse Brewing Company in Prince Rupert.

Where to Stay in Dease Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Northway Motor Inn - This motel-style accommodation offers basic amenities and is located in the centre of Dease Lake.

  2. Cassiar RV Park - This RV park offers a range of accommodations, including RV spots, cabins, and tent sites.

What to Buy in Dease Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Traditional Indigenous Crafts - Look out for traditional First Nations crafts made by local artisans. Expect to find carvings, woven baskets, and handmade jewellery.

Local Culture and Customs of Dease Lake

The local Indigenous people respect their traditions, culture and laws. Visitors are encouraged to be mindful and respectful of their customs. Smoking or utilizing other substances in public places is not allowed, and visitors must be aware and be sensitive to the cultural norms of the people living in Dease Lake.

Best Time to Visit Dease Lake

The best time to visit Dease Lake is during the summer months of May to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are plentiful. However, be mindful of the bugs in June and July, which can be quite numerous.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dease Lake

Do:

  1. Respect the local Indigenous culture and traditions.
  2. Practice Leave No Trace principles when camping or hiking.
  3. Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  1. Smoke in public places.
  2. Disturb the wildlife.
  3. Ignore signs and warnings regarding potentially dangerous animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dease Lake

Dease Lake is a remote community, so visitors should exercise caution when exploring the wilderness. Bring appropriate clothing and gear when going on outdoor excursions, such as insect repellant, bear spray, and emergency communication devices. Always practice safe hiking, camping, and fishing practices.

Health in Dease Lake

There are no pharmacies in Dease Lake, so it’s important to bring any necessary medications with you. It’s also important to bring adequate water and food supplies, as the availability of supplies may be limited. Remember to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Dease Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dease Lake

The nearest airport to Dease Lake is the Dease Lake Airport.

Bus Stations Near Dease Lake

The town of Dease Lake does not have a bus station. The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in Smithers.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to reach Dease Lake. However, it’s important to note that many roads are unpaved, and it’s important to check road conditions before embarking on any journey.

How to Get Around in Dease Lake

The best way to get around Dease Lake is by car. There are no public transportation options, and many of the outdoor attractions are located outside of town.

Where to Go Next from Dease Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Alaska Highway - This iconic highway runs through British Columbia and Yukon, passing through stunning wilderness and historic towns.

  2. Stewart-Cassiar Highway - This highway offers stunning views of the surrounding wilderness and provides access to several remote Provincial Parks.

Conclusion

Dease Lake is a hidden gem in British Columbia, offering a range of outdoor activities and scenic beauty. Plan your trip carefully, and be sure to pack appropriately for your outdoor excursions. Respect the local Indigenous people and culture, and be sure to try some of the local cuisine and drink offerings. With its serene beauty and peaceful pace of life, Dease Lake is sure to be an unforgettable destination for any traveller.
