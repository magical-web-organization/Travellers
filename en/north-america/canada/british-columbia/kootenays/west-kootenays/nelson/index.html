Introduction to Nelson

Nelson is a charming small town located in the West Kootenays, in Kootenays, in British Columbia, in Canada, in North America. It is surrounded by mountains and a pristine lake, which make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, hikers, and bikers. This town is also known for its rich cultural heritage, historic architecture, vibrant arts scene, and homegrown food and drink culture.

List of Places to Visit in Nelson

Here is a list of some of the top-rated tourist attractions of Nelson:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Touchstones Nelson: Museum of Art and History
  • Nelson Courthouse: A historic courthouse turned museum
  • Kootenay Lake Historical Society Archives
  • West Kootenay Railway Station: A National Historic Site

Natural Attractions of Nelson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kokanee Glacier Provincial Park: a popular spot for hiking, camping, and wildlife watching
  • Nelson City Beach: A breathtaking lakeside beach area in the heart of Nelson
  • Columbia Mountains: A beautiful mountain range with awe-inspiring views
  • Nelson Community Forest: A forested area with hiking trails and picnic spots

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nelson

Nelson offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Nelson-Salmo Great Northern Trail: A 48 km trail that runs from Nelson to the neighboring town of Salmo
  • Kokanee Creek Provincial Park: A popular hiking spot with breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding mountains
  • Morning Mountain: A popular mountain biking trail with various difficulty levels

Best Things to Do in Nelson: Activities and Tours

Nelson offers plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors:

  • Whitewater Rafting on Slocan River: Experience the thrill of rafting on the Slocan River with some breathtaking views
  • Ainsworth Hot Springs Cave: A natural cave that features a warm underground stream
  • Kaslo Jazz Etc. Summer Music Festival: A three-day festival that takes place every year in August
  • The BC Ale Trail: Explore the best craft breweries in Nelson and the surrounding areas

Week-Long Itinerary for Nelson

Day 1 - Arrival and check-in at the hotel, Explore the town on foot, Visit the Touchstones Museum and the local markets

Day 2 - Hiking at the Kokanee Glacier Provincial Park, Lunch at the park, Explore the local craft breweries

Day 6 - Hiking at the Nelson Community Forest, Dinner at a local restaurant

Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Nelson

Nelson is known for its vibrant food and drink scene, which includes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian dish made of fries, gravy, and cheese
  • Kootenay Wild Mushroom Risotto: A local favorite
  • Oso Negro Coffee: A famous artisanal coffee roaster in the town

Local Beverages

  • The BC Ale Trail: A trail that covers the best breweries in Nelson and surrounding areas
  • Artisanal Wines: A selection of handcrafted wines from local vineyards
  • Oso Negro Coffee: A famous artisanal coffee roaster in the town

Where to Stay in Nelson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best options for accommodation in Nelson:

  • Prestige Lakeside Resort: A beautiful lakeside resort with modern amenities
  • Dancing Bear Inn: A cozy, affordable hostel with a central location
  • Hume Hotel: A historic, luxurious hotel in the heart of the town

What to Buy in Nelson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy while in Nelson:

  • Local crafts and artworks from the local markets and galleries
  • Artisanal chocolates and jams made from local produce
  • Handcrafted jewelry and pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Nelson

Nelson is a welcoming and diverse town, known for its vibrant arts scene, music festivals, and outdoor activities. The town respects the First Nations culture and has a strong commitment to sustainability and environmental conservation.

Best Time to Visit Nelson

The best time to visit Nelson is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The town also has a vibrant arts and music scene during the summer, with festivals and events taking place throughout the season. However, if you’re interested in winter sports, Nelson is also a popular destination during the winter months (December to February).

Do’s and Don’ts of Nelson

Do:

  • Visit the local markets and galleries to buy local crafts and artwork
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the town on foot or bike

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment
  • Ignore the safety guidelines while hiking or biking
  • Disrespect the First Nations culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Nelson

Nelson is a peaceful and safe town. However, like any other tourist destination, it’s important to stay alert and cautious at all times.

Health in Nelson

Medical facilities are available in Nelson, and it’s always advisable to carry your necessary medication while traveling.

Getting to Nelson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nelson

The nearest international airport to Nelson is the Castlegar Airport, located 35 km away from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Nelson

Greyhound Bus lines operate daily services to Nelson from Vancouver, Kelowna, Kamloops, and Calgary.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for travelers who prefer to drive at their leisure and explore the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Nelson

Buses, taxis, and bikes are readily available in Nelson, and it’s easy to navigate the town on foot.

Where to Go Next from Nelson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Nelson has plenty to offer, here are some of the nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Revelstoke: A beautiful mountain town known for its winter sports and outdoor activities
  • Rossland: A charming, heritage town known for its historic architecture and vibrant arts scene
  • Kaslo: A historic town known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities

Conclusion

Nelson is a beautiful town that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, vibrant arts scene, and homegrown food and drink culture. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, rafting, or exploring local galleries, markets, and cafes, Nelson has something for everyone. So plan your perfect trip to this stunning town and experience the real beauty of British Columbia.
