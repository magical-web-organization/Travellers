Introduction to Nakusp

Located in the heart of the West Kootenays region of British Columbia, Nakusp is a picturesque town surrounded by majestic mountains, crystal-clear lakes, and dense forests. From cultural landmarks to scenic vistas, Nakusp offers plenty of opportunities for exploration and adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Nakusp including must-visit attractions, trails for hiking and biking, delicious local cuisine, places to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Nakusp

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nakusp Museum: Learn about the town’s rich history and culture by visiting the Nakusp Museum, which features exhibits on the area’s indigenous peoples, European settlers, and more.

Natural Attractions of Nakusp: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hot Springs: Indulge in the natural hot springs that bubble up from the surrounding mountains.
  • Kuskanax Creek Footbridge: Experience the scenic views at Kuskanax Creek Footbridge, which spans the creek and meanders through the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakusp

  • Rosehip Trail: This easy 2km trail is perfect for novice hikers and bikers. Spot wildlife such as deer, moose, and bear along the way.
  • Mount Abriel: For experienced hikers, Mount Abriel offers a challenging trek with stunning views from the top.

Best Things to Do in Nakusp: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take a guided tour or rent your own canoe or kayak and explore Nakusp’s waters.
  • Fishing: Whether you’re a seasoned angler or a beginner, you can enjoy fishing for rainbow trout, Dolly Varden, and Kokanee Salmon in the area’s rivers and lakes.
  • Sailing: Take in the mountain views while cruising on the Upper Arrow Lake through the beautiful Selkirk Mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakusp

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mild Sausage: This local delicacy combines pork meat and spices like nutmeg and garlic to make a tasty sausage.
  • Bannock: A traditional Indigenous bread, which can be enjoyed plain, as a side dish, or sweetened with berries.

Local Beverages

  • Nakusp Beer: Enjoy a locally brewed beer at the Arrowhead Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Visit Skimmerhorn Winery and Sample some of the region’s finest wines.

Where to Stay in Nakusp: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Halcyon Hot Springs Resort: Stay in one of the resort’s cabins or hotel rooms and enjoy the hot springs.
  • Nakusp Hostel: Budget-friendly accommodations perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Nakusp: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade jewelry and pottery: Visit the local artisan shops to purchase unique jewelry and pottery made by local artists.
  • Fruit and Vegetable produce: Purchase baskets of fruit and vegetables at the Nakusp farmer’s market every Saturday from May to October.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakusp

Nakusp is situated on traditional territories of the Sinixt and Ktunaxa First Nations. Understand that when visiting this region, respect for their traditions and culture is a must.

Best Time to Visit Nakusp

Nakusp has something to offer year-round, but the best time to visit is from June to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed to the fullest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakusp

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Nakusp area.
  • Plan ahead and book accommodations and activities in advance.
  • Prepare for the outdoors with proper gear and clothing.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the environment.
  • Disturb the wildlife or their habitats.
  • Trespass onto Indigenous lands without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakusp

  • Make sure to have bear spray when hiking or camping.
  • Always follow the directions of your guide or park rangers when exploring outdoors.

Health in Nakusp

  • Stay hydrated while enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against the sun and bugs.

Getting to Nakusp: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nakusp

  • Kelowna International Airport
  • Spokane International Airport

Bus Stations Near Nakusp

  • Arrow and Slocan Lakes Community Service
  • Greyhound Canada

Other Options

  • Self-Drive: A car rental or personal car is a great way to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Nakusp

  • Walking: Nakusp is pedestrian-friendly, and you can explore the town on foot.
  • Biking: Rent a bike or bring your own to explore the trails.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great choice if you plan on travelling further from the town.

Where to Go Next from Nakusp: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Castlegar: Explore galleries and museums or go skiing on the mountains surrounding the city.
  • Kelowna: One of the most popular tourist destinations in the area, with world-class wineries and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Nakusp is a beautiful small town with a wealth of natural and cultural attractions to explore. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, a foodie, or simply seeking relaxation, Nakusp has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Nakusp, and be sure to take part in the country’s cultural richness.
