Introduction to Greenwood

Greenwood is a small town located in the West Kootenays region of British Columbia, Canada. Known as the smallest city in Canada, Greenwood has a rich history that dates back to the late 1800s, during the gold rush era. Nowadays, Greenwood is a popular destination for tourists looking to explore the town’s natural beauty and cultural significance. Here’s a guide to discovering the best things to do, sights to see, places to stay, and more in Greenwood, British Columbia.

List of Places to Visit in Greenwood

Whether you’re interested in learning about the town’s history, hiking through its picturesque trails, or exploring the local art scene, there’s something for everyone in Greenwood. Below is a list of places to visit in Greenwood.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Greenwood has a deep-rooted history that you can experience through visiting its museums and historical landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit places for history buffs:

  • (1) Greenwood Museum: The Greenwood Museum showcases artifacts and memorabilia from the town’s mining, forestry, and agriculture industries.
  • (2) Boundary Museum & Interpretive Centre: The Boundary Museum & Interpretive Centre, housed in a historic railway station, provides insights into the town’s past with exhibits on mining, railroads, and Indigenous people.

Natural Attractions of Greenwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Greenwood also boasts of stunning natural attractions that include scenic vistas, parks, and trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular natural attractions in Greenwood:

  • (1) Broader View Park: This park is located on a hill overlooking Greenwood town, providing a panoramic view of the surrounding area.
  • (2) Boundary Creek Provincial Park: This provincial park offers hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and picnic areas on the banks of the Kettle River.
  • (3) Phoenix Mountain: A skiing and hiking destination located near Greenwood, Phoenix Mountain features more than 24 kilometers of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greenwood

Greenwood has diverse opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with numerous trails that cater to different levels of expertise. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Greenwood:

  • (1) Phoenix Trail: This trail around Phoenix Mountain offers fantastic views of the landscape and wildlife and is suitable for both hiking and mountain biking.
  • (2) Rock Candy Mountain Trail: Located north of Greenwood, this trail leads to the top of a peak and offers panoramic views of the surrounding region.
  • (3) Trans Canada Trail: The Trans Canada Trail, a former rail line, is accessible from Greenwood. It’s a great route for hikers and bikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Greenwood: Activities and Tours

Greenwood is packed with exciting things to do for visitors of all ages, from outdoor adventures to cultural events. Here are some of the best things to do in Greenwood:

  • (1) Take a walking tour of the town centre to learn about its history and architecture.
  • (2) Visit Greenwood’s galleries and studios to discover its vibrant art scene.
  • (3) Take a guided fishing trip to the Kettle River or the beautiful Jewel Lake.
  • (4) Attend Music in the Park, a free weekly concert series that runs during the summer months.

Food and Drink Scene of Greenwood

Greenwood offers a range of options for visitors looking to try the local cuisine and beverages. From local breweries to classic Canadian cuisine, here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Greenwood:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Poutine: A quintessential Canadian dish made from fries and cheese curds, covered in gravy.
  • (2) Kootenay Sourdough Breads: These artisanal loaves are made locally and are a must-try for bread lovers.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Boundary Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers, ciders, and wines.
  • (2) Kootenay Meadows Farm: Known for its organic dairy products, Kootenay Meadows Farm also produces fresh, organic juices and milkshakes.

Where to Stay in Greenwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Greenwood offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Greenwood:

  • (1) Greenwood Motel & RV Park: This motel offers budget-friendly accommodation with RV options and is located near the downtown area.
  • (2) The Deadwood Lodge: This lodge offers comfortable accommodation with a rustic atmosphere, located near Phoenix Mountain and Boundary Creek Provincial Park.

What to Buy in Greenwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Greenwood offers several unique souvenirs and products, from handmade crafts to organic produce. Here are some things to consider buying in Greenwood:

  • (1) Artisanal bread from Kootenay Sourdough Breads
  • (2) Handmade pottery and jewelry from local artists
  • (3) Organic dairy products, juices, and milkshakes from Kootenay Meadows Farm

Local Culture and Customs of Greenwood

Greenwood has a diverse culture that reflects both its historical roots and modern influences. Visitors to Greenwood should be respectful of its customs and traditions, such as the annual Canada Day parade and traditional Indigenous ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Greenwood

The best time to visit Greenwood is in the summer, between June and September, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greenwood

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Greenwood:

Do:

  • (1) Take a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of the town.
  • (2) Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • (3) Enjoy the scenery and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • (1) Litter or damage the environment.
  • (2) Disrespect the town’s history and architecture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greenwood

Greenwood is generally a safe town to visit. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or biking in the wilderness. It’s recommended to carry a map and bear spray when exploring the trails.

Health in Greenwood

Greenwood has medical facilities available, including a hospital and a medical clinic.

Getting to Greenwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greenwood

The closest international airport to Greenwood is the Spokane International Airport, located in Spokane, Washington, about a two-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Greenwood

Greyhound Bus operates regular bus services between Greenwood and surrounding cities such as Grand Forks and Castlegar.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Greenwood or take a car rental from the nearest big city.

How to Get Around in Greenwood

Greenwood is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bikes or cars from the town’s rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Greenwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Greenwood is located in the heart of Kootenay, with several attractions nearby for visitors to explore, such as the Ainsworth Hot Springs and the Doukhobor Discovery Centre.

Conclusion

Greenwood, British Columbia, is a hidden gem that offers spectacular natural beauty, rich history, and a vibrant local culture. Whether you’re looking to hike scenic trails, explore the art scene or taste the local cuisine, you’ll find something to suit your interests in Greenwood. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and let the adventure begin!
