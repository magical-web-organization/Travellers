Introduction to Field, BC

Field is a small unincorporated community located in the Kootenays region of British Columbia, Canada. The town is situated at the western entrance to Yoho National Park, and is a popular tourist destination for those seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Field is located about 276 km from Calgary and 232 km from Banff, making it an ideal stop for those exploring the Canadian Rockies.

List of Places to Visit in Field, BC

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Burgess Shale Geoscience Foundation: This museum offers exhibits on the Burgess Shale fossils, and showcases the importance and impact of these 500 million-year-old discoveries.

  • The Kicking Horse Pass National Historic Site: This site celebrates the completion of the Canadian Pacific Railway and the ceremonial driving of the last spike in 1885.

Natural Attractions of Field: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yoho National Park: This national park offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and fishing. The park also has stunning natural attractions such as glaciers, waterfalls, and forests.

  • Takakkaw Falls: This stunning waterfall offers a 254-meter drop, making it one of the highest falls in North America.

  • Emerald Lake: This lake has a beautiful emerald color and is surrounded by mountains and forests. Visitors can kayak, hike, or simply enjoy the view from the shore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Field, BC

  • The Iceline Trail: This trail offers a stunning view of the Yoho Valley and the glaciers that surround it.

  • The Burgess Pass Trail: This trail leads hikers to the Burgess Shale fossils site. The trail is rated as intermediate level and takes around 5-6 hours to complete.

  • The Wapta Falls Trail: This trail leads hikers to a stunning waterfall in the park and is relatively easy for most skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Field, BC: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: Field offers several operators who provide half and full-day guided rafting trips on the Kicking Horse River.

  • Helicopter Tours: Visitors can enjoy a scenic helicopter tour of the Canadian Rockies and get a bird’s eye view of the majestic mountains and glaciers.

  • Horseback Riding: Several operators provide guided horseback rides through the stunning backcountry of the Canadian Rockies.

Food and Drink Scene in Field, BC

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: This Canadian classic is a dish of French fries, gravy, and cheese curds, and can be found in most restaurants.

  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: Not for the faint of heart, this dish comprises of deep-fried bull testicles and is a regional delicacy.

  • Wild Salmon: British Columbia is famous for its wild salmon cuisine, and most local restaurants serve smoked or grilled salmon dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Kicking Horse Coffee: This coffee roaster is based in Field and offers a range of organic blends that have won several awards worldwide.

  • Craft Beer: Several microbreweries have opened up in British Columbia in recent years, offering a range of craft beers and ales.

Where to Stay in Field, BC: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Emerald Lake Lodge: This historic lodge is located on Emerald Lake and offers comfortable rooms, fine dining, and stunning views of the Rockies.

  • Hostelling International: Field has a youth hostel that offers budget-friendly accommodation for backpackers and solo travelers.

  • Canadian Rocky Mountain Resorts: This chain of resorts offers luxury accommodations in the Rockies, including the famous Buffalo Mountain Lodge.

What to Buy in Field, BC: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yoho National Park Souvenirs: The park gift shop stocks a range of souvenirs such as postcards, t-shirts, and stuffed animals.

  • Local Honey: British Columbia is known for its honey production, and several outlets stock raw honey and beeswax candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Field, BC

Field is a small community, and locals take pride in preserving their natural surroundings. Visitors are expected to respect the environment and follow the principles of Leave No Trace.

Best Time to Visit Field, BC

The best time to visit Field is in the summer, between June to September. The weather is pleasant and the hiking trails are open during this time. Winter can be harsh with heavy snowfall, making it unsuitable for most outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Field, BC

Do:

  • Respect the environment and follow the principles of Leave No Trace.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and bring adequate gear for outdoor activities.

  • Try the local delicacies, such as poutine and wild salmon.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Ignore the safety rules while undertaking adventure activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Field, BC

Field is a safe community, but visitors are always advised to take necessary precautions while exploring the outdoors. Check for wildlife warnings and obey safety signs on hiking trails.

Health in Field, BC

Field does not have a hospital, but there is a health center that can treat minor injuries and illnesses. Visitors should carry adequate health insurance and familiarize themselves with medical facilities in neighboring towns.

Getting to Field, BC: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Field, BC

The closest international airport to Field is Calgary International Airport, which is 276 km away from Field.

Bus Stations Near Field, BC

Most buses to Field stop at the neighbouring towns of Banff and Lake Louise.

Other Options

Visitors can rent cars or hire taxis from Calgary or Banff to reach Field.

How to Get Around in Field, BC

Most of Field’s attractions are within walking distance, and visitors can explore the town on foot. However, for those looking to venture out into the mountains, it is recommended to rent a car or sign up for guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Field, BC: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Field is situated in the Kootenays region, which is known for its stunning landscapes and adventure activities. Visitors can explore nearby towns such as Banff, Lake Louise, and Revelstoke, or visit other national parks such as Jasper or Glacier National Park.

Conclusion

Field, BC is an ideal destination for those looking to explore the Canadian Rockies and experience the great outdoors. With its stunning natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and adventure activities, Field promises visitors an unforgettable experience.
