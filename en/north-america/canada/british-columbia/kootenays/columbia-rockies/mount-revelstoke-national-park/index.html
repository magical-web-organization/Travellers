Mount Revelstoke National Park is located in the Columbia-Rockies region of British Columbia, Canada. It is a popular tourist destination throughout the year, offering stunning scenic views and a range of outdoor activities. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Mount Revelstoke National Park, including places to visit, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, and more.

Introduction to Mount Revelstoke National Park

Mount Revelstoke National Park is a stunning natural wilderness that offers breathtaking panoramic views of the towering mountains, glittering glaciers, and pristine lakes. The park spans over an area of 260 square kilometres and has a rich cultural heritage, including the history of Indigenous Peoples and early explorers. Some of the popular attractions in the park include the Meadows in the Sky Parkway, the Giant Cedars Boardwalk, and the Nels Nelsen Ski Jump.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Revelstoke National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Revelstoke Railway Museum: The museum houses a collection of railway artifacts, including locomotives, rolling stock, and railway equipment from the Canadian Pacific Railway and the Canadian National Railway.
  2. The Revelstoke Museum and Archives: The museum showcases the history of the region, including early settlement, the timber industry, and mining operations.
  3. The First Peoples Gallery: The Gallery showcases the cultural heritage of the Ktunaxa peoples, including their way of life, art, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Mount Revelstoke National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Meadows in the Sky Parkway: The 26 km scenic drive takes visitors through forests, meadows, and alpine flower gardens, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Giant Cedars Boardwalk: The boardwalk takes visitors through the ancient cedar forest, towering up to 60 meters in height.
  3. Nels Nelsen Ski Jump: The historic ski jump is a must-visit for anyone interested in skiing or snowboarding history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Revelstoke National Park

  1. Summit Trail: The 1.4 km trail is a challenging hike that leads visitors to the summit of Mount Revelstoke, offering scenic views of the surrounding area.
  2. Eva Lake Trail: The 7.4 km hike takes visitors through the meadows and alpine flower gardens, leading to the pristine Eva Lake.
  3. Jade Lakes Trail: The 13 km trail takes visitors through the dense forests and meadows, and past a chain of sparkling emerald lakes.

Best Things to Do in Mount Revelstoke National Park: Activities and Tours

  1. Camping: The park offers a range of camping options, including car camping, backcountry camping, and RV camping.
  2. Fishing: Visitors can fish for trout in the pristine lakes and rivers of Mount Revelstoke National Park.
  3. Skiing: The park has a ski area, which offers a range of skiing and snowboarding trails.
  4. Photography: Mount Revelstoke National Park is a photographer’s paradise, offering stunning views of the natural wilderness.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Revelstoke National Park

Day 1-2: Visit the cultural attractions such as the Revelstoke Railway Museum, the Revelstoke Museum and Archives, and the First Peoples Gallery.

Day 3-4: Explore the natural attractions, such as the Meadows in the Sky Parkway, Giant Cedars Boardwalk, and Nels Nelsen Ski Jump.

Day 5-6: Discover the hiking/biking routes such as the Summit Trail, Eva Lake Trail, and Jade Lakes Trail.

Day 7: Enjoy the outdoor activities such as camping, fishing, and skiing.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Revelstoke National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Poutine: A Canadian classic, poutine is a dish of hot fries topped with cheese curds and gravy.
  2. BeaverTails: A popular Canadian street food, it is a pastry that is stretched in the shape of a beaver’s tail and topped with cinnamon, sugar, and other toppings.
  3. Smoked Salmon: A local delicacy, smoked salmon is served as an appetizer, in a sandwich or as a main course.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft beer: Revelstoke is known for its craft breweries, offering a range of beers and ales.
  2. Icewine: A specialty of British Columbia, icewine is a sweet dessert wine made from grapes frozen on the vine.

Where to Stay in Mount Revelstoke National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sutton Place Hotel Revelstoke Mountain Resort: A luxurious hotel located at the base of Revelstoke Mountain Resort.
  2. Glacier House Resort: A rustic, eco-friendly resort offering cottages, cabins, and chalets.
  3. Revelstoke Lodge: A budget-friendly option located in the center of Revelstoke.

What to Buy in Mount Revelstoke National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Local art: The park offers a range of local artwork, including paintings, photographs, and sculptures.
  2. Handmade crafts: Visitors can purchase handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Revelstoke National Park

Visitors should be respectful of Indigenous Peoples and their culture. It’s important to learn about their traditions and customs, as well as the park’s rules and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Mount Revelstoke National Park

The best time to visit Mount Revelstoke National Park is from June to September, when the weather is warm and the hiking trails are open. However, winter sports enthusiasts can enjoy skiing and snowboarding from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Revelstoke National Park

Do:

  1. Respect the wildlife and their habitat.
  2. Pack out what you pack in.
  3. Follow the park’s rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  1. Feed the wildlife.
  2. Leave trash in the wilderness.
  3. Disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Revelstoke National Park

  1. Bring appropriate clothing, safety gear, and equipment for your outdoor activities.
  2. Stay on designated hiking trails.
  3. Be aware of wildlife and their behavior.

Health in Mount Revelstoke National Park

  1. Bring necessary medications and first aid supplies.
  2. Stay hydrated and bring enough water for your trips.
  3. Avoid hiking or engaging in outdoor activities when you are sick.

Getting to Mount Revelstoke National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Revelstoke National Park

  1. Kelowna International Airport: 240 km away from Mount Revelstoke National Park.
  2. Calgary International Airport: 400 km away from Mount Revelstoke National Park.

Bus Stations Near Mount Revelstoke National Park

  1. Greyhound Canada: offers services to Revelstoke.
  2. BC Transit buses: services are available within Revelstoke.

Other Options

  1. Car/rental car: The easiest and most convenient way to get to Mount Revelstoke National Park.
  2. Shuttle/taxi services: Some shuttle and taxi services are available in Revelstoke.

How to Get Around in Mount Revelstoke National Park

The best way to get around Mount Revelstoke National Park is by car. However, visitors can also explore the park on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Mount Revelstoke National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Glacier National Park: Located adjacent to Mount Revelstoke National Park, Glacier National Park offers a range of hiking trails, wildlife viewing opportunities, and scenic drives.
  2. Banff National Park: A world-renowned national park located in Alberta, Banff National Park offers stunning scenic views, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
