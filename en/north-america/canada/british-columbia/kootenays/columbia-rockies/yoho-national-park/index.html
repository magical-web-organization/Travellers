Introduction to Yoho National Park

Yoho National Park is located in the Columbia-Rockies region of British Columbia, Canada, and is a part of the larger Canadian Rocky Mountains Parks UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for its snow-capped mountain peaks, glaciers, alpine lakes, and waterfalls, Yoho offers numerous outdoor adventures and scenic views that attract thousands of visitors every year.

If you’re planning a trip to Yoho National Park, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip to Yoho National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Yoho National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Yoho National Park is home to several culturally significant attractions that offer a glimpse into the area’s history and culture. Here are some must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Field Visitor Information Centre: Located in the town of Field, this center provides information about the park’s history, wildlife, and geology, as well as a gift shop.
  • Burgess Shale: This UNESCO World Heritage Site features some of the world’s oldest and best-preserved fossils of marine animals from the Middle Cambrian era.
  • Wapta Falls: The falls was a historical site for the Stoney-Nakoda First Nations people, and there more than just a surge of beauty and pristine water here.

Natural Attractions of Yoho National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Yoho’s natural attractions are what draw most visitors to the park, and you’ll find plenty of opportunities for adventure and scenic sightseeing. Here are some of the park’s most popular natural attractions:

  • Emerald Lake: This stunning turquoise-colored glacial lake is a must-see destination and provides fantastic opportunities for boating, fishing, hiking, and photography.
  • Takakkaw Falls: This 373-meter waterfall is the third-highest in Canada and is a popular spot for picnicking and panoramic views.
  • Lake O’Hara: This pristine alpine lake is accessible only by shuttle bus or a strenuous 11-kilometer hike, making it a secluded paradise for hiking, camping, and nature photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yoho National Park

Yoho National Park offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that cater to all skill levels. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  • Iceline Trail: This 20-kilometer trail is considered one of the park’s best hiking trails and offers stunning views of mountain peaks, glaciers, and alpine lakes.
  • Burgess Pass: This 8.5-kilometer hike starts at the famous Burgess Shale fossil site and provides breathtaking views of the Rockies.
  • Great Divide Trail: This 29-kilometer trail takes you through stunning natural scenery and offers an excellent opportunity to spot some of the park’s wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Yoho National Park: Activities and Tours

Yoho National Park has no shortage of activities and unique experiences to fill your itinerary. Here are some things to do:

  • White-Water Rafting: Enjoy a thrilling white-water rafting adventure down the Kicking Horse River and see the park’s natural beauty from a different perspective.
  • Horseback Riding: Discover the park’s stunning natural beauty on horseback with guided tours and trail rides that cater to all skill levels.
  • Stargazing Tours: Experience the park’s majestic night sky while amateur astronomers guide you through telescopes to explore the constellations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yoho National Park

Here is a sample week-long itinerary to help you plan your visit to Yoho National Park and get the most out of your trip.

Day 1: Check-in at a hotel/hostel and visit the Field Visitor Information Centre. If you’re looking for a quick hike in the evening, walk around the Emerald Lake Circuit.

Day 2: Drive to Takakkaw Falls to take in the breathtaking views and enjoy a picnic lunch. Spend the afternoon hiking the Iceline Trail.

Day 3: Take a guided horseback ride through the park’s picturesque backcountry, followed by an afternoon dip in the glacier-fed Emerald Lake.

Day 4: Enjoy scenic drives along the Trans-Canada Highway to explore the park’s scenic viewpoints and stop by the Wapta Falls historical site.

Day 5: Join a stargazing tour on the shores of Lake O’Hara to see the park’s natural light show.

Day 6: Embark on a whitewater rafting adventure down the Kicking Horse River with an experienced guide and end the day with al fresco dinner and sharing campfires alongside other adventure seekers.

Day 7: Take a shuttle bus up to Lake O’Hara and spend the day exploring the pristine alpine wilderness and its vast trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Yoho National Park

While Yoho National Park may not offer a wide variety of dining options, there are plenty of places to try locally sourced, traditional Canadian dishes that are sure to delight your taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A delicious Quebecois dish whose ingredients include fries, gravy, and cheese curds
  • Smoked salmon: Freshly caught fish cooked in a classic Pacific Coast style
  • Beavertails: Fried dough with sweet or savory toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Canadian beer: Local breweries offer a variety of beer types, from light lagers to heavier ales.
  • Ice wine: A popular Canadian dessert wine made with grapes left on the vine until they freeze, resulting in a sweet and distinct flavor.

Where to Stay in Yoho National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in and around Yoho National Park that cater to all budgets and travel styles.

  • Emerald Lake Lodge: A luxurious mountain lodge located on the shores of Emerald Lake, complete with outdoor hot tubs and a lakeside restaurant.
  • Alpine Club of Canada - Wates-Gibson Hut: A rustic backcountry hut perched atop the mountains and a perfect home base for hikers and backcountry adventures.
  • The Whiskey Jack Hostel: A cozy and affordable hostel located in the town of Field and a great option for budget-minded travelers.

What to Buy in Yoho National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yoho National Park has several shops where visitors can buy local souvenirs, including:

  • Gallery at Emerald lake: A store next to Emerald Lake Lodge that offers handcrafted Canadian art and souvenirs.
  • Truffle Pigs Bistro and Lodge Gift Shop: A boutique store offering souvenirs, clothing, and handmade crafts.
  • Canadian Rocky Mountain Resorts: The resort gift shops offer a range of unique and locally produced mementos for visitors

Local Culture and Customs of Yoho National Park

The Canadian Rockies and Yoho National Park have a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the First Nations people. Respect and observe their customs and ceremonies, like buffalo hunting and Pow Wow celebrations, while exploring the park.

Best Time to Visit Yoho National Park

Yoho National Park is open all year round, but the best time to visit depends on what activities you’re most interested in. May to September offers the best weather conditions for hiking and biking, while November to March is ideal for ice skating and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yoho National Park

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and keep a safe distance
  • Observe the Leave No Trace principles to protect the natural environment
  • Be prepared for weather changes and always carry essential gear.

Don’t:

  • Feed or approach wildlife, and do not litter.
  • Take any animals or plants from the park and engage in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Yoho National Park

Yoho National Park is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s important to take precautions when out in the wilderness. Here are some safety tips:

  • Carry bear spray and know how to use it
  • Stay on designated trails
  • Follow the park’s fire safety regulations.

Health in Yoho National Park

While exploring parks, visitors should remain hydrated at all times, protect against insect bites and know how to identify and treat (pre-existing) medical conditions. No emergency health facility is available within the park, but Field Medical Clinic, located close, can serve minor to difficult medical cases.

Getting to Yoho National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yoho National Park

Calgary International Airport is the nearest international airport to Yoho National Park, located around 3 hours’ drive away.

Bus Stations Near Yoho National Park

Greyhound offers several routes that run from Calgary to various cities close to the park, including Golden, Banff, and Jasper. Parks Canada offers shuttle bus services within Yoho National Park as well during peak seasons.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Yoho National Park is accessible via the Trans-Canada Highway 1, which runs through the middle of the park.

How to Get Around in Yoho National Park

The best way to get around Yoho National Park is by car, bus, bike, or foot. Rental car services are available in major cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Yoho National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to keep you busy after your visit to Yoho National Park, including:

  • Banff National Park (1.5 hours’ drive away)
  • Jasper National Park (3 hours’ drive away)
  • Lake Louise (40 minutes’ drive away)
  • Kananaskis Country (2 hours’ drive away)

Conclusion

Visiting Yoho National Park is an experience of a lifetime, offering visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in some of North America’s most breathtaking natural scenery. With the right amount of planning and preparation, you can make the most out of your trip to Yoho National Park and create unforgettable memories.
