Introduction to Golden

Golden is a small town located in the eastern part of British Columbia, Canada. The town is nestled in the Columbia River Valley, surrounded by stunning mountains, pristine waterways, and an abundance of wildlife. The town is a popular gateway to the Canadian Rockies and attracts visitors from around the world for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Golden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Golden and District Museum: Learn about the history of the town, from the early days of the First Nations People to the present day through various exhibits and artifacts.
  • Kicking Horse Pedestrian Bridge: A unique, historic suspension bridge made of wood, it offers spectacular views of the Kicking Horse River.
  • Golden Cinema: A local cinema that screens current movies and art-house films.

Natural Attractions of Golden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kicking Horse Mountain Resort: A popular ski resort located in the Purcell Range of the Rocky Mountains. In summer, it offers scenic chairlift rides, hiking, and mountain biking trails.
  • Yoho National Park: Just a short drive from Golden, Yoho National Park offers spectacular vistas of waterfalls, glaciers, mountains, and forests.
  • Columbia River Wetlands: A unique ecosystem comprising marshes, basins, lakes, and channels. You can kayak, canoe and hike around the wetlands to see a wide variety of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Golden

Golden offers numerous hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging trekking routes. Some popular routes include:

  • Kicking Horse River Trail: A flat and scenic trail that follows the Kicking Horse River, starting from Golden.
  • Mount 7 Trails: An extensive network of trails starting from the top of Mount 7 with a range of options for all levels of hikers.
  • Mummery Glacier: A strenuous route leading to a spectacular glacier that traverses steep ridges and boulder fields.

Best Things to Do in Golden: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: Enjoy a thrilling rafting experience on the wild and scenic Kicking Horse River.
  • Helicopter Tours: Take a scenic helicopter tour to admire the breath-taking landscapes of the Canadian Rockies.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic beauty of the trails on horseback, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Golden

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A classic Canadian dish of fries, gravy and cheese curds.
  • Elk Burgers: A local delicacy, made from locally-sourced elk meat.
  • Rocky Mountain Trout: Freshly caught in the nearby streams and served in various styles.

Local Beverages

  • BC Wine: Enjoy a glass of local wine, produced from fruits grown in BC’s Okanagan Valley.
  • Kicking Horse Coffee: A local coffee roaster that offers strong, rich coffee, made from fair-trade beans.

Where to Stay in Golden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Golden, ranging from luxurious hotels to backpacker hostels. Some popular options include:

  • Kicking Horse River Lodge: A cozy lodge situated on the banks of the Kicking Horse River, offering comfortable rooms and a beautiful setting.
  • Prestige Inn Golden: A modern hotel located close to the town center, offering clean and spacious rooms.
  • HI Golden: A budget hostel that offers dormitory-style accommodation for backpackers and travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Golden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artisan Jewelry: Handcrafted jewelry made by local artists using natural materials like stones, beads and wood.
  • Honey and jams: Locally made honey and jams using fresh fruits from the region.
  • Handmade Pottery: Unique pottery items made by local potters and artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Golden

Golden has a rich culture and history, dating back to the First Nations people who first inhabited the region. The town is proud of its heritage, and you can experience it through the various cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Golden

Golden is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm and attractions. However, the months from June to August are the most popular due to pleasant weather and abundant outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Golden

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the town.
  • Take care of the natural environment, as Golden is located amidst pristine mountain and river ecosystems.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and try new dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety precautions while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local people or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Golden

Golden is a safe town, but as with any outdoor destination, visitors should take necessary safety precautions, such as:

  • Wearing appropriate gear while participating in outdoor activities like hiking and mountain biking.
  • Being aware of wildlife and keeping a safe distance.
  • Following safety guidelines while participating in water sports.

Health in Golden

Golden has a small hospital and a few clinics that offer medical services. Visitors should also take care of their health by carrying any necessary medications and health insurance.

Getting to Golden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Golden is accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Golden

  • Calgary International Airport (YYC) is the closest major airport, located approximately three hours away.
  • Kelowna International Airport (YLW) is located approximately four hours away.

Bus Stations Near Golden

  • Greyhound Canada offers bus services to and from Golden.

Other Options

  • Golden is easily accessible by car, with a well-maintained highway taking travelers from the east and west.
  • Via Rail also operates a train service, connecting Golden with various cities in British Columbia and Alberta.

How to Get Around in Golden

The best way to get around in Golden is by car. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or use taxi services to explore the town and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Golden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Banff National Park: Located just a few hours away, Banff offers stunning vistas of mountains, lakes, and glaciers.
  • Glacier National Park: Located a short drive away, Glacier National Park offers beautiful alpine scenery and a chance to spot wildlife.
  • Revelstoke: A small, charming town located nearby, Revelstoke offers a peaceful break from the hustle and bustle of a larger town.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Golden, British Columbia, is a beautiful and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. Visitors can explore the scenic beauty, thrilling outdoor activities, cultural and historical attractions, and enjoy the local cuisine and culture. By following the dos and don’ts of the town and taking necessary safety precautions, visitors can have a memorable and safe trip to Golden.
