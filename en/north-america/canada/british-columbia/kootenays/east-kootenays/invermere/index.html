Your Ultimate Guide to Invermere, Canada

Invermere is a delightful town located in the heart of the Rockies, in the province of British Columbia, Canada. With its pristine natural beauty, abundant wildlife, and world-class outdoor recreation, Invermere is a perfect getaway for adventure enthusiasts, history lovers, and culture seekers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have put together everything you need to know about Invermere, from the top places to visit and things to do to the best restaurants, accommodations, and local products to bring home.

List of Places to Visit in Invermere

Invermere is home to a range of culturally important attractions and natural wonders that will make your trip unforgettable. Here are the top places to visit in Invermere:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Windermere Valley Museum - Discover the history of the valley and its pioneers through interactive exhibits, photographs, and artifacts.
  2. Invermere Visitor Centre - Get information about local events, activities, and businesses, and learn about the history and culture of Invermere.
  3. Pynelogs Cultural Centre - This historic building houses art exhibitions, concerts, and cultural events in the heart of Invermere.

Natural Attractions of Invermere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Radium Hot Springs - Soak in the mineral-rich waters of this natural hot springs and take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Kootenay National Park - Immerse yourself in the wilderness and discover the beauty of the Rocky Mountains with numerous hiking trails, camping options, and picnic areas.
  3. Lake Windermere - Enjoy the crystal-clear waters of this beautiful lake with activities such as kayaking, paddle-boarding, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Invermere

Invermere is a paradise for hikers, bikers, and outdoor enthusiasts. The town boasts of a range of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels and interests. Here are our top picks for hiking and biking in Invermere:

  1. Mount Swansea - This hike will reward you with panoramic views of the valley and Rocky Mountains and is highly recommended for experienced hikers.
  2. Columbia River Trail - A popular biking trail that runs along the Columbia River Wetlands and offers stunning views of the river and wildlife.
  3. Westside Legacy Trail - An easy biking trail that offers breathtaking views of Lake Windermere and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Invermere: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a thrilling adventure or a relaxing experience, Invermere has something for everyone. Here are our top recommendations for things to do in Invermere:

  1. Take a scenic flight to see the Rockies from above.
  2. Experience white water rafting on the Kootenay River.
  3. Go on a guided hike with a local expert.

Week-Long Itinerary for Invermere

Day Activities
1 Visit the Windermere Valley Museum, stroll around downtown Invermere, and relax at a local spa.
2 Hike up Mount Swansea or explore the natural hot springs in Radium Hot Springs.
3 Discover the natural beauty of Kootenay National Park or go on a guided adventure tour.
4 Take a scenic flight to see the Rocky Mountains from above or go on a wildlife-watching tour.
5 Visit the Pynelogs Cultural Centre or explore the local art galleries and boutiques.
6 Relax on the sandy beaches of Lake Windermere or go paddle-boarding or kayaking.
7 Go on a scenic drive through the Rockies and explore nearby national parks and attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Invermere

From local cuisine to international flavors, Invermere has it all. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rocky Mountain Welder – This dish is a mix of smoked bacon, potatoes, and cheesy goodness.
  2. Rocky Mountain Trout - This locally caught fish is one that you have to try when you’re in the area.
  3. Wild Game Meat – Try Elk, Buffalo, and Venison which are all popular meats in the region.

Local Beverages

  1. Red Cedar IPA – This drink is a locally brewed beer from Invermere Brewing Company that is a must-try.
  2. Valley Vodka and Gin – A small distillery in the area that is known for producing high-quality spirits.
  3. Sunflower Juice – A refreshing drink that is locally made from organic sunflowers.

Where to Stay in Invermere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Invermere offers a range of accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Here are our top recommendations for places to stay in Invermere:

  1. Copper Point Resort - A luxurious resort located close to many natural attractions and outdoor activities.
  2. Invermere Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that offers a home-like ambiance and all necessary amenities.
  3. Lake Windermere Pointe - A well-appointed guesthouse located right on the shores of Lake Windermere.

What to Buy in Invermere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Invermere is known for its local products and souvenirs. Here are some ideas for what to buy in Invermere:

  1. Handmade Bath and Body - natural and locally sourced ingredients come together for some of the best bath and body products around!
  2. Jade Ceramics – Jade Ceramics is a local business that creates stunning ceramic pieces including mugs, bowls, and plates.
  3. Rocky Mountain Soap – this is a popular soap that is made from locally sourced lavender.

Local Culture and Customs of Invermere

Invermere has a rich cultural heritage, and its residents take pride in their customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Invermere:

  1. Invermere is a town that takes pride in its environment. Do your part to keep it clean and respect local wildlife.
  2. Make sure that you dress accordingly for the season because temperatures can change for the region rapidly.

Best Time to Visit Invermere

Invermere is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer and fall months when temperatures are balmy, and nature is at its finest. June to September is the perfect time for outdoor activities, whereas the fall season is ideal for leaf-peeping and enjoying the colors of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Invermere

Do:

  1. Explore the great outdoors and enjoy everything that nature has to offer in Invermere.
  2. Learning local customs and taking part in the different festivities.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or harm wildlife when visiting natural areas.
  2. Drive under the influence, it’s a safety hazard and is not taken lightly by the residents of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Invermere

Invermere is a safe place to visit, but there are some things to keep in mind to stay safe:

  1. Carry bear spray when visiting natural areas and be careful when hiking, especially during bear season.
  2. Lock your car doors when parking your car and be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Invermere

Invermere has access to health care which is available at the Invermere and District Hospital and Health Centre which has a variety of services available for minor or major medical needs. Remember to always, where possible and safe, wear protective gear when partaking in physical activities.

Getting to Invermere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Invermere can be reached via several means of transportation, including air, bus, and car. Here are some options:

Airports Near Invermere

  1. Calgary International Airport - located around a 3-hour drive away from Invermere.
  2. Cranbrook/Canadian Rockies International Airport - around a 1-hour drive away from Invermere.

Bus Stations Near Invermere

  1. Greyhound - Provides scheduled bus services across Canada and stops in Invermere.
  2. Columbia Valley Transit System – Provides transportation services to Invermere and the surrounding area.

Other Options

Invermere is a short 90-minute drive from Banff, Alberta, and many visitors combine a trip to Invermere with an excursion to this popular tourist destination. Keep in mind parking in Banff can be pricey and looking in advance can save money.

How to Get Around Invermere

Invermere is a small town with everything within walking distance. However, renting a vehicle is an option for those wishing to go a bit further out to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Invermere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Invermere is surrounded by numerous attractions, national parks, and scenic drives that are worth exploring. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Banff National Park - A UNESCO world heritage site that is globally recognized for its charm and beauty.
  2. Yoho National Park - Best known for its glaciers, waterfalls, and crystal-clear lakes that provide great photo opportunities.
  3. Kananaskis Country - An expansive area with a variety of outdoor activities for all ages and abilities.

Conclusion

Invermere is a hidden gem in the Rockies known for its natural beauty, vibrant culture, and world-class recreational activities. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to soak in all that Invermere has to offer and get the most out of your trip to this stunning town.
