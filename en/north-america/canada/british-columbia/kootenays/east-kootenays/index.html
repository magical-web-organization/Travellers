Introduction to East Kootenays

East Kootenays is a picturesque region located in British Columbia, Canada. As the easternmost region of the Kootenays, this area features a plethora of magnificent natural attractions, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the area’s rich history, appreciate abundant wildlife and scenic views, and take part in a variety of exciting activities, making it an excellent vacation spot for families or adventurers.

List of Places to Visit in East Kootenays

Here are some of the must-see places in East Kootenays that will add meaning to your journey:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East Kootenays is home to a range of museums and historical landmarks that offer visitors a chance to learn more about the region’s history and culture.

  • Fort Steele Heritage Town: This historic site is a living tribute to the gold rush era of the late 19th century. Visitors can explore the restored buildings, chat with historic characters in period clothing, and enjoy costumed interpretive presentations.

  • The Canadian Museum of Rail Travel: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into Canada’s railway history. It features an impressive collection of restored vintage locomotives, cars, and memorabilia.

  • The Ktunaxa Interpretive Centre: Learn more about the history, culture, and traditions of the Ktunaxa people at this museum. The center offers exhibitions, interactive displays, and cultural programs.

Natural Attractions of East Kootenays: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Kootenays’ outdoor attractions are a huge draw for visitors who love to take part in exciting activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions you should not miss:

  • Kootenay National Park: This park is the crown jewel of East Kootenays. It features glorious mountain scenery and endless possibilities for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, wildlife watching, and camping.

  • Columbia River Wetlands: This vast wetland ecosystem is a bird-watcher’s paradise, and home to numerous species of birds and other wildlife. Canoeing and kayaking are also popular activities.

  • Radium Hot Springs: Soak in the relaxing, hot mineral waters of Radium Hot Springs. This hot spring is well-known for having therapeutic and healing properties.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Kootenays

East Kootenays offers endless possibilities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. From the easy afternoon strolls to a multi-day trekking expedition, there is something for everyone. Here are some popular recommendations:

  • Mount Fisher: This 13 km hike offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding peaks, including the Rocky Mountains.

  • Kootenay River Trail: This 11 km trail is perfect for an afternoon hike. It winds through scenic forests, along the river, and offers opportunities to see wildlife.

  • Mountain Biking in Fernie: Fifteen mountain biking trails located at Fernie Alpine Resort offer thrill-seekers and mountain bike enthusiasts a chance to ride through the wilderness and mountains of East Kootenays.

Best Things to Do in East Kootenays: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, East Kootenays offers many exciting activities to enjoy during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Whitewater Rafting: The area’s many rivers offer fantastic rafting opportunities, with Class II, III, and IV rapids to conquer.

  • Wildlife Watching: East Kootenays offers fantastic opportunities to observe various wild animals in their natural habitats. Elk, deer, big horn sheep, bears, and wolves are just some of the animals that you can spot here.

  • Horseback Riding: More than 450 km of trails offer horseback riders a chance to explore beautiful landscapes, forests, and meadows in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Kootenays

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in East Kootenays that offers a perfect balance of outdoor adventure and cultural exploration:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Fort Steele Heritage Town, take aguided tour of the site.
Day 2 Take a hike on Mount Fisher and enjoy the beautiful panorama of valley.
Day 3 Experience the fascinating history of the Ktunaxa people at Ktunaxa Interpretive Centre.
Day 4 Enjoy the hot springs of Radium Hot Springs.
Day 5 Go horseback riding along one of East Kootenays’ scenic trails.

Food and Drink Scene of East Kootenays

East Kootenays is a region that offers fantastic cuisine and artful dining experiences. It is an ideal place to experience the growing farm-to-table dining trend. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burgers: Try the delicious bison burgers at the many local cafes and restaurants in East Kootenays.

  • Pemberton Potatoes: The Pemberton potato is famous for its unique texture and distinct flavour. It’s a must-try for foodies and is served at many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Wine Tasting: The Columbia River Valley is home to numerous vineyards and wineries. Don’t miss the opportunity to sample their wines.

  • Kootenay Coffee: For coffee lovers, try the amazing artisanal blends of Kootenay Coffee Company.

Where to Stay in East Kootenays: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Kootenays offers a variety of accommodations for visitors to choose from. Here are some suggestions:

  • Fairmont Hot Springs Resort: This family-friendly resort features stunning mountain views, an award-winning golf course, and several indoor and outdoor swimming pools.

  • Creston Hotel & Suites: This hotel is located in downtown Creston, close to many restaurants and shops. It also offers bike rentals, perfect for exploring the area’s trails.

  • St. Eugene Golf Resort and Casino: Located along the Kootenay River, this hotel features a European-inspired spa and an award-winning golf course.

What to Buy in East Kootenays: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many locally made products that make excellent souvenirs to bring home from East Kootenays. Here are some ideas for what to buy:

  • Kootenay Knives: Handcrafted knives made with local wood make excellent souvenirs to bring home.

  • Stained Glass Artifacts: East Kootenays is home to many talented glass artists who specialize in beautiful stained glass artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of East Kootenays

The Kootenay people are the original inhabitants of the region of East Kootenays. The area has a fascinating history, blended with modern elements that make it unique.

Best Time to Visit East Kootenays

East Kootenays enjoys a moderate climate for most of the year. The best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is warmest, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Winter activities like skiing and snowboarding are popular in December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Kootenays

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and their habitats.

  • Follow all park and trail regulations.

  • Enjoy the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter the natural environment.

  • Approach or disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Kootenays

The region is safe for visitors, but it is important to take general safety precautions such as locking car doors when parking in remote areas. Be aware of wildlife and follow the safety guidelines.

Health in East Kootenays

There are no serious health concerns in East Kootenays. It is recommended to drink bottled water, and it is important to bring any necessary prescription medication with you.

How to Get Around in East Kootenays

There are several transportation options available in East Kootenays. Car rentals, bikes, and taxis are available at various locations.

Where to Go Next from East Kootenays: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Banff National Park

Just a short drive away, Banff National Park is a world-renowned destination. It features stunning mountain vistas, glacier-fed blue lakes, and outdoor recreational opportunities.

Okanagan Valley

The Okanagan valley is home to world-renowned wineries and orchards. The area attracts wine lovers and culinary enthusiasts from around the world.

Conclusion

East Kootenays is an incredible region of Canada that offers beautiful scenery, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, and an amazing food scene. It’s a perfect destination for family vacations and solo adventures alike.
