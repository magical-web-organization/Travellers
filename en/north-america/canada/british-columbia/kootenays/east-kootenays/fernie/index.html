Introduction to Fernie

Nestled in the stunning East Kootenays of the Kootenays region in British Columbia, Canada, Fernie is a hidden gem that boasts an irresistible charm. Surrounded by the Canadian Rockies, Fernie offers breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, shimmering lakes, and lush green forests. With an array of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and finely-tuned cuisine, Fernie has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Fernie

Fernie has a rich culture that is preserved and celebrated through art, music, and festivals. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and respect for nature and wildlife is highly emphasized.

Best Time to Visit Fernie

Fernie is a year-round destination with something to offer in every season. Summers are mild and perfect for outdoor adventures, while winters are snowy and ideal for skiing and snowboarding. Spring and fall offer pleasant weather for hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fernie

Do:

  • Respect and preserve the natural environment
  • Embrace the local culture
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Admire the wildlife from a safe distance

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Disturb the wildlife
  • Ignore the weather forecast

Safety and Health

Safety in Fernie

Fernie is generally safe, but precautions should be taken when outdoors, especially in the mountains. Ensure to:

  • Beware of avalanches in winters
  • Wear proper hiking and ski equipment
  • Carry a first-aid kit

Health in Fernie

Fernie has a health clinic to handle any medical emergencies. It’s advisable to carry health insurance and carry prescription medicine when traveling.

Getting to Fernie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fernie

The closest major airport to Fernie is Calgary International Airport, which is approximately 3 hours away. Alternatively, you can fly to Canadian Rockies International Airport located in Cranbrook, which is just an hour away.

Bus Stations Near Fernie

Greyhound and BC Transit operate regular bus services to Fernie.

Other Options

Driving is a popular option to reach Fernie. Car rentals are available at both Calgary International Airport and Canadian Rockies International Airport.

How to Get Around in Fernie

Fernie is a small town, and most of the places are easily accessible by foot. Alternatively, a car or bicycle can be rented.
