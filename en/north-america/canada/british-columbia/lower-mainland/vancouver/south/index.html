Introduction to South

South is a neighborhood located in the southern part of Vancouver in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Canada. It is a culturally and ethnically diverse region where you can experience the local customs, taste international cuisines, and enjoy outdoor activities amidst natural lush surroundings. In this travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, food and drink scene, accommodation options, and many more that South has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Anthropology at UBC

The Museum of Anthropology at UBC is one of the major attractions in South, showcasing the culture and history of Indigenous people from all over the world, particularly the First Nations people of British Columbia. The museum has an impressive collection of artifacts, including masks, totem poles, and carvings.

Gulf of Georgia Cannery National Historic Site

Gulf of Georgia Cannery National Historic Site is a former salmon-canning factory and a popular attraction that offers a glimpse into the fishing industry that once dominated the British Columbia economy. The site presents a great opportunity to learn about the cans-making process, fishing techniques, and the life of the canners who worked under tough conditions.

Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Classical Chinese Garden

Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Classical Chinese Garden is an authentic garden based on the principles of feng shui, featuring colorful flowers, delicate pavilions, and a tranquil koi pond. It is an ideal spot for leisure walks, meditation, and exploring Chinese culture.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

VanDusen Botanical Garden

VanDusen Botanical Garden is a 55-acre park boasting an impressive collection of more than 7,500 plants from around the world. The garden has a variety of trails, including a stone garden, an Elizabethan hedge maze, and a Korean Pavilion.

Queen Elizabeth Park

Queen Elizabeth Park is home to the famous sunken gardens with a vast array of perennials, trees, and numerous walking trails. Visitors can also enjoy the spectacular views of the city from the park’s highest point.

Pacific Spirit Regional Park

Pacific Spirit Regional Park comprises over 5000 acres of greenery, including dense forests, picturesque streams, and an extensive network of hiking trails. Visitors can also rent bikes, watch birds, and witness the Salish Sea at specific points.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Pacific Spirit Regional Park Trails

With over 70 kilometers of hilly trails, Pacific Spirit Regional Park offers a diverse experience for hikers and bikers alike. The trails have varying levels of difficulty and take visitors deep into nature.

Seaside Greenway

This scenic 28 km route connects South to Vancouver’s iconic Stanley Park, Olympic Village, and more. Rent a bike, take a leisurely stroll and enjoy beautiful views overlooking the ocean.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Granville Island

Granville Island is a quirky and colorful destination featuring an abundance of artisans, food vendors, and entertainment options. There is always something to see and do on Granville Island, including boat tours, shopping experiences, and the daily produce market.

Brewery Tours

South also boasts a variety of breweries known for their artisan beers. You can join a brewery tour to learn more about the brewing process and, of course, sample some for yourself.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Day Activities
1 Visit the Museum of Anthropology and explore the surrounding village of talented artists.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

Japadog

Japadog is a popular street food vendor offering traditional Japanese hot dogs paired with unexpected condiments like seaweed, wasabi, and teriyaki sauce. This playful twist on the classic hot dog is not to be missed.

Fresh Seafood

South is also home to a variety of restaurants featuring locally caught seafood such as fish and chips, salmon chowder, and Atlantic lobster. Stop by a local restaurant and taste the local flavors.

Local Beverages

Beer

South is home to many craft breweries that offer a variety of locally brewed beers. If beer is in your wheelhouse, make sure to visit some of the local pubs to taste the different types.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pacific Gateway Hotel

The Pacific Gateway Hotel is a modern upscale hotel with rooms offering stunning views of the mountains, city, and Fraser River. It is situated in close proximity to the Richmond Olympic Oval.

YWCA Hotel Vancouver

The YWCA Hotel Vancouver is a budget-friendly option for travelers, located downtown and close to Granville Island. The hotel offers private rooms, suites, and hostel-style shared accommodations.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South has an abundance of artisan shops and vendors offering locally produced goods including handmade jewelry, pottery, and art. Make sure to buy souvenirs and take home unique and authentic treasures as memories.

Local Culture and Customs of South

South is a culturally diverse community, and its residents take pride in their heritage. Respect for different norms is important while visiting here.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is between May and September when the weather is warm, and the days are longer. During these months, visitors get to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and exploring the scenic beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s many art and design shops.
  • Try local cuisines and indulge in the diverse food scene of South.

Don’t:

  • Litter, as the locals are respectful of the environment and maintain clean surroundings.
  • Be intolerant of different cultures or religions.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South is generally a safe neighborhood to visit, with low crime rates. However, as with any destination, be mindful of your surroundings and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in South

Visitors should have travel insurance, particularly if planning to engage in outdoor activities, and take the necessary precautions, such as wearing sunscreen, drinking enough water, and staying up to date with recommended vaccinations.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

The Vancouver International airport (YVR) is located just 10 minutes away from South, making it easy to travel here. Several public transport options, taxis and ride-hailing services connect the airport to South.

Bus Stations Near South

South is also serviced by several bus routes, including the RapidBus lines. These buses offer transportation to and from different parts of town.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore South and its attractions better.

How to Get Around in South

South is well connected via public transport and buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services. Visitors can take advantage of car and bike rentals to have more autonomy when exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South is just a stone’s throw away from several popular destinations. Some of these include:

  • Richmond, home to the famous and colorful night market
  • Stanley Park, a must-visit in Vancouver
  • Downtown Vancouver

Conclusion

South Vancouver is a fascinating neighborhood, boasting an impressive array of attractions, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences to suit every kind of traveler. This guide has provided a comprehensive overview of what to see and do, eat and drink, local customs and health concerns. From the great outdoors to the local food and drink scene, there’s plenty to discover in South Vancouver.
