Introduction to Harrison Hot Springs

Located in Fraser Valley, in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Canada, Harrison Hot Springs is a charming lakeside village famous for its natural hot springs. Surrounded by panoramic views of mountains, forests, and crystal-clear water, the village offers a perfect getaway for tourists seeking relaxation, adventure, and culture.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Harrison Hot Springs. So grab a pen and paper and start planning your unforgettable trip today.

List of Places to Visit in Harrison Hot Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Harrison Hot Springs has a rich history and culture worth exploring. Here are some must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Harrison Resort & Spa Public Pool and Hot Springs: Soak in the therapeutic mineral waters of Harrison Hot Springs’ natural hot springs. This experience is not to be missed.

  • Kilby Historic Site: Kilby is a restored 1906 farmstead and contains the General Store Museum, Interurban Tram Car, and Heritage Post Office. Take a tour to learn about early 20th-century rural life.

  • Agassiz-Harrison Museum: Exhibits displayed at the Agassiz-Harrison Museum will show you the area’s extensive history.

Natural Attractions of Harrison Hot Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Harrison Hot Springs has a plethora of natural attractions that will fill your itinerary with outdoor activities and adventures. Here are some of the best:

  • Bridal Veil Falls Provincial Park: Take a hike through lush forests to reach the 60-meter Bridal Veil Falls. The views at the top are breathtaking.

  • Harrison Lake: The largest lake in southern British Columbia, Harrison Lake, is perfect for swimming, kayaking, fishing, boating, and more.

  • Sasquatch Provincial Park: Ideal for camping, hiking, and picnicking with the family. This park is perfect for spotting wildlife, including bears, owls, and eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harrison Hot Springs

Harrison Hot Springs offers a variety of hiking and biking routes suitable for all ages and skill levels. Here are some of the best:

  • Hicks Lake Trail: A 10-kilometer loop trail featuring the lake and forest view. This loop is not too strenuous.

  • Spirit Trail: A short, 3-kilometer waterfront path that follows the edge of Harrison Lake. This trail is ideal for sunset walks.

  • Sandy Cove Trail: A 5-kilometer medium-difficulty trail that passes through forests, steep cliffs and rocks, and offers beautiful views of Harrison Lake.

Best Things to Do in Harrison Hot Springs: Activities and Tours

Harrison Hot Springs’ attractions extend beyond its natural offerings. Here are some popular activities and tours to include in your itinerary:

  • Jet Boat Tours: Explore Harrison River, the Fraser Valley’s oldest river, on a thrilling jet boat tour that will take you through stunning river canyons.

  • Farmer’s Market: Every Saturday, browse local artisanal goods while listening to live music and enjoying some of the best local foods and drinks.

  • Aviation Museum: Explore the fascinating history of Canadian aviation and immerse yourself in hands-on exhibits at the Canadian Museum of Flight.

Food and Drink Scene of Harrison Hot Springs

Must-Try Dishes

Harrison Hot Springs’ food scene is thriving, with restaurants serving up some of the freshest local ingredients. Be sure to try the following dishes:

  • Smoked Trout Dip: A popular appetizer made with fresh, locally sourced trout and served with crusty bread.

  • Pacific Northwest-Style Chowder: A comforting dish made with fresh seafood like mussels, clams, and salmon.

  • Harrison Hot Springs Signature Bloody Mary: A special cocktail made with Harrison Hot Springs’ unique artisanal Bloody Mary mix.

Local Beverages

Harrison Hot Springs also has a vibrant beverage scene worth exploring:

  • Harrison Beer Works: A local brewery that specializes in handcrafted and unique beer flavors.

  • Spruce Collective: A cozy coffee shop that serves different coffee blends using locally sourced beans.

  • The Springs Bar & Grill: A bar that offers a wide range of beverages and live music on weekends.

Where to Stay in Harrison Hot Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation in Harrison Hot Springs, regardless of your budget or preferences. Here are some options:

  • Harrison Beach Hotel: A beachfront hotel with stunning lake views and spacious rooms.

  • Rowena’s Inn: A luxurious guesthouse with colonial-style architecture, beautiful gardens, and a golf course.

  • Harrison Hot Springs Resort & Spa: A full-service resort with indoor and outdoor pools, hot springs, and access to scenic hiking trails.

What to Buy in Harrison Hot Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Look for these local products to take back home to loved ones:

  • Handmade Pottery: Local art galleries in Harrison Hot Springs sell beautiful and unique handmade pottery from local artists.

  • Maple Syrup: A staple of Canadian cuisine, Harrison Hot Springs is a great place to buy premium-quality maple syrup.

  • Artisanal Soap: Many shops sell soap made with locally sourced ingredients like goat milk and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Harrison Hot Springs

Harrison Hot Springs is home to various indigenous communities with their unique cultures and traditions. Visitors to Harrison Hot Springs must respect the traditional territories of the Sts’ailes, and respect the land and the community’s customs.

Best Time to Visit Harrison Hot Springs

The best time to visit Harrison Hot Springs is between May and October. During these months, the temperatures are mild, and the days are perfect for outdoor activities. The peak season for tourism is between July and August, so expect crowds at this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harrison Hot Springs

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment. Harrison Hot Springs boasts a stunningly beautiful natural landscape, so visitors must be responsible and leave no trace.

  • Learn about the local indigenous cultures and traditions. Harrison Hot Springs’ indigenous communities have rich traditions and customs that visitors can learn about and respect.

Don’t:

  • Litter. Keep the beaches and hiking trails clean by disposing of trash properly.

  • Ignoring personal safety precautions. Always make sure to take necessary safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities in Harrison Hot Springs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harrison Hot Springs

Harrison Hot Springs is generally safe for visitors. However, it is always essential to take precautions to keep safe, especially when hiking or engaging in water activities. Always be vigilant and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Harrison Hot Springs

The natural hot springs in Harrison Hot Springs are known to have therapeutic properties and can be a rejuvenating experience. However, visitors with medical conditions are advised to consult their doctor before soaking in the hot springs.

Getting to Harrison Hot Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harrison Hot Springs

Vancouver International Airport, located about 130 kilometers west of Harrison Hot Springs, is the closest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Harrison Hot Springs

Greyhound service to Chilliwack, where a local bus/shuttle service is then available to transport you to Harrison Hot Springs.

Other Options

There are also taxi services and car rental options available for visitors who prefer to travel independently.

How to Get Around in Harrison Hot Springs

Walking is the most common way to get around Harrison Hot Springs. Visitors can also rent bikes or electric bikes to explore the nearby areas. There are also taxi services available for visitors who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Harrison Hot Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harrison Hot Springs is ideally located to explore other nearby destinations in British Columbia. Here are some destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Vancouver: A dynamic and culturally diverse city located just a few hours away from Harrison Hot Springs.

  • Whistler: A world-renowned mountain town known for winter sports and outdoor adventures.

  • Barkerville Historic Town & Park: A historic park showcasing the gold rush era, with tons of historical activities to experience.

Conclusion

Harrison Hot Springs, located in beautiful Fraser Valley, British Columbia, Canada, is an ideal vacation destination, offering natural wonders, outdoor activities, culture, and relaxation. From hiking and observing wildlife to indulging in the local food and drink scene, there is something for everyone in Harrison Hot Springs. Plan your trip with our comprehensive travel guide and create lasting memories among picturesque views of mountains, forests, and crystal-clear waters.
