Introduction to Kent

Located in the heart of Fraser Valley, Kent is a hidden gem in Lower Mainland, British Columbia. Home to stunning natural landscapes and rich culture and history, this small community offers a unique experience for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. From outdoor activities to museums and historical sites, there’s something for everyone in Kent.

List of Places to Visit in Kent

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Agassiz-Harrison Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, this museum showcases the rich heritage of the region with exhibits on Indigenous history, early settlers, and farming practices.
  • Kilby Historic Site: This heritage site offers a glimpse into the early 1900s with its historic farm buildings, antique farm equipment, and unique gift shop.
  • The Canadian Museum of Flight: Located nearby in the city of Langley, this museum features a collection of vintage aircraft and provides visitors with the unique opportunity to witness the restoration of historic planes.

Natural Attractions of Kent: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bridal Veil Falls Provincial Park: A stunning waterfall that cascades down a rocky cliff, Bridal Veil Falls is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Cheam Lake Wetlands Regional Park: This park is a birdwatcher’s paradise, home to a wide variety of bird species including eagles, herons, and swans. There are also hiking trails and fishing opportunities.
  • Hemlock Valley Resort: A year-round mountain resort with skiing and snowboarding in the winter and hiking, mountain biking, and kayaking in the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kent

  • Cheam Ridge Hiking Trail: A challenging hike that rewards visitors with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and forest.
  • Vedder Ridge Trail: This moderate hike offers beautiful views of the Fraser Valley and is great for hikers of all skill levels.
  • Rotary Trail: A scenic paved trail that follows the banks of the Fraser River for 10km, passing through stunning wetlands and forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Kent: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing on the Fraser River: Kent is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, and the Fraser River is home to a variety of fish species including salmon, sturgeon, and trout.
  • Kayaking on Harrison River: Rent a kayak and paddle through some of the most scenic areas of Kent, including Harrison River and nearby lakes.
  • Helicopter Tours: See Kent from a bird’s eye view with a heli-tour, taking in panoramic views of the stunning surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Kent

Must-Try Dishes

  • BC Salmon Burger: Savor the flavor of fresh, local salmon with this iconic Canadian dish.
  • Maple-Glazed Bacon Donut: A unique breakfast item that combines sweet and savory flavors into one delicious bite.
  • Poutine: A quintessential Canadian dish, poutine features crispy french fries smothered in gravy and cheese curds.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Sample some of the locally-brewed craft beers at one of the many breweries in the Fraser Valley.
  • BC Wine: The region is known for producing delicious, world-class wines. Visit one of the local wineries for a tasting.

Where to Stay in Kent: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Harrison Hot Springs Resort & Spa: A luxurious resort overlooking Harrison Lake.
  • The Empress Bed & Breakfast: A charming B&B located in the heart of Kent, with easy access to nearby attractions.
  • Genevieve’s Bed & Breakfast: A cozy and comfortable B&B situated in a quiet neighborhood in the town of Agassiz.

What to Buy in Kent: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork: Visit one of the many galleries or shops featuring local artists to find a unique piece for your home.
  • Fresh Produce: Kent is known for producing an abundance of fresh, high-quality fruits and vegetables. Visit a local farmers’ market to stock up.
  • Maple Syrup: A staple of Canadian cuisine, Kent produces some of the best-quality maple syrup in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Kent

The culture of Kent is heavily influenced by the natural beauty of the surrounding area, with a strong emphasis on outdoor activities and sustainability. Respect for the environment and local First Nations communities is also an important aspect of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Kent

The best time to visit Kent is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall months also offer beautiful views of the changing foliage, while the winter months offer great opportunities for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kent

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife by staying on designated trails and respecting designated areas.
  • Support local businesses by shopping at farmers’ markets and buying local products.
  • Learn about the rich history and culture of the region by visiting museums and heritage sites.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind or engage in any activities that may harm the environment.
  • Disrespect local First Nations communities by taking photos of or disturbing sacred sites.
  • Ignore safety regulations when engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kent

Kent is generally a safe place for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities and exploring new areas. Always stay on designated trails, and be aware of any potential hazards such as wildlife and unstable terrain.

Health in Kent

Health services are readily available in Kent, with several hospitals and medical clinics in nearby towns and cities. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication with you and pack a first aid kit when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Kent: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kent

The closest major airport to Kent is Abbotsford International Airport, which is approximately 24km away. Vancouver International Airport is another option, with a travel time of approximately 90 minutes by car.

Bus Stations Near Kent

Greyhound provides bus services to nearby towns and cities from its station in Chilliwack, approximately 25km from Kent. Local buses are also available in the area.

Other Options

Renting a car is one of the best ways to explore Kent and the surrounding area, with several car rental services available in nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Kent

The best way to get around Kent is by car or bike, with several rental services available in nearby towns and cities. There are also local bus services available for transportation within the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kent: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chilliwack: A nearby city with a bustling downtown area and several outdoor recreational opportunities.
  • Vancouver: A world-renowned city known for its art, culture, and natural scenery.
  • Whistler: A popular ski resort town located in the nearby Coast Mountains, offering world-class skiing and snowboarding.

Conclusion

Kent may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of natural beauty and cultural attractions for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path adventure. From hiking and skiing to museums and historical sites, Kent has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the hidden gem of Fraser Valley.
