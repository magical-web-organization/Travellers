Introduction to Langley

Langley is a city located in Fraser Valley, Lower Mainland of British Columbia, Canada. With a population of around 25,000, Langley is known for its lush green spaces, rich history, and vibrant community culture. Visitors will find plenty of things to do and places to see in Langley, making it an ideal destination for both outdoor enthusiasts and those looking to learn about Canadian history.

List of Places to Visit in Langley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Langley has a rich history, and visitors can immerse themselves in it by visiting the museums and historical attractions located in the city. The following are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Fort Langley National Historic Site: This is a must-visit attraction in Langley. Visitors can learn about the city’s history, dating back to the 1800s when Fort Langley was a fur trading post. The site also includes exhibits that showcase the local Indigenous culture and its influences on the community.
  • Langley Centennial Museum: This museum features exhibits that celebrate Langley’s history and culture. There are artifacts, documents, and photographs that showcase the city’s growth from the early days of fur trading to the present day.
  • Canadian Museum of Flight: Aviation enthusiasts must not miss this museum, which features over 25 aircrafts, both modern and vintage, from helicopters to fighter jets.

Natural Attractions of Langley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Langley’s natural beauty is there for all to see, and visitors to the city will find plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the natural attractions in Langley are:

  • Campbell Valley Regional Park: This huge, 535-hectare park, is a nature lover’s dream come true. Here you can find woodland, meadows, and trails to explore. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, owls, and coyotes.
  • Brae Island Regional Park: Situated on the banks of the Fraser River, this park is a great spot for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, picnicking, and wildlife viewing.
  • Aldergrove Regional Park: With over 117 hectares of forest and wetland, this park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts year-round. Visitors can hike, mountain bike, or observe the wildlife, which includes beavers and herons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Langley

Langley has plenty of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Some of the best routes include:

  • Nicomekl Riverfront Park: This park features a 3.5-kilometer trail that runs along the Nicomekl River. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and wildlife viewing along the way.
  • Derby Reach Regional Park: With 11 km of trails, visitors can explore the park on foot or by bike. The park is home to a variety of birds, such as eagles and herons.
  • Langley City Park: Located in Langley City, this park features a 1.2-kilometer trail that takes visitors through gardens, by a water feature and around a lake.

Best Things to Do in Langley: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Langley will never be bored as there are plenty of activities to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do in Langley:

  • Attend a Wine Tasting Tour: Langley is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours, many of which overlook beautiful vineyards. Some recommended wineries include Backyard Vineyards, Chaberton Estate Winery, and Vista D’ Oro Farms & Winery.
  • Shop at the Farmers Market: From May to October, the Langley Farmers Market is open every Wednesday and features local produce and wares from vendors.
  • Visit Krause Berry Farms: This family-owned farm offers berry picking experiences, a winery, and a bakery. There are plenty of seasonal events throughout the year that can be enjoyed by visitors of all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Langley

For visitors looking to experience some of the best attractions and activities in Langley, the following itinerary will guide you:

  • Day 1: Start your week in Langley with a visit to the Fort Langley National Historic Site, explore the area and take in the history of this region.
  • Day 2: Visit one of the local wineries and join a wine tasting tour, enjoy the beautiful scenery of Langley’s wine country.
  • Day 3: Hike or bike one of Langley’s breathtaking trails, such as Nicomekl Riverfront Park, Campbell Valley Regional Park, or Derby Reach Regional Park.
  • Day 4: Visit the Canadian Museum of Flight and learn about the history of aviation.
  • Day 5: Visit Krause Berry Farms, pick your favorite fruit and try their baked goods.
  • Day 6: Shop at the Langley Farmers Market, pick up something to take home with you.
  • Day 7: Spend your final day of the trip by exploring Langley City’s quaint shops and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Langley

Must-Try Dishes

Langley is home to several fantastic restaurants that serve delicious food. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Beef Burgundy: This traditional French dish is served at Beatniks Bistro, a cozy establishment that serves hearty meals made with local ingredients.
  • Seafood Linguini: In Fort Langley, try the seafood linguini dish at Little Donkey Food & Drink, this dish is made with fresh local seafood and aromatics.
  • Bison Burger: Visit Trading Post Brewing for a bison burger that features local ingredients.

Local Beverages

Langley has plenty of local breweries to try, as well as the local wineries. Here are some local drinks to try:

  • Backcountry Brewing: Enjoy a refreshing craft beer at this brewery that serves a variety of IPAs, pilsners, and stouts.
  • Trading Post Brewing: Sip on an ale made with locally sourced ingredients or try one of their S2S (sea to sky) cocktails, featuring a variety of unique flavours.
  • Langley’s wineries: With over 10 wineries to choose from, visitors can look forward to a wide variety of reds and whites, including Chardonnay, Pinot Gris, Merlot, and Syrah.

Where to Stay in Langley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re budget-conscious or in search of luxury, Langley has options for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Best Western Plus Langley Inn: Located in the heart of Langley, this hotel has everything a traveler might need, from a gym to an indoor pool to a restaurant.
  • Coast Hotel & Convention Centre: A spacious hotel that offers guests a fitness center, pool, hot tub, and several dining options.
  • Westward Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly accommodation is located in the heart of Langley City, offers affordable rooms and has plenty of nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Langley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Langley can take a piece of the city home with them by purchasing souvenirs or local products. Here are some great options:

  • Local honey: Langley has several apiaries that produce delicious honey that visitors can take home as gifts or to enjoy for themselves.
  • Handcrafted pottery: Many local artisans sell their pottery products in Langley’s shops and galleries.
  • Local wines: Visitors of legal drinking age can find Langley’s delicious wines in local wine stores or at the wineries themselves.

Local Culture and Customs of Langley

Langley is a multi-cultural community with a vibrant arts and culture scene. Visitors can experience this through annual events such as the Langley International Festival or the Fort Langley Cranberry Festival.

Best Time to Visit Langley

The best time to visit Langley is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can take advantage of the many outdoor activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Langley

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore Langley’s many trails and green spaces.
  • Do sample the local honey and wine.
  • Do check out the local art and craft galleries.
  • Do attend the annual events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the wine tasting tours.
  • Don’t forget to bring your outdoor gear for hiking and biking.
  • Don’t litter or damage any of the natural or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Langley

Langley is a safe city, and visitors can feel secure when exploring the city and its sites. As with any city, visitors should take usual precautions such as locking up valuables when leaving vehicles and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Langley

Visitors to Langley should be aware of the risks of sun exposure and bring plenty of sunscreen. Visitors should also be aware of the dangers of dehydration, particularly when hiking and biking. Be sure to bring plenty of water and stay hydrated throughout the day.

Getting to Langley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Langley can arrive via Vancouver International Airport or Abbotsford International Airport. From there, they can opt for the following transportation options:

  • Renting a car
  • Taking a taxi or ride-share service
  • Taking public transit

Visitors are advised to research public transit options or services in advance of their arrival.

How to Get Around in Langley

Getting around Langley is easy. A rental car is recommended for those who want to explore the city’s many attractions. Visitors can also take advantage of local taxi services or public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Langley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Langley is situated within easy reach of several interesting nearby destinations, such as:

  • White Rock, for its stunning beach and waterfront promenade (a 30-minute drive from Langley).
  • Harrison Hot Springs, a scenic lake that has mineral-rich hot springs (a 1.5-hour drive from Langley).
  • Vancouver, for its vibrant city life and iconic attractions such as Stanley Park, Granville Island, and Gastown (a 45-minute drive from Langley).

Conclusion

Langley is an excellent destination for those looking to get away from busy city life and enjoy the serene surroundings of nature. Visitors will find plenty of things to do, from exploring the city’s rich history to hiking and biking through its many parks and trails. Its warm weather and beautiful landscape make Langley an ideal destination for travelers looking for a tranquil and peaceful retreat.
