Introduction to Maple Ridge

Nestled in the beautiful Fraser Valley of British Columbia, Maple Ridge is a scenic town that offers breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Surrounded by stunning mountains, dense forests, and serene lakes, Maple Ridge is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The town is also home to a vibrant community that prides itself in preserving its local culture and traditions, evident in its museums, galleries, and festivals.

This comprehensive travel guide to Maple Ridge will help you explore and appreciate all that this charming town has to offer. Whether you are a history buff, a foodie, an adventure seeker, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Maple Ridge has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Maple Ridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you want to explore Maple Ridge’s rich cultural heritage, start by visiting its museums and historical landmarks. The following sites are must-visit destinations for history enthusiasts:

  • Maple Ridge Museum and Archives: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the town’s past, with exhibits and artifacts that showcase its industrial, agricultural, and cultural history.
  • Haney House Museum: Built-in 1876, this historic house turned museum is a well-preserved example of Victorian architecture and offers a glimpse into the lives of early settlers in Maple Ridge.
  • Albion Pioneers Cemetery: This cemetery is the final resting place of many of the town’s early settlers and offers a fascinating insight into the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Maple Ridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Maple Ridge is known for its stunning natural beauty, and there are many outdoor activities and sites to explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in Maple Ridge:

  • Golden Ears Provincial Park: This park offers stunning views of mountains, lakes, and waterfalls, and is ideal for hiking, camping, fishing, and boating.
  • Whonnock Lake: This serene lake is surrounded by a beautiful park and offers opportunities for boating, fishing, picnicking, and birdwatching.
  • Kanaka Creek Regional Park: This park offers several walking trails that take you through dense forests, scenic canyons, and a fish hatchery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maple Ridge

Maple Ridge is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a vast network of trails that offer stunning views of the town’s natural beauty. Some of the top trails to explore include:

  • Golden Ears Trail: This trail offers a challenging hike to the summit of Golden Ears Mountain, which is one of the highest peaks in the region and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kanaka Creek Trail: This trail takes you through dense forests, scenic canyons, and along the banks of crystal clear streams, and is ideal for hikers of all skill levels.
  • Alouette Lake Trail: This trail offers a scenic hike along the shores of Alouette Lake and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Maple Ridge: Activities and Tours

Maple Ridge offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Some of the top things to do in Maple Ridge include:

  • River Rafting: The local rivers are ideal for rafting, and several companies offer guided tours.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the town’s natural beauty on horseback with guided tours and lessons available.
  • Farm Tours: Maple Ridge is surrounded by farms that offer tours and tastings of fresh organic produce.

If you are planning to spend a week in Maple Ridge, here is a suggested itinerary that includes the best experiences and sights in the town:

Food and Drink Scene of Maple Ridge

Must-Try Dishes

Maple Ridge is known for its fresh and organic produce and has many excellent restaurants that offer farm-to-table cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes in Maple Ridge include:

  • Maple Bacon Donut: This unique dish is a local specialty and is a delicious combination of bacon and maple syrup.
  • Wild Salmon: Fresh salmon from the local streams is a must-try in Maple Ridge.
  • Organic Produce: Maple Ridge is known for its fresh and organic produce, and many restaurants offer farm-to-table cuisine that showcases local ingredients.

Local Beverages

Maple Ridge has a vibrant craft beer scene, with many local breweries that offer excellent brews. Some of the top breweries to visit include:

  • Ridge Brewing Company: This brewery offers a wide range of beers, including IPAs and stouts, and has a cozy tasting room.
  • Maple Meadows Brewing Company: This brewery is known for its lagers and pilsners and has a spacious patio for outdoor tasting.

  • Best Western Maple Ridge: This hotel offers spacious rooms, a pool, and a fitness center, and is ideal for families and business travelers.

Local Culture and Customs of Maple Ridge

Maple Ridge has a vibrant community that prides itself in preserving its local culture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and etiquette, including:

  • Respect for Nature: Maple Ridge is known for its stunning natural beauty, and visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and minimize their impact on the natural landscape.
  • Friendliness and Courtesy: Maple Ridge residents are known for their friendly and courteous nature, and visitors are encouraged to reciprocate these values.
  • Appreciation for Local Culture: Maple Ridge has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to appreciate local traditions, including art, music, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Maple Ridge

The best time to visit Maple Ridge is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also the peak season for outdoor activities and festivals. Fall and spring are also excellent times to visit, with milder weather and fewer crowds. Winter can be cold and rainy, but it is a great time to enjoy cozy indoor activities and holiday events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maple Ridge

Overall, Maple Ridge is a safe and welcoming town. However, like any destination, there are some safety concerns to be aware of, including:

  • Wildlife Encounters: Visitors should be aware of the risk of wildlife encounters, including bears and cougars, when hiking or camping in the local parks.
  • Traffic Safety: Visitors should be cautious when driving on the local roads, especially in areas with heavy traffic or steep terrain.
  • Water Safety: Visitors should exercise caution when swimming or boating in local lakes and rivers, as strong currents and cold water temperatures can pose a risk.

Health in Maple Ridge

Maple Ridge has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors are encouraged to have comprehensive travel insurance and to carry any necessary medications and documents with them.

Getting to Maple Ridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Maple Ridge is located approximately 50 kilometers east of Vancouver and is easily accessible by several transportation options, including:

  • Car: Maple Ridge is accessible via the Trans-Canada Highway and several local roads that connect to major cities and towns in the region.
  • Bus: Several bus companies operate in the region and connect Maple Ridge to Vancouver, Surrey, and other nearby towns and cities.
  • Train: The West Coast Express train service connects Maple Ridge to downtown Vancouver and other areas in the region.

Airports Near Maple Ridge

The nearest airport to Maple Ridge is Vancouver International Airport, which is located approximately 60 kilometers to the west of the town. Abbotsford International Airport is another nearby airport, located approximately 25 kilometers to the east of Maple Ridge.

Bus Stations Near Maple Ridge

The main bus station in Maple Ridge is located at Haney Place Exchange, which provides connections to several bus routes to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Maple Ridge for visitors who prefer to have their own transportation.

How to Get Around in Maple Ridge

Maple Ridge is a small town and is easy to navigate on foot, bicycle, or car. Taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who prefer to have their own transportation. Several bus routes operate in the town and provide connections to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Maple Ridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maple Ridge is located in the beautiful Fraser Valley of British Columbia and is a gateway to several nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Vancouver: Explore the vibrant city of Vancouver, located just 50 kilometers to the west of Maple Ridge and offering world-class attractions, shopping, and dining.
  • Whistler: Visit the renowned ski resort of Whistler, located approximately 140 kilometers to the north of Maple Ridge, and enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.
  • Harrison Hot Springs: Relax in the natural hot springs of Harrison Hot Springs, located approximately 80 kilometers to the east of Maple Ridge, and enjoy stunning mountain views.
