Introduction to Surrey

Surrey is a vibrant city located in the southern suburbs of Vancouver, a fast-growing area in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Canada. Known for its vibrant cultural scene, beautiful parks, and natural attractions, Surrey offers plenty of activities for tourists of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Surrey

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or art lover, Surrey has something to offer. Here is a list of some of the top attractions in Surrey:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Surrey Museum: Explore the local history of Surrey at this award-winning museum.
  • Historic Stewart Farm: Step back in time to the 1800s and experience life on a farm.
  • Peace Arch Provincial Park: Learn about the international relationship between the US and Canada at this historic park.
  • Surrey Art Gallery: Discover contemporary art through rotating exhibitions and public programs.

Natural Attractions of Surrey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crescent Beach: Relax and sunbathe at this picturesque beach with stunning views of the ocean.
  • Bear Creek Park: An ideal spot for families with playgrounds, gardens, and a mini train ride.
  • Redwood Park: Take a stroll through this beautiful forest with towering trees and greenery.
  • Blackie Spit Park: Enjoy a peaceful walk along the water and spot local wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surrey

Surrey is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts with many hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Green Timbers Urban Forest: This park offers hiking and biking trails through 183 hectares of second-growth forest.
  • Serpentine Fen Nature Reserve: A popular spot for birdwatchers, the nature reserve offers a leisurely hike and scenic views.
  • Tynehead Regional Park: This park boasts over 260 hectares of wildlife habitat, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Surrey: Activities and Tours

  • Sky Zone Trampoline Park: Get active and have fun at this indoor trampoline park.
  • Fraser Downs Racetrack & Casino: Place your bets and enjoy live horse racing.
  • Holland Park: Attend free concerts and events in the summer, and ice skate in the winter.
  • Surrey Night Market: Experience the local culture and cuisine at the popular night market.

Week-Long Itinerary for Surrey

Day 1: Explore the local history at the Surrey Museum and Historic Stewart Farm.

Day 2: Take a scenic hike through the Green Timbers Urban Forest and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Spend the day at Crescent Beach, swimming and soaking up the sun.

Day 4: Enjoy a leisurely bike ride through Serpentine Fen Nature Reserve and visit the picturesque Redwood Park.

Day 5: Visit the Surrey Art Gallery and attend a free concert at Holland Park in the evening.

Day 6: Try your luck at Fraser Downs Racetrack & Casino and enjoy dinner at one of Surrey’s best restaurants.

Day 7: Visit the Peace Arch Provincial Park and end your day with a night out at the Surrey Night Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Surrey

Surrey’s diverse cuisine scene reflects its multicultural population. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Samosas: Fried or baked, this popular Indian snack is a local favorite.
  • Fish and Chips: Enjoy this classic British dish served with coleslaw and tartar sauce.
  • Malaysian Laksa: A spicy soup made with seafood, noodles, and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Phillips Soda Works: This soda company has been a Surrey staple since 1912, with a wide range of flavors to choose from.
  • Central City Brewers + Distillers: Try their award-winning beers and spirits at the local brewery.

Where to Stay in Surrey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Surrey offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Civic Hotel: A popular luxury hotel with modern amenities and stunning city views.
  • Econo Lodge: A budget-friendly choice with clean, comfortable rooms.
  • Surrey Central Inn: Conveniently located near public transportation and popular attractions.

What to Buy in Surrey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • BC Wild Salmon: Buy fresh or smoked salmon to savor the flavors of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Maple Syrup: A Canadian staple and a sweet souvenir to take home.
  • Local Handicrafts: Shop for handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Surrey

Surrey is home to a diverse population, with a mix of Indian, Chinese, Filipino, and English cultures. Respect for cultural differences and open-mindedness is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Surrey

Surrey has a mild climate, with warm summers and cool winters. The best time to visit is from June to September for outdoor activities, festivals, and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surrey

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be mindful of your belongings in public places.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in the car or unattended.
  • Ignore local traffic laws and regulations.
  • Litter or damage natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surrey

Surrey is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions against theft and follow basic safety rules.

Health in Surrey

Surrey has modern healthcare facilities with a network of hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. International visitors should have travel insurance with coverage for medical emergencies.

Getting to Surrey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Surrey

Vancouver International Airport is the nearest major airport, about 35 minutes away from Surrey.

Bus Stations Near Surrey

Surrey is well-connected by bus, with several stations within the city and surrounding areas.

Other Options

SkyTrain is a popular option for getting around Surrey and Vancouver, with frequent service and affordable rates.

How to Get Around in Surrey

Surrey’s public transportation network includes buses, trains, and taxi services. Most attractions are accessible by foot or bike.

Where to Go Next from Surrey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vancouver: Explore Canada’s vibrant and multicultural metropolis, just a short drive or train ride away.
  • Whistler: Hit the slopes and enjoy outdoor adventures in this beautiful mountain resort town.
  • Seattle: Cross the border into the US and visit the iconic city of Seattle, just a two-hour drive away.

Conclusion

Surrey is a diverse and exciting city that offers something for everyone. From cultural attractions and natural beauty to sports and entertainment, Surrey is a destination worth visiting. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Surrey.
