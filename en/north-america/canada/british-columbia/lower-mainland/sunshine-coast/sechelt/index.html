Sechelt Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tourist Handbook

Sechelt is one of the many small towns of Sunshine Coast, British Columbia Canada, that offers a stunning mix of natural beauty and cultural richness. It is home to one of the densest settlements of the Sechelt Nation, and proudly showcases its native history, art, and traditions. The coastal town is also a perfect destination for outdoor activities and a foodie’s paradise. Plan your next holiday in Sechelt, and let our comprehensive travel guide help you navigate your way around.

List of Places to Visit in Sechelt

From culturally significant museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Sechelt has plenty of tourist attractions. Here are the must-visit places in Sechelt:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tem sháw̓ (House of Shíshálh Archaeological Centre): A museum with exhibits showcasing ancient artifacts of the Shíshálh people.
  2. Káy X̱ts’ḵwáýəm Leq’á:mel Community Art Garden: A community-based project showcasing traditional and modern art made by Sechelt residents.
  3. St. Hilda’s Anglican Church: A historic Anglican church built in the late 1800s.
  4. Sechelt Heritage Hidden Groves: A well-preserved remnant of the coastal forest with a network of trails.

Natural Attractions of Sechelt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Porpoise Bay Provincial Park: A scenic park for swimming, kayaking, camping, and picnicking.
  2. Sechelt Inlets Marine Provincial Park: A beautiful park with a network of hiking trails.
  3. Chapman Creek Trail: A hiking trail with scenic waterfalls and lush forests.
  4. Miles Beach: A quiet beach for picnicking and beachcombing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sechelt

Sechelt is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Suncoaster Trail: A 37km trail with beautiful views of the coastline, mountains, forests, and lakes.
  • Pender Hill Trail: An easy 5km trail with beautiful forest views and views of Pender Harbour.
  • Mount Richardson Trail: A challenging 10km trail with stunning panoramic views of the Sechelt inlet and surrounding mountains.
  • Hidden Grove Trail: A 2km trail through a lush forest with towering trees and a hidden waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Sechelt: Activities and Tours

Sechelt offers a long list of activities that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Sechelt:

  • Sightseeing Boat Tour: Enjoy a scenic tour of the Sechelt inlet and coastlines.
  • Kayaking: Explore the Sechelt Inlets by kayaking.
  • Fishing: Enjoy a fishing trip in the Sechelt inlet.
  • Skydiving: Experience Sechelt’s scenic views through a skydiving adventure.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore Sechelt’s lush forests while horseback riding.

Food and Drink Scene of Sechelt

Sechelt offers a diverse range of dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A signature Canadian dish with a unique taste.
  • Seafood Chowder: A flavorful soup made with a mix of seafood.
  • Smoked Salmon: A classic dish that showcases the town’s coastal bounty.
  • Bannock: A type of bread made with flour and water. A traditional dish of the Sechelt Nation.

Local Beverages

  • Gibsons Tapworks: A local microbrewery offering a range of craft beers.
  • Star Anise: A popular café with a vast selection of teas, coffee, and baked goods.
  • The 101 Brewhouse A cool and casual brewpub offering a range of beers and pub-style food.

What to Buy in Sechelt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sechelt offers a vast selection of souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing home. Here’s a list of items to check out:

  • Native Art: Art pieces made by the Sechelt Nation are available throughout the town.
  • Smoked Salmon: A classic dish that you can bring home.

  • Traditional Woven Blankets: Woven blankets by the Sechelt Nation that showcase their traditional designs are available in local stores and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Sechelt

Sechelt is the ancestral home of the Shíshálh people, one of the many indigenous tribes in the region. Their language, history, art, and traditions are still prevalent throughout the town, and their respect for the land, sea, and animals is a central value. Visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and cultures.

Best Time to Visit Sechelt

Sechelt is an all-year tourist destination. However, the best time to visit Sechelt is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can participate in outdoor activities, such as hiking, kayaking, and fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sechelt

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling to Sechelt:

Do:

  • Respect the land, sea, and animals.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn about the local indigenous tribes and their culture.
  • Follow the local laws and regulations.

Don’t:

  • Litter or cause damage to the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Ignore the safety measures for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Sechelt is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors must follow standard safety measures for outdoor activities and respect the natural environment. Here are some safety and health tips for visitors:

Safety in Sechelt

  • Follow the rules and regulations of outdoor activities, such as kayaking and hiking.
  • Wear appropriate safety gear, such as helmets and life jackets.
  • Follow standard safety precautions while swimming or engaging in water sports.

Health in Sechelt

  • Drink plenty of water and carry a water bottle while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Be wary of the sun’s heat and apply sunscreen.
  • Check for ticks and bug bites, especially while hiking in trails.

Where to Go Next from Sechelt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sechelt is located in Sunshine Coast, surrounded by many beautiful destinations that are worth visiting. Here are the nearby destinations and excursions from Sechelt:

Conclusion

Sechelt is a beautiful coastal town that offers a perfect mix of outdoor activities, cultural richness, and delicious cuisine. Plan your next visit to Sechelt and immerse yourself in the natural beauty, local history, and warm hospitality of the town.
