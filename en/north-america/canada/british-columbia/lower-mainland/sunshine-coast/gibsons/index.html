Introduction to Gibsons

Nestled on the Sunshine Coast, in the Lower Mainland of British Columbia, Canada, Gibsons is a charming seaside town that offers stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a laid-back lifestyle. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s plenty to see and do in Gibsons. In this guide, we will take you on a tour of the best attractions, activities, and experiences that Gibsons has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Gibsons

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Gibsons:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gibsons Museum: Explore the rich history of Gibsons, from its First Nations origins to pioneer settlement and beyond.
  • Sunshine Coast Museum and Archives: Learn about the natural and cultural history of Sunshine Coast, from its early inhabitants to the present day.
  • Molly’s Reach Restaurant: Visit the iconic restaurant that served as the primary filming location for the popular TV series “The Beachcombers.”

Natural Attractions of Gibsons: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Soames Hill: Hike up this 420-meter peak for breathtaking views of Gibsons, Keats Island, and the Howe Sound.
  • Gibsons Landing: Stroll along the waterfront and take in the picturesque views of the marina, the mountains, and the ocean.
  • Smitty’s Oyster House: Dig into some fresh, locally-sourced oysters while enjoying the spectacular views of the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gibsons

Gibsons offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in British Columbia, from easy, scenic walks to challenging mountain treks. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Gospel Rock: A 3.5 km hike with stunning views of Keats Island and Howe Sound.
  • Soames Hill: A challenging 7 km hike with panoramic views of Gibsons and the surrounding mountains.
  • Gibsons to Sechelt Trail: A 28 km bike ride along the scenic coast, passing by several picturesque towns and beaches.

Best Things to Do in Gibsons: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or culture, Gibsons has something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to do in Gibsons:

  • Kayaking: Paddle along the scenic coastline and explore the hidden coves and beaches of Gibsons.
  • Whale watching: Take a boat tour and spot some of the magnificent marine life, including orcas, humpback whales, and seals.
  • Art Crawls: Join a local art crawl and discover the vibrant art scene of Gibsons.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gibsons

Here’s a recommended itinerary for a week-long visit to Gibsons:

Day 1: Arrive in Gibsons and explore the waterfront, the shops, and the restaurants. Day 2: Hike up Soames Hill and grab lunch at Smitty’s Oyster House. Day 3: Kayak along the coastline and take a swim at Secret Beach. Day 4: Visit Gibsons Museum and learn about the town’s heritage and culture. Day 5: Take a boat tour and spot some whales and seals. Day 6: Visit Sunshine Coast Museum and Archives and discover the natural and cultural history of the region. Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at the beach and enjoying the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Gibsons

Gibsons boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with an abundance of locally-sourced seafood, craft beer, and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Enjoy some crispy, fresh-caught fish and chips, served with tartar sauce and lemon wedges.
  • Oysters: Savor some of the best oysters in Canada, served raw, grilled, or in a hearty chowder.
  • Smoked Salmon: Try some savory, smoked salmon, served on a bagel, a cracker, or as an appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Sample some of the best craft beers in British Columbia, from IPAs to lagers to stouts.
  • Wine: Sip some of the local wines from the Sunshine Coast, including pinot noirs, chardonnays, and cabernets.

  • Pillar Rock Bed and Breakfast: This cozy B&B offers a peaceful retreat with an ocean view and delicious homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Gibsons: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gibsons is home to many local artisans and craftspeople, who create unique souvenirs and products that reflect the town’s culture and heritage. Here are some of the best things to buy in Gibsons:

Local Culture and Customs of Gibsons

Gibsons has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the diverse influences of its First Nations, European settlers, and modern-day residents. The town is known for its friendly and laid-back atmosphere, where people value community, nature, and creativity. Some customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting Gibsons include:

  • Respect for nature: Gibsons is home to many protected parks and natural areas, so make sure to follow the local regulations and leave no trace.
  • Friendliness and hospitality: Gibsons residents are known for their welcoming and generous spirit, so don’t be surprised if you strike up a conversation with a local at a coffee shop or a park.
  • Creativity and innovation: Gibsons has a thriving arts and culture scene, with many local musicians, painters, writers, and artisans pushing boundaries and experimenting with new forms.

Best Time to Visit Gibsons

The best time to visit Gibsons depends on your preferences and interests. Generally, the town experiences mild and pleasant weather throughout the year, with occasional rainfalls in the fall and winter. Some factors to consider when planning your trip to Gibsons include:

  • Peak season: The summer months (June-August) are the busiest and most expensive times to visit Gibsons, with many tourists flocking to the coast for the warm weather, festivals, and events.
  • Shoulder season: The spring and fall months (March-May and September-November) offer more moderate weather and lower crowds, making them ideal for outdoor activities and relaxation.
  • Winter season: The winter months (December-February) can be chilly and rainy, but also less crowded and more peaceful, with many indoor events and activities taking place.

Getting to Gibsons: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gibsons is located on the Sunshine Coast, which is accessible by ferry and road. Here are some transportation options and logistics for getting to Gibsons:

  • By ferry: Take a ferry from Horseshoe Bay, located in West Vancouver, to Langdale, located in Gibsons. The ferry ride takes around 40 minutes, and tickets can be reserved online or in person.
  • By car: Drive from Vancouver or other parts of British Columbia to Gibsons, using the Trans-Canada Highway and the Sunshine Coast Highway. The drive takes around 1-2 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.
  • By bus: Take a bus from Vancouver to Gibsons, using the BC Transit service. The bus ride takes around 2-3 hours, depending on the route and schedule.

Airports Near Gibsons

If you’re flying to Gibsons, the closest airports are:

  • Vancouver International Airport: Located in Richmond, British Columbia, this airport is the main hub for international and domestic flights.
  • Nanaimo Airport: Located on Vancouver Island, this airport offers regional flights to and from Gibsons.

Bus Stations Near Gibsons

If you’re arriving in Gibsons by bus, the closest bus stations are:

  • Langdale Ferry Terminal: Located in Gibsons, this terminal is accessible by bus and ferry from Vancouver and other parts of British Columbia.
  • Gibson’s Landing Terminal: Located in the heart of Gibsons, this terminal is accessible by local buses and taxis.

Other Options

If you’re traveling to Gibsons by private boat, there are several marinas and docks available for mooring and docking. If you’re traveling by bike or on foot, there are several trails and paths that connect the neighboring towns and attractions.
