Introduction to Pemberton

Located in the picturesque Sea to Sky region of British Columbia, Pemberton is a charming mountain town that attracts visitors from all over the world with its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities. Nestled at the foot of Mount Currie, Pemberton is surrounded by pristine forests, rivers, and lakes and is a gateway to some of the most popular outdoor destinations in Canada, including Whistler, Joffre Lakes Provincial Park, and the Coastal Mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Pemberton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pemberton District Museum: The Pemberton District Museum showcases the town’s rich history through a variety of exhibits, including photographs, artefacts, and documents. Visitors can explore the traditional First Nations pit houses, a relocated railway station, and other historic buildings.

  • Nairn Falls Provincial Park: This beautiful park is home to the stunning Nairn Falls and is a popular spot for hiking, fishing, picnicking, and camping. The park is also steeped in history, with interpretative signage sharing stories about local First Nations culture and the area’s logging and mining industry.

Natural Attractions of Pemberton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Joffre Lakes Provincial Park: This breathtaking park is home to three stunning glacial lakes, each one lovelier than the last. Visitors can enjoy hiking the trail, which offers access to the lakes as well as stunning views of the surrounding peaks and glaciers.

  • Pemberton Wildlife Association: The Pemberton Wildlife Association offers visitors the opportunity to learn about and get involved with local conservation efforts. The association hosts regular events and activities, as well as guided nature walks, birdwatching trips, and other outdoor adventures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pemberton

  • The Mackenzie Basin Trail: This popular mountain biking trail offers stunning views of the Pemberton Valley and is suitable for intermediate and advanced riders.

  • The Nairn Falls Trail: This scenic hiking trail leads visitors through old-growth forests, alongside the rushing Green River, and past beautiful waterfalls before arriving at the spectacular Nairn Falls.

Best Things to Do in Pemberton: Activities and Tours

  • Helicopter tours: Visitors can take a helicopter tour over the surrounding mountains and glaciers to get a bird’s eye view of the stunning natural beauty of the region.

  • Horseback riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Pemberton, with several local outfitters offering guided tours through the lush forests and pastures surrounding the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Pemberton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic, Pemberton’s poutine is not to be missed. With crispy fries, squeaky cheese, and rich gravy, it’s the ultimate comfort food.

  • Wild salmon: Pemberton is home to some of the freshest and tastiest wild salmon in Canada, especially during the summer months.

Local Beverages

  • Pemberton Distillery: The Pemberton Distillery is known for its organic vodka and gin, crafted from local ingredients like potatoes and botanicals.

  • The Beer Farmers: This local craft brewery produces some of the finest small-batch beers in the region, with unique and innovative brews like their Strawberry Sour and Three Peaks Pale Ale.

Where to Stay in Pemberton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pemberton Valley Lodge: This spacious and luxurious lodge is located just a short drive from the town centre and offers stunning mountain views, a fitness centre, and an outdoor pool and hot tub.

  • Big Sky Golf and Country Club: This charming country club and guesthouse offers a range of accommodation options, from deluxe suites to cozy cabins, as well as access to a world-renowned golf course and a range of outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Pemberton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pemberton Distillery spirits: Bring home a bottle of locally produced organic vodka or gin from the Pemberton Distillery as a unique and memorable souvenir.

  • Local arts and crafts: Pemberton is home to a thriving arts community, with many local artists and artisans producing unique and beautiful crafts and artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Pemberton

Pemberton is a diverse and welcoming community that prides itself on its rich cultural heritage and strong First Nations roots. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and take the time to learn about the unique history and culture of the region.

Best Time to Visit Pemberton

The best time to visit Pemberton depends on your interests and what you’re looking for in your trip. Summer (June to August) is the busiest and most popular time to visit, with warm weather and a wide range of outdoor activities and festivals. Fall (September to November) is a quieter time, with cooler temperatures and stunning fall foliage. Winter (December to February) is the perfect time for skiing and snowboarding at nearby Whistler Blackcomb, while spring (March to May) offers milder temperatures and vibrant wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pemberton

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the surrounding natural beauty of Pemberton, including nearby parks and hiking trails.

  • Do sample the local food and drink scene, including trying the local craft beer and poutine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrupt or damage the natural environment, and respect all park rules and regulations.

  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs and traditions, and be mindful of the sensitive cultural history of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pemberton

Pemberton is a relatively safe town, with low levels of crime and a friendly, welcoming community. Visitors should take all necessary precautions when travelling and hiking in the surrounding mountains, including carrying appropriate gear and checking local weather conditions.

Health in Pemberton

Pemberton has a range of health services available, including a hospital, clinic, and several pharmacies. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance and take precautions to stay healthy while travelling, including drinking plenty of water and staying active.

Getting to Pemberton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pemberton

The closest airport to Pemberton is Vancouver International Airport (YVR), which is approximately a two-hour drive away. There are several car rental options available at the airport, as well as shuttle services to Pemberton and the surrounding area.

Bus Stations Near Pemberton

Greyhound Canada offers bus service to Pemberton through their Whistler Interchange stop. Visitors can also take public transit from Vancouver to the town centre.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Pemberton, with several highways connecting the town to Vancouver and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Pemberton

Pemberton is a small town that is easily walkable, and visitors can take advantage of the local public transit system for longer trips or to visit nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Pemberton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Whistler: Located just a short drive from Pemberton, Whistler is a world-renowned skiing and snowboarding destination, with stunning mountain views, luxurious accommodations, and a wide range of outdoor activities and attractions.

  • Squamish: Located approximately an hour’s drive from Pemberton, Squamish is a picturesque town with a rich cultural history and a range of outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, mountain biking, and climbing.

Conclusion

Pemberton is a beautiful and welcoming town that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Whether you’re looking to hike through pristine forests, sample local craft beer, or simply relax and soak up the stunning mountain views, Pemberton has something to offer for everyone.
