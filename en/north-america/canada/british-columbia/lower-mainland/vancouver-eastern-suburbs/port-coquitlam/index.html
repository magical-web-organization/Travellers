Port Coquitlam is a small and charming city located in Vancouver’s eastern suburbs, in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Canada. Despite its modest size, Port Coquitlam offers a vibrant cultural scene, stunning natural beauty, and a plethora of outdoor activities, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do in Port Coquitlam, from hiking and biking trails to cultural attractions, mouth-watering cuisine, and much more.

Introduction to Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam, often referred to as “PoCo,” is a city located between the Fraser River and the Coast Mountains. The city has a rich history, dating back thousands of years to the First Nations’ settlement, and it has grown significantly since its incorporation in 1913. Today, Port Coquitlam is a thriving city with a population of around 60,000 residents.

List of Places to Visit in Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam offers a range of attractions for visitors of all ages. In this section, we’ll highlight some of the city’s must-visit places, categorized by cultural and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about Port Coquitlam’s history and culture, make sure to visit the following attractions:

  • Port Coquitlam Heritage and Cultural Society: This non-profit organization operates a museum and archives that showcase the city’s history and cultural heritage. The museum displays artifacts, photographs, and documents related to Port Coquitlam’s past, and visitors can also attend workshops and events.
  • Terry Fox Library: Named after the famous Canadian athlete Terry Fox, this library is a community hub that offers a range of programs and services, including book clubs, storytimes, and computer classes. The library also hosts special events throughout the year.
  • Traboulay PoCo Trail: This 25-kilometer trail loops around the city, showcasing its natural beauty and heritage sites, such as the Terry Fox Plaza and the Colony Farm Regional Park.

Natural Attractions of Port Coquitlam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Port Coquitlam’s location next to the Coast Mountains and the Fraser River provides an abundance of natural beauty and outdoor activities. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions in the city:

  • Minnekhada Regional Park: This park offers hiking trails through dense forests, wetlands, and scenic views of the Pitt River. Visitors can also see wildlife such as bears, beavers, and coyotes.
  • Pitt River Hot Springs: Located in the nearby mountains, these natural hot springs are accessible by a short hike and offer a relaxing soak with beautiful views of the surrounding nature.
  • Coquitlam River Park: This park has a network of trails that are ideal for walking, jogging, or cycling. The lush forest, riverfront views, and abundance of wildlife make it a must-visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Coquitlam

In addition to the Traboulay PoCo Trail and Coquitlam River Park, Port Coquitlam also offers several other hiking and biking routes that cater to different levels of experience and fitness. Here are some of the best trails in the city:

  • Shaunghessy Park Trail: This 3-km trail is ideal for a leisurely walk or bike ride, with paved paths and beautiful views of the mountains and the Fraser River.
  • Burke Mountain: This mountain range offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. The trails offer stunning views of the city, river, and surrounding mountains.
  • Colony Farm Regional Park: This park offers several trails that pass through fields, forests, and marshes. Visitors can see various bird species, as well as other wildlife like rabbits and deer.

Best Things to Do in Port Coquitlam: Activities and Tours

Port Coquitlam offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Paddlewheeler Riverboat Tours: This tour takes visitors on a 2-hour cruise on the Fraser River, showcasing the river’s natural beauty and history.
  • Port Coquitlam Farmers Market: This is a weekly event that showcases local produce, crafts, and other goods. Visitors can also enjoy live music and food truck fare.
  • Port Coquitlam Recreation Complex: This center offers a range of recreational and fitness activities, including swimming, basketball, and yoga.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Coquitlam

If you have a week to spend in Port Coquitlam, here is a suggested itinerary that will help you experience the city’s diversity and beauty:

Day 1: Visit the Port Coquitlam Heritage and Cultural Society Museum and Archives to learn about the city’s history and culture. Then, take a walk on the Traboulay PoCo Trail to see some of the city’s natural beauty and heritage sites.

Day 2: Visit Coquitlam River Park and hike or bike along the trails. Afterwards, head to the Paddlewheeler Riverboat Tours for a scenic river cruise.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Minnekhada Regional Park and hike on the trails while enjoying views of the Pitt River and wildlife.

Day 4: Visit the Terry Fox Library and attend a workshop or event. Then, head to Colony Farm Regional Park and explore the park’s trails and wildlife.

Day 5: Take a tour of the nearby Pitt Meadows Airport and see vintage aircraft and other aviation artifacts.

Day 6: Relax in the Pitt River Hot Springs and enjoy the soothing natural hot springs and beautiful mountain views.

Day 7: Visit the Port Coquitlam Farmers Market and enjoy local produce and crafts. Alternatively, visit the Port Coquitlam Recreation Complex and take part in a fitness or recreational activity.

Food and Drink Scene of Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam offers a range of dining options that cater to different tastes and styles, from casual cafes to upscale restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in the city:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sushi: Port Coquitlam has several sushi restaurants that offer fresh and delicious sushi rolls.
  • Fish and chips: The city’s proximity to the Fraser River means that there are several seafood restaurants that offer fresh fish and chips.
  • Korean cuisine: From bulgogi to kimchi, Port Coquitlam has several Korean restaurants that offer tasty dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: There are several local breweries in Port Coquitlam that offer craft beer and tasting rooms, such as the Northpaw Brewery and Tin House Brewing Company.
  • Wine: The city is close to several vineyards and wineries, such as the Ridge Brewing Company and the Pacific Breeze Winery.

Where to Stay in Port Coquitlam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Port Coquitlam offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Ovation: This hotel offers spacious and modern rooms, as well as a fitness center and a rooftop terrace.
  • Poco Inn and Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, as well as a restaurant, a heated indoor pool, and a hot tub.
  • Coquitlam Sleepy Lodge: This budget-friendly hostel offers private and shared rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Port Coquitlam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Port Coquitlam has several local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best options:

  • Fraser Valley hazelnuts: The local hazelnuts are well-known for their quality and taste, and they make a great snack to bring home.
  • Local crafts: The city has several local artists and crafters who sell their products at markets and festivals.
  • Wine and beer: The city’s local breweries and wineries offer craft beer and wine, respectively, that make great gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam is a multicultural city with a diverse population. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly in religious sites and treating the elderly with respect.

Best Time to Visit Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam can be visited year-round, although the best time to visit is during the summer months (June to September), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Coquitlam

Here are some general do’s and don’ts when visiting Port Coquitlam:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take your trash with you and leave no trace when hiking or camping.
  • Dress in layers and prepare for changing weather conditions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or litter in public areas.
  • Feed or approach wildlife.
  • Ignore safety warnings and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam is a safe city, but visitors should take common-sense safety precautions, such as avoiding dark and isolated areas at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam has modern and well-equipped medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Travelers should have comprehensive health insurance and take necessary precautions, such as getting recommended vaccinations and carrying necessary medication.

Getting to Port Coquitlam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Port Coquitlam is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some of the best options:

Airports Near Port Coquitlam

  • Vancouver International Airport: This is the nearest major airport, located approximately 40 kilometers from Port Coquitlam.
  • Abbotsford International Airport: This airport is located approximately 45 kilometers from Port Coquitlam and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Port Coquitlam

  • The West Coast Express: This train service connects downtown Vancouver and Port Coquitlam with stops at several other cities in between.
  • Local buses: The city has several bus routes that connect different neighborhoods and attractions.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a good option if you plan to explore nearby destinations.
  • Taxi and rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services are available in the city.

How to Get Around in Port Coquitlam

Port Coquitlam has an efficient public transportation system, including local buses and the West Coast Express train service. Taxis and rideshare services are also available in the city. Alternatively, visitors can bike or walk to see the city’s attractions and natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Port Coquitlam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Port Coquitlam is located in a scenic region that offers several nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best options:

  • Vancouver: This cosmopolitan city is located approximately 30 kilometers from Port Coquitlam and offers a range of cultural attractions, restaurants, and shopping.
  • Whistler: This ski resort town is located approximately 150 kilometers from Port Coquitlam and offers stunning mountain views and outdoor activities such as skiing and snowboarding.
  • Harrison Hot Springs: This scenic town is located approximately 100 kilometers from Port Coquitlam and offers natural hot springs, hiking trails, and a tranquil atmosphere.

Conclusion

Port Coquitlam offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures that make it a perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. Whether you’re interested in history, hiking, or dining, Port Coquitlam has something to offer. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to the city and make the most of your visit.
