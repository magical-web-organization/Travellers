Introduction to Coquitlam

Nestled in the eastern suburbs of Vancouver, in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Canada, Coquitlam is a picturesque city that boasts a thriving cultural scene, scenic parks, and a variety of natural attractions. The city takes pride in offering a diverse array of activities to both locals and tourists alike. From museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Coquitlam won’t cease to amaze visitors with its beauty and charm.

List of Places to Visit in Coquitlam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mackin House Museum - This museum showcases the life and times of the earliest settlers in Coquitlam, dating back to the early 1900s.
  2. Westminster Abbey - Located in Mission, BC, this abbey is an important religious site dating back to the Catholic missions of the region’s early history.
  3. The Bailey Theatre - A local performing arts center that features plays, concerts, and other cultural performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Coquitlam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Pinecone Burke Provincial Park - The park has rugged peaks, pristine alpine lakes, old-growth forests, and abundant wildlife for hikers and campers.
  2. Coquitlam Crunch Trail - A challenging 4.5 km hike that offers scenic views of the city and its surrounding mountains.
  3. Minnekhada Regional Park - Minnekhada is home to several trails leading to lovely sights such as Pitt River, marshlands, and open fields.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coquitlam

  1. Burke Mountain - This trail stretches over 10km and varies in difficulty with incline and rough terrain ensuring a proper workout and stunning views.
  2. Coquitlam River Trail - The path winds through the forest and alongside the Coquitlam River, offering a peaceful ride to bike enthusiasts and walkers alike.
  3. Crystal Falls - This trail runs along the heart of Coquitlam, with a waterfall, trees, and leaves the view is very impressing, especially during fall foliage.

Best Things to Do in Coquitlam: Activities and Tours

  1. Play and have fun wave and diving in City Centre Aquatic Complex.
  2. Get active with friends at Ziba Fitness Studio, a full-service gym with exercise regimens.
  3. Go shopping at Coquitlam Centre, a premier shopping mall with international brands and recreational spots like Cineplex

Food and Drink Scene of Coquitlam

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Japadog is a popular hot dog stand that offers hot dogs with Japanese-inspired toppings such as seaweed, wasabi, and teriyaki sauce.
  2. Duck confit at the Greenhouse is a savory and sumptuous dish that will make you want to come back for more.
  3. The Cumin lamb from Legend House is a spicy stir-fried Chinese dish that is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  1. Central City Brewing offers a variety of microbrews on tap as well as glasses of wine and cider.
  2. Mariner Brewing is a craft brewery that offers a variety of house-brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Coquitlam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Executive Plaza Hotel Coquitlam is the best luxury option in the city with upscale amenities, including a poolside restaurant, fitness center, and heated outdoor rooftop pool.
  2. Best Western Plus Coquitlam Inn Convention Centre is a budget-friendly option with easy access to local attractions and amenities, such as a fitness center and restaurant.
  3. Poco Inn and Suites Hotel is a family-friendly accommodation option with a restaurant, indoor pool, fitness center, and free parking.

What to Buy in Coquitlam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. A bottle of Central City’s Red Racer beer and a package of Japadog hot dogs make for great souvenirs.
  2. Local honey from Honeybee Centre or artisanal cheese from Golden Ears Cheesecrafters also make for good gift ideas.

Local Culture and Customs of Coquitlam

Coquitlam is a melting pot of different cultures, so visitors will feel welcome. Be respectful and open to learning and experiencing new cultures.

Best Time to Visit Coquitlam

The best time to visit Coquitlam is during early summer when the weather is mild, perfect for outdoor activities and festivities. With fewer crowds and lower prices, late autumn and early winter is also a great time to visit The picturesque leaves of fall and snow blanketing the city and mountains makes it an ideal getaway for those seeking a peaceful winter escape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coquitlam

Do:

  • Hike in Pinecone Burke Provincial Park
  • Have a picnic at Lafarge Lake
  • Visit the Mackin House Museum

Don’t:

  • Litter in natural environments
  • Smoke in public places
  • Exceed the city’s speed limit of 60 km/h

Safety and Health

Safety in Coquitlam

Coquitlam is a safe city with a low crime rate, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions.

Health in Coquitlam

Visitors should purchase travel insurance, take care of hydration and bring mosquito repellent during the summer and fall seasons.

Getting to Coquitlam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coquitlam

The closest international airport is Vancouver International Airport, which is about 45 minutes away from Coquitlam. Abbotsford International Airport and Bellingham International Airport are alternatives nearby airports.

Bus Stations Near Coquitlam

The closest bus station is Coquitlam Central Station, serviced by the BC Transit, SkyTrain, and West Coast Express trains.

Other Options

Visitors can take the Trans-Canada Highway from Vancouver or drive westward from the Canadian Rockies and enter the city through downtown Vancouver.

How to Get Around in Coquitlam

BC Transit buses, West Coast Express trains, and the SkyTrain provide convenient and accessible transport to the city, even exploring by bike or foot, is an option.

Where to Go Next from Coquitlam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the Fraser Valley, go hiking in Golden Ears Provincial Park, or visit the quaint town of Fort Langley on a day trip from Coquitlam.

Conclusion

Coquitlam is a perfect destination for a memorable vacation. From scenic hikes and outdoor adventures to historical landmarks and cultural experiences, there is something for everyone. Visitors will also enjoy the food, shopping, and accommodations in the city. Regardless of the season, Coquitlam guarantees an incredible time.
