Introduction to Port Moody

Nestled in Vancouver’s eastern suburbs, in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, Port Moody is a charming town with a rich history and stunning natural attractions. Originally founded as a logging town in the late 1800s, it sits at the head of Burrard Inlet and offers picturesque views of the surrounding mountains. Today, Port Moody has become a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, food lovers, and cultural seekers alike. This travel guide will take you on an exciting journey through the best the town has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Port Moody

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Port Moody: Learn about the town’s fascinating history through exhibits showcasing its origins as a boomtown and its growth into a modern-day suburb.
  • Port Moody Station Museum: Housed in Port Moody’s original Canadian Pacific Railway Station, this museum offers a glimpse into the town’s railway roots, with interactive exhibits and gift shop.
  • Rocky Point Park: A beloved community gathering spot, this waterfront park was once the site of a bustling sawmill. Enjoy relaxing walks along the pier and scenic views of the Burrard Inlet.

Natural Attractions of Port Moody: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buntzen Lake: A popular spot for swimming, hiking, and picnicking, this crystal-clear body of water is surrounded by stunning mountain views.
  • Sasamat Lake: Surrounded by dense forest, this peaceful lake is perfect for kayaking, fishing, and hiking along its trails.
  • Old Orchard Park: A hidden gem in the heart of Port Moody, this park features rocky beaches and walking trails that provide stunning views of the Inlet.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Moody

  • Shoreline Trail: A 25-km trail that winds around Burrard Inlet, offering breathtaking views of mountains and waterfalls.
  • Coquitlam Crunch: A steep stairway leading up to a scenic view of the town and mountains. This route is popular for its intense workout.

Best Things to Do in Port Moody: Activities and Tours

  • Port Moody Arts Centre: A community hub for all things arts, with rotating exhibitions by local artists and classes in various media.
  • Port Moody Farmer’s Market: Runs from May to October, offering up fresh produce, artisanal foods, and handmade crafts.
  • Port Moody Brewery Row: Tour the local breweries including Yellow Dog Brewing, Twin Sails Brewing, and Parkside Brewery, taste their craft beers, and enjoy the friendly atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Port Moody: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Plus Coquitlam Inn Convention Centre: Offers spacious rooms and suites with mountain views, as well as an indoor pool, hot tub, and fitness centre.
  • Eagle Mountain Bed and Breakfast: A scenic B&B with luxurious rooms boasting breathtaking views of the mountains and ocean.
  • Poco Inn and Suites Hotel and Conference Centre: Comfortable and affordable rooms and suites, as well as an indoor pool, fitness centre, and sauna.

  • Artisanal cheese and gourmet foods from Arthur’s Cheese Shop
  • Local honey and beeswax products from Honeybee Centre

Local Culture and Customs of Port Moody

  • The town has a strong commitment to sustainability and is actively working towards becoming a more eco-friendly community. Visitors are welcome to do their part by making use of the town’s recycling and composting programs.
  • Respect for the natural environment is also an essential part of Port Moody’s culture. Visitors are encouraged to take care when hiking, biking, or kayaking and to leave no trace behind.

Best Time to Visit Port Moody

The best time to visit Port Moody is between June to August when the weather is mostly dry and mild. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s natural beauty during the fall season (September to November) when the leaves start changing colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Moody

Do:

  • Respect the town’s commitment to sustainability by properly disposing of waste.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and scenic views the town offers.
  • Try the town’s local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces or natural areas.
  • Disturb the natural surroundings or wildlife while enjoying outdoor activities.
  • Ignore the town’s commitment to sustainability and eco-friendliness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Moody

Port Moody is a safe town with low crime rates, but visitors should exercise standard safety precautions, such as locking up valuables and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Port Moody

The town offers numerous medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, for any health concerns visitors may have during their stay.

Getting to Port Moody: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port Moody

  • Vancouver International Airport: 35-minute drive south of Port Moody.
  • Abbotsford International Airport: 1-hour drive east of Port Moody.

Bus Stations Near Port Moody

Greyhound and Bolt Bus stops in neighboring Coquitlam and Vancouver.

Other Options

SkyTrain connects Port Moody to downtown Vancouver, and taxis and rental cars are readily available.

How to Get Around in Port Moody

Port Moody is easily navigable on foot or bike, with plenty of walking and biking trails throughout the town. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, and the town is well-connected via public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Port Moody: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Coquitlam: A neighboring suburb with many excellent parks, lakes, and hiking trails.
  • Vancouver: A vibrant cosmopolitan city with numerous attractions, including Stanley Park, Granville Island, and its world-famous food scene.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, food lover, or cultural seeker, Port Moody is an ideal destination that offers a little something for everyone. This comprehensive guide provides all the information you need to plan a fantastic trip to this charming town.
