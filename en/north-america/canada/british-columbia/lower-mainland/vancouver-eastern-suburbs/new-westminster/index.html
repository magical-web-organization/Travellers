Introduction to New Westminster

Located in the eastern suburbs of Vancouver in Lower Mainland, British Columbia, New Westminster is a city with a rich culture, breathtaking nature, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, New Westminster has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll find all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in New Westminster

New Westminster has a variety of attractions that cater to different interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Irving House: Built-in 1865, Irving House is a well-preserved heritage home that offers a glimpse into the life of a middle-class family in the late 1800s.

  2. Fraser River Discovery Centre: This interactive museum highlights the history and ecology of the Fraser River.

  3. New Westminster Museum and Archives: The museum showcases the city’s history, from the First Nations to the present day.

Natural Attractions of New Westminster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Queen’s Park: Named after Queen Victoria, this 75-acre park has a playground, a spray park, picnic areas, and a petting zoo.

  2. Glenbrook Ravine: This scenic trail runs along a lush, forested ravine and offers excellent bird-watching opportunities.

  3. Westminster Pier Park: Situated along the Fraser River, this park features a boardwalk, a playground, and a sandy beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Westminster

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, New Westminster has many hiking and biking trails that offer beautiful scenic views. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Queensborough Bridge Trail: This scenic trail runs along the Fraser River and connects New Westminster to the Queensborough neighbourhood.

  2. Brownsville Bar Trail: Located in the Brunette River Greenway, this trail features a restored salmon creek and a variety of plant and bird species.

  3. Sapperton Landing Park Trail: This trail runs along the Fraser River and features a beach, a boat launch, and a pier.

Best Things to Do in New Westminster: Activities and Tours

New Westminster offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  1. Royal City Farmer’s Market: Open every Saturday from May to October, this market features local produce, artisanal products, and live music.

  2. New West Craft: This bi-monthly craft market features handmade items by local artisans.

  3. Sapperton Day Street Festival: This annual festival celebrates the community with live music, food and drinks, and a parade.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Westminster

If you’re planning a week-long trip to New Westminster, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1 Visit Irving House and Queen’s Park

Food and Drink Scene of New Westminster

New Westminster has a thriving food and drink scene that showcases local ingredients and cultural influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Wild salmon: This local delicacy is sustainably caught and served fresh.

  2. Poutine: This Canadian classic is made with French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

  3. Sushi rolls: New Westminster has many sushi restaurants that serve fresh, high-quality sushi rolls.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft beer: New Westminster has many craft breweries that serve a variety of beers, from IPAs to stouts.

  2. Wine: The Fraser Valley is known for its excellent wines, and New Westminster has many bars and wine shops that offer local wines.

  3. Coffee: New Westminster has many cozy coffee shops that roast their own beans and serve delicious coffee.

Where to Stay in New Westminster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Westminster has many options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommended options:

  1. Inn at the Quay: This waterfront hotel offers stunning views of the Fraser River and comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  2. Arundel Mansion: This heritage guesthouse has spacious, elegant rooms and is located in the heart of the city.

  3. Queen’s Park Bed and Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in the historic Queen’s Park neighbourhood and offers home-cooked breakfasts and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in New Westminster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

New Westminster has many shops and markets where you can buy souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommended items:

  1. New Westminster-themed souvenirs: You can find t-shirts, magnets, and other souvenirs that showcase the city’s landmarks and culture.

  2. Local produce and artisanal products: New Westminster has many farmers’ markets and craft markets that sell local produce and handmade goods.

  3. Fraser River salmon: You can buy fresh or smoked salmon from local fishmongers or at the farmers’ markets.

Local Culture and Customs of New Westminster

New Westminster has a rich culture that is influenced by the city’s history and diverse population. Here are some local customs and traditions:

  1. Multiculturalism: New Westminster is a diverse city with a rich blend of cultural influences from First Nations, Asian, and European communities.

  2. Heritage preservation: The city has a strong commitment to preserving its heritage buildings and landmarks.

  3. Festivals and events: New Westminster has many festivals and events throughout the year, celebrating everything from food and drink to sports and music.

Best Time to Visit New Westminster

New Westminster has a mild climate, with temperatures ranging from 0°C to 20°C throughout the year. The best time to visit the city is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you want to avoid the crowds, you can visit during the shoulder seasons (March to April or November to December).

Do’s and Don’ts of New Westminster

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting New Westminster:

Do:

  1. Try the local food and drinks.

  2. Explore the city’s heritage buildings and landmarks.

  3. Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural areas.

  2. Ignore the city’s rich cultural diversity.

  3. Be disrespectful to others.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Westminster

New Westminster is generally a safe city, but like any urban area, it’s important to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  1. Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

  2. Keep valuables out of sight and locked in a safe place.

  3. Follow traffic rules and be cautious of cars and bicycles on the roads.

Health in New Westminster

New Westminster has a good healthcare system, and many hospitals and clinics offer high-quality medical care. However, it’s essential to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies or accidents.

Getting to New Westminster: Transportation Options and Logistics

New Westminster is easily accessible by plane, train, or bus. Here are some transport options:

Airports Near New Westminster

  1. Vancouver International Airport: Located 30 minutes from New Westminster, this airport has many international and domestic flights.

  2. Abbotsford International Airport: Located 50 minutes from New Westminster, this airport focuses on low-cost carriers and regional flights.

Bus Stations Near New Westminster

  1. New Westminster SkyTrain Station: This station connects to the Greater Vancouver SkyTrain network and has buses that connect to other parts of the city.

Other Options

  1. Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in New Westminster.

How to Get Around in New Westminster

New Westminster has an extensive network of buses, SkyTrains, and ferries that make it easy to get around the city. You can also rent a bike or walk to explore the city’s attractions and nature trails.

Where to Go Next from New Westminster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Westminster is a central location from which to explore other cities and destinations in British Columbia. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Vancouver: Located a 30-minute drive from New Westminster, Vancouver is a vibrant city with a thriving food and drink scene and many outdoor activities.

  2. Whistler: This world-famous resort town is a 2-hour drive from New Westminster and offers excellent skiing, snowboarding, and outdoor adventures.

  3. Victoria: Located on Vancouver Island, Victoria is a charming city with many historical buildings, gardens, and whale watching tours.

Conclusion

New Westminster is a hidden gem in British Columbia that offers a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, New Westminster has something to offer. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan the perfect trip to this beautiful city.
