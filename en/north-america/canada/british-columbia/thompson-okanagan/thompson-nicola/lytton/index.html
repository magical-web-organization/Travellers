Lytton may be a small village, but what it lacks in size it makes up for in natural beauty, rugged landscapes, and cultural significance. Situated in Thompson-Nicola, in Thompson-Okanagan, in British Columbia, in Canada, in North America, this tiny village is known for its stunning views of the Fraser and Thompson rivers, as well as an array of outdoor activities. If you like hiking, fishing, camping, or biking, then Lytton is an excellent destination for you.

Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to Lytton.

Introduction to Lytton

Lytton is a small village that is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. This place was once occupied by Nlaka’pamux people and is now considered a historical site for First Nations people in Canada. Located at the junction of the Fraser and Thompson Rivers, Lytton is known for its rocky terrain, scenic views, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Lytton

Here are some of the top attractions in Lytton that you must see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nlaka’pamux Nation Tribal Council Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Nlaka’pamux people. You can learn about the traditional lifestyle, customs, and rituals of the First Nations people through various exhibits, artifacts, and guided tours.

  • St. Bartholomew’s Anglican Church: This historic church dates back to the late 19th century and is a designated heritage site in Canada. The church is known for its beautiful interior and stained glass windows.

  • Kumsheen Rafting Resort: If you want to experience the thrill of white water rafting, Kumsheen Resort is the place to be. It offers rafting trips along the Thompson River, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Lytton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Skihist Provincial Park: This park is located just a few kilometers north of Lytton and offers stunning views of the Thompson River. It’s an excellent spot for hiking, fishing, and camping.

  • Lynton Canyon Trail: This 7.5 km trail is a popular hiking route that leads to a stunning waterfall and natural swimming pool. It’s an ideal spot for a summer picnic.

  • Fraser River Lookout: This lookout offers panoramic views of the Fraser River and the surrounding mountains. It’s a great spot for photography and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lytton

Lytton offers an array of hiking and biking routes that suit all fitness levels. Here are some of the top routes be sure to check out:

  • Kumsheen Trail: This 12 km trail runs along the Thompson River and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It’s a great route for intermediate bikers and hikers.

  • Botanie Mountain Trail: This 7 km trail is located in the nearby Botanie Valley and is a great destination for hikers. It’s a moderate level hike that offers beautiful views of the valley.

Best Things to Do in Lytton: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours to do in Lytton:

  • White Water Rafting: Experience the thrill of white water rafting on the Thompson River with Kumsheen Rafting Resort and other companies offering tours.

  • Fish the Rivers: The Thompson and Fraser rivers offer excellent opportunities for fishing enthusiasts to catch salmon and other fish.

  • Visit the Nearby Villages: There are several small communities nearby that are worth exploring to experience more of the scenic beauty and culture of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lytton

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most out of your stay in Lytton:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Nlaka’pamux Nation Tribal Council Museum and learn about the history and culture of the First Nations people.
2 Hike the Lynton Canyon Trail and swim in the natural pool.
3 Enjoy a day of white water rafting with Kumsheen Rafting Resort.
4 Visit the nearby villages to experience the scenic beauty and culture of the region.
5 Fish the Fraser River and Thompson River.
6 Hike the Botanie Mountain Trail.
7 Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of Skihist Provincial Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Lytton

Lytton offers a unique culinary experience that is a blend of traditional First Nations and Canadian cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Salmon: This is a traditional First Nations delicacy that is a must-try for seafood lovers.

  • Bannock: This is a traditional First Nations bread that is served with meals.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Thompson-Okanagan region is known for its world-class wines, so be sure to try some while you are in Lytton.

Where to Stay in Lytton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lytton offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the top options:

  • Kumsheen Rafting Resort: This resort offers a range of accommodations, from campgrounds to cabins, as well as white water rafting tours.

  • Retasket Lodge and RV Park: This lodge offers a range of accommodations, from hostel-style dorms to private rooms.

  • Lytton Hotel: This historic hotel dates back to the early 20th century and offers cozy rooms and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Lytton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Lytton without these souvenirs:

  • Handmade First Nations Crafts: The Nlaka’pamux Nation Tribal Council Museum has a gift shop that sells traditional First Nations crafts such as jewelry, pottery, and carvings.

  • Local Wines: The Thompson-Okanagan region is known for its excellent wines, so bring home a bottle or two as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lytton

Lytton has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in First Nations traditions and customs. It’s essential to respect the cultural heritage of the place and learn about the local customs.

Best Time to Visit Lytton

The best time to visit Lytton is from May to October when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lytton

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the First Nations people.

  • Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and white water rafting.

Don’t:

  • Litter: keep the area clean and pristine.

  • Disrespect the natural environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lytton

Lytton is a generally safe place, but it’s always good to exercise caution when exploring the outdoors. Be sure to bring appropriate gear and follow safety precautions while indulging in outdoor activities.

Health in Lytton

Lytton has a small medical clinic, but there is no hospital in the area. Be sure to bring any required medication and have a contingency plan in case of a medical emergency.

Getting to Lytton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lytton

The closest airport to Lytton is Kamloops Airport, which is about 2.5 hours drive away. Vancouver International Airport is another alternative, which is about 4 hours drive.

Bus Stations Near Lytton

Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to Lytton from nearby cities.

Other Options

Driving or renting a car is the best way to explore Lytton and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Lytton

Lytton is a small village, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Driving or renting a car is the best option if you want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Lytton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Kamloops: This city is located about 2.5 hours east of Lytton and offers several tourist attractions such as parks, museums, and restaurants.

  • Whistler: This mountain resort town is located about 4 hours north of Lytton and is known for skiing, hiking, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Lytton is a hidden gem in British Columbia and is worth exploring for its natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventures. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, Lytton offers something for everyone. Plan your trip to Lytton, and experience the natural beauty and cultural heritage of this small town.
