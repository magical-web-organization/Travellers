Introduction to Keremeos

Located in Similkameen, in Thompson-Okanagan, British Columbia, Keremeos is a charming and picturesque town that offers a glimpse into Canada’s rural lifestyle. Set amidst lush green valleys and surrounded by stunning mountains, the town is an ideal spot to escape the noise and chaos of city life. With a rich history, diverse culture, and friendly locals, Keremeos promises a wonderful vacation experience for travelers of all ages and interests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keremeos

Keremeos Grist Mill and Gardens Trail

A scenic and easy trail, the Keremeos Grist Mill and Gardens Trail is around 2.8 kilometers long and is suitable for all skill levels. The trail features beautiful gardens, a historic gristmill, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Kettle Valley Railway Trail

The Kettle Valley Railway Trail is an extensive network of hikes and biking trails that spans across 600 kilometers. The trail starts at the western shore of Lake Okanagan and passes through Keremeos. Highlights of the trail include tunnels, trestles, and stunning views of the countryside.

Local Culture and Customs of Keremeos

The town of Keremeos has a rich indigenous history dating back thousands of years. Visitors can find a plethora of indigenous arts and crafts at local shops and learn about the local history and culture at the Cawston Museum.

Best Time to Visit Keremeos

The best time to visit Keremeos is from June to September when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keremeos

Do:

  • Tread lightly on the indigenous lands and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local wines and fresh fruits and vegetables.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the wildlife.
  • Ignore the local culture and history.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keremeos

Keremeos is generally a safe town, but visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid theft and stay safe while hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Keremeos

Keremeos has decent medical facilities, including pharmacies and hospitals. Visitors must carry necessary medication and travel insurance.
