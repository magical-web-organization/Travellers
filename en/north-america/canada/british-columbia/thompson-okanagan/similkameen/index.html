Introduction to Similkameen

Similkameen is a beautiful region located in Thompson-Okanagan, British Columbia, Canada. This region is known for its charming small towns, scenic landscapes, and outdoor adventures. Similkameen is a perfect destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city, with opportunities for camping, hiking, biking, and wine tasting.

List of Places to Visit in Similkameen

Similkameen offers a wide variety of attractions to explore, from historical sites to natural wonders. Here are some places to visit in Similkameen:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Grist Mill and Gardens: Visit this 1877 water-powered flour mill, which is now a museum with guided tours, exhibitions, and special events.
  • Keremeos Museum and Visitor Centre: Discover the history of Keremeos and the Similkameen Valley through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • Princeton and District Museum and Archives: Explore the cultural heritage of Princeton, from First Nations history to modern-day life in the town.

Natural Attractions of Similkameen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manning Provincial Park: This park offers over 700km of trails for hiking and mountain biking, stunning views of the Cascade Mountains, and an abundance of wildlife.
  • Similkameen River: Go rafting or kayaking down this beautiful river that flows through the valley.
  • Summit Peak Trail: This 8km round-trip hike offers breathtaking panoramic views of the valley and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Similkameen

Similkameen offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for all skill levels. Here are some popular routes:

  • Kettle Valley Rail Trail: This 650km trail runs through the Similkameen Valley and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Manning Park Resort: Explore over 60km of hiking trails and 20km of mountain bike trails in this beautiful park.
  • Similkameen Trail: This 30km trail runs along the Similkameen River and offers stunning views of the valley and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Similkameen: Activities and Tours

Similkameen offers a variety of activities and tours for travelers to enjoy. Here are some popular things to do in Similkameen:

  • Wine Tours: Explore the many wineries and vineyards in the valley and sample some of the best wines in British Columbia.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of the Similkameen Valley while horseback riding with local outfitters.
  • Fishing: The Similkameen River is a prime location for fishing, with opportunities to catch rainbow trout, salmon, and steelhead.

Week-Long Itinerary for Similkameen

Here is a week-long itinerary for Similkameen that covers the best places to visit and things to do:

  • Day 1: Visit the Grist Mill and Gardens and enjoy a picnic in the park.
  • Day 2: Go hiking in Manning Provincial Park and have a picnic.
  • Day 3: Spend the day on a wine tour and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.
  • Day 4: Visit the Keremeos Museum and Visitor Centre and go horseback riding.
  • Day 5: Go fishing on the Similkameen River and enjoy lunch by the river.
  • Day 6: Explore the summit peak trail and have a picnic with panoramic views.
  • Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at a local spa and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Similkameen

Similkameen is known for its fresh, local food and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fruit: The Similkameen Valley is known for its fresh, local fruit, especially cherries and apples.
  • Bison: Enjoy a bison burger or steak at a local restaurant.
  • Bannock: This traditional First Nations bread is a staple of the Similkameen Valley.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Similkameen is home to many wineries and vineyards that produce some of the best wines in British Columbia.
  • Cider: Enjoy a crisp and refreshing glass of local cider.
  • Beer: Sample some of the best beers made in British Columbia at a local brewery.

Where to Stay in Similkameen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Similkameen offers a variety of accommodation options, from hotels to hostels to guesthouses. Here are some popular places to stay in Similkameen:

  • Manning Park Resort: Enjoy the beauty of Manning Provincial Park while staying in a cozy cabin or lodge room.
  • Keremeos Creek RV Park and Campground: Camp in the heart of the Similkameen Valley with amenities like laundry, showers, and a playground.
  • Copper Valley Resort: Stay in a cozy cabin with access to hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Similkameen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Similkameen offers many unique and locally-made products that make great souvenirs or gifts. Here are some things to buy in Similkameen:

  • Fruit: Bring home some of the fresh and delicious local fruit.
  • Artwork: Purchase a beautiful piece of art from a local artist.
  • Wine: Bring home a bottle (or several) of local wine to enjoy and share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Similkameen

The Similkameen Valley is home to many indigenous communities, including the Upper Similkameen Indian Band and the Lower Similkameen Indian Band. Visitors should be respectful of these communities and their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Similkameen

The best time to visit Similkameen is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and wine tours year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Similkameen

Do:

  • Be respectful of local cultures and customs.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the Similkameen Valley.
  • Try the local food and wine.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in natural areas.
  • Ignore posted signs and warnings.
  • Be disrespectful to others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Similkameen

Similkameen is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should take appropriate safety precautions, like:

  • Be aware of wildlife in the area.
  • Stay on designated trails and areas.
  • Watch for and obey any posted warnings or advisories.

Health in Similkameen

Similkameen has adequate healthcare facilities, but visitors should always travel with appropriate medical insurance and medication.

How to Get Around in Similkameen

The best way to get around Similkameen is by car, but visitors can also rent bikes, walk, or take local transportation.

Where to Go Next from Similkameen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Similkameen is located in a beautiful area of British Columbia, with many nearby destinations to explore. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Kelowna: Discover the beautiful Okanagan Valley and enjoy wine tours and outdoor activities.
  • Vancouver: Visit this vibrant city with access to restaurants, museums, shopping, and more.
  • Whistler: Enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, skiing, and mountain biking in this beautiful mountain town.

Conclusion

Similkameen is a beautiful destination with many attractions and activities to enjoy. Visitors can explore the natural beauty of the valley, sample local food and wine, and learn about the region’s cultural heritage. With this comprehensive travel guide to Similkameen, travelers can plan their perfect trip to this charming region of British Columbia.
