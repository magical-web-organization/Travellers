Introduction to Manning Provincial Park

Manning Provincial Park is a stunning natural reserve located in the Similkameen region of the Thompson-Okanagan area in British Columbia, Canada. The park encompasses an area of over 70,000 hectares and is home to diverse ecosystems, including subalpine meadows, forests, and lakes. The park offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Manning Provincial Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Manning Park Museum

Manning Park Museum is a must-visit destination for history lovers. The museum features exhibits that showcase the natural and cultural history of the park, from the indigenous people to the fur traders, gold miners, and early park rangers.

2. Allison Pass Summit

Allison Pass Summit offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is a great spot to learn about the history of the Trans-Canada Highway and the route used by First Nations people before European settlement.

Natural Attractions of Manning Provincial Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Cascade Lookout

Cascade Lookout is a popular day hike within the park, offering panoramic views of the Cascade Mountains. The trail is 6.5 km round trip and moderately challenging.

2. Lightning Lake

Lightning Lake is a stunning body of water surrounded by mountains and forests. The lake offers opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing. The Lightning Lake Loop Trail is a peaceful and scenic 9 km hike around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manning Provincial Park

Manning Provincial Park boasts over 200 km of hiking trails and numerous mountain biking routes. Some of the popular hiking/biking routes include:

  1. Monument 78 Trail
  2. Strawberry Flats Trail
  3. Windy Joe Trail
  4. Three Falls Trail

Local Culture and Customs of Manning Provincial Park

The park is home to the Syilx/Okanagan people, and cultural tourism experiences are available throughout the year. Visitors are encouraged to respect the natural environment and cultural heritage of the park.

Best Time to Visit Manning Provincial Park

The best time to visit Manning Provincial Park is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and dry, and the park’s trails and lakes are open to visitors. Winter activities, including skiing and snowboarding, are available from December to April.

Don’t:

  3. Damage or disturb park resources

Safety and Health

Safety in Manning Provincial Park

Visitors to the park should be aware of potential hazards, including wildlife encounters, steep cliffs, slippery trails, and extreme weather. Visitors are encouraged to come prepared with proper safety equipment and to follow park safety guidelines.

Health in Manning Provincial Park

Visitors to the park should be aware of potential health hazards, including exposure to sun and heat, dehydration, and food and waterborne illnesses. Visitors are encouraged to come prepared with proper health equipment and to follow park health guidelines.
