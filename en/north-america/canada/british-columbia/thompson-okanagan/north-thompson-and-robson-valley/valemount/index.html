Introduction to Valemount

Valemount is a charming mountain village located in the Robson Valley region of British Columbia, Canada. It is situated in a picturesque location surrounded by the Rocky Mountains, making it the perfect destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts. With its stunning natural beauty, small-town charm, and rich cultural heritage, Valemount is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for an authentic Canadian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Valemount

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Valemount Museum and Archives: Located in downtown Valemount, the museum offers a glimpse into the region’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • George Hicks Regional Park: This park has a traditional First Nations burial site and is home to a memorial that honours the contributions of local Japanese-Canadian pioneers.

Natural Attractions of Valemount: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Robson Provincial Park: This park is home to the second highest peak in the Canadian Rocky Mountains and offers numerous hiking trails and breathtaking views.
  • Kinbasket Lake: Located just outside Valemount, this stunning lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Canoe Mountain: The mountain offers scenic views of the valley below and is also a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valemount

  • Berg Lake Trail: This popular trail in Mount Robson Provincial Park is one of the most scenic hikes in Canada, offering stunning views of glaciers, waterfalls, and alpine meadows.
  • Cranberry Marsh Trail: Located in Valemount, this easy trail is perfect for hiking or biking and offers views of the local wildlife and wetlands.
  • Lost Creek Trail: This trail is popular for hiking, biking, and horseback riding and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Valemount: Activities and Tours

  • River Rafting: The swift waters of the Fraser River offer an adrenaline-pumping experience for adventure seekers.
  • ATV Tours: Explore the backcountry of Valemount on an ATV tour, offering stunning views and a chance to see local wildlife.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a leisurely ride through the countryside with one of Valemount’s local horseback riding tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valemount

Day Activity
1 Visit Valemount Museum and Archives, stroll through downtown Valemount, and enjoy a meal at one of the local restaurants.
2 Experience the wilderness with an ATV tour or horseback riding excursion.
3 Explore the beautiful Kinbasket Lake; fishing, swimming, and boating are popular activities.
4 Take an exhilarating river rafting trip down Fraser River.
5 Hike or bike the Cranberry Marsh Trail and Lost Creek Trail.
6 Visit Mount Robson Provincial Park, hike to Berg Lake, and take in the breathtaking views.
7 Spend a relaxing day in Canoe Mountain, enjoying the scenic views and soft hiking trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Valemount

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burgers: Made with local bison meat, this burger is a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Moose Stew: A traditional Canadian dish made with tender moose meat and root vegetables.
  • Wild BC Salmon: Served fresh, grilled, or smoked, the salmon in Valemount is rich in flavour and a local favourite.

Local Beverages

  • Valemount Beer: Made with locally-sourced ingredients, Valemount beer is a refreshing and flavourful choice.
  • Canadian Wine: Although not from Valemount, Canadian wine is praised for its flavour and quality and can be found at many local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Valemount: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Plus Valemount Inn & Suites: A hotel that offers luxury services at an affordable price.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Valemount: A comfortable and budget-friendly hotel that is conveniently located near local attractions.
  • Caribou Lodge: A guesthouse that offers a more intimate and traditional experience with its rustic cabins and friendly hosts.

What to Buy in Valemount: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork: The small town of Valemount is home to numerous talented artists who create unique paintings, jewellery, and pottery.
  • Maple Syrup: As a mainstay of Canadian cuisine, maple syrup is a popular souvenir for those travelling through Canada.
  • Adventure Gear: With Valemount being an adventure-seeker’s paradise, there are numerous local retailers selling high-quality outdoor gear and the latest equipment.

Local Culture and Customs of Valemount

Valemount has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and it is important to respect and appreciate the local customs and traditions. The community places a strong emphasis on environmental conservation and sustainability, and visitors are encouraged to minimize their impact on the local surroundings and support local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Valemount

The best time to visit Valemount is during the summer months (June - August). During this time, there are numerous outdoor activities and events taking place, from hiking and biking to music festivals and cultural events. However, autumn (September - November) is also a popular time to visit, as the fall colours are exceptionally beautiful and the weather is usually mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valemount

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and limit your impact on the surroundings.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the environment.
  • Ignore the advice of local guides or park rangers.
  • Cause disturbances to the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valemount

Valemount is a safe and welcoming destination for visitors, with a low crime rate compared to other Canadian cities. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows, avoiding dark and secluded areas at night, and being wary of pickpockets and scams.

Health in Valemount

Visitors to Valemount should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage, as healthcare services can be expensive in Canada. It is important to take care when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking and rafting, to avoid injury or accident. Visitors should also be aware of the signs of altitude sickness, as Valemount is located at a high elevation.

Getting to Valemount: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Valemount

The closest airport to Valemount is the Kamloops Airport, approximately 290 km away.

Bus Stations Near Valemount

Valemount is serviced by the Greyhound Bus Terminal, which connects Valemount with other cities within British Columbia, such as Vancouver and Kelowna.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Valemount, as the town is located on the Yellowhead Highway. However, it is important to note that winter driving conditions can be challenging, and visitors should ensure that they have snow tires and chains on their vehicles.

How to Get Around in Valemount

Valemount is a small and walkable town, and visitors can easily explore downtown and its attractions on foot. However, to access the outdoor activities and attractions outside of Valemount, such as the nearby parks and lakes, visitors will need to drive or take a tour.

Where to Go Next from Valemount: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jasper National Park: Located just over the border in Alberta, Jasper National Park is a world-renowned destination for outdoor adventure and wildlife viewing.
  • Wells Gray Provincial Park: Located approximately 200 km south of Valemount, Wells Gray Provincial Park is a wilderness area with numerous hiking trails and stunning waterfalls.
  • Mount Revelstoke National Park: Located approximately 200 km southwest of Valemount, this park is known for its stunning alpine scenery and hiking trails.

Conclusion

With its stunning scenery, rich culture, and numerous outdoor activities, Valemount is a destination that should not be missed. Whether you are hiking to Berg Lake, rafting down Fraser River, or simply enjoying the local cuisine, Valemount has something for everyone. We hope that this travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your visit to this beautiful mountain town.
