Introduction to Wells Gray Provincial Park

Nestled in the heart of Thompson-Okanagan, Wells Gray Provincial Park is a nature lover’s paradise, offering breathtaking landscapes, tranquil lakes, and awe-inspiring waterfalls. The park spans over 540,000 hectares of unspoiled wilderness and is home to an array of flora and fauna that are native to the region.

Named after Arthur Wellesley Gray, a British MP and advocate of Canadian Pacific Railway, Wells Gray Provincial Park is one of the most scenic and biodiverse parks in Canada. Whether you’re a seasoned nature enthusiast or a novice traveler looking for adventure, Wells Gray Provincial Park has something for everyone.

Best Time to Visit Wells Gray Provincial Park

The best time to visit Wells Gray Provincial Park is between May and October when the weather is warm and the flora and fauna are at their peak. The park is open year-round, but some areas may be inaccessible during winter due to heavy snowfall.

Safety in Wells Gray Provincial Park

Wells Gray Provincial Park is safe for visitors, but it pays to take precautions and be prepared. Here are some safety tips:

  • Let someone know your itinerary if you’re hiking alone

Health in Wells Gray Provincial Park

Wells Gray Provincial Park has a low incidence of diseases, but it’s still important to apply good hygiene practices. Here are some health tips:
