Introduction to Apex Mountain Ski Resort

Nestled in the Okanagan Valley, in the heart of British Columbia’s Thompson-Okanagan region, you’ll find Apex Mountain Ski Resort. This hidden gem is a paradise for winter sports enthusiasts. With an annual snowfall of 600cm (20 feet), Apex has something to offer for everyone, including skiing, snowboarding, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and more.

Apart from the winter activities, the resort also offers year-round entertainment, including hiking, biking, and a host of other outdoor adventures. And when you’re done with a day on the mountain, you can relax and enjoy the vibrant après-ski culture, complete with delicious local cuisine and award-winning wine.

List of Places to Visit in Apex Mountain Ski Resort

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Apex Mountain Resort History and Heritage Exhibit: A free museum located inside the Apex Mountain Resort Lodge. Here you’ll learn about the history of the resort, which dates back to the 1960s, and how it has evolved over the years. You can also see memorabilia and artifacts from Apex’s past.

  • Penticton Museum and Archives: Located in Penticton, a 50-minute drive from Apex, this museum pays homage to the area’s First Nations, pioneers, and European settlers. You’ll find artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits that show the region’s rich cultural and natural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Apex Mountain Ski Resort: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nickel Plate Lake: A beautiful glacial lake located about 30 minutes from the Apex Mountain Resort. In summer, you can swim, hike, or fish for trout, while in winter, you can ice skate or go ice fishing.

  • Apex Mountain Resort Bike Park: With over 35km of bike trails, this intermediate to advanced mountain bike park has something for everyone. You can push your skills on dirt jumps, berms, and rock gardens, or take a scenic ride through the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apex Mountain Ski Resort

  • Mount Baldy Hiking Trail: A must-do 12km hike that takes you to the summit of Mount Baldy, where you’ll have breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, valleys, and lakes. The trail is moderate to difficult, with an elevation gain of 700m/2300ft.

  • Apex Mountain Resort Bike Park Trails: With over 35km of bike trails, this intermediate to advanced mountain bike park has something for everyone. You can push your skills on dirt jumps, berms, and rock gardens, or take a scenic ride through the forest.

Best Things to Do in Apex Mountain Ski Resort: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: With over 100 ski runs and a mix of terrain for all levels, Apex Mountain Resort is a winter sports enthusiast’s dream destination. You can take lessons, improve your skills or enjoy the fun of skiing with friends and family.

  • Nordic Skiing: The resort has 56km of cross-country skiing trails that offer beautiful mountain vistas.

  • Snowshoeing: If you want to experience the mountain without the crowds and explore trails not open to skiers, snowshoeing can be the best option.

Week-Long Itinerary for Apex Mountain Ski Resort

Day 1: Arrive at the resort, check-in, and get familiarized with the slopes by taking a beginner ski lesson.

Day 2: Take the Mount Baldy Hiking Trail. It’s a 12km hike that takes about 6 hours.

Day 3: Nordic skiing, with 56km of trails it certainty won’t be boring.

Day 4: Take a ski lesson to improve your skills or explore trails in the resort you haven’t yet visited.

Day 5: Snowshoeing, for a quieter and more peaceful adventure around the mountain.

Day 6: Hit the Apex Mountain Resort Bike Park and ride some of the 35km+ of trails

Day 7: Depart from Apex Mountain Ski Resort

Local Culture and Customs of Apex Mountain Ski Resort

The local residents have deep ties to the land and have built a community around the winter and summer activities in the area. The First Nations peoples’ history of the region is important. Respect is expected towards the environment.

Best Time to Visit Apex Mountain Ski Resort

The best time to visit Apex Mountain Ski Resort is during the winter season, from December to April, when the gathering snow peaks at 600cm. However, visitors still can enjoy the amazing natural environment in the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apex Mountain Ski Resort

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife

  • Try locally-produced food, beverages, and wine

Don’t:

  • Damage the flora or fauna

Safety and Health

Safety in Apex Mountain Ski Resort

  • Follow the signs and warning signs, and never stray off the designated trails.

  • Ski in groups to ensure your safety. Have an avalanche safety kit.

Health in Apex Mountain Ski Resort

  • Protect yourself from the sun and wind by wearing appropriate clothing and sunscreen.

  • The high altitude can affect visitors’ physical stamina, give time to adjust.

Getting to Apex Mountain Ski Resort: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apex Mountain Ski Resort

  • Kelowna International Airport is located about 90 minutes from the resort.

Bus Stations Near Apex Mountain Ski Resort

  • Penticton bus terminal, located about 50 minutes’ drive from the resort.

Other Options

  • Car rental is the best option to get to the resort and reach the sights in the surrounding region.

How to Get Around in Apex Mountain Ski Resort

  • You can get around Apex Mountain Ski Resort by foot or bike during the summer season when the roads are open. During winter, skiing and snowshoeing are the best methods.

Where to Go Next from Apex Mountain Ski Resort: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Penticton: A small, laid-back town at the south end of Okanagan Lake.

  • Summerland: A picturesque town. Include wine tasting as a part of your itinerary.

Conclusion

Apex Mountain Ski Resort is a stunning location that is packed with things to see and do, year-round. With its picturesque views, beautiful terrain, and friendly people, Apex has something for everyone. From skiing and snowboarding in the winter to hiking and biking in the summer, this resort is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Plus, with an excellent food and drink scene, cultural and natural attractions, and a myriad of accommodation options, Apex is an experience you’ll never forget.
