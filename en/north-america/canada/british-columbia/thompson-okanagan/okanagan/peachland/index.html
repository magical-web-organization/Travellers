Introduction to Peachland

Located in the Okanagan Valley, Peachland is a charming town in British Columbia, Canada. Named after the peach orchards that once flourished in the area, Peachland offers a delightful blend of outdoor adventure, culture, and scenic views. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or history buff, Peachland has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Peachland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Peachland Museum
    Located in the heart of downtown, the Peachland Museum offers a glimpse into the town’s rich history. The museum has a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents showcasing the early settlement days, railway history, and local industries.

  • Peachland Historical Society Heritage Park
    Nestled on a hill overlooking Okanagan Lake, the Peachland Historical Society Heritage Park is a beautiful open-air museum that takes visitors back in time. The park has several historic buildings, including a schoolhouse, church, and blacksmith shop.

Natural Attractions of Peachland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Peachland Pier
    Stretching over 200 meters into Okanagan Lake, the Peachland Pier is a popular spot for fishing, strolling, and taking in the panoramic views. The pier is also a great place to watch the sunset.

  • Scenic Drive on Highway 97
    Driving along Highway 97 is a must-do experience for anyone visiting Peachland. The highway offers spectacular views of the lake, mountains, and vineyards.

  • Trepanier Creek Greenway Regional Park
    Located in the heart of Peachland, the Trepanier Creek Greenway Regional Park is a beautiful place for hiking, birdwatching, and exploring nature. The park has several trails that wind through forests, meadows, and streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peachland

Peachland offers several scenic hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Pincushion Mountain Trail
    One of Peachland’s most challenging hikes, the Pincushion Mountain Trail offers breathtaking views of Okanagan Lake. The trail is 6.8 kilometers long and takes around 3-4 hours to complete.

  • Peachland Creek Loop Trail
    A moderate hike that takes visitors through a forested trail, past a waterfall, and along a creek. The trail is 5.4 kilometers long and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

  • Mount Drought Trail
    A short but challenging hike that rewards visitors with a stunning view of the lake and valley. The trail is 3.5 kilometers long and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Peachland: Activities and Tours

  • Okanagan Lake Boat Tour
    Take a boat tour of Okanagan Lake and explore the hidden coves, beaches, and vineyards. The tour includes a visit to the Rattlesnake Island, the home of the legendary Ogopogo.

  • Peachland Farmer’s and Craft Market
    Held every Sunday from May to September, the Peachland Farmer’s and Craft Market is a great place to sample local produce, artisanal cheeses, and handmade crafts.

  • Peachland Zipline Adventures
    Experience the thrill of ziplining over a pristine canyon and see Peachland from a whole new angle. The zipline adventure includes six different ziplines and a suspension bridge.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peachland

Day 1: Visit Peachland Museum and Historical Society Heritage Park
Day 2: Explore Trepanier Creek Greenway Regional Park and go hiking
Day 3: Take a boat tour on Okanagan Lake and visit Rattlesnake Island
Day 4: Drive along Highway 97 and visit local wineries Day 5: Go ziplining at Peachland Zipline Adventure

Food and Drink Scene of Peachland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Okanagan Cherries
    Peachland is famous for its juicy and sweet Okanagan cherries. Visitors can find them at local farmer’s markets and fruit stands.

  • Gourmet Pizzas
    Peachland has several restaurants that serve delicious gourmet pizzas made with local ingredients. Be sure to try the Seafood Pizza at Gasthaus on the Lake.

  • Local Craft Beer
    Peachland has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors can sample local brews at any of the town’s microbreweries. Peak Brewery’s Peachland Pale Ale is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Okanagan Wine
    Peachland is part of the Okanagan Wine Region, which produces some of the world’s best wines. Visitors can sample award-winning wines at any of the local wineries.

  • Craft Cider
    Peachland has several craft cideries that produce refreshing and delicious Apple and Pear ciders. Be sure to try the Lost Orchards’ Peach Cider.

Where to Stay in Peachland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peachland has several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the most popular places include:

  • Peachland Eagles Nest B&B
    A cozy and comfortable bed and breakfast that offers breathtaking views of the lake and valley.

  • Ponderosa Point Resort
    A lakeside resort that offers fully equipped cottages, cabins, and campsites.

  • Canadian Lakeview Estates
    A family-friendly resort that offers fully equipped vacation rentals and townhouses.

What to Buy in Peachland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Okanagan Wine
    Bring home a bottle of Okanagan wine, and savor the tastes and flavors of the region long after your trip is over.

  • Artisanal Cheeses
    Peachland has several artisanal cheese makers that produce delicious cheeses made with local ingredients. Be sure to try the Lavender Gouda at Carmelis Goat Cheese.

  • Local Honey
    Peachland’s local honey is famous for its rich and distinct flavor. Visitors can find it at any of the local farmer’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Peachland

Peachland is a small town that prides itself on its warm hospitality and friendly people. Visitors should respect the town’s culture and customs and be courteous to their fellow residents.

Best Time to Visit Peachland

The best time to visit Peachland is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm, and the town is buzzing with activity. Visitors can enjoy outdoor adventures, farmer’s markets, and local events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peachland

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and customs.
  • Explore the local vineyards, orchards, and farmer’s markets.
  • Take part in outdoor activities like hiking, ziplining, and boating.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to other visitors or residents.
  • Ignore local customs or laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peachland

Peachland is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Some of the safety tips include:

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings.
  • Avoid hiking alone and stick to designated trails.
  • Lock your car and secure your valuables when parking.

Health in Peachland

Peachland has a range of medical facilities, including clinics, hospitals, and pharmacies. Visitors should bring their necessary medications and ensure they have travel insurance.

Getting to Peachland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Peachland is easily accessible by road, air, and bus.

Airports Near Peachland

  • Kelowna International Airport (YLW): located 36 km (35 mins) away from Peachland

  • Penticton Regional Airport (YYF): located 53 km (45 mins) away from Peachland

Bus Stations Near Peachland

  • Greyhound Bus Station: located in Kelowna, 38 km (40 mins) away from Peachland

Other Options

  • Uber and Taxi Services: available in Peachland and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Peachland

Visitors can explore Peachland on foot, by bike, or by car. The town is compact and easy to navigate, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Peachland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Peachland is located in the heart of the Okanagan Valley and is surrounded by several exciting destinations. Some of the popular nearby destinations include:

  • Kelowna: 38 km (40 mins) away from Peachland. A vibrant city that offers a range of cultural and outdoor activities.

  • Penticton: 53 km (45 mins) away from Peachland. A beautiful lakeside town that offers world-class wineries and outdoor adventures.

  • Naramata: 50 km (45 mins) away from Peachland. A picturesque village that’s famous for its wineries, orchards, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Peachland is a hidden gem that offers an enchanting blend of outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Peachland has something to offer for everyone.
