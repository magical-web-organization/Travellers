Introduction to Lake Country

Lake Country is a small, picturesque town located in the Thompson-Okanagan region of British Columbia, Canada. It is situated between two big lakes, Okanagan Lake and Kalamalka Lake, offering visitors spectacular views of both water bodies.

Lake Country is popular with visitors for its beautiful natural surroundings, outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage. The town is the home to many heritage sites, art galleries, museums, and historical buildings, which reflect the region’s fascinating history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Country

Lake Country has a fascinating history and culture, which visitors should experience. Okanagan people have been residing in the region for thousands of years. These indigenous people are known to be hunters who lived along the water bodies during the summer season and moved upland to hunt bigger games in the winter season.

Best Time to Visit Lake Country

The best time to visit Lake Country is during the summer season, which lasts from June to September. This period provides visitors with ample opportunities to indulge in water sports, go for hikes, and explore other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Country

Do:

  2. Carry your trash with you.
  3. Try to learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  2. Touch or disturb the animals
  3. Use portable barbecues in parks or natural surroundings
