Introduction to Big White

Located in the Thompson-Okanagan region of British Columbia, Canada, Big White is a winter wonderland that offers a wide range of activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. It is a renowned ski resort that boasts over 118 runs and ten lifts, making it the third-largest ski resort in British Columbia. Besides skiing and snowboarding, Big White also offers many other winter sports, such as ice skating, snowshoeing, tubing, and even dog sledding.

This travel guide will help you explore Big White and make the most of your trip with a comprehensive list of things to see and do, recommendations for food and accommodations, tips for safety, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Big White

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Big White is primarily known for its winter sports, there are a few culturally significant attractions that visitors should not miss.

Kettle Valley Trestles

A popular attraction for hiking and cycling enthusiasts, the Kettle Valley Trestles are a collection of historic railway bridges that were built more than 100 years ago and now form part of the Trans-Canada Trail. Visitors can take a scenic journey through the mountains and valleys while learning about British Columbia’s fascinating history in this area.

Kelowna Art Gallery

The Kelowna Art Gallery is a contemporary art space located in the nearby town of Kelowna that showcases both local and international art. With over 800 pieces in its permanent collection and a rotating selection of exhibitions, there is always something new to see.

Natural Attractions of Big White: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Big White is famous for its scenic natural beauty, and there are many opportunities to explore its natural wonders.

Snowshoeing

Snowshoeing is a fun and unique way to explore the winter landscape at Big White. Visitors can rent snowshoes and follow a guided tour or explore the trails on their own.

Ice Skating

One of the best ways to enjoy the winter wonderland is to go ice skating. Big White offers several ice rinks, including an outdoor rink, perfect for families looking for a fun activity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big White

Big White is an excellent destination for those seeking adventure on foot or by bike. Here are a few of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

The Alpine Trail

This hiking trail is a moderate-difficulty, four-kilometre trek through the alpine wilderness. Along the trail, hikers can enjoy stunning views of the Monashee Mountains and explore the unique flora and fauna of the area.

The Big White Bike Park

The Big White Bike Park features 19 trails that range in difficulty from beginner to expert. It is a great destination for mountain bikers of all skill levels, with stunning views of the mountains and endless mountain biking adventures to be had.

Best Things to Do in Big White: Activities and Tours

Big White has a wealth of winter activities on offer that will keep visitors entertained from dawn until dusk. Here are a few of the best things to do in Big White.

Skiing and Snowboarding

With over 118 runs and ten lifts, Big White Ski Resort is a world-renowned destination for skiing and snowboarding.

Tubing

Visitors can experience the thrill of tubing at Big White’s Happy Valley Adventure Park. The park is home to over 20 snow tubing lanes, making it a fun, family-friendly activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Big White

To make the most out of your trip to Big White, here is a week-long itinerary that includes the best activities and attractions in the area.

Day 1: Arrival and Check-in

Arrive in Big White and check into your accommodation. Familiarize yourself with the area and enjoy a relaxing night in.

Day 2: Skiing and Snowboarding

Enjoy a full day of skiing and snowboarding at Big White Ski Resort.

Day 3: Snowshoeing

Take a guided snowshoeing tour through the alpine, taking in the stunning views and unique natural features of the area.

Day 4: Ice Skating and Curling

Spend a day at the ice rink, either practicing your ice skating skills or enjoying a friendly game of curling.

Day 5: Kelowna Art Gallery

Take a day trip to the Kelowna Art Gallery to explore the latest exhibitions and contemporary art on display.

Day 6: Tubing and Dog Sledding

Spend the day at Happy Valley Adventure Park tubing or taking a dog sledding tour.

Day 7: Departure

Check out of your accommodation and depart Big White.

Food and Drink Scen of Big White

Big White has an excellent food and drink scene. Here is what to eat and drink while you are there.

Must-Try Dishes

Poutine

This traditional Canadian dish is made with crispy fries, melted cheese curds, and rich gravy. It is a hearty meal served at most restaurants and cafes in Big White.

Local Beverages

Okanagan Wine

The Okanagan Valley is known for producing some of the world’s best wines. While in Big White, be sure to sip on a glass of local varietals.

Where to Stay in Big White: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Big White has several accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay:

White Crystal Inn

The White Crystal Inn is a ski-in/ski-out hotel located at the base of the mountain. It offers comfortable rooms and suites perfect for families and couples on a mid-range budget.

Stonebridge Lodge

These luxurious mountain lodges boast spacious living areas with stone fireplaces and modern amenities, such as heated floors, hot tubs, and private balconies.

What to Buy in Big White: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Big White Apparel

Big White apparel, including hoodies, t-shirts and hats, are perfect souvenirs to remind you of your winter holiday. You can purchase this merchandise at any of the retail stores located in the resort area.

Local Culture and Customs of Big White

The local culture in Big White is heavily influenced by its European roots and alpine traditions. You will find excellent food, sports, and entertainment opportunities that reflect this culture. Locals are friendly and passionate about the unique beauty of their home.

Best Time to Visit Big White

Big White is a winter resort, and the best time to visit is from November to April when the ski resorts are in operation and there are plentiful outdoor activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big White

Do:

  • Dress warmly
  • Be mindful of others on the slopes
  • Respect the environment and wildlife

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Break rules at the ski resort
  • Overexert yourself, especially if you aren’t used to the altitude.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big White

Big White’s ski resort is well-equipped to handle any emergencies, including accidents on the slopes. Beware of possible avalanches and ensure you’re well-prepared before embarking on any outdoor adventure in the winter.

Health in Big White

The high elevation and cold weather can be challenging for those who are not used to it. Stay hydrated to avoid altitude sickness, and take extra precautions to protect yourself from slip-and-fall accidents on the snow and ice.

Getting to Big White: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Big White

The closest airports to Big White are Kelowna International Airport (YLW) and Vancouver International Airport (YVR).

Bus Stations Near Big White

Greyhound offers bus services from Vancouver to Kelowna or Big White. From Kelowna, other bus services will take you to the snow-covered resort.

Other Options

If you’re hiring a rental car, the journey from Kelowna or Vancouver is scenic, so take the time to enjoy the drive. There are plenty of service stations and convenience stores on the way too.

How to Get Around in Big White

Most of Big White’s attractions and accommodations are conveniently located within walking distance of each other, but if you need transportation, shuttle services and taxis are available.

Where to Go Next from Big White: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kelowna

Kelowna is a short drive away from Big White, making it the perfect day trip destination. Kelowna is the largest city in the Okanagan Valley and is famous for its wineries, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

Silver Star Mountain Resort

Located two hours from Big White, Silver Star Mountain Resort is another popular ski resort that boasts over 130 trails and modern amenities.

Conclusion

Big White provides visitors with a magical winter experience, with endless outdoor activities, delicious food, and excellent accommodations. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your visit to Big White, and be sure to take time to check out all the winter wonderland has to offer.
