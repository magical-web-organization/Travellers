Armstrong is a small town located in the heart of Okanagan Valley, in Thompson-Okanagan, British Columbia, Canada. Although it is often overshadowed by larger cities in the region, such as Kelowna and Vernon, Armstrong offers a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities that make it a popular destination for travelers seeking a more laidback and authentic Canadian experience.

Introduction to Armstrong

Founded in the late 1890s, Armstrong owes its name to a former British Navy officer who settled in the area and opened a hotel. Today, the town has a population of approximately 5,000 people and is known for its agricultural heritage, especially for its production of apples, cherries, and other fruits. Visitors to Armstrong can expect to find a charming main street lined with heritage buildings, local shops, and restaurants, as well as plenty of outdoor recreation opportunities in the surrounding mountains, valleys, and lakes.

List of Places to Visit in Armstrong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Armstrong Spallumcheen Museum and Art Gallery: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Armstrong Spallumcheen region through exhibits of early pioneers, First Nations, and local artists.

  • Caravan Farm Theatre: This outdoor theater is situated on a 80-acre farm and produces unique plays and musicals that utilize the natural surroundings and farm animals.

Natural Attractions of Armstrong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silver Star Mountain Resort: Located just a 30-minute drive from Armstrong, Silver Star offers year-round activities such as mountain biking, hiking, skiing, and snowboarding. It also boasts stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • The Shuswap River: Flowing through Armstrong, the Shuswap River is a popular spot for tubing, kayaking, and canoeing. The scenery along the river is spectacular, with rocky outcroppings, forests, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Armstrong

  • Armstrong-Spallumcheen Rail Trail: A 13-kilometer trail that follows an abandoned rail line through the countryside, passing orchards, farms, and vineyards. Ideal for biking or walking.

  • Grizzly Peak Trail: A challenging 4.5-kilometer hike with steep elevation gain that rewards hikers with panoramic views of the valley and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Armstrong: Activities and Tours

  • Armstrong Farmers Market: Open every Saturday from April to October, the market is a great place to meet locals and sample fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products.

  • Okanagan Lavender & Herb Farm: This local farm offers tours of its fields of aromatic herbs and flowers, as well as a shop selling essential oils, soaps, and other lavender products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Armstrong

Day Activity
Monday Explore downtown Armstrong, visit the museum, and walk the Rail Trail.
Tuesday Drive to Vernon and visit the Allan Brooks Nature Centre and the Vernon Museum.
Wednesday Bike the Okanagan Rail Trail from Vernon to Lake Country.
Thursday Spend the day at Silver Star Mountain Resort, hiking, biking, or skiing depending on the season.
Friday Take a guided tour of local orchards and vineyards, such as Covert Farms or Ex Nihilo Vineyards.
Saturday Visit the Farmers Market in the morning and hike the Grizzly Peak Trail in the afternoon.
Sunday Relax by the Shuswap River, paddle down the river, or visit nearby Salmon Arm for more sightseeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Armstrong

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple pie: Made with local apples and buttery pie crust, apple pie is a staple dessert in Armstrong.

  • Cherry cider: The town’s cherry orchards produce some of the best cider around, available from local producers like BX Press Cidery.

Local Beverages

  • Okanagan wine: The region is known for its cool-climate varietals such as Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, with wineries such as Arrowleaf, Ex Nihilo, and Gray Monk offering tastings and tours.

  • Craft beer: Breweries such as Marten Brewing and The BX Press specialize in small-batch beers, often featuring locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Armstrong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Armstrong Inn: A cozy and affordable motel with updated rooms and a continental breakfast included.

  • The Castle at Swan Lake: This stylish hotel offers suites with kitchenettes, a pool, hot tub, and beautiful lake views.

  • Cedar Gate Resort: A quirky and rustic cottage resort situated on a quiet lake, with kayaks, paddle boats, and bikes available for guests.

What to Buy in Armstrong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cherry products: From cherry jam to cherry wine, there are plenty of cherry-infused products to bring home from Armstrong.

  • Local art: The art scene in Armstrong is thriving, with many local galleries and studios offering unique pieces by talented artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Armstrong

The town of Armstrong prides itself on its agricultural roots and community involvement. Visitors can expect to find a friendly and welcoming atmosphere, where events such as the annual IPE (Interior Provincial Exhibition) and rodeo draw crowds from all over the region.

Best Time to Visit Armstrong

The best time to visit Armstrong depends on your preferences and activities. Summer (June to August) offers warm and sunny weather for outdoor activities and festivals, while fall (September to November) is ideal for orchard visits and autumn foliage. Winter (December to February) brings snow sports and holiday festivities, and spring (March to May) offers wildflower blooms and shoulder-season prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Armstrong

Do:

  • Try the local produce and cuisine, such as fresh cherries, apple pie, and craft beer.

  • Respect the natural surroundings, including wildlife, forests, and waterways.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in outdoor spaces.

  • Ignore safety guidelines for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, or boating.

Safety and Health

Safety in Armstrong

Armstrong is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should always take precautions against theft and other crimes, especially in tourist areas during peak season.

Health in Armstrong

Healthcare facilities in Armstrong are limited, so visitors should be prepared with any necessary medications and first-aid supplies. Emergency services are available through Ambulance and Fire services.

Getting to Armstrong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Armstrong

  • Kelowna International Airport: Located approximately 78 kilometers south of Armstrong, Kelowna Airport is the closest major airport to Armstrong, with regular flights from destinations in Canada and the U.S.

  • Kamloops Airport: Located approximately 120 kilometers to the east of Armstrong, Kamloops Airport serves several Canadian cities with daily flights.

Bus Stations Near Armstrong

  • BC Transit: Offers regional bus service from Vernon and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A car rental is the most convenient way to explore the region and get to Armstrong.

  • Bike rental: Many local bike shops in the region offer bike rentals for the Okanagan Rail Trail and other nearby routes.

How to Get Around in Armstrong

A car is the most convenient way to get around in Armstrong and the surrounding area. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or take local bus services. Walking is also a great way to explore downtown Armstrong and nearby trails.

Where to Go Next from Armstrong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kelowna: Just a short drive south of Armstrong, Kelowna is a bustling city with plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

  • Vernon: Another nearby town, Vernon offers breathtaking views of Okanagan Lake, as well as historical sites and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Armstrong may be small, but it packs a big punch when it comes to natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a romantic weekend retreat or a family-friendly vacation, Armstrong is the perfect destination for anyone who loves the great outdoors and small-town charm. So pack your bags, hit the road, and get ready to discover all that this hidden gem of Okanagan has to offer.
