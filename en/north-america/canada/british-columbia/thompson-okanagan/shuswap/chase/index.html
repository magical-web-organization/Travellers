Introduction to Chase

Located in Thompson-Okanagan, British Columbia, Chase boasts a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and culturally significant attractions. A vibrant community awaits visitors, with a range of outdoor pursuits, delicious local cuisine, and a relaxed pace of life. Join us as we explore the best of Chase, from the must-visit museums to the most scenic treks.

List of Places to Visit in Chase

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chase is steeped in history, and visitors are spoilt for choice when it comes to exploring its cultural landmarks. Check out the Chase Museum to gain an insight into the area’s intriguing past, or visit the Art in the Park exhibition in Memorial Park, featuring a host of local artists.

Natural Attractions of Chase: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

As well as its rich history, Chase is also renowned for its breathtaking natural scenery. Take a leisurely stroll around Neskonlith Lake or embark on a more challenging hike up Mount Chase, which boasts unparalleled views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chase

For those seeking an active vacation, the nearby trails offer a range of options for both hikers and bikers. The Shuswap Lake Trail is a popular choice, with over 50 km of trails winding along the shoreline. For a more adrenaline-fueled experience, the Bear Creek Ridge trail offers challenging terrain and beautiful vistas.

Best Things to Do in Chase: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, Chase offers an array of activities, from zip lining and ATV tours, to lazy afternoons spent fishing on the lake. Don’t miss the opportunity to go horseback riding along the beautiful hillsides, or take a boat tour to soak up the stunning natural surroundings from the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chase

Planning a week in Chase? Take a look at our suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Chase

Must-Try Dishes

Chase is a haven for foodies, with a variety of local dishes to suit all tastes. Be sure to try the local Chinese cuisine at Dragon’s Palace and indulge in a hearty homemade burger at the Artisan House. For lighter bites, check out the Meridian Farm Market, which serves up fresh produce sourced from the surrounding farms.

Local Beverages

Wash down your local fare with a refreshing beverage. The Chase Distillery is a must-visit spot for fans of craft beer and spirits, while the nearby wineries offer award-winning wines served up with stunning views of the valley.

Where to Stay in Chase: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chase offers a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. From cozy bed and breakfasts, to lakeside cabins and budget-friendly motels. Check out Quaaout Lodge and Spa for an indulgent treat, or kick back in a more laid-back setting at Chase Country Inn.

What to Buy in Chase: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Chase empty-handed - take home a unique souvenir or local product. Try a jar of homemade jam from the Meridian Farm Market, or pick up a piece of locally-made pottery from Plough Hearth and Hollow. For adventurous foodies, the Chase Smoke & Gift Shop offers a range of locally-made beef jerky and artisan chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Chase

The people of Chase are friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of community. Take note of local customs, such as respecting the environment and adhering to campfire restrictions. Relaxed and familial, the people of Chase embody the best of a small town.

Best Time to Visit Chase

The best time to visit Chase is during the summer months, when temperatures hover in the comfortable 20-25°C range. This is also the peak season, so expect some crowds and higher prices. For a quieter experience, consider visiting in the fall, when the foliage turns bright red and gold, and prices drop.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chase

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Take advantage of the stunning outdoor activities
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Leave campfires unattended
  • Ignore fire bans
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

Safety and Health

Safety in Chase

Chase is a safe and welcoming community, but remember to take basic precautions such as locking your car and valuables. Stick to marked trails when hiking and biking, and respect all local laws and regulations.

Health in Chase

Visitors should take care to use sunscreen and bug spray when enjoying outdoor activities, and stay hydrated throughout the day. For medical emergencies, the Chase Medical Clinic is on hand to offer assistance.

Getting to Chase: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chase

The nearest airport is Kamloops Airport, roughly 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Chase

Greyhound buses service Chase year-round.

Other Options

Renting a car is the simplest and most convenient way to get to Chase and explore the surrounding region.

How to Get Around in Chase

Walking and biking are the most convenient ways to travel within Chase. Some visitors opt to rent a car to explore the area beyond Chase itself.

Where to Go Next from Chase: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There’s plenty to explore beyond Chase’s city limits. Venture east to Salmon Arm, a bustling hub of activity on Shuswap Lake; or head west to Kamloops, a city with a rich history and an abundance of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Chase has something for everyone, whether you’re seeking an active outdoor adventure, a cultural break, or a chilled-out retreat. From the rich local cuisine to the stunning scenery, plan your perfect getaway with our in-depth guide.
