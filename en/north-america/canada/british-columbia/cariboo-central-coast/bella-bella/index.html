Introduction to Bella Bella

Bella Bella is a hidden gem in the British Columbia region of Canada, known for its breathtaking beauty and ecological significance. Its coastal location, stunning landscapes, rich cultural history, and vibrant community make it a top destination for tourists looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the must-visit attractions of Bella Bella, the local culture, the food and drink scene, the best places to stay, and important travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Bella Bella

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to learn about the rich culture and history of Bella Bella is by visiting its famous museums and historical sites. The ‘Heiltsuk Cultural Centre’ is a must-visit destination that showcases the art, culture, and traditions of the Heiltsuk Nation. Another notable destination is the ‘Kettles Inlet Cannery,’ an old canning facility that has been transformed into a museum and now displays historical artifacts and exhibits related to the fishing industry.

Natural Attractions of Bella Bella: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bella Bella is home to several natural wonders that are unique to this region. The ‘Great Bear Rainforest’ is one such attraction and is known for its stunning waterfalls, alpine meadows, and rich wildlife. Take a guided boat tour to explore the coastlines and witness the horde of marine creatures, including whales, dolphins, and seals. Visit ‘Shearwater Marine Resort’ to enjoy kayaking, hiking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bella Bella

Bella Bella has a vast network of hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views and exciting challenges. The ‘Bella Bella Community Forest’ has several trails that can accommodate all levels of hikers and bikers, from novice to expert. The ‘Central Coast Trail’ is a 50-kilometer track that runs along the coast and will take you through several beaches, estuaries, and riverbanks.

Best Things to Do in Bella Bella: Activities and Tours

While in Bella Bella, participate in its numerous activities and tours to get a complete experience of the region. One of the most exquisite activities is whale watching. Witness migratory humpback whales, orcas, and porpoises in their natural habitat. Another must-do activity is fishing, where you can catch Chinook Salmon, Coho Salmon, Halibut, and Lingcod under the guidance of experienced guides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bella Bella

For a week-long itinerary in Bella Bella, follow this plan to cover most of the must-visit attractions:

  • Day 1: Kettles Inlet Cannery and Bella Bella Community Forest
  • Day 2: Village of Klemtu and ‘Spirit Bear Lodge’
  • Day 3: ‘Great Bear Rainforest’ and Whale Watching Tour
  • Day 4: Hiking to Bella Bella Peak and Bella Coola Valley
  • Day 5: Fishing at Namu Bay or Shearwater Marine Resort
  • Day 6: Heiltsuk Cultural Centre and Bella Bella Museum
  • Day 7: Kayaking and Paddle Boarding at Price Island Provincial Park

Food and Drink Scene of Bella Bella

Must-Try Dishes

Bella Bella’s local cuisine is influenced by the traditional Heiltsuk Nation. Try their signature dish ‘Smoked Fish,’ made from smoked salmon, halibut, or herring. ‘Bannock,’ a local bread made from flour and water, is a traditional bread that is often served as an accompaniment to the main course.

Local Beverages

Bella Bella’s cold climate makes it a suitable place for growing grapes, and several vineyards have opened up in the region. Try their sweet wine, the ‘Black Bear’, and the ‘Bella Bella IPA’, a locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Bella Bella: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

‘Bear Island Motel’ and ‘Bella Bella Inn’ are popular budget options for tourists. For a more luxurious stay, book a room at ‘Spirit Bear Lodge,’ a world-class eco-lodge.

What to Buy in Bella Bella: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Look out for ‘Bentwood Boxes,’ a Heiltsuk traditional art form that comprises intricately carved pieces of wood. Bella Bella’s pristine waters are also home to several marine creatures, which means that you can purchase fresh seafood like salmon, lingcod, and halibut.

Local Culture and Customs of Bella Bella

The Heiltsuk community is an essential part of Bella Bella’s culture and heritage. Respect their beliefs, respect sacred sites, and follow their customs when visiting their land.

Best Time to Visit Bella Bella

The best time to visit Bella Bella is from May to September, when the weather is mild and warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bella Bella

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Heiltsuk Nation.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrupt wildlife in any way.
  • Ignore the importance of safety precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bella Bella

Bella Bella is a relatively safe place to travel, and incidents of crime are rare. However, it’s always better to keep your belongings safe and not travel alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Bella Bella

There are no major health concerns in Bella Bella, but it’s always better to carry any necessary medication and take precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Bella Bella: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bella Bella

Bella Bella is served by ‘Campbell River Airport,’ which is around six hours from Bella Bella by road.

Bus Stations Near Bella Bella

Several bus companies operate buses to Bella Bella from major cities in British Columbia, including Vancouver and Victoria.

Other Options

Water taxis regularly operate between Bella Bella and several coastal towns in the region.

How to Get Around in Bella Bella

Bella Bella is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore the outer parts of the town, renting a car is the best option.

Where to Go Next from Bella Bella: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visit the ‘Great Bear Rainforest’ for experiencing more natural beauty. Another great option is to travel to Port Hardy and take the ‘Inside Passage Ferry’ to the other coastal cities of British Columbia.

Conclusion

Bella Bella is a place of natural beauty, rich culture, and unique experiences. With this comprehensive guide, you can get the best out of your trip to Bella Bella and enjoy all that this beautiful Canadian town has to offer.
