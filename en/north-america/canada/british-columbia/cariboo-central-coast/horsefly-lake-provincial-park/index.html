A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Located in Cariboo-Central Coast, in British Columbia, Canada, Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to experience the best of nature. With its pristine lake, rugged mountains, and beautiful forests, Horsefly Lake is a picturesque destination that offers a wide range of activities, from hiking and biking to fishing and swimming.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything you need to know to plan your next visit to Horsefly Lake Provincial Park, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get there.

Introduction to Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Horsefly Lake Provincial Park covers an area of approximately 825 hectares and is situated on the eastern shore of Horsefly Lake. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including moose, beaver, and black bears. Visitors can also get a chance to explore the history and culture of the area by visiting historical monuments, museums, and galleries.

List of Places to Visit in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Horsefly Museum and Heritage Society - This museum is dedicated to the preservation and promotion of the history of Horsefly and the surrounding area. Visitors can explore the exhibits or go on a guided tour to learn more about the region’s past.

  • Soda Creek Indian Band Historic Site - This historic site was once a hub for fur trade and gold rush activity in the area. Today, visitors can explore the historic buildings and structures and learn about the cultural significance of the site to the local Indigenous communities.

Natural Attractions of Horsefly Lake Provincial Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horsefly Lake - This beautiful lake is the centerpiece of the park and the perfect spot for swimming, fishing, and paddling. Visitors can rent a boat or canoe to explore the quiet bays and inlets or simply relax on the sandy beaches.

  • Hiking and Biking Trails - The park has a network of trails that wind through the forest and offer stunning views of the lake and mountains. One of the most popular trails is the Horsefly Lake Trail, which takes hikers along the shoreline and through the forest.

  • Wildlife Viewing - Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is home to a variety of wildlife, and visitors can spot moose, deer, bears, and other animals while exploring the park’s trails and waterways.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Horsefly Lake Provincial Park has a network of trails that are suitable for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Horsefly Lake Trail - A 10km loop trail that follows the eastern shore of the lake, offering stunning views of the water and surrounding mountains.

  • Miwahla Loop Trail - A 12km loop trail that takes hikers through the forest and along the river, offering glimpses of wildlife and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing - Horsefly Lake is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, with rainbow trout and kokanee salmon being the most common catches. Visitors can book a guided fishing tour or simply cast their line from the shore.

  • Canoeing and Kayaking - The calm waters of Horsefly Lake make it the perfect spot for paddling, and visitors can rent a canoe or kayak to explore the lake at their leisure.

  • Horseback Riding - Visitors can explore the park’s trails on horseback, enjoying the peaceful scenery and fresh air.

Week-Long Itinerary for Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Day 1: Arrive in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park and check into your accommodations. Spend the afternoon exploring the park’s trails or relaxing on the beach.

Day 2: Go fishing on Horsefly Lake, either on your own or with a guided tour.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Soda Creek Indian Band Historic Site and learn about the cultural significance of the area.

Day 4: Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the lake at your own pace.

Day 5: Go horseback riding on one of the park’s trails.

Day 6: Visit the Horsefly Museum and Heritage Society to learn more about the history of the area.

Day 7: Depart Horsefly Lake Provincial Park and continue your Canadian adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Salmon - Fresh, locally caught salmon is a popular dish in the area and is often served grilled or smoked.

  • Bannock - A traditional Indigenous bread that is often served with a variety of toppings, such as butter, jam, or honey.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - There are several microbreweries in the area that produce delicious craft beer using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Horsefly Lake Resort - This family-run resort offers spacious cabins and RV sites, as well as a restaurant and boat rentals.

  • Elysia Resort - This remote wilderness resort provides guests with an immersive retreat experience, featuring luxury accommodations, an on-site spa, and a private marina.

What to Buy in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art - The region is home to a thriving arts community, and visitors can find a variety of unique artwork and crafts made by local artists.

  • Wildflower Honey - Horsefly Lake is known for its delicious honey, which is produced by local beekeepers using wildflower nectar.

Local Culture and Customs of Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is located on the traditional territory of the Tsilhqot’in people, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the area’s Indigenous culture and history.

Best Time to Visit Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

The best time to visit Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is in the summer months, between June and September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and the lake is perfect for swimming and paddling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife

  • Take advantage of the park’s activities and attractions

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or trash behind

  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats

Safety and Health

Safety in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Visitors to Horsefly Lake Provincial Park should be aware of the potential dangers posed by wildlife, particularly bears and cougars. It is important to take precautions when hiking or exploring the park, such as carrying bear spray and making noise to avoid surprising wildlife.

Health in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

Visitors should also be aware of the risk of tick bites and should take precautions, such as wearing long pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Horsefly Lake Provincial Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

The most convenient way to reach Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is by car. The park is located approximately 77km from Williams Lake, and visitors can follow BC-97 N and Horsefly Road to reach the park. There is also a small airport in Williams Lake for those traveling by air.

How to Get Around in Horsefly Lake Provincial Park

The best way to get around Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is by hiking or biking on the park’s trails. Visitors can also rent a canoe or kayak to explore the lake.

Where to Go Next from Horsefly Lake Provincial Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Williams Lake - This charming city is known for its rodeo and cowboy culture and is home to several museums and galleries.

  • Quesnel - Located along the historic Gold Rush Trail, Quesnel offers visitors a chance to explore the area’s mining history.

Conclusion

Horsefly Lake Provincial Park is a stunning destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the best of nature, culture, and cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing escape or an adventure-packed vacation, Horsefly Lake has something for everyone. Be sure to plan your visit today and discover the beauty of this hidden gem.
