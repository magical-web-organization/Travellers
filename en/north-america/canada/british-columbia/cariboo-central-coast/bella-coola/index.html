Introduction to Bella Coola

Nestled amidst the stunning mountains and dense forests of British Columbia, Bella Coola is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of nature, culture, and adventure. The name “Bella Coola” is derived from the indigenous Nuxalk language and translates to “valley of the eagles.” Home to a vibrant community of people who possess an intimate understanding of the region’s history and natural wonders, Bella Coola proudly showcases the area’s heritage and traditions.

From hiking and wildlife viewing to exploring rich cultural sites and enjoying local delicacies, Bella Coola promises to be an unforgettable experience. This travel guide provides comprehensive details on the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, safety tips, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Bella Coola

Bella Coola boasts of a range of sites that cater to different interests and tastes. Below is a list of must-visit places in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bella Coola Valley Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the region’s history, culture, and artifacts. Visitors can learn about the Nuxalk First Nations people, the colonial fur trade era, and the challenges of early settlers.
  • Nuxalk Nation Cultural Centre: A stunning modern facility that presents the culture and traditions of Nuxalk nation through exhibitions, live performances, and art galleries.
  • Bella Coola Grizzly Tours Cultural Experience: A unique opportunity to learn about the Nuxalk culture, traditions, and beliefs through storytelling, dance, and song.

Natural Attractions of Bella Coola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tweedsmuir Provincial Park: One of British Columbia’s largest provincial parks that offers stunning views of glaciers, snow-capped peaks, and alpine meadows. Visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, kayaking, fishing, and wildlife watching.
  • Clayton Falls Recreation Site: A picturesque park ideal for picnicking, camping and swimming. The falls are a popular spot for people seeking a refreshing swim on hot days.
  • Bella Coola Helisports: A unique opportunity to traverse the stunning terrain of Bella Coola through helicopter expeditions. Visitors can enjoy guided heli-skiing or heli-hiking trips that offer breathtaking views of the valley and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bella Coola

Bella Coola is a hiker’s paradise, with an abundance of trails that cater to beginners and experienced hikers. Here are some popular hiking trails in the area:

  • Snootli Creek Regional Park: Two loops trails provide fantastic views of the Snootli Creek Valley and Coast Mountains, ranging from 1.5 km to 4 km in length.
  • Rainbow Range: A 25 km trek into the heart of Coast Mountains that offer spectacular views of alpine meadows, glaciers, and valleys.
  • Alexander Mackenzie Heritage Trail: A historic 420km trek that commemorates the 1793-94 exploration of Alexander Mackenzie.

Best Things to Do in Bella Coola: Activities and Tours

Bella Coola offers a range of activities to keep visitors entertained. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Whale Watching Tour: Experience the thrill of watching humpback whales, dolphins, orca, and porpoises in their natural habitats.
  • Fishing Expedition: Bella Coola is home to an abundance of fish species, including salmon, steelhead, and sockeye. Visitors can enjoy the thrill of angling while taking in the stunning scenery.
  • Horseback Riding Tour: Embark on a beautiful ride through pristine meadows, forests and along the banks of rushing rivers. A great way to explore the stunning landscapes of Bella Coola.

Food and Drink Scene of Bella Coola

Bella Coola offers some of the most delicious local cuisine and beverages that will tantalize your taste buds. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Salmon: Bella Coola is famous for its salmon, which is considered by many as the best in the world. Try the smoked salmon, grilled salmon or salmon chowder for an authentic Bella Coola experience.
  • Bannock: A traditional Indigenous bread that is deep-fried and served with honey or jam.
  • Wild Berries: Bella Coola is home to an abundance of wild berries, such as huckleberries, blackberries, and raspberries. Try them in jams or as a garnish on your salmon.

Local Beverages

  • Bella Coola Tea: A unique tea prepared by steeping cedar bark in hot water. It has a medicinal taste with a hint of sweetness.
  • Heliski Hefe: A craft beer made with locally harvested blueberries that offers a tart fruity flavour.
  • Nuxalkmade: A mead made with locally sourced honey that is a perfect accompaniment to wild salmon.

Where to Stay in Bella Coola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bella Coola offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Twin Peaks Resort: A cozy resort offering comfortable cabins that include private bathrooms, kitchens, and patios with mountain views.
  • Eagle Lodge: A luxurious lodge offering spacious rooms with en suite bathrooms, flat-screen TVs, and DVD players. It also has an outdoor hot tub and sauna.
  • The Bella Coola River Lodge: A family-owned lodge set amidst stunning natural surroundings that offer comfortable rooms with private bathrooms and access to a garden and BBQ facilities.

What to Buy in Bella Coola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bella Coola boasts unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for taking back home.

  • Native Art: The Bella Coola valley boasts an array of indigenous art and crafts featuring intricate carvings, paintings, and jewelry. Check out the Nuxalk Nation Cultural Center for the best selection.
  • Local Honey: Bella Coola is home to an abundance of bees, and their honey is known for its purity and rich flavor.
  • Wild Berries: Take home some locally sourced wild berries that are perfect for making jams and pastries.

Local Culture and Customs of Bella Coola

Bella Coola has a rich culture that is deeply rooted in the land, history, and traditions of the Nuxalk First Nations people. Show respect for the indigenous culture and traditions when visiting by attending cultural events and performances. Take note of any customs that are unique to the area and learn from the local residents.

Best Time to Visit Bella Coola

The best time to visit Bella Coola is during the warm summer months from June through August. This is the peak tourist season when the weather is perfect, and visitors can take advantage of the outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bella Coola

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife and follow the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and bring adequate gear for any outdoor activities.
  • Learn about and respect the Nuxalk culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or protected areas.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.
  • Bring pets or firearms into protected areas without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bella Coola

Bella Coola is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Wildlife can pose a danger, and it is essential to follow the guidelines and regulations provided by park officials.

Health in Bella Coola

Visitors should bring any necessary medication as the healthcare facilities in the area are limited. It is also essential to bring adequate sunscreen and bug sprays, especially if spending time outdoors.

Getting to Bella Coola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bella Coola

Bella Coola Regional Airport is the closest airport to the town, which is serviced by Pacific Coastal Airlines. Flights are available from Vancouver, Port Hardy, and Anahim Lake.

Bus Stations Near Bella Coola

There are no bus stations in Bella Coola, but visitors can take buses that service the neighbouring communities of Anahim Lake and Williams Lake.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Bella Coola via car, ferry, or private charter aircraft.

How to Get Around in Bella Coola

Visitors can rent cars or bicycles to explore Bella Coola independently. Taxis and shuttles are also available for transportation within the town and to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bella Coola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tweedsnair Provincial Park: One of the largest provincial parks in British Columbia, Tweedsnair offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.
  • Haida Gwaii: A stunning archipelago located off the coast of British Columbia, Haida Gwaii offers a range of cultural sites, outdoor activities, and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Bella Coola is an adventure and a touring paradise offering natural beauty and cultural experiences. Whether you choose to hike, fish, or attend cultural events, the area has something for everyone. Get ready to explore the heavenly Bella Coola valley!
