Introduction to Saint Pierre and Miquelon

Located off the coast of Newfoundland, Canada, Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a self-governing overseas collectivity of France. It is the only territory of the European Union in North America. Saint Pierre and Miquelon consists of eight islands, with Saint Pierre being the largest and most populated.

The islands are steeped in French history and culture, with a unique blend of North American and European influences. Visitors can enjoy the island life, explore the rich history, and indulge in delicious French cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Pierre and Miquelon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Pierre Cathedral: This beautiful cathedral is an iconic landmark in St. Pierre. It was built in 1905 and features beautiful stained glass windows.
  • Musee Heritage: This museum showcases the history and culture of St. Pierre and Miquelon. It houses over 6000 items related to the islands’ past.
  • Cemetery of British Sailors: This cemetery is a unique historical site, as it is the final resting place of over 180 British sailors who died in the Battle of the Islands in 1793.

Natural Attractions of Saint Pierre and Miquelon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grand Barachois: This scenic area is perfect for hiking, nature walks, and bird watching. It is also a great spot for outdoor picnics.
  • Pointe aux Canons Lighthouse: This stunning lighthouse is the perfect spot to take in the views of the ocean and the surrounding islands.
  • Langlade Island: The largest uninhabited island in the archipelago, Langlade is a haven for wildlife. Visitors can see nesting puffins, seals, and whales, among other species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Pierre and Miquelon

  • Pointe aux Canons Lighthouse Trail: This trail is a moderate hike that takes you through beautiful scenery and offers spectacular views of the ocean and the surrounding islands.
  • Miquelon-Langlade Trail: This trail is perfect for those looking for a challenge. It is a 26 km hike that takes you through rugged terrain and offers breathtaking views of the islands.

Best Things to Do in Saint Pierre and Miquelon: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour around the islands, which gives them the opportunity to see the islands from a different perspective and spot whales and other marine life.
  • Scuba Diving: Saint Pierre and Miquelon is known for its clear waters, making it the perfect place for scuba diving.
  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity on the islands, with anglers coming from all over the world to try their luck at catching cod and other fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint Pierre and Miquelon

Day 1: Explore the historic St. Pierre Cathedral and Musee Heritage Day 2: Hike the Pointe aux Canons Lighthouse Trail and enjoy the stunning views Day 3: Take a boat tour around the islands and spot whales and other marine life Day 4: Enjoy a relaxing day at one of the islands’ beautiful beaches Day 5: Go scuba diving and explore the underwater world of Saint Pierre and Miquelon Day 6: Visit Langlade Island and see nesting puffins and other wildlife Day 7: Experience the local culture and cuisine, including trying traditional dishes like salt cod and seafood stew.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Pierre and Miquelon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salt Cod: This is a traditional dish in Saint Pierre and Miquelon. The fish is salted and dried before being rehydrated and cooked with potatoes and other vegetables.
  • Seafood Stew: This hearty stew is made with a variety of seafood, including lobster, scallops, and shrimp, and is served with crusty bread.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Visitors can enjoy a variety of French wines, including Bordeaux and Burgundy.
  • Rhum Arrangé: This is a local specialty that is made by infusing rum with fruits, herbs, and spices.

Where to Stay in Saint Pierre and Miquelon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Robert: This hotel is located in the heart of St. Pierre and offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Gîte du Mont Sainte-Anne: This guesthouse is located on the picturesque Mont Sainte-Anne and offers breathtaking views and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Saint Pierre and Miquelon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Artwork: Visitors can buy beautiful artwork from local artists, including paintings, sculptures, and textiles.
  • Wine: Wine is a popular souvenir, and visitors can buy bottles of French wine to take home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Pierre and Miquelon

The people of Saint Pierre and Miquelon are proud of their French heritage and culture. Visitors can experience this through the local cuisine, music, and art.

The islands also have unique customs, such as the “Burning of the Sardine” festival, which is held on the eve of Lent and involves burning a sardine-shaped effigy.

Best Time to Visit Saint Pierre and Miquelon

The best time to visit Saint Pierre and Miquelon is during the summer months (June to August). The weather is mild, and the days are long and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint Pierre and Miquelon

Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Saint Pierre and Miquelon

There are no major health concerns in Saint Pierre and Miquelon. Visitors should make sure their routine vaccinations are up-to-date and take precautions against ticks and mosquitoes.

Getting to Saint Pierre and Miquelon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint Pierre and Miquelon

  • St. Pierre Airport: This is the main airport on the islands, and it offers flights to and from Canada and France.

Bus Stations Near Saint Pierre and Miquelon

There are no bus stations in Saint Pierre and Miquelon.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Saint Pierre and Miquelon by ferry from Fortune, Newfoundland.

How to Get Around in Saint Pierre and Miquelon

The best way to get around Saint Pierre and Miquelon is by car or bike. Visitors can rent cars or bikes from local rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Saint Pierre and Miquelon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newfoundland, Canada: Visitors can take a ferry from Saint Pierre and Miquelon to Newfoundland and explore this beautiful Canadian province.
  • Saint-Pierre and Miquelon-Langlade: A small island that is located just a short boat ride from the main islands.

Conclusion

Saint Pierre and Miquelon is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a unique blend of North American and European culture. From the island’s rich history to its stunning natural scenery, there is something for everyone to enjoy.
