If you’re planning a trip to Guatemala, don’t miss out on the Western Highlands region, where you’ll discover the charming and historic town of Solola. Nestled in the mountains overlooking stunning Lake Atitlan, Solola is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the beauty, culture, and history of Guatemala. In this comprehensive tour guide to Solola, we’ll show you the best things to do, places to visit, and more in this unforgettable destination.

Introduction to Solola

Solola is a small town in the Western Highlands region of Guatemala, located about 70 miles northwest of Guatemala City. The town is perched on a mountainside overlooking Lake Atitlan, one of the most stunning natural wonders in Guatemala. Solola is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and breathtaking scenery, making it a popular destination for travelers from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Solola

Here are some of the top places to visit in Solola:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Solola Market: One of the oldest and most historic markets in Guatemala, the Solola Market is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning about the local culture and traditions. The market features hundreds of vendors selling everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts.

  • Galeria de Arte Solola: This popular art gallery features the work of local artists and showcases the rich artistic heritage of Solola.

  • Iglesia del Calvario: This historic church dates back to the 16th century and is one of the oldest structures in Solola.

Natural Attractions of Solola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Atitlan: This stunning lake is one of the most popular destinations in Guatemala, known for its crystal-clear waters and dramatic mountain backdrop. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities on the lake, including kayaking, swimming, and fishing.

  • Chicabal Volcano: Chicabal Volcano is a popular destination for hiking and exploring. Visitors can hike to the top of the volcano for stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Nahualate Hot Springs: These natural hot springs are perfect for a relaxing soak after a long day of exploring Solola.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Solola

Solola is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking routes in Guatemala, including:

  • San Pedro Volcano Hike: This challenging hike takes visitors up to the peak of San Pedro Volcano for stunning views of Lake Atitlan and the surrounding area.

  • Eco Biking Tours: These guided tours take visitors on a scenic bike ride through the countryside surrounding Solola, with opportunities to stop and explore local villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Solola: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Solola:

  • Cooking Classes: Take a cooking class to learn about the traditional cuisine of Solola and Guatemala.

  • Mayan Spirituality Tour: This tour offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore Mayan spirituality and the local traditions of Solola.

  • Boat Tours of Lake Atitlan: Take a relaxing boat tour of Lake Atitlan for stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Solola

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Solola:

Day 1 Arrival in Solola, explore town center and market
Day 2 Hike up San Pedro Volcano
Day 3 Visit Lake Atitlan, take a boat tour
Day 4 Learn about traditional cooking in a cooking class
Day 5 Explore local villages on a guided bike tour
Day 6 Visit the Galeria de Arte Solola and other cultural attractions
Day 7 Departure from Solola

Food and Drink Scene of Solola

Solola is known for its delicious traditional cuisine and unique local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepian: A savory stew made with chicken or beef and a blend of spices, served with rice and tortillas.

  • Chiles Rellenos: A traditional dish made with bell peppers stuffed with meat and vegetables, then fried or baked.

  • Tamales: Corn masa dough stuffed with meat and spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and steamed.

Local Beverages

  • Atol: A traditional corn-based drink flavored with cinnamon, vanilla, and sugar.

  • Cervesa Gallo: The most popular beer in Guatemala, brewed locally in Solola.

  • Café Solola: A rich and flavorful coffee made from locally grown beans.

Where to Stay in Solola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Solola offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers on all budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay in Solola:

  • Casa Palopo: A luxurious boutique hotel located on the shores of Lake Atitlan, offering stunning views and world-class amenities.

  • Hostal Fe: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Solola, offering clean and comfortable accommodations.

  • Posada de Santiago: A charming guesthouse located on the outskirts of Solola, offering a peaceful and relaxing retreat.

What to Buy in Solola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Solola is known for its vibrant and colorful textiles, as well as its unique handicrafts and art. Here are some local products to bring home as souvenirs:

  • Huipiles: A traditional Mayan garment worn by women, featuring intricate and colorful embroidery.

  • Masks: Hand-carved wooden masks are a popular souvenir in Solola, representing traditional Mayan beliefs and rituals.

  • Candles: Local artisans in Solola create beautiful hand-poured candles in a variety of sizes and scents.

Local Culture and Customs of Solola

Solola is a proud and vibrant community with a deep respect for its rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Solola should be aware of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Respect for Elders: Traditional Mayan culture places great respect for elders and visitors to Solola should follow this custom.

  • Dress Code: When visiting churches or other cultural sites, it is recommended to dress modestly and respectfully.

  • Language: Spanish is the primary language spoken in Solola, although many locals also speak the indigenous language of Tz’utujil.

Best Time to Visit Solola

Solola can be visited year-round, with the most popular time being during the dry season from December to April. However, visitors should be prepared for cooler temperatures during the months of December and January. The rainy season runs from May to October, which can make hiking and outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Solola

Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow when visiting Solola:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially traditional dishes like pepian and chiles rellenos.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and language.
  • Do talk to locals to learn more about their culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wander around alone at night, especially in the town center.
  • Don’t take photos of people without their permission.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local traditions or culture.

Safety and Health

Solola is a safe destination for travelers, although visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing. Medical care is available in Solola, although visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance in case of emergency.

Safety in Solola

  • Be vigilant with your belongings and avoid walking around alone at night.
  • Stick to well-lit and busy areas when exploring the town center.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of money or valuables on you.

Health in Solola

  • Make sure you have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to Solola.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Drink bottled water or boil tap water before drinking.

Getting to Solola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Solola is accessible by both air and land transportation. Here are some of the options for getting to Solola:

Airports Near Solola

  • La Aurora International Airport (GUA): The nearest major airport located in Guatemala City, approximately 70 miles from Solola.

Bus Stations Near Solola

  • Terminal de Buses (Solola): The main bus station in Solola, offering connections to destinations throughout Guatemala.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: Hire a private car or shuttle to transfer you directly from the airport to Solola.

How to Get Around in Solola

Solola is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by taxi. Taxis are widely available throughout the town and can be flagged down on the street or hired from a taxi stand.

Where to Go Next from Solola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Solola is located near many other popular destinations in Guatemala, including:

  • Panajachel: A popular tourist town located on the shores of Lake Atitlan, known for its lively markets and stunning scenery.

  • Antigua: The former capital of Guatemala, located about 2 hours by car from Solola, known for its Spanish colonial architecture and vibrant cultural scene.

Conclusion

Solola is a charming and historic town located in the heart of the Western Highlands region of Guatemala. With its stunning scenery, rich culture, and vibrant traditions, there’s something for everyone in this unforgettable destination. Plan your trip today and start exploring all that Solola has to offer!
