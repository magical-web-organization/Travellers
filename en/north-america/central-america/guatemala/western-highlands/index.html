Introduction to Western Highlands

Located in Guatemala’s Western Highlands, this region boasts a stunning natural landscape with volcanic peaks, towering mountains, and shimmering lakes. The Western Highlands of Guatemala offers an unparalleled immersion into the authentic Maya culture, with friendly locals and bustling markets. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Western Highlands and explore its cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Western Highlands

The Western Highlands are full of exciting places to visit that offer a mix of cultural, scenic, and adventure experiences. Here are some places you should consider visiting on your trip to Western Highlands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chichicastenango: Famous for its vibrant market and 400-year-old church of Santo Tomás, which blends Catholicism with Mayan spirituality
  • Panajachel: A popular town located on the shore of Lake Atitlán, known for its vibrant nightlife, handicrafts, and water sports activities
  • Quetzaltenango: The second-largest city in Guatemala, known for its colonial architecture, hot springs, and local food scene
  • Xela: A university town located in the highlands, known for its relaxing atmosphere, language schools, and hiking trails
  • Iximche: A well-preserved ancient Maya city and one of the few that was not abandoned before the arrival of the Spanish

Natural Attractions of Western Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Atitlán: A breathtaking natural masterpiece surrounded by steep hills and three towering volcanoes, popular for kayaking, hiking, and swimming
  • Volcanoes: Three main volcanoes, Pacaya, Acatenango, and Fuego, offer an impressive view of the region from their peaks and excellent hiking opportunities
  • Semuc Champey: A natural wonder, consisting of crystal-clear emerald pools and freshwater rivers interconnected by cascading waterfalls surrounded by lush forests.
  • Huehuetenango: A mountainous region that offers hiking trails and bird watching activities

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western Highlands

The Western Highlands have numerous hiking trails and biking routes you can choose from, offering different levels of difficulty and meandering through the best natural wonders.

  • The Acatenango volcano hike: An overnight hike to the summit of Acatenango volcano offers a spectacular view of the Fuego volcano as it erupts.
  • San Pedro La Laguna Village to San Juan La Laguna bike ride: A scenic ride that includes breathtaking views of Lake Atitlán.
  • The Ixil Triangle hike: A trek to remote indigenous villages in the Cuchumatanes mountains offers unique insight into the indigenous cultures and traditions.
  • Candelaria to Santa María hike: A beautiful hike to the summit of the Santa Maria volcano, offering a stunning view of Lake Atitlán and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Western Highlands: Activities and Tours

The Western Highlands offer an array of activities that cater to different tastes, from gentle strolls through markets to adrenaline-pumping adventures. Here are some of the top things to do in the Western Highlands:

  • Visit Chichicastenango Market: Explore this all-week market selling handmade crafts and textiles, fresh produce, and spiritual offerings.
  • Take a boat tour on Lake Atitlan: Experience the spectacular scenery of the volcanoes and learn about the history and culture of the lake’s ancient Maya people.
  • Visit Quirigua ruins: Discover the ancient Maya city of Quirigua with impressive stelae and series of zoomorphic and anthropomorphic sculptures.
  • Soak in hot springs in Fuentes Georginas: Embrace the serenity of natural hot springs located in a stunning forested ravine.
  • Take an Arabic coffee tour: Experience the story of the coffee-making process, from bean to cup, and sample some exquisite coffee varieties.
  • Whale watch in Guatemala’s Pacific Coast: Witness the majestic whales frolicking in the ocean in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Western Highlands

If you have a week to explore Western Highlands, we recommend:

  • Day 1-2: Head to Panajachel and discover the lively local markets with colorful textiles, pottery, and traditional Maya clothing. Take a boat tour to explore different lakeside villages.
  • Day 3-4: Hike up to the summit of Volcan Pacaya and sip on authentic coffee from the local plantations.
  • Day 5-6: Visit the ancient ruins of Iximché, experience traditional textile making, and take a scenic drive along the Altiplano.
  • Day 7: Explore Quetzaltenango and its mineral springs before taking some time to visit the San Francisco El Alto market.

Food and Drink Scene of Western Highlands

Guatemalan cuisine wide range of options that feature traditional food enhanced by regional influences. Some local delicacies you should try are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chiles rellenos: A delicious, heartwarming dish made from peppers stuffed with minced meat, rice, and veggies.
  • Jocón: A hearty stew made with pieces of chicken, vegetables, and tomatillos, flavored with coriander, mint, and parsley.
  • Pepián: A national dish made by grinding ingredients like pumpkin and sesame seeds and flavored with spices like coriander, cumin, and chili pepper.
  • Kak’ik: A winter favorite for many locals, Kak’ik is a spicy turkey stew flavored with traditional ingredients and served with rice and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Coffee is one of Guatemala’s best-known products, with coffee plantations in the Western Highlands producing some of the best beans worldwide.
  • Maya Ka’an: This fruit liqueur, made from prickly pear cactus, is a delicacy of the region.
  • Atol de elote: A traditional drink made from corn, flavored with sugar, cinnamon, and vanilla.
  • Chocolate: The Western Highlands produce some of the best cacao beans, which are used to make a variety of chocolate products.

Where to Stay in Western Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Western Highlands offer a variety of accommodations, including luxury resorts, hostels, guesthouses, and B&Bs. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Atitlán: A luxury hotel with a beautiful view of Lake Atitlán.
  • Casa Palopo: A colorful boutique hotel located in Santa Catarina Palopo.
  • Hotel Posada de Don Rodrigo: A charming hotel with traditional Mayan architecture in Antigua Guatemala.
  • La Casa de Don Ismael: A budget-friendly guesthouse in the heart of Quetzaltenango

What to Buy in Western Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Western Highlands are famous for their colorful textiles, traditional clothing, and crafts. Here are some top picks for souvenirs and local products to take home:

  • Hand-woven textiles including colorful fabrics, rugs, and blouses, in vivid patterns and local styles
  • Handmade pottery: A fundamental traditional craft in Western Highland, pottery is a unique souvenir to take back home.
  • Coffee, chocolate, and honey: The Western Highlands produce some of the best coffee, chocolate, and honey, making them ideal souvenirs for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Western Highlands

The Western Highlands are home to several indigenous community groups, who mostly identify as Maya. Most of the population still keeps traditional clothes adorned with colorful embroidery and numerous bead works. When visiting the local markets, always be respectful of the vendors and haggle respectfully.

Best Time to Visit Western Highlands

The best time to visit Western Highlands is during the dry season, from late November to April, which offers warm days and a cooler night climate. Between June and November, The rainy season, brings landslides and roadblocks, making it difficult to navigate the region’s hilly terrain. Therefore, it is advisable to avoid the rainy season when touring Western Highlands.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western Highlands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and take part in local culture where possible
  • Hire local guides and support the small local vendors
  • Dress conservatively while visiting Mayan villages and churches

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs at ceremonies and rituals without permission
  • Don’t venture alone at night or in isolated areas
  • Don’t throw litter in public; try keeping the environment clean and healthy

Safety and Health

Safety in Western Highlands

Ensure you take the necessary measures to ensure your safety while in Western Highlands. Keep your valuable property safe and in a zipped bag. Avoid carrying too much cash or jewelry with you. Stay alert and cautious while walking in isolated areas.

Health in Western Highlands

Visitors traveling to Western Highlands should be up to date on routine vaccinations. Also, it’s advisable to take preventative measures against common viruses found in the region, common ones being Dengue and Zika.

Getting to Western Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Western Highlands

The nearest airport to the Western Highlands is the La Aurora International Airport in Guatemala City. Alternatively, the region is accessible through the airport in the Quetzaltenango city.

Bus Stations Near Western Highlands

Several bus companies offer transportation services to Western Highlands from the capital and other nearby cities. The most comfortable way to get around is with the express bus.

Other Options

Taxis and private shuttles are common modes of transportation in Western Highlands.

How to Get Around in Western Highlands

When touring around the regions in Western Highlands, options such as minibusses, taxis, and tuk-tuks are available. You may hire a driver who can take you around the entire region.

Where to Go Next from Western Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations that you could consider visiting from Western Highlands include:

  • Antigua Guatemala, known for its colonial architecture, cobbled streets, and scenic cityscape.
  • Tikal National Park, known for its ancient Mayan ruins and ruins of monumental scale
  • Livingston, a unique town on the Caribbean coast, rich in Garifuna culture and seafood cuisine.
  • Quiriguá, known for its impressive intricate stelae of Mayan rulers in southeast Guatemala.

Conclusion

The Western Highlands of Guatemala is a truly unique destination that offers a mix of historical, cultural and natural landmarks that has something for everyone. From the beautiful landscapes to amazing cuisine, the Western Highlands will give you memories that will last a lifetime.
