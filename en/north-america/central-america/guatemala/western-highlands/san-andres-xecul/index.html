San Andrés Xecul, located in the Western Highlands of Guatemala, is a hidden gem that travelers should put on their list. The town is known for its brightly painted church and other cultural and natural attractions. In this comprehensive guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, the food scene, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to San Andrés Xecul

San Andrés Xecul is a town in the Department of Totonicapán, in the western highlands of Guatemala. It’s situated about 125 km from Guatemala City and 34 km from Quetzaltenango. The town is located in a valley surrounded by mountains and forests, which makes it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in San Andrés Xecul

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of San Andrés Xecul: The town’s main attraction is the vibrant yellow and green church dedicated to St. Andrew. The church has a unique façade and is decorated with murals both inside and outside. The murals feature Mayan iconography and Catholic symbols, making it a blend of Spanish and indigenous culture.
  • The Museum of Totonicapán: This museum is located in the town center of Totonicapán and exhibits the history of the Department of Totonicapán, including its cultural heritage and traditional weaving techniques. The museum also has a collection of archaeological finds from the region.

Natural Attractions of San Andrés Xecul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Los Aposentos: Los Aposentos is a private nature reserve that boasts a diversity of flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, birdwatching, and take a picnic while enjoying the scenic views.
  • Cerro de Cristo Rey: The hill offers a magnificent view of the town. Visitors can hike the trail, approx 1 hour long, to the summit and enjoy a panoramic view of the town and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Andrés Xecul

San Andrés Xecul and its nearby surroundings offer several hiking and biking options, including the following:

  • Los Aposentos Nature Reserve Trails, 3 different routes to cover the whole reserve
  • Santa Maria, a town located 11 km outside San Andrés Xecul where a one-day hike to the mountain top and a tour around the town can be enjoyed.

Best Things to Do in San Andrés Xecul: Activities and Tours

  • Taking a guided tour to the church of San Andrés Xecul and learn about its history and cultural significance.
  • Joining a weaving workshop in Totonicapán and learn about the traditional weaving techniques and the local culture of the region.
  • Visit a local market to see and taste the local products such as local fruits and vegetables, spices, and handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Andrés Xecul

Day 1: Arrival and settling in at your hotel. Day 2: Guided tour to the church of San Andrés Xecul. Day 3: Hiking Los Aposentos Trails. Day 4: Day trip to Santa Maria to hike the mountains and visit the town. Day 5: Visit the Museum of Totonicapán in the morning, and enjoy a traditional meal in one of the small restaurants. Day 6: Take a weaving workshop to learn traditional techniques. Day 7: Shop local handicrafts and products in town markets.

Food and Drink Scene of San Andrés Xecul

San Andrés Xecul has a variety of restaurants, cafes, and crafty food markets. Among the must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepián: A typical dish from Totonicapán that consists of chicken served with thick tomato sauce with peanuts and pumpkin seeds.
  • Tostadas: fried corn tortillas, spread with refried beans, lettuce, and meat, usually chicken or beef.
  • Rellenitos: Dessert made of mashed sweet plantains with black bean paste.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: A refreshing drink made from rice, cinnamon, and milk. It’s a perfect drink to cool down on a hot day.
  • Atol de Elote: A warm, thick drink made from corn and flavored with milk, cinnamon, and sugar.

Where to Stay in San Andrés Xecul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Andrés Xecul offers a variety of lodging options ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Hotel Posada de San Andrés is located next to the church and provides a great view of the town and the hills
  • Casa Los Cerros is a Bed & Breakfast located in the surrounding mountains, it offers outdoor activities as well the possibility to relax or getting pampered in the spa.

What to Buy in San Andrés Xecul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The town market is an excellent place to buy local handicrafts, including:

  • Textile products: San Andrés Xecul is known for its traditional weaving techniques and fabrics. Visitors can buy colorful textiles such as tablecloths, shawls, and blankets.
  • Ceramic arts: Visitors can buy handcrafted ceramics such as bowls, plates, and vases.
  • Coffee: Guatemala is known for its coffee, and San Andrés Xecul has some of the best coffee farms and products. Visitors can buy locally grown and roasted coffee beans.

Local Culture and Customs of San Andrés Xecul

San Andrés Xecul is known for its Mayan and Catholic cultural blend. Some of the local customs you might encounter in town are:

  • Traditional clothing: Women usually wear colorful and handwoven huipiles (blouses), whereas men wear traditional shirts and pants.
  • Celebrations: San Andrés Xecul celebrates its patron saint’s festivities in November every year.
  • Language: In addition to Spanish, many locals speak a Mayan dialect known as K’iche’.

Best Time to Visit San Andrés Xecul

The best time to visit San Andrés Xecul is from November to March. The dry season guarantees sunny days, making it ideal for outdoor adventures and hikes.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Andrés Xecul

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take some cash with you as cards are not widely accepted.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.
  • Take photos without asking for permission, especially of religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Andrés Xecul

San Andrés Xecul is relatively safe for tourists. However, visitors should be careful with their valuables and avoid walking alone at night on isolated streets.

Health in San Andrés Xecul

Visitors should take precautions to avoid insect bites, especially malaria and dengue fever. It’s advisable to use mosquito repellent and wear long sleeves and pants.

Getting to San Andrés Xecul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Andrés Xecul

The closest airport to San Andrés Xecul is La Aurora International Airport, located in Guatemala City, which is about 125 km away from town.

Bus Stations Near San Andrés Xecul

Traveling by bus is the easiest and most affordable way to get to San Andrés Xecul. The major bus station in Guatemala City is the Terminal de Transportes that offers several routes to Xela (Quetzaltenango) and from there, visitors can take a localized bus to San Andrés Xecul.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to take them to San Andrés Xecul from Guatemala City and other regions.

How to Get Around in San Andrés Xecul

Walking is the best way to explore the town’s attractions, but visitors can also take taxis or motorized rickshaws for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from San Andrés Xecul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Andrés Xecul is a great starting point for exploring the rest of the Western Highlands. Some of the nearby destinations are:

  • Quetzaltenango (Xela): A large city located about 35 km from San Andrés Xecul. Xela is known for its vibrant culture, markets, and outdoor activities.
  • Lake Atitlán: A picturesque lake surrounded by volcanoes located about 75 km from San Andrés Xecul. Visitors can hike, kayak, or relax in the lake’s charming towns.

Conclusion

San Andrés Xecul is a charming and culturally significant destination in the Western Highlands of Guatemala. From the vibrant yellow and green church to the Los Aposentos nature reserve and surrounding mountains, visitors will find plenty of things to do and see. With a diverse food scene, weaving workshops, and local customs, San Andrés Xecul is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to uncover the hidden gems of Guatemala.
