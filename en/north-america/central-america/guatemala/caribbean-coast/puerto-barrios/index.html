Introduction to Puerto Barrios

Located on the coast of the Caribbean Sea, Puerto Barrios is a bustling port town in Guatemala. The town is an important commercial hub for the country and serves as the gateway to explore the eastern region of Guatemala.

With its rich cultural history and stunning natural beauty, Puerto Barrios offers something for everyone – whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a foodie.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a deep dive into the must-visit places, exciting activities, local customs, and much more in Puerto Barrios.

List of Places to Visit in Puerto Barrios

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Parque Nacional Rio Dulce: Located just a few miles from Puerto Barrios, this national park is famous for its lush greenery and diverse wildlife. The park is also home to the impressive Castillo de San Felipe, a historic Spanish fortress built in the 16th century.

  • Museo Multicultural: This museum highlights the cultural diversity of Guatemala and features artifacts from the ancient Mayan civilization.

  • Iglesia de San Antonio: Built-in the 1890s, this church is one of the oldest and most beautiful buildings in the town.

Natural Attractions of Puerto Barrios: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Semuc Champey: A natural wonder in the Alta Verapaz region, this series of turquoise pools and cascading waterfalls is a must-see destination for nature lovers.

  • Livingston: A charming coastal town known for its vibrant Afro-Caribbean culture and vibrant street art scene.

  • Rio Dulce: A picturesque river surrounded by lush green forests, this is the perfect spot for a relaxing boat ride or fishing trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puerto Barrios

  • Sierra de las Minas: Spanning an area of over 220,000 acres, this mountain range is home to some of the most challenging hiking trails in Guatemala.

  • Pacaya Volcano: This active volcano offers a thrilling hiking experience that will take you through lush forests, rocky terrain, and ultimately to the top of the volcano where you can see molten lava flows.

Best Things to Do in Puerto Barrios: Activities and Tours

  • Boat tour of Rio Dulce: Take a scenic boat ride down the Rio Dulce and experience the natural beauty of Guatemala’s eastern region.

  • Birdwatching tour: Home to over 700 species of birds, including toucans and scarlet macaws, Puerto Barrios is a paradise for birdwatchers.

  • Scuba diving or snorkeling: The Caribbean Sea surrounding Puerto Barrios is home to several coral reefs, making it a prime location for underwater adventures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puerto Barrios

Food and Drink Scene of Puerto Barrios

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tapado: A traditional Garifuna dish made with coconut milk and seafood.

  • Sopa de Res: A hearty beef soup served with vegetables and rice.

  • Tostadas: Crispy corn tortillas topped with beans, cheese, and avocado.

Local Beverages

  • Gallo beer: Guatemala’s national beer, a refreshing lager that pairs well with spicy food.

  • Cafe de Olla: A strong and sweet coffee infused with cinnamon and other spices.

  • Atol de Elote: A sweet corn drink served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Puerto Barrios: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Puerto Libre: A budget-friendly hotel located near the waterfront.

  • Bruno’s Hotel: A mid-range hotel with an outdoor pool and restaurant.

  • Hotel Mansion del Rio: A luxurious hotel with spa services and a private beach area.

What to Buy in Puerto Barrios: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Guatemalan textiles: Handmade textiles are the perfect souvenir to take home, the town has the excellent quality of weaving and fabrics of Guatemala.

  • Jade jewelry: Guatemala is well known for its high-quality jade, and many shops in Puerto Barrios sell beautiful jade jewelry.

  • Coffee: Guatemala is known for its coffee, so taking a bag of specialty coffee beans home is a must.

Local Culture and Customs of Puerto Barrios

The town’s population is a mix of Indigenous Mayans, Afro-Caribbeans, and Ladinos, making it a rich cultural melting pot. The local culture is heavily influenced by the Garifuna people, who have a unique language, cuisine, and music.

Respecting local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly, will help visitors immerse themselves in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Puerto Barrios

The best time to visit Puerto Barrios is from January to May when the weather is dry and the skies are clear. These months also coincide with several cultural festivals in the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puerto Barrios

Do:

  • Respect local cultures and traditions.

  • Explore the town’s stunning natural beauty.

  • Visit during the local festivals for a real insight into the town’s culture.

Don’t:

  • Stray away from well-lit and popular tourist areas, especially at night.

  • Disrespect the local customs, traditions, and people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puerto Barrios

While Puerto Barrios is generally considered safe for tourists, it is advised to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Petty theft and pick-pocketing can occur, so it’s essential to keep valuables safe and secure.

It’s also recommended to avoid traveling at night, and sticking to well-lit and busy streets.

Health in Puerto Barrios

Before traveling to Puerto Barrios, it’s essential to consult your doctor or healthcare provider for any necessary vaccinations or advice. The town does have medical facilities in case of emergencies.

Getting to Puerto Barrios: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puerto Barrios

  • Puerto Barrios Airport (PBR): Serves domestic flights from Guatemala City and several other locations in the country.

Bus Stations Near Puerto Barrios

  • Lanquin Bus Station: Located in nearby Lanquin, this station is a popular hub for minivans and shuttles.

Other Options

  • Taxis and car rentals are available in the town.

How to Get Around in Puerto Barrios

Taxis and tuk-tuks are the most common modes of transportation in the town. Bike rentals are also available for those who want to explore the town at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Puerto Barrios: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Flores: A picturesque town located near the Mayan ruins of Tikal.

  • Antigua: A charming colonial town known for its beautiful architecture and vibrant nightlife.

  • Peten: Home to several national parks and reserves, a prime location for nature lovers.

Conclusion

Puerto Barrios is a hidden gem on the eastern coast of Guatemala, with its rich cultural history, stunning natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an action-packed adventure, Puerto Barrios has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well equipped to explore all that this beautiful town has to offer.
