Introduction to Livingston

Nestled in the Caribbean Coast of Guatemala, Livingston is a vibrant and colorful town where Garifuna culture, Mayan traditions, and colonial architecture meet. Known as the “La Buga” by locals, the streets and beaches of this hidden gem are always buzzing with energy, making it an ideal destination for adventurers, culture enthusiasts, and beach lovers alike. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through all the essentials you need to know before you plan your trip to Livingston.

List of Places to Visit in Livingston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa de la Cultura - The Cultural House is an iconic landmark of Livingston, where the Garifuna community celebrates their traditional dance and music.
  • Garifuna Museum - This museum showcases the unique culture and history of the Garifuna people.
  • Iglesia Catolica de Nuestra Senora de la Asuncion - This beautiful church was built in colonial times, and it is still used as a place for religious celebrations.

Natural Attractions of Livingston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Siete Altares Waterfalls - Located a few kilometers east of Livingston, these waterfalls are a must-see for nature lovers.
  • Rio Dulce Canyon - Take a boat tour from Livingston to explore this breathtaking canyon that connects Guatemala’s largest lake to the Caribbean Sea.
  • Los Tres Brazos Ecological Reserve - Hike through this magnificent reserve, home to a variety of flora and fauna, including howler monkeys, exotic birds, and butterflies.
  • Playa Blanca - Relax and spend the day at this beautiful white sandy beach where you can enjoy the sun, swim, and indulge in delicious seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Livingston

Exploring the natural wonders of Livingston is an adventure on its own. There are several hiking and biking routes to choose from, catering to all levels of fitness. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Los Tres Brazos Eco Reserve Trail - This three-hour hike takes you through the lush forest and offers stunning views of the Rio Dulce.
  • Waterfall Trail - This easy hike takes you to the Siete Altares waterfalls, a series of cascading waterfalls that end in a beautiful natural pool.
  • Punta de Palma Biking Trail - For experienced mountain bikers, this trail offers a challenging route through a dense forest and deserted beaches.

Best Things to Do in Livingston: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Tours - Join a local fisherman and learn about traditional fishing techniques while capturing a few fish to cook your own meal.
  • Garifuna Cultural Tour - Immerse yourself in the Garifuna culture and traditions by attending drumming and dance sessions, and trying local dishes cooked with fresh seafood.
  • Boat Tours - Take a boat tour through the Rio Dulce Canyon, where you can spot exotic birds, iguanas, and even crocodiles.
  • Snorkeling and Diving - Explore the abundant marine life that surrounds Livingston’s coral reefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Livingston

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Livingston, here is a sample itinerary to ensure you get the most out of your trip:

Day Activity
1st Arrive in Livingston and check-in at hotel. Enjoy a lazy day on the beach.
2nd Take a Garifuna cultural tour, dance to the rhythms of the drums, and try some delicious local dishes.
3rd Hike the Los Tres Brazos Eco Reserve Trail, and cool off by swimming in the river.
4th Take a boat tour through the Rio Dulce Canyon and explore the lush jungle that surrounds it.
5th Go fishing with the locals and cook up your catch for dinner.
6th Take a snorkeling or diving tour to explore the vibrant marine life and coral reefs around Livingston.
7th Spend the day on Playa Blanca, relax, and indulge in seafood before departing for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Livingston

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tapado - A delicious soup made with seafood, coconut milk, yucca, plantains, and other spices.
  • Hudutu - Garifuna stew seasoned to perfection and served with mashed plantains.
  • Pan de coco - Coconut bread, a popular side dish to enjoy with any Garifuna meal.
  • Serenade - A sweet dessert that’s a blend of coconut milk, plantains, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Cashew Wine - A drink made from fermented cashew fruits and aged for several years.
  • Hibiscus Tea (Jamaica) - A refreshing drink made from dried hibiscus flowers.
  • Guaro - A clear liquor made from sugar cane, perfect for mixing in cocktails.

Where to Stay in Livingston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Livingston to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Casa Rosada - A stunning hotel near the beach, offering modern rooms with balconies facing the Caribbean Sea.
  • Hotelito Perdido - A traditional hostel located in the heart of Livingston, ideal for backpackers on a budget.
  • Villa Caribe - A luxurious resort with a private beach, multiple restaurants, and swimming pools, perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Livingston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Garifuna Woven Baskets/Platters - Beautifully woven baskets with intricate designs, handmade by the Garifuna people.
  • Cashew Wine - A perfect souvenir to gift, remember the exquisite taste of the Garifuna famous drink.
  • Garifuna Music - A way to keep the rhythm of traditional Garifuna drums back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Livingston

Livingston’s culture is deeply influenced by the Garifuna people who have their traditions and unique style of living. Visitors should respect the customs that all cultures here follow, such as their music and dance style that includes drumming.

Best Time to Visit Livingston

The peak season in Livingston starts from December to April, when the weather is dry, sunny, and perfect for a beach getaway. However, visiting during the quieter months May-Nov can be equally interesting, with less crowds and lower rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Livingston

Do:

  • Engage with the local community, learn about their culture and traditions through kindly asking questions and being curious to know more.
  • Dress appropriately and comfortably, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Take precautions to avoid sunburn and insect bites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t enter the sacred sites, churches, or special places without permission or inappropriately.
  • Don’t litter in public areas, especially beaches and parks.
  • Don’t engage in any activities that may hurt the environment or harm any animal species.

Safety and Health

Safety in Livingston

Like with any destination, visitors should always be cautious and aware of their surroundings. Street crimes are possible, so travelers should not leave their valuables unattended, and travel in groups.

Health in Livingston

Always make sure you are drinking clean water, by only drinking bottled water or purified water. Be cautious of what food you consume on the streets, and take precautions to avoid mosquito bites to avoid any possible diseases.

Getting to Livingston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Livingston

The closest airport to Livingston is La Aurora International in Guatemala City; however, you can fly domestically to Mundo Maya International in Flores and take a connecting flight to Livingston.

Bus Stations Near Livingston

Buses run daily from Guatemala City to Puerto Barrios, and from there, take a taxi boat to Livingston.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car from nearby cities such as Puerto Barrios and Rio Dulce, and drive directly to Livingston.

How to Get Around in Livingston

The town and inner areas can be easily accessible by foot, but boats and taxis are the primary mode of transportation for traveling longer distances. Renting a bicycle or a motorbike is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Livingston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tikal - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized as a significant ancient Maya civilization, known for its stunning ruins.
  • Semuc Champey - A beautiful natural site, where visitors can see several gorgeous cascading pools, ideal for swimming or hiking around.
  • Rio Dulce - A town located at the mouth of Rio Dulce, offers an intimate adventure as Belize boarder and several marinas are accessible.

Conclusion

Livingston is a magnificent mix of cultures with beautiful beaches, ecotourism, and healthy cuisine. The town is an ideal destination for adventurers, culture enthusiasts, and beach lovers alike, with a never-ending list of things to do and see. Plan your trip to Livingston today and experience a unique and unforgettable vacation.
