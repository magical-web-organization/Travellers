Introduction to Tikal

Tikal is an ancient Mayan city located in the Northern Lowlands of Guatemala, amidst dense rainforests and towering pyramids. It was one of the biggest and most powerful Mayan cities from the 3rd to the 10th centuries AD, with impressive architecture, advanced art and literature, and sophisticated astronomical observations.

Today, Tikal is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a unique opportunity to experience Mayan culture, explore ancient ruins, and immerse themselves in nature.

List of Places to Visit in Tikal

Tikal boasts an array of cultural and natural attractions that are must-visits for any traveler. Here are some of the most important places to visit in Tikal:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tikal National Park: the heart of the ancient city, with over 3,000 structures, including pyramids, plazas, ballcourts, and stelae
  • Tikal Museum: a modern museum showcasing Mayan art, pottery, tools, and artifacts discovered in the excavation of Tikal
  • Temple of the Jaguar: a pyramid adorned with a jaguar sculpture, reflecting the importance of this animal in Mayan mythology
  • Great Plaza: the main ceremonial center of Tikal, featuring two large pyramids, stelae, and altars
  • North Acropolis: a complex of tombs and palaces of the Mayan elite, with intricate stone carvings and decorations

Natural Attractions of Tikal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tikal National Park Trails: a network of hiking trails amidst lush rainforests and wildlife, offering breathtaking views of ancient ruins and wildlife
  • Temple IV: the tallest pyramid in Tikal, providing a panoramic view of the entire city and the surrounding jungle
  • Lake Petén Itzá: a serene freshwater lake popular for swimming, kayaking, and fishing, situated amidst scenic hills and pine forests
  • Yaxhá-Nakum-Naranjo National Park: a nearby national park featuring three other significant Mayan cities, with ancient pyramids, ballcourts, and shrines

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tikal

Exploring Tikal on foot or bike can be a great way to experience the city’s beauty and history up close. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Tikal:

  • Tikal Basic Tour: an easy guided tour of the main ruins and attractions in Tikal National Park, suitable for all ages and fitness levels
  • Temple IV Sunrise Hike: an early-morning trek to the top of Temple IV, to watch the sunrise over the jungle canopy and the ancient city
  • El Zotz Trail: a challenging 5-mile hike through dense jungle and steep terrain to reach the remote Mayan city of El Zotz, famous for its beautiful pyramids and stelae
  • Mountain Biking Adventure: a thrilling bike ride through the rugged terrain of Tikal, with stops at key attractions and scenic vistas along the way

Best Things to Do in Tikal: Activities and Tours

There are numerous fun activities and tours in Tikal that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are a few popular ones:

  • Birdwatching Tour: a guided tour of the Tikal National Park’s abundant birdlife, including toucans, macaws, and parrots
  • Photography Tour: a tour that focuses on capturing the most stunning views and moments in Tikal, with expert tips and techniques from professional photographers
  • Mayan Cooking Class: a class that teaches visitors how to prepare traditional Mayan dishes, using fresh local ingredients and ancient recipes
  • Sunset Tour: a tour that takes visitors to hidden corners of the park during the magical hours of dusk, to witness the sunset and the park’s nocturnal wildlife

Food and Drink Scene of Tikal

No trip to Tikal is complete without savoring the flavors and aromas of Mayan cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pepián: a delicious meat stew cooked with tomatoes, chilies, and spices, served with rice and tortillas
  • Kak’ik: a hearty turkey soup with a rich flavor, thickened with roasted pumpkin seeds and scented with coriander and achiote
  • Chirmol: a fresh salsa made with tomatoes, onions, chilies, and coriander, served with grilled meat or tortillas

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: a refreshing drink made with rice milk, cinnamon, and sugar, often served cold with ice
  • Atol: a thick hot beverage made with ground corn and seasoned with cinnamon, nutmeg, and sugar, served in the morning with bread or tamales
  • Guaro: a strong and potent sugarcane liquor, popular for special occasions and celebrations in Mayan culture

Where to Stay in Tikal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tikal offers a range of accommodation options to cater to all budgets and preferences, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses and backpacker hostels. Here are some popular options:

  • Tikal Lodge: a top-rated lodge situated right next to Tikal National Park, featuring comfortable rooms, a pool, and a bar with stunning views of the jungle
  • Hotel Jaguar Inn: a cozy hotel located within walking distance of the park, offering clean rooms, good food, and friendly service
  • Tikal Inn: a budget-friendly option with basic rooms and shared bathrooms, located just outside the park with a rustic charm
  • Hostal Los Amigos: a hostel popular with backpackers and budget travelers, offering dorms and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a friendly atmosphere

What to Buy in Tikal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tikal offers a variety of souvenir shops and local markets where visitors can purchase unique items to remind them of their trip. Here are some popular souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Handmade textiles: traditional Mayan fabrics and garments with vibrant colors and intricate designs, made from cotton or wool
  • Mayan art and crafts: carvings, sculptures, and pottery made by local artisans, depicting Mayan symbols and motifs
  • Coffee and chocolate: high-quality coffee beans and chocolate bars, grown and processed in the nearby hills and rainforests

Local Culture and Customs of Tikal

To fully appreciate Tikal’s cultural heritage and respect local customs, visitors should keep in mind a few important guidelines:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting religious or historical sites
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or traditional ceremonies
  • Respect indigenous traditions and beliefs, and avoid disrespectful behavior or remarks
  • Learn some basic Spanish or Mayan phrases to communicate with locals and show appreciation for their culture

Best Time to Visit Tikal

The best time to visit Tikal is during the dry season, between November and April, when the weather is mild and dry, and the skies are clear. However, this is also the peak tourist season, and crowds and prices tend to be higher. Another good time to visit is during the shoulder seasons of May-June or September-October, when the weather is still pleasant, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tikal

To ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to Tikal, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable walking shoes
  • Carry cash and small bills for tips and purchases, as credit cards may not be accepted everywhere
  • Respect the wildlife and avoid feeding or touching animals
  • Bring a reusable water bottle and refill it at the many fountains and taps available in the park

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on ancient ruins or structures, as they are fragile and may be damaged easily
  • Ignore warnings or advice from park rangers or guides, especially if related to safety or weather conditions
  • Drink tap water or eat food from street vendors, as the hygiene and quality standards may not be reliable

Safety and Health

Tikal is a relatively safe and healthy destination, but visitors should take some precautions to avoid common risks and hazards.

Safety in Tikal

  • Always stick to the marked trails and avoid wandering into remote or unmarked areas
  • Use a safe or locker to store your valuables, and avoid carrying large sums of cash or expensive accessories
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone or in the dark
  • Always ask for a reputable taxi or transportation service, and avoid unmarked or unofficial vehicles.

Health in Tikal

  • Drink only bottled or purified water, and avoid ice or uncooked food that may be contaminated
  • Use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue or Zika
  • Be prepared for tropical diseases such as malaria or yellow fever, and get vaccinated or take prophylaxis if needed
  • Bring a basic first-aid kit with essential items, such as band-aids, antihistamines, and painkillers.

Getting to Tikal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tikal is located in the remote Northern Lowlands of Guatemala, and reaching it requires some planning and logistics.

Airports Near Tikal

The closest airport to Tikal is Mundo Maya International Airport in Flores, Guatemala, which receives daily flights from major cities in Central America, such as Guatemala City and San Salvador.

Bus Stations Near Tikal

Flores is the main hub for bus transportation in the region, with several companies offering routes to Tikal and other nearby destinations, such as Belize city or Chetumal in Mexico.

Other Options

Some visitors prefer to hire a private shuttle or driver to reach Tikal, which offers more convenience and flexibility but may be more expensive.

How to Get Around in Tikal

Inside the Tikal National Park, visitors can explore the ruins on foot or by bike, but there are also guided tours and shuttle buses available for those who prefer a more organized visit. Outside the park, visitors can hire taxis or rent a car to explore nearby attractions or towns.

Where to Go Next from Tikal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tikal is just one of the many gems of the Northern Lowlands, and there are several other notable destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Lake Atitlán: a stunning volcanic lake surrounded by picturesque Mayan villages and natural hot springs, located in the Guatemalan Highlands
  • Antigua Guatemala: a colonial city with colorful architecture, charming cobblestone streets, and lively markets, located in central Guatemala
  • Copán Ruinas: a well-preserved Mayan city with impressive stelae and hieroglyphs, located in western Honduras

Conclusion

Tikal is a fascinating and beautiful destination, offering visitors a deeper understanding of Mayan culture, history, and nature. By following these tips and guidelines, visitors can ensure a safe, enjoyable, and unforgettable trip to this ancient wonder of the world.
