Introduction to Dos Pilas

Located in the Northern Lowlands of Guatemala, Dos Pilas is a beautiful ancient Mayan city that was once at the epicenter of Mayan civilization. The city boasts breathtaking ruins, surrounded by lush forests and stunning natural landscapes. Dos Pilas is a must-visit destination for history and culture buffs and outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Dos Pilas

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Dos Pilas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Great Acropolis
  • Temple 7
  • Hieroglyphic Stairway
  • Ballcourt Complex
  • Casa de los Sacerdotes

Natural Attractions of Dos Pilas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laguna La Machaquila
  • The Dos Pilas Archaeological Ruins
  • The Biotopo Protected Area

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dos Pilas

Dos Pilas offers scenic trails that allow hikers and bikers to explore the lush jungle surrounding the ancient ruins. Some popular routes include:

  • Dos Pilas - Aguateca: This scenic 3-hour hike takes you through the jungle and offers breathtaking views of the nearby Aguateca ruins.
  • Laguna La Machaquila: A hiking trail that leads to the stunning La Machaquila Lagoon, a popular spot for swimming and picnics.

Best Things to Do in Dos Pilas: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Maya Ruins: Guided tours of the ruins are available, offering insight into the history and culture of the ancient Mayan civilization.
  • Explore the Biotopo Protected Area: Take a guided tour of the protected area and see some exotic plant and wildlife species.
  • Visit the Casa de los Sacerdotes: This house of the priests is an interesting place to visit, with rich historical and cultural significance.

Food and Drink Scene of Dos Pilas

No trip to Dos Pilas is complete without tasting the local food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: Try the local tamales, which are made with corn and stuffed with meat or vegetables.
  • Pollo Asado: This traditional Guatemalan dish features grilled chicken marinated in a blend of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Atol de Elote: A sweet and creamy drink made with corn and spices.
  • Horchata: A refreshing rice drink flavored with cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Dos Pilas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dos Pilas offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some popular options:

  • Maya Lounge Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel offering clean rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Dos Pilas Ecolodge: This eco-friendly lodge offers comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • La Casa del Este: A guesthouse with beautiful gardens and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Dos Pilas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Dos Pilas:

  • Handcrafted pottery: Dos Pilas has a rich pottery making tradition, and you can find some beautiful handcrafted pieces here.
  • Traditional textiles: Guatemala is famous for its colorful textiles, and you can find many beautiful pieces here.

Local Culture and Customs of Dos Pilas

The local culture of Dos Pilas is a mix of Mayan and Spanish influences. The local people are friendly and hospitable, and you’ll find a warm welcome wherever you go. Respect for the local customs and traditions is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Dos Pilas

The best time to visit Dos Pilas is between November and April, which is the dry season. The weather is pleasant, and there’s little rainfall, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dos Pilas

Do:

  • Respect local culture and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately to enter the temples and other sacred areas.
  • Carry cash as most businesses do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures without permission.
  • Approach the wild animals in the protected area.
  • Disrespect the ancient ruins by climbing on them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dos Pilas

Dos Pilas is generally a safe place for tourists, but you should take basic precautions like not carrying valuables around and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Dos Pilas

It’s recommended to get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B and typhoid before traveling to Dos Pilas. Also, use mosquito repellent at all times.

Getting to Dos Pilas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dos Pilas is located about 200 km (124 miles) from Guatemala City. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Dos Pilas

  • Guatemala City International Airport (GUA)
  • Mundo Maya International Airport (FRS)

Bus Stations Near Dos Pilas

  • Santa Elena de la Cruz Bus Station in Flores
  • Benque Viejo Bus Station in Belize

Other Options

  • Private taxi: You can hire a private taxi from Flores or Belize to Dos Pilas.

How to Get Around in Dos Pilas

The best way to get around Dos Pilas is on foot. The hiking trails are well-marked, making it easy to explore the ruins and surrounding forests.

Where to Go Next from Dos Pilas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit after exploring Dos Pilas:

  • Tikal National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that boasts some of the best-preserved Mayan ruins in the world.
  • The town of Flores: A charming town located on an island in Lake Petén Itzá.
  • Yaxhá National Park: A beautiful natural reserve with hiking trails and stunning Mayan ruins.

Conclusion

Dos Pilas is a hidden gem that offers a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Dos Pilas has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and explore this magical Mayan city.
