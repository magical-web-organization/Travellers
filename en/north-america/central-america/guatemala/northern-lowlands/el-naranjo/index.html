Introduction to El Naranjo

Located in the Northern Lowlands region of Guatemala, El Naranjo is a small town that’s often overlooked by tourists. But the town and the surrounding area have a rich cultural and historical significance, as there are notable archaeological sites and structures from the ancient Maya civilization. Along with the cultural attractions, there are also many natural wonders to experience in El Naranjo, including lush green forests, towering mountains, and beautiful lakes. This travel guide to El Naranjo will provide you a comprehensive list of things to do, see, eat, and stay, along with helpful tips and advice to make your trip as pleasurable as possible.

Local Culture and Customs of El Naranjo

El Naranjo is home to the K’iche’ Maya community, and their culture and traditions remain strong. You will witness vibrant and colorful traditional dress and the calendar of events and festivals is full of celebrations invoking Maya, Catholic, and regional traditions.

Best Time to Visit El Naranjo

The best time to visit El Naranjo is between December and March, which is the dry season. The weather is mostly sunny, and you can explore the town and surrounding area without any unpleasant weather interruptions. The rainy season is from May to October and can make travel challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Naranjo

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Pack mosquito repellent and sunscreen
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases
  • Drink bottled water
  • Try the local food and drinks

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Touch or climb on the archaeological structures
  • Use drugs or consume substances banned in Guatemala

Safety and Health

Safety in El Naranjo

El Naranjo is generally a safe town. However, beware of petty theft, and don’t wander alone at night. Always use official taxis or transportation provided by your hotel.

Health in El Naranjo

Consult your doctor to be up-to-date on necessary vaccinations and pack a first aid kit with over-the-counter medication. Drinking bottled water and eating at clean restaurants are your best options to stay healthy.

How to Get Around in El Naranjo

Walking is the best way to explore El Naranjo. For day trips, you can rent a bicycle or hire a taxi, which you can arrange via your hotel or at the bus station.
