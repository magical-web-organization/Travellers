Are you looking for a travel destination that offers a perfect blend of culture and adventure? Look no further than Uaxactun, a historical and natural gem located in Northern Lowlands, Guatemala. This charming city boasts a rich Mayan history and is home to several ancient ruins, museums, and natural wonders. Visitors can trek through the region’s lush forests, view its abundant wildlife, and learn from its friendly locals. This travel guide will help you plan your Uaxactun itinerary, including the best places to visit, delicious food to taste, comfortable accommodations to stay, and transportation options to consider.

Introduction to Uaxactun

Uaxactun is a small Mayan village situated within the northern Petén Basin in Guatemala. The village is known for its ancient ruins and was discovered in 1916 by archaeologists who were exploring the region. Uaxactun’s name means “Eight Stones.” Visitors are welcomed by friendly locals that are proud of their rich Mayan culture and archeological heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Uaxactun

Uaxactun features many cultural and natural attractions that both locals and tourists can enjoy. Here are some of the top destinations to add to your Uaxactun bucket list.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Uaxactun Ruins - The ruins are the main attraction and represent the largest astronomical observatory in ancient Mesoamerica.

  • Museo Arqueológico Rafael Girón Pacheco – Learn more about the history of the region and the excavations of the Mayan ruins at this museum.

Natural Attractions of Uaxactun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Las Guacamayas Biological Station - A rich and varied ecosystem and an important biodiversity center in the region home to a wildlife rehabilitation center, eco-trails, and beautiful views of the Macal River valley.

  • Cueva de las Ventanas - A mysterious and eerie cave located about 10 kilometers from Uaxactun that offers adventurous visitors the opportunity to explore its inner chambers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uaxactun

There are plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore around Uaxactun, making it easy to experience the natural beauty of the region outdoors. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Uaxactun Birdwatching Tour – Watch birds in their natural habitat like coati, jaguars, spider monkeys, pumas, tapirs, and more.

  • El Mirador - A challenging 52 km hike that takes you through the lush forest and offers stunning views of the Mayan pyramids.

Best Things to Do in Uaxactun: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and visiting the historical sites, there are many other activities you can do while visiting Uaxactun:

  • Visit Local Villages to Learn About Their Customs and Artisan Products - Take a tour of surrounding Mayan villages and witness the traditional way of life and hand-crafting cultural artifacts.

  • Participate in Traditional Mayan Ceremonies – Watch Mayan ceremonies, which are a staple of this area’s entertainment and best way to get in touch with Mayan culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Uaxactun

Food and Drink Scene of Uaxactun

Uaxactun has a range of authentic cuisines to choose from, all of which are heavily influenced by the Mayan history of the region. While in Uaxactun, try some of the following local dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chaya Soup - This is a popular soup made from a spinach-like plant called chaya, and is packed with antioxidants and vitamins.

  • Tamales - These are a staple in Mayan cuisine and are usually filled with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Posh - A traditional Mayan beverage made from corn and usually served as a side dish during meals.

  • Horchata de arroz - A sweet drink made from rice, cinnamon, and sugar, and is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Uaxactun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Uaxactun offers a range of options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Casa Bella - This luxurious hotel offers guests an experience of traditional Guatemalan hospitality and offers spectacular views of the valley.

  • Maya International Hostel - This hostel has an excellent central location and is perfect for budget backpackers.

What to Buy in Uaxactun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Uaxactun is known for its local artisan goods and Mayan traditions, making it easy to find one-of-a-kind souvenirs and gifts to bring home. Here’s a list of some popular items:

  • Pottery

  • Handmade jewelry

  • Local woven fabrics

Local Culture and Customs of Uaxactun

Uaxactun has a rich Mayan culture, with the locals being proud of their traditions and customs. Visitors can witness traditional Mayan ceremonies and attend local artisan fairs that showcase the region’s diverse cultures.

Best Time to Visit Uaxactun

The best time to visit Uaxactun is from November to April, which is the dry season. During this time, the temperatures are higher, and outdoor activities like hiking and exploring the ruins are some of the most enjoyable.

Safety and Health

Uaxactun is a relatively safe place to visit. However, visitors should always take precautions to protect their health and safety.

Safety in Uaxactun

  • Keep valuables safe at all times

  • Always lock up your accommodations

  • Be mindful of your surroundings

Health in Uaxactun

  • Be cautious of the sun, which can cause dehydration and heatstroke

  • Drink bottled water and avoid street food, which may be contaminated

Getting to Uaxactun: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to consider when planning your trip to Uaxactun:

Airports Near Uaxactun

The closest airport to Uaxactun is Mundo Maya International Airport in Flores. From there, visitors can take a taxi to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Uaxactun

There are several buses daily from Flores to Uaxactun.

Other Options

Uaxactun is accessible by road from the capital city, Guatemala City, the journey takes up to 10 hours but road conditions can be poor.

How to Get Around in Uaxactun

The easiest way to get around is by walking or renting a bike. Uaxactun is relatively small, and most attractions are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Uaxactun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guatemala has several other popular travel destinations that are easily accessible from Uaxactun. Some of the most popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Visit nearby Tikal ruins

  • Head towards the Caribbean coast and visit Belizian islands

  • Travel to Guatemala City

Conclusion

Uaxactun is a cultural and natural paradise that offers visitors a unique Mayan experience. From exploring its historical ruins to hiking and zip-lining, there is no shortage of outdoor activities to enjoy in Uaxactun. The city’s friendly locals, authentic cuisines, comfortable accommodations, and convenient transportation options make it an ideal destination for any traveler. So pack your bags, and come explore this hidden gem of Northern Lowlands, Guatemala!
