Introduction to El Remate

Nestled in the Northern Lowlands of Guatemala, El Remate is a small town with lots to offer travelers. Located near the stunning Lake Peten Itza, it serves as a gateway to the famous Tikal archaeological site - Guatemala’s most visited attraction. El Remate, surrounded by vast forests, where tourists can explore the natural wonders of the region and enjoy a variety of exciting activities, from hiking and kayaking to zip-lining and cave tours.

Local Culture and Customs of El Remate

The majority of the population in El Remate is indigenous and identifies as Q’eqchi’. Visitors should be respectful of the locals’ cultural practices and ask permission before taking any pictures.

Best Time to Visit El Remate

The best time to visit El Remate is during the dry season, from November to April when rainfall is minimal, and temperatures are warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Remate

Do:

  • Carry light clothes
  • Pack sunscreen and a hat
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Disregard the locals’ customs

Safety and Health

Safety in El Remate

El Remate is a safe town, but visitors should remain cautious when traveling to remote areas or hiking trails. Tourists are also advised not to wander around the town alone at night.

Health in El Remate

El Remate has a clinic and a pharmacy. It is advisable to carry essential medication.

How to Get Around in El Remate

El Remate is a small town that travelers can easily explore on foot. Alternatively, visitors can use a taxi or rent a bicycle to explore the surrounding areas.
