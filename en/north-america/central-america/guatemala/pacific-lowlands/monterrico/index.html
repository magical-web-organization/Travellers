Introduction to Monterrico

Monterrico is a tranquil village located on the Pacific coast of Guatemala, known for its black sand beaches, stunning sunsets, and diverse wildlife. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, birdwatchers, and surfers looking for uncrowded waves. Plus, Monterrico is only a few hours away from Guatemala City, making it an ideal weekend getaway for both locals and tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Monterrico

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Although Monterrico is mostly known for its natural attractions, there are also several cultural landmarks worth exploring, including:

  • Museo Natural Monterrico: This museum showcases local fauna and flora, as well as the threats they face from human activities. It also offers educational programs for schools and tourists.

  • Casa del Maestro: A historic house built in the early 20th century, which preserves the memory of the Guatemalan teacher Víctor Mariá de León who fought for educational rights for indigenous people.

Natural Attractions of Monterrico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Without a doubt, nature is the main attraction in Monterrico, and you won’t be disappointed by the many outdoor activities and scenic views you can encounter, including:

  • Monterrico Natural Reserve: A protected area of mangrove forests, wetlands, and seagrass beds, which serves as a nesting ground for endangered sea turtles. You can join a guided tour to see them hatch and be released into the ocean.

  • Hawaii Beach: A secluded beach known for its alluring black sand, strong waves, and impressive rock formations. It is a favorite spot for surfers and nature photographers.

  • Biotopo Monterrico-Hawaii: This nature reserve is located between the Monterrico and Hawaii beaches and offers a diverse ecosystem of mangroves, lagoons, and wetlands. You can spot aquatic birds, crocodiles, and turtles on a guided boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monterrico

While Monterrico itself is mostly flat, the surrounding areas offer many hiking and biking trails with breathtaking views of the coast and the volcanoes in the distance, such as:

  • Volcan de Fuego: A popular destination for adventure seekers looking to climb Guatemala’s most active volcano. The ascent takes around six hours and is not recommended for beginners.

  • Los Cebollales Natural Reserve: A forested mountain range with several trails ranging from easy to strenuous. You can camp overnight or stay at a nearby lodge to explore the area more extensively.

Best Things to Do in Monterrico: Activities and Tours

Besides nature tours and hiking options, Monterrico offers a variety of unique activities and tours that are worth trying, such as:

  • Horseback Riding: A relaxing way to explore the beaches and countryside of Monterrico, led by experienced guides who know the best routes.

  • Seafood Tasting: You can sample the local catch of fish, shrimp, and crab prepared in traditional Guatemalan recipes. Most restaurants in Monterrico offer fresh seafood dishes.

  • Sunset Sailboat Tour: A romantic and scenic way to watch the sunset over the Pacific Ocean while sipping on cocktails served onboard by the crew.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Monterrico, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Monterrico

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine in Monterrico is heavily influenced by the coastal culture of Guatemala, featuring fresh seafood, coconut milk, and spicy chilis. Here are some dishes you should try:

  • Tapado: A seafood soup made with coconut milk, plantains, and green bananas, and garnished with cilantro and lime.

  • Hilacho: A hearty stew made with shredded beef, tomatoes, onions, peppers, and potatoes, served with rice and tortillas.

  • Pescado Frito: Whole fried fish, usually caught that same day, served with a side of fried plantains. You can find it in almost any beachside restaurant in Monterrico

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Monterrico offers several refreshing options, including:

  • Cervesa Gallo: A lager beer that originated in Guatemala that has a mildly bitter flavor and refreshing taste. It’s a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.

  • Coco Loco: This cocktail is made with coconut water, white rum, and pineapple juice, served inside a fresh coconut, and garnished with a slice of pineapple and a paper umbrella.

  • Atol de Elote: A sweet and warm beverage made with fresh corn, cinnamon, and sugar, usually served as a breakfast drink.

Where to Stay in Monterrico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monterrico has accommodation options for all types of travelers and budgets, ranging from luxury hotels to simple guesthouses and hostels. Some popular choices include:

  • Hotel Utz Tzaba: A boutique hotel located on a private beach with a pool, restaurant, and yoga studio. Rooms start at around $120 per night.

  • Hostal El Delfin: A casual and friendly hostel located in the city center, offering shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a rooftop terrace. Dorm beds start at around $10 per night.

  • Casa del Mar: A guesthouse located on the beach, offering individual bungalows with private terraces, a pool, and a restaurant. Bungalows start at around $60 per night.

What to Buy in Monterrico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping for souvenirs or local products in Monterrico, you can find a variety of handmade crafts and artisanal goods, such as:

  • Huipiles: A traditional blouse worn by indigenous women in Guatemala, made with colorful fabrics and embroidery patterns.

  • Wooden Carvings: Skilled artisans in Monterrico create beautiful sculptures and figurines out of recycled wood, such as masks, animals, and birdhouses.

  • Café de la Selva: A coffee brand that promotes sustainable farming practices and fair trade in the region, offering a range of organic blends and single-origin beans.

Local Culture and Customs of Monterrico

The locals in Monterrico are known for their friendly and laid-back attitude towards life, as well as their dedication to preserving the natural environment and the traditions of their Mayan ancestors. Some customs and beliefs you might encounter include:

  • Mayan Spiritualism: Monterrico is surrounded by ancient Mayan sites and ruins, and many locals still practice traditional rituals and ceremonies to honor their ancestors and nature.

  • Dia de los Muertos: A Mexican and Mayan holiday that celebrates the deceased loved ones and their lives, usually held on November 1 and 2. In Monterrico, you can witness colorful altars, parades, and offerings made to the dead.

Best Time to Visit Monterrico

The best time to visit Monterrico is between November and April, during the dry season, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the waves are consistent for surfing. However, keep in mind that this is also the high season, so prices and crowds can be higher.

If you’re looking for more affordable and quieter options, consider visiting during the low season (May to October) when prices drop, and the rainforest is lush, but expect afternoon rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monterrico

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting sacred Mayan sites or participating in local ceremonies.

  • Protect the environment by not littering, not disturbing the wildlife, and using eco-friendly products.

  • Learn a few basic Spanish phrases before your trip or hire a local tour guide to help you communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t swim in the ocean at night, as it is unsafe and prohibited.

  • Don’t wear flashy jewelry or carry large amounts of cash when walking alone at night.

  • Don’t ignore the warnings and advice of the local authorities and tour operators regarding the natural hazards and risks in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monterrico

Monterrico is generally a safe and peaceful place, but like any tourist destination, theft and petty crime can happen, especially in crowded areas. It is advisable to keep your valuables close to you and use common sense when walking alone at night.

Regarding natural hazards, be aware of the strong currents and undertows in the ocean, as well as the risk of sunburn and dehydration, especially during the hot and dry season.

Health in Monterrico

The medical facilities in Monterrico are limited, and serious medical emergencies may require air evacuation to Guatemala City. It is advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses and evacuation.

Regarding health conditions, the main risk factors in Monterrico are waterborne illnesses and mosquito-borne diseases, such as Dengue fever and Zika, so make sure to drink only bottled water and use mosquito repellent and protective clothing.

Getting to Monterrico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Monterrico is located approximately two hours from Guatemala City, and there are several transportation options to get there:

Airports Near Monterrico

  • La Aurora International Airport (GUA): This is the largest airport in Guatemala, served by several airlines, including Avianca, American Airlines, and Delta Airlines. It is located around two hours by car from Monterrico.

  • Reu Airport (REX): This small airport is located about 90 minutes from Monterrico and offers domestic flights to Guatemala City and other destinations in Central America.

Bus Stations Near Monterrico

  • Puente El Cebollito: Located on the Interamerican Highway, this bus station services Monterrico and other destinations in the Pacific coast. Buses run every hour from Guatemala City and other towns.

  • Terminal de Autobuses Chiquimulilla: Located in Chiquimulilla, a nearby town, this bus station offers connections to Monterrico and other Pacific coast destinations.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or a shuttle service to get to Monterrico from Guatemala City or other hubs. Make sure to book in advance with a reputable operator, as some may charge exorbitant prices or have poor safety records.

How to Get Around in Monterrico

Since Monterrico is a small village, you can easily explore it on foot or by bike. You can rent a bike at many hotels and shops in town for around $5 per day. Alternatively, you can hire a tuk-tuk or a taxi to take you around town or to nearby destinations, such as the airport or the bus station.

Where to Go Next from Monterrico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore Guatemala, there are many nearby destinations and excursions you can try, such as:

  • Antigua: A charming colonial town, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its colorful architecture, cobblestone streets, and volcanoes. It is a 3-hour drive from Monterrico.

  • Lake Atitlan: A breathtaking lake surrounded by three volcanoes, offering many watersports and hiking options, as well as Mayan villages and markets. It is a 4-hour drive from Monterrico.

  • Rio Dulce: A scenic river that flows from Lake Izabal to the Caribbean Sea, offering boat tours, hot springs, and wildlife watching. It is a 5-hour drive from Monterrico.

Conclusion

Monterrico is a hidden gem in Guatemala that offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural traditions, and peaceful vibes. Whether you’re looking for a beach vacation, an adventure trip, or a spiritual retreat, Monterrico has something for everyone. So pack your bags and come to discover the beauty of this Pacific coast paradise.
