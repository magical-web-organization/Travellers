Introduction to Braulio Carrillo National Park

Located in Central Valley, in Costa Rica, Braulio Carillo National Park is a lush tropical paradise covering over 115,000 acres of land. The park is named after Braulio Carrillo, a former Costa Rican president, and it is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including jaguars, pumas, monkeys, and hundreds of bird species. With its numerous hiking and biking trails, cultural attractions, and stunning scenic views, Braulio Carrillo National Park is the perfect place to explore for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Braulio Carrillo National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Nacional de Costa Rica: Located in the nearby capital, San Jose, the National Museum of Costa Rica houses the country’s cultural and natural relics, including pre-Columbian artifacts and information on the indigenous tribes who once inhabited Costa Rica.
  • Braulio Carrillo Train Adventure: A restored colonial railway, the train chugs through the park and nearby valleys, allowing visitors to view the national park’s scenic views and the communities lining the railway.

Natural Attractions of Braulio Carrillo National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Selva Biological Station: A research station and eco-lodge located in the heart of Braulio Carrillo National Park. With its many hiking trails, visitors can explore the unique flora and fauna of the region, including the park’s diverse bird population.
  • Waterfall Gardens: A national landmark within the park, the Waterfall Gardens are a series of cascading waterfalls amidst lush green forest which is especially beautiful during the rainy season.
  • El Volador Lookout Point: A scenic lookout that provides a stunning view of the Braulio Carrillo National Park, including the San Jose metropolitan area at night.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Braulio Carrillo National Park

  • The Sendero Los Tucanes Trail: One of the best trails in the park, the Sendero Los Tucanes trail is a 3-mile hike that features scenic views of the park’s rainforest, waterfalls, and lagoons.
  • The Sendero Las Palmas Trail: A 2-mile hike located near the La Selva Biological Station, this trail takes visitors through the park’s lowland rainforest and offers scenic views of the Tarcoles River.
  • Biking Route: Starting from Puerto Viejo de Sarapiqui, this 16-mile biking route is an excellent way to explore the park’s scenic beauty. The route is relatively easy and takes you through Sarapiqui’s tropical fruits amid other attractive flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Braulio Carrillo National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Night Hikes: Experience the park in a different light with a guided night hike, where you will get a chance to see the park’s nocturnal animals, including bats, owls and certain frog species.
  • White Water Rafting: Visitors can go white-water rafting on the park’s various rivers, including the Sarapiqui, Puerto Viejo, and Pacuare Rivers.
  • Horseback Riding: The park’s entryway offers a horseback riding tour of the park with various routes, ranging from waterfalls to viewpoints making the visitor feel they are in a special paradise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Braulio Carrillo National Park

Day 1: Arrival in the park

Day 2: Guided hiking tour of the park’s scenic trails, including the Sendero Los Tucanes trail

Day 3: Cultural day trip to the National Museum, followed by a guided Braulio Carrillo Train Adventure

Day 4: Waterfall Gardens

Day 5: White water rafting or horseback riding

Day 6: El Volador Lookout Point

Day 7: Departure from the park

Food and Drink Scene of Braulio Carrillo National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gallo Pinto: Costa Rica’s national dish, gallo pinto is made with beans, rice, and spices and goes perfectly well with any dish
  • Casado: A local dish, casado is a traditional lunch dish comprising rice, beans, plantains, salad, and a choice of grilled meat, usually tuna or chicken.
  • Arroz con Pollo: A popular dish of chicken, rice, and peas seasoned with garlic and onion.

Local Beverages

  • Café Britt Coffee: Costa Rica is known for its quality coffee, and Café Britt Coffee is no exception. It is a must-try for all coffee lovers!
  • Fruit Juices: Hit any of the region’s cafes or restaurants for a fresh fruit juice of mango, pineapple, passionfruit, and others, which are a refreshing thirst quencher
  • Imperial Beer: Costa Rica’s national beer, Imperial Beer is available everywhere and is a perfect accompaniment to any lunch or dinner.

Where to Stay in Braulio Carrillo National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Selva Biological Station: Visitors with a knack for biology can stay at this research station. La Selva offers reasonably priced dormitory-style accommodations, breakfast, and dinner included for all guests to begin the day in the best way possible.
  • El Toucanet Lodge: The Lodge has comfortable, well-appointed rooms with fantastic views, a full breakfast for guests, and an on-site swimming pool for relaxation.
  • Ara Ambigua Lodge: A luxurious stay in the forest with various activities such as horse riding, bird watching, and zip lining, to mention a few.

What to Buy in Braulio Carrillo National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee and Cacao Products: A bag of Costa Rican coffee beans or locally produced chocolate is the perfect souvenir to bring home from the park, both for its quality and taste.
  • Jade and Precious Stones: Local craft stores sell souvenirs made of local stones and gemstones, which is excellent for a precious yet memorable gift for family or friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Braulio Carrillo National Park

The area is fond of their way of life and has a close relationship with nature. Costa Ricans often lead a charming and relaxed lifestyle, which the visitors are free to indulge in. In addition, locals speak Spanish, and tourists can find it useful to learn some basic Spanish phrases.

Best Time to Visit Braulio Carrillo National Park

The ideal time to visit Braulio Carrillo National Park is from December to March when the weather is relatively dry. The region receives heavy rain during May to November, and during this season, the park may be closed due to landslides and floods, significantly affecting visitors’ movement and, as a result, their experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Braulio Carrillo National Park

Do:

  • Dress according to the weather, comfortable hiking boots, and raincoats
  • Respect the ecosystem; visitors should not throw trash or try to feed the animals in the park - it is not allowed in the park.
  • Keep personal hygiene: visitors should maintain their hygiene to control the spread of communicable diseases both to themselves and others.

Don’t:

  • Visitors should not smoke or light a fire given the high risk of forest fires. Smoking within the park is strictly prohibited.
  • Visitors should avoid petting or touching wild animals they may stumble upon in the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Braulio Carrillo National Park

The park is generally safe to visit, but visitors should take necessary precautions such as carrying a first aid kit, sunscreen, and insect repellent. Visitors should also carry ample water as the park’s activities may get strenuous.

Health in Braulio Carrillo National Park

Braulio Carrillo National Park is in a tropical region, and visitors should exercise caution to avoid contracting illnesses such as malaria or dengue fever. Visitors should ensure that they take all necessary vaccines ahead of their visit and pack sufficient medication to assist with their personal care.

Getting to Braulio Carrillo National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Braulio Carrillo National Park

  • San Jose International Airport
  • Limon International Airport

Visitors can easily find a taxi to Braulio Carrillo National Park from San Jose International Airport or Limon International Airport. For visitors who prefer, various car rental options are available.

Bus Stations Near Braulio Carrillo National Park

  • San Jose Bus Station

There are various buses heading to Puerto Viejo, near Braulio Carrillo National Park. Visitors may board buses leaving San Jose Bus Station and stop at their respective lodgings around the area.

Other Options

Visitors may hire personal convoys or book private transportation to the Park.

How to Get Around in Braulio Carrillo National Park

Braulio Carrillo National Park is a large tract of land that one can explore using various modes of transportation, including hiking, cycling, horseback riding, or taking a guided tour in the area.

Where to Go Next from Braulio Carrillo National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tortuguero National Park

  • Only accessible by boat, Tortuguero National Park is a remote nature reserve that’s home to green sea turtles and other aquatic creatures.

Arenal Volcano National Park

  • About two hours from Braulio Carrillo National Park, Arenal Volcano is famous for its hot springs and magnificent views of the volcano.

Puerto Viejo de Talamanca

  • A beach town less than two hours from Braulio Carrillo National Park, Puerto Viejo de Talamanca has got the perfect beach spot to relax and continue your scenic experience.

Conclusion

Braulio Carrillo National Park is a tropical paradise, with its stunning scenic views, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures that visitors can enjoy. Visitors to the park can explore its forest trails, scenic waterfalls, and wild animals like, jaguars and howler monkeys. Furthermore, visitors can venture out to nearby destinations, such as Tortuguero National Park, Arenal Volcano National Park, or Puerto Viejo de Talamanca for an exciting, scenic experience. It is indeed a place worth considering when planning a trip to Costa Rica.
