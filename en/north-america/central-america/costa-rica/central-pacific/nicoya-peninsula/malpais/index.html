Malpaís is a hidden gem located in the Nicoya Peninsula of Costa Rica’s Central Pacific region. With its tranquil beaches, lush forests, and diverse wildlife, Malpaís is a popular destination for eco-tourism and outdoor adventure enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Malpaís, from hiking and biking routes to culturally significant attractions. We’ll also cover important logistical information, such as transportation options and where to stay, to help you plan your dream vacation to this unspoiled paradise.

Introduction to Malpaís

Malpaís is a secluded coastal town in the Nicoya Peninsula, known for its pristine beaches, excellent surfing, and abundant wildlife. The region is largely undeveloped, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a more authentic and peaceful experience. Malpaís is located about 150 km west of San José and can be accessed via a scenic 5-hour drive or a domestic flight to Tambor Airport. Once you arrive, you’ll be greeted by a tropical paradise that offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Malpaís

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cabuya Island Cemetery: This rustic island cemetery has a unique history and is only accessible during low tide.
  • La Gloria Indigenous Reserve: This cultural reserve offers a glimpse into the indigenous traditions and way of life.

Natural Attractions of Malpaís: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve: Explore Costa Rica’s first and largest nature reserve and witness the diverse flora and fauna.
  • Montezuma Waterfall: Hike through the lush rainforest to reach these gorgeous cascading waterfalls.
  • Playa Hermosa: This beautiful beach is a must-visit destination for surfing enthusiasts.
  • Birdwatching: Malpaís is home to a variety of species, including toucans, parrots, and macaws.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malpaís

  • Malpaís to Santa Teresa Beach Trail: This 4 km trail takes you through the lush tropical forests of the region, and the views at the end are definitely worth it.
  • Cabo Blanco Absolute Natural Reserve Trail: Take the self-guided hike through the heart of the reserve and experience the wonders of the forest and the ocean.

Best Things to Do in Malpaís: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: Malpaís is known for its consistent waves and ideal surf conditions, making it a destination for surfers of all skill levels.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided tour along the beaches and explore the lush tropical forests on horseback.
  • Yoga and Meditation: Malpaís has a thriving yoga community, offering daily classes and workshops.
  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: The crystal blue waters surrounding Malpaís offer an abundance of underwater adventures.

Food and Drink Scene of Malpaís

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gallo Pinto: A traditional Costa Rican dish that consists of rice and beans. Often served with eggs or meat.
  • Casado: A typical lunch dish that includes rice, beans, plantains, salad, and your choice of meat or fish.

Local Beverages

  • Guaro: A popular local liquor made from sugar cane that can be found in many cocktails.
  • Pipa: A refreshing and healthy drink made from coconut water.

Where to Stay in Malpaís: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Casa Chameleon Hotel: This luxurious hotel offers stunning ocean views and a private pool.
  • Santa Teresa Boutique Hotel: This stylish hotel offers easy access to the beach and an infinity pool.
  • Hostel Dos Monos: This budget-friendly option is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Malpaís: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally made ceramics, textiles, and handicrafts.
  • Coffee and chocolate which make great authentic Costa Rican gifts to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Malpaís

The local culture in Malpaís is heavily influenced by its indigenous roots, and community members are known for their welcoming and friendly nature. As a visitor, it’s important to respect the area’s cultural traditions and customs, which include speaking Spanish and practicing eco-friendly tourism.

Best Time to Visit Malpaís

The best time to visit Malpaís is during the dry season, which spans from December to April. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and excursions. The rainy season can cause access issues to otherwise possible places and terrain to hike or traverse, as well as may exacerbate potential health concerns.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malpaís

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and customs of the area.
  • Explore the diverse wildlife and natural wonders of the region.
  • Take part in eco-friendly tourism practices.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the local communities or cultures in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malpaís

Malpaís is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to exercise basic precautions such as securing your belongings and avoiding walking alone at night, especially by the beach and at secluded areas where accessibility is low and infrequent.

Health in Malpaís

Visitors should take steps to protect themselves from the sun and stay hydrated as they explore the outdoors. Be aware of the local wildlife in the area and the potential occurrence of various health concerns such as insect bites or food and water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Malpaís: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malpaís

The nearest airport to Malpaís is Tambor Airport, which has daily flights to San José and other domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Malpaís

Bus services to Malpaís are available from San José and other nearby towns, with regular schedules throughout the year. Albeit, the routes may vary due to the seasonality or specific travel package.

Other Options

Shared shuttles and private transfers are available from San José and other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Malpaís

Renting a car or scooter is the most convenient way to get around Malpaís, although bicycles are also available for rent for a more eco-friendly experience.

Where to Go Next from Malpaís: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve: Visit the oldest remaining cloud forest and enjoy breathtaking views of the Pacific coast.
  • Arenal Volcano: The Arenal Volcano offers a unique natural experience with luxury amenities, with thermal spas and nearby hiking trails to explore the surrounding lush forests and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

Malpaís is a destination that offers a perfect blend of adventure, relaxation, and natural beauty for travelers seeking a unique experience. Whether you’re a surfer, hiker, or simply looking for an eco-friendly holiday, Malpaís is the ideal spot. Plan your dream vacation today, and don’t forget to take home a piece of this tropical paradise with you by purchasing local handicrafts and other treasures for keepsake.
