Introduction to Esterillos

Located in the Central Pacific region of Costa Rica, Esterillos is a quiet coastal town famous for its palm-lined beaches, pristine waterfalls, and lush tropical forests. This destination offers a perfect blend of nature, adventure, history, and culture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience.

List of Places to Visit in Esterillos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Histórico de Esterillos: A small museum that showcases the history and culture of the town through photographs, artifacts, and documents.
  • Quepos La Marina Pez Vela: A marina located near Esterillos that is a hub for recreational fishing and water sports.
  • Feria del Agricultor de Parrita: A popular farmer’s market held every Saturday morning, featuring locally grown fruits, vegetables, and handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Esterillos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Esterillos Beach: A stunning stretch of beach that runs for miles and offers great surfing and swimming opportunities.
  • Playa Bejuco: A quiet beach that is perfect for long walks and shell collecting.
  • Rainmaker Conservation Project: A nature reserve that offers hiking trails, suspended bridges, and amazing views of the surrounding rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Esterillos

  • Fila Costeña: A challenging 10-mile trail that takes you through the lush forest and offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Quebrada Ganado Wildlife Refuge: A scenic 4-mile trail that leads to a stunning waterfall and offers a chance to spot local wildlife, including monkeys and sloths.

Best Things to Do in Esterillos: Activities and Tours

  • Learn to surf: Esterillos is known for its great waves and surfing schools.
  • Take a beach horseback ride: Explore the town’s coastline on horseback.
  • Go sportfishing: Join a fishing tour and try your luck catching mahi-mahi, tuna, marlin, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Esterillos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Casado: A traditional dish that consists of rice, black beans, plantains, salad, and a choice of meat.
  • Ceviche: A seafood dish typically made with raw fish, marinated in lime juice and mixed with vegetables and spices.
  • Gallo Pinto: A breakfast dish made with rice and beans, served with eggs and tortillas.

Local Beverages

  • Imperial Beer: A popular lager beer brewed in Costa Rica.
  • Coconut water: Fresh and local, coconut water is a refreshing drink to enjoy on a warm day.
  • Chicha: A sweet and sour fermented drink made from maize and sugar.

Where to Stay in Esterillos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Pelicano: A beachfront hotel with an outdoor pool, bar, and restaurant.
  • Hostel Vista Serena: A laid-back hostel that offers affordable private rooms and dormitories.
  • Hotel La Dolce Vita: A cozy boutique hotel with a tropical garden and ocean views.

What to Buy in Esterillos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts: Locally made pottery, baskets, and carvings make for unique souvenirs.
  • Coffee: Costa Rica is known for its high-quality coffee, and you can find plenty of locally grown options.
  • Vanilla: This sweet and fragrant spice is an essential ingredient in many Costa Rican dishes and makes for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Esterillos

As in much of Costa Rica, the locals in Esterillos are warm and friendly. It is common to greet people with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek. The primary language spoken is Spanish, although many locals speak English as well. Tipping is not mandatory but is appreciated in restaurants and for services like tour guides.

Best Time to Visit Esterillos

The dry season (December to April) is the best time to visit Esterillos, as the weather is sunny and there is little rain. However, this is also the peak tourist season, meaning that prices are higher and there are more crowds. The rainy season (May to November) can still be enjoyable, with fewer visitors and lush flora, but the strong winds can make beach activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Esterillos

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish phrases.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Take photos of people without asking for their permission.
  • Ignore safety warnings and advice from locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Esterillos

Esterillos is considered a safe destination for travelers, but it is always wise to take precautions. Keep your valuables close, avoid walking alone at night, and be cautious when swimming in the ocean, as the waves and currents can be strong.

Health in Esterillos

The tap water in Costa Rica is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available. Mosquitoes can be a problem, especially during the rainy season, so be sure to pack plenty of insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing in the evenings.

Getting to Esterillos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Esterillos

The closest airport to Esterillos is Juan Santamaria International Airport in San Jose, which is about a 2.5-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Esterillos

The nearest bus terminal is in Jaco, about 30 minutes away from Esterillos by car.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are widely available in San Jose and Jaco.

How to Get Around in Esterillos

The best way to get around Esterillos is by rental car, as public transportation is limited. Taxis are also available but can be expensive. Bicycles can be rented in town for those looking for a more environmentally-friendly option.

Where to Go Next from Esterillos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manuel Antonio National Park: A stunning nature reserve with hiking trails and white-sand beaches, located about an hour’s drive south of Esterillos.
  • Montezuma: A small town on the Nicoya Peninsula, known for its bohemian vibe, yoga retreats, and beautiful beaches.
  • Arenal Volcano National Park: A popular destination for its volcano hikes, hot springs, and scenic views, located about 4 hours from Esterillos.

Conclusion

Esterillos is a hidden gem in Costa Rica, offering a perfect mix of adventure, relaxation, and culture. From the stunning beaches and lush forests to the rich history and cuisine, this destination has something for everyone. By following this guide and being respectful of the local customs and traditions, you can have an unforgettable experience in Esterillos.
