Introduction to Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Limón, a province located on the Caribbean coast of Costa Rica, is a vibrant and culturally rich destination that offers scenic landscapes, beautiful beaches, and delicious local cuisine. Filled with unspoiled natural beauty, Limón is a perfect destination for those looking for a tropical getaway filled with adventure, relaxation and a taste of local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Limón Museum

Located in the center of Limón, this museum features exhibits about the history and culture of the Afro-Caribbean people who originally settled in the region.

Tortuguero National Park

Tortuguero is a remote national park located on the northern Caribbean coast known for its turtle nesting sites. The park features a network of waterways that can be explored by boat, giving visitors an opportunity to see a wide variety of wildlife including monkeys, crocodiles and numerous bird species.

Natural Attractions of Limón (Province, Costa Rica): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gandoca-Manzanillo Wildlife Refuge

Located further south on the coast from Limón, this wildlife refuge offers visitors a chance to explore the lush rainforests and stunning coral reefs with a variety of activities including hiking, snorkeling and bird-watching.

Cahuita National Park

Offering one of the best coral reef systems in the country, Cahuita National Park is a beautiful coastal park where visitors can enjoy hiking through the jungle, bird-watching and snorkeling along the reefs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Chirripó National Park

Hiking the highest peak in Costa Rica is an incredible experience that rewards those who make the trek with stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The hike is challenging, but well worth it for the beautiful scenery.

Coastal Highway: From Cahuita to Puerto Limón

Ride your bike along the coastline, and enjoy the spectacular countryside views of the Caribbean coast.

Best Things to Do in Limón (Province, Costa Rica): Activities and Tours

Surfing

There are excellent surfing spots along the beaches in the Limón Province, with popular spots including Salsa Brava and Playa Cocles.

White Water Rafting

The Pacuare River is one of the best rafting spots in the country, with challenging rapids and stunning jungle scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Day 1: Arrive in Puerto Limón

After you arrive, check in at your hotel, and unwind with a stroll around this colorful Caribbean town.

Day 2: Visit Tortuguero National Park

Explore one of Costa Rica’s most stunning national parks by taking a boat tour through its intricate network of waterways.

Day 3: Visit Cahuita National Park

Explore Cahuita’s gorgeous beaches, coastal forest, and coral reef ecosystem.

Day 4: Relax at the Beach

Spend the day relaxing, swimming, or surfing at Playa Cocles or Punta Uva Beach.

Day 5: Go on a Jungle Hike

Hike through the jungle trails of the Gandoca-Manzanillo Wildlife Refuge and see its fantastic wildlife.

Day 6: White Water Rafting

Experience adrenaline-fueled adventure by white water rafting on the Pacuare River.

Day 7: Depart

Take a relaxing morning, and then pack up and head out on your journey, remembering the beautiful sights, sounds, and experiences of Limón.

Food and Drink Scene of Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Must-Try Dishes

Rice and Beans

A popular local dish, this coconut-infused rice-and-beans mix is usually served alongside various meats, seafood, plantains, or avocado.

Pati

Pati is a delicious Caribbean pastry filled with beef or chicken and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Coco Loco

This refreshing drink is a mix of coconut water, rum, and other fruit juices.

Caribbean-style Rum Cocktails

The freshest fruit juices are mixed with local rum to make some of the Caribbean’s most delicious cocktails.

Where to Stay in Limón (Province, Costa Rica): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puerto Viejo Hostel

A budget-friendly option, this hostel is located in the heart of the town and offers dorm-style and private rooms.

Le Cameleon Boutique Hotel

A luxury hotel located on Cocles Beach, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Limón (Province, Costa Rica): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Chocolate

Limón is known for its high-quality, locally produced chocolate, which can be found in shops throughout the region.

Colorful Art and Crafts

Limón’s Afro-Caribbean culture reflects a vibrant artistic movement, with shops and markets selling handcrafted souvenirs and pieces that will remind you of your journey.

Local Culture and Customs of Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Pay attention to the musical and artistic influence of Afro-Caribbean culture which are vibrant in Limón. Also, it’s good to know that tipping in restaurants is standard practice, with 10% being the usual amount.

Best Time to Visit Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

The best time to visit Limón is during the dry season, from December to April, when there is the least amount of rain and the weather is most pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

Do:

  • Take advantage of the beautiful beaches.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the Afro-Caribbean culture.
  • Carry an umbrella during rainy season.

Don’t:

  • Stray from marked trails or swim in unmarked areas.
  • Take valuable things to the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

While Limón is generally safe, be sure to use the usual safety measures, such as not carrying large sums of money, not leaving valuables in your car, and avoiding solitary places at night.

Health in Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

While it’s good to take mosquito repellent, there are no vaccination requirements for entering Costa Rica. Keep in mind some pharmacies may require a prescription for certain medications.

Getting to Limón (Province, Costa Rica): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

The Limón International Airport is a smaller airport that mostly offers domestic flights from San José and other locations in Costa Rica.

Bus Stations Near Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

There are bus stations in Puerto Limón and other towns in Limón that offer frequent bus service to San José and other destinations throughout the country.

Other Options

Most visitors prefer renting a car, which offers the most flexibility and is the easiest way to navigate the region’s roads. Taxis are also readily available throughout the area.

How to Get Around in Limón (Province, Costa Rica)

The easiest way to get around Limón is by car or taxi. But you can also rent a bicycle or scooter, take public transportation, or simply walk around town.

Where to Go Next from Limón (Province, Costa Rica): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San José

A visit to the capital of Costa Rica, with its museums, historic landmarks, and buzzing street life, is a great way to experience more of the country.

Turrialba

This lush green town is situated in the central mountains of Costa Rica, and features a stunning active volcano - Turrialba Volcano - and whitewater rafting opportunities.

Conclusion

Limón offers a compelling combination of natural beauty, cultural significance, and sumptuous cuisine, making it the perfect destination for travelers interested in adventure and relaxation. By following this guide, you’ll have insider knowledge to make the most out of your time in this stunning corner of Costa Rica on the Caribbean coast.
