Introduction to Punta Uva

Punta Uva is a stunning beach town located in the Limón province of Costa Rica. The pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and crystal-clear waters make Punta Uva the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

The lively town is known for its friendly locals, vibrant culture, and a wide range of outdoor activities. You can enjoy a variety of adventures such as kayaking, surfing, snorkeling, hiking, and much more. Once you’re in Punta Uva, you’ll be spoiled for choice in terms of things to do.

Food and Drink Scene of Punta Uva

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gallo Pinto, a traditional dish made with rice and beans

  • Casado, a typical Costa Rican dish made up of rice, beans, salad, fried plantains, and your choice of protein.

  • Seafood, with fresh catches from the Caribbean Sea

Local Beverages

  • Costa Rican Coffee

  • Guaro Sour, a cocktail made with a popular Costa Rican liquor called Guaro

Local Culture and Customs of Punta Uva

Costa Rica is known for its friendly locals, who are welcoming and open to visitors. Some cultural customs to keep in mind when visiting Punta Uva:

  • Ticos (Costa Ricans) greet each other with a kiss on the cheek.

  • Ticos tend to be more laid back and relaxed than North Americans and Europeans.

Best Time to Visit Punta Uva

The best time to visit Punta Uva is between December and April, during the dry season. The temperatures are warm and sunny, with little rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Punta Uva

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine

  • Take a dip in the warm waters of the Caribbean Sea

  • Respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended on the beach

  • Wear flashy jewelry

  • Walk alone at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Punta Uva

Punta Uva is a safe destination for travelers, but like any other tourist destination, crime can occur. Follow these tips to stay safe:

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended

  • Avoid walking alone at night

  • Use licensed taxis

Health in Punta Uva

There aren’t any major health concerns in Punta Uva, but it’s advisable to take mosquito repellent and sunscreen with you.

How to Get Around in Punta Uva

You can easily get around Punta Uva on foot. If you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can rent a bike or hire a taxi.
