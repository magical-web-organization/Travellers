Introduction to Tortuguero

Tortuguero, located in Limón Province of Costa Rica, is a small village situated on the northeastern part of the country, known for its amazing national park that attracts nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers from all over the world. With an abundance of flora and fauna and its breathtaking scenery, Tortuguero has become one of the must-visit destinations in Costa Rica. The region is also a prime nesting ground for sea turtles, making it a popular destination for eco-tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Tortuguero

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tortuguero might not be known for its cultural and historical sites, but there are still some attractions worth exploring:

  1. The Sea Turtle Conservancy – a non-profit organization that focuses on research and conservation of sea turtles and their habitats.

  2. The Jungle Tom Safaris – a wildlife tour that offers visitors the chance to explore the Tortuguero canals while spotting amazing creatures in their natural habitats.

  3. The Tortuguero Village – a picturesque village with a small museum, souvenir shops, and local restaurants that reflect the culture of the area.

Natural Attractions of Tortuguero: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The absolute highlight of Tortuguero is its national park - Tortuguero National Park - which comprises over 19,000 hectares of protected land that includes canals, lagoons, and beaches.

  1. Tortuguero Canals – the canals of Tortuguero offer amazing opportunities for bird watching, and animal spotting. Tour operators offer small boats, kayaks, and canoes for visitors to explore the canals.

  2. Tortuguero Beach – one of the few beaches in the country that remains completely untouched and natural. Home to nesting sea turtles, The beach area is also perfect for surfing and sunbathing.

  3. The Cerro Tortuguero – a hiking trail for adventure seekers that offers visitors scenic views of the national park and a chance to spot exotic wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tortuguero

Tortuguero has several trails and paths to explore, making it a hiker’s paradise. Visitors can hike through the national park and spot rare wildlife species such as jaguars, monkeys, and sloths. Biking enthusiasts can also rent bikes and explore the village and surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Tortuguero: Activities and Tours

From nature tours to cultural experiences, Tortuguero offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours:

  1. Turtle spotting – visitors can take night tours to see the nesting of the Green Sea Turtles, the Hawksbill Sea Turtles, and the Loggerhead Sea Turtles.

  2. Jungle canopy tours – visitors can zip-line through the jungle treetops to observe stunning views of the national park.

  3. Chocolate Tours – learn about the production of chocolate from start to finish with a visit to the local cacao plantations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tortuguero

  • Day 1 – Arrival and check-in at your preferred hotel.

  • Day 2 – Hiking and wildlife spotting in the Tortuguero National Park.

  • Day 3 – Kayaking and paddleboarding in the canals of Tortuguero.

  • Day 4 – Learn about the culture of the village and try some Costa Rican dishes in local restaurants.

  • Day 5 – Sea Turtle spotting at night.

  • Day 6 – Tour the Tortuguero jungle and zip-line through the treetops.

  • Day 7 – Departure from Tortuguero.

Food and Drink Scene of Tortuguero

Must-Try Dishes

Tortuguero is known for its fresh seafood. One of the most popular dishes is the ceviche, a dish made of raw fish served in citrus sauce. Other local dishes worth trying are:

  1. Rice and beans – a staple dish in Costa Rica

  2. Casado – a mixed plate of rice, beans, meat, and vegetables.

  3. Sopa Negra – a black bean soup with hard-boiled eggs and cilantro.

Local Beverages

  1. Horchata – a creamy non-alcoholic drink made with rice and cinnamon.

  2. Guaro – known as the national drink of Costa Rica, a distilled sugarcane liquor that is often mixed into cocktails.

Where to Stay in Tortuguero: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tortuguero offers several lodging options for visitors in the village or within the national park. Options include budget-friendly hostels, luxury hotels, and cabins. Some of the top choices include:

  1. Turtle Beach Lodge – offering an outdoor pool and private beach access.

  2. Tortuguero Eco Lodge – features eco-friendly bungalows, an on-site restaurant, and a garden area.

  3. Mawamba Lodge – a luxury lodge that features an outdoor pool, on-site restaurant, and tours.

What to Buy in Tortuguero: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase souvenirs from the gift shops and craft stalls in the village center, and some of the local products that visitors can take home include:

  1. Handmade jewelry featuring sea turtle designs.

  2. Colorful hammocks.

  3. Local chocolate and coffee.

Local Culture and Customs of Tortuguero

Costa Ricans, or Ticos as they are locally known, are known for being friendly, laid back, and hospitable. Visitors to Tortuguero should be mindful of local religious customs and respect for conservation efforts.

Best Time to Visit Tortuguero

The best time to visit Tortuguero is during the dry season, from December to April. During this time, the waters are calmer and ideal for exploring the canals, and it is also the best time for turtle nesting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tortuguero

Do:

  1. Stay in eco-lodges to support conservation efforts.

  2. Respect local customs and culture.

  3. Learn some basic Spanish phrases before traveling there.

Don’t:

  1. Buy souvenirs made from endangered wildlife.

  2. Feed or touch wildlife.

  3. Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tortuguero

Tortuguero is a relatively safe destination. Visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as keeping valuables secured and not walking alone late at night in unlit areas.

Health in Tortuguero

Travelers should be up to date on routine vaccinations. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as Dengue, Zika, and Chikungunya.

Getting to Tortuguero: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travelers can access Tortuguero by:

Airports Near Tortuguero

  1. San Jose International Airport – international flights, domestic flights

  2. Limon International Airport – domestic flights

Bus Stations Near Tortuguero

  1. San Jose Bus Station Terminal 7-10 – regular bus services to Limon, then a water taxi to Mothership Landing in Tortuguero

  2. Limon Bus Station – regular bus services to Cariari, then a water taxi

Other Options

Visitors can also use private shuttle and taxi services from various locations such as San Jose, Arenal, or Puerto Viejo, connected with a boat shuttle.

How to Get Around in Tortuguero

Tortuguero is a small village, and the best way to get around is on foot, especially in the village itself. Water taxis, kayaks, and canoes are also popular ways of exploring the canals.

Where to Go Next from Tortuguero: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Tortuguero, visitors can explore nearby destinations such as:

  1. Arenal Volcano National Park – a two-hour drive from Tortuguero, this national park features a still-active volcano and hot springs.

  2. Cahuita National Park – located on the southern Caribbean coast of Costa Rica, this park is home to coral reefs, wildlife, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Tortuguero is an amazing destination that offers visitors a variety of experiences, from wildlife sightings to the exploration of the jungle canopy. With its picturesque village, pristine beaches, and lush forests, Tortuguero is a must-visit destination in Costa Rica. Plan your trip today and immerse yourself in the unique culture and natural beauty of Tortuguero.
