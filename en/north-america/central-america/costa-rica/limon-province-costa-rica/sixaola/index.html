Travel Guide: Sixaola, Costa Rica

Located in the Limón Province of Costa Rica, Sixaola is a quaint little town nestled near the Costa Rican border with Panama. This town is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic Costa Rican experience. In this comprehensive guide, we will take a deep dive into all the things you need to know to plan your trip to Sixaola.

List of Places to Visit in Sixaola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wilson Botanical Garden: This garden is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts and bird lovers. The garden features a vast collection of plants and flowers native to Costa Rica. You can also go on an informative guided tour that takes you through different habitats and teaches you about the local flora and fauna.
  2. Bri Bri indigenous reserve: This indigenous reservation allows you to explore, interact and learnmore about one of the earliest settled groups in Costa Rica, The Bri Bri- Native American tribe. The Bri Bri culture datesback centuries, and their traditions continue to thrive today. You can go on a guided cultural tour of the reserve that includes hikes, traditional ceremonies, and cultural experiences.
  3. Escalante ruins: This is a great place to visit for history lovers. These ruins date back as far as 1500BC and remain today. Visitors can tour and learn about the indigenous people ofCosta Rica who lived there centuries ago, experiencing their customs, traditions, and way of life.

Natural Attractions of Sixaola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sixaola River: You can take a canoeing trip down the Sixaola River, offering breathtaking views of the lush Costa Rican rainforest and a chance to experience the biodiversity of the area. You can also take a guided tour of the river, with the opportunity to see various native animals in their natural habitats such as the howler monkey, white-faced capuchin monkeys, sloths, toucans, and many more.
  2. La Amistad International Park: This park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a must-visit for any nature lover. The park has an extensive network of hiking trails, and the scenery includes waterfalls, dense forests, and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  3. La Isla Moin: This beautiful island is located just off the coast of Sixaola, and it is a great spot for surfing and sunbathing. The crystal-clear waters offer an excellent opportunity for snorkeling, and you can also explore the local fishing village and learn about their cultural and customs.

Best Things to Do in Sixaola: Activities and Tours

  1. Canopy Tours: The Canopy tours offer an adrenaline-pumping experience that will take you on a zip-lining journey through the rainforest. It is a fantastic experience for those looking for an adventure and a different perspective on the rainforest.
  2. White water rafting: The Sixaola River offers an exciting white water rafting experience for those looking for an adventure. You can choose from various rapids’ levels and a range of tours that cater to both beginners and the experienced.
  3. Chocolate Tours: Learn about the chocolate-making tradition of the indigenous Bribri tribe and sample some of the delicious chocolates made from organic cacao beans during this informative and interactive tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sixaola

  • Day 1-2: Arrive in Sixaola and explore the town. Visit the indigenous Bri Bri reserve and learn about their culture and traditions.
  • Day 3: Go on a guided tour of the Wilson Botanical Garden and enjoy the beautiful flora and fauna of the region.
  • Day 4-5: Visit the Sixaola river and go on a guided canoeing tour. You can also explore the white-water rafting and the amazing outdoor activities in the area.
  • Day 5-6: Explore La Amistad International Park and experience the breathtaking views and natural beauty of the region.
  • Day 7: Visit La Isla Moin and spend a day soaking up the sun, surfing, and swimming.

Food and Drink Scene of Sixaola

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Casado: A traditional Costa Rican dish that consists of rice and beans, a protein(fish, beef or chicken), plantains, salad & Tortilla not excluded.
  2. Gallo Pinto: The most typical and national dish of Costa Rica is fragrant rice and fried beans, contributed by onions, sweet peppers, and cilantro.
  3. Tamales: A dish prepared from masa (corn dough) which is stuffed with a wide range of options such as beef, chicken, cheese vegetables, and sometimes chilies. The stuffed Masa wrapped up in a banana leaf is then steam cooked to give a moist and soft texture to the dough.

Local Beverages

  1. Guaro: A Costa Rican spirit distilled from sugar cane juice and is a popular choice in cocktails like chili guaro, mojito tico.
  2. Imperial Beer: The signature beer of Costa Rica and is a perfect drink to accompany traditional Costa Rican dishes. Try the creamy, smooth, and refreshing imperial beer.
  3. Lizano Sauce: A mild but tangy hot sauce made with vegetables, including carrot, cucumber, and onion. It’s found on most of Costa Rican tables (condiment jar) and is perfect for spicy food lovers.

Where to Stay in Sixaola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Rios Tropicales Lodge: The Lookout Lodge consists of cabins that are situated amongst rainforest surroundings and offers one of the best scenic views of Sixaola. The lodge offers different outdoor activities ranging from hiking, white-water rafting, canopying to more indulgent activities such as couples massage.
  2. Selva Bananito Lodge: A lot of hammocks and one can have jungle walks and hike to the waterfalls or learn a bit of Costa Rican culture through tours. They offer awesome food that comes with fresh ingredients.
  3. Casa Marbella: A great option for budget travelers looking for a clean and comfortable room in Sixaola. This hostel offers both private and shared rooms and is walking distance to many of the town’s main tourist attractions.

What to Buy in Sixaola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade pottery: local artisans make and sell the pottery, which is traditionally decorated in bright colours and designs, making it a perfect souvenir gift.
  2. Handmade jewelry and crafts: You can purchase the beautiful and uniquely made handcrafteds from the Bri Bri indigenous tribe, which incorporates the traditional materials and designs of their culture.
  3. Organic coffee: Costa Rica is famous for its coffee, and you can buy some freshly roasted coffee beans to take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sixaola

Costa Rica’s culture is richly influenced by Indigenous, African, and Spanish cultures, and the Bri Bri indigenous tribe is among the oldest and most influential. Sixaola Town celebrates the indigenous tradition every August through food festivals, music, and dance to pay homage to the Bri Bri culture and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Sixaola

The best time to visit Sixaola is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. The weather during this time is sunny and warm, perfect for outdoor activities and exploring. However, if you don’t mind the rain, the rainy season from May to November offers cooler temperatures, lower tourism, and discounted prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sixaola

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs, including the Bri Bri indigenous tribe’s traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine and delicacies and learn about the coffee production process.
  3. Do bring enough cash because ATM machines are not readily available.

Don’t:

  1. Do not disrespect or disturb the natural habitats of the area’s wildlife.
  2. Do not buy souvenirs or products that are made from endangered species, including sea turtle shells and ivory.
  3. Do not leave any trash or waste behind while hiking, visiting the reserves, or exploring the natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sixaola

Generally, Sixaola is a safe town to visit, and any security concerns are typical in popular tourist destinations. However, tourists should always be cautious when exploring new places and take the necessary precautions.

Health in Sixaola

Visitors to Sixaola should have their standard vaccinations up to date. Beware of the mosquito-borne diseases present in the region and always use mosquito repellent and cover exposed skin. Use only clean and safe drinking water or, better still, invest in a drinking water bottle that uses a filter.

Getting to Sixaola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sixaola

The nearest airport to Sixaola is Limon International Airport, which is located about 39 km away. However, Juan Santamaria International Airport is the main airport in Costa Rica and is located in San Jose.

Bus Stations Near Sixaola

The bus station in the town is located close to the Sixaola Bridge crossing and is a reliable and affordable mode of transport that operates daily to other places in Costa Rica.

Other Options

Taxis and car rental services are available in the town, and it a great way to get around the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Sixaola

Bicycles and walking are the best ways to get around the town and explore most of the attractions close by. There is no regular public transportation within the town, so renting a car is advisable for those that want to freely explore around the outskirts of the town.

Where to Go Next from Sixaola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cahuita National Park: A great place for visitors to explore and discover the reef and marine wildlife.
  2. Siquirres: A beautiful town known for its white-water rafting and the scenic views it offers.
  3. Puerto Limon: A perfect place for those who love to experience the nightlife and beaches, the town is situated near many tourist locations nearby.

Conclusion

Sixaola is a hidden gem in Costa Rica and is an excellent place to experience the region’s culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With its rich indigenous history, beautiful surroundings, and friendly locals, Sixaola offers something for everyone. Plan your visit, explore the attractions, enjoy the food, and create unforgettable memories during your stay in Sixaola!
