Introduction to Bijagua de Upala

Bijagua de Upala is a small town situated in the Northern part of Costa Rica, nestled between two active volcanoes, Arenal National Park in La Fortuna and Tenorio Volcano National Park. This charming village is surrounded by lush greenery, mountainous landscapes, pristine rivers, and hot springs. Bijagua de Upala is a destination perfect for nature lovers, thrill-seekers, and those who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Bijagua de Upala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Despite being a small town, Bijagua de Upala has a rich history and culture. The following are must-visit culturally significant attractions:

  • Bijagua Art Gallery: The Bijagua Art Gallery features the works of local artists and internationally recognized artists. Visitors can buy art pieces and other handmade crafts to take home as souvenirs.
  • Church of Bijagua de Upala: The church is a prominent landmark and a perfect example of colonial architecture that is still used today.

Natural Attractions of Bijagua de Upala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The town’s breathtaking natural beauty provides plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore in Bijagua de Upala:

  • Rio Celeste: This heavenly blue river located in Tenorio Volcano National Park is one of the most popular attractions in the town. Visitors can trek through the lush rainforest trail and witness the blue waters of the Rio Celeste.
  • Cerro Echandi: This mountain range offers stunning views of the cloud forests and surrounding volcanoes. Visitors can go zip-lining, horseback riding, and hiking to explore the region’s natural beauty.
  • Cano Negro Wildlife Refuge: The wildlife refuge is a sanctuary for a variety of species such as white-faced, howler, and spider monkeys, sloths, caimans, crocodiles, and a variety of birds. Visitors can take a boat ride through the refuge and spot wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bijagua de Upala

Bijagua de Upala is also a paradise for those who love hiking and biking. The town has a few trails that offer a unique way to explore its beauty. The following are some of the popular hiking and biking routes:

  • Heliconias Hanging Bridges: This scenic 1.5 km trail features seven hanging bridges over the Bijagua river, offering spectacular views of the forest.
  • Mystical Secret Waterfall: A short hike leads to a hidden waterfall where visitors can swim in the cool waters of the falls.
  • Biking to Lago Coter and the Arenal Volcano views: Visitors can rent bicycles and cycle to Lago Coter, known for its beautiful sunsets and breathtaking views of the Arenal Volcano.

Best Things to Do in Bijagua de Upala: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor adventure and scenic views, Bijagua de Upala offers an array of activities and tours. The following are some of the best things to do in Bijagua de Upala:

  • Hot Springs and Spa: Visitors can relax and rejuvenate in the natural hot springs that are known for their healing properties and serene environment.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the lush green landscapes on horseback and witness the natural beauty of the town.
  • Bird Watching: Bijagua de Upala is a haven for bird lovers, and visitors can spot many unique bird species in the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Bijagua de Upala

Must-Try Dishes

Costa Rican cuisine is famous for its delicious flavors and fresh ingredients. Here are some traditional dishes that you must try in Bijiagua de Upala:

  • Gallo Pinto: A breakfast dish, made with rice, beans, and spices.
  • Casado: A Costa Rican dish comprising grilled meat, beans, rice, salad, and fried plantains.
  • Ceviche: A seafood dish made with raw fish and lime juice.

Local Beverages

Costa Rica is also known for its delicious drinks and beverages. Here are some local beverages that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Coffee: Costa Rican coffee is world-famous for its unique flavors and delicious aroma.
  • Guaro: A popular local liquor made from sugarcane.
  • Fresco: A refreshing drink made from fruit juice and water.

Where to Stay in Bijagua de Upala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bijagua de Upala offers various accommodation options for visitors with different budgets and travel styles. Here are some of the best options for where to stay in Bijagua de Upala:

  • Finca Mei Tai: A cozy guesthouse located near the Rio Celeste, offering private cabins with unique amenities.
  • Cabinas el Bosque: A budget-friendly option comprising private cottages with a kitchenette and outdoor pool.
  • Casa Rio Celeste: A luxurious and eco-friendly hotel offering beautiful views of the Tenorio volcano and Rio Celeste.

What to Buy in Bijagua de Upala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bijagua de Upala is famous for its handmade crafts, woodwork, and artworks. Here are some souvenir options to bring home:

  • Handmade crafts: Local artisans make handmade crafts from recycled materials, including bags, bracelets, sculptures, and other decorative items.
  • Coffee: Costa Rican coffee is a must-have souvenir item, and you can find the best coffee beans in Bijagua de Upala.
  • Woodwork: Local woodworkers make beautiful cutting boards, figurines, and other decorative items that are perfect for gifting.

Local Culture and Customs of Bijagua de Upala

Bijagua de Upala is a small town with a rich culture and customs. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to respect their culture and customs. Costa Ricans are generally polite and use formal language while interacting with strangers.

Best Time to Visit Bijagua de Upala

The best time to visit Bijagua de Upala is between December and April when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the town can be visited all year round, and each season offers something unique to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bijagua de Upala

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Carry cash as most places do not accept credit cards.
  • Wear comfortable shoes while hiking and exploring the town.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Interact with wild animals.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bijagua de Upala

Bijagua de Upala is a safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution, especially at night. Keep your valuables safe and take care of your belongings while travelling.

Health in Bijagua de Upala

Costa Rica has a good public healthcare system, but visitors should ensure they have travel insurance that includes medical coverage.

Getting to Bijagua de Upala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bijagua de Upala

The nearest airport to Bijagua de Upala is the Daniel Oduber Quiros International Airport in Liberia, approximately two hours away.

Bus Stations Near Bijagua de Upala

The Alajuela Bus Station in San Jose is the closest major bus station to Bijagua de Upala.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bijagua de Upala via private cab or rented car. Taxis and Uber services are also available in the town.

How to Get Around in Bijagua de Upala

Walking is the best way to explore the town’s beauty, but visitors can also rent bikes, cars, or hire taxis to commute.

Where to Go Next from Bijagua de Upala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bijagua de Upala is close to many must-visit destinations in Costa Rica. Visitors can explore the following nearby destinations:

  • Arenal Volcano National Park: Arenal Volcano National Park offers breathtaking views of the volcano and hot springs.
  • Monteverde: A town known for its Cloud Forest Reserve and adrenaline-pumping activities such as zip-lining.
  • Liberia: Liberia is a vibrant town and access point to some of the most beautiful beaches and landscapes in Costa Rica.

Conclusion

Bijagua de Upala is a magical town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, and culture.
