Introduction to Península de Burica

Nestled in the South Pacific of Costa Rica, Península de Burica provides travelers with a unique opportunity to experience the beautiful and unspoiled natural environments of Central America. Located in the province of Puntarenas, Península de Burica is home to some of the most biodiverse ecosystems, from lush rainforests to stunning beaches and coral reefs. This comprehensive travel guide provides all the information you’ll need to plan your dream vacation to Península de Burica.

List of Places to Visit in Península de Burica

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Las Ruinas de Ciudad de Panamá
  • Ballena Beach
  • Golfo Dulce Forest Reserve
  • Playa Pavones
  • Corcovado National Park

Natural Attractions of Península de Burica: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Península de Burica offers outdoor enthusiasts a wide range of activities, including:

  • Hiking through Corcovado National Park
  • Whale watching in Ballena Beach
  • Surfing in Playa Pavones, known for having the second longest left-hand break in the world
  • Snorkeling and scuba diving in Golfo Dulce and Marino Ballena National Park
  • Kayaking through mangrove forests and exploring caves and waterfalls

Hiking/Biking Routes of Península de Burica

  • Corcovado National Park hiking trails
  • Osa Peninsula circuit
  • Piedras Blancas National Park
  • Golfo Dulce Forest Reserve
  • Ciudad Cortés

Best Things to Do in Península de Burica: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there’s something for everyone in Península de Burica. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Take a horseback ride through the rainforest
  • Go on a dolphin and whale-watching tour
  • Spend a day at a luxury spa
  • Take a sunset sailboat trip
  • Visit a coffee plantation and learn about the coffee-making process

Food and Drink Scene of Península de Burica

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: a seafood dish made with fresh fish, lime juice, cilantro, onions, and peppers
  • Gallo Pinto: a traditional Costa Rican rice and beans dish
  • Platano Maduro: Sweet fried plantains served as a side dish or snack
  • Casado: a typical Costa Rican dish of a meat, rice, beans, salad, and platanos
  • Chifrijo: a layered dish of rice, beans, chicharon, tomato salsa, and avocado

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Costa Rican coffee is well-known for its robust flavour, and the region is home to several coffee plantations where you can explore the coffee-making process and sample some of the best crops.
  • Guaro: a clear, sugarcane-based liquor distilled in Costa Rica.
  • Coconut Water: a refreshing drink that can be found throughout Península de Burica thanks to the abundance of coconuts growing in the area.

Where to Stay in Península de Burica: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options for every budget in Península de Burica, including:

  • Luxury resorts such as The Harmony Hotel and Latitude 10 Resort
  • Mid-range lodges such as Hotel Cuna del Ángel and Villas San Jose
  • Budget-friendly hostels such as the Coco Island Inn, and Hostel Lili with shared dorm-style rooms

What to Buy in Península de Burica: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Costa Rican coffee beans or ground coffee
  • Traditional handmade souvenirs such as coconut shell jewelry, handmade woven basket, and pottery.
  • Chocolate bars from Caribeans Chocolate

Local Culture and Customs of Península de Burica

Península de Burica is known for its laid-back and friendly locals. As a visitor to the region, it is essential to respect the local customs and traditions. The region also shares a rich multicultural history, so visitors should take the opportunity to learn more about the indigenous and colonial roots that make up Península de Burica.

Best Time to Visit Península de Burica

The best time to enjoy Península de Burica’s outdoor activities is between December and April, which tends to be the drier period, while the rainy season is from May to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Península de Burica

Do:

  • Learn basic Spanish phrases before arriving in the area, locals appreciate it when visitors make an effort to communicate with them in their language.
  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife, stick to designated trails in national parks, and don’t disturb the animals.
  • Learn more about the culture and traditions of the region’s indigenous populations.

Don’t:

  • Buy items made from endangered species, such as turtle shells, coral, or ivory.
  • Leave any trash behind when visiting natural areas or national parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Península de Burica

Península de Burica is one of the safer regions to travel in Costa Rica. Visitors should still exercise common sense while travelling and keep a keen eye on their belongings in public places.

Health in Península de Burica

There are no major health concerns in Península de Burica. Still, visitors should come prepared with appropriate insect repellent to avoid the risk of mosquito-borne diseases, such as Dengue Fever.

Getting to Península de Burica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Península de Burica can be reached by air or bus, with the nearest airport being San Jose International Airport. From there, visitors can either fly to the regional airport in Golfito or take a bus to Ciudad Neily, where they can transfer for local transport.

Airports Near Península de Burica

  • San Jose International Airport
  • Palmar Sur Airport
  • Golfito Airport

Bus Stations Near Península de Burica

  • Ciudad Neily Bus Station
  • Golfito Bus Station

Other Options

  • Private shuttle services
  • Car rental services

How to Get Around in Península de Burica

The best way to get around Península de Burica is by renting a vehicle or hiring a private driver. The roads in the region can be challenging, so it’s essential to drive carefully and minimize night travel due to limited road lighting in some areas
