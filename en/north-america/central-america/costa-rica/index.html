Introduction to Costa Rica

Costa Rica is a small country located in Central America, bordering Nicaragua to the north and Panama to the south. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Costa Rica receives millions of tourists each year looking to experience the country’s lush forests, pristine beaches, and abundant wildlife.

But Costa Rica is much more than just a paradise for eco-tourists. It is also a country with a rich culture and history, where visitors can explore museums, ancient ruins, and sample delicious local dishes.

Planning a trip to Costa Rica? Read on to discover the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and important safety and transportation tips.

Local Culture and Customs of Costa Rica

Costa Rica has a laid-back and friendly culture, with a strong emphasis on family and community. Punctuality is not a priority, and it is common for events to start late or be rescheduled.

Ticos, as Costa Ricans are called, take pride in their country’s natural beauty and work to protect it. Visitors should respect the local customs and be mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Costa Rica

The best time to visit Costa Rica is during the dry season, from December to April. During this time, there is little rain, and the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so expect crowds.

The rainy season, from May to November, can be a good time to visit for those looking to save money and avoid the crowds. But be prepared for occasional downpours and muddy trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Costa Rica

Overall, Costa Rica is a safe destination for travelers. However, like any destination, visitors should take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables in a safe place and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Costa Rica

Visitors to Costa Rica should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and Zika virus. It is also important to drink only bottled water and be mindful of food safety.

How to Get Around in Costa Rica

Visitors to Costa Rica can get around by rental car, private shuttle, public bus, or taxi. Note that road conditions vary widely, with some roads unpaved and difficult to navigate.
