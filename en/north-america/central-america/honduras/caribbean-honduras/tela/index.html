Introduction to Tela

Tela is a coastal town located in the Atlantida department of Honduras, in Central America. Known for its long sandy beaches, lush green forests, and outstanding natural beauty, Tela is a hidden gem that has something for everyone, from adventurous travelers to lazy beachgoers.

List of Places to Visit in Tela

When visiting Tela, there are several must-visit places and activities to consider:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lancetilla Botanical Garden: A must-visit spot for nature lovers, Lancetilla Botanical Garden is one of the largest botanical gardens in Central America, with over 4,000 species of plants.
  • Tela Railroad Station: An old structure that dates back to the early 1900s, the Tela Railroad Station is an iconic landmark in Tela. It once played a crucial role in Tela’s economy as a transportation hub, connecting the town with other regions of the country.
  • Tela’s Garifuna Museum: A small museum that provides a glimpse into the unique culture and history of the Garifuna people, a group with African and indigenous roots.

Natural Attractions of Tela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Punta Sal National Park: Located at the tip of a rocky peninsula, Punta Sal National Park is a perfect spot for hiking and enjoying stunning views of the coast.
  • Cacao Lagoon: Surrounded by mangroves and tropical vegetation, Cacao Lagoon is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Jeanette Kawas National Park: A protected natural area that is home to over 400 species of birds, Jeanette Kawas National Park offers visitors opportunities for swimming, hiking, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tela

Tela is a paradise for hikers and bikers looking to explore the region’s scenic trails and untouched nature. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Lancetilla Trail: A 6-mile trail that takes you through the lush rainforest of Lancetilla Botanical Garden.
  • The Punta Izopo Wildlife Refuge Trail: A 2-mile trail that winds through the dense mangrove forest of Punta Izopo Wildlife Refuge, offering stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.
  • The Cusuco National Park Trail: A challenging 6-mile trail that leads to the highest peak in Cusuco National Park and rewards you with spectacular views of the surrounding valleys and rainforests.

Best Things to Do in Tela: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: With consistent waves and uncrowded beaches, Tela is a great destination for surfers of all skill levels. Some of the best surfing spots include Playa El Venado and Punta Sal.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Experience the underwater world of the Caribbean Sea by scuba diving or snorkeling in Tela’s crystal-clear waters. Several dive shops offer tours and certification courses.
  • Sport Fishing: Tela’s coastal waters are teeming with big game fish, making it a popular destination for sport fishing enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tela

Day 1: Visit Lancetilla Botanical Garden and Tela Railroad Station Day 2: Explore Punta Sal National Park and relax on Punta Sal Beach Day 3: Go birdwatching and kayaking in Cacao Lagoon Day 4: Join a scuba diving or snorkeling tour Day 5: Hike the Punta Izopo Wildlife Refuge Trail and enjoy a beach picnic Day 6: Take a day trip to Cusuco National Park and go on a canopy tour Day 7: Relax on the beach and indulge in Tela’s delicious seafood

Food and Drink Scene of Tela

Tela’s cuisine is a blend of Spanish, Garifuna, and indigenous influences, with an emphasis on fresh seafood and tropical fruits. Don’t miss these must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tapado: A flavorful seafood soup made with coconut milk, plantains, and cassava.
  • Baleada: A popular snack made with a thick flour tortilla, refried beans, cheese, and avocado.
  • Sopa de Caracol: A hearty conch soup.

Local Beverages

  • Honduran Coffee: Honduras is known for producing some of the best coffee in the world, so don’t miss the chance to taste a cup of freshly brewed coffee from Tela.
  • Guifiti: A potent herbal drink made with rum, honey, and a blend of herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Tela: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tela offers plenty of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Garífuna Hostel
  • Grissy’s Hotel
  • Indura Beach and Golf Resort

What to Buy in Tela: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Beaded Jewelry: The Garifuna people are known for their intricate beaded jewelry, which makes for a unique and meaningful souvenir.
  • Cigars: Honduras is home to some of the finest tobacco in the world, so consider buying a box of hand-rolled cigars as a gift for yourself or a loved one.

Local Culture and Customs of Tela

The Garifuna culture is an integral part of Tela’s history and identity. They are known for their lively music and dance, colorful clothing, and delicious cuisine. When visiting Tela, it’s essential to be respectful of the local customs and learn about their culture and history.

Best Time to Visit Tela

The best time to visit Tela is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. During this time, the weather is sunny and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and beach days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tela

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine, including seafood and Garifuna specialties
  • Take a guided tour of the natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Purchase products made from endangered species, such as sea turtle shells

Safety and Health

Safety in Tela

Tela is a relatively safe town, but it’s always advisable to take precautions when traveling. Always keep an eye on your belongings, avoid walking alone at night, and be vigilant of your surroundings.

Health in Tela

It’s essential to take necessary precautions to avoid health issues when visiting Tela, such as drinking bottled water, wearing insect repellent, and taking malaria medication if necessary.

Getting to Tela: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Tela is relatively easy, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Tela

  • Ramón Villeda Morales International Airport (SAP): Located in San Pedro Sula, approximately 1.5 hours from Tela.

Bus Stations Near Tela

  • San Pedro Sula Bus Terminal: Offers frequent bus services to Tela.

Other Options

  • Private Shuttle: Several private shuttle companies offer transportation services to Tela from San Pedro Sula.

How to Get Around in Tela

Tela is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a bike, car, or motorcycle is a great option.

Where to Go Next from Tela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tela is an excellent starting point for exploring other nearby destinations in Honduras, such as:

  • Roatán Island: A Caribbean paradise known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and vibrant nightlife.
  • La Ceiba: A lively coastal city located just an hour away from Tela, known for its rich history, authentic cuisine, and diverse culture.
  • Copán Ruins: A UNESCO World Heritage site located in Western Honduras, famous for its Maya ruins and intricate hieroglyphic carvings.

Conclusion

Tela is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, and relaxation. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality, Tela is a perfect destination for all types of travelers.
