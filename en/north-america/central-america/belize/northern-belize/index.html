Introduction to Northern Belize

Northern Belize offers travelers a chance to experience a unique blend of culture and nature. Home to ancient Maya ruins, stunning beaches, bustling cities and wildlife reserves, this region has something to offer every type of traveler. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, and must-visit destinations in Northern Belize.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Belize

If you’re planning a trip to Northern Belize, there’s no shortage of places to visit. Here are our top picks for must-see destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lamanai Archaeological Reserve: This is one of the largest and most impressive Maya ruins in Northern Belize. Explore the ancient temples, plazas, and ball courts in this beautifully preserved site.
  • Altun Ha: Known for its iconic temples and striking architecture, Altun Ha is another must-visit site for history buffs.
  • Museum of Belize: Located in the vibrant city of Belize City, the Museum of Belize provides a fascinating insight into the country’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Northern Belize: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hol Chan Marine Reserve: Experience the beauty of Belize’s marine life with a snorkeling or diving excursion at this world-renowned marine reserve.
  • Rio Grande River: Take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear waters of this scenic river, surrounded by lush vegetation.
  • Jaguar Nature Reserve: Home to a wide variety of exotic animals, including jaguars, monkeys and ocelots, Jaguar Nature Reserve is a must-visit destination for wildlife lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Belize

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will find plenty of trails to explore in Northern Belize. Here are our top picks for scenic routes:

  • Bacab Eco-Park: This popular nature preserve offers hiking and biking trails, along with zip lining, swimming and canoeing opportunities.
  • Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary: Known as the “Jaguar Reserve,” this park boasts over 128,000 acres of pristine rainforest, perfect for hiking and bird watching.
  • Mountain Pine Ridge Forest Reserve: This vast reserve is a paradise for hikers, featuring dramatic waterfalls, breathtaking vistas, and diverse wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Northern Belize: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Northern Belize offers plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Belize Zoo: This is a must-visit destination for animal lovers, featuring a vast collection of over 150 native Belizean animals, including jaguars, tapirs, and monkeys.
  • Swimming with Sharks: Experience the thrill of a lifetime with a cage-diving excursion to swim with the majestic nurse sharks at Shark Ray Alley.
  • Cave Tubing: This popular activity takes you on an underground adventure through a series of dark and mysterious caves, full of stalactites, stalagmites and ancient artifacts.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Belize

Northern Belize offers a wide variety of mouth-watering cuisine and refreshing local beverages. Here are some of our top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Jacks: These fluffy, deep-fried pockets of dough are a staple in Belizean cuisine, often served with eggs and refried beans for breakfast.
  • Sere: This hearty seafood soup is a Belizean classic, made with fresh fish, plantains, and coconut milk.
  • Rice and Beans: A staple in Belizean cuisine, this savory dish is made with red beans, coconut milk, and traditional Belizean spices.

Local Beverages

  • Belikin Beer: The national beer of Belize, Belikin is a refreshing lager that pairs perfectly with Belizean cuisine.
  • Cashew Wine: Made with locally grown cashew fruit, this sweet and tangy wine is a unique Belizean specialty.
  • Panty Ripper: A popular tropical cocktail, made with coconut rum and pineapple juice, that will transport you to the beaches of Northern Belize.

Where to Stay in Northern Belize: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Northern Belize has something to fit every traveler’s needs. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Las Terrazas Resort: This luxurious oceanfront resort offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea, along with an array of amenities, including a spa, restaurant, and infinity pool.
  • Midas Resort: This budget-friendly resort offers comfortable accommodations, a swimming pool, and easy access to hiking and biking trails in the Mountain Pine Ridge Forest Reserve.
  • The Funky Dodo Backpackers Hostel: A popular hostel in the vibrant city of San Ignacio, offering a range of dormitory and private rooms, along with a lively social atmosphere.

What to Buy in Northern Belize: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Northern Belize, here are some of our top picks:

  • Marie Sharp’s Hot Sauce: This locally produced hot sauce is a staple in Belizean cuisine, featuring a unique blend of habanero peppers, carrots, and lime juice.
  • Belizean Chocolate: Made with locally grown cocoa beans, Belizean chocolate is a must-try for chocolate lovers.
  • Handmade Garifuna Drums: These beautifully crafted drums are made by hand in traditional Garifuna style, and make a unique and authentic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Belize

Northern Belize is known for its rich cultural heritage, including the traditional music and dance of the Garifuna people, colorful Creole festivals, and vibrant street art. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting cultural sites and places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Northern Belize

The best time to visit Northern Belize is from November to April, when the weather is dry and comfortable. However, travelers should be aware that this is peak season, and accommodation prices may be higher. May and October are also good months to visit, when the weather is still dry, but with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Belize

Do:

  • Do visit the ancient Maya ruins and cultural landmarks of Northern Belize.
  • Do explore the natural wonders, including the beaches, wildlife reserves, and hiking trails.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including the traditional dishes and local beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to apply sunscreen and insect repellent, as Northern Belize can get hot and buggy.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural sites or places of worship.
  • Don’t touch or disturb the wildlife in the nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Belize

As with any travel destination, visitors to Northern Belize should be aware of their surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions. Travelers should avoid unlit areas and keep valuable belongings locked up in a hotel safe. Additionally, visitors should be aware of potentially dangerous wildlife, such as crocodiles and snakes, and take necessary precautions when hiking or exploring nature reserves.

Health in Northern Belize

Visitors to Northern Belize should ensure that all routine vaccinations are up to date before traveling. Additionally, travelers should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and Zika virus, by wearing insect repellent and protective clothing. Visitors should also avoid drinking tap water and only consume bottled or filtered water.

Getting to Northern Belize: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Belize

Northern Belize is served by several airports, including:

  • Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport in Belize City
  • Corozal Airport in Corozal
  • San Pedro Airport in Ambergris Caye

Bus Stations Near Northern Belize

Northern Belize is also accessible by bus. Major bus stations include:

  • Belize City Bus Terminal
  • San Ignacio Bus Terminal
  • Corozal Bus Terminal

Other Options

Additionally, visitors to Northern Belize can rent a car or hire a private driver for more flexibility and convenience.

How to Get Around in Northern Belize

While public transport is available in Northern Belize, visitors may want to consider renting a car or hiring a private driver for more flexibility and convenience. Taxis are also available, but visitors should agree on a fare in advance.

Where to Go Next from Northern Belize: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Belize, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Caye Caulker: This laid-back island is a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling, along with beautiful beaches and delicious seafood.
  • Placencia: Known for its pristine beaches and world-class seafood, Placencia is a charming coastal town that’s perfect for relaxation and adventure.
  • Tikal National Park: Located just across the border in Guatemala, Tikal National Park is home to some of the most impressive Maya ruins in Central America.

Conclusion

Northern Belize is a unique and charismatic destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural wonders and cultural heritage of this enchanting region. From ancient Maya ruins to idyllic beaches, lush rainforests to bustling cities, Northern Belize has something to offer every type of traveler. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to start planning your own adventure to Northern Belize.
