Introduction to Lamanai

Lamanai is a Mayan archaeological site located in Northern Belize, Central America. The name Lamanai means “submerged crocodile” in the Mayan language and refers to the many crocodiles that reside in the New River Lagoon, where Lamanai is located. The site was inhabited continuously for over 3,000 years and is home to several impressive structures, including the High Temple, Jaguar Temple, and Mask Temple.

List of Places to Visit in Lamanai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lamanai Museum: A museum showcasing artifacts excavated from the site, including pottery, jewelry, and weapons.
  • Jaguar Temple: An impressive temple structure featuring a jaguar headdress on the top.
  • High Temple: The tallest structure at Lamanai, with stunning views from the top.
  • Stela 9: A carved stone structure depicting a ruler from ancient times.
  • Mask Temple: A structure featuring a large mask carved into the stone.

Natural Attractions of Lamanai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • New River Lagoon: Take a boat tour of this beautiful lagoon and spot crocodiles, manatees, and other wildlife.
  • Lamanai Eco Tours: Offering guided tours of the site and surrounding jungle.
  • Jungle Hikes: Explore the jungle surrounding Lamanai on foot with a guided tour.
  • Sunset from the High Temple: Watch the sunset over the jungle from the top of the High Temple for a breathtaking view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lamanai

  • Jungle Trail: A 3-mile hike through the rainforest, featuring a diverse array of flora and fauna.
  • Lamanai Archaeological Trail: A 1.5-mile hike through the site, passing by many of the key structures.
  • Mountain Bike Trail: A guided mountain bike tour through the surrounding jungle and nearby villages.

Best Things to Do in Lamanai: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of the New River Lagoon: Observe wildlife, such as manatees and crocodiles, along the beautiful river.
  • Sunrise or Sunset Tour: Catch the sun rising or setting over Lamanai’s beautiful scenery.
  • Jungle Hike: Explore the jungle around Lamanai with a guided tour.
  • Visit the Archaeological Site: Discover the history of Lamanai by exploring the impressive structures and excavations.

Food and Drink Scene of Lamanai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rice and Beans: A staple in Belizean cuisine, featuring rice and beans cooked in coconut milk.
  • Fry Jack: A fried dough dish often eaten for breakfast, accompanied by beans or meat.
  • Stewed Chicken: Chicken stewed in a flavorful sauce with vegetables and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Belikin Beer: A popular Belizean beer, available in several different varieties.
  • Cashew Wine: Made from cashew fruit, this sweet wine is a popular local drink.
  • Rum Punch: Made with rum, fruit juice, and spices, this local cocktail is a refreshing treat.

Where to Stay in Lamanai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lamanai Outpost Lodge: A beautiful, eco-friendly lodge located in the heart of the jungle.
  • Lamanai Hotel and Marina: A waterfront hotel offering stunning views and easy access to the New River Lagoon.
  • The Las Cuevas Research Station: A research station located within the nearby Chiquibul National Park, offering basic and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Lamanai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Purchase locally-made crafts, such as woven baskets, pottery, and wood carvings, as souvenirs.
  • Belizean Coffee: Take home a bag of locally-grown coffee.
  • Chocolate: Belize is known for its high-quality cacao, and many chocolate makers offer tours and tastings.

Local Culture and Customs of Lamanai

The Mayan people have inhabited the area surrounding Lamanai for over 3,000 years and have a rich culture and history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as not touching or climbing on any of the ancient structures and dressing modestly when visiting villages.

Best Time to Visit Lamanai

The best time to visit Lamanai is during the dry season, from December to April. The weather is cooler, and wildlife is more active. However, the rainy season, from May to November, can still offer fantastic opportunities to explore the lush jungle surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lamanai

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Bring insect repellent to combat mosquitoes and other insects.
  • Stay hydrated in the tropical climate.

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on any of the ancient structures.
  • Leave trash behind in the jungle or on the site.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting local villages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lamanai

Although Lamanai is a safe place to visit, visitors should still take the necessary precautions to stay safe. This includes taking a guided tour of the site and surrounding jungle and avoiding leaving the designated paths.

Health in Lamanai

It is recommended that visitors to Lamanai get vaccinated against common tropical diseases such as yellow fever and hepatitis A. Visitors should also bring enough insect repellent and sunscreen to stay protected from the sun and bugs.

Getting to Lamanai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lamanai

The closest airport to Lamanai is Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport in Belize City. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight to either Orange Walk or Corozal before taking a taxi or shuttle to Lamanai.

Bus Stations Near Lamanai

Visitors can take a bus from Belize City to Orange Walk or Corozal and then hire a taxi or take a shuttle to Lamanai.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a private driver is also an option for getting to Lamanai. However, visitors should be aware that roads in Belize can be rough and difficult to navigate.

How to Get Around in Lamanai

Guided tours are the best way to get around Lamanai, as visitors are not allowed to leave designated paths or touch any of the ancient structures.

Where to Go Next from Lamanai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Altun Ha: Another Mayan archaeological site located in Belize.
  • Ambergris Caye: A beautiful island off the coast of Belize, offering white sand beaches and clear blue waters.
  • Tikal: A Mayan site located just over the border in Guatemala.

Conclusion

Lamanai is a fascinating place to visit for anyone interested in history, culture, or nature. With its impressive archaeological site, beautiful natural surroundings, and friendly local culture, Lamanai is a must-visit destination in Belize. By following the tips and recommendations in this guide, visitors can experience the best that Lamanai has to offer while staying safe and healthy.
