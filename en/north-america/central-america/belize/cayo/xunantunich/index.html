Xunantunich Travel Guide: Explore the Mayan Ruins in Belize

Located in Cayo, Belize, Xunantunich is an ancient Mayan city dotted with impressive ruins that hint at a glorious past. Famed for its pyramids, plazas, ballcourts, and carvings, Xunantunich has managed to remain an essential tourist attraction in Central America. This comprehensive travel guide takes you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, food, culture, safety, and transportation options in Xunantunich.

Introduction to Xunantunich

Xunantunich is a Mayan archaeological site that dates back to the classical period, somewhere between 200 and 900 AD. The site features a central plaza surrounded by several plazas, ten main structures, a ballcourt, and several smaller buildings.

The name Xunantunich means “Stone Woman” in Mayan language. The site’s most impressive structures include El Castillo, a 130-foot pyramid with excellent views of the surrounding jungle, and the Stelaes, which are carvings that tell the story of Xunantunich’s past.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xunantunich

Xunantunich is a hub of hiking and biking trails, and adventurers can explore the surrounding jungle by following the miles of well-marked trails. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Ceremonial Plaza to El Castillo trail
  • The Stelae trail
  • The Tapir Mountain Reserve trail
  • The Green Hills Butterfly Ranch trail

Best Things to Do in Xunantunich: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Xunantunich include:

  • Exploring the ancient Mayan ruins
  • Learning about the Mayan Culture at the Museum of Xunantunich
  • Bird-watching and butterfly spotting at the Green Hills Butterfly Ranch
  • Taking a dip in the Macal River
  • Hiking and biking through the jungle

Week-Long Itinerary for Xunantunich

Day 1: Arrive in Belize City, transfer to Cayo, and check into your accommodations.

Day 2: Visit the Xunantunich ruins and hike/bike the Ceremonial Plaza to El Castillo Trail.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the Tapir Mountain Reserve and the Green Hills Butterfly Ranch.

Day 4: Enjoy a day of relaxation and some local food and drink at one of the best restaurants in San Ignacio.

Day 5: Explore the Iguana Project and learn about endangered iguanas in Belize.

Day 6: Take a guided bird-watching tour through Xunantunich’s rainforest.

Day 7: Spend your last day kayaking the Macal River and enjoy the beauty of the Belizean jungle.

Local Culture and Customs of Xunantunich

Mayan culture is an essential part of Xunantunich’s attraction, and visitors can witness ancient customs and traditions during their stay. The Mayan culture is rich with art, music, dance, and oral traditions.

Best Time to Visit Xunantunich

Belize has a tropical climate, which means that the weather is warm and humid year-round. The best time to visit Xunantunich is during the dry season, which runs from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xunantunich

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xunantunich

Overall, Xunantunich is relatively safe for visitors. However, tourists should take the same precautions they would in any unfamiliar place, such as locking doors and windows and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Xunantunich

Belize is generally safe concerning health concerns, but visitors should make sure they have proper vaccinations and take adequate precautions against insect bites and mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Xunantunich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xunantunich

Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport in Belize City is the closest airport to Xunantunich. From there, visitors can take a taxi, shuttle, or rent a car to reach Cayo, where Xunantunich is located.

Bus Stations Near Xunantunich

Bonito Buses and James Bus Line are the two main bus operators that run between Belize City and San Ignacio, which is the largest town near Xunantunich.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are both available in Belize City and San Ignacio, providing visitors with many options to reach Xunantunich and navigate the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Xunantunich

The best way to get around Xunantunich is by hiking or biking. Tour operators also offer guided hikes as well as bike rentals.
