Introduction to Belize City

Belize City, the bustling commercial and cultural hub of the tiny Central American nation of Belize, is a melting pot of different cultures, from the Garinagu and Creoles to the Mestizos, Maya, and expats from all over the world. Despite its small size, Belize City offers a plethora of activities and sights, from visiting historical and cultural landmarks to exploring the great outdoors and indulging in local cuisine and drinks.

Local Culture and Customs of Belize City

Belize City is a culturally diverse place, and respecting these various customs will make your trip more pleasant. These include:

  • Being respectful of the language spoken by locals.
  • Greeting people with an acknowledgement like a hug, handshake, or verbal hello, depending on how well you know the person.
  • Dressing appropriately for the occasion.
  • Taking off your shoes when entering a home or place of worship.
  • Using your right hand for gestures and eating.

Best Time to Visit Belize City

The best time to visit Belize City is from late November to mid-April, during the country’s dry season when the weather is warm but not too humid, and the beaches and outdoor attractions are at their most inviting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belize City

Do:

  • Be self-aware and respectful of your surroundings.
  • Engage with locals; they are friendly and open to conversations.
  • Take part in cultural activities and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Put your valuables on display.
  • Wander around the city after dark.
  • Litter or damage Belize City’s natural and cultural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belize City

Although Belize City is generally safe to travel to, travelers should be aware of their surroundings at all times. Some parts of the city, especially at night, can be dangerous. Travelers should avoid areas such as the south and west of Belize City, which have high crime rates.

Health in Belize City

Travelers to Belize City should be up to date on routine vaccinations and Hepatitis A and Typhoid vaccines. They should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like Zika, Chikungunya, and Dengue.
