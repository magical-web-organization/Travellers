A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Ambergris Caye

Ambergris Caye is a dream destination for those seeking an idyllic beach getaway with a generous dose of adventure and culture. Situated in the Belize District, North America, Ambergris Caye is the largest island off the coast of Belize and boasts a diverse range of natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and thrilling activities that cater to every taste and budget. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and practical travel tips to help you plan a hassle-free and memorable trip to Ambergris Caye.

List of Places to Visit in Ambergris Caye

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Belizean Melody Art Gallery: A charming gallery that showcases the works of local artists and craftsmen.
  • Belize Chocolate Company: Take a tour of this cocoa farm and learn about the history of chocolate making in Belize, plus indulge in some delicious chocolate samples.
  • Marco Gonzalez Maya Site: Visit this ancient Maya site to explore historic ruins and gain insight into the rich Mayan history and culture of Belize.

Natural Attractions of Ambergris Caye: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hol Chan Marine Reserve and Shark Ray Alley: Dive or snorkel in the crystal-clear waters to see diverse marine life, including rays and colorful tropical fish.
  • Secret Beach: This secluded beach provides a peaceful escape from the bustling town center with white sand and crystal-clear water, perfect for swimming or kayaking.
  • Ambergris Caye Barrier Reef: Explore this breathtaking natural wonder, the second-largest barrier reef in the world, with a local guide while enjoying snorkeling and diving opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ambergris Caye

  • Blue Hole Trail: This thrilling trail is perfect for experienced hikers and adventure enthusiasts and provides a picturesque view of the natural beauty of Ambergris Caye.
  • Boca Del Rio Park: A peaceful park located in the heart of San Pedro town that offers bike rental services and various trails to explore the island’s beauty.

Best Things to Do in Ambergris Caye: Activities and Tours

  • Sailing Adventures: Rent a private charted yacht and explore the stunning coastline while enjoying locally-brewed beverages and tasty meals.
  • Cave Tubing: Experience an extraordinary journey and glide by waterway, through underground caves filled with crystal clear water and natural formations.
  • Skydiving: Enjoy breathtaking panoramas while diving above the beautiful island of Ambergris Caye for an unforgettable adrenaline rush.

Food and Drink Scene of Ambergris Caye

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pibil: A signature dish of the Yucatán, made with barbecued pork or chicken marinated in achiote paste and wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Ceviche: This refreshing seafood dish is prepared with different types of fish, shrimp, or lobster, marinated in citrus juice and typically served with onions, cucumbers, and avocado.
  • Jerk Chicken: Jamaican-inspired chicken dish with a spicy marinade served with Caribbean-style rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Rum Punch: Prepared with local rum, fruit juice, and simple syrup, rum punch is a classic Caribbean cocktail and a common drink in Ambergris Caye.
  • Belikin Beer: Belize’s national beer, Belikin comes in various styles and is known for its refreshing taste.
  • Coconut Water: This refreshing drink is very popular in Ambergris Caye and is served straight out of freshly cut green coconuts.

Where to Stay in Ambergris Caye: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Victoria House Resort & Spa: A luxurious resort located on a stunning beach, known for its excellent amenities and top-notch service.
  • Pedro’s Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable accommodations and a lively social atmosphere.
  • Cocotal Inn & Cabanas: A charming guesthouse that provides comfortable and affordable accommodations with access to a stunning beach.

What to Buy in Ambergris Caye: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Belizean Rum: Bring back a bottle of this delicious local rum to enjoy with family and friends.
  • Belizean Hot Sauce: The perfect souvenir for hot sauce enthusiasts, Belizean hot sauce has a unique and spicy flavor that adds a kick to any dish.
  • Handmade Crafts and Artwork: Visit one of the local art galleries or markets to purchase authentic handmade crafts and artisanal artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Ambergris Caye

The locals in Ambergris Caye are friendly and welcoming, and the island’s culture is heavily influenced by Spanish, British, and Mayan traditions. It’s important to dress respectfully to show consideration for local customs, and it is customary to tip around 10-15% for good service at restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit Ambergris Caye

The best time to visit Ambergris Caye is from December to May, when temperatures are pleasant and rainfall is minimal. The peak tourist season is from December to March, so expect higher prices during this time. June to November is hurricane season, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ambergris Caye

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress appropriately.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

Don’t:

  • Litter the island and beaches.
  • Step on or touch the coral reefs while snorkeling or diving.
  • Participate in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ambergris Caye

Ambergris Caye is generally a safe destination. However, tourists should exercise caution, especially at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Ambergris Caye

Tourists should take precautions against mosquito bites, and it is recommended to get a hepatitis A and B vaccine before traveling to Ambergris Caye. Medical facilities are available on the island, but it is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Ambergris Caye: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ambergris Caye

The main international airport in Belize City is the Philip S.W. Goldson International Airport. It is possible to take a short 15-minute flight to Ambergris Caye from here.

Bus Stations Near Ambergris Caye

There are no buses on Ambergris Caye, but there are many water taxis that run frequently and can take you to different parts of the island.

Other Options

Another way to get to Ambergris Caye is by taking a ferry or water taxi from Belize City. It’s a scenic 90-minute boat ride from Belize City to Ambergris Caye.

How to Get Around in Ambergris Caye

The island is small and can easily be explored by foot, bike or golf carts. Golf carts are the most popular mode of transportation for tourists on the island and can be rented from various vendors.

Where to Go Next from Ambergris Caye: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Belize City: A bustling and historic city with plenty of local culture, restaurants, and museums.
  • Caye Caulker: A smaller and quieter island nearby that is known for its diving and snorkeling opportunities.
  • Mayan Ruins: Explore the ancient Mayan ruins located near the borders of Belize, El Salvador, and Guatemala.

Conclusion

Ambergris Caye is a beautiful and unique destination that offers an abundance of natural beauty, outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and delicious food and drink. By following the tips and recommendations in this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan an unforgettable trip to this island paradise.
