Introduction to Altun Ha

Hidden away in the lush green forests of Belize are the ancient Mayan ruins of Altun Ha. This site was once a thriving trading center and is home to some of the most iconic structures in the region. Visitors can explore the rich history of this fascinating site, hike or bike through its scenic trails, and experience the vibrant local culture of Belize.

List of Places to Visit in Altun Ha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors can learn about Altun Ha’s rich cultural heritage at the on-site Museum of Altun Ha. The museum contains a collection of artifacts, ceramics, and tools that were once used by the Mayan people. The most famous of Altun Ha’s temple structures is the Temple of the Masonry Altars, which was used for sacrifices and as a religious center.

Natural Attractions of Altun Ha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The highlight of any visit to Altun Ha is the chance to explore the beautiful surrounding natural scenery. Visitors can take a nature walk through the adjacent forest reserve and spot an array of tropical birds, butterflies, and animals. Adventure enthusiasts can go zip-lining or horseback riding through the lush jungle, or take a scenic boat tour down the nearby river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Altun Ha

The nearby forest reserve surrounding Altun Ha provides an extensive network of hiking and biking trails. A popular trail option includes a hike or bike ride through the forest to the nearby Crooked Tree Wildlife Sanctuary. Here, visitors can observe over 300 species of exotic birds, animals, and reptiles in their natural habitat.

Best Things to Do in Altun Ha: Activities and Tours

Visitors can explore the ancient Mayan ruins by joining a guided tour. Guides typically provide in-depth historical and cultural information about Altun Ha and the Mayan people. Additionally, visitors can indulge in local foods, shop for souvenirs, and take boat tours to nearby attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Altun Ha

Day 1-2: Explore Altun Ha ruins and Museum of Altun Ha.

Day 3: Take a day tour to nearby Crooked Tree Wildlife Sanctuary.

Day 4-5: Go on a cultural food and drink tour, visit the nearby village community, and shop for locally made products.

Day 6-7: Take a day trip to the nearby Belize City and experience its vibrant nightlife and cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Altun Ha

Must-Try Dishes

Belizean cuisine is a blend of African, Spanish, and Mayan influences. Visitors must try the traditional stew chicken dish, which is served with rice and beans, as well as the fried plantains, conch fritters, and the fresh seafood platter.

Local Beverages

Belize’s local drink is the Belikin beer, a refreshing pale ale, but visitors can also try the locally made rum punch or, for those seeking a caffeine kick, Belizean coffee.

Where to Stay in Altun Ha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options available for visitors in Altun Ha. Options range from luxurious hotels and resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses or hostels. Some recommended options include The Black Orchid Resort, Radisson Fort George Hotel, and The Howler Monkey Lodge.

What to Buy in Altun Ha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors will have ample opportunity to shop for souvenirs and locally made products in Altun Ha. Visitors can purchase handmade crafts, traditional artwork, and indigenous products, including locally made jewelry, woven baskets, pottery, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Altun Ha

Belize is a melting pot of different cultures and customs, including Mayan, Creole, and Spanish. As such, visitors should respect the diverse beliefs and customs of the local people, always ask before taking photos, and avoid touching any ancient artifacts or structures.

Best Time to Visit Altun Ha

The dry season, from November to May, is the best time to visit Altun Ha, as weather conditions are optimal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Altun Ha

Do:

  • Dress appropriately, with comfortable and lightweight clothing.
  • Plan for plenty of sun protection, Mosquito repellant, and sufficient hydration.
  • Try as much local cuisine and drinks as possible.
  • Hire a local guide to enhance the visit.
  • Embrace and respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Do not touch or climb on the temple structures and ancient artifacts.
  • Do not buy or consume illegal drugs.
  • Do not excessively consume alcohol, or leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Altun Ha

Belize is generally safe for tourists, but visitors are encouraged to take precautions such as avoiding secluded areas, securing valuables, and traveling in groups. Altun Ha, in particular, is a safe and serene area, but it’s still recommended to hire local guides for the best experience.

Health in Altun Ha

Visitors should be up to date with their routine vaccinations before traveling, and may also need vaccinations for diseases like Hepatitis A and Typhoid. As Altun Ha is located in a mosquito-prone area, visitors should take preventative measures including wearing protective clothing, using mosquito nets, and using insect repellent.

Getting to Altun Ha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Altun Ha

The Philip Goldson International Airport in Belize City is the closest airport to Altun Ha. From there it’s a 30-minute drive to Altun Ha.

Bus Stations Near Altun Ha

There are regular bus services from Belize City, Orange Walk, and another municipality to Altun Ha.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to travel by water taxi, car rental, private shuttle, or taxi.

How to Get Around in Altun Ha

Visitors can hire a local guide or rent bicycles, ATVs, and golf carts to navigate around the small area.

Where to Go Next from Altun Ha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to explore the nearby cities and towns of San Ignacio, Ambergris Caye, or Placencia. Nearby excursions also include Cave Tubing and Zip-lining.

Conclusion

Altun Ha offers a complete package of historical sites, natural beauty, and cultural richness. Visitors can immerse themselves in vibrant local culture, explore the ancient Maya ruins, and indulge in local delicacies. With comprehensive planning, visitors can ensure they make the most of their time in Altun Ha to experience it all.
