Introduction to Five Blues Lake National Park

Five Blues Lake National Park is one of the hidden gem attractions in the Stann Creek District of Belize, Central America. The park is a unique site that offers tourist exquisite natural wonders and cultural experiences. Despite its natural beauty and historical significance, the site has retained its pristine conditions, offering a quiet and peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. The park is truly a paradise that will take you back in time and make your Central American voyage even more unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Five Blues Lake National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The national park boasts of several cultural attractions that have been maintained through the ages. Some of the must-visit destinations include:

  • The Maya Ruins: Explore the historical significance of the park through this landmark. It represents the vibrant culture of the Mayan civilization.
  • The Five Blues Lake: This is a unique attraction that got its name from the varying shades of blue water that occur naturally. It is believed to be a spiritual site for the Mayans, and has cultural significance.
  • The Waterfall: This breathtaking natural feature of the national park is a wonder that you cannot miss while you are there.
  • The Visitor’s Center: For a comprehensive understanding of the national park, a visit to the visitor’s center is a must. Here, you will get to know the history of the national park, its geological features, and its legends.

Natural Attractions of Five Blues Lake National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Five Blues Lake National Park boasts of a variety of natural attractions that offer excellent opportunities to witness nature’s wonders. Some of the natural attractions that will make your trip more fulfilling include:

  • A hike around the Five Blues Lake: Enjoy a scenic nature hike around this natural wonder and marvel at its beauty. You won’t miss out on different colors of blue that form the lake.
  • Snorkelling and swimming at the waterfall: Cool off at the waterfall by taking a plunge into the calm blue pools and indulge in snorkeling activities to explore the rich marine life of the region.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: While at the park, indulge in a canoe or kayak expedition, which is one of the most popular activities at Five Blues Lake. The boats are available on hire, and this is a perfect activity for adventure lovers or team bonding and skill development exercises.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Five Blues Lake National Park

For the hiking and biking enthusiasts, expect to have a moment of a lifetime as Five Blues Lake National Park presents a unique opportunity to blend adventure with nature. When on any of the following trails, listen keenly to the chirping of birds, enjoy the solitude, observe the natural environment and religiously follow the signs posted along the trails. These hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Ruin Trail - 2.5 miles
  • The Waterfall Trail - 1 mile
  • The Howler Monkey Trail - 1.5 miles
  • The Shortest Trail - 1.5 miles
  • The Longest Trail - 3.5 miles
  • The Horseback Riding Trail - 3 miles

Food and Drink Scene of Five Blues Lake National Park

Must-Try Dishes

A visit to Belize is never complete without savoring the local delicacies. While in this national park, be sure to try out:

  • Rice and Beans, the traditional Belizean dish accompanied by stewed chicken
  • Soursop juice, a refreshing drink made from the fruits of the Soursop tree
  • Fry Jacks, A breakfast dish that resembles a pancake
  • Conch Fritters, A seafood specialty that is a must-try while in Belize

Local Beverages

  • Belikin Beer, Belize’s most famous beer
  • One Barrel Rum, a Belizean rum distilled from sugarcane molasses
  • Cashew wine, a local alcoholic drink made from cashew fruits

Best Time to Visit Five Blues Lake National Park

The best time to visit Five Blues Lake National Park is between the months of December to May when the weather is usually dry and pleasant.

Safety and Health

Safety in Five Blues Lake National Park

Staying safe in Five Blues Lake National Park starts with taking personal responsibility for your safety. It would help to follow the park rules and guidelines, remain vigilant about your belongings, and be cautious when interacting with wildlife.

Health in Five Blues Lake National Park

While in Belize, travelers should be aware of diseases that are endemic to the country. These include dengue, malaria, and Zika virus, which are mosquito-borne illnesses. Travelers are advised to use mosquito repellents, wear long sleeve shirts, and pants.

Getting to Five Blues Lake National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Five Blues Lake National Park

Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport is the main airport that serves Belize City and is the closest airport to Five Blues Lake National Park (approximately 103 km or 64 miles).

Bus Stations Near Five Blues Lake National Park

The nearest Bus Station is in Dangriga Town, about 20 miles or 30 km from the park.

Other Options

  • Rent a car from the Belize City Airport or Dangriga Town to get to the national park
  • Hire a taxi from the airport or bus station to get to the national park

How to Get Around in Five Blues Lake National Park

The best ways to get around Five Blues Lake National Park include hiking, biking, and walking. Visitors can also rent canoes and kayaks on-site. Guided tours are also available.

Where to Go Next from Five Blues Lake National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The following are some of the recommended destinations for those departing from Five Blues Lake National Park:

  • The Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary & Jaguar Preserve
  • Hopkins Village, a quaint coastal village with sandy beaches.
  • Dangriga Town, the cultural and artistic capital of Belize.

Five Blues Lake National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Belize. It provides a perfect escape from the city life and a chance to connect with nature.
