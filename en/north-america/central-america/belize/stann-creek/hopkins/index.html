Introduction to Hopkins

Welcome to Hopkins, a beautiful coastal village in Stann Creek, Belize, in Central America. Hopkins is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a peaceful and authentic experience, away from the crowds and the tourism buzz. Hopkins boasts long stretches of sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking views of the Caribbean Sea.

This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Hopkins, providing you with all the information you need on the best places to visit, food to try, activities to do, and local culture to discover. We’ll also give you tips on how to get around, where to stay, and how to stay safe and healthy during your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Hopkins

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hopkins is a village rich in history and culture, and there are many museums and historical places worth visiting. One of them is the Hopkins Cultural Center, which showcases the traditions and crafts of the Garifuna people, the indigenous community of Hopkins. You can also visit the Drums of Our Fathers Monument, a tribute to the Garifuna culture’s musical heritage, or the Lebeha Drumming Center, where you can learn to play the drums and dance to the rhythm of the Garifuna beats.

Natural Attractions of Hopkins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hopkins is a paradise for nature lovers, with plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to enjoy. You can go snorkeling or diving in the Belize Barrier Reef, one of the world’s most extensive coral reef systems, or take a boat tour to explore the nearby cayes and islands. If you prefer land activities, you can hike or bike in the Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary, a protected rainforest home to jaguars, pumas, howler monkeys, and other exotic wildlife. You can also visit the Mayan ruins of Xunantunich, Caracol, or Altun Ha, a glimpse into Belize’s ancient history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hopkins

Hopkins to Sittee River

One of the most popular hiking routes in Hopkins is the trail that connects the village with the Sittee River, a beautiful waterway that flows into the Caribbean Sea. The trail winds through the jungle and the savannah, offering stunning views of the mountains and the sea. You can also take a bike tour on this route, exploring the surroundings while enjoying a challenging yet rewarding ride.

Best Things to Do in Hopkins: Activities and Tours

Garifuna Cooking Class

If you want to experience the Garifuna culture and learn a new skill, take a Garifuna cooking class. You’ll learn to prepare traditional dishes such as hudut, fish stew, cassava bread, and coconut rice and beans, using fresh and local ingredients. You’ll also learn about the history and significance of these dishes and how they relate to the Garifuna culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hopkins

If you’re spending a week in Hopkins, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Hopkins, beach relaxation, and sunset-watching
  • Day 2: Snorkeling or diving in the Belize Barrier Reef
  • Day 3: Hiking or biking in the Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Day 4: Mayan ruins tour to Xunantunich or Caracol
  • Day 5: Garifuna cooking class and drumming workshop
  • Day 6: Boat tour to nearby cayes and islands
  • Day 7: Relaxation and farewell to Hopkins

Food and Drink Scene of Hopkins

Must-Try Dishes

When in Hopkins, you must try these delicious local dishes:

  • Hudut: a fish stew made with coconut milk, green plantains, cassava, and other spices
  • Gibnut: a large rodent, also known as royal rat, that is traditionally roasted and served with rice and beans
  • Fry jacks: small fried doughnuts, usually served as breakfast or snacks, with various toppings

Local Beverages

To wash down your meal or cool off on a hot day, try these local beverages:

  • Belikin beer: the national beer of Belize, available in several variations, including stout, lager, and premium
  • Panty ripper: a tropical cocktail made with coconut rum, pineapple juice, and coconut cream
  • Cashew wine: a sweet and fruity wine made from cashew fruit, popular in Belize and neighboring countries

Where to Stay in Hopkins: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hopkins has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury resorts to rustic guesthouses and hostels. Some of the best places to stay in Hopkins are:

  • Hopkins Bay Resort: a luxury beachfront resort with excellent amenities, such as a spa, a restaurant, and a swimming pool
  • The Funky Dodo Backpackers Hostel: a budget-friendly hostel with a fun and relaxed atmosphere, located in the heart of the village
  • Jaguar Reef Lodge & Spa: an eco-friendly resort that offers comfortable rooms, an infinity pool, and a wide range of activities and tours

What to Buy in Hopkins: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home some souvenirs or local products from Hopkins, you can visit the village’s markets and shops or the nearby Maya Center Village. Here are some ideas:

  • Handcrafted Garifuna drums and maracas
  • Belizean chocolate and coffee
  • Local spices and hot sauces

Local Culture and Customs of Hopkins

Hopkins is a predominantly Garifuna community, and the Garifuna culture is an essential part of the village’s identity and lifestyle. When visiting Hopkins, it’s essential to respect the local culture and be aware of its customs and traditions. Some of the most important things to keep in mind are:

  • Garifuna drumming and dancing are hallmarks of the culture, and you can witness them at various events and festivals
  • Garifuna cuisine is distinct and delicious, and you can try it at local restaurants and food stalls
  • The Garifuna language is a unique blend of African, Caribbean, and indigenous languages, and you can learn some words and phrases during your stay

Best Time to Visit Hopkins

The best time to visit Hopkins is from December to April, when the weather is dry, sunny, and warm, and the sea is calm and clear. The high season also coincides with the peak of the Garifuna cultural events, such as the Hopkins Day celebrations in March and the Garifuna Settlement Day in November. However, keep in mind that this period is also the busiest and the most expensive, and you’ll need to book your accommodation and activities well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hopkins

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the delicious Garifuna cuisine
  • Protect the environment and the wildlife
  • Bring a good sunscreen and mosquito repellent

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Litter or leave trash on the beach or the streets
  • Swim or snorkel in areas marked as unsafe or prohibited
  • Wear camouflage clothing, which is prohibited by law

Safety and Health

Safety in Hopkins

Hopkins is a safe and welcoming village, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s always wise to take some precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night and in remote areas. Some safety tips to keep in mind are:

  • Keep your valuables and documents in a safe place
  • Use a reliable transportation service, especially at night
  • Avoid walking alone in poorly lit or deserted areas
  • Drink responsibly and avoid getting involved in drug-related activities

Health in Hopkins

Hopkins is a malaria-free area, but it’s advisable to take some precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and zika virus. Wear long sleeves and pants, use insect repellent, and sleep under a mosquito net, especially if you’re camping or staying in a rustic accommodation. It’s also recommended to drink only bottled or treated water and avoid raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Hopkins: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hopkins is located on the southern coast of Belize, approximately 130 km (80 miles) from Belize City. Here are some ways to get to Hopkins:

Airports Near Hopkins

The closest international airport to Hopkins is Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport in Belize City, which receives flights from the US, Canada, and other Central American countries. From there, you can take a domestic flight to Dangriga, which is the nearest airport to Hopkins, or arrange a transfer by shuttle or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Hopkins

If you’re traveling by bus, you can take a local bus or a coach from Belize City to Dangriga and from there to Hopkins. The journey takes approximately 3-4 hours and costs around $10-$15.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or a bike in Belize City or Dangriga and drive to Hopkins. However, keep in mind that the roads in Belize can be rough and unmarked, and you may need to cross some rivers and wade through some mud.

How to Get Around in Hopkins

Hopkins is a small and compact village, and you can easily explore it on foot or by bike. If you want to venture out to the surrounding areas or go on a tour, you can arrange a transfer by taxi or shuttle, or rent a car or a bike. Some tour operators also offer transport services as part of their packages.

Where to Go Next from Hopkins: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hopkins is a great base for exploring the nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Placencia: a charming and laid-back town on the southern coast of Belize, renowned for its beaches, seafood, and nightlife
  • The Blue Hole: a natural sinkhole in the sea, a favorite spot for diving and snorkeling
  • The Toledo District: a remote and pristine region in southeastern Belize, home to numerous Mayan villages, waterfalls, and nature reserves

Conclusion

Hopkins is a hidden gem in Belize, offering a unique blend of nature, culture, and relaxation. Whether you’re seeking adventure or tranquility, this coastal village is the perfect destination. By following this travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip to Hopkins and make the most of your stay.
