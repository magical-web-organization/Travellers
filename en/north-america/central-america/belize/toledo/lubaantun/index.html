Introduction to Lubaantun

Lubaantun, located in the southernmost district of Belize, is an ancient Mayan city that was established in around 730 AD. The city was prominent from around 730 to 890 AD and served as an important trade center. Lubaantun is known for its unique style of architecture, the absence of stone monuments, and its bizarre artifacts. Visitors can explore the ruins and experience the fascinating history of this ancient Mayan city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lubaantun

  • Rio Blanco Waterfalls Trail - 1.5 km hike to a beautiful waterfall
  • Nim Li Punit Trail - 4 km round trip hike to the Nim Li Punit Mayan site
  • Jaguar Trail - 5 km round trip hike through the Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary

Best Things to Do in Lubaantun: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Mayan Temples and the Royal Plaza
  • Visit the Tomb of the Crystal Skull
  • Take a guided tour of the Lubaantun Ruins
  • Go cave tubing in the Blue Creek Cave
  • Take a jungle hike to the Rio Blanco Waterfalls

Week-Long Itinerary for Lubaantun

Day 1 Arrival in Lubaantun, check into hotel, and explore the Lubaantun Ruins.
Day 2 Visit the Tomb of the Crystal Skull, Rio Blanco National Park, and the Museum of Maya Archaeology.
Day 3 Take a guided tour of Blue Creek Cave, and then visit Nim Li Punit Archaeological Reserve.
Day 4 Hike the Jaguar Trail in the Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary, and then go cave tubing in Hokeb Ha Cave.
Day 5 Relax at the hotel or participate in a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Mayan dishes.
Day 6 Take a day trip to the nearby Punta Gorda and explore the town and its local markets.
Day 7 Departure from Lubaantun.

Local Culture and Customs of Lubaantun

The Mayan culture is deeply rooted in Lubaantun, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions. It is customary to greet people with a “good morning” or “good afternoon,” and it is respectful to remove your hat when entering a building or speaking with an elder. Visitors should dress conservatively and avoid wearing revealing clothing, especially when visiting the Mayan Ruins.

Best Time to Visit Lubaantun

The best time to visit Lubaantun is during the dry season, which is from December to May. The rainy season can make hiking and outdoor activities difficult, and the humidity can be oppressive. If you are looking for a more affordable trip, consider visiting during the off-season from September to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lubaantun

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress conservatively
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of local people

Don’t:

  • Swim in the rivers or waterfalls unless with a licensed tour guide
  • Touch or disturb any artifacts in the Mayan Ruins
  • Litter or damage the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lubaantun

Lubaantun is considered a safe destination for travelers, but it is recommended to take normal precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended or wandering around at night. Visitors should use a reputable taxi service and avoid walking alone in unpopulated areas.

Health in Lubaantun

While visiting Lubaantun, travelers should take the standard precautions such as drinking bottled water to avoid gastrointestinal illnesses. It is recommended to bring insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites that could carry diseases such as malaria or Zika.

How to Get Around in Lubaantun

The best way to get around Lubaantun is by hiring a local guide, arranging transportation with a local tour company, or renting a car. The Mayan Ruins are accessible by car, but hiking is the only option for exploring many of the natural attractions nearby.

Where to Go Next from Lubaantun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hopkins Village - a charming beach town on the Caribbean coast with a vibrant Garifuna culture.
  • San Ignacio - a bustling town known for its outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and proximity to the ATM Cave.
  • Tikal National Park - one of the largest Mayan archaeological sites in existence

Conclusion

Lubaantun, Belize is a fascinating destination for travelers interested in ancient Mayan history, outdoor adventures, and cultural exploration. With its unique architecture, natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage, visitors will find plenty to see and do in this enchanting destination.
