Introduction to Rama

Rama is a small town located in Caribbean Nicaragua, in the southern part of the country. The place is known for its beautiful natural surroundings, wetlands, and diverse wildlife. Rama is considered a hub for ecotourism in Nicaragua, and the town has a rich culture and history that visitors should explore.

List of Places to Visit in Rama

If you are planning a trip to Rama, you should visit the following places.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Rama: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the history of the indigenous Miskito people of Rama. Visitors can learn about their traditional way of life, culture, and customs.
  • Fortaleza El Castillo: This fort was built by Spanish colonizers to protect the city from pirate attacks. It is now a cultural heritage site that visitors can explore.

Natural Attractions of Rama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rio Indio Maiz Biological Reserve: This protected area covers over 4,000 sq. km of land and is home to a wide variety of wildlife, such as howler monkeys and jaguars. Visitors can explore the reserve through guided tours and hikes.
  • Los Guatuzos Wildlife Reserve: This wetland area is home to a variety of birds and animals, including scarlet macaws and caimans. Visitors can take boat tours through the wetlands to spot wildlife.
  • Kayaking on Escondido River: The Escondido River is one of the most scenic rivers in Nicaragua and is perfect for kayaking. Visitors can enjoy the tranquil beauty of the river while kayaking through the lush jungle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rama

Rama has several hiking and biking routes that visitors can explore to enjoy the beauty of the town’s surroundings. The most popular hiking trails include:

  • Sendero El Guardián: This trail is located in the Rio Indio Maiz Reserve and passes through the lush rainforest. Visitors can spot a variety of wildlife during the hike.
  • Sendero Cascada del Toro: This trail leads to the beautiful cascading waterfall and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Rama: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, visitors can enjoy other activities and tours in Rama, including:

  • Guided canoe tours on Rio Escondido: Visitors can enjoy the tranquil beauty of the river while spotting wildlife such as monkeys and exotic birds.
  • Visit local indigenous communities: Visitors can learn about the Miskito people’s traditional way of life and customs.
  • Fishing Tour: Rama is famous for its fishing tours, and visitors can enjoy fishing for exotic fish species such as tarpon and snook.

Food and Drink Scene of Rama

Rama is a small town with limited options when it comes to food and drink. However, visitors can enjoy local cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rondon: This is a local dish made with fish, yucca, and plantain. It is cooked in coconut milk and served with rice.
  • Tostones: These are fried plantains that are served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Cacao: A traditional hot chocolate drink made with cocoa and spices.
  • Tiste: This is a local drink made with corn, cocoa, and spices. It is often served as a refreshing drink on hot days.

Where to Stay in Rama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are limited options for accommodations in Rama, but visitors can choose from various hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Some popular options include:

  • Casa Arbol Guesthouse
  • Hotel Rinconcito Serrano
  • Casa El Misionero

What to Buy in Rama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rama is famous for its local handicrafts made by the Miskito indigenous people. Visitors can buy handmade baskets, traditional wooden furniture, and pottery. They can also buy locally made coconut oil and other products made of coconut.

Local Culture and Customs of Rama

Rama is known for its rich cultural heritage, shaped by the customs and traditions of the Miskito indigenous people. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and make an effort to learn about them. The town celebrates various festivals, such as the El Viejo festival in August, which celebrates the town’s patron saint, and the Miskito Indian Day on September 1st.

Best Time to Visit Rama

The best time to visit Rama is from December to April when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors should avoid the rainy season, which runs from May to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rama

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the town.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Spanish.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without their permission.
  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large sums of money, particularly in crowded areas.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rama

Rama is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise normal precautions, such as not walking alone late at night and avoiding areas that are poorly lit. Visitors should also make an effort to protect themselves from mosquito bites by using insect repellant and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

Health in Rama

Rama is located in a remote area, and visitors should make sure they are up-to-date on their vaccinations before traveling. They should also take precautions against diseases such as dengue fever and malaria, which are prevalent in the region. Visitors should also carry a basic first-aid kit, as medical facilities are limited in the town.

Getting to Rama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rama

The closest airport to Rama is the Bluefields Airport, which is about a 40-minute drive from the town. Visitors should book a taxi or shuttle to reach Rama after arriving at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Rama

There are several bus stations near Rama, including the Bluefields Bus Station, which has frequent connections to Rama. Visitors can also take a bus from Managua to reach Rama.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Rama by taking a water taxi from Bluefields. The boat journey is scenic and takes about an hour to reach Rama.

How to Get Around in Rama

Rama is a small town and is easy to explore on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a taxi to visit places outside town.

Where to Go Next from Rama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other destinations near Rama after their visit. Some popular options include:

  • Pearl Lagoon: A small coastal town famous for its beautiful beaches and fishing tours.
  • Corn Islands: These two islands are known for their stunning beaches, diving, and snorkeling.

Conclusion

Rama is a beautiful, nature-filled town in Nicaragua. Visitors can explore the town’s rich culture and history, as well as enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking. We hope this guide will help you plan your trip to Rama, Nicaragua, and make the most of your visit.
