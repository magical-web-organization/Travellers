Esteli is a captivating city located in the Northern Highlands of Nicaragua, where lush greenery and misty mountain peaks surround its colonial streets. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and adventure opportunities. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking, biking, or trying local cuisine, you’ll find plenty of things to do in Esteli. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat, stay, and shop.

Introduction to Esteli

Located in the heart of the Northern Highlands of Nicaragua, Esteli is a charming city known for its vibrant culture, exquisite cuisine, and breathtaking natural landscapes. The city’s history dates back to the 16th century, when it was originally inhabited by indigenous tribes. Spanish settlers arrived in the 17th century and established Esteli as a stopping point for those traveling to Nicaragua’s Caribbean coast. Today, Esteli is a bustling city with a fascinating mix of cultures, traditions, and history.

List of Places to Visit in Esteli

If you’re planning a trip to Esteli, there are plenty of exciting places to visit. Here are some of our favorite cultural and natural attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Revolución: A must-visit museum for anyone interested in Nicaraguan history. The museum tells the story of the Sandinista revolution and the struggle for liberation from the Somoza regime.

  • Catedral de la Inmaculada Concepción: The cathedral is a beautiful example of neoclassical architecture, and it houses a fascinating collection of religious art and artifacts.

  • Centro de Arte Fondos de los Artistas: An art center and gallery that showcases the work of local artists. Visitors can browse paintings, sculptures, pottery, and other artisanal crafts.

Natural Attractions of Esteli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tisey Nature Reserve: A nature reserve located in the mountains surrounding Esteli. Visitors can hike through the lush forest, spot exotic birds, and admire stunning panoramic views.

  • Somoto Canyon: A breathtaking canyon located near Esteli that offers a variety of adventure activities, including kayaking, swimming, and hiking.

  • La Garnacha: A small village located in the mountains near Esteli that is famous for its pottery and ceramics. Visitors can watch local artisans at work and purchase unique, handmade souvenirs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Esteli

Esteli is a paradise for adventurers and outdoor enthusiasts. There are several hiking and biking trails in the area that offer stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Cerro Apante: A challenging hiking trail that leads to the top of a mountain overlooking Esteli. The trail is steep and rocky, but the views from the top are absolutely breathtaking.

  • Loma de Tiscapa: A picturesque hiking trail that winds through the lush forest surrounding Esteli. The trail is easy to moderate in difficulty and offers plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting.

  • Pan American Highway: A popular biking route that stretches from Alaska to Argentina. The section of the highway near Esteli is known for its stunning scenery and adrenaline-pumping descents.

Best Things to Do in Esteli: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for cultural experiences or outdoor adventures, Esteli has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of our favorite things to do in the city:

  • Take a cigar factory tour: Esteli is known for its high-quality cigars, which are made using traditional methods and locally grown tobacco. Visitors can take a tour of a cigar factory and learn about the art of cigar-making.

  • Go birdwatching: Nicaragua is home to over 700 species of birds, many of which can be found near Esteli. Take a guided bird-watching tour and spot colorful toucans, parrots, and other exotic species.

  • Attend a festival: Esteli is known for its vibrant cultural scene, and there are several festivals and celebrations throughout the year. The Fiestas Patronales de San Juan, held in June, is one of the city’s most popular events, featuring traditional music, dance, and food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Esteli

If you have a week to spend in Esteli, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the city center and visit the Catedral de la Inmaculada Concepción and Museo de la Revolución.

Day 2: Take a hiking trip to Tisey Nature Reserve and spend the day exploring the lush forest.

Day 3: Visit Somoto Canyon and try kayaking, swimming, or hiking.

Day 4: Explore the pottery village of La Garnacha and shop for unique souvenirs.

Day 5: Take a guided bird-watching tour and spot colorful toucans, parrots, and other exotic species.

Day 6: Attend a festival or celebration in Esteli.

Day 7: Take a tour of a local cigar factory and learn about the art of cigar-making.

Food and Drink Scene of Esteli

Nicaragua is known for its vibrant cuisine, and Esteli is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vigorón: A traditional dish made with yuca (cassava), chicharrón (fried pork rinds), and curtido (pickled cabbage salad).

  • Gallo pinto: A beloved Nicaraguan breakfast dish made with rice, beans, and spices.

  • Quesillo: A type of cheese that is wrapped in a soft tortilla and served with pickled onions and sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Macuá: A popular Nicaraguan cocktail made with rum, guava juice, lime juice, and sugar.

  • Pinolillo: A traditional drink made with cornmeal, cocoa, and water or milk.

  • Tiste: A refreshing drink made with ground corn, cacao powder, and water.

Where to Stay in Esteli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Esteli has a variety of accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Hostel Luna: A popular backpacker hostel that offers affordable dorm rooms and private suites.

  • Hotel Los Altos: A mid-range hotel located in the heart of Esteli, with spacious rooms and a rooftop terrace.

  • Casa Vinculos: A charming guesthouse with colorful rooms, free breakfast, and a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Esteli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Esteli is famous for its pottery, cigars, and coffee. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics from La Garnacha.

  • High-quality cigars from a local factory, such as Joya de Nicaragua or Nica Sueno.

  • Premium coffee beans from the nearby Las Segovias region.

Local Culture and Customs of Esteli

The people of Esteli are known for their warm hospitality and friendly demeanor. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or local communities.

  • Always ask permission before taking photos of people or their homes.

  • Avoid loud or disruptive behavior, especially in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Esteli

The best time to visit Esteli is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is mild and comfortable, with plenty of sunshine and clear skies. The rainy season, from May to October, can be unpredictable and may limit outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Esteli

To make the most of your trip to Esteli, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including gallo pinto, quesillo, and vigorón.

  • Explore the natural wonders of the Northern Highlands, including Tisey Nature Reserve and Somoto Canyon.

  • Learn about the history and culture of Nicaragua by visiting museums and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Flash valuables or walk alone at night, especially in the city center.

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

  • Leave trash or litter in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Esteli is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Esteli:

Safety in Esteli

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in the city center.

  • Keep valuable items, such as passports and money, in a secure location.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid flashing valuables in public.

Health in Esteli

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid waterborne illnesses.

  • Use insect repellent to protect yourself from mosquitoes and other bugs.

  • Consult a doctor or health professional about vaccinations and travel medicine before visiting Esteli.

Getting to Esteli: Transportation Options and Logistics

Esteli is located in the Northern Highlands of Nicaragua, approximately 150 kilometers north of Managua, the capital city. Here are some transportation options for getting to Esteli:

Airports Near Esteli

  • Augusto C. Sandino International Airport: Located in Managua, approximately 150 kilometers south of Esteli.

  • AERODOMO Esteli: A small airport located in the city of Esteli, with limited flights.

Bus Stations Near Esteli

  • Granada Bus Station: Located in Granada, approximately 120 kilometers south of Esteli.

  • Managua Bus Station: Located in Managua, approximately 150 kilometers south of Esteli.

  • Jinotega Bus Station: Located in Jinotega, approximately 26 kilometers north of Esteli.

Other Options

  • Private transfer: Many hotels and tour operators offer private transfer services from Managua or other nearby cities.

  • Rental car: Rental cars are available at the airport and in major cities, but international drivers’ licenses are required.

How to Get Around in Esteli

The easiest way to get around Esteli is on foot or by taxi. Taxis are readily available and inexpensive, but be sure to negotiate the fare before getting in. Buses and minivans are also available for travel to nearby cities and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Esteli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore the Northern Highlands, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Jinotega: A scenic city located approximately 26 kilometers north of Esteli, known for its coffee plantations and stunning views.

  • Matagalpa: A charming city located approximately 66 kilometers east of Esteli, known for its mountainous landscapes and coffee culture.

  • Rio San Juan: A lush nature reserve located approximately 176 kilometers southeast of Esteli, with abundant wildlife and beautiful waterfalls.

Conclusion

Esteli is a vibrant and picturesque city that offers a wealth of cultural and natural experiences for travelers. From exploring museums and cultural sites to hiking through lush forests and trying local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Esteli. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your trip to this captivating city in Nicaragua’s Northern Highlands.
