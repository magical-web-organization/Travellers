Introduction to Balgue

Balgue is a stunning village nestled in the center of the Isla de Ometepe, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. The island is dominated by the twin volcanoes Concepción and Maderas. Balgue is an ideal starting point for exploring the island’s natural beauty, and it is worth spending a few days in this charming village.

List of Places to Visit in Balgue

Here is a list of must-visit attractions in Balgue:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zanabria Family Museum: This small museum showcases the history of the Zanabria family and their role in the development of Isla de Ometepe.
  • Altagracia Convent: The convent is a great example of colonial architecture and houses a small museum with artifacts from pre-Columbian times.
  • El Ceibo Museum: This is a private collection of pre-Columbian art and artifacts and is the largest of its type in Central America

Natural Attractions of Balgue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • San Ramon Waterfall: A short hike takes you to this beautiful waterfall, which is about 50 meters high.
  • Charco Verde: A nature reserve with forest trails, a lagoon, and a stunning view of Lake Nicaragua.
  • Ojo de Agua: A natural pool surrounded by lush tropical gardens, perfect for swimming on hot days.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balgue

Ometepe Island is a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers. Some popular routes you can explore are:

  • Volcan Maderas: A challenging hike to the top of the dormant volcano and rewarding views of the crater’s lagoon.
  • Concepcion Volcano: A difficult climb to the summit, but the views from the top are breathtaking.
  • Charco Verde Trail: A 6 km trail that leads you from the village to Charco Verde Reserve, offering stunning views of Lake Nicaragua.

Best Things to Do in Balgue: Activities and Tours

Here are some activities and tours you can do in Balgue:

  • Horseback Riding Tour: A guided horseback ride through the countryside, with stops at cultural sites and scenic views of the volcanoes.
  • Coffee Tour: Learn about the coffee production process, from the bean to the cup, and enjoy a tasting session.
  • Kayaking Tour: Explore the shores of the island and the lagoons by kayak, an exciting adventure for all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Balgue

Day 1-2: Visit Zanabria Family Museum and Altagracia Convent

Day 3: Hike to San Ramon Waterfall

Day 4: Take a horseback riding tour and enjoy a coffee tasting session

Day 5: Take a kayaking tour

Day 6: Hike to Charco Verde Reserve

Day 7: Relax at Ojo de Agua

Food and Drink Scene of Balgue

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nacatamales: A traditional Nicaraguan dish made of masa, seasoned pork, and vegetables, wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.
  • Gallo Pinto: A popular dish made of rice and beans, served with eggs or meat.
  • Quesillo: A type of cheese served with tortillas, onions, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Macuá: A cocktail made with white rum, guava juice, lemon juice, and sugar.
  • Toña Beer: A light and refreshing lager beer, the most popular in Nicaragua.
  • Pinolillo: A traditional drink made of roasted corn and cacao, sweetened with sugar.

Where to Stay in Balgue: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hospedaje Soma: A family-run guesthouse with affordable prices and beautiful views of Maderas Volcano.
  • Finca Mystica: An ecolodge with rustic cabins and a restaurant serving organic and vegetarian food.
  • Hacienda Merida: A boutique hotel with colonial-style architecture and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Balgue: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Ometepe’s indigenous communities are known for their beautiful clay pottery, handmade and hand-painted.
  • Coffee: Nicaragua is famous for its coffee, and in Balgue, you can find some of the best locally produced beans.
  • Hammocks: A perfect souvenir to bring home and a way to enjoy the lazy afternoons in the garden.

Local Culture and Customs of Balgue

The locals are friendly and hospitable, and their culture is unique. Some customs and traditions you may encounter are:

  • La Gigantona: During festivals and celebrations, you may see a giant puppet being paraded around the town.
  • El Güegüense: A traditional play that depicts the Spanish conquest from the indigenous perspective.
  • Semana Santa: Holy Week is a significant celebration, with processions and religious events throughout the island.

Best Time to Visit Balgue

The best time to visit Balgue is between November and April, the dry season. The weather is sunny and warm, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balgue

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Wear comfortable and light clothing
  • Carry cash as many places do not accept cards.

Don’t:

  • Take sand from the beaches or natural sites
  • Litter or leave trash lying around
  • Swim in the lagoons or waterfalls without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Balgue

Balgue is a relatively safe village. However, it is recommended to take precautions, such as:

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and do not leave valuables unattended.
  • Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.
  • Be vigilant when withdrawing money or exchanging currency.

Health in Balgue

It is essential to take precautions to avoid potential health risks such as:

  • Drink bottled water or purified water.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Seek medical attention if you have any symptoms of illness.

Getting to Balgue: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balgue

The nearest airport to Balgue is Augusto C. Sandino International Airport in Managua.

Bus Stations Near Balgue

The bus station in Rivas is the closest to Balgue. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to Moyogalpa or Altagracia, the main towns of the island.

Other Options

The ferry from San Jorge to Moyogalpa is the most common way to reach Ometepe. You can also rent a bike or a motorbike to explore the island.

How to Get Around in Balgue

The best way to get around Balgue and the island is by renting a bike or a motorbike. Alternatively, you can take a taxi or a bus to the main towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Balgue: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Nicaragua: Take a boat ride and explore one of the largest lakes in Central America.
  • San Juan del Sur: A popular beach town on the Pacific coast, famous for its surfing.

Conclusion

Balgue is a charming village that offers visitors an opportunity to experience the beauty and culture of Isla de Ometepe. Whether you want to explore the natural attractions, learn about the local culture, or relax in a hammock, Balgue has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover this hidden gem of Nicaragua!
