Introduction to Rivas

Located on the Pacific Coast of Nicaragua, Rivas is a charming city that is often overlooked by tourists in favor of more popular destinations such as Granada and San Juan del Sur. However, this small city has plenty to offer travelers seeking an authentic Nicaraguan experience. Bordered by Lake Nicaragua to the east and the Pacific Ocean to the west, Rivas boasts stunning natural scenery, a rich history, and a vibrant cultural scene. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do and see in Rivas, including where to stay, what to eat, and how to get to this hidden gem in Central America.

List of Places to Visit in Rivas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rivas has a long and fascinating history that’s reflected in its many museums and historical landmarks. Key places to check out include:

  • The San Pedro Parish Church, a stunning colonial-era church that dates back to the 18th century.
  • The National Museum of Rivas, which offers a glimpse into the city’s history and cultural heritage.
  • The Old Railway Station, which once served as the main transportation hub for the region and now houses an art gallery.
  • The Balneario de San Juan de la Concepción, a natural spring and popular swimming spot that has been used for centuries for spiritual and medicinal purposes.

Natural Attractions of Rivas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whether you’re a nature lover or an adventure seeker, Rivas is the perfect destination for you. Some of the most popular natural attractions in the area include:

  • The Isla de Ometepe, a volcanic island in Lake Nicaragua featuring two majestic peaks and a wealth of flora and fauna. Hiking, kayaking, and biking are popular activities on the island.
  • Playa Yankee, a beautiful and secluded beach popular with surfers.
  • The Chocoyero-El Brujo Nature Reserve, a stunning protected area that’s home to several waterfalls, hiking trails, and a wide variety of bird species.
  • The Masaya Volcano, a volcano park with a smouldering caldera. From the edge crater, you can witness a spectacular view of the lava lake in the depths of the volcano.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rivas

Rivas is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with countless trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Tola Loop, a scenic 50-kilometer loop through rural Nicaragua that features a mix of dirt roads and paved highways.
  • The Maderas Volcano Hike, a challenging hike that takes you to the summit of the Maderas Volcano on Isla de Ometepe.
  • The Chocoyero-El Brujo Reserve Trail, which follows the Chocoyero river and leads to several spectacular waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Rivas: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring its many natural wonders, Rivas has plenty of other activities to offer visitors. Here are some of its top tours and activities:

  • Kayaking on Lake Nicaragua, where you can explore the island scenery at a leisurely pace.
  • Surfing classes and board rentals available at Playa Yankee.
  • Zip-lining through the trees of the Chocoyero-El Brujo Nature Reserve.
  • Enrich your senses with a Spanish language immersion course and get introduced to a different culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rivas

For a weeklong trip in Rivas, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore the Isla de Ometepe, hike to the Maderas Volcano, and enjoy the island’s unique culture and scenery.

Day 3-4: Take a trip to Playa Yankee for some surfing lessons and beach relaxation.

Day 5-6: Visit the Chocoyero-El Brujo Nature Reserve for some zip-lining and waterfall hikes.

Day 7: Head back to Rivas and explore some of the city’s cultural attractions, such as the San Pedro Parish Church and the National Museum of Rivas.

Food and Drink Scene of Rivas

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Rivas is complete without sampling some of its delicious local cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Gallo Pinto, a traditional Nicaraguan rice and beans dish that’s served for breakfast.
  • Nacatamales, a hearty dish made with corn masa and stuffed with meat, veggies, and herbs before being steamed.
  • Caballo Bayo, a local beer made with Nicaraguan hops and malt.

Local Beverages

In addition to Caballo Bayo, Rivas has a variety of other local beverages to try, including:

  • Chicha, a fermented corn drink that has been enjoyed in Central America for centuries.
  • Tiste, a sweet and refreshing drink made from ground corn and cacao.
  • Cacao tea, a hot and comforting drink made with cacao beans and spices.

Where to Stay in Rivas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rivas has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of our top picks include:

  • Hotel La Estación, a converted railway station that offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a terrace with panoramic views.
  • Hostal Elena, a budget-friendly hostel with dorms and private rooms, a shared kitchen, and a common area with hammocks.
  • San Simian Lodge, a luxurious eco-lodge that offers spacious cottages, a restaurant, a swimming pool, and breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.

What to Buy in Rivas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to pick up some souvenirs and support local businesses, head to one of Rivas’ many markets or artisan shops. Some top things to buy include:

  • Pottery and ceramics, which are a specialty in the area and come in a wide range of styles and colors.
  • Handwoven textiles and clothing, which are made by local artists using traditional techniques.
  • Coffee, which is grown and produced in Nicaragua and is widely regarded as some of the best in the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Rivas

Nicaragua has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and Rivas is no exception. Some key customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Nicaraguan Spanish, which is distinct from other Spanish dialects and incorporates many indigenous words and phrases.
  • The Gritería, a religious holiday celebrated on December 7th that involves shouting “Who causes so much joy? La Purísima Concepción!” to celebrate the Virgin Mary.

Best Time to Visit Rivas

The best time to visit Rivas is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. During these months, the weather is sunny and pleasant with low humidity levels, perfect for exploring Rivas’ many outdoor attractions. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so be prepared for higher prices and larger crowds. If you’re looking to save money and avoid the crowds, consider visiting during the rainy season, which runs from May to November. While the weather can be unpredictable, you’ll also have a chance to see Rivas at its greenest and most lush.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rivas

Do:

  • Keep an open mind and embrace the local culture.
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before your trip.
  • Explore the natural wonders of the area.

Don’t:

  • Flash your valuables in public
  • Wear overly revealing clothing
  • Use public transportation at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Rivas

Rivas is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to take common-sense precautions while traveling. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or wearing expensive jewelry in public.
  • Stick to well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.

Health in Rivas

While Rivas doesn’t present any major health risks, it’s still a good idea to take some basic precautions to stay healthy while traveling. Some tips include:

  • Drink only bottled or treated water.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against the sun and/or mosquito bites.
  • Consider getting vaccinated before your trip, especially if you plan to do a lot of outdoor activities.

Getting to Rivas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rivas is easily accessible by both air and land. Some of the best ways to get there include:

Airports Near Rivas

The closest airport to Rivas is the Augusto C. Sandino International Airport in Managua, which is about a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Rivas

Buses run frequently between Rivas and other major Nicaraguan cities. Some of the main bus stations in the area include:

  • Roberto Huembes Bus Terminal in Managua
  • UCA Bus Station in Managua
  • Granada Bus Station in Granada

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also easy to come by in the area, although be aware that driving in Nicaragua can be challenging for inexperienced drivers.

How to Get Around in Rivas

The best way to get around Rivas is by car or taxi. While there is also a public bus system, it can be confusing and unreliable for tourists. If you’re comfortable driving in Nicaragua, renting a car can be a good option, as it will give you more flexibility and freedom to explore the area at your own pace. Taxis are also relatively inexpensive and easy to find, although be sure to agree on a price beforehand to avoid any surprises.

Where to Go Next from Rivas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rivas is the perfect base for exploring some of Nicaragua’s other top destinations. Some places you might want to visit include:

  • Granada, a charming colonial-era town with plenty of historical landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • San Juan del Sur, a bustling surf town with beautiful beaches and an energetic nightlife.
  • León, a university town with rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and a up-close lava adventure within less than an hour of distance from the city.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for adventure, culture, or relaxation, Rivas has it all. From hiking and biking to enjoying local cuisine and exploring the city’s many museums and historical landmarks, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem of Nicaragua. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your trip to Rivas today!
