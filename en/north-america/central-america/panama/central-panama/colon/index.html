Introduction to Colon

Located in Central Panama, Colon is a vibrant city with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant culture. The city is situated at the northern end of the Panama Canal and is an important gateway to the Atlantic Ocean. Colon has a lot to offer travelers, from its historical landmarks and museums to gorgeous beaches and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Colon

Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions, outdoor activities, or just a relaxing beach vacation, Colon has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Colon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Panama Canal Railway Museum: This museum tells the story of the construction of the Panama Canal Railway and its importance in the history of the Canal.
  • Fort San Lorenzo: This 17th-century fort was built to protect the Panama Canal from pirates and other invaders. Today, it’s a popular tourist attraction and offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.
  • Gatun Locks: One of the most popular attractions in Colon, the Gatun Locks allows visitors to witness firsthand the impressive feat of engineering that is the Panama Canal.

Natural Attractions of Colon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Soberania National Park: This park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering hiking and biking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and more.
  • Portobelo National Park: This park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and historical significance.
  • Isla Grande: Located just off the coast of Colon, Isla Grande is a picturesque island with crystal clear waters, soft sand beaches, and lush forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colon

Colon offers a range of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Pipeline Road: This is one of the most popular trails in Soberania National Park, offering a chance to see exotic birds and other wildlife.
  • The Fort San Lorenzo Trail: This trail is a challenging trek that rewards hikers with stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.
  • The Gatun Lake Trail: This trail follows the eastern shore of Gatun Lake and offers beautiful views of the surrounding rainforest.

Best Things to Do in Colon: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Colon. Here are some top activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Take a boat tour of the Panama Canal to learn about its history and see the locks in action.
  • Visit the Achiote Road farmers’ market to experience local culture and cuisine.
  • Take a snorkeling or diving tour to explore the coral reefs and marine life off the coast of Colon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colon

If you have a week to spend in Colon, here’s one possible itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Colon

Panama is known for its delicious cuisine, and Colon is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sancocho: This hearty stew is made with chicken, vegetables, and herbs.
  • Empanadas: These savory pastries are filled with beef, chicken, or cheese.
  • Tamales: These steamed corn cakes are often filled with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Abuelo: This rum is made locally in Panama and is perfect for sipping on the beach.
  • Chicheme: This sweet corn drink is a popular refreshment in Panama.
  • Seco: This clear liquor is made from sugarcane and is commonly mixed with fruit juices or soda.

Where to Stay in Colon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Colon has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Hotel Melia Panama Canal: This luxury hotel is located on the banks of the Panama Canal and offers stunning views and top-notch amenities.
  • The Red Frog Beach Island Resort: This eco-friendly resort is located on a private island and offers secluded beach getaways.
  • El Otro Lado: This boutique resort is located in Portobelo and offers a unique cultural experience and luxurious accommodations.

What to Buy in Colon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Colon, here are some top picks:

  • Panama hats: These iconic hats are made from straw and are perfect for staying cool in the hot Colon weather.
  • Handmade crafts: Look for handmade baskets, textiles, and wood carvings at local markets and shops.
  • Coffee: Panama produces some of the world’s best coffee, so be sure to bring some home to enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Colon

When visiting Colon, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or conservative areas.
  • Always ask permission before taking photos of people or places of worship.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, including food and drink etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Colon

The best time to visit Colon is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and there is very little rain. However, keep in mind that this is also the busiest time of year, so expect crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colon

When visiting Colon, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including empanadas, sancocho, and tamales.
  • Take a boat tour of the Panama Canal.
  • Visit the historical landmarks and museums in Colon.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas or religious sites.
  • Take photos of people or places of worship without permission.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

While Colon is relatively safe for travelers, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Colon

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Exercise caution when swimming or snorkeling in the ocean, as there may be strong currents and riptides.

Health in Colon

  • Drink bottled water to avoid getting sick from tap water.
  • Use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to avoid mosquito bites and prevent diseases such as Zika and dengue fever.
  • Get vaccinated before traveling to Panama to protect against diseases such as hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Colon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Colon is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Colon

  • Tocumen International Airport: This is the main airport in Panama, located in Panama City, about an hour’s drive from Colon.

Bus Stations Near Colon

  • Albrook Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Panama City and offers frequent connections to Colon.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the countryside around Colon or visit other nearby destinations.
  • Private shuttle: Private shuttles are available from Panama City and other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Colon

The best way to get around in Colon is by car or taxi. However, if you’re on a budget, there are also bus options available. Keep in mind that roads in Colon can be narrow and winding, so it’s important to drive carefully and follow local traffic laws.

Where to Go Next from Colon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Colon, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Panama City: Located just an hour’s drive from Colon, Panama City is a vibrant metropolis with a rich history and plenty of things to do.
  • Bocas del Toro: This picturesque archipelago is located in the Caribbean Sea and offers stunning beaches, snorkeling, and nightlife.
  • San Blas Islands: These stunning islands are home to the Kuna indigenous people and offer a chance to experience a unique and fascinating culture.

Conclusion

Colon is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Panama. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or just a relaxing beach vacation, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant city. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Colon.
