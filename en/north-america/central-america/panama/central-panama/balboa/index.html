Introduction to Balboa

Located in central Panama, Balboa offers an authentic blend of cultural and natural wonders. Named after Vasco Núñez de Balboa, a Spanish explorer who discovered the Pacific Ocean, this charming town has a significant historical background.

Whether you are a history buff or adrenaline junkie, Balboa has something for everyone. From visiting museums and historical sites to hiking and biking, there’s no end to the adventures this area can offer.

List of Places to Visit in Balboa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Panama Canal Museum: Learn about the history of the Panama Canal and how it impacted Balboa’s growth.
  2. Casco Antiguo: Also known as Panama City’s Old Quarter, this UNESCO World Heritage site will take you back in time with its colonial architecture and narrow streets.
  3. San Jose Church: Adjacent to Casco Antiguo, this beautiful church is one of the oldest in Panama City, dating back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Balboa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ancon Hill: The highest point in Panama City, Ancon Hill offers panoramic views of the city and its surroundings.
  2. El Valle de Anton: A beautiful valley located two hours away from Balboa, this area offers hiking trails, hot springs, and a vibrant local market.
  3. Pipeline Road: Located in Soberanía National Park, this eight-mile trail offers birdwatching and wildlife sightings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balboa

  1. Culebra Cut Trail: An easy, 3-mile trail along the Panama Canal, offering views of the locks and ships passing by.
  2. Camino De Cruces National Park: A 6.3-mile trail that takes you through the rainforest and the Old Panama Road.
  3. Metropolitan Park: A 265-acre park within Panama City, offering hiking and biking trails for all levels.

Best Things to Do in Balboa: Activities and Tours

  1. Panama Canal Boat Tour: Take a boat tour of the Panama Canal and learn about its history and operation.
  2. Soberania National Park Guided Tour: A guided tour of the park with a naturalist guide, perfect for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.
  3. Casco Viejo Walking Tour: A walking tour of Panama City’s Old Quarter, with knowledgeable guides explaining the history and culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Balboa

Day 1 Visit Panama Canal Museum and take a Panama Canal Boat Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Balboa

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ceviche: A popular seafood dish made with raw fish or shellfish marinated in lime juice and spices.
  2. Panamanian Empanadas: Fried or baked pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  3. Arroz con Pollo: Chicken and rice dish cooked with vegetables and spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Ron Abuelo: A locally produced rum that’s perfect for cocktails.
  2. Seco Herrerano: The national drink of Panama, made from sugarcane and usually served with grapefruit.
  3. Chicha: A traditional drink made with fermented corn that’s popular in rural areas of Panama.

Where to Stay in Balboa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Bristol Panama: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Panama City with a rooftop pool and bar.
  2. Hostal Amador Familiar: A budget hostel located near the Amador Causeway with clean and comfortable rooms.
  3. OYO Hotel Riande Aeropuerto Tocumen: A hotel located near the Tocumen International Airport, perfect for layovers or early flights.

What to Buy in Balboa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Mola: Handmade textiles created by Kuna women, featuring vibrant colors and intricate designs.
  2. Panama Hat: A classy straw hat that’s perfect for any occasion.
  3. Coffee: Panama produces some of the best quality coffee in the world, so be sure to try and bring some home.

Local Culture and Customs of Balboa

Panama’s culture is a mix of Afro-Caribbean, indigenous, and Spanish influences. Family values, religion, and respect for others are essential elements of Panama’s culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Balboa

The best time to visit Balboa is between December and April when the weather is dry and sunny. However, keep in mind that this is the peak tourist season, so prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balboa

Do:

  1. Learn some basic Spanish phrases.
  2. Try local foods and drinks.
  3. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water.
  2. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or government buildings.
  3. Use drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Balboa

Balboa is generally safe, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s essential to take precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Balboa

No specific vaccines are required to visit Panama. However, it’s recommended to update routine vaccines like measles, mumps, and rubella. Also, make sure to drink bottled water and eat at reputable restaurants to avoid foodborne illness.

Getting to Balboa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balboa

  1. Tocumen International Airport (PTY) - The primary airport in Panama, located 20 miles away from Balboa.
  2. Albrook “Marcos A. Gelabert” International Airport (PAC) - A domestic airport located six miles from Balboa, with flights to other destinations in Panama.

Bus Stations Near Balboa

  1. Terminal de Transporte Nacional de Albrook - The central bus terminal in Panama City, located three miles from Balboa.
  2. Terminal de Transporte David - A bus terminal located in David, a city four hours away from Balboa.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Panama. However, keep in mind that traffic in Panama City can be intense.

How to Get Around in Balboa

Walking and biking are excellent ways to get around Balboa. Additionally, taxis and buses are readily available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Balboa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Boquete: A charming mountain town three hours away from Balboa, popular for coffee tours and hiking trails.
  2. Bocas del Toro: A group of islands located on the Caribbean coast, famous for snorkeling, surfing, and beaches.
  3. San Blas Islands: A series of over 300 islands located on the Caribbean coast, known for their crystal clear waters and cultural significance among the Kuna indigenous people.

Conclusion

Balboa offers a perfect mix of history, culture, and adventure. With so many attractions to explore, ranging from the Panama Canal to the rainforest trails and beaches, there’s no end to the adventures this charming town can offer. So, pack your bags and get ready to experience the best of Balboa, Panama!
