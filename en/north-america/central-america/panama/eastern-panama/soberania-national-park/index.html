Introduction to Soberanía National Park

Soberanía National Park is located in Eastern Panama, in Central America, and covers over 55,000 hectares of land. The park is a popular destination for naturalists, adventure-seekers, and anyone looking to immerse themselves in Panama’s rich culture and heritage. The park is located near the Panama Canal, making it a popular stop for cruise tourists.

The park is widely regarded as one of the most accessible tropical forests in the world, boasting diverse flora and fauna. Within the park, visitors can find over 525 species of birds, 105 species of mammals, and 79 species of reptiles. Additionally, the park offers numerous outdoor activities like hiking, biking, birdwatching, and kayaking.

List of Places to Visit in Soberanía National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Miraflores Locks

Miraflores Locks is a must-visit attraction located on the outskirts of the park. The locks provide a glimpse into Panama’s historical past and the construction of the Panama Canal. Visitors can explore the museum that takes visitors on a journey through the canal’s history, watch ships passing through the locks up close and personal, or have a meal with a view in the restaurant.

Old Gamboa Road

Old Gamboa Road is a hidden gem, showcasing the park’s wildlife and natural beauty. Hike down the abandoned road and appreciate the historical importance of the area, once a bustling town where canal employees, soldiers, and their families lived.

Pipeline Road

Pipeline Road is a significant part of the canopy birding circuit and is known to birdwatchers around the world. Located in the heart of Soberanía National Park, it is one of the best-known and most accessible places to spot rare birds found only in the area.

Natural Attractions of Soberanía National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Plantation Road

Plantation Road is a popular hiking trail in Soberanía that provides breathtaking views of the Chagres River and the surrounding forest. The trail is also home to numerous bird species and animals like sloths, monkeys, and snakes.

Aerial Tram

The Aerial Tram in Soberanía National Park provides visitors with a unique view of the park’s flora and fauna. The Tram is a unique, 1,200-meter long cableway ride, showcasing the flora and fauna of the area, an in depth introduction to Panama’s tropical ecosystem, and an opportunity for wildlife watching.

Monkey Island

Monkey Island offers visitors the opportunity to see Panama’s famous Capuchin monkeys in the wild. Visitors can take a guided kayaking tour, boat ride or speedboat tour of Gatun lake and surrounding islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soberanía National Park

Camino de Cruces Trail

The Camino de Cruces Trail is a 20-kilometer trail that runs through Soberanía National Park, connecting it to the Chagres River National Park. The trail is recognized for its historical significance and provides views of Panama City and the surrounding forests.

Plantation Road

As mentioned before, Plantation Road offers visitors stunning views of the Chagres River and the surrounding forest, and is a great option for both hikers and bikers.

Pipeline Road

Pipeline Road is the most famous trail in Soberanía National Park, covering approximately 17 kilometers of trail that meanders through the forest and provides ample opportunities to spot different species of birds and animals.

Best Things to Do in Soberanía National Park: Activities and Tours

Kayaking on the Chagres River

Kayaking on the Chagres River is a popular activity in Soberanía National Park. Visitors can hire a guide and kayak the river to see diverse wildlife like Capuchin monkeys, toucans, and jaguars.

Zip-Line Adventure

Get an adrenaline rush with a zip-line adventure through the forest canopy of Soberanía National Park. The tour offers a unique and exciting way to see the park’s beauty and wildlife. Visitors can also learn about the ecosystem and efforts to preserve it while enjoying a fun, family-friendly experience.

Birdwatching Tours

Soberanía National Park is a birdwatcher’s paradise, home to over 525 different bird species. Visitors can book a guided birdwatching tour to see rare bird species like the Harpy Eagle, a critically endangered bird of prey.

Week-Long Itinerary for Soberanía National Park

Day 1: Miraflores Locks

Take a morning tour of Miraflores Locks to learn about the history of the Panama Canal.

Day 2: Aerial Tram and Plantation Road

Experience the park’s flora and fauna by taking the Aerial Tram ride, and then hike Plantation Road to see diverse wildlife like Capuchin monkeys and sloths.

Day 3: Old Gamboa Road

Enjoy a hike on Old Gamboa Road to learn about the history of Soberanía National Park and appreciate the park’s natural beauty.

Day 4: Kayaking on the Chagres River

Take a guided tour on the Chagres River to see wildlife like toucans and Greater Ani birds.

Day 5: Pipeline Road

Spend the day exploring Pipeline Road to see the park’s rare and exotic bird species like the Keel-billed Toucan and the Scarlet Macaw.

Day 6: Zip-Line Adventure

Get a new perspective of the forest canopy by trying the zip-line adventure, an exhilarating experience for the whole family.

Day 7: Monkey Island

Take a boat ride to Monkey Island and end your week-long itinerary by observing Capuchin monkeys and other wildlife in their natural habitat.

Food and Drink Scene of Soberanía National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Sancocho

Sancocho is a traditional Panamanian soup made with chicken, yucca, corn, and vegetables. It is a hearty and flavorful dish usually served with white rice.

Tamales

Tamales are a corn-based dish filled with a mix of meats, vegetables, and spices. They are steamed in banana leaves and served with hot sauce.

Local Beverages

Seco Herrerano

Seco Herrerano is a clear distilled liquor made from sugar cane, and is a national drink of Panama. It is often served with ice and lime juice.

Chicha

Chicha is a non-alcoholic drink made from maize and spices. It is a popular beverage native to Panama and other Latin American countries.

Where to Stay in Soberanía National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gamboa Rainforest Resort

Gamboa Rainforest Resort is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Soberanía National Park, providing stunning views of the Chagres River and the surrounding forest. The resort provides a wide variety of activities and tours to explore the park.

Canopy Bed and Breakfast

Canopy Bed and Breakfast is a quaint and cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Soberanía National Park. The guesthouse provides a comfortable stay and is in close proximity to hiking trails and outdoor activities.

Hostal Gaia

Hostal Gaia is a budget-friendly option for backpackers or travelers looking for a more rustic experience. The hostel is located in a quiet area and provides easy access to hiking trails and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Soberanía National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wounaan Baskets

The Wounaan tribe of Panama creates beautiful woven baskets using local materials and unique designs.

Embera Art

The Embera people of Panama are known for their intricate wood carvings and intricate beadwork, showcasing Panama’s rich culture and traditional heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Soberanía National Park

Panama Canal

The Panama Canal is an important part of Panama’s economy and history. Visitors to the park can explore and learn about the canal’s history and construction.

Traditional Music

Panamanian traditional music is a mix of African, European, and Indigenous influences. Visitors can listen to traditional music at festivals and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Soberanía National Park

The best time to visit Soberanía National Park is during the dry season, from December to April. The dry season offers the best opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and other outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soberanía National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s natural habitat and wildlife.
  • Bring insect repellent and sunscreen.
  • Hire a guide for a more immersive experience.

Don’t:

  • Feed the animals or disturb their natural habitat.
  • Leave any trash or food in the park.
  • Enter restricted areas or disturb cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Soberanía National Park

Soberanía National Park is considered to be a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always advised to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Soberanía National Park

Visitors to Soberanía National Park should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations and may need additional vaccinations depending on their itinerary. Visitors should also wear long-sleeved clothing and use insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like Dengue fever or the Zika virus.

Getting to Soberanía National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Soberanía National Park

The closest international airport to Soberanía National Park is the Tocumen International Airport, located in Panama City.

Bus Stations Near Soberanía National Park

Several bus companies provide transportation to Soberanía National Park. From Panama City, visitors can take a bus to Gamboa and walk to the park’s entrance.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rental car to reach Soberanía National Park.

How to Get Around in Soberanía National Park

Visitors can explore the park by foot or by bike, but hiring a guide is recommended for a more immersive experience. There are also several tour operators that provide transportation and guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Soberanía National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Panama City

Panama City is a vibrant and modern city located just a short distance from the park. Visitors can explore the city’s historical landmarks, shopping centers, and nightlife.

Chagres River National Park

The Chagres River National Park is a wilderness area located just outside of Soberanía National Park. The park is home to the scenic Chagres River and diverse wildlife like panthers, jaguars, and tapirs.

Conclusion

Soberanía National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Panama. With stunning natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and cultural attractions, Soberanía National Park offers something for everyone. Whether you are a nature lover, adventure-seeker, or history buff, let this guide help you plan an unforgettable trip.
