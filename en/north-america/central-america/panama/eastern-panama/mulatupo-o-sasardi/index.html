Introduction to Mulatupo o Sasardi

Mulatupo o Sasardi is a small village located in Eastern Panama, a region known for its lush rainforests, picturesque beaches, and vibrant culture. Visitors to this hidden gem can immerse themselves in the local way of life, enjoy outdoor adventures, and explore historical attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Mulatupo o Sasardi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mulatupo o Sasardi Museum: Founded in 1975, this museum is dedicated to preserving the culture and history of the indigenous Embera people. Visitors can learn about traditional crafts, music, dance, and rituals.

  • Sacred Cemeteries: The Embera people have a deep spiritual connection to their ancestors and the natural world. Visitors can visit sacred cemeteries, where they can see intricately crafted tombs and learn about traditional burial practices.

Natural Attractions of Mulatupo o Sasardi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mulatupo o Sasardi Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is located just a short hike from the village. Visitors can take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear water and enjoy the surrounding rainforest.

  • Beaches: The nearby beaches of Mulatupo o Sasardi are some of the most beautiful in Panama. Visitors can relax on the white sandy shores or go snorkeling among the colorful coral reefs.

  • Rainforest Hiking Trails: With its rich biodiversity and abundant wildlife, the rainforest surrounding Mulatupo o Sasardi is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers. Visitors can explore the jungle on guided hikes and see rare animal species such as howler monkeys and toucans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mulatupo o Sasardi

  • The Embera Trail: This 3-day trek takes visitors through the heart of the Embera territory, where they can discover hidden waterfalls, visit remote villages, and learn about the traditional life of the Embera people.

  • The Coastal Trail: This scenic trail takes visitors along the stunning Pacific coast, through deserted beaches, and across the lush rainforest. Biking enthusiasts will love the challenge of the steep climbs and thrilling descents.

Best Things to Do in Mulatupo o Sasardi: Activities and Tours

  • Embera Village Visits: Visitors can take guided tours of Embera villages and experience traditional life firsthand. They can learn about indigenous crafts, music, and dance, and sample authentic dishes.

  • Kayaking Tours: The calm waters surrounding Mulatupo o Sasardi are perfect for kayaking. Visitors can paddle through the mangroves and spot wildlife, or explore the nearby islands and beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Mulatupo o Sasardi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz con Coco: This traditional dish combines coconut rice with fresh fish and plantains.

  • Sancocho: This hearty stew is made with chicken, yucca, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chicha: This fermented corn drink is a staple of Embera culture.

  • Ron Abuelo: This locally produced rum is smooth and flavorful.

Where to Stay in Mulatupo o Sasardi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Venta Dorada: This family-owned guesthouse offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and guided tours.

  • Eco-Lodges: Visitors can stay in one of the many eco-lodges in the area, where they can enjoy the natural surroundings and learn about sustainable tourism.

What to Buy in Mulatupo o Sasardi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woven Baskets: The Embera are famous for their intricate basket weaving. Visitors can buy beautiful baskets in a variety of sizes and styles.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: The Embera make stunning jewelry using colorful beads and natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Mulatupo o Sasardi

The Embera people have a deep respect for nature and believe in maintaining a harmonious relationship with the environment. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, ask for permission before taking photographs, and buy local products to support the local economy.

Best Time to Visit Mulatupo o Sasardi

The best time to visit Mulatupo o Sasardi is during the dry season, from December to April. During this time, visitors can enjoy sunny days and comfortable temperatures. However, it’s important to note that the area can be humid and rainy year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mulatupo o Sasardi

  • Buy local products to support the local economy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mulatupo o Sasardi

Mulatupo o Sasardi is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take common sense precautions such as not walking alone at night.

Health in Mulatupo o Sasardi

Visitors should visit their doctor before traveling to Mulatupo o Sasardi to get up-to-date on their vaccinations and discuss any health concerns. The area is at risk for mosquito-borne diseases such as Zika and dengue fever, so visitors should take precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Mulatupo o Sasardi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mulatupo o Sasardi

The nearest airport to Mulatupo o Sasardi is Tocumen International Airport in Panama City, which is approximately 5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Mulatupo o Sasardi

Visitors can take a bus from Panama City to Chepo, then transfer to another bus that goes to Mulatupo o Sasardi.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to Mulatupo o Sasardi.

How to Get Around in Mulatupo o Sasardi

The best way to get around Mulatupo o Sasardi is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a local guide to take them on tours and trips to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Mulatupo o Sasardi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Panama City: The capital city of Panama is just a few hours away by car or bus. Visitors can explore the historic Casco Viejo district, visit the Panama Canal, and shop for souvenirs.

  • San Blas Islands: The stunning San Blas Islands are just a short boat ride from Mulatupo o Sasardi. Visitors can relax on the pristine beaches, swim in crystal-clear waters, and learn about the indigenous Kuna people.
