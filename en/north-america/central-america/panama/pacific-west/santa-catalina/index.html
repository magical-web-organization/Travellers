Introduction to Santa Catalina

Santa Catalina is a hidden gem located in the Pacific West region of Panama, Central America, and North America. It is known for its pristine beaches, world-class surf breaks, untouched natural beauty, and rich biodiversity. The small fishing village attracts tourists all year round who come to explore and experience the laid-back lifestyle and adventurous activities offered in this destination. In this travel guide, you will find everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Santa Catalina.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Catalina

Below, you will find a list of cultural and natural attractions to visit in Santa Catalina:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iglesia de Santa Catalina de Siena: This small church is located in the center of the town.

Natural Attractions of Santa Catalina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa Santa Catalina: This beach is an ideal place for swimming, lounging, and taking in the beautiful views.
  • Coiba National Park: Located off the Pacific coast of Panama, this park is an ecological paradise and World Heritage Site that offers snorkeling, diving, hiking, and birdwatching.
  • Isla Cebaco: A beautiful and secluded island with stunning beaches and wildlife, perfect for scuba diving and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Catalina

  • La Cresta: This ridge hike offers panoramic views of the ocean, town, and mountains. It’s a 2-hour round trip hike.

Best Things to Do in Santa Catalina: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Santa Catalina is known as one of the best surf spots in Panama, with consistent waves year-round.
  • Fishing: Sport fish for marlin, sailfish, and tuna in the waters surrounding Santa Catalina.
  • Kayaking: Paddle through the mangrove forests around Santa Catalina and explore the nearby islands.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Experience the rich marine life of Coiba National Park through scuba diving and snorkeling tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Catalina

Santa Catalina offers a variety of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: A dish made of raw fish cured in citrus juices, served with onions, cilantro, and avocado.
  • Arroz con pollo: Rice cooked with chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Abuelo: A popular brand of rum originating from Panama.
  • Tropical Fruit Juices: A refreshing drink made from fresh tropical fruits.

Where to Stay in Santa Catalina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Santa Catalina Panama: A beachfront hotel with private balconies and an outdoor pool.
  • Casa Maya Hostel: A relaxed hostel with a communal kitchen.
  • Sol y Mar Eco Lodge: A lodge surrounded by jungle, perfect for nature lovers.

What to Buy in Santa Catalina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tapestries and Molas: Handmade textiles made by local indigenous people.
  • Panama Hats: A traditional hat made from toquilla straw, originating from Ecuador but commonly sold in Panama.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Catalina

  • Language: Spanish is the official language of Panama.
  • Religion: The majority of the population in Panama are Roman Catholics.
  • Dress Code: Dress casually and modestly in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Santa Catalina

The best time to visit Santa Catalina is during the dry season, from December to April. The temperature during this time ranges from 25°C to 30°C, and the weather is sunny and warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Catalina

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Bring suitable clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Engage in illegal activities, including drug use and trafficking.
  • Disrespect locals or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Catalina

Santa Catalina is a safe destination. However, travelers should always take precautions to ensure their safety, such as not carrying valuable items in public areas and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Santa Catalina

Medical facilities in Santa Catalina are limited. Travelers are advised to bring any necessary medication with them, including for mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Santa Catalina: Transportation Options and Logistics

The following are transportation options to get to Santa Catalina:

Airports Near Santa Catalina

The closest international airport is Tocumen International Airport in Panama City.

Bus Stations Near Santa Catalina

The bus stands are located in Santiago and Sona. From Santiago, you will take a bus to Sona and transfer to a bus headed for Santa Catalina. Alternatively, you can hire a private transfer to Santa Catalina from Santiago.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for transportation.

How to Get Around in Santa Catalina

Walking or biking are the best ways to get around Santa Catalina. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Santa Catalina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Panama City: The capital of Panama, and a vibrant city filled with rich culture and history.
  • Boquete: A mountain town known for its coffee, amazing scenery, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Santa Catalina is a beautiful and unique destination in Panama, with its pristine beaches, unspoiled natural beauty, and charming local culture. Whether you are looking for adventure or relaxation, Santa Catalina has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty of this Central American gem.
