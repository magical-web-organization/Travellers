Introduction to Pacific West

Located in the western region of Panama, Pacific West offers an ultimate combination of natural beauty and cultural richness. With its pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and charming colonial towns, Pacific West attracts travelers from all over the world. From adventurous outdoor activities to culturally significant attractions, Pacific West offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we explore the best of Pacific West, including places to visit, things to do, and local customs and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Pacific West

Pacific West is a foodie’s paradise, offering a range of delicious local dishes and refreshing beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sancocho: a hearty soup made with chicken, vegetables, and yuca.
  2. Empanadas: crispy fried dough filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  3. Arroz con guandu: rice and pigeon peas cooked in coconut milk.
  4. Ceviche: fish or shrimp marinated in lime juice and spices.
  5. Chicheme: a creamy sweet corn beverage.

Local Beverages

  1. Seco Herrerano: a clear spirit made from sugar cane.
  2. Ron Abuelo: Panamanian rum.
  3. Balboa beer: a refreshing local beer.
  4. Chicha Fuerte: a fermented corn drink.
  5. Coffee: Panama is known for its high-quality coffee.

Local Culture and Customs of Pacific West

Panamanians are warm and friendly people, and the country has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its diverse ethnic makeup. Here are some local customs and cultural practices to be aware of:

  1. Greetings: handshakes and hugs are common forms of greetings.
  2. Respect for elders: Panamanians hold great respect for their elders, and it is customary to address them with a title such as “abuelo” or “abuela”.
  3. Siesta: many businesses shut down for a few hours in the afternoon to allow for a midday nap or relaxation.
  4. Dress code: Panamanians tend to dress formally for social events or business meetings.
  5. Spanish language: Spanish is the official language of Panama, and English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit Pacific West

The best time to visit Pacific West is during the dry season from December to April. During this period, the weather is pleasant, and the rain is infrequent. However, if you are looking for lower hotel rates, the rainy season from May to November is a good option.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pacific West

Here are some helpful tips on things to do and avoid when visiting Pacific West:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and culture.
  2. Carry a copy of your passport with you at all times.
  3. Wear sunscreen and insect repellent.
  4. Keep your valuables secure.
  5. Try local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water.
  2. Wear revealing clothing in places of worship.
  3. Take photos of people without asking first.
  4. Leave your belongings unattended.
  5. Buy souvenirs made from endangered species.

Safety and Health

Panama is a safe country for tourists, but petty crime such as pickpocketing and theft can occur in tourist areas. Here are some tips on staying safe and healthy in Pacific West:

Safety in Pacific West

  1. Keep your valuables secure.
  2. Avoid walking in deserted areas at night.
  3. Stay in well-lit areas.
  4. Use a registered taxi or reputable transportation service.
  5. Be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Pacific West

  1. Drink bottled water.
  2. Wear insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  3. Use sunscreen to prevent sunburn.
  4. Check with your doctor for vaccinations recommended for Panama.
  5. Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness.
