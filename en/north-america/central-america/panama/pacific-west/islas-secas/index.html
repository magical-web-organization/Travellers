Introduction to Islas Secas

Islas Secas is a beautiful archipelago made up of 16 islands located in the Pacific West of Panama. Established in 2019 as an eco-resort, this pristine destination has rapidly become a dream paradise for travelers around the world seeking an immaculate, private, and all-inclusive experience. Islas Secas is far from busy cities and hectic crowds, an ideal place for relaxation and rejuvenation. Travelers can expect deep-sea fishing, snorkeling, cultural tours, beachcombing, and more amid an unspoiled environment.

List of Places to Visit in Islas Secas

Islas Secas offers various activities and attractions for travelers. Whether it’s getting a glimpse of local culture and customs or appreciating the natural wonders of the archipelago, there’s always something to see and do on your trip. Here are the top places to visit in Islas Secas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Islas Secas is rich with history and culture. Travelers can learn more about the archipelago’s roots by visiting museums and historical places such as:

  • Chanquín: A village that preserves the pre-Columbian and colonial history of the island.
  • Cebaco Island: An uninhabited island that was once used as a hideout for pirates.

Natural Attractions of Islas Secas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Islas Secas is blessed with natural beauty. With its crystal-clear waters, exotic wildlife, and lush vegetation, travelers can enjoy a variety of adventures and exploration options. Here are the must-visit sites:

  • Iguana Island: An uninhabited island with a unique habitat that supports a large population of iguanas.
  • Cala Mia Nature Reserve: A private reserve boasting an abundance of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Islas Secas

Islas Secas is the perfect place for hikers and bikers who like to explore the natural beauty of the forest and enjoy scenic views. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking trails in Islas Secas:

  • Cerro Picacho Trail: A challenging hike that takes you to the highest peak on the island, with stunning views of the surrounding archipelago.
  • Chiriqui Grande Trail: A popular route for biking enthusiasts that runs through hilly terrain and diverse landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Islas Secas: Activities and Tours

Islas Secas offers many activities and tours for travelers of all ages. Here are some of the popular things to do in Islas Secas:

  • Deep-Sea Fishing: Boasting a variety of fish, this is a popular activity for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Snorkeling: The crystal-clear waters of Islas Secas are perfect for exploring underwater life and coral reefs.
  • Kayaking: If you prefer a more active activity, kayaking is a great way to explore small islands and nearby beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Islas Secas

The food and drink scene in Islas Secas is influenced by its maritime culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Islas Secas:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sancocho: A classic Panamanian soup with chicken, yucca, corn, and vegetables.
  • Ceviche: A traditional dish of raw fish marinated in lemon juice and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Cortez: A popular rum made in Panama using local sugar cane.
  • Seco: A distilled liquor made from sugar cane that is flavored with ginger or fruits like passion fruit and pineapple.

Where to Stay in Islas Secas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Islas Secas has various accommodations to suit any traveler’s preferences. Here are the top places to stay in Islas Secas:

  • Islas Secas Resort: An exclusive, all-inclusive eco-resort that offers a luxurious stay on a private island.
  • Cala Mia Boutique Resort: A boutique resort that offers private villas with stunning sea views.
  • Boca Brava Island Resort: Another eco-resort located in neighboring Boca Brava Island, providing access to its natural attractions.

What to Buy in Islas Secas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers can buy various souvenirs and local products to bring home from Islas Secas. Here are some of the best things to purchase.

  • Molas: Traditional hand-made textiles made by the Kuna indigenous community in Panama.
  • Panama Hat: A straw hat that has become synonymous with Panama.

Local Culture and Customs of Islas Secas

Panama’s culture is a diverse blend of Indigenous, Spanish, African, and American influences. Islas Secas has retained much of its cultural heritage, some of which is celebrated through its history, cuisine, and religion. Travelers can expect a warm and welcoming atmosphere, where the locals are hospitable and eager to share their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Islas Secas

Islas Secas is an all-year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between December and April when rainfall is minimal and humidity is low. However, travelers should always check the weather forecast and plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Islas Secas

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Islas Secas:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor adventures.
  • Stay hydrated and use sunblock.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrupt the natural habitat or wildlife.
  • Wear swimwear outside of designated areas.

Safety and Health

Travelers planning a trip to Islas Secas should take the necessary safety and health precautions needed when visiting a tropical destination.

Safety in Islas Secas

  • Use caution when swimming or participating in water sports.
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended.
  • Always follow the instructions of trained professionals when participating in organized tours or activities.

Health in Islas Secas

  • Use insect repellant to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Drink bottled or purified water only.
  • Bring prescription medication for any pre-existing medical conditions.

Getting to Islas Secas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to get to Islas Secas:

Airports Near Islas Secas

  • Tocumen International Airport: Located in Panama City, this is the primary airport for international flights.
  • Enrique Malek International Airport: Located in David, this is the closest airport to Islas Secas.

Bus Stations Near Islas Secas

  • Albrook Bus Terminal: Located in Panama City, this is the primary bus terminal for long-distance travel within the country.
  • David Terminal: Located in David, this terminal provides transportation to surrounding areas and cities.

Other Options

  • Boat: Travelers can also take private boats or chartered boats to Islas Secas.

How to Get Around in Islas Secas

Travelers can explore Islas Secas by boat, bike, or on foot. Since most of the archipelago is not heavily populated, there are limited options for public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Islas Secas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby places to visit and excursion options from Islas Secas:

  • Panama City: The capital and largest city of Panama, located 370 kilometers east of Islas Secas.
  • Santa Catalina: A small fishing village located west of Islas Secas, known for its surfing and diving opportunities.

Conclusion

Islas Secas is a one-of-a-kind destination for travelers looking for a retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its pristine beaches, abundance of wildlife, and rich cultural heritage, Islas Secas is undoubtedly an excellent place to visit. By following our travel guide, you can take full advantage of what the archipelago has to offer and leave with unforgettable memories.
