Introduction to Changuinola

Changuinola is a city located in the northwestern part of Panama, in the province of Bocas del Toro. It is a small town that is known for its beautiful outdoor destinations, cultural sites, and vibrant local customs. This tropical paradise is home to a variety of activities that allow visitors to take in the local culture, enjoy the stunning scenery, and explore the natural wonders of the region. In this ultimate guide, we will explore the best things to do and see in Changuinola, including outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, food and drink scenes, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Changuinola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Changuinola is full of cultural treasures, including historic buildings, museums, and cultural centers. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Changuinola:

  • Museo del Bano: This small museum is dedicated to the history of the bathroom. It showcases a variety of artifacts, including antique toilets, sinks, and bathtubs.

  • Changuinola Library and Cultural Center: This center houses a library, an art gallery, and a cultural museum. It features exhibits on local history, culture, and art.

  • Iglesia de San Juan Bautista Changuinola: This historic church dates back to the early 20th century and is known for its unique architectural style. It features beautiful stained glass windows and intricate details.

Natural Attractions of Changuinola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main draws of Changuinola is its beautiful natural scenery. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Changuinola:

  • Parque Nacional La Amistad: This national park is located on the border between Panama and Costa Rica. It features a diverse range of ecosystems, including rainforest, cloud forest, and high-altitude paramo. It is known for its rich biodiversity and stunning scenery.

  • Rio Changuinola: This river runs through the heart of Changuinola and is a popular destination for kayaking and rafting. It passes through the stunning Changuinola Valley and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Isla Bastimentos National Marine Park: This national park is located on the nearby island of Bastimentos and features a diverse range of marine ecosystems. Visitors can go snorkeling, diving, or take a boat tour to explore the park’s coral reefs and beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Changuinola

Changuinola is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes in Changuinola:

  • Sendero El Pianista: This hiking trail follows the Changuinola River and offers spectacular views of the surrounding valley. It is a moderately difficult hike that is approximately 9 kilometers long.

  • Bocas del Toro Bicycle Loop: This scenic bike route takes visitors through the beautiful Bocas del Toro archipelago. It is approximately 14 kilometers long and offers stunning views of the region’s beaches and rainforests.

Best Things to Do in Changuinola: Activities and Tours

Changuinola offers a variety of activities and tours that allow visitors to experience the best of the region’s natural and cultural attractions. Here are some of the top things to do in Changuinola:

  • Kayaking and Rafting: The Changuinola River is a popular destination for kayaking and rafting. Visitors can take a guided tour or rent equipment to explore the river on their own.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Changuinola is located near some of the best snorkeling and diving destinations in Panama, including Isla Bastimentos National Marine Park and Zapatilla Cays.

Food and Drink Scene of Changuinola

Must-Try Dishes

Changuinola is known for its delicious and unique cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes in Changuinola:

  • Sancocho: This hearty stew is made with chicken, yucca, plantains, and other vegetables. It is a popular dish in Panama and is especially popular in Changuinola.

  • Arroz con Pollo: This classic rice dish is made with chicken, vegetables, and spices. It is a staple of Panamanian cuisine and can be found in many local restaurants in Changuinola.

  • Rondon: This traditional seafood soup is made with coconut milk, vegetables, and various species of fish or shellfish. It is a popular dish in the Caribbean region and is a must-try in Changuinola.

Local Beverages

Changuinola is also known for its unique and refreshing beverages. Here are some local drinks you should try in Changuinola:

  • Chicha: This traditional drink is made from fermented corn and is popular throughout Central and South America. It has a slightly sweet taste and is often served in local restaurants and bars in Changuinola.

  • Ron Ponche: This sweet and creamy rum-based drink is a popular dessert drink in Panama. It is made with rum, condensed milk, egg yolks, and spices and is often served during the holiday season.

  • Jugo de Soursop: This refreshing fruit juice is made from the soursop fruit, which is native to Central America. It has a sweet and tangy flavor and is commonly served in local restaurants and cafes in Changuinola.

Local Culture and Customs of Changuinola

Changuinola is home to a vibrant and diverse culture that is influenced by its indigenous roots as well as its colonial past. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting Changuinola:

  • Language: Spanish is the official language of Panama, but many people in Changuinola also speak English and Ngäbe, the indigenous language of the Ngäbe people.

  • Festivals and Celebrations: Changuinola is home to a variety of festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including the Ngäbe Buglé Carnival and the Festival del Mar.

  • Indigenous Culture: Changuinola is home to the Ngäbe people, one of Panama’s largest indigenous groups. Visitors can learn about their traditions, customs, and beliefs at local cultural centers and museums.

Best Time to Visit Changuinola

The best time to visit Changuinola is from December to April, which is considered the dry season. The rainy season starts in May and lasts until November, which can make some areas difficult to access.
