Introduction to Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands is a hidden gem that can be found in the St. Lawrence River, which flows from the Great Lakes to the Atlantic Ocean, marking the border between the United States and Canada. It is an archipelago containing over 1,800 islands, and it is known for its scenic views, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions. Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or simply seeking a serene escape, Thousand Islands is a must-visit destination that offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Thousand Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history enthusiast, then Thousand Islands has plenty to offer. From museums to historical landmarks, here are some of the must-visit spots:

  • Boldt Castle: A grand 120-room mansion that was built by George C. Boldt, the proprietor of the Waldorf Astoria Hotel. The castle offers tours that introduce visitors to the history and architecture of the building.
  • Antique Boat Museum: A museum that is dedicated to preserving the history of boating in the Thousand Islands region. It features over 300 boats ranging from canoes to yachts, and hosts numerous exhibits and events throughout the year.
  • Thousand Islands Arts Center: An art center that showcases the work of local artists and offers hands-on classes and workshops for visitors of all ages.
  • Clayton Island Tours: A company that offers boat tours of the Thousand Islands, highlighting the region’s beauty and history.

Natural Attractions of Thousand Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its rich cultural heritage, Thousand Islands is also known for its natural beauty. Here are some of the best places to enjoy outdoor activities and scenic views:

  • Wellesley Island State Park: The largest state park in the region, with over 2,500 acres of natural beauty. It offers camping, hiking, fishing, and boating opportunities for visitors to explore.
  • Thousand Islands Bridge: A bridge that connects the United States and Canada, and offers breathtaking views of the St. Lawrence River.
  • Thousand Islands National Park: The only national park in the region, it is home to a wide array of flora and fauna, as well as the Dark Sky Preserve, which provides the perfect spot for stargazing.
  • Alexandria Bay: A charming village that is known for its scenic boat tours of the Thousand Islands, as well as the annual Pirate Festival that takes place in August.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers, with numerous routes that offer stunning views of the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Keewaydin State Park: A state park that is ideal for hiking and cycling, with trails that wind through the forest and along the river.
  • Minna Anthony Common Nature Center: A nature center that offers a variety of hiking trails through the woods and along the waterfront.
  • Thousand Islands International Bridge: Offering both pedestrian and bike paths, the bridge is an ideal path for those who wish to explore the border between the United States and Canada on foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Thousand Islands: Activities and Tours

Thousand Islands offers a diverse range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Fishing: Thousand Islands is renowned for its excellent fishing opportunities, with anglers coming from all around the world to test their skills.
  • Scenic cruises: Take a boat tour around the islands and enjoy the breathtaking scenery that Thousand Islands has to offer.
  • Kayaking: Explore Thousand Islands’ secluded coves and inlets by kayak for a unique perspective of the region’s natural beauty.
  • Golfing: With plenty of golf courses to choose from, Thousand Islands is a golfer’s paradise.
  • Wine tasting: Thousand Islands is home to several wineries that offer guided tours and tasting, providing a unique experience for tourists that are lovers of fine wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thousand Islands

To get the best out of your visit to Thousand Islands, why not spend a week in the region? Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit:

Day 1: Arrival and Orientation

Spend your first day exploring the region and getting your bearings. Take a boat tour to get a feel of the area and visit the visitor center to learn about the region’s history.

Day 2: Boldt Castle and Nearby Sights

Spend the day exploring Boldt Castle and the surrounding area, including the Boldt Yacht House and the Heart Island.

Day 3: Outdoor Adventures in Wellesley Island State Park

Spend a day in the outdoors by exploring Wellesley Island State Park, which offers hiking, fishing, and boating opportunities.

Day 4: Antique Boat Museum and Clayton Island Tours

Visit the Antique Boat Museum to learn about the history of boating in the region, and take a boat tour with Clayton Island Tours for a scenic view of the Thousand Islands.

Day 5: Alexandria Bay

Spend a day in Alexandria Bay, exploring the village, visiting shops, and enjoying the Pirate Festival.

Day 6: Wine Tasting and Golfing

Visit some of Thousand Islands’ wineries for a wine tasting experience, followed by a round of golf at one of the region’s golf courses.

Day 7: Departure

Take some time to soak up the last of the region’s beauty before departing back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands offers a diverse range of dining options that cater to different tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Poutine: A Canadian classic, fries drowning in gravy and topped with cheese curds. A fan favorite in the region.
  • Seafood Chowder: With the Thousand Islands being a major fishing hub, it’s no surprise that seafood chowders and other seafood dishes feature prominently in local menus.
  • Beef on Weck: A sandwich unique to the region, with roast beef piled high on a salted bun and served with Au Jus.

Local Beverages

  • Thousand Islands Wineries: The region is home to several wineries, which produce high-quality wines that cater to different palates.
  • Craft Breweries: From ales to lagers, the Thousand Islands’ craft breweries offer a wide range of beers.

Where to Stay in Thousand Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thousand Islands offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit different needs and budgets. Whether you are looking for a cozy guesthouse or a luxury hotel, there is something for everyone in the region. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Riveredge Resort: A waterfront resort that offers luxurious amenities such as a pool, spa, and fine dining, and is located close to Boldt Castle.
  • The Clayton Inn: A charming and cozy guesthouse, located in the heart of Clayton village.
  • Island View Inn: A family-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.

What to Buy in Thousand Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thousand Islands offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or personal mementos. Here are some ideas:

  • Thousand Islands hot sauce: Locally made hot sauces that combine sweet and spicy flavors and are perfect for those who love heat.
  • Local Art and Crafts: From pottery to paintings, the Thousand Islands has an abundance of local art and crafts that reflect the region’s natural beauty and history.
  • Thousand Islands Beer: A perfect gift for beer lovers, the Thousand Islands’ breweries offer unique and flavorful beers that are perfect souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands’ culture and heritage are deeply rooted in the region’s history and geography. Some customs and traditions that are unique to the area include:

  • The Pirate Festival: A festival held annually in Alexandria Bay, which celebrates the region’s pirate history and is a family-friendly event, featuring pirate reenactments, treasure hunting, and balloon festivals.
  • Fishing: Thousand Islands’ fishing culture is deeply embedded in the region’s history, and many of its residents and visitors come to fish for a wide variety of species.

Best Time to Visit Thousand Islands

The best time to visit Thousand Islands is between May through October, when the weather is mild, and the region is buzzing with activity. The summer months are particularly popular, as the region offers a wide range of outdoor activities and events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thousand Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Damage the natural environment by littering or engaging in other harmful activities.
  • Ignore safety warnings when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands is known for its safety, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, such as wearing appropriate safety gear when cycling or hiking, and following safety guidelines when boating or fishing.

Health in Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands has good medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, visitors should carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medication with them, and be aware of common health risks such as sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Thousand Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands has several airports nearby, including Watertown International Airport and Syracuse Hancock International Airport, both of which offer connecting flights to major cities in the United States and Canada.

Bus Stations Near Thousand Islands

There are several bus services that operate in the Thousand Islands region, including Greyhound and Trailways, which offer connections to major cities such as New York City and Toronto.

Other Options

Thousand Islands can also be reached by car, with major highways providing easy access to the region.

How to Get Around in Thousand Islands

Thousand Islands offers several transportation options to help visitors explore the region, including car rentals, bike rentals, and boat rentals.

Where to Go Next from Thousand Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thousand Islands is an ideal base from which to explore neighboring areas and attractions, including:

  • Adirondacks: A mountain range and natural park in upstate New York that is known for its hiking and skiing trails.
  • Ottawa: The capital city of Canada, and a major cultural and historical hub.
  • Montreal: A vibrant and cosmopolitan city that is renowned for its food and nightlife.

Conclusion

Thousand Islands is a hidden gem that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. By following this guide, visitors can experience the best that the region has to offer, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
