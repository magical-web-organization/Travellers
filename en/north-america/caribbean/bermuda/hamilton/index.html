Introduction to Hamilton

Located in the Caribbean paradise of Bermuda, Hamilton is the capital city of the island. Despite being small in size, Hamilton has plenty to offer visitors, from stunning natural attractions to impressive cultural landmarks. With warm weather and friendly locals, Hamilton offers a unique vacation experience that is perfect for travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Hamilton

With so much to see and do in Hamilton, it can be overwhelming to plan your itinerary. Here are some of our top picks for places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Bermuda National Gallery: This museum hosts a collection of Bermudian artwork, including paintings, sculpture, and photography.

  2. The Royal Navy Dockyard: This historic site offers tours of old military structures, a maritime museum, and a craft market.

  3. Fort Hamilton: Built in the 19th century, this fort offers panoramic views of the city and harbor, as well as exhibitions on Bermuda’s military history.

Natural Attractions of Hamilton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Bermuda Botanical Gardens: This 36-acre park boasts an impressive collection of tropical flora and fauna. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll or enjoy a picnic in the gardens.

  2. Horseshoe Bay Beach: This pink-sand beach is a must-visit attraction in Hamilton. The crystal-clear waters and soft sand make it a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.

  3. Crystal Caves: These underground caves are a geological marvel, featuring brilliantly colored stalactites and stalagmites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hamilton

For those who enjoy hiking or cycling, Hamilton offers some fantastic routes that showcase the city’s natural beauty.

  1. Railway Trail: This 18-mile trail winds through some of the island’s most stunning landscapes. Visitors can rent a bike or hike along the trail to enjoy beautiful views.

  2. South Shore Park: This park offers several hiking trails and a scenic biking route along the coastline. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the ocean and rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Hamilton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there are plenty of things to do in Hamilton. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Catamaran Sailing: Explore the clear waters of the Caribbean on a catamaran sail. Visitors can take a guided tour or charter their own boat to enjoy the beautiful scenery.

  2. Walking Tours: Guided walking tours of the city offer visitors a chance to explore Hamilton’s rich history and culture.

  3. Scuba Diving: The clear waters surrounding Bermuda offer some of the best diving spots in the world. Visitors can take a guided tour or rent diving equipment and explore on their own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hamilton

To make the most of your Hamilton vacation, we recommend the following itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Hamilton

Bermuda’s cuisine is a blend of flavors from around the world, including influences from African, Caribbean, and European cultures. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish Chowder: A traditional Bermudian soup made with fish, vegetables, and spices.

  2. Spiny Lobster: A local delicacy, spiny lobster is often prepared grilled or as a creamy lobster bisque.

  3. Bermuda Fishcake: Made with salted cod, potato, and local seasonings, this dish is a staple of Bermudian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Dark and Stormy: A classic Bermudian cocktail made with ginger beer and dark rum.

  2. Swizzle: A fruity cocktail made with rum, fruit juices, and bitters.

  3. Gombey Smash: A refreshing drink made with rum, mint, fruit juices, and ginger beer.

Where to Stay in Hamilton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hamilton has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  1. The Fairmont Hamilton Princess: This luxury hotel offers stunning ocean views and a range of amenities, including a pool, spa, and multiple on-site restaurants.

  2. Oxford House: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of the city, Oxford House offers cozy rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

  3. Aunt Nea’s Inn: This historic property offers a range of accommodation options, including private cottages and suites in a charming garden setting.

What to Buy in Hamilton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Bermuda, here are some local products and souvenirs to consider:

  1. Bermuda Rum: Made with local sugar cane, Bermuda rum is a popular souvenir and can be purchased at local liquor stores.

  2. Bermuda Shorts: A classic Bermuda fashion item, these colorful shorts can be found in many local clothing stores.

  3. Handmade Crafts: Bermuda has a rich tradition of crafting, and visitors can find a range of handmade items, including pottery, jewelry, and wooden carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Hamilton

Bermudian culture is a vibrant mix of African, British, and Caribbean influences. Visitors can expect to encounter friendly locals who take pride in their island’s history and traditions. English is the official language, and the currency is the Bermudian dollar.

Best Time to Visit Hamilton

The best time to visit Hamilton is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, so visitors should expect larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hamilton

Do:

  1. Do try the local cuisine and drinks.

  2. Do bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

  3. Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t wear beachwear in public places.

  2. Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  3. Don’t forget to pack proper attire for visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hamilton

Bermuda is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Hamilton

There are no major health risks in Bermuda. Visitors should ensure that their routine vaccines are up to date and bring any necessary prescription medication with them.

Getting to Hamilton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hamilton

The L.F. Wade International Airport is located just a short drive from Hamilton and offers daily flights to destinations throughout North America and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Hamilton

The Hamilton bus terminal is located in the city center and offers bus service to destinations throughout the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Bermuda by cruise ship or private yacht.

How to Get Around in Hamilton

Walking and cycling are the best ways to get around Hamilton, with several rental options available. Taxis and buses are also available for longer trips or for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Hamilton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bermuda offers several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring, including:

  1. St. George’s: This historic town is located on the northeastern tip of the island and has a range of historic buildings, museums, and quaint shops.

  2. Gibbs Hill Lighthouse: This iconic lighthouse is located in Southampton Parish and offers stunning views of the island and ocean.

  3. Somerset Village: This charming village is located on the western end of the island and offers quiet beaches and a range of accommodation options.

Conclusion

Hamilton is a hidden gem in the heart of the Caribbean, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. With plenty to see and do, visitors are sure to create lasting memories in this island paradise.
