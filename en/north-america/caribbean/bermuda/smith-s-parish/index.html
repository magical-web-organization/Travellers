Introduction to Smith’s Parish

Located in Bermuda, Smith’s Parish is a destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you are interested in exploring the natural beauty of the outdoors, learning more about the local culture and history, or simply relaxing at a beachside resort, Smith’s Parish has it all.

This comprehensive guide to Smith’s Parish will help you plan your trip, whether it is for a weekend or a week. From the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and what to eat, our guide has everything you need to know to make the most of your time in Smith’s Parish.

List of Places to Visit in Smith’s Parish

Smith’s Parish is home to a number of culturally important attractions, as well as natural wonders that offer breathtaking scenery and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-see attractions in Smith’s Parish is the Verdmont Museum, which dates back to the 18th century and is a prime example of the Georgian architecture that is prevalent throughout Bermuda. Another popular museum is the Bermuda Aquarium, Museum and Zoo, where you can see a variety of marine life and animals from around the world.

For those interested in the island’s history, a visit to St. Mark’s Church is a must. This historic Anglican church was built in 1846 and is one of the oldest churches in Bermuda. Another important historical site is the Cartwheel Cafe, which was once a 19th-century prison.

Natural Attractions of Smith’s Parish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy exploring the numerous hiking trails and parks in Smith’s Parish. Spittal Pond Nature Reserve is a popular spot for bird watching and a great place to take a leisurely walk. For those in search of a more challenging hike, Tom Moore’s Jungle offers steep trails and stunning views of the island’s coastline.

If you prefer to spend time on the water, Smith’s Cove is a great spot for swimming and snorkeling. You can also take a boat tour of the island for a unique perspective on Bermuda’s natural beauty and history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smith’s Parish

While Smith’s Parish may be small, it has no shortage of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular hiking routes in the area include the Spittal Pond Nature Reserve Trail, the Tom Moore’s Jungle Trail, and the Devil’s Hole Trail.

Cyclists will enjoy exploring the Bermuda Railway Trail, which runs from one end of the island to the other. This 22-mile trail offers stunning views of the island’s coastline and is an excellent way to see the sights at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Smith’s Parish: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Smith’s Parish offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. One of the most popular activities is horseback riding, with a number of stables offering guided tours through the island’s scenic countryside.

For a unique perspective on Bermuda’s military history, the Crystal Caves offers a tour of an underground cave system that was used during World War II. You can also take a tour of the island’s shipwrecks and see the remnants of centuries-old vessels that still lie on the ocean floor.

Week-Long Itinerary for Smith’s Parish

If you are planning to spend a week in Smith’s Parish, there are plenty of activities to keep you busy. Here is a suggested itinerary for your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore the natural beauty of Spittal Pond Nature Reserve and take a leisurely walk along the trails.

  • Day 2: Take a horseback riding tour to see the island’s stunning countryside.

  • Day 3: Learn more about Bermuda’s history with a visit to St. Mark’s Church and the Cartwheel Cafe.

  • Day 4: Spend the day at Smith’s Cove, enjoying swimming, snorkeling, and soaking up the sun.

  • Day 5: Take a boat tour of the island, seeing the sights from a unique vantage point.

  • Day 6: Rent a bike and explore the Bermuda Railway Trail on your own.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the amenities at your accommodations or take a final adventure on the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Smith’s Parish

Bermuda’s food and drink scene is a fusion of British, African, and Caribbean flavors. Smith’s Parish offers a variety of options for foodies, from casual beachside cafes to upscale fine dining restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Smith’s Parish include fish chowder, cassava pie, and rum cake. If you are feeling adventurous, you can also try the island’s famous spiny lobster or the “triangle fish,” a local delicacy that is actually a type of snapper.

Local Beverages

Rum is the drink of choice in Bermuda, with the island producing its own signature brand, Gosling’s Rum. You can sample the island’s famous rum in many of the local bars and restaurants, or book a tour of the Gosling’s Rum distillery for an up-close look at the production process.

Where to Stay in Smith’s Parish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Smith’s Parish offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. If you are looking for a luxury beachside resort, there are plenty of options in the area, including the Rosewood Bermuda and the Grotto Bay Beach Resort.

If you prefer a more low-key vibe, there are also numerous guesthouses and bed and breakfasts in the area that offer a more intimate experience. For those on a budget, there are also several hostels and campgrounds in the area.

What to Buy in Smith’s Parish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Smith’s Parish, including handmade jewelry and crafts, Bermuda shorts, and Gosling’s Rum. The island’s famous pink sand is also a unique souvenir that you can take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Smith’s Parish

Bermudians are known for their hospitality and friendly demeanor. The island has a laid-back vibe, and locals often refer to the concept of “island time” where things move a little slower. English is the primary language spoken on the island, but many locals also speak a dialect known as “Bermudian English.”

Best Time to Visit Smith’s Parish

The best time to visit Smith’s Parish is between April and October, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also a popular time for tourists, so expect higher prices and bigger crowds. If you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy smaller prices, visit during November to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smith’s Parish

Do:

  • Explore all the natural beauty that Bermuda has to offer

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

  • Respect the island’s culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches or trails

  • Touch or disturb the local wildlife

  • Be disrespectful towards the locals or their customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Smith’s Parish

Bermuda is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, as in any tourist spot, visitors should exercise caution, including safety practices such as not leaving valuables unattended and keeping away from isolated areas. Visitors can contact local authorities or resort security services if they ever feel uncomfortable.

Health in Smith’s Parish

Bermuda is a relatively healthy island with no major diseases or epidemics. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions like drinking bottled water or tap water that has been boiled. Visitors require no particular immunizations to enter Bermuda.

Getting to Smith’s Parish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Smith’s Parish

Bermuda’s L.F. Wade International Airport is the island’s only airport, which is located in the eastern part of Smith’s Parish.

Bus Stations Near Smith’s Parish

Buses are the island’s main mode of public transportation. They run along most of the popular routes, including Hamilton and St. George’s. The closest bus station to Smith’s Parish is the Grotto Bay station, located at the eastern end of the parish.

Other Options

Taxis, ferries, and rental cars are also available to visitors. Many resorts and guesthouses offer shuttle services to and from the airport, so check with your accommodations provider to see if this service is available.

How to Get Around in Smith’s Parish

While public transportation is a good option for getting around the island, renting a scooter or moped is also a popular choice for visitors. This will give you more flexibility to explore the island at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Smith’s Parish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have the time and desire to keep moving, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Just a short distance from Smith’s Parish is the historic town of St. George’s, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to a number of museums and attractions.

Hamilton, the capital of Bermuda, is also a popular destination for visitors, with plenty of shopping, dining, and nightlife options available. Finally, if you are looking for a more remote adventure, consider taking a trip to the nearby islands for a unique and exciting experience.

Conclusion

Smith’s Parish is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the natural beauty and rich culture of Bermuda. With a wide range of activities and attractions, as well as plenty of accommodations and dining options to choose from, there is something for everyone here. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Smith’s Parish.
