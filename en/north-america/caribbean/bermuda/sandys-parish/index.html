Introduction to Sandys Parish

Sandys Parish is a scenic and historic area located on the western part of Bermuda. It is named after Sir Edwin Sandys, a former member of the Virginia Company, who was instrumental in the colonization of Bermuda in the early 17th century. Sandys Parish is a popular destination for travelers seeking a mix of culture, history, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Sandys Parish

If you’re planning a trip to Sandys Parish, be sure to add the following places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Scaur: This historic fort, built in the 1860s, offers stunning views of the Great Sound and the surrounding islands. Visitors can explore the ramparts, tunnels, and barracks of the fort as well as stroll through the peaceful gardens surrounding the fort.

  • Somerset Bridge: The smallest drawbridge in the world is also one of Bermuda’s most recognizable landmarks. This bridge connects Somerset Island to the mainland and is a popular site for photo ops.

  • Bermuda Maritime Museum: This museum, located in the former Royal Navy Dockyard, tells the story of Bermuda’s maritime history, including shipwrecks, navigation, and the island’s role in the slave trade.

Natural Attractions of Sandys Parish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dockyard Glassworks: Watch glassblowers at work at this studio in the Royal Naval Dockyard, or purchase one-of-a-kind glass souvenirs.

  • Hog Bay Park: This park is home to one of Bermuda’s most unspoiled beaches, offering serene, clear waters and a secluded setting.

  • Horseshoe Bay Beach: Named one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, Horseshoe Bay offers stunning pink sands, crystal-clear waters, and plenty of opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandys Parish

One of the best ways to explore Sandys Parish is by foot or bike. Here are some of the best options:

  • Scaur Hill Fort Park Nature Trail: This easy, 1-mile trail loops through the serene Scaur Hill Fort Park, offering views of the Great Sound and the surrounding islands.

  • Somerset Long Bay Park Nature Trail: This 1-mile trail winds through the peaceful Somerset Long Bay Park, past mangroves and wetlands, and along a secluded beach.

  • Railway Trail: This 18-mile trail stretches from one end of the island to the other, offering opportunities for hiking, biking, and sightseeing along the way.

Best Things to Do in Sandys Parish: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Sandys Parish. Here are some of the best:

  • Bermuda Triangle Nightlife: This guided tour explores some of the island’s most famous nightlife spots, including bars and nightclubs in Hamilton, St. George’s, and Dockyard.

  • Crystal Caves: These stunning, natural underground caves are located in Hamilton Parish, just a short ferry ride from Sandys Parish. Visitors can take a guided tour through the crystal-adorned chambers and learn about the caves’ formation.

  • Snorkeling Excursions: Sandys Parish is home to some of the best snorkeling spots in Bermuda, including Daniel’s Head Beach, Elys Harbor, and West Whale Bay.

If you have a week to spend in Sandys Parish, here’s how to spend it:

Food and Drink Scene of Sandys Parish

Bermuda is known for its delicious cuisine and unique local drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Sandys Parish:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Sandwich: A Bermudian staple, this sandwich is made with fresh, locally caught fish, served on raisin bread or a bun, and topped with coleslaw and hot sauce.

  • Bermuda Fish Chowder: This hearty soup is made with fish, tomatoes, onions, carrots, and spices, and is often served with a dash of sherry pepper sauce.

  • Bermuda Codfish Breakfast: This hearty breakfast dish features boiled salt fish with potatoes, hard-boiled eggs, and tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Dark N’ Stormy: This refreshing cocktail is made with ginger beer and dark rum, and is often served on the rocks with a slice of lime.

  • Rum Swizzle: This fruity cocktail is a mix of dark rum, lime juice, pineapple juice, and grenadine, and is perfect for sipping on a hot day.

  • Gosling’s Black Seal Rum: This locally produced rum is a Bermudian favorite, and is often used in cocktails or enjoyed on its own.

Where to Stay in Sandys Parish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sandys Parish offers a variety of accommodation options, from upscale resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Fairmont Southampton: This luxurious resort is located on a private beach and offers stunning ocean views, multiple restaurants, and a spa.

  • Willowbank Resort: This mid-range resort offers cozy cottages and a serene setting, with views of the harbor and easy access to Somerset Village.

  • Cambridge Beaches Resort & Spa: This adults-only resort offers upscale accommodations, multiple pools, a private beach, and a spa.

What to Buy in Sandys Parish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Sandys Parish:

  • Rum Cake: This delicious cake is made with rum and local ingredients, and is a popular gift to bring home.

  • Bermuda Shorts: These colorful, casual shorts are a Bermudian tradition and make a unique souvenir.

  • Local Artwork: Bermuda is home to many talented artists, and you can find unique paintings, pottery, and other artwork in many of the island’s galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Sandys Parish

Bermuda has a rich and unique culture, with influences from the island’s indigenous people, African slaves, and European settlers. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Formal Attire: Bermudians take their dress code seriously, and it’s not uncommon to see businessmen wearing jackets and ties in hot weather.

  • Respect for the Sea: The ocean is an important part of Bermudian culture, and it’s important to respect its power and beauty.

  • Public Drinking: It is illegal to drink alcohol in public in Bermuda, and violators can be fined.

Best Time to Visit Sandys Parish

The best time to visit Sandys Parish is between April and October, when the weather is warm and sunny and the ocean is perfect for swimming and snorkeling. However, this is also the busiest time of year, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices. If you’re looking for a quieter time to visit, consider visiting in the off-season (November to March), when the weather is cooler but still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandys Parish

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Sandys Parish:

Do:

  • Use Public Transportation: Bermuda has an excellent public transportation system, with buses and ferries that can take you all around the island.

  • Respect Private Property: Bermuda is a small island, and many areas are still privately owned. Be sure to respect private property and avoid trespassing.

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Bermuda has some delicious local dishes, and it’s worth trying as many as you can during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Wear Beachwear in Town: While Bermuda is a beach destination, it’s important to dress appropriately when you’re in town. Avoid wearing your swimsuit or beachwear outside of the beach areas.

  • Swim Alone: Bermuda’s ocean currents can be strong, and it’s always safer to swim with a buddy or in a designated swimming area.

  • Ignore Road Signs: Bermuda’s roads can be narrow and winding, and it’s important to pay attention to road signs and speed limits.

Safety and Health

Bermuda is a generally safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to take precautions when traveling abroad. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Sandys Parish

  • Watch for Motorcycles: Bermuda’s roads are often narrow and winding, and it’s important to keep an eye out for motorcycles and other vehicles.

  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: Bermuda is sunny and warm year-round, so it’s important to wear sunscreen and a hat, and stay hydrated.

  • Be Careful near the Cliffs: Bermuda’s cliffs can be steep and dangerous, so it’s important to exercise caution when exploring the coastline.

Health in Sandys Parish

  • Drink Bottled Water: While Bermuda’s tap water is generally safe to drink, it’s always safer to stick with bottled water.

  • Beware of Mosquitoes: Bermuda has mosquitoes year-round, and they can carry diseases like Zika and dengue fever. Use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to protect yourself.

  • Pack Your Prescription Medications: If you take prescription medications, be sure to pack enough for your entire trip, and bring a copy of your prescriptions with you.

Getting to Sandys Parish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bermuda is an island destination, so getting there requires some planning. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Sandys Parish

  • L.F. Wade International Airport: This is the main international airport in Bermuda and is located on the eastern side of the island, about 30 minutes from Sandys Parish.

Bus Stations Near Sandys Parish

  • Somerset Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Sandys Parish, with buses traveling all over the island.

Other Options

  • Ferries: Bermuda has an excellent ferry system, with boats traveling between Sandys Parish and other parts of the island.

  • Taxis: Taxis are available throughout Bermuda, but fares can be expensive. Be sure to negotiate the fare with the driver before getting in.

How to Get Around in Sandys Parish

Bermuda has a great public transportation system, with buses and ferries that can take you all around the island. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive. If you prefer to explore on your own, you can rent a bike or scooter to explore the island at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Sandys Parish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore beyond Sandys Parish, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • St. George’s: This historic town on the eastern side of the island is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to many historic landmarks and museums.

  • Hamilton: Bermuda’s capital city is a bustling hub of culture, nightlife, and shopping, with plenty of restaurants and bars to explore.

  • Island Hopping: Bermuda is home to several other islands that are worth exploring, including St. David’s Island, Smith’s Island, and Cooper’s Island.

Conclusion

Sandys Parish is a beautiful and vibrant destination that offers something for every traveler. Whether you’re looking to explore history and culture, enjoy outdoor adventures, or relax on the beach, Sandys Parish is a must-visit destination in Bermuda. With this comprehensive travel guide in hand, you’ll be ready to explore all that this stunning area has to offer.
