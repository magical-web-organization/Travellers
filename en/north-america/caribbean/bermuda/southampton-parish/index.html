Introduction to Southampton Parish

Southampton Parish is a small town located in the southern part of Bermuda, covering an area of approximately 6.5 square miles. The town is famous for its natural beauty, with its pristine beaches, lush green forests, and stunning cliffs. The town has a population of around 3,752, and the majority of the people here speak English. Southampton Parish is a wonderful place to visit for those who love to explore nature, history, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Southampton Parish

There are many places to visit in Southampton Parish. Whether you want to explore the island’s rich cultural heritage, indulge in outdoor adventures, or simply relax on the beach, Southampton Parish has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Southampton Parish:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gibbs Hill Lighthouse: Built-in 1846, Gibbs Hill Lighthouse is one of the oldest cast-iron lighthouses in the world. Visitors can climb up to the top of the lighthouse to enjoy panoramic views of the island’s coastline.
  • Whale Bay Fort and Park: Located on the western side of the island, Whale Bay Fort and Park is a historic site that showcases Bermuda’s military heritage. The fort offers excellent views of the sea.
  • Church Bay: This is considered one of the most beautiful beaches in Bermuda, and it is famous for its calm waters that are great for swimming and snorkeling. Visitors can explore the vibrant coral reefs and see a range of tropical fish species.

Natural Attractions of Southampton Parish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseshoe Bay Beach: This is one of the most popular beaches in Bermuda, and it is known for its pink sand and crystal-clear water. Visitors can relax on the beach or enjoy water sports like snorkeling, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Scaur Hill Fort Park: A historic site located on a hilltop overlooking Ely’s Harbour, Scaur Hill Fort Park offers breathtaking views of the island’s coastline. Visitors can explore the fort and enjoy a picnic in the park.
  • South Shore Park: This is a popular spot for hiking and biking, and it offers stunning views of the ocean. Visitors can explore the park’s trails and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southampton Parish

Southampton Parish is a great place for hiking and biking. There are several routes that one can take to explore the island’s natural beauty. One of the popular routes is the Railway Trail, which runs along the old railway line and offers spectacular views of the ocean and beautiful landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Southampton Parish

Southampton Parish is home to many great restaurants and bars that offer a range of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Southampton Parish:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Chowder: This is a local specialty made with fish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Bermuda fish sandwich: A popular local sandwich made with fried fish, coleslaw, and tartar sauce.
  • Grilled rockfish: A local delicacy that is grilled and served with a side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Dark and Stormy: A rum-based cocktail made with ginger beer and dark rum.
  • Swizzle: A sweet and tangy cocktail made with rum, fruit juice, and grenadine.

Local Culture and Customs of Southampton Parish

Bermudian culture is a beautiful blend of African, British, and West Indian influences. The people of Southampton Parish are friendly and warm, and visitors are always welcome. Bermuda is a conservative society, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Southampton Parish

The best time to visit Southampton Parish is from April to October when the weather is warm, and the skies are usually sunny. The peak tourist season is from June to August, so visitors are advised to book their accommodations well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southampton Parish

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Tip restaurant servers and taxi drivers.

Don’t:

  • Wear beachwear outside the beach or pool area.
  • Touch or move items in museums or historical places.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southampton Parish

Southampton Parish is a safe place for visitors, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors are advised to take usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables in their car and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Southampton Parish

Bermuda has excellent health care facilities, and visitors can access medical services in case of emergencies. It is recommended that visitors have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Southampton Parish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southampton Parish is easily accessible by air and sea. Visitors can fly into L.F. Wade International Airport, which is located in St. George’s Parish, and take a taxi to Southampton Parish. Visitors can also take a ferry from Hamilton to Rockaway, which is just a short distance from Southampton Parish.
