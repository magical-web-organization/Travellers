Introduction to Warwick Parish

Warwick Parish is one of the nine parishes of Bermuda, a popular tourist destination in the Caribbean. It is located in the western part of the island and covers an area of 2.3 square miles. The parish is known for its stunning natural scenery, safe and secluded beaches, and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Warwick Parish

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Warwick Parish Post Office: A historic building dating back to the nineteenth century, the Warwick Parish Post Office is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. It is located in the heart of the parish and is an excellent example of colonial architecture.
  • Warwick Camp: Once a military training ground, Warwick Camp is now a popular venue for community events and public ceremonies. Visitors can explore the old barracks and watchtowers, as well as learn about the history of the base.

Natural Attractions of Warwick Parish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseshoe Bay Beach: One of the most picturesque beaches in the world, Horseshoe Bay Beach is a crescent-shaped stretch of pink sand and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can go swimming, snorkeling, and take a leisurely stroll along the shore.
  • South Shore Park: A popular destination for nature lovers, South Shore Park is a 35-acre park that offers stunning views of the coastline, cliffs, and rock formations. Visitors can go hiking, biking, and have a picnic while taking in the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Warwick Parish

  • The Railway Trail: A 22-mile trail that spans the entire length of Bermuda, the Railway Trail offers stunning views of the island’s coastline, hidden coves, and historic landmarks. Visitors can go hiking, jogging, or biking along the trail, which is suitable for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Warwick Parish: Activities and Tours

  • Jet Ski Tours: Visitors can take part in an exciting jet ski tour of Warwick Parish, which takes them along the coastline, and to hidden coves and beaches.
  • Fishing Charters: Warwick Parish is a great place for fishing, and visitors can take part in a fishing charter to catch some of the region’s best fish, including tuna, wahoo, and marlin.

Food and Drink Scene of Warwick Parish

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Chowder: Bermuda’s national dish, fish chowder is a thick and savory soup made with fish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Rum Cake: A popular dessert in Bermuda, rum cake is made with dark rum, butter, and sugar, and is a perfect way to end a meal.

Local Beverages

  • Dark ‘n Stormy: Made with dark rum and ginger beer, the Dark ‘n Stormy is Bermuda’s national cocktail.
  • Gosling’s Black Seal Rum: A locally produced rum, Gosling’s Black Seal Rum is a staple in Bermuda and is used in many of the island’s cocktails and desserts.

Where to Stay in Warwick Parish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fairmont Southampton: A luxurious resort located on the south shore of Bermuda, the Fairmont Southampton offers stunning views of the ocean, access to a private beach, and a variety of dining options.
  • Elbow Beach Resort: An upscale beachfront resort located on Elbow Beach, Elbow Beach Resort offers guests access to an array of water sports, a spa and fitness center, and multiple dining options.
  • Sandpiper Guest Apartments: A more affordable option, Sandpiper Guest Apartments offers guests access to a private beach and self-contained apartments with kitchens and living spaces.

What to Buy in Warwick Parish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bermuda Shorts: A colorful and comfortable piece of clothing, Bermuda shorts are a great souvenir and are available in many shops throughout the island.
  • Rum Cake: Made with local rum, Bermuda rum cake is a popular and delicious souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Warwick Parish

The culture in Bermuda is a mix of African, Caribbean, British, and Native American influences. Bermuda is a conservative society, and it is important to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. English is the official language of Bermuda, but many Bermudians also speak local dialects, including the Gombey dance, a traditional dance that is performed during festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Warwick Parish

The best time to visit Warwick Parish is between April and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the water is perfect for swimming and water sports. The peak season is between June and August when the island is busiest, and prices are highest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Warwick Parish

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of Warwick Parish, including the beaches and parks.
  • Do try local dishes and beverages, such as fish chowder and Dark ‘n Stormy.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly and showing respect for the Gombey dance.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment. Bermuda is known for its stunning natural scenery, and it is important to preserve it for future generations.
  • Don’t take photographs of locals without their permission. Bermudians value their privacy and may become offended if they are photographed without consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Warwick Parish

Warwick Parish is generally safe, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoiding secluded areas after dark.

Health in Warwick Parish

Bermuda has a high standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and medical facilities located throughout the island. Visitors should take precautions against sunburn and insect bites, as well as avoiding tap water and consuming only bottled water.

Getting to Warwick Parish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Warwick Parish

The nearest airport to Warwick Parish is L.F. Wade International Airport, located on the east side of the island. The airport is served by several major airlines, including Delta, American Airlines, and British Airways.

Bus Stations Near Warwick Parish

There are several bus stops located throughout Warwick Parish, connecting it to the rest of the island. The bus is an affordable and convenient way to get around Bermuda and connect with other parishes.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent scooters, motorbikes, or cars to explore Warwick Parish and other parts of the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Warwick Parish

The best way to get around Warwick Parish is on foot, by bicycle, or by scooter. Taxis are also available but can be pricey. The Railway Trail is an excellent option for hikers and bikers and offers stunning views of the coastline and hidden coves.

Where to Go Next from Warwick Parish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. George’s: A historic town located on the east side of the island, St. George’s is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is known for its charming streets, historic landmarks, and vibrant culture.
  • Hamilton City: The capital city of Bermuda, Hamilton City is a bustling metropolis with excellent shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Gibbs Hill Lighthouse: Located on the highest point of the island, Gibbs Hill Lighthouse offers stunning views of the island and surrounding ocean and is a popular attraction for visitors.

Conclusion

Warwick Parish is a hidden gem in Bermuda, offering visitors stunning natural scenery, rich history, and authentic local culture. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing beach vacation, Warwick Parish has something for everyone.
