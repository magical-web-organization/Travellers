Introduction to Virgin Islands

Virgin Islands is located in the Caribbean and is a group of islands divided into two parts: the British Virgin Islands and the United States Virgin Islands. The British Virgin Islands consist of about 60 islands, including the well-known Tortola, Jost Van Dyke, Virgin Gorda, and Anegada. The United States Virgin Islands consist of St. Thomas, St. John, and St. Croix. Virgin Islands is known for its white sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, water sports, and vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Virgin Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of St. Croix: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Virgin Islands, featuring artwork, artifacts, and exhibits that showcase the island’s diverse heritage.

  • The Virgin Islands Folk Museum: Located in St. Thomas, this museum gives visitors a glimpse into the island’s past, with displays of traditional crafts, artifacts, and rooms decorated in historical fashion.

  • Fort Christiansvaern: Built by the Danish in 1774, this fort is now a National Historic Site and features exhibits about the island’s history and culture.

  • The 99 Steps: A staircase built in the 1700s out of ballast bricks that were used in Danish ships and is now a popular tourist attraction.

Natural Attractions of Virgin Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Baths: the most famous site in Virgin Gorda, featuring breathtaking natural boulders, crystal-clear pools and grottoes to explore.

  • Buck Island Reef National Monument: A protected reef off the coast of St. Croix, home to stunning coral gardens, colorful sea life, and a fantastic beach to soak up the sun.

  • Caneel Bay: Located on St. John, Caneel Bay offers visitors the chance to enjoy the island’s lovely beaches, miles of hiking trails, and stunning natural landscapes.

  • Magens Bay: Offering one of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean, Magens Bay is a must-visit destination on St. Thomas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Virgin Islands

  • Reef Bay Trail: This three-mile trail offers visitors a chance to enjoy the island’s stunning landscapes, including lush rainforests, ancient petroglyphs, and towering waterfalls.

  • Annaberg Sugar Plantation: Hike to explore a historic sugar cane plantation on St. John Island and enjoy stunning views of the ocean.

  • Mt. Sage National Park: Go on a scenic hike in the largest national park in British Virgin Islands to see tallest peak in the Virgin Islands, stunning natural landscapes, and scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Virgin Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Conch Fritters - Deep-fried dough balls with conch meat.
  • Lobster - Grilled or boiled served with butter and lemon sauce.
  • Saltfish and Johnny Cakes - Fried dough drizzled with maple syrup and salted fish cooked with peppers and onions.
  • Roti - A type of flatbread stuffed with curried meat or veggies.
  • Bush Tea - A fragrant herbal tea made from local herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Dark and Stormy - A popular island cocktail made with ginger beer and dark rum.
  • Painkiller - A sweet tropical cocktail made with rum, pineapple juice, and coconut cream.
  • Virgin Islands Brewing Company - Located in St. Thomas, the brewery makes a variety of craft beers, including the Virgin Islands Pale Ale and the Island Summer Ale.

Where to Stay in Virgin Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Caneel Bay Resort, St. John: An exclusive resort set within beautiful national park land, featuring seven beaches and a range of luxury amenities.

  • Scrub Island Resort, Spa & Marina, British Virgin Islands: A luxurious resort with private-island atmosphere that includes a spa and marina.

  • The Westin St. John Resort and Villas: A family-friendly resort tucked away in lush tropical gardens, featuring a large pool and beachfront location.

  • Cruz Bay Boutique Hotel: A classic island hotel located in the heart of Cruz Bay.

What to Buy in Virgin Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cruzan Rum: Produced in St. Croix, this rum is a famous Virgin Islands specialty sold all over the islands.

  • Hook Bracelet: A famous hand-made, nautical-themed bracelet representing love and acceptance.

  • Batik Clothing: Handcrafted fabrics are available in different stores on the island, but the Batik fabrics are the perfect gift to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Virgin Islands

The people of Virgin Islands have a rich cultural heritage, consisting primarily of West African, Spanish, Dutch, and British influences. The official language is English, but many locals speak Creole. The majority of Virgin Islanders practices Christianity, and the traditional families are matriarchal. It is customary to greet people with a smile and say hello when passing them on the street. Tipping is recommended at restaurants, taxis, and other service-related industries.

Best Time to Visit Virgin Islands

The Virgin Islands have a tropical climate, and the temperature usually remains between 80°F and 90°F throughout the year. The high season lasts from December to April, when the weather is dry and warm. However, the hurricanes in late summer and early fall may cause disruptions. The shoulder season, May to August, offers warm waters, fewer crowds, and lower rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Virgin Islands

Do:

  • Do enjoy the excellent selection of food, fresh seafood and local drinks the islands offer.

  • Do bring sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and adequate clothing for the beach and boat tour activities.

  • Do join in the local celebrations like the Carnival, music festivals, and other local events.

  • Do respect the local culture such as barefoot walking is considered disrespectful in public places.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places or on the beaches.

  • Don’t forget to carry cash for small purchases on the island.

  • Don’t bypass the opportunity to rent a car or hire a taxi.

  • Don’t sunbathe nude on the beaches; public nudity is prohibited on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Virgin Islands

The Virgin Islands are generally safe, with a low crime rate. Safety concerns include strong currents at some beaches, so always pay attention to local warning signs. Avoid walking alone at night or in dark areas.

Health in Virgin Islands

There are no particular health concerns for travelers to the Virgin Islands, but you should watch out for mosquito-borne diseases like Zika and Dengue fever. Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated while exploring the islands.

Getting to Virgin Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Virgin Islands

  • Cyril E. King Airport (STT) - Located on the island of St. Thomas, is one of the most important airports serving the Virgin Islands.
  • Henry E. Rohlsen Airport (STX) - Located on the island of St. Croix is the largest airport on the US Virgin Islands.

Bus Stations Near Virgin Islands

  • There are no public bus systems in Virgin Islands.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available on the islands, but always negotiate prices before going around.

  • Ferries: You can take ferries from St. Thomas to St. John and other nearby islands in the British Virgin Islands.

How to Get Around in Virgin Islands

The easiest way to get around the islands is to rent a car or motor scooter. Taxis are readily available, with set fares, or you can take a Safari pickup truck, which is a shared taxi bus and cost-effective. Bike rentals and golf carts are other alternative means of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Virgin Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puerto Rico: accessible by a 30-minute flight, Puerto Rico offers travelers a diverse culture with rich history, bustling cities, and magnificent landscapes.

  • St. Martin/St. Maarten: A 1.5-hour flight will bring you to the island governed both by the French and the Dutch and is famous for its nightlife, exotic beaches, and a wide range of water sports.

  • Anguilla: A 20-minute ferry ride from St. Martin, Anguilla is an idyllic island, with stunning, uncrowded beaches and a relaxed atmosphere.
