Long Island, located in the Bahamas in the Caribbean, is a stunning destination that boasts white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a rich culture. This picturesque island offers a unique experience to travelers who want to explore its natural beauty while immersing themselves in the local culture. In this comprehensive guide to Long Island, you will discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Long Island

Long Island is a hidden gem located in the southern part of the Bahamas. The island is famous for its breathtaking coastlines, stunning reefs, and scenic vistas. The warm and friendly locals of Long Island are a mix of African, European, and Indigenous people, creating a unique culture that is worth experiencing.

List of Places to Visit in Long Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Long Island has a rich history, and there’s no better way to discover it than to visit the local museums and historical places. The following are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Columbus Point: This is where Christopher Columbus landed during his first voyage to the Americas.
  • Dean’s Blue Hole: It is the world’s deepest saltwater blue hole that reaches a depth of 202 meters.
  • Stella Maris Resort Club: It has a Catholic Church and a Martello Tower from the 19th century with sweeping ocean views.

Natural Attractions of Long Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Long Island is known for its natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. The following are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Cape Santa Maria Beach: This beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and white sandy beach.
  • Long Island Bird Watching: With an incredible 100 bird species on Long Island in the Bahamas ranging from the Greater Antillean Bullfinch, Thick-billed Vireo, and colorful Bahama Parrots.
  • Hamilton’s Cave: It is an extraordinary natural wonder of stalactites and underground lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Island

Long Island has many beautiful hiking and biking trails for those who enjoy outdoor adventures. The following are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Mutton Fish Point Trail: This trail is a scenic route that winds through pine forests and mangrove wetlands.
  • Dean’s Blue Hole to Long Island Peak: This trail offers a challenging hike with stunning views of the island from the top of Long Island Peak.

Best Things to Do in Long Island: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy while on Long Island. The following are some of the best things to do:

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: The clear waters surrounding Long Island are perfect for underwater exploration.
  • Fishing: Long Island is known for its world-class bonefishing, and there are plenty of tour operators who can help you catch these elusive fish.
  • Island Hopping: Long Island is a great starting point to explore nearby islands such as Rum Cay, San Salvador, and Cat Island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Island

Here’s an example itinerary for a week-long trip to Long Island:

Day Activity
1 Explore the local culture in the capital city of Clarence Town
2 Relax and unwind on the stunning Cape Santa Maria Beach
3 Go scuba diving in the world-famous Dean’s Blue Hole
4 Take a tour of the local historical sites such as Columbus Point
5 Explore the natural wonder of Hamilton’s Cave
6 Hike to the top of Long Island Peak for panoramic views of the island
7 Island hop to nearby Rum Cay for snorkeling and relaxation

Food and Drink Scene of Long Island

The Bahamian cuisine is worth experiencing, and Long Island is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Conch Salad: A classic Bahamian dish, which is a simple and refreshing salad made with raw conch and a mix of vegetables, herbs, and spices.
  • Peas and Rice: A traditional dish made with pigeon peas and rice cooked in coconut milk and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Goombay Punch: A sweet delicious soda well-known across the Bahamas.
  • Sky Juice: A creamy Bahamian drink made from condensed milk, coconut water, and gin.

Where to Stay in Long Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Island has a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget and style. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Stella Maris Resort Club: A luxury beach resort on Long Island with private beaches and an infinity pool.
  • Cape Santa Maria Beach Resort & Villas: A stunning beachfront property with houses and villas for rent.
  • Chez Pierre Bahamas: A budget-friendly hotel in the heart of Clarence Town.

What to Buy in Long Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Long Island has many unique souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing home. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Hand-crafted baskets and bags: Made from local materials like palm leaves and straw, these baskets and bags are perfect for carrying beach essentials.
  • Conch shells and jewelry: The conch shell is an icon of the Bahamas, and you can find beautiful handmade jewelry and ornaments made from this shell everywhere on the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Island

Long Island has a unique culture that is worth experiencing. Here are some of the customs and traditions of the island:

  • Junkanoo: A colorful carnival parade that takes place every New Year’s Day.
  • Rake and Scrape music: A unique type of Bahamian music played with traditional instruments like the saw, oil drum, and guitar.

Best Time to Visit Long Island

The best time to visit Long Island is between November and April when the weather is mild and dry. The summer months can be hot and humid, with occasional tropical storms and hurricanes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Island

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: The Bahamian cuisine is delicious and worth exploring.
  • Respect the local culture: Long Island has a unique culture, and it is essential to respect it.
  • Take part in the local festivities: Long Island has many cultural events throughout the year, and it is worth participating in them.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach: Long Island’s pristine beaches are one of its main attractions, and it is essential to keep them clean.
  • Disrespect the local customs: Long Island has a unique culture, and it is essential to respect it.
  • Leave valuables unattended: Petty theft can be a problem in some areas, so it is essential to keep your valuables with you at all times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Island

Long Island is a safe destination, but travelers should still take precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night: Stick to well-lit areas and avoid secluded areas.
  • Be aware of petty theft: Keep your valuables with you at all times.

Health in Long Island

Long Island has excellent medical facilities, but travelers should still take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid drinking tap water: Stick to bottled water.
  • Use sunscreen: The tropical sun can be intense, so it is essential to use sunscreen to avoid sunburns.

Getting to Long Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Island

The closest airport to Long Island is Deadman’s Cay Airport, which is located in the center of the island.

Bus Stations Near Long Island

There are no bus stations on Long Island, but there are many taxi and rental car options available.

Other Options

Another option for getting to Long Island is to take a ferry from nearby islands such as Nassau or George Town.

How to Get Around in Long Island

The best way to get around Long Island is by car or scooter rental. Taxis are also available but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Long Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Long Island is a great starting point to explore nearby islands such as Rum Cay, San Salvador, and Cat Island, and Eleuthera which can all be reached by ferry or plane.

Conclusion

Long Island is a beautiful destination with plenty of natural wonders and a unique culture to explore. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip and experience all that Long Island has to offer. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, a rich cultural experience, or just a relaxing beach vacation, Long Island has it all.
