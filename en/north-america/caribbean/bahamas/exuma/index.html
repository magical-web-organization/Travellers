Introduction to Exuma

Situated in the Bahamas in the Caribbean, Exuma is a cluster of 365 islands that offer an ideal tropical getaway for travelers looking for serene beaches, challenging adventure sports, and a chance to explore the local culture. With its white sand beaches, turquoise waters, limestone cliffs, and impressive marine life, Exuma is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, honeymooners, and families alike.

List of Places to Visit in Exuma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Exuma has a rich history, and its local culture is deeply influenced by the African, European, and American traditions. To understand the local culture, visit the following places:

  • Hermitage Exuma, where you can see the ruins of an old cotton plantation and a slave burial ground.
  • Pompey Museum of Slavery and Emancipation, which showcases artefacts and documents related to the slave trade and the struggle for freedom.
  • St. John’s Anglican Church, which dates back to the 1800s and has a graveyard where many locals are buried.

Natural Attractions of Exuma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Exuma is famous for its natural attractions, which are perfect for adventure sports and photography. Some must-visit places are:

  • Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park, where you can snorkel and dive in the clear waters and spot colorful marine life.
  • Thunderball Grotto, which is a mythical underwater cave famous for its crystal-clear waters and exotic fish.
  • Moriah Harbour Cay National Park, which is home to mangroves, sand flats, and birdlife.
  • Tropic of Cancer Beach, which is a beautiful stretch of white sand beach with clear waters and shallow tides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Exuma

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Exuma has several hiking and biking routes that offer gorgeous views of the island’s natural vistas. Some popular routes are:

  • Stocking Island Trail, which is a scenic hiking route that takes you through the beautiful beach and lush green hills of Stocking Island.
  • Moriah Harbour Cay Trail, which is a 4-mile biking trail that takes you through mangroves and sand flats on Moriah Harbour Cay and provides breathtaking views of the ocean.
  • Boiling Hole Trail, which is a serene hiking route that takes you through a range of landscapes- cliffs, mangroves, and beautiful beaches of Stocking Island.

Best Things to Do in Exuma: Activities and Tours

With its plethora of outdoor adventures, local experiences, and water sports, Exuma offers no shortage of things to do. Some must-try activities and tours in Exuma are:

  • Swimming with the pigs, which is an unusual experience and a delightful one too.
  • Snorkelling in Thunderball Grotto, which is an exotic adventure that takes you underwater through a magical cavern.
  • Jet skiing over the clear waters of the Caribbean, which is a thrilling experience and an ideal way to explore the island’s waters.
  • Exploring the Exuma Islands on a boat tour, which is a great way to discover the stunning marine life, remote coves, and unspoiled beaches of the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Exuma

Must-Try Dishes

Exuma offers a range of local delicacies that are sure to please your taste buds. Some must-try dishes are:

  • Conch salad, which is a traditional Bahamian dish made with raw conch, onion, tomato, green pepper, and lime juice.
  • Peas and rice, which is a staple dish made of boiled pigeon peas, rice, thyme, and spices.
  • Johnnycake, which is a type of fried bread made of cornmeal or wheat flour and is a common breakfast dish.
  • Guava Duff, which is a dessert made of guava fruit, butter, flour, and sugar.

Local Beverages

If you’re looking for some refreshments to beat the heat, try these local beverages:

  • Goombay Punch, which is a fruity soda that is popular throughout the Bahamas.
  • Kalik Beer, which is the national beer of the Bahamas and is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Bahama Mama, which is a cocktail made of rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, and grenadine.

Where to Stay in Exuma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Exuma has several accommodation options that cater to all types of budgets and preferences, from luxurious resorts to cozy guesthouses. Some of the best options are:

  • Sandals Emerald Bay, which is an all-inclusive luxury resort that offers private beach access, fine dining, and a range of adventure sports.
  • Peace and Plenty, which is a charming guesthouse that offers stunning sea views, free breakfast, and access to a private beach.
  • St. Francis Resort, which is a cozy and affordable option that offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a private dock.

What to Buy in Exuma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique souvenirs and local products to take home, check out these options:

  • Handmade straw hats and bags, which are a common product of the Bahamas and are great for beach outings.
  • Local shell jewelry, which is available in many shops and stalls across the island and is a perfect reminder of your exotic trip.
  • Local hot sauces and spices, which make perfect gifts for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Exuma

Exuma has a rich history and a vibrant local culture that is deeply influenced by African, European, and American traditions. It is a matriarchal society where family and community values are highly respected, and hospitality is a way of life. The local customs and traditions vary, but some common ones include:

  • Greeting people with a smile and a warm hello.
  • Dressing appropriately for formal and religious occasions.
  • Removing your shoes before entering a private house.

Best Time to Visit Exuma

The best time to visit Exuma is between November and April when the weather is mild, and there is little chance of hurricanes and storms. The island has a tropical climate, which means it is hot and humid throughout the year, with occasional showers and thunderstorms. It’s advisable to avoid the peak season between December and January when the island gets crowded, and the prices are higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Exuma

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Tip the service staff if you’re satisfied with their service.
  • Carry your sunscreen, hat, and water bottle.

Don’t:

  • Wear your swimwear outside the beaches and swimming areas.
  • Litter the beaches or the marine environment.
  • Carry or use drugs, which is illegal in the Bahamas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Exuma

Exuma is a safe destination if you take the necessary precautions, as with any other place. Some safety tips are:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Check the weather forecast and avoid going to the beach during storms and hurricanes.

Health in Exuma

Exuma has a moderate to a high risk of contracting diseases such as Dengue fever, Zika, and Chikungunya. It’s advisable to take the following precautions:

  • Carry mosquito repellent, and use it regularly.
  • Wear long sleeves and pants if possible.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid food from street vendors.

Getting to Exuma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Exuma is accessible by air and sea, and there are several options available for transportation.

Airports Near Exuma

Exuma International Airport (GGT) is located in George Town, which is the capital of the island. Many airlines operate flights to Exuma from different parts of the world, including the United States, Canada, and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Exuma

There are no bus stations in Exuma; however, there are car rental services and taxis available for hire.

Other Options

Water taxis and ferries are also available from Nassau and other nearby islands, which makes island hopping easy and affordable.

How to Get Around in Exuma

Exuma has several transportation options to explore the island’s natural attractions and local culture, including:

  • Car rentals, which are available at the airport and major hotels.
  • Taxis, which are affordable and convenient for short distances.
  • Boats, which are a great way to explore the island’s remote beaches and coves.

Where to Go Next from Exuma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions in the Bahamas that you can visit and explore:

  • Nassau, the capital of the Bahamas, which is known for its British colonial architecture, museums, and shopping.
  • Andros Island, which is famous for its coral reefs and blue hole diving spots.
  • Eleuthera Island, which is famous for its pink sand beaches and water sports.

Conclusion

Exuma is an island paradise that offers a blend of natural wonders, adventure sports, local culture, and luxury accommodation options. Whether you’re looking to relax on a white sand beach, explore the vibrant marine life, or try the local delicacies, Exuma has something for everyone. So, pack your bags, book your tickets, and get ready to experience the island’s magic and charm.
