Introduction to Paradise Island

Paradise Island, located in the Bahamas in the Caribbean region of North America, is a stunning destination that offers a rich variety of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, beautiful beaches, and a lively food and drink scene. This travel guide will provide a comprehensive overview of the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay and eat, local culture and customs, transportation options, safety and health information, and much more. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure enthusiast, a beach lover, or a foodie, Paradise Island has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Paradise Island

Here are the top tourist attractions and landmarks to visit while exploring Paradise Island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pirates of Nassau Museum
  • The National Art Gallery of The Bahamas
  • The Queen’s Staircase
  • Government House
  • Fort Charlotte
  • The Bahamas Historical Society Museum

Natural Attractions of Paradise Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paradise Island Beach
  • Cabbage Beach
  • Versailles Gardens and French Cloister
  • The Dolphin Cay
  • Blue Lagoon Island
  • The Cloisters

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paradise Island

  • The Harbour Walk/Harbour Drive is a 2-mile walking/biking path that offers stunning views of the harbor, Paradise Island’s high-end resorts, and yachts.

Best Things to Do in Paradise Island: Activities and Tours

  • Water sports: swimming, snorkeling, diving, paddleboarding, kayaking, and more.
  • Golfing: Paradise Island is home to the Ocean Club Golf Course, an 18-hole course designed by Tom Weiskopf.
  • Fishing: guided tours for deep-sea fishing are available from local charter companies.
  • Tours to nearby islands: take a boat ride to Rose Island or Blue Lagoon Island for a day trip.
  • Beach day: relax and unwind on the pristine white beaches of Paradise Island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Paradise Island

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Paradise Island:

Day Activities
Day 1 Snorkeling at Cabbage Beach and exploring the Cloisters
Day 2 Swimming with dolphins at the Dolphin Cay and golfing at Ocean Club Golf Course
Day 3 Visiting the Pirates of Nassau Museum and taking a boat ride to Blue Lagoon Island
Day 4 Shopping for souvenirs and local products at the Straw Market and the Marina Village
Day 5 Beach day at Paradise Island Beach and watching the sunset at Versailles Gardens
Day 6 Exploring the historic sites of Nassau, including the Queen’s Staircase and Fort Charlotte
Day 7 Departure day for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Paradise Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood: conch fritters, grilled fish, and lobster
  • Bahamian-style cracked conch
  • Peas and rice
  • Plantain chips

Local Beverages

  • Goombay Smash (rum-based cocktail)
  • Bahama Mama (rum and fruit juice cocktail)
  • Kalik (local beer)

Where to Stay in Paradise Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Atlantis Paradise Island Resorts: high-end resorts with beach access, pools, and many amenities.
  • The Cove Atlantis: sophisticated hotel with oceanfront rooms, pools, and casino.
  • Comfort Suites Paradise Island: affordable hotel with easy access to attractions and beaches.

What to Buy in Paradise Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Straw bags and hats from the Straw Market
  • Local art, jewelry, and souvenirs from the Nassau Straw Market
  • Rum and other local liquors from the Bahamian Brewery and Beverage Company

Local Culture and Customs of Paradise Island

Bahamian culture is a blend of African, European, and other indigenous cultures, featuring vibrant music, dance, art, and cuisine. Respect for elders, hospitality, and politeness are highly valued in the Bahamas.

Best Time to Visit Paradise Island

The best time to visit Paradise Island is from mid-December to mid-April when the weather is dry and the temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paradise Island

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bring cash for local transactions
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen
  • Stay hydrated

Don’t:

  • Wear swimwear outside of the beach or pool area
  • Litter on beaches or in public areas
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Paradise Island

Paradise Island is a safe and crime-free destination for tourists. However, visitors should avoid walking alone at night and should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Paradise Island

The Bahamas has a high standard of medical care. A yellow fever vaccine is required for travelers coming from endemic countries. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and Zika.

Getting to Paradise Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paradise Island

  • Nassau International Airport (NAS)

Bus Stations Near Paradise Island

  • None available directly on the island.

Other Options

  • Taxis, rental cars, or private car services are available for transportation to and from the airport.

How to Get Around in Paradise Island

Walking, biking, taxis, and rental cars are all great options for getting around the island. Many major attractions, shopping areas, and hotels are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Paradise Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nassau, the capital city of the Bahamas, is located just across the harbor from Paradise Island and offers a wide range of cultural and historical attractions, as well as shopping and dining options. Other nearby islands, such as Rose Island and Blue Lagoon Island, are great for day trips.

Conclusion

Paradise Island is a beautiful destination with a rich variety of cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, stunning beaches, and a lively food and drink scene. This travel guide provides a comprehensive overview for planning your perfect trip. Whether you’re looking to explore the historic sites, relax on the beach, or immerse yourself in the local culture, there’s something for every type of traveler on Paradise Island.
