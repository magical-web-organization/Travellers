Introduction to Higüey

Higüey is a city located in the eastern part of the Dominican Republic, in the province of La Altagracia. It is the capital of the province and a major economic center with a population of over 250,000 people. Higüey is known for its religious significance, natural beauty, and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Higüey

Here are some of the top places to visit in Higüey:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Basilica of Our Lady of Altagracia: A major pilgrimage site and the largest cathedral in the country dedicated to the Catholic patroness of the Dominican Republic.
  • San Dionisio Church: One of the oldest churches in the city built in the 16th century.
  • Altagracia Museum: A museum showcasing the history and culture of the province of La Altagracia.
  • La Otra Banda: A historic neighborhood with colorful houses, cobblestone streets, and a vibrant cultural scene.

Natural Attractions of Higüey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saona Island: A picturesque island with white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and abundant marine life.
  • Cueva de las Maravillas (Cave of Wonders): A natural wonder with stalactites, stalagmites, and prehistoric paintings.
  • Los Haitises National Park: A protected area with mangrove forests, caves, and ancient Taino settlements.
  • Isla Catalina: An uninhabited island with a coral reef and diverse marine fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Higüey

  • Hoyo Azul: A natural swimming pool in a cenote with a hiking trail through the forest and limestone cliff formations.
  • Parque Nacional del Este: A vast national park with hiking and biking trails through the wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Higüey: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding: Explore the countryside and beaches on a guided horseback tour.
  • Zip-lining: Soar through the treetops on a thrilling zip-line adventure.
  • Snorkeling and diving: Discover the colorful marine life in the clear waters surrounding the island.
  • Golfing: Enjoy a round of golf at one of the luxurious golf courses in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Higüey

If you’re planning to spend a week in Higüey, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Visit the Basilica of Our Lady of Altagracia, San Dionisio Church, and La Otra Banda for a cultural and historical experience.
  • Day 3: Explore the natural wonders of Cueva de las Maravillas and Hoyo Azul.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Saona Island for a beach getaway.
  • Day 5: Visit Los Haitises National Park for a wilderness adventure.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a round of golf or other outdoor activities.
  • Day 7: Relax and indulge in local cuisine and shopping for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Higüey

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sancocho: A hearty stew made with meat, vegetables, and plantains.
  • Mofongo: Mashed plantains with pork, seafood, or vegetables.
  • Empanadas: Savory turnovers filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Chimichurri: Grilled meat with a flavorful herb sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Mamajuana: A traditional drink made with rum, red wine, honey, and herbs.
  • Presidente Beer: A popular local beer brand.
  • Coco Loco: Fresh coconut water mixed with rum and fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Higüey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Excellence Punta Cana: A luxury resort with all-inclusive amenities and beachfront location.
  • Hard Rock Hotel & Casino Punta Cana: Another upscale resort with a massive casino and rock-themed decor.
  • Hotel Boutique RD68: A stylish and affordable boutique hotel in the city center.
  • Hostal Oviedo: A budget-friendly hostel with a central location and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Higüey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber jewelry: The Dominican Republic is known for its high-quality amber stones and jewelry.
  • Cigars: Tobacco is a major industry in the country, and you can find a variety of premium cigars in Higüey.
  • Coffee: Higüey and the surrounding areas produce some of the best coffee beans in the country.
  • Mangú: A popular breakfast dish made with mashed plantains and served with eggs and salami.

Local Culture and Customs of Higüey

The people of Higüey are proud of their cultural heritage and religious traditions. The city’s most important festival is the Altagracia Day on January 21st, which celebrates the patroness of the Dominican Republic. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Higüey

The best time to visit Higüey is between December and April when the weather is dry and sunny. The temperature ranges from 75°F to 85°F, making it perfect for outdoor activities and water sports. The rainy season is from May to November, and the humidity can be high during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Higüey

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and indulge in the local culture.
  • Carry a sunscreen, insect repellent, and a water bottle.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, and only drink bottled water.
  • Accept rides or favors from strangers.
  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry expensive items in public.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Higüey

While Higüey is generally safe for tourists, it’s always wise to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables, and keep a photocopy of your passport and other important documents. Use licensed taxis and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Higüey

The Dominican Republic is a tropical country, and visitors should take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Use insect repellent, wear long-sleeved shirts and pants, and avoid outdoor activities during dawn and dusk. Drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent digestive issues.

Getting to Higüey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Higüey is easily accessible via air, road, and bus. The nearest international airport is the Punta Cana International Airport, which is located about 40 kilometers from the city center. Taxis, private cars, and shuttle services are available from the airport to the city. Buses and minibusses also run regularly between Higüey and other major cities like Santo Domingo and La Romana.

Airports Near Higüey

  • Punta Cana International Airport
  • La Romana International Airport
  • Santo Domingo Las Americas International Airport

Bus Stations Near Higüey

  • La Romana Bus Station
  • Santo Domingo Bus Station
  • Bavaro Bus Station

Other Options

Car rentals are available at the airport and in the city center, but driving in the Dominican Republic can be challenging due to the traffic and road conditions. Motorbikes and scooters are also available for rent, but make sure to wear a helmet and follow traffic rules.

How to Get Around in Higüey

Walking is a great way to explore the city center and the historic landmarks of Higüey. Taxis and motoconchos (motorcycle taxis) are available for short distances and can be negotiated for a fair price. Public buses and minibusses are also available for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Higüey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Punta Cana: A popular tourist destination with miles of beaches, luxury resorts, and exciting nightlife.
  • Bayahibe: A charming fishing village with beautiful beaches and a relaxed vibe.
  • La Romana: A historic city with a rich cultural heritage and an impressive marina.

Conclusion

Higüey is a fascinating and diverse city with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or outdoor adventure, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see in this vibrant city. Plan your visit today and discover the best of Higüey!
