Introduction to Eastern Cibao

Located in the northern part of the Dominican Republic, Eastern Cibao is a magical destination offering a perfect blend of culture and natural beauty. It comprises several cities and towns, including San Francisco de Macoris and Nagua, known for their vibrant history, culinary delights, and scenic treasure. The region offers the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of larger cities, and you can experience authentic Dominican culture and hospitality.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with a complete overview of Eastern Cibao, including a list of the must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, local culture and customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Cibao

Here are some of the top places to visit in Eastern Cibao:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Francisco Antonio Salazar: Located in San Francisco de Macorís, this local museum has an impressive collection of indigenous Taíno artifacts, pottery, and local art.

  • Parque los Trinitarios: This beautiful park is located at the heart of San Francisco de Macoris and was named after the founding fathers of the Dominican Republic. It’s a great place to relax, take a stroll, and learn about the country’s independence history.

  • Fortaleza San Felipe: San Felipe Fort was built to protect the country from pirate attacks. Visitors can learn about its history and enjoy the stunning panoramic views from the top.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Cibao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salto del Limón: A spectacular 52-meter waterfall located in the town of El Limón. To reach the waterfall, visitors can take a horseback ride or hike through the lush tropical forests.

  • Laguna Gri-Grí: This serene lagoon is located in the town of Río San Juan and is a bird-watcher’s paradise.

  • Montana Redonda: Known as the “Round Mountain,” this hill offers breathtaking panoramic views of the region. It’s also a great spot for zip-lining and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Cibao

The region’s natural beauty makes it perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Ruta de Las Flores: A 5.5 km trail located in Gaspar Hernández. It’s a moderately challenging trail, taking you through lush forests and colorful gardens.

  • Ruta del Café: A 12 km hiking trail in Jarabacoa surrounded by coffee plantations.

  • Bike paths in Nagua: The town has several bike routes, including the Cuesta Norte Path and the Nagua-Sánchez Path.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Cibao: Activities and Tours

  • Experience coffee culture in Jarabacoa: Take a tour of a coffee plantation and learn about the coffee-making process.

  • Go on a river adventure: Take a kayak or tubing trip, or go rafting down the Yaque del Norte River in Jarabacoa.

  • Visit tobacco plantations: Take a tour of tobacco plantations and learn about the Dominican Republic’s famous tobacco culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Cibao

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Eastern Cibao:

Day 1-2: Explore San Francisco de Macorís and visit the Museo Francisco Antonio Salazar, Fortaleza San Felipe, and Parque los Trinitarios.

Day 3: Head to the town of El Limón and hike to the Salto del Limón waterfall.

Day 4: Visit Río San Juan and take a boat ride to the beautiful Laguna Gri-Gri.

Day 5: Experience the coffee culture of Jarabacoa and go on a coffee plantation tour.

Day 6: Take a kayak or tubing trip, or go rafting down the Yaque del Norte River in Jarabacoa.

Day 7: Visit Nagua and go on a bike tour of the town or hit the beach!

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Cibao

Dominican cuisine is a fusion of European, African, Taino, and other influences, making it a unique experience for foodies. Here’s what you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • La Bandera: A national dish made with rice, beans, meat, and salad.

  • Sancocho: A hearty stew made with various meats, vegetables, plantains, and yucca.

  • Chicharrón: Fried pork rinds.

Local Beverages

  • Presidente Beer: Light beer brewed in the Dominican Republic.

  • Mamajuana: A popular local liquor made with rum, honey, wine, and a combination of herbs and tree barks.

Where to Stay in Eastern Cibao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastern Cibao has a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Casa Bonita Tropical Lodge: This luxury lodge in Jarabacoa offers stunning views, an outdoor pool, and spa services.

  • Hotel Casablanca: Located in San Francisco de Macorís, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  • Hostal Mama Chila: A budget-friendly hostel in Nagua with shared kitchen facilities and a garden.

What to Buy in Eastern Cibao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the unique souvenirs and local products to buy in Eastern Cibao:

  • Coffee: Jarabacoa is known for its coffee, and there are plenty of coffee shops where you can buy fresh beans.

  • Hand-rolled cigars: The Dominican Republic is famous for its cigars, and you can purchase high-quality cigars at tobacco plantations or local shops.

  • Amber: The town of Santiago is known for its amber, which is a fossilized tree resin.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Cibao

Dominican culture is lively, and visitors will be amazed by the enthusiasm and charm of the locals. Here are some of the customs and traditions:

  • Music and dance: Merengue and Bachata are two of the most famous music genres, and you’re sure to hear them playing during your visit.

  • Family-oriented: Family and community are at the core of Dominican culture, and visitors will feel welcome.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Cibao

The best time to visit Eastern Cibao is from mid-December to mid-April when the weather is pleasant and the skies are blue.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Cibao

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish: Most locals do not speak English, so learning some Spanish phrases will help you communicate better.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Do not drink tap water; Ensure you have bottled water with you at all times.

  • Do not wear revealing clothing; as the region is predominantly conservative.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Cibao

Dominican Republic has a high crime level, so it’s best to be cautious about your surroundings when you’re out exploring. Avoid dark streets and alleys, and do not walk alone late at night.

Health in Eastern Cibao

Mosquito-borne illnesses, including Zika virus, chikungunya, and dengue fever, are a risk in the Dominican Republic. Ensure you take proper precautions by wearing long sleeves and pants, using mosquito repellent and sleeping in insecticide-treated bedsheets and nets.

Getting to Eastern Cibao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Cibao

  • Cibao International Airport: Located near Santiago, this is the closest airport to Eastern Cibao.

  • Gregorio Luperón International Airport: Located in Puerto Plata, this airport is a bit further away but still within driving distance of Eastern Cibao.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Cibao

The primary bus terminal in the region is located in San Francisco de Macorís, with buses running to various towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

The most popular way to explore Eastern Cibao is by car, either by renting one or hiring a driver.

How to Get Around in Eastern Cibao

There are various transportation options available in Eastern Cibao, including taxis, buses, bike rentals, and car rentals. However, the most convenient way to get around the region is by car.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Cibao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cabarete: A town on the north coast famous for its kiteboarding and windsurfing.

  • Santiago: A bustling city with plenty of cultural and historical landmarks, including the Monumento a los Héroes de la Restauración.

Conclusion

Eastern Cibao is a fantastic destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. It’s a perfect place to escape the city and experience authentic Dominican lifestyle and hospitality. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip, ensuring you don’t miss any of the top attractions and experiences in the region.
