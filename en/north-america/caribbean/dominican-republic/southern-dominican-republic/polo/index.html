Your Essential Travel Guide to Polo, Dominican Republic

Welcome to Polo, a hidden gem in Southern Dominican Republic that offers a perfect blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or just looking to unwind on a stunning Caribbean beach, Polo has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the must-see sights and activities in Polo, and help you plan your perfect trip.

Introduction to Polo

Polo is a small town located in the province of Barahona, in the Southern region of the Dominican Republic. It is known for its beautiful beaches, lush green mountains, and rich cultural heritage. The town is nestled in a valley between the Sierra de Bahoruco mountain range and the Caribbean Sea, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Polo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Casona: This historic house turned museum dates back to the 18th century and offers a glimpse into the colonial past of Polo.
  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora del Rosario: This beautiful church was built in the 19th century and is one of the most iconic landmarks of Polo.
  • Cueva de las Maravillas: Located just a short drive from Polo, this breathtaking cave system features stunning rock formations and ancient Taino art.

Natural Attractions of Polo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bahia de las Aguilas: This pristine beach is often ranked among the best beaches in the world for its crystal-clear waters and powdery sand.
  • Sierra de Bahoruco National Park: This protected area is a haven for wildlife and offers visitors the chance to hike through lush tropical forests.
  • Lago Enriquillo: This saltwater lake is located just a short drive from Polo and is home to a unique ecosystem that includes crocodiles and flamingos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Polo

  • Polo to La Cueva: This 5 km hike takes you through stunning mountain scenery and ends at the fascinating Cueva de las Maravillas.
  • El Arroyazo Trail: This 3 km trail takes you through a lush forest to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Paraiso Trail: This 5 km trail takes you through some of the most scenic spots in the Sierra de Bahoruco National Park.

Best Things to Do in Polo: Activities and Tours

  • Beach Hopping: From Bahia de las Aguilas to Playa San Rafael, Polo boasts some of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean.
  • Whale Watching: From January to March, humpback whales can be seen off the coast of Polo.
  • Coffee Plantation Tour: The region around Polo is known for its coffee production, and visitors can take a tour to learn about the production process and sample some of the local coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Polo

Food and Drink Scene of Polo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mangu: A Dominican staple made from mashed green plantains, often served for breakfast.
  • Sancocho: A hearty stew made with meat, yucca, plantains, and other vegetables.
  • Locrio: A flavorful rice dish, often made with chicken or seafood and served with beans.

Local Beverages

  • Mamajuana: A potent Dominican drink made from rum, red wine, honey, and a mixture of herbs and tree bark.
  • Presidente Beer: The most popular beer in the Dominican Republic, known for its refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Polo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several great options for accommodation in Polo, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Some of the top-rated options include:

  • Casa Bonita Tropical Lodge: A luxurious eco-lodge situated in the heart of the Sierra de Bahoruco mountains.
  • Rancho Platon: A budget-friendly option that offers charming rooms and cabins in a beautiful natural setting.
  • Club Náutico de Polo: A beachfront resort that offers a wide range of activities and amenities, including water sports and a spa.

What to Buy in Polo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee: The region around Polo is known for its high-quality coffee, which makes for a great souvenir or gift.
  • Handicrafts: Visitors can find a wide range of locally made handicrafts, including handmade baskets, pottery, and woven textiles.
  • Larimar Jewelry: Larimar is a rare blue stone that can only be found in the Dominican Republic, and makes for a unique and beautiful piece of jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Polo

The people of Polo are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors are always greeted with warm hospitality. Respect for local customs and traditions is also important in Polo, especially when it comes to religious practices. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites, and ask permission before taking photos.

Best Time to Visit Polo

Polo can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season, from December to April. This is the perfect time to enjoy outdoor activities and soak up the sun on the stunning beaches. Keep in mind that the peak tourist season is from December to February, so be sure to book your accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Polo

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the region

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Disrespect religious sites or practices
  • Engage in illegal activities such as drug use

Safety and Health

Safety in Polo

Polo is generally a safe destination for visitors, but it’s important to take the usual precautions such as not leaving your valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings. It’s also best to avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Polo

Visitors to Polo should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella, as well as hepatitis A and B. It’s also recommended to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Visitors should also avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water.

Getting to Polo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Polo is easily accessible via a number of transportation options:

Airports Near Polo

  • Barahona Airport: Located just 30 minutes from Polo, Barahona Airport offers domestic flights from other cities in the Dominican Republic.
  • Punta Cana International Airport: This is the largest airport in the Dominican Republic and offers international flights from a number of destinations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Polo

  • Barahona Bus Station: Located in the city of Barahona, just a short drive from Polo. This is the best option for those traveling from other cities within the Dominican Republic.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: There are several companies that offer private car or taxi transfers from other cities directly to Polo.
  • Rental Car: Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Polo themselves. It’s recommended to rent from a reputable company and to be aware of road conditions.

How to Get Around in Polo

The best way to get around Polo is by car or taxi, as public transportation can be limited. Visitors can also rent a scooter or bicycle to explore the local area. It’s important to note that roads in the region can be narrow and winding, so it’s best to drive carefully and be aware of other drivers.

Where to Go Next from Polo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Barahona: This city is located just a short drive from Polo and is known for its colonial architecture and stunning beaches.
  • Pedernales: This town is located near the border with Haiti and is a great base for exploring the nearby Jaragua National Park.
  • Santo Domingo: This vibrant city is the capital of the Dominican Republic and offers a unique blend of history, culture, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Polo is a hidden gem in Southern Dominican Republic, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences. Whether you’re looking to explore the lush green mountains, soak up the sun on a stunning beach, or learn about the region’s fascinating history, Polo has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this beautiful part of the world.
