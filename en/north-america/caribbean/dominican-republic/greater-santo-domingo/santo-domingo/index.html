A Comprehensive Guide to Santo Domingo: History, Culture, Nature, and More

Santo Domingo, the capital and largest city of the Dominican Republic, is also the oldest continuously inhabited European settlement in the Americas. Founded by Bartholomew Columbus, the younger brother of Christopher Columbus, in 1496, Santo Domingo is a city rich in history, culture, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s colonial architecture, learning about its cultural heritage, or enjoying its outdoor adventures, Santo Domingo has something to offer every traveler. In this guide, we’ll take a detailed look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to buy, and how to stay safe and healthy while visiting Santo Domingo.

Local Culture and Customs of Santo Domingo

Santo Domingo is a city with a strong identity and distinct customs. To have an easy and enjoyable time while visiting, here’s what you need to know about its local culture and customs:

  • Dress appropriately for churches and other religious sites
  • Always carry cash
  • Tipping is expected in restaurants and for tour guides

Best Time to Visit Santo Domingo

Santo Domingo has a warm climate year-round, but the best time to visit is from December to April when the weather is cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santo Domingo

To ensure you have a safe and enjoyable trip, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while in Santo Domingo:

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Spanish phrases
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Be friendly towards locals

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended
  • Drink tap water
  • Walk alone at night in unknown areas

Safety and Health

While in Santo Domingo, it’s important to stay safe and healthy. Here are some tips and precautions to keep in mind:

Safety in Santo Domingo

  • Avoid wearing expensive jewelry
  • Use only licensed taxis

Health in Santo Domingo

  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites
  • Drink bottled water or bring a water bottle with you
