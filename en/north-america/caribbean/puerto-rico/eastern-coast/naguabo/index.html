Introduction to Naguabo

Located on the eastern coast of Puerto Rico, Naguabo is a hidden gem that boasts stunning natural beauty, fascinating history, and a vibrant local culture. The town is home to approximately 23,000 residents, and its name comes from a Taíno word that means “between waters,” a nod to its coastal location.

Visitors to Naguabo can expect to be dazzled by its pristine beaches, lush tropical forests, and mouth-watering local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a tranquil retreat, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Naguabo.

List of Places to Visit in Naguabo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Casa Roig: This fascinating museum is dedicated to the life and work of artist Francisco Roig and features his impressive collection of artwork from throughout his career.
  • Parroquia San José: This beautiful church, located in the heart of Naguabo, is a stunning example of Spanish Colonial architecture and contains a number of unique religious artifacts.
  • Farallón Light: A lighthouse dating back to the 1800s, Farallón Light provides breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding coastline.

Natural Attractions of Naguabo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Yunque National Forest: This lush tropical forest is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including towering trees, stunning waterfalls, and panoramic views of the island.
  • Playa Húcares: This picturesque beach is a great spot for sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying the natural beauty of Naguabo.
  • Cayo Santiago: Take a boat tour to this small island, home to a colony of rhesus macaques, and observe these playful primates up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naguabo

  • La Cordillera: This scenic hiking trail winds through the mountains of El Yunque National Forest and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • CicloRuta: For cyclists, the CicloRuta is a network of bike trails that connects several towns along the eastern coast of Puerto Rico, including Naguabo.

Best Things to Do in Naguabo: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore the stunning coastline of Naguabo, including hidden coves and pristine beaches.
  • Fishing: Charter a boat and spend the day fishing in the crystal-clear waters of the Caribbean Sea.
  • Food Tours: Sample some of the local cuisine on a guided food tour of Naguabo, including traditional dishes like arroz con gandules and mofongo.

Food and Drink Scene of Naguabo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz con gandules: This classic Puerto Rican dish consists of rice and pigeon peas, cooked with sofrito and other spices.
  • Mofongo: A dish made with fried plantains, garlic, and an assortment of meats or seafood, mofongo is a must-try when visiting Naguabo.
  • Empanadillas: Similar to empanadas, these delicious turnovers are stuffed with a variety of meats, cheeses, and vegetables, and make a great snack any time of day.

Local Beverages

  • Piña colada: With Puerto Rico being the birthplace of the piña colada, it’s no surprise that this refreshing tropical drink is a must-try when visiting Naguabo.
  • Mojito: This classic cocktail is made with rum, sugar, mint, and lime juice, and is the perfect refreshment after a day in the sun.
  • Café con leche: For a morning pick-me-up, try a traditional Puerto Rican coffee with milk, brewed fresh and served piping hot.

Where to Stay in Naguabo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wyndham Grand Rio Mar Puerto Rico Golf & Beach Resort: Located just a short drive from Naguabo, this luxurious resort offers stunning ocean views, world-class golf, and a wide variety of amenities for guests.
  • El Conquistador Resort, A Waldorf Astoria Resort: With its own private island and stunning grounds, this resort in nearby Fajardo is an excellent choice for those seeking a luxurious getaway.
  • Bahia Marina Yachting Club: For a more intimate and relaxed experience, this charming guesthouse in Naguabo offers cozy rooms and beautiful views of the marina.

What to Buy in Naguabo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally made jewelry: From handcrafted seashell necklaces to gorgeous silver earrings, Naguabo is a great place to shop for unique and beautiful jewelry.
  • Puerto Rican rum: No trip to Puerto Rico is complete without a bottle of delicious rum to take home, and Naguabo is home to several distilleries that produce the island’s signature spirit.
  • Artisanal crafts: From beautiful ceramics to handmade textiles, Naguabo is home to many talented artisans whose work makes great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Naguabo

Puerto Ricans are proud of their rich culture and history, and visitors to Naguabo can expect to be warmly welcomed by locals. The Spanish language is spoken throughout the town, and the island’s unique blend of African, Spanish, and indigenous Taíno cultures is evident in the local cuisine, music, and dance.

It’s also important to note that Puerto Rico is a U.S. territory, and visitors will find familiar conveniences like ATMs, fast food restaurants, and familiar chain stores.

Best Time to Visit Naguabo

With its tropical climate, Naguabo can be enjoyed year-round. However, peak tourist season runs from mid-December to mid-April, when the weather is typically dry and the crowds are at their largest.

For those seeking a quieter experience, the shoulder seasons of May-June and September-November are great times to visit, with mild temperatures, fewer crowds, and lower prices. August tends to be the hottest and rainiest month of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naguabo

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and take common-sense safety precautions.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone speaks English.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Underestimate the power of the sun and always wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naguabo

Overall, Naguabo is a safe destination for visitors. However, as with any travel destination, it’s important to take common-sense precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Naguabo

There are no major health risks in Naguabo, but it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions such as washing your hands frequently and avoiding street food that may not have been prepared in sanitary conditions. It’s also recommended to bring any necessary prescription medications with you, as they may not be readily available on the island.

Getting to Naguabo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Naguabo is accessible by both air and ground transportation.

Airports Near Naguabo

The closest airport to Naguabo is Luis Muñoz Marín International Airport (SJU) in San Juan, which is approximately one hour’s drive away. From there, visitors can rent a car or hire a shuttle to reach Naguabo.

Bus Stations Near Naguabo

The main bus terminal in Naguabo is located in the town center and provides connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Taxis and private car services are also available for hire in Naguabo and can be arranged through most hotels or by using ride-hailing apps like Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Naguabo

Naguabo is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. Rental cars and taxis are also available for those looking to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Naguabo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fajardo: Just a short drive from Naguabo, Fajardo is known for its stunning beaches, excellent dining, and fantastic snorkeling and scuba diving opportunities.
  • San Juan: Puerto Rico’s capital city is home to a wealth of historic landmarks, museums, restaurants, and shopping options, making it a great destination for those looking for a more urban experience.
  • Vieques and Culebra Islands: These two small islands, located off the coast of Puerto Rico, are known for their stunning natural beauty, pristine beaches, and excellent snorkeling and diving opportunities.

Conclusion

Naguabo is a fantastic destination for travelers looking to experience the natural beauty, rich culture, and fascinating history of eastern Puerto Rico. With its stunning beaches, lush forests, and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Naguabo. Use this guide to plan your dream trip to Naguabo, and get ready to experience one of Puerto Rico’s best-kept secrets.
