Introduction to Fajardo

Located on the eastern coast of Puerto Rico, Fajardo is a picturesque town known for its crystal-clear waters, breathtaking beaches, vibrant culture, and abundant outdoor adventures. This Caribbean gem serves as a gateway to the famous El Yunque Rainforest and the surrounding islands of Vieques and Culebra. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation on the beach or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, Fajardo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Fajardo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Roig Museum: This historic building is now a museum showcasing the art and history of Puerto Rico.
  • Fortin Conde de Mirasol: A 19th-century fortress turned museum, with exhibits on the history and culture of Fajardo.
  • The Humacao Nature Reserve: A historic nature reserve that offers a glimpse into the island’s natural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Fajardo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Yunque Rainforest: The only tropical rainforest in the United States National Forest system, with hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic views.
  • Bio Bay: One of the few bioluminescent bays in the world, where you can go kayaking or take a guided tour to see the glowing aquatic life.
  • Seven Seas Beach: A beautiful beach with turquoise waters, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fajardo

  • El Yunque Rainforest hiking trails: Explore the lush greenery and scenic views of the only tropical rainforest in the United States National Forest system.
  • Las Cabezas de San Juan Reserve hiking trail: A nature trail that leads to the iconic lighthouse of Fajardo.
  • Bike tour of Isla Culebra: Rent a bike and explore the beautiful island of Culebra on your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Fajardo: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling or scuba diving at Icacos Island: Explore the underwater beauty of the Caribbean Sea and its colorful marine life.
  • Deep-sea fishing: Charter a boat and go fishing for tuna, marlin, and other big game fish.
  • Catamaran tour of the Bio Bay: Experience the magical bioluminescent bay at night on a catamaran tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fajardo

Day Activities
1 Explore the beaches of Seven Seas and Luquillo.
2 Kayak or take a guided tour of the Bio Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Fajardo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mofongo: A delicious traditional Puerto Rican dish made of mashed plantains with garlic, olive oil, and pork cracklings.
  • Arroz con Gandules: A flavorful rice and pigeon peas dish, cooked with pork and various spices.
  • Empanadillas: Fried pastry filled with meat, seafood, or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Piña colada: A popular cocktail made with rum, coconut cream, and pineapple juice.
  • Medalla: The local beer of Puerto Rico that complements the island’s cuisine.

Where to Stay in Fajardo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • El Conquistador Resort: A luxurious resort offering stunning ocean views, a private island, and a water park.
  • Fajardo Inn: A family-friendly hotel with a pool, spa, and restaurant.
  • Casa Picaflores: A cozy guesthouse with a tropical garden, pool, and a stunning view.

What to Buy in Fajardo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Visit the local market for handmade crafts such as jewelry, clothes, and local artwork.
  • Puerto Rican Coffee: Bring home a bag of freshly roasted Puerto Rican coffee to enjoy long after your trip is over.
  • Rum: Puerto Rico is known for its high-quality rum, and Fajardo offers several distilleries with tastings and tours.

Local Culture and Customs of Fajardo

Puerto Rico is a melting pot of cultures, with Spanish, African, and indigenous Taino influences. Due to its colonial history, Fajardo is rich in culture and traditions. The local language is Spanish, with English being a second language. Visitors are welcomed with open arms, and the locals take pride in their hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Fajardo

The best time to visit Fajardo is from late November to April, as it is the drier season and the weather is usually a pleasant 80°F. However, Fajardo is a tropical destination, and rainfall can occur throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fajardo

Do:

  • Do try local delicacies like mofongo and arroz con gandules.
  • Do bring your sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.
  • Do respect the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural habitats during outdoor activities.
  • Don’t forget to bring cash, as not all businesses accept credit cards.
  • Don’t be overly loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fajardo

Fajardo is a safe town, but visitors should still use caution and be aware of their surroundings. It is not recommended to wander alone at night in isolated areas or to leave personal belongings unattended on the beach.

Health in Fajardo

Tap water in Fajardo is generally safe to drink, but visitors should always use common sense when consuming food and drink. It is advisable to wear insect repellent and protective clothing to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and Zika.

Getting to Fajardo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fajardo is conveniently located about an hour’s drive from San Juan, the capital city of Puerto Rico. Visitors can arrive at San Juan International Airport and rent a car to reach Fajardo, or take a taxi or shuttle service. There are also buses and ferries available to explore nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Fajardo

The most convenient way to get around Fajardo is by renting a car. Taxis and shuttle services are also available, but can be expensive. Many of the attractions and outdoor activities are outside of the main town, so having a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Fajardo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Juan: The capital city of Puerto Rico, with historic buildings, vibrant nightlife, and beautiful beaches.
  • Culebra: A small island with some of the best beaches in the world and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • Vieques: Another small island known for its bioluminescent bay, stunning beaches, and a unique history.

Conclusion

Fajardo is a hidden gem in the Caribbean, with a perfect mix of history, culture, nature, and adventure. Whether you want to explore the tropical rainforest, relax on the beach, or experience the local cuisine, Fajardo has it all.
