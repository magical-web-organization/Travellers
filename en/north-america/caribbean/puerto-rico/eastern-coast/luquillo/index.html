Introduction to Luquillo

Found in the eastern coast of Puerto Rico, Luquillo is a charming Caribbean town located within El Yunque National Forest and near several gorgeous beaches. Whether you’re traveling solo, as a couple, or with your family, Luquillo offers an unforgettable experience through a blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Luquillo

Here are some of the top attractions in Luquillo you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fuerte San Felipe del Morro - This 16th-century fortress in Old San Juan has been declared a UNESCO World Heritage site and it’s an insightful way to delve into Luquillo’s past.
  • Luquillo Beach - This picturesque, crescent-shaped beach is undeniably one of the town’s most beautiful natural attractions where you can experience some of the Caribbean’s clearest water.
  • Museo de Arte de Puerto Rico - This gem located in San Juan presents nearly 4,000 pieces of art from Puerto Rico and Spain, including contemporary and classic collections.

Natural Attractions of Luquillo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Yunque National Forest - This verdant paradise covers over 28,000 acres and features hiking trails, waterfalls, and camping areas that provide plenty of opportunities to immerse yourself in the tropical rainforest.
  • Bioluminescent Bay - Take a nighttime kayaking trip through this sparkling bay, filled with a bio-luminescent micro-organism, giving the water an ethereal glow.
  • Luquillo Kiosks - This open-air marketplace features more than 60 beachfront kiosks cuisine, bars, and souvenirs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luquillo

Luquillo has a diverse selection of hiking and biking trails that cater to every level of fitness and experience. Here are some to check out:

  • El Toro Trail - this intermediate 5.8-mile hike offers stunning views of the Luquillo Mountains, surrounding orchids, and the island of Vieques in the distance.
  • El Yunque Trail - this is a popular tour, perfect for beginners, that will take you through lush vegetation and past several waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Luquillo: Activities and Tours

From outdoor activities to historic tours, there’s an abundance of things to do in Luquillo. Here are some must-dos:

  • Island Hopping - Book a day trip with one of the local tour companies and explore some of the neighboring islands for an entirely different outlook on Puerto Rico.
  • Surfing Lessons - Take surfing classes and ride the waves along Luquillo’s beaches. There are several entry-level surfing options available for newbies.
  • Horse-Back Riding - Get a different perspective on the island landscape and culture by taking guided tours on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for Luquillo

If you have a week to explore Luquillo, we’d suggest the following itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Relax at Luquillo beach. Try kiteboarding or windsurfing on the soft sands of La Pared.
Day 2 Take a trip up to El Yunque National Forest and hike the trails around the La Mina falls and challenge yourself with the higher peaks.
Day 3 Visit the El Junque rainforest and La Coca falls.
Day 4 Tour the San Juan historic district and visit local museums such as the Museum Del Mar or El Morro
Day 5 Kayak to the Bio Bay of Fajardo
Day 6 Horseback riding on the Luquillo beaches
Day 7 Visit the Luquillo Kiosks for delicious Puerto Rican cuisine and shopping

Food and Drink Scene of Luquillo

Luquillo has some of the best Puerto Rican cuisine and beverages available on the island.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mofongo - this popular traditional dish is made by mashing fried plantains and stuffing it with seafood, meats, or stew.
  • Asopao - a tasty, hearty soup made of chicken, rice, and vegetables that is the perfect lunch option.
  • Arroz con Gandules - A Puerto Rican signature dish that is a staple of any festive occasion and recommended to try in a local non-touristic restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Pina Colada - One of the island’s signature drinks is Pina Colada, made of pineapples and rum. Try it at a local bar!
  • Malta India - A popular non-alcoholic beer that features a Caribbean twist on classic malt beers.
  • Coquito - A creamy, coconut-based, non-alcoholic beverage that is typically flavored with spices (cinnamon and nutmeg). It’s perfect for a warm evening.

Where to Stay in Luquillo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options to stay at Luquillo. Here are some of the top-rated accommodations:

  • Wyndham Grand Rio Mar Beach Resort and Spa - a luxury resort featuring a casino, pool, and spa.
  • Luquillo Sunrise Beach Inn - a cozy guesthouse located right next to the town’s public beach.
  • Malibu Beach apartments Guesthouse - European pension-style accommodations located just off the coastline.

What to Buy in Luquillo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Luquillo has several great choices for souvenirs. Here are some recommended items to bring home:

  • Coffee - Puerto Rico produces some of the world’s best-quality coffee, and it’s a perfect souvenir and gift for coffee lovers.
  • Cigars - Puerto Rican cigars come with a unique flavor and aroma that is beloved by cigar enthusiasts worldwide.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry and Arts - Local artists create stunning jewelry pieces by using shells, sea glass, or other recycled materials, while some of the best artists create stunning artworks all around the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Luquillo

Puerto Rico’s culture is rich with history and traditions rooted in African, Amerindian, and Latin American ancestry. Here are some customs you may experience while exploring Luquillo:

  • Language - Spanish is the native language, and many locals speak English.
  • Food - Puerto Ricans love food and often select mealtimes to spend quality time with family and friends.
  • Music and Dance - Music and dance are an essential part of Puerto Rico’s cultural identity.
  • Festivals and Celebrations - Puerto Rico hosts many festivals throughout the year, including the Three Kings Day, Fiestas de la Calle San Sebastian, and the La Feria de Ponce.

Best Time to Visit Luquillo

The best time to visit Luquillo is from mid-April to June when the temperatures are warm, and the hotel prices are more budget-friendly. The hurricane season, which runs from June to November, is also a factor to consider when planning your visit. Note that travel is limited to El Yunque National Forest during hurricane season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luquillo

When exploring Luquillo, it’s essential to follow local etiquette guidelines to show respect to the people and culture. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional dishes, such as Mofongo, Asopao, and Arroz con Gandules.
  • Take advantage of the natural landscapes and outdoor activities.
  • Take care of the fragile areas and follow the Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Don’t waste water conservation is essential in Puerto Rico.
  • Don’t forget to take mosquito protection while visiting El Yunque National Park.
  • Don’t ignore the ocean safety signs - the current can be dangerous along some beach areas.

Safety and Health

Luquillo is generally safe, but as with any travel destination, it’s important to take precautions to stay healthy and safe.

Safety in Luquillo

Use common sense, take care when withdrawing and carrying cash, and avoid carrying valuables, backpacks, and cameras in public areas. Be sure to lock your doors when visiting the beach or other high-risk areas.

Health in Luquillo

Pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and basic over-the-counter medication. Zika virus and dengue fever are present in Puerto Rico year-round, so it’s essential to pack insect repellent and mosquito nets. The tap water in Luquillo and the rest of Puerto Rico is safe to drink.

Getting to Luquillo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Luquillo is easy, and there are several transportation options to choose from.

Airports Near Luquillo

  • Luis Munoz Marin International Airport - Located in San Juan, this airport is about 30 miles away from Luquillo.

Bus Stations Near Luquillo

  • Estacion de Guaguas de Palmas - This bus terminal is a hub point for many local transit companies providing intercity services mainly from the eastern region.

Other Options

Renting a car can be a great way to explore Luquillo and neighboring towns, such as Fajardo and San Juan. It’s best to book before arriving on the island as car rental availability may be limited during peak travel periods.

How to Get Around in Luquillo

The best way to get around Luquillo is by car. The nearest car rental options are at San Juan Luis Muñoz Marín Airport, or you can hire a local taxi or Uber ride-share. Buses and public transportations are also available, but the routes and schedules can be challenging to understand without Spanish fluency.

Where to Go Next from Luquillo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Luquillo is an excellent base for day-tripping some surrounding regions:

  • San Juan - Explore Puerto Rico’s capital city and visit its historical places, museums, skyscrapers, and art galleries.

  • Vieques Island - This small island, known for its pristine beaches, bioluminescent bay, and wild horses, is accessible by ferry or plane from Fajardo.

  • Culebra Island - Often referred to as the best-kept secret of the Caribbean, Culebra Island boasts a stunning National Wildlife Refuge, and its sandy beaches are recognized as a leading eco-destination.

Conclusion

Luquillo is an incredible destination that offers many activities and attractions for every type of traveler. From the beautiful beaches, lush rainforests, vibrant culture, and delicious food, Puerto Rico’s hidden gem is undoubtedly worth visiting when exploring the beautiful Caribbean area. The above guide is just a start - there is so much to do and see in Luquillo. Plan your next trip today and discover the Caribbean’s remarkable beauty.
