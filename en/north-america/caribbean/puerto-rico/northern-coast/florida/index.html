Introduction to Florida

Florida is an alluring destination that attracts travelers from around the world. This Sunshine State has something for everyone, from gorgeous beaches to thrilling theme parks, bustling cities to serene natural parks. Florida is located in the southeastern region of the United States, bordered by Alabama to the west and Georgia to the north. The state is well-known for its balmy weather, pristine beaches, and top-notch hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Florida

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Florida has a rich history and diverse cultural heritage that can be explored in its many museums and historical sites. Here are a few of the must-visit attractions:

  • Kennedy Space Center: This iconic NASA space center is a fascinating site that highlights the history of space exploration. Visitors can take part in various experiences like astronaut training, simulated space flights, and guided tours.
  • Vizcaya Museum and Gardens: A magnificent example of Renaissance architecture, this historic estate built by James Deering is a popular attraction. The museum houses a vast collection of European antiques and artwork, while the gardens are a beautiful oasis of greenery.
  • The Ringling: This art museum in Sarasota is home to a vast collection of European art, as well as American and Asian art. The complex also features a circus museum, gardens, and the historic mansion of John and Mable Ringling.

Natural Attractions of Florida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Florida has an abundance of natural attractions that offer visitors a chance to explore the great outdoors. From gorgeous beaches to pristine parks, here are a few popular places to visit:

  • Everglades National Park: This vast wetland is one of the most unique natural attractions in the world, covering over 1.5 million acres. Visitors can experience the diverse ecosystem by taking an airboat ride, hiking trails, or observing wildlife like alligators, manatees, and panthers.
  • The Florida Keys: This picturesque chain of islands is one of the most popular destinations in Florida. Visitors can enjoy beach activities like snorkeling and diving, fishing, and exploring charming towns like Key West and Marathon.
  • Clearwater Beach: This award-winning beach on the Gulf of Mexico is a hotspot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports like parasailing and jet skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florida

Florida has a wide range of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the state. Here are a few popular routes:

  • The Tamiami Trail: This scenic road runs through the heart of the Everglades National Park and offers spectacular views of the diverse ecosystem and wildlife.
  • Sanibel Island Bike Path: This 26-mile path is a popular cycling route that takes visitors through picturesque beach towns like Captiva and Sanibel and provides stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico.
  • The Tallahassee-St. Marks Historic Railroad State Trail: This 16-mile trail is a popular route for hikers and bikers that offers a chance to explore the beautiful natural scenery of North Florida.

Best Things to Do in Florida: Activities and Tours

Florida offers visitors an array of activities and tours that cater to all tastes and interests. Here are a few of the best things that you can do in Florida:

  • Walt Disney World Resort: This world-famous theme park in Orlando offers visitors a chance to experience thrilling rides, shows, and attractions. It is a favorite destination for families, and visitors can choose from four main theme parks and two water parks.
  • Miami Beach: This famed beach destination in South Florida is a hotspot for partygoers, sunseekers, and foodies. Visitors can explore the Art Deco architecture, indulge in diverse cuisine, and soak up the vibrant nightlife.
  • St. Augustine: This historic town is the oldest continuously inhabited city in the United States and is home to an array of cultural attractions like the Castillo de San Marcos National Monument and the St. Augustine Lighthouse & Maritime Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Florida

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Florida:

Food and Drink Scene of Florida

Must-Try Dishes

Florida is known for its diverse cuisine that reflects its cultural heritage and unique ingredients. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Key Lime Pie: A tart and creamy dessert that is made with Key limes and graham cracker crust, this is a must-try for any visitor to Florida.
  • Cuban Sandwich: A popular sandwich that originated in Florida and is made with ham, roasted pork, Swiss cheese, and pickles, all pressed between Cuban bread.
  • Stone Crabs: Florida’s official state crustacean is a local delicacy that is sweet in flavor and firm in texture.

Local Beverages

Florida is famous for its refreshing and delicious local beverages. Here are a few to try:

  • Orange Juice: Florida is known for producing some of the best oranges in the world, and the state’s signature fruit is used to make delicious, sweet orange juice.
  • Cuban Coffee: This strong and sweet coffee is made with darkly roasted beans and is typically served in small amounts called “cafecitos.”
  • Rum: Florida has a rich history of rum production and is home to many distilleries that produce high-quality rum.

Where to Stay in Florida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Florida is home to a wide variety of accommodations, ranging from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Here are a few popular options:

  • The Breakers Palm Beach: This iconic resort in Palm Beach is a historic landmark and features luxurious amenities like a beach club, golf courses, and multiple pools.
  • Hostelling International Miami Beach: This budget-friendly option offers dormitory-style accommodations and is located close to the beach and popular attractions like the Art Deco district.
  • The Tides Inn & Suites: A charming beachfront guesthouse in Sanibel Island that offers comfortable, cozy rooms and stunning views of the Gulf of Mexico.

What to Buy in Florida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Florida is home to many unique and locally made products that make great souvenirs. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

  • Citrus Fruits and Products: Florida is known for producing delicious citrus fruits like oranges, grapefruits, and lemons, which can be purchased as fresh produce or in the form of candies, jams, and sauces.
  • Key Lime Products: Key limes are a signature fruit of Florida, and you can find many locally made products like key lime pie, juice, and even key lime-scented candles.
  • Seashells and Coral: Florida’s pristine beaches are home to many beautiful seashells and coral that make great souvenirs. You can find many shops that sell unique and locally made seashell jewelry and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Florida

Florida has a diverse cultural heritage and a unique way of life that can be experienced through its many festivals, events, and traditions. Some popular cultural customs and events include:

  • The Florida State Fair: Held annually in Tampa, this fair is a celebration of all things Floridian, from agriculture and cattle farming to local arts and crafts.
  • The Gasparilla Pirate Festival: This unique festival is held annually in Tampa and features a parade of pirate ships and a mock pirate invasion of the city.
  • The Calle Ocho Festival: Held annually in Miami, this vibrant festival celebrates the city’s Latin cultural heritage with music, dance, food, and art.

Best Time to Visit Florida

Florida’s climate is generally warm and sunny throughout the year, with mild winters and hot summers. The best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. If you prefer pleasant weather and fewer crowds, the best time to visit is between March and May or between September and November. However, if you’re looking to indulge in beach activities and watersports, the best time to visit is during the summer months of June, July, and August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florida

Do:

  • Apply sunscreen regularly, especially when spending time outdoors.
  • Respect the local wildlife and ecosystem, particularly in natural parks and beaches.
  • Be aware of hurricane season, which runs from June to November, and follow the local weather reports.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches or natural parks.
  • Approach or feed wild animals.
  • Swim or engage in water activities during a thunderstorm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florida

Florida is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should exercise caution and common sense. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in busy areas like tourist attractions and downtown areas.
  • Secure your valuable belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or wearing expensive jewelry.
  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean, and follow the warnings and advisories posted by local authorities.

Health in Florida

Florida is home to many world-class medical facilities and hospitals, but travelers should take basic precautions to protect their health. Here are a few tips:

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of fluids, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Apply mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites, as Florida is sometimes affected by the Zika virus.
  • Be cautious when eating seafood, especially raw or undercooked shellfish.

Getting to Florida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Florida is well-connected to the rest of the United States and the world, with plenty of transportation options. Here are a few ways to get to Florida:

  • By plane: Florida is home to many international airports, including Miami International Airport, Orlando International Airport, and Tampa International Airport.
  • By bus: Greyhound buses run throughout Florida and connect major cities like Miami, Tampa, and Orlando.
  • By car: Florida is easily accessible by car, with many major highways running through the state.

Other Options

  • Train: Amtrak offers train services to Florida from major cities like New York, Washington, DC, and New Orleans.
  • Cruise: Florida is one of the world’s top cruise destinations and is home to many major cruise ports like Port Miami and Port Canaveral.

How to Get Around in Florida

Florida is a large state, and getting around can take some planning. Here are a few options:

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Florida, as it allows flexibility and access to remote areas.
  • Public Transit: Many major cities in Florida have public transportation systems, including buses and trains.
  • Ride-sharing: Services like Uber and Lyft are available throughout Florida.
  • Bike Rentals: Many cities and beach towns in Florida offer bike rentals, which are a fun and eco-friendly way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Florida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Florida is located close to many other popular destinations in the United States and the Caribbean. Here are a few suggestions:

  • The Bahamas: This Caribbean archipelago is located just 50 miles off the coast of Florida and is a popular destination for its crystal-clear waters, white-sand beaches, and vibrant culture.
  • Savannah, Georgia: This charming southern city is known for its Spanish moss-draped oak trees, antebellum architecture, and rich history.
  • New Orleans, Louisiana: This vibrant city is a melting pot of cultures and is known for its food, music, and unique architecture.

Conclusion

Florida is a diverse and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a beach lover, thrill-seeker, or culture enthusiast, Florida has plenty to explore and experience. By following this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your time in the Sunshine State.
