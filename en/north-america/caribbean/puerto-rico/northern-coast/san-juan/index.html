San Juan, the beautiful and vibrant capital of Puerto Rico, is truly a gem of the Caribbean. This historic city is full of colorful streets, stunning beaches, delicious cuisine, and a rich culture that seamlessly blends Spanish, indigenous Taíno, and American influences. Whether you’re seeking a relaxing beach vacation, the thrill of outdoor adventures, or a cultural immersion, San Juan has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best things to see and do in San Juan, where to stay and what to eat, and offer valuable tips for exploring this amazing city safely and efficiently.

Introduction to San Juan

San Juan is located on the Northern Coast of Puerto Rico, which is a Caribbean island that belongs to the United States. It is the largest city and capital of Puerto Rico, with a population of around 2.5 million people. San Juan is famous for its historic Old Town, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site and boasts a range of impressive architectural styles, the most prominent being Spanish colonial. The city is also known for its lively nightlife, gorgeous beaches, world-class museums, and incredibly flavorful cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in San Juan

No trip to San Juan is complete without a visit to some of the city’s top attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in San Juan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castillo San Cristobal: This massive fort is the largest fortress ever built by the Spanish in the Americas and offers stunning views of the city.
  • Castillo San Felipe del Morro: Another impressive fort, this one on the western tip of Old San Juan, was built to protect against sea attacks.
  • La Fortaleza and San Juan National Historic Site: This is a UNESCO World Heritage site featuring the fortress, walls, and defensive structures built to protect San Juan from invaders. La Fortaleza is the residence of the governor of Puerto Rico.
  • Museo de Arte de Puerto Rico: Puerto Rico’s premier museum of art, with over 1,100 works from all over the world.
  • Museo de Arte Contemporáneo de Puerto Rico: showcasing contemporary art from Puerto Rico and beyond.
  • Museo de las Américas – a museum dedicated to telling the story of Puerto Rico’s indigenous peoples, African heritage, and Spanish history.
  • El Yunque Rainforest – a stunning tropical rainforest located in the eastern part of Puerto Rico, featuring lush scenery, wildlife, and waterfalls.

Natural Attractions of San Juan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isla Verde Beach: This is one of the best beaches in San Juan, with crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand. There are plenty of water sports and beach activities to enjoy, along with some fantastic restaurants.
  • Luquillo Beach: Located about 45 minutes from San Juan, Luquillo Beach is a must-visit for those who want to experience some of the most beautiful and pristine beaches in the world. It provides amazing scuba diving and snorkeling opportunity and plenty of nature trails for nature lovers.
  • Rio Camuy Caves: Discovered in 1958, the Rio Camuy Caves are an underground network of caves and waterfalls that create an awe-inspiring and unique ecosystem.
  • El Yunque National Forest: El Yunque is a tropical rainforest located in the eastern part of Puerto Rico, featuring lush scenery, wildlife, and waterfalls. Visitors can swim in the natural pools, hike the trails, and admire the unusual flora and fauna.
  • Culebra Island: This small island is a wildlife refuge offering crystal clear waters and pristine beaches, home to sea turtles and an abundance of marine life. It’s perfect for snorkeling and scuba diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Juan

San Juan features some of the most scenic and varied landscapes in the Caribbean, from rugged mountains and dense rainforests to beautiful beaches and bustling cityscapes. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes include the Paseo Caribe, Condado Lagoon Trail, and the Piñones Bike Trail.

Best Things to Do in San Juan: Activities and Tours

  • Take a historic walking tour of Old San Juan to explore the impressive architecture, charming streets, and colorful buildings.
  • Take to the waters surrounding San Juan and go surfing, paddleboarding, kayaking, or jet-skiing.
  • Visit Plaza Las Américas, the largest mall in the Caribbean, boasting more than 300 stores and restaurants.
  • Explore the nearby Bacardi Distillery, the largest distillery in the world, and learn about the history of rum and the distilling process.
  • Head to Condado Beach for some fun and glamorous nightlife while sipping cocktails and enjoying the beach’s ambiance.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Juan

Day 1: Arrival in San Juan, exploring the old San Juan Day 2: Visit Isla Verde Beach and Carolina Beach, water adventures, and beach relaxation Day 3: El Yunque Rainforest hiking or mountain biking Day 4: Explore San Juan’s museums and historical sites Day 5: Day trip to the Bacardi Rum Distillery and Casa Bacardi Visitor Center Day 6: Visit Plaza Las Américas for shopping and Luquillo Beach for relaxation Day 7: Visit the Rio Camuy Caves, then back to San Juan for some local culinary experiences and nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of San Juan

San Juan offers a delicious and unique culinary scene, with a blend of local Caribbean, Spanish, and American influences.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mofongo – is a classic Puerto Rican dish made with mashed green plantains and a variety of meat options, traditionally served in a pilón (wooden mortar).
  • Tostones – are crispy fried plantains, dipping sauce on the side.
  • Arroz con Gandules – a traditional Puerto Rican rice dish made with sautéed rice, pigeon peas, sofrito, and saffron.
  • Lechon Asado – slow-roasted pork seasoned with adobo, and served with rice and beans.
  • Empanadillas – fried pastry pouches stuffed with meat, vegetables, and cheese.
  • Churrasco – grilled skirt steak, served with beans, rice and a chimichurri-style sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Piña Colada – a classic Puerto Rican drink made with rum, coconut cream, and pineapple juice.
  • Medalla – the local beer brand is Medalla, proud of its local heritage and refreshingly light taste.
  • Coquito – an eggnog-like drink made with rum, coconut milk, and spices.
  • Ron del Barrilito – a premium rum with smooth and full-bodied flavor, aged up to 30 years.

Where to Stay in San Juan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer to stay in style or budget, San Juan has a range of options for all tastes and preferences. Some of the top choices include:

  • Hotel El Convento – a luxurious boutique hotel in Old San Juan, located in a beautifully restored 17th-century convent.
  • La Concha Resort – a modern and chic hotel offering beachfront views, a lively pool area, and a casino.
  • Casa Isabel Bed and Breakfast – a charming and cozy bed and breakfast in Old San Juan featuring floral patios and upper-level decks.
  • Dream’s Hotel Puerto Rico – walking distance to the beach and at a very reasonable price.

What to Buy in San Juan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Puerto Rico is known for its exquisite art, crafts, and souvenirs that make perfect reminders of your trip. Some of the top buys include:

  • Puerto Rican Coffee – there are various coffee shops featuring locally sourced beans, so take a pack home to relive the Puerto Rican coffee experience.
  • Handmade jewelry – a unique handmade product creating an island-style piece of art in a variety of styles, from contemporary to cultural.
  • Salsa and Ponchos – Puerto Rican traditional salsa and clothing woven for the warmth of the tropics.
  • Local Food Products – take home some local spices, rum or unique sauces and salsa.

Local Culture and Customs of San Juan

Culture is an essential part of San Juan’s vibrant heritage, influenced mainly by the three dominant cultures of the Tainos, Spanish, and American. The residents embrace their cultural heritage, playing with different languages, music, dance, and food. A unique custom of San Juan is to greet friends and strangers with a warm smile and a friendly “hola!” and when conversing in Spanish speaking a little bit slower so as not to confuse the listener.

Best Time to Visit San Juan

The best time to visit San Juan is from late October to late December, and from the middle of April to mid-June, when the crowds are less and the weather is just right as an average of 24 Celsius. From July to mid-September, San Juan attracts many tourists, which makes it the busiest time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Juan

Do:

  • Take advantage of the beautiful beaches!
  • Try a “Bacalaito” – Fried fishcake served with dipping sauce, it’s very delicious.
  • Wear loose and comfortable clothing, given the warm, humid climate.
  • Learn some Spanish phrases – locals appreciate your effort to communicate in their language.

Don’t:

  • Don’t try to park in Old San Juan.
  • Don’t drink tap water; drink bottled water to avoid an upset stomach.
  • Don’t forget your sunscreen.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Juan

When traveling to any area, regardless of how safe it is, it is essential to be vigilant and take precautions. San Juan is considered relatively safe, the city has the same types of crime seen in most major tourist destinations, so it’s a good idea to avoid walking alone at night and to be mindful of your belongings.

Health in San Juan

Vaccinations such as Tetanus, Hepatitis A, and B are recommended before traveling to San Juan. Also, protect yourself from mosquito bites to avoid diseases like dengue, chikungunya, and Zika.

Getting to San Juan: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Juan is easily accessible with many transportation options available. The main modes of transportation used by tourists are plane, taxi, bus, and car. Some options include:

Airports Near San Juan

  • San Juan Luis Muñoz Marin International Airport (SJU)
  • Mercedita International Airport in Ponce (PSE)

Bus Stations Near San Juan

  • The Metropolitan Bus Authority (AMA) is the most popular form of transportation.
  • The Tren Urbano – is an aboveground train system that runs from Bayamon to San Juan.

Other Options

  • Uber, Lyft, and Taxis are readily available in San Juan.
  • The best option to explore San Juan is to rent a car.

How to Get Around in San Juan

The best way to get around San Juan is to rent a car or catch a taxi or Uber. For an eco-friendly option, rent a bike, and take advantage of the bike routes along the coastline.

Where to Go Next from San Juan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Culebra Island is a nearby Island for natural adventures such as beaches, watersports, and ferry tours.
  • Vieques Island for bioluminescent bay, scuba diving, and history tours.
  • Ponce is a city on the south coast of Puerto Rico where you can explore history and culture and enjoy hiking and sightseeing.
  • Rincon is the perfect relaxing vacation spot with surfing and a quiet family-friendly escape.

Conclusion

San Juan is undoubtedly one of the Caribbean’s most intriguing and beautiful cities, offering something for everyone. With fascinating historical landmarks, vibrant cultural heritage, and breathtaking natural beauty, this vibrant city is sure to inspire and move you. We hope this comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect trip to San Juan and get ready to experience the magic!
