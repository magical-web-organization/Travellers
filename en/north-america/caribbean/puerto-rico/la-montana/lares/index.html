Introduction to Lares

Lares is a municipality situated in the central-mountainous region of Puerto Rico, known for its beautiful rural countryside, hiking trails, and cultural heritage. This picturesque town has a rich history and is the birthplace of the Puerto Rican flag. Lares draws visitors looking for an authentic cultural experience, outdoor recreation, and rural charm.

List of Places to Visit in Lares

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lares Museum: Located in the main plaza of Lares, this museum offers a glimpse into the town’s history, including its role in the Puerto Rican uprising of 1868.
  • La Casa Natal de Ramón Emeterio Betances: The birthplace of Ramón Emeterio Betances, one of the leaders of the aforementioned uprising, is a preserved building that now operates as a museum.
  • Paseo de la Revolución Franquista: An open-air plaza commemorating the 1868 insurrection, where visitors can appreciate the artwork, reflecting the revolutionary movement.

Natural Attractions of Lares: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Montaña Trail: This famous mountain trail rewards hikers with beautiful views of the town and the surrounding tropical landscape.
  • Charco El Guano: A beautiful natural swimming pool, surrounded by lush vegetation and a small waterfall
  • Hacienda La Elena: A popular visitor’s center featuring many outdoor activities, including horseback riding, zip lining, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lares

Lares has several prominent hiking and biking areas throughout the Rio de la Plata Valley, including the Lares Forest Reserve and the Bosque Estatal de la Guajataca. These areas cover more than 6,000 acres of lush tropical forest, riverbanks, and lakes, offering scenic routes for hikers and bikers of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Lares: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: The picturesque countryside of Lares is ideal for horseback riding. Several tour operators provide riding tours through the heart of Lares.
  • Coffee Tours: Lares has a booming coffee industry, and many farms offer guided tours to learn about the coffee-making process and taste the finished product.
  • River Tubing: Lares has several rivers suitable for tubing, including the Toro Negro River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lares

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Lares Museum, do a coffee farm tour, and grab lunch at a local eatery.
Day 2 Hike the La Montaña Trail, swim in Charco El Guano and relax in the evening in your accommodation.

Food and Drink Scene of Lares

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mofongo: A delicious local specialty dish consisting of mashed plantains, garlic, and pork cracklings, often topped with a choice of meat or vegetables.
  • Asopao de Gandules: A hearty stew featuring pigeon peas and meat, often spiced with cilantro and turmeric.
  • Lechón: Roasted suckling pig is a dish commonly served at celebrations.

Local Beverages

  • Café Colao: Locally brewed black coffee, often served with sugar and cinnamon.
  • Pitorro: A Puerto Rican rum-based spirit brewed in different flavors, usually made at home.

Where to Stay in Lares: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Four Winds Parador: A luxury hotel situated in the countryside with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains, excellent food, and a golf course.
  • Villa la Aguada: An eco-friendly guesthouse with stunning views and an infinity pool.
  • Rodríguez Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house situated in the center of town.

What to Buy in Lares: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee: Lares coffee is regarded as one of the best in the Caribbean; be sure to pick up a bag of freshly roasted beans to bring home.
  • Local handicrafts: Visit shops in Lares and surrounding municipalities for unique, handmade souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Lares

Lares has a strong cultural heritage and has long been associated with the Puerto Rican independence movement. Visitors will notice the town’s pride in its history.

Best Time to Visit Lares

The best time to visit Lares is during the dry season, which runs from December to April, offering pleasant temperatures and clear skies for outdoor activities. The wet season, from May to November, can see heavy rains, making some trails and rivers impassable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lares

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Sample local foods

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that destroy or harm the environment
  • Be rude to the locals or disrespect their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lares

Lares is considered a safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should keep their valuables safe and be aware of their surroundings when traveling at night.

Health in Lares

Visitors to Lares should take necessary precautions as advised by their doctors before leaving such as vaccinations, staying hydrated, using insect repellents, and avoiding eating undercooked food.

Getting to Lares: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lares

The closest airport to Lares is Rafael Hernández Airport in Aguadilla, which is approximately 27.2 km away.

Bus Stations Near Lares

The town does not have a central bus terminal, but there are several bus lines that provide transportation throughout Puerto Rico. Visitors can catch bus services at the Aguadilla or Mayagüez bus station.

Other Options

There are several car rental options and private car services available at Rafael Hernández Airport and in Aguadilla.

How to Get Around in Lares

Car rentals are available, but visitors may also opt to hire a taxi or use public transportation to navigate the town. As a rural area, it is suggested to have a car to see the different sights available.

Where to Go Next from Lares: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Juan: The capital of Puerto Rico is two hours away by car and offers history, cultural experiences, and miles of beaches.
  • Moca: A nearby municipality known for its coffee farms and scenic mountain areas with panoramic views.
  • Rincón: A popular tourist destination on the west coast of Puerto Rico, famous for its water sports, beaches, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Lares offers an authentic cultural experience in a beautiful rural setting. Visitors can explore the town’s history, hike the beautiful mountains, sample locally produced coffee and cuisine, and rest in charming accommodations. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Lares has plenty to offer. With its traditional Puerto Rican charm, Lares is not to be missed on any trip to Puerto Rico.
