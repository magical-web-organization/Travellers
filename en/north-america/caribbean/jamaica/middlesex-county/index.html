Introduction to Middlesex County

Welcome to Middlesex County, a hidden gem in Jamaica that boasts of a rich cultural history, diverse experiences, and beautiful natural landscapes. Located in the Caribbean region, Middlesex County sits within the North American continent, making it an easy and convenient destination for travelers seeking a unique Caribbean experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Middlesex County

Middlesex County has a rich cultural history that dates back to the Taino and Arawak indigenous peoples. It is renowned for its music, food, and sports, including cricket and football. Locals in Middlesex County are friendly and hospitable, and it is common to greet strangers and ask about their wellbeing.

Best Time to Visit Middlesex County

The best time to visit Middlesex County is between November and mid-April when the weather is dry and sunny. The temperature ranges from 25 to 30 degrees Celsius, making it ideal for outdoor activities and beach bumming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Middlesex County

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and light clothing during hot and sunny days.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of locals.

Don’t:

  • Swim in rivers and falls without a guide.
  • Wear camouflage clothing.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Middlesex County

Middlesex County is generally safe for travelers, but it is always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking around alone at night and keep a close eye on your belongings when in public places.

Health in Middlesex County

Middlesex County does not have a high risk of communicable diseases. However, tourists should still take necessary precautions such as bringing mosquito repellent and staying hydrated to avoid any health issues.
