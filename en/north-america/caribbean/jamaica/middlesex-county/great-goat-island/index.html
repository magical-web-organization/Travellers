Introduction to Great Goat Island

Great Goat Island is a small island located off the southern coast of Jamaica’s mainland. Despite its small size, the island boasts a wide range of natural and cultural attractions that make it a popular destination for tourists. The island’s history dates back to the colonial era, but it remains largely untouched by the modern world, making it a perfect destination for peace-seekers.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Goat Island

Great Goat Island has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by its African and European roots. The islanders are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature, and visitors are advised to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Great Goat Island

The best time to visit Great Goat Island is from December to April when the weather is dry and sunny. The island can be visited throughout the year, but the rainy season from May to November can bring occasional showers and high humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Goat Island

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Leave no trace policy.
  3. Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Take coral or shells from the island’s beaches.
  2. Litter or damage the island’s environment.
  3. Disrespect the locals or their heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Goat Island

Great Goat Island is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take basic precautions to avoid petty theft or pickpocketing. Always be vigilant and avoid carrying a lot of valuables with you.

Health in Great Goat Island

Visitor’s do not need any vaccinations to enter the island but should be careful of mosquito-borne illnesses such as Zika and Dengue. It is essential to use insect repellent and mosquito nets while on the island and to wear clothing that covers your arms and legs.
