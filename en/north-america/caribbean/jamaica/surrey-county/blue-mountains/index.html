Introduction to Blue Mountains

Blue Mountains is a mountain range located in the Surrey County of Jamaica, in the Caribbean. The range extends from Catherine’s Peak in the south to the Blue and John Crow Mountains National Park in the north. The highest point in the range is Blue Mountain Peak, which stands at 7,402 feet and is the highest point in Jamaica. Blue Mountains is known for its breathtaking scenery, coffee plantations, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Blue Mountains

Blue Mountains is home to a number of museums, historical places, outdoor adventures, and scenic views that are worth visiting. Here is a list of places to visit in Blue Mountains:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bob Marley Museum: Located in Kingston, the museum is dedicated to the life and music of Jamaica’s most famous son, Bob Marley. The museum is housed in Marley’s former residence and features his personal artifacts, recordings, and memorabilia.
  • Devon House: Built in the 19th century, Devon House is a historic landmark in Kingston and a great place to learn about Jamaica’s colonial past. The house is now a museum and features a collection of antique furniture and art.
  • Trench Town Culture Yard Museum: Located in Kingston, the museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Trench Town, a neighborhood that was home to many of Jamaica’s most famous musicians, including Bob Marley and Peter Tosh.

Natural Attractions of Blue Mountains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Mountain Peak: The highest point in Jamaica offers a rewarding hiking experience. The stunning views along the way and from the top makes it worth the trek.
  • Blue and John Crow Mountains National Park: The park is a UNESCO World Heritage site and home to many endemic species of plants and animals. Visitors can explore hiking trails and experience Jamaica’s natural beauty.
  • Coffee Plantations: Jamaica is known for its world-famous Blue Mountain Coffee. A visit to a coffee plantation provides insights into the growing, picking, and processing of coffee people have grown to love.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blue Mountains

Blue Mountains is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. The scenery is breathtaking, and the many trails offer varying degrees of difficulty:

  • Blue Mountain Peak Trail: The challenging but rewarding trek to the highest point in Jamaica - typically either overnight or early-morning hike, and it’s advised to go with a guide.
  • Catherine’s Peak Trail: A scenic day hike that offers breathtaking views of the Jamaican landscape.
  • Coffee Trailing: A guided tour of Blue Mountains and a view of Jamaica’s well-known coffee plantations.

Best Things to Do in Blue Mountains: Activities and Tours

A wide range of things to do in Blue Mountains are available to travelers. Here are some suggestions:

  • Explore the Culture: Experience Jamaica’s rich history and culture by visiting the museums and historical sites in Kingston and other nearby cities.
  • Outdoor Adventures: Enjoy the Blue Mountains by hiking trails, bike riding, or by exploring the natural beauty the country offers.
  • Coffee Tours: Visit one of the coffee plantations and experience the coffee-making process, enjoy a farm-to-cup cup of coffee, and bring back a few bags as souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Blue Mountains

Jamaican cuisine is full of rich flavors and spices. Blue Mountains offers a plethora of food options that cater for various preferences and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jerk chicken: Spicy and flavorful, chicken marinated in jerk sauce is one of Jamaica’s most iconic dishes.
  • Ackee and saltfish: Jamaica’s national dish, made with the local fruits and salted cod.
  • Curried goat: A popular dish that is slow-cooked in a spicy curry sauce, full of flavor.
  • Blue Mountain Coffee: Grown in the Blue Mountains, it is one of the best coffee in the world you can taste in this region.

Local Beverages

  • Rum: Jamaica’s iconic beverage, it is a must-try for any traveler. Jamaican rum is available in different flavors, colors, and brands.
  • Sorrel drink: A sweet and tangy beverage made with hibiscus flowers, ginger, and spices.
  • Jamaican Blue Mountain Coffee: After your coffee tour, how about an espresso or latte from the plantation you just visited?

Where to Stay in Blue Mountains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blue Mountains offers various types of accommodation for travelers, ranging from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some options:

Luxurious Resorts

  • Strawberry Hill: A luxurious resort located high in the Blue Mountains, providing serene panoramic views of the island.
  • Mockingbird Hill: A peaceful eco-boutique hotel in Port Antonio that offers spectacular views of the Caribbean Sea.

Budget-Friendly Guesthouses

  • EITS Cafe & Accommodation: Budget-friendly guesthouse in Kingston that specializes in Jamaican food.
  • Blue Mountain Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse that specializes in outdoor adventures, located in the heart of Blue Mountains.

What to Buy in Blue Mountains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Blue Mountains is full of locally produced artisan crafts, souvenirs, and other unique items. Here are some recommended local products to bring home:

  • Blue Mountain Coffee: Buy a couple of bags of fresh coffee directly from the Blue Mountains, which would make an excellent souvenir or gift.
  • Wooden Carvings: You can find unique wooden carvings handcrafted by locals that reflect Jamaica’s mythology, flora, or fauna.
  • Spices: Jamaican spices like jerk seasoning, pimento berries, and pepper sauce are popular take-home items.

Local Culture and Customs of Blue Mountains

Jamaica has a rich cultural heritage, and Blue Mountains is no exception. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Jamaican Creole is the most commonly spoken language, but English language is also used extensively.
  • Remember to greet locals with “good morning,” “good afternoon,” or “good evening,” and use polite phrases, like “please” and “thank you.”
  • Jamaican culture values modesty and respectfulness, so please dress conservatively when visiting public places like churches, temples, and museums.

Best Time to Visit Blue Mountains

The best time to visit Blue Mountains is between November and April when the weather is dry, and the temperature is pleasant. The wettest months are between May and October, so it may not be suitable for outdoor activities during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blue Mountains

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and safety.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Make unwanted advances towards locals.
  • Buy or use illegal drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blue Mountains

Jamaica’s crime rate is higher than other Caribbean islands, and while Blue Mountains is a relatively safe area, visitors should exercise caution. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings.
  • Avoid unlit and isolated areas.
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash and expensive items.

Health in Blue Mountains

Visitors are advised to take precautions to avoid any health-related issues. Here are some tips:

  • Only drink bottled or filtered water.
  • Pack insect repellent to avoid dengue fever or Zika virus.
  • Consult your doctor for any vaccinations or medication necessary for travel to Jamaica.

Getting to Blue Mountains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Blue Mountains can be accessed via ground transportation from Kingston in about an hour’s drive from the capital. Visitors can also fly into nearby airports from other Caribbean locations and with direct flights from the United States.

Airports Near Blue Mountains

  • Norman Manley International Airport: Located in Kingston with direct flights from the United States and other major cities.
  • Ian Fleming International Airport: Located in Boscobel, St. Mary is about an hour’s drive from the Blue Mountains.

Bus Stations Near Blue Mountains

Buses are the most popular form of ground transportation in Jamaica, and Blue Mountains can be accessed via bus from Kingston.

  • MegaMart: The bus stop is located near the MegaMart shopping complex in Kingston.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available for visitors who want flexibility and convenience to get around the island.

How to Get Around in Blue Mountains

Visitors can get around Blue Mountains by renting a car, hiring a taxi, or taking public transportation. It is advisable to hire a guide that could take care of the transportation for the entire period of stay.

Where to Go Next from Blue Mountains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blue Mountains is a great place to explore on its own, but if you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations to check out:

  • Port Antonio: Known for its beaches, waterfalls, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Kingston: Jamaica’s capital city and the cultural hub of the island, offers various attractions, like museums and music venues.
  • Ocho Rios: Visit the world-famous Dunn’s River Falls and enjoy the beach and other water sport activities.

Conclusion

Blue Mountains offers visitors a mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and delicious cuisine. By following this comprehensive guide, visitors can explore the best of what Blue Mountains has to offer.
