A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

If you’re planning a trip to Cuba and looking for a destination that will give you the opportunity to explore the country’s natural beauty and cultural richness, Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario should be on top of your list. Located in Western Cuba, Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario is a protected area known for its lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and diverse wildlife. The reserve is also home to several historical landmarks and museums that offer insight into Cuba’s past and present.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario, we’ll take a closer look at some of the must-visit places in the reserve, the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

Introduction to Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario is a designated biosphere reserve and protected area in the Sierra del Rosario mountain range in Western Cuba. The reserve covers an area of 25,000 hectares and is home to over 800 species of plants and animals, many of which are endemic to Cuba. The reserve was established in 1985 to protect the region’s unique ecosystem and promote sustainable development in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about Cuba’s history and culture, there are several museums and historical sites in Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario that you shouldn’t miss. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Museo de la Revolución: The Museum of the Revolution in Havana is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum tells the story of the Cuban Revolution and the country’s struggle for independence from colonial rule.
  • Casa Museo Oswaldo Guayasamín: This museum is dedicated to the works of Ecuadorian artist Oswaldo Guayasamín, who was a friend of Fidel Castro. The museum showcases some of Guayasamín’s most famous pieces, as well as personal objects and photographs from his life.
  • Cueva de los Portales: This cave system was used by Che Guevara during the Cuban Missile Crisis as a command post. Today, visitors can explore the cave and see some of the relics left behind by Che and his men.

Natural Attractions of Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Las Terrazas: This eco-village is located in the heart of Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario and is home to several hiking trails, a zip-line course, and a freshwater swimming hole.
  • Cascada el Salto: This waterfall is one of the most popular attractions in the reserve. Visitors can swim in the natural pool at the base of the waterfall and take in the stunning views.
  • Soroa: Soroa is a small town near Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario that is known for its orchid garden and panoramic views of the surrounding hills.
  • Gran Caverna de Santo Tomás: This cave system is the largest in Cuba and one of the largest in the Americas. Visitors can explore over 45 kilometers of tunnels and see natural rock formations, underground rivers, and bat colonies.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario is a great destination for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes:

  • La Ceiba Trail: This trail is a 10-kilometer hike that takes you through lush forests and past several natural swimming holes.
  • Sendero la Cruz: This 5-kilometer hike offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The trail ends at the top of a hill, where you can see a large cross that was erected in honor of the Virgin Mary.
  • El Taburete: This biking trail is a 10-kilometer loop that takes you through the heart of Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario. The trail is suitable for all skill levels and offers great views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Best Things to Do in Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario: Activities and Tours

Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Zip-lining: Las Terrazas offers a zip-line course that takes you through the heart of the reserve and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Swimming: There are several natural swimming holes in and around Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario where you can take a dip and cool off from the heat.
  • Cycling: Rent a bike and explore the reserve at your own pace. The roads in the reserve are well-maintained and offer great views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Guided tours: There are several guided tours available in the reserve that offer insight into the area’s history, culture, and natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

Best Time to Visit Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

The best time to visit Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario is between December and May, which is the dry season in Cuba. The temperatures during this time are mild, and there is little rainfall. The wet season, between June and November, can be hot and humid, with heavy rains.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

Cuba is generally a safe country, with low rates of violent crime. However, visitors should take precautions to protect their belongings and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra del Rosario

Visitors to Cuba should be up to date on their routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine. It’s also recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and yellow fever. Visitors should also take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as dengue fever and Zika virus are present in Cuba.
