If you’re looking for a hidden gem in Cuba, look no further than Soroa. Known as the “Rainbow of Cuba,” Soroa offers a stunning landscape of lush tropical forests, cascading waterfalls, and misty hills, making it a nature lover’s paradise. This travel guide will walk you through everything you need to know, from the best places to visit and things to do to where to stay and what to eat.

Introduction to Soroa

Located in Western Cuba, Soroa is a small rural town nestled in the province of Artemisa. The town is known for its stunning natural beauty, which attracts thousands of tourists every year. It’s a perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse yourself in the tranquility of nature. Soroa is also home to several cultural attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers interested in history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Soroa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Del Veguero: A museum dedicated to the history of tobacco farming in Cuba, featuring exhibits and artifacts related to the Cuban cigar industry.
  • Museo Ernesto Che Guevara: A museum dedicated to the life and legacy of revolutionary leader Che Guevara, featuring exhibits and personal belongings of the iconic figure.
  • Castillo Las Ruinas: An abandoned castle built in the early 20th century, featuring stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Natural Attractions of Soroa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salto de Soroa: A stunning waterfall located in the heart of the forest, offering an excellent opportunity for swimming and hiking.
  • Orquideario Soroa: A botanical garden featuring a vast collection of native Cuban orchids and other tropical plants.
  • Mirador de Soroa: A viewpoint located in the top of a hill, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soroa

Soroa is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • El Salto hike: A three-kilometer hike through the lush forest, leading to the stunning Salto de Soroa waterfall.
  • Cerro La Comadre Trail: A 10-kilometer trek through the mountains, offering panoramic views of the region.
  • Biking tour to Las Terrazas: A four-hour bike ride to Las Terrazas, a nearby town known for its eco-tourism and sustainable community.

Best Things to Do in Soroa: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback riding: Take a horseback ride through the forest and discover the natural beauty of Soroa.
  • Zip-lining: Experience the adrenaline rush of zip-lining through the forest canopy.
  • Tobacco farm tour: Learn about the process of tobacco farming and enjoy a hand-rolled Cuban cigar.

Food and Drink Scene of Soroa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ropa Vieja: A traditional Cuban dish made with shredded beef, onions, and peppers, served with rice and black beans.
  • Moros y Cristianos: A rice and black bean dish seasoned with cumin, garlic, and onions, often served alongside roasted pork.
  • Tostones: Fried plantain slices served with garlic sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Mojito: A refreshing cocktail made with white rum, sugar, lime juice, soda, and mint leaves.
  • Piña Colada: A tropical cocktail made with pineapple juice, coconut cream, and white rum.
  • Café Cubano: Strong, sweet espresso served with sugar.

Where to Stay in Soroa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel La Moka: A luxury eco-hotel located in the heart of the forest, offering stunning views and top-notch amenities.
  • Villa Soroa: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the town center, offering comfortable accommodations and friendly service.
  • Hotel Castillo: A historic castle turned hotel, offering stunning views and unique accommodations.

What to Buy in Soroa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cigars: Cuban cigars are world-renowned for their high quality and rich flavor, making them the perfect souvenir.
  • Coffee: Cuban coffee is known for its smooth, rich flavor, and makes an excellent gift for coffee lovers.
  • Handicrafts: Local artisans in Soroa produce a wide variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, textiles, and woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Soroa

Soroa is a small, rural town, where many of the locals still hold traditional customs and beliefs. Visitors should be respectful of these customs, including dressing modestly, refraining from public displays of affection, and avoiding loud or disruptive behavior. It’s also customary to greet locals with a smile and a friendly “Hola!”

Best Time to Visit Soroa

The best time to visit Soroa is from November to April, when the weather is mild and dry. May to October is the rainy season, with heavy rainfall common during these months. However, even during the rainy season, Soroa is lush and green, making it a beautiful destination year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soroa

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Greet locals with a smile and friendly “Hola!”
  • Be respectful of local customs and beliefs
  • Remember that Spanish is the primary language

Don’t:

  • Engage in loud or disruptive behavior
  • Publicly display affection
  • Assume that everyone speaks English
  • Ignore the rules of outdoor areas, including no littering

Safety and Health

Safety in Soroa

Soroa is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety, including being aware of their surroundings, not carrying large amounts of cash, and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Soroa

Cuba has an excellent healthcare system, and most major cities and tourist areas have medical facilities staffed with trained professionals. Visitors should be up to date on all routine vaccinations before traveling to Cuba and should also consider the hepatitis A and typhoid vaccines.

Getting to Soroa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Soroa

The nearest international airport to Soroa is Havana’s José Martí International Airport, located approximately 2 hours away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Soroa

Soroa has a bus station that serves both local and long-distance routes. The Víazul bus company also operates from Havana to Soroa with daily schedules.

Other Options

Private taxis are also available for hire from Havana or nearby towns. Also, local drivers often work as taxi drivers and can offer transport services upon arrival.

How to Get Around in Soroa

The best way to explore Soroa is by foot, but taxis and rental cars are also available. Bicycles are also available to rent if you’re feeling more adventurous or want to cover more ground.

Where to Go Next from Soroa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Las Terrazas: A nearby eco-tourism village known for its sustainable development and natural beauty.
  • Viñales: A small town known for its stunning landscapes and rocky outcrops, with opportunities for horseback riding and exploring tobacco farms.
  • Havana: The vibrant capital of Cuba, known for its stunning architecture, lively music scene, and rich history.

Conclusion

Soroa is a hidden gem in Cuba, offering stunning natural beauty, exciting outdoor activities, and rich cultural history. From hiking through the forest to exploring the local culture, Soroa has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to relax in nature or immerse yourself in authentic Cuban culture, Soroa is the perfect destination.
