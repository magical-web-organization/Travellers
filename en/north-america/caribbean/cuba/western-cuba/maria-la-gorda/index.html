Introduction to Maria la Gorda

Maria la Gorda is a remote and isolated beach town located in western Cuba. Known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and abundance of marine life, it is a haven for scuba diving enthusiasts and beach lovers alike. If you’re looking for a quiet and peaceful getaway, Maria la Gorda is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Maria la Gorda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pinar del Rio Province: The province of Pinar del Rio is home to several interesting museums and historical sites, including the Museum of Natural History and the Francisco Donatien Cigar Factory.
  • Viñales Valley: This picturesque valley is dotted with limestone cliffs and tobacco farms, and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • San Juan y Martinez: This small town is known for its tobacco industry and is home to several tobacco factories, including the El Paraiso cigar factory.

Natural Attractions of Maria la Gorda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scuba Diving: Maria la Gorda is best known for its exceptional scuba diving opportunities, with several dive sites located just off shore. Expect to see a variety of sea life, including sea turtles, sharks, and colorful fish.
  • Beaches: The beaches in Maria la Gorda are some of the most beautiful in Cuba, with crystal clear waters and soft, white sand. Some of the most popular beaches include Playa Blanca and Playa Las Tumbas.
  • La Guira National Park: This national park is located just north of Maria la Gorda and is home to diverse wildlife, including hawks and parrots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maria la Gorda

  • Viñales Valley Hike: This hike through the picturesque Viñales Valley takes you through tobacco farms and other stunning landscapes.
  • La Guira National Park Hike: There are several hiking trails throughout La Guira National Park, ranging from easy to difficult. The trails take you through various landscapes and offer stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Maria la Gorda: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving: As mentioned, scuba diving is a must-do activity in Maria la Gorda. There are several dive shops in town that offer courses for beginners as well as excursions for experienced divers.
  • Beach Activities: Aside from swimming and sunbathing, many visitors enjoy other beach activities like kayaking and snorkeling.
  • Tours: There are several tour operators in Maria la Gorda that offer guided tours of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Maria la Gorda

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ropa Vieja: This is a traditional Cuban dish made from shredded beef, vegetables, and spices.
  • Congri: This is a popular side dish made from black beans and rice.
  • Lechon: A Cuban roasted pork dish, often served with plantains and yuca.

Local Beverages

  • Mojitos: This refreshing cocktail is made from rum, sugar, mint, and lime.
  • Pina Colada: A tropical favorite made with rum, pineapple juice, and coconut cream.

Where to Stay in Maria la Gorda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Maria la Gorda Hotel: As the only hotel in the area, Maria la Gorda Hotel offers comfortable accommodations and amenities, including a restaurant and dive shop.
  • Casa Particulares: There are several privately-owned guesthouses and hostels in the area that offer affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Maria la Gorda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cigars: Pinar del Rio is known for its high-quality tobacco, and there are several cigar factories in the area.
  • Salsa Music CDs: Cuban salsa is an iconic part of the country’s culture and music scene.
  • Local Artwork: There are several local artists in the area whose work can be found in art galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Maria la Gorda

  • Language: The official language of Cuba is Spanish, and while some locals may speak English, it’s always helpful to have some basic Spanish skills.
  • Tipping: It’s customary to leave a tip for restaurant servers and taxi drivers in Cuba.
  • Etiquette: Cubans are generally friendly and hospitable, but it’s important to be respectful of local customs, especially around topics like politics and religion.

Best Time to Visit Maria la Gorda

The best time to visit Maria la Gorda is from December to May, when the weather is dry and temperatures are mild. June to November is hurricane season, and while storms are rare in this part of Cuba, they can still occur.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maria la Gorda

Do:

  • Try out scuba diving or snorkeling.
  • Explore the nearby natural attractions and parks.
  • Interact with locals and learn about the Cuban culture and way of living.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Overstay your visa or travel past authorized areas.
  • Bring banned items into the country.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maria la Gorda

Cuba is generally a safe country, with low levels of crime. Visitors to Maria la Gorda should still take basic precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secured.

Health in Maria la Gorda

Visitors to Cuba should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, rubella, and diphtheria-pertussis-tetanus. It’s also important to take measures to prevent mosquito bites, as Zika and other mosquito-borne illnesses can be present in the area.

Getting to Maria la Gorda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maria la Gorda

The nearest airport to Maria la Gorda is the Vinales Airport, located approximately 70 km away. International travelers often fly into Jose Marti International Airport, located in the capital city of Havana.

Bus Stations Near Maria la Gorda

Viazul is the primary bus company in Cuba and offers services to and from many major cities, including Havana. The bus station in nearby Pinar del Rio is the closest to Maria la Gorda.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also options for getting to and around Maria la Gorda.

How to Get Around in Maria la Gorda

Walking and biking are popular options for exploring the town of Maria la Gorda. Taxis and car rentals are available for those wanting to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Maria la Gorda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Havana: Cuba’s vibrant capital city, known for its classic cars, impressive architecture, and rich culture.
  • Viñales: This picturesque valley town is known for its limestone cliffs, tobacco farms, and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Maria la Gorda is a hidden gem in the western part of Cuba. With its beautiful beaches, exceptional scuba diving opportunities, and nearby natural attractions, it’s a destination that should not be missed. Plan your trip accordingly and be prepared to enjoy a relaxing and memorable vacation in this unique part of the world.
