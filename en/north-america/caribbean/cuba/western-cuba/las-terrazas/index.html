Introduction to Las Terrazas

Nestled in the heart of the Sierra del Rosario mountain range in Western Cuba, Las Terrazas is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The small eco-village of Las Terrazas was established in 1968 as part of a reforestation project and is now a UNESCO biosphere reserve with a thriving community and a rich cultural heritage. This beautiful rural community offers visitors a unique opportunity to discover the natural beauty of Cuba while experiencing the rich culture and traditions of its people.

List of Places to Visit in Las Terrazas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Cueva: This museum is located inside a limestone cave and features exhibitions on the history and culture of the area.
  • Hacienda La Union: This former coffee plantation offers a glimpse into the colonial history of Cuba and its economic past.
  • Orquideario: This garden is home to numerous species of orchids and is a must-see for nature lovers.

Natural Attractions of Las Terrazas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bañadero de San Juan: This natural pool is a popular spot for swimming and relaxing.
  • Salto del Cafetal: This waterfall is located in the heart of the forest and is a popular spot for hiking.
  • Mirador del Xanhuitl: This viewpoint offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Las Terrazas

  • El Taburete: This hiking trail takes visitors to the summit of a hill with a panoramic view of the forest and surrounding area.
  • La Serafina: This mountain biking trail is a challenging route that takes visitors through the stunning scenery of the Sierra del Rosario mountain range.
  • Sendero El Infierno: This hiking trail is named after its steep incline, but the stunning views make it worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Las Terrazas: Activities and Tours

  • La Vega Farm: Visitors can tour this organic farm and enjoy a delicious farm-to-table lunch.
  • Canopy Tour: A thrilling zip line adventure through the lush forest canopy.
  • Boat Tour of Lake San Juan: This tour takes visitors on a relaxing boat ride through the tranquil waters of Lake San Juan.

Food and Drink Scene of Las Terrazas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ropa Vieja: Shredded beef in a tasty tomato sauce.
  • Arroz congrí: A traditional Cuban dish made with rice, black beans, and spices.
  • Tostones: Fried plantains served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Mojito: A refreshing cocktail made with rum, lime, mint, and soda.
  • Cafecito: A strong, sweet espresso served in a small cup.
  • Cerveza Cristal: A light lager beer brewed in Cuba.

Where to Stay in Las Terrazas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Moka: This unique hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and is built around a towering tree.
  • La Moka: An eco-hotel built with sustainable materials and surrounded by natural beauty.
  • Casa Grande: A charming guesthouse with a beautiful garden and friendly host.

What to Buy in Las Terrazas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cigars: Cuba is famous for its high-quality cigars, and Las Terrazas has several shops selling the best brands.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local artisans sell a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, woven baskets, and traditional clothing.
  • Coffee: Las Terrazas is known for its organic coffee, which is available at many local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Las Terrazas

Cubans are warm and welcoming, and their culture is a blend of African, Spanish, and indigenous influences. Visitors should take the time to learn about the local customs and traditions, including the importance of family and community, and the vibrant music and dance scene.

Best Time to Visit Las Terrazas

The best time to visit Las Terrazas is during the dry season from November to April when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. However, visitors can enjoy this beautiful destination year-round, with activities and tours available throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Las Terrazas

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Learn some basic Spanish to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Expect everything to be perfect.
  • Be disrespectful towards the Cuban government or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Las Terrazas

Cuba is a safe country with low crime rates, and Las Terrazas is no exception. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as avoiding dark alleys and carrying valuables in a secure location. It is also advisable to purchase travel insurance before traveling to Cuba.

Health in Las Terrazas

Cuba has a well-developed healthcare system, and Las Terrazas has a small clinic that can handle minor medical issues. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid contracting food-borne illnesses, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding street food.

Getting to Las Terrazas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Las Terrazas

The nearest airport to Las Terrazas is Jose Marti International Airport in Havana, which is approximately 50 miles away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to Las Terrazas from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Las Terrazas

The Viazul Bus Terminal in Havana is the main bus station for travel in Cuba and has connections to Las Terrazas.

Other Options

Private taxis and shared cars, known as “Collectivos,” are also available for transport to Las Terrazas from Havana or other cities in Cuba.

How to Get Around in Las Terrazas

Exploring Las Terrazas on foot, bike, or horseback is the best way to see the natural beauty of the area. Car rentals are available in Havana or other cities in Cuba, but visitors should be aware that the roads in rural areas can be challenging to navigate. Local taxis and buses are also available but may not run on a regular schedule.

Where to Go Next from Las Terrazas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Viñales: This picturesque town is famous for its tobacco plantations and stunning karst landscape.
  • Soroa: Known as the “Rainbow of Cuba,” Soroa is home to a beautiful waterfall and lush gardens.
  • Havana: Cuba’s capital and largest city is a vibrant destination with a rich cultural heritage and world-class museums and restaurants.

Conclusion

Las Terrazas is a unique and beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of Cuba and experience the warmth and hospitality of its people. From hiking and biking to cultural attractions and delicious Cuban cuisine, Las Terrazas has something to offer everyone.
