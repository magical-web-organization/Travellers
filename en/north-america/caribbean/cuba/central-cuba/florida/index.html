Florida, a state located in the southeastern region of the United States, is known for its sunny beaches, theme parks, rich cultural history, and natural scenic beauty. It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to explore outdoor adventures, engage in cultural activities, or simply relax by the beach.

In this comprehensive tour guide of Florida, we will guide you through the must-visit attractions, activities, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, local customs, safety measures, transport options, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Florida

Florida is known as the “Sunshine State” because of its warm and sunny weather throughout the year. The state covers an area of over 65,000 square miles and has a population of over 21 million, making it the third-most populous state in the US. The state’s economy is mainly dependent on tourism, which brings in millions of visitors each year.

Florida is located in Central Cuba, in Cuba, in the Caribbean, in North America. It shares borders with Georgia and Alabama to the north and the Gulf of Mexico to the west. The state is linked to the mainland US by several roads and bridges, including the famous Overseas Highway that connects the Florida Keys to the mainland.

List of Places to Visit in Florida

  1. Miami Beach - A popular destination for its white-sand beaches and vibrant nightlife.

  2. Walt Disney World - The world’s most popular themed amusement park that attracts visitors of all ages.

  3. Everglades National Park - A unique ecosystem that is home to several endangered species, including the manatee and Florida panther.

  4. Kennedy Space Center - A famous NASA facility that offers visitors an opportunity to learn about space exploration.

  5. Universal Studios - Another popular theme park featuring movie-inspired rides and attractions.

  6. St. Augustine - A historical city with beautiful architecture and a rich cultural heritage dating back to the Spanish colonial period.

  7. Clearwater Beach - A picturesque beach town located on the Gulf of Mexico, famous for its soft white sand and clear blue waters.

  8. Key West - A laid-back island city that is known for its art galleries, luxurious resorts, and vibrant nightlife.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Florida has a rich cultural and historical heritage, which can be explored through its many museums and historical attractions. Here are some of the most notable places:

  1. The Ringling Museum of Art - Located in Sarasota, this museum is home to an impressive collection of artworks, including pieces by Rubens, Velázquez, and other renowned European artists.

  2. The Castillo de San Marcos - A historic fort in St. Augustine that dates back to the 17th century and was used by the Spanish to defend Florida.

  3. The Vizcaya Museum and Gardens - A former villa of a wealthy businessman turned museum in Miami that features stunning Italian Renaissance-style architecture and beautiful water gardens.

  4. The Museum of Science and Industry - A popular museum in Tampa that features interactive exhibits on technology, space exploration, and more.

Natural Attractions of Florida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Florida is home to several natural wonders, from its beautiful beaches to its unique ecosystems. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  1. Cape Canaveral National Seashore - A 24-mile long stretch of pristine beach located along the Atlantic coast, known for its natural beauty and abundant wildlife.

  2. The Dry Tortugas - A group of islands located off the coast of Key West, the site of a historic fort and home to an abundance of marine life, including sea turtles and colorful tropical fish.

  3. The Florida Keys - A chain of islands located off the southern coast of Florida that offer stunning sunsets, white sand beaches, and some of the best snorkeling and scuba diving spots in the US.

  4. The Everglades National Park - A unique ecosystem consisting of swamps, marshes, and mangroves, teeming with wildlife such as alligators, panthers, and manatees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Florida

Florida’s flat terrain and beautiful natural scenery make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. The Tamiami Trail - A scenic highway that runs from Miami to Tampa and offers breathtaking views of the Everglades.

  2. The Legacy Trail - A 10-mile long paved trail in Sarasota County that is ideal for both biking and hiking.

  3. The Sanibel Island Bike Trail - A 22-mile long trail that takes bikers through the scenic beauty of Sanibel Island.

  4. The Blackwater Heritage Trail - A 10-mile long trail in Milton, Florida, that takes bikers through the beautiful Blackwater River State Forest.

Best Things to Do in Florida: Activities and Tours

Florida offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to visitors of all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Florida:

  1. Take an airboat ride in the Everglades and experience the unique ecosystem up close.

  2. Explore the Kennedy Space Center and learn about the history of space exploration.

  3. Take a sunset catamaran cruise along the Gulf of Mexico and enjoy breathtaking views.

  4. Go on a food tour of Miami and taste some of the city’s famous Cuban cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Florida

To make the most of your visit to Florida, here is a week-long itinerary that covers some of the state’s most famous attractions and activities:

Day 1: Visit the Everglades National Park and experience the unique natural ecosystem through an airboat ride.

Day 2: Spend the day at Walt Disney World, enjoying the rides, shows, and attractions.

Day 3: Explore the city of St. Augustine and its historic landmarks and architecture.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the Dry Tortugas and go snorkeling in its crystal-clear waters.

Day 5: Visit the Kennedy Space Center and learn about the history of space exploration.

Day 6: Relax at Clearwater Beach and enjoy the beautiful white sand and blue waters.

Day 7: Spend the day in Miami, exploring its famous beaches, art museums, and Cuban cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Florida

Florida’s cuisine is heavily influenced by Latin American and Caribbean flavors, with seafood being a popular local delicacy. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Key Lime Pie - A tart and sweet dessert made with Florida’s famous Key limes.

  2. Conch fritters - A popular appetizer made with conch meat and spices.

  3. Stone Crab claws - Freshly caught crab claws that are served chilled with mustard sauce.

  4. Cuban sandwich - A classic sandwich made with roasted pork, ham, cheese, and pickles.

Local Beverages

  1. Cuba Libre - A rum and coke cocktail that has become popular in Florida due to its Cuban influence.

  2. Key West Sunset Ale - A popular local beer made with Key lime juice.

  3. Gatorade - A popular sports drink that was invented in Florida and named after the Florida Gators football team.

Where to Stay in Florida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Florida offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  1. The Breakers Palm Beach - A luxurious resort in Palm Beach that offers stunning ocean views and top-notch amenities.

  2. The Generator Miami Hostel - A trendy hostel in Miami Beach that is perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

  3. The Postcard Inn - A charming beach hotel in Islamorada that offers an authentic Florida Keys experience.

  4. The Pelican Grand Beach Resort - A family-friendly resort in Fort Lauderdale that offers oceanfront views and a range of activities for kids and adults.

What to Buy in Florida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Florida offers a unique shopping experience, with plenty of options for souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  1. Orange Blossom Honey - A sweet and fragrant honey made from the nectar of Florida orange trees.

  2. Citrus fruits - Florida is known for its citrus fruits, especially oranges and grapefruits.

  3. Seashells - Florida’s beaches offer a wide range of seashells, which make for great souvenirs or decorations.

  4. Key lime flavored products - Key lime pie, Key lime jelly, and Key lime cookies are popular products available throughout the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Florida

Florida is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from Latin America, the Caribbean, and the Deep South. Its residents are known for their laid-back attitude and love for outdoor activities. In terms of customs, tipping is expected in restaurants, bars, and other service establishments. It’s also a good idea to dress appropriately when visiting churches or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Florida

The best time to visit Florida is during the winter months, between December and February, when the weather is mild and sunny, and the crowds are less. However, this is also the peak season, so prices tend to be higher. For a more affordable and less crowded experience, visit Florida in the shoulder seasons of March-May or September-November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Florida

Do:

  1. Wear sunscreen to protect yourself from the strong Florida sun.

  2. Respect the wildlife and natural habitats; do not feed or disturb the animals.

  3. Try the local cuisine, especially the seafood and Cuban specialties.

  4. Dress appropriately for the occasion, whether it’s a day at the beach or a night out in the city.

Don’t:

  1. Swim in areas that are not designated for swimming.

  2. Litter on the beaches or in natural areas.

  3. Underestimate the strength of the currents or tides in the ocean.

  4. Forget to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Florida

Florida is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Avoid displaying expensive jewelry or cash in public, stay alert in busy tourist areas, and lock your car doors when driving. If you’re planning to visit any natural areas, be aware of the local wildlife and weather conditions.

Health in Florida

Florida’s healthcare system is generally of good quality, with several hospitals and clinics located throughout the state. If you’re traveling from outside the US, it’s a good idea to purchase travel health insurance before your trip. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as Zika and West Nile virus have been reported in Florida, so it’s important to take measures to protect yourself, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Florida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Florida is easily accessible from other parts of the US and beyond, with several airports, bus stations, and major highways located throughout the state.

Airports Near Florida

  2. Orlando International Airport
  3. Tampa International Airport

Bus Stations Near Florida

Other Options

Florida is also accessible by car, train, and boat. Amtrak has several train routes that serve Florida, and several major highways, including the I-95 and I-75, run through the state.

How to Get Around in Florida

Public transportation in Florida is limited, so it’s recommended to rent a car or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft to get around. Taxis are also available in major cities, but they can be expensive. For those visiting the theme parks, most offer free shuttles or tram service from parking lots to the park entrance.

Where to Go Next from Florida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Florida is close to several other popular tourist destinations, making it an ideal starting point for further travel. Here are some of the best nearby destinations:

  1. The Bahamas - Located just a short boat or plane ride from Florida, the Bahamas offer stunning beaches, luxury resorts, and crystal-clear waters.

  2. New Orleans - Known for its vibrant music scene, delicious Creole cuisine, and unique cultural heritage, New Orleans is a must-visit destination for any traveler.

  3. Atlanta - Known for its rich history, lush forests, and southern charm, Atlanta is a great destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and foodies alike.

  4. Savannah - A charming and historic southern city with beautiful squares, parks, and architecture that offer a glimpse into colonial America.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural activities, or just a simple beach vacation, Florida has something to offer for everyone. With its warm weather, beautiful natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage, it’s no wonder that it’s one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Florida. Happy travels!
