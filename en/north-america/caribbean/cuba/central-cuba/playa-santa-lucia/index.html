Introduction to Playa Santa Lucia

Playa Santa Lucia is a hidden gem located on the north coast of Cuba. This beautiful beach paradise is known for its crystal-clear waters and pristine white sand beaches. Whether you’re looking to soak up some sun, discover the local culture, or embark on an adventure, Playa Santa Lucia has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know to plan your trip to this incredible destination in Cuba.

List of Places to Visit in Playa Santa Lucia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Playa Santa Lucia: This museum offers an insight into the history and culture of Playa Santa Lucia and is a great place to start your trip.
  • Chorro de Maita Museum: A fascinating archaeological site that showcases the history of the Taíno culture, featuring ancient burial grounds and intricate pottery displays.
  • La Loma de la Cruz: A historical site famous for its cross, this is a popular spot for visitors looking to take in some stunning views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Playa Santa Lucia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa Santa Lucia Beach: The main attraction of Playa Santa Lucia is its stunning beach. Stretching over 13 miles, its clear waters and soft sand provide a perfect place for relaxation.
  • Laguna Grande: A beautiful lagoon surrounded by lush mangroves that is perfect for kayaking.
  • Cayo Sabinal Island: A secluded and peaceful retreat that offers a glimpse of untouched nature and beautiful sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Playa Santa Lucia

  • El Gato Natural Park: A stunning natural park that offers several hiking and biking routes through the stunning Caribbean scenery.
  • Cayo Cruz: A scenic spot where you can enjoy a leisurely bike ride or hike and take in breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Playa Santa Lucia: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: With crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life, Playa Santa Lucia is a prime location for snorkeling and diving.
  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity in Playa Santa Lucia, with many charter companies offering excursions to catch local fish species.
  • Boating: You can take a boat tour to explore the stunning coastline and experience the beautiful sunsets of Playa Santa Lucia.

Food and Drink Scene of Playa Santa Lucia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Congri: A traditional Cuban dish made with black beans and rice.
  • Ropa Vieja: A savory dish made with shredded beef stewed in a tomato-based sauce.
  • Yuca con Mojo: A tasty side dish made with yucca and garlic sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Mojito: A refreshing cocktail made with rum, sugar, lime juice, soda water, and mint leaves.
  • Piña Colada: A sweet cocktail made with rum, pineapple, and coconut cream.
  • Cristal beer: A light beer and a popular drink choice in Cuba.

Where to Stay in Playa Santa Lucia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Club Amigo Mayanabo: A beachfront paradise with all-inclusive options and multiple dining options.
  • Gran Club Santa Lucia: A popular resort with several bars, restaurants, and water activities.
  • Hotel Brisas Santa Lucia: A family-friendly hotel with a beautiful pool and stunning beachfront views.

What to Buy in Playa Santa Lucia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cuban Cigars: Cuba is famous for its cigars, and Playa Santa Lucia is no exception. Be sure to visit a local tobacco shop to pick up some high-quality cigars.
  • Guayabera: A traditional Cuban shirt that features two or four pockets and beautiful embroidery.
  • Rum: Cuba is famous for its high-quality rum, and Playa Santa Lucia is no exception. Be sure to try some of the local rum and pick up a bottle to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Playa Santa Lucia

Cuban culture is warm and welcoming, and this can be seen in Playa Santa Lucia. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Tipping: It is customary to tip in Cuba, especially in the service industry. We recommend tipping 10% in restaurants and bars.
  • Greetings: Cubans are warm and friendly and will often greet each other with a hug or kiss on the cheek. Be prepared for this if you’re meeting locals.
  • Language: Spanish is the primary language in Cuba. While many locals speak some English, it’s a good idea to learn a few basic phrases to get by.

Best Time to Visit Playa Santa Lucia

The best time to visit Playa Santa Lucia is from December to April. The weather during this time is dry and sunny, with temperatures ranging from 22°C to 28°C. May to November is the rainy season in Cuba, but Playa Santa Lucia is less affected by this than other parts of the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Playa Santa Lucia

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Prioritize safety when engaging in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or culture.
  • Engage in illegal activities.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Playa Santa Lucia

Playa Santa Lucia is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to be cautious and aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring local areas at night. We recommend keeping your valuables safe and using a safe or locker at your hotel.

Health in Playa Santa Lucia

Cuba has a high standard of healthcare, and there are medical facilities throughout the country. We recommend purchasing travel insurance for your trip and packing any necessary medications you may need. Be sure to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses by using insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing in the evenings.

Getting to Playa Santa Lucia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Playa Santa Lucia

The nearest airport to Playa Santa Lucia is Ignacio Agramonte International Airport in Camagüey, which is about an hour’s drive away. Another option is Jardines del Rey International Airport in Ciego de Avila, which is about two hours’ drive from Playa Santa Lucia.

Bus Stations Near Playa Santa Lucia

Viazul is Cuba’s main bus company and has several daily buses to Playa Santa Lucia from cities such as Camagüey and Havana.

Other Options

Private taxis are also a popular choice for transportation in Cuba, and many can be booked through your hotel.

How to Get Around in Playa Santa Lucia

The best way to get around Playa Santa Lucia is by taxi or rented car. There are several car rental companies available, and taxis can be easily found throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Playa Santa Lucia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Camagüey: A beautiful colonial city with stunning architecture and lively streets.
  • Ciego de Avila: Known for its art and culture, this city is a great place to immerse yourself in the local scene and visit museums and galleries.
  • Cayo Coco: Another island paradise located just a short drive away from Playa Santa Lucia, featuring stunning beaches and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Playa Santa Lucia is a truly stunning destination with something for everyone, from beautiful beaches to cultural attractions and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for relaxation or adventure, this captivating place is sure to leave a lasting impression. With this guide, you have everything you need to plan your perfect trip to Playa Santa Lucia in Cuba.
