Introduction to Cayo Coco

Cayo Coco is a stunning island located in Central Cuba, which boasts of pristine beaches, clear turquoise waters, lush mangrove forests, and excellent opportunities for exploring marine life. There are many places to visit and many things to do on this beautiful island, and this travel guide aims to provide a comprehensive overview of everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Cayo Coco.

List of Places to Visit in Cayo Coco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jardines del Rey: A collection of islands of which Cayo Coco is a part of, each offering its unique charm, and the entire area is named Jardines del Rey (Gardens of the King) in honor of Spanish monarch Ferdinand II who reportedly believed this beautiful place to be the property of the gods.
  • El Baga Natural Park: Located in the northern part of the island, this park is perfect for exploring the natural scenery of Cayo Coco. The area has become an ecotourism destination in recent years, and you can immerse yourself in the diverse flora and fauna and get to know the Cuban ecosystem.
  • Pueblo La Estrella: An old town that has been converted into an attraction popular with tourists. The site has a beautiful lake with flamingos, a small train ride, and a live show of Cuban folklore dancing.
  • Moron: Considered the biggest city of the region. Placed in the mainland, offers historical landmarks like the Museum of Cuisine and the Railway Museum.

Natural Attractions of Cayo Coco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laguna La Redonda: A lagoon with a diameter of 200 meters, is a serene and lovely place to visit. The water is crystal clear and reflects all kinds of colors, which makes it an excellent spot for photography.
  • Playa Pilar: The most famous beach of the area, it was named after the boat of Ernest Hemingway and offers one of the best views of the Caribbean.
  • La Cueva del Jabali: A natural cave, which translates to the cave of the boar. The cave’s surroundings are perfect for exploring nature and sightseeing the island.
  • Pilar Beach: Known for both its stunning scenery and its great location for water sports. The beach has a couple of bars, chairs, and umbrellas to spend a day under the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cayo Coco

Cayo Coco has plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, with many scenic routes located throughout the island. Here are a few of the best options:

  • El Bagá Natural Park: This park offers several walking routes that will take you through the diverse natural environment of Cayo Coco.
  • Playa Larga: A long and uncrowded beach along the island, which provides an excellent chance to walk and watch the stars at night.
  • Moron: A bigger city with an urban appeal that offers a bike rental service. A lovely way to see the old town.

Best Things to Do in Cayo Coco: Activities and Tours

Cayo Coco offers plenty of exciting things to do for visitors, including.

  • Kayaking and Scuba Diving: Take a tour boat to explore the corals and other sea life. Supposedly there are more than 200 fish species to be seen on these reefs.
  • Fishing Tours: Go fishing on the Kimbarra, or rent a cheap rod to fish from the beach. Whatever way in which you’ll like to make your mark on the fishing scene, you can do it here.
  • Catamaran Tour: Head out to the sea in a catamaran and explore the coastline of Cayo Coco from the water. The activity also includes a snorkeling stop.

Food and Drink Scene of Cayo Coco

Must-Try Dishes

Cayo Coco’s food scene offers visitors the chance to taste some of the region’s classic and exotic local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Cuban Sandwich: A traditional sandwich originating from Cuba, in which ham, cheese, and roast pork are pressed together in white bread. This sandwich is a must-try when visiting Cayo Coco.
  • Ropa Vieja: A stew made with shredded beef, tomatoes, peppers, onions, and other spices. This dish is usually served with white rice and black beans.
  • Arroz con Pollo (Rice with Chicken): A popular Cuban dish that combines chicken with yellow rice, vegetables, and spices. One of the most popular dishes in Cuba.
  • Ceviche: Fresh fish marinated in lime juice, onions, and peppers served with plantain chips or bread. One of the staple dishes of the whole Caribbean region.

Local Beverages

Here are some of the most popular local beverages you must try during your visit to Cayo Coco:

  • Mojito: Made with rum, sugar, lime juice, mint leaves, and soda water, the refreshing drink makes a perfect cocktail for the tropical climate.
  • Cuban Coffee: Strong coffee that is usually served in small shots and has a strong flavor and aroma. Cubans love to add sugar while brewing the coffee.
  • Cuban Beer: Refreshing lagers like Cristal and Bucanero and popular dark beer called Mayabe.

Where to Stay in Cayo Coco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cayo Coco has plenty of accommodation options to suit a range of budgets and needs, whether you’re looking for a luxurious beach resort, a small local hostel, or a budget-friendly guesthouse.

  • Iberostar Cayo Coco: A fantastic all-inclusive five-star resort that has its private beach and offers plenty of dining, bars, and entertainment options.
  • Pestana Cayo Coco: A mid-range resort offering excellent value for money with a private beach, diving and snorkeling excursions, and many other recreational activities.
  • Hotel Colonial Moron: As in the mainland, there are many hotel options too. This historic-style hotel has a restaurant, free parking, and free Wi-Fi for leisure travelers.

What to Buy in Cayo Coco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cayo Coco has many gift shops, markets, and street vendors that sell a wide range of locally made products representing Cuban culture and traditions. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Cayo Coco:

  • Cuban Cigars: This item is probably on the top of the list of things to bring from Cuba; they are known everywhere as the quintessential Cuban product.
  • Mojito Mix: If you fell in love with this refreshing drink, a mix of the famous mint and sugar drink can be found in local stores around the island.
  • Rum: Grab a bottle of rum from Cuba, the best known (and official) drink of the country.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: Local souvenirs include wood and plaster carvings, paintings, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Cayo Coco

Cuba is a unique country with a rich cultural history that blends European and African influences. The people of Cayo Coco are friendly, and visitors will experience their hospitality during their stay. Learning a few customs and expressions can go a long way:

  • Greetings: Always greet people when you meet them and say goodbye when leaving. Address people as Senor or Senorita followed by the person’s last name.
  • Tipping: Tipping is an essential part of the Cuban tourism industry. Give the appropriate tip to people who provide good service.
  • When it gets crowded, be patient. Cubans get crowded or stand close to each other a lot, so it’s important not to get irritable with them.
  • Be respectful of Cuban culture: Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions of the country.

Best Time to Visit Cayo Coco

The best time to visit Cayo Coco is during the dry season, which starts in November and ends in April. This period sees less rainfall and an average temperature of 25°C. The peak season is between December and January when vacationers from different countries come to celebrate Christmas and New Year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cayo Coco

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Cayo Coco:

Do:

  • Learn a few Spanish phrases: Even though the people in Cayo Coco are friendly and talkative, it’s always better to keep some basic Spanish phrases or words with yourself.
  • Eat the local cuisine: Cuban food is unique, and visitors should try different dishes to experience the local culture.
  • Buy local souvenirs: Purchasing handmade crafts, artwork, or other significant items is a great way to support small businesses and artisans and bring back a special memory of your trip.

Don’t:

  • Engage in prohibited activities: Drugs are strictly prohibited in Cuba, and other illegal activities, such as prostitution, are also punished severely.
  • Photograph locals without permission: Always ask before taking a photo of a person as it might be considered an invasion of their privacy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cayo Coco

Cayo Coco is generally a safe destination for tourists, with a low crime rate compared to other Caribbean countries. However, visitors are cautioned not to be complacent and always take basic safety measures, such as:

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended: Always keep a watchful eye over your belongings and be mindful of the people around you.
  • Avoid showing off your valuables: Keep expensive items out of sight when walking in public places.

Health in Cayo Coco

Visitors to Cayo Coco should take note of the following health tips:

  • Food and drink: Tap water is not suitable for drinking, so visitors should opt for bottled water. Also, try to eat meals in reputable restaurants instead of street vendors.
  • Sunburn: Cayo Coco is a tropical destination, and visitors are therefore advised to protect themselves from the sun’s harsh rays.

Getting to Cayo Coco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cayo Coco

Cayo Coco has its international airport: Ignacio Agramonte International Airport, located between the island and the mainland. Many hotels have free shuttles from the airport, so check with your hotel before arrival.

Bus Stations Near Cayo Coco

Travelling by bus is an affordable and comfortable way to get around Cuba. Some bus companies operate regular services between Cayo Coco and Moron. However, be prepared for long waits due to delays.

Other Options

Taxis are also available. You can take a shared cab too, which is much in demand due to the shared fare and it being the quickest way to travel from one place to another in Cuba.

How to Get Around in Cayo Coco

Cayo Coco is small and can be easily explored on foot, but there are also other transportation options available for visitors.

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car from the airport or from town. This is a convenient option that offers more flexibility for exploring the different parts of Cayo Coco and its surroundings.
  • Bicycle Rental: Renting a bike is an excellent way to explore the island, whether on your own or with a guide.

Where to Go Next from Cayo Coco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of amazing destinations near Cayo Coco worth exploring. Here are a few examples:

  • Varadero: A popular beach resort destination on the north coast of Cuba, with 20km of white pristine sandy beaches lined with luxury resorts and hotels.
  • Havana: The capital city of Cuba, offering a unique blend of Spanish-Caribbean culture, architecture, and nightlife with a vibrant music scene.
  • Cayo Guillermo: Another island located just next to Cayo Coco with similar beaches, is a quieter location and a true paradise for those seeking peace.

Conclusion

Cayo Coco is a beautiful island destination in Central Cuba that offers stunning natural scenery, cultural attractions, activities & tours, and many other must-visit places. Visitors will enjoy the warm weather, lovely beaches, and the friendly culture of the people while creating unforgettable memories. With this comprehensive travel guide, you now have all the information you need to plan a trip to this lovely island.
