Introduction to Central Cuba

With a rich and vibrant history and plenty of outdoor activities, Central Cuba is a destination that caters to all types of travelers. This region of Cuba has a unique culture and a ton of attractions that make it a must-visit destination. From the bustling city of Santa Clara to the lush landscapes of Topes de Collantes Natural Park, Central Cuba has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Central Cuba

If you’re looking to explore the best of Central Cuba, then make sure to add these places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Che Guevara Mausoleum, Santa Clara
  • Museum of Decorative Arts, Trinidad
  • Museum of the Revolution, Havana
  • Monument to the Tren Blindado, Santa Clara
  • La Caridad Theater, Santa Clara

Natural Attractions of Central Cuba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Topes de Collantes Natural Park, Escambray Mountains
  • Cienfuegos Botanical Garden, Cienfuegos
  • El Nicho Waterfalls, Sierra del Escambray
  • Caburni Waterfall, Topes de Collantes Natural Park
  • Playa Ancon, Trinidad

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Cuba

For the adventurous traveler, Central Cuba has some of the best biking and hiking trails in the country. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Sierra del Escambray (Canopy Tour and Hiking Trail)
  • Rio Hanabanilla (Hiking Trail)
  • Topes de Collantes (Hiking Trail)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ropa Vieja (Shredded Beef)
  • Arroz con Pollo (Chicken and Rice)
  • Yuca con Mojo (Cassava with Garlic Sauce)
  • Moros y Cristianos (Rice and Beans)
  • Tostones (Fried Plantains)

Local Beverages

  • Cuba Libre (Rum and Coke)
  • Mojito (Rum, Lime, Mint, and Sugar)
  • Daiquiri (Rum, Lime, and Sugar)
  • Piña Colada (Rum, Coconut Milk, Pineapple, and Sugar)
  • Canchanchara (Rum, Honey, Lime, and Water)

Local Culture and Customs of Central Cuba

Central Cuba has a rich cultural tapestry that can be seen in its music, dance, and festivals. Some local customs to keep in mind include:

  • Saying “Hola” and “Buenos Dias” when entering a store or greeting someone on the street.
  • Dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Avoiding wearing camouflage clothing, as it is reserved for the military.

Best Time to Visit Central Cuba

The best time to visit Central Cuba is from December to May, as this is the dry season and the temperatures are mild. The summer months can be hot and humid, and hurricane season runs from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Cuba

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts for travelers visiting Central Cuba:

Do:

  • Do learn a few Spanish phrases before your trip.
  • Do try some local cuisine.
  • Do bring cash, as credit cards are not widely accepted.
  • Do purchase travel insurance before your trip.
  • Do explore beyond the tourist areas.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water.
  • Don’t exchange money on the street.
  • Don’t touch the coral reefs when snorkeling or diving.
  • Don’t take pictures of military installations or personnel.
  • Don’t overpack, as luggage space is limited on buses and taxis.

Safety and Health

Before traveling to Central Cuba, make sure to consider your safety and health. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Central Cuba

Central Cuba is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s still essential to take precautions:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Don’t walk alone at night, and avoid dimly-lit streets.
  • Only ride in official taxis and buses.

Health in Central Cuba

Make sure to take these health precautions before and during your trip to Central Cuba:

  • Consult with a doctor or travel clinic before your trip to update your vaccinations.
  • Pack insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid ice cubes in drinks.
  • Wear sunscreen, as the sun can be intense.
