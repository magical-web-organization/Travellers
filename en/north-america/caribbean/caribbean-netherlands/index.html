Introduction to Caribbean Netherlands

Welcome to Caribbean Netherlands, a group of three special municipalities that lies in the Caribbean Sea and is part of the Netherlands. These municipalities include Bonaire, Saba, and Sint Eustatius, all of which offer visitors a unique blend of European and Caribbean cultures, crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and pristine natural surroundings.

Caribbean Netherlands is known for its year-round tropical weather and vibrant marine life, making it a popular destination for scuba diving, snorkeling, and other water-based activities. The islands also boast a rich history and culture, with well-preserved colonial architecture, museums, and traditional customs that have been passed down through generations.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with a list of must-visit places, outdoor adventures, food and drink options, cultural insights, safety tips, transportation options, and more. Let’s explore the best of Caribbean Netherlands together!

List of Places to Visit in Caribbean Netherlands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about Caribbean Netherlands’ history, culture, and art, there are several museums and historical places worth visiting. Some of the top cultural attractions include:

  • Bonaire Museum: This museum showcases the island’s history, from the indigenous people to modern times, as well as exhibits on natural science, geology, and art.
  • Saba Heritage Center: Discover the island’s history through exhibits of artifacts, photographs, and documents, as well as displays of the island’s famous Saba Lace.
  • The Old Gin House: This restored 18th-century plantation house is now a boutique hotel and restaurant that serves as a reminder of Sint Eustatius’ colonial past.

Natural Attractions of Caribbean Netherlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Caribbean Netherlands offers a variety of outdoor activities and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions to add to your itinerary:

  • Washington Slagbaai National Park: This park in Bonaire boasts a diverse range of geological formations, plant and animal species, and several hiking trails.
  • Mount Scenery: At 887 meters, this is the highest point in the Netherlands and can be hiked from the town of Windwardside on Saba.
  • Quill National Park: This park on Sint Eustatius features a dormant volcano surrounded by hiking trails, as well as flora and fauna unique to the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caribbean Netherlands

Caribbean Netherlands is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with scenic routes that offer breathtaking views of the islands’ natural beauty. Some of the top routes include:

  • Jibe City to Lac Bay Loop (Bonaire): This scenic biking route takes you through the lush mangroves of Lac Bay and offers stunning coastal views.
  • Crispeen Trail (Saba): This moderately challenging hiking trail leads you to the top of Mount Scenery, offering panoramic views of the island and neighboring islands.
  • The Borderline Trail (Sint Eustatius): This hiking trail follows the southern coast of the island, passing through old plantation ruins, tropical forests, and unique rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Caribbean Netherlands: Activities and Tours

Caribbean Netherlands offers a variety of activities and tours that showcase the islands’ unique culture, history, and natural beauty. Here are some of the top things to do during your visit:

  • Snorkeling and diving: Caribbean Netherlands boasts an abundance of marine life, coral reefs, and shipwrecks that can be explored through snorkeling and diving tours.
  • Island hop: Take a ferry or plane to explore neighbouring islands, such as St. Maarten, Anguilla, and St. Barths.
  • Bird watching: Sint Eustatius is home to a variety of bird species, including the Red-billed Tropicbird, Lesser Antillean Bullfinch, and Antillean Crested Hummingbird.
  • Banana boat rides: Experience the thrill of bouncing over the waves on a banana boat ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caribbean Netherlands

To make the most of your week-long visit to Caribbean Netherlands, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1 - Bonaire: Explore Washington Slagbaai National Park, snorkel at Lac Bai, and visit Bonaire Museum.

Day 2 - Bonaire: Take a tour to Klein Bonaire for some beach time and snorkeling.

Day 3 - Saba: Hike up to the top of Mount Scenery, visit Saba Lace Museum, and enjoy local cuisine at The Hideaway Restaurant.

Day 4 - Saba: Go scuba diving at Saba Marine Park, explore the underwater Statia Marine Park, or shop at Bottom Time Dive Shop.

Day 5 - Sint Eustatius: Take a tour of The Old Gin House, hike to the top of the Quill, and visit the Sint Eustatius Historical Foundation Museum.

Day 6 - Sint Eustatius: Snorkel at Oranje Bay or Zeelandia Beach and enjoy a sunset cruise.

Day 7 - Sint Eustatius: Take a day trip to St. Kitts or St. Maarten for some island hopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Caribbean Netherlands

Must-Try Dishes

Caribbean Netherlands offers a variety of unique dishes and flavors that are a blend of Dutch, Caribbean, and Latin American cuisines. Here are some of the must-try dishes during your visit:

  • Keshi Yena: A traditional dish that is made with leftover Edam or Gouda cheese, chicken, raisins, and olives.
  • Saba Lobster: The island of Saba is known for its succulent and delicious Caribbean lobster.
  • Conch: A conch shell dish that is often served with creole sauce, garlic, and wine.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Caribbean Netherlands serves up a variety of refreshing and unique options. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Cadushy of Bonaire Liqueur: Made from the cactus plant, this sweet and tangy liqueur is unique to Bonaire and can be found at Cadushy Distillery.
  • Dutch beer: The Netherlands is famous for its beer, and Caribbean Netherlands is no exception. Try a locally brewed beer like Heineken or Amstel Beer.
  • Guavaberry rum: A traditional Caribbean drink made from guavaberries, a tiny red fruit that is used to make jam and tea.

Where to Stay in Caribbean Netherlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Caribbean Netherlands offers a range of accommodations to fit every budget and preference. From luxury resorts to backpacker hostels and cozy guesthouses, you’ll find something to suit your needs. Some top options include:

  • Harbour Village Beach Club (Bonaire): This luxurious resort features beachfront villas, a private beach, and top-notch amenities like a spa, fitness center, and tennis courts.
  • The Old Town Guesthouse (Saba): A charming guesthouse located in the heart of Windwardside, with four comfortable and cozy rooms.
  • The Old Gin House (Sint Eustatius): A beautifully restored 18th-century plantation house turned boutique hotel, with 20 rooms and two restaurants.

What to Buy in Caribbean Netherlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping, Caribbean Netherlands offers a variety of unique and locally made souvenirs and products. Here are some top items to bring home as a memento:

  • Saba Lace: This beautiful and intricate lace is handmade on the island of Saba and can be found at the Saba Lace Museum and the Sea Witch Boutique.
  • Dutch Cheese: Caribbean Netherlands is a part of the Netherlands, so you’ll find a variety of delicious Dutch cheeses like Edam and Gouda to bring home.
  • Paintings and pottery: The islands are home to many local artists, and you’ll find unique paintings, pottery, and ceramics in galleries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Caribbean Netherlands

Caribbean Netherlands has a rich and diverse culture, influenced by both its European and Caribbean heritage. Here are some cultural insights to keep in mind:

  • Language: The official language of Caribbean Netherlands is Dutch, but English and Spanish are widely spoken.
  • Etiquette: When greeting someone, a handshake is common. It’s also polite to arrive on time for appointments and never visit someone’s home uninvited.
  • Dress code: Swimwear is prohibited in public places, and it’s appropriate to dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and government buildings.

Best Time to Visit Caribbean Netherlands

The best time to visit Caribbean Netherlands is between December and April when the weather is dry, sunny, and less humid. Hurricane season is from June to November, and while the risk of a hurricane is low, it’s still important to be prepared.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caribbean Netherlands

Do:

  • Do take a scuba diving or snorkelling tour to explore the crystal-clear waters and marine life.
  • Do try local dishes and drinks, like Saba Lobster and Guavaberry rum.
  • Do be respectful of the islands’ natural beauty and marine habitats.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment when exploring.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caribbean Netherlands

Caribbean Netherlands is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions, like not leaving valuables unattended on the beach or in your rental car.

Health in Caribbean Netherlands

Caribbean Netherlands has a well-equipped medical center on each island, and visitors can access medical care easily. It’s also essential to protect yourself from mosquito bites to reduce the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Caribbean Netherlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Caribbean Netherlands

Bonaire, Saba, and Sint Eustatius all have airports that connect to the nearby islands and mainland. The main gateway airport is Flamingo International Airport in Bonaire, which has connections to major airports in the Caribbean, Europe, and North America.

Bus Stations Near Caribbean Netherlands

Caribbean Netherlands doesn’t have a public bus service, but there are taxis and rental cars available for transportation.

Other Options

There are several ferry and boat services that connect Caribbean Netherlands to neighboring islands like St. Maarten, St. Barths, and Anguilla.

How to Get Around in Caribbean Netherlands

The best way to get around Caribbean Netherlands is by renting a car or scooter, as public transportation is limited. Taxis are also available on each island, and there are bike rental options as well.

Where to Go Next from Caribbean Netherlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Caribbean Netherlands is a popular destination that can be combined with other nearby islands and destinations. Some of the top options include:

  • Aruba: A tropical paradise known for its beaches, casinos, and nightlife.
  • Barbados: An island nation with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful beaches.
  • Saint Lucia: A picturesque island that boasts the world’s only drive-in volcano and a famous jazz festival.

Conclusion

Caribbean Netherlands offers visitors a unique blend of European and Caribbean cultures, crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and pristine natural surroundings. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore the islands’ rich history and culture, or embark on an outdoor adventure, there’s something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next unforgettable trip to Caribbean Netherlands!
