Introduction to Les Saintes

Les Saintes is a small archipelago located in the Lesser Antilles, in the Caribbean Sea. It is a part of Guadeloupe, a French overseas region, and it comprises two main islands, Terre-de-Haut and Terre-de-Bas, along with a few smaller islets. Les Saintes is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Les Saintes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Les Saintes offers a range of museums and historical sites for visitors to explore. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Fort Napoleon: A historical fort that dates back to the 18th century and now serves as a museum showcasing the archipelago’s natural and cultural heritage.
  • Le Chameau: A museum that commemorates the history of the slave trade in Guadeloupe using artifacts and photographs.
  • Eglise de Terre-de-Haut: A beautiful Catholic church that features vibrant stained-glass windows and boasts stunning views of the archipelago.

Natural Attractions of Les Saintes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Les Saintes is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking landscapes. Here are some of the most spectacular natural attractions to explore:

  • Plage de Pompierre: A picturesque beach with white sand, crystal-clear water, and lined with coconut trees.
  • Le Pain de Sucre: A tiny islet with a stunning viewpoint and a great spot for snorkeling.
  • Chutes du Carbet: A series of three waterfalls nestled in the heart of Guadeloupe National Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Les Saintes

Les Saintes is a great destination for hikers and cyclists. There are several trails that you can explore to discover the archipelago’s natural beauty, including:

  • Chemin des Ananas: A scenic trail that leads to the cross at the top of Terre-de-Haut’s Mount Morel.
  • Sentier de l’Hermitage: A challenging trail that takes you to the top of Terre-de-Haut’s Fort Napoleon.
  • Loop of Terre-de-Haut: A cycling trail that circles the island and offers stunning coastal views.

Best Things to Do in Les Saintes: Activities and Tours

Les Saintes offers a wide array of activities and tours to cater to diverse interests, including:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Explore the vibrant underwater world of Les Saintes with its coral reefs and abundant marine life.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour around the archipelago and discover its hidden coves and beaches.
  • Island Hopping: Explore the surrounding islands of Guadeloupe and discover their unique character and beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Les Saintes

Must-Try Dishes

Les Saintes offers a delicious culinary scene that reflects the archipelago’s cultural diversity. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Accras: Deep-fried balls made with fish, vegetables, and spices, served as a snack or appetizer.
  • Colombo: A spicy stew made with chicken, goat meat, or seafood, seasoned with curry and other spices.
  • Tourment d’Amour: A local dessert made with coconut, sugar, and vanilla, usually served in small individual portions.

Local Beverages

Les Saintes offers a range of refreshing beverages that you should try, including:

  • Ti Punch: A traditional cocktail made with rum, lime, and sugar.
  • Coco Punch: A refreshing drink made with coconut water, rum, and sugar.
  • Thé de Comptoir: A local tea brewed with a mix of spices and flowers, served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Les Saintes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Les Saintes offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Bois Joli: A charming hotel nestled in the heart of Terre-de-Haut, offering comfortable rooms and stunning views of the archipelago.
  • Les Petits Saints: A guesthouse that boasts a relaxing atmosphere, an outdoor pool, and a beautiful garden.
  • Auberge Les Petits Saints aux Anacardiers: A cozy and affordable hotel located in a serene and residential area of Terre-de-Haut.

What to Buy in Les Saintes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Les Saintes offers a range of unique and authentic souvenirs that reflect its cultural heritage and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Rum: Guadeloupe produces some of the finest rum in the world, and Les Saintes offers a range of local brands to try.
  • Local Crafts: Les Saintes offers a range of handmade crafts, including pottery, baskets, and jewelry, that make perfect souvenirs.
  • Vanilla: Guadeloupe produces some of the best vanilla in the world, and Les Saintes offers a range of local brands to buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Les Saintes

Les Saintes has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its diverse history and traditions. The archipelago boasts a strong Creole identity that combines African, European, and indigenous influences. Some of the local customs to keep in mind include:

  • Dress Code: Les Saintes is a conservative community, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and cover their shoulders and knees when visiting religious sites.
  • Language: While French is the official language, most locals speak Creole, an Afro-French dialect.
  • Festivals: Les Saintes hosts several festivals and cultural events throughout the year that offer a vibrant display of music, dance, and food.

Best Time to Visit Les Saintes

The best time to visit Les Saintes is during the dry season, from December to April, when the weather is sunny and pleasant. However, this is also the peak tourist season, and prices tend to be higher. If you’re looking for a more affordable and less crowded experience, consider visiting during the shoulder season, from May to June or October to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Les Saintes

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and rum.
  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions of the archipelago.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Disrespect the wildlife and natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Les Saintes

Les Saintes is a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take common sense precautions to avoid theft and petty crime. Avoid walking alone at night or carrying large amounts of cash or valuables in public.

Health in Les Saintes

Les Saintes has a good healthcare system that offers quality medical care. However, visitors are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Zika and Dengue Fever. Apply mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing when outdoors.

Getting to Les Saintes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Les Saintes is accessible by boat from Guadeloupe’s mainland, either by ferry or private boat. There are no airports on the archipelago, but visitors can fly into Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport on Guadeloupe’s mainland and then take a ferry to Les Saintes. There are also several bus and taxi services available on the islands.

Airports Near Les Saintes

  • Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP), Guadeloupe

How to Get Around in Les Saintes

Les Saintes is a small archipelago, and most of the attractions and amenities are within walking distance. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or electric scooters to explore the islands at their own pace. Taxis and private tours are also available for visitors who prefer not to walk or cycle.

Where to Go Next from Les Saintes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Les Saintes is a great base for exploring Guadeloupe’s neighboring islands and attractions. Some of the destinations and excursions worth exploring include:

  • La Soufriere Volcano: A challenging hike to the summit of Guadeloupe’s active volcano, offering stunning views of the archipelago.
  • Marie-Galante: A neighboring island that boasts pristine beaches, sugar cane fields, and a unique cultural heritage.
  • Les Salines Beach: A beautiful beach located in Guadeloupe’s southwest, boasting calm waters and a stunning coral reef.

Conclusion

Les Saintes offers a unique and authentic Caribbean experience that combines natural beauty, rich culture, and laid-back charm. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your vacation to Les Saintes and discover the archipelago’s best attractions, activities, and experiences.
