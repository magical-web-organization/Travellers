Introduction to Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is a small town located on the southern coast of Grande-Terre, the eastern half of Guadeloupe, a French overseas territory in the Caribbean. The town is known for its palm-lined beaches, turquoise waters, and lively markets with colorful handicrafts, spices, and fresh seafood. Sainte-Anne is also famous for its protected nature reserve, the Pointe-à-Pitre Marine Park, which offers opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and sailing.

List of Places to Visit in Sainte-Anne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ecomusée de la Guadeloupe: A cultural museum that features local artifacts, traditional music, and dance performances, and workshops for artisanal crafts and culinary techniques.
  • Musée du Rhum: A rum distillery that showcases the history of rum-making in Guadeloupe and offers guided tours and tastings.
  • La Porte d’Enfer: A natural monument that marks the entrance to the village and offers panoramic views of the countryside.

Natural Attractions of Sainte-Anne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Salines Beach: A sandy beach located on the southern tip of the island, popular for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Pointe-à-Pitre Marine Park: A protected area that covers over 15,000 hectares of mangroves, seagrasses, and coral reefs, and harbors a rich variety of marine life, such as dolphins, sea turtles, and colored fish.
  • Morne-à-l’Eau Cemetery: A peculiar cemetery that features ornate tombs decorated with seashells, flowers, and glass mosaics, made by local families in memory of their loved ones.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sainte-Anne

  • Trace des Caps: A 12 km-long trail that connects Sainte-Anne to Saint-François, passing through cliffs, beaches, and forests that offer breathtaking panoramas of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Plage de Bois Jolan: A natural reserve that offers bird-watching, hiking, and swimming opportunities, with 2 km of pristine coastline and a freshwater lagoon with mangrove trees.

Best Things to Do in Sainte-Anne: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving: With its crystal-clear waters and diverse marine life, Sainte-Anne is an ideal place for diving enthusiasts, who can explore shipwrecks, reefs, and underwater caves.
  • Kitesurfing: The steady winds and shallow waters of the nearby Helleux Bay make it a favorite spot for kitesurfers of all levels, who can rent gear or take lessons from experienced instructors.
  • Boat tours: Visitors can explore the hidden coves, lagoons, and beaches of the island aboard a catamaran, a sailboat, or a motorboat, and enjoy a picnic or a cocktail while admiring the sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Sainte-Anne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bokit: A deep-fried sandwich made with dough and filled with tuna, chicken, lamb, or vegetables, often seasoned with spicy sauces, onions, and pickles.
  • Accras: Fried dough fritters made with salt cod, herbs, and Caribbean spices, served as a snack or an appetizer.
  • Colombo: A fragrant stew made with chicken, fish, or goat, marinated in a blend of Indian and Creole spices, and served with rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Ti-Punch: The national cocktail of Guadeloupe, made with white rum, lime, and a dash of cane syrup or honey, served with fresh fruit or a slice of coconut.
  • Planteur: A fruit punch made with a blend of tropical juices, rum, and spices, served cold with ice and a slice of lime.

Where to Stay in Sainte-Anne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Toubana Hotel and Spa: A luxurious retreat overlooking the ocean, with spacious suites, private pools, spas, and restaurants that offer French and Caribbean cuisine.
  • Copacabana Hotel Residence and Spa: A family-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, a spa, and a beach club that offers water sports and activities.
  • Chez Anais: A charming guesthouse with colorful rooms, a tropical garden, and a terrace that offers panoramic views of the sea and the hills.

What to Buy in Sainte-Anne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Spices and condiments: Guadeloupe is famous for its aromatic spices, such as vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice, which can be bought in local markets or specialty stores.
  • Handicrafts: The island is known for its skilled artisans who create colorful baskets, hats, and bags made of woven straw or palm leaves, as well as wood carvings, pottery, and jewelry.
  • Rum: Guadeloupe is home to several rum distilleries that produce high-quality rum, aged in oak barrels and flavored with tropical fruits, such as pineapple, coconut, or mango.

Local Culture and Customs of Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is a multicultural town that reflects the heritage of its African, European, and Carib inhabitants. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and they enjoy celebrating their culture through music, dance, and food. The official language is French, but English and Creole are widely spoken. The majority of the population is Roman Catholic, and there are several churches and shrines throughout the town.

Best Time to Visit Sainte-Anne

The best time to visit Sainte-Anne is from December to April, when the weather is dry and sunny, and the temperatures range from 25°C to 30°C. The peak season is from Christmas to Easter, and during Carnival (February or March), when the town comes alive with festive parades, music, and costumes. The low season is from May to November, when the weather gets hotter and wetter, with occasional rainfall, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sainte-Anne

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, and ask for permission before taking photos of people or their possessions.
  • Wear comfortable and light clothes, sunscreen, and insect repellent, and drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Be adventurous and try new foods, drinks, and activities, and mingle with the locals to learn more about their lives and stories.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended or walk alone in dark or isolated areas, and stay informed about the safety measures and warnings issued by the local authorities.
  • Be impatient or offensive towards the locals or other tourists, and respect the local culture and environment.
  • Refuse to bargain or negotiate prices at the markets or shops, and be respectful of the vendors’ craftsmanship and creativity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is generally safe for visitors, as long as they take basic precautions and avoid risky situations, such as walking alone at night or leaving valuables in plain sight. The town has a medical center and several pharmacies that offer basic health services and medications. The emergency number is 15 for medical emergencies, 17 for police, and 18 for firefighters.

Health in Sainte-Anne

Visitors to Sainte-Anne should take the necessary precautions to prevent common health issues, such as gastroenteritis, sunburn, or insect bites. They should drink bottled water or boiled water, avoid tap water, ice cubes, and salads that may have been washed with tap water, and use mosquito repellent and protective clothing to avoid getting bitten by mosquitoes or other insects that may transmit diseases, such as dengue, chikungunya, or Zika.

Getting to Sainte-Anne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sainte-Anne is located about 20 km southeast of the main airport of Guadeloupe, Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP), which serves as a hub for several international and domestic flights. The easiest way to get to Sainte-Anne from the airport is to rent a car or take a taxi, which can cost around 40-50 euros. There are also several bus lines that serve Sainte-Anne from nearby towns, such as Pointe-à-Pitre, Le Gosier, or Saint-François, but they may have limited schedules and availability.

Airports Near Sainte-Anne

  • Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport (PTP): The main airport of Guadeloupe, located about 20 km northwest of Sainte-Anne, with several international and domestic flights.
  • Les Saintes Airport (SFC): A small airport located on Terre-de-Haut Island, about 30 minutes by boat from Sainte-Anne, with flights to Guadeloupe and Martinique.

Bus Stations Near Sainte-Anne

  • Pointe-à-Pitre Bus Station: Located about 20 km northwest of Sainte-Anne, with several local and regional bus lines that serve the main towns and villages of Grande-Terre and Basse-Terre.
  • Saint-François Bus Station: Located about 8 km east of Sainte-Anne, with several local and regional bus lines that serve the eastern coast of Grande-Terre.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from several car rental companies that operate at Pointe-à-Pitre Airport or other locations in Guadeloupe, such as Hertz, Europcar, or Avis.
  • Taxi: Visitors can take a taxi from Pointe-à-Pitre Airport or other locations in Guadeloupe, which can be booked in advance or found at taxi stands.
  • Boat ferry: Visitors can take a boat ferry from Pointe-à-Pitre or Saint-François to the nearby islands of Marie-Galante, La Desirade, or Les Saintes, which offer unique landscapes, beaches, and cultural experiences.

How to Get Around in Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne can be explored on foot or by bike, as the town is relatively small and pedestrian-friendly. Visitors can also rent a car or a scooter, which can be convenient for longer trips or exploring the nearby towns and villages. Taxis and buses are also available, but they may have limited availability and schedules, especially outside the peak season.

Where to Go Next from Sainte-Anne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pointe-à-Pitre: The capital of Guadeloupe, located about 20 km northwest of Sainte-Anne, with several historical and cultural attractions, such as the Schoelcher Museum, the Saint-Pierre and Saint-Paul Church, and the Spice Market.
  • Saint-François: A quaint fishing village located about 8 km east of Sainte-Anne, with a lively marina, a golf course, and a casino, as well as several beaches and natural reserves.
  • Les Saintes: A group of small islands located south of Guadeloupe, accessible by boat from Pointe-à-Pitre or Saint-François, and featuring stunning landscapes, white sand beaches, and colorful houses.

Conclusion

Sainte-Anne is a hidden gem in the Caribbean, offering a unique blend of French, African, and Carib culture, pristine beaches, exotic nature, and delicious cuisine. Visitors can discover the rich history and traditions of Guadeloupe by exploring its museums and historical sites, or by immersing themselves in its natural beauty through hiking, diving, or sailing. They can also taste the flavors and aromas of the tropics by trying the local dishes and drinks, or by shopping for souvenirs and handicrafts in the colorful markets and shops. With its warm and welcoming atmosphere, Sainte-Anne is a destination that will capture the hearts and minds of all travelers who seek adventure, relaxation, and inspiration.
