Introduction to Saint John’s

Saint John’s, the capital city of Antigua and Barbuda, is a charming Caribbean destination that offers a perfect blend of a rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and exciting activities. Named after Saint John the Divine, Saint John’s is one of the oldest colonial towns in the Caribbean and is home to many historical and cultural attractions. From stunning beaches and lush green hills to a bustling food scene and vibrant nightlife, Saint John’s has something to offer to every kind of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Saint John’s

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Antigua and Barbuda: This museum showcases the history and culture of Antigua and Barbuda through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Fort James: A historic fort from the 18th century that offers stunning views of the city and the Caribbean Sea.
  • Cathedral of St. John the Divine: A beautiful church that is considered one of the finest examples of Baroque architecture in the Caribbean.

Natural Attractions of Saint John’s: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dickenson Bay: A beautiful white sand beach with crystal-clear waters that is perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Shirley Heights: A must-visit spot for panoramic views of the island and sunset drinks with live music.
  • Nelson’s Dockyard National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and the only remaining Georgian naval dockyard in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint John’s

  • Monk’s Hill Hike: A strenuous hike to the top of Monk’s Hill that offers breathtaking views of the island and the sea.
  • Heritage Quay to Fort James Bike Ride: A scenic bike ride that takes you through the city’s historic landmarks and ends at the beautiful Fort James beach.

Best Things to Do in Saint John’s: Activities and Tours

  • Swim With Stingrays: An unforgettable experience where you get to swim and interact with these majestic creatures in their natural habitat.
  • Antigua Rainforest Canopy Tour: A thrilling zipline adventure that takes you through the lush rainforest canopy.
  • Island Safari Tour: A jeep tour that takes you to the island’s hidden gems and offers a glimpse into the local culture and lifestyle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint John’s

Day Activities
1 Visit the Museum of Antigua and Barbuda, explore the city’s historic landmarks, and enjoy a sunset sail.
2 Hike to Monk’s Hill for a breathtaking view, followed by an afternoon at Dickenson Bay.
3 Take a snorkeling tour to explore the island’s reefs and marine life.
4 Spend the day at Nelson’s Dockyard National Park and end the day with a sunset party at Shirley Heights.
5 Go on an island safari tour to discover Antigua’s hidden gems and local lifestyle.
6 Take a boat tour to explore the neighboring islands and their scenic beauty.
7 Indulge in a day at the beach and enjoy the local food and drinks before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint John’s

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fungie: A dish made of cornmeal and okra that is similar to polenta. It is a local staple and is usually served with seafood.
  • Ducana: A sweet potato dumpling that is boiled in coconut milk and often served with saltfish.
  • Roti: A flatbread filled with curried chicken, beef, or vegetables and is a popular street food.

Local Beverages

  • Rum Punch: A refreshing cocktail made with rum, fruit juice, and spices.
  • Wadadli Beer: A locally brewed beer that is made with Antiguan water and hops.
  • Mauby: A drink made from the bark of a tree and flavored with spices.

Where to Stay in Saint John’s: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Heritage Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of the city and is famous for its charming and comfortable rooms.
  • Siboney Beach Club: A beachfront hotel with a beautiful pool, stunning views, and a private beach.
  • Hibiscus Villa: A luxurious villa with a private pool that is perfect for a family or group vacation.

What to Buy in Saint John’s: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antigua Rum: A bottle of local rum that is perfect for a souvenir or a gift.
  • Handmade Pottery: Beautiful and colorful pottery pieces can be found at local markets and craft shops.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Unique jewelry pieces made from local materials like sea glass and shells can be found at local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint John’s

  • Antigua and Barbuda is a deeply religious country, and it is important to respect religious customs and practices.
  • It is customary to greet people with a “Good Morning” or “Good Afternoon” before starting a conversation.
  • Dress is generally casual, but it is recommended to dress modestly when visiting religious places and government buildings.

Best Time to Visit Saint John’s

The best time to visit Saint John’s is from mid-December to mid-April when the weather is dry and sunny. The temperatures range from the mid-70s to mid-80s, making it perfect for beach activities and outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint John’s

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and take safety precautions.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended on the beach.
  • Engage in illegal activities.
  • Wear camouflage clothing, as it is reserved for military personnel.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint John’s

Saint John’s is a safe destination for tourists, but it is essential to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables in a safe place.

Health in Saint John’s

Antigua and Barbuda have no major health risks, but it is recommended to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and chikungunya. It is also essential to have travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Saint John’s: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint John’s

The V.C Bird International Airport is located 10 minutes from Saint John’s and is the main airport in Antigua and Barbuda. It offers flights to and from major destinations in the Caribbean, North America, Europe, and South America.

Bus Stations Near Saint John’s

There are no bus stations in Saint John’s, but there are several bus stops in the city that offer transportation to other parts of the island. The bus system is not very reliable, and it is recommended to use other modes of transportation.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are readily available in Saint John’s. Taxis are usually unmarked, and it is important to negotiate the fare before getting in. Rental cars are available at the airport and in the city, and it is recommended to book in advance.

How to Get Around in Saint John’s

The best way to get around Saint John’s is by taxi or rental car. Taxis are readily available in the city and are affordable, while rental cars offer more flexibility and freedom to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Saint John’s: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Barbuda: Visit this sibling island of Antigua for stunning pink sand beaches and an untouched natural environment.
  • Montserrat: Explore this volcanic island that offers dramatic landscapes and rich history.
  • St. Kitts and Nevis: Take a short flight to these neighboring islands for more Caribbean adventures.

Conclusion

Saint John’s, Antigua, and Barbuda offer a perfect blend of rich culture, scenic beauty, and exciting activities, making it an ideal destination for any kind of traveler. With stunning beaches, delicious food, and vibrant nightlife, Saint John’s is a Caribbean gem that should not be missed. So pack your bags and head to Saint John’s for an unforgettable vacation!
