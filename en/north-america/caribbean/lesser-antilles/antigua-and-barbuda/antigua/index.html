Introduction to Antigua

Antigua is a paradise island located in the Lesser Antilles, in the Caribbean ocean, in North America. The island is famous for its 365 white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and perfect climate. The island also possesses a rich culture and history that reflects its colonial past. Antigua boasts a plethora of sightseeing attractions, including cultural landmarks, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. In this guide, we will provide you with the ultimate tour itinerary to discover the best of Antigua.

1. List of Places to Visit in Antigua

1.1 Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nelson’s Dockyard

Nelson’s Dockyard is the largest national park in Antigua and one of the most historic places on the island. The dockyard is a restored 18th-century British naval base where Admiral Horatio Nelson was stationed. Visitors can explore the idyllic harbor, admire the historic buildings, and learn about the island’s history.

Museum of Antigua and Barbuda

The Museum of Antigua and Barbuda is located in St. John’s, the capital city. The museum houses exhibits showcasing the country’s history tracing back over 6000 years. Here visitors can learn about the life of the native Arawak people, the arrival of the Europeans, slavery, and the island’s Independence movement.

St. John’s Cathedral

St. John’s Cathedral is a beautiful architectural marvel in St. John’s. The cathedral’s construction began in 1845, and it took over 20 years to complete. The beautiful church features impressive towers, intricate hand-carved doors and windows, and stunning stained-glass windows.

1.2 Natural Attractions of Antigua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Devil’s Bridge

Devil’s Bridge is a geological formation located on the eastern coast of Antigua. The bridge is made of limestone and was formed over thousands of years by the Atlantic Ocean waves crashing into the rock. Visitors can explore the natural beauty of the surrounding cliffs and stunning sea views.

Stingray City

Stingray City is a popular attraction for visitors looking for an unforgettable experience. Here visitors can interact with friendly southern stingrays in their natural habitat. Visitors can feed, pet, and swim with the stingrays, making it an exhilarating experience.

Fig Tree Drive

Fig Tree Drive is a scenic drive through the island’s tropical rainforest, which offers stunning views of the lush greenery. There are also several stops along the route to visit historic churches, sugar mills, and antique shops.

2. Hiking/Biking Routes of Antigua

2.1 Mount Obama

Mount Obama, or Boggy Peak, is the highest point on the island with an altitude of 1,319 feet. Visitors can hike to the summit, which offers incredible views of the sea and the island’s lush vegetation. The trail is rugged and requires a moderate level of fitness.

2. 2 Biking in Antigua

Antigua is an excellent destination for cycling enthusiasts. The island offers several cycling routes, including scenic rides along the beach and challenging terrains such as hilly trails and rocky roads. Some popular bike rental shops include Tropical Adventures, Adventure Antigua, and Antigua E-Bikes.

3. Best Things to Do in Antigua: Activities and Tours

3.1 Swim with the Dolphins at Dolphins Fantasy

Dolphins Fantasy offers visitors a chance to swim with bottlenose dolphins in their natural habitat. Visitors can enjoy the company of these intelligent creatures, learn about their behavior, and even kiss them.

3.2 Snorkeling and Scuba Diving

Antigua possesses one of the world’s most beautiful coral reefs, making it an ideal spot for snorkeling and scuba diving enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the underwater world and swim alongside colorful fish, turtles, and other sea creatures.

5. Food and Drink Scene of Antigua

5.1 Must-Try Dishes

Antiguan Black Pineapple

The Antiguan black pineapple is an indigenous species of the island and has become a staple in Antiguan cuisine. The fruit is known for its exceptional sweetness and distinct flavor.

Fungee and Pepperpot

Fungee, also known as fungi, is a cornmeal-based dish, and pepperpot is a meat stew made with spinach, okra, and other spices. These two dishes are usually paired together and are a popular comfort food in Antigua.

5.2 Local Beverages

Rum

Antigua is famous for its rum, which comes in various flavors, including coconut, spiced, and silver. Visitors can tour the Antigua Distillery, which produces Cavalier, English Harbour, and other premium rum brands.

Wadadli Beer

Wadadli Beer is the only locally brewed beer on the island. The beer is made from locally grown ingredients and has a refreshing taste.

6. Where to Stay in Antigua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Antigua offers visitors a wide range of accommodation options, ranging from luxurious resorts to modest hotels and guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

6.1 Luxury Resorts

Curtain Bluff

Curtain Bluff Resort is a five-star resort situated on the south coast of Antigua. The resort boasts two beaches, a world-class spa, tennis courts, and restaurants.

Hermitage Bay

Hermitage Bay is an all-inclusive, eco-luxury resort located on the west coast of Antigua. The resort features breathtaking views, a romantic atmosphere, and a private beach.

6.2 Budget-Friendly Accommodation

Pineapple Beach Club

Pineapple Beach Club is an all-inclusive resort located on the beautiful Long Bay Beach. The resort offers affordable rates, comfortable accommodations, and a range of amenities, including watersports.

Trade Winds Hotel

Trade Winds Hotel is a boutique hotel located in a stunning hillside location overlooking Dickenson Bay. It offers affordable rates, modern amenities, and an outdoor pool.

7. What to Buy in Antigua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

7.1 Antiguan Rums and Wines

Antigua is known for its premium rum and wine production. Visitors can take home a bottle of Cavalier or English Harbour Rum for themselves or as a gift for friends and family.

7.2 Handmade Crafts

Antigua is famous for its locally made handicrafts, such as pottery, jewelry, wood carving, and basket weaving. Visitors can purchase these items at local markets and shops, as well as from street vendors.

8. Local Culture and Customs of Antigua

8.1 Music and Dance

Antiguan culture is heavily influenced by African rhythms and beats. Visitors can enjoy the traditional music that is played at festivals and carnivals, accompanied by dancing.

8.2 Sports

Cricket is the national sport of Antigua and is played at the famous Sir Vivian Richards Stadium. Visitors can also enjoy other sports, such as basketball and football, which are widely played on the island.

9. Best Time to Visit Antigua

The best time to visit Antigua is between December and April, as the weather is sunny and the temperature is warm. The peak season is from mid-December to mid-April, with higher rates for accommodations and activities.

10. Do’s and Don’ts of Antigua

10.1 Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bring sunscreen and bug spray
  • Try the local rum and dishes
  • Learn about the island’s history and culture

10.2 Don’t:

  • Wear camouflage clothing
  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Engage in illegal activities
  • Swim in the water after consuming alcohol

11. Safety and Health

11.1 Safety in Antigua

Antigua is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take precautionary measures, such as avoiding isolated areas and dark alleys, keeping valuables in a safe place, and being aware of pickpocketing.

11.2 Health in Antigua

Visitors should take appropriate measures to prevent mosquito bites and avoid contracting mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and Zika virus. It is advisable to carry insect repellent and wear appropriate clothing, such as long-sleeved shirts and pants.

12. Getting to Antigua: Transportation Options and Logistics

12.1 Airports Near Antigua

Antigua’s major airport is the V.C. Bird International Airport, which is located in St. George’s Parish. The airport is serviced by several airlines, including American Airlines, British Airways, and Air Canada.

12.2 Bus Stations Near Antigua

Antigua does not have a public bus service. However, visitors can use the island’s minibus system, which operates on a set route and is affordable.

12.3 Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to explore the island at their leisure.

13. How to Get Around in Antigua

Visitors can get around Antigua by using a taxi, hiring a car, or using the minibus system. Taxis are widely available and can be hailed on the street or arranged through a hotel. Car rental services are located in the airport, and visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters from local shops.

14. Where to Go Next from Antigua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

14.1 Barbuda

Barbuda is a small island located just north of Antigua, which offers visitors a relaxing getaway and a chance to explore the island’s natural beauty. Barbuda is famous for its pink sand beaches, exotic wildlife, and serene environment. Visitors can take a ferry or charter a boat to reach Barbuda.

14.2 St. Kitts and Nevis

St. Kitts and Nevis are two small islands located east of Antigua. The islands offer beautiful beaches, stunning landscapes, and a rich cultural history. Visitors can take a ferry or a short flight to reach St. Kitts and Nevis.

15. Conclusion

Antigua is a tropical paradise that offers visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of daily life. The island’s natural beauty, exciting attractions, and rich culture make it an ideal destination for anyone seeking adventure and relaxation. Whether you are a beach lover, an outdoor enthusiast, or a culture buff, Antigua has something for everyone.
