Introduction to Carriacou

Carriacou is a small, charming island located in the Eastern Caribbean, part of the three-island nation of Grenada, Carriacou, and Petite Martinique. It is a popular destination for travelers looking for a relaxing getaway, with its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and friendly locals. Its size, natural beauty, and laidback culture make it a perfect spot for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. In this comprehensive tour guide for Carriacou, we will explore the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and tips on how to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Carriacou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, here are some places you should definitely visit on your trip to Carriacou:

  • Carriacou Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the island. It has a collection of artifacts, pictures, and information about the island’s past and present.

  • Belair National Park: This park is home to the remains of a 19th-century cotton plantation, including the great house, slave quarters, and a cotton gin. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a glimpse into the island’s past.

  • High North Nature Reserve: This reserve features a turtle sanctuary and hiking trails, offering visitors the chance to see turtles, rare birds, and other wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Carriacou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carriacou boasts some of the most beautiful natural sites in the Caribbean. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Sandy Island: A breathtaking small island off the coast of Carriacou, known for its crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches, perfect for snorkeling and swimming.

  • Anse La Roche Beach: One of the most secluded and peaceful beaches on the island with stunning views of the turquoise blue waters, and a great spot for picnics and beach walks.

  • Petite Carenage Bay: A sheltered bay with calm waters, ideal for water sports like kayaking and paddleboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carriacou

If you’re looking for an active vacation, Carriacou has some excellent hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the island.

  • Mt. Royal Trail: The highest point on the island offers a panoramic view of Carriacou and Grenada. It’s a moderately strenuous hike, but the view from the top is worth it.

  • White Island Trail: A scenic hike followed by a refreshing dip in the White Island lagoon.

  • Petit Carenage Trail: This trail offers stunning coastal views along with the opportunity to see wildlife like iguanas, pelicans, and hummingbirds.

Best Things to Do in Carriacou: Activities and Tours

Carriacou has much to offer besides sunbathing, swimming, and hiking. These are some of the best activities, tours, and experiences:

  • Diving and snorkeling: The island boasts some of the most beautiful coral reefs and underwater life in the Caribbean.

  • Island Tours - Whether by land or sea, taking a tour of Carriacou gives visitors the chance to see the best of the island’s natural beauty and history.

  • Kiteboarding: Carriacou has great wind conditions, which makes it perfect for the ever-growing sport of kiteboarding.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Carriacou, here’s a sample itinerary you can follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Carriacou

Must-Try Dishes

Carriacou’s cuisine is influenced heavily by African and Caribbean cooking styles and ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Oil Down: This is the national dish of Grenada, Carriacou, and Petite Martinique, made with salted meat, breadfruit, and vegetables slow-cooked in coconut milk.

  • Callaloo Soup: A soup made with dasheen leaves, coconut milk, and spices, it is similar to a West African green soup.

  • Lambi (conch) fritters: A popular Caribbean snack made using locally caught conch.

Local Beverages

The island has a variety of local beverages to try that are unique to the Caribbean. Here are some popular ones:

  • Rum Punch: Made with local rum, lime juice, sugar, and nutmeg, this is a classic Caribbean drink enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

  • Mauby: This thirst-quenching drink is made by boiling the bark of a tree with sugar and spices, and is an acquired taste.

  • Sorrel: Made using the red petals of the hibiscus plant, this bright red drink is refreshing and usually sweetened with sugar and spiced with cinnamon, clove, and ginger.

Where to Stay in Carriacou: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carriacou has a range of accommodations to suit any budget. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a budget-friendly guesthouse, you’re sure to find something that suits your style and pocket.

Some recommended places to stay include:

  • Green Roof Inn: A tranquil retreat with stunning views of the island.

  • Carriacou Grand View Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers beautiful views over Hillsborough Bay.

  • Moringa Homestay: A peaceful retreat with stunning sea views.

What to Buy in Carriacou: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Carriacou’s markets are the best places to find unique local handicrafts, souvenirs, and other local products to bring back home. Some of the popular items include:

  • Handmade jewelry: Made from local materials like sea glass, sea beans, and coconut shells, this jewelry is unique and makes for great souvenirs.

  • Local spices: Carriacou is famous for its spices, especially nutmeg, and you can buy them in local markets or gift shops.

  • Local art: The island has a rich tradition of art, including pottery, carvings, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Carriacou

Carriacou has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience the local customs and traditions through various festivals and events held throughout the year.

Some of the festivals and events to look out for include:

  • Carriacou Carnival: This is a two-day festival held in February or March, featuring colorful costumes, lively music, and dancing.

  • Maroon Festival: Held annually in April, this festival celebrates the history and culture of the island’s native Carib ancestors.

  • Christmas Boat Lighting: A festive event where boats in the harbor are decorated with lights and ornaments, signaling the start of the holiday season.

Best Time to Visit Carriacou

The best time to visit Carriacou is from December to April, which is the dry season. The weather is typically sunny, and the temperature ranges from 75°F to 84°F. July to September is hurricane season, so it’s best to avoid visiting the island during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carriacou

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent while outdoors.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or skimpy clothing in public places.

  • Litter, honk your horn, or use offensive language.

  • Take seashells or other natural artifacts from beaches or protected areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carriacou

Carriacou is generally a safe place to visit, but like anywhere else, it’s essential to exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid crime.

  • Keep valuables in a safe place.

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Carriacou

Visitors are advised to take precautions to avoid contracting mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus, which are prevalent in the Caribbean.

Getting to Carriacou: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carriacou

The closest airport is Maurice Bishop International Airport, located in Grenada. From there, visitors can take a connecting flight to Lauriston Airport in Carriacou.

Bus Stations Near Carriacou

There are no bus stations on the island. However, visitors can use taxi services or rent a car to get around.

Other Options

The Osprey Ferry runs daily service between Grenada and Carriacou. The ferry takes around two and a half hours and offers a scenic ride over the water.

How to Get Around in Carriacou

The best way to get around Carriacou is by rental car, scooter, or taxi. Buses are also available but run on a limited schedule.

Where to Go Next from Carriacou: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations like Grenada or Petite Martinique, or take a boat trip to the Tobago Cays, a group of small uninhabited islands with stunning coral reefs and marine life.

Conclusion

Carriacou is one of the Caribbean’s hidden gems, with its beautiful beaches, natural beauty, friendly people, and rich cultural heritage. It’s a perfect destination for those looking for a laidback vacation with plenty of opportunities to explore and experience the local culture and customs. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to make the most of your trip to Carriacou and create memories that will last a lifetime.
