Introduction to Couva

Located in the central region of Trinidad and Tobago, the vibrant town of Couva is known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty. It is the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the best of Trinidad and Tobago. This article will provide a comprehensive guide to the best places to visit, things to do, and travel tips for Couva.

List of Places to Visit in Couva

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Couva Joylanders Steel Orchestra: Visit this iconic orchestra that presents Calypso and Soca music during the famous Carnival festivities.

  • Temple in the Sea: Learn about the history behind the temple built by Indian immigrant laborer Siewdass Sadhu, who inspired the creation of this enclave in the water.

  • Indian Caribbean Museum: Explore the history of Indian indentured laborers in the Caribbean at this informative museum.

Natural Attractions of Couva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Vega Estate: Stroll through the lush landscape, orchid gardens, and bird sanctuary of La Vega Estate, one of the most popular eco-tourism hotspots in Trinidad and Tobago.

  • Pitch Lake: This natural phenomenon is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. With its unique geological features and natural asphalt, Pitch Lake is the largest natural deposit and is still an active mining location for asphalt mining.

  • Brickfield Hill: Take a hike up the scenic trail and marvel at the stunning view of the town and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Couva

  • Brickfield Hill: Explore the scenic trail on a hike up to the peak of the hill, and soak up the breathtaking views of Couva and its surroundings.

  • La Vega Trace: This easy, circular route is perfect for cyclists, while hikers can enjoy a scenic walk and natural views along the route.

Best Things to Do in Couva: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing and boat tours: Take a fishing tour excursions to enjoy a relaxing day on the water and enjoy the scenery of the ocean. The relaxing trip can be completed with a BBQ catch of the day.

  • Horseback riding: Experience the beauty of the Trinidadian countryside on horseback and take a picnic lunch; the perfect way to spend an afternoon.

  • Carnival celebrations: Trinidad and Tobago is known for its famous Carnival, which takes place every February or March. Join the local celebrations and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture and spectacular artwork reflected in the costumes and music.

Food and Drink Scene of Couva

Must-Try Dishes

  • Doubles: This local street food is a popular dish made up of a fried bread called bara, a delicious chickpea curry and cucumber and tamarind sauce, that is enjoyed by Trinidadians at any time during the day and night.

  • Pelau: This hearty rice and chicken dish is a favorite Trini style comfort food where the rice is browned with sugar first before cooking to achieve a caramelized taste, and it contains pigeon peas, carrots, and coconut to add to the flavor.

  • Callaloo: This is a soup-like dish of African-Caribbean origin made with okra, coconut milk, spinach, and seasoned with various fresh herbs. It is usually eaten with crab and dumplings or bread.

Local Beverages

  • Rum Punch: Made with rum, lime juice, sugar, and nutmeg, this drink is perfect for a night out.

  • Stag Beer: Trinidad and Tobago’s national beer that can be consumed at any time and is not just drunk during sports events.

  • Mauby: This local drink is made by boiling the bark of the mauby tree along with herbs like aniseed and ginger. It has a bitter taste and is usually sweetened with sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of Couva

Trinidad and Tobago’s culture is influenced by a diverse range of cultural traditions from around the world. The native culture is an amalgamation of Amerindian, Caribbean, and African. The fusion of different cultures has given Trinidad and Tobago their unique national identity, which is reflected in their art, music, and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Couva

The best time to visit Couva is from December to May when the weather is dry and comfortable, which is suitable for outdoor tourist activities. However, the carnival season which runs from late-February to early-March is the most exciting time to visit, so be ready to celebrate with the locals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Couva

Do:

  • Do respect local traditions and customs.

  • Do take a dip in the natural hot springs.

  • Do try the local dishes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places.

  • Don’t forget your sunscreen because of the sunny weather.

  • Don’t wear beachwear in town, as it is considered inappropriate.

Safety and Health

Safety in Couva

Like most places, common sense goes a long way in ensuring your safety while traveling in Couva. Avoid wandering alone at night and, if you rent a vehicle, avoid leaving valuables visible in it.

Health in Couva

There is no need to take specific health precautions when traveling to Couva. The standard vaccinations for the Caribbean are usually advised, so check with the doctor before traveling. Always drink bottled water and avoid street foods.

Getting to Couva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Couva

The Piarco International Airport is about 20 miles away from Couva, and travelers can take a taxi or rent a car from the airport to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near Couva

The best option to get to Couva is from the capital city of Port of Spain, through the transportation hub locally known as City Gate. This hub provides access to different routes throughout the country.

Other Options

Car rentals are available and offer the most flexibility in exploring Couva, as well as the rest of the island, for independent travels.

How to Get Around in Couva

Taxis or rental cars are the most convenient ways to get around in Couva. Public buses and private bus services also operate on fixed routes throughout the island, so visitors can easily get around with assistance from the locals.

Where to Go Next from Couva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chaguaramas: Famous for its stunning beaches, tropical rainforest, and naval history museums, Chaguaramas is a popular destination that is located 35 miles from Couva.

  • Port of Spain: As the capital city, Port of Spain is the cultural and commercial hub of Trinidad and Tobago. It is located 20 miles from Couva and offers a wide range of attractions including parks, museums, and restaurants.

  • Tobago: Visitors can take a ferry ride and enjoy a peaceful oasis on this nearby island that has spectacular beaches, snorkeling, and scuba diving experiences, while still being able to soak in the island’s relaxed vibe.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and vibrant atmosphere, Couva is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique Caribbean experience. From museums and historical tours to outdoor adventures and scenic routes, there is something for everyone in this town. With the helpful tips and suggestions outlined in this guide, visitors are sure to have a memorable trip to Couva, Trinidad, and Tobago.
