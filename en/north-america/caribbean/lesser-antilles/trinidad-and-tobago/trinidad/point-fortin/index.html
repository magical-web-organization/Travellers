Introduction to Point Fortin

Located on the southwestern coast of Trinidad and Tobago in the Caribbean, Point Fortin is a small coastal town known for its oil and gas industry, nature reserves, and historic landmarks. With its rich cultural heritage and scenic surroundings, Point Fortin is a hidden gem that offers tourists a chance to experience the natural beauty and cultural diversity of Trinidad and Tobago.

List of Places to Visit in Point Fortin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Guapo Cemetery: A historic cemetery that dates back to the 19th century and features ornate tombstones and a striking mausoleum. Visitors can pay their respects to the town’s early settlers and prominent figures.

  2. Point Fortin Borough Hall: A grand, colonial-era building located in the town square that serves as the home of the Point Fortin Borough Corporation. Visitors can admire its imposing facade and take a peek inside to appreciate its ornate architecture.

  3. Point Fortin Museum: A small but informative museum that showcases the town’s history, from its beginnings as a sugar plantation to its development into a bustling oil town. The museum also features exhibits on the town’s local flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Point Fortin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Vessigny Beach: A popular beach that is great for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports. The beach is easily accessible and has amenities such as restrooms, showers, and snack bars.

  2. Guapo Beach: A quieter and more secluded beach that is perfect for a peaceful day by the sea. Visitors can take a stroll on the soft sand or watch the sunset over the Caribbean Sea.

  3. La Brea Pitch Lake: The largest natural asphalt lake in the world, the La Brea Pitch Lake is a unique geological wonder that is a must-see attraction in Point Fortin. Visitors can walk on the glistening surface of the lake and learn about its formation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Point Fortin

  1. Soldier’s Bay Trail: This scenic hiking trail runs along the coastline from Point Fortin to the neighboring village of La Brea. The trail offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea and passes by a number of secluded beaches.

  2. Point Fortin to Vance River Trail: A challenging hiking trail that is approximately 8km long and takes about four hours to complete. The trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Point Fortin: Activities and Tours

  1. Birdwatching at the Point Fortin Wildfowl Trust: A popular activity among nature lovers, the Point Fortin Wildfowl Trust is a 30-acre bird sanctuary that is home to over 100 species of birds, including the rare Blue-Headed Macaw.

  2. Paddleboarding and Kayaking: Visitors can rent paddleboards or kayaks from the local rental companies and explore the mangrove-lined waterways near Point Fortin.

  3. Island Hopping: A number of local boat operators offer island-hopping tours that take visitors to nearby islands such as Chacachacare and Gaspar Grande.

Food and Drink Scene of Point Fortin

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Doubles: A popular street food that consists of two spicy chickpea flour patties stuffed with curried channa, tamarind sauce, and a variety of condiments.

  2. Roti: A traditional flatbread that is filled with curried chicken, beef, or vegetables, and is often served with chutney and hot sauce.

  3. Fish and Chips: A local twist on the British classic, fish and chips in Point Fortin is made with fresh-caught fish and is usually served with a side of coleslaw.

Local Beverages

  1. Carib Beer: The most popular beer in Trinidad and Tobago, Carib Beer is a light lager that is perfect for hot days on the beach.

  2. Rum Punch: A fruity and refreshing cocktail made with local rum, fruit juice, and spices.

  3. Sorrel: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made from the petals of the sorrel flower, which is a type of hibiscus.

Where to Stay in Point Fortin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Forks: A cozy bed and breakfast that is located in the heart of Point Fortin and offers comfortable rooms and friendly service.

  2. Salybia Nature Resort and Spa: A luxurious eco-resort that is located on the outskirts of Point Fortin and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  3. The Oyster Bed: A charming guesthouse that is located on the beachfront and offers a laid-back vibe and personalized service.

What to Buy in Point Fortin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hand-Crafted Jewelry: Point Fortin has a number of talented jewelry makers who create unique pieces using shells, beads, and other natural materials.

  2. Local Artwork: Visitors can find a number of galleries and art shops that offer locally-made paintings, sculptures, and other artwork.

  3. Spices and Condiments: Trinidad and Tobago is known for its flavorful seasonings and spices, which makes for great souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Point Fortin

Trinidad and Tobago is a diverse country that is known for its vibrant and colorful culture. Visitors to Point Fortin should expect to see a mix of African, Indian, and European influences in the local customs and traditions. It is also important to know that Trinidad and Tobago is a religious country, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions when visiting places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Point Fortin

The best time to visit Point Fortin is during the dry season, which runs from January to April. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and there is little to no rainfall. However, visitors should be aware that this is also peak tourist season, so accommodations and activities may be more expensive and crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Point Fortin

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting places of worship.
  3. Carry sunscreen, insect repellent, and a hat when going outdoors.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of people without their permission.
  2. Swim in prohibited areas of the beach.
  3. litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Point Fortin

Trinidad and Tobago is a relatively safe country, but visitors should still take basic precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Point Fortin

Visitors to Trinidad and Tobago should be up to date on their routine vaccinations and should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and chikungunya. It is also important to drink bottled water or boil tap water before drinking.

Getting to Point Fortin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Point Fortin

  1. Trinity Hills: A small airport that is located in Point Fortin and serves domestic flights.

  2. Piarco International Airport: The main international airport in Trinidad and Tobago, located in the capital city of Port of Spain.

Bus Stations Near Point Fortin

  1. Chaguaramas Bus Station: A major bus station that is located in Port of Spain and offers connections to Point Fortin.

  2. San Fernando Bus Station: Another major bus station that is located in the nearby city of San Fernando, which offers frequent connections to Point Fortin.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available for hire in Trinidad and Tobago. Visitors are advised to use reputable taxi companies or rental agencies and to negotiate fares in advance.

How to Get Around in Point Fortin

The easiest way to get around Point Fortin is by taxi or rental car. There is also a local bus system that connects the town with nearby cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Point Fortin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. San Fernando: Known as the “Industrial Capital” of Trinidad and Tobago, San Fernando is a bustling city that offers a number of cultural and historic attractions.

  2. Toco: Located on the northeastern coast of Trinidad, Toco is a scenic destination that is known for its secluded beaches and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor adventures, Point Fortin has something to offer. With its laid-back vibe, friendly locals, and stunning natural scenery, Point Fortin is a destination that is not to be missed. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and culture of Trinidad and Tobago.
