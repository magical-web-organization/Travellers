Introduction to Crown Point

Located in Tobago, in Trinidad and Tobago, in Lesser Antilles, in the Caribbean, in North America, Crown Point is a small town known for its pristine beaches, vibrant nightlife, and undisturbed natural beauty. The town is situated on the southwestern tip of Tobago and is easily accessible by air and road. Crown Point offers a perfect blend of adventure, relaxation, and culture, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all kinds.

List of Places to Visit in Crown Point

Crown Point has a wide range of attractions that cater to all interests. Here are some of the top places to visit in Crown Point.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Milford: This 19th-century fort offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea and comprises of five buildings with barracks, magazines, and a powder store.
  • Tobago Museum: A museum dedicated to Tobago’s history and culture, it houses a fascinating collection of Amerindian artifacts and colonial memorabilia.
  • The Mystery Tombstone: The mystery surrounding this tombstone in Plymouth’s churchyard dates back to the Plymouth uprising after a volcanic eruption in 1792.

Natural Attractions of Crown Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pigeon Point Beach: Arguably the most photographed beach on the island and famous for its white sand and turquoise water.
  • Nylon Pool: This natural swimming pool in the middle of Tobago’s Buccoo Reef is said to have healing powers.
  • Argyle Waterfall: A picturesque waterfall located in the Main Ridge Forest Reserve, with a 15-meter drop and a pool at the bottom.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crown Point

Crown Point boasts some of the best hiking and biking routes on the island. Here are some of the top trails:

  • Fort King George Heritage Park: A scenic park offering a trail that begins at the bottom of the hill and leads to Fort George at the top of the hill.
  • Main Ridge Forest Reserve: A well-trodden track that takes visitors through the lush rainforest and ends at Argyle Waterfall.
  • Englishman’s Bay: A challenging trail that requires a good level of fitness, it offers scenic views of the north coast.

Best Things to Do in Crown Point: Activities and Tours

Crown Point offers a wide range of activities and tours for travelers of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Crown Point:

  • Glass Bottom Boat Tours: A unique way to explore the coral reefs and marine life of Tobago.
  • Scuba Diving: Tobago’s clear turquoise waters offer some of the best scuba diving locations in the Caribbean.
  • Snorkeling: A great way to see the coral reefs and marine life up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Crown Point

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Crown Point:

1 Relax and unwind at Pigeon Point Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Crown Point

Crown Point offers a variety of culinary experiences, ranging from fancy restaurants to local food stalls. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab and Callaloo: A delicacy made with callaloo (a vegetable) and crab.
  • Doubles: A popular street food in Trinidad and Tobago, it is a sandwich made with two pieces of flatbread filled with curried chickpeas.
  • Roti: A soft, fluffy bread filled with meat or vegetable curry.

Local Beverages

  • Rum: Tobago’s own award-winning label, Angostura Rum, is a must-try.
  • Local beers: Carib, Stag, Mackeson, and George Lite are popular beers in Tobago.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh, cold coconut water is available all over the island.

Where to Stay in Crown Point: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crown Point has a range of accommodation options depending on your budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Crown Point:

  • Coco Reef Resort & Spa: A luxury resort with a private beach, 2 outdoor pools, and a full-service spa.
  • Tropikist Beach Hotel & Resort: A beachfront hotel with an outdoor pool, 2 restaurants, and a bar.
  • Crown Point Beach Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with a prime location on Pigeon Point Beach.

What to Buy in Crown Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Crown Point offers a range of local products and souvenirs for visitors to take home. Here are some popular items:

  • Hand-woven baskets: Made by local artisans, these baskets come in various colors, shapes, and sizes.
  • Local art: Paintings, sculptures, and carvings made by Tobago’s local artists.
  • Caribbean Spice Mix: A fragrant mix of locally grown Caribbean spices that adds flavor to any dish.

Local Culture and Customs of Crown Point

Tobago has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its music, dance, and festivals. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering homes and places of worship.
  • Respect elders and address them with respect.

Best Time to Visit Crown Point

The best time to visit Crown Point is between December and May, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the humidity is low. However, this is also peak season, so expect higher prices and more crowds. From June to November, the island experiences rainy season and the possibility of hurricanes, but this is also when hotels and flights are often cheaper.

Safety and Health

Safety in Crown Point

Crown Point is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should exercise common sense and take necessary precautions such as not leaving valuables in plain sight, avoiding isolated areas at night, and using licensed taxis.

Health in Crown Point

Visitors should take necessary precautions to protect themselves from mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and COVID-19. Travelers should consider taking anti-malaria medication and wear long-sleeved clothing and insect repellent when exploring the rainforest or engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Crown Point: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Crown Point

Crown Point International Airport (TAB) is the only airport on Tobago and serves direct flights to major cities like Miami, London, and Toronto.

Bus Stations Near Crown Point

Public transportation by bus is available in Tobago; however, it can be infrequent and unreliable. Private taxi services are available in the major cities, but rates can vary depending on the distance traveled.

Other Options

Renting a car or motorcycle is a popular option for exploring the island, but visitors must have a valid driver’s license and be over 25 years old. Sea transport is another option for getting around the island, with several ferry services available.

How to Get Around in Crown Point

The most popular way to get around Crown Point is by renting a car, scooter, or motorcycle. Taxis are also available but can be expensive. Public transportation by bus is available, but can be infrequent and unreliable.

Where to Go Next from Crown Point: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Crown Point:

  • Little Tobago Island: A small uninhabited island popular for bird watching and snorkeling.
  • Buccoo Bay: A beautiful bay with a white sand beach and offshore coral reefs.
  • Charlotteville: A quiet fishing village located on the northeastern tip of Tobago.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and range of activities and attractions, visitors will leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
