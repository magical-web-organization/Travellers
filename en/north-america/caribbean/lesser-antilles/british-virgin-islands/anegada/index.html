Introduction to Anegada

Anegada is a breathtaking limestone island located in the British Virgin Islands. Unlike other islands in the region, Anegada is flat and low-lying, making it a unique destination worth exploring. The island’s tranquil and secluded beaches are perfect for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of busy tourist destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Anegada

If you’re wondering what to do in Anegada, here are some of the top places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Anegada Museum – located in The Settlement, the museum offers visitors a glimpse into the island’s fascinating history and culture, including exhibits on the salt industry and shipwrecks.
  • The Wreck of the HMS Astraea – a 19th-century British naval ship that sank off the coast of Anegada.

Natural Attractions of Anegada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loblolly Bay Beach – a secluded, picturesque beach with crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.
  • Anegada Flamingo Pond – home to a flock of Caribbean flamingos and other wildlife, this tidal pond is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Cow Wreck Beach – a stunning beach featuring turquoise waters and powdery white sand. It is also home to Cow Wreck Beach Bar, a popular spot for drinks and Caribbean cuisine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anegada

While Anegada might be flat, it doesn’t mean that there is no way to explore the island. Visitors can go for a bike ride around the island and explore the beautiful landscape. One of the most scenic routes is from The Settlement to Cow Wreck Beach. Additionally, hiking enthusiasts can check out the popular Lobolly Bay trail - a scenic walk that will take you through the forest, past Loblolly Bay Beach, and eventually back to the starting point near the ferry dock.

Best Things to Do in Anegada: Activities and Tours

If you’re visiting Anegada, here are some of the best experiences you don’t want to miss:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving – with its clear waters and beautiful coral reefs, Anegada is a great place for underwater adventures.
  • Kiteboarding and Windsurfing – the island’s shallow waters and steady winds make Anegada the perfect destination for these two sports.
  • Sunset Cruise – there’s nothing quite like watching the sun dip below the horizon with a cold drink in hand.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anegada

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Anegada, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival and check-in. Explore The Settlement.
Day 2 Rent a bike and ride to Cow Wreck Beach. Visit the Flamingo Pond.
Day 3 Snorkeling or scuba diving excursion.
Day 4 Relax and enjoy the beaches – Loblolly Bay Beach, Windlass Bight Beach, and Flash of Beauty Beach.
Day 5 Take a tour of Anegada’s salt ponds and learn about the centuries-old salt industry.
Day 6 Kiteboarding or Windsurfing Session.
Day 7 Departure Day.

Food and Drink Scene of Anegada

Anegada has a thriving food and drink scene that features delicious Caribbean cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster – Anegada is well-known for its succulent and fresh lobster dishes, including grilled lobster tails and lobster burritos.
  • Conch Fritters – a popular Caribbean appetizer made with conch meat, peppers, spices, and fried to perfection.
  • Anegada Lobster Festival is held annually in November, offering travelers a chance to indulge in fresh lobster for a week.

Local Beverages

  • Painkiller – a cocktail made with rum, pineapple, orange juice, and coconut milk.
  • Bushwacker – a creamy cocktail made with rum, Kahlua, and cream of coconut.

Where to Stay in Anegada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re looking for a place to stay in Anegada, here are some great accommodation options:

  • Anegada Beach Club – a luxurious beachfront resort boasting a stunning ocean view and private dock with free boat shuttle services.
  • Anegada Reef Hotel – featuring a family-friendly environment and beachfront location, this hotel offers a casual and relaxing atmosphere.
  • Cow Wreck Beach Resort – offering charming rooms with outdoor dining at its beach bar, Cow Wreck Beach Resort is the perfect budget-friendly option for couples and families.

What to Buy in Anegada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home, here are some of the best local products you can buy in Anegada:

  • Anegada Salt – a popular local product made from sea salt harvested from the island’s salt ponds. Anegada salt is perfect for seasoning food and has a unique flavor.
  • Handmade crafts – made from conch shells, driftwood, and other natural materials, these crafts offer unique gift options and souvenirs.
  • Anegada Lobster Sauce – bottled lobster sauce made from locally caught lobsters. The sauce is great as a dip or marinade for seafood dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Anegada

Anegada’s culture is rooted in its history as a fishing and sailing hub. Islanders are proud of their heritage and island traditions, including fishing and boat building. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including modest dress and quietness during times of mourning.

Best Time to Visit Anegada

The best time to visit Anegada is from December to April, the region’s dry season. During this time, visitors can expect clear skies, calm waters, and pleasant temperatures. However, it’s worth noting that peak season can be more crowded and expensive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anegada

When visiting Anegada, here are some things you should and shouldn’t do:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the lobster dishes and local beverages.
  • Take a sailing excursion around the island.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island’s beaches or in the water.
  • Approach the wildlife, including the flamingos and turtles.
  • Disrupt the island’s primary industry of salt production.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Anegada, it’s essential to be aware of any potential safety or health issues.

Safety in Anegada

Anegada is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions against petty theft and crime. Travelers should also be cautious when swimming and boating in the open ocean, as there can be strong currents and rough seas.

Health in Anegada

There are no specific health risks associated with traveling to Anegada. However, visitors should pack insect repellent as mosquitoes can be present. Additionally, travelers should ensure that they have all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to lessen any potential risks.

Getting to Anegada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Medication tourists arriving in Anegada will have to take a connecting flight or a helicopter ride from Virgin Gorda airport through Cape Air, Island Birds, or private plane charters. Another way to travel to the island is by ferry from Tortola or Virgin Gorda.

Airports Near Anegada

The nearest airports to Anegada are “Virgin Gorda Airport” and “Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport.” Flying to one of these airports before taking a connecting flight or helicopter ride to Anegada is the most efficient route to take.

Bus Stations Near Anegada

As Anegada is a small island, there are no bus stations available. Instead, visitors can rent bicycles or golf carts to travel around the island.

Other Options

The most popular way to travel to Anegada is by ferry from Tortola or Virgin Gorda. The ferry service is operated by “Speedys Ferry Services” and “Native Son Ferry.” However, visitors can also take a private charter boat if they prefer a more exclusive experience.

How to Get Around in Anegada

As Anegada is a small island, visitors can easily walk or bike around. Additionally, golf cart rentals are available from various shops on the island.

Where to Go Next from Anegada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Caribbean adventure, there are many other nearby destinations worth exploring.

  • Virgin Gorda – known for its beautiful beaches and famous rock formations, “The Baths”.
  • Tortola – the capital of the British Virgin Islands and a hub for shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Anegada is a fantastic destination for visitors looking for an off-the-beaten-path Caribbean experience. The island’s beautiful landscape, delicious cuisine, and rich history make it a must-visit for travelers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or just to relax on the beach, Anegada has something for everyone.
