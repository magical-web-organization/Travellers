Introduction to Southern Islands

The Southern Islands are a stunning group of islands located in the British Virgin Islands, which can be found in the Lesser Antilles of the Caribbean. The picturesque islands are known for their crystal-clear waters, soft trade winds, and sandy beaches that are perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing. There are numerous things to do in the Southern Islands, from exploring historical sites to enjoying water sports and scenic hiking trails. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation, the Southern Islands are a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Southern Islands have a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. The following museums and historical sites offer an insight into the region’s heritage:

  • (1) The Virgin Islands Folk Museum: Learn about the lifestyle, customs, and traditions of the Virgin Islands over the years through folk art, exhibits and artifacts.
  • (2) The Wreck of the Rhone Museum: Discover the history and artifacts of the Rhone, the British mail steamer that sank near Salt Island in 1867. This museum holds the remains of the ship that is now protected as an underwater park within the sea.
  • (3) The Old Government House Museum: Step back in time to the late 1700s through displays of colonial artifacts, paintings, furniture, and silver.

Natural Attractions of Southern Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural attractions of the Southern Islands are truly breathtaking, whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or an adrenaline rush.

  • (1) The Baths: Famous for its natural rock formations, the Baths is one of the most famous attractions on Virgin Gorda Island. It offers scenic swimming in the grottoes and passing beneath naturally formed arches throughout the boulders
  • (2) Devil’s Bay National Park: Home to an abundance of hiking trails and offering some of the finest snorkeling spots in the BVI, Devil’s Bay is perfect for those seeking adventure and beautiful views.
  • (3) Sailing: Renting out a catamaran or smaller day boats are popular activities. The day can be filled with sailing, snorkeling, swimming, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Islands

If you’re an adventurous person, you’ll enjoy exploring the rugged terrains of the Southern Islands. The following hiking and biking routes offer stunning views of the region:

  • (1) Gorda Peak National Park: This park offers a picturesque 1.5-mile hiking trail with stunning views of the islands, with Bolongo Bay Resort as a must-see location on the way up.
  • (2) Sage Mountain National Park: This is the highest elevation in all the British Virgin Islands with a 2.5-mile hiking trail which culminates in spectacular views of several of the British Virgin Islands and the warm blue waters.

Best Things to Do in Southern Islands: Activities and Tours

There are a plenty of activities and tours that the visitors may enjoy. make sure to consider these options:

  • (1) Island-Hopping: Southern Islands have many stunning islands, and a variety of ways one can experience them. Tour operators include day trips and sunset cruises, island hopping from Tortola coastal highlights to oil baths.
  • (2) Scuba Diving: There are a plenty of dive operators in the Southern Islands with experienced professionals offering different levels of certification to both novice and experienced divers.
  • (3) Kiteboarding and windsurfing: If you ‘re more of a adrenaline junkie, you might consider going on a kiteboarding or windsurfing. The island offers calm shallow waters with a steady trade wind.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Islands

The Southern Islands are known for their fresh seafood and island-style cuisine. While in the Southern Islands, make sure to give the following dishes and drinks a try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Conch Fritters: Popular as a snack, these fritters are made from conch and flour batter and deep-fried.
  • (2) Lobster: Cooked fresh out of the sea, the lobsters are perfectly seasoned and served to perfection.
  • (3) Roti: A blend of curried meats, vegetables, and spices wrapped in a tortilla or naan bread.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Painkiller: Made with rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, and coconut cream - this is the quintessential BVI cocktail.
  • (2) Bushwacker: Another signature rum drink, carefully blended with chocolate and coconut.
  • (3) Frozen Rum Drinks: Can’t decide? Check out the Banana Daiquiri, Mango Margarita or the Love City.

Where to Stay in Southern Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many places to stay in the Southern Islands to fit every budget. The following options are worth considering:

  • (1) Rosewood Little Dix Bay: Luxurious rooms which feature scenic views with a beautiful pool and private beach.
  • (2) Anegada Beach Club: Rustic luxury rooms on the island of Anegada features its own beach and kiteboarding school.
  • (3) Sebastian’s on the Beach: This hotel offers prime location and budget-friendly boutique rooms right on the beach.

What to Buy in Southern Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The BVI’s offer many locally-made products that make for great souvenirs to bring back home. A few popular items to pick up are:

  • (1) Local Soaps: Some of the most popular scents include lemongrass, mango and coconut.
  • (2) Local accessories: Featuring a range of beach jewelry, bags, locally-made clothing, and more - try local specialty stores such as Hemp and Dope, Botanical Traders.
  • (3) Hot Sauces: You won’t have to search long the island’s flavors and sauces are popular and tasty.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Islands

The locals of Southern Islands are known for their welcoming hospitality. It’s important to bear in mind the following customs and cultural norms for an unforgettable experience:

  • (1) Be respectful of their culture while visiting.
  • (2) Make sure that you always greet people warmly.
  • (3) The Islanders are known to be religious, so dress appropriately if visiting a religious site or church

Best Time to Visit Southern Islands

The best time to visit the Southern Islands is between December and April. During this period, the weather is ideal and offers the perfect opportunity to enjoy the white sandy beaches and clear waters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Islands

Do:

  • (1) Spend time on one of the many beaches.
  • (2) Explore the natural scenery and hiking trails.
  • (3) Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • (1) Leave trash behind.
  • (2) Overlook cultural customs.
  • (3) Ignore wildlife protection laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Islands

The Southern Islands are generally safe, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take normal precautions such as not leaving valuable items unattended or in plain sight and locking your car.

Health in Southern Islands

While staying in the Southern Islands, make sure to avoid drinking tap water. Make sure to pack any necessary medications, and have adequate protection from the sun.

Getting to Southern Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Islands

The main airport serving Southern Islands is the Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport on Beef Island. The airport is located on Tortola Island, connected by bridge to Beef Island which provides easy access to both the USVI and the rest of the British Virgin Islands.

Bus Stations Near Southern Islands

Unfortunately, there are no bus systems or stations for the Southern Islands. Taxis, ferries and water taxis, and bike and car rentals are the common modes of transportation.

Other Options

Ferry vessels service the Southern Islands regularly.

How to Get Around in Southern Islands

The best way to get around in the Southern Islands is by water taxi and taxi cars can be rented at major access points.

Where to Go Next from Southern Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some options for nearby destinations and excursions might include:

  • (1) St. John’s US Virgin Islands: The small Caribbean island offers sandy shores, coral reefs, and national parks, all of which are part of the Virgin Islands National Park system.
  • (2) Anegada Reef: At one of the most isolated islands of British Virgin Islands, this coral dreamland is an idyllic spot for swimming and snorkeling.
  • (3) Virgin Gorda: Offering distinct panoramic views, this island features the famous Baths rock formations and hiking trails.

Conclusion

The Southern Islands offer a perfect tropical getaway, with its beautiful landscapes, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Regardless of your interests, the Southern Islands are a destination worth considering for an unforgettable experience. With careful planning and consideration, you can have an enjoyable and safe trip to the Southern Islands.
