Introduction to Jost Van Dyke

Jost Van Dyke is a small island located in the British Virgin Islands in the Lesser Antilles of the Caribbean. With a population of just around 300, Jost Van Dyke is a quiet and serene tourist destination known for its white sandy beaches, clear blue waters, and unspoiled natural beauty. The island has a laid-back atmosphere that is perfect for travelers seeking relaxation and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Jost Van Dyke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bomba Shack - This popular beach bar is a must-visit destination for tourists looking for a unique experience. The bar features a collection of art that has been painted and drawn by visitors over the years.

  • Foxy’s Tamarind Bar - Foxy’s is a legendary bar and restaurant that has been serving customers for over 50 years. The bar is famous for its live music, beach parties, and friendly atmosphere.

  • St. Michael’s Church - St. Michael’s Church is the oldest church on Jost Van Dyke and is an important cultural and historical landmark. The church was built by local craftsmen in the 1860s and is known for its beautiful stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Jost Van Dyke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Bay Beach - White Bay is one of the most popular beaches on Jost Van Dyke and is famous for its crystal clear waters and soft white sand. Visitors can relax on the beach and take part in various water activities such as snorkeling and swimming.

  • Great Harbour - Great Harbour is a picturesque bay that is popular among yacht enthusiasts. The harbor offers stunning views and is a great place to relax and soak up the sun.

  • Sandy Cay - Sandy Cay is a tiny uninhabited island located just off the coast of Jost Van Dyke. The island is a nature reserve and offers visitors a chance to see a range of marine life and bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jost Van Dyke

  • Mount Sage National Park - Mount Sage National Park is located on Tortola, the largest island in the British Virgin Islands. The park offers visitors a chance to hike through lush rainforests and see some of the island’s unique flora and fauna.

  • Jost Van Dyke Scenic Trail - The Jost Van Dyke Scenic Trail is a hiking trail that takes visitors through some of the island’s most scenic areas. The trail offers stunning views of the island’s coastline and is a great way to explore the island’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Jost Van Dyke: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping Tour - Jost Van Dyke is located close to other popular tourist destinations such as Tortola and Virgin Gorda. Visitors can book an island-hopping tour to explore these nearby destinations.

  • Sail and Snorkel Tour - Jost Van Dyke is known for its beautiful reefs and marine life. Visitors can book a sail and snorkel tour to explore these stunning underwater landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Jost Van Dyke

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Fungi - Fish and Fungi is a traditional dish that is popular in the British Virgin Islands. The dish consists of fried fish and a side dish made from cornmeal, okra, and spices.

  • Lobster - Jost Van Dyke is known for its delicious lobster dishes. The local restaurants offer a variety of lobster dishes, including grilled lobster and lobster bisque.

Local Beverages

  • Painkiller - The Painkiller is a popular rum-based cocktail that is made with rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, cream of coconut, and nutmeg.

  • Jost Van Dyke Island Ale - Jost Van Dyke Island Ale is a locally brewed beer that is popular among tourists and locals.

Where to Stay in Jost Van Dyke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sandcastle Hotel - Sandcastle Hotel is a luxury hotel located on White Bay Beach. The hotel offers guests a range of amenities, including a pool, restaurant, and beachfront cabanas.

  • Ivan’s Campground - Ivan’s Campground is a budget-friendly accommodation option that is popular among backpackers and budget travelers. The campground offers basic amenities such as shower facilities and is located near White Bay Beach.

What to Buy in Jost Van Dyke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art - Jost Van Dyke is known for its vibrant local art scene. Visitors can purchase a variety of locally made art, including paintings, sculptures, and jewelry.

  • Rum - The British Virgin Islands are known for their delicious rum. Visitors can purchase a variety of locally made rum products, including bottles of rum and rum cakes.

Local Culture and Customs of Jost Van Dyke

Jost Van Dyke has a rich cultural heritage that is heavily influenced by its African and European heritage. The island is known for its music, dancing, cuisine, and art. Visitors are encouraged to respect the island’s local customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Jost Van Dyke

The best time to visit Jost Van Dyke is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. The weather during this time is warm and dry, with temperatures ranging from 75°F to 85°F. The island can be overcrowded during peak season, so travelers looking for a quieter experience may want to consider visiting during the shoulder season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jost Van Dyke

Do:

  • Respect the island’s local customs and beliefs.
  • Take plenty of sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Be friendly and respectful to the local people.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the island’s natural resources.
  • Wear swimwear outside of the beach areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jost Van Dyke

Jost Van Dyke is generally considered a safe tourist destination. However, visitors are advised to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and not leaving belongings unattended.

Health in Jost Van Dyke

Visitors to Jost Van Dyke are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Travelers should also make sure they are up to date on routine vaccinations before visiting the island.

Getting to Jost Van Dyke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jost Van Dyke

The nearest airport to Jost Van Dyke is the Cyril E. King Airport in St. Thomas, which is located approximately 21 miles away. From there, visitors can take a ferry to Jost Van Dyke.

Bus Stations Near Jost Van Dyke

Jost Van Dyke does not have a bus station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Jost Van Dyke by ferry from other islands in the British Virgin Islands such as Tortola and Virgin Gorda.

How to Get Around in Jost Van Dyke

The best way to get around Jost Van Dyke is by foot or taxi. The island is small enough to explore on foot, and the taxis are affordable and reliable.

Where to Go Next from Jost Van Dyke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Jost Van Dyke can take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Tortola, Virgin Gorda, and Anegada. These islands offer a range of tourist activities, including hiking, sailing, and snorkeling.

Jost Van Dyke is a hidden gem in the Caribbean that is perfect for travelers seeking relaxation and adventure. With its unspoiled beaches, vibrant local culture, and unique cuisine, Jost Van Dyke is sure to be a hit with tourists of all ages and backgrounds. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable island getaway!
