This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in the British Virgin Islands.

Introduction to British Virgin Islands

The British Virgin Islands (BVI) consist of over 60 islands and cays, with several of them uninhabited. With a population of around 32,000, most of whom are of African descent, the British Virgin Islands maintain a distinct culture and heritage from its colonial roots. The official language is English, and the currency is the US dollar. The islands have a tropical climate, with temperatures ranging from 75 °F to 85 °F all year round. The peak tourist season is from November to April, but the islands are a year-round destination.

Local Culture and Customs of British Virgin Islands

The British Virgin Islands have a rich culture and local customs worth experiencing.

One of the intrinsic culture is the annual Foxy’s Wooden Boat Regatta, where more than 100 boats compete in races, cultural activities, and nightly entertainment.

The festival of the August First (Emancipation Day) is another significant cultural event on the island. In this day, it commemorates the end of slavery and features mocko-jumbie dancers, traditional drum, and music.

The people on BVI are generally very friendly and welcoming, it’s customary to greet locals back as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit British Virgin Islands

The best time to visit the British Virgin Islands is from November to April, which is a dry season of 25-85°F. May to October is historically a rainy season where you’ll see more rainfall and high humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of British Virgin Islands

Here are some important things to keep in mind while visiting the British Virgin Islands.

Do:

  • Respect local customs
  • Wear restraint clothing at religious places and formal settings
  • Always ask permission before taking photographs
  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute local beaches or bays
  • Take expensive jewelry or valuables when snorkeling or swimming
  • Public nudity or public displays of affection are banned

Safety and Health

Safety in British Virgin Islands

The British Virgin Islands are a safe place to travel for tourists. However, petty crime, such as theft, can occur. Be vigilant, avoid ostentatious displays of wealth, and don’t leave valuable items unattended on the beaches.

Health in British Virgin Islands

The British Virgin Islands have various healthcare facilities. However, it’s best to have travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to British Virgin Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near British Virgin Islands

The Terrance B. Lettsome International Airport is the main airport in the British Virgin Islands. It’s situated on Beef Island, just east of Tortola.

Bus Stations Near British Virgin Islands

There are no bus stations on the British Virgin Islands. Visitors can explore many of the larger islands by taxi or rental car.

Other Options

You can also arrive in the British Virgin Islands by ferry from St Thomas, St Croix, Puerto Rico, and the US Virgins.

How to Get Around in British Virgin Islands

The best way to get around the British Virgin Islands is to rent a car or motor scooter. Taxis and water taxis are also available, but can be expensive. Ferries are a great way to visit the nearby islands.
