Introduction to Union Island

Union Island is a small island located in the southern section of the Grenadines, which are a collection of islands that form part of the nation of St. Vincent and the Grenadines. Union Island is known for its scenic views and outdoor activities such as snorkeling and hiking. It is also home to a variety of cultural attractions, museums, and historical places.

List of Places to Visit in Union Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Union Island boasts a number of museums and historical places that are of cultural significance. Some of them include:

  • Union Island Museum
  • Fort Hill
  • Ashton Salt Pond
  • The Grotto

Local Culture and Customs of Union Island

Union Island is known for its relaxed and friendly atmosphere. The island is primarily English-speaking, but many locals speak other languages such as French and Spanish. Here are some of the local customs visitors should keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Tipping is expected in restaurants and bars
  • Islanders are proud of their rich cultural heritage, so show respect when interacting with them

Best Time to Visit Union Island

The best time to visit Union Island is between November and May when the weather is warm and dry, and the sea is calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Union Island

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their traditions
  • Be prepared for rainy weather
  • Take a boat tour to explore the nearby islands

Don’t:

  • Wear camouflaged clothing as it is illegal on the island
  • Litter or damage the local environment
  • Touch or feed the marine life

Safety and Health

Safety in Union Island

Union Island is generally safe for visitors, as crime rates are low. However, it’s always recommended to take precautionary measures such as locking your valuables and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Union Island

Visitors are advised to be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus. It is recommended to take necessary precautions such as using mosquito repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Union Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Union Island

The nearest airports to Union Island are the Maurice Bishop International Airport in Grenada and the Argyle International Airport in St. Vincent. From there, visitors can take a direct flight or charter a flight to Union Island.

Bus Stations Near Union Island

There are no bus stations on Union Island. Visitors can instead rent a car or scooter from one of the rental companies on the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Union Island by taking a ferry or sailing one of the many charter vessels that operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Union Island

Visitors can easily get around Union Island by renting a car, scooter, or bicycle. Taxis are also available to transport visitors to various locations on the island.
