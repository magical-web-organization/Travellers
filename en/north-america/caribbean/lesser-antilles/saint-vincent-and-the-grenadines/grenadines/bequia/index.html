Introduction to Bequia

Bequia is a small island located in the Grenadines, which is part of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines in Lesser Antilles, Caribbean, North America. Bequia is a picturesque island known for its lush greenery, pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and friendly locals. The island boasts a rich culture and history, which is evident through its museums, historic sites, and cultural attractions. Whether you are seeking a peaceful retreat or an adventure-filled vacation, Bequia has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bequia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Churches, and Historical Places

  1. The Bequia Maritime Museum: This museum showcases the island’s rich maritime history and features exhibits on boatbuilding, whaling, and fishing industries.
  2. The Old Hegg Turtle Sanctuary: Visitors can see and learn about the endangered species of Hawksbill Turtle, which this sanctuary rescues and rehabilitates.
  3. The St Mary’s Anglican Church: Over 150 years old, this church has stunning stained glass windows and an enchanting graveyard to explore.
  4. Hamilton Fort: A historical site in Bequia, this fort offers stunning panoramic views of the island and the surrounding ocean.

Natural Attractions of Bequia: Beaches, Waterfalls, and Hiking

  1. Princess Margaret Beach: This unspoiled beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.
  2. Lower Bay Beach: A quieter option, this beach is perfect for families, and vacationers seeking a relaxing atmosphere.
  3. Mount Pleasant Hill: A popular hiking spot among tourists, this hill offers breathtaking views and bird watching opportunities.
  4. Bequia’s Waterfalls: There are several beautiful waterfalls to explore in Bequia, such as Falls of Baleine and Dark View Falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bequia

For those who enjoy hiking and biking, the trails in Bequia offer scenic views of the island’s flora, fauna, and wildlife.

  1. La Pompe Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike, this trail takes you through lush forest, and up to impressive viewpoints.
  2. Spring Plantation: A scenic route, this trail takes you through old plantations, and leads to a secluded and idyllic bay.

Best Things to Do in Bequia: Activities and Tours

  1. Turtle watching tour: Take a tour to see and learn about the Hawksbill Turtles, which Bequia is home to.
  2. Scuba diving and snorkeling: Get up close with the island’s vibrant marine life on a dive or snorkel trip.
  3. Sailing adventures: Rent a sailboat and explore the crystal clear waters and stunning landscapes of Bequia.
  4. Local Art and Crafts tours: Explore the island’s artistic side and bring back unique handmade souvenirs and mementos.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bequia

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrival and check-in at hotel. Walk along the harbor and explore the town’s main shops and streets.
Day 2 Take a sailing tour to visit some of the local ecosystems around Bequia, explore the coves and beaches nearby

Food and Drink Scene of Bequia

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lobster: Bequia is well known for its delicious lobster dishes, especially when it is in season (October to May).
  2. Saltfish: An island favorite, this dish is usually served mixed with onions, peppers, and tomatoes in a sandwich or with crackers.
  3. Callaloo Soup: A soup made from callaloo leaves, which are a local ingredient found on the island, and mixed with okra and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  1. Sunset Rum Punch: A concoction made with rum, fresh-squeezed fruit juice, and bitters, and served at sunset on the resort or hotel.
  2. Hairoun beer: This is the local beer that can be found all over Saint Vincent and the Grenadines and Bequia island.

Where to Stay in Bequia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bequia offers several options for accommodations, with hotels and guesthouses located close to the beaches and town centers.

  1. Bequia Beach Hotel: A luxury hotel with a private beach, spa, and fine dining options.
  2. Keegan’s Beachside: This hotel is located near the beach and offers ocean view rooms and suites.
  3. Frangipani Hotel: A charming hotel located near Port Elizabeth, offering serene garden rooms.

What to Buy in Bequia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Local Artifacts: Local artisans make handcrafted wares such as pottery, baskets, and wood carvings, perfect to bring back as a souvenir.
  2. Bequia’s famous model boats: These boats are miniature replicas of the traditional whaling boats that Bequia is famous for, and make great decorations for your home or office.

Local Culture and Customs of Bequia

Bequians are friendly people with warm hospitality. The island has a Caribbean culture with elements of British influence, which is reflected in its architecture and traditions. As an island, it has a laid-back and relaxed atmosphere with rhythms, music, and an island way of life

Best Time to Visit Bequia

Bequia is a year-round destination, with consistent weather throughout the year. The peak season is between December and April when the weather is drier, and it brings the highest numbers of tourists to the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bequia

Do:

  1. Do visit the historical sites and cultural attractions of the island.
  2. Do support the local vendors and craftsmen by purchasing their handmade goods and souvenirs.
  3. Do learn some basic Caribbean creole language.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter the environment; keep the island clean.
  2. Don’t remove any natural articles such as corals or shells from the beaches.
  3. Don’t forget to pack sunblock and insect repellent when packing for the trip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bequia

Bequia is a generally safe destination with friendly people. However, it is recommended to use common sense and caution when visiting any foreign destination.

Health in Bequia

Visitors to Bequia should have up-to-date vaccinations and preventative medications for mosquito-borne illnesses, such as Dengue and Chikungunya. The island has a small clinic but is not equipped for medical emergencies, so it is recommended to purchase travel insurance.

Getting to Bequia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bequia

Bequia has a small airport called JF Mitchell Airport (BQU). Direct international flights are not available; however, regional flights from Barbados, Saint Lucia, and Saint Vincent are available with LIAT or SVG Air.

Bus Stations Near Bequia

The island has a small public bus system, which goes to the local villages and tourist attraction spots.

Other Options

Taxis are readily available for hire outside the airport and can be arranged with your hotel or accommodation.

How to Get Around in Bequia

Taxis, buses, and water taxis are some of the transportation options in Bequia, Or, renting a car or scooter can also provide more flexibility to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Bequia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The following are recommended nearby destinations and excursions to visit after Bequia:

  1. Tobago Cays - A collection of five small uninhabited islands with breathtaking landscapes and turquoise waters.
  2. Mustique Island - An exclusive and luxurious area that is a popular vacation spot among celebrities.
  3. St Vincent Island - The largest island in the Grenadines, St Vincent is the best option for visitors seeking cultural attractions and nature reserves.

Conclusion

Bequia is a hidden gem of an island that is waiting to be discovered. The island is a perfect destination for beach lovers, hikers, history buffs, and cultural enthusiasts. The warm and friendly people of Bequia provide an accommodating and hospitable environment, making it a popular destination for travelers. With its pristine beaches, stunning landscapes, and rich culture, it is no surprise that Bequia has become a destination of choice for many vacationers.
