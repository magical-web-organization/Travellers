Introduction to Anguilla

Anguilla is a beautiful Caribbean island located in the Lesser Antilles, North America. It is a destination known for its white sandy beaches, coral reefs, crystal clear waters, and tranquility. With a population of only 14,000 people, Anguilla is a peaceful getaway for travelers seeking a slower pace of life. The island has a fascinating history, with influences from indigenous peoples, European colonizers, and African slaves. Anguilla offers food, culture, adventure, relaxation, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for solo travelers, couples, families, or groups.

List of Places to Visit in Anguilla

Anguilla is paradise for adventure lovers, nature enthusiasts, and culture seekers. Here are some must-visit sights in Anguilla.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wallblake House - A restored 1787 plantation house and museum showcasing Anguillian history, culture, and architecture.
  2. Heritage Collection Museum - A museum and art gallery exhibiting Anguillian and Caribbean art, crafts, and artifacts.
  3. The Valley - The main town and a cultural hub of Anguilla, with colonial-era buildings, shops, and restaurants.
  4. Fountain Cavern National Park - A prehistoric cave dwelling and archaeological site, exhibiting Amerindian culture and lifestyle in Anguilla’s past.

Natural Attractions of Anguilla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sandy Island - A coral cay with pristine beaches, vibrant marine life, and water sports activities.
  2. Rendezvous Bay - A two-mile-long beach with soft sand, calm waters, and scenic views.
  3. Prickly Pear Cays - A pair of uninhabited islets with coral reefs, beach bars, and snorkeling spots.
  4. Crocus Bay - A secluded bay with crystal clear waters, mangroves, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anguilla

Anguilla has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views, exercise, and adventure. Some of the popular routes include:

  1. Meads Bay Trail - A 2.5-mile trail starting from Meads Bay Beach and ending at West End Bay. It offers stunning views of the sea, beaches, and cliffs.
  2. Island Harbour to Scrub Island Trail - A 5-mile trail starting from Island Harbour and ending at Scrub Island Beach. It offers a panoramic view of the Eastern end of Anguilla.
  3. The Forest Loop - A 1.5-mile trail starting from Little Harbour and ending at Crocus Bay. It offers a shady walk through the Forest Reserve and scenic views of the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Anguilla: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba Diving and Snorkeling - Explore the underwater world of Anguilla with a variety of dive sites, including shipwrecks, reefs, and caves.
  2. Boat Tours and Sailing - Enjoy a day or sunset cruise around Anguilla’s coasts, cays, and lagoons.
  3. Anguilla Art Trail - Discover the rich art and culture of Anguilla by visiting galleries, museums, and studios, and interacting with local artists.
  4. Beach Hopping - Visit Anguilla’s pristine beaches with different sand colors, water textures, and activities, such as sunbathing, swimming, kite surfing, and beach volleyball.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anguilla

Day 1 - Arrival and Relaxation in Rendezvous Bay Day 2 - Snorkeling and Beach Hopping in Crocus Bay Day 3 - Island Tour: The Valley, Heritage Collection Museum, and Wallblake House Day 4 - Boat Tour to Prickly Pear Cays Day 5 - Bike Tour: Meads Bay Trail and Island Harbour to Scrub Island Trail Day 6 - Spa and Wellness in Shoal Bay Village Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Anguilla

Anguilla offers a unique blend of Caribbean, African, and European cuisine, with fresh seafood, grilled meats, spicy sauces, and tropical fruits. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Crayfish Soup - Spicy soup with crayfish, vegetables, and island spices.
  2. Anguillian Fish and Chips - Fried fish fillets served with fries and tartar sauce.
  3. Johnny Cakes - Deep-fried dough balls served with saltfish stew and hot sauce.
  4. Lobster Thermidor - Broiled lobster with wine, cheese, and cream sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Rum Punch - A popular cocktail made with rum, fruit juice, and grenadine syrup.
  2. Banks Beer - A light beer brewed in Barbados and widely available in Anguilla.
  3. Mauby - A sweet, carbonated drink made from tree bark, with a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Anguilla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anguilla has several accommodations to choose from to suit every budget or preference. Here are some top-rated options:

  1. Malliouhana, Auberge Resorts Collection - A luxury boutique resort on a high bluff overlooking Meads Bay beach, with a spa, golf course, and fine dining.
  2. Carimar Beach Club - A family-friendly beachfront resort with spacious suites, a pool, and a restaurant.
  3. Allamanda Beach Club - A beachfront hotel with studio and apartment-style accommodations, a pool, and a beach bar.

What to Buy in Anguilla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Anguilla offers many unique souvenirs and local products that represent its culture and heritage. Some popular items include:

  1. Sea Salt - Pure salt harvested from sea water in Anguilla, with a distinctive flavor and texture.
  2. Handmade Crafts - Handcrafted baskets, hats, jewelry, and pottery made by local artisans.
  3. Hot Sauce - A spicy condiment made with peppers, vinegar, and island spices, available in many flavors and heat levels.
  4. Artwork - Original paintings, sculptures, and prints from Anguillian artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Anguilla

Anguillian culture is a blend of African, Caribbean, and European influences, reflected in its art, music, dance, cuisine, and lifestyle. Anguillians are known for their warmth, hospitality, and resilience, as they have overcome natural disasters and political challenges in their history. Visitors should respect their customs and etiquette, such as taking off their shoes before entering a home, dressing modestly in public, and using island slang (e.g. “lime” for hanging out).

Best Time to Visit Anguilla

The best time to visit Anguilla is from December to April, when the weather is dry, sunny, and cool, with temperatures ranging from 75°F to 85°F. The off-season from May to November is also a good time to visit if you don’t mind some rain or hurricanes. The peak season from Christmas to New Year and Easter can be crowded and expensive, so book in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anguilla

Do:

  1. Respect local culture and customs.
  2. Wear sunscreen and insect repellent.
  3. Try local cuisine and beverages.
  4. Enjoy the beaches, nature, and activities.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in illegal drugs or activities.
  2. Leave valuables unattended.
  3. Disrespect locals or their property.
  4. Cause damage to nature or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anguilla

Anguilla is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, like any other place, travelers should take precautions to avoid theft, scams, or accidents, such as locking doors, not carrying too much cash, and following safety instructions for water sports.

Health in Anguilla

Anguilla has a good healthcare system, with public hospitals, clinics, and private doctors available. Visitors should have full medical insurance coverage and follow standard health protocols, such as drinking bottled water, avoiding raw or undercooked food, and getting vaccinated for routine diseases.

Getting to Anguilla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Anguilla is accessible by air and sea, with several options available.

Airports Near Anguilla

  1. Clayton J. Lloyd International Airport (AXA) - The main airport on Anguilla, located in the south of the island, with direct flights from regional and international destinations.
  2. Princess Juliana International Airport (SXM) - A major airport in St. Maarten, with connecting flights or ferry service to Anguilla.

Bus Stations Near Anguilla

There are no bus stations on Anguilla, as the island has limited public transportation options. Taxis, rental cars, or private transfers are recommended.

Other Options

  1. Ferry - A popular and scenic way to reach Anguilla from St. Maarten, with frequent departures and cheap fares.
  2. Private Boat - A luxury and flexible option to reach Anguilla from neighboring islands or resorts.

How to Get Around in Anguilla

The best way to get around in Anguilla is by car rental, scooter rental, or taxi. Anguilla has a small road network with some unpaved or narrow roads, so drivers should be careful and aware of local traffic laws. Cycling and walking are also possible options for short distances or leisurely exploration.

Where to Go Next from Anguilla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anguilla is located near several other Caribbean islands or destinations that are worth visiting as well. Some possible options include:

  1. St. Maarten/St. Martin - Another Caribbean island known for its beaches, casinos, and Dutch/French culture.
  2. St. Barthélemy - A French island with upscale resorts, shopping, and cuisine.
  3. Saba - A Dutch island with lush rainforests, hiking trails, and diving spots.
  4. Puerto Rico - A U.S. territory with historical, cultural, and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Anguilla is a hidden gem in the Caribbean, with a unique blend of history, culture, nature, and relaxation. From its pristine beaches to its spicy cuisine, from its vivid art scene to its unspoiled forests, Anguilla offers something for everyone. We hope this tour guide helps you plan your next trip to Anguilla and discover its hidden treasures. Happy travels!
