Introduction to Philipsburg

Philipsburg is a stunning coastal town located on the Dutch side of the Caribbean island of Saint Martin. The town is situated on a narrow strip of land between Great Bay and Salt Pond, and it’s known for its beautiful beaches, colorful buildings, and rich history.

This Dutch colonial town provides a unique blend of Caribbean and European cultures, which can be seen in its architecture, cuisine, and language. In this guide, we’ll explore all the best things to see and do on your trip to Philipsburg, including its natural attractions, cultural landmarks, lively food and drink scene, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Philipsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Maarten Museum: This museum explores the history and culture of Saint Martin through a range of exhibits and artifacts, including traditional clothing, colonial-era maps, and information about the island’s indigenous people. Visitors can also learn about the African slave trade and the island’s role in the Caribbean economy.

  • Front Street: This historic street is the main shopping area in Philipsburg, and it’s lined with colorful colonial-era buildings that house shops, restaurants, and bars. You can explore the street’s rich history on a walking tour, or simply stroll and enjoy the views.

  • Fort Amsterdam: This 17th-century fort was built by the Dutch in order to protect their colony from pirates and other invaders. The fort is open to visitors, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding bay and coastline.

Natural Attractions of Philipsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great Bay Beach: This gorgeous white-sand beach is one of the most popular spots in Philipsburg, and it offers turquoise waters and stunning views of the Caribbean Sea. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and watersports like snorkeling and kayaking.

  • Little Bay Beach: This secluded beach is located just south of Philipsburg, and it’s known for its crystal-clear waters and peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking, or explore the surrounding hills and coastline on foot.

  • Guana Bay Beach: This beautiful beach is located on the eastern side of Saint Martin, and it’s less crowded than the beaches in Philipsburg. Visitors can enjoy the turquoise waters, soft sand, and scenic views of the surrounding hills and cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Philipsburg

  • Red Rock Trail: This 1 mile (1.6 km) trail is situated in the beautiful Red Rock nature reserve, which is located just outside Philipsburg. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and coastline, and visitors can enjoy hiking, birdwatching, and picnicking.

  • Bicycle Tour of Philipsburg: Visitors can explore the town and surrounding countryside on a guided bike tour. This tour showcases Philipsburg’s rich history and culture, as well as its beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Philipsburg: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of the surrounding waters, which offers beautiful views of the island’s coastline and wildlife. Tours can include cruising around the island, visiting nearby islands, or simply enjoying the sunset.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The waters around Philipsburg are home to a variety of colorful marine life, including corals, fish, and sea turtles. Visitors can explore these underwater wonders on a snorkeling or diving trip, which can be arranged through a local tour operator.

  • Jet Skiing: Visitors can rent a jet ski and explore the island’s coastline on their own, or join a guided tour that takes them to remote beaches and hidden coves.

Food and Drink Scene of Philipsburg

Must-Try Dishes

  • Johnny Cakes: These are a staple of Caribbean cuisine, and consist of fried dough often served at breakfast or as a snack.

  • Saltfish: The fish is soaked overnight in water and then boiled until tender, then served with a variety of ingredients.

  • Stewed goat: Slow cooked with herbs and spices, goat is a local favorite and is often paired with rice and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Guavaberry Liqueur: This sweet local liqueur is made from the berries of the guavaberry tree, which can only be found in the Caribbean. It’s often served neat or mixed with other ingredients.

  • Rum Cocktails: Caribbean rum is the base for some of the best cocktails in the world. Don’t forget to try a Mai Tai or a Rum Punch.

  • Caribbean Beers: Many local breweries offer beers that complement the island’s cuisine. Try beers from the island’s own brewery, or Carib and Heineken that are brewed locally.

Where to Stay in Philipsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Philipsburg offers a wide range of accommodations, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Depending on your preference, you can choose from beachfront views, pool amenities, or off-the-beaten-path lodgings. Among the best options are:

  • Sonesta Great Bay Beach Resort: A luxurious all-inclusive resort with stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.

  • Holland House Beach Hotel: A boutique hotel located on the beach with a colonial-style look.

  • Pasanggrahan Boutique Hotel: A charming boutique hotel housed in a colonial building, located near Front Street.

What to Buy in Philipsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Guavaberry Liqueur: This sweet local specialty makes an excellent gift or souvenir.

  • Handmade Crafts: Many local artisans sell their handmade crafts at markets and street vendors.

  • Jewelry: St. Martin is a hub of jewelry-making, which makes it the perfect place to bring back a little silver or gold.

Local Culture and Customs of Philipsburg

Philipsburg is a multicultural town that embraces its diverse heritage. The islanders are known for their hospitality, and locals are famously welcoming to visitors. The official languages of the island are Dutch and English, but most people also speak Papiamento, a Creole language that reflects the island’s mix of cultures.

Best Time to Visit Philipsburg

The best time to visit Philipsburg is in the winter, between December and April, when the weather is dry and sunny. In the summer months, from June to November, the weather can be rainy and hot. Hurricane season is between August and September, so be sure to check weather reports before traveling.

## Do’s and Don’ts of Philipsburg

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, which is the perfect reflection of the island’s diverse cultures.

  • Do explore the island’s natural beauty, whether by hiking, swimming, or boating.

  • Do take time to enjoy Philipsburg’s nightlife, with its fun mix of local bars and clubs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent, to protect yourself from Caribbean mosquitos and sunburn.

  • Don’t forget to respect the local culture and customs, including dress codes at religious landmarks or while in town centers

  • Don’t forget to keep an eye on your personal belongings while exploring the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Philipsburg

Philipsburg is a safe destination for travelers, although visitors should take normal precautions to safeguard their belongings. Also, make sure to avoid the areas outside Philipsburg after dark, to guarantee your safety.

Health in Philipsburg

Visitors to Philipsburg should avoid drinking tap water, which may have microbes that can cause stomach illness. Visitors to Philipsburg should also protect themselves from mosquito bites, which can cause dengue fever or other illnesses. It is also a good idea to check with your healthcare provider for any vaccinations required before traveling.

Getting to Philipsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Philipsburg

The closest airport to Philipsburg is Princess Juliana International Airport (SXM), which is located on the Dutch side of the island and is served by many airlines from the US, Europe and Canada. The airport is only a short taxi ride from Philipsburg.

Bus Stations Near Philipsburg

Philipsburg has a bus station in the center of town where several local buses and taxis stop. The buses are inexpensive and a great way to explore the surrounding areas in St. Martin.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car, a scooter, or a bike to explore Philipsburg and St. Martin. Bike rentals are available downtown, while car rentals are located near the airport or can be arranged through your hotel.

How to Get Around in Philipsburg

Options include walking, cycling or taking a taxi. Walking or cycling is an easy way to explore the town and its surroundings, whereas taxis are readily available and offer a comfortable way to travel between locations. Visitors can also rent a car or scooter to explore the island freely.

Where to Go Next from Philipsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Maho Beach: This beach is located near the airport and is famous for the low-flying planes that arrive and depart from Princess Juliana International Airport.

  • Marigot: This town is the capital of the French side of the island and offers a fun mix of shopping, dining, and cultural events.

  • Pinel Island: This small island located off the coast of Saint Martin offers snorkeling, swimming, and a secluded beach experience.

Conclusion

Philipsburg is an enchanting town with lots of attractions. Whether you’re into exploring the rich cultural heritage of the island, relaxing on a secluded beach, or enjoying the Caribbean nightlife, Philipsburg has something to offer for everyone. With this guide, you’ll be well-prepared to enjoy your time in this amazing destination.
