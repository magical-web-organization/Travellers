Introduction to Scott’s Head

Located in Dominica in the Lesser Antilles region of the Caribbean, Scott’s Head is a small village on the southern coast of the island. This picturesque destination is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, making it the perfect place to experience the best of Dominica.

List of Places to Visit in Scott’s Head

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Scott’s Head Pinnacles: This is a popular spot for divers and snorkelers who come to see the diverse marine life around the pinnacles.
  • Soufriere Bay: This is a beautiful bay that offers a peaceful setting for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Scott’s Head Village Ruins: This historic site provides a glimpse into the island’s past, with ruins dating back to the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Scott’s Head: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Champagne Reef: This remarkable underwater hot spring is a must-visit destination for divers and snorkelers.
  • Scott’s Head Peninsula: This is a great place for hiking, with amazing views of the bay and the island’s rugged coastline.
  • Salton Waterfall: A hidden gem located in the hills above Scott’s Head, this beautiful waterfall is a great place for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scott’s Head

  • Scott’s Head to Soufriere Trail: This scenic route takes you through the hills above Scott’s Head and offers stunning views of the island’s coastline.
  • Scott’s Head Peninsula Trail: This trail takes you along the coast of the Scott’s Head Peninsula and offers breathtaking views of the bay and the pinnacles.
  • Freshwater Lake Trail: This trail starts from Laudat and takes you to the beautiful Freshwater Lake, offering stunning views of the island’s interior along the way.

Best Things to Do in Scott’s Head: Activities and Tours

  • Diving and Snorkeling: Scott’s Head is well-known for its excellent diving and snorkeling opportunities, with some of the best sites located around the Scott’s Head Pinnacles and Champagne Reef.
  • Whale Watching Tour: Dominica is one of the best destinations in the world for whale watching, and Scott’s Head is a great place to start your tour.
  • Kayaking Tour: Explore the island’s beautiful coastline and hidden bays with a kayaking tour from Scott’s Head.

Food and Drink Scene of Scott’s Head

Must-Try Dishes

  • Callaloo Soup: Made from the leaves of the taro plant, this hearty soup is a local favorite.
  • Fish and Chips: This classic British dish has been given a Caribbean twist with the use of freshly caught fish and locally sourced ingredients.
  • Mango Chutney: This tangy condiment is made from fresh, ripe mangoes and is the perfect accompaniment to any meal.

Local Beverages

  • Kubuli: This is the local beer of Dominica and is a must-try for beer lovers.
  • Sorrel: This refreshing drink is made from hibiscus flowers and is a popular beverage during the Christmas season.
  • Rum Punch: A classic Caribbean cocktail, made with rum, pineapple juice, and grenadine.

Where to Stay in Scott’s Head: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Anchorage Hotel: This waterfront hotel offers stunning views of the bay and a range of amenities, including a restaurant, bar, and pool.
  • The Old Stone House Inn: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Scott’s Head village and offers comfortable rooms and a small restaurant.
  • The Nutmeg Tree Hotel: This family-friendly hotel offers spacious rooms, a pool, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

What to Buy in Scott’s Head: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewellery: Made by local artisans, this jewellery is crafted from a variety of materials, including sea glass and natural stones.
  • Spices and Herbs: Dominica is known for its high-quality spices and herbs, including cinnamon, nutmeg, and bay leaf. These can be found at local markets and spice shops.
  • Crafts and Souvenirs: From woven baskets to hand-carved figurines, there are many souvenirs to choose from in Scott’s Head.

Local Culture and Customs of Scott’s Head

The people of Scott’s Head are warm and friendly, and visitors are always welcome. The local culture is a blend of African, European, and indigenous influences, with music, dance, and food playing an important role in daily life.

Best Time to Visit Scott’s Head

The best time to visit Scott’s Head is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scott’s Head

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Take part in outdoor activities and tours
  • Be friendly with the locals

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Stop in the middle of the road or block traffic

Safety and Health

Safety in Scott’s Head

Scott’s Head is a generally safe place for tourists, but it’s important to take common sense precautions to avoid theft or other crimes. It’s also recommended to stay on marked trails if hiking or exploring the island’s interior.

Health in Scott’s Head

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations, as well as hepatitis A and typhoid. It’s also recommended to take precautions against mosquito bites due to the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as Zika and dengue fever.

Getting to Scott’s Head: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scott’s Head

The nearest airport to Scott’s Head is Douglas-Charles Airport, which is located about an hour’s drive from the village. Taxis and rental cars are available for transportation from the airport to Scott’s Head.

Bus Stations Near Scott’s Head

There are several bus stations on the island, with the main station located in the capital city of Roseau. Buses run frequently between Roseau and Scott’s Head, but it’s recommended to confirm schedules and fares with the bus company before traveling.

Other Options

Rental cars and taxis are widely available on the island, making it easy to explore on your own or with a guided tour. Ferries also run between Dominica and neighboring islands, including Martinique and Guadeloupe.

How to Get Around in Scott’s Head

The best way to explore Scott’s Head is on foot, with many attractions and activities located within walking distance of the village. Taxis and rental cars are also available for transportation to other parts of the island.

Where to Go Next from Scott’s Head: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Roseau: The capital city of Dominica is a great place to explore the island’s history and culture, with many museums, restaurants, and shops to visit.
  • Morne Trois Pitons National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to many of the island’s natural wonders, including waterfalls, hot springs, and hiking trails.
  • Cabrits National Park: Located on the northern coast of the island, this park is home to the historic Fort Shirley and offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.

Conclusion

Scott’s Head is a destination that has something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions to delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an action-packed adventure, this charming village is the perfect place to experience the best of Dominica.
