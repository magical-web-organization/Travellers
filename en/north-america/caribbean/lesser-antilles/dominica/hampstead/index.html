Introduction to Hampstead

Nestled in the lush, green hills of Dominica, Hampstead is a small but vibrant town that’s become a true hidden gem of the Caribbean. Far from the crowds of the more popular destinations, Hampstead boasts world-class outdoor adventures, stunning natural scenery, and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for secluded hikes, fascinating historic sites, or just a taste of authentic Caribbean life, Hampstead has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hampstead

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Amerindian Museum

Located in the heart of Hampstead, the Amerindian Museum offers a fascinating look into the history and culture of the island’s indigenous inhabitants. From ancient artifacts to interactive exhibits, this museum is a must-see for anyone interested in Dominica’s rich cultural heritage.

The Carib Cultural Village

Just a short drive from Hampstead lies the Carib Cultural Village, a living museum where visitors can experience the traditional way of life of Dominica’s original inhabitants. From thatched-roof huts to native dances and crafts, this village is a vibrant and immersive tribute to Carib culture.

Natural Attractions of Hampstead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Boiling Lake

A spectacular natural wonder, the Boiling Lake is a must-see for anyone visiting Hampstead. The trail up to the lake is challenging, but the reward is a breathtaking view of this massive, turquoise pool of boiling water surrounded by dense jungle.

Morne Trois Pitons National Park

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a vast expanse of rainforest, waterfalls, and geothermal activity. Visitors can hike, explore natural hot springs, and take in stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hampstead

The Waitukubuli National Trail

This 115-mile trail is the perfect way to explore the island’s natural beauty. With stunning scenic views and challenging terrain, the trail is divided into 14 segments that can be hiked individually or over the course of several days.

Best Things to Do in Hampstead: Activities and Tours

Whale and Dolphin Watching Tours

Dominica is known as the whale-watching capital of the Caribbean, and Hampstead is the perfect place to book a tour. Visitors can spot a variety of whale and dolphin species in the waters off the coast, as well as other marine life such as sea turtles and manatees.

River Tubing Adventures

For a unique outdoor adventure, visitors can try river tubing in Hampstead’s crystal-clear streams and rivers. From gentle floats to more thrilling rapids, there’s a tubing adventure for every age and skill level.

Food and Drink Scene of Hampstead

Must-Try Dishes

Fish and Fungi

A traditional Dominica dish made with salted fish and cornmeal, fish and fungi is a staple of the island’s cuisine.

Callaloo Soup

A hearty vegetable soup made with taro leaves and often served with dumplings, callaloo soup is a delicious taste of local Caribbean cuisine.

Local Beverages

Sorrel Drink

A refreshing and tangy drink made with hibiscus flowers, sorrel drink is a popular beverage in Dominica and throughout the Caribbean.

Rum Punch

No visit to the Caribbean would be complete without trying a refreshing rum punch, made with the island’s famous rum and a variety of fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Hampstead: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, Hampstead has a range of accommodation options for every traveler. Some popular options include:

Jungle Bay Resort and Spa

This eco-friendly resort offers luxurious bungalows nestled in the lush rainforest, as well as a range of spa treatments and outdoor activities.

Rainforest Shangri-La

A budget-friendly option with friendly staff and comfortable rooms, the Rainforest Shangri-La offers a peaceful and relaxing stay in the heart of Hampstead.

What to Buy in Hampstead: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Crafts

From woven baskets to intricate woodcarvings and pottery, Hampstead is a hub of local crafts and artisans.

Spices and Herbs

Dominica is known for its flavorful spices and herbs, such as cinnamon, nutmeg, and bay leaves, which can be found at local markets throughout Hampstead.

Local Culture and Customs of Hampstead

With a rich history and a vibrant cultural scene, Hampstead offers visitors a unique look into Caribbean island life. From the indigenous Carib culture to the island’s colonial past, visitors can explore Dominica’s diverse history and customs.

Best Time to Visit Hampstead

The best time to visit Hampstead is from December to April, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors can also find good deals and fewer crowds during the rainy season from May to November.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hampstead

Hampstead is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should take basic precautions such as avoiding isolated areas after dark and securing valuables while out and about.

Health in Hampstead

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Dominica, and should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites due to the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Hampstead: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hampstead

The closest airport to Hampstead is Dominica’s Douglas-Charles Airport, located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Hampstead

Public buses are available throughout Dominica, but visitors may prefer to use a private shuttle service or rent a car for more flexibility.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Dominica by ferry from nearby islands such as Martinique or Guadeloupe.

How to Get Around in Hampstead

While public transportation is available, visitors may prefer to rent a car for more flexibility. Taxis and private shuttles are also available for getting around the island.

Where to Go Next from Hampstead: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roseau

The capital city of Dominica, Roseau is home to a variety of historic landmarks, cultural attractions, and vibrant street markets.

Scotts Head

This small fishing village at the southern tip of Dominica is a popular destination for snorkeling and diving, thanks to its crystal-clear waters and diverse marine life.

Conclusion

Hampstead is a vibrant and welcoming destination that offers visitors a unique look into Caribbean island life. From its lush rainforests and stunning natural wonders to its rich cultural heritage and delicious local cuisine, Hampstead is the perfect destination for anyone looking to discover the true beauty of the Caribbean.
