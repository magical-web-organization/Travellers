Introduction to Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is a charming coastal town located in the southern part of the Caribbean island of Martinique. The town is known for its stunning white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and lively atmosphere. The town is also a hub for cultural activities, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Sainte-Anne

Here is a list of the best places to visit in Sainte-Anne:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ecomusee de Martinique: A living museum that showcases the traditional life and culture of Martinique.
  • Chateau Dubuc: A historic fortress that served as one of the island’s most important sugarcane plantations in the 18th century.
  • La Savane des Esclaves: A slave museum that offers a unique perspective on the history of slavery in Martinique.

Natural Attractions of Sainte-Anne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Plage des Salines: One of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean, with crystal-clear waters, soft white sand, and palm trees swaying in the breeze.
  • Pointe Marin: A popular destination for scuba diving, snorkeling, and other water sports.
  • Jardin de Bonneville: A lovely botanical garden that showcases the island’s flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is home to several hiking and biking routes that offer spectacular views of the island’s lush rainforest and coastline. Some of the best routes include:

  • Trace des Caps: A challenging 22-km trail that takes you through the island’s rugged terrain and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Montagne Pelee: A challenging hike to the summit of the island’s highest peak, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • La Caravelle: A scenic coastal trail that takes you through a wildlife reserve, offering stunning views of the ocean and the nearby islands.

Best Things to Do in Sainte-Anne: Activities and Tours

  • Catamaran Cruise: Take a catamaran cruise around the island, enjoying the stunning coastline, crystal-clear water, and the colorful marine life.
  • Kite Surfing: The steady winds and crystal-clear waters of Sainte-Anne make it an ideal destination for kite surfing enthusiasts.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the island’s rugged terrain on horseback, taking in the breathtaking views of the lush rainforest and the turquoise sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sainte-Anne

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Sainte-Anne:

  • Day 1: Relax at Plage des Salines
  • Day 2: Explore the historic Chateau Dubuc and the La Savane des Esclaves Museum
  • Day 3: Hike the Montagne Pelee trail
  • Day 4: Take a catamaran cruise around the island
  • Day 5: Explore the Jardin de Bonneville and Pointe Marin
  • Day 6: Try kite surfing or horseback riding
  • Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Sainte-Anne

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Sainte-Anne:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Accras: Fried dough balls made with salt cod, herbs, and spices.
  • Boudin: A sausage made with pig’s blood, onions, and spices.
  • Colombo: A flavorful curry that combines meat, fish, or vegetables with a blend of spices, including turmeric, coriander, and cumin.

Local Beverages

  • Ti Punch: The national drink of Martinique, made with local white rum, lime juice, and sugar syrup.
  • Planteur: A refreshing fruit punch that combines local fruit juices with rum.
  • Coco Loco: A cocktail made with fresh coconut water and rum.

Where to Stay in Sainte-Anne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sainte-Anne offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and tastes. Whether you’re looking for a luxury resort or a cozy guesthouse, you’ll find plenty of options.

Luxury Hotels

  • Club Med La Caravelle: A luxury all-inclusive resort that offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.
  • La Suite Villa: A boutique hotel that offers spacious suites, an infinity pool, and breathtaking views of the Bay of Fort de France.
  • Le Cap Est Lagoon Resort & Spa: A secluded resort that features a private beach, a spa, and spacious villas with private pools.

Mid-Range Hotels

  • Hotel Cap Macabou: A welcoming hotel on the outskirts of Saint-Anne, offering comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • La Pagerie Hotel: A stylish hotel with a relaxed atmosphere, located in the town’s center.

Budget Options

  • Residence Robinson: A cozy guesthouse that offers functional rooms, an outdoor pool, and free wifi.
  • Hotel Corail Residence: An affordable hotel that offers functional rooms and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Sainte-Anne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some great souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Rhum Agricole: A local rum made from pressed sugar cane, aged in oak barrels and has a unique aroma and taste.
  • Spices: The island is known for its unique blend of spices, especially curry, which can make a great addition to any pantry.
  • Wood Carvings: The island has a rich tradition of woodcarving, and you can find beautifully crafted pieces in the local markets and artisan shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is a melting pot of cultures, and its customs and traditions reflect the island’s rich history. The locals are passionate about their food, music, and dance. Islanders also enjoy frequent festivals and events year-round.

Best Time to Visit Sainte-Anne

The best time to visit Sainte-Anne is from December to May, when the weather is sunny and dry. The rainy season is from June to November, and temperatures can be a bit higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sainte-Anne

Do:

  • Explore the island’s natural beauty and cultural treasures.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the freshly caught seafood.
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat when exploring outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Forget to take cash, as some restaurants and stores may not accept credit cards.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sainte-Anne

Sainte-Anne is generally a safe destination, but as with any new place, it’s important to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Be cautious of pickpockets and theft at crowded areas or on dark streets.

Health in Sainte-Anne

Make sure to update your routine vaccinations and take precaution against common mosquito-borne diseases, like dengue fever

Getting to Sainte-Anne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sainte-Anne

The Martinique Aime Cesaire International Airport is the primary gateway to Sainte-Anne, and the hub for several airlines.

Bus Stations Near Sainte-Anne

There are no direct bus services to Sainte-Anne from the airport, but visitors can use the Martinique public bus system to get there from Fort-de-France or other destinations around the island.

Other Options

Visitors can rent cars, taxis, or use shared shuttle services to get around the island and get to Sainte-Anne.

How to Get Around in Sainte-Anne

The best way to get around Sainte-Anne is by renting a car or scooter. Visitors can also take taxis or use public transport: Line A or B.

Where to Go Next from Sainte-Anne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several amazing destinations close to Sainte-Anne that offer visitors unique and exciting experiences. Head to Trois-Ilets to enjoy the town’s rich culture, history, and delicious cuisine. If you’re looking for stunning natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere, visit the lush rainforests and volcanic peaks of Mount Pelee National Park.

Conclusion

Sainte-Anne is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a mix of sun, surf, stunning scenery, outdoor adventures, and cultural activities. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to this beautiful town in Martinique with ease. Whatever you do in Sainte-Anne, you’re sure to create lasting memories.
