Introduction to Martinique

Martinique is a beautiful French island located in the Lesser Antilles of the Caribbean. Known for its stunning natural beauty, incredible cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, Martinique is a must-visit travel destination for anyone looking to experience the best of the Caribbean.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore all that Martinique has to offer, from its top natural attractions and cultural landmarks, to its best hiking and biking routes, most delicious dishes, and much more. Read on to start planning your dream trip to Martinique today.

List of Places to Visit in Martinique

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, Martinique has something to offer everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Martinique:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Savane des Esclaves - A living museum that explores the history of slavery in Martinique
  • Habitation Céron - An 18th-century plantation home that has been converted into a museum showcasing the history of sugar production on the island
  • Balata Garden - A stunning botanical garden filled with tropical plants and flowers

Natural Attractions of Martinique: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Salines Beach - A beautiful white-sand beach with crystal-clear waters, popular for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports
  • Mount Pelée - An active volcano that offers breathtaking hiking trails and stunning views of the island from the summit
  • Diamond Rock - A large rocky islet that’s perfect for snorkeling and diving, where you can explore the colorful underwater world of Martinique

Hiking/Biking Routes of Martinique

Martinique offers incredible hiking and biking trails that showcase the island’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Trace des Caps - A scenic trip that will take you through the northern part of the island.
  • La Caravelle Peninsula - A popular 2-hour hike that offers incredible views of the sea and the coastline.
  • Le Morne Vert - A challenging hike that takes you through lush rainforests and up to the summit of Mount Pelée, where you’ll be rewarded with stunning panoramic views of the island.

Best Things to Do in Martinique: Activities and Tours

Martinique offers a range of exciting activities and tours that will make your trip unforgettable. Here are some of the best things to do in Martinique:

  • Take a Catamaran Tour of the Island - Enjoy the stunning views of the Caribbean Sea with a guided catamaran tour of the island.
  • Visit a Rum Distillery - Martinique is famous for its rum, and there are several distilleries on the island that offer tours and tastings.
  • Go Kayaking in the Mangroves - Explore Martinique’s natural beauty up close with a guided kayaking tour through its mangrove forests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Martinique

To help you plan your trip to Martinique, here’s a week-long itinerary that takes you through some of the island’s top attractions:

Food and Drink Scene of Martinique

Martinique has a unique cuisine that blends French and Caribbean flavors and offers a range of delicious dishes and drinks that reflect the island’s rich history and culture.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Accras de Morue - Fried codfish fritters that are a popular street food in Martinique
  • Colombo de poulet - A spicy curry made with chicken, vegetables, and a blend of aromatic spices
  • Grilled Lobster - A seafood lover’s delight, Martinique offers some of the freshest grilled lobster in the Caribbean.

Local Beverages

  • Ti’ Punch - A popular rum-based cocktail that’s made with lime juice and cane sugar
  • Planteur - A fruity rum punch that’s perfect for sipping on hot days
  • Coconut Water - Fresh coconut water is widely available and makes for a refreshing and healthy drink option.

Where to Stay in Martinique: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Martinique has an array of options for lodging, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best options for where to stay in Martinique:

  • La Suite Villa - A 5-star hotel that offers stunning views of the Caribbean Sea and easy access to some of the island’s top attractions.
  • French Coco - A boutique hotel that offers a unique blend of modern design and traditional Caribbean charm.
  • Mangerie Guesthouse - A cozy, affordable option that’s located in the heart of Fort-de-France.

What to Buy in Martinique: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Martinique, make sure to bring home some of these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Madras Fabric - A colorful and bold pattern fabric that’s often used in traditional Caribbean clothing.
  • Rum - Martinique is famous for its rum, so make sure to bring home a few bottles to enjoy later.
  • Spices - Martinique boasts a range of local spices like cinnamon, vanilla, and nutmeg that make great gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Martinique

Martinique has a rich cultural heritage that’s influenced by both French and Caribbean traditions. One notable aspect of Martinique’s culture is their Carnival celebrations, which occur in February or March and feature colorful parades, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Martinique

The best time to visit Martinique is between December and May when the weather is sunny and dry. However, visiting during July and August can be a more affordable and quieter time to travel, despite the possibility of humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Martinique

Do:

  • Learn some basic French phrases - While many locals speak English, French is the official language, and it can be helpful to know some basic phrases.
  • Embrace the local culture - Martinique’s culture is unique and vibrant, so make sure to embrace it and try new things.
  • Stay hydrated - The Caribbean heat can be intense, so make sure to stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended - As with any destination, it’s important to be vigilant of your belongings and make sure to keep them safe.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing - While Martinique is a laidback destination, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Forget your sunscreen - The Caribbean sun is strong, so make sure to wear sunscreen to avoid sunburns.

Safety and Health

Martinique is a generally safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to take precautions like drinking bottled water and wearing bug spray to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Chikungunya.

Safety in Martinique

  • Be aware of your surroundings - Like with any destination, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and avoid unlit or isolated areas at night.
  • Stay on marked trails - When hiking, it’s important to stay on marked trails and avoid wandering off the beaten path to avoid getting lost.
  • Follow safety procedures - If you’re participating in activities like kayaking or diving, make sure to follow all safety procedures and regulations.

Health in Martinique

  • Drink Bottled Water - When in Martinique, it’s recommended to stick to bottled water to avoid any waterborne illnesses.
  • Protect Against Mosquitoes - Mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Chikungunya are common in Martinique. Be sure to wear bug spray, especially during dawn and dusk hours.
  • Seek Medical Attention if Needed - In case of any medical emergency, the island has a great healthcare system.

Getting to Martinique: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Martinique

  • Martinique Aimé Césaire International Airport (FDF) - The main international airport that handles direct and connecting flights from the US and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Martinique

  • Fort-de-France bus station - From here, you can take buses to various parts of the island.

Other Options

  • Ferry - There are frequent ferry services from Fort-de-France to regional islands like Dominica and Guadeloupe.

How to Get Around in Martinique

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is the most popular way to get around Martinique, and it’s easy to do so at any of the airports or major cities.
  • Public Transportation - The island has a comprehensive bus service that’s affordable and convenient for getting around to destinations.
  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available in major cities, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Martinique: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Caribbean, Martinique is a great jumping-off point for several nearby destinations, including:

  • Guadeloupe - A French overseas territory that boasts incredible beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Dominica - A nature lover’s paradise known for its lush rainforests and stunning waterfalls.
  • St. Lucia - A beautiful island that offers a range of activities from hiking and water sports to relaxing on the beach.

Conclusion

Martinique is a true Caribbean gem that offers something for every type of traveler. From its stunning natural attractions and fascinating cultural landmarks to its incredible cuisine and exciting outdoor activities, Martinique is a destination that you don’t want to miss. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your dream trip to Martinique. Bon voyage!
