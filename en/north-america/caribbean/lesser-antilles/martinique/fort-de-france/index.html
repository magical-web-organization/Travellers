Introduction to Fort-de-France

Fort-de-France, the capital city of Martinique in the Caribbean, is a vibrant blend of French and Caribbean culture. It is a fascinating city with historical and natural sights scattered around and a buzzing food and drink scene.

Visitors to Fort-de-France can explore the city’s ancient forts, experience Martinique’s diverse culture, and enjoy outdoor adventures. The city is a convenient base for exploring other parts of Martinique, where you will find stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and picturesque villages.

In this travel guide to Fort-de-France, we will provide a comprehensive list of things to do, places to see, local food and drinks, where to stay, and how to travel around this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Fort-de-France

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the highlights of Fort-de-France is its rich cultural heritage. The Musee Departemental d’Archeologie et de Prehistoire showcases the island’s ancient heritage with artifacts from pre-Columbian Amerindian civilization. The Musee de la Pagerie, a museum located in Empress Josephine’s childhood home, presents a glimpse into the life of perhaps the most famous Martinican, who went on to marry Napoleon Bonaparte and become Empress of France.

Fort Saint-Louis, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an essential landmark in the city’s history. Visitors can explore the well-preserved fort and its iconic tower, with views of the Caribbean sea and the city. The Schoelcher Library, designed by Gustave Eiffel, is a beautiful neoclassical building that houses over 125,000 books and 250 manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Fort-de-France: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy the serene surroundings of Fort-de-France. Visit La Savane, a spacious park in the heart of the city with lush gardens, walking paths, and statues. The Jardin de Balata is a beautiful botanical garden with over 3,000 species of tropical plants, including rare and exotic varieties.

To continue an outdoor adventure, take a hike to Mount Pelee, which towers over Fort-de-France and is the highest peak in Martinique. The Cascade Didier is a stunning waterfall located in a National Park within walking distance of the city. Visitors can also enjoy a dip or sunbathe on the nearby beaches of Anse Mitan or Pointe Marin.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort-de-France

The city and surrounding areas are suitable for all levels of exploration, whether you prefer a leisurely stroll or a challenging trek. The trail to Mount Pelee is around 4.7 kilometers and takes about two and a half hours to complete. On the other hand, the Didier Nature Trail is a 3.5-kilometer loop with a waterfall and river views.

Biking is the best way to explore Fort-de-France, and many bike tours will take you to the city’s best attractions, including its markets, food stalls, and bookstores.

Best Things to Do in Fort-de-France: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for adrenaline-filled activities, visitors can try their hand at kite-surfing or windsurfing at Anse Madame beach. Embrace Martinique’s vibrant culture with a tour around the city’s markets, which sell everything from fresh fruit and vegetables to handicrafts and souvenirs.

For a taste of luxury, spend a day at the Spa La Suite Villa. The spa offers a range of treatments, including massages, facials, and body treatments, where visitors can relax in a luxurious setting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort-de-France

Day 1: Explore the historic sites of the city - the Schoelcher Library, Fort Saint-Louis, and the Musee de la Pagerie.

Day 2: Visit the botanical garden Jardin de Balata and explore the lively marketplaces of the city.

Day 3: Take a hike or bike around the National Park of Mount Pelee, Martinique’s highest peak.

Day 4: Experience a day of kitesurfing or windsurfing at Anse Madame Beach.

Day 5: Tour around the city to try out the food and drink scene.

Day 6: Enjoy a day at the luxurious Spa La Suite Villa.

Day 7: Spend a day at one of the nearby beaches, like Anse Mitan or Pointe Marin.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort-de-France

Must-Try Dishes

Fort-de-France offers a diverse culinary scene that blends French and Caribbean flavors. Visitors can try out the local dish of Accras - a mix of salted codfish, flour, and seasoning, formed into small balls and deep-fried. Another staple is the Bokit, a fried bread sandwich filled with meat, cheese, and vegetables.

Gourmet lovers can indulge in the city’s high-end dining options and enjoy Creole-influenced cuisine with a French twist.

Local Beverages

Martinique’s local drink is the famous rum agricole. Visitors can visit the award-winning Distillerie Neisson to sample some of the country’s finest rums and learn about its production.

The city also has a variety of fresh fruit juices available, such as tamarind and guava, and traditional Caribbean drinks like Planter’s Punch and Ti Punch.

Where to Stay in Fort-de-France: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort-de-France offers a range of accommodations that cater to all kinds of travelers. Visitors can stay in luxury hotels like the Hotel La Bateliere or the Hotel Simon, which offers stunning views of the Caribbean sea and is located in the city center.

Budget travelers can opt to stay in hostels and guesthouses such as Hotel L’Imperatrice, located in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Fort-de-France: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Fort-de-France can pick up a range of souvenirs and local goods, such as rum, hot pepper sauce, and traditional carnival masks. There are also many local artists, designers, and makers who sell unique and authentic creations, such as pottery, jewelry, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort-de-France

The city is deeply influenced by French and Martinican culture, with traditional values embedded in art, music, dance, and food. Visitors can participate in the many festivals and celebrations throughout the year, such as Carnival and the Fete de la Mer.

Best Time to Visit Fort-de-France

The best time to visit Fort-de-France is during the dry season, from December to April. However, be prepared for the city’s high season to be busier and more expensive than other times of the year. From June to November, visitors can experience a more relaxed atmosphere with fewer crowds and some great deals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort-de-France

Do:

  • Do try out the local dishes and drinks
  • Do wear comfortable walking shoes and clothing
  • Do plan ahead for outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended in public places
  • Don’t walk around alone at night in unfamiliar areas
  • Don’t speak loudly or aggressively in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort-de-France

Fort-de-France is a safe city to visit. However, as with any major city, visitors should be cautious with their belongings, particularly in crowded areas. Visitors should avoid walking alone at night, especially in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Fort-de-France

There is no need for any specific vaccinations or health treatments to visit Fort-de-France. Any emergencies or health issues that arise while in Fort-de-France can be treated in the city’s hospitals or clinics.

Getting to Fort-de-France: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort-de-France

Visitors to Fort-de-France can fly into Aime Cesaire International Airport, which serves both international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Fort-de-France

Buses, locally known as ‘Bus de ville,’ operate frequently throughout the city, traveling to the surrounding towns and beaches. There is also a well-established intercity bus network that will take visitors to other parts of the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also explore Fort-de-France by bike, with many bike rental options available. Taxis are abundant in the city, and there are also car rental companies operating across Fort-de-France.

How to Get Around in Fort-de-France

Visitors can get around Fort-de-France on foot, by bike, or by taxi. The city’s roads are well-maintained, and it is easy to navigate around the city center, including the cobbled streets.

Where to Go Next from Fort-de-France: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many places and activities to choose from to continue your trip beyond Fort-de-France. Visitors can take a day trip to the historic town of Saint Pierre or explore the island’s beaches and coastlines by ferry or boat tour. There are also options for hiking, boating, and exploring the cave networks of nearby Trou de Madame.

Conclusion

Fort-de-France, Martinique, is a beautiful and dynamic city with something to offer everyone. Whether you are interested in history, art, nature, or food, Fort-de-France is sure to delight. With this comprehensive travel guide, plan your trip to Fort-de-France, and experience this lively cultural hub for yourself.
