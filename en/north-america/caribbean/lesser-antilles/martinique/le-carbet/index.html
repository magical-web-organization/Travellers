Introduction to Le Carbet

Le Carbet is a quaint town located in the northwest coast of Martinique, Lesser Antilles, in the Caribbean. This quiet fishing village is known for its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and outdoor adventures. The town is surrounded by lush greenery, picturesque beaches, and panoramic views of the sea and mountains.

Travelers who visit Le Carbet appreciate its laid-back and serene atmosphere, which is an ideal getaway for those who seek solitude and peacefulness. The town’s rich cultural heritage and colorful festivals are also an attraction for visitors who want to immerse themselves in the local traditions and customs.

This comprehensive tour guide for Le Carbet provides useful information about the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Le Carbet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Château Dubuc

One of the town’s most famous landmarks is the Château Dubuc. This 17th-century castle was built by the Dubuc family, who were slave owners and sugar cane producers. The castle serves as a museum that exhibits the history of the Dubuc family, slavery, and the sugar industry in Martinique.

St. James Distillery

St. James Distillery is another popular attraction in Le Carbet, known for its premium rum. Visitors can tour the distillery, learn about the rum-making process, and taste a selection of their finest rums.

Natural Attractions of Le Carbet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Pelée

Mount Pelée is an active volcano located about 30 minutes away from Le Carbet. The volcano erupted in 1902 and destroyed the town of Saint-Pierre, leaving only one survivor. Visitors can hike to the volcano, enjoy panoramic views of the island, and learn about the history and geology of the area.

Plage du Coin

Plage du Coin is a gorgeous beach that is popular among tourists and locals. The beach offers crystal-clear waters, white sand, and a magnificent view of the Caribbean Sea. It is an ideal location for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Le Carbet

Le Carbet offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the town and its surroundings. Some of the popular routes are:

Trace des Jésuites

This trail stretches over 6 km and leads up to the top of Morne Céron, providing breathtaking views of the sea and the town of Carbet.

Le Canal des Esclaves

Le Canal des Esclaves is a trail that follows the remains of an ancient aqueduct built by slaves to transport water from the Carbet River to the sugar plantations.

Best Things to Do in Le Carbet: Activities and Tours

Le Carbet Beach

Le Carbet Beach is a popular spot for beach lovers. The beach offers swimming, sunbathing, and relaxation. It also has restaurants and cafes where visitors can experience local cuisines and cocktails.

Scuba Diving

Le Carbet has excellent scuba diving locations that offer crystal clear waters and a wide range of marine life. Visitors can choose from several diving companies that offer guided tours and equipment rental.

Week-Long Itinerary for Le Carbet

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at hotel

Day 2: Visit Château Dubuc and explore the town

Day 3: Trek to Mount Pelée and visit St. Pierre

Day 4: Go scuba diving or snorkeling

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby village of Case-Pilote

Day 6: Visit St. James Distillery and taste their finest rums

Day 7: Relax on Le Carbet Beach and enjoy the local cuisine and cocktails.

Food and Drink Scene of Le Carbet

Must-Try Dishes

One of the must-try dishes in Le Carbet is Accras, a local fried delicacy made with saltfish, flour, and spices. Another popular dish is Colombo, which is a spicy curry made with meat or fish, and served with rice.

Local Beverages

Rum is undoubtedly the most popular beverage in Le Carbet. St. James Distillery produces some of the finest rums in the Caribbean. Visitors can also taste exotic fruit juices and cocktails made from local fruits such as guava, passion fruit, and mango.

Where to Stay in Le Carbet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Le Carbet offers a variety of accommodations that cater to different types of travelers. Some of the most popular options are:

Habitation Céron

Habitation Céron is a luxurious hotel that offers spacious rooms, excellent service, and a breathtaking view of the sea and the mountains.

Le Poirier du Roy

Le Poirier du Roy is a charming guest house that offers comfortable rooms, delicious breakfast, and easy access to the beach.

Les Rouleaux

Les Rouleaux is a budget-friendly hostel that offers dorm rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Le Carbet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy a variety of souvenirs and local products in Le Carbet, including:

  • Rum
  • Handmade pottery
  • Jams and preserves made with local fruits
  • Vanilla and other spices

Local Culture and Customs of Le Carbet

Le Carbet has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its food, art, and festivals. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and being respectful of the local residents.

Best Time to Visit Le Carbet

The best time to visit Le Carbet is between December and April when the weather is dry, and temperatures are mild. This period is also the peak tourist season, and visitors are advised to book accommodation and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Le Carbet

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some French, the official language of Martinique.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Le Carbet

Le Carbet is generally safe for visitors. However, tourists should be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Le Carbet

Visitors should drink bottled water and be up to date on their vaccinations. It is also a good idea to bring mosquito repellent since mosquitoes can carry diseases such as Zika and Dengue fever.

Getting to Le Carbet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Le Carbet is easily accessible by plane and ferry.

Airports Near Le Carbet

The nearest major airport is Martinique Aimé Césaire International Airport, located in Fort-de-France, which is about a 45-minute drive from Le Carbet.

Bus Stations Near Le Carbet

Visitors can take a local bus from Fort-de-France to Le Carbet. Buses run regularly, and the trip takes about an hour.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Le Carbet.

How to Get Around in Le Carbet

Le Carbet is a small town, and most places are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Le Carbet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint-Pierre

Saint-Pierre is a nearby town that was once the cultural and economic center of Martinique. The town has a rich history and culture and is known for its theater and art scene.

Le Prêcheur

Le Prêcheur is a small fishing village located about 20 minutes away from Le Carbet. It offers beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and panoramic views of the sea and mountains.

Conclusion

Le Carbet is a hidden gem in Martinique, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. This comprehensive tour guide unveils the best things to do, places to visit, and cultural experiences to try in this charming town. Visitors will enjoy the picturesque beaches, scenic views, and local cuisine, making for an unforgettable trip to Le Carbet, Martinique.
