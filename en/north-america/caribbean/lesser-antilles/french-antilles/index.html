Introduction to French Antilles

French Antilles is a group of islands located in the Lesser Antilles in the Caribbean region of North America. Primarily composed of two major island groups: the Guadeloupe Islands and the Martinique Islands, French Antilles is famous for its striking natural beauty, diverse culture, and rich history. The warm and welcoming people of French Antilles, combined with the picturesque landscapes, clear blue waters, and vibrant food scene, make the island an idyllic holiday destination.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best things to do in French Antilles, places to stay, the best time to visit, and where to go next for a longer trip throughout the region. So, get ready to discover the vibrant, colorful world of French Antilles.

Local Culture and Customs of French Antilles

The culture of French Antilles is as diverse as its ancestry. Caribbean, African, and French influences coexist to create a unique fusion. The people of French Antilles are proud of their heritage, which is celebrated through music, art, and cultural festivals.

Best Time to Visit French Antilles

The peak season to visit French Antilles is from December to May when the weather is warm and dry. You’ll find loads of travelers during this time, with prices high. Low season lasts from June until November. Still, given the tropical climate, you may face stormy weather, non-stop rains, and the possibility of a hurricane.

Do’s and Don’ts of French Antilles

Getting to French Antilles: Transportation Options and Logistics

To get to French Antilles, you can travel via air or sea. The region’s international airports are located in the two major islands’ cities, Guadeloupe Pointe-à-Pitre on Grande-Terre and Fort-de-France on Martinique. Additionally, inter-island ferries connect the Guadeloupe Islands and the Martinique Islands.

Airports Near French Antilles

  • Pointe-à-Pitre International Airport
  • Fort-de-France International Airport

Bus Stations Near French Antilles

  • Basse-Terre bus station
  • Pointe-à-Pitre bus station
  • Fort-de-France bus station

How to Get Around in French Antilles

To move around the islands, you can take buses, taxis, car rentals, and bicycles. Be sure to have proper documentation like driving license, insurance, and license plates.
