Introduction to Mexican Riviera

Mexican Riviera is situated on the west coast of Mexico, and it is a popular tourist destination due to its beautiful beaches, warm climate, vibrant culture, and rich history. Mexican Riviera comprises of several states, including Nayarit, Jalisco, Colima, and Guerrero, each with its own unique charm and attractions. If you’re planning to visit the Mexican Riviera, this comprehensive guide is perfect for you. Here, we’ll cover everything you need to know - from the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Mexican Riviera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history lover, you’ll be delighted to know that Mexican Riviera is home to several culturally important attractions. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in the region include:

  • The Museo de las Culturas Afromexicanas in Guerrero: This museum showcases the contributions and history of Afro-Mexicans, who are an essential part of the region’s culture.
  • The Cathedral of Our Lady of the Rosary in Nayarit: This beautiful cathedral is a masterpiece of Baroque architecture and is worth a visit.
  • The Regional Museum of Nayarit in Nayarit: This museum displays pre-Hispanic artifacts and colonial-era art, giving visitors insight into the region’s rich history.
  • The Archaeological Zone of Ixtapa in Jalisco: This site, which dates back to 500 BC, gives visitors a glimpse into the region’s pre-Hispanic past.

Natural Attractions of Mexican Riviera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mexican Riviera is also known for its natural attractions, which provide ample opportunity for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the most popular natural attractions of the region include:

  • Las Marietas Islands in Nayarit: These islands, located in Banderas Bay, are a protected nature reserve and are home to several species of birds and marine life. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling, scuba diving, and kayaking here.
  • Los Arcos National Marine Park in Jalisco: This park is home to several species of marine life, including giant manta rays and sea turtles. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling and scuba diving in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Colima Volcano National Park in Colima: This national park is home to the Colima Volcano, which is an active volcano that erupts periodically. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and scenic drives here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mexican Riviera

Mexican Riviera offers several hiking and biking routes that provide breathtaking views of the region’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Sierra Madre Occidental Trail in Nayarit: This trail takes visitors through the Sierra Madre Occidental mountains, providing stunning views of the region’s flora and fauna.
  • The El Salto Trail in Jalisco: This trail takes visitors through a lush jungle to a beautiful waterfall.
  • The Nevado de Colima Trail in Colima: This trail takes visitors through the Colima Volcano National Park, providing stunning views of the Colima Volcano.

Best Things to Do in Mexican Riviera: Activities and Tours

Mexican Riviera offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Some of the best things to do in the region include:

  • Whale watching in Guerrero: Every year, thousands of humpback whales migrate to the region’s warm waters to breed and give birth. Visitors can take a whale-watching tour to get up close and personal with these gentle giants.
  • Tequila tasting in Jalisco: Jalisco is the birthplace of tequila, the famous Mexican liquor. Visitors can take a tequila tasting tour and learn about the process of tequila making.
  • Surfing in Nayarit: Mexican Riviera is home to several world-class surf spots, including Sayulita and San Pancho. Visitors can take surfing lessons or rent a board and hit the waves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mexican Riviera

If you’re planning to spend a week in Mexican Riviera, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the old town of Puerto Vallarta, visit the Cathedral of Our Lady of Guadalupe, and enjoy a sunset cruise.

Day 2: Take a tequila tasting tour in Jalisco, visit the town of Tequila, and stop by the Agave Landscape and Ancient Industrial Facilities of Tequila, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Day 3: Take a hiking tour to the El Salto waterfall in Jalisco and enjoy a traditional Mexican lunch in the nearby town.

Day 4: Visit the Museo de las Culturas Afromexicanas in Guerrero, explore the old town of Zihuatanejo, and enjoy a seafood dinner on the beach.

Day 5: Take a whale-watching tour in Guerrero and enjoy lunch on a secluded beach.

Day 6: Visit the Las Marietas Islands in Nayarit and enjoy snorkeling, kayaking, and swimming in the crystal-clear waters.

Day 7: Relax on the beach, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy a farewell dinner in one of the region’s top restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Mexican Riviera

Mexican Riviera is renowned for its delicious cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos al pastor: This dish, which originated in Jalisco, consists of pork marinated in adobo sauce and cooked on a rotisserie. The pork is then served on a tortilla and topped with onion, pineapple, and cilantro.
  • Pozole: This hearty stew, which originated in Guerrero, consists of hominy, pork, and chili peppers. It is traditionally served with avocado, lime, and radish.
  • Ceviche: This dish, which is popular in Nayarit, consists of raw seafood marinated in lime juice and mixed with cilantro, onion, and tomato.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: This famous Mexican liquor is made from the blue agave plant and is popular worldwide.
  • Raicilla: This lesser-known liquor is similar to tequila but is made from a different type of agave plant.
  • Michelada: This refreshing beer cocktail, which originated in Jalisco, consists of beer, lime juice, and spices.

Where to Stay in Mexican Riviera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mexican Riviera offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • Four Seasons Resort Punta Mita in Nayarit: This luxurious resort offers stunning ocean views, world-class dining, and an array of activities, including golf, surfing, and kayaking.
  • Villa Ganz Boutique Hotel in Jalisco: This charming boutique hotel is located in Guadalajara and offers comfortable rooms, a tranquil courtyard, and easy access to the city’s top attractions.
  • Casa Kaukan in Guerrero: This eco-friendly guesthouse is located in Zihuatanejo and offers stunning ocean views, a tranquil setting, and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Mexican Riviera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mexican Riviera offers plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that make the perfect mementos. Some top picks include:

  • Tequila: Bring home a bottle (or two) of the region’s famous liquor.
  • Talavera pottery: This colorful pottery, which is made in nearby Puebla, is a popular souvenir.
  • Huichol art: This intricate bead and yarn art is created by the Huichol people, who reside in the region’s mountains.

Local Culture and Customs of Mexican Riviera

Mexican Riviera has a rich culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some key things to keep in mind:

  • Mexicans are warm and welcoming people who take pride in their culture and heritage.
  • Spanish is the language spoken in the region, although some locals may speak English.
  • Mexicans are family-oriented, and family gatherings are an essential part of their culture.

Best Time to Visit Mexican Riviera

The best time to visit Mexican Riviera is from November to May, when the weather is warm and dry. June to October is the rainy season, which can make outdoor activities challenging. However, the rainy season also brings lush vegetation and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mexican Riviera

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other houses of worship.
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Wear expensive jewelry or carry large amounts of cash.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mexican Riviera

Mexican Riviera is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid traveling alone after dark.
  • Keep a close eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Use only authorized taxi services.

Health in Mexican Riviera

Mexican Riviera is generally safe in terms of health, but travelers should take precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid ice made from tap water.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Practice safe sex.

Getting to Mexican Riviera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mexican Riviera is well-connected to the rest of Mexico and the world. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Mexican Riviera

  • Licenciado Gustavo Díaz Ordaz International Airport (PVR) in Puerto Vallarta, Jalisco
  • Don Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla International Airport (GDL) in Guadalajara, Jalisco
  • Ixtapa-Zihuatanejo International Airport (ZIH) in Guerrero
  • Playa de Oro International Airport (ZLO) in Manzanillo, Colima

Bus Stations Near Mexican Riviera

  • Puerto Vallarta Bus Station in Jalisco
  • Guadalajara Bus Station in Jalisco
  • Zihuatanejo Bus Station in Guerrero
  • Colima Bus Station in Colima

Other Options

  • Renting a car: This is a great option for exploring the region at your own pace.
  • Taking a private shuttle: This is a convenient option for traveling between cities.

How to Get Around in Mexican Riviera

Here are some transportation options for getting around Mexican Riviera:

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in most cities and towns.
  • Buses: Buses are a cheap and convenient option for traveling between cities.
  • Rental cars: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the region at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Mexican Riviera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mexican Riviera is located in an excellent location, making it easy to explore nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Mexico City: The capital of Mexico is located just a few hours’ drive from the Mexican Riviera and is home to several must-see attractions, including the Zócalo, the National Museum of Anthropology, and the Frida Kahlo Museum.
  • The Copper Canyon: Located in the state of Chihuahua, the Copper Canyon is a stunning natural wonder that is larger and deeper than the Grand Canyon.
  • San Blas: This charming colonial town, located in Nayarit, is known for its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and excellent birdwatching opportunities.

Conclusion

Mexican Riviera is an excellent tourist destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in culture, history, nature, adventure, or relaxation, Mexican Riviera has it all. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip. Enjoy your visit to this beautiful region!
