Introduction to Puebla (state)

Puebla is a beautiful state located in central Mexico. Known as the “Land of Enchantment,” Puebla is famous for its colonial architecture, rich history, and delicious cuisine. The state’s capital, Puebla City, is home to a UNESCO World Heritage site, the Historic Centre of Puebla, which boasts a stunning collection of well-preserved colonial buildings and cathedrals. The state is also well-known for its beautiful landscapes and natural attractions, including stunning volcanoes and picturesque valleys.

Whether you are a history buff, foodie, outdoor enthusiast, or simply seeking a new adventure, Puebla has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, local culture, and more in Puebla.

List of Places to Visit in Puebla (state)

If you are planning a trip to Puebla, make sure to include these must-visit destinations in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Centre of Puebla: A beautiful UNESCO World Heritage site with stunning colonial architecture, including the Cathedral of Puebla and the Chapel of the Rosary.
  • Museo Amparo: A world-class museum that features a stunning collection of pre-Hispanic, colonial, and modern art.
  • Fort Loreto: A historic military fortress located in the city of Puebla, with a beautiful view of the city from the top.
  • Great Pyramid of Cholula: An archaeological site in the city of Cholula that includes the largest pyramid in the world by base area.
  • Casa de los Munecos: A beautifully restored 18th-century mansion located in the city of Puebla, which features a stunning collection of antiques and art.

Natural Attractions of Puebla (state): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Popocatepetl and Iztaccihuatl National Park: A beautiful national park showcasing Mexico’s two tallest volcanoes, breath-taking views, hiking trails, and camping sites.
  • Atlixco Botanical Garden: A beautiful botanical garden that features over 2,500 species of plants from all over the world, including a butterfly house.
  • Cuetzalan: A charming town located in the Sierra Norte Mountains, popular for its scenic views, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Africam Safari: A perfect place for a family day out, with an interactive drive-through zoo with animals from all over the world.
  • Las Estrellas Caves: A stunning cave system located in Xicotepec, featuring unique rock formations and underground waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puebla (state)

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Puebla has several incredible hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best:

  • Izta-Popo National Park: With its well-marked trails and spectacular views, this park is perfect for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Malintzi Volcano National Park: A beautiful national park which offers several hiking trails and is perfect for a day trip.
  • La Malinche: A popular hiking and camping site, the peak offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • La Sierra Norte: A mountain range located in the northern part of Puebla with stunning trails for both hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Puebla (state): Activities and Tours

Puebla offers a wide range of activities and tours, catering to every type of traveler. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • Cooking classes: Learn how to cook traditional Puebla dishes, such as mole poblano, pipian, and chiles en nogada.
  • Street food tours: Sample some of the best street food in Puebla, such as tacos arabes, cemitas, and tamales.
  • Hot air balloon rides: Take a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque valleys of Puebla.
  • City sightseeing tours: Explore the stunning colonial architecture of Puebla’s cities, including Puebla City and Cholula.
  • Mezcal and tequila tastings: Discover Mexico’s famous agave spirits, such as mezcal and tequila, with a tasting tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puebla (state)

If you have a week to spare, here’s the perfect itinerary to make the most of your trip to Puebla:

  • Day 1: Explore the city of Puebla and its historical centre.
  • Day 2: Visit Cholula and tour the Great Pyramid.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Cuetzalan and hike the scenic landscapes.
  • Day 4: Visit the Atlixco Botanical Garden and see the butterfly house.
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque valleys of Puebla.
  • Day 6: Visit Popocatepetl and Iztaccihuatl National Park and hike its hills.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at Africam Safari and visit the interactive drive-through zoo.

Food and Drink Scene of Puebla (state)

Puebla is known for its rich food and drink culture. Here are some must-try dishes, local beverages, and food markets in Puebla:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mole Poblano: Puebla’s most famous dish, typically served with chicken and a side of rice.
  • Chiles en Nogada: A seasonal, traditional dish made with poblano peppers stuffed with meat, fruits, and nuts, and covered with a walnut cream sauce.
  • Tacos Arabes: A delicious, Middle Eastern-inspired dish featuring marinated pork, sliced and served in a pita-like flatbread.
  • Cemitas: A type of sandwich made with sesame-seed-covered bread, cheese, meat, and avocado.
  • Tamales: A traditional, steamed dish made from masa (dough made from corn flour), filled with meat, cheese, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: A popular agave spirit, often compared to tequila but with a stronger, smokier flavor.
  • Tejate: A pre-Hispanic beverage made with cacao and corn, typically served at local markets.
  • Pulque: A fermented drink made from the maguey plant, often with different flavors added.
  • Mole Dulce: A traditional hot drink made with mole, Mexican chocolate, and spices.

Where to Stay in Puebla (state): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puebla offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s budget and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Luxury hotels: La Purificadora, Rosewood Puebla, and Nueve Nueve Hotel.
  • Boutique hotels: Meson Sacristia de la Compania, Hotel Cartesiano, and Hotel Concept.
  • Hostels: Hostel 1915, Hostal Santo Domingo, and Casa Nomada.
  • Guesthouses: Casona Maria, La Casa del Mendrugo, and Casona de la China Poblana.

What to Buy in Puebla (state): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Puebla is famous for its beautiful arts and crafts, including Talavera pottery, traditional textiles, and woodcarvings. Here are some great places to buy souvenirs and local products:

  • El Parian Market: A 16th-century market located in Puebla City, famous for its Talavera pottery and traditional crafts.
  • Street vendors: Look for local street vendors selling everything from traditional textiles to hand-carved wooden figures.
  • Art galleries: Puebla is home to several world-class art galleries, including the Amparo Museum, where visitors can purchase art from well-known artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Puebla (state)

Puebla’s culture and customs are deeply influenced by its rich history and traditions. Here are some interesting aspects of Puebla’s culture and customs:

  • Day of the Dead: A Mexican holiday celebrating the dead, which takes place on November 1st and 2nd. In Puebla, the holiday is celebrated with colorful altars and offerings.
  • Talavera Pottery: Puebla is famous for its beautiful handmade pottery, known as Talavera, which is traditionally blue and white.
  • Fiestas Patrias: Puebla celebrates Mexico’s Independence Day on September 16th with parades, fireworks, and street festivals.
  • Mariachi Bands: Mariachi music is an important part of Mexico’s culture and traditions, and Puebla is no exception.

Best Time to Visit Puebla (state)

The best time to visit Puebla is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. The weather during this time is mild, and there is less chance of rain, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puebla (state)

Do:

  • Try the local food, especially mole poblano, chiles en nogada, and tacos arabes.
  • Learn a few words in Spanish, as English may not be widely spoken in some areas.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque valleys of Puebla.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as the Day of the Dead celebrations and mariachi music.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.
  • Wear flashy jewelry or carry large sums of money in public places.
  • Photograph or touch religious artifacts without permission.

Safety and Health

Puebla is a safe destination for travel, but it is always advisable to take necessary precautions, especially in crowded tourist areas. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Puebla (state)

  • Use only licensed taxis or ride-sharing services, such as Uber.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas, especially in big cities.
  • Be vigilant and watch your belongings in crowded places, such as markets and tourist attractions.
  • Keep a photocopy of your passport and other important documents in a safe place.

Health in Puebla (state)

  • Avoid eating street food from unlicensed vendors.
  • Drink only bottled water and avoid ice in your drinks.
  • Pack medications and a first-aid kit, including anti-diarrhea medication.

Getting to Puebla (state): Transportation Options and Logistics

Puebla is well-connected to the rest of Mexico, with several transportation options.

Airports Near Puebla (state)

  • Puebla International Airport (PBC): Located 30 minutes away from Puebla City, with both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Puebla (state)

  • Puebla Bus Station: Located in Puebla City, with regular bus services to Mexico City, Veracruz, and other Mexican cities.

Other Options

  • Private transfers: Private transfers are a convenient option for transportation between cities or for exploring nearby attractions.
  • Car rental: Hiring a car is a great option if you want to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Puebla (state)

The best way to explore Puebla is by bus or taxi. There are also several companies that offer guided tours to the region’s top attractions.

Where to Go Next from Puebla (state): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Puebla is located in a central location in Mexico, making it easy to explore nearby cities and attractions. Here are some great places to visit near Puebla:

  • Oaxaca City: A charming colonial city known for its colorful architecture and rich culture, located 250 km from Puebla.
  • Mexico City: The bustling capital of Mexico is located just 120 km from Puebla and is home to some of the country’s most famous landmarks, including the National Palace and the Plaza de la Constitucion.
  • Chignahuapan: A picturesque town known for its hot springs, located 100 km from Puebla.
  • Acapulco: A beautiful coastal destination located 400 km from Puebla, known for its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Puebla (state) is a charming and culturally rich destination in Mexico, with something for every traveler. Whether you are interested in history, food, nature, or outdoor activities, Puebla has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan the perfect trip to Puebla, and make the most of your time exploring all that this beautiful state has to offer.
