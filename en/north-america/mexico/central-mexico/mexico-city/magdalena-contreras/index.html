Introduction to Magdalena Contreras

Magdalena Contreras is one of the 16 boroughs forming Mexico City. Located on the south-western edge of the city, this borough is home to a diverse range of attractions and activities that make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Mexico City. Magdalena Contreras offers scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions and more. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have listed some of the top places to visit in Magdalena Contreras, along with tips on where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to make the most of your trip to this beautiful borough.

List of Places to Visit in Magdalena Contreras

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Magdalena Contreras is home to several cultural attractions that will give you a glimpse into its rich history and heritage.

1. Casa del Risco

Built in the 16th century, Casa del Risco is a historical building that now serves as a museum. Located in the heart of Magdalena Contreras, this museum houses a collection of contemporary Mexican art and the works of Mexican artist, Rufino Tamayo.

2. El Centinela

El Centinela is a lookout tower located on Cerro del Estrella, offering panoramic views of Mexico City. Built in the 18th century, this tower has been beautifully restored and is now a cultural center where you can attend concerts, exhibitions, and other events.

3. Ex Convento del Carmen

Located in the neighborhood of San Jerónimo, Ex Convento del Carmen is a former 17th-century Carmelite monastery that now serves as a cultural center and museum. The museum houses a collection of religious art and artifacts from the colonial era, and also hosts temporary exhibitions and cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Magdalena Contreras: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Magdalena Contreras is surrounded by natural beauty, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

1. Ecological Park Los Dinamos

Located in the heart of Magdalena Contreras, Ecological Park Los Dinamos offers beautiful hiking trails that wind through forests and mountain ranges. You can also enjoy horseback riding, rock climbing, and mountain biking, or simply take in the scenic views of the city from one of the park’s lookout points.

2. Desierto de los Leones National Park

Desierto de los Leones National Park is a protected natural area that covers over 1,500 hectares of forest, meadows, and canyons. The park offers hiking trails, camping sites, and picnic areas, and is home to a wide range of wildlife, including deer, coyotes, and eagles.

3. Glass Museum

Located in the neighborhood of San Jerónimo, the Glass Museum is dedicated to the art and history of glassmaking. The museum houses a collection of glass pieces made by local artisans, including vases, bowls, and sculptures. You can also attend glassblowing workshops and other events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Magdalena Contreras

Magdalena Contreras offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Mexico City, with different difficulty levels catering to all kinds of outdoor enthusiasts.

1. Ajusco Mountain

Ajusco Mountain is the highest peak in the Valley of Mexico, and offers a challenging hike that rewards you with panoramic views of the city. The ascent takes around four to six hours, and the trail starts at the entrance of Ajusco National Park.

2. Presa Madin Dam

This is a relatively easy hike that offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape. The trailhead starts at the entrance to Desierto de los Leones National Park and leads to Presa Madin Dam, a man-made lake surrounded by lush forests and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Magdalena Contreras: Activities and Tours

1. Street Art Tour

Magdalena Contreras is home to some of the best street art in Mexico City, with murals and graffiti adorning buildings and walls throughout the borough. Take a walking tour to discover the hidden gems of the neighborhood and learn about the local street art scene.

2. Horseback Riding

Explore the scenic trails of Ecological Park Los Dinamos on horseback, accompanied by experienced guides who will take you through forests, streams, and mountain ranges. This is a great way to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Magdalena Contreras.

Week-Long Itinerary for Magdalena Contreras

With so much to see and do in Magdalena Contreras, a week-long itinerary will allow you to explore the borough at a leisurely pace.

Day 1: Explore the cultural attractions of Magdalena Contreras, including Casa del Risco and El Centinela.

Day 2: Hike to Presa Madin Dam and enjoy a picnic in the surrounding forests.

Day 3: Take a street art tour of the neighborhood and discover the hidden gems of the local art scene.

Day 4: Explore the natural wonders of Ecological Park Los Dinamos, from forests to canyons and mountain ranges.

Day 5: Visit the Glass Museum and attend a glassblowing workshop or other event.

Day 6: Enjoy a horseback riding tour of Ecological Park Los Dinamos.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Cuernavaca, the “City of Eternal Spring,” and explore its colonial architecture and beautiful gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Magdalena Contreras

Must-Try Dishes

Magdalena Contreras offers a variety of local dishes that are not to be missed, including:

1. Tlayudas

Tlayudas are large, crispy tortillas topped with beans, cheese, and meat, and are a popular street food in Mexico City. Try the tlayudas at El Tizoncito in Magdalena Contreras for an authentic taste of local cuisine.

2. Tostadas

Tostadas are crispy tortillas topped with beans, vegetables, and meat, and are a staple of Mexican cuisine. Try the tostadas at El Fogoncito for fresh, authentic flavors.

Local Beverages

Mexico is known for its tequila, mezcal, and beer, and Magdalena Contreras offers plenty of options for trying these local beverages.

1. Tequila

Tequila is a distilled spirit made from the blue agave plant, and is the national drink of Mexico. Head to any bar or restaurant in Magdalena Contreras to try a tequila shot or a margarita.

2. Mezcal

Mezcal is another type of distilled spirit made from the agave plant, but is made in a different way than tequila. Try the mezcal at La Botica in Magdalena Contreras for a taste of this unique Mexican beverage.

Where to Stay in Magdalena Contreras: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Magdalena Contreras offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

1. Hotel Boutique Villa Condesa

Located in the neighborhood of Condesa, Hotel Boutique Villa Condesa is a beautiful boutique hotel with stylish rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a garden courtyard.

2. Hostel Condesa Chapultepec

Hostel Condesa Chapultepec is a budget-friendly option located in the heart of the Condesa neighborhood. The hostel offers dorms and private rooms, a shared kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with city views.

What to Buy in Magdalena Contreras: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Handmade Glassware

Magdalena Contreras is home to several glassmaking artisans who create beautiful handmade glassware. Visit the Glass Museum to buy unique souvenirs to bring home.

2. Handcrafted Jewelry

Magdalena Contreras is also known for its artisanal jewelry, which is made using traditional techniques and incorporates local materials such as stones and metals.

Local Culture and Customs of Magdalena Contreras

Magdalena Contreras has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its traditional crafts, music, and dance.

1. Traditional Crafts

Magdalena Contreras is home to several artisans who create beautiful handmade crafts using traditional techniques. Look out for local markets and shops selling handmade textiles, ceramics, and jewelry.

2. Music and Dance

Magdalena Contreras has a lively music and dance scene, with traditional dances and festivals celebrated throughout the year. The most popular festival is the Day of the Dead, which is celebrated in November and features colorful parades and performances.

Best Time to Visit Magdalena Contreras

The best time to visit Magdalena Contreras is between December and March, when the weather is dry and mild. However, if you’re interested in seeing traditional festivals and celebrations, plan your trip around the Day of the Dead in November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Magdalena Contreras

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks, including tlayudas, tostadas, tequila, and mezcal.
  • Take a street art tour to discover the hidden gems of the local art scene.
  • Explore the natural wonders of Ecological Park Los Dinamos.
  • Attend a glassblowing workshop or other event at the Glass Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water - stick to bottled water to avoid stomach upset.
  • Don’t leave valuable belongings in plain sight, especially in crowded areas.
  • Don’t buy drugs from street vendors - it is illegal and unsafe.

Safety and Health

Safety in Magdalena Contreras

Magdalena Contreras is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, it is recommended to take normal safety precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuable belongings out of sight.

Health in Magdalena Contreras

The tap water in Magdalena Contreras is not safe to drink, so make sure to stick to bottled water. It is also recommended to get up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to Mexico.

Getting to Magdalena Contreras: Transportation Options and Logistics

The closest airport to Magdalena Contreras is Mexico City International Airport, which is located approximately 30 kilometers from the borough. From there, you can take a taxi or public transportation to reach your destination.

Bus Stations Near Magdalena Contreras

The main bus station in Mexico City is the Central de Autobuses del Norte, which offers connections to various destinations across the city and the country. From there, you can take a taxi or public transportation to reach Magdalena Contreras.

Other Options

Magdalena Contreras is also easily accessible by car or by bike if you’re up for a challenge.

How to Get Around in Magdalena Contreras

Magdalena Contreras is is a walkable borough, but if you want to cover more ground, you can also take public transportation, such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Magdalena Contreras: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Cuernavaca

Located approximately 85 kilometers from Magdalena Contreras, Cuernavaca is known as the “City of Eternal Spring,” and is famous for its colonial architecture and beautiful gardens.

2. Tepoztlán

Tepoztlán is a charming town located approximately 70 kilometers from Magdalena Contreras. The town is famous for its traditional markets, archaeological sites, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Magdalena Contreras is a beautiful and diverse borough that offers something for everyone, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures and scenic views. With its rich history and heritage, friendly locals, and vibrant culture, Magdalena Contreras is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Mexico City.
