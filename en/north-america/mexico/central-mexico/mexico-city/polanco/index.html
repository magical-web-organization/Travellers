Introduction to Polanco

Polanco is an upscale neighborhood located in Mexico City, the bustling capital of Mexico. It is known for its luxurious shopping, fine dining, and beautiful architecture. Polanco offers a unique blend of modern cosmopolitanism and rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best things to do, see, and eat in Polanco.

List of Places to Visit in Polanco

Polanco has numerous attractions for travelers to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Polanco:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Soumaya: This museum is known for its unique architecture and a vast collection of artworks, including works by renowned artists such as Salvador Dali and Diego Rivera.
  • Chapultepec Castle: A historical landmark that has witnessed some of the most crucial events in Mexico’s history, Chapultepec Castle is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  • Anthropology Museum: This museum boasts an impressive collection of pre-Columbian artifacts representing the diverse indigenous cultures of Mexico.

Natural Attractions of Polanco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bosque de Chapultepec: This vast park is a great place for a leisurely walk, jog, or picnic. It also hosts several museums and attractions.
  • Parque Lincoln: A small park located in the heart of Polanco where you can relax and admire the greenery and sculptures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Polanco

Polanco offers many hiking and biking routes that you can enjoy alone or with a guide. Some popular routes include:

  • Reforma Avenue: This is one of the most famous streets in Mexico City. It is a great place to go for an early morning bike ride or a late evening stroll.
  • Lomas de Chapultepec: This residential area offers some of the best hiking trails in Polanco.

Best Things to Do in Polanco: Activities and Tours

Polanco offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you occupied during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Food tour: Polanco is known for its culinary scene, and a food tour is an excellent way to experience the local cuisine.
  • Art tour: Polanco has many art galleries and museums, and an art tour is a great way to appreciate the local art scene in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Polanco

Polanco is renowned for its upscale restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos de Canasta: These small, soft tacos are filled with a variety of meat and vegetable fillings and are a popular street food in Mexico City.
  • Mole: A traditional Mexican sauce made with over 20 ingredients is served with chicken, pork, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Margarita: Made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec, the Margarita is a popular cocktail in Mexico and beyond.
  • Horchata: A sweet rice drink flavored with cinnamon and vanilla, perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Polanco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Polanco offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Las Alcobas: This luxury hotel offers spacious and comfortable rooms with modern amenities and an excellent spa.
  • Casa Anzures: A beautiful guest house with a garden, terrace and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Polanco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Polanco has many souvenir shops where you can buy unique gifts and local products. Some popular items include:

  • Handcrafted jewelry made with precious stones and metals from Taxco.
  • Talavera pottery, a famous Mexican folk art.

Local Culture and Customs of Polanco

Mexican culture is deeply rooted in its indigenous heritage. The people of Polanco are proud of their cultural identity, and visitors are encouraged to learn and participate in cultural events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Polanco

The best time to visit Polanco is between March and May, when the weather is mild. The city can be hot and humid during the summer months, and it can get chilly in the winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Polanco

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases. Most locals speak Spanish.
  • Explore the city on foot or bike. It’s the best way to experience the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Display valuables such as jewelry or electronics in public places.
  • Drink tap water. Stick to bottled water or filtered water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Polanco

Polanco is generally a safe neighborhood. However, as with any tourist destination, you should take precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid walking alone at night and be cautious of pickpockets and petty thefts.

Health in Polanco

It’s advisable to get travel insurance before traveling to Polanco. The tap water is not safe to drink, and you should strictly stick to bottled water or filtered water.

Getting to Polanco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Polanco

Mexico City International Airport is the primary airport serving Polanco and is located approximately 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Polanco

Terminal Central de Autobuses del Norte is the main bus station in Mexico City and is about 30 minutes from Polanco by car.

Other Options

Taxis, buses, and the metro are widely available in the city and are relatively affordable and convenient.

How to Get Around in Polanco

The best way to get around Polanco is by walking or cycling. Taxis and Ubers are available, but traffic can be heavy, and the public transportation system can be daunting for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Polanco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Polanco is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Mexico. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to take:

  • Teotihuacan: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located 50 km from Mexico City. It is known for its well-preserved ancient Mesoamerican pyramids.
  • Xochimilco: An area of impressive man-made canals that were once critical to Mexico City’s network of waterways, located 25 km south of Polanco.

Conclusion

Polanco is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience Mexico City’s metropolitan hustle and bustle while enjoying its rich cultural heritage. With this ultimate Polanco travel guide, we hope you have all the necessary information to plan your trip to this beautiful neighborhood.
