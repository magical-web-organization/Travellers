Introduction to Santa Fe

Santa Fe is a neighborhood located in the west of Mexico City, in the state of Mexico. It is considered to be a more upscale and modern part of the city, with a thriving business district and a wide variety of things to do. It is known for its contemporary architecture, luxurious shopping malls, and high-end restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Fe

Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, Santa Fe has plenty of attractions to keep you busy. Here are some of the must-visit places in Santa Fe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Objeto del Objeto - This unique museum features a collection of everyday objects from the past century, providing a fascinating glimpse into the lives of Mexican people over the years.
  • Museo Tamayo Arte Contemporáneo - For modern art lovers, this museum is a must-visit. It features an impressive collection of contemporary art from both Mexican and international artists.
  • Bosque de Chapultepec - Located just a short walk from Santa Fe, this park is home to several museums and attractions, including the Anthropology Museum and the Rufino Tamayo Museum of Contemporary Art.

Natural Attractions of Santa Fe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque La Mexicana - This park is a popular spot for running, cycling, and enjoying a stroll. It features several paths, lakes, and gardens, making it a true oasis in the middle of the city.
  • Desierto de los Leones National Park - Just a short drive from Santa Fe, this national park offers beautiful hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Toluca Volcano National Park - A bit further from Santa Fe but still worth a visit, this park offers the opportunity to hike up to the top of a volcano and take in stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Fe

Santa Fe has several great routes for hiking and biking, including:

  • Las Lomas Loop - This 7-mile trail is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking. It offers beautiful views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Parque La Mexicana Loop - This 2-mile loop is perfect for a quick run or bike ride. It features beautiful gardens and a lake.

Best Things to Do in Santa Fe: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a luxury shopping mall - Santa Fe is known for its high-end shopping malls, like Centro Santa Fe and Arcos Bosques. Take a stroll through these upscale malls and window shop, or treat yourself to a luxurious shopping experience.
  • Take a cooking class - Mexican cuisine is known around the world for its bold flavors and unique ingredients. Take a cooking class in Santa Fe and learn how to make some of the most popular Mexican dishes.
  • Go on a city tour - See the highlights of Santa Fe with a guided tour, which can include stops at museums, parks, and other popular attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Fe

If you have a week to spend in Santa Fe, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Museo del Objeto del Objeto and Parque La Mexicana

5 Hike the Las Lomas Loop and enjoy a dinner at a high-end restaurant

7 Relax and enjoy your last day in Santa Fe

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Fe

Mexican cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique ingredients, and Santa Fe’s food scene offers the opportunity to try some of the best Mexican dishes. Here’s a look at some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Santa Fe:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos al Pastor - These tacos feature marinated pork cooked on a spit, topped with pineapple and served on a corn tortilla. They’re a popular street food in Mexico City, and Santa Fe has several great places to try them.
  • Chiles en Nogada - This dish is a traditional Mexican dish made with poblano chiles stuffed with meat, fruits, and spices, and topped with a creamy sauce made with walnuts.
  • Tortas - These Mexican sandwiches are served on a soft bread roll and filled with a variety of meats and toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila - No trip to Mexico would be complete without trying some tequila, which is made from the blue agave plant. Santa Fe has several bars and restaurants where you can sample different types of tequila.
  • Micheladas - This popular drink is made with beer, lime juice, hot sauce, and spices, and is often served in a salt-rimmed glass. It’s a great way to cool off on a hot day in Santa Fe.

Where to Stay in Santa Fe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Fe has plenty of options when it comes to accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top choices:

  • Live Aqua Urban Resort Mexico - This luxury resort offers beautiful views of the city and a rooftop infinity pool.
  • NH Santa Fe Hotel - This modern hotel features a restaurant, a fitness center, and a shuttle service to the local area.
  • Mexiqui Zocalo Hotel - Located in the heart of Santa Fe, this budget-friendly hotel features a rooftop terrace and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Santa Fe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home from Santa Fe, here are some great options:

  • Artisan crafts - Mexico is known for its unique artisan crafts, like pottery, textiles, and jewelry. Visit a local market or shop to find one-of-a-kind handmade items.
  • Tequila - Bring home a bottle of tequila as a unique souvenir that represents the culture of Mexico.
  • Mexican chocolate - Mexico is known for its delicious chocolate, and Santa Fe has several great chocolatiers where you can buy locally made chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Fe

Mexico has a rich culture, and Santa Fe is no exception. Here are some things to be aware of when visiting Santa Fe:

  • Respect for elders - In Mexican culture, it’s important to show respect for elders. Refer to older people as “señor” or “señora” out of respect.
  • Using “usted” vs. “tu” - In Mexico, there are two forms of “you” that are used depending on the situation. If you’re speaking formally to someone, use “usted.” If you’re speaking informally, use “tu.”
  • Religious customs - Mexico is a predominantly Catholic country, and there are many religious customs and traditions that are important to the culture. Be respectful of these customs when visiting churches or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Santa Fe

The best time to visit Santa Fe is between March and May, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The rainy season starts in June and lasts until October, so you may want to avoid visiting during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Fe

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Santa Fe:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Be open to new experiences and challenges.

Don’t:

  • Carry large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Leave your belongings unattended.
  • Drink tap water, stick to bottled water.
  • Smoke in public spaces.

Safety and Health

When traveling to any foreign country, it’s important to be aware of any safety and health concerns.

Safety in Santa Fe

Santa Fe is a relatively safe neighborhood, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation.
  • Only use authorized taxis and avoid unmarked vehicles.

Health in Santa Fe

Mexico City has higher levels of air pollution than many other cities around the world, so it’s important to take precautions if you have respiratory issues. In addition, keep in mind:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Wear sunscreen to protect against the sun’s rays.
  • Eat well-cooked food and avoid street food if you have a sensitive stomach.

Getting to Santa Fe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Santa Fe is located around 16 kilometers from the center of Mexico City. Here are some transportation options for getting to Santa Fe:

  • Airports Near Santa Fe

  • Mexico City International Airport is the main airport serving the area. From here, you can take a taxi or public transportation to Santa Fe.

  • Bus Stations Near Santa Fe

  • Central del Norte Bus Station serves routes from all over Mexico. From here, you can take a taxi or public transportation to Santa Fe.

  • Other Options

  • Uber and other ride-hailing services are available in Mexico City, and can be a convenient and affordable option for getting around.

How to Get Around in Santa Fe

Here are some transportation options for getting around Santa Fe:

  • Public transportation - Santa Fe has a bus rapid transit system that connects the area to other parts of the city.
  • Taxi - Taxis are widely available in Santa Fe, but make sure to only use authorized taxis to avoid scams.
  • Walk or bike - Santa Fe is a relatively compact area, which makes it easy to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Santa Fe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Mexico after visiting Santa Fe, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Toluca - This city is located around an hour from Santa Fe, and is home to the Cosmovitral Botanical Garden, which features impressive stained-glass murals.
  • Puebla - This charming colonial city is located around two hours from Santa Fe, and is known for its delicious food and colorful buildings.
  • Teotihuacan - This ancient archaeological site is located around an hour from Santa Fe, and features impressive pyramids and ruins from the pre-Columbian era.

Conclusion

Santa Fe is a unique and modern neighborhood in the heart of Mexico City. With its luxurious shopping malls, beautiful parks, and delicious food, it’s no wonder why it’s become a popular destination for visitors from around the world. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, Santa Fe has something for everyone.
