Introduction to San Angel

San Angel is one of the most charming neighborhoods in Mexico City, located in the southern part of the city. It’s a unique place that beautifully mixes old colonial architecture with modern art galleries, museums, and parks. San Angel is famous for its cobblestone streets, colorful houses, and tranquil atmosphere.

This guide will introduce you to San Angel’s top attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and travel tips so that you can plan a perfect trip to this beautiful neighborhood.

List of Places to Visit in San Angel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Casa Estudio Diego Rivera y Frida Kahlo: Explore the house where two of Mexico’s most famous artists lived and worked. The museum showcases their artwork, personal belongings, and the stunning gardens they designed.
  • Museo Casa del Risco: This 18th-century colonial mansion houses a museum that displays a broad range of artefacts from Mexico’s pre-Columbian, colonial, and independence periods.
  • Bazar del Sabado: The Saturday Bazaar is a well-known craft market located in San Jacinto Square. Local artists sell handmade handicrafts, clothing, jewelry, and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of San Angel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Bosque de Tlalpan: This nature reserve is one of the largest green areas in Mexico City. It offers many different trails, picnic areas, and natural scenery.
  • Cerro del Elefante: This hilltop offers incredible views of the Mexico City skyline and is a popular spot for hiking and picnics.
  • Plaza San Jacinto: A beautiful square with a central fountain and surrounded by historic buildings. A popular spot for people-watching and enjoying the laid-back atmosphere of San Angel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Angel

San Angel is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. There are several trails and routes to choose from, including:

  • Viveros de Coyoacan: These nurseries are home to many different trees, flowers, and plants. The surrounding area is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride.
  • Ciudad Universitaria: Take a bike ride through Mexico City’s vast university campus, where you’ll find beautiful gardens, several cultural centers, and more.
  • Parque Ecologico Loreto y Peña Pobre: This park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, as well as rock climbing, bird watching, and more.

Best Things to Do in San Angel: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional Mexican dishes like tamales, tacos, and mole.
  • Walking Tour: A walking tour of San Angel is a great way to explore the neighborhood and learn about its history and culture.
  • Art Galleries: San Angel is home to several art galleries that showcase both contemporary and traditional Mexican art.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Angel

Day 1: Explore San Angel’s historic house museums, including Museo Casa Estudio Diego Rivera y Frida Kahlo and Museo Casa del Risco.

Day 2: Take a bike ride through Ciudad Universitaria and visit the cultural centers and botanical gardens.

Day 3: Visit the Saturday Bazaar at Plaza San Jacinto and shop for locally-made handicrafts and souvenirs.

Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional Mexican dishes.

Day 5: Go hiking in El Bosque de Tlalpan and enjoy a picnic in nature.

Day 6: Take a walking tour of San Angel’s art galleries and admire both contemporary and traditional Mexican art.

Day 7: Relax in one of San Angel’s many parks and enjoy the neighborhood’s laid-back atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of San Angel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chilaquiles: A traditional Mexican breakfast dish made with corn tortillas, eggs, cheese, and salsa.
  • Tamales: Steamed corn dough filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Mole: A savory sauce made with chocolate and spices. It’s typically served with chicken or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Agua de Jamaica: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers.
  • Pulque: A fermented beverage made from the sap of the maguey plant.
  • Tequila: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the blue agave plant. It’s typically served with lime and salt.

Where to Stay in San Angel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Casa Jacinta Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of San Angel. It offers comfortable rooms, a garden, and a terrace.
  • Villa Condesa: A boutique hotel with luxurious rooms and suites. It’s located in the lively Condesa neighborhood, just a short drive from San Angel.
  • Hostel Home: A budget-friendly hostel with shared dorms and private rooms. It’s located in Coyoacan, a nearby neighborhood known for its bohemian vibe.

What to Buy in San Angel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: San Angel is famous for its handmade pottery, which is sold at many of the local craft markets.
  • Mexican textiles: You can find beautiful textiles, including blankets, rugs, and clothing, at many of the local markets.
  • Mexican chocolate: Take home some authentic Mexican chocolate, which is made with cocoa, cinnamon, and other spices.

Local Culture and Customs of San Angel

San Angel is known for its rich culture and vibrant arts scene. The neighborhood is home to many artists, musicians, and writers and is a haven for creatives. Mexicans are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors, but it’s always a good idea to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit San Angel

The best time to visit San Angel is from November to April when the weather is mild and dry. The summer months can be hot and muggy, and the rainy season lasts from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Angel

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drinks.
  • Do visit the local museums and art galleries.
  • Do take a bike ride or walk around the neighborhood.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the historic buildings.
  • Don’t over-tip at restaurants, as it’s not expected in Mexico.
  • Don’t forget to carry cash, as some local businesses may not accept credit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Angel

San Angel is a relatively safe neighborhood to visit, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in San Angel

Mexico City has a high altitude, which can cause altitude sickness for some people. Be sure to drink plenty of water and take it easy for the first few days of your trip. Also, be cautious of street food and always wash your hands before eating.

Getting to San Angel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Angel

  • Mexico City International Airport: The largest airport in Mexico City and the primary international gateway.

Bus Stations Near San Angel

  • Terminal Central de Autobuses del Norte: The bus station offers connections to many different parts of Mexico.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Mexico City, but be sure to insist on using only officially licensed cabs or use ride-sharing services like Uber.

How to Get Around in San Angel

San Angel is a small neighborhood that’s easy to explore on foot or by bike. Alternatively, you can use the local bus system, which is both convenient and affordable.

Where to Go Next from San Angel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Coyoacan: A nearby neighborhood known for its colonial architecture, bohemian vibe, and the Frida Kahlo Museum.
  • Xochimilco: A neighborhood with beautiful canals and colorful boats called trajineras. It’s a perfect place for a relaxing boat ride or a picnic on the water.
  • Teotihuacan: An ancient Mesoamerican city renowned for its impressive pyramids. It’s located just a short drive north of Mexico City.

Conclusion

San Angel is a hidden gem that should be on any traveler’s itinerary when visiting Mexico City. Whether you’re interested in history, art, culture, or outdoor adventures, San Angel has something for everyone. Plan your trip well, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful neighborhood.
