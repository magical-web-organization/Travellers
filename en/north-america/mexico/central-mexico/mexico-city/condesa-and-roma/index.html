Introduction to Condesa and Roma

Condesa and Roma are two of the trendiest neighborhoods in Mexico City, located in the heart of the city. These two adjacent neighborhoods form an area that is famous for its picturesque streets, vibrant cultural scene, and beautiful parks.

The Condesa and Roma neighborhoods boast a unique blend of pre-Revolutionary architecture, modern art, and a vibrant nightlife. These neighborhoods are home to artists, urban professionals, and students, all co-existing in perfect harmony.

If you are planning a trip to Mexico City, Condesa and Roma should be at the top of your list of places to explore. Here is a guide to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Condesa and Roma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Frida Kahlo Museum (Casa Azul) - Explore the life and works of the famous Mexican artist, Frida Kahlo, at her former home turned museum.
  • Museum of Modern Art - This museum features an impressive collection of contemporary Mexican art and hosts rotating exhibitions.
  • Templo Mayor - Visit the ruins of an Aztec temple, located in the heart of the city, and learn about the rich history of Mexico City.

Natural Attractions of Condesa and Roma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bosque de Chapultepec – This urban park is one of the largest in the world, and home to several attractions, including several museums and a zoo.
  • Bike Tour of Condesa and Roma - Explore the neighborhood by bike and take in the beautiful architecture and street art.
  • Mercado de Medellín - This market offers a wide range of fresh produce, traditional food stalls, and local handicrafts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Condesa and Roma

Biking routes:

  1. Start at Alvaro Obregon Avenue and head east towards the city centre.
  2. Cruise down Amsterdam Avenue, one of the most picturesque streets in Condesa and Roma.
  3. Stop at Glorieta de la Cibeles, a roundabout with a fountain.
  4. Take a break at Parque Mexico and enjoy the greenery.
  5. Head towards Parque España and admire the Art Deco architecture in the area.
  6. End your bike ride at Mercado Roma, a foodie’s paradise.

Best Things to Do in Condesa and Roma: Activities and Tours

  • Mexican Food Tour – The food scene in Condesa and Roma is diverse and delicious. Sample traditional foods and drinks with a local guide.
  • Pub Crawl – The nightlife in Condesa and Roma is unbeatable, and a pub crawl is a great way to experience it.
  • Walking Tour – Take an architecture or street art tour and learn about the history and culture of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Condesa and Roma

Day 1: Arrive and settle in your accommodation in Condesa or Roma Day 2: Explore the museums in the area, such as the Frida Kahlo Museum and the Museum of Modern Art. Day 3: Take a bike tour around the neighborhoods and learn about the history and culture of the area. Day 4: Visit Bosque de Chapultepec and see the Chapultepec Castle Day 5: Take a food tour and explore the local cuisine. Day 6: Discover the nightlife in Condesa and Roma Day 7: Visit the markets and buy some souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Condesa and Roma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos al Pastor - Try this popular street food consisting of marinated pork meat, served with onions, cilantro, and pineapple on a tortilla.
  • Churros - It is a fried dough pastry coated in sugar, usually served with hot chocolate for dipping.
  • Mezcal – Mezcal is a traditional Mexican spirit made from the agave plant, and it is enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  • Café de Olla - This is a traditional Mexican coffee made with cinnamon and raw sugar.
  • Michelada - This is a Mexican beer served in a salt-rimmed glass, mixed with a blend of lime juice, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, and tomato or Clamato juice.

Where to Stay in Condesa and Roma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Condesa DF - This boutique hotel has a modern design and a central location. Prices from $150 per night.
  • Casa Goliana - This guest house has a lovely garden where you can relax, a shared kitchen, and a free breakfast. Prices from $50 per night.
  • Hostel Home - This hostel offers budget-friendly dorms, private rooms, and a free breakfast. Prices from $15 per night.

What to Buy in Condesa and Roma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artesanías La Ciudadela - This market sells traditional Mexican handicrafts, souvenirs, and clothing.
  • La Lagunilla Market - This outdoor market offers a wide range of antiques, art, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Condesa and Roma

Mexico has a rich and diverse culture that is reflected in the customs and traditions of Condesa and Roma. Mexicans are warm and welcoming people, and visitors are often greeted with a smile and a friendly “hola.” When greeting someone, it is common to shake hands, hug, or even kiss on the cheek.

Best Time to Visit Condesa and Roma

The best time to visit Condesa and Roma is between March and May, when the temperatures are mild and the rainfall is low. The temperatures tend to be cooler at night, so bring some warm clothes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Condesa and Roma

Do:

  • Do take a walking tour of the area to learn about the history and culture of Condesa and Roma.
  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do visit the outdoor markets and buy some souvenirs to take home.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t engage in illegal activities like drug use.

Safety and Health

Safety in Condesa and Roma

Condesa and Roma are generally safe neighborhoods, but it is still important to take precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid walking alone at night, and keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Condesa and Roma

It is important to hydrate well with bottled water, protect yourself from excessive sun exposure, and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses. Mexico also has an excellent private and public healthcare system if you need medical assistance.

Getting to Condesa and Roma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Condesa and Roma

  • Mexico City International Airport - The airport is about 20 km from Condesa and Roma. The most convenient way to get to your accommodation is by taking a taxi or an Uber.

Bus Stations Near Condesa and Roma

  • Terminal Central del Norte - This bus station is located in the northern part of Mexico City and services several destinations throughout Mexico.
  • Terminal de Autobuses del Sur - This bus station is located in the southern part of Mexico City and services destinations towards the south of Mexico.

Other Options

  • Metro - Metro stations located around Condesa and Roma allow for easy travel around the city.
  • Bike Rental - Rent a bike from one of the many bike rental shops to explore Condesa and Roma at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Condesa and Roma

Walk or bike around the neighborhood to enjoy the picturesque streets and architecture. Taxis and Ubers are also readily available to transport you around the city.

Where to Go Next from Condesa and Roma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Teotihuacan Pyramids - Visit Mexico’s most famous archaeological site, located just outside of Mexico City.
  • Xochimilco Floating Gardens – Take a boat ride and explore the beautiful canals filled with colorful boats and traditional mariachi music.
  • Guanajuato City - This beautiful colonial town, located in Central Mexico, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is known for its lively cultural scene.

Conclusion

Condesa and Roma are two of the most vibrant neighborhoods in Mexico City. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, there is plenty to explore and discover in these popular neighborhoods. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Condesa and Roma.
