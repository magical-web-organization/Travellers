Introduction to Huamantla

Huamantla is a charming town located in the state of Tlaxcala, Central Mexico. Known for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural scenery, it is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. The town’s history dates back to pre-Hispanic times, and its architecture and traditions reflect a blend of Mesoamerican and colonial influences.

List of Places to Visit in Huamantla

There are plenty of things to see and do in Huamantla, whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Templo de la Merced: A 16th-century church with an impressive baroque facade and ornate interior. It is considered one of the most beautiful churches in Tlaxcala.
  • Museo Nacional del Títere: A museum dedicated to the art of puppetry, with a collection of over 2000 puppets from around the world.
  • Ex-Convento de San Francisco: A former Franciscan monastery that now serves as a cultural center, with art exhibitions, music performances, and workshops.
  • Museo Taurino: A museum dedicated to bullfighting, a popular sport in Mexico. It showcases the history and culture of the sport, as well as the costumes and equipment used by matadors.

Natural Attractions of Huamantla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Reserva Ecológica La Malinche: A protected natural area that includes the slopes of the La Malinche volcano. It offers hiking trails, camping sites, and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Parque Nacional Lagunas de Zempoala: A national park located 30 km from Huamantla, with 12 small lakes and ponds surrounded by pine and fir forests. It is a great spot for birdwatching, hiking, and picnicking.
  • Grutas de Cacahuamilpa: A set of limestone caves located 90 km from Huamantla, with mesmerizing rock formations and underground rivers. Guided tours are available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huamantla

Huamantla is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. These are some of the most popular trails:

  • Sendero Cerro Blanco: A challenging hike that takes you to the top of Cerro Blanco, a mountain located on the outskirts of Huamantla. The trail is steep but rewarding, with stunning views of the town and the surrounding valley.
  • Ruta del Pueblo Mágico: A 10 km bike route that takes you through the historic center of Huamantla and the nearby villages of San Francisco Atexcatzinco and Xalpatlahuaya. It is a great way to explore the local culture and architecture.
  • La Malinche National Park: The national park offers several hiking trails of varying difficulty, ranging from the easy 2 km trail to the challenging 14 km trail to the summit of La Malinche volcano.

Best Things to Do in Huamantla: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Huamantla, such as:

  • Mercado Municipal: A colorful market where you can sample local delicacies, buy souvenirs, and interact with the locals.
  • Feria de Huamantla: The town’s most famous festival, held every year in August. It includes parades, music, dancing, and a bullfighting competition.
  • Cine Teatro Morelos: A historic cinema that shows classic Mexican films and hosts cultural events such as concerts, plays, and lectures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huamantla

Visitors can easily spend a week exploring Huamantla and its surroundings. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the town’s historic center, including the Templo de la Merced and the Ex-Convento de San Francisco.
  • Day 3: Visit the Museo Nacional del Títere and take a bike ride along the Ruta del Pueblo Mágico.
  • Day 4: Hike to the summit of Cerro Blanco and enjoy the panoramic views.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the Grutas de Cacahuamilpa and explore the underground caves.
  • Day 6: Visit the Parque Nacional Lagunas de Zempoala and go birdwatching.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Huamantla

Huamantla is known for its delicious and diverse cuisine, which includes both traditional Mexican dishes and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cecina con nopales: Thinly-sliced beef marinated in spices and grilled, served with cactus paddles (nopales) and salsa.
  • Escamoles: Ant larvae, a delicacy in Mexican cuisine. They can be served alone or in dishes such as omelets or tacos.
  • Chileatole: A soup made with corn, chili peppers, and herbs, typically served with chicken or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: A fermented drink made from the sap of the maguey plant. It has a fruity, slightly sour flavor and a low alcohol content.
  • Tepache: A sweet and spicy drink made from fermented pineapple, cinnamon, and sugar.
  • Tequila: A distilled drink made from the blue agave plant, which is native to Mexico. It is typically served as a shot with salt and a slice of lime.

Where to Stay in Huamantla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huamantla offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget and preference. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Hotel Refugio de Don Carlos: A colonial-style hotel located in the heart of the town, with spacious rooms and a rooftop terrace.
  • Hostal Nuestra Casa Huamantla: A cozy hostel with private and shared rooms, a communal kitchen, and a garden.
  • Casa Bugambilias: A guesthouse with a rustic-chic vibe, surrounded by gardens and orchards.

What to Buy in Huamantla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huamantla is known for its handicrafts and traditional products, which make great souvenirs or gifts for friends and family. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Puppets: Huamantla is famous for its puppetry tradition, so it’s natural to find a great selection of puppets in the town’s markets and shops.
  • Ceramics: Tlaxcala is known for its colorful pottery, which ranges from rustic to sophisticated. You can find pottery of all shapes and sizes in Huamantla’s markets.
  • Textiles: The artisans of Huamantla are skilled at weaving and embroidery, and their products include colorful tablecloths, bedspreads, and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Huamantla

Huamantla has preserved many of its pre-Hispanic and colonial traditions, which are still visible in its architecture, festivals, and daily life. Some of the most important ones are:

  • Dia de Muertos: The Day of the Dead is a Mexican holiday that celebrates deceased loved ones, with offerings of altars, flowers, and food. In Huamantla, the celebration includes a procession of giant puppets and music performances.
  • Feria de Huamantla: As mentioned earlier, this festival is the town’s most important event of the year, attracting thousands of visitors. It is a celebration of the town’s history and culture, with parades, dances, and bullfighting.

Best Time to Visit Huamantla

Huamantla enjoys a temperate climate year-round, with occasional rains in the summer. However, the best time to visit is from July to October, when the town comes alive with festivals and celebrations, and the surrounding hills are covered in wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huamantla

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drinks, which are delicious and diverse.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions, especially during festivals and ceremonies.
  • Do wear comfortable shoes, as many of the streets and sidewalks are made of cobbled stones.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, as it may cause stomach problems. Stick to bottled water or boiled water.
  • Don’t take pictures without permission, especially of people, as it may be considered disrespectful.
  • Don’t litter, as Huamantla is a clean and well-maintained town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huamantla

Huamantla is a safe and peaceful town, with a low crime rate. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should take basic precautions such as:

  • Avoiding walking alone at night.
  • Keeping valuables such as wallets and phones out of sight.
  • Being aware of their surroundings and following local customs and norms.

Health in Huamantla

Huamantla has good healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, visitors should still take precautions such as:

  • Drinking bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach problems.
  • Using insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever.
  • Wearing sunscreen and taking breaks in the shade to avoid sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Huamantla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huamantla

The closest international airport to Huamantla is Mexico City International Airport (MEX), which is about 150 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Huamantla. Alternatively, visitors can fly into Puebla International Airport (PBC), which is 100 km away, and take a taxi or a bus.

Bus Stations Near Huamantla

Huamantla has a bus station located in the town center, which offers regular services to other towns in Tlaxcala and nearby states. Visitors can also take a bus from Mexico City or Puebla to Huamantla.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or a private car to get to Huamantla from Mexico City or Puebla. Another option is to take a shared shuttle, which is a more affordable but less flexible way to travel.

How to Get Around in Huamantla

Huamantla is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, visitors can also take a taxi or a colectivo (shared taxi) to go further afield. If you want to explore the surrounding areas, it may be best to rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Huamantla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Huamantla is located in the heart of Mexico, and there are plenty of other destinations and excursions nearby. Some popular options are:

  • Puebla: A historic colonial city located 90 km from Huamantla, with beautiful architecture, museums, and markets.
  • Tlaxco: A historic town located 50 km from Huamantla, known for its silver jewelry and textiles.
  • Mexico City: The vibrant capital city of Mexico, located 150 km from Huamantla, with a wide range of attractions, museums, and food.

Conclusion

Huamantla is a hidden gem in the heart of Mexico, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, hiking in the hills, or enjoying the local cuisine, Huamantla has something for everyone. So why not plan your trip today and discover the magic of this Central Mexican town?
