Introduction to Tlaxcala (State)

Tlaxcala (State) is a small state in Central Mexico with a rich cultural heritage. It is located near the cities of Puebla and Mexico City, making it easily accessible. The state is known for its colonial architecture, local handicrafts, and delicious cuisine. The archeological sites and natural landscapes of Tlaxcala (State) make it a popular destination for travelers who are interested in exploring Mexico off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Tlaxcala (State)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tlaxcala Cathedral: A beautiful 16th-century cathedral located in the heart of Tlaxcala city that has a rich history and incredible architecture.
  • Museo Regional de Tlaxcala: A museum located in the Casa de Piedra castle that showcases the history and culture of the state.
  • Xochitécatl: An important pre-Columbian archeological site with impressive pyramids and structures.

Natural Attractions of Tlaxcala (State): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Malinche National Park: A protected natural area known for its stunning views, hiking trails, and camping spots.
  • La Trinidad Community: A small mountainous community that offers adventurous activities like zip-lining, horseback riding, and hiking trails.
  • Los Venados Waterfall: A beautiful waterfall and swimming spot located in Xaltocan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tlaxcala (State)

  • La Malinche Volcano: A popular hiking destination with trails leading to the summit of the volcano.
  • Sierra Nevada National Park: A natural area with beautiful hiking and bike trails through the mountains and forests of Tlaxcala (State).
  • Huamantla: A picturesque town with scenic bike routes and a bustling city center.

Best Things to Do in Tlaxcala (State): Activities and Tours

  • Tlaxcala Bullfight: A cultural experience that takes place three times a year in the city’s bullring.
  • Tours of Local Craftsmanship: A chance to visit local artisans and see how they make Tlaxcala’s famous rebozos (shawls), pottery, and silverware.
  • Cultural Festivals: Tlaxcala (State) has a rich cultural heritage and celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year, such as the Carnival of Hueyotlipan, the Day of the Dead, and the Huamantla Fair.

Food and Drink Scene of Tlaxcala (State)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbacoa: Slow-cooked marinated lamb or beef served with tortillas, avocado, and salsa.
  • Molotes: A fried masa cake stuffed with potato, cheese, and chorizo.
  • Tacos de Cecina: Thinly sliced beef or pork served in a tortilla with avocado and salsa.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: Made from the sap of agave plants, pulque is a refreshing, slightly sweet beverage that has been a traditional drink in Mexico for centuries.
  • Tlaxcal-tequila: A unique tequila made from blue agave plants grown in Tlaxcala (State).
  • Agua de cebada: A refreshing, non-alcoholic beverage made from barley.

Where to Stay in Tlaxcala (State): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hacienda Soltepec: A luxurious hotel in the countryside, perfect for a quiet and relaxing getaway.
  • Hostal La Posada del Tren: A charming hostel located in the heart of Tlaxcala city.
  • Casa de Piedra: A historic guesthouse with beautiful architecture and stunning views.

What to Buy in Tlaxcala (State): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rebozos: A beautiful, brightly colored shawl that is a staple of Tlaxcala (State) culture and fashion.
  • Pottery: Tlaxcala is known for its traditional pottery, with glazed and unglazed pieces available in a wide range of colors and styles.
  • Silverware: Tlaxcala (State) has a long history of working with silver, so there is an abundance of high-quality silverware available to purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Tlaxcala (State)

Tlaxcala (State) is a traditional and conservative state, with a strong sense of community and family values. The state’s culture is heavily influenced by pre-Columbian, Spanish colonial, and Catholic traditions. It is important to be respectful to local customs and traditions, such as dressing conservatively, using formal titles when addressing elders, and participating in religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Tlaxcala (State)

The best time to visit Tlaxcala (State) is during the dry season from November to May. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities. However, it is important to note that Tlaxcala (State) can get crowded during the high season, especially during festivals and holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tlaxcala (State)

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Visit the local artisan workshops to learn about traditional craft techniques.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public.
  • Photograph people or their houses without permission.
  • Wander into dangerous neighborhoods alone at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tlaxcala (State)

Tlaxcala (State) is generally a safe place to travel, but it is always important to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding unlit streets at night and keeping your valuables hidden in crowded areas. It is also advisable to carry a photocopy of your passport and ID with you at all times.

Health in Tlaxcala (State)

Tap water is not safe to drink in Tlaxcala (State), so it is recommended to drink bottled water or boiled water. Mosquitoes can be a problem, particularly during the rainy season, so it is advisable to bring insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Tlaxcala (State): Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Tlaxcala (State) is easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Tlaxcala (State)

The closest airports to Tlaxcala (State) are Mexico City International Airport and Puebla International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Tlaxcala (State)

Several bus companies operate in Tlaxcala (State), with frequent services to Mexico City, Puebla, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Tlaxcala (State) is easily accessible by car, with good quality roads leading to the state from other parts of Mexico.

How to Get Around in Tlaxcala (State)

Bus services operate within Tlaxcala (State), but the most convenient way to get around is by car or taxi. It is also possible to hire a bike or go hiking in some areas.

Where to Go Next from Tlaxcala (State): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puebla: Located just a short distance from Tlaxcala (State), Puebla is a charming colonial city known for its stunning architecture and delicious cuisine.
  • Mexico City: Mexico’s capital city is one of the largest cities in the world, with a rich history and colorful culture that is sure to captivate any traveler.

Conclusion

Tlaxcala (State) is a hidden gem in Mexico, with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious cuisine. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your visit to Tlaxcala (State) and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Mexico.
