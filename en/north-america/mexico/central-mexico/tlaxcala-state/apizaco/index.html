Introduction to Apizaco

Located in Central Mexico in the state of Tlaxcala, Apizaco is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, outdoor adventures, and culinary delights. Established in 1594, the town boasts impressive colonial architecture, colorful markets, and a laid-back atmosphere that gives visitors a glimpse into the authentic Mexican countryside lifestyle. Visitors can expect to experience a low-key version of Mexican glamour, history, and culture at Apizaco.

List of Places to Visit in Apizaco

Apizaco has an impressive list of places to visit and things to do, catering to every type of traveler. Visitors can explore the town’s rich culture and history, indulge in outdoor activities, or take a culinary journey through the local food scene.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town center is home to notable museums and historical sites that preserve Apizaco’s rich history and culture. The Casa de Piedra Museum is a must-visit attraction that houses a collection of archaeological artifacts from ancient Mexican civilizations. The San Francisco Convent and the San Antonio del Divino Salvador Church are other significant historical landmarks that visitors can explore. The Municipal Palace is an excellent place to get a glimpse of the town’s political history and architectural grandeur.

Natural Attractions of Apizaco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The town is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes that are perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The Malinche National Park is a popular spot for hiking and mountain climbing, offering spectacular views of the Mexican countryside. The Apizaquito Dam is a great place to spend a lazy afternoon fishing, swimming or picnicking. Visitors can also ride horses and bike around the beautiful hills and forests of Apizaco.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apizaco

Apizaco is a haven for hikers and bikers, with well-maintained trails that offer breathtaking views of the countryside. Visitors can hike or bike through the Malinche National Park, Apizaquito Dam, and the El Ocote Ecological Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Apizaco: Activities and Tours

Apizaco offers numerous activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s preferences, including historical tours of the town’s monuments, tasting local food, biking, and hiking trips. Visitors can also experience the famous Mexican rodeos, bullfights, and traditional festivals like Dia de Muertos (Day of the Dead).

Week-Long Itinerary for Apizaco

A week in Apizaco gives visitors enough time to explore every corner of this charming town. From its historical landmarks to outdoor adventures, visitors can enjoy the best that Apizaco has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Apizaco

Apizaco is known for its delicious local cuisine and drinks, which visitors must try.

Must-Try Dishes

Some signature dishes include:

  • Tlaxcalteca soup
  • Tacos de Barbacoa
  • Cemitas de Pierna
  • Mole Poblano

Local Beverages

Visitors must try the Pulque, a local alcoholic beverage made from the fermented sap of an agave plant. The smooth and creamy consistency and sweet taste make it a favorite among locals. Agua de Jamaica, made from Jamaica flowers, is a refreshing non-alcoholic drink that visitors must try.

Where to Stay in Apizaco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Apizaco offers various accommodation options suitable for every budget range. Visitors can choose from luxurious hotels, guesthouses, and hostels depending on their preferences. Some of the popular options include Hotel Casablanca, Hotel Real Tlaxcala, and Casa Frailes.

What to Buy in Apizaco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy a range of unique souvenirs and local products in Apizaco. Handwoven textiles, pottery, and silver jewelry make great gifts and memorabilia. Visitors can find these items in the local markets, such as Plaza del Charro or Mercado Miguel Hidalgo.

Local Culture and Customs of Apizaco

Apizaco’s culture is unique and deeply rooted in tradition. Visitors must respect the locals’ customs and behavior, such as being friendly, courteous, and avoiding loud music or noise late at night.

Best Time to Visit Apizaco

The best time to visit Apizaco is from December to February, when the weather is mild and cool. Visitors can also attend the town’s traditional festivals going on during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apizaco

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions
  • Explore the town’s history and landmarks
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Engage with locals and make new friends

Don’t:

  • Make noise late at night
  • Dress inappropriately or offensively
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Apizaco

Apizaco is a safe town for visitors, and violent crimes are rare. Visitors must take the usual safety precautions such as careful walking on uneven streets, avoiding flashing jewelry, and not leaving valuables in their car.

Health in Apizaco

Visitors must take the usual precautions while traveling to a foreign country. The water in Apizaco is safe to drink, but visitors can choose to carry bottled water.

Getting to Apizaco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apizaco

The closest international airport to Apizaco is Benito Juarez International Airport in Mexico City, about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Apizaco

Apizaco has its own bus station and is well-connected to major cities in Mexico, including Puebla and Mexico City.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Apizaco or drive themselves. However, it is essential to obtain Mexican car insurance before entering Mexico.

How to Get Around in Apizaco

Apizaco is a small town, and visitors can easily walk to the town center and other popular landmarks. Taxis and buses are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Apizaco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Apizaco is an ideal place to explore the surrounding towns and attractions. Day trips include visiting Cholula, known for its ancient pyramids and churches, and Puebla, known for its colonial architecture and culinary delights.

Conclusion

Apizaco is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. With its rich cultural heritage, delicious local cuisine, and vibrant scenery, Apizaco is a must-visit destination for any traveler seeking an authentic Mexican experience.
