Introduction to Tlaxcala

Tlaxcala is a beautiful and historically rich state located in the central part of Mexico, often overlooked by tourists. The state’s capital city, Tlaxcala de Xicohténcatl, is a charming colonial town with old buildings, colorful streets, and friendly people. From stunning natural attractions and outdoor adventures to rich cultural experiences, Tlaxcala has something to offer every traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, local cuisine, and culture of Tlaxcala.

List of Places to Visit in Tlaxcala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tlaxcala Museum of Art: This museum showcases the work of local artists and houses a collection of colonial art, with paintings, sculptures, and pottery from the 16th to the 18th centuries.

  • Xicohténcatl Theater: This theater, located in Tlaxcala city’s historic center, has a beautiful facade with neoclassical elements. It hosts a variety of shows, including plays, concerts, and dance performances.

  • Tlahuicole Park and Monument: This park is dedicated to Tlahuicole, a legendary warrior who lived in the region before the Spanish conquest. The monument is an impressive sculpture that honors his heroic deeds.

Natural Attractions of Tlaxcala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Malinche National Park: This park has stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The main attraction is La Malinche, a dormant volcano with hiking trails that lead to its summit, offering incredible panoramic views.

  • Parque Nacional Lagunas de Zempoala: This national park is a hidden gem with a chain of lagoons surrounded by forests. It’s a perfect spot to go hiking, bird watching, or to have a picnic while enjoying nature.

  • Huamantla Pueblo Mágico: This charming town has narrow streets, colonial architecture, and colorful facades. It’s a popular destination for visitors during the annual Feria Nacional de Huamantla, which takes place in August, attracting thousands of people to watch the traditional “callejoneadas” and the “huamantlada” bullfight.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tlaxcala

If you’re an adventure traveler, Tlaxcala has many natural spots to explore. The most popular hiking/biking routes are:

  • La Malinche Trail: This route takes you to the summit of La Malinche. The trail is about 5 miles long, with steep sections and rocky terrain; it takes around 4-5 hours to complete the hike.

  • Sierra Nevada Trail: This trail is excellent for mountain biking, with a distance of 12 miles, crossing rivers and forests.

  • The Great Tlaxcala Bull-Ring Circuit: This route connects different bullrings in the state, starting in Huamantla, and passing through Nativitas, Tlaxcala, and Apizaco; it’s a perfect way to explore the cultural significance of bullfights in the region.

Best Things to Do in Tlaxcala: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: You can enjoy a stunning panoramic view of Tlaxcala from a hot air balloon, floating over the volcanoes and valleys.

  • Craft Workshops: Tlaxcala is famous for its crafts, such as clay pottery, weaving, and basketry. You can take part in workshops to learn these ancient techniques directly from the artisans.

  • Horseback Riding Tours: You can explore the countryside on horseback, passing through fields, forests, and rivers while admiring the landscape. Some tours include a visit to a traditional hacienda and a meal with local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tlaxcala

Day 1: Arrival in Tlaxcala, explore downtown and visit the Tlaxcala Museum of Art.

Day 2: Hike the La Malinche Trail, have lunch in a local restaurant, and rest in the afternoon.

Day 3: Take a hot air balloon tour in the morning, visit the Tlahuicole Park and Monument, and have dinner in the old hacienda of Santa Rosa.

Day 4: Bike the Sierra Nevada Trail, have a picnic in Parque Nacional Lagunas de Zempoala, and enjoy the sunset in the town of San José Teacalco.

Day 5: Take a craft workshop to learn about local crafts, visit the Xicohténcatl Theater, and have dinner at a local market.

Day 6: Horseback riding tour to Huamantla and visit the church of Nuestra Señora de la Caridad to see the famous murals of Desiderio Hernández Xochitiotzi.

Day 7: Visit the town of Tlaxco, visit the Tlaxcala State Fairgrounds, and buy souvenirs before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Tlaxcala

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cemitas Puebla: A sandwich filled with shredded meat, avocado, cheese, and chipotle sauce on a sesame-seed bun.

  • Chiles en Nogada: A dish made with poblano peppers stuffed with meat and fruit, covered with a walnut sauce, and garnished with pomegranate seeds and parsley.

  • Tlacoyos: A tortilla filled with beans, cheese, or meat, then cooked on a comal and served with salsa and fresh cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: A traditional alcoholic drink made from fermented maguey sap, with a sour-tangy taste and a slightly viscous texture.

  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the maguey plant, with smoky flavor and a high alcohol content.

  • Tejuino: A refreshing drink made from fermented corn, mixed with lime, salt, sugar, and ice.

Where to Stay in Tlaxcala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Boutique Posada La Casona de Cortés: A historic building with elegant rooms, a charming central patio, and a cozy restaurant. Located in Tlaxcala city’s historic center, a few blocks from the main square.

  • Hotel Posada San Francisco Tlaxcala: A colonial-style hotel with comfortable rooms, a garden, and a terrace with panoramic views of the city. Located near the Zocalo of Tlaxcala.

  • Hostal Casa de Barro: A traditional guesthouse with shared rooms, a garden, and a patio with a fire pit, perfect for relaxing after a busy day. Located in the picturesque town of Huamantla.

What to Buy in Tlaxcala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Tlaxcala is known for its handicrafts, particularly its pottery, weaving, and basketry. You can find beautiful pieces made by local artisans in markets and shops throughout the state.

  • Traditional Clothing: The state’s traditional clothing is colorful and features intricate embroidery and lace. You can find dresses, blouses, and skirts in shops or from street vendors in many towns and cities.

  • Mezcal and Pulque: You can bring home a bottle of mezcal or pulque, a traditional drink made from fermented cactus sap, as a unique and authentic gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Tlaxcala

Tlaxcala has a rich cultural heritage that has been influenced by its pre-Hispanic roots, colonial history, and contemporary art scene. Visitors can witness traditional festivals, food fairs, and art exhibitions throughout the year. Tlaxcala also has a vibrant music scene, with many groups showcasing traditional and contemporary styles.

Best Time to Visit Tlaxcala

The best time to visit Tlaxcala is between November and March when the weather is pleasant and dry. You can enjoy outdoor activities, local festivals, and markets during this time. However, make sure to avoid traveling during the rainy season as it can affect your plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tlaxcala

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Visit traditional crafts markets.

  • Respect the cultural and religious traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in religious sites.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tlaxcala

Tlaxcala is considered a safe state for tourists, but it’s always essential to take precautions. Avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas or crowded streets with valuables. Keep your belongings safe in public spaces and use certified taxis or transportation services.

Health in Tlaxcala

For travelers, it is always advisable to check with your healthcare provider before visiting Tlaxcala to ensure that you are up to date on routine vaccines. It’s also essential to take precautions against mosquito bites and carry a first aid kit, including insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Tlaxcala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tlaxcala

The nearest international airport to Tlaxcala is Mexico City International Airport, which is about 2 hours drive from Tlaxcala city.

Bus Stations Near Tlaxcala

Tlaxcala has a central bus station with routes to different cities in the country, including Puebla, Mexico City, and Veracruz. The bus station is located near the city’s historic center, making it easily accessible by foot or taxi.

Other Options

The best way to travel around Tlaxcala is by car, as it gives you the flexibility to explore the state’s many attractions. You can rent a car at the Mexico City airport and drive to Tlaxcala, taking the Mexico-Tlaxcala highway.

How to Get Around in Tlaxcala

Tlaxcala has a reliable public transportation system with buses and taxis that connect every town and city in the state. However, if you want to explore more remote areas or rural communities, renting a car or hiring a local guide is the best option.

Where to Go Next from Tlaxcala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puebla: This colonial city is a treasure trove of history, art, and culture, with charming neighborhoods, museums, and churches.

  • Mexico City: The country’s capital is a vast metropolis with impressive architecture, museums, parks, and food scenes.

  • Cacaxtla Archeological Site: This site is located in the eastern part of the state and features ancient murals and sculptures from the Olmeca-Xicalanca culture.

Conclusion

Tlaxcala is an undiscovered gem in Mexico, with stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly people. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure traveler, or a foodie, Tlaxcala has something for everyone. We hope this guide has inspired you and provided you with useful information to plan your trip to Tlaxcala. Get ready to explore this beautiful state and create unforgettable memories!
