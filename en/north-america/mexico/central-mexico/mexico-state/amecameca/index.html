Introduction to Amecameca

Located in the state of Mexico, Amecameca is a charming town that’s nestled at the foot of the Iztaccihuatl volcano. This quaint Mexican town is known for its rich history, cultural significance, and breathtaking natural scenery. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a food lover, Amecameca has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Amecameca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Cultura Tepetlaoxtoc: This museum is a wonderful place to visit for those who love history and culture. It houses a collection of pre-Hispanic objects, colonial artifacts, and ethnographic material. One of the most interesting exhibits is a ritual ball game of the Mesoamerican period.
  • Ex Convento de San Francisco: This 16th-century former monastery now houses a museum with a wide variety of exhibits on natural history, archaeology, and art. The highlight of the museum is a stunning replica of the city of Tenochtitlan, the heart of the Aztec civilization.
  • Iglesia de la Asuncion: The beautiful church of the Assumption dates back to the 16th century and has an impressive baroque facade. Inside is a stunning piece of art, the Cristo de Amecameca, a life-sized sculpture of Jesus carved out of a single piece of wood.

Natural Attractions of Amecameca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Iztaccihuatl and Popocatepetl National Park: This park is home to two of the most iconic volcanoes in Mexico: Iztaccihuatl and Popocatepetl. It’s a great place to enjoy hiking trails, mountain biking, or to simply enjoy the beautiful natural scenery.
  • La Perla Amecameca: A unique natural phenomenon, La Perla is a pool of crystal-clear water that is located inside a volcanic crater. This site is ideal for swimming, picnicking, and enjoying the views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Cerro de Amecameca: The Cerro de Amecameca provides visitors with beautiful panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amecameca

  • El Pinalito Hike: This hike is a favorite among adventure seekers and offers a challenging trail with beautiful views of the Iztaccihuatl and Popocatepetl volcanoes.
  • La Joya Trail: This moderate trail is located in the Iztaccihuatl and Popocatepetl National Park and is perfect for hikers who wish to take in the picturesque views of the mountains and valley below.
  • Vuelta al Sol Trail: This biking trail is a fast-paced adventure that takes cyclists through the winding streets of Amecameca and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Amecameca: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a local market: One of the best ways to experience local culture is by visiting a bustling market, such as the Tianguis. These markets offer a wide variety of goods ranging from handmade crafts to fresh produce, and local cuisine.
  • Take a guided tour: For those looking to learn more about the culture and history of Amecameca, there are many guided tours available. These tours offer visitors a unique insight into the town’s rich heritage and stunning natural scenery.
  • Experience the local food scene: Amecameca is famous for its delicious cuisine. From street food to high-end restaurants, there’s something to suit every palate. Be sure to try the local specialty; barbacoa, slow-cooked beef served with tortillas and fresh salsa.

Food and Drink Scene of Amecameca

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbacoa: Slow-roasted beef that’s marinated in spices and served with tortillas and salsa.
  • Sopa Azteca: A hearty soup made with pasilla chilies, tomatoes, tortillas, and avocado.
  • Chile Relleno: A mild pepper stuffed with cheese or meat, then fried and served in a tomato sauce.
  • Huitlacoche: A mushroom-like fungus that grows on corn, often used to flavor tamales.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: A popular distilled spirit made from blue agave that’s grown in the Jalisco region.
  • Pulque: An alcoholic beverage made from the fermented sap of the maguey plant.
  • Tepache: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made from fermented pineapple, cinnamon, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Amecameca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel San Carlos: Located in the town center, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and great views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Casa San Angel Bed & Breakfast: This guesthouse is located in a beautifully restored colonial building and offers a tranquil and comfortable stay.
  • Hostal San Roque: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Amecameca, this hostel is perfect for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Amecameca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafts: Amecameca boasts a thriving arts and crafts community. Visitors can find anything from woven textiles to pottery and furniture.
  • Lapidary Products: The surrounding mountains are home to a variety of gemstones. Visitors can shop for jewelry or take a class in lapidary to make their own piece of jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Amecameca

Amecameca is a town rich in cultural heritage, and the locals take great pride in their traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the customs and traditions of the region. One unique aspect of the local culture is the annual Passion Play, a theatrical representation of the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ, which takes place during Holy Week.

Best Time to Visit Amecameca

The best time to visit Amecameca is between November and March when the weather is mild and rainfall is low. However, the town is lovely year-round, with beautiful views of the mountains and scenic drives through the countryside.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amecameca

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take a guided tour of the town’s many museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended.
  • Wear expensive jewelry or flashy clothing.
  • Ignore the state of your health.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amecameca

Amecameca is a relatively safe town for tourists. However, visitors should exercise caution and avoid walking on deserted streets at night. It’s also important to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Amecameca

Visitors to Amecameca should take precautions to stay healthy, such as drinking only bottled water and avoiding street food that’s not properly cooked or prepared. Visitors should also pack sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Amecameca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amecameca

  • Mexico City International Airport: The nearest airport to Amecameca, located approximately 30 miles away. It’s a major air transportation hub, with connections to numerous destinations across the globe. Taxis and shuttles are available to take passengers from the airport to Amecameca.

Bus Stations Near Amecameca

  • North Bus Station: This is the busiest bus station in Mexico City, with connections to a variety of destinations across the country.
  • Central de Autobuses del Norte: Another busy bus station in Mexico City, with connections to many major Mexican cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors to Amecameca can rent a car at the Mexico City International Airport or in the downtown area.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Amecameca and are a convenient way to get around town.

How to Get Around in Amecameca

Amecameca is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Taxis are also readily available for visitors who want more convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Amecameca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mexico City: The capital city of Mexico is a vibrant metropolis that’s known for its rich history, culture, and food scene.
  • Puebla: This charming colonial town is located just a few hours from Amecameca and is home to stunning colonial architecture and delicious cuisine.
  • Oaxaca: This city is the center for indigenous cultures in Mexico and is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful art.

Conclusion

Amecameca is a charming and beautiful town that is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a history buff, or a food lover, there’s something for everyone in Amecameca. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your visit to this hidden gem in the heart of Mexico.
