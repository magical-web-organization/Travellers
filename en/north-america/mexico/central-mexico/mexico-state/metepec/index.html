Introduction to Metepec

Located in the heart of Mexico State, Metepec offers an unbeatable combination of cultural and natural attractions. The town’s rich history, colonial architecture, and vibrant indigenous traditions make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a truly authentic Mexican experience. Known as the “City of Pottery,” Metepec is famous for its beautiful handmade ceramics, which are sold in the town’s numerous shops and markets.

List of Places to Visit in Metepec

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo del Barro: This impressive museum houses one of the largest collections of Mexican ceramics in the country, showcasing the diversity of this ancient art form and its evolution over time. Visitors can learn about the history and techniques of pottery-making and see stunning examples of traditional and contemporary ceramics.
  • San Juan Bautista Church: This stunning colonial church is one of the town’s most iconic landmarks, with its distinctive pink stone façade and intricate stucco artwork. Inside, visitors can admire the beautiful altar and religious paintings, as well as the ornate wooden ceiling and pulpit.
  • Casa de la Cultura: This cultural center is located in a beautiful colonial building and hosts a variety of art exhibitions, concerts, theater performances, and workshops throughout the year. Visitors can also take classes in traditional arts and crafts, such as pottery-making, weaving, and embroidery.

Natural Attractions of Metepec: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Sierra Morelos: This vast nature reserve is a paradise for hikers, mountain bikers, and nature lovers, with over 5000 hectares of forested hills, canyons, and waterfalls to explore. Visitors can hike to the summit of Cerro Viejo, the highest peak in the park, for panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Laguna de la Tlayuda: This scenic lagoon is a hidden gem in the outskirts of Metepec, surrounded by lush vegetation and home to a variety of bird species. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake, enjoy a picnic on the shore, or go fishing for trout and carp.
  • Parque Juárez: This historic park in the heart of Metepec is a popular spot for a relaxing stroll, picnicking, or people-watching. Visitors can admire the colorful flower beds, fountains, and sculptures, or rent a bike to explore the park’s winding paths.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Metepec

  • Cerro Viejo Trail: This challenging hiking trail leads to the summit of Cerro Viejo, the highest peak in Parque Sierra Morelos. The trail is over 10 km long and features steep ascents and descents, but the stunning views from the top are well worth the effort.
  • Bicentenario Trail: This intermediate-level mountain biking trail follows a circuit of about 8 km through the forested hills of Parque Sierra Morelos, with scenic vistas of the surrounding valleys and canyons.
  • Parque Juárez Loop: This easy walking or biking trail follows a circuit of about 3 km around Parque Juárez, passing by the park’s main attractions and landmarks, such as the kiosk, the bandstand, and the fountain.

Best Things to Do in Metepec: Activities and Tours

  • Ceramics Workshop: Visitors can take a pottery-making class at one of Metepec’s many ceramics workshops and learn the techniques and secrets of this traditional craft from local artisans. Some workshops also offer guided tours of their studios and galleries.
  • Tepozteco Pyramids Tour: This full-day excursion takes visitors from Metepec to the nearby town of Tepoztlán, where they can hike to the top of the Tepozteco pyramid and admire the stunning views of the valley below. The tour also includes a visit to the town’s colorful market and its beautiful colonial church.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: For a unique and unforgettable experience, visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic landscapes of Metepec and its surroundings, enjoying breathtaking views of the forests, lakes, and mountains from the sky.

Food and Drink Scene of Metepec

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbacoa: This traditional slow-cooked lamb or beef dish is a staple of Mexican cuisine, and Metepec’s version is especially flavorful and tender. The meat is usually seasoned with a mix of chili peppers, herbs, and spices and served with tortillas, salsa, and other toppings.
  • Tamales: These steamed corn dough parcels filled with various ingredients, such as meat, vegetables, cheese, or chili peppers, are a classic Mexican comfort food. Metepec’s tamales are typically wrapped in banana leaves and served with a side of beans and rice.
  • Churros: This crispy fried dough pastry sprinkled with sugar and cinnamon is a popular snack and dessert in Mexico. Metepec’s churros are especially delicious when dipped in hot chocolate or dulce de leche.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: This traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented maguey sap has been enjoyed in Mexico for centuries and is known for its slightly sour and sweet taste, as well as its supposed health benefits. Metepec is famous for its high-quality pulque, which can be enjoyed plain or flavored with fruits like guava or strawberry.
  • Tequila: This iconic Mexican spirit, made from blue agave plants grown in the state of Jalisco, is renowned for its smoothness, complexity, and versatility. Metepec has a variety of bars and restaurants where visitors can sample some of the best tequila brands in the country.
  • Aguas Frescas: These refreshing non-alcoholic drinks made from fresh fruits, herbs, and flowers are a popular and healthy choice in Mexico. Metepec’s aguas frescas come in many flavors, such as jamaica (hibiscus), tamarind, or horchata (rice milk), and are perfect for quenching thirst on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Metepec: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel La Mansión del Burro Azul: This beautiful colonial-style hotel is located in the heart of Metepec and features spacious and comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, a restaurant, and a garden courtyard. The hotel’s name means “the mansion of the blue donkey,” a nod to the town’s pottery tradition.
  • Camping and Glamping in Parque Sierra Morelos: For nature lovers and adventure seekers, camping or glamping (luxury camping) in Parque Sierra Morelos is a thrilling and unique experience. The park has several camping areas with basic facilities and stunning views, as well as a glamping site with comfortable tents, a communal kitchen, and fire pits.
  • Posada Familia Boutique Hotel: This charming and cozy guesthouse in the outskirts of Metepec offers personalized service, comfortable rooms, and a peaceful atmosphere, perfect for a romantic getaway or a family vacation. The guesthouse also has a restaurant and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Metepec: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Metepec’s ceramics are famous for their beauty, quality, and variety, ranging from traditional plates, bowls, and figurines to modern lamps, vases, and sculptures. Visitors can buy ceramics at the town’s markets, shops, and workshops, such as the famous Artesanías de Metepec market.
  • Textiles: Metepec’s indigenous culture is reflected in its vibrant and colorful textiles, which include blankets, rugs, tablecloths, and shawls, decorated with intricate patterns and symbols. The best places to buy textiles are the local markets, such as the Plaza Juárez market.
  • Maguey Mezcal: This artisanal alcoholic beverage made from the fermented and distilled sap of the maguey plant is a unique and flavorful souvenir from Metepec. Visitors can buy mezcal at the town’s shops and markets, such as the Toluca Central de Abastos market.

Local Culture and Customs of Metepec

Metepec has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in its indigenous Náhuatl origins and its colonial history. The town’s pottery tradition, its colorful dances and music, and its religious festivals and rituals are some of the most distinctive expressions of its culture.

One of the most famous festivals in Metepec is the Feria Nacional del Árbol y la Esfera, held annually in December, which showcases the town’s handmade ceramic Christmas tree ornaments and its rich gastronomy. Other important festivals and events in Metepec include the Día de Muertos, the Fiesta de San Isidro, and the Fiesta de San Juan Bautista, among others.

Visitors to Metepec are encouraged to respect and appreciate the town’s cultural and artistic traditions by attending cultural events, visiting museums and galleries, and supporting local artisans and businesses.

Best Time to Visit Metepec

Metepec has a pleasant and temperate climate all year round, with average temperatures ranging from 15-25°C. However, the best time to visit Metepec is between October and December, when the town’s festivals and holidays take place, and the weather is usually sunny and mild. The Feria Nacional del Árbol y la Esfera is a particularly festive and colorful event that attracts visitors from all over Mexico and the world.

Do’s and Don’ts of Metepec

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, such as barbacoa, pulque, and maguey mezcal.
  • Do explore the cultural and natural landmarks of the town, such as Museo del Barro, Parque Sierra Morelos, and San Juan Bautista Church.
  • Do respect the traditions and customs of the local people, such as the indigenous dances and rituals, and the colonial architecture and art.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural or cultural sites of the town, such as Sierra Morelos or Casa de la Cultura.
  • Don’t behave disrespectfully or rudely to the locals, such as by taking their photos without permission or using offensive language or gestures.
  • Don’t underestimate the altitude, climate, or terrain of the town, especially if engaging in outdoor activities or hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Metepec

Metepec is generally a safe and peaceful town, with a low crime rate and friendly and welcoming locals. However, visitors should take some precautions to ensure their safety and avoid any incidents:

  • Avoid walking alone or in poorly lit areas at night, especially in the outskirts of the town.
  • Keep your valuables and documents in a safe place, such as a hotel safe or a hidden pouch.
  • Use authorized and reliable taxi services or public transportation, and be wary of unofficial or unmarked taxis.

Health in Metepec

Metepec has good healthcare facilities and services, with several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies available. Visitors should take some precautions to ensure their health and avoid any illnesses:

  • Consult a doctor or travel clinic before departing for Metepec, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medication.
  • Drink only bottled or purified water, and avoid eating uncooked or undercooked food, especially street food.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent, especially if engaging in outdoor activities or hiking.

Getting to Metepec: Transportation Options and Logistics

Metepec is conveniently located in central Mexico, with easy access to several transportation options:

Airports Near Metepec

The closest airport to Metepec is the Toluca International Airport (TLC), which is about 15 km away from the town center. The airport has domestic and international flights to several destinations in Mexico and the United States.

The Mexico City International Airport (MEX) is also located nearby, about 45 km away from Metepec, but has more flight options and better connections to other cities in Mexico and abroad.

Bus Stations Near Metepec

Metepec has several bus stations that offer transportation to nearby and distant cities in Mexico:

  • Central de Autobuses de Toluca: This large and modern bus station is located in Toluca, about 10 km away from Metepec, and offers bus services to various destinations in Mexico and the United States.
  • Terminal de Autobuses de Metepec: This small and local bus station is located in the town center of Metepec and offers bus services to nearby cities and towns in Mexico, such as Tenango del Valle and Toluca.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a private shuttle service to get to Metepec from the airports or bus stations. The town is also well-connected to the main highways and roads in the region, such as the Mexico-Toluca highway.

How to Get Around in Metepec

Metepec is a relatively small town that can be easily explored on foot, by bike, or by public transportation. Some of the best ways to get around Metepec are:

  • Walking: Most of Metepec’s main attractions, landmarks, and shops are located in the town center and can be easily reached on foot. Walking also allows visitors to soak up the town’s atmosphere and charm.
  • Biking: Metepec has several bike rental services and bike-friendly routes, such as Parque Juárez or the Cerro Viejo trail, making biking a fun and healthy way to explore the town and its surroundings.
  • Public Transportation: Metepec has several local buses, taxis, and colectivos (shared taxis) that can take visitors to different parts of the town and surrounding areas. The town’s main bus station, the Terminal de Autobuses de Metepec, is also located in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Metepec: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Metepec is conveniently located near several other interesting and attractive destinations in Mexico, which make for great day trips or excursions:

  • Tepoztlán: This picturesque town, located about 60 km away from Metepec, is famous for its lush vegetation, its traditional crafts, and its mystical pyramids and caves. Visitors can hike to the top of the Tepozteco pyramid, shop at the local market, and enjoy the town’s bohemian and artistic vibe.
  • Tenango del Valle: This charming and historic town, located about 20 km away from Metepec, is famous for its colorful and intricate textiles, its ancient pyramids and temples, and its delicious maguey mezcal. Visitors can take a guided tour of the town’s museum, market, and pyramid, and learn about its rich cultural heritage.
  • Nevado de Toluca National Park: This stunning nature reserve, located about 40 km away from Metepec, is home to one of Mexico’s highest and most impressive volcanoes, as well as several lagoons, forests, and hiking trails. Visitors can hike to the volcano’s summit, ski in the winter, or camp in the summer.

Conclusion

Metepec is a true gem of Mexico, offering a perfect blend of culture, nature, and adventure. From its colorful ceramics and textiles to its stunning landscapes and outdoor activities, Metepec has something to offer for every type of traveler. By following this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their time in Metepec and discover the town’s hidden treasures and secrets.
