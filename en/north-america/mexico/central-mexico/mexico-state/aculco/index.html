Introduction to Aculco

Aculco is a charming town located in Mexico State, in Central Mexico, in North America. Known for its picturesque landscapes, vibrant culture, rich history, and warm hospitality, Aculco attracts nature enthusiasts, adventurers, and culture buffs from all around the world. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, traditional architecture, or delicious cuisine, Aculco has it all. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and more, to help you plan a memorable trip to Aculco.

List of Places to Visit in Aculco

If you’re visiting Aculco, ensure you don’t miss out on these must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aculco is rich in history and culture, reflected in its numerous museums and historical landmarks. Some of the most popular places to visit include:

  • Parroquia de San Jerónimo: One of the most iconic landmarks in Aculco, this neoclassical-style church dates back to the 18th century and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate interiors.

  • La Casa de la Cultura: A cultural center that exhibits and preserves Aculco’s rich history and traditions through its art collections, archaeological finds, and temporary exhibitions.

  • La Casa del Prebendado: A restored colonial house that showcases the architecture, furniture, and daily life of the 19th century in Aculco.

Natural Attractions of Aculco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aculco’s natural beauty offers a wealth of outdoor activities and scenic views that will not disappoint. Here are a few places that should be on your list:

  • Mirador Los Cocos: A popular lookout point that offers breathtaking views of Aculco’s lush vegetation and nearby mountains. Perfect for a scenic photo opportunity.

  • El Cerro del Guajolote: A hiking trail that takes you through Aculco’s natural landscapes and leads to the top of the Guajolote hill, where you can witness panoramic views of the town and surrounding areas.

  • Parque el Descanso: A serene park situated along the Calvario River that offers shaded picnic areas, playgrounds, and walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aculco

Aculco is a paradise for enthusiasts of hiking and biking. Some of the most notable hiking/biking routes include:

  • Ruta el Huarache: A 12 km route that takes you through Aculco’s mountainous terrain and offers panoramic views of the region.

  • Ruta Pueblos Mágicos: A challenging bike ride that takes you through the heart of Aculco and its neighboring Pueblos Mágicos, where you can witness the rich culture and heritage of Mexico.

Best Things to Do in Aculco: Activities and Tours

Aculco’s vibrant culture and outdoor activities are bound to leave you spoilt for choice when it comes to things to do. Here are a few must-do activities:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience the stunning landscapes of Aculco from a unique vantage point in a hot air balloon ride.

  • Gastronomic Tour: Explore Aculco’s culinary offerings on a gastronomic tour that takes you through the town’s top eateries, street food stalls, and markets.

  • Zip-Lining and Canopy: Get your adrenaline pumping with zip-lining and canopy tours through Aculco’s forests.

Food and Drink Scene of Aculco

Aculco’s cuisine takes influence from its cultural diversity, with a range of traditional Mexican and international dishes on offer. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicharrón prensado: A traditional pork dish made from pressed pork rinds and herbs, served with salsa and tortillas.

  • Gorditas de nata: A savory pastry filled with cream, cheese, and green or red salsa.

  • Barbacoa: Slow-roasted meats (usually beef or lamb) served with tortillas, salsas, and cilantro.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: A fermented drink made from the sap of the maguey plant. It has a slightly sour taste and is often served with lime and chili powder.

  • Tepache: A sweet, fruity drink made from fermented pineapple, cinnamon, and brown sugar.

Where to Stay in Aculco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aculco has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are a few top-rated options:

  • Casa Victoria Boutique Hotel: A boutique-style hotel situated in the heart of Aculco, with luxurious rooms and suites and an outdoor pool and spa.

  • Hostal Juquila: A budget-friendly hostel located near Aculco’s town center, with shared and private rooms and communal spaces to socialize with other travelers.

  • La Casita de Rosario: A cozy guesthouse with charming, rustic décor and affordable rates. Located near Aculco’s cultural landmarks.

What to Buy in Aculco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aculco is known for its traditional crafts, artisanal products, and locally made foods. Some of the best things to bring home as souvenirs or gifts include:

  • Pottery and ceramics: Aculco is famous for its handmade pottery and ceramics, with various shops and bazaars selling unique pieces.

  • Chocolates: Aculco is home to some of the best artisanal chocolate makers in Mexico, including Xikua and Ki Xocolatl. Treat your taste buds to some local chocolate delights.

  • Textiles and clothing: Aculco’s traditional clothing, including huipils and embroidery, make for beautiful and authentic souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Aculco

While visiting Aculco, it is important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Religious traditions: Aculco is a deeply religious town, and many of its customs and celebrations revolve around religious observances.

  • Dress code: Modesty is highly valued in Aculco, so it is recommended to dress conservatively while visiting religious sites.

  • Greeting etiquette: Mexicans are generally friendly, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake or a hug. However, be sure to ask for permission before giving a hug.

Best Time to Visit Aculco

The best time to visit Aculco is from October to April, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Plan your trip around Aculco’s annual festivals and celebrations, such as the Day of the Dead (November 1-2) and the Feast of Our Lady of Guadalupe (December 12), to experience the town’s vibrant culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aculco

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Aculco:

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish: While many locals in Aculco speak English, it is recommended to learn some basic Spanish phrases to help you communicate with the locals better.

  • Respect the environment: Aculco’s natural landscapes are a treasure to be preserved, so make sure to dispose of your waste properly and avoid littering.

  • Try the local food: Aculco has a rich gastronomic culture, and trying the local food is an excellent way to experience its traditions and flavors.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful: Mexicans value respect and manners, so avoid any behavior that may come across as rude or disrespectful, such as talking loudly or interrupting someone.

  • Ignore cultural customs: Understanding and respecting cultural customs is essential while traveling to a new place. Be mindful of Aculco’s customs and traditions.

  • Haggle excessively: While haggling is common in Mexico, haggling excessively can come across as rude to locals, so be mindful while negotiating prices.

Safety and Health

While Aculco is a safe and friendly town, travelers should take certain precautions to ensure their safety:

Safety in Aculco

  • Keep your valuables safe: Petty thefts can occur, so it is recommended to keep your valuables secure and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

  • Be cautious while driving: Aculco’s mountainous terrain can make driving challenging, so exercise caution while driving, especially at night.

  • Avoid traveling alone at night: While Aculco is generally safe, it is recommended to avoid traveling alone at night, and always take safe transportation options.

Health in Aculco

  • Stay hydrated: Aculco’s high altitude and sunny weather can cause dehydration, so ensure you drink plenty of water and carry a water bottle while exploring the town.

  • Get vaccinated: While Aculco is a generally healthy town, it is recommended to get vaccinated against diseases like hepatitis A and B, typhoid fever, and measles before traveling.

Getting to Aculco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aculco is easily accessible by air, bus, and car. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Aculco

  • Mexico City International Airport: Located 120 km away from Aculco, Mexico City International Airport is the closest airport with international and domestic connections.

  • Toluca International Airport: Located 140 km away from Aculco, Toluca International Airport offers domestic connections and is a popular choice for those traveling from within Mexico.

Bus Stations Near Aculco

  • Central de Autobuses de Tula: A bus station located 40 km away from Aculco that offers connections to various parts of Mexico.

  • Estrella de Oro: A bus company with various routes to Aculco from popular destinations like Mexico City and Puebla.

Other Options

  • Car rental: If you’re traveling with a group, renting a car is a good option, as it provides greater flexibility while exploring the town.

How to Get Around in Aculco

Aculco is a small town, and most of its attractions are easily accessible on foot. If you wish to explore the surroundings, taxis and rented bikes or cars are easily available.

Where to Go Next from Aculco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aculco is situated in a prime location that allows easy access to some of Mexico’s most beautiful destinations. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions:

  • San Miguel de Allende: A UNESCO World Heritage city with a vibrant art and culture scene, located around 200 km from Aculco.

  • Tepotzotlán: A picturesque colonial town known for its baroque architecture and impressive monastery, located around 80 km from Aculco.

  • Valle de Bravo: A scenic town perched on a lake and surrounded by mountains, offering outdoor activities like kayaking and paragliding. Located around 140 km from Aculco.

Conclusion

Aculco is a gem of a town that promises to leave you enthralled with its natural beauty, rich culture, mouth-watering cuisine, and friendly locals. With something for everyone, from history buffs to adventure seekers, Aculco is a must-visit destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town in Central Mexico.
