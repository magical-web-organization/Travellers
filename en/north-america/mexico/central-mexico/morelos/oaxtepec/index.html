Introduction to Oaxtepec

Nestled in the state of Morelos in Central Mexico, Oaxtepec is a small, charming town, known for its stunning natural beauty and diverse cultural attractions. This off-the-beaten-path destination offers visitors the chance to immerse themselves in Mexican culture, sample delicious local cuisine, and enjoy a range of outdoor activities.

Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or a short break, Oaxtepec has something for everyone. Read on for a comprehensive guide to Oaxtepec, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, top things to do, where to stay, and much more!

List of Places to Visit in Oaxtepec

There’s no shortage of things to do in Oaxtepec, from exploring the town’s rich cultural heritage to taking in its stunning natural beauty. Here are some of the must-visit places in and around Oaxtepec:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oaxtepec is home to many cultural attractions that showcase the region’s history and heritage. These are a few:

  • Ex-Convento de San Juan Bautista: This stunning 16th-century convent is one of the town’s most iconic landmarks and is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture.

  • Casa de la Cultura: Located just a few minutes away from the convent, the Casa de la Cultura is a cultural center that hosts a range of events and exhibitions throughout the year.

  • Museo de Arte Prehispánico Carlos Pellicer: This museum is home to a vast collection of pre-Columbian art and artifacts, providing a unique look into the region’s ancient history.

Natural Attractions of Oaxtepec: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love Oaxtepec’s stunning natural beauty. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Tepozteco National Park: This incredible park is home to an ancient Aztec temple, stunning mountain views, and a range of hiking and biking trails.

  • Las Estacas: This gorgeous ecotourism park is known for its crystal-clear waters, natural pools, and lush surrounding vegetation, making it the perfect destination for swimming and canoeing.

  • Hacienda Cocoyoc: This sprawling hacienda offers visitors the chance to relax, unwind, and soak in the beauty of its lush gardens and charming water features.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oaxtepec

If you’re looking to get some exercise while enjoying the natural beauty of Oaxtepec, there are plenty of great hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are a few of the best:

  • Tepozteco Mountain Trail: This challenging hike takes you up the Tepozteco Mountain to an ancient Aztec temple, providing stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • Cocoyoc to Yautepec Bike Route: This easy bike route takes you through some of the town’s charming streets, vibrant local markets, and stunning mountain views.

Best Things to Do in Oaxtepec: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Oaxtepec, from exploring the region’s cultural heritage to enjoying outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top things to do in the town:

  • Explore the Ex-Convento de San Juan Bautista and other historical landmarks.

  • Visit Las Estacas Ecotourism Park.

  • Hike to the ancient Tepozteco Temple in Tepozteco National Park.

  • Take a relaxing stroll through the beautiful gardens of Hacienda Cocoyoc.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oaxtepec

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Oaxtepec, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1-2: Explore Oaxtepec’s cultural attractions, including the Ex-Convento de San Juan Bautista and Casa de la Cultura.

Day 3: Visit Tepozteco National Park and hike to the ancient Aztec temple.

Day 4: Relax and unwind at Hacienda Cocoyoc.

Day 5: Explore Las Estacas Ecotourism Park.

Day 6-7: Take a bike tour of the town and surrounding areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Oaxtepec

No trip to Oaxtepec is complete without trying some of the town’s delicious traditional cuisine and refreshing local drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cochinita Pibil: This succulent pork dish is cooked in a traditional Mayan marinade and served with fresh tortillas.

  • Tamales: These savory pockets of corn masa are stuffed with a range of fillings and steamed in corn husks.

Local Beverages

  • Agua de Jamaica: This refreshing hibiscus tea is a staple in Mexican cuisine and is served chilled with a slice of lime.

  • Mezcal: Made from the agave plant, mezcal is a smoky-flavored spirit that’s traditionally consumed with a glass of orange slices and salt.

Where to Stay in Oaxtepec: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly option or a luxurious getaway, there are plenty of great accommodation options in Oaxtepec. Here are some of the best:

  • Hotel Hacienda Cocoyoc: This stunning hotel is located in a restored hacienda and offers visitors a range of luxurious amenities, including an on-site restaurant and spa.

  • Hotel Oaxaca: This charming mid-range hotel is located just a few minutes away from the Ex-Convento de San Juan Bautista and offers visitors a range of comfortable amenities, including an outdoor pool and spacious rooms.

  • Hostel Casa Tamarindo: This budget-friendly hostel is ideal for backpackers and features a range of communal spaces, including a shared kitchen and outdoor patio.

What to Buy in Oaxtepec: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home from Oaxtepec:

  • Handmade pottery: Oaxtepec is known for its traditional ceramics, including beautifully crafted bowls, plates, and vases.

  • Mezcal: This smoky-flavored spirit is the perfect way to bring a taste of Oaxtepec’s local culture home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Oaxtepec

Oaxtepec is a town with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some of the key customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Celebrating Dia de los Muertos: This Mexican holiday is celebrated every year on November 1st and 2nd and honors deceased loved ones with brightly colored altars and offerings.

  • Traditional dances and music: Oaxtepec is known for its vibrant traditional dance performances and live music, often featuring locally-made instruments like maracas and wooden flutes.

Best Time to Visit Oaxtepec

The best time to visit Oaxtepec is between the months of December and April when the weather is warm and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking and exploring the town’s cultural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oaxtepec

While Oaxtepec is a welcoming and friendly destination, there are some important do’s and don’ts to be aware of:

Do:

  • Respect the town’s cultural customs and traditions, including Dia de los Muertos and traditional dance performances.

  • Try the local cuisine, including cochinita pibil and tamales.

Don’t:

  • Engage in disrespectful or inappropriate behavior.

  • Take photos without asking permission.

Safety and Health

It’s essential to take the necessary precautions to ensure a safe and healthy trip to Oaxtepec:

Safety in Oaxtepec

  • Be vigilant when traveling alone or at night.

  • Keep your valuables secured and out of sight.

Health in Oaxtepec

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites by using insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing and pants.

Getting to Oaxtepec: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the best transportation options for getting to and around Oaxtepec:

Airports Near Oaxtepec

  • Mexico City International Airport (MEX): The closest airport to Oaxtepec, located roughly 110 kilometers away.

  • Cuernavaca International Airport (CVJ): Located approximately 30 kilometers away from Oaxtepec.

Bus Stations Near Oaxtepec

  • Autobuses Unidos: Located in the town center, this station offers buses to and from Mexico City and other local destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is an excellent option for travelers who want more flexibility and independence when exploring Oaxtepec and its surroundings.

  • Private transfer: Private transfers are available for travelers who prefer a more comfortable and hassle-free transportation option.

How to Get Around in Oaxtepec

There are several transportation options available for getting around in Oaxtepec, including taxis, buses, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Oaxtepec: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Central Mexico, here are some great nearby destinations to consider:

  • Cuernavaca: Known as the “City of Eternal Spring,” Cuernavaca is a stunning destination that’s known for its picturesque downtown and rich cultural heritage.

  • Tepoztlán: This quaint town is home to a beautiful 16th-century church, a vibrant arts and crafts market, and a range of hiking trails.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Oaxtepec offers something for everyone. We hope that this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Oaxtepec and explore everything that this charming town has to offer. Happy travels!
