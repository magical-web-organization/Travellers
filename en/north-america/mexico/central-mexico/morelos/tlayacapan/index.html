Introduction to Tlayacapan

Tlayacapan is a small and charming town located in the state of Morelos, in central Mexico. It is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture and rich cultural heritage, as well as its stunning natural surroundings. Tlayacapan is an ideal destination for those looking to experience Mexico’s history, culture, and outdoors while avoiding the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Tlayacapan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ex-Convent of San Juan Bautista

The Ex-Convent of San Juan Bautista is a beautiful 16th-century Franciscan monastery that was partially destroyed during the Mexican Revolution. Today, it houses a museum that showcases the history and art of the region, including pre-Hispanic artifacts, colonial paintings, and religious objects. The cloister and the church are particularly noteworthy for their intricate decoration and architecture.

Museo del Ferrocarril de Cuautla

The Museo del Ferrocarril de Cuautla is a railway museum that preserves the history of Mexico’s railroads. It displays a collection of antique locomotives, freight cars, and other train-related artifacts, as well as information about the railway industry and its impact on Mexican society. The museum is located in Cuautla, a nearby town that is easily accessible from Tlayacapan.

Casa de la Cultura

The Casa de la Cultura is a cultural center that hosts exhibitions, workshops, and performances related to the arts and culture of the Tlayacapan region. It is housed in a beautifully restored colonial building, and its courtyard is often used for concerts and other events. The center also has a library and a café.

Natural Attractions of Tlayacapan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parque Nacional El Tepozteco

Parque Nacional El Tepozteco is a protected natural area that covers over 22,000 hectares of mountains, forests, and streams. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several endangered species. The park’s main attraction is the Tepozteco pyramid, an ancient temple that sits atop a steep hill and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Las Estacas Natural Park

Las Estacas is a natural water park that features crystal-clear springs, natural pools, and lazy rivers. It is a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking, as well as for picnicking and camping in the lush vegetation. The park also has a restaurant, a souvenir shop, and a children’s playground.

Sierra de Montenegro

The Sierra de Montenegro is a mountain range that runs through Morelos and offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region. The trails wind through pine forests, rocky ridges, and scenic valleys, and are suitable for all levels of experience. Some of the most popular routes in the Sierra de Montenegro include La Pirámide, El Cerro del Jumil, and La Pera.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tlayacapan

Tlayacapan is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with its numerous hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of experience. Some of the most popular trails in the area are:

  • La Pirámide: A 3-hour hike to the top of the Tepozteco pyramid, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • El Cerro del Jumil: A 2-hour hike to the top of a rocky hill, with panoramic views of Tlayacapan and Cuautla.
  • La Pera: A challenging 6-hour hike to the top of a volcanic plug, with breathtaking views of the Sierra de Montenegro.
  • Ruta de las Artesanías: A 25-kilometer bike ride that passes through several nearby towns known for their handicrafts, such as Oaxtepec and Yautepec.
  • Ruta de los Conventos: A 40-kilometer bike ride that visits several colonial-era monasteries and churches, including the Ex-Convent of San Juan Bautista in Tlayacapan.

Best Things to Do in Tlayacapan: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the cobblestone streets and colonial buildings of Tlayacapan’s historic center, particularly the Ex-Convent of San Juan Bautista and the Casa de la Cultura.
  • Visit Parque Nacional El Tepozteco and climb to the top of the Tepozteco pyramid for stunning views of the valley.
  • Swim and relax at Las Estacas Natural Park, and enjoy the park’s many amenities, including restaurants, shops, and children’s activities.
  • Take a biking or hiking tour of the Sierra de Montenegro with a local guide, and discover the region’s natural beauty and hidden treasures.
  • Attend a traditional fiesta or cultural event in Tlayacapan, such as the Carnaval or the Day of the Dead celebrations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tlayacapan

Day 1: Arrive in Tlayacapan and explore the historic center, visiting the Ex-Convent of San Juan Bautista and the Casa de la Cultura.

Day 2: Take a guided hiking tour of Parque Nacional El Tepozteco, and climb to the top of the Tepozteco pyramid for panoramic views of the valley.

Day 3: Visit nearby Cuautla and tour the Museo del Ferrocarril de Cuautla, learning about Mexico’s railway history and its impact on society.

Day 4: Spend the day at Las Estacas Natural Park, swimming, snorkeling, and relaxing in the lush vegetation.

Day 5: Take a bike tour of the Ruta de los Conventos, visiting several colonial-era monasteries and churches in the region.

Day 6: Explore the Sierra de Montenegro on a guided hiking tour, discovering its natural beauty and hidden treasures.

Day 7: Attend a traditional fiesta or cultural event in Tlayacapan, such as the Carnaval or the Day of the Dead celebrations.

Food and Drink Scene of Tlayacapan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbacoa: Slow-cooked lamb or beef, traditionally prepared underground with a blend of spices and herbs.
  • Chiles en nogada: Stuffed poblano peppers with a creamy walnut sauce, typically served during the Independence Day celebrations in September.
  • Sopes: Thick, fried tortillas topped with beans, meat, cheese, and salsa.
  • Tlayacapan-style enchiladas: Rolled tortillas filled with chicken or cheese and covered in a mild tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: A milky, fermented drink made from the sap of the maguey plant, with a slightly sour and sweet flavor.
  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from agave, with a smoky and earthy flavor.
  • Tequila: A type of mezcal made specifically from blue agave, with a sweeter and smoother taste.

Where to Stay in Tlayacapan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hacienda Cocoyoc: A luxurious resort with spacious, colonial-style rooms, multiple swimming pools, and a golf course.
  • Casa del Angel Tlayacapan: A charming guesthouse with colorful rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a central location in the historic center.
  • Casa Tlayacapan: A cozy hostel with shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a garden patio.

What to Buy in Tlayacapan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Tlayacapan is known for its pottery, woodcarvings, and embroidery, which make for unique and authentic souvenirs.
  • Mezcal and tequila: Take home a bottle of these delicious and traditional Mexican beverages from a local distillery.
  • Honey and sweets: Morelos is famous for its sweet, natural honey, as well as its candy and pastries made from sugar and fruit.

Local Culture and Customs of Tlayacapan

Tlayacapan has a strong and vibrant cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, art, and traditions. The town is particularly famous for its lively Carnaval celebrations, which take place in February and feature colorful parades, costumes, and music. Tlayacapan is also known for its gastronomy, which blends pre-Hispanic and colonial influences in dishes like barbacoa, chiles en nogada, and sopa de piedra.

Best Time to Visit Tlayacapan

The best time to visit Tlayacapan is during the dry season, from November to April, when the weather is sunny and mild. The town can get crowded during the peak tourist season (December to February), so it’s advisable to plan your trip in advance and book accommodations early. The Carnaval in February is a particularly busy time, but also a unique and exciting cultural experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tlayacapan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the regional cuisine and support local businesses.
  • Take a guided tour of the natural and cultural attractions to learn more about the region’s history and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any illegal activities, such as drug use or trafficking.
  • Disrespect the natural environment, and follow the rules and regulations of the parks and protected areas.
  • Drink tap water, and be cautious when eating street food or drinking from unfamiliar sources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tlayacapan

Tlayacapan is a relatively safe town, but as with any travel destination, visitors should exercise caution and common sense. It’s advisable to avoid walking alone at night, particularly in secluded areas, and to keep valuables and documents in a secure place. The local police and emergency services are generally reliable, but it’s a good idea to have a copy of important documents and emergency phone numbers.

Health in Tlayacapan

Morelos has a moderate risk of contracting mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever, Zika, and chikungunya, particularly during the rainy season (May to October). Visitors should use mosquito repellent, wear long sleeves and pants, and sleep under mosquito nets when possible. It’s also advisable to drink bottled water and avoid eating raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Tlayacapan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tlayacapan

The closest international airport to Tlayacapan is Mexico City International Airport, which is about 90 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or shuttle to Tlayacapan, which takes about 2 hours.

Bus Stations Near Tlayacapan

Tlayacapan has a bus station that services both local and regional routes. There are frequent buses from Cuernavaca and Mexico City, as well as nearby towns like Oaxtepec and Yautepec.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or private transportation to get to Tlayacapan from nearby cities or towns.

How to Get Around in Tlayacapan

Tlayacapan is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Buses and taxis are also available for longer distances or for reaching natural attractions outside of the town.

Where to Go Next from Tlayacapan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cuernavaca: The “city of eternal spring” is a bustling metropolis known for its historic architecture, museums, and gardens.
  • Tequesquitengo: This nearby lake resort offers aquatic activities, adventure sports, and comfortable accommodations.
  • Taxco: The “silver city” is a picturesque colonial town famous for its silver shops and markets.

Conclusion

Tlayacapan is a hidden gem in the heart of Mexico, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a hiking enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Tlayacapan has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover this charming town for yourself!
