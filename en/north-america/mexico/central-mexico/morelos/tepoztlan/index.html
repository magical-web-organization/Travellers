Introduction to Tepoztlan

Tepoztlan is a charming town located in the Morelos state of Central Mexico. Famous for its ancient Aztec pyramid, Tepozteco, and surrounded by stunning natural beauty, Tepoztlan is a popular destination among both locals and tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Tepoztlan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tepozteco Pyramid - A historic Aztec pyramid that offers incredible views of the city and scenic surroundings.
  • Museum Carlos Pellicer - Featuring collections of pre-Hispanic art and artifacts, colonial-era paintings, and contemporary art exhibitions.
  • Ex-Convento de la Natividad - A stunning 16th-century church with an intricate baroque altar and beautiful frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Tepoztlan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tepozteco National Park - A national park with hiking trails, waterfalls, and diverse fauna and flora.
  • El Tepozteco Trail - A steep but rewarding trekking trail to the Aztec pyramid, providing unparalleled views of the city and countryside.
  • Las Estacas Natural Water Park - A picturesque water park with crystal clear waters, waterslides, and beautiful river beds for swimming and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tepoztlan

  • Tepozteco Trail - A challenging but rewarding hike to the ancient Aztec Pyramid.
  • Los Venados Trail - Known for its scenic beauty and wildlife, this trail leads up to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Biking Trail to the Amatlan Dam - A stunning bike ride that takes you past small towns, farms, and breathtaking mountain views.

Best Things to Do in Tepoztlan: Activities and Tours

  • Bristol Balloon Fiesta - A fabulous annual festival featuring hot air balloons in different shapes and colors.
  • San Francisco de Asis Festivity - A colorful celebration honoring Saint Francis of Assisi featuring parades, fireworks, and religious ceremonies.
  • Temazcal Ritual - An ancient Aztec ritual of physical and spiritual cleansing in a sweat lodge, performed by a native shaman.

Food and Drink Scene of Tepoztlan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tepoznieves - A delicious local ice cream made with fresh fruit and natural ingredients.
  • Tlacoyos - A popular street food made with masa (a corn dough) and stuffed with beans, cheese, or meat, served with delicious salsa.
  • Cecina - A local dish made from thinly sliced and grilled beef or pork, served with fresh tortillas and salsa.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque - A traditional Mexican drink made from fermented agave juice.
  • Mezcal - A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the maguey plant.
  • Tequila - A distilled beverage made from the blue agave plant.

Where to Stay in Tepoztlan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Amomoxtli - A stylish and luxurious hotel with a beautiful pool and serene atmosphere.
  • Posada del Tepozteco - A charming hotel located at the foot of the Tepozteco Pyramid.
  • Hostal de la Luz - A peaceful retreat with beautiful gardens, a pool, and spa services.

What to Buy in Tepoztlan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tepoztlan’s Handmade Crafts - Beautiful handmade pottery, textiles, and other handicrafts made by local artisans.
  • Tepoztlan’s Jewelry - Unique and intricate jewelry made with silver, precious stones, and other materials.
  • Tepoztlan’s Handmade Soaps - Beautifully crafted soaps made with natural ingredients and local herbs.

Local Culture and Customs of Tepoztlan

Tepoztlan is a town rich in cultural traditions and customs. Here are some of them:

  • Dances of the Chinelos - A colorful dance that originated in Tepoztlan and is performed during the Carnivals.
  • Fiestas de San Isidro - A religious festival honoring the patron saint of farmers and crops, celebrated in May with processions, pilgrimages, and food offerings.
  • Day of the Dead - A traditional Mexican holiday honoring deceased loved ones, celebrated on November 1st and 2nd with altars, offerings, and processions.

Best Time to Visit Tepoztlan

The best time to visit Tepoztlan is from November to April when the weather is milder and more comfortable. However, it’s important to note that the town is relatively busy year-round, especially during festivals and holidays, so you should book accommodations in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tepoztlan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Explore the beautiful nature and national parks.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission.
  • Disrespect religious symbols and traditions.
  • Litter in the streets or natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tepoztlan

Tepoztlan is a relatively safe town, but as with any destination, it’s important to take precautions to avoid theft and scams. Here are some tips:

  • Use official taxis or transportation services.
  • Keep your valuables with you at all times.
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Tepoztlan

Tepoztlan is a safe and healthy destination. However, it’s important to use common sense when it comes to hygiene and food. Here are some tips:

  • Drink only bottled water.
  • Wash your hands frequently.
  • Avoid street food that looks unappetizing or undercooked.

Getting to Tepoztlan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Tepoztlan is easy, as the town is located close to major cities and attractions in the region. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Tepoztlan

  • Mexico City International Airport
  • Cuernavaca International Airport

Bus Stations Near Tepoztlan

  • Pullman de Morelos
  • Estrella de Oro

Other Options

  • Private transportation and tours
  • Taxis and rental cars

How to Get Around in Tepoztlan

Getting around Tepoztlan is easy, as the town is relatively small and walkable. Taxis and local buses are also available for transportation to other neighboring areas.

Where to Go Next from Tepoztlan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

*Tepoztlan is located in a prime location, making it easy to explore nearby destinations and attractions:

  • Cuernavaca - Known as the “City of Eternal Spring,” Cuernavaca is a charming colonial city with beautiful architecture, museums, and gardens.

  • Xochicalco - An impressive Mayan archaeological site located about 28 miles from Tepoztlan, featuring impressive pyramids and buildings.

  • Mexico City - The bustling capital of Mexico, located about 46 miles north of Tepoztlan, offering countless museums, galleries, historic sites, and popular attractions.

Conclusion

Tepoztlan is a picturesque town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and recreational adventures. Whether you’re looking to hike to the top of an ancient pyramid, relax in hot springs, or try delicious local cuisine, Tepoztlan has something for everyone. Now that you know all the information and tips on what to do and where to go, you can start planning your unforgettable trip to this magical destination in Mexico.
