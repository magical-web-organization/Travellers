Introduction to Morelos

Located in Central Mexico, Morelos is a state in which visitors can find a mix of colonial and pre-Hispanic history, as well as natural beauty. The capital of the state is Cuernavaca, and the region is known for its cultural sites, stunning gardens, and outdoor adventures. This travel guide will take you through the top things to do and see in Morelos, including a list of places to visit, the best hiking and biking routes, where to stay, and local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Morelos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palace of Cortes: This iconic palace is the site where the Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés once lived and ruled over the city of Cuernavaca. Visitors can see a range of archaeological artifacts from the region, as well as colonial-era paintings.

  • Robert Brady Museum: This museum was once the home of American artist Robert Brady. Visitors will appreciate the unique collections of art and artifacts from across the world, including Pre-Hispanic, African, and Asian.

  • Tepozteco Archaeological Site: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located in the town of Tepoztlán and contains the remains of a pyramid used for ceremonial purposes by the Aztecs.

  • Jardín Borda: The Jardín Borda is a stunning garden that is located in the heart of Cuernavaca and was once owned by the Spanish viceroy José de la Borda. Visitors can explore the gardens, fountains, and sculptures on the site.

Natural Attractions of Morelos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Las Estacas: This natural water park is perfect for a day of outdoor adventure. Visitors can zip line, swim in natural pools, and go on a river rafting excursion.

  • Lagunas de Zempoala National Park: This national park is located in the highlands of Morelos and is known for its stunning views of mountains, forests, and lakes. Visitors can hike through the park’s trails, go bird watching, or have a picnic.

  • El Tepozteco National Park and Pyramid: This park is located in the town of Tepoztlán and is known for its beautiful views of the surrounding mountains. The park also contains the remains of an Aztec temple, the Tepozteco Pyramid, which visitors can hike up to.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morelos

  • Barranca de Amanalco: This hiking and biking route takes visitors through a deep canyon in the town of Amanalco. Visitors can see waterfalls, streams, and vegetation unique to the area.

  • Cerro del Judio: This hiking route takes visitors up to the Cerro del Judio summit, where they can see stunning views of Morelos and the neighboring state of Mexico.

Best Things to Do in Morelos: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Visitors can take authentic Mexican cooking classes in Cuernavaca and learn how to make traditional dishes like mole and chiles en nogada.

  • Temazcal: This is a traditional Mexican sweat lodge experience that combines heat therapy, herbs, and steam to detoxify the body. Visitors can find this experience in many places throughout Morelos.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque landscapes of Morelos, with stunning views of mountains, forests, and lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of Morelos

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chiles en Nogada: This is a patriotic Mexican dish made with poblano peppers filled with a mixture of meat and fruits, covered in a walnut sauce and topped with pomegranate seeds. It is typically served during Mexican Independence Day celebrations.

  • Mole Moreliano: This is a type of mole sauce made with chili peppers, spices, chocolate, and nuts. It is typically served over chicken or pork.

  • Tacos de Carnitas: These are tacos made with slow-cooked pork, served with diced onions, cilantro, lime, and salsa. They are a Mexican street food favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: This Mexican alcoholic beverage is made with blue agave and is a staple for celebrations and parties.

  • Mezcal: Similar to tequila, mezcal is a spirit made from agave, but is known for its smoky flavor.

  • Atole: This is a traditional Mexican hot beverage made with masa dough, water, and sugar, often flavored with cinnamon or chocolate. It is typically served during breakfast or as a snack.

Where to Stay in Morelos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Las Mañanitas: This luxury hotel in Cuernavaca offers beautiful gardens, a spa, and a swimming pool.

  • Convento de la Luz: This 17th-century convent turned boutique hotel in Tepoztlán offers stunning views of the town and surrounding mountains.

  • Hostal de la Luz: This eco-friendly hotel in Amatlán de Quetzalcóatl offers guests comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and sustainability tours.

What to Buy in Morelos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Talavera Pottery: This type of hand-painted ceramic pottery originated in the city of Puebla, but can also be found in Morelos.

  • Handcrafted Textiles: The town of Tlayacapan is known for its traditional hand-woven textiles made by local artisans.

  • Charro Hats: This type of cowboy hat is a traditional piece of Mexican attire and can be found in many shops throughout Morelos.

Local Culture and Customs of Morelos

Morelos is a state with a rich cultural heritage, and its people are proud of their history and traditions. Visitors can expect to see festivities and celebrations throughout the year, including the Day of the Dead, Carnival, and Mexican Independence Day. The state also has a strong presence of indigenous communities, who continue to preserve their cultural heritage through language, dance, and music.

Best Time to Visit Morelos

The best time to visit Morelos is in the winter months, between December and February, when the climate is mild and pleasant. However, visitors can also enjoy the state’s attractions during the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures tend to be higher and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morelos

Do:

  • Do try traditional Mexican food, including dishes like chiles en nogada, mole, and tacos de carnitas.

  • Do learn some basic Spanish phrases, as it will help you navigate the region and communicate with locals.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions, like taking your shoes off before entering a home or not wearing a hat inside a church.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, as it is not safe for consumption. Stick to bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.

  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen or a hat, as the sun can be intense during the day.

  • Don’t haggle over prices too much, as it is not common practice in Morelos.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morelos

Morelos is generally a safe state to visit, but visitors should take standard precautions to avoid becoming a victim of petty crime. This includes being aware of your surroundings, not flashing valuables, and avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Morelos

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, like dengue fever and Zika virus. This includes wearing insect repellent and protective clothing, and staying in accommodations with screens on windows and doors.

Getting to Morelos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morelos

The closest international airport to Morelos is Mexico City International Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus, taxi, or rental car to reach their destination in Morelos.

Bus Stations Near Morelos

The bus station in Cuernavaca is the main hub for long-distance buses, with connections to many cities in Mexico.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Morelos from nearby cities like Mexico City, Puebla, and Toluca.

How to Get Around in Morelos

The best way to get around Morelos is by rental car or taxi. Public transportation is available, but it can be crowded and slow at times. Visitors can also explore some areas on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Morelos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mexico City: The capital city of Mexico is just a short drive from Morelos and offers visitors a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, as well as delicious food and shopping opportunities.

  • Puebla: Known for its colonial architecture and cuisine, Puebla is a vibrant city located just over two hours from Morelos.

Conclusion

Morelos is a state that offers visitors a mix of cultural, natural, and adventure tourism opportunities. From exploring the historic sites in Cuernavaca to hiking through the natural parks of Tepoztlán, there is something for everyone in this region of Mexico. Visitors can expect to find delicious food, friendly locals, and a rich cultural heritage that is unique to Morelos.
