Introduction to El Chico National Park

El Chico National Park is a nature reserve located in Hidalgo in Central Mexico. Established in 1982, the park covers 2,739 hectares and is home to various plant and animal species, as well as culturally important sites. Its unique geographic features like the monolithic rock formations, caves, and canyons make it a popular destination for hikers, climbers, nature lovers, and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in El Chico National Park

Whether you’re a local or tourist, El Chico National Park offers plenty to see and experience. To help you plan your trip, here’s a list of places to visit in El Chico National Park.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Culture vultures will love El Chico National Park as it’s home to several culturally significant sites. There are:

  1. The House of Culture Museum, an old building that was once a Jesuit monastery.
  2. The San Miguelito Hacienda, a historical site where you can learn about the park’s mining past and the relationship between Haciendas and Natural Reservations.
  3. The Indigenous Site of Huamantla, an archeological site that’s located on the park’s western border which is fully accessible.
  4. The Jesuits Aqueduct - a true architectural wonder, you’ll need to plan your visit carefully to ensure you see it.

Natural Attractions of El Chico National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Witness the untouched beauty of the national park with its natural attractions that cater to outdoor adventurers and nature lovers alike, here are the places to visit:

  1. Los Frailes Canyon - lose yourself in the striking beauty of the canyons and streams.
  2. Espinazo del Diablo - a beautifully unique ridge that offers some of the most breathtaking views of the Mexican landscape.
  3. Basaltic Prisms - a spectacular wonder where basalt columns were formed by cooling multiple lava layers.
  4. El Chico Peak - for those looking to challenge themselves physically, the El Chico peak offers a challenging climb but when you get to the top the view is worth it.
  5. Paseo de las Hamacas - consider taking a relaxing walk along the hammock walkway, perfect for taking in the scenery and enjoying the fresh air.
  6. Álamo Plateau - Crevices and gorges come alive along this scenic spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Chico National Park

Hiking and biking trails abound in El Chico National Park, offering varying degrees of difficulty and distance. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Los Pinos Trail - beginner level 2.1km/1.31m trail perfect for hiking or mountain biking.
  2. Los Cuartos Trail - an intermediate 8.5km/5.3m trail with beautiful viewpoints, especially around Mirador del Fraile.
  3. Peña del Cuervo Trail - a difficult, steep trail and an estimated total of 4 hours hiking time, but the views along the way make it worth it.
  4. Camino de las Herraduras Trail - a moderate 4.5km/2.7m trail. The majority of the route is downhill against a backdrop of beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in El Chico National Park: Activities and Tours

El Chico National Park is an outdoor haven offering visitors an array of exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in El Chico:

  1. Extreme sports - try your hand at rappelling, zip-lining through the treetops, or rock climbing.
  2. Environmental education and guided tours: You can book a tour company or guide to help you explore the park who will introduce you to the local flora and fauna species and show you breathtaking vistas.
  3. Horseback riding - take the scenic route on horseback with a guide or rental horses available in the park.
  4. Camping - experience the ultimate outdoor adventure by sleeping under the stars and enjoying the tranquility of your surroundings.
  5. Picnic - grab a picnic basket and head to one of the many picnic areas located in the park and enjoy a relaxing day in a unique environment.

Food and Drink Scene of El Chico National Park

Nothing completes a trip to El Chico National Park better than experiencing the local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Barbacoa Steamed Meat and Stew - available in nearby towns where you can find it party every weekend. It’s tender meat and savory flavor will leave you wanting more.
  2. Handmade Corn Tortillas - a staple food in Mexico, these tortillas are made fresh daily and are a perfect addition to any meal.

Local Beverages

  1. Tequila - a famed Mexican spirit, tequila is a popular choice among locals and tourists alike. Be sure to tour a distillery and sample some before leaving Mexico.
  2. Pulque - a fermented drink similar to kefir, it has a creamy texture and pleasant yeast flavor that may not be for everyone but definitely worth a try.
  3. Atóle - made from corn, sugar, and spices. It’s served warm and is a popular drink in the area during the cold season.

Where to Stay in El Chico National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

El Chico National Park offers various types of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels and camping to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations for lodgings:

  1. El Año Lodge - a rustic cabin-style hotel with comfortable beds and hot water. It’s popular with hikers but lacks charging points and Wi-Fi.
  2. Hotel Huracán - a family-friendly hotel located in the heart of the national park. Enjoy the onsite restaurant, huge gardens, and cozy rooms.
  3. Comunal Hostel - this eco-friendly hostel offers comfortable beds, high speed Wi-Fi, and a peaceful atmosphere. It’s affordable and perfect for backpackers.

What to Buy in El Chico National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of El Chico National Park home with you by purchasing souvenirs and local products from the park. Here are some of the things you may want to consider buying:

  1. Artisanal Items - native hand-crafted embroidery, artwork, and textiles are especially popular souvenirs to take home from the park.
  2. Silver from Mineral del Monte - located nearby, the handicrafts are a wonderful keepsake in memory of your trip to Mexico.

Local Culture and Customs of El Chico National Park

El Chico National Park’s local culture is a mosaic of traditions and customs, reflective of Mexico’s diverse history. To respect the park’s culture and customs, here are some things to keep in mind:

  1. Use simple, basic Spanish words as much as possible
  2. It’s customary to greet people with a “buen día/tarde/noche” before asking anything.
  3. Speak in a moderate or low voice, especially when you are entering local communities as locals tend to appreciate peacefulness and calmness.
  4. Don’t touch any ancient ruins or monuments.

Best Time to Visit El Chico National Park

El Chico National Park can be visited all year round as it has a moderate climate. The best times to visit are from October to May when there are fewer crowds and the weather conditions are pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Chico National Park

Following some basic guidelines can go a long way in enjoying your trip to El Chico National Park.

Do:

  1. Carry plenty of water, as temperatures can rise throughout the day, especially during summer, and there’s no drinking water supply.
  2. Wear comfortable, sturdy shoes that provide good support for hiking or walking. Having insect repellent and sunscreen easily accessible is essential too.
  3. Follow the park’s guidelines while camping or enjoying recreational activities in the reserve.
  4. Ask the locals for directions; they’re usually happy to help.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter in the park; bring all trash from your visit for proper disposal.
  2. Don’t disturb the natural environment or the wildlife in the park.
  3. Don’t go off-trail in the park; it’s easy to get lost and disturb the conservationist efforts.

Safety and Health

Visitors to El Chico National Park should keep safety and health guidelines in mind. Here are some tips that can help you enjoy your trip without any worries:

Safety in El Chico National Park

  1. Take caution when hiking or exploring the park’s rugged landscape. Carry a first-aid kit and a phone at all times for emergencies.
  2. Don’t hike off-trail, as it’s easy to get lost in rough areas and rocky terrain.
  3. Be aware of sudden changes in the weather which can impede your progress or intensity of your excursion.
  4. When driving keep an eye out for changing road conditions in the park.
  5. Avoid hiking alone, as the park’s remote location can make getting help in an emergency difficult.

Health in El Chico National Park

  1. Dehydration can be a problem, carry fluids, water must be part of your packed items.
  2. Apply sunscreen and insect repellent when you are spending a lot of time outdoors.
  3. If you have allergies, carry antihistamines or other necessary medication at all times.

Getting to El Chico National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

El Chico National Park is located in Central Mexico and is well-connected to the major cities in the region. Visitors can use the following transportation options to reach the park:

Airports Near El Chico National Park

The closest airport is Pachuca Airport, which is approx 36 km from the National Park. The nearest major airport is Mexico City International Airport, which is 150 km away from the park.

Bus Stations Near El Chico National Park

Buses are available from larger cities in the region. There are several stations nearby like Pachuca Central Bus Station, which is 26 km from the park.

Other Options

There are various transportation services available once you arrive to El Chico National Park like taxis and local tour companies which can also take you to neighboring attractions or towns.

How to Get Around in El Chico National Park

El Chico National Park is vast, making transportation options within the park limited. The park can be explored on foot, and cycling or mountain biking excursions are also popular. Many areas within the park are accessible by vehicles and there are auto tours that ford the needs of people with disabilities. There are signs to help guide your way but it’s important to carry a map or have a park guide that you can consult during your trip.

Where to Go Next from El Chico National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

El Chico National Park’s central location makes it easy to explore other destinations nearby. Here are some suggestions:

  1. The Peña de Bernal - located east of the National park, it’s a well-known regional attraction with beautiful landscapes, especially rock formations.
  2. The Real del Monte, a colonial mining town, home to numerous small silver mines and a beautiful downtown.
  3. Huasca de Ocampo, a unique town located South-Eastern of the National park, it’s known for its pastoral sceneries, hothouse flowers and La Hacienda San Miguel Regla.

Conclusion

El Chico National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to El Chico National Park with ease, from the best things to do, accommodations, travel guidance and recommended sights to see. There’s something for everyone in El Chico, whether you prefer to appreciate cultural and historical landmarks, hiking and biking, tour the outdoors, or taste the local cuisine. Visit El Chico National Park today and make memories that will last a lifetime.
