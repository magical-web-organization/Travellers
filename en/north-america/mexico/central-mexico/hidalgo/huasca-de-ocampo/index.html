Huasca de Ocampo is a picturesque village nestled in the heart of Mexico’s Hidalgo state. Known for its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant food scene, Huasca de Ocampo is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Mexican experience. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Huasca de Ocampo and make the most out of your visit.

Introduction to Huasca de Ocampo

Located in Central Mexico, Huasca de Ocampo is a small town in the Sierra Madre Oriental mountain range, about two hours away from Mexico City. Huasca de Ocampo is famous for its natural wonders, including the waterfalls, canyons, and panoramic views. It is also known for its rich cultural heritage, which includes colonial architecture, museums, and archaeological sites.

List of Places to Visit in Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo is a destination that caters to both nature lovers and history buffs. Here are some of the places you shouldn’t miss on your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hacienda San Miguel Regla is a beautifully restored 17th-century hacienda, surrounded by lush gardens and fountains. The hacienda has a museum that showcases the history, architecture, and lifestyle of the estate.
  • Ex-Hacienda de Santa Maria Regla is a historic estate with a beautiful baroque-style church and gardens. It is home to the Museo de los Duendes (Museum of Goblins), which explores the local legends of magical creatures.
  • The Prismas Basalticos are towering hexagonal columns of volcanic rock that create a breathtaking natural monument. It is situated on the outskirts of the town and makes for a great day trip.

Natural Attractions of Huasca de Ocampo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Los Prismas Basalticos is a stunning natural wonder of hexagonal-shaped columns of volcanic rock. You can walk around the basalt formations and hear the sound of the waterfall that flows through them.
  • Parque Nacional El Chico is a vast natural park that offers hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails. The park is known for its rugged terrain, evergreen forests, waterfalls, and scenic views.
  • Barranca de Aguacatitla is a canyon that provides breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valley. It is an excellent spot for hiking, rock climbing, and rappelling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo has an abundance of outdoor activities for adventurers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Huasca de Ocampo:

  • The San Miguel Regla and Santa Maria Regla Hike would take you through the beautiful countryside of Huasca de Ocampo, where you’ll catch glimpses of the stunning ex-haciendas and the waterfalls.
  • The Hike to the Tres Diamantes takes you to a peak, from which you can witness the harmonious coupling of valley and mountains.
  • The El Chico National Park Biking Tour is an excellent way to explore the park’s rugged landscapes and forests. One can also experience rock climbing, rappelling, and camping.

Best Things to Do in Huasca de Ocampo: Activities and Tours

Apart from the places to visit and hike/bike routes, Huasca de Ocampo offers other experiences. Here are the best things you can do on your visit:

  • Try the regional food and drink scene of Huasca de Ocampo, including the must-try dishes and local beverages.
  • Visit the archeological sites surrounding Huasca de Ocampo.
  • Explore the countryside by taking a hot air balloon ride or a picturesque horseback ride.
  • Learn about sustainable agriculture and try your hand at picking your herbs at one of the farms in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo is famous for its rich food and drinks scene, based on the staples of the cuisine of Central Mexico. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gorditas - A small, stuffed tortilla that can be eaten as a snack or a meal.
  • Chalupas - A fried tortilla topped with meat, beans, cheese, and salsa.
  • Escamoles - The larvae of ants, considered a delicacy in Mexico.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque - A fermented drink made from the sap of maguey cactus.
  • Tejate - A cold drink made from corn masa, cocoa beans, and flowers.
  • Mezcal - A distilled spirit that is similar to tequila but has a smokier flavor.

Where to Stay in Huasca de Ocampo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huasca de Ocampo offers a wide range of accommodation options. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • La Casa de los Duendes - A charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable and cozy accommodations surrounded by natural beauty.
  • Hotel Casas Cariñanflas - A boutique hotel located in the heart of Huasca de Ocampo that features rustic décor and warm ambiance.
  • Mision San Miguel Regla - A four-star hotel set in a restored 17th-century hacienda that features a heated outdoor pool, spa, and a restaurant serving traditional Mexican cuisine.

What to Buy in Huasca de Ocampo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Huasca de Ocampo has many local products that are a must-try. Here are some of the best things to buy and bring home:

  • Woven Rugs - Colorful rugs handwoven from local wool are famous in the region and make for great souvenirs.
  • Jams and Preserves - Huasca de Ocampo is home to many small businesses that make preserves and jams from regional fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo is mostly rural, and the locals are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. The town is known for celebrating the Día de Muertos, or the Day of the Dead, Mexico’s most significant annual event.

Best Time to Visit Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo can be visited any time of the year. The best time to visit is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. During this time, you can enjoy the outdoors and explore the countryside without getting drenched in rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huasca de Ocampo

Do:

  • Be adventurous with your food choices and try the local cuisine.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Do not engage with drug dealers or illegal activities.
  • Do not litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo is a safe destination to visit. However, it is best to avoid venturing out alone after dark and keep your valuables secure.

Health in Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo is a relatively safe and healthy destination. It is recommended that you take precautions such as drinking only bottled water and carrying sunscreen to prevent any sunburns.

Getting to Huasca de Ocampo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options and logistics information for getting to Huasca de Ocampo:

Airports Near Huasca de Ocampo

The closest airport to Huasca de Ocampo is Mexico City International Airport, which is about two hours away.

Bus Stations Near Huasca de Ocampo

The bus service in Mexico is efficient and covers Huasca de Ocampo. The nearest bus stations are in Pachuca and Tulancingo, which are about 30 minutes away.

Other Options

Renting a car in Mexico is highly recommended as it gives you the flexibility to explore the countryside and visit nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Huasca de Ocampo

The best way to get around Huasca de Ocampo is by car. You can also hire a taxi or take a bus to visit the nearby attractions. Walking and biking are great options for exploring the town center and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Huasca de Ocampo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can plan from Huasca de Ocampo:

  • Pachuca - A historic city that is famous for its mineral springs and the Museo del Rehilete.
  • Real del Monte - A picturesque village that is known for its silver mines and colonial architecture.
  • Tulancingo - The second-largest city in the state, which is known for its beautiful parks and gardens.

Conclusion

Huasca de Ocampo is a destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, culture, and adventure. It is a destination that should be on any traveler’s bucket list who is seeking an authentic and offbeat Mexican experience. We hope our comprehensive guide has helped you plan your visit to Huasca de Ocampo and enjoy all that this charming village has to offer.
