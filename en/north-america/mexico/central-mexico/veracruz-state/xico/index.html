Introduction to Xico

Xico is a picturesque town nestled in the mountains of Veracruz, Mexico. It is known for its colonial architecture, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural scenery. Xico offers visitors a range of activities, from hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical landmarks. The town is also known for its delicious food and drink scene, with many local specialties to try. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Xico, so you can make the most of your time there.

List of Places to Visit in Xico

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Casa de la Estrella. This museum is housed in a former convent and features exhibits on the town’s history and indigenous cultures.
  • El Palacio Municipal. This historic town hall is a beautiful example of colonial architecture and boasts a stunning courtyard and a small museum.
  • El Templo de Santa María Magdalena. This beautiful church was built in the 18th century and features stunning baroque architecture.

Natural Attractions of Xico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cascada de Texolo. This stunning waterfall is surrounded by lush forest and is a popular spot for hiking and picnics.
  • Cerro de la Cruz. This hill offers breathtaking views of the town and surrounding countryside and is popular for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Reserva Ecológica de la Cuenca del Río Blanco. This natural reserve is home to a variety of flora and fauna and offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xico

  • The Waterfall Circuit. This route takes visitors through several of the town’s stunning waterfalls, including Cascada de Texolo and Cascada de Xico.
  • Cerro de la Cruz Trail. This hiking trail takes visitors up the hill to the famous cross, offering stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Rio de Oro Trail. This trail takes visitors along the river and through the stunning forested landscape of the area.

Best Things to Do in Xico: Activities and Tours

  • Coffee Tour. Xico is known for its coffee production, and visitors can take a tour of local coffee plantations and learn about the process of growing and roasting coffee.
  • Horseback Riding. Visitors can take a guided horseback ride through the scenic countryside and forested trails around Xico.
  • Chocolate Making. Visitors can take a class on how to make traditional Mexican chocolate, using local ingredients and techniques.

Food and Drink Scene of Xico

Xico is famous for its delicious food and drink scene. Visitors can expect to find a range of local specialties, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chilatole. A traditional dish made from masa, chicken, and a spicy chili sauce.
  • Tamales. A staple of Mexican cuisine, these tasty treats are made from masa dough and filled with a variety of ingredients.
  • Mole. A rich sauce made from chili peppers, chocolate, and spices, served over chicken or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Café de Olla. A traditional Mexican coffee made with cinnamon, sugar, and piloncillo cane sugar.
  • Agua de Sabor. A refreshing fruit drink made from local fruits such as jamaica and tamarind.
  • Mezcal. A traditional Mexican spirit made from the agave plant, similar to tequila but with a smokier flavor.

Where to Stay in Xico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Casa Azul. A charming guesthouse with colorful rooms and a beautiful courtyard.
  • Posada del Café. A boutique hotel with unique rooms decorated in a traditional Mexican style.
  • Hostal Casa Maguey. A cozy hostel with a communal kitchen and a rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Xico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coffee. Xico is known for its delicious coffee, and visitors can purchase bags of locally grown beans to take home.
  • Handmade Crafts. Xico is home to a variety of artisan producers, offering a range of handmade products such as pottery, textiles, and jewelry.
  • Mezcal. Visitors can purchase bottles of locally distilled mezcal to take home as a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Xico

Xico has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong indigenous influence. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as the Day of the Dead celebrations, which are an important part of the town’s cultural identity.

Best Time to Visit Xico

The best time to visit Xico is during the dry season, from December to April. The weather is pleasant and sunny during these months, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, Xico is a year-round destination, and visitors can expect beautiful scenery and a range of activities no matter when they visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xico

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink specialties.
  • Explore the natural scenery and hiking trails around Xico.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Be careless with personal belongings, as petty theft can occur in tourist areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xico

Xico is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take common sense precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Xico

Xico has a good standard of medical care, and visitors can find hospitals and clinics in nearby towns such as Xalapa and Coatepec. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Xico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xico

The nearest airport to Xico is El Lencero Airport, located in the nearby city of Xalapa. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Xico.

Bus Stations Near Xico

Xico has its own bus station, which offers connections to nearby towns and cities. Visitors can also take buses from the central bus station in Xalapa.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to transport them to Xico.

How to Get Around in Xico

Xico is a small town and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and local buses are also available for visitors who wish to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Xico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Xalapa. The nearby city of Xalapa is known for its museums, galleries, and lively cultural scene.
  • Coatepec. This charming town is famous for its coffee production and is a popular destination for coffee lovers.
  • Catemaco. This small town on the Gulf Coast is known for its beautiful natural scenery and indigenous culture.

Conclusion

Xico is a hidden gem in central Mexico, offering visitors a range of activities and attractions in a picturesque setting. With its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and delicious food and drink scene, it is a must-visit destination for anyone travelling to Mexico. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Xico and make the most of your time there.
