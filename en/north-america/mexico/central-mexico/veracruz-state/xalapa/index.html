Xalapa, a city nestled in the heart of Veracruz State, is known for its vibrant culture, natural beauty, and rich history. The name Xalapa comes from the Nahuatl word “Xallapan,” which means “place in the sand.” Despite being overshadowed by other popular tourist destinations in Mexico, Xalapa attracts visitors from around the world with its museums, parks, and countless outdoor adventures. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or an extended stay, Xalapa has plenty to offer. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, local Food and Drink Scene, cultural traditions, and more.

Introduction to Xalapa

Xalapa is a bustling city with a population of around 500,000. The city is surrounded by lush mountains, local parks, and a multitude of outdoor activities waiting to be discovered. Visitors who come to Xalapa can expect to find a laid-back, peaceful atmosphere, with plenty of opportunities to explore different cultural experiences and the breathtaking views of Central Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Xalapa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

A visit to Xalapa is not complete without exploring its historical sites, museums, and local landmarks. Xalapa has more than 50 museums, and each one is an opportunity to learn something new about the city’s past and its traditions.

  • Museo de Antropología de Xalapa: A leading museum that features exhibits on Mexico’s pre-Columbian history. Visitors can explore various ancient artifacts and learn about the customs and traditions of the indigenous people of Mexico.
  • Museo Interactivo de Xalapa: A museum that focuses on science, technology, and interactive exhibits. This museum is perfect for children and adults alike, and its exhibitions offer an enjoyable learning experience.
  • The Museum of Xalapa: A museum housed in a colonial-era building that features exhibits of contemporary art, photography, and regional history. It’s a great opportunity to understand the cultural mosaic of Xalapa and Mexico.
  • Cathedral of Xalapa: A beautiful 17th-century cathedral that offers stunning views of the city. The cathedral is a must-visit for history buffs and those looking for peaceful yet impactful moments.
  • Palacio de Gobierno: The seat of the state government that dates back to the 19th century. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of Veracruz state and Mexico’s political history.

Natural Attractions of Xalapa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Xalapa is a city renowned for its natural beauty, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re into hiking, cycling, exploring parks, or just enjoying a relaxing atmosphere, Xalapa has something to offer.

  • Parque Natura: A park located in the heart of the city, with abundant flora, fauna, and natural landscapes. Visitors can walk, jog, or picnic while exploring the park’s flora and fauna, or simply relax in its natural surroundings.
  • Cerro de Macuiltépetl: A hill that overlooks the city and offers panoramic views. It’s an ideal spot for hiking enthusiasts, offering splendid views of Xalapa from the upper end of the city.
  • Botanic Garden Francisco Javier Clavijero: The oldest Botanic Garden in Mexico, founded in 1971. It houses over 6500 specimens from more than 850 genera, illustrating the diversity of tropical flora and vegetation.
  • Ecological Reserve of Macuiltépetl: A protected area created with the objective of conserving natural resources and providing recreation, with some options for cycling, routes for mountain bikes, a viewpoint, and canyoneering.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xalapa

Xalapa has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that cater to various fitness levels. The city has a perfect balance of natural surroundings and urban amenities, making hiking and biking adventurous but still accessible.

  • Parque Natura: Multiple trails for hiking and jogging, covering different terrains and lengths.
  • Ecological Park La Loma (City Park): An outdoor park with different sections for walking, yoga, table games, and a variety of trails for hiking and mountain cycling.
  • Botanic Garden Francisco Javier Clavijero: An outdoor park where you can experience various hiking trails, explore the tropical flora and fauna, and witness the beauty of nature.

Best Things to Do in Xalapa: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours for visitors that showcase Xalapa’s culture, history, and outdoor adventures.

  • Join a coffee tour: Learn about sustainable and ecologically responsible coffee farming while enjoying some Xalapa’s organic coffee, one of Mexico’s leading coffee producing regions.
  • Take a salsa class: Learn this vibrant Latin American dance in Xalapa, where the salsa music and dance are an integral part of the culture.
  • Visit a traditional dance performance: Witness a performance of one of the traditional dances of Veracruz, including the Danzón, Son Jarocho, and Son Huasteco, which are part of the cultural heritage of Mexico.
  • Explore the Caucomate river with butterflies on its shores: A unique experience to encounter and appreciate the beauty of the wildlife.

For a comprehensive Xalapa experience, visitors should consider a week-long itinerary that includes visits to local museums, outdoor adventures, and food, and drink experiences:

We recommend starting with cultural attractions, and then move to outdoor adventures, local food, and drink experiences. For the last days, visitors can take a tour to nearby attractions or relax in Xalapa’s scenic environments.

Food and Drink Scene of Xalapa

Xalapa takes its food and drink culture seriously. With local ingredients, traditional foods, and an array of drinks and beverages, Xalapa’s food scene is a true reflection of its diverse culture.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Elotes: A corn on a cob dish, boiled and served with a variety of toppings such as mayonnaise, cheese, and chili powder.
  • Chile Relleno(s): A dish made with peppers stuffed with meat, cheese, or veggies, and covered in tomato sauce, a classic of Mexican cuisine.
  • Chocolates enchilados: Xalapa is known for its spicy version of Chocolate, with a blend of cocoa and spices like cinnamon and cayenne pepper
  • Papantla vanilla: A popular Mexican vanilla variety used in cooking in Xalapa and one of the most demanded vanilla beans worldwide.

Local Beverages

  • Café de Olla: A traditional coffee beverage made with spiced cinnamon and sugar, brewed in clay pots.
  • Tuba: a popular Mexican fermented drink made from palm trees’ sap and served with coconut and cinnamon.
  • Tequila: As Xalapa is on the verge of Jalisco State, Tequila is famous too!. It’s made from blue agave plant, a succulent plant from Jalisco, and aged for months or years.

Where to Stay in Xalapa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xalapa has plenty of accommodation options to cater to all budgets. Visitors can choose from high-end hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels.

  • Hotel Xalapa: A high-end hotel located in the city center, with a pool, fitness center, and easy access to attractions.
  • Hostal del Ángel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable bunk beds and private rooms, with a shared kitchen.
  • Posada del Cafeto: A cozy guesthouse located near the city center, with a garden, terrace, and ample parking space.

What to Buy in Xalapa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Xalapa is home to a variety of local products, from traditional handicrafts to organic coffee and vanilla beans. Visitors can take home a piece of Xalapa by purchasing the following items:

  • El Arte de Café: Premium organic coffee beans that are sustainably and locally grown and harvested.
  • Forals (Flower) Vanilla: Grown and processed in Veracruz, this type of Vanilla gives the final products a unique and delightful aroma.
  • Handmade embroidery: Xalapa is also famous for its handmade embroidery, which features intricate designs and intricate patterns.
  • Huipil-blouses: Traditional blouses worn by indigenous communities in different Mexican regions, with distinct patterns and materials, make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Xalapa

Xalapa has a rich history and a unique cultural heritage. Visitors can learn and appreciate this through their customs and traditions.

  • Music and Dance: Xalapa has Grammy-award-winning artists and is famous for its vibrant music and dance, including the Danzón, Son Jarocho and Huasteco.
  • Food and Drink: Xalapa’s food and drink scene is diverse and unique, with traditional and locally sourced ingredients.
  • Day of the Dead: A celebration that honors deceased loved ones. It occurs on October 31 and November 1, and Xalapa’s celebration is one of the most colorful and vibrant in Mexico.

Best Time to Visit Xalapa

The best time to visit Xalapa is between December and April, when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, visitors can visit at any time of the year, as the city has a pleasant climate year-round.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xalapa

Xalapa is a safe city for tourists, but visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Use official taxis or ride-sharing apps like Uber or DiDi instead of unlicensed taxis.
  • Avoid travelling in deserted areas alone, especially at night.
  • Carry only the necessary cash and keep your belongings safe and secure.

Health in Xalapa

Visitors should be aware of the following health concerns while in Xalapa:

  • The sun can be strong in Xalapa, so use sunscreen to avoid sunburn and wear sunglasses and a hat.
  • Drink bottled water, and avoid drinking tap water, to prevent stomach upsets.
  • Stay hydrated as Xalapa’s climate is dry and hot.

Getting to Xalapa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xalapa

The most convenient airport near Xalapa is Veracruz International Airport, approximately one hour’s drive from the city center. Taxis and rental cars are available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Xalapa

Visitors can travel to Xalapa by bus. There are direct buses to Xalapa from Mexico City, Puebla, and other towns and cities. The bus station is located in the city center and is easily accessible.

Other Options

Visitors can also take taxis, Ubers, or rental cars to get around the city. Xalapa is a small town, and most attractions are located near the city center, making it easy to explore on foot.

How to Get Around in Xalapa

Visitors can get around Xalapa by using public transportation, walking, biking, or taking a taxi. Buses run frequently, and fares are affordable. Taxis are also readily available and are a convenient mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Xalapa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Xalapa, such as:

  • Papantla: Known for its vanilla production and Totonac culture, Papantla is about an hour and a half’s drive from Xalapa.
  • Coatepec: A small scenic town ten kilometers from Xalapa, known for its celebrated coffee production and its colonial architecture.
  • Villa Rica: A small beach town known for its beautiful beaches and water sports, around an hour’s drive from Xalapa.

Conclusion

Xalapa is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be explored, with its culture, history, and outdoor adventures. With this guide in hand, visitors can explore Xalapa’s rich heritage and experience everything the city has to offer.
