Introduction to Iguala

Iguala, also known as Iguala de la Independencia, is a charming city located in the Guerrero state of Mexico. It is famous for its rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful landscapes. The city is situated on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to explore the coastal region of North America.

Iguala is known for its role in Mexican history, playing an essential role in the Mexican War of Independence. The city also boasts a thriving arts and culture scene, with many museums, galleries, and theaters. Visitors can also enjoy the outdoor adventures, which include hiking, biking, and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Iguala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Bandera: This museum is a tribute to the Mexican flag, its history, and symbolism. Visitors can learn about the creation and evolution of the Mexican flag through the museum’s collection of flags, banners, and other artifacts.
  • Parish of San Juan Bautista: This historical church dates back to the 1700s and is known for its beautiful architecture and artwork. Visitors can marvel at the intricate details of the church’s facade, as well as the stunning stained glass windows inside.
  • Casa Figueroa Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of the Figueroa family, one of the most prominent families in Iguala’s history. Visitors can explore the family’s colonial-era mansion and learn about their contributions to Iguala’s culture and development.

Natural Attractions of Iguala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Soledad Ecological Park: This park is situated on the outskirts of Iguala and is a popular destination for outdoor adventurers. Visitors can hike through the park’s lush vegetation, which includes a variety of over 200 species of flora and fauna.
  • Cerro del Tehuehue: This mountain offers visitors a challenging hike to its peak, which boasts a breathtaking panoramic view of the city and surrounding landscapes.
  • Iguala’s main square: Also known as the Zocalo, is a central gathering place for locals and tourists alike, surrounded by beautiful architecture and plenty of cafes to sit and watch the world go by.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iguala

  • Cerro del Tehuehue: As mentioned earlier, this mountain has a challenging hiking trail that leads to its peak, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscape.
  • La Soledad Ecological Park: The park offers visitors several hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult, leading to beautiful views and waterfalls. Visitors can also bike on designated trails, providing a different perspective of the park’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Iguala: Activities and Tours

  • City Tour: A guided tour of the city, which includes visits to its historical sites, museums, and landmarks.
  • Rappelling: Adventure seekers can rappel down the beautiful waterfalls of La Soledad Ecological Park
  • Shopping at Mercado Municipal: Visitors can take in the hustle and bustle of Iguala’s main market, where they can find souvenirs, local produce, and artisanal crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Iguala

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pozole: A traditional Mexican dish made with hominy, pork, and other seasonings.
  • Tamales: Corn masa stuffed with various fillings such as meats, vegetables, and cheese, wrapped in corn husks and steamed.
  • Guerrero-style Salsa: Salsa made with the unique blend of chiles, tomatoes, and other local ingredients, perfect for spice lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Tejate: A refreshing drink made from maize, cacao beans, and other ingredients, with a sweet and nutty flavor.
  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the agave plant, with a smoky and earthy flavor.
  • Pulque: An alcoholic beverage made from fermented sap of the agave plant, with a sweet, slightly sour taste.

Where to Stay in Iguala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Los Arcos: A centrally-located hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent services.
  • La Casa del Tekolote: A beautiful guesthouse with spacious rooms and a relaxing garden.
  • Hostal Huitzilopochtli: A backpacker’s hostel with affordable dorms and private rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Iguala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silver Jewelry: Taxco, a nearby town known for its silver mining, is a great place to buy silver jewelry and crafts.
  • Handmade Crafts: Visitors can find numerous artisanal crafts, such as ceramics, textiles, and pottery, at Iguala’s markets and shops.
  • Chocolate: Guerrero is known for its delicious chocolate, which visitors can buy at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Iguala

Iguala’s culture is heavily influenced by its pre-Hispanic roots, Spanish colonial history, and Catholic traditions. Visitors will find the city’s locals warm and welcoming, who take pride in their city’s unique history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Iguala

The best time to visit Iguala is from November to April, the dry season. During this period, visitors can enjoy the city’s natural beauty and cultural attractions without having to worry about rainfall or humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iguala

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Explore Iguala’s rich history and culture
  • Buy souvenirs and crafts to support local artisans

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the strong sun and high altitude
  • Disrespect the city’s cultural and religious traditions
  • Engage with suspicious or unknown persons, particularly at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iguala

While Iguala has a reputation for being an unsafe city, the situation has improved in recent years. Visitors should practice common safety measures, such as avoiding isolated areas and keeping valuables safe; use a safe when available.

Health in Iguala

Visitors should take standard precautions when visiting Iguala, such as drinking bottled water, avoiding street food vendors, and carrying medication for common illnesses such as headaches, stomach aches, and diarrhea. It’s also imperative to get travel insurance covering medical expenses.

Getting to Iguala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iguala

  • Hermanos Serdans International Airport: The nearest international airport to Iguala, located in Puebla.
  • General Juan N. Álvarez International Airport: An international airport located in Acapulco, about a four-hour drive from Iguala.

Bus Stations Near Iguala

  • Terminal de Autobuses de Iguala: The city’s main bus terminal, with many regular bus services connecting with other major destinations in Mexico.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from nearby cities such as Mexico City, Cuernavaca, or Acapulco, giving them greater freedom to explore the region at their own pace and time.

How to Get Around in Iguala

The easiest way to get around Iguala is by walking or by taxi. Taxis are a common mode of transportation in Iguala and can be easily hailed on the street. Visitors can also rent bikes, which is a convenient way to explore natural attractions and city landmarks.

Where to Go Next from Iguala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taxco: A colonial town, renowned for its silver mines and quaint cobbled streets.
  • Tlaltenango: A picturesque village known for its thermal waters and natural springs.
  • La Poza de los Enanos: A natural swimming pool in the mountains, surrounded by lush vegetation and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Iguala is a culturally rich city with a unique history, vibrant art scene, and beautiful landscapes. With a week-long itinerary of activities and adventures to choose from, visitors can explore the best that Iguala has to offer. Keep the do’s and don’ts in mind while practicing common sense; visitors can enjoy a safe, healthy and memorable vacation in this stunning city in Mexico.
