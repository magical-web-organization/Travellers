Introduction to Acapulco

Acapulco, located on the Pacific Coast of Guerrero, Mexico, is a popular tourist destination known for its vibrant nightlife, stunning beaches, and unique cultural experiences. From visiting historical attractions to exploring natural wonders, there are endless things to see and do in Acapulco.

List of Places to Visit in Acapulco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fuerte de San Diego - This 17th-century Spanish fortress is now a museum showcasing the history and culture of the region. Visitors can explore the exhibits, watch a multimedia show, and experience a traditional Mexican dance performance.
  • Zocalo - This central square is lined with shops, restaurants, and historic buildings. It is a great place to people-watch, soak up the local atmosphere, and hear live music.
  • La Quebrada Cliff Divers - Watch local daredevils leap off a 148-foot cliff into the ocean below in a traditional performance that’s been entertaining crowds in Acapulco since the 1930s.

Natural Attractions of Acapulco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isla de la Roqueta - Take a boat or kayak trip to this beautiful island for a day of snorkeling, hiking, and relaxing on secluded beaches.
  • Parque Papagayo - This sprawling park is a great place for families with its playgrounds, go-kart tracks, and mini-golf course. It’s also home to a zoo, botanical garden, and cultural center.
  • Pie de la Cuesta - A 20-minute drive from central Acapulco, this stunning beach is known for its dramatic sunsets, surfing, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Acapulco

  • La Cima - This day hike takes you to the summit of Acapulco’s highest peak, with stunning views of the city and ocean.
  • Laguna de Tres Palos - Rent a bike and explore the scenic shores of this inland lake, where you can also go fishing and boating.

Best Things to Do in Acapulco: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of Acapulco Bay - See the city from a different perspective on a boat tour, which includes stops at iconic landmarks like La Quebrada.
  • Water Sports - Acapulco is a paradise for water sports enthusiasts, offering everything from surfing and scuba diving to parasailing and Jet Skiing.
  • Day Trip to Taxco - This picturesque town, located about 2.5 hours’ drive from Acapulco, is famous for its silver jewelry and colonial architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Acapulco

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos de pescado - Fresh fish tacos served with traditional toppings like cilantro, onion, and lime.
  • Tamales - A Mexican staple made with masa dough and stuffed with various ingredients like meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Ceviche - A refreshing seafood dish made with raw fish marinated in citrus juice and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Michelada - A spicy beer cocktail made with lime juice, hot sauce, and seasoning.
  • Margarita - A classic tequila-based cocktail that pairs well with salty snacks and seafood.
  • Aguas frescas - A refreshing drink made with fruit, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Acapulco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Las Brisas Acapulco - A luxurious resort with stunning views of Acapulco Bay and private plunge pools.
  • Selina Acapulco - A trendy hostel with beach access, a rooftop bar, and a co-working space.
  • Casa Yalma Kaan - A charming guesthouse in the heart of Old Acapulco, with a pool, garden, and beautiful views.

What to Buy in Acapulco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mexican handicrafts - From pottery and textiles to jewelry and leather goods, there are plenty of unique souvenirs to buy in Acapulco.
  • Mezcal - This smoky agave-based spirit is produced in various regions of Mexico, including Guerrero.
  • Silver jewelry - Taxco is known for its silver production, so be sure to pick up a piece of handmade jewelry while you’re there.

Local Culture and Customs of Acapulco

Acapulco has a rich cultural heritage that blends indigenous, Spanish, and African influences. The locals take pride in their traditions, music, and food, and visitors are encouraged to participate in festivals and cultural events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Acapulco

The best time to visit Acapulco is from December to April, when the weather is sunny and dry. However, peak season means higher prices and more crowds, so if you’re looking for a quieter and cheaper trip, consider visiting in the shoulder season from May to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Acapulco

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Spanish to communicate with locals.
  • Dress appropriately for cultural and religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water or ice made from tap water.
  • Flash your valuables in public.
  • Take unmarked taxis or venture into unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Acapulco

Acapulco has a reputation for crime, so it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety while visiting the city. Stay in well-lit and populated areas, avoid walking alone after dark, and don’t leave your belongings unattended on the beach. It’s also recommended to use official taxis and avoid taking public transportation at night.

Health in Acapulco

Mexican cuisine can be spicy and heavy, so be mindful of your stomach when trying new foods. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, avoid ice made from tap water, and use sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Acapulco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Acapulco

Acapulco International Airport (ACA) is located about 18 km east of the city center and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Acapulco

The Estrella de Oro and Estrella Blanca bus lines provide transportation to and from various cities in Mexico, including Mexico City and Guadalajara.

Other Options

Renting a car is another option for getting to Acapulco, but keep in mind that traffic in the city can be chaotic, and parking can be challenging to find.

How to Get Around in Acapulco

Taxis and buses are readily available in Acapulco, but be sure to use official taxis and avoid taking public transportation at night. Walking is also a great way to explore the city, but be mindful of traffic and stay on well-lit and populated streets.

Where to Go Next from Acapulco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zihuatanejo - This charming fishing village is known for its beaches, seafood, and laid-back atmosphere, located about 3 hours’ drive from Acapulco.
  • Cuernavaca - This colonial city boasts beautiful architecture, history, and culture, located about 4 hours’ drive from Acapulco.

Conclusion

Acapulco is a vibrant and diverse destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Plan your trip well, take safety measures, and enjoy the beauty and wonders of this beautiful city.
