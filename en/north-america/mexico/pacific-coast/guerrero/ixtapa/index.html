Introduction to Ixtapa

Ixtapa is a resort town located in the sunny state of Guerrero, in the Pacific Coast of Mexico. The town offers beautiful beaches, plenty of outdoor recreational activities, an abundance of cultural landmarks, and an exciting nightlife. Ixtapa is an ideal destination for both solo travelers and families looking to escape the mundane routine of everyday life.

List of Places to Visit in Ixtapa

Ixtapa has a fantastic array of attractions for tourists to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town of Ixtapa has a rich and interesting history, with some remarkable landmarks that are worth visiting. These include:

  • Museo Arqueológico de la Costa Grande: A museum that showcases the pre-hispanic artifacts, which are remarkably unique to the region.
  • Cihuatlán Archaeological Museum: A beautiful museum housing a vast collection of pre-Columbian artifacts of the Nahuatl speaking peoples who lived in the region.
  • Zihuatanejo Museum and Cultural Center: A museum exhibiting the work of contemporary local artists and providing a glimpse of The Mexican Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Ixtapa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ixtapa is well-known for its scenic views and outdoor activities. Some of the most popular natural attractions in Ixtapa are:

  • Playa La Ropa: The most picturesque beach in Ixtapa.
  • Ixtapa Island: A World famous island that is known for its clear and bright water.
  • El Refugio de Potosí: A wildlife reserve that is home to many different species of animals and birds. This reserve is perfect for anyone interested in birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ixtapa

Ixtapa offers great opportunities for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes for these activities include:

  • Parque Aventura Ixtapa: A park that has a variety of challenging mountain biking trails.
  • Parque El Limon: A scenic park that offers hiking and trekking through its beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Ixtapa: Activities and Tours

Ixtapa offers a wide variety of activities and tours that cater to the needs of every tourist. Here are some of the best things to do when in Ixtapa:

  • Snorkeling at Isla Ixtapa: A tour that takes you to the coral reef of Isla Ixtapa for a chance to explore the underwater world.
  • Horseback Riding at Caballos de Mar: A tour that allows you to experience the beauty of the coastline of Ixtapa atop a horse.
  • Sunset Cruise: A tour that takes you for a San Francisco-style tour of Ixtapa, allowing you to witness the breathtaking horizon and the setting sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Ixtapa

The food scene in Ixtapa is a blend of regional Mexican cuisine and international cuisine that caters to both locals and tourists. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages when in Ixtapa:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos de Cabeza: Tacos made using the cow’s head and is a specialty of the region.
  • Tamales: Made from masa dough with fillings of meat, cheese, or other ingredients, which is then wrapped in corn husks and steamed.
  • Quesadilla de Huitlacoche: A quesadilla made with corn, cheese, and Mexican truffle.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: Made from agave, tequila is the national drink of Mexico.
  • Micheladas: A beer cocktail made with tomato juice, lime, salt, and spices.
  • Mezcal: A spirit made from the agave plant, which is similar to tequila but with a smokier flavor.

Where to Stay in Ixtapa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ixtapa provides a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Ixtapa:

  • Las Brisas Ixtapa: A leading family resort that sits comfortably on its private beach.
  • Villa Mexicana: A boutique hotel that offers views of the Ixtapa landscape and is surrounded by lush tropical verdure.
  • Hostal Ixtapa: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Ixtapa.

What to Buy in Ixtapa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ixtapa offers a wide range of souvenirs that you can take home with you. Here are some of the popular items:

  • Huipiles: Colorful Mayan tunics synonymous with the region, made by local artisans.
  • Maquesote-Honey: High-quality honey produced by the local bees.
  • Crafts: Handcrafted souvenirs made by local artisans from different materials such as ceramics, glass, and fabrics.

Local Culture and Customs of Ixtapa

Mexican culture is well-preserved in Ixtapa and is visible through its traditions, music, and cuisine. The people of Ixtapa and Mexico are known for their friendliness, warmth, and generosity to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Ixtapa

The best time to visit Ixtapa is from November to May. These months have the perfect temperature and ideal weather for outdoor activities. The summer months are extremely hot in Ixtapa and are not the ideal time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ixtapa

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen: The sun is quite hot in Ixtapa, and you should wear sunscreen to avoid getting a sunburn.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Spanish: This helps break the ice among the locals and will help you connect with the people around you.
  • Be respectful: Mexico is a country with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of that.

Don’t:

  • Drink the tap water: Only drink purified bottled water.
  • Overstay your visa: Stay within the legal limit of your visa, and avoid unpleasant legal situations.
  • Wear flashy jewelry or clothes: This can make you a target for theft.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ixtapa

Ixtapa is considered a generally safe place to visit, but you should always take basic precautions like keeping valuable belongings safe and practicing caution.

Health in Ixtapa

To safeguard against sickness, ensure that you drink bottled water and wash your hands regularly. You should also use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Ixtapa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ixtapa is easily accessible via different transportation options, including airports, bus stations, and other transportation options.

Airports Near Ixtapa

The nearest airport to Ixtapa is Ixtapa-Zihuatanejo International Airport (ZIH).

Bus Stations Near Ixtapa

The bus station in Ixtapa is just a short walk from most hotels and nearby locations.

Other Options

There are plenty of other options to get to Ixtapa, including rental cars, taxis, and ride-sharing services.

How to Get Around in Ixtapa

You can easily maneuver around Ixtapa by walking or biking around, especially if staying in the central area. ATM machines are available throughout Ixtapa, and most shops and markets accept credit cards.

Where to Go Next from Ixtapa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ixtapa is an excellent base for visiting other nearby towns and neighboring cities like:

  • Acapulco: A famous resort town known for its beaches and nightlife.
  • Taxco: A historic silver town known for its colonial buildings and world-class silver art collection.

Conclusion

Ixtapa is a tourist paradise, with endless recreational activities, natural sites, historical landmarks, and cultural experiences that will make your visit worthwhile. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Ixtapa has everything you need for a memorable trip. Plan in advance, follow the local customs and be open-minded while exploring and interacting with the locals to get the most out of Ixtapa.
