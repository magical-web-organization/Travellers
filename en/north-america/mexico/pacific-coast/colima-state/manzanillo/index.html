Manzanillo is a charming coastal city located in the state of Colima, Mexico. Known for its stunning beaches, top-notch fishing, and picturesque landscapes, Manzanillo is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Mexican experience. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, or cultural experiences, Manzanillo has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

Introduction to Manzanillo

Nestled on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, Manzanillo is a small but thriving city with a rich history and culture. Founded in 1522, the city has long been a hub for trade and commerce, and is now a popular tourist destination. With its pristine beaches, lush jungles, and vibrant local culture, Manzanillo is the ideal place for travelers seeking an authentic taste of Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Manzanillo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Manzanillo has a rich cultural heritage that can be experienced through its many museums and historical attractions. Some of the must-visit cultural hotspots in the city include:

  • Museo Universitario de Artes Populares de Colima - Showcasing the traditional arts and crafts of Colima, this museum is a great place to learn about the local culture.
  • Templo de San Antonio - A beautiful 18th-century church located in the heart of the city, the Templo de San Antonio is a popular stop for tourists and locals alike.
  • La Campana Archaeological Zone - A fascinating archeological site featuring ancient ruins from the pre-Columbian era.

Natural Attractions of Manzanillo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Manzanillo is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Some of the best natural attractions in the area include:

  • Playa Miramar - One of the most beautiful beaches in Manzanillo, Playa Miramar is known for its clear waters and pristine sands.
  • Barra de Navidad - A small fishing village located just outside of Manzanillo, Barra de Navidad is a great place to experience local culture and cuisine.
  • Cerro de la Cruz - A scenic hill located in the heart of the city, Cerro de la Cruz offers great panoramic views of Manzanillo and the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manzanillo

For those who enjoy hiking and biking, Manzanillo offers a variety of scenic routes and trails. Some of the most popular options include:

  • El Salto del Aguacero - A picturesque waterfall located just outside of Manzanillo, El Salto del Aguacero offers a scenic hiking trail through the jungle.
  • Cerro de San Francisco - A challenging but rewarding hike, Cerro de San Francisco offers stunning views of the city and the Pacific Ocean.
  • Biking Tour of the City - For those who prefer biking to hiking, a bike tour of the city is a great way to experience Manzanillo’s local culture and scenery.

Best Things to Do in Manzanillo: Activities and Tours

From fishing to snorkeling, Manzanillo offers a wide range of activities and tours for travelers of all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do in the area include:

  • Fishing Charters - Manzanillo is known for its top-notch fishing, and there are plenty of fishing charters available for those looking to catch some fish.
  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving - The waters around Manzanillo are home to a variety of marine life, making it a great place for snorkeling and scuba diving.
  • Local Food Tour - A food tour is a great way to experience the local cuisine and culture, and there are plenty of options available in Manzanillo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manzanillo

To help you plan your trip to Manzanillo, here’s a week-long itinerary that includes some of the best things to do and see in the city:

Day 1: Explore the local countryside and visit the La Campana archaeological zone.

Day 2: Spend the day at Playa Miramar, one of the most beautiful beaches in Manzanillo.

Day 3: Take a bike tour of the city to experience local culture and scenery.

Day 4: Go on a fishing charter or take a snorkeling trip to experience the rich marine life in the area.

Day 5: Visit the Museo Universitario de Artes Populares de Colima to learn about the local arts and crafts.

Day 6: Hike to Cerro de San Francisco for panoramic views of the city and the ocean.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the city’s many spas and wellness centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Manzanillo

Must-Try Dishes

Manzanillo is known for its delicious seafood, street food, and regional cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes in the city include:

  • Ceviche - A popular dish made with raw fish marinated in citrus juices and spices.
  • Tortas Ahogadas - A local specialty, tortas ahogadas are sandwiches soaked in a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Chilate - A refreshing drink made from coconut milk, cinnamon, and sugar.

Local Beverages

Manzanillo is home to a vibrant local beverage scene, with a variety of cocktails, juices, and smoothies available. Some of the must-try beverages in the city include:

  • Margaritas - A classic cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and sugar.
  • Piña Coladas - A refreshing cocktail made with coconut cream, pineapple juice, and rum.
  • Aguas Frescas - Refreshing fruit drinks that are perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Manzanillo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manzanillo offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some of the best places to stay in the city include:

  • Barcelo Karmina - A five-star resort located on the beach, the Barcelo Karmina offers luxurious amenities and stunning views.
  • Las Hadas Golf Resort & Marina - A classic Manzanillo resort, Las Hadas Golf Resort & Marina offers a variety of rooms and suites, as well as a private marina.
  • Hostal Olas Altas - A budget-friendly hostel located just a few blocks from the beach, Hostal Olas Altas is a great option for travelers on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Manzanillo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Manzanillo is home to a variety of local products and souvenirs that are perfect for bringing home as a memento of your trip. Some of the best things to buy in the city include:

  • Artesanias - Handcrafted arts and crafts, such as pottery and textiles.
  • Tequila - A classic Mexican spirit, tequila is the perfect souvenir for those who enjoy a good drink.
  • Local Spices and Sauces - Manzanillo is known for its delicious cuisine, and local spices and sauces are a great way to bring a taste of the city home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Manzanillo

Manzanillo is a relaxed and friendly city that values family, tradition, and community. When visiting the city, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in public places and being mindful of noise levels in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Manzanillo

Manzanillo is a year-round destination, with warm temperatures and plenty of sunshine throughout the year. The best time to visit the city is from November to April, when temperatures are cooler and there is less chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manzanillo

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine - Manzanillo is known for its delicious seafood and regional cuisine, so be sure to try some of the local dishes and beverages.
  • Explore the outdoors - With its stunning beaches and natural attractions, Manzanillo is the perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Respect local customs and traditions - When visiting the city, be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in public places.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water - To avoid getting sick, it’s best to stick to bottled water and avoid drinking tap water in Manzanillo.
  • Leave valuables unattended - Like any city, Manzanillo can have its share of petty crime. It’s important to be vigilant and keep your valuables with you at all times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manzanillo

Manzanillo is generally a safe city for tourists, with friendly locals and a relaxed vibe. However, like any destination, it’s important to be mindful of your surroundings and keep your valuables with you at all times.

Health in Manzanillo

As with any destination, it’s important to take precautions to stay healthy while visiting Manzanillo. Be sure to drink plenty of bottled water and wear sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun. It’s also a good idea to bring insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Manzanillo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manzanillo is easily accessible by air and road. Some of the best ways to get to the city include:

Bus Stations Near Manzanillo

Other Options

How to Get Around in Manzanillo

  • Taxis - Taxis are readily available in Manzanillo and are a convenient way to get around the city.
  • Local Buses - Local buses are a budget-friendly option for getting around the city, with regular service to popular destinations.
  • Rental Cars - Rental cars are a great way to explore the area at your own pace, and there are plenty of rental car options available in Manzanillo.

Where to Go Next from Manzanillo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Manzanillo is a great base for exploring other destinations and attractions in the area. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a little rest and relaxation, Manzanillo has something for everyone. With its rich history, stunning scenery, and vibrant local culture, Manzanillo is the perfect destination for your next Mexican getaway.
