Introduction to Comala

Are you dreaming of a getaway to a quiet and picturesque town in Mexico? Look no further than Comala - a magical destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, nature, and cuisine. Located in the state of Colima, on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, Comala boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and warm hospitality.

Comala is also known as the “White Village” for its whitewashed colonial houses, cobbled streets, and colorful gardens. The town dates back to the pre-Hispanic era and is named after the Nahuatl word “Comalli,” which means “vessel made of clay.” Over the centuries, Comala has been shaped by the influences of Spanish conquistadores, African slaves, and indigenous communities, giving rise to a unique and diverse culture.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a cultural traveler, Comala has something to offer. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink, accommodation, local customs, and more in Comala.

List of Places to Visit in Comala

Comala is a small town, but it packs a big punch when it comes to attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit in Comala:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Historico de Comala: This museum showcases the history and culture of Comala through exhibits of pre-Hispanic artifacts, ceramics, weaponry, and antique furniture.
  • Templo del Señor del Perdón: This 18th-century church is one of the most iconic landmarks in Comala. The interior boasts ornate altarpieces, murals, and saintly artifacts.
  • Casa de la Cultura: This cultural center hosts art exhibitions, workshops, and performances throughout the year and is an excellent place to learn about the local arts and traditions.
  • Hacienda de Nogueras: This historic estate is now a museum and environmental center that offers a glimpse into the life of wealthy colonial landowners in the 19th century. You can tour its rooms, gardens, aqueduct, sugar mill, and butterfly sanctuary.

Natural Attractions of Comala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Volcán de Fuego National Park: This park surrounds the active volcano of the same name and offers hiking trails, camping, birdwatching, and coffee plantations tours. The views of the volcano and the Colima Valley are breathtaking.
  • Laguna de Carrizalillo: This lagoon is a haven for birdlife and aquatic plants and is a popular spot for swimming, kayaking, and picnicking.
  • Nevado de Colima National Park: This park encompasses the snow-capped peak of Mount Colima (the highest summit in the state) and the surrounding forests, meadows, and rivers. You can hike, mountaineer, and camp in the park, or take a guided tour to see the flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Comala

Comala’s natural landscapes are ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some top routes:

  • La Campana Trail: This 7 km (4.3 mi) trail leads to the top of La Campana Hill, which offers stunning views of Comala and the volcanoes. The trail is moderate in difficulty and takes about 2-3 hours to complete.
  • Cerro de San Juan Trail: This 6 km (3.7 mi) trail goes through the forests and hills surrounding Comala and offers scenic vistas of the town and the countryside. The trail is easy to moderate in difficulty and takes about 2 hours to complete.
  • Ruta del Cafe: This 15 km (9.3 mi) trail takes you through the coffee plantations and estates of Comala and gives you the opportunity to learn about the cultivation, harvesting, and roasting of coffee beans. The trail is easy and takes about 4 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Comala: Activities and Tours

Comala is known for its laid-back and welcoming atmosphere, which invites visitors to relax and enjoy themselves. Here are some of the best things to do in Comala:

  • Visit a local cafe: Comala is famous for its coffee culture, and there are plenty of cozy and stylish cafes where you can savor a cup of freshly brewed coffee while enjoying the colonial charm of the town.
  • Take a horse carriage ride: This traditional way of transportation is a fun and romantic way to explore the town’s narrow streets, parks, and monuments. You can arrange a ride at the main plaza or your hotel.
  • Attend a fiesta or festival: Comala is a town that loves to celebrate, and there are several festivals and events throughout the year that showcase the local music, dance, food, and traditions. The most famous one is the Feria de la Nuez y el Chile, which takes place in October and features a culinary competition and a rodeo.
  • Take a cooking class: If you’re a foodie, you’ll love learning how to make the traditional dishes of Comala, such as pozole, tamales, and mole. Many local restaurants and culinary schools offer cooking classes that include a visit to the market and a hands-on experience in the kitchen.

Food and Drink Scene of Comala

Comala’s gastronomy is a fusion of the indigenous, Spanish, and African influences that shaped its history. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pozole: This hearty stew made with pork, hominy, and spices is a traditional dish in Mexico, and Comala has its own version.
  • Chilate: This warm drink made with maize, cocoa, and cinnamon is a favorite among locals and is perfect for chilly evenings.
  • Tamales: These stuffed cornmeal cakes are a staple food in Mexico, and Comala makes them with various fillings, such as beans, chicken, or mole sauce.
  • Gorditas de Cuachala: These small tortillas filled with a spicy stew of cactus leaves, chili peppers, and pork are a culinary experience that you shouldn’t miss.

Local Beverages

  • Café de olla: This spiced coffee infused with cinnamon, cloves, and piloncillo (unrefined sugar) is a signature drink in Comala.
  • Tejuino: This carbonated drink made with fermented corn and cane sugar is refreshing and tangy.
  • Pulque: This fermented drink made with the sap of the maguey cactus is an acquired taste but worth trying.

Where to Stay in Comala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Comala offers a range of accommodation options that suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • Hotel Hacienda de Comala: This colonial-style hotel is located in the heart of Comala and features elegant rooms, a swimming pool, a restaurant, and a spa.
  • Casa Alvarada: This guesthouse is a cozy and rustic place that offers comfortable rooms, a garden, a terrace, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Eco Hostal El Mexicano: This hostel is an eco-friendly and budget-friendly option that provides shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a tour desk.

What to Buy in Comala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Comala is a town that celebrates its artisanal traditions, and you can find many local products that make great souvenirs or gifts for your loved ones. Here are some ideas:

  • Pottery: Comala is known for its clay crafts, such as ceramic plates, bowls, vases, and sculptures, which are beautifully decorated with floral motifs and bright colors.
  • Handicrafts: Comala’s markets offer a range of handicrafts, such as embroidered textiles, woven baskets, wooden toys, and leather goods, that reflect the local culture and skills.
  • Coffee: Comala’s coffee beans are among the best in Mexico, and you can buy them roasted or green at specialty stores or local markets.
  • Nuez de la India: This indigenous nut is grown in the nearby forests and used in traditional sweets and snacks. You can buy it at local markets and try it with a cup of coffee.

Local Culture and Customs of Comala

Comala’s culture is rooted in its history, traditions, and beliefs, and reflects the influences of indigenous, Spanish, and African cultures. Here are some aspects of Comala’s culture and customs:

  • Language: Spanish is the official language in Comala, but some locals speak Nahuatl, the indigenous language that was spoken before the conquest.
  • Religion: Catholicism is the predominant religion in Comala, and many of the festivals and celebrations are based on the Catholic calendar.
  • Arts and crafts: Comala’s artisans are skilled in various craft techniques, such as ceramics, woodcarving, metalwork, and embroidery, and their products are often displayed in markets and galleries.
  • Music: Comala’s music scene is diverse and includes genres such as folk, classical, and contemporary. The town hosts music festivals and concerts throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Comala

Comala has a tropical climate with an average temperature of 25°C (77°F) throughout the year. However, the best time to visit Comala is from November to April when the weather is drier and cooler, and the flora and fauna are at their best. The peak season is from December to February, so expect higher prices and crowds during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Comala

To have a pleasant and respectful stay in Comala, here are some tips on what to do and what not to do:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases and use them to communicate with locals
  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites or attending cultural events
  • Try the local food and drink, and discover new flavors and combinations
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or private properties

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling or filtering it first
  • Bargain aggressively in markets, as this is considered disrespectful
  • Throw trash or litter on the streets or natural areas
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions, and maintain an open and curious attitude

Safety and Health

Comala is a relatively safe and friendly town for tourists, but it’s always advisable to take some precautions to avoid any inconvenience or risks. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Comala

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place, such as a hotel safe or a hidden pocket
  • Use official taxis or public transportation to avoid scams or muggings
  • Avoid walking alone at night in empty or dark streets
  • Respect the local laws and regulations, such as the ban on smoking in public spaces

Health in Comala

  • Drink bottled or purified water instead of tap water
  • Apply sunscreen, wear a hat and sunglasses, and stay hydrated during outdoor activities
  • Bring a first-aid kit and any medication you need
  • Consult your doctor for any specific health concerns or vaccinations needed for your trip

Getting to Comala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Comala is located about 20 km (12 mi) northwest of Colima City, the capital of Colima state. Here are some transportation options to get to Comala:

Airports Near Comala

  • Licenciado Miguel de la Madrid Airport: This airport is the closest to Comala and is located in Manzanillo City, about 100 km (62 mi) away. It offers national and international flights, and you can take a shuttle or a taxi to Comala.

Bus Stations Near Comala

  • Central de Autobuses de Colima: This is the main bus station in Colima City, and it offers connections to major cities in Mexico, such as Guadalajara and Mexico City. You can take a taxi or a local bus to Comala from there.
  • Central de Autobuses de Comala: This smaller bus station is located in the center of Comala and offers connections to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

  • Car rental: You can rent a car at the airport or in Colima City and drive to Comala. The roads are well-maintained, and the drive takes about 30-40 minutes.
  • Private transfer: You can arrange a private transfer from the airport or Colima City to Comala with a local tour operator or taxi service.

How to Get Around in Comala

Comala is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, if you prefer to use public transportation or are traveling with a group, here are some options:

  • Local buses: The local buses in Comala are cheap and frequent, but they can be overcrowded and noisy. You can ask your hotel or local residents for the routes and schedules.
  • Taxis: The taxis in Comala are safe and reliable, and you can hail one on the street or ask your hotel to call one for you. However, make sure to agree on the price before getting in the taxi.
  • Bicycle rental: If you want to explore the countryside or the coffee plantations, you can rent a bicycle from a local rental shop or your hotel.

Where to Go Next from Comala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Comala is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas, which offer more natural beauty, history, and culture. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Colima City: This vibrant city is the capital of Colima state and offers a mix of colonial architecture, archaeological museums, and street markets. You can also visit the nearby Cerro de la Estrella, an ancient hilltop observatory.
  • Manzanillo: This coastal city is known for its beaches, seafood restaurants, and water sports activities. You can also take a boat tour to see the nearby coral reefs and mangrove swamps.
  • Tecomán: This town is famous for its fruit orchards, such as mangoes and avocados, and its pre-Hispanic ruins. You can also relax in its thermal spas and pools.
  • Barra de Navidad: This seaside village is a hidden gem that offers a tranquil and rustic atmosphere, away from the crowds and noise. You can stroll along its beaches, watch the sunset at the lighthouse, and try the local seafood.

Conclusion

Comala is a town that captivates the hearts and imaginations of travelers who seek authenticity and inspiration. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, delicious food, and warm people, Comala is a place that leaves a lasting impression on your soul. We hope that this guide has given you a glimpse of what Comala has to offer and inspires you to plan your next adventure in this magical town.
