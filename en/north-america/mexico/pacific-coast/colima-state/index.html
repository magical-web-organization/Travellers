Introduction to Colima State

Located on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, Colima State is a hidden gem for travelers seeking authentic experiences and natural beauty. The state is named after its capital, Colima City, which is famous for its colonial architecture and charming plazas. Colima State is a land of contrasts, where you can explore stunning beaches, hike through lush jungles, dive into crystal clear waterfalls, and bask in the warm hospitality of the locals.

List of Places to Visit in Colima State

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Regional de Historia: This museum is located in the heart of Colima City and offers visitors a glimpse into the state’s rich history and culture. The exhibits include pre-Hispanic artifacts, colonial paintings, and folk art.

  • Nogueras Museum: This museum is situated in an old hacienda and showcases the works of famous Mexican artists such as Jose Clemente Orozco and Gabriel Orozco.

  • Cerro de la Cruz: This hilltop lookout point offers stunning views of the city and surrounding area, as well as a monument to the Virgin of Guadalupe.

Natural Attractions of Colima State: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Volcán de Colima: This active volcano is one of Mexico’s highest peaks and attracts hikers and birdwatchers from around the world. You can hike up to the crater’s edge, or take a scenic drive through the surrounding national park.

  • Grutas de San Gabriel: These caves are located near the town of Comala and offer visitors a chance to explore the underground world of stalactites, stalagmites, and natural pools.

  • El Salto Waterfall: This waterfall is located near the town of Minatitlán and is a popular spot for swimming, cliff-jumping, and picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colima State

  • Nevado de Colima National Park: This park offers a variety of hiking trails, including the summit trail to the peak of the Colima Volcano, and the Camino Real trail, which follows the ancient path used by the Purépecha people.

  • Cerro Grande Trail: This trail is located near the town of Manzanillo and offers stunning views of the coast and surrounding jungle.

  • Colima City Bike Tour: This self-guided bike tour takes visitors through the historic district of Colima City, including its many plazas, churches, and museums.

Best Things to Do in Colima State: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour from Manzanillo during the winter months to see humpback whales and dolphins up close.

  • Tequila Tasting: Learn about the history and production of Mexico’s national drink with a tour of a local tequila distillery.

  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy a scenic ride through the mountains and forests of Colima State, led by local guides who will share their knowledge of the area’s flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colima State

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Colima City, explore the historic district, visit Museo Regional de Historia, and have dinner at a local restaurant.
2 Take a day trip to Nogueras Museum and the nearby town of Comala, known for its coffee and colonial architecture.
3 Hike to the peak of the Colima Volcano, or take a scenic drive through Nevado de Colima National Park.
4 Spend the day relaxing on the beaches of Manzanillo, or take a whale-watching tour.
5 Explore the underground world of Grutas de San Gabriel, or go on a horseback riding tour.
6 Visit El Salto Waterfall and enjoy a picnic lunch, then take a tequila tasting tour.
7 Depart Colima State for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Colima State

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pescado Zarandeado: A traditional grilled fish dish, marinated in adobo sauce and served with grilled onions and peppers.

  • Tatemado: A slow-cooked stew made with pork, chicken, or beef, with a smoky flavor from being cooked over an open flame.

  • Ceviche: Fresh seafood marinated in lime juice, with onions, cilantro, and avocado.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: Colima State is known for its high-quality tequila, made with locally grown blue agave plants.

  • Café de Olla: A traditional Mexican coffee, brewed with cinnamon and piloncillo (unrefined sugar).

  • Michelada: A beer cocktail made with lime juice, hot sauce, and Worcestershire sauce.

Where to Stay in Colima State: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Ceballos: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Colima City and offers comfortable rooms and a large outdoor pool.

  • Las Alamandas: This luxury resort is located on a secluded stretch of beach and offers guests privacy and relaxation, with five-star amenities.

  • El Estero Hotel: This eco-friendly hotel is located near the El Salto waterfall and offers guests a chance to immerse themselves in nature, with comfortable rooms and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Colima State: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mexican Handicrafts: Colima State is known for its beautiful pottery, woven baskets, and carved wooden figurines, all made by local artisans.

  • Tequila: Take home a bottle of the region’s famous tequila, made with blue agave plants grown on the local volcanic soil.

  • Coffee: Buy a bag of fresh-roasted coffee beans from one of the many coffee plantations in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Colima State

  • Religion: The majority of the population is Catholic, and religious festivals and processions are an important part of the local culture.

  • Language: The official language is Spanish, but many locals also speak indigenous languages such as Nahuatl and Purepecha.

  • Music and Dance: Traditional music and dance are an important aspect of Colima State’s cultural heritage, with lively shows performed at festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Colima State

The best time to visit Colima State is during the winter months, from December to March, when the weather is mild and dry, and the whale-watching season is in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colima State

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly and be respectful of religious traditions and cultural practices.

  • Try Local Food and Drink: Colima State has a rich culinary heritage, with many delicious dishes and unique drinks to try.

  • Learn Some Spanish: Knowing a few basic phrases in Spanish can go a long way in making meaningful connections with locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Environment: Colima State’s natural beauty is fragile, so be mindful of your impact on the environment and support sustainable tourism practices.

  • Disrespect the Locals: Treat locals with kindness and respect, and avoid any behavior or language that could be considered offensive or disrespectful.

  • Take Risks: While Colima State is generally safe, it’s important to take precautions and avoid any risky behavior or situations that could put you in danger.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colima State

Colima State is generally safe for tourists, but it’s always important to take precautions and stay alert. Petty crime such as theft and pickpocketing can occur in tourist areas, so be vigilant with your possessions and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. Always follow the advice of local authorities and avoid any areas with a high crime rate.

Health in Colima State

It’s important to take precautions to stay healthy while traveling in Colima State. Make sure to drink bottled water and only eat food that has been properly cooked and prepared. Protect yourself from the sun with sunscreen and a hat, and use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites and the potential spread of illnesses such as dengue fever or Zika virus.

Getting to Colima State: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colima State

The closest international airport is Manzanillo International Airport (ZLO), which offers flights to and from destinations in Mexico, the United States, and Canada. Another nearby airport is Guadalajara International Airport (GDL), which offers more options for international flights.

Bus Stations Near Colima State

The most convenient bus station for Colima City is the Central de Autobuses de Colima, with frequent services to and from Mexico City, Guadalajara, and other major cities in Mexico.

Other Options

You can also rent a car from one of the major rental companies at the airports or in Colima City, which gives you more freedom to explore the state at your own pace. Taxis and local buses are also available, although they can be less reliable and more difficult to navigate.

How to Get Around in Colima State

The best way to get around Colima State is by car, either by renting one or hiring a local driver. Public transportation options include local buses and taxis, but they can be less reliable and more difficult to navigate, especially if you don’t speak Spanish.

Where to Go Next from Colima State: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Guadalajara: Mexico’s second-largest city is known for its vibrant culture, historic architecture, and delicious food.

  • Tlaquepaque: This charming town near Guadalajara is famous for its traditional pottery and handicrafts, as well as its lively street markets.

  • Puerto Vallarta: This popular beach destination is located on the Pacific Coast and offers visitors a chance to relax on the sand, explore the historic downtown area, and enjoy a variety of water sports.

Conclusion

Colima State is a perfect destination for travelers who seek natural beauty, rich history and culture, and warm hospitality. With its stunning national parks, colonial architecture, and delicious food and drink scene, there’s something for everyone in Colima State. Plan your trip today and discover all that this beautiful state has to offer.
