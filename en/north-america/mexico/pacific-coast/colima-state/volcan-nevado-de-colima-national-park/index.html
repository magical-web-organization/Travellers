Introduction to Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park is a beautiful and diverse natural area located in the Colima state of Mexico. The park boasts of the Nevado de Colima volcano, one of Mexico’s most active volcanoes, and is home to several species of flora and fauna. Rich in culture and history, Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and nature lovers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Regional de Historia de Colima: A museum that showcases the pre-columbian history and culture of the Colima region.

  • El Chanal Archaeological Site: An ancient archaeological site that exhibits a series of shaft tombs and artifacts from the pre-classical period.

  • La Campana Hacienda: A historical site that features hacienda-era architecture and a museum that documents the region’s agricultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nevado de Colima Volcano: A majestic, active volcano that provides a scenic view of the park.

  • Cascada El Salto: A beautiful waterfall that offers a natural swimming pool and picnic spots.

  • Laguna de María: A serene lagoon that allows for bird watching and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

  • La Cumbre Trail: This trail takes you through lush vegetation, and you can enjoy views of the volcanoes and surrounding valleys along the way.

  • El Nevado Trail: This hike takes you to the summit of Nevado de Colima volcano, offering stunning views of the park and surrounding areas.

  • Laguna de María Trail: This tranquil trail takes you to the Laguna de Maria.

Best Things to Do in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: You can rent horses and explore the park while enjoying the scenic views.

  • Mountain Biking: Rent bicycles and ride through the beautiful routes of the park.

  • Bird Watching: The diverse ecosystem of the park allows for a great bird watching experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a hotel in Colima city.

Day 2: Visit Museo Regional de Historia de Colima and El Chanal Archaeological Site for a cultural experience.

Day 3: Hike to the top of Nevado de Colima volcano.

Day 4: Relax at Cascada El Salto.

Day 5: Bike through the park.

Day 6: Take a horseback ride around the park.

Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tortas de camaron: A shrimp sandwich that is a regional specialty.

  • Birria: A slow cooked, spicy meat stew that is served with tortillas and lime.

  • Tamales Colimenses: A type of tamale that is filled with chicken and pork.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: The famous Mexican beverage is produced in the state of Jalisco, near Colima.

  • Tuba: A refreshing drink made from coconut palm sap.

  • Café de olla: A sweet coffee beverage with cinnamon, sugar, and it is traditionally served in earthenware.

Where to Stay in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Hacienda de San Antonio: A luxurious hotel that is located on a former hacienda that is set amidst a coffee plantation.
  • Eco-Hotel Maria Gorda: A budget-friendly hostel that offers private rooms and dorm-style accommodations.
  • Villa de la Rosa: A cozy guesthouse that is located near the park’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Colima pottery is an ancient art form that has been practiced since pre-columbian times.

  • Coffee: The park’s surrounding regions produce some of the finest coffee in Mexico.

  • Handcrafted Goods: You can find beautifully handcrafted items such as textiles, wood carvings, and other traditional crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

  • Fiesta Charro: A rodeo-style event that is held annually and reflects the region’s charro culture.

  • Dia de Los Muertos: Celebrated annually on November 1-2, this day is marked by colorful parades, which pays homage to departed souls.

  • Traditional Music and Dance: The region is known for its traditional music and dance forms such as Son Jarocho and La Bamba.

Best Time to Visit Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

The best time to visit Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park is between November to April when the weather is cooler, and the chances of rainfall are minimal. Summer months are hot, and there can be heavy rains, and the park can become muddy and challenging to navigate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Carry enough water, especially while hiking or biking.

  • Stay hydrated and use sunscreen while exploring the park.

Don’t:

  • Litter or deface any natural or cultural site.

  • Touch or disturb the wildlife in the park.

  • Ignore signs or warnings about volcanic activity.

Safety and Health

Safety in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

  • Carry a first-aid kit, and be prepared for any emergencies.

  • Stay alert and wary of slippery trails, mudslides, or uneven terrain.

  • Be watchful of aggressive animals, such as scorpions or rattlesnakes.

Health in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

  • Get vaccinated against typhoid and hepatitis A.

  • Carry mosquito repellent and other necessary medications as per your doctor’s advice.

  • Beware of altitude sickness while trekking higher up in the mountains.

Getting to Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

The nearest airport to Volcan Nevado de Colima National park is in Colima city, approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

Several bus companies operate regular buses from major cities in Mexico to Colima city. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to the park.

Other Options

You can also opt for renting a car and driving to the park, which is a convenient way to get around and explore the area.

How to Get Around in Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park

The best way to get around Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park is by hiking, biking, or on horseback. Rental bicycles and horses are available near the park’s entrance.

Where to Go Next from Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manzanillo: A bustling coastal town with serene beaches and a picturesque harbor.

  • Guadalajara: The second-largest city in Mexico that boasts of historical architecture, museums, and a vibrant food scene.

  • Mazamitla: A charming mountain town with a landscape of pine forests and towering mountains.

Conclusion

Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience Mexico’s natural beauty and rich history. With several cultural and natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drinks, Volcan Nevado de Colima National Park is the perfect place for an unforgettable travel experience. So pack your bags and embark on an adventure to this beautiful park!
