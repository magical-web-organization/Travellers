Introduction to Tecomán

Tecomán is a peaceful city located in the state of Colima in Mexico. Its name means “Land of Braves” in Nahuatl and it is known for its rich history and natural beauty. Tourists often flock to Tecomán to admire the pre-Columbian artifacts and colonial architecture that abound the city. The city is also surrounded by beautiful natural attractions such as beaches and mountains, making it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Tecomán

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Regional Museum of Tecomán: This museum houses an impressive collection of pre-Hispanic and colonial artifacts, as well as exhibits on the history, culture, and geography of Tecomán.
  • San Francisco de Asis Church: This Baroque-style church was built in the 18th century and is considered one of the most important religious buildings in Tecomán.
  • La Campana Cultural Center: This center is housed in a restored colonial house and offers exhibitions of contemporary and folk artwork, as well as music and dance performances.

Natural Attractions of Tecomán: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Salto Waterfall: This picturesque waterfall is surrounded by lush vegetation and is a popular destination for hiking and sightseeing.
  • Palma Sola Archaeological Site: This ancient archaeological site is located on a hill overlooking Tecomán and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding forests.
  • Cuyutlan Beach: This beautiful black-sand beach is the perfect place to relax and soak up the sun. It also offers water activities such as swimming, kayaking, and surfing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tecomán

  • La Piedra del Zorrillo Trail: This trail takes hikers through a beautiful forest to the top of a hill where they can enjoy stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside.
  • El Salto Trail: This trail starts from the El Salto Waterfall and takes hikers through a scenic forest and along a river to a picturesque swimming hole.
  • Los Tepames Trail: This trail offers hikers a glimpse of local countryside life as they pass through small farms and villages.

Best Things to Do in Tecomán: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Guided Tour of the Regional Museum of Tecomán: A professional guide will lead you through the museum’s exhibits and provide insight on the city’s history and culture.
  • Visit the Mercado Municipal: This bustling market offers a variety of local handicrafts, fresh produce, and delicious street food.
  • Take a Day Trip to Colima: The neighboring city of Colima is home to several museums, parks, and colonial architecture that are worth a visit.

Food and Drink Scene of Tecomán

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pescado Zarandeado: This grilled fish dish is seasoned with a blend of herbs and spices and served with fresh tortillas and salsa.
  • Sopa de Mariscos: This hearty seafood soup is made with a variety of shellfish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Tamales Dulces: These sweet tamales are made with corn dough, raisins, cinnamon, and sugar, and are perfect for dessert or breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: This quintessential Mexican beverage is made from fermented agave and is often served as a shot with lime and salt.
  • Colima Beer: This local brewery produces a variety of craft beers that are perfect for relaxing after a day of sightseeing.
  • Agua de Jamaica: This refreshing hibiscus tea is often served with meals and is a perfect thirst quencher on hot days.

Where to Stay in Tecomán: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Casa Blanca: This hotel is located in the heart of Tecomán and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful courtyard.
  • Hostal Don Potro: This hostel is located in a restored colonial house and offers beautiful rooms and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Eco Hotel Xavi: This eco-friendly hotel is located near El Salto Waterfall and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and countryside.

What to Buy in Tecomán: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery: Tecomán is known for its pottery, and there are several shops that sell hand-crafted ceramics and other pottery items.
  • Handicrafts: The Mercado Municipal is a great place to find local handicrafts such as woven baskets, wood carvings, and embroidered textiles.
  • Coffee: Tecomán is known for its delicious coffee, which can be purchased in local markets and specialty shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Tecomán

Tecomán is a traditional Mexican city, and locals are proud of their culture and history. It is important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Tipping is common in restaurants and cafes, and locals are generally friendly and welcoming to tourists.

Best Time to Visit Tecomán

The best time to visit Tecomán is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this time, temperatures are mild and there is little rainfall, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tecomán

Do:

  • Do try the local food and beverages.
  • Do visit the Regional Museum of Tecomán.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t walk alone at night.
  • Don’t litter on the beaches.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tecomán

Tecomán is generally a safe city, but it is always important to take precautions when traveling. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep your belongings secure. It is also a good idea to carry a copy of your passport and other important documents with you.

Health in Tecomán

There are several hospitals and clinics in Tecomán that provide medical care to locals and tourists. It is important to bring any necessary medications with you, and to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Tecomán: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tecomán

The nearest international airport to Tecomán is Manzanillo International Airport, which is located approximately 20 miles from the city center. There are several airlines that offer direct flights to Manzanillo from major cities in Mexico and the United States.

Bus Stations Near Tecomán

Tecomán has a large bus station with connections to major cities throughout Mexico. There are also several local bus companies that offer connections to nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Tecomán, and are a convenient way to explore the city and surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Tecomán

The best way to get around Tecomán is by taxi or rental car. There are also several local buses that run throughout the city and surrounding areas, but they can be unpredictable and crowded.

Where to Go Next from Tecomán: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Colima: The neighboring city of Colima offers several museums, parks, and colonial architecture that are worth a visit.
  • Nevado de Colima National Park: This national park features mountains, lakes, and forests, and offers several hiking and camping opportunities.
  • Barra de Navidad: This charming beach town is located approximately 60 miles from Tecomán and offers beautiful beaches, water activities, and local seafood.

Conclusion

Tecomán is a hidden gem in Mexico that offers visitors a glimpse of authentic Mexican culture and natural beauty. With its rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and delicious food, Tecomán is the perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful and unique travel experience.
