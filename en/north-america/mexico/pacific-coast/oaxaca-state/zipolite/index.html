Are you on the lookout for a serene, unspoiled destination for your next vacation? Head to Zipolite, a hidden gem in the state of Oaxaca in the Pacific Coast region of Mexico. This secluded beach town enchants visitors with its pristine natural beauty, laid-back lifestyle, and authentic culture.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Zipolite, you will discover the top attractions, outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, food scene, where to stay, shopping options, and much more. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Zipolite

Zipolite is a small, picturesque beach town in the southern part of Oaxaca state, Mexico, located on the Pacific coast. The word “Zipolite” comes from the Nahuatl language and means “beach of the dead,” named after the powerful currents that make it dangerous for swimmers. However, the locals have taken steps to make the beach safer by placing warning flags and buoys.

Despite its name, Zipolite is a lively and charming town that has long been a favorite destination for backpackers, surfers, and wellness enthusiasts. Its long, crescent-shaped beach stretches for 1.5 miles and is flanked by stunning cliffs and rock formations. The town’s narrow streets are lined with colorful houses, restaurants, and shops. The atmosphere is relaxed and welcoming, with a strong emphasis on community and sustainability. Come and experience the magic of Zipolite for yourself!

List of Places to Visit in Zipolite

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Zipolite is primarily known for its natural beauty, it also has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the pre-colonial era. Here are some of the top cultural landmarks to explore in Zipolite:

  • San Pedro y San Pablo Church: This historic church, located in the heart of the town, was built in the 16th century by Spanish friars. It features traditional baroque architecture and ornate façades.

  • Ruins of San Agustin de la Barra: These ancient ruins are located on a hill overlooking the sea, about 3 miles from Zipolite. They are believed to be the remains of a Zapotec city from around 500 B.C. They offer a fascinating glimpse into the region’s indigenous history and architecture.

  • Museo de la Roca: This small museum, located on the outskirts of Zipolite, showcases a collection of petrified wood and fossils found in the area. It also offers guided tours of the nearby rock formations and caves.

Natural Attractions of Zipolite: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zipolite is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Punta Cometa: This stunning viewpoint, located at the westernmost point of Zipolite beach, offers panoramic views of the coast and the Pacific Ocean. It is also a great spot for whale watching during the winter months.

  • Zipolite Beach: This long, wide stretch of sand is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and surfing. The waves can be strong, though, so it’s recommended to swim only in the designated areas and under the supervision of a lifeguard.

  • Lagunas de Chacahua National Park: This protected area, located about 12 miles from Zipolite, features mangrove forests, lagoons, and beaches. It is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including crocodiles, iguanas, and migratory birds. Visitors can explore the park’s trails, go kayaking, and take boat tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zipolite

Zipolite is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery that is best explored on foot or by bike. Whether you are a seasoned hiker or a casual stroller, here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Sierra Madre Trail: This challenging, 10-mile trail takes trekkers through the rugged mountains and hills surrounding Zipolite. It offers stunning views of the coast and the forests, as well as glimpses of local flora and fauna.

  • Mazunte Route: This easy, 2-mile walk takes visitors from Zipolite to the neighboring beach town of Mazunte. Along the way, you can enjoy beautiful coastal views, spot sea turtles, and browse local artisan shops and restaurants.

Best Things to Do in Zipolite: Activities and Tours

Zipolite offers a wealth of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best options:

  • Surfing: Zipolite is renowned for its excellent surfing conditions, thanks to its consistent waves and warm water. Several local surf schools offer lessons and equipment rental for beginners and advanced surfers.

  • Yoga and Meditation: Zipolite is also a popular destination for wellness tourism, with a wide range of yoga studios, meditation centers, and spiritual retreats. Many of them offer drop-in classes or multi-day packages.

  • Cacao Farm Tour: This guided tour takes visitors to a nearby cacao farm, where they can learn about the history and cultivation of this ancient crop. They can also taste different types of chocolate and buy organic products to take home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zipolite

If you have a week to spare, here is a sample itinerary that allows you to explore the best of Zipolite and its surroundings:

Day 1: Arrival in Zipolite. Check into your hotel or hostel and relax on the beach.

Day 2: Explore the town’s landmarks and museums. Visit the San Pedro y San Pablo Church, the Ruins of San Agustin de la Barra, and the Museo de la Roca.

Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking adventure. Try the Sierra Madre Trail or the Mazunte Route.

Day 4: Take a boat tour of the Lagunas de Chacahua National Park. Discover the park’s mangroves, lagoons, and wildlife.

Day 5: Learn to surf. Sign up for a surfing lesson with a local school or rent a board and hit the waves on your own.

Day 6: Relax and rejuvenate with a yoga or meditation session. Try one of the many yoga studios or spiritual retreats in the area.

Day 7: Departure from Zipolite. Say farewell to this beautiful town and start planning your next trip!

Food and Drink Scene of Zipolite

Zipolite offers a delicious and diverse culinary scene, with a focus on fresh seafood, local ingredients, and traditional recipes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tlayudas: These large, crispy tortillas are topped with refried beans, cheese, meat or vegetables, and salsa. They are a staple of Oaxacan cuisine and can be found in many restaurants in Zipolite.

  • Ceviche: This refreshing dish features raw fish or seafood marinated in lime juice, spices, and herbs. It’s perfect for a hot summer day on the beach.

  • Mole Negro: This signature dish of Oaxaca consists of chicken in a rich, dark sauce made of chocolate, chiles, nuts, and spices. It’s a complex and flavorful dish that takes hours to prepare.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: This distilled alcoholic beverage, made from the agave plant, is a cultural icon of Oaxaca. It’s often served with orange slices and salt, and has a strong, smoky flavor.

  • Horchata: This sweet, milky drink is made from ground rice and cinnamon. It’s a refreshing alternative to sodas and juices.

  • Agua Fresca: This fruit-infused water is a popular thirst-quencher in Mexico. You can find it in many flavors, such as pineapple, watermelon, and hibiscus.

Where to Stay in Zipolite: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zipolite offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Posada Mexico: This charming guesthouse, located on the hillside overlooking the beach, offers comfortable rooms, a communal pool, and a restaurant with Mexican and international cuisine.

  • El Alquimista: This eco-friendly hotel, located on the beach, is an oasis of tranquility and luxury. It features stylish bungalows, a spa, a yoga studio, and an ocean-view restaurant.

  • Hostal Casa del Angel: This budget-friendly hostel, located in the heart of Zipolite, offers dorm rooms and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a rooftop terrace with sea view.

What to Buy in Zipolite: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zipolite offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or memory keepsakes. Here are some of the best options:

  • Textiles: Oaxaca is famous for its colorful and intricate textiles, such as woven blankets, rugs, and clothing. Many local shops in Zipolite offer handmade textiles made by indigenous cooperatives.

  • Jewelry: If you are looking for unique and eye-catching jewelry, check out the local artisan shops that sell silver, gold, and beaded pieces.

  • Organic Products: Zipolite is home to several organic farms and markets that offer a range of natural and sustainable products, such as coffee, honey, soaps, and cosmetics.

Local Culture and Customs of Zipolite

Zipolite prides itself on its strong community spirit and respect for local customs and traditions. Here are some tips to make the most of your stay:

  • Respect the dress code: Zipolite is a liberal and accepting town, but it’s important to be mindful of the dress code on the beach and in public areas. Nudity is allowed only in the designated areas, and it’s recommended to wear modest clothing when visiting landmarks or cultural events.

  • Learn some Spanish: While many locals speak English, it’s always appreciated when visitors make an effort to speak Spanish or learn some common phrases.

  • Practice sustainability: Zipolite is committed to reducing its environmental impact and promoting eco-friendly practices. Be mindful of your water use, waste disposal, and energy consumption.

Best Time to Visit Zipolite

The best time to visit Zipolite depends on your preferences and priorities. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Low Season: From May to November, the weather is hot and humid, and there may be some occasional rain. However, this is the best time to find lower prices and fewer crowds.

  • High Season: From December to April, the weather is mild and sunny, and there are many festivals and cultural events. However, this is also the busiest and most expensive time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zipolite

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience in Zipolite, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Always carry cash: Most establishments in Zipolite don’t accept credit cards, so make sure you have enough cash on hand.

  • Try local street food: Some of the tastiest and most authentic food in Zipolite can be found in the street vendors and markets. Don’t be afraid to try new flavors and dishes!

  • Respect the local customs and beliefs: Whether it’s the dress code, the religious festivals, or the social norms, make sure you are aware and respectful of the local cultural practices.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: To avoid getting sick from contaminated water, always drink bottled water, and use it for brushing your teeth and washing your fruit and vegetables.

  • Disrespect the environment: Zipolite’s natural beauty is fragile and unique, and it’s important to preserve it for future generations. Don’t litter, and be mindful of your waste and carbon footprint.

  • Be reckless on the beach: While Zipolite is a great place for swimming and surfing, it’s important to follow the safety guidelines and respect the lifeguards’ instructions.

Safety and Health

Zipolite is a relatively safe and healthy destination, with relatively low crime rates and basic medical facilities. However, it’s always important to take precautions and be prepared. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Zipolite

  • Avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas or beaches.

  • Keep your valuables close and secure, and don’t leave them unattended.

  • Follow the guidelines for swimming, surfing, and other outdoor activities, and don’t take unnecessary risks.

Health in Zipolite

  • Consult your doctor before traveling to make sure you are up-to-date on your vaccines and medications.

  • Use mosquito repellent and protective clothing to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus.

  • Drink bottled water, and be cautious of raw or undercooked food that could cause stomach problems.

Getting to Zipolite: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zipolite is relatively close to major cities and tourist destinations, but it can be challenging to navigate the local transportation systems. Here are some options and tips:

Airports Near Zipolite

  • Bahías de Huatulco International Airport (HUX): This airport is the closest to Zipolite, about 35 miles away. It offers flights from several major Mexican and international cities.

  • Xoxocotlán International Airport (OAX): This airport is located in Oaxaca city, about 200 miles away. It offers flights from several Mexican cities, as well as direct flights from Houston and Los Angeles.

Bus Stations Near Zipolite

  • Pochutla Bus Station: This station is located in the nearby town of Pochutla, about 6 miles away from Zipolite. It offers frequent buses to and from major cities in Oaxaca and other states.

  • Oaxaca City Bus Station: This station is located in the capital city of Oaxaca, about 200 miles away from Zipolite. It offers buses to and from several destinations in Mexico.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: If you prefer to drive yourself, rental cars are available at the airports and major cities. Keep in mind that Mexican traffic laws and driving practices may differ from those in other countries.

  • Shared Taxis: Shared taxi vans, called “colectivos,” run between Zipolite and other nearby towns and destinations. They are relatively cheap and convenient, but may not have set schedules or fixed prices.

How to Get Around in Zipolite

Once you arrive in Zipolite, there are several options for getting around:

  • Walking: Zipolite is a small and compact town, and most of the attractions and amenities are within walking distance.

  • Bicycle Rentals: There are several bike rental shops in Zipolite that offer affordable rates and quality equipment. Bicycles are a great way to explore the town and its surroundings at your own pace.

  • Public Transportation: There are local buses and shared taxis that run along the nearby coastal highway. They can take you to other towns, beaches, and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Zipolite: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Puerto Escondido: This larger beach town, located about 50 miles from Zipolite, offers a wider range of amenities and activities, such as fishing, ecotourism, and nightlife.

  • Huatulco: This resort area, located about 30 miles from Zipolite, features nine bays and 36 beaches, as well as golf courses, spas, and ecotourism opportunities.

  • Oaxaca City: This vibrant colonial city, located about 200 miles from Zipolite, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a cultural hub of Mexico. It offers a rich history, art, music, and gastronomy scene.

Conclusion

Zipolite is a true hidden gem of Mexico, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and laid-back lifestyle. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, wellness retreats, or simply relaxation on the beach, Zipolite has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to this enchanting town. Buen viaje!
