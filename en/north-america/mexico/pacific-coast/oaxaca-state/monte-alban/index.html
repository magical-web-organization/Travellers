Introduction to Monte Alban

Monte Alban, located in the Oaxaca state of Mexico in the Pacific Coast region of North America, is one of the most picturesque and historically significant attractions in the country. Monte Alban is an ancient Mesoamerican city that was built by the Zapotecs between the 5th and 8th centuries AD. The ruins of Monte Alban are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are also considered to be one of the most important archaeological sites in Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Monte Alban

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Monte Alban Archaeological Site: This ancient city has been around for more than 1,500 years and is home to some of the most important cultural artifacts of the Zapotec civilization.
  • Monte Alban Museum: The Monte Alban Museum houses a collection of artifacts that have been found in and around Monte Alban.
  • Atzompa Archaeological Site: Atzompa was originally a satellite city of Monte Alban, and the archaeological site contains several significant structures and neighborhoods that date to the same period as Monte Alban.
  • Santo Domingo de Guzman Church and Cultural Center: This 16th-century Baroque-style church contains an impressive collection of pre-Columbian artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Monte Alban: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Monte Alban Viewpoint: A breathtaking view of the ruins and the city of Oaxaca can be seen from this viewpoint. Visitors can take a cable car to the top of the mountain to reach this viewpoint.
  • Los Pinos Lookout Point: Located on the backside of the Monte Alban Archaeological Site, visitors can enjoy a panoramic view of Oaxaca and the mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monte Alban

One great way to experience Monte Alban is by hiking or biking in and around the site. There are many trails in the area that offer stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Monte Alban: Activities and Tours

  • Monte Alban Archaeological Tour: Learn about the ancient Zapotec civilization and the significance of Monte Alban with a guided tour.
  • Market Tour: Experience the hustle and bustle of a local market, where you can buy artisanal crafts, handmade textiles, and traditional Oaxacan foods.
  • Cooking Classes: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Oaxacan dishes from a local chef.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monte Alban

Day 1: Arrive in Oaxaca and check-in at your hotel Day 2: Visit the Monte Alban Archaeological Site Day 3: Take a cooking class Day 4: Visit Santo Domingo Church and Cultural Center Day 5: Go on a hike or bike ride through the surrounding forests and mountains Day 6: Visit the Atzompa Archaeological Site Day 7: Enjoy a day trip to the nearby city of Mitla

Food and Drink Scene of Monte Alban

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tlayudas: A large homemade tortilla topped with refried beans, cheese, and meat.
  • Mole: A rich, savory sauce made with more than 20 different ingredients, including chiles, nuts, and chocolate.
  • Tamales: A traditional Mexican dish that consists of a corn-based dough stuffed with meat or vegetables and steamed in a banana leaf.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the agave plant, mezcal is a signature drink of Oaxaca.
  • Tejate: A non-alcoholic beverage made from ground corn, cacao beans, and flor de cacao (a type of flower).

Where to Stay in Monte Alban: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation in Oaxaca that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Casa de la Tía Tere: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Oaxaca and offers comfortable rooms, a sparkling outdoor pool, and a delicious complimentary breakfast.
  • Hostal La Noria: This is a great option for budget travelers looking for a comfortable and affordable place to stay. The hotel offers private and shared rooms, a breakfast buffet, and a sunny outdoor patio.

What to Buy in Monte Alban: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oaxacan Handicrafts: The markets are filled with beautiful textiles, ceramics, jewelry, and other handicrafts produced by local artisans.
  • Mezcal: Don’t forget to bring back a bottle (or two!) of this beloved Oaxacan drink.

Local Culture and Customs of Monte Alban

Oaxaca is a culturally rich region, and travelers should respect the local customs and traditions, which include:

  • Language: Although Spanish is the official language, many indigenous languages are also spoken in the area, including Zapotec.
  • Religion: The majority of the population is Roman Catholic, and visitors should be respectful of local religious practices.
  • Greetings: Handshakes and kisses on the cheek are common forms of greetings in Oaxaca.

Best Time to Visit Monte Alban

The best time to visit Monte Alban is from November to March, when the weather is mild and the rainy season is over. The site is open year-round, but it can get very hot and crowded during the peak season, from April to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monte Alban

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting historical and religious sites
  • Practice sustainable tourism by refraining from littering or damaging the environment
  • Support local businesses and artisans by purchasing locally made products

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on any archaeological structures
  • Take photographs of local people without their permission
  • Use illegal drugs or engage in other illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Monte Alban

Monte Alban is generally a safe place to visit, but as with any tourist destination, there are precautions visitors should take:

  • Avoid carrying large sums of cash or important documents on your person.
  • Be wary of pickpockets in crowded areas, such as markets and bus stations.
  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas, particularly at night.

Health in Monte Alban

It’s important to always prioritize your health and safety when traveling. Here are some ways to stay healthy in Monte Alban:

  • Bottled water is readily available and is recommended over tap water
  • Wear sunscreen and appropriate clothing to avoid sunburn
  • Seek medical attention if you experience symptoms of altitude sickness, such as nausea, dizziness, or shortness of breath.

Getting to Monte Alban: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monte Alban

The nearest international airport is Oaxaca International Airport (OAX), which is approximately 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Monte Alban

ADO Bus Station is located in the city of Oaxaca and offers regular bus services to Monte Alban.

Other Options

Taxis and vans are also available and can be arranged through hotels or tour companies.

How to Get Around in Monte Alban

The best way to get around Monte Alban is by foot, as the archaeological site is not very large. However, taxis and vans are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Monte Alban: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many other amazing places to visit in the Oaxaca region, including:

  • The City of Oaxaca: Home to many cultural and historical attractions, such as the Santo Domingo de Guzman Church and Cultural Center.
  • The City of Mitla: Known for its many well-preserved ancient structures and buildings from the Zapotec culture.
  • Hierve el Agua: A natural wonder that features mineral springs and cascading pools in a stunning mountain setting.

Conclusion

Monte Alban is one of Mexico’s most spectacular cultural and historical sites, offering visitors a unique opportunity to explore and learn about the ancient Zapotec civilization. With its stunning architecture, beautiful natural surroundings, and rich cultural heritage, Monte Alban is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to experience the best that Mexico has to offer.
