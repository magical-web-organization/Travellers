Introduction to Hierve el Agua

Located in the Pacific Coast of Mexico, in the state of Oaxaca, Hierve el Agua is a natural wonder that tourists should not miss. Hierve el Agua, which means “the water boils,” is a series of mineral terraces that look like cascading waterfalls. The terraces were formed over millions of years from calcium carbonate deposits in the water, and they are the only such formations in the world that are not made of limestone. The scenery surrounding Hierve el Agua is stunning, with mountains, valleys and a clear blue sky, making it a perfect place for outdoor activities.
