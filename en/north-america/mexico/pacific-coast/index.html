Introduction to Pacific Coast

Located on the northwestern coast of Mexico, Pacific Coast is a breathtaking place that offers visitors an incredible travel experience. With its stunningly beautiful beaches, towering mountains, lush forests and vibrant culture, Pacific Coast is a paradise for photographers, adventure enthusiasts and people in search of a laid-back vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Pacific Coast

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Pacific Coast that are must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Mazatlan Museum: This museum curates a large number of historical artifacts, art pieces, and displays about the history and culture of Mazatlan.

  2. The Palacio de Gobierno: The Palacio de Gobierno, or the Government Palace is a magnificent building that houses the government of Sinaloa.

Natural Attractions of Pacific Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Isla Isabel National Park: This national park is renowned for its diverse ecosystem and is home to some of the most unique species of flora and fauna.

  2. Copper Canyon: The Copper Canyon is a fascinating natural attraction and is considered to be one of the most significant natural wonders of Mexico.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pacific Coast

If you’re an adventure lover, Pacific Coast offers a variety of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few popular routes to consider:

  1. Huatulco Coastal hike: A scenic hike that offers spectacular views of the Pacific Ocean, beaches and tropical forests.

  2. The Sierra Madre Trail: A rugged mountainous trail that offers a challenging yet rewarding experience of natural wonders.

Best Things to Do in Pacific Coast: Activities and Tours

  1. Whale Watching: The annual migration of humpback whales is a popular attraction that is a must-see sight.

  2. Surfing: If you’re into surfing, Pacific Coast is one of the leading destinations for surfers due to the availability of diverse surf breaks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pacific Coast

Here is a weeklong itinerary that will enable you to experience the best of Pacific Coast:

Day 1: Arrive in Mazatlan, spend an afternoon relaxing and exploring the Old Town.

Day 2: Explore the Museums and Historical Places and visit some iconic landmarks.

Day 3: Head to the beautiful Isla Isabel National Park.

Day 4: Visit the Copper Canyon.

Day 5: Take a hike on the Huatulco Coastal trail.

Day 6: Go Whale watching and surfing for the day.

Day 7: Spend your last day shopping for souvenirs and savouring local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Pacific Coast

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the dishes that are unique to Pacific Coast and are must-try delicacies include:

  1. Shrimp Tacos: Pacific Coast is famous for its shrimp tacos, the perfect blend of seafood and Mexican seasoning.

  2. Pozole: Pozole is an authentic Mexican dish consisting of hominy soup and meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Margarita: The classic tequila-based cocktail that originated in Mexico and is a must-try drink.

  2. Michelada: A unique Mexican beer cocktail that is a refreshing blend of beer, tomato juice, hot sauce, lime juice and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Pacific Coast

The culture of Pacific Coast is vibrant, and it has many indigenous communities that have retained their unique identity and heritage. Respect for elders, extended family, and hospitality are the hallmarks of Pacific Coast culture.

Best Time to Visit Pacific Coast

The best time to visit Pacific Coast is between November and April when temperatures are comfortable, and rainfall is low. Plan your travel in advance to avoid peak season crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pacific Coast

Do:

  1. Respect the customs and traditions of local communities.

  2. Carry your travel documents and identification at all times.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t wear expensive jewelry or anything that may attract unnecessary attention.

  2. Don’t patronize illegal drug trades that take place in some regions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pacific Coast

While Pacific Coast is generally a safe place, it is advisable to keep an eye out for your belongings in public places, avoid going out late at night and to always be alert and cautious.

Health in Pacific Coast

Medical facilities in the major cities of Pacific Coast are reliable, but it is advisable to take precautions to avoid illness. Use bottled water for drinking purposes, avoid street food and take necessary vaccination before traveling.

How to Get Around in Pacific Coast

The most common form of public transport in Pacific Coast is via buses. The buses are usually inexpensive and operate on defined routes. For shorter distances, taxi services are available.
