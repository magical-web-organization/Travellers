Located in the Pacific Coast of the State of Michoacan, Maruata is a picturesque beach destination that offers visitors a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. From swimming in crystal-clear waters to hiking along scenic routes, exploring historical landmarks and tasting local cuisine, there are countless reasons to visit Maruata. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Maruata and make the most of your experience.

Introduction to Maruata

Maruata is a small fishing village that has gradually gained popularity among tourists for its stunning beaches and relaxed atmosphere. Surrounded by lush vegetation and mountains, Maruata is a perfect escape for nature lovers and those seeking a tranquil retreat. The village has a distinctly bohemian vibe, with colorful murals adorning the streets, and local artisans selling handmade crafts. Whether you’re a backpacker on a budget or a luxury seeker, Maruata offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Maruata

Maruata is filled with interesting places to visit, from historical landmarks to natural wonders. Here’s a list of some of the must-see locations in Maruata:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Templo San Nicolás de Tolentino: This colonial-style church was built in the 16th century and is known for its baroque architecture and intricate carvings. It hosts an annual festival in September.
  • Museo Comunitario de Maruata: This community-run museum showcases the history, art, and culture of the indigenous Purepecha people in the region. It has a collection of artifacts and exhibits dating back to pre-Columbian times.
  • Casa de la Cultura: This cultural center offers workshops on art, music, and dance. It also hosts events and exhibitions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Maruata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa Maruata: This pristine beach is the main attraction in Maruata. The turquoise waters and soft sand make it the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
  • Caleta de Campos: This nearby fishing town has a small beach with clear waters and fresh seafood restaurants.
  • Cerro de la Cruz: This hill offers panoramic views of Maruata and the surrounding mountains. It’s a popular spot for hiking and watching the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maruata

Maruata is also a great place for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes:

  • Cascada Boca de Iguanas: This trail leads to a beautiful waterfall with a natural pool. It’s a great spot for swimming and picnicking.
  • Playa de la Eréndira: This beach is accessible through a scenic coastal trail. It’s also a good spot for birdwatching.
  • Cerro del Cacique: This mountain offers challenging hikes and stunning views of the coastline. It’s best to go early in the morning to avoid the heat.

Best Things to Do in Maruata: Activities and Tours

Apart from swimming and hiking, Maruata offers many other activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best include:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The waters around Maruata are home to a variety of marine life, including sea turtles, manta rays, and colorful fish. There are several tour operators that offer snorkeling and diving trips.
  • Kayaking and Stand-up Paddleboarding: The calm waters of Caleta de Campos are perfect for kayaking and paddleboarding. You can rent equipment in town.
  • Boat Tours: You can take a boat tour to explore the coastline and nearby islands. Some tours include lunch and snorkeling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maruata

If you have a week to spend in Maruata, here’s an itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Itinerary
1 Arrive in Maruata, settle into your accommodation, and explore the town center.
2 Spend the day at Playa Maruata swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.
3 Take a hiking trip to Cascada Boca de Iguanas and have a picnic lunch by the waterfall.
4 Visit the Casa de la Cultura and attend a workshop or event. In the afternoon, walk along the coastal trail to Playa de la Eréndira.
5 Take a boat tour to explore the nearby islands.
6 Spend the day relaxing at Caleta de Campos beach and have lunch at a seafood restaurant.
7 Hike to the top of Cerro del Cacique and watch the sunset. In the evening, have a farewell dinner in town.

Food and Drink Scene of Maruata

Maruata has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for seafood lovers and vegetarians. Here are some dishes and beverages you must try during your visit to Maruata:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales Purepechas: These tamales are made with a mixture of ground corn, chilies, and meat or vegetables, wrapped in a corn husk and steamed.
  • Pescado Zarandeado: This grilled fish dish is seasoned with spices and served with tortillas and salsa.
  • Chilate: This drink is made with cornmeal, chocolate, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: This traditional agave-based spirit is popular in Michoacan. You can try it neat or in a cocktail.
  • Cerveza Artesanal: Maruata has a local beer scene, with some breweries offering tours and tastings.
  • Aguas Frescas: These non-alcoholic drinks are made with fresh fruit and water or milk.

Where to Stay in Maruata: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maruata has accommodations for all budgets and preferences. You can choose from beachfront hotels, eco-lodges, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • Cabañas Member’s: This eco-lodge has sea-view bungalows made of wood and palm leaves. It’s located within walking distance to Playa Maruata.
  • El Pirata: This hostel has private rooms and shared dorms. It has a rooftop terrace with ocean views.
  • Villas Xichu: These rustic-chic villas have private pools and gardens. They’re located in a secluded area, surrounded by nature.

What to Buy in Maruata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maruata is a great place to find unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts: You can buy handmade pottery, textiles, and jewelry from local artisans in the town center.
  • Mezcal and Beer: There are some microbreweries and artisanal mezcal producers in the area that sell their products to take home.
  • Salsa and Spices: You can find some delicious salsas and spices made with local ingredients in the markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Maruata

Maruata is home to indigenous communities that have preserved their traditions and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect their culture and engage with them in a respectful and responsible way. Some cultural practices you might encounter in Maruata include:

  • Music and Dance: The Purepecha people have a rich musical and dance tradition, and there are often performances and workshops held in town.
  • Art and Crafts: Local artisans use traditional techniques to create unique pottery, textiles, and other crafts. You can visit their workshops and learn about their craft.

Best Time to Visit Maruata

Maruata has a tropical climate, with warm temperatures and high humidity year-round. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to April, when there’s less rainfall and more sunshine. However, be prepared for higher hotel rates and crowds during this time. The rainy season, from May to October, can also be a good time to visit if you don’t mind occasional showers and humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maruata

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Maruata:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Engage with the local community in a respectful and responsible way.
  • Try the local cuisine and support small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Buy souvenirs made from endangered species or illegal items.
  • Disrespect the environment or litter on the beaches.
  • Disrupt local celebrations or religious practices.

Safety and Health

Maruata is generally considered a safe destination, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Maruata

  • Take care of your belongings in crowded areas and on public transportation.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.
  • Follow safety instructions for water activities and outdoor adventures.

Health in Maruata

  • Drink bottled water and avoid eating food from street vendors that might not be properly cooked.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburns.
  • Bring insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Maruata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Maruata is located about 3 hours from the nearest airport, in the city of Morelia. Here are some transportation options to get to Maruata:

Airports Near Maruata

  • General Francisco J. Mujica International Airport: This airport is located in the city of Morelia, about 3.5 hours from Maruata. It has domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Maruata

  • Central de Autobuses de Morelia: This bus station has regular buses to the nearby town of Coalcomán. From there, you can take a taxi to Maruata.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: You can hire a private transfer service from Morelia to Maruata, which can be more comfortable and convenient.

How to Get Around in Maruata

Maruata is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you want to visit nearby beaches or go on a hiking trip, you might need transportation. Here are some options:

  • Taxi: There are taxis available in town that can take you to nearby locations.
  • Bicycle Rental: You can rent a bicycle in town and explore the surrounding areas.
  • Car Rental: There are car rental services available in the nearby cities of Morelia and Uruapan.

Where to Go Next from Maruata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Maruata is located in a beautiful region of Mexico with many other interesting destinations nearby. Here are some ideas for your next trip:

  • Lázaro Cárdenas: This port city is about 1.5 hours from Maruata and has a mix of urban and coastal attractions, including a malecón and a lighthouse.
  • La Ticla: This small beach town is about 30 minutes from Maruata and is known for its consistent surf breaks and relaxed atmosphere.
  • Uruapan: This colonial town is about 4 hours from Maruata and is famous for its waterfalls, markets, and traditional cuisine.

Conclusion

Maruata is a hidden gem in Mexico, offering visitors a unique and authentic travel experience. From its stunning beaches and natural attractions to its rich culture and delicious cuisine, Maruata has something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to Maruata and discover all the best things to see and do in this beautiful destination.
