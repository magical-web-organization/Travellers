Jiquilpan is a charming town located on the Pacific Coast of Michoacan, Mexico. It is a place of historical and cultural significance, as it was the birthplace of the famous Mexican revolutionary and national hero, Lázaro Cárdenas. With its beautiful colonial architecture, mouth-watering food, and friendly locals, Jiquilpan offers an authentic taste of Mexico that every traveler should experience. In this travel guide, we will take you on an exciting journey to explore the best of Jiquilpan, covering everything from the town’s top attractions to the local culture and customs.

Introduction to Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan is a small town located in the northern part of Michoacan, Mexico. It is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, including the Cerro de la Cruz mountains and Lake Chapala. The town’s architecture is heavily influenced by the colonial period, with many of its buildings dating back to the 17th and 18th centuries. Jiquilpan has a rich history and culture and is known for its traditional arts and crafts, delicious cuisine, and vibrant festivals. The town is also home to many legends and myths that have been passed down from generation to generation.

List of Places to Visit in Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan is an ideal tourist destination, with many attractions to explore. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Jiquilpan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lázaro Cárdenas Birthplace Museum - This museum is dedicated to the life and works of Lázaro Cárdenas, a national hero and former President of Mexico. The museum features exhibits that showcase his life, contributions to the country, and the revolution.

  2. Parish of San Francisco de Asís - This church is one of the most historic and significant buildings in Jiquilpan. Built in the 16th century, it features impressive colonial architecture and beautiful frescoes.

  3. Casa de las Artesanías - This museum showcases traditional arts and crafts of Michoacan. It has a collection of pottery, textiles, and wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Jiquilpan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Chapala - Located around 15 km from Jiquilpan, this lake is one of the largest in Mexico. It is an ideal spot for fishing, boating, and swimming.

  2. Cerro de la Cruz - This mountain offers breathtaking views of the town and the surrounding landscape. You can hike to the top and enjoy a panoramic view of Jiquilpan.

  3. Sierra de Coalcomán Natural Reserve - This reserve is located to the west of Jiquilpan and offers a great opportunity to explore the local flora and fauna. The reserve has several hiking trails that pass through the natural forests and grasslands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan offers excellent opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Cerro de la Cruz Hiking Trail - This hiking trail starts at the base of Cerro de la Cruz and takes you to the summit. The trail offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding mountains.

  2. La Quinta Mountain Biking Trail - This trail takes you through the beautiful countryside and offers a chance to explore the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Jiquilpan: Activities and Tours

Jiquilpan has a lot to offer to its visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Jiquilpan:

  1. Festival de la Candelaria - This festival is held in the first week of February and is dedicated to the town’s patron saint, the Virgin of Candelaria. The festival features traditional dances, music, and food.

  2. Museo Regional Michoacano - This museum showcases the history and culture of Michoacan. It has a vast collection of artifacts, including pottery, textiles, and art.

  3. La Limonera - This restaurant is known for its delicious Mexican food, including traditional dishes like chiles en nogada and mole.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jiquilpan

If you are planning a week-long trip to Jiquilpan, here is a sample itinerary that you can follow:

Day 1-2: Explore the town’s historic attractions, including the Lázaro Cárdenas Birthplace Museum and Parish of San Francisco de Asís.

Day 3: Visit Lake Chapala and spend the day fishing, boating, or swimming.

Day 4-5: Explore the local natural reserves, including the Sierra de Coalcomán Natural Reserve and Cerro de la Cruz mountain.

Day 6: Take a tour of Museo Regional Michoacano and Casa de las Artesanías.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at La Limonera, one of the town’s best restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan is known for its delicious cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the local culture and traditions. Here are some of the best dishes and beverages to try in Jiquilpan:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chiles en Nogada - This traditional dish is made of poblano chili peppers stuffed with meat, fruits, and spices and served with a creamy walnut sauce.

  2. Tamales - These are steamed corn husks filled with meat, vegetables, or cheese and wrapped in banana leaves.

  3. Pozole - This is a traditional Mexican soup made of hominy and meat, usually pork or chicken.

Local Beverages

  1. Tequila - This is a popular alcoholic drink made from the blue agave plant and is known for its strong taste and aroma.

  2. Mezcal - This is another popular alcoholic drink made from the agave plant and is known for its smoky and woody flavor.

Where to Stay in Jiquilpan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jiquilpan has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jiquilpan:

  1. Hotel Posada del Parque - This hotel is located in the heart of Jiquilpan and offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and great views of the town.

  2. Hospedería del Real - This guesthouse is ideal for budget travelers and offers clean and comfortable rooms.

  3. Villa Montaña - This hotel is located on the outskirts of Jiquilpan and offers a secluded and peaceful environment. The rooms are spacious and well-appointed, and the hotel has a great pool and spa facilities.

What to Buy in Jiquilpan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jiquilpan is known for its traditional arts and crafts, which make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Jiquilpan:

  1. Pottery - Jiquilpan is famous for its traditional pottery, which is made using local clay and features intricate designs and patterns.

  2. Textiles - The town is also known for its colorful textiles, including woven shawls, ponchos, and blankets.

  3. Wood Carvings - The local craftsmen also make beautiful wood carvings, including figurines, bowls, and trays.

Local Culture and Customs of Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their traditions and customs. Some of the most important customs and traditions of Jiquilpan include:

  1. The Day of the Dead - This is a popular Mexican holiday that is celebrated in Jiquilpan with parades, processions, and offerings of food and flowers for the dead.

  2. The Dance of the Viejitos - This is a traditional dance performed by locals wearing masks and costumes that represent old men.

Best Time to Visit Jiquilpan

The best time to visit Jiquilpan is during the winter months, from December to February. The weather is pleasantly cool and comfortable during this time, and you can also witness the vibrant Festival de la Candelaria in February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jiquilpan

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Jiquilpan:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  3. Carry cash as some places may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the local customs or traditions.

  2. Drink tap water - Always drink bottled water to avoid getting sick.

  3. Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public places, especially religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan is generally a safe place for travelers; however, it is always important to take necessary precautions. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  1. Avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

  2. Keep your valuables in a safe place.

  3. Take a licensed taxi instead of walking at night.

Health in Jiquilpan

It is essential to take necessary precautions to avoid getting sick while traveling to Jiquilpan. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  1. Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

  2. Avoid consuming food from street vendors.

  3. Always carry a first aid kit with essential medications.

Getting to Jiquilpan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jiquilpan

The nearest airport to Jiquilpan is Guadalajara International Airport, located around 150 km from the town. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to reach Jiquilpan.

Bus Stations Near Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan has a bus station that connects it to various parts of Michoacan and other cities in Mexico. Some of the major bus operators that serve Jiquilpan include ETN, Primera Plus, and Flecha Amarilla.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a private transfer to reach Jiquilpan.

How to Get Around in Jiquilpan

Jiquilpan is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus.

Where to Go Next from Jiquilpan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jiquilpan is located in the heart of Michoacan, making it an ideal base to explore the surrounding areas. Some of the most popular destinations near Jiquilpan include:

  1. Morelia - This historic city is located around 100 km from Jiquilpan and is known for its impressive colonial architecture, museums, and cathedrals.

  2. Pátzcuaro - This charming town is located around 80 km from Jiquilpan and is known for its vibrant festivals, handicrafts, and local cuisine.
