Introduction to Patzcuaro

Patzcuaro is a small town located in the heart of Michoacan, Mexico. This beautiful town is best known for its cultural and historical significance, as well as its picturesque location on the shore of Lake Patzcuaro. Numerous festivals and events occur yearly, making it a culturally significant destination. Here, we offer a comprehensive guide of what to see, eat, and do during your visit to Patzcuaro.

List of Places to Visit in Patzcuaro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Basilica of Our Lady of Health: This popular church symbolizes the strong colonial influence that took hold during the 16th century. Its architectural design and intricate interior works are a must-see for any visitor.

  • Casa de los 11 Patios: This is a stunning mansion that belonged to the former mayor of Patzcuaro in the early 18th century. It now serves as a museum, boasting an impressive collection of jewelry, textiles, ceramics, and weapons from throughout the ages.

Natural Attractions of Patzcuaro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isla Janitzio: This enchanting island is situated in the middle of Lake Patzcuaro, reachable by boat, and offers a unique blend of nature and culture for adventure seekers.

  • Cerro de la Estrella: This beautiful hill offers an incredible panoramic view of the town, surrounded by ancient ruins of a Pre-Columbian civilization.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Patzcuaro

  • Los Finos trail: This accessible trail located in the outskirts of Patzcuaro offers a somewhat easy trail through a lush pine forest with great spots for picnicking and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Patzcuaro: Activities and Tours

  • Day of the Dead: Patzcuaro is one of the best locations in all of Mexico to celebrate Day of the Dead. This is the time when locals uphold a tradition of honoring their ancestors and departed loved ones.

  • Artisan handicraft shops: Patzcuaro is famous for its traditional handicrafts such as copperware, pottery, and textiles. Exploring these local shops is an ideal way to learn about the town’s culture and find unique souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Patzcuaro

Here is a recommended week-long itinerary to explore the beauty and local wonders of Patzcuaro:

Day 1: Arrive in town and explore the Historical Centre.

Day 2: Visit Isla Janitzio and enjoy the sun setting from the boat ride back to Patzcuaro.

Day 3: Take a cultural day visiting the museums, and Casa de las 11 Patios.

Day 4: Discover the natural beauty of Cerro de la Estrella, hike and picnic in the vicinity.

Day 5: Explore the Lake Patzcuaro and its surrounding villages.

Day 6: Immerse in traditional dishes of Patzcuaro - such as corundas or uchepos - or take a cooking class and learn about the local cuisine.

Day 7: Take one final adventure, bike along Los Finos trail

Food and Drink Scene of Patzcuaro

Patzcuaro is known for its delicious and traditional Michoacan cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pescado Blanco: Early morning fish in the local market cooked in a buttery garlic sauce is a must-try.

  • Tacos al Pastor: Tender well-carved pork tacos with fresh pineapple and herbs for a perfect bite.

Local Beverages

  • Rompopes: This Mexican version of eggnog is made with a mixture of cream, vanilla, and cinnamon, often served during the festive season.

  • Cerveza Michoacana: The traditional local brewery produces unique flavors of beer, like the dark Boquita de Tuna.

Where to Stay in Patzcuaro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Patzcuaro caters to various kinds of travel preferences. Here are some options for hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Hotel Misión Pátzcuaro: Located in the downtown of Patzcuaro, this hotel offers comfortable rooms with views of the surrounding area and lake.

  • Casa de la Real Aduana: A historic property converted into a boutique hotel with antique furnishings, located in the Patzcuaro’s historic center.

  • Posada de la Salud: With its peaceful setting and a traditional decor, this guesthouse located next to the Basilica of Our Lady of Health, offers a relaxing and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Patzcuaro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Patzcuaro is widely known for its crafts, such as copperware, pottery, and textiles. Don’t leave Patzcuaro without taking home some of the town’s best souvenirs, such as:

  • Copperware: You can find a broad range of copper kitchenware such as pans, pots, and cups, or decorations such as lamps or candle holders.

  • Pottery: Patzcuaro is known for its artisan vessels and pots made with local clay. These can be ideal for cooking or as decorative pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Patzcuaro

Patzcuaro carries a colonial heritage with a pre-Columbian influence, blending both in its festivities and traditions. It preserves patron saint festivities with prehispanic (so called “purhépecha”) rituals, the “Fiesta de las Ánimas” and the “Fiesta de Calaca” for Day of the Dead, among others.

Best Time to Visit Patzcuaro

The best time to visit Patzcuaro is between October and May. This period offers a pleasant climate, fewer crowds, and ample access to festivals and cultural programs.

Do’s and Don’ts of Patzcuaro

Do:

  • Observe cultural customs and traditions.

  • Try as much local food as you can.

  • Bring local crafts home as souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture, beliefs, and traditions.

  • Engage in illegal activities, such as using drugs.

  • Be overly revealing with your clothing, especially when visiting a place of worship.

Safety and Health

Safety in Patzcuaro

Patzcuaro is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it is best to be aware of your surroundings and take general precautions such as not carrying too much cash and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Patzcuaro

The tap water in Patzcuaro may cause discomfort for people not used to it. It is advisable to drink bottled water or boiled water instead.

Getting to Patzcuaro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Patzcuaro can be accessed by several transportation options:

Airports Near Patzcuaro

The closest airport is General Francisco Mujica International Airport (MLM), located around 45 minutes from Patzcuaro.

Bus Stations Near Patzcuaro

Patzcuaro has a bus station located in the downtown area, with frequent services to Mexico City and other places within Michoacan.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber are also available in Patzcuaro.

How to Get Around in Patzcuaro

Walking is convenient for most of Patzcuaro’s historic center, but if you’d rather rent a car, there are several companies at the airport and bus station.

Where to Go Next from Patzcuaro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Morelia: Visit the colonial capital of Michoacan and discover its rich architecture and cultural heritage.

  • Uruapan: Enjoy the full beauty of the famed Paricutin Volcano and indulge in local cuisine and traditions.

Conclusion

With a rich historical and cultural backdrop, picturesque landscapes, and unique cuisine and crafts, Patzcuaro serves its visitors with a diverse and unforgettable experience. We hope this travel guide will help you plan your itinerary and make the most of your visit.
