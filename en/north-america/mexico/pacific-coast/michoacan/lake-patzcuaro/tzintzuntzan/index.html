Introduction to Tzintzuntzán

Welcome to Tzintzuntzán, a charming town located in the beautiful Lake Patzcuaro region of Michoacan, Mexico. Tzintzuntzán is overflowing with rich culture, history, and outdoor adventures. From natural attractions to historical sites and delicious cuisine, Tzintzuntzán has something to offer every traveler.

This travel guide will provide you with the essential information you need for a memorable trip to Tzintzuntzán. You’ll discover the best places to visit, activities, hiking/biking routes, where to stay, and local customs. Get ready to immerse yourself in the magic of Tzintzuntzán!

List of Places to Visit in Tzintzuntzán

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tzintzuntzan Archeological Site

The Tzintzuntzan Archeological Site was once home to Purépecha royalty. It is now an impressive archeological site that should not be missed. The site includes several pyramids, ball courts, temples, and plazas.

Casa de los Once Patios

Casa de los Once Patios is a 16th-century building that has been restored to its former glory. It now serves as a cultural center, showcasing local arts and crafts and traditional dances.

Basilica of Our Lady of Health

The Basilica of Our Lady of Health is a striking 17th-century church with a unique fusion of Purépecha, Spanish, and Moorish architecture. It is home to one of the most significant celebrations in the region, the Day of the Dead.

Natural Attractions of Tzintzuntzán: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Patzcuaro

Lake Patzcuaro is the highlight of Tzintzuntzán. It is the only lake in Mexico where fishermen still use traditional techniques. Visitors can rent paddleboards, kayaks, or take a boat tour around the lake and its islands. You can also visit the Janitzio Island, famous for its Day of the Dead celebrations.

Francisco J. Múgica National Park

Francisco J. Múgica National Park is a vast protected area between mountain ranges and forests. Visitors can explore the park on horseback, hike its trails, and admire its waterfalls and hot springs.

Barranca del Cupatitzio National Park

Barranca del Cupatitzio National Park is a gorgeous park located in the city of Uruapan, just a short drive from Tzintzuntzán. Visitors can walk through the park’s botanic gardens and admire its many fountains and streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tzintzuntzán

Tzintzuntzán and the surrounding areas offer an abundance of hiking and biking routes. You can experience the stunning views of the region’s nature on your own or with a local guide. Some of the most popular trails in the region include:

  • Lago de Camécuaro National Park Trail
  • Cerro La Cruz Trail
  • Patamban to Los Reyes Trail

Best Things to Do in Tzintzuntzán: Activities and Tours

Artisanal Market

The Artisanal Market is a popular spot among visitors and locals alike. Visitors can buy traditional handicrafts such as pottery, textiles, and sculptures, and watch local artisans at work.

Guided Tours

Several tour operators offer tours of Tzintzuntzán and the surrounding areas. They come in all shapes and sizes, from cultural tours to outdoor adventures.

Horseback Riding at Lake Patzcuaro

Horseback riding around the lake is an unforgettable experience. Visitors can take guided tours or venture out on their own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tzintzuntzán

Day 1: Arrival in Tzintzuntzán, visit Casa de los Once Patios, and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Visit the Tzintzuntzan Archeological Site and hike Cerro La Cruz Trail.

Day 3: Visit the Basilica of Our Lady of Health, take a boat tour around Lake Patzcuaro, and visit Janitzio Island.

Day 4: Visit Francisco J. Múgica National Park and hike its trails.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of Tzintzuntzán and its surrounding areas.

Day 6: Visit Barranca del Cupatitzio National Park and explore its botanical garden.

Day 7: Enjoy horseback riding around Lake Patzcuaro and an afternoon at the Artisanal Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Tzintzuntzán

Must-Try Dishes

Carnitas

Carnitas are slow-cooked pork that has been seasoned with spices. It’s the most famous dish in Michoacan and a must-try when visiting Tzintzuntzán.

Corundas

Corundas are similar to tamales, but they have a distinct triangular shape and are steamed in corn husks. They typically have a filling of cheese, chicken, or pork.

Local Beverages

Charanda

Charanda is a local distilled sugarcane spirit that is similar to rum. It has a unique flavor and is often served with lime and salt.

Atole

Atole is a warm drink made with masa (corn dough), water or milk, and sweeteners such as cinnamon, vanilla, or chocolate.

Where to Stay in Tzintzuntzán: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Mansur

Hotel Mansur is a family-owned hotel that offers simple and affordable rooms in the heart of Tzintzuntzán.

Casa de la Real Aduana Boutique Hotel

Casa de la Real Aduana Boutique Hotel is located in a renovated 17th-century building. It offers comfortable rooms and a tranquil atmosphere.

Hotel Los Escudos

Hotel Los Escudos is a small and charming hotel that offers views of Lake Patzcuaro. It has beautiful gardens and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Tzintzuntzán: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pottery

Tzintzuntzán is known for its pottery, especially for the traditional canaletas. These multi-colored jugs make for great souvenirs and can be used as decorative pieces.

Textiles

Local artisans create beautiful textiles such as embroidered shirts, woven handbags, and shawls. These items make excellent souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tzintzuntzán

Tzintzuntzán is rich in pre-Hispanic culture, and its people are proud to keep it alive. The Purépecha are the indigenous people of the region and have preserved their traditions and customs over the centuries.

Best Time to Visit Tzintzuntzán

The best time to visit Tzintzuntzán is from November to February, when the weather is mild and dry. The town is famous for its colorful Day of the Dead celebrations in November, which is a must-see for travelers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tzintzuntzán

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Litter in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tzintzuntzán

Tzintzuntzán is a safe town, but travelers should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Tzintzuntzán

Drink only bottled or filtered water and avoid drinking tap water. Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when outdoors, particularly during the summer months.

Getting to Tzintzuntzán: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tzintzuntzán

The nearest airport to Tzintzuntzán is General Francisco J. Mujica International Airport in Morelia, about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Tzintzuntzán

The main bus station in Tzintzuntzán is located in the town center. Several bus companies serve the area, including ETN and Primera Plus.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available in Tzintzuntzán. However, it’s recommended to use a reputable taxi service or rent from a reliable car rental company.

How to Get Around in Tzintzuntzán

Tzintzuntzán is best explored on foot. However, taxis are available for longer journeys, and rental cars can be rented for more extended explorations.

Where to Go Next from Tzintzuntzán: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Morelia

Morelia, the capital of Michoacan, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Mexico’s most beautiful colonial cities. It’s known for its stunning architecture, delicious cuisine, and lively festivals.

Patzcuaro

Patzcuaro is a charming town located near Tzintzuntzán. It is known for its preserved colonial architecture, traditional crafts, and annual Day of the Dead celebration.

Uruapan

Uruapan is a beautiful city located east of Tzintzuntzán. It is famous for its waterfalls, colonial architecture, and national parks.

Conclusion

Tzintzuntzán is a hidden gem in Michoacan, Mexico. With its rich culture, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious cuisine, it’s an ideal destination for any traveler seeking an authentic Mexican experience. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and discover all that Tzintzuntzán has to offer.
