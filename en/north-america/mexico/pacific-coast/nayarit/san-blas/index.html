Introduction to San Blas

San Blas is a beautiful coastal town located in the Mexican state of Nayarit. This hidden gem is a paradise for those who love nature, history, and culture. The town is surrounded by lush green mountains and pristine beaches, making it a popular destination for tourists. San Blas is also a culturally rich town with a rich history and traditions that are still alive today.

List of Places to Visit in San Blas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Blas has a fascinating history, and its museums and historical places are a testimony to it. Here are a few places that you should not miss:

  • The Church of Our Lady of the Rosary:

This church is an excellent example of colonial architecture and one of the best-preserved buildings in San Blas. It is located in the town square and has beautiful stained glass windows and a stunning altar.

  • The Old Customs House:

This building was built in 1769 and is now a museum that houses various artifacts and exhibits related to San Blas’s history. It has a beautiful courtyard and a stunning view of the ocean. The museum is open from Tuesday to Sunday, from 10 am to 5 pm.

  • The La Marinera Museum:

This museum is dedicated to the traditional dance of San Blas – La Marinera. It has a collection of costumes, pictures, and videos that showcase the tradition’s history and evolution. The museum is open from Tuesday to Sunday, from 10 am to 5 pm.

Natural Attractions of San Blas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

San Blas has a lot to offer in terms of outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are a few places that you should not miss:

  • La Tovara National Park:

This national park is a biodiverse paradise located just a few kilometers from San Blas. It has a long boat ride that takes you through mangroves and past multiple bird species, crocodiles, and alligators. You can also take a short hike to a waterfall.

  • Playa Las Islitas:

This beach is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. It has clear blue water, white sands, and a variety of beachfront restaurants and bars. You can also rent kayaks and paddleboards or take a boat tour to nearby islands.

  • La Contaduria:

This is an excellent lookout point located on top of a hill. It offers a panoramic view of San Blas and its surroundings, and the sunset from this point is breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Blas

San Blas has several scenic routes that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few that you should not miss:

  • The El Farallon Hike:

This hike takes you to the top of a hill that offers a panoramic view of San Blas and its surroundings. The trail is well-marked and takes approximately an hour to complete.

  • The San Blas Biking Trail:

This biking trail takes you through the town’s scenic countryside, past rice fields, and local farms. The trail is approximately 10 km long and is suitable for all levels of bikers.

Best Things to Do in San Blas: Activities and Tours

San Blas is a town full of vibrant activities and tours that are perfect for all ages. Here are a few that you should not miss:

  • Bird Watching Tours:

San Blas is known for its diverse and abundant birdlife. You can take a bird watching tour with a local guide who will take you to the best spots to view the different species of birds.

  • Sunset Boat Tours:

San Blas has some of the most beautiful sunsets in the world, and you can witness them from the ocean. You can book a sunset boat tour that includes drinks and snacks as you sail through the calm waters.

  • Turtle Release Tours:

San Blas is home to a sea turtle sanctuary, and you can be a part of the conservation efforts by releasing baby turtles into the ocean. This tour is usually only available during the turtle nesting season, which is from July to December.

Food and Drink Scene of San Blas

Must-Try Dishes

San Blas has a unique blend of traditional and modern cuisine. Here are a few dishes that you should not miss:

  • Pescado Zarandeado:

This is a traditional dish that is prepared by grilling fish on a wire mesh. It is a must-try dish, and you can find it in almost every restaurant in San Blas.

  • Huachinango a la Talla:

This is another traditional dish that is made with red snapper. The fish is marinated in spices and then grilled over charcoal. The dish is spicy, and the fish is cooked to perfection.

  • Tostadas:

San Blas’s tostadas are unique and delicious. They are made with a crispy tortilla and topped with ceviche or seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Michelada:

This is a popular Mexican drink that is made with beer and lime juice. It is often served in a salt-rimmed glass and garnished with spices like chili powder or hot sauce.

  • Tequila:

Tequila is a well-known Mexican drink, and San Blas is no exception. You can try different kinds of tequila in San Blas, including silver, reposado, and añejo.

Where to Stay in San Blas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Blas has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are a few options:

  • Hotel Garza Canela:

This hotel is a stunning boutique hotel that offers ocean views, beautiful gardens, and a pool. It is located in the town center and has some of the most attentive staff.

  • Casa Roxanna:

This guest house is located in a quiet neighborhood and offers cozy and comfortable rooms. It has an outdoor pool and garden and is perfect for those who want to relax and unwind.

  • Hostal Albatros:

This hostel is perfect for budget travelers who want to experience San Blas’s beauty at an affordable price. It is clean, comfortable, and centrally located.

What to Buy in San Blas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Blas is a great place to shop for souvenirs and local products. Here are a few shops that you should visit:

  • El Pollito De Oro:

This shop sells handmade crafts and souvenirs made by local artisans. It has a diverse collection of items, including paintings, pottery, and jewelry.

  • Taller De Corte Y Costura La Familia:

This shop specializes in traditional clothing, including huipiles and rebozos. The clothes are handmade and unique, and you can even watch the artisans work.

Local Culture and Customs of San Blas

San Blas has a rich cultural heritage that is still alive today. Here are a few cultural practices and customs that you should be aware of:

  • La Marinera:

La Marinera is a traditional dance that originated in San Blas. It is a dance between a man and a woman and is often performed during festivals and celebrations.

  • Dia de los Muertos:

San Blas celebrates Dia de los Muertos, a traditional Mexican holiday that honors the deceased. The town is decorated with colorful altars, and there are parades and festivities throughout the day.

Best Time to Visit San Blas

The best time to visit San Blas is from November to April when the weather is pleasant and dry, and the crowds are thin. The summer months can be hot and humid and may not be ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Blas

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Carry cash as not all places accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Ignore the weather conditions.
  • Take pictures of people without asking for their permission first.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Blas

San Blas is a relatively safe town, but you should always take precautions. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your belongings close and secure.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs.

Health in San Blas

San Blas has a reliable healthcare system, and there are several clinics and hospitals in the area. It is advisable to have health insurance when traveling to San Blas and to take precautions against common illnesses like sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to San Blas: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Blas is well-connected, and there are several ways to get there. Here are a few options:

Airports Near San Blas

The nearest airports to San Blas are:

  • Licenciado Gustavo Diaz Ordaz International Airport (PVR)
  • Tepic International Airport (TPQ)

Bus Stations Near San Blas

There are several bus stations near San Blas, including:

  • Tepic Bus Station
  • Puerto Vallarta Bus Station

Other Options

You can also book a private transfer or rent a car to get to San Blas.

How to Get Around in San Blas

San Blas is a small town, and most places are within walking distance. However, you can also rent a bike or a scooter to explore the town. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from San Blas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Blas is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are a few options:

  • Sayulita: This bohemian town is known for its surf culture and beautiful beaches.

  • Puerto Vallarta: This popular tourist destination has everything from beaches and nightlife to culture and history.

  • Tepic: This capital city of Nayarit is a hidden gem with a rich cultural heritage and stunning colonial architecture.

Conclusion

San Blas is a hidden gem that has something to offer for everyone. From its natural beauty and outdoor adventures to its rich culture and history, San Blas is a town that will leave you mesmerized. So pack your bags, grab your travel guide, and head to San Blas for an unforgettable experience.
