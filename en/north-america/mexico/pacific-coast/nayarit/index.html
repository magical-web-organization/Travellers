Introduction to Nayarit

Located on the Pacific Coast in Mexico, Nayarit is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This stunning state is home to unique culture, beautiful beaches, captivating history, and natural wonders. It’s a perfect destination for travelers looking to escape crowded tourist spots and experience authentic Mexican hospitality.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Nayarit. We’ll cover everything from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, from local cuisine to accommodation options. So, let’s dive in and begin our journey to Nayarit!

List of Places to Visit in Nayarit

From museums to beaches and natural wonders, there’s no shortage of places to visit in Nayarit. Here are some of the most notable ones to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Museo de Arte Contemporaneo de Nayarit

Museo de Arte Contemporaneo de Nayarit is located in the heart of Tepic, the capital of Nayarit. It’s a modern museum showcasing contemporary art and installations from all over Mexico. Visitors can spend hours exploring the galleries and admiring the incredible artwork.

2. Ruins of Los Toriles

The ancient ruins of Los Toriles are a must-visit for history lovers. These well-preserved ruins date back to the pre-Columbian era and offer a glimpse into the past. You can explore the site and see the remains of ancient temples, ball courts, and housing structures.

3. La Tovara National Park

La Tovara National Park is an incredibly biodiverse ecosystem located in San Blas. It’s home to a wide range of wildlife, including crocodiles, turtles, and hundreds of bird species. You can take a boat tour through the mangroves and experience this unique natural wonder up close.

Natural Attractions of Nayarit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Islas Marietas

Islas Marietas is a group of small islands located off the coast of Nayarit. These islands offer secluded beaches, crystal-clear waters, and incredible marine life. You can take a boat tour or go snorkeling to explore the vibrant coral reefs and encounter exotic sea creatures.

2. Sayulita

Sayulita is a charming surf town that’s famous for its vibrant beach culture. It has a relaxed vibe and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy, such as surfing, paddleboarding, and yoga. You can take a walk around the colorful streets and immerse yourself in the laid-back lifestyle.

3. Bahia de Banderas

Bahia de Banderas is a beautiful bay on the coast of Nayarit. It’s surrounded by lush vegetation, rolling hills, and spectacular views. You can take a boat ride and explore the pristine beaches, hidden coves, and towering rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nayarit

Nayarit is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking and biking are some of the best ways to experience its natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Nayarit:

1. Sierra de Vallejo

Sierra de Vallejo is a mountain range that offers some of the most scenic hiking trails in Nayarit. You can explore the lush forests, crystal-clear rivers, and stunning views from the mountain peaks. It’s a perfect place to escape the heat and enjoy a refreshing hike.

2. Las Varas

Las Varas is a small town located in the heart of Nayarit. It’s surrounded by picturesque valleys, rolling hills, and charming villages. You can rent a bike and explore the countryside, stop at local farms, and taste fresh produce.

3. San Blas

San Blas is a beautiful coastal town located on the Pacific coast of Nayarit. It has a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscapes. You can explore the jungle, the mangroves and admire the sunset from the top of a hill.

Best Things to Do in Nayarit: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a culture lover, Nayarit has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Nayarit:

1. Surfing and Paddleboarding

Sayulita is one of the best places to experience the thrill of surfing and paddleboarding. The beaches are perfect for beginners and advanced surfers alike. You can take a lesson from an experienced instructor or rent a board and hit the waves.

2. Birdwatching

Nayarit is home to more than 500 species of birds, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. You can explore La Tovara National Park, San Blas Estuary, and other natural reserves to see exotic species like the Roseate spoonbill, Great blue heron, and mangrove vireo.

3. Tequila Tasting

Nayarit is known for producing some of the best tequila in Mexico. You can visit local distilleries and learn about the production process of this spirit. You can also participate in a tequila tasting and try different flavors and types of tequila.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nayarit

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Nayarit, here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the best places to visit:

Day 1: Tepic - Visit Museo de Arte Contemporaneo de Nayarit

Day 2: Los Toriles - Explore ancient ruins and nearby archaeological sites

Day 3: La Tovara National Park - Take a boat tour through the mangroves

Day 4: Sayulita - Surf or paddleboard in the beach town

Day 5: San Blas - Hike or bike through the jungle trails

Day 6: Islas Marietas - Take a boat tour and go snorkeling

Day 7: Relax on one of the many beautiful beaches around Nayarit

Food and Drink Scene of Nayarit

Exploring the local cuisine is an essential part of any travel experience. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Nayarit:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Tamales Norteños

Tamales Norteños is a traditional northern Mexican dish, and Nayarit has its own unique version of it. The tamale is made with corn dough and stuffed with flavorful meat, vegetables, and spices. It’s usually served with salsa on the side.

2. Sopes

Sopes are another local delicacy made with corn dough. The dough is shaped into a cup and filled with refried beans, meat, cheese, and guacamole. It’s then topped with lettuce, tomato, and salsa.

3. Huachinango a la Tallabardon

Huachinango a la Tallabardon is a classic seafood dish from Nayarit. It’s made with red snapper and flavored with garlic, spices, and lime juice. It’s usually served with rice, vegetables, and tortillas.

Local Beverages

1. Tequila

Tequila is the most famous alcoholic beverage in Nayarit. It’s made from the blue agave plant and has a distinct flavor and aroma. You can enjoy tequila neat, mixed in cocktails, or as a chaser with lime and salt.

2. Raicilla

Raicilla is a traditional liquor from Nayarit made from the agave plant. It has a smoky flavor and is usually enjoyed straight or mixed in cocktails.

Where to Stay in Nayarit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nayarit offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Nayarit:

Luxury Hotels

1. Four Seasons Resort Punta Mita

Four Seasons Resort Punta Mita offers a luxurious, beachfront experience with top-notch amenities and service. The rooms are spacious and well-equipped, and the resort has multiple dining options, a spa, and a private beach club.

2. Marival Distinct Luxury Resort

Marival Distinct Luxury Resort is a modern, all-inclusive resort located on the coast of Nayarit. The rooms are stylish and comfortable, and the resort has multiple swimming pools, a private beach, and a variety of restaurants and bars.

Budget Options

1. Hostel Sayulita

Hostel Sayulita is a budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers. It’s located in the heart of Sayulita, close to the beach and other attractions. The hostel has dormitory-style rooms and shared bathrooms, and offers free Wi-Fi and breakfast.

2. Hotel Las Palmas

Hotel Las Palmas is a no-frills hotel located in Tepic, the capital of Nayarit. The rooms are basic but clean and well-maintained, and the hotel offers free parking and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Nayarit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nayarit is known for its fantastic handicrafts, art pieces, and traditional souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Nayarit:

Handicrafts

1. Huichol Art

Huichol art is a traditional form of art from Nayarit made by indigenous Huichol people. The artwork includes beaded figurines, sculptures, and paintings with intricate designs and bright colors.

2. Jewelry

Nayarit is also known for its beautiful jewelry made with silver, gold, and precious stones. You can find unique pieces of jewelry in local markets and shops throughout the state.

Local Products

1. Tequila

Tequila is the most famous local product from Nayarit. You can find different brands and types of tequila in local stores and distilleries.

2. Raicilla

Raicilla is another traditional spirit from Nayarit. You can find it in local markets and specialty shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Nayarit

Nayarit has a rich cultural heritage influenced by its pre-Columbian roots and Spanish colonization. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it’s common to greet each other with a hug or kiss on the cheek.

The state has numerous festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including the San Blas International Migratory Bird Festival, the Nuestra Señora de la Candelaria Festival, and the Tepic Carnival.

Best Time to Visit Nayarit

The best time to visit Nayarit is between November and May, when the weather is mild and dry. The summer months can be hot and humid, and the rainy season (June-October) can bring heavy rains and hurricanes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nayarit

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before your trip
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore off-the-beaten-path destinations and small towns

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling it first
  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear outside of the beach
  • Be too loud or rowdy in public places
  • Disrespect religious sites or ceremonies

Safety and Health

Safety in Nayarit

Nayarit is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid flashing expensive items like jewelry and cameras
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean and follow lifeguard instructions

Health in Nayarit

It’s important to be aware of any potential health risks before traveling to Nayarit. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink bottled or purified water and avoid street food that may have been washed with tap water
  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and Zika virus
  • Check with your doctor before traveling and make sure all your necessary vaccinations are up-to-date.

Getting to Nayarit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nayarit is easily accessible by air and bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Nayarit

  • Licenciado Gustavo Diaz Ordaz International Airport in Puerto Vallarta is the closest airport to Nayarit
  • Tepic International Airport is also located in Nayarit but has limited international flights

Bus Stations Near Nayarit

  • Central de Autobuses de Tepic is located in the capital city and offers direct connections to major cities in Mexico.
  • Autobuses Pacifico is the largest bus company in Nayarit and offers services across the state and beyond.

How to Get Around in Nayarit

The best way to get around Nayarit is by car, but there are also other transportation options:

Car Rental

Renting a car is the most convenient way to explore Nayarit at your own pace. You can rent a car at the airport or in major cities and towns.

Taxis

Taxis are easy to find in Nayarit, and most drivers speak some English. Make sure to negotiate the fare before getting in the taxi.

Buses

Buses are an affordable option for getting around Nayarit, and they run frequently between major cities and towns. Keep in mind that bus schedules may not be very reliable.

Where to Go Next from Nayarit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nayarit is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Puerto Vallarta

Puerto Vallarta is a popular beach destination located just south of Nayarit. It’s known for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and historical landmarks.

Guadalajara

Guadalajara is the second-largest city in Mexico and is located approximately 5 hours east of Nayarit. It’s known for its vibrant culture, beautiful architecture, and incredible food scene.

San Sebastian del Oeste

San Sebastian del Oeste is a small town located in the mountains just north of Nayarit. It has a rich history and is known for its colonial architecture and stunning scenery.

Conclusion

We hope this travel guide has inspired you to visit Nayarit, Mexico. From the stunning beaches to the unique culture and history, Nayarit has something for everyone. Plan your trip, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure!
