Introduction to San Patricio-Melaque

Welcome to San Patricio-Melaque, a charming coastal town in the State of Jalisco, on the Pacific Coast of Mexico. Here, you’ll find sweeping golden beaches, friendly locals, and a laid-back atmosphere that’s perfect for a relaxing vacation. In this travel guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in San Patricio-Melaque. Plan your trip with our expert tips and recommendations.

List of Places to Visit in San Patricio-Melaque

San Patricio-Melaque offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions, including museums, historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Here’s a list of must-see places to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Museo Jose Clemente Orozco: This museum celebrates the life and work of Jose Clemente Orozco, one of Mexico’s most renowned muralists.
  • Parroquia San Patricio: A beautiful church with a distinct Irish influence.
  • La Huerta Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the region, from pre-Columbian times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of San Patricio-Melaque: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Barra de Navidad Beach: This long, wide beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing.
  • Isla Navidad Golf Course: Designed by Robert von Hagge, this 27-hole golf course offers breathtaking ocean views.
  • El Tamarindo Beach: Known for its secluded coves and turquoise waters, El Tamarindo Beach is a perfect spot for snorkeling and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Patricio-Melaque

For outdoor enthusiasts, San Patricio-Melaque offers several hiking and biking trails that wind through the lush jungle and along the coast. Here are some of our favorites.

  • Pueblo Nuevo Riverbed Hike: This easy hike takes you along the riverbed, through forests and farmland, and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • El Pedregal and Zaragoza Trails: These two trails are ideal for mountain biking, offering a challenging ride through hilly terrain and forested areas.
  • Parroquia-San Antonio Walk: Take a leisurely stroll through the charming town of San Antonio, taking in the local architecture and culture.

Best Things to Do in San Patricio-Melaque: Activities and Tours

In addition to hikes, bike rides, and beach activities, San Patricio-Melaque offers a variety of activities and tours for all ages and interests. Here are some must-try experiences.

  • Whale Watching: From December to March, you can take a boat tour to see humpback whales in their natural habitat.
  • Surf Lessons: Learn to ride the waves with a professional surf instructor.
  • Tequila Tour: Discover the history and production of Mexico’s iconic drink on a Tequila tasting and distillery tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Patricio-Melaque

Planning a week-long trip to San Patricio-Melaque? Here’s a suggested itinerary.

Day Itinerary
1 Arrive in San Patricio-Melaque. Relax on the beach and explore the town.
2 Take a hike through the Pueblo Nuevo Riverbed. In the evening, attend a traditional cultural show at Parroquia San Patricio.
3 Enjoy a day of beach activities, such as kayaking or paddleboarding.
4 Take a day trip to nearby Barra de Navidad to visit the lighthouse and enjoy a seafood lunch.
5 Play a round of golf at the Isla Navidad Golf Course.
6 Take a Tequila tour and enjoy a sunset dinner on the beach.
7 Explore the town’s markets and shops for souvenirs before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of San Patricio-Melaque

Mexican cuisine is renowned worldwide for its bold flavors and fresh ingredients, and San Patricio-Melaque is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Tacos: Made with fresh, locally caught fish, these tacos are a staple of the region’s cuisine.
  • Pozole: A hearty soup made with hominy and pork, typically served with vegetables and a variety of condiments.
  • Sopes: A type of Mexican street food, made with a corn base, topped with beans, salsa, avocado, and various types of meat.

Local Beverages

  • Michelada: A refreshing drink made with beer, lime juice, and a variety of spices, typically served in a salt-rimmed glass.
  • Tequila: The iconic Mexican spirit is a must-try, whether you prefer it straight or in a cocktail.
  • Margarita: A classic cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and a touch of sweetness.

Where to Stay in San Patricio-Melaque: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Patricio-Melaque offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxurious beachfront resorts to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top recommendations.

  • Grand Isla Navidad Resort: A luxurious, all-inclusive resort with stunning ocean views, world-class dining, and exceptional service.
  • Hotel Club Nautico El Dorado: A mid-range hotel with comfortable rooms, a pool, and a prime location on the beach.
  • Hostal San Patricio: A budget-friendly guesthouse with basic amenities and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in San Patricio-Melaque: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring back home? Here are some unique local products to consider.

  • Handmade Pottery: San Patricio-Melaque is known for its ceramics, and you can find a wide variety of handcrafted pieces in local shops.
  • Huichol Art: These intricate beaded artworks are often sold by local vendors and make for a colorful and unique souvenir.
  • Tequila: Take home a bottle of the region’s most famous export, along with a set of hand-painted shot glasses.

Local Culture and Customs of San Patricio-Melaque

Mexican culture is rich and diverse, and San Patricio-Melaque offers a glimpse into the local way of life. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of during your visit.

  • Religion: The majority of the population is Catholic, and religious festivals and celebrations are an important part of the community.
  • Hospitality: Mexicans are known for their warmth and hospitality, and locals are typically welcoming and friendly towards visitors.
  • Language: Spanish is the official language, but English is commonly spoken in tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit San Patricio-Melaque

San Patricio-Melaque enjoys a warm, tropical climate year-round, with temperatures ranging from 80-90°F. The best time to visit is from December to April when the weather is sunny and dry, and the whale-watching season is in full swing. However, despite being the peak tourist season, San Patricio-Melaque remains relatively uncrowded, offering a peaceful and relaxing experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Patricio-Melaque

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to San Patricio-Melaque, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind.

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful or insensitive towards locals or their culture.
  • Engage in illicit drug use or other illegal activities.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in public areas.

Safety and Health

San Patricio-Melaque is generally a safe and welcoming destination for tourists. However, it’s always important to take common-sense precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in San Patricio-Melaque

  • Avoid walking alone at night or in dimly lit areas.
  • Keep your valuables in a secure location.
  • Be cautious when swimming in the ocean and heed local warnings about strong currents and riptides.

Health in San Patricio-Melaque

  • Drink bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Pack sunscreen and wear a wide-brimmed hat to protect yourself from the sun’s rays.

Getting to San Patricio-Melaque: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Patricio-Melaque is located on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, about 25 miles south of Manzanillo and 125 miles west of Guadalajara. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip.

Airports Near San Patricio-Melaque

  • Playa de Oro International Airport (ZLO) in Manzanillo: 25 miles from San Patricio-Melaque.
  • Licenciado Gustavo Diaz Ordaz International Airport (PVR) in Puerto Vallarta: 120 miles from San Patricio-Melaque.

Bus Stations Near San Patricio-Melaque

  • Terminal de Autobuses de Manzanillo: Served by several companies, including Primera Plus and ETN.
  • Central Camionera de Puerto Vallarta: Served by several companies, including Primera Plus and ETN.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: Arrange for a private car or shuttle to transport you from the airport directly to your hotel in San Patricio-Melaque.
  • Car Rental: Rent a car at the airport and drive to San Patricio-Melaque, enjoying the scenic coastal highways along the way.

How to Get Around in San Patricio-Melaque

San Patricio-Melaque is a small town that’s easily navigable on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, there are several transportation options available.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available and relatively affordable.
  • Bicycle Rental: Several shops in town offer bicycle rentals.
  • Car Rental: Rent a car to explore the surrounding areas on your own.

Where to Go Next from San Patricio-Melaque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Patricio-Melaque is an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations and attractions. Here are some nearby destinations to consider visiting.

  • Barra de Navidad: A nearby town known for its picturesque lighthouse and fresh seafood.
  • Melaque Estuary: A beautiful and peaceful natural reserve known for its birdwatching and wildlife.
  • Colima Volcano: A unique geological formation that’s recognizable for its snowcapped peak.

Conclusion

San Patricio-Melaque offers a unique mix of culture, nature, and relaxation that’s hard to find elsewhere. Whether you’re a solo traveler seeking adventure, a family looking for a beach vacation, or a couple in search of romance, San Patricio-Melaque has something for everyone. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip and discover the best that San Patricio-Melaque has to offer. Happy travels!
