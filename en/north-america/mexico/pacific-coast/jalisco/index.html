Introduction to Jalisco

Jalisco is a state located in the western region of Mexico and is well known for birthplace of mariachi music. The state is the seventh-largest in Mexico, and it features a diverse terrain of mountains, deserts, and beaches. Jalisco is famous for its tequila, traditional cuisine, and festivities, which include the Day of the Dead, Las Fiestas de Octubre, and Guelaguetza.

List of Places to Visit in Jalisco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jalisco is home to cultural attractions, including museums and historical places that showcase the state’s vibrant heritage. Some of the most visited cultural attractions include:

  • Guadalajara Cathedral: The Cathedral is a historic landmark in the city of Guadalajara and is known for its neo-gothic architecture.

  • Teatro Degollado: This historic theater located in the heart of Guadalajara was founded in 1866 and is considered one of the finest examples of Italian neoclassical architecture in Mexico.

  • Instituto Cultural Cabanas: This UNESCO World Heritage site was built in the early 19th century and is renowned for its stunning murals that depict Mexico’s history

  • Palacio de Gobierno: The Governor’s Palace is a beautifully crafted building in the heart of Guadalajara that houses a notable collection of murals by Jose Clemente Orozco.

Natural Attractions of Jalisco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jalisco also features natural attractions that offer visitors the chance to explore the great outdoors.

  • Lake Chapala: This is the largest freshwater lake in Mexico, and it’s known for its peaceful atmosphere, stunning landscapes, and water activities

  • Barranca de Huentitan: This natural canyon located in Guadalajara boasts unique rock formations, a scenic waterfall, and is a great spot for hiking and mountain biking

  • Los Grillos Mountain Range: This mountain range located in Tequila, Jalisco, offers its visitors panoramic views and the opportunity to explore nature trails and trekking

Hiking/Biking Routes in Jalisco

Jalisco offers many miles of scenic and meandering paths for walking, hiking, and mountain biking. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • The Sierra Madre Occidental: This mountain range located in the northeast part of Jalisco features beautiful forests, dramatic cliffs, and breathtaking scenic views.

  • The Casa Herradura Tequila Tour: An informative and scenic bike tour that explores the landscape of the Highlands of Jalisco

Best Things to Do in Jalisco: Activities and Tours

Jalisco offers ample opportunities for visitors to enjoy, from boutique shopping in the Artisan markets to horseback riding tours. Here are some must-try local activities:

  • Tequila Tasting: Tequila is one of the region’s most popular drinks, with several towns in the state producing this agave-based liquor. Visitors can take Tequila tours and tasting that offer an introduction to the spirit and highlight the history of the town of Tequila.

  • Mariachi Music: Jalisco is home to Mariachi music, the heart and soul of Mexican culture, Made up of a group of musicians playing instruments like trumpets, violins, and guitars, you can enjoy live Mariachi music in many corners of Jalisco.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jalisco

If you have a week to explore Jalisco, here’s a recommended itinerary for the state:

  • Day 1: Arrive at Guadalajara International Airport and check into your accommodations. Explore the city of Guadalajara and visit architectural wonders like Guadalajara Cathedral, Teatro Degollado, and Instituto Cultural Cabanas

  • Day 2: Take a bike tour with Casa Herradura and learn about the history of tequila around the town of Tequila and the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 3: Visit the Guachimontones Pyramids in the town of Teuchitlan. The pyramids are a fascinating representation of the pre hispanic culture in Western Mexico

  • Day 4: Spend a day in Lake Chapala. The waters of this large lake are warm enough to swim in year-round.

  • Day 5: Take a drive down to Tapalpa and spend the day in the pueblo magico. Tapalpa is known for its rustic feel, beautiful stonehouses, and great hiking trails.

  • Day 6: Head down to the famous “the big loop” route around Los Grillos. This route covers a scenic 80km drive around this beautiful natural reserve in Tequila.

  • Day 7: Departure day for your flight back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Jalisco

Must-Try Dishes

Jalisco’s cuisine is diverse and pervasive, with plenty of street vendors, and traditional restaurants. Here are some of the dishes you should try:

  • Birria: Consisting of a spicy soup with meat, Birria is a Jalisco specialty that’s served piping hot and best enjoyed with corn tortillas

  • Pozole: This popular dish is a stew made with pork or chicken, hominy, and red chiles. It’s served with traditional garnishes such as lettuce, radish, onion, and lime.

  • Tacos: There are innumerable varieties of tacos in Jalisco, but they are always freshly made, always delicious, with a variety of meat and vegetarian fillings

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: A globally acclaimed spirit made from the blue agave plant, some of the most prominent distilleries in the world call Jalisco home.

  • Tejuino: A light alcoholic beverage, made from fermented corn and sugar, with lemon, salt and chile added.

Where to Stay in Jalisco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jalisco offers plenty of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top-rated properties:

  • Hotel Demetria: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the trendy Lafayette neighborhood in Guadalajara

  • Hostal Central: A budget-friendly hostel located in the historic center of Guadalajara that boasts excellent service and amenities

  • Casa Habita: A modern design-focused hotel located in the trendy neighborhood of Lafayette in Guadalajara

  • Posada de las Minas: A charming guesthouse located in the pueblo magico of Mineral de Pozos.

What to Buy in Jalisco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jalisco has a ton of charming artisan markets like the Mercado San Juan de Dios where visitors can buy unique souvenirs from the region.

  • Tequila: A bottle of this agave-based liquor is a must-buy for anyone visiting Jalisco

  • Guadalajara Crafts: From pottery to textiles, Guadalajara’s artisan markets have one-of-a-kind handicrafts that make for unique souvenirs

  • Silver: The region is famous for its high-quality silver jewelry and items such as Charro belts, which are perfect souvenirs

Local Culture and Customs of Jalisco

Jalisco is a friendly region, and visitors can expect warm hospitality no matter where they go.

  • Language: The official language is Spanish, and while most locals know English, it is always appreciated if you show an effort to speak Spanish.

  • Greetings: Greetings are essential in Mexican culture. When meeting someone for the first time, it’s customary to shake hands and introduce yourself.

Best Time to Visit Jalisco

Jalisco has a semi-arid climate with two seasons: dry and rainy. The best time to visit is during the dry season between November and February. The weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jalisco

Do:

  • Try the region’s most famous tequila and mezcal varieties

  • Learn a few Spanish words to enhance your experience

  • Respect the culture, customs, and traditions of the locals

Don’t:

  • Stay out late in areas you don’t know well, especially if you’re traveling alone

  • Be too flashy with high-end accessories or jewelry

  • Assume that everyone speaks English

Safety and Health

Safety in Jalisco

Jalisco is a safe region, but visitors should always take precautions, like any other city or region in the world. Some tips for staying safe include:

  • Always be aware of your surroundings, especially at night

  • Be attentive to your belongings such as your wallet, phone, and other valuables

  • If you’re exploring remote locations, it’s best to travel in groups with experienced guides

Health in Jalisco

Before traveling to Jalisco, it’s a good idea to speak to your doctor about the necessary vaccinations. It’s also essential to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and seek medical attention if you feel unwell.

Getting to Jalisco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jalisco

  • Guadalajara International Airport: This airport services the entire state and is located in the city of Guadalajara.

  • Puerto Vallarta International Airport: Located on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, a short drive away from Jalisco, this airport serves cities and towns nearby.

Bus Stations Near Jalisco

  • Guadalajara Central Bus Station: This is the region’s main bus terminal and connects to cities and towns around Jalisco.

Other Options

  • Car rental companies: If you’re looking for more freedom and flexibility to explore Jalisco, renting a car is a great option.

  • Private Tours: For personalized service and a more curated experience of Jalisco, visitors can opt for private tours.

How to Get Around in Jalisco

Jalisco has an efficient transportation network that visitors can use to explore the region. Options include rental cars, buses, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Jalisco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Miguel de Allende, Guanajuato: Known for its Baroque colonial architecture and cobblestone streets.

  • Puerto Vallarta: Famous for its beautiful beaches, historical landmarks, and great nightlife.

Conclusion

Jalisco is a region that offers its visitors an immersion into Mexico’s culture, heritage, and natural beauty. With its tequila tours, stunning landscapes, and vibrant attractions, Jalisco is a must-visit destination for those looking to explore Mexico’s western region!
