Introduction to Lagos de Moreno

Lagos de Moreno is a beautiful colonial town located in Jalisco, a state in the Pacific Coast region of Mexico. It is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture, historical landmarks, natural beauty, and cultural significance. Lagos de Moreno is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the rich history and authentic beauty of Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Lagos de Moreno

If you are planning to visit Lagos de Moreno, here are the top places you should include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa-Museo Juan Rulfo: This museum is dedicated to the work of Juan Rulfo, a Mexican writer who was born in Lagos de Moreno. It features exhibits on his life and works, as well as thematic exhibits on aspects of Mexican culture and history.

  • Templo de la Merced: This church was constructed in the 16th century and is considered to be one of the most beautiful churches in Mexico. It features a stunning Baroque-style façade and an ornate interior with intricate carvings and decorations.

  • Palacio Municipal: This historic building houses the local government offices and features neoclassical architecture. It’s definitely worth a visit to admire its grandeur.

Natural Attractions of Lagos de Moreno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sierra de Lagos de Moreno: This area offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region, overlooking stunning views of the town and the surrounding hills. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery, fresh air and take some time out from a busy urban routine.

  • Lagos de Moreno Municipal Park: Ideal for families, this park includes a lake and plenty of green space to enjoy a picnic, play sports, or just relax.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lagos de Moreno

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will appreciate the outdoor opportunities available in Lagos de Moreno. Sierra de Lagos de Moreno offers hiking trails at all levels, from easy to advanced, take in the scenic views of the surrounding countryside. Biking trails are also available in the area, and it’s an excellent way to explore the landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Lagos de Moreno: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Lagos de Moreno:

  • Horseback Riding: One of the most popular activities in Lagos de Moreno is horseback riding, where you can explore the natural beauty of the area on horseback.

  • Local Food Tour: Lagos de Moreno has a rich culinary culture, and a local food tour offers you a chance to sample the traditional cuisine.

  • Fairs and Festivals: Throughout the year, Lagos de Moreno hosts several fairs and festivals like Feria de Lagos, which is an annual celebration of local agriculture.

Food and Drink Scene of Lagos de Moreno

Lagos de Moreno is known for its rich culinary culture, featuring a blend of traditional Mexican flavors with some of its own unique dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Birria: A savory stew made with goat meat, spices, and chilies.

  • Carnitas: Succulent fried pork, the best part is the crispy exterior.

  • Banderillas: A popular street food of Lagos de Moreno which includes skewered meat, pineapple, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: Made from the blue agave plant, tequila is the best-known Mexican drink. Mexico produces some of the world’s best tequilas, and Lagos de Moreno has a long-standing tequila-making tradition.

  • Pulque: Made from the fermented sap of the agave plant, it’s a traditional Mexican probiotic drink. It’s sour and sweet at the same time and packs a punch.

Where to Stay in Lagos de Moreno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are options for different budgets and preferences when it comes to accommodations in Lagos de Moreno. Here are some of the best:

  • Hotel Posada Señorial: A colonial-style hotel, located in the heart of the historic center, featuring beautiful gardens and comfortable rooms.

  • Hotel Casona de Teté: A small boutique hotel housed in a beautiful colonial-style building that’s a great choice for those seeking privacy and tranquility.

  • Hostal de la Abuela: A lovely hostel located in the heart of Lagos de Moreno, with a friendly staff that will help you plan your activities in the town.

What to Buy in Lagos de Moreno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lagos de Moreno offers many traditional crafts and products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Pottery: The locally produced pottery of Lagos de Moreno is famous for its quality and the intricate designs.

  • Tequila: Tequila is a popular locally produced beverage, and you can find the best quality bottles in many shops and markets around town.

Local Culture and Customs of Lagos de Moreno

The culture of Lagos de Moreno is a diverse mix of Mexican and Spanish influences. People in Lagos de Moreno are friendly and hospitable, and traditions and festivals are a significant part of their culture.

Best Time to Visit Lagos de Moreno

The best time to visit Lagos de Moreno is between November to February when the weather is mild, and the rainfall is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lagos de Moreno

When visiting Lagos de Moreno, keep the following do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions; dress conservatively while visiting religious places.

  • Try the local street food and food market, it’s some of the best food in Mexico.

  • Attend local festivals, be sure to check the dates for festivals like Feria de Lagos in advance.

Don’t:

  • Touch the colonial buildings, many of them are very aged so keep hands to yourself.

  • Leave trash behind; please clean up after yourself.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lagos de Moreno

Lagos de Moreno is a relatively safe city, but travelers should always take precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone late at night in isolated areas.

  • Keep your valuables safe and avoid taking them out in crowded areas.

  • Follow recommended COVID-19 protocols, including wearing a mask and practicing social distancing.

Health in Lagos de Moreno

Overall, Lagos de Moreno is a safe city when it comes to health, and common sense precautions should keep most travelers from contracting any illness.

Getting to Lagos de Moreno: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lagos de Moreno is well-connected to major cities in the region, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Lagos de Moreno

  • Guadalajara International Airport: The nearest airport to Lagos de Moreno is the Guadalajara International Airport, which is approximately 2 hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Lagos de Moreno

  • Central de Autobuses Guadalajara: This bus station is the main link for transportation to and from Lagos de Moreno.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is an option for visitors who enjoy self-driving and want more flexibility in their travels.

How to Get Around Lagos de Moreno

The best way to get around Lagos de Moreno is by foot. The historic center of the town is compact and pedestrian-friendly. Taxis and local buses are available for those traveling outside of the town.

Where to Go Next from Lagos de Moreno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some exciting nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • San Juan de los Lagos: A beautiful colonial town, with a famous basilica that attracts millions of pilgrims each year.

  • El Salto Waterfalls: This stunning natural attraction is perfect for a day trip, great for outdoor recreation - hiking, swimming or just enjoying the views.

Conclusion

Lagos de Moreno is a charming colonial town in Mexico that offers plenty of cultural, historical, and outdoor experiences. From exploring the historic center and local food, drink, and culture to enjoying the natural beauty of the area, Lagos de Moreno has something for every traveler. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to this hidden gem and discover charming Mexico at its finest.
