Introduction to Barra de Navidad

Barra de Navidad is a beautiful harbor village located on the Pacific Coast of Mexico. Surrounded by the Sierra Madre Mountains and the Pacific Ocean, it’s a popular destination for outdoor adventurers. The town is well-known for its cultural attractions and warm hospitality, making it an ideal vacation spot for anyone who wants to experience the true essence of Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Barra de Navidad

If you’re planning a visit to Barra de Navidad, you’ll have plenty of exciting places to explore. Here are some of the must-see sites in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sanctuary of Our Lady of the Rosary: This beautiful church and historic landmark is known for its intricate design and stunning artwork.

  • Santiago Apostol Parish: A historic church with jaw-dropping architecture and a peaceful atmosphere.

  • Melaque Square: A small plaza where locals usually gather, shop and eat street food.

Natural Attractions of Barra de Navidad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Barra de Navidad Beach: This has been voted one of the most beautiful beaches in Mexico and famous for its crystal-clear water and wide array of outdoor activities.

  • Isla Navidad: The island is full of natural beauty, where you can explore the mangrove forest, watch colorful birds, and soak in the scenic ocean views.

  • Colimilla Bay: Feeling adventurous? Take a boat tour to this nearby bay and enjoy some spectacular views of Barra de Navidad from the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barra de Navidad

For outdoor enthusiasts, there are plenty of hiking and biking routes in Barra de Navidad that you can try. Among the popular routes are:

  • Cerro del Toro: This hilltop offers you a panoramic view of the beautiful town.

  • La Placita: This flat biking route will take you around the town center and the beach.

  • Colimilla to Barra de Navidad: If you’re up for an adventure, this 12-kilometer route will take you along the coast and breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Barra de Navidad: Activities and Tours

Barra de Navidad is full of fun activities and tours that will make your visit a memorable one. Consider adding the following to your itinerary:

  • Fishing tour: Take a fishing tour and catch your own dinner.

  • Kayaking: Get up close with nature as you explore the lagoon and see migratory birds.

  • Horseback riding: Enjoy a horseback ride along the beach and watch the stunning sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Barra de Navidad

Barra de Navidad has a diverse culinary scene with a mix of local and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: A traditional Mexican dish made of masa dough and filled with various ingredients, including meats and vegetables.

  • Chilate: A local dish made with rice, milk, and cinnamon.

  • Tinga: A popular chicken dish marinated in tomato sauce, onions, and chipotle peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: A world-famous Mexican beverage made from the blue agave plant.

  • Michelada: A beer cocktail made with lime juice, hot sauce, and tomato juice.

  • Agua de Jamaica: A refreshing hibiscus-flavored water.

Where to Stay in Barra de Navidad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barra de Navidad has a range of options when it comes to accommodations. You can choose from hotel resorts to local guesthouses, depending on your budget. The following are some recommended places to stay:

  • Grand Isla Navidad Resort: An upscale resort with ocean views and plenty of amenities.

  • Hotel Barra de Navidad: This modern hotel is situated right in the heart of the town.

  • Villa Santa Barbara: A cozy guesthouse that provides excellent service at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Barra de Navidad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs you can buy as a way to remember your trip to Barra de Navidad:

  • Mexican poncho: A traditional triangular-shaped cloth used as a garment.

  • Tequila: Take home a bottle (or two) of one of Mexico’s most iconic drinks.

  • Handmade crafts: Visit local markets and buy some handmade jewelry, clothes, or pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Barra de Navidad

Barra de Navidad is a town with deep-rooted cultural traditions that are still admired by the locals. Respect for elders is highly valued, and it is considered polite to greet people with a smile and nod or a “Buenos días” (good morning). The locals also take pride in celebrating their heritage and culture with vibrant parades, festivals, and fairs.

Best Time to Visit Barra de Navidad

The best time to visit Barra de Navidad is during the dry season, which is from November to April. The weather is less humid, and you’ll be able to enjoy the outdoor activities without being interrupted by the monsoon season. If you’re a budget-conscious traveler, consider planning your trip in late spring or early fall months when room rates are more affordable than peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barra de Navidad

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Barra de Navidad:

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ customs and traditions.

  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes when going for outdoor adventures and activities.

  • Try traditional Mexican foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore littering regulations and leave trash outside the designated areas.

  • Touch or disturb wildlife.

  • Walk around at late at night in secluded areas.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy while in Barra de Navidad:

Safety in Barra de Navidad

  • When going outside, carry essential items and leave any valuables at your hotel.

  • Watch out for traffic when crossing the road or while cycling.

  • Only get into a taxi from an official taxi stand.

Health in Barra de Navidad

  • Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid consuming uncooked food.

  • Wear sunscreen and use insect repellent when outdoors to protect from the sun and prevent mosquito bites.

  • Visit a local clinic or hospital if feeling unwell or facing any health issues.

How to Get Around in Barra de Navidad

Getting around in Barra de Navidad is quite easy, depending on your preference. You can rent a car, ride a taxi, or ride a bicycle or scooter. Walking is also an option if you prefer a slow pace and want to enjoy the small-town atmosphere.

Where to Go Next from Barra de Navidad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time and want to add other destinations, Barra de Navidad offers access to nearby towns of Jalisco that you can explore:

  • Melaque: A small beach town with charming streets and affordable accommodations.

  • La Manzanilla: A town known for its pristine beaches and sea turtle sanctuary.

  • Puerto Vallarta: A coastal town famous for its outdoor adventures, vibrant nightlife, and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Barra de Navidad is a perfect destination if you’re seeking an authentic and intimate look into Mexico’s culture and natural beauty. A combination of outdoor adventures, culinary experiences, and cultural attractions makes it an ideal vacation spot for everyone, from solo travelers to families.
