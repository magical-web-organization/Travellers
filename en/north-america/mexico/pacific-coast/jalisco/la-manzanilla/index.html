La Manzanilla is a charming coastal town in the Pacific coast region of Jalisco, Mexico. Known for its timeless beauty and laidback lifestyle, La Manzanilla is a perfect getaway for beach lovers, adventure seekers, and culture enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking to soak up the sunshine, explore the local cuisine, or immerse yourself in the rich history of the region, La Manzanilla has plenty to offer.

In this La Manzanilla travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey into the heart of Mexico’s Pacific coast. From the town’s cultural attractions to its natural landscapes, food scene, accommodation options, and local customs, we’ve got everything you need to know to make the most of your La Manzanilla vacation.

Introduction to La Manzanilla

Nestled between the Sierra Madre mountains and the Pacific Ocean, La Manzanilla is a small fishing village that has grown into a popular tourist destination. The town is situated on a scenic bay and boasts some of the most picturesque beaches in the region. La Manzanilla has a rich history that is reflected in its traditional architecture, museums, and festivals. The town is also known for its eco-tourism initiatives, which have helped preserve the local environment.

List of Places to Visit in La Manzanilla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

La Manzanilla has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in La Manzanilla:

  • Casa de la Cultura: A cultural center that hosts exhibitions, workshops, and events related to art, music, and literature.
  • Boca de Iguanas: An archaeological site that dates back to the pre-Columbian era. Visitors can see the remains of a pyramid, a ball court, and other structures.
  • Church of Our Lady of the Rosary: A beautiful church that was built in the 18th century and features Baroque and Neoclassical architecture.
  • The Malecon: a seawall with stunning sunrise and sunset views on the bay.

Natural Attractions of La Manzanilla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Manzanilla is surrounded by natural beauty and offers plenty of outdoor activities for adventure seekers. Here are some of the top natural attractions in La Manzanilla:

  • Tenacatita Bay: A beautiful bay with clear waters and a coral reef that is perfect for snorkeling and swimming.
  • La Manzanilla Beach: A long and wide beach near the town center, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Boca de Iguanas Beach: A secluded beach with calm waters and white sand, perfect for a romantic picnic.
  • La Reserva de la Biosfera Sierra de Manantlán: A UNESCO-recognized biosphere reserve that is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species. Visitors can go hiking, birdwatching, or simply enjoy the natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Manzanilla

La Manzanilla is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes in La Manzanilla:

  • La Loma Trail: A challenging hike that takes you up a hillside overlooking the town and the bay.
  • Boca de Iguanas Trail: A relatively easy hike that takes you through a forest and ends at a remote beach.
  • La Manzanilla Bike Trail: A scenic trail that takes you through the town’s hills and beaches, perfect for mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in La Manzanilla: Activities and Tours

La Manzanilla has plenty of opportunities for visitors to immerse themselves in the local culture and lifestyle. Here are some of the best things to do in La Manzanilla:

  • Learn to Surf: La Manzanilla’s clear waters and gentle waves make it an ideal place to learn how to surf. There are several surf schools in town that offer lessons.
  • Take a boat tour: Explore the Tenacatita Bay and nearby beaches on a boat tour. You can book a tour with a local operator in town.
  • Join a cooking class: Learn how to prepare authentic Mexican dishes from a local chef. There are several cooking classes in La Manzanilla that offer hands-on cooking experiences.
  • Attend a local festival: La Manzanilla hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Day of the Dead, the Feast of Our Lady of Guadalupe, and the Fisherman’s Festival.
  • Take a yoga class at La Casa de la Cultura: Relax and rejuvenate with a yoga class at La Casa de la Cultura.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Manzanilla

If you’re planning to spend a week in La Manzanilla, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your vacation:

  • Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Church of Our Lady of the Rosary and a sunset on the Malecon.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of Tenacatita Bay and nearby beaches.
  • Day 3: Spend a day at La Manzanilla Beach, enjoying the sun, sand, and sea.
  • Day 4: Go on a hiking or biking adventure and explore the town’s hills and forests.
  • Day 5: Discover the local cuisine by taking a cooking class or sampling the local restaurant scene.
  • Day 6: Attend a local festival or cultural event.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of La Manzanilla.

Food and Drink Scene of La Manzanilla

La Manzanilla’s food scene is a fusion of traditional Mexican and seafood dishes with a modern twist. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: A seafood dish that is prepared with fresh raw fish, lime juice, and vegetables.
  • Tamales: A traditional Mexican dish made of corn masa, usually with pork or chicken filling.
  • Pozole: A hearty soup made of hominy and pork or chicken, with a range of garnishes.
  • Tacos: The classic Mexican street food, try the fish tacos for the seafood flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: A traditional Mexican spirit made from the agave plant mash, similar to tequila, which is produced in a distinct building type called Palenque.
  • Margarita: A classic tequila cocktail made with lime juice, triple sec, and salt around the rim of the glass.
  • Michelada: A refreshing beer cocktail made with lime juice, hot sauce, and Worcestershire sauce.

Where to Stay in La Manzanilla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Manzanilla offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in La Manzanilla:

  • La Casa de la Iguana: A boutique hotel located on the beach, with rooms and suites that offer stunning ocean views.
  • Coco Loco: A hostel that offers shared and private rooms, located close to the beach and town center.
  • Casa de la Cultura B&B: A budget-friendly bed and breakfast that offers comfortable rooms and a central location.

What to Buy in La Manzanilla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

La Manzanilla is known for its arts and crafts, as well as its local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the best souvenirs and products to bring home from La Manzanilla:

  • Handcrafted pottery: La Manzanilla is known for its beautiful pottery, which is made by local artisans using traditional techniques.
  • Organic coffee: La Manzanilla is surrounded by coffee plantations, and the local coffee is considered some of the best in the region.
  • Sea Salt: La Manzanilla’s sea salt is harvested using traditional methods and is prized for its flavor and texture.
  • Huichol Art: A traditional art form of the Huichol people that is characterized by intricate beadwork and colorful patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of La Manzanilla

La Manzanilla is a town that is deeply rooted in Mexican culture and traditions. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and etiquette while in town. Here are some of the customs to keep in mind:

  • Greeting others is important: It’s customary to greet people with a smile and a “Buenos dias,” “Buenas tardes,” or “Buenas noches” depending on the time of day.
  • Respect religious traditions: Many locals are devout Catholics, and religious festivals and processions are an important part of the town’s culture. Visitors are expected to respect these traditions.
  • Dress appropriately: La Manzanilla is a casual town, but visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Respect the local wildlife: La Manzanilla is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including sea turtles and crocodiles. Visitors should not disturb or harm these animals.

Best Time to Visit La Manzanilla

The best time to visit La Manzanilla is from November to May when the weather is warm and dry, and the sea is calm. The months of July and August can be rainy and humid, while June and September are considered transition months.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Manzanilla

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region.
  • Be polite and courteous to locals.

Don’t:

  • Disturb local wildlife.
  • Be disrespectful towards local customs and traditions.
  • Be loud or rowdy late at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Manzanilla

La Manzanilla is a safe town, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other tourist destination. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid displaying expensive jewelry or electronics in public.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place, such as a hotel safe.
  • Stick to well-lit and populated areas at night.

Health in La Manzanilla

La Manzanilla is located in a tropical region, so visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and chikungunya. Here are some health tips:

  • Wear insect repellent, long sleeves, and pants when outdoors.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water or ice made with tap water.
  • Use sunblock with SPF 30 or higher to protect against sunburn and skin damage.

Getting to La Manzanilla: Transportation Options and Logistics

La Manzanilla is located approximately 3.5 hours from Guadalajara, Mexico’s second-largest city. Here are some transportation options for getting to La Manzanilla:

Airports Near La Manzanilla

  • Playa de Oro International Airport (ZLO): Located in Manzanillo, is the closest airport to La Manzanilla, approximately 50 miles (80 km) away.
  • Guadalajara International Airport (GDL): located in Guadalajara, is the second-closest airport, approximately 125 miles (200 km) from La Manzanilla.

Bus Stations Near La Manzanilla

  • Primera Plus: A bus company that offers service from Guadalajara to La Manzanilla.
  • ETN: A luxury bus company that offers service to both Manzanillo and Puerto Vallarta.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the region at your own pace. Several car rental companies have offices at the airports in Guadalajara and Manzanillo.
  • Private Transfer: Private transfer services are available that will pick you up from the airport and take you directly to your hotel.

How to Get Around in La Manzanilla

La Manzanilla is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Motorized taxis are also available for longer journeys. Golf carts and ATV’s are also available for rent in town.

Where to Go Next from La Manzanilla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

La Manzanilla is located in a region with many other interesting destinations. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Barra de Navidad: A quaint fishing village that is famous for its seafood restaurants and charming architecture.
  • Puerto Vallarta: A vibrant beach town that offers a wide range of activities and attractions, including art galleries, nightlife, and zip-lining.
  • Colima: A colonial city that is home to a UNESCO-recognized archaeological site, as well as several museums and historic sites.

Conclusion

La Manzanilla is a hidden gem of Mexico’s Pacific coast, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. From its scenic beaches and forests to its delicious cuisine and friendly locals, there’s something for everyone in La Manzanilla. Whether you’re planning a week-long vacation or just a weekend getaway, be sure to put La Manzanilla on your travel bucket list.
