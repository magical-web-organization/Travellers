Introduction to Puerto Vallarta

Located in the Pacific Coast state of Jalisco, Mexico, Puerto Vallarta is a popular tourist destination known for its beautiful beaches, rich culture, and warm weather. The city is surrounded by mountains and is adorned with cobblestone streets, red-tiled roofs, and white-washed buildings that portray a traditional Mexican charm. Puerto Vallarta has become one of the busiest vacation spots for people around the world because of its excellent food, scenic beauty, and wide array of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Puerto Vallarta

There is a plethora of places to visit while in Puerto Vallarta. We have categorized the top tourist spots to include culturally and historically significant attractions, natural attractions, cycling and hiking routes, best things to do, where to stay, what to buy, local customs, health and safety tips, and transportation options.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • (1) The Cuale Archaeological Museum and Cultural Center is a must-visit museum where you can get a glimpse into ancient Mesoamerican cultures. It has exhibitions on pottery, art, and other historical artifacts of civilizations that thrived in this region.

  • (2) The Church of Our Lady of Guadalupe is a famous landmark in Puerto Vallarta and attracts a lot of tourists. It is a breathtakingly beautiful church that honors the patron saint of Mexico, Virgin Mary, and has a unique architecture style.

Natural Attractions of Puerto Vallarta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • (1) The Bay of Banderas is one of the largest bays in the world and is home to numerous marine species like dolphins, whales, and sea turtles. You can take a boat tour to explore the bay or go scuba diving to enjoy the underwater beauty.

  • (2) Los Arcos is a protected marine area that showcases a natural arch-shaped formation of rocks rising from the water. A boat ride through these soaring formations will leave you breathless.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puerto Vallarta

  • (1) The Gay Ridge Trail is a moderate hiking trail that takes approximately 3 hours to complete. It passes through a dense jungle and offers breathtaking views of the coastline.

  • (2) The Sayulita Trail is a relatively easy trail and takes only an hour or two to complete. It offers some scenic views and takes you through a small town along the way.

Best Things to Do in Puerto Vallarta: Activities and Tours

  • (1) The Los Veranos Canopy Tour is an adventurous canopy tour that takes you through the lush Sierra Madre Mountains with adrenaline-inducing ziplines, swinging bridges, and rappelling.

  • (2) The Dolphin Adventure Puerto Vallarta is a unique experience where you can interact with playful dolphins and get to know these friendly creatures up close and personal.

Week-Long Itinerary for Puerto Vallarta

Day Activity
1 Explore Downtown Puerto Vallarta and see the famous El Malecon boardwalk.
2 Visit the breathtaking Church of Our Lady of Guadalupe.
3 Go on a shopping spree in the busy neighborhoods of Zona Romantica and Gringo Gulch.
4 Take a boat tour of Los Arcos and Bay of Banderas.
5 Hike the Gay Ridge Trail for some scenic views of the coastline.
6 Participate in the Los Veranos Canopy Tour for an adventurous day.
7 Take a morning dip into the pristine waters of Playa Las Gemelas.

Food and Drink Scene of Puerto Vallarta

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Birria, a spicy stew made with goat meat, is a staple dish in Jalisco and a must-try in Puerto Vallarta.

  • (2) Tacos al Pastor, consisting of pork meat slow-roasted in spices and cooked on a spit, is the most popular taco variety in Puerto Vallarta.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Tequila, produced in the state of Jalisco, is the popular drink of the region. Locals prefer drinking it in shots, but tequila-based cocktails are also available.

  • (2) Michelada is a refreshing drink that’s a mix of beer, lime juice, tomato juice, and spices.

Where to Stay in Puerto Vallarta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Puerto Vallarta offers numerous options for accommodation, from luxury hotels to backpacker hostels. The top-rated accommodations in the city include The St. Regis Punta Mita Resort, Grand Miramar All Luxury Suites & Residences, and Hacienda San Ángel.

What to Buy in Puerto Vallarta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • (1) Ceramics and pottery are popular among tourists in Puerto Vallarta. Hand-painted ceramic plates, vases, and figurines make great souvenirs.

  • (2) Huichol art is a traditional form of beadwork found in Puerto Vallarta. The intricate designs and vibrant colors make them unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Puerto Vallarta

Puerto Vallarta is a conservative city where people value family, tradition, and culture. It’s essential to respect local customs and dress appropriately in public places.

Best Time to Visit Puerto Vallarta

The best time to visit Puerto Vallarta is during the winter months, from December to February, when the weather is dry and mild, and the humidity is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puerto Vallarta

Do:

  • (1) Respect local customs and traditions.

  • (2) Carry local currency with you.

Don’t:

  • (1) Drink tap water, only drink filtered or bottled water.

  • (2) Wear beachwear or miniskirts on the street.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puerto Vallarta

Puerto Vallarta is a safe city, but like any other tourist destination, it’s advisable to take precautionary measures against petty theft, pickpocketing, and scams.

Health in Puerto Vallarta

Ensure that you drink only bottled or filtered water and carry at least one mosquito repellent as the dengue fever and Zica virus cases are rare but do happen in the rainy season.

Getting to Puerto Vallarta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puerto Vallarta

The Licenciado Gustavo Díaz Ordaz International Airport is the nearest airport to Puerto Vallarta.

Bus Stations Near Puerto Vallarta

The main bus station is located downtown, and from there, buses connect to the rest of the country.

Other Options

Taxis, Uber, and rental cars are also available to navigate the city.

How to Get Around in Puerto Vallarta

Buses, taxis, Uber, and car rentals are available to travel within the city. The yellow ATMs and local buses are cheaper and easily accessible options.

Where to Go Next from Puerto Vallarta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • (1) San Sebastian is a mining town located in the mountains and known for its 18th-century architecture and cobblestone streets.

  • (2) Sayulita is a popular surf town with a bohemian vibe and a laid-back lifestyle.

Conclusion

Puerto Vallarta is a fabulous destination that has a lot to offer in terms of culture, natural beauty, and fun activities. This travel guide is beneficial for tourists looking for an unforgettable Mexican vacation in Puerto Vallarta.
