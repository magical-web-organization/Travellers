Introduction to Tepatitlan

Tepatitlan is a charming city in the Jalisco state of Mexico, situated in the Pacific Coast region of North America. It is known for its delicious food, rich culture, and stunning natural attractions. Tepatitlan is also close to some of the most popular tourist destinations in Mexico, making it an ideal base for your Mexican adventure. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and food to try in Tepatitlan.

List of Places to Visit in Tepatitlan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de la Cuidad: This museum showcases the history and culture of Tepatitlan through various exhibits and artifacts.
  • Templo de San Francisco: This iconic church is a blend of baroque and neoclassical architecture and has been a prominent religious site in Tepatitlan since the 18th century.
  • Plaza de Toros: Bullfighting is a popular sport in Mexico, and Tepatitlan’s bullring is one of the most important in the country.

Natural Attractions of Tepatitlan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Nacional Barranca de Huentitan: This national park is a beautiful natural wonderland located just outside Tepatitlan. It offers hiking trails, river rafting, and stunning views of the Barranca de Huentitan canyon.
  • Presa de la Luz: This man-made lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Cerro Gordo: This hill provides panoramic views of Tepatitlan and is an excellent spot for hiking and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tepatitlan

  • La Campana Trail: This is a popular trail that takes you to the top of the Cerro Gordo hill. It is a challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning views of Tepatitlan.
  • El Capulin Trail: This is a moderate-level hike that takes you through the Barranca de Huentitan canyon. It offers spectacular views of the waterfall and surrounding lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Tepatitlan: Activities and Tours

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over Tepatitlan’s gorgeous countryside.
  • Take a cultural walking tour of Tepatitlan to learn more about the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Visit a local tequila distillery and learn how Mexico’s most famous drink is made.

Food and Drink Scene of Tepatitlan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Birria: This local dish is made with marinated goat or beef and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Carnitas: This slow-cooked pork is served with tortillas, onion, and cilantro and is a must-try street food in Tepatitlan.
  • Pozole: This hearty stew made with pork, hominy, and spices is a popular breakfast dish in Tepatitlan.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: Mexico’s national drink is made from the blue agave plant and is produced in the surrounding region.
  • Raicilla: This is another distilled drink made from agave, similar to tequila, but with a more complex flavor profile.
  • Agua Fresca: This refreshing drink is made from fresh fruit, sugar, and water and is a popular non-alcoholic beverage in Tepatitlan.

Where to Stay in Tepatitlan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Posada Santa Fe: This charming hotel is situated in the heart of Tepatitlan and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Casa de Huespedes Elvira: This guesthouse is located in a tranquil neighborhood and offers clean, spacious rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Hostal Chimalli: This hostel is a great option for budget travelers and offers comfortable dormitories and private rooms.

What to Buy in Tepatitlan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artisanal Ceramics: Tepatitlan is known for its high-quality pottery and ceramic products, which make for excellent souvenirs.
  • Tequila: Bring back a bottle of Mexico’s famous drink to share with friends and family back home.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Tepatitlan is home to many skilled artisans who create beautiful handmade jewelry using locally-sourced materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Tepatitlan

Tepatitlan is home to a vibrant and friendly local community. The people of Tepatitlan are warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to dive into the local culture. It’s important to remember to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when entering religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Tepatitlan

The best time to visit Tepatitlan is between October and May when the weather is mild and pleasant. Avoid visiting during the rainy season, which typically runs from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tepatitlan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and sample Mexico’s famous tequila.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs and engage with the friendly locals.
  • Take in the stunning natural beauty of Tepatitlan’s surrounding mountains and canyons.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when entering religious sites.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English – it’s always best to try and communicate in Spanish.
  • Leave valuable items unattended in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tepatitlan

Tepatitlan is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions when traveling to a new destination. Keep your valuables close and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Tepatitlan

Ensure that you have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Tepatitlan. It’s recommended to have all standard vaccinations up-to-date before traveling to Mexico.

Getting to Tepatitlan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tepatitlan is easily accessible by plane, bus, or car.

Airports Near Tepatitlan

The nearest major airport to Tepatitlan is the Guadalajara International Airport, located approximately 83 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Tepatitlan

The main bus station in Tepatitlan is the Central Camionera de Tepatitlan, which offers frequent connections to other cities in Jalisco.

Other Options

You can also drive to Tepatitlan or hire a private transfer from Guadalajara International Airport.

How to Get Around in Tepatitlan

Tepatitlan is a small city, and it’s easy to explore on foot. Taxis and public transportation are also available.

Where to Go Next from Tepatitlan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations to visit from Tepatitlan include:

  • Guadalajara: Mexico’s second-largest city is full of history, culture, and excellent food.
  • Lake Chapala: This beautiful lake is the largest in Mexico and offers stunning views and outdoor activities.
  • Tequila: Explore the birthplace of Mexico’s most famous drink and learn more about its production process.

Conclusion

Tepatitlan is a hidden gem in Mexico and is an excellent destination for those looking to experience the rich history, culture, and stunning natural beauty of Mexico.
