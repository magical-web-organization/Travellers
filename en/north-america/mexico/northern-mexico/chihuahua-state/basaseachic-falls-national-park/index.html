Introduction to Basaseachic Falls National Park

Located in Northern Mexico, in the state of Chihuahua, Basaseachic Falls National Park is a natural wonder that attracts tourists from all over the world. The park is home to the stunning Basaseachic Falls, which is one of the tallest waterfalls in Mexico, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

The park covers an area of 11,850 hectares and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including the famous Chihuahuan Desert, oak forests, and pine forests. The park also houses traces of ancient civilizations, making it a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Basaseachic Falls National Park

Here’s a list of the top attractions and places to explore in Basaseachic Falls National Park:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cueva de las Monas: a cave that displays the impressive rock formations formed over the years.

  • Cusárare: a small mountain village known for its picturesque church and indigenous crafts.

  • Cerro Grande: a historical site containing a 20th century fortress.

Natural Attractions of Basaseachic Falls National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Basaseachic Falls: a breathtaking waterfall that stands majestic at a height of 246 meters, and is surrounded by lush forests.

  • Recreational Areas: located throughout the park offering a variety of recreational activities like campground, picnic areas, grills and climbing walls.

  • Rio Conchos: a river that runs through the park offering lots of outdoor recreational opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basaseachic Falls National Park

The park offers several hiking and biking trails that offer a chance to explore the park’s natural beauty. Here are a few popular routes to discover in Basaseachic Falls National Park:

  • Cañón del Pegüis: a 4 km long canyon trail that passes through various vegetation landscapes, cliffs, and streams.

  • El Hechizero: a 5.6 km trail that takes you through the forest to the lookout point of the waterfall.

  • Mirador: a 6 km trail that provides panoramic views of the park, the Basaseachic Falls, and Rio Conchos.

Best Things to Do in Basaseachic Falls National Park: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, there are several other fun things to do in Basaseachic Falls National Park:

  • Take a guided tour of the park to learn about its flora, fauna and history.

  • Go camping in one of the park’s campgrounds to experience nature up close.

  • Try out rock climbing on one of the climbing walls in the recreational areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Basaseachic Falls National Park

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Basaseachic Falls National Park:

Day 1: Visit Basaseachic Falls

Day 2: Explore the nearby indigenous villages of San Ignacio Arareko and Cusárare.

Day 3: Visit Cueva de las Monas.

Day 4: Explore Cerro Grande.

Day 5: Hike to Mirador.

Day 6: Go camping and try rock climbing.

Day 7: Take guided tours for nature and culture exploration.

Food and Drink Scene of Basaseachic Falls National Park

When it comes to food and drink in Basaseachic Falls National Park, here are a few local dishes and beverages that you must not miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Birria: a spicy stew made with goat or sheep meat.

  • Enchiladas: tortillas stuffed with beef, chicken or cheese, and smothered in tomato sauce.

  • Sopaipillas: deep-fried pastries that are served with honey.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: a traditional alcoholic drink made from the sap of agave plants.

  • Tequila: a famous Mexican liquor made from blue agave plants.

  • Margarita: a cocktail made with lime juice, tequila, and triple sec.

Where to Stay in Basaseachic Falls National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For accommodation, there are several options for tourists to choose from in and around Basaseachic Falls National Park. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Hotel Mision: strategically located near the park’s entrance, it offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.

  • Cabañas Basaseachic: located within the park, it offers the experience of staying amidst nature.

  • Hacienda Bustillos: located in San Ignacio Arareko, it offers historical charm and has a restaurant on site.

What to Buy in Basaseachic Falls National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While in the park, there are several local products and souvenirs to buy, including:

  • Handmade indigenous crafts like pottery, baskets, and textiles.

  • Mexican blankets and rugs.

  • Woven belts and bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Basaseachic Falls National Park

The local culture and customs of Basaseachic Falls National Park revolve around the park’s indigenous communities. The Raramuri (Tarahumara) people are one of the main indigenous populations in the region, and their culture can be explored through visiting indigenous villages and experiencing their festivals and rituals. It’s important to respect their customs and values when visiting such sites.

Best Time to Visit Basaseachic Falls National Park

The best time to visit Basaseachic Falls National Park is between November and April when the climate is cooler and the waterfalls are at their fullest. The park gets crowded from December to January, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Basaseachic Falls National Park

Here are a few Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Follow marked trails and leave no trash to protect the environment.

  • Respect the customs and culture of the local indigenous communities.

  • Carry enough water and sun protection while hiking.

Don’t:

  • Enter any prohibited areas or disturb the wildlife.

  • Litter or damage any natural or cultural resources.

  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Basaseachic Falls National Park

While Basaseachic Falls National Park is generally safe for tourists, it is still important to take necessary precautions:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid shady or isolated areas.

  • Don’t carry valuable items while hiking or wandering.

  • Follow the rules and guidelines at camping and recreational areas.

Health in Basaseachic Falls National Park

While the park is surrounded by natural beauty, it is still necessary to keep the following health concerns in mind:

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent stomach problems.

  • Carry essential medication and insect repellent.

  • Protect against sun exposure by using hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen.

Getting to Basaseachic Falls National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Basaseachic Falls National Park

The closest airport is Ciudad Juarez International Airport, which is about 312 km away from Basaseachic Falls National Park.

Bus Stations Near Basaseachic Falls National Park

There is a regular bus service available to reach Creel, a nearby town from where another bus or taxi can be taken to reach the park.

Other Options

Hiring a taxi or renting a car is also an option to reach the park.

How to Get Around in Basaseachic Falls National Park

There are several options available for tourists to get around the park, including hiking, biking, and car rental. The park also has local guides who offer guided tours for cultural and natural exploration.

Where to Go Next from Basaseachic Falls National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that are worth visiting:

  • Copper Canyon: a series of canyons located close to the park that is known for its scenic beauty and hiking.

  • Chihuahua City: the capital city of the state, located about 340 km away from the park, known for its historic sites and museums.

Conclusion

Basaseachic Falls National Park is undoubtedly a majestic destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. This comprehensive guide will surely help plan your trip to this destination, and help you experience all the wonders and adventure it has to offer.
