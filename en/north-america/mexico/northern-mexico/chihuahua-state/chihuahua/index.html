Introduction to Chihuahua

Chihuahua is a state located in Northern Mexico and is known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and colonial architecture. The state boasts dozens of indigenous groups, each with their own unique customs and traditions. The city of Chihuahua, the capital of the state, is home to several historic locations, museums, and monuments that highlight the state’s history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Chihuahua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Chihuahua Museum: This museum is housed in an 18th-century colonial mansion and features exhibits on the state’s history, art, and culture.
  • Cathedral of Chihuahua: Built in the 18th century, this cathedral features a mix of Baroque and Churrigueresque styles.
  • Quinta Gameros: A beautiful mansion in the Art Nouveau style, now converted into a cultural center.
  • Pancho Villa Museum: A museum dedicated to the famous Mexican Revolutionary general and his role in the revolution.
  • Museo de Arte Contemporáneo de Chihuahua (MACCH): This Museum features contemporary art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Chihuahua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Copper Canyon: Known as “Barranca del Cobre” in Spanish, this is a must-visit natural wonder in Chihuahua. It is a complex of six canyons that are deeper and larger than the Grand Canyon.
  • Grutas de Nombre de Dios: These are limestone caves that feature impressive natural rock formations and underground streams.
  • Basaseachi National Park: This is a beautiful national park that boasts the stunning Basaseachi Waterfall, the second-highest waterfall in Mexico.
  • Cumbres de Majalca National Park: A stunning natural park with hiking trails that offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Creel: A charming, picturesque town located in the Sierra Madre mountain range.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chihuahua

Chihuahua has several amazing hiking and biking routes with stunning views of the landscape. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Copper Canyon Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike that spans 37 miles through the Copper Canyon complex.
  • Divisadero Barrancas Circuit: A moderate hiking trail in the Copper Canyon that offers panoramic views of the canyons.
  • Cascada de Basaseachi: A 7.5-mile hiking route that leads to the stunning Basaseachi Waterfall.
  • Ajusco Trail: A hiking trail located within Cumbres de Majalca National Park that offers panoramic views of Chihuahua city.
  • Biking Along the Rio Grande: A scenic bike route that follows the Rio Grande river and offers breathtaking views of the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Chihuahua: Activities and Tours

  • Train ride through Copper Canyon: This is one the best ways to see Copper Canyon. The train route goes through several amazing viewpoints, including El Divisadero, and can be taken for a day trip or longer.
  • Pueblos Mágicos: Chihuahua has several “Magical Towns” that are nearby scenic towns renowned for their charm, history, and culture. Some popular towns include Batopilas, Creel, and Urique.
  • Horseback riding in the countryside: One of the best ways to explore Chihuahua’s countryside is by horseback. Many ranches and tour operators offer horseback riding tours.
  • Hot Springs: Chihuahua has several hot springs that are known for their therapeutic qualities; the most famous ones are in Ojo Caliente, in the town of Cuauhtémoc.
  • Stargazing: The clear skies in Chihuahua make it a perfect destination for stargazing. Many tour operators offer stargazing tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chihuahua

If you are planning to spend a week in Chihuahua, here’s an itinerary that can help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore Chihuahua City’s historic center, including the Casa Chihuahua Museum, Cathedral of Chihuahua, and the Pancho Villa Museum.
  • Day 2: Take the train to Copper Canyon and explore it for the day.
  • Day 3: Explore the city of Creel and its surroundings, including Valley of the Monks, Lake Arareko, and Cusarare Falls.
  • Day 4: Horseback riding in the countryside and hot spring relaxation.
  • Day 5: Visit the Basaseachi National Park and hike to see the Basaseachi Waterfall.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to the historic town of Batopilas and explore its silver mines and colonial architecture.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy Chihuahua’s local cuisine, such as machaca con huevos, and explore local artisan markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Chihuahua

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machaca con huevos: Dried shredded beef mixed with scrambled eggs.
  • Menudo: Traditional Mexican soup made with cow stomach and tripe.
  • Gorditas: Cornmeal cakes filled with various toppings, including refried beans, cheese, and meat.
  • Burritos: Flour tortillas filled with various ingredients such as meats, beans, cheese, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Sotol: A spirit made from the desert spoon plant that is unique to Chihuahua and other northern states of Mexico.
  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the agave plant.
  • Bacanora: Another Mexican spirit made from the agave plant.

Where to Stay in Chihuahua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quinta Gameros Hotel: A stylish hotel located in an Art Nouveau mansion in the center of Chihuahua city.
  • Casa Grande Hoteles: A chain of hotels located in several cities in Chihuahua, offering luxury accommodations.
  • Hacienda de la Barranca: A ranch-style bed and breakfast located within the Copper Canyon.
  • Hotel Plaza Chihuahua: A modern hotel located in the heart of Chihuahua city.
  • Hostal San Francisco: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms.

What to Buy in Chihuahua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sotol: An alcoholic spirit that is unique to Chihuahua.
  • Papier-mâché figurines: Handcrafted figurines made from paper and painted by local artists.
  • Leather goods: Chihuahua is known for its leather products such as belts, boots, and bags.
  • Pottery: Chihuahua has a rich indigenous pottery tradition, and you can find beautiful pottery items in local markets.
  • Peanut candies: Chihuahua is known for its delicious peanut candies, which come in various flavors and textures.

Local Culture and Customs of Chihuahua

Chihuahua has a rich and diverse cultural tradition, with several indigenous groups, including the Tarahumara, who live in the mountains. The state has a vibrant artistic community, and you can see exhibits of various art forms, including music, painting, and sculpture. The state is also famous for its wild west history and cowboy culture. It is recommended to dress modestly, especially when visiting traditional indigenous communities.

Best Time to Visit Chihuahua

The best time to visit Chihuahua is from September to November, and from March to May, due to the mild weather and low humidity levels. If you are visiting the Copper Canyon, spring and fall are the best seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chihuahua

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks, especially sotol and mezcal.
  • Learn about the indigenous culture of Chihuahua, including the Tarahumara people.
  • Visit the historic sites and museums in the city of Chihuahua.
  • Take a train ride through Copper Canyon.
  • Shop for souvenirs and local products at the markets.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in public places.
  • Disrespect the indigenous communities’ customs and traditions.
  • Visit unsafe neighborhoods or areas after dark.
  • Overindulge in the local alcoholic beverages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chihuahua

Overall, Chihuahua is a safe destination for travelers. However, like any other destination, it is recommended to exercise common sense and take normal safety precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables around and avoiding unsafe neighborhoods or areas after dark.

Health in Chihuahua

Chihuahua does not have any specific health hazards; however, it is recommended to take standard malaria and diarrhea precautions, especially in rural areas. It is also recommended to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Getting to Chihuahua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chihuahua

  • General Roberto Fierro Villalobos International Airport (CUU)

Bus Stations Near Chihuahua

  • Central de Autobuses de Chihuahua
  • Estrella Blanca Chihuahua

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi
  • Train: Chihuahua-Pacific Railway

How to Get Around in Chihuahua

The best way to get around Chihuahua is by car; you can rent a car at the international airport and several car rental agencies. Taxis and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Chihuahua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Paquime Archaeological Zone

A site of a former pre-Columbian city, this UNESCO world heritage site showcases the culture and history of the Paquime culture.

Durango

A neighboring state with attractions such as waterfalls and hot springs, Durango has a rich history and culture.

Juarez

A city located across the US-Mexico border that is known for its vibrant nightlife and shopping.

Conclusion

Chihuahua is an amazing destination for those who love nature, culture, history, and adventure. From the stunning Copper Canyon to the local cuisine and indigenous communities, Chihuahua has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have everything you need to plan the perfect trip to Chihuahua, Mexico.
