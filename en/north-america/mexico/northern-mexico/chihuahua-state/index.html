Introduction to Chihuahua (state)

Chihuahua is a state located in Northern Mexico, bordering the United States to the north and the states of Sonora and Durango to the west and south, respectively. It is the largest state in the country, known for its rugged landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. The state capital, also called Chihuahua, is a bustling city with a mix of modern and colonial architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Chihuahua (state)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Chihuahua Museum in the city of Chihuahua showcases the history and art of the state with exhibits on local culture and traditions.
  • Paquime Archaeological Zone features ancient ruins of a pre-Hispanic settlement of the Casas Grandes culture. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • El Palacio de Gobierno in the city of Chihuahua is home to a collection of murals painted by famous Mexican artist Diego Rivera.

Natural Attractions of Chihuahua (state): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Copper Canyon is a stunning natural wonder with towering cliffs and a series of interconnected canyons. Visitors can take a scenic train ride through the canyon or hike along its trails.
  • Basaseachi Waterfall is a majestic waterfall that drops over 800 feet into a scenic canyon. It is the second-highest waterfall in Mexico.
  • Lake Arareko is a tranquil lake surrounded by pine trees and mountains. It is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chihuahua (state)

  • Copper Canyon Trail is a 37-mile trek through Copper Canyon, with stunning views of the canyons and the Tarahumara indigenous communities.
  • Sumidero Canyon Trail is a challenging 18-mile hike through a series of canyons and ridges, with panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness.
  • Sierra Madre Trail is a 60-mile bike trail that winds through the mountain range, with scenic stopovers at picturesque towns along the way.

Best Things to Do in Chihuahua (state): Activities and Tours

  • Train ride through Copper Canyon is a must-do activity, offering breathtaking views of the canyons and a glimpse into the traditional way of life of the Tarahumara indigenous communities.
  • Guided tour of Paquime Archaeological Zone gives visitors insights into the history and culture of the Casas Grandes people who once inhabited the region for centuries.
  • Food and drink tasting tour is a great way to try the local culinary specialties and sample some of the region’s traditional beverages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chihuahua (state)

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Chihuahua City and visit Casa Chihuahua Museum.
2 Take a train ride through Copper Canyon and visit Tarahumara indigenous communities.

Food and Drink Scene of Chihuahua (state)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asado de Boda is a hearty wedding stew made with beef and a variety of herbs and spices.
  • Cabrito Asado is roasted goat meat, a popular dish in the state’s northern regions.
  • Chile Relleno is a stuffed poblano pepper, often filled with cheese, beef, or other meats.

Local Beverages

  • Sotol is a local spirit made from the desert spoon plant and is often compared to tequila or mezcal.
  • Garnacha is a refreshing fruit-flavored punch, perfect for hot weather.
  • Atol is a hot corn beverage that can be enjoyed with cinnamon or vanilla.

Where to Stay in Chihuahua (state): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Mirador in Creel offers cozy rooms with mountain views and a restaurant serving traditional Mexican cuisine.
  • Casa de las Bugambilias in Chihuahua City is a spacious guesthouse with a lush garden and a central location near many local attractions.
  • Hostel Amigos de Parral in the city of Parral provides affordable dorm-style rooms and a communal kitchen for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Chihuahua (state): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts such as pottery, basketry, and textiles are popular souvenirs that reflect the state’s traditional art and crafts.
  • Silver Jewelry is a specialty of the northern regions, with intricate designs that showcase the state’s history and culture.
  • Sotol is a unique local spirit that makes a great gift for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Chihuahua (state)

Chihuahua has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, with influences from the indigenous population, Spanish colonization, and northern regions of Mexico. The state is known for its traditional art and crafts, including pottery, basketry, and weaving. The Tarahumara indigenous communities have a rich culture, known for their long-distance running and traditional music and dance.

Best Time to Visit Chihuahua (state)

The best time to visit Chihuahua is in the spring and fall months, from March to June and September to November. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The summer months can be very hot, while the winter months can be cold and snowy, particularly in the higher elevations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chihuahua (state)

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Spanish to make communication easier.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and cultural customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages to get a taste of the state’s culinary specialties.

Don’t:

  • Engage in disrespectful behavior towards the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry large amounts of cash or expensive belongings in crowded areas.
  • Travel alone at night in unknown areas.

Safety and Health

Chihuahua is generally safe for travelers, but like any other destination, visitors should exercise caution and take necessary safety measures while traveling. It is recommended to keep valuables hidden and avoid flashing them in public places. Visitors should also stay aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions while hiking or visiting natural attractions.

In terms of health, visitors should drink only bottled or boiled water and be up to date on their routine vaccinations. It is always advisable to consult a doctor before traveling for any recommended immunizations or health precautions.

Getting to Chihuahua (state): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chihuahua (state)

The main airport in Chihuahua is General Roberto Fierro Villalobos International Airport (CUU). It offers direct flights to several major cities in Mexico, as well as to Dallas and Houston in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Chihuahua (state)

Chihuahua is well-connected by bus to other major cities in Mexico such as Monterrey, Mexico City, and Guadalajara. The city’s main bus station, Central de Autobuses Chihuahua, offers a range of routes and services to other destinations in the state and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a scenic train ride through Copper Canyon, with services provided by the Chihuahua-Pacific Railway.

How to Get Around in Chihuahua (state)

The best way to get around Chihuahua is by car or public transportation, such as buses or taxis. Car rentals are available at the airport and in major cities, but it is advisable to check with rental agencies about driving conditions in the state. Buses are a more affordable option for those who prefer not to drive, with frequent services to most destinations across the state.

Where to Go Next from Chihuahua (state): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Durango is a neighboring state with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, including the Sierra Madre Occidental mountain range.
  • Coahuila is another neighboring state known for its colonial architecture and captivating desert landscapes.
  • El Paso is a major city in Texas, just across the border, and is a popular destination for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Chihuahua is a fascinating destination that offers a mix of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and traditional cuisine. With its rich history and vibrant culture, visitors can experience something truly unique in Northern Mexico. From the stunning Copper Canyon to the bustling city of Chihuahua, there is something for everyone in this diverse and captivating state.
