Introduction to Western suburbs

Western suburbs is a charming neighborhood in Monterrey, Nuevo León, known for its bustling streets, scenic views and welcoming people. Although it’s not as large as other Monterrey boroughs, Western suburbs boasts of a rich cultural heritage and various outdoor adventures for locals and travelers alike. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll know everything you need to explore and enjoy all of what Western suburbs have to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Western suburbs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Western suburbs is known for its culturally rich museums and historical places that depict its history. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Museo del Ferrocarril: This Railroad Museum is an excellent place for railroad enthusiasts that has a collection of railway engines, tools, and other equipment that date back to the early days of the railway industry in Mexico.

  • Museo de Arte Contemporáneo: This Modern Art Museum is a beautiful place that features temporary exhibitions and contemporary art that are sure to appeal to art lovers.

  • Paseo Santa Lucía: This man-made canal is a picturesque walkway, with breathtaking views of the city skyline, bridges, and statues. It is a perfect place for a romantic walk or a relaxing afternoon stroll.

Natural Attractions of Western suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Western suburbs is gifted with many natural attractions that offer adventure-seekers breathtaking views and challenging hikes. Here are some of the must-see natural attractions:

  • Cerro de la Silla: Located in the Sierra Madre Oriental mountain range, Cerro de la Silla is one of the most iconic symbols of Monterrey, which offers an exhilarating hiking experience.

  • La Estanzuela Natural Park: This is an eco-park that offers a great experience, with its numerous hiking trails of different difficulty levels, scenic views, and natural waterfalls.

  • Chipinque National Park: This scenic park offers an impressive panoramic view of the city and a perfect escape from the bustling city life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Western suburbs

Western suburbs offers some fantastic trails for hikers and bikers who are eager for some adventure. Some of the best trails not to miss are:

  • La Huasteca: This is a popular hiking destination that offers different trails ranging from beginner to the advanced level that overlooks the stunning view of Northern Mexico.

  • Parque Rufino Tamayo: This park has a beautiful bike path that runs through it, providing bikers a different view of Monterrey city skyline.

  • Parque Natural La Huasteca: This park is perfect for trail runners, hikers, and bikers, as it offers a diverse range of terrains and tunnels to explore.

Best Things to Do in Western suburbs: Activities and Tours

Western suburbs is full of interesting things to do and unique things to see. Here are some activities and tours that should not be missed:

  • Local Food Tour: This tour offers the chance to take a food journey and savor the local specialties of Monterrey’s food scene.

  • Art Walk Tour: This is an excellent way to discover Monterrey’s contemporary art scene with its many galleries, street art murals, and public sculpture gardens.

  • Off-road Adventure Tour: Western suburbs provide the perfect setting to explore Monterrey from a different perspective, with an off-road adventure tour that combines impressive views and adrenaline-pumping activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Western suburbs

Day 1: Explore the Paseo Santa Lucía and see the city from a different perspective. Day 2: Visit the Museo del Ferrocarril and learn about the rail history of Monterrey. Day 3: Hike to the top of Cerro de la Silla to catch the stunning sunset view over the city. Day 4: Discover the local gastronomic scene with a food tour. Day 5: Visit Chipinque National Park and take in the breathtaking views of Monterrey. Day 6: Take a bike ride through Parque Rufino Tamayo and explore all it has to offer. Day 7: Experience the contemporary art scene in the city with an Art Walk tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Western suburbs

Must-Try Dishes

Western suburbs has a diverse food scene, with something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Cabrito: This is a signature dish of Monterrey, which is slow-roasted baby goat that’s tender and savory.

  • Enchiladas Norteñas: This dish includes fried tortillas smothered in a thick, spicy tomato sauce, and filled with beans, potatoes, and your choice of meat.

  • Machacado con Huevo: This delicious breakfast dish consists of shredded dried beef, scrambled eggs, and fried tortilla chips.

Local Beverages

Western suburbs offers a wide variety of drink options, ranging from local beers to unique cocktails. Here are some must-try local beverages:

  • Michelada: This is a refreshing mixture of beer, lime, salt, and various spices that is perfect on a hot day.

  • Tequila: A national drink of Mexico, tequila is a must-try during your visit to Western suburbs.

  • Pulque: A traditional Mexican drink made from fermented agave sap, and it is a must-try drink that dates back to ancient times.

Where to Stay in Western suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Western suburbs offers an excellent range of accommodation options to cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Crowne Plaza Hotel: This is a luxurious hotel that offers rich and modern amenities and is located in the heart of the bustling city, making it an ideal location for visitors.

  • Ibis Hotel: This is a mid-range hotel that provides a comfortable and cozy stay, and it’s conveniently located near the central business district.

  • Hostel Santa Lucia: This friendly and affordable hostel offers a homey atmosphere that is perfect for budget-conscious travelers who want to enjoy the city.

What to Buy in Western suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Western suburbs offers an extensive range of products that are unique to Northern Mexico and make perfect souvenirs of your visit. Here are some items to consider:

  • Hand-Painted Ceramic Tiles: These decorative tiles feature bright colors, intricate patterns, and are hand-painted by skilled artisans in the region.

  • Handmade Leather Products: Western suburbs is well-known for its high-quality leather products that include wallets, belts, and bags.

  • La Rosa Candy: This is a unique mexican candy made from sweetened pumpkin that comes in various flavors like pineapple, strawberry, and coconut, making it an ideal treat to take home to friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Western suburbs

Western suburbs is an iconic neighborhood in Monterrey, which has a unique blend of history and modernity. The inhabitants of Western suburbs are welcoming, amicable people who are proud of their cultural heritage. The locals are open to visitors, and it is essential to remember to be respectful of the cultural values of the community and its traditions.

Best Time to Visit Western suburbs

The Western suburbs can be visited all year round, and the weather is generally pleasant, with mildly cold winters and warm summers. The best time to visit Western suburbs is between March and May, and between September and November, when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Western suburbs

Do:

  • Respect and embrace the local customs and traditions.
  • Make an effort to learn some basic Spanish phrases to ease communication with the locals.
  • Experience the local cuisine and specialties of the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t walk alone in the dark or poorly-lit areas.
  • Don’t litter, as the Western suburbs are committed to keeping the streets clean and garbage-free.

Safety and Health

Safety in Western suburbs

Western suburbs is generally a safe neighborhood, but it’s advisable to take precautions like anywhere else. The following safety tips should be put into practice:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in public places.
  • Do not leave valuables in visible places in your car.

Health in Western suburbs

Ensure to take care of your health during your visit to Western suburbs by:

  • Sanitize your hands regularly, especially before eating.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect your skin from the sun during outdoor activities.

Getting to Western suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Western suburbs

The General Mariano Escobedo International Airport in Monterrey City is the nearest airport to the Western suburbs. It is located about 22 km from the Western suburbs, and it’s well connected to the city via taxis, buses.

Bus Stations Near Western suburbs

The Central de Autobuses de Monterrey bus station is approximately 15 km from Western suburbs, and it connects Monterrey with major Mexican cities like Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Tijuana. From the bus station, taxis and public transportation are available to reach Western suburbs.

Other Options

Airport shuttles and private transfers are also available at an additional cost to cater to your specific requirements.

How to Get Around in Western suburbs

Western suburbs is easily accessible via taxis, public transport, or self-driven car. Public transport is a cheap and reliable way to navigate the area, and if you are not fluent in Spanish, the app “Easy Taxi” is helpful to use to book taxis.

Where to Go Next from Western suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Western suburbs is strategically located, and it’s only a few minutes drive from nearby destinations that are worth exploring. Here are a few next places to visit:

  • Cumbres de Monterrey National Park: Go for a hike and take in the scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Santiago: A quaint town about thirty minutes from Western suburbs that boasts of a beautiful and historic town center, and great shopping and dining opportunities.

Conclusion

The Western suburbs of Monterrey, Mexico has something for everyone, be it history buffs, adventure seekers, or foodies. With this travel guide, you will have an unforgettable experience as you explore its culturally-significant attractions and breathtaking natural wonders, its delicious gastronomic delights, and friendly people.
