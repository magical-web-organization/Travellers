Introduction to Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Located in the state of Durango in Northern Mexico, Santa María del Oro is a small town with stunning natural attractions that offer visitors hiking, camping, and other outdoor adventures. The town’s name is derived from the nearby Lake Santa María which has sparkling, crystal clear waters. Additionally, there are several historical and cultural attractions that visitors can explore making Santa María del Oro a perfect destination for nature and culture enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Santa María del Oro has several culturally significant attractions that showcase the history and culture of the town. The most notable attractions are:

  • Santa María de la Asunción Parish: Dating back to the 17th Century, this historical church exhibits the Baroque style and is the oldest building in Santa María del Oro.

  • Museum of Ancient Mining: The museum showcases a collection of ancient mining tools, equipment, and minerals reflecting the town’s mining history.

Natural Attractions of Santa María del Oro (Durango): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Santa María del Oro is home to several beautiful natural attractions that offer visitors stunning scenic views and opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, swimming, and camping. The most popular attractions are:

  • Lake Santa María: This natural freshwater lake is the town’s most significant attraction, and visitors can spend hours swimming, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Cerro de la Cruz: A small hill located on the edge of town, offering a stunning view of the town and surroundings. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill to enjoy this panoramic view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Santa María del Oro is a perfect destination for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts. Visitors have options of several beautiful trails in the town and its surroundings. The most popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Cerro de la Cruz Trail: A moderate hiking trail leading to the top of the hill offering a panoramic view of the town and surroundings.
  • The Santa María del Oro Lake Trail: An easy, scenic trail that surrounds the Lake Santa María with beautiful views of the crystal-clear waters.

Best Things to Do in Santa María del Oro (Durango): Activities and Tours

Santa María del Oro has plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy during their stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Santa María del Oro:

  • Camping: Visitors can camp near Santa María del Oro Lake where they can experience the beautiful natural scenery and enjoy outdoor adventures such as hiking, swimming, and fishing.
  • Swimming: Visitors can enjoy swimming in the clear waters of Lake Santa María, where there are several areas designated for swimming.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Visitors can rent kayaks and paddleboards to explore Lake Santa María’s crystal-clear waters.
  • Historical and Cultural Tours: Visitors can explore the historical and cultural attractions in Santa María del Oro organized through town tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Day 1: Visit Santa María de la Asunción Parish and Museum of Ancient Mining. Day 2: Hiking/biking on the Cerro de la Cruz Trail. Day 3: Explore Lake Santa María and enjoy swimming, kayaking, or paddleboarding. Day 4: Camping trip near the Santa María del Oro Lake. Day 5: Visit the nearby beautiful mountain town, Mapimi. Day 6: Historic town tour of Santa María del Oro. Day 7: Relaxation day with a visit to a nearby day spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Must-Try Dishes

Santa María del Oro offers visitors an array of traditional Mexican cuisine. The must-try dishes include:

  • Asado de boda: A colorful wedding stew that is cooked over an open fire and served in a large cazuela.
  • Chilorio: A traditional dish made with pork, which is cooked with a blend of spices and chillies and served with warm tortillas.

Local Beverages

Santa María del Oro has some go-to drinks that visitors should try. These include:

  • Pozole: A traditional and refreshing drink made with maize, cinnamon, and fruit that has a unique sweet and sour taste.
  • Mescal: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from the agave plant. Mezcal tasting is a must-do activity here.

Where to Stay in Santa María del Oro (Durango): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa María del Oro offers visitors several lodging options for their stay. The most popular lodging options include:

  • Hotel Santa María: A budget-friendly hotel that offers visitors clean rooms and modern amenities.
  • Cabañas El Paraiso: If you want to stay near the Lake Santa María, this rustic cabin-like accommodation is perfect for you.
  • Casa de Piedra: A guesthouse made entirely of stone and offers visitors a cozy and comfortable lodging experience.

What to Buy in Santa María del Oro (Durango): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Santa María del Oro offers visitors several locally made products that they can take home as souvenirs. The most popular local products include:

  • Handmade Jewelry: Visitors can buy handmade jewelry made with natural stones found in the local hills.
  • Glassware: Visitors can purchase intricately-designed glassware created by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa María del Oro (Durango)

The people of Santa María del Oro practice a combination of traditional and modern customs and traditions. They are warm and hospitable people who welcome visitors with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Santa María del Oro has a mild climate throughout the year and can be visited anytime. The best time to visit is during the months of August to September when the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy summer activities such as swimming, kayaking, and camping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Do:

  • Explore the Lake Santa María and enjoy the scenic beauty of the crystal-clear waters.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages such as Mezcal and Pozole.
  • Take part in outdoor adventures such as hiking and camping.

Don’t:

  • Never litter while you are at a natural attraction.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and sufficient water while exploring outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Santa María del Oro is a safe destination for visitors, but they should take the necessary precautions while exploring outdoor adventures. Visitors should also be careful while checking in the hotel because there have been reports of theft.

Health in Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Santa María del Oro does not offer high-quality medical facilities, and visitors should carry essential medication with them. They should also be careful while trying street food during their visit.

Getting to Santa María del Oro (Durango): Transportation Options and Logistics

Santa María del Oro has several transportation options available for visitors to reach the town and move around.

Airports Near Santa María del Oro (Durango)

The nearest airports to Santa María del Oro are:

  • El Paso International Airport: Four-and-a-half hours away from Santa Maria del Oro.
  • General Guadalupe Victoria International Airport: One hour away from Santa Maria del Oro via road.

Bus Stations Near Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Visitors can take the bus from several major cities to reach Santa María del Oro. The nearest bus station is:

  • Durango Bus Station, Durango: Two-and-a-half hours away from Santa Maria del Oro.

Other Options

Visitors can rent cars in nearby cities or opt for taxi services.

How to Get Around in Santa María del Oro (Durango)

Santa María del Oro is a small town, and visitors can easily navigate the town on foot. Alternatively, they can rent a car or take a taxi service to reach nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Santa María del Oro (Durango): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby attractions from Santa María del Oro. Some of the recommended destinations include:

  • Mapimi: A beautiful mountain town that offers visitors stunning views and outdoor activities such as hiking and camping.
  • Cuatro Ciénegas: This stunning natural reserve is five hours away and offers visitors scenic views, hiking trails, and unique wildlife.

Conclusion

Santa María del Oro is a town with stunning natural attractions, historical and cultural attractions, and several enjoyable activities and tours. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their visit to Santa María del Oro and explore the town’s unique beauty and charm.
