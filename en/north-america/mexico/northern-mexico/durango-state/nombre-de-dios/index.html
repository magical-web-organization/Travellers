Introduction to Nombre de Dios

Located in the northern state of Durango, Mexico, Nombre de Dios is a charming and lesser-known destination packed with natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure, making it a perfect weekend or week-long getaway. The town’s namesake comes from the Spanish “Name of God,” a reference to the region’s conquistador past. Whether you’re into hiking, photography, sampling local cuisine, or exploring museums, there’s something for everyone in Nombre de Dios.

List of Places to Visit in Nombre de Dios

Here is our list of the top must-see destinations in Nombre de Dios:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • (1) Museo de la Cultura Tarahumara: This museum showcases the indigenous Tarahumara culture, traditions, and way of life through a collection of artifacts, textiles, and exhibits.

  • (2) Iglesia de Nombre de Dios: The town’s most iconic landmark, this church dates back to the 17th century and features impressive baroque architecture and a magnificent bell tower.

  • (3) Casa de Cultura: A cultural center that hosts exhibitions, workshops, and events on local art, music, and history.

Natural Attractions of Nombre de Dios: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • (1) Cascada de Nombre de Dios: A breathtaking waterfall surrounded by lush vegetation and hiking trails.

  • (2) Parque Ecológico los Cactus: A scenic nature reserve dedicated to preserving the local flora and fauna, featuring species such as cacti, agaves, and lizards.

  • (3) Mirador de las Antenas: A panoramic viewpoint overlooking the town and surrounding mountains, perfect for photography and sunset watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nombre de Dios

For outdoor enthusiasts, Nombre de Dios offers an array of hiking and biking trails that let you immerse yourself in the region’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • (1) Circuito de Cañas: A 10-mile loop that takes you through the hills and valleys around Nombre de Dios, passing by waterfalls and rock formations.

  • (2) Cerro de la Cruz: A challenging hike that rewards you with stunning views of the town and surrounding mountains from its summit.

  • (3) Cañón de Fernández: A scenic canyon with a winding river and lush vegetation, ideal for hiking, swimming, and bird-watching.

Best Things to Do in Nombre de Dios: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and sightseeing, Nombre de Dios offers plenty of other activities and tours that allow you to experience the town’s culture and lifestyle firsthand. Here are a few ideas:

  • (1) Visit a mezcal distillery: Durango is known for its mezcal, so why not take a tour of a local distillery and learn about the process of making this traditional spirit?

  • (2) Take a horseback ride: Hire a local guide and ride through the countryside, enjoying the scenic views and fresh air.

  • (3) Attend a local festival: Nombre de Dios hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Fiestas de Mayo, a celebration of the town’s patron saint.

Food and Drink Scene of Nombre de Dios

Mexico is famous for its delicious cuisine, and Nombre de Dios is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Chile en nogada: A seasonal dish made with poblano peppers stuffed with meat and fruit, topped with a walnut-based cream sauce and pomegranate seeds.

  • (2) Tamales: Corn-based dough wrapped in a corn husk and filled with savory or sweet ingredients such as chicken, pork, beans, or fruit.

  • (3) Carnitas: Slow-cooked pork seasoned with herbs and spices, often served with rice, beans, and tortillas.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Mezcal: A distilled spirit made from the agave plant, similar to tequila but with a smokier flavor.

  • (2) Tejuino: A refreshing fermented drink made from corn, sugar, and lime, served cold with salt and chili powder.

  • (3) Agua fresca: A fruity and sweet drink made from fresh fruits such as watermelon, pineapple, or mango, mixed with water and sugar.

Where to Stay in Nombre de Dios: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nombre de Dios offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some top-rated places to stay include:

  • (1) Hotel Colonial: A charming colonial-style hotel in the heart of the town, featuring cozy rooms, a courtyard, and a restaurant.

  • (2) Casa de la Abuela: A family-run guesthouse with a rustic decor, comfortable rooms, and a friendly atmosphere.

  • (3) Hostal del Refugio: A hostel in a restored 18th-century building, with a rooftop terrace, a communal kitchen, and bunk beds.

What to Buy in Nombre de Dios: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Nombre de Dios, here are some unique souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • (1) Handwoven textiles: Look for colorful blankets, ponchos, and rugs made by local artisans using traditional techniques.

  • (2) Mezcal: Bring back a bottle of this famous spirit, which comes in various flavors and styles.

  • (3) Silver jewelry: Durango is known for its silver mines, and local jewelers craft unique and intricate pieces such as earrings, necklaces, and bracelets.

Local Culture and Customs of Nombre de Dios

Durango is a state rich in culture and traditions, and Nombre de Dios is no exception. Here are some customs and practices to bear in mind:

  • (1) Respect for the elderly: In Mexican culture, elder people are held in high regard and often receive special treatment and honor.

  • (2) Family values: Mexican families are usually close-knit and prioritize their relationships with immediate and extended family members.

  • (3) Religion: Catholicism is the dominant religion in Mexico, and many towns and villages have traditions and rituals associated with their patron saints.

Best Time to Visit Nombre de Dios

The best time to visit Nombre de Dios is in the dry season, from November to April, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. In the summer months, temperatures can reach over 100°F and humidity can be high, which can make hiking and exploring challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nombre de Dios

To enjoy your visit to Nombre de Dios fully, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • (1) Learn some basic Spanish phrases: While many locals speak English, it can be helpful and respectful to know some Spanish to communicate better.

  • (2) Try the local food: Mexican cuisine is world-renowned, and Nombre de Dios is no exception. Be adventurous and sample some of the traditional dishes mentioned earlier.

  • (3) Be respectful of local customs: As in any unfamiliar place, it’s essential to be mindful of local beliefs, practices, and sensitivities.

Don’t:

  • (1) Assume everyone speaks English: While many Mexicans do speak English, don’t take it for granted and be patient and understanding if there are language barriers.

  • (2) Overpack: Bring comfortable, versatile clothing and footwear, and be mindful of the weight and size of your luggage.

  • (3) Be reckless or disrespectful: Always keep your safety and that of others in mind and be respectful of public spaces, cultural heritage, and the environment.

Safety and Health

Like most destinations in Mexico, Nombre de Dios is generally safe for visitors, but it’s always advisable to exercise caution and common sense. Here are some tips on safety and health:

Safety in Nombre de Dios

  • (1) As in any town or city, be mindful of pickpockets and theft, especially in crowded areas or if you carry valuable items.

  • (2) Avoid walking alone at night, and stick to well-lit and busy streets.

  • (3) Follow any safety protocols or recommendations provided by your accommodation or tour operators.

Health in Nombre de Dios

  • (1) Drink bottled or purified water to avoid any risk of water-borne illnesses.

  • (2) Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun, which can be strong and intense in Mexico.

  • (3) If you have any pre-existing medical conditions or concerns, consult your doctor before traveling to Mexico.

Getting to Nombre de Dios: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nombre de Dios is located about 130 miles from Durango City, the state capital, and can be reached by various means of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Nombre de Dios

  • (1) Durango International Airport: Located about 45 miles from Nombre de Dios, this airport serves several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Nombre de Dios

  • (1) Terminal de Autobuses de Durango (TAD): This is the primary bus station in Durango City, and several bus companies offer daily services to Nombre de Dios.

  • (2) Terminal de Autobuses Estrella Blanca: Another option in Durango City, with frequent departures and arrivals to Nombre de Dios.

Other Options

  • (1) Car rental: If you prefer to drive yourself, renting a car is a good option, as there are major highways connecting Durango City and Nombre de Dios.

  • (2) Private transfer: Many hotels and tour companies offer private transfers from Durango City to Nombre de Dios.

How to Get Around in Nombre de Dios

Nombre de Dios is a small town that can be explored on foot or by taxi. There are also local buses that connect the town with nearby villages and attractions. If you plan to hike or bike, you can either rent equipment or bring your own.

Where to Go Next from Nombre de Dios: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • (1) Durango City: The state capital is only a few hours’ drive from Nombre de Dios and offers a wealth of cultural, historical, and gastronomic attractions.

  • (2) Copper Canyon: One of Mexico’s most stunning natural wonders, this series of canyons and gorges is located about 170 miles from Nombre de Dios and can be reached by train or car.

  • (3) Parras de la Fuente: A charming colonial town famous for its vineyards, wineries, and colonial architecture, located about 110 miles from Nombre de Dios.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers natural beauty, culture, and adventure, Nombre de Dios should definitely be on your list. This comprehensive tour guide has given you a taste of what this town has to offer, but there’s nothing like experiencing it firsthand. So pack your bags, bring your sense of adventure, and explore Nombre de Dios, Durango, Mexico.
