Introduction to El Salto

Located in Northern Mexico, El Salto is renowned for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. The town is situated in the state of Durango and attracts nature enthusiasts, hikers, and history buffs who are looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience. With its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, El Salto has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in El Salto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

El Salto has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its many museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in El Salto include:

  • Museo Regional de la Laguna: This museum showcases the history and culture of the region, including the indigenous peoples who lived in the area before the arrival of the Spanish.

  • Hacienda la Ferrería: This 19th-century hacienda offers a glimpse into the life of wealthy landowners during the colonial era. Visitors can take a tour of the estate and explore its beautifully preserved architecture and gardens.

  • Casa del Centenario: Built in 1908, this historic building is one of the most iconic structures in El Salto. Today, it serves as a cultural center and hosts a variety of events and exhibitions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

El Salto is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for outdoor adventures and sightseeing. Some of the top natural attractions to explore in El Salto are:

  • Parque Nacional Dinosaurs: This national park is home to a variety of dinosaur fossils and offers visitors a chance to see these magnificent creatures up close.

  • El Saltito: This beautiful waterfall is situated in a canyon just outside of El Salto and offers a refreshing respite from the heat of the day. Visitors can swim in the clear waters, hike around the area, or simply relax and take in the stunning scenery.

  • Cebollas Dam: Located on the outskirts of El Salto, this dam is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and other water sports. The surrounding landscape is also beautiful, with rolling hills and verdant forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Salto

El Salto is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Northern Mexico. Whether you’re an experienced outdoorsman or just looking for a leisurely stroll, there is something for everyone in El Salto. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Parque Nacional Dinosaurs: This national park features several hiking trails that wind through the rugged terrain and offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • El Cristo Rey: This trail is a favorite among hikers and bikers alike. It leads to a large statue of Christ that overlooks the town and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the landscape.

  • Laguna de Santiaguillo: This beautiful lake is surrounded by hiking trails that pass through dense forests and rolling hills. Visitors can swim, fish, or simply enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in El Salto: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to see and do in El Salto, whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures. Some of the top activities and tours to consider include:

  • Paragliding: El Salto has become a popular destination for paragliding enthusiasts. Visitors can take a guided tour of the area and experience the thrill of soaring through the skies.

  • Horseback Riding: For a more leisurely pace, horseback riding tours are available through many of the local ranches and farms. This is a great way to see the countryside and get a taste of the local culture.

  • Wine Tours: Durango is home to several excellent wineries, and El Salto is no exception. Visitors can take a guided tour of the local vineyards and sample some of the region’s best wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Salto

If you’re planning a trip to El Salto, here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you get the most out of your visit:

Day 1: Arrive in El Salto and get settled in at your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town and checking out some of the local sights and attractions.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Museo Regional de la Laguna and learn about the history and culture of the region. In the afternoon, head to Parque Nacional Dinosaurs for a scenic hike or bike ride.

Day 3: Spend the day at El Saltito, one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the region. Swim, hike, or simply relax and take in the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Visit Hacienda la Ferrería and learn about life on a colonial-era estate. In the afternoon, take a guided wine tour of some of the local vineyards.

Day 5: Explore the beautiful Laguna de Santiaguillo and enjoy hiking or fishing in the surrounding area.

Day 6: Take a paragliding tour of the area and experience the thrill of soaring through the skies. In the evening, enjoy a traditional Mexican dinner at one of the local restaurants.

Day 7: Depart El Salto and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of El Salto

Must-Try Dishes

El Salto is home to some of the most delicious and unique cuisine in Northern Mexico. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include:

  • Queso Asadero: Made from the milk of local cows, this delicious cheese is a staple ingredient in many of the region’s dishes.

  • Cabrito al Pastor: This slow-roasted goat dish is seasoned with a variety of spices and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

  • Chiles Rellenos: These stuffed peppers are filled with meat, cheese, and other flavorful ingredients and are a popular dish in many Mexican restaurants.

Local Beverages

Durango is known for its excellent wines and tequilas, and El Salto is no exception. Some of the top local beverages to try include:

  • Vino de Durango: This delicious wine is made from grapes grown in the surrounding vineyards and has a unique flavor and aroma.

  • Tequila: El Salto is known for its high-quality tequilas, which are made from blue agave plants grown in the region.

Where to Stay in El Salto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, El Salto has something for every type of traveler. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area include:

  • Hotel El Salto: This charming hotel is located in the heart of town and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • Hostal de la Laguna: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers. It offers clean and comfortable dormitory-style accommodations and a friendly and welcoming environment.

  • Casa Bonita: This guesthouse is located just outside of El Salto and offers a quiet and peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of town. Guests can enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding hills and forests.

What to Buy in El Salto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and authentic souvenirs to bring home from your trip to El Salto, some of the top items to consider include:

  • Handmade pottery: El Salto is known for its traditional pottery, which is made by local artisans using techniques that have been passed down for generations.

  • Local wines and liquors: Durango is home to several excellent wineries and distilleries, and El Salto is the perfect place to stock up on your favorite local beverages.

  • Local crafts and artwork: The region is known for its vibrant and colorful folk art, which is sold in many of the local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of El Salto

El Salto has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Some of the most important cultural events and customs in the area include:

  • Dia de los Muertos: This traditional Mexican holiday is celebrated throughout the country, and El Salto is no exception. During this holiday, families gather together to remember and honor their loved ones who have passed away.

  • Semana Santa: El Salto has a rich Catholic heritage, and Semana Santa is one of the most important religious holidays of the year. Visitors can attend processions and other events throughout the week.

Best Time to Visit El Salto

The best time to visit El Salto is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this time, the weather is mild and comfortable, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. However, visitors can enjoy a visit to El Salto year-round, as there are plenty of indoor activities and attractions to explore during the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Salto

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, which is unique and delicious.

  • Do take a guided tour of the area to learn about the history and culture of El Salto.

  • Do be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the local environment.

  • Don’t ignore the safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to the local people or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Salto

El Salto is a safe and welcoming destination for visitors. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take certain safety precautions to ensure a pleasant and trouble-free visit. Some of the top safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Be cautious when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or paragliding.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables secure at all times.

Health in El Salto

El Salto has high-quality medical facilities and experienced healthcare providers. Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations and take precautions to avoid food and water-borne illnesses. It’s also a good idea to bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and other protective measures to ensure a safe and healthy visit.

Getting to El Salto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Salto

The nearest airport to El Salto is the Durango International Airport, located approximately 191 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near El Salto

El Salto is well-connected by bus to the surrounding towns and regions. The nearest bus station is located in the town center.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option for visitors who want to explore the countryside and surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in El Salto

El Salto is a small and walkable town, and many of the local attractions and restaurants are located within easy walking distance of each other. Taxis are also available for visitors who want to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from El Salto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Mexican adventure after visiting El Salto, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Some of the top options include:

  • Durango City: The city of Durango is located approximately 200 kilometers from El Salto and is known for its beautiful architecture, museums, and cultural events.

  • Copper Canyon: This series of canyons is located in the Sierra Madre Occidental mountain range and is one of the most stunning natural wonders in Mexico.

  • Pacific Coast: The beaches and resort towns of the Pacific Coast are located within a few hours’ drive of El Salto.

Conclusion

El Salto is a hidden gem in Northern Mexico, offering visitors an unforgettable experience of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor adventures. From hiking trails and scenic vistas to museums and wineries, there is something for every type of traveler in El Salto. Plan your visit today and discover one of the most captivating destinations in Mexico.
