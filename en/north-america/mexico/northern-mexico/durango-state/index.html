Introduction to Durango (state)

Durango is a state located in northern Mexico, bordering the states of Chihuahua, Coahuila, Zacatecas, and Nayarit. The state is known for its mountainous terrain, rugged landscapes, and charming colonial architecture. Tourists interested in Mexican history and culture must visit this charming state and explore its iconic landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Durango (state)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Durango Cathedral - Located in the heart of the city, this iconic cathedral is a must-visit attraction. The stunning architecture of the cathedral, coupled with its rich history, makes it a perfect place to explore.
  2. Museo de Arte Contemporáneo de Durango - This art gallery displays works by renowned local and international artists. You’ll find many unique contemporary artworks in this museum.
  3. Paseo del Viejo Oeste - The “Old West” theme park is designed to showcase Durango’s history and culture. Here, visitors can explore the Old West ambiance and attend live action shows.

Natural Attractions of Durango (state): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Barranca del Cobre - Known as the “Copper Canyon,” this enormous canyon is larger than the Grand Canyon and has breathtaking views. The canyon provides a natural adventure through mountain climbing, bicycle rides, and zip-lining.
  2. Parque Guadiana - A public park located in the heart of Durango, the Guadiana Park is an ideal place for a picnic or a stroll. It’s full of lush trees and colorful flowers, and you can rent bicycles to explore its peaceful paths.
  3. Cerro de Los Remedios - A hill standing 2,352 meters above sea level, it offers a beautiful panoramic view of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Durango (state)

The mountain terrain of Durango creates a fantastic backdrop to numerous hiking and biking routes, including the Barranca del Cobre and the Paseo del Viejo Oeste. Make sure to bring appropriate gear and provisions with you before venturing out into the wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Durango (state): Activities and Tours

  1. La Feria Nacional de Durango - This annual festival takes place around July, and it features themed park rides, cultural shows, and concerts.
  2. Cinemex Durango - For film buffs, the Cinemex in Durango is a great place to watch movies in Mexico’s oldest cinema hall.
  3. Rancho Ecológico Los Venados - This ranch offers horseback riding, fishing, and even bird watching tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Durango (state)

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cabrito - Grilled goat is a delicacy in Durango and is a must-try dish.
  2. Caldo de Oso - A traditional soup made with bear meat, corn, and herbs, this soup is hearty and delicious.
  3. Enchiladas Duranguenas - This is a quintessential dish of the state made with warm tortillas cooked with a spice blend, cheese, chicken, cream, and beans.

Local Beverages

  1. Sotol - An alcoholic drink made from the Desert Spoon plant.
  2. Pozol - A traditional Mexican drink made of corn flour and cocoa. This drink is ideal for those who want to indulge in a chocolate drink with a traditional twist.

Where to Stay in Durango (state): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Roma - Located in the heart of the city, this hotel is ideal for those who prefer luxury and comfort.
  2. Hostel Edredón - This hostel is perfect if you’re looking for a budget-friendly experience and want to meet fellow travelers.
  3. Hotel Gobernador - If you’re looking for a restored colonial mansion with modern amenities, this hotel is a perfect choice.

What to Buy in Durango (state): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Chiles y Salsas - The spicy dressings are a popular local product that your taste buds will never forget.
  2. Rebozos - Colorful shawls woven by the local people are handy keepsakes.
  3. Papier Mache Masks - The intricately designed masks are an ideal conversation starter and add a unique flair to your home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Durango (state)

Durango culture is highly influenced by its long history, and it reflects in its architecture, traditions, and cuisine. The locals are welcoming and show respect and pride to their heritage.

Best Time to Visit Durango (state)

The best time to visit Durango is in the winter months from December to February when the weather is cold and dry, with occasional snowfalls in the higher elevations. The weather is warm and tropical in other months, with occasional rainfall. Be sure to monitor weather reports throughout the year before your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Durango (state)

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions.
  2. Learn some Spanish and engage in small talk with the locals.
  3. Enjoy the natural beauty and the surroundings.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t become obsessed with tourist hotspots and miss the natural wonders.
  2. Don’t wander around late at night, particularly in off-the-beaten-track areas.
  3. Don’t travel alone, particularly at night, far into wilderness areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Durango (state)

Durango is a relatively safe place for tourists, but as with any other city, cautiousness is always advised. Watch your belongings and lock them up when not in use, be aware of your surroundings, and do not go to risky areas alone, particularly at night.

Health in Durango (state)

Make sure you take all necessary health precautions, including vaccinations, before traveling to Durango. Check with your doctor to clarify what vaccinations are required. Carry insect repellent and use it to protect yourself from mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases like dengue fever.

Getting to Durango (state): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Durango (state)

Francisco Sarabia International Airport is the most convenient airport near Durango. It’s situated on the outskirts of the city and handles both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Durango (state)

If you’re coming from Mexico City, you can take a bus to Durango from the Mexico City Terminal Central de Autobuses. There are several buses, including ETN, Primera Plus, and Omnibus de Mexico to choose from.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great way to explore Durango, but caution is advised when driving around the state.

How to Get Around in Durango (state)

Taxis and public transportation are available in the city. Be sure to ask your hotel staff for taxi recommendations or use a trustworthy taxi booking app. Walking or biking is ideal for exploring the city’s cultural landmarks and the city center. Private car rental is an option if you want to explore the nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Durango (state): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mazatlan - A nearby coastal city with magnificent beaches and an active nightlife.
  2. Chihuahua - A destination that shares a border with Durango, and known for its canyons and UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  3. Copper Canyon - Located in Chihuahua, the Copper Canyon is a natural phenomenon and one of the most spectacular canyons in the world.

Conclusion

Durango, with its combination of beautiful natural landscapes, rich history, and culture, and delicious cuisine, is a must-visit destination in Mexico. Book your trip now, and use this travel guide in planning a memorable experience that will leave you with cherished memories forever.
