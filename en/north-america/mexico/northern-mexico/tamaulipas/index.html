Introduction to Tamaulipas

Tamaulipas is a beautiful state located in the northeastern region of Mexico. It is bordered by the Gulf of Mexico to the east, the states of Nuevo Leon and San Luis Potosi to the west, and the states of Coahuila, Nuevo Leon, and Texas (United States) to the north.

With a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, Tamaulipas has become a popular tourist destination in Mexico. The state offers a diverse range of attractions, including beautiful beaches, historic sites, natural parks, and adventure activities.

In this article, we will take you through a comprehensive tour guide of Tamaulipas, including the best places to visit, where to stay, what to do, and much more.

Local Culture and Customs of Tamaulipas

Tamaulipas is known for its vibrant culture, which is a blend of indigenous, Mexican and Spanish traditions. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • Traditional Huasteca music and dance styles
  • Flamenco dance performances
  • Celebrating the Day of the Dead festival in November

Best Time to Visit Tamaulipas

The best time to visit Tamaulipas is during the winter months (December to February), when the weather is cooler and there is less rainfall. However, if you’re a beach-lover, then the summer months (June to August) are the perfect time to visit Tamaulipas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tamaulipas

When visiting Tamaulipas, it’s important to follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish
  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites
  • Take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water
  • Leave valuables in an unattended car
  • Wander alone on dark streets at night

Safety and Health

When traveling to Tamaulipas, it’s important to keep these safety and health concerns in mind:

Safety in Tamaulipas

  • Avoid traveling at night
  • Stay in well-lit and populated areas
  • Always carry copies of important documents in case of emergency

Health in Tamaulipas

  • Take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases
  • Avoid street food to prevent food poisoning
  • Stay hydrated
