Introduction to Calvillo

Located in the Bajio region of Mexico, Calvillo is a charming town that delights visitors with its blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This sleepy town is surrounded by stunning mountains, fertile valleys, and lush orchards, making it a great place to relax, unwind, and explore. Calvillo is known for its picturesque streets, colonial architecture, and traditional festivals, which celebrate the town’s rich cultural heritage. Whether you are a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie, Calvillo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Calvillo

There are plenty of things to see and do in Calvillo. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sanctuary of Guadalupe: This beautiful church dates back to the 19th century and is famous for its carved wooden altarpiece and colorful stained-glass windows.
  • Miguel Hidalgo House Museum: Located in the birthplace of Miguel Hidalgo, one of the heroes of Mexican independence, this museum showcases the life and achievements of the revolutionary leader.
  • The Main Square: Known as Plaza Principal, this square features a beautiful kiosk, a stone fountain, and some of the town’s most important buildings.

Natural Attractions of Calvillo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Piedra Blanca: This stunning natural landmark is an enormous white rock formation that sits atop a hill overlooking the town. Visitors can hike to the top for breathtaking views of the landscape.
  • La Chona Waterfall: Located just outside Calvillo, this picturesque waterfall is nestled in a lush canyon and is a great spot for swimming, picnicking and hiking.
  • Los Tepozanes Hill: This hill is a popular spot for paragliding, offering spectacular views of the valley and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calvillo

Calvillo and its surrounding areas offer some great hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Sierra Fría: This mountain range is a paradise for hikers, offering trails of varying degrees of difficulty. The most popular hike is to Pico del Águila (Eagle’s Peak), which stands at 10,800 feet (3,300 meters) tall.
  • La Panoramica: This scenic road offers breathtaking views of the valley and mountains. Visitors can cycle or drive along the road and stop at some of the viewpoints along the way.
  • Cerro del Muerto: This prominent hill is located just outside the town and offers a moderate hike with stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Calvillo: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun things to do in Calvillo, no matter what your interests are. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Food Tour: Calvillo is known for its delicious food, from traditional dishes to fragrant fruits. Take a food tour to discover the local gastronomy.
  • Horseback riding: Explore the countryside on horseback and take in the stunning views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Fiesta de la Guayaba: This festival is held in February every year and celebrates the town’s most famous fruit, the guava. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, from guava tastings to guava-eating contests.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride and experience stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calvillo

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Calvillo:

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Sanctuary of Guadalupe. Day 2: Take a horseback riding tour and enjoy a picnic in the countryside. Day 3: Hike to La Piedra Blanca and enjoy the stunning views of the town. Day 4: Visit the Miguel Hidalgo House Museum and learn about the town’s revolutionary past. Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the town and surrounding landscapes. Day 6: Visit the Los Tepozanes Hill and go paragliding. Day 7: Spend the day swimming in La Chona Waterfall and enjoying a relaxing picnic.

Food and Drink Scene of Calvillo

Calvillo is known for its delicious and authentic food, which is heavily influenced by the town’s agricultural heritage. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carnitas: This popular dish features slow-cooked pork that is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. It is usually served with tortillas and various toppings, such as guacamole and salsa.
  • Aguachile: This spicy seafood dish is made with shrimp, lime juice, chili peppers, and cilantro.
  • Guava: This sweet fruit is a local specialty and is incorporated into many sweet and savory dishes, such as guava paste, candied guava, and guava tamales.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: This famous Mexican spirit is made from the blue agave plant and is a staple at any bar in Calvillo.
  • Pulque: This alcoholic beverage is made from fermented agave sap and is known for its thick, milky texture and sour taste.
  • Flavored Waters: Aguas frescas, or flavored waters, are a refreshing way to cool off on a hot day. Some popular flavors include hibiscus, tamarind, and pineapple.

Where to Stay in Calvillo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calvillo offers a range of accommodation options for different budgets and tastes. Some of our top picks include:

  • Hacienda El Cenizo: This charming hotel is located just outside Calvillo and features rustic decor, a pool, and a restaurant.
  • Hotel Posada de los Arcos: This cozy hotel is located in the heart of town and offers comfortable rooms, a garden patio, and a restaurant.
  • Hostal de las Flores: This budget-friendly guesthouse is located in a quiet neighborhood and offers clean rooms, a communal kitchen, and a patio with hammocks.

What to Buy in Calvillo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Calvillo is a great place to shop for souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Guava Products: Stock up on guava paste, guava jam, and other guava-based products to take home with you.
  • Handicrafts: Browse the local handicraft markets and pick up some traditional pottery, woven textiles, or leather goods.
  • Tequila: Bring home a bottle of tequila, the town’s most famous drink.

Local Culture and Customs of Calvillo

Calvillo has a rich and unique culture that is heavily influenced by its agricultural heritage. The town is famous for its traditional festivals, such as the Fiesta de la Guayaba and the Huanimaro Fair, which showcase the town’s music, dance, and gastronomy. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to participate in the town’s cultural activities.

Best Time to Visit Calvillo

Calvillo has a mild, Mediterranean climate, with warm summers and cool winters. The best time to visit is from October to April when the weather is pleasant and there are fewer crowds. The town can get crowded during the festivals, so if you want to attend one, make sure to plan ahead and book your accommodation early.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calvillo

Do:

  • Try the food: Calvillo is known for its delicious food, so make sure to sample some of the local specialties.
  • Explore the countryside: The surrounding countryside is stunning, so be sure to take a hike, bike ride, or horseback ride.
  • Attend a festival: If you are in town during a festival, don’t miss the opportunity to immerse yourself in the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: The tap water in Calvillo is not considered safe to drink, so make sure to buy bottled water instead.
  • Disregard local customs: Be respectful of the local customs and traditions, especially during religious festivals.
  • Leave your valuables unattended: Although Calvillo is generally safe, it’s always a good idea to be vigilant and keep an eye on your belongings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calvillo

Calvillo is generally a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding dark alleys at night. The town has a low crime rate, but petty theft can occur, especially in crowded areas. It’s always a good idea to keep a copy of your passport and valuables in a safe place.

Health in Calvillo

Calvillo has a good public health system, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid getting sick. It’s recommended to drink bottled water, avoid eating street food, and take mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and Zika.

Getting to Calvillo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calvillo

  • Licenciado Jesús Terán Peredo International Airport: This airport is the closest international airport and is located about 24 miles (39 kilometers) from Calvillo.

Bus Stations Near Calvillo

  • Central de Autobuses de Aguascalientes: This major bus station is located in the city of Aguascalientes, about 26 miles (42 kilometers) from Calvillo.
  • Central de Autobuses de Leon: Located in the city of Leon, this bus station is about 87 miles (140 kilometers) from Calvillo.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the surrounding areas.
  • Private Transfer: You can arrange a private transfer from the airport or bus station to Calvillo with a local tour operator.

How to Get Around in Calvillo

Calvillo is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. If you want to visit some of the surrounding attractions, you can rent a bike, hire a taxi, or join a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Calvillo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calvillo is located in the Bajio region, which is famous for its colonial cities, scenic landscapes, and delicious food. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to add to your itinerary:

  • Aguascalientes: This vibrant city is located about 45 minutes from Calvillo and is known for its colonial architecture, hot springs, and lively festivals.
  • San Miguel de Allende: This historic town is famous for its colorful buildings, cobblestone streets, and artistic community.
  • Tequila: This town is located about 5 hours from Calvillo and is the birthplace of tequila. Visitors can visit the distilleries, learn about the production process, and sample some of the tequila.
  • Guanajuato: This colonial city is located about 2 hours from Calvillo and is known for its beautiful architecture, underground tunnels, and rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Calvillo may be a small town, but it packs a punch when it comes to culture, history, and nature. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway, an outdoor adventure, or a cultural immersion, Calvillo has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Calvillo today and discover one of Mexico’s hidden gems.
