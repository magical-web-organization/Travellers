Introduction to Aguascalientes

Nestled in the heart of Bajío, Mexico, Aguascalientes is a city steeped in rich history and culture. The name “Aguascalientes” translates to “hot waters,” referring to the abundance of hot springs found throughout the region. Visitors flock to Aguascalientes to explore its fascinating museums, admire the stunning architecture, and take part in a variety of outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Aguascalientes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aguascalientes is home to a wealth of museums and historical sites, providing visitors with a glimpse into the city’s past. The top cultural attractions include:

  • Museo Nacional de la Muerte: This museum is dedicated to the Day of the Dead, a beloved holiday in Mexico. Exhibits include altars, offerings, toys, and more, all related to the Day of the Dead.
  • Museo Nacional de la Arqueología: This museum houses one of the largest collections of pre-Columbian art in the country, with artifacts ranging from the Olmec to the Aztec civilizations.
  • Templo de San Antonio: This beautiful temple was built in the 18th century and is a fine example of Baroque architecture. Visitors can admire the elaborate frescoes and sculptures that adorn the walls, as well as the impressive bell tower.

Natural Attractions of Aguascalientes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views, Aguascalientes has plenty to offer. The top natural attractions include:

  • Parque Natural Sierra Fría: This national park boasts a stunning landscape of mountains, valleys, and forests. Visitors can hike, bike, and camp in the park, as well as explore the many caves and waterfalls.
  • Cerro del Muerto: This hill offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill, or take the cable car for a more leisurely ascent.
  • Ojo Caliente: This hot spring is located just outside the city and is a popular spot for swimming and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aguascalientes

Aguascalientes is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and paths to explore. Some of the best routes include:

  • Cerro del Muerto Trail: This 4-mile round trip hike takes visitors to the top of Cerro del Muerto, offering stunning views of the city and surrounding landscape.
  • Sierra Fria Trail: This 12-mile round trip hike takes visitors through the Parque Natural Sierra Fria, passing by waterfalls, caves, and forests.
  • Ecological Park Trail: This 5-mile round trip hike takes visitors through the Ecological Park, passing by a variety of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Aguascalientes: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, Aguascalientes has something to offer for everyone. The top things to do in the city include:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic city center, admiring the colonial architecture and visiting the top museums and sites.
  • Catch a performance at the beautiful Teatro Morelos, which hosts a variety of concerts, plays, and dance performances throughout the year.
  • Join a hot air balloon tour, taking in the stunning views of the city and countryside from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aguascalientes

For those planning a week-long trip to Aguascalientes, the following itinerary is recommended:

  • Day 1: Explore the city center, visiting the top museums and historical sites.
  • Day 2: Take a hot air balloon tour, followed by a hike in Sierra Fria.
  • Day 3: Visit Ojo Caliente hot springs for a relaxing day of swimming and soaking.
  • Day 4-5: Take a day trip to nearby Guanajuato, exploring the stunning colonial architecture and museums.
  • Day 6: Join a bike tour of Ecological Park, followed by a performance at Teatro Morelos.
  • Day 7: Hike up Cerro del Muerto for stunning panoramic views of the city and countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Aguascalientes

No trip to Aguascalientes is complete without indulging in the delicious local cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Birria: A flavorful stew made with beef, goat, or lamb, served with tortillas and a variety of toppings.
  • Enchiladas Aguascalientes: Enchiladas filled with beef, pork, or chicken, served with a spicy red sauce and topped with cheese and onion.
  • Machacado con Huevo: Dried beef mixed with scrambled eggs and served with tortillas and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: A well-known Mexican liquor made from the agave plant. Visitors can sample various tequila brands and learn about the distilling process on a tequila tour.
  • Ponche: A hot drink made with fruit, spices, and sugar, perfect for cold winter days.

Where to Stay in Aguascalientes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aguascalientes offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top options include:

  • Hotel Aguascalientes: This downtown hotel is centrally located and offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.
  • Hostel Casa del Sol: This charming hostel is located in a historic building and offers dorm rooms as well as private rooms for couples or families.
  • Casa del Jardín: This beautiful guesthouse is located in a quiet residential area and offers spacious rooms with a garden view.

What to Buy in Aguascalientes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aguascalientes is known for its fine craftsmanship and unique souvenirs. Some of the top products to bring home include:

  • Talavera pottery: Beautifully painted ceramics that make perfect decorative pieces for any home.
  • Textiles: Bright and colorful woven goods, from blankets to tablecloths.
  • Handmade jewelry: Unique designs using a variety of materials, including silver and precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Aguascalientes

Aguascalientes has a vibrant and welcoming culture, with a strong sense of community and tradition. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs, such as:

  • Participating in Day of the Dead festivities, including visiting the museums and cemeteries and creating offerings for loved ones who have passed away.
  • Attending a traditional fiesta or parade, such as the San Marcos Fair, which takes place in April and May each year.
  • Respectfully greeting locals with a handshake or hug, depending on the level of familiarity.

Best Time to Visit Aguascalientes

The best time to visit Aguascalientes is in the fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. The city also celebrates the San Marcos Fair during this time, which is a must-see cultural event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aguascalientes

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including tequila and ponche.
  • Take part in local cultural events and festivals.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without filtering or boiling it first.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Engage in any illegal activities, such as drug use or prostitution.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aguascalientes

Aguascalientes is a relatively safe city, with low levels of violent crime. However, visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Aguascalientes

Visitors to Aguascalientes should ensure they have all necessary vaccinations prior to travel. It is also recommended to only drink bottled or filtered water, and to use insect repellent to guard against mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Aguascalientes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aguascalientes is easily accessible by air or land. The following transportation options are available:

Airports Near Aguascalientes

  • Aguascalientes International Airport: Located just 14 km from the city center, this airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Aguascalientes

  • Central de Autobuses de Aguascalientes: This central bus station offers both domestic and international service, with connections to major cities in Mexico and the US.

Other Options

  • The city is also accessible by car, with well-maintained highways connecting the city to nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Aguascalientes

The best way to get around Aguascalientes is by walking or using public transportation. Taxis and buses are readily available throughout the city, and fares are reasonable.

Where to Go Next from Aguascalientes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore beyond Aguascalientes, the following destinations are recommended:

  • Guanajuato: This city is known for its stunning architecture and rich culture, and is just a short drive from Aguascalientes.
  • San Miguel de Allende: This charming colonial city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a variety of cultural experiences.
  • Zacatecas: This colonial gem is situated among the mountains and boasts an impressive array of museums and historical sites.

Conclusion

Aguascalientes is a hidden gem in the heart of Mexico, offering visitors a wealth of cultural, natural, and culinary experiences. Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, there is something for everyone in this beautiful city.
