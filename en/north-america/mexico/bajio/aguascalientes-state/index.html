Introduction to Aguascalientes (state)

Located in Bajío, Mexico, Aguascalientes is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and breathtaking landscapes. The state’s name translates to “hot waters,” which refers to the natural hot springs that are a popular attraction for tourists visiting the region. Aguascalientes is also known for its festivals, such as the San Marcos Fair and the Day of the Dead celebrations. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural landmarks or embarking on outdoor adventures, Aguascalientes has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Aguascalientes (state)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aguascalientes Cathedral: Built-in 1738, the Aguascalientes Cathedral is a magnificent structure with a stunning façade featuring a mix of Baroque and neoclassical styles. Visitors can stroll through the cathedral’s halls and admire its ornate altarpieces, paintings, and sculptures.

  • National Museum of Death: This museum offers a fascinating look at how death has been viewed in Mexico throughout history. The exhibitions feature a wide range of artifacts, from pre-Hispanic times to the present day, including skulls, altars, and figurines.

  • Casa de la Cultura: A cultural center that houses exhibitions, concerts, and other artistic events throughout the year, the Casa de la Cultura is a must-visit for anyone interested in the arts.

Natural Attractions of Aguascalientes (state): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sierra Fria National Park: Spanning over 1.2 million acres, the Sierra Fria National Park offers incredible hiking and camping opportunities, with over 50 miles of trails to explore. The park is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including species such as black bears, eagles, and pumas.

  • San Jose Dam: One of the largest dams in the region, the San Jose Dam provides a scenic spot for visitors to relax and enjoy the sun. The surrounding area is popular for picnics, fishing, and boating.

  • Ojocaliente Hot Springs: Aguascalientes is known for its hot springs, and visiting the Ojocaliente Hot Springs is an excellent way to unwind and experience the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aguascalientes (state)

  • Cerro del Muerto: A popular hiking trail that leads to the top of a beautiful mountain, Cerro del Muerto offers stunning views of the Aguascalientes landscape.

  • Ecological Park El Sabinal: Located in the city of Aguascalientes, this park boasts over 100 acres of lush greenery and offers excellent hiking and cycling opportunities.

  • Cañon de Huaricho: A beautiful canyon located on the outskirts of Aguascalientes, Cañon de Huaricho is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Best Things to Do in Aguascalientes (state): Activities and Tours

  • San Marcos Fair: Held annually in April, the San Marcos Fair is one of Mexico’s most significant festivals. Visitors can enjoy live music, delicious food, and a variety of family-friendly activities.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: With a growing reputation as a wine-producing region, Aguascalientes offers several vineyards and wineries to explore.

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can take guided horseback riding tours to explore the scenic countryside of Aguascalientes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aguascalientes (state)

  • Day 1: Explore the historic downtown area of Aguascalientes and visit the Aguascalientes Cathedral and the National Museum of Death.

  • Day 2: Visit the Sierra Fria National Park and take a hike through the beautiful scenery.

  • Day 3: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Zacatecas and explore its historic architecture and cultural landmarks.

  • Day 4: Relax at the Ojocaliente Hot Springs and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

  • Day 5: Visit the San Jose Dam and spend the day fishing, boating, or enjoying a picnic.

  • Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and sample the best wines produced in Aguascalientes.

  • Day 7: Enjoy horseback riding and explore the beautiful countryside of Aguascalientes.

Food and Drink Scene of Aguascalientes (state)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Birria: A traditional Mexican meat stew made with goat or other meats and served with tortillas, fresh lime, and onions.

  • Barbacoa: Slow-cooked meat, usually beef or lamb, served with tortillas, fresh lime, and salsa.

  • Enchiladas: Tortillas filled with meat or cheese and topped with tomato sauce, beans, and fresh cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: A famous alcoholic drink made from Blue Agave that is widely produced in the area.

  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic drink made from the agave plant, often served with a slice of orange and a spicy salt mix on the rim.

  • Agua de Jamaica: A refreshing drink made from hibiscus flowers and sugar served over ice.

Where to Stay in Aguascalientes (state): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Las Trojes: A luxurious hotel in Aguascalientes with a beautiful outdoor pool and spa facilities.

  • Hostel Casa San Marcos: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Aguascalientes, perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

  • Casa del Jardin: A charming guesthouse located in the countryside of Aguascalientes, offering a peaceful and relaxing environment.

What to Buy in Aguascalientes (state): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: Aguascalientes is known for its beautiful handmade pottery, which makes for an excellent souvenir.

  • Local textiles: Visitors can find textiles made by local artisans, including woven shawls and embroidered traditional dresses.

  • Tequila and Mezcal: Take home a bottle of locally produced tequila or mezcal to enjoy back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Aguascalientes (state)

Aguascalientes is proud of its heritage, which is reflected in its vibrant celebrations, colorful architecture, and rich cultural traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering someone’s home and dressing appropriately when visiting religious or cultural landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Aguascalientes (state)

The best time to visit Aguascalientes is from October to May, which is the dry season and offers comfortable temperatures for outdoor activities. It is essential to note that the San Marcos Fair takes place in April, so it is a great time to experience the state’s biggest cultural event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aguascalientes (state)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try traditional dishes and beverages.

  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and explore the scenic countryside.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect cultural landmarks and traditions.

  • Be disrespectful to locals or use offensive language.

  • Neglect safety precautions when exploring outdoor areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aguascalientes (state)

Like anywhere else, visitors to Aguascalientes should take necessary precautions to stay safe, such as being aware of their surroundings and avoiding traveling alone at night.

Health in Aguascalientes (state)

Visitors should make sure they have up-to-date vaccinations before visiting Aguascalientes. They should also use mosquito repellent and practice good hygiene to avoid contracting any illnesses.

Getting to Aguascalientes (state): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aguascalientes (state)

The Aguascalientes International Airport is the most convenient airport for visitors traveling by air.

Bus Stations Near Aguascalientes (state)

The Central de Autobuses de Aguascalientes is the main bus station in the area and offers services to several nearby cities and regions.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Aguascalientes (state)

Visitors can use public transportation, such as buses and taxis, to get around the city and neighboring areas easily. Renting a car is also a convenient option for those who prefer to explore at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Aguascalientes (state): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Luis Potosi: Located to the north of Aguascalientes, San Luis Potosi is a historic city with a stunning cathedral and beautiful architecture.

  • Guanajuato: A city filled with colorful buildings and narrow streets, Guanajuato is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers a unique glimpse into Mexican history and culture.

  • Mexico City: A bustling metropolis with a rich cultural scene, Mexico City offers world-class museums, art galleries, and incredible food.

Conclusion

Aguascalientes (state) offers a unique blend of history, tradition, and natural beauty that makes it a must-visit destination in Mexico. Whether visitors are interested in exploring cultural landmarks, embarking on outdoor adventures, or trying traditional dishes and beverages, Aguascalientes has something to offer for everyone.
