Introduction to Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce is a charming, historic town located in the Central Plateau (Altiplano) region of San Luis Potosi state in the Bajío region of Mexico. This magical town, set at 9,000 feet above sea level, is surrounded by breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage.

Once a booming silver-mining town, Real de Catorce is now a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its fascinating history, stunning architecture, and spectacular natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Real de Catorce

Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, a nature lover, or a culture enthusiast, Real de Catorce has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of the top attractions to visit in Real de Catorce.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Real de Catorce: A historic building that used to be the old Justice Palace, the museum houses excellent exhibitions showcasing Real de Catorce’s history and culture.
  • Templo De La Purisima Concepcion: A magnificent church built in the 18th century, boasting beautiful architecture and a rich religious history.
  • Casa de la Moneda: This old mint museum displays coins, old machinery, and other pieces of history from the town’s silver mining days.
  • El Jardin: Real de Catorce’s main square, lined with stunning colonial buildings. It is an excellent spot to sit, sip a cup of coffee, and watch people go about their daily lives.

Natural Attractions of Real de Catorce: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cerro Quemado: A popular hiking site that offers expansive views of Real de Catorce and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Ojo de Agua: A natural spring where you can swim and admire the beautiful scenery.
  • Hierve el Agua: A stunning set of cascading natural rock formations that form pools for swimming and soaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce has several hiking and biking routes that let you explore the beautiful landscapes surrounding the town. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Camino de la Vía: A 20-kilometer flat trail that runs along an old rail bed between Real de Catorce and the neighboring village of Estación Catorce.
  • Sendero La Presa: A 4-kilometer trail that offers scenic views of the Las Águilas dam and the surrounding mountains.
  • La Bufa: A popular 2-hour hike that offers stunning views of the sunset over the mountains and the town below.

Best Things to Do in Real de Catorce: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the beautiful landscapes surrounding Real de Catorce on horseback by taking a guided tour.
  • Star Gazing: Real de Catorce is an excellent place for stargazing due to the lack of light pollution.
  • Artisanal Shopping: Explore the local artisanal shops and buy unique souvenirs to bring back home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Real de Catorce

Here’s a suggested seven-day itinerary to help you make the most of your time in Real de Catorce:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Real de Catorce, check into your hotel, and explore the town center.
  • Day 2: Visit the Museo de Real de Catorce and Templo De La Purisima Concepcion.
  • Day 3: Take a guided horseback riding tour to explore the surrounding mountains.
  • Day 4: Hike to Cerro Quemado for panoramic views of Real de Catorce.
  • Day 5: Visit Hierve el Agua and swim in the natural pools.
  • Day 6: Shop for souvenirs at the local artisanal shops and join a stargazing tour at night.
  • Day 7: Depart from Real de Catorce.

Food and Drink Scene of Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce has a rich culinary heritage, and the town boasts several traditional dishes and beverages that you must try during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Enchiladas Potosinas: A type of tortilla stuffed with cheese and meat served with a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Gorditas de Maiz: A thick, stuffed tortilla filled with meat or vegetables and cooked on a griddle.
  • Tacos de Lengua: Tacos filled with juicy, tender beef tongue.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: An ancient alcoholic beverage made from fermented agave sap, known for its thick, creamy texture and unique flavor.
  • Mezcal: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from the maguey plant and smoked in underground pits to give it a distinct smoky flavor.

Where to Stay in Real de Catorce: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Real de Catorce has several accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hotel Mesón Real de Catorce: A beautiful colonial-style hotel located in the heart of the town center.
  • Posada Pallin: An affordable guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodation with a cozy atmosphere.
  • Hospedaje la Milagrosa: A charming hostel offering both shared and private rooms.

What to Buy in Real de Catorce: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Real de Catorce is known for its exceptional artisanal products, including silver jewelry, handmade textiles, and handcrafted pottery. Be sure to stop by the local artisanal shops to pick up unique souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the town’s festivals and events. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, such as not taking photographs during religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Real de Catorce

The best time to visit Real de Catorce is from October through April when the weather is mild, and the town hosts several local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Real de Catorce

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Stock up on snacks and water before going on a hike or bike ride.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs during religious ceremonies.
  • Buy souvenirs made from endangered animals or plants.
  • Leave trash behind in the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always best to take precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables and being aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce is located at a high altitude, and visitors may experience altitude sickness, such as headache, nausea, and shortness of breath. It is recommended to drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol and cigarettes during your visit to prevent altitude sickness.

Getting to Real de Catorce: Transportation Options and Logistics

Real de Catorce is accessible by car or bus from the nearby cities of Matehuala, San Luis Potosi, and Monterrey.

Airports Near Real de Catorce

The closest airports to Real de Catorce are San Luis Potosi Airport and Monterrey International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Real de Catorce

Several bus companies operate services from Matehuala and San Luis Potosi to Real de Catorce.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or shared van from the bus station in Matehuala to Real de Catorce.

How to Get Around in Real de Catorce

Real de Catorce is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a horse-drawn carriage to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Real de Catorce: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Real de Catorce is surrounded by several natural attractions and historic towns that are worth visiting, including:

  • Guanajuato: A UNESCO World Heritage site and former silver-mining town with charming architecture and a vibrant cultural scene.
  • Catorce Desert: A stunning natural reserve that offers hiking and biking trails.
  • Xilitla: A quaint town famous for its unique sculpture garden, Las Pozas, created by British artist Edward James.

Conclusion

Real de Catorce is an incredible destination in Mexico that boasts fascinating history, stunning architecture, and breathtaking natural beauty. Plan your trip to Real de Catorce by using our comprehensive tour guide, and make unforgettable memories in this magical town.
