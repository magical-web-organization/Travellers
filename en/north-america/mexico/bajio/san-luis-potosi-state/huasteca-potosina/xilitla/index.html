Introduction to Xilitla

Welcome to Xilitla, a hidden gem nestled in the heart of Huasteca Potosina, in San Luis Potosi state, Bajío, Mexico. This charming town boasts a vibrant culture, rich history, and breathtaking natural beauty. From its awe-inspiring mountains to its ancient architecture and vibrant markets, Xilitla has something to offer for every traveler.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay while in Xilitla. Whether you are a solo traveler or visiting with family, there is something for everyone in Xilitla. Let’s explore!

List of Places to Visit in Xilitla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Las Pozas: This artistic wonderland consists of surreal concrete sculptures and waterfalls scattered throughout a lush garden. Created by English poet Edward James, Las Pozas is a must-visit destination in Xilitla.

  • Casa de la Cultura: Explore local art exhibits and cultural performances at this historic house, which was once the residence of Leonora Carrington, a famous surrealist artist.

  • Museo Leonora Carrington: This museum showcases the works of Leonora Carrington, a renowned surrealist painter and sculptor who lived in Xilitla for many years.

  • Iglesia de San Agustín: This beautiful, colonial-style church dates back to the 18th century and is a magnificent example of baroque style architecture.

Natural Attractions of Xilitla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Puente de Dios (Bridge of God): This natural wonder consists of a series of cascading pools and waterfalls amidst a stunning canyon with crystal-clear water. Visitors can swim in the cool waters or take a refreshing dip in the pool.

  • Sótano de las Golondrinas (Cave of Swallows): This cave is home to thousands of swallows that fly in and out of it daily. Watch the birds take off at sunrise or sunset and marvel at the incredible sight.

  • Cascada de Tamul (Tamul Waterfall): This stunning waterfall is one of Mexico’s most impressive natural wonders. Reachable only by boat or hiking, it’s an amazing sight to behold.

  • El Salto: This beautiful waterfall features a natural pool for swimming and a cliff jumping spot for the daring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xilitla

  • Sierra Gorda: This mountainous region offers fantastic hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the valley.

  • El Jabalí: This challenging hike leads to a panoramic view of Xilitla and the surrounding mountains.

  • Cerro de San Miguel: This moderate hike rewards visitors with breathtaking vistas of the town and the Huasteca Potosina region.

Best Things to Do in Xilitla: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting and Kayaking: Enjoy an adventurous day exploring the rapids of the Huasteca Potosina River.

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of Xilitla’s mountainous landscape on a leisurely horseback ride.

  • Canopy Tours: Admire the stunning scenery of Xilitla from high above the trees with thrilling zipline rides.

  • Coffee Tours: Learn about the coffee-making process and indulge in the rich flavors of Xilitla’s locally grown coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xilitla

Looking to spend a week in Xilitla? Here’s an itinerary to guide your travels:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the town center, visit Casa de Cultura, and Museo Leonora Carrington.

  • Day 3: Take a dip in the Puente de Dios and El Salto.

  • Day 4: Hike to El Jabalí and relax under the sun in one of the town’s plazas.

  • Day 5: Travel to the Tamul waterfall and embark on a boat ride adventure.

  • Day 6: Explore Las Pozas and admire the unique sculptures and beautiful gardens.

  • Day 7: Go on a coffee tour and enjoy the flavors of the local coffee.

Food and Drink Scene of Xilitla

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tewaka: This traditional dish is a staple in Xilitla and is made up of roasted pork, tomato, and chillies.

  • Pachitas: Beef tripe cooked in a spicy sauce.

  • Enchiladas: These tasty and spicy corn tortillas rolled around seasoned meat, cheese, or vegetables and topped with sauce and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Pulque: A milky-colored, fermented drink made from the sap of the maguey plant.

  • Mezcal: This smoky liquor is made from the maguey plant and is similar in taste to tequila.

  • Cerveza Artesanal: Try a local craft beer from one of the many breweries in the area

Where to Stay in Xilitla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Casa Catrina: A charming, eco-friendly, boutique hotel located in the heart of Xilitla.

  • El Sagrado Lodge: Nestled amidst lush jungles, this lodge offers luxurious rooms with private hot tubs and breathtaking forest views.

  • Posada El Castillo: This family-run guesthouse offers comfortable and affordable accommodations in the hills of Xilitla.

What to Buy in Xilitla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Candied fruits: Try the local artisanal candy made from a variety of fruits like pumpkin, figs, and tamarind.

  • Handmade textiles: Purchase intricate fabrics and tapestries woven by local artisans in traditional methods.

  • Huastecan Coffee: Bring back some of the locally grown and roasted coffee beans to enjoy a taste of Xilitla at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Xilitla

Xilitla is home to a warm and welcoming community that values family, tradition, and celebrations. The town celebrates its Indigenous heritage and showcases its unique cultural influences in its celebrations and traditional architecture.

Best Time to Visit Xilitla

Xilitla is a year-round destination, although the months between November to February are the busiest, and accommodations may be harder to come by. The rainy season typically runs from June to September, which can affect some outdoor activities and tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xilitla

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially at sacred sites.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Use environmentally responsible travel practices and be mindful of litter and waste.

Don’t:

  • Bring or purchase any illegal substances.

  • Disrespect local culture, including taking unauthorized photos or entering restricted areas.

  • Cause harm to the environment during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xilitla

Xilitla is a generally safe destination for travelers, although it is recommended to exercise caution and common sense when exploring unfamiliar areas.

Health in Xilitla

It is recommended to ensure up-to-date vaccinations and medical treatments before traveling. Be cautious when consuming street food and bottled water is recommended.

Getting to Xilitla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xilitla

The closest airport to Xilitla is San Luis Potosi Airport (SLP), which is approximately a 3-hour drive.

Bus Stations Near Xilitla

Buses depart from the San Luis Potosi bus station, or travelers can take a direct bus from Mexico City, which takes approximately 5 hours.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a private driver is also an option for travelers looking for more flexibility and independence.

How to Get Around in Xilitla

Walking is the best way to explore the town center, and taxi services are available for longer-distance travel.

Where to Go Next from Xilitla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tamazunchale: This beautiful town is approximately a 1-hour drive from Xilitla and is well-known for its caves and archaeological sites.

  • Real de Catorce: This historic town is located approximately 3 hours from Xilitla and is famous for its silver mines and colonial architecture.

Conclusion

Xilitla is an incredible destination that offers a perfect mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. With so much to do and see in this charming town, there is something to delight every traveler. Plan your unforgettable Xilitla trip today!
