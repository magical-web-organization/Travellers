Introduction to Zacatecas (State)

Located in the heart of Mexico, Zacatecas is a state known for its rich culture, fascinating history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene. This hidden gem off the beaten path is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Mexican experience.

Zacatecas is a land of contrasts, with its colonial architecture and ancient archaeological sites blending seamlessly with modern facilities and amenities. Whether you enjoy hiking in the mountains, exploring historical places, or savoring delicious local cuisine, Zacatecas has something to offer to everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and more in Zacatecas, Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Zacatecas (State)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zacatecas is home to a rich cultural legacy that’s evident in its many museums and historical places.

Museo Zacatecano

Situated in the heart of Zacatecas City, the Museo Zacatecano is a must-see attraction for those interested in Mexican history and culture. This museum features exhibits on pre-Columbian civilizations, colonial-era art and architecture, and Mexico’s fight for independence from Spain.

La Quemada Archaeological Zone

Located in the municipality of Villanueva, La Quemada is an ancient archaeological site that dates back to 800 AD. This pre-Columbian city was once a bustling hub of commerce and culture and is now a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Zacatecas Cathedral

The Zacatecas Cathedral, built in the 18th century, is a stunning example of Baroque architecture. Inside, you’ll find intricate wood carvings, beautifully painted ceilings, and a striking altar made of silver.

Natural Attractions of Zacatecas (State): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zacatecas is also home to many natural wonders that are sure to leave you in awe.

Sierra de Órganos National Park

Located near the town of Sombrerete, Sierra de Órganos National Park is a stunning natural park that boasts incredible rock formations, hiking trails, and scenic views. Don’t forget to bring your camera to capture the beauty of the area.

Cañón de Juchipila

Just a short drive from Zacatecas City, Cañón de Juchipila is a breathtaking canyon with sparkling streams, lush vegetation, and towering sandstone walls. The best way to explore the canyon is by hiking or horseback riding.

Los Organos

A geological wonder located near the town of Teúl de González Ortega, Los Organos is a series of columnar basalt formations that resemble the pipes of an organ. This natural attraction is a must-see on your trip to Zacatecas.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Zacatecas (State)

Zacatecas is every hiker and biker’s heaven on earth. The picturesque landscapes and natural beauty of the state provides a perfect backdrop for biking and hiking enthusiasts.

Cerro de la Bufa

A hiking and biking hotspot, Cerro de la Bufa lies on the outskirts of the state capital, Zacatecas City. The summit of this mountain offers a panoramic view of the city and its surroundings.

Fresnillo Bike Trail

This 17-kilometer mountain bike trail located in the city of Fresnillo is ideal for intermediate and advanced bikers. It weaves through the Sierra de Fresnillo and provides spectacular views of the region.

Mineral del Monte Route

The Mineral Del Monte Route is a fascinating journey through the mining town of Mineral Del Monte. The route starts at the entrance of the town and runs through the mountains, showcasing a unique blend of old and new architecture.

Best Things to Do in Zacatecas (State): Activities and Tours

Zacatecas has a wealth of activities and tours that will keep you entertained during your stay.

Tequila Tasting Tour

Learn about the making and history of tequila on this tour that takes you through the distilleries of the state. Sample some of Mexico’s most famous tequilas and enjoy scenic views of the countryside during the drive.

Silver Route Tour

Explore the state’s rich mining history by taking a tour of the Silver Route. The tour takes you through the ancient mining towns of Zacatecas, Fresnillo, and Rio Grande and offers insights into the state’s rich colonial past.

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Experience the beauty of Zacatecas from a new perspective on a hot air balloon ride. You’ll soar over the state’s canyons, historical buildings, and picturesque landscapes, making this a truly unforgettable experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Zacatecas (State)

Zacatecas has many delicious traditional dishes and refreshing local beverages to try out.

Must-Try Dishes

Asado de Boda

Asado de Boda is a traditional beef dish that’s usually served at weddings. It’s made by simmering beef in a red chili broth and is served with rice, beans, and tortillas.

Enchiladas Zacatecanas

These enchiladas are different from what you may know. They are made of a tortilla that is filled with locally-made cheese, bathed in a guajillo chili sauce, and then sprinkled with onion and cheese.

Caldillo

Caldillo is a hearty beef stew that’s flavored with tomatoes, potatoes, and vegetables. It is usually served with rice and beans, making it a filling and satisfying meal.

Local Beverages

Tequino

Tequino is a traditional Zacatecas drink that’s made from fermented corn. It is usually served in a clay mug and has a slightly sweet, tangy flavor.

Jarritos

Jarritos is a popular Mexican soda brand, and Zacatecas has its own unique flavors, including tamarindo, mandarina, and jamaica. These fruity and refreshing sodas are perfect on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Zacatecas (State): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zacatecas offers a variety of lodging options, from budget-friendly hostels to indulgent luxury hotels.

Hotel Casa Santo Domingo

Located in the heart of the city, Hotel Casa Santo Domingo is an elegant boutique hotel that’s perfect for those looking for a touch of luxury.

Hostal Villa Colonial

This budget-friendly hostel in the town of Zacatecas features comfortable dorms and private rooms at affordable prices.

Casa Bertha Guesthouse

Family-owned and located near the Zacatecas Cathedral, Casa Bertha Guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a warm welcome to guests.

What to Buy in Zacatecas (State): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Zacatecas without buying some of its unique souvenirs and local products as a reminder of your visit.

Zacatecas Silver Jewelry

Zacatecas has a rich mining history, and its silver jewelry is famous for its exquisite craftsmanship and beautiful designs. Make sure to check out the local markets for some unique silver jewelry pieces.

Zacatecas Artisanal Products

Zacatecas is known for its artisanal products, including pottery, ceramics, and hand-woven textiles. These items are perfect for bringing a touch of Zacatecas to your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Zacatecas (State)

Zacatecas is a state rich in history and culture, with a proud and distinct identity.

The people of Zacatecas are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and visitors are always welcomed with warmth and enthusiasm. The state also has a strong tradition of music, art, and dance, with several festivals and cultural events held throughout the year, including the Festival Cultural Zacatecas and the Festival Internacional de la Guitarra.

Best Time to Visit Zacatecas (State)

The best time to visit Zacatecas is during the fall and winter months, from October to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, keep in mind that this is also the peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zacatecas (State)

Do:

  • Follow local customs and show respect for the culture and traditions of the people of Zacatecas.
  • Try the local cuisine and traditional beverages.
  • Visit the many historical sites and museums of the state to learn about its rich history.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public, as it may be considered disrespectful.
  • Engage in any illicit activities, as this can lead to serious legal repercussions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zacatecas (State)

Zacatecas is generally a safe state for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Make sure to keep your valuables secure, avoid carrying too much cash, and stay in well-lit and populated areas at night.

Health in Zacatecas (State)

Before traveling to Zacatecas, it’s essential to check with your doctor or travel clinic to ensure you’re up-to-date on any necessary vaccines or medications. Additionally, make sure to drink only bottled water and avoid street food to prevent water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Zacatecas (State): Transportation Options and Logistics

Zacatecas is easily accessible by air, bus, or car.

Airports Near Zacatecas (State)

The General Leobardo C. Ruiz International Airport is the main airport serving Zacatecas. It provides daily flights to and from Mexico City, Guadalajara, Tijuana, and some major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Zacatecas (State)

Zacatecas is well-connected to several cities across Mexico through its many bus stations. The most popular bus lines serving Zacatecas are ETN, Primera Plus, and Omnibus de Mexico.

Other Options

Driving is also an option if you prefer to have more control over your travel itinerary. The highways in Zacatecas are well-maintained, and car rental services are available at the airport and throughout the state.

How to Get Around in Zacatecas (State)

The most convenient way to get around the state is by car or taxi. You can also rent a bike or join a guided tour to explore the many attractions of Zacatecas.

Where to Go Next from Zacatecas (State): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zacatecas is situated in the heart of Mexico and is a great launching pad to explore other nearby destinations, including:

San Luis Potosi

Located just a few hours away from Zacatecas, San Luis Potosi is a charming colonial city known for its Baroque architecture, cobblestone streets, and beautiful plazas.

Guanajuato

Guanajuato is another picturesque colonial city near Zacatecas. It is famous for its colorful houses, underground tunnels, and the Mummy Museum, which features naturally preserved mummies from the region.

Conclusion

Zacatecas is a hidden gem that offers visitors a one-of-a-kind experience. From its rich history and culture to its stunning natural beauty and delicious cuisine, Zacatecas has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, this state is sure to leave a lasting impression on you. Plan your visit to Zacatecas today and start exploring this fascinating destination!
