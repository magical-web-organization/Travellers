Introduction to Zacatecas

Located in North Central Mexico’s Bajio region, Zacatecas is a city dating back to the Colonial era. Also fondly referred to as the “City of Stones,” it was declared a World Heritage Site in 1993 by UNESCO. Zacatecas is known for its rich colonial architecture, its rosy-colored stones, and its silver mining traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Zacatecas

When visiting Zacatecas, you will be spoilt for choice, from its charming colonial architecture to museums and parks. Here are some of the best to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rafael Coronel Museum

The Rafael Coronel Museum houses an exceptional collection of Mexican folk art, with pieces dating back to the 16th century. The museum is located in the Convent of San Francisco, built-in 1591, and has an incredible collection of masks, textiles, and ceramics.

Zacatecas Cathedral

The Zacatecas Cathedral, dating back to the 18th century, is a fine example of Baroque architecture. It is renowned for its impressive twin towers and boasts stunning frescoes. Visitors can climb the bell tower and capture a breathtaking view of the city.

Natural Attractions of Zacatecas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sierra de Órganos National Park

Sierra de Órganos National Park is a must-visit place for nature lovers. It’s famous for its stunning rock formations that resemble an organ, hence its name. Visitors can enjoy hiking and nature walks while admiring its natural beauty.

Cerro de la Bufa

Cerro de la Bufa is one of Zacatecas’s iconic landmarks, towering over the city at over 2400 meters. Visitors can take a cable car to the top and enjoy panoramic views of the city from the lookout point. At night, visitors can enjoy the sound and light show that depicts the history and legends of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zacatecas

Zacatecas has many hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. A popular one is the Cerro de San Simón trail. This hiking route offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and takes visitors through beautiful woods, hills, and stunning vistas. Guided tours can be arranged for tourists to navigate the trails.

Best Things to Do in Zacatecas: Activities and Tours

There are various activities and tours available for tourists in Zacatecas. Depending on your interests, here are some of the top ones:

Cultural Tours

City tours are the best way to explore the distinct colonial heritage of Zacatecas. Visitors can take a walking tour of the city’s central plaza, visit the family-run silver mines, and discover the artisans’ workshops.

Adventure Tours

Zacatecas’s countryside offers plenty of adventure activities such as horseback riding, rafting in nearby rivers, and zip-lining through its hill country.

Food and Drink Scene of Zacatecas

Zacatecas offers a diverse range of culinary experiences. Its traditional cuisine includes a blend of indigenous and Spanish flavors, with a distinct influence from the mining culture.

Must-Try Dishes

A must-try dish while in Zacatecas is the asado de boda (wedding roast). This is a slow-cooked beef dish that has a unique mix of flavors and spices with aromatic ingredients such as guajillo, pasilla chilies, and clove.

Local Beverages

With its long-standing mining traditions, Zacatecas is also known for its local beverages such as mezcal and tequila, which often feature in local celebrations and festivals. Local craft beer has also become increasingly popular, with several microbreweries in the city.

Where to Stay in Zacatecas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zacatecas offers a range of accommodation to suit varying budgets.

At the high-end, Quinta Real Zacatecas is a luxury hotel housed in a former storied bullring. At the mid-range, Casa de la Palma, a renovated colonial mansion, offers a unique boutique experience. On the budget end, hostels like Casa Lupita provide clean and comfortable lodgings for backpackers.

What to Buy in Zacatecas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silver is Zacatecas’s most outstanding local product and makes an excellent souvenir. You’ll find jewelry, sculptures, and other decorative items made from high-quality silver on offer all over the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Zacatecas

Zacatecas has a host of cultural and traditional customs worth partaking in. June sees the annual San Pedro Fiesta, dedicated to the city’s patron saint, and the Zacatecas Cultural Festival in October. Visitors can witness music, dance, and gastronomic showcases and soak up the vibrant festive atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Zacatecas

The best time to visit Zacatecas is between December and February when the weather is dry and mild. Spring and fall are also lovely times to visit as the flowers and trees blossom, and the temperatures are comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zacatecas

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Carry light clothing for the daytime heat, sunscreen and a hat.
  • Explore the city on foot to discover hidden gems.

Don’t:

  • Wear flashy jewelry or display large amounts of money when walking around the city.
  • Drink tap water or eat uncooked food.
  • Visit unsafe or remote areas without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zacatecas

Zacatecas is generally safe for tourists when the usual precautions are taken, such as safely storing valuables and avoiding unsafe neighborhoods. The city is also equipped with tourist police who offer helpful advice and information.

Health in Zacatecas

Before visiting Zacatecas, visitors must check that they have all the required vaccinations. Visitors should also bring an ample supply of medication for any pre-existing conditions. It is advisable to use bottled water for drinking and brushing teeth.

Getting to Zacatecas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zacatecas

The General Leobardo C. Ruiz International Airport offers daily flights to and from major airports in Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey.

Bus Stations Near Zacatecas

Zacatecas also has a major bus station, Centro de Transferencia Modal, with public transportation services to various parts of the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive into Zacatecas, but it is essential to note that several roads are narrow and winding.

How to Get Around in Zacatecas

While walking is the best way to discover the city center, visitors can also get around by taxi. Public transportation such as buses and shared cabs known as colectivos are also available.

Where to Go Next from Zacatecas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zacatecas is just a short journey away from other picturesque cities in North Central Mexico, such as Guadalajara, Aguascalientes, and San Luis Potosi.

Conclusion

A trip to Zacatecas is more than just a visit to a colonial-era city. It’s an opportunity to explore Mexico’s culture, vast history, and natural beauty. With so much to see and do, you’ll want to plan a return visit.
