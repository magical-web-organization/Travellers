Introduction to Uxmal

Uxmal is a significant archaeological site located in the Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico. This pre-Columbian city is one of the most important archaeological sites among the Mayan civilization. The name Uxmal (yoo-sh-mahl) is derived from the five pyramids in the city and means ‘three times built’. The city flourished during the late Classic period, between AD 700 and AD 1000. Its architecture is characterized by intricate stone mosaics that have been preserved even after centuries.

These ruins of the ancient Mayan city of Uxmal offer a glimpse into the historical and cultural legacy of the Mayan civilization. In addition to its historical significance, Uxmal is also known for its natural wonders boasting of stunning views, hiking and biking routes, and cultural shows and festivals.

Food and Drink Scene of Uxmal

Must-Try Dishes

Uxmal is famous for its rich history and culture, as well as its scrumptious local dishes. Some must-try dishes are:

  • Cochinita Pibil – marinated pork in citrus and achiote paste
  • Sopa de Lima – a delicious chicken soup with lime and crispy tortilla strips
  • Poc Chuc – grilled meat seasoned with sour orange juice and spices
  • Queso Relleno – stuffed cheese with meat and spices

Local Beverages

Some of the must-try beverages when visiting Uxmal are:

  • Xtabentun – a sweet liqueur made from fermented honey and anise
  • Horchata de Chufa – a sweet rice milk drink flavored with cinnamon and vanilla
  • Margarita de Piloncillo – a classic Mexican cocktail with Piloncillo as a sweetener

Local Culture and Customs of Uxmal

The Mayan culture and language are still very much alive among the locals of Uxmal. They celebrate annual festivals such as the Day of the Dead, Hanal Pixan, and Carnival. Some of the cultural customs you can experience in Uxmal include:

  • Attending a traditional Mayan ceremony
  • Learning about the history of the Mayan civilization at the Chocolate Museum

Best Time to Visit Uxmal

The best time to visit Uxmal is between November and April when the weather is dry and pleasant. However, Uxmal is open all year round, and each season offers its own charm and beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uxmal

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take plenty of water especially when hiking
  • Try local cuisine and flavors

Don’t:

  • Climb the structures or touch the stone mosaics
  • Litter or disturb the surrounding environment
  • Wear inappropriate clothing

Safety and Health

Safety in Uxmal

Mexico has a reputation for crime, but Uxmal is generally safe. Nonetheless, travellers should take necessary precautions such as avoiding isolated areas, not travelling alone after dark, and being cautious when withdrawing money from ATMs.

Health in Uxmal

Travellers should take precautionary measures such as drinking bottled water, using insect repellent, and protecting themselves from mosquito bites to avoid contracting dengue fever or malaria.

Uxmal is a small town that is easily traversed on foot or bicycle. Taxis and buses are also available, though it’s recommended to negotiate the fare with the driver.
