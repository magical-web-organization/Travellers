Introduction to Tulum

Tulum is a charming coastal town located in Quintana Roo, Yucatán and the South, in Mexico, in North America. Known for its stunning beaches, rich history and cultural heritage, Tulum is the perfect destination for those looking to experience the best of both worlds.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan an amazing trip to Tulum. From the most popular tourist attractions to hidden gems and local tips, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Tulum

Tulum has a lot to offer, from ancient ruins to beautiful beaches and natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit in Tulum:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tulum Archaeological Site: Explore the ruins of an ancient Mayan city, including the iconic El Castillo temple.
  • Museo de la Prehistoria: Learn about the history and culture of the region, including the prehistoric animals that once roamed the area.
  • Cenote Dos Ojos: This unique natural wonder features underwater caverns filled with crystal-clear water and stunning rock formations.

Natural Attractions of Tulum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sian Ka’an Biosphere Reserve: This protected area is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including monkeys, jaguars, and sea turtles.
  • Tulum Beach: Relax on the soft white sand and soak up the sun, or take a dip in the turquoise waters.
  • Gran Cenote: This stunning cenote features clear water, caves, and underground rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tulum

Tulum is also a great destination for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Tulum Ruins Beach Hike: This trail takes you from the ancient Mayan ruins to the beach, offering stunning views of the Caribbean Sea.
  • Tulum to Tankah Bike Ride: This scenic route takes you through the jungle, past ancient ruins and natural wonders, and ends at the beautiful Tankah Bay.

Best Things to Do in Tulum: Activities and Tours

Tulum is a great destination for adventure seekers, with plenty of activities and tours to choose from:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Explore the vibrant underwater world of Tulum, including the Mesoamerican Reef and various cenotes.
  • Mayan Jungle Experience: Take a guided tour through the jungle, learning about the history and culture of the Mayan people.
  • Coba Ruins Bike Tour: This tour takes you on a bike ride through the jungle to the ancient Mayan city of Coba, where you can climb to the top of a pyramid for stunning views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tulum

If you’re planning to spend a week in Tulum, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Tulum Ruins and Tulum Beach
2 Visit the Sian Ka’an Biosphere Reserve and Gran Cenote
3 Take a snorkeling tour to the Mesoamerican Reef
4 Go on a Mayan Jungle Experience tour
5 Relax on the beach and enjoy the local food and drink scene
6 Take a bike tour to the ancient Mayan city of Coba

Food and Drink Scene of Tulum

Tulum is known for its unique and delicious cuisine, which blends traditional Mayan flavors with modern techniques. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: Freshly caught fish marinated in lime juice, served with onions, tomatoes, and chili peppers.
  • Tacos al Pastor: Pork marinated in achiote sauce, served on a corn tortilla with pineapple, onions, and cilantro.
  • Pibil: Slow-roasted pork or chicken, marinated in achiote and sour orange juice and wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: A sweet rice milk beverage flavored with cinnamon and vanilla.
  • Agua de Jamaica: A refreshing hibiscus tea served over ice.
  • Mezcal: A smoky and flavorful liquor made from agave.

Where to Stay in Tulum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tulum offers a variety of accommodations to fit every budget and preference. Here are some options:

  • Posada Yum Kin: This boutique hotel offers bright and spacious rooms and suites, as well as a stunning pool and outdoor lounge area.
  • Che Tulum Hostel and Bar: This affordable hostel is located near the beach and offers both private and dormitory-style rooms.
  • Casa Santiago: This charming guesthouse features traditional Mayan architecture and a peaceful garden setting.

What to Buy in Tulum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and authentic souvenirs to bring home from Tulum, here are some ideas:

  • Mexican Folk Art: Tulum is home to many talented artisans who create beautiful pottery, textiles, and other crafts.
  • Mezcal: Bring home a bottle of this iconic Mexican liquor to enjoy with friends and family.
  • Mayan Hammocks: These handmade hammocks are not only comfortable and durable, but also a beautiful example of traditional Mayan craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Tulum

Tulum has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by both Mayan and Spanish traditions. Here are some customs and local practices to be aware of:

  • Respect for Nature: Tulum is known for its pristine natural beauty, and locals take great pride in preserving the environment.
  • Language: While Spanish is the official language of Mexico, many locals in Tulum also speak Maya, the language of the ancient Mayan civilization.
  • Religion: The majority of Tulum’s population is Catholic, and religion plays an important role in many aspects of daily life.

Best Time to Visit Tulum

The best time to visit Tulum is from November to April, when the weather is dry and warm, with average temperatures around 80°F. The rainy season runs from May to October, but even during this time, the weather is generally pleasant, with occasional brief downpours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tulum

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Tulum:

Do:

  • Try the Local Food and Drinks: Tulum is known for its unique cuisine and delicious cocktails, so be sure to try as many different dishes and drinks as possible.
  • Respect the Environment: Tulum is a protected natural area, so be sure to follow all rules and regulations regarding trash disposal, use of natural resources, and preservation of the fauna and flora.
  • Explore Beyond the Tourist Areas: While there are plenty of popular attractions in Tulum, don’t be afraid to venture off the beaten path to discover hidden gems and local favorites.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: While the water in Tulum is generally safe, it’s still advisable to stick to bottled water to avoid any potential stomach issues.
  • Touch the Wildlife: Tulum is home to a variety of wildlife, including monkeys, crocodiles, and sea turtles. While it’s tempting to get close for a photo, it’s important to keep a safe distance and respect the animals’ natural habitat.
  • Ignore the Local Customs: Tulum has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions, such as dress codes at religious sites and respect for the environment.

Safety and Health

Tulum is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips:

Safety in Tulum

  • Keep Your Valuables Safe: Petty theft can occur, so be sure to keep your valuables, including cash and passports, in a secure location.
  • Stay Alert at Night: While Tulum is generally safe, it’s always a good idea to be cautious at night and stick to well-lit areas.
  • Use Reliable Transportation: Stick to registered taxis or transportation services to avoid any potential scams or safety risks.

Health in Tulum

  • Drink Bottled Water: While the tap water in Tulum is generally safe, it’s still advisable to drink bottled water to avoid any potential stomach issues.
  • Protect Yourself from the Sun: Tulum can get very hot and sunny, so be sure to wear sunscreen, a hat, and lightweight clothing.
  • Be Aware of Food Safety: Stick to reputable restaurants and avoid street food to minimize the risk of foodborne illness.

Getting to Tulum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tulum is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some options:

Airports Near Tulum

  • Cancun International Airport: The closest airport to Tulum, located about 90 minutes north by car.

Bus Stations Near Tulum

  • ADO Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Tulum, with regular service to Cancun, Playa del Carmen, and other destinations in the area.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Renting a car can be a great way to explore Tulum and the surrounding areas at your own pace.
  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available and can be a good option for shorter trips within Tulum.

How to Get Around Tulum

Tulum is a relatively small town, and many attractions are within walking or biking distance. Taxis are also widely available, as are local buses and colectivos, which are shared vans that run along specific routes and are an inexpensive way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Tulum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tulum is located in a prime location for exploring other popular destinations in the area. Here are some options:

  • Cozumel: Take a ferry from Playa del Carmen to this popular island destination, known for its incredible snorkeling and diving opportunities.
  • Chichen Itza: This ancient Mayan city is located a few hours’ drive from Tulum and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Isla Mujeres: This charming island off the coast of Cancun is known for its stunning beaches and laid-back vibe.

Conclusion

Tulum is a beautiful and unique destination that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, adventure, or simply relaxing on the beach, Tulum has it all. We hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your trip to this incredible destination. Safe travels!
