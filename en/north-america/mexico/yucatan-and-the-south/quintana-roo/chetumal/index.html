Introduction to Chetumal

Located in the southernmost part of Mexico, Chetumal is the capital of the state of Quintana Roo. Dubbed as the “Gateway to Belize,” Chetumal is a popular entry point for those traveling to Belize from Mexico. Despite its relatively small size, Chetumal boasts a rich history and culture, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Chetumal

Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s rich history and culture, or simply want to unwind amidst nature, Chetumal has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in Chetumal:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Mayan Culture: This museum offers a comprehensive overview of the Mayan civilization and their contributions to the region’s history and culture.
  • Museum of the City: Learn about Chetumal’s fascinating history through its exhibits and displays in this museum located in the former governor’s palace.
  • Old Railway Station: Once a bustling railway station, this historic site has been restored into a tourist attraction and now houses various small handicraft markets and shops.

Natural Attractions of Chetumal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bacalar Lagoon: Known as the “Lagoon of Seven Colors,” Bacalar Lagoon is a must-visit for anyone visiting Chetumal. Take a dip in its crystal-clear waters and admire the stunning natural surroundings.
  • Chetumal Bay: This bay is known for its vibrant marine life, making it a great spot for snorkeling and diving.
  • Cayos Cochinos: Located near the border with Belize, this archipelago of islands offers breathtaking views and is a popular spot for camping and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chetumal

Chetumal offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best:

  • Mayan Route Bike Tour: This bike tour takes you through the charming town of Calderitas, the ancient Mayan ruins of Oxtankah, and the beautiful Bacalar Lagoon.
  • Hiking in Bacalar: Explore the natural beauty of Bacalar while hiking through its lush jungles and spotting exotic wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Chetumal: Activities and Tours

Chetumal offers a plethora of activities and tours for travelers of all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Chetumal:

  • Explore the city: Take a walk through the historic downtown area to admire the charming colonial-era architecture and colorful street art.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Go on a snorkeling or diving tour to discover the vibrant marine life of Chetumal Bay.
  • Mayan Ruins Tour: Visit the ancient Mayan ruins of Oxtankah situated just outside the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chetumal

Day 1-2: Explore downtown Chetumal and visit the Museum of Mayan Culture.

Day 3-4: Spend a day relaxing at Bacalar Lagoon, followed by a Mayan Route Bike Tour the next day.

Day 5: Go on a diving or snorkeling tour of Chetumal Bay.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the Mayan ruins of Oxtankah.

Day 7: Spend the day hiking in Cayos Cochinos.

Food and Drink Scene of Chetumal

No trip to Chetumal is complete without trying its delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: A popular seafood dish that consists of fresh raw fish marinated in lime juice and seasoned with chili peppers and onions.
  • Panuchos: Traditional Mayan dish consisting of tortillas stuffed with refried beans, meat (usually chicken or pork), and topped with avocado and pickled onions.
  • Sopa de Lima: This soup is made with shredded chicken, lime juice, and a variety of vegetables and spices, making it a perfect dish for colder weather days.

Local Beverages

  • Xtabentun: A traditional Mayan liquor made with honey, anisette, and fermented anise.
  • Agua de Chaya: A refreshing drink made with chaya, a leafy green vegetable with medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Chetumal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chetumal offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the recommended hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Hotel Villanueva: Affordable hotel located in the heart of downtown Chetumal.
  • Chetumal Hostel: A clean and comfortable hostel offering both dorms and private rooms.
  • Villas Bambu by the Bay: A beautiful guesthouse located in a quiet neighborhood, offering stunning views of the lagoon.

What to Buy in Chetumal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chetumal offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Some of the best items to buy include:

  • Handmade Hammocks: Chetumal is known for its beautiful, intricately woven hammocks. These make for great souvenirs and gifts for loved ones.
  • Ceramic Products: From traditional pottery to hand-painted tiles and decorative figurines, Chetumal offers a wide range of locally made ceramic products.

Local Culture and Customs of Chetumal

Chetumal is a multicultural city with a rich history and a vibrant local culture. Some customs and traditions that you may encounter during your visit include:

  • Dia de Muertos: The Day of the Dead is a popular Mexican holiday celebrated on November 1st and 2nd. During this time, locals honor their deceased loved ones by creating colorful altars and offering food and drink to their spirits.
  • Mayan Culture: Chetumal is located in an area with a strong Mayan influence, and as such, visitors can observe various customs and traditions unique to Mayan culture.

Best Time to Visit Chetumal

The best time to visit Chetumal is during the dry season, which falls between November and April. During this time, the weather is cooler and dryer, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chetumal

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash, as not all establishments accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended.
  • Don’t take photos of religious ceremonies or local people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chetumal

Chetumal is considered to be a safe place to travel to. However, as with any destination, visitors should exercise common sense and take necessary precautions to stay safe.

Health in Chetumal

Visitors to Chetumal should take necessary precautions to avoid contracting mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue and Zika. It is also recommended to only drink bottled water and avoid consuming street food to avoid digestive issues.

Getting to Chetumal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chetumal is easily accessible by air, land, and sea. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Chetumal

The Chetumal International Airport is located just outside the city and offers daily flights to various domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Chetumal

The Chetumal Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers daily bus services to various parts of Mexico.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can also travel to Chetumal by car or boat from nearby destinations like Belize and Guatemala.

How to Get Around in Chetumal

Chetumal is a relatively small city and is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer motorized transportation.

Where to Go Next from Chetumal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chetumal is surrounded by various exciting destinations and excursion options. Some recommended places to visit next include:

  • Belize: Just across the border, Belize is known for its stunning natural landscapes, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine.
  • Tulum: Located just a few hours away, Tulum is known for its stunning beaches and Mayan ruins.
  • Cozumel: A popular destination for diving and snorkeling, Cozumel is an island located off the coast of Mexico’s Yucatan Peninsula.

Conclusion

Chetumal is a charming city with a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant culture. From exploring its historic museums and sites to indulging in its delicious cuisine and outdoor activities, there’s something for everyone in Chetumal.
