Akumal is an incredible destination located in Quintana Roo, in Yucatán and the South, in Mexico, in North America. This small beach town offers breathtaking natural scenery, amazing cultural attractions, excellent cuisine, and a unique local culture. If you’re planning a trip to Akumal, this comprehensive guide is a must-read. Here, you’ll discover everything you need to know to make the most of your visit, from the best places to visit to the most exciting things to do, and much more.

Introduction to Akumal

Akumal is a small coastal town located in the heart of the Riviera Maya. Its name means “Place of the Turtles” in the Mayan language, and it’s considered one of the best snorkeling and diving destinations in Mexico.

The town is home to a variety of restaurants, bars, and accommodation options, ranging from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels. It’s also conveniently close to several iconic attractions in the Yucatan region, including the Tulum ruins, Sian Ka’an Biosphere Reserve, and Xcaret Eco Park.

List of Places to Visit in Akumal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Akumal Monkey Sanctuary: This wildlife sanctuary is home to a variety of rescued animals, including monkeys, macaws, deer, and more.
  • San Francisco de Asis Church: A beautiful historic church located in the heart of Akumal
  • Tulum Archaeological Site: A must-visit for anyone interested in history and culture. These iconic ruins are located just a short drive from Akumal.

Natural Attractions of Akumal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yal-Ku Lagoon: A stunning lagoon with crystal clear water, perfect for snorkeling and swimming.
  • Akumal Beach: This beautiful beach is home to a large population of sea turtles and is a great place to go snorkeling.
  • Aktun Chen Natural Park: A wonderful park where you can hike through the jungle, swim in underground rivers, and explore caves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akumal

  • Akumal Ecological Center: Offers guided hikes and bike tours to explore the local flora and fauna.
  • Tres Rios Nature Park: A fantastic place to go hiking, this nature park offers breathtaking views of the coastline and jungle.

Best Things to Do in Akumal: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Akumal is one of the best places in the world to go snorkeling and diving.
  • Swimming with Sea Turtles: You can swim with these gentle creatures just off the coast of Akumal.
  • Cenote Diving: Cenotes are natural sinkholes filled with crystal clear water, and Akumal has several of the most beautiful ones in Mexico.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akumal

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Akumal, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Akumal Beach and swim with the sea turtles.
  • Day 2: Visit the Tulum ruins and shop at the local markets.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Yal-Ku Lagoon and explore the local wildlife.
  • Day 4: Take a guided bike tour of the Akumal Ecological Center.
  • Day 5: Go diving in one of the local cenotes.
  • Day 6: Visit the San Francisco de Asis Church and learn about the local culture.
  • Day 7: Relax at a local spa and enjoy some delicious Mexican cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Akumal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ceviche: Fresh seafood marinated in citrus juice and spices.
  • Tacos al Pastor: Grilled pork tacos served with fresh pineapple and cilantro.
  • Chiles en Nogada: A traditional Mexican dish made with stuffed peppers, fruit, and a walnut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Margaritas: The classic Mexican cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec.
  • Horchata: A sweet and refreshing drink made with rice and cinnamon.
  • Mexican beer: Try one of the many delicious Mexican beers, like Dos Equis or Modelo.

Where to Stay in Akumal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Secrets Akumal Riviera Maya: A luxury, adults-only resort with beautiful views of the ocean.
  • Akumal Bay Beach and Wellness Resort: A family-friendly resort with lots of amenities.
  • Hostel Che: A budget-friendly option for travelers looking for a hostel experience.

What to Buy in Akumal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade jewelry: Akumal is known for its beautiful handmade jewelry made by local artisans.
  • Mayan handicrafts: Look for handmade pottery, woven textiles, and other traditional Mayan crafts.
  • Tequila and Mezcal: Take home a bottle of Mexico’s famous spirits as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Akumal

When visiting Akumal, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and culture. Some things to keep in mind include dressing conservatively when visiting churches and other cultural attractions, and avoiding public displays of affection. Additionally, tipping is customary in restaurants and for tour guides.

Best Time to Visit Akumal

The best time to visit Akumal is during the dry season, from November to April. The weather is warm and dry during this time, and there are fewer crowds than during the peak summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akumal

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take advantage of the amazing outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Get too drunk or rowdy in public.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or other cultural sites.
  • Hesitate to ask for help if you need it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akumal

Akumal is generally a safe destination, but it’s important to use common sense and take precautions like not leaving valuables unattended on the beach. Additionally, it’s important to pay attention to local weather warnings, as hurricanes and other natural disasters can occur in the region.

Health in Akumal

Visitors to Akumal should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as there is a risk of Zika and other mosquito-borne illnesses in the area. It’s also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Akumal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akumal

The nearest international airport is Cancun International Airport, which is located approximately 1.5 hours from Akumal by car.

Bus Stations Near Akumal

The ADO bus company operates regular routes to and from Akumal, with buses departing from major cities and towns throughout the region.

Other Options

Many visitors to Akumal choose to rent a car, which provides greater flexibility for exploring the area.

How to Get Around in Akumal

Taxis are widely available in Akumal, and there are also local buses that connect the town with other destinations in the region. Biking and walking are also popular options for getting around town.

Where to Go Next from Akumal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tulum: Just a short drive from Akumal, the Tulum ruins are a must-visit for anyone interested in history and culture.
  • Sian Ka’an Biosphere Reserve: A stunning nature reserve that’s perfect for hiking, birdwatching, and other outdoor activities.
  • Playa del Carmen: This vibrant beach town is just a short drive from Akumal and is known for its excellent nightlife and shopping.

Conclusion

Akumal is an amazing destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or just relaxing on the beach, this small coastal town has it all.
