San Cristóbal de las Casas is a charming town in the southern Mexican state of Chiapas. With its rich indigenous heritage, it is a cultural hub, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic travel experience. In this travel guide, we will show you the best places to visit, things to do, local food and drinks to try, where to stay, culture and customs, and essential safety and health information.

Introduction to San Cristóbal de las Casas

San Cristóbal de las Casas is a colonial town with colorful buildings, cobblestone streets, and a vibrant cultural scene. It is located in the highlands of Chiapas, where the Mayan culture is still alive and prevalent. It is a perfect place to explore old churches, learn about the indigenous people, go on outdoor adventures, and appreciate the local food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in San Cristóbal de las Casas

There are so many places to visit in San Cristóbal de las Casas, here are some of the must-sees:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de La Ciudad: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of San Cristóbal de las Casas.
  • Museo del Jade: A museum showcasing the history and significance of Jade in the Mayan culture.
  • Templo de Santo Domingo: A beautiful church built in the 16th century.
  • Centro Cultural de los Altos de Chiapas: A cultural center where you can learn about the local indigenous people’s history and the region’s flora and fauna.
  • Na Bolom Cultural Center: An institution that was formerly the home of Frans Blom and Gertrude Duby, who are known for exploring and documenting the Lacandon culture and preserving the region’s biodiversity.

Natural Attractions of San Cristóbal de las Casas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Arcotete: A natural reserve known for its rock formations, hiking trails, and caves.
  • Cascadas El Chiflón: A series of waterfalls that can be reached through a hiking trail.
  • Lagos de Montebello National Park: A park consisting of over 50 lakes and lagoons, each with a unique color.
  • Cañón del Sumidero: A canyon with cliffs that rise as high as 1,000 meters and boasts scenery that is perfect for boat rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Cristóbal de las Casas

San Cristóbal de las Casas is a destination that caters well to hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Hike to the top of Cerro Huitepec: A good place for hiking and bird watching.
  • Bike to the nearby indigenous village of San Juan Chamula and learn about their unique culture and traditions.

Best Things to Do in San Cristóbal de las Casas: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of San Cristóbal de las Casas to learn about its colonial history and local culture.
  • Take an adventure tour that includes activities like zip-lining, rappelling, and rock climbing.
  • Plan a day trip to nearby indigenous villages to experience their customs and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Cristóbal de las Casas

To make the most of your visit to San Cristóbal de las Casas, we suggest a week-long itinerary, including:

  • Day 1-4: Explore San Cristóbal de las Casas: Visit museums, attend cultural events, go shopping, and try out local foods and drinks.
  • Day 5: Hike to Cerro Huitepec, a glorious trail dotted with birds, flowers, and stunning vistas. Spend the rest of the day relaxing.
  • Day 6: Take an adventure tour that includes zip-lining and other outdoor activities.
  • Day 7: Visit indigenous villages around San Cristóbal de las Casas and learn about their traditions and way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of San Cristóbal de las Casas

San Cristóbal de las Casas has a diverse food and drink scene, ranging from local tacos to international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: Corn masa stuffed with various fillings such as chicken, pork, cheese, and vegetables.
  • Cochinita Pibil: Slow-roasted pork wrapped in banana leaves and served with corn tortillas and pickled onions.
  • Pozol: A traditional drink made with corn, cacao, and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: A distilled liquor made from the agave plant.
  • Café de Olla: A traditional Mexican spiced coffee flavored with cinnamon and piloncillo (unrefined cane sugar).

Where to Stay in San Cristóbal de las Casas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Cristóbal de las Casas is known for its charming accommodations. Here are the best options:

  • Hotel Bo: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Hostal La Isla: A budget-friendly hostel located in the city center.
  • Casa Lum: A boutique guesthouse located on a quiet street in San Cristóbal de las Casas.

What to Buy in San Cristóbal de las Casas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Cristóbal de las Casas is known for its handicrafts and unique souvenirs. Here are some items you should consider bringing back home:

  • Hand-woven textiles: You can find colorful garments, accessories, and home goods made using traditional techniques.
  • Amber jewelry: San Cristóbal de las Casas is known for its high-quality, hand-crafted silver jewelry with amber accents.
  • Coffee: Chiapas is one of Mexico’s top coffee producers, and San Cristóbal de las Casas has some of the best roasteries in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of San Cristóbal de las Casas

San Cristóbal de las Casas is a melting pot of Spanish, Mayan, and contemporary Mexican culture. To respect the local culture and customs, we advise:

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting churches and sacred indigenous sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of indigenous people or their belongings.

Best Time to Visit San Cristóbal de las Casas

The best time to visit San Cristóbal de las Casas is during the dry season, which runs from December to April. The weather is mild, and there’s not much rain, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Cristóbal de las Casas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting San Cristóbal de las Casas:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Learn a few words in Spanish to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Assume the indigenous people are performers or part of a theme park.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or sacred sites.

Safety and Health

San Cristóbal de las Casas is generally safe, but as with any tourist destination, it is essential to maintain caution. Here’s what you need to know about safety and health:

Safety in San Cristóbal de las Casas

  • Take a licensed taxi or go with a reputable tour operator when wandering about town.
  • Use a money belt to protect your valuables.
  • Do not leave your belongings unattended.

Health in San Cristóbal de las Casas

  • Drink bottled or purified water.
  • Use mosquito repellents to avoid getting bitten.
  • Visit a doctor before traveling and get necessary vaccinations if required.

Getting to San Cristóbal de las Casas: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Cristóbal de las Casas can be reached by bus, car or plane.

Airports Near San Cristóbal de las Casas

  • The nearest airport is Angel Albino Corzo International Airport, located in Tuxtla Gutiérrez, which is about 77 kilometers (48 miles) away from San Cristóbal de las Casas.

Bus Stations Near San Cristóbal de las Casas

  • Terminal OCC: The main bus station located in downtown San Cristóbal de las Casas.

Other Options

  • Private car rental: You can rent a car in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in San Cristóbal de las Casas

San Cristóbal de las Casas is a walking-friendly town, but there are various options available for transportation:

  • Taxis: Available throughout the city.
  • Colectivos: Shared taxis that run on fixed routes.
  • Buses: Both local and regional buses are available.

Where to Go Next from San Cristóbal de las Casas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Cristóbal de las Casas is an excellent place to base yourself to explore the nearby destinations:

  • Palenque: A UNESCO World Heritage site that features Mayan ruins, temples, and pyramids.
  • Agua Azul Waterfalls: A series of tiered waterfalls with crystal-clear blue water.
  • Ocosingo: A small town known for its indigenous crafts and delicious local food.

Conclusion

San Cristóbal de las Casas is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and unique cuisine, there’s something for everyone here. Take this travel guide with you when exploring the town to ensure you have an unforgettable experience.
