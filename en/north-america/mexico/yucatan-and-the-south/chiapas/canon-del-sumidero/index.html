Introduction to Cañón del Sumidero

Cañón del Sumidero is a natural wonder and a must-see destination for anyone traveling to Chiapas, Mexico. This stunning canyon runs through the Grijalva River and is surrounded by abundant wildlife, towering cliffs, and breathtaking waterfalls. It’s located in the Yucatán and South region of Mexico and is easily accessible from several major cities.

Visitors to Cañón del Sumidero can not only enjoy the beauty of nature but also take part in cultural and adventure activities. From visiting museums to hiking through lush forests, there is something for everyone in this majestic region.

List of Places to Visit in Cañón del Sumidero

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sumidero Canyon National Park: located on the outskirts of Tuxtla Gutiérrez, this park offers several trails for visitors to explore the canyon.
  • Chiapa de Corzo: a charming colonial town with notable architecture and a central square, offering insight into the region’s history.
  • El Palacio de Gobierno: this colonial-style building, located in Tuxtla Gutiérrez, serves as the governor’s office and features beautiful murals depicting the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Cañón del Sumidero: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chorreadero Waterfall: located near Tuxtla Gutiérrez, this waterfall offers a beautiful and refreshing place to swim and relax.
  • La Venta Museum: located in Villahermosa, this museum is dedicated to the pre-Hispanic Olmec civilization.
  • Boating on the Grijalva River: visitors can take a boat tour to explore the canyon and its surrounding scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cañón del Sumidero

There are many hiking and biking routes in Cañón del Sumidero where visitors can take in breathtaking views and experience the region’s unique flora and fauna. Some popular routes include:

  • Mirador de los Chiapa: a steep hike with a rewarding view of the canyon from the top.
  • El Chorreadero: a beautiful route that leads to the Chorreadero waterfall.
  • Ecological Reserve La Pera: a nature reserve featuring hiking trails to explore the canyon and its surrounding creatures.

Best Things to Do in Cañón del Sumidero: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour through the canyon to see its towering cliffs up-close.
  • Visit the Sumidero Canyon National Park to explore its many trails and enjoy its natural beauty.
  • Learn about the region’s culture at the La Venta Museum.

  • Take some time to relax and enjoy the amenities of your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Cañón del Sumidero

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: a traditional corn-based dish filled with meat or vegetables.
  • Pescado a la Talla: grilled fish with smoky and spicy flavors.
  • Pozol: a refreshing and unique beverage made from corn, cocoa, and water.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: a traditional distilled spirit made from the agave plant.
  • Chiapas coffee: a high-quality coffee with a unique flavor and aroma.
  • Tejate: a refreshing beverage made from corn, cacao, and other ingredients.

Where to Stay in Cañón del Sumidero: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Cañón del Sumidero, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some recommended options include:

  • Hotel Cañón del Sumidero: a comfortable and affordable hotel with stunning views of the canyon.
  • Casa Mexicana: a cozy and charming guesthouse in Tuxtla Gutiérrez.
  • Marriott Bonvoy: an upscale hotel with a serene and relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Cañón del Sumidero: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handwoven textiles: these unique and colorful textiles are made by local artisans and are a perfect souvenir to bring home.
  • Chocolate: Chiapas is famous for its high-quality chocolate, and visitors can stock up on bars and other chocolate products.
  • Mezcal: a bottle of mezcal from one of the region’s distilleries is a unique and delicious souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Cañón del Sumidero

The local culture of Cañón del Sumidero is strongly influenced by the region’s indigenous communities and colonial heritage. Visitors should respect local customs, such as dressing modestly and asking for permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Cañón del Sumidero

The best time to visit Cañón del Sumidero is during the dry season, from late October to early May. During this time, the weather is mild and comfortable, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without the risk of rain or flooding. However, the region can be visited year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cañón del Sumidero

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Pack light and comfortable clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and a reusable water bottle.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people or religious sites without permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Litter or damage natural or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cañón del Sumidero

Cañón del Sumidero is generally a safe and welcoming destination for visitors, but it’s important to take some basic safety precautions. Visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas and avoid walking alone at night. In addition, visitors should exercise caution when participating in adventure activities, such as hiking or boating.

Health in Cañón del Sumidero

Visitors to Cañón del Sumidero should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and consider getting vaccinated against Hepatitis A, typhoid, and rabies. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Cañón del Sumidero: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cañón del Sumidero

The nearest international airport to Cañón del Sumidero is located in Tuxtla Gutiérrez.

Bus Stations Near Cañón del Sumidero

Tuxtla Gutiérrez has bus connections to several nearby cities, including San Cristobal de las Casas and Palenque.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Cañón del Sumidero

The best way to get around Cañón del Sumidero is by car, but visitors can also use taxis or public transportation to get around.

Where to Go Next from Cañón del Sumidero: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Cristobal de las Casas: a charming colonial town located in the highlands of Chiapas.
  • Palenque: an ancient Mayan city with impressive ruins and archaeological sites.

Conclusion

Cañón del Sumidero is an extraordinary destination that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural richness, and adventure activities.
