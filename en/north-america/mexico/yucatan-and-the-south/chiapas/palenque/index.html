Palenque Travel Guide: Discover this Ancient City Nestled in the Jungle

Palenque is a city in the state of Chiapas, Mexico, known for its beautiful natural scenery, ancient Maya ruins, and rich cultural heritage. Nestled in the heart of the jungle, Palenque offers visitors a unique mix of history, culture, and adventure.

If you’re planning a trip to Palenque, read our comprehensive travel guide to discover the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more!

List of Places to Visit in Palenque

Palenque is a city rich in history and culture, offering visitors a plethora of places to visit. Here are some of the must-see attractions in Palenque:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palenque Archaeological Site: The Palenque Archaeological Site is the main attraction in Palenque, featuring Maya ruins dating back to the 7th century. The site is surrounded by lush jungle and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Museo de Sitio de Palenque: Located near the Palenque Archaeological Site, the Museo de Sitio de Palenque is a museum dedicated to the history and culture of the Maya civilization. It features a collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the ancient Maya city of Palenque.

  • Templo del Sol: This temple is part of the Palenque Archaeological Site and is dedicated to Kinich Ahau, the Maya sun god. It features impressive carvings and bas-reliefs.

Natural Attractions of Palenque: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Agua Azul Waterfalls: The Agua Azul Waterfalls are a series of cascading waterfalls located about an hour’s drive from Palenque. The water is a bright blue color, making them a popular spot for swimming and relaxing.

  • Misol-Ha Waterfall: Another popular waterfall near Palenque is the Misol-Ha Waterfall. It drops 35 meters into a natural pool and is surrounded by jungle, making it a great spot for photos and relaxation.

  • Palenque National Park: Palenque National Park is a protected area of jungle and forests located near the Palenque Archaeological Site. It’s home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including jaguars, pumas, and howler monkeys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palenque

Palenque’s beautiful natural scenery is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Jungle Trail to El Panchan: This route takes you through the jungle and ends at El Panchan, a small village near the Palenque Archaeological Site known for its eco-lodges and restaurants.

  • Sendero Jaguar: The Sendero Jaguar is a hiking trail that takes you through Palenque National Park, offering beautiful views of the jungle and the ancient Maya ruins.

Best Things to Do in Palenque: Activities and Tours

Palenque offers visitors a range of activities and tours to explore its rich culture and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Palenque:

  • Sunrise Tour of the Palenque Archaeological Site: Take a guided tour of the Palenque Archaeological Site at sunrise to avoid crowds and experience the ruins in a different light.

  • Rafting Trip on the Usumacinta River: The Usumacinta River is the largest river in Mexico and offers visitors a thrilling rafting trip through the jungle and rapids.

  • Cultural Tour of San Cristobal de las Casas: Take a day trip to San Cristobal de las Casas, a charming colonial city known for its vibrant culture and indigenous crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Palenque

Palenque’s food and drink scene is a mix of traditional Mexican cuisine and regional specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: Tamales are a traditional Mexican dish made of masa (corn dough) that’s steamed or boiled in a corn husk. They can be filled with a variety of meats, vegetables, and sauces.

  • Chilaquiles: Chilaquiles are a breakfast dish made of fried tortilla chips that are simmered in a sauce and topped with cheese, beans, and eggs.

  • Pozol: Pozol is a traditional Maya beverage made of ground corn, cocoa, and water. It’s often served with bananas or other fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Mezcal: Mezcal is a distilled alcoholic beverage made from the agave plant. It’s similar to tequila but has a smokier, more complex flavor.

  • Cerveza Chiapaneca: Cerveza Chiapaneca is a local beer produced in the state of Chiapas. It’s a light and refreshing beer that pairs well with spicy food.

Where to Stay in Palenque: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palenque offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. Some of the best places to stay in Palenque include:

  • Hotel Chablis Palenque: This hotel is located near the Palenque Archaeological Site and features a swimming pool, restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.

  • El Panchan: El Panchan is a village near the Palenque Archaeological Site known for its eco-lodges and restaurants. It’s a great place to stay if you’re looking for a unique and rustic experience.

  • Mayabell: Mayabell is a budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Palenque. It features dorm rooms and private rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Palenque: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palenque is known for its traditional crafts and textiles, including ceremonial masks, pottery, and woven baskets. Some of the best places to buy souvenirs in Palenque include:

  • Mercado de Artesanias: This market is located near the Palenque Archaeological Site and features a variety of crafts and textiles made by local artisans.

  • Tienda de Artesanias: Tienda de Artesanias is a shop located in downtown Palenque that features a range of traditional crafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Palenque

Palenque is a city rich in history and culture, with a population that’s largely Maya. In Palenque, it’s important to respect local customs and etiquette, including:

  • Dress Modestly: Palenque is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Respect Cultural Norms: The Maya culture is deeply rooted in tradition and ceremony. It’s important to respect local customs and cultural norms when visiting Palenque.

Best Time to Visit Palenque

The best time to visit Palenque is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is warm and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the city. The rainy season is from May to October and can make hiking and outdoor activities more difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palenque

When visiting Palenque, it’s important to follow some basic do’s and don’ts to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Do:

  • Carry Cash: Many businesses in Palenque, especially smaller ones, do not accept credit cards. Be sure to carry cash for purchases and tips.

  • Learn Some Spanish: While many people in Palenque speak English, it’s helpful to know some basic Spanish to communicate with locals and navigate the city.

Don’t:

  • Flash Valuables: Palenque is a relatively safe city, but it’s still important to be cautious and avoid flashing valuables like cameras and smartphones.

  • Drink Tap Water: The tap water in Palenque is not safe to drink. Stick to bottled water and avoid ice cubes in drinks.

Safety and Health

Palenque is a safe city, but it’s still important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health during your trip.

Safety in Palenque

  • Stay in Well-Lit Areas: Palenque is a safe city, but it’s still important to stay in well-lit areas, especially at night.

  • Protect Your Valuables: Be cautious with your belongings in crowded areas, and don’t leave them unattended.

Health in Palenque

  • Drink Bottled Water: The tap water in Palenque is not safe to drink. Stick to bottled water and avoid ice cubes in drinks.

  • Protect Against Mosquitoes: Palenque is located in a tropical area, so it’s important to protect against mosquito bites to prevent diseases like dengue and Zika.

Getting to Palenque: Transportation Options and Logistics

Palenque is located in the state of Chiapas, about 130 kilometers from the city of San Cristobal de las Casas. Here are some transportation options and logistics for getting to and around Palenque:

Airports Near Palenque

  • Palenque International Airport: This airport is located about 5 kilometers outside of Palenque and offers domestic flights within Mexico.

Bus Stations Near Palenque

  • ADO Bus Station: This bus station is located in downtown Palenque and offers connections to cities throughout Mexico.

  • Comitan de Dominguez Bus Station: This bus station is located about 130 kilometers from Palenque and offers connections to cities throughout Mexico.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental is a popular option for visitors to Palenque, especially those looking to explore the surrounding areas at their own pace.

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Palenque and are a convenient way to get around the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Palenque

Palenque is a small city that’s easy to navigate on foot or by taxi. Taxis are readily available and are a convenient way to get around the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Palenque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palenque is located in the state of Chiapas, a region known for its beautiful natural scenery and rich cultural heritage. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • San Cristobal de las Casas: This charming colonial city is located about 130 kilometers from Palenque and is known for its vibrant culture and indigenous crafts.

  • Sumidero Canyon: This canyon is located about 180 kilometers from Palenque and offers beautiful views of the canyon and the surrounding jungle.

  • Yaxchilan: This Maya ruin site is located about 150 kilometers from Palenque and can only be accessed by boat along the Usumacinta River.

Conclusion

Palenque is a city that’s rich in history, culture, and adventure. Whether you’re exploring ancient Maya ruins, hiking through the jungle, or trying local cuisine, Palenque offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. With our comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to make the most of your trip to this beautiful city.
