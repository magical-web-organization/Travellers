Introduction to Catazajá

Catazajá is a small town located in the state of Chiapas, in Yucatán and the South region of Mexico. It is surrounded by lush rainforests, rivers, and natural reserves, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. If you’re looking for a place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse yourself in nature and culture, Catazajá is the perfect spot. In this guide, we’ll share our top recommendations for places to visit, activities to do, and where to stay and eat.

List of Places to Visit in Catazajá

Here are the top attractions that you should add to your itinerary when visiting Catazajá.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Site of Palenque: This ancient Mayan city is located about 2.5 hours from Catazajá and is one of the most impressive archaeological sites in Mexico.
  • Museum of the Lacandón Maya: Learn about the lifestyle and customs of the Lacandón Maya, a local indigenous group, at this museum located in the nearby town of Naha.

Natural Attractions of Catazajá: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palenque National Park: This park is home to the stunning Palenque Archaeological site, as well as waterfalls, caves, and an incredible array of wildlife.
  • Bonampak: A lesser-known Mayan archaeological site that boasts some of the most incredibly well-preserved vibrant frescos in Mexico.
  • Cascades of Agua Azul: These turquoise waters cascading down the cliffsides are a photographer’s dream.
  • Lacanja Chansayab: A local reserve perfect for bird watching and adventure activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Catazajá

Catazajá is a paradise for hiking and biking lovers. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • La Venta River Loop
  • Lacanjá-Chansayab Reserve Loop

Best Things to Do in Catazajá: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Lacanja and Azul rivers
  • Kayaking at Cascadas Agua Azul
  • Zip-lining in the jungle
  • Horseback riding tours to Naha to visit the Palenque Museum of the Lacandón Maya

Week-Long Itinerary for Catazajá

Day 1: Arrival in Catazajá Day 2-3: Explore Palenque National Park and visit the Palenque Archaeological Site Day 4: Hiking or Biking on the trails of the La Venta River Loop Day 5: Excursion to Cascadas Agua Azul Day 6: Rafting or kayaking on Lacanja or Azul rivers Day 7: Horseback riding to the Museum of the Lacandón Maya

Food and Drink Scene of Catazajá

When in Catazajá, don’t miss out on these delicious local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: A classic Meso-American dish made of masa dough and stuffed with filling, then steamed in corn husks or banana leaves.
  • Chocholá Cheese: A local cheese made from cow’s milk that’s perfect for snacking or melting over dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Pozol: A popular drink made from fermented corn dough and cocoa.
  • Xtabentún: A traditional Mayan liqueur made with anise seed and honey.

Where to Stay in Catazajá: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are the top-rated accommodations in Catazajá:

  • Hotel Boutique Quinta Chanabnal: A luxurious jungle retreat near the Palenque Archaeological Site.
  • Hotel Hacienda Tikal: A budget-friendly hotel with an outdoor pool and gardens.
  • Posada Jol Shan: A charming guesthouse surrounded by lush rainforests, with spacious rooms and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Catazajá: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Catazajá, don’t forget to buy these souvenirs and local products:

  • Handcrafted textiles: Made by local artisans using traditional methods, these colorful textiles are great for adding some Mexican flair to your home.
  • Agave-based liquor: A local specialty, purchased from the local stores or direct from the producer.

Local Culture and Customs of Catazajá

The local culture of Catazajá is a vibrant mixture of indigenous and Mexican traditions. The main language in the town is Spanish, though some people also speak various Mayan languages. The Lacandón Maya people, among other indigenous communities, have a strong presence in the area, and their traditions and customs can be seen in the local arts, food, and music.

Best Time to Visit Catazajá

The best time to visit Catazajá is between November and April, as this is the dry season when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Catazajá

Here are some tips for things to do and not do in Catazajá:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Hike the trails and explore the national parks.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that may harm the local environment or wildlife.
  • Disrespect cultural symbols or religious sites.
  • Wander around at night in poorly lit areas alone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Catazajá

Catazajá is a safe town, but tourists should still be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to avoid being the target of petty theft. Use common sense when walking around with your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or flashy jewellery.

Health in Catazajá

Make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical costs. It is important to be aware of the risks of malaria and dengue fever when visiting Catazajá. Taking precautions like using insect repellent, wearing long sleeves and pants, and sleeping under mosquito nets can help prevent these diseases.

How to Get Around in Catazajá

Transportation options for getting around Catazajá include shared taxis, motorbikes, or bicycles. Most hotels will offer hire these options, or they can be found in town.

Where to Go Next from Catazajá: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you can visit after Catazajá:

  • San Cristóbal de las Casas: A charming colonial town located about 4 hours from Catazajá, famous for its colorful architecture, markets, and handicrafts.
  • Mérida: A beautiful city located about 8 hours from Catazajá, known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning Spanish architecture.
  • Ciudad del Carmen: A port town and growing tourist destination located about 4 hours from Catazajá, known for its stunning beaches and rich history.

Conclusion

With its incredible natural beauty, fascinating cultural heritage, and fantastic outdoor adventures, Catazajá is a hidden gem just waiting to be explored. Hopefully, this comprehensive guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful region of Mexico.
