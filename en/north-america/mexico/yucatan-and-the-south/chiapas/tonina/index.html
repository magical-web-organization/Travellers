Toniná is one of the most fascinating ancient sites in Chiapas, Mexico. Located in the Yucatán Peninsula and the South region of North America, Toniná boasts breathtaking ruins, impressive pyramids, and a rich cultural heritage. In this travel guide, we will explore the hidden gem of Toniná, highlighting the best places to visit, activities to do, and attractions to see.

Introduction to Toniná

Toniná is a Mayan archaeological site that dates back to 514 A.D. It was one of the most important cities in the Maya civilization and played a significant role in the development of the region. The name Toniná means “Stone House,” and the site consists of more than 100 structures, including towering pyramids, palaces, and ball courts. The ruins at Toniná extend over an area of more than 4 kilometers, providing visitors with a unique opportunity to explore the stunning ancient architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Toniná

Visitors to Toniná can engage in a range of cultural and natural activities, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Start your Toniná exploration journey by visiting the Museum of the Site, which houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits about the Mayan civilization. The Ball Court is also a must-see attraction, where you can witness the remains of a game that was played for fun and religious ritual.

Natural Attractions of Toniná: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Toniná is surrounded by lush forest, which provides a spectacular backdrop to the stunning pyramids and structures. Hike to the top of the Toniná Pyramid for scenic views of the surrounding landscape. Alternatively, take a jungle trek and explore the Reserva Ecológica de Toniná, which is home to several species of flora and fauna, including howler monkeys and colorful birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toniná

Visitors can explore the natural beauty of Toniná through hiking and biking routes. Hike the Toniná Pyramid to reach the highest point and enjoy the panoramic vista of the surrounding landscape. Alternatively, take a bike ride and explore the Reserva Ecológica de Toniná and its various trails.

Best Things to Do in Toniná: Activities and Tours

Toniná offers visitors a range of diverse and unforgettable activities and tours to explore the site. Visitors can opt for guided tours by the knowledgeable site staff, rent bikes to explore the Reserva Ecológica de Toniná, or take a horseback riding tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Toniná

Here is a suggested itinerary to explore Toniná in a week:

  • Day 1: Visit Toniná Museum and the Ball Court
  • Day 2: Explore the Reserva Ecológica de Toniná and hike the Toniná Pyramid
  • Day 3: Take a bike tour of the Toniná ruins
  • Day 4: Explore the Palacio Mayas and the Templo de los Suenos
  • Day 5: Go horseback riding and visit the Mirador de toniná with a beautiful sunset view
  • Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Tonina ruins and temple
  • Day 7: Relax, wander, and delve into the local spirit of Toniná

Food and Drink Scene of Toniná

Toniná may not be known for its food scene, but there are some must-try local dishes and beverages that visitors should try out.

Must-Try Dishes

One dish that Tonina is famous for is Tamales, a local favorite made with masa, often mixed with meat or cheese and wrapped in a banana leaf.

Local Beverages

The local specialty is Pozol, a refreshing drink made of masa, cacao, and water or milk.

Where to Stay in Toniná: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Toniná offers a range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. For a traditional Mayan experience, stay at the Cabañas El Mirador de Toniná, a group of cabins near the site. Alternatively, consider the Hotel Toniná, a modern hotel located in downtown Toniná.

What to Buy in Toniná: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Toniná home with you by shopping for local crafts and souvenirs. Consider purchasing handmade pottery, woven textiles, and intricate beadwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Toniná

Toniná has the distinction of being one of the most traditional and authentic Mayan sites in Mexico. The local community is primarily indigenous and retains a strong sense of local culture, customs, and traditions. Respect for these customs is vital to making the most of your Tonina experience.

Best Time to Visit Toniná

Toniná can be visited year-round, but the best time to go is from November to February when the weather is mild and comfortable. It is essential to avoid the rainy season from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toniná

Here are some helpful tips for getting the most out of your Toniná trip:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Carry enough water and snacks
  • Take a guided tour of the ruins for more informative knowledge

Don’t:

  • Climb on the structures or remove any artifacts
  • Drink tap water
  • Consume alcoholic drinks in a public place
  • Ignore the safety instructions

Safety and Health

Toniná is a safe destination, but visitors should still take some precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Toniná

It is best to avoid wandering around alone at night in Toniná.

Health in Toniná

Visitors are advised to drink filtered or bottled water and avoid street food to avoid stomach-related illnesses.

Getting to Toniná: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors have several options for getting to Toniná, including:

Airports Near Toniná

The closest airport to Toniná is the Ángel Albino Corzo International Airport in Tuxtla Gutiérrez, located approximately 95 kilometers away from Toniná.

Bus Stations Near Toniná

The central bus station in Toniná is La Estación Tuxtla, which is served by several buses from neighboring states and cities of Mexico.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent cars, hire private taxis or use tour buses to travel to Toniná.

How to Get Around in Toniná

The best way to explore Toniná is by foot, bicycles, or horseback riding.

Where to Go Next from Toniná: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Toniná is a great starting point for exploring some of the other cultural and natural attractions of Mexico. You can explore the nearby Agua Azul Waterfalls, located within the Cajon del Sumidero National Park.

Conclusion

Toniná is a fantastic destination that offers an unforgettable mix of cultural and natural attractions.
