If you’re after a little slice of paradise on earth, look no further than Comitán. Nestled deep in the heart of Mexico’s beautiful Chiapas region, this stunning little town has something for everyone.

Comitán is a town known for its vibrant culture, scenic landscapes, and fascinating history. Whether you want to take in the sights and sounds of a bustling city center, explore ancient ruins, or just relax and soak up the local atmosphere, Comitán has everything you need to create the perfect vacation.

So without further ado, let’s dive into this comprehensive travel guide to Comitán, Mexico.

List of Places to Visit in Comitán

Get ready to be dazzled, because Comitán is a town bursting with must-see attractions and sights. Here’s a quick rundown of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museo Fuerte de San Carlos
  2. Museo de Arqueologia Maya
  3. Templo del Calvario
  4. Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzman

Natural Attractions of Comitán: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lagos de Montebello National Park
  2. Cascadas el Chiflon
  3. Parque Ecoturistico Huitepec
  4. Grutas de Rancho Nuevo

Hiking/Biking Routes of Comitán

Comitán is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  1. Parque Ecoturistico Huitepec
  2. Cascadas el Chiflon
  3. Lagos de Montebello National Park
  4. Grutas de Rancho Nuevo

Best Things to Do in Comitán: Activities and Tours

Comitán is bursting with activities and tours for travelers of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Take a tour of the Templo del Calvario and the Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzman.
  2. Visit the Museo de Arqueologia Maya and learn about the history of the region.
  3. Explore the stunning natural beauty of the Lagos de Montebello National Park.
  4. Go hiking or biking on one of the many scenic routes in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Comitán

If you’re planning to stay a week in Comitán, you won’t be disappointed. Here’s an itinerary for a week-long stay:

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Templo del Calvario and Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzman. Day 2: Take a tour of the Museo Fuerte de San Carlos and Museo de Arqueologia Maya. Day 3: Go hiking or biking in the Parque Ecoturistico Huitepec. Day 4: Take a scenic drive through Lagos de Montebello National Park. Day 5: Visit the stunning Cascadas el Chiflon. Day 6: Explore the Grutas de Rancho Nuevo. Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Tuxtla Gutierrez.

Food and Drink Scene of Comitán

Mexico is known for its incredible food and drink, and Comitán is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chalupas - a small fried tortilla topped with meat, cheese, and salsa.
  2. Tamales - a traditional Mexican dish made from masa (a type of corn dough) and filled with various meats or vegetables.
  3. Street Tacos - a staple of Mexican street food, tacos are filled with various meats (like carne asada or al pastor), onion, cilantro, and salsa.
  4. Quesadillas - a type of cheese-filled tortilla, often filled with a variety of meats or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Pozol - a traditional drink made from fermented corn and cacao.
  2. Tascalate - a traditional drink made from roasted corn, chocolate, achiote, cinnamon, vanilla, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Comitán: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Comitán has a variety of options for travelers looking to stay in town. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Casa Cafeina: A chic guesthouse located in the heart of the city center.
  2. Hotel Casa Mexicana: An affordable hotel with excellent amenities.
  3. Mision Casa de Adobe: A beautiful hotel with spacious rooms and a pool.
  4. El Pueblito: A charming hostel located in the town center.

What to Buy in Comitán: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir from your trip to Comitán, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  1. Handcrafted textiles - Comitán is home to many skilled artisans who create beautiful textiles using traditional techniques.
  2. Coffee - Chiapas is known for its excellent coffee, so make sure to stock up before you leave.
  3. Hand-painted pottery - Comitán is home to many talented potters who create beautiful and unique pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Comitán

Comitán is a town steeped in tradition and culture. Here are some things you should know before you go:

  • The local language is Spanish, but many people also speak indigenous languages like Tsotsil and Tseltal.
  • The town is known for its colorful and lively festivals, so try to plan your trip around one of these events if you can.
  • It is common to haggle over prices when shopping at markets or buying souvenirs.

Best Time to Visit Comitán

The best time to visit Comitán is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is mild and dry, making it the perfect time to explore the town’s many outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Comitán

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, it’s delicious!
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Take plenty of photos- Comitán is a picturesque town.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Comitán is generally a safe and healthy place to visit, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Here’s what you should know:

Safety in Comitán

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid unlit or isolated areas.
  • Keep your belongings close to you, and don’t leave them unattended.
  • Use reputable transport companies for all your travel needs.

Health in Comitán

  • Make sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated in the hot weather.
  • Be mindful of the high altitude, which may cause altitude sickness for some people.

Getting to Comitán: Transportation Options and Logistics

Comitán is located in Chiapas, Mexico, and can be easily reached by a variety of transportation options. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Comitán

  • Angel Albino Corzo International Airport (TGZ): The closest airport to Comitán, located around 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Comitán

  • Comitan de Dominguez Bus Station: Located in the town center, this station serves many bus routes within Chiapas and other parts of Mexico.

Other Options

  • Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire in Comitán.

How to Get Around in Comitán

Comitán is a relatively small town, making it easy to navigate on foot. For longer journeys, taxis and buses are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Comitán: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Chiapas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. San Cristobal de las Casas: A vibrant, colonial town with an abundance of history and culture.
  2. Palenque: Famous for its impressive Mayan ruins.
  3. Tapachula: A bustling city located on the coast, famous for its delicious food and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Comitán is a beautiful and fascinating town, bursting with culture, history, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking to explore ancient ruins, hike through some of Mexico’s most stunning landscapes or just soak up the local atmosphere, Comitán has everything you need to create the perfect vacation. So start planning your trip today - you won’t regret it!
