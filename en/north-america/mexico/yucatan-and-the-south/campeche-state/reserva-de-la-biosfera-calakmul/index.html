Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul Travel Guide

Located in the heart of the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico, Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul is a hidden gem for nature and culture enthusiasts alike. With its ancient ruins, natural wonders, and vibrant local culture, this biosphere reserve offers a truly immersive travel experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul has to offer, including where to stay, what to see and do, and how to make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

Spanning over 700,000 acres of tropical forest, Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul is a UNESCO World Heritage site that offers a glimpse into Mexico’s rich cultural and natural history. Home to a vast array of plant and animal species, including the elusive jaguar, the reserve is teeming with life and is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calakmul Archeological Site: One of the largest and most important Mayan ruins in Mexico, Calakmul boasts towering pyramids and intricate carvings that offer a glimpse into the Mayan civilization’s rich history.

  • Xpujil Museum: This small but fascinating museum houses a collection of artifacts from the region’s ancient Mayan culture and is a great place to learn more about the indigenous people who once called this area home.

Natural Attractions of Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laguna de Bacalar: Also known as the “Lake of Seven Colors,” this stunning lagoon boasts crystal-clear water and a rainbow of hues that change throughout the day.

  • Balamkú: Surrounded by lush jungle, Balamkú is a lesser-known Mayan ruin that boasts intricate carvings and a peaceful, uncrowded atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

  • Sendero Jaguar: This 2-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest and is a great place to spot jaguars, monkeys, and other wildlife.

  • Yaxchilan Route: This 10-mile route takes visitors through the heart of the reserve and offers glimpses of ancient ruins, exotic birds, and other natural wonders.

Best Things to Do in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul: Activities and Tours

  • Bird watching: With over 358 bird species recorded in the reserve, Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul is a paradise for bird watchers.

  • Zipline: Get a bird’s eye view of the forest canopy by ziplining over the treetops with local tour companies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

Day 1: Explore Calakmul Archeological Site and hike the Sendero Jaguar trail. Day 2: Visit Xpujil Museum and take a guided bird-watching tour. Day 3: Zipline over the forest canopy and explore Balamkú ruin. Day 4: Day trip to Laguna de Bacalar for swimming and relaxation. Day 5: Bike the Yaxchilan Route and explore lesser-known ruins. Day 6: Take in the culture and food of local villages, such as Conhuas and Chunchintok. Day 7: Relax and unwind at your hotel’s pool or spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cochinita Pibil: Slow-roasted pork marinated in citrus juice, achiote, and spices.

  • Sopa de Lima: Chicken soup flavored with fresh lime juice and crispy tortilla strips.

Local Beverages

  • Xtabentún: A sweet and fragrant liqueur made from anise and fermented honey.

  • Agua de Jamaica: A refreshing hibiscus-flavored tea.

Where to Stay in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Villas Kin-Ha: These luxurious villas offer jungle views, private swimming pools, and personalized service.

  • Chicanná Ecovillage Resort: This eco-friendly resort offers comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and easy access to nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hammocks: Colorful and comfortable hammocks handmade by local artisans.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Unique and intricate jewelry made from local materials such as jade and coconut shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

The local culture of Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul is heavily influenced by the Mayan civilization, and visitors can expect to encounter traditional crafts, music, and dance. Visitors should also be respectful of local customs, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

The best time to visit Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul is from November to March when the weather is dry and mild. However, visitors should be prepared for cooler temperatures at night and the possibility of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Spanish to communicate with locals.

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in temples and removing shoes before entering a home.

Don’t:

  • Swim in cenotes or other natural bodies of water without a guide.

  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

The reserve is generally safe for visitors, but it is always wise to take precautions such as locking valuables in hotel safes and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

Visitors to Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus are present in the region, so it is important to use insect repellent and wear protective clothing.

Getting to Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

The closest international airport is Cancun International Airport, which is approximately a 7-hour drive from the reserve. Domestic flights are also available to Campeche International Airport, which is 3 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

There are several bus stations in nearby cities, including Campeche, Chetumal, and Bacalar. Visitors can take a bus to Xpujil and hire a taxi or shuttle to the reserve.

Other Options

Renting a car is also an option, but visitors should be aware that roads in the area can be narrow and winding.

How to Get Around in Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul

The best way to get around the reserve is by car or bike. Visitors can rent cars or bikes from Xpujil and other nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sian Ka’an Biosphere Reserve: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a stunning marine reserve that offers snorkeling, wildlife encounters, and breathtaking coastal views.

  • Mérida: The capital city of Yucatan, Mérida is a vibrant cultural hub that boasts beautiful architecture, lively festivals, and mouth-watering cuisine.

Conclusion

Reserva de la Biósfera Calakmul is a true paradise for nature and culture lovers, offering a range of activities and attractions that will satisfy even the most discerning traveler. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality, this biosphere reserve is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Yucatan Peninsula.
