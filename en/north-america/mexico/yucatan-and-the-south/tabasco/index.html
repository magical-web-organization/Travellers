Introduction to Tabasco

Nestled in the southern region of Mexico, Tabasco is a state with a rich historical past and is home to some of the most beautiful landscapes, museums, and archaeological sites in the country. It is world-famous for its spicy pepper sauce, Tabasco sauce, which is exported globally.

List of Places to Visit in Tabasco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Venta Museum-Park: This museum is home to a stunning collection of Olmec art and is famous for its colossal stone heads.
  • Comalcalco Archaeological Site: The site houses the brick ruins of an ancient Mayan city, dating back over 2,000 years.
  • Casa de los Azulejos: A beautiful 19th-century building featuring exquisite blue tile murals.

Natural Attractions of Tabasco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural de la Sierra: One of the most stunning natural parks in the region, which is perfect for hiking and exploring the verdant jungle views.
  • Laguna de las Ilusiones: A beautiful lagoon district that is great for canoeing and kayaking.
  • Sierra de Tabasco: A beautiful mountain range, where visitors can hike and experience breathtaking views of Tabasco’s green landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tabasco

  • Parque Natural de la Sierra: This is a great place for hiking and biking in the heart of nature.
  • Parque Estatal Agua Blanca: It is a beautiful park that offers several scenic biking routes.

Best Things to Do in Tabasco: Activities and Tours

  • Tabasco Hot Sauce Factory: Take a guided tour to the Tabasco Hot Sauce Factory, where you’ll learn about the production process and get to sample their famous sauce.
  • Balloon Tour: Take an early morning hot air balloon ride over the lush forests of Tabasco.
  • Parque Yumka: An amusement park where visitors can enjoy animal exhibits and activities like zip-lining and canoeing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tabasco

Food and Drink Scene of Tabasco

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chontalpa-style fish: A delicious dish made with Tilapia fish and served with green peppers and onions in a banana leaf.
  • Tamale Sos: A spicy tamale dish served in a tomato-based sauce with shredded chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Pozol: A refreshing drink made of corn, chocolate, and sugar.
  • Tepache: A fermented pineapple drink, which is sweet and tangy in taste.

Where to Stay in Tabasco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hyatt Regency Villahermosa: One of the top-rated hotels in the region, known for its luxurious amenities and services.
  • Sleep Inn Villahermosa: A comfortable and affordable hotel offering all basic amenities.
  • Hostel Inn Villahermosa: A budget-friendly hostel with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Tabasco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tabasco Hot Sauce: Arguably the most famous product of the region, which is a must-buy for hot sauce lovers.
  • Olmec Artifacts: The La Venta Museum-Park has a gift shop that sells souvenirs, including miniature replicas of Olmec heads.
  • Hammocks: Hand-woven hammocks by Tabascan artisans make for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tabasco

The residents of Tabasco are friendly, hospitable, and proud of their rich cultural heritage. Be respectful of local customs and traditions by dressing appropriately and behaving respectfully.

Best Time to Visit Tabasco

The best time to visit Tabasco is between February and April when the climate is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tabasco

Do:

  • Try the famous Tabasco hot sauce.
  • Visit the La Venta Museum-Park and explore the beautiful collection of Olmec art.

Don’t:

  • Wear offensive clothing or accessories to religious sites.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tabasco

Tabasco is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors are advised to be careful and aware of their surroundings when visiting crowded areas and avoid visiting any remote area at night.

Health in Tabasco

Tourists should avoid drinking tap water and only drink bottled water. It is recommended to take precautions against insect bites to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like chikungunya.

How to Get Around in Tabasco

The best way to get around Tabasco is by rental car or taxi. Public transportation options such as buses and trains are also available but can be difficult to navigate.
