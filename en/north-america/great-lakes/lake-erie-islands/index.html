Introduction to Lake Erie Islands

Lake Erie Islands is a group of 30 islands located in the western region of the Great Lakes of North America, and is known for its stunning views, rich history, and outdoor adventures. Popular among tourists, Lake Erie Islands offer a plethora of activities that make it a perfect destination for travelers of all kinds. Here’s everything you need to know about experiencing Lake Erie Islands to the fullest.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Erie Islands

When planning your trip to Lake Erie Islands, it’s essential to research the most popular attractions and destinations before you go. Here are some of the must-visit places that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Perry’s Victory and International Peace Memorial: A popular attraction that commemorates the Battle of Lake Erie and honors those who fought and died.
  • Johnson’s Island Confederate Officers’ Prison Cemetery: Visit the site of a notorious Civil War-era prison for Confederate officers.
  • Lake Erie Islands Historical Society Museum: A historical museum that features exhibits on the history of the Lake Erie Islands.

Natural Attractions of Lake Erie Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South Bass Island State Park: This scenic park offers hiking trails, fishing, and camping.
  • Kelleys Island State Park: The park features several hiking trails, bird watching, and a secluded beach.
  • Put-in-Bay Winery: A local winery that offers wine-tasting and vineyard tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Erie Islands

There are several hiking trails and biking routes available on Lake Erie Islands, each with its unique features and scenery. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking spots on Lake Erie Islands:

  • Kelleys Island State Park Hiking Trails: This 1,200-acre park features several hiking trails ranging from easy to moderate.
  • South Bass Island State Park Hiking Trails: A popular park that features hiking trails, bird watching spots, and camping grounds.
  • North Bass Island: A hidden gem located off the coast of Put-in-Bay, this island offers a natural and serene atmosphere that’s perfect for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Lake Erie Islands: Activities and Tours

Lake Erie Islands offer a plethora of activities, adventures, and tours that you can experience. Here are some of the best things to do on Lake Erie Islands:

  • Island Hopping: This tour lets you explore different islands in Lake Erie, each with unique offerings.
  • Put-in-Bay Golf Carts: This is an exciting and unique way to explore the island of Put-in-Bay.
  • Lake Erie Fishing Charters: Experience world-class fishing on Lake Erie from one of the many fishing charters available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Erie Islands

A week-long itinerary will give you enough time to experience all the must-visit places and activities in Lake Erie Islands. Here is a rough outline of a week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive at Lake Erie Islands and check-in at your hotel.

Day 2: Explore South Bass Island State Park and visit Perry’s Victory and International Peace Memorial.

Day 3: Take an Island Hopping tour and explore the neighboring islands.

Day 4: Visit Kelleys Island State Park and Put-in-Bay Winery.

Day 5: Try out fishing or other outdoor activities available on Lake Erie.

Day 6: Rent a golf cart and explore the picturesque island of Put-in-Bay on your own.

Day 7: Check out local souvenir shops and craft markets before departing Lake Erie Islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Erie Islands

Lake Erie Islands offer a unique culinary experience, with plenty of local dishes and beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lake Erie Perch: A popular dish served in most restaurants on the island.
  • Lobster Bisque: This delicious soup is served in many popular restaurants and cafes on Lake Erie Islands.
  • Pecan Caramel Fudge: A locally crafted dessert that’s perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Put-in-Bay Brewery and Distillery: Enjoy hand-crafted beers and spirits that are ready to satisfy your thirst after a long day of exploring the island.

Where to Stay in Lake Erie Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lake Erie Islands have numerous options for accommodation, ranging from cozy cottages to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay on Lake Erie Islands:

  • Island Club Rentals: Offers rental homes, condos, and cottages that are perfect for family getaways.
  • Put-in-Bay Condos: Next to the Put-in-Bay Airport, these elegant and luxurious condos feature two and three-bedroom suits with stunning views of the lake and surrounding islands.
  • Kelleys Island Venture Resort: This resort features comfortable rooms, cottages, and suites, as well as an outdoor pool and a tiki bar.

What to Buy in Lake Erie Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several local shops and markets on Lake Erie Islands that feature a variety of selections to take home. Here are some of the best local products to consider:

  • Local wines and spirits: Take home local and hand-crafted wines, beers, and spirits that are unique to Lake Erie Islands.
  • Artwork and crafts: Browse the local shops and galleries to find unique art pieces and crafts made by local artists and artisans.
  • Beachwear and accessories: Bring home a little bit of Lake Erie Islands’ island-style by shopping for beachwear and accessories from the island’s shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Erie Islands

Lake Erie Islands have their unique culture and customs that reflect their history and personality. Islanders are known for being friendly, welcoming, and hospitable to visitors. Here are some general customs to remember while visiting Lake Erie Islands:

  • Always be polite and respectful to locals and fellow visitors.
  • Try to blend in with the island culture, dress appropriately, and follow their dress codes.
  • Be responsible and environmentally friendly while traveling.

Best Time to Visit Lake Erie Islands

The peak tourist season of Lake Erie Islands is from Memorial Day in May to Labor Day in September. However, several activities and attractions are available year-round, such as fishing and hiking. Consider visiting off-season if you want to experience the islands’ serene and laid-back atmosphere without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Erie Islands

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to remember while visiting Lake Erie Islands:

Do:

  • Follow all rules and regulations in public areas.
  • Book accommodation and activities in advance.
  • Respect the local island culture and customs while exploring.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitats on the island.
  • Leave without trying the local specialties and delicacies.
  • Disrespect locals or fellow visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Erie Islands

Lake Erie Islands are generally safe, but visitors should still exercise caution while exploring the islands. Here are some safety tips to remember:

  • Always carry sunscreen and appropriate clothing to avoid heatstroke and sunburn.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of fluids.
  • Check weather forecasts in advance and plan activities accordingly.

Health in Lake Erie Islands

Lake Erie Islands have a small number of medical facilities available. It is essential to bring basic medications and travel insurance to be prepared for any medical emergencies.

Getting to Lake Erie Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Lake Erie Islands, including air, bus, and boat.

Airports Near Lake Erie Islands

  • Island Airways operates flights to Put-in-Bay Airport on Put-in-Bay Island.

Bus Stations Near Lake Erie Islands

  • Lake Front Lines offers bus services to Sandusky Ohio, the gateway to the Lake Erie Islands.

Other Options

  • Miller Ferry: The ferry operates regular trips throughout the day and night from Catawba Island to Put-In-Bay and Middle Bass Islands.
  • Jet Express: High-speed watercraft operates scheduled ferries between ports in Sandusky, Port Clinton, and Kelleys Island.

How to Get Around in Lake Erie Islands

Bicycles, golf carts, and mopeds are the most popular modes of transport on Lake Erie Islands. Visitors can also take seasonal Island Taxis for easy access from one attraction to another.

Where to Go Next from Lake Erie Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can consider exploring after Lake Erie Islands, such as:

  • Cedar Point: Known as the “Roller Coaster Capital of the World,” Cedar Point features plenty of amusement park rides, games, and attractions.
  • Toledo: Toledo offers several attractions, such as the Toledo Museum of Art, Toledo Zoo, and the National Museum of the Great Lakes.
  • Cleveland: Home to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, the Cleveland Museum of Art, and the Cleveland Orchestra, Cleveland has plenty to offer to visitors.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for natural attractions, outdoor adventures, or a relaxing getaway, Lake Erie Islands have everything you need to experience a memorable trip. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit to Lake Erie Islands and make the most out of your vacation.
