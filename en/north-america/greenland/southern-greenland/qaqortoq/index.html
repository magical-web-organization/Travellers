Introduction to Qaqortoq

Located in Southern Greenland, Qaqortoq is a charming town with a unique character. With its colorful houses and stunning fjord views, it is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to connect with nature and explore Greenland’s rich cultural heritage.

Qaqortoq is a small town with a population of around 3,000, which means that it is easy to explore on foot and to get to know the locals. Here, you’ll experience the beauty of untouched nature, the thrill of adventure, and the warmth of native Greenlandic hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Qaqortoq

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Greenland National Museum and Archives: The museum houses collections from Greenland’s history from the Saqqaq culture up to the modern era, as well as art exhibitions from both Greenlandic and international artists.

Qaqortoq Museum: The museum displays the history of Qaqortoq, from the prehistoric times over colonial settlement era to present day with the fishing industry.

Natural Attractions of Qaqortoq: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Qaqortoq and its surroundings offer outdoor enthusiasts plenty of opportunities for adventure and scenic exploration. Some of the most popular attractions include:

Biggest fjord system of the world: The crystalline, blue-green fjord system in Qaqortoq is a natural wonder that should not be missed. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the magnificent fjord system and to witness the breathtaking icebergs.

Hiking trails: Qaqortoq offers several trekking trails through the pristine wilderness. The best moment to explore them is during the summer months when daylight hours are longer. The trails vary in length and difficulty, providing options for both novice and experienced hikers.

Northern Lights: If you’re lucky, you might see the Northern Lights during your visit to Qaqortoq. Many travelers to the area recommend a visit during the dark winter months for the best chance of seeing the elusive aurora borealis.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qaqortoq

Hiking and biking are some of the best ways to get around and explore the untouched natural beauty of Qaqortoq. Here are some of the best routes to take:

Qassiarsuk— Narsarsuaq: a 12km long mountainous trail connecting two settlements in the vicinity of Qaqortoq. It offers splendid views on the surrounding wilderness and coastal cliffs.

Appamiut— Qaqortoq: a hike that follows the highest part of a mountain ridge overlooking the wide Kangerluarsunnguaq fjord. Green valleys and beautiful views of cascading rivers reward those who challenge themselves to this trek.

Best Things to Do in Qaqortoq: Activities and Tours

Southern Greenland offers a vast range of activities to explore stunning and breathtaking landscapes of the area. Some of the unmissable activities that visitors can enjoy in Qaqortoq include:

Boat Tours: The town of Qaqortoq lies at the heart of the fantastically beautiful fjords system, so a boat tour of the area is an excellent way to experience the scenery and varying colors of southern Greenland.

Kayaking: There is no better way to explore the region than experiencing it up-close with a kayaking tour. Through the glassy waters, paddlers can observe the marine life of southern Greenland.

Fishing: Southern Greenland is famous for its sea angling. Local providers offer fishing tours in the fjords and around the coastline to provide visitors with a unique and personal experience of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Qaqortoq

Day 1: Arrival and modern town orientation walking tour. Day 2: Hike to the ridges of mountains to experience the scenic beauty of Qaqortoq. Day 3: Explore the charming colonial era settlement of Hvalsey, or choose a sea angling tour. Day 4: Boat tour of the grand fjord system of Greenland. Day 5: Travel to the beautiful sheep farmers village of Nanortalik. Day 6: Visit the Greenland National Museum and Archives in Nuuk and the Art Museum. Day 7: Take a helicopter tour over the stunning icebergs and valleys of Southern Greenland.

Food and Drink Scene of Qaqortoq

Greenlandic cuisine is a beautiful blend of traditional cuisine and modern styles. Here are some of the must-try dishes of the region:

Kiviak: This highly traditional dish involves fermenting small sea birds in a seal’s skin for several months, which creates a unique, authentic taste.

Suaasat: A traditional soup prepared using reindeer or the meat of other wild animals that you can experience in many local restaurants.

Coffee and hot chocolate: Some local providers sell unique varieties of hot chocolate, with the secret ingredient being the local chocolate made out of cocoa beans from the region.

Where to Stay in Qaqortoq: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qaqortoq offers plenty of options for accommodations from traditional guesthouses to newly constructed hotel facilities. Here are some recommendations to help you narrow down your search:

Hotel Qaqortoq: This hotel is conveniently located as a starting point to Qaqortoq’s attractions and offers a comfortable stay with a fantastic view of the surrounding wilderness.

The Red House: A traditional guesthouse that gives a homely feel while one is on the road in Greenland.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qaqortoq

Qaqortoq is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always useful to take the standard precautions when exploring any new place. Stay away from sea ice as it may break off unexpectedly, and avoid areas without an official trail. Wear waterproof gear if kayaking or boating, and use sunscreen to shield skin from the harsh sun.

Health in Qaqortoq

It is recommended that visitors should take malaria medication depending on the length and location of the trip before arriving in Greenland. Drinking water is generally safe in Qaqortoq, but it can help to bring iodine tablets or a filtration system if you plan to take on multi-day treks or courses where access to clean water might not be available.

Getting to Qaqortoq: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qaqortoq

Qaqortoq Heliport: A small airport with only scheduled flights to Nuuk in the Summer Months, which is a 45-minute helicopter ride away.

Keflavik International Airport: The closest gateway to Qaqortoq, with daily flights to Nuuk.

Bus Stations Near Qaqortoq

There is no direct bus service to Qaqortoq, but buses connect towns throughout the region.

Other Options

Helicopter: Visitors can book a helicopter tour of the area for a unique way to explore the beautiful Greenlandic landscapes.

Sea Ferry: A slow and steady sea ferry provides a unique opportunity to experience the coastline of southern Greenland.

How to Get Around in Qaqortoq

As only 3,000 people call the city home, exploring the charming town of Qaqortoq on foot is recommended. Alternatively, bicycle rentals are available as a different way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Qaqortoq: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Narsarsuaq: A settlement to the north of Qaqortoq, with a rich history, beautiful mountain views, and Europe’s largest icecap at its doorstep.

Nuuk: The capital of Greenland, known for its unique architecture and museums, is the perfect place to experience how Greenlandic living has evolved over the past few decades.

Kangerlussuaq: A town located near the ice cap, where visitors can take a scenic flight over the ice cap and try dogsledding.

Conclusion

Qaqortoq is a fantastic destination for travelers who are looking to explore the natural beauty and unique culture of Southern Greenland. With its welcoming community, charming colonial architecture, and magnificent scenery, it’s a place that’s hard to resist. From its breathtaking fjords and hiking opportunities to its unique culinary delights and rich cultural heritage, Qaqortoq is one of the most magical places in the world and an unforgettable place to visit.
