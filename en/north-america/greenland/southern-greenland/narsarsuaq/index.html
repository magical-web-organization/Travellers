Introduction to Narsarsuaq

Narsarsuaq is a small town located in Southern Greenland, known for its stunning natural beauty and fascinating history. The town is situated on the edge of the famous Greenlandic ice cap, and it offers visitors an opportunity to explore the rugged outdoors and experience the local culture.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Narsarsuaq, along with practical information on how to get there and around. Join us as we uncover the hidden gem of Southern Greenland.

List of Places to Visit in Narsarsuaq

Here are some of the must-visit places to explore while in Narsarsuaq:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Narsarsuaq has a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. Here are some of the essential places to visit:

  • Greenlandic Norse Ruins: These ruins are some of the most significant historical sites in Greenland. They date back to the Viking ages and offer a glimpse into the past.
  • Blue West One Museum: Learn about the history of aviation in Southern Greenland at this museum.
  • Narsarsuaq Museum: Discover the local history and culture at this small museum.

Natural Attractions of Narsarsuaq: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Narsarsuaq is famous for its natural beauty and the numerous outdoor activities that visitors can do. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Greenland Ice Cap: Take a trip to the edge of the vast Greenlandic ice cap, and marvel at the stunning scenery.
  • Flower Valley: Experience a beautiful Nordic valley filled with wildflowers and hills.
  • Qooroq Ice Fjord: Take a boat trip to see the glacier calvings, and enjoy the serene surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narsarsuaq

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Narsarsuaq. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Narsarsuaq Valley: Explore the picturesque and scenic valley on foot.
  • Igaliku: This old Norse settlement is ideal for a bike ride along the glacier-fed stream and the scenic hills.

Best Things to Do in Narsarsuaq: Activities and Tours

Narsarsuaq has a range of activities suitable for different interests and ages. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Glacier walks: Explore the stunning glacier in Southern Greenland.
  • Boat tours: Take a boat trip to the Qooroq Ice Fjord and enjoy the impressive calving of the glacier.
  • Arctic fishing: Try out this traditional Greenlandic activity, and catch your dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Narsarsuaq

Narsarsuaq has a unique and delicious gastronomy worth trying. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mattak: Raw whale skin and blubber served with sauce
  • Kiviak: Birds fermented inside seal skin for several months

Local Beverages

  • Kaffemik: A traditional coffee social event with local snacks, cakes, and biscuits
  • Greenlandic Beer and Akvavit: Try the unique Greenlandic beer and Akvavit, a flavored spirit.

Where to Stay in Narsarsuaq: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Narsarsuaq has several accommodation options to cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hotel Narsarsuaq: A comfortable and cozy hotel with stunning views of the ice cap.
  • Leif Erikson Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers dorm rooms and private rooms.
  • Igaliku Country Hotel: Located in a small, picturesque village, this hotel offers a unique and tranquil wilderness experience.

What to Buy in Narsarsuaq: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Narsarsuaq, there are several items that you can bring back home as souvenirs and keepsakes. These include:

  • Greenlandic jewelry: Purchase traditional Greenlandic jewelry made from precious and semi-precious stones.
  • Souvenirs from museums and local galleries: Visit the local museums and galleries and buy souvenirs made by local artists and artisans.
  • Hand-knitted clothing: Traditional Greenlandic clothing like mittens, hats, and socks make for great souvenirs for both adults and children.

Local Culture and Customs of Narsarsuaq

Greenlanders have a unique and rich culture that is worth exploring. Here are some of the cultural practices that you can experience while in Narsarsuaq:

  • Kaffemik: Traditional coffee social event where locals socialize and share refreshments.
  • Ullortuneq: A Greenlandic practice of sharing resources and helping each other out during tough times.
  • Kayak dances: Participate in traditional Greenlandic kayak dances, a unique and enchanting experience.

Best Time to Visit Narsarsuaq

The best time to visit Narsarsuaq is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and conducive for outdoor activities. The winter months from December to March offer a unique opportunity to see the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narsarsuaq

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Narsarsuaq:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and cultures: Participate respectfully in cultural activities and respect cultural norms.
  • Be environmentally conscious: Greenland is pristine, and visitors must take care not to disrupt or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Dress appropriately: Dress for the weather and ensure that you wear sturdy boots suitable for walking on rocky terrain.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife: Visitors must not disturb wildlife by littering or making unnecessary noise.
  • Touch or walk on archaeological sites: Arctic soil is fragile, and walking or touching archaeological sites in the area can damage the artifacts.
  • Ignore safety protocols: Visitors must follow safety protocols when engaging in outdoor activities to avoid accidents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Narsarsuaq

Greenland is overall a safe country. Visitors must, however, remain vigilant and take the necessary precautions when engaging in outdoor activities. These include:

  • Avoiding lone hikes or walks: Visitors should not engage in lone activities outdoors.
  • Using the services of a licensed guide: Visitors should use the services of a licensed guide when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Carrying bear spray: Visitors should carry bear spray when traveling in areas where there is potential for wildlife encounters.

Health in Narsarsuaq

The health system in Greenland is modern and well-equipped. Visitors are, however, advised to take precautions when traveling in the country. These include:

  • Getting a travel insurance: Visitors must purchase a comprehensive travel insurance package that covers all their medical needs.
  • Carrying medicines: Visitors must carry all necessary medicines, including prescriptions, when traveling to Greenland.
  • Drinking bottled water: Visitors must drink only bottled water.

Getting to Narsarsuaq: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Narsarsuaq via air travel and then using local transportation. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Narsarsuaq

The main airport that serves Narsarsuaq is the Narsarsuaq Airport.

Bus Stations Near Narsarsuaq

There are no bus stations in Narsarsuaq.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Narsarsuaq via boat or helicopter from other parts of Greenland.

How to Get Around in Narsarsuaq

The best way to explore Narsarsuaq is by foot, bike, or boat. Visitors can hire bikes or join organized tours to explore the town and neighboring areas.

Where to Go Next from Narsarsuaq: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby towns and villages when in Narsarsuaq. Some of the options include:

  • Igaliku: A picturesque village located at the end of the Igaliku fjord.
  • Qaqortoq: A charming town located on the southern coast of Greenland, known for its colorful houses and museums.

Conclusion

Narsarsuaq is a unique and beautiful destination that is worth exploring. From glaciers to ancient ruins, this small town in Southern Greenland has plenty to offer visitors. Follow our guide and discover the hidden gem of Southern Greenland today.
