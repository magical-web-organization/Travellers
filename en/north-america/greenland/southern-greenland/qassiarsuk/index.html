Qassiarsuk Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour of Southern Greenland

Nestled in Southern Greenland, Qassiarsuk is a quaint and charming village that offers its visitors an unparalleled insight into the rich history, culture, and natural beauty of Greenland. From hiking and biking to indulging in local cuisine and immersing in the local way of life, Qassiarsuk has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we bring to you an ultimate relishing experience that highlights the best places to visit, the top things to do, and much more.

Introduction to Qassiarsuk

Qassiarsuk (or Brattahlid in its original Norse name) is a small village in the Narsaq municipality of Southern Greenland. It is situated on the eastern shore of the Sermilik Fjord and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers. Despite its incredibly remote location, Qassiarsuk has a rich history that dates back over a millenia.

List of Places to Visit in Qassiarsuk

  • Erik the Red’s House: This ancient turf-roofed house is the birthplace of Leif Eriksson, the son of Erik the Red. Visitors can learn about the Viking-age history of Qassiarsuk and the Greenlandic way of life in the Viking era.

  • The Qassiarsuk Sheep Farm: This farm offers visitors the opportunity to observe the traditional sheep farming techniques that are unique to Greenland. Visitors can also sample the farm’s fresh cheese, milk, and meat.

  • The Church of Hvalsey: The ruins of this ancient church are a testament to the past’s Christianity in Greenlandic history and the fascinating architectural techniques of the ancient times.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Norse ruins: These ruins date back to around 1000 AD and are a UNESCO World Heritage site. They are part of a Greenlandic-Norwegian project that aims to preserve the cultural heritage of the area.

  • The Blue West One Museum: This is a small museum that has a fascinating collection of artefacts and exhibits about Norse and Inuit culture.

Natural Attractions of Qassiarsuk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Qassiarsuk Ice Fjord: This fjord is perfect for kayaking, boating and admiring the icy landscapes of southern Greenland.

  • The Qaleraliq Glacier: Visitors can take a helicopter tour to the glacier and admire the vast expanse of ice from above. You can also go hiking or participate in camping activities nearby.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qassiarsuk

The rolling mountains of Qassiarsuk are perfect for hiking and biking. Some of the most popular trails which cater to every level of adventurers are:

  • The Summit Ridge Hike (5.5km): This challenging trail takes you to the highest peak above Qassiarsuk, offering panoramic views of the area.

  • The Tasiusaq Hike (10km): This moderate walk leads you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the area, such as idyllic valleys and cascading waterfalls

Best Things to Do in Qassiarsuk: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Witness the beauty of the waters surrounding Qassiarsuk, navigate your boat or just enjoy the beauty of the area.

  • Fishing: Visit for one of the most popular recreations in Greenland, fishing in Qassiarsuk provides an opportunity to experience one of the local’s favourite recreational activity.

Food and Drink Scene of Qassiarsuk

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiviak: A traditional Greenlandic dish consisting of raw seabirds that are fermented in sealskin under a rock pile for several months.

  • Suaasat: A popular Greenlandic soup made with fish or meat, rice, onions, potatoes, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Kaffemik: a tradition of Greenlandic culture is to have coffee with bread and cake. It is an entertainment activity and socialising event to share moments with people.

  • Greenlandic Beer: With their cosmopolitan taste, the local beers of Greenland are a hit with the beer lovers and tourists alike.

Where to Stay in Qassiarsuk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Narsarsuaq: This is a comfortable and modern hotel located close to the airport and many attractions in the village.

  • Leif Eriksson Hostel: A great budget option that offers affordable dorm rooms, private rooms, and basic kitchen facilities.

  • Igdslaktin Guesthouse: A comfortable guesthouse located in the heart of the village, offering views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers.

What to Buy in Qassiarsuk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Qiviut Clothing: Qiviut is a fine wool-like fiber shed by muskoxen and is used to produce warm clothing that is perfect for the cold climate of Greenland.

  • Greenlandic Art: Greenlands art is a unique blend of traditional techniques and modern influences, making it a great souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Qassiarsuk

Greenlandic culture is rich and diverse, with both Inuit and Norse influences. Visitors should make an effort to learn about the local customs and traditions while visiting Qassiarsuk, such as the Kaffemik, a coffee-based social gathering, and the local fishing techniques and culture.

Best Time to Visit Qassiarsuk

The best time to visit Qassiarsuk is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. During the long days of the Greenlandic summer, Visitors can have more time to explore outdoor activities and enjoy the stunning landscapes of southern Greenland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qassiarsuk

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress warmly as the weather can easily change at a moment’s notice.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter behind in natural areas.

  • Walk on the tundra or other fragile ecosystems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qassiarsuk

Qassiarsuk is an incredibly safe destination, with very little crime. Visitors should, however, still take precautions such as not walking alone at night and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Qassiarsuk

Visitors should take precautions against exposure to the cold, especially if they are participating in outdoor activities such as hiking or camping. Pack enough warm clothes and gear for the activity. Visitors must have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Qassiarsuk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qassiarsuk

The nearest airport to Qassiarsuk is Narsarsuaq Airport, which is located about 5 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Qassiarsuk.

Bus Stations Near Qassiarsuk

There are no bus stations in Qassiarsuk as it is a small village, but buses run regularly between the nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can take a ferry from the nearby towns which is a comfortable option for adventure and sightseeing.

How to Get Around in Qassiarsuk

The best way to get around Qassiarsuk is by foot or by bicycle. The village is small, and most attractions and points of interest are within easy walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles from local shops or hotels to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Qassiarsuk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Narsaq: A small town that is known for its colourful houses, beautiful scenery, and outdoor activities.

  • Sisimiut: The second-largest town in Greenland, which offers a glimpse into modern Greenlandic culture.

Conclusion

Qassiarsuk is an incredibly unique destination full of history, culture, natural wonder, picturesque scenarios and a dose of adventure. This Greenlandic village offers endless possibilities for both comfort and adventure-seekers looking to satisfy their thirst for natural beauty and cultural exploration. Visiting Qassiarsuk is an excellent chance to discover embracing culture, scenic routes, mouth-watering dishes, and unique experiences that one can never forget.
