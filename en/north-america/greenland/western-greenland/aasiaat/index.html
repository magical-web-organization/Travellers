Introduction to Aasiaat

Located in Western Greenland, Aasiaat is a picturesque town with a population of just over 3,000 people. It is situated right on the coast of the Davis Strait with an incredible view of the ocean and the mountains. Aasiaat offers visitors a unique taste of Greenlandic culture, outdoor adventures, and stunning natural wonders.

In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the necessary information to make your trip to Aasiaat an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Aasiaat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aasiaat Museum

Aasiaat Museum is a great place to start exploring the town’s history and culture. The museum houses artifacts and exhibits that give visitors insight into the town’s Inuit heritage, trading history, and Arctic explorations.

Poul Egede Church

Poul Egede Church is the oldest wooden church in Greenland and is a beautiful example of Danish-Norwegian Baroque-style architecture. The church was built in 1778 and is still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Aasiaat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Disko Bay

Disko Bay is a popular destination for whale watching, where you can see humpback whales, fin whales, minke whales, and even the occasional pod of orcas.

Qeqertarsuaq Island

Qeqertarsuaq Island is a short boat ride away from Aasiaat, and it offers excellent hiking trails, hot springs, and incredible views of the surrounding mountains and ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aasiaat

Aasiaat Valley

The Aasiaat Valley is an easy route and is suitable for travelers of all levels. It offers stunning views of the town and the Davis Strait.

Sermersooq Fjord

The Sermersooq Fjord is a more challenging route that takes visitors through breathtaking landscapes of ice-capped mountains, lakes, rivers, and glaciers.

Best Things to Do in Aasiaat: Activities and Tours

Dogsledding

Dogsledding is one of the most popular activities in Aasiaat and offers visitors a unique way to explore the stunning natural beauty of the town.

Kayaking

Kayaking is an excellent way to get closer to nature and explore the tranquil waters of the Arctic Ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aasiaat

1 Explore the town of Aasiaat and visit the Aasiaat Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Aasiaat

Must-Try Dishes

Kaffemik

Kaffemik is a traditional Greenlandic custom of inviting people to enjoy coffee and food together. Visitors can experience this traditional custom at local homes and enjoy fishcakes, berries, smoked fish, and seal meat.

Mattak

Mattak is a traditional Eskimo/Inuit dish that consists of whale skin and blubber.

Local Beverages

Greenlandic Coffee

Greenlandic coffee is unique to Greenland and is served hot with coffee, whisky, and Grand Marnier.

Where to Stay in Aasiaat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Disko Bay

Hotel Disko Bay is one of the most popular hotels in Aasiaat. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, stunning views, and excellent service.

Hostel Aasiaat

Hostel Aasiaat is a great budget option for travelers. The hostel is clean, well-maintained, and has a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Aasiaat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Art

Aasiaat is known for its Inuit art and visitors can purchase carvings, prints, and sculptures from local artists.

Qiviut

Qiviut is the fine undercoat of the muskox and is known for its softness and warmth. Visitors can purchase hats, scarves, and gloves made from qiviut.

Local Culture and Customs of Aasiaat

The town has rich Inuit tradition, and visitors are encouraged to respect and appreciate their culture. Visitors should also be aware that hunting and fishing are still an important part of the local subsistence economy.

Best Time to Visit Aasiaat

The best time to visit Aasiaat is from June to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aasiaat

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try Greenlandic cuisine and local products.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the town and surrounding areas.
  • Disturb the wildlife and marine life.
  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aasiaat

Aasiaat is a safe destination with a low crime rate. Visitors should follow standard safety precautions.

Health in Aasiaat

Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and should purchase bottled water. It is also recommended that visitors receive the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Greenland.

Getting to Aasiaat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aasiaat

Aasiaat Airport is located just outside the town, and it offers daily flights to and from Kangerlussuaq Airport.

Bus Stations Near Aasiaat

Bus transportation is limited in Aasiaat, but visitors can take the local town bus to get around.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to arrive in Aasiaat by boat or ferry.

How to Get Around in Aasiaat

Walking is the best way to explore the town and its surroundings. Visitors can also rent bicycles or vehicles from local rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Aasiaat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ilulissat

Ilulissat is a small town located north of Aasiaat and is known for its stunning icebergs and hiking trails.

Niaqornat

Niaqornat is a small village located south of Aasiaat and is known for its traditional Inuit culture and subsistence lifestyle.

Conclusion

Aasiaat offers travelers a unique opportunity to experience Greenlandic culture, explore stunning natural wonders, and enjoy outdoor adventures. Visitors will leave with memories that will last a lifetime.
