Introduction to Qasigiannguit

Tucked away in the heart of Western Greenland, Qasigiannguit is a hidden gem that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. This small town, with a population of just over 1,100 people, offers breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and adventure opportunities that are hard to find elsewhere.

The town is situated on Disko Bay, surrounded by towering mountains and crystal-clear waters teaming with marine life. It’s perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers who want to explore the Arctic wilderness of Greenland.

In this ultimate travel guide, we’ve gathered everything you need to know to plan your trip to Qasigiannguit, including the best places to visit, things to do, local culture, food and drink scene, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Qasigiannguit

Qasigiannguit’s diverse attractions cater to different interests, from museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic vistas. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Qasigiannguit has a rich history and culture that is well documented in its museums and historical sites. The following are some of the must-visit places:

  • Qasigiannguit Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the region with artifacts, photographs, and exhibitions from the early Inuit settlements to modern-day activities.
  • Disko Island: Take a boat trip to the Disko Island; it is a UNESCO World Heritage site and well-known for its glaciers, hiking trails, and natural beauty.
  • Uummannarsuaq: This historical site is a museum located in the Old Church that documents the history of the 18th-century French-Danish trading post that once occupied the area.

Natural Attractions of Qasigiannguit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Qasigiannguit is a nature-lover’s paradise; you’ll find stunning landscapes, glaciers, fjords, and wildlife at every turn. Here are some of the natural attractions to experience:

  • Ilulissat Icefjord: This iconic natural wonder draws visitors from across the globe for its calving glaciers and stunning views.
  • Qasigiannguit Archipelago: This beautiful collection of islands is home to a diverse range of wildlife such as whales, seals, and various seabirds.
  • Hiking trails: There are several hiking trails around Qasigiannguit that allow travelers to experience the natural beauty and breathtaking views of Greenland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qasigiannguit

If you are searching for an outdoor escape that offers pure beauty, the hiking and biking routes of Qasigiannguit are a must. Here are some of the great routes to dive into:

  • Disko Island Trail: This trail is perfect for experienced hikers who are looking for a challenge and want to see the beauty of the natural world up close.
  • Qasigiannguit to Ilimanaq Trail: A moderately challenging trail that allows hikers to experience the natural beauty of the region while hiking between two quaint towns.
  • Around Qasigiannguit: Several hiking trails are available in the town area, suitable for all ages and experiences.

Best Things to Do in Qasigiannguit: Activities and Tours

Qasigiannguit offers a plethora of activities and tours for adventure lovers, cultural buffs, and foodies alike. Here are the best things to do while in Qasigiannguit:

  • Northern Lights Viewing: Experience the Northern Lights from different viewpoints and in different ways, including dog sledding and snowshoeing.
  • Fishing: Whether it is ice fishing or fishing in the sea, Qasigiannguit offers excellent opportunities for anglers to catch fish.
  • Whale Watching: Take a tour to watch whales in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking: Enjoy kayaking tours around the lakes, rivers, and coastline that offer picturesque views of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Qasigiannguit

Greenland’s unique location and harsh climate have influenced its cuisine, making it a must-try for foodies. Here are some of the best food and drink options available in Qasigiannguit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiviak: It is a traditional dish made of raw sea birds, stuffed in the skins of seals, and left to ferment for several months.
  • Suaasat: It is a traditional Inuit soup made with meat, fish, or seal blubber commonly eaten during the winter months.
  • Mattak: It is a delicacy made from the skin and fat of whales.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Greenland’s coffee culture is famous for its unfiltered, boiled coffee, served with milk and sugar crystals.
  • Greenlandic Beer: Try the locally brewed beer found in most bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Qasigiannguit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qasigiannguit has various options for accommodations suitable for different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the places where visitors can stay while in Qasigiannguit:

  • Hotel Disko: Considered as the best hotel in town, offering comfortable rooms and excellent dining experiences.
  • Hostel Dodu: It’s a budget-friendly hostel option for travelers with basic amenities.
  • Kangeroo Inn: It is a cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Qasigiannguit, offering comfortable rooms and self-catering facilities.

What to Buy in Qasigiannguit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Qasigiannguit offers a unique collection of souvenirs and local products that reflect the town’s traditions and culture. Some of the top things to buy in Qasigiannguit are:

  • Greenlandic Polar Bear Carving: Buy handmade polar bear carvings, made by local artisans from natural materials.
  • Qiviut Clothing: It is a traditional fiber of musk ox wool used to make warm, cozy hats, gloves, and mittens perfect for arctic weather.
  • Kangerlussuaq Musk: Buy this handmade perfume made from musk ox and flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Qasigiannguit

Greenland has a unique and diverse cultural heritage that is fascinating to explore. Here are some customs and local cultures to familiarize yourself with while in Qasigiannguit:

  • Kaffemik: It’s a traditional Inuit feast where locals celebrate a significant event or achievement by inviting family and friends over to drink coffee and eat cake and other homemade food.
  • Shopping: Locals judge visitors based on politeness and patience while shopping, so avoid bargaining or being rude to merchants.

Best Time to Visit Qasigiannguit

The best time to visit Qasigiannguit is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is milder and there are longer daylight hours. The winter months from October to April offer the best opportunities for viewing the Northern Lights.

Airports Near Qasigiannguit

How to Get Around in Qasigiannguit

Visitors can explore Qasigiannguit’s beauty on foot and can hire taxis, boats, or snowmobiles to travel to nearby attractions. Some of the local guides offer their services for hiking and touring expeditions.

Where to Go Next from Qasigiannguit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations surrounding Qasigiannguit that visitors can explore, such as Ilulissat and the Arctic Circle Trail. Visitors can also take boat tours to Disko Island or Uummannaq, two of the most popular destinations near Qasigiannguit.
