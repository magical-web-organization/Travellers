Introduction to Sisimiut

Nestled in Western Greenland, Sisimiut is a picturesque town located on the coast of Davis Strait, offering mesmerizing views of the surrounding mountain ranges and the Arctic Ocean. Boasting a rich cultural heritage, Sisimiut is home to a friendly community of locals who warmly welcome visitors with their unique customs and traditions. With a plethora of exciting adventures and activities, Sisimiut is a destination that caters to everyone, whether you’re a history buff, an avid hiker, or an outdoor enthusiast. Read on to discover the hidden gems of this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Sisimiut

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sisimiut is home to a diverse range of museums and historical places, showcasing the town’s rich cultural heritage. The Museum of Sisimiut is a must-visit destination, offering an insight into the town’s history and the daily lives of its inhabitants. You can also pay a visit to the historic church of Sisimiut, built-in 1764, which is one of the oldest wooden churches in Greenland. Another notable attraction is the graveyard of Sisimiut, where you can find the tombs of some of the most prominent figures in the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Sisimiut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers are in for a treat in Sisimiut, with a wide range of stunning natural attractions and outdoor adventures. The Arctic Circle Trail is a popular destination among hikers, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys, rivers, and mountain ranges. You can also explore the icefjord of Sisimiut, which is an ideal spot for kayaking or sailing enthusiasts. Other popular natural attractions in Sisimiut include the Taseralik Fjord, where you can witness the magical Northern Lights, and the Sisimiut Ski Resort, which offers thrilling skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sisimiut

Sisimiut is a hiker’s paradise, with a plethora of exciting hiking and biking routes across the town’s stunning natural landscapes. The Arctic Circle Trail is one of the most popular hiking routes, covering a total distance of 160 kilometers across the Greenlandic wilderness. For avid mountain bikers, the Midnight Sun Bike Race is a must-attend event, offering a challenging race across the rugged landscapes of Sisimiut. Other popular hiking and biking routes in Sisimiut include the Kangerluarsunnguaq Valley, the Nasaasaaq Mountain, and the Assaqutaq Trail.

Best Things to Do in Sisimiut: Activities and Tours

Sisimiut offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours to cater to the diverse interests of tourists. You can go dog sledding and witness the magical Northern Lights, explore the fjord system on a kayaking or sailing trip, or participate in a cultural tour to learn more about the town’s unique customs and traditions. Other popular activities in Sisimiut include birdwatching, fishing, snowmobiling, and skiing.

Food and Drink Scene of Sisimiut

Must-Try Dishes

Sisimiut offers a unique culinary experience, with traditional Greenlandic cuisine being the highlight of the town’s food scene. Make sure to taste the famous Greenlandic delicacy, Mattak, which is raw whale skin or blubber, usually served with soy sauce or onions. You can also try the Greenlandic national dish, called suaasat, a traditional soup made from seal, fish, or whale meat. Other notable dishes to try include kiviak (raw bird buried in sealskin), dried reindeer meat, and fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

Greenlandic coffee, also known as Kaffemik, is a traditional hot beverage served with a shot of akvavit (a Scandinavian spirit) and a sweet biscuit. You can also try the famous Greenlandic beer, called Ice Cap Beer, which is a local favorite among residents and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Sisimiut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sisimiut offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. For those looking for a luxury experience, Hotel Sisimiut offers comfortable rooms with stunning sea views and modern amenities. The Hotel Arctic is another popular choice, with its central location in the heart of Sisimiut and its on-site restaurant offering delicious Greenlandic cuisine. Backpackers and budget travelers can opt for the Sisimiut Hostel or the Greenland Extreme guesthouse, both offering comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Sisimiut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sisimiut offers an array of souvenirs and local products to bring home as a memory from your trip. The town is famous for its handmade jewelry and clothing, made by local artisans using traditional Greenlandic techniques. You can also purchase locally produced handicrafts, such as sealskin bags, carvings, and wall hangings, as well as Greenlandic coffee and liquor.

Local Culture and Customs of Sisimiut

Greenlandic culture is deeply rooted in ancient traditions and customs, which are still prevalent in modern times. The hunting of marine animals, such as whales, seals, and polar bears, is an essential part of the Greenlandic culture and economy. The Kaffemik ceremony is also a significant part of the Greenlandic culture, where friends and family gather to enjoy coffee and cakes and celebrate special occasions.

Best Time to Visit Sisimiut

The best time to visit Sisimiut is during the summer months, from June to August, when the temperature ranges from 5C to 15C (41F to 59F), and the days are long, with up to 24 hours of daylight. This is the ideal time to participate in outdoor adventures and explore the town’s natural landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sisimiut

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately for the weather.
  3. Try the traditional Greenlandic cuisine and beverages.
  4. Participate in cultural tours and activities.
  5. Take part in local celebrations and festivals.

Don’t:

  1. Approach wildlife, such as polar bears or seals.
  2. Disturb the local environment.
  3. Disrespect or disregard local customs and traditions.
  4. Wander off designated hiking trails or camping spots.
  5. Leave trash or waste behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sisimiut

Sisimiut is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals who are always willing to help. However, it’s essential to be cautious when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or kayaking, and follow the advice of experienced guides. You should also be aware of the dangers of polar bears, which are known to roam the surrounding areas.

Health in Sisimiut

Sisimiut offers modern healthcare facilities, including the Sisimiut Health Center, which provides medical care and emergency services. Travelers are advised to obtain travel insurance before embarking on their journey to Sisimiut and carry a basic first-aid kit with them.

Getting to Sisimiut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sisimiut is accessible by air and sea, with regular connections to major cities in Greenland and Europe. The Sisimiut Airport is located just 5 kilometers from the town center, with regular flights from Kangerlussuaq and Nuuk. You can also travel to Sisimiut by sea, with regular ferry services from Ilulissat and other nearby towns.

Airports Near Sisimiut

  1. Sisimiut Airport - 5 km from the town center
  2. Kangerlussuaq Airport - 178 km from Sisimiut

Bus Stations Near Sisimiut

There are no bus stations in Sisimiut, but you can take a taxi or arrange for a shuttle service from the airport or ferry terminal to the town center.

Other Options

You can also travel to Sisimiut by boat or helicopter, with private charters or tourist packages available.

How to Get Around in Sisimiut

Sisimiut is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. However, you can also hire a taxi or rent a bicycle to explore the town’s surroundings or travel to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Sisimiut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sisimiut is a good starting point for exploring the natural wonders of Western Greenland. You can visit nearby towns, such as Ilulissat, Disko Island, or Kangerlussuaq, which offer a range of exciting adventures and activities. You can also embark on a cruise to the nearby fjords and glaciers or book a hiking tour to explore the surrounding mountains.

Conclusion

Sisimiut is a hidden gem in Western Greenland, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. With its friendly locals, rich history, and stunning landscapes, Sisimiut is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Sisimiut and explore the town’s hidden wonders. Safe travels!
