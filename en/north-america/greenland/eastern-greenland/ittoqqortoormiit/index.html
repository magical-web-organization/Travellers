Introduction to Ittoqqortoormiit

Ittoqqortoormiit is a small village located in Eastern Greenland, on the southern side of the world’s largest fjord system, Scoresbysund. It has a population of around 450 people, and its isolation from the rest of the world, stunningly rugged terrain, and unique cultural heritage make it an ideal destination for adventurous travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Ittoqqortoormiit

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Ittoqqortoormiit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Ittoqqortoormiit: This museum showcases the history and culture of the region with fascinating exhibits about the traditional Inuit way of life, hunting, and fishing techniques.
  • Navy Building: A historic building that was used as a military base in the past. It is now open to the public.

Natural Attractions of Ittoqqortoormiit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ilvinaq: A nearby viewpoint that offers breathtaking views of Scoresbysund fjord system and glaciers.
  • Northern Lights: Visit Ittoqqortoormiit during the winter months to experience the captivating Northern Lights.
  • Boat Tour: Take a boat tour through the fjords, which is the best way to explore the stunning landscape and spot wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ittoqqortoormiit

Ittoqqortoormiit offers several hiking and biking opportunities, including:

  • The Tasiilaq Trail: This trail is approximately 5 km long, which can be challenging but offers a rewarding view of the town.
  • The Glacier Trail: It is a 10 km mountain bike and hiking trail leading up to a glacier for magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Mountain Trail: You can ride a snowmobile or hike up the mountain trail to enjoy the scenic views and also spot wildlife such as reindeer and arctic foxes.

Best Things to Do in Ittoqqortoormiit: Activities and Tours

Check out the following activities and tours to do in Ittoqqortoormiit:

  • Dog-Sledding Tour: Touring through the wilderness by a dog sled is an adventure you should not miss. Enjoy the beautiful landscape and feel the warmth of the huskies while exploring offbeat routes.
  • Fishing Tour: Ittoqqortoormiit is ideal for fishing, and you can catch Greenlandic halibut and arctic char with the help of a guide.
  • Kayaking Tour: Ittoqqortoormiit has several kayaking tours for tourists providing the opportunity to witness the iceberg formations and whales.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ittoqqortoormiit

Day 1-2: Arrive in Ittoqqortoormiit, explore the town center, and visit Museum Ittoqqortoormiit

Day 3: Go dog sledding through the wilderness

Day 4: Take a boat tour to explore the stunning fjords and glaciers

Day 5: Experience the Northern Lights and take in the beauty of the skies

Day 6: Go on a fishing tour or kayaking tour

Day 7: Hike or bike through the mountain trails and enjoy the scenic views

Food and Drink Scene of Ittoqqortoormiit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suaasat: A traditional Greenlandic soup made with seal, whale, or other meats.
  • Mattak: Whale skin and blubber, served as a delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Kaffeemik: A local social gathering with coffee, tea, and plenty of sweets.
  • Greenlandic Beer: Try the local beer made with arctic herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Ittoqqortoormiit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ittoqqortoormiit accommodations are limited, but there are a few options including:

  • Polar Lodge: This place offers comfortable rooms with shared facilities.
  • Gardar Hostel: A budget-friendly option with basic amenities.
  • Arctic Dream: A luxury lodge with private rooms and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Ittoqqortoormiit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Qiviut: Muskox woolen garments which are a famous local product.
  • Inuit Art: Purchase traditional Inuit carvings made from bone, ivory and soapstone.

Local Culture and Customs of Ittoqqortoormiit

Greenland is predominantly an Inuit culture, and Ittoqqortoormiit retains its unique traditional lifestyle. The locals follow Inuit customs, including hunting and fishing for their food and preserving their food items. Visitors should be respectful and learn about the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Ittoqqortoormiit

The best time to visit Ittoqqortoormiit is between May and October when the temperature is milder, and you can enjoy various outdoor activities like sightseeing, hiking, and hunting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ittoqqortoormiit

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and comply with their customs.
  • Learn about the Inuit way of life through museum visits and cultural activities.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter and maintain the cleanliness of the destination.
  • Do not invade the privacy of local Greenlandic people during hunts and other activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ittoqqortoormiit

Ittoqqortoormiit is a safe destination with a low crime rate. Do not leave your valuables unattended during excursions, and always follow the advice of your guide while exploring the wilderness.

Walking and snowmobiling are the common modes of transportation in Ittoqqortoormiit. You can also hire a local guide to take you through various tours and activities.

Where to Go Next from Ittoqqortoormiit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tasiilaq: A small town located near Ittoqqortoormiit with unique culture and wooden houses. You can visit the town via a snowmobile or the more traditional local way by dog sled.
  • Scoresby Sund: A vast ice-filled fjord system that is located close to Ittoqqortoormiit. It is the perfect destination for a boat tour.

Conclusion
