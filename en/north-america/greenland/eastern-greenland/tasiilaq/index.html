Introduction to Tasiilaq

Located in Eastern Greenland, Tasiilaq is a quaint town with colorful houses, stunning natural scenery, and a rich cultural heritage. It’s an excellent destination for those looking for an adventure, a peaceful escape, or a chance to learn about Greenlandic culture. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll help you discover the best places to visit, local food, must-do activities, and essential tips to make your trip to Tasiilaq unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Tasiilaq

Tasiilaq is a small town, but there are plenty of places to explore. Here are some of the must-visit spots in Tasiilaq:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tasiilaq Museum: Discover the unique history, culture, and traditions of Greenlandic Inuit people at Tasiilaq Museum.
  • Church of the Saviour: A beautiful wooden church situated on a hill with panoramic views of Tasiilaq.
  • Ilimanaq Settlement: A small settlement nearby Tasiilaq where you can witness authentic Inuit culture and beautiful natural scenery.
  • Ammassalik Old Cemetery: A peaceful place to visit and explore the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Tasiilaq: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hvitsekur Glacier: A stunning glacier that you can visit on a hiking tour or a boat trip.
  • Kulusuk Island: A tiny island with breathtaking landscapes and an opportunity to see icebergs, whales, and seals.
  • Tasermiut Fjord: One of the most beautiful fjords in Greenland, with steep mountains and glaciers.
  • Aputsiaq Waterfall: A mesmerizing waterfall that drops into the Tasermiut Fjord.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tasiilaq

Tasiilaq is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes you can take:

  • Valley of Flowers: A hiking trail that leads you through a valley filled with colorful flowers.
  • Tasiilaq to Kulusuk: A challenging but rewarding trek that takes you from Tasiilaq to Kulusuk Island.
  • Ammassalik Island: A scenic route that lets you explore the local landscapes and wildlife.
  • Summit of Polheim: A challenging trail that takes you to the highest peak in the area.

Best Things to Do in Tasiilaq: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to do and tours to take in Tasiilaq. Here are some of the best:

  • Dog Sledding: A unique and exciting way to explore the snowy landscapes of Tasiilaq.
  • Fishing: A popular activity that lets you catch some of the freshest fish in the world.
  • Boat Tours: A chance to see the beautiful glaciers, fjords, and icebergs up close.
  • Northern Lights Watching: A magical experience to witness the Aurora Borealis light up the night sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tasiilaq

If you’re planning to spend a week in Tasiilaq, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Tasiilaq and explore the town
2 Take a boat tour to Kulusuk Island
3 Hike the Valley of Flowers trail
4 Visit the Hvitsekur Glacier and go dog sledding
5 Go fishing and enjoy a seafood dinner
6 Take a boat tour to Tasermiut Fjord and Aputsiaq Waterfall

Food and Drink Scene of Tasiilaq

Tasiilaq has a unique cuisine that revolves around seafood, wild game, and local produce. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kiviak: A traditional dish made of fermented seabirds, usually served during winter.
  • Mattak: Raw whale skin and blubber, a delicacy among the locals.
  • Smoked and Salted Fish: A staple food that comes in various flavors, including halibut, arctic char, and salmon.
  • Greenlandic-style Sushi: Made with local ingredients, such as seal, muskox, and reindeer.

Local Beverages

  • Kaffeemik: A traditional coffee ceremony where locals invite guests to their homes for coffee, cake, and conversation.
  • Greenlandic Beer: Made with arctic herbs and barley, several local breweries offer unique beer flavors.
  • Inuit Herbal Tea: Made from local herbs, such as crowberry, Labrador tea, and fireweed.

Where to Stay in Tasiilaq: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tasiilaq has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Angmagssalik: A cozy hotel with beautiful views of the fjord and stylish rooms.
  • Seaman’s Home: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and excellent local food.
  • The Red House: A charming hostel with affordable dorms, private rooms and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Tasiilaq: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tasiilaq offers several unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home. Here are some of the best:

  • Qiviut: A soft and warm wool made from the undercoat of muskoxen.
  • Ulu Knife: A traditional Inuit knife that comes in various shapes and sizes.
  • Inuit Artifacts: A wide variety of handcrafted Inuit artifacts, such as seal skin boots, tupilaks, and kayaks.
  • Greenlandic Music: A unique style of music that blends traditional Inuit and modern sounds.

Local Culture and Customs of Tasiilaq

Greenlandic Inuit culture is deeply rooted in Tasiilaq. The locals are friendly, hospitable, and eager to share their traditions and customs. Here are some essential things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the local customs: The Inuit culture has unique traditions, such as Kaffeemik and storytelling. Be respectful and ask before participating.
  • Dress accordingly: The weather can be unpredictable, so bring warm and waterproof clothes.
  • Learn some words in Greenlandic: The locals appreciate it when visitors try to learn a few words in their language.
  • Take off your shoes when entering homes: It’s customary to take off your shoes when entering a Greenlandic home.

Best Time to Visit Tasiilaq

The best time to visit Tasiilaq is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is mild, and the days are long, which gives you more time to explore the beautiful landscapes. The winter months from December to February are also a great time to visit if you want to witness the Northern Lights and participate in winter activities like dog sledding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tasiilaq

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Tasiilaq:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs: Greenlandic Inuit culture is unique and should be respected and appreciated.
  • Bring warm and waterproof clothes: The weather can be unpredictable, and it’s better to be prepared.
  • Try local food and drinks: Greenlandic cuisine is one of the most unique in the world and something you shouldn’t miss.
  • Learn some words in Greenlandic: The locals appreciate it when visitors try to learn a few words in their language.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: The natural landscapes of Tasiilaq are breathtaking and should be kept clean.
  • Don’t touch or disturb wildlife: Greenlandic wildlife, such as whales and seals, should be observed from a safe distance and never disturbed or touched.
  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping is not customary in Greenland, but it’s always appreciated.

Safety and Health

Tasiilaq is a safe and healthy place to visit, but like any other place, there are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Tasiilaq

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Tasiilaq is a small town, but it’s always best to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Be cautious when hiking and biking: The hiking trails and biking routes can be challenging, and it’s better to go with a guide who knows the area.
  • Respect the wildlife: The wildlife in Tasiilaq is unique and should be observed from a safe distance.

Health in Tasiilaq

  • Bring medications: There are limited medical facilities in Tasiilaq, so it’s best to bring your medications.
  • Stay hydrated: The weather can be dry, and it’s essential to stay hydrated.
  • Be prepared for motion sickness: If you’re taking a boat or plane, be prepared for motion sickness.

Getting to Tasiilaq: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tasiilaq can only be reached by plane or boat. Here are some options to get to Tasiilaq:

Airports Near Tasiilaq

  • Kulusuk Airport: The closest airport to Tasiilaq, located on Kulusuk Island.
  • Nerlerit Inaat Airport: Located in southwest Greenland, you can take a boat or helicopter to reach Tasiilaq.

Other Options

  • Boat: You can take a boat from other towns in Greenland, such as Nuuk or Ilulissat, to reach Tasiilaq.
  • Helicopter: A faster and more expensive option, you can take a helicopter from other towns in Greenland.

How to Get Around in Tasiilaq

Tasiilaq is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot. However, if you want to explore more of the surrounding areas, you can consider renting a bike or taking a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Tasiilaq: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several other beautiful destinations to explore near Tasiilaq, including:

  • Sermilik Fjord: A stunning fjord located 25 km from Tasiilaq.
  • Kangerlussuaq: A small town with a unique desert landscape and the largest airport in Greenland.
  • South Greenland: A region famous for its Viking history and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

Tasiilaq is a unique and beautiful destination that’s perfect for adventure seekers, culture lovers, and nature enthusiasts. With this comprehensive tour guide, we hope you’re inspired to visit Tasiilaq and discover all the must-visit places, unique cuisine, and local traditions. Remember to prepare for the weather, respect the locals and wildlife, and enjoy the beautiful landscapes.
