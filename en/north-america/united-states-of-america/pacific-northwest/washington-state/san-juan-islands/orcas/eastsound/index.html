A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Eastsound, Washington

Are you looking for an adventure-packed city in the heart of the Pacific Northwest? Look no further than Eastsound, Washington! Nestled in the San Juan Islands, Eastsound boasts a unique combination of stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, a foodie, a history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Eastsound has something for everyone.

Introduction to Eastsound

Eastsound is a charming community located on the eastern shore of Orcas Island in the San Juan Islands, Washington. It is accessible by ferry, plane or car, and offers a diverse landscape of shoreline, forests, and mountains. The town has a strong sense of community, with a vibrant arts scene, cultural events, and outdoor activities for all ages. Whether you’re visiting for a day or a week, there’s plenty to see and do in Eastsound.

List of Places to Visit in Eastsound

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eastsound has a rich history, which is reflected in its many cultural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • Orcas Island Historical Museum: Discover the history and culture of the San Juan Islands at this fascinating museum. Exhibits showcase the region’s natural and cultural heritage, including Native American artifacts, farm equipment, and vintage photographs.

  • Crow Valley School Museum: Explore a restored one-room schoolhouse from Orcas Island’s early days. The museum displays antique furnishings, educational materials, and historical photographs.

  • Moran Mansion Museum: This museum offers guided tours of the restored 1908 Moran Mansion, which was once the summer home of Seattle industrialist Robert Moran. Visitors can learn about Moran’s life and times, as well as the history of the San Juan Islands.

Natural Attractions of Eastsound: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eastsound is blessed with a stunning natural landscape, which offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Moran State Park: This expansive park covers over 5,000 acres of forest, lakes, and mountain peaks. Visitors can hike, bike, swim, fish, camp, and picnic in the park, as well as take in stunning views of the San Juan Islands, the Olympic Mountains, and Mount Baker.

  • Turtleback Mountain Preserve: This nature preserve boasts over 1000 acres of meadows, forests, wetlands, and shoreline. Visitors can hike, bike, or horseback ride on the miles of trails that wind through the preserve, offering breathtaking views of the San Juan Islands, Mount Constitution, and Vancouver Island.

  • Orcas Island Winery: This family-owned winery produces award-winning wines from locally-grown grapes. Visitors can tour the vineyard and winery, sample the wines, and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastsound

Eastsound is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with miles of trails through forests, shoreline, and mountains. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Eastsound:

  • Mount Constitution Trail: This challenging hike leads to the summit of the highest peak in the San Juan Islands, offering breathtaking views of the islands, the Olympics, and the Cascades.

  • Turtleback Mountain Trail: This moderate hike offers stunning views of the San Juan Islands, Mount Constitution, and Vancouver Island.

  • Cascade Lake Loop Trail: This easy hike circles around a beautiful mountain lake, offering peaceful scenery and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Eastsound: Activities and Tours

Eastsound offers a wide range of activities and tours for all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Eastsound:

  • Whale Watching Tours: Explore the waters around the San Juan Islands and spot majestic whales, porpoises, seals, and sea lions.

  • Kayaking: Paddle through the pristine waters around Eastsound and discover hidden coves, sea caves, and wildlife.

  • Lavender Farm Tours: Explore the fragrant fields of local lavender farms and learn about the history and uses of this aromatic herb.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastsound

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Eastsound, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Explore Eastsound Village by foot, visiting local shops and restaurants.

  • Day 2: Take a whale watching tour and spend the afternoon kayaking.

  • Day 3: Hike to the top of Mount Constitution and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Day 4: Visit the Orcas Island Winery for a wine tasting and tour.

  • Day 5: Take a tour of the Moran Mansion Museum and the Orcas Island Historical Museum.

  • Day 6: Spend the day exploring Turtleback Mountain Preserve on horseback.

  • Day 7: Go on a Lavender Farm Tour and relax with a picnic at one of Eastsound’s beautiful parks.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastsound

Eastsound offers a diverse food and drink scene, with local restaurants, wineries, and breweries. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Eastsound:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Island Grown at Buck Bay: This seafood restaurant specializes in fresh, local seafood, including oysters, crab cakes, and salmon chowder.

  • The Kitchen: This farm-to-table restaurant offers a rotating menu of seasonal dishes, using locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Chimayo: This Mexican restaurant serves up delicious tacos, enchiladas, and margaritas.

Local Beverages

  • Orcas Island Distillery: Taste award-winning apple brandy, gin, and cider at this family-owned distillery.

  • Island Hoppin’ Brewery: Enjoy a cold craft beer and live music at this popular local brewery.

Where to Stay in Eastsound: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastsound offers a range of accommodations for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Eastsound:

  • Outlook Inn: This historic inn offers elegant rooms, a waterfront location, and stunning views of Eastsound Bay.

  • Orcas Island Campgrounds: This expansive campground offers tent and RV sites, as well as cabins, fire pits, and scenic views.

  • Turtleback Inn: This charming B&B offers cozy rooms, a homemade breakfast, and stunning views of Turtleback Mountain.

What to Buy in Eastsound: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eastsound offers a variety of unique local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to bring home from Eastsound:

  • Lavender products: Eastsound is famous for its fragrant lavender farms, offering everything from soaps and candles to essential oils and teas.

  • Orca souvenirs: Take home a piece of the San Juan Islands with a souvenir featuring the region’s iconic orca whales.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastsound

Eastsound is a close-knit community with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including:

  • Treading lightly: Eastsound is dedicated to preserving its beautiful natural environment, so visitors are encouraged to minimize their impact and leave no trace.

  • Supporting local businesses: Eastsound’s economy relies on tourism, so supporting local businesses is a great way to contribute to the community’s sustainability.

Best Time to Visit Eastsound

The best time to visit Eastsound is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the quieter seasons of spring and fall, when the crowds are smaller, and the scenery is breathtaking. Winter can be chilly and rainy, but it offers a chance to experience Eastsound’s cozy charms and holiday events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastsound

Do:

  • Explore the great outdoors: Eastsound is renowned for its natural beauty, so make sure to spend plenty of time hiking, biking, and kayaking.

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Eastsound has a strong sense of community, so please be respectful to locals and preserve the natural environment.

  • Support local businesses: Eastsound’s economy relies on tourism, so support local businesses to help the community thrive.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in the parks or on the trails: Please pack out what you pack in to maintain the natural beauty of Eastsound.

  • Disturb the wildlife: Eastsound is home to diverse wildlife, so please observe from a distance and avoid disturbing them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastsound

Eastsound is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. Here are some safety tips:

  • Always wear appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.

  • Watch for weather changes and be prepared for sudden storms.

  • Follow the rules of the road when driving or biking.

Health in Eastsound

Eastsound has a small medical center and pharmacy, but visitors should still take precautions to maintain their health. Here are some health tips:

  • Bring any necessary medications and first aid supplies.

  • Drink plenty of water and eat well-balanced meals.

  • Use insect repellent and sunscreen to avoid sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Eastsound: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastsound is accessible by ferry, plane or car. Here are some transportation options to get to Eastsound:

  • By ferry: From Anacortes, take a ferry to Orcas Island, and then drive to Eastsound.

  • By airplane: Orcas Island Airport offers several daily flights from Seattle, Bellingham, and other regional airports.

  • By car: Drive to Anacortes, and then take a ferry to Orcas Island.

How to Get Around in Eastsound

Eastsound is a compact town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, for those who prefer to drive or bike, car and bike rentals are available.

Where to Go Next from Eastsound: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eastsound makes an excellent base for exploring the San Juan Islands and the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • San Juan Island: Explore the charming town of Friday Harbor and visit the San Juan Islands National Monument.

  • Lopez Island: Enjoy the rural charm of this quiet island, with its beaches, parks, and nature reserves.

  • Victoria, BC: Take a day trip across the border to experience the beautiful city of Victoria and its famous landmarks, like the Butchart Gardens and the Royal BC Museum.

Conclusion

Eastsound, Washington, is a unique and beautiful destination in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, or just relaxing and enjoying the scenery, Eastsound has something for everyone. We hope this guide has given you the inspiration and information you need to plan your next trip to Eastsound – we can’t wait to welcome you!
