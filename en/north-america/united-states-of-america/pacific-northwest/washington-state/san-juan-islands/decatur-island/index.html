Decatur Island is a secluded gem located in the San Juan Islands of Washington state. Known for its stunning natural beauty and peaceful ambiance, this island is an ideal destination for anyone looking to escape the bustle of city life and immerse themselves in serene surroundings. In this comprehensive tour guide to Decatur Island, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip. From the top attractions to the best accommodations and more, let’s get started.

Introduction to Decatur Island

Located in the heart of the San Juan Islands, Decatur Island is a secluded paradise that boasts stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a welcoming community. With a population of just 77 people, the island offers a peaceful escape from the fast-paced city life. Visitors can expect breathtaking views of the Salish Sea, lush forests, and an abundance of wildlife, including bald eagles, otters, and seals. The island is accessible only by private boat or charter plane, adding to its secluded charm.

List of Places to Visit in Decatur Island

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply seeking a relaxing retreat, Decatur Island offers an array of attractions and activities. Here are some of the top places to visit on the island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Decatur Island Historical Museum: This small yet charming museum showcases artifacts and exhibits that highlight the island’s rich history, including its Native American heritage and early European settlements.
  • Decatur Island Community Center: A hub of the island’s community, this center hosts various cultural events and programs throughout the year, including live music and art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Decatur Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lover’s Lane: A picturesque hiking trail that winds through a dense forest and offers stunning views of the Salish Sea.
  • Decatur Island Beach: This secluded beach provides a peaceful escape from bustling tourist spots and an opportunity to observe local wildlife such as marine birds and seals.
  • Kayaking and Paddle Boarding: With calm waters and serene surroundings, Decatur Island is an ideal location for kayaking or paddleboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Decatur Island

For outdoor enthusiasts, Decatur Island offers a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some of the top routes include:

  • Lover’s Lane Trail: A 1.5-mile trek that winds through a dense forest and offers stunning views of the Salish Sea.
  • Clayton Beach Trail: A 2-mile hike through a lush forest and along the coastline that takes hikers to Clayton Beach, a secluded spot that provides for a perfect picnic location.
  • Decatur Island Loop: A 16-mile biking route that circles the island and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Decatur Island: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking relaxation or adventure, Decatur Island offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are some of the top things to do on the island:

  • Whale-watching tours: The San Juan Islands are renowned for their abundant marine wildlife, including orcas, humpback whales, and gray whales. Whale-watching tours are available from various tour operators on the island.
  • Kayaking and paddleboarding: With calm waters and serene surroundings, Decatur Island is an ideal location for kayaking or paddleboarding.
  • Camping: Decatur Island is home to two campgrounds that provide stunning views of the Salish Sea, peaceful surroundings, and various amenities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Decatur Island

If you’re planning a week-long vacation on Decatur Island, here is a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Arrival and settle into your accommodation. Day 2: Explore the island’s history and heritage by visiting the Decatur Island Historical Museum and the Decatur Island Community Center. Day 3-5: Enjoy outdoor adventures such as hiking, biking, kayaking and paddleboarding. Day 6: Take a whale-watching tour and observe the abundant marine wildlife. Day 7: Relax and spend time exploring the island’s scenic beauty or participate in a cultural event or program organized by the Decatur Island Community Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Decatur Island

Despite being a small island, Decatur Island features a variety of dining options that cater to all tastes and preferences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood: Being surrounded by water, Decatur Island offers an abundance of fresh seafood such as oysters, clams, and salmon.
  • Local cheeses and wines: The island is known for its delicious artisanal cheeses and wines, which make for a perfect accompaniment to any meal.

Local Beverages

  • San Juan Island Brewery: This brewery is located on nearby San Juan Island and offers delicious craft beers that are made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Buck Bay Shellfish Farms: This seafood farm, located on Lopez Island, offers a variety of wine and beer options that complement their fresh seafood.

Local Culture and Customs of Decatur Island

The local community of Decatur Island is warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect the island’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. The island has a strong conservation focus and encourages visitors to practice sustainable tourism practices.

Best Time to Visit Decatur Island

The best time to visit Decatur Island is during the summer months (June - September) when the weather is warm, and the island is lush and vibrant. However, all seasons offer distinct experiences and provide opportunities for various activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Decatur Island

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural beauty and wildlife.
  • Read up on sustainable tourism practices and practice them while on the island.
  • Embrace the local culture and participate in cultural events and programs organized by the Decatur Island Community Center.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural surroundings.
  • Ignore wildlife warnings and attempt to approach animals too closely.
  • Disrespect or disregard the island’s cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Decatur Island

Overall, Decatur Island is a safe and peaceful destination, with a low incidence of crime. However, visitors should take usual precautions such as locking doors and securing personal belongings.

Health in Decatur Island

Decatur Island features a small clinic that can deal with minor injuries or illnesses, but visitors are advised to bring all necessary medications and seek medical attention on nearby San Juan Island for any serious medical issues.

Getting to Decatur Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Decatur Island

The nearest airports to Decatur Island are the San Juan Island Airport and the Bellingham International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Decatur Island

There are no bus stations on Decatur Island. Travelers can take a ferry from nearby Anacortes to reach the island.

Other Options

Private boat and charter plane are also popular options for reaching Decatur Island.

How to Get Around in Decatur Island

Decatur Island is a small island, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bike. Rental cars and taxis are also available for those who prefer a more straightforward mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Decatur Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Decatur Island, nearby destinations such as Lopez Island, San Juan Island, and Orcas Island offer a range of attractions and activities.

Conclusion

Decatur Island is a hidden gem that offers a peaceful and serene escape from the fast-paced city life.
