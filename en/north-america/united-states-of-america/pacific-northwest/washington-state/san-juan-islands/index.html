Introduction to San Juan Islands

Located in the Pacific Northwest of the United States, the San Juan Islands are an archipelago of 172 named islands and reefs, most of which are uninhabited. Known for their stunning natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and charming small towns, the San Juan Islands attract visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re interested in hiking, kayaking, whale watching, or simply enjoying a peaceful retreat, there’s something for everyone on these islands.

List of Places to Visit in San Juan Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Juan Island National Historical Park: This park includes several sites that commemorate the joint military occupation of the island by the United States and Great Britain in the mid-19th century. Highlights include the American Camp Visitor Center and the English Camp Historic District.
  • Whale Museum: Learn about the natural history and conservation efforts of whales and other marine mammals in the Pacific Northwest. The museum also features exhibits on the local Native American tribes and the cultural significance of whales in their heritage.
  • San Juan Islands Museum of Art: This contemporary art museum showcases works by regional and national artists and hosts a variety of exhibitions and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of San Juan Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lime Kiln Point State Park: Known as one of the best places in the world to view Orcas from land, this park also boasts several scenic hiking trails and a historic lighthouse.
  • Moran State Park: Located on Orcas Island, this park offers miles of hiking trails, panoramic views of the surrounding islands, and several scenic drives.
  • San Juan Island Scenic Byway: This 24-mile route takes you through charming towns, rolling farmland, and breathtaking coastal views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Juan Islands

  • Mount Finlayson: This moderately challenging hike on San Juan Island offers beautiful views of the surrounding islands and Vancouver Island.
  • Turtleback Mountain Preserve: Located on Orcas Island, this preserve features several hiking trails with stunning views of the island and surrounding waterways.
  • San Juan Island Trail: This 31-mile trail takes you around the perimeter of San Juan Island and showcases its diverse landscapes and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in San Juan Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Several tour operators offer whale watching tours, which are a great way to see Orcas, humpbacks, and other marine mammals in their natural habitat.
  • Kayaking: The calm waters around the San Juan Islands are perfect for kayaking, with several tour operators offering guided tours or equipment rentals.
  • Wine Tasting: The San Juan Islands are home to several vineyards and wineries, which offer tastings and tours of their facilities.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Juan Islands

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your lodging. Explore Friday Harbor and check out the Whale Museum. Day 2: Go kayaking or take a whale watching tour. End the day with a sunset picnic at Lime Kiln Point State Park. Day 3: Hike to the top of Mount Finlayson and enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding islands. Day 4: Take a ferry to Orcas Island and explore Moran State Park. Day 5: Visit the San Juan Islands Museum of Art and then go wine tasting at a local vineyard. Day 6: Rent a bike and explore San Juan Island on the San Juan Island Trail. Day 7: Departure day

Food and Drink Scene of San Juan Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dungeness Crab: A local favorite, you can find this delicious crab in many island restaurants.
  • Oysters: Several local oyster farms offer fresh, delicious oysters, which can be eaten raw or cooked.
  • Salmon: With an abundance of wild salmon in the surrounding waters, this is a staple of the island cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wine: Several vineyards on the islands produce award-winning wines, including varietals such as Pinot Noir and Pinot Gris.
  • San Juan Island Distillery: This family-owned distillery produces a variety of fruit-based spirits, including apple brandy, pear liqueur, and cherry vodka.
  • Local Beer: San Juan Island Brewing Company and Island Hoppin’ Brewery are two of the island’s popular breweries, offering a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in San Juan Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Friday Harbor House: A popular boutique hotel located in Friday Harbor.
  • Earthbox Inn & Spa: A family-friendly hotel with a spa and pool located in Friday Harbor.
  • Lakedale Resort: A picturesque resort located on a lake on San Juan Island.

What to Buy in San Juan Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art and Craftwork: From pottery to jewelry to paintings, several local artists offer unique and beautiful pieces.
  • Lavender Products: The San Juan Island Lavender Farm produces a variety of lavender-based products, including soaps, lotions, and oils.
  • Local Booze: Bring home a bottle of local wine, spirits, or beer as a delicious reminder of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of San Juan Islands

The San Juan Islands have a rich cultural history, with the local Native American tribes playing a significant role in the area’s heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the traditions and customs of the tribes and to learn about their history and contributions to the community.

Best Time to Visit San Juan Islands

The best time to visit the San Juan Islands is during the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the wildlife is most active. However, this is also the busiest time of year, and prices for accommodations and activities may be higher. Spring and fall are also good times to visit, with milder weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Juan Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and maintain a safe distance from marine mammals such as Orcas and seals.
  • Pack warm clothing and rain gear, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Use caution when driving, as many of the roads are narrow and winding.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or collect any natural artifacts or wildlife.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions of the Native American tribes. Treat all cultural sites and artifacts with respect and care.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Juan Islands

The San Juan Islands are generally safe, with low levels of crime. Visitors should take normal precautions when leaving valuables in their vehicles and when walking alone at night.

Health in San Juan Islands

Visitors should always carry a sufficient supply of any necessary medications and should be aware that medical facilities on the islands are limited. In case of medical emergency, dial 911.

Getting to San Juan Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Juan Islands

The nearest major airport is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is located about 100 miles from the San Juan Islands. Several smaller airports also serve the area, including Friday Harbor Airport, Orcas Island Airport, and Lopez Island Airport.

Bus Stations Near San Juan Islands

Greyhound buses serve the nearby city of Anacortes, where visitors can take a ferry to the San Juan Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the San Juan Islands by private boat or ferry. Washington State Ferries operates several routes between the islands and the mainland, with schedules varying by season.

How to Get Around in San Juan Islands

The best way to explore the islands is by car or bike. Visitors can rent cars, bikes, or even mopeds, at several locations throughout the islands. A local shuttle service is also available, as well as taxis and public transportation.

Where to Go Next from San Juan Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Olympic Peninsula: Head south to explore the rugged coastline, ancient rainforests, and stunning mountain views of the Olympic Peninsula.
  • Vancouver Island: Take a ferry north to visit our Canadian neighbors and explore the beautiful landscapes and cultural attractions of Vancouver Island.
  • North Cascades National Park: Located east of the San Juan Islands, this park offers spectacular mountain scenery and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing retreat, the San Juan Islands have something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and charming small towns, the islands are the perfect destination for your next Pacific Northwest getaway.
