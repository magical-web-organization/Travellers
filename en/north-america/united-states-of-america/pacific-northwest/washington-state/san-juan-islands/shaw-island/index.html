Introduction to Shaw Island

Shaw Island is a quiet, secluded gem located in the San Juan Islands of the Pacific Northwest in Washington state, USA. Measuring only 7.7 square miles in size, Shaw Island’s rugged landscape and pristine natural beauty provide a perfect oasis for adventurers and nature enthusiasts eager to explore the outdoors, enjoy scenic views, and experience the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Shaw Island

Looking for things to do in Shaw Island? Make sure to check out the following attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Shaw Island Historical Society Museum: learn about the island’s rich history and its past inhabitants, including Native Americans and European settlers.
  • Our Lady of the Rock Monastery: a Catholic Benedictine monastery is a must-visit for its stunning architecture, beautiful gardens, and peaceful atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Shaw Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shaw County Park: a great spot for sunset watching, picnicking, and camping with beachfront campsites available.
  • Shaw Island Nature Preserve: bird watching, hiking, and nature observation.
  • Indian Cove Beach: a lovely, secluded beach accessible by hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shaw Island

Shaw Island is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Horseshoe Loop Trail: a moderate 3.4-mile loop through forests, pastures, and beaches that provides spectacular views of Mt. Baker and the surrounding islands.
  • Shaw Island Scenic Byway: a 7.5-mile paved road that makes for an excellent bike ride while taking in the island’s stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Shaw Island: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and paddleboarding: rent kayaks or paddleboards from Shaw Island Kayak Tours and explore the coastline and nearby islands.
  • Fishing: try your hand at fishing on the island’s rivers and lakes or charter a boat from nearby Anacortes.
  • Whale watching: head to the nearby San Juan Islands for a chance to see orcas, humpback whales, and other marine wildlife in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shaw Island

Here’s a sample itinerary for a one-week visit to Shaw Island:

Day Activity
1 Arrive and settle in at Shaw County Park
2 Hike the Horseshoe Loop Trail
3 Explore Our Lady of the Rock Monastery and its gardens
4 Kayak around Shaw Island
5 Visit the Shaw Island Historical Society Museum
6 Fish at a nearby lake or river
7 Whale watching in the San Juan Islands

Food and Drink Scene of Shaw Island

Shaw Island’s food and drink scene might not be extensive, but it offers excellent quality and delicious flavors. Make sure to try the following dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Island-raised beef: Shaw Island’s farms produce high-quality beef, which you can find at the island’s farm stands or on the menu at local restaurants.
  • Seafood: fresh seafood is abundant in the San Juan Islands, and Shaw Island is no exception. Try dishes like salmon, crab, and oysters.
  • Organic produce: many farms on the island grow organic produce, which is often used in local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • San Juan Island Brewing Company: a family-owned brewery that makes a variety of craft beers.
  • Westcott Bay Cider: a small, locally-owned cidery that makes delicious, hand-crafted ciders.

Where to Stay in Shaw Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shaw Island doesn’t have many accommodation options, but the ones available are excellent choices for those looking for a peaceful, secluded retreat.

  • Shaw Island County Park: a popular camping spot with beachfront campsites available.
  • The Inn at Ship Bay: a charming bed and breakfast with stunning water views.
  • Shaw Island Lodge: a guesthouse with cozy and comfortable rooms and fantastic views of the surrounding forest and water.

What to Buy in Shaw Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Shaw Island, make sure to grab some souvenirs and local products to bring home.

  • Local honey: the island is home to many beekeepers who produce high-quality honey.
  • Lavender products: several farms on the island grow lavender, and you can find many lovely lavender-based products in local stores and farm stands.
  • Handmade pottery: many artists on the island make beautiful pottery pieces that would make lovely souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Shaw Island

Shaw Island is known for being a peaceful, rural community with a rich history and a strong connection to nature. Visitors are expected to show respect for the island’s natural assets and follow sustainable tourism practices.

Best Time to Visit Shaw Island

The best time to visit Shaw Island is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shaw Island

Consider the following do’s and don’ts when visiting Shaw Island:

Do:

  • Follow “leave no trace” principles and pack out what you pack in.
  • Respect the island’s wildlife and natural resources.
  • Patronize local businesses and support the island’s economy.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disturb wildlife.
  • Break any local rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shaw Island

Shaw Island is generally a very safe and peaceful place, but visitors should take the usual precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. It’s always best to be prepared with proper gear and supplies.

Health in Shaw Island

Shaw Island has limited medical services, so visitors should ensure they have access to emergency medical care if needed and bring any necessary medications.

Getting to Shaw Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shaw Island

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA): 2 hours and 15 minutes by car to Anacortes Ferry Terminal(126 mi ) , where you can board a ferry to Shaw Island.

Bus Stations Near Shaw Island

The closest bus station to Shaw Island is in Anacortes, so visitors should arrange transportation to the island in advance.

Other Options

  • Drive: if you have access to a car, you can drive to Anacortes and take a ferry to Shaw Island.
  • Bicycle: ride your bike to Anacortes and take it on the ferry to Shaw Island.

How to Get Around in Shaw Island

The best way to get around Shaw Island is by car or bicycle. Visitors can also explore the island on foot by hiking its many trails.

Where to Go Next from Shaw Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shaw Island is a great starting point for exploring the San Juan Islands. Visitors can take a ferry to nearby islands like Orcas Island and San Juan Island and explore their many attractions.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and small-town charm, Shaw Island is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to escape into nature and experience the Pacific Northwest’s unique culture. Whether you’re interested in hiking, kayaking, or simply soaking up the island’s peaceful atmosphere, Shaw Island has something to offer everyone.
