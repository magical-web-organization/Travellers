Introduction to Mount St. Helens

Mount St. Helens is a stratovolcano located in the southwest corner of Washington State, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. It is known for its catastrophic eruption in 1980, which was the deadliest and most economically destructive volcanic event in U.S. history. Today, visitors can explore the volcanic landscape of Mount St. Helens and the surrounding area, which is home to several outdoor recreational opportunities, cultural attractions, museums, and fresh local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Mount St. Helens

There are plenty of places to visit in and around Mount St. Helens. Here is a list of top attractions and landmarks in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mount St. Helens Visitor Center: Located at Silver Lake in Castle Rock, this interpretive center features exhibits, films, and displays detailing the natural history of the mountain and its eruption history. It is a great starting point for hiking and other outdoor activities.
  • Science and Learning Center: The Johnston Ridge Observatory offers exhibits and programs about the geological and human history of Mount St. Helens, and a stunning view of the volcano itself, about 5 miles away. It’s only open in the summer, accessible via Highway 504 East from Castle Rock.

Natural Attractions of Mount St. Helens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ape Cave: This lava tube is one of the longest and most popular in the U.S, a great place to explore with flashlights. The cave has two entrances of 1.5-2 miles in length, making it a great place for a day-long hike.
  • Lahar Viewpoint: A unique viewpoint depicting a geological hazard, a Lahar, a catastrophic debris flow, which can flood towns and cities as it did in 1980, this viewpoint is located near milepost 31 on State Route 504.
  • Lava Canyon & Falls: A moderate to advanced trail that leads to a beautiful waterfall and a fascinating canyon formed after a recent eruption of Mount St. Helens in the 1980s.
  • Johnson Ridge Observatory: A science and learning center located on Johnston Ridge, five miles from the mountain, opened seasonally. Visitors can learn more about the geology of the area and the impact of the volcanic eruption while enjoying the beautiful view of the volcanic landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount St. Helen

Mount St. Helens is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best trails include:

  • Hummocks Trail: A family-friendly walk through forests and meadows of the hummocky terrain created by the 1980 eruption.
  • Boundary Trail: A 14-mile trail, offers unparalleled views of the valley below and the mountain itself.
  • Harry’s Ridge Trail: A moderately difficult hike that offers excellent views of the volcanic crater and Spirit Lake, which was famously impacted by the 1980 eruption.

Best Things to Do in Mount St. Helens: Activities and Tours

For a complete experience of Mount St. Helens, make sure you don’t miss these activities and tours:

  • Helicopter Tours: Get a stunning aerial view of the mountain and surrounding landscape on a helicopter.
  • Kayaking Tours: Enjoy the scenery of Silver Lake, the Toutle River, or the nearby Lake Merwin on a guided kayak tour.
  • Fishing: Cast your line into the Toutle, Cowlitz, Kalama, or Lewis River for salmon, trout, steelhead, and other fish species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount St. Helens

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Mount St. Helens:

Day Activity
1 Visit Mount St. Helens Visitor Center, Johnston Ridge Observatory
2 Hike the Hummocks Trail, explore Ape Cave
3 Birdwatching and photography at Coldwater Lake or Yale Lake
4 River rafting on the Toutle River
5 Go fishing on the Kalama River
6 Take a helicopter tour of the mountain and surrounding area
7 Visit local wineries and vineyards in the nearby region

Food and Drink Scene of Mount St. Helens

Must-Try Dishes

When in Mount St. Helens, make sure to try these local dishes and delicacies:

  • Smoked Salmon: One of the best dishes to try in the area, the smoked salmon is often caught in the nearby rivers and smoked fresh for a delicious taste.
  • Blueberry and Huckleberry: These berries grow in the wild near Mount St. Helens. They are used in a variety of dishes such as pancakes, pies, tarts, and syrups.
  • Cascade Creamery Cheese: Made from the milk of local grass-fed cows, Cascade Creamery cheeses come in a variety of flavors and textures, including blue, brie, and cheddar.

Local Beverages

The Mount St. Helens region is known for its local microbreweries and craft beer scene. Some of the best local beverages include:

  • Mt. St. Helens cellars: A local winery specializing in red and white wines made from locally grown grapes.
  • North Jetty Brewing: A local brewery that serves a variety of beers on tap, from IPAs to stouts to lagers.

Where to Stay in Mount St. Helens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in and around Mount St. Helens. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a cozy hostel, you’ll find something that suits your budget and travel style. Some popular options include:

  • The Chalet at Johnston Ridge Observatory: A cozy cabin near the Johnston Ridge Observatory. It sleeps up to 6 people and offers stunning views of the volcano.
  • Silver Lake Resort: Located near the Mount St. Helens Visitor Center, this resort offers waterfront lodging and RV camping.
  • Best Western Oak Meadows Inn: A hotel located in the nearby town of Woodland. It offers a variety of amenities, including an indoor pool and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Mount St. Helens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Mount St. Helens, here are some great local products and souvenirs to consider:

  • Volcanic Ash: A unique souvenir that is available in pottery, glass, and jewelry made from ash and debris from the 1980 volcanic eruption.
  • Local Crafts and Artwork: The region is home to many talented artists and craftsmen, many of whom sell their creations in local gift shops.
  • Cascade Creamery Cheese: A delicious souvenir and a great gift for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount St. Helens

The native Americans of the Pacific Northwest region (of which the Mount St. Helens tribes were a part) have a rich cultural history that encompasses storytelling, art, fishing, and hunting. Visitors can learn more about this history at the regional museums and interpretive centers.

Best Time to Visit Mount St. Helens

The best time to visit Mount St. Helens is during the summer months of June through early September, when weather conditions are most favorable for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, the region is beautiful year-round, and each season offers its own unique charms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount St. Helens

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and sunscreen when hiking.
  • Take advantage of the interpretive centers and educational programs to learn more about the region’s history and geology.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines and warnings.
  • Touch or take any object from the natural environment, whether living or non-living.
  • Smoke or use fire in the surrounding forests and wilderness areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount St. Helens

Mount St. Helens is an active volcanic mountain, and visitors should exercise caution and be aware of potential hazards. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be mindful of weather conditions, especially during thunderstorms or high winds.
  • Stay on designated trails and follow all posted rules and regulations.
  • Be aware of the danger of falling into the volcano’s crater, which is a forbidden zone.

Health in Mount St. Helens

The region is located at high altitude and may pose a challenge for those with respiratory or heart conditions. Visitors with these issues should consult with their healthcare provider before planning a visit.

Getting to Mount St. Helens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount St. Helens

The closest airports to Mount St. Helens are Portland International Airport in Oregon and Seattle-Tacoma International Airport in Washington State.

Bus Stations Near Mount St. Helens

Greyhound Bus Lines and other private operators run regular services to the towns near Mount St. Helens, including Castle Rock and Woodland.

Other Options

  • Car rental agencies offer services in nearby cities, including Portland and Seattle.
  • Amtrak train station is located in Kelso and connects to major cities in Oregon and Washington.

How to Get Around in Mount St. Helens

The best way to explore the area is by car, public transportation, or tour buses. Visitors may also use bikes and kayaks to explore the region by themselves.

Where to Go Next from Mount St. Helens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of other beautiful places to visit in the Pacific Northwest region after exploring Mount St. Helens. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Columbia River Gorge: Located to the south and east of Mount St. Helens, this scenic area offers hiking, boating, and sightseeing opportunities.
  • Multnomah Falls: A famous waterfall located in the Columbia River Gorge, it is an Instagram favorite and one of the most photographed attractions in the area.
  • Portland, Oregon: A vibrant city with a thriving food, art, and culture scene, located just an hour’s drive south of Mount St. Helens.

Conclusion

Mount St. Helens, with its geological landmarks and beautiful outdoor recreation spots, is a destination worth exploring. From hiking and biking trails to kayaking tours, helicopter tours, and local cuisine options, there’s an activity for every type of traveler. Keep this comprehensive travel guide handy for exploring the best of Mount St. Helens and the surrounding area.
