Introduction to Southwest Washington

Located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Southwest Washington is known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and diverse history. The area is home to several cities, including Vancouver, Longview, and Olympia. With its stunning coastline, dense forests, and scenic mountain ranges, Southwest Washington is one of the most picturesque areas in the United States.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with a detailed itinerary for exploring the best of Southwest Washington, including must-visit attractions, delicious local cuisine, and a variety of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Southwest Washington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southwest Washington has a rich history dating back to the early Native American settlements. It is no surprise that the region is home to several museums and historical attractions that provide a glimpse into its past. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical attractions in Southwest Washington:

  • Columbia River Maritime Museum: Located in Astoria, Oregon, just across the river from Southwest Washington, this museum showcases the history of river and ocean commerce in the Pacific Northwest. It is an excellent place to learn about ancient maritime history and explore various exhibits and programs.

  • Fort Vancouver National Historic Site: This famous historical site features a fully restored 19th-century fort that served as a trading post for traders, trappers, and Native Americans. Visitors can learn about Northwest history, walk around the beautiful gardens, and explore the site’s recreational and educational activities.

  • The Pearson Air Museum: Located next to the Fort Vancouver National Historic Site, the Pearson Air Museum features a collection of historic aircraft, engines, and other related artifacts. The museum also hosts various events throughout the year, including aviation-themed shows and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Southwest Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Southwest Washington is its most significant draw, from its stunning coastline to its majestic mountain ranges, and lush forests. Here are some of the must-visit destinations for outdoor enthusiasts in Southwest Washington.

  • Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument: Located in the Gifford Pinchot National Forest, Mount St. Helens is one of the most famous active volcanoes in the world. Visitors can hike around the volcano, check out the Johnston Ridge Observatory, and explore the blast zone.

  • Cape Disappointment State Park and Lighthouse: Located on the Long Beach Peninsula, Cape Disappointment is a beautiful park showcasing stunning Pacific coastline, several trails, lighthouses, and historical forts that date back to WWII. It is an excellent place to hike, fish, and enjoy the scenic beauty of the Pacific Northwest.

  • Olympic National Park: This park is one of the most popular destinations in Southwest Washington. Olympic National Park features rugged mountains, serene beaches, vast wilderness areas, and lush rainforests. Visitors can explore several lodges, campgrounds, hiking trails, and wildlife watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southwest Washington

Southwest Washington offers some of the most exhilarating hiking and biking trails in the Pacific Northwest. Whether you are looking for scenic mountain views or stunning waterfronts, Southwest Washington has it all. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Southwest Washington:

  • Mount St. Helens: Worm Flows Route: This 12-mile round trip hike takes you through the outer flanks of the Mount St. Helens Volcano. The hike offers spectacular views of the valley, glaciers, and scenic vistas.

  • Lake Sacajawea Park: Located in Longview, Lake Sacajawea Park is a great destination for a family-friendly bike ride. The park features several trails, including a 3.5-mile paved loop, beautiful gardens, and a playground.

  • Cape Disappointment State Park: North Head Lighthouse Trail: This 2.2-mile hike takes you through one of the most scenic coastal trails in Southwest Washington. The trail offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, coves, and rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Southwest Washington: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of activities and tours that visitors can indulge in Southwest Washington. Here are some of the best things to do in the region.

  • Whale Watching Tours: The coastal areas of Southwest Washington are home to several local whale species. There are several whale watching tours that take visitors out to get a glimpse of these creatures in their natural habitat.

  • River Rafting Trips: There are several rivers in Southwest Washington with rapids ranging from mild to wild. Visitors can enjoy rafting trips that take them through the scenic areas of the region.

  • Bird Watching: Southwest Washington is home to several migratory bird species that come to the region during the fall. There are several bird-watching tours available that take visitors to the prime birding spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southwest Washington

To truly experience the best that Southwest Washington has to offer, it is recommended to spend at least a week in the region. Here is an itinerary that covers all major highlights of the region.

Day 1 - Arrive in Vancouver, explore the city, visit the Fort Vancouver National Historic Site

Day 2 - Drive to Astoria, Oregon, visit the Columbia River Maritime Museum

Day 3 - Visit Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument, hike around the volcano

Day 4 - Drive to Long Beach, explore the Bay Center’s oyster farms, and view the Willapa National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 5 - Visit Olympic National Park, explore the beaches, and hike the trails.

Day 6 - Visit the Red Mountain AVA Wine Region and taste some delicious wines.

Day 7 - Spend the day in Olympia, explore the Capitol Building, the Washington State History Museum, and the Hands-On Children’s Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Southwest Washington

Southwest Washington’s thriving food scene features a diverse range of cuisines, from Pacific Northwest seafood to international fare. Here are some of the must-try foods and local beverages in Southwest Washington.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood: Southwest Washington’s stunning coastline is home to several seafood delicacies. One must-try dish is the local fish n’ chips, made with freshly caught fish.

  • Marionberry Pie: Marionberry pie is a classic dessert in Southwest Washington made with seasonal berries that grow in the region.

  • Local Cheeses: Southwest Washington produces several varieties of artisan cheeses that are a must-try for cheese lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Southwest Washington is home to several craft beer breweries, including the famous Fort George Brewery.

  • Coffee: Pacific Northwest is known for its love of coffee. In Southwest Washington, one must-try coffee is the ‘Black and White’ made with white chocolate and espresso.

  • Wine: Southwest Washington produces excellent local wines that are a must-try for wine aficionados. The Columbia Valley AVA and the Red Mountain AVA produces some of the best wines in the region.

Where to Stay in Southwest Washington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southwest Washington offers several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Heathman Lodge: This beautiful lodge is located in Vancouver and features stunning lodge-style architecture, a great restaurant, and beautiful outdoor spaces.

  • The Kalama River Inn: Located in Kalama, This Inn features excellent amenities, including a pool and free breakfast.

  • The Sou’Wester Lodge: This classic roadside motel is located in Long Beach and features rustic charm, a hot tub, and fire pits.

What to Buy in Southwest Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southwest Washington offers several unique souvenirs and local products to take home as mementos. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs from the region.

  • Local Wine: Southwest Washington is known for its delicious wines. Bringing home a bottle of wine from a local winery is a great way to savor your memories of the region.

  • Handmade Ceramics: Several local artists make beautiful handcrafted ceramics that are a unique souvenir of your trip.

  • Crafts from the Olympia Farmers Market: The Olympia Farmers Market features several local artisans selling handmade crafts, including jewelry, pottery, and artworks.

Local Culture and Customs of Southwest Washington

Southwest Washington is home to a rich indigenous culture, and it is essential to respect local customs and traditions. The region has a vibrant arts scene, including several live music venues, theaters, and art galleries.

Best Time to Visit Southwest Washington

The best time to visit Southwest Washington is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. Fall is also a great time to visit, especially during the harvest season when the wineries and farms are bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southwest Washington

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, especially those of the local indigenous communities.

  • Try local cuisine, wines, and craft beers.

  • Dress in layers as the weather can change quickly.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of Southwest Washington.

  • Enter restricted areas without permission.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southwest Washington

Southwest Washington is a safe region to visit. However, visitors should be aware of the potential for wildfires during the summer months and pay attention to local weather reports.

Health in Southwest Washington

Southwest Washington has excellent medical facilities, and visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of insect-borne infections such as Lyme disease.

How to Get Around in Southwest Washington

Southwest Washington is easily accessible by car, and several car rental services are available. The region also has a well-connected public transportation system, including buses, trains, and light rail.

Where to Go Next from Southwest Washington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southwest Washington is in proximity to several other tourist destinations in the Pacific Northwest. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore:

  • Portland, Oregon: Located less than two hours from Southwest Washington, Portland is famous for its vibrant culture, craft breweries, and food scene.

  • Seattle, Washington: The Emerald City is just a few hours away from Southwest Washington and features a wide range of attractions, including the iconic Space Needle and Pike Place Market.

Conclusion

Southwest Washington is a beautiful and diverse region that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors. From its stunning natural beauty to its rich culture and history, Southwest Washington has something for everyone. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a history buff, Southwest Washington is a destination that should be on your travel bucket list.
