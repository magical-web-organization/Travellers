Introduction to Stevenson

Stevenson is a charming town located in Southwest Washington state, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. Nestled between the Columbia River and the Cascade Mountains, Stevenson is known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities, as well as its rich history and culture. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat in nature or an action-packed adventure, Stevenson has something to offer for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Stevenson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Columbia Gorge Interpretive Center Museum: This museum showcases the natural and cultural history of the Columbia River Gorge, including exhibits on geology, Native American culture, and the Lewis & Clark Expedition.
  • Skamania County Pioneer Museum: Learn about the early settlers and pioneers who shaped Stevenson and the surrounding area at this fascinating museum.
  • Bonneville Lock and Dam: Visit this impressive feat of engineering to learn about the history of hydropower production on the Columbia River.

Natural Attractions of Stevenson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beacon Rock State Park: Climb to the top of Beacon Rock for panoramic views of the Columbia River Gorge.
  • Cape Horn Trail: Hike through lush forests and scenic viewpoints on this popular trail in the Columbia River Gorge.
  • Wind River: Go fishing, kayaking, or hiking in the beautiful Wind River area, located just outside of Stevenson.
  • Bridge of the Gods: Walk or drive across this iconic bridge spanning the Columbia River for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stevenson

Stevenson is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Pacific Crest Trail: This renowned long-distance trail passes through Stevenson and offers some of the best views in the area.
  • Hamilton Mountain Trail: This challenging hike rewards visitors with sweeping views of the Columbia River Gorge.
  • Cape Horn Loop Trail: This moderate hike takes visitors through lush forest, past waterfalls, and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Washougal Bike Park: This state-of-the-art bike park offers features for all skill levels, from beginner-friendly pump tracks to advanced dirt jumps.

Best Things to Do in Stevenson: Activities and Tours

  • Whitewater Rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the beautiful White Salmon River.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit local wineries and tasting rooms to sample some of the best wines in the Pacific Northwest.
  • Zip Lining: Soar through the trees on a zip line adventure with Columbia Gorge Zip Line.
  • Art Galleries: Explore Stevenson’s thriving arts scene by visiting local galleries and studios, including the Columbia River Gallery and North Bank Artists Gallery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stevenson

Day 1-2: Explore Columbia River Gorge Interpretive Center Museum and nearby hiking trails.

Day 3-4: Raft down the White Salmon River, try zip lining with Columbia Gorge Zip Line, and sample local wines at nearby wineries.

Day 5-6: Go hiking in Beacon Rock State Park, visit Skamania County Pioneer Museum, and explore downtown Stevenson’s art galleries and shops.

Day 7: Take a leisurely drive down the historic Columbia River Highway and admire the stunning views of the Gorge.

Food and Drink Scene of Stevenson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Salmon: Enjoy fresh-caught wild salmon prepared in a variety of delicious ways, including grilled, smoked, and baked.
  • Huckleberry Pie: Satisfy your sweet tooth with this regional favorite dessert made with fresh huckleberries.
  • Local Cheese Plate: Sample a variety of locally-made cheeses at Dickey Farms, a family-owned farm and cheese shop.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Try some of the best craft beers in the Pacific Northwest at Walking Man Brewery, located in downtown Stevenson.
  • Wine: Visit local wineries such as Wind River Cellars and Maryhill Winery to sample some of the region’s best wines.

Where to Stay in Stevenson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Skamania Lodge: This luxurious mountain resort offers breathtaking views of the Columbia River Gorge and a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Carson Hot Springs Resort: Relax in natural mineral hot springs at this historic hotel and spa.
  • Columbia Gorge Inn: Enjoy comfortable accommodations in the heart of downtown Stevenson, just steps away from shops, restaurants, and galleries.

What to Buy in Stevenson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam: Bring home a jar of locally-made huckleberry jam to remember your trip.
  • Arts and Crafts: Visit local galleries and studios to find unique handmade crafts and artwork.
  • Wine: Pick up a bottle of your favorite local wine to savor at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Stevenson

Stevenson is proud of its rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its art, music, food, and festivals. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of local customs and traditions, which include a deep appreciation for nature and a laid-back, friendly attitude.

Best Time to Visit Stevenson

The best time to visit Stevenson is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and dry, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the fall and winter months also offer their own unique charms, including stunning fall foliage and cozy winter activities like hot springs soaking and wine tasting by firelight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stevenson

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by practicing Leave No Trace principles.
  • Try local cuisine, wines, and craft beers.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities in the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas or on hiking trails.
  • Ignore posted signs and warnings regarding dangerous areas or seasonally closed trails.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stevenson

Stevenson is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Stevenson

Stevenson has a good selection of medical facilities, including a modern hospital and several clinics. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for heat stroke or dehydration during the hot summer months, and take appropriate precautions such as drinking plenty of water and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Stevenson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stevenson is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Stevenson

The nearest airport to Stevenson is Portland International Airport, located approximately 45 miles away in Portland, Oregon.

Bus Stations Near Stevenson

Greyhound and other bus lines offer service to nearby cities such as Vancouver and Portland.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Stevenson by train, with Amtrak offering service to nearby cities such as Vancouver and Portland.

How to Get Around in Stevenson

The most convenient way to get around Stevenson is by car, although bicycles and walking are also popular options for exploring the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Stevenson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland, Oregon: Explore the eclectic neighborhoods, famous food and drink scene, and many cultural attractions of this vibrant city.
  • Mount Hood: Hit the slopes at this majestic mountain, or enjoy summer activities such as hiking, fishing, and camping.
  • Hood River: Visit this charming town located on the banks of the Columbia River, known for its excellent wind sports, breweries, and wineries.

Conclusion

Stevenson is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, offering stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and many opportunities for outdoor adventure and relaxation. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect trip to Stevenson and experience all that this scenic town has to offer.
