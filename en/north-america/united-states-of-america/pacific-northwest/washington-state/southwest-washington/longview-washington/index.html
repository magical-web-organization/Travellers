Longview, located in Southwest Washington, is a popular travel destination in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. With its scenic natural attractions, culturally significant sites, and exciting outdoor activities, Longview attracts visitors of all ages. This comprehensive travel guide will take you on a tour of the beautiful town of Longview and highlight the best things to see and do.

Introduction to Longview (Washington)

Longview is a thriving city that prides itself on its beautiful parks and the Cowlitz River. It is a great place to explore and relax while enjoying all that nature has to offer. The city is small, but full of character. It is strategically located between major cities, Seattle and Portland, making it an ideal stop if you are traveling around the Pacific Northwest region.

List of Places to Visit in Longview (Washington)

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Longview:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Columbia Theatre - A historic theater that showcases a range of live performances and events.

  2. Cowlitz County Historical Museum - This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Cowlitz County. It is home to a range of exhibits that showcase the area’s rich history and culture.

  3. Lewis and Clark Bridge - This unique bridge connects Longview to Rainier, Oregon, and provides fantastic views of the Columbia River.

Natural Attractions of Longview (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Sacajawea - This beautiful lake offers a range of outdoor activities, including fishing, boating and walking trails.

  2. Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument - This stunning monument is a must-see for all nature lovers. It is home to some of the most spectacular views you will ever see, and it’s an excellent place to learn about the area’s volcanic history.

  3. Lake Sacajawea Park - This park is a popular spot for families and offers spacious grounds, hiking trails, and play areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longview (Washington)

Longview is hiker’s paradise, and you can find plenty of beautiful hiking and biking trails throughout the area. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  1. Ape Cave Trail - this trail is located within miles of Mount St. Helen’s National Volcanic Monument. The cave is over 20 miles long and offers some of the most incredible views of the area’s local flora and fauna.

  2. Mint Valley Trail - this trail stretches four miles and offers spectacular views of the surrounding forests and wilderness.

  3. Beacon Hill Trail - located in the heart of Longview, this trail is perfect for hiking and jogging and provides sweeping views of the Columbia River and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Longview (Washington): Activities and Tours

  1. Riverwalk - Experience a beautiful walk or bike ride along the banks of the Columbia River.

  2. Cowlitz County Fair - A must-see attraction for anyone visiting Longview. This annual fair features rides, games, livestock exhibits, and food.

  3. Three Rivers Golf Course - This stunning golf course features well-maintained fairways and a fantastic clubhouse.

Week-Long Itinerary for Longview (Washington)

Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip to Longview:

Day 1: Visit the Columbia Theatre and Cowlitz County Historical Museum.

Day 2: Explore Lake Sacajawea and the surrounding park.

Day 3: Journey to the Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument and hike the Ape Cave Trail.

Day 4: Take a relaxing stroll through the Beacon Hill Trail and see some of the best views of the Columbia River.

Day 5: Discover the Cowlitz County Fair.

Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Three Rivers Golf Course.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of the local cuisine and nightlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Longview (Washington)

When it comes to food and drink, Longview is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Smoked Salmon - This local delicacy can be found at several restaurants and is known for its rich and smoky taste.

  2. Cowlitz Chili - This hearty dish is a combination of savory beef and flavorful spices.

  3. Blackberry Pie - Longview is known for its delicious blackberry pie, and you’ll find plenty of it in local bakeries.

Local Beverages

  1. Local Beer - Longview is home to several breweries and is known for its craft beer scene.

  2. Coffee - The Pacific Northwest is famous for its coffee, and Longview is no exception. Stop in at one of the local cafes, and enjoy a cup of freshly brewed coffee.

Where to Stay in Longview (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Longview has several options for accommodation, including hotels, motels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  1. The Monticello Hotel - Located in the heart of Longview, this hotel is an excellent option for anyone looking for a comfortable and stylish place to stay.

  2. Hudson House Bed and Breakfast - This charming bed and breakfast is perfect for visitors who want to experience the local culture and charm.

  3. Motel 6 - This affordable option provides a comfortable stay and is an excellent choice for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Longview (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping, Longview has plenty to offer visitors. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. Artisanal Honey - Longview is home to several honey producers that create high-quality, locally sourced honey products.

  2. Handmade Pottery - The Pacific Northwest is known for its unique pottery, and Longview boasts several galleries that showcase local artists’ work.

  3. Local Artwork - Longview has no shortage of talented artists, and you’ll find several galleries showcasing their work.

Local Culture and Customs of Longview (Washington)

Longview is known for its friendly and welcoming locals. Visitors will enjoy experiencing the local culture and traditions, which are deeply rooted in the area’s history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Longview (Washington)

The best time to visit Longview is during the summer months, from June to September. This is when the weather is at its best and visitors can enjoy all the outdoor activities and natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longview (Washington)

Do:

  1. Visit local museums and cultural sites to learn about the area’s history and culture.

  2. Take time to explore the natural beauty of Longview, including its parks, lakes, and hiking trails.

  3. Try the local cuisine and craft beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public areas, always dispose of waste in designated trash cans.

  2. Ignore safety signs or venture off marked trails when hiking or exploring the wilderness.

  3. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Longview (Washington)

Longview is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking doors and windows, not leaving valuables in plain sight, and being aware of their surroundings, especially when walking at night.

Health in Longview (Washington)

Travelers to Longview should ensure their vaccinations are up to date, especially if they plan to spend time outdoors. Visitors should also protect themselves from the sun, as the Pacific Northwest region is known for its rainy weather but can still get hot during the summer months.

Getting to Longview (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Longview (Washington)

The nearest major airport to Longview is Portland International Airport, located approximately 50 miles away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take public transportation to get to Longview.

Bus Stations Near Longview (Washington)

Longview is serviced by several bus companies, including Greyhound and Northwest Trailways.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Longview, as it is located off Interstate 5.

How to Get Around in Longview (Washington)

The best way to get around Longview is by car. Visitors can rent a car at Portland International Airport or in Longview. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Longview (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Astoria, Oregon - Located approximately 35 miles west of Longview, Astoria is a charming coastal city with plenty of shops, restaurants, and museums to explore.

  2. Mount Rainier National Park - Located approximately 140 miles east of Longview, Mount Rainier National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the natural beauty of the Pacific Northwest.

Conclusion

Longview, Washington, is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. With its beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and friendly locals, Longview is a fantastic destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the big city. Whether you’re interested in hiking, exploring the local culture, or just relaxing and enjoying the scenery, Longview has something for everyone.
