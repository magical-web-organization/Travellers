Introduction to Camas (Washington)

Nestled in Southwest Washington, Camas is a small but charming city in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. Known for its picturesque views, outdoor adventures, and rich history, Camas is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Camas (Washington)

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Camas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Two Rivers Heritage Museum: Learn about the history of Camas and Washougal through exhibits that include an early street scene, Native American artifacts, and logging and mill equipment.

  • Lacamas Heritage Trail: This trail offers a scenic walk through the beautiful Lacamas Lake Regional Park and highlights the history of the mill town of Lacamas.

Natural Attractions of Camas (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lacamas Lake: This pristine lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. You can also take a leisurely stroll along the trails or have a picnic on the shores.

  • Round Lake: This small lake is surrounded by beautiful forest and offers a quiet and serene setting for fishing and boating.

  • Washougal River: This river is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and rafting. Its crystal-clear waters provide a refreshing dip on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Camas (Washington)

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Camas offers many hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Round Lake Trail: A 2.5-mile trail that offers scenic lake views and is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride.

  • Lacamas Lake Loop Trail: A 7.5-mile loop that takes you through the beautiful forest, along serene lakes, and past the historic mill town of Lacamas.

  • Camas Heritage Trail: A 3.3-mile out-and-back trail that features historical markers about the area’s logging and paper mill industries.

Best Things to Do in Camas (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Camas Farmer’s Market: This market runs from May to September and offers locally grown produce, baked goods, and artisan crafts.

  • Camas Days: This annual event in July features a parade, live music, food vendors, and a carnival.

  • Camas Plant and Garden Fair: This event in May brings together over 100 vendors of plants, garden-related items, and artisan crafts.

  • Downtown Camas First Friday: On the first Friday of every month, downtown Camas comes alive with live music, art exhibits, and extended hours of local shops and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Camas (Washington)

Day 1: Arrive in Camas and explore the downtown area. Have dinner at The Hammond Kitchen and Craft Bar, known for its farm-to-table cuisine and craft cocktails.

Day 2: Start your day with a hike on the Lacamas Lake Loop Trail and have a picnic lunch on the shores. In the afternoon, rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the lake from a different perspective.

Day 3: Visit the Two Rivers Heritage Museum and learn about the history of Camas and Washougal. In the afternoon, take a scenic drive on the Washougal River Road and enjoy the beautiful forest views.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument and hike the Ape Caves or Johnston Ridge Observatory.

Day 5: Visit the Camas Farmer’s Market and pick up some locally grown produce for a picnic lunch. In the afternoon, explore the Washougal River Greenway and enjoy the beautiful river views.

Day 6: Visit the Lacamas Creek Trail and hike through the forest to the cascading waterfalls. Have dinner at Nuestra Mesa, a Mexican restaurant known for its street food and tequila selection.

Day 7: Visit the Camas Plant and Garden Fair and pick up some unique plants or garden-related items. End your trip with a relaxing afternoon at Skamania Lodge, where you can enjoy the spa or take a dip in the pool.

Food and Drink Scene of Camas (Washington)

Camas has a diverse food and drink scene, featuring farm-to-table cuisine, craft beer, and artisan cocktails.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hazel Dell Mushroom Risotto at The Hammond Kitchen and Craft Bar: This dish features locally sourced mushrooms, arborio rice, parmesan cheese, and truffle oil.

  • Smoked Beef Brisket at Birch Street Uptown Lounge: This dish features house-smoked beef brisket, coleslaw, and garlic fries.

  • Chicken and Waffles at Twilight Pizza Bistro: This dish features buttermilk fried chicken, homemade waffles, maple syrup, and whipped butter.

Local Beverages

  • Camas IPA at Grains of Wrath: This local brewery features a wide variety of craft beers, but the Camas IPA is a must-try for hop lovers.

  • Vodka Martini at The Hammond Kitchen and Craft Bar: This classic cocktail is elevated with house-infused vodka and a variety of garnishes, including pickled onions, blue cheese olives, and bacon-wrapped dates.

Where to Stay in Camas (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Camas Hotel: This historic hotel features 23 cozy and elegantly decorated rooms and is located in the heart of downtown Camas.

  • The Atlantis Resort Bed and Breakfast: This beautiful bed and breakfast is located on 5 acres of lush forest and features 5 luxurious guest rooms.

  • Lacamas Lake Lodge: This rustic lodge is located on the shores of Lacamas Lake and features 6 cozy cabins and a bed and breakfast-style guest room.

What to Buy in Camas (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Soaps and Lotions at Attic Gallery: This boutique features a wide variety of local artisan goods, including handmade soaps and lotions.

  • Artwork at Second Story Gallery: This gallery features a diverse collection of local artwork, including paintings, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Camas (Washington)

Camas is a friendly and welcoming community that values its rich history and natural beauty. The city is committed to sustainability and preserving its natural resources, and residents are proud to support local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Camas (Washington)

The best time to visit Camas is between May and September when the weather is mild and sunny, and when many outdoor events and festivals take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Camas (Washington)

Do:

  • Do explore the local trails and parks, including Lacamas Lake Regional Park and the Washougal River Greenway.

  • Do support the local businesses, including the farmer’s market, artisan boutiques, and restaurants.

  • Do take a scenic drive on the Washougal River Road and enjoy the stunning forest views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities, including sunscreen, bug spray, and proper hiking shoes.

  • Don’t miss out on the local festivals and events, including Camas Days and the Camas Plant and Garden Fair.

Safety and Health

Safety in Camas (Washington)

Camas is a safe and peaceful city, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, including locking doors and windows, and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Camas (Washington)

Camas has excellent medical facilities, including PeaceHealth Southwest Medical Center and Kaiser Permanente.

Getting to Camas (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Camas is located 20 miles east of Portland, Oregon and is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Camas (Washington)

  • Portland International Airport: This airport is located 14 miles west of Camas and offers a wide variety of domestic and international flights.

  • Vancouver Pearson Airport: This small airport is located 9 miles northeast of Camas and offers private and charter flights.

Bus Stations Near Camas (Washington)

  • C-Tran: This local bus service serves the Vancouver and Camas areas.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental companies are available at both Portland International Airport and Vancouver Pearson Airport.

How to Get Around in Camas (Washington)

The best way to get around Camas is by car, but the city is also bike-friendly and has designated bike lanes and trails.

Where to Go Next from Camas (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland, Oregon: Located just 20 miles west of Camas, Portland is a vibrant city known for its food scene, craft beer, and live music.

  • Mount Hood National Forest: Located 75 miles east of Camas, Mount Hood National Forest offers numerous outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.

  • Columbia River Gorge: Located 30 miles east of Camas, the Columbia River Gorge offers breathtaking views, hiking trails, and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Camas, Washington, is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, with a rich history, outdoor adventures, and a charming downtown. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an outdoor adventure, Camas has something to offer for every traveler.
