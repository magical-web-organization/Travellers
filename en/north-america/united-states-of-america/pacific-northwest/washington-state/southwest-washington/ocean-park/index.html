Ocean Park is a hidden gem in Southwest Washington, located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. Known for its picturesque beaches, charming local culture, and fantastic outdoor activities, Ocean Park offers a unique experience to travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip and make the most out of your time in this beautiful destination.

Introduction to Ocean Park

Ocean Park is a small town located in southwestern Washington, near the Pacific Ocean. The town is known for its long beach and scenic views, which make it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The town has a rich history deeply connected to the Pacific Northwest culture, which you can experience through its various museums and historical attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ocean Park has a rich history and culture, which can be experienced by visiting some of its museums and historical places. The Pacific County Courthouse, Cranberry Museum, and Chinook Indian Nation are among the top attractions in the town. Other notable places to visit include the Marsh Interpretive Center, the Pioneer Cemetery, and the Oysterville Historic District.

Natural Attractions of Ocean Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ocean Park is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities. Admire the scenic view of the ocean from the Cranberry Road Observation Tower, or glide through the peaceful waters of Loomis Lake in a kayak. The Leadbetter Point State Park and Willapa National Wildlife Refuge, provide opportunities for birdwatching, hiking, and biking. For a unique experience, head to the Pacific Ocean and watch for whales, seals, and seabirds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean Park

For outdoor enthusiasts, Ocean Park offers a variety of hiking/biking routes suitable for different levels of expertise. The Discovery Trail, a 8.5-mile paved trail, runs from Ilwaco to Long Beach and is perfect for bike riders. The Willapa Hills Trail and Long Island Loop Trail are more challenging hikes and provide stunning views of the coastal range and the Pacific Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Ocean Park: Activities and Tours

Experience Ocean Park’s unique charm and culture by taking part in its various activities and tours. The Peninsula Arts Association Gallery showcases local talent, while Dennis Company offers a variety of unique souvenirs. For the thrill-seekers, there are ATV tours on the beach, parasailing, and zip-lining available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ocean Park

To make the most out of your trip, we have put together a week-long itinerary for Ocean Park. Spend your first day getting to know the town, visiting the Pacific County Courthouse and Cranberry Museum. The second day, take a bike ride along the Discovery Trail. On the third day, explore the natural beauty of the area with a kayak ride on Loomis Lake or a hike through Willapa National Wildlife Refuge. Day four, take a historical tour through the Oysterville Historic District. On the fifth day, take an ATV tour of the beach or go parasailing. Spend the sixth day browsing local shops and picking up souvenirs at Dennis Company. On the last day, enjoy a peaceful whale watching tour before departing town.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean Park

No trip is complete without indulging in some local cuisine. Ocean Park offers a variety of must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Do not miss out on Ocean Park’s seafood cuisine. Try some of the local specialties like oyster sliders at The Cove Restaurant or razor clam chowder at The Ark.

Local Beverages

Wash down your meals with some of the area’s local beverages. The Peninsula Beer Works offers locally brewed beer, while the Adrift Distillers produces gin and vodka using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Ocean Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ocean Park has a variety of accommodation options that suit all types of travelers, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The Ocean Park Resort and Inn is a great option for budget travelers. The Wisteria Chalet provides a more luxurious stay, with beautiful ocean views and a heated pool.

What to Buy in Ocean Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take back a piece of Ocean Park with you by purchasing some souvenirs or local products. The Dennis Company and Bay Avenue Gallery offer unique, locally-made items perfect to bring back as gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean Park

Ocean Park has a unique culture that’s heavily influenced by Pacific Northwest’s Native American heritage. It’s important to respect local customs and traditions when visiting the town.

Best Time to Visit Ocean Park

The best time to visit Ocean Park is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocean Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be mindful of your environmental impact
  • Take note of any restricted areas before exploring

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beach
  • Disturb the wildlife
  • Ignore any warning signs or guidelines posted at the attractions and beaches

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocean Park

Ocean Park is generally a safe place for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to keep their belongings safe.

Health in Ocean Park

Visitors to Ocean Park should keep in mind that the Pacific Northwest region is known for its rainy weather, so packing for different weather conditions is recommended.

Getting to Ocean Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean Park

The nearest airport to Ocean Park is Portland International Airport, which is around 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Ocean Park

The nearest bus station is located in Long Beach, which is around 20 minutes away by car.

Other Options

The easiest way to get to Ocean Park is to rent a car or take a taxi from a nearby city, such as Portland or Seattle.

How to Get Around in Ocean Park

The best way to explore Ocean Park and the surrounding area is by car. Renting a car is the most popular option, but taxis and ride-hail services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Ocean Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ocean Park is surrounded by other beautiful destinations that are worth a visit. Take a ferry from Port Angeles to Victoria, Canada to explore the city’s beautiful architecture and history. Or head south to Astoria, Oregon for a taste of Pacific Northwest history and culture.

Conclusion

Ocean Park offers a unique experience to travelers that blends the beauty of the Pacific Ocean with Pacific Northwest culture and traditions. This comprehensive guide covers all the essential information you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem of Washington state.
