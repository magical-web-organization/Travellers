Introduction to Long Beach (Washington)

Located in the southwest region of Washington state, Long Beach is a small coastal town known for its breathtaking scenic vistas and a rich cultural history that dates back over 200 years. The town sits on the shores of the Pacific Ocean, and some of the most popular attractions include wide sandy beaches, lush forests, and a diverse wildlife population. Additionally, Long Beach hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Washington State International Kite Festival, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique and memorable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Long Beach (Washington)

Whether you enjoy outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Long Beach offers a wide variety of tourist attractions that cater to different interests. Below is a list of some of the must-visit places in Long Beach that will leave you with lasting memories.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history enthusiast, you will be delighted to discover Long Beach’s rich cultural heritage that includes historic sites and museums showcasing the area’s unique history and cultural influence.

  • Cape Disappointment Lighthouse: Located at the entrance of the Columbia River, this historic lighthouse dates back to 1856 and offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • World Kite Museum and Hall of Fame: Celebrating the long history of kite flying in Long Beach, this museum is a must-visit attraction showcasing various types of kites and their cultural significance.
  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center: This museum celebrates the expedition of Lewis and Clark through exhibits, displays, and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Long Beach (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Long Beach’s natural landscape offers beautiful sights and outdoor activities that will keep you active throughout your stay. From hiking to surfing to fishing, there is something for everyone.

  • Long Beach Boardwalk: At 28 miles long, the Long Beach Boardwalk is one of the longest continuous beach walks in the United States, offering stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Cape Disappointment State Park: This park offers a vast range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, camping, and bird watching.
  • Leadbetter Point State Park: Located on the Long Beach Peninsula, this park is home to coastal dunes, grassy plains, and sandy beaches that provide a perfect spot for sightings of gray whales and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Beach (Washington)

Long Beach boasts several hiking and biking trails that take you through some of the most beautiful natural surroundings in the area. Here are some of the best trails that offer a unique outdoor experience.

  • Discovery Trail: This trail runs for 8.4 miles from Ilwaco to Long Beach, offering breathtaking views of the ocean, salt marshes, and forests.
  • Cape Disappointment State Park Loop Trail: Explore the stunning natural beauty of Cape Disappointment State Park on this 3.4-mile loop trail that takes you through lush forests and panoramic vistas.
  • Black Lake Trail: This 5.5-mile trail is one of the most popular hiking trails in the area, leading you through dense forests and offering some of the most stunning views in the region.

Best Things to Do in Long Beach (Washington): Activities and Tours

Long Beach offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. From scenic tours to wine tasting, there is something for everyone. Below are some of the best things to do to enjoy your stay in Long Beach.

  • Horseback Riding on the Beach: Explore the serene beauty of the coastline on horseback and watch the sun set over the ocean.
  • Whale Watching Tours: Get a chance to see the majestic grey and humpback whales up close on these tours.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Visit one of the many wineries in the area for a taste of local wines and learn about the wine-making process.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Beach (Washington)

Long Beach offers a diverse food and drink scene with a mix of local and international flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages during your stay.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood: Enjoy some of the best fresh seafood in the Pacific Northwest, including crab, clams, and salmon.
  • Local Oysters: Long Beach is known for its delicious oysters, which are served in various restaurants in the area.
  • Wild Mushrooms: The local forests are home to a variety of wild mushrooms, which are featured in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Washington Wines: Visit one of the many wineries in the area for a taste of local wines, including the renowned Merlot and Cabernet Sauvignon.
  • Local Breweries: Long Beach has a variety of local breweries that serve craft beers, including North Jetty Brewing, Fort George Brewery, and Reach Break Brewing.

Where to Stay in Long Beach (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Beach offers a wide range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options in the area.

  • Adrift Hotel and Spa: This oceanfront boutique hotel offers stunning views and modern amenities.
  • Heidi’s Inn Ilwaco: This cozy bed and breakfast is known for its friendly staff and delicious homemade breakfast.
  • Camping Grounds at Cape Disappointment State Park: If you prefer a more outdoorsy experience, this park offers several camping grounds, including cabins, tents, and RV sites.

What to Buy in Long Beach (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Long Beach is known for its unique artisanal products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy during your stay.

  • Kites: Long Beach is famous for its kite culture, so grab a kite to bring home as a souvenir.
  • Artwork: The area is home to many talented artists, so consider purchasing a piece of original artwork to take home.
  • Wild Mushroom Products: Long Beach is known for its wild mushrooms, so consider purchasing products made from local mushrooms, such as oils, soaps, and seasonings.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Beach (Washington)

Long Beach has a diverse cultural heritage that is reflected in its unique traditions, festivals, and celebrations. Here are some of the customs and traditions that you should be aware of during your visit.

  • Kite Festival: The Washington State International Kite Festival is a week-long international festival that takes place every August and celebrates the art of kite flying.
  • Local Seafood: Long Beach is known for its fresh seafood, and it is customary to try some of the local seafood dishes.
  • Beachcombing: Beachcombing, or collecting shells and driftwood on the beach, is a popular activity in Long Beach that is part of the local culture and tradition.

Best Time to Visit Long Beach (Washington)

The best time to visit Long Beach is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months are the most popular, but the beaches can get crowded. The fall season offers cooler temperatures, fewer crowds, and beautiful fall foliage, making it an ideal time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Beach (Washington)

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Long Beach.

Do:

  • Try the local seafood: Long Beach is known for its fresh seafood, so make sure to try some of the local dishes.
  • Attend local festivals and events: Long Beach hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the popular Washington State International Kite Festival, so make sure to attend them.
  • Respect the environment: Long Beach’s natural beauty is one of its most significant attractions, so make sure to respect the environment and leave no trace.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car: Long Beach is generally safe, but it is best to keep valuables out of sight in your car.
  • Litter: As with any natural environment, it is essential to keep the beaches clean and avoid littering.
  • Disturb wildlife: Long Beach is home to a diverse wildlife population, so make sure to observe them from a safe distance and avoid disturbing them.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to ensure a safe and healthy stay in Long Beach.

Safety in Long Beach (Washington)

  • Be aware of rip currents: Long Beach is known for its strong ocean currents, so make sure to swim in designated areas and stay aware of rip currents.
  • Watch out for wildlife: Long Beach is home to a variety of wildlife, including bears and cougars, so make sure to observe them from a safe distance and avoid attracting them with food.
  • Avoid walking alone at night: Long Beach is generally safe, but it is best to walk in pairs or groups, especially at night.

Health in Long Beach (Washington)

  • Stay hydrated: The Pacific Northwest has a cool climate, but it is still essential to stay hydrated.
  • Use sunscreen: Even on overcast days, the sun’s rays can still damage your skin, so make sure to use sunscreen.
  • Pack medications: If you have any medical conditions, make sure to pack any necessary medications.

Getting to Long Beach (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Long Beach is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options to help you plan your trip.

Airports Near Long Beach (Washington)

  • Portland International Airport: Located 120 miles south of Long Beach.
  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport: Located 134 miles north of Long Beach.

Bus Stations Near Long Beach (Washington)

  • Astoria Transit Center: Located 12 miles south of Long Beach.
  • Greyhound Bus Station in Ilwaco: Located 3 miles north of Long Beach.

Other Options

  • Car Rental Services: Several car rental services are available at both Portland and Seattle airports.
  • Amtrak Train Service: Amtrak offers train service to Vancouver, Washington, and Portland, with connecting buses to Long Beach.

How to Get Around in Long Beach (Washington)

Long Beach is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, if you need to go somewhere further, there are several transportation options.

  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available in the area.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the area.
  • Bicycles: Bike rentals are available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Long Beach (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Long Beach is located near several nearby destinations and excursions that can make for a fun day trip or weekend getaway. Here are some recommendations.

  • Astoria, Oregon: The historic town of Astoria is located across the Columbia River and is known for its charming Victorian architecture and maritime history.
  • Mount Saint Helens: A two-hour drive from Long Beach, Mount Saint Helens is a famous volcanic peak and a popular destination for nature enthusiasts.
  • Olympic National Park: A three-hour drive from Long Beach, the Olympic National Park offers some of the most stunning natural landscapes in the Pacific Northwest.

Conclusion

Long Beach is a hidden gem on the Pacific Northwest coast that offers an authentic and unique experience that combines adventure, nature, and culture. Whether you are a history enthusiast, food lover, or outdoor enthusiast, Long Beach has something to offer. Plan your visit to Long Beach today to explore this fantastic destination.
