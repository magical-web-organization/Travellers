Ilwaco is a charming coastal town located in Southwest Washington, in the United States of America. Nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, Ilwaco is a fantastic destination for travelers seeking a unique mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures.

Introduction to Ilwaco

Ilwaco is a small town with a big personality. Situated at the mouth of the Columbia River, Ilwaco boasts a rich history as a fishing village and trading post. Today, the town is a popular tourist destination, with a thriving arts scene, pristine beaches, and ample opportunities for outdoor recreation.

List of Places to Visit in Ilwaco

If you’re visiting Ilwaco for the first time, you’ll want to check out these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Columbia Pacific Heritage Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look at the history and culture of the Lower Columbia River region. Exhibits include artifacts from local tribes, fishing vessels, and exhibits on the region’s diverse ecology.
  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center: Located on Cape Disappointment, this interpretive center marks the end of the trail for Lewis and Clark’s expedition. The center features exhibits on the expedition, the local flora and fauna, and the history of the area.
  • North Head Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse sits atop a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean. Visitors can climb the spiral staircase to the top for sweeping views of the coastline.

Natural Attractions of Ilwaco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Disappointment State Park: This sprawling state park boasts over 2,000 acres of hiking trails, beaches, and scenic viewpoints. Be sure to explore the park’s rugged coastline, tide pools, and towering evergreen forests.
  • Leadbetter Point State Park: Located on the northern tip of the Long Beach Peninsula, Leadbetter Point State Park is a great place for bird watching, hiking, and clamming.
  • Discovery Trail: This 8.5-mile trail stretches from Ilwaco to Long Beach and follows the Pacific Ocean coastline. The trail is perfect for biking, walking, or jogging with gorgeous views of the Pacific Ocean and the rolling sand dunes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ilwaco

Ilwaco is a paradise for nature enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Cape Disappointment State Park Trails: With over 27 miles of hiking trails, Cape Disappointment State Park has something for everyone. Some of the most popular trails include the Coastal Forest Loop, the North Head Trail, and the Cape Disappointment Trail.
  • Discovery Trail: This 8.5-mile trail stretches from Ilwaco to Long Beach and follows the Pacific Ocean coastline. The trail is perfect for biking, walking, or jogging with gorgeous views of the Pacific Ocean and the rolling sand dunes.

Best Things to Do in Ilwaco: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with family and friends, there’s plenty to do in Ilwaco. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Fishing Charters: The waters around Ilwaco are teeming with salmon, tuna, and halibut, making it a popular spot for fishing charters. Visitors can book a tour with one of the local fishing companies for a day of fishing on the open sea.
  • Whale Watching Tours: From March to May, visitors can embark on a whale watching tour to spot migrating gray whales as they travel along the Pacific Coast.
  • Art Walks: Ilwaco is home to a vibrant arts scene, with numerous galleries and studios showcasing the work of local artists. Visitors can take a self-guided art walk to explore the town’s vibrant arts community.

Food and Drink Scene of Ilwaco

Ilwaco is home to a diverse food and drink scene, with restaurants, pubs, and cafes serving up fresh seafood, local brews, and international cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A Pacific Northwest classic, fish and chips are a must-try in Ilwaco. Many local seafood restaurants serve up this iconic dish made with locally caught fish and crispy batter.
  • Oysters: Ilwaco is famous for its oysters, which are harvested from the beds in nearby Willapa Bay. Visitors can try fresh oysters on the half-shell, grilled, or in a variety of other dishes.
  • Crab Cakes: Made with local Dungeness crab, crab cakes are a popular dish in Ilwaco. The savory cakes are often served with a side of fresh greens and lemon wedges.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Ilwaco is home to several local craft breweries, serving up everything from hoppy IPAs to crisp lagers. Some popular breweries include North Jetty Brewing, Fort George Brewery, and Buoy Beer Company.
  • Wine: Washington state is known for its wine production, and Ilwaco is no exception. Visitors can enjoy locally produced wines at several local tasting rooms, including Long Beach Peninsula Winery and Chateau Ste. Michelle.
  • Coffee: Ilwaco is home to several cozy cafes serving up locally roasted coffee. Some favorites include Madeline’s Cafe, The Depot Restaurant, and Salt Hotel & Pub.

Where to Stay in Ilwaco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious beachfront hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Ilwaco has plenty of accommodation options. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Salt Hotel & Pub: This historic hotel offers stylish rooms with stunning ocean views. The hotel also has an on-site pub serving up craft beer and locally sourced cuisine.
  • Adrift Hotel & Spa: This eco-friendly hotel features modern rooms with private decks overlooking the ocean. There’s also an on-site spa offering a range of treatments and therapies.
  • China Beach Retreat: This charming guesthouse is located in a secluded spot just outside of Ilwaco. The guesthouse features spacious rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lush garden with views of the ocean.

What to Buy in Ilwaco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Ilwaco, here are some popular items:

  • Fresh Seafood: Visitors can purchase fresh seafood from several local fish markets, including Jessie’s Ilwaco Fish Company. Be sure to grab some smoked salmon or Dungeness crab to enjoy at home.
  • Local Artwork: Ilwaco is home to a thriving arts scene, with numerous galleries and studios showcasing the work of local artists. Visitors can purchase paintings, sculptures, and other artwork to take home.
  • Craft Beer: Ilwaco is home to several local craft breweries, offering a variety of unique beers and ales. Visitors can purchase bottled or canned beer to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ilwaco

Ilwaco is a friendly and laid-back town, with a strong sense of community and appreciation for the environment. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and be sure to leave the town’s beaches, parks, and trails as they found them.

Best Time to Visit Ilwaco

The best time to visit Ilwaco depends largely on your interests. Summer months (June-August) are the busiest and offer the best weather for outdoor activities. Fall (September-November) is a quieter time of year but provides stunning autumnal views. Winter (December-February) is the least busy and can be quite rainy, but offers cozy indoor activities and storm watching opportunities. Spring (March-May) is a great time for wildlife viewing, as many migratory birds pass through the area during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ilwaco

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the town’s beaches, parks, and trails.
  • Do sample fresh seafood and locally-produced beer and wine.
  • Do be respectful of the town’s natural resources and environment.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute natural areas.
  • Don’t disturb local wildlife.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ilwaco

Ilwaco is a relatively safe town, but visitors should always take precautions when exploring natural areas. Be sure to follow posted signs and stay on trails to avoid dangerous areas like cliff edges or swift-moving water.

Health in Ilwaco

Ilwaco has a small medical clinic, but for more serious medical emergencies, visitors may need to travel to nearby cities like Astoria, Oregon, or Long Beach, Washington. It’s recommended that visitors bring any necessary medication with them, as pharmacies in Ilwaco may have limited supplies.

Getting to Ilwaco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ilwaco is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Ilwaco

  • Portland International Airport: Located approximately 2.5 hours drive from Ilwaco, Portland International Airport is the largest airport in the region and offers non-stop flights to many major cities in the US and around the world.

Bus Stations Near Ilwaco

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Astoria): Located approximately 20 miles from Ilwaco, the Greyhound Bus Station in Astoria offers scheduled bus service to many locations throughout the Pacific Northwest.

Other Options

  • Car: Ilwaco is located approximately 2.5 hours drive from Portland, Oregon, and just over 3 hours drive from Seattle, Washington. Visitors can rent a car at either airport or in nearby cities like Astoria or Long Beach.
  • Ferry: Visitors can also take a scenic ferry from Astoria, Oregon, to nearby towns like Westport and Chinook.

How to Get Around in Ilwaco

Ilwaco is a small town that is easily walkable or bikeable. Visitors can also take advantage of a local taxi service or rental car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Ilwaco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you still have time to explore the Pacific Northwest after your visit to Ilwaco, there are plenty of exciting destinations nearby. Consider visiting:

  • Astoria, Oregon: This charming town across the Columbia River is known for its colorful Victorian homes, historic downtown, and stunning river views.
  • Long Beach, Washington: Just a short drive from Ilwaco, Long Beach is known for its long, sandy beach, boardwalk, and amusement park.
  • Cannon Beach, Oregon: This picturesque coastal town is known for its iconic Haystack Rock, upscale boutiques, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Ilwaco is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re seeking a relaxing beach vacation, a thrilling fishing excursion, or a chance to explore the region’s history and culture, Ilwaco is the perfect destination for your next trip.
