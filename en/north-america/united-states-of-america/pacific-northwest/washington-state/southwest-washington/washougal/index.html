Introduction to Washougal

Nestled in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Washougal is a charming city located in Southwest Washington. With its scenic landscapes, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage, Washougal offers visitors an authentic taste of the Pacific Northwest.

List of Places to Visit in Washougal

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply seeking a relaxing getaway, Washougal has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Washougal:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Two Rivers Heritage Museum: Learn about the history and cultural heritage of Washougal and the surrounding region at this interactive museum.
  • The Pendleton Woolen Mill: Visit the historic woolen mill and learn about the traditional techniques used to create their iconic woolen blankets and fabrics.
  • Captain William Clark Park at Cottonwood Beach: This park is home to several historical attractions, including the Sacajawea Monument and a replica of the historical navigational landmark, Captain’s Point.

Natural Attractions of Washougal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Washougal River Greenway Trail: Explore the natural beauty of Washougal on this 5.2-mile hiking and biking trail that follows the picturesque Washougal River.
  • Steigerwald Lake National Wildlife Refuge: This protected area is home to a diverse range of bird species, making it a must-visit spot for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.
  • North Shore Road Scenic Byway: Take a scenic drive along this beautiful byway for stunning views of the Columbia River Gorge and the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Washougal

Washougal is a haven for hikers and bikers, with its many trails and scenic routes. Here are some top hiking and biking routes you can explore:

  • Cape Horn Trail: This 7.6-mile loop trail offers stunning views of the Columbia River Gorge and is perfect for intermediate-level hikers.
  • The Washougal River Greenway Trail: This 5.2-mile trail is a popular spot for hiking and biking, with picturesque views of the Washougal River.
  • The Lacamas Heritage Trail: This 3.5-mile trail features beautiful views of Lacamas Creek and leads to several scenic waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Washougal: Activities and Tours

From wine tasting to hot air balloon rides, there’s no shortage of fun things to do in Washougal. Here are some top activities and tours to try:

  • Wine Tasting: Visit the local wineries and vineyards for a taste of Washougal’s delicious wines.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience breathtaking views of Washougal from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Kayaking: Explore the Washougal River by kayaking, with several rental and tour options available.

Food and Drink Scene of Washougal

Washougal’s food scene offers a unique blend of Pacific Northwest flavors and local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cedar Plank Salmon: This Pacific Northwest classic is a must-try when visiting Washougal.
  • Marionberry Pie: Made with locally sourced marionberries, this pie is a delicious dessert option.
  • Dungeness Crab: Freshly caught Dungeness Crab is a popular seafood dish in Washougal.

Local Beverages

  • Washougal Coffee Company: This local coffee shop roasts their own coffee beans and offers a cozy atmosphere for coffee lovers.
  • English Estate Winery: Try the local wines made with grapes grown on the estate’s vineyard.
  • Old Town Battle Grounds Tea Co.: This tea shop offers a variety of loose-leaf teas and has a cozy atmosphere to enjoy a cup of tea.

Where to Stay in Washougal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Washougal offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Camas Hotel: This historic hotel offers a cozy atmosphere and is located near several shops and restaurants.
  • Best Western Plus Parkersville Inn and Suites: This hotel offers modern amenities and is conveniently located near several attractions.
  • River View Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast overlooks the Washougal River and offers a relaxing getaway.

What to Buy in Washougal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From locally made crafts to delicious preserves, there are plenty of souvenirs to bring back home from Washougal. Here are some top shopping spots:

  • Washougal River Mercantile: This store offers a variety of locally made crafts and products.
  • Sauvie Island Lavender Farm Store: This store features handcrafted lavender products made with locally grown lavender.
  • Grandma’s Fruit & Produce: This farm store offers delicious local jams, jellies, and preserves made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Washougal

Washougal has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of Pacific Northwest and Native American influences. Visitors are encouraged to respect and appreciate the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Washougal

The best time to visit Washougal is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Washougal

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Washougal:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow trail guidelines and practice Leave No Trace principles.
  • Support locally owned businesses.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety instructions for outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect private property or landmarks.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind when visiting Washougal:

Safety in Washougal

  • Be aware of the risks of hiking and outdoor activities and take necessary precautions.
  • Follow posted safety guidelines at attractions and outdoor sites.
  • Be mindful of wildlife and stay a safe distance away.

Health in Washougal

  • Carry plenty of water and snacks when hiking and exploring.
  • Wear sunscreen and appropriate outdoor gear.
  • Seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Washougal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Washougal is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Washougal

  • Portland International Airport: The nearest airport to Washougal (approximately 17 miles away).
  • Hillsboro Airport: This regional airport is located approximately 28 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Washougal

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Portland, Oregon (approximately 20 miles away).
  • Amtrak Station: Located in Vancouver, Washington (approximately 10 miles away).

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate at the airports and in nearby cities.
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Washougal

Washougal is a pedestrian-friendly city, with several walking and biking trails. Public transportation options are also available, including a local bus system.

Where to Go Next from Washougal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Washougal is located near several other popular destinations in the Pacific Northwest region. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Portland, Oregon: Approximately 20 miles away from Washougal, Portland offers a vibrant city atmosphere, world-class dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Mount Hood National Forest: This stunning forest is located approximately 50 miles away from Washougal and offers hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Seattle, Washington: Located approximately 176 miles away from Washougal, Seattle is a popular destination for its iconic landmarks, historic neighborhoods, and thriving arts scene.

Conclusion

Washougal offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re looking to explore local history, indulge in Pacific Northwest cuisine, or simply relax in a scenic setting, Washougal has something for everyone to enjoy. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and charm of this Southwest Washington gem.
