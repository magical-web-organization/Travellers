Introduction to Colville

Nestled in the foothills of the Selkirk Mountains, Colville is a charming small town located in Northeastern Washington. Known for its scenic beauty and rich history, Colville is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone seeking a peaceful retreat away from the city.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to all the must-see attractions in Colville, including cultural landmarks, natural wonders, and exciting activities in and around town. We’ll also offer insider tips on dining, accommodations, transportation, and more, to help you make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Colville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stevens County Historical Society and Museum: Learn about the history of Colville and the surrounding area at this fascinating museum, which features exhibits on Native American culture, early pioneers, and the area’s mining and logging industries.

  • Fort Colville Museum: Housed in a historic 1880s building, this museum showcases the history of the U.S. Army outpost that once protected the area from Native American attacks.

  • Douglas Falls Grange Hall: Built in 1905, this historic building now serves as a community center and event space, and features restored original architecture and furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Colville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Little Pend Oreille Wildlife Refuge: This 41,000-acre wildlife refuge is a paradise for nature lovers, with hiking, bird-watching, camping, and fishing opportunities. Keep an eye out for bald eagles, black bears, and moose.

  • Colville National Forest: With more than 1.1 million acres of forest, this protected area offers endless hiking, camping, and fishing possibilities, including the popular Sherman Pass Scenic Byway.

  • Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area: Located just a short drive from Colville, this sprawling recreation area boasts over 130 miles of shoreline for water sports, picnicking, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colville

  • Kettle Crest Trail: This challenging 45-mile trail traverses the mountain ridges of the Kettle Range, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and diverse wildlife.

  • Little Pend Oreille Trail System: With over 100 miles of trails through forests, meadows, and wetlands, this trail system offers hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing opportunities for all skill levels.

  • Cannon Street Walkway: A short, easy walk through downtown Colville, this pathway offers attractions such as historic buildings, colorful murals, and the scenic Colville River.

Best Things to Do in Colville: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: With nearby rivers, streams, and lakes, Colville offers unparalleled fishing opportunities for trout, bass, and other game fish.

  • Hunting: Colville is a popular destination for big game hunting, with opportunities for deer, elk, moose, and more.

  • Snowmobiling: In the winter, Colville transforms into a snowmobiling wonderland, with countless miles of groomed trails and fresh powder.

Week-Long Itinerary for Colville

Day Activities
1 Explore Colville’s downtown, including the Stevens County Historical Society and Museum and the Douglas Falls Grange Hall.
2 Hike the Little Pend Oreille Trail System or go fishing at one of the nearby lakes or rivers.
3 Drive the Sherman Pass Scenic Byway through Colville National Forest, stopping at overlooks and trailheads along the way.
4 Visit the Fort Colville Museum and take a guided tour of the historic army outpost.
5 Day trip to Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area, with opportunities for boating, swimming, and picnicking.
6 Take a snowmobiling tour or go skiing at nearby resorts.
7 Depart from Colville or extend your trip to explore more of the Pacific Northwest.

Food and Drink Scene of Colville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Jam: Colville’s signature flavor, huckleberries make a sweet and tangy topping for pancakes, biscuits, and toast.

  • Fresh-Caught Trout: Whether grilled, baked, or smoked, the locally caught trout is a staple of Colville cuisine.

  • Wild Game: For adventurous eaters, Colville offers a range of game meats such as elk, venison, and bison.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Colville has a burgeoning microbrew scene, with several local breweries offering unique flavors and styles.

  • Fruit Wines: With the abundance of berries in the area, fruit wines made from huckleberries, blackberries, and raspberries are a popular local beverage.

Where to Stay in Colville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Selkirk Motel: A classic Pacific Northwest motel with clean, comfortable rooms and scenic views.

  • Colville Comfort Inn: A family-friendly hotel with indoor pool and hot tub, complimentary breakfast, and easy access to downtown.

  • The Tamarack: A cozy bed and breakfast in a historic home, featuring comfortable furnishings, homemade breakfasts, and personalized service.

What to Buy in Colville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Products: In addition to jam, huckleberry-flavored candies, syrups, and teas are popular local treats.

  • Crafts and Artifacts: From Native American pottery and jewelry to locally made handcrafted items, Colville offers unique souvenirs reflective of the area’s history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Colville

Colville has a rich history, with several Native American communities calling the area home before European settlers arrived in the late 1800s. Today, Colville is a mix of rural, outdoor-oriented residents and tourists, with a strong appreciation for nature and a laid-back pace of life.

Best Time to Visit Colville

Colville offers something appealing in every season, from hiking and fishing in the summer to snowmobiling and skiing in the winter. However, the peak tourist season is generally May through September, when the weather is mild and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colville

Do:

  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the area, including hiking trails, lakes and rivers, and scenic drives.

  • Respect the history and culture of the Native American communities in the area.

  • Sample the local food and beverages, including huckleberry products and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage or litter behind when hiking or camping.

  • Trespass on private property or disturb the local wildlife.

  • Drive recklessly on the narrow, winding mountain roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colville

Colville is generally a safe and peaceful place, but it’s important to take precautions when enjoying outdoor activities. Be aware of potential hazards such as steep cliffs, fast-moving rivers, and wild animals. Wear appropriate footwear and clothing, and carry plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Colville

Colville has several medical facilities and pharmacies in town, but visitors with specific medical needs should bring necessary medications and supplies.

Getting to Colville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colville

The nearest major airport to Colville is Spokane International Airport (GEG), located approximately 75 miles south of town. From Spokane, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to Colville.

Bus Stations Near Colville

Greyhound bus service is available in Spokane, with shuttle service to Colville.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Colville from major cities such as Seattle, Portland, or Vancouver, or take a scenic Amtrak train ride through the Pacific Northwest.

How to Get Around in Colville

The best way to get around Colville is by car, as the town is spread out and many attractions are located outside of town. Visitors can rent a car at the Spokane airport or in town. Biking and walking are also popular options for exploring downtown and nearby trails.

Where to Go Next from Colville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Priest Lake, Idaho: Just a short drive across the border into Idaho, Priest Lake offers stunning scenery, hiking and biking trails, and boating and fishing opportunities.

  • Lake Chelan, Washington: A popular summer destination, Lake Chelan offers water sports, wineries, and charming small towns along the picturesque lake shore.

  • Spokane, Washington: Washington’s second-largest city is just a short drive south of Colville and offers numerous attractions, including museums, galleries, and an active downtown scene.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, historic landmarks, or simply a peaceful retreat, Colville has something to offer every visitor. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of the Pacific Northwest.
