Introduction to Kettle Falls

Kettle Falls is a charming town nestled in Northeast Washington. Located in the heart of the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Kettle Falls boasts a rich history and culture that will take you back in time. From picturesque landscapes to adventurous outdoor activities, Kettle Falls has something to offer everyone. Explore this guide to discover the best places to visit, eat, and stay, and experience the best of what Kettle Falls has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kettle Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Kettle Falls Hotel Museum: This museum tells the story of the town’s history through artifacts and photographs. It’s located in the heart of the town and offers free admission.
  • St. Paul’s Mission: The mission was founded in the early 1840s and was one of the first Catholic missions in the area. Today, the mission serves as a museum where you can learn about the history of the early settlers in the area.
  • Kettle Falls Historical Center: The center showcases the history of Kettle Falls, including the town’s Native American heritage and its early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Kettle Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area: The recreation area offers breathtaking views of the lake and surrounding mountains. It’s a great place for boating, fishing, camping, hiking, and wildlife watching.
  • Kettle River: The river offers excellent opportunities for kayaking and fishing.
  • Colville National Forest: The forest features stunning landscapes and offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, mountain biking, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kettle Falls

  • Kettle Crest Trail: The trail covers over 40 miles of the Kettle River Range and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • 7 Devils Trail: The trail offers a challenging hike with breathtaking views of Lake Roosevelt and the surrounding area.
  • Inchelium Trail: This 7-mile trail takes hikers through meadows and forests and offers beautiful views of the Kettle River.

Best Things to Do in Kettle Falls: Activities and Tours

  • Kettle Falls Zipline Adventure: Experience the thrill of ziplining through the scenic forests of Kettle Falls.
  • Floods Peak Trail Ride: Take a scenic horseback ride along the Floods Peak Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Lake Roosevelt Charters: Explore the beauty of Lake Roosevelt with a guided boat tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kettle Falls

Day 1: Visit the New Kettle Falls Hotel Museum and explore the historic downtown area.

Day 2: Take a hike on the Kettle Crest Trail, then visit St. Paul’s Mission.

Day 3: Spend the day at Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area, go boating, fishing, and hiking.

Day 4: Go on a scenic horseback ride on the Floods Peak Trail.

Day 5: Visit the Kettle Falls Historical Center and take a walking tour of the town’s historic sites.

Day 6: Explore Colville National Forest, go hiking, mountain biking, and camping.

Day 7: Take a boat tour of Lake Roosevelt with Lake Roosevelt Charters, then enjoy a relaxing evening at a local restaurant or bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Kettle Falls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kettle Falls Burger: A classic burger made with locally sourced beef and served with all the fixings.
  • Wild Huckleberry Pie: A delicious dessert made with locally grown huckleberries.
  • Cedar Plank Salmon: Fresh salmon grilled on a cedar plank and served with a side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kettle Falls Brewery: The brewery offers a range of locally crafted beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Kettle River Winery: The winery offers a range of locally produced wines, including reds, whites, and dessert wines.

Where to Stay in Kettle Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kettle Falls Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Kettle Falls.
  • Camp Kettle Falls: A campground located within Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area.
  • Columbia River Inn: A family-owned motel located on the Columbia River.

What to Buy in Kettle Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Products: Huckleberry jams, syrups, and candies are popular souvenirs in Kettle Falls.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: The town has a number of local artisans who create beautiful handmade jewelry.
  • Locally Made Soaps and Lotions: A number of local shops carry natural, locally made soaps and lotions.

Local Culture and Customs of Kettle Falls

Kettle Falls has a rich history and culture that reflects the importance of the outdoors and nature. The town’s Native American heritage is also an important part of its culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the cultural traditions of the town and its residents.

Best Time to Visit Kettle Falls

The best time to visit Kettle Falls is during the summer months when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season is also a great time to visit, with beautiful foliage and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kettle Falls

Do:

  • Respect the cultural traditions of the town and its residents.
  • Take advantage of the town’s outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Try the local food and beverage scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the town’s cultural heritage.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kettle Falls

Kettle Falls is a relatively safe town, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities. Always follow safety guidelines and carry appropriate gear when hiking, camping, or participating in other outdoor activities.

Health in Kettle Falls

Visitors to Kettle Falls should take precautions to protect themselves from sunburn and dehydration during the summer months. It’s also important to be aware of tick-borne illnesses when hiking or camping in the area.

Getting to Kettle Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kettle Falls

  • Spokane International Airport (GEG): About 85 miles from Kettle Falls.

Bus Stations Near Kettle Falls

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Spokane (84 miles from Kettle Falls)

Other Options

  • Private Car Service: Various car rental options are available at Spokane International Airport.

How to Get Around in Kettle Falls

The best way to get around Kettle Falls is by car, but visitors can also explore the town on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Kettle Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest: This forest is located about 60 miles south of Kettle Falls and offers a range of outdoor activities.
  • Spokane: The city of Spokane is about 85 miles south of Kettle Falls and has a range of cultural attractions and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Kettle Falls is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural heritage, outdoor activities, and scenic beauty.
