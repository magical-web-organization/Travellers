Introduction to Northeast Washington

Located in the heart of the Pacific Northwest in the United States, Northeast Washington is a nature lover’s paradise. Blessed with pristine lakes, breathtaking views, and miles of hiking and biking trails, this region is perfect for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and connect with nature. It is also home to a rich culture and history, with dozens of museums and historical sites to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Northeast Washington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Spokane Visitor Center - Explore the military history of the region and the impact of the Spokane Tribe on the area.
  • Colville National Forest - Discover the rich flora and fauna that make this region so beautiful.
  • Northern Ales - Enjoy locally made craft beer and great food.

Natural Attractions of Northeast Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area - Enjoy fishing, boating, and swimming in this beautiful lake.
  • Sherman Pass Scenic Byway - Drive through this scenic byway and marvel at the beautiful terrain of the area.
  • Kettle Falls Trail - This trail offers stunning views of the Kettle Falls and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast Washington

Northeast Washington offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails that range from easy to challenging. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Okanogan National Forest - Explore the beauty of the area on the West Fork Methow Trail.
  • Kettle Crest Trail - This trail offers breathtaking views of the area from high elevations.
  • Colville National Forest - The Terry Trail is a great option for those looking for an easy and scenic hike.

Best Things to Do in Northeast Washington: Activities and Tours

  • Lake Roosevelt Adventures - Take a boat tour of Lake Roosevelt and discover the hidden gems of the area.
  • Zipline Tours - Get your adrenaline pumping with a zipline tour of the area.
  • Rafting Tours - Enjoy a rafting tour of the Spokane River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northeast Washington

Day 1: Explore the museums and historical sites in Fort Spokane. Day 2: Go hiking on the Kettle Crest Trail. Day 3: Take a boat tour of Lake Roosevelt. Day 4: Go ziplining in the area. Day 5: Visit the Northern Ales brewery and enjoy locally made craft beer. Day 6: Go on a rafting tour of the Spokane River. Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of the many beautiful parks in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast Washington

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Jam - This jam made from locally grown huckleberries is a must-try when in the area.
  • Apple Dumplings - This delicious dessert made with locally grown apples will leave your taste buds wanting more.
  • Smoked Salmon - Northeast Washington is known for its delicious smoked salmon.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - The area is home to several breweries that make some of the best craft beer in the region.
  • Wine - Northeast Washington is also known for its excellent wines, with several vineyards in the area.

Where to Stay in Northeast Washington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Northern Inn - This cozy inn in Kettle Falls offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  • Fifth Avenue Suites - This historic hotel in Colville is perfect for those looking for a unique and comfortable stay.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites - This modern hotel in Spokane offers great service and amenities.

What to Buy in Northeast Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Jam and Honey - Take home some of the delicious locally made jam and honey.
  • Local Crafts - The area is known for its talented artisans who make beautiful pottery, jewelry, and other crafts.
  • Local Wine and Beer - Don’t forget to take home some of the delicious wine and beer from the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast Washington

Northeast Washington is home to several Native American tribes, including the Spokane Tribe. It is important to respect their culture and customs, particularly when visiting museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Northeast Washington

The best time to visit Northeast Washington is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeast Washington

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry a map and avoid getting lost in the wilderness.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind when hiking or camping.
  • Ignore safety warnings when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Take photos of Native American people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northeast Washington

Northeast Washington is a safe area to visit, but it is important to take the necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities. Always carry a map and make sure someone knows your route before heading out.

Health in Northeast Washington

The area has several excellent hospitals and clinics that offer top-notch medical care. It is important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities.

How to Get Around in Northeast Washington

The best way to get around Northeast Washington is by car or bike. There are also several local taxis and public transportation options available.

Where to Go Next from Northeast Washington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Seattle, WA - Explore the beautiful city of Seattle and experience the best of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Vancouver, BC - Take a trip to Canada and explore the beautiful city of Vancouver.
  • Coeur d’Alene, ID - This beautiful lakeside town is a great destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors or immerse yourself in local culture, Northeast Washington has something for everyone. From breathtaking scenic views to delicious food and drinks, this region is sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits.
