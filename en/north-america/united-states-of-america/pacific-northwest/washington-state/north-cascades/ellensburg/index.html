Introduction to Ellensburg

Nestled in the heart of Washington State’s Kittitas Valley, Ellensburg offers a unique blend of natural beauty, vibrant downtown, and rich history, making it a popular destination for solo travelers, families, and couples alike. It is home to the beautiful Central Washington University and is a great starting point for exploring the lush North Cascades region. In this guide, you will find the best places to visit, things to do, local culture and customs, and practical tips for making the most of your time in Ellensburg. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Ellensburg

Ellensburg is a small town with a lot of character. From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and scenic drives, here are the top places to visit in and around Ellensburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clymer Museum of Art: This museum features a collection of Western art that highlights the beauty of the American West. The museum is housed in a historic building and admission is free.
  • Kittitas County Historical Museum: Learn about the history of Kittitas County and the Pacific Northwest through exhibits, artifacts, and photos.
  • Ellensburg Rodeo Hall of Fame: Discover the rich heritage of the Ellensburg Rodeo, one of the oldest in the country, and its impact on the town.
  • Historic Downtown Ellensburg: Stroll through the town’s main street and soak up the charm of its well-preserved architecture, quaint shops, and cozy restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Ellensburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olson Kundig’s Solitude Canyon: Take a scenic drive or hike through this beautiful canyon, located 12 miles from Ellensburg. The canyon’s rugged beauty features colorful rock formations, waterfalls, and abundant wildlife.
  • Manastash Ridge Observatory: Catch stunning views of the sun’s surface and other celestial objects at this observatory located five miles west of Ellensburg. Visitors can also attend educational programs and stargaze at night.
  • Iron Horse State Park: This 100-mile trail attracts hikers, bikers, and horse riders seeking to explore the beauty of Washington’s backcountry. The trail runs through tunnels and over trestles, offering breathtaking views of the Cascade Mountains.
  • Lake Easton State Park: A popular spot for fishing, boating, picnicking, camping, and hiking, this state park is just 17 miles west of Ellensburg. It provides easy access to the Northern Cascades and its many natural attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellensburg

Located in the heart of Kittitas Valley, Ellensburg is surrounded by beautiful trails for hiking and biking. Here are some of the favorite routes to explore:

  • John Wayne Pioneer Trail: This 300-mile non-motorized trail traverses the state of Washington, with a section running through Ellensburg. It is perfect for hikers, bikers, and horse riders looking to explore the stunning natural scenery of the area and its historical heritage.
  • Umptanum Ridge Crest Trail: This challenging trail runs along the ridge of the Yakima River Canyon, offering spectacular views of the canyon. It is a favorite among hikers and horse riders looking for a scenic adventure.
  • Manastash Ridge Trail: This moderate trail offers beautiful views of the town and the surrounding mountains, with wildflowers and wildlife commonly seen along the way.

Best Things to Do in Ellensburg: Activities and Tours

With so many things to do in Ellensburg, it’s hard to know where to start! Here are some of the top activities and tours to make your trip even more memorable:

  • Ellensburg Rodeo: Watch cowboys showcase their skills and compete for prizes during the town’s annual rodeo, held every Labor Day Weekend.
  • Wine Tasting: Ellensburg is home to a burgeoning wine scene, with many local wineries offering tours and tastings. Try some of the region’s best wines at Kana Winery, Thrall and Dodge Winery, and others.
  • Aerial Tours: Take in the breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding landscapes from above with an aerial tour.
  • Geocaching: This treasure hunt-style game is popular in the area and an excellent way to explore the town and its surroundings while having fun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ellensburg

Day 1: Explore the Clymer Museum of Art and enjoy a scenic drive through Solitude Canyon. Day 2: Visit the Kittitas County Historical Museum and historic downtown Ellensburg. Day 3: Take a hike on the Umptanum Ridge Crest Trail or the Manastash Ridge Trail. Day 4: Go wine tasting at local wineries, such as Kana Winery and Thrall and Dodge Winery. Day 5: Attend the Ellensburg Rodeo and enjoy the festivities. Day 6: Take an aerial tour of the area and go geocaching in the afternoon. Day 7: Explore the Iron Horse State Park and Lake Easton State Park for a scenic drive, hike, or picnic.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellensburg

Ellensburg is famous for its local cuisine and craft beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lamb Burgers: Delicious lamb burgers are a local specialty and can be found at local restaurants like The Yellow Church Cafe and Valley Cafe.
  • Fresh Produce: Try fresh, locally-grown produce from Kittitas Valley Farmers Market, located in the heart of downtown Ellensburg.
  • Beef Jerky: Sample the famous Beef Jerky from Owens Meats, a local meat shop that has been in business since 1887.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: With over 15 wineries located in and around Ellensburg, there are plenty of local wines to try, including those made from a variety of grapes, including Chardonnay, Riesling, and Cabernet Sauvignon.
  • Beer: Ellensburg is home to its own craft breweries, such as Iron Horse Brewery and Whipsaw Brewing.
  • Coffee: Enjoy fresh, locally roasted coffee at D&M Coffee and other local cafes, serving espresso, latte, and cappuccino.

Where to Stay in Ellensburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ellensburg offers a variety of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay in the area:

  • Best Western Plus Ellensburg Hotel: This centrally-located hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and fitness center.
  • Red Lion Hotel and Conference Center: Situated on the Yakima River, this hotel features a heated outdoor pool and beautiful river views.
  • Windtower Guesthouse: This eco-friendly bed and breakfast is located just outside Ellensburg and offers scenic mountain views and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ellensburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home? Here are some of the top local products to buy in Ellensburg:

  • Beef Jerky: Owens Meats’ Beef Jerky is a one-of-a-kind treat that can only be found in Ellensburg.
  • Autumn Leaf Jewelry: The Autumn Leaf Jewelry collection is made with real, dried leaves and captured in copper and silver, creating beautiful, unique jewelry pieces.
  • Local Wine: Bring home a bottle of the region’s best wines, only found in Ellensburg’s local wineries.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellensburg

Ellensburg is a small town with a strong sense of community. Here are some tips to help you experience the local culture and customs:

  • Respect nature: The North Cascades region is home to many protected areas and wildlife habitats; be respectful and follow the Leave No Trace principles when exploring the outdoors.
  • Be friendly: Ellensburg is known for its friendly atmosphere, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with the locals or fellow travelers.
  • Appreciate history: The town’s rich history and cultural traditions are intertwined with its land and its people. Take the time to learn about its past to better understand its present.

Best Time to Visit Ellensburg

Ellensburg has something to offer year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. Here’s a look at the best time to visit, based on seasonal activities:

  • Spring/Summer: From April to September, you can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing. The town is also home to several music festivals, rodeos, and outdoor concerts during this time.
  • Fall: September through November is a great time to visit the town during the harvest season. Local wineries host tastings, and the foliage is in full bloom.
  • Winter: From December to March, the region is transformed into a winter wonderland. Visitors can go skiing, snowshoeing, or simply enjoy the snowy scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellensburg

Here are some helpful tips for making the most out of your trip to Ellensburg:

Do:

  • Bring comfortable walking/hiking shoes: Many of the town’s attractions are within walking distance, and the surrounding trails require sturdy hiking shoes.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages: The town is home to a rich culinary scene, with local specialties and craft beverages worth trying.
  • Be respectful of local wildlife and nature: North Cascades region is famous for its wildlife and natural scenery. Be respectful and follow the Leave No Trace principles when exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Littering is prohibited in the area, and visitors are encouraged to dispose of their trash properly.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions: The town’s cultural heritage is important to its residents, so don’t ignore or dismiss it.
  • Overlook the importance of safety: The region is famous for its rugged natural beauty, but it can also be hazardous for those who are not prepared. Always take necessary precautions before going on hikes or nature walks.

Safety and Health

Ellensburg is a safe and welcoming place to visit, with plenty of medical clinics and hospitals in the area. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Ellensburg

  • Take necessary precautions when exploring the outdoors: The surrounding North Cascades region is beautiful but can also be dangerous. Visitors are encouraged to take necessary precautions and be aware of potential hazards, such as slippery rocks and steep terrain.
  • Watch out for wildlife: The North Cascades is home to many wildlife species, including bears, cougars, and coyotes. Visitors are advised to keep a safe distance from wildlife and never approach them.
  • Be aware of weather conditions: The town is prone to sudden weather changes, and visitors are advised to check the forecasts and prepare accordingly.

Health in Ellensburg

  • Stay hydrated: The region is arid with low humidity levels, and visitors are advised to drink plenty of water and avoid prolonged sun exposure.
  • Protect yourself from insects: The town is home to many insects, including mosquitoes and ticks that carry diseases. Visitors are advised to wear insect repellent and long-sleeve shirts and pants to protect themselves.

Getting to Ellensburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ellensburg is accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options for getting to and around the area:

Airports Near Ellensburg

  • Yakima Air Terminal: Located 43 miles from Ellensburg, Yakima Air Terminal is the closest airport, with domestic flights to and from Seattle-Tacoma International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Ellensburg

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located downtown, this station connects Ellensburg to major cities in Washington state and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option, with several rental companies located at the airport and in the town.
  • Taxis and Rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are available in the town.
  • Bikes and Scooters: Ellensburg has a bike-share program and allows electric scooters for rent as well.

How to Get Around in Ellensburg

Ellensburg is a walkable town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi or rideshare for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Ellensburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in the area, there are many nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Leavenworth: This Bavarian-style town is located 90 miles east of Ellensburg and is a popular destination for its holiday festivities and quaint shops.
  • Yakima: Located just 45 miles south of Ellensburg, Yakima is famous for its wineries, breweries, and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Suncadia Resort: This luxury resort is located 30 miles from Ellensburg and features a championship golf course, spa, and several dining options.

Conclusion

Ellensburg is a small town with a lot of character and offers something for everyone, from natural beauty and outdoor adventures to culture and history. We hope this comprehensive guide helped you plan your trip and provided valuable tips for making your time in Ellensburg unforgettable. Safe travels!
