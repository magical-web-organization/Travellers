Introduction to Lynden

Nestled in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Lynden, Washington, is a charming city steeped in historic traditions and natural wonders. Its quaint downtown lined with unique restaurants, boutique shops, and restored brick buildings evokes a bygone era while providing an inviting atmosphere for visitors. Lynden prides itself on warm hospitality, vibrant culture, and a range of outdoor activities that include hiking, biking, and kayaking. Whether you’re looking for family fun or a quiet retreat, Lynden is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Lynden

Lynden’s natural beauty is unparalleled and coexists with the city’s historic landmarks. The following list seeks to highlight both.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lynden is a city steeped in rich history and culture, and it is essential to visit some of its cultural and historical landmarks. The top three must-visit cultural and historical places to visit are:

  1. Lynden Pioneer Museum: Since 1972, the Lynden Pioneer Museum has been a centerpiece of community heritage preservation for the area. Over 40,000 square feet, and four floors of exhibits packed full of Lynden, and Whatcom County artifacts, and hands-on displays and interactive exhibits guide visitors through the area’s history dating back to the early 1800s.

  2. Jansen Art Center: The Jansen Art Center is a stunning gallery in the heart of downtown Lynden. While there, visitors can appreciate the artwork, attend a show or event, or participate in a class or workshop.

  3. Lynden Dutch Bakery: Not only is Lynden Dutch Bakery one of the best places to enjoy a delicious pastry, but it is also a historical landmark as the building has been around for over a century.

Natural Attractions of Lynden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lynden is surrounded by natural beauty, so it is no surprise that visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities. The top three natural attractions of Lynden are:

  1. Berthusen Park: Berthusen Park is a beautiful place to spend the day. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, fishing, and picturesque picnic spots along the Nooksack River.

  2. Lynden City Park: Perfect for a quick stroll or a family picnic, Lynden City Park features a large grassy field, playground equipment, two basketball courts, and its iconic windmill.

  3. Homestead Golf and Country Club: Located on the outskirts of Lynden, Homestead Golf and Country Club, is an 18-hole championship golf course that features four sets of tees, winding streams, and rolling greens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lynden

Nature lovers visiting Lynden will have plenty of opportunities to explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Lynden.

  1. Ten Mile Loop Trail: This 12-mile hiking and biking trail is located in Whatcom County and offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains and valley.

  2. Nooksack Loop : This flat, scenic and bike-friendly trail starts near Lynden’s city park and winds through miles of farmland, the river valley, and a beautiful historic covered bridge.

  3. North Lake Whatcom Trail: This 19-mile hiking and biking trail is located in the nearby city of Bellingham and features stunning views of Lake Whatcom.

Best Things to Do in Lynden: Activities and Tours

Lynden offers no shortage of exciting and engaging activities for all ages. Here are some of the top things to do in Lynden:

  1. Fairway Centre: the biggest little arcade in Lynden, where visitors can play various games, including pinball, air hockey, skee-ball and more!

  2. Sportsman Chalet: It provides visitors with access to an outdoor gun range, archery range, and a fly fishing pond all in one place.

  3. Lynden Farmers Market: Locals can enjoy locally sourced produce, foods, and crafts. Occurring weekly from May to October, both locals and visitors alike flock to this market.

Lynden offers visitors plenty to do, so planning a full week’s itinerary is never difficult. Here’s an example of a full week’s worth of Lynden activities:

Food and Drink Scene of Lynden

Lynden also has a fantastic food scene that visitors simply cannot miss. From vibrant farm to table options to luxurious dining experiences visitors will enjoy the cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lynden Dutch Bakery’s Stroopwafel: An absolute must-try for visitors is the Lynden Dutch Bakery’s delicious Stroopwafel.

  2. Jansen Art Center Café’s Roasted Beet Salad: This roasted beet salad, made in-house, features goat cheese, beet root salad, and a delicious citrus dressing.

  3. Over Easy’s Bacon and Brie Burger: Over Easy provides visitors with access to an incredibly delicious Bacon and Brie Burger, made with locally sourced Angus beef patties.

Local Beverages

  1. Full Bloom Lavender’s Lavender Lemonade: This lavender lemonade is a refreshing drink that can cool visitors off on a hot day.

  2. Bellewood Acres Hard Cider: Bellewood Acres Hard Cider is a locally made cider that features an incredible blend of apples for a wonderfully bold cider.

  3. Lynden Coffee Company’s Espresso: While in Lynden, visitors should try some delicious espresso from Lynden Coffee Company. Locals and visitors alike swear by it.

Where to Stay in Lynden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lynden is home to several fantastic hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are the top three places to stay in Lynden:

  1. Inn at Lynden: The Inn at Lynden is located in the heart of downtown Lynden and features comfortable rooms and a vast collection of books.

  2. The Mill Inn: The Mill Inn is a storybook-style restored inn with cozy rooms and soft jazz music floating throughout the communal areas.

  3. The Dutch Room House: A separate “casita” adjacent to the main house provides visitors with privacy and comfort and a quiet place to stay.

What to Buy in Lynden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lynden has an array of items that visitors can buy and bring home as souvenirs. They include:

  1. Windmill Cookies: These delicious cookies come in a range of flavors but are most popular for their traditional almond flavor.

  2. Wooden Boxes: These handmade wooden boxes offer visitors a unique souvenir, made from locally sourced materials, and are perfect to take home.

  3. Full Bloom Lavender’s Products: At Full Bloom Lavender, visitors can buy an array of products ranging from body lotion to lavender sachets.

Local Culture and Customs of Lynden

Lynden is rich in culture and steeped in tradition, with many customs and quirks that visitors should be aware of. Here are a few customs and cultural norms to keep in mind:

  • The community has deep Dutch roots and celebrates this cultural heritage through wooden shoes and tulips.
  • The locals are friendly and welcoming, so be prepared to engage in conversations with the community.
  • Visitors should also visit the Lynden Chamber of Commerce and Visitors Bureau for more information about the community’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Lynden

The peak tourist season in Lynden is between June and September, when the weather is pleasant and several events occur. However, Lynden is beautiful all year round, so visitors should plan their trip based on what works best for them.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lynden

While visiting Lynden, it’s essential to follow the local customs and maintain a level of politeness. Here are some Do’s and Don’ts for Lynden:

Do:

  • Do engage in conversations with the locals and experience the community.
  • Do explore Lynden’s history and take in the charm of the town.
  • Do visit during the peak season when more events are occurring.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the religious traditions of the locals.
  • Don’t litter, littering is a huge offense in Lynden and considered disrespectful.
  • Don’t go out after dark in unlit areas alone, always take safety precautions.

Safety and Health

Safety and health concerns are paramount anywhere visitors go, Lynden is no exception to that. Visitors should be familiar with these following tips:

Safety in Lynden

  • Visitors should take appropriate safety measures when engaged in outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.
  • Visitors should ensure that they leave valuable items in the hotel room safe or carry them in a secured bag or purse while walking through town.
  • Visitors should travel in well-lit areas during night-time and should avoid looking lost and unsure.

Health in Lynden

  • Visitors should drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  • Insect repellent should be used to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Visitors should use sunscreen to avoid sunburn in the summer.

Getting to Lynden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lynden is relatively accessible and straightforward to navigate. Visitors should keep the following modes of transportation in mind:

Airports Near Lynden

  • Bellingham International Airport: Located only 14 miles west of Lynden. Car rentals are available and Taxi services are accessible outside the terminal.

Bus Stations Near Lynden

  • The Greyhound station in Bellingham is located 16 miles away from Lynden. Reservations can be made online or in person.

Other Options

  • Taxis are available in Bellingham, and Ubers and Lyfts are also accessible in the region.

How to Get Around in Lynden

Lynden is a relatively small town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, visitors can rent bicycles, cars, and taxis as well, to get around easier.

Where to Go Next from Lynden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While in Lynden, visitors can explore the nearby attractions such as Mount Baker or the San Juan Islands, which are easily accessible options for visitors.

Conclusion

Lynden is a beautiful destination that combines natural beauty with rich history and culture, making it an excellent choice of destination for visitors of all kinds. With an array of outdoor activities, delicious food, and friendly locals, visitors to Lynden are sure to have an experience of a lifetime!
