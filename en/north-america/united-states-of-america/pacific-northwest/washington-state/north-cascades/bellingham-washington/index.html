Introduction to Bellingham (Washington)

Bellingham is a charming city located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States of America. Nestled in the North Cascades, Bellingham is surrounded by stunning natural beauty and boasts a rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Bellingham can enjoy a variety of outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, kayaking, and skiing, as well as explore the city’s many museums, historical sites, and local restaurants. Whether you’re looking for an action-packed vacation or a relaxing getaway, Bellingham has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bellingham (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Whatcom Museum: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of Whatcom County. Visitors can explore the eclectic collection of artifacts, photographs, and artwork, as well as take part in various educational programs and events.

  • SPARK Museum of Electrical Invention: This museum features a collection of vintage electrical devices and interactive exhibits that explore the history and science of electricity.

  • Old City Hall: This historic building was constructed in 1892 and served as the city’s primary governmental center until 1933. Today, visitors can tour the building and learn about its fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Bellingham (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Whatcom Falls Park: This scenic park features a picturesque waterfall, hiking trails, picnic areas, and a playground. Visitors can also enjoy fishing, swimming, and boating in the park’s several lakes.

  • Mount Baker: This extinct volcano is located about 50 miles east of Bellingham and is a popular destination for skiing, snowboarding, and hiking.

  • Larrabee State Park: This park is located on the coast of Bellingham Bay and features stunning views of the water and nearby islands. Visitors can explore the park’s hiking trails, beach, and campsites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bellingham (Washington)

Bellingham is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some popular routes include:

  • Interurban Trail: This 6-mile trail runs from Fairhaven to Larrabee State Park and offers scenic views of the bay and surrounding mountains.

  • Oyster Dome Trail: This 5.2-mile hike takes visitors to the top of Oyster Dome, which provides panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  • Lake Padden Loop: This 2.6-mile loop around Lake Padden is a popular destination for runners and walkers.

Best Things to Do in Bellingham (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Chuckanut Drive: This scenic drive offers stunning views of the bay and surrounding mountains.

  • Bellingham Farmers Market: This vibrant market features local produce, crafts, and live music.

  • Boundary Bay Brewery: This local brewery offers tours and tastings of its award-winning craft beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bellingham (Washington)

Day 1: Explore the city’s museums and historical sites, including the Whatcom Museum and Old City Hall.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Oyster Dome and take in the panoramic views.

Day 3: Visit Mount Baker and enjoy a day of skiing or snowboarding.

Day 4: Relax at Whatcom Falls Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive on Chuckanut Drive and stop at some of the vista points along the way.

Day 6: Visit the Bellingham Farmers Market and sample some local produce and crafts.

Day 7: Explore the city’s food and drink scene, including a tour of Boundary Bay Brewery and dinner at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Bellingham (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Bellingham is known for its fresh seafood, and fish and chips is a local favorite. Some popular spots include Captain’s Nautical Supplies and The Fairhaven Fish and Chips.

  • Kulshan Pizza: This local pizzeria offers wood-fired pies topped with fresh, local ingredients.

  • Taylor Shellfish Farms: This oyster farm and restaurant offers some of the freshest oysters in the area, as well as other seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Boundary Bay Brewery: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

  • Chuckanut Brewery: This brewery specializes in German-style beers, including hefeweizens and pilsners.

  • Mount Baker Vineyards: This local winery produces a variety of reds and whites, made with grapes grown in the nearby foothills.

Where to Stay in Bellingham (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Chrysalis Inn and Spa: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of Bellingham Bay and a full-service spa.

  • Hotel Bellwether: This waterfront hotel features an on-site restaurant and marina.

  • The Heliotrope Hotel: This boutique hotel offers stylish rooms and a central location in downtown Bellingham.

What to Buy in Bellingham (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chuckanut Bay Foods: This local company produces a variety of gourmet foods, including smoked salmon and artisanal cheeses.

  • Whatcom Art Market: This art market features a wide selection of locally-made crafts, including jewelry, pottery, and paintings.

  • Fran’s Chocolates: This Seattle-based chocolate company has a location in Bellingham and offers a variety of hand-crafted chocolates and truffles.

Local Culture and Customs of Bellingham (Washington)

Bellingham has a laid-back and friendly culture, with a strong appreciation for the outdoors and local arts. Visitors should be prepared for all types of weather, as Bellingham experiences a wide range of temperatures and precipitation throughout the year. It is also worth noting that Bellingham is home to a large student population, thanks to its proximity to Western Washington University.

Best Time to Visit Bellingham (Washington)

The best time to visit Bellingham is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and dry, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors should be aware that this is also the busiest time of year, and hotels and attractions may be more crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bellingham (Washington)

Do:

  • Do explore the city’s vibrant art scene, including galleries and street art.

  • Do take advantage of the area’s many outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and kayaking.

  • Do try the local seafood, including fish and chips and fresh oysters.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate outdoor gear, including rain jackets and sturdy hiking boots.

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment while exploring the outdoors.

  • Don’t neglect to visit some of the city’s museums and cultural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bellingham (Washington)

Bellingham is a generally safe city, with low levels of violent crime. Visitors should be cautious of their surroundings and take normal precautions to avoid theft and other petty crimes.

Health in Bellingham (Washington)

Visitors to Bellingham should be aware of the area’s high incidence of seasonal allergies and respiratory illnesses. It is also important to take precautions against ticks and other biting insects while hiking or exploring the outdoors.

Getting to Bellingham (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Bellingham is easily accessible by car, with Interstate 5 running directly through the city. There is also a small airport, Bellingham International Airport, which offers direct flights to several major cities. Additionally, visitors can take a train or bus to Bellingham from Seattle or Vancouver.

Airports Near Bellingham (Washington)

  • Bellingham International Airport (BLI)

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA)

  • Vancouver International Airport (YVR)

How to Get Around in Bellingham (Washington)

Bellingham is a walkable city, with many attractions located within easy walking distance of each other. Visitors can also take advantage of the public bus system, WTA, which runs throughout the city. Taxis and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Bellingham (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vancouver, Canada: This cosmopolitan city is located just a few hours north of Bellingham and offers international culture, dining, and entertainment.

  • San Juan Islands: These picturesque islands are located just off the coast of Bellingham and are home to a variety of wildlife, including whales and bald eagles.

  • North Cascades National Park: This stunning national park is located just east of Bellingham and features some of the most beautiful alpine scenery in the United States.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or food enthusiast, Bellingham has something to offer. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals, Bellingham is truly a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest.
