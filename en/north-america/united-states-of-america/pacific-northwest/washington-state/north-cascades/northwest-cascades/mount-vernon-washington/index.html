Introduction to Mount Vernon (Washington)

Nestled in the heart of the Northwest Cascades, Mount Vernon is a city steeped in historic charm and natural beauty. Famous for its tulip fields that bloom from March to April, this quaint city is perfect for a relaxing getaway or outdoor adventure. Mount Vernon boasts of picturesque landscapes, an alluring downtown, delicious food, and a thriving arts scene. Discover everything you need to know in this comprehensive guide to Mount Vernon (Washington).

List of Places to Visit in Mount Vernon (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tulip Town: Witness the colorful tulip fields and gardens, take guided tours, and shop for souvenirs at Tulip Town. This landmark is open from March through May.
  • Skagit Valley Historical Museum: Explore exhibits portraying the history and culture of Skagit Valley, including Native American artifacts, logging equipment, and military relics.
  • Lincoln Theatre: Catch a movie, live performance, or event at the beautifully restored Lincoln Theatre built in 1926.
  • Heritage Flight Museum: See the historic aircraft used during various wars and participate in interactive exhibits in the museum.

Natural Attractions of Mount Vernon (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest: Hike the trails, camp under the stars, or go fishing in the rivers of this vast wilderness area.
  • Deception Pass State Park: Walk the iconic bridge over Deception Pass, hike the scenic trails, and take a refreshing dip in Cranberry Lake.
  • Padilla Bay Reserve: Take a guided tour or hike the trails to explore the tidal flats and estuaries of Padilla Bay, a vital habitat for migratory birds and marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Vernon (Washington)

  • Tommy Thompson Trail: This paved trail winds through scenic bay views and farmland from Anacortes to La Conner, perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a morning jog.
  • Oyster Dome: The 6.6-mile trail takes you through forests and meadows up to the impressive Oyster Dome, which overlooks the vast Skagit Valley.
  • Mount Erie: Hike or bike to the summit of Mount Erie, where you can enjoy a panoramic view of the San Juan Islands, Skagit Valley, and Olympic Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Mount Vernon (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • La Conner Daffodil Festival: Admire the beautiful blooming daffodils in March and participate in fun events, such as a parade, vendors, and concerts.
  • Skagit Valley Tulip Festival: Take a self-guided tour or ride in a trolley through the tulip fields and gardens during April.
  • Whale Watching Tour: Take a boat tour from nearby Anacortes to watch the majestic whales and soak in the natural beauty of the Salish Sea.
  • Mount Vernon Farmer’s Market: Shop for locally grown produce, homemade artisan crafts, and listen to live music every Saturday during mid-June to October.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Vernon (Washington)

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Vernon (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salmon: Freshly caught salmon cooked to perfection is a local favorite and a must-try dish.
  • Baked goods and pastries: Indulge in an array of delicious baked goods, such as croissants, muffins, and bread, at Calico Cupboard.
  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: Savor the taste of locally sourced and freshly prepared farm-to-table cuisine at Nell Thorn.

Local Beverages

  • Whidbey Island Wine: Sample award-winning wines made with grapes grown on Whidbey Island at the Hellams Vineyard and Bloom’s Winery.
  • Skagit River Brewery: Enjoy a pint of locally brewed craft beer and grab a bite to eat at Skagit River Brewery.
  • Rexville Grocery and Bar: Try the Bloody Mary or other signature cocktails while enjoying live music at Rexville Grocery and Bar.

Where to Stay in Mount Vernon (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Conner Country Inn: A charming bed and breakfast with a cozy fireplace and modern amenities.
  • Candlewood Suites Burlington: A budget-friendly choice with spacious rooms and a fully equipped kitchen.
  • Swinomish Casino and Lodge: A luxury resort with a casino, spa, and gorgeously appointed rooms.

What to Buy in Mount Vernon (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tulips and Daffodils: Bring home a bouquet or bulbs of these colorful flowers as a memory of the Tulip and Daffodil Festivals.
  • Handmade Artisan Crafts: Find handmade jewelry, pottery, and woodwork by local artisans at the farmers’ market or boutiques in La Conner.
  • Fresh Produce: Buy locally grown fruits and vegetables at Skagit Valley Farmer’s Market or a local farm shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Vernon (Washington)

  • Friendliness: People in Mount Vernon are warm and friendly, so take time to engage in a conversation with locals and get to know their stories.
  • Respect for Nature: The people of Mount Vernon take pride in preserving and protecting the natural beauty of the place, so always respect nature and the environment.
  • Festivals and Events: The people of Mount Vernon love to celebrate, so be sure to join in the fun during the various festivals and events that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Mount Vernon (Washington)

The ideal time to visit Mount Vernon is from March to October when the weather is pleasant, and the town is alive with various festivals. The Tulip and Daffodil Festivals are the highlights of springtime, while summer and fall offer the perfect weather for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Vernon (Washington)

Do:

  • Explore the Town: Take a leisurely walk and explore the various shops and restaurants in downtown Mount Vernon.
  • Pack for the Weather: Dress in layers, and bring rain gear if you intend to visit during the rainy season.
  • Reserve Ahead: Book accommodations, tours, and attractions in advance during the peak travel season.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Always dispose of trash properly and help keep the town and its surroundings clean.
  • Ignore Backpacking Regulations: Follow backpacking regulations in the national parks, forests, and recreation areas to ensure your safety and protect the environment.
  • Trespass Private Property: Always respect private property signs and avoid trespassing in orchards, fields, and other private lands.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Vernon (Washington)

Mount Vernon is a safe town with a low crime rate. Always exercise caution while hiking or camping in the wilderness areas and follow the safety guidelines provided by the national parks and forests.

Health in Mount Vernon (Washington)

Mount Vernon has excellent healthcare facilities and hospitals that provide top-quality care. However, always carry travel insurance and take precautions to avoid ticks, mosquitoes, and other insects that can cause illnesses.

Getting to Mount Vernon (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Vernon (Washington)

  • Bellingham International Airport: 35 miles from Mount Vernon.
  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport: 70 miles from Mount Vernon.

Bus Stations Near Mount Vernon (Washington)

  • Greyhound Bus Line: Stops at Skagit Station in Burlington, which is six miles from Mount Vernon.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Rent a car from Bellingham or Seattle and drive to Mount Vernon.
  • Taxi and Rideshare Services: Uber, Lyft, and taxi services are available in Mount Vernon.

How to Get Around in Mount Vernon (Washington)

The best way to get around Mount Vernon is by car. However, if you prefer public transportation, Skagit Transit provides bus services that connect Mount Vernon to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Mount Vernon (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Anacortes: Explore the beautiful San Juan Islands, take a ferry tour, and hike the scenic trails in Washington Park.
  • Bellingham: Visit the historic Fairhaven District, hike Oyster Dome, and enjoy the beach at Larrabee State Park.
  • Seattle: Explore the famous Space Needle, the Pike Place Market, and other iconic tourist attractions in this bustling city.

Conclusion

Mount Vernon is a hidden gem in the Northwest Cascades, offering a perfect mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural delights. Whether you are a nature lover or a history buff, Mount Vernon has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town and experience all that it has to offer.
