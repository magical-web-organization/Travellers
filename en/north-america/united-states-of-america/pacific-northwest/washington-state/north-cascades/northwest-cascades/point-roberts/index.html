Introduction to Point Roberts

Point Roberts is a small, picturesque peninsula located in the Northwest Cascades region of Washington State, surrounded on three sides by water and on the fourth by the Canadian border. It’s a unique place that has a special relationship with nearby Vancouver and the rest of British Columbia, as the only land route to Point Roberts is through Canada. Despite its isolated location, Point Roberts is a beautiful destination that boasts scenic views, outdoor adventures, and a quaint small-town atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Point Roberts

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Despite its small size, Point Roberts has several culturally important attractions that are worth visiting:

Point Roberts Historical Society Museum

The Point Roberts Historical Society Museum showcases the art, history and culture of Point Roberts. The museum has many exhibits that capture the spirit of Point Roberts and highlight the area’s rich history.

Lighthouse Marine Park

Located on the western edge of the peninsula, Lighthouse Marine Park is a picturesque park that has a small lighthouse perched on a rocky outcropping. The park is a popular spot for picnics, hiking, and bird watching.

Natural Attractions of Point Roberts: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Point Roberts is a nature lover’s paradise, with a wide range of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenic views:

Lily Point Marine Park

Lily Point Marine Park is an expansive, natural park that has several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the Strait of Georgia and the San Juan Islands. The park is also home to a variety of wildlife, such as bald eagles, sea otters, and seals.

Lighthouse Park

Lighthouse Park is nestled on the southern side of the peninsula and is renowned for its stunning sunset views that stretch across the Strait of Georgia. The park has several walking trails, beaches, and beautiful trees that provide shade and shelter.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Point Roberts

Point Roberts has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the landscape. Here are some notable ones:

Lily Point Trail

Lily Point Trail is a two-mile trail that starts at the end of Benson Road and winds its way through a forest of Douglas firs, alongside bluffs, and through meadows. The trail offers breathtaking views of the Strait of Georgia and the San Juan Islands.

Sunlight Beach Trail

Sunlight Beach Trail is a short but scenic trail that leads to a small beach with picnic tables and restrooms. The trail is a great spot for a leisurely walk, and you can also bring your bike along for a quick ride.

Best Things to Do in Point Roberts: Activities and Tours

Point Roberts offers a range of activities and tours that cater to everyone’s interests. Here are some notable ones:

Whale Watching Tours

Whale watching tours are an excellent way to experience the beauty of marine life in the area. You can expect to see orcas, humpback whales, and gray whales during the tours.

Kayak Rentals

Kayak rentals are a great way to explore the coastline and islands surrounding Point Roberts. Rentals are typically available from early June through late September.

Food and Drink Scene of Point Roberts

Must-Try Dishes

Point Roberts has a variety of unique and delicious dishes worth trying:

Fish and Chips

Fresh, local seafood is abundant in Point Roberts, and nothing beats a plate of fish and chips at a local seafood restaurant.

Clam Chowder

Clam Chowder is another local specialty that is an absolute must-try. Made with fresh clams, potatoes, and cream, it’s a warm and satisfying dish.

Local Beverages

Local Beer and Wine

Point Roberts has several microbreweries and wineries that produce delicious local beer and wine.

Where to Stay in Point Roberts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Point Roberts has a limited number of accommodation options, but here are some of the best:

Point Roberts Guesthouse

The Point Roberts Guesthouse is a charming bed and breakfast that provides guests with an authentic Pacific Northwest experience. The cozy rooms are beautifully decorated and offer stunning views of the ocean.

The Point Roberts Marina Resort

The Point Roberts Marina Resort offers a range of accommodations, including cabins, RV sites, and tent sites. The resort has a marina, a restaurant, and a grocery store.

What to Buy in Point Roberts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Point Roberts has several gift shops and boutiques that sell unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the best:

Seafoam Lavender

Seafoam Lavender is a local farm that produces a variety of lavender-based products, such as body care, culinary, and fragrance products.

Blue Heron Gallery

The Blue Heron Gallery is a lovely art gallery that showcases the work of local artists. You can find paintings, sculptures, jewelry, and pottery, among other things.

Local Culture and Customs of Point Roberts

Point Roberts has a distinct culture and customs that reflect its history and isolated location. The local community is friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Point Roberts

The best time to visit Point Roberts is during the summer months of June through September. The weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Point Roberts

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Explore the local culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect the local community and culture.
  • Ignore safety regulations when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Point Roberts

Point Roberts is a relatively safe place, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions when exploring the outdoors. Always stay on marked trails, listen to warnings from park rangers, and follow safety guidelines when participating in water activities.

Health in Point Roberts

Point Roberts has limited healthcare facilities, so visitors should bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with them.

Getting to Point Roberts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Point Roberts

The closest airport to Point Roberts is Vancouver International Airport, which is located in British Columbia. From there, you can take a rented car or a taxi to Point Roberts.

Bus Stations Near Point Roberts

The closest bus station to Point Roberts is in Blaine, Washington, which is about 6 miles away. From there, you can take a taxi or rented car to Point Roberts.

Other Options

If you’re coming from Vancouver, you can take a ferry to Tsawwassen Ferry Terminal, then drive to the Point Roberts border crossing. Alternatively, you can take a charter plane or boat to Point Roberts.

How to Get Around in Point Roberts

The best way to get around Point Roberts is by car or bike, as there aren’t any public transportation options available.

Where to Go Next from Point Roberts: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Point Roberts is a great base for exploring the surrounding area, with several nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

Vancouver, British Columbia

Vancouver is a cosmopolitan city that is just a short drive from Point Roberts. It is known for its mild climate, scenic location, and cultural diversity.

Seattle, Washington

Seattle is a bustling city that is known for its unique neighborhoods, thriving food scene, and iconic landmarks.

Conclusion

Point Roberts is an incredible destination that offers stunning scenery, outdoor adventures, and a rich history and culture. It’s the perfect spot for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation. Whether you’re interested in hiking, kayaking, or simply enjoying the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Point Roberts.
