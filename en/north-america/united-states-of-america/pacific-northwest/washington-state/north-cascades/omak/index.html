Introduction to Omak

Located in the North Cascades of Washington, Omak is a city known for its scenic beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities. The town is home to cultural attractions as well, including museums and historical sites that showcase the area’s rich history. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to Omak and discover everything the city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Omak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Omak Heritage Days: This annual festival takes place in August and celebrates the history and culture of Omak. The event includes a parade, street fair, live music, and more.

  • Okanogan County Historical Society Museum: Learn about the history of the region at this local museum, which features exhibits on Native American culture, pioneer life, and local industries such as farming and mining.

  • Omak Civic League Park and Museum: This unique museum features exhibits on the history of Omak and the surrounding area, including a collection of vintage cars and farm equipment.

Natural Attractions of Omak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Omak Lake: This scenic lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. The surrounding park also hosts community events and has picnic areas.

  • Omak Mountain: Hike to the top of Omak Mountain for breathtaking views of the city and surrounding area. The trail is just over three miles long and is rated as moderate.

  • Omak Community Forest: This 90-acre forest is located just outside the city and offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and a playground. The forest is also home to a variety of wildlife, including deer and elk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Omak

If you enjoy hiking or biking, Omak has several routes for you to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Omak Mountain Trail: A moderate 3.3-mile hike to the summit of Omak Mountain, with stunning views of the city.

  • Omak Community Forest: This forest has several trails for hiking and biking, ranging from easy to moderate.

  • Omak Lake Trail: A 4-mile loop around Omak Lake, with beautiful views of the water and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Omak: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Omak offers a range of activities for visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • Omak Stampede: This annual rodeo takes place in August and features bull riding, barrel racing, and other events.

  • Omak Performing Arts Center: This venue hosts a variety of performances throughout the year, including plays, musicals, and concerts.

  • Omak Film Festival: This festival takes place every fall and showcases independent films from around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Omak

Day 1: Visit the Okanogan County Historical Society Museum and the Omak Civic League Park and Museum.

Day 2: Hike the Omak Mountain Trail for stunning views of the city and surrounding area.

Day 3: Explore the Omak Community Forest and have a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Take a tour of the area’s wineries and taste some of the region’s best wines.

Day 5: Discover the local food scene by visiting one of the city’s farmers’ markets or restaurants.

Day 6: Attend a performance at the Omak Performing Arts Center or see a film at the Omak Film Festival.

Day 7: Relax on the shores of Omak Lake, swim, and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Food and Drink Scene of Omak

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Pie: Omak is known for its delicious apple pie, made with locally grown apples and a flaky crust.

  • Fresh Berries: The region is also known for its fresh berries, including blueberries, raspberries, and strawberries.

  • Wild Game: Hunting is popular in the area, and many restaurants offer dishes featuring venison, elk, and other wild game.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wines: Omak is home to several wineries, known for their delicious wines made with grapes grown in the surrounding area.

  • Craft Beers: Omak has several local breweries, offering a variety of craft beers brewed on-site.

Where to Stay in Omak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • 12 Tribes Resort Casino: This resort offers luxurious accommodations, a casino, and a variety of dining options.

  • Omak Inn: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Omak and offers comfortable rooms and free breakfast.

  • K Diamond K Guest Ranch: This ranch offers rustic lodging in a beautiful setting, with horseback riding and other activities available.

What to Buy in Omak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Crafts: Omak has several artisan studios and shops that offer handmade crafts, including pottery, textiles, and jewelry.

  • Fresh Fruit: Bring home some of the region’s fresh produce, including apples, berries, and cherries (in season).

Local Culture and Customs of Omak

Omak is a community with a rich Native American heritage, and visitors should be respectful of this culture. It is also important to remember that hunting and fishing are popular activities in the area and to follow all local regulations regarding these activities.

Best Time to Visit Omak

The best time to visit Omak is from May to October when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, avoid traveling during the Omak Stampede and Omak Heritage Days if you prefer a more relaxed atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Omak

Do:

  • Respect the Native American culture of the area.

  • Follow all local regulations regarding hunting and fishing.

  • Visit the local wineries and breweries to taste the region’s best wines and beers.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect public lands by littering or damaging natural resources.

  • Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Omak

Overall, Omak is a safe city for travelers. However, it is important to practice basic safety precautions like locking car doors and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Omak

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude of the area and take precautions to avoid altitude sickness. It is also important to carry plenty of water and sunscreen when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Omak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Omak

The closest airport to Omak is the Omak Airport, but the airport has limited flights from Seattle.

Bus Stations Near Omak

Greyhound bus lines have a stop in Omak, making it easy to get there from other cities.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to reach Omak and get around the area.

How to Get Around in Omak

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Omak and the surrounding area, but visitors can also bike or walk in town.

Where to Go Next from Omak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Chelan: This scenic lake is just an hour’s drive from Omak and offers water sports, wineries, and outdoor recreation opportunities.

  • Methow Valley: This picturesque valley is known for its outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, biking, and skiing.

Conclusion

Omak is a beautiful city with a rich culture and history, offering an abundance of outdoor recreation opportunities and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or exploring local museums and historic sites, Omak has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this wonderful city has to offer!
