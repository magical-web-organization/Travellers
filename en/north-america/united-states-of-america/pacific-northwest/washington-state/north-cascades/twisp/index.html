Introduction to Twisp

Located in the stunning North Cascades, Twisp is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Nestled in Washington State’s heart, the town boasts incredible natural beauty, combined with a rich history and unique culture. Spanning over six square miles, Twisp offers a variety of experiences that make it a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer stay.

List of Places to Visit in Twisp

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shafer Historical Museum – Established in 1907, this museum features exhibits on Twisp’s history and the Methow Valley. The museum is home to many artifacts, including vintage automobiles, antique machinery, and local Native American artifacts.

  • Winthrop National Fish Hatchery – This hatchery has been in operation since the 1940s and is one of the largest in the region. Visitors can explore the hatchery, view the fish, and learn about the importance of preserving the region’s aquatic resources.

Natural Attractions of Twisp: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Methow River – The Methow River offers excellent opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and rafting. The turquoise water is crystal clear and flows from the Methow Valley through Twisp and beyond.

  • North Cascades National Park – Located just a short drive from Twisp, the North Cascades National Park boasts majestic peaks, clear alpine lakes, glaciers, and numerous hiking trails.

  • Twisp River Trail – This scenic trail is ideal for hiking and biking and meanders alongside the Twisp River through forests, meadows, and different ecosystems.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Twisp

Twisp offers several excellent trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Methow River Trail - Running along the banks of the Methow River, this 30-mile trail offers beautiful views, varied terrain, and easy accessibility.

  • Cutthroat Lake Trail – This 3.8-mile trail in the North Cascades offers breathtaking views. The trail is rated moderate and takes hikers through subalpine forests, meadows, and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Twisp: Activities and Tours

  • TwispWorks – This former school campus has been restored and now hosts several artisan workshops, eateries, and galleries.

  • Winthrop Rink – The Winthrop Rink is a great place to go ice-skating and offers a fun activity for families and couples.

  • Twisp Farmer’s Market – The farmer’s market is open on Saturday mornings and features fresh produce, handmade crafts, and locally-made items.

Week-Long Itinerary for Twisp

Day 1: Explore Twisp’s history via a tour of Shafer Historical Museum and a visit to the exhibitions at the Winthrop National Fish Hatchery.

Day 2: Head to the North Cascades National Park and choose a hiking trail based on your experience and fitness level.

Day 3: Enjoy the Methow River Trail for hiking or biking.

Day 4: Take a leisurely drive to nearby Winthrop to explore its historic downtown area or stop by the Winthrop Rink.

Day 5: Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of Twisp’s natural surroundings.

Day 6: Take a tour of TwispWorks and experience local art and culture.

Day 7: Round off your week-long stay by exploring the quaint cafes and shops of Twisp.

Food and Drink Scene of Twisp

Must-Try Dishes

  • Methow Valley Ciderhouse – A range of fruity hard ciders made from local apples and pears.

  • Wagon Wheel deli – Delicious sandwiches and soups made from locally sourced ingredients.

  • Sunny Pine Farm – Stop at this farm stand on Highway 20 to try their homemade ice cream that uses fresh, local ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Methow Valley Ciderhouse – Apart from cider, this place also serves delicious locally brewed beer and wine.

  • Lost River Winery Tasting Room – a local winery that offers visitors a range of award-winning red and white wines.

Where to Stay in Twisp: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Twisp River Suites – This elegant hotel offers suites with fireplaces, jetted tubs, and views of the surrounding forest.

  • Cedar Suite Motel – This centrally located motel offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.

  • Mt Gardner Inn – This family-run inn features cozy rooms and suites, including a hot tub and breakfast buffet.

What to Buy in Twisp: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Farmers Market Goods – Pick up locally made crafts and produce at the weekly farmer’s market.

  • TwispWorks Shopping and Studio Area – Stop by the studios and workshops to buy locally made crafts, ceramics and pottery, handmade clothing, jewelry, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Twisp

Twisp is located in the traditional territory of the Okanogan people, who have lived in the region for thousands of years. The community celebrates local indigenous culture and heritage through various events and monuments across town.

Best Time to Visit Twisp

The best time to visit Twisp is during the summer months between June and August. The weather is mild, and the natural beauty of the region is at its finest, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Visitors can also enjoy the annual Twisp River Music Festival that is held in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Twisp

Do:

  • Respect and embrace the local culture and customs.

  • Invest in appropriate gear for outdoor activities, including hiking shoes and rain gear.

Don’t:

  • Camp in non-designated areas.

  • Litter or leave trails and forests dirty.

Safety and Health

Safety in Twisp

The crime rate in Twisp is relatively low, making it a safe place to visit. Nevertheless, travelers are advised to take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Twisp

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water as the water quality of the region is not good. Visitors should also be aware of wildlife and their surroundings while out and about in nature.

Getting to Twisp: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Twisp

  • Pangborn Memorial Airport – This airport is about 2.5 hours away from Twisp.

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport – This airport is approximately 4.5 hours away from Twisp.

Bus Stations Near Twisp

  • Twisp Transportation Bus Station – This bus station connects Twisp to neighboring cities like Winthrop, Okanogan, and Omak.

Other Options

  • Car rental – Visitors can rent a car from the nearest airports or in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Twisp

Twisp is best explored on foot or by bike. However, visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Twisp: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • North Cascades National Park – The park is located approximately 40 minutes away from Twisp, making it an ideal day trip destination.

  • Winthrop – This historic town is located just half an hour from Twisp and offers visitors a range of activities, including shopping, dining, and ice skating.

Conclusion

Twisp is a hidden gem situated in the stunning North Cascades, and it’s perfect for anyone seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. This town boasts incredible natural beauty, a rich history and culture, and a vibrant local community. Now that you have read our comprehensive travel guide to Twisp, you’re ready to pack your bags and start exploring!
