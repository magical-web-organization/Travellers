Introduction to Cashmere

Cashmere is a small town in North Central Cascades, nestled in the heart of the Wenatchee Valley. The town is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and welcoming community. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Cashmere has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cashmere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cashmere Museum and Pioneer Village: Located in the historic downtown area, the Cashmere Museum and Pioneer Village is a must-visit for history lovers. The museum showcases the town’s rich history through exhibits and artifacts. The Pioneer Village allows visitors to step back in time and experience life in the late 1800s.
  2. Aplets & Cotlets Factory Tours: Take a free tour of Aplets & Cotlets Factory and learn about the history and production of these famous fruit candies.
  3. Liberty Orchard: Visit the Liberty Orchard and enjoy the scenic views while picking your own fresh fruits and berries.

Natural Attractions of Cashmere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ohme Gardens: Wander through the stunning Ohme Gardens and admire the lush vegetation, serene pools, and striking views of the Cascade Mountains.
  2. Lake Wenatchee State Park: Enjoy camping, hiking, fishing, and swimming at Lake Wenatchee State Park. The park offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.
  3. Leavenworth Ski Hill: Hit the slopes and experience world-class skiing at Leavenworth Ski Hill.
  4. Wenatchee River: Go rafting, kayaking, or paddleboarding along the Wenatchee River and experience the best of the Pacific Northwest’s outdoor adventures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cashmere

  1. Enchantment Lakes: Take the iconic hike to Enchantment Lakes and see the stunning alpine lakes, meadows, and wildlife.
  2. Devil’s Gulch Trail: Explore Devil’s Gulch Trail and admire the stunning scenery, including the Wenatchee River and the surrounding mountains.
  3. Icicle Ridge Trail: Hike the Icicle Ridge Trail and take in the breathtaking views of the Icicle Creek Valley.

Best Things to Do in Cashmere: Activities and Tours

  1. Cashmere Cider Mill: Visit the Cashmere Cider Mill and taste cider made the old-fashioned way.
  2. Cashmere’s Bavarian Village: Take a stroll through Cashmere’s Bavarian Village, filled with unique shops, restaurants, and charming architecture.
  3. Rocky Reach Dam: Take a tour of Rocky Reach Dam and learn about the hydroelectricity production and its impact on the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Cashmere

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Aplets and Cotlets: Try the famous fruit candies made at the Aplets & Cotlets Factory.
  2. Apple Pie: Savor the sweet, homemade apple pies from local orchards.
  3. Salmon: Sample the fresh salmon caught in the nearby Wenatchee River.

Local Beverages

  1. Hard Cider: Try the refreshing and delicious hard cider made from the apples grown in the orchards around Cashmere.
  2. Wine: Sip on the locally produced wine and enjoy the unique flavors of the region.

Where to Stay in Cashmere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Cashmere Mountain Bed & Breakfast: Enjoy the luxurious suites, stunning views of the mountains, and delicious breakfast at this quaint bed and breakfast.
  2. Cascadia Inn: Relax in the modern rooms, take a dip in the pool, and enjoy the central location of Cascadia Inn.

What to Buy in Cashmere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Aplets and Cotlets: Take home a bag of the famous fruit candies as a sweet souvenir.
  2. Apple Pie: Bring home a homemade apple pie and savor the taste of Cashmere.
  3. Handmade Crafts: Browse the local shops for unique and handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Cashmere

Cashmere is a friendly and traditional community, deeply connected to the region’s natural beauty and rich history. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and attend community events, including the Apple Blossom Festival and the Cashmere Founder’s Day Parade.

Best Time to Visit Cashmere

The best time to visit Cashmere is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring and fall are also beautiful seasons in Cashmere, with pleasant temperatures and stunning fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cashmere

Do:

  1. Respect the local nature and wildlife.
  2. Participate in community events and festivals.
  3. Shop at local businesses and support the local economy.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  2. Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  3. Ignore safety warnings while engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cashmere

Cashmere is a safe and peaceful town. However, visitors should take precautions while engaging in outdoor activities and follow safety guidelines and rules.

Health in Cashmere

Cashmere has access to quality health care facilities. Visitors should come prepared with adequate health insurance and take precautions to avoid exposure to nature-related risks.

Getting to Cashmere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cashmere can be accessed by road and air.

Airports Near Cashmere

  1. Pangborn Memorial Airport (Wenatchee, WA)
  2. Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (Seattle, WA)

Bus Stations Near Cashmere

  1. Wenatchee Columbia Station
  2. Leavenworth Station

Other Options

  1. Taxi and private transportation services are available.

How to Get Around in Cashmere

The best way to get around Cashmere is by car. Rental cars are available at the airports and in the nearby towns. Bicycles and scooters can also be rented for local trips.

Where to Go Next from Cashmere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Leavenworth: Visit the nearby Bavarian town of Leavenworth and enjoy the unique culture, food, and outdoor activities.
  2. Wenatchee: Explore the town of Wenatchee and its charming downtown, parks, and trails.
  3. Chelan: Head to the stunning Lake Chelan and enjoy fishing, boating, and swimming in the crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Cashmere is a hidden gem in the North Cascades, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, the town has something for everyone. Come and experience the best of the Pacific Northwest in Cashmere, Washington.
