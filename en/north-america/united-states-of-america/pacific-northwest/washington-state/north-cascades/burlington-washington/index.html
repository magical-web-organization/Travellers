Introduction to Burlington (Washington)

Burlington (Washington) is a charming city situated on the Skagit River. It was founded in the late 19th century and has a rich history. Today, Burlington is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning nature, outdoor adventures, picturesque views, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Burlington (Washington)

There are plenty of things to see and do in Burlington (Washington). Let’s take a look at some of its top-rated attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Children’s Museum of Skagit County: It is a fun-filled attraction for kids that offers interactive exhibits, educational programs, and plenty of play areas.

  • The Heritage Flight Museum: This museum showcases aviation history and has an extensive collection of vintage aircraft.

  • The Burlington Historical Museum: You can learn about the history of Burlington (Washington) and its surrounding areas at this museum.

Natural Attractions of Burlington (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Skagit Valley Tulip Festival: The festival takes place in April and May, and you can see millions of tulips in bloom.

  • The Little Mountain Park: It has over six miles of hiking trails and offers stunning views of the Skagit Valley.

  • The North Cascades National Park: This park offers breathtaking views, hiking trails, camping opportunities, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burlington (Washington)

Burlington (Washington) has several hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best options:

  • The Skagit River Walk: It is a scenic trail along the Skagit River that connects several parks and green spaces in Burlington (Washington).

  • The Cascade Trail: The trail is a 22-mile long flat rail-trail that connects Sedro-Woolley and Concrete.

  • The Mount Vernon Trail: The trail is a 10-mile long flat trail that runs along the Skagit River and offers stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Burlington (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • The Skagit River Bald Eagle Interpretive Center: You can see bald eagles in the winter months from this center.

  • The La Conner Channel Lodge Whale Watching Tour: You can spot whales, sea lions, seals, and other marine life on this tour.

  • The Tulip Town Observation Deck: You can see the tulip fields from a height of 80 feet from this deck.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burlington (Washington)

  • Day 1: Visit the Skagit Valley Tulip Festival.
  • Day 2: Explore the North Cascades National Park.
  • Day 3: Hike the Little Mountain Trail.
  • Day 4: Visit the Children’s Museum of Skagit County.
  • Day 5: Go on a whale watching tour.
  • Day 6: Explore the Cascade Trail on a bike.
  • Day 7: Visit the Heritage Flight Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Burlington (Washington)

Burlington (Washington) has a fantastic food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood: The city has several seafood restaurants that serve fresh and delicious seafood dishes.

  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: You can taste the local produce at several farm-to-table restaurants in Burlington (Washington).

  • Burgers and Fries: The city has several burger joints that serve juicy burgers and crispy fries.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Skagit Valley is known for its excellent wine, and you can taste it at several wineries in Burlington (Washington).

  • Craft Beer: The city has several craft breweries that offer a wide range of beer and cider.

What to Buy in Burlington (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burlington (Washington) has several local products and souvenirs that you can bring home as a memory of your trip. Here are some of the best options:

Local Culture and Customs of Burlington (Washington)

Burlington (Washington) has a rich cultural heritage that reflects in its art, music, and festivals. The Skagit Valley Tulip Festival, the Summer Meltdown Music Festival, and the Anacortes Arts Festival are some of the top-rated cultural events in the city.

Best Time to Visit Burlington (Washington)

The best time to visit Burlington (Washington) is from April to October, when the weather is pleasant, and several outdoor activities and events take place.

Bus Stations Near Burlington (Washington)
