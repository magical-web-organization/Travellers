Introduction to Colfax (Washington)

Nestled in the heart of Palouse in Washington state, Colfax is a picturesque town rich in history and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, this charming town has something for everyone. In this guide, we will take you on a tour of Colfax, highlighting the best places to visit, must-try dishes, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Colfax (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Whitman County Historical Society Museum - This museum offers a glimpse into the region’s history with a wide range of exhibits, including Native American artifacts, agricultural tools, and photographs.
  • St. Ignatius Church - This stunning 19th-century church boasts of intricate stained glass windows and beautiful paintings on the ceiling.
  • Rosalia Pioneer Museum - Located just outside of Colfax, this museum showcases the region’s rich history with a wide range of exhibits, including antique cars, old-fashioned telephones, and vintage clothing.

Natural Attractions of Colfax (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palouse Falls State Park - A popular spot for hiking and camping, Palouse Falls State Park offers breathtaking views of the majestic Palouse Falls and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Kamiak Butte County Park - A haven for outdoor lovers, Kamiak Butte County Park boasts of stunning vistas, hiking trails, and picnic spots.
  • Steptoe Butte State Park - Towering 3,612 feet above the surrounding landscape, Steptoe Butte offers stunning panoramic views of the region and is a popular spot for hiking and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colfax (Washington)

  • Bill Chipman Palouse Trail - This scenic 8.3-mile trail connects Colfax to the nearby town of Pullman and is ideal for a leisurely bike ride or a relaxing walk.
  • McCroskey State Park - With over 5,000 acres of lush forests and scenic vistas, McCroskey State Park offers several hiking trails for outdoor enthusiasts of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Colfax (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Farmers’ Market - Held weekly during the summer, the Colfax Farmers’ Market is a great place to sample local produce, baked goods, and handicrafts.
  • Wheatland Express - Explore Colfax and the surrounding region with the Wheatland Express, a sightseeing tour that takes you through the stunning landscapes of Palouse.
  • Art Walk - Every August, Colfax hosts an art walk that brings together a diverse range of artists, musicians, and performers for a weekend of cultural exchange and entertainment.

Food and Drink Scene of Colfax (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Ice Cream - This regional specialty is the perfect treat during a hot summer day.
  • Palouse Burger - A juicy burger made with locally sourced beef and topped with fresh produce.
  • Huckleberry Jam - Bring a jar of this delicious jam home as a souvenir.

Local Beverages

  • Palouse Winery - This award-winning winery offers a wide range of wines made from locally grown grapes.
  • Beer at the Bell - Located in the heart of downtown Colfax, this watering hole serves up a variety of craft brews and pub fare.

Where to Stay in Colfax (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Belair Inn - This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of downtown Colfax.
  • Robin’s Nest Bed and Breakfast - A cozy and charming bed and breakfast, perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • Colfax RV Park - If you’re traveling in an RV, this park offers affordable accommodations and convenient amenities.

What to Buy in Colfax (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Palouse Empire Fair memorabilia - The Palouse Empire Fair is a beloved annual event in Colfax, and you can take a piece of it home with you with commemorative t-shirts, hats, and other items.
  • Handmade Crafts - Local artists and craftsmen sell a range of handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Colfax (Washington)

  • Farming - The region’s agricultural heritage is still very much alive in Colfax, and visitors can witness the farming practices and traditions of the local community.
  • Festivals - Colfax celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Palouse Empire Fair and the Art Walk.

Best Time to Visit Colfax (Washington)

The best time to visit Colfax is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colfax (Washington)

Do:

  • Do visit the local museums and historical attractions to learn about the region’s rich history.
  • Do explore the surrounding natural wonders, like Palouse Falls and Steptoe Butte.
  • Do try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to be respectful of the local community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colfax (Washington)

Colfax is an extremely safe town, with very low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or camping.

Health in Colfax (Washington)

Visitors should make sure to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months when temperatures can be high. Additionally, visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, like hiking or camping, and make sure to take the necessary precautions.

Getting to Colfax (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colfax (Washington)

The closest major airport to Colfax is Spokane International Airport, which is located about 75 miles west of the town.

Bus Stations Near Colfax (Washington)

Greyhound offers bus services to Pullman, which is about 15 miles from Colfax. From Pullman, visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to reach Colfax.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent a car or hire a private car service to reach Colfax.

How to Get Around in Colfax (Washington)

The town is small enough to be explored on foot, but visitors can also rent a bike or a car to get around.

Conclusion

Colfax is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, with something to offer for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or cultural immersion, this charming town is a must-visit destination. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of Colfax for yourself!
