Introduction to Clarkston (Washington)

Clarkston is a charming town located in Washington state, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. It sits at the confluence of Snake and Clearwater rivers, making it a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts. The town has a rich history dating back to the Nez Perce tribe, who used to fish and trade in the area. Today, it is a modern town with a small-town feel and a vibrant arts and culture scene.

List of Places to Visit in Clarkston (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nez Perce National Historic Park: Learn about the history and culture of the Nez Perce tribe through exhibits, ranger-led talks, and interactive demonstrations.
  • Asotin County Museum: Explore the history of Asotin County through artifacts, photographs, and exhibits showcasing the area’s natural and cultural heritage.
  • Brackenbury Square: This historic area in downtown Clarkston features shops, restaurants, and an outdoor amphitheater that hosts concerts and events.

Natural Attractions of Clarkston (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hells Gate State Park: This scenic park on the Snake River offers hiking trails, picnic areas, swimming beaches, and boat docks for water-based activities.
  • Lewis-Clark Valley: Take a scenic drive along this highway that runs through the hills and valleys of the region, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Chief Timothy Park: Nestled on the banks of the Snake River, this park is a popular spot for picnicking, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clarkston (Washington)

  • Lindsay Creek Trail: This scenic trail winds through the forested hills above the town, offering stunning views of the valley and the river below.
  • Greenbelt Trail: This paved trail runs along the Snake River, connecting parks and scenic overlooks along the way.
  • Riverfront Park Trail: A scenic trail that runs through the park in the heart of downtown, offering views of the river and the cityscape.

Best Things to Do in Clarkston (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Snake River Adventures: Take a guided tour of the Snake River, exploring its history, ecology, and wildlife.
  • Hells Canyon Adventures: Explore the scenic Hells Canyon wilderness by jet boat, experiencing its rugged beauty and wildlife.
  • Silver Eel Outfitters: Rent paddleboards, kayaks, and other water sports equipment to explore the Snake River on your own.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clarkston (Washington)

Day 1: Arrive in Clarkston, visit downtown shops and restaurants
Day 2: Hike Lindsay Creek Trail or explore Riverfront Park
Day 3: Take a jet boat tour of Hells Canyon
Day 4: Visit Nez Perce National Historic Park
Day 5: Explore Lewis-Clark Valley and nearby wineries
Day 6: Rent paddleboards or kayaks and explore Snake River
Day 7: Depart Clarkston

Food and Drink Scene of Clarkston (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry pancakes: Made with local huckleberries, these sweet and savory pancakes are a local favorite.
  • Salmon and steelhead: Freshly caught from the Snake River, these fish are a staple of local cuisine.
  • Palouse burgers: Made with locally raised beef and topped with applewood-smoked bacon and blue cheese, these burgers are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Hells Canyon Winery: This local winery produces award-winning wines made from locally grown grapes.
  • Riverport Brewing Company: Sample a variety of locally brewed beers at this popular brewery in downtown Clarkston.
  • Clearwater Canyon Cellars: Enjoy stunning views of the valley as you sip on handcrafted wines at this local winery.

Where to Stay in Clarkston (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western RiverTree Inn: This family-friendly hotel in the heart of downtown offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Quality Inn & Suites: Located near Hells Gate State Park, this hotel offers affordable accommodations and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Riverfront Park Vacation Rentals: These vacation rentals offer stunning views of the Snake River and access to nearby parks and trails.

What to Buy in Clarkston (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry products: From jams and syrups to candies and pies, huckleberry products make a delicious and unique souvenir.
  • Local art and crafts: Support local artists and craftsmen by purchasing handcrafted pottery, jewelry, and other souvenirs.
  • Wheat products: Take home a taste of the Palouse region with locally grown wheat products like pancake mixes, flours, and breads.

Local Culture and Customs of Clarkston (Washington)

Clarkston has a strong community feel, with locals who take pride in their heritage and traditions. The town hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, including the Asotin County Fair, the Lewiston-Clarkston Bridal Fair, and the Native American Indian Art Show.

Best Time to Visit Clarkston (Washington)

The best time to visit Clarkston is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the landscapes are at their most beautiful. Summer is a popular time for water sports and festivals, but temperatures can get hot, reaching up to 100 degrees Fahrenheit. Winter can be cold and snowy, making it a less popular time for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clarkston (Washington)

Clarkston is a generally safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards when participating in outdoor activities, like hiking, boating, and water sports. Always follow safety guidelines, respect local regulations, and be aware of potential risks and hazards.

Health in Clarkston (Washington)

Clarkston has several medical facilities and pharmacies in the area, offering basic medical care and prescription services. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medications with them and to have appropriate health insurance coverage for the duration of their trip.

Getting to Clarkston (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Clarkston (Washington): The nearest major airport is in Spokane, Washington, approximately 100 miles north of Clarkston. Smaller regional airports are also available in Lewiston, Idaho, and Pullman, Washington.
  • Bus Stations Near Clarkston (Washington): Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to Clarkston from several surrounding cities, including Spokane, Boise, and Missoula.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also drive to Clarkston, as several major highways run through the region, including Highway 12 and Highway 95.

How to Get Around in Clarkston (Washington)

Clarkston is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors who plan to explore the surrounding area may want to rent a car or use a rideshare service. Rental cars are available through several major rental companies, and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Clarkston (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lewiston, Idaho: This neighboring town across the river offers several cultural and outdoor attractions, including the Lewis-Clark Center for Arts & History and the Hells Gate State Park.
  • Spokane, Washington: Located approximately 100 miles north of Clarkston, Spokane offers several urban attractions, including the Riverfront Park and the Northwest Museum of Arts & Culture.
  • Moscow, Idaho: This college town offers several cultural and outdoor attractions, including the Appaloosa Museum and Heritage Center and the Moscow Farmers Market.
