Introduction to Goldendale

Goldendale is a charming city located in the Columbia River Plateau in Washington (state). Situated in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, it is surrounded by picturesque natural beauty, including the Yakama Reservation, Mount Adams, and the Columbia River Gorge. It is a perfect vacation spot for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture vultures alike. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and more in Goldendale, WA.

List of Places to Visit in Goldendale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Presby Mansion: Built-in 1898, this beautifully restored Victorian-style mansion is a historical treat and is open for tours for visitors.
  • Stonehenge Memorial: A replica of the famous Stonehenge in England, this monument was built in 1918 to honor local soldiers who died in World War I.
  • Goldendale Observatory State Park: This observatory offers breathtaking views of the night sky, and visitors can also check out the telescopes and learn about astronomy.

Natural Attractions of Goldendale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Columbia River Gorge: This scenic area is a must-visit spot for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The area offers some of the best hiking trails, fishing spots, and kayaking opportunities in the region.
  • Klickitat Trail: This popular hiking and biking trail runs through a beautiful wilderness area and offers stunning views of the Klickitat River.
  • Maryhill State Park: This park is located on the Columbia River and offers a range of outdoor activities, including camping, hiking, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goldendale

  • Klickitat Trail: This 31-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with scenic views of the Klickitat River.
  • Columbia Plateau Trail: This trail runs for more than 100 miles and passes through many small towns, making it a great way to experience the local culture and history.
  • Goldendale Observatory Trail: This short, easy trail is perfect for families and offers stunning views of Mount Adams and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Goldendale: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Goldendale is known for its many wineries, and visitors can enjoy tastings and tours at local vineyards, including Maryhill Winery and Jacob Williams Winery.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the beautiful countryside of Goldendale on horseback with a guided tour from Goldendale Trail Rides.
  • River Rafting: Book a tour with Zooraft and experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the nearby Klickitat River.

Food and Drink Scene of Goldendale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tamales: Goldendale has an excellent Mexican food scene, and tamales are a local favorite at La Teresita.
  • Wild Game: For something a little different, try some wild game dishes at Glass Onion restaurant.
  • Craft Beer: Goldendale’s breweries are famous for their craft beers, including Everybody’s Brewing and Dwinell Country Ales.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The vineyards of Goldendale produce some of the best wines in the region, including premium merlots, cabernets, and chardonnays.
  • Beer: The local craft breweries offer a range of delicious beers, including ales, lagers, and stouts.
  • Coffee: Goldendale has a thriving coffee scene, with local coffee shops like the French Market offering unique blends and cute atmospheres.

Where to Stay in Goldendale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ponderosa Motel: This family-owned and operated motel offers comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a prime location in downtown Goldendale.
  • Golden Pine RV Park: This RV park offers spacious campsites, rental cabins, and a range of amenities, including a swimming pool and laundry facilities.
  • Pine Crest Bed & Breakfast: This beautiful B&B offers elegant guest rooms, home-cooked breakfast, and stunning views of the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Goldendale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine and Beer: Make sure to bring home a bottle of wine or craft beer from one of Goldendale’s many vineyards and breweries.
  • Klickitat County Honey: This locally sourced honey is famous for its unique sweetness and is available at local farmers’ markets.
  • Handmade Crafts: Goldendale has a vibrant community of artisans, and you can find handmade pottery, textiles, and jewelry at local craft fairs and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Goldendale

Goldendale is a small town with a close-knit community. Visitors should expect a warm welcome and be prepared to experience local customs and traditions during their stay. The town has a rich Native American history, and visitors can learn about the Yakama Reservation and its cultural significance at the Yakama Nation Cultural Center.

Best Time to Visit Goldendale

The best time to visit Goldendale is from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall months offer stunning foliage, and the winter months are perfect for a cozy getaway with holiday events like the “Christmas in the Gorge” celebration.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goldendale

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring sunscreen and plenty of water if you’re planning to spend time outdoors.
  • Explore the local hiking and biking trails.
  • Attend local events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Ignore fire safety rules.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Be disrespectful to the local community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goldendale

Goldendale is a safe town, but visitors should still take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, including locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Goldendale

Goldendale has a small hospital and a few pharmacies, but visitors with specific medical needs should consult with their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Goldendale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Goldendale

  • Tri-Cities Airport: Located about two hours from Goldendale, this airport offers flights from major airlines, including Delta and United.
  • Portland International Airport: Located about two and a half hours from Goldendale, this airport is a major international hub.

Bus Stations Near Goldendale

  • Greyhound Bus: The nearest Greyhound Bus Station is located in The Dalles, Oregon, about an hour from Goldendale.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great option for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area at their own pace.
  • Private Car: Private cars can be hired through transportation services like Lyft and Uber.

How to Get Around in Goldendale

The best way to get around in Goldendale is by car. Taxis are also available, but visitors should plan to call ahead since service is limited.

Where to Go Next from Goldendale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mt. Hood: Located about two hours from Goldendale, this stunning mountain is perfect for hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.
  • Bend: Located about three hours from Goldendale, this vibrant city is famous for its art scene, breweries, and outdoor adventures.
  • Portland: Located about two and a half hours from Goldendale, this bustling city provides endless options for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Goldendale is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest and offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. With stunning views, outdoor activities, and a thriving wine and beer scene, it is an ideal vacation spot for visitors of all ages. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan an unforgettable trip to Goldendale, Washington.
