Introduction to College Place

Nestled in the heart of Columbia River Plateau in Washington state, College Place is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Known for its stunning scenic views and outdoor adventures, College Place is the perfect destination for those seeking a relaxing and rejuvenating getaway.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through this charming town, showcasing the top things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more. Whether you’re here for a day trip or an extended stay, College Place is sure to leave you captivated and inspired.

List of Places to Visit in College Place

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

College Place is home to several museums and historical places that offer a fascinating glimpse into the town’s rich cultural heritage. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • Walla Walla University Museum of Natural History: This museum boasts an impressive collection of over 10,000 specimens of plants, animals, and fossils from all over the world. The exhibits showcase the biological diversity and evolution of life on earth.

  • Fort Walla Walla Museum: Located just a short drive from College Place, this museum features exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of the Pacific Northwest’s pioneer days. With a focus on military and agricultural history, it’s a great place to learn about the town’s roots and heritage.

  • Whitman Mission National Historic Site: This historical site is dedicated to preserving the memory of the Whitman Mission, a 19th-century Christian mission to Native Americans. Visitors can explore the mission’s buildings, learn about the people who lived and worked there, and gain insights into the challenges faced by early settlers in the West.

Natural Attractions of College Place: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

College Place is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Palouse Falls State Park: This state park is home to one of the most spectacular waterfalls in the Pacific Northwest. The 198-foot-tall falls are a sight to behold, and visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and picnicking in the park’s beautiful surroundings.

  • Walla Walla River: This scenic river flows through College Place and offers plenty of opportunities for kayaking, fishing, and hiking. With its crystal-clear waters and picturesque surroundings, it’s a popular spot for outdoor recreation.

  • Lyon’s Ferry State Park: Located just a short drive from College Place, this state park is a hidden gem that’s well worth a visit. With its stunning views of the Snake River and its charming campground, it’s the perfect place to unwind and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of College Place

College Place is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the region. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, there’s a trail for everyone. Here are a few top routes to check out:

  • Mill Creek Trail: This 3-mile trail is a great choice for beginners, with a relatively flat terrain and beautiful views of Mill Creek.

  • Johnson Ridge Trail: This 4.5-mile trail is more challenging, with some steep inclines and rocky terrain. The reward is stunning panoramic views of the Walla Walla Valley.

  • Touchet River Trail: This 10-mile trail follows the scenic Touchet River and offers hikers and bikers plenty of opportunities to spot wildlife and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.

Best Things to Do in College Place: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a culture vulture, College Place has something to offer. Here are some of the top activities and tours to enjoy:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: College Place is located in one of Washington’s premier wine regions, and wine tasting tours are a popular activity. You can visit local wineries and sample some of the world’s best wines.

  • Birdwatching: College Place is home to an amazing array of bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. You can spot eagles, hawks, hummingbirds, and many other birds in their natural habitats.

  • Culinary Tours: Foodies will love the culinary tours of College Place, where you can sample local dishes and learn about the town’s food culture.

Food and Drink Scene of College Place

Must-Try Dishes

College Place’s food scene is a reflection of the town’s cultural and agricultural heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Walla Walla Sweet Onion Soup: This savory soup is made with Walla Walla sweet onions, a local specialty that’s famous for its sweet flavor.

  • Chukar Cherry Chicken Salad: This mouthwatering salad features locally sourced Chukar cherries and grilled chicken.

Local Beverages

College Place is located in the heart of Washington’s wine country, and wine tasting tours are a popular activity. In addition to wine, here are some other local beverages to try:

  • Walla Walla Roastery Coffee: This locally roasted coffee is a favorite of coffee connoisseurs, with its smooth flavor and rich aroma.

  • Walla Walla Soda Company: This soda company produces natural, small-batch sodas that are made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of College Place

College Place is a friendly and welcoming town that values community and family. The town has a rich Christian heritage and is home to several churches and religious institutions.

Best Time to Visit College Place

The best time to visit College Place is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town’s natural beauty can be enjoyed year-round, and each season offers its own unique charm.

Getting to College Place: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near College Place

The closest airport to College Place is the Walla Walla Regional Airport, which is located just a few miles away. Other nearby airports include the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport and the Portland International Airport.

Bus Stations Near College Place

The closest bus station to College Place is the Greyhound Bus Station in Walla Walla, just a short drive away. Other nearby bus stations include the Tri-Cities Airport and the Pasco Transportation Center.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to College Place or arrange for a private shuttle or taxi service. Biking and walking are also popular options for those staying in town.

How to Get Around in College Place

Most of the top attractions, hotels, and restaurants in College Place are accessible on foot or by bike. Visitors can also arrange for car rentals or hire private transportation services for longer distances.
