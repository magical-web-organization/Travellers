Introduction to Soap Lake

Located in the Columbia River Plateau of Washington State, Soap Lake is one of the most unique destinations in the Pacific Northwest. Known for its mineral-rich waters and unique geological features, this small town has a lot to offer visitors looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and a chance to unwind and relax.

List of Places to Visit in Soap Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Soap Lake is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural heritage. Some of the highlights include:

  • Soap Lake Museum: Located in a historic bathhouse, the Soap Lake Museum offers a fascinating look at the town’s history, from its Native American roots to the early days of tourism.
  • Masquers Theater: This community theater hosts regular performances throughout the year, showcasing local talent and bringing in touring acts from around the region.
  • Scenic Byway: Take a scenic drive along the Coulee Corridor National Scenic Byway and explore the area’s history and natural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Soap Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Soap Lake is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, and there are plenty of opportunities to get outside and explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Soap Lake: Of course, the main attraction here is the lake itself – known for its mineral-rich waters and unique healing properties. Take a dip in the lake or explore the shoreline on foot.
  • Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park: Just a short drive from Soap Lake, this state park is home to some of the most stunning scenery in the region, including Dry Falls – a massive ancient waterfall that is now a dry cliff face.
  • Gorge Amphitheater: This world-famous outdoor music venue is located on the banks of the Columbia River, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Soap Lake

Soap Lake and the surrounding area offer some great options for hiking and biking, whether you’re looking for an easy stroll or a more challenging trek. Some popular routes include:

  • The Coulee Corridor Trail: This scenic route follows the historic railroad grade along the eastern edge of the Coulee Corridor, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park Trails: With several different trails to choose from, this state park is a great place to explore on foot or bike. Highlights include the Dry Falls Trail, which offers stunning views of the region’s geological history, and the Deep Lake Trail, which takes you past a beautiful mountain lake.

Best Things to Do in Soap Lake: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun things to see and do in Soap Lake, whether you’re traveling alone, with friends, or with family. Some top activities and tours include:

  • Watersports on Soap Lake: With its mineral-rich waters and sandy beaches, Soap Lake is a great place to go swimming, kayaking, or paddleboarding.
  • Masquers Theater Performances: Check the schedule at this community theater to see if there are any performances happening during your visit – it’s a great way to experience the local arts scene.
  • Historic Walking Tour: Take a self-guided walking tour of the town’s historic district, stopping in at the Soap Lake Museum and other local landmarks along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Soap Lake

If you’re planning on spending a week in Soap Lake, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s historic district, including the Soap Lake Museum and local landmarks like the historic bathhouses.
  • Day 2: Take a drive along the Coulee Corridor National Scenic Byway, exploring the region’s history and natural beauty along the way.
  • Day 3: Go for a hike or bike ride at Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park, taking in the stunning scenery of Dry Falls and Deep Lake.
  • Day 4: Relax on the beach at Soap Lake, trying out some of the watersports available on the lake.
  • Day 5: Visit the Gorge Amphitheater for a concert or event, taking in the stunning views of the Columbia River.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Quincy, exploring the town’s historic downtown district and enjoying local food and drink.
  • Day 7: Explore more of the region’s natural beauty with a visit to the Moses Coulee Preserve or the Columbia National Wildlife Refuge.

Food and Drink Scene of Soap Lake

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Soap Lake is known for its classic American cuisine, with plenty of hearty dishes to choose from. Some local specialties to try include:

  • Burgers and Fries: Head to one of the local diners to enjoy a classic burger and fries, made with fresh local ingredients.
  • Fresh Seafood: While Soap Lake is landlocked, there are plenty of nearby towns that offer fresh seafood dishes – try some local trout or salmon for a true taste of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Homestyle Breakfast: Start your day off right with a hearty breakfast at one of the local cafes, featuring locally sourced eggs, bacon, and other breakfast favorites.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Soap Lake has plenty of options to choose from, whether you’re in the mood for a cold beer or a local wine. Some top picks include:

  • Soap Lake Brewing Co.: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers made with local ingredients, including IPAs, amber ales, and stouts.
  • Local Wineries: While Soap Lake isn’t known for its wine, there are several nearby wineries that offer tastings and tours, including Jones of Washington and Cave B Estate Winery.

Where to Stay in Soap Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Soap Lake has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget or travel style. Some top picks include:

  • Soap Lake Natural Spa and Resort: This fully-equipped resort offers everything from mineral baths to outdoor swimming pools, making it the perfect place to relax and unwind.
  • Smokiam RV Resort & Campground: If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, this RV park and campground offers a variety of sites, as well as easy access to hiking and biking trails.
  • Local Guesthouses: Many local residents offer guestroom and Airbnb rentals, allowing visitors to experience the town like a true local.

What to Buy in Soap Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Soap Lake has plenty to offer. Some top picks include:

  • Mineral Products: Take home a jar of the famous Soap Lake mud or mineral salts, known for their healing properties.
  • Local Crafts: Check out the town’s artisan shops and galleries for handmade jewelry, ceramics, and other unique items.
  • Local Foods: Visit one of the local farmers’ markets to stock up on fresh produce, honey, and other local treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Soap Lake

Soap Lake has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Native American, European, and African American influences. Visitors should respect the town’s history and traditions, including the local customs around bathing and mineral use.

Best Time to Visit Soap Lake

The best time to visit Soap Lake is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is also beautiful in the spring and fall, offering stunning views of the changing seasons and a quieter, more relaxed atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soap Lake

Do:

  • Try the Watersports: Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a first-timer, Soap Lake is a great place to go swimming, kayaking, or paddleboarding.
  • Explore the Natural Beauty: Take a hike or a bike ride and explore the stunning landscape of Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park and the surrounding areas.
  • Respect the Local Culture: Visitors should respect the town’s history and traditions, including the local customs around bathing and mineral use.

Don’t:

  • Litter or Pollute the Lake: Soap Lake is a natural wonder, and visitors should do their part to keep it clean and pristine.
  • Ignore Safety Warnings: While Soap Lake is generally a safe place to visit, visitors should pay attention to any posted safety warnings or precautions.
  • Be Disrespectful to Locals: Visitors should be polite and respectful to the locals, taking care not to disrupt daily life or cause offense.

Safety and Health

Safety in Soap Lake

Soap Lake is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere. Visitors should still take basic safety precautions, however, like locking their cars and valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Soap Lake

The mineral-rich waters of Soap Lake are known for their healing properties, but visitors should still exercise caution when using any new health or wellness products. Always consult with a healthcare professional before using any minerals or supplements, and follow the instructions carefully.

Getting to Soap Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Soap Lake

The closest major airport to Soap Lake is the Spokane International Airport, which is about 1.5 hours away by car. There are also smaller regional airports in nearby towns like Moses Lake and Ephrata.

Bus Stations Near Soap Lake

There is no direct bus service to Soap Lake, but visitors can take a Greyhound bus to nearby towns like Moses Lake and then rent a car or take a taxi to Soap Lake.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Soap Lake, as it’s easily accessible from major highways like I-90 and US Route 2. Visitors can also take a train to nearby towns like Ephrata and then rent a car or take a taxi to Soap Lake.

How to Get Around in Soap Lake

Soap Lake is a small town, and most sights and attractions are easily accessible on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi for longer trips, or use one of the local shuttles or buses for transportation within the town.

Where to Go Next from Soap Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Pacific Northwest, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Some top picks include:

  • Lake Chelan: This beautiful lake is just a short drive from Soap Lake, offering opportunities for swimming, boating, and hiking.
  • Leavenworth: This charming Bavarian-style town is a great place to explore local shops and restaurants, as well as nearby hiking trails and scenic drives.
  • North Cascades National Park: This stunning national park is a bit farther from Soap Lake, but offers some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in the region.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or a chance to relax and unwind, Soap Lake has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and hospitality of this unique Pacific Northwest destination!
