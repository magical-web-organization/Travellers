Introduction to Coulee City

Located in the heart of the Columbia River Plateau, Coulee City is a small town in the state of Washington, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. It is known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich history and culture, and outdoor adventures.

Coulee City offers a wealth of activities for travelers seeking a dose of relaxation or an adrenaline-filled adventure. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking to get away from the hustle and bustle of city life, Coulee City has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, places to visit, food to try, and tips to know to make your trip to Coulee City an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Coulee City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Grant County Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of Grant County and the surrounding region, including exhibits on Native American artifacts, pioneer life, and local wildlife.
  • Banks Lake and Coulee City Historical Society: This society preserves the history of Coulee City and the surrounding areas. They have an extensive collection of artifacts and photographs, and they host special events throughout the year.
  • Dry Falls Visitor Center: This center offers a glimpse of the geological history of the region. Visitors can learn about prehistoric animals, see a scale model of the Dry Falls, and take a scenic drive to the top of the falls.

Natural Attractions of Coulee City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dry Falls: This 3.5-mile long canyon was formed by the Missoula Floods. Visitors can take a scenic drive or hike the trails to get the full experience.
  • Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park: This park is home to a network of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. Visitors can also fish, swim, or rent a boat on one of the lakes.
  • Lake Lenore Caves: These unique caves were formed by volcanic activity over 20,000 years ago. Visitors can hike the trails to explore the caves and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coulee City

For outdoor enthusiasts, Coulee City offers a variety of hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Banks Lake Trail: This 27-mile long trail runs along the northern shore of Banks Lake. Visitors can hike, bike, or horseback ride the trail while enjoying the beautiful scenery.
  • Sun Lakes-Dry Falls State Park Trails: This network of trails offers a variety of options for hikers, bikers, and horseback riders, ranging from easy to challenging.
  • Dry Falls Trail: This short, paved trail offers stunning views of the Dry Falls canyon.

Best Things to Do in Coulee City: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Coulee City Swimming Pool: Cool off during the summer months at the Coulee City Swimming Pool. They offer public swim times, swimming lessons, and pool parties.
  • Take a Boat Tour of Banks Lake: Discover the beauty of Banks Lake on a guided boat tour. Visitors can see wildlife, learn about the history of the area, and enjoy the cool lake breeze.
  • Go Fishing: Coulee City is home to a variety of fish species, including rainbow trout, bass, and walleye. Anglers can fish on Banks Lake or one of the many nearby rivers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coulee City

Day 1: Visit the Grant County Historical Museum and Banks Lake.

Day 2: Explore the Dry Falls Visitor Center and take a scenic drive along the canyon.

Day 3: Hike the Lake Lenore Caves trail and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 4: Rent a boat and spend the day fishing on Banks Lake.

Day 5: Take a guided boat tour of Banks Lake and learn about the history and wildlife of the area.

Day 6: Bike the Banks Lake Trail and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 7: Relax at the Coulee City Swimming Pool and enjoy the summer sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Coulee City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rocky Coulee Brewing: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers and pub food, including burgers and wings.
  • Bobby D’s BBQ: Enjoy delicious barbecue favorites such as pulled pork, brisket, and ribs.
  • Harvest Foods: This local grocery store offers a deli with made-to-order sandwiches and salads.

Local Beverages

  • Rocky Coulee Brewing: As mentioned above, this local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Banks Lake Pub and Eatery: This neighborhood pub offers a variety of beers on tap and a full bar.

Where to Stay in Coulee City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Coulee House Inn & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with free Wi-Fi and a fitness center.
  • Sunbanks Lake Resort: This resort offers cabins, RV and tent camping sites, and a marina on Banks Lake.
  • Coulee Playland Resort: This resort offers cabins, RV and tent camping sites, and a boat launch on Banks Lake.

What to Buy in Coulee City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banks Lake T-Shirts and Hats: Show your love for Banks Lake with some local apparel.
  • Rocky Coulee Brewing Beer: Take home a six-pack of craft beer from the local brewery.
  • Local Honey: Pick up a jar of honey from a local beekeeper.

Local Culture and Customs of Coulee City

Coulee City is home to a tight-knit community that values outdoor recreation and a simpler way of life. Visitors should be respectful of the natural landscapes and the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Coulee City

The best time to visit Coulee City is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy the many outdoor activities and events hosted in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coulee City

Do:

  • Respect the natural landscapes and wildlife of the area.
  • Try the local beer and barbecue.
  • Attend local events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Leave valuables in your car when parked.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coulee City

Coulee City is a safe and welcoming community. Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and boating, and take necessary precautions.

Health in Coulee City

There are no major health concerns in Coulee City. Visitors should bring necessary medications and be prepared for outdoor activities.

Getting to Coulee City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coulee City

  • Spokane International Airport (GEG): Located approximately 110 miles from Coulee City.
  • Columbia Basin Regional Airport (EPH): Located approximately 60 miles from Coulee City.

Bus Stations Near Coulee City

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Serves several cities in Washington, with a stop in Ephrata, located approximately 43 miles from Coulee City.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car from Spokane or Seattle and drive to Coulee City.
  • Private Shuttle: Several private shuttle services offer transportation to and from Coulee City.

How to Get Around in Coulee City

The best way to get around Coulee City is by car. Visitors can rent a car or bring their own. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation for those looking to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Coulee City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Coulee Dam: Located approximately 25 miles from Coulee City, this engineering marvel offers tours and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lake Chelan: This popular vacation spot is home to crystal-clear waters, wineries, and outdoor adventures. Located approximately 80 miles from Coulee City.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural landscapes, rich history and culture, and outdoor adventures, Coulee City is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a unique and unforgettable experience. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful Pacific Northwest town.
