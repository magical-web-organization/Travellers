Introduction to Walla Walla

Nestled in the Columbia River Plateau of Washington State, Walla Walla is a charming city that seamlessly blends natural beauty, culture, and history. Located in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, Walla Walla offers its visitors a unique mix of outdoor adventures, scenic landscapes, cultural attractions, and delicious food and drink scene that is simply unmissable.

Walla Walla got its name from the Native American Walla Walla tribe residing in this region for centuries. The city has a rich history that dates back to the early 1800s, marked by the first permanent American settlement in the state of Washington.

List of Places to Visit in Walla Walla

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Walla Walla has plenty of museums and historical sites that offer insights into the history of this fascinating city. Here are some of the top-rated cultural attractions in Walla Walla:

  • Walla Walla Fort Museum: Showcasing the city’s military and pioneer history, this museum has a remarkable collection of artifacts and photographs.

  • Kirkman House Museum: Built-in 1880, this historic residence of the Kirkman family is now a museum that provides a glimpse into their lifestyle and household.

  • Whitman Mission National Historic Site: This site commemorates the Oregon Trail’s tragic event in 1847, where 13 people were killed, including missionaries Marcus and Narcissa Whitman.

  • Maxey House Museum: Built-in 1880, this Victorian-era house hosts exhibits on the early pioneers and settlers in the Walla Walla Valley.

Natural Attractions of Walla Walla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Walla Walla, with its mild climate, offers visitors numerous opportunities for outdoor adventures and wonderful scenic landscapes. Here are some of the best natural attractions of Walla Walla:

  • Pioneer Park: Famous for its rose garden, this park is a popular destination for picnicking, cycling, walking, and playing.

  • Bennington Lake: Nestled in the foothills of the Blue Mountains, this lake offers great opportunities for fishing, boating, and hiking.

  • Palouse Falls State Park: Located just a short drive from Walla Walla, this park offers stunning views of the majestic Palouse Falls and the surrounding features.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Walla Walla

Whether you prefer hiking or biking, Walla Walla has some of the best trails that you can explore. Here are some of the top-rated hiking and biking trails in Walla Walla:

  • Mill Creek Trail: A 3.3-mile trail that runs through Mill Creek Natural Area, with great views of the Mill Creek Larkspur flowers.

  • Apple Capital Recreation Loop Trail: A 5.1-mile trail that takes you through lush green orchards and offers beautiful views of the city and the Blue Mountains.

  • Whitman Mission National Historic Site Trail: A 5.9-mile trail that explores the historic site of the 1847 massacre and the surrounding scenic landscape.

Best Things to Do in Walla Walla: Activities and Tours

Walla Walla offers endless possibilities for activities and tours that ensure you have a memorable stay in the city. Some of the popular things to do in Walla Walla are:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Walla Walla is known for its world-class wine scene, and no visit is complete without a wine-tasting tour to some of the renowned wineries.

  • Biking Tours: Go on a biking tour with an experienced guide and discover the scenic countryside, beautiful vineyards, and other hidden gems.

  • Golfing: Walla Walla has several golf courses, including Wine Valley Golf Club, that offers a world-class golfing experience and breathtaking views.

Food and Drink Scene of Walla Walla

Walla Walla’s food and drink scene is diverse and delicious, reflecting the city’s farm-to-table ethos. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Walla Walla:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Braised Short Ribs: Served with polenta and a sweet and sour jus, the tender short ribs offer hearty comfort food.

  • Crispy Pork Belly: Served with sauerkraut, grapes, and mashed potatoes, this dish features the melt-in-your-mouth pork belly.

  • Mussels and Fries: A classic French dish, the mussels are cooked to perfection in a broth of white wine, garlic, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Walla Walla is known for its award-winning wines produced in the Walla Walla Valley AVA (American Viticultural Area)

  • Craft Beer: The city has several breweries, including Burwood Brewing Company and Water Buffalo Brewery, offering an eclectic range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Walla Walla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Walla Walla has several options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in Walla Walla:

  • Marcus Whitman Hotel: Located in Downtown, this elegant hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, fine dining, and exceptional service.

  • Green Gables Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast that offers beautifully furnished rooms, a lovely garden, and a scrumptious breakfast.

  • The Maple Counter Guest House: A stylish and affordable guesthouse located in the heart of the city, offering private suites with comfortable amenities.

What to Buy in Walla Walla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Walla Walla’s local products and souvenirs are excellent representations of the city’s unique culture and history. Here are some of the top things to buy in Walla Walla:

  • Wine: Walla Walla’s award-winning wines make an excellent gift or souvenir.

  • Art and Crafts: The city has several galleries and shops selling locally made art and crafts that make for unique and thoughtful gifts.

  • Chocolates and Confections: Walla Walla’s chocolate shops, like Bright’s Candies and L’ecoDulce, offer exquisite chocolates and confections that are perfect for sweet-toothed visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Walla Walla

Walla Walla’s culture and customs are rooted in the city’s history and Native American traditions. Residents of the city are warm, friendly, and hospitable, making visitors feel welcome and at home.

Best Time to Visit Walla Walla

Walla Walla’s mild climate makes it an ideal destination all year round. However, the peak tourism season is during the summer months (June-August), when visitors flock to the city for outdoor activities and wine tastings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Walla Walla

Do:

  • Do try the local wine: Walla Walla is known for its exceptional wines, so don’t miss out on trying some of the finest wines produced in the region.

  • Do make reservations: Many of the popular wineries and restaurants require reservations, so plan ahead and make your bookings in advance.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Walla Walla takes pride in its clean streets and beautiful landscapes, so do your part and keep the city clean by disposing of your trash in designated bins.

  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping is customary in Walla Walla, so be sure to tip your servers, drivers, and guides accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Walla Walla

Walla Walla is a safe city, and visitors should not face any significant safety issues as long as they follow common-sense safety practices. However, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as locking your car and valuables, and avoiding unlit and isolated areas at night.

Health in Walla Walla

Walla Walla has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, to provide medical services to residents and visitors.

Getting to Walla Walla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Walla Walla is well-connected to several major cities in the region, offering visitors multiple transportation options and logistics to reach the city.

Airports Near Walla Walla

  • Walla Walla Regional Airport: Located just a few miles outside of the city, this airport offers daily flights to and from several major cities in the region.

Bus Stations Near Walla Walla

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The Greyhound bus station is located in Downtown Walla Walla, offering bus services to several major cities and destinations.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car from several car rental agencies to explore the city and its surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Walla Walla

Walla Walla is a small city, and most of the popular attractions and locations can be accessed on foot, bicycle, or car. Several rental car agencies and taxi services operate within the city, and visitors can also use ride-hailing services like Uber and Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Walla Walla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Walla Walla is situated in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, with several other destinations and excursions nearby that visitors can explore. Some popular nearby destinations are Dayville, Hermiston, and Pendleton.

Conclusion

Walla Walla is an enchanting city that offers visitors a perfect blend of culture, history, outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s museums and historical sites, taste its award-winning wines, or immerse yourself in the natural landscapes, Walla Walla is the perfect destination for you. Plan your visit today and experience all that this charming city has to offer!
