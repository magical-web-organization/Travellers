Introduction to Moses Lake

Moses Lake is a picturesque city located in the heart of the Columbia River Plateau, in Washington (state), in Pacific Northwest, in United States of America, in North America.

With its perfect blend of natural beauty and urban charm, visitors can explore stunning views of the Cascade Mountains, hike and bike along scenic trails, shop for local treasures and dine on delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Moses Lake

If you are planning a visit to Moses Lake, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the most popular places you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Moses Lake Museum & Art Center
  • Larson Gallery
  • Grant County Historical Society Museum
  • Masquers Theatre of Soap Lake

Natural Attractions of Moses Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Potholes State Park
  • Moses Lake Sand Dunes
  • Surf and Slide Water Park
  • Moses Lake Farmers Market

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moses Lake

Moses Lake provides an excellent backdrop for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the area:

  • Moses Lake Sand Dunes Trail
  • Escure Ranch Trail Head
  • Sagebrush Flat Wetland Trail
  • McConihe Flats Summit Trail

Best Things to Do in Moses Lake: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities to do in Moses Lake. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Kayak or paddleboard on Moses Lake
  • Go fishing for bass, trout, and walleye
  • Golf at the Moses Lake Golf Club
  • Visit the Moses Lake Aquatic Center

Week-Long Itinerary for Moses Lake

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to the Moses Lake Museum & Art Center, and end the day at the Surf and Slide Water Park.

Day 2: Rent a paddleboard or kayak and spend the day on the lake.

Day 3: Explore Potholes State Park and hike the Sagebrush Flats Wetland Trail.

Day 4: Take a scenic drive to the Westons Antique Apples Farm and buy some local produce.

Day 5: Learn about local history at the Grant County Historical Society Museum and Masquers Theatre of Soap Lake.

Day 6: Tee off at the Moses Lake Golf Club.

Day 7: Relax at Moses Lake Aquatic Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Moses Lake

When visiting Moses Lake, it is important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some important things to know:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Be respectful when shopping at the farmers market and haggling with vendors.
  • Learn a few words in the local language to show respect and appreciation.

Best Time to Visit Moses Lake

The best time to visit Moses Lake is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, fall is also an excellent time to visit to view the colorful foliage and take advantage of the lower accommodation prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moses Lake

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the city’s outdoor attractions, such as Potholes State Park.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect religious or cultural sites.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are important considerations when visiting Moses Lake. Here is some information to help you stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Moses Lake

  • Crime levels in Moses Lake are generally low, but it’s wise to take precautions such as locking your car and not leaving valuables visible.
  • When hiking or biking, be aware of your surroundings and carry a map.

Health in Moses Lake

  • Moses Lake has a hospital and several clinics in case of medical emergencies.
  • It is important to stay hydrated outdoor activities.

Getting to Moses Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moses Lake is accessible by car, plane, bus, and train. Here are some options for travelers:

Airports Near Moses Lake

  • Grant County International Airport (MWH) - located four miles outside of Moses Lake.

Bus Stations Near Moses Lake

  • Greyhound Bus Station - located in Moses Lake.

Other Options

  • Train: Amtrak has a station in Wenatchee, which is a two-hour drive from Moses Lake.

How to Get Around in Moses Lake

The best way to get around Moses Lake is by car. Many of the city’s attractions are within a few miles of each other. Taxis and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Moses Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in your itinerary, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The city of Spokane, located about 90 miles north of Moses Lake, offers world-class museums, art galleries, and nature parks.
  • Lake Chelan, located about 60 miles east of Moses Lake, is a scenic destination for hiking, boating and wine tasting.

Conclusion

Moses Lake is a charming city with something for everyone. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, delicious cuisine, and unique shopping experiences, there is no shortage of activities to enjoy. Whether you’re a solo traveler or visiting with family and friends, Moses Lake is a must-visit destination in Washington (state).
