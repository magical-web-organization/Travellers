Introduction to Davenport (Washington)

Davenport is a charming small town located in Columbia River Plateau, in Washington (state), in Pacific Northwest, in United States of America, in North America. This quiet and laid-back town may not be as popular as other cities in the state, but it has a lot of attractions to offer. Tourists looking for a relaxing and stress-free vacation will find that Davenport is a perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Davenport (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best things to do in Davenport is to visit their museums and historical sites. Here are some of the best places worth checking out:

  • Lincoln County Historical Museum - This museum showcases the history of the region through exhibits and artifacts.
  • McGregor Museum - It’s a 1910s house converted into a museum that explores the town’s history, from its Native American roots to modern-day life.
  • Fort Spokane - This former army fort turned into a national historic site features fascinating exhibits on the life of soldiers in the 19th century and a beautiful view of the Spokane River.

Natural Attractions of Davenport (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty of things to do in Davenport as well. Here are some of the best natural tourist attractions in the area:

  • Porcupine Bay Campground - Located along the Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area, Porcupine Bay is a great spot for picnicking and camping. Guests can swim, fish, boat and hike through the forests.
  • Hawk Creek Falls – A beautiful waterfall located within the Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area.
  • Lake Lenore Caves - This unique natural attraction is a must-visit. It features lava tubes that have been carved out by ancient floods and volcanic activity. Visitors can tour the tubes and see ancient pictographs inside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Davenport (Washington)

Davenport and the surrounding areas have plenty of hiking and biking trails that let visitors explore the beauty of nature. Well-known trails for mountain biking include:

  • The Fort Spokane Trail - This 15-mile biking trail is located in the Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area. It’s a challenging trial, but features beautiful views of the Spokane River from above.
  • Lincoln Loop Trail - It is a 13-mile long, moderate level biking trail that offers scenic views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Davenport (Washington): Activities and Tours

Aside from natural and cultural attractions, there are plenty of things to do in Davenport and the surrounding areas. Here are some of the best tours and activities in the area:

  • Antiquing - There are many antique stores that can be visited in Davenport and the surrounding county. Visitors may find one-of-a-kind collectibles and vintage items.
  • Winery Tours - Visit a local vineyard and enjoy locally-produced wines.
  • Fishing Tours - It’s a great way to enjoy the Columbia Basin Waters while catching a variety of fish species.

Week-Long Itinerary for Davenport (Washington)

To help tourists organize their travel plans, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Davenport:

Day 1: Visit the Lincoln County Historical Museum.

Day 2: Take a hike through the Lincoln Loop trail in the morning. In the afternoon, head to the McGregor Museum.

Day 3: Visit the Fort Spokane historic site. In the evening, take a winery tour.

Day 4: Take a dip in the Lake Roosevelt area at Porcupine Bay Campground. In the afternoon, visit Lake Lenore Caves.

Day 5: Go fishing in the surrounding area.

Day 6: Go biking through the Fort Spokane Trail.

Day 7: Spend the day antiquing in Davenport.

Food and Drink Scene of Davenport (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

Davenport may be a small town, but it’s home to some great local dishes that are worth trying, including:

  • Beef brisket - A popular local dish that is slow-cooked and seasoned to perfection.
  • Huckleberry pies - Huckleberries are native to the area and make for a delicious pie.
  • Potato salad- A classic side dish that can be found in most local dinners in town.

Local Beverages

Davenport has a growing beer and wine scene. Some of the best local beverages to try include:

  • Davenport Cellars- A family-owned winery that produces a variety of wines such as chardonnay and cabernet sauvignon.
  • Odessa Brewing Company- Regional craft brewery with a beer garden. It’s a great spot to enjoy the patio seating and try different beers.

Where to Stay in Davenport (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of places to stay in Davenport, ranging from budget to luxury accommodations. The list below contains some of the best options in town:

  • Davenport Motel - An affordable option that offers comfortable and clean rooms.
  • Black Bear Motel - Located in Davenport city center, provides guests with free Wi-Fi, parking, and air conditioning.
  • Lake Roosevelt Bed and Breakfast - A charming B&B with a beautiful lakeside view that offers a quiet and serene stay.

What to Buy in Davenport (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists looking for unique gifts and souvenirs can check out these great local stores:

  • Beans 1955 - It’s a favorite stop for locals and tourists, known for coffee drinks, ice cream and candy.
  • Meadow Boutique - Offers a variety of trendy and fashionable clothing for women.
  • Lincoln Hardware and Mercantile - Visitors can buy souvenirs, local products, and daily necessities.

Local Culture and Customs of Davenport (Washington)

Davenport is a small town with a warm community, where locals are friendly and helpful. Tourists are expected to follow the same common decency and respect for the people and culture of the town.

Best Time to Visit Davenport (Washington)

The best time to visit Davenport is during the summer months between June to August when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities. The fall season is also a great time to visit if tourists want to see the changing colors of the leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Davenport (Washington)

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine like beef brisket and huckleberry pie.
  • Do visit local museums and historic sites.
  • Do enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public areas.
  • Don’t take any souvenirs from natural or historical sites.
  • Don’t take photos of locals without asking their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Davenport (Washington)

Davenport is a small town with a low crime rate, but tourists must still take necessary safety precautions and always be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Davenport (Washington)

Davenport has a hospital and medical center. But visitors should still bring their necessary medication and be careful about any allergies, injuries, or illness.

Getting to Davenport (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Davenport (Washington)

  • Spokane International Airport - located 57 miles northeast of Davenport.

Bus Stations Near Davenport (Washington)

  • Omni Bus Terminal - located just down Main Street in Spokane.

Other Options

There are plenty of rental car options from the Spokane airport.

How to Get Around in Davenport (Washington)

The best way to get around Davenport is by car. Visitors should rent a car at the Spokane airport to get around with ease.

Where to Go Next from Davenport (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that tourists may be interested in:

  • Spokane - Just a short drive from Davenport, Spokane is the second-largest city in Washington.
  • Lake Chelan - A beautiful lake surrounded by large vineyards with wine-tasting tours offered.

Conclusion

Davenport is a fantastic destination for tourists looking for a peaceful getaway with beautiful natural and cultural attractions, friendly locals, and a slow pace of life. A week-long visit is recommended to fully enjoy what this lovely town has to offer.
