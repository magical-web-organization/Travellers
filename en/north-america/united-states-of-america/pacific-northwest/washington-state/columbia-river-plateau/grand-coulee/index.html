Introduction to Grand Coulee

Grand Coulee is a scenic destination located in the Columbia River Plateau of Washington state. This small town is home to some of the most impressive man-made and natural attractions in the Pacific Northwest. Grand Coulee is best known for the Grand Coulee Dam, which is the largest electric power producing facility in the United States. But the town also offers a lot of outdoor adventures, scenic views, cultural attractions, and fantastic food and drink experiences. Whether you’re looking for history or outdoor activities, Grand Coulee has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Grand Coulee

Here is a list of must-visit places in Grand Coulee.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Grand Coulee Dam Visitor Center: This museum showcases the history and technology of the Grand Coulee Dam, and the role it played in shaping the Pacific Northwest. There are interactive exhibits, videos, and tours of the dam available.
  • Fort Spokane: A former United States Army installation, Fort Spokane is now a historic site that tells the story of the interactions between Native Americans and Europeans in the Pacific Northwest.
  • Colville Confederated Tribes Museum: This museum displays the history and culture of the Confederated Tribes of the Colville Reservation, which includes 12 Native American tribes.

Natural Attractions of Grand Coulee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area: This beautiful lake provides opportunities for boating, fishing, camping, and swimming. It is also home to hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife viewing.
  • Steamboat Rock State Park: This park is named after a massive basalt rock formation, which is easily visible from miles away. The park offers hiking, boating, fishing, and camping opportunities.
  • Dry Falls State Park: This park features the remnants of a massive waterfall, which was once the largest waterfall in the world. There are hiking trails and scenic viewpoints that provide stunning views of the falls and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand Coulee

Grand Coulee has a variety of hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Banks Lake Trail: This scenic trail follows the shoreline of Banks Lake, and offers views of the surrounding hills and wildlife.
  • Spring Canyon Trail: This trail in Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area offers views of the Grand Coulee Dam and the Columbia River. It also passes through tunnels and over bridges, providing a unique hiking experience.
  • Coulee Playland Trail: This paved trail is perfect for biking or walking, and it provides views of the surrounding lakes and hills.

Best Things to Do in Grand Coulee: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Grand Coulee. Some of the best options include:

  • Grand Coulee Dam Tour: This tour takes visitors inside the Grand Coulee Dam to see the power generation facilities and learn about the history and technology of the dam.
  • Jet Boat Tours: These tours take visitors on a thrilling ride through the Grand Coulee Dam area and Lake Roosevelt, providing views of the surrounding landscape and wildlife.
  • Wine Tasting: The Grand Coulee area is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours, allowing visitors to experience the region’s unique wine culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grand Coulee

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for exploring Grand Coulee:

  • Day 1: Visit the Grand Coulee Dam Visitor Center and take a tour of the dam.
  • Day 2: Explore Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area and go hiking or swimming.
  • Day 3: Visit the Colville Confederated Tribes Museum and learn about the Native American culture and history of the region.
  • Day 4: Take a jet boat tour of the Grand Coulee Dam area and Lake Roosevelt.
  • Day 5: Visit Fort Spokane and learn about the interactions between Native Americans and Europeans in the Pacific Northwest.
  • Day 6: Go wine tasting at one of the local wineries and try some of the region’s unique wines.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenery at Steamboat Rock State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Grand Coulee

Grand Coulee has a variety of food and drink options for visitors to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry bread: A traditional Native American dish, fry bread is a deep-fried dough that can be served with a variety of toppings, such as honey or powdered sugar.
  • Cougar Gold Cheese: This cheese is made at Washington State University’s creamery, and it is aged for at least one year. It is a popular souvenir for visitors to bring home.
  • Apple Pie: Washington state is known for its apple production, and Grand Coulee is no exception. Try a slice of freshly baked apple pie at one of the local cafes.

Local Beverages

  • Columbia River microbrews: Grand Coulee has a variety of local microbreweries that produce unique and delicious beers. Try a pint of a local brew at one of the town’s many bars.
  • Washington State wines: The Grand Coulee area is home to several wineries, which offer wine tastings and tours. Try some of the region’s unique wines, such as Riesling or Merlot.

Where to Stay in Grand Coulee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grand Coulee has a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best options:

  • Lake Roosevelt Inn: This cozy inn is located near Lake Roosevelt and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the lake and hiking trails.
  • Banks Lake Lodge: This lodge is located near Banks Lake and offers spacious rooms and views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Coulee House Inn and Suites: This hotel is located in the heart of Grand Coulee and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Grand Coulee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grand Coulee has a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. Here are some of the best options:

  • Cougar Gold Cheese: This iconic cheese is made at Washington State University’s creamery, and it is a popular souvenir for visitors to bring home.
  • Native American Art: The Colville Confederated Tribes Museum offers a variety of art and crafts made by local Native American artists.
  • Local Wines: The Grand Coulee area is known for its unique wines, which are a great souvenir for wine lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand Coulee

Grand Coulee is located in a region with a rich Native American history and culture. Visitors should respect the customs and traditions of the local tribes and be aware of the impact of European settlement on the area.

Best Time to Visit Grand Coulee

The best time to visit Grand Coulee is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are lower. However, the summer months offer more outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grand Coulee

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take a tour of the Grand Coulee Dam.
  • Go hiking or boating in Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or plants.
  • Leave trash or litter in the parks.
  • Ignore safety signs or warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grand Coulee

Grand Coulee is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as boating and hiking. Always follow safety guidelines and wear appropriate gear.

Health in Grand Coulee

There are no particular health risks associated with visiting Grand Coulee. Visitors should be prepared for the outdoors and bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and other essentials.

Getting to Grand Coulee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Grand Coulee is located in a remote area, and visitors should plan accordingly. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Grand Coulee

The nearest major airport to Grand Coulee is Spokane International Airport, which is approximately 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Grand Coulee

Greyhound bus lines serve several cities near Grand Coulee, including Spokane and Wenatchee.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest and most convenient way to get to Grand Coulee and to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Grand Coulee

Grand Coulee is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors may want to rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Grand Coulee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after visiting Grand Coulee:

  • Wenatchee National Forest: This national forest offers hiking, fishing, and camping opportunities, as well as scenic drives and wildlife viewing.
  • Mount Rainier National Park: This stunning park is located a few hours’ drive from Grand Coulee and offers hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Spokane: This city is located an hour and a half’s drive from Grand Coulee and offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining experiences.

Conclusion

Grand Coulee is a unique and beautiful destination in the Pacific Northwest that offers a combination of history, nature, and culture. Visitors can explore the impressive Grand Coulee Dam, hike or boat in Lake Roosevelt National Recreation Area, try local wines and cheese, and immerse themselves in the local Native American culture. Plan your trip to Grand Coulee and discover the charm and beauty of this small town.
