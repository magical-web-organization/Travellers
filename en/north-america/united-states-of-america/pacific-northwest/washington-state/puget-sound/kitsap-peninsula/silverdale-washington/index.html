Introduction to Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale is a charming town located in Kitsap Peninsula, in Puget Sound, Washington. It is a popular tourist destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the Pacific Northwest. The town has a rich history, and its vast array of cultural attractions makes it a perfect location for both history buffs and adventure enthusiasts alike. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see in Silverdale (Washington), including popular attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, food and drink options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Silverdale (Washington)

If you’re planning a trip to Silverdale, you won’t be disappointed! Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Silverdale:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Silverdale has a fascinating history that dates back to the early 19th century. You’ll find numerous museums and historical places that showcase the town’s heritage. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Kitsap County Historical Museum: Located in downtown Bremerton, this museum portrays the history of Kitsap County, which includes Silverdale. It features exhibits, artifacts, and photographs that reveal the region’s cultural and economic shifts over time.

  • Naval Undersea Museum: This naval museum in Keyport explores the fascinating world of undersea warfare. It showcases the Navy’s works and scientific research in fields like acoustics, oceanography, and marine biology. You can also discover the history of submarines and the role they played in World War II.

  • Suquamish Museum: Located in the nearby Suquamish reservation, the Suquamish Museum reveals the life and legacy of the Suquamish tribe. With interactive exhibits, art, and stories, you can learn about the tribe’s history, culture, and way of life.

Natural Attractions of Silverdale (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Silverdale is renowned for its outdoor beauty, and visitors can experience a range of outdoor activities. The town is known for its lush green forests, sparkling waters, and breathtaking mountain ranges. These are some of the natural attractions that await you:

  • Kitsap Memorial State Park: Located on Hood Canal, this state park is a nature lover’s paradise. It features breathtaking views of Olympic Mountain, saltwater beaches, hiking trails, and camping facilities. You’ll also find picnic tables, fishing docks, and water sports like boating and swimming.

  • Clear Creek Trail: This 4.4-mile trail meanders through Silverdale, offering a scenic adventure to walkers, bikers, and joggers. You’ll explore residential areas, farmlands, forests, and wetlands.

  • Newberry Hill Heritage Park: This park offers quiet and peaceful trails that are perfect for hikers and joggers alike. With over 1000 acres of forest, wetlands, and wildlife, this park offers a stunning panoramic view of Dyes Inlet.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with trails ranging from easy to challenging levels. You can explore nature, discover wildlife, and experience breathtaking scenery while you exercise. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Silverdale:

  • Green Mountain State Forest: This park offers numerous trails that range from easy to the most strenuous. It covers over 6,000 acres and offers views of the Bremerton waterfront, Puget Sound, and Seattle.

  • Gazzam Lake Nature Preserve: Gazzam Lake Nature Preserve is located on beautiful Bainbridge Island. It offers hikers and bikers a fragrant forest, marshy areas, and glimpses of wildlife.

  • Navy Yard Park: This is a 1.4-mile paved trail that features some of the town’s historic landmarks and stunning views of Sinclair Inlet and Bremerton.

Best Things to Do in Silverdale (Washington): Activities and Tours

Silverdale is an excellent place to explore and experience a range of activities and tours. From water sports to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone. Here are some must-do activities and tours in Silverdale:

  • Water Sports: Hood Canal provides ideal conditions for kayaking, swimming, fishing, and boating.

  • Shopping: Silverdale has a range of retail destinations, including the Kitsap Mall, the Silverdale Farmers Market, and various shops and boutiques.

  • Cultural Experiences: Discover the history and culture of Silverdale through visiting museums, art galleries, and cultural centers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Silverdale (Washington)

Here’s a week-long itinerary for visitors to Silverdale:

Day 1: Discover the town’s history with a visit to the Kitsap County Historical Museum

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Kitsap Memorial State Park

Day 3: Experience a fun day trip to Bainbridge Island and the Gazzam Lake Nature Preserve

Day 4: Go hiking in the Green Mountain State Forest

Day 5: Visit the Suquamish Museum to discover the life and legacy of the Suquamish tribe

Day 6: Enjoy some shopping at Kitsap Mall and other shopping destinations

Day 7: Take a sunset boat cruise of the Puget Sound.

Food and Drink Scene of Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale’s culinary scene is vibrant, with plenty of local and international cuisine to suit any taste. Here’s what you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish & Chips: Enjoy a classic dish made with fresh fish and locally sourced ingredients.

  • Oysters: Silverdale’s location offers a unique opportunity to enjoy freshly shucked oysters.

  • Salmon: Discover Pacific Northwest cuisine with a perfectly cooked salmon.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Seattle is famous for its coffee, and Silverdale is no exception. Enjoy a hot or cold brew from any of the town’s coffee shops.

  • Wine: The Pacific Northwest is famous for its wine, and Silverdale offers a variety of wineries and tasting rooms for wine enthusiasts.

  • Beer: If you love craft beer, you’ll find plenty of local breweries offering delicious beer.

Where to Stay in Silverdale (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silverdale offers many accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly options. Here are some top picks:

  • Oxford Suites Silverdale: This hotel offers modern amenities and elegant decor, including an indoor pool, hot tub, and fitness center.

  • Best Western Plus Silverdale Beach Hotel: This is a waterfront hotel with a stunning view of the Dyes Inlet and Mount Rainier. It also features an outdoor pool, hot tub, and free breakfast.

  • Midway Inn: This is a budget-friendly motel that offers clean, comfortable, and affordable accommodations. It is located close to the shops and restaurants in the heart of the town.

What to Buy in Silverdale (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silverdale is famous for its local products, including jewelry, art, and other souvenirs. Here are some things to buy in Silverdale:

  • Local arts and crafts: Silverdale has a range of art galleries and studios featuring local artists and their works.

  • Jewelry: Explore the many unique jewelry shops in Silverdale to find something special.

  • Seafood: Silverdale is famous for its fresh seafood, and visitors can take home some locally sourced fish and shellfish.

Local Culture and Customs of Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale values a friendly and welcoming environment, and visitors will appreciate the small-town hospitality during their stay. The town celebrates a variety of events and festivals throughout the year, showcasing the town’s rich heritage, arts, and culture.

Best Time to Visit Silverdale (Washington)

The best time to visit Silverdale is from June through September when the weather is sunny and warm, and town events are abundant. Visitors can also choose to visit during the spring to witness the blooming of cherry blossoms and the rhododendrons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale is generally safe, and visitors should stick to the usual safety measures when traveling. It is always best to keep an eye on your belongings, lock your car, and avoid going out alone at night.

Health in Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale is well-equipped with hospitals, clinics, and medical facilities to address any health issues that visitors may encounter.

Getting to Silverdale (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Silverdale is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Silverdale (Washington)

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA): This airport is located about two hours from Silverdale and is well-connected to major cities throughout the world.

  • Bremerton National Airport (PWT): This is a regional airport that offers frequent flights to Seattle.

Bus Stations Near Silverdale (Washington)

  • Kitsap Transit: Kitsap Transit is the public transportation system in Kitsap County and offers regular bus services to and from Silverdale.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at either Seattle’s airport or in downtown Bremerton.

  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are all available in Silverdale and are a convenient way to get around.

How to Get Around in Silverdale (Washington)

Silverdale is a small town that is easy to navigate. Visitors can use their cars, rental cars, public transportation, or ride-sharing services to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Silverdale (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Silverdale is located in a picturesque part of Washington that offers several destinations within a short driving distance. Here are some nearby destinations to explore:
