Introduction to Lakebay

Nestled on the Kitsap Peninsula in Puget Sound, Washington, Lakebay is a charming seaside village that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its natural beauty, friendly locals, and plenty of outdoor activities, Lakebay is a great vacation destination for families and solo travelers alike. In this guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Lakebay, along with insider tips on where to stay, what to eat, and how to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Lakebay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Penrose State Park: This 165-acre park offers hiking trails, saltwater beach access, and stunning views of the Puget Sound.
  • Glen Cove Cemetery: A historic cemetery that dates back to the 1800s, Glen Cove Cemetery is the final resting place of many of Lakebay’s early settlers.
  • Key Peninsula Historical Society Museum: Explore the rich history of the area at this small museum, which features exhibits and artifacts from the region’s early days.
  • Joemma Beach State Park: Located on the eastern shore of Key Peninsula, this park offers camping facilities, picnic areas, and beautiful views of the water.

Natural Attractions of Lakebay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cutts Island State Park: This small island is just a short boat ride from Lakebay and offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and a stunning view of Mount Rainier.
  • Island View Market: Located on the shores of Case Inlet, this market features a small beach and a pier, making it a popular spot for kayaking and fishing.
  • Peninsula Gardens: This award-winning garden features a wide variety of plants and flowers, along with a stunning view of the Puget Sound.
  • Lakebay Marina: A popular spot for boating and fishing, Lakebay Marina also features a small store and a restaurant.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakebay

  • Joemma Beach to Tower Trail: This 5-mile trail takes hikers through beautiful forested areas and offers stunning views of the Puget Sound and Mount Rainier.
  • Penrose Point Loop Trail: This 2.4-mile loop trail takes hikers through dense forest and along the beach, offering beautiful views of the sound and plenty of wildlife spotting opportunities.
  • Cutts Island Trail: This 3.8-mile out-and-back trail takes hikers around Cutts Island and offers stunning views of the sound and Mount Rainier.
  • Anderson Point County Park Trail: This 1.6-mile loop trail takes hikers through forests and offers beautiful views of the sound and the Olympic Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Lakebay: Activities and Tours

  • Boating and Fishing: Lakebay Marina offers boat rentals and fishing charters for those looking to explore the waterways around Lakebay.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Key Pen Parks offers kayak and paddleboard rentals, along with guided tours of the area’s waterways.
  • Wine Tasting: The nearby Longbranch Improvement Club features a wine-tasting room that offers tastings of local wines and ciders.
  • Farm Tours: The Key Peninsula Farm Tour is a popular annual event that showcases the area’s many farms and agriculture.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakebay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pacific Northwest Seafood: Fresh seafood is abundant in Lakebay, with many restaurants offering locally sourced oysters, clams, crab, and salmon.
  • Fish and Chips: A staple of seaside towns, Lakebay’s fried fish and chips are some of the best in the region.
  • Huckleberry Pie: Huckleberries are a local specialty and can be found in many of Lakebay’s bakeries and cafes.
  • Craft Beer: The Pacific Northwest is known for its craft beer scene, and Lakebay is no exception, with several local breweries offering flights of their latest creations.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: The Pacific Northwest is famous for its coffee shops, and Lakebay is no exception, with several cafes offering artisanal coffee and pastries.
  • Wine and Cider: Lakebay is home to several wineries and cideries that offer tastings of their locally made beverages.
  • Craft Beer: As mentioned above, Lakebay is home to several local breweries that offer a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Lakebay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Glen Cove Inn: This cozy inn offers comfortable rooms and easy access to Glen Cove Cemetery and other local attractions.
  • Cutts Island Cottage: This charming cottage is located on Cutts Island and can only be accessed by boat, offering a unique and secluded getaway experience.
  • Lakebay Beachcomber B&B: This beachfront bed and breakfast features stunning views of the sound and comfortable rooms.
  • Penrose Point Cabins: Located in Penrose State Park, these cabins offer a rustic camping experience with all the modern amenities.

What to Buy in Lakebay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam: Huckleberries are a local specialty and can be found in many of Lakebay’s stores, along with huckleberry jam.
  • Local Art and Crafts: Several local galleries and shops feature the work of Pacific Northwest artists and crafters.
  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Lakebay’s beaches are a popular spot for sea glass hunting, and local artisans have turned this sea glass into beautiful jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakebay

Lakebay is a small, close-knit community that prides itself on its maritime heritage. The area’s many beaches and waterways offer plenty of opportunities for boating and fishing, while local farms and wineries provide a taste of the region’s agricultural heritage. Visitors to Lakebay should be prepared for a laid-back, friendly atmosphere and plenty of opportunities to connect with locals.

Best Time to Visit Lakebay

Lakebay is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique set of attractions. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with plenty of outdoor activities and festivals on offer. Fall is a great time to visit for those looking for quieter beaches and fall foliage, while winter offers opportunities for cozy nights by the fire and winter sports. Spring is a great time to visit for those looking to see the area’s famous wildflowers in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakebay

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the area’s outdoor activities and natural beauty.
  • Do try the locally sourced seafood and other Pacific Northwest specialties.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the area’s natural resources.
  • Don’t take more than your fair share of resources such as seafood or firewood.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakebay

Lakebay is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should exercise caution when boating, fishing, or participating in other outdoor activities, as the waterways can be unpredictable. Visitors should also be aware of the area’s wildlife, including bears, cougars, and other potentially dangerous animals.

Health in Lakebay

Visitors to Lakebay should be aware of the area’s high pollen count during the spring and summer months. Those with allergies should bring appropriate medication. Visitors should also be aware of the area’s potentially hazardous flora and fauna, including poison oak and rattlesnakes.

Getting to Lakebay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakebay

The nearest airport to Lakebay is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away. Visitors can rent a car or take public transportation to get to Lakebay.

Bus Stations Near Lakebay

The nearest bus station is in Tacoma, approximately 40 miles away. Visitors can take a bus or rent a car to get to Lakebay.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from Seattle or Tacoma to the nearby town of Port Orchard, then drive to Lakebay.

How to Get Around in Lakebay

Visitors to Lakebay will need a car or bicycle to get around, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can also rent a boat or kayak to explore the area’s waterways.

Where to Go Next from Lakebay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Orchard: This nearby town offers quaint shops and restaurants, along with a maritime museum.
  • Gig Harbor: Just a short drive from Lakebay, Gig Harbor features a charming downtown area and several recreation options.
  • Bremerton: Home to the USS Turner Joy Museum Ship and the Puget Sound Navy Museum, Bremerton is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Olympic National Park: Just a few hours’ drive from Lakebay, Olympic National Park offers stunning natural beauty and plenty of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

From its stunning beaches to its charming downtown area, Lakebay is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or a quiet escape from the city, Lakebay offers something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your perfect trip to this beautiful seaside village.
