Introduction to Keyport (Washington)

Located in the Kitsap Peninsula in the Puget Sound region of Washington state, Keyport is a charming coastal town that offers a mix of natural beauty and exciting adventures. Whether you’re looking for outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing coastal getaway, Keyport has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Keyport (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • U.S. Naval Undersea Museum: This museum offers a unique look into the history of undersea exploration and naval technology. Visitors can learn about everything from deep-sea diving to underwater vehicles and submarines.
  • Keyport Historical Society and Museum: Located in the historic Keyport Exchange Building, this museum offers a glimpse into Keyport’s rich maritime history, including exhibits on boat-building and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Keyport (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Keyport Marina: This marina offers stunning views of the water and a chance to go boating or fishing.
  • Keyport Wildlife Area: This protected area is home to a variety of wildlife, from bald eagles to beavers.
  • Poulsbo Waterfront Walkway: A 1.5-mile scenic walkway along Liberty Bay, with views of the Olympic Mountains and the charming town of Poulsbo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keyport (Washington)

  • Heritage Park: This park offers hiking trails that wind through 26 acres of forest and wetlands, with views of Liberty Bay.
  • Clear Creek Trail: A 6.5-mile paved trail perfect for biking or hiking, featuring scenic views of Clear Creek and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Keyport (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the waters around Keyport.
  • Washington State Ferries: Take a ferry ride from nearby Bremerton or Bainbridge Island to explore the beautiful San Juan Islands.
  • Whale Watching: Take a guided tour from Port Townsend to see magnificent whales in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keyport (Washington)

Day 1 Morning: Explore the Keyport Marina. Afternoon: Visit the U.S. Naval Undersea Museum.
Day 2 Hike in Heritage Park. Afternoon: Explore the historic town of Poulsbo.
Day 3 Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the waters around Keyport.
Day 4 Visit the Keyport Historical Society and Museum.
Day 5 Take a ferry ride to one of the nearby San Juan Islands.
Day 6 Explore Sequim’s lavender farms and scenic views.
Day 7 Whale watching tour from Port Townsend.

Food and Drink Scene of Keyport (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: Keyport is famous for its delicious clam chowder, made with local ingredients.
  • Fresh Seafood: You can’t visit a coastal town without trying fresh seafood – Keyport has several restaurants that serve delicious seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Local breweries and distilleries: The Pacific Northwest is known for its craft breweries and distilleries. Try a local beer or spirit while in town.

Where to Stay in Keyport (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Anchor House Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a historic home dating back to 1897 and is within walking distance to the marina and several restaurants.
  • Eagle Tree RV Park: This RV park offers stunning views of Liberty Bay and is a short drive to Keyport’s attractions.

What to Buy in Keyport (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maritime-themed souvenirs: Keyport’s rich maritime history is reflected in the town’s many shops, where you’ll find everything from nautical decor to boat models.
  • Local artisan crafts: Support local artisans by purchasing unique handmade crafts and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Keyport (Washington)

  • Respect for nature: Keyport’s residents value the natural beauty of the area and strive to protect it, so visitors are asked to do the same.
  • Military presence: Keyport is home to a U.S. Navy base, so visitors may encounter military personnel and should be respectful of their service.

Best Time to Visit Keyport (Washington)

The best time to visit Keyport is during the late spring to early fall when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keyport (Washington)

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Keyport’s natural beauty is one of its main attractions, so make sure to spend time exploring the parks and waterfront.
  • Try local cuisine: Keyport’s seafood is renowned for its flavor and quality, so be sure to indulge in some delicious local dishes.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Be sure to dispose of trash properly and respect the natural environment.
  • Trespass on private property: Some waterfront areas are private property, so be sure to respect the property owners’ rights.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keyport (Washington)

Keyport is a safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take normal precautions, such as locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Keyport (Washington)

Keyport has a few medical clinics nearby, but for serious medical needs, visitors are advised to seek medical attention in nearby Bremerton or Silverdale.

Getting to Keyport (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keyport (Washington)

The closest major airport is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is around an hour’s drive from Keyport.

Bus Stations Near Keyport (Washington)

Greyhound offers bus service to nearby Bremerton.

Other Options

There are several car rental agencies near the Seattle-Tacoma airport, making renting a car an easy option for getting to Keyport. There is also a ferry service from Seattle to Bainbridge Island, which is a short drive to Keyport.

How to Get Around in Keyport (Washington)

The best way to get around Keyport is by car. There is no public transportation within the town, but the downtown area is walkable.

Where to Go Next from Keyport (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bainbridge Island: An idyllic island town known for its charming shops, galleries, and parks.
  • Port Townsend: A Victorian-era town with stunning architecture and a thriving arts scene.
  • Olympic National Park: A nature-lover’s paradise with stunning views, hiking trails, and more.

Conclusion

Keyport is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, with its mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing coastal vacation or an active getaway, Keyport has something for everyone.
