Introduction to Olalla (Washington)

Nestled in the Kitsap Peninsula, in Puget Sound on the coast of Washington State, Olalla is a charming community surrounded by lush forests and spectacular waterways. Despite being just a short drive from Seattle, Olalla feels worlds away from the hustle and bustle of the city. This small town is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered.

List of Places to Visit in Olalla (Washington)

If you’re planning a trip to Olalla, make sure to check out these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Olalla has a rich history and culture, and visitors can learn about it by visiting these local museums:

  • Olalla Valley Vineyard and Winery – Take a tour of this family-owned winery and vineyard, and try their award-winning wines.
  • Puget Sound Navy Museum – This museum tells the story of the US Navy’s role in Pacific Northwest history.
  • Olalla Pioneer Cemetery – Take a walk through this old, small cemetery, and explore the historical tombs and memorials.

Natural Attractions of Olalla (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Olalla is surrounded by an abundance of natural beauty. Check out these must-see outdoor spots:

  • Port of Manchester – The port has a public park that offers fantastic views of Puget Sound, a perfect spot for fishing, or just soaking up the sun.
  • Banner Forest Heritage Park – This is a fantastic place to go hiking, running, or mountain biking with over 30 miles of trails in second-growth forest with views of Mount Rainier.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Olalla (Washington)

For outdoor enthusiasts, some of the best hiking and biking trails in the state are located in Olalla. Here are a few trails worth visiting:

  • Lena Lake Trail – This charming trail in the Olympic Mountains is a moderate hike that offers stunning views of Lena Lake and the surrounding area.
  • Northington Loop Trail – This four-mile trail offers easy hiking with lovely views of the surrounding forest.
  • Wildwood Park Trail – A shorter, easier hike along the creek with numerous trails and picnic spots.

Best Things to Do in Olalla (Washington): Activities and Tours

If outdoor activities aren’t your thing, don’t worry. Olalla offers a range of fun activities, from cultural experiences to wine tastings and much more!

Here are some of the must-see attractions in Olalla:

  • Olalla Valley Vineyard and Winery Tour – Take a guided tour of the winery and learn about winemaking, and enjoy tastings.
  • Olalla Bluegrass and Beyond Festival – A music festival that celebrates Bluegrass and other musical genres, with a 3-day event every June.
  • Fishing and Boating Excursions – You can spend a day out on the water fishing for salmon or explore the coast by boat to see the scenic views along the South Kitsap Peninsula.

Week-Long Itinerary for Olalla (Washington)

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for those planning to stay in Olalla:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your favorite hotel. Day 2: Visit Port of Manchester and take a walk to the park to watch the sunset. Day 3: Visit the Puget Sound Navy Museum and gain a historical insight to the Pacific Northwest. Day 4: Hike Lena Lake Trail and revel in the scenic beauty. Day 5: Go wine tasting at Olalla Valley Vineyard and winery, followed by a picnic at a nearby park. Day 6: Take a stroll through Banner Forest Heritage Park and enjoy the amazing views of Mt. Rainier. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Olalla (Washington)

When it comes to dining in Olalla, you will not be disappointed. From locally sourced oysters to fresh organic vegetables, there is something for everyone here.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are a few local dishes you must-try during your visit:

  • Oysters – Impressively fresh and flavorful oysters are a must-try when you are in town. Live and local, crack them open and enjoy the fresh taste of the ocean.
  • Burgers – Local beef burgers are famous, thanks to the fresh meat and delicious seasoning.
  • Seasonal Salads – In Olalla, chefs keep to the seasons, creating dishes that utilize the best produce possible.

Local Beverages

Olalla has plenty of breweries and wineries that make delightful wines and beers. Some of the must-visit spots in Olalla are:

  • Olalla Valley Vineyard and Winery – A family-owned vineyard and winery that offers fantastic wine tours.
  • Ghostfish Brewing Company – A craft beer brewery that offers gluten-free beer with a variety of flavors and styles.

Where to Stay in Olalla (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Olalla has plenty of choices. Here are a few options:

  • Best Western Plus – Located near the Port of Manchester. Offers rooms with ocean views, complimentary breakfast, and free Wi-Fi.
  • The Cottage Bed and Breakfast – A cozy bed and breakfast with scenic views of the Pacific Northwest and beautiful gardens to explore.

What to Buy in Olalla (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olalla has a range of shops that sell local souvenirs that make great gifts. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Olalla Vineyard and Winery – A variety of locally made wines and vineyard artifacts.
  • Ghostfish Brewing Company – T-shirts and growlers with the brewery’s logo.
  • Olalla Valley Vintage Marketplace – Vintage clothing and local artisanal goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Olalla (Washington)

Olalla is a tiny community in the Pacific Northwest with a rich history. One of the most famous customs here is the Olalla Bluegrass and Beyond Festival. This three-day event has been held annually since 1991 and features regional and national Bluegrass bands.

Best Time to Visit Olalla (Washington)

Summer is the best time to visit Olalla because of the pleasant weather and outdoor events like the Bluegrass and Beyond Festival. Winter in Olalla can be quite cold and rainy, so it is still good to pack some warm clothes even during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Olalla (Washington)

Do:

  • Do try local food and drinks.
  • Do keep Olalla clean and litter-free.
  • Do be respectful to the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t litter everywhere.
  • Don’t ignore customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Olalla (Washington)

Olalla is a safe place to visit, but as with any place, visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking their cars when leaving them parked on the street. Always be aware of your surroundings - especially if you are hiking.

Health in Olalla (Washington)

Olalla has one medical clinic that serves the community’s healthcare needs. It is important to have travel insurance when visiting Olalla, in case of any illness or accidents.

Getting to Olalla (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Olalla through these transport options:

Airports Near Olalla (Washington)

The nearest airports are Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA) and Boeing Field/King County International Airport (BFI).

Bus Stations Near Olalla (Washington)

Local buses run between Olalla and other towns in the Kitsap Peninsula. Still, visitors can also use Greyhound buses that pass through Port Orchard, a nearby city.

Other Options

Hiring a car is the best way to go around in Olalla, as it offers flexibility and the ability to go off-the-beaten-path.

How to Get Around in Olalla (Washington)

While visitors can explore Olalla by car or bicycle, walking is also an option for those who prefer to move slowly and take in the surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Olalla (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other parts of the Kitsap Peninsula, including the town of Port Orchard, Poulsbo, and Bremerton. They have well-known museums, parks, and scenic vistas. Visitors can also explore Seattle to the east, Victoria to the west, or Olympic National Park to the northwest.

Conclusion

Olalla may be a small community, but it offers a wide range of activities, attractions, and experiences for those willing to explore. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, wineries, breweries, and fantastic dining options - the possibilities are endless. Don’t miss out on the chance to discover this hidden gem on your next trip!
