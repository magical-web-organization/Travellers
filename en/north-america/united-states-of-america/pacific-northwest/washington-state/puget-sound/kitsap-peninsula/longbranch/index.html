Introduction to Longbranch

Longbranch is a small community located in Kitsap Peninsula, in Puget Sound, Washington. Though it may not be as well-known as other tourist destinations in the state, Longbranch has plenty to offer visitors seeking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in the beauty of the Pacific Northwest. With stunning natural vistas, culturally significant museums, and a thriving food and drink scene, Longbranch is the perfect destination for those looking to experience the best that Washington has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Longbranch

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Museum of the North Beach: This museum is dedicated to the history of the area, with exhibits ranging from Native American artifacts to maritime history. Don’t miss the incredible collection of vintage photographs and maps.

  2. The Longbranch Improvement Club Museum: Located in the historic Longbranch Schoolhouse, this museum tells the story of the community’s early settlers and features exhibits on logging, fishing, and farming in the area.

  3. The Key Center Library: Housed in a historic schoolhouse, the library offers a wide variety of books and resources on local history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Longbranch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Taylor Bay: This beautiful bay offers stunning views of the Olympic Mountains and is a great spot for kayaking, picnicking, and wildlife watching.

  2. Filucy Bay: A popular spot for birdwatching and crabbing, Filucy Bay offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

  3. Joemma Beach State Park: With over 3,000 feet of saltwater shoreline, Joemma Beach State Park is perfect for swimming, fishing, and beachcombing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longbranch

  1. Herron Island: Accessible only by ferry, Herron Island offers a variety of hiking trails that wind through old-growth forests and along the island’s picturesque beaches.

  2. Longbranch Loop Trail: This 2.6-mile trail takes hikers through dense forests and over rolling hills, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

  3. Joemma Beach State Park: In addition to its beach and water activities, Joemma Beach State Park has several miles of hiking trails that offer stunning views of the bay and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Longbranch: Activities and Tours

  1. Luxury Yacht Charters: Experience the beauty of Puget Sound on a private yacht tour. With charters that can accommodate up to twelve people, this is a great way to explore the area in style.

  2. Kayak Tours: Explore Longbranch’s stunning coastline and wildlife up-close on a guided kayak tour. Tours are offered year-round and are suitable for all skill levels.

  3. Oyster Tours: Learn about the history and culture of oysters in Puget Sound on a guided tour of a local oyster farm.

Week-Long Itinerary for Longbranch

Day 1: Visit the Museum of the North Beach and explore the surrounding area.

Day 2: Take a kayak tour of Taylor Bay and explore the many coves and inlets along the coast.

Day 3: Explore the Longbranch Loop Trail and visit the Longbranch Improvement Club Museum.

Day 4: Take a luxury yacht charter and explore the stunning coastline of the Puget Sound.

Day 5: Visit Joemma Beach State Park and hike the many trails in the area.

Day 6: Take an oyster tour and learn about the history and culture of oysters in Puget Sound.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of Longbranch.

Food and Drink Scene of Longbranch

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fresh Oysters: Sample some of the freshest oysters in the world, harvested from local farms in Puget Sound.

  2. Dungeness Crab: Another local delicacy, Dungeness crab is a must-try for seafood lovers.

  3. Washington Wines: With over 900 wineries in the state, Washington is quickly becoming known for its world-class wines.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Washington is home to over 400 breweries and is quickly becoming a hub for craft beer enthusiasts.

  2. Coffee: Seattle may be the epicenter of coffee culture, but Longbranch has plenty of local cafes serving up delicious coffee and pastries.

Where to Stay in Longbranch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Lakebay Marina: This family-friendly marina offers cabins and RV sites and is perfect for those looking to experience the beauty of Puget Sound firsthand.

  2. Longbranch Marina and Motel: Located on Filucy Bay, this motel offers stunning views of the water and easy access to nearby activities.

What to Buy in Longbranch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Soap: Made with local ingredients and scented with natural fragrances, handmade soap is a great way to bring a piece of Longbranch home with you.

  2. Local Honey: Support local beekeepers and enjoy the delicious taste of honey made from local wildflowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Longbranch

Longbranch has a rich cultural history, with a strong tradition of logging, fishing, and farming. Visitors can learn about the area’s history at the many museums and historical sites in the area, or experience it firsthand by participating in activities like oyster farming and crabbing.

Best Time to Visit Longbranch

The best time to visit Longbranch is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the days are long. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like kayaking and hiking, and can sample the many delicious seafood dishes that the area is known for.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longbranch

Do:

  1. Explore the stunning natural beauty of the area.

  2. Learn about the local history and culture at the area’s many museums and historical sites.

  3. Sample the many delicious seafood dishes that the area is known for.

Don’t:

  1. Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking.

  2. Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Longbranch

Longbranch is a relatively safe area, but visitors should exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking. Always follow safety guidelines and be prepared for changing weather conditions.

Health in Longbranch

Visitors to Longbranch should take precautions to protect themselves from the sun and other environmental hazards. Always wear sunscreen and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Longbranch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Longbranch can fly into Seattle-Tacoma International Airport and rent a car to reach the area. Alternatively, visitors can take a bus to nearby cities like Seattle and Olympia, and then rent a car or take a taxi to Longbranch.

Airports Near Longbranch

  1. Seattle-Tacoma International Airport

  2. Olympia Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Longbranch

  1. Seattle Greyhound Station

  2. Olympia Transit Center

Other Options

Visitors who prefer not to drive can take a taxi or hire a private car service to reach Longbranch from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Longbranch

Visitors to Longbranch will need to rent a car or rely on taxis to get around the area. Bicycles are also a popular way to explore the area, and many local shops offer rentals.

Where to Go Next from Longbranch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mount Rainier National Park: Just a few hours’ drive from Longbranch, Mount Rainier National Park offers stunning views, hiking trails, and wildlife watching opportunities.

  2. Seattle: The Emerald City is just a short drive from Longbranch and offers world-class museums, shopping, and dining.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and thriving food and drink scene, Longbranch is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the best that Washington has to offer. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Longbranch has something to offer everyone. Start planning your visit today and discover all that this hidden gem in the Kitsap Peninsula has to offer.
