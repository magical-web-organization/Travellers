Introduction to Dewatto

Tucked away in the Kitsap Peninsula, Dewatto is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and exciting outdoor activities, Dewatto has something for everyone.

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, this small town in Puget Sound, Washington, is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Dewatto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dewatto’s rich history is on display in many of its museums and historical sites, which offer a glimpse into the region’s past.

  • The Dewatto Museum: This small museum features artifacts from Dewatto’s logging and fishing industries, as well as displays on the town’s Native American history.

  • The Dewatto Cemetery: This historical cemetery is the final resting place for many of Dewatto’s earliest settlers.

Natural Attractions of Dewatto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dewatto is surrounded by some of the most beautiful natural scenery in the United States, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Dewatto Bay: The bay is a popular spot for water activities like kayaking and fishing, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

  • Green Mountain State Forest: The forest boasts miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as campsites for overnight stays.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dewatto

Dewatto is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with many scenic trails to explore. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Dewatto River Trail: This 10-mile trail follows the Dewatto River and is a great place to spot wildlife like bald eagles and deer.

  • Gold Creek Trail: This 7-mile trail offers stunning views of the Hood Canal and Olympic Mountains, and is a popular spot for mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Dewatto: Activities and Tours

Dewatto offers many exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Fishing: Dewatto Bay is a popular spot for salmon and trout fishing.

  • Kayaking: Explore the bay and nearby waterways by kayak, and take in the scenic beauty of the surrounding area.

Food and Drink Scene of Dewatto

Must-Try Dishes

Dewatto is known for its fresh seafood and locally sourced ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Grilled Pacific oysters
  • Cedar-planked salmon
  • Dungeness crab cakes

Local Beverages

Washington is known for its craft beer and wine, and Dewatto is no exception. Some of the local beverages to try include:

  • Hood Canal Brewery’s IPA
  • Stottle Winery’s Chardonnay

Where to Stay in Dewatto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dewatto has a variety of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from hotels and guesthouses to campsites and cabins.

  • The Dewatto Bed & Breakfast is a cozy option for those looking for a more traditional lodging experience.
  • The Green Mountain Cabin offers a rustic escape in the heart of the state forest.

What to Buy in Dewatto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dewatto offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products for visitors to take home as mementos. Some of the top options include:

  • Handmade wooden bowls and utensils
  • Local honey and jams
  • Artwork from local artists

Local Culture and Customs of Dewatto

Dewatto has a rich Native American history and culture, which is celebrated throughout the town. Visitors should be respectful of the culture and traditions of the area, and take the time to learn about its history and customs.

Best Time to Visit Dewatto

The best time to visit Dewatto is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the area is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy snowshoeing and skiing in the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dewatto

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the town’s history and culture.
  • Respect the natural beauty of the area and practice Leave No Trace principles.
  • Try the locally sourced cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or vegetation in the state forest.
  • Ignore posted signs and warnings on trails or other outdoor areas.
  • Take more than you need from the natural resources of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dewatto

Dewatto is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of any weather or trail conditions before starting an activity.

Health in Dewatto

There are no major health concerns in Dewatto, but visitors should be prepared for the outdoors by bringing appropriate clothing, sunscreen, and bug spray. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications and check with a doctor before embarking on any strenuous activities.

Getting to Dewatto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dewatto

The nearest airport to Dewatto is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Dewatto

There are no bus stations in Dewatto, but visitors can take a bus to nearby Belfair and rent a car or arrange for transportation from there.

Other Options

The most convenient way to get around Dewatto is by car, but visitors can also rent a bike or walk to nearby attractions.
