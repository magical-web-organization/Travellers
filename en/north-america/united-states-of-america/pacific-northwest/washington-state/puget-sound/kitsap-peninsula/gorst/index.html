Planning a trip to the Pacific Northwest? Be sure to add Gorst, Washington, to your itinerary. Located in the Kitsap Peninsula in Puget Sound, Gorst offers a delightful mix of cultural and natural attractions, making it a perfect place for exploring. Whether you’re an active traveler or just looking to relax and unwind, the town has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide to Gorst, we’ve curated the must-visit places, the best things to do, the where to stay, and where to dine, so you can make the most of your time in this charming town.

Introduction to Gorst

Gorst is a small unincorporated community in Kitsap County, Washington. It’s located on the east side of Sinclair Inlet, about 10 miles west of the city of Tacoma. The town is named after William H. Gorst, who came to the area in 1855. It was once a bustling center for sawmills and boat yards during the early years of the 20th century. Today, it is known for its scenic beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities.

List of Places to Visit in Gorst

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northwest Carriage Museum

The Northwest Carriage Museum is a must-visit attraction for those interested in horses, wagons, and carriages. The museum showcases the history of transportation from the 19th century to the present day. You can see the world’s largest collection of antique carriages and vehicles, including a horse-drawn hearse from the early 1900s.

Bremerton Historic Ships Association

The Bremerton Historic Ships Association is located in nearby Bremerton and is one of the best maritime museums in the region. The museum is home to several historic ships, including the USS Turner Joy, a destroyer that served in the Vietnam War.

Natural Attractions of Gorst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Port Orchard Waterfront Park

The Port Orchard Waterfront Park is located just south of Gorst and is a great spot for a picnic or a walk. The park features a beach, playground, and plenty of space for kite flying. You can also catch a ferry to nearby Bremerton from here.

Gold Mountain Golf Club

If you’re a golfer, be sure to check out the Gold Mountain Golf Club. It offers two stunning 18-hole championship courses, both set amongst the beautiful Pacific Northwest scenery. The Cascade course is known for its dramatic terrain and challenging layout, while the Olympic course offers stunning views of the Olympic Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gorst

Illahee State Park

The Illahee State Park is a perfect destination for those who want to enjoy nature and breathtaking views. It features over 5000 feet of shoreline, three miles of hiking trails, and plenty of picnic areas. You can also rent kayaks and paddleboards, or go fishing in the park’s freshwater creek.

Clear Creek Trail

The Clear Creek Trail is a 6.5-mile trail that runs through Silverdale to Poulsbo. It’s an excellent destination for hikers, bikers, or joggers. The trail offers breathtaking views of the Clear Creek Valley and lush forests. Several trails branch off from the main trail, including one that leads to the Kitsap Mall.

Best Things to Do in Gorst: Activities and Tours

Kitsap Tours

Take a Kitsap Tour to experience the best of the area. They offer a variety of tours, including wine tasting, hiking, and city tours. You can also book a custom tour, depending on your interests and preferences.

Kitsap Harbor Festival

If you’re in Gorst in May, be sure to check out the Kitsap Harbor Festival. The festival offers a variety of activities, including live music, arts and crafts, and delicious food. You can also watch the Navy’s Parade of Ships, where you can see the Navy’s fleet up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gorst

Day 1: Visit the Northwest Carriage Museum and check out some historic carriages and vehicles.

Day 2: Head to the Clear Creek Trail for a hike or a bike ride. Visit the Poulsbo Waterfront Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Take a Kitsap Tour and experience the best of the area.

Day 4: Visit Bremerton Historic Ships Association and explore a bit of naval history.

Day 5: Visit Illahee State Park for an afternoon by the beach. Rent a kayak or a paddleboard or go fishing in the park’s freshwater creek.

Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Gold Mountain Golf Club.

Day 7: Take in the scenic views of the Puget Sound and enjoy the beautiful scenery of Gorst.

Food and Drink Scene of Gorst

Must-Try Dishes

The Garage Rotisserie & Taproom

The Garage Rotisserie & Taproom is a popular spot for food and drinks, and it prides itself on serving fresh locally sourced ingredients. The chicken rotisserie is a particular standout, with half or whole chicken options. The Outdoor seating area offers a great atmosphere.

Local Beverages

Hale’s Barrelhouse

There’s no better place than Hale’s Barrelhouse to enjoy local brews. The brewery serves a wide selection of handcrafted beers ranging from Hoppy IPAs to Smooth Cream Stout.

Where to Stay in Gorst: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hampton Inn & Suites Bremerton

The Hampton Inn & Suites Bremerton is located nearby in Bremerton. The hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and breathtaking views of the Puget Sound. You can also take advantage of the many hotel facilities, including a fitness center and indoor pool.

What to Buy in Gorst: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Crabtree & Evelyn

Stop by Crabtree & Evelyn in nearby Silverdale, which sells different types of bath and body products, fragrances and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Gorst

The people of Gorst are friendly and welcoming to visitors. The culture here is deeply influenced by the maritime history of the region.

Best Time to Visit Gorst

The best time to visit Gorst is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and dry, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gorst

Gorst is a relatively safe town, but it’s always wise to take necessary precautions while traveling, such as locking doors and windows, keeping valuables out of sight, and avoiding any unknown areas after dark.

Health in Gorst

Gorst residents have access to some of the best healthcare facilities in the US. While traveling, it’s always wise to carry basic medication and a copy of your medical records.

Getting to Gorst: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gorst

The nearest airport to Gorst is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is about 60 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Gorst

The Kitsap Transit bus service offers connections to several destinations in the Kitsap Peninsula, including Gorst.

Other Options

Ferry services from Seattle, Tacoma, and Bainbridge Island dock at nearby Bremerton, which is easily accessible from Gorst by car or bus.

How to Get Around in Gorst

The best way to get around Gorst is by car. You can also rent a bike or explore the town on foot.

Where to Go Next from Gorst: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
