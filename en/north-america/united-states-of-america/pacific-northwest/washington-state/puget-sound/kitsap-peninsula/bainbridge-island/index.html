Introduction to Bainbridge Island

Bainbridge Island is one of the must-visit places in Kitsap Peninsula, Washington, United States. The island is located in Puget Sound and is known for its rich history and scenic views. The island is just a 35-minute ferry ride from Seattle, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Bainbridge Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bainbridge Island Historical Museum

The Bainbridge Island Historical Museum is the perfect place to learn about the history and culture of Bainbridge Island. The museum showcases the island’s rich history and culture through various exhibits and artifacts.

Suquamish Museum

The Suquamish Museum is a great place to learn about the Suquamish people who call the Pacific Northwest their home. The museum features a collection of artifacts, artwork, and photographs that tell the story of the tribe’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Bainbridge Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bloedel Reserve

The Bloedel Reserve is a 150-acre public garden that features a diverse collection of plants, trees, and wildlife. The reserve is open year-round, and visitors can enjoy the beauty of nature while taking a stroll around the garden.

Bainbridge Island Japanese American Exclusion Memorial

The Bainbridge Island Japanese American Exclusion Memorial is dedicated to the Japanese Americans who were forcibly removed from the island during World War II. The memorial features a beautiful sculpture and a wall with the names of those who were affected by the exclusion.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bainbridge Island

Bainbridge Island is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking trails to choose from. Some popular trails are:

  • Grand Forest
  • Gazzam Lake Nature Preserve
  • Fort Ward State Park

Best Things to Do in Bainbridge Island: Activities and Tours

Bainbridge Island Winery and Tasting Room

Located in a charming historic building, the Bainbridge Island Winery and Tasting Room is a great place to try some delicious local wines. Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting and learn about the winemaking process in the Pacific Northwest.

Kitsap Tours

Kitsap Tours offers a variety of activities and tours, including wine tasting tours, brewery tours, and sightseeing tours. Visitors can explore the beauty of Bainbridge Island with experienced guides who will show them the best spots on the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bainbridge Island

If you’re planning to visit Bainbridge Island for a week, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

  • Day 1: Explore the island and visit the Bainbridge Island Historical Museum and Suquamish Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride around some of the island’s beautiful hiking trails.
  • Day 3: Visit the Bloedel Reserve and the Bainbridge Island Japanese American Exclusion Memorial.
  • Day 4: Take a ferry ride to Seattle and explore the city.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a wine tasting at the Bainbridge Island Winery and Tasting Room.
  • Day 6: Take a Kitsap Tour and explore the island’s beauty with experienced guides.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the island’s beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Bainbridge Island

Must-Try Dishes

Local Oysters

Bainbridge Island is famous for its fresh, delicious oysters that are harvested just a few miles away from the island.

Farm-to-Table Dining

The island has a vibrant farm-to-table dining scene, with several restaurants sourcing their ingredients from local farms and producers.

Local Beverages

Local Wine

Bainbridge Island is home to several wineries that produce delicious local wines. Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting and learn about the winemaking process in the Pacific Northwest.

Craft Beer

The island has several craft breweries that produce delicious, locally brewed beers. Visitors can enjoy a pint of beer while taking in the island’s beauty.

Where to Stay in Bainbridge Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bainbridge Island Beach House

The Bainbridge Island Beach House is a charming bed and breakfast located right by the water. The rooms are comfortable and cozy, offering a great retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Island Country Inn

The Island Country Inn is a great choice for those who like to stay in a hotel with all the amenities. The hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast served daily.

What to Buy in Bainbridge Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bainbridge Island is known for its locally produced items, including:

  • Local wine
  • Local honey
  • Local artisanal cheeses

Visitors can also buy souvenirs from the island’s many gift shops and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Bainbridge Island

Bainbridge Island has a rich history and culture, with a strong Native American influence. Visitors are encouraged to respect the island’s culture and customs, including:

  • Respect the Suquamish Tribe’s traditions and cultural heritage.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles while hiking and exploring the island’s natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Bainbridge Island

The best time to visit Bainbridge Island is during the summer months (June-September) when the weather is mild and dry. However, visitors can also enjoy the island’s beauty in the fall and winter months when the island is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bainbridge Island

Do:

  • Respect the island’s culture and customs.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles while hiking and exploring the island’s natural beauty.
  • Enjoy the island’s local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Suquamish Tribe’s traditions and cultural heritage.
  • Leave trash or litter in the island’s natural areas.
  • Cause disruption or noise in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bainbridge Island

Bainbridge Island is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors are encouraged to take standard safety precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Health in Bainbridge Island

Visitors to Bainbridge Island are encouraged to take standard health precautions, including staying hydrated, protecting against sun exposure, and being aware of poisonous plants and animals.

Getting to Bainbridge Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bainbridge Island

The closest major airport to Bainbridge Island is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, located about 28 miles from the island. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or ride-sharing service to the ferry terminal.

Bus Stations Near Bainbridge Island

The Kitsap Transit bus service provides public transportation to Bainbridge Island and other destinations in Kitsap County.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the ferry from Seattle or other nearby cities to Bainbridge Island.

How to Get Around in Bainbridge Island

Visitors can get around Bainbridge Island via bike, car, or foot. The island is easy to navigate, and visitors are encouraged to explore the island’s beauty at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Bainbridge Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Olympic National Park

Just a few hours from Bainbridge Island, visitors can explore the beauty of Olympic National Park. The park is home to diverse wildlife, stunning vistas, and some of the best hiking trails in the country.

Seattle

Seattle is just a short ferry ride away from Bainbridge Island, making it a perfect day trip destination. Visitors can explore the city’s landmarks, cultural attractions, and food scene.

Conclusion

Bainbridge Island is one of the most beautiful and charming destinations in the Pacific Northwest. With its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene, visitors are sure to enjoy their time on the island. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or just a relaxing getaway, Bainbridge Island has something to offer everyone.
