Introduction to Fox Island

Fox Island is a small island located in the Kitsap Peninsula, in the heart of Puget Sound, Washington. This charming island is surrounded by beautiful views of the Olympic Mountains, the Cascade Range, and the Emerald City. Fox Island is a popular destination for those who seek adventure, relaxation, or a break from the bustling city life. Here, you can enjoy various outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, boating, fishing, or simply take in the scenery and breathe in the fresh sea breeze.

List of Places to Visit in Fox Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Fox Island Historical Society Museum: This museum tells the story of the island’s history, from the earliest Native American inhabitants to the present day, through exhibits and artifacts. You’ll get a glimpse into the maritime heritage of the island and the important role it played during the lumber industry boom.
  • The Chapel on Echo Bay: This historic building was built in the early 1900s as a schoolhouse, and was later converted into a chapel. Today, it serves as a venue for events such as weddings and concerts, and is also open for tours.

Natural Attractions of Fox Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Neis Beach: This pebbly beach is a perfect spot for kayaking, swimming, or picnicking. It offers a panoramic view of the Puget Sound and the neighboring islands.
  • Parson’s Garden: A beautiful garden on the northern tip of the island, Parson’s Garden is a great place for a stroll or a quiet picnic. The garden has a stunning view of the Sound and is filled with native plants and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fox Island

  • The Fox Island Loop Trail: A 2.5-mile scenic trail through the forested interior of the island, the loop trail offers stunning views of the Sound and the Olympic Mountains.
  • The South Beach Trail: A trail that leads to a secluded beach on the southern coast of the island, the South Beach Trail is a great place for a leisurely walk or a bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Fox Island: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking: Explore the shoreline, secret coves, and hidden beaches of Fox Island by kayak. Rent one from a local outfitter, or join a guided tour.
  • Fishing: Enjoy some of the best fishing in the region with an experienced local guide. You’ll have the opportunity to catch salmon, halibut, crab, and more.
  • Boating: Rent a boat and explore the Sound at your own pace. You’ll have the freedom to visit nearby islands, fish, or simply take in the views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fox Island

Day Activities
1 Arrive, explore the island, and check out the Fox Island Loop Trail
2 Take a kayaking tour around the island, and visit the Fox Island Historical Society Museum
3 Go fishing in the Sound or rent a boat for the day
4 Relax on Neis Beach, or take a walk through Parson’s Garden
5 Take a guided boat tour to nearby islands, or go whale watching
6 Explore nearby towns such as Gig Harbor or Tacoma
7 Depart Fox Island

Food and Drink Scene of Fox Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh seafood: Try the local oysters, clams, and salmon at the Netshed No. 9, a popular waterfront restaurant that specializes in Pacific Northwest cuisine.
  • Burgers: For a casual and tasty meal, visit the Fox Island Grocery & Deli and try their famous burgers.
  • Coffee: Sip a latte or a cappuccino at the Fox Island Trading Post, a cozy café that also sells local crafts and gifts.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beer: Visit the Fox Island Brewing Company, a small brewery that produces a variety of handcrafted beers, including IPAs and stouts.
  • Wines: Visit the nearby tasting rooms of the Fox Island Wine Co. or the Harbor Wine & Spirits for a selection of locally produced wines.

Where to Stay in Fox Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Fox Island Studio Cottage: A cozy and private cabin that sleeps up to two people, the Fox Island Studio Cottage is a great place to stay for a weekend escape.
  • The Island Inn at 123: A charming and comfortable inn with three guest rooms, the Island Inn at 123 is a great choice for those who seek a more personalized experience.

What to Buy in Fox Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery: Visit the studio of the local potter, Kate Lore, and buy some of her unique and beautiful pieces.
  • Artwork: Visit the nearby galleries such as the Harborview Art & Frame or the Artondocks, and buy some of the works of local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Fox Island

  • Respect the environment: Fox Island is a delicate ecosystem, and it’s important to make sure you don’t disturb the natural habitat. Follow leave-no-trace principles, and don’t litter.
  • Be courteous: Fox Island is a small community, and locals value politeness and consideration. Say hello to people you meet on the street, and be respectful of private property.

Best Time to Visit Fox Island

The best time to visit Fox Island is during the summer months, from June to September. During this time, you’ll enjoy mild, sunny weather, and many outdoor activities and festivals. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and cooler temperatures. Winter can be beautiful, but it’s also cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fox Island

Do:

  • Do explore the island: Take your time to discover the island’s hidden gems, and meet its friendly locals.
  • Do try the local seafood: Fox Island is famous for its fresh and delicious seafood, so don’t miss out on the opportunity to try some.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack warm clothing: Even during the summer months, the weather on Fox Island can change quickly, and it can get chilly at night.
  • Don’t be disrespectful: Fox Island is a small and close-knit community, so it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fox Island

Fox Island is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s still important to take precautions. Always lock your car and your accommodation, and be aware of your surroundings. If you plan on hiking or biking, make sure to bring a map, plenty of water, and a charged phone.

Health in Fox Island

Fox Island has no medical facilities of its own, so it’s important to plan ahead. The nearest hospitals are in Tacoma and Gig Harbor. Don’t forget to pack any medications you need.

Getting to Fox Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fox Island

The nearest airport to Fox Island is the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, located about one hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Fox Island

The nearest bus station is in Gig Harbor, where you can catch a bus to Tacoma or Seattle.

Other Options

If you have your own car, the easiest way to get to Fox Island is by taking the Ferry from Tacoma, which departs hourly. You can also take a taxi or a ride-sharing service from Tacoma or Gig Harbor.

How to Get Around in Fox Island

The best way to get around Fox Island is by car, bike, or on foot. There is no public transportation on the island.

Where to Go Next from Fox Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gig Harbor: A charming town with a maritime heritage, Gig Harbor is home to a variety of shops, restaurants, and museums.
  • Tacoma: A vibrant city with a rich cultural scene, Tacoma offers a variety of museums, art galleries, and theaters.
  • Olympic National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Olympic National Park is home to stunning mountain landscapes, pristine lakes, and ancient forests.

Conclusion

Fox Island is a beautiful and unique destination that offers a variety of experiences for all kinds of travelers. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or simply a break from the city life, Fox Island has something to offer. With its natural beauty, local culture, and friendly community, a visit to Fox Island is an unforgettable experience.
