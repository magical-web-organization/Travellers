Introduction to Holly (Washington)

Nestled in the Kitsap Peninsula, in Puget Sound, lies the picturesque city of Holly, a breathtaking destination in Washington State. Known for its awe-inspiring outdoor spaces, cultural landmarks, and abundance of year-round activities, Holly offers something for everyone.

Whether you are a nature enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Holly is the perfect place to escape the daily hustle and bustle of city life and reconnect with mother nature. With the Olympic Mountains to the west and the Cascade Range to the east, Holly is a playground for hiking, biking, and skiing enthusiasts.

Here’s a comprehensive guide to Holly (Washington) featuring the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Holly (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Holly has a rich history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to visit in the city. Here are some of the must-visit places in Holly:

  • Holly Historical Museum: Located in the heart of downtown Holly, the Holly Historical Museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents depicting the history of Holly and the surrounding area. The museum is a true gem in Holly and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • The Holly Naval Shipyard Museum: The Holly Naval Shipyard Museum offers an immersive experience into the history of the Holly Naval Shipyard, which played a vital role in the development of Kitsap Peninsula. The museum is home to a vast collection of naval memorabilia and artifacts that will transport you to a different era.
  • Pope Resources Port Gamble Historic Museum: Port Gamble is a small town located just a few miles from Holly. The Pope Resources Port Gamble Historic Museum is a must-visit if you want to learn about the town’s rich history and culture. The museum is home to a vast collection of historic photographs, artifacts, and documents.

Natural Attractions of Holly (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Holly is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in Washington State. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the city:

  • Green Mountain State Forest: Green Mountain State Forest is a paradise for nature lovers. The forest is home to some of the most breathtaking scenery in Washington State, from old growth forests to crystal clear streams and waterfalls.
  • Wildcat Lake: Wildcat Lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and other water activities. The lake is located in a quiet and peaceful forested area, offering a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Clear Creek Trail: Clear Creek Trail is a popular hiking destination in Holly that boasts stunning views of the Olympic Mountains. The trail is located in a lush forested area and is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Holly (Washington)

Holly is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails for hikers and bikers to explore. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Holly:

  • Mount Walker: Mount Walker is a popular hiking destination in Holly that offers stunning views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains. The trail offers a challenging hike, making it ideal for experienced hikers.
  • Twanoh State Park Trail: Twanoh State Park Trail is a 3.4-mile round trip trail that takes hikers through lush forested areas and along the beach, offering stunning views of Hood Canal.
  • Rhododendron Trail: The Rhododendron Trail is a 3.4-mile trail that takes hikers through a beautiful forested area filled with rhododendron bushes. The trail is located in Holly’s Green Mountain State Forest and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Holly (Washington): Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Holly:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Kitsap Peninsula is home to some of the most scenic waterways in Washington state, and kayaking or canoeing is the perfect way to explore them.
  • Fishing and Crabbing: Holly is a paradise for fishing enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities to catch various fish species, including salmon, trout, and bass.
  • Wine Tasting and Brewery Tours: Kitsap Peninsula is home to several wineries and breweries, offering some of the best wines and beers in Washington state.

Week-Long Itinerary for Holly (Washington)

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Holly:

Day 1: Visit the Holly Historical Museum and explore the city’s downtown area.

Day 2: Hike up Mount Walker and enjoy the stunning views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.

Day 3: Go kayaking or canoeing at Kitsap Peninsula.

Day 4: Visit Port Gamble historic town and Pope Resources Port Gamble Historic Museum.

Day 5: Take a fishing tour of the beautiful Hood Canal.

Day 6: Relax at Wildcat Lake and enjoy a picnic with family and friends.

Day 7: Visit a local winery and brewery tour of Kitsap Peninsula.

Food and Drink Scene of Holly (Washington)

Holly has a thriving food and drink scene, so make sure you explore some of the following places:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pacific Northwest Seafood: Holly is home to some of the best seafood in the Pacific Northwest, and you must try the salmon, trout, and crab.
  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: Holly takes pride in using locally sourced ingredients when preparing their dishes. We recommend trying the Orcas Island Duck, Dungeness Crab, and Bacon-Wrapped Prawns.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wine: Kitsap Peninsula is home to several wineries, including the Olympic Peninsula Wineries, Eleven Winery, and Long Cellars.
  • Craft Beer: Kitsap Peninsula is home to several breweries, including Rainy Daze Brewing, Sound Brewery, and Slaughter County Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Holly (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Holly:

  • The Holly Inn: The Holly Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of downtown Holly, offering comfortable rooms and delicious breakfast options.
  • Oxford Suites Silverdale: The Oxford Suites Silverdale is a modern hotel located just a few miles from Holly and offers top-notch amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Best Western Plus Silverdale Beach Hotel: Located just a few miles from Holly, Best Western Plus Silverdale Beach Hotel offers stunning views of Dyes Inlet and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Holly (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olympic Mountain Ice Cream: Local ice cream flavors with all natural ingredients and amazing selection of vegan options
  • Holly Farmer’s Market: Fresh and locally sourced fruits, vegetables, and other goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Holly (Washington)

Holly is a welcoming and friendly place, and it is customary to greet people with a smile or a nod. Residents are proud of their city’s heritage and culture, which heavily influences local cuisine, music, and arts.

Best Time to Visit Holly (Washington)

Holly is a destination that can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is between April and October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the natural attractions are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Holly (Washington)

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Holly:

Do:

  • Embrace the outdoors and try some of the adventurous activities.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Ignore local customs or treat locals with disrespect.

Safety and Health

Safety in Holly (Washington)

Holly is a safe place, but it is always advisable to exercise caution and take necessary precautions when exploring the natural attractions.

  • Always carry essential items, including water bottles, maps, sunscreen, and insect repellent.
  • Stay on the designated trails when exploring the natural attractions.
  • Be mindful of wildlife when hiking or exploring nature.

Health in Holly (Washington)

Holly is a healthy place, and you will be able to find plenty of healthy food options. Remember to carry any necessary medications with you during your stay.

How to Get Around in Holly (Washington)

The most convenient way to get around Holly is by renting a car or bike. There are several car rental services available at the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport or Bremerton National Airport. You can also explore the city using the Kitsap Transit buses or Bremerton Ferry services which are available.

Where to Go Next from Holly (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Holly:

  • Bainbridge Island: Just a short ferry ride from Holly, Bainbridge Island offers a quaint town with a thriving art scene, plenty of natural attractions, and stunning waterfront views.
  • Olympic National Park: The Olympic National Park is home to some of the most breathtaking wilderness areas in the United States and offers endless opportunities for hiking, camping, and exploring.
  • Seattle: Seattle offers a thriving food and drink scene, a diverse and vibrant culture, and plenty of attractions to explore, including the iconic Space Needle, Pike Place Market, and the Museum of Flight.

Conclusion

Holly (Washington) is a magical destination that offers something for everyone. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, top-notch restaurants, and luxurious accommodations, Holly has it all.
