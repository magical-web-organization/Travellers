Introduction to Chico (Washington)

Chico, a suburb located in Kitsap Peninsula, is a hidden gem worth exploring in the Pacific Northwest region of Washington State. Nestled in the heart of Puget Sound, Chico offers scenic views of the Olympic Mountains and is an outdoor lover’s paradise. It is an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails to discover. Chico is also rich in cultural and historical significance, making it a must-visit destination for those looking to immerse themselves in the region’s local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Chico (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main historical attractions in Chico is the Seabeck Conference Center, originally established in the early 1920s. This picturesque center is surrounded by a beautiful forest and located on the Hood Canal. The center is perfect for history buffs and those wanting to venture back in time.

Museum lovers will enjoy visiting the Naval Undersea Museum, which is located a short distance away in Keyport. This museum offers visitors a unique insight into the history and importance of undersea warfare.

Natural Attractions of Chico (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chico boasts picturesque scenic locations that bring out the best of nature in this small town. Visitors can enjoy long walks around the Kitsap Peninsula State Park, which houses the Wildcat Lake, perfect for cooling off or having a picnic.

Likewise, the Chico Salmon Park is also situated within the town with a picturesque view of the Chico Creek. The park is ideal for salmon and trout fishing, bird watching, and enjoying the beautiful waterscape.

Suppose you’re looking for something a little more adventurous, then explore the popular Illahee State Park. With its rich natural beauty, visitors can engage in kayaking, beachcombing, and swimming in the waters near the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chico (Washington)

If you’re interested in hiking and biking during your stay at Chico, you won’t be disappointed as the place is chock-full of trails perfect for a good adventure. One popular option is the Clear Creek Trail, which spans over six miles across the North Kitsap’s region. It is renowned for its lush greenery, stunning bird sightings, and serene waters.

Another excellent hiking option for adventurous visitors is the Green Mountain Trail, with a length of over 13 miles. This trail overlooks the gorgeous peaks of the North and South Cascades.

Best Things to Do in Chico (Washington): Activities and Tours

Tourists can enjoy numerous activities and tours in Chico. For example, the Kitsap Tours Organization offers visitors significant insight and history into the Kitsap Peninsula area. Tourists can explore the naval bases, discover abandoned forts, hike pristine forests and charming beaches, and much more.

Another great activity is a helicopter tour of the Olympic Mountains. Visitors can take a tour via a helicopter to explore the aerial view of the rugged Olympic Mountains in the Olympic National Park, which offers a fantastic bird’s eye view of the region’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Chico (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

Chico treats visitors to exemplary local cuisine. One dish worth trying is the fresh seafood, locally caught and served by the restaurants. Razor clams, fresh Pacific oysters, grilled salmon, and fresh Dungeness crab are popular choices.

Another dish worth trying is the apple pie, served with ice cream, and regarded as the local favorite.

Local Beverages

The Pacific Northwest is home to an excellent craft beer scene, and Chico’s local breweries do not disappoint. Although small in number, the best brewery to try is the Silver City Brewery, known for its award-winning beers.

Where to Stay in Chico (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chico, being a small town, only has a handful of lodging options. A popular option is the Oxford Suites, recognized for its luxury and comfort. Other options include the Silverdale Beach Hotel, West Coast Suites at Chico Bay, and the Clearwater Casino and Resort.

What to Buy in Chico (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chico and the Puget Sound region are famous for unique works of art and locally-made artisanal products. Some popular ones to buy include Carriage Square for crafts and pottery, Martha and Mary shop for knick-knacks, and Silverdale Village Square for some window shopping.

Local Culture and Customs of Chico (Washington)

Chico’s locals are welcoming, friendly and close-knit. Visitors are bound to meet locals engaged in outdoor activities like fishing, hiking, and biking. It’s common to say hello and thank the store employees, bartenders, and waitresses.

Best Time to Visit Chico (Washington)

The best time to visit Chico is during the late spring months of May and June, followed by the fall months from September to November. The weather is ideal for outdoor activities, and there are fewer crowds.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chico (Washington)

Chico is a safe community with little to no incidence of severe crimes. Visitors are advised to take precautionary measures when engaging in outdoor activities, like wearing appropriate clothing and carrying adequate equipment and supplies.

Health in Chico (Washington)

Chico offers a high standard of healthcare facilities to all its residents and visitors. Pharmacies and medical clinics are readily available.

Getting to Chico (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Chico can reach the area via various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Chico (Washington)

The nearest airport for Chico is the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Chico (Washington)

The nearest bus station is in Silverdale, approximately ten minutes from Chico.

Other Options

A popular option is to drive to Chico. It is located on the Kitsap Peninsula and can be accessed via the Tacoma Narrows Bridge.

How to Get Around in Chico (Washington)

The best way of getting around Chico is by car or bike. Rental car companies are available at the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport.

Where to Go Next from Chico (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chico provides convenient access to a plethora of nearby communities rich in culture and beauty, including Seattle, Port Orchard, Bremerton, and Bainbridge Island.

Conclusion

Chico, situated in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, offers visitors a unique combination of natural beauty and cultural significance. Hiking and biking opportunities, outdoor activities, local cuisine, and numerous tourist attractions make it a must-visit destination for travelers to Washington State.
