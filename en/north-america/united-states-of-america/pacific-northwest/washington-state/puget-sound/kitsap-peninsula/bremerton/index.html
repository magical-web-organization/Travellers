Introduction to Bremerton

Nestled in Kitsap Peninsula, Bremerton is a captivating destination for travelers looking for a mix of natural and cultural attractions. It is a naval town that offers a glimpse of the Pacific Northwest’s rich history while providing a gateway to its stunning natural beauty.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you an overview of the best things to see and do in Bremerton. We’ve included everything from impressive museums and scenic hiking trails to local food and drink scenes and the best places to stay while you’re in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Bremerton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bremerton is steeped in naval history, so it’s no surprise that the city’s museums showcase this unique past. The Puget Sound Navy Museum invites visitors to explore the country’s maritime heritage, with exhibits that feature ships, weapons, and artifacts.

Another significant historical attraction is the USS Turner Joy Museum Ship. Visitors can explore the destroyer and learn about the ship’s fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Bremerton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Escape the city and explore Bremerton’s natural beauty. A must-see destination is the Harborside Fountain Park, an interactive water feature that delights visitors on hot summer days.

Nature lovers will appreciate the wooded parks and open green spaces sprinkled throughout Bremerton. Wildcat Lake Park is an ideal spot to take in Bremerton’s natural beauty, where visitors can hike, picnic, or fish on the tranquil lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bremerton

Bremerton is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in Washington State. The Gold Mountain Trail System is a network of interconnected trails that traverse stunning hillsides and alpine meadows.

Closer to town, Illahee State Park offers more leisurely trails that wind through old-growth forests and along the saltwater shorelines.

Best Things to Do in Bremerton: Activities and Tours

For those looking for more adventurous activities, consider a kayaking tour of the Puget Sound or a Whale Watching excursion. Bremerton is also known for its enormous flea market, held every weekend throughout the summer months.

Visit the Bremerton Boardwalk and waterfront area and take a stroll along the beach or pop into the shops and local boutiques. There is no shortage of things to do in Bremerton!

Week-Long Itinerary for Bremerton

Day 1: Visit Puget Sound Navy Museum and USS Turner Joy Museum Ship.

Day 2: Hike the Gold Mountain Trail System.

Day 3: Explore Illahee State Park and its surrounding area.

Day 4: Take a kayaking tour or whale watching excursion.

Day 5: Shop at the waterfront flea market and explore Bremerton Boardwalk.

Day 6: Visit the Harborside Fountain Park and nearby waterfront shops.

Day 7: Relax at Wildcat Lake Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Bremerton

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Bremerton would be complete without indulging in some of the local cuisine. Seafood lovers must sample fresh oysters and clam chowder at Anthony’s at Sinclair Inlet or the famous fish tacos at Taqueria Los Cazadores.

For those looking for something more upscale, visit Sogno di Vino, which offers an extensive wine list and Italian-inspired dishes.

Local Beverages

Bremerton is not necessarily known for its alcohol scene, but there are a few key spots that are worth a visit. The award-winning Slaughter County Brewing Company serves up craft beer with a laid-back atmosphere. After-dinner drinks at The Garage Bar and Grill is particularly popular amongst locals.

Where to Stay in Bremerton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

If you’re looking for a luxurious hotel experience, the Hampton Inn & Suites or the Fairfield Inn & Suites are both great options. Alternatively, the Flagship Inn is a reasonably priced and comfortable option in a central location.

Hostels and Guesthouses

If you’re looking for something more budget-friendly, the HI Bremerton Hostel or the Coyote Ridge Bed and Breakfast are both charming options. The latter is a private room in a beautiful log cabin home set on tranquil Coyote Ridge.

What to Buy in Bremerton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bremerton is known for its naval history and boasts many unique souvenirs. Visit the Puget Sound Navy Museum gift shop for navy-inspired memorabilia, or head to Gallery Row for local handmade art and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bremerton

Bremerton is a friendly and welcoming town with a strong sense of community. As with any destination, it’s essential to respect the locals and their customs. Keep in mind that this town, in particular, is a hub of naval activity, so be respectful of the U.S. Navy’s presence.

Best Time to Visit Bremerton

Summer and early fall are the best times to visit Bremerton when the days are longer and warmer. Autumn foliage is also a significant attractor of tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bremerton

Do:

  • Respect the U.S. Navy’s operations and presence.
  • Explore Bremerton’s natural beauty through hiking, kayaking, or whale watching.
  • Shop for unique souvenirs and gifts.
  • Check out Bremerton’s food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the safety warnings of outdoor areas and hiking trails.
  • Eat at chain restaurants while other unique and local options are available.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bremerton

Bremerton is a safe and friendly town, and most neighborhoods are safe to walk around during the day. As in any city, it is essential to be aware of your surroundings and use common sense when out at night.

Health in Bremerton

Bremerton has a few hospitals and clinics, including Harrison Medical Center and The Doctors Clinic. Keep in mind that healthcare in America comes with a higher cost than most countries, so check your travel insurance coverage before you arrive.

Getting to Bremerton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bremerton

Bremerton is most easily accessible through Seattle-Tacoma International Airport. From there, visitors can ride the ferry that connects Seattle and Bremerton.

Bus Stations Near Bremerton

Multiple bus routes connect Bremerton to the greater Puget Sound area. Greyhound and Kitsap Transit both offer service to this region of Washington State.

Other Options

For those looking to take a scenic trip to Bremerton, consider taking the ferry from Seattle, which offers stunning views of the Puget Sound.

How to Get Around in Bremerton

Bremerton is relatively small and easy to navigate, with many sights within walking distance of one another. Alternatively, renting a car or using a taxi service is also an option. To explore the surrounding areas, consider renting bikes or taking a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Bremerton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bremerton is conveniently situated for day trips to Seattle, Olympic National Park, and the San Juan Islands. Other nearby destinations include the Bainbridge Island, Hood Canal, and Port Townsend.

Conclusion

Bremerton is a fantastic travel destination for all types of travelers. Whether you’re looking for an exciting outdoor adventure or want to explore the town’s naval history and cultural offerings, this Washington gem has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Bremerton and make unforgettable memories.
