Introduction to Kingston (Washington)

Located in the Kitsap Peninsula of Washington State, Kingston is a charming maritime town that promises a tranquil and scenic retreat from the bustling city life. This idyllic town is renowned for its stunning waterfront, robust food and drink scene, and excellent outdoor recreational activities, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers, foodies, and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Kingston (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kingston boasts of a rich cultural heritage that is best reflected in its museums and historical places. Explore the following attractions to learn more about the town’s history:

  • The Kingston Historical Museum: Discover the intriguing history of Kingston through its collection of photographs, artifacts, and exhibits, all housed in a restored 1880s carpenter-gothic church.
  • Heritage Park: This historic park is home to three landmark buildings, including a quaint restored one-room schoolhouse that dates back to 1918 and a log cabin from the 1870s that serves as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Kingston (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kingston offers a wealth of natural beauty and recreational activities, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Kingston include:

  • Kingston Beach: Enjoy stunning views of the Puget Sound while relaxing on this sandy beachfront. There are plenty of water-based activities available, such as swimming, kayaking, and fishing.
  • North Kitsap Heritage Park: This vast 800-acre park offers 10 miles of hiking and biking trails and stunning views of the Olympic Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingston (Washington)

Whether you’re an avid hiker or a casual biker, Kingston offers a range of scenic trails and routes that you won’t want to miss. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Northwest Maritime Center Trail: This 3-mile trail features breathtaking water views and picturesque woods, making it ideal for hikers and bikers of all levels.
  • Heritage Park to Point No Point Lighthouse Trail: This 5.5-mile trail offers picturesque views of the Puget Sound and is perfect for those seeking a more challenging hike or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Kingston (Washington): Activities and Tours

From food and drink tastings to kayaking and sailing adventures, Kingston offers a range of exciting activities and tours to appease every type of traveler. Don’t miss the following activities when in Kingston:

  • Waterfront Park: Enjoy a stroll along the scenic boardwalk or join a guided tour on the beach to learn more about the local wildlife.
  • Hood Canal Adventures: Take a private kayak or sailing tour of the beautiful Hood Canal and enjoy the stunning views and wildlife sightings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kingston (Washington)

Planning a week-long itinerary for Kingston can be overwhelming, considering the abundance of attractions and activities offered by this town. Here’s a suggested itinerary for your week-long stay in Kingston:

  • Day 1 - Explore the downtown area and treat yourself to a scrumptious breakfast at one of the local cafes. Then, head to the Kingston Beach for some fun water-based activities.
  • Day 2 - Visit the Kingston Historical Museum and explore the Heritage Park.
  • Day 3 - Go on a whale watching tour with Puget Sound Express and experience breathtaking views of whales and other wildlife.
  • Day 4 - Take a scenic hike or bike ride on one of the trails in North Kitsap Heritage Park.
  • Day 5 - Indulge in a food and drink tour of Kingston and savor the town’s local delicacies.
  • Day 6 - Relax and rejuvenate with a spa session at one of Kingston’s top-rated spas.
  • Day 7 - Take a private kayak or sailing tour of the nearby wildlife-rich Hood Canal and savor the stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Kingston (Washington)

Kingston’s diverse food and drink scene is not to be missed. Visit these local eateries to sample the town’s best offerings:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Savory and succulent crab cakes are a must-try when in Kingston.
  • Oysters: Fresh and juicy oysters are another local specialty that can be savored at several of the town’s seafood shacks and restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Visit the Hood Canal Brewing Company or Downpour Brewing to sample some of Kingston’s best craft beers.
  • Wine: Head to Wind Rose Cellars and indulge in a tasting of its award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Kingston (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kingston offers a diverse range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s budget and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • The Point Casino & Hotel: This 4-star hotel is located near the beach and offers excellent amenities such as a full-service spa, indoor pool, and on-site casino.
  • The Guest House Kingston: This cozy and affordable guesthouse is located in downtown Kingston and offers easy access to nearby attractions and eateries.

What to Buy in Kingston (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Famous for its locally made products, Kingston makes for a great shopping destination. Be sure to pick up these unique souvenirs before you leave:

  • Artisan Crafts: Check out Kingston’s numerous galleries and studios for unique handmade crafts, including ceramics, textiles, and jewelry.
  • Fresh Produce: Head to the Kingston Farmers Market and pick up locally grown fruits, vegetables, and flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Kingston (Washington)

As a small town, Kingston takes pride in its culture and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and manners, which include:

  • Respect for the Environment: Kingston is home to a thriving wildlife ecosystem, and visitors are expected to respect the natural environment and refrain from littering or damaging the wildlife.
  • Politeness and Civility: Kingston locals place a high value on politeness and civility, so visitors are likely to find that they are greeted with a friendly smile and welcoming attitude wherever they go.

Best Time to Visit Kingston (Washington)

Kingston is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. The summertime is ideal for water activities and outdoor adventures, while the fall offers perfect weather for hiking and exploring. The winter is less crowded, and there are several indoor activities to enjoy, such as visiting the museums or wineries.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingston (Washington)

Do:

  • Do explore the local beaches and state parks for stunning views and recreational activities.
  • Do indulge in the local cuisine and sample the town’s famous seafood and craft beer.
  • Do respect the local environment and wildlife by following the park and beach rules.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t park in non-designated areas or block the private property.
  • Don’t forget to carry mosquito repellent and other necessary safety equipment during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kingston (Washington)

Kingston is a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take standard precautions to ensure their safety. Some safety tips include:

  • Be mindful of the surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Lock the doors and windows of your accommodation before leaving.
  • Stay updated on weather conditions and be prepared for sudden changes.

Health in Kingston (Washington)

Kingston’s healthcare infrastructure is well-equipped to handle emergencies, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the area. Visitors are advised to carry their health insurance cards and be prepared for unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Kingston (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Kingston is well-connected, and visitors can choose from several transportation options to reach the town.

Airports Near Kingston (Washington)

The nearest major airport is the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, located about 31 miles from Kingston.

Bus Stations Near Kingston (Washington)

The Washington State Ferry takes visitors from Seattle to Kingston, but visitors can also take a bus to reach Kingston. Some of the major bus stations near Kingston include:

  • Kitsap Transit
  • Jefferson Transit

Other Options

Kingston can be reached by car or bicycle. Visitors can rent a car from either of the airports or take a shuttle.

How to Get Around Kingston (Washington)

Kingston is a small and compact town, so walking or cycling is the most convenient way to explore the town and its surroundings. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to visit places that are farther away.

Where to Go Next from Kingston (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kingston’s proximity to several exciting destinations makes it an excellent base for further exploration. Here are some popular nearby destinations:

  • Port Angeles: Take a ferry from Kingston to Port Angeles and enjoy unspoiled beaches and rugged mountains.
  • Bainbridge Island: Take a ferry to Bainbridge Island and explore its charming small-town vibe and scenic vistas.
  • Olympic National Park: Visit the nearby Olympic National Park to explore the wild backcountry and enjoy stunning vistas of natural beauty.

Conclusion

Kingston is a hidden gem that has something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, unique local cuisine, or a relaxing beach atmosphere, Kingston promises a memorable and rewarding experience. Plan your itinerary using our comprehensive guide and get ready to explore the beauty of this charming town.
