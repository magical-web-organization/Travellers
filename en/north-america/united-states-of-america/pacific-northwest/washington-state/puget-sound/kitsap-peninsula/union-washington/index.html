Introduction to Union (Washington)

Located in Kitsap Peninsula, Union is a picturesque small town in Puget Sound. A perfect getaway for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, Union has plenty of scenic trails, deep blue waters, and pristine forests to explore. The town is also known for its arts and crafts scene, with numerous galleries and artist studios. Plan a trip to Union, and experience the perfect blend of outdoor adventure and cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Union (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Potlatch Historical State Park: This 200-acre park has a rich cultural significance and was once the site of a Native American Potlatch ceremony.
  • Union City Market & Community Center: This historic building was once a hub of the Union community, and today it houses a market, event space, and art gallery.
  • Hood Canal Visitor Center: Learn about the history and ecology of Hood Canal at this visitor center, which features exhibits and interactive displays.

Natural Attractions of Union (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alderbrook Resort & Spa: This waterfront resort offers breathtaking views of the Hood Canal and offers various water activities like paddling, rowing, and kayaking.
  • Twanoh State Park: With its 3,167 feet of saltwater shoreline, hiking trails, and picnic areas, Twanoh State Park is an ideal spot for a family outing.
  • Jarrell Cove State Park: This park is a favorite spot for boaters and kayakers due to its convenient dock and moorage facilities.
  • Tahuya State Forest: This forest has over 100 miles of trails through dense forests of fir, cedar, and hemlock, along with multiple freshwater streams and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Union (Washington)

  • Lady Alderbrook Trail: A beautiful 1.5-mile trail that winds through the woods and offers a stunning view of the canal.
  • Hoodsport Trailhead: This trailhead has access to various hiking trails through the mountains. The trails vary in difficulty, so there is something for everyone!
  • Green Mountain State Forest: Offering stunning views of the Olympics, this state forest has an extensive trail network for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Union (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Kayak and Paddleboards Rentals: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the beautiful waters of the Hood Canal.
  • Scenic Flights: Take a scenic flight to see the stunning views of Union and the Olympic Mountains from above.
  • Wine Tours: Visit the nearby vineyards and enjoy an afternoon of wine tasting and gourmet food.

Food and Drink Scene of Union (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters: The Hood Canal is famous for its fresh and delicious oysters. Be sure to try them in one of the local seafood restaurants.
  • Northwest Clam Chowder: This creamy chowder made with fresh clams is a must-try when in Union.
  • Cedar-Plank Salmon: Cedar-plank Salmon is a Pacific Northwest delicacy. Look for it in one of the local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer and Cider: The region is home to several breweries and cideries offering unique and flavorful brews.
  • Washington Wines: The nearby vineyards produce some of the best wines in Washington. Be sure to try the local favorites.

Where to Stay in Union (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Alderbrook Resort & Spa: This luxurious waterfront resort offers comfortable rooms, spa treatments, and access to various outdoor activities.
  • Robin Hood Village Resort: This quaint resort offers cozy and rustic cabins on the water, perfect for a relaxing getaway.
  • Staybridge Suites Olympia South: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with a fully equipped kitchen and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Union (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts: The town has several artist studios and galleries that sell beautiful Native American arts and crafts.
  • Sea Salt: The salt produced on the shores of Hood Canal is considered some of the best in the world. Bring some home as a unique and delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Union (Washington)

The town has a rich history of Native American culture, and their influence can still be seen in the art and craft scene. The locals take pride in their natural surroundings and work hard to preserve its beauty and ecology.

Best Time to Visit Union (Washington)

Summer months (June to August) are the best time to visit Union as the weather is warm and dry, and the area is bustling with outdoor activities. However, spring (April to May) and fall (September to October) are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and beautiful autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Union (Washington)

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and environment.
  • Check the weather before heading out on any outdoor adventures.
  • Try the local oysters and seafood.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the posted signs and warnings.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Underestimate the tides while exploring the Hood Canal.

Safety and Health

Safety in Union (Washington)

Union is a safe town, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when enjoying outdoor activities. Be sure to check the weather and tide schedules before heading out.

Health in Union (Washington)

Medical facilities are limited in Union, so visitors should plan accordingly. It’s advisable to bring any necessary medications, including over-the-counter remedies, as well as personal protective equipment.

Getting to Union (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Union is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA), which is approximately a 2-hour drive away. Several car rental companies operate from the airport, making hiring a car an easy way to get to Union.

Airports Near Union (Washington)

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA)
  • William R. Fairchild International Airport (CLM)

Bus Stations Near Union (Washington)

Greyhound and Jefferson Transit provide bus services to Hood Canal and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Union (Washington)

The best way to get around Union is by car, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car from one of the car rental companies at Seattle-Tacoma International Airport or make use of the rideshare services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Union (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Olympic National Park: This stunning national park is located less than an hour’s drive away from Union and offers incredible hiking, wildlife, and scenic views.
  • Poulsbo: This quaint town on Kitsap Peninsula is known for its Scandinavian heritage and charming downtown area.
  • Seattle: The bustling city of Seattle is approximately a 2-hour drive away from Union and offers a lively arts, culture, and food scene.

Conclusion

Union, Washington, is a hidden gem on the Kitsap Peninsula, offering a perfect blend of outdoor adventure and cultural immersion. With stunning views, miles of hiking trails, fresh seafood, and lively local culture, Union is a must-visit destination for any traveler visiting the Pacific Northwest.
