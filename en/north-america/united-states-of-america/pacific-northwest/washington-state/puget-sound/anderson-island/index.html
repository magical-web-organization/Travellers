Introduction to Anderson Island

Nestled in the heart of Puget Sound in Washington state, Anderson Island is a tranquil oasis that offers visitors the chance to experience the natural beauty of the Pacific Northwest. This cozy island boasts picturesque scenery, abundant wildlife, and a rich cultural heritage that is waiting to be discovered. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing escape, Anderson Island has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Anderson Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Johnson Farm: A historical landmark that showcases rural life on Anderson Island during the early 1900s.
  • Anderson Island Historical Society: A museum that details the island’s history, including Native American culture, early settlement, and the Island’s relationship to the military base at Fort Lewis.

Natural Attractions of Anderson Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Ada Trail: A scenic hiking trail that offers stunning views of Mount Rainier and the Puget Sound.
  • Andy’s Marine Park: A public beach park that is perfect for swimming, picnicking, and beachcombing.
  • Eagle Island State Park: A nature preserve that features lush forests, rocky beaches, and an abundance of wildlife, including bald eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anderson Island

  • The Ridge Trail: A 6.2-mile hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the Puget Sound and Mount Rainier.
  • The Anderson Island Bike Loop: An 8.4-mile bike ride that takes you on a scenic tour of the island’s natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Anderson Island: Activities and Tours

  • Kayak Tours: Explore the Puget Sound and see Anderson Island from a different perspective with guided kayak tours.
  • Fishing: With its abundance of wildlife, Anderson Island is a hot spot for fishing enthusiasts to catch salmon, trout, and other fish.

Food and Drink Scene of Anderson Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Anderson Island Cider: Made from apples grown on the island, this refreshing cider is a local favorite.
  • Anderson Island Seafood Chowder: A hearty soup made with fresh seafood caught in the Puget Sound.

Local Beverages

  • Anderson Island Wine & Hard Cider Company: A local winery that offers a variety of wines and hard ciders made from local fruits.
  • Jacob’s Java: A cozy coffee shop that serves up hot and cold beverages made from locally sourced beans.

Where to Stay in Anderson Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Anderson Island Vacation Rentals: A selection of cabins and homes that offer visitors a cozy place to stay during their visit.
  • Andy’s Hilltop Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B with stunning views of the Puget Sound.

What to Buy in Anderson Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Anderson Island Cider: Bring home a taste of Anderson Island with a bottle of this local cider.
  • Native American Crafts: Handmade items such as jewelry, baskets, and pottery can be found at local shops owned by Native American artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Anderson Island

Anderson Island is home to a number of Native American communities who have lived on the island for thousands of years. Visitors should be respectful of the island’s indigenous traditions and customs, and dress modestly when visiting cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Anderson Island

The best time to visit Anderson Island is during the summer months, when the weather is consistently warm and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anderson Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the island and take only photos and memories
  • Explore local communities and support small businesses
  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities and cultural attractions

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or remove any natural resources
  • Trespass on private property or enter restricted areas
  • Engage in any activities that may cause harm to the environment or cultural heritage of Anderson Island

Safety and Health

Safety in Anderson Island

Anderson Island is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities. It is also important to be aware of the risks associated with swimming in the Puget Sound, which can have strong currents and cold water temperatures.

Health in Anderson Island

Visitors should be aware of the potential for tick-borne illnesses when hiking or spending time outdoors in wooded areas. It is important to check for ticks and take appropriate precautions to prevent infection.

The best way to get around Anderson Island is by car or bike. Visitors can also explore the island’s natural beauty on foot by hiking one of the many scenic trails.
