Introduction to Auburn (Washington)

Auburn is a charming city located in the heart of King County, nestled in the scenic valley of the Green River. With a rich history dating back to the 1800s, Auburn has transformed into a thriving community full of exciting cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drink offerings. Whether you’re a local or a first-time visitor, there’s plenty to see and do in this picturesque city.

List of Places to Visit in Auburn (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Auburn has a fascinating history, which is preserved in its many museums and historical landmarks. Visit the following places to learn more about Auburn’s past:

  • White River Valley Museum: Explore Auburn’s history through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Mary Olson Farm: Step back in time to the 1800s and experience life on a working farm.
  • Soos Creek Botanical Garden: Enjoy the beautiful gardens and learn about the local flora.
  • Neely Mansion: Learn about the lives of early 20th century settlers in this historic home museum.

Natural Attractions of Auburn (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Auburn is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty and opportunities for outdoor adventures. Be sure to check out these top spots:

  • Flaming Geyser State Park: Relax by the river, hike through the woods, and watch the flaming geyser erupt.
  • Game Farm Park: Enjoy a relaxing picnic or explore the walking trails.
  • Green River Gorge Conservation Area: Take a walk through the picturesque gorge and enjoy stunning views of the river.
  • Lake Tapps: Go fishing, swimming, or kayaking in this beautiful lake surrounded by parks and trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auburn (Washington)

Auburn is surrounded by rolling hills, green forests, and scenic rivers, making it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Try out these top routes:

  • Green River Trail: This 20-mile paved trail takes you through forests, fields, and parks along the Green River.
  • Interurban Trail: Explore the beautiful countryside of Auburn while riding this scenic trail.
  • Soos Creek Trail: A 6.5-mile paved trail that connects with the nearby Interurban Trail and provides stunning views of the Green River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Auburn (Washington): Activities and Tours

Auburn offers an array of exciting activities and tours for every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Muckleshoot Casino: Try your luck at the casino and enjoy live entertainment performances.
  • Emerald Downs: Experience the thrill of horse racing at this iconic track.
  • Outdoor paintball park: Challenge your friends to a game of paintball in a beautiful outdoor setting.
  • Guided fishing tours: Cast your line and explore the scenic rivers and lakes surrounding Auburn.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auburn (Washington)

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for your Auburn visit:

Food and Drink Scene of Auburn (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

Auburn is a food lover’s paradise, offering an array of delightful dishes that represent the city’s diverse culture. Some of Auburn’s top treats include:

  • Fresh seafood at the Sunfish Seafood Restaurant.
  • Tasty burgers and fries at Frugals.
  • A classic pizza from Gino’s Bistro.
  • Traditional Vietnamese pho at Pho King.

Local Beverages

Auburn is home to several breweries, wineries, and distilleries, offering unique, locally-made beverages. Sample some of the following during your visit:

  • Craft beer from Geaux Brewing.
  • Distilled spirits from Sidetrack Distillery.
  • Wine from the nearby White River Amphitheater.

What to Buy in Auburn (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Auburn with you by visiting one of the many unique shops located in the city. Some of Auburn’s best shopping destinations include:

  • Outlet Collection Seattle: Shop for designer brands at a discount.
  • Flaming Geyser Trading Post: Pick up souvenirs and local products at this gift shop located in Flaming Geyser State Park.
  • Doxon Toyota: Shop for Toyota-branded merchandise and apparel.

Local Culture and Customs of Auburn (Washington)

Auburn has a unique culture rooted in the city’s rich history and diversity. Some of the customs and traditions that make Auburn special include:

  • Farmer’s Markets: The local farmer’s market is held every Sunday, year-round, at Les Gove Park. This is a great place to sample local produce, crafts, and food.
  • Festivals and Parades: Auburn hosts several annual events, including the Auburn Festival of the Arts, the Veteran’s Day Parade, and the Fourth of July Fireworks Celebration.
  • Music and Theater: The Auburn Symphony Orchestra and Auburn Community Players offer an array of concerts, plays, and performances throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Auburn (Washington)

Auburn is a great destination year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months of June through August. During this time, the weather is mild, and the city hosts several outdoor events and festivals. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, as they offer mild temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auburn (Washington)

Do:

  • Take advantage of Auburn’s outdoor activities and attractions.
  • Explore the city’s rich culture and history.
  • Try the local food and drink offerings.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car while visiting parks or outdoor areas.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Auburn (Washington)

Auburn is a generally safe city, but visitors should take standard safety precautions. Avoid wandering alone in deserted areas at night, and don’t leave valuables exposed in cars. Watch for traffic when walking or biking, and stay alert around waterways and natural hazards.

Health in Auburn (Washington)

Auburn has several medical facilities, including urgent care centers and hospitals. In case of an emergency, call 911. Check with your health insurance provider to ensure you have coverage in Auburn.

Getting to Auburn (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Auburn is easy, whether you’re driving, flying, or taking public transportation. Here are your options:

Airports Near Auburn (Washington)

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA): This is the closest international airport to Auburn, located approximately 15 miles north of Auburn.

Bus Stations Near Auburn (Washington)

  • Auburn Station: This station offers convenient access to commuter rail as well as local and regional bus service.

Other Options

  • Car rental options are available at the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport.

How to Get Around in Auburn (Washington)

Auburn is a small city, and most attractions can be easily reached by car or public transportation. The city also has an excellent network of bike trails and walking paths, making it easy to explore the outdoors on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Auburn (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

During your visit to Auburn, be sure to explore nearby destinations, including:

  • Seattle: This vibrant city is located just 30 miles north of Auburn and offers an array of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining.
  • Mount Rainier National Park: This stunning national park is a must-see, offering breathtaking views of the iconic Mount Rainier and plenty of hiking trails and outdoor adventures.
  • Tacoma: Just a short drive from Auburn, Tacoma offers an array of cultural attractions, a vibrant art scene, and a scenic waterfront.

Conclusion

Auburn is a hidden gem, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and exciting activities. From hiking and biking to exploring local history and cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Start planning your visit today!
