Introduction to North Bend (Washington)

Nestled in the King County of Puget Sound, North Bend is a tranquil city that offers a sublime mix of natural beauty and modernity. The town is famous for its awe-inspiring mountains and prestigious picturesque landscapes that attract tourists worldwide. North Bend has a rich history, having been home to the magnificent Snoqualmie Falls, the Snoqualmie Pass, and the North Bend Railroad Depot Museum. In this comprehensive tour guide, we explore everything North Bend has to offer, including the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in North Bend (Washington)

Whether you are seeking cultural immersion or outdoor adventures, North Bend, Washington has it all. Here are the top attractions to visit while in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • North Bend Rail Depot Museum: Discover the rich history and cultural significance of North Bend Railroad Depot, which is perfectly preserved to reflect the Pacific Northwest’s early railroad days.
  • Snoqualmie Valley Historical Museum: This museum is located in the iconic historic King County, and it showcases a plethora of exhibits that highlight the rich history of the Snoqualmie Valley.

Natural Attractions of North Bend (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Snoqualmie Falls: This 270-feet waterfall is an iconic landmark located in North Bend, and it offers an excellent opportunity to enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding environment. The waterfall is easily accessible and features a fantastic trail that takes hikers to the base of the strength.
  • Mount Si: If you are looking for adventure, Mount Si offers a perfect hiking challenge. Its trail is a sheer 8-mile round journey, leading visitors to the breathtaking summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Bend (Washington)

North Bend has a diverse range of hiking trails that cater to all skill levels, from beginners to advanced. The city offers renowned biking routes and walking trails, ensuring that there is an activity for everyone. Here are the top hiking and biking routes that you should try out:

  • Mount Si Trail: This trail is an excellent hiking and biking destination that provides a picturesque view of the surroundings while passing through the forested region.
  • Middle Fork Snoqualmie River Trail: Located within proximity to North Bend, this trail takes you through the dense forested area that surrounds the Snoqualmie River.

Best Things to Do in North Bend (Washington): Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling alone or with friends and family, North Bend offers a wide range of activities to try out. Here are some of the best things to do in North Bend:

  • North Bend Adventure Tours: This adventure tour takes visitors on an exciting trip through the surroundings. You can try out kayaking, stand-up paddling, rock climbing, and much more.
  • Snoqualmie Falls and Seattle Winery Tour: Enjoy a tour that combines the magnificence of the Snoqualmie Falls with wine tasting at Seattle winery.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Bend (Washington)

Day 1: Visit the North Bend Rail Depot Museum and the Snoqualmie Valley Historical Museum.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Middle Fork Snoqualmie River Trail, followed by a trip to Snoqualmie Falls and dinner at the Twede’s Café.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to Mount Si and hike up the trail.

Day 4: North Bend Adventure Tours.

Day 5: Snoqualmie Falls and Seattle Winery Tour.

Day 6: Relax and enjoy the local ambiance by exploring local cafes and diners, trying out local cuisine.

Day 7: Explore the nearby towns and cities, including Seattle and Bellevue.

Food and Drink Scene of North Bend (Washington)

North Bend’s food and drink scene offers a perfect blend of local and international cuisines, with many local cafes, diners and restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Twin Peaks Cherry Pie: This pie from the North Bend bakery is a staple of the town and was featured in the legendary ‘Twin Peaks’ TV series, filmed in North Bend.
  • Wood-Fired Pizza: The local pizzerias serve world-class pizzas made from authentic ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Full Throttle Bottles: A local bottle shop that has a vast collection of local and international beers on tap.

Where to Stay in North Bend (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer luxury lodging or backpacker hostels, North Bend has a range of accommodation options to suit your preferences. Some of the best places to stay in North Bend include:

  • Salish Lodge and Spa: A beautiful resort that overlooks the Snoqualmie Falls. The lodge offers top-notch spa treatments and high-end accommodation options.
  • Meadowbrook Farm Bed and Breakfast: A cozy bed and breakfast nestled in the heart of North Bend, featuring comfortable, homely rooms.

What to Buy in North Bend (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home from North Bend, Washington:

  • Twin Peaks Memorabilia: Take home memorabilia from the legendary “Twin Peaks” TV show, which was filmed in North Bend.
  • North Bend Outlet Mall: This outlet mall located in North Bend features a vast collection of local and international brands.

Local Culture and Customs of North Bend (Washington)

North Bend has a beautiful local culture that highlights the diverse history of the Pacific Northwest, including its storied past in the railroad industry. The town is known for its welcoming, friendly community that values mutual respect and embraces diversity.

Best Time to Visit North Bend (Washington)

The best time to visit North Bend is in the summer when the weather is mild and the days are long, perfect for outdoor activities and adventures.

Getting to North Bend (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Bend (Washington)

  • Seattle Tacoma International Airport: The closest airport that serves North Bend. It is approximately 36 miles from the city.

Bus Stations Near North Bend (Washington)

  • King County Metro Transit: Provides transit services to the town, and it has a stop located in the city.

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available at Seattle Tacoma International Airport

How to Get Around in North Bend (Washington)

North Bend has a range of options for getting around the city, including cars, bikes, and buses. A car rental is a popular option for travelers who prefer the flexibility of exploring the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from North Bend (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Seattle: The largest city in the Pacific Northwest and a hub of culture, entertainment, and tourism.
