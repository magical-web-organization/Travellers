Introduction to Federal Way

Nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, Federal Way is a charming city located in King County, Washington. With its stunning natural landscapes, diverse cultural attractions, and exciting activities, Federal Way offers something for every visitor.

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, foodie, or culture-hound, Federal Way is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Federal Way, along with essential tips to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Federal Way

Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary while in Federal Way.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pacific Bonsai Museum: Discover the beauty and artistry of bonsai trees at the Pacific Bonsai Museum, which houses over 150 bonsai trees from around the world.
  • Historic Weyerhaeuser Mansion: Built-in 1923 by lumber magnate Frederick Weyerhaeuser, the mansion is an excellent example of the grandeur of the early 20th century.
  • Federal Way Performing Arts and Event Center: Catch live music and theatre performances, dance shows and art exhibitions at the Federal Way Performing Arts and Event Center.
  • PowellsWood Garden: This peaceful garden oasis is renowned for its exceptional beauty, where you can stroll along winding paths and admire the stunning flora, fauna, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Federal Way: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dash Point State Park: Located on Puget Sound, this park features hiking trails, a sandy beach and picnic tables, making it an ideal spot for a day trip.
  • Rhododendron Species Botanical Garden: This beautiful garden holds the largest collection of Rhododendron species in the world with more than 10,000 individual plants spanning over 22 acres.
  • Wild Waves Theme & Water Park: Thrill-seekers will love this amusement park with over 50 rides and attractions, including waterslides, roller coasters, and a wave pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Federal Way

Exploring the great outdoors is a must-do when in Federal Way. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to take during your trip:

  • West Hylebos Wetlands Trail: A moderate 4.5-mile hike with beautiful views of waterfalls, marshes and forested areas to discover.
  • BPA Trail (Bonneville Power Administration Trail): An easy 3.6-mile walk fantastic for families or beginners. Walk early in the morning to see the birds feeding on berries.
  • Dash Point Pier Trail: With stunning views of Puget Sound, this 1.6-mile trail runs along the water’s edge, where you can watch the boats sail by.

Best Things to Do in Federal Way: Activities and Tours

Federal Way offers plenty of exciting activities and tours that you don’t want to miss.

  • Experience the thrill of Indoor Skydiving: Get the ultimate adrenaline rush at iFLY Seattle’s indoor skydiving centre that provides a unique and safe way to experience skydiving.
  • Fishing Tours at Puget Sound: Go on a fishing tour at Puget Sound to hunt down Giant Octopus, Pacific Halibut, Three kinds of Salmon, Lingcod, Dungeness Crab and Razor Clams.
  • Kayaking at Lake Tapps: Take a peaceful kayak trip through the tranquil waters of Lake Tapps and enjoy the breathtaking scenery that surrounds you.

Week-Long Itinerary for Federal Way

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Federal Way:

Day 1 Pacific Bonsai Museum, Dash Point State Park, & Dick’s Drive-In Seattle for dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Federal Way

From seafood to traditional American cuisine, Federal Way has plenty of delicious food options to satisfy any taste bud.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Salmon: Pacific salmon freshly caught from nearby waters - an unmatched exquisite taste.
  • Burger and Fries: Enjoy a classic American meal at the famous Dick’s Drive-In Seattle.
  • Dungeness Crab: Indulge in the sweet and succulent meat of Dungeness Crab that comes straight from Pacific Northwest waters.

Local Beverages

  • Washington Wines: Savor the unique flavor of local wines of Washington and their rich history.
  • Puget Sound Brews: Try the local craft beers of the region that will surely make your taste buds dance.

Where to Stay in Federal Way: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Federal Way has several lodging options to help you make the most of your trip.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Seattle/Federal Way: A cozy, comfortable hotel near the Wild Waves Water Park and Pacific Bonsai Museum with excellent service.
  • Extended Stay America - Seattle - Federal Way: A budget-friendly hotel that offers simple yet comfortable rooms equipped with kitchenettes.
  • Seatac Airport & Federal Way Hotel: Another cost-effective option equipped with free Wi-Fi, laundry service, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Federal Way: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products, Federal Way offers several options.

  • Rhododendron Species Botanical Garden Gift Shop: Grab some exceptional memorabilia and unique gifts featuring the best of Rhododendron.
  • Uptown Funk Boutique: Hunt for vintage clothing and accessories, unique art pieces or handmade accessories that reflect the local culture.
  • Fisherman’s Terminal: If you’re looking for fresh seafood, then don’t forget to visit Fisherman’s Terminal and take some home to your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Federal Way

Federal Way is a multicultural city, and you’ll find a diverse mix of traditions and customs here. Be respectful of people’s differences and learn from them!

Best Time to Visit Federal Way

The best time to visit Federal Way is early spring to autumn when the weather is perfect for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions. September to November is the best time if you’re a fan of fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Federal Way

Do:

  • Do take hikes in the fresh air: Outdoor enthusiasts should take hikes on some of the state’s best trails in Federal Way and enjoy nature.
  • Do visit the cultural and historical sites in Federal Way: There’s so much to see and learn about the region’s history and culture at Museum and Historical Places.
  • Do try local food: Local cuisines of Federal Way are one of the major attractions that should not be missed by visitors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Keep Federal Way’s beautiful parks and attractions clean by disposing of your rubbish correctly.
  • Don’t ignore pedestrian rules and traffic signals: Look both ways before crossing the street and always follow traffic signals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Federal Way

Federal Way is a safe city with friendly locals. However, it’s always advisable to keep your valuables in a safe place and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Federal Way

Getting to Federal Way: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Federal Way

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport: Also known as Sea-Tac airport, it’s only 18.5 miles from Federal Way.

Bus Stations Near Federal Way

  • King County Metro: Offers a dedicated route to Federal Way from downtown Seattle.
  • Sound Transit Route: Operates along the Highway 99 corridor serving Northeast Shoreline, Edmonds, Lynnwood, and Aurora Village.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental companies are open in Federal Way health and safety measures, providing a safe and accessible transport option.

How to Get Around in Federal Way

Federal Way can be accessed by car, but also has a great public transportation system, with local buses running to all parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Federal Way: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re up for a day trip, there are plenty of amazing destinations to explore near Federal Way. Some of the most popular places to visit include:

  • Seattle: Only 30 minutes north of Federal Way, the Emerald City has something for everyone.
  • Tacoma: A charming city with vibrant culture and a range of fascinating museums, only 16.8 miles from Federal Way.
  • Olympia: The picturesque state capital of Washington is only 50 miles from Federal Way and is home to the popular Olympia Farmers Market.
