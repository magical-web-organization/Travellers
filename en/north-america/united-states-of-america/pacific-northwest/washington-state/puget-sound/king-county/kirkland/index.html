Kirkland, a city in King County, Washington, is a picturesque destination that showcases the natural beauty of the Pacific Northwest region. It is located in Puget Sound, overlooking the Olympic Mountains, and is surrounded by waters, forests, and parks, making it a perfect tourist destination.

Introduction to Kirkland

Kirkland is a thriving city that offers plenty to see and do for visitors. It is famous for its waterfront parks, vibrant art scene, unique shopping, and dining experiences. Additionally, the city is located near many other places in Washington state that are ideal for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Kirkland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kirkland is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the city’s culture and history. The top attractions in this category include:

  • Kirkland Arts Center
  • Juanita Beach Park
  • Heritage Hall
  • Peter Kirk Park
  • Kirkland Heritage Society

Natural Attractions of Kirkland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to the cultural attractions, Kirkland is abundant with outdoor activities, scenic views, and natural wonders. Some of the popular natural attractions include:

  • Waverly Beach Park
  • Bridle Trails State Park
  • Lake Washington
  • Bridle Crest Trail
  • Big Finn Hill Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirkland

For those who love to explore nature, Kirkland offers a wide variety of hiking and biking routes that showcase beautiful views of the city. Some of these popular routes include:

  • Cross Kirkland Corridor Trail
  • Lake to Lake Trail
  • Bridle Crest Trail
  • Bridle Trails State Park

Best Things to Do in Kirkland: Activities and Tours

Kirkland offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained for days. Some of the exciting things to do in the city include:

  • Wine tasting tours
  • Sunset cruises
  • Segway tours
  • Kayaking and paddleboarding
  • Beachcombing

Week-Long Itinerary for Kirkland

For visitors who want to spend a week exploring the city, we’ve created the perfect itinerary to help them make the most of their time in Kirkland:

Day 1: Explore downtown Kirkland and visit the city’s museums and art galleries. Day 2: Go hiking in the nearby parks and nature reserves. Day 3: Take an afternoon cruise on Lake Washington and watch the sunset. Day 4: Go shopping at the local boutiques and souvenir shops. Day 5: Visit the nearby wineries and enjoy wine-tasting tours. Day 6: Go kayaking and paddleboarding in Lake Washington. Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as Seattle or Mount Rainier National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Kirkland

Kirkland is a foodie’s paradise, offering plenty of eateries that cater to all taste buds. Seafood is a specialty in the area, with fresh fish and shellfish being offered in many restaurants. Some of the must-try dishes in Kirkland include:

  • The seafood platter at Anthony’s Homeport
  • The burger at Burgermaster
  • The Mediterranean platter at AK’s
  • The pizza at Ducci’s Italian Cuisine

In addition to the food, Kirkland is also famous for its local beverages, including craft beer, wine, and cocktails. Some of the popular places to enjoy a drink include:

  • The Lake Washington waterfront bars
  • Chainline Brewing Company
  • Columbia Winery
  • Flatstick Pub

Where to Stay in Kirkland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kirkland offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top-rated options in the city include:

  • The Heathman Hotel
  • Woodmark Hotel & Still Spa
  • Motif Seattle
  • Hotel Bellevue
  • Courtyard Seattle Downtown/Lake Union

What to Buy in Kirkland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Kirkland can bring back a piece of the city by buying souvenirs and local products from the various shops and boutiques. Some of the popular items to bring home include:

  • Handcrafted jewelry
  • Art pieces and ceramics
  • Local wines and craft beers
  • Specialty chocolates and gourmet foods

Local Culture and Customs of Kirkland

Kirkland is a laid-back city with friendly locals who enjoy welcoming visitors and showing off their city. As a multicultural and diverse city, Kirkland’s culture is influenced by various traditions and customs, making it a unique destination to explore and learn about.

Best Time to Visit Kirkland

While Kirkland is a year-round destination, the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Visitors can also experience the city’s culture by attending local events such as the Kirkland Classic Car Show or the Kirkland Farmers’ Market, which both take place during the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kirkland

Do:

  • Take a tour of the waterfront parks and enjoy a picnic.
  • Visit the local wineries and breweries.
  • Rent a kayak and explore the beautiful Lake Washington.
  • Enjoy fresh seafood at one of the city’s many restaurants.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring warm clothing, even in the summer.
  • Miss the chance to see the sunset from a cruise on Lake Washington.
  • Ignore the local cultural customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Kirkland is a safe city for tourists to visit, with low levels of crime and high safety standards. As in any destination, visitors should take the usual precautions while exploring the city. Additionally, the city offers plenty of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, to cater to visitors’ health needs.

How to Get Around in Kirkland

Kirkland is a small city, and many of the tourist attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, visitors can also get around by using public transportation, rental cars, or bicycles.

Where to Go Next from Kirkland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kirkland is located within easy distance of several other tourist attractions and destinations that visitors can enjoy. Some of these nearby places include:

  • Seattle
  • Mount Rainier National Park
  • Olympic National Park
  • Leavenworth

Conclusion

Kirkland is a charming city that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re spending the weekend or a week exploring the city, there’s never a dull moment in Kirkland. Make sure to plan your trip ahead and visit this beautiful city to create unforgettable memories.
