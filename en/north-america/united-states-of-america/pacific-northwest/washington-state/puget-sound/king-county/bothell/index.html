Introduction to Bothell

Bothell is a picturesque city located in King County, Washington, United States of America, and is a part of the Pacific Northwest region. The city is situated in the easternmost part of the Puget Sound region, surrounded by mountains, waterfalls, and forests. Bothell has a rich history and culture, and is home to several museums and historical sites.

This comprehensive travel guide covers everything you need to know about visiting Bothell. From the best places to visit, to where to stay and what to eat, we have got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Bothell

Bothell has plenty of attractions for tourists to explore. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Bothell:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bothell Historical Museum: The Bothell Historical Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Bothell and its surrounding areas. Visitors can explore the exhibits and galleries showcasing antique artifacts and learn about the early settlers of the region.

  • Park at Bothell Landing: With a beautiful riverside location, the Park at Bothell Landing is a perfect destination to enjoy a picnic, walk along the river, or learn about the historic buildings in the area.

  • Cascadia Art Museum: The Cascadia Art Museum showcases a wide range of artwork from the Pacific Northwest region, including works from the early 20th century to contemporary pieces.

Natural Attractions of Bothell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sammamish River Trail: A hike or bike ride along the Sammamish River Trail is the perfect way to explore Bothell’s natural beauty. The trail stretches for 10 miles and is a serene haven for nature lovers.

  • St. Edward State Park: St. Edward State Park is a perfect destination for hikers, mountain bikers, and campers. The park offers stunning views of Lake Washington and has over 100 campsites.

  • North Creek Trail: The North Creek Trail spans 7.3 miles and runs through Bothell’s stunning wetlands. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and bird-watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bothell

Bothell is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes in the area:

  • Burke-Gilman Trail: The Burke-Gilman Trail is a 27-mile trail that runs from Seattle to the city of Bothell. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and walking.

  • Wayne Golf Course Trail: The Wayne Golf Course Trail is a 3.5-mile trail that runs through the Wayne Golf Course in Bothell. The trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or jog.

  • Tolt Pipeline Trail: The Tolt Pipeline Trail is a 16-mile trail that runs from Bothell to Redmond. The trail is perfect for biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Bothell: Activities and Tours

Bothell has plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are the best things to do in Bothell:

  • Canyon Park Brewery Tour: The Canyon Park Brewery Tour takes visitors on a journey through some of the best breweries in Bothell, with stops at the area’s most notable craft beer establishments.

  • Woodinville Wine Country Tour: Woodinville Wine Country is just a short drive from Bothell and is home to some of Washington’s best wineries. Visitors can take a tour of the wineries and sample some of the region’s finest wines.

  • Bothell Ghost Walk: The Bothell Ghost Walk is a spooky yet enjoyable experience. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about Bothell’s history and legends.

Food and Drink Scene of Bothell

Bothell has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in the area:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips at Alexa’s Cafe: Alexa’s Cafe serves up some of the best fish and chips in the region. The fish is perfectly cooked and crispy, and the chips are hand-cut and delicious.

  • Burgers at Mr. Kleen’s: Mr. Kleen’s burgers are juicy and flavorful, and their fries are perfectly crispy.

  • Sushi at Sushi Chinoise: Sushi Chinoise serves up innovative sushi rolls that are both delicious and beautiful.

Local Beverages

  • Ciders at Locust Cider: Locust Cider offers a wide range of delicious ciders made from locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Beers at Beardslee Public House: Beardslee Public House is a local brewery that produces award-winning beers.

  • Wines at The Woodhouse Wine Estates: The Woodhouse Wine Estates is a popular winery that features a wide range of wines, including reds, whites, and rosés.

Where to Stay in Bothell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bothell offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bothell:

  • McMenamins Anderson School: McMenamins Anderson School is a unique hotel that features a brewery, movie theater, and several restaurants. Each room is uniquely decorated and offers a comfortable stay.

  • Residence Inn by Marriott Seattle: Residence Inn by Marriott Seattle offers guests comfortable accommodations with a range of amenities, including a fitness center and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Bothell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bothell offers several great options for souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best places to shop in Bothell:

  • Bothell Farmer’s Market: The Bothell Farmer’s Market offers a wide range of locally grown produce, artisanal foods, and crafts.

  • Country Village Shops: Country Village Shops is a collection of charming stores and restaurants that offer unique gifts, handmade crafts, and antique items.

  • The Pottery Bug: The Pottery Bug is a great place to find locally-made ceramics and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Bothell

Bothell has a relaxed and friendly atmosphere, and its residents are known for their love of outdoor activities and craft beer. Visitors should be respectful of the natural surroundings and be mindful of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Bothell

The best time to visit Bothell is between June and September when the weather is warm and sunny. The city is also full of life during the holiday season, making it an excellent destination to visit during winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bothell

Do:

  • Do explore the city’s natural surroundings and take advantage of its numerous hiking, biking, and walking trails.

  • Do visit the local wineries and breweries and try their remarkable beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t make excessive noise or disturb local residents. Keep noise levels to a minimum and be respectful of others in the area.
