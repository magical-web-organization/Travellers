Introduction to Burien

Located in King County, in Puget Sound, in Washington (state), in Pacific Northwest, in the United States of America, Burien is a small yet charming city that offers various attractions for tourists. Its proximity to Seattle makes it a great option for those who want to explore the Puget Sound area without staying in a big city. With its rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural scenery, and culinary delights, Burien has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Burien

If you’re looking for things to do and places to visit in Burien, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the top destinations and attractions to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Highline Heritage Museum: This museum exhibits the history and cultural heritage of Burien and the Highline area in King County. The museum’s exhibits primarily focus on the early pioneers, local industries, and military history.

  • Moshier Art Center: The Moshier Art Center is a community arts center located in Burien. It offers various art classes and workshops for all ages, as well as exhibitions and events.

  • Burien Actors Theatre: If you’re a fan of performing arts, don’t miss the Burien Actors Theatre. This non-profit establishment showcases plays, musicals, and other performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Burien: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seahurst Park: This beautiful park is located on the Puget Sound shoreline and offers stunning views of the water and the Olympic Mountains. Seahurst Park has several hiking trails, picnic areas, and a beachfront area.

  • Eagle Landing Park: Another great park to visit is the Eagle Landing Park. It is located on a bluff overlooking Puget Sound and offers dramatic views of the water below.

  • Three Tree Point: If you’re looking for a scenic drive, take a trip to Three Tree Point. This beautiful area is located on a small peninsula that extends into Puget Sound. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the water and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burien

For those who love outdoor activities, Burien has several hiking and biking trails to explore:

  • Waterland Loop Trail: The Waterland Loop Trail is a 5.3-mile trail that offers scenic views of Burien and the surrounding landscape. This trail is ideal for biking and hiking.

  • Des Moines Creek Trail: The Des Moines Creek Trail is an easy, 2.6-mile trail that runs along the Des Moines Creek and offers great views of the surrounding forested areas.

  • Lake Burien Park Trail: This 0.6-mile trail is a great option for those who are looking for a quick hike. The trail runs through the forested area of Lake Burien Park and offers beautiful views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Burien: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or with family, there are plenty of things to do in Burien. Here are some popular activities and tours that shouldn’t be missed:

  • Beachcombing: Burien is home to several beaches, including Seahurst Park Beach, that are ideal for beachcombing. Visitors can search for shells, rocks, and other beach treasures.

  • Olde Burien Walking Trail: This self-guided walking trail takes visitors through the historic Olde Burien neighborhood and highlights some of the area’s historic landmarks.

  • Seattle Wine Tours: If you’re a wine lover, take a Seattle Wine Tour to explore the wineries in the nearby Woodinville area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burien

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Burien, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Seahurst Park and enjoy a picnic on the beach.
2 Take a scenic drive to Three Tree Point and enjoy the panoramic views.
3 Visit the Highline Heritage Museum and learn about the rich history of Burien.

5 Take a Seattle Wine Tour in the nearby Woodinville area.

Food and Drink Scene of Burien

Burien has a diverse food and drink scene that offers something for every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to savor during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Being a coastal city, Burien has some of the best fish and chips in the area. Some of the prominent places to try this popular dish are B-Town Fish Shack and Elliott Bay Brewhouse and Pub.

  • Pho: Pho is a beloved Vietnamese soup that’s become a staple in Burien’s culinary scene. Try Pho 99 - a popular spot among locals.

  • Mexican cuisine: For some authentic Mexican cuisine, visit El Dorado West and try their fish tacos, burritos or tacos.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Burien has several breweries that craft their own ales, IPAs, and other beers. Try beers from Elliott Bay Brewhouse and Pub, Tin Dog Brewing, and Burien Press.

  • Coffee: Burien is home to several independent coffee shops that offer delicious drinks, made from locally sourced beans. Try Burien Press and Seattle Best Hazelnut Latte and inspire your taste buds!

Where to Stay in Burien: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation in Burien, you’ll find a variety of options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Three Tree Point Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B offers stunning views of the Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains. It is located in a quiet residential area, making it a perfect choice for a peaceful stay.

  • Burien Guest Suites: A great option for those who are looking for an independent stay. Located in the heart of downtown Burien, it is a stone’s throw away from local shops, restaurants, and parks.

What to Buy in Burien: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some local souvenirs or products to bring home? Here are some recommendations:

  • Moshier Art Center Gift Shop: The gift shop at the Moshier Art Center offers a wide variety of locally made art and craftwork, including jewelry, pottery, paintings, and prints.

  • Seattle Chocolate Factory: The Seattle Chocolate Factory is located in Burien and offers a range of handcrafted chocolates made from the finest ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Burien

Burien has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to explore and appreciate the local customs and way of life. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather: The weather can be unpredictable in Burien, so it’s best to check the forecast and dress accordingly.

  • Respect the environment: Burien is home to several parks and natural habitats, so visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and follow any rules or guidelines that may be in place.

Best Time to Visit Burien

The best time to visit Burien is during the summer months. The weather is warm and pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, visitors should be prepared for crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burien

Here are some basic Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Burien:

Do:

  • Do explore the natural scenery: Burien has several parks and natural habitats that are worth taking the time to explore.

  • Do try local cuisine: Burien has a diverse food and drink scene that offers many delicious options to try.

  • Don’t litter: It’s important to respect the environment in Burien, so be sure to dispose of any litter properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burien

Overall, Burien is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should exercise caution and avoid walking alone at night in certain areas. It’s also recommended to keep valuables hidden and locked in a safe at your accommodation.

Health in Burien

Visitors to Burien should take standard precautions to maintain good health, such as washing their hands regularly, staying hydrated, and getting enough rest. If you require any medical attention during your stay, there are several hospitals and clinics nearby, including Highline Medical Center and Group Health Cooperative of Puget Sound.

Getting to Burien: Transportation Options and Logistics

Burien is easy to get to, with several transport options available. Here are some options to consider:

  • Airports Near Burien: Seattle-Tacoma International Airport is the nearest major airport to Burien, located about 10 miles north of the city.

  • Bus Stations Near Burien: Greyhound Bus Lines and BoltBus both provide service to Seattle, which is a short drive from Burien.

  • Other Options: Uber, Lyft, and taxis are all readily available in Burien and can be used to travel to nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Burien

The best way to get around in Burien is by car, as most of the attractions are spread out and public transportation is limited. Renting a car is recommended for those who want to explore the surrounding areas, like Seattle and Tacoma. Alternatively, biking or walking is a good option if you’re staying in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Burien: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for other destinations to explore near Burien, here are some options to consider:

  • Seattle: Seattle is just a short drive or bus ride from Burien and offers plenty of attractions, including the Space Needle, Pike Place Market, and the Museum of Pop Culture.

  • Tacoma: Tacoma is another nearby city to explore, with attractions such as the Museum of Glass, Point Defiance Zoo & Aquarium, and the LeMay - America’s Car Museum.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Burien is a charming city located in the heart of Puget Sound. It offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or delicious cuisine, Burien has something for everyone. So, don’t wait any longer and plan your visit to this wonderful city today!
