Introduction to North

As locals boast, North is where Seattle really shines, with a plethora of cultural, historical, and natural attractions that reflect its diverse and forward-thinking character. Named after its northern location within the city, North borders the trendy neighborhoods of Ballard and Fremont to the west, the scenic stretch of Puget Sound to the north, and downtown Seattle to the south. From museums and theaters to parks and beaches, from coffee shops and seafood joints to bars and breweries, North has it all.

List of Places to Visit in North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nordic Heritage Museum

Located in the heart of Ballard, the Nordic Heritage Museum showcases the rich and diverse history and culture of the Nordic and Scandinavian peoples, as well as their contributions to Seattle’s growth and development.

Museum of History and Industry

Located on the south shore of Lake Union, the Museum of History and Industry, aka MOHAI, is a must-visit for history buffs, with its exhibits and artifacts showcasing the city’s past, present, and future.

Seattle Center

Home to the iconic Space Needle, Seattle Center is a cultural hub that boasts several museums, theaters, galleries, and performance venues, as well as outdoor art installations, parks, and gardens.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Golden Gardens Park

Named after its golden-hued, sandy beach, Golden Gardens Park is a favorite destination for locals and tourists, with its breathtaking views of the Olympic Mountains, the Puget Sound, and the sunsets.

Woodland Park Zoo

Spread over 92 acres of lush greenery, Woodland Park Zoo is one of the top-rated zoos in the country, featuring over 1,100 animals, from lions and tigers to bears and wolves, as well as immersive exhibits and conservation programs.

Discovery Park

Located on the northern tip of Magnolia, Discovery Park is Seattle’s largest public park, with over 500 acres of forests, meadows, beaches, and views of the Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

Burke-Gilman Trail

Spanning over 27 miles, the Burke-Gilman Trail is Seattle’s premier multi-use trail, connecting North Seattle with the University of Washington, Lake Washington, and the Sammamish River Trail.

Carkeek Park Trail

Located on the shoreline of Puget Sound, Carkeek Park Trail offers a scenic and peaceful hiking and biking experience, with its lush forests, creek, beach, and views of the Sound and the Olympics.

Gas Works Park Trail

Located on the north shore of Lake Union, Gas Works Park Trail offers a unique and urban hiking and biking experience, with its industrial remnants, hilltop picnic areas, and panoramic views of the Seattle skyline and the lake.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

Walking Tour of Ballard

Led by knowledgeable and passionate guides, Ballard Historical Society’s walking tours offer a fascinating glimpse into the unique history and architecture of Ballard, from its Scandinavian roots to its shipbuilding and salmon canning industries.

Kayaking in Shilshole Bay

Offered by Ballard Kayak & Paddleboard, kayaking in Shilshole Bay is a fun and eco-friendly activity that allows you to explore the pristine waters, rugged shoreline, and abundant wildlife of the Puget Sound.

Beer Tasting at Fremont Brewing

Located in the heart of Fremont, Fremont Brewing offers a wide and delicious selection of craft beers, as well as brewery tours, tastings, and events that cater to beer lovers and casual drinkers alike.

Food and Drink Scene of North

Must-Try Dishes

Fish and Chips

A classic British dish that has become a quintessential part of Seattle’s culinary scene, fish and chips is a must-try dish in North, especially at places like Spud Fish and Chips in Green Lake and Red Mill Totem House in Ballard.

Seafood Chowder

Another seafood delight that captures the essence of Seattle’s maritime history and contemporary cuisine, seafood chowder can be found in many restaurants and cafes in North, such as Pike Place Chowder in Pacific Place.

Scandinavian Pastries

A legacy of the neighborhood’s Nordic and Scandinavian settlers, Scandinavian pastries offer a sweet and delicious treat that reflects the local culture and flavors. Try them at Larsen’s Bakery and Cafe Besalu in Ballard.

Local Beverages

Coffee

Seattle’s claim to fame, coffee is more than just a drink in North, where you can find countless coffee shops, roasters, and cafes that offer artisanal blends, latte art, and cozy atmospheres. Some of the must-visit places include PCC Community Markets, Morsel, and Caffe Fiore.

Beer

Another local obsession, beer is the perfect companion for any occasion in North, which boasts some of the best craft breweries and taprooms in the country. Try the award-winning beers at Stoup Brewing, Reuben’s Brews, and Peddler Brewing.

Wine

If you’re a wine connoisseur or enthusiast, North has plenty to offer, from the sophisticated tasting rooms of Chateau Ste. Michelle and Matthews Winery to the urban vibe of the Nine Hats Wine Gallery and the underground character of the Old World Red winery.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Ballard

Located in the heart of Ballard, Hotel Ballard is a boutique hotel that combines elegance, comfort, and convenience, with its spacious rooms, signature restaurant, and rooftop pavilion.

The Northwest Hostel

Located in the University District, The Northwest Hostel is a budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style and private rooms, as well as free breakfast, kitchen, lounge, and bike rentals.

Marco Polo Motel

Located in Magnolia, Marco Polo Motel is a retro-style motel that offers affordable and cozy rooms, as well as free parking, Wi-Fi, and continental breakfast.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ballard Farmers Market

One of the largest and most vibrant farmers markets in the city, Ballard Farmers Market offers a wide array of local and seasonal produce, meats, seafood, cheeses, baked goods, crafts, and artwork.

Archie McPhee

A quirky and fun store that celebrates the odd and eccentric, Archie McPhee offers a wide selection of toys, novelties, costumes, and souvenirs that will make you go “wow” and “haha”.

Fremont Sunday Market

Another popular and eclectic market in the neighborhood, Fremont Sunday Market features over 200 vendors and artists, selling everything from vintage clothing and antiques to handmade soap and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of North

As a melting pot of cultures, ethnicities, and lifestyles, North embraces diversity and creativity, and celebrates its local traditions and identities through various festivals, events, and arts. Some of the notable ones include:

  • Ballard SeafoodFest
  • Fremont Solstice Parade
  • Wooden Boat Festival
  • Leif Erikson Day Festival
  • Moisture Festival

Best Time to Visit North

Although North is a year-round destination, the best time to visit is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild, the skies are clear, and the outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Do dress in layers, as the weather can be unpredictable and chilly even in the summer.
  • Do take public transportation or bike, as parking can be difficult and pricey in some areas.
  • Do try the local seafood, coffee, beer, and wine, as they are some of the best in the world.
  • Do explore the neighborhood’s diverse and vibrant communities, and respect their cultures and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the parks, beaches, or trails, and stay on designated paths and areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and cafes, as it’s a customary and appreciated practice in the US.
  • Don’t neglect safety and health precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, staying hydrated, and seeking medical help if necessary.
  • Don’t hesitate to ask locals or official sources for information or assistance, as they are friendly and helpful.

Safety and Health

Safety in North

North is generally a safe and walkable neighborhood, but as in any urban area, it’s important to take basic safety measures to avoid theft, scams, or harassment. Some tips include:

  • Avoid walking alone or in isolated areas at night.
  • Keep your valuables in a secure place and don’t leave them unattended.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and don’t accept unsolicited offers or invitations.
  • Use common sense and caution when using public transportation or renting a bike.

Health in North

Although North has a high-quality healthcare system and facilities, it’s important to take care of your health and well-being while traveling. Some tips include:

  • Bring any necessary prescription medications and first aid supplies.
  • Drink plenty of water and avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect your skin from the sun and pests.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness or injury.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

The nearest airport to North is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA), located about 20 miles south of the neighborhood. Other airports within a reasonable distance include:

  • Snohomish County Airport (Paine Field)
  • Kenmore Air Harbor

Bus Stations Near North

The main bus terminals near North are:

  • Northgate Transit Center
  • University of Washington Station
  • Seattle Center Monorail

Other Options

Other options for getting to North include:

  • Driving or renting a car, although parking can be challenging and expensive in some areas.
  • Taking a taxi, Uber, or Lyft, which are widely available and affordable.
  • Riding a bike, which is a popular and eco-friendly way to explore the neighborhood.

How to Get Around in North

Public Transportation

North is well-served by public transportation, including:

  • Metro buses
  • Sound Transit light rail
  • Seattle Streetcar

Bike

North is also a bike-friendly neighborhood, with several bike lanes, racks, and rental services available.

Car

Although driving in North can be congested and challenging, having a car can provide convenience and flexibility, especially if you plan to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bainbridge Island

Located just a short ferry ride from downtown Seattle, Bainbridge Island offers a scenic and charming getaway, with its historic downtown, picturesque beaches, and wineries.

Olympic National Park

Located about 2 hours west of Seattle, Olympic National Park is one of the most breathtaking and diverse national parks in the country, featuring rugged coastline, ancient forests, alpine meadows, and glaciated peaks.

San Juan Islands

Located about 3 hours northwest of Seattle, the San Juan Islands offer a serene and secluded retreat, with their pristine waters, wildlife, and charming communities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, a foodie, or a traveler looking for unique and authentic experiences, North has something for everyone. From cultural landmarks and natural wonders to gastronomic delights and adrenaline-pumping activities, North is a vibrant and dynamic neighborhood that embodies the spirit of Seattle and the Pacific Northwest. Come and explore it for yourself!
