Introduction to South

Welcome to South Seattle, a vibrant and diverse neighborhood that offers a blend of urban attractions and natural scenery. Located in King County, South is part of the Puget Sound region, which is known for its lush forests, sparkling waters, and stunning mountain vistas. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, South has something to offer. In this guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, hiking trails, food scene, and cultural customs of South Seattle.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Flight

One of the most popular attractions in South Seattle, the Museum of Flight is a must-visit for aviation enthusiasts. This world-class museum has a vast collection of aircraft, artifacts, and exhibits that chronicle the history of flight. From the Wright Brothers’ first flight to modern-day space exploration, the Museum of Flight covers it all.

Columbia City

If you are interested in exploring Seattle’s history and culture, then Columbia City is a great place to visit. This vibrant neighborhood has a rich history that is reflected in its architecture, art, and cuisine. You can explore the local shops, restaurants, and galleries, or take a guided walking tour to learn about the area’s past.

Kubota Garden

Located in the Rainier Beach neighborhood, the Kubota Garden is a hidden gem that is worth exploring. This beautiful Japanese garden was designed by Fujitaro Kubota, a Seattle landscaper who blended Japanese and Pacific Northwest styles to create a unique and tranquil space. Stroll through the garden’s lush foliage, stone bridges, and water features, and soak up the peaceful atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Rainier National Park

If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, then Mt. Rainier National Park is the perfect destination. Located about 80 miles south of Seattle, this park is home to majestic glaciers, rugged peaks, and abundant wildlife. You can hike the trails, bike the scenic routes, or simply admire the breathtaking views.

Alki Beach

Enjoy a day at the beach at Alki Beach, which offers stunning views of downtown Seattle across the water. This long sandy beach has plenty of picnic areas, playgrounds, volleyball courts, and other amenities. You can also rent kayaks, paddleboards, and other watercraft to explore the sound.

Seward Park

Located on a peninsula in Lake Washington, Seward Park is a beautiful nature reserve that offers spectacular views of the lake and the surrounding mountains. You can hike the park’s trails, swim in the lake, or take a picnic on the beach. The park is also home to one of Seattle’s old-growth forests, which is perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Seward Park Loop Trail

At only 2.4 miles long, the Seward Park Loop Trail is an easy hike that is perfect for families and beginners. The trail follows the park’s shoreline, offering stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

Rattlesnake Ledge Trail

For a more challenging hike, the Rattlesnake Ledge Trail is a popular option. This 4-mile trail climbs steeply through the forest, offering stunning views of the Snoqualmie Valley from the top.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Underground Tour of Pioneer Square

Discover the hidden history of Seattle’s first neighborhood on the Underground Tour of Pioneer Square. This guided tour takes you through the subterranean passages that were once at street level, giving you a glimpse into the city’s past.

Farwest Sports Fishing

Take a fishing charter with Farwest Sports Fishing, and experience the thrill of catching salmon, lingcod, halibut, and more. This family-owned business has been operating in the Puget Sound area for over 20 years, and their experienced guides will take you to the best fishing spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

  • Day 1: Explore world-class aircraft at the Museum of Flight, and end the day with a stroll through the scenic Kubota Garden.
  • Day 2: Take a guided walking tour of Columbia City, and discover the neighborhood’s history and culture.
  • Day 3: Hike the Seward Park Loop Trail in the morning, and spend the afternoon relaxing at Alki Beach.
  • Day 4: Head out to Mount Rainier National Park for a day of hiking and exploration.
  • Day 5: Take a fishing charter with Farwest Sports Fishing, and catch some local delicacies.
  • Day 6: Discover the hidden history of Seattle’s first neighborhood on the Underground Tour of Pioneer Square.
  • Day 7: Spend a leisurely day at Seward Park, exploring the old-growth forest and swimming in the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

Piroshki

Piroshki is a Russian pastry that is filled with meat, potatoes, or cheese. You can find them at Piroshky Piroshky Bakery, a famous bakery located in Pike Place Market.

Pho

Pho is a Vietnamese soup that is made with beef or chicken broth, rice noodles, and various herbs and spices. You can find some of the best pho in South Seattle at Pho Bac.

Local Beverages

Coffee

Seattle is known for its coffee, and some of the best roasters can be found in South Seattle. Caffe Vita and Lighthouse Roasters are two popular options.

Brewery Tours

If you’re a beer lover, then a brewery tour is a must-do activity in South Seattle. You can visit Georgetown Brewing Company or Flying Lion Brewing for a taste of local brews.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silver Cloud Hotel Seattle - Stadium

Located near CenturyLink Field, the Silver Cloud Hotel is a comfortable and convenient option for visitors to South Seattle. The hotel offers spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

The Bacon Mansion Bed and Breakfast

For a more intimate and charming lodging option, the Bacon Mansion Bed and Breakfast is a great choice. This historic mansion has been restored to its original beauty, and offers luxurious guest rooms and homemade breakfasts.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chukar Cherries

Chukar Cherries is a local company that produces a variety of cherry-based products, including chocolate-covered cherries, cherry salsa, and cherry barbecue sauce. You can find their products at their store in Pike Place Market.

Theo Chocolate

Theo Chocolate is a Seattle-based chocolatier that produces high-quality, organic chocolate. You can take a factory tour and sample their creations at their store in Fremont.

Local Culture and Customs of South

One of the defining characteristics of South Seattle is its diversity. The neighborhood is home to people from many different backgrounds, and this is reflected in its cuisine, traditions, and festivals. The Seattle International Film Festival, the Lunar New Year Festival, and the Rainier Valley Heritage Parade and Festival are just a few examples.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South Seattle is during the summer months, from June through September. The weather is mild and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the seafood and coffee.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area, including the parks and scenic drives.
  • Get to know the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car or unattended in public areas.
  • Ignore traffic laws or street signs.
  • Be disrespectful to other people’s cultural or religious traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South Seattle is generally safe, but there are some areas that can be less safe at night. It’s best to stay in well-lit areas and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in South

The tap water in South Seattle is safe to drink. If you’re planning on spending time outdoors, make sure to wear sunscreen, bug spray, and appropriate clothing to protect yourself from the elements.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

The closest airport to South Seattle is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is about 10 miles south of the neighborhood. You can take a taxi, Uber, or Lyft from the airport to your hotel.

Bus Stations Near South

King Street Station is the main bus and train station in Seattle, and is located in the Pioneer Square neighborhood. You can take a bus or train from King Street Station to South Seattle.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car at the airport or in downtown Seattle. South Seattle is also accessible by bike or on foot, depending on your location.

How to Get Around in South

The best way to get around South Seattle is by car or public transportation. King County Metro operates buses throughout the area, and there are several light rail stations that connect South Seattle to downtown.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bellevue

Located across the lake from Seattle, Bellevue is a bustling city that offers shopping, dining, and entertainment options. You can take a scenic drive across the lake or hop on a ferry to visit this vibrant city.

Snoqualmie Falls

Located about 30 miles east of Seattle, Snoqualmie Falls is a natural wonder that is worth a visit. You can hike the trails to the falls, or simply admire the view from the observation deck.

Conclusion

South Seattle is a vibrant neighborhood that offers something for everyone. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and local cuisine, you can explore this area for weeks and still discover something new. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in South Seattle.
