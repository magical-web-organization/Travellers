Introduction to Kent (Washington)

Located in King County in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Kent is a bustling city that offers something for everyone. Boasting a blend of urban and suburban areas, Kent offers a unique experience for travelers looking to explore the region. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie, Kent is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kent (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Flight

One of the top museums in the Pacific Northwest, the Museum of Flight offers a one-of-a-kind experience. The museum boasts an impressive collection of over 175 aircraft and spacecrafts, including the original Boeing 747. Visitors can partake in interactive exhibits and experience the thrill of flight.

Kent Historical Museum

A great destination for history enthusiasts, the Kent Historical Museum offers a glimpse into Kent’s past. The museum features exhibits on the region’s Native American history, early pioneer days, and World War II era. Visitors can also explore the museum’s historical farming equipment and walk through recreations of early Kent businesses.

Natural Attractions of Kent (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Clark Lake Park

Located in the heart of Kent, Clark Lake Park is a serene oasis in the midst of the urban landscape. The park boasts a 30-acre lake perfect for boating and fishing, as well as hiking trails and picnic areas.

Soos Creek Trail

For those looking for a peaceful walk through the area’s natural beauty, the Soos Creek Trail is a must-visit destination. The trail weaves through forests, fields, and even wetlands, giving visitors a chance to experience the region’s diverse habitats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kent (Washington)

Interurban Trail

A popular bike trail that spans 14 miles from Kent to Tukwila, the Interurban Trail is a must-visit for cycling enthusiasts. The trail offers scenic views of Mount Rainier and the Green River Valley, as well as multiple access points to nearby parks and amenities.

Best Things to Do in Kent (Washington): Activities and Tours

Emerald Downs

For sports fans, a day at Emerald Downs is a must. The popular horse racing track offers family-friendly activities and events all season long. Visitors can enjoy a day at the track, partake in betting and enjoy live entertainment.

Kent Farmer’s Market

For a taste and look at Kent’s local bounty, visit the Kent Farmer’s Market. Strolling through the outdoor market you can find locally crafted goods, fresh baked goods, organic produce, flowers and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Kent (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

The Berliner

Located in downtown Kent, The Berliner is a bakery and café known for their outstanding baked goods. German pretzels, homemade breads, frittatas and sweet treats all made fresh daily.

Morrel

For a upscale and romantic night out, visit Morrel and enjoy Northwest cuisine in stylish and luxurious surroundings

Local Beverages

Airways Brewing Company

A local brewery, Airways Brewing Company offers a variety of hand-crafted brews on tap. Visitors can partake in brewery tours or enjoy a pint in the taproom.

Where to Stay in Kent (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hampton Inn & Suites

Located just minutes away from downtown Kent. This hotel offers comfortable rooms, breakfast, pool and hot tub.

GuestHouse Seattle/Kent

Conveniently located near the Kent Station and other downtown shops, GuestHouse Seattle/Kent offers clean and comfortable accommodations on a budget.

What to Buy in Kent (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Johnson’s Home & Garden

A Kent staple since the 1920s, Johnson’s Home & Garden offers a wide range of garden supplies, locally sourced plants, and gifts. They specialize in indoor/outdoor furniture and decor, perfect for any souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kent (Washington)

Kent is known for its diverse community and welcoming atmosphere, proud of it’s multi-culture and ever growing population. We encourage visitors to open themselves up to the rich and diverse experiences within the community.

Best Time to Visit Kent (Washington)

The best times to visit Kent are in the spring and fall seasons where mild temperatures offer a comfortable climate to explore all of wonders and activities Kent has to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kent (Washington)

  • Do tap into the local craft beer

Safety and Health

Kent is generally safe to navigate. It is advised to use caution when being in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Kent (Washington)

It is important to drink plenty of water when participating in any outdoor activities. It is recommended to check with a doctor before engaging in such activities.

Where to Go Next from Kent (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion
