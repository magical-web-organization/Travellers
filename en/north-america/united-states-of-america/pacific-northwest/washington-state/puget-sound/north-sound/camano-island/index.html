Introduction to Camano Island

Located in the North Sound region of Puget Sound in Washington, Camano Island offers an escape from busy city life with its serene natural beauty. Whether you’re into hiking, fishing, bird-watching, or just relaxing by the beach, Camano Island is the perfect destination for you. In this tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, activities to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Camano Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Camano Island Historical Society Museum

Discover Camano Island’s rich history at the Camano Island Historical Society Museum. The museum showcases artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that tell the story of the island’s past.

English Boom Historical Park

Located at the southern end of Camano Island, English Boom Historical Park offers a glimpse into the island’s past as a logging community. Take a stroll along the trails and check out the historical buildings that have been restored.

Natural Attractions of Camano Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Camano Island State Park

Camano Island State Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, kayaking, and beachcombing. There are several picnic areas and campsites available as well.

Cama Beach State Park

Located on the west side of Camano Island, Cama Beach State Park is a great place to experience the island’s natural beauty. The park features several hiking trails and a sandy beach perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Camano Island

Camano Island Loop Trail

The Camano Island Loop Trail is a 9-mile paved trail that circles the island. The trail offers scenic views of the Puget Sound and Cascade Mountains. It’s a perfect route for biking or walking.

Iverson Trail

The Iverson Trail is a 3.5-mile trail that offers a mix of forest and beach views. The trail is a bit rocky and steep, so it’s best for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Camano Island: Activities and Tours

Whale Watching Tour

Take a whale watching tour to see Orcas, Gray Whales, Humpback Whales, and other marine life. The tour also includes informative guides who will share information about the whales and their habitats.

Kayaking Tour

Explore the scenic beauty of Camano Island by taking a kayaking tour. The tour takes you through the island’s waterways, where you can get up close and personal with the island’s marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Camano Island

Day 1: Camano Island State Park

Start your trip off by exploring the Camano Island State Park. Spend the day hiking, fishing, and beachcombing. End the day by setting up camp and enjoying a campfire at one of the park’s campsites.

Day 2: Whale Watching Tour

Take a whale watching tour and experience the beauty of Camano Island’s marine life.

Day 3: Iverson Trail

Take a hike on the Iverson Trail and enjoy the island’s natural beauty.

Day 4: Cama Beach State Park

Visit the Cama Beach State Park and spend the day swimming and sunbathing at the beach.

Day 5: Kayaking Tour

Explore the island’s waterways by taking a kayaking tour.

Day 6: English Boom Historical Park

Visit the English Boom Historical Park and learn about the island’s logging history.

Day 7: Relax and Unwind

Spend your final day on the island relaxing and unwinding. Take a walk along the beach or just enjoy the island’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Camano Island

Must-Try Dishes

Penn Cove Mussels

Camano Island is famous for its Penn Cove Mussels. These mussels are locally grown and served in many of the island’s seafood restaurants.

Crusty Crab Sandwich

The Crusty Crab Sandwich is a popular dish on Camano Island. It features fresh Dungeness Crab served on a crusty roll with a side of fries.

Local Beverages

Camano Island Coffee Roasters

Visit the Camano Island Coffee Roasters and try their locally roasted coffee. They offer a variety of flavors and blends available for purchase.

Camano Island Brewing

Sample some of the island’s local beers at Camano Island Brewing. They have a variety of beers on tap, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

Where to Stay in Camano Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Camano Island Inn

The Camano Island Inn offers a luxurious stay on the island. They offer spacious rooms and suites with stunning ocean views.

Tyee Beach Resort

The Tyee Beach Resort offers a cozy stay on the island. They have cabins available for rent, many of which have beautiful waterfront views.

What to Buy in Camano Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Camano Island Honey

Take home some locally harvested honey from Camano Island. The honey is made from bees that have pollinated the island’s diverse flora.

Camano Island Coffee

Take home some locally roasted coffee from Camano Island Coffee Roasters. They offer a variety of flavors and blends available for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Camano Island

Camano Island is known for its close-knit community and appreciation for nature. The island has a strong art scene, with several local galleries and art shows throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Camano Island

The best time to visit Camano Island is during the summer months, from June to September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Camano Island

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Take advantage of the island’s natural beauty by participating in outdoor activities
  • Visit the local galleries and art shows

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the island’s beaches or trails
  • Disturb the island’s wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Camano Island

Camano Island is generally a safe destination. However, it’s always important to take safety precautions while participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Camano Island

Camano Island has several medical clinics and hospitals in the surrounding area. It’s always important to bring any necessary medication or supplies with you while traveling.

How to Get Around in Camano Island

The best way to get around Camano Island is by car or bike. There are also several walking trails available for those who prefer to walk.

Where to Go Next from Camano Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Seattle

Seattle is just a short drive from Camano Island and offers plenty of urban attractions, including the iconic Space Needle and Pike Place Market.

San Juan Islands

Take a ferry from Anacortes to the San Juan Islands and explore more of the Pacific Northwest’s natural beauty.

Conclusion

Camano Island offers a peaceful escape from city life, with its beautiful natural landscapes and rich history. Whether you’re into hiking, kayaking, or just relaxing on the beach, there’s something for everyone on Camano Island. Plan your trip today and experience the unique charm of this Pacific Northwest destination.
