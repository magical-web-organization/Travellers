Introduction to Snohomish

Nestled in the breathtaking North Sound region of Washington, Snohomish is a charming town that offers visitors a myriad of culturally significant attractions, natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and amazing food. Snohomish boasts incredible views of the Cascade Mountains, Snoqualmie River, and the serenity of western Washington forests. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, or simply seeking a quiet escape, Snohomish has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Snohomish

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Snohomish has a rich history that is evident in the many museums and historical places scattered throughout the town. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in Snohomish:

  • Blackman House Museum: This iconic Queen Anne-style house was built-in 1878 and is the oldest standing original family home in Snohomish.

  • Snohomish Carnegie Library: The library features a history room with exhibits about the Snohomish area, a children’s area, and daily events for adults and children alike.

  • Snohomish County Centennial Trail: This 30-mile trail connects to a range of historical sites including Nakashima Barn, the Machias Railway Trestle, and the Thomas Stillwagon House.

Natural Attractions of Snohomish: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Snohomish provides an array of outdoor adventures ranging from scenic routes to adrenaline-fueled activities:

  • Lord Hill Regional Park: An excellent picnic location that provides horseback riding locations, natural hiking trails, and beautiful scenery.

  • Brickyard Park: A scenic view park that includes a sports field, riverbank, hiking trail, barbecue pits, and a children’s play area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Snohomish

Snohomish provides access to a series of beautiful trails that are ideal for every kind of hiker. You can opt for lazy strolls along the Snohomish River, take a relaxing hike on one of the many trails, or opt for a challenging mountain biking adventure. Some of the popular trails that you can explore while in Snohomish include:

  • Lake Stevens Trail: This gentle trail features beautiful wildflowers and is ideal for hikers of all levels.

  • Brighton Beach Trail: This is an easy hike that will take you through a series of picturesque wildflower meadows and forests.

Best Things to Do in Snohomish: Activities and Tours

From visiting the downtown area’s historical landmarks to outdoor adventures, Snohomish has a variety of things to do.

  • Antique Shopping: The historic downtown area boasts a collection of antique shops to satisfy all shopping desires.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the breathtaking beauty of Snohomish from above with a hot air balloon ride. Airial Balloon Company offers year-round balloon rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Snohomish

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Snohomish:

Day 1 Visit Snohomish County Centennial Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Snohomish

Must-Try Dishes

Snohomish offers a variety of delicious, local foods. When you visit Snohomish, you must try these popular dishes:

  • Kringle: This is a Danish pastry offered by Hometown Pastry which is famous for its flavorful and unique taste.

  • Auntie M’s Kitchen: A family run restaurant that serves home cooked dishes, with options for both meat lovers and vegans

Local Beverages

Snohomish boasts a rich history of brews, including craft beers, ciders, and spirits. Some of the award-winning local breweries, wineries, and distilleries that you should visit in Snohomish include:

  • Skip Rock Distillery: This is a locally-owned and operated distillery that offers award-winning spirits made from locally sourced ingredients.

  • Haywire Brewing Co: This is a popular brewery that has been producing beer since 2016 and offers a range of unique flavors.

Where to Stay in Snohomish: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Snohomish offers an array of accommodation options based on your preferences and budget. You can opt for family-owned boutique hotels, historic homes converted to B&Bs, and stylish guesthouses.

Some of the notable accommodations include:

  • Aero Inn: An affordable motel located near downtown Snohomish, ideal for those on a budget.

  • Belle Chapel Guesthouse: This is a beautiful bed and breakfast that is perfect for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.

What to Buy in Snohomish: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Snohomish is renowned for its unique shopping experience, particularly the antique shops in the Historic Downtown district, where you can find a wide range of vintage and antique merchandise. You can also find a variety of delicious and locally made foods.

Some of the local products that you can purchase for souvenirs include:

  • Locally made gourmet chocolates at Zachary’s Chocolate
  • Locally sourced honey at Sweet Success Honey
  • Handcrafted artisan salsas at Seattle Salsa Company

Local Culture and Customs of Snohomish

People in Snohomish are known for their welcoming manner, kindness, and general respect for each other’s rights. Snohomish has a rich history, full of stories that demonstrate the town’s culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the town’s values and practices.

Best Time to Visit Snohomish

The best time of year to visit Snohomish is typically from late spring to early fall, especially from June to September, when temperatures remain moderate and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Snohomish

Do:

  • Take advantage of the Antique Shopping in Historic Downtown Snohomish
  • Visit the scenic view parks, and explore the natural beauty of Snohomish
  • Attend local events such as the Kla-Ha-Ya Days festival, held annually in July

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces — Snohomish residents and visitors take great pride in their clean environment
  • Block parking or sidewalk areas by parking illegally
  • Disrespect local cultural traditions and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Snohomish

Snohomish has an overall low crime rate making it a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should still adhere to general safety precautions such as locking their valuables and avoid walking in secluded areas alone at night.

Health in Snohomish

Snohomish provides ample access to medical and emergency facilities in case of health emergencies. It is recommended that visitors have medical insurance given unforeseen circumstances.

Getting to Snohomish: Transportation Options and Logistics

Snohomish is an easily accessible town located within Snohomish County, Washington. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip:

  • Airports Near Snohomish: The nearest airports to Snohomish are the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA), located about 32 miles south of Snohomish, followed by Paine Field Snohomish County Airport (PAE), located about 13 miles southwest of Snohomish.

  • Bus Stations Near Snohomish: Community Transit operates regular bus services in Snohomish.

  • Other Options: You can rent a car or opt for ride-sharing services for ease of transportation.

How to Get Around in Snohomish

Navigating Snohomish is easy and convenient with the array of transportation options available such as local bus services and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Snohomish: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Snohomish is near many cities and towns that you can visit for excursions such as:

  • Seattle: Located about 30 miles southwest of Snohomish, Seattle is a hub of diverse cultures and activities, with iconic landmarks such as the Space Needle and Pike Place Market.

  • Everett: Located about 10 miles northwest of Snohomish, Everett provides visitors with breathtaking views of the Puget Sound and is home to popular attractions such as the Boeing Factory Tour and Angel of the Winds Arena.

  • Whidbey Island: A short drive and ferry ride away, Whidbey Island provides visitors with scenic views, hiking, kayaking, and cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Snohomish is a charming town that provides visitors with a range of activities to suit every taste and budget. It is an excellent destination for a family vacation, romantic getaway, or solo outdoor adventure trip. From scenic views to cultural experiences and outdoor adventures, Snohomish has something to offer every visitor. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of North Sound.
