Introduction to Everett (Washington)

Located in the North Sound region of Puget Sound, Everett is a scenic city in Washington (state), part of the Pacific Northwest region in the United States of America. Everett boasts a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural diversity, and urban conveniences. It is home to a plethora of outdoor activities, world-class museums, and delicious local cuisine. Read on for a comprehensive guide to Everett that will help you plan your trip to this charming city.

List of Places to Visit in Everett (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Imagine Children’s Museum

Imagine Children’s Museum is an interactive museum that houses exhibits and activities designed for children up to 12 years old. It features eight themed galleries, which include Pioneer Peak, Water Works, and an art studio. The museum also hosts daily workshops and special events.

Historic Flight Foundation

The Historic Flight Foundation is a nonprofit museum that showcases restored vintage aircraft from the 1920s to the 1950s. Visitors can explore 16 planes, including the iconic Boeing 247-D, B-25D Mitchell, and P-51D Mustang. The museum also offers tours and flight demonstrations.

Schack Art Center

The Schack Art Center is a contemporary visual and performing arts space that features rotating exhibits of regional and national artists. The center includes a glassblowing studio, pottery studio, and gift shop. Visitors can also take classes in glass art, painting, and drawing.

Natural Attractions of Everett (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jetty Island

Jetty Island is a two-mile-long island accessible by a ferry ride from Everett’s 10th Street Boat Launch. The island features sandy beaches, hiking trails, picnic areas, and kiteboarding opportunities. Visitors can also spot wildlife, including seals and sea birds.

Forest Park

Forest Park is a 197-acre community park that boasts a range of outdoor activities for all ages and abilities. It includes a playground, swimming pool, tennis courts, picnic areas, and hiking trails. The park also hosts events throughout the year, such as an annual fishing derby and outdoor movie nights.

Japanese Gulch

Japanese Gulch is a natural area that spans 140 acres of forest, wetlands, and streams. It includes over six miles of hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult, and offers scenic views of the Puget Sound. Visitors can also spot wildlife, such as deer, owls, and eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Everett (Washington)

Everett offers numerous hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular trails include the Interurban Trail, Lowell Riverfront Trail, and the Everett Arboretum Trail.

Interurban Trail

The Interurban Trail is a 21-mile-long trail that stretches from Everett to Seattle. It is a popular route for biking and hiking and offers views of the Puget Sound and surrounding forests. The trail also connects to several parks and natural areas, including Boeing Creek Park and Hamlin Park.

Lowell Riverfront Trail

The Lowell Riverfront Trail is a scenic trail that follows the Snohomish River, passing through historical mill sites and offering views of Mount Baker. It is a 3.3-mile-long flat trail perfect for biking, walking, and jogging.

Everett Arboretum Trail

The Everett Arboretum Trail is a 2.3-mile-long trail that winds through a lush forest and showcases over 80 species of trees and plants. It is an easy trail suitable for all ages and abilities.

Best Things to Do in Everett (Washington): Activities and Tours

Whale Watching Tour

Everett is located near the migration paths of gray and humpback whales, making it an ideal location for whale watching. Several tour companies offer whale watching tours, which typically last 3-4 hours and include a knowledgeable guide and stunning views of the Puget Sound.

XFINITY Arena

The XFINITY Arena is a state-of-the-art event center that hosts concerts, sporting events, and entertainment shows. It is also home to the Everett Silvertips, a junior ice hockey team. Visitors can check out the schedule and purchase tickets to upcoming events on the arena’s website.

Historic Everett Theatre

The Historic Everett Theatre is a renovated 1901 theatre that hosts live theatrical performances, movies, and concerts. It has also been used as a filming location for several movies and television shows. Visitors can check out the schedule and purchase tickets on the theatre’s website.

Week-Long Itinerary for Everett (Washington)

Day 1: Explore the Imagine Children’s Museum and visit the Historic Flight Foundation. Day 2: Hike the Everett Arboretum Trail and then bike the Lowell Riverfront Trail. Day 3: Take a whale watching tour and relax at Jetty Island. Day 4: Visit the Schack Art Center and grab a bite at one of the numerous local eateries. Day 5: Watch a Silvertips game at the XFINITY Arena and then catch a movie at the Historic Everett Theatre. Day 6: Explore Forest Park and have a picnic. Day 7: Take the Interurban Trail to Boeing Creek Park and then venture out to Mill Creek Town Center for shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Everett (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

Fish and Chips

Everett is known for its fresh seafood, particularly fish and chips. Local favorites include Ivar’s Seafood Bar and The Totem Family Fish House.

Burger

For a hearty meal, grab a burger at The Old Spaghetti Factory or Brooklyn Bros. Pizzeria.

Vietnamese Pho

Vietnamese Pho is a popular dish in Everett, with several restaurants offering their own variations. Try Garlic Jim’s Famous Gourmet Pizza or The Pho Spot.

Local Beverages

Coffee

The Pacific Northwest is known for its delicious coffee, and Everett is no exception. Try Zippy’s Java Lounge, Caffe Ladro, or Soul Work Coffee.

Craft Beer

Everett has several breweries that offer unique craft beers, including Crucible Brewing and Lazy Boy Brewing. Try their seasonal selections or classic favorites.

Where to Stay in Everett (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Western Cascadia Inn

The Best Western Cascadia Inn is a family-friendly hotel located in the heart of Everett. It offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

Travelodge by Wyndham Everett City Center

The Travelodge by Wyndham Everett City Center is a budget-friendly option located near downtown Everett. It offers cozy rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.

Totem Tree Motel

The Totem Tree Motel is a charming mid-century motel that has been renovated to provide modern amenities while maintaining its nostalgic charm. It features comfortable rooms, a garden, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Everett (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fresh Seafood

Bring home the taste of Everett by purchasing fresh seafood from local markets, such as the Fisherman’s Market or T&T Seafood.

Handmade Crafts

The Schack Art Center gift shop offers handmade crafts from local artisans, including glass art and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Everett (Washington)

Everett prides itself on its local culture and customs, which include a love for nature, music, and community. Visitors should respect these values and be courteous to the locals.

Best Time to Visit Everett (Washington)

The best time to visit Everett is from June to September, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Everett (Washington)

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and craft beer.
  • Explore the city’s natural beauty.
  • Respect local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm local wildlife.
  • Ignore traffic and pedestrian signals.
  • Make excessive noise in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Everett (Washington)

Everett is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take precautions, such as avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Everett (Washington)

Everett has several hospitals and medical clinics for emergencies or healthcare needs. Visitors should also bring sunscreen and wear protective clothing during outdoor activities.

Getting to Everett (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Everett (Washington)

The closest airport to Everett is Paine Field Airport, which is served by Alaska Airlines and United Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Everett (Washington)

Everett Station is a transit center that offers local and regional bus service, including Greyhound and Amtrak.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Everett or take a ferry from nearby Whidbey Island.

How to Get Around in Everett (Washington)

Everett is a walkable city with several bike trails and sidewalks. Visitors can also take the local bus or hail a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Everett (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Seattle

Seattle is about 30 miles south of Everett and offers a range of attractions, from the Space Needle to Pike Place Market.

Whidbey Island

Whidbey Island is a scenic destination located just a ferry ride away from Everett. It offers outdoor activities, wineries, and charming towns.

Conclusion

Everett is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, offering natural beauty, cultural attractions, and a vibrant food scene. Whether you’re a lover of the outdoors or a fan of the arts, Everett is the perfect destination for your next trip.
