Introduction to Arlington (Washington)

Arlington, situated in North Sound, Puget Sound, Washington, is a charming small town known for its rich history, picturesque parks, and stunning outdoor activities. With its friendly locals, outdoor adventures, and fantastic food scene, Arlington has everything you need for a perfect vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Arlington (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arlington has a rich cultural and historical heritage that has resulted in several museums and historical sites worth visiting. These include:

  • Stillaguamish Valley Pioneer Museum: Explore the rich history of the Stillaguamish Valley through the museum’s collection of photographs, documents, artifacts, and tools.
  • Haller House: A well-preserved 19th-century mansion, Haller House reflects the lifestyle and culture of early pioneers in Arlington.
  • Arlington Aviation Museum: A fine collection of aircraft and aviation artifacts, the Arlington Aviation Museum, attracts aviation enthusiasts and historians.

Natural Attractions of Arlington (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arlington’s beautiful natural surroundings are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and wildlife watching. Here are some of the natural attractions in Arlington:

  • Centennial Trail: A popular hiking and biking route that traverses for 29 miles through beautiful scenery. The trail is flat, with several amenities, making it ideal for families.
  • Lake Ki: A serene lake with beautiful hiking trails and opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding. Bring a picnic and enjoy the beauty of Lake Ki.
  • River Meadows County Park: A peaceful park with breathtaking views of the river, River Meadows County Park is ideal for a leisurely picnic or a nature walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arlington (Washington)

Arlington is renowned for its stunning hikes and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in Arlington:

  • White Horse Trail: A 27-mile trail that takes hikers and bikers through beautiful natural landscapes with stops at Darrington and Arlington.
  • Oso Loop Trail: A serene 5-mile trail that takes bikers along the river’s edge, passing small waterfalls and wooded areas.
  • Jim Creek Trail: A scenic trail that traverses for 13.1 miles, is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many picturesque spots and rugged landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Arlington (Washington): Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to explore in Arlington to suit every taste:

  • Waide Park Splash Pad: A fun activity for families, the Waide Park Splash Pad is a fun water playground with various water games and features that children will love.
  • Arlington Farmers’ Market: This market is a fantastic venue to sample local produce and regional cuisine, as well as purchase handmade crafts.
  • Wine Tours: Washington state is renowned for its wine, and Arlington has numerous wineries that offer tasting tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arlington (Washington)

Here is a recommended week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your vacation:

Day 1: Explore the Stillaguamish Valley Pioneer Museum in the morning, followed by a hike on the beautiful Centennial Trail in the afternoon.

Day 2: Enjoy Lake Ki in the morning and spend the afternoon touring the Arlington Farmers' Market.

Day 3: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the beautiful Lake Ki in the morning. Spend the afternoon relaxing at Haller Park, with its picturesque river views.

Day 4: Visit the Arlington Aviation Museum in the morning, followed by a scenic hike on the Jim Creek Trail in the afternoon.

Day 5: Explore the Little Mountain Park in the morning, with its many hiking trails. Spend the afternoon touring local wineries, sampling some of the region's famous wines.

Day 6: Spend the day at River Meadows County Park, enjoying the peaceful river views and picnicking.

Day 7: Wrap up your visit to Arlington with a leisurely bike ride along the stunning Oso Loop Trail in the morning. Spend the afternoon shopping for souvenirs in the town's historic district.

Food and Drink Scene of Arlington (Washington)

Arlington is renowned for an exciting food and drink scene, which includes several must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked salmon: Arlington’s proximity to the Pacific Ocean has made it a mecca for seafood lovers, with smoked salmon being a favorite among foodies.
  • Huckleberry preserves: This unique berry, commonly found in the Pacific Northwest, makes an excellent spread for toast or a topping for dessert.
  • Rainier cherries: These cherries, grown worldwide but known for their succulent sweetness ideal for snacking or topping.
  • Cedar plank salmon: Another salmon dish worth mentioning, Cedar plank salmon is a Pacific Northwest favorite that features salmon slowly grilled to smoky perfection on cedar wood planks.

Local Beverages

  • Washington wine: Washington state is renowned for its wine industry, and Arlington has numerous wineries that offer tasting tours.
  • Local beer: Arlington’s microbreweries have gained a national reputation for their unique offerings, perfect for beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Arlington (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arlington has several options for visitors when it comes to accommodations. These include:

  • Quality Inn Arlington: With free breakfast, an indoor heated swimming pool, and a hot tub, Quality Inn Arlington is a popular choice for those seeking comfort and convenience.
  • Inn at Port Gardner: A tastefully decorated bed and breakfast with a picturesque garden, the Inn at Port Gardner is ideal for couples and those seeking a romantic atmosphere.
  • Arlington Motor Inn: A low-cost but comfortable hotel, the Arlington Motor Inn is popular with budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Arlington (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home? Look no further. Here are a few things to purchase in Arlington:

  • Souvenir T-shirt: A must-have for anyone visiting Arlington, souvenir T-shirts are available at most local souvenir shops.
  • Marquess Studios Pottery: One of the region’s most talented artists, Marquess Studios pottery pieces are both beautiful and functional, making them a popular souvenir choice.
  • Maple Syrup: Arlington is known for its flavorful maple syrup, which can be purchased at most local stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Arlington (Washington)

Arlington’s local culture and customs are an important part of the destination experience. Visitors are encouraged to embrace local customs, immerse themselves in the local culture and learn as much about the area’s history and unique attractions as possible.

Best Time to Visit Arlington (Washington)

Arlington is an year-round destination, but visitors are recommended to visit between July and September for the best weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arlington (Washington)

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the local hiking and biking routes; they offer a great way to experience nature at its finest.
  • Do try the local cuisine and wines; they are an essential part of the town’s heritage.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute in the parks or natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip when eating out.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arlington (Washington)

Arlington is a safe place to visit, with most of the crime-related issues being petty thefts. Visitors are advised to take precautions with their personal belongings, be aware of their surrounding areas, and report any suspicious activity to the local authorities.

Health in Arlington (Washington)

Visitors to Arlington must ensure they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations and be cautious when consuming tap water. Those with pre-existing health conditions must ensure they have adequate health insurance and medication in case of a medical emergency.

Getting to Arlington (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Arlington via air or ground transport. Here are some of the transport options available:

Airports Near Arlington (Washington)

  • Bellingham International Airport: 86 miles to the north of Arlington, Bellingham International Airport is a great option for visitors coming from the north or Canada.
  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport: 53 miles south of Arlington, the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport is the most convenient option for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Arlington (Washington)

  • The Oso General Store: Approximately 13 miles east of Arlington, The Oso General Store is a Greyhound bus stop and serves as a connection to Seattle and Vancouver.

Other Options

  • Driving: Travelers can rent a car and explore Arlington and the surrounding areas at their own pace.
  • Train: Amtrak runs a service through Seattle, where travelers can board buses that connect to Arlington.

How to Get Around in Arlington (Washington)

Driving around Arlington is recommended as public transportation can be limited. Visitors can rent a car or use ride-sharing services to get around the town.

Where to Go Next from Arlington (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • North Cascades National Park: A three-hour drive from Arlington, North Cascades National Park, is a perfect option for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and camping opportunities.
  • San Juan Islands: A two-hour drive and ferry ride from Arlington, the San Juan Islands, is an idyllic destination known for its whale watching and kayaking.
  • Seattle: A one-hour drive from Arlington, Seattle is a large city with a vibrant arts and culture scene, fascinating attractions, and an excellent food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Arlington, Washington, is a fantastic destination for those seeking nature, outdoor adventures, and a thriving food culture. Visitors can explore the town’s unique attractions, engage with the local culture, and experience the town’s rich historical heritage. Plan your trip to Arlington today and create memories that will last a lifetime.
