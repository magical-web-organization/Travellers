Introduction to Edmonds

Located in the North Sound region of Puget Sound, Edmonds is a charming town in Washington, United States. This beautiful city is known for its historic downtown area, stunning waterfront views, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Edmonds is an ideal destination for all ages and interests. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and where to eat in Edmonds.

List of Places to Visit in Edmonds

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Edmonds is a cultural hub in North Sound, offering visitors a chance to understand the area’s rich history and unique culture. Here are some of the must-visit cultural sites in Edmonds:

  • Edmonds Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of Edmonds, from the time of early Native American settlers to the present day.
  • Cascadia Art Museum: This museum displays the best of Northwest art and highlights the contributions of Northwest artists.
  • Edmonds Center for the Arts: This center presents a variety of performing arts events, from music to theater to dance.

Natural Attractions of Edmonds: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Edmonds is blessed with natural beauty, thanks to its location near the Puget Sound. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Edmonds:

  • Brackett’s Landing North: This park is located on the waterfront, offering stunning views of the Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.
  • Yost Park: This 48-acre park has hiking trails and shaded picnic areas, making it the perfect spot for a family day out.
  • Edmonds Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is a haven for bird enthusiasts, with over 150 species of birds that make their home in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edmonds

Edmonds has a range of hiking and biking routes to choose from, suiting all levels of fitness and preference. Here are some top hiking and biking trails to explore in Edmonds:

  • Meadowdale Beach Park Trail: This 1.7-mile easy hike takes you through the forest and offers views of the Puget Sound.
  • Big Gulch Trail: This 2.6-mile moderate hike offers stunning views of the city and the Sound.
  • Interurban Trail: This 4-mile paved trail is perfect for bikers, joggers, and walkers alike.

Best Things to Do in Edmonds: Activities and Tours

Edmonds offers a range of activities and tours to suit all interests and age-groups. Here are some of the top activities and tours to experience in Edmonds:

  • Edmonds Marsh Tour: Join an experienced guide for a tour of the Edmonds Marsh, exploring the important flora and fauna of the area.
  • Whale Watching: Take a tour to spot the region’s resident Orca whales from the Puget Sound.
  • Crabbing with locals: Join a crabbing tour and learn to catch some of the area’s famous Dungeness crabs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edmonds

Day 1: Explore Downtown Edmonds - Visit the iconic fountain, coffee shops, art galleries, and unique small boutiques. The Edmonds Farmer’s Market is every Saturday April through October.

Day 2: Visit Brackett’s Landing North - Hike the Cascade Trail, soak up the sun in the park, or have a picnic. Rent a paddleboard or kayak if you want to take to the water.

Day 3: Enjoy the Edmonds Marina - Walk the marina and watch the boats come and go. Insider’s tip: don’t miss Red Twig for your coffee and pastry fix!

Day 4: Make a day trip to the San Juan Islands - The stunning islands are only a short ferry ride away and offer a range of activities, from hiking and whale watching to exploring unique local art.

Day 5: Take a Whale Watching Tour - Experience the excitement of spotting Orcas in their natural habitat.

Day 6: Go Crabbing with locals - Learn about the local crabbing techniques, and cook up your Dungeness crabs for dinner!

Day 7: Enjoy a scenic drive - Take a drive up to the Olympic Mountains and enjoy the stunning views.

Food and Drink Scene of Edmonds

Must-Try Dishes

Edmonds is located in an abundant region for fishing and agriculture, which means delicious local cuisine, farm-to-table, and seasonal dining. Here are some must-try dishes in Edmonds:

  • Fresh Seafood: Enjoy locally sourced seafood, including Dungeness crab, oysters, and salmon, at Anthony’s Home Port or Arnies.
  • Thai cuisine: Get your spice fix at Thai Cottage or Prima Bistro.
  • Wood-fired pizza: Try the delicious wood-fired pizza at Girardi’s Osteria.

Local Beverages

Edmonds is famous for its coffee and craft beer. Here are some of the must-visit coffee shops and breweries in Edmonds:

  • Cafe Louvre: This cozy cafe offers a variety of delicious coffees and pastries.
  • Salish Sea Brewing Company: This brewery is perfect for a relaxed evening with friends, enjoying a pint of their finest craft beers.
  • Walnut Street Coffee: A great spot for coffee on the go and a light breakfast or lunch.

Where to Stay in Edmonds: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Edmonds provides a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top places to stay in Edmonds:

  • Best Western Plus Edmonds Harbor Inn: Located in the heart of downtown Edmonds, this hotel has a wide range of amenities, including a free breakfast, pool, and hot tub.
  • Hotel Indigo Seattle Edmonds: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Edmonds and offers luxurious amenities, such as a spa and onsite restaurant.
  • Five Bays B & B: This charming bed and breakfast is perfect for a romantic getaway or small family.

What to Buy in Edmonds: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Edmonds has plenty of shops and boutiques that offer unique and interesting souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best local products to bring home from Edmonds:

  • Handmade pottery: Visit the gallery at Edmonds Artworks and pick up some unique pottery pieces.
  • Local honey: Beekeeper’s Cottage offers delicious local honey in various flavors.
  • Handmade soaps: Pick up luxury handmade soaps from Nama’s Candy.

Local Culture and Customs of Edmonds

Edmonds is known for its laid-back and friendly people. Visitors should keep in mind the town’s sustainability and eco-friendly mandates. Local events include March for Science, Fourth of July Fireworks and Parades, and the Edmonds Art Festival. Be sure to smile and say hi to locals when you’re walking around, and respect the area’s nature and history.

Best Time to Visit Edmonds

The best time to visit Edmonds is from April to October when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are plentiful. Summers (June to September) are warm and dry, perfect for hiking, swimming, and outdoor activities. Winters (November to March) are mild but rainy, yet Christmas light-watching makes chilly walks worth it.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edmonds

Do:

  • Kayak on the Puget Sound
  • Visit local coffee shops
  • Visit nearby San Juan Islands on a day trip
  • Respect the environment

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t touch wildlife
  • Don’t feed wildlife
  • Don’t smoke in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Edmonds

Generally, Edmonds is a safe city. However, visitors should keep in mind the following common-sense safety tips:

  • Always lock your car and hotel rooms
  • Avoid walking alone at night in quiet, unlit areas

Health in Edmonds

Edmonds has excellent medical facilities. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Edmonds: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edmonds

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA): This is the nearest international airport, located about 30 miles south of Edmonds.

Bus Stations Near Edmonds

  • Amtrak-Edmonds Station: This station is located in downtown Edmonds, close to major attractions and hotels.

Other Options

  • Driving: Edmonds is easily accessible by car, with good highways linking the town to nearby cities.
  • Ferry: Visitors can take the Edmonds-Kingston Ferry or Edmonds Ferry to the San Juan Islands and beyond.

How to Get Around in Edmonds

Explore Edmonds by foot, bicycle, or rental car. Edmonds is a walkable city, with rental bikes, including electric bikes, available at several locations. The Edmonds station bus routes offer transportation around the city, to nearby bus stops, and to the ferry terminal.

Where to Go Next from Edmonds: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Edmonds is located in an ideal position to explore other nearby destinations. Here are some of the top places to visit from Edmonds:

  • Mukilteo: Located just south of Edmonds, Mukilteo offers visitors stunning views of Puget Sound. Don’t miss the Mukilteo Lighthouse Park.
  • Seattle: Drive south to visit the biggest city in the Pacific Northwest, Seattle. Explore the famous Pike Place Market, the Space Needle, and the Seattle Gum Wall.
  • San Juan Islands: Take an exciting ferry ride from Edmonds to the San Juan Islands and explore the stunning natural beauty of the islands.

Conclusion

Edmonds is an ideal destination for anyone looking for a relaxing and enjoyable vacation, offering stunning natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and a rich culture and history. From hiking and biking trails to museums and art galleries, there is something for everyone to experience in this beautiful town.
