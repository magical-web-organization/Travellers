Introduction to Steilacoom

Located in South Sound, in Puget Sound, in Washington (state), in Pacific Northwest, in the United States of America, and in North America, Steilacoom is a hidden gem for travelers searching for an authentic Pacific Northwest experience. This small town, with a population of around 6,000 people, has a rich history dating back to the 19th century and a beautiful natural landscape. In this ultimate guide, we will explore what makes Steilacoom so special and provide the best tips for enjoying your trip to this quaint and charming destination.

List of Places to Visit in Steilacoom

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or natural beauty, Steilacoom has a wide range of attractions to explore. Here is a list of the top places to visit while in town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Steilacoom Tribal Cultural Center & Museum - learn about the history, culture, and traditions of the Steilacoom tribe, the town’s original inhabitants.
  • Steilacoom Historical Museum - housed in a restored 1857 homestead cabin, this museum showcases the history of Steilacoom and its role in Washington State’s early development.
  • Nellie’s Restaurant - a popular destination for foodies, Nellie’s Restaurant is Steilacoom’s oldest restaurant, serving up classic American cuisine since 1937.

Natural Attractions of Steilacoom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sunset Beach Park - a beautiful sandy beach with views of Mt. Rainier and the Olympic Mountains, perfect for swimming, picnicking, and watching the sunset.
  • Pioneer Orchard Park - a 1-acre historic orchard with a variety of fruit trees, a covered picnic area, and playground.
  • Chambers Creek Regional Park - located just a short drive away, this expansive park offers hiking and biking trails, a championship golf course, and stunning views of the Puget Sound.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Steilacoom

One of the best ways to experience Steilacoom’s natural beauty is by hitting the trails. Here are some of our top picks for hiking and biking routes in and around town.

  • Steilacoom to Chambers Bay Trail - a 3.5-mile trail that follows the Puget Sound shoreline and connects Steilacoom to Chambers Creek Regional Park.
  • Lafayette Trail - a 3-mile trail that winds through historic neighborhoods and natural areas.
  • Saltars Point Beach Trail - a short 0.4-mile trail that leads to a secluded beach with stunning views of the Sound.

Best Things to Do in Steilacoom: Activities and Tours

Steilacoom offers a variety of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained throughout their stay. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Steilacoom Ferry Ride - take a scenic ferry ride to Anderson Island and explore this peaceful and secluded destination.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding - rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the Puget Sound at your own pace.
  • Steilacoom Chocolates & Coffee - indulge your sweet tooth with delicious handmade chocolates and fresh roasted coffee.

Week-Long Itinerary for Steilacoom

If you’re planning on spending a week in Steilacoom, here’s a sample itinerary to help you get the most out of your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Steilacoom and explore the town’s historic downtown area.
  • Day 2: Visit the Steilacoom Historical Museum and take a walk around Pioneer Orchard Park.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Sunset Beach Park, swimming, sunbathing, and having a picnic.
  • Day 4: Take the Steilacoom Ferry to Anderson Island and spend the day exploring this peaceful destination.
  • Day 5: Walk or bike the Steilacoom to Chambers Bay Trail and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.
  • Day 6: Visit the Steilacoom Tribal Cultural Center & Museum and have lunch at Nellie’s Restaurant.
  • Day 7: Say goodbye to Steilacoom and head home.

Food and Drink Scene of Steilacoom

Steilacoom offers a variety of dining options, from classic American fare to fresh seafood. Here are some of our recommended must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips - a Pacific Northwest classic, Steilacoom’s fish and chips are some of the best around. Check out Steilacoom Pub & Grill for a delicious plate.
  • Oysters - Steilacoom is located near some of the best oyster beds in the country. Try some fresh local oysters at Topside Bar & Grill.
  • Burger - for a classic American meal, head to Nellie’s Restaurant and try their famous burger.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Steilacoom is home to several breweries, including the popular Top Rung Brewing. Check out their taproom for a selection of delicious beers.
  • Coffee - Steilacoom Chocolates & Coffee offers fresh roasted coffee and espresso drinks, perfect for a morning pick-me-up.
  • Wine - head to the nearby town of DuPont and visit the Ste. Michelle Wine Estates Tasting Room for a selection of local wines.

Where to Stay in Steilacoom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Steilacoom may be a small town, there are still plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Inn at Saltar’s Point - a charming bed and breakfast located just steps from the Sound with stunning water views.
  • Chambers Bay RV Resort - if you’re traveling by RV, this resort offers full hookups and beautiful views of Chambers Bay.
  • Cottage by the Sound - a cozy guesthouse with a private entrance and beautiful gardens, located just a short walk from downtown Steilacoom.

What to Buy in Steilacoom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home, check out these local shops:

  • Little House Mercantile - a boutique gift shop featuring handmade items from local artisans, including jewelry, candles, and home decor.
  • Madrona Books & Gifts - a cozy bookstore with a selection of new and used books, as well as locally made gifts and souvenirs.
  • Steilacoom Tribal Cultural Center & Museum Gift Shop - purchase authentic Native American art, jewelry, and crafts to support the Steilacoom tribe.
