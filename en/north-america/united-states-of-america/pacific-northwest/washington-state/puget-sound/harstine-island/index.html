Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Harstine Island

If you’re looking for a peaceful, nature-filled getaway in the Pacific Northwest, Harstine Island in Puget Sound, Washington, is the perfect destination. From scenic views to outdoor adventures, this island offers something for every type of traveler. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Harstine Island with ease and make the most out of your visit.

Introduction to Harstine Island

Harstine Island is located in Puget Sound and is a part of Mason County in Washington State. Its lush forests and stunning coastline make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The island covers an area of 15 square miles and is home to a small community of friendly residents who take pride in their island’s beauty and charm.

List of Places to Visit in Harstine Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Harstine Island is rich in history and culture with many museums and historical places to visit. Some of the must-visit places on the island include:

  • Harstine Island Historical Society Museum - Learn about the island’s history and heritage at this museum, which features exhibits on Native American culture, early settlers, and the logging industry.
  • Jarrell Cove State Park - This park is one of the oldest state parks in Washington and has a deep connection to the island’s maritime history.
  • The Church of Saint Nicholas of Myra - This charming little church was built in 1905 and is a must-see for anyone interested in historical architecture.

Natural Attractions of Harstine Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Harstine Island’s stunning natural beauty is the main attraction for many visitors. From hiking trails to kayaking, there are many outdoor activities to enjoy on the island. Here are some of the best natural attractions on Harstine Island:

  • Tolmie State Park - Located on the eastern part of Harstine Island, this state park offers stunning views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains. It has over 3 miles of hiking trails and is a popular spot for fishing, clamming, and birdwatching.
  • Oakland Bay Historical Park - This park is a fantastic place to go kayaking or canoeing along the shores of Oakland Bay. It also has several hiking trails and a playground for families with children.
  • Harstine Island State Park - This state park is only accessible by boat and is a great place for camping, hiking, and fishing. It has several primitive campsites and a boat ramp for launching kayaks and canoes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harstine Island

Harstine Island has many hiking and biking trails that vary in difficulty, length, and terrain. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Midden Creek Trail - This 2.6-mile trail is rated as easy and takes you through lush forests and over a boardwalk that spans a marsh area.
  • Mount Harstine Trail - This 6.5-mile trail is rated as moderate and takes you to the highest point on the island, which offers stunning panoramic views of Mount Rainier, Puget Sound, and the Olympic Mountains.
  • Jarrell Cove Trail - This 1-mile trail is rated as easy and takes you through a dense forest and over a bridge that crosses a small creek.

Best Things to Do in Harstine Island: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available on Harstine Island that are perfect for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do on the island:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing - Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the shores of Oakland Bay and Puget Sound. Many local companies offer rentals and guided tours.
  • Fishing and Clamming - Harstine Island is a great place for fishing and clamming, and there are many guided tours that can take you to the best spots.
  • Beachcombing - Explore the beaches of Harstine Island and discover treasures like shells, sea glass, and driftwood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harstine Island

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Harstine Island, this itinerary will help you make the most of your time on the island:

Day 1: Check-in to your accommodations and visit the Harstine Island Historical Society Museum.

Day 2: Spend the day at Tolmie State Park, hiking the trails, and exploring the beach.

Day 3: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the shores of Oakland Bay.

Day 4: Visit The Church of Saint Nicholas of Myra and hike the Midden Creek Trail.

Day 5: Take a guided fishing or clamming tour.

Day 6: Hike the Mount Harstine Trail and enjoy stunning views from the summit.

Day 7: Spend the day at Harstine Island State Park, camping, fishing, and hiking.

Food and Drink Scene of Harstine Island

Harstine Island has a small but vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood - Harstine Island is known for its seafood, and local restaurants serve up some of the freshest fish and shellfish in the region.
  • Clam Chowder - Many restaurants on the island serve delicious clam chowder made with locally caught clams.
  • Cheese and Wine - Harstine Island has several wineries and cheese-makers that produce world-class artisanal products.

Local Beverages

  • Harstine Island Lemonade - This refreshing beverage is made with local blackberries and raspberries and is perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Local Wines - Harstine Island is home to several wineries, including the popular Harstine Island Winery, which produces delicious white and red wines.
  • Craft Beer - Many local breweries in the area serve up delicious craft beer, including Harstine Island Brewery and Rainy Daze Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Harstine Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harstine Island has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Harstine Island RV and Campground - This family-owned campground offers tent and RV sites, as well as cabins for rent.
  • The Island House - This cozy guesthouse offers charming rooms and breathtaking views of Puget Sound.
  • Harstine Island Guest House - This spacious vacation home offers full amenities and can accommodate up to 12 guests.

What to Buy in Harstine Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Harstine Island has plenty to offer. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Artisanal Cheese - Several local cheese-makers produce high-quality artisanal cheese, including the acclaimed Harstine Island Goat Cheese.
  • Handmade Crafts - Harstine Island has many artisans who produce handmade crafts like pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Fresh Seafood - Take home locally caught seafood like salmon, oysters, and clams that are some of the freshest in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Harstine Island

Harstine Island has a small but tightly-knit community that embraces their island’s natural beauty and heritage. Visitors should be respectful of the island’s culture and customs, including the following:

  • Environmental Consciousness - Harstine Island residents are proud of their island’s natural beauty and want to maintain it. Visitors should adhere to the island’s strict waste-management and environmental practices.
  • Slow Pace of Life - Harstine Island is a place where visitors can slow down and embrace the island’s relaxed pace of life. Visitors should embrace this custom and take time to unwind and enjoy the slower pace of the island.

Best Time to Visit Harstine Island

The best time to visit Harstine Island is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, the island is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy seasonal activities like clamming and fishing in the fall and winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harstine Island

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural beauty and follow local environmental practices.
  • Embrace the island’s slow pace of life and relax.
  • Take part in outdoor activities like kayaking, fishing, and hiking.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Speed or drive recklessly on the island’s narrow roads.
  • Ignore local customs and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harstine Island

Overall, Harstine Island is a safe destination for visitors, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be cautious while hiking and avoid dangerous areas near the water’s edge.
  • Watch out for wildlife like deer and raccoons that can be a road hazard.
  • Be aware of these island’s limited healthcare resources and have a basic first-aid kit at your disposal.

Health in Harstine Island

Harstine Island does not have any major health risks, but visitors should be aware of the island’s remote location and limited medical facilities. It’s a good idea to bring any necessary medication with you and to have travel insurance in case of unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Harstine Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Harstine Island is accessible only by boat or the state ferry system. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Harstine Island

The nearest airport to Harstine Island is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is 70 miles away. From there, you can rent a car or take a bus to Olympia, where you can catch a ferry to the island.

Bus Stations Near Harstine Island

The Olympia Transit Center is the nearest bus station to Harstine Island. From there, you can take public transportation or a taxi to the nearest ferry terminal.

Other Options

If you have your own boat, you can easily reach Harstine Island from the surrounding areas. Another option is to rent a car and drive to the nearest ferry terminal in Olympia, where you can catch a ferry to Harstine Island.

How to Get Around in Harstine Island

Harstine Island is small enough to explore by foot or bicycle, but many visitors choose to rent a car for added convenience. Kayaking and canoeing are also great ways to explore the island from the water.

Where to Go Next from Harstine Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harstine Island is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas of the Pacific Northwest. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Olympic National Park - A short ferry ride will take you to the Olympic Peninsula, where you can explore the breathtaking landscapes of Olympic National Park.
  • Hood Canal - This scenic waterway is a popular destination for kayaking and fishing and is home to several charming towns worth exploring.
  • Seattle - Less than 2 hours away by car, Seattle is a must-visit destination for any Pacific Northwest traveler.

Conclusion

Harstine Island is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest, offering stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and a relaxed pace of life. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip with ease. From the best places to visit to local customs and culture, you’ll be prepared to make the most of your visit to Harstine Island.
