Introduction to Freeland (Washington)

Freeland is a charming town located on Whidbey Island, in Puget Sound, Washington. This small but vibrant community is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and great food scene.

With its stunning natural setting, Freeland offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, boating, kayaking, and fishing. Visitors can also explore a variety of museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks, or sample delicious local cuisine and wines.

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway, an adventurous outdoor experience, or a cultural immersion, Freeland has something for every type of traveler. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive tour guide for Freeland, including the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, what to buy, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Freeland (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Freeland Art Studios

Freeland Art Studios is a collection of creative spaces and studios located in the heart of Freeland. Visitors can walk around the studios and galleries to see local artists in action. Some of the studios offer art classes and workshops for visitors of different ages and skill levels, allowing them to learn a new skill or create their own masterpiece.

South Whidbey Historical Society

Located at the corner of Highway 525 and Crawford Road, the South Whidbey Historical Society is a great place to learn about the island’s history and culture. The museum features exhibits and displays on early settlers, native inhabitants, and the history of the island’s logging and fishing industries. Visitors can also explore the grounds and view the historic buildings and structures, such as the 1904 Dairy Barn – one of the oldest remaining barns in the area.

Natural Attractions of Freeland (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Double Bluff Beach

Double Bluff Beach is a popular spot for beachcombing and picnicking. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains while taking a leisurely stroll along the sandy beach. The beach is a good place to search for seashells and watch for wildlife, such as seabirds and marine mammals.

Holmes Harbor

Holmes Harbor is a serene bay located in the northern part of the island and offers some of the most beautiful views of the natural surroundings. Visitors can rent a kayak, paddleboard, or boat to explore the bay and its abundant marine life, including seals, otters, and herons. The area is also popular for fishing, crabbing, and clamming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Freeland (Washington)

Freeland Trail System

The Freeland Trail System is a network of trails suitable for hiking, jogging, and mountain biking. The trails wind through beautiful forests, meadows, and wetlands, offering visitors an opportunity to see fauna and flora up close. The paths are relatively easy and well-maintained, making them ideal for all levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Freeland (Washington): Activities and Tours

Whidbey Island Kayaking Company

Whidbey Island Kayaking Company offers guided kayaking tours of the island’s coastline and waterways. Visitors can enjoy a scenic paddle through tidal lagoons, saltwater estuaries, and quiet bays while observing the abundant marine life and bird species that call the area home.

Whidbey Island Distillery

Whidbey Island Distillery is a small, family-owned distillery that produces artisanal spirits from locally sourced ingredients. Visitors can take a guided tour of the distillery to learn about the distillation process and sample some of the award-winning spirits, such as Loganberry Liqueur and Blackberry Liqueur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Freeland (Washington)

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Freeland:

Day 1: Explore Freeland

Get acquainted with the town by strolling around the center and checking out the local shops, galleries, and restaurants. If you have time, visit the Freeland Art Studios and the South Whidbey Historical Society.

Day 2: Outdoor Adventure

Take a hike on one of the trails in the Freeland Trail System or rent a kayak to explore Holmes Harbor.

Day 3: Whidbey Island Wineries

Spend the day visiting some of the island’s wineries, such as Spoiled Dog Winery or Island Winery.

Day 4: Langley Day Trip

Take a day trip to the charming town of Langley, located about 30 minutes from Freeland, and explore its art galleries, shops, and restaurants.

Day 5: Whidbey Island Kayaking Company Tour

Join a guided kayaking tour of the island’s waterways with Whidbey Island Kayaking Company.

Day 6: Farmers Market and Beach Day

Spend the morning at the local farmers market, picking up fresh produce and homemade goodies. In the afternoon, head to Double Bluff Beach for a picnic and a walk along the shore.

Day 7: Departure Day

Before you leave, check out some of the local art galleries and shops to pick up some souvenirs and gifts.

Food and Drink Scene of Freeland (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

Saltwater Fish House and Oyster Bar

Saltwater Fish House and Oyster Bar is a local favorite and offers some of the freshest seafood dishes on the island. Try the oysters on the half shell, fish and chips, or the clam chowder – all made with local ingredients and served with a smile.

Whidbey Pies Cafe

Located on the roadside in Greenbank, Whidbey Pies Cafe has been a local institution for over two decades. Their signature pies – made with locally grown fruits and berries – are the perfect sweet treat after a long day of exploring.

Local Beverages

Mutiny Bay Distillery

Mutiny Bay Distillery is a craft distillery producing small-batch apple brandy, cider and perry from fruit grown on Whidbey Island. Visit their tasting room in Freeland to sample their award-winning spirits.

Ott & Murphy Wines

Ott & Murphy Wines is a small winery producing wines in the Rhone and Bordeaux styles. Their tasting room in Langley features a cozy atmosphere and great views of the surrounding vineyards.

Where to Stay in Freeland (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Country Cottage of Langley

Country Cottage of Langley is a cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Langley. The rooms are beautifully decorated and feature all the amenities you’d expect from a luxury hotel. Guests can enjoy a leisurely breakfast on the terrace overlooking the garden.

Harbor Inn

Harbor Inn is a family-owned lodging located in the heart of Freeland. The rooms are spacious and clean, and the owners go out of their way to make guests feel at home. The inn is located close to all the local attractions and restaurants.

What to Buy in Freeland (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Freeland Art Studios Gallery

The Freeland Art Studios Gallery features a variety of artwork from over 30 local artists, including painting, pottery, glassworks, and jewelry. Visitors can find unique and beautiful pieces to bring home as a reminder of their trip.

Useless Bay Coffee Company

Useless Bay Coffee Company offers a selection of locally made artisanal products, including coffee beans, granola, and honey. These make the perfect gift for foodies and coffee lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Freeland (Washington)

Freeland is a small community that prides itself on its natural beauty and traditional values. Visitors should respect the local way of life and take care not to disrupt the tranquility of the surroundings. It is also important to be mindful of the wildlife and to follow proper rules and regulations when engaging in outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Freeland (Washington)

The best time to visit Freeland is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. August is the peak tourist season, so be prepared for higher prices and more crowds. The fall months of September and October are also a good time to visit, as the foliage changes to beautiful autumn colors, and the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Freeland (Washington)

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and the wildlife.
  • Explore the local art scene and try the delicious local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or disturb the locals.
  • Leave trash or litter behind when enjoying the outdoors.
  • Speed or drive recklessly on the island’s roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Freeland (Washington)

Freeland is a relatively safe community with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables, as they would in any other town.

Health in Freeland (Washington)

There are some hospitals and medical clinics located on the island, but for serious medical emergencies or specialized treatment, visitors may need to travel to the mainland. It is a good idea to bring any necessary medication and to have a travel insurance policy that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Freeland (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Freeland (Washington)

The nearest major airport to Freeland is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, located about 2 hours away by car. A smaller airport, Whidbey Island Naval Air Station (Ault Field), located in Oak Harbor, is also an option for those traveling by air. Car rental or taxi service is available at both airports.

Bus Stations Near Freeland (Washington)

The closest bus station to Freeland is located in Coupeville, about 20 minutes away by car. The Island Transit bus system provides service to several island towns and destinations, including Freeland.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to travel to Freeland via the ferry service operated by Washington State Ferries that runs between Mukilteo and Clinton. The ferry ride is about 20 minutes long, and reservations are recommended during peak tourist season.

How to Get Around in Freeland (Washington)

The best way to get around in Freeland is by car or bicycle. Visitors can rent a car at one of the airports or in nearby towns, and there are several bike rental shops on the island. The Island Transit buses also provide service to many local destinations, but schedules can be limited.

Where to Go Next from Freeland (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Deception Pass State Park

Located about an hour’s drive from Freeland, Deception Pass State Park is one of the most popular destinations in the area. The park features miles of hiking trails, scenic views of the waterfalls and jagged cliffs, and opportunities for boating and fishing.

Ebey’s Landing National Historical Reserve

Ebey’s Landing is a historic district located on the western shore of Whidbey Island, about 40 minutes from Freeland. The area features many historical landmarks and structures, including the Jacob and Sarah Ebey House – the oldest house on the island.

Conclusion

Freeland is a perfect destination for visitors looking for a relaxing getaway in a stunning natural setting. With its outdoor activities, cultural attractions, great food, and beautiful scenery, Freeland has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to help plan your trip and make the most of your stay on Whidbey Island.
