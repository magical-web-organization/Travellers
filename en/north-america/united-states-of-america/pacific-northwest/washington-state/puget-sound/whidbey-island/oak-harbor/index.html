Introduction to Oak Harbor

Nestled in Whidbey Island, in Puget Sound, Washington, Oak Harbor is a charming coastal town that offers a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and plenty of natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation, Oak Harbor has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to see, and where to stay in Oak Harbor.

List of Places to Visit in Oak Harbor

Here are some of the top places to visit in Oak Harbor.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oak Harbor is filled with rich history and museums that reflect the area’s heritage. Some of the best historical places you can visit here include:

  • PBY Naval Air Museum that showcases the history of WW2 aircraft that were flown at the Naval Air Station Whidbey Island.
  • Oak Harbor Freight House which is a popular landmark for its historical significance as the central business district for Whidbey Island
  • Island County Historical Society Museum showcasing artifacts from Whidbey Island and Camano Island

Natural Attractions of Oak Harbor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oak Harbor offers a variety of outdoor adventures and scenic views, making it a great destination for nature lovers. Some of the top natural attractions here include:

  • Deception Pass State Park that offers an array of outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Windjammer Park which is a waterfront park where you can enjoy a picnic with stunning views of the bay.
  • Fort Ebey State Park which is a perfect spot for hiking, camping, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oak Harbor

Oak Harbor has some of the most exciting hiking and biking trails that will let you explore the beauty of nature. Here are some of the most popular hiking trails:

  • Goose Rock and Mount Erie is a 6.5-mile trail that offers panoramic views of the Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.
  • Hoypus Point Trail is a 2.5-mile trail that goes through the forest to the shoreline and gives you a glimpse of wildlife.
  • Fort Ebey State Park Trails is a 4.6-mile trail that offers beautiful views of the Pacific Ocean and the Strait of Juan de Fuca.

Best Things to Do in Oak Harbor: Activities and Tours

From outdoor activities to cultural experiences, Oak Harbor has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Oak Harbor:

  • Whale Watching Tour that will take you to the best spots where you can witness the Orcas and other marine life.
  • Zip-lining is an adrenaline-filled experience where you can glide through the treetops and witness the mesmerizing beauty of Whidbey Island.
  • Oak Harbor Music Festival is an annual event that takes place in September and showcases the best local and regional talent.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oak Harbor

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will let you explore the best of Oak Harbor:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the PBY-Naval Air Museum and have lunch at Captains Whidbey Inn, a historic hotel and restaurant
Day 2 Go for a hike at Fort Ebey State Park and explore the scenic views
Day 3 Take a tour to Deception Pass on a kayak or hike to the high viewpoints
Day 4 Explore the art scene of Oak Harbor and visit the local galleries
Day 5 Take a day trip to San Juan Island and explore the island’s natural beauty
Day 6 Go for a zip line adventure and enjoy the thrill

Food and Drink Scene of Oak Harbor

Oak Harbor has a variety of local restaurants and bars that offer delicious food and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mussels and Clams are a specialty of the area and can be found at most restaurants.
  • Fish and Chips made with fresh seafood and paired with tartar sauce and fries is a local favorite.
  • Fresh Berries are abundant in the summer season and can be found at various farmers’ markets and stands.

Local Beverages

  • Local Whidbey Island Wine is produced by Whidbey Island Winery, a family-owned vineyard that has been in the area since 1988.
  • Penn Cove Brewing Company offers various artisanal beers with unique flavors and has been a local favorite since 2011.
  • Local Ciders offered at various breweries and bars in town are made from locally sourced fruits.

Where to Stay in Oak Harbor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oak Harbor offers various accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Best Western Plus Oak Harbor offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • Acorn Motor Inn is a budget-friendly option located near the beach and offers clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Coachman Inn & Suites offers spacious rooms with kitchenettes, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.

What to Buy in Oak Harbor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oak Harbor offers many souvenirs that reflect the culture and heritage of Whidbey Island. Some of the unique products that you can bring home include:

  • Handmade Soaps and Candles made by local artisans using natural ingredients.
  • Whidbey Island Coffee is a local coffee roaster that offers various blends and flavors and has been a local staple since 1989.
  • Local Artwork from the galleries and markets that showcase the beauty of the area and the Pacific Northwest.

Local Culture and Customs of Oak Harbor

Oak Harbor has a friendly and welcoming community that values nature, art, and culture. Here are some of the local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for Nature is crucial in Oak Harbor, where people value their natural surroundings deeply.
  • Seattle-like weather can be expected in Oak Harbor, so bring a rain jacket even in the summer.
  • Small town feel is prevalent in the area, and people are friendly and open to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Oak Harbor

The best time to visit Oak Harbor is from late spring to early fall, between May and September. This time of year offers mild temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oak Harbor

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to follow while visiting Oak Harbor:

Do:

  • Hike and explore the natural beauty of Oak Harbor.
  • Try Local Seafood and Wine to get a taste of the area’s culture and heritage.
  • Respect Nature and Wildlife while exploring the outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural surroundings.
  • Skip sunscreen or rain gear as weather can be unpredictable, especially during the summer season.
  • Disrespect the locals by being rude or inconsiderate.

Safety and Health

Oak Harbor is generally a safe and healthy place to visit. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Oak Harbor

  • Take precautions while hiking or exploring the outdoors to avoid accidental injuries or falls.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and avoid hiking during thunderstorms or extreme weather conditions.
  • Be vigilant and keep an eye on your belongings while visiting crowded areas or festivals.

Health in Oak Harbor

  • Drink Plenty of Water while participating in outdoor adventures to avoid dehydration.
  • Wear Sunscreen and insect repellent to avoid sunburn and bug bites.
  • Be aware of food allergies and communicate with servers before ordering.

Getting to Oak Harbor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oak Harbor is located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes north of Seattle, Washington. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Oak Harbor

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport is about 90 minutes from Oak Harbor.
  • Paine Field is located in Everett, Washington, which is 45 minutes from Oak Harbor.

Bus Stations Near Oak Harbor

  • Island Transit Bus Service offers free public transportation services throughout the island for visitors in Oak Harbor.

Other Options

  • Driving is an option to get to Oak Harbor, and parking is available at various locations throughout the town.

How to Get Around in Oak Harbor

Walking, biking, and driving are the most common ways to get around in Oak Harbor. If you don’t have a car, you can take advantage of the free public transportation services offered by Island Transit.

Where to Go Next from Oak Harbor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oak Harbor is located in a highly scenic area that offers many nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular places to visit:

  • Anacortes is a picturesque coastal town that offers beaches and outdoor activities.
  • Lopez Island is a part of the San Juan Islands that offers incredible sunsets and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Seattle is an excellent option for a big-city experience, and it’s just an hour and a half south of Oak Harbor.

Conclusion

Oak Harbor is Washington’s hidden gem, with its natural beauty, rich history, and welcoming community. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just some relaxation, Oak Harbor has everything you need for a memorable vacation. Plan your trip with this guide and get ready to experience the beauty of Puget Sound!
