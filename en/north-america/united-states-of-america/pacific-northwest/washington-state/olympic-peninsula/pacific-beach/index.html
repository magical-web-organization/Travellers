Introduction to Pacific Beach

Pacific Beach is a hidden gem located in the Olympic Peninsula of the Pacific Northwest region, making it an ideal location for outdoor enthusiasts. It is an excellent destination for those seeking stunning ocean views, coastal adventures, and a chance to immerse themselves in nature.

Though Pacific Beach is relatively small, it is home to natural marvels, including vast stretches of sandy beaches, scenic hiking, and biking trails.

List of Places to Visit in Pacific Beach

There is much to explore and discover in Pacific Beach. Here are some of the top attractions, categorized into culturally important and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ocean Crest Resort: It is a historic building dating back to the 1950s. The resort features grand ocean views, a restaurant, and a spa.

  • Seabrook: It is a beach town that has a unique appeal to visitors. The town has a blend of traditional architecture with modern amenities, creating a quaint and charming atmosphere. The town’s colorful houses are worth exploring.

Natural Attractions of Pacific Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Roosevelt Beach: This beach is located just north of Pacific Beach. It offers a beautiful sandy beach, tide pools to explore, and a view of offshore sea stacks.

  • Moclips Beach: The beach is known for its soaring cliffs, sandy beaches, and a sense of isolation from the hustle and bustle of city life.

  • Pacific Beach State Park: The park is located on the beach and has campsites and picnic areas. There are also hiking trails that offer a panoramic view of the ocean.

  • Olympic National Forest: It is a vast expanse of the wilderness, located in the surrounding area. The forest offers extensive hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and an enchanting view of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pacific Beach

Pacific Beach has plenty of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for seasoned hikers and bikers or beginners looking to enjoy the outdoors. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes:

  • Joe Creek Trail: The trail spans over one mile through the Joe Creek area and offers a challenging route for experienced hikers.

  • Pacific Beach Trail: The trail starts on the north end of Pacific Beach State Park and weaves up the bluff, offering a breathtaking view of the Pacific Ocean.

  • Griffiths-Priday Ocean State Park Trails: The park trails span over two miles and offer a fantastic opportunity for novice hikers and bikers to enjoy the outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Pacific Beach: Activities and Tours

Pacific Beach offers visitors an array of fun activities. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Kayaking Tours: Exploring the region by sea is a must-do in Pacific Beach. Visitors can take a guided kayaking tour, which offers an exciting and unique way to explore the coast.

  • Beachcombing: Pacific Beach has plenty of beachcombing opportunities, including hunting for agates, moonstones, and other rocks.

  • Fishing: Visitors can try fishing in Moclips River, Copalis River, or any of the surrounding streams and rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of Pacific Beach

There is an exciting range of food and drink options in Pacific Beach. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: A classic dish, best enjoyed at one of the local seafood restaurants.
  • Burger and Milkshake: Pacific Beach has a fantastic diner called the Seagate, which is famous for its burgers and vintage milkshakes.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Pacific Beach has several award-winning wineries, including Westport Winery and Ocean’s Daughter Distillery.
  • Beer: The area is also home to a few local breweries, such as Bushnell Craft Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Pacific Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pacific Beach has a range of accommodation options, from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Ocean Crest Resort: A historic hotel located on a bluff overlooking the beach. It offers spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a spa.

  • Sand Dollar Condos: It is a lovely self-catering option for those looking to stay in a private apartment close to the ocean.

  • Seabrook Cottage Rentals: It is a unique lodging option offering visitors a chance to stay in a charming beach town while enjoying the comfort of a modern house.

What to Buy in Pacific Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pacific Beach has an extensive collection of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Moonstones: They are a popular rock found on the local beaches, and visitors can purchase exquisite jewelry made from them.

  • Westport Winery Olive Oil and Balsamic Vinegar: Visitors can bring home some olive oil and balsamic vinegar made from the olives grown on the winery property.

Local Culture and Customs of Pacific Beach

Pacific Beach’s culture is heavily influenced by its natural surroundings. The locals are proud of their coastline and protective of their forest. Visitors are encouraged to enjoy the outdoors respectfully and take care not to litter.

Best Time to Visit Pacific Beach

The best time to visit Pacific Beach is from mid-June to early September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the beauty of Pacific Beach during other seasons, each with its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pacific Beach

Do:

  • Respect the environment.
  • Follow all national park regulations.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter.
  • Disturb wildlife.
  • Ignore signs and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pacific Beach

Pacific Beach is a safe destination. Visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety, such as not leaving valuables in their cars and locking their hotel rooms.

Health in Pacific Beach

Visitors should carry any necessary medication with them and check with their doctor before traveling. For any medical emergency, an ambulance can be called by dialing 911.

Getting to Pacific Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pacific Beach

  • Bellingham International Airport (Approximately 200 miles away)
  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (Approximately 125 miles away)

Bus Stations Near Pacific Beach

  • Grays Harbor Transit in Aberdeen
  • Jefferson Transit in Port Townsend

Other Options

The easiest way to travel around Pacific Beach is by renting a car or bringing your vehicle. However, visitors can also use taxis to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Pacific Beach

Visitors can get around Pacific Beach using a car or a bicycle.

They can also walk to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Pacific Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Olympic National Park: It is located a short drive from Pacific Beach and offers stunning natural beauty, towering mountains, and vast forests.

  • Seattle: A vibrant city located just a few hours away from Pacific Beach, Seattle offers visitors a chance to explore local culture, music, food, and history.

Conclusion

Pacific Beach is an exceptional destination to unwind and relax. It offers visitors breathtaking views, miles of sandy beaches, and endless outdoor adventures. Plan your trip to the region today and discover this Pacific Northwest treasure.
