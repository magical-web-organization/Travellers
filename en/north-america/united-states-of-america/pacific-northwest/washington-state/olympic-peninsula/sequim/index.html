Introduction to Sequim

Located in the Olympic Peninsula of Washington, Sequim is a charming and picturesque city that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Known for its stunning lavender fields, outdoor activities, and friendly community, Sequim is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a detailed look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat while you’re in Sequim.

List of Places to Visit in Sequim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sequim Museum & Arts Center: Learn about the city’s history and culture through various exhibits and events.
  • Dungeness Schoolhouse: This beautifully restored schoolhouse offers a glimpse into the past of education in Sequim.
  • Olympic Game Farm: Get up close and personal with various wildlife in a drive-through safari-style adventure.

Natural Attractions of Sequim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dungeness Spit: A natural reserve featuring a picturesque beach and a 5-mile-long sand spit, perfect for hiking and bird-watching.
  • Olympic Discovery Trail: A 130-mile multi-use trail that runs through Sequim, offering scenic views of the Olympic Mountains and the Strait of Juan de Fuca.
  • Lavender Farms: Whether you’re in town during the Lavender Festival in July or not, you can’t miss the stunning lavender fields that dot the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sequim

  • Hurricane Ridge: A 17-mile scenic drive from Sequim, this trail offers breathtaking views of the Olympic Mountains and surrounding areas.
  • Railroad Bridge Park Trail: A family-friendly, easy hike that is perfect for all ages with stunning views of the Dungeness River.
  • Mount Walker Viewpoint Trail: An intermediate hiking trail that features stunning panoramic views of the Puget Sound and Olympic Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Sequim: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Sequim is home to several wineries, including the award-winning Wind Rose Cellars.
  • Golfing: Visit Sunland Golf and Country Club, a beautiful 18-hole golf course that is open to the public.
  • Kayaking: The calm waters of Sequim Bay make it the perfect location for kayaking, especially in the summer months.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sequim

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historical sites in Sequim, including Sequim Museum and Dungeness Schoolhouse.
Day 2 Hike Dungeness Spit, one of the most scenic hikes in Washington.
Day 3 Visit nearby Olympic National Park, including Hurricane Ridge and Lake Crescent.
Day 4 Spend the day wine tasting at Wind Rose Cellars and other local wineries.
Day 5 Take a kayaking tour of Sequim Bay, led by a local guide.
Day 6 Golf at Sunland Golf and Country Club, followed by a leisurely afternoon at one of the city’s many lavender farms.
Day 7 Take a day trip to nearby Port Townsend, a charming Victorian-era seaport town with plenty of shopping and dining opportunities.

Food and Drink Scene of Sequim

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dungeness Crab: A Pacific Northwest classic, fresh Dungeness crab is a must-try when visiting Sequim.
  • Lavender-Infused Treats: From lavender scones to lavender ice cream, there are a variety of unique culinary treats that feature the city’s signature flower.
  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: With several local farms in the area, many of the city’s restaurants feature fresh, locally sourced produce on their menus.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Visit one of Sequim’s several microbreweries, including Double Eagle and Olympic Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Sequim is home to several wineries, including the award-winning Wind Rose Cellars.
  • Cider: Visit one of the city’s several cideries, including Finnriver Farm & Cidery.

Where to Stay in Sequim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Red Lion Inn & Suites: A comfortable, affordable hotel located within walking distance of downtown Sequim.
  • Dungeness Bay Cottages: Cozy cottages that offer stunning waterfront views of the Strait of Juan de Fuca.
  • Juan de Fuca Cottages: Luxury private cottages that feature stunning waterfront views and outdoor fire pits.

What to Buy in Sequim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lavender Products: From soaps to lotions to essential oils, there are dozens of lavender-based products available for purchase.
  • Artisan Crafts: Several local artists and craftsmen sell their wares at various shops throughout the city, offering a wide range of unique and beautiful products.
  • Local Wine and Cider: Bring home a bottle of wine or cider from one of the city’s many wineries or cideries.

Local Culture and Customs of Sequim

Sequim is a town that values community involvement and respect for the environment. Visitors are encouraged to take part in community events and to take responsibility for keeping the area clean and pristine.

Best Time to Visit Sequim

The best time to visit Sequim is during the summer months, from June through August. This is when the lavender fields are in bloom, and there are many outdoor activities available to visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sequim

Do:

  • Respect the environment and leave no trace behind after any outdoor activity.
  • Take part in community events and support local businesses.
  • Be courteous and friendly to fellow visitors and locals alike.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave any trash behind.
  • Harass or harm any wildlife.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sequim

Sequim is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still exercise caution, especially when engaging in outdoor activities. Make sure to carry plenty of water and to wear proper clothing and sun protection.

Health in Sequim

Sequim is a healthy city with quality healthcare available. Visitors should make sure to carry their insurance cards and any necessary medications, and to alert their doctor of any pre-existing health conditions before traveling.

Getting to Sequim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sequim

  • William R. Fairchild International Airport, Port Angeles (20 miles)
  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, Seattle (80 miles)

Bus Stations Near Sequim

  • Greyhound Bus Lines, Port Angeles (17 miles)
  • Olympic Bus Lines, Sequim (1 mile)

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several major car rental companies are available at both the Seattle-Tacoma and William R. Fairchild International Airports.
  • Private Transportation: Several private transportation companies offer shuttle services to and from Sequim.

How to Get Around in Sequim

The best way to get around Sequim is by car, as the city is spread out and many of the natural attractions are located outside of town. Bicycling is also popular for those looking to explore the Olympic Discovery Trail.

Where to Go Next from Sequim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Port Townsend: A charming Victorian-era seaport town with plenty of shopping and dining opportunities.
  • Olympic National Park: A vast natural reserve that offers hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Victoria, British Columbia: A scenic coastal city located on Vancouver Island, just a short ferry ride from Port Angeles.

Conclusion

Sequim, Washington offers visitors a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions that are sure to make any trip unforgettable. Whether you’re looking to relax and enjoy the stunning lavender fields or to engage in outdoor activities, this picturesque city is well worth a visit.
