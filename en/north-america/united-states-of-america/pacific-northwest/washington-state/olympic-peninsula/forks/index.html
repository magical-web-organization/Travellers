Forks Travel Guide - Explore the Olympic Peninsula

Nestled in the Olympic Peninsula of Washington State, Forks is a small town that offers a blend of natural beauty and unique culture. With forests, rivers, and the Pacific Ocean within reach, this town is perfect for outdoor adventures. Forks is also known for its rich history and folklore, particularly its association with vampires. Whether you’re a fan of the “Twilight” saga or simply looking for a serene escape, Forks is the perfect destination for you. Explore Forks with this comprehensive travel guide that covers everything from top attractions to food and drink scene.

Introduction to Forks

Forks is a small town in Clallam County and is part of the Olympic Peninsula. It is located in the northwest corner of Washington State. The town was named after the forks of the Sol Duc, Calawah, and Bogachiel Rivers, which converge at the west end of the town. Forks has a population of around 3,200 people and is approximately 80 miles west of Port Angeles.

List of Places to Visit in Forks

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Forks Timber Museum - This museum chronicles the history of the timber industry in Forks and the surrounding area. The museum features exhibits such as logging equipment, historic photographs, and significant artifacts from the timber industry.

  • Rainforest Arts Center - This center is located in downtown Forks and features local art exhibits. The center also hosts events such as concerts and theater performances.

Natural Attractions of Forks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olympic National Park - Forks is just a gateway to one of the most majestic national parks in the country. The park covers 1,400 square miles and is home to the Olympic Mountains, the Hoh Rainforest, and stunning coastal scenes.

  • Rialto Beach - Located just a few miles west of Forks, this beach offers dramatic seascapes. The beach has tide pools, rock formations, and sandy beaches. You can even see sea otters, seals, and bald eagles.

  • Hoh Rainforest - The unique ecosystem of Hoh Rainforest, located within Olympic National Park, is worth exploring. You can take a leisurely hike among the ancient trees and the lush greenery, or opt for more extended trails for a more immersive experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forks

  • Sol Duc Falls Trail - A moderate hike that features a waterfall and a forest with old-growth trees.

  • Marymere Falls Trail - Suitable for all ages and abilities, this trail is known for the scenic Marymere Falls.

  • Olympic Discovery Trail - This trail spans over 130 miles and offers stunning views of the coastline and forests.

Best Things to Do in Forks: Activities and Tours

  • Forks Twilight Tours - Visit locations made famous by the “Twilight” book series, including “Bella’s House” and “Jacob’s Reservation.”

  • Fishing and Hunting - Fishing enthusiasts can enjoy fishing at one of the rivers, or explore nearby fishing villages like La Push and Neah Bay.

  • Beachcombing - There is no better place to hunt for treasures than the beaches around Forks on the Olympic Coast, including those offshore.

Food and Drink Scene of Forks

Must-Try Dishes

  • Northwestern chowder - Slow-cooked creamy chowder with clams, bacon, and potatoes. This regional delicacy is a must-try.

  • Wild caught salmon - Indulge in fresh salmon, cooked to perfection with locally sourced herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Rainforest Roast - A speciality coffee blend made with beans sourced from various farms around town.

  • Northwest beers - Savor the taste of locally crafted beers in downtown Forks.

Where to Stay in Forks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Forks Motel - Affordable motel in the downtown area, within walking distance to shops, restaurants, and museums.

  • Pacific Inn Motel - Clean and comfortable rooms with a cozy atmosphere. This motel offers amenities like free Wi-Fi and continental breakfast options.

  • Miller Tree Inn - This inn is famous for its luxurious amenities like a garden filled with birds, private decks, hot tubs and a forest full of enchanting surroundings exclusive to this place only.

  • Quillayute River Resort - Affordable cabins that offer a unique camping experience while still having access to modern amenities.

What to Buy in Forks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Twilight merchandise - You cannot leave Forks without a piece of official merchandise from “Twilight” saga. These products celebrate Forks unique association with the supernatural saga.

  • Locally crafted art - This area of Washington is well-known for its artists. There are many galleries around town that offer locally produced artwork that is unique to this region.

Local Culture and Customs of Forks

The town of Forks is a tight-knit community known for its love of the outdoors, arts, and wildlife. Many locals are employed in the fishing, logging, and outdoor recreation industries. The town is very friendly and welcoming to visitors, especially those who have come to explore the beauty of the Olympic Peninsula.

Best Time to Visit Forks

The best time to visit Forks is in the summer between June and August. The weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities, and the days are long. However, if you want to experience the lush beauty of Hoh Rainforest, plan to visit in the autumn so you can see the changing colours of the forest foliage. Winter truly is a serene time to visit as the visitor traffic is limited but snowfall can make it difficult to visit some areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forks

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural scenery - Forks is well-known for its natural beauty and greenery.

  • Respect the wildlife - Threatened species like the salmon, elk, and moss is found in this area. Be respectful of their habitat while exploring.

  • Shop locally - Support the local economy by buying souvenirs and products from Forks.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Be mindful of the environment. Help keep Forks free of litter.

  • Damage the forests - Do not disturb the natural habitats of trees, plants, and wildlife.

  • Trespass on private property - Respect private property, and always ask for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forks

Forks is generally a safe town for visitors. However, it is always advised to be cautious and aware of your surroundings while exploring. Avoid areas that seem unsafe, and do not leave valuables in plain sight.

Health in Forks

Forks has a clinic and a pharmacy, but medical emergencies, major illness, and surgery require travel outside the town. Local mosquitoes do exist, but pose a minimal threat. Ensure you wear mosquito repellent and use safety precautions for any outdoor activities.

Getting to Forks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Forks

The nearest airport to Forks is William R. Fairchild International Airport, which is located in Port Angeles. You can fly into this airport and take a taxi or rent a car to get to Forks.

Bus Stations Near Forks

Greyhound Bus Line and Clallam Transit provides services to Forks. They operate on regular schedules and connect Forks with neighboring towns such as Port Angeles and SeaTac Airport.

Other Options

You can opt to rent a car to explore the surrounding areas of Forks. Alternatively, walking and biking are popular options.

How to Get Around in Forks

The town is small and easy to navigate on foot, especially around the downtown area. Taxis and car rental services are also available if you want to venture outside of Forks.

Where to Go Next from Forks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • La Push - A popular destination for beachcombing and surfing located less than 20 minutes from Forks.

  • Olympic National Park - A must-visit is Olympic National Park, which is less than 20 miles from Forks.

  • Port Angeles - Explore the Victorian seaport located 80 miles from Forks.

Conclusion

Forks is a small town that offers a unique blend of culture and nature. Whether you’re a fan of the “Twilight” saga or simply looking for outdoor adventures and scenic beauty, Forks has something for everyone. Plan your visit to this picturesque town and soak in the rich history, food, and cultures of the Pacific Northwest.
