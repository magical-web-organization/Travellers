Introduction to Port Townsend

Experience the perfect blend of rich history, stunning scenery, and vibrant cultural scene in Port Townsend. Tucked away in the Olympic Peninsula region of Washington state, this charming city is a hidden gem that deserves a spot on your travel list. Wander through the picturesque Victorian-style architecture, explore the intriguing museums, hike through the lush forests, or chow down on delicious local cuisine - there is something for everyone in Port Townsend.

List of Places to Visit in Port Townsend

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Port Townsend Marine Science Center: Dive into the fascinating underwater world at this center that features interactive exhibits, touch tanks, and aquariums. Don’t miss the opportunity to learn about local marine life.

  • Jefferson Museum of Art and History: Discover the city’s rich history through an impressive collection of artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that showcase Jefferson County’s past.

  • Fort Worden State Park: Once a military base, Fort Worden State Park is a cultural hub that offers visitors the chance to participate in music festivals, art exhibitions, and other events around the year.

  • Port Townsend Historic District: A designated National Historic Landmark, this district features 100 buildings with ornate Victorian-style architecture that take you back in time.

Natural Attractions of Port Townsend: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olympic National Park: Just a short drive from Port Townsend, Olympic National Park is a breathtaking natural wonderland. Hike through dense forests, marvel at towering waterfalls, spot wildlife, and soak in the hot springs.

  • Chetzemoka Park: Take a stroll through this stunning park that overlooks Admiralty Inlet. Watch the ferries maneuver in and out of the harbor or catch a spectacular sunset.

  • Port Townsend Farmers Market: Savor the flavors of the Pacific Northwest by visiting the weekly farmers market, held every Saturday from April to December. Indulge in fresh organic produce, artisan cheeses, baked goods, and handcrafted goods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Townsend

  • Larry Scott Memorial Trail: This 7.3-mile paved route takes you on a scenic journey through lush forests, scenic bridges, and stunning views of the Strait of Juan de Fuca.

  • Fort Worden State Park Trails: With over 12 miles of trails, Fort Worden State Park is a hiker’s paradise. Wander through historic bunkers, take in the panoramic views, and spot wildlife along the way.

  • Cape George Trail: This 5-mile trail offers panoramic views of the Olympic Mountains, Strait of Juan de Fuca, and the Cascade Mountains. Easily accessible and ideal for beginners, this trail is perfect for a family-friendly hike.

Best Things to Do in Port Townsend: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking Adventures: Explore the Puget Sound by kayaking through the tranquil waters. Rent kayaks or opt for a guided tour to discover hidden coves, marine life, and stunning views.

  • Whale Watching Tours: One of the best ways to get up close and personal with marine life is by taking a whale watching tour. Observe majestic orcas, humpbacks, gray whales, and other wildlife.

  • Cycling Tours: Experience the stunning scenery of Port Townsend on two wheels. Rent a bike or join a guided cycling tour through parks, beaches, and forests.

Food and Drink Scene of Port Townsend

Must-Try Dishes

  • Olympic Mountain Ice Cream: Satisfy your sweet tooth with a scoop (or two!) of this locally made ice cream. Their flavors range from classic to creative, featuring seasonal ingredients.

  • Seafood Chowder: Experience the famous Pacific Northwest cuisine by sampling the seafood chowder that features fresh fish, clams, and mussels.

  • Farm-to-Table Cuisine: Enjoy the flavors of the season with locally sourced produce at one of the many farm-to-table restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Finnriver Farm and Cidery: Taste the flavors of Washington by indulging in the crisp, refreshing ciders that are made from organic apples grown on the farm.

  • Port Townsend Brewing Company: Sample the locally crafted beers that range from traditional to innovative flavors.

  • Sirens Pub: Enjoy a cocktail made from locally sourced ingredients, or indulge in one of the many whiskies that are offered.

Where to Stay in Port Townsend: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Swan Hotel: A boutique hotel housed in a historic building that offers luxurious accommodations, fine dining, and a stunning waterfront view.

  • Fort Worden State Park Lodging: Choose from a variety of lodging options that range from historic officer’s quarters to comfortable cabins.

  • The Ravenscroft Inn: A bed and breakfast located in a restored Victorian mansion that offers comfort, elegance, and views of the Puget Sound.

What to Buy in Port Townsend: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Items: Explore the local artisan markets and discover unique, one-of-a-kind handcrafted items like jewelry, pottery, and textiles.

  • Lavender Products: Known as the Lavender Capital of North America, Port Townsend is home to a variety of lavender farms that sell soaps, lotions, and other items made from fragrant lavender.

  • Local Wine and Cider: Bring home a taste of Washington by purchasing locally sourced wine and cider.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Townsend

Port Townsend is a creative and inclusive community that is proud of its history and heritage. Visitors can expect to encounter a warm and friendly atmosphere, where they can engage with locals, dance to live music, or participate in community events. To get the most out of your visit, respect the local customs and be open to new experiences.

Best Time to Visit Port Townsend

The best time to visit Port Townsend is from late spring to early fall (May to September) when the weather is mild and makes sightseeing and outdoor activities more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Townsend

Do:

  • Respect the local architecture and history by not touching or damaging the historic buildings.
  • Participate in community events and explore local culture and customs.
  • Tread lightly and follow Leave No Trace principles when taking part in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas and wildlife habitats.
  • Consume alcohol or drugs in public spaces.
  • Take items or artifacts from protected areas or historic buildings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Townsend

Port Townsend is a safe and friendly community where visitors can feel comfortable exploring on their own. As with any city, exercise caution in unfamiliar areas and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Port Townsend

While Port Townsend has medical facilities, it is recommended that visitors have travel insurance and carry necessary medications. Be aware of any allergies or health concerns before taking part in outdoor activities or consuming local food and drink.

Getting to Port Townsend: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port Townsend

The nearest airport to Port Townsend is the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA) located about 108 miles away. From there, visitors can hire a rental car, take a shuttle, or use public transportation like the ferry or bus to reach Port Townsend.

Bus Stations Near Port Townsend

Port Townsend is served by the Jefferson Transit bus system that connects to nearby cities like Seattle and Tacoma.

Other Options

For a unique and scenic experience, visitors can take a ferry from Seattle to Port Townsend. The ferry ride offers stunning views of the Olympic Mountains and the Puget Sound.

How to Get Around in Port Townsend

Port Townsend is a walkable city, and many of the attractions are located in a compact area. Visitors can rent bikes, take a cab, or use the public transit system for areas that are farther away.

Where to Go Next from Port Townsend: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sequim: Located just a short drive from Port Townsend, Sequim is a charming town known for its lavender fields, festivals, and natural beauty.

  • Olympia: The state capital of Washington, Olympia, is located about 60 miles from Port Townsend. Visitors can explore museums, parks, and the waterfront area.

  • Victoria, B.C.: Take a ferry from Port Townsend to Victoria, British Columbia, to experience the history, beauty, and culture of this charming Canadian city.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and thriving arts scene, Port Townsend is a destination that offers something for every kind of traveler. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect trip, giving you a glimpse into the adventures and experiences that await you in this charming city.
