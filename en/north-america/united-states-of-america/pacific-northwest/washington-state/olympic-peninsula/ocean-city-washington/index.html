Introduction to Ocean City (Washington)

Nestled in the Olympic Peninsula in the Pacific Northwest, Ocean City (Washington) offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. This charming seaside town boasts pristine beaches, scenic trails, and a rich history that is waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure seeker, or curious traveler, Ocean City has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean City (Washington)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ocean Shores Interpretive Center: Learn about the natural history and cultural significance of the region through exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Museum of the North Beach: Explore the fascinating history of the local logging industry and the impact it had on the area.
  • Grays Harbor Historical Seaport: Step aboard a historic tall ship and experience life on the high seas.

Natural Attractions of Ocean City (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ocean City State Park: Enjoy a day at the beach with stunning views of the ocean and towering sand dunes.
  • Pacific Beach State Park: Embark on a scenic hike and catch a glimpse of the local wildlife.
  • Quinault Rainforest: Explore the lush rainforest and discover waterfalls, lakes, and ancient trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean City (Washington)

  • Damon Point Trail: Marvel at spectacular views of the ocean and the surrounding forests while hiking along this scenic trail.
  • North Beach Trail: Enjoy a leisurely bike ride through the charming town of Moclips and take in the stunning coastal vistas.
  • Coastal Interpretive Trail: Follow this nature walk and learn about the unique ecosystem of the area.

Best Things to Do in Ocean City (Washington): Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tours: Witness majestic whales in their natural habitat with guided tours departing from Ocean City.
  • Outdoor Activities: Enjoy a day of fishing, crabbing, kayaking, or canoeing on the nearby rivers and lakes.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic trails on horseback and take in the breathtaking views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ocean City (Washington)

Day Activities
1 Visit Ocean Shores Interpretive Center and Museum of the North Beach.
2 Hike the Coastal Interpretive Trail and enjoy a day at Pacific Beach State Park.
3 Take a whale watching tour in the morning and go kayaking in the afternoon.
4 Go horseback riding and explore the lush Quinault Rainforest.
5 Embark on a scenic bike ride on the North Beach Trail and relax at Damon Point Beach.
6 Learn about the local logging industry at Grays Harbor Historical Seaport.
7 Enjoy a day of fishing or crabbing on the nearby rivers and lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean City (Washington)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dungeness Crab: Savor the fresh flavors of this local delicacy, either boiled or in a seafood platter.
  • Wild Salmon: Try this renowned Pacific Northwest specialty, either grilled or smoked.
  • Razor Clams: Relish the juicy, succulent taste of these locally sourced clams.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beers: Sample a range of local brews from renowned breweries such as Rainy Daze Brewing and Ocean Pours Taproom.
  • Wines: Explore the local vineyards and taste the rich flavors of Pacific Northwest wines.

Where to Stay in Ocean City (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quinault Beach Resort & Casino: This luxurious resort features comfortable rooms, a range of amenities, and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Grey Gull: This cozy hotel offers easy access to the nearby beaches and has a charming rustic ambiance.
  • Loge at the Sands: This stylish boutique hotel offers a unique surfing-inspired experience and is perfect for adventurous travelers.

What to Buy in Ocean City (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Browse through a stunning collection of handmade jewelry made from sea glass found along the beaches.
  • Local Art: Discover a range of artwork that captures the spirit of the Pacific Northwest and showcases the local talent.
  • Lavender Products: Take home a range of fragrant lavender products such as essential oils, soaps, and lotions.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean City (Washington)

  • Respect nature and leave no trace while exploring the pristine outdoors.
  • Follow common courtesies and respect the locals and their culture.
  • Support local businesses and ensure sustainable tourism practices.

Best Time to Visit Ocean City (Washington)

The best time to visit Ocean City (Washington) is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities and beach relaxation. However, the offseason can also be a great time to experience the town’s peaceful solitude and explore the natural beauty of the region.

Getting to Ocean City (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean City (Washington)

  • William R. Fairchild International Airport (CLM): This airport is about 50 miles away from Ocean City and offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Ocean City (Washington)

  • Aberdeen Station: This bus station is about 25 miles away from Ocean City and offers connections to nearby cities such as Seattle and Portland.

Other Options

  • Driving: Ocean City is easily accessible by car via Highway 109, which connects it to nearby cities and towns.
  • Private Tours: Various private tour operators offer convenient and customized travel services for visitors to Ocean City.

How to Get Around in Ocean City (Washington)

  • Car: Renting a car is the most convenient mode of transport for exploring the surrounding areas and nearby towns.
  • Bike: Rent a bike and explore the quaint seaside town of Ocean City and experience the charm of the local neighborhoods.
  • Walking: Discover the local landmarks and hidden gems of the town by taking leisurely walking tours.

  • Portland: Discover the unique charm of Portland and experience its thriving arts and music scene, exquisite cuisine, and historic neighborhoods.
