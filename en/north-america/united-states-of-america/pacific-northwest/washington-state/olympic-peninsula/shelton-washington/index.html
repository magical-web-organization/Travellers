Introduction to Shelton (Washington)

Shelton is a charming city in Mason County, located in Washington (state). This small city, surrounded by mountains, water, and lush green vegetation, provides visitors with a memorable experience. It is a perfect destination for those who love outdoor activities, adventure, and cultural exploration. The city has established its prominence with its museums and historical places alongside stunning natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Shelton (Washington)

Shelton offers plenty of things to visit and do. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mason County Historical Museum: The museum exhibits artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia of the early settlers of Mason County. It provides valuable insights into the lives of the region’s pioneers.
  • Squaxin Island Museum, Library and Research Center: This museum is a tribute to the Squaxin Island Tribe’s history, language, and culture and has exhibits, artwork, and interactive displays.

Natural Attractions of Shelton (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olympic National Forest: Enjoy the hiking trails, fishing, and boating experiences at the Olympic National Forest. It is home to flora and fauna that is native to the region.
  • Mason Lake: A stunning lake with crystal clear water with opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming. Admire the picturesque beauty of Mt. Rainier and Olympic Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shelton (Washington)

Shelton has several scenic biking and hiking trails that provide a close-up view of nature’s breathtaking beauty. Some of these trails are:

  • Gold Creek Trail: This 5-mile route passes through the Olympic Peninsula, and it has a moderate difficulty level.
  • Green Mountain Trail: This stunning trail is perfect for hikers and offers breathtaking views.
  • Union Peak Trail: It is a 4-mile trail that offers stunning mountain, river, and valley views.

Best Things to Do in Shelton (Washington): Activities and Tours

Shelton offers several activities and tours for visitors to engage in, such as:

  • Fishing: Visitors of all ages can enjoy fishing for salmon, steelhead, bass, perch, and more at the Hood Canal.
  • Kayaking: Enjoy kayaking in the calm and serene waters of Lake Cushman.
  • Wildlife Watching: Home to a diverse range of wildlife, Shelton is perfect for wildlife watching.

Food and Drink Scene of Shelton (Washington)

Shelton is known for its delectable and flavorful cuisine and locally produced drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked Salmon: A dish that represents the Pacific Northwest, smoked salmon is an absolute must-try.
  • Oysters: Largely cultivated in the region, Shelton is home to the sweetest and the most delicious oysters.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Sip on award-winning wines produced in Shelton’s wineries.
  • Beer: Experience the taste of Shelton beer brewed from Shelton-grown ingredients.

Where to Stay in Shelton (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shelton has plenty of accommodation facilities that range from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the popular accommodations in Shelton are:

  • Little Creek Casino Resort: A luxury resort that offers premium accommodation and casino services.
  • SummerTide Resort and Marina: If you are looking for an affordable countryside stay, SummerTide Resort is a great option.

What to Buy in Shelton (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shelton offers several locally produced items that serve as excellent souvenirs. Some of the best local products to bring home are:

  • Oysters: Bring home fresh and delicious oysters from the famous oyster farms in the city.
  • Hand-Crafted Pottery: Shelton is home to skilled and talented potters who create unique and beautiful pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Shelton (Washington)

Shelton is home to the Squaxin Island Tribe, which has contributed significantly to Shelton’s culture, history, and traditions. Visitors are welcomed and encouraged to learn about the tribal customs and traditions that are still practiced today.

Best Time to Visit Shelton (Washington)

The best time to visit Shelton is during the summer months from June to August when the temperatures are pleasant, and the city is buzzing with cultural and outdoor events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shelton (Washington)

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Help preserve the area’s natural beauty.
  • Pack comfortable hiking and outdoor gear.

Don’t:

  • Litter.
  • Intrude on private tribal areas.
  • Disturb the natural habitats of animals.

Getting to Shelton (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Shelton can be reached via multiple transportation options.

Airports Near Shelton (Washington)

The nearest airport to Shelton is the Seattle–Tacoma International Airport located approximately 65 miles from the city.

Bus Stations Near Shelton (Washington)

Buses run regularly between Shelton, Olympia, and Seattle, and there are several bus stations located throughout the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Shelton via driving and water transportation.

How to Get Around in Shelton (Washington)

The best way to get around in Shelton is via a car rental or taxi services.
