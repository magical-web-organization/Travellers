Introduction to Elma

Nestled in the heart of Washington’s Olympic Peninsula, the small town of Elma is a hidden gem that offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and a warm local community, Elma is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and families alike. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Elma has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Elma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Satsop Business Park: This abandoned nuclear power plant is a fascinating example of post-industrial architecture, and a popular spot for photography and urban exploration.
  • Simpson Avenue Historic District: This picturesque street is lined with historic buildings dating back to the early 1900s, and is a great place for a leisurely stroll.
  • Summit Lake State Park: This beautiful state park is home to a historic CCC camp and offers hiking, fishing, and swimming opportunities.

Natural Attractions of Elma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wynoochee River: A popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and hiking.
  • Schafer State Park: This state park boasts scenic views of the river and offers hiking and picnicking facilities.
  • Olympic National Forest: This stunning national forest is home to miles of hiking trails, stunning waterfalls, and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elma

  • Wynoochee Lake Loop: A 16-mile loop trail that offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding forest.
  • Colonel Bob Trail: A strenuous but rewarding hike to the top of a mountain peak that offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Schaefer Lake Trail: A 2.5-mile hike through beautiful forest and alongside a scenic lake.

Best Things to Do in Elma: Activities and Tours

  • Experience the Arts: Take a visit to the locally owned and operated Gallery 105 or attend a live music show at the 1923 theatre.
  • Golf at Oaksridge Golf Course: Enjoy 18 holes of pristine fairways and challenging greens.
  • Attend a Festivals & Events: Summer festivals such as the Satsop River Music Festival and Elma Valley Festival have great food and enjoyable music.

Food and Drink Scene of Elma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dungeness Crab Cakes: Elma is within close proximity to the Pacific Ocean and offers some of the freshest seafood on the West Coast.
  • Grays Harbor Oysters: Elma is located within Grays Harbor, where restaurants across the area take great pride in serving fresh oysters.
  • Northwest Salmon: A must-try Pacific Northwest classic is the salmon that is caught wild in its natural habitat and made to near perfection in the surrounding eateries.

Local Beverages

  • Wine Tasting: Take a trip down to the award-winning Stottle Winery tasting room and enjoy some of the best wines Elma has to offer.
  • Beer Tasting: Local breweries such as Steam Donkey Brewing offer outdoor seating, great beer, and incredible view as you enjoy your tasty brew.

Where to Stay in Elma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Stay at the The Stay Beyond Inn & Suites near the I-5 freeway. It provides easy access to local restaurants and great amenities for a great value, with an indoor pool, hot tub, and fitness center.
  • Experience warm hospitality and a family-friendly vibe at the GuestHouse Elma, where each room comes equipped with a work desk and free Wi-Fi access.
  • Set on a beautiful waterfront of Lake Quinault, stay at the Lake Quinault Lodge. A locally historic hotel built in 1926, visitors can unwind and take up on nearby outdoor recreation such as fishing, hiking, and kayaking.

What to Buy in Elma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Check out the whimsical pottery designs at Cheryl Borden’s shop.
  • Local Artwork: Purchase some of the local artwork and photography at Gallery 105, which features work from regional artists.
  • Local Products: Check out the unique store selling rustic wood crafts and wooden kitchen utensils at the MOUNTAIN AIRE mercantile.

Local Culture and Customs of Elma

Elma’s cultural scene offers a rich and diverse experience that showcases the region’s local talents and history. Locals take pride in their independent culture, where they offer live music, painting exhibitions, and community festivals that are one of a kind. Be sure to check out the Elma Chamber of Commerce to learn about the season’s local events and festivities.

Best Time to Visit Elma

The best time to visit Elma is from summer to early autumn, as these seasons offer great weather for exploring the outdoors and taking part in local events. Travel during the peak season presents more crowds and higher hotel prices, and the outdoor activities may not suit a traveler’s schedule due to the rainfall the Pacific Northwest experiences during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elma

Do:

  • Do be mindful of nature: The local community takes a lot of pride in the surrounding nature and encourages visitors to preserve the area’s natural beauty.
  • Do learn about Native Culture Histories and Traditions. The Chehalis Tribe of west-central Washington has a rich history and visitors are encouraged to learn more about their culture and traditions.
  • Do check out the locally owned and run restaurants. They offer a wide range of culinary experiences, showcasing the locally produced ingredients and flavors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack a raincoat: The Pacific Northwest experiences abundant rainfall and a traveler will not be effective without an appropriate raincoat.
  • Don’t forget your hiking boots or gear: With Elma’s surrounding landscape, a traveler must be prepared to explore the beautiful trails and areas that only hiking legs can take you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elma

Visitors should take the usual safety precautions when out and about. Always keep locks on valuables and keep them out of sight when parked. Use caution when traveling alone, especially after dark or in secluded areas, and follow the state’s guidelines for hiking and safety in parks and forests.

Health in Elma

Basic healthcare facilities are available in Elma and the surrounding community. Visitors should be aware of the existence of Lyme disease in areas of vegetation and take measures to protect themselves.

Getting to Elma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elma

The closest major airport to Elma is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA), located about 80 miles northeast of the town. From there, visitors can rent a car or a private car service to reach Elma.

Bus Stations Near Elma

The local bus station is located in Olympia, WA. Bus routes are scheduled throughout the day and offer an affordable option to get to Elma.

Other Options

Visitors can always opt to rent a car in Seattle, WA, or Portland, OR, for easy transportation to Elma.

How to Get Around in Elma

With a small population, limited public transportation is available for travelers. A car rental is the recommended and most convenient mode of transportation for exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Elma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Olympic National Park: Take a day trip to explore the area’s winding trails, verdant forests, and stunning mountain views.
  • The coastline: Experience the wild and scenic coastline of Washington while exploring nearby coastal towns such as Westport and Ocean City.
  • Seattle: Spend a few days in the vibrant city of Seattle, home to iconic landmarks such as the Space Needle and Pike Place Market.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation amid nature, a cultural escape, or an assortment of outdoor activities, Elma’s hidden gem has got you covered. So, what’s holding you back? Pack your bags, get in your car, and embark on an adventure of a lifetime in this picturesque Pacific Northwest town.
