Introduction to Westport (Washington)

Westport is a scenic coastal town located in Grays Harbor County, Washington State. Known for its distinctive harbor and outdoor recreation opportunities, Westport is the perfect weekend getaway for nature-lovers. The town is home to unique cultural attractions, exciting outdoor activities, and delicious seafood, making it an ideal destination for tourists from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Westport (Washington)

No matter what your interests are, Westport offers something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Westport:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Westport Maritime Museum showcases the region’s maritime history with fascinating exhibits, a restored Coast Guard rescue boat, and stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Grays Harbor Lighthouse is a historic landmark that has been guiding ships into Grays Harbor since 1898. Visitors can climb the 135-foot tower and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding coastline.
  • Westport Winery is a family-owned winery that produces world-class wines using locally sourced fruit. Visitors can take a wine-tasting tour of the vineyards and enjoy a delicious meal at the on-site restaurant.

Natural Attractions of Westport (Washington): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Westport Light State Park is a picturesque park that boasts sandy beaches, towering dunes, and sweeping ocean views. Visitors can hike, fish, swim, and enjoy a picnic on the beach.
  • Westhaven State Park is a popular spot for beachcombing, picnicking, and fishing. The park has several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the coast.
  • Charter Fishing is a favorite activity in Westport. Visitors can rent a boat and set out into the Pacific Ocean for a day of fishing for salmon, crab, and bottom fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Westport (Washington)

Westport is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Cranberry Road Trail is a scenic 2.8-mile trail that takes visitors through the cranberry bogs and offers a stunning view of Bowerman Basin.
  • John’s River Wildlife Area Trail is a 7.5-mile trail that winds through forests and wetlands and offers opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife watching.
  • Westport Light State Park Beach Trail is a 2-mile trail that takes visitors along the sandy beach and offers views of the Pacific Ocean and the Grays Harbor Lighthouse.

Best Things to Do in Westport (Washington): Activities and Tours

Westport is full of exciting activities and tours. Here are a few must-do activities:

  • Fishing Charter is a popular activity in Westport. Visitors can rent a boat and set out into the Pacific Ocean for a day of fishing for salmon, crab, and bottom fish.
  • Whale Watching is a must-do activity for nature-lovers visiting Westport. Visitors can take a tour that will take them out into the Pacific Ocean for a chance to see whales, dolphins, and sea lions.
  • Westport Brewing Company offers visitors a chance to taste locally brewed beer and learn about the brewing process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Westport (Washington)

If you are planning a week-long trip to Westport, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Westport Maritime Museum, Grays Harbor Lighthouse, and Westport Winery

Food and Drink Scene of Westport (Washington)

Westport is known for its fresh seafood and delicious locally brewed beer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips is a popular dish that features fresh fish fried in a crispy batter and served with french fries.
  • Crab Cakes is a local delicacy that features crabmeat mixed with spices and coated in breadcrumbs before being pan-fried.
  • Oysters Rockefeller features fresh oysters topped with a mixture of spinach, bacon, and cheese before being broiled.

Local Beverages

  • Westport Brewing Company is a family-owned brewery that produces a variety of locally brewed beers. Visitors can enjoy a pint of beer at the on-site taproom or take a tour of the brewery.
  • Chateau Ste. Michelle is a nearby winery that produces world-class wines using locally sourced fruit. Visitors can take a wine-tasting tour of the vineyards and enjoy a delicious meal at the on-site restaurant.

Where to Stay in Westport (Washington): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Westport offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Westport:

  • Westport Marina Cottages offer visitors a chance to stay in cozy cottages located on the marina. The cottages are equipped with all the necessary amenities and offer stunning views of the harbor.
  • Ocean Avenue Inn is a boutique hotel located just blocks from the beach. The rooms are spacious, well-appointed, and offer stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Westport Inn is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Westport. The rooms are clean, comfortable, and offer all the necessary amenities.

What to Buy in Westport (Washington): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Westport offers a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Grey’s Harbor Lighthouse Souvenirs are available at the gift shop located at the lighthouse. Visitors can purchase t-shirts, magnets, and other souvenirs to commemorate their visit.
  • Westport Winery Wines are available for purchase at the winery’s gift shop. Visitors can take a bottle of their favorite wine home with them.
  • Handmade Soaps and Candles are available for purchase at The Mermaid’s Purse, a local gift shop that features handmade items from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Westport (Washington)

Westport is a laid-back town with friendly locals and a strong fishing culture. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are a few tips:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting cultural sites and religious places.
  • Fishing is an essential part of the local economy, so expect to see fishing boats and nets around the harbor.
  • Carry cash as some local shops and restaurants may not accept credit or debit cards.

Best Time to Visit Westport (Washington)

The best time to visit Westport is during the summer months of June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and the water is perfect for swimming. Fall is also a great time to visit as the trees turn golden, making for a beautiful sight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Westport (Washington)

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Westport:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Take your trash with you and dispose of it properly.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local safety regulations, especially on the beach and ocean.
  • Littering is prohibited in Westport.
  • Disturb the marine life, especially while on a whale watching tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Westport (Washington)

Westport is a safe town, but visitors should take proper precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Follow local safety regulations, especially on the beach and ocean.
  • Use caution when swimming in the ocean.
  • Keep an eye on the weather forecast and be prepared for sudden changes in weather.

Health in Westport (Washington)

Visitors to Westport should take precautions to protect their health:

  • Apply sunscreen to avoid sunburn.
  • Bring insect repellent to avoid bug bites.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

Getting to Westport (Washington): Transportation Options and Logistics

Westport is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Westport (Washington)

The nearest airport is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which is located approximately 120 miles from Westport.

Bus Stations Near Westport (Washington)

Greyhound offers bus services to Aberdeen, which is located approximately 17 miles from Westport.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car to reach Westport or taking a private shuttle service.

How to Get Around in Westport (Washington)

Westport is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Those who prefer to drive can rent a car from a local rental agency.

Where to Go Next from Westport (Washington): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Westport is located close to several popular destinations that are well worth a visit. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Olympic National Park is located approximately 90 miles from Westport and offers stunning views of mountains and rainforests.
  • Seattle is located approximately 120 miles from Westport and is known for its iconic landmarks, including the Space Needle and Pike Place Market.
  • Astoria, Oregon is located approximately 80 miles from Westport and is a historic town that boasts stunning Victorian-era architecture.

Conclusion

Westport is a hidden gem in the Pacific Northwest that offers visitors a chance to enjoy nature and outdoor activities in a laid-back setting. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or adventure, Westport has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and enjoy the natural beauty and charm of this coastal town.
