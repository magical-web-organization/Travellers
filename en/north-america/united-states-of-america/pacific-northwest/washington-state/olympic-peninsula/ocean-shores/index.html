Introduction to Ocean Shores

Located in the Olympic Peninsula of Washington, Ocean Shores is a coastal city offering beautiful scenery, outdoor recreational activities, and vibrant culture. This charming destination is perfect for travelers who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and reconnect with nature. With sandy beaches, forests, and abundant wildlife, Ocean Shores is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all that this beautiful coastal city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean Shores

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Coastal Interpretive Center - This museum offers a unique learning experience for visitors to understand the history of the coastal region and its ecosystem. The center has interactive exhibits, a gift shop, and plenty of artifacts to interest visitors of all ages.
  2. Museum of the North Beach - This small museum showcases the history of Ocean Shores. Exhibits on display include photographs of historic homes, businesses, and logging operations.
  3. Grays Harbor Lighthouse - Located in the nearby Westport, this 107-foot lighthouse has been guiding ships to safety since 1898. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean.

Natural Attractions of Ocean Shores: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Damon Point - This 61-acre peninsula features sandy beaches, hiking trails, and scenic views of the Pacific Ocean. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching, beachcombing, and fishing while taking in the natural beauty of the area.
  2. North Jetty - Located at the entrance to Grays Harbor, North Jetty is a popular spot for fishing and surfing. It also has a sandy beach where visitors can enjoy sunbathing and beachcombing.
  3. Ocean City State Park - This state park features over 170 acres of beach and dunes. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, clamming, and picnicking while taking in the stunning ocean views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean Shores

  1. Ocean Shores Interpretive Trail - This 1.5-mile trail runs through a pine forest and sand dunes and is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride.
  2. Oyhut Wildlife Recreation Area - This 683-acre recreation area has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the coastal landscape and the wildlife that call it home.
  3. North Beach Trail - This 20.5-mile trail runs along the coast and is perfect for a scenic bike ride or hike. The trail offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Ocean Shores: Activities and Tours

  1. Beachcombing - Ocean Shores has several miles of sandy beaches that are perfect for beachcombing. Visitors can find seashells, agates, and even driftwood at low tide.
  2. Horseback Riding - Horseback riding is a popular activity in Ocean Shores, and several stables offer guided tours.
  3. Golfing - Ocean Shores has several golf courses, including the Ocean Shores Golf Course and the Ocean Shores Country Club.
  4. Water Sports - Visitors can enjoy kayaking, paddleboarding, and even surfing in the Pacific Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean Shores

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish and Chips - Ocean Shores is known for its delicious fish and chips. Several restaurants, including Bennett’s Fish Shack and Galway Bay, offer this classic dish.
  2. Clam Chowder - Clam chowder is another popular dish in Ocean Shores. It’s served in many of the local restaurants, including the Green Lantern Pub and Salty Dogs.
  3. Seafood Platter - If you’re a seafood lover, you’ll love the seafood platters in Ocean Shores. Several restaurants, including Ocean Crest Dining, offer fresh seafood platters.

Local Beverages

  1. Cranberry Wine - Ocean Shores is located in the heart of Washington’s cranberry country. Several wineries, including the Westport Winery, offer cranberry wine.
  2. Craft Beer - Several local breweries, including the North Jetty Brewery and Sideways Brewing, offer a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Ocean Shores: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Quinault Beach Resort and Casino - This resort offers luxurious accommodations and a variety of amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and casino.
  2. Surfcrest Condominiums - These condominiums offer stunning ocean views and private beach access.
  3. The Canterbury Inn - This charming inn offers cozy accommodations and a central location in Ocean Shores.

What to Buy in Ocean Shores: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Seashells - The sandy beaches of Ocean Shores offer plenty of seashells to take home as a souvenir.
  2. Cranberry Products - As mentioned earlier, Ocean Shores is located in the heart of Washington’s cranberry country. Visitors can bring home cranberry jams, jellies, and other local products.
  3. Beach Themed Gifts - Several local shops, including Sharky’s and Ocean Shores Kites, offer unique beach-themed gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean Shores

Ocean Shores is a friendly and welcoming community that values its natural surroundings. Visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and take care of the wildlife and local flora. The city also has a rich Native American history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn more about it through the interpretive center and other local attractions.

Best Time to Visit Ocean Shores

The best time to visit Ocean Shores is during the summer months when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. However, spring and fall can also be beautiful, with smaller crowds and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocean Shores

Do:

  1. Respect the natural environment
  2. Learn about the local culture and history
  3. Enjoy the seafood and local products

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash on the beaches or in the parks
  2. Feed the wildlife
  3. Disturb the natural habitat of the local flora and fauna

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocean Shores

Ocean Shores is a safe community, but visitors should take precautions when swimming in the Pacific Ocean. Rip currents can be dangerous, and visitors should always swim near a lifeguard station.

Health in Ocean Shores

Visitors should be aware of the sun and take precautions to avoid sunburn. It’s also a good idea to bring insect repellent as mosquitoes and other insects can be present in the area.

Getting to Ocean Shores: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean Shores

The closest major airport to Ocean Shores is the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, located approximately 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Ocean Shores

The closest bus station to Ocean Shores is the Greyhound station in Aberdeen, located approximately 20 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Ocean Shores by car or by booking a shuttle service.

How to Get Around in Ocean Shores

Visitors can rent a car or bike to get around Ocean Shores. Walking is also a great option, as many of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Ocean Shores: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Olympic National Park - Located approximately 2 hours away by car, Olympic National Park features miles of hiking and biking trails and beautiful natural scenery.
  2. Portland - Located approximately 3 hours away by car, Portland is a vibrant city known for its food, arts, and cultural scene.
  3. Seattle - Located approximately 2.5 hours away by car, Seattle offers a wide variety of attractions, including the Space Needle, Pike Place Market, and the Museum of Pop Culture.

Conclusion

Ocean Shores is a beautiful coastal city offering plenty of outdoor recreational activities, stunning natural scenery, and a rich cultural history. Visitors can enjoy hiking, kayaking, beachcombing, and exploring local museums and historical sites. With its friendly community, welcoming local culture, and variety of attractions, Ocean Shores is the perfect destination for a relaxing and memorable vacation.
