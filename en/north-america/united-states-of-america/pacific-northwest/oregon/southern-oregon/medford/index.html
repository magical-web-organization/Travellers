Introduction to Medford

Located in Southern Oregon, Medford is a charming city with plenty of outdoor activities and cultural attractions to offer. Known for its mild climate and beautiful scenery, this city enjoys a reputation for being the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. In this guide, we will take you through some of the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and what to buy in Medford, Oregon.

List of Places to Visit in Medford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southern Oregon Historical Society

This museum talks about the history of Southern Oregon, dating back to the days of the Native American tribes that called the area home. The museum features exhibits on local pioneers, mining, and logging. Additionally, it offers guided tours, educational programs, and events throughout the year.

Crater Rock Museum

The Crater Rock Museum is situated in Central Point and focuses on geological artifacts from both Oregon and around the world. The museum has several exhibits, including rocks, minerals, and fossils that are educational and informative for both adults and children.

Natural Attractions of Medford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Crater Lake National Park

This national park is just a few miles from Medford and is one of the most picturesque destinations in the region. The lake was formed by the explosion of Mount Mazama, and it’s known for its crystal-clear waters and stunning views of surrounding cliffs. Visitors can go on guided tours, hikes, or simply take in the incredible views of the lake.

Table Rocks

Located just outside of Medford, Table Rocks offers scenic views of the surrounding valley and is perfect for hiking and bird watching. The hiking trails are well-marked, and visitors can spend the day on one of the picnic tables available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medford

Cascade-Siskiyou National Monument

This national monument is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The hiking trails are ideal for both beginners and experienced hikers. The monument also features more than 100 miles of off-road bike trails, making it a favorite destination for bikers too.

Best Things to Do in Medford: Activities and Tours

Rogue River Rafting

The Rogue River Rafting Company offers a fun and exciting whitewater rafting experience for visitors. With half-day or full-day trips, visitors will enjoy the beautiful scenery as they coast down the river. Trained guides are available to take them through the rapids and provide information about the area.

Harry & David Factory Tours

Harry & David is a company that produces gourmet food products and gift baskets. A visit to the factory offers visitors an opportunity to take a guided tour and learn about the production process. Visitors can also purchase souvenirs and try samples of their gourmet foods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Medford

Day 1: Visit Rogue River Gorge and Natural Bridge State Park

Day 2: Go on a guided Crater Lake tour

Day 3: Get lost in the beauty of Ashland’s Lithia Park

Day 4: Indulge in some local food at Medford’s vibrant downtown

Day 5: Explore the passionate world of wine in the surrounding vineyards

Day 6: Go on an adventure ATV tour in the Table Rocks

Day 7: Visit Jacksonville and enjoy their eclectic mix of shops, restaurants, and art galleries

Food and Drink Scene of Medford

Must-Try Dishes

Humboldt Fog Cheese

This local favorite is a goat cheese, which is spreadable with a smooth texture and a sharp flavor. It is often served with crackers or crusty bread.

Roasting Company

The Roasting Company is a coffee shop that has been around for over 20 years, known for its excellent coffee and espresso drinks.

Local Beverages

BeerWorks Medford

This is a local brewpub that serves up a variety of craft beers and ciders on draft. Visitors can enjoy their beer flights accompanied by mouth-watering pizzas, sandwiches, and salads.

Where to Stay in Medford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget

Motel 6

A perfect place to spend the night before early-morning flights or to make as a base for exploring the surrounding area.

Mid-Range

SpringHill Suites Medford

Located downtown, this hotel offers its guests a comfortable and convenient location, with modern rooms that feature a kitchenette.

Luxury

Ashland Springs Hotel

This iconic hotel is situated in the heart of Ashland’s charming downtown and offers its visitors an old-world charm combined with modern amenities such as a spa, a restaurant, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Medford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pear Cider

This beverage is a local favorite and is a delicious way to enjoy fall in Southern Oregon.

Oregon Cheese

Southern Oregon has an abundance of artisan cheese makers, and you will find unique and flavorful cheeses that will make perfect souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Medford

Medford is a friendly community with a laid-back vibe, so visitors are expected to be polite while respecting the local customs. If you’re hiking or on a trail walk, don’t forget to carry lots of water, and always be mindful of the area’s wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Medford

The best time to visit Medford is during the fall, from September through November, when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medford

Do:

  • Explore the natural landscape through hiking and outdoor adventures
  • Enjoy locally crafted beer and wine
  • Embrace the local arts and culture scene

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the natural environment
  • Forget to carry loads of water when hiking
  • Make unnecessary noise and disturb residents

Safety and Health

Safety in Medford

Medford is a safe city, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions against petty theft, especially in crowded public places.

Health in Medford

The city has adequate health facilities with several hospitals and health clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have insurance coverage before visiting the area.

Getting to Medford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Medford

The Rogue Valley International-Medford Airport is located in the city of Medford and serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Medford

Greyhound Bus Lines serves Medford with regular bus trips that run to destinations near and far.

How to Get Around in Medford

Buses, taxis, and car rental services are available in the town centre, making it easy to travel the area’s many attractions.

Where to Go Next from Medford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ashland

This unique and lovely city is only 20 minutes from the town center. It is home to the famed Oregon Shakespeare Festival, with year-round shows, and plays held at the historic Allen Elizabethan Theatre.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a trip to Medford is worth every minute with its beautiful scenery, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. This guide provides visitors with an itinerary that covers all the must-see sites, traditional cuisine, and local culture while considering health, safety, and transportation options. We hope you enjoy your visit to this lovely city.
