Introduction to Grants Pass

Grants Pass is a small town located in Southern Oregon, in Pacific Northwest, United States. It is situated at the confluence of the Rogue and Applegate Rivers, surrounded by scenic mountains and forests. Grants Pass offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and a slow-paced lifestyle. With its warm and welcoming community, Grants Pass is the perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Grants Pass

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rogue River Indian War Museum: A tribute to the Native American tribes and their struggles during the Rogue River Wars.
  • Grants Pass Museum of Art: A showcase of local and regional art, sculptures, and paintings.
  • Schwarz Park Historical Marker: This spot marks the site of the long-lost town of Leland, which was swept away in a flood in 1890.

Natural Attractions of Grants Pass: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rogue River: A serene and scenic river that offers fishing, rafting, kayaking, and jet boat tours.
  • Hellgate Canyon: A popular spot for hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing.
  • TouVelle State Recreation Site: A beautiful park with picnic areas, fishing spots, and swimming holes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grants Pass

  • Lower Rogue River Trail: A 3-mile trek that follows the banks of the Rogue River, passing through lush forests and scenic vistas.
  • Rogue River Trail: A 40-mile trail that runs along the Rogue River, offering diverse terrain and beautiful views.
  • Mount Ashland Trail: A challenging hike that offers panoramic views of the Cascade Range and Siskiyou Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Grants Pass: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting and Fishing: Rogue River is famous for its whitewater rafting and salmon fishing.
  • Jet Boat Tours: Experience the thrill of a jet boat tour down the Rogue River.
  • Wine Tasting: Grants Pass is home to several award-winning wineries, offering wine tasting tours and events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grants Pass

Food and Drink Scene of Grants Pass

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oregonian cuisine: Featuring fresh local produce, grilled salmon, and artisan wines.
  • Southern-style barbecue: Slow-roasted meats, homemade sauces, and classic side dishes.
  • Thai cuisine: Exotic spices, savory curries, and vibrant flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Rogue River Brewing Company: Award-winning microbrewery offering beer flights and pub fare.
  • Wooldridge Creek Winery: Organic winery offering tastings and cheeses made on-site.
  • Dutch Bros Coffee: A local coffee chain famous for its delicious coffee and smoothies.

Where to Stay in Grants Pass: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Redwood Hyperion Suites: Located in the heart of downtown, this boutique hotel offers spacious suites with modern amenities.
  • Best Western Grants Pass Inn: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and an outdoor pool.
  • The Willows Bed and Breakfast: A charming Victorian-style B&B with cozy rooms and personalized service.

What to Buy in Grants Pass: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rogue Creamery cheeses: Award-winning artisanal cheeses made with local milk and traditional techniques.
  • Cedarwood products: Handcrafted wooden bowls, boxes, and utensils made from Oregon cedar.
  • Rogue Valley wines: Regional wines including Tempranillo, Pinot Noir, and Chardonnay.

Local Culture and Customs of Grants Pass

Grants Pass is a friendly and laid-back community, with a strong appreciation for nature and local traditions. People here are warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to interact with locals and learn about their way of life. Respect for nature, wildlife, and the environment is highly valued, and visitors are expected to follow local customs and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Grants Pass

The best time to visit Grants Pass is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall are also pleasant times to visit, with cooler temperatures and fewer crowds. Winter can be rainy and cold, but offers opportunities for indoor activities and snow sports in nearby mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grants Pass

Do:

  • Do respect the environment and follow the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Do engage with locals and learn about their culture and traditions.
  • Do try local food and drinks, and support small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, smoke, or deface public property.
  • Don’t disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grants Pass

Grants Pass is a safe community, with low crime rates and a friendly and helpful police force. However, visitors should use common sense and take precautions against theft, especially in crowded areas and tourist spots. Emergency services are available 24/7, and visitors should consult local authorities for any safety concerns or emergencies.

Health in Grants Pass

Grants Pass has several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, providing quality healthcare services to residents and visitors. Visitors should have travel insurance and be prepared for any illnesses or injuries. The town is also home to several wellness centers, spas, and holistic health facilities, offering a range of treatments and therapies.

Getting to Grants Pass: Transportation Options and Logistics

Grants Pass is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. It is located off Interstate 5, and several major airlines and bus companies have direct routes to the town. There are also several regional airports and bus stations nearby. Visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi or Uber for easy transportation within the town and surrounding areas.

Airports Near Grants Pass

  • Rogue Valley International-Medford Airport (MFR): 30 miles northeast of Grants Pass
  • Klamath Falls Airport (LMT): 80 miles southeast of Grants Pass

Bus Stations Near Grants Pass

  • Greyhound Bus Station: 2 miles south of downtown Grants Pass

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is in Klamath Falls, 78 miles southeast of Grants Pass.

How to Get Around in Grants Pass

Grants Pass is a small town, and visitors can easily walk or bike around the downtown area. There are also several options for public transportation, such as buses and taxis. Car rentals are available for those who prefer to explore the town and surrounding areas at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Grants Pass: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ashland: A charming town known for its Shakespearian plays, art galleries, and scenic parks.
  • Crater Lake National Park: A stunning natural wonder with a deep blue lake and rugged mountains.
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: A unique park with volcanic landscapes, hot springs, and scenic trails.

Conclusion

Grants Pass offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure, making it a perfect destination for travelers looking for a peaceful getaway. From scenic river tours, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions to excellent food, wine, and souvenirs, Grants Pass has something for everyone. Whether you are planning a romantic escape, family vacation or solo adventure, you will find this travel guide helpful in planning your itinerary and experiencing the best of Grants Pass.
