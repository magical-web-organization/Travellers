Introduction to Merlin

Merlin is a small unincorporated community located in Southern Oregon, in Josephine County. It is surrounded by the beautiful Rogue River and its tributaries, offering stunning natural attractions and outdoor adventures for visitors. Aside from nature, there are also cultural and historical sites worth exploring. Merlin is a peaceful and welcoming community, perfect for those seeking a serene getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Merlin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Schmidt House Museum. The museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Josephine County and the pioneers who made it their home. Visitors can learn about the region’s early inhabitants, mining industry, and the Great Depression.
  • Grants Pass Museum of Art. The museum features local artists and works from across the globe. Exhibits and events celebrate the region’s rich creativity and artistic talent.

Natural Attractions of Merlin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rogue River. The river runs through the heart of Merlin, providing opportunities such as swimming, fishing, and rafting.
  • Hellgate Jetboat Excursions. Take a scenic journey up the Rogue River with Hellgate Jetboats. The company offers a range of tours, from mild to wild, with knowledgeable guides to share stories of the area.
  • Wildlife Safari. The safari allows visitors to observe various animals in their natural habitat. From giraffes to bears, the park has a range of creatures to fascinate.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merlin

  • Rogue River Greenway. This is an easy hike and bike trail that runs along the Rogue River and Grants Pass. The trail is perfect for families and offers scenic views.
  • Hellgate Canyon Trail. This is a moderate hike in the Rogue River Canyon. The trail offers views of rock formations and the river.
  • Upper Rogue River Trail. This is a challenging hike through old-growth forest and beautiful streams. The trail is best suited for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Merlin: Activities and Tours

  • Rogue River Rafting. The rapids of the Rogue River provide a thrilling adventure for visitors. There are different rafting companies to choose from, each offering various tours and levels of difficulty.
  • Fishing. The Rogue River is home to a variety of fish, including salmon and steelhead. Fishing enthusiasts can hire a guide or bring their gear for a chance to catch a big one.
  • Wine Tasting. Southern Oregon is famous for its wine. Visitors can sign up for wine-tasting tours, or visit the wineries themselves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Merlin

Food and Drink Scene of Merlin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rogue Valley Cheese. A local cheese made with milk from Southern Oregon’s dairies. It has a nutty and mild flavor, perfect for pairing with crackers and wine.
  • Wild-Caught Salmon. Southern Oregon is known for its wild salmon, which can be found in many restaurants in the area.
  • Huckleberry Pie. Huckleberries grow wild in the region and are used to make delicious pies.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer. The Southern Oregon brewing scene has been growing rapidly in recent years. There are many breweries in the area offering unique and flavorful beers.
  • Wine. Southern Oregon is home to several prestigious vineyards and wineries. Tasting rooms are located throughout the area for visitors to sample the local wines.

Where to Stay in Merlin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Inn at Rogue River. This is a cozy bed and breakfast situated along the Rogue River. Guests can enjoy a hot tub, sauna, and comfortable rooms.
  • Entire House with River View. A beautiful and spacious house with a stunning river view. The house can accommodate up to 4 guests and is perfect for families or groups.
  • Wildlife Inn. This inn is located near Wildlife Safari and offers comfortable rooms with all the necessary amenities.

What to Buy in Merlin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Products. There are various huckleberry products, such as jams, syrups, and chocolates, available in souvenir and gift shops.
  • Local Art. Grants Pass Museum of Art has a gift shop that sells unique and beautiful pieces by local artists.
  • Rogue Valley Cheese. Purchase some cheese made from the local dairies to take home and share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Merlin

The people of Merlin are friendly and hospitable. The region has a rich cultural heritage, with a focus on preserving the natural environment. Visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and to be mindful of their impact on it.

Best Time to Visit Merlin

The best time to visit Merlin is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and rafting. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit with the changing colors of the leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Merlin

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Ask locals for recommendations and advice

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind
  • Disturb the wildlife
  • Trespass on private property

Safety and Health

Safety in Merlin

Merlin is a safe community with a low crime rate. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take extra precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Merlin

The nearest hospital to Merlin is Three Rivers Medical Center in Grants Pass, which provides excellent care for both residents and visitors. It is recommended to bring essential medications and make sure to have proper healthcare insurance when traveling to Merlin.

Getting to Merlin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Merlin

  • Rogue Valley International-Medford Airport (30 miles)

Bus Stations Near Merlin

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Grants Pass (10 miles)

Other Options

  • Renting a car is the best option to explore Merlin and the surrounding areas thoroughly.

How to Get Around in Merlin

The best way to get around Merlin is by car. There are rental car companies in Grants Pass and the nearest airport. Cabs and Ubers are available as well, but they might not be as readily available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Merlin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Crater Lake National Park (125 miles). One of the most beautiful national parks with a stunning lake and hiking trails.
  • Ashland (50 miles). A charming town with a renowned Shakespeare festival, interesting boutique shops, wineries, and restaurants.
  • Redwoods National Park (120 miles). An iconic park with giant redwood trees and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Merlin offers an enchanting getaway for visitors who love nature, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences. With the stunning Rogue River and tributaries, visitors can expect unforgettable adventures and relaxation. From hikes through old-growth forests to rafting in the rapids, there is something for everyone in Merlin.
