Travel Guide to Southern Oregon

Nestled in the Pacific Northwest, Southern Oregon boasts stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and endless opportunities for adventure. From top-rated museums and historical sites to scenic hiking and biking trails, you will find something for everyone.

Introduction to Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon is a region that covers a swath of land from the Cascade Mountains to the Pacific Ocean. It is home to cities like Medford, Ashland, Grants Pass, and dozens of small towns. The region is known for its vast outdoor spaces, wineries, and cultural attractions. With four distinct seasons, Southern Oregon offers something unique all year round.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Oregon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southern Oregon has a rich cultural history that is well preserved in its museums and historical sites. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Crater Lake National Park: Known for its picturesque beauty, Crater Lake is one of Southern Oregon’s most iconic attractions. It’s the deepest lake in North America and sits inside a caldera formed by the explosion of Mount Mazama over 7,000 years ago.
  • Oregon Caves National Monument: This natural wonder is carved into marble and limestone and takes visitors through winding passageways and unique geological formations.
  • Historic Jacksonville: This 1850s gold mining town is preserved as an outdoor museum and offers a glimpse into Oregon’s past.
  • Southern Oregon Historical Society: This organization preserves local history through its many museums and exhibits located throughout the region.

Natural Attractions of Southern Oregon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Oregon is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Rogue River: The Rogue River stretches over 200 miles, providing ample opportunities for rafting, fishing, and hiking.
  • Applegate Valley: This picturesque valley is dotted with vineyards and offers one of the best wine tours in the region.
  • Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area: Visitors can explore the “Sahara of the West” by hiking or riding ATVs over towering sand dunes.
  • Mount Ashland: This mountain offers an array of outdoor activities from skiing to hiking in the summer months; it’s a must-visit for adventure lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon boasts spectacular hiking and biking trails, from easy walks to challenging treks. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Cascade-Siskiyou National Monument: This monument has trails for all skill levels, and visitors can enjoy stunning views of wildflowers, wildlife, and geological formations.
  • Rogue River Trail: This winding trail follows the Rogue River, providing a beautiful glimpse into the region’s natural beauty.
  • Pacific Crest Trail: The Pacific Crest Trail runs along the Cascade Range and offers endless opportunities for hiking and backpacking.

Best Things to Do in Southern Oregon: Activities and Tours

Southern Oregon offers numerous activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Oregon Shakespeare Festival: This world-renowned festival stages plays by the legendary playwright, William Shakespeare, as well as other classics and contemporary works.
  • Wine Tours: Southern Oregon has some of the world’s best wine, and there are numerous wineries offering tours and tastings.
  • Rafting and Fishing: The Rogue River provides ample opportunities for rafting and fishing.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Visitors can take in Southern Oregon’s stunning scenery while floating over the region in a hot air balloon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Oregon

For a perfect Southern Oregon experience, here is a sample week-long itinerary:

Day 1 Arrival in Medford, explore Downtown Medford
Day 2 Visit Crater Lake National Park, drive to Ashland
Day 3 Explore Ashland and attend the Oregon Shakespeare Festival
Day 4 Visit Jacksonville and tour the historic sites
Day 5 Go hiking on the Pacific Crest Trail and stop at Applegate Valley on the way back
Day 6 Take a hot air balloon ride over the region
Day 7 Rafting trip on the Rogue River and night camping

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon has a thriving food and drink scene, with a focus on local produce and regional specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pear Pizza: Made with fresh pears grown in the region, this thin-crust pizza is a favorite in Southern Oregon.
  • Rogue Creamery Cheese: This award-winning cheese is made in the market town of Central Point, Oregon, and is a must-try for cheese lovers.
  • Hazelnuts: Oregon hazelnuts are said to be the best in the world and can be found in many dishes throughout the region.

Local Beverages

  • Rogue Ale: This beer is brewed with locally grown hops and is a favorite among beer enthusiasts.
  • Applegate Valley Wine: This wine is world-class and should not be missed by wine lovers.
  • Pear Cider: Made with fresh pears, this refreshing drink is a local favorite.

Where to Stay in Southern Oregon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Oregon has a wide variety of accommodations, from five-star hotels to rustic campgrounds. Here are some options:

  • Ashland Springs Hotel: A beautiful historic hotel located in the heart of Ashland, offering exceptional service and comfort.
  • Morrison’s Rogue River Lodge: A rustic lodge located on the Rogue River, perfect for those wanting a peaceful retreat.
  • Rogue River Ranch: This former cattle ranch has been transformed into a rustic destination for nature lovers and offers cabins and campsites.

What to Buy in Southern Oregon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Oregon has many unique products that make for great souvenirs. Here are some:

  • Oregon Lavender Products: Southern Oregon is renowned for its lavender fields, and you can find lavender-based products like lotions, soaps, and candles.
  • Oregon Wines and Craft Beers: Bring home a taste of the region with a bottle of wine or a craft beer from one of Southern Oregon’s many wineries and breweries.
  • Handmade Crafts from Artisan Fairs: Southern Oregon has numerous artisan fairs where you can find unique handmade crafts like pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by Native American, Spanish, and American settlers. The region has a relaxed vibe, with outdoor activities playing a significant role in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon is beautiful all year round, but the best time to visit is during the shoulder season spring and fall months (April, May, September, and October). The weather is mild, and the crowds tend to be smaller, making it easier to enjoy the many attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Oregon

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the region
  • Try the local wines and craft beers
  • Take the time to explore the many outdoor activities the region has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Take anything from protected areas like Crater Lake National Park.
  • Ignore safety precautions for outdoor activities like rafting and hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon is generally safe, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when exploring the wilderness. Here are some tips:

  • Always carry a first-aid kit when hiking or camping.
  • Know the weather forecast and plan your activities accordingly.
  • Don’t stray off marked trails, and be aware of wildlife in the area.

Health in Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon has an excellent healthcare system, with several medical centers and emergency services located throughout the region. Visitors should be prepared with adequate travel health insurance coverage.

Getting to Southern Oregon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southern Oregon is accessible via several transportation options.

Airports Near Southern Oregon

  • Rogue Valley International-Medford Airport (MFR): This airport is centrally located in the region and serves various international and domestic destinations.

Bus Stations Near Southern Oregon

  • Greyhound: This national bus service offers various routes to and from major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular way to explore the region independently.
  • Shuttle Services: Several shuttle services offer transportation to and from major attractions and destinations within Southern Oregon.

How to Get Around in Southern Oregon

Southern Oregon has a well-connected transportation system with various options:

  • Car: Renting a car is a popular and practical way to explore Southern Oregon.
  • Bike: Biking is a popular way to explore the many trails and routes in the region.
  • Public Transportation: Several local bus services, including Rogue Valley Transportation District and Josephine Community Transit, serve the region.

Where to Go Next from Southern Oregon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Oregon is located in a region full of exciting and beautiful destinations that are worth exploring, including:

  • Northern California: Explore the diverse landscape of Northern California, from the beaches of the Pacific Coast to the mountains of the Sierra Nevada.
  • Portland, Oregon: Known for its vibrant arts scene, food scene, and vintage shopping.
  • Seattle, Washington: Discover the Emerald City’s many attractions, from the Space Needle to the Pike Place Market.

Conclusion

Southern Oregon has something for everyone, from natural beauty to rich cultural heritage to exciting outdoor activities. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or exploring the region for the first time, Southern Oregon should be on your bucket list. Plan your trip today and explore one of the most beautiful regions in the world.
