Introduction to North Bend

North Bend is a charming city located on the Southern Oregon Coast in the United States. It sits in Coos County, a region that is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor recreation, and rich history. The city is home to a population of over 9,700 residents and provides visitors with an array of fun activities, attractions, and amenities.

North Bend’s history is steeped in the fishing industry. The deep-water port of Coos Bay has always been a bustling hub of commercial fishing. North Bend was founded in the early 1850s when the first shipping dock was built here. The city has since grown to become a major hub for tourism, with visitors coming from all over the world to explore its many attractions. Whether you’re looking for outdoor activities, cultural landmarks, fantastic food, or simply a chance to relax on the coast, North Bend has it all.

List of Places to Visit in North Bend

Here are some of the top attractions and places to visit in North Bend:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Coos Art Museum

The Coos Art Museum is a fantastic place to visit for lovers of art and culture. It features a wide range of exhibits and collections that showcase the history of Coos County, regional arts, and contemporary art.

The Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area

The Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area is a natural wonder that should not be missed. It spreads across a 40-mile stretch of coastline and is home to an array of unique landscapes that include sand dunes, beaches, forested areas, and wetlands. The area is also known for its rich plant and animal life, and there are many opportunities for hiking, biking, camping, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Watson Ranch Historical Complex

The Watson Ranch Historical Complex provides visitors with a glimpse into the region’s past. It features several historic buildings, including a schoolhouse, a church, and a general store. There are also exhibits that showcase the settlement of the area by Native Americans and early pioneers.

Natural Attractions of North Bend: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shore Acres State Park

Shore Acres State Park is a popular destination for those who love nature. It has a rugged coastline, stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, beautiful gardens, and a historic mansion. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails that meander through the forested areas or relax on the beach and watch the waves roll in.

Cape Arago State Park

Cape Arago State Park is another beautiful park located in North Bend. It features stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, hiking trails, tide pools, and an array of wildlife. Visitors can also see the historic Cape Arago Lighthouse, which is perched on a rocky promontory overlooking the sea.

South Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve

The South Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve is a great place to learn about the ecology of the region. It features over 5,000 acres of marshes, tidal channels, and forests that are home to an array of plant and animal life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Bend

North Bend has an array of hiking and biking trails that cater to both beginners and experienced hikers.

Golden and Silver Falls Trail

The Golden and Silver Falls Trail is a popular hike that takes visitors through a scenic forested area to two stunning waterfalls. The trail is 4.6 miles long and is considered moderate in difficulty.

Oregon Dunes Trail

The Oregon Dunes Trail is a fantastic place to explore on foot or by bike. It is a 6-mile trail that takes visitors through the dunes and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in North Bend: Activities and Tours

Crabbing and Fishing

Crabbing and fishing are two popular activities to enjoy in North Bend. Visitors can rent a boat or join a guided tour to explore the waters off the coast of North Bend and try their hand at catching a variety of fish species.

Coos Historical and Maritime Center Tours

The Coos Historical and Maritime Center offers guided tours that take visitors through the history of the Coos County region, as well as its maritime heritage. The tours are led by knowledgeable guides and showcase both indoor and outdoor exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Bend

Day 1: Arrival and Exploration

Take a stroll around North Bend’s charming downtown area and explore its many restaurants, cafes, and shops. Visit the Coos Art Museum, and get a taste of the region’s art and culture.

Day 2: Outdoor Adventures at the Oregon Dunes

Head to the Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area and explore its hiking and biking trails. Take a ride on an ATV or go horseback riding on the dunes.

Day 3: Discovering the Beaches

Visit Shore Acres State Park and enjoy the stunning ocean views. Take a stroll on the beach and explore the tide pools.

Day 4: Fishing and Crabbing

Join a guided fishing or crabbing tour and try your hand at catching some seafood. Learn about the region’s commercial fishing industry and the methods used.

Day 5: Cultural Experience

Visit the Watson Ranch Historical Complex and Coos Historical and Maritime Center, and learn about the history of the Coos County region, as well as its maritime heritage.

Day 6: Relaxation

Take a day to relax and unwind. Visit a local spa, beach, or simply spend the day lounging at your hotel.

Day 7: Departure

Before departing, make sure to stop by some of North Bend’s local shops to pick up some souvenirs and gifts to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of North Bend

Must-Try Dishes

Oregon Bay Shrimp

Oregon Bay Shrimp is a popular local dish that is enjoyed in many restaurants in the region. It is often served as a salad or in a sandwich.

Crab

Crab is another popular seafood dish that can be found in many restaurants in North Bend. It is often served steamed or in a soup.

Local Beverages

Beer

North Bend is home to several local breweries that produce some of the best beer in the region. Some of the best-known local breweries include 7 Devils Brewing Co. and North Bend Ale Trail.

Wine

Wine lovers can also enjoy some of the local wines produced in the nearby vineyards, such as Girardet Vineyards.

Where to Stay in North Bend: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Bend has an array of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from upscale hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts.

The Mill Casino Hotel & RV Park

The Mill Casino Hotel & RV Park is a fantastic place to stay if you’re looking for comfortable accommodations and easy access to the region’s top attractions. It features luxurious guest rooms, a casino, and several restaurants and bars.

Bay Bridge Motel

The Bay Bridge Motel is a family-friendly motel that offers comfortable accommodations at affordable prices. It is located in the heart of North Bend and provides visitors with easy access to the region’s top attractions.

What to Buy in North Bend: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Seafood

North Bend is famous for its seafood, and visitors can bring home some of the best seafood products, including smoked salmon, Dungeness crab, and seafood sauces.

Local Art and Crafts

Visitors can purchase locally-made art and crafts that showcase the region’s vibrant culture, including pottery, paintings, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of North Bend

North Bend has a rich and diverse culture that is celebrated through art, music, and food. Visitors should respect the locals’ customs by dressing appropriately, being mindful of the environment, and learning about the region’s cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit North Bend

The best time to visit North Bend is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the beautiful landscapes and scenic views during the spring and fall months.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Bend

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be mindful of the environment and leave no trace.
  • Go on guided tours to learn about the region’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Ignore safety precautions while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Bend

North Bend is a safe and friendly destination for visitors. However, visitors should be cautious while participating in outdoor activities and follow safety precautions to avoid accidents.

Health in North Bend

North Bend has a range of health facilities that cater to visitors’ needs, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should also bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect themselves from the sun and mosquitoes.

Getting to North Bend: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Bend is easily accessible by several transportation modes, including:

Airports Near North Bend

The Southwest Oregon Regional Airport is the closest airport to North Bend, with several airlines offering daily flights to and from the region.

Bus Stations Near North Bend

North Bend is also connected to several bus stations in the region, including the Greyhound Bus Station in Coos Bay.

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at the Southwest Oregon Regional Airport.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the region.

How to Get Around in North Bend

Visitors can get around North Bend by renting a car, taking a taxi or ride-sharing service, or by using public transport.

Where to Go Next from North Bend: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coos Bay

Coos Bay is North Bend’s sister city and is located just a few miles away. It is home to several cultural attractions, including the Coos History Museum and the Marshfield Sun Printing Museum.

Bandon

Bandon is a charming coastal town located 25 miles south of North Bend. It is famous for its picturesque harbor and stunning beaches, and it also features several golf courses and artisanal shops.

Conclusion

North Bend is a fantastic destination for visitors who want to experience the best of the Southern Oregon coastline. It has everything from natural wonders and outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks, fantastic food, and comfortable accommodations. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation, North Bend is the perfect destination.
