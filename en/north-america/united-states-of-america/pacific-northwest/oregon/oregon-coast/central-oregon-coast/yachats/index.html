Yachats Travel Guide: Explore the Best of Central Oregon Coast

Located in Central Oregon Coast, Yachats is a charming small town that offers an ideal combination of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, historical attractions, and unique cultural experiences. Whether you are looking for a place to relax and unwind, explore the great outdoors, or learn about the local history and traditions, Yachats is the perfect destination for you. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover the best things to do in Yachats, the top attractions, must-try dishes and drinks, best places to stay, and much more. Let’s explore this unique coastal town together!

Introduction to Yachats

Yachats is a small coastal town in Lincoln County, Oregon, situated between Florence and Newport on the Central Oregon Coast. The name “Yachats” comes from the Siletz language and means “dark water at the foot of the mountain.” With a population of around 700, Yachats is a quiet and peaceful town that attracts visitors who want to enjoy the natural beauty of the Pacific Ocean, hike in lush forests, and explore the unique local culture and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Yachats

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yachats Commons - The Yachats Commons is a historic building that is now used as a community center for events, workshops, and gatherings. It was built in 1921 and served as a school and Masonic hall.
  • Yachats Historical Museum - The Yachats Historical Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Yachats and its surrounding areas. It features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the region’s past, including Native American history, logging, and fishing industries.
  • Cape Perpetua Visitor Center - The Cape Perpetua Visitor Center is situated on a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean. It offers breathtaking views of the coast and hosts educational programs and events related to the area’s natural and cultural history.

Natural Attractions of Yachats: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Perpetua Scenic Area - The Cape Perpetua Scenic Area is a 2,700-acre forested park that features stunning coastal scenery, old-growth forests, and tide pools. It offers many hiking trails, including the 2.2-mile Captain Cook Trail and the 6.5-mile Oregon Coast Trail.
  • Thor’s Well - Thor’s Well is a natural wonder that is located near Cape Perpetua. It is a collapsed underwater cave that creates a powerful, mesmerizing effect as the waves crash into it and then recede.
  • Yachats Ocean Road State Natural Site - The Yachats Ocean Road State Natural Site is a beautiful shoreline area that stretches for 20 miles. It offers opportunities for beachcombing, birdwatching, and tidepooling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yachats

Yachats is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Cape Perpetua Trail System - This network of trails includes several options for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. The trails meander through lush forests, along rocky shorelines, and up to scenic viewpoints.
  • Oregon Coast Trail - The Oregon Coast Trail is a 382-mile trail that runs along the entire Oregon Coast, passing through Yachats. It offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and the rugged coastline.
  • Yachats 804 Trail - The Yachats 804 Trail is a scenic paved path that runs along the Yachats coast. It offers easy access to the beach and excellent views of the Pacific Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Yachats: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Yachats:

  • Whale Watching - Yachats is a prime location for whale watching. Gray whales migrate along the coast twice a year, in the spring and fall.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing - There are several opportunities to kayak or canoe along the peaceful and picturesque Yachats River.
  • Shopping at Local Art Galleries and Gift Shops - Yachats has many local gift shops and art galleries that offer unique souvenirs and handcrafted goods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yachats

Day 1: Arrive in Yachats, check in at your hotel/accommodation, and explore the historic downtown area.

Day 2: Hike in Cape Perpetua Scenic Area. Don’t miss Thor’s Well and Devil’s Churn.

Day 3: Participate in a whale watching tour or visit the Hatfield Marine Science Center in Newport.

Day 4: Visit the Yachats Historical Museum and explore the town’s local gift shops and art galleries.

Day 5: Go kayaking or canoeing on the Yachats River.

Day 6: Take a drive on the Pacific Coast Highway and explore nearby towns such as Florence and Depoe Bay.

Day 7: Relax on the beach, enjoy the sunset, and get ready for your departure the next day.

Food and Drink Scene of Yachats

When it comes to food and drinks, Yachats offers a unique blend of locally-sourced ingredients, fresh seafood, and craft beers. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes - Made from fresh Dungeness crab, these cakes are a popular local delicacy.
  • Fish and Chips - Yachats is known for its fresh and delicious fish and chips, made with locally-sourced fish.
  • Wild Mushroom Risotto - This savory and creamy dish incorporates fresh mushrooms for a delicious treat.

Local Beverages

  • Oregon Pinot Noir - Oregon is home to some of the world’s best Pinot Noir, and Yachats has several wineries that offer tastings.
  • Local Craft Beer - Yachats has several microbreweries that offer a wide variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and sours.

Where to Stay in Yachats: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yachats has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxurious oceanfront hotels to quaint bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Overleaf Lodge & Spa - This luxurious oceanfront hotel offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean, a spa, and a hot tub.
  • The Dublin House Motel - This cozy motel offers affordable rooms that are just a short walk from the beach.
  • Deane’s Oceanfront Lodge - This oceanfront lodge offers comfortable rooms, many of which have private balconies with gorgeous ocean views.

What to Buy in Yachats: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Pottery - Yachats has several local pottery studios that offer unique handmade pieces.
  • Locally-Sourced Honey - Yachats is home to several beekeepers who produce high-quality honey.
  • Artisanal Soaps and Skincare Products - Yachats has several shops that offer locally-made soaps, skincare products, and aromatherapy oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Yachats

Yachats has a strong sense of community and a deep appreciation for nature and the outdoors. The town hosts several events and festivals throughout the year, such as the Yachats Farmers Market, the Yachats Music Festival, and the Yachats Arts and Crafts Festival. It is also known for its commitment to sustainability and environmental stewardship.

Best Time to Visit Yachats

Yachats has a mild and temperate climate, with cool summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Yachats is during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall, when the weather is mild and crowds are smaller. However, summer is also a great time to visit, as the weather is warm and sunny, and there are many outdoor events and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yachats

  • Follow proper hiking and camping practices.
  • Take advantage of the many locally-sourced ingredients and fresh seafood.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife.
  • Ignore safety warnings or guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yachats

Yachats is a relatively safe place for tourists, but it’s always important to exercise caution and common sense while traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of weather conditions and ocean tides.
  • Stick to designated trails and avoid off-trail hiking.
  • Lock your car and don’t leave valuables visible.

Health in Yachats

Yachats has a small medical clinic that offers basic healthcare services. For more serious medical issues, you may need to travel to nearby cities such as Newport or Florence. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication and consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Yachats: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yachats

The closest airport to Yachats is the Eugene Airport, which is about 1.5 hours away by car. Other nearby airports include the Portland International Airport and the Seattle-Tacoma International Airport, which are both about 4 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Yachats

There are no bus stations in Yachats, but you can take a bus to nearby towns such as Newport or Florence. The nearest Greyhound station is in Eugene.

Other Options

The Pacific Coast Highway (US-101) runs through Yachats, making it easily accessible by car. If you’re traveling from Portland or Seattle, you can take the Amtrak Cascades train to the Eugene station and then rent a car or take a bus to Yachats.

How to Get Around in Yachats

Yachats is a small town that is easy to explore on foot or by bicycle. There are also several local companies that offer guided tours and transportation services.

Where to Go Next from Yachats: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yachats is located in a beautiful area of the Central Oregon Coast, and there are several nearby towns and attractions worth visiting:

  • Newport - Newport is a charming coastal town that is home to the Oregon Coast Aquarium, historic lighthouses, and scenic beaches.
  • Florence - Florence is another coastal town that offers opportunities for hiking, camping, and exploring the Siuslaw National Forest.
  • Cape Lookout State Park - Cape Lookout State Park is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and beachcombing.

Conclusion

Yachats is a unique and beautiful coastal town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or just a place to relax, Yachats is the perfect destination.
