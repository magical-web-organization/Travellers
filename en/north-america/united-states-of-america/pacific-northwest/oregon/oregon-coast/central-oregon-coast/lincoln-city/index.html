Introduction to Lincoln City

Located in Central Oregon Coast, Lincoln City is a charming coastal town with an impressive oceanfront. The city comprises seven miles of pristine beaches, serene waterways, lush hiking trails, and cultural landmarks. It’s a place where visitors can indulge in a range of outdoor activities while also enjoying the vibrant shopping scene, art galleries, museums, and excellent local cuisine. This guide will help you plan your trip to Lincoln City with suggestions for attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, you’ll enjoy exploring the cultural landmarks and museums in Lincoln City.

North Lincoln County Historical Museum

Established in 1987, the North Lincoln County Historical Museum is a treasure trove of local history. The museum displays an extensive collection of relics, artifacts, and memorabilia that showcase the story of the city’s evolution from a small fishing town to a popular tourist destination.

Lincoln City Cultural Center

The Lincoln City Cultural Center is a hub for art and culture in Lincoln City. The center hosts several events and workshops throughout the year, including music concerts, theater performances, art exhibits, and more.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lincoln City is full of natural wonders, including pristine beaches, scenic hiking trails, and serene waterways.

Devil’s Lake State Recreation Area

Devil’s Lake is a popular destination for water sports and outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, hiking, camping, and more at this picturesque state park.

Siletz Bay National Wildlife Refuge

The Siletz Bay National Wildlife Refuge is a bird watcher’s paradise. The refuge is home to over 250 species of birds, including eagles, ospreys, and pelicans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln City

Lincoln City has several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding landscape.

Cascade Head Trail

The Cascade Head Trail is a 6.5-mile round trip hike that offers stunning views of Cascade Head, the Pacific Ocean, and the Siletz Bay.

Roads End State Recreation Site

Roads End State Recreation Site is a popular spot for hiking and beachcombing. Visitors can explore the beach, hike the hill, and enjoy stunning views of the ocean.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln City: Activities and Tours

Lincoln City offers several exciting activities and tours that are perfect for visitors of all ages.

Whale Watching

Whale watching is one of the most popular activities in Lincoln City. Visitors can spot gray whales and humpback whales as they migrate along the coast.

Glass Blowing

Lincoln City is home to several glass blowing studios where visitors can watch artisans create stunning glass sculptures and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lincoln City

Day 1: Check into your hotel and explore local landmarks, such as the North Lincoln County Historical Museum and the Chinook Winds Casino Resort.

Day 2: Enjoy a scenic hike or bike ride on the Cascade Head Trail or Roads End State Recreation Site.

Day 3: Take a whale watching tour or visit a glass blowing studio.

Day 4: Hit the beach and enjoy the sun, surf, and sand.

Day 5: Explore the shops, art galleries, and restaurants in the city center.

Day 6: Discover the natural wonders at Devil’s Lake State Recreation Area or Siletz Bay National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 7: Relax and soak up the scenery before checking out and heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln City

Lincoln City offers a diverse range of dining options, from seafood shacks to fusion restaurants.

Must-Try Dishes

Clam Chowder

Clam chowder is a local specialty in Lincoln City. The creamy soup is made with fresh clams and potatoes, and seasoned with bacon and spices.

Dungeness Crab

Dungeness crab is a local delicacy that can be enjoyed in several restaurants in Lincoln City. The tender meat is served steamed, fried, or as part of a seafood platter.

Local Beverages

Oregon Pinot Noir

Oregon Pinot Noir is a world-renowned wine that is produced in several vineyards near Lincoln City. Visitors can tour the vineyards and sample the wine at the tasting rooms.

Craft Beer

Lincoln City has several breweries that produce craft beer. Visitors can enjoy a pint of their favorite brews and learn about the art of beer making.

Where to Stay in Lincoln City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lincoln City offers several accommodations options to meet different budgets and preferences.

Hotels

Overleaf Lodge & Spa

Overleaf Lodge & Spa is a luxurious oceanfront hotel that offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. The hotel features a spa, a pool, a hot tub, and a fitness center.

Best Western Plus Lincoln Sands Suites

Best Western Plus Lincoln Sands Suites is a comfortable and affordable hotel that offers spacious suites, an indoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast.

Hostels

International Hostel

International Hostel is a cozy and affordable hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms. The hostel features a kitchen, a lounge, and a garden.

Guesthouses

The Coho Oceanfront Lodge

The Coho Oceanfront Lodge is a charming guesthouse that offers cozy rooms with ocean views, a hot tub, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Lincoln City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lincoln City has several shops and boutiques that offer unique souvenirs and local products.

Glass Blown Artwork

Lincoln City is famous for its glass blowing art. Visitors can buy beautiful glass sculptures and souvenirs from local studios.

Saltwater Taffy

Saltwater Taffy is Lincoln City’s signature candy. Visitors can buy the sweet treat from several candy shops in the city center.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln City

Lincoln City is known for its vibrant arts and culture scene. Visitors are encouraged to explore the city’s museums, galleries, and cultural landmarks to discover the local heritage.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln City

The best time to visit Lincoln City is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is mild, and the water is warm, making it ideal for outdoor activities and beachcombing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln City

Do:

  • Do explore the city’s outdoor attractions, such as hiking trails and state parks.
  • Do sample the local cuisine, including seafood and craft beer.
  • Do visit the city’s museums and cultural landmarks to learn about the local heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in public areas.
  • Don’t swim alone, as the water can be treacherous in some areas.
  • Don’t miss out on the whale watching tours during the migration season.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln City

Lincoln City is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors are advised to take precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their valuables in their cars and being aware of their surroundings when walking in public areas.

Health in Lincoln City

Lincoln City has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Lincoln City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lincoln City

The closest airport to Lincoln City is the Portland International Airport, located about 120 miles north of the city. Visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle bus to reach Lincoln City.

Bus Stations Near Lincoln City

The closest bus station to Lincoln City is located in Salem, about 60 miles south of the city. Visitors can then take a taxi or rent a car to reach their destination.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Lincoln City from neighboring cities, including Portland, Eugene, and Salem.

How to Get Around in Lincoln City

The easiest way to get around Lincoln City is by car. Visitors can rent a car from the Portland International Airport or from several rental agencies in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Lincoln City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lincoln City is surrounded by several neighboring cities and attractions that are worth exploring.

Pacific City

Pacific City is a small town located about 20 miles south of Lincoln City. The town is home to the famous Haystack Rock, a massive rock formation that juts out from the ocean.

Newport

Newport is a seaside city located about 30 miles north of Lincoln City. The city is home to several attractions, including the Oregon Coast Aquarium, the Yaquina Head Lighthouse, and the Newport Historic Bayfront.

Conclusion

Lincoln City is a charming coastal town that offers something for everyone, from outdoor activities to cultural attractions, and a vibrant food scene. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan a memorable trip to Lincoln City and make the most of your time in this beautiful destination.
