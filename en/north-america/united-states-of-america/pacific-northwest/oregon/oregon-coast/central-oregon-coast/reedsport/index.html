Welcome to Reedsport, a charming city located in the heart of Central Oregon Coast. This seaside community is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, rich history, and abundance of outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore local museums and cultural attractions, or immerse yourself in the great outdoors, Reedsport has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top things to do in Reedsport, including where to stay, what to eat, and how to plan your visit.

Introduction to Reedsport

Reedsport is a small city in Douglas County, Oregon, situated at the convergence of the Umpqua River and the Pacific Ocean. It was founded in the late 19th century as a timber town, and later became a hub for shipping and fishing. Today, Reedsport is a popular tourist destination, offering activities ranging from hiking and biking to fishing and crabbing. The city has a population of around 4,000 people, making it a peaceful and relaxed place to unwind and enjoy the beauty of the Oregon Coast.

List of Places to Visit in Reedsport

Reedsport has a plethora of attractions to explore, from culturally important landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit in Reedsport:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Umpqua Discovery Center: This museum showcases the natural and cultural history of the Umpqua River and the Oregon Coast. Exhibits include interactive displays, live animals, and artifacts from local Native American tribes.

  • Historic Downtown Reedsport: This area boasts a variety of unique shops, restaurants, and historical buildings. You can take a self-guided walking tour of the downtown area to learn more about the city’s history and culture.

  • Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area: These towering dunes, which stretch for 40 miles along the coast, are the largest expanse of coastal sand dunes in North America. You can take a dune buggy tour or go hiking and camping in the area.

Natural Attractions of Reedsport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Winchester Bay: This small fishing village is home to a picturesque marina and sandy beach. You can go fishing, crabbing, and clamming in the bay, or simply relax on the beach and soak up the sun.

  • Dean Creek Elk Viewing Area: This wildlife area offers a chance to see hundreds of Roosevelt elk in their natural habitat. There are viewing areas and educational displays to learn more about these majestic creatures.

  • Smith River Falls: This stunning waterfall is located in the Siuslaw National Forest and is accessible via a short hike. It’s an ideal spot for picnicking and swimming on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reedsport

Reedsport is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with a variety of trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the top options:

  • Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area: This area offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking, including the John Dellenback Trail and the Tahkenitch Dunes Trail.

  • Reedsport to Coos Bay Rail Trail: This 32-mile trail runs along the Oregon Coastline and offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. You can bike or hike the trail at your own pace.

  • Umpqua Riverfront Trail: This 3.5-mile trail runs along the Umpqua River and offers scenic views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Reedsport: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day on the beach or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, Reedsport has plenty of activities to keep you busy. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Fishing and Crabbing: Reedsport is a popular destination for fishing and crabbing, with abundant opportunities to catch salmon, steelhead, and trout in the Umpqua River and nearby coastal waters.

  • Dune Buggy Tours: You can take a guided dune buggy tour through the Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area for an unforgettable adventure.

  • Umpqua River Jet Boat Tour: Experience the thrill of a high-speed jet boat tour through the beautiful Umpqua River Canyon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Reedsport

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Reedsport, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Check into your hotel and explore Historic Downtown Reedsport.

  • Day 2: Spend the day hiking the Oregon Dunes and taking a dune buggy tour.

  • Day 3: Visit the Umpqua Discovery Center and learn more about the history and culture of the area.

  • Day 4: Take a jet boat tour through the Umpqua River Canyon.

  • Day 5: Go fishing or crabbing in Winchester Bay.

  • Day 6: Hike to Smith River Falls and spend the rest of the day relaxing on the beach.

  • Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day in Reedsport, shopping, dining, and soaking up the local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Reedsport

Reedsport is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of local specialties to try and a great selection of restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oregon Coast Clam Chowder: This hearty soup is a staple in Reedsport and can be found at just about any local restaurant.

  • Dungeness Crab: This sweet, succulent crab is a local favorite and can be caught fresh in the nearby waters.

  • Pacific Northwest Salmon: Fresh, wild-caught salmon is a must-try when visiting Reedsport.

Local Beverages

  • Oregon Pinot Noir: This region is known for producing some of the world’s best Pinot Noir wines.

  • Oregon Microbrews: Reedsport has a variety of local breweries that produce some of the best craft beers in the Pacific Northwest.

  • Dungeness IPA: This hoppy, flavorful beer is brewed with locally grown hops and pays homage to the Dungeness crab.

What to Buy in Reedsport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Reedsport

Reedsport has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the Native American tribes who inhabited the region for centuries. Today, the city is a melting pot of cultural influences, with a thriving arts community and a love of outdoor adventure.

Best Time to Visit Reedsport

Reedsport is a great destination year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months, from June through September. During this time, you will experience warm weather and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and water sports.

Getting to Reedsport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Reedsport is most easily accessible by car, and the city is located off Highway 101. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Reedsport

  • Eugene Airport (EUG): Approximately a 2-hour drive from Reedsport.

  • Portland International Airport (PDX): Approximately a 4-hour drive from Reedsport.

Bus Stations Near Reedsport

  • Greyhound (Florence, OR): Approximately a 45-minute drive from Reedsport.

  • Sunset Empire Transportation District (Astoria, OR): Approximately a 3-hour drive from Reedsport.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The nearest Amtrak station is located in Eugene, approximately a 2-hour drive from Reedsport.

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental options available in Eugene and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Reedsport

Reedsport is a small city that is easily navigable by car and walking. Biking is also a popular option, and there are several rental companies available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Reedsport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
