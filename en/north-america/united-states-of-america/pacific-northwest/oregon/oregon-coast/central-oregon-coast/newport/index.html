Introduction to Newport

Newport is a picturesque city in central Oregon Coast, located on the Pacific Ocean. The city is the county seat of Lincoln County and is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, charming local culture, and rich history. Visitors to Newport can expect an eclectic mix of outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, delicious food, and much more. In this guide, we will explore the best things to do and see in Newport, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, hiking/biking routes, food and drink, accommodation options, local customs, and safety tips.

List of Places to Visit in Newport

Whether you’re visiting Newport for a week-long vacation or a quick weekend getaway, there’s plenty to see and do. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Newport.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oregon Coast Aquarium: A popular attraction showcasing the diverse marine life found in the Pacific Northwest. Visitors can witness sea otters, sharks, jellyfish, and other ocean creatures, as well as learn more about ocean conservation efforts.
  • Yaquina Head Lighthouse: Built in 1873, this lighthouse has been guiding ships into Newport’s Yaquina Bay for over a century. Visitors can tour the lighthouse and learn more about its history and significance.
  • Pacific Maritime & Heritage Center: This museum showcases the maritime history of the Pacific Northwest, including exhibits on shipwrecks, fishing, and boatbuilding. Visitors can also view old photos and artifacts from Newport’s past.

Natural Attractions of Newport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yaquina Bay State Park: This expansive park features a sandy beach, hiking trails, and picnic areas. Visitors can also explore the Yaquina Bay Bridge, a historic landmark that offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Devil’s Punchbowl State Natural Area: A scenic natural wonder featuring a large rock formation with a crater-like hole that fills with seawater during high tide. Visitors can watch the waves crash against the rocks and explore nearby tidepools.
  • Yaquina Head Outstanding Natural Area: This scenic area features a stunning rocky coastline and tidepools teeming with marine life. Visitors can hike along the trails and view the historic Yaquina Head Lighthouse.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newport

Exploring Newport’s natural beauty on foot or by bike is highly recommended. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • South Jetty Trail: A scenic 2.5-mile trail along the coast near the mouth of Yaquina Bay. Visitors can see a variety of wildlife and enjoy beautiful views of the ocean.
  • Mike Miller Educational Trail: A one-mile interpretive trail through a coastal forest that leads to a secluded beach.
  • Yaquina Bay Trail: A 4.4-mile trail that runs along the bay and offers stunning views of the bridge and nearby wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Newport: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Newport. Here are a few must-try options:

  • Charter Fishing: Newport is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, and visitors can take a charter boat out to try their hand at catching salmon, halibut, and other fish.
  • Keystone Thrust Boat Tours: This guided tour takes visitors on a thrilling ride through Yaquina Bay and into the Pacific Ocean. You’ll be able to spot whales, sea lions, and other marine life.
  • Glassblowing Demonstrations: Watch expert glassblowers create beautiful works of art at the Oregon Coast Glassworks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newport

To make the most of your visit to Newport, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Oregon Coast Aquarium and take a stroll through the waterfront district
2 Tour the Yaquina Bay Lighthouse and take a hike in Yaquina Bay State Park

5 Take a Glassblowing demonstration tour in the morning and go on a whale-watching tour in the afternoon
6 Explore the Newport Historic Bayfront and take a walk along the beach

Food and Drink Scene of Newport

Newport is home to a variety of delicious dining options, from seafood shacks to fine dining establishments. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oregon Coast Clam Chowder: A creamy and hearty chowder made with fresh clams and local vegetables.
  • Fish and Chips: Fresh fish, battered and fried to crispy perfection, served with french fries and tartar sauce.
  • Dungeness Crab: A local specialty, grilled or steamed to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Rogue Ales & Spirits: A local craft brewery that offers a wide variety of brews, from IPAs to stouts.
  • South Beach Distillery: A small-batch distillery that produces hand-crafted spirits using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Newport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newport offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are a few popular options:

  • The Sylvia Beach Hotel: A unique oceanfront hotel that features themed rooms inspired by famous authors.
  • Elizabeth Oceanfront Suites: A luxurious hotel that offers stunning ocean views and spacious suites.
  • Newport Belle Bed & Breakfast: A charming guesthouse located on a restored paddle-wheel boat.

What to Buy in Newport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift? Here are a few local products to consider:

  • Saltwater Taffy: A classic Oregon Coast treat, available in a variety of flavors.
  • Handcrafted Glassware: Beautiful glassware handcrafted by local artisans at Oregon Coast Glassworks.
  • Local Seafood: Freshly caught seafood, including Dungeness crab, salmon, and halibut, can be purchased at the Newport Bayfront.

Local Culture and Customs of Newport

Newport has a rich cultural heritage that’s still evident in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Visitors should respect the local way of life and embrace the laid-back, beachy vibe.

Best Time to Visit Newport

Newport can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months (June-September) when the weather is at its best and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newport

To ensure a safe and enjoyable visit to Newport, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Take the time to explore Newport’s natural beauty by foot or bike.
  • Respect the area’s marine life and wildlife, and avoid feeding or disturbing them.
  • Check the weather before heading out to the beach or on an outdoor adventure.

Don’t:

  • Pick up or collect shells or rocks at the beach (this is prohibited).
  • Ignore warnings or signs about dangerous surf or tide conditions.
  • Leave valuables in your car or unattended on the beach.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Newport should take measures to ensure their safety and health during their stay.

Safety in Newport

  • Be aware of your surroundings and lock car doors when parked.
  • Always follow water safety guidelines and avoid swimming alone or in rough surf.
  • Avoid hiking alone and always carry water and proper gear.

Health in Newport

  • Be aware of the risks associated with sun exposure and heatstroke, and take appropriate precautions (such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated).
  • Seek out medical attention if you notice any signs of illness or injury.

Getting to Newport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Newport is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Newport

  • Eugene Airport (EUG)
  • Portland International Airport (PDX)
  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA)

Bus Stations Near Newport

  • Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Newport.
  • Chinook Winds Casino Resort in Lincoln City offers a shuttle service to Portland.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also drive to Newport via Highway 101, which runs along the coast and offers stunning ocean views.
  • Car rentals are available at nearby airports and in Newport.

How to Get Around in Newport

To explore Newport and the surrounding area, visitors can use a variety of transportation options, including:

  • Biking: Newport offers several bike rental shops and bike trails throughout the city.
  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car or bring their own to explore the area.
  • Walking: Many of Newport’s attractions and points of interest are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Newport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to extend your coastal adventure? Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Lincoln City: Just 25 miles north of Newport, Lincoln City offers scenic views, outdoor activities, and a variety of dining and shopping options.
  • Depoe Bay: Located just 10 miles south of Newport, Depoe Bay is known for its whale-watching tours and charming atmosphere.
  • Cape Perpetua: A scenic coastal area located just 22 miles south of Newport, featuring hiking trails, tide pools, and stunning ocean views.

Conclusion

Newport is a must-visit destination on the Oregon Coast, offering something for everyone. With its natural beauty, cultural attractions, delicious food, and friendly locals, Newport is the perfect place to unwind and enjoy all that the Pacific Northwest has to offer. We hope this guide has helped you plan your next coastal adventure in Newport, and we look forward to welcoming you soon.
