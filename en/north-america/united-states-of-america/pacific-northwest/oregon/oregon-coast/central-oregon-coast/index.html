Introduction to Central Oregon Coast

The Central Oregon Coast is a picturesque stretch of coastline that spans over 60 miles and is part of the Oregon Coast in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. The area is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural history, as well as its diverse range of outdoor activities, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Central Oregon Coast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Central Oregon Coast is home to a variety of museums and historical sites that are worth visiting:

  • Yaquina Head Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse, constructed in 1873, is a popular landmark and provides breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding coastline.
  • Oregon Coast Aquarium: Home to over 15,000 animals, the Oregon Coast Aquarium offers an immersive experience into the diverse marine life of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Cape Perpetua Visitor Center: This visitor center offers stunning views of the coastline and is home to exhibits and educational programs related to the natural history of the area.
  • Lincoln City Cultural Center: The Lincoln City Cultural Center is a hub for arts and culture in the area, hosting a variety of performances, workshops, and events throughout the year.
  • Tillamook County Pioneer Museum: This museum offers insight into the history and heritage of the region, showcasing artifacts and exhibits related to the Native American, pioneer, and maritime cultures of the area.

Natural Attractions of Central Oregon Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to explore on the Central Oregon Coast:

  • Devil’s Punchbowl State Natural Area: This stunning natural wonder is a collapsed sea cave that has created a unique bowl-like formation along the coastline.
  • Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area: Spanning approximately 40 miles, the Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area is a must-visit for those interested in hiking, camping, fishing, and off-road vehicle activities.
  • Cape Kiwanda State Natural Area: Located in Pacific City, Cape Kiwanda offers incredible views of the coastline and is a popular spot for hiking, surfing, and sandboarding.
  • Siuslaw National Forest: This expansive national forest covers over 630,000 acres and offers ample opportunities for hiking, camping, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Heceta Head Lighthouse: Built in 1894, this picturesque lighthouse stands at over 200 feet tall and offers stunning views of the ocean and surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Oregon Coast

The Central Oregon Coast is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views and opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the area:

  • Drift Creek Falls Trail: This moderate trail is a 3-mile round trip hike that leads to a stunning waterfall.
  • Cape Falcon Trail: Located in Oswald West State Park, this moderate trail is a 4.6-mile round trip hike that offers stunning views of the coastline and mountains.
  • South Jetty Trail: This easy trail is a 2.5-mile round trip hike that leads to the South Jetty overlook, offering stunning views of the Siuslaw River and surrounding landscapes.
  • Old Growth Ridge Trail: Located in Cape Perpetua, this moderate trail is a 3.2-mile round trip hike that winds through old-growth forest and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Horse Creek Trail: This moderate trail is a 4.4-mile round trip hike that leads to a stunning waterfall and offers opportunities for fishing and wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Central Oregon Coast: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from on the Central Oregon Coast to make your trip a memorable one:

  • Whale Watching: The area is known for whale watching, with peak seasons being from December through January and from March through June.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the coastline and rivers of the area on a kayak or canoe tour.
  • Golfing: The Central Oregon Coast is home to several golf courses, including the Salishan Golf Links in Gleneden Beach and Agate Beach Golf Course in Newport.
  • Wine Tasting: The area is home to several wineries, including Depoe Bay Winery and Flying Dutchman Winery.
  • Shopping: Browse unique local shops and boutiques for handmade treasures and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Oregon Coast

If you have a week to explore the Central Oregon Coast, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 - Arrive in Newport, check into a hotel, and explore the Yaquina Head Lighthouse and Oregon Coast Aquarium.

Day 2 - Drive to Cape Perpetua, go on a hike, and visit the visitor center.

Day 3 - Explore the town of Florence, take a dune buggy tour, and visit the nearby Heceta Head Lighthouse.

Day 4 - Drive to Pacific City, go surfing or sandboarding at Cape Kiwanda, and enjoy some local seafood.

Day 5 - Head to Lincoln City, visit the local glass blowing studios, and check out the Lincoln City Cultural Center.

Day 6 - Drive to Depoe Bay, go whale watching, and visit the shops and galleries.

Day 7 - Return to Newport, explore the historic bayfront, and enjoy a sunset cruise.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Oregon Coast

From seafood and craft beers to wines and locally roasted coffee, the Central Oregon Coast has a lot to offer in terms of food and drink:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: A staple of the Pacific Northwest, clam chowder is a must-try when visiting the area.
  • Fish and Chips: With plenty of fresh seafood available, fish and chips is a popular local dish.
  • Dungeness Crab: This sweet and succulent crab is a local delicacy and can be found in many restaurants and markets.
  • Tillamook Cheese: Produced in the nearby town of Tillamook, this cheese is a must-try for cheese lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beers: The area is home to several microbreweries, including Pelican Brewing and Rogue Ales.
  • Wines: The Central Oregon Coast is home to several wineries, including Flying Dutchman Winery and Nehalem Bay Winery.
  • Coffee: There are plenty of local coffee roasters and cafes to choose from, including Pacific Grind Cafe and Dutch Bros Coffee.

Where to Stay in Central Oregon Coast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Central Oregon Coast offers a range of accommodations to suit different tastes and budgets:

  • Hotels: From luxurious resorts to budget-friendly options, there are plenty of hotels to choose from in the area, including Inn at Nye Beach and Elizabeth Oceanfront Suites.
  • Hostels: Budget-friendly options include the Seaside Hostel and the Northwest Portland Hostel.
  • Guesthouses: For a more personal touch, consider staying at a guesthouse such as the Ocean House Bed & Breakfast in Newport or the Riverhouse Inn in Florence.

What to Buy in Central Oregon Coast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, consider the following:

  • Local Artwork: The area is home to several galleries and studios featuring local artisans and their work.
  • Seafood: Take home some fresh seafood, including Dungeness crab and smoked salmon.
  • Tillamook Cheese: Bring home some of the famous Tillamook Cheese produced in nearby Tillamook.
  • Handmade Crafts and Jewelry: Browse local shops and boutiques for handmade crafts and jewelry, such as the Silver Heron Gallery in Depoe Bay.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Oregon Coast

The Central Oregon Coast is known for its laid-back and friendly culture, with a strong focus on the natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities of the area. Locals are proud of their heritage and culture, and often celebrate with festivals, concerts, and other events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Central Oregon Coast

The best time to visit the Central Oregon Coast is from late spring through fall, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor recreational activities to enjoy. However, visitors can also enjoy the area’s natural beauty and cultural attractions year-round, with winter storms providing a dramatic and awe-inspiring sight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Oregon Coast

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities of the area.
  • Do try the local seafood and other culinary delights.
  • Do pack for the weather, which can change quickly and often.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb natural habitats.
  • Don’t forget to take caution when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking and surfing.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and cultures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Oregon Coast

Overall, the Central Oregon Coast is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Be aware of changing weather conditions, particularly in the winter months when storms can cause hazardous conditions.
  • Respect the ocean and take caution when swimming or participating in water activities.
  • Respect natural habitats and wildlife.

Health in Central Oregon Coast

The area provides adequate healthcare services, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should take caution when participating in outdoor activities to prevent injuries.

Getting to Central Oregon Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Central Oregon Coast

The nearest major airports to the area are Portland International Airport and Eugene Airport, both of which offer regular flights to major domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Central Oregon Coast

Greyhound and other bus companies provide service to several towns in the area, including Newport, Lincoln City, and Florence.

Other Options

The area is also accessible by car, with several major highways passing through the region, including US-101 and OR-18.

How to Get Around in Central Oregon Coast

The easiest way to get around the Central Oregon Coast is by car or bicycle. Several rental car companies are available in the area, as well as local bike rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Central Oregon Coast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, consider visiting nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Columbia River Gorge: Located approximately 180 miles north of the Central Oregon Coast, the Columbia River Gorge offers stunning vistas and opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, and water activities.
  • Crater Lake National Park: Located approximately 175 miles east of the Central Oregon Coast, Crater Lake National Park is home to the deepest lake in the United States and offers stunning natural beauty and opportunities for hiking and camping.
  • Portland: Located approximately 120 miles northeast of the area, Portland is a vibrant city known for its food, art, and culture.

Conclusion

The Central Oregon Coast offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor recreational activities. From exploring historic lighthouses and museums to hiking and surfing along the rugged coastline, there’s something for everyone. With a friendly and laid-back culture, delicious local cuisine, and plenty of accommodations to choose from, a visit to the Central Oregon Coast is sure to be a memorable one.
