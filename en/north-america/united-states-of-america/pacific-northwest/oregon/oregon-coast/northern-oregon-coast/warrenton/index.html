Introduction to Warrenton

Nestled on the Northern Oregon Coast, Warrenton is a charming city with a rich history and beautiful natural wonders. Visitors can explore culturally important attractions, go on outdoor adventures, enjoy delicious local cuisine, and experience the unique culture and customs of the area.

List of Places to Visit in Warrenton

Here are some of the must-see places to visit in Warrenton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Stevens State Park: This park preserves the military fortifications and Battery Russell, a military installation from the 19th century. Visitors can explore the history of the fort and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and camping.
  • Columbia River Maritime Museum: The museum features exhibits on the maritime history of the Columbia River. Visitors can learn about the various shipwrecks, fishing, and recreational activities that took place in the area.
  • Warrenton Historical Museum: This museum displays artifacts and exhibits that highlight the local history of Warrenton.

Natural Attractions of Warrenton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sunset Beach State Recreation Site: Visitors can enjoy a scenic day at the beach while exploring the wildlife and natural dunes.
  • Hammond Marina: The marina offers a great spot for fishing and crabbing with its picturesque views of the Columbia River.
  • Lewis and Clark National Wildlife Refuge: The refuge offers hiking trails, bird watching, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Warrenton

Warrenton is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Fort to Sea Trail: This trail offers a beautiful view of Lewis and Clark National Historical Park and ends at Sunset Beach State Recreation Site.
  • Lewis and Clark Discovery Trail: This trail provides an opportunity to explore the area’s fascinating maritime history with stunning views of the coast.
  • Skipanon Trail: This trail is perfect for bird watchers and runs along Skipanon River and Youngs Bay.

Best Things to Do in Warrenton: Activities and Tours

From water sports to local breweries, Warrenton offers plenty of fun activities to explore. Here are some of the best things to do in Warrenton:

  • Go fishing or crabbing at Hammond Marina.
  • Take a kayaking tour on Youngs Bay.
  • Visit Buoy Beer Company and sample local brews.
  • Go on a horseback ride at C&M Stables.

Week-Long Itinerary for Warrenton

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Warrenton:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Fort Stevens State Park and visit the Columbia River Maritime Museum.
  • Day 3-4: Spend time at Sunset Beach State Recreation Site and Hammond Marina.
  • Day 5-6: Take a day trip to Astoria and visit the Astoria Column and the Flavel House Museum.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine at Buoy Beer Company and explore the shops and souvenirs before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Warrenton

Warrenton’s food scene reflects Pacific Northwest cuisine, with a variety of seafood and local brewery options. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish n’ chips from Bowpicker: This restaurant serves delicious seafood and is a local favorite.
  • Dungeness crab: This Northwest delicacy is a must-try while visiting Warrenton.
  • Clam chowder: This soup is a great way to warm up on a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Buoy Beer Company: This brewery is known for its Dockside Series, a collection of beers inspired by the Columbia River estuary.
  • Fort George Brewery + Public House: This brewery offers a variety of year-round and seasonal beers.

Where to Stay in Warrenton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a pet-friendly hotel, Warrenton offers a variety of accommodations options. Here are some of the best places to stay in Warrenton:

  • Astoria Crest Motel: This charming motel offers affordable rooms with great views of the coast.
  • Cannery Pier Hotel & Spa: This luxurious hotel is situated right on the Columbia River and offers beautiful views of the water.
  • Sea Sprite at Haystack Rock: This charming B&B is located near the beach and offers a cozy and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Warrenton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Warrenton offers plenty of local souvenirs and products to bring home. Here are some unique items to purchase on your visit:

  • Local artisanal cheese from Tillamook Cheese Factory.
  • Moth-eaten clothing from the Moe’s Vintage Store.
  • Souvenir T-shirts from the Warrenton Visitor Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Warrenton

Warrenton is a small town with a rich history and a unique culture. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions while exploring the city.

Best Time to Visit Warrenton

The best time to visit Warrenton is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is warm and sunny, making it a great time to enjoy outdoor activities and explore the coastline.

Do’s and Don’ts of Warrenton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while appreciating Warrenton’s culture and customs:

Do:

  • Be respectful of the environment and wildlife.
  • Tread lightly on the dunes and avoid damaging the vegetation.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beach or trails.
  • Disturb the wildlife by getting too close or feeding them.
  • Ignore the respectful local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Before traveling to Warrenton, visitors should review basic safety and health precautions.

Safety in Warrenton

  • Follow all posted signs and warnings.
  • Take precautions against sunburn and dehydration.
  • Carry bear spray when hiking in the backcountry.

Health in Warrenton

  • Use insect repellent to prevent tick and mosquito bites.
  • Take precautions against food and water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Warrenton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Warrenton is located near several transportation hubs and offers multiple options for travelers.

Airports Near Warrenton

  • Astoria Regional Airport is the closest airport, located 10 miles northeast of Warrenton.
  • Portland International Airport is located 95 miles southeast of Warrenton.

Bus Stations Near Warrenton

  • NW Point Bus Station offers bus services to several destinations throughout the Pacific Northwest.
  • Greyhound Bus Station in nearby Astoria offers nationwide services.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available at the airports and in nearby towns.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Warrenton

While Warrenton is a small town, having a vehicle is necessary to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Warrenton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Warrenton is located near several other destinations worth exploring. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Visit nearby Astoria and explore its historic downtown.
  • Go on a day trip to Cannon Beach and see its famous Haystack Rock.
  • Explore the hiking trails at Ecola State Park.

Conclusion

Warrenton is a charming city with a rich history and beautiful natural wonders. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, Warrenton offers something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the unique culture and beauty of the Pacific Northwest.
