St. Helens is a charming small city located in Northern Oregon Coast, a region known for its incredible natural beauty and numerous outdoor activities. Visitors to St. Helens can enjoy a variety of attractions, including museums, historical sites, hiking and biking trails, and scenic views. Plus, the city’s food and drink scene is not to be missed, with a wide range of local dishes and beverages to try. In this comprehensive guide to St. Helens, we’ll explore all that the city has to offer and help you plan the perfect trip.

Introduction to St. Helens

St. Helens is a small city with a population of just over 13,000 people, located in Columbia County, Oregon. The city was named after the famous volcano, Mount St. Helens, which is visible from the city on a clear day. St. Helens is known for its charming downtown area, which features numerous historic buildings and lively local businesses.

List of Places to Visit in St. Helens

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Helens has a rich history, and visitors to the city can learn about its past at several museums and historical sites. Some of the top attractions include:

  1. Columbia County Museum: This museum has a variety of exhibits detailing the history of Columbia County, including items related to the timber, fishing, and shipping industries that fueled the city’s growth.

  2. McCormick House: This historic home was built in 1884 and has been beautifully preserved, offering visitors a glimpse into life in St. Helens in the late 19th century.

  3. South County Historical Society Museum: This museum has a collection of artifacts related to the history of Columbia County, including Native American artifacts and items related to the Oregon Trail.

Natural Attractions of St. Helens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St. Helens is situated in a beautiful natural setting, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation. Here are some of the top things to do in the area:

  1. St. Helens Marina: This marina offers boat rentals, fishing charters, and scenic tours of the Columbia River.

  2. Sauvie Island Wildlife Area: Just a short drive from St. Helens, this wildlife area offers over 11,000 acres of beautiful wetlands and forests, as well as plenty of opportunities for birdwatching and hiking.

  3. Scappoose Bay Paddling Center: This center offers kayak and paddleboard rentals, as well as guided tours of the picturesque waterways near St. Helens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Helens

For visitors who enjoy hiking and biking, St. Helens has several excellent trails to explore. Some of the best options include:

  1. Columbia View Park: This park has several miles of hiking trails that offer stunning views of the Columbia River and the surrounding mountains.

  2. Crown Zellerbach Trail: This former logging road has been converted into a multi-use trail that is perfect for hiking and biking.

  3. Trojan Park Loop: This scenic trail offers a moderate hike through beautiful forests and fields, with plenty of opportunities to spot local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in St. Helens: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions in the area, St. Helens also has a variety of other activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are a few top choices:

  1. St. Helens Ghost Walks: Take a spooky tour of the city and learn about its haunted history.

  2. St. Helens Farmers Market: This weekly market offers fresh produce, local crafts, and other artisan goods from the St. Helens area.

  3. Portland Spirit River Cruises: Take a scenic tour of the Columbia River on a luxurious riverboat.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Helens

If you’re planning to spend a week in St. Helens, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Explore the downtown area and check out the local shops and restaurants.

Day 2: Visit the Columbia County Museum and McCormick House to learn about the city’s history.

Day 3: Spend the day on the water, with a boat rental or tour with St. Helens Marina.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Sauvie Island Wildlife Area for hiking, birdwatching, and more outdoor adventures.

Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the Crown Zellerbach Trail, or do some kayaking at Scappoose Bay Paddling Center.

Day 6: Visit the South County Historical Society Museum and take a ghost tour of the city at night.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at the Portland Spirit River Cruises before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Helens

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, St. Helens has a variety of dishes that are unique to the area. Here are a few must-try options:

  1. Smoked Salmon: The Columbia River is known for its incredible salmon, and you can find smoked salmon at many local restaurants.

  2. Huckleberry Jam: Huckleberries are a local plant that grows in the region, and huckleberry jam is a delicious treat that you won’t want to miss.

  3. Dungeness Crab: Another local seafood favorite, Dungeness crab is fresh and delicious in St. Helens.

Local Beverages

St. Helens is home to several local breweries and wineries, offering plenty of choices for beer and wine enthusiasts. Here are a few that you might want to try:

  1. Thunder Island Brewing Company: This brewery has a great selection of craft beers, as well as delicious pub food.

  2. St. Helens Cider Company: If you prefer cider, this local cidery has some of the best options around.

  3. Bethany Vineyards: This local winery offers a variety of wines, as well as stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Where to Stay in St. Helens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Helens has a variety of accommodation options to fit every budget. Here are a few top choices:

  1. Best Western Oak Meadows Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location near downtown St. Helens.

  2. Deer Island Manor: This charming bed and breakfast is a great choice for visitors who want a more intimate, personalized experience.

  3. Scappoose Creek Inn: This historic inn offers luxury accommodations and a peaceful setting in the countryside near St. Helens.

What to Buy in St. Helens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or local products to bring home from St. Helens, here are a few options:

  1. Huckleberry Jam and Other Local Food Products: Several local stores offer huckleberry jam and other locally made food products, perfect for gifts or as a treat for yourself.

  2. Crafts and Artisan Goods: Check out the St. Helens Farmers Market or local shops for handmade crafts and artisan goods.

  3. Timber Products: St. Helens was once a major timber industry town, and you can find products made from local wood, such as cutting boards and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Helens

St. Helens is known for its friendly small-town atmosphere and welcoming community. Visitors to the area should respect local customs, such as being polite and courteous to others and following local regulations and laws.

Best Time to Visit St. Helens

The best time to visit St. Helens is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, if you prefer to avoid crowds, you might consider visiting in the shoulder season, in April, May, or October.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Helens

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, such as smoked salmon, huckleberry jam, and Dungeness crab.

  • Respect local customs and laws.

  • Be prepared for outdoor recreation, such as hiking or kayaking, by bringing appropriate gear and clothing.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.

  • Enter private property without permission.

  • Ignore local safety regulations, such as wearing a life jacket when boating or swimming.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Helens

St. Helens is generally a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Some tips include:

  • Always lock your car and hotel room and keep valuables out of sight.

  • Use caution when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities, as terrain can be uneven or hazardous.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas after dark.

Health in St. Helens

St. Helens has a few medical facilities in the area, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should take steps to protect their health by practicing good hygiene habits, such as washing hands frequently and covering mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.

Getting to St. Helens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Helens

The closest major airport to St. Helens is Portland International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near St. Helens

St. Helens does not have a bus station, but visitors can take a bus to nearby Rainier or Scappoose and then take a taxi to St. Helens.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to St. Helens from Portland.

How to Get Around in St. Helens

Many of St. Helens’ attractions can be reached on foot, but visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car or take a taxi. Biking is also a popular option, with several trails in the area.

Where to Go Next from St. Helens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Oregon adventure after visiting St. Helens, there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Here are a few ideas:

  • Portland: Just an hour’s drive from St. Helens, Portland is a vibrant city known for its food and drink scene, cultural attractions, and beautiful parks.

  • Mount St. Helens: This famous volcano is a bit farther from St. Helens, but is definitely worth a visit for its stunning views and unique geological features.

  • The Oregon Coast: Head west to explore the stunning Oregon Coast, with its charming small towns, beautiful beaches, and incredible natural landscapes.

Conclusion

St. Helens is a hidden gem in the beautiful Northern Oregon Coast, offering visitors a variety of attractions and activities that are perfect for a weekend trip or longer vacation. With its friendly small-town atmosphere, delicious food and drink scene, and stunning natural setting, St. Helens is a must-visit destination for any Oregon explorer.
