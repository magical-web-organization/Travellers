Introduction to Pacific City

Pacific City is a gem on the Northern Oregon Coast. Its wilds, natural beauty, and charming small-town vibe make it a favorite among tourists looking for an authentic coastal experience.

This guide will help you explore the many attractions, activities, and local culture that Pacific City has to offer. Whether you’re interested in hiking, cultural attractions, or enjoying the local cuisine, our guide will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Pacific City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nestucca Bay National Wildlife Refuge - A protected bay area offering scenic views and a chance to see various bird species. There are several hiking trails along the marshes and forest, making it a perfect destination for a relaxed hike with family and friends.
  • Kiawanda Beach - A picturesque beach with stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. Visitors can go for a swim, do some beachcombing, or enjoy a picnic. The beach is also known for great photo opportunities and provides a good backdrop for family portraits or engagement shoots.
  • Cape Lookout Lighthouse - A lighthouse built in the early 1900s, providing breathtaking views of the coastline.

Natural Attractions of Pacific City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Kiwanda State Natural Area - A beach formed by sandstone cliffs that offer amazing views of the ocean and the coastline. It’s a popular spot for surfing, hang gliding, and fishing.
  • Bob Straub State Park - A state park named after Oregon’s former governor, providing over 2 miles of pristine coastline along the Nestucca River. Home to diverse flora and fauna and offers several hiking trails leading to scenic viewpoints.
  • Pacific City Beach - A long, sandy beach with picturesque views of Haystack Rock. Visitors can surf, paddleboard, or hike up the nearby sand dune to take in the view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pacific City

  • Cape Lookout Trail - A scenic 2.5-mile hike that takes you through a beautiful old-growth forest and offers stunning views of the ocean, beach, and the nearby lighthouse.
  • The Three Capes Scenic Loop - A 35-mile driving or biking loop that takes you through Cape Meares, Cape Lookout, and Cape Kiwanda.
  • Stimulus Loop Trail - A 6.6-mile trail that takes you through green forests and provides stunning views of rocky outcroppings, the ocean, and the high cliffs of Cape Lookout.

Best Things to Do in Pacific City: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing - Pacific City is one of the top spots for surfing on the Oregon Coast. The beach breaks are perfect for both beginners and experienced surfers.
  • Fishing - The Nestucca River is a popular spot for fishing enthusiasts. Rent a boat or kayak and try your luck at catching salmon and steelhead.
  • Haystack Rock Tide Pooling - Explore the fascinating tide pools around Haystack Rock, home to diverse marine life, including sea stars, anemones, and crabs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pacific City

Day Activity
1 Arrive and settle in. Take a stroll along Kiawanda Beach and dine at Pelican Brewing.
2 Hike the Cape Lookout Trail or Stimulus Loop Trail. Visit Nestucca Bay National Wildlife Refuge.
3 Take a surfing lesson or rent a paddleboard at Pacific City Beach.
4 Explore the tide pools at Haystack Rock.
5 Visit one of the local museums or historical sites.
6 Take a scenic drive or bike along the Three Capes Scenic Loop.
7 Fishing trip on the Nestucca River or head out for a day trip to other nearby destinations.

Food and Drink Scene of Pacific City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dungeness Crab - A Pacific Northwest staple, which can be enjoyed in various dishes at several local restaurants.
  • Fish and Chips - Fresh, locally caught fish served with fries and tartar sauce.
  • Pelican Brews and Pizza - Pelican Brewery is a Pacific City favorite, serving delicious pizza, salads, and their famous Thirsty Beaver Amber Ale.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Pacific City is home to several breweries, including Pelican Brewing, Twist Wine Company, and Nestucca Bay Brewery.
  • Wine - Pacific City and the surrounding areas have several excellent wineries, such as Nehalem Bay Winery, Clay Pigeon Winery, and Elk Cove Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Pacific City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Headlands Coastal Lodge & Spa - A luxurious lodge with ocean views, fireplaces, and fine dining options.
  • Sheltered Nook on Tillamook Bay - Quaint, tiny houses with modern amenities such as wifi, a hot tub, and a fire pit.
  • Sea Breeze Court - A budget-friendly option with access to a private beach and a fire pit.

What to Buy in Pacific City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oregon Wine - Pacific City and the surrounding areas have several excellent wineries worth visiting.
  • Hand-Crafted Soaps and Candles - Pacific City has several shops selling locally made soaps, candles, and other crafts.
  • Nestucca Bay National Wildlife Refuge Merchandise - Support the National Wildlife Refuge by purchasing merchandise, such as t-shirts, hats, and bags, sold at the visitor center.

Local Culture and Customs of Pacific City

  • Respect for Nature - Pacific City is a place where nature is cherished, and visitors are encouraged to preserve its beauty and natural habitat.
  • Small-Town Vibe - The town prides itself on its close-knit community and its friendly locals.
  • Sustainability - Pacific City is a town that places importance on reducing waste and being conscious of the environment. Keep this in mind when visiting and try to reduce your waste wherever possible.

Best Time to Visit Pacific City

The best time to visit Pacific City is from May through October when the weather is warm and pleasant and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the offseason (November through April) provides visitors with fewer crowds and more affordable prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pacific City

Do:

  • Respect the Environment - Keep the beaches clean and minimize your waste.
  • Explore the Outdoors - Take advantage of the many hiking trails and outdoor activities that Pacific City has to offer.
  • Try the Local Cuisine - Pacific City has an excellent food scene, from fresh seafood to craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave Your Trash Behind - Littering is not acceptable and could harm the local wildlife and their habitat.
  • Disturb the Marine Life - Respect the creatures living in the tide pools around Haystack Rock and do not take them out of their natural habitat.
  • Ignore the Weather - Be prepared for changing weather conditions and dress accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pacific City

Overall, Pacific City is a safe and friendly place to visit. Nonetheless, be mindful of the following:

  • Hiking & Surfing Safety - Be mindful of the weather and tides when venturing out on hikes or surfing.
  • Driving Safety - The Three Capes Scenic Loop can become challenging to navigate during the offseason due to severe weather conditions.
  • Sunburn Protection - The Oregon Coast can have a high UV index, even on overcast days, so protect your skin with sunscreen and clothing.

Health in Pacific City

The nearest hospital to Pacific City is Tillamook Regional Medical Center. It’s essential to pack any necessary medications and consult your doctor if you have any pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Pacific City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pacific City

  • Portland International Airport - 1 hour and 45 minutes drive.
  • Eugene Airport - 2 hours and 30 minutes drive.

Bus Stations Near Pacific City

  • Greyhound Station in Tillamook - 30 minutes away by car.

Other Options

  • Rent a Car - Renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore the area independently.
  • Private and Shared Shuttles - There are several private and shared shuttle options that could take you to and from Pacific City.

How to Get Around in Pacific City

Pacific City is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance if you’re staying in town. Alternatively, you can rent a bike or take a taxi to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Pacific City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tillamook - A town known for its cheese factory, air museum, and scenic railway.
  • Cannon Beach - A nearby coastal town known for its state park beaches, restaurants and shops, and beachfront hotels.
  • Oregon Coast Aquarium - An award-winning aquarium located in Newport, just a two-hour drive from Pacific City.

Conclusion

Pacific City has it all - breathtaking views, friendly locals, natural details to explore, and a growing food and drink scene. Our guide will help you explore everything this charming coastal town has to offer and create unforgettable memories.
