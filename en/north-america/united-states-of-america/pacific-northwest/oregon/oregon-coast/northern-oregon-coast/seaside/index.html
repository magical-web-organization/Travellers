Introduction to Seaside

Seaside is a coastal city located in Clatsop County, Oregon. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations on the Oregon Coast, drawing visitors with its stunning beaches and rich history. Seaside is a vibrant and bustling city with plenty to see and do for visitors of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Seaside

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seaside Museum & Historical Society: This museum explores the history of Seaside, including its Native American roots and the town’s evolution as a popular destination for tourists.
  • Lewis and Clark Salt Works: This historical site is the location of a salt works established by Lewis and Clark during their exploration of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Tillamook Rock Lighthouse: A historic landmark and beacon located on a rocky island just off the coast of Seaside that can be seen from several viewpoints in the town.

Natural Attractions of Seaside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seaside Beach: A 1.5-mile stretch of beach that offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and a range of water activities, including surfing, kayaking, and swimming.
  • Ecola State Park: A stunning coastal area featuring cliffs, hiking trails, and stunning views of the Tillamook Rock Lighthouse and the Pacific Ocean.
  • Haystack Rock: An iconic rock formation that is located just off the coast of Seaside, and can be reached via a short walk on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seaside

  • Tillamook Head Trail: This 6.8-mile trail is a popular destination for hikers, offering stunning coastal views and access to secluded beaches.
  • The Promenade: A paved walkway that runs parallel to the Seaside Beach boardwalk, offering picturesque views of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Neahkahnie Mountain: A 3.8-mile hike that offers stunning views of the coastline and is accessible from Seaside via a short drive to Nehalem Bay State Park.

Best Things to Do in Seaside: Activities and Tours

  • Seaside Aquarium: A must-visit spot for families with children, this aquarium features a variety of marine life, including local species and exotic creatures.
  • Funland Arcade: A classic beachside arcade with vintage games and attractions, perfect for a nostalgic day out.
  • Seaside Carousel Mall: A unique shopping experience with a historic carousel and a range of shops featuring local and handmade goods.

Food and Drink Scene of Seaside

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: A Pacific Northwest specialty, Seaside offers local variations of this classic soup that features fresh clams and hearty potatoes.
  • Dungeness Crab: A local seafood favorite, this buttery and rich crustacean can be found in a variety of dishes, from crab cakes to pasta dishes.
  • Salt Water Taffy: A classic sweet treat, Seaside is famous for its local saltwater taffy, available in a rainbow of flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Oregon Wine: Known for its excellent Pinot Noir, Willamette Valley wineries are a must-visit for any wine-lover visiting Seaside.
  • Microbrews: The Pacific Northwest is a hub for craft beer, and Seaside offers some delicious local brews, including those from Seaside Brewing Company and Pelican Brewing Company.
  • Coffee: Seaside has a thriving coffee culture, featuring local coffee shops that serve up delicious coffee from a range of roasters from around the Pacific Northwest.

Where to Stay in Seaside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rivertide Suites Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Seaside, offering amenities such as a pool and spa, free continental breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Seaside Oceanfront Inn: A classic beachfront hotel with stunning views and direct access to the Seaside Beach boardwalk.
  • Hostelling International Seaside: A budget-friendly hostel that offers private and dorm-style rooms, as well as access to a communal kitchen and lounge.

What to Buy in Seaside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Salt Water Taffy: The quintessential Seaside souvenir, available in a variety of flavors and packaging options.
  • Handmade Crafts: Seaside is known for its vibrant arts scene, and visitors can find a range of handmade crafts and artworks at local shops and galleries.
  • Local Wine and Beer: Take a piece of the Pacific Northwest’s delicious wine and beer scene home with you by purchasing a bottle or two from a local winery or brewery.

Local Culture and Customs of Seaside

Seaside is a welcoming and friendly community, known for its laid-back, beachy vibe. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and the natural environment, as well as follow general beach safety guidelines.

Best Time to Visit Seaside

The best time to visit Seaside is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and the beach is at its most inviting. However, visitors can also enjoy Seaside’s beautiful scenery and outdoor activities during the shoulder season of spring and fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seaside

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Follow beach safety guidelines.
  • Support local businesses and artists.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beach or in public spaces.
  • Disrupt local wildlife and habitats.
  • Be disrespectful to other visitors or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Seaside

Seaside is generally a safe destination for visitors, but as with any popular tourist spot, visitors should be aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also be aware of the dangers of the ocean, including rip currents and changing tides.

Health in Seaside

Seaside has several clinics and medical facilities, but visitors should also be prepared with any necessary medications or treatments before their trip.

Getting to Seaside: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Seaside

  • Portland International Airport: Located about 100 miles southeast of Seaside, this is the closest major airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Seaside

  • Greyhound: The closest Greyhound bus station to Seaside is located in Astoria, approximately 15 miles north of the city.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Visitors to Seaside can rent a car from several major car rental companies located at the Portland International Airport.

How to Get Around in Seaside

Visitors can explore Seaside on foot, bike, or car. There is also a local bus system, the Sunset Empire Transportation District, which offers limited service throughout the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Seaside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Astoria: Located just 15 miles north of Seaside, Astoria is home to a historic downtown area, several museums, and a range of outdoor activities.
  • Cannon Beach: A scenic and picturesque beach town located just south of Seaside, Cannon Beach features stunning views of Haystack Rock and a thriving arts scene.
  • Tillamook: Known for its dairy farms and cheese factory, Tillamook is located approximately 50 miles south of Seaside and offers a range of outdoor activities and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Seaside offers a unique mix of natural beauty, cultural interest, and modern amenities, making it an ideal destination for a wide variety of visitors. From stunning beaches and hiking trails to iconic landmarks and delicious local cuisine, Seaside has something for everyone. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and culture, enjoy outdoor activities and adventures, and relax in top-notch accommodations, ensuring that their trip will be unforgettable.
