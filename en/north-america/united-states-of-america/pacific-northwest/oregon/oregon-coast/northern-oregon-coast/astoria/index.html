Introduction to Astoria

Astoria is a charming city located in the northern part of the Oregon Coast, in Oregon, United States. The city has a rich history and culture that attracts visitors from all over the world. Astoria is known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and diverse food scene. This guide will help you plan your trip to Astoria and discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Astoria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Columbia River Maritime Museum

The Columbia River Maritime Museum is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in maritime history. The museum features exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays that showcase the rich marine history of the region. Visitors can also learn about the Columbia River Bar, one of the most dangerous waterways in the world.

Fort Clatsop National Historical Park

Fort Clatsop is a beautiful national park located near Astoria. The park is home to a re-creation of the fort used by Lewis and Clark during their expedition in the early 19th century. Visitors can explore the fort, learn about the history of the expedition, and take a hike through the beautiful forested trails in the park.

Natural Attractions of Astoria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Astoria Column

The Astoria Column is a popular attraction that offers stunning panoramic views of Astoria, the Columbia River, and the Pacific Ocean. Visitors can climb the 164 stairs to the top of the column, which stands at 125 feet high, and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

The Lewis and Clark National Historical Park is a great place to hike, camp, and learn about the history of the Lewis and Clark expedition. Visitors can explore the trails, visit the Fort Clatsop replica, and learn about the cultural and natural resources of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Astoria

Astoria Riverwalk

The Astoria Riverwalk is a picturesque 6.4-mile trail that runs along the Columbia River. The trail is perfect for walking, running, and biking, with stunning views of the river and the surrounding area.

Fort to Sea Trail

The Fort to Sea Trail is a 6.9-mile trail that starts at Fort Clatsop National Historical Park and ends at Sunset Beach. The trail passes through the forest, across the dunes, and reaches the ocean. Hikers can enjoy the scenic views and wildlife along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Astoria: Activities and Tours

Go on a River Cruise

Astoria is located on the Columbia River and visitors can take a cruise to see the river up close. There are several tours available, including sunset cruises and wildlife cruises.

Visit the Farmer’s Market

The Astoria Farmer’s Market is a great place to explore local produce, crafts, and food. The market is open on Sundays and offers a unique shopping experience.

Food and Drink Scene in Astoria

Must-Try Dishes

Clam Chowder

Clam chowder is a popular dish in Astoria made with fresh clams, potatoes, onions, and bacon. The soup is creamy and filling, perfect for a cold day.

Fish and Chips

Astoria is famous for its fish and chips, made with fresh, locally caught fish. The fish is battered and fried to perfection, served with crispy fries and tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

Astoria Brewing Company

The Astoria Brewing Company is a local brewery that offers a wide range of craft beers. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery, enjoy a pint, and buy bottles to take home.

Where to Stay in Astoria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Cannery Pier Hotel

The Cannery Pier Hotel is a luxurious accommodation located on the Astoria waterfront. The hotel offers stunning views of the river, a spa, and complimentary breakfast for guests.

Hostelling International Astoria

Hostelling International Astoria is a cozy hostel located in downtown Astoria. The hostel offers shared and private rooms, a common area, and a kitchen for guests to use.

What to Buy in Astoria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salt Water Taffy

Saltwater taffy is a classic candy that originated in Atlantic City, New Jersey, but is also popular in Astoria. Visitors can buy saltwater taffy in various flavors and colors as a sweet souvenir.

Local Wine

Astoria is located in a region known for its delicious wine. Visitors can buy local wines, such as Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Riesling, to take home as a tasty souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Astoria

Astoria is a town with a rich history and culture. The locals are proud of their heritage and appreciate visitors who are respectful and knowledgeable about the community. Visitors should take time to learn about the history and culture of Astoria and participate in local events and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Astoria

The best time to visit Astoria is during the summer months, from June to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and there are many outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy. However, visitors who prefer fewer crowds and lower prices may want to visit during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Astoria

Do:

  • Respect the local community and learn about the history and culture of Astoria.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty and outdoor activities in the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind or damage natural resources.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Astoria

Astoria is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Astoria

Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications with them to Astoria, as there may not be many pharmacies or medical facilities available.

Getting to Astoria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Astoria

The closest airports to Astoria are Portland International Airport and Seattle-Tacoma International Airport. Both airports offer rental car services and public transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Astoria

Greyhound Bus Lines offer service to Astoria from Portland and Seattle.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Astoria or take a shuttle service from Portland.

How to Get Around in Astoria

Visitors can get around Astoria by car, bike, or walking. Bike rentals and local taxi services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Astoria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is a beautiful seaside town located about 30 miles south of Astoria. Visitors can enjoy the stunning beaches, hike the local trails, and explore the charming downtown area.

Tillamook

Tillamook is a town located about 65 miles south of Astoria. Visitors can visit the Tillamook Cheese Factory, explore the local wineries, and hike in the nearby mountains.

Conclusion

Astoria is a beautiful city with plenty to explore, from the stunning natural beauty to the rich history and culture. With this guide, visitors can plan their trip and discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Astoria.
