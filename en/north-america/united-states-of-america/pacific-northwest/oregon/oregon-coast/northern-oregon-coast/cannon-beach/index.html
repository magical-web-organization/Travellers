Introduction to Cannon Beach

Located on the beautiful Northern Oregon Coast, Cannon Beach is a charming coastal town that attracts visitors from all over the world. With its miles of pristine beaches, stunning natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage, Cannon Beach is the ultimate vacation destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies alike. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, learn about local history and culture, or indulge in delicious cuisine, Cannon Beach has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cannon Beach

For those looking to experience all that Cannon Beach has to offer, here’s a list of the town’s must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cannon Beach History Center and Museum: This small museum showcases the town’s rich history through exhibits, artifacts, and photos.
  • Tillamook Rock Lighthouse: You can view this iconic lighthouse from Ecola State Park or take a tour to learn about its fascinating history.
  • The Coaster Theatre Playhouse: This historic theater showcases a range of plays and musicals throughout the year.
  • Haystack Rock: This 235-foot sea stack is a popular landmark and home to a diverse range of marine life.

Natural Attractions of Cannon Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ecola State Park: This park offers sweeping views of the coast, as well as hiking and biking trails.
  • Neahkahnie Mountain: This scenic mountain offers panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding landscape.
  • Oswald West State Park: This park is known for its beautiful beaches and hiking trails.
  • Indian Beach: This secluded beach is a popular spot for surfing, fishing, and beachcombing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cannon Beach

For those who want to experience the natural beauty of Cannon Beach up close, here are some of the town’s best hiking and biking routes:

  • Tillamook Head Trail: This challenging trail offers stunning ocean views and lush forest scenery.
  • Ecola State Park Trail: This easy hike offers stunning views of the coast and ends at Indian Beach.
  • Neahkahnie Mountain Trail: This moderate hike offers panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding landscape.
  • Cape Falcon Trail: This moderate hike takes you through lush forest and offers stunning views of the coast.

Best Things to Do in Cannon Beach: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Cannon Beach! Here are some of the town’s must-try activities and tours:

  • Surfing: Cannon Beach is a popular surfing destination with world-class waves.
  • Tide Pooling: At low tide, you can explore the tide pools at Haystack Rock and see a variety of marine life.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride on the beach with Sea Ranch Stables.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit the nearby Willamette Valley for a day of wine tasting and vineyard tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cannon Beach

To make the most of your trip to Cannon Beach, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore downtown Cannon Beach and visit the Cannon Beach History Center and Museum
2 Hike the Tillamook Head Trail and visit Indian Beach
3 Take a surfing lesson or go tide-pooling at Haystack Rock
4 Visit Ecola State Park and Neahkahnie Mountain
5 Take a horseback ride on the beach with Sea Ranch Stables
6 Visit the Willamette Valley for a day of wine tasting
7 Relax on the beach and shop for souvenirs in town

Food and Drink Scene of Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is known for its fresh seafood, local wines, and craft beers. Here are some of the town’s must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clam Chowder: This creamy soup is a staple of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Fish and Chips: Enjoy fresh local fish fried to perfection and served with crispy fries.
  • Dungeness Crab: Sample this sweet, flavorful crab in a variety of dishes.
  • Marionberry Pie: This local berry pie is a must-try dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Oregon Pinot Noir: This versatile wine pairs well with seafood and is produced in the nearby Willamette Valley.
  • Craft Beer: Try local brews from Pelican Brewing or Public Coast Brewing.
  • Coffee: Sample some of the region’s best coffee at Sleepy Monk Coffee Roasters.

Where to Stay in Cannon Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cannon Beach offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the town’s top options:

  • Stephanie Inn: This luxurious oceanfront inn offers stunning views and high-end amenities.
  • Hallmark Resort: This modern hotel features oceanfront rooms, a spa, and an indoor pool.
  • Surfsand Resort: This family-friendly resort offers beachfront rooms and suites with ocean views.
  • Hostelling International: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Cannon Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Cannon Beach, here are some of the town’s best souvenirs and local products:

  • Saltwater Taffy: This classic candy is a staple of Cannon Beach.
  • Local Art: Visit the town’s many galleries and shops to find unique artwork.
  • Beach Gear: Stock up on sunscreen, flip flops, and other beach essentials.
  • Seafood: Bring home fresh seafood to cook at home from the nearby Cannon Beach Smokehouse.

Local Culture and Customs of Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is home to a vibrant arts community and long-standing traditions. The town is known for its annual Sandcastle Contest, which attracts artists from around the world. Local residents also celebrate the Winter Holidays with a fun-filled celebration known as “The Stormy Weather Festival.” Visitors should be aware of the town’s strict beach rules, which include no smoking, fires, or dogs on the beach.

Best Time to Visit Cannon Beach

The best time to visit Cannon Beach is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s natural beauty and activities during the other seasons. Spring and fall offer mild temperatures and fewer crowds, while winter is a great time for storm-watching and cozying up by the fireplace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cannon Beach

Here are some helpful tips for visitors to Cannon Beach:

Do:

  • Respect the beach rules and keep the beach clean.
  • Try fresh local seafood and wine.
  • Visit the town’s many art galleries and shops.
  • Take a scenic hike or bike ride in the nearby parks.

Don’t:

  • Smoke or have fires on the beach.
  • Bring dogs on the beach.
  • Ignore the tide warnings when tide pooling.
  • Park in restricted areas or block driveways.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should be aware of the potential hazards of the beach. Follow these safety tips to stay safe on the beach:

  • Be aware of the tide schedule and potential rip currents.
  • Stay off the rocks and cliffs.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and don’t leave valuables unattended.

Health in Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach has a small medical clinic, but visitors with specific health concerns should bring any necessary medications and consult with their doctor before traveling. Visitors should also be aware of the potential health hazards of the ocean, including hypothermia and stingrays.

Getting to Cannon Beach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cannon Beach is located on the Northern Oregon Coast, about 80 miles west of Portland. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Cannon Beach

  • Portland International Airport: This is the closest major airport, located about 90 minutes from Cannon Beach.

Bus Stations Near Cannon Beach

  • Greyhound: This bus station is located in nearby Astoria.
  • Oregon Point: This shuttle service operates between Cannon Beach and Portland.

Other Options

  • Car: Renting a car is a popular option for exploring the Oregon Coast.
  • Train: Amtrak provides service to Portland from major cities across the US.

How to Get Around in Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is a small, walkable town with plenty of bike trails and sidewalks. Visitors can also take advantage of the local shuttle service. Driving is also an option, but parking can be limited during peak season.

Where to Go Next from Cannon Beach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore more of the Pacific Northwest, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Astoria: This nearby town is known for its rich history and scenic views.
  • Portland: Visit the “City of Roses” for a day of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.
  • Columbia River Gorge: This scenic area offers hiking, waterfalls, and stunning views of the Columbia River.

Conclusion

Cannon Beach is a hidden gem on the Northern Oregon Coast, offering visitors stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing beach vacation, Cannon Beach has something for everyone.
