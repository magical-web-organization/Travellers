Introduction to Tigard

Located in the Portland Metropolitan Area in Oregon, Tigard is a vibrant community that offers a blend of urban and natural attractions. Home to scenic parks, museums, hiking and biking trails, and a thriving food and drink scene, Tigard is a destination worth exploring in the Pacific Northwest. This comprehensive tour guide for Tigard will introduce you to the best places to visit, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, and transportation options to make your trip to Tigard a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Tigard

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, Tigard has several museums and historical sites worth visiting. The John Tigard House Museum is a restored pioneer-era house and one of the oldest buildings in Washington County. The museum offers guided tours that showcase the life and times of the Tigard family. Another historical site worth visiting is the Old Tigard Church, which has been a landmark in the community since 1880. The church has been restored and is open to the public for tours and events.

Natural Attractions of Tigard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tigard is home to several parks and natural areas that offer a variety of outdoor activities. The Tualatin River National Wildlife Refuge is a 1,200-acre sanctuary that provides habitat for a wide range of wildlife, including over 200 species of birds. The refuge offers hiking trails, wildlife observation platforms, and guided tours. Another natural area worth exploring is the Cook Park, which features picnic areas, playgrounds, and a boat ramp that provides access to the Tualatin River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tigard

If you enjoy hiking and biking, Tigard has several trails that offer scenic views and a chance to explore the surrounding area. The Fanno Creek Trail is a 4.5 mile paved trail that follows the path of the creek through parks and natural areas. The trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride. The Tualatin River Greenway Trail is another popular trail that runs along the Tualatin River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Tigard: Activities and Tours

Tigard offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. If you’re a wine lover, you can take a tour of the local wineries and vineyards that produce some of the finest wines in Oregon. Tigard also has several breweries that offer tours and tastings of their locally brewed beers. For a unique experience, you can take a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful Willamette Valley and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Tigard

Must-Try Dishes

Tigard has a thriving food and drink scene that offers a range of cuisines and flavors. Some of the must-try dishes in Tigard include:

  • Salmon: Tigard is located near the Columbia River, which is known for its salmon. Many local restaurants serve fresh and delicious salmon dishes.
  • Hazelnuts: Oregon produces over 90% of the US’s hazelnut crop, so it’s no surprise that hazelnuts are a popular ingredient in many local dishes and desserts.
  • Marionberry Pie: The marionberry is a type of blackberry that is native to Oregon. Marionberry pie is a popular dessert that you can find in many local bakeries.

Local Beverages

Tigard is known for its local wineries and breweries, but it also has several coffee shops and tea houses that are a must-visit. Some of the must-try beverages in Tigard include:

  • Pinot Noir: Oregon is famous for its Pinot Noir wines, and Tigard has several wineries that produce this variety of wine.
  • Craft Beer: Tigard has several breweries that produce a wide range of craft beers, from IPAs to stouts.
  • Artisan Coffee: Tigard has several coffee shops that serve freshly brewed coffee made from locally roasted beans.

What to Buy in Tigard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Tigard has a range of options. Some of the best places to shop in Tigard include:

  • Tigard Farmers Market: This outdoor market sells fresh produce, artisanal foods, and handmade crafts.
  • Washington Square: This shopping mall has over 170 stores and restaurants, including several local and national brands.
  • Main Street: This street in downtown Tigard has several boutiques and antique stores that sell unique and one-of-a-kind items.

Local Culture and Customs of Tigard

Tigard is a welcoming community that values diversity and inclusivity. When visiting Tigard, it’s important to respect local culture and customs, such as:

  • Tipping: It’s customary to tip at restaurants, cafes, and bars in Tigard.
  • Recycling: Tigard has a strict recycling program, and it’s important to separate recyclable materials from other waste.
  • Politeness: Tigardians are known for their politeness and friendliness, so it’s important to be respectful and courteous.

Best Time to Visit Tigard

The best time to visit Tigard is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Tigard has a mild climate year-round, so you can visit any time of the year and still enjoy its attractions and activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tigard

Tigard is a safe community, but it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions:

  • Make sure to lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation, especially at night.
  • Follow safety rules when engaging in outdoor activities, such as wearing a helmet while biking or following marked trails while hiking.

Health in Tigard

Tigard has several medical facilities and pharmacies that provide essential healthcare services. If you need medical assistance, you can visit the Tigard Medical Clinic or the Providence St. Vincent Medical Center.

Getting to Tigard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tigard

The nearest airport to Tigard is the Portland International Airport, which is located about 20 miles away. From the airport, you can take a taxi, shuttle, or car rental to reach Tigard.

Bus Stations Near Tigard

Tigard is served by several bus stations, including the Tigard Transit Center and the Southwest Corridor Transit Center. These stations provide bus services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

If you’re traveling to Tigard by car, you can take the I-5 or the VESTA Highway to reach the city. Tigard is also located near several major highways and interstates, including the I-205 and the I-84.

How to Get Around in Tigard

Tigard is a walkable city, and many attractions and activities are located within walking distance of downtown. However, if you prefer to use public transportation, Tigard has several options, including:

  • Bus: Tigard is served by several bus lines that connect it to nearby cities and towns.
  • MAX Light Rail: The MAX Light Rail provides convenient and affordable transportation to downtown Portland and other nearby areas.
  • Bike: Tigard has several bike routes and trails that make it easy to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Tigard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tigard is located near several popular destinations and excursions, including:

  • Portland: Just a short drive away, Portland offers a range of attractions and activities, including museums, galleries, and live music venues.
  • Mount Hood National Forest: This national forest is located about 50 miles east of Tigard and offers hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Tillamook Bay: Located on the Oregon Coast, Tillamook Bay is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and beachcombing.

Conclusion

Tigard is a vibrant and welcoming community that offers a range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages.
