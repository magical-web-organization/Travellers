Introduction to Gladstone

Gladstone, located in the Clackamas County of Oregon, is a charming city that offers visitors a perfect blend of outdoor recreation, cultural experiences, and incredible scenery. From hiking and biking trails to stunning natural attractions, there’s never a shortage of things to see and do in Gladstone. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or an extended vacation, our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your itinerary and make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Gladstone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gladstone has a rich history and culture that is reflected in the various museums and historical places that exist in the city. The most noteworthy attractions are:

  • The Gladstone Historical Society Museum: This museum offers visitors a glimpse into the history of Gladstone and Clackamas County. The museum has several exhibits that depict the history of the town and its people.

  • The Stone Cliff Inn: This restaurant is located in a historic building that was originally a quarry office. The building has been beautifully restored and now serves delicious food and drinks.

Natural Attractions of Gladstone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gladstone is home to several natural attractions that offer visitors the perfect opportunity to bask in the beauty of Oregon. Some of the top natural attractions in Gladstone are:

  • The Clackamas River: The Clackamas River is a beautiful river that runs through Gladstone. Visitors can fish, swim, and kayak in the river.

  • The Gladstone Nature Park: The Gladstone Nature Park is a stunning place that offers visitors a chance to hike, bike, and explore the natural beauty of the region. The park has several trails that range in difficulty, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gladstone

Gladstone is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The city is surrounded by several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the region. Some of the top hiking and biking trails in Gladstone are:

  • The Old River Road Trail: The Old River Road Trail is a popular hiking trail that runs along the Clackamas River. The trail is suitable for hikers of all skill levels and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding forests.

  • The Trolley Trail: The Trolley Trail is a 6-mile paved trail that runs from Gladstone to Milwaukie. The trail is suitable for both walkers and bikers and offers beautiful views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Gladstone: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions and hiking trails, Gladstone offers various activities and tours that cater to all types of visitors. Some of the top things to do in Gladstone are:

  • The Gladstone Aquatic Park: The Gladstone Aquatic Park is a perfect place to take your family for fun in the sun. The park has several water slides, pools, and splash pads that will keep your kids entertained for hours.

  • The Oregon City Farmers Market: The Oregon City Farmers Market is a popular place that offers visitors the chance to buy fresh, locally grown produce, and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gladstone

If you’re planning to spend a week in Gladstone, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Visit the Gladstone Nature Park and take a hike on one of the many trails. Day 2: Take a leisurely bike ride on the Trolley Trail and visit the Oregon City Farmers Market. Day 3: Explore the Gladstone Historical Society Museum and learn about the city’s rich history. Day 4: Visit the Clackamas River and go fishing or kayaking. Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Portland and explore its many attractions. Day 6-7: Spend the last two days of your trip relaxing at the Gladstone Aquatic Park or exploring the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Gladstone

Gladstone has a flourishing food and drink scene that is a mix of local cuisine and international flavors. Some of the top food and drink experiences that you shouldn’t miss are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The famous giant burger at the Tik Tok Restaurant: This burger is as big as your head and is a popular local dish that you must try.

  • The steamed mussels at the Rivershore Bar and Grill: The steamed mussels at the Rivershore Bar and Grill are a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • The local craft beer at the Gladstone Pub: The Gladstone Pub offers a variety of brews that are crafted by local breweries.

  • The coffee at the Clackamas River Growlers: The Clackamas River Growlers serves some of the best coffee in town.

Where to Stay in Gladstone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gladstone has a range of accommodation options that cater to all types of visitors. Whether you’re on a budget or want to splurge on luxury accommodations, you’ll find something that suits your needs. Some of the top accommodation options in Gladstone are:

  • The Gladstone Suites: These suites offer comfortable and spacious accommodations and are perfect for families.

  • The Holiday Inn Express: This hotel is located in a convenient location and offers clean rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Gladstone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gladstone has several stores that sell unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the popular items that you should consider buying are:

  • Local honey from the Gladstone Farmers Market: The local honey is known for its unique flavor and is a perfect souvenir to take home.

  • Handmade crafts and jewelry from the Oregon City Arts and Craft Fair: The Oregon City Arts and Craft Fair is held annually and is a great place to buy handmade souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Gladstone

Gladstone has a friendly and welcoming community that is proud of its history and traditions. While the city doesn’t have any specific customs or traditions, visitors are advised to be respectful of local customs and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Gladstone

The best time to visit Gladstone is from late spring to early fall (June - September). The temperatures are mild, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gladstone

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment when hiking and biking on local trails.
  • Be polite and courteous to locals and fellow travelers.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Engage in any dangerous activities that can endanger oneself or others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gladstone

Gladstone is a safe city, and visitors seldom encounter any significant safety issues. However, visitors are advised to practice basic safety precautions such as locking doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Gladstone

Gladstone has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services to both locals and visitors. Visitors are advised to carry their medical insurance and seek medical attention in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Gladstone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gladstone is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Some of the transportation options available in Gladstone are:

Airports Near Gladstone

  • Portland International Airport: The airport is located approximately 18 miles from Gladstone and offers several domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Gladstone

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Greyhound Bus Station is located in Portland, and visitors can take a bus to Gladstone from the station.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station: The Amtrak Train Station is located in Portland and offers several trains to nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Gladstone

Gladstone is a small city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance or a short drive from each other. However, visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use the public transportation system to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Gladstone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gladstone is located in a prime location and is surrounded by several cities and attractions that are worth visiting. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Gladstone are:

  • Portland: Portland is a vibrant city that offers visitors a range of attractions such as museums, parks, and gardens.

  • Mount Hood: Mount Hood is a popular skiing destination that is located approximately 73 miles from Gladstone.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a unique and exciting travel experience, Gladstone should be at the top of your list. With its incredible natural attractions, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences, Gladstone has something to offer everyone.
