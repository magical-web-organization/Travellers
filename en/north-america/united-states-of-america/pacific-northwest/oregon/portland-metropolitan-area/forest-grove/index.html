Introduction to Forest Grove

Forest Grove is a charming city located in the Portland Metropolitan Area, in Oregon, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States of America. Known for its beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and thriving arts scene, Forest Grove has something for everyone. Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Forest Grove is the perfect travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Forest Grove

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Washington County Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Washington County, Oregon. It has fascinating exhibits on the region’s native people, pioneers, and modern developments.

  • Old College Hall: Built in 1854, Old College Hall is the oldest building in the Pacific Northwest with its original function as an institution of higher education. The building also houses the University of Pacific’s Center for Civic Engagement.

  • Pacific University: Founded in 1849, Pacific University is one of the oldest universities in the western United States. Visitors can see stunning architecture while strolling the halls of this historic university.

Natural Attractions of Forest Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gales Creek Trail: This 18-mile trail takes hikers through beautiful forests, along streams and cascading waterfalls. Hikers will experience the serenity of nature with well maintained trails that create an easy, enjoyable experience.

  • Hagg Lake: Known as the “Gem of the Washington County Parks,” Hagg Lake is a lovely spot for boating, fishing, kayaking, picnicking, and hiking. The local park offers several sites for recreational activities, making it an adventure-filled day for families and individuals alike.

  • Fernhill Wetlands: Located just north of town, Fernhill Wetlands is a great spot for birding, hiking and observing wildlife. The 700-acre preserve is a duck habitat and is also home to deer, bald eagles, and otters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forest Grove

Forest Grove has numerous hiking and biking trails, some of which are multi-use recreational trails. While there are many to choose from, a few outstanding ones include:

  • Banks-Vernonia State Trail: This 21-mile trail runs from Banks to Vernonia, passing through peaceful farmlands and scenic forests. The paved trail is suitable for hikers, bikers, and horseback riders.

  • Tualatin Valley Scenic Bikeway: This 50-mile bike route showcases the vineyards and landscapes that Oregon is famous for. Cyclists can choose from the leisurely or challenging route, both beginning north of Forest Grove.

  • Patton Valley Loop Trail: This 10-mile hike is perfect for experienced hikers looking for a more challenging route. It may be difficult, but the pristine views make it worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Forest Grove: Activities and Tours

  • SakeOne: Tour this award-winning sake brewery, learn about the age-old brewing techniques, and sample some of their exquisite products. Sake lovers will definitely not want to miss this experience.

  • Montinore Estate: This winery is not your run-of-the-mill vineyard! A tour of Montinore Estate takes visitors on a journey from the wine grape’s beginning to the finished product. The tour, which includes a tasting, gives visitors an understanding of the winemaking process that produces the estate’s popular varietals.

  • Rusty Truck Brewing: Sample some of Forest Grove’s locally brewed beers at Rusty Truck Brewing. Visitors are welcome to take tours of the brewery, or sit back enjoy food and beverages while taking in live music and the area’s rustic charm.

Where to Stay in Forest Grove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • McMenamins Grand Lodge: This historic lodge was originally built as a Masonic home in 1922, and has since been renovated into a hotel. It has a unique, vintage feel, stunning gardens, and offers onsite dining and a spa.

  • Forest Grove Inn: The Forest Grove Inn is a quaint, cozy hotel with a friendly, attentive staff. It is conveniently located to walk to many dining and shopping establishments in the area.

  • Style ‘n’ comfort bed and Breakfast: This historic bed and breakfast was built in 1906 and features charming architecture, luxurious bedding and a delicious country-style breakfast. A great place to stay for those who love experiencing the comforts of home.

What to Buy in Forest Grove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Momokawa Sake: A bottle of locally brewed sake can be a perfect souvenir. Pick one up while you’re touring SakeOne.

  • Handmade Crafts: There are several craft stores in the area, like Art On Broadway, where visitors can find unique, handcrafted souvenirs to take home.

  • Farmers Market: For food-lovers, this market has locally sourced produce and locally made goods, like cheese and meats, as well as handmade crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Forest Grove

Though not as widely known, Forest Grove has a rich culture and a unique community spirit. The historic downtown area is filled with locally owned shops, restaurants and cafes that give the city its charming character. People are friendly and often stop to say hello to complete strangers. The town is also home to several festive events throughout the year, like the Pacific University Music in May Festival, Independence Day Fireworks, and Harvest Festival, that highlights October’s culinary harvest.

Best Time to Visit Forest Grove

The best time to visit Forest Grove would be during the summer months, between June and August when the weather is warm and events are ongoing. However, due to the miles of hiking trails and outdoor activities, visitors can also enjoy the beauty of spring and fall months with fewer crowds. Winter has fewer outdoor activities, but still has indoor charm with City holiday events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forest Grove

Do:

  • Do try out the local cuisine: Forest Grove has a wide range of options from vegan restaurants and cafes to fine dining experiences.

  • Do explore the outdoors: The town is home to several parks, hiking trails, and state parks that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Do take advantage of the wine and beer scene: Whether it’s visiting a brewery and winery or enjoying the varieties offered in town, Forest Grove is ideal for drinks lovers.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass on private properties: Forest Grove has some farmland areas, so it’s important to respect the boundaries.

  • Don’t litter: Always make sure to throw trash in a bin.

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable walking shoes: With tons of outdoor spots, comfortable walking shoes are essential.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forest Grove

Forest Grove is considered safe, but visitors should take normal precautions and always aware of their surroundings. Crime here does not often happen, but visitors should remain alert and attentive.

Health in Forest Grove

The town has access to healthcare services should an emergency occur. Visitors should keep a list of emergency numbers on hand.

Getting to Forest Grove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Forest Grove

  • Portland International Airport: Located 30 miles away, PDX is the best airport for visitors to fly into.

Bus Stations Near Forest Grove

  • Forest Grove Transit Center: Serves riders on TriMet and SMART, transit services throughout Washington County.

  • Hillsboro Transit Center: In nearby Hillsboro, this transit center offers connection routes to Portland, Pacific University and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car may be the best option as it allows the flexibility to drive to nearby attractions and throughout the countryside.

  • Taxi: A few local taxi companies operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Forest Grove

Visitors can easily get around Forest Grove on foot or bike. Some off-campus attractions may require a drive.

Where to Go Next from Forest Grove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Portland: Forest Grove’s proximity to Portland makes it easy to hop into the city for a day trip and check out the famous Pioneer Square, Powell’s Bookstore, and its many breweries and museums.

  • Oregon Coast: The coast of Oregon, only an hour and a half away, is an excellent choice for those looking for a picturesque beach break.

  • Mount Hood: Located less than two hours away, Mt. Hood is a must-visit for travelers who enjoy skiing, snowboarding, or hiking.

Conclusion

Forest Grove is an excellent travel destination for those wanting to explore the Pacific Northwest. Visitors can discover the town’s history, taste its local cuisine and beverages, and enjoy outdoor adventures that feature lush forested hikes and stunning lakes. With ample activities, wide expanse of natural beauty, and its proximity to other major destinations, visitors will love every moment spent in Forest Grove.
