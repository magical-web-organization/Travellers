Introduction to Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego is a captivating city located in the Portland Metropolitan Area, in Oregon, in Pacific Northwest, in the United States of America. It is known for its affluent neighborhoods, exceptional schools, and stunning natural beauty.

Lake Oswego sits on the banks of the picturesque Oswego Lake and offers a wide range of recreational activities such as hiking trails, watersports, and fishing opportunities. Visitors to Lake Oswego can expect to find a wealth of attractions ranging from museums, art galleries, and parks, as well as a thriving food and drink scene.

In this article, we’ll provide a comprehensive tour guide that covers everything you need to know about planning a trip to Lake Oswego. We’ve included information on places to visit, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, where to stay, what to buy, local customs and culture, safety and health, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Oswego

When it comes to attractions, Lake Oswego has plenty to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit on your trip to Lake Oswego:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Marylhurst Museum: The Marylhurst Museum is a museum located on the campus of Marylhurst University, just south of Lake Oswego. It showcases works of art that date back to ancient times.

  • Oswego Heritage House: Located in the heart of downtown Lake Oswego, the Oswego Heritage House offers a fascinating insight into the city’s rich history and culture.

  • The Iron Furnace: The Iron Furnace in George Rogers Park is a remnant of one of the earliest industries in Lake Oswego. It was in operation from the 1860s to the 1890s, and produced pig iron used to make stoves and other items.

Natural Attractions of Lake Oswego: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oswego Lake: At the heart of Lake Oswego is Oswego Lake, a private lake that was created in the early 1900s. You can explore the lake by signing up for a boat tour, or by renting a paddleboard, kayak, or canoe.

  • Tryon Creek State Park: Just a short drive from Lake Oswego is Tryon Creek State Park, a 658-acre park that offers hiking trails, guided nature walks, and a visitors center.

  • Foothills Park: Foothills Park is a 400-acre park located on the west shore of Lake Oswego. It features hiking trails, picnic areas, and a boat dock.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego has a multitude of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding natural beauty. Here are a few of the best routes to explore:

  • The Iron Mountain Trail: This trail is located in the Tryon Creek State Park and is perfect for hikers looking for a moderate challenge. It is 1.9 miles and is accessible year-round.

  • West Waluga Park Trail: This trail is just over a mile long and is perfect for both hikers and bikers. It offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is accessible year-round.

  • Springbrook Park Trail: This trail is located in the Springbrook Park and is 1.1 miles long. It is perfect for hikers and is accessible year-round.

Best Things to Do in Lake Oswego: Activities and Tours

  • Oswego Hills Vineyard and Winery: The vineyard is an excellent choice for those looking to sample some top-quality wine. You can book a tour and taste the wine while enjoying the stunning view of the surrounding countryside.

  • Oswego Lake Country Club: If you’re looking to play a round of golf, then the Oswego Lake Country Club is the place to be. The golf course is open year-round and features stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Lake Theater & Cafe: Take in a show or movie at the iconic Lake Theater & Cafe. It’s been a beloved institution in Lake Oswego for more than 80 years.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Oswego

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Lake Oswego, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Marylhurst Museum and explore downtown Lake Oswego.
2 Spend the day paddleboarding or kayaking on Oswego Lake.
3 Take a guided nature walk at Tryon Creek State Park.
4 Hike the Iron Mountain trail in Tryon Creek State Park.
5 Take a wine tasting tour at the Oswego Hills Vineyard and Winery.
6 Play a round of golf at the Oswego Lake Country Club.
7 Enjoy a show or movie at the Lake Theater & Cafe before departing from Lake Oswego.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego offers a wide range of dining options. Whether you’re looking for food on-the-go or a sit-down meal, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burger at Stickmen Brewery and Skewery: The burger at Stickmen Brewery and Skewery is considered one of the best in Lake Oswego. It’s juicy, well-seasoned, and comes in big portions.

  • Pizza from Pizzicato: Pizzicato specializes in Neapolitan-style pizza that’s made with the freshest ingredients. They also offer gluten-free and vegan options.

  • Seafood at Café del Sol: Café del Sol is known for its fresh seafood dishes, like the pan-seared salmon with a creamy beurre blanc sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Beer from Stickmen Brewery and Skewery: Along with their delicious burger, Stickmen Brewery and Skewery also brew their beer. Their beer selection ranges from light to dark, and they have many variations to try.

  • Wine from Oswego Hills Vineyard and Winery: The vineyard offers wine tastings that showcase their award-winning wines.

  • Coffee from Bellagios Coffee: Bellagios Coffee is an award-winning artisan coffee roaster that sources their beans from around the world, roasts them in-house, and serves them up fresh.

Where to Stay in Lake Oswego: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Lake Oswego, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hotel deLuxe: This charming hotel is located in the heart of downtown Portland, just a short drive from Lake Oswego. It features beautifully appointed rooms and suites, as well as a rooftop bar.

  • The Society Hotel: This boutique hotel is also located in downtown Portland and features tastefully decorated rooms, a rooftop deck, and a communal kitchen.

  • The Iron Gate Inn: The Iron Gate Inn is a Victorian-style bed and breakfast located in Lake Oswego itself. It features cozy rooms and a garden courtyard.

What to Buy in Lake Oswego: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you’re in Lake Oswego, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementos. Here are a few ideas:

  • Handcrafted pottery from the Attic Gallery: The Attic Gallery is an art gallery that showcases the work of local artisans, including handcrafted pottery.

  • Locally made chocolates from Moonstruck Chocolate Co: Moonstruck Chocolate Co is a local chocolatier that creates delicious and unique chocolate treats.

  • Handmade jewelry from the Art Heads Custom Framing & Gallery: This gallery showcases the work of local artists, including handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego has a friendly and laid-back culture that emphasizes hard work and the importance of community. It is a place that values natural beauty and outdoor recreation.

When visiting Lake Oswego, it’s essential to respect the locals and the community. You can show your respect by following basic etiquettes, being mindful of littering, and following traffic and city rules.

Best Time to Visit Lake Oswego

The best time to visit Lake Oswego is during the summer months, from June to August. The warm weather allows for ample time to enjoy the city’s beautiful outdoor attractions and take part in water sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates. Visitors can protect themselves by taking the usual precautions, like locking their cars and securing their belongings while out exploring.

Health in Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego has several medical facilities to cater to the health needs of tourists and residents alike.

Getting to Lake Oswego: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Oswego

The nearest airport to Lake Oswego is Portland International Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Lake Oswego

Lake Oswego has several bus stops that are serviced by TriMet. Buses are frequent and connect to other parts of Portland.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars for more flexibility when getting around Lake Oswego.

How to Get Around in Lake Oswego

The best way to get around Lake Oswego is by car. Rental cars are available at the airport or in nearby Portland.

Where to Go Next from Lake Oswego: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lake Oswego is just a short drive away from several nearby destinations. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Portland: The city of Portland is just a short drive away and offers a wealth of attractions, including museums, art galleries, and parks.

  • Mount Hood National Forest: This national forest is located about an hour’s drive from Lake Oswego and is home to several hiking and biking trails, as well as skiing opportunities in the winter.

Conclusion

Lake Oswego is an excellent destination for anyone looking to immerse in natural beauty, outdoor recreation, and a pleasant atmosphere. With our comprehensive guide, you’ll have everything you need to plan a memorable trip to Lake Oswego. Whether you’re looking to hike or bike, explore museums, or enjoy local cuisine and drink, Lake Oswego has something for everyone.
