Introduction to Portland Metropolitan Area

Portland Metropolitan Area is a beautiful region situated in Oregon, Pacific Northwest, United States of America. It is known for its extraordinary natural landscapes, rich history, and cultural attractions. The area comprises of Portland, the largest city in Oregon, and several surrounding cities and towns in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Portland Metropolitan Area

The Portland Metropolitan area is filled with unique and interesting places to explore. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, here is a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Oregon Museum of Science and Industry – a popular museum for all ages, featuring interactive exhibits, hands-on activities, and an IMAX theater.
  2. Pittock Mansion – a historic home turned museum that offers stunning views of the city and fascinating insights into the history of Portland and the Pacific Northwest.
  3. Portland Art Museum – home to an extensive collection of American, European, Asian, and Native American art, this museum is a great place to appreciate fine art.
  4. Lan Su Chinese Garden – a tranquil oasis in the heart of the city, offering visitors a chance to immerse themselves in Chinese culture and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Portland Metropolitan Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Forest Park – a vast urban forest with over 5,000 acres of wooded trails, perfect for hiking and biking.
  2. Columbia River Gorge National Scenic Area – a breathtakingly beautiful area with hiking trails, waterfalls, and incredible views of the Columbia River.
  3. Multnomah Falls – a stunning waterfall that is one of the state’s most iconic landmarks.
  4. Mount Hood – a ski resort with incredible views of Mount Hood and numerous hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portland Metropolitan Area

The Portland Metropolitan Area has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. Wildwood Trail – a 30-mile hiking trail that winds through Forest Park and offers stunning views of the city.
  2. Springwater Corridor – a 21-mile paved bike trail that connects downtown Portland to suburban Boring.
  3. Powell Butte – a hiking and mountain biking trail that offers panoramic views of the city and Mount Hood.
  4. Silver Falls State Park – a stunning state park with several waterfalls and 35 miles of hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Portland Metropolitan Area: Activities and Tours

Portland Metropolitan Area has no shortage of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some favorites:

  1. Bike and Brew Tour – a guided bike tour of the city’s craft brewery scene.
  2. Wine Country Tour – a tour of the Willamette Valley’s wineries and tasting rooms.
  3. Portland Saturday Market – a lively outdoor market that features local artists, crafters, and food.
  4. Japanese Garden – a serene garden that offers visitors a chance to relax and find inner peace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Portland Metropolitan Area

Day 1: Explore downtown Portland, visit the Portland Art Museum, and grab lunch at one of the food carts.

Day 2: Hike or bike through Forest Park followed by a visit to Pittock Mansion for stunning city views.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the Columbia River Gorge National Scenic Area or Mount Hood.

Day 4: Explore the historic Old Town/Chinatown neighborhood and take a tour of the Lan Su Chinese Garden.

Day 5: Visit the Oregon Museum of Science and Industry followed by a bike and brew tour of the city’s craft breweries.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the Willamette Valley wine country for a wine tasting tour.

Day 7: Relax at the Japanese Garden and take in the serene beauty of the gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Portland Metropolitan Area

Portland Metropolitan Area is known for its food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Voodoo Doughnuts – Portland’s most famous doughnut shop.
  2. Pok Pok – serves delicious Thai food.
  3. Salt and Straw – is a popular ice cream shop with unique flavors.
  4. Portland Food Carts - the city has a vast variety of food cars.

Local Beverages

  1. Portland Coffee – a city known for its coffee culture and home to several coffee roasters.
  2. Portland Craft Breweries – with over 75 craft breweries in the city, beer lovers will be in heaven.

Where to Stay in Portland Metropolitan Area: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Portland Metropolitan Area offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best choices:

  1. Ace Hotel Portland – centrally located in downtown Portland, this boutique hotel has modern decor and a lively bar/restaurant.
  2. Kennedy School – a unique and historic hotel that was once an elementary school - now turned into a beautiful lodging option.
  3. Hi-Portland Hawthorne Hostel – a budget-friendly option for travelers on a shoestring budget.

What to Buy in Portland Metropolitan Area: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Stumptown Coffee – a local coffee roaster with a national following.
  2. Powell’s Books – a massive independent bookstore with three levels of books to satisfy any reader.
  3. Pendleton Woolen Mills – a company that makes high-quality wool blankets, clothing, and other woolen goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Portland Metropolitan Area

Portland Metropolitan Area is known for its laid-back and quirky culture. Locals are passionate about food, art, and sustainability. Visitors can expect to encounter a vibrant music and arts scene, as well as a thriving food and craft beer scene.

Best Time to Visit Portland Metropolitan Area

The best time to visit Portland Metropolitan Area is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. Fall can also be a beautiful time to visit, with colorful foliage and milder temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portland Metropolitan Area

Do:

  1. Try the food carts – Portland has over 500 food carts serving everything from sushi to falafel.
  2. Visit Forest Park – the vast urban forest with miles of hiking and biking trails.
  3. Take public transportation – Portland has an excellent public transportation system with a light rail, buses, and streetcars.
  4. Support local businesses – Portland is known for its “shop local” mentality, so support the local businesses.

Don’t:

  1. Litter – Portland is known for its commitment to sustainability – so remember to recycle or throw trash properly.
  2. Drive during rush hour – Portland’s traffic can be challenging during peak hours.
  3. Smoke in public – smoking or vaping is not allowed in public places in Oregon.
  4. Forget a raincoat – Portland is known for its rainy weather, so always be prepared.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portland Metropolitan Area

Portland Metropolitan Area is generally considered a safe place to visit. However, as with any city, visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night and keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas.

Health in Portland Metropolitan Area

The tap water is safe to drink in Portland Metropolitan Area. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of Lyme disease and other illnesses carried by ticks in the wooded areas.

Getting to Portland Metropolitan Area: Transportation Options and Logistics

Portland Metropolitan Area is well connected to the rest of the country by air, train, and bus.

Airports Near Portland Metropolitan Area

  1. Portland International Airport (PDX) – the primary airport servicing the region.

Bus Stations Near Portland Metropolitan Area

  1. Greyhound Bus – provides bus services to several cities in the region.

Other Options

  1. Amtrak Cascades – train services linking Portland to Seattle and Eugene.
  2. Car Rental – renting a car can be a great way to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Portland Metropolitan Area

Portland Metropolitan Area has an excellent public transportation system, including light rail, buses, and streetcars. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Portland Metropolitan Area: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cannon Beach – a charming coastal town with picturesque beaches and stunning natural scenery.
  2. Oregon Coast – a scenic drive along the Oregon coastline with pristine beaches, historic lighthouses, and charming coastal towns.
  3. Mount St. Helens – a volcanic mountain that erupted in 1980 - now a national monument with scenic trails and views.
  4. Seattle – a cosmopolitan city with world-class museums, restaurants and iconic landmarks like the Space Needle.

Conclusion

Portland Metropolitan Area is a vibrant and unique region with something to offer every visitor. From outdoor adventures to renowned food and drink scene, cultural landmarks to beautiful natural wonders, this area has it all. Plan a trip and enjoy the best of this beautiful region.
