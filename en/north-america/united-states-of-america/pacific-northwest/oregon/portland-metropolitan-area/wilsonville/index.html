Introduction to Wilsonville

Nestled in the heart of the Portland Metropolitan Area in Oregon, Wilsonville is a charming small town known for its natural beauty, rich history, and thriving local culture. With its convenient location just a short drive away from Portland, Wilsonville makes for an ideal base for exploring the stunning Pacific Northwest region of the United States.

List of Places to Visit in Wilsonville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re looking to dive deeper into Wilsonville’s vibrant culture and history, there are plenty of museums and historical sites to visit.

  • World of Speed Motorsports Museum - This museum showcases the history and evolution of motorsports, including race cars, motorcycles, and boats.

  • Charbonneau District - This historic district features several beautifully preserved houses and buildings dating back to the early 20th century.

  • Graham Oaks Nature Park - This 250-acre park offers hiking trails, a picnic area, and a playground, as well as educational exhibits showcasing the park’s unique natural features.

Natural Attractions of Wilsonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking outdoor adventures and stunning scenic views, Wilsonville has plenty to offer.

  • Memorial Park - This 126-acre park boasts several hiking trails, a playground, a baseball and softball field, and an off-leash dog park.

  • Family Fun Center & Bullwinkle’s Restaurant - This family-friendly entertainment complex features go-kart racing, miniature golf, bumper boats, and laser tag.

  • Villebois Trail System - This extensive network of trails provides plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and getting in touch with nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilsonville

If you’re looking to explore the great outdoors on foot or by bike, Wilsonville provides plenty of scenic hiking and biking routes.

  • Willamette Greenway Trail - This trail runs alongside the Willamette River and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding landscape.

  • Graham Oaks Nature Park Trail - This 2.5-mile loop trail takes you through beautiful forests and past several wildlife exhibits.

  • Wilsonville Loop Trail - This 6-mile trail circles around the town of Wilsonville, offering lovely views of the city and the surrounding scenery.

Best Things to Do in Wilsonville: Activities and Tours

No matter your interests, Wilsonville has plenty of exciting activities and tours to keep you entertained.

  • Wine Tasting Tours - The Willamette Valley, located just a short drive away from Wilsonville, is home to some of the best wineries in the country.

  • Tualatin River Kayak Adventure - Glide down the Tualatin River and take in the stunning scenery as you paddle through calm waters.

  • Escape Rooms - Test your problem-solving skills with a thrilling escape room experience at Wilsonville’s Escapism Portland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilsonville

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Wilsonville and its surrounding area, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your stay.

Day 1-2: Explore Wilsonville’s museums and historical sites, including the World of Speed Motorsports Museum and the Charbonneau District.

Day 3-4: Take in the natural beauty of Wilsonville with hikes at Graham Oaks Nature Park and Villebois Trail System, as well as a visit to Memorial Park.

Day 5-6: Indulge in local wine tasting tours in the nearby Willamette Valley and paddle down the Tualatin River on a kayaking adventure.

Day 7: End your week with a thrilling escape room experience at Escapism Portland followed by a relaxing evening at one of the town’s many local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilsonville

When it comes to food and drink, Wilsonville offers an array of local dishes and beverages that are not to be missed.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oregon Dungeness Crab - The Pacific Northwest is known for its delicious crab, and Wilsonville is no exception. Try it steamed or in a rich crab chowder.

  • Marionberry Pie - Made from the delicious local Marionberries, this sweet pie is a tasty treat for dessert.

  • Tillamook Cheddar - This creamy and tangy cheese is a Pacific Northwest staple and makes a perfect accent to any burger or sandwich.

Local Beverages

  • Willamette Valley Wines - The Willamette Valley is one of the most well-known wine regions in the United States and offers an array of award-winning wines to sample.

  • Craft Beers - Wilsonville is home to several craft breweries, including McMenamins and Oregon Brewing Company, which offer a variety of unique brews.

Where to Stay in Wilsonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wilsonville offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences.

  • Best Western Wilsonville Inn & Suites - This hotel boasts a convenient location near many of Wilsonville’s top attractions and offers comfortable rooms and suites.

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites - This pet-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Wilsonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Wilsonville has plenty to offer.

  • Marionberry Jam - This sweet and tangy jam is made from fresh, locally grown Marionberries.

  • Lavender Products - Wilsonville is home to several lavender farms, which produce an array of beauty and wellness products made from this fragrant herb.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilsonville

Wilsonville’s local culture is characterized by its love of the outdoors, appreciation for local history and rich natural resources, and deep sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Wilsonville

The best time to visit Wilsonville is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilsonville

Do:

  • Enjoy the town’s beautiful natural surroundings by exploring the hiking and biking trails and visiting local parks.

  • Try local wines and craft beers at one of Wilsonville’s many wineries and breweries.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen and appropriate outdoor gear to protect yourself from the sun and wind on outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wilsonville

Wilsonville is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. However, as with any place you travel, be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in Wilsonville

Wilsonville has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, should you need medical attention while visiting the town.

Getting to Wilsonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wilsonville is conveniently located just off of Interstate 5, and offers several transportation options to help you get to and from the town.

Airports Near Wilsonville

  • Portland International Airport (PDX) - Located approximately 30 miles north of Wilsonville, PDX is the nearest major airport serving the town.

Bus Stations Near Wilsonville

  • Greyhound - Greyhound offers bus service to Portland and surrounding areas, with several stops near Wilsonville.

Other Options

  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services - Several taxi and ride-sharing services, including Uber and Lyft, are available in Wilsonville and can help you get around the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Wilsonville

Wilsonville is a pedestrian-friendly town, and many of its top attractions are within walking distance of each other. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation in Wilsonville, and the town offers several bike-friendly trails.

Where to Go Next from Wilsonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Pacific Northwest adventure, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Portland - Just a 20-minute drive away, Portland offers a wealth of attractions and activities, including museums, shopping, and dining.

  • Cannon Beach - This scenic coastal town is located approximately 80 miles northwest of Wilsonville and offers stunning beaches, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re in search of natural beauty, historic charm, or exciting outdoor adventures, Wilsonville has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and get ready to explore the best of the Pacific Northwest.
