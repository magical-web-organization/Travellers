Introduction to Beaverton

Located in the Portland metropolitan area, Beaverton is a vibrant city worth exploring. This bustling community is known for its parks, hiking and biking trails, fantastic food and drink scene, and thriving culture. With its close proximity to the city, Beaverton provides a perfect balance of urban and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Beaverton

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, shopping, or outdoor activities, Beaverton has something for every traveler. Here are some must-visit attractions in Beaverton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Beaverton History Center

The Beaverton History Center is a great place to learn about the city’s heritage. The center is home to a variety of exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of the area from the early days of Native American habitation to the currents times.

Tualatin Hills Nature Center

The Tualatin Hills Nature Center is a fascinating place for nature lovers. The center has several informative exhibits that feature the plant and animal species native to the region. Visitors can explore the nearby trails, including the interpretive bird watching trail.

Pittock Mansion

Pittock Mansion is a historic home located in Portland. The mansion is a popular spot for tourists and locals alike, offering a glimpse into the lifestyle of Portland’s most prominent citizens in the early 1900s. The mansion provides visitors with a blend of history, architecture, and scenic views.

Natural Attractions of Beaverton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cooper Mountain Nature Park

Cooper Mountain Nature Park is a 231-acre park in Beaverton. The park is known for its stunning views of the Tualatin Valley and its vast network of walking trails. Visitors can enjoy hiking, picnicking, and bird watching.

Tualatin River National Wildlife Refuge

The Tualatin River National Wildlife Refuge includes over 1,000 acres of wetlands, forests, and grasslands. The refuge is home to an impressive variety of wildlife species, including migratory birds, otters, and beavers. The refuge offers hiking and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Jenkins Estate Park

Jenkins Estate Park is a 68-acre park located in Beaverton. The park features beautiful gardens, wooded trails, and stunning views of the Tualatin Valley. The park also has several historic buildings, including the Jenkins Estate mansion and Carriage House, which can be reserved for events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaverton

Beaverton is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The city has many trails suitable for both beginner and experienced hikers. Some popular hiking and biking trails in Beaverton include:

  • Waterhouse Trail
  • Fanno Creek Trail
  • Rock Creek Trail
  • Greenway Park Trail

Week-Long Itinerary for Beaverton

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in Beaverton:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Beaverton Farmers Market in the morning. Spend the afternoon exploring the Beaverton History Center.
Day 2 Take a hike on the Waterhouse Trail in the morning. In the afternoon, explore the Tualatin Hills Nature Center.
Day 3 Take a day trip to downtown Portland. Explore the Portland Art Museum and have lunch at one of the food carts in the city.
Day 4 Rent a bike and explore the Rock Creek Trail in the morning. In the afternoon, visit the Pittock Mansion in Portland.
Day 5 Visit the Tualatin River National Wildlife Refuge in the morning. Spend the afternoon shopping at Washington Square Mall.
Day 6 Explore the Jenkins Estate Park in the morning. In the afternoon, visit Das Rec and experience the world of virtual reality.
Day 7 Spend your last day exploring the Beaverton City Library and enjoying a show at the Beaverton Civic Theatre in the evening.

Best Time to Visit Beaverton

The best time to visit Beaverton is between June and August. During this time, the weather is warm and dry, and there are plenty of outdoor events and activities to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beaverton

Beaverton is a safe city with low crime rates. However, it is always advised to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and staying aware of your surroundings.

Health in Beaverton

Beaverton has excellent medical facilities and hospitals, including Adventist Health Portland and Providence St. Vincent Medical Center.

Getting to Beaverton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beaverton

The closest airport to Beaverton is the Portland International Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Beaverton

Beaverton has a Greyhound bus station located at 4055 SW Lombard Avenue.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, Beaverton is easily accessible via Highways 26 and 217.

How to Get Around in Beaverton

Beaverton has an excellent public transportation system, including buses, MAX light rail, and streetcars. Additionally, several rental car companies operate in the area.

Conclusion

Beaverton is a fantastic destination full of exciting attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious food and drinks. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, Beaverton has something to offer every traveler. With its close proximity to Portland and the surrounding natural beauty, Beaverton is a must-visit destination on any Oregon travel itinerary.
