Introduction to Oregon City

Welcome to Oregon City, a charming suburban town in the Portland Metropolitan Area. Oregon City is often overlooked by tourists as they head straight to Portland, but this hidden gem offers a variety of attractions and activities. The town is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a thriving food and drink scene.

In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Oregon City to plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Oregon City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oregon City has a rich cultural history, and you can explore it in the museums and historical sites around the town.

  • Oregon City Municipal Elevator: Take a ride on the oldest outdoor municipal elevator in the United States, which takes you to the top of a cliff for stunning views of Willamette Falls.

  • End of the Oregon Trail Interpretive Center: This museum is dedicated to the pioneers who traveled the Oregon Trail. Explore interactive exhibits and learn about life on the wagon train.

  • Museum of the Oregon Territory: This museum is housed in a historic building and features artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Oregon City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oregon City offers a wealth of natural beauty, including stunning falls, parks, and green spaces that are perfect for outdoor activities.

  • Willamette Falls: One of the largest and most beautiful falls in the Pacific Northwest, offering spectacular views of the waterfalls and Willamette River.

  • Clackamette Park: This riverside park is perfect for a day out with family, recreational activities, or a picnic by the river.

  • Mount Talbert Nature Park: A great spot for hiking with a variety of trails to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oregon City

Explore the outdoors and get some exercise while taking in the natural beauty of the region.

  • Oregon City Trail Loop: A 5.8-mile hiking and biking route that starts at the Meldrum Bar Park and circles Oregon City, offering views of the Willamette River, the historic downtown and more.

  • McLoughlin Promenade: A short, 0.7-mile walk offering panoramic views of Willamette Falls, Mount Hood, and the surrounding countryside.

  • Rivercrest Park Trail: A scenic hike that winds through the Rivercrest neighborhood and ends up at Clackamette Park.

Best Things to Do in Oregon City: Activities and Tours

Oregon City offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all tastes and budgets.

  • Oregon City Ghost Tours: Take a spooky tour of the town and learn about its dark past and haunted sites.

  • Willamette River Kayak Tour: Get on the water and explore the river on a guided kayak tour.

  • Oregon City Farmers Market: Held on Saturdays, this farmers market offers fresh produce, local goods, and artisan crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oregon City

Day 1: Explore downtown Oregon City, visit the Municipal Elevator and the Museum of Oregon Territory.

Day 2: Hike the Oregon City Trail Loop and enjoy views of the Willamette River and the town.

Day 3: Visit the End of the Oregon Trail Interpretive Center and learn about the pioneers who traveled the Oregon Trail.

Day 4: Take a Willamette River Kayak Tour and explore the river.

Day 5: Visit the historic Abernethy Green, spend time at Clackamette Park and enjoy a picnic by the river.

Day 6: Take an Oregon City Ghost Tour and learn about the town’s haunted past and stories.

Day 7: Explore the natural beauty of the town with a visit to Mount Talbert Nature Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Oregon City

Must-Try Dishes

Oregon City offers a variety of dining options, ranging from fancy restaurants to local dives with delicious food. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Oregon City Bakery: Known for its fresh baked bread, pastries, and savory pies.

  • Yvonne’s: A southern-style restaurant that serves delicious soul food.

  • Kley’s: A famous local BBQ restaurant that serves up smokey, delicious meats.

Local Beverages

Oregon City also has a thriving local beverage scene, including craft beer, cider houses, and wineries.

  • Coin Toss Brewing: A craft brewery that brews a variety of beers.

  • Arch Bridge Taphouse: A cozy beer bar that offers a variety of locally brewed beers.

  • St. Josef’s Winery: A family-owned winery that offers a variety of high-quality wines.

Where to Stay in Oregon City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oregon City offers a variety of options for accommodation, ranging from budget options to luxury hotels.

  • Oregon City Inn: A budget hotel that offers clean rooms and basic amenities.

  • Best Western Plus Rivershore Hotel: A mid-range hotel that offers spacious rooms and river views.

  • Barclay House Bed & Breakfast: A charming B&B that offers cozy rooms and homemade breakfast.

Local Culture and Customs of Oregon City

Oregon City has a rich cultural history, and locals take pride in its heritage. Some of the customs and traditions you may come across are:

  • Oregon City is home to a significant population of Indigenous Peoples, and it is essential to respect their culture and traditions.

  • The town is home to a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, including the Oregon City Festival of the Arts, Oregon Trail Rally, and Fourth of July Parade.

Best Time to Visit Oregon City

Oregon City has a mild climate and can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is in the summer months when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oregon City

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the town, and do not litter or harm the wildlife.

  • Visit the local farmers market and try fresh, locally sourced produce.

  • Learn about the town’s history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect the indigenous culture or the natural beauty of the region.

  • Do not smoke in public spaces as it is illegal

  • Do not feed the wildlife, as it can harm them and disrupt the natural ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oregon City

Oregon City is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions to stay safe, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping their valuables safe.

Health in Oregon City

The town has adequate medical facilities to cater to tourists and locals in case of an emergency.

Getting to Oregon City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oregon City

The nearest passenger airports to Oregon City are Portland International Airport and Salem Municipal Airport.

Bus Stations Near Oregon City

Greyhound and Bolt Bus provide bus services to Portland, OR, which is about 20 miles north of Oregon City.

Other Options

If driving, the town is easily accessible via Interstate 205, which intersects with several highways. Visitors can also take the Amtrak train, which stops in Portland.

How to Get Around in Oregon City

The best way to explore the town is by car, bike, or on foot. Visitors can rent bikes from local bike shops, or hire taxis or ride-sharing services to get around.

Where to Go Next from Oregon City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oregon City is within easy reach of several nearby destinations and attractions, such as:

  • Portland, Oregon: The biggest city in the region and a must-visit destination for its food, arts, and culture.

  • Mt. Hood National Forest: A scenic park offering a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.

  • Silver Falls State Park: A beautiful park with gorgeous waterfalls and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Oregon City is a perfect destination for those looking for a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With its museums, hiking trails, and local dining options, it offers something for every type of traveler. Plan your trip with our guide and get ready to explore this charming town nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest.
