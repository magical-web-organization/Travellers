Introduction to Huntington

Nestled in the beautiful landscape of Eastern Oregon, Huntington is a town surrounded by rolling hills, dramatic canyons, and stunning mountain peaks. If you’re looking for a unique travel experience, Huntington is the place for you. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and breathtaking natural scenery, Huntington offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Huntington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Henrietta Park: Walk through history at Fort Henrietta Park, which was established by early pioneers in the 1800s. This park offers a glimpse into the past with beautifully preserved historical buildings, including a schoolhouse and general store.
  • Huntington Museum: Learn about the town’s history and culture at the Huntington Museum, which houses exhibits on local Native American tribes and early pioneers.
  • Saint Andrews Episcopal Church: Marvel at the stunning stained-glass windows and sweeping architecture of this historic church, which dates back to 1884.

Natural Attractions of Huntington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crane Hot Springs: Soak in the natural beauty of Eastern Oregon at Crane Hot Springs, where you can relax in hot mineral water surrounded by stunning mountain views.
  • Owyhee Canyonlands: Explore the dramatic canyons and breathtaking vistas of the Owyhee Canyonlands, a vast expanse of wilderness that spans across Oregon, Idaho, and Nevada.
  • Riverside Park: Experience the great outdoors at Riverside Park, a lush park situated on the banks of the Snake River. Perfect for fishing, picnicking, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntington

  • Owyhee Uplands Backcountry Byway: Take a scenic drive through the Owyhee Uplands Backcountry Byway, a 100-mile route that takes you through stunning rock formations, deep canyons, and breathtaking vistas.
  • Painted Hills Trail: Hike the Painted Hills Trail, a 3.2-mile loop that takes you through the vibrant landscapes of the Painted Hills, featuring colorful rock formations and breathtaking views.
  • Snake River Greenbelt Trail: Bike or hike along the Snake River Greenbelt Trail, a scenic 8-mile route that follows the Snake River and takes you through beautiful parks and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Huntington: Activities and Tours

  • Rafting on the Snake River: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Snake River, a popular activity for adventurers of all levels.
  • Fossil Hunting: Explore the rich geological history of Eastern Oregon by fossil-hunting in the nearby hills and canyons.
  • Birdwatching: Enjoy the abundance of bird species in the area, including bald eagles, great blue herons, and sandhill cranes.

Food and Drink Scene of Huntington

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huckleberry Pie: Taste the local flavor with a slice of huckleberry pie, a regional specialty.
  • Elk Burgers: Try the flavorful and lean elk burgers, a popular dish in this area.
  • Basque Cuisine: Indulge in the unique and delicious cuisine of the Basque population in Eastern Oregon, with its signature lamb and seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wines: Savor the local wines produced in the nearby vineyards of Eastern Oregon, which are known for their distinctive flavor and quality.
  • Craft Beer: Enjoy the local craft beer scene, with brews made from locally sourced hops and grains.

Where to Stay in Huntington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Huntington Bed and Breakfast: Relax in the cozy and comfortable accommodations of the Huntington Bed and Breakfast, which offers a charming vintage decor and personalized service.
  • Oasis Motel: Enjoy the convenience and affordability of the Oasis Motel, which offers clean and comfortable rooms and a convenient location near the town center.
  • Juniper Hill RV Park: Camp out in the beautiful countryside at Juniper Hill RV Park, which offers spacious and well-maintained facilities.

What to Buy in Huntington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam and Syrup: Bring home the sweet and tangy taste of huckleberries with jars of homemade jam and syrup.
  • Native American Crafts: Shop for unique and authentic Native American crafts, including jewelry, pottery, and woven baskets.
  • Local Wine and Beer: Take home a taste of Eastern Oregon with locally produced wine and beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntington

Huntington has a rich cultural heritage shaped by the Basque population, as well as the Native American tribes that have called this area home for centuries. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including the importance of nature and natural resources, and avoid littering or damaging the environment.

Best Time to Visit Huntington

The best time to visit Huntington is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, making outdoor adventures and activities more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huntington

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Eastern Oregon.
  • Try the local cuisine and products.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Ignore safety precautions during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Huntington

Visitors should be cautious during outdoor activities such as hiking, rafting, or exploring the countryside. Follow safety guidelines, wear appropriate safety gear, and be aware of hazards such as flash floods or sudden changes in weather.

Health in Huntington

Visitors should drink plenty of water and use sunscreen to protect against sun exposure during outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of ticks and other insect bites that can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease.

Getting to Huntington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huntington

The closest airports to Huntington are Boise Airport, located approximately 150 miles away, and Portland International Airport, located approximately 260 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Huntington

The nearest bus stations to Huntington are located in La Grande, Oregon, approximately 40 miles away, and Boise, Idaho, approximately 140 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Huntington by car via Interstate 84, which runs through the town.

How to Get Around in Huntington

Visitors can explore Huntington and its surrounding areas by car or bike. In-town transportation options include taxis and rental cars, as well as local shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Huntington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Boise, Idaho

Explore the vibrant city of Boise, located approximately 140 miles from Huntington. Enjoy the city’s culture, history, and outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and skiing.

Hell’s Canyon National Recreation Area

Discover the rugged and beautiful landscapes of Hell’s Canyon National Recreation Area, located approximately 100 miles from Huntington. Enjoy outdoor activities such as rafting, hiking, and fishing in this stunning natural wonder.

Huntington offers a unique travel experience for visitors seeking natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture.
