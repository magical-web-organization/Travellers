Introduction to Kimberly

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Oregon, Kimberly is a tiny town bursting with small-town charm, surrounded by stunning natural beauty. The town offers plenty of exciting outdoor activities and opportunities to connect with nature as well as unique cultural attractions. Kimberly makes an excellent destination for a relaxing weekend getaway, a romantic vacation, or an adventure holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Kimberly

Here are some top places to visit and things to do in Kimberly, Oregon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kam Wah Chung and Co. Museum: This living testament to the Chinese pioneers who helped build America is a National Historic Landmark that offers a glimpse into what life was like in Oregon during the 1800s.
  • Grant County Historical Museum: This museum features exhibits detailing the history of Eastern Oregon and offers guided tours of the region and its past.

Natural Attractions of Kimberly: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • John Day Fossil Beds National Monument: Explore three breathtaking geological formations - Clarno, Painted Hills, and Sheep Rock.
  • Malheur National Forest: The perfect destination for anyone looking for a peaceful hike, beautiful views, or a family outing. The forest offers a range of recreational activities, from hiking and camping to boating and fishing.
  • Strawberry Mountain Wilderness: This area offers a diverse range of rocky canyons, glacial lakes, meadows, and alpine settings to explore via foot, horseback, or bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kimberly

One of the best things about Kimberly is the wealth of trails that permeate the natural surroundings, from well-maintained paths to more remote, wild terrain. Here are some highlights:

  • Sumpter Valley Railway Trail: From tunnels and bridges to trestles and scenic vistas, this 18-mile leisurely hike is ideal for soaking up the natural beauty of the area.
  • McClellan Trail: This beginner hike takes you through lush forest and meadows and is only about 1.2 miles long.
  • Big Creek Trail: This 5.3-mile moderate hike is perfect for those seeking a more active challenge and rewards hikers with stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Kimberly: Activities and Tours

Kimberly offers plenty of opportunities to try new things and expand your horizons. Below are just a few of the many things to do in the area:

  • Wine Tasting: Visit a vineyard in the Snake River Valley, one of Idaho’s newest and most exciting wine regions, which is located only an hour from Kimberly.
  • Bird Watching: The town of Kimberly is a bird watcher’s paradise; there are over 150 different species of birds that call the John Day River Basin home.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the stunning natural beauty of the area from horseback, trotting through the forests and meadows of Eastern Oregon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kimberly

Here’s a suggested seven-day itinerary for your trip to Kimberly:

Day 1 Arrive in Kimberly, Check-In, Explore Downtown
Day 2 Malheur National Forest, Strawberry Mountain Wilderness, Big Creek Trail
Day 3 Kam Wah Chung and Co. Museum, Grant County Historical Museum, City Parks
Day 4 John Day Fossil Beds National Monument, Sheep Rock, and Painted Hills
Day 5 Oregon Trail Interpretive Center, Shaw Mountain Lookout, Horseback Riding
Day 6 Wine Tasting in Snake River Valley, Bird Watching in Day John River Basin
Day 7 Leave Kimberly for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Kimberly

Kimberly boasts a diverse culinary scene featuring local and regional ingredients and beverage options you won’t find anywhere else.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wagon Wheel Meatball: The famous Wagon Wheel Restaurant serves up the best meatballs in town with tangy tomato sauce.
  • Chicken Fried Steak: If you’re craving a classic American dish, try the Chicken Fried Steak at Big John’s, which is known for its delicious home-style cooking.
  • Basque-style Lamb Stew: The Apple Peddler Restaurant serves up hearty stew made with tomatoes, lamb, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Oregon Wines: With its perfect growing conditions and climate, Oregon has become one of the country’s top wine regions. Try a local wine tasting in Snake River Valley.
  • Craft Beers: Brewery hop in the area and taste cold IPA or ales like the local’s favorite Barley Brown’s Brewpub, 1188 Brewing Company or Terminal Gravity Brewing.

Where to Stay in Kimberly: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

With only a few accommodation options, Kimberly is a small-town getaway offering unique and comfortable places to rest your head after a day out exploring.

  • The Lodge at Sumpter: This comfortable lodge is close to one of the most beautiful hikes in the area, the Sumpter Valley Railway Trail.
  • The Apple Inn: Located in the middle of Kimberly, this guesthouse gives easy access to local restaurants and shops.
  • The Big Pines RV and Tent Park: For avid campers, Big Pine RV offers 24 sites for tent camping and has two electric and water sites so guest can camp-in without roughing it.

What to Buy in Kimberly: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kimberly, Oregon, and its surrounding areas offer a variety of unique shopping opportunities to purchase items you just can’t find anywhere else. Here are the top items to shop for:

  • Jewelry: Tuck your shopping bag full of handmade jewelry such as copper or turquoise-themed pieces, that are available at various shops in Kimberly.
  • Local Honeys: Take some honey home from local producers like Gold Creek Valley, John and Cathy’s Honey House in Baker County, or Imnaha Honey Company.
  • Antiques and Thrift Shopping: Whether you’re a collector, a bargain hunter, or just looking for unique and nostalgic gifts, Kimberly has many antique shops and thrift shops worth exploring.

Local Culture and Customs of Kimberly

Youngsters are brought up to be respectful to their elders and a handshake or eye-to-eye contact is mandatory when introducing yourself or bidding farewell. The town follows a relaxed, slow-paced, and warm culture where everybody knows everybody else, and neighbors often stop by to chat and see how you are doing.

Best Time to Visit Kimberly

The best time to explore Eastern Oregon is during the summer months - from June to August. This is when the weather is usually dry and pleasant, making it ideal for hiking, biking, and exploring. The fall season is also a terrific time to visit Kimberly. Despite shorter daylight hours, fall brings gorgeous colorful foliage with unique starting points within the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kimberly

Do:

  • Do pack layers - weather can turn quickly to an abrupt downpour or temperature change throughout the day.
  • Do go off the beaten path – While the main attractions are lovely, the surrounding area is also worth exploring in-depth!
  • Do respect the land and culture – From respecting local cultural norms to practicing Leave No Trace Principles

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore the weather!! – You’ll want to know if a storm is coming to avoid any safety issues or closures.
  • Don’t forget cash - Several small-town like businesses still only except cash.
  • Don’t feed wild animals - Feeding wildlife has multiple negative outcomes. Stay a respectful distance and appreciate wildlife from afar.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kimberly

Kimberly is a safe and peaceful town, but some natural hazards do exist, like wildlife and occasional natural disasters. It’s recommended to check weather conditions before heading out on an outdoor adventure and notify someone trustworthy of your plans. This action is to prevent dangers or accidents from occurring while on a trail, especially unprepared.

Health in Kimberly

Kimberly isn’t necessarily bustling with healthcare options however, emergency responders are available. Visitors with a medical condition should carry their medication with them, especially if they have specific health needs. It’s also wise to communicate with your doctor or insurance provider before traveling.

Getting to Kimberly: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Kimberly isn’t difficult, though it can be slightly challenging to get there by public transport. The easiest way to get here is by car through scenic roads and highways in a picturesque landscape

Airports Near Kimberly:

  • Eastern Oregon Regional Airport (PDT): this airport is based in Pendleton, which is approximately 90 miles from Kimberly.

Other commercial airports will be a substantial distance away; Portland International Airport (PDX) is 280 miles from Kimberly.

Bus Stations Near Kimberly:

Greyhound has a bus service which stops in Baker City, 14 miles (nearly 23 km) southwest of Kimberly.

Other Options:

Several car rental providers can be found in either Baker City or Pendleton for those who didn’t come with their vehicles.

How to Get Around in Kimberly

While transportation isn’t a major issue in Kimberly, traveling on foot is a recommended option in this small town. Visitors who would like to venture further out and see more of the area can access car rentals.

Where to Go Next from Kimberly: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Pacific Northwest has several beautiful destinations perfect for continuing your vacation. Travelers to Kimberly can consider visiting the following close-by destinations:

  • Bend, Oregon: Known for its outdoor recreational opportunities like biking, hiking, and skiing, it’s approximately 160 miles through the scenic drive through Highway 20 from Kimberly.
  • Boise, Idaho: Enjoy a taste of the urban area without wandering too far from nature in the cultural capital of Idaho. Though Boise is a 3 plus hours drive away from Kimberly, there’s a lot to see along the way.

Conclusion

Kimberly is deserving of its place as a top Pacific Northwest destination. It’s small charming town, with authentic experiences of Oregon, makes it an ideal spot for travelers looking for a relaxing taste of local culture. With its stunning natural beauty, plethora of outdoor activities, and unique cultural attractions, visitors to Kimberly can’t help but be enchanted by its charms.
