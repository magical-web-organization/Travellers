Introduction to Fossil

Tucked away in Eastern Oregon, Fossil is a small town that boasts big attractions. The town’s name is derived from the ancient fossil beds that lay in the area, giving visitors a peek into the region’s past. With its beautiful natural landscapes and rich cultural history, Fossil appeals to outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Fossil, you’ll discover the town’s top attractions, including natural and cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, food and drink scene, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Fossil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fossil Beds National Monument: Marvel at the fossil beds that make the area famous at this national monument. Exhibits, trails, and tours offer an up-close look at the region’s ancient past.
  • Shaniko Historic District: Explore one of Oregon’s most well-preserved Old West towns. The district features numerous well-preserved buildings and is a great spot for a day of exploration.
  • Wheeler County Courthouse: Admire the beautiful architecture of this stunning courthouse, which was built in the 1920s.

Natural Attractions of Fossil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • John Day Fossil Beds National Monument: Experience the unique geology of the area, which provides an incredible opportunity to explore ancient ecosystems through fossils and rocks exposed by erosion.
  • Sheep Rock Unit Trails: Enjoy hiking through the stunning terrain of the Sheep Rock Unit. Trails range in difficulty from easy to challenging and boast stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Painted Hills: Visit one of the seven wonders of Oregon, the Painted Hills, and admire the vibrant hues of these land formations that have been shaped by millions of years of erosion.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fossil

  • Sheep Rock Unit Trails: With breathtaking views, the Sheep Rock Unit trails are a favorite among hikers and amateur naturalists. Trails range in difficulty, from an easy 0.5-mile trek to a challenging 6.8-mile loop.
  • Oregon Paleo Lands Center and Gallery Trail: This 1-mile trail offers an easy stroll through the stunning landscapes of Central Oregon. The trail features interpretive panels and an outdoor exhibit area which displays fossils and geological specimens.

Best Things to Do in Fossil: Activities and Tours

  • Fossil Hunting Tours: Take a guided tour to explore the area’s fascinating fossil beds. You can collect and take home what you find, making it a unique and exciting activity.
  • Antiquing: Fossil is home to many antique shops that sell unique treasures from the past. A treasure hunt through these shops makes for a fun and eclectic day of shopping.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the incredible scenery of the area’s national monuments on horseback. Many local outfitters offer guided trail rides for riders of all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Fossil

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue from the Fossil General Store and Cafe: Sink your teeth into delicious barbecue that is slow-cooked for 12 hours over an applewood fire.
  • Wild Game at Thirteen Moons Ranch: Treat yourself to the unique flavors of elk, bison, and wild boar while taking in the stunning views of Thirteen Moons Ranch.

Local Beverages

  • Wine Tasting at Marrowstone Vineyards: Head to nearby Marrowstone Vineyards for a tasting of its award-winning wines.
  • Breweries in The Dalles: Pop over to The Dalles for a taste of local beer at one of the many breweries.

Where to Stay in Fossil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fossil Inn Bed & Breakfast: Stay in the heart of town at this cozy B&B that boasts stunning mountain views and comfortable accommodations.
  • Wilson Ranches Retreat Bed & Breakfast: Enjoy a uniquely Oregon experience at this working cattle and hay ranch. Cozy rooms and ranch-style meals make for a memorable stay.
  • Camping: For those who prefer a more outdoor adventure, there are numerous campsites in the area.

What to Buy in Fossil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fossils and Rocks: Take a piece of the region’s past home with you by purchasing a fossil or rock from one of the local shops.
  • Handmade Crafts and Art: Shop for handmade items and art from local artists, including pottery, paintings, and other unique objects.

Local Culture and Customs of Fossil

The area’s culture is defined by its rugged terrain, Western heritage, and fossil-rich soil. Visitors should be respectful of this unique culture and follow any relevant guidelines or customs. Expect friendly and down-to-earth locals along your journey.

Best Time to Visit Fossil

The best time to visit Fossil is during the late spring, summer, and early fall, when temperatures are mild, and outdoor activities are abundant. Winter can be cold, and access to certain areas may be restricted due to weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fossil

Do:

  • Take time to explore the area’s many hiking and biking trails.
  • Respect the area’s natural and cultural resources.
  • Visit the local museums and historical sites to learn more about the town’s past.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property to collect fossils or rocks.
  • Leave trash or disturb the landscape in any way.
  • Ignore any warnings or restrictions posted in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fossil

Fossil is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors visiting sites in the desert locations, like fossil area or painted hills, should stay alert and be aware of the weather condition as it can change quickly.

Health in Fossil

No medical facilities are available in Fossil, so visitors should ensure they bring any necessary medication with them. Those with allergies should be aware that the area has many farms.

Getting to Fossil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fossil

The nearest airport to Fossil is the Portland International Airport (PDX), located approximately 4 hours west of Fossil.

Bus Stations Near Fossil

There are no public bus stations directly in Fossil. However, several Greyhound lines have stops in The Dalles, which is approximately an hour’s drive from Fossil.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Fossil, taking either I-84 west to the Route 218 exit or US Highway 26.

How to Get Around in Fossil

Road conditions in Fossil are good, and the area is easily navigable. The best way to get around is by car. Many of the attractions in the area are within a short driving distance from each other.

Where to Go Next from Fossil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pendleton: Explore the Old West city of Pendleton, located approximately 2 hours from Fossil.
  • Mt. Hood: Discover stunning views and outdoor activities at Mount Hood, located approximately 3 hours from Fossil.
  • Portland: Visit the eclectic city of Portland, located approximately 4 hours from Fossil.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Fossil offers visitors a unique look into Oregon’s ancient past, stunning natural landscapes, and warm local hospitality. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or casual traveler, Fossil promises an unforgettable experience. Explore the town’s many natural and cultural attractions, indulge in local food and drink, and soak up the incredible beauty of the Pacific Northwest.
