Introduction to Ontario

Ontario is a small city located in eastern Oregon, United States. While small in size, it is packed with attractions and activities that make it a popular destination for travelers. Ontario offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for both history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts. On top of that, the local food and drink scene is something to look forward to. In this tour guide, we will explore the best of Ontario and everything this amazing city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ontario

If you’re wondering what places to visit in Ontario, here are some popular options:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ontario boasts several museums and historical sites that are a must-see for any traveler:

  • Four Rivers Cultural Center & Museum
  • Lizard Butte Historical Society
  • Malheur County Historical Society and Museum
  • Vale Oregon Oregon Trail Interpretive Center

Each of these sites offers a unique perspective of Ontario’s history and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Ontario: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts will love the scenic views and outdoor adventures that Ontario offers:

  • Treasure Valley Park & Snake River Greenbelt
  • Succor Creek State Natural Area
  • Owyhee Canyonlands
  • Malheur National Forest

From hiking, fishing, camping, and sightseeing, these natural attractions promise a memorable experience for any visitor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ontario

Ontario also has several hiking and biking routes that are worth exploring. These include:

  • Oregon Trail Interpretive Park Loop: an easy 1.2-mile trail that offers scenic views of the Malheur River and surrounding nature.
  • Rim Rock Trail: a challenging 2.5-mile hike that goes through rocky terrain and provides stunning views of the city.
  • Malheur River Scenic Bikeway: a 120-mile trail that starts in Ontario and goes through stunning natural landscapes, including the Malheur National Wildlife Refuge.

Best Things to Do in Ontario: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting popular attractions and hiking trails, here are some other activities and tours to enjoy in Ontario:

  • Explore Ontario’s art scene at the Ontario Art Center and Gallery.
  • Take a guided tour at Four Rivers Cultural Center & Museum and learn about the history and culture of the region.
  • Catch a show or movie at Ontario’s historic movie theater, the Nyssa Nite Rodeo Movie Theatre.
  • Visit the Harney County Migratory Bird Festival in the nearby city of Burns.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ontario

If you have a week to explore Ontario, here is an itinerary that covers the city’s top attractions:

Day Activities
1 Visit Four Rivers Cultural Center & Museum and explore the Snake River Greenbelt
2 Hike Rim Rock Trail and visit the Vale Oregon Oregon Trail Interpretive Center
3 Explore the Malheur County Historical Society and Museum and take a brewery tour at Heirloom Orchards
4 Take a day trip to the Owyhee Canyonlands
5 Visit Treasure Valley Park & Snake River Greenbelt and take a tour of the Malheur National Wildlife Refuge
6 Enjoy local cuisine at food establishments like Johnny’s Ontario Steakhouse and Lounge

Food and Drink Scene of Ontario

Ontario offers a diverse food and drink scene that can keep any foodie satisfied. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sunset Inn’s deep-fried mushrooms
  • Johnny’s Ontario Steakhouse and Lounge’s steak and seafood dishes
  • The Packing House’s burgers and sandwiches
  • Red Rooster Cafe’s breakfast options, including their famous biscuits and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Vale Brewing Company’s craft beer
  • Snake River Winery’s wine selection
  • Sleeping Indian Brewing Company’s beer options
  • Owl’s Nest Brewery’s artisanal beer collection

Where to Stay in Ontario: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ontario offers several accommodation options that cater to any budget:

  • Budget-friendly options include Econo Lodge, Quality Inn Ontario, and Super 8 by Wyndham Ontario.
  • Luxury stays can be found at Clarion Inn Ontario, Holiday Inn Express & Suites Ontario, and Best Western Inn & Suites.

What to Buy in Ontario: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for mementos to bring back home, here are some popular options:

  • Four Rivers Cultural Center & Museum’s souvenir shop offers Native American jewelry and handcrafted pottery.
  • Vale Oregon Oregon Trail Interpretive Center’s gift shop sells books, postcards, and memorabilia related to the Oregon Trail.
  • Heirloom Orchards has a farm stand that sells locally grown fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Ontario

Ontario’s cultural diversity is reflected in its museums, food, and festivals. The city celebrates its Native American, Idahoan, and Oregonian heritage through events like the Harney County Migratory Bird Festival, Vale 4th of July Rodeo, and Winter Wonderland Holiday Light Parade.

Best Time to Visit Ontario

The best time to visit Ontario is from May to October when the weather is warm and suitable for outdoor activities. The city’s mild winters also make it a popular destination year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ontario

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ontario:

Do:

  • Follow basic outdoor etiquette when hiking or camping.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.
  • Visit the Malheur National Wildlife Refuge.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Ignore safety precautions while engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ontario

Ontario is a generally safe city, but visitors are recommended to take basic safety precautions. This includes keeping track of your belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Ontario

Ontario has several healthcare facilities for any emergencies, including Saint Alphonsus Medical Center - Ontario, Brookside Landing Assisted Living and Memory Care, and Ontario Physical Therapy.

Getting to Ontario: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ontario is easily accessible via several transportation options:

Airports Near Ontario

  • Eastern Oregon Regional Airport, located 2.6 miles from the city center
  • Boise Airport, located 60.7 miles from Ontario

Bus Stations Near Ontario

  • Greyhound Bus Station is the nearest bus station, located 1.6 miles from the city center.

Other Options

  • Taxis and rideshare services are available for around-town transportation.
  • Rental cars are available at the airport and in town.

How to Get Around in Ontario

The best way to explore Ontario is by car. Several car rental options are available at the airport and in town. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for shorter trips.

Where to Go Next from Ontario: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Ontario:

  • Burns, located 80 miles from Ontario, is known for its hot springs and Harney County Migratory Bird Festival.
  • Boise, located 67 miles from Ontario, is the most populous city in Idaho and offers several cultural and outdoor attractions.
  • McCammon, located 107 miles from Ontario, is a small town in Southeastern Idaho known for its natural hot springs and scenic driving routes.

Conclusion

Ontario is a city that perfectly balances nature and culture, making it an ideal destination for any traveler. From its museums and historical sites to its scenic outdoor adventures, there’s something to capture everyone’s interest. The city’s food and drink scene is also a must-try. So why wait? Start planning your trip to Ontario today!
