Introduction to Baker City

Baker City is a charming city located in Eastern Oregon. This city has a rich history of gold-mining and cowboy culture, which can be seen in its historic buildings and museums. Baker City is also known for its outdoor recreational opportunities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and camping. If you are planning a trip to Oregon, Baker City is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Baker City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baker Heritage Museum - This museum features artifacts and exhibits that portray the history of Baker City and the surrounding region.
  • Oregon Trail Interpretive Center - This center provides visitors with a chance to learn about the pioneers who traveled through the region in the 19th century.
  • Geiser Grand Hotel - This historic hotel was built in 1889 and has been carefully restored to its original grandeur.

Natural Attractions of Baker City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Anthony Lakes Ski Resort - This ski resort offers excellent skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.
  • Hells Canyon National Recreation Area - As North America’s deepest river gorge, Hells Canyon is a stunning destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can hike, raft, or fish here.
  • Sumpter Valley Railroad - Take a ride on this scenic and historic railroad for a unique view of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baker City

Baker City is a top destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Oregon Trail Interpretive Center Trails - These trails offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape and the opportunity to experience the history of the Oregon Trail.
  • Leo Adler Memorial Parkway - This paved pathway runs through Baker City and provides a peaceful and scenic route for biking or walking.
  • Elkhorn Crest Trail - This trail traverses the Elkhorn Mountains and offers stunning views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Baker City: Activities and Tours

  • Oregon Trail Trolley Tour - Take a tour of Baker City’s historic district on a trolley, and learn about the city’s rich history.
  • Whitewater Rafting - Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Snake or Salmon River.
  • Ghost Tour - Take a spooky ghost tour of Baker City’s haunted buildings and cemeteries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baker City

Day 1: Explore downtown Baker City and visit the Baker Heritage Museum.

Day 2: Take a trolley tour of Baker City and visit the Geiser Grand Hotel.

Day 3: Spend the day hiking or biking on the Elkhorn Crest Trail.

Day 4: Visit the Oregon Trail Interpretive Center and learn about the pioneers who traveled through the region.

Day 5: Take a whitewater rafting trip on the Snake River.

Day 6: Embark on a ghost tour of Baker City’s haunted buildings and cemeteries.

Day 7: Relax at Anthony Lakes Ski Resort or Sumpter Valley Railroad.

Food and Drink Scene of Baker City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Baker City Cream Pie - This signature dessert is a must-try when visiting Baker City.
  • Huckleberry Jam - Baker City is known for its delicious huckleberry jam, which can be found at local farmers’ markets.
  • Oregon Truffle - Baker City is home to some of the country’s best truffles, so be sure to try some during your visit.

Local Beverages

  • Barley Brown’s Brew Pub - This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are perfect for beer enthusiasts.
  • Lefty’s Taphouse - This pub offers a rotating selection of local and regional beers on tap.
  • Copper Belt Winery - Sample some locally-made wines at this charming winery.

Where to Stay in Baker City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Hotels in Baker City

  • Geiser Grand Hotel - This historic hotel is located in the heart of downtown Baker City and offers luxurious rooms and exceptional service.
  • The Churchill School Bed and Breakfast - This charming B&B features lovely gardens, comfortable rooms, and a delicious breakfast.
  • Bridge Street Inn - This cozy and affordable inn is located in a historic building and is within walking distance to downtown.

Best Hostels in Baker City

  • Baker City Hostel - This hostel is located just minutes from downtown Baker City and offers clean and comfortable accommodations at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Baker City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam - As mentioned above, Baker City is known for its delicious huckleberry jam.
  • Oregon Truffles - Bring home some of the best truffles in the country as a memorable souvenir.
  • Local Crafts - Browse the farmers’ markets and local shops to find unique crafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Baker City

Baker City is known for its cowboy culture and friendly locals. When visiting, be sure to respect the local customs and traditions, such as dressing in casual, comfortable clothing and being respectful to others.

Best Time to Visit Baker City

The best time to visit Baker City is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the outdoor recreational opportunities are plentiful. However, winter visitors can enjoy skiing and other winter sports at Anthony Lakes Ski Resort.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baker City

Do:

  • Do visit the Oregon Trail Interpretive Center to learn about the pioneers who traveled through the region.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor recreational opportunities.

  • Do try some of the local food and drink, such as Baker City Cream Pie and Oregon Truffles.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t ignore the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baker City

Baker City is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should always take appropriate safety precautions, such as locking their car doors and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Baker City

Baker City has a hospital and several clinics that can provide medical care if needed. Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications with them.

Getting to Baker City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baker City

The nearest commercial airport to Baker City is Boise Airport, located approximately 2.5 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Baker City

Greyhound operates a bus station in neighboring La Grande, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Baker City by car or via Amtrak’s Empire Builder route, which stops in nearby Spokane, Washington.

How to Get Around in Baker City

Baker City is a walkable city, and many of the downtown attractions are within easy walking distance. Visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car at the Boise Airport or in Baker City.

Where to Go Next from Baker City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wallowa Lake - This stunning lake is located approximately two hours away from Baker City and offers boating, fishing, and hiking opportunities.
  • Crater Lake National Park - This national park, located approximately five hours away, features the deepest lake in the United States and some of the most stunning views in Oregon.
  • Pendleton, Oregon - This charming city is located approximately one hour away from Baker City and is known for its rodeo and woolen mill.

Conclusion

Baker City is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience a combination of history, culture, and outdoor recreational opportunities. From hiking and biking to museums and historical sites, there is something for everyone in this charming Oregon town.
