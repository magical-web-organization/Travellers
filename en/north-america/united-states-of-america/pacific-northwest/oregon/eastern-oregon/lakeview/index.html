Introduction to Lakeview

Located in Eastern Oregon, Lakeview is a small town that offers a variety of attractions for tourists looking for outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. Surrounded by scenic landscapes and with ample opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities, Lakeview is perfect for nature lovers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Lakeview.

List of Places to Visit in Lakeview

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lake County Museum: Discover the history of Lakeview and the surrounding areas at this local museum that showcases the region’s cultural heritage.
  • Old Perpetual: Visit the world’s oldest continually erupting hot spring located in Lake County and learn about its geological significance.
  • Fort Rock State Natural Area: Explore this ancient volcanic rock formation and learn about the history of the indigenous people who once lived in the area.

Natural Attractions of Lakeview: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fremont-Winema National Forest: Enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and camping in this beautiful forest that covers more than 2 million acres of land.
  • Crater Lake National Park: Admire the breathtaking views of the deepest lake in North America and explore the park’s hiking trails and scenic drives.
  • Hart Mountain National Antelope Refuge: Spot antelopes and other wildlife in their natural habitat, and enjoy scenic views of the mountains and prairies.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakeview

  • Warner Summit Trail: A challenging hike that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, including lake views and mountain ranges.
  • Petroglyph Lake Trail: A moderate hike that leads to a beautiful lake with ancient petroglyphs near its shore.
  • Oregon Outback Scenic Bikeway: Explore the scenic beauty of Eastern Oregon on this 171-mile bike route that takes you through Lakeview and other towns in the region.

Best Things to Do in Lakeview: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Springs Tour: Experience the relaxing and therapeutic benefits of hot springs by visiting some of the natural hot springs in the area.
  • Wildlife Watching Tour: Join a guided tour to spot local wildlife, including pronghorns, coyotes, and birds of prey.
  • Star Gazing Tour: Enjoy the clear night skies of Lakeview and stargaze with an expert guide who will show you the constellations and planets visible from the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakeview

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Bread: A traditional Native American dish made of fried dough and usually served with honey or jam.
  • Huckleberry Jam: A sweet and tangy spread made from local huckleberries that can be enjoyed on bread or biscuits.
  • **Lamb: **A delicious and flavorful meat that is commonly raised in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Crater Lake Spirits: A family-owned distillery that produces high-quality gin and vodka using local ingredients.
  • Coaltrain Coffee: A locally roasted coffee that offers a range of blends and single-origin options.

Where to Stay in Lakeview: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lakeview Lodge Motel: A comfortable motel with an outdoor swimming pool, free parking, and easy access to local attractions.
  • Interstate 8 Motel: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a convenient location near the town center.
  • Juniper Springs Ranch Bed and Breakfast: A charming and cozy B&B that offers a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere for guests.

What to Buy in Lakeview: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Huckleberry Jam or Syrup: Bring home a jar of locally made huckleberry jam or syrup as a tasty souvenir of your trip to Lakeview.
  • Handmade Native American Crafts: Shop for handmade baskets, pottery, and other crafts made by local Native American artists.
  • Gemstones: Lakeview is known for its gemstones such as sunstones, opals, and agates, and you can buy them as jewelry or raw stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakeview

Lakeview has a rich cultural heritage that honors the indigenous people who once lived in the region. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs, especially when visiting places such as Old Perpetual and Fort Rock State Natural Area.

Best Time to Visit Lakeview

The best time to visit Lakeview is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the fall and spring offer lower prices and fewer crowds, making it a great time to visit if you prefer a quieter experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakeview

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife by following leave no trace principles when hiking or camping.
  • Support local businesses by shopping for locally made products and dining at locally owned restaurants.
  • Take time to learn about the history and cultural heritage of Lakeview.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activities that harm the environment, including littering or disturbing wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Use fireworks or campfires during high-risk fire seasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakeview

Lakeview is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions such as securing their valuables and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Lakeview

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude and dry climate of Lakeview, which can cause altitude sickness and dehydration. It’s important to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Lakeview: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakeview

The nearest airports to Lakeview are:

  • Klamath Falls Airport (KLB): 90 miles away, offers domestic flights via United and Alaska Airlines.
  • Rogue Valley International-Medford Airport (MFR): 141 miles away, offers domestic and international flights via Delta, American, and United Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Lakeview

Greyhound operates a bus service that stops in Lakeview.

Other Options

It’s also possible to reach Lakeview by car or RV, and there are several rental car companies in the area.

How to Get Around in Lakeview

The best way to get around Lakeview is by car, and there are several car rental companies in the area. Biking is also an option for short distances, and some hotels offer shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Lakeview: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bend, Oregon: A vibrant city known for its outdoor recreation, craft beer, and scenic beauty located 195 miles away.
  • Crater Lake National Park: A must-visit destination located just 68 miles from Lakeview.
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park, California: Visit this stunning park located just 150 miles south of Lakeview and explore its geothermal features and scenic trails.

Conclusion

Lakeview is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. With its beautiful landscapes, outdoor activities, and rich cultural heritage, Lakeview is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves nature and adventure. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Lakeview.
