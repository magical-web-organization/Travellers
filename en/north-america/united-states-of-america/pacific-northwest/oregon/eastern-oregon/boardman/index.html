Introduction to Boardman

Located in Eastern Oregon, Boardman is a small town known for its picturesque natural beauty and abundant outdoor recreation opportunities. Situated in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Boardman is a popular destination for those seeking a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Boardman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Morrow County Museum: This museum showcases the history of Morrow County, with exhibits featuring artifacts from the region’s pioneer days through to the present.
  • Columbia River Exhibition of History, Science, and Technology: This center provides interactive exhibits on the geology, history, and technology of the Columbia River Basin.
  • The SAGE Center: This science and agriculture center features interesting exhibits that focus on the technological advancements in agriculture.

Natural Attractions of Boardman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cottonwood Canyon State Park: This park boasts stunning views of the John Day River and its surrounding canyons, making it an excellent location for hiking, biking, and boating.
  • Boardman Tree Farm: This former tree farm has been transformed into a nature reserve. Boardman Tree Farm provides visitors with hiking trails and breathtaking views of the Columbia River Gorge.
  • Umatilla National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge spans across 25,000 acres, with a focus on providing habitats for migratory birds and other wildlife. Visitors can hike trails, birdwatch, or catch fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boardman

  • Cottonwood Canyon State Park Trail: A 7.2-mile trail featuring spectacular views of the state park.
  • Boardman Tree Farm Trail: Offering three different trails, visitors can hike through the nature reserve with views of the Columbia River and surrounding scenery.
  • The Riverfront Trail: This trail is a paved recreational trail that passes through a park and alongside the Columbia River.

Best Things to Do in Boardman: Activities and Tours

  • Columbia River Gorge Boat Tours: A perfect option for visitors wanting to experience the beauty of Columbia River Gorge from the water.
  • Jet Boat Tours of the Snake River: Visitors can experience the adventure of a jet boat ride while enjoying views of the wildlife and the natural beauty of the Snake River.
  • The Wildhorse Resort and Casino: This casino hotel resort features slot machines, table games, golf course, and concerts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boardman

Food and Drink Scene of Boardman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lava Bread: A kind of bread with tangy and salty flavor, which is perfect with soups.
  • Mashed Potato Bowls: Comfort food at its finest, topped with savory meats, cheese, and other ingredients.
  • Huckleberry Pie: A sweet dessert made with local berries that grow in abundance during the summer months.

Local Beverages

  • Pinot Noir: A light-bodied red wine that pairs well with the region’s cuisine and scenic views.
  • Cider: Local cider makers are known for their delicious and refreshing alcoholic and non-alcoholic ciders, often made with local fruits.

Where to Stay in Boardman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • River Lodge and Grill: A riverfront hotel offering modern amenities and a prime location.
  • Riverfront Motel: This motel features affordable rooms with an outstanding view of the Columbia River.
  • Boardman Motel: An economical option, the Boardman Motel features clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Boardman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Huckleberry Jam: This delicious spread is made with locally-sourced huckleberries.
  • Handmade Crafts: Boardman has a thriving arts and crafts community, and you can find a wide range of locally-made goods from pottery to woodwork.
  • Local Wines and Ciders: Don’t forget to bring back a bottle of the region’s best wines or ciders.

Local Culture and Customs of Boardman

Boardman is a region with deep historical and cultural ties to the land. Visitors are encouraged to show respect for the land and its people.

Best Time to Visit Boardman

The best time to visit Boardman is from April to November when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The winter months can be harsh, with cold temperatures and heavy snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boardman

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Take advantage of all the outdoor recreation opportunities in the area.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages while enjoying the views.

Don’t:

  • Act insensitive towards the Native American culture and local residents.
  • Go hiking or exploring without proper gear and preparation.
  • Litter in the natural spaces or damage the ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boardman

Boardman is a safe area to visit. However, visitors are advised to take usual precautions while outdoors in the area. Water-related activities can have risks, so proper safety measures should be taken if you’re new to them.

Health in Boardman

Visitors are advised to be aware of the risks of exposure to smoke and dust. Waterborne illnesses from the area’s lakes, rivers, and streams can also be a concern. Therefore, always take adequate precautions and consult with the local authorities for your safety.

Getting to Boardman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Boardman

  • Tri-Cities Airport: Located about 62 miles from Boardman, offering both international and domestic flights.
  • Eastern Oregon Regional Airport: Located about 100 miles from Boardman, serving domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Boardman

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in the city of Pendleton, about 35 miles from Boardman.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Boardman from the nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Boardman

Most visitors will require a car to get around in Boardman, as the area is vast and remote. Other options include chartering a taxi or ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Boardman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pendleton: Located just 35 miles away, Pendleton is home to a historic downtown, unique shops and restaurants, and the famous Pendleton Round-Up.
  • Columbia River Gorge: An area known for hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning scenic drives.
  • Boise: The state capital of Idaho, featuring a thriving arts and culture scene, outdoor recreation opportunities, and trendy bars and restaurants.

Conclusion

Boardman is a beautiful and remote area that has a lot to offer its visitors. From outdoor recreation to cultural attractions and delectable cuisine, the area will not disappoint. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Boardman with ease, making the most of your time in this unique and stunning region.
