Introduction to Pendleton

Located in Eastern Oregon, Pendleton is a city steeped in rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Its location at the foot of the Blue Mountains adds to its charm, making it a popular tourist destination in the Pacific Northwest. Pendleton is known for its world-famous Pendleton Woolen Mills, the Pendleton Round-Up rodeo, and its Native American heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Pendleton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pendleton is home to numerous museums and historical sites that showcase the city’s fascinating past. Some of the must-visit museums and historical sites include:

  • Tamástslikt Cultural Institute: This museum offers an in-depth look at the history, culture, and traditions of the Confederated Tribes of the Umatilla Indian Reservation.
  • Pendleton Underground Tours: Join a guided tour that will take you through the tunnels beneath the city that were once used for illegal activities such as gambling and bootlegging.
  • Pendleton Woolen Mills: A visit to Pendleton is not complete without stopping by the world-famous Pendleton Woolen Mills. Learn about the history of the mill and its role in making Pendleton blankets and other wool products over the years.

Natural Attractions of Pendleton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pendleton is surrounded by natural beauty, making it an ideal outdoor adventure destination. Some of the top natural attractions to visit include:

  • Pendleton River Parkway: This scenic 2.5-mile trail runs along the Umatilla River and offers breathtaking views of the Blue Mountains.
  • McNary Wildlife Nature Area: Home to more than 200 species of birds and other wildlife, this 3,900-acre nature area is perfect for hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.
  • Emigrant Springs State Heritage Area: This state park is located just a few miles outside of Pendleton and offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning mountain views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pendleton

Pendleton is surrounded by beautiful hiking and biking trails, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the must-visit hiking and biking routes include:

  • Mount Emily Recreation Area: This recreation area contains more than 30 miles of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Pendleton River Parkway: This asphalt trail is perfect for biking and walking, and offers scenic views of the Umatilla River.
  • Emigrant Springs State Heritage Area: This state park has several nature trails that provide hikers with stunning views of the surrounding Blue Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Pendleton: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun things to do in Pendleton. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Pendleton Round-Up: Known as the “World’s Most Famous Rodeo,” the Pendleton Round-Up takes place every September and showcases some of the best rodeo riders in the world.
  • Helicopter Tours: Get a bird’s eye view of Pendleton and its surrounding areas with a helicopter tour.
  • Golfing: Pendleton is home to a few world-class golf courses, including the Pendleton Country Club and the Wildhorse Golf Course.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pendleton

Day 1-2: Visit museums and historical sites such as the Tamástslikt Cultural Institute and Pendleton Underground Tours.

Day 3-4: Explore Pendleton’s natural beauty by hiking or biking Mount Emily Recreation Area, Pendleton River Parkway, and Emigrant Springs State Heritage Area.

Day 5-6: Participate in fun activities such as the Pendleton Round-Up, helicopter tours, and golfing.

Day 7: Spend the day exploring downtown Pendleton, enjoying the local culture, and shopping for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Pendleton

Must-Try Dishes

Pendleton’s food scene is a mix of old and new, with many restaurants serving up delicious comfort food and local specialties. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Pendleton Burger: This thick and juicy burger is a local favorite and can be found on many restaurant menus in town.
  • Huckleberry Pie: Made with locally grown huckleberries, this pie is a must-try for anyone visiting Pendleton.
  • Indian Fry Bread: This traditional Native American dish is a staple in Pendleton and can be found at the Tamástslikt Cultural Institute.

Local Beverages

The Pacific Northwest is known for its craft beer scene, and Pendleton is no exception. Some of the best local beverages to try include:

  • Proletariat Red Ale: This locally brewed ale is made with Northwest hops and has a deep, rich flavor.
  • Chief Joseph IPA: Named after the famous Nez Perce leader, this IPA is brewed with local grains and hops and has a refreshing, hoppy taste.
  • Pendleton Whisky: This smooth and mellow whisky is made using water from Pendleton’s nearby spring-fed wells.

Where to Stay in Pendleton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pendleton offers a wide variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Oxford Suites Pendleton: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Pendleton and offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • Red Lion Hotel Pendleton: This hotel is situated on the banks of the Umatilla River and offers a relaxing outdoor pool, an on-site restaurant, and comfortable rooms.
  • Pendleton House Airbnb: This guesthouse offers a cozy and homey feel, with comfortable beds, a full kitchen, and a garden patio with mountain views.

What to Buy in Pendleton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pendleton is known for its high-quality wool products, including blankets, clothing, and accessories. Some other locally-made products to bring home include:

  • Huckleberry Jam: Made with local huckleberries, this jam is a delicious and unique souvenir to bring home.
  • Native American Jewelry: Pendleton is home to several Native American-owned shops that sell beautiful and unique handmade jewelry.
  • Pendleton Whisky: Take home a bottle of Pendleton’s signature whisky as a gift for friends or family.

Local Culture and Customs of Pendleton

Pendleton is known for its rich Native American heritage and Western cowboy culture. Locals are proud of their heritage and are often happy to share their traditions with visitors. It is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as the Nez Perce Powwow and the Pendleton Round-Up.

Best Time to Visit Pendleton

The best time to visit Pendleton is during the summer months when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The Pendleton Round-Up takes place in September and is a must-see event for anyone visiting the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pendleton

Do:

  • Do visit the Tamástslikt Cultural Institute to learn about Native American history and traditions.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including the Pendleton Burger and huckleberry pie.
  • Do attend the Pendleton Round-Up, the city’s most famous event.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the importance of respecting local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing if visiting in the winter months.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on things to see and do in the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pendleton

Pendleton is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it is still important to take precautions while traveling, such as keeping valuables safely stored and avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Pendleton

Pendleton has a few medical facilities in town, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. It is always a good idea to have travel insurance when visiting a new destination.

Getting to Pendleton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pendleton

The nearest airport to Pendleton is the Eastern Oregon Regional Airport (PDT), which is about 3 miles away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Pendleton

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Pendleton, making it convenient for those traveling by bus.

Other Options

Pendleton is also accessible by car, with several major highways running through the city. For those traveling from Portland, it is about a 3-hour drive east on Interstate 84.

How to Get Around in Pendleton

Pendleton is a relatively small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, for those who prefer other modes of transportation, there is a local bus service, taxis, and rental cars available.

Where to Go Next from Pendleton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pendleton is located in Eastern Oregon, making it an ideal base for exploring the surrounding areas. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Hells Canyon: Located just over an hour’s drive from Pendleton, Hells Canyon is known for its rugged scenery and outdoor adventures.
  • La Grande: This charming town is located about an hour’s drive from Pendleton and is known for its historic downtown and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Baker City: This historic town is located about 1.5 hours’ drive from Pendleton and offers visitors a glimpse into Oregon’s mining past.

Conclusion

Pendleton, Oregon offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation opportunities. Whether you’re looking to learn about Native American traditions, enjoy the great outdoors, or simply relax and take in the stunning scenery, Pendleton has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit today and experience the best of Eastern Oregon.
