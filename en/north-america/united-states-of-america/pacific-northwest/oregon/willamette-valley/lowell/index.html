Introduction to Lowell

Lowell is a charming town located in Willamette Valley, Oregon. This small town of just over 1,000 residents is nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Lowell is known for its natural beauty, unique culture, and friendly locals. Visitors to Lowell can enjoy exploring the town’s rich history, indulging in local cuisine, and participating in outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and canoeing.

List of Places to Visit in Lowell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lowell Covered Bridge: Originally built in 1945, this beautiful covered bridge is the only one of its kind in Lane County. Visitors can enjoy views of the Middle Fork of the Willamette River as they explore this unique attraction.
  • Blackberry Jam Festival: This annual festival celebrates the town’s history and culture. Visitors can enjoy live music, arts and crafts, and of course, plenty of delicious blackberry jam.
  • Lowell Grange: This community center provides a glimpse into the history of the area. Visitors can attend events and meetings, or just take a tour and learn about the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Lowell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dexter Lake: This beautiful lake is the perfect spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the water and surrounding mountains.
  • Fall Creek Reservoir: Another popular spot for outdoor activities, including swimming, fishing, and hiking. Visitors can also enjoy a scenic drive along the reservoir’s shoreline.
  • Lowell State Recreation Site: This park provides access to the Middle Fork of the Willamette River, where visitors can enjoy fishing, canoeing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lowell

  • South Shore Trail: This easy loop trail provides stunning views of Dexter Lake. The trail is about three miles long and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Moon Point Trail: This moderate trail offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and can be completed in about three hours.
  • Lowell Covered Bridge Bike Tour: This scenic bike ride takes visitors along country roads and provides opportunities to explore the Lowell Covered Bridge and other local attractions.

Best Things to Do in Lowell: Activities and Tours

  • Willamette Valley Wine Tasting Tour: Visitors can explore the nearby Willamette Valley wine country and sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Fishing and Canoeing Tour: Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy a guided fishing or canoeing tour of the Middle Fork of the Willamette River.
  • Live Music at the Wildwood Hotel: Visitors can enjoy live music and a delicious meal at the Wildwood Hotel, a historic property located in nearby Pleasant Hill.

Food and Drink Scene of Lowell

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blackberry Pie: This delicious dessert is a local favorite and features fresh, sweet blackberries baked into a flaky crust.
  • Grilled Salmon: With numerous rivers and lakes in the area, fresh salmon is a popular local dish.
  • Huckleberry Jam: Another local favorite, huckleberry jam is a must-try for visitors looking for a taste of the Pacific Northwest.

Local Beverages

  • Willamette Valley Wines: The Willamette Valley is known for its excellent wines, particularly Pinot Noir. Visitors can sample a variety of wines at local wineries.
  • Rogue Ales and Spirits: This popular brewery and distillery offers a variety of handcrafted beers and spirits, including their famous Dead Guy Ale.

Where to Stay in Lowell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wildwood Hotel: This historic hotel is located in Pleasant Hill, just a short drive from Lowell. Rooms are spacious and comfortable and feature modern amenities.
  • Lowell Anchor Inn: This cozy inn offers a charming, rustic atmosphere and is located just steps from the Lowell Covered Bridge.
  • Camping: There are several campgrounds in the area, including the Lowell State Recreation Site.

What to Buy in Lowell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Blackberry Jam: A jar of local blackberry jam makes for a great souvenir and is a tasty reminder of your trip to Lowell.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Several local artisans create beautiful pottery inspired by the area’s natural beauty.
  • Huckleberry Products: Visitors can find a variety of huckleberry products, including jam, candy, and syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of Lowell

Lowell is a friendly, welcoming community with a strong sense of pride in its history and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of the local environment and follow Leave No Trace principles when exploring the area. Locals are friendly and happy to chat with visitors about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Lowell

The best time to visit Lowell is from May to October when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is a particularly beautiful time to visit, as the leaves on the surrounding trees change colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lowell

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the area’s natural beauty.
  • Do try the local blackberry jam and other Pacific Northwest delicacies.
  • Do be respectful of the local environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Don’t ignore the area’s history and culture.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate gear for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lowell

Lowell is a safe community, but visitors should take appropriate precautions when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and canoeing. It’s important to be aware of potential hazards and to follow safety guidelines.

Health in Lowell

Medical facilities are located in nearby Eugene, just a short drive from Lowell. It’s important to bring any necessary medications and to be prepared for outdoor activities.

Getting to Lowell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lowell

The nearest major airport is the Eugene Airport, which is located about 30 miles from Lowell. Another option is the Portland International Airport, which is about 125 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Lowell

There is no bus station in Lowell, but visitors can take a bus to nearby Eugene and then rent a car or take a taxi to Lowell.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Lowell or take a train to nearby Eugene and then rent a car or take a taxi.

How to Get Around in Lowell

The best way to get around Lowell is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car in Eugene and drive to Lowell, or rent a bike and explore the area’s numerous bike trails.

Where to Go Next from Lowell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Eugene: Just a short drive away, Eugene is a vibrant city known for its arts scene, outdoor recreation opportunities, and excellent dining.
  • Cottage Grove: This charming town is known for its historic covered bridges and beautiful outdoor scenery.
  • Willamette National Forest: This nearby national forest offers endless opportunities for hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Lowell is a small town with big charm and plenty of natural beauty. Whether visitors are looking to explore local history, participate in outdoor adventures, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery, there is something for everyone in this Pacific Northwest gem.
