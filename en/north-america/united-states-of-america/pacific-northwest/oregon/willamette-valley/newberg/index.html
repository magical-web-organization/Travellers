Introduction to Newberg

Nestled in the heart of Oregon’s Willamette Valley, Newberg is a charming city known for its lush landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Home to world-class wineries, museums, and natural wonders, Newberg offers a plethora of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Newberg.

List of Places to Visit in Newberg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Newberg is a city steeped in history and has many fascinating cultural attractions to visit. A few must-sees include:

  • The Hoover-Minthorn House Museum: Home to President Herbert Hoover’s parents, this museum offers a glimpse into the early life of the 31st U.S. president.
  • Chehalem Cultural Center: This beautiful facility is home to art exhibitions, educational classes, and cultural events.
  • Newberg Heritage Center: This museum showcases Newberg’s rich history, including its founder’s life, the implementation of women’s rights, and its works in progress toward growth and prosperity.

Natural Attractions of Newberg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers and adventure seekers, Newberg offers various outdoor activities and scenic views perfect for fun and relaxation. Some top-rated options include:

  • Champoeg State Heritage Area: A popular park that offers visitors a picturesque river, ancient trees, and an extensive network of hiking and biking trails.
  • Willamette River: Offering a variety of adventures, from fishing, boating, and kayaking for water enthusiasts to strolls on the riverside for those who want to relax and take in the scenic views.
  • Rogers Landing County Park: A perfect spot for a picnic, beach walk, or birdwatching, which can provide visitors with a serene getaway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newberg

One of the best ways to explore the beauty of Newberg is through its network of hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes include:

  • Chehalem Ridge Trail: A 3.3-mile hike that offers outstanding views of the Willamette Valley and the Oregon Coast Range.
  • Bald Peak State Park: A beautiful park and recreational area that includes a challenging hike to the summit of Bald Peak for a 360-degree panoramic view of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Champoeg State Heritage Area Trail System: The perfect route for hikers and bikers, with over 15 miles of trails. A loop trail is connecting 13 historical landmarks of the area.

Best Things to Do in Newberg: Activities and Tours

Newberg offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit all interests. Here are a few highlights:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Newberg is known for its world-class vineyards and wineries. Visitors can enjoy tasting rooms and serene vineyard settings while learning about the winemaking process.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the beauty of Oregon from above with a hot air balloon ride. Admire the stunning scenery while floating gently in the sky.
  • Horseback Riding Tours: Enjoy a unique perspective of Newberg’s beautiful landscapes on a horseback riding tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newberg

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to The Hoover-Minthorn House Museum and Chehalem Cultural Center. Day 2: Explore Champoeg State Heritage Area and enjoy a picnic. Day 3: Go on a hot air balloon ride and experience Newberg from above. Day 4: Take a horseback riding tour, followed by wine tasting in any of the local vineyards. Day 5: Hike the Chehalem Ridge Trail and experience the scenic views. Day 6: Head to Willamette River for a kayak tour or just calm your mind over a river stroll. Day 7: Wander around a Sunday farmer’s market and check out local produce and artisan products.

Food and Drink Scene of Newberg

Must-Try Dishes

Newberg’s local food scene boasts farm-to-table cuisine that is sure to please everyone’s palate. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Hazelnut-Crusted Salmon: This dish is a local specialty, with fresh-caught salmon coated in a crust made from handpicked local hazelnuts.
  • Marionberry Pie: This Oregon classic features the state’s signature berry in a delicious flaky crust.
  • Indian Fry Bread Tacos: A food truck delicacy, fry bread is topped with savory beef, cheese, and fresh vegetable toppings.

Local Beverages

Oregon is known for its craft beer and wine, and Newberg is no exception. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Pinot Noir: Newberg is home to some of the best Pinot Noir wine in the world.
  • Microbrews: There are many local microbreweries that can quench your thirst for craft beer.
  • Kombucha: This fermented tea beverage is a healthy and tasty alternative to sugary soda.

Where to Stay in Newberg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newberg has many options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some highlights:

  • The Allison Inn & Spa: A five-star hotel that offers luxurious amenities, stunning views, and world-class service.
  • DreamGiver’s Inn: A serene bed and breakfast that offers a relaxing haven with all the comforts of home.
  • Chehalem Ridge Bed and Breakfast: A cozy and charming bed and breakfast with breathtaking views of the Willamette Valley.

What to Buy in Newberg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Newberg has many unique and locally made products that make for perfect souvenirs. Here are some options:

  • Hazelnuts: Oregon is known for its high-quality, locally grown hazelnuts.
  • Pinot Noir: Bring home a bottle of Newberg’s famous Pinot Noir wine.
  • Handmade Crafts: Visit the local artisan markets for handmade jewelry, pottery, and art.

Local Culture and Customs of Newberg

Newberg is a culturally diverse city that celebrates its history and values its residents. Visitors should respect local customs and follow the culture while enjoying the city’s many attractions.

Best Time to Visit Newberg

The best time to visit Newberg is during the summer, from June to September when temperatures are mild, and the natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newberg

Do:

  • Explore the vineyards and wineries.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take a balloon ride over the scenic landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces.
  • Don’t drive recklessly or under the influence of drugs and alcohol.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newberg

Newberg is a safe city with a low crime rate. Visitors should take basic precautions, such as locking their vehicles and securing their belongings, to ensure safety.

Health in Newberg

Newberg has excellent medical facilities with many doctors and hospitals available to visitors.

Getting to Newberg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newberg

  • Portland International Airport: A major airport located about 40 minutes drive from Newberg.

Bus Stations Near Newberg

  • Greyhound Station: Located in Portland, about 40 minutes drive from Newberg.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available and preferred for sightseeing in Oregon.

How to Get Around in Newberg

Driving a car would be the best way to explore Newberg and the surrounding areas. Visitors can also rent bikes from local shops and explore the town or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Newberg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newberg is close to many nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Portland: A vibrant and eclectic city just 40 minutes away from Newberg.
  • Willamette Valley: Experience the beautiful landscapes of the Willamette Valley in a hot air balloon ride or visit Mulnomah Falls, offering a scenic drive.

Conclusion

Newberg is a beautiful and diverse city that offers a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors. With world-class wineries, scenic landscapes, and rich cultural history, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Oregon. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your Newberg experience.
