Introduction to Aurora

Nestled in the Willamette Valley, Aurora is a charming small town in Oregon’s wine country. This historic town is filled with picturesque farms, stunning vineyards, and unique antique shops. It’s perfect for visitors who are looking for a peaceful escape into nature, surrounded by beautiful scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Aurora

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Old Aurora Colony Museum

The Old Aurora Colony Museum is a top-notch historic museum that offers visitors an insight into life during the 19th century. The museum has several buildings that are well-preserved, and visitors can take guided tours to learn about the Aurora Colony’s storied past. This museum is a must-visit for history buffs.

2. Aurora Cultural Center

The Aurora Cultural Center, a former church, is an artistic haven that offers visitors a chance to interact with local artists and artisans. This center is entirely run by volunteers and serves as a venue for local theater productions, concerts, and more.

Natural Attractions of Aurora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Aurora State Park

Aurora State Park is a beautiful spot for outdoor enthusiasts looking for a peaceful place to spend a day out. It encompasses the 144-acre Upper and Lower Dells of the Pudding River. Visitors can enjoy several activities here, from hiking and fishing to picnicking.

2. Champoeg State Heritage Area

The Champoeg State Heritage Area is a well-manicured park and is perfect for families. The park provides beautiful views of the Willamette River, and visitors can take part in several activities, such as hiking, cycling, and boating. Visitors can also visit the park during the summer months for outdoor concerts and theater productions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aurora

Aurora Colony Trail

The Aurora Colony Trail is a 4-mile path that connects the historic town of Aurora to the city of Salem. This trail provides a beautiful view of Oregon’s stunning countryside and is popular with families, bikers, and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Aurora: Activities and Tours

1. Aurora Antique District

Aurora’s Antique District is well-known for its unique, vintage shops that offer visitors a stroll through the past. Visitors can find everything from vintage jewelry to antique furniture.

2. Abiqua Falls

Located close by, Abiqua Falls is a breathtaking waterfall that is well worth the trip. The falls are only accessible by foot, and visitors have to hike into the forest for about a mile.

Food and Drink Scene of Aurora

Must-Try Dishes

1. Marionberry Pie

Marionberry Pie is a popular dessert in Aurora, and visitors can try a slice at the Aurora Mills Cafe or at The Fields Restaurant.

2. Oregon Wine

Oregon wines are famous for their crisp flavors and distinct taste. Aurora has several wine tasting rooms where visitors can sample some of the best wines in the region.

Local Beverages

1. Coffee

Aurora has many coffee shops where visitors can grab a cup of coffee. The coffee at the Lone Pine Coffee Roasters is unique and a must-try for coffee lovers.

2. Local Brews

Aurora has a budding craft beer scene, and visitors can try some of the best local brews at the Aurora Colony Pub or Cider House.

Where to Stay in Aurora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. The DreamGiver’s Inn

The DreamGiver’s Inn, located in the heart of Aurora, offers visitors unique accommodation, such as cottages and suites with private entrances.

2. Blue Heron French Cheese Company

The Blue Heron French Cheese Company offers visitors a chance to stay in their deluxe treehouse that boasts stunning views of Aurora’s farms and vineyards.

What to Buy in Aurora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy a range of local and unique souvenirs from Aurora’s antique stores, such as vintage clothing, handmade jewelry, and local art.

Local Culture and Customs of Aurora

Aurora has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience local customs by attending the town’s annual events, such as the Aurora Colony Days and the Oregon Lavender Festival.

Best Time to Visit Aurora

The best time to visit Aurora is during the summer months when the weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aurora

Do:

  • Take time to explore Aurora’s antique district.
  • Do try Marionberry pie and Oregon wines.
  • Do visit Champoeg State Heritage Area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on Abiqua Falls.
  • Don’t forget to explore Aurora’s farms and vineyards.
  • Don’t underestimate the importance of comfortable footwear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aurora

Aurora is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or exploring the town’s natural attractions.

Health in Aurora

Visitors should carry a basic first aid kit when hiking in Aurora, and it’s essential to stay hydrated.

Getting to Aurora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aurora

The closest airport to Aurora is Portland International Airport, which is about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Aurora

The Salem Greyhound station is the closest station to Aurora, located about 15 miles away.

How to Get Around in Aurora

Walking or cycling is the best way to get around Aurora, but visitors can also rent a car if they plan to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Aurora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mount Hood

Mount Hood is a stunning mountain that is located about two hours away from Aurora. Visitors can embark on several adventures, such as skiing, hiking, and snowboarding.

Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is a well-known seaside town that is located about an hour and a half away from Aurora. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views, build sandcastles, and explore tide pools.

Conclusion

Aurora is an excellent travel destination for visitors who are looking for a peaceful escape into nature. This charming town has something to offer for everyone, from historic museums and vineyards to outdoor adventures and antique shops. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery, rich culture, and local cuisine, making Aurora a must-visit destination in Oregon’s Wine Country.
