Introduction to Salem

Salem, the capital of Oregon, is a scenic city located in the Willamette Valley of the Pacific Northwest region in the United States of America. Rich in history and culture, Salem is a popular destination for travelers seeking a mix of urban and natural experiences. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Salem, Oregon.

List of Places to Visit in Salem

There are plenty of attractions to explore in Salem, from culturally important museums to natural outdoor adventures. Below are some of the best places to visit in Salem:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Oregon State Capitol - A magnificent building located on a hill in downtown Salem, the Oregon State Capitol showcases the imposing beauty of such an important institution.
  • The Willamette Heritage Center - A living history museum that showcases the agricultural and industrial heritage of the area.
  • The Hallie Ford Museum of Art - A popular art museum on the campus of Willamette University.
  • Bush House Museum - A historic mansion that offers a glimpse into the lives of Salem’s early history.
  • Mark O. Hatfield Library - A beautiful library at Willamette University that was designed by the renowned Finnish architect, Alvar Aalto.

Natural Attractions of Salem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silver Falls State Park - A beautiful park located only a few minutes from downtown Salem, with cascading waterfalls and miles of hiking trails.
  • The Riverfront Park - A popular city park with scenic views of the Willamette River, a playground, and picnic areas.
  • Minto-Brown Island Park - A nature preserve with over 1,200 acres of wetlands, woodlands, and farmland. It offers miles of trails that are perfect for hiking and bird watching.
  • Biking or Walking on the Salem Riverfront - Enjoy beautiful views while you walk or bike along the Willamette River.
  • Salem Saturday Market - A famous market that sells fresh and local produce, arts and crafts and is perfect for Sunday morning.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salem

Whether you prefer hiking or biking, Salem has a variety of trails waiting to be explored. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Minto-Brown Island Park Trail - A scenic trail that runs through the park’s wetlands, woodlands, and farmland.
  • Salem Riverfront Park Trail - A paved trail that runs for more than two miles along the Willamette River.
  • Croisan Creek Trail - A 2.3-mile trail that offers spectacular views of the city and the Willamette Valley.

Best Things to Do in Salem: Activities and Tours

From exploring historic sites to shopping, Salem offers a range of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Salem include:

  • Visit vineyards and wineries - Salem is home to several vineyards and wineries that are open to the public, including the Willamette Valley Vineyards and the Salem Wine Trail.
  • Discover Salem’s history - Explore the Bush House Museum, Willamette Heritage Center, and the historic Oregon State Capitol.
  • Shop at the Salem Saturday Market - Indulge in local crafts, arts and produce at Salem’s most popular market.
  • Join a ghost tour - Learn about the city’s infamous past with one of Salem’s famous ghost tours.
  • Explore the local parks - Take a leisurely stroll or have a picnic at the Riverfront Park, Minto-Brown Island Park or Bush’s Pasture Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salem

If you’re considering a week-long trip to Salem, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Oregon State Capitol in the morning and explore the Willamette Heritage Center in the afternoon
2 Explore the Bush House Museum and spend time in Riverfront Park
3 Spend the day in Silver Falls State Park
4 Visit local vineyards and wineries
5 Take a ghost tour in the evening
6 Spend the day at Minto-Brown Island Park or Bush’s Pasture Park
7 Go shopping at the Salem Saturday Market or visit the Hallie Ford Museum of Art

Food and Drink Scene of Salem

Salem has a thriving food and drink scene, with a variety of local cuisine and drinks to enjoy. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marionberry Pie - A dessert made of local Oregon marionberries and served in several cafes and restaurants throughout Salem.
  • Miso Ramen - A delicious Japanese soup that can be found at several restaurants in Salem, including Gohan.
  • Hawaiian Poke - Salem has several good seafood restaurants and Poke Bowl is one of the best places to sample Hawaiian poke.
  • Burgers at Bo & Vine - Salem’s Bo & Vine serves some of the best burgers in the city.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beers - Salem is home to several breweries that serve local craft beers, including the Gilgamesh Brewing and the Wandering Goat.
  • Pinot Noir - Salem is also known for its local Pinot Noirs, which can be sampled at several vineyards and wineries, including Illahe Vineyards and Eola Hills Wine Cellars.

Where to Stay in Salem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re on a budget or looking for a luxury experience, Salem has a variety of accommodations to suit every need. Below are some of the best places to stay:

  • Grand Hotel in Salem - A top-rated hotel located in the heart of downtown Salem with a fitness center and complimentary breakfast.
  • Howard Johnson by Wyndham Salem - A budget-friendly hotel with a pool and free breakfast.
  • The Salem Hostel - A cozy hostel that offers affordable accommodations in a great location.
  • The Oregon Garden Resort - A luxurious resort in Silverton that is perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Salem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salem offers a variety of local products and souvenirs that are perfect for bringing home. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Oregon Wine - Bring home a bottle of Pinot Noir or other varietals from one of Salem’s many vineyards and wineries.
  • Marionberry Jam - Made from Oregon’s signature fruit, Marionberries, this jam is a popular souvenir that can be found in several stores throughout Salem.
  • Local honey - Salem has several honey businesses that sell locally sourced honey.
  • Handmade Pottery - Several shops in Salem sell handmade pottery and ceramics made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Salem

Salem is a friendly and welcoming city that respects diversity and values the arts and nature. The city has a rich history and heritage, and visitors are encouraged to explore and learn about it.

Best Time to Visit Salem

The best time to visit Salem is from mid-April to early October when the weather is mild and pleasant. This is also the peak season when several outdoor activities and events occur, such as the Salem Art Fair and Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salem

Do:

  • Be respectful of the city’s rich history and heritage.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions and cultural hubs of the city.
  • Try out local cuisine, including marionberry pie.
  • Attend the annual Salem Art Fair and Festival.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions or public property.
  • Disrespect local customs or cultures.
  • Ignore safety precautions while biking on the trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salem

Salem is a safe and friendly city, but it’s always good to take precautions, such as:

  • Be aware of your surroundings while exploring the city.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Lock your car and hotel room.
  • Use crosswalks, wear a helmet while biking and follow road safety guidelines.

Health in Salem

Salem has several hospitals and healthcare facilities, including Salem Hospital and Oregon State Hospital. It’s always wise to carry a medical kit with you while exploring the city.

Getting to Salem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Salem is easily accessible by road, train, or air. Below are some transportation options to reach Salem:

  • By Air - Salem Municipal Airport is the closest airport to the city, but the Portland International Airport is the largest airport, located a little over an hour away from Salem.
  • By Train - Amtrak’s Cascade train runs from Seattle to Eugene, stopping at the Salem station.
  • By Bus - Salem has several bus services that connect the city to other Oregon cities and the Pacific Northwest region.
  • By Car - Salem is well-connected to major highways, including Interstate 5 and Highway 22.

How to Get Around in Salem

Salem is a walkable city, but cycling and public transportation options are also available. Below are some ways to get around in Salem:

  • Public Transportation - Salem-Keizer Transit (also known as Cherriots) offers several bus routes that service the city for a low cost.
  • Biking - Salem has several bike-friendly trails and bike lanes, making it easy to commute or explore the city.
  • Walk - Salem’s downtown area is walkable, and several attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Salem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra days in Salem, you can explore nearby destinations, such as:

  • Portland - Salem is only an hour away from Portland, the largest city in Oregon, known for its food, music, art, and nightlife.
  • Silverton - This charming town is just 20 minutes away from Salem and is known for its popular Oregon Garden attraction
  • Willamette Valley Vineyards - This vineyard is located in Turner, just a 25-minute drive from Salem, and is a great place to sample some of Oregon’s famous wines.

Conclusion

Salem has a lot to offer, from natural attractions to cultural activities, wonderful food and drink scene, and plenty of accommodations to choose from. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you a good idea of what to see, do, and experience in this beautiful city. Plan your trip to Salem today and start exploring the city’s many wonders.
