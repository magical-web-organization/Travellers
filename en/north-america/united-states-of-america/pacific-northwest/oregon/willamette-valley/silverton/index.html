Introduction to Silverton

Located in the heart of Willamette Valley, in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Silverton is a charming town known for its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. The town is home to a number of cultural and natural attractions, making it a popular destination for travelers looking for an authentic experience.

List of Places to Visit in Silverton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Silverton is steeped in history and culture, and there are several must-visit museums and historical places that will take you back in time.

1. The Oregon Garden

The Oregon Garden is a serene oasis that boasts over 80 acres of beautifully manicured gardens and natural areas. Visitors can explore various themed gardens, walk through the oak savanna, and even take a tram tour. The garden also features a number of art installations and hosts several events throughout the year.

2. The Silverton Country Museum

The Silverton Country Museum is a small but fascinating museum that showcases the town’s history through exhibits and artifacts. The museum is housed in a converted church and features collections related to local industries, agriculture, and pioneer life.

3. The Gallon House Bridge

The Gallon House Bridge is a historic wooden covered bridge that dates back to the mid-1800s. The bridge is one of the few remaining covered bridges in Oregon and is a popular spot for photos. Visitors can walk across the bridge and admire the scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Silverton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Silverton is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views waiting to be discovered.

1. Silver Falls State Park

Silver Falls State Park is a popular destination for hiking and picnicking. The park features 10 waterfalls, including the stunning South Falls, which drops 177 feet. Visitors can hike along the Trail of Ten Falls, which is a 7.2-mile loop that takes hikers past all 10 waterfalls.

2. Mount Angel Abbey

Mount Angel Abbey is a Benedictine monastery located just outside of Silverton. The abbey is set on a hilltop and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valley. Visitors can take a guided tour of the abbey and its gardens, attend a mass or prayer service, or simply enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.

3. Champoeg State Heritage Area

Champoeg State Heritage Area is located just a short drive from Silverton and is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and camping. The park is situated on the banks of the Willamette River and features several historic sites, including the Champoeg Pioneer Cemetery and the Butteville Store.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silverton

Silverton is surrounded by beautiful natural areas, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Silverton.

1. Silver Falls State Park - Trail of Ten Falls

The Trail of Ten Falls is a 7.2-mile loop that takes hikers past all 10 waterfalls in Silver Falls State Park. The trail is rated as moderate and takes about 4-5 hours to complete. Bikers are not permitted on this trail.

2. Abiqua Falls Trail

The Abiqua Falls Trail is a challenging 13-mile hike that offers breathtaking views of the Abiqua Creek and the surrounding forest. The trail is rated as difficult and takes about 6-7 hours to complete.

3. Valley of the Giants Trail

The Valley of the Giants Trail is a 3.8-mile loop that takes hikers through a forest of massive Douglas fir trees. The trail is rated as moderate and takes about 2 hours to complete. Bikers are not permitted on this trail.

Best Things to Do in Silverton: Activities and Tours

Silverton offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Silverton.

1. Tram Tour of The Oregon Garden

The Oregon Garden offers a tram tour that takes visitors on a 40-minute guided tour of the gardens. The tour provides insight into the history and development of the gardens, as well as a close-up look at the plants and flowers.

2. Wine Tasting Tours

Willamette Valley is known for its excellent wines, and Silverton is no exception. Visitors can take a wine tasting tour of the local wineries to sample some of the best wines the region has to offer. Most tours provide transportation between wineries.

3. Farmer’s Market

The Silverton Farmer’s Market takes place on Saturdays from May through October. The market features locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and live music.

Food and Drink Scene of Silverton

Silverton is home to a variety of restaurants, cafes, and bars that serve delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Marionberry pie

Marionberry pie is a local favorite in Silverton. It’s made with Oregon’s famous Marionberries, which are a type of blackberry, and is typically served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

2. Hazelnut encrusted trout

Hazelnut encrusted trout is a popular dish in Silverton. The trout is coated in crushed hazelnuts and then pan-fried to perfection.

3. Wood-fired pizza

Silverton has several restaurants that specialize in wood-fired pizza. Visitors can choose from a variety of toppings and enjoy a delicious and authentic pie.

Local Beverages

1. Willamette Valley Wines

Willamette Valley is known for its excellent wines, and Silverton is no exception. Visitors can sample a variety of local wines at the many wineries in the area.

2. Oregon Craft Beer

Oregon is known for its craft beer, and Silverton has several breweries that serve excellent beer. Visitors can sample a variety of styles, from IPAs to stouts.

3. Coffee

Silverton has several coffee shops that serve locally roasted coffee. Visitors can enjoy a delicious cup of coffee while taking in the charming atmosphere of the town.

Where to Stay in Silverton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silverton has several options for accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Silverton.

1. Oregon Garden Resort

The Oregon Garden Resort is a luxurious hotel that is located on the grounds of The Oregon Garden. The hotel features cozy rooms, two restaurants, and an outdoor pool and hot tub.

2. Silverton Inn and Suites

The Silverton Inn and Suites is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and complimentary breakfast. The hotel is located in downtown Silverton, making it a convenient choice.

3. Silver Falls Lodge and Conference Center

The Silver Falls Lodge and Conference Center is located within Silver Falls State Park and offers rustic cabins and lodges. The accommodations are surrounded by nature, making it an ideal choice for those looking for a peaceful getaway.

What to Buy in Silverton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silverton has a variety of shops that sell souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best places to shop in Silverton.

1. Moonstone Books and Gifts

Moonstone Books and Gifts is a charming bookstore and gift shop that features a variety of local products, including books, candles, and handmade crafts.

2. Garden Stone

Garden Stone is a shop that specializes in hand-carved stone products, including birdbaths, fountains, and planters. Visitors can find unique and beautiful products to bring home.

3. The Potters’ Place Gallery

The Potters’ Place Gallery features handcrafted pottery made by local artisans. Visitors can purchase functional and decorative pottery, including dinnerware, vases, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Silverton

Silverton is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors should respect its local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind.

  • Silverton is a small town, so visitors should be mindful of their noise levels, especially in residential areas.
  • The town has a strong sense of community, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • The majority of the population is Caucasian, but there are also several Hispanic and Native American communities in the area.

Best Time to Visit Silverton

The best time to visit Silverton is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and the gardens and parks are in full bloom. Visitors can also enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silverton

Do:

  • Visit the local wineries and sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Hike the Trail of Ten Falls in Silver Falls State Park.
  • Enjoy a slice of Marionberry pie.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Be loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silverton

Silverton is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Stay on designated trails while hiking to avoid getting lost or injured.

Health in Silverton

The town of Silverton has a hospital and several clinics that provide medical care. Visitors should also bring their own medication, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Getting to Silverton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Silverton is located about 45 minutes south of Portland, Oregon. Here are the transportation options and logistics for getting to Silverton.

Airports Near Silverton

  • Portland International Airport (PDX) is the closest airport to Silverton, located about 45 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Silverton

  • Greyhound Bus Station is located in Salem, Oregon, about 20 minutes from Silverton.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car at Portland International Airport and drive to Silverton.
  • Private Shuttles: Several shuttle companies provide transportation between Portland and Silverton.

How to Get Around in Silverton

Silverton is a small town, and visitors can easily walk or bike around the downtown area. Visitors can also use rental cars or private shuttles to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Silverton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Silverton is located in the heart of Willamette Valley, making it an ideal base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider.

  • Salem, Oregon: Salem is the state capital of Oregon and is located about 20 minutes from Silverton. Visitors can explore historic sites and museums, as well as enjoy shopping and dining.
  • Mt. Hood National Forest: Mt. Hood National Forest is located about 90 minutes from Silverton and is a popular destination for hiking and skiing.
  • Oregon Coast: The Oregon Coast is located about 2 hours from Silverton and features beautiful beaches and charming coastal towns.

Conclusion

Silverton is a charming town that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Visitors can explore museums and historical sites, hike through state parks, and enjoy delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for an outdoor adventure or a peaceful retreat, Silverton has something to offer everyone.
