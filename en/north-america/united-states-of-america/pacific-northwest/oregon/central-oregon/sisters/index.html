Introduction to Sisters

Located in the heart of Central Oregon, Sisters is a vibrant western-themed town that attracts visitors with its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and endless recreational opportunities. With its charming downtown area, towering mountain peaks, scenic drives, and outdoor adventures, Sisters offers a unique mix of modern amenities and rugged wilderness.

List of Places to Visit in Sisters

From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor activities, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Sisters. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a history buff, here are the top places to visit on your trip to Sisters:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sisters Art Works: This gallery features a diverse collection of art, including paintings, sculptures, and mixed media.
  • Sisters Historical Museum: Discover the rich history of Sisters and the surrounding area at this fascinating museum, which features exhibits on the Native American cultures, pioneers, cowboys, and loggers who shaped the region.
  • Aspen Lakes Golf Course: Explore the stunning natural beauty of Central Oregon while practicing your swing on this 18-hole championship course.

Natural Attractions of Sisters: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Three Sisters Wilderness Area: This designated wilderness area is a must-visit destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts, offering stunning views of the Three Sisters mountain range and a variety of hiking trails for all skill levels.
  • Smith Rock State Park: Experience the thrill of rock climbing or hiking through massive rock formations at Smith Rock State Park, which also features stunning river views and scenic trails.
  • Deschutes National Forest: Covering more than 1.8 million acres, Deschutes National Forest is home to an incredible variety of wildlife, stunning natural landscapes, and numerous recreational opportunities, including hiking, fishing, camping, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Sisters

With its abundance of natural beauty and scenic trails, Sisters is the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore on foot or on wheels:

  • Black Butte Trail: This 3.6-mile hike provides stunning views of the surrounding landscape and leads to the summit of Black Butte, one of the most prominent volcanic peaks in the area.
  • Peterson Ridge Trail System: With more than 25 miles of trails through pine forests and open meadows, the Peterson Ridge Trail System is a popular destination for mountain bikers and hikers alike.
  • Salmon Creek Trail: This scenic 4.2-mile trail takes hikers through old-growth forests, along a beautiful creek, and past several waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Sisters: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Sisters has something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out on your trip:

  • Cascade Wilderness Outfitters: Explore the beauty of the Three Sisters Wilderness Area on horseback with Cascade Wilderness Outfitters, offering guided tours and camping trips for all skill levels.
  • Sisters Movie House: Catch a movie at this recently renovated theater, which offers comfortable seating, high-quality sound, and a wide variety of films for all ages.
  • Sisters Farmers Market: Shop for fresh produce, artisanal goods, and handmade crafts at the weekly Sisters Farmers Market, held every Sunday from June to September.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sisters

To make the most of your trip to Sisters, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Monday Explore Sisters’ historic downtown area, visit the Sisters Historical Museum, and enjoy a stroll along the miles of surrounding walking trails.
Tuesday Hike the Black Butte Trail and enjoy stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area.
Wednesday Take a day trip to nearby Smith Rock State Park for rock climbing or hiking.
Thursday Discover the beauty of the Three Sisters Wilderness Area on horseback with Cascade Wilderness Outfitters.
Friday Visit the Sisters Farmers Market and sample locally grown produce, artisanal goods, and handmade crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Sisters

With its rich agricultural heritage and thriving culinary scene, Sisters offers a variety of delicious and locally sourced dining options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burger with Bacon Jam from The Open Door: Featuring a juicy, flavorful patty topped with smoky bacon jam, this burger is a local favorite.
  • Brewer’s Grain Bowl from Melvin Brewing: This hearty and healthy bowl is made with quinoa, mixed greens, sweet potato, avocado, and roasted cauliflower, all topped with a honey-balsamic vinaigrette.
  • Lamb Pie from The Suttle Lodge: Made with tender lamb and a flaky crust, this savory pie is the perfect comfort food.

Local Beverages

  • Deschutes Brewery Beers: Sample a variety of award-winning beers from one of Oregon’s most popular breweries, which features a tasting room and brewery tours.
  • Aspen Lakes Sauvignon Blanc: This crisp and refreshing white wine is made with grapes grown in the nearby Aspen Lakes vineyard.
  • Sisters Coffee Company Roasts: Start your day off right with a cup of coffee from one of the best roasters in the Pacific Northwest.

Where to Stay in Sisters: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a cozy bed and breakfast, Sisters has a variety of lodging options to suit any budget or preference. Here are some top-rated options:

  • FivePine Lodge and Spa: This luxurious lodge features spacious suites, a full-service spa, and easy access to hiking and biking trails.
  • Sisters Bunkhouse: This budget-friendly hostel offers clean and comfortable accommodations, a communal kitchen, and easy access to downtown Sisters.
  • Black Butte Ranch: This sprawling resort features multiple lodging options, including vacation rentals, condos, and hotel rooms. Amenities include golf courses, swimming pools, and onsite restaurants.

What to Buy in Sisters: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From locally made crafts and apparel to fresh food and wine, Sisters offers a variety of unique products and souvenirs to bring home. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Sisters Meat and Smokehouse Jerky: Made with locally sourced meats and smoked to perfection, this jerky is a delicious and savory snack to bring home.
  • Sisters Soap Company Soaps: Handmade with all natural ingredients, these soaps come in a variety of scents and make the perfect gift or personal indulgence.
  • Sisters Coffee Company Beans: Bring home a bag of freshly roasted coffee beans from one of the best roasters in the Pacific Northwest.

Local Culture and Customs of Sisters

Sisters is a welcoming and friendly community that embraces its western heritage and adventurous spirit. If you want to fully experience its local culture and customs, be sure to:

  • Attend a rodeo: Sisters hosts several rodeos throughout the year, featuring bull riding, barrel racing, and other classic western events.
  • Visit the Sisters Quilt Show: This annual event, held every July, attracts thousands of quilters and quilt enthusiasts from around the world, showcasing the region’s rich history of quilting and textile arts.
  • Shop at a local farmer’s market: Celebrate Central Oregon’s agricultural heritage by sampling locally grown produce and artisanal products at the weekly downtown Sisters Farmer’s Market.

Best Time to Visit Sisters

The best time to visit Sisters depends on your personal preferences and interests. With mild temperatures and endless recreational opportunities, summer is the most popular season for tourists, particularly for outdoor enthusiasts. Fall offers stunning foliage and quieter crowds, while winter transforms the landscape into a winter wonderland, perfect for skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter activities. Spring offers milder temperatures and blooming wildflowers, ideal for hiking and exploring.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sisters

If you want to make the most of your trip to Sisters, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Bring comfortable walking shoes: Sisters features an abundance of sidewalks and trails, so be sure to bring comfortable shoes for exploring the town and surrounding area.
  • Try local cuisine: Sample the delicious and locally sourced food and drink options in Sisters, from craft beer to savory burgers.
  • Respect local wildlife: Central Oregon is home to a variety of wildlife, including protected species like the gray wolf and bald eagle. Keep a safe distance and never feed or disturb wild animals.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car: Central Oregon has one of the highest rates of car theft in the state, so be sure to lock your doors and take your valuables with you.
  • Forget sunscreen: With its high elevation and clear skies, Sisters can be surprisingly bright and sunny. Be sure to wear sunscreen to avoid sunburns and skin damage.
  • Litter: Help keep Sisters clean and beautiful by disposing of your trash properly and respecting the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Sisters is a safe and welcoming community, but it’s important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings, particularly when enjoying outdoor activities and exploring the wilderness. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Sisters:

Safety in Sisters

  • Be aware of wildlife: Central Oregon is home to a variety of wildlife, including bears, mountain lions, and coyotes. Be sure to research the proper safety precautions for encountering these species and avoid leaving food or trash in your campsite.
  • Be prepared for emergencies: Make sure you have a first aid kit, plenty of water, and a map of the area when hiking or exploring the wilderness.
  • Stay hydrated: Central Oregon is known for its dry and sunny weather, so be sure to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

Health in Sisters

  • Watch out for altitude sickness: Sisters is located at high elevation, so be sure to take it easy and stay well-hydrated if you’re accustomed to lower altitudes.
  • Bring allergy medication: If you have allergies, be sure to bring medication to manage your symptoms, particularly during the spring and fall when pollen counts are high.
  • Use sunscreen: With its bright and sunny skies, Sisters can be surprisingly harsh on skin. Be sure to apply sunscreen and wear a hat and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun’s rays.

Getting to Sisters: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re flying in from out of state or driving in from a nearby city, there are several transportation options for getting to Sisters. Here are some of the most convenient options:

  • Redmond Municipal Airport: Located just 20 miles from Sisters, Redmond Municipal Airport offers daily flights to major cities in the western United States, including Seattle, Portland, and San Francisco.
  • Bend Greyhound Station: Located in nearby Bend, the Greyhound station offers daily bus service to Bend from major cities throughout the northwest.
  • Renting a Car: If you’re driving in from out of town, renting a car is a convenient way to explore Sisters and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Sisters

Sisters is a relatively small town and can be easily explored on foot or by bike. However, if you’re looking to venture further afield, or if you need to get to the nearby cities of Bend or Redmond, renting a car is your best bet. Alternatively, several local taxi companies offer transportation services within Sisters, and public transportation is available through several bus lines that stop in town.

Where to Go Next from Sisters: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Pacific Northwest, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to check out from Sisters. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Crater Lake National Park: Located just a few hours’ drive from Sisters, Crater Lake National Park is home to one of the deepest and clearest lakes in the world, surrounded by stunning mountain scenery.
  • Mt. Bachelor: This popular ski and snowboarding destination is located just south of Sisters and offers snow sports enthusiasts a wide variety of slopes and trails.
  • Painted Hills: Explore the stunning geological formations and otherworldly landscapes of this unique national monument, located just a few hours’ drive east of Sisters.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a little bit of both, Sisters, Oregon, has something to offer all visitors. From its stunning natural landscapes and abundance of recreational opportunities to its charming downtown area and rich cultural heritage, Sisters is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of the Pacific Northwest. Plan your trip today and discover all that Sisters has to offer!
