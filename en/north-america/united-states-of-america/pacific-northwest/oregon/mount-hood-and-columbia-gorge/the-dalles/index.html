Introduction to The Dalles

The Dalles, located in the heart of the spectacular Columbia River Gorge, is a charming city situated in the eastern part of Oregon, known for its picturesque beauty, fascinating history, and vast outdoor recreation opportunities. The city was named after the French word “les dalles,” which translates to “the flagstones” or “the rapids,” referring to a rocky stretch on the Columbia River. The Dalles is also known for its warm and sunny climate and offers an ideal escape for nature lovers, adventure seekers, history buffs, and anyone looking for a pleasant getaway.

List of Places to Visit in The Dalles

The Dalles offers a range of attractions that cater to diverse interests. Here is a list of places to visit during your trip to The Dalles:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Columbia Gorge Discovery Center and Museum: A treasure trove of information about the natural history and cultural heritage of the Columbia River Gorge, including interactive exhibits, artifacts, and live raptor shows.
  • Fort Dalles Museum and Anderson Homestead: A historic military base turned museum that houses collections and exhibits showcasing the life and culture of pioneers and settlers in the region.
  • The Dalles Art Center: A hub for local and regional artists to showcase their artwork, offering a range of contemporary, traditional, and modern art exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of The Dalles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rowena Crest: A scenic viewpoint situated on the Columbia River Gorge, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills, river, and wildflowers.
  • Tom McCall Nature Preserve: A 1000-acre nature reserve, home to a variety of flora and fauna, including rare wildflowers, birds, and wildlife, offering hiking trails and stunning views of the Columbia River.
  • The Dalles Riverfront Trail: A popular walking and biking trail that runs along the Columbia River, offering panoramic views, benches, and interpretive signage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Dalles

The Dalles offers a range of hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of experience. Here is a list of some of the popular routes:

  • Catherine Creek Trail: A 3.3-mile trail that winds through a picturesque meadow, passing through wildflowers, trees, and a small waterfall, offering stunning views of the Columbia River.
  • The Dalles Riverfront Trail: A 3.3-mile paved trail that runs along the Columbia River, offering beautiful views and a chance of spotting wildlife.
  • Rowena Plateau Loop: A 2.3-mile trail that offers beautiful views of the gorge, wildflowers, and the Columbia River, with moderate difficulty.

Best Things to Do in The Dalles: Activities and Tours

The Dalles offers several exciting activities and tours that cater to different interests:

  • Gorge White Water Adventures: An adrenaline-filled white water rafting trip down the Columbia River Gorge is a perfect way to experience nature and adventure.
  • The Dalles Farmers Market: Open every Saturday, the market offers fresh produce, artisanal products, and live music.
  • Hood River Fruit Loop: A scenic countryside drive with stops at various farms and orchards to enjoy fresh fruit, ice cream, and other local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for The Dalles

A week in The Dalles will allow you to explore the city and its surroundings. Here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Columbia Gorge Discovery Center and Museum and Fort Dalles Museum and Anderson Homestead.
  • Day 2: Visit Rowena Crest, Catherine Creek Trail, and Tom McCall Nature Preserve.
  • Day 3: Hike the Rowena Plateau Loop and visit The Dalles Art Center.
  • Day 4: Go white water rafting or kayaking on the Columbia River Gorge.
  • Day 5: Visit nearby wineries and tasting rooms or take a guided tour of local orchards and farms on the Hood River Fruit Loop.
  • Day 6: Relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region or take a scenic drive to nearby attractions.
  • Day 7: Explore the local food scene, visit The Dalles Farmers Market, and check out local shops and galleries.

Food and Drink Scene of The Dalles

The Dalles has a thriving food and drink scene, influenced by its location and the local agriculture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cherry-smoked brisket: A popular dish made from locally sourced beef, cherrywood-smoked, and served with barbecue sauce and sides.
  • Columbia River Steelhead: A freshwater fish famous for its mild flavor and tender texture, served grilled or smoked.
  • Hood River pears: A variety of pears grown in the region, famous for their juicy and sweet taste, used in pies, salads, and jams.

Local Beverages

  • Oregon Pinot Noir: A famous wine variety produced in the Willamette Valley, known for its fruity and complex flavor.
  • Breweries: The Dalles is home to several breweries, offering a range of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and ales.
  • Hood River Distillers: A locally owned distillery that produces a range of spirits, including whiskey, vodka, and gin.

Where to Stay in The Dalles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Dalles offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  • The Dalles Inn: centrally located, offering comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.
  • Celilo Inn: Situated on a hill, offering stunning views of the Columbia River, offering spacious rooms, a hot tub, and free breakfast.
  • Cousin’s Country Inn & Suites: A family-owned hotel that offers spacious suites, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in The Dalles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Dalles offers several souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Cherry products: The Dalles is known for its cherry production, offering products like cherry jams, syrups, and preserves.
  • Hudson Bay blankets: The Dalles has a rich history of trade and commerce with Hudson Bay, offering woolen blankets, capotes, and other products.
  • Local artisanal products: The city is home to several local artists and artisans, offering handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of The Dalles

The Dalles has a rich history and culture, which reflects in its food, art, and festivals. The local culture is influenced by the Native American tribes, settlers, and pioneers who have inhabited the region. The region is also famous for its cherry production, which is celebrated annually during the Cherry Festival.

Best Time to Visit The Dalles

The best time to visit The Dalles is during the spring and fall seasons when the climate is mild, and the tourist crowds are relatively sparse. The summers can get hot and crowded, while the winters can be chilly and rainy.

Safety and Health

Safety in The Dalles

The Dalles is a generally safe city, and the crime rates are relatively low. However, it is best to be cautious and aware of one’s surroundings, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in The Dalles

The Dalles has quality healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. However, it is advisable to carry any necessary medication and travel insurance during the trip.

Getting to The Dalles: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Dalles is located along Interstate 84, which connects it to Portland and other major cities in Oregon. It is also accessible through the scenic highway 30, which runs through the Columbia River Gorge. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near The Dalles

Portland International Airport is the nearest airport to The Dalles, about 87 miles away.

Bus Stations Near The Dalles

Greyhound offers bus services to The Dalles from Portland and other nearby cities.

Other Options

The Columbia Gorge Express offers bus services from Portland to The Dalles during the peak tourist season.

How to Get Around in The Dalles

The Dalles has several transportation options to get around the city, including car rentals, taxi services, and public buses. Biking and walking are popular options for exploring the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from The Dalles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Dalles is located in the heart of the Columbia River Gorge, offering a range of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some options:

  • Hood River: A charming town located on the Oregon-Washington border, known for its scenic beauty, outdoor recreation opportunities, and local breweries.
  • Mount Hood: An iconic peak in Oregon, famous for its year-round skiing and hiking opportunities, pristine lakes, and wildflower meadows.
  • Portland: The largest city in Oregon, known for its vibrant food scene, cultural offerings, and urban parks.

Conclusion

The Dalles is a charming city located in the heart of the Columbia River Gorge, offering a range of attractions, outdoor recreation opportunities, and a rich history and culture. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or simply a break from the hustle and bustle of the city, The Dalles is an ideal destination to explore and unwind.
