Introduction to Mount Hood

Mount Hood is a dormant volcano that can be found in the beautiful region of Mount Hood and Columbia Gorge, in Oregon. It is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and rich culture. At 11,249 feet, this majestic peak is the highest point in Oregon and is situated within the Mount Hood National Forest, which offers a wide range of recreational activities for tourists of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Hood

There are plenty of amazing places to visit in Mount Hood, ranging from culturally important attractions to natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Timberline Lodge: Located at the base of Mount Hood, Timberline Lodge is a National Historic Landmark and a popular tourist attraction. It was built in the 1930s and is known for its rustic architecture and stunning views.
  2. Mount Hood Cultural Center & Museum: This museum showcases the rich cultural history of the area, featuring exhibits on the Native American tribes that have called this region home for thousands of years.
  3. The Hood River Valley: This area is known for its fruit orchards and vineyards, making it a popular destination for foodies and wine-lovers.

Natural Attractions of Mount Hood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Trillium Lake: This beautiful lake is surrounded by the stunning Mount Hood scenery and is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.
  2. Multnomah Falls: One of the most famous waterfalls in the region, Multnomah Falls is a must-visit for any tourist exploring Mount Hood.
  3. Mount Hood Meadows: This ski resort is located on the south side of Mount Hood and offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the region.
  4. Mount Hood Scenic Byway: This 105-mile drive offers stunning views of Mount Hood and the surrounding area, passing through picturesque small towns and scenic vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Hood

Mount Hood has over 1,200 miles of hiking and biking trails, offering endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Some of the most popular routes include:

  1. Timberline Trail: This 40-mile hiking trail circles the base of Mount Hood and offers stunning views of the mountain and surrounding forests.
  2. Lost Lake Loop: This 3.2-mile hiking trail offers incredible views of Mount Hood and leads to the beautiful Lost Lake.
  3. Surveyor’s Ridge Trail: This 20-mile mountain biking trail is a great option for experienced riders, offering challenging terrain and beautiful views.

Best Things to Do in Mount Hood: Activities and Tours

  1. Skiing and Snowboarding: Mount Hood is known for its incredible ski resorts, including Timberline, Mt. Hood Meadows, and Ski Bowl.
  2. Whitewater Rafting: The White Salmon River offers some of the best whitewater rafting in the region, with rapids suitable for all skill levels.
  3. Wine Tasting: The Hood River Valley is home to a number of wineries and vineyards, offering visitors the chance to sample some of the region’s best wines.
  4. Zipline Tours: There are several zipline tours available in the Mount Hood area, offering a thrilling way to see the stunning scenery from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Hood

Day 1: Explore the Mount Hood Scenic Byway Day 2: Hike the Timberline Trail Day 3: Ski or snowboard at Mt. Hood Meadows or Timberline Day 4: Whitewater rafting on the White Salmon River Day 5: Visit the Mount Hood Cultural Center & Museum Day 6: Wine tasting in the Hood River Valley Day 7: Zipline tour over the Mount Hood National Forest

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Hood

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Marionberry pie: Made with a local type of blackberry known as the marionberry, this sweet and tart pie is a Mount Hood specialty.
  2. Fresh seafood: With the Columbia River running through the region, there are plenty of delicious seafood options available.
  3. Hood River cherries: This area is known for its delicious cherries, available in pies, preserves, and more.

Local Beverages

  1. Oregon Pinot Noir: This region is known for producing some of the best Pinot Noir wines in the world, thanks to the unique growing conditions.
  2. Craft beer: There are several breweries in the Mount Hood area, offering a range of delicious beers.
  3. Hard apple cider: The Hood River Valley is known for its delicious apples, which are used to produce some of the best hard cider in the region.

Where to Stay in Mount Hood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Timberline Lodge: This historic lodge offers stunning views of Mount Hood and a range of accommodation options, from cozy rooms to luxury suites.
  2. Best Western Mt. Hood Inn: This hotel is located in the charming town of Government Camp and offers comfortable rooms and great amenities.
  3. Lost Lake Resort: This resort offers cabins, campsites, and RV spots, as well as stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

What to Buy in Mount Hood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hood River Fruit: The Hood River Valley is known for its delicious cherries, apples, and pears, which make great souvenirs.
  2. Local wine or beer: Bring home a bottle of the delicious local wine or beer to enjoy later.
  3. Handcrafted jewelry and art: The region is home to a number of talented artists and craftsmen, offering unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Hood

The Native American tribes of the area have a rich culture and history, and visitors should be respectful of their traditions and customs. Additionally, Mount Hood is known for its outdoors culture, with a strong emphasis on environmental sustainability and conservation.

Best Time to Visit Mount Hood

The best time to visit Mount Hood is from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are at their best. However, visitors can also enjoy the region’s winter sports from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Hood

Do:

  1. Respect the natural environment and practice Leave No Trace principles.
  2. Take appropriate safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities.
  3. Learn about and respect the culture and history of the area.

Don’t:

  1. Damage or remove any natural resources.
  2. Ignore warning signs or take unnecessary risks.
  3. Disrespect the traditions and customs of the local Native American tribes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Hood

Visitors to Mount Hood should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities and take appropriate safety precautions. This includes being prepared for changing weather conditions, carrying appropriate gear and supplies, and taking the necessary precautions when participating in activities such as skiing, rock climbing, or whitewater rafting.

Health in Mount Hood

Visitors should also take care of their health when visiting Mount Hood, including staying hydrated and protecting themselves from the sun. Additionally, visitors who are not used to high altitudes should be aware of the risks associated with altitude sickness and take the necessary precautions.

Getting to Mount Hood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Hood

  1. Portland International Airport (PDX): This is the closest major airport to Mount Hood, located about 50 miles west of the mountain.

Bus Stations Near Mount Hood

  1. Greyhound: Greyhound operates a bus station in Hood River, about 20 miles from Mount Hood.

Other Options

  1. Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car from Portland International Airport and drive to Mount Hood.
  2. Private Transportation: Private transportation companies, such as shuttle services, are also available.

How to Get Around in Mount Hood

The easiest way to get around Mount Hood is by car. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a guided tour to see the sites.

Where to Go Next from Mount Hood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Columbia River Gorge: This stunning river gorge is just a short drive from Mount Hood and offers a range of outdoor activities and natural wonders.
  2. Portland: The vibrant city of Portland is located just an hour’s drive from Mount Hood and offers a range of cultural attractions, food and drink options, and more.

Conclusion

Mount Hood is a stunning destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventurers to foodies to culture enthusiasts. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and incredible recreational opportunities, it is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Pacific Northwest.
