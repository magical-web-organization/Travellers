Introduction to Cascade Locks

Cascade Locks is a scenic city situated in the foothills of Mt. Hood and the Columbia River Gorge region of Oregon. The city became famous for being the only town in the US located on the Pacific Crest Trail. The town is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and food lovers. Cascade Locks features a rich history and stunning natural scenery, making it a must-visit destination in Oregon.

List of Places to Visit in Cascade Locks

Cascade Locks offers a wide range of attractions ranging from culturally important landmarks to natural wonders. Here is a list of some of the top places to explore in Cascade Locks.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cascade Locks has a rich history, and visitors can learn about the cultural and historical significance of the region through the following museums and landmarks:

  • Cascade Locks Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of the town, including the Native American presence, the Lewis and Clark Expedition, and the town’s development as a major transportation hub.
  • The Bridge of the Gods: The Bridge of the Gods is a historic toll bridge that spans the Columbia River Gorge between Oregon and Washington states. The bridge has a fascinating mythological background and is a popular spot for hikers and cyclists.
  • The Bonneville Dam: The Bonneville Dam is a hydroelectric dam that is part of the Columbia River Gorge National Scenic Area. The dam features a fish ladder and several exhibits that showcase the history of the dam’s construction and its impact on the region.

Natural Attractions of Cascade Locks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cascade Locks is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes that provide a wide range of outdoor recreational opportunities. Here are some of the best natural attractions that Cascade Locks offers:

  • The Pacific Crest Trail: The Pacific Crest Trail is a hiking trail that runs from Mexico to Canada, and Cascade Locks is the only town on the entire trail. The trail offers stunning views of the Columbia River Gorge and the surrounding areas.
  • Multnomah Falls: Multnomah Falls is the tallest waterfall in Oregon and is located just a few miles outside of Cascade Locks. Visitors can take a short and scenic hike to the top of the falls for a stunning view.
  • Mount Hood National Forest: Mount Hood National Forest is located just east of Cascade Locks and is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and skiing. The forest features several trails, including the Timberline trail that runs around the mountain’s base.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cascade Locks

Cascade Locks is a paradise for outdoor lovers, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top trails to check out:

  • Pacific Crest Trail: The Pacific Crest Trail is a 2,650-mile hiking trail that runs from Mexico to Canada, and Cascade Locks is the only town on the entire trail. Visitors can hike the trail for a day or several, depending on their interest.
  • Herman Creek Trail: This trailhead is located west of Cascade Locks and offers a beautiful hike along the Herman Creek to the Gorton Creek Trail, where hikers can see Gorton Creek Falls.
  • Historic Columbia River Highway State Trail: This trail runs parallel to the Columbia River Gorge, and visitors can access it from Cascade Locks. The trail spans over 73 miles and boasts breathtaking views of the Columbia River and the surrounding mountains.
