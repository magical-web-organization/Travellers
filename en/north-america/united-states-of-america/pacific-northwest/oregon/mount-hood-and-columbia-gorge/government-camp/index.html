Introduction to Government Camp

Located in Mount Hood and Columbia Gorge in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, Government Camp is a small community known for its natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities. It is a popular gateway to Mount Hood, the highest peak in Oregon, and is surrounded by forests, streams, and picturesque views. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, Government Camp offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Government Camp

If you’re planning a trip to Government Camp, here are some of the must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mount Hood Cultural Center and Museum

The Mount Hood Cultural Center and Museum is dedicated to showcasing the cultural heritage and natural history of Mount Hood and the communities that reside around it. The museum features exhibits on Native American artifacts, volcanic activity, skiing, and more.

Summit Historical Society Museum

Located in the Summit Ski Area, the Summit Historical Society Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of skiing in the Pacific Northwest. The museum showcases a collection of historic ski equipment, clothing, and photographs, and provides visitors with an interactive experience of the evolution of skiing.

Natural Attractions of Government Camp: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Hood National Forest

Mount Hood National Forest is a natural wonderland that covers over one million acres of forests, mountains, and streams. It is home to several hiking trails, fishing spots, campgrounds, and snowshoeing trails.

Trillium Lake

Trillium Lake is a serene lake nestled in the heart of the Mount Hood National Forest. It offers visitors with breathtaking views of Mount Hood and serves as a popular spot for camping, fishing, swimming, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Government Camp

Government Camp is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

Mirror Lake Trail

The Mirror Lake Trail is a moderate 3.7-mile hike that provides stunning views of the Mount Hood wilderness. It is an excellent trail for hiking during the summer and snowshoeing during the winter.

Timberline Trail #600

The Timberline Trail is a 40-mile loop that encircles Mount Hood and showcases some of the Pacific Northwest’s most beautiful landscapes. It is an ideal trail for experienced hikers and backpackers.

Best Things to Do in Government Camp: Activities and Tours

Skiing at Mount Hood

Mount Hood is a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding. The area has several ski resorts that offer a variety of skiing and snowboarding terrains. Among the popular ski resorts in the area include Timberline Lodge, Mt. Hood Meadows, and Ski Bowl.

Mount Hood Adventure Park at Ski Bowl

The Mount Hood Adventure Park at Ski Bowl is a family-friendly attraction that offers a range of outdoor activities, including ziplining, bungee jumping, scenic chairlift rides, and mini-golf.

Week-Long Itinerary for Government Camp

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to Government Camp

Day 1: Arrival and Exploration of the Village

Spend your first day exploring the village of Government Camp, check out Mount Hood Cultural Center and Museum, and have dinner at Mount Hood Brewing Company.

Day 2: Snowshoe on Trillium Lake

Start your day with breakfast at Huckleberry Inn, rent snowshoes, and explore Trillium Lake.

Day 3: Sightseeing on Mount Hood

Take a scenic drive out to the Timberline Lodge, have lunch at the Ram’s Head Bar, and enjoy the scenic views of Mount Hood.

Day 4: Skiing at Ski Bowl

Spend the day skiing at Ski Bowl. The resort has a variety of skiing terrains that cater to different skill levels.

Day 5: Explore Hood River

Take a day trip to Hood River and visit Mount Hood Winery, have lunch at Solstice Café, and enjoy a hike at Mosier Plateau Trail.

Day 6: Spa Day

Spend the day relaxing at the Resort at the Mountain, have a massage, and enjoy the various amenities the resort has on offer.

Day 7: Departure

Have breakfast at the Huckleberry Inn, check out of your accommodation and depart Government Camp.

Food and Drink Scene of Government Camp

Must-Try Dishes

Huckleberry Pancakes

Huckleberry Inn is a famous restaurant in Government Camp known for its huckleberry pancakes, a must-try dish.

Mount Hood Brewing Company

Mount Hood Brewing Company is popular for its craft beer and sumptuous pizza.

Local Beverages

Mt. Hood Winery

Mt. Hood Winery is known for its exceptional wines and picturesque vineyard that offers scenic views of Mount Hood.

Where to Stay in Government Camp: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Timberline Lodge

Timberline Lodge is a historic hotel located on the southern slope of Mount Hood. It is the only ski-in, ski-out hotel in North America.

Collins Lake Resort

Collins Lake Resort offers luxurious and spacious condominiums that come with private hot tubs, fireplaces, and kitchens. The resort is a short walking distance from Government Camp.

What to Buy in Government Camp: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kachka

Kachka is a Russian restaurant that sells traditional Russian goods such as chocolates, candies, and other treats.

The Village Store

The Village Store is a gift shop that sells a variety of souvenirs, including clothing, accessories, and local artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Government Camp

Government Camp has a rich history of skiing and the outdoors. They are known to be friendly, hospitable, and enjoy the outdoors. Visitors are encouraged to respect the area’s beauty by being responsible and following the Leave No Trace principles.

Best Time to Visit Government Camp

The best time to visit Government Camp is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is pleasant, and all outdoor recreational activities are available. However, if skiing is your thing, head out during the winter months (December to March), when the area is covered in snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Government Camp

Do:

  • Respect the community and environment by following the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Carry extra food, water, and warm clothing when hiking.
  • Take necessary precautions when skiing and snowboarding.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage or litter on the trail or around the village.
  • Approach wildlife.
  • Smoke in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Government Camp

Government Camp is a safe community. However, visitors are encouraged to take appropriate safety precautions when partaking in outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.

Health in Government Camp

The area is at a higher altitude, and visitors are encouraged to adapt slowly to avoid altitude sickness. Visitors should also stay hydrated and carry extra water when hiking.

Getting to Government Camp: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Government Camp

The closest airport to Government Camp is Portland International Airport (PDX), which is approximately 60 miles away from the village.

Bus Stations Near Government Camp

The Greyhound Bus station is located in Portland, and travelers can take a shuttle or rental car to Government Camp.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Government Camp, and there is a range of rental car companies to choose from in Portland.

How to Get Around in Government Camp

Government Camp is a walking village, and most attractions are located within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles, take shuttles, or drive around the area.

Where to Go Next from Government Camp: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hood River

Hood River is a town located east of Portland known for its kiteboarding, windsurfing, and wine tasting.

Portland

Portland is a vibrant city known for its food, culture, and outdoor recreation. The city is a popular destination for tourists and offers a range of attractions to explore.

Conclusion

Government Camp is a beautiful destination offering visitors a chance to explore the outdoors and immerse themselves in the local culture. The village has a range of attractions, accommodations, food, and drink options to cater to different preferences. Whether you’re planning a family vacation or a solo adventure, Government Camp has something for everyone, and you’re guaranteed to leave with unforgettable memories.
