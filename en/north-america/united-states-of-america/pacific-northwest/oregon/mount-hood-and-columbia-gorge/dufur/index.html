Dufur, nestled in Mount Hood and Columbia Gorge, is an idyllic destination located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States of America. The quaint town is known for its rural charm, natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich history. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, Dufur has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we have covered everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Dufur.

Introduction to Dufur

Dufur is a small unincorporated community in Wasco County, Oregon, situated on the eastern side of the Cascade Range. The town was founded in the early 1870s and was named after the Dufur family, who were pioneers in the area. The town’s charming main street is lined with historic buildings, quaint shops, eateries and is a must-visit spot. Dufur has a population of fewer than 700 people and offers visitors a peaceful break from the hustle and bustle of city life. It is an ideal destination to unwind and indulge in outdoor activities surrounded by nature.

List of Places to Visit in Dufur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kramers Market - Step back in time at Kramers Market, which has been in continuous operation since 1939. The market is known for its fresh produce, homemade sausage, and smoked ham.

  2. Dufur Historical Society - Visit the Dufur Historical Society Museum, located in the oldest commercial building in Dufur, to explore the town’s rich history. The museum features exhibits and artifacts from the early 19th century.

  3. The Balch Hotel - The historic Balch Hotel, built-in 1907, offers visitors a glimpse into the past. The hotel has been restored to its original grandeur and offers comfortable accommodation as well as spa services.

Natural Attractions of Dufur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Deschutes River - Rafting, kayaking, and fishing are popular activities at the Deschutes River. You can take a guided tour or rent equipment to explore the river on your own.

  2. The Dalles Mountain Ranch - Situated in the Columbia River Gorge National Scenic Area, the Dalles Mountain Ranch is a popular spot for hiking. The area features well-maintained trails and outstanding views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. The Historic Columbia River Highway - The Columbia River Gorge Scenic Area boasts many historic places and scenic drives. The Historic Columbia River Highway is a must-visit and offers some of the most magnificent views of the gorge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dufur

  1. Chenoweth Rim Trail - A moderate 5.6-mile hike that takes you along the rim of the Chenoweth Plateau, offering stunning views of the Columbia River Gorge.

  2. Historic Columbia River Highway State Trail - A scenic 13-mile paved path that follows the original route of the historic highway, offering some of the most beautiful views in the Pacific Northwest.

  3. Mosier Twin Tunnels Trail - An easy 3.3-mile hike that takes you through two tunnels and offers stunning views of the Columbia River Gorge.

Best Things to Do in Dufur: Activities and Tours

  1. White River Falls State Park - Beautiful waterfalls, hiking trails, and fishing make White River Falls State Park a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

  2. Fly-fishing - With the Deschutes River located nearby, Dufur is a prime destination for fly-fishing enthusiasts. Local guides are available to show you the best fishing spots.

  3. Wine Tasting - The Columbia River Gorge is home to over 30 wineries, and a wine tasting tour is a must-do activity for any wine lover.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dufur

Day 1: Arrive in Dufur and explore the town’s charming main street.

Day 2: Hike the Chenoweth Rim Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the Columbia River Gorge.

Day 3: Visit White River Falls State Park and indulge in outdoor activities.

Day 4: Take a wine tasting tour in the Columbia River Gorge and sample the region’s best wines.

Day 5: Explore the local history by visiting the Dufur Historical Society Museum and Kramers Market.

Day 6: Enjoy a day of fly-fishing at the Deschutes River.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the spa services at the historic Balch Hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Dufur

Must-Try Dishes

  2. Fresh produce from the Farmers Market - Dufur’s Farmers Market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

  3. Huckleberry jam - Huckleberries are a local delicacy in the Pacific Northwest, and huckleberry jam is a must-try for anyone visiting Dufur.

Local Beverages

  1. Local wine - The Columbia River Gorge is home to many wineries, and a bottle of local wine is an excellent souvenir to take home.

  2. Craft beer - Several breweries in the town and the nearby areas offer excellent craft beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Dufur

The people of Dufur are proud of their rural charm and community spirit. Visitors should respect the town’s peaceful way of life and be mindful of local customs, including quiet hours and speed limits.

Best Time to Visit Dufur

The best time to visit Dufur is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dufur

Dufur is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards in the natural environment, such as wildlife and inclement weather. Proper gear, including sturdy hiking shoes and warm clothing, should be worn during outdoor activities.

Health in Dufur

Health services in Dufur are limited. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and first aid supplies with them. In cases of emergency, the nearest hospital is located in The Dalles, approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Dufur

How to Get Around in Dufur

Dufur is a small town, and most places are within walking distance. A car rental is recommended for exploring nearby attractions and outdoor activities.
