Lewis and Clark National Historical Park is a preserved memorial to the historic journey of exploration across the Pacific Northwest made by the legendary explorers Meriwether Lewis and William Clark. The park is located on the coast of the Pacific Ocean and includes over 3,700 acres of lush forests and sandy beaches. This comprehensive guide will explore everything you need to know about Lewis and Clark National Historical Park, from its culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, to where to stay, what to eat, and how to get there.

Introduction to Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Lewis and Clark National Historical Park is located in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States of America and covers a vast area that encompasses scenic natural landscapes and ancient cultural sites. The park is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the area and is famous for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The park boasts an array of historic landmarks, monuments, and museums that are both culturally and historically significant, including:

  • Fort Clatsop National Memorial: Built in 1805 as a winter encampment for the Lewis and Clark Expedition, the fort is now a museum and a memorial to the expedition.
  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center: This state-of-the-art museum is nestled on a scenic overlook and provides visitors with an interactive experience that tells the story of the expedition.

Natural Attractions of Lewis and Clark National Historical Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The park has an abundance of natural attractions that provide visitors with endless outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the most popular natural attractions include:

  • Sunset Beach: A wide sandy beach where visitors can find solitude and witness breathtaking sunsets.
  • Fort to Sea Trail: A six-mile trail that links Fort Clatsop and Sunset Beach.
  • Cape Disappointment: A beautiful headland at the mouth of the Columbia River that offers dramatic coastal views and scenic hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

The park offers visitors some of the most scenic and challenging hiking and biking trails in the Pacific Northwest, including:

  • The Fort to Sea Trail: A six-mile trail that weaves through a wide variety of ecosystems, including old-growth forests, saltwater estuaries, and sandy beaches.
  • The Fort Clatsop Trail: A paved walking path that loops visitors around the reconstructed fort.

Best Things to Do in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park: Activities and Tours

The park offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to tourists of all ages, including:

  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center Tours: Led by knowledgeable park rangers, these tours take visitors through the museum exhibits and provide them with insights into the expedition’s history.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking: Visitors can explore the waterways that Lewis and Clark traveled on a rented canoe or kayak.
  • Wildlife Watching: The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including bald eagles, elk, and seals.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine is a delight to the taste buds and includes dishes such as:

  • Fresh seafood: Enjoy a variety of seafood dishes, including Pacific salmon, Dungeness crab, and Razor clams.
  • Huckleberry treats: These berries are unique to the Pacific Northwest Coast and can be found in pies, jams, and syrups.

Local Beverages

Craft breweries and wineries are popular in the region, and some of the local favorites include:

  • Fort George Brewery: This brewery is situated in a renovated warehouse and serves up an array of locally brewed beers.
  • Buoy Beer Co.: This brewery is located on the coast and offers visitors stunning views of the water as they enjoy a cold brew.

Where to Stay in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The park offers several accommodations, from campsites to hotels, that cater to travelers on a budget or those seeking luxury accommodations. Some popular options include:

  • The Astoria Riverwalk Inn: A comfortable hotel located in the heart of Astoria, offering scenic river views.
  • Fort Stevens State Park: This picturesque park offers visitors an array of lodging options, including safari tents and yurts.

What to Buy in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase souvenirs and locally made products at the following locations:

  • Museum stores: The Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center and Fort Clatsop National Memorial gift shops offer a wide variety of souvenirs and books.
  • Local seafood markets: Pick up some fresh seafood to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

The region is rich in history and culture, and visitors can learn about local customs and traditions at cultural festivals and events. Some popular events include:

  • Astoria Warrenton Crab, Seafood and Wine Festival: A wine and seafood festival that takes place every year in Astoria.
  • Scandinavian Midsummer Festival: A festival celebrating Scandinavian heritage with traditional music, crafts, and food.

Best Time to Visit Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

The best time to visit the park is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is pleasant and the days are long. Winter (December to February) can be cold and rainy, but visitors can still enjoy some winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Stay on designated trails
  • Pack plenty of water, sunscreen, and food

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or trash in the park
  • Feed the wildlife
  • Smoke in the park

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

The park is generally safe, but visitors should be aware of the following safety tips:

  • Watch for high tide and strong currents at the beach
  • Be cautious when hiking steep trails
  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife

Health in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Visitors should pack a first aid kit and be aware of the closest medical facilities in the area. The park also has several park rangers on staff who can provide assistance in case of any emergencies.

Getting to Lewis and Clark National Historical Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

The nearest international airports are Portland International Airport (PDX) and Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA).

Bus Stations Near Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station located in Astoria.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the nearest airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Lewis and Clark National Historical Park

Visitors can get around the park by car, bike, or on foot. The park also offers shuttle services during peak season.

Where to Go Next from Lewis and Clark National Historical Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The park is located in a region with many other destinations worth visiting, including:

  • Astoria: A bustling coastal town with many local attractions, including museums and restaurants
  • Tillamook: A beautiful coastal town famous for its cheese and ice cream

Conclusion

A trip to Lewis and Clark National Historical Park is a must-see for any outdoor enthusiast or history buff. With its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and endless activities, the park offers a unique experience that visitors will treasure for years to come.
