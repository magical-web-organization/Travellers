Introduction to Ozarks

Ozarks is a scenic region in the southern Midwest of the United States. It covers parts of Missouri, Arkansas, Oklahoma, and Kansas. Known for its abundant natural beauty, the Ozarks offer stunning mountain views, crystal-clear streams, diverse wildlife, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Besides, this region is rich in history and culture, making Ozarks a must-visit destination for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Ozarks

Ozarks have several must-visit places that offer something unique to every traveler. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • George Washington Carver National Monument: This national monument in Diamond, Missouri, honors the life and accomplishments of George Washington Carver, an African American scientist and inventor.
  • Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art: Located in Bentonville, Arkansas, this art museum boasts an impressive collection of American art, spanning five centuries. Its architecture is also worth admiring.
  • Wilson’s Creek National Battlefield: This site in Republic, Missouri, commemorates the Civil War battle of Wilson’s Creek, fought on August 10, 1861.

Natural Attractions in Ozarks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buffalo National River: This national river in northern Arkansas offers some of the best canoeing, fishing, and hiking opportunities in the Ozarks. The scenery is breathtaking, with towering bluffs and cascading waterfalls.
  • Table Rock Lake: Located in southwestern Missouri, this lake is a popular recreational spot, with plenty of opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming. The shoreline is dotted with resorts and marinas.
  • HaHa Tonka State Park: This state park in Camdenton, Missouri, features a series of natural bridges, caves, and sinkholes. Its most iconic landmark is a castle-like ruin of a late 19th-century mansion.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Ozarks

For outdoor enthusiasts, Ozarks offer a vast network of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy strolls to strenuous mountain treks. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Ozark Highlands Trail: This 218-mile-long trail in northern Arkansas is a favorite among hikers, with diverse terrain and stunning views. It passes through several state parks and wilderness areas.
  • Katy Trail State Park: This converted railway trail spans 240 miles across Missouri, from Machens to Clinton. It’s ideal for bicycling or walking, with easy access to towns and communities.
  • White River Valley Trail System: This network of trails in Branson, Missouri, offers over 12 miles of varied terrain for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The scenery includes forests, bluffs, and creeks.

Best Things to Do in Ozarks: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Ozarks have plenty of other activities and tours to offer. Here are some ideas:

  • Float Trips: Many rivers in the Ozarks, such as the Meramec, Current, and Elk, offer guided rafting or tubing trips, where you can enjoy the scenery and the thrill of the rapids.
  • Cave Exploration: Ozarks are a treasure trove of caves and caverns, with over 6,000 known caves. Guided tours are available at many sites, such as Onondaga Cave State Park or Fantastic Caverns.
  • Wildlife Watching: Ozarks are home to diverse species of wildlife, including elk, deer, bald eagles, and black bears. Several nature centers or wildlife refuges, such as the National Tiger Sanctuary or the Wonders of Wildlife Museum, offer an opportunity to get up close and learn about them.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ozarks

To truly experience the best of Ozarks, we suggest this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Branson, Missouri. Check into a hotel or resort and explore the city.
2 Take a scenic drive or hike in the Mark Twain National Forest. Visit the HaHa Tonka State Park.
3 Go on a float trip on the Current River. Visit the Big Spring State Park or Alley Spring Grist Mill Historic Site.
4 Drive to Eureka Springs, Arkansas. Visit the Thorncrown Chapel and Turpentine Creek Wildlife Refuge.
5 Hike or bike the Ozark Highlands Trail, or visit the Triple Falls and Hemmed-in-Hollow Falls.

Food and Drink Scene of Ozarks

Ozarks have a rich culinary tradition, with influences from Southern, Appalachian, and Ozarkian cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A Southern staple, catfish is often coated in cornmeal and deep-fried. It’s served with hushpuppies, coleslaw, and tartar sauce.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A quintessential Ozarkian breakfast dish, biscuits and gravy consist of fluffy biscuits smothered in a creamy white sauce made with sausage, milk, and black pepper.
  • BBQ: Ozarks’ BBQ is typically made with pork or beef, slow-cooked over oak or hickory wood. The meat is then slathered with a tangy sauce and served with sides like baked beans or cornbread.

Local Beverages

  • Ozark Beer Company: This brewery in Rogers, Arkansas, offers a selection of craft beers, ranging from pale ales to stouts. Their flagship beer is the American Pale Ale, made with locally sourced hops.
  • St. James Winery: This winery in St. James, Missouri, produces a variety of award-winning wines, such as Norton, Concord, and Chardonel. Their tasting room offers a scenic view of the vineyards.
  • Silver Dollar City’s Sippin’ Chili: This non-alcoholic beverage is a popular drink at the Silver Dollar City theme park. It’s made with apple cider, chili seasoning, and cinnamon sticks.

Where to Stay in Ozarks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ozarks offer a variety of accommodation options, ranging from modern hotels to rustic lodges. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Big Cedar Lodge: This wilderness retreat in Ridgedale, Missouri, offers luxurious cabins and lodges, along with a host of activities, such as fishing, boating, and golfing.
  • The Crescent Hotel: This historic hotel in Eureka Springs, Arkansas, is said to be haunted and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It offers stylish rooms and suites, a spa, and a rooftop bar.
  • Econo Lodge Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel in Fayetteville, Arkansas, provides comfortable rooms and suites, along with free Wi-Fi, breakfast, and parking.

What to Buy in Ozarks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ozarks have several unique items that make great souvenirs or gifts, such as:

  • Ozark handcrafts: Ozarkians are known for their skills in weaving, quilting, pottery, and woodcarving. Handcrafted items, such as baskets, blankets, or furniture, are available at many shops and galleries.
  • Ozark chocolates and candies: Ozarks have a sweet tooth, and several local shops, such as the Ferguson Candy Company in Springfield, Missouri, offer an array of candies, chocolates, and fudge.
  • Ozark wines and spirits: Besides St. James Winery, several other wineries and distilleries in Ozarks produce unique wines and spirits, such as the Copper Run Distillery in Walnut Shade, Missouri, or the Ozark Distillery in Osage Beach, Missouri.

Local Culture and Customs of Ozarks

Ozarks have a unique blend of cultural influences, from Native American to European to African American. Here are some aspects of Ozarkian culture and customs:

  • Music: Ozarkians have a rich musical heritage, ranging from old-time fiddle tunes to bluegrass to gospel. Ozarks host several music festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Eureka Springs Bluegrass Festival or the Silver Dollar City’s Bluegrass & BBQ Festival.
  • Folklore and Legends: Ozarks are known for their colorful myths, legends, and folklore, such as the tale of the “Boo Boo Hole,” a haunted swimming hole in Springfield, Missouri, or the legend of the “Baldknobbers,” a vigilante group that operated in the Ozarks in the 19th century.
  • Hospitality: Ozarkians are renowned for their friendly and welcoming nature. They take pride in their neighborly spirit and often go out of their way to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit Ozarks

The best time to visit Ozarks depends on your preferences and interests. Summers are hot and humid, but offer plenty of opportunities for water sports and outdoor events. Fall is the peak season for foliage viewing and festivals. Winter is relatively mild but offers fewer outdoor activities. Spring is ideal for hiking, biking, and wildlife watching. Just check the weather forecast before you visit, as Ozarks can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ozarks

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ozarks:

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and environment. Don’t litter or disturb natural habitats.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages. Ask the locals for recommendations.
  • Take your time and enjoy the scenic drives and outdoor activities.
  • Learn about the Ozarkian culture and history. Visit museums and attend festivals.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property. Follow the posted signs and respect the landowners’ rights.
  • Get too close to wild animals. Keep a safe distance and never approach them.
  • Ignore safety warnings. Ozarks have natural hazards, such as flash floods, rockfalls, or snake bites. Follow the safety guidelines and be prepared.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs. Avoid clichés and stereotypes.

Safety and Health

While Ozarks are generally safe and healthy, there are some precautions to take:

Safety in Ozarks

  • Be aware of natural hazards, such as flash floods, lightning strikes, or rockfalls. Check the weather forecast and the trail conditions before you go out.
  • Watch out for wildlife, especially snakes and ticks. Wear appropriate clothing and use insect repellent.
  • Be cautious on the roads, especially on winding mountain roads. Drive defensively and obey the speed limits.
  • Keep your belongings secure. Lock your car and store your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Ozarks

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during hot weather or physical activities.
  • Protect yourself from the sun. Wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses.
  • Watch out for poison ivy and other plants that can cause skin irritations or allergies.
  • Be prepared for emergencies. Carry a first aid kit and a phone with charged batteries.

Getting to Ozarks: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ozarks are accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Ozarks

  • Northwest Arkansas Regional Airport: Located in Bentonville, Arkansas, this airport offers direct flights to several domestic airlines.
  • Fort Smith Regional Airport: Located in Fort Smith, Arkansas, this airport offers direct flights to several regional destinations.

Bus Stations Near Ozarks

Other Options

  • Driving: Ozarks are easily accessible by car, with several highways and scenic routes connecting the various locations. Consider renting a car or driving your own.
  • Ridesharing: Several ridesharing companies, such as Uber or Lyft, operate in Ozarks. Check their websites for availability and fares.

How to Get Around in Ozarks

While driving is the most convenient way to get around Ozarks, other options are available:

  • Public Transportation: Several cities in Ozarks, such as Springfield, MO, or Fayetteville, AR, offer public transportation options, such as buses or trolleys. Check the local websites for routes and schedules.
  • Biking/Hiking: Ozarks have an extensive network of bike/walk trails, ranging from urban bike lanes to scenic nature trails. Consider renting a bike or walking to explore the surroundings.
  • Guided Tours: Several tour operators in Ozarks offer guided tours, such as the Branson Scenic Railway or the Ozark Mountain Adventure Tours. Check their websites for prices and itineraries.

Where to Go Next from Ozarks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Kansas City, Missouri: This vibrant city is known for its BBQ, jazz, and fountains. Visit the National World War I Museum or the Country Club Plaza.
  • Tulsa, Oklahoma: This cosmopolitan city has a rich Native American heritage, with several museums and galleries dedicated to their art and culture. Visit the Philbrook Museum of Art or the Gilcrease Museum.
  • Little Rock, Arkansas: This capital city has a charming downtown, with a riverfront promenade, a market, and several museums. Visit the Clinton Presidential Library or the Arkansas Arts Center.

Conclusion

Ozarks are a fascinating and breathtaking region, offering a mix of natural beauty, rich culture, and outdoor adventures. By following our guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, the most exciting activities to try, and the most delicious foods to savor. Be respectful, stay safe and healthy, and enjoy your Ozarkian adventure!
