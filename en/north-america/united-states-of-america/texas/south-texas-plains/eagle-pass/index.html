Introduction to Eagle Pass

Located in South Texas Plains, Eagle Pass is a city in the Maverick County of Texas, United States. It shares its border with Piedras Negras, a city in the Mexican state of Coahuila. Eagle Pass is named after its proximity to the Eagle Pass Creek and is known for its rich cultural heritage, outdoor activities, and friendly locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Eagle Pass

Eagle Pass has a diverse culture and customs influenced by the Spanish, Mexican, and Native American heritage. Visitors are advised to respect the local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Use “Sir” and “Ma’am” when addressing seniors
  • Tipping is customary
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites

Best Time to Visit Eagle Pass

The best time to visit Eagle Pass is from October to April when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be scorching and humid, so it’s ideal to avoid visiting during the hotter months.
