Introduction to Alice

Located in South Texas Plains, Alice is a small yet charming city known as the “birthplace of Tejano music.” It offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that attracts tourists from all over the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the must-visit places and experiences in Alice, Texas.

List of Places to Visit in Alice

Alice has a plethora of attractions to visit, including culturally important museums and historical places and natural attractions for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tejano Roots Hall of Fame Museum - A museum dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Tejano music and musicians.
  2. South Texas Museum - A museum showcasing the history of South Texas, including the struggles of the Native American tribes and the Spanish colonization.
  3. Jim Wells County Historical Museum - A museum with exhibits showcasing the history of Jim Wells County, including the oil boom of the early 20th century.
  4. Duval County Veterans Museum - A museum dedicated to honoring the men and women who have served in the Armed Forces.

Natural Attractions of Alice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Findley - A 50-acre freshwater lake that’s ideal for fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  2. Choke Canyon State Park - A park offering several recreational activities such as hiking, bird watching, camping, and fishing.
  3. Alice Nature Park - A park with a walking trail, picnic tables, a playground, and a pond.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alice

Alice offers several hiking/biking routes through its natural scenic beauty. The Alice Nature Park offers an easy hiking trail. For an advanced hiking trail, check out Choke Canyon State Park or the Brushy Creek Trail.

Best Things to Do in Alice: Activities and Tours

  1. Attend a Tejano music concert or festival.
  2. Visit the South Texas Museum.
  3. Go fishing at Lake Findley.
  4. Take a tour of the oil wells at the Jim Wells County Historical Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alice

Day 1: Visit the Tejano Roots Hall of Fame Museum and attend a Tejano music concert in the evening.

Day 2: Spend the day at Choke Canyon State Park and hike through the scenic trails.

Day 3: Visit the South Texas Museum and learn about the Spanish colonization of Texas.

Day 4: Relax by the Lake Findley and go fishing.

Day 5: Take a tour of the oil wells at the Jim Wells County Historical Museum.

Day 6: Explore the Alice Nature Park and enjoy a picnic.

Day 7: Visit Duval County Veterans Museum and honor the men and women who served in the Armed Forces.

Food and Drink Scene of Alice

Alice’s food and drink scene is rich with authentic Tex-Mex dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tamales - A savory dish wrapped in corn husks and filled with meat, beans, or cheese.
  2. Enchiladas - A dish made with tortillas, meat, cheese, and sauce.
  3. Fajitas - Grilled meat with peppers, onions, and tortillas.
  4. Chalupas - A crispy tortilla topped with beans, meat, cheese, and salsa.

Local Beverages

  1. Margaritas - A cocktail made with lime juice, tequila, and triple sec.
  2. Micheladas - A beer cocktail made with tomato juice, lime, hot sauce, and spices.

Where to Stay in Alice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hampton Inn Alice - A hotel located in the heart of the city with modern amenities.
  2. Alice Inn - A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and easy access to the city center.
  3. Best Western Executive Inn - A hotel with an outdoor pool and complimentary breakfast.
  4. Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites - A hotel with modern facilities, including a fitness center and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Alice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Tejano music CDs and merchandise from the Tejano Roots Hall of Fame Museum.
  2. Handcrafted jewelry and artwork from the local artisans.
  3. Spices and hot sauce from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Alice

Alice celebrates its rich cultural heritage through music, cuisine, and art. The majority of the population speaks Spanish and English.

Best Time to Visit Alice

The best time to visit Alice is from October to April when the weather is pleasant, and the tourist crowd is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alice

Do:

  1. Try the authentic Tex-Mex cuisine.
  2. Attend a Tejano music concert.
  3. Explore the natural attractions of Alice.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of Alice.
  2. Don’t share your personal information with strangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alice

Alice is a relatively safe city, but it’s always good to exercise precaution and stay alert, especially at night.

Health in Alice

Alice has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer excellent healthcare services.

Getting to Alice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alice

The closest airport to Alice is the Corpus Christi International Airport, about 43 miles away. The San Antonio International Airport is also within driving distance.

Bus Stations Near Alice

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Alice.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option to travel around Alice, as public transportation is not very popular.

How to Get Around in Alice

The best way to get around Alice is by car. Alice is a small city, so it’s easy to navigate, and parking is widely available.

Where to Go Next from Alice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Corpus Christi - A coastal city known for its beaches and water sports activities.
  2. San Antonio - A historic city with several attractions, including the Alamo.
  3. Laredo - A city with a unique blend of Mexican and American cultures.

Conclusion

Alice, Texas, is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its museums and natural attractions to its authentic Tex-Mex cuisine and rich cultural heritage, Alice has something for everyone. Plan your next trip to Alice and explore this charming city.
