Introduction to Neartown

Neartown is a neighborhood located in the heart of Houston, Texas. Known for its thriving nightlife, eclectic art scene, diverse cuisine, and dynamic culture, it is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.

This comprehensive Neartown travel guide aims to help travelers make the most of their time in this vibrant neighborhood. It covers all the essential information, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Neartown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Neartown has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston: One of the largest art museums in the United States, it has a vast collection of over 70,000 works of art from around the world.

  • Rothko Chapel: A nondenominational chapel with fourteen large-scale paintings by Mark Rothko.

  • The Menil Collection: A museum that houses the private art collection of John and Dominique de Menil, consisting of over 17,000 paintings, sculptures, and other artworks.

  • Houston Center for Photography: A non-profit organization dedicated to promoting photography as an art form.

  • The African American Library at the Gregory School: A historical library and research center that holds original documents and artifacts related to African American history in Houston.

Natural Attractions of Neartown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Neartown boasts several parks and outdoor spaces, perfect for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Buffalo Bayou Park: A 160-acre park with trails, a skate park, a dog park, and paddleboarding and kayaking opportunities.

  • Spotts Park: A hidden gem with hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the city skyline.

  • Montrose Boulevard: A tree-lined boulevard with several parks, outdoor sculptures, and walking and biking trails.

  • Houston Arboretum & Nature Center: A 155-acre nature reserve with hiking trails, a pond, and native plant gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neartown

Neartown is an excellent destination for cycling and hiking enthusiasts, boasting several scenic routes that offer a unique perspective of the city. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Buffalo Bayou Hike and Bike Trail: A 10-mile trail that follows the winding Buffalo Bayou and offers stunning views of the city skyline.

  • Brays Bayou Greenway Trail: A 16-mile trail that takes you through some of the most scenic locations in Houston, including Hermann Park, the Museum District, and Rice University.

  • Memorial Park Trail: A 2.93-mile loop trail that offers a challenging workout for runners and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Neartown: Activities and Tours

Neartown offers many exciting activities and tours that cater to all interests. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Houston Museum District Walking Tour: Explore the neighborhood’s rich cultural heritage on a walking tour that takes you through some of the best museums and historical landmarks.

  • Food Tours of Houston: Enjoy the diverse cuisine of Neartown on a food tour that takes you to some of the best restaurants and cafes in the area.

  • Kayaking on Buffalo Bayou: Experience the beauty of Buffalo Bayou on a guided kayaking tour that takes you through some of the city’s most scenic spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neartown

If you plan to spend a week in Neartown, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Museum Day - Visit the Museum of Fine Arts, Houston, The Menil Collection, and the Museum of Natural Science.

Day 2: Outdoor Adventure Day - Go kayaking on Buffalo Bayou, hike the Memorial Park Trail, and visit the Houston Arboretum & Nature Center.

Day 3: Food Tour Day - Take a food tour of Houston, exploring the diverse cuisine of Neartown.

Day 4: Art and Culture Day - Visit Rothko Chapel, The Houston Center for Photography, and The African American Library at the Gregory School.

Day 5: Shopping and Entertainment Day - Explore the eclectic shops and boutiques on Westheimer Road, followed by a night of entertainment at one of the many theaters and music venues.

Day 6: Historical Landmarks Day - Visit the Houston Museum of African American Culture, the Holocaust Museum, and the Houston Fire Museum.

Day 7: Relaxation Day - Enjoy a leisurely day at one of the many parks in Neartown, followed by a relaxing spa treatment at a nearby hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Neartown

Neartown’s food and drink scene is as diverse as its culture. From traditional Texan barbecue to exotic fusion cuisine, there’s something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texan-style barbeque: Brisket, ribs, and sausage are all staples of Texan barbeque and can be found at several restaurants throughout Neartown.

  • Vietnamese pho: Neartown has a sizeable Vietnamese community, and the neighborhood is home to several renowned Vietnamese restaurants that serve delicious pho.

  • Tacos al pastor: A popular Mexican street food, tacos al pastor are made with marinated pork, pineapple, cilantro, and onions, making them a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  • St. Arnold Brewing Company beer: A local favorite, St. Arnold Brewing Company is the oldest craft brewery in Texas.

  • Fruitcup wines: A refreshing local beverage made from fruit and served over ice.

  • Kolaches: A pastry that originated in the Czech Republic and has become a local Houston specialty.

Where to Stay in Neartown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Neartown that cater to various budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • La Colombe d’Or: A luxurious boutique hotel that exudes old-world charm and is home to an impressive art collection.

  • The Westin Houston: A contemporary hotel that offers comfortable rooms and state-of-the-art amenities.

  • HI Houston: The Morty Rich Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable dorms and private rooms.

  • Robin’s Nest Bed and Breakfast Inn: A beautiful guesthouse that offers a cozy and intimate atmosphere.

What to Buy in Neartown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Neartown has plenty of shops and boutiques that offer unique souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Artwork from local artists: Neartown has a thriving art scene, and several galleries and shops sell original artwork by local artists.

  • Vintage clothing and accessories: The neighborhood has several stores that specialize in vintage clothing and accessories, offering a unique shopping experience.

  • Cowboy hats and boots: A quintessential Texan souvenir, cowboy hats and boots can be found at several shops in Neartown.

Local Culture and Customs of Neartown

Neartown has a diverse population, and its culture and customs reflect this. Some of the things to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for diversity: Neartown is a melting pot of cultures and ethnicities, and it’s essential to respect the diversity of the community.

  • LGBTQ+ friendly: Neartown is known for its LGBTQ+ friendly community and is home to several gay bars and establishments.

Best Time to Visit Neartown

The best time to visit Neartown is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. The neighborhood is bustling with activity during these months, with several cultural events and festivals taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neartown

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts when visiting Neartown:

  • Visit the museums: Neartown has several world-class museums that are worth a visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neartown

Neartown is a relatively safe neighborhood, but it’s essential to take certain precautions. Some tips to ensure your safety include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: It’s essential to be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

  • Avoid carrying large sums of cash: It’s advisable to carry only the necessary cash and avoid wearing expensive jewelry or carrying expensive electronic devices.

  • Secure your valuables: Keep your valuables in a safe place, and avoid leaving them unattended.

Health in Neartown

Neartown has several hospitals and medical centers that offer quality healthcare services. In case of an emergency, dial 911.

Getting to Neartown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neartown

Neartown is located approximately 20 minutes from the Houston Hobby Airport and 30 minutes from the George Bush Intercontinental Airport.

Bus Stations Near Neartown

The neighborhood is serviced by several bus stations, including the Metropolitan Transit Authority of Harris County and Greyhound Bus Lines.

Other Options

Aside from buses, taxis, and Uber, bike rentals and car rentals are also available in Neartown.

How to Get Around in Neartown

Neartown is a relatively walkable neighborhood and is easy to navigate on foot. Bike rentals and car rentals are also available for those who prefer to explore on wheels.

Where to Go Next from Neartown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Neartown is located in the heart of Houston and is a gateway to several nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the must-visit places include:
