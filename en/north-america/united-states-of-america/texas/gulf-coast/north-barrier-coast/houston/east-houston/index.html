Introduction to East Houston

East Houston is a bustling city that is home to over 90,000 residents. The city was founded in the early 1900s and has a rich history that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. East Houston is also famous for its stunning natural beauty, from the sparkling Gulf waters to the lush greenery of the nearby parks and nature preserves. Whether you are looking for an outdoor adventure or a cultural experience, East Houston has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in East Houston

If you are planning a trip to East Houston, there are several must-visit attractions that you should include on your itinerary. Here are some of the top places to see and things to do in East Houston.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East Houston has a rich history that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. Here are some of the top attractions that should be on every visitor’s list:

  • San Jacinto Battleground State Historic Site: This historic site is the location of the famous Battle of San Jacinto, which was the decisive battle of the Texas Revolution. Visitors can take a guided tour of the battlefield and visit the San Jacinto Monument, which stands over 500 feet tall and is the tallest monument in the world.
  • The Heritage Society: The Heritage Society is a non-profit organization that is dedicated to preserving the history of Houston’s diverse communities. Visitors can take a tour of the society’s museum, which is housed in a beautiful 19th century mansion.
  • The Houston Museum of Natural Science: The Houston Museum of Natural Science is one of the most popular attractions in the city and features a wide range of exhibits, including an extensive collection of dinosaur fossils and a planetarium.

Natural Attractions of East Houston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Houston is home to several nature parks and reserves that offer visitors the chance to get up close and personal with some of the region’s most stunning natural scenery. Here are some of the top natural attractions in East Houston:

  • Armand Bayou Nature Center: The Armand Bayou Nature Center is a 2,500-acre nature preserve that is home to over 370 species of birds, mammals, reptiles, and amphibians. Visitors can take guided boat tours, hike the many nature trails, and explore the center’s many exhibits and displays.
  • Galveston Island: Galveston Island is located just a short drive from East Houston and is a popular destination for beachgoers and nature lovers alike. The island is home to several nature reserves, including the Galveston Island State Park, which features several miles of pristine beaches and hiking trails.
  • Houston Arboretum and Nature Center: The Houston Arboretum and Nature Center is a 155-acre nature reserve that is located in the heart of the city. The reserve features several hiking trails, a nature center with interactive exhibits, and a butterfly garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Houston

East Houston is a great destination for hikers and bikers, with several scenic trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Buffalo Bayou Trail: The Buffalo Bayou Trail is a 10-mile paved trail that winds through the heart of the city. The trail features several scenic overlooks and is a great option for bikers and hikers of all skill levels.
  • The San Jacinto Battleground Trail: The San Jacinto Battleground Trail is a 5-mile trail that takes visitors on a tour of the historic battleground site. This trail is a great option for history buffs and nature lovers alike.
  • The Armand Bayou Nature Center Trail: The Armand Bayou Nature Center Trail is a 5-mile hiking trail that takes visitors through several diverse habitats, including wetlands and forests. The trail is a great option for hikers who want to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in East Houston: Activities and Tours

In addition to its many attractions and natural wonders, East Houston offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in East Houston:

  • Visit the Space Center Houston: The Space Center Houston is a must-visit attraction for anyone who is interested in space exploration and science. Visitors can take a guided tour of the center, explore interactive exhibits, and see real spacecraft and artifacts.
  • Take a Neighborhood Food Tour: East Houston is home to a diverse array of restaurants and eateries, from classic Texas barbecue joints to trendy international cuisine spots. Taking a neighborhood food tour is a great way to sample some of the area’s culinary delights.
  • Catch a Houston Texans Game: If you are a fan of football, catching a Houston Texans game at NRG Stadium is a must-do. The stadium is located just a short drive from East Houston and hosts several games each season.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Houston

If you have a week to spend in East Houston, here is a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in the city:

Day 1: Visit the San Jacinto Battleground State Historic Site and the San Jacinto Monument.

Day 2: Explore the Houston Museum of Natural Science and the Hermann Park Conservancy.

Day 7: Spend the day at the Galveston Island State Park, exploring the nature trails and soaking up the sun on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of East Houston

East Houston is known for its diverse culinary scene, which includes everything from classic Texas barbecue to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you should sample during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texas barbecue: No visit to East Houston is complete without trying some classic Texas-style barbecue. Some of the area’s top barbecue restaurants include Pappa Charlies Barbeque and Pinkerton’s Barbecue.
  • Crawfish boil: Crawfish boils are a popular local tradition in East Houston. Head to Crawfish Shack or BB’s Cafe for some of the best crawfish in town.
  • Tex-Mex: Tex-Mex cuisine is a staple of the Houston food scene. Be sure to try dishes like fajitas, enchiladas, and queso at local restaurants like El Tiempo Cantina and Ninfas.

Local Beverages

  • St. Arnold Brewing Company: St. Arnold Brewing Company is a local craft brewery that produces a wide range of award-winning beers. Visitors can take a brewery tour and sample some of the company’s latest creations.
  • Lemonade: East Houston is known for its refreshing and tangy lemonade. Be sure to try a cup at local spots like the Lemonade Company.
  • Sweet tea: Sweet tea is a popular beverage in Texas, and East Houston is no exception. Order a glass at local spots like Republic House.
