Introduction to West Houston

Located in the North Barrier Coast region of Texas and overlooking the Gulf Coast, West Houston is a thriving city that offers a blend of modern amenities and natural beauty. The city is home to a myriad of tourist attractions, from museums and historical landmarks to lush parks and outdoor adventures. It attracts a diverse range of visitors, from foodies and shopaholics to outdoor enthusiasts and culture buffs. In this comprehensive tour guide for West Houston, we will explore all the must-visit places to go, things to do, and local culture.

List of Places to Visit in West Houston

Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, West Houston has plenty to offer. From art and history museums to theme parks and botanical gardens, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in West Houston:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history or culture buff, West Houston has a rich legacy waiting to be explored. A few must-visit museums and historical landmarks include:

  • Houston Museum of Natural Science: This world-class museum is home to a vast collection of specimens, including dinosaur fossils, gems, and minerals. A visit to the museum is not complete without exploring its Butterfly Center, Planetarium, and the Cockrell Butterfly Center.
  • Buffalo Soldiers National Museum: The museum celebrates the contributions of African American soldiers in America’s wars, from the 19th century through present-day. The extensive collection of exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays will transport you to another era.
  • The Heritage Society: A historic open-air museum located in the heart of downtown Houston, The Heritage Society gives you a glimpse of Houston’s past. The museum’s ten historic buildings, including a 19th-century church, a prairie-style mansion, and a log cabin, offer an immersive experience of Houston’s cultural history.

Natural Attractions of West Houston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

West Houston’s natural beauty, parks, and outdoor attractions will leave you mesmerized by their serene landscape. Here are some of the popular natural attractions of West Houston:

  • Houston Arboretum & Nature Center: Located inside Memorial Park, the nature center features five miles of walking trails that take you through native Texas habitats. The center also offers a variety of educational programs for visitors of all ages.
  • George Bush Park: One of the largest parks in Houston, George Bush Park offers activities like fishing, biking, hiking, and wildlife viewing.
  • Terry Hershey Park: This park is perfect for biking and jogging enthusiasts, with nearly eleven miles of trails that follow Buffalo Bayou and offer scenic views along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Houston

West Houston offers a host of hiking and biking trails that cater to both beginners and experienced adventurers. Here are some of the top hiking/biking trails of West Houston:

  • Memorial Park: With over two miles of carefully maintained trails, Memorial Park is a perfect destination for biking and jogging enthusiasts.
  • Brays Bayou Greenway Trail: The trail stretches for over thirty miles, following Brays Bayou from the UH main campus all the way down to the Buffalo Bayou Trail.
  • White Oak Bayou Greenway Trail: This trail spans about seven miles from the Heights to the North Loop.

Best Things to Do in West Houston: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and spending time outdoors, West Houston has plenty of exciting activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are some of the things you must do while in the city:

  • Space Center Houston: A must-visit attraction for anyone interested in space exploration, Space Center Houston is the official visitor center for NASA’s Johnson Space Center. The center offers a range of attractions, including simulators and exhibits, giving visitors a glimpse of NASA’s space program.
  • Houston Zoo: The Houston Zoo features over 6,000 animals across 900 species, making it one of the most visited attractions in the city. The zoo also offers several interactive experiences, such as feeding the giraffes and petting the animals.
  • Discovery Green: Spanning twelve acres in the heart of downtown Houston, Discovery Green offers entertainment, art, and culinary delights.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Houston

A week-long visit to West Houston can be planned with a mix of outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and some downtime. Here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the best things to do in the city:

Day 1: Visit the Houston Museum of Natural Science, the Cockrell Butterfly Center, and the Planetarium.

Day 2: Spend a day exploring Memorial Park, where you can bike or jog along the trails.

Day 3: Visit the Space Center Houston and take a behind-the-scenes tour of NASA’s Johnson Space Center.

Day 4: Go on a shopping spree to the famous Galleria Mall and explore the mall’s high-end retail stores.

Day 5: Enjoy a day at the Houston Zoo and get up close with the various animals.

Day 6: Have a relaxing day by visiting the Houston Arboretum & Nature Center and spend time walking along the lush trails.

Day 7: Explore the city’s food scene and sample local cuisine in some of its top-rated restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of West Houston

West Houston has a thriving food and drink scene that caters to every taste and budget. Whether you’re in the mood for healthy eats or indulgent cuisine, there’s a restaurant for you. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ Brisket: Texas is famous for its BBQ, and West Houston is no exception. Head to CorkScrew BBQ for some of the best smoked brisket in the city.
  • Authentic Mexican Tacos: Visit La Reyna Tortilleria for tasty street tacos that come with handmade tortillas and fresh salsa.
  • Crawfish: Head to The Seafood Spot for authentic Louisiana-style crawfish, boiled to perfection with a mix of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Saint Arnold Brewing Company: Houston’s oldest craft brewery, Saint Arnold Brewing Company, offers visitors a taste of local beer in its taproom.
  • Locally Roasted Coffee: Sip on locally roasted coffee at Blacksmith, a hip café located in the heart of Montrose.
  • Sweet Tea: Get the taste of Southern-style sweet tea at The Teahouse.

Where to Stay in West Houston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Houston offers a variety of options for staying, whether you’re looking for the ultimate luxury experience or a budget-friendly option. Some of the popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city include:

  • The Westin Houston: A five-star hotel located in the heart of the Energy Corridor, the Westin Houston offers luxury accommodation and world-class amenities.
  • HI Houston: The Morty Rich Hostel: A budget-friendly option, the hostel offers shared and private rooms, lounge areas, and a communal kitchen.
  • The Whitehall Houston: A classic hotel located downtown, the Whitehall offers elegant suites, a fitness center, and a rooftop pool.

What to Buy in West Houston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

West Houston offers a variety of shopping options for tourists, from high-end retail stores to local boutiques. If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some places to visit:

  • Houston Texans Team Store: If you’re a sports fan, then a visit to the Houston Texans Team Store is a must. The store offers Texans’ merchandise, including jerseys, hats, and other sports-related items.
  • Market Square: Located in the heart of the city, Market Square is the perfect destination for those looking to explore the local culture. The market offers a range of locally made products, including jewelry, clothing, and art.
  • Houston Galleria Mall: One of the largest retail establishments in the city, Houston Galleria Mall offers luxury-brand boutiques and high-end stores for shoppers.

Local Culture and Customs of West Houston

West Houston is known for its welcoming and friendly people, who take pride in their culture and traditions. As a visitor, it’s important to respect the local customs and etiquette. A few things to keep in mind include:

  • Eye Contact: Making eye contact is considered a sign of respect in West Houston culture.
  • Politeness: West Houston is known for its friendly and courteous people, so make sure to be polite and respectful in your interactions.
  • Greeting: It’s customary to greet people with a warm and friendly smile in West Houston, especially if it’s your first time meeting them.

Best Time to Visit West Houston

The best time to visit West Houston is between March and May when the city experiences pleasant weather and beautiful blooms. The summer months (June to August) can be quite hot and humid, with temperatures soaring above 90 degrees Fahrenheit. The winter season (December to February) can be chilly, with temperatures dipping below freezing. However, this is also the time when you can expect a range of Christmas and New Year’s events.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Houston

When visiting West Houston, there are some do’s and don’ts that you should keep in mind to ensure a smooth and enjoyable vacation.

Do:

  • Keep an Eye on Your Belongings: West Houston is a big city, and petty theft can happen, so make sure you keep an eye on your belongings in public spaces.
  • Learn Some Basic Spanish: While English is the primary language spoken in West Houston, many locals speak Spanish. Knowing some basic Spanish phrases can help you communicate better with the locals.
  • Carry Sun Protection: The Texas sun is fierce; make sure you carry sun protection, such as sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses.

Don’t:

  • Litter: West Houston is committed to being a green city, so littering is heavily frowned upon.
  • Over-Tip: While tipping for good service is appreciated, over-tipping can be seen as wasteful, so make sure to tip moderately.
  • Disrespect Local Customs and Traditions: West Houston is a city that values its culture and traditions, so it’s important to respect them.

Safety and Health

West Houston is one of the safest cities in Texas, with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to take care of your belongings and be aware of your surroundings. As for health, West Houston has excellent medical facilities, including world-class hospitals and clinics.

Safety in West Houston

  • Take Precautions Against Mosquitos: West Houston is known for its humid climate, which makes it an ideal breeding ground for mosquitos. Make sure you carry mosquito repellent with you.
  • Be Careful While Driving: Houston has a reputation for heavy traffic, so make sure you familiarize yourself with local driving laws and regulations.

Health in West Houston

  • Stay Hydrated: The Houston climate can be quite humid, especially during summer months, so make sure you stay hydrated by carrying water with you.
  • Pack All Necessary Medications: If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, ensure that you pack all necessary medications and medical supplies.

Getting to West Houston: Transportation Options and Logistics

West Houston is well connected by air, road, and rail, making it easily accessible from anywhere in the world. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near West Houston

  • George Bush Intercontinental Airport: The airport serves domestic and international destinations and is located 22 miles north of Houston.
  • William P. Hobby Airport: The airport primarily serves domestic destinations and is located 7 miles south of downtown Houston.

Bus Stations Near West Houston

  • Houston Greyhound Station: The station offers a range of bus services and is located in downtown Houston.
  • Houston Megabus Station: The station offers daily bus services to several destinations across Texas and the neighboring states.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: There are several Amtrak train stations in Houston that serve major cities across the country.
  • Car Rental Services: For visitors looking to explore the city on their terms, car rental services are readily available.

How to Get Around in West Houston

West Houston’s public transportation system comprises of buses, trains, and bikes. The Metropolitan Transit Authority of Harris County (METRO) oversees the city’s bus and train system, while bike-sharing services are provided by BCycle. Taxis and ride-share services are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from West Houston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While West Houston has plenty to offer tourists, nearby destinations and excursions are worth exploring. Here are a few options:

  • Galveston Island: Located just 50 miles south of Houston, Galveston Island offers a range of attractions, including beaches, amusement parks, and historical landmarks.
  • San Antonio: Located about three hours from Houston, San Antonio offers several cultural landmarks, such as the Alamo and the River Walk.
  • Austin: The state capital of Texas, Austin is known for its eclectic culture, live music, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

West Houston offers a blend of modern amenities and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for leisure travelers. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re ready to explore and experience everything West Houston has to offer, from outdoor adventures and cultural experiences to shopping and nightlife activities. Remember to respect local customs, take care of your belongings, and have fun exploring this North Barrier Coast gem.
