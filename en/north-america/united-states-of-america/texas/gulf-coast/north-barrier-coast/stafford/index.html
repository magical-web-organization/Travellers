Introduction to Stafford

Located in North Barrier Coast, on the Gulf Coast of Texas, Stafford is a charming city known for its rich history, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and welcoming locals. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Stafford. This travel guide will help you plan your trip by highlighting the top places to visit, things to do, and local secrets you won’t want to miss.

List of Places to Visit in Stafford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stafford Centre: This contemporary performing arts center hosts a variety of shows, concerts, and events throughout the year. Check their schedule for upcoming performances.
  • Fort Bend County Library: Stafford Branch: This public library offers a wide range of books, comics, and multimedia resources, as well as free Wi-Fi and classes for all ages.
  • Sugar Land Heritage Museum & Visitor Center: Learn about the history of sugar cane cultivation and the impact it had on the region’s economy and society. The museum has interactive exhibits, artifacts, and archives.
  • Fort Bend Children’s Discovery Center: Perfect for families with kids, this interactive museum has a science lab, a play area, and educational exhibits on culture, art, and nature.

Natural Attractions of Stafford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buffalo Run Park: This expansive park offers hiking and biking trails, fishing ponds, and picnic areas. Don’t miss the views from the observation tower overlooking the Brazos River.
  • Brazos Bend State Park: This state park is a short drive from Stafford and boasts 37 miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for camping, fishing, and wildlife watching. Keep an eye out for alligators, deer, and birds.
  • George Ranch Historical Park: Step back in time and experience life in a Texan ranch in the 1800s. The park has historic buildings, live demonstrations of farm chores and cowboy skills, and horseback rides.
  • Houston Museum of Natural Science at Sugar Land: This museum features fascinating exhibitions on science, space, and natural history, as well as a planetarium and a butterfly center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stafford

Stafford and its surroundings offer fantastic opportunities for outdoor activities, from leisurely strolls to challenging hikes. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Oyster Creek Trail: This 4-mile paved trail runs from Sugar Land to Missouri City and passes through scenic parks, wetlands, and neighborhoods. Good for cycling, jogging, and dog walking.
  • Brazos Bend State Park Trails: With 37 miles of trails, this park has routes for all skill levels, from easy nature walks to backcountry camping. Check the park map and watch for wildlife.
  • Brazoria National Wildlife Refuge: This large preserve has several hiking trails, including the 1-mile Hornsby Bend Trail, which leads to a bird observation deck. Bring binoculars and bug spray.
  • Stafford City Park Trail: This 1-mile trail around the city’s largest park is perfect for a morning jog or an afternoon stroll. There are playgrounds, basketball courts, and a pond with fishing docks.

Best Things to Do in Stafford: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a solo traveler or part of a group, there are plenty of fun and unique things to do in Stafford. Consider these options:

  • Kayak or Canoe on the Brazos River: Rental companies offer guided tours or self-guided trips down the scenic Brazos River. Paddle through lush forests, sandbars, and calm waters.
  • Take a Ghost Tour in Old Town Spring: About an hour’s drive from Stafford, this charming historic district offers ghost tours, antique shops, and restaurants. Hear spooky tales and legends while strolling through the Victorian architecture.
  • Visit the Houston Space Center: Only a half-hour drive from Stafford, this iconic center offers intriguing exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays on space exploration and NASA’s mission. Don’t miss the tram tour of the Johnson Space Center.
  • Attend a Rodeo or a Fair: Depending on the season, Stafford and the surrounding towns host rodeos, fairs, and festivals. Check the local events calendar for dates and tickets. Some popular events include the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo and Fort Bend County Fair.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stafford

For a full week of fun and relaxation in Stafford and its vicinity, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Sugar Land Heritage Museum & Visitor Center and the Fort Bend County Library. Have lunch at Fountains on the Lake.
2 Hike the Oyster Creek Trail and have a picnic at Brazos Bend State Park.
3 Take a kayak or canoe trip on the Brazos River and see the sunset at Buffalo Run Park.

5 Explore the George Ranch Historical Park and have a cowboy-style barbecue.

Food and Drink Scene of Stafford

Stafford offers a variety of dining options, from Tex-Mex to seafood, and from casual to fine dining. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pecan Pie: A classic Southern dessert made of pecans, sugar, butter, and eggs. Some restaurants serve it with ice cream or whipped cream.
  • Barbecue Brisket: A Texan specialty made of slow-cooked beef brisket, seasoned with dry rub or marinade, and served with barbecue sauce and sides such as coleslaw and baked beans.
  • Fajitas: A sizzling dish of marinated beef or chicken served on a hot skillet, accompanied by grilled onions and peppers, tortillas, and condiments such as salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.
  • Crawfish Boil: A Cajun-style feast of crawfish, corn, potatoes, and spices, boiled together and served in a large pot with Cajun seasoning and beer.

Local Beverages

  • Shiner Beer: A popular Texan beer made in Shiner, Texas, since 1909. The flagship brew is Shiner Bock, a dark lager with smooth caramel notes.
  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing and sweetened iced tea, commonly served in the Southern United States. Some restaurants add lemon or mint to it.
  • Margarita: A classic cocktail made of tequila, lime juice, and triple sec, served on the rocks or blended with ice. Some variations include fruit flavors such as strawberry or mango.

Where to Stay in Stafford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer a cozy bed and breakfast or a large hotel chain, Stafford has a variety of lodging options for all budgets and styles. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Houston/Sugar Land: Located in the Sugar Land area, this modern and comfortable hotel offers free Wi-Fi, a fitness center, and a restaurant. Rooms have flat-screen TVs and mini-fridges.
  • Residence Inn Houston Sugar Land: A great option for extended stays, this hotel has spacious suites with full kitchens, separate living areas, and free breakfast. There’s a pool, a gym, and a barbecue area on site.
  • Best Western Plus Sugar Land: This affordable hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and free parking. There’s a pool, a hot tub, and a gym for guests.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Houston-Stafford: This all-suite hotel has a convenient location near the Stafford Centre and offers free breakfast, free Wi-Fi, and a pool. Rooms have kitchens and sofa beds.

What to Buy in Stafford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Stafford without grabbing some local goodies and souvenirs. Here are some ideas:

  • Texas Pecans: These nuts are a delicacy in the Lone Star State and come in various flavors, such as salted, roasted, or candied. Look for them in local grocery stores or specialty shops.
  • Cowboy Hats: Get yourself a classic Stetson or a modern rancher hat from a Western wear store or a souvenir shop. They come in different colors, sizes, and materials.
  • Texan Flag Merchandise: Show off your Lone Star pride with a flag T-shirt, mug, keychain, or magnet. Look for them in souvenir shops or online retailers.
  • Bluebonnet Seeds: Bring a piece of Texas’ state flower home and start your own bluebonnet garden with seeds from a local nursery or online seller.

Local Culture and Customs of Stafford

When visiting Stafford, it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some tips:

  • Tipping: Tipping is common in the United States, especially in the service industry. Restaurant staff, hotel maids, and tour guides usually expect a 15-20% tip based on the total bill.
  • Hospitality: Texans are known for their warm and friendly hospitality. Don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you or offer to help you out.
  • Weather: Texas can have extreme weather conditions, from hot and dry to humid and stormy. Check the weather forecast and pack accordingly, including sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Attire: Dress codes can vary depending on the occasion and the venue. Casual dress is acceptable in most places, but some restaurants and events may require more formal attire.

Best Time to Visit Stafford

The best time to visit Stafford is from March to May or from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, with temperatures reaching the 90s, while winters can be chilly, with occasional freezing temperatures and precipitation. Keep in mind that some attractions may have reduced hours or closures during holidays or special events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stafford

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Stafford:

Do:

  • Do try Tex-Mex cuisine, a fusion of Texan and Mexican flavors that’s popular in the region.
  • Do explore the city’s parks and trails, which offer scenic views and recreational opportunities.
  • Do attend a rodeo or a festival, which are deeply rooted in the local culture and draw crowds from all over the state.
  • Do respect the wildlife and natural environment, especially in state parks and wildlife refuges.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or vandalize public places, which can result in fines and penalties.
  • Don’t drink and drive, which is highly illegal and dangerous.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent, especially if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Don’t underestimate the distances and traffic between Stafford and other cities or attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stafford

Overall, Stafford is a safe and welcoming city for visitors. However, like in any urban area, there are some precautions to take:

  • Crime: Petty theft and car break-ins can occur, especially in areas with dense tourist traffic. Keep your valuables in sight and your car locked. Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Traffic: Some areas of Stafford can have heavy traffic, especially during rush hour or on weekends. Plan your routes in advance and be patient while driving.
  • Weather: Texas can have extreme weather conditions, including thunderstorms, tornadoes, and hurricanes. Pay attention to local weather alerts and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Stafford

Stafford has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. However, it is recommended that visitors have travel insurance and take basic health precautions:

  • Vaccinations: Make sure your routine vaccinations are up to date, and consider getting vaccinated against hepatitis A and B, depending on your travel plans and activities.
  • Sun Protection: The sun in Texas can be strong and harmful, especially during the summer. Wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses when outdoors.
  • Water Safety: If swimming or boating, be aware of the water conditions and follow safety rules. Some areas may have strong currents, deep drop-offs, or hidden obstacles.

Getting to Stafford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stafford

Stafford is located about 30 minutes drive from the Houston William P. Hobby Airport (HOU) and about 50 minutes drive from the George Bush Intercontinental Airport (IAH). Both airports have domestic and international flights, as well as shuttle buses, taxis, and car rental services.

Bus Stations Near Stafford

The nearest Greyhound bus station is in downtown Houston, about 25 minutes drive from Stafford. Some local buses and shuttles run between Stafford and Houston, but they have limited schedules and routes.

Other Options

Some visitors to Stafford may arrive by car, as the city is conveniently located near major highways such as Interstate 69 and Highway 90. There are also private shuttles and taxis available for hire, as well as ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft.

How to Get Around Stafford

While Stafford is relatively small and walkable, some attractions and destinations may require a car or public transportation. Here are some options:

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies, such as Enterprise and Hertz, have locations in Stafford and nearby cities. Check rates and availability in advance.
  • Public Transportation: The Fort Bend Express bus service connects Stafford to downtown Houston and some suburbs. There are also local buses and shuttles run by Fort Bend County Transit, but they have limited schedules and routes.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing: There are several taxi companies and ride-sharing apps, such as Uber and Lyft, available in Stafford and nearby areas. Some hotels and attractions may offer shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Stafford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stafford is conveniently located near several other attractions and destinations worth exploring. Here are some examples:

  • Galveston Island: A popular Gulf Coast beach town, with historic architecture, seafood restaurants, and family-friendly attractions such as Moody Gardens and Schlitterbahn Water Park.
  • Houston: The fourth-largest city in the United States, Houston is a hub of culture, arts, sports, and cuisine. Highlights include the Houston Museum of Natural Science, the Museum of Fine Arts, and the NASA Space Center.
  • San Antonio: About a three-hour drive from Stafford, San Antonio is a historic city with a lively Riverwalk, the Alamo, and world-famous Tex-Mex food.
  • Austin: The state capital of Texas, about two and a half hours drive from Stafford, Austin is known for its music scene, food trucks, and outdoor activities such as kayaking and hiking.

Conclusion

Stafford, Texas, is a great destination for visitors who want to experience Texan hospitality, history, nature, and culture. With its variety of attractions and activities, as well as its convenient location near other great destinations, Stafford is a perfect place for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and discover the best of Stafford and its surroundings.
