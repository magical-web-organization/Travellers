Introduction to Orange

Located in the heart of Bayous, in Gulf Coast, Texas, Orange is a beautiful and vibrant city that is known for its rich history, cultural significance, and natural beauty. This small town is truly a hidden gem, with plenty to offer for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food lover, Orange has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Orange

Orange is home to some of the most culturally significant and historically important attractions in the United States. From museums dedicated to the city’s past to beautiful natural parks, here are some of the places you should visit during your trip to Orange:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stark Museum of Art: This museum boasts an impressive collection of American Western and Wildlife art. The museum also features a vast collection of rare books and manuscripts.

  • W.H. Stark House: This historical house, built in 1894, belonged to William Henry Stark, a prominent businessman in the lumber industry. Today, visitors can tour the house and learn about its rich history.

  • Shangri La Botanical Gardens and Nature Center: This beautiful botanical garden and nature center is located in the heart of Orange. The garden features a wide variety of plants and wildlife, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Orange: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sabine River: This scenic river forms a natural border between Texas and Louisiana and provides visitors with plenty of outdoor adventures, including kayaking, boating, and fishing.

  • Mauriceville Heritage Association Community Park: This 15-acre community park features several miles of hiking trails and is surrounded by beautiful lush greenery, making it a perfect place for a picnic or a hike.

  • Blue Elbow Swamp Preserve: Located just outside of Orange, this beautiful swamp is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including alligators and turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orange

For outdoor enthusiasts, Orange offers a wide variety of hiking and biking routes. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced hiker, there’s a trail for you. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Orange:

  • Shangri La Botanical Gardens Loop Trail: This 1.5-mile loop trail takes you through the beautiful botanical garden and offers stunning views of the surrounding natural beauty.

  • Blue Elbow Swamp Preserve Trail: This 2.5-mile trail takes hikers through the beautiful swamp and offers plenty of opportunities for wildlife viewing.

  • Mauriceville Heritage Association Community Park Trail: This 3.5-mile trail takes hikers through the lush greenery of the park and offers stunning views of the Sabine River.

Best Things to Do in Orange: Activities and Tours

Orange offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors. From historical tours to outdoor adventures, here are some of the best things to do in Orange:

  • Take a Historical Tour of W.H. Stark House: This guided tour takes visitors through the historic house and provides an in-depth look at its rich history.

  • Go Fishing in Sabine River: Sabine River is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities to catch catfish and other fish.

  • Take a Kayaking Tour of Sabine River: For a more adventurous experience, visitors can take a kayaking tour of the Sabine River and explore the beautiful natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Orange

Day 1: Visit the Stark Museum of Art and learn about the history of American Western and Wildlife art.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the W.H. Stark House and learn about its rich history.

Day 3: Explore the beautiful surroundings of Blue Elbow Swamp Preserve and go wildlife viewing.

Day 4: Take a kayaking tour of Sabine River and explore the natural beauty of the area.

Day 5: Visit the Shangri La Botanical Gardens and Nature Center and explore the beautiful botanical garden.

Day 6: Take a hike on the Mauriceville Heritage Association Community Park Trail and enjoy stunning views of the Sabine River.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the many spas in Orange.

Food and Drink Scene of Orange

Orange offers a wide variety of local dishes and beverages that are not to be missed. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Orange:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gumbo: This traditional Cajun soup is a local favorite in Orange.

  • Fried Catfish: Sabine River is home to plenty of catfish, making it a popular local dish in the area.

  • Boudin: This spicy sausage is a popular local delicacy in Orange.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: This southern classic beverage is a popular choice in Orange.

  • Orange Blossom: This cocktail, made with gin and orange juice, is a refreshing drink that is perfect for a hot summer day.

Local Culture and Customs of Orange
