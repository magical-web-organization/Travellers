Introduction to Goliad

Goliad is a small city in the South Barrier Coast region of the Gulf Coast of Texas, USA. It is known for its rich history that dates back to the Spanish colonial era and the Texan Revolution. Today, Goliad offers visitors a mix of cultural, historical, and natural attractions.

Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Goliad has something to offer. In this comprehensive tour guide, we have compiled a list of places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and where to eat to make your Goliad experience unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Goliad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Presidio La Bahia

Presidio La Bahia is one of the most significant historical sites in Goliad. It was an important Spanish fort during the colonial period and later served as a Mexican garrison during the Texan Revolution. Today, it is a museum that showcases the history of the presidio and the role it played in shaping Goliad’s past. Visitors can see the chapel, courtyard, and several exhibits showcasing the fort’s history.

Goliad State Park and Historic Site

Goliad State Park and Historic Site is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in history and nature. It is the site of the Goliad Massacre, one of the darkest events in Texas history. Visitors can explore the park’s trails and learn about its flora and fauna, while also reflecting on the historical significance of the site.

Fannin Battleground State Historic Site

The Fannin Battleground State Historic Site marks the site of the last battle of the Texan Revolution. Visitors can see the memorial, the battlefield, and a replica of the Texan camp. The site also has a visitor center where you can learn more about the history of the battle and the war.

Natural Attractions of Goliad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Goliad Paddling Trail

The Goliad Paddling Trail is a perfect way to experience the beauty of the San Antonio River. It is a 6.3-mile trail that starts at Goliad State Park and ends at the Goliad Water Tower. Visitors can rent kayaks or canoes and enjoy the scenic views of the river and its surrounding landscape.

Goliad Market Trail

The Goliad Market Trail is a unique way to explore the local area’s agriculture and farming heritage. It is a self-guided tour that takes visitors to several farms and markets where they can buy fresh produce, interact with the farmers, and learn about the local agricultural practices.

Goliad Wildlife Management Area

The Goliad Wildlife Management Area is a 2,200-acre park that offers visitors opportunities to hunt, fish, hike, and camp. It is home to several species of wildlife, including deer, turkey, and quail, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goliad

Goliad Loop Trail

The Goliad Loop Trail is a 9.3-mile hiking trail that starts and ends at the Goliad State Park. It offers visitors stunning views of the San Antonio River and its surrounding lush forests.

Historical Goliad Bike Trail

The Historical Goliad Bike Trail is a 10-mile bike trail that takes visitors through Goliad’s historic sites and beautiful countryside. It is a perfect way to combine a scenic bike ride with a historical tour of the city.

Best Things to Do in Goliad: Activities and Tours

Goliad Ghost Tours

Goliad Ghost Tours is a unique way to explore the city’s spooky past. Visitors can join a walking tour that takes them to several haunted sites, such as the hanging tree and the old Goliad Jail.

Texas Heroes Square

Texas Heroes Square is a beautiful park that celebrates Goliad’s heroes from the Texan Revolution. Visitors can see several statues and monuments dedicated to Texas heroes like James Fannin, William Barret Travis, and Sam Houston.

Goliad Brewery

Goliad Brewery is a local craft brewery that produces some of the best beer in Goliad. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery, sample their beers, and enjoy the brewery’s fun and relaxed atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Goliad

Must-Try Dishes

Chicken Fried Steak

Chicken Fried Steak is a classic dish in Texas, and Goliad serves up some of the best. It is a battered and fried steak served with cream gravy and mashed potatoes.

Choke Canyon Barbecue

Choke Canyon Barbecue is a local barbecue joint that serves up some of the best brisket, ribs, and sausage in Goliad. Their homemade sauce is a must-try.

Local Beverages

Goliad Brewing Company Beer

Goliad Brewing Company produces some of the best craft beer in Goliad. Visitors to the brewery can sample their beers and enjoy their relaxed taproom.

Mesquite Bean Wine

Mesquite Bean Wine is a unique local delicacy made from the beans of the mesquite tree. It has a sweet and crisp taste and is a perfect alternative to traditional wines.

Where to Stay in Goliad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn on Angelina

The Inn on Angelina is a charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Goliad. It features comfortable rooms, a garden, and a patio where guests can relax.

Best Western Plus Goliad Inn & Suites

The Best Western Plus Goliad Inn & Suites is a modern hotel that offers guests spacious and comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Goliad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hand-Crafted Pottery

Goliad is known for its hand-crafted pottery, which makes for a beautiful and unique souvenir or gift.

Mesquite Wood Products

Mesquite is abundant in the Goliad area, and visitors can find several shops selling mesquite wood products such as cutting boards and bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Goliad

Goliad has a rich history and culture that is deeply rooted in the Texan Revolution and its Spanish Colonial past. Visitors should be respectful of the city’s historical significance and cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Goliad

The best time to visit Goliad is in the fall and winter months when the weather is cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goliad

Do:

  • Visit the historical sites and museums
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the city’s historical and cultural heritage

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the natural areas
  • Disrespect the local community

Safety and Health

Safety in Goliad

Goliad is a safe city, but visitors should always take standard safety precautions such as being aware of their surroundings and belongings.

Health in Goliad

Visitors should ensure they have travel insurance and be up to date on their routine vaccinations before visiting Goliad.

Getting to Goliad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Goliad

The nearest airports to Goliad are Corpus Christi International Airport and San Antonio International Airport, both of which are approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Goliad

Greyhound buses stop at both Corpus Christi and San Antonio bus stations. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Goliad.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Goliad from other cities within Texas or take a private car service.

How to Get Around in Goliad

The best ways to get around Goliad are by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car from one of the several car rental agencies or borrow a bike from their hotel or bed and breakfast.

Where to Go Next from Goliad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Victoria

Victoria is a charming, small city located just 30 minutes away from Goliad. It is known for its stunning architecture, local cuisine, and cultural attractions.

Corpus Christi

Corpus Christi is a thriving coastal city that is just an hour’s drive away from Goliad. Visitors can enjoy a range of water activities, visit museums, or relax on the beach.

Conclusion

Goliad is a hidden gem in Texas that offers visitors a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. By following this tour guide, visitors can learn about Goliad’s history, explore its natural beauty, and experience the local culture and cuisine. Goliad is a small town with a big heart, and visitors are sure to fall in love with this charming Texan city.
