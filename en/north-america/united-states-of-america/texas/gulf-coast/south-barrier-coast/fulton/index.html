Introduction to Fulton

Fulton is a charming coastal town located in South Barrier Coast, in the Gulf Coast region of Texas. The town attracts visitors from all over the country for its beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, museums, historical sites, and delicious seafood. Whether you are coming for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Fulton has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and more. Read on to plan your perfect trip to Fulton, Texas.

List of Places to Visit in Fulton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fulton Mansion State Historic Site: Built in 1877, the Fulton Mansion is a beautiful example of Second Empire architecture. It features original furniture, paintings, and clothing from the late 1800s and early 1900s. The guided tour takes visitors through 12 rooms, including the library and music room.
  • Rockport Center for the Arts: This non-profit organization showcases local art, hosts workshops for adults and children, and holds events throughout the year. The center also features a sculpture garden overlooking Aransas Bay.
  • Texas Maritime Museum: This museum highlights the state’s rich maritime history, from Spanish exploration to modern oil drilling. The museum features hands-on exhibits, a theater, and a research center.

Natural Attractions of Fulton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Goose Island State Park: This 321-acre state park is known for its 1,620-year-old Big Tree, one of the largest live oak trees in Texas. Visitors can take a self-guided nature trail, fish, boat, or camp out in one of the 44 campsites.
  • Aransas National Wildlife Refuge: This 114,657-acre wildlife refuge is home to many species of birds, including the endangered whooping crane. Visitors can take a driving tour, hike, fish, or hunt (in designated areas).
  • Fulton Beach Park: This park offers a great view of Aransas Bay, as well as picnic tables, a fishing pier, and a playground. Visitors can also rent kayaks and paddleboards from the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fulton

  • South Texas Botanical Gardens & Nature Center: This 182-acre nature center features walking trails, gardens, and exhibits of native South Texas flora and fauna. Visitors can also take a guided birding tour.
  • Copano Bay Fishing Pier State Park: This state park features a fishing pier and a walking trail along Copano Bay. Visitors can also fish and picnic in the park.

Best Things to Do in Fulton: Activities and Tours

  • Bay Fishing and Dolphin Watch Tour: Take a guided boat tour of the Aransas Bay and discover dolphins, local seabirds, and the many species of fish.
  • Sea Turtles Inc.: Visit the Sea Turtles Inc. rescue and rehabilitation center in South Padre Island and learn about endangered sea turtles.
  • Texas Wine Trail: Take a scenic drive through Texas’s wine country and visit local wineries for tastings and tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Fulton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shrimp and Grits: A classic southern dish featuring shrimp and creamy grits, often served with a spicy tomato sauce.
  • Fish Tacos: Freshly caught fish served in a tortilla with lettuce, tomatoes, and spicy sauce.
  • Oysters: Raw, grilled, or fried, oysters are a local specialty in Fulton.

Local Beverages

  • Texas Beer: Try one of the many local craft beers, such as Saint Arnold’s or Shiner Bock.
  • Margarita: Relax with a refreshing margarita, made with fresh lime juice and tequila.
  • Sweet Tea: A southern classic, sweet tea is a perfect complement to hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Fulton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lighthouse Inn: A Victorian-style inn featuring 78 rooms and a pool, located in downtown Rockport.
  • The Fulton Mansion Historic Inn: A luxury bed and breakfast located in a restored 19th-century mansion, featuring four rooms overlooking the Aransas Bay.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: A modern hotel offering 80 rooms, located just a short drive from the town center.

What to Buy in Fulton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Texas Scents: These scented candles, featuring local Texas fragrances, are a perfect souvenirs to bring home.
  • Beachwear: Shop for beachwear and accessories at one of the many local boutiques.
  • Local Art: Support local artists by purchasing a piece of art at the Rockport Center for the Arts.

Local Culture and Customs of Fulton

Fulton’s culture is heavily influenced by its coastal location. The town is known for its friendly locals, seafood restaurants, and outdoor activities. Visitors are encouraged to dress comfortably and bring sunscreen and bug spray. While Texas is known for its conservative values, Fulton and the surrounding areas tend to be more liberal, especially when it comes to environmental issues.

Best Time to Visit Fulton

The best time to visit Fulton is during the fall (September to November) or the spring (March to May), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer (June to August) can be extremely hot and humid, and winter (December to February) can be rainy and cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fulton

Do:

  • Do try the local seafood.
  • Do bring sunscreen and bug spray.
  • Do respect the locals’ privacy.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beach.
  • Don’t forget to wear comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fulton

Fulton is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when it comes to swimming in the Gulf of Mexico and hiking in natural areas. Follow all posted warnings and watch out for strong currents, rip tides, and venomous snakes.

Health in Fulton

Visitors should drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration, especially during the hot summer months. Mosquitoes can be a problem in the area, so wear protective clothing and use insect repellent.

Getting to Fulton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fulton

  • Corpus Christi International Airport: Located 43 miles from Fulton, this airport offers connections to major US airports.
  • San Antonio International Airport: Located 145 miles from Fulton, this airport offers connections to major US airports and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Fulton

  • Greyhound: Located 10 miles from Fulton, this bus station offers service to major Texas cities.
  • Omnibus Express: Located 10 miles from Fulton, this bus station offers service to major Texas cities.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at both the Corpus Christi and San Antonio airports.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the town center and can be arranged through local hotels.

How to Get Around in Fulton

Fulton is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Bicycles can be rented at the South Texas Botanical Gardens & Nature Center or the Fulton Mansion State Historic Site. Taxis are available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Fulton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rockport: This nearby town features a beach, dining, and shopping options, as well as the Rockport Center for the Arts.
  • Corpus Christi: This bustling coastal city features museums, aquariums, shopping, and dining options, as well as a beautiful beach.
  • San Antonio: This cultural hub is known for its history, architecture, shopping, and dining options, as well as the Alamo and the River Walk.

Conclusion

Fulton is a hidden gem on the Texas coast, offering beautiful beaches, outdoor activities, museums, and delicious seafood. With our comprehensive guide, you can plan the perfect trip to Fulton, whether it’s a weekend getaway or a week-long adventure. Don’t forget to try the local shrimp and grits!
