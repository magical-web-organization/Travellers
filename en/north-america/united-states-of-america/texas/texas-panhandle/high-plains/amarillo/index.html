Introduction to Amarillo

Located in the Texas Panhandle, Amarillo is known for its big skies, western heritage, and natural beauty. With its fascinating history, incredible outdoor attractions, and unique culture, Amarillo is a destination that has something to offer any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Amarillo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amarillo has an intriguing history that reflects its position on the frontier and the influences of various cultures. Visitors who want to know more about the city’s past can’t miss the following museums and historical places:

  • American Quarter Horse Hall of Fame and Museum: This museum celebrates the American Quarter Horse and its contributions to Western culture. It’s perfect for horse lovers and history buffs eager to learn more about the breed’s history.

  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park: Visitors can explore the second-largest canyon system in the United States. This park offers numerous outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and camping. At the center of the park is the Visitor Center.

  • The Railroad Museum of Texas: This museum tells the story of the railroad’s role in shaping Texas’s history. A fascinating collection of train artifacts and memorabilia is displayed in the two-story museum.

Natural Attractions of Amarillo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors and enjoy exploring all that nature has to offer, Amarillo has plenty to inspire you. To make the most of the natural attractions of Amarillo, visit the following locations:

  • Palo Duro Canyon: Located just outside of Amarillo is the epic outdoor playground of Palo Duro Canyon. Visitors can hike, bike, or drive through the rugged terrain and marvel at the canyon’s beauty.

  • Alibates Flint Quarries: For history and nature lovers, the Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument is a must-see. The site features historical artifacts and is an excellent place to learn more about the indigenous people who lived in the area long ago.

  • Lake Meredith: This lake provides an abundance of recreational activities, including sailing, camping, fishing, and hiking. It’s the perfect place to relax and get lost in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amarillo

Amarillo is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, especially for hikers and bikers looking for challenging routes and breathtaking views. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Amarillo:

  • Lighthouse Trail: The Lighthouse Trail is a six-mile round trip hike that is moderately difficult. Hikers can enjoy the gorgeous views of the Palo Duro Canyon and reach the highlight of the trail, the Lighthouse formation.

  • Comanche Trail: The Comanche Trail follows the shore of Lake Meredith for 2.6 miles. It is a well-maintained trail that is easy to hike and bike. The tranquil lake views provide the perfect backdrop for a relaxing excursion.

  • John Stiff Memorial Park Trail: The John Stiff Memorial Park Trail is an easy, 2.6-mile paved trail that weaves through John Stiff Memorial Park. It’s the perfect trail for beginners, and the beautiful scenery is sure to impress.

Best Things to Do in Amarillo: Activities and Tours

Amarillo has plenty of opportunities to indulge in activities and tours that cater to any interests. Check out the following must-do activities and tours in Amarillo:

  • The Big Texan Steak Ranch: Challenge your appetite with the 72-ounce steak challenge at the Big Texan Steak Ranch. It’s an iconic Amarillo attraction that has been featured on TV multiple times.

  • Texas Camel Safari: The Texas Camel Safari provides a unique and unforgettable outdoor excursion in the Texas Panhandle. The tour includes a guided trip through scenic sand dunes while riding camels.

  • Helium Memorial Park: In this quirky attraction, visitors can learn about the history of helium in Amarillo, which even has its own helium monument.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amarillo

Amarillo offers a perfect mixture of history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures catering to everyone’s interests. To help you make the most of your time and experience the best of what Amarillo has to offer, here’s a seven-day itinerary:

  • Day 1: Palo Duro Canyon State Park
  • Day 2: American Quarter Horse Hall of Fame and Museum
  • Day 3: Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument
  • Day 4: Visit Route 66 landmarks and the Amarillo downtown area
  • Day 5: Lake Meredith and the Canadian River Breaks
  • Day 6: The Big Texan Steak Ranch and Cadillac Ranch
  • Day 7: Texas Camel Safari tour and Helium Monument

Food and Drink Scene of Amarillo

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Amarillo is famous for its steaks and Tex-Mex cuisine. Here are some of the best must-try dishes you should enjoy in Amarillo:

  • 72-Ounce Steak: The Big Texan Steak Ranch offers the challenge of eating a 72-ounce steak along with other dishes. It’s a must-try dish for those who want to enjoy delicious food and a unique experience.

  • Chicken Fried Steak: Chicken Fried Steak is a Texan staple and a must-try when in Amarillo. Featuring juicy, marinated, and breaded steak, this dish is a culinary delight that you’ll never forget.

  • Tex-Mex Food: Amarillo is known for its Tex-Mex food scene, which features a fusion of Texan and Mexican culture. Visitors must try some of the delicious Tex-Mex food available, including authentic salsa, tacos al pastor, and burritos.

Local Beverages

Amarillo has plenty of great drinks to help quench your thirst. Some of the best local beverages include:

  • Beer: With plenty of locally brewed beer options, Amarillo offers good options for beer enthusiasts. Some of the breweries include Long Wooden Spoon Brewing, Six Car Pub, and Pondaseta Brewing Co.

  • Margaret: Margaret is a frozen drink that Amarillo natives love. It’s a combination of Sangria and Margarita.

  • Dr Pepper: Dr Pepper is a staple in Texas and Amarillo, and this locally produced drink is a must-try for visitors.

Where to Stay in Amarillo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amarillo offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference. Some of the best places to stay in Amarillo include:

  • Drury Inn & Suites: This hotel offers complimentary breakfast, Wi-Fi, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

  • Red Roof Inn Amarillo West: This budget-friendly hotel features comfortable rooms and a location close to many of Amarillo’s top attractions.

  • Hostel on Van Buren: This hostel is perfect for travelers on a budget. Located in the heart of Amarillo’s historic cannabis district, it offers a clean, comfortable, and secure accommodation option.

What to Buy in Amarillo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amarillo offers unique shopping experiences, and visitors can take home a range of souvenirs and locally produced products. Some of the best things to buy in Amarillo include:

  • Cowboy Boots: Amarillo is known for their boot makers, and visitors can take home a pair of handmade cowboy boots made from high-quality leather and suede.

  • Texan Food Specialties: Visitors can bring home bags or jars of homemade snickerdoodle cookies, homemade jerky, or a bottle of Texas-style barbecue sauce.

  • Antiques and Route 66 memorabilia: Antique shops and vintage stores line Amarillo’s historic Route 66 district, making it a must-visit spot for visitors looking to pick up some Americana.

Local Culture and Customs of Amarillo

Amarillo has a rich western heritage and a friendly, welcoming culture. Visitors should keep the following cultural customs and traditions in mind:

  • Respect for National Parks and Monuments: There are a lot of National Parks and Monuments in Amarillo, and visitors should treat these cultural and natural treasures with the utmost respect.

  • Cowboy Culture and Rodeos: Amarillo’s cowboy culture is central to the city’s identity. Visitors should respect and embrace this culture by visiting the American Quarter Horse Hall of Fame and Museum and attending local rodeos.

Best Time to Visit Amarillo

The best time to visit Amarillo is during the spring and fall months. In the spring, the city is in full bloom, and the wildflowers are breathtaking. During the fall, the city’s mild temperatures provide the perfect weather for enjoying outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amarillo

Do:

  • Visit the Cadillac Ranch: The Cadillac Ranch is a must-visit destination that epitomizes the quirky spirit and free-spiritedness of Amarillo.

  • Try the Texan Steaks and Tex-Mex food: Amarillo is famous for its food, and its steaks and Tex-Mex dishes are must-tries.

  • Explore the outdoors: Amarillo offers an array of outdoor activities that will make any adventure enthusiast feel right at home.

Don’t:

  • Litter or Damage Natural Attractions: Visitors should respect nature and preserve its natural resources to ensure the preservation of Amarillo’s pristine wild places.

  • Overlook the Local Culture: The cowboy culture in Amarillo is an essential part of the city’s identity and should be embraced and celebrated.

  • Underestimate the UV exposure: Visitors are advised to be careful of the sun’s harsh rays and bring sunglasses, sunscreen, and a hat to protect against the UV exposure.

Safety and Health

Amarillo is a safe and healthy community, but visitors should keep the following tips in mind:

Safety in Amarillo

  • Stick to well-lit areas at night: Visitors should stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid any deserted or dark alleyways.

  • Store valuables in safe locations: Visitors should store their valuables in safe locations, such as lockers provided by their accommodation.

  • Be mindful of road safety: Visitors must drive cautiously and be aware of their surroundings while driving, as Amarillo is known for its hectic traffic.

Health in Amarillo

  • Stay hydrated: Visitors should drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, particularly during the hot summer months.

  • Protect against ticks: When hiking or exploring the outdoors, visitors should wear long-sleeved shirts, pants, and bug spray to protect themselves against ticks.

  • Be aware of high altitudes: Visitors should be aware of the high altitude in Amarillo and take proper precautions to prevent altitude sickness.

Getting to Amarillo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amarillo has several transportation options that make getting around the city easy and convenient.

Airports Near Amarillo

  • Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA): This airport is only 15 minutes from downtown Amarillo and offers a range of flights to many destinations around the United States.

Bus Stations Near Amarillo

  • Greyhound: This bus station is located in the downtown area and offers affordable and comfortable transportation to destinations nationwide.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent cars from several major car rental companies at the airport or downtown area to explore the city on their own.

How to Get Around in Amarillo

Amarillo is a car-centric city, and most visitors prefer to rent a car or use taxi services to get around. Public transportation options are also available, including the Amarillo City transit, which offers a bus service throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Amarillo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Amarillo, there are numerous nearby destinations and excursions that make incredible additions to any trip plan. Some of the best places to visit near Amarillo include:

  • Santa Fe, New Mexico: This historic city is known for its incredible art scene and adobe architecture.

  • Balloon Fiesta Park, Albuquerque: Balloon Fiesta Park is the perfect place to enjoy breathtaking balloon festivals, especially during the fall months.

  • Pecos National Park, Texas: Pecos National Park is filled with spectacular natural scenery, including canyons, mesas, and unique rock formations.

Conclusion

Amarillo is a must-visit location for travelers looking to experience the best of Texas. From incredible natural beauty and outdoor adventures to the cowboy culture and delicious cuisine, Amarillo has a little something for everyone. Plan your trip today, and experience the magic of the Texas Panhandle!
