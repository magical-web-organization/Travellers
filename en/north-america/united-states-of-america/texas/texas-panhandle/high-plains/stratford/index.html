Introduction to Stratford

Located in the heart of High Plains, in Texas Panhandle, Stratford is a small, but charming town that boasts a rich history and stunning natural beauty. This hidden gem is a perfect destination for adventurers looking to explore the great outdoors, history buffs interested in learning more about the Wild West, and foodies eager to try out delicious regional dishes.

With this comprehensive travel guide, you will discover all the best places to visit, things to do, and food to try in Stratford.

List of Places to Visit in Stratford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sherman County Depot Museum - Explore the history of the town and the Texas Panhandle through artifacts, exhibits, and photographs in the restored train depot.
  • C.H. Collins Athletic Complex - Visit the historic sports complex that served as the summer home of the Chicago Cubs for 32 years.
  • Georgia O’Keeffe’s Canyon - Take a tour of the canyon where world-renowned artist, Georgia O’Keeffe, painted some of her most iconic landscapes.

Natural Attractions of Stratford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Meredith National Recreation Area - Go boating, fishing, hiking, and wildlife watching in this stunning natural area that boasts over 200 square miles of land and water.
  • Optima National Wildlife Refuge - Get up close and personal with the wildlife of the Texas Panhandle, including migratory birds, prairie dogs, and bison.
  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park - Hike, bike, or horseback ride through the second-largest canyon in the United States, known for its dramatic red-rock formations and stunning vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stratford

  • Lake Meredith Hiking Trail - This 7.5-mile trail takes you through the beautiful Lake Meredith National Recreation Area, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscape.
  • Comanche Trail - Discover the history of the Comanche tribes as you hike this 2.5-mile trail that passes by ancient rock formations and historical sites.
  • Paseo Del Rio Trail - Bike or walk along this 2.7-mile trail that follows the Canadian River and offers scenic views of the riverbanks and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Stratford: Activities and Tours

  • Wildcat Extreme Off-Road Adventure - Get your heart pumping as you drive an off-road vehicle through the rugged terrain of the Texas Panhandle.
  • XIT Museum - Learn more about the legendary XIT Ranch and the cowboy way of life at this fascinating museum.
  • Texas Panhandle Birding Tours - Explore the diverse bird species of the Texas Panhandle with a guided bird-watching tour led by experienced guides.

Food and Drink Scene of Stratford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak - Try this hearty Texan dish made with a tenderized beef cutlet that is battered and fried to perfection.
  • Frito Pie - This Texas favorite is a mixture of Fritos, chili, and cheese, baked together for a delicious and savory dish.
  • Texas Chili - Enjoy the flavors of the Wild West with this spicy and meaty stew that is perfect for cold winter nights.

Local Beverages

  • Texas Wine - Sample some of the delicious wines from the nearby High Plains Wine Region, which is known for its bold reds and crisp whites.
  • Local Craft Beer - Try some of the locally-brewed beer from the many microbreweries that call the Texas Panhandle home.

Where to Stay in Stratford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Stratford Inn - This cozy hotel offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to many of Stratford’s top attractions.
  • The Inn at Palo Duro Canyon - Stay in a cozy cabin or tent and wake up to stunning views of the Palo Duro Canyon.
  • The Red Room Guesthouse - This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Stratford’s historic district and offers a unique and comfortable experience.

What to Buy in Stratford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • XIT Ranch Memorabilia - Take home a piece of Texas history with items from the legendary XIT Ranch.
  • Stratford Downtown Goods - Browse the unique shops in Stratford Downtown and find the perfect souvenir to remember your trip.
  • High Plains Wine - Bring home a bottle or two of the delicious wines produced in the nearby High Plains Wine Region.

Local Culture and Customs of Stratford

Stratford prides itself on its strong cowboy culture and Western traditions. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the laid-back and friendly atmosphere of the town, which values honesty, integrity, and good old-fashioned hard work.

It is also important to note that many towns in the Texas Panhandle are dry, meaning that the sale and consumption of alcohol is prohibited. Visitors should be aware of these laws and plan accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Stratford

The best time to visit Stratford is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and dry, while winters can be cold and windy, so it is important to pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stratford

Do:

  • Embrace the cowboy culture and tradition of the town.
  • Enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the Texas Panhandle.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Texas Chili and High Plains Wine.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s dry laws, which prohibit the sale and consumption of alcohol.
  • Disrespect the local way of life and customs.
  • Leave your trash in the outdoor areas, always clean up and dispose of it properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stratford

Stratford is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should take care when exploring the outdoors and participating in outdoor activities. Always wear appropriate clothing and sun protection, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Stratford

Visitors should pack any necessary medication, and be aware that medical facilities in Stratford and the surrounding areas can be limited.

Getting to Stratford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stratford

The closest airport to Stratford is Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport, which is approximately 80 miles away. We recommend renting a car or taking a bus to get to Stratford from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Stratford

Greyhound offers regular bus service to nearby towns such as Dumas and Dalhart, which are both within an hour’s drive from Stratford.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies with locations near the airport and in nearby towns. Visitors can also opt to drive their own vehicle to Stratford, which is easily accessible from Highway 287.

How to Get Around in Stratford

The best way to get around in Stratford is by car or by bike. Many of the town’s top attractions are located outside of the town center and are best reached by car. Bikes are a great option for exploring the downtown area and nearby trails.

Where to Go Next from Stratford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amarillo - Explore the vibrant culture and unique attractions of this nearby city, known for its historic Route 66 landmarks, cowboy culture, and delicious steak.
  • Pueblo - Discover the rich history and stunning natural beauty of this nearby town, located in the heart of Colorado.
  • Oklahoma City - Head east and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture, arts, and history of this bustling city.

Conclusion

Stratford is a hidden gem of the Texas Panhandle, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are a fan of outdoor adventures, history, or great food, Stratford has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect trip and make unforgettable memories.
