Introduction to Memphis

Located in the High Plains of the Texas Panhandle in the United States of America, Memphis is a small town with a big punch! With a population of just over 2,000 people, Memphis is an oasis of charm with plenty of activities, adventure, and places to explore for its visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Memphis

Whether you’re interested in culture, history, nature, or just want to find some fun activities to enjoy during your stay, Memphis has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture enthusiasts, Memphis presents a plethora of options for exploration and discovery. The following are some of the most culturally significant attractions to check out during your trip:

  • Memphis Heritage Museum - Take a step back in time and explore the history and heritage of Memphis in this fascinating museum.
  • Hall County Historical Museum - Discover the history of the Texas Panhandle and Hall County with a visit to this engaging museum.
  • Ozark Trail Station - Built in 1910 as a stop on the Ozark Trail, this station is now a museum and community center showcasing the history of the area.

Natural Attractions of Memphis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Memphis has plenty of natural attractions to explore. The following are some of the best outdoor destinations to visit in and around Memphis:

  • Caprock Canyons State Park - Hiking, biking, camping, and horseback riding are just a few of the outdoor activities that this state park offers. This 15,314-acre park is a popular spot for family outings and overnight camping.
  • Lakeview Park - A popular spot for fishing and boating, Lakeview Park is also home to an RV park and campground with plenty of outdoor recreational opportunities.
  • Water Tower Park - An excellent place for hiking, biking, and catching breathtaking views, the Water Tower Park’s trails offer a true taste of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Memphis

Memphis is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore during your trip:

  • The Lakeview Park Trail: This 1.5-mile hiking trail takes you through the scenic countryside of Memphis.
  • Caprock Canyons Trailway: This 64-mile hiking and biking trail is a must-visit for experienced hikers and bikers, with mesmerizing views of the rugged canyons and striking scenery.

Best Things to Do in Memphis: Activities and Tours

There are lots of fun and exciting things to do in Memphis. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try out during your stay:

  • Visit Trewett’s Sweet Shop - A Memphis staple since 1929, Trewett’s Sweet Shop is a must-visit for candy lovers of all ages.
  • Visit the Memphis Theatre - Don’t miss a chance to catch a performance at the historic Memphis Theatre, which has been showcasing live shows for over a century.

Week-Long Itinerary for Memphis

If you’re looking for an itinerary to follow during your week-long visit to Memphis, this is what we suggest:

Day of the Week Activity
Monday Visit the Memphis Heritage Museum
Tuesday Hike the Caprock Canyons Trailway

Food and Drink Scene of Memphis

No visit to Memphis is complete without trying out some of the local delicacies and staples. Here are some of the dishes and drinks you should try out:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bar-B-Q - Memphis-style Bar-B-Q is world-famous. Don’t miss out the chance to try it at either Snappy’s BBQ or Joe’s BBQ.
  • Fried Chicken - Memphis is famous for its crispy and flavorful Southern fried chicken dishes. Stop by the Dixie Pig for some finger-licking good chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Dr Pepper - A well-loved soda brand in Texas, grab a bottle of Dr. Pepper as part of your food and drink exploration in Memphis.

Where to Stay in Memphis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For accommodations, Memphis offers a range of options to fit any budget and taste. Here are some top choices:

  • Quality Inn Memphis - Located at the heart of Memphis, Quality Inn is the best option for budget travelers looking for a comfortable stay.
  • Memphis Lodge and Suites - For mid-range accommodation with all essential amenities, Memphis Lodge and Suites is a great choice.
  • Holiday Inn Express and Suites - If you’re looking for a more upscale option, the Holiday Inn Express and Suites is an excellent choice to try out.

What to Buy in Memphis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some souvenirs to bring back home? Here are some great options to check out:

  • Memphis T-shirts and Caps - Take home a memento of your visit to Memphis by grabbing a T-shirt and cap of the town.
  • Bar-B-Q Sauce - Bring home the taste of Memphis with some Bar-B-Q sauce as a souvenir to share with your friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Memphis

Memphis, like most of the Southern United States, is an area rich in culture and traditions. Conservative values, hospitality, and a laidback lifestyle are hallmarks of the region.

Best Time to Visit Memphis

The best time to visit Memphis is from April to May and September to October. The mild weather during these months makes it the perfect time to explore the outdoor attractions of Memphis.

Do’s and Don’ts of Memphis

When visiting Memphis, here are some essential things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs - As with any new place, always try to be respectful of the local customs and values.
  • Try out the Local Cuisine - Don’t miss out experiencing the local culture and traditions through its food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Be Loud and Disruptive - Be mindful of other people’s peace and quiet, and don’t create unnecessary noise or disturbances.
  • Be Rude or Disrespectful - Be kind and polite to everyone, especially to local residents, and respect their values and traditions.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Memphis, ensure that you take adequate measures to stay safe and healthy during your stay.

Safety in Memphis

Memphis is a relatively safe place to visit, and violent crime is not common. However, petty crime can sometimes occur, so always be mindful of your belongings and bags when out in public.

Health in Memphis

Ensure that you follow general health precautions, such as washing your hands frequently and wearing mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Memphis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Memphis

The two nearest airports to Memphis are:

  • Amarillo International Airport (AMA)
  • Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA)

Bus Stations Near Memphis

The closest bus stations to Memphis are:

  • The Greyhound in Amarillo, TX
  • The Trader’s Station Bus Stop

Other Options

Visitors can choose to rent a car or take a taxi to Memphis. However, there are limited ride-sharing services available in the city.

How to Get Around in Memphis

Driving is the most popular way to get around Memphis. For those who don’t have a car, taxis are readily available.

Where to Go Next from Memphis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to spare after exploring Memphis, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out:

  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park - Located just a few miles south of Amarillo, Palo Duro Canyon State Park is the second-largest canyon in the United States, an ideal spot for hiking, biking, horseback riding, and other outdoor activities.
  • Route 66 – A historic roadway in North America, Route 66 is a popular scenic drive that includes Amarillo and many other tourist destinations along the way.

Conclusion

Memphis is a fascinating place full of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are all set to experience the best of Memphis. From exploring the cultural and historical attractions to indulging in the local cuisine and exploring the surrounding natural beauty, Memphis will surely leave you with unforgettable memories.
