Introduction to Wheeler

Wheeler is a small town located in the High Plains of Texas, a region renowned for its vast landscape, wild nature, and fascinating history. This charming town is rich in Texan culture and offers plenty of opportunities for travelers to explore the great outdoors, soak up the local culture, and experience genuine Texan hospitality. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Wheeler has something to offer.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and accommodation options to consider for your trip to Wheeler. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Wheeler

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wheeler is home to significant cultural landmarks that offer insight into the town’s rich history and heritage. If you’re a history buff, you’ll enjoy visiting:

1. Old Town Museum

Located in the heart of Wheeler, Old Town Museum is a fantastic place to learn about the town’s history, culture, and people. The museum features a wide range of exhibits, artifacts, and collections that showcase the pioneer lifestyle and cattle ranching traditions of the area. Don’t miss the famous Devil’s Rope Barbed Wire collection, the largest of its kind in the world.

2. Wellington-Napoleon Bonaparte Dowling House

This elegant house museum provides visitors with a glimpse into the lives of Wheeler’s most prominent citizens. Built-in the 1800s, the Wellington-Napoleon Bonaparte Dowling House is a unique example of the Queen Anne architectural style. The house is now a museum and features antique furnishings, vintage clothing, and other interesting exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Wheeler: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wheeler is surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, making it a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and biking to fishing and wildlife watching, there’s no shortage of thrilling outdoor activities to enjoy in Wheeler. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions to explore:

1. McLean Lake Wildlife Management Area

Located ten miles east of Wheeler, the McLean Lake Wildlife Management Area is a haven for nature lovers. This 3,000-acre park features diverse habitats, including wetlands, grasslands, and woodlands, making it an ideal place to spot a variety of wildlife. There are several hiking trails to explore, including the self-guided nature trail around McLean Lake.

2. Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument

Located about 35 miles north of Wheeler, Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument is a fascinating place to visit for history and nature lovers. The park features spectacularly colored rocks and flint which the paleontologists claim date back millions of years. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about quarrying techniques used by native tribes or hike some of the park’s trails, which offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wheeler

With its beautiful scenery and landscapes, Wheeler is known for its numerous hiking and biking trails. These are some of the most popular routes and paths to follow:

1. Hodges Park Trail

Located in the eastern part of Wheeler County, Hodges Park Trail is a well-marked and well-maintained hiking path that is perfect for beginners as well as experienced hikers. The trail runs for about three miles and offers stunning views of wildflowers, wildlife, and Lake MacKenzie.

2. Lakeview Trail

Lakeview Trail is a 1.5-mile long hiking and biking path that loops around Lake MacKenzie. The trail is easy to navigate and is perfect for families with strollers, young children, and pets. You’ll enjoy stunning views of the lake, surrounding hills, and native vegetation.

Best Things to Do in Wheeler: Activities and Tours

Wheeler offers plenty of exciting things to do and see, ranging from cultural attractions to outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or education, you’ll find something that piques your interest in this charming town. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

1. Chimney Rock

Chimney Rock is a geological wonder and an excellent spot for rock climbers to test their skills. The towering rock formation, which rises 315 feet above the surrounding plains, is visible from miles away.

2. Texas Jailhouse Art Museum

The Texas Jailhouse Art Museum hosts unique and interesting local art exhibits in an old jailhouse building. Visit this museum to view art by some of the region’s most talented and intriguing artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wheeler

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Wheeler, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Wheeler and check into your hotel. Visit Old Town Museum and learn about the history of the region.

Day 2: Take a hike at Lakeview Trail and relax near Lake MacKenzie afterward.

Day 3: Explore the Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument and learn more about the ancient history of the region.

Day 4: Go fishing at McLean Lake Wildlife Management Area.

Day 5: Take a rock climbing adventure at Chimney Rock.

Day 6: Visit the Texas Jailhouse Art Museum in downtown Wheeler.

Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day, shopping for souvenirs, and strolling around town.

Food and Drink Scene of Wheeler

Texas is known for its unique cuisine and culinary excellence, and Wheeler is no exception. From classic Texan BBQ to delicious desserts, the town has much to offer in terms of food and drink.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Smoked BBQ Brisket

Texas is famous for its BBQ, and Wheeler offers plenty of mouth-watering options. Try the smoked BBQ brisket, which is tender and juicy and falls off the bone.

2. Catfish

Catfish is another popular dish in Wheeler. The crisp and flaky fish is typically served deep-fried and accompanied by hushpuppies and tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

1. Dr. Pepper

One of Texas’s most iconic beverages is Dr. Pepper, which was invented in Waco, Texas, in 1885. You’ll find this delicious drink throughout the town of Wheeler.

2. Local Beer

Try some of the fantastic locally crafted beer that Wheeler has to offer at one of the local bars or breweries. You won’t be disappointed.

Where to Stay in Wheeler: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wheeler offers several accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

1. La Quinta Inn & Suites

This budget-friendly hotel is conveniently located near the town center and offers comfortable and clean rooms, breakfast, and free Wi-Fi.

2. The Wagon Wheel Motel

The Wagon Wheel Motel is a vintage-style motel that offers cozy and unique rooms, friendly staff, and a fun retro vibe. The motel also features an outdoor pool and complimentary breakfast.

Local Culture and Customs of Wheeler

Wheeler is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who take pride in their heritage and customs. Texans are known for their hospitality, so don’t be surprised if someone strikes up a conversation with you or offers you a warm welcome.

Best Time to Visit Wheeler

The best time to visit Wheeler is from April to October when the temperatures are mild, and the weather is typically dry. The spring and summer months are ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing, while the fall season is great for foliage and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wheeler

Before you travel to Wheeler, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Do bring sunscreen and hats to protect yourself from the sun.

  • Do wear comfortable and durable shoes for hiking and outdoor activities.

  • Do take your time to explore the local culture and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm wildlife.

  • Don’t trespass on private property or protected areas.

  • Don’t engage in dangerous or reckless activities that could harm yourself or others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wheeler

Wheeler is a small and safe community, but it’s always best to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid leaving valuables in your car or unattended in public areas.

  • Take care when driving on country roads as wildlife can appear suddenly.

  • Keep your belongings secure when hiking or outdoor activities.

Health in Wheeler

There are no known health risks in Wheeler. However, it’s wise to take precautions such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated during the hot summer months. Keep in mind that McLean Lake Wildlife Management Area has several types of mosquitos and may carry Zika virus, so make sure to bring mosquito repellents and protective clothing.

Getting to Wheeler: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Wheeler is relatively easy, although it is a little off the beaten track. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Wheeler

The closest airport to Wheeler would be Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport in Amarillo. From there, you can then rent a car or take a bus to Wheeler.

Bus Stations Near Wheeler

Greyhound Bus offers daily services to Amarillo, the nearest city near Wheeler. You have to take the Amarillo Shuttle Bus to Wheeler to get there.

Other Options

If you prefer driving, hiring a car or taking a road trip is a great option. You can hire a car from the airport or from Amarillo and make your way to Wheeler. Alternatively, you can take a taxi from Amarillo to Wheeler, or take the metro which runs daily.

How to Get Around in Wheeler

It’s best to have your car or rent a car in Wheeler as public transport is very limited. If you don’t have your car, it’s best to take a taxi, hire a driver, or walk.

Where to Go Next from Wheeler: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wheeler is an excellent base for exploring the wider High Plains region of Texas. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

1. Amarillo

The nearest city, Amarillo features some of Texas’ most iconic attractions, including Palo Duro Canyon State Park, Cadillac Ranch, and The Big Texan Steak Ranch.

2. Canadian

Canadian is a small town in the Texas Panhandle, known for its scenic views, rugged terrain, and historic architecture. Visit the historic buildings or the nearby Canadian River for outdoor thrills.

Conclusion

Wheeler is a picturesque and charming town with an exciting history and a fantastic outdoor scene. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, delicious food to comfortable accommodation, Wheeler has everything to offer for an unforgettable trip. Plan your visit today!
