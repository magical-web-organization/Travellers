Introduction to Conway

Located in the High Plains region of the Texas Panhandle, Conway is a small city that offers visitors a glimpse into the unique history and charm of Texas. The city is home to several museums, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Conway is known for its friendly locals, diverse culture, and breathtaking sceneries.

Here is our comprehensive tour guide for Conway that highlights the best things to see and do, and where to stay and eat in the city.

List of Places to Visit in Conway

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Red River War Historical Marker is a historical landmark that commemorates the Red River War of 1874-1875, when the United States Army fought several Native American tribes in the region for control of the Great Plains. The marker is located in the middle of a scenic pasture and provides a beautiful view of the plains.

  • The Nicodemus-Kielman House Museum is a 19th-century Victorian-style house that belonged to two prominent families in the area. Visitors can tour the house and learn about the history and lifestyles of the wealthy families who once lived there.

  • The Tulia Cemetery Association Chapel is a historic chapel with beautiful stained glass windows and intricate woodwork. The chapel is located in Tulia Cemetery and is often used for weddings and other special events.

Natural Attractions of Conway: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Palo Duro Canyon State Park is a world-renowned natural wonder that offers stunning views of the Texas Panhandle’s unique landscapes. The park also offers numerous outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, horseback riding, and camping.

  • The Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument is a fascinating site that showcases the importance of flint to ancient cultures in the area. Visitors can view the quarries, learn about the geology of the region, and take guided tours of the park.

  • The John Stiff Memorial Park is a spacious park that provides visitors with a peaceful and relaxing environment. The park features walking trails, playgrounds, and a large pond for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Conway

  • The Comanche Trail is a 4.25-mile trail that offers visitors some of the most breathtaking views of Palo Duro Canyon. Hikers can enjoy the trail’s stunning vistas while exploring the unique geological formations of the area.

  • The Antelope Creek Trail is a paved biking and walking trail that follows the creek through the city of Amarillo. The trail offers visitors a scenic and relaxing way to explore the city.

Best Things to Do in Conway: Activities and Tours

  • Take a horseback riding tour through Palo Duro Canyon. The tour provides visitors with a unique perspective of the canyon and offers a one-of-a-kind outdoor adventure.

  • Visit the American Quarter Horse Hall of Fame & Museum in Amarillo, Texas, and learn about the history and significance of the American Quarter Horse breed. The museum features interactive exhibits, educational programs, and a stunning collection of art and artifacts.

Food and Drink Scene of Conway

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Texas-style BBQ is a must-try in Conway. Barry’s BBQ offers some of the best smoked meats and traditional sides in the area.

  • The Chicken Fried Steak is a Texas classic that visitors must try when in the state. Chick-n-Pickle serves up a delicious version of the dish, along with homemade gravy and sides.

Local Beverages

  • The Amarillo Brewing Supply is a popular spot for beer lovers in the area. The shop offers a wide selection of brewing supplies and equipment, as well as brewing classes and workshops.

  • The Six Car Pub & Brewery is a local favorite that specializes in craft beer and pub fare. The pub features a rotating selection of beers on tap and a friendly atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Conway: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Holiday Inn Express & Suites is a comfortable hotel that offers visitors a convenient location and modern amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.

  • The Vintage Autohaus & Imports is a unique bed and breakfast that caters to car enthusiasts. The B&B features luxurious rooms and suites, as well as a collection of vintage and classic cars on display.

What to Buy in Conway: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Green Chile Willy’s is a local shop that specializes in hot sauces, salsas, and other spicy treats. Visitors can purchase their signature products as well as other local delicacies.

  • The Hobbies ‘N Stuff is a hobby and gift store that has been serving the Conway community for over 30 years. Visitors can find a wide selection of unique gifts and souvenirs, including crafts, toys, and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Conway

Conway is known for its friendly locals, who are always willing to help visitors with directions or recommendations. The city celebrates its unique history and culture with various festivals and events throughout the year, including the West Texas Fair & Rodeo and the Panhandle-Plains Historical Museum’s annual Christmas event.

Best Time to Visit Conway

The best time to visit Conway is during the spring months of March to May or the fall months of September to November. During this period, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. However, visitors must be aware that west Texas weather can change quickly, so it’s essential to check the forecast regularly and prepare accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Conway

Do:

  • Explore the natural wonders of Palo Duro Canyon State Park.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the BBQ and Chicken Fried Steak.
  • Visit the local museums and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather conditions in the area.
  • Miss out on the exciting outdoor adventures and activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Conway

Conway is a safe city for tourists, and visitors must take the necessary precautions, especially when hiking or biking in the natural areas. It’s important to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and carry a first-aid kit when exploring the outdoors.

Health in Conway

Travelers to Conway should always carry proof of vaccination and plan for preventative medical care to avoid exposure to diseases like West Nile virus. It’s also essential to practice good hygiene, maintain good hydration, and avoid eating or drinking contaminated food or water.

Getting to Conway: Transportation Options and Logistics

Conway is easily accessible by car or bus. The nearest airports are Amarillo International Airport, which is about 40 miles north of the city, and Lubbock Preston Smith International Airport, which is around 115 miles south. Visitors can also take a bus or rent a car to get to Conway.

Airports Near Conway

  • Amarillo International Airport (AMA)
  • Lubbock Preston Smith International Airport (LBB)

Bus Stations Near Conway

  • Greyhound Bus Station (AMA)

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Rental cars are available at the airports and in the city.

How to Get Around in Conway

Conway is a small city, and visitors can easily get around by car or bike. Taxis and ridesharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Conway: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The city of Amarillo, Texas, is located just 20 miles north of Conway. Amarillo offers visitors a vibrant downtown area, several museums and historical landmarks, and a range of dining and shopping options.

  • The Caprock Canyons State Park is located approximately 100 miles southeast of Conway and features beautiful canyons, unique rock formations, and Texas bison herds.

Conclusion

Conway, Texas, is a charming city that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to explore the natural wonders of Palo Duro Canyon, learn about the city’s history and culture, or relax and unwind in a peaceful environment, Conway has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and experience the best of what this fascinating city has to offer.
