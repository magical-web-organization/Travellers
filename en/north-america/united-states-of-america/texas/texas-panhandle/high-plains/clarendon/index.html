Introduction to Clarendon

Clarendon, located in the High Plains of Texas, is a charming small town filled with historic buildings and friendly locals. Founded in 1878, Clarendon has a rich western heritage that has been well-preserved. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, there’s plenty to see and do in Clarendon.

List of Places to Visit in Clarendon

Here’s a list of top places to visit in Clarendon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saints’ Roost Museum: This museum showcases the city’s fascinating history through exhibits that span from the area’s prehistoric past to the present day.
  • Donley County Courthouse: Built-in 1891, the Donley County Courthouse is a beautiful example of late 19th century architecture.
  • Greenbelt Lake: This popular recreational spot offers fishing, camping, and hiking trails.

Natural Attractions of Clarendon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clarendon Community Park: Perfect for picnics and family outings with plenty of outdoor amenities like basketball courts and playgrounds.
  • Palo Duro Canyon: Known as the “Grand Canyon of Texas,” this beautiful natural formation located just an hour from Clarendon boasts stunning trails, bird watching opportunities, and stunning scenery.
  • Sandell Drive-In Theater: Watch your favorite movies under the stars and experience a piece of American history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clarendon

Clarendon offers many hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the area. Here’s a list of the best hiking and biking routes in Clarendon:

  • Caprock Canyon Trailway: A 64.25-mile hike spanning from South Plains to Caprock Canyon State Park.
  • Comanchero Canyons Trailhead: A 9.5-mile out-and-back hike known for its stunning views of the Caprock Escarpment.
  • Tule Canyon Trailway: A 6.2-mile hike that offers scenic views of the surrounding canyonlands and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Clarendon: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Clarendon:

  • Take a walking tour of downtown Clarendon
  • Visit the Saints’ Roost Museum
  • Take a sunset horseback ride through Palo Duro Canyon
  • Go fishing or camping at Greenbelt Lake

Week-Long Itinerary for Clarendon

Day 1: Arrive in Clarendon and explore downtown Day 2: Hike the Caprock Canyon Trailway Day 3: Take a tour of the Saints’ Roost Museum and Donley County Courthouse Day 4: Visit Palo Duro Canyon for horseback riding and hiking Day 5: Visit Greenbelt Lake for fishing, camping, and outdoor activities Day 6: Go to the Sandell Drive-In Theater for a movie night Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Amarillo for shopping and dining

Local Culture and Customs of Clarendon

Clarendon has a strong western heritage, with a focus on ranching and farming. Visitors should be aware of the local customs and traditions, such as respect for the land, animals, and each other.

Best Time to Visit Clarendon

The best time to visit Clarendon is during the fall months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is changing colors. Avoid visiting during the summer months when temperatures can reach well into the 100s.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clarendon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Clarendon:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas
  • Disrespect animals or livestock
  • Ignore parking and traffic regulations

Safety and Health

Safety in Clarendon

Clarendon is a safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Keep valuables secured and use caution when exploring remote areas of the town or outlying areas.

Health in Clarendon

Visitors to Clarendon should bring any necessary prescriptions and medical supplies. The nearest hospital is in nearby Amarillo, approximately 45 minutes from Clarendon.

Getting to Clarendon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clarendon is located in the Texas Panhandle, and the nearest city is Amarillo. Here are some transportation options to get to Clarendon:

Airports Near Clarendon

  • Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA), approximately 68 miles from Clarendon

Bus Stations Near Clarendon

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Amarillo, approximately 50 minutes from Clarendon

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Clarendon via Interstate 40 or Highway 287.

How to Get Around in Clarendon

The best way to get around in Clarendon is by car, as public transportation is not well-developed.

Where to Go Next from Clarendon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Clarendon:

  • Amarillo, Texas: Known for its iconic Route 66 landmarks, Amarillo also boasts world-famous art installations and museums.
  • Lubbock, Texas: Home of Texas Tech University and a thriving arts scene, Lubbock is a city rich in cultural diversity and innovation.
  • Canyon, Texas: Home of West Texas A&M University and Palo Duro Canyon State Park, Canyon is a hub of outdoor adventure and educational opportunities.

Conclusion

Clarendon, Texas, offers visitors a unique experience immersed in the state’s western heritage, surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty. With a variety of outdoor recreation opportunities, cultural attractions, and friendly locals, Clarendon is the perfect place for a memorable Texas getaway.
