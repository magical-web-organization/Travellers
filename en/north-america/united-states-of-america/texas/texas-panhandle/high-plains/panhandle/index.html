Introduction to Panhandle

Panhandle, Texas, is a small, rural community nestled in the High Plains region of the Texas Panhandle. Known for its stunning scenery, rich history, and friendly folks, Panhandle is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic Texan experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll be diving deep into everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Panhandle.

List of Places to Visit in Panhandle

Whether you’re interested in exploring culturally important attractions, getting out into nature, or simply experiencing the best of small-town Texas hospitality, Panhandle has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit while you’re in town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carson County Square House Museum: Explore over 23,000 square feet of exhibits and artifacts dedicated to the history of the Texas Panhandle.
  • Panhandle-Plains Historical Museum: This world-renowned museum features over 2 million artifacts and objects spanning 14,000 years of Panhandle history.
  • Tex Randall Statue: Standing at 47 feet tall, this iconic cowboy statue is a beloved symbol of Texas spirit.

Natural Attractions of Panhandle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park: Known as the Grand Canyon of Texas, this stunning park offers over 30 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails, as well as opportunities for camping and stargazing.
  • Lake Meredith National Recreation Area: This sprawling reservoir is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and wildlife watching.
  • Caprock Canyons State Park: Home to the official Texas State Bison Herd, Caprock Canyons offers visitors the chance to see these majestic creatures up close, as well as miles of hiking and biking trails and opportunities for camping and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Panhandle

If you’re looking for a more immersive outdoor adventure, or simply want to enjoy the stunning natural scenery of Panhandle at your own pace, hit the trails! Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore.

  • Lighthouse Trail: This 5.75-mile hike in Palo Duro Canyon State Park offers unparalleled views of the canyon’s iconic Lighthouse rock formation.
  • Sunflower Trail: This 12-mile trail in Lake Meredith National Recreation Area is popular with hikers, trail runners, and mountain bikers alike.
  • Haynes Ridge Overlook Trail: Located in Caprock Canyons State Park, this easy 1.4-mile hike offers stunning views of the surrounding canyon landscape.

Best Things to Do in Panhandle: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for even more adventure and excitement in Panhandle, check out some of these awesome activities and tours.

  • Rockin’ Zipline Adventure: Soar over the stunning Palo Duro Canyon on this thrilling zipline experience.
  • Bison Tour: Take a guided tour of the official Texas State Bison Herd in Caprock Canyons and witness these majestic creatures up close.
  • Sandie Cheer Camp: If you’re visiting with kids, this popular cheerleading camp is a great way to keep them active and engaged while you explore the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Panhandle

To help you make the most of your time in Panhandle, we’ve put together this week-long itinerary, packed with all the top attractions and activities the area has to offer.

  • Day 1: Arrive in town and check into your accommodation of choice. Spend the afternoon exploring the Carson County Square House Museum and Tex Randall Statue.
  • Day 2: Experience the natural beauty of Palo Duro Canyon State Park on the Lighthouse Trail. Head back to town for dinner at the popular Chow Down Family Restaurant.
  • Day 3: Take a guided tour of the official Texas State Bison Herd at Caprock Canyons State Park. Stop by the Wild Turkey Distillery for some local spirits.
  • Day 4: Rent a kayak or stand-up paddleboard and spend the day exploring Lake Meredith. Stop for lunch at the Lone Star Grill in nearby Fritch.
  • Day 5: Head out for a horseback ride through the scenic landscape of Palo Duro Canyon.
  • Day 6: Soar over the canyon on the Rockin’ Zipline Adventure. End the day with a picnic dinner and stargazing at Palo Duro.
  • Day 7: Spend your last day in town hiking the Haynes Ridge Overlook Trail. Head to Feldman’s Bakery for some local sweets before you depart.

Food and Drink Scene of Panhandle

No trip to Texas is complete without indulging in some delicious Southern comfort food and local libations. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in Panhandle.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken fried steak: This hearty dish is a Southern classic, and you’ll find it on menus throughout the area. Try it at the Chow Down Family Restaurant in Panhandle.
  • Barbecue: Texas is famous for its slow-cooked, smoky meats, and Panhandle is no exception. Check out Smokey Joe’s BBQ in Borger for some of the best.
  • Pie: Whether you prefer fruit pies, cream pies, or savory pies, you’ll find something to love at Feldman’s Bakery in Hereford.

Local Beverages

  • Wild Turkey 101: This iconic bourbon is distilled at the Wild Turkey Distillery in Lawrenceburg, Kentucky, but its roots are in Panhandle, where it was first produced in the 1940s.
  • Ranch Water: This refreshing cocktail is a beloved Texas staple, made with tequila, lime juice, and sparkling water.
  • Texas wines: Though not quite as well-known as California wines, Texas wines are gaining popularity, and Panhandle is home to several great wineries, including Bar Z Winery in Canyon.

Where to Stay in Panhandle

From cozy bed and breakfasts to budget-friendly chain hotels, there are plenty of options for lodging in Panhandle. Here are a few of our favorites.

  • The Z Bar Motel: This charming motel in Panhandle offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.
  • The Nat: Located in nearby Canadian, this cozy bed and breakfast features themed guest rooms and delicious home-cooked breakfasts.
  • Staybridge Suites Amarillo-Western Crossing: If you’re looking for a more upscale, modern experience, this hotel in nearby Amarillo is a great choice.

What to Buy in Panhandle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted goods to locally produced foods and drinks, there are plenty of unique souvenirs to bring home from Panhandle. Here are just a few ideas.

  • Panhandle Plains Historical Museum Gift Shop: This museum gift shop carries a wide range of locally made goods, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Wild Turkey Distillery Gift Shop: Don’t leave without picking up a bottle (or two) of this iconic bourbon, as well as some branded glassware or apparel.
  • Prickly Pear Cactus Jelly: This sweet and tangy spread, made from the fruit of the prickly pear cactus, is a uniquely Texan treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Panhandle

Panhandle is known for its friendly, welcoming community, rich Western heritage, and love of all things outdoors. It’s considered polite to greet people with a smile and a nod or a friendly “Howdy!” And if you’re invited to a local cookout or barbecue, be sure to show up hungry!

Best Time to Visit Panhandle

While Panhandle is a great destination year-round, spring and fall are the best times to visit, when temperatures are mild, and the weather is generally pleasant. Summer can be hot and dry, while winter can be cold and icy, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Panhandle

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the scenic natural beauty of Panhandle.
  • Do try local favorites like chicken fried steak and Ranch Water.
  • Do be friendly with locals - Texans are known for their Southern hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the size of Texas - it can take longer to drive from place to place than you might think.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and take other necessary precautions if you’re spending time outdoors.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural or historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Panhandle

Panhandle is a relatively safe destination, but as with any travel experience, it’s important to take necessary precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, keep your belongings secure, and use caution when exploring remote or unfamiliar areas.

Health in Panhandle

Panhandle has a low risk of communicable diseases and generally good air quality, but it’s always a good idea to pack any necessary medications and take appropriate measures to protect against the sun and insects.

Getting to Panhandle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Panhandle is located about 35 miles northeast of Amarillo and is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Panhandle

  • Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA)

Bus Stations Near Panhandle

  • Greyhound: Amarillo Bus Station

Other Options

  • Car rental
  • Taxi and ride-sharing services

How to Get Around in Panhandle

The best way to get around and see all that Panhandle has to offer is by car. You can rent a car from the airport or other local rental agencies, or bring your own.

Where to Go Next from Panhandle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip or explore more of West Texas, here are a few nearby destinations to consider.

  • Amarillo: Just a short drive from Panhandle, Amarillo offers plenty of sights and activities, including the famous Cadillac Ranch art installation and the American Quarter Horse Hall of Fame.
  • Lubbock: About a two-hour drive south of Panhandle, Lubbock is the birthplace of Buddy Holly and offers a thriving arts and culture scene.
  • Santa Fe: If you’re willing to venture a bit further afield, Santa Fe, New Mexico, is about a six-hour drive west of Panhandle and is renowned for its stunning natural scenery, rich Native American history, and vibrant arts community.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing, authentic Texas experience, Panhandle has it all. From its stunning natural beauty to its rich Western heritage and friendly locals, this charming destination is guaranteed to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits.
