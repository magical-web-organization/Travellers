Introduction to Cross Plains

Cross Plains is a quiet town located in the North Central Plains region of Texas. Nestled in the Texas Panhandle, this charming town offers an array of outdoor adventures, historical attractions, and cultural experiences. Cross Plains is the perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in Texas’ rich heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Cross Plains

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cross Plains is steeped in history and boasts a number of museums and historical attractions worth visiting. The Robert E. Howard Museum is a must-see for fans of literature. Robert E. Howard, the creator of Conan the Barbarian, lived in Cross Plains and wrote many of his legendary tales while here. The museum is home to artifacts and exhibits related to the life and work of Howard. Another historically significant site is the Cross Plains Cemetery, where many of the town’s early settlers are buried. Visitors can take a self-guided tour of the cemetery and learn about the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Cross Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cross Plains also offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. Brownwood State Park is located just a short drive from Cross Plains and features hiking trails, fishing, camping, and swimming. The Lake Brownwood State Park is another popular destination, where visitors can rent paddleboats, go fishing, or enjoy a picnic while taking in the beautiful scenery. For wildlife lovers, the Cross Plains Nature Park is home to a variety of animals, including deer, coyotes, and a variety of bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cross Plains

With its abundance of natural beauty, Cross Plains is a great place for hiking and biking. The Cross Plains Nature Park offers a number of trails for visitors to explore. The Battle Creek Trail is particularly popular among hikers, offering scenic views and a moderate level of difficulty. The Rails-to-Trails Railshead Park Trail is perfect for those looking for an easier, family-friendly hike. This trail is 1.8 miles long and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Cross Plains: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, Cross Plains offers a variety of other activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. The Baker Ranch Rodeo provides a unique opportunity to experience the excitement of a real-life rodeo with authentic cowboy culture. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the Cross Plains Fire Department to learn about the town’s firefighting history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cross Plains

For visitors staying in Cross Plains for a week, here is a suggested itinerary for an exciting and fun-filled vacation:

Day 1 Visit the Robert E. Howard Museum and explore downtown Cross Plains

Food and Drink Scene of Cross Plains

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Cross Plains is complete without sampling some of the town’s local dishes. Barbecue is a Texas staple, and Cross Plains is no exception. Smokin’ Bros BBQ is a local favorite, offering some of the best brisket and ribs in the state. Another must-try dish is the chicken-fried steak, a beloved Texas classic. The Chuckwagon Cafe and Bakeshop is a great place to sample this hearty meal.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with some of the local beverages. Cross Plains is home to several wineries, such as Brennan Vineyards and Pillar Bluff Vineyards, where visitors can sample the local varietals. For beer lovers, the nearby town of Brownwood is home to the Brownwood Brewing Company, where visitors can enjoy a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Cross Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cross Plains has a few options for accommodation, including motels, hotels, and vacation rental homes. Cross Plains Inn & Suites is a popular choice for those looking for comfortable and affordable accommodation. For travelers seeking a more rustic experience, the nearby Rough Creek Lodge & Resort offers luxury cabins set in the beautiful Texas countryside.

What to Buy in Cross Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home a slice of Texas by picking up some souvenirs from Cross Plains. The Cross Plains General Store carries a variety of local, handmade items, including pottery and woodwork. The town’s boutiques also offer a selection of unique souvenirs, such as antiques and vintage items.

Local Culture and Customs of Cross Plains

Texas has a unique and rich culture, which is reflected in the customs and traditions of Cross Plains. Rodeo and country music are deeply ingrained in the town’s culture, and visitors can experience this firsthand at the Baker Ranch Rodeo. Visitors should also be aware that Texans take their barbecue seriously, and it is considered a point of pride for many locals.

Best Time to Visit Cross Plains

The best time to visit Cross Plains is in the fall or spring, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be quite hot, and winters can be chilly, with occasional snowfall. Visitors should also be aware that the town can get quite busy during major holidays, such as the Fourth of July and Christmas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cross Plains

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites.
  • Try the local foods and beverages.
  • Explore the great outdoors and go hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the Texan heat and sun, be sure to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.
  • Forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking.
  • Trespass on private property while hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cross Plains

Cross Plains is a safe town, however, visitors should still exercise caution, especially when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Be sure to dress appropriately, stay hydrated, and inform someone of your plans if you plan on going on a hike.

Health in Cross Plains

Cross Plains has several medical facilities, including the Eastland Memorial Hospital, for any medical emergencies. Visitors with specific health concerns should be aware that the town is quite small and may not have specialized services.

Getting to Cross Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cross Plains

The nearest airport to Cross Plains is the Abilene Regional Airport, located 60 miles away. From here, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Cross Plains

Greyhound has a bus station in Abilene, which is approximately one hour from Cross Plains by car.

Other Options

Cross Plains is a small town and does not have its own public transportation system. Visitors who plan on exploring the town and its surroundings should consider renting a car.

How to Get Around in Cross Plains

Cross Plains is a small town and everything is within walking distance. However, a rental car is recommended for those who wish to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Cross Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cross Plains is located near several other interesting Texas towns. Brownwood, located about 30 miles away, is home to the Brownwood State Park and the Lehnis Railroad Museum. Abilene, located 60 miles away, offers many museums and cultural attractions, including the Abilene Zoo and the Frontier Texas! museum.

Conclusion

Cross Plains may be a small town, but it offers visitors a rich experience of Texan history, culture, and traditions. From its picturesque natural parks to its delightful restaurants and wineries, Cross Plains is a must-see destination for those wanting to immerse themselves in the heart of Texas.
