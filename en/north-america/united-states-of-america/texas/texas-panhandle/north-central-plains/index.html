Introduction to North Central Plains

North Central Plains is a region located in the heart of Texas Panhandle, United States of America. It encompasses several counties, including Gray, Wheeler, Donley, Collingsworth, and Childress, among others. Known for its vast expanse of rolling plains, historical sites, and outdoor recreational opportunities, North Central Plains is a popular destination for adventurers and history buffs. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations in North Central Plains.

List of Places to Visit in North Central Plains

If you’re visiting North Central Plains for the first time, it can be overwhelming to decide which sites to visit. Here’s a list of must-see attractions in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Donley County Courthouse: This historical courthouse, built in 1890, is a stunning example of Romanesque architecture and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the courthouse’s fascinating history.
  • Panhandle-Plains Historical Museum: This museum is where you can discover the cultural and natural history of Texas. You will find historical artifacts, interactive exhibits, and a vast collection of art exhibits.
  • Amarillo Museum of Art: This museum displays modern and contemporary art from around the world. You can take part in educational programs or attend special events too.
  • US Route 66 Museum: This museum highlights the history, significance, and nostalgia associated with US Route 66.

Natural Attractions of North Central Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Caprock Canyons State Park and Trailways: The park, which covers over 15,000 acres, includes rugged canyons, riparian areas and numerous trails for hiking and biking. A popular activity here is bison viewing.
  • Lake McClellan: Lake McClellan located covers about 5200 acres and is a perfect place to go fishing, boating, or bird watching.
  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park: This park is often referred to as the Grand Canyon of Texas, and with good reason. At 120 miles long and 800 feet deep, the canyon boasts stunning vistas and endless outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • The Alibates Flint Quarries National Monument: This protected area preserves a prehistoric quarry that was one of the major sources of flint in North America before European contact. Visitors can take ranger-guided tours and hikes to see the remnants of quarrying operations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Central Plains

North Central Plains offers numerous hiking and biking trails for adventurers. Here’s a few of the best:

  • Black Kettle National Grassland Trail: A moderate 3.5-mile hike for bird-watching and wildlife viewing.
  • Lake McClellan Trails: With over 20 miles of hike and bike trails, Lake McClellan is ideal for a day of hiking and mountain biking adventure.
  • Silver Falls Trail: This 4.7-mile out-and-back trail in Caprock Canyons State Park takes hikers to see the beautiful Silver Falls.

Best Things to Do in North Central Plains: Activities and Tours

North Central Plains offers a wealth of activities and tours for visitors, from nightlife and dining to outdoor adventure and cultural events.

  • Attend a rodeo: Rodeo is a popular sport in the area, and you can experience a live event at various locations around North Central Plains.
  • Bring the Kids to the Waterpark: If traveling with children, stop by in Castaway Cove.
  • Take a Texas Panhandle Wine Tour: Panhandle wine tours are a fun way to explore the region while enjoying local wines.
  • Visit Historic Landmarks: Explore some of the region’s historical markers, including the English Field Air Museum in Amarillo.

Food and Drink Scene of North Central Plains

Texas is known for its rich culinary scene, and North Central Plains is no exception. Here are some essential food and drink experiences to try in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chili con carne: The state dish of Texas can be found all over North Central Plains.
  • Chicken-fried steak: A traditional Texas dish of breaded and fried steak. Try it at The Stockyard Café, Wheeler, TX.
  • Barbecue: North Central Plains has some of the best barbecue restaurants in Texas. Try the famous ribeye steak at Gibby’s Dining and Drinks in Wheeler.

Local Beverages

  • Texas Wine: The climate and soil of Texas make it an ideal place for growing wine grapes, and there are many vineyards dotted around the North Central Plains.
  • Shiner Beer: This beer has been brewed in Texas since 1909 and has a loyal following.

Where to Stay in North Central Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Central Plains offers a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some options to consider:

  • The Wellington Hotel: This historic hotel built in 1928 is located in downtown Amarillo and boasts elegant rooms and suites.
  • Best Western Red River Inn: Located in Clarendon, this hotel offers affordable rates and a range of amenities, including a pool and fitness center.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: Located in Childress, this hotel is ideal for families and offers a free breakfast, pool, and fitness center.
  • The Cottages at Lake McClellan: Stay in a charming cottage nestled in the scenic surroundings of Lake McClellan.

What to Buy in North Central Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The region is full of unique local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some products to consider:

  • Texas wines: Bottle of local wine is a perfect way to remember your trip to North Central Plains.
  • Quilts and Texan crafts: You can purchase quilt and custom boots with intricate designs in the region.
  • Steaks for home: Get steaks to cook at home from Lonesome Cowboy or other providers.

Local Culture and Customs of North Central Plains

North Central Plains has a unique history and a rich cultural heritage. Here are a few things to keep in mind about local customs:

  • Texan hospitality: People in Texas are generally friendly and hospitable to visitors.
  • Respect the Flag: Texans value their flag very highly, so avoid disrespecting it in any way.

Best Time to Visit North Central Plains

North Central Plains is an all-year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from March to October when the weather is warm and favorable for outdoor activities. Winters are pretty cold, and there is often snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Central Plains

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the barbecue and Tex-Mex.
  • Do take a wine tour and bring home a bottle of local wine for souvenirs.
  • Do enjoy the scenic beauty of the canyons and lakes in the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t pick wildflowers or disturb the natural habitats of animals.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothes and footwear for hiking and outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Central Plains

North Central Plains is generally a safe destination, and crime rates are low. But, here are a few safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be careful while driving in rural areas and keep an eye on stray cattle or animals.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions when planning outdoor activities.
  • Always carry enough water and emergency supplies while hiking or exploring.

Health in North Central Plains

Insect bites and stings are the most common health concerns in the region. Be sure to pack insect repellent and carry an EpiPen for allergic reactions.

Getting to North Central Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Central Plains is easily reachable from major cities like Amarillo, Lubbock, and Oklahoma City. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near North Central Plains

  • Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA)
  • Lubbock Preston Smith International Airport (LBB)

Bus Stations Near North Central Plains

  • Greyhound- Located in Amarillo, TX
  • Texhoma Bus in Wheeler

Other Options

  • Cab and Taxis in Amarillo and other major cities
  • Car rentals: There are several car rental companies operational in the area.

How to Get Around in North Central Plains

Driving is the most convenient mode of transportation in North Central Plains, and it’s ideal for exploring remote areas. It’s important to note that public transportation options are limited, and having a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from North Central Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more destinations to explore, here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park
  • Lubbock, TX
  • Oklahoma City, OK

Conclusion

North Central Plains is a beautiful and unique region within Texas, with a rich cultural heritage and challenging hiking trails. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or art and cuisine, North Central Plains has something for everyone. Plan your adventure today!
