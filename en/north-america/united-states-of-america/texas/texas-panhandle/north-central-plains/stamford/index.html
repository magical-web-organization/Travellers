Stamford, a small town in the North Central Plains region of Texas Panhandle, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. From the historic attractions to the scenic beauty, Stamford has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, hiking/biking routes, local food and drinks, where to stay, and more to help you plan your trip to Stamford.

Introduction to Stamford

Stamford is a town steeped in history. It’s home to numerous landmarks and historic sites, including the Texas Cowboy Reunion, a world-famous rodeo that takes place annually. Founded in 1900, Stamford is a beautiful blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With a population of less than 4,000, Stamford is a small town full of friendly faces and welcoming locals.

List of Places to Visit in Stamford

Stamford offers a variety of attractions to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stamford Carnegie Library: A beautiful historic building built in 1911, now home to the Stamford Carnegie Library and the Stamford Museum.
  • Stamford Museum: Boasts an impressive collection of artifacts, exhibits, and memorabilia showcasing the history of Stamford and the surrounding area.
  • The Lela Latch Lloyd Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, the museum houses artifacts, photographs and documents from Stamford and Jones County history.
  • Fort Griffin State Historic Site: A restored 19th-century military post that includes barracks, officer quarters, and a stone powder house.

Natural Attractions of Stamford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Stamford: Offering fantastic fishing and boating opportunities, Lake Stamford is a popular recreational spot that’s great for a picnic or a relaxing day out in nature.
  • La Nana Creek Trail: A 2.5-mile trail, La Nana Creek Trail is perfect for a leisurely hike through the beautiful Texan wilderness.
  • Stamford Golf Course: Take in the beautiful views of the surrounding countryside while practicing your swing on the rolling greens of Stamford’s very own golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stamford

Stamford boasts a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes to explore are:

  • La Nana Creek Trail
  • Texas Forts Trail: A 650-mile loop trail that takes you through some of the state’s most scenic regions.
  • Stamford City Park Trail: A 2.5-mile trail located in Stamford City Park that features beautiful natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Stamford: Activities and Tours

Stamford has a lot to offer in terms of fun activities and tours. Some of the best experiences include:

  • Texas Cowboy Reunion: A world-renowned rodeo that celebrates the cowboy way of life.
  • Stamford Watermelon Festival: Held each year on July 4th, this festival features a parade, live music, and of course, plenty of juicy watermelon.
  • Farm and Ranch Tour: Take a tour of the farms and ranches in the surrounding area and learn about the local agriculture industry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stamford

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your trip to Stamford:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Stamford Museum and Carnegie Library, followed by a round of golf at Stamford Golf Course.
2 Hike the La Nana Creek Trail and have a picnic at the nearby Lake Stamford.
3 Take a tour of the local farms and ranches.
4 Explore Fort Griffin State Historic Site and learn about the history of the region.
5 Attend the Texas Cowboy Reunion and watch some of the world’s best rodeo artists in action.
6 Take a leisurely bike ride through the city and enjoy the sights and sounds of Stamford.
7 Relax and unwind at the Stamford City Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Stamford

Stamford has a variety of local food and drink options that are worth exploring. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texas-style Barbecue: Feast on some of the best barbecue in the state at The Hold’em Café.
  • Fried Catfish: Enjoy crispy and juicy fried catfish with hush puppies and coleslaw at Lawrence Brothers Grocery and Cafe.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern staple, sweet tea is available at most restaurants and cafes in Stamford.
  • Ice-Cold Beer: After a day of exploring, unwind with a cold beer at one of Stamford’s local bars.

Where to Stay in Stamford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stamford offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top choices:

  • The Stamford Inn: A charming boutique hotel located in the heart of town, offering comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • The Elk Shack: A cozy cabin located on a working ranch, perfect for a rustic getaway.
  • The Old Chamberlin Hotel: A historic hotel that has been restored to its former glory, featuring an outdoor pool and charming decor.

What to Buy in Stamford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Stamford:

  • Local Honey: Native honey from the surrounding ranches is available for purchase at the Lawrence Brothers Grocery and Cafe.
  • Cowboy Hats: No trip to Texas is complete without a cowboy hat. Pick one up at Boot Hill located at Stamford Junction.
  • Mesquite Grilled Beef Sticks: Take a taste of Texas home with you with some mesquite-grilled beef sticks from McCarty Mesquite Beef located at New Castle.

Local Culture and Customs of Stamford

Stamford is a proud Texan town with a strong cowboy culture. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, such as keeping up with the formalities of polite conversation. Texan hospitality is warm and friendly, and visitors can expect to be welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Stamford

The best time to visit Stamford is during the fall when the weather is mild and the foliage is changing colors. Spring is also an excellent time to visit when the wildflowers are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stamford

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Take part in the many cultural events that take place throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be disruptive or rude to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stamford

Stamford is a safe and friendly town. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking their cars and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Stamford

Visitors should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations and take precautions to protect themselves from mosquito-borne diseases during the summer months.

Getting to Stamford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stamford is well-connected with easy access to several transportation options. Here are some of the best ways to reach Stamford:

Airports Near Stamford

  • Abilene Regional Airport: Located about 45 miles from Stamford, Abilene Regional Airport is the closest airport to Stamford.
  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport: Located about 200 miles from Stamford.

Bus Stations Near Stamford

  • Greyhound: Located in Abilene, Texas, about 45 miles from Stamford.

Other Options

  • Driving: Stamford is easily accessible by car via US highways 277 and 283.

How to Get Around in Stamford

The best way to get around Stamford is by car. Rental cars are available at the Abilene Regional Airport and in nearby Abilene.

Where to Go Next from Stamford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stamford is perfectly situated for exploring other popular destinations in Texas. Here are some of the nearby places to visit:

  • Abilene: Just a short drive from Stamford, Abilene boasts a thriving arts scene and numerous attractions, including the Abilene Zoo and the Frontier Texas! museum.
  • Lubbock: Located about 130 miles from Stamford, Lubbock is home to a plethora of museums, parks, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Stamford is a wonderful destination that is sure to delight visitors of all ages and interests. With its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality, Stamford is a place where visitors can experience the true heart of Texas. We hope this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip and make the most of your time in Stamford.
