Turkey is a melting pot of cultures, history, and landscapes that always leaves visitors in awe. From the bustling streets of Istanbul to the surreal landscapes of Cappadocia, this country offers a range of experiences that will satisfy any traveler’s wanderlust. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll dive into the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Turkey

Turkey is located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, with a rich history that dates back to ancient times. It is home to numerous civilizations, including the Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, and Ottoman Empire. Today, Turkey is a modern country with a vibrant culture, bustling cities, and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Turkey

Turkey has many cities, small towns, and natural attractions that are worth a visit. Here’s a list of the top places to include on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Istanbul: The Hagia Sophia, Topkapi Palace, Blue Mosque
  • Troy: The ancient city of Troy
  • Ephesus: The ancient city of Ephesus
  • Ani: The ancient Armenian city of Ani
  • Cappadocia: Fairy Chimneys, underground cities, and cave churches

Natural Attractions of Turkey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pamukkale Hot Springs: Thermal springs and cascading terraces of calcium carbonate
  • Mount Nemrut: Stone heads and mausoleum at the top of a mountain
  • Oludeniz: Blue Lagoon with a stunning beach and paragliding opportunities
  • Butterfly Valley: A hidden gem with a stunning waterfall and thousands of butterflies
  • Fethiye: A beautiful port city with crystal clear waters and nature parks

Hiking/Biking Routes of Turkey

Turkey is home to several scenic hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the landscape. Here are a few worth checking out:

  • Lycian Way: A long-distance hiking trail that runs along the Mediterranean coast
  • St Paul Trail: A 500km trail from the Mediterranean coast to the Anatolian plateau
  • Mount Ararat: A challenging trek to the summit of Turkey’s highest mountain
  • The Aegean Trail: A coastal route that passes through quaint villages, olive groves and beaches
  • The Cappadocia Swordland: A three-day mountain biking route through stunning lunar landscapes

Best Things to Do in Turkey: Activities and Tours

Turkey offers a range of activities and tours to suit every taste. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over Cappadocia
  • Cruise along the Bosphorus Strait in Istanbul
  • Swim in the thermal waters of Pamukkale
  • Visit the beautiful beaches of Antalya
  • Explore the ancient ruins of Ephesus

Week-Long Itinerary for Turkey

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Turkey:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Istanbul, explore the historic district
2 Visit Hagia Sophia, Topkapi Palace, and Blue Mosque
3 Travel to Cappadocia, take a hot air balloon ride and explore fairy chimneys
4 Hike the Lycian Way in the Antalya region
5 Take a day trip to Pamukkale and swim in its thermal waters
6 Visit Ephesus, an ancient Roman city near Izmir
7 Return to Istanbul, shopping and departure

Food and Drink Scene of Turkey

Turkish cuisine is world-renowned, with a variety of authentic dishes that will make your taste buds sing. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebabs: A classic Turkish dish with meat, vegetables, and spices cooked on a skewer
  • Börek: A pastry made with thin sheets of dough and filled with cheese, spinach, or meat
  • Baklava: A sweet dessert made with layers of filo pastry, chopped nuts, and honey syrup
  • Turkish coffee: A strong coffee brewed in small cups with thick foam on top
  • Meze: A selection of small dishes to share, including hummus, stuffed grape leaves, and cacik (yogurt dip)

Local Beverages

  • Raki: An anise-flavored liquor served with water and ice
  • Turkish tea: A traditional black tea served in small glasses with sugar cubes
  • Ayran: A cold beverage made from yogurt, water, and salt
  • Boza: A sweet and thick drink made with fermented millet or wheat

Where to Stay in Turkey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Turkey has a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels in Istanbul to budget-friendly hostels and guest houses. Here are a few options:

  • Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at Sultanahmet: A luxury hotel in the heart of Istanbul’s historic district
  • Agora Guesthouse Istanbul: A budget-friendly guesthouse in the Sultanahmet area
  • Cave Suites Cappadocia: A unique hotel with cave rooms and stunning views in Cappadocia
  • Anatolia Resort Hotel: A seaside resort on the Aegean coast near Bodrum

What to Buy in Turkey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Turkey is known for its beautiful textiles, ceramics, and spices. Here are a few things to buy:

  • Turkish carpets and kilims
  • Ceramic bowls and plates from Iznik
  • Turkish Delight (lokum)
  • Spices from the Grand Bazaar in Istanbul
  • Handmade soap and olive oil

Local Culture and Customs of Turkey

Turkey is a predominantly Muslim country with a rich cultural heritage. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially in mosques and other religious sites
  • Remove your shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s home
  • Tip at restaurants and cafes (10-15% is standard)
  • Respect the culture and beliefs of the locals

Best Time to Visit Turkey

The best time to visit Turkey is during the spring (April-May) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and crowds are thinner. Summer (June-August) can be very hot, especially in the southern regions, and crowded with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Turkey

Here are a few things to keep in mind while traveling in Turkey:

Do:

  • Bargain at the Grand Bazaar
  • Try the local cuisine and street food
  • Explore the smaller towns and villages
  • Visit a hamam (Turkish bath) for a traditional spa experience

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection
  • Make derogatory comments about Islam or Turkish culture
  • Take pictures of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Turkey

Turkey is generally a safe country to visit. However, it is important to take precautions while traveling, such as:

  • Avoid visiting border regions with Syria and Iraq
  • Stay alert in crowded areas and be aware of pickpockets
  • Use caution when traveling at night

Health in Turkey

Turkey has a modern healthcare system, but it is important to have travel insurance in case of emergencies. Here are a few health tips:

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat to protect from the sun
  • Seek medical attention if you feel unwell

Getting to Turkey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Turkey

The main international airports in Turkey are:

  • Istanbul Airport (IST)
  • Sabiha Gökçen International Airport (SAW)
  • Ankara Esenboğa Airport (ESB)

Bus Stations Near Turkey

Turkey has a comprehensive long-distance bus network, with several bus companies operating throughout the country. Some of the major bus stations are:

  • Istanbul Esenler Bus Station
  • Ankara Bus Station
  • Antalya Bus Station

Other Options

Turkey has several train stations that connect major cities, but trains can be slower and less comfortable than buses. Taxis, car rentals, and ferries are also available for transportation.

How to Get Around in Turkey

Turkey has a comprehensive transportation network. Here are some of the ways to get around:

  • Long-distance buses: A cheap and reliable way to travel between cities
  • Domestic flights: Flights are available between major cities
  • Trains: Trains connect some of the major cities in Turkey
  • Car rentals: Car rentals are available in many cities
  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in cities and towns

Where to Go Next from Turkey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Turkey, there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting, including:

  • Greece: Take a ferry from Istanbul or visit the island of Rhodes
  • Bulgaria: Visit the charming city of Plovdiv or travel to the Black Sea Coast
  • Georgia: Explore the stunning mountains and vineyards of Georgia
  • Cyprus: Visit the beach resorts of Cyprus or explore the ancient ruins

Conclusion

Turkey is a country with a rich history, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant culture. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Turkey and explore all it has to offer. Happy travels!
