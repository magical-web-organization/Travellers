Introduction to Vernon

Welcome to Vernon, a charming city located in North Central Plains in Texas Panhandle. With a population of around 11,000, Vernon is famous for its rich history, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and friendly locals. Whether you’re here for a quick weekend escape or a week-long vacation, Vernon has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Vernon

Here are some of the must-visit places in Vernon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Red River Valley Museum: showcases the cultural heritage and history of the region through exhibits, displays, and artifacts.

  • Wilbarger County Historical Museum: discover the past of Wilbarger County via historical collections, galleries, and library.

  • Doan’s Adobe: a historic site and museum that tells the story of the Doan family and the early settlers of Vernon.

Natural Attractions of Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Copper Breaks State Park: enjoy hiking, mountain biking, camping, stargazing, birdwatching and more in this natural wonderland.

  • Lake Kemp: a serene lake that offers great fishing and water sports opportunities.

  • Pease River Battlefield: a notable historic trail that offers a scenic walk and an insight into Texas’s history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vernon

The nature trails in Vernon are ideal for hikers and bikers of all abilities. Explore scenic lakes, historic sites, and natural woodlands on these routes:

  • Mesquite Grove Golf Course Trail: A well-maintained path of around 2 miles that is perfect for a brisk walk or bike ride.

  • Lake Kemp Trail: A 3.5-mile hike that takes you around the beautiful lake, with stunning views along the way.

  • Copper Breaks Trail: A moderate 3-mile hike that will reward you with great views of the park’s rugged geological formations.

Best Things to Do in Vernon: Activities and Tours

Apart from the attractions mentioned above, there are plenty of exciting things to do in Vernon. Here are some of the best:

  • Go on a ghost tour of the historic downtown area.
  • Visit one of the many local farmers’ markets to try delicious local produce.
  • Explore the arts district of N. Wilbarger St for paintings, ceramics, jewelry, and more.
  • Take a scenic drive along the Red River Valley National Scenic Byway.

Food and Drink Scene of Vernon

Vernon offers a range of eateries from Southern home-cooked meals to Tex-Mex and Mexican cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken-fried steak: a local favorite usually served with white gravy and mashed potatoes.

  • Tex-Mex nachos: crispy chips, melted cheese, beans, jalapenos, and beef or chicken make for a delicious appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Dr. Pepper: this hometown favorite was originally invented in nearby Dublin, TX.

  • Big Red: a native favorite soda of Texans that can be found throughout the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Vernon

Vernon has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its museums and art galleries. The locals are friendly and welcoming and appreciate having visitors in their city.

Best Time to Visit Vernon

The best time to visit Vernon is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild, and the city is less crowded with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vernon

Do:

  • Visit the historical sites and museums

  • Shop at the local farmers’ market

  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural sites and parks.

  • Trespass on private land

  • Ignore the cultural norms and traditions of Vernon

Safety and Health

Safety in Vernon

Vernon is a safe city for visitors and locals, but keep in mind the following tips to ensure your safety:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night

  • Lock your car and keep valuable items out of sight

  • Don’t leave bags unattended in public areas

Health in Vernon

Vernon has a good healthcare system with several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Pack all necessary prescription medications and any essential health items before your trip.

Getting to Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors have several options when it comes to getting to Vernon.

Airports Near Vernon

  • Sheppard AFB/Wichita Falls Municipal Airport

  • Lawton-Fort Sill Regional Airport

  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport

Bus Stations Near Vernon

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Wichita Falls, TX

Other Options

  • Renting a car from any of the rental companies at nearby airports

  • Taking a taxi or rideshare service from the airport or a nearby city

How to Get Around in Vernon

The best way to get around Vernon is by car as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can also rent a bicycle to explore the city’s parks and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions for travelers who want to explore more of Texas:

Vernon is a hidden gem in Texas Panhandle, known for its rich history, cultural attractions, natural beauty, and friendly locals.
