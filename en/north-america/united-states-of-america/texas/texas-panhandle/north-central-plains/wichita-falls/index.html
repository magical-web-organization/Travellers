Introduction to Wichita Falls

Located in North Central Plains, in Texas Panhandle, Wichita Falls is a charming city that offers a perfect blend of nature, history, and outdoor adventures. Its prime location near the Red River, the Wichita Mountains, and the Great Plains makes it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts, hikers, bikers, and adventurers. The city also boasts a rich cultural heritage with a range of museums, galleries, and historical sites that reflect its diverse history and traditions. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Wichita Falls has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Wichita Falls

Here are some of the top attractions and things to do in Wichita Falls:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of North Texas History: This museum showcases the history of North Texas, from prehistoric times to the present day, through a range of exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Kell House Museum: Built in 1909, this historic house museum offers a glimpse into the lifestyle of a prominent Wichita Falls family in the early 20th century.
  • Wichita Falls Railroad Museum: Housed in a restored 100-year-old depot, this museum showcases the history of the railroad industry in the region.

Natural Attractions of Wichita Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lucy Park: This 170-acre park on the Wichita River offers a range of recreational activities, including hiking and biking trails, playgrounds, fishing spots, and picnic areas.
  • Lake Wichita Park: This scenic park on the banks of Lake Wichita features a range of amenities, including a boat ramp, fishing pier, playground, and picnic tables.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: Located about an hour’s drive from Wichita Falls, this sprawling wildlife refuge offers a range of outdoor adventures, including hiking, camping, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wichita Falls

  • Wee-Chi-Tah Trail: This 20-mile-long trail on the Wichita River offers scenic views of the city and surrounding nature, with a range of difficulty levels for hikers and bikers of all abilities.
  • Lake Wichita Trail: This 11-mile-long trail around Lake Wichita offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding wildlife, with several access points and amenities along the way.
  • Holliday Creek Trails: These three trails in the Holliday Creek Nature Preserve offer a range of difficulty levels and scenic views of the surrounding nature, with a variety of flora and fauna to discover.

Best Things to Do in Wichita Falls: Activities and Tours

  • Wichita Falls Airshow: Held annually in April, this thrilling airshow features acrobatic displays, military aircraft, and aerial performers from around the country.
  • River Bend Nature Center: This nature center offers a range of educational programs, exhibits, and outdoor activities for all ages, including bird watching, gardening, and astronomy.
  • Backdoor Theatre: This community theatre presents a range of live performances, from classic plays to modern productions, throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wichita Falls

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring the best of Wichita Falls:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Museum of North Texas History, explore downtown Wichita Falls, and have lunch at the famous McBride’s Steakhouse.
Day 2 Take a hike on the Wee-Chi-Tah Trail, have a picnic lunch at Lucy Park, and enjoy an evening at the Backdoor Theatre.
Day 3 Drive to the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, and spend the day hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing.
Day 4 Explore the Kell House Museum, visit the Wichita Falls Railroad Museum, and have dinner at the Maplewood restaurant.
Day 5 Take a guided kayak tour of the Wichita River, and have lunch at the Iron Horse Pub in downtown Wichita Falls.
Day 6 Visit the Lake Wichita Park, go fishing or boating, and have a picnic lunch by the lake.
Day 7 Take a nature walk at the River Bend Nature Center, and have a farewell dinner at the Gypsy Kit restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Wichita Falls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tex-Mex cuisine: Wichita Falls is known for its delicious Tex-Mex cuisine, with a range of traditional dishes like enchiladas, fajitas, and chili con carne. Some popular Tex-Mex restaurants in Wichita Falls include El Fenix, Casa Manana, and On the Border.
  • Barbecue: Texas is famous for its barbecue, and Wichita Falls is no exception. Some of the best barbecue joints in town include The Burn Shop, Hog & Pit, and Uncle Ernie’s Pit Barbecue.
  • Burgers and fries: Wichita Falls has a range of burger joints that serve huge burgers, crispy fries, and delicious milkshakes. Some popular options include Willie’s Place, Freddy’s Frozen Custard & Steakburgers, and Who’s on First.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Wichita Falls has a thriving craft beer scene, with several breweries and taprooms that offer a range of local and regional beers. Some popular options include Wichita Falls Brewing Company, The Iron Horse Pub, and Denim & Diamonds.
  • Local wines: Texas is also known for its excellent wines, and Wichita Falls has a range of wineries and tasting rooms that offer a variety of red, white, and sparkling wines. Some popular options include The Winery at Willow Creek, Horseshoe Bend Cellars, and 9th Street Wine Market.

Where to Stay in Wichita Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wichita Falls offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Wichita Falls: This modern hotel is located downtown, close to several attractions and restaurants, and offers a range of amenities, including an indoor pool, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Wichita Falls - MSU Area: This budget-friendly hotel is located near the Midwestern State University, and offers clean and comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a range of amenities, including a pool and fitness center.
  • The Wellington Place Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a historic house in the downtown area, and offers a range of luxurious rooms and suites, as well as a delicious breakfast and personalized service.

What to Buy in Wichita Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Western wear and accessories: Wichita Falls is located in the heart of Texas cowboy country, and offers a range of shops and boutiques that sell western wear and accessories, such as hats, boots, and belt buckles. Some popular options include Cavender’s Western Outfitter, Boot Barn, and Hoots & Hollers.
  • Local honey and preserves: Texas is known for its delicious honey and preserves, and Wichita Falls has several farmers’ markets and shops that sell locally produced honey, jams, and jellies. Some popular options include The Downtown Wichita Falls Farmers Market, Larue’s Farm Market, and The Red River Emporium.
  • Art and crafts: Wichita Falls has a thriving arts and crafts scene, with several galleries and shops that sell locally produced artworks, pottery, and jewelry. Some popular options include the Kemp Center for the Arts, The Gypsy Kit, and Odd Duck Coffee & Art.

Local Culture and Customs of Wichita Falls

Wichita Falls has a rich cultural heritage, with a range of traditions and customs that reflect its diverse history and population. Some of the key aspects of the local culture include:

  • Tex-Mex cuisine and cowboy culture: As mentioned, Wichita Falls is known for its delicious Tex-Mex cuisine and western wear and accessories, which reflect the city’s cowboy culture and Hispanic heritage.
  • Red River rivalry: Wichita Falls is home to the Midwestern State University, which has a longstanding sports rivalry with the nearby University of North Texas in Denton.
  • Military presence: Wichita Falls is also home to the Sheppard Air Force Base, which brings a strong military presence and culture to the city.

Best Time to Visit Wichita Falls

The best time to visit Wichita Falls is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the nature is at its most vibrant. The summer months can be hot and humid, but offer a range of outdoor activities and festivals. The winter months can be cold and snowy, but also offer a range of holiday events and indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wichita Falls

Do:

  • Do explore the local nature and wildlife, and take advantage of the city’s many parks and outdoor adventures.
  • Do try the local Tex-Mex cuisine, barbecue, and craft beer, and explore the city’s food and drink scene.
  • Do visit the local museums and historical sites, and learn about the city’s diverse and rich cultural heritage.

  • Don’t ignore the local customs and traditions, and be respectful of the city’s diverse population and history.
  • Don’t overlook the nearby attractions and destinations, such as the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge and other nearby towns and cities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wichita Falls

Wichita Falls is generally a safe city, with a low crime rate and a friendly and welcoming population. However, as with any city, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and securing your belongings, especially in tourist areas and public transportation.

Health in Wichita Falls

Wichita Falls has a range of healthcare facilities and services, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, that offer quality care for residents and visitors. It’s important to have travel insurance and to carry necessary medications and prescriptions when visiting the city.

Getting to Wichita Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wichita Falls

The closest airport to Wichita Falls is the Wichita Falls Regional Airport (SPS), which offers several daily flights to Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) via American Eagle. Other nearby airports include the Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) and the Oklahoma City Will Rogers World Airport (OKC), both of which offer a range of domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Wichita Falls

Wichita Falls also has a Greyhound bus station, which offers bus services to and from several destinations in the region and across the country. Other bus operators that serve the city include Jefferson Lines and Tornado Bus Company.

Other Options

Wichita Falls is located near several major highways, including Interstate 44, US-281, and US-287, which offer easy access to and from the city. The city also has a range of car rental services, taxi companies, and ride-sharing apps, such as Uber and Lyft, that offer convenient and affordable transportation options.

How to Get Around in Wichita Falls

Wichita Falls is a walkable and bike-friendly city, with several miles of hiking and biking trails, and several bike rental and share programs. The city also has a range of public transportation options, including a bus system operated by Falls Ride, and several taxi and shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Wichita Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wichita Falls is located near several popular destinations and excursions, including:

  • Dallas/Fort Worth: Located about 2 hours’ drive from Wichita Falls, Dallas and Fort Worth offer a range of attractions, including museums, galleries, restaurants, and shopping.
  • Oklahoma City: Located about 2.5 hours’ drive from Wichita Falls, Oklahoma City offers a range of cultural and historical attractions, including museums, parks, and botanical gardens.
  • Route 66: Located just north of Wichita Falls, the historic Route 66 offers a range of roadside attractions, museums, and nostalgic Americana, with several charming towns and cities along the way.

Conclusion

Wichita Falls is a hidden gem in the heart of Texas, offering a range of natural and cultural attractions, delicious food and drink, and friendly and welcoming locals. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Wichita Falls has something for everyone, and we hope this travel guide helps you explore the best of this charming city.
