If you are searching for a relaxing and off-the-beaten-path destination in Texas, look no further than Snyder. Known as the biggest town in Scurry County, Snyder is situated in the west-central region of Texas Panhandle, in the heart of Llano Estacado.

Whether you want to explore the rich history of Snyder, admire its stunning natural beauty, or simply indulge in its delicious local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this charming town. In this comprehensive Snyder travel guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit attractions, top hikes and bike routes, best things to do, and where to stay. So pack your bags, and let’s explore the hidden gem of Texas Panhandle - Snyder.

Introduction to Snyder

Snyder, originally named “Hide Town” due to a nearby buffalo hide camp, was established in 1882 as a railway center by the Santa Fe Railroad Company. The town’s name was later changed to Snyder after William Henry Snyder, one of the company’s directors.

Snyder boasts a rich history that dates back to the time of Comanches, who roamed the area for centuries before the arrival of Spanish explorers. The town witnessed the arrival of settlers who transformed it into a bustling agricultural and oil center. Today, Snyder epitomizes the warm Texan hospitality and charming small-town vibe that attracts visitors to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Snyder

Snyder has an abundance of cultural and natural attractions that are worth exploring. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Snyder.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Scurry County Museum: Located in the old Railway Depot, the museum showcases the history of Scurry County through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and research facilities. You can learn about the lives of early settlers and the oil boom that transformed Snyder into an industrial center.

  • National WASP WWII Museum: This museum commemorates the Women Airforce Service Pilots (WASP), who trained in 1943 to fly military aircraft during WWII. The museum features exhibits, memorabilia, and a restored WWII aircraft.

  • Snyder Cemetery: This peaceful cemetery is the final resting place of prominent figures from Snyder’s past, including the founder William Henry Snyder.

Natural Attractions of Snyder: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake J.B. Thomas: This serene lake offers ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as fishing, boating, and camping. You can also enjoy stunning views of the sunset and relax by the lakeshore.

  • Scurry County Wind Farm: The Scurry County Wind Farm is a sight to behold. It consists of 100 wind turbines that generate renewable energy. You can take a self-guided tour of the wind farm to learn about the turbines and their operation.

  • Mt. Blanco Fossil Museum: This museum is a must-visit for fossil enthusiasts. It features fossil exhibits, including dinosaur skeletons, plants, and mammals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Snyder

Snyder offers several scenic trails that are perfect for hiking or biking enthusiasts. The top routes include:

  • Scurry County Park Trail: This 3.5 mile-long trail takes you around the Scurry County Park and offers beautiful views of Lake J.B. Thomas.

  • Hillside Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for a short hike and provides an opportunity to spot various bird species and wildlife.

  • Big Creek Trail: This trail runs along Big Creek and is ideal for an easy bike ride or hike. You can enjoy scenic views of the creek and visit the nearby picnic area.

Best Things to Do in Snyder: Activities and Tours

Snyder offers many fun-filled activities that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Snyder:

  • Explore the Downtown: Stroll through the charming downtown of Snyder, where you can find many interesting shops, restaurants, and boutiques.

  • Attend the Scurry County Rodeo: This annual rodeo showcases the Western culture and traditions of Texas and is a must-attend event for visitors.

  • Take a Brewery Tour: The Square Root is a local microbrewery that offers tours of their facility, where you can learn about the brewing process and taste their renowned craft beers.

Food and Drink Scene of Snyder

Snyder boasts a vibrant food culture that is influenced by Texan and Mexican cuisine. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texas-style Barbecue: Snyder is famous for its barbecue. Visit places like Cornerstone BBQ for the best brisket and ribs in town.

  • Tex-Mex Delights: Enjoy some authentic Tex-Mex cuisine at El Nuevo Vallarta or South of the Border. These restaurants serve delicious tacos, burritos, and fajitas.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: The Square Root offers some of the best craft beers in town, including Roaster’s Red Ale and Opal Peach Wheat.

  • Bar Drinks: Enjoy a margarita or a Long Island iced tea at The Grill if you are in the mood for a classic cocktail.

  • Snyder Inn: A budget-friendly motel with clean rooms and friendly staff.

  • 1812 Ancestral Trails Inn: A charming bed and breakfast with antique furnishings and a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Snyder: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home some authentic Texan souvenirs, here’s a list of things you should consider buying:

  • Barbecue Sauce: Buy some of the local barbecue sauce from Southwest Specialty Foods.

  • Handmade Pottery: The Clay Co. pottery studio offers unique ceramic pieces and pottery classes.

  • Western Wear: Check out the Cowpokes Boots & Saddles or Pardner’s clothing store to buy western wear and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Snyder

Snyder is known for its warm hospitality and laid-back charm. People here are friendly, courteous, and are proud of their rich history and traditions. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as tipping at restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit Snyder

The best time to visit Snyder is from late spring to fall when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The average temperature in Snyder during the summer is around 97°F, and during winter, it drops to 34°F.

Safety and Health

Snyder is a safe and secure town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as while driving on highways at night, and always lock their vehicles and valuables in their accommodation.

Safety in Snyder

  • Stay alert while driving on the highways, especially at night.

  • Keep your valuables safe and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

  • Follow the local guidelines and rules while indulging in outdoor activities.

Health in Snyder

  • Snyder has various medical facilities, including Snyder Regional Medical Center and Scurry County Health Unit.

  • Visitors should carry adequate health insurance and take precautions while engaging in outdoor activities, such as wearing appropriate gear and sunscreen.

Getting to Snyder: Transportation Options and Logistics

Snyder is located within a four-hour drive from major cities like Dallas, Austin, and Midland. The town has no direct railway station or airport, but visitors can access Snyder via:

Airports Near Snyder

  • Midland International Airport (85 miles)

  • Lubbock Preston Smith International Airport (90 miles)

  • Abilene Regional Airport (110 miles)

Bus Stations Near Snyder

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Sweetwater (30 miles)

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Abilene (100 miles)

Other Options

  • Drive: Snyder is well connected via highways and is easily accessible through private transportation.

  • Taxi: Local taxis like Lucky 7 Taxi and KD Limo and Taxi offer transportation services in and around Snyder.

How to Get Around in Snyder

Snyder is a small town, and most of the attractions are within a short driving distance from each other. Visitors can rent cars or drive their private vehicles to explore Snyder’s various attractions. Alternatively, visitors can opt for taxi services or rent a bike to explore Snyder’s scenic trails and natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Snyder: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Snyder is a great base for exploring the surrounding attractions and destinations.

  • Lubbock: Known as the “Hub City,” Lubbock is a vibrant city that offers cultural and natural attractions like the Buddy Holly Center, Texas Tech University, and Lubbock Lake Landmark.

  • Abilene: Abilene is a historic town with attractions like the Frontier Texas Museum, Abilene Zoo, and the 12th Armored Division Memorial Museum.

Conclusion

Snyder is a hidden gem in Texas Panhandle, offering visitors a blend of culture, nature, and gastronomy. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality, Snyder is a must-visit destination for those looking to explore the offbeat path of Texas. So pack your bags, and get ready to experience the charms of Snyder.
