Introduction to Post

Post is a small town located in the Texas Panhandle, in the heart of the Llano Estacado region. The town is named after C.W. Post, who founded it in 1907 as a model community. Today, Post is known for its historic buildings and cultural attractions, as well as its outdoor adventures and scenic beauty. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor activities, Post has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Post

Post is home to a number of culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to visit in Post:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Garza Theatre: This historic theater was built in 1920 and is now home to a performing arts center.
  • The Post Dispatch Museum: This museum showcases the history of the town’s newspaper, which has been in operation since 1907.
  • The Mercantile Building: This historic building was once home to a department store and now houses a gift shop and museum.
  • The Heritage House Museum: This museum is housed in an old Presbyterian church and showcases the history of the town and its founder, C.W. Post.

Natural Attractions of Post: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Caprock Canyons State Park: Just a short drive from Post, this park offers stunning views of the Texas Panhandle’s rugged landscape, as well as hiking, biking, and fishing opportunities.
  • Mesa Vista Ranch: This 100,000-acre ranch offers guided tours of its beautiful landscapes, including canyons, mesas, and native wildlife.
  • Lake Alan Henry: This lake is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and fishing, and is surrounded by scenic countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Post

Post and the surrounding area offer a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging multi-day treks. Some of the top routes include:

  • The Caprock Canyons Trailway: This 64-mile trail runs through the heart of the Texas Panhandle and offers stunning views of the region’s rugged beauty.
  • The North Ridge Trail: This 4-mile trail offers scenic views of Lake Alan Henry and the surrounding countryside.
  • The Running Water Draw Trail: This 6-mile trail takes hikers through the heart of Post and offers stunning views of the town’s historic buildings and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Post: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Post offers a variety of other activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Post include:

  • Take a historic walking tour: Post’s downtown area is filled with historic buildings and landmarks, making it the perfect spot for a walking tour.
  • Go birdwatching: Post and the surrounding area are home to a variety of birds, including the endangered Texas horned lizard and the golden eagle.
  • Take a photography tour: From the rugged canyons to the picturesque countryside, Post and the surrounding area offer plenty of opportunities for stunning photos.

Week-Long Itinerary for Post

If you’re planning a trip to Post and want to make the most of your time, consider following this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Post and take a historic walking tour of the downtown area.
  • Day 2: Take a tour of Mesa Vista Ranch and enjoy the stunning scenery and wildlife.
  • Day 3: Visit Caprock Canyons State Park and hike the Caprock Canyons Trailway.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at Lake Alan Henry, swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Day 5: Take a photography tour of the countryside and capture some stunning shots of the Texas Panhandle.
  • Day 6: Visit The Post Dispatch Museum and The Heritage House Museum to learn more about the town’s history.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy your final day in Post, perhaps by visiting a local restaurant or catching a show at the Garza Theatre.
