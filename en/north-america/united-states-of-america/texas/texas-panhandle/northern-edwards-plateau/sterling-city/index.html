Introduction to Sterling City

Nestled in the Northern Edwards Plateau, Sterling City is a charming Texan town that promises outdoor adventure, rich history, and warm hospitality. The town was founded in the late 19th century and is named after W. S. Sterling, a famous Texas rancher and politician.

Sterling City serves as the county seat of Sterling County and has a population of around 1,000 people. Its small size and quaint atmosphere make it the perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat in the heart of Texas.

List of Places to Visit in Sterling City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sterling City has a rich history that can be explored in its many museums and historical places. The highlights include:

  • Sterling County Museum: This museum showcases the history of Sterling County and the surrounding area. Exhibits include artifacts from the early pioneers, Native American tribes, and the oil boom in the early 20th century.

  • Fort Concho National Historic Landmark: This fort, located in nearby San Angelo, was a vital part of the western frontier during the 19th century. Visitors can explore the fort’s buildings and learn about life on the Texas frontier.

  • The McCarty House: Built in 1909, this historic home belonged to a prominent local family. The house has been lovingly restored and is now open to the public for tours.

Natural Attractions of Sterling City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sterling City and the surrounding area boast a wealth of natural beauty, from rolling hills to picturesque rivers. Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to explore, including:

  • North Concho River: This scenic river winds its way through Sterling City and is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.

  • Eckert James River Bat Cave: This natural wonder is located just outside Sterling City and is home to millions of Mexican free-tailed bats. Visitors can witness the bats’ nightly emergence from the cave from April through October.

  • Colorado River Municipal Water District Trails: These scenic trails are perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding. The trails wind through the picturesque countryside and offer stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sterling City

Sterling City’s natural beauty makes it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following routes are highly recommended:

  • Sterling City Lake Trail: This easy 1.5-mile trail circles the town’s lake and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Eckert James River Bat Cave Trail: This moderate 2.8-mile trail leads to the Eckert James River Bat Cave. The trail offers scenic views of the river and the surrounding hills.

  • Colorado River Municipal Water District Trails: These extensive trails cover over 100 miles and offer a variety of routes for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Sterling City: Activities and Tours

Sterling City offers a wide variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Sterling City Rodeo: This annual rodeo, held in July, features bull riding, steer roping, barrel racing, and more.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Sterling City is located in the heart of Texas wine country. Visitors can take a tour of the local wineries and enjoy tastings of some of the region’s best wines.

  • Rockhounding: The surrounding countryside is rich in minerals and fossils. Visitors can explore the area for agate, jasper, and petrified wood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sterling City

To make the most of your visit to Sterling City, we recommend the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Sterling City’s downtown, including the Sterling County Museum and local shops and restaurants.

  • Day 2: Take a scenic drive to nearby San Angelo and visit Fort Concho National Historic Landmark.

  • Day 3: Enjoy an outdoor adventure, such as a hike on the Colorado River Municipal Water District Trails or a visit to the Eckert James River Bat Cave.

  • Day 4: Visit the McCarty House and learn about the history of this charming town.

  • Day 5: Tour local wineries and enjoy a Texas wine tasting experience.

  • Day 6: Attend the annual Sterling City Rodeo.

  • Day 7: Explore the countryside for gems and minerals.

Food and Drink Scene of Sterling City

Must-Try Dishes

Sterling City is home to a variety of dining options, from Tex-Mex to BBQ. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Brisket: This Texas classic can be found at many of the local barbecue restaurants.

  • Tacos: Whether you prefer breakfast tacos or traditional Mexican tacos, Sterling City has plenty of options to choose from.

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This hearty dish is a Texas staple, and Sterling City serves up some of the best.

Local Beverages

No trip to Sterling City is complete without trying some of the region’s famous beverages. The following drinks are highly recommended:

  • Texas Wine: Visit local wineries for a taste of some of the region’s best wines.

  • Shiner Beer: This Texas brewed beer is a favorite of locals and can be found at many of the town’s bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Sterling City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sterling City has a variety of accommodations to suit any traveler’s needs. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Hammerhead Lodge: This cozy lodge offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Sterling Country Inn: This locally owned inn offers spacious rooms and a warm Texas welcome.

What to Buy in Sterling City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sterling City offers a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Agate and Jasper: These gemstones are abundant in the surrounding area and can be found at local shops and markets.

  • Handmade Crafts and Artwork: Local artisans create a variety of handmade crafts and artwork, including pottery, quilts, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Sterling City

Sterling City is a friendly and welcoming town with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs, including:

  • Don’t litter: Texans take pride in their countryside and expect visitors to do the same.

  • Do dress appropriately: Loose-fitting, lightweight clothing is recommended for the hot Texas summers.

Best Time to Visit Sterling City

The best time to visit Sterling City is in the fall and spring when the weather is milder. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be chilly and wet.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sterling City

Sterling City is a safe and friendly town with low rates of crime. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Sterling City

Sterling City has several medical facilities, including a hospital and a clinic. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Sterling City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sterling City

The closest airport to Sterling City is Midland International Air and Space Port, located about 100 miles to the west. San Angelo Regional Airport is another option, located about 50 miles to the south.

Bus Stations Near Sterling City

There are no bus stations located in Sterling City. However, Greyhound offers bus service to nearby San Angelo and Midland.

Other Options

Many visitors to Sterling City choose to drive. The town is located along Highway 87, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around Sterling City

The best way to get around Sterling City is by car. The town is small and easy to navigate, with plenty of free parking available downtown.

Where to Go Next from Sterling City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sterling City is just a short drive from several other popular Texas destinations. Some of the best nearby excursions include:

  • San Angelo: This charming Texan town is located about an hour’s drive south of Sterling City. Highlights include the historic downtown and the San Angelo State Park.

  • Fort Davis: This historic fort, located about 150 miles west of Sterling City, played a vital role in the Texas frontier. Visitors can explore the fort’s buildings and learn about life on the western frontier.

  • Big Bend National Park: One of Texas’ most beloved natural treasures, Big Bend National Park is located about 200 miles southwest of Sterling City. Visitors can explore its rugged terrain, including the Chisos Mountains and the Rio Grande River.

Conclusion

Sterling City may be a small town, but it offers adventure, history, and hospitality in spades. From exploring the North Concho River to enjoying a glass of Texas wine, there’s something for everyone in Sterling City. Plan your visit today and discover the charm of this Texan gem.
