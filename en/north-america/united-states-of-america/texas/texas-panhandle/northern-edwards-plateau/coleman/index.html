Coleman is a small town located in the Northern Edwards Plateau region of Texas Panhandle. Despite its size, this charming town is rich in history and natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for a relaxing weekend getaway or a longer vacation. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and provide practical information to help you plan your next trip to Coleman.

Introduction to Coleman

Located approximately two hours from both Fort Worth and Lubbock, Coleman is a small town that is big on personality. Historically known for its ranching and oil industries, the town is now a popular destination for tourists looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. With a population of just over 5,000 people, Coleman is a place where visitors can explore local history, enjoy stunning natural scenery, and experience true Texas hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Coleman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coleman County Historical Museum: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the history and culture of the region, with exhibits on ranching, oil drilling, and early settlers.
  • Coleman County Jail Museum: Housed in the former county jail, this museum offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of early inmates with artifacts, recreated cells, and even the original hanging rope.
  • Santa Anna Battlefield: History buffs will appreciate a visit to the site of the Battle of San Jacinto. Visitors can explore the battlefield and learn more about the Texas Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Coleman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Coleman: This 1,600-acre lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. Visitors can rent boats or kayaks from the marina or simply relax on the sandy beach.
  • Coleman City Park: This beautiful park is perfect for hiking, picnicking, or just enjoying the natural beauty of the area. Visitors can explore the walking trails, playgrounds, and even a frisbee golf course.
  • Coleman Nature Trail: This scenic trail winds through oak trees, cedar breaks, and limestone ridges, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coleman

  • Coleman Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely stroll, with benches and interpretive signs along the way.
  • Coleman City Park Trail: This trail offers a more challenging terrain, with rocky paths and steep inclines, but rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Lake Coleman Trail: This 11.5 mile trail loops around the lake, offering a mix of easy and difficult terrain for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Coleman: Activities and Tours

  • Coleman Rodeo: Held every summer, the annual Coleman Rodeo is a must-see event. Visitors can enjoy the thrill of bull riding, barrel racing, and more.
  • Pecan Capital Trade Days: Held on the third weekend of every month, this outdoor flea market offers a variety of local crafts, food, and art.
  • Hunting and Fishing: With an abundance of deer, turkey, and bass, the area around Coleman is popular with hunters and fishermen.

Food and Drink Scene of Coleman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: For an authentic Texas dining experience, head to Buzzard Billy’s Smokehouse and Grill where succulent brisket and tender ribs are cooked low and slow over hickory wood.
  • Tex-Mex: For delicious Tex-Mex fare, try Mi Familia Mexican Restaurant, where you can enjoy sizzling fajitas, crispy tacos, and refreshing margaritas.
  • Pies: If you have a sweet tooth, you won’t want to miss the homemade pies from Bugz’s BBQ & Grill. Be sure to try their award-winning pecan pie.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The Coleman Wine & Beer Depot is a local favorite for beer lovers. With a rotating selection of Texas craft beers, visitors can sample brews from all around the state.
  • Wine: For wine enthusiasts, the neighboring town of Brownwood is home to several wineries, such as Spirit of Texas Winery and Wedding Oak Winery.

Where to Stay in Coleman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Coleman Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms with basic amenities, such as free Wi-Fi and a 24-hour reception desk.
  • Best Western Coleman Inn: This mid-range hotel offers spacious rooms with modern amenities, such as an outdoor pool and fitness center.
  • The Wells Guest House: For a unique and intimate stay, this charming guest house offers individually decorated rooms, a home-cooked breakfast, and a lovely garden for guests to relax in.

What to Buy in Coleman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pecans: Given that Coleman is known as the Pecan Capital of Texas, it’s no surprise that pecans make for a unique and delicious souvenir. Head to Miller Pecan Company for a wide selection of treats, including pecan brittle, flavored nuts, and even pecan oil for cooking.
  • Western Wear: No trip to Texas would be complete without some authentic western wear. Check out The Feedlot, a local shop that sells cowboy boots, hats, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Coleman

Coleman is a friendly and welcoming town where visitors can experience true Texas hospitality. As with most small towns, locals take pride in their community, and visitors are expected to be respectful and mindful of the town’s cultural norms and customs. Texans are known for their politeness, so be sure to greet locals with a friendly “Howdy!” when you meet them.

Best Time to Visit Coleman

The best time to visit Coleman is in the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and the humidity is low. Summers can be hot and humid, making outdoor activities less enjoyable, and winters can be chilly and wet, with occasional snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coleman

Do:

  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring.
  • Do try the local food and beverages.
  • Do be respectful of locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and bring plenty of water.
  • Don’t forget to obey local laws and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coleman

Coleman is a safe town with relatively low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise common sense safety precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Coleman

Visitors to Coleman are advised to bring any necessary prescription medications with them as pharmacies may not be readily available. Emergency medical facilities are available in nearby towns such as Abilene and Brownwood.

Getting to Coleman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coleman

The closest major airport to Coleman is Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW), which is approximately 150 miles away. A smaller airport, Abilene Regional Airport, is 60 miles away and offers regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Coleman

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in nearby Abilene, which is approximately 60 miles from Coleman.

Other Options

Driving is the most practical way of getting to Coleman, and visitors can rent cars from major rental companies such as Avis and Hertz. Alternatively, visitors can take a taxi or rideshare service from nearby towns to reach Coleman.

How to Get Around in Coleman

While Coleman is a small town, visitors will still need a car to easily explore the surrounding countryside and attractions. Alternatively, visitors can rent bikes to explore the town’s hiking and biking trails.

Where to Go Next from Coleman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Abilene: Located just 60 miles from Coleman, Abilene is home to numerous cultural attractions, such as the Grace Museum and the Paramount Theatre.
  • Brownwood: This charming town is known for its historic downtown district, craft breweries, and wineries. Brownwood is located approximately 45 miles from Coleman.

Conclusion

Coleman, Texas, is a hidden gem in the Northern Edwards Plateau region of Texas. Visitors can explore local history, experience natural beauty, and enjoy authentic Texas hospitality. With its mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine, Coleman is a perfect destination for a relaxing getaway or a longer vacation.
