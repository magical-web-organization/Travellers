Introduction to Big Spring

Located in the Northern Edwards Plateau in Texas Panhandle, Big Spring is a hidden gem often overlooked by most travelers. This small yet charming town offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and a warm welcoming community. From scenic drives and outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and local specialities, Big Spring has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Big Spring

Big Spring has a plethora of places to explore, catering to all types of travelers. We have categorized them into two sections: Culturally Important Attractions and Natural Attractions

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Heritage Museum of Big Spring: This museum showcases the rich history, culture, and traditions of Big Spring and its surrounding areas. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, photographs, and exhibits, making it an ideal place for history lovers.
  • Hangar 25 Air Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving aviation history and honors the contributions of military personnel to the development of aviation. Visitors can see a variety of vintage aircraft, military vehicles, and memorabilia.
  • The Potton House: Built-in 1909, this restored house offers visitors a glimpse into the lifestyle and architecture of the early 1900s. It is a perfect place to learn about the history of Big Springs and its residents.

Natural Attractions of Big Spring: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Spring State Park: This park is a nature lover’s paradise, offering several hiking trails, picnic spots, and scenic views. Visitors can also explore the park’s natural springs and creek, making it an ideal place for a day trip.
  • Comanche Trail Park: This park covers over 400 acres and has something to suit every outdoor enthusiast, from fishing and camping to hiking and mountain biking. It also has a golf course, playgrounds, and picnic areas, making it an excellent place for families to spend a day out.
  • Borden County Museum: Located in nearby Gail, Texas, this museum showcases the natural history of the area and has an impressive collection of dinosaur fossils, making it a must-visit for anyone interested in paleontology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Spring

Big Spring has some fantastic hiking and mountain biking trails that cater to both beginners and experienced outdoor enthusiasts.

Comanche Trail

This trail is about four miles long and runs along the beautiful Comanche Creek. It is suitable for both hiking and mountain biking and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountainous landscape.

Big Spring State Park Trail

This park has several scenic trails, each offering unique views and terrain. The 1.5-mile Scenic Mountain Trail offers panoramic views of the Big Spring valley, making it an excellent choice for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Big Spring: Activities and Tours

Big Spring is home to a variety of activities and tours, catering to all ages and interests. Here are some of our top picks:

Horseback Riding

Explore the beautiful Big Spring countryside on horseback with local operators such as The Rusty Spur or Rockin’ R Ranch.

Guided Tours

Discover the best of Big Spring with guided tours offered by local companies like Permian Basin Tours. Visitors can explore local museums, natural attractions, and historical sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Big Spring

Day 1: Visit the Heritage Museum of Big Spring and learn about the town’s rich history and culture.

Day 2: Explore the natural springs and scenic views at Big Spring State Park.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of some of the historical sites in Big Spring, including The Potton House and Hangar 25 Air Museum.

Day 4: Go on a hiking or biking adventure at Comanche Trail Park.

Day 5: Indulge in some retail therapy and buy local products at the Big Spring Farmers Market.

Day 6: Check out the local food and drink scene and try some of the must-try dishes or beverages (more on this in the next section).

Day 7: Take a day trip to Borden County Museum and dig into the fascinating history of this area.

Food and Drink Scene of Big Spring

Big Spring has a vibrant food and drink scene, serving up local delicacies that visitors shouldn’t miss out on.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brisket: Texas is known for its barbecue scene, and Big Spring is no exception. Head to local favorites like Que’d Up BBQ for some mouth-watering brisket.
  • Chicken Fried Steak: This hearty meal is a local favorite, and visitors can try it at restaurants such as K-Bob’s Steakhouse and Penny’s Diner.
  • Mexican Cuisine: Big Spring has many excellent Mexican restaurants such as Maria’s Mexican Restaurant and Casa Blanca, serving up traditional and modern dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Big Spring has an emerging craft beer scene, with local breweries such as Settles and Deadman’s earning recognition for their unique brews.
  • Sweet Tea: The South is known for its sweet tea, and Big Spring is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing glass of sweet tea at local restaurants and diners.

Where to Stay in Big Spring: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Big Spring has several accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Settles: This iconic hotel, built-in 1930, has been restored to its former glory and now offers visitors a luxurious stay with modern amenities.
  • Baymont by Wyndham Big Spring: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Big Spring Motel: Visitors looking for a quaint and cozy place to stay can opt for this guesthouse, offering comfortable rooms and a personalized touch.

What to Buy in Big Spring: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Big Spring has a vibrant local produce and arts scene, and visitors can take home some unique and authentic souvenirs.

  • Local Honey: Big Spring has many local beekeepers, and visitors can buy their honey at stores like Happy Hives Honey and Wolf Creek Honey.
  • Artifacts: The Heritage Museum of Big Spring has a gift shop that sells authentic artifacts and souvenirs.
  • Pecans: Texas is famous for its pecans, and visitors can get their hands on the local variety at the Big Spring Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Spring

Big Spring has a warm and welcoming community, and visitors should respect local customs and norms.

  • Visitors should greet locals with a smile and a kind word, as hospitality is highly valued in this part of Texas.
  • Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending local events.
  • Visitors should always ask for permission before taking pictures of locals or their property.

Best Time to Visit Big Spring

Big Spring has a warm and dry climate, making it an ideal destination throughout the year. However, spring and fall are the best times to visit as the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Spring

Do:

  • Visit the local farmers market and buy fresh produce and local products.
  • Try the local cuisine, including barbecue, Mexican food, and chicken-fried steak.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or pollute the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t be disrespectful or insensitive towards locals or their beliefs.
  • Don’t visit religious sites in revealing or inappropriate clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Spring

Big Spring is a safe town, but visitors should exercise common sense and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night.
  • Lock your car doors and windows when leaving the car.
  • Keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Big Spring

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to protect themselves from the harsh sun and heat:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin.
  • Wear hats and loose-fitting clothes to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Big Spring: Transportation Options and Logistics

Big Spring is well connected and easy to access. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Big Spring

  • Midland International Air and Spaceport: This airport is about an hour away from Big Spring.

Bus Stations Near Big Spring

  • Greyhound: The bus station is located in downtown Big Spring.

Other Options

  • Driving: Big Spring is easily accessible by car as it is located on the crossroads of Highway 87 and Interstate 20.

How to Get Around in Big Spring

Big Spring is a small town, and most places can be accessed easily on foot or by car. Visitors can also rent bicycles from local operators such as Pedego Electric Bikes.

Where to Go Next from Big Spring: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Big Spring has several nearby destinations that visitors can explore:

  • Fort Davis National Historic Site: Located about two hours away from Big Spring, this site offers visitors a glimpse into the history and culture of the old west.
  • Monahans Sandhills State Park: This park is about an hour away from Big Spring and offers visitors the opportunity to explore sand dunes and go sledding.

Conclusion

Big Spring is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and rich cultural history. From outdoor adventures and scenic views to local delicacies and souvenirs, Big Spring has something to offer everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Big Spring, Texas, and make the most of your visit.
