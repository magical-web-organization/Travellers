Introduction to Del Rio

Located in the heart of Big Bend Country, Del Rio offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. The city sits on the banks of the Rio Grande, providing plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. With a population of about 35,000 people, Del Rio retains a small-town charm while also offering all the amenities of a larger city.

List of Places to Visit in Del Rio

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Val Verde Winery - Established in 1883, Val Verde Winery is the oldest continuously operated winery in Texas. Visitors can tour the winery and enjoy tastings of its award-winning wines.
  2. Whitehead Memorial Museum - This museum showcases exhibits and artifacts that chronicle the history and culture of Del Rio and the surrounding area.
  3. Amistad National Recreation Area - Amistad National Recreation Area is best known for its beautiful lake and rugged canyons. The park has a number of historical sites, including ancient rock paintings and the ruins of the 19th century Fort Clark.

Natural Attractions of Del Rio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Amistad - This reservoir on the Rio Grande is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. It’s surrounded by spectacular scenery and offers great views of the canyons and cliffs that rise up from the water.
  2. Seminole Canyon State Park - Seminole Canyon is home to some of the oldest rock art in North America, including vivid paintings created by the ancient people who once lived in the area. Visitors can hike scenic trails, take guided tours, and learn about the rich cultural history of the region.
  3. San Felipe Springs - This natural spring is a popular spot for picnics, swimming, and other outdoor activities. The crystal-clear water is also home to a variety of fish and other aquatic creatures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Del Rio

  1. Amistad National Recreation Area - The park has a network of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the lake, canyons, and surrounding landscape.
  2. Val Verde Park - This park is located on the banks of the Rio Grande and has a number of scenic trails that wind through the park’s lush vegetation.
  3. Pecos River Trail - This 12-mile trail follows the course of the Pecos River and offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Del Rio: Activities and Tours

  1. Bird Watching - Del Rio is a popular spot for birdwatchers, with the area home to over 300 species of birds. Seminole Canyon State Park and the Amistad National Recreation Area are two of the best spots for birding in the region.
  2. Wine Tasting - Visitors can sample wines at Val Verde Winery, the oldest winery in Texas, and learn about the winemaking process.
  3. River Rafting - The Rio Grande provides excellent opportunities for river rafting and kayaking. There are a number of local outfitters who offer guided trips down the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Del Rio

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Mesquite-grilled steak - Texas is known for its steaks, and Del Rio is no exception. Some of the best steaks in the region can be found at local restaurants like The Cactus Cafe and Vallarta’s.
  2. Chili con carne - This classic Tex-Mex dish is a staple of Del Rio cuisine. It’s served in many local restaurants, including Shelby’s Del Rio Restaurant and The Chili Pepper Cafe.
  3. Fajitas - Another Tex-Mex classic, fajitas can be found on menus throughout Del Rio. Be sure to try them at El Patio or El Charro restaurants.

Local Beverages

  1. Wine - Val Verde Winery produces a range of award-winning wines, including reds, whites, and sparkling varieties.
  2. Beer - Del Rio is home to several local breweries, including Laughing Barrel Brewing Co and Big Bend Brewing Company.
  3. Margaritas - No trip to Texas is complete without trying a margarita. Del Rio has many local restaurants that serve up this classic cocktail, including Chili’s and Carlos Cantina.

Where to Stay in Del Rio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Best Western Inn of Del Rio - This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and a hot tub. It’s conveniently located near many of Del Rio’s top attractions.
  2. Ramada by Wyndham Del Rio - The Ramada is another great option for visitors to Del Rio. It offers clean, comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a pool and a fitness center.
  3. Amistad Lodge - This rustic lodge is located within the Amistad National Recreation Area, making it the perfect spot for nature lovers. It offers cabins and campsites, as well as a restaurant and gift shop.

What to Buy in Del Rio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Mesquite Wood - Del Rio is located in a region that’s known for its mesquite trees, which produce a dense and fragrant wood that’s prized by woodworkers and barbecue enthusiasts.
  2. Mexican-style blankets - These colorful blankets are a common sight in Del Rio’s numerous gift shops.
  3. Local Art - Del Rio is home to many talented artists, and visitors can find unique pieces at galleries like Gallery 313 and Fragments of the Past.

Local Culture and Customs of Del Rio

Del Rio has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its food, music, and traditions. The city has a strong Mexican-American influence, and celebrations like Cinco de Mayo and Dia de los Muertos are important cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Del Rio

The best time to visit Del Rio is from October to April, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, with temperatures often reaching into the 90s.

Do’s and Don’ts of Del Rio

Do:

  1. Do try the local food - Del Rio has a great food scene, with a mix of Tex-Mex and traditional Mexican cuisine.
  2. Do bring comfortable shoes - Many of Del Rio’s top attractions are best explored on foot, so be sure to bring comfortable shoes.
  3. Do learn about the history and culture of the area - Del Rio has a rich history and a unique blend of cultures, so take some time to learn about it.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t underestimate the heat - Summers in Del Rio can be hot and humid, so be sure to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.
  2. Don’t forget your camera - Del Rio has some of the most stunning natural scenery in Texas, so be sure to bring your camera to capture it.
  3. Don’t ignore the local customs - Del Rio has a rich cultural heritage, so be sure to be respectful and learn about local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Del Rio

Del Rio is a safe city overall, but visitors should exercise caution, especially at night. Be sure to lock your car and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.

Health in Del Rio

Del Rio has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should always carry any necessary medication with them and take necessary precautions when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Del Rio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Del Rio

Del Rio International Airport is located just a few miles outside of the city. It offers daily flights to and from major cities like Dallas and Houston.

Bus Stations Near Del Rio

Greyhound operates a bus station in Del Rio, offering connections to cities throughout Texas and the United States.

Other Options

Del Rio is located on the major transportation routes through Texas, so driving is also a popular option. Rental cars are available at the airport and throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Del Rio

The best way to get around Del Rio is by car. Rental cars are available at the airport and throughout the city. Taxis and rideshare services like Uber are also available.

Where to Go Next from Del Rio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Big Bend National Park - Located a few hours south of Del Rio, Big Bend National Park is one of the most stunning natural areas in the United States, with canyons, mountains, and desert landscapes.
  2. San Antonio - Located a few hours east of Del Rio, San Antonio is home to the Alamo, River Walk, and many other attractions.
  3. Houston - Located about five hours east of Del Rio, Houston is a major city with a thriving arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Del Rio is a hidden gem in the heart of Texas, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re here to explore the ancient rock paintings at Seminole Canyon State Park or sample award-winning wines at Val Verde Winery, Del Rio has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this vibrant city has to offer.
