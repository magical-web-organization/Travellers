Introduction to Fort Davis

Located in the Big Bend Country region of Trans-Pecos, Fort Davis is a historic town that takes you back in time. Its rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures make it a must-visit destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Davis

There are several places to visit in Fort Davis that offer unique experiences. Here’s a list of top attractions to visit in Fort Davis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Davis National Historic Site: Explore the well-preserved frontier military post that was active during the Indian Wars of the late 1800s.

  • Jeff Davis County Historical Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Jeff Davis County through its extensive collection of exhibits, artifacts, and archives.

  • Overland Trail Museum: Trace the historic route of the Overland Trail stagecoach route by visiting this museum that showcases artifacts, exhibits, and a stagecoach.

Natural Attractions of Fort Davis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Davis Mountains State Park: Trek through the mountains, camp, stargaze, and enjoy outdoor activities such as biking, hiking, and fishing.

  • McDonald Observatory: Observe the stars and planets through high-powered telescopes at one of the world’s premier astronomical research centers.

  • Chihuahuan Desert Nature Center and Botanical Garden: Explore the beauty and diversity of the Chihuahuan Desert region by visiting this center that features botanical gardens, nature trails, and wildlife habitats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Davis

The mountains and trails of Fort Davis are perfect for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore in Fort Davis:

  • Skyline Drive Trail: This 4.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the Davis Mountains and the town of Fort Davis.

  • Indian Lodge Trail: This 10-mile trail passes through the Indian Lodge State Park and offers breathtaking scenery, waterfalls, and historic landmarks.

  • Davis Mountains Preserve: This preserve offers more than 38 miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through canyons, mountains, and forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Fort Davis: Activities and Tours

  • Stargazing at McDonald Observatory: Witness the clear, starry skies of Fort Davis by joining a stargazing tour at the McDonald Observatory.

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the mountains of Fort Davis on guided horseback rides through the Davis Mountains State Park.

  • Bird Watching: Discover the abundant birdlife in Fort Davis, including the Montezuma Quail, Gray Flycatcher, and Black-capped Vireo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Davis

Day 1 - Arrive and explore Day 2 - Visit the Fort Davis National Historic Site and Jeff Davis County Historical Museum Day 3 - Hike and camp at the Davis Mountains State Park Day 4 - Tour the McDonald Observatory and the Chihuahuan Desert Nature Center and Botanical Garden Day 5 - Bike at the Davis Mountains Preserve Day 6 - Horseback ride at the Indian Lodge State Park Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Davis

Fort Davis has a diverse and unique food scene with plenty of options for foodies. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and beverages in Fort Davis:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Big Bend Burrito: A signature dish of Fort Davis, this burrito is stuffed with beans, cheese, and your choice of meat, reminiscent of southwestern cuisine.

  • Enchiladas: These tortillas filled with meat and cheese and slathered with a chili sauce will excite your taste buds and give you a taste of Tex-Mex culture.

  • Chili Relleno: Stuffed with cheese and chili, this classic dish offers a perfect combination of sweetness and spiciness.

Local Beverages

  • Texas Beer: Enjoy some of the best craft beer and local brews in Texas.

  • Margarita: This classic cocktail is a refreshing treat after a long day of exploring Fort Davis.

Where to Stay in Fort Davis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Hotel Limpia: Stay in the heart of historic Fort Davis in this charming hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

  • The Indian Lodge: Experience an authentic southwestern-style stay at the Indian Lodge that offers cozy rooms, a pool, hiking trails, and horseback riding facilities.

What to Buy in Fort Davis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Shop unique and beautiful handcrafted ceramic pottery that reflects the local art and culture of Fort Davis.

  • Local Wines: Enjoy the fruity wines of the Davis Mountains region that are locally produced and bottled.

  • Gemstones and Rocks: Browse the collection of precious and semiprecious stones, crystals, and rocks that are mined locally.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Davis

Fort Davis is steeped in western history and culture. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the customs reflect the frontier heritage and independent spirit of Texas.

Best Time to Visit Fort Davis

The best time to visit Fort Davis is from October to April when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are accessible. Avoid visiting during the summer months as the heat can be extreme.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Davis

Do:

  • Be respectful of local culture and customs.

  • Dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources.

  • Stray from marked trails.

  • Disrespect the natural or cultural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Davis

Fort Davis is a safe and friendly town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions against snakes and other wildlife they might encounter during outdoor activities.

Health in Fort Davis

Fort Davis has a small clinic and one pharmacy. Visitors should bring their medications and be prepared for minor health issues.

Getting to Fort Davis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Davis

The closest major airport is El Paso International Airport (ELP), which is 200 miles away from Fort Davis. There are also smaller airports in Alpine, Texas, and Ruidoso, New Mexico, which are nearby.

Bus Stations Near Fort Davis

Due to its remote location, there are no bus services to Fort Davis.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most practical and flexible option for getting to Fort Davis and exploring the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Fort Davis

There are no public transportation services in Fort Davis. Visitors can rent a car or bicycle to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Fort Davis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Big Bend National Park: Explore one of the largest national parks in the United States that offers breathtaking landscapes, diverse flora and fauna, and outdoor adventures.

  • Marfa: Visit the hip and trendy art town of Marfa, which is known for its contemporary art scene, trendy eateries, and unique boutiques.

Conclusion

Fort Davis is a hidden gem in Texas that offers a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. With plenty of things to see, do, and experience, Fort Davis is a perfect vacation destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure.
