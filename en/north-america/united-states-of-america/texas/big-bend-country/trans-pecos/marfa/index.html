Introduction to Marfa

Located in Trans-Pecos, Big Bend Country, Marfa is a charming town in the west of Texas, United States of America. This small town is famous for its unique art installations and beautiful natural scenery, which attracts both artists and nature enthusiasts. With a population of just over 1,700, Marfa is a great place to experience small-town life and escape the hustle and bustle of the big city.

Whether you’re interested in art, culture, history, natural scenery, or outdoor adventures, Marfa has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll dive deep into everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Marfa.

List of Places to Visit in Marfa

Here’s a list of all the must-visit places in Marfa, organized by type of attraction.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marfa is a town rich in history and culture. Here are some of the museums and historical places you must visit:

  • Chinati Foundation: This contemporary art museum features the works of world-renowned artists, including Donald Judd, who resided in Marfa before his death.
  • Marfa and Presidio County Museum: This museum features artifacts from Marfa’s history, including Native American artifacts, the early days of ranching, and the town’s military past.
  • Marfa Lights Viewing Area: It’s said that strange lights can be seen in the sky above Marfa. This viewing area provides the perfect vantage point to witness the phenomenon.

Natural Attractions of Marfa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Marfa is situated in a stunning landscape that is rugged and remote. Here are some of the best natural attractions and outdoor activities to try:

  • Davis Mountains State Park: This state park is known for its scenic views, hiking trails, and camping spots.
  • McDonald Observatory: Located in nearby Fort Davis, this observatory is one of the most important astronomical research facilities in the world and offers tours and stargazing events.
  • Big Bend National Park: Though it’s not in Marfa, this national park is a must-visit for its stunning scenery, including rugged canyons, crystal-clear streams, and diverse wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marfa

Marfa is surrounded by pristine wilderness, which makes it an excellent place to hike and bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Marfa Mystery Lights Hike: This easy hike offers a closer look at the Marfa Lights phenomenon.
  • Balmorhea State Park: This state park features a spring-fed swimming pool and several hiking trails.
  • Chinati Mountains Trail: This trail is known for its scenic views and diverse plant and animal life.

Best Things to Do in Marfa: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums, natural attractions, and hiking/biking routes, Marfa has plenty of other activities to offer, including tours, galleries, and events. Here are some of the best things to do in Marfa:

  • Judd Foundation Tour: Take a tour of the homes and studios of the late artist, Donald Judd.
  • Marfa Gallery Night: This event happens on the second Saturday of every month and features art exhibitions, food trucks, and live music.
  • Marfa Film Festival: This annual festival celebrates independent films from around the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Marfa

Marfa has a unique food scene that blends Tex-Mex and West Texas comfort food. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gorditas: These fried, stuffed tortillas are a local specialty and often served at festivals.
  • Marfa Burger: This burger features beef from the local ranches and is served with homemade fries.
  • Marfa Maid Cheese: This artisanal cheese is made from the milk of local goats and is sold at several shops and restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Big Bend Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers and hosts live music events.
  • Cobra Rock: This local shoemaker also operates a coffee bar and offers local coffee and pastries.
  • Planet Marfa: This outdoor bar offers ice-cold beer, cocktails, and local wines in a relaxed atmosphere.

Where to Stay in Marfa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marfa has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels. Here are some options to consider:

  • El Cosmico: This stylish campground offers unique lodging options, including restored trailers, tepees, and yurts.
  • Hotel Saint George: This luxury hotel features elegant rooms, a rooftop bar, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Thunderbird Hotel: This retro-chic hotel features a heated pool, a courtyard, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Marfa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Marfa has many unique shops and boutiques that offer artisanal products and souvenirs. Here are some items to consider buying:

  • Marfa Brand Soap: This handmade soap is made with locally sourced ingredients and comes in various scents and sizes.
  • Wrong Marfa: This quirky gift shop offers books, stickers, and other souvenirs that celebrate the town’s eccentricity.
  • Mano Mercantile: This shop features clothing, jewelry, and accessories made by local designers and artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Marfa

Marfa is known for its unique art scene, laid-back atmosphere, and friendly locals. The town is a mix of artists, cowboys, and outdoor enthusiasts, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and experience the town’s eclectic energy.

Best Time to Visit Marfa

The best time to visit Marfa depends on your preferences. If you want to experience the town’s famous art scene, visit during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and several art exhibitions and festivals are happening. If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, consider visiting during the summer when the weather is hot, or during the winter when temperatures are cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marfa

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Marfa:

Do:

  • Do embrace the town’s artistic spirit and visit the art galleries and museums.
  • Do take advantage of the natural scenery and go hiking or biking in the surrounding wilderness.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, including the Marfa Burger and Marfa Maid Cheese.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t trespass on private property to take pictures of the iconic Prada Marfa installation.
  • Don’t expect a bustling nightlife scene or fancy restaurants - Marfa is all about simplicity and authenticity.

Safety and Health

Marfa is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take precautions when hiking or biking in the wilderness and always carry plenty of water and sunscreen.

Safety in Marfa

  • Simple precautions should apply, such as locking the doors at night and keeping valuables out of sight in vehicles.
  • Never leave a child unattended inside a vehicle, especially on a hot day.

Health in Marfa

  • Drink plenty of water when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Use sunscreen when spending time outside to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Marfa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marfa is a remote town, but there are several ways to get there, including by plane, bus, or car.

Airports Near Marfa

  • El Paso International Airport (ELP)
  • Midland International Air and Space Port (MAF)

Bus Stations Near Marfa

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • El Paso-Los Angeles Limousine Express

Other Options

  • Renting a car is recommended as it offers more flexibility and the opportunity to explore the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Marfa

The best way to get around Marfa is by car. However, the town is compact enough to navigate by foot or bicycle. If you’re interested in exploring the surrounding wilderness, consider renting a car or booking a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Marfa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marfa is a great base to explore the surrounding area, which is filled with natural wonders and historic sites. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Big Bend National Park: This stunning national park is located just 2.5 hours south of Marfa and offers some of the most breathtaking scenery in Texas.
  • Chisos Basin: This scenic spot is located in the middle of Big Bend National Park and offers hiking trails, camping spots, and beautiful views.
  • McDonald Observatory: Located just 45 minutes away in Fort Davis, this world-renowned observatory is a must-visit for astronomy enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Marfa is a unique and special place that should be on everyone’s travel bucket list. With its rich history, diverse art scene, and beautiful natural scenery, Marfa offers something for everyone. Plan your trip today and get ready to fall in love with this charming Texas town.
