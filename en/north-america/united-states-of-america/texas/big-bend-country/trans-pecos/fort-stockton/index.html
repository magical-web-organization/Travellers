Fort Stockton is a former military post turned western town located in the heart of Big Bend Country in west Texas. This historic town is known for its unique blend of Native American, Hispanic, and Anglo cultures, as well as its stunning natural landscapes and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply looking for a charming Texan getaway, Fort Stockton has something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll explore all the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Fort Stockton.

Introduction to Fort Stockton

Fort Stockton was founded in 1859 as a military post to protect westward expansion and trade routes in Texas. It was named after Robert Field Stockton, a prominent naval officer during the Mexican-American War. Today, the town has a rich history, with the well-preserved Fort Stockton Historic Site and Museum as a prime example of the region’s past.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Stockton

There are numerous places to visit in Fort Stockton, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fort Stockton has a rich history that is celebrated in its museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Fort Stockton Visitors Center: This center is the starting point for any visitor, where they can get all the information about the town and its attractions. The center also hosts special events and showcases local artisans’ work.
  • Fort Stockton Historic Site and Museum: Explore the preserved U.S. Army post from the frontier era. The museum houses artifacts and displays from the post and surrounding area.
  • Annie Riggs Memorial Museum: This museum is named after one of Fort Stockton’s early settlers, is a house museum with 14 rooms of exhibits on local history.

Natural Attractions of Fort Stockton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fort Stockton is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions of Fort Stockton:

  • Historic Comanche Springs Pool: This park offers visitors to Fort Stockton a chance to admire the hot springs in the middle of the desert and even cool off in the large public swimming pool fed by its crystal-clear waters.
  • Big Bend National Park: Located just a few hours away from Fort Stockton, Big Bend National Park is a breathtaking wilderness area with majestic peaks, scenic trails, and picturesque vistas that offer visitors the chance to experience a true #VanLife paradise. A must-visit during any traveler’s time in Fort Stockton.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Stockton

With its rugged terrain and stunning landscapes, Fort Stockton offers numerous hiking and biking routes for a perfect escape into nature. These are some of our recommended routes:

  • Historic Old Pecos Trail: This trail is perfect for hikers and bikers who want to explore the area’s unique natural beauty, abandoned settlements, and rock formations while traveling an ancient Native American trade route.
  • Elephant Mountain Wildlife Management Area: This park offers hiking and wildlife viewing opportunities in the Chihuahuan Desert. The trail is known for its rugged terrain and amazing views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Fort Stockton: Activities and Tours

Fort Stockton offers visitors a variety of activities to suit any travel style. Some of our recommendations include:

  • Fort Stockton Rodeo: The annual Fort Stockton Rodeo celebrates the town’s Western heritage with rodeo events, carnival rides, and live music.
  • Pecos County Golf Course: Golf enthusiasts will enjoy the 9-hole Pecos County Golf Course with its beautiful scenery and challenging greens.
  • Pecos County Dragway: Racing aficionados can experience high-speed thrills at the Pecos County Dragway.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Stockton

Fort Stockton has an exciting food and drink scene, with a variety of local specialties and flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pecan Pie: This sweet treat is a staple of Texas cuisine. You can enjoy a slice at any local bakery or restaurant in Fort Stockton.
  • Chili: Fort Stockton is home to the annual World Championship Chili Cook-off. Hence, it’s no wonder that chili is a must-try dish in this town.

Local Beverages

  • Mesquite Bean Pod Tea: This caffeine-free tea is made from mesquite bean pods and is a locally produced specialty drink.
  • Big Bend Brewery: It is a locally produced beer made from the Chihuahuan Desert’s mineral-rich water.

Where to Stay in Fort Stockton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fort Stockton offers many options for accommodations, from luxury hotels to basic guesthouses.

  • Best Western Plus Fort Stockton Hotel: This hotel offers guests comfortable rooms and excellent amenities like a swimming pool, free breakfast, and Wi-Fi.
  • Crystal Motel: This motel offers clean and affordable rooms with all the basic amenities you need for a comfortable stay.
  • Annie Riggs Bed & Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in a beautifully restored historic home and offers guests a unique experience.

What to Buy in Fort Stockton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Before leaving Fort Stockton, be sure to grab some souvenirs and local products to take home.

  • Pecans: Fort Stockton is known for its pecan orchards. Therefore, picking up some fresh pecans or a pecan pie is a great way to relive your trip.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: You can purchase beautiful handmade pottery pieces made by local artisans in any of the town’s art galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Stockton

Fort Stockton is a culturally diverse town that celebrates its Western history, religions, and traditions in unique ways. Guests can experience it through their local music forms, dance, and food culture.

Best Time to Visit Fort Stockton

The best time to visit Fort Stockton is in the fall and winter seasons when the temperatures are mild and ideal for outdoor activities. However, For many visitors, the sweltering heat underlines its tantalizing, historic charm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Stockton

Fort Stockton is generally a safe town, but visitors are advised to take precautions while traveling through the region, including avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Fort Stockton

Fort Stockton is considered safe in terms of health, though visitors are advised to bring plenty of water bottles to stay hydrated while exploring its natural landscapes.

Getting to Fort Stockton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fort Stockton is located near several major highways and is easily accessible by car or bus.

Airports Near Fort Stockton

  • Midland International Air and Space Port is the nearest airport, located about 1 hour and 45 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Fort Stockton

  • Greyhound buses stop at the Fort Stockton Bus Station, located in the heart of town.

How to Get Around in Fort Stockton

The easiest way to get around Fort Stockton is by car, though it’s also possible to explore the town on foot or by bicycle.
