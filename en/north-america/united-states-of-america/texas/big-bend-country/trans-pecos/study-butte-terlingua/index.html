Introduction to Study Butte-Terlingua

Located in Trans-Pecos, Texas, Study Butte and Terlingua are two communities that offer visitors a chance to explore the breathtaking natural scenery of Big Bend Country. With a deep history and a thriving arts and culture scene, Study Butte-Terlingua is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking adventure and inspiration.

List of Places to Visit in Study Butte-Terlingua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chisos Mining Co. Motel and Vacation Rentals: This historic motel was built in the 1940s during the mining boom in Terlingua. The motel has been renovated and converted into a vacation rental property that offers unique accommodations and an opportunity to learn about the history of the area.
  • Terlingua Ghost Town: This ghost town is the site of a former mercury mining operation and offers visitors a glimpse into the history of the area. With unique shops, restaurants, and galleries, Terlingua Ghost Town is a great place to spend an afternoon exploring the local culture.
  • Study Butte Cemetery: This historic cemetery is a tribute to the many pioneers who settled in the area. With graves dating back to the late 1800s, the cemetery is a reminder of the rugged and adventurous spirit of the early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Study Butte-Terlingua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Bend National Park: With over 800,000 acres of breathtaking natural beauty, Big Bend National Park offers endless opportunities for hiking, camping, and exploration. From the rugged canyons to the flowing Rio Grande, the park is a wonderland of nature’s splendor.
  • Santa Elena Canyon: A hike through Santa Elena Canyon offers visitors stunning views of the majestic Rio Grande and the towering cliffs that line the river. With the canyon walls reaching 1500 feet in some places, this is a truly awe-inspiring destination.
  • Big Bend Ranch State Park: For those seeking a more remote and rugged hiking experience, Big Bend Ranch State Park offers over 300,000 acres of terrain to explore. With stunning vistas and challenging trails, the park is a must-visit for serious hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Study Butte-Terlingua

  • Lost Mine Trail: This moderate trail in Big Bend National Park offers visitors stunning views of the Chisos Mountains and the surrounding landscape. The trail is approximately 4.8 miles round trip and takes around 3-4 hours to complete.
  • Santa Elena Canyon Trail: This trail is a moderate hike that offers visitors the chance to explore the stunning Santa Elena Canyon. The trail is approximately 1.7 miles round trip and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.
  • Chimneys Trail: This challenging hike in Big Bend National Park is approximately a 4.8-mile round trip and takes around 3-4 hours to complete. With stunning views of the desert landscape and the surrounding mountains, this trail is a favorite for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Study Butte-Terlingua: Activities and Tours

  • River Tours: Kayaking and rafting tours down the Rio Grande offer visitors a unique perspective on the stunning scenery of Big Bend Country. With experienced guides leading the way, these tours are a great way to explore the area’s natural beauty.
  • Horseback Riding: A horseback ride through the rugged terrain of Big Bend Ranch State Park is a unique and unforgettable experience. With experienced guides leading the way, these tours offer visitors a chance to see the park in a new and exciting way.
  • Stargazing: With some of the darkest skies in the United States, Study Butte-Terlingua is a popular destination for stargazing enthusiasts. Visitors can take a guided tour of the night sky or simply lay back and enjoy the view on their own.
