Introduction to Alpine

Located in Trans-Pecos, in Big Bend Country, Texas, Alpine is a charming town filled with stunning natural landscapes and culturally rich attractions. If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure or a city escape, Alpine serves as the perfect destination. With its diverse array of things to do, beautiful parks to explore, and mouth-watering food to savor; Alpine has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Alpine

Here is a list of must-visit places in Alpine:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Big Bend
  • Alpine Historical Society Museum
  • Sul Ross State University

Natural Attractions of Alpine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Bend National Park
  • Davis Mountains State Park
  • Chihuahuan Desert Nature Center and Botanical Gardens

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alpine

Alpine is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Alpine:

  • Big Bend National Park Trails: Featuring over 150 miles of scenic hiking trails.
  • Davis Mountains Preserve: Offering a vast network of trails for all levels of experience.
  • Elephant Mountain Wildlife Management Area: Rent a mountain bike and explore this sprawling wilderness area.

Best Things to Do in Alpine: Activities and Tours

Alpine offers a diverse array of things to do for all types of travelers, whether you’re looking for a laid-back day or an adrenaline-pumping adventure. Here’s a list of the best things to do in Alpine:

  • Explore the Marfa Lights
  • Horseback Riding at Lajitas Stables
  • Stargazing at McDonald Observatory
  • Visit the Alpine Cowboys Baseball Club

Week-Long Itinerary for Alpine

Here’s a one-week itinerary that will allow you to experience the best of Alpine:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Museum of the Big Bend and explore Sul Ross State University
Day 2 Explore the natural beauty of Davis Mountains State Park
Day 3 Visit the Alpine Historical Society Museum and the Chihuahuan Desert Nature Center

Day 7 Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene of Alpine

Food and Drink Scene of Alpine

Alpine doesn’t disappoint food and drink lovers, with its variety of local cuisines and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Traditional Tex-Mex Tacos
  • Cowboy-style Ribeye
  • Huevos Rancheros, a classic breakfast dish

Local Beverages

  • Big Bend Brewing Company’s Beer
  • Cactus Margarita from the Reata Restaurant
  • Lonestar State Wine

Where to Stay in Alpine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alpine offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the recommended options:

  • Holland Hotel: A historical hotel that offers contemporary amenities and is located in the city center.
  • Hampton Inn Alpine: A stylish and modern hotel located near Sul Ross State University
  • The Maverick Inn: a cozy and affordable option located near Alpine’s downtown area

What to Buy in Alpine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alpine is known for its unique local products and handmade souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Alpine:

  • Handmade pottery from Alpine Pottery
  • Western-style clothing and souvenirs from Big Bend Saddlery
  • Natural skincare products from Big Bend Soap Factory

Local Culture and Customs of Alpine

Alpine is proud of its Texan culture and traditions. The cowboy lifestyle is celebrated throughout the town with many rodeos and western-themed events. It’s essential to be respectful of the local traditions and embrace the diversity of the town.

Best Time to Visit Alpine

The best time to visit Alpine is during the spring (March to May) when the weather is mild, and the wildflowers are in bloom. The summertime(June-August) is hot, but it’s an ideal time for hikers and adventurers. The fall (September to November) offers pleasant temperatures and stunning foliage, while winter (December to February) brings cooler temperatures, which are perfect for winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alpine

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Embrace the diverse community and listen and learn about their culture
  • Pack for outdoor activities and sun protection

Don’t:

  • Litter; leave only footprints and take only memories.
  • Break laws or regulations
  • Distract wildlife or intentionally harm them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alpine

Alpine is generally a safe town, but like any other city, it’s essential to be vigilant and aware of your surroundings. Don’t leave your belongings unattended, and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Alpine

Alpine’s high altitude may affect visitors who are not acclimated to it. It’s essential to drink plenty of water and wear appropriate clothing during outdoor activities.

Getting to Alpine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alpine

  • Midland International Air and Space Port (MAF) is the nearest major airport, located about 150 miles east of Alpine.

Bus Stations Near Alpine

  • Greyhound buses regularly run from the El Paso, TX station to Alpine.

Other Options

  • A rental car from major companies like Enterprise or Hertz is a convenient way to explore Alpine.

How to Get Around in Alpine

The best way to explore the town is by car. However, if you don’t have a car, you can rent a bike or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Alpine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alpine serves as a gateway to other stunning attractions in the area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Marfa, a small and artsy town, is located around 25 miles from Alpine.
  • Terlingua, a ghost town near Big Bend National Park, is about 50 miles from Alpine.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, Alpine is a must-visit destination for any traveler. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide and enjoy every moment of your Alpine adventure.
