A Comprehensive Guide to Lajitas: Discover the Best of Texas

Nestled in the heart of Trans-Pecos, in Big Bend Country, Texas, Lajitas is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With its vast and rugged landscape, rich culture, and exciting adventures, Lajitas is a destination that should not be missed. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or thrill-seeker, Lajitas has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Lajitas

Lajitas is a beautiful town in Brewster County, covering an area of about six square miles. It is located along the border of Texas and Mexico and offers stunning views of the Rio Grande river. Lajitas is home to a diverse range of wildlife and plant species, making it an oasis for nature lovers. The town is also known for its rich history and culture, with several historical landmarks and museums worth visiting. Visitors to Lajitas can enjoy outdoor adventures and scenic views, try local cuisine, and learn about the local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Lajitas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lajitas Trading Post: Established in 1899, the Lajitas Trading Post is a historic landmark that sells souvenirs, clothing, and other local products. The post also serves as a post office, gas station, and grocery store.
  • Terlingua Ghost Town: Located about 10 miles from Lajitas, Terlingua Ghost Town is a historic mining town that dates back to the early 20th century. Visitors can explore the old buildings, mines, and cemeteries and learn about the history of the town.
  • Fort Leaton State Historic Site: Built in 1848, Fort Leaton State Historic Site is a restored adobe fort that served as a trading post and military fort. Visitors can take a guided tour of the fort and learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Lajitas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Bend National Park: Located about 100 miles from Lajitas, Big Bend National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The park offers breathtaking views of the Chisos Mountains, Rio Grande River, and other natural wonders.
  • Big Bend Ranch State Park: Covering over 300,000 acres, Big Bend Ranch State Park offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and river rafting.
  • Santa Elena Canyon: Located in Big Bend National Park, Santa Elena Canyon is a 1,500-foot-deep canyon that offers stunning views of the Rio Grande river and surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lajitas

  • Closed Canyon Trail: This easy 1.4-mile hike leads visitors through a narrow canyon and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Contrabando Trail: This moderate 4.3-mile trail takes hikers and bikers through the rugged landscape of Big Bend Ranch State Park.
  • Lajitas Golf Resort Trail: This easy 4.5-mile trail takes visitors through the Lajitas Golf Resort and offers scenic views of the Rio Grande and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Lajitas: Activities and Tours

  • Rio Grande River Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour along the Rio Grande river and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can take a horseback riding tour through the rugged landscape of Lajitas and experience the area’s rich cowboy heritage.
  • Stargazing: With clear skies and low light pollution, Lajitas is the perfect place for stargazing. Visitors can take a guided tour of the night sky and learn about the constellations and planets.

Food and Drink Scene of Lajitas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chili Con Carne: A traditional Texan dish made with beef and chili peppers.
  • Barbecue Brisket: Slow-cooked beef brisket, often served with barbecue sauce.
  • Tex-Mex Tacos: Tacos with a Texan twist, often filled with ingredients like barbacoa, brisket, or fajita meat.

Local Beverages

  • Texas Beer: Texas is known for its craft beer scene, and Lajitas has several local breweries worth trying.
  • Margaretville: A popular cocktail made with tequila, orange liqueur, and lime juice.
  • Pecos Punch: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made with iced tea, lemonade, and cranberry juice.

Where to Stay in Lajitas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lajitas Golf Resort: A luxury resort with golfing, horseback riding, and spa services.
  • El Dorado Hotel: A historic hotel that offers guests a taste of cowboy life in the Wild West.
  • La Posada Milagro: A charming guesthouse with traditional adobe architecture and beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Lajitas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Lajitas has several local artisans who sell handmade crafts like pottery, woven baskets, and jewelry.
  • Local Products: Visitors can buy locally-sourced honey, pecans, and other delicious snacks to bring home.
  • Cowboy Hats and Boots: Lajitas is known for its cowboy heritage, and visitors can pick up a classic cowboy hat or pair of boots as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lajitas

The town of Lajitas has a rich cowboy heritage, and visitors are likely to encounter locals wearing Western attire, such as cowboy hats and boots. Texans are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors to Lajitas can expect to be treated with warmth and hospitality. It is important to respect the local culture and customs, such as not wearing hats indoors and using “yes, sir” and “no, ma’am” when speaking to elders.

Best Time to Visit Lajitas

The best time to visit Lajitas is from October to April when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers in Lajitas can be scorching, with temperatures soaring above 100 degrees Fahrenheit. Winter months can be chilly, with temperatures dropping below freezing at night. Visitors should plan their trip according to their preferred activities and weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lajitas

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and customs.
  • Respect the natural environment by practicing Leave No Trace principles.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the area’s rich history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Wander off-trail or attempt dangerous activities without proper guidance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lajitas

Lajitas is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take precautions while exploring the surrounding wilderness. Visitors should always carry plenty of water and wear appropriate clothing and footwear. It is also recommended to travel in pairs or groups, especially when hiking or biking in remote areas. Visitors should also be aware of wildlife, such as snakes and scorpions, and take appropriate safety precautions.

Health in Lajitas

Visitors to Lajitas should take precautions to prevent heat exhaustion and dehydration, especially during the summer months. It is essential to drink plenty of water and avoid hiking or engaging in other strenuous activities during the hottest parts of the day. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of sunburn and bring sunscreen and protective clothing.

Getting to Lajitas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lajitas is located about 325 miles west of San Antonio and 312 miles southeast of El Paso. Visitors can reach Lajitas by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Lajitas

  • El Paso International Airport: Located about 312 miles from Lajitas, El Paso International Airport is the closest major airport serving the area.
  • Midland International Air and Space Port: Located about 250 miles from Lajitas, Midland International Airport is another option for visitors traveling to the area.

Bus Stations Near Lajitas

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Alpine, Texas, about 60 miles from Lajitas, the Greyhound Bus Station offers several daily connections to major cities in Texas and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer not to drive can hire a shuttle service from El Paso or Midland to Lajitas. Several shuttle services also operate within the Lajitas area, offering transportation to and from various attractions and activities.

How to Get Around Lajitas

The best way to explore Lajitas is by car, as many of the area’s attractions are located outside of town. Visitors can rent a car from the El Paso or Midland airports or from rental agencies in the Lajitas area. Taxis and shuttle services are also available for visitors who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Lajitas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marfa: Located about 100 miles from Lajitas, Marfa is a small town known for its art galleries, museums, and unique desert landscape.
  • Presidio: Located about 60 miles from Lajitas, Presidio is a small border town with a rich history and stunning views of the Rio Grande.
  • Texas Wine Country: Located about 200 miles from Lajitas, Texas Wine Country is a must-visit destination for wine lovers, with several wineries offering tours and tastings.

Conclusion

Lajitas is a destination that should not be missed by anyone visiting the state of Texas. With its natural beauty, rich culture and history, and exciting outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Lajitas.
