A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Marathon, Texas

Welcome to Marathon, a charming town tucked away in the Trans-Pecos region of Texas, USA. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, Marathon has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and how to get around in Marathon.

Introduction to Marathon

Marathon is located in the Big Bend Country region of Texas. It’s surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, including the Big Bend National Park, the Davis Mountains, and the Chinati Mountains. The town has a rich history dating back to the late 1800s when it was a railroad stop. Today, Marathon is a popular tourist destination known for its picturesque scenery, unique local culture, and abundant outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Marathon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gage Hotel: This iconic hotel in the heart of Marathon is a popular tourist destination. It has a rich history dating back to 1927 and has hosted many famous guests over the years. The hotel also has a museum with exhibits about the history of Marathon and the surrounding region.
  • Marathon Motel and RV Park: This historic motel dates back to the 1940s and has hosted many famous guests over the years, including President Lyndon B. Johnson. The motel has been beautifully restored and maintains its vintage charm.
  • Post Park: This public park has a rich history dating back to the early 1900s when it was a private park owned by the Gage family. Today, the park has picnic tables, playgrounds, and a walking trail. It also features a historic gazebo and a monument to the Marathon 2-Step, a popular dance move that originated in Marathon.

Natural Attractions of Marathon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Bend National Park: This world-renowned national park is located just 40 minutes from Marathon. It’s home to stunning canyons, rugged mountains, and the Rio Grande River. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, kayaking, and stargazing in this truly unique wilderness area.
  • Davis Mountains State Park: This state park is located about an hour’s drive from Marathon and features some of the most breathtaking scenery in the region. Visitors can hike, bike, camp, and watch wildlife in this stunning natural area.
  • Chinati Mountains State Natural Area: This natural area is located just outside Marathon and is home to unique rock formations and stunning vistas. Visitors can hike, mountain bike, and go horseback riding in this rugged landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marathon

  • Marathon Loop: This scenic route takes hikers and bikers through the heart of Marathon and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and desert landscapes.
  • Burro Mesa Pour-Off Trail: This moderate trail in Big Bend National Park offers stunning views of a natural pour-off formation as well as sweeping views of the Chisos Mountains.
  • Skyline Drive Trail: This advanced trail in Davis Mountains State Park offers a challenging hike with rewarding panoramic views of the mountains and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Marathon: Activities and Tours

  • Stargazing: Marathon is known for its clear skies and fantastic stargazing opportunities. Visitors can take part in stargazing tours and workshops to learn more about the constellations and the night sky.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can take guided horseback rides through the stunning landscapes of the Big Bend region.
  • Wine Tasting: Marathon is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours of their vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Marathon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mesquite-smoked brisket: This Texas classic is given an extra twist with mesquite wood smoke.
  • Chili Rellenos: A delicious dish made with roasted poblano peppers stuffed with cheese or meat and coated in egg batter.
  • Green Chile Cheeseburger: This burger features a juicy patty topped with melted cheese and spicy green chilies.

Local Beverages

  • Big Bend Brewing Company: This local brewery produces a range of craft beers inspired by the surrounding landscapes.
  • Saddle Blanket Wine: This local winery produces award-winning wines made from grapes grown in the surrounding region.

Where to Stay in Marathon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gage Hotel: This iconic hotel in the heart of Marathon offers luxurious accommodations and a range of amenities, including a restaurant and spa.
  • Marathon Motel and RV Park: This historic motel offers comfortable, affordable accommodations in the heart of Marathon.
  • Eve’s Garden Organic Bed and Breakfast: This eco-friendly bed and breakfast offers luxurious accommodations surrounded by stunning gardens.

What to Buy in Marathon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven blankets and rugs: These beautiful textiles are made by local artisans and are a great way to bring home a piece of Marathon’s unique culture.
  • Mesquite wood products: Mesquite wood is a local specialty and is often used to create beautiful cutting boards, bowls, and other household items.
  • Agave skincare products: These natural skincare products are made from agave plants grown in the surrounding desert landscapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Marathon

Marathon has a unique culture shaped by its location in the desert landscapes of West Texas. Visitors can experience this culture through local festivals and events, such as the annual Marathon Easter Egg Hunt and the Christmas Lights Festival. Visitors should also be respectful of local customs, such as its laid-back and friendly atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Marathon

The best time to visit Marathon is in the fall and winter when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Spring and summer can be extremely hot, with temperatures often exceeding 100 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marathon

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat when exploring the outdoors.
  • Bring plenty of water and snacks when hiking or biking.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on trails or in public areas.
  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • Drive off-road or outside designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marathon

Overall, Marathon is a safe destination. Visitors should take typical safety precautions when exploring the outdoors, such as carrying a first aid kit and avoiding hiking alone after dark.

Health in Marathon

Visitors to Marathon should be aware of the risk of dehydration and heat stroke, especially during the hot summer months. Make sure to drink plenty of water and avoid outdoor activities during the hottest parts of the day.

Getting to Marathon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marathon

The closest airport to Marathon is Midland International Air and Space Port, located about 160 miles away. From there, visitors can rent a car or use a shuttle service to travel to Marathon.

Bus Stations Near Marathon

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in nearby Alpine, Texas, about 30 miles from Marathon. From there, visitors can rent a car or use a shuttle service to travel to Marathon.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Marathon using their own vehicle or rent a car from a nearby city.

How to Get Around in Marathon

The best way to get around Marathon is by car. Many of the local attractions are located outside of town and require a vehicle. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the local area.

Where to Go Next from Marathon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Big Bend National Park: This world-renowned park offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and stunning natural landscapes.
  • Fort Davis National Historic Site: This historic site features preserved military forts and offers insight into the region’s military history.
  • Alpine, Texas: This quaint town is located about 30 minutes from Marathon and features shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Marathon is a hidden gem in the heart of West Texas. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and unique culture, it’s no wonder that visitors flock here from around the world. Whether you’re here for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just to soak up the laid-back atmosphere, Marathon has something for everyone.
