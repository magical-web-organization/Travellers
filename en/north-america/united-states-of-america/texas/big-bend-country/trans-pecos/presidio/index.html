Introduction to Presidio

Presidio is a small city located in the Trans-Pecos region of Big Bend Country in Texas. It is situated along the Rio Grande River and borders with Ojinaga, Chihuahua (Mexico) as well as Brewster and Jeff Davis counties in Texas. This charming city is surrounded by beautiful mountains, natural parks, and breathtaking scenic views. Presidio is home to a rich culture and heritage that dates back several centuries, as displayed in its fascinating museums, historical sites, and local traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Presidio

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Leaton State Historic Site: This 19th-century fort established by Ben Leaton was once a trading post along the Chihuahua Trail. The site has preserved several buildings, including a massive adobe-walled fort, a trading post, and a store, showcasing the life of a 19th-century trader.

  • Museum of the Big Bend: This museum near Sul Ross State University exhibits the culture, wildlife, art, and history of the Big Bend Region. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, including prehistoric fossils, Native American pottery, and cowboy memorabilia.

  • Presidio County Courthouse: This charming courthouse is a historic building that was built in 1887, and it is part of the National Register of Historic Places. The courthouse is a significant landmark in Presidio, known for breathtaking architecture and remarkable interior design.

Natural Attractions of Presidio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Bend Ranch State Park: It is the largest state park in Texas, with over 300,000 acres of desert wilderness, canyons, mountains, and breathtakingly beautiful scenic views. The park features several hiking trails, mountain biking routes, and opportunities for horse riding, wildlife viewing, stargazing, and primitive camping to explore natural beauty.

  • Chinati Hot Springs: This natural formation is a must-visit destination, featuring bubbling hot springs surrounded by awe-inspiring natural beauty, relaxing, and rejuvenating in the middle of nowhere. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, bird watching, and stargazing while basking in the warm waters.

  • Ruidosa Canyon: This scenic canyon offers a hiking trail that leads to an 80-foot waterfall. The trail is approximately 2.6 miles and is best suited for experienced hikers. Exploring this hidden beauty is a worthwhile venture for nature lovers and hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Presidio

  • Lobo Trail: It is a 2.2-mile round trip trail in Big Bend Ranch State Park that offers a moderate level of difficulty to hikers. The trail takes visitors to its namesake Lobo Peak, rising 1,680 feet above sea level.

  • Closed Canyon Trail: This 1.4-mile trail is a stunning desert hike through a narrow canyon. The trail rises up to 450 feet in elevation during the hike and features a limestone walled canyon.

  • Cottonwood Trail: This 6-mile round trip trail in Big Bend Ranch State Park is deemed one of the best mountain biking trails in the USA. The trail leads to Oak Canyon, showcasing scenic views and a 1,000-foot elevation gain.

Best Things to Do in Presidio: Activities and Tours

  • River Rafting: Rio Grande River is a popular site for river rafting and kayaking. Visitors can enjoy scenic village views while enjoying a mild float or some class III rapids for the more adventurous river-goers.

  • ATV Tours: Traveling through the rugged terrain of Big Bend National Park via guided tour on an ATV is an action-packed way of taking in the natural beauty of the region.

  • Stargazing/Theater Shows: Catching a show at the Mc Donald Observatory is a must for space enthusiasts. The facility has an 82 inch, 45-ton telescope, as well as more than 150 interactive exhibits on astronomy, celestial navigation, and space satellites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Presidio

Day 1: Visit the Fort Leaton State Historic Site and enjoy a scenic drive through old Highway 170.

Day 2: Experience nature in its truest form as you hike through the Chihuahuan Desert and visit the Big Bend Ranch State Park.

Day 3: Visit the Museum of the Big Bend and take a walk through the Sul Ross State University.

Day 4: Embark on an ATV tour in the Big Bend region and catch a flight at the Big Bend Aviation.

Day 5: Experience stargazing and catch a theater show at McDonald Observatory.

Day 6: Explore the Ruidosa Canyon or enjoy the bath and steam at Chinati Hot Springs.

Day 7: Take part in river rafting or relax at the border town of Presidio.

Food and Drink Scene of Presidio

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chile Relleno: Roasted green peppers filled with cheese or meat and served with red or green sauce.

  • Enchiladas: Tortillas stuffed with meat, cheese, and smothered in chili sauce.

  • Quesadillas: Grilled tortilla stuffed with cheese, meat, and veggies.

  • Tacos: Soft or crispy tortilla filled with a combination of seasoned meat, cheese, lettuce, tomato, and onion.

Local Beverages

  • Margartias: A classic margarita with a twist of southern hospitality to enjoy in Presidio.

  • Tequila: A wide variety of tequilas, both aged and unaged, to cater to everyone’s liking.

  • Mexican beers: Cerveza is a must-try local beer. Presidio has several local breweries to explore.

Where to Stay in Presidio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • El Capitan Hotel: A historic hotel that was once a Harvey House, located in downtown Van Horn.

  • Lajitas Golf Resort and Spa: An upscale guesthouse resort with golf courses, spa treatments, and luxurious rooms.

  • Big Bend Resort and Adventures: Located minutes from Big Bend National Park, the resort has rooms, tents, and RVs for all types of visitors.

What to Buy in Presidio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mexican Pottery: Handmade ceramics and pottery from across the Rio Grande border.

  • Local Wines and Beers: Featuring a wide range of local alcohol options to enjoy in Presidio.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Handcrafted gemstones and jewelry add an authentic Presidio flavor to any wardrobe.

Local Culture and Customs of Presidio

The people of Presidio celebrate a mix of American and Mexican cultures with a distinctive Texan flavor. Visitors will enjoy the hospitality and friendliness of the locals, as well as their unique traditions and customs, such as rodeos, festivals, and fairs.

Best Time to Visit Presidio

Presidio has a hot desert climate throughout the year. The best time to visit Presidio is from October to May as the temperatures are not too severe. Winter offers moderate tempts during the day and cold nights, perfect for stargazing. Spring brings refreshing mornings and crisp air, and fall is an excellent time for outdoor adventure and sight-seeing tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Presidio

Do:

  • Plan ahead for outdoor activities and pack accordingly.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Drive safely and follow traffic rules.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of Presidio.

  • Attempt to cross the border without proper documentation.

  • Disturb the local wildlife or flora.

Safety and Health

Safety in Presidio

Presidio is generally safe for visitors. However, visitors must be mindful of their surroundings and take precautions when exploring remote areas and natural parks. Visitors should come prepared with plenty of water, essential medications, and first aid kits while exploring the rough terrain of Big Bend National Park. It is also essential to follow guidelines and advice from local authorities, especially during inclement weather.

Health in Presidio

The city has limited healthcare facilities and medical professionals, so visitors must prepare accordingly. Visitors should carry prescribed medications with them and consult with their healthcare professionals before traveling to the city. It is essential to maintain hydration, especially during the day time, and protect oneself from the sun in the hot climate.

Getting to Presidio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Presidio

Presidio International Airport is the closest airport to the city, with limited commercial flight services. Other major airports nearby are the Midland International Airport and El Paso International Airport, both three to four-hour drives to Presidio.

Bus Stations Near Presidio

Several bus services are available within West Texas connecting Presidio to other major cities. Greyhound runs the bus services in Presidio.

Other Options

Taxis, rental car services, and private car services are available for transportation in and around Presidio.

How to Get Around in Presidio

Presidio has limited public transportation, so it is best to rely on rental cars, taxis or personal vehicles during your stay in the city. Visitors can rent an ATV or mountain bike for an adventure through the remote trails of Big Bend National Park, although, for longer journeys, a car rental would be better suited.

Where to Go Next from Presidio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marfa: Marfa is a small city about 60 miles north of Presidio, famous for its minimalist art galleries, natural beauty, and foodie scenes.

  • Fort Davis: Fort Davis is a National Historic Site that served as a military post from 1854 until 1891. It features preserved buildings, heritage sites, and information on the Buffalo Soldiers.

  • Big Bend National Park: Big Bend National Park is under an hour away from Presidio, featuring vast expanses of rugged wilderness, mountains, canyons, and vistas right on the US-Mexico Border.

Conclusion

Presidio, Texas, is a frequently overlooked gem in the Trans-Pecos region. With stunning natural beauty, rich heritage, and friendly people, Presidio offers a travel experience that visitors will remember for a lifetime. Whether visitors are enjoying scenic drives, outdoor adventure, or immersing themselves in local customs and traditions, Presidio provides a unique getaway from the hustle and bustle of modern life.
