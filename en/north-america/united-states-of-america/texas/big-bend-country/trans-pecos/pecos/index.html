Introduction to Pecos

Pecos is a city situated in the Trans-Pecos region of Texas, within the United States of America. This lovely city is also a part of Big Bend Country and is known for its incredible natural beauty, historical significance and rich cultural heritage. The city draws visitors from all over the world throughout the year, offering an abundance of outdoor adventures including hiking, biking, and camping opportunities. In this Pecos tour guide, you will discover everything from the attractive tourist destinations to the unique food scene, suitable accommodations, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Pecos

One of the main reasons visitors flock to Pecos is the diversity of exciting attractions that await them. There’s always something for everyone from cultural attractions and historical places to natural beauty spots.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pecos is steeped in history, and it boasts a range of historically significant locations and fascinating museums.

  1. West of the Pecos Museum - here, visitors can explore the history of Reeves County and the West of the Pecos region through exhibits, including native artifacts and photos.
  2. Judge Roy Bean Visitor Center - this center honors the memory of the Old West saloon keeper, who was an important figure in Pecos history.
  3. Monahans Sandhills State Park - take a day trip to explore the white singing sands of Monahans, a wonderful place to enjoy outdoor activities such as sand tobogganing or hiking.

Natural Attractions of Pecos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pecos holds some of the most captivating natural resources. Visitors who enjoy being in nature and living for outdoor adventures would find Pecos, a paradise with numerous alluring sites.

  1. Pecos National Historic Park - travelers enjoy learning about the country’s early settlers, petroglyphs, and archeological discoveries here.
  2. Balmorhea State Park - this state park features a 1.3-acre natural spring pool that extends nine million gallons of crystal-clran water. Visitors can swim and enjoy the views or start on a diving adventure.
  3. Davis Mountains State Park - here, visitors have the opportunity to explore more than 2,700 acres of wildlife habitat, including different hiking trails, a bicycle trail, and lots of birdwatching zones.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pecos

Pecos offers a trail for every level of hiker. Here’s a list of some of the most popular hiking and biking trails in the city:

  1. Frank X. King’s Nature Trail - this easy hike is located at the Maxey Park and features an interpretive walk highlighting various plants native to the Trans-Pecos region.
  2. Tobe Judd Trail - a moderately-sized hike featuring a beautiful setting and ideal for hiking and trailing.
  3. Pecos River RV Park Trail - a beautiful trail overlooking the Pecos River and suitable for long walks, hiking, and cycling adventures.

Best Things to Do in Pecos: Activities and Tours

Pecos has plenty of things to do, and the options are endless for tourists of any kind. Here are some of the best things to do in Pecos:

  1. Enjoy a leisurely walk at the Historic Downtown District - you’ll enjoy a stroll along the galleries, antique stores, and unique boutiques with original gifts.
  2. Go stargazing - Pecos is blessed with incredibly clear skies with perfect constellations stars look like iridescent in the night sky, which explains the popularity of star watching tours in the area.
  3. Get the complete experience of the city by attending the West of the Pecos Rodeo. The event holds in June, with a rodeo parade and cementing of cowboys.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pecos

With so many options, you might need an itinerary to cover everything Pecos has to offer. Here is a fulfilling seven-day travel itinerary for Pecos:

Day 1: Arrive in Pecos and visit West of the Pecos Museum. Day 2: Hiking and breakfast at the Tobe Judd Trail and eat out at the Cowboys Cafe for lunch. Day 3: Participate in the West of the Pecos Rodeo and enjoy a leisurely outing at Historic Downtown District. Day 4: Visit the Judges Roy Bean Museum in the morning, followed by a picnic at the Davis Mountains State Park. Day 5: Spend the day visiting Balmorhea State Park and have lunch at the park restaurant. Day 6: Go monster trucking at the Sandhills State Park and eat out at the Wild Horse Desert Trading Post. Day 7: Enjoy extended breakfast at Wheeler’s Cafe and leave with a box of goodies as a souvenir.

Food and Drink Scene of Pecos

In Pecos, you will find exceptional restaurants offering mouth-watering dishes, whether you crave traditional American cuisine or local Texan favorites, you will find it all in Pecos. Here are things you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. The Pecos Classic Burger - this doubles stacked patty burger from the Wooly Burger is a local favorite and is served with fresh fries.
  2. Beef Fajitas - try out the Texas proud beef fajitas, served at almost every local restaurant.
  3. The Taco Salad - A perfect mix of crispy lettuce, juicy tomatoes, cheese, beans, and savory meat from the Cowboys Cafe.

Local Beverages

  1. Pecos River Red Ale - a crisp and refreshing ale named after the famous Pecos River, brewed at Big Bend Brewery.
  2. Caffeine Fix - Pecos, Texas, has a vibrant coffee scene with Wheela’s Cafe serving up an impressive variety of coffee blends.

Where to Stay in Pecos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Depending on your budget, preference, and style of travel, you can find an array of accommodation options in Pecos from budget-friendly motels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of our favorite picks:

  1. Oak Tree Inn Pecos - travelers will enjoy the comfortable and well-equipped guest rooms in this motel.
  2. Best Western Swiss Clock Inn - located between Carlsbad and El Paso and perfect for visitors going on a road trip in the region.
  3. La Quinta Inn & Suites Pecos- bringing modern rooms and wonderful hospitality to the area.

What to Buy in Pecos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pecos is known for its handmade leather goods, authentic Texan attire, colorful and unique pottery exhibits, and pottery crafts. Make sure to check out these places:

  1. Jan’s Jewelry and Gifts - visit this gift shop to find perfect souvenirs like Western-style belts, hats and horse-themed jewelry, and cowboy boots.
  2. West of the Pecos Museum Shop - Stop by to check out homemade crafts, including pottery and paintings from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Pecos

The city of Pecos prides itself on its diverse cultural heritage ranging from Apache tribes to early Hispanic settlers. Visitors are encouraged to respect and learn about the local customs to have a fulfilling experience.

Best Time to Visit Pecos

Visitors can visit Pecos at any time during the year, but the city’s high season is from late June to October when the climate is warmest. Visitors can also enjoy the West of the Pecos Rodeo in June during the peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pecos

Do:

  1. Always try local cuisine, it’s delicious.
  2. Be respectful of local cultures.
  3. Take plenty of water with you on hikes and outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  1. Always remember not to litter.
  2. Avoid using ATVs or any motorized vehicles on eco-sensitive areas.
  3. Don’t board pets along on your travels.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pecos

Pecos is generally safe for travelers, but it is always good to be cautious and secure belongings and valuables. Avoid deserted areas at night, and consider using paid parking spots instead of leaving your vehicle on the street.

Health in Pecos

It’s advisable to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months, and to use sunscreen and insect repellent while climbing the hiking trails or attending outdoor activities.

Getting to Pecos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Pecos by different means, including by bus and air.

Airports Near Pecos

The closest international airports to Pecos are the Midland International Airport and El Paso International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Pecos

Visitors have bus transportation to different stations, including the Greyhound station and First Class Tours.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and shared-ride arrangements, including Uber and Lyft, are available for transportation in Pecos.

How to Get Around in Pecos

Visitors can travel around Pecos via cabs, rental cars, or shuttlebus. Still, the best way to get around the city is by driving a personal vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Pecos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the best places to continue your journey after exploring Pecos:

  1. Big Bend National Park -a massive park with plenty hiking trails, fantastic campsites, and an opportunity to go stargazing.
  2. Carlsbad Caverns National Park - about one hour and thirty minutes from Pecos, travelers can explore the natural stunning geological formation in the caves.

Conclusion

Pecos, Texas, has something for everyone, from the outdoor adventure enthusiasts to the history buffs and cultural enthusiasts. With unique cuisine, accommodations, outdoor adventure attractions, and picturesque views, you can have an unforgettable experience in this lovely city. Enjoy!
