Introduction to Athens

Athens is a small town located in the Blackland Prairie region of Texas. This charming town has a rich history, beautiful scenery, and a friendly community that welcomes visitors with open arms. In this guide, we’ll take a look at the best things to do in Athens, including the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, and cultural experiences. We’ll also cover where to stay, what to buy, and the local customs and culture of Athens.

List of Places to Visit in Athens

If you’re planning a trip to Athens, make sure to check out the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Athens has a rich history and is full of culturally important attractions. Visit the following museums and historical places to learn about the town’s heritage:

  • Henderson County Historical Museum - This museum has exhibits that showcase the history and culture of Henderson County.
  • East Texas Arboretum and Botanical Society - An oasis of peace and nature, the East Texas Arboretum and Botanical Society has diverse flora and fauna, a butterfly garden, and even a live beehive.
  • Athens Scuba Park - An old quarry turned into a summer aquatic park and scuba diving destination, it has sunken statues, training platforms, and sunken boats for divers of all experience levels.

Natural Attractions of Athens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast or just enjoy natural beauty, Athens has plenty of options for you. The following outdoor attractions should be on your must-visit list:

  • Texas Freshwater Fisheries Center - An aquatic nature center, TFFC showcases the life cycles of freshwater fish and gives visitors hands-on activities, fishing, and tours of the hatchery.
  • Lake Athens - A popular destination for fishing, Lake Athens has several public boat ramps, kayak and paddleboard rentals, and camping spots. The smooth, clear water and surrounding forests make it a serene spot for a picnic or hike.
  • Purtis Creek State Park - A nature reserve with plenty of outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, swimming, horseback riding, bird watching, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Athens

Athens offers various trails and routes for cycling, jogging, or walking, while taking in scenic views and enjoying the outdoors. These are some of the best hikes and bike routes around Athens:

  • Trinity Valley Community College Nature Trail–A paved trail around the college’s campus
  • Tara Vineyard and Winery– Offers a scenic, moderate-grade hike around the vineyard and ranch views.
  • Cedar Creek Loop Trail–A 9.2-mile moderately trafficked loop trail with wildflower viewing.

Best Things to Do in Athens: Activities and Tours

While in Athens, make sure to check out these fun things to do and tours:

  • Texas Freshwater Fisheries Center daily hatchery tours
  • Athens Scuba Park Dive Weekends
  • Athens Annual Fiddlers Contest and Reunion– Featuring traditional music, jam sessions, and food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Athens

To make the most out of your stay in Athens, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Henderson County Historical Museum and East Texas Arboretum and Botanical Society Day
2 Go fishing or kayaking at Lake Athens
3 Tour the Texas Freshwater Fisheries and opt to go fishing
4 Hike the trails around Tara Vineyard and Winery
5 Spend the day scuba diving at Athens Scuba Park
6 Explore Purtis Creek State Park
7 Check out the Athens Annual Fiddlers Contest and Reunion

## Food and Drink Scene of Athens

Athens is known for its delicious Southern cooking, which should not be missed. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish with hush puppies, fried okra, and coleslaw from Catfish Plantation.
  • Barbecue brisket sandwich from Big Daddy’s BBQ and Grill.
  • Sweet potato pie and homemade ice cream from the Pie Lady.

Local Beverages

  • The Athens Brewery Serve a wide variety of beers that can be enjoyed onsite or taken home.
  • Roasted Coffee Depot offers freshly roasted coffee that can be enjoyed in a café or taken home.

Where to Stay in Athens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Athens has several hotel and lodging options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Best Western - A chain hotel with clean, comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • Victorian Inn - A bed and breakfast located in a 19th-century home with a great breakfast and gracious hosts.
  • Athens RV Park - For campers and RVers who want a comfortable and scenic place to stay.

What to Buy in Athens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to take home, these are some of the best places to shop in Athens:

  • Henderson County Farmer’s Market - Local produce, handmade crafts, and artisan products.
  • Athens Emporium - Antiques, vintage items and collectibles.
  • Tara Vineyard and Winery - Locally made wines and winery merchandise.

Local Culture and Customs of Athens

Athens is a small town that places great value on community and hospitality. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Always greet people when you pass them on the street and be friendly.

  • Respect local traditions and culture when visiting historical sites.

  • Smoking is prohibited in many public places and establishments.

Best Time to Visit Athens

The best time to visit Athens is in the Spring/Fall when the weather is mild. Summers can be hot, humid and a bit uncomfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Athens

Here are some do’s and don’ts when visiting Athens:

Do:

  • Visit local farms and farmers’ markets for fresh fruits and vegetables.

  • Explore Athens Scuba Park or go fishing in Lake Athens.

  • Take hiking and biking trails of Athens to enjoy scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind when in outdoor attractions.

  • Don’t ignore local customs and manners of the community.

  • Don’t forget to carry water bottles to stay hydrated in the heat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Athens

Athens is a small safe town, but be aware of your surroundings and belongings:

  • Don’t leave valuables visible in your car, and be aware of car break-ins around popular recreational spots.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when outside, and check for ticks regularly.

Health in Athens

Athens has a humid subtropical climate that can be dazzling for first-time visitors:

  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration and heat exhaustion when outdoors.
  • Use sunscreen and carry bug repellent to avoid skin damage or insect bites.

Getting to Athens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Athens

Athens can be accessed by the following airports:

  • Tyler Pounds Regional Airport (TYR), 40 miles from Athens.
  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW), 110 miles from Athens.

Bus Stations Near Athens

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Tyler, Texas, is the closest terminal located 33 miles away.

Other Options

  • A rental car or ride-sharing services is the most convenient option for getting around Athens.

How to Get Around in Athens

The best way to get around Athens is by car, whether renting or by your own vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Athens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring after your time in Athens:

  • Tyler, Texas
  • Palestine, Texas
  • Canton, Texas

Conclusion

Athens, Texas, is a gem of Southern hospitality, historical sites, and beautiful nature spots. We hope this guide has provided you with all of the information you need to plan your trip to Athens, including where to stay, what to see and do, and local customs. Enjoy your visit to this charming Texas town!
