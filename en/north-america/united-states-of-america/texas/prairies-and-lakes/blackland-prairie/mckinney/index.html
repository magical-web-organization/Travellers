Introduction to McKinney

McKinney is a charming city located in the heart of Blackland Prairie in Prairies and Lakes region of Texas. With its well-preserved historic downtown, family-friendly attractions, outdoor recreational opportunities, and renowned food and drink scene, McKinney offers endless opportunities for travelers to explore and discover.

List of Places to Visit in McKinney

Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or with friends, McKinney has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of the must-see places to visit in McKinney:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chestnut Square Historic Village: A beautifully restored village that showcases life in McKinney from 1854-1930.
  • Collin County History Museum: A museum that tells the story of Collin County from early settlers to the present day.
  • McKinney Performing Arts Center: A state-of-the-art venue that hosts a range of cultural events such as music concerts, theater performances, and dance shows.
  • Myers Park and Event Center: A sprawling park that features a rose garden, a maze, a butterfly house, and a range of historic buildings.

Natural Attractions of McKinney: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Erwin Park Hike and Bike Trail: A 212-acre park that offers miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Heard Natural Science Museum & Wildlife Sanctuary: A sprawling wildlife sanctuary that features a nature trail, educational exhibits, and various animals indigenous to North Texas.
  • Towne Lake Recreation Area: A picturesque park that offers fishing, boating, and picnic facilities.
  • Arbor Hills Nature Preserve: A 200-acre nature preserve that features walking trails, bike paths, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McKinney

McKinney is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking trails in McKinney:

  • Towne Lake Recreation Area Trail: A 2.17-mile trail that circles the lake, offering beautiful views of the water and surrounding greenery.
  • Erwin Park Hike and Bike Trail: A 12.9-mile trail that runs through the scenic park.
  • Bonnie Wenk Park Trail: A 1.75-mile trail that loops around the park with beautiful views of the park’s pond and garden.
  • Arbor Hills Nature Preserve Trail: A 2.3-mile trail that features wooded areas, meadows, and views of the city.

Best Things to Do in McKinney: Activities and Tours

From shopping to wine-tasting, here are some of the best things to do in McKinney:

  • Historic Downtown McKinney: Take a stroll through the historic downtown area and check out the unique shops, restaurants, and bars.
  • Mitas Hill Vineyard: Enjoy wine-tasting and a beautiful view of the vineyard and gardens.
  • Lone Star Wine Cellars: A winery that features a tasting room, outdoor patio, and live music.
  • McKinney Farmers Market: A weekly market featuring locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and live music.

Food and Drink Scene of McKinney

McKinney’s food and drink scene is renowned for its wide range of cuisine from BBQ to Thai. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: Hutchins BBQ, Red Hot & Blue, and Rick’s Chophouse are all highly recommended for their delicious Texas-style barbecue.
  • Hamburger: Square Burger, 5th Street Patio Cafe, and Rye are all local favorites.
  • Tex-Mex: Fajitas Tex-Mex and El Fenix both offer delicious Tex-Mex cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Tupps Brewery and Franconia Brewing Company both feature outdoor patios and beer tasting.
  • Wine: Landon Winery and Mitas Hill Vineyard both offer wine-tasting and beautiful views.

Where to Stay in McKinney: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a budget-friendly option, McKinney has a range of options to choose from:

  • The Grand Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of downtown that offers luxurious amenities.
  • Sheraton McKinney: A modern hotel that features an outdoor pool and fitness center.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham McKinney: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in McKinney: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring back home from McKinney, including:

  • Handmade Crafts: From pottery to jewelry, the McKinney Farmers Market and the Chestnut Square Historic Village have plenty of unique handmade crafts to choose from.
  • Texas-Sized Souvenirs: Stock up on cowboy hats, boots, and other Texas-themed souvenirs from local shops such as Lulu’s Boutique & Gifterie and CTA Dancewear.
  • Local Food Products: Grab a jar of locally-made jam or honey from McKinney Farmers Market or Texas Olive Oil Company.

Local Culture and Customs of McKinney

McKinney’s culture is deeply influenced by its history and the natural environment. Locals are known for their hospitality and friendly nature. McKinney also has a thriving arts community with a range of cultural events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit McKinney

The best time to visit McKinney is during the spring (March-May) when the weather is mild and the flowers are in bloom. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit when the leaves change colors and the weather is pleasant. Summers in McKinney can be very hot, so be prepared to stay cool and hydrated.

Do’s and Don’ts of McKinney

Do:

  • Do try Tex-Mex, BBQ, and other local cuisine.
  • Do visit the local museums and historical places.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor recreational opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t drink and drive.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and/or insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in McKinney

McKinney is a safe city, but it’s always best to take precautions. Lock your doors and keep valuables out of sight when leaving your car. Use crosswalks when crossing the street and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in McKinney

It’s important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen during the hot Texas summers. Mosquitoes can also be an issue, so bring insect repellent. If you need medical attention, there are several hospitals and clinics located in McKinney.

Getting to McKinney: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near McKinney

The closest airport to McKinney is Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW).

Bus Stations Near McKinney

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in McKinney.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way to get around McKinney, but Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in McKinney

Most of McKinney’s attractions are located within a few miles of each other, so walking or biking is a great option. If you’re traveling further, it’s best to drive or use ride-sharing apps.

Where to Go Next from McKinney: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

McKinney is located near several other interesting destinations, including:

  • Dallas: A quick 30-minute drive takes you to the bustling city of Dallas, where you can explore museums, shops, and restaurants.
  • Fort Worth: The city of Fort Worth is less than an hour away and offers a range of cultural and historic attractions.
  • Denton: A charming college town located 30 minutes north of McKinney, Denton is a great destination for music and art lovers.

Conclusion

McKinney is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. Whether you’re looking for a romantic getaway, a family vacation, or a solo adventure, McKinney has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and experience all that McKinney has to offer!
