Welcome to Bonham, a charming city tucked away in the Blackland Prairie, located in Prairies and Lakes, Texas, United States of America. Bonham promises a perfect blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure, making it a popular destination for tourists. From its fascinating museums and historical sites to its striking natural wonders and scenic hiking trails, Bonham has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do and see in Bonham, where to stay, and how to get around.

Introduction to Bonham

Named after James Bonham, a hero of the Alamo, Bonham boasts a rich history dating back to the mid-19th century. The city has stayed true to its roots, with numerous historic sites and landmarks throughout the city.

List of Places to Visit in Bonham

  • Sam Rayburn House Museum: This museum showcases the life and accomplishments of Sam Rayburn, a former Speaker of the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Fort Inglish: Built in 1840, Fort Inglish was used as a military base during the Republic of Texas era.
  • Sam Rayburn Library and Museum: Located on the campus of Austin College, this museum is dedicated to the memory of Sam Rayburn.
  • Bonham State Park: This park features a 65-acre man-made lake, perfect for fishing and swimming.
  • Bonham Heritage Museum: Housed in a former church, this museum highlights the history of Fannin County and the city of Bonham.
  • Fannin County Courthouse: Built in 1889, this iconic courthouse is a must-visit for history buffs.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bonham is known for its rich cultural heritage, and there are several museums and historical sites to check out. The Sam Rayburn House Museum, Fort Inglish, Sam Rayburn Library, and Bonham Heritage Museum are just some of the top attractions in Bonham that highlight its past.

Natural Attractions of Bonham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bonham is home to several state parks, lakes, and hiking trails, offering visitors a wealth of opportunities to experience the great outdoors. Bonham State Park is a popular destination known for its fishing, swimming, and hiking trails. Pat Mayse Lake and Lake Crockett are also great for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bonham

The Bonham area has several hiking and biking trails. Bonham State Park’s trails offer views of the lake and surrounding vegetation. The Fort Inglish Trail incorporates part of the red river trail used by Cherokee Indians and early settlers.

Best Things to Do in Bonham: Activities and Tours

Bonham has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors engaged. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Visit the Sam Rayburn House Museum: Tour the house where Sam Rayburn, a former Speaker of the House of Representatives, lived for over 50 years.
  • Hike or Swim at Bonham State Park: This state park offers several hiking trails and a 65-acre man-made lake for swimming.
  • Take a Drive Around the City: Bonham has several streets lined with historic homes and buildings that make for a beautiful scenic drive.
  • Fishing at Pat Mayse Lake or Lake Crockett: These scenic lakes offer the perfect spot to cast a line and enjoy the great outdoors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bonham

Day 1: Explore the Bonham Heritage Museum and the Fannin County Courthouse; Day 2: Take a hike at Bonham State Park and enjoy a picnic; Day 3: Visit Fort Inglish and the Sam Rayburn House Museum; Day 4: Enjoy a relaxing day at Pat Mayse Lake or Lake Crockett; Day 5: Explore the Sam Rayburn Library and Museum; Day 6: Take a drive around Bonham’s scenic views; Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products in downtown Bonham.

Food and Drink Scene of Bonham

Bonham has a rich culinary scene, with several dining options that visitors will enjoy. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: A Southern staple dish that Bonham is famous for.
  • Chili con Carne: A classic dish with a Texas twist.
  • Barbecue: Texas and barbecue are almost synonymous, and Bonham has several top-notch options.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: A refreshing drink that Bonham is known for.
  • Local Brews: Several local breweries in Bonham offer unique craft beers.

Where to Stay in Bonham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Several accommodation options are available in Bonham, ranging from budget-friendly hotels to luxurious resorts. The Holiday Inn Express & Suites Bonham and the Limestone Inn Bed & Breakfast are popular choices for visitors.

What to Buy in Bonham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bonham is a haven for souvenir hunters and shoppers. Downtown Bonham has several shops that sell handmade crafts, antiques, and unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bonham

Bonham, like most of Texas, has a unique culture and customs influenced by its history and geography. Bonham residents pride themselves on their Southern hospitality and warm personalities.

Best Time to Visit Bonham

The best time to visit Bonham is in the fall or spring when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing colors, making it perfect for outdoor activities and scenic drives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bonham

Do:

  • Take time to appreciate Bonham’s historic sites and natural wonders.
  • Try the local cuisine, including fried catfish and chili con carne.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions while exploring natural attractions such as hiking trails and lakes.
  • Forget to check the weather forecast when planning outdoor activities.
  • Litter or damage natural attractions and historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bonham

Bonham is considered a safe city, but visitors should always exercise caution and take typical safety precautions such as locking car doors, storing valuables out of sight, and not leaving belongings unattended.

Health in Bonham

Bonham has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, to cater to the health needs of visitors.

Getting to Bonham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Several transportation options are available to get to and around Bonham, including:

Airports Near Bonham

The closest airports to Bonham are Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) and Dallas Love Field (DAL). From there, visitors can rent a car or take a bus to Bonham.

Bus Stations Near Bonham

Greyhound has a station in Sherman, Texas, which is about 25 miles from Bonham.

Other Options

Guests can also drive to Bonham via Highway 121 or Highway 78.

How to Get Around in Bonham

The best way to get around Bonham is by car. Visitors can rent a car from the airport or downtown Bonham.

Where to Go Next from Bonham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bonham’s location makes it an ideal base for exploring other nearby destinations such as:

  • Dallas: A vibrant city known for its arts, history, and sports.
  • Denison: A city known for its historic downtown and museums, only 30 miles from Bonham.
  • Sherman: A bustling city with several museums and cultural attractions, situated just 25 miles from Bonham.

Conclusion

Bonham is a hidden gem in Texas, offering unique experiences that blend history, culture, and natural beauty. From hiking and fishing at Bonham State Park to exploring the city’s museums and historic sites, Bonham has something for everyone. Visit Bonham today and immerse yourself in Texas’s rich culture and warm hospitality.
