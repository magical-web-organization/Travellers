Introduction to Sherman

Nestled in the heart of Blackland Prairie, Sherman is a charming city in Grayson County, Texas. With a rich history, thriving arts scene, and diverse range of attractions, this city offers something for everyone.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie looking to indulge in local cuisine, Sherman has everything to make your trip unforgettable. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best that Sherman has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Sherman

To make the most of your trip to Sherman, here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sherman Jazz Museum: Learn about the history and impact of jazz music in America through interactive exhibits and historical artifacts.

  • Red River Railroad Museum: Immerse yourself in the history of trains and railroading in Texas with this fascinating museum.

  • Eisenhower Birthplace State Historic Site: Explore the childhood home of former U.S. President Dwight D. Eisenhower and see how it shaped his life and political views.

  • Harber Wildlife Museum: This unique museum features over 1,000 exotic animals from all over the world, including a full-size Bengal tiger, elephants, and more.

Natural Attractions of Sherman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hagerman National Wildlife Refuge: This expansive wildlife sanctuary is home to over 350 species of birds, making it a must-visit destination for birdwatchers.

  • Herman Baker Park: This scenic 48-acre park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful scenery to enjoy.

  • Eisenhower State Park: Located on the shores of Lake Texoma, this park is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming.

  • Waterloo Lake: With its fishing piers, walking trails, and picnic areas, this park is an ideal spot for a family outing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sherman

Looking to explore Sherman on foot or by bike? Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the city:

  • The Hagerman Rail Trail: This 2.8-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking, with scenic views of the Hagerman National Wildlife Refuge.

  • Herman Baker Park Trail: This beautiful 1.2-mile loop trail takes you on a scenic journey through the park, passing by picturesque lakes and winding creeks.

  • Pecan Grove Park Trail: This 1.4-mile trail runs along a creek and is great for both hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Sherman: Activities and Tours

From exciting activities to informative tours, Sherman has plenty to offer visitors:

  • Texoma Jet Ski and Boat Rentals: For an adrenaline-packed day on the water, rent a jet ski or boat and explore Lake Texoma.

  • Grayson County Frontier Village: Take a step back in time and learn about life in the 19th century with a visit to this living history museum.

  • Sherman Ghost Tours: Experience the city’s haunted history with a spooky nighttime walking tour through Sherman’s historic downtown district.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sherman

To make the most of your week in Sherman, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Sherman Jazz Museum and explore downtown Sherman
Day 2 Hike the Hagerman Rail Trail and enjoy a picnic at Hagerman National Wildlife Refuge
Day 3 Take a boat tour of Lake Texoma and explore Eisenhower State Park
Day 4 Visit the Red River Railroad Museum and see the Harber Wildlife Museum
Day 5 Enjoy a day of water sports and fun at Waterloo Lake
Day 6 Take a walking tour of Sherman’s historic downtown district
Day 7 Visit the Eisenhower Birthplace State Historic Site and relax at Herman Baker Park

Food and Drink Scene of Sherman

Discover the unique flavors of Sherman with this guide to the city’s food and drink scene:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texas-style BBQ: Get a taste of authentic Texas BBQ at places like Dickey’s Barbecue Pit or Shugabees BBQ.

  • Fried Chicken: Head to Fulbelli’s Restaurant for some of the best fried chicken in town.

  • Tex-Mex: Satisfy your Tex-Mex cravings at Casa Mama’s or Aranda’s Taqueria.

Local Beverages

  • Sherman Cellars Winery: Sample local wines and enjoy a glass or two at this charming winery.

  • 903 Brewers: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers inspired by Southern flavors and traditions.

Where to Stay in Sherman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Sherman to fit any budget. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hampton Inn Sherman: This popular hotel offers comfortable rooms and great amenities for a reasonable price.

  • Grand Central Hotel: Located in the heart of downtown Sherman, this historic hotel offers a charming and unique experience.

  • Sherman RV Park: For those traveling by RV, this park offers convenient and affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Sherman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to bring home a piece of Sherman with these souvenir and local product ideas:

  • Art by local artists: Visit The X-Change Art Gallery to shop for unique and beautiful pieces by local artists.

  • Pickles and preserves: Check out the homemade pickles and preserves at The Pickle Store for a tasty and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sherman

Texan hospitality is world-renowned, and Sherman is no exception. Visitors can expect warm and friendly locals who are always ready to lend a hand.

Best Time to Visit Sherman

Spring and fall are the best times to visit Sherman, with pleasant temperatures and beautiful scenery. Summer can be hot, but the abundance of water activities and outdoor adventures make it a great time to visit as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sherman

Make the most of your visit to Sherman with these helpful tips:

Do:

  • Try a local craft beer or wine

  • Take a walking tour of Sherman’s historic downtown district

  • Explore the natural beauty of Hagerman National Wildlife Refuge

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen

  • Leave valuables in your car

  • Be afraid to ask locals for recommendations

Safety and Health

Traveling to Sherman is generally safe, but it’s always important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Sherman

Sherman is a safe city, but like anywhere else, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in Sherman

Sherman has a range of medical facilities and pharmacies for any health needs you may have.

Getting to Sherman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Sherman is easy, with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Sherman

  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (83 miles)

  • Dallas Love Field Airport (73 miles)

Bus Stations Near Sherman

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Denison (10 miles)

Other Options

  • Rent a car to explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Sherman

Getting around Sherman is easy by car, bike, or on foot. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Sherman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sherman is the perfect basecamp for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations to add to your itinerary:

  • Denison: Just a few miles away from Sherman, Denison offers a charming downtown area and the Eisenhower Birthplace State Historic Site.

  • Dallas: Roughly an hour’s drive from Sherman, Dallas offers a wealth of attractions and amenities, from world-class museums to fantastic dining and nightlife.

  • Lake Texoma: Located on the border with Oklahoma, Lake Texoma is a popular destination for outdoor recreation and boasts some of the best fishing and boating in the area.

Conclusion

Sherman, Texas, is a hidden gem of the Lone Star State, offering a wealth of attractions, events, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-packed trip, Sherman has everything you need to make your stay unforgettable.
