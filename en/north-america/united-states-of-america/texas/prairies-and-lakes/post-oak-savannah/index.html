Introduction to Post Oak Savannah

Post Oak Savannah is one of the seven natural regions in Texas, which covers the easternmost part of the state between Dallas and Houston. The region is characterized by rolling plains, grassy fields, and woodlands filled with post oaks, pecan trees, and mesquite.

Post Oak Savannah is a popular destination for travelers who seek a glimpse of old-fashioned Texas charm. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, explore historical landmarks, and indulge in great food and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Post Oak Savannah

Post Oak Savannah has plenty of interesting places to visit. Below are some of the top cultural and natural attractions in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brazos Valley Farmers Market - located in Bryan-College Station, the market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and crafts made by local farmers and artisans.
  • Grand Stafford Theater - This historic theater in downtown Bryan features live music from Texas and beyond.
  • Fanthorp Inn State Historic Site - Step back in time to the mid-19th century at this preserved inn in Anderson. Visitors can see the original furniture and artifacts as well as learn about the history of the inn and the region.
  • Bonham Heritage Museum - The museum showcases the cultural and historical heritage of Bonham and Fannin County.
  • Chappell Hill Historical Museum - Visitors can learn about the history of Chappell Hill and Washington County from Native American times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Post Oak Savannah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Bryan - This man-made lake is a great spot for fishing, boating, swimming, and picnicking.
  • Sam Houston National Forest - This pristine forest covers more than 163,000 acres and provides opportunities for hiking, camping, horseback riding, and fishing.
  • Lick Creek Park - Located in College Station, the park offers trails for hiking, biking, and bird watching.
  • Camp Creek Park - This park in Franklin has trails, picnic areas, and a fishing lake.
  • Lake Somerville State Park - This park has two lakes for fishing, boating, and swimming, as well as hiking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Post Oak Savannah

Post Oak Savannah has plenty of natural trails for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Lone Star Hiking Trail - This trail is the longest continuous hiking trail in Texas, covering over 128 miles. The trail passes through diverse terrain and habitats, including hardwood forests, prairies, and wetlands.
  • Brazos Valley Trail System - This 16-mile trail system connects the cities of Bryan and College Station and passes through parks, natural areas, and neighborhoods.
  • Lake Somerville Trailway - This 14-mile trailway connects the Birch Creek Unit and the Nails Creek Unit of Lake Somerville State Park and passes through wooded areas, grasslands, and creeks.

Best Things to Do in Post Oak Savannah: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Post Oak Savannah. Whether you want to spend a day outdoors or explore local culture, here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Texas Star Winery Tour and Tasting - Located in Richards, this winery produces award-winning wines from estate-grown grapes. Visitors can take a guided tour of the vineyard and winery, taste the different wines, and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Aggieland Safari - This drive-thru safari park in Bryan offers a unique experience to see exotic animals from around the world without leaving Texas.
  • Camp Hearne Historic Site Tour - This WWII prisoner-of-war camp turned historic site in Hearne is a great place to learn about the history of the region and the war.
  • Messina Hof Winery and Resort Tour - This winery in Bryan offers tours and tastings that provide a glimpse into the winemaking process, history of the winery, and the local labels.
  • Santa’s Wonderland - Located in College Station, this Christmas-themed park offers a winter wonderland experience that includes ice-skating, train rides, light shows, and food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Post Oak Savannah

If you’re a food lover, Post Oak Savannah won’t disappoint. The region has a rich culinary heritage that includes traditional Southern cuisine and unique Texan flavors. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brisket - As the central dish in Texas BBQ, smoked brisket is a staple of Post Oak Savannah cuisine. You can find it in most local BBQ restaurants, such as Fargo’s Pit BBQ in Bryan and C&J Barbeque in College Station.
  • Fried Catfish - This is a classic Southern dish that is popular in Post Oak Savannah. You can try it at The Catch in College Station or Catfish Willy’s in Navasota.
  • Chicken Fried Steak - Another Southern classic, chicken-fried steak is a hearty meal that’s perfect for meat lovers. You can get it at The Farmhouse in College Station or The Feed Lot in Hearne.
  • Pecan Pie - Pecans are a signature crop of Texas, and pecan pie is a must-try dessert when you’re in Post Oak Savannah. You can get it at Royers Pie Haven in Round Top or Must Be Heaven in Brenham.

Local Beverages

  • Texas Wine - Post Oak Savannah has several wineries that produce excellent Texas wines. Messina Hof Winery in Bryan and Windy Winery in Brenham are two of the most popular.
  • Craft Beer - Beer enthusiasts should visit New Republic Brewing Company in College Station or Brazos Valley Brewery in Brenham to taste unique Texas-style craft beers.
  • Sweet Tea - Sweet iced tea is a Southern classic that’s a perfect thirst-quencher on hot days. You can get it at most restaurants in Post Oak Savannah.

Where to Stay in Post Oak Savannah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Post Oak Savannah, from budget-friendly motels to luxury resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Vintage Farmhouse in Brenham - This bed and breakfast in Brenham offers cozy rooms, a home-cooked breakfast, and a picturesque farm setting.

Local Culture and Customs of Post Oak Savannah

Post Oak Savannah has a unique cultural heritage and customs that reflect its rural roots and traditional way of life. Some local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Respect for elders - The region has a strong sense of community and respect for elders, and it’s customary to greet older people with a smile and a nod or a “sir” or “ma’am.”
  • Southern Hospitality - Hospitality is a big part of the Southern culture, and people in Post Oak Savannah are known for their friendliness, helpfulness, and generosity.
  • Country Music and Line Dancing - Country music and line dancing are popular pastimes in Post Oak Savannah, and you can find dance halls and clubs that offer live music and dancing.
