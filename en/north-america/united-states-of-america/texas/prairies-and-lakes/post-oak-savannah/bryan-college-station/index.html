A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Bryan-College Station

Located in Post Oak Savannah in the Prairies and Lakes region of Texas, Bryan-College Station is a vibrant college town known for its excellent museums, outdoor adventures, and historic sites. With a bustling food and drink scene and a rich local culture, there’s something here for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bryan-College Station

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, Bryan-College Station has plenty to offer. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Texas A&M University: Take a tour of the sprawling campus and learn about the school’s rich history and traditions.
  2. Brazos Valley African American Museum: Explore the history and culture of the African American community in the Brazos Valley.
  3. Bonfire Memorial: Pay homage to the 12 students who lost their lives in the 1999 Bonfire collapse at Texas A&M.

Natural Attractions of Bryan-College Station: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Bryan: Go fishing, swimming, or kayaking on this scenic lake.
  2. Lick Creek Park: Hike or bike through the beautiful wooded trails of this nature preserve.
  3. Brison Park: Enjoy a picnic or go birdwatching at this tranquil park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station is a bike-friendly town with plenty of scenic routes to explore. Here are some of the best:

  1. Carter Creek Trail: Take an easy ride through this wooded trail that loops around Lake Bryan.
  2. Wolf Pen Creek Trail: This 2.7-mile paved trail winds through the heart of College Station, passing by several parks and attractions.
  3. The Brazos Valley Farmers Market Ride: This 20-mile loop takes you through scenic farmland and ends at the farmers market in downtown Bryan.

Best Things to Do in Bryan-College Station: Activities and Tours

  1. Aggieland Safari: Take a safari ride and see exotic animals from around the world.
  2. Messina Hof Winery: Sample award-winning wines and take a tour of the vineyard.
  3. Brazos Valley Brewery: Enjoy locally brewed beer at this laid-back brewery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bryan-College Station

For the ultimate Bryan-College Station experience, follow this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Tour Texas A&M University and visit the Bonfire Memorial
2 Go for a hike or bike ride at Lick Creek Park
3 Explore the Brazos Valley African American Museum
4 Relax and swim at Lake Bryan
5 Visit the Aggieland Safari and Brazos Valley Brewery
6 Take a tour and sample wine at Messina Hof Winery
7 Check out the local farmers market and go on the Brazos Valley Farmers Market Ride

Food and Drink Scene of Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station is home to plenty of delicious dining options, from classic Southern comfort food to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. BBQ brisket: Enjoy smoky, melt-in-your-mouth brisket at Rudy’s BBQ.
  2. Chicken fried steak: Satisfy your cravings for Southern comfort food at Hubert’s BBQ.
  3. Kolaches: Sample these baked treats filled with sausage or fruit at Kolache Rolf’s.

Local Beverages

  1. Aggie Ale: Try this refreshing beer made by the New Republic Brewing Company.
  2. Maroon Wine: Savor a glass of this cabernet sauvignon produced in the Brazos Valley.
  3. Sweet Tea: Quench your thirst with the classic Southern drink served at many local restaurants and cafes.

Where to Stay in Bryan-College Station: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bryan-College Station offers a variety of options for accommodations, whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly hostel or a luxurious hotel. Here are some options:

  1. The Stella Hotel: This boutique hotel offers upscale rooms and amenities, including a rooftop pool and bar.
  2. Staybridge Suites: This all-suite hotel is perfect for families and offers a free breakfast buffet.
  3. Abigaile’s Treehouse: Experience the charm of a bed and breakfast at this cozy guesthouse.

What to Buy in Bryan-College Station: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, Bryan-College Station has plenty of options. Here are some ideas:

  1. Aggie gear: Show your Texas A&M pride with a t-shirt or hat from the campus bookstore.
  2. Baked goods: Pick up some fresh-baked kolaches or pies from one of the local bakeries.
  3. Local art: Browse galleries like the Village Cafe Art Gallery to find unique paintings and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station has a rich local culture, centered around the traditions and values of Texas A&M University. Visitors are encouraged to respect and participate in these customs, such as:

  • Aggie Muster: Held annually on April 21st, Aggies gather to honor fellow Aggies who have passed away.
  • Yell Practice: Join in on the tradition of practicing yells before Texas A&M football games.
  • Midnight Yell: Attend the raucous pep rally held on the night before home football games.

Best Time to Visit Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station has a mild climate year-round, although summers can be hot and humid. The best time to visit is in the spring or fall, when the temperature is mild and the weather is pleasant. Avoid visiting during home football games, as hotels and restaurants can be crowded and expensive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bryan-College Station

Do:

  • Visit the Texas A&M campus and learn about the school’s history and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine, including BBQ and kolaches.
  • Participate in local customs, such as Yell Practice and Midnight Yell.

Don’t:

  • Wear burnt orange, as it’s the color of Texas rivals, the University of Texas.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions of Bryan-College Station.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station is a safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Keep your valuables locked away and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station has several hospitals and medical clinics, including the Baylor Scott & White Medical Center and St. Joseph Regional Health Center.

Getting to Bryan-College Station: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bryan-College Station

The closest airport to Bryan-College Station is Easterwood Airport, located just 6 miles from Texas A&M University.

Bus Stations Near Bryan-College Station

Greyhound bus station is located just outside of downtown Bryan.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are available in Bryan-College Station.

How to Get Around in Bryan-College Station

Bryan-College Station is a bike-friendly town, with several bike rental stations throughout the city. There are also several taxi and ride-sharing services available, as well as a public transit system that connects the major attractions and hotels.

Where to Go Next from Bryan-College Station: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Texas, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Austin: Visit the state capital and experience its vibrant music and arts scene.
  • Houston: Explore the largest city in Texas and visit the Space Center Houston.
  • The Gulf Coast: Relax on the beach or go fishing at one of the many coastal towns, such as Galveston or Corpus Christi.

Conclusion

Bryan-College Station is a vibrant and diverse town with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, there are plenty of attractions to explore. With a thriving food and drink scene and a rich local culture, you won’t be disappointed by your visit to this Texas gem.
