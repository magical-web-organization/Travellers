Introduction to Weimar

Located in Post Oak Savannah, Prairies and Lakes region of Texas, Weimar is a small town of approximately 2,100 residents. Founded in 1873, Weimar was named after the city of Weimar in Germany.

Weimar is a charming town that offers visitors a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Weimar has something to offer every visitor.

List of Places to Visit in Weimar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Michael Catholic Church: The oldest Czech Catholic Church in the United States, St. Michael Catholic Church is a must-visit attraction in Weimar. The church is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful stained-glass windows.
  • Texas Polka Music Museum: Dedicated to preserving the history of polka music in Texas, this museum is a must-visit for music lovers. The museum features exhibits and displays that showcase the rich history of polka music in Texas.
  • Weimar Heritage Society: This society aims to preserve the history of Weimar. Visitors can explore the society’s museum and see artifacts and exhibits that document the rich history of the town.

Natural Attractions of Weimar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Fayette: Located just a short drive from Weimar, Lake Fayette is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and other water sports. The lake also offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Schonest Valley Trail: This 10-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is perfect for nature lovers.
  • Colorado River Trail: This 15-mile trail follows the Colorado River and is perfect for hiking, biking, or horseback riding. The trail offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding countryside.
