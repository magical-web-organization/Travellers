Introduction to Burton

Nestled in the heart of Post Oak Savannah, Burton is a small town in Washington County, Texas, that has preserved its rich history and natural heritage. The town is known for its German heritage, architecture, and culture and is a popular destination for travelers seeking a quiet and peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Burton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Burton Heritage Society Museum showcases the town’s history and culture through exhibits that highlight the life of early settlers, the railroad era, and the German culture.
  • The Texas Cotton Gin Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and offers a glimpse into the cotton industry’s history and processes through exhibits and demonstrations.
  • The Saints Peter and Paul Catholic Church is a stunning example of German architecture and craftsmanship. The church was built in 1913 and is still in use today, serving the local Catholic community.

Natural Attractions of Burton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Brazos River is a popular spot for kayaking, fishing, and exploring the local flora and fauna. Visitors can rent kayaks or canoes from local outfitters and enjoy a leisurely paddle down the river.
  • The Lake Somerville State Park is located just a few miles from Burton and offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, camping, and wildlife observation.
  • The Newman’s Castle is a unique attraction that offers tours of a medieval-style castle and features beautiful gardens, a moat, and a drawbridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burton

  • The Burton Rail Trail is a 3.5-mile route that follows the former train tracks through the town. The trail is a popular spot for walking, cycling, and birdwatching and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • The Lake Somerville State Park has a network of trails that range from easy to challenging and offer beautiful views of the lake and the surrounding landscape. The park also hosts guided hikes and educational programs for visitors.

Best Things to Do in Burton: Activities and Tours

  • The Texas Star Winery offers wine tastings and tours of its vineyard and winery. Visitors can sample a range of award-winning wines and enjoy the scenic views of the Texas countryside.
  • The Antique Rose Emporium is a beautiful garden that features over 500 varieties of antique roses and other native plants. The garden offers guided tours and educational programs for visitors.
  • The Royer’s Cafe is a beloved local restaurant that serves homemade pies, comfort food, and Texan-style hospitality. Visitors can enjoy a delicious meal while soaking in the small-town charm of Burton.

Food and Drink Scene of Burton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken-fried steak - a Texan classic and a must-try at the local diners and cafes.
  • German sausage - Burton has a strong German heritage, and the local sausages are a delicious treat.
  • Bluebell ice cream - a Texas institution that offers a range of flavors, including pecan praline, banana split, and rocky road.

Local Beverages

  • Bluebonnet Beer Co. - a local microbrewery that serves a range of craft beers made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Peeka Ranch Winery - a family-owned and operated winery that offers tastings of its award-winning wines, including the Blanc du Bois and the Cabernet Sauvignon.

Where to Stay in Burton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Burton Hotel and Conference Center offers comfortable rooms and suites at the heart of town, with easy access to local attractions and amenities.
  • The Pecan Grove RV Park is a popular option for travelers looking for a budget-friendly accommodation option. The park offers full hookups, a range of amenities, and easy access to the Lake Somerville State Park.

What to Buy in Burton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bluebonnet-themed souvenirs - from t-shirts and hats to keychains and magnets, Bluebonnet-themed souvenirs are a must-buy in Burton.
  • Local wines and beers - bring home a taste of Texas with local wines and beers from Peeka Ranch Winery and Bluebonnet Beer Co.

Local Culture and Customs of Burton

  • Burton has a strong German heritage, and visitors can experience it through the architecture, food, and festivals such as the Oktobertfest and the German Christmas Market.
  • The town is known for its hospitality and friendly locals, who are always willing to share a story or a recommendation.

Best Time to Visit Burton

The best time to visit Burton is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and the wildflowers and fall foliage are in full bloom. The town hosts several festivals and events during these seasons, including the Bluebonnet Festival and the Burton Cotton Gin Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burton

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and heritage and participate in festivals and events.
  • Try the local food and drink scene, which is a unique blend of German and Texan cuisine.
  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the town and its surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burton

Burton is a safe and peaceful town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should exercise normal precautions and avoid leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Burton

There are several medical facilities in Burton, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should have adequate health insurance and carry any necessary prescription medications.

Getting to Burton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burton

The nearest airports to Burton are the George Bush Intercontinental Airport in Houston and the Austin-Bergstrom International Airport in Austin, both of which are about 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Burton

The nearest bus station to Burton is the Greyhound bus station in Brenham, about 15 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option for getting around Burton and its surroundings. There are several car rental companies in nearby towns, including Brenham and College Station.

How to Get Around in Burton

The best way to get around Burton is by car or bike. There are several bike rental companies in nearby towns, and the Burton Rail Trail and the Lake Somerville State Park have bike-friendly trails.

Where to Go Next from Burton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Brenham - a charming town known for its Blue Bell Creameries and picturesque downtown.
  • Chappell Hill - a historic town that offers a range of antique shops, restaurants, and natural attractions.
  • College Station - home to Texas A&M University and a range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Burton is a hidden gem in the heart of Texas, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Explore the museums and historical sites, hike the trails, and savor the local cuisine, and you’ll understand why Burton is such a beloved destination among travelers.
