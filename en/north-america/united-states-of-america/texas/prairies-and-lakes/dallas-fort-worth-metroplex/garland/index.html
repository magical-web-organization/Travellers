Welcome to Garland, Texas – a vibrant city located in the heart of the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex. This lively destination boasts a rich history, exciting culture, and an abundance of outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in Garland.

List of Places to Visit in Garland

Garland has a variety of attractions to explore, ranging from cultural landmarks to outdoor adventures. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Garland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Garland Landmark Museum – Discover the history of Garland, Texas, by visiting the Garland Landmark Museum. Located in the former Santa Fe Depot, this museum boasts exhibits that showcase the city’s WWII contributions, the first African-American policemen, and more.
  • Granville Arts Center – The Granville Arts Center is Garland’s performing arts venue. This multifaceted facility houses a variety of performance spaces, including a proscenium theatre, an intimate theatre, and an outdoor amphitheater.

Natural Attractions of Garland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spring Creek Forest Preserve – Meander through the forests at Spring Creek Forest Preserve, the largest urban forest in North Texas. This 100-acre wilderness preserve offers hiking trails, wildlife observation areas, and scenic views.
  • Breckinridge Park – Breckinridge Park is a 400-acre park that offers hiking trails, a fishing lake, picnic pavilions, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Garland

Garland has a variety of hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Spring Creek Nature Trail – This 4.5-mile paved trail follows the Spring Creek Forest Preserve, offering sweeping views of the forest.
  • Rowlett Creek Trail – The Rowlett Creek Trail is a 15-mile trail that winds through Garland and neighboring cities, offering scenic views and access to several parks.

Best Things to Do in Garland: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours in Garland. Here are some must-try experiences:

  • Garland Food Tours – Embark on a culinary journey and discover the rich flavors of Garland with Garland Food Tours. Sample local cuisine and learn about the city’s history and food culture.
  • Surf and Swim Waterpark – Cool off from the Texas heat at Surf and Swim Waterpark, Garland’s premier water park. The park boasts a wave pool, lazy river, slides, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Garland

Garland has so much to offer that you may need a week to experience it all. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Garland Landmark Museum, Historic Downtown Garland, Patty Granville Arts Center
  • Day 2: Spring Creek Forest Preserve, Hawaiian Falls Garland
  • Day 3: Garland Food Tours, Firewheel Town Center, and Breckinridge Park
  • Day 4: Rowlett Creek Trail, Firewheel Golf Park, Firewheel Brewing Company
  • Day 5: Surf and Swim Waterpark, Plaza Theatre Downtown Garland, and Eastern Treasures
  • Day 6: Garland Symphony Orchestra, Bass Pro Shops, and Evolving Texas Style BBQ
  • Day 7: Firewheel Farmers Market, Shenaniganz, and the Garland Opry

Food and Drink Scene of Garland

Garland’s diverse culinary scene offers something for everyone, from Southern comfort food to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue Brisket – Texas is known for its barbecue, and Garland is no exception. For a mouth-watering brisket, head to Meshack’s BBQ.
  • Enchiladas – Experience the flavors of Mexico at Desperados Mexican Restaurant, where the enchiladas are a fan favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Firewheel Coffee – Support local by sipping on a cup of coffee at Firewheel Coffee.
  • Craft Beer – Garland is home to several craft breweries, including Intrinsic Smokehouse and Brewery and Lakewood Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Garland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Garland has a variety of lodging options to choose from, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hyatt Place Dallas/Garland/Richardson – This modern hotel offers spacious rooms and easy access to the city’s top attractions.
  • Motel 6 Garland - Dallas - Northwest Highway – For budget-friendly accommodation, consider staying at Motel 6 Garland.

What to Buy in Garland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Garland home with you by purchasing a local souvenir or product. Here are some suggestions:

  • Handmade Items – Explore the boutiques and galleries in Historic Downtown Garland, where you’ll find unique handmade items by local artisans.
  • Firewheel Town Center – Shop at the Firewheel Town Center, Garland’s premier shopping destination. This open-air mall boasts over 100 stores, including major retailers and local boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Garland

Garland has a rich history and diverse culture, shaped by its community of immigrants and a lively arts scene. The city is known for its friendly locals and welcoming atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Garland

The best time to visit Garland is in the fall and spring, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer is also a popular time to visit, although temperatures can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Garland

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and craft beer.
  • Do visit the cultural landmarks and outdoor attractions.
  • Do explore the boutiques and galleries in Historic Downtown Garland.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the chance to experience a live performance at the Granville Arts Center.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals – Garland is known for its friendly residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Garland

Garland is a safe city, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when exploring.

Health in Garland

Garland has several hospitals and clinics to ensure that you stay healthy during your visit. You can also find pharmacies and drugstores throughout the city.

Getting to Garland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Garland is easily accessible from major cities in Texas and neighboring states. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Garland

  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport – Located 32 miles southwest of Garland.
  • Dallas Love Field – Located 18 miles southwest of Garland.

Bus Stations Near Garland

  • Greyhound Bus Lines – Located in downtown Dallas, approximately 20 miles southwest of Garland.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals – Several major car rental companies operate in Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport and downtown Dallas.
  • Public Transportation – DART (Dallas Area Rapid Transit) offers bus and train service throughout Dallas and its surrounding cities.

How to Get Around in Garland

Garland has several transportation options to help you get around the city, including:

  • Car Rentals – Several major car rental companies operate in Garland.
  • Public Transportation – DART offers bus service throughout Garland.
  • Rideshare Services – Uber and Lyft operate in Garland.

Where to Go Next from Garland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Garland is located in the heart of the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, making it an ideal base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Dallas – Explore the vibrant city of Dallas, located just 20 miles southwest of Garland.
  • Fort Worth – Visit the cowboys and culture of Fort Worth, located approximately 40 miles southwest of Garland.

Conclusion

Garland, Texas, is a lively city that has something to offer for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, food, or outdoor adventures, you’re sure to find something to love in this vibrant destination. Plan your trip today and start exploring all that Garland has to offer!
