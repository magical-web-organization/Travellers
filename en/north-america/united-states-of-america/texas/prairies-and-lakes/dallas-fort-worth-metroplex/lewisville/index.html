Introduction to Lewisville

Located in the heart of the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, Lewisville offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in learning about the city’s history, exploring its many hiking trails, or simply enjoying great food and drink, Lewisville has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lewisville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning more about the history and culture of Lewisville, there are several must-see attractions to add to your itinerary. These include:

  • Lewisville Lake Environmental Learning Area (LLELA): This nature preserve features more than 2,000 acres of hiking trails, wildlife viewing areas, and educational exhibits.

  • Lewisville Lake: Boasting 29,000 acres and 233 miles of shoreline, this recreational lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, boating, and more.

  • Old Town Lewisville: A quaint and charming historic district featuring unique shops, restaurants, and live music venues.

Natural Attractions of Lewisville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lewisville is home to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Texas. Here are a few of the best outdoor attractions to explore:

  • Lewisville Lake Park: This sprawling park offers miles of walking and biking trails, picnic areas, playgrounds, and more.

  • Lake Park Golf Club: Consistently ranked as one of the best golf courses in Texas, Lake Park Golf Club features 27 challenging holes and breathtaking views of Lewisville Lake.

  • Oakbend Trail: This scenic mountain biking trail winds through Lewisville’s gorgeous natural terrain, offering riders stunning views and exhilarating challenges.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewisville

If you’re an avid hiker or mountain biker, Lewisville offers a wide array of trails and routes to explore. Here are a few of the most popular:

  • Sunset Trail: This 1.7-mile hiking trail winds through the LLELA nature preserve, offering visitors stunning views of the sunset over Lewisville Lake.

  • North Shore Trail: This challenging 9.4-mile mountain biking trail is one of the best in Texas, featuring breathtaking views and plenty of technical challenges.

  • Eagle Point Trail: This 1.6-mile hiking trail is perfect for those looking for a leisurely stroll through Lewisville’s beautiful natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Lewisville: Activities and Tours

From live music to outdoor adventures to food and drink tours, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Lewisville. Here are a few of our favorite activities and tours:

  • Lewisville Lake Symphony: Whether you’re a music lover or just looking for a fun night out, the Lewisville Lake Symphony offers a variety of concerts and events throughout the year.

  • Lewisville Ghost Tours: Explore Lewisville’s spooky side with this fun and educational ghost tour, which takes visitors to some of the city’s most haunted locations.

  • Foodie Tour of Lewisville: Sample some of Lewisville’s best cuisine with this guided food and drink tour, featuring stops at some of the city’s most popular restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Lewisville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly hostel or a luxurious hotel, Lewisville offers a variety of accommodations to suit every taste and budget. Here are a few of the best:

Local Culture and Customs of Lewisville

When you visit Lewisville, it’s important to remember to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Texans are known for their hospitality, so don’t be shy about striking up a conversation with locals.

  • Dress for the weather, as temperatures in Lewisville can be warm and humid, especially in the summer months.

  • When dining out, it’s common to leave a 15-20% tip for your server.

Best Time to Visit Lewisville

Lewisville can be enjoyed year-round, but the best time to visit is from March to May and from September to November, when temperatures are mild and the city’s outdoor attractions are at their most enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewisville

Do:

  • Explore Lewisville’s many hiking and biking trails.
  • Sample some of the city’s delicious cuisine and local beverages.
  • Take a guided tour of the city to learn more about its fascinating history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the city’s natural or historic attractions.
  • Drive after consuming alcohol, as Texas has strict laws against drunk driving.
  • Forget to tip your server when dining out.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewisville

Lewisville is generally a very safe city, but visitors should still take standard precautions to ensure their safety. These include locking car doors, avoiding unfamiliar areas after dark, and being aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Lewisville

There are several good hospitals and medical clinics in Lewisville, including Medical City Lewisville and Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital.

Getting to Lewisville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewisville

The closest airport to Lewisville is Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport, which is located just 13 miles away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Lewisville

Greyhound operates a bus station in nearby Denton, which is just a 20-minute drive from Lewisville.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore Lewisville and the surrounding area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Lewisville

For those who don’t want to rent a car, Lewisville also offers several public transportation options, including buses and a light rail system. Hiking and biking are also popular options for getting around in the city.

Where to Go Next from Lewisville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring the surrounding area, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The city of Dallas, which is just a 30-minute drive from Lewisville and offers a wide variety of cultural attractions and activities.
  • The Fort Worth Stockyards, which offer a look at Texas’ cowboy culture and history.
  • The nearby city of Denton, which is home to several great museums, music venues, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

With its unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, Lewisville is the perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Texas experience. Whether you’re hiking a scenic trail, exploring a museum, or enjoying a delicious meal, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this vibrant and welcoming city.
