Frisco Travel Guide: Discover the Best of Texas’ Hidden Gem

Welcome to Frisco! This charming town located in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex offers a mix of cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and modern amenities. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, a foodie, or a nature lover, Frisco has something to offer you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Frisco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Frisco Heritage Museum: Take a journey back in time at this museum that showcases the history of Frisco and Texas through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • National Videogame Museum: This museum is a must-visit for gamers of all ages. It takes you on a trip through the evolution of gaming consoles and technology.
  • Frisco Athletic Center: This sports and fitness complex offers diverse amenities from basketball courts to a rock-climbing wall.
  • Shawnee Trail: Learn about the historic Shawnee Cattle Trail and its significance in Frisco’s history.

Natural Attractions of Frisco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Frisco Commons Park: This park offers a natural escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. It has over 63 acres of vast open spaces, walking trails, a pond, and a playground for kids.
  • Harold Bacchus Community Park: A park well known for its beautiful playground and The Monarch butterfly garden. It also features a youth fishing pond, tennis and basketball courts, and much more.
  • Stonebriar Centre: A fantastic modern-day shopping center that features notable retailers, restaurants, and a movie theatre.
  • Toyota Stadium: A stadium that is home to FC Dallas, Texas’ Major League Soccer team. You can catch a game, take a tour of the facility, or attend one of the many live concerts or events hosted here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frisco

  • Frisco Athletic Center Nature Trail: Experience 63 acres of beauty by hiking around Frisco Commons Park. The trails are perfect for hiking and biking, and you can enjoy the serenity of the outdoors.
  • Preston Ridge Trail: Take a scenic route through Frisco and The Colony by biking with the family or walking with friends and taking the view in.
  • Shawnee Trail: This trail takes you on a journey through the history of Frisco. It stretches over 6.5 miles from B.F. Phillips Community Park to the town hall and the Frisco Athletic Center.

Best Things to Do in Frisco: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Rooms: Test your thinking, memory and problem-solving skills by participating in fun and exciting escape rooms all over Frisco. Get off the “real world” and enjoy the puzzle experience.
  • Topgolf: Take your chance in driving the golf ball at one of Texas’ largest Topgolf locations right in Frisco!
  • I Fly Indoor Skydiving: If you’re looking for an unforgettable experience, I Fly Indoor Skydiving is the place to be. You can find out what freefalling feels like without jumping out of an airplane.
  • Mercedes-Benz Stadium Tours: Take a self-guided walking tour of the 24,000-capacity stadium with over 16,000 seats for concerts and major events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frisco

Looking to spend a week in Frisco? Here’s a suggested itinerary to make your trip an unforgettable one:

  • Day 1: Visit the Frisco Athletic Center, Frisco Heritage Museum, and The Shawnee Trail.
  • Day 2: Explore the natural attractions of Frisco, including Harold Bacchus Community Park and Stonebriar Centre.
  • Day 3: Hike and bike the trails of Frisco, including the Preston Ridge Trail and Frisco Athletic Center Nature Trail.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby destinations, such as the Dallas Arboretum, the Perot Museum of Nature and Science, or the African American Museum.
  • Day 5: Attend a live event at the Toyota Stadium, such as an FC Dallas game, a concert, or a festival.
  • Day 6: Take part in Fun Activities day, including Escape Rooms, Topgolf and I Fly Indoor Skydiving.
  • Day 7: Shop, dine and unwind at the Stonebriar Centre.

Food and Drink Scene of Frisco

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: Texas is renowned for its delicious barbecue, and Frisco is no exception. Find some of the best BBQ joints, such as Hutchins BBQ, 3 Stacks Smoke & Tap House, and TEN50 BBQ.
  • Tex-Mex: Enjoy some authentic Tex-Mex favorites, such as fajitas, burritos, and tacos, at restaurants such as Miriam Cocina Latina, Mexi-Go Restaurant & Bar, and Cantina Laredo.
  • Burgers: Get your burger fix at popular burger joints, such as Liberty Burger, The Community Grill, and MOOYAH Burgers, Fries & Shakes.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Quench your thirst with some locally brewed craft beer at breweries such as Frisco Rail Yard, Unlawful Assembly Brewing Co, and Tupps Brewery.
  • Wine and Spirits: Get a taste of Texas wine or a craft cocktail at restaurants like Silver Fox Steakhouse, Perry’s Steakhouse & Grille, or Rye Craft Food & Drink.

Where to Stay in Frisco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Embassy Suites: This all-suite hotel is located in the heart of Frisco, close to Frisco Athletic Center and Dr. Pepper Ballpark.
  • Hilton Dallas/Plano Granite Park: A luxurious hotel set in a stunning location, this hotel offers easy access to Stonebriar Centre and Comerica Center.
  • The Westin Stonebriar Hotel & Golf Club: A serene hotel offering a relaxing atmosphere and stunning golf course views. This hotel is perfect for those looking to escape the chaos of the city.

What to Buy in Frisco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Frisco T-shirts and hats: Represent Frisco and Texas with a souvenir T-shirt or hat from one of the many local stores.
  • Furniture and Home Decor: Browse unique stores like the Rustic Warehouse and Julie’s Home Furnishings for exquisite furniture items.
  • Gourmet Food Products: Bring home some delicious locally produced snacks, jams and spices from the Frisco Fresh Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Frisco

Frisco is known for its welcoming attitude and friendly residents. Respect locals by learning some Texan slang and phrases to get you through your trip. Always ask for permission before taking someone’s photo, and remember to tip your servers and waitstaff.

Best Time to Visit Frisco

The best time to visit Frisco is during the shoulder seasons of fall (September-November) and spring (March-May) when temperatures are mild, and the crowds are manageable. Winters can be harsh, and summers can be sweltering with the crowds at peak levels.

Do’s and Don’ts of Frisco

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture
  • Explore the vast nature and outdoor activities of Frisco.
  • Visit the local attraction spots in town.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment; keep Frisco clean and tidy.
  • Drink and drive or break any laws - Follow the rules and regulations.
  • Be rude to the locals - Show everyone you come across with respect and kindness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Frisco

Frisco is a very safe town, and the crime rate is exceptionally low. Ensure that you take precautions like not leaving your valuables unattended in public spaces and locking your car doors to avoid any loss or theft.

Health in Frisco

There are many medical facilities in Frisco, ranging from urgent care centers to hospitals. Remember to have complete medical insurance cover for your trip in case of any health emergencies.

Getting to Frisco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Frisco

  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport: located 30 miles away from Frisco.
  • Dallas Love Field Airport: located 25 miles away from Frisco.

Bus Stations Near Frisco

  • Greyhound: located in Downtown Dallas, a 40-minute drive from Frisco.

Other Options

  • Uber/Lyft: Transportation services are very popular in Frisco, and they have increased in availability over the years.
  • Car Rental: There are also several car rental companies in Frisco.

How to Get Around in Frisco

The best ways to get around in Frisco is by renting a car or ridesharing services like Uber/Lyft. The town is well-connected and has an easy to navigate highway system. Also, the city is accommodating to cyclists and pedestrians.

Where to Go Next from Frisco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dallas: The city of “Big D” is just a 30-minute drive from Frisco. There you can visit the Dallas Arboretum, the Perot Museum of Nature and Science, and more.
  • Plano: The neighboring city of Frisco and offers several attractions such as Arbor Hills Nature Preserve and The Shops at Legacy.
  • Fort Worth: Home to several world-class museums, such as the Kimbell Art Museum and the Amon Carter Museum of American Art.

Conclusion

Frisco is a beautiful town that offers something for everyone. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, this charming town keeps its visitors engaged and captivated. So, plan your trip to Frisco today and immerse yourself in the beauty of Texas’ hidden gem.
