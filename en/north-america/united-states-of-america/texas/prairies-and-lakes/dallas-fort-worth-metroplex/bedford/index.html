Introduction to Bedford

Located in the heart of the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, Bedford is a small city with a lot of charm. This quiet suburb is a dream destination for anyone looking for a getaway from the hustle and bustle of the big city. Bedford is packed with things to see and do, from hiking trails to tasty food institutions. Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or with family, Bedford has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Bedford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Bedford School

Built-in 1915, the Old Bedford School is a restored building that has been reused as a community center. The school is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and is a great spot to visit if you’re interested in the history of Bedford.

Bedford Boys Ranch

The Bedford Boys Ranch is a 52-acre park that includes a community center, playgrounds, fishing ponds, and sports fields. The park is named after a group of young men from Bedford who died in WWII and is an important historical site in the city.

Bedford Public Library

The Bedford Public Library is a great place to visit if you’re looking for some quiet time and a good book. The library also offers events and programs for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Bedford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bear Creek Park

Bear Creek Park is a 30-acre park in the heart of Bedford that offers walking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas. The park is also home to the Bedford Bark Park, an off-leash dog park that is perfect for four-legged friends.

River Legacy Park

Located just a short drive from Bedford, River Legacy Park is a 1,300-acre park that stretches across both Arlington and Grand Prairie. The park offers hiking trails, biking trails, fishing ponds, and even an equestrian trail for horseback riding.

Lake Grapevine

Lake Grapevine is a beautiful reservoir just north of Bedford that offers boating, fishing, kayaking, and other water sports. The lake is also surrounded by hiking and biking trails, making it a great spot for outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bedford

North East Mall Trail

The North East Mall Trail is a 3.5-mile paved trail that connects the NE Mall and the Bedford Boys Ranch. The trail is perfect for families and beginners who want a gentle, scenic walk.

Colleyville Nature Center Trail

Located just a short drive from Bedford, the Colleyville Nature Center Trail is a 3.5-mile trail that winds through 46 acres of natural beauty. The trail offers plenty of space for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

River Legacy Park Trail

The River Legacy Park Trail is a 10-mile unpaved trail that winds through the heart of the park. The trail is perfect for more experienced hikers and bikers who want to explore the park’s natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Bedford: Activities and Tours

Painting with a Twist

Unleash your inner artist at Painting with a Twist in Bedford. This paint and sip studio offers classes for painters of all skill levels, complete with wine and snacks.

Bedford Splash

Beat the heat at Bedford Splash, a family-friendly water park with pools, slides, and a splash pad. The park also offers swimming lessons and fitness classes.

Urban Air Adventure Park

Get your adrenaline pumping at Urban Air Adventure Park, a trampoline park with a zip line, ropes course, and ninja warrior courses. The park is perfect for families and active travelers.

Food and Drink Scene of Bedford

Must-Try Dishes

Boomerjack’s Grill and Bar

Boomerjack’s Grill and Bar is a Texas institution, serving up delicious burgers and appetizers since 1999.

Tony’s Pizza and Pasta

Tony’s Pizza and Pasta is a family-owned restaurant that serves up delicious Italian cuisine.

Latin Pig

Latin Pig is a popular food truck that serves up mouth-watering Cuban sandwiches and other Latin-inspired dishes.

Local Beverages

Rabbit Hole Brewing

Rabbit Hole Brewing is a local brewery that specializes in small-batch craft beer. The taproom is a great spot to try some unique brews and meet fellow beer lovers.

Central Market

Central Market is a local grocery store that sells a wide selection of beer and wine, perfect for a picnic or evening in.

Where to Stay in Bedford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Courtyard Dallas DFW Airport West/Bedford

The Courtyard Dallas DFW Airport West/Bedford is a modern hotel with comfortable rooms and easy access to both Dallas and Fort Worth.

TownePlace Suites Dallas Bedford

The TownePlace Suites Dallas Bedford is a great choice for extended stays, offering spacious suites with kitchenettes and free breakfast.

Homewood Suites by Hilton Ft Worth-Bedford

The Homewood Suites by Hilton Ft Worth-Bedford is a popular choice for families, offering spacious suites with separate living areas and fully equipped kitchens.

What to Buy in Bedford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

BedQuarters

BedQuarters is a local bedding store that offers high-quality linens and pillows, perfect for a cozy night’s sleep.

Prime Farm to Market

Prime Farm to Market is a local store that sells fresh, locally-sourced produce and other food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Bedford

Annual Art Festivals

Bedford hosts two annual festivals, the Bedford Blues and BBQ Festival and the Art in the Square Festival, that celebrate the city’s love of music and art.

Family-Oriented Culture

Bedford is known for its family-oriented culture, with plenty of parks, playgrounds, and community events for families to enjoy.

Best Time to Visit Bedford

The best time to visit Bedford is in the fall or spring when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and occasionally snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bedford

Do:

  • Try some local cuisine at Tony’s Pizza and Pasta or Boomerjack’s Grill and Bar.
  • Visit the Old Bedford School for a history lesson on the city.
  • Explore the beautiful hiking trails in River Legacy Park.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray for outdoor activities.
  • Miss the Bedford Blues and BBQ Festival or the Art in the Square Festival.
  • Leave without trying some local craft beer at Rabbit Hole Brewing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bedford

Bedford is a safe city, but it’s always important to practice basic safety precautions like locking your car and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Bedford

There are several hospitals and medical centers in and around Bedford, including Texas Health Harris Methodist Hospital Hurst-Euless-Bedford and Baylor Scott & White Medical Center - Grapevine.

Getting to Bedford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bedford

The closest airport to Bedford is the Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport, which is just a 20-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bedford

The Trinity Metro bus system offers several stops in and around Bedford, making it easy to get around the city by public transportation.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a ride-sharing service like Uber or Lyft is also a great option for getting around Bedford and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Bedford

The best way to get around Bedford is by car, but there are also several bike paths and public transportation options available.

Where to Go Next from Bedford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fort Worth

Just a 20-minute drive from Bedford, Fort Worth is a vibrant city with plenty of museums, restaurants, and shopping.

Dallas

Dallas is just a 30-minute drive from Bedford and has even more world-class museums, shopping, and dining options.

Conclusion

Bedford may be a small city, but it’s packed with things to see and do. From beautiful natural scenery to delicious local cuisine, this charming suburb has something to offer for every traveler. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an active adventure, Bedford is a destination not to be missed.
