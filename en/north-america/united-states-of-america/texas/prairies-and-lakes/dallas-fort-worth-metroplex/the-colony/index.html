Introduction to The Colony

The Colony is a small suburban town in Denton County, within the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, known for its beautiful natural scenery and rich cultural heritage. Originally founded as a housing community for employees of Texas Instruments, The Colony has grown into a bustling town attracting visitors looking for a relaxing getaway from the Dallas city hustle. With a mix of outdoor activities, historic landmarks and modern amenities, The Colony offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in The Colony

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Colony boasts a rich culture and history, evident in the many museums and historical sites that dot the town. The following are must-visit cultural attractions:

  • The Colony Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history and heritage of The Colony through an impressive collection of artifacts and photographs.

  • Stewart Creek Park: This park offers a glimpse into the Native American history of the region through its impressive collection of Indian artifacts.

  • Kern Park: This impressive park and cemetery sits on the site of a Civil War battle and boasts beautiful walking trails, historic markers, and impressive views.

Natural Attractions of The Colony: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Colony offers breathtaking views and a range of outdoor adventures for nature lovers. Here are some top outdoor attractions worth visiting:

  • Lewisville Lake: This massive lake offers camping, fishing, boating, and swimming activities in the summer.

  • Hawaiian Falls: Enjoy a day of family fun at this water park, complete with water slides, lazy river, and wave pool.

  • Little Elm Park: This sprawling park has everything from a beach to a boat dock to picnic areas, perfect for a day out in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Colony

The Colony has some of the most picturesque hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are the top ones to try out:

  • The Colony Five Star Hike and Bike Trail: This beautiful trail begins at The Colony Shoreline Trail Park and offers 2.6 miles of scenic views of Lewisville Lake.

  • Cedar Hill State Park: Enjoy over 1,200 acres of trekking and biking activities with a mix of easy and moderate trails.

  • Oak Point Park & Nature Preserve: This park offers over 800 acres of land with miles of trails, a lake, and a pavilion perfect for peaceful strolls.

Best Things to Do in The Colony: Activities and Tours

From leisurely activities to adrenaline-pumping experiences, the town of The Colony offers plenty of fun things to do. Check out some ideas below:

  • The Escape Game Dallas: Enjoy a fun and challenging escape room experience that’s sure to leave you entertained.

  • The Tribute Golf Course: This world-renowned golf course offers breathtaking views of Lake Lewisville and is perfect for any golf enthusiast.

  • The City by Nature: Take a guided tour of the town and get an up-close view of its rich history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of The Colony

The Colony has a vibrant food and drink scene that’s worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brisket: The Colony is known for its delicious and tender brisket. Try some at The Smokehouse at The Colony.

  • Tex-Mex: You can’t visit Texas without trying some good ol’ Tex-Mex. Head to Mi Dia From Scratch for some authentic and delicious cuisine.

  • Pie: Visit Buttermilk Sky Pie Shop for some delicious homemade pies that are hard to resist.

Local Beverages

  • Shiner Bock: The Colony’s favorite beer is the Shiner Bock, brewed by the Spoetzl Brewery in Shiner, Texas, about three hours away.

  • Whiskey: Enjoy some local whiskey at the Witherspoon Distillery and Tasting Room.

  • Wine: Savor some of the exquisite wines at the Lone Star Wine Cellars.

Local Culture and Customs of The Colony

The residents of The Colony are known for their friendliness and laid-back attitude. Here are some customs and cultural practices to bear in mind during your visit:

  • Respect Diversity: The Colony is a diverse community, and visitors are advised to respect and embrace different cultural backgrounds.

  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in The Colony, with the standard being 15% to 20% of the total bill amount.

Best Time to Visit The Colony

The best time to visit The Colony is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are at their peak. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can get chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Colony

Do:

  • Enjoy local cuisine: Get a taste of Texan cuisine and local beer.

Don’t:

  • Neglect Safety: Stay safe with the right gear and follow safety guidelines while enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in The Colony

The Colony is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions like:

  • Avoiding deserted areas: Stay to well-lit areas and avoid empty streets at night.

  • Protecting your valuables: Keep your wallets, purses, and other possessions safe while visiting crowded areas.

Health in The Colony

The health infrastructure in The Colony is well-developed, with several hospitals and clinics available to handle medical emergencies.

The Colony is a small town that is easy to navigate. Visitors can opt for rental cars, bicycles, or taxis to get around. Public transportation services, such as bus services, are limited.
