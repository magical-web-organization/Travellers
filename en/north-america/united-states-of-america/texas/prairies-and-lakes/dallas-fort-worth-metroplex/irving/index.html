Introduction to Irving

Irving, a city in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, is a culturally rich and naturally beautiful destination in Texas. It is located in the Prairies and Lakes region of the state, making it a perfect base for exploring nearby places as well as enjoying the city’s many offerings.

With numerous attractions, hiking and biking trails, delicious food, and vibrant culture, Irving promises a memorable experience for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at things to do, places to visit, and everything else that makes Irving such an exciting destination.

List of Places to Visit in Irving

To truly appreciate everything that Irving has to offer, it is essential to explore some of the top attractions in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Irving has a deep cultural history that is apparent in its many museums and historical sites. Here are some of the most popular cultural attractions in Irving:

  • Irving Arts Center: The center is home to several art galleries, studios, and theaters that showcase local and international artists’ works.
  • Irving Archives and Museum: The museum houses many historical exhibits dedicated to the city’s past.
  • Mustangs of Las Colinas Museum: This museum features works from artist Robert Glen, who created a sculpture collection of nine bronze mustangs in Cowboy Dressage, Irving.
  • Ruth Paine House Museum: This museum showcases the site where Lee Harvey Oswald stored his rifle before assassinating President John F Kennedy.

Natural Attractions of Irving: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Irving boasts several natural attractions that offer visitors the chance to explore nature’s beauty in the city. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Irving:

  • Bird’s Fort Trail Park: The park consists of hiking trails, playgrounds, and other outdoor facilities that visitors can enjoy.
  • Campión Trail: An ideal place for hiking and biking enthusiasts, the trail is one of the best places to explore the city’s natural beauty.
  • Elm Fork Nature Preserve: The preserve is home to several bird species, and visitors can enjoy nature hikes and bird watching.
  • Lake Carolyn: The lake offers many water activities, including kayaking, paddleboarding, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Irving

Irving offers an extensive network of trails perfect for hiking and biking. Here are some of the most popular trail routes in Irving:

  • Campion Trail: The trail is 22 miles long and passes through several scenic trails, including Elm Fork Trail and Trinity River Trail.
  • Northwest Recreation Center Trail: This 1.5-mile trail offers a scenic view of the city and has several outdoor amenities perfect for families.

Best Things to Do in Irving: Activities and Tours

In addition to Irving’s many attractions and hiking routes, there are also several activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do in Irving:

  • Toyota Music Factory: Located in the heart of Irving, the Music Factory offers music concerts, dining, and other live entertainment events.
  • Kayaking and Paddle Boarding on Lake Carolyn: Rent kayaks or paddleboards and explore the lake at your own pace.
  • Segway Tours: Discover Irving’s hidden gems in style with a segway tour.
  • Gondola Adventures: Enjoy a romantic ride along the Mandalay Canal in a Venetian-style gondola.

Week-Long Itinerary for Irving

If you’re planning to stay in Irving for a week, here is a suggested itinerary of the best things to do and see:

  • Day 1: Explore the Irving Arts Center and the Irving Archives and Museum.
  • Day 2: Bike on the Campion Trail and visit the Mustangs of Las Colinas Museum.
  • Day 3: Visit the Ruth Paine House Museum, and take a Segway tour.
  • Day 4: Hike North West Recreation Centre Trail followed by a visit to the Bird’s Fort Trail Park.
  • Day 5: Visit Elm Fork Nature Preserve and go kayaking or paddle boarding on Lake Carolyn.
  • Day 6: Enjoy a romantic gondola ride on Mandalay Canal and visit the local craft breweries and wineries.
  • Day 7: Relax and explore the city’s food scene.

Where to Stay in Irving: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Irving has plenty of accommodation options to choose from; it all depends on your preference and budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Irving:

  • Omni Mandalay Hotel at Las Colinas: A luxurious hotel with a beautiful scenic view of Lake Carolyn.
  • NYLO Las Colinas Hotel: A boutique hotel with a rooftop pool and lounge area.
  • Element Dallas Fort Worth Airport North: A spacious hotel with modern amenities perfect for families and business travelers.

What to Buy in Irving: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tradition and history are essential elements of Irving, and many local shops and boutiques sell traditional merchandise and local products that make perfect souvenirs. Some of the popular items that visitors can bring back include:

  • Texan spices and sauces
  • Handmade jewellery

Local Culture and Customs of Irving

Texas is known for its friendly and down-to-earth people, and Irving is no different. Visitors should take a cue from the locals and embrace the city’s laid-back personality.

Best Time to Visit Irving

The best time to visit Irving is from March to May and from September to November. The temperature averages between the 60s to 70s, making it perfect for outdoor activities or exploring the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Irving

Do:

  • Try local food and drink, including barbecue and Texan wines.
  • Explore the numerous hiking trails and parks.
  • Visit art galleries and museums that showcase the city’s history.

Don’t:

  • Do not miss out on the opportunity to visit Irving’s many attractions and outdoor adventures.
  • Do not forget to be respectful of the culture and norms of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Irving

Irving is generally safe, but visitors should exercise caution and take the necessary safety precautions when exploring the city.

Health in Irving

Visitors to Irving should ensure they have proper health insurance before visiting. There are several medical facilities in Irving, including the Baylor Emergency Medical Centre and the Las Colinas Medical Center, should you need them.
