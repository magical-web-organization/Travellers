Introduction to Carrollton

Located in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex in the Prairies and Lakes region of Texas, Carrollton is a vibrant community that offers a bit of everything, from outdoor adventures and cultural attractions to great food and shopping. The city is home to many parks, including the Elm Fork Nature Preserve and Greenbelt Corridor Park, making it a great destination for those who love hiking and biking. Additionally, Carrollton boasts several museums and historical landmarks that showcase the city’s rich culture and history.

Here’s a comprehensive tour guide to help you plan your perfect trip to Carrollton, Texas.

List of Places to Visit in Carrollton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • A.W. Perry Homestead Museum - Visit this restored 197-acre site that was once the home of one of Carrollton’s earliest settlers. The museum features artifacts and exhibits that showcase the life of early settlers in the area.

  • Cavanaugh Flight Museum - This museum offers visitors a chance to see over 30 aircraft that range from World War I to modern times. Exhibits also include vintage cars and motorcycles.

Natural Attractions of Carrollton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elm Fork Nature Preserve - With 40-acres of woodland and prairie, this preserve offers visitors a chance to observe local wildlife such as deer, coyotes, and more than 200 species of birds. It’s the perfect destination for hiking, biking, and picnic.

  • Greenbelt Corridor Park - Providing over 12 miles of trails, this park features beautiful scenery of streams, ponds, and natural habitats of several animals and plant species. It’s popular with cyclists, walkers, joggers, and family picnic-goers.

Best Things to Do in Carrollton: Activities and Tours

  • Spa Castle - Take a rejuvenating spa day at Spa Castle, which features saunas, hot tubs, and pools that can transform one’s mood and energy level.

  • Kayak Excursions - Rent a kayak, and paddle through the Elm Fork Trinity River, and experience breathtaking natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carrollton

Day 1: Visit the A.W. Perry Homestead Museum and learn about the city’s early settlers.

Day 2: Explore the Elm Fork Nature Preserve and take a nature hike or picnic.

Day 3: Visit the Cavanaugh Flight Museum and see vintage airplanes, and cars.

Day 4: Go on a shopping spree at Carrollton Square Plaza, Downtown Carrollton, or Stoneridge Marketplace.

Day 5: Rent a bike and cycle the trails of Greenbelt Corridor.

Day 6: Enjoy a relaxing day at Spa Castle.

Day 7: Rent a kayak, and paddle through the Elm Fork Trinity River.

Food and Drink Scene of Carrollton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tex-Mex – Try delicious and authentic Tex-Mex dishes like fajitas or tacos at Chili’s, Casa Linda Mexican Cuisine, or El Paisa Cocina Mexicana.

  • Barbeque – Sample some delicious barbeque briskets and ribs at Slow Bones BBQ or Hard Eight BBQ.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Unwind with some local craft beer at any of the following breweries: 3 Nations Brewing Co, Noble Rey Brewing Company, or Switchback Brewing Company.

  • Wine - Wind down your day with a glass of wine at the Four Seasons Wine Room or any other wine bars in downtown Carrollton.

Where to Stay in Carrollton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Courtyard Dallas Carrollton Conference Center - Convenient location, comfortable and spacious rooms.

  • Rodeway Inn Carrollton - Affordable no-frills stay near downtown Carrollton.

What to Buy in Carrollton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carrollton Farmers Market - The Carrollton Farmers Market is the perfect place to buy organic fruits and vegetables and locally made crafts and souvenirs.

  • Dallas Galleria - Located in Dallas, the Galleria offers couture and exclusive items that are perfect for souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Carrollton

  • Being hospitable and welcoming hold great importance amongst locals. A simple “How you doin’?” and a smile will get you anywhere.

Best Time to Visit Carrollton

The best time to visit Carrollton is in spring or fall when the weather is cool and perfect for hiking and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carrollton

Do:

  • Enjoy the outdoor activities

  • Be polite and email companies and people while planning ahead of time. If you are looking for a job, introduce yourself and your skills in a professional email.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry a sunhat and sunscreen while spending time outside

Safety and Health

Safety in Carrollton

Carrollton is a very safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s advisable to stay alert and watch your belongings in crowded places.

Health in Carrollton

Take necessary precautions while visiting Carrollton as the summers can get hot and humid, so it’s essential to carry water bottles to stay hydrated.

Getting to Carrollton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carrollton

  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport - Just 18 miles away from Carrollton, the Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport is the closest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Carrollton

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - The station is located in Dallas.

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft run around the city.

How to Get Around in Carrollton

  • The best way to get around Carrollton is by renting a car.

  • Alternatively, buses and taxis are available too.

Where to Go Next from Carrollton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dallas - Located just 14 miles south of Carrollton, Dallas offers a range of exciting experiences like art galleries, museums, and entertainment venues.

  • Fort Worth - Fort Worth is located 30 miles west of Carrollton and is known for its cowboy culture and western heritage.

  • Grapevine - Grapevine is a charming town located 8 miles north of Carrollton and is home to several award-winning wineries and vineyards.

Conclusion

Carrollton offers something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to culture vultures. Plan your trip accordingly and have a fantastic time exploring all that the city has to offer.
