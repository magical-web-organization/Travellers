Introduction to Arlington

Located in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, in Prairies and Lakes, Texas, Arlington is a vibrant, bustling city that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for travelers. Known as the Entertainment Capital of Texas, Arlington is home to popular attractions like Six Flags Over Texas, Globe Life Park, and AT&T Stadium. Whether you’re a sports enthusiast, a history buff, or just looking for a fun-filled vacation, Arlington has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Arlington

Arlington is full of exciting attractions that cater to a wide variety of interests. Here are some top places to visit during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Arlington Museum of Art: showcasing the work of local and national artists.
  • International Bowling Museum: dedicated to the history of bowling.
  • Planetarium at University of Texas at Arlington: designed for children and adults alike, perfect for educational and recreational purposes.
  • Top O’Hill Terrace: a historic hotel and casino converted into a museum displaying artifacts from the location’s notorious past.

Natural Attractions of Arlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • River Legacy Parks: designated as one of the top 10 parks in Texas, River Legacy boasts over 8 miles of paved trails.
  • Levitt Pavilion for the Performing Arts: a huge outdoor performing arts venue with a scenic and natural setting.
  • Lake Arlington: offering a wide variety of recreational activities like fishing, boating, kayaking, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arlington

If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, there are several hiking and biking routes in Arlington that are worth exploring:

  • River Legacy Park Trail: 8.4 miles, flat and easy terrain, perfect for beginners.
  • Bowman Springs Park Trail: 3.3 miles, challenging terrain with scenic views of Lake Arlington.
  • Johnson Creek Park Trail: 1.3 miles, a loop trail with lake views and shaded areas.

Best Things to Do in Arlington: Activities and Tours

  • Six Flags Over Texas: one of the top amusement parks in the world featuring a variety of thrilling rides.
  • Globe Life Park: home to the Texas Rangers baseball team and perfect for sports enthusiasts.
  • AT&T Stadium: home to the Dallas Cowboys football team and a great attraction for football fans.
  • Arlington Highlands: a shopping and entertainment district that offers a variety of restaurants, shops, and cinemas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arlington

If you’re planning to visit for a week, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to Six Flags Over Texas.
  • Day 2: Spend the day exploring museums and historical places like the Arlington Museum of Art and the International Bowling Museum.
  • Day 3: Hike or bike along one of the trails in River Legacy Park.
  • Day 4: Visit the planetarium at the University of Texas at Arlington followed by a show at the Levitt Pavilion for the Performing Arts.
  • Day 5: Check out Lake Arlington and indulge in some water-based activities like fishing and kayaking.
  • Day 6: Take a tour of AT&T Stadium and then grab dinner at one of the many restaurants in Arlington Highlands.
  • Day 7: End your trip with a visit to Top O’Hill Terrace Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Arlington

Arlington’s food and drink scene offers a taste of everything from local favorites to international cuisines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chili Cheese Dogs: made with famous Texas-style chili served over grilled beef hot dogs.
  • Texas-style BBQ: slow-cooked meats like brisket and ribs smothered in flavorful sauces.
  • Fried Catfish: served with hushpuppies and tartar sauce, catfish is a classic Southern dish.

Local Beverages

  • Martin House Brewery: one of the best microbreweries in Texas, known for its innovative craft beers.
  • Sweet Iced Tea: a staple in the South, sweet iced tea is a refreshing beverage that pairs well with spicy foods.

Where to Stay in Arlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arlington offers a range of lodging options to fit any budget. Here are some popular choices:

  • Hilton Arlington: located near AT&T Stadium and Six Flags Over Texas, the Hilton Arlington offers comfortable accommodation and great amenities.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Arlington North 6 Flags Dr: a budget-friendly option that offers easy access to major attractions.
  • Sheraton Arlington Hotel: a luxury option with spacious rooms, a pool and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Arlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home? Here are some great options:

  • Texas-themed Apparel: T-shirts, hats, and other clothing that celebrates Texas pride are popular souvenirs.
  • Fossil Creek Honey: A gourmet honey with natural flavors.
  • Cowboys Merchandise: for fans of the Dallas Cowboys football team, a souvenir shop located at AT&T Stadium offers a wide variety of merchandise.

Local Culture and Customs of Arlington

As a part of Texas, Arlington has a rich cultural heritage that you can experience while visiting. Southern hospitality is a trademark of Texas, which means you can expect friendly locals and an inviting atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Arlington

The best time to visit Arlington is during the shoulder season between March and May or September and November, the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arlington

Here are some tips for making the most of your trip:

Do:

  • Do check the weather and dress appropriately.
  • Do visit some of the museums and historical places in Arlington.
  • Do sample Texas’s famous BBQ and other local cuisines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to stay hydrated during the hot weather.
  • Don’t leave valuable items in your car as in any big city, crime and break-ins can occur.
  • Don’t miss out on Six Flags Over Texas and other popular attractions in the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arlington

Arlington is a relatively safe city, but it is still important to take some precautions. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in dodgy areas.

Health in Arlington

Arlington has excellent medical facilities with trained and accredited professionals. Be sure to travel with valid insurance that covers you if you need medical attention.

Getting to Arlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arlington

Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport is the closest major airport and located just 15 miles from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Arlington

The Greyhound bus station is located at 916 E Division St, and offers frequent services to major destinations in the region.

Other Options

Rideshare companies like Uber and Lyft are popular in Arlington.

How to Get Around in Arlington

Arlington is a car-centric city, and renting a car is the most convenient way to get around. Taxis and ridesharing companies are also available.

Where to Go Next from Arlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore nearby destinations, here are some suggestions:

  • Fort Worth: a bustling city that offers a range of cultural attractions and activities, located just 15 miles west of Arlington.
  • Dallas: the cosmopolitan city is located about 18 miles east of Arlington, offering vibrant nightlife and cultural attractions.
  • Waco: it is 99 miles from Arlington, home to the famous Magnolia Silos

Conclusion

Arlington, Texas is a great place to visit for those seeking entertainment, outdoor adventure, and unique cultural experiences. With so many attractions, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences to explore, Arlington offers an unforgettable vacation destination.
