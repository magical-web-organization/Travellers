A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Driftwood, Texas

Nestled in the heart of Central Hill Country in Texas, Driftwood is a small-town gem that boasts natural beauty, rich culture, delicious food, and a laid-back way of life that will leave you refreshed and rejuvenated. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, a history buff, a foodie, or simply looking for a relaxing escape, Driftwood has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you on a virtual tour of Driftwood, highlighting its top attractions, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Driftwood

Driftwood is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. Here are some of the top places to visit in this charming town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dr. Pound Historical Farmstead: This historic farmstead offers a glimpse into the rich agricultural history of the Texas Hill Country. Visitors can tour the farmhouse, barn, and outbuildings, and learn about the daily lives of early pioneers and farmers.

  • Salt Lick BBQ: This world-famous BBQ restaurant has been a local institution since the 1960s. The restaurant features an outdoor seating area with live music and serves up mouth-watering brisket, ribs, and sausage.

  • Wimberley Glassworks: This artisan glass studio and gallery features a wide range of handcrafted glass artwork, including chandeliers, vases, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Driftwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hamilton Pool Preserve: This natural pool is a popular spot for swimming, hiking, and picnicking. The pool is surrounded by a 50-foot limestone cliff and features a breathtaking waterfall.

  • Salt Lick Cellars: This beautiful winery is surrounded by rolling vineyards and offers tastings of the winery’s award-winning wines. Visitors can also enjoy live music and take a guided tour of the winery.

  • Onion Creek Kitchens at Juniper Hills Farm: This farm-to-table restaurant features a menu that highlights local ingredients and flavors. The restaurant is located on a working farm and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Driftwood

Driftwood is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails that showcase the area’s natural beauty. Here are some top routes to explore.

  • Pedernales Falls State Park: This scenic state park features over 10 miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through the rugged canyons and rolling hills of the Hill Country. Visitors can also go swimming, fishing, and birdwatching.

  • Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center: This beautiful botanical garden and preserve features over 650 species of native Texas plants and offers several hiking trails that highlight the area’s flora and fauna.

  • Violet Crown Trail: This 30-mile trail system stretches from downtown Austin to the Barton Creek Greenbelt and offers stunning views of the Austin skyline and surrounding hill country.

Best Things to Do in Driftwood: Activities and Tours

Driftwood offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in this charming town.

  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through the Hill Country and experience the area’s natural beauty up close.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Explore the area’s many wineries and vineyards on a guided tasting tour, and sample some of the region’s finest Texas wines.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take to the skies and enjoy a breathtaking view of the Hill Country from above.

To help you make the most of your visit to Driftwood, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that showcases the town’s top attractions and experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Driftwood

Driftwood is home to some of the best food and drink the Hill Country has to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample.

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ Brisket: Slow-smoked over pecan wood, this BBQ classic is a Texas staple.

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This savory dish features a tenderized steak coated in flour and spices and fried until golden brown.

  • Pecan Pie: Made with locally grown pecans, this sweet and nutty pie is a Driftwood favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Driftwood Estate Winery: This local winery produces some of the finest wines in the Hill Country, including award-winning Tempranillo and Syrah.

  • Twisted X Brewing Co.: This craft brewery specializes in handcrafted beers made with locally sourced ingredients, including their popular Cow Creek Lager.

  • Deep Eddy Vodka: This Austin-based distillery produces a wide range of flavored vodkas, including Ruby Red Grapefruit and Lemon.

Where to Stay in Driftwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Driftwood offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks for where to stay in town.

  • Solstice Driftwood: This luxury bed and breakfast offers stunning views of the Hill Country and features a heated outdoor pool, hot tub, and fire pit.

  • Camp Lucy: This upscale resort features luxury guesthouses and villas, a beautiful event center, and a signature restaurant.

  • Rustic Retreats: This cozy vacation rental offers a peaceful retreat surrounded by nature, with private access to the Blanco River.

What to Buy in Driftwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Driftwood is known for its unique artisans and locally made products. Here are some items to pick up on your visit.

  • Handcrafted Glassware: Wimberley Glassworks produces some of the most beautiful and unique glassware in the Hill Country.

  • Local Honey: Driftwood Honey Company produces pure, raw honey sourced from local beekeepers.

  • Handmade Soaps and Lotions: Driftwood Soap Company creates luscious soaps and lotions made from all-natural, organic ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Driftwood

Driftwood is a relaxed and friendly town that values its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Visitors should be respectful of these values and the local people.

Best Time to Visit Driftwood

The best time to visit Driftwood is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Driftwood

Visitors to Driftwood should keep in mind these do’s and don’ts to make the most of their visit.

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty and wildlife in the area.

  • Try the local BBQ and wine. They are some of the best in the country.

  • Follow trail rules and regulations and stay on designated paths.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.

  • Damage or disturb plants, animals, or wildlife habitats.

  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Driftwood is a safe and healthy town, but visitors should follow basic safety and health precautions while in the area.

Safety in Driftwood

  • Always wear appropriate shoes and clothing while hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

  • Watch out for wildlife, such as snakes and spiders, while walking on trails or in natural areas.

  • Avoid walking or hiking alone, especially in remote or unfamiliar areas.

Health in Driftwood

  • Stay hydrated while hiking or participating in outdoor activities, especially during hot weather.

  • Protect yourself from the sun by wearing sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses.

  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites and tick bites.

Getting to Driftwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Driftwood is easily accessible by car and is located approximately 30 minutes from Austin-Bergstrom International Airport.

Airports Near Driftwood

  • Austin-Bergstrom International Airport

  • San Antonio International Airport

  • Houston George Bush Intercontinental Airport

Bus Stations Near Driftwood

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Austin

  • Greyhound Bus Station, San Antonio

Other Options

  • Ride-Sharing Services: Uber and Lyft are available in the area.

  • Car Rentals: Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis offer car rentals in the area.

How to Get Around in Driftwood

The best way to get around Driftwood is by car, as many of the attractions and activities are spread out. Visitors can also take guided tours or rent bikes to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Driftwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Driftwood is located in the heart of the Texas Hill Country, which offers numerous nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some top picks.

  • Austin: The vibrant capital city of Texas is just a short drive from Driftwood and is known for its live music, food scene, and outdoor recreation opportunities.

  • San Antonio: Home to the Alamo, the River Walk, and numerous historic sites, San Antonio is a must-visit destination for history buffs.

  • Fredericksburg: This charming town is located in the heart of Texas wine country and features numerous wineries, antique shops, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking natural beauty, rich history, delicious food, or a relaxing escape, Driftwood, Texas, provides the perfect backdrop for your Hill Country adventure. With this comprehensive guide, you are equipped with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to this charming town.
