Discovering Georgetown: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Located in the heart of Central Hill Country, Georgetown is a charming city in Texas known for its rich history and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a foodie, Georgetown has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and more in Georgetown.

List of Places to Visit in Georgetown

Georgetown is home to a variety of cultural and natural attractions, each offering a unique glimpse into the city’s rich heritage and stunning landscapes.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Williamson Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look at the history of Williamson County. With its extensive collection of artifacts, photographs, and exhibits, the Williamson Museum provides visitors a glimpse into the county’s past.
  2. Georgetown Art Center: This art center is a haven for art lovers. With its rotating exhibits featuring both local and national artists, the Georgetown Art Center is the perfect place to experience contemporary art.
  3. Inner Space Cavern: This geological wonder is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Georgetown. Visitors can explore the cave’s underground tunnels and caverns, marvel at its impressive stalagmites and stalactites, and learn about the cave’s history and geology.

Natural Attractions of Georgetown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. San Gabriel River: Known for its crystal-clear waters and scenic views, the San Gabriel River is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, kayaking, and hiking. The river is home to a variety of wildlife, including river otters, fish, and birds.
  2. Lake Georgetown: This man-made lake is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and swimming. The lake’s tranquil waters and scenic views make it a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  3. Blue Hole: This natural swimming hole is a hidden gem in Georgetown. With its crystal-clear water and pristine surroundings, Blue Hole is the perfect spot for a refreshing dip on a hot summer day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Georgetown

Georgetown is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its numerous hiking trails and biking routes offering a glimpse into the city’s stunning natural beauty.

  1. Good Water Loop Trail: This 26-mile hiking and biking trail is an excellent option for outdoor enthusiasts. The trail winds through dense forests and scenic landscapes and offers beautiful views of the San Gabriel River.
  2. San Gabriel Park Trail: This easy, 2.5-mile trail is perfect for beginner hikers and bikers. The trail offers stunning views of the San Gabriel River and is famous for its springtime wildflowers.
  3. Lake Georgetown Trail: This 25-mile trail circles around Lake Georgetown and offers breathtaking views of the lake’s scenic vistas and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Georgetown: Activities and Tours

From historical tours to outdoor adventures, Georgetown is home to a plethora of exciting activities and tours.

  1. Historic Downtown Georgetown Walking Tour: This tour takes visitors on a journey back in time, exploring the city’s beautiful historic downtown district. Visitors can admire the architecture of the city’s iconic buildings and learn about the city’s rich history.
  2. Georgetown Winery Tour: This tour takes visitors on a tasting journey of the region’s finest wines. Visitors can sample a variety of wines and learn about the region’s unique winemaking history.
  3. San Gabriel River Adventure: This outdoor adventure tour takes visitors on a kayaking journey of the San Gabriel River. Visitors can explore the region’s scenic landscapes, spot wildlife, and enjoy a refreshing dip in the river’s crystal-clear waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Georgetown

Georgetown’s food and drink scene is a mix of old and new, with its diverse culinary offerings and local specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pie 95 Pizzeria: This iconic pizzeria is famous for its wood-fired pizzas, made with fresh ingredients and cooked to perfection.
  2. Rentsch Brewery & Tap Room: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including its famous Rentschler Lager. Visitors can enjoy a pint of beer, play a game of pool, or enjoy a round of cornhole.
  3. Dos Salsas: This Tex-Mex restaurant serves up some of the best tacos, fajitas, and enchiladas in town. Visitors can enjoy a cold margarita, savor the spicy camarones al chipotle, and enjoy the lively atmosphere.

Local Beverages

  1. Sweet Lemon Inn & Kitchen: This charming cafe serves up some of the best coffee, tea, and baked goods in Georgetown. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing cup of tea, savor a buttery croissant, and soak in the cozy atmosphere.
  2. Georgetown Winery: This local winery offers a variety of fine wines, including its popular Tempranillo and Viognier. Visitors can enjoy a wine-tasting, learn about the region’s unique winemaking history, and purchase a bottle of wine to take home.

Where to Stay in Georgetown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Georgetown has a range of accommodation options to fit all budgets and preferences.

  1. Sheraton Austin Georgetown Texas Hotel & Conference Center: This luxurious hotel offers spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and a full-service spa. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing stay, complete with a delicious breakfast buffet and excellent customer service.
  2. The Olive Tree Court: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Georgetown’s historic district. Visitors can enjoy a cozy stay in a beautifully restored 1930s bungalow, complete with a private courtyard and modern amenities.
  3. Georgetown RV Resort: This family-friendly resort offers RV parking spaces, tent sites, and cabins. Visitors can enjoy a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, a playground, and a campfire area.

What to Buy in Georgetown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors who want to bring home a piece of Georgetown, here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  1. Georgetown Baking Co.: This local bakery offers a variety of freshly baked goods, including its popular pecan pralines and cinnamon rolls. Visitors can purchase a box of treats to take home or enjoy a cup of coffee in the cozy cafe.
  2. Georgetown Winery: This local winery offers a variety of fine wines, perfect for gifting or drinking. Visitors can purchase a bottle of wine, learn about the region’s unique winemaking history, and take home a piece of Georgetown.
  3. Austin Rocks Texas: This gift shop specializes in Texas-themed souvenirs, including t-shirts, mugs, and accessories. Visitors can purchase a unique gift for family and friends or a token of their visit to Georgetown.

Local Culture and Customs of Georgetown

Georgetown is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should take time to explore its colorful history and unique traditions, including:

  • Red Poppy Festival: Held every April, this festival celebrates Georgetown’s official flower, the red poppy. Visitors can enjoy live music, food, and drinks, and a parade featuring the red poppy.
  • Georgetown Art & Wine Trail: This annual event features a showcase of local artists and wineries in Georgetown. Visitors can enjoy a wine-tasting, explore the art exhibits, and learn about the region’s cultural offerings.

Best Time to Visit Georgetown

The best time to visit Georgetown is in the fall and spring seasons, when the weather is mild, and the outdoor scenery is at its best. Additionally, visitors may want to plan their travel around the annual events, such as the Red Poppy Festival or the Georgetown Art & Wine Trail.

Do’s and Don’ts of Georgetown

Do:

  • Take time to explore the city’s historic downtown district
  • Visit the San Gabriel River and Blue Hole for a refreshing dip on a hot summer day
  • Enjoy a wine-tasting at Georgetown Winery or Rentsch Brewery & Tap Room

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen, hat, and comfortable shoes for outdoor activities
  • Fail to try the local Tex-Mex cuisine or wood-fired pizza
  • Leave without taking a tour of Inner Space Cavern or Williamson Museum.

Safety and Health

Safety in Georgetown

Georgetown is a safe city, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, avoid leaving valuable belongings in unattended vehicles, and trust their instincts.

Health in Georgetown

Georgetown has a range of medical facilities and hospitals for any medical emergencies. Visitors should also pack sunscreen, bug spray, and any prescription medication they may need during their trip.

Getting to Georgetown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Georgetown is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Georgetown

  • Austin–Bergstrom International Airport (AUS): Located 35 miles south of Georgetown, this airport serves multiple airlines with domestic and international flights.
  • Killeen-Fort Hood Regional Airport (GRK): Located 50 miles northwest of Georgetown, this airport serves domestic airlines and some low-cost carriers.

Bus Stations Near Georgetown

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Austin, this station offers multiple routes to Georgetown and other nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available at both Austin–Bergstrom International Airport and Killeen-Fort Hood Regional Airport.
  • Georgetown is also easily accessible by car, with major highways connecting to the city from all directions.

How to Get Around in Georgetown

The best way to get around Georgetown is by car or bike. Visitors can find car rental services at the airport or in town. Alternatively, visitors can rent bikes at San Gabriel Park Trailhead and explore the city’s scenic trails and routes.

Where to Go Next from Georgetown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Georgetown is located in the heart of Hill Country, offering visitors plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Some popular nearby excursions include:

  • Fredericksburg: Located 65 miles west of Georgetown, Fredericksburg is a charming town known for its German heritage, wineries, and scenic vistas.
  • Waco: Located 80 miles north of Georgetown, Waco is a bustling city known for its vibrant culture, landmarks, and outdoor activities.
  • San Antonio: Located 130 miles south of Georgetown, San Antonio is a historic city famous for its tourist attractions, including the Alamo and the River Walk.

Conclusion

Georgetown is a hidden gem in Texas, offering visitors a perfect blend of history, nature, and culture. From hiking and biking to wine-tasting and historical tours, Georgetown has something for every traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their visit to Georgetown and explore the city’s unique offerings to the fullest.
