Introduction to Round Rock

Located in the beautiful Central Hill Country of Texas, Round Rock is a thriving city that blends history, art, and nature. The city’s name comes from a large “round rock” that marks the low-water crossing for wagons and cattle.

Round Rock has grown from a small community to a bustling city with a variety of attractions for visitors of all ages. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a culture-lover, or a foodie, this city has something to offer. Read on to learn about the best places to visit and things to do in Round Rock.

List of Places to Visit in Round Rock

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Williamson Museum

The Williamson Museum showcases the history and culture of Williamson County. Visitors can explore exhibits on early settlers, Native Americans, cattle ranching, and more. Admission is free, and the museum is open Tuesday-Saturday.

2. Old Settlers Park

Old Settlers Park is a 645-acre park that includes a lake, a playground, sports fields, and picnic areas. Visitors can also explore a historic village that includes a log cabin, a schoolhouse, and a blacksmith shop.

Natural Attractions of Round Rock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Brushy Creek Trail

The Brushy Creek Trail is a 6-mile paved trail that winds through the heart of Round Rock. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and jogging. At several points along the trail, visitors can stop to enjoy the scenery and view the creek.

2. Lake Travis

Lake Travis is a beautiful reservoir that offers boating, fishing, swimming, and other water activities. Visitors can also enjoy hiking and picnicking in the parks that surround the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Round Rock

1. Cedar Rock Railroad Trail

The Cedar Rock Railroad Trail is a 1.7-mile trail that winds through a scenic area of Round Rock. This trail is perfect for hiking and biking, and visitors can stop to take in the views of Brushy Creek.

2. Round Rock West Greenbelt Trail

The Round Rock West Greenbelt Trail is a 2.5-mile trail that offers a mix of paved and unpaved paths. This trail is great for hiking and mountain biking, and visitors can enjoy views of the creek and the surrounding native plants and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Round Rock: Activities and Tours

1. Urban Air Trampoline and Adventure Park

Urban Air Trampoline and Adventure Park is a fun-filled attraction for visitors of all ages. With trampolines, zip lines, climbing walls, and a ropes course, there are plenty of activities to keep you busy for hours.

2. Round Rock Express Baseball

The Round Rock Express is a minor league baseball team that plays in the Pacific Coast League. Visitors can enjoy a game at the Dell Diamond Stadium, where the team plays home games.

Food and Drink Scene of Round Rock

Must-Try Dishes

1. Texas Barbecue

Round Rock is home to several excellent barbecue restaurants, including The Salt Lick and Louie Mueller Barbecue. Visitors can enjoy brisket, ribs, pulled pork, and other meaty delights.

2. Tex-Mex

Tex-Mex cuisine is a blend of Mexican and Texan flavors and ingredients. Visitors can enjoy dishes like tacos, enchiladas, and fajitas at local restaurants such as Dos Salsas and Chuy’s.

Local Beverages

1. Craft Beer

Round Rock is home to several craft breweries, including Bluebonnet Beer Company and Flix Brewhouse. Visitors can enjoy a variety of beer styles, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

2. Wine

Texas has a thriving wine industry, and Round Rock is within easy reach of several excellent wineries. Visitors can enjoy tastings and tours at spots like Georgetown Winery and Flat Creek Estate.

Where to Stay in Round Rock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Hilton Garden Inn Round Rock

The Hilton Garden Inn Round Rock is a modern hotel with comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities, including an outdoor pool and fitness center. The hotel is conveniently located near the Round Rock Premium Outlets and other local attractions.

2. Austin Marriott North

The Austin Marriott North is a stylish hotel with luxurious rooms and a variety of on-site dining options. The hotel is within easy reach of Round Rock and other nearby cities.

What to Buy in Round Rock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Round Rock Honey

Round Rock Honey is a local producer of pure, raw honey. Visitors can purchase jars of honey at the Round Rock Farmers Market or online.

2. Vintage Finds

Round Rock is home to several antique shops and vintage stores, where visitors can find unique furniture, clothing, and other treasures.

Local Culture and Customs of Round Rock

Round Rock is a friendly and welcoming city, where visitors can enjoy a mix of Texan and southern hospitality. Visitors should be prepared for hot weather and should respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Round Rock

The best time to visit Round Rock is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Round Rock

Do:

  • Try the local barbecue and Tex-Mex cuisine.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Round Rock’s parks and trails.
  • Attend a Round Rock Express baseball game.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage local attractions.
  • Wear offensive clothing or use offensive language in public.
  • Try to engage with wildlife or stray animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Round Rock

Round Rock is a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as locking car doors and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Round Rock

Round Rock has several hospitals and medical facilities, including St. David’s Round Rock Medical Center and Baylor Scott & White Medical Center.

Getting to Round Rock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Round Rock

The Austin-Bergstrom International Airport is about 30 minutes from Round Rock.

Bus Stations Near Round Rock

Greyhound and Megabus both have stations in Austin, about 30 minutes from Round Rock.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service to Round Rock.

How to Get Around in Round Rock

Round Rock has a variety of transportation options, including rental cars, taxis, and ride-sharing services. Visitors can also use the local bus system, CapMetro, to get around the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Round Rock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Austin

The vibrant city of Austin is just a short drive from Round Rock, offering world-class museums, music venues, and restaurants.

2. San Antonio

San Antonio is home to the iconic Alamo and other historic sites, as well as lively neighborhoods and excellent culinary options.

Conclusion

With its mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine, Round Rock is a great destination for visitors to Texas. From hiking and biking to shopping and dining, there is something for everyone in this charming city.
