Introduction to Stonewall

Located in the Central Hill Country region of Texas, Stonewall is a charming small town with rich historical and cultural significance. Despite its small size, the town has a lot to offer to visitors, from natural attractions to culturally important landmarks. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at Stonewall and everything it has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Stonewall

When planning your trip to Stonewall, there are many places worth visiting. Here are some of the top attractions to consider:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lyndon B. Johnson State Park & Historic Site: A historical site dedicated to showcasing the legacy of the 36th President of the United States, Lyndon B. Johnson, and his wife, Lady Bird Johnson.

  • The National Museum of the Pacific War: A museum dedicated to the Pacific theater of World War II, featuring exhibits, artifacts, and educational programs.

  • Grape Creek Vineyards: A winery offering tastings, tours, and events on a beautiful Hill Country property.

Natural Attractions of Stonewall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pedernales Falls State Park: A state park featuring a stunning waterfall, hiking trails, and activities such as swimming, fishing, and camping.

  • Enchanted Rock State Natural Area: A preserved natural area that features a massive pink granite dome, hiking trails, and stargazing events.

  • LBJ Ranch: A working cattle ranch that includes a visitors’ center, a self-guided driving tour, and the Texas White House, where President Lyndon B. Johnson conducted official business.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stonewall

Stonewall has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the Texas Hill Country. Some recommended routes are:

  • The Pedernales Falls Trail: A 7.5-mile trail that leads to the beautiful Pedernales Falls, with lots of wildlife to see along the way.

  • The Willow City Loop: A 13-mile route that takes you through beautiful countryside and past wildflowers in the spring.

  • The Enchanted Rock Loop Trail: A 4-mile trail that offers stunning views of the natural area’s iconic pink granite dome.

Best Things to Do in Stonewall: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Stonewall also offers visitors a variety of other activities and tours to choose from. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Touring the local wineries, such as Grape Creek Vineyards and Becker Vineyards, for tastings and events.

  • Participating in wildflower tours during the spring, where you can see fields of Texas bluebonnets and other native flowers in bloom.

  • Taking a scenic drive through the Hill Country, with stops for picnics, shopping, and exploring small towns like Fredericksburg and Johnson City.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stonewall

If you’re planning to spend a week in Stonewall, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Lyndon B. Johnson State Park & Historic Site and the National Museum of the Pacific War

Day 2: Explore Enchanted Rock State Natural Area and Pedernales Falls State Park

Day 3: Take a wildflower tour and visit local wineries

Day 4: Visit the LBJ Ranch and take a driving tour of the Hill Country

Day 5: Hike the Willow City Loop Trail and visit more wineries

Day 6: Hike the Enchanted Rock Loop Trail and enjoy the sunset over the Hill Country

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene, or take a day trip to nearby Fredericksburg or Johnson City.

Food and Drink Scene of Stonewall

Stonewall is famous for its authentic Texas cuisine and local drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ brisket: Slow-cooked to perfection, this classic Texan dish is a must-try in Stonewall.

  • German sausage: A nod to the town’s German heritage, the sausage is juicy and flavorful and can be found at local eateries.

  • Tex-Mex cuisine: Enjoy classic Tex-Mex dishes such as fajitas, tacos, and queso dip during your visit.

Local Beverages

  • Texas wines: Stonewall boasts numerous vineyards and wineries that produce some of the best wines in Texas.

  • Craft beer: Local breweries and taprooms offer a variety of crafted beers made locally.

  • Whiskey and bourbon: Several distilleries around Stonewall offer tours and tastings of locally made whiskey and bourbon.

Where to Stay in Stonewall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stonewall offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Hoffman Haus: A quaint bed and breakfast located in the heart of Stonewall, boasting beautiful gardens and comfortable rooms.

  • Cotton Gin Village: A historic property that offers individual cottages with full amenities and rustic decor.

  • The Vineyard Trail Cottages: A collection of cozy cottages surrounded by vineyards and secluded for privacy.

What to Buy in Stonewall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Stonewall, don’t forget to bring home some souvenirs and local products. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Texas wine: Bring home a bottle of wine from one of the many local vineyards as a souvenir or a gift.

  • Local jams and jellies: Many small businesses in the area produce delicious jams, jellies, and preserves made with local fruits.

  • Handcrafted wooden items: Local craftsmen in Stonewall produce handmade wooden kitchenware and furniture that are both beautiful and practical.

Local Culture and Customs of Stonewall

Stonewall is a charming town that warmly welcomes visitors. The local culture is deeply rooted in Texas history, and many locals celebrate their German heritage with food and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Stonewall

The best time to visit Stonewall is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and wildflowers and autumn foliage are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stonewall

To make the most of your visit in Stonewall, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do visit the local wineries and breweries and try the local food and drinks.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling in the gorgeous Texas Hill Country.

  • Do take the time to explore the cultural and historical landmarks such as Lyndon B. Johnson State Park and Historic Site and the National Museum of the Pacific War.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural areas when hiking or exploring the beautiful outdoors.

  • Don’t forget to bring and drink plenty of water during outdoor activities, as the Texas sun can be scorching.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stonewall

Overall, Stonewall is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take precautions when hiking or exploring the parks and natural areas to ensure their safety.

Health in Stonewall

Visitors to Stonewall should take precautions against the Texas heat, especially during the summer months. It’s important to stay hydrated and to wear sunscreen and a hat to prevent sunburn.

Getting to Stonewall: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stonewall is located approximately 70 miles north of San Antonio and 85 miles west of Austin. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Stonewall

The closest international airports are San Antonio International Airport (SAT) and Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (AUS).

Bus Stations Near Stonewall

Greyhound Bus Lines and Megabus both have stops in nearby Fredericksburg, roughly 10 miles from Stonewall.

Other Options

Renting a car is a common option for visitors to Stonewall, as it provides the most flexibility and convenience for exploring the Hill Country. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available in surrounding cities.

How to Get Around in Stonewall

The best way to get around Stonewall is by car or bicycle. Many attractions and landmarks are spread out across town and the Texas Hill Country, so having a personal vehicle is highly recommended.

Where to Go Next from Stonewall: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time after exploring Stonewall, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Fredericksburg: A charming German town located nearby, offering more local shopping, dining, and outdoor adventures.

  • Johnson City: The birthplace of President Lyndon B. Johnson and home to several attractions, including the Sauer-Beckmann Living History Farm.

  • Hill Country Wine Trail: Follow the Texas Wine Trail through several Hill Country towns, including Stonewall, Fredericksburg, and Johnson City, to taste local wines and enjoy beautiful countryside.

Conclusion

Stonewall is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With plenty of outdoor activities, local wineries and breweries, and charming accommodations, it’s a must-see destination for anyone looking to explore the best of the Texas Hill Country.
