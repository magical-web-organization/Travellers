Introduction to Northwest

Located in the heart of Hill Country, Austin, Texas, Northwest offers a colorful mix of outdoor adventures, historic landmarks, and cultural experiences. This vibrant neighborhood captures the essence of Austin by a mix of live musical performances, museum exhibitions, and scenic natural vistas. Join us on a journey through Northwest Austin as we explore all the must-visit places, exciting hiking trails, food and drink spots, nearby attractions, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest

Whether you are looking for a culturally rich experience or outdoor adventures, Northwest has something to offer everyone. Check out some of the top destinations in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Texas Military Forces Museum: This museum is the perfect place for history buffs, especially military history enthusiasts. The museum exhibits the history of the Texas National Guard and Texas Military Forces from the mid-19th century to the present.

  • The Contemporary Austin – Laguna Gloria: This art museum features contemporary art exhibitions and installations that showcase a mix of local and international artists. The stunning Mediterranean-style villa in the heart of 14-acre gardens provides the perfect backdrop for contemporary exhibits.

  • The Bullock Texas State History Museum: The museum offers a diverse range of exhibits and artifacts on Texas history, culture, and people. The museum also screens a variety of films and documentaries on Texas history in the popular Texas Spirit Theater.

Natural Attractions of Northwest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Bonnell: Standing at 775 ft, Mount Bonnell, also known as “Covert Park,” offers panoramic views of Austin, including Lake Austin. The park also provides a peaceful trail to hike and a picnic area. A popular spot for sunrise or sunset viewing.

  • Wild Basin Wilderness Preserve: This nature reserve features several hiking trails, scenic views, and diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the Creekside, the Hill Country Trail, or the Fern Trail. The preserve is located just 10 minutes from Downtown Austin, and a beautiful respite from urban life.

  • Mayfield Park and Nature Preserve: This stunning park is home to a historic cottage and 23 acres of picturesque gardens. Visitors can relax and admire the natural beauty of the area or explore the wildlife found in the park’s nature preserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest

Northwest offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors of all levels. Here are a few of the most popular routes to explore.

  • River Place Nature Trail: This 5-mile trail offers a rigorous workout and is ideal for fitness enthusiasts. The hike provides varied terrain, including steep inclines, rocky trails, and outstanding views of the hill country.

  • Bull Creek Greenbelt: The Bull Creek Trail is an ideal location for quick hikes and nature walks. Visitors can observe the local wildlife while viewing the falls.

  • Balcones Canyonlands Preserve: With over 30 miles of trails and stunning natural vistas, Balcones Canyonlands Preserve offers a range of hiking and biking trails for visitors of all levels. Be sure to explore the birding areas in the preserve.

Best Things to Do in Northwest: Activities and Tours

Northwest offers a multitude of activities and tours for visitors. Here are a few of the best places and things to do in the area

  • Music Venues: Catch a live music performance at some of the best music venues in Austin, including the famous Antone’s Nightclub, Continental Club, and The Mohawk.

  • Austin Zoo: Austin Zoo is not only a zoo but also an animal sanctuary that is ideal for animal lovers. Visitors can observe over hundreds of animals, from lions and tigers to exotic birds and reptiles.

  • Austin Nature and Science Center: This science museum offers interactive exhibits and programs that are educational and fun-filled for all ages. Visitors can explore the Dino Pit, a living butterfly exhibit, and a range of hands-on science exhibits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northwest

Day 1: Visit the historic Bullock Texas State History Museum and enjoy a film at the Texas Spirit Theater.

Day 2: Explore Wild Basin Wilderness Preserve and take in stunning views of the creek and hill country.

Day 3: Head to Mount Bonnell and catch a sunrise or sunset; hiking & picnicking.

Day 4: Visit Laguna Gloria estate, museum and natural areas.

Day 5: Take a hike in Balcones Canyonlands Preserve or enjoy a leisurely ATX food tour of the district.

Day 6: Explore local natural attractions, such as Austin Zoo or Mayfield Park.

Day 7: Take a bike ride through the city and end your day with a live music performance at Antone’s Nightclub.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest

Northwest Austin offers a diversity of options for food and drink that are sure to satisfy every taste bud craving. Here are some of the amazing cuisines and beverages you can find in Northwest.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: Texas is renowned for its barbeque, and the city of Austin is no exception. Visit Cooper’s Old Time Pit, Terry Black’s Barbecue, or Stiles Switch BBQ for some of Texas’ best brisket and ribs.

  • Tex-Mex: With a wide range of Tex-Mex options in Northwest, it can be difficult to pick just one dish. Try the famous breakfast tacos at Torchy’s Tacos or the fajitas at Sway.

  • Food Trucks: Austin’s food trucks are a must-try for any foodie. Head to The Picnic, a popular food truck park, or explore individual eateries such as Dee Dee Southeast Asian or Micklethwait Craft Meats.

Local Beverages

  • Breweries: Austin is known for its local breweries, with Northwest playing host to several. Try the beer flight at 4th Tap Brewing Cooperative or Adelbert’s Brewery.

  • Wineries: Texas Hill Country is known for its wine, with several wineries located within a short drive of Northwest. Check out Lake Travis Wine Trader for exceptional views and upscale wine tasting.

Where to Stay in Northwest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwest Austin offers several accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly to luxury. Here are a few places to stay.

  • Holiday Inn Express and Suites Austin NW Austin – Arboretum Area: This hotel offers a mix of affordable and comfortable rooms, as well as easy access to restaurants and bars nearby.

  • Fairmont Austin: For those looking for luxury accommodations, the Fairmont Austin is unbeatable. The hotel is ideally located near several restaurants, shops, and theaters and has incredible views of downtown Austin.

What to Buy in Northwest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art: Visit the shops and galleries located around Northwest to find authentic Texan art and handmade crafts. The Blue Genie Art Bazaar and Austin Art Garage offer an eclectic mix of locally crafted art.

  • Cowboy Hats and Boots: Indulge in Texas culture and style with a genuine cowboy hat or boots. Head to Cavender’s Boot City or Allen’s Boots for the perfect pair.

  • Texan Pecans: Austin is known for its love for pecans, which are grown locally and sold throughout the city at several shops and farmer’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest

Austin’s laid-back and friendly culture can be seen throughout Northwest in its music, food, and local markets. The city’s love for music is celebrated through a multitude of live performances and events across the area.

Best Time to Visit Northwest

Northwest, Austin is enjoyable all year long. The spring months of March to May bring pleasant weather and a lot of outdoor activities to indulge in. The summer months of June to August are hot, but perfect to enjoy water sports and RV camping. Fall arrives in September to November, offering cooler weather, fall foliage, and perfect trails for hiking and biking. Winter months from December to February are chilly, but the city illuminates with Christmas festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest

Do:

  • Participate in the local food scene, with hundreds of amazing restaurants, food trucks, bars, and local breweries.

  • Visit historical landmarks and museums that showcase the rich culture and history of Texas.

  • Take a hike on the diverse and scenic trails around the area. Take safety precautions for a secure experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and wear protective gear while exploring outdoor activities and hiking trails.

  • Don’t hesitate to ask locals for suggestions on where to find the best food and live music performances.

  • Don’t litter on trails or parks and respect the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwest

Northwest, Austin is generally a safe area, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings at all times and take necessary safety precautions while hiking or cycling. Always keep your belongings within sight and maintain a complete situational awareness.

Health in Northwest

Visitors can contact local hospitals or clinics in case they need medical assistance during their visit. Contact the nearest hospital or clinic to you in case of emergency.

Getting to Northwest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northwest

  • Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (ABIA): Located 16 miles southeast of Northwest Austin. Accessible via public transportation, rental cars, and ride-sharing services.

Bus Stations Near Northwest

  • Capital Metro Rail and Bus: They offer convenient service to Northwest Austin with a few routes, including routes 383, 392, and 990. The nearest station is Lakeline Station or Kramer Station.

Other Options

  • Visitors can rent cars or use ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Northwest

Visitors can explore Northwest Austin by using the Capital Metro Rail and Bus service or access the shared bike program, BCycle. They offer electric scooters available for rent in the downtown area. The area is bike-friendly with numerous bike lanes and bike-share services.

Where to Go Next from Northwest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located within a short drive of Northwest, here are some attractive destinations to explore.

  • Lake Travis: Head over to this beautiful lake that spans over 18,000 acres for water sports and boat rides.

  • Driftwood: Take a trip to Driftwood, known for its numerous wineries and vineyards.

  • San Antonio: Visit the bustling city of San Antonio, home to famous attractions such as the Alamo and River Walk.

Conclusion

Northwest, Austin is a vibrant neighborhood that offers a mix of cultural, natural, and outdoor experiences that are unique to Texan culture. The area is the ideal destination for those who love art, music, food, and the great outdoors. A visit to Northwest Austin is an unforgettable experience that visitors will cherish for years to come.
