Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Austin is a vibrant, bustling area that is home to many historic landmarks, live music venues, museums, and more. The area is known for its vibrant nightlife, local food scene, and natural beauty. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures or exploring local culture, there is plenty to see and do in Downtown Austin. In this travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know to have an enjoyable trip to Downtown Austin.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Here are the must-visit destinations in Downtown Austin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Texas State Capitol - This historic building is an iconic landmark in Austin and a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Bullock Texas State History Museum - This museum offers an interactive look at the history of Texas.
  • The Contemporary Austin - This museum showcases modern and contemporary art from around the world.
  • Harry Ransom Center - This research library and museum houses one of the largest cultural collections in the United States.
  • LBJ Presidential Library - This library pays tribute to the life of former US President Lyndon B. Johnson.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lady Bird Lake - This scenic lake is perfect for outdoor activities such as kayaking, stand-up paddleboarding, and hiking.
  • Ann and Roy Butler Hike and Bike Trail - This 10-mile trail loops around Lady Bird Lake and offers stunning views of the city skyline.
  • Zilker Park - This scenic park features a botanical garden, a nature preserve, and hiking trails.
  • Mount Bonnell - This popular hiking spot offers breathtaking views of Austin and the surrounding area.
  • Barton Springs Pool - This spring-fed pool is a popular spot for swimming and relaxing in the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Downtown Austin:

  • Ann and Roy Butler Hike and Bike Trail - This 10-mile trail loops around Lady Bird Lake and offers stunning views of the city skyline.
  • Shoal Creek Greenbelt - This trail is perfect for hikers and bikers looking to escape the bustle of the city and connect with nature.
  • Wildflower Center Trail - This trail winds through the Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center and offers an up-close look at Texas’ native plant life.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

Here are the best things to do and see in Downtown Austin:

  • Catch a concert at the Moody Theater - This iconic music venue is home to Austin City Limits Live and features live music shows almost every night of the week.
  • Visit the Texas State Capitol - Take a tour of the historic building and learn about the history of the state.
  • Explore the music scene on Sixth Street - This vibrant street is lined with live music venues and a unique blend of bars and restaurants.
  • Take a dip in Barton Springs Pool - This spring-fed pool is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.
  • Experience the Congress Avenue Bridge Bat Colony - Every evening at sunset, over a million bats take flight from the Congress Avenue Bridge.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Downtown Austin is known for its vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Franklin Barbecue - This legendary barbecue joint is known for its slow-cooked meats, including brisket and pulled pork.
  • Breakfast Tacos - Start your day with a breakfast taco from one of Austin’s many food trucks.
  • Tex-Mex - Try classic Mexican dishes with a Texas twist, such as queso, nachos, and tacos.
  • Gourdough’s Big. Fat. Donuts. - Indulge in one of their epic, Instagram-worthy donuts.
  • For vegans, try burgers from Arlo Burgers

Local Beverages

  • Topo Chico - This Mexican sparkling water is a staple in Texas and can be found at almost every restaurant and bar in Austin.
  • Craft Beer - Austin is known for its craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering a wide variety of unique brews.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Austin offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Saint Cecilia - This laid-back luxury hotel features uniquely designed rooms and suites, a pool, and a bar.
  • The Driskill - This grand historic hotel offers a classic Austin experience, with elegant rooms and multiple dining options.
  • Native Hostels - This hip hostel offers private rooms and dorms, as well as a bar and restaurant.
  • Firehouse Hostel - This historic firehouse has been converted into a quirky hostel with private rooms and dorms.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Boots - Shop for custom handmade boots at legendary stores like Allen’s Boots.
  • Local Art - Austin is known for its thriving art scene, with many local artists selling their work in galleries and markets.
  • Hot Sauce - Spice up your meals with a bottle of locally made hot sauce from one of Austin’s many hot sauce shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Austin is known for its laid-back, friendly culture. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Tip generously - In Austin, it’s customary to tip at least 20% for good service.
  • Embrace the weird - Austin is known for its quirky, offbeat culture, so don’t be afraid to embrace the weirdness and try something new.
  • Dress comfortably - Austin’s warm climate means that casual attire is the norm, even when dining at upscale establishments.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown Austin is between March and May or September and November, when the weather is mild and there are fewer tourists. However, if you’re a music lover, the best time to visit is during Austin’s many music festivals, such as SXSW and Austin City Limits Music Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Downtown Austin:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the historic landmarks and museums.
  • Explore the live music scene.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the Texas heat - be sure to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Leave valuables in your car - break-ins are not uncommon in downtown Austin.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Austin is generally a safe area, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas.
  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.

Health in Downtown

Downtown Austin has many medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. If you require medical attention, head to one of these facilities or contact emergency services.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

  • Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (AUS)

Bus Stations Near Downtown

  • Greyhound Bus Station

Other Options

  • Ride-Sharing Services - Uber and Lyft both operate in Austin.
  • Car Rental - Many car rental companies have offices at the airport.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Public Transit

Downtown Austin has a comprehensive public transportation system, including buses, light rail, and commuter trains.

Ride-Sharing Services

Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in Austin.

Walking and Biking

Downtown Austin is a highly walkable and bikeable area, with many bike lanes and pedestrian-friendly streets.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Downtown Austin:

  • Hill Country Wineries - Take a day trip to one of the many wineries in the surrounding Hill Country region.
  • Hamilton Pool Preserve - Visit this natural swimming pool, located just outside of Austin, for a refreshing dip.
  • San Antonio - Explore the history and culture of this nearby city with a visit to the Alamo and River Walk.

Conclusion

Downtown Austin is a lively and exciting area with plenty of attractions, activities, and culinary experiences. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a music fan, Downtown Austin has something for everyone. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan the perfect trip to this vibrant Texas destination.
