Introduction to East

Located in Texas Hill Country, East is a hidden gem with spectacular natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. This region is famous for its breathtaking landscapes, impressive landmarks and ancient traditions. It’s a perfect getaway for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and those seeking a dose of history and culture. Here’s a complete guide to East, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and more.

List of Places to Visit in East

East is home to an abundance of culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and unique experiences. Here are some of the top places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lyndon B. Johnson Presidential Library and Museum: Located in nearby Austin, this museum features exhibits on the life and presidency of the 36th President of the United States.
  • Texas State Capitol: Another Austin landmark, this building showcases impressive architecture and informative exhibits on Texas history and government.
  • The Alamo: A historic mission from the 18th century, located in San Antonio and pivotal in the Texas Revolution.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseback Riding in the Hill Country: Explore the beautiful landscapes and vistas of the region on horseback.
  • Pedernales Falls State Park: With its cascading waterfalls, serene river beaches, and miles of hiking trails, this park offers something for everyone.
  • Enchanted Rock State Natural Area: Hike up this massive pink granite dome for stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East is a paradise for hikers and bikers with countless trails that stretch across the region. Here are some routes worth exploring:

  • Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center: Home to 650 species of native Texas plants, this center offers hiking trails and educational exhibits.
  • Colorado River Trail: A 10-mile trail that follows the Colorado River near Bastrop.
  • Guadalupe River Trail: A picturesque 9.2-mile trail along the Guadalupe River, complete with swimming holes and beautiful views.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, East has something to offer. Here are some of the best activities and tours you can experience while in the region:

  • Wine Tours: Take a tour of the numerous wineries in the region, including the world-renowned Wimberley Valley Winery.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take to the rivers of East for a day of paddling, including the picturesque San Marcos River.
  • Spa Days: Relax and unwind with a day of pampering at one of the many luxurious spas in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

Here is a recommended itinerary for a week-long trip to East:

Food and Drink Scene of East

East is home to a unique food and drink scene that blends Texan and Southern comfort with Western cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: East is famous for its authentic, slow-cooked barbecue. Try the brisket and ribs at the iconic Salt Lick BBQ.
  • Chicken Fried Steak: A Texas classic, chicken-fried steak is a breaded and fried piece of steak usually served with gravy, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Kolaches: A Czech pastry filled with sweet or savory ingredients, kolaches are a popular snack in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Shiner Bock: A popular Texas beer brewed in Shiner, TX.
  • Whiskey: Dripping Springs Distilling and Garrison Brothers Distillery are both located in the region, offering tours and tastings.
  • Mexican Martinis: A signature cocktail in Austin, made with tequila, lime juice, and olive juice, and served in a martini glass.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort, a cozy bed and breakfast, or a rustic cabin in the woods, you’ll find it here. Some great options include:

  • Cypress Creek Cottages: Located a few miles from Wimberley, these cottages offer privacy and seclusion surrounded by nature.
  • Hill Country RV Resort & Cottage Rentals: Ideal for families or those traveling with pets, this resort offers cozy cabins, RV rentals, and numerous amenities.
  • Travaasa Austin Wellness Resort: A luxury resort that offers spa treatments, fitness classes, and farm-to-table dining.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East is known for its unique crafts and products, many of which are handmade by locals. Here are some items to bring home as souvenirs:

  • Pottery: Wimberley Pottery creates handmade pottery using local materials, making a perfect souvenir from the region.
  • Leather Goods: In San Antonio, visit the Alamo Leather Goods Company to purchase handcrafted leather products like belts, wallets, and purses.
  • Sculptures: Discover unique sculptures by local artists at the Wimberley Glassworks studio and gallery.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East is a proud region, steeped in history and tradition. Some of the customs and cultural highlights of the region include:

  • Rodeos: Experience a true Texas rodeo at one of the many events held throughout the region, including the San Antonio Stock Show and Rodeo.
  • Festivals: Celebrate local culture at one of the region’s many festivals, including the Wimberley Arts Fest and the Texas Book Festival.
  • Music: Listen to live music at one of the many world-famous venues in Austin, such as the Continental Club or the Elephant Room.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is in the spring (April-June) or the fall (September-November), when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its most vibrant. Summers in Texas can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Do:

  • Attend a Rodeo: Get a glimpse of the region’s cowboy culture by attending a local rodeo event.
  • Explore the Outdoors: With numerous state parks, hiking trails, and river adventures, East is ideal for those who love the great outdoors.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: From barbecue to Tex-Mex to craft beer, East has a unique culinary scene that should not be missed.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on Private Property: Respect private property and do not trespass on land that is not open to the public.
  • Litter: Keep the region beautiful by disposing of trash properly and following the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Underestimate the Weather: Texas weather can be unpredictable, so be sure to check the forecast and prepare accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East is a relatively safe region, but visitors should take normal precautions such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Be aware of snakes and other wildlife on hiking trails and follow park regulations.

Health in East

Visitors should drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially in the hot summer months. Wear sunscreen and mosquito repellent to protect against the sun and insects.

How to Get Around in East

The best way to get around East is by car, as public transportation is limited in the region. Rental cars are readily available at the airports and other locations. Bikes and hiking trails are also a great way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East is located in close proximity to many other great destinations in Texas Hill Country. Here are some options for your next adventure:

  • Fredericksburg: Known for its German heritage and wine country, Fredericksburg is a charming town less than an hour from East.
  • San Antonio: One hour south of East, San Antonio offers a rich cultural experience, including the Alamo and the famous River Walk.
  • Marble Falls: Located only 30 minutes from East, Marble Falls is a peaceful community on the shores of Lake Marble Falls.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing retreat or an action-packed adventure, East offers something for everyone. From outdoor excursions and historic landmarks to unique cuisine and cultural experiences, this hidden gem in Texas Hill Country is a must-visit destination. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and wonder of East.
