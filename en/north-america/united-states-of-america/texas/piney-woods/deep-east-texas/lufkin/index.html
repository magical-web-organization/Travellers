Introduction to Lufkin

Lufkin is a small city located in Deep East Texas, nestled in the heart of the Piney Woods region, about 120 miles north of Houston. This charming city has a population of approximately 35,000 people and is known for its rich history, beautiful outdoor scenery, and vibrant art and culture scene.

Lufkin offers a great range of activities and experiences for visitors of any age. Whether you want to explore the great outdoors, discover fascinating museums, or savor the local cuisine, Lufkin has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lufkin

Here is a comprehensive list of places to visit when in Lufkin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ellen Trout Zoo

A visit to the Ellen Trout Zoo is a must when in Lufkin. It is home to over 100 species of animals, including tigers, giraffes, and sea lions, as well as a reptile house, bird aviary, and petting zoo.

Texas Forestry Museum

The Texas Forestry Museum is perfect for anyone interested in history. It features exhibits about the history of logging, sawmills, and papermaking in Texas. The museum includes interpretive displays, antique forestry equipment, and an outdoor exhibit area.

Museum of East Texas

The Museum of East Texas includes a diverse collection of exhibits. It showcases paintings, photos, and sculptures from various periods in Texas history.

Natural Attractions of Lufkin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Sam Rayburn

Lake Sam Rayburn is a great location for water enthusiasts. The lake offers boating, fishing, and swimming activities. Additionally, you can take a stroll or a bike ride along its scenic trails.

Davy Crockett National Forest

The Davy Crockett National Forest is located just outside Lufkin city. It is the perfect place for an outdoor adventure. The forest spans over 160,000 acres of land and includes hiking and biking trails, camping, fishing, and hunting.

Kiwanis Park

Kiwanis Park features trails, picnic areas, and pavilions for family outings. This park hosts annual events throughout the year, such as the East Texas Germanfest and The Texas Blueberry Festival.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lufkin

Here are some great hiking/biking routes to explore in Lufkin:

Ellen Trout Park Trails

The Ellen Trout Park trails are a great way to explore the natural beauty of Lufkin. The trails are perfect for hiking and mountain biking.

Pineywoods Purgatory

If you’re looking for something more challenging, Pineywoods Purgatory offers a difficult 50-mile bike ride. The route takes you through steep hills and beautiful forests.

Angelina National Forest Trail System

The Angelina National Forest Trail System offers over 130 miles of hiking and biking trails near Lufkin. The trails pass through picturesque landscapes that you will appreciate.

Best Things to Do in Lufkin: Activities and Tours

Lufkin offers many tours and activities to visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

Kayaking on the Angelina River

Kayaking on the Angelina River is a great way to explore the beauty of Lufkin’s natural surroundings. Angelina River Kayak Rentals offers rentals and guided tours for kayaking down the peaceful river.

Downtown Lufkin Food & Wine Tour

The Downtown Lufkin Food & Wine Tour is a perfect way to indulge in local cuisine and sample many Texas-based wines. It is a culinary tour that highlights the city’s food scene, and you can take a bite of delicious bites and enjoy a glass of wine.

Motorcycle Riding Tours

Plan a trip with East Texas Motorcycle Tours for an exciting adventure through the backroads of Lufkin. You can choose from different tour packages starting from a day’s ride, weekend tour, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lufkin

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Lufkin:

Day 1

Arrive in Lufkin, check-in to your hotel, and take a stroll around historic downtown Lufkin. Savor a delicious meal at a local eatery before returning to your hotel for the night.

Day 2

Start with exploring Ellen Trout Park trails in the morning, followed by lunch in the park. After lunch, head to the Davy Crockett National Forest for an afternoon hike and be sure to head back to the hotel for some well-deserved rest.

Day 3

Enjoy breakfast at a local café before beginning your day at the Texas Forestry Museum. Don’t forget to browse the gift shop before leaving the museum. Later, head to Lake Sam Rayburn for some fun in the water before having dinner at a lakeside restaurant.

Day 4

Start your day with a Downtown Lufkin Food & Wine Tour. After the tour, take a leisurely walk around the East Texas Artisans in the historic downtown area.

Day 5

Take a drive or go for a guided motorcycle tour to explore Lufkin’s beautiful surrounding areas, including Lufkin State Park.

Day 6

Plan for a picnic morning at Kiwanis Park followed by kayaking or tubing on the Angelina River. Spend evenings back in town exploring the local music scene in different venues.

Day 7

Enjoy breakfast before leaving Lufkin. If time permits, stop by some local shops to purchase a souvenir before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Lufkin

Now let’s take a look at Lufkin’s local food and drink scene:

Must-Try Dishes

Gator Platter at the Chilis Restaurant

If you want to try something different, head to Chilis Restaurant for a Gator Platter. This is a plate of deep-fried alligator bites served with fries, coleslaw, and sauce.

Chicken Fried Chicken at Cotton Patch

The Cotton Patch Cafe’s special Chicken Fried Chicken is always a hit. It’s a juicy chicken breast covered in crispy batter, served with mashed potatoes, green beans, and white gravy.

Barbeque Brisket at Brookshire Brothers

Brookshire Brothers is famous in the area for its delicious barbeque brisket. You can get it sandwiched in a bun, or have a platter with a side and a drink.

Local Beverages

Red 55 Winery

Red 55 Winery offers a vast array of Texas wines, including fruit wines made from local products. You should take a guided tour of the winery and attend their tasting sessions.

Stand Pipe Coffee House

The Stand Pipe Coffee House is a Lufkin staple and a perfect place to get your caffeine fix. Their coffee and sweets are locally sourced and delicately roasted.

Craft Beer at Standpipe Brewery

Standpipe Brewery offers a variety of locally brewed craft beers. They have a charming atmosphere, making it feel like the perfect neighborhood pub.

Where to Stay in Lufkin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lufkin has several options for accommodation, from luxurious hotels to comfortable guesthouses. Here are some of our recommendations:

Hotel Fredonia

Hotel Fredonia is a notable landmark that has been around since 1955. It features cozy, comfortable rooms with modern amenities and amenities like its serene patio and pool area.

Main Street House Bed and Breakfast

Main Street House Bed and Breakfast is a restored historic home that offers charming, luxurious accommodation. The property is quaint and feels like a home away from home, with its refined hospitality and a wholesome, homecooked breakfast.

Holiday Inn Express

Holiday Inn Express of Lufkin offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and is situated in a central location, providing guests easy access to major attractions in and around Lufkin.

What to Buy in Lufkin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Lufkin without picking up some unique souvenirs! Here are some of our suggested shops:

Simon & Company

Simon & Company is a lovely boutique that features a variety of artisanal candles, locally made jewelry, and handmade soaps.

Lufkin Farmers Market

If you’re looking for local produce, stop by the Lufkin Farmers Market. They feature fresh, locally derived fruit and vegetables besides homemade jams, jellies, and cheese.

Pineywoods Seafood

Bring back some of the Gulf Coast’s best seafood from Pineywoods Seafood, where you can find fresh seafood straight from the Gulf Coast like shrimp, crab, and various Gulf fish varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Lufkin

Lufkin has a rich mixture of multiple traditional cultures that it retains through the generations, primarily influenced by East and South Texas. Overall, visitors to the town are urged to be respectful of the local community.

Best Time to Visit Lufkin

The best time to visit Lufkin is between the months of March and May when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lufkin

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Lufkin by going for hikes and bike rides.
  • Do try the local food and drink scene.
  • Do support local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the locals.
  • Don’t walk alone in the night where there is less to zero visibility.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lufkin

Lufkin is known for its low crime rates compared to the national average, so visitors should feel secure when visiting the area. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as being cautious while exploring outdoor areas and not being out alone after dark.

Health in Lufkin

Lufkin has excellent medical care facilities. Visitors can visit Angelina County and Cities Health District for their medical assistance and guidance.

Getting to Lufkin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lufkin

The nearest airport to Lufkin is the Lufkin Angelina County Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lufkin

Greyhound Bus Station is the only bus station in Lufkin, making it easy to catch a bus from most major cities.

Other Options

Driving to Lufkin is the easiest way to reach, regardless of where you are coming from. Those with rental cars should navigate using the city maps or other navigational technologies to stress-free travel.

How to Get Around in Lufkin

The most comfortable and convenient way to get around Lufkin is by renting a car. However, if you prefer public transportation, the city has a network of taxis and rideshare services like Lyft and Uber.

Where to Go Next from Lufkin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lufkin is located in the heart of the Piney Woods, making it easy for you to explore neighboring areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

Nacogdoches

Nacogdoches is just a short drive from Lufkin, offering many museums, historic buildings, local shops, and cafes. The city’s well-preserved structures and landmarks offer a glimpse of its Louisiana-style architecture.

Blueberry Festival

The Texas Blueberry Festival Sealy-Ward in Nacogdoches is an annual event held in June, featuring a parade, live music, and blueberry-themed treats.

Conclusion

Lufkin is a gem in the Piney Woods of Texas, offering visitors a wide array of activities. From history to nature, to culinary experiences - Lufkin has a lot to offer. Plan your visit to this charming city today to discover everything it has to offer!
