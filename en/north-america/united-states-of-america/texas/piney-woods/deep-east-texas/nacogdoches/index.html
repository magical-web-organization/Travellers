Introduction to Nacogdoches

Nacogdoches is a small city located in the heart of Piney Woods in East Texas, known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and scenic beauty. As one of the oldest towns in Texas, it’s home to numerous museums, historical sites, and landmarks, making it an ideal destination for history buffs and culture lovers.

But it’s not just history that attracts visitors to Nacogdoches - the city is also home to several natural attractions, such as state parks, rivers, and lakes, offering outdoor enthusiasts opportunities for hiking, fishing, and camping. With its friendly locals, charming downtown, and vibrant cultural scene, Nacogdoches is a great place to experience the best of Texas.

List of Places to Visit in Nacogdoches

Here’s a list of places to visit in Nacogdoches, categorized by their respective attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stone Fort Museum: Located on the Stephen F. Austin State University campus, this museum showcases the history of Nacogdoches and East Texas and features artifacts and exhibits from the earliest Native American settlements to modern times.

  • Old University Building: Once the home of Nacogdoches University, the building now serves as a museum and offers guided tours that give visitors a glimpse into the life of students and professors in the 19th century.

  • Durst-Taylor Historic House and Gardens: This early 20th-century house-turned-museum is a beautiful example of East Texas architecture from a bygone era. The gardens feature indigenous plants and trees and make for a peaceful stroll.

  • Ted and Fay Shine Memorial Museum: Housed in a former bank building, this museum showcases the community’s history through photographs and other artifacts, including a rare collection of quilts.

Natural Attractions of Nacogdoches: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nacogdoches Azalea Trail: This self-guided tour takes visitors through the city’s historic neighborhoods, featuring colorful azaleas and dogwoods in bloom during the springtime.

  • Lake Nacogdoches: Located just outside the city limits, this lake is perfect for fishing, boating, and other water activities. With nearly 2300 acres of water and abundant wildlife, it’s a popular spot for locals and visitors alike.

  • Lanana Creek Trail: This scenic trail runs through the heart of Nacogdoches and along the banks of Lanana Creek, providing a peaceful escape and a chance to observe local flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nacogdoches

Nacogdoches offers numerous hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts, some of which are listed below:

  • The Sawmill Hike and Bike Trail: This 7-mile trail loops through the heart of the city and takes visitors past downtown landmarks, forests, and creeks.

  • Terrapin Tangle Trail at Stephen F. Austin State University: This trail is a favorite of students and locals alike and offers a chance for a quick hike or bike ride through the woods.

  • Etoile Trail and Campground: Located about 15 miles southwest of Nacogdoches, this trail takes visitors through dense forests and along the shores of Lake Sam Rayburn.

Best Things to Do in Nacogdoches: Activities and Tours

  • Stephen F. Austin State University Campus Tours: Take a guided tour of the beautiful campus of SFA, the largest employer in Nacogdoches and the alma mater of several notable figures.

  • Pilgrimage Tours: Each year, Nacogdoches hosts a spring tour of historic homes and gardens, showcasing the unique architecture and design of local homes.

  • Pecan and Wine Festival: Held each October, this festival celebrates two of East Texas’s favorite staples: pecans and wine. Enjoy live music, local food vendors, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nacogdoches

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Nacogdoches:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Nacogdoches, check into hotel, explore downtown, and visit the Stone Fort Museum
2 Take a guided tour of the SFA campus in the morning, hike the Sawmill Hike and Bike Trail in the afternoon
3 Visit the Old University Building and the Durst-Taylor Historic House in the morning, have lunch at a local restaurant, spend the afternoon fishing at Lake Nacogdoches
4 Take a morning stroll through the Lanana Creek Trail, have lunch at a local café, shop for souvenirs downtown, and attend a live performance at the Lamp-Lite Theatre in the evening
5 Visit the Ted and Fay Shine Memorial Museum in the morning, have a picnic lunch at the Lake Nacogdoches Park, take a scenic drive through the countryside in the afternoon
6 Take a day trip to nearby Davy Crockett National Forest, hike, and have a picnic lunch
7 Check out of hotel and depart from Nacogdoches

Food and Drink Scene of Nacogdoches

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A Southern classic, this dish is found at many local restaurants and features a tender cube steak breaded and fried until golden brown and served with gravy.

  • Barbecue: No visit to Texas is complete without indulging in some good ol’ fashion barbecue. Check out The Old Creamery in Nacogdoches for some traditional Texas-style barbecue.

  • Peanut Butter Ice Cream: A unique and delicious treat, this ice cream is made with locally grown peanuts and is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Loblolly Sweet Tea: A sweet and refreshing drink that’s perfect for a hot Texas day, this tea is a staple in many Nacogdoches restaurants and cafes.

  • Craft Beer: Nacogdoches has a handful of craft breweries, including Fredonia Brewery and Front Porch Distillery, offering visitors a chance to sample some local brews.

Where to Stay in Nacogdoches: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nacogdoches has several accommodation options that cater to a range of budgets and preferences. Some of the best options are:

  • The Fredonia Hotel & Convention Center: This newly-renovated downtown hotel features upscale rooms and suites, luxurious amenities, and on-site dining options.

  • Comfort Suites: This budget-friendly option is conveniently located near the university, offering comfortable rooms and amenities, including free hot breakfast and an outdoor pool.

  • Airbnb: For a more local experience, consider renting a private room or entire apartment through Airbnb, where you can stay with locals and learn more about the city.

What to Buy in Nacogdoches: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pecans: Nacogdoches is known as the “Pecan Capital of the World,” so it’s no surprise that pecans are a popular souvenir item. You can find pecan pies, candies, and other pecan products at local gift shops.

  • Handmade Crafts: The city is home to several talented artists and craftsmen, offering unique and handmade crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

  • Local Wine and Beer: Don’t leave Nacogdoches without trying some of the local wine and beer, either from a brewery or a winery located in the surrounding area.

Local Culture and Customs of Nacogdoches

Nacogdoches is a friendly and hospitable town, but it’s important to respect the local customs and culture. Here are a few tips:

  • Greet People: Texans are known for their hospitality, so don’t be surprised if locals greet you with a smile and a “Howdy!” Be sure to return the friendly gesture.

  • Respect the Outdoors: Nacogdoches is a nature lover’s paradise, but it’s important to respect the environment and leave no trace when visiting state parks and other natural areas.

  • Know the Dress Code: Texas is known for its relaxed dress code, but it’s still important to dress appropriately for certain occasions and events.

Best Time to Visit Nacogdoches

The best time to visit Nacogdoches is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is pleasant, and the outdoors are at their best. The springtime is especially ideal for witnessing the blooming azaleas and dogwoods during the Azalea Trail Tour.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nacogdoches

Do:

  • Do say “Howdy” to locals when you meet them.

  • Do take advantage of the state parks and natural areas around Nacogdoches.

  • Do try the local beer and wine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment when visiting natural areas.

  • Don’t wear inappropriate clothing to formal events or restaurants.

  • Don’t forget to try the local cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nacogdoches

Nacogdoches is a relatively safe city, but it’s still important to take precautions when traveling. Some tips include:

  • Stick to well-traveled routes when hiking or exploring state parks.

  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.

  • Don’t walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Nacogdoches

Nacogdoches has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, both public and private, serving the community. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and to know where to go in case of an emergency.

Getting to Nacogdoches: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nacogdoches

The nearest commercial airport to Nacogdoches is the Shreveport Regional Airport, located about 100 miles away. Other options include the George Bush Intercontinental Airport in Houston, which is about 150 miles away, or the Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport, which is about 200 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Nacogdoches

Greyhound operates a bus station in nearby Lufkin, which is about 20 miles away from Nacogdoches.

Other Options

Renting a car or driving your own is the most convenient way to get around Nacogdoches and the surrounding areas. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

How to Get Around in Nacogdoches

The best way to get around in Nacogdoches is by car or bike. Taxis and rideshare services are also available, but public transportation is not widely available. The city is relatively small and easy to navigate, but parking can be limited in downtown.

Where to Go Next from Nacogdoches: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nacogdoches is a great base from which to explore other nearby destinations in East Texas, such as:

  • Davy Crockett National Forest: Located about an hour away, this national forest offers miles of hiking trails, camping sites, and scenic drives.

  • Tyler: Known as the “Rose Capital of the World,” Tyler is about an hour and a half away from Nacogdoches and offers attractions such as the Tyler Rose Garden and the Caldwell Zoo.

  • Cherokee Trace Drive-Thru Safari: About an hour away from the city, this safari offers visitors a chance to see exotic animals up close and personal from their own vehicles.

Conclusion

Nacogdoches may be a small town, but it’s full of charm, history, and natural beauty that can keep travelers busy for days. From exploring the museums and historical sites to hiking the scenic trails and trying the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem tucked away in Piney Woods.
