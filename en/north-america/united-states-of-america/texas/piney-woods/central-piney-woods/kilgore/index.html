Introduction to Kilgore

Kilgore is a city located in Gregg and Rusk Counties in the Piney Woods region of Texas. It is known for its rich oil history, vibrant arts scene, and outdoor attractions. The city is home to Kilgore College, the Texas Shakespeare Festival, and the East Texas Oil Museum. Visitors will enjoy exploring Kilgore’s historical landmarks, natural parks, and various cultural activities.

List of Places to Visit in Kilgore

Here are some of the top places to visit in Kilgore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • East Texas Oil Museum: This museum showcases the history of oil drilling in East Texas with interactive exhibits, artifacts, and a replica oil derrick.
  • Anne Dean Turk Fine Arts Center: The center houses galleries, art studios, and classrooms, and is home to Kilgore College’s theater and music productions.
  • World’s Richest Acre Park: Located in the heart of downtown Kilgore, this park is a historical landmark that commemorates the city’s oil boom.

Natural Attractions of Kilgore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cotton Belt Depot Museum: This 19th-century train depot now serves as a museum with exhibits that detail the history of the railway industry and its impact on Kilgore.
  • Kilgore City Park: This park has a large playground, picnic areas, walking trails, a lake with fishing opportunities, and a seasonal swimming pool.
  • Meadowbrook Golf & Event Center: This golf course is surrounded by the beauty of East Texas and is perfect for both beginners and seasoned golfers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kilgore

Kilgore has several beautiful hiking and biking routes:

  • Martin Creek Lake State Park: This park has six miles of hiking trails that take you through tall trees and diverse habitats.
  • Lacy J Ranch Nature Preserve: This preserve has four miles of hiking and biking trails, ranging in difficulty from easy to challenging.
  • Gregg County Hike & Bike Trail: This trail stretches for six miles and winds through forests and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Kilgore: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Kilgore include:

  • Texas Shakespeare Festival: This festival runs during the summer months and showcases performances of Shakespearean plays in a beautiful outdoor setting.
  • Oilfield Triathlon: This annual event includes a 400-meter swim, a 12-mile bike ride, and a 5k run, all taking place in Kilgore.
  • Kilgore Rangerettes Showcase: This famous dance team hosts an annual showcase that features the latest dance routines and performances.The team also offers dance classes throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kilgore

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long visit to Kilgore:

Day Activity
1 Visit the East Texas Oil Museum and World’s Richest Acre Park.
2 Hike or bike the trails at Martin Creek Lake State Park.
3 Learn about the railway industry at the Cotton Belt Depot Museum.
4 Tee off at the Meadowbrook Golf & Event Center.
5 Attend a Texas Shakespeare Festival performance.
6 Watch the Kilgore Rangerettes perform or take a dance class.
7 Relax at Kilgore City Park and go swimming in the pool.

Food and Drink Scene of Kilgore

Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages available in Kilgore:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smoked brisket: Texas-style, slow-cooked brisket is a staple in Kilgore.
  • Fried catfish: Served with hush puppies and coleslaw, catfish is a favorite dish in many Southern states.
  • Pecan pie: Made with locally grown pecans, this dessert is both sweet and savory.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet tea: This is the classic Southern drink, and it’s easy to find in Kilgore.
  • Texas beer: There are many local breweries in Texas, and Kilgore has some great options.

Where to Stay in Kilgore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best options for accommodation in Kilgore:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Kilgore North: This hotel has an indoor pool, fitness center, and free breakfast.
  • Hampton Inn Kilgore: This hotel offers free breakfast and an outdoor pool.
  • Lodge at Tres Rios: This lodge has cabins and campsites that are perfect for those who prefer a more rustic experience.

What to Buy in Kilgore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Kilgore:

  • Oil field memorabilia: Kilgore’s history is intertwined with the oil industry, so look for souvenirs like oil rig models, antique oil cans, and other memorabilia.
  • Handmade crafts: Arts and crafts are an important part of Kilgore’s culture, so take home some handmade pottery or textiles.
  • Local wine: There are several wineries in the area that produce quality wines using Texas-grown grapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Kilgore

Kilgore is known for its friendly and welcoming community. People often refer to Kilgore as a “small town feel with big-city amenities.” Here are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Southern hospitality: Expect to be greeted with a warm welcome and friendly conversation.
  • Church culture: Religion plays an important role in many people’s lives in Kilgore.
  • Football: Like many cities in Texas, Kilgore is big on football.

Best Time to Visit Kilgore

The best time to visit Kilgore is during the spring (March - May) or fall (September - November) when the weather is mild. Summers in Kilgore can be hot and humid, and winters can be cool and wet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kilgore

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Kilgore:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Kilgore’s natural beauty is one of its biggest draws, so be sure to take advantage of the hiking trails and parks.
  • Learn about Kilgore’s history: The city’s oil history is fascinating and is a significant part of Kilgore’s identity.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep Kilgore clean and beautiful by properly disposing of trash and recycling.
  • Forget to be courteous: As in any city, be courteous to other visitors and residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kilgore

Kilgore is generally a safe city. Here are some tips to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Keep your car doors locked: as with any city, it’s important to keep your car doors locked and valuables out of sight.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings: Pay attention to your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Kilgore

There are several hospitals and health clinics in Kilgore, with East Texas Medical Center being the largest.

Getting to Kilgore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kilgore

The closest airport to Kilgore is East Texas Regional Airport, which is about 25 miles away. Other options include Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport and George Bush Intercontinental Airport in Houston, both around a three-hour drive from Kilgore.

Bus Stations Near Kilgore

Greyhound and Megabus both have stations in nearby cities like Longview and Tyler.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best way to get around Kilgore and explore nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Kilgore

Kilgore is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, renting a car is recommended for those who want more mobility to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Kilgore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Kilgore include:

  • Tyler: A city located about an hour away from Kilgore, known for its rose gardens and museums.
  • Caddo Lake State Park: A state park located about an hour and a half away that is perfect for fishing and boating.
  • Shreveport: A city located about an hour away from Kilgore that offers casinos, food, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Kilgore is a wonderful destination for those looking for a unique mix of history, adventure, and relaxation. Its natural beauty, interesting culture, and friendly people make it an excellent place to explore and experience. Whether you’re interested in hiking, museums, or delicious food, there is something for everyone in Kilgore.
