A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Tubac, Arizona

Nestled in the foothills of the Santa Rita Mountains, Tubac is a charming small town located in southern Arizona. With its rich culture, stunning natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, it’s no surprise that more and more travelers are discovering the magic of Tubac. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the many things to see and do in Tubac, including cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scenes, and much more. So pack your bags and join us on an unforgettable journey to Tubac, Arizona!

Introduction to Tubac

Tubac is a small town located in Santa Cruz County, Arizona, which is situated in the southern part of the state. It is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and stunning natural beauty. The town was founded more than 250 years ago by Spanish colonists, and it has been designated as a National Historic District due to its historical importance. Today, the town is a popular tourist destination, drawing visitors from all over the world who come to explore its charming streets, cultural attractions, and beautiful natural surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Tubac

If you’re planning a trip to Tubac, you’ll want to make sure that you don’t miss these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Tubac Presidio State Historic Park is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. The park features a reconstructed 18th-century Spanish presidio (fort) that includes a Visitor Center with exhibits and a gift shop.

The Tubac Center of the Arts is another popular cultural attraction, which features rotating art exhibits, workshops, and classes for both adults and children.

Natural Attractions of Tubac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tubac is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The town is situated in the Santa Cruz River Valley, which is surrounded by beautiful mountains and natural landscapes. You can explore the area by taking a hike or bike ride on one of the many trails that wind through the mountains and valleys.

Another popular outdoor activity is birdwatching. The area is home to many different species of birds, including hawks, eagles, and hummingbirds. There are also several birdwatching tours available that will take you to some of the best birdwatching spots in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tubac

Tubac is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several great trails in the area, including the Anza Trail, which traces the journey of Spanish explorer Juan Bautista de Anza as he traveled through the area in the late 1700s. The Tubac Golf Resort also offers several scenic hiking trails that wind through the nearby mountains.

Best Things to Do in Tubac: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other fun activities to do in Tubac. You can take a hot air balloon ride over the surrounding mountains and valleys, or explore the area on horseback. There are also several guided tours available, including tours of local vineyards, art galleries, and historical sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tubac

If you’re planning to spend a week in Tubac, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Tubac Presidio State Historic Park and the Tubac Center of the Arts.
  • Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride on the Anza Trail or one of the other popular trails in the area.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a hot air balloon ride over the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour of local vineyards and sample some of the region’s award-winning wines.
  • Day 5: Explore the town’s charming streets and art galleries.
  • Day 6: Take a horseback riding tour of the local area.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the town’s many spas and wellness centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Tubac

No trip to Tubac is complete without sampling some of the town’s delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chimichangas: A deep-fried burrito filled with meat, beans, and cheese.
  • Green chili stew: A hearty soup made with pork, green chili peppers, and potatoes.
  • Sopaipillas: A type of bread that is deep-fried and served with honey for dipping.

Local Beverages

  • Prickly Pear Margaritas: A refreshing cocktail made with tequila and prickly pear cactus juice.
  • Arizona Wine: The region is known for producing some of the best wines in the country, including Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, and Syrah.

Where to Stay in Tubac: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tubac offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Tubac Golf Resort and Spa: A beautiful resort that features a spa, golf course, and several restaurants.
  • The Tubac Secret Garden Inn: A charming bed and breakfast that is located in the heart of the town.
  • Los Arboles Hotel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean, comfortable rooms and a great location.

What to Buy in Tubac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tubac is known for its unique arts and crafts scene, and there are many great shops and galleries in the town where you can find one-of-a-kind souvenirs and gifts. Some popular items to look for include handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Tubac

Tubac is a multicultural town, with influences from Spanish, Mexican, and Native American cultures. Residents celebrate several festivals throughout the year, including the Anza Days Festival in October, which celebrates the arrival of Juan Bautista de Anza and his expedition, and the Tubac Festival of the Arts in February, which features more than 200 artists from around the country.

Best Time to Visit Tubac

The best time to visit Tubac is in the fall or winter, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. Spring is also a great time to visit, as the area comes to life with wildflowers and other blooms. Summer can be hot, but many travelers still visit during this time to take advantage of the area’s outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tubac

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, and be courteous to residents.
  • Take advantage of the town’s many outdoor activities and natural beauty.
  • Sample the delicious local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect historical sites or landmarks.
  • Disrupt the peace and quiet of local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tubac

Tubac is generally a safe town, but you should always take precautions when traveling in a new place. Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas. If you plan to hike, make sure to bring plenty of water and wear appropriate footwear and clothing.

Health in Tubac

Overall, Tubac is a healthy place to visit. The town has several medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics, should you need medical attention during your trip.

Getting to Tubac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tubac is located about 45 miles south of Tucson, Arizona, and is easily accessible by car. There are also several shuttle services that will transport you to and from the town.

Airports Near Tubac

The closest airport to Tubac is the Tucson International Airport, which is located approximately 40 miles north of the town.

Bus Stations Near Tubac

There are several bus stations located in Tucson, which offer service to and from Tubac.

Other Options

Many visitors to Tubac choose to rent a car, which allows for flexibility and easy transportation around the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Tubac

The best way to get around Tubac is by car. There are several rental car companies that operate in the area, and most attractions and amenities are located within a short driving distance.

Where to Go Next from Tubac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tubac is located in a beautiful part of Arizona, with many nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Some popular options include:

  • The town of Nogales, Arizona, which is located just a few miles south of Tubac, and is known for its charming streets and beautiful natural surroundings.
  • The Saguaro National Park, which is located about 30 miles north of Tubac, and features stunning desert landscapes and hiking trails.
  • The city of Tucson, which is located about 45 miles north of Tubac, and is known for its vibrant culture and arts scene.

Conclusion

Tubac is a hidden gem in southern Arizona, and it’s the perfect destination for travelers who are looking for a mix of history, culture, outdoor adventures, and relaxation. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all of the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Tubac. So pack your bags and get ready to explore the magic of this charming small town!
