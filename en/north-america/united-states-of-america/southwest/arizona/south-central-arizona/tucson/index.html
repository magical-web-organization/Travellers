Introduction to Tucson

Tucson is a vibrant city located in South Central Arizona, surrounded by the beautiful Sonoran Desert. It is a welcoming destination for tourists who are interested in outdoor adventures, historical museums, and authentic Southwest cuisine. With more than 300 days of sunshine per year, Tucson is the perfect place to visit all year round. In this guide, we’ll discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Tucson.

List of Places to Visit in Tucson

With a thriving art scene and a rich history, Tucson is a city that offers a variety of attractions for every type of tourist. Here is a list of the most popular places to visit in Tucson:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arizona-Sonora Desert Museum
  • Pima Air and Space Museum
  • Mission San Xavier del Bac
  • University of Arizona
  • Museum of the Horse Soldier

Natural Attractions of Tucson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saguaro National Park
  • Sabino Canyon Recreation Area
  • Mount Lemmon Scenic Byway
  • Kartchner Caverns State Park
  • Tucson Mountain Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tucson

Tucson is surrounded by mountains, and it offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in the world. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Tanque Verde Ridge Trail (Saguaro National Park)
  • Romero Canyon Trail (Catalina State Park)
  • Ventana Canyon Trail (Sabino Canyon Recreation Area)
  • Sweetwater Preserve
  • Fantasy Island Trail

Best Things to Do in Tucson: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and museum-hopping, there are many other fun and adventurous things to do in Tucson. Here are some of the best:

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides
  • Horseback Riding
  • Jeep Tours
  • Segway Tours
  • Astronomy Nights

Week-Long Itinerary for Tucson

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in Tucson:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Arizona-Sonora Desert Museum, Saguaro National Park, and the Pima Air and Space Museum
2 Hike or bike in Sabino Canyon Recreation Area or Catalina State Park
3 Take a hot air balloon ride and visit Mission San Xavier del Bac
4 Go horseback riding and explore the University of Arizona campus
5 Tour the Museum of the Horse Soldier and enjoy a jeep or Segway tour
6 Explore downtown Tucson and try some authentic Southwest cuisine
7 Relax and unwind by the hotel pool or take a day trip to a nearby attraction

Food and Drink Scene of Tucson

Tucson’s food and drink scene are a blend of traditional Mexican flavors and Southwestern cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sonoran-style hot dogs
  • Chimichangas
  • Carne asada tacos
  • Tamales
  • Chiles Rellenos

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer from local breweries, such as Borderlands Brewing Company and Sentinel Peak Brewing Company
  • Margaritas made with fresh-squeezed lime juice
  • Horchata, a sweet rice milk drink

What to Buy in Tucson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tucson has a variety of shops, markets, and stores where you can buy local products as souvenirs for yourself or to bring back home as gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Tucson:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics
  • Silver and turquoise jewelry
  • Mexican handcrafted art
  • Salsa and hot sauce
  • Local honey and spices

Local Culture and Customs of Tucson

Native American culture is an important aspect of Tucson’s history, and you’ll see it reflected in the art, cuisine, and architecture of the city. The cowboys and ranchers also played a significant role in Tucson’s development, and you’ll hear stories and see memorabilia from this era in the local museums and shops.

Best Time to Visit Tucson

Tucson has a warm and sunny climate all year round, but the best time to visit is between November and April, when the average temperature is in the mid-70s. If you want to avoid the crowds and save money on accommodations, consider visiting during the summer months when the temperature can exceed 100 degrees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tucson

Do:

  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors
  • Try the authentic Southwest cuisine, especially the Mexican food
  • Drive slowly and cautiously when approaching mountain roads
  • Visit the local museums and learn about Tucson’s history
  • Respect the local cultures and customs

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car when parking
  • Walk alone at night in deserted areas
  • Ignore the natural warnings and hazards in the desert
  • Litter or damage the local environment and attractions
  • Disrespect the local cultures and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Tucson

Tucson is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, like any urban area, it’s important to take some precautions to stay safe. Here are some safety tips:

  • Keep your valuables concealed and secure
  • Stay in well-lit areas at night
  • Use common sense and trust your instincts
  • Lock your car doors and windows
  • Be aware of your surrounding and report suspicious activity to the authorities

Health in Tucson

If you’re visiting Tucson from a different climate, it’s important to stay hydrated and protect your skin from the desert sun. Always carry water with you and drink it regularly, especially when spending time outdoors. If you have any medical concerns or emergencies, you can find medical facilities and doctors in Tucson.

Airports Near Tucson

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport (PHX)

Bus Stations Near Tucson

  • Greyhound Station (Tucson)
  • Tufesa Bus Lines (Tucson)

Other Options

  • Amtrak train from Los Angeles to Tucson
  • Renting a car and driving to Tucson

How to Get Around in Tucson

Tucson has a variety of transportation options, from public buses to bike rentals. Here are some options:

  • Sun Tran public transportation system
  • Walking around downtown Tucson
  • Biking on Tucson’s scenic trails
  • Car rentals
  • Taxi and ride-sharing services

Where to Go Next from Tucson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of South Central Arizona, here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Tucson:

  • Phoenix, Arizona
  • Sedona, Arizona
  • Grand Canyon National Park
  • Bisbee, Arizona
  • Tombstone, Arizona

Conclusion

Tucson is a city that offers a rich blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in hiking, museum-hopping, or trying out authentic Southwest cuisine, Tucson has something for everyone. Follow this Guide and Discover the best of South Central Arizona.
