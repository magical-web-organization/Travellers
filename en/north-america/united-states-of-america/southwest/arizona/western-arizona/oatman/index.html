Introduction to Oatman

Oatman is a small town located in Mohave County, Arizona, on historic Route 66. The town is notable for its intact Wild West history, picturesque scenery, and unique attractions. Oatman was founded in 1906, during the height of the gold rush, and was named after Olive Oatman, a young girl who was kidnapped by Native Americans and was later found alive and well in the area. Today, Oatman is a popular tourist destination, and its old-world charm attracts thousands of visitors every year.

List of Places to Visit in Oatman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oatman Hotel

The Oatman Hotel is a historic hotel that has been in operation since the early 1900s. Famous guests have included Clark Gable and Carole Lombard, who spent their honeymoon there in 1939. The hotel is also said to be haunted by past guests and staff.

The Oatman Jail

The Oatman Jail is a small building located in the center of town, which was used to hold prisoners in the early days of the town’s history. Today, it serves as a museum that displays artifacts and memorabilia of Oatman’s Wild West past.

Natural Attractions of Oatman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oatman Highway

The Oatman Highway is a scenic road that winds through the mountains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. The road is also known for its twists and turns, making it a popular choice for motorcycle enthusiasts.

Hualapai Mountain Park

Hualapai Mountain Park is located 20 miles southeast of Oatman, and offers an array of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and horseback riding. The park is also renowned for its scenic views and well-maintained trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oatman

Black Mountains Hiking Trail

The Black Mountains Hiking Trail is a 10-mile trail that offers a unique opportunity to experience the rugged desert terrain of Oatman’s surroundings. The trail is best suited for experienced hikers, as it can be challenging and rocky in places.

Best Things to Do in Oatman: Activities and Tours

Ghost Tours

The Ghost Tours of Oatman provide visitors with a glimpse into the town’s haunted history. The tours take visitors on a journey through the streets of Oatman, stopping at the haunted locations and telling the spooky stories that have earned the town its reputation as a paranormal hot spot.

Oatman Burro Tour

The Oatman Burro Tour is a popular choice for families and animal lovers. The tour takes visitors on a 30-minute ride through the town, led by friendly and well-behaved burros. Visitors can also purchase carrots to feed the burros along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Oatman

Must-Try Dishes

Oatman Fudge

The Oatman Fudge is a popular treat that tourists and locals alike cannot resist. The fudge is made fresh daily and comes in a variety of flavors, including traditional chocolate, peanut butter, and caramel.

Wild West Coffee

The Wild West Coffee is a specialty drink that is served in most of Oatman’s cafes and restaurants. The coffee is made with a blend of dark roasted beans and is served black with a side of whiskey.

Local Beverages

Hopi Blue Star Beer

Hopi Blue Star Beer is a locally brewed craft beer that is a popular choice among locals. The beer is made with all-natural ingredients, including Arizona-grown hops and barley.

Prickly Pear Margarita

The Prickly Pear Margarita is a sweet and tangy drink that is made with prickly pear cactus fruit. The drink is a refreshing choice on a hot day and pairs well with most local dishes.

Where to Stay in Oatman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oatman Hotel

The Oatman Hotel is a historic hotel that is considered the heart of the town. The hotel offers comfortable rooms in a charming setting and is only a short walk from all of Oatman’s attractions.

Burro Inn Motel

The Burro Inn Motel is a popular choice for budget-conscious travelers. The motel offers clean and comfortable rooms, a friendly staff, and easy access to all of Oatman’s attractions.

What to Buy in Oatman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Route 66 Memorabilia

Oatman is located on historic Route 66, and visitors can find an array of Route 66 memorabilia in the local shops. Items include T-shirts, hats, maps, and postcards.

Turquoise Jewelry

Turquoise Jewelry is a popular souvenir that is unique to the Southwest region of the U.S. Oatman’s local shops offer a variety of rings, bracelets, necklaces, and earrings in traditional Navajo designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Oatman

Oatman is a town steeped in Wild West history and culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s traditions and customs, including the preservation of its historic buildings and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Oatman

The best time to visit Oatman is between November and April when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summertime temperatures can be scorching, with temperatures often exceeding 100 degrees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oatman

Do:

  • Respect the town’s heritage and culture.
  • Take a burro tour.
  • Try the local fudge and coffee.

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize historic landmarks.
  • Feed the wild burros. They can become aggressive and dangerous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oatman

Oatman is generally a safe town to visit, but visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking car doors.

Health in Oatman

Visitors to Oatman should drink plenty of water and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun, particularly during the summer months when temperatures can be extreme.

Getting to Oatman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oatman

The nearest airport to Oatman is Laughlin/Bullhead International Airport, which is located 30 miles south of the town.

Bus Stations Near Oatman

Greyhound offers daily bus service to Kingman, Arizona, which is located approximately 30 miles west of Oatman. From Kingman, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Oatman.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to Oatman, as it is located on historic Route 66 and offers scenic views of the surrounding landscapes.

How to Get Around in Oatman

Oatman is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also take a burro tour or rent a bike from a local shop.

Where to Go Next from Oatman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grand Canyon National Park

Grand Canyon National Park is located two hours north of Oatman and is one of the most popular destinations in the U.S. The park is home to the famous Grand Canyon, which offers breathtaking views of the natural wonder.

Lake Havasu City

Lake Havasu City is located 50 miles south of Oatman and is known for its water sports and outdoor activities. Visitors can rent a boat or jet ski and spend a day on the lake or explore the area’s hiking trails.

Conclusion

Oatman is a unique destination that offers a glimpse into the Wild West history of the American Southwest. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and outdoor activities or explore the town’s historic landmarks and museums. With a wide range of attractions and activities, it’s easy to see why Oatman is a popular destination for tourists from around the world. So, pack your bags and head to Oatman, Arizona, for an unforgettable adventure!
