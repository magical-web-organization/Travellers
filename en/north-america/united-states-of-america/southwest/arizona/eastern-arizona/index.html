Introduction to Eastern Arizona

Eastern Arizona is a region that is rich in culture and history, as well as natural beauty. This part of the state is often overlooked by tourists, making it a hidden gem for those looking for a unique and authentic experience in Arizona.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Arizona

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa Malpais Archaeological Park and Museum

Casa Malpais is a prehistoric archaeological site with a preserved Great Kiva, a ceremonial room with a unique design. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of the Mogollon people who lived in the area over 1,000 years ago.

Fort Apache Historic Park

Fort Apache was established in the late 1800s and played a key role in the Indian Wars. Visitors can explore the historic buildings, museum, and see a reenactment of the Apache Wars.

Navajo Nation Museum

The Navajo Nation Museum in Window Rock showcases the art, history, and culture of the Navajo people. The museum also hosts events, traditional dances, and demonstrations of Navajo culture.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Arizona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Petrified Forest National Park

Petrified Forest National Park is known for its fossilized trees and unique rock formations. Visitors can hike through the park, see petroglyphs, and learn about the geology and natural history of the area.

Salt River Canyon Scenic Drive

The Salt River Canyon is known for its stunning views, rugged terrain, and twisting roads. Visitors can take a scenic drive, go whitewater rafting, or hike along the canyon.

White Mountains

The White Mountains are a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can hike, fish, camp, and go horseback riding in this scenic area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Arizona

Arizona Trail

The Arizona Trail is a 800-mile long trail that runs through Eastern Arizona and the rest of the state. The trail offers a variety of hiking and biking opportunities, including the Mogollon Rim section, which offers stunning views and challenging terrain.

Sunrise Park Resort

Sunrise Park Resort offers a variety of trails for beginners and experienced hikers alike. The resort offers guided tours, as well as options for mountain biking and skiing in the winter months.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Arizona: Activities and Tours

Mining Tours

Eastern Arizona is known for its mining history, and visitors can take tours of historic mines like the Copper Queen Mine in Bisbee or the Lavender Pit Mine in Globe.

Stargazing

Eastern Arizona has some of the clearest skies in the country, making it a great destination for stargazing. Visitors can take guided tours or enjoy the celestial view on their own.

Birdwatching

Eastern Arizona is home to a variety of bird species, including bald eagles, hawks, and owls. Birdwatching tours and guided hikes are available through local organizations.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Arizona

Must-Try Dishes

Eastern Arizona is home to a variety of local dishes, such as the Navajo taco, a style of taco with fry bread as the shell. Other must-try dishes include green chili stew, prickly pear cactus jelly, and cowboy coffee.

Local Beverages

Arizona is known for its craft beer scene, and Eastern Arizona has a variety of breweries to choose from. The region is also home to award-winning wineries that offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Eastern Arizona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

Eastern Arizona has a variety of hotel options, ranging from luxury resorts to budget-friendly options. Some popular choices include the Apache Gold Casino & Resort, the Inn at Sunrise Park, and the Grand Canyon Railway Hotel in Williams.

Hostels

Hostels are not as common in Eastern Arizona, but there are some options available. One popular choice is the Globetrotter Lodge in Holbrook, which offers affordable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

Guesthouses

Guesthouses are a popular choice for those looking for a more intimate and unique experience. Eastern Arizona has a variety of guesthouses and bed and breakfasts, such as the X Diamond Ranch in Greer or the Canyon Rose Suites in Winslow.

What to Buy in Eastern Arizona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eastern Arizona is known for its Native American crafts and jewelry. Visitors can find traditional woven baskets, pottery, and silver jewelry in shops and markets throughout the region. Other popular souvenirs include locally made hot sauce, prickly pear cactus syrup, and cowboy hats.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Arizona

Eastern Arizona has a rich history and culture, and visitors should be respectful of the traditions and customs of the region. The Navajo Nation, which occupies a large portion of Eastern Arizona, has its own language and unique cultural practices. Visitors should familiarize themselves with these customs and traditions before visiting.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Arizona

The best time to visit Eastern Arizona is from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer months can be hot and crowded, while winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Arizona

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do carry plenty of water and sunscreen while exploring the outdoors.
  • Do bring cash for small businesses and markets.
  • Do try the local cuisine and craft beer scene.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass on private land.
  • Don’t take artifacts or disturb historic sites.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in natural areas.
  • Don’t ignore warnings about dangerous wildlife, such as rattlesnakes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Arizona

Eastern Arizona is a safe region to visit, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and camping. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for flash floods, especially during the monsoon season.

Health in Eastern Arizona

Eastern Arizona has limited medical facilities, especially in rural areas. Visitors should bring any necessary medication with them and plan for emergencies. It is also important to drink plenty of water and avoid overexertion in the heat.

Getting to Eastern Arizona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Arizona

The closest major airport to Eastern Arizona is Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle or taxi to Eastern Arizona.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Arizona

Greyhound buses stop in major cities throughout Eastern Arizona, including Globe, Show Low, and Winslow.

Other Options

Driving is the most common way to get around Eastern Arizona. Visitors can also take a train on the historic Grand Canyon Railway from Williams to the South Rim of the Grand Canyon.

How to Get Around in Eastern Arizona

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Eastern Arizona, especially if visitors want to explore the outdoor attractions. There are also several shuttle companies that offer transportation between major cities.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Arizona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grand Canyon National Park

The Grand Canyon is one of the most iconic destinations in the United States, and it is located just a few hours from Eastern Arizona. Visitors can take a day trip to the South Rim or spend several days exploring the park.

Tucson

Tucson is a vibrant city with a rich cultural scene, outdoor activities, and delicious food. Visitors can explore the historic downtown area, hike in the nearby mountains, or visit the University of Arizona campus.

Sedona

Sedona is known for its stunning red rock formations and spiritual energy. Visitors can take a jeep tour, get a psychic reading, or simply enjoy the scenery.

Conclusion

Eastern Arizona has something for everyone, from outdoor adventure to cultural immersion. Plan your trip today and discover the hidden gems of this unique and unforgettable region of Arizona.
