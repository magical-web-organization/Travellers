Introduction to Bisbee

Tucked away in a remote corner of Eastern Arizona lies the charming and historic town of Bisbee. Surrounded by rolling hills and dramatic landscapes, this quaint mining town has a rich history and a vibrant culture that draws visitors from all over the world. Bisbee is not only a popular vacation destination but also a vibrant community with a thriving arts scene, unique shops, and welcoming locals. Whether you’re interested in exploring Bisbee’s rich mining heritage or seeking outdoor adventure, this guide has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Bisbee

Bisbee offers an array of attractions that will satisfy every traveler’s interest. Here are some of the top-rated things to do in Bisbee.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bisbee Mining and Historical Museum: Learn about Bisbee’s mining history and the town’s evolution over the years. Prepare to be amazed by the impressive exhibits and the captivating stories told by the experienced and passionate museum staff.
  • The Muheim Heritage House Museum: Step back in time and experience life in Bisbee at the turn of the 20th century by visiting this renovated Victorian-style house. The museum provides a glimpse of the day-to-day life of Bisbee’s early pioneers.
  • The Warren Ballpark: Take a tour of the world’s oldest ballpark that’s still in use today. The ballpark dates back to 1909 and has hosted some of baseball’s greatest players.

Natural Attractions of Bisbee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bisbee Lavender Pit: Hike to the rim of the Bisbee Lavender Pit, a former open-pit copper mine that is now a geological wonder and a popular spot for sightseeing.
  • Queen Mine Tour: Descend into the underground world of the Queen Mine on this guided tour. This immersive experience provides visitors with a glimpse of the tools and techniques used by miners in Bisbee’s past.
  • Chiricahua Mountains: Explore the picturesque Chiricahua Mountains that are just a short drive from Bisbee. This mountain range offers some of the best hiking trails, camping, and bird watching opportunities in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bisbee

Bisbee is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails that offer awe-inspiring views of the town’s rugged landscapes. Here are some popular trails that you can explore:

  • Mule Mountains Trail: This 4-mile trail starts near downtown Bisbee and leads to the top of the Mule Mountain ridge. The trail offers spectacular panoramic views of the town and surrounding valleys.
  • The Copper Queen Mine Trail: Explore this 3.2-mile trail that takes hikers through the heart of Bisbee’s mining past. The trail passes through old mining buildings, recuperation areas, and towering tailing piles.
  • The Arizona Trail: This 800-mile trail runs from the Arizona-Mexico border to the Arizona-Utah border. The trail offers diverse landscapes, from cactus-filled deserts to alpine forests, and it’s perfect for hikers and mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Bisbee: Activities and Tours

Bisbee provides plenty of opportunities for visitors to engage in various activities. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Bisbee:

  • Old Bisbee Ghost Tour: Take a spooky night tour of Bisbee’s old mining district. The tour is filled with ghost stories, local legends, and tales of supernatural sightings.
  • Brewery Gulch Pub Crawl: Explore Bisbee’s local beer scene by visiting some of the town’s most popular bars and breweries. Enjoy locally brewed beers and chat with other beer enthusiasts.
  • Bisbee Farmers Market: Visit Bisbee’s vibrant farmers market and shop for fresh local produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal products every Saturday from 8:00 am to noon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bisbee

To make the most of your trip to Bisbee, we suggest the following itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Bisbee

Bisbee has a diverse culinary scene that ranges from casual eateries to fine-dining experiences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Bisbee:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Bisbee Breakfast at Bisbee Breakfast Club: This hearty breakfast dish includes eggs, bacon, potatoes, and toast.
  • The Fries at Cafe Roka: Cafe Roka’s crispy fries are a local favorite and come with a creamy and spicy dipping sauce.
  • The Pizza at Pizzarama: This family-owned restaurant offers thin-crust, New-York-style pizza made with fresh local ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Queen’s Crown Brewery: This local brewery offers a wide variety of craft beers that are brewed on-site. Enjoy a pint or flight of beer in the brewery’s rustic taproom.
  • St. Elmo’s Bar: This quirky bar has been a fixture in Bisbee for over a hundred years and serves classic cocktails and cold beers. Enjoy a drink while chatting with locals and soaking in the ambiance.
  • Bisbee Coffee Company: This cozy coffee shop offers artisanal coffee drinks, pastries, and breakfast items. Relax on the outdoor patio and enjoy the perfect cup of coffee.

Where to Stay in Bisbee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bisbee offers an array of accommodations that cater to all types of travelers. For a unique and memorable experience, we recommend staying at one of the town’s historic bed and breakfasts:

  • The Letson Loft Hotel: This elegantly restored hotel offers luxurious rooms with modern amenities and breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.
  • The Inn at Castle Rock: This charming bed and breakfast is housed in an old mining building and offers cozy rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a hot tub.
  • The Courtyard Suites: Located in the heart of Old Bisbee, these boutique suites offer spacious rooms with antique decor and modern touches.

What to Buy in Bisbee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bisbee is home to a vibrant arts scene and offers plenty of unique and locally made items that make for great souvenirs and gifts. Here are some popular products to bring home:

  • Bisbee Olive Oil: This local olive oil is made by pressing olives grown in the sunny hills surrounding Bisbee. The oil’s unique flavor makes it a great addition to any kitchen.
  • Bisbee Soap and Sundry: This local shop offers handmade soaps, lotions, and other bath and body products in unique scents and packaging.
  • Bisbee Turquoise: Bisbee is known for its high-quality turquoise that’s mined from the local hills. Purchase a piece of turquoise jewelry or a raw stone to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Bisbee

Bisbee has a unique and welcoming culture that values creativity, community, and history. The town’s locals take pride in their heritage and are always eager to share their town’s unique story with visitors. It’s customary to greet locals with a friendly smile and engage in conversation. Be sure to honor Bisbee’s heritage by exploring the town’s rich mining history and participating in local events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Bisbee

Bisbee has mild weather year-round, making it an ideal vacation destination. The best time to visit Bisbee is during the fall or spring when the weather is pleasant and the town’s outdoor activities are in full-swing. Summer can be hot, but it’s still worth visiting if you can withstand the heat. Winter is a quiet season in Bisbee, but it’s still a great time to visit for a cozy and relaxing retreat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bisbee

Bisbee is a safe and welcoming town that attracts a lot of visitors each year. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions when visiting:

  • Always be aware of your surroundings
  • Keep valuables out of sight
  • Lock your car and hotel room when not in use
  • Stay on designated hiking and biking trails
  • Follow all safety guidelines during outdoor activities

Health in Bisbee

Bisbee is home to a medical center and a pharmacy, but it’s always best to bring any necessary medication and medical supplies with you. The town’s high altitude may affect visitors with respiratory or cardiovascular issues, so plan accordingly and consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Bisbee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bisbee is relatively remote, and getting there may require some planning and research. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Bisbee

  • Tucson International Airport: 90 miles from Bisbee
  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport: 180 miles from Bisbee

Bus Stations Near Bisbee

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Sierra Vista, AZ - 23 miles from Bisbee

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is the most convenient and flexible transportation option for exploring Bisbee and the surrounding areas.
  • Shuttle Service: Several shuttle services offer transportation from the airports to Bisbee. Be sure to research and book in advance.

How to Get Around in Bisbee

Bisbee is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors can also use the following transportation options:

  • Bisbee Taxi Service: This local taxi service provides transportation within Bisbee and to neighboring communities.
  • Bisbee Free Shuttle: This free shuttle service runs on weekends and holidays and serves the Old Bisbee area and several hiking trailheads.
  • Bisbee Electric Vehicle Rentals: Rent an electric bike or scooter to explore Bisbee’s hills and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bisbee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bisbee is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding areas and taking excursions. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Tombstone, AZ: A historic mining town that was the site of the famous gunfight at the O.K. Corral.
  • Kartchner Caverns State Park: A stunning cave system that offers guided tours and educational programs.
  • Chiricahua National Monument: A natural wonder that features dramatic rock formations and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Bisbee is a hidden gem that offers unique history, breathtaking scenery, and a welcoming community. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s mining heritage or seeking outdoor adventure, Bisbee has something for everyone. We hope this guide has provided you with valuable information and inspiration for your next visit to Bisbee, Arizona. Safe travels!
