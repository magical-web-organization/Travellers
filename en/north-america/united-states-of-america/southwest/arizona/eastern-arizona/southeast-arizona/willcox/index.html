Introduction to Willcox

Willcox is a charming small town located in Southeast Arizona, in Eastern Arizona, in United States of America. The town is surrounded by picturesque scenery and rich history, making it a popular destination for tourists looking for unique experiences. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Willcox.

List of Places to Visit in Willcox

When visiting Willcox, there are several places that you must explore. From museums to hiking trails, here’s a list of places to visit in Willcox.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rex Allen Museum: This museum is dedicated to the famous cowboy singer and actor, Rex Allen. The museum showcases his life and career with a collection of his clothing, photos, and other memorabilia.
  • Chiricahua Regional Museum: This museum traces the history of Cochise County, from the Native American tribes who lived there to the pioneers who settled the area.
  • Gammons Gulch: A living museum of the old west, Gammons Gulch is a recreation of an 1880s western town and offers a unique experience to visitors.

Natural Attractions of Willcox: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chiricahua National Monument: Famous for its rock formations, this national monument offers scenic drives, hiking trails, and camping opportunities.
  • Coronado National Forest: Spanning over 1.78 million acres, Coronado National Forest boasts of outdoor recreation opportunities including hiking, biking, fishing, and camping.
  • Keiller Park: A popular location for picnics and other outdoor activities, Keiller Park is a great spot for a family day out.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Willcox

Willcox is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails, with routes suitable for all skill levels. Here are some of the must-try trails in the area:

  • Cochise Stronghold Trail: This trail is located in the Coronado National Forest and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Heart of Rocks Loop Trail: Located in the Chiricahua National Monument, this short loop trail takes you through the famous rock formations of the area.
  • Railroad Park Trail: This easy trail follows the path of the historic Southern Pacific Railroad and offers views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Willcox: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other things to do in Willcox. Here are some of the top activities and tours to experience:

  • Winery Tours: Willcox is home to several wineries and offers wine tasting and tours through the vineyards.
  • Bird Watching: The area is known for its diverse bird population, and the Willcox Playa Wildlife Area is a great spot for bird watching.
  • Golfing: The Turquoise Valley Golf Course is a beautiful 18-hole course located in Naco, just outside of Willcox.

Week-Long Itinerary for Willcox

If you have a week to spend in Willcox, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Rex Allen Museum and Gammons Gulch
2 Hike the Cochise Stronghold Trail
3 Take a winery tour and enjoy wine tasting in the afternoon
4 Explore the Chiricahua National Monument
5 Play a round of golf at the Turquoise Valley Golf Course

7 Relax and unwind at one of the local spas

Food and Drink Scene of Willcox

Willcox’s food and drink scene is diverse and flavorful. From traditional western cuisine to modern American fare, you’ll have plenty of options to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak: A classic western dish, Willcox is known for its juicy steaks, and there are several restaurants that serve them.
  • Mesquite Grilled Chicken: A local specialty, this dish is made with chicken marinated in a blend of spices and grilled over mesquite wood.
  • Tamales: A traditional Mexican dish, tamales are a popular snack in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Willcox is home to many award-winning wineries and is known as an up-and-coming wine region in Arizona.
  • Craft Beer: Willcox has a great craft beer scene with several breweries, including Railroad Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in Willcox: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to budget-friendly motels, Willcox has plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Arizona Sunset Inn: A highly rated bed and breakfast that offers guests a luxurious experience.
  • Days Inn: A budget-friendly motel located in the heart of town.
  • Roper Lake State Park Cabins: Located just outside of Willcox, the cabins at Roper Lake State Park offer a unique camping experience.

What to Buy in Willcox: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Willcox, don’t forget to pick up some souvenirs and local products to take home with you. Here are some of the best things to buy in the area:

  • Wine: Willcox is known for its wine, so make sure to pick up a bottle or two to bring home.
  • Local Crafts: Several local artists sell handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.
  • Pistachios: Arizona is one of the largest producers of pistachios in the United States, and you’ll find several stores selling them in Willcox.

Local Culture and Customs of Willcox

When visiting Willcox, it’s important to be respectful of the local customs and culture. The area has a strong cowboy and western culture, and residents take pride in their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Willcox

The best time to visit Willcox is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. The summers can be very hot, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Willcox

Do:

  • Explore the local wineries and vineyards.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local residents and their way of life.
  • Ignore safety warnings when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Willcox

Willcox is a relatively safe destination for tourists, but it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid walking alone at night, stick to well-lit areas, and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Willcox

The area is located at high elevation, and visitors should be aware of the potential for altitude sickness. It’s also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Willcox: Transportation Options and Logistics

Willcox is centrally located in Southeast Arizona, making it easily accessible by car. There are also several other transportation options available, including:

Airports Near Willcox:

  • Tucson International Airport (TUS): Located approximately 80 miles west of Willcox.

Bus Stations Near Willcox:

  • Greyhound: Located in nearby Benson, about 20 miles north of Willcox.

Other Options:

  • Car Rentals: There are several car rental companies located in nearby towns, including Benson, Tucson, and Sierra Vista.

How to Get Around in Willcox

The best way to get around Willcox is by car or bike. There are also several tour companies that offer guided tours of the area.

Where to Go Next from Willcox: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, there are several other destinations nearby that are worth a visit, including:

  • Bisbee: A historic mining town located 60 miles southeast of Willcox.
  • Tombstone: The site of the famous gunfight at the O.K. Corral, located 45 miles southeast of Willcox.
  • Chiricahua Mountains: A beautiful mountain range located east of Willcox that offers scenic drives, hiking, and camping opportunities.

Conclusion

Willcox is a hidden gem in Arizona that offers visitors a unique experience. From outdoor adventures to winery tours, there’s something for everyone in this charming small town. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable time in Willcox.
