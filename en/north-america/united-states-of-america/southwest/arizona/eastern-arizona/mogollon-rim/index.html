Introduction to Mogollon Rim

Located in Eastern Arizona, the Mogollon Rim is known for its stunning natural beauty, awe-inspiring landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. The Rim stretches for over 200 miles and ranges in elevation from 5,000 to 7,500 feet. It’s characterized by dense forests, deep canyons, and dramatic cliffs that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

List of Places to Visit in Mogollon Rim

The Mogollon Rim has plenty to offer visitors, with a wide variety of attractions and activities to enjoy. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in the Mogollon Rim region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rim Country Museum: This fascinating museum showcases the history and culture of the Rim Country region, with exhibits on Native American art and artifacts, pioneer life, and the early days of the area’s mining industry.
  • Tonto Natural Bridge State Park: Known for its stunning natural bridge, this park also features a visitor center where you can learn about the geology and history of the area.
  • Zane Grey Cabin: This historic cabin was once the home of famed author Zane Grey, who wrote many of his Western novels here. Today, it’s a museum that offers a look into Grey’s life and legacy.

Natural Attractions of Mogollon Rim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mogollon Rim Trail: This 52-mile trail is a popular hiking and biking route that winds through the forests and canyons of the Rim. Along the way, you’ll enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside and encounter an array of wildlife.
  • Woods Canyon Lake: Located in the heart of the Rim, this beautiful lake is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and picnicking. There are also several hiking trails that start from the lake.
  • Tonto National Forest: Covering over 2.8 million acres, this vast forest offers endless opportunities for outdoor exploration, from hiking and camping to fishing and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mogollon Rim

The Mogollon Rim region is full of incredible hiking and biking routes, with something for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. Here are some of the most popular trails in the area:

  • Highline Trail: This 51-mile trail runs along the Mogollon Rim, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It’s a challenging hike that typically takes several days to complete.
  • West Baldy Trail: This 7.5-mile trail leads to the summit of Mount Baldy, the highest peak in the Mogollon Rim region. It’s a difficult, but rewarding hike that winds through some of the area’s most beautiful scenery.
  • Canal Trail: This 4.5-mile trail is a popular biking route that follows an old canal through the forests of the Rim. It’s an easy ride that’s suitable for cyclists of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Mogollon Rim: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in the Mogollon Rim region. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Horseback riding: There are several stables in the area that offer guided horseback rides through the forests and canyons of the Rim.
  • Fishing: The Rim is home to several lakes and streams that are stocked with trout and other fish, making it a popular destination for anglers.
  • Jeep tours: If you want to explore the more remote areas of the Rim, a guided Jeep tour is a great option. You’ll be able to access areas that are otherwise inaccessible to most visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mogollon Rim

If you have a week to spend in the Mogollon Rim region, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore the Rim Country Museum and the nearby town of Payson.
  • Day 2: Hike the Highline Trail or another of the Rim’s numerous hiking trails.
  • Day 3: Spend the day fishing at one of the Rim’s lakes or streams.
  • Day 4: Take a Jeep tour to explore some of the more remote areas of the Rim.
  • Day 5: Visit Tonto Natural Bridge State Park and hike the trails in the area.
  • Day 6: Go horseback riding through the forests and canyons of the Rim.
  • Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at Woods Canyon Lake, enjoying a picnic and taking in the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Mogollon Rim

The Mogollon Rim region is known for its delicious food and drink, with an array of local dishes and beverages to enjoy. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Bread: This delicious Native American staple is a flat dough that’s fried until golden brown, and can be served sweet or savory.
  • Navajo Tacos: This dish features fry bread topped with beans, cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, and other toppings.
  • Sonoran Hot Dogs: A beloved street food, these hot dogs are wrapped in bacon and topped with beans, cheese, onions, and other toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Prickly Pear Margarita: Made with tequila, fresh lime juice, and prickly pear syrup, this regional twist on the classic margarita is both refreshing and delicious.
  • Sotol: This regional spirit is made from the Desert Spoon plant, and has a smoky, earthy flavor that’s unique to the area.
  • Local Craft Beer: The Mogollon Rim region is home to several breweries that produce delicious craft beers, with a variety of styles and flavors to choose from.

Where to Stay in Mogollon Rim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of lodging options in the Mogollon Rim region, from hotels and resorts to cabins and campgrounds. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Kohl’s Ranch Lodge: Located in the heart of the Rim, this rustic resort offers comfortable cabins and plenty of recreational opportunities, including fishing, hiking, and horseback riding.
  • Strawberry Inn: This charming inn offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere, as well as easy access to the area’s hiking trails and fishing spots.
  • Mogollon Rim Campground: If you love camping, this scenic campground is the perfect place to pitch your tent or park your RV. It’s located near several hiking trails and fishing spots.

What to Buy in Mogollon Rim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Mogollon Rim region is full of unique and interesting products that make great souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Native American Art and Jewelry: The Rim is home to several Native American communities that sell beautiful art and jewelry, including pottery, rugs, and silverwork.
  • Local Honey: The Rim is known for its delicious honey, which comes from the area’s thriving beekeeping industry.
  • Regional Products: Look for locally made jams, jellies, and other food products that showcase the flavors and ingredients of the Mogollon Rim region.

Local Culture and Customs of Mogollon Rim

The Mogollon Rim region has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong Native American presence and a long history of mining and ranching. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s cultural traditions and customs, and take the time to learn about the region’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Mogollon Rim

The best time to visit the Mogollon Rim region is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be cold and snowy, although the Rim is still beautiful during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mogollon Rim

To ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to the Mogollon Rim region, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the area’s natural beauty and help preserve it for future generations.
  • Stay on designated trails and roads to avoid damaging fragile ecosystems.
  • Be friendly and respectful to local residents and businesses.
  • Take your time and enjoy the area’s beauty and unique culture.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or other debris in the wilderness.
  • Disturb wildlife or their habitats.
  • Ignore warning signs or advice from local authorities.
  • Drive off-road or in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

While the Mogollon Rim region is generally safe, visitors should take proper precautions to ensure their safety and health during their trip.

Safety in Mogollon Rim

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid hiking or camping alone.
  • Carry plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Be prepared for sudden changes in weather, as storms can develop quickly in the Rim.
  • Avoid getting too close to the edge of cliffs or other dangerous areas.

Health in Mogollon Rim

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Bring insect repellent to protect yourself from ticks and other biting insects.
  • Be aware of altitude sickness if you’re not used to the high elevation of the Rim.
  • Bring any necessary medications with you, as medical services may be limited in the area.

Getting to Mogollon Rim: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Mogollon Rim region is located about 100 miles northeast of Phoenix, and can be reached by car, bus, or airplane.

Airports Near Mogollon Rim

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport: This major airport is located in Phoenix and is the closest airport to the Rim.
  • Flagstaff Pulliam Airport: This smaller airport is located about 75 miles north of the Rim and offers regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Mogollon Rim

  • Greyhound Bus Station: There are several Greyhound bus stations in the Phoenix area that offer service to Payson, the closest town to the Rim.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the Rim region, as it gives you the flexibility to travel at your own pace.
  • Private Shuttle: There are several private shuttle companies that offer transportation from Phoenix to the Rim.

How to Get Around in Mogollon Rim

While driving is the easiest way to get around the Rim region, there are also several other options available, including:

  • Hiking: Many of the Rim’s attractions and trails are accessible on foot, making hiking a great option for those who love to explore the outdoors.
  • Biking: With its miles of scenic roads and trails, the Rim is a popular destination for cyclists of all skill levels.
  • Horseback Riding: Guided horseback rides are a great way to explore the Rim’s backcountry and see the area’s wildlife up close.

Where to Go Next from Mogollon Rim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore the area around the Mogollon Rim, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Sedona: Known for its stunning red rock formations and spiritual energy, Sedona is located about 80 miles west of the Rim.
  • Grand Canyon National Park: One of the world’s most famous natural wonders, the Grand Canyon is located about 150 miles north of the Rim.
  • Petrified Forest National Park: This unique park is home to some of the largest and most colorful petrified wood deposits in the world. It’s located about 100 miles east of the Rim.

Conclusion

The Mogollon Rim region is a stunning and unique destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a peaceful escape from the city, the Rim has it all. So why wait? Start planning your trip today and experience the beauty and wonder of the Mogollon Rim for yourself!
