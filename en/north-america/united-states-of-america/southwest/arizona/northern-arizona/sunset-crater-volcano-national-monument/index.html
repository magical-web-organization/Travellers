Introduction to Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument, located in Northern Arizona, is a unique natural landscape that attracts visitors from all over the world. This incredible destination was established in 1930 to protect the remains of the Sunset Volcano eruption that occurred over 900 years ago. Today, the park encompasses over 3,000 acres of land filled with unique geological formations, stunning vistas, and an abundance of flora and fauna.

List of Places to Visit in Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

Whether you’re interested in learning about the local culture, embarking on a thrilling outdoor adventure, or simply taking in the stunning natural scenery, Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument has something for everyone. Here are some of the places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Sunset Crater Visitor Center - This is the best place to start your journey through the park. Here, you’ll find detailed information about the geology, ecology, and history of the area.

  2. Lomaki and Box Canyon Pueblos - These ancient ruins give visitors a glimpse into the lives of the Sinagua people who lived in the area over 900 years ago.

  3. Wupatki National Monument - Adjacent to Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument, this park features an extensive collection of well-preserved pueblo ruins that you won’t want to miss.

Natural Attractions of Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sunset Crater - The main attraction of the park, Sunset Crater is a 1,000-foot-tall volcanic cone that was created during a volcanic eruption over 900 years ago. You can take a self-guided tour of the area and learn about the unique geological formations that were created by the eruption.

  2. Lenox Crater Trail - This easy 1-mile trail leads to the top of a small volcano that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. A’a Trail - This difficult 2.5-mile trail takes you through a lava field and offers views of the surrounding cinder cones, rock formations, and the San Francisco Peaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of the park is on foot or on bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  1. Sunset Trail - This 3-mile trail starts at the Sunset Crater parking lot and takes you through a forest of ponderosa pines and offers amazing views of the volcanic cones.

  2. Bonito Vista Trail - This 1.5-mile trail takes you through a beautiful meadow and offers views of the San Francisco Peaks.

  3. Lava Flow Trail - This 1-mile trail takes you through a field of lava and offers views of the San Francisco Peaks and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  1. Guided tours - Take a guided tour of the park and learn about the history, geology, and ecology of the area from expert guides.

  2. Ranger-led programs - Join a ranger-led program and learn about the local wildlife, plant life, and history of the area.

  3. Stargazing - Take advantage of the park’s proximity to the city of Flagstaff and enjoy stargazing under some of the clearest skies in the country.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument:

  1. Day 1 - Explore the Sunset Crater Visitor Center and take a self-guided tour of Sunset Crater.

  2. Day 2 - Hike the Bonito Vista Trail, visit the Lomaki and Box Canyon Pueblos, and take a guided tour of the area.

  3. Day 3 - Explore the Lenox Crater Trail, visit the Wupatki National Monument, and take a ranger-led program.

  4. Day 4 - Take a day trip to Flagstaff and enjoy the city’s vibrant culture and arts scene.

  5. Day 5 - Explore the A’a Trail and take a guided tour of the area.

  6. Day 6 - Enjoy stargazing under the clear Arizona skies.

  7. Day 7 - Relax and take it easy by enjoying the natural scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

When it comes to the food and drink scene in Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Navajo Fry Bread - This traditional dish is a must-try when visiting the area. It’s a chewy and delicious bread that’s fried to perfection.

  2. Chili Rellenos - This spicy and flavorful dish is a local favorite and is made with roasted chili peppers stuffed with cheese and other ingredients.

  3. Sonoran Hot Dog - This delicious hot dog is wrapped in bacon and served with plenty of toppings, including beans, tomatoes, onions, and mayonnaise.

Local Beverages

  1. Sarsaparilla - This sweet, bubbly drink is a favorite amongst locals and is made with sarsaparilla root and other ingredients.

  2. Prickly pear cactus juice - This refreshing and unique juice is made from the prickly pear cactus fruit and is a local specialty.

  3. Arizona wines - Arizona has a thriving wine industry, and there are plenty of local wineries to visit and taste some amazing wines.

Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

  • Stay on designated trails and paths.

  • Be aware of and avoid potential hazards, such as loose rock and steep drop-offs.

  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife.

Health in Sunset Crater Volcano National Monument

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.

  • Be aware of the potential for altitude sickness, as the park is located at high elevation.
