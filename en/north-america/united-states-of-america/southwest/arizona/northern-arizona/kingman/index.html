Kingman Travel Guide - An Ultimate Tour Guide

Kingman is a city located in the heart of Northern Arizona, USA. This city is both a tourist destination and a transition point to many nearby national parks and attractions. Kingman is a great place to explore for those who are looking for an adventure, or simply to enjoy the desert scenery and experience the southwestern culture. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, eat, and stay in Kingman, Arizona.

Introduction to Kingman

Kingman is a high desert oasis located in Mohave County. Located at the intersection of Route 66 and I-40, it is an ideal spot for travelers, adventurers, and vacationers alike. This vibrant desert community is set against a scenic backdrop of mountains, and offers a diverse range of activities and attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Kingman

Kingman is a city that offers a wide range of attractions for visitors. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, here are the top places for tourists to visit in Kingman.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mohave Museum of History and Arts - A museum that showcases the history and culture of the Mohave County.
  • Bonelli House - A historical house museum that dates back to 1915, furnished as it would have been during its early years.
  • Mohave County Courthouse - Built in 1915, this courthouse is registered as a historical place and showcases some amazing architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kingman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hualapai Mountain Park - An outdoor adventure park, perfect for hiking, camping, and bird-watching. The park offers great natural scenery, with some of the best views in Arizona.
  • Colorado River Canyon - A scenic drive that offers breathtaking views of the river and the surrounding desert landscape.
  • Kingman Wash - A great spot for ATV and mountain bike enthusiasts, Kingman Wash offers a range of trails for different skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingman

  • Hualapai Peak Trail - 7.6 miles of a moderately challenging trail located in the beautiful Hualapai Mountain Park.
  • Beale Loop Trail - A 1.6-mile loop trail located within the city limits. This trail offers some great views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Cerbat Foothills Trail System - A collection of trails that offer over 30 miles of hiking and mountain biking opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Kingman: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Towns Tour - An educational tour that takes visitors through some of the most historic ghost towns around the area. The tour includes a guide that provides historical information and points of interest.
  • Grand Canyon Tour - A must-do activity for anyone visiting Kingman, the Grand Canyon tour offers some breathtaking views and experiences for visitors.
  • Zipline Adventure - A thrill-seeker’s dream, Kingman offers a unique zipline experience that’s great for the whole family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kingman

Day 1: Arrive in Kingman and explore the city.

Day 2: Drive to Hualapai Mountain Park and hike the Hualapai Peak Trail.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Ghost Towns around the area.

Day 4: Visit the Mohave Museum of History and Arts and the Bonelli House.

Day 5: Take a Grand Canyon Tour.

Day 6: Go ATV-ing or mountain biking in Kingman Wash.

Day 7: Visit the many local shops and enjoy the food scene of Kingman.

Food and Drink Scene of Kingman

Kingman’s food and drink scene is a unique blend of southern cowboy cooking and southwestern cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages to enjoy while in Kingman.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sonoran Hot Dog - A local specialty that’s a spin on the classic hot dog. This dish features a bacon-wrapped hot dog, topped with onions, tomatoes, peppers, and beans.
  • Navajo Taco - A taco made with Indian Fry Bread and topped with beans, lettuce, tomato, cheese, and ground beef.
  • Mesquite-Grilled Steak - A local favorite, the steak is cooked over a mesquite-grill for an added smoky flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sarsaparilla - A root beer-like soda that’s a local favorite.
  • Prickly Pear Margarita - Made with prickly pear fruit, this margarita is sweet and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Kingman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kingman offers a range of accommodation options for visitors. From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the top choices for visitors.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Kingman - A hotel that offers comfortable rooms and great amenities, such as an outdoor pool and a fitness center.
  • Red Roof Inn Kingman - A budget-friendly hotel that’s located close to many of Kingman’s attractions.
  • El Trovatore Motel - A historic motel that’s located on Route 66. This motel offers an authentic Route 66 experience with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Kingman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turquoise Jewelry - Kingman is known for its high-quality turquoise jewelry. Visitors can purchase handmade pieces from local artisans.
  • Western-style Boots - Kingman is famous for its cowboy culture, and what better way to embrace the local culture than by buying a pair of western-style boots?
  • Route 66 Memorabilia - Visitors can take home a piece of Route 66 history with unique souvenirs that celebrate the iconic American highway.

Local Culture and Customs of Kingman

Kingman has a rich cultural heritage that’s rooted in the history of the southwest. Visitors should take some time to learn about the local customs and traditions, such as the cowboy culture and the influence of Route 66 on the area.

Best Time to Visit Kingman

The best time to visit Kingman is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the temperatures are mild and comfortable. During the summer months, the temperatures can be extremely hot, so visitors should be prepared for the heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingman

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the great outdoors and natural attractions.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and try new dishes.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Litter in the parks and public areas.
  • Disrespect the local people or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kingman

Kingman is generally a safe city, but visitors should exercise caution, as with any urban area. Visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night. It’s always a good idea to lock doors and windows.

Health in Kingman

Kingman has a well-established healthcare system, with a range of medical facilities available to both residents and visitors. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should ensure they bring sufficient medication and inform their medical service provider about their travel plans.

Getting to Kingman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kingman is a well-connected city with a range of transportation options available to visitors.

Airports Near Kingman

  • McCarran International Airport - Located in Las Vegas, McCarran International Airport is the nearest major airport to Kingman.
  • Laughlin/Bullhead International Airport - Located approximately 40 miles south of Kingman, this airport offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kingman

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in downtown Kingman, the Greyhound Bus Station offers daily routes to nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Visitors can rent a car from one of the many car rental companies available at the airports and bus stations.
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft - Taxi, Uber, and Lyft services are all available in Kingman.

How to Get Around in Kingman

Kingman is a city that is easily navigable by car or public transportation. Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Kingman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that can be reached from Kingman. Here are some of the top choices for visitors:

  • Grand Canyon National Park - One of the most popular attractions near Kingman, the Grand Canyon National Park is a must-see for any visitor to Arizona.
  • Lake Havasu City - Known for its beautiful lake and beaches, Lake Havasu City is a great place to relax and enjoy the Arizona sunshine.
  • Sedona - A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Sedona offers great hiking and biking trails, as well as stunning natural scenery.

Conclusion

Kingman is a city that offers a unique blend of southwestern culture, history, and adventure. With its diverse range of attractions and activities, it’s an ideal spot for visitors looking to explore Arizona and experience the beauty of the southwest. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Kingman and create lasting memories of this incredible destination.
