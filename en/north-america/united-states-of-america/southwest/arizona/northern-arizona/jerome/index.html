Introduction to Jerome

Nestled in the Verde Valley of Northern Arizona, Jerome is a quirky and charming town known for its rich mining history, breathtaking desert scenery, and vibrant arts scene. At an elevation of 5,200 feet above sea level, Jerome offers stunning views of the nearby Mingus Mountain, Clarkdale, Cottonwood and the greater Verde Valley area. It is also home to numerous historical landmarks including landmarks from the old mining days and quaint shops from local artists. Jerome has something to appeal to everyone, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts and art lovers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey of discovery through the best and most interesting parts of Jerome, Arizona.

List of Places to Visit in Jerome

Jerome has a variety of attractions to suit all interests. In this section, we’ll take you through some of the must-visit places to check out during your visit to Jerome.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jerome State Historic Park: Located on the hillside, the state park boasts the remains of an old copper mining plant, the Douglas Mansion, which has been converted into a museum. Visitors can get a glimpse into the booming copper mining era, see mining paraphernalia and hear stories of life during the early 20th century. ​

  • Audrey Headframe Park: Audrey Headframe Park gives visitors a true sense of what mining was like in Jerome by giving tours and demonstrations of the tools and techniques used for mining in the olden days.

  • Jerome Historical Society Mine Museum: Located on Main Street, the museum showcases the history of mining and the role it played in shaping the town’s development, as well as the daily lives of the miners themselves.

  • Sliding Jail: The Historic Jerome State Park is also home to the famous sliding jail. This unique piece of history is part of the old city jail and moved 225 feet downhill in 1910 due to its unstable foundation.

Natural Attractions of Jerome: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gold King Mine Ghost Town: This is a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts looking for some old-fashioned family fun. Visitors can tour the old mine and check out some of the antique cars on the property.

  • Jerome Historical Marker: Located on the road to Jerome, visitors can stop to take pictures and appreciate the magnificent view of the Verde Valley while also learning more about the history of Jerome.

  • Mingus Mountain: The stunning Mingus Mountain offers outdoor enthusiasts a variety of hiking trails, mountain biking trails, and scenic drives with views of Prescott National Forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Jerome

Jerome has plenty of options available for those looking for some exercise and adventure. There are several hiking trails and biking routes, offering varying levels of difficulty and breathtaking views of the surroundings.

  • Trail #161: The Trail #161 offers visitors the perfect gateway into the Prescott National Forest while passing through old mining machinery and along the beautiful Woodchute Wilderness trail. This trail is considered moderate and requires an Adventure Pass.

  • Trail #391: For those looking for a more challenging hiking trail, Trail #391 offers stunning views of the mountain ranges and Verde Valley. The trail is around 7.1 miles long and considered difficult.

  • First Born Trail #162: Lying within the Chino Valley area, First Born Trail #162 offers a moderate trek through a sandy trail while providing beautiful views of the Chino Valley.

Best Things to Do in Jerome: Activities and Tours

Jerome has something for everyone to enjoy. Here are some of our top recommendations for things to do during your visit:

  • Ghost Tours: Jerome is famous for its ghostly past, and taking a ghost tour is the best way to learn about the paranormal activity around town. Be ready to learn about times gone by, and take numerous ghostly photos along the way.

  • Art Gallery Hop: If you’re interested in arts and culture, we recommend taking a leisurely stroll through the town’s art galleries. Be prepared to fall in love with incredible pieces of art from talented local artists who call the area home.

  • Wine Tasting: Jerome has some excellent wineries that offer fantastic views of the surrounding landscape. Be sure to try out some local wines during your visit, and maybe even take home a bottle or two.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jerome

Spend a week exploring Jerome and the surrounding area through the following itinerary:

Day 1 – Check-in and visit the Jerome State Park. Day 2 – Mingus Mountain Hiking Trail Adventure. Day 3 – Visit the Old King Mine Ghost Town. Day 4 – Wine tasting at local wineries in the Jerome Clemenceau Wine Country area. Day 5 – History and art experience with a visit to Jerome Historical Society Mine Museum and Art Galleries. Day 6 – Nature trail experience at National Forest – Tuzigoot National Monument. Day 7 – Get unique souvenirs from Jerome for family and friends before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Jerome

Jerome offers an exceptional dining and drinking experience. The region has an influence from the Southwest, so expect plenty of flavors and spices in your dining experience.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jerome Lamb Burger: This burger is quite famous and can be found in most restaurants in the area. The lamb is locally raised for an authentic and flavorful taste.

  • Corned Beef and Cabbage: This dish is commonly served in most restaurants and is renowned for its flavorful spices and tender beef.

  • Green Chili Stew: This dish is native to the Southwest and is typically served in a bowl. The dish contains traditional ingredients such as pork tenderloin and New Mexican Saffron.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Verde Valley’s wine scene has gained nationwide recognition, and numerous local wineries offer wine tasting.

  • Beer: Brewsters’Jerome was established in 1998 and is famous for its craft burgers and local beer brews.

Where to Stay in Jerome: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jerome has various accommodation options at your disposal. Here are some of our top choices:

  • The Grand Hotel: This hotel offers stunning views of the mountains and is perfect for those looking for a luxurious and comfortable stay.

  • Connor Hotel: The Connor hotel is located in the heart of Jerome and offers accommodation options that range from private suites to shared rooms.

  • The Surgeon’s House Bed and Breakfast: Nestled on the hill and boasting a warm atmosphere, this B&B is perfect for those looking for a quiet and romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Jerome: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jerome offers several shopping experiences. Be sure to purchase some souvenirs and local products to bring home.

  • Locally Made Jewelry: Many of the retail stores in the town offer beautiful handcrafted jewelry, designed by local artists.

  • Pottery: Pottery is an important part of Jerome’s Native American history and can be found in different forms throughout most gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Jerome

The locals in Jerome area are known for their warm and welcoming approach to visitors. They place a premium on the preservation of their history and traditions. Jerome is primarily composed of galleries and museums, each showcasing unique art pieces and a story.

Best Time to Visit Jerome

The summer months, from May to September, make for the perfect time to visit and enjoy outdoor activities, as the temperatures can reach over 100 degrees. Winters range from the 40s to 60s, making it a bit chilly for hiking and other outdoor adventures. Spring and fall remain the best seasons to visit, with perfect weather conditions for outdoor activities. Both seasons are also less crowded with visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jerome

Do:

  • Take your time: Jerome is a town with a rich history that begs to be explored fully.

  • Be respectful to the locals: Residents place a premium on welcoming visitors and helping them make an enjoyable stay in the community.

  • Explore the outdoors: Jerome has several trails that showcase the area’s natural beauty, making it the perfect location to partake in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Dispose of all trash properly to keep the environment and town clean.

  • Photograph privately owned buildings without permission: Some of the buildings in the town are privately owned, and photographers need permission from owners to photograph the structures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jerome

Jerome is a safe town, but visitors should take necessary precautions during their stay. Travelers are encouraged to stay in well-lit and common areas, be aware of their surroundings, and keep their valuables safely secured.

Health in Jerome

Jerome sits at an elevation of over 5,000 feet, so visitors are susceptible to altitude sickness. To prevent altitude sickness symptoms, drink plenty of fluids and avoid consuming alcohol.

Getting to Jerome: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jerome has several transport options, including:

Airports Near Jerome:

  • Prescott Municipal Airport: The airport is located about 30 miles north of Jerome.

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport: It is located about 112 miles southeast of Jerome.

Bus Stations Near Jerome:

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound buses stop in the nearby town of Cottonwood, around 8 miles from Jerome.

  • Sedona-Phoenix Shuttle: A shared shuttle operates between the Sedona-Verde Valley area and Phoenix’s Sky Harbor airport with a stop in Cottonwood.

Other Options:

  • Renting a Car: Renting a car is an excellent option for those who want more freedom to move around and explore different nearby sights.

How to Get Around in Jerome

The best transportation for touring the town is on foot, as Jerome is a walkable town, and parking can be challenging. Although, renting a bike is always a fun hiking alternative that makes life a little more exciting.

Where to Go Next from Jerome: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jerome is located in close proximity to some incredible landmarks and attractions. Here are some of our top recommendations to explore after visiting Jerome:

  • Sedona: Sedona has a stunning natural landscape with amazing red rock formations suitable for hiking, rock climbing, and viewing art galleries.

  • Clarkdale: Clarkdale offers tours of former copper mining sites while providing scenic views of Verde Canyon.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, wine tasting or exploring a quirky and charming town, Jerome has everything you need for an unforgettable vacation. Take in the stunning views, enjoy some outdoor adventures, and immerse yourself in the local culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to have a fantastic time on your visit to Jerome, Arizona.
