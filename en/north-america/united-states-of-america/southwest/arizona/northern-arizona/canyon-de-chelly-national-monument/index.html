Food and Drink Scene of Canyon de Chelly National Monument

Canyon de Chelly National Monument has a handful of restaurants and cafes serving traditional Navajo cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Navajo Fry Bread: A fluffy fried bread that is a staple in Navajo cuisine
  • Stew: A hearty and aromatic dish made with beef or mutton and vegetables
  • Blue Corn Mush: A traditional Navajo dish made with blue cornmeal that is similar to polenta

Local Beverages

  • Navajo Tea: A tea made from a blend of herbs that is said to have medicinal properties
  • Cactus Juice: A refreshing and slightly sweet drink made from the juice of prickly pear cactus

Where to Stay in Canyon de Chelly National Monument: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Canyon de Chelly National Monument offers a variety of lodging options for visitors. Some of the popular options include:

  • Thunderbird Lodge: A historic hotel located within the Monument that offers rooms and suites
  • Best Western Canyon de Chelly Inn: A modern hotel located in nearby Chinle
  • Sacred Canyon Lodge: A rustic lodge located within the Monument that offers rooms and cabins
  • Spider Rock Campground: A camping area located within the Monument that offers tent and RV camping

What to Buy in Canyon de Chelly National Monument: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can purchase traditional Navajo crafts and souvenirs from various vendors near the Visitor Center or on guided tours. Here are some popular items to consider:

  • Navajo rugs and blankets
  • Jewelry made with turquoise and other semi-precious stones
  • Pottery and ceramics
  • Kachina dolls

Local Culture and Customs of Canyon de Chelly National Monument

As a sacred site for the Navajo people, visitors are expected to respect the culture and customs of the Navajo. Some general etiquette tips to keep in mind include:

  • Seek permission before taking photographs of people or their property
  • Avoid touching or tampering with any cultural or historical sites within the Monument
  • Observe quiet hours (10 pm - 6 am) in camping areas and lodges
  • Refrain from littering or leaving any trash in the Canyon

Best Time to Visit Canyon de Chelly National Monument

The best time to visit Canyon de Chelly National Monument is during the spring and fall months (March to May and September to November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. Summer months (June to August) can be very hot, and the number of visitors peaks during this time. Winter months (December to February) can be cold and snow-covered, but offer a unique and peaceful experience for visitors who don’t mind the weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canyon de Chelly National Monument

Do:

  • Seek permission before taking photographs of people or their property
  • Respect the culture and customs of the Navajo people
  • Carry plenty of water and sunscreen during outdoor activities
  • Observe quiet hours (10pm - 6am) in camping areas and lodges

Don’t:

  • Touch or tamper with any cultural or historical sites within the Monument
  • Litter or leave any trash in the canyon
  • Disturb any wildlife within the Monument

Safety and Health

Safety in Canyon de Chelly National Monument

Visitors should always be cautious when hiking, climbing or partaking in any outdoor activities within the Monument. Here are some general safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Carry plenty of water and wear appropriate clothing and shoes
  • Check weather conditions before setting out on any hike or outdoor activity
  • Stay on designated trails when hiking or exploring any area within the Monument

Health in Canyon de Chelly National Monument

Visitors should be aware of the high elevation and possible effects of altitude sickness. Here are some general health tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
  • Avoid alcohol and caffeine, which can exacerbate the effects of altitude sickness
  • Give your body time to acclimate to the altitude before engaging in any strenuous activity

How to Get Around in Canyon de Chelly National Monument

The best way to explore the Canyon is by car, bike, or on foot. Visitors can drive along the North or South Rim drives and take in the scenic views from multiple overlooks. Mountain biking is also allowed on some trails within the Monument. Guided tours are also available for visitors who prefer to explore the Canyon with a knowledgeable guide.

Where to Go Next from Canyon de Chelly National Monument: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Canyon de Chelly National Monument is close to several other popular destinations in the Southwest. Here are some recommendations for places to visit next:

  • Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park: A stunning natural landmark that is featured in many Western movies
  • Grand Canyon National Park: One of the most famous natural attractions in the world and a must-visit for any nature lover
  • Petrified Forest National Park: A fascinating destination featuring ancient petrified trees that have turned to stone over time

Conclusion

Canyon de Chelly National Monument offers visitors a unique combination of ancient culture and stunning natural beauty.
