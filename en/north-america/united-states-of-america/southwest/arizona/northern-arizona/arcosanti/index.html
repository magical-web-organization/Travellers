The Ultimate Travel Guide to Arcosanti, Arizona

Arcosanti is a unique and progressive urban laboratory in the high desert of Northern Arizona. The town is a thriving community of about 100 residents who live and work in the experimental village, which aims to demonstrate an environmentally conscious approach to urban design. The community continues to evolve, offering both residents and visitors an opportunity to experience sustainable living, explore the outdoors, and engage with the local culture and customs. This guide will take you through the best things to do in Arcosanti, including hiking/biking routes, cultural attractions, natural wonders, accommodations, and local culture and customs while also learning about transportation options and logistics.

Introduction to Arcosanti

Arcosanti was founded in 1970 by Italian-American architect Paolo Soleri, who dreamed of creating a sustainable urban community in the high desert of Arizona. Since then, the community has evolved into a thriving village that showcases Soleri’s unique approach to urban design and sustainable living. Arcosanti’s buildings are designed to maximize energy efficiency, promote social interaction, and minimize the impact on the surrounding environment. Visitors can spend a day exploring the community, taking workshops, or attending events.

List of Places to Visit in Arcosanti

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Visitors can explore the Arcosanti Visitor Center to learn more about the history and philosophy behind Arcosanti’s unique design
  • The Ceramics Apse allows visitors to watch residents at work in the ceramics studio and purchase handmade pottery
  • The Soleri Archives house an extensive collection of Paolo Soleri’s artwork and writings

Natural Attractions of Arcosanti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Agua Fria National Monument offers hiking, mountain biking, and scenic drives through a diverse landscape of high desert and riparian areas
  • The Verde River, just west of Arcosanti, is a popular spot for kayaking and canoeing
  • The rugged Bradshaw Mountains offer opportunities for rock climbing, off-road adventures, and scenic drives

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arcosanti

  • The Agua Fria National Monument offers over 450,000 acres of land with hiking and mountain biking trails of varying lengths and difficulties.
  • The Black Canyon Trail is a 78-mile trail that can be accessed from the Agua Fria National Monument and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Granite Mountain Trail offers a challenging 3-mile hike with impressive views of the Bradshaw Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Arcosanti: Activities and Tours

  • Visitors can take guided tours of the Arcosanti community to learn more about its history, architecture, and sustainable living practices.
  • The Community Music and Performance Space offers performances by residents and various artists as well as workshops and cultural events.
  • Visitors can take workshops in ceramics, metalworking, and other crafts through the Cosanti Foundation.

Food and Drink Scene of Arcosanti

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Arcosanti Community Kitchen serves vegetarian and vegan meals sourced from the community’s gardens and local farms.
  • The Pita Jungle offers Mediterranean-inspired cuisine with a focus on healthy ingredients and sustainable practices.
  • The Raven Cafe in nearby Prescott specializes in farm-to-table cuisine and features an impressive craft beer selection.

Local Beverages

  • The Verde Valley Wine Trail features several wineries and tasting rooms within driving distance of Arcosanti.
  • The Prescott Brewing Company offers a selection of craft beers brewed on-site.
  • The Thumb Butte Distillery in Prescott produces small-batch spirits using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Arcosanti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Arcosanti community offers guest rooms and apartments for rent, allowing visitors to stay within the experimental village.
  • The Sky Ranch Lodge in nearby Sedona offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and easy access to outdoor attractions.
  • The Hassayampa Inn in Prescott offers historic charm and modern amenities in a beautifully restored building.

What to Buy in Arcosanti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade ceramics from the Ceramics Apse.
  • Windbells and other metalwork from the Cosanti Foundation.
  • Local produce and artisanal goods from the Prescott Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Arcosanti

Visitors are encouraged to embrace the community’s values of sustainability, creativity, and social responsibility. Visitors should respect the privacy of residents and ask permission before taking photos of people or buildings. Additionally, visitors should adhere to the community’s sustainability guidelines, such as minimizing waste and conserving water.

Best Time to Visit Arcosanti

The best time to visit Arcosanti is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are abundant. Summer can be hot, and winter can be cold, with occasional snowfall in the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arcosanti

Do:

  • Respect the privacy of residents
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people or buildings
  • Adhere to sustainable practices

Don’t:

  • Make loud noises
  • Dispose of waste improperly
  • Leave pets unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Arcosanti

Arcosanti is a safe community, with low crime rates, and friendly residents. Visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Arcosanti

Visitors should stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun when spending time outdoors. Limited health services are available in the community, so visitors should bring any necessary medications with them.

How to Get Around in Arcosanti

The community is small enough to explore on foot, but a car can be useful for exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Arcosanti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sedona, AZ (45-minute drive) offers stunning red rock scenery, hiking trails, and spas.
  • Prescott, AZ (30-minute drive) offers historic charm, art galleries, and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Grand Canyon National Park (2-hour drive) offers stunning views of one of the world’s most famous natural wonders.

Conclusion

Arcosanti offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience sustainable living and engage with the local community. Whether exploring the stunning natural landscape, taking part in community events and workshops, or indulging in the local food and drink scene, visitors to Arcosanti are sure to be inspired by this progressive urban laboratory.
