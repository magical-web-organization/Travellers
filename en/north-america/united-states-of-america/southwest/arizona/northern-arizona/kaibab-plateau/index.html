Introduction to Kaibab Plateau

Located in Northern Arizona, Kaibab Plateau is a 1.5 million-acre plateau that sits between the Grand Canyon and the Colorado Plateau. The plateau is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, as well as numerous outdoor recreational opportunities. Whether you’re an avid hiker or just looking to soak up some breathtaking views, Kaibab Plateau is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kaibab Plateau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the culture and history of Kaibab Plateau, be sure to check out the following museums and historical places:

  • Tusayan Museum and Ruin: This museum showcases the Pueblo Indian history and culture of the Grand Canyon region, and includes a partially excavated Puebloan ruin.
  • Buckskinner Park: This historical park features a reconstructed 1890s mountain man trader’s store, animal displays, and a blacksmithing shop.

Natural Attractions of Kaibab Plateau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Kaibab Plateau is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with numerous opportunities for recreational activities. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • North Rim of the Grand Canyon: The North Rim is higher and less crowded than the South Rim, offering spectacular views of the Grand Canyon.
  • Kaibab National Forest: This national forest offers over 1.6 million acres of predominantly ponderosa pine forest, scenic drives, trails, and camping.
  • Marble Viewpoint: From this viewpoint, visitors can see the Vermilion and Echo Cliffs, the Colorado River, and Marble Canyon.
  • Jacob Lake: A popular spot for picnicking, fishing, and camping, Jacob Lake is known for its world-famous cookies.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaibab Plateau

Kaibab Plateau offers numerous hiking and biking routes for visitors of all skill levels. Some of the most popular include:

  • Bright Angel Point Trail: A short half-mile trail with breathtaking views of the Grand Canyon and Kaibab Plateau.
  • Ken Patrick Trail: An 11-mile trail that offers stunning views of the Grand Canyon and surrounding areas.
  • Arizona Trail: A 4,000-mile trail that spans the length of Arizona, including portions of the Kaibab Plateau.

Best Things to Do in Kaibab Plateau: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some exciting activities to do while in Kaibab Plateau, here are a few options:

  • Helicopter Tours: Get a bird’s eye view of the Grand Canyon and Kaibab Plateau.
  • Jeep Tours: Explore the rugged terrain of Kaibab Plateau on a guided Jeep tour.
  • Horseback Riding: See the beautiful landscape of Kaibab Plateau on horseback.
  • Stargazing: With its clear night skies, Kaibab Plateau is the perfect place to enjoy stargazing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kaibab Plateau

Day 1: Visit North Rim of the Grand Canyon and hike Bright Angel Point Trail. Day 2: Explore the Kaibab National Forest and visit Jacob Lake. Day 3: Go on a guided Jeep tour of the plateau. Day 4: Take a helicopter tour of the Grand Canyon and Kaibab Plateau. Day 5: Go horseback riding on a scenic trail. Day 6: Explore Tusayan Museum and Ruin and Buckskinner Park. Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at Marble Viewpoint or go fishing at Jacob Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaibab Plateau

Must-Try Dishes

Kaibab Plateau offers a range of dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining restaurants. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Navajo Tacos: A popular dish made with fry bread, ground beef, beans, lettuce, cheese, and salsa.
  • Stuffed Trout: A local specialty, this dish features a trout stuffed with herbs, garlic, and lemon.
  • Horns Over Heels Burger: This burger is made with locally sourced beef, goat cheese, and jalapeño relish.

Local Beverages

Try one of these locally made beverages during your visit:

  • Juniper Berry Tea: This tea is made with crushed juniper berries and is a common drink in the Southwest.
  • Arizona Wine: Arizona has a developing wine industry, with wineries located throughout the region.

Where to Stay in Kaibab Plateau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Kaibab Plateau has something to offer. Here are a few options:

  • Grand Canyon Lodge: Located on the North Rim of the Grand Canyon, this lodge offers stunning views and comfortable accommodations.
  • Jacob Lake Inn: A rustic inn that offers rooms, cabins, and campgrounds.
  • Kaibab Lodge: A historic lodge that offers beautiful views and rustic accommodations.

What to Buy in Kaibab Plateau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a souvenir from your trip, here are a few ideas:

  • Navajo Blankets: Beautifully woven blankets made by Navajo artisans.
  • Kachina Dolls: Colorful dolls made by the Hopi tribe.
  • Juniper Berry Products: Products made with juniper berries, such as soap, candles, and tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Kaibab Plateau

The Kaibab Plateau has a rich cultural history, with the Hopi and Navajo tribes being two of the most prominent groups. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly and asking for permission before taking photographs of tribal members.

Best Time to Visit Kaibab Plateau

The best time to visit Kaibab Plateau is from May to September, when the weather is warm and outdoor recreational opportunities are plentiful. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional thunderstorms during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaibab Plateau

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Stay on designated paths and trails.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of tribal members without permission.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Approach or disturb wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaibab Plateau

Visitors to Kaibab Plateau should be aware of the potential hazards of outdoor activities, such as hiking and horseback riding. It’s important to stay on designated trails and follow all safety guidelines.

Health in Kaibab Plateau

Visitors should also be aware of the potential health risks of high altitude. It’s important to stay hydrated, avoid overexertion, and be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness.

Getting to Kaibab Plateau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaibab Plateau

The closest major airport to Kaibab Plateau is Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, which is approximately a 4-hour drive from the plateau.

Bus Stations Near Kaibab Plateau

There are no bus stations near Kaibab Plateau.

Other Options

The best way to get around Kaibab Plateau is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Kaibab Plateau

The best way to get around Kaibab Plateau is by car or on foot. Many of the attractions and recreational opportunities are located in remote areas, so having a car is essential.

Where to Go Next from Kaibab Plateau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your exploration of the Southwest, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument: A stunning national monument in Utah with beautiful hiking and scenic drives.
  • Monument Valley: A unique geological formation that has been featured in many Western movies.
  • Bryce Canyon National Park: A beautiful national park known for its colorful hoodoos and natural amphitheaters.

Conclusion

Kaibab Plateau offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural history, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Southwest. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Kaibab Plateau has something to offer everyone.
