Introduction to Tempe

Located in the Greater Phoenix area of Arizona, Tempe is a vibrant city known for its outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and bustling nightlife. Home to Arizona State University, Tempe attracts visitors from all over the world who come to explore its museums, hiking trails, and unique desert landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Tempe

With so much to do and see in Tempe, it can be difficult to know where to start. Here is a list of must-see attractions in Tempe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • ASU Art Museum: This museum is dedicated to contemporary art and features works from local and international artists.

  • Tempe History Museum: Discover the history of Tempe and its surrounding areas in this museum that features interactive exhibits and displays.

  • Pueblo Grande Museum: Located in nearby Phoenix, this museum features exhibits about the ancient Hohokam culture and its impact on the region.

Natural Attractions of Tempe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Papago Park: This park is a popular destination for hiking, rock climbing, and exploring the unique desert landscapes of Arizona.

  • Hole in the Rock: Located within Papago Park, this natural sandstone formation offers stunning views of Tempe and the surrounding area.

  • Hayden Butte Preserve: This preserve is a popular destination for hiking and offers breathtaking views of Tempe and Arizona State University.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tempe

If you love to hike or bike, Tempe has plenty of trails and routes to explore. Here are a few of the best:

  • Tempe Town Lake path: This flat, paved trail runs along the scenic Tempe Town Lake and offers stunning views of the city.

  • Kiwanis Park: This park offers several trails for hiking and biking, including the popular 2.5-mile trail around the lake.

  • South Mountain Park and Preserve: Located in nearby Phoenix, this park has over 50 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, including the popular Beverly Canyon Trail.

Best Things to Do in Tempe: Activities and Tours

Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Tempe has something to offer. Here are a few of the best things to do in Tempe:

  • Hot air balloon ride: Experience the beauty of Arizona from above with a hot air balloon ride over Tempe and the surrounding area.

  • Tempe Center for the Arts: Catch a show, concert, or performance at this state-of-the-art venue that features local and international artists.

  • Tempe Marketplace: Shop, dine, and be entertained at this popular outdoor mall that features over 75 stores and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tempe

If you have a week to explore Tempe, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Tempe Town Lake path and visit the ASU Art Museum.

Day 2: Hike in Papago Park and visit the Pueblo Grande Museum in Phoenix.

Day 3: Relax at Kiwanis Park and catch a show at the Tempe Center for the Arts.

Day 4: Explore downtown Tempe and dine at one of the city’s many restaurants.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride and visit the Tempe History Museum.

Day 6: Hike in South Mountain Park and Preserve and explore the local food and drink scene.

Day 7: Visit Hayden Butte Preserve and do some shopping at Tempe Marketplace.

Food and Drink Scene of Tempe

Tempe is known for its vibrant food and drink scene that features everything from local favorites to international cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Habanero’s Carne Asada burrito: This local favorite features tender, grilled beef wrapped in a large flour tortilla.

  • Nocawich’s Chicken Schnitzel sandwich: This restaurant offers a unique take on the classic German dish with a fried chicken sandwich topped with arugula and lemon-mustard aioli.

  • Cornish Pasty Co.’s Cornish Pasty: This local chain offers a traditional meat-filled pastry that dates back to medieval England.

Local Beverages

  • Four Peaks Brewing Company: This local brewery serves up a variety of beers, including the popular Kilt Lifter Scottish-style ale.

  • Cartel Coffee Lab: This local coffee shop roasts its beans on-site and offers a variety of unique flavors and blends.

  • Topo Chico: This Mexican sparkling water is a popular choice among locals and can be found at most restaurants and stores in Tempe.

Where to Stay in Tempe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tempe offers a variety of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are a few of the best places to stay in Tempe:

  • Graduate Tempe: This boutique hotel is located in downtown Tempe and features retro decor and modern amenities.

  • Hostelling International Phoenix – The Metcalf House: This hostel offers affordable accommodations and is located within walking distance of downtown Tempe.

  • Tempe Mission Palms: This upscale hotel is located in the heart of Tempe and features comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and a variety of on-site dining options.

What to Buy in Tempe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Tempe has plenty of options. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Tempe Marketplace: This outdoor mall features over 75 stores and restaurants, including many local boutiques and shops.

  • Changing Hands Bookstore: This independent bookstore offers a variety of books by local authors, as well as unique gifts and souvenirs.

  • Zia Records: This local record store offers a variety of vinyl records, CDs, and DVDs for music and movie lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Tempe

Tempe has a unique culture that is shaped by its history, geography, and people. Visitors to Tempe should be aware of the following customs and traditions:

  • Sun protection: Tempe is located in the desert and can get extremely hot, so it is important to wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses while exploring the city.

  • Respect for the environment: Tempe is home to a variety of unique plant and animal species, so visitors should be respectful of the environment and avoid littering or damaging natural habitats.

  • Appreciation of the arts: Tempe has a thriving arts scene, and visitors are encouraged to attend local performances, festivals, and events.

Best Time to Visit Tempe

The best time to visit Tempe is during the fall and winter months, when temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers in Tempe can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching triple digits, so visitors should be prepared for high temperatures if visiting during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tempe

Tempe is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are a few tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any city, Tempe has areas that are safer than others, so be aware of your surroundings and avoid areas that seem unsafe.

  • Travel in groups: It is always safer to travel in groups, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas.

  • Use common sense: Use common sense and good judgment when traveling in Tempe, and be aware of your belongings and surroundings at all times.

Health in Tempe

Tempe is located in the desert, so visitors should be prepared for the dry air and high temperatures. It is important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen and protective clothing while exploring the city.

Getting to Tempe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tempe

The closest airport to Tempe is Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, which is located just four miles from downtown Tempe.

Bus Stations Near Tempe

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in downtown Phoenix, which is located just 15 minutes from Tempe.

Other Options

Tempe is easily accessible by car from Phoenix and the surrounding areas. There are also several taxi and ride-sharing services available in Tempe for those who do not have access to a car.

How to Get Around in Tempe

Tempe is a walkable city, with many attractions, restaurants, and shops located within walking distance of each other. There are also several bike rental companies in Tempe for those who prefer to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Tempe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
