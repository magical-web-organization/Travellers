Introduction to Peoria

Located in the northwestern region of the Greater Phoenix area, Peoria is a charming city with a rich history and vibrant culture. Visitors can explore a variety of attractions in the area, from museums and natural wonders to hiking and biking trails. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or an extended stay, Peoria offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Peoria

Peoria is home to a variety of top-notch attractions, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arizona Broadway Theatre: Enjoy a night out at this beloved dinner theatre, which hosts a variety of Broadway shows throughout the year.
  • Peoria Center for the Performing Arts: Located in downtown Peoria, this cultural hub hosts a variety of musical and theatrical performances.
  • Pioneer Living History Museum: Explore Arizona’s frontier history at this living history museum, which features historic buildings and exhibits.
  • Rio Vista Recreation Center: This community center offers a variety of classes and activities for visitors of all ages, including dance, art, and fitness classes.

Natural Attractions of Peoria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Pleasant Regional Park: This scenic park offers a variety of outdoor activities, from boating and fishing to hiking and camping.
  • White Tank Mountains Regional Park: Explore the natural beauty of the Sonoran Desert at this popular outdoor recreation area, which features miles of hiking and biking trails.
  • New River Trail: This scenic trail follows the path of the New River and offers stunning views of the surrounding desert landscape.
  • West Wing Mountain Preserve: Enjoy a challenging hike or a leisurely stroll at this mountain preserve, which features a variety of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peoria

Peoria offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore, including:

  • Sunrise Mountain Trail: This challenging trail offers stunning views of the Sonoran Desert and the Phoenix metropolitan area.
  • Black Canyon Trail: Explore the natural beauty of the region on this scenic mountain biking trail, which runs for more than 75 miles.
  • Acoma Trail: This moderate hiking trail offers beautiful views of the surrounding desert landscape and is accessible year-round.
  • Paloma Trail: Located in the White Tank Mountains Regional Park, this easy trail is perfect for families and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Peoria: Activities and Tours

Peoria offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors, including:

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Soar above the desert landscape on a hot air balloon ride, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and wildlife.
  • Segway Tours: Explore the city in style on a Segway tour, which takes visitors to top attractions and hidden gems throughout Peoria.
  • Wine Tasting: Enjoy a delicious variety of local wines at one of the region’s many wineries and tasting rooms, which offer tastings, tours, and more.
  • Golf: Peoria is home to dozens of championship golf courses, making it a top destination for golf enthusiasts.

  • Start your trip off with a visit to Lake Pleasant Regional Park, where you can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating. -Day 2: Learn About Arizona’s Frontier History Visit Pioneer Living History Museum to immerse yourself in Arizona’s frontier history and see historic buildings and exhibits. -Day 3: Explore the Cultural Scene of Peoria Visit the Peoria Center for the Performing Arts to catch a theatrical or musical performance, then head to Arizona Broadway Theatre for dinner and a show. -Day 4: Hiking Trails and Mountain Views Take on the Sunrise Mountain Trail or Paloma Trail for epic views of the Sonoran Desert and surrounding mountains. -Day 5: Hot Air Balloon Ride Take an early-morning hot air balloon ride for stunning views of the desert landscape and wildlife. -Day 6: Golfing and Wine Tasting Spend your day enjoying a round of golf at one of Peoria’s many championship golf courses, then visit a local winery or tasting room for a sampling of Arizona’s best wines. -Day 7: Relax and Recharge Spend your final day in Peoria relaxing and recharging at one of the many local parks or spas in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Peoria

Peoria offers a variety of dining and drinking options, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arizona BBQ: Indulge in delicious, slow-cooked barbecue dishes at one of Peoria’s many BBQ restaurants.
  • Mexican Cuisine: Sample authentic Mexican cuisine at one of the region’s many Mexican restaurants, which offer traditional dishes like tacos, burritos, and enchiladas.
  • Southwestern Cuisine: Try Southwestern-inspired dishes like green chili stew and mesquite-grilled steaks at a local restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Arizona Wine: Sample the region’s best wines at one of Peoria’s many wineries and tasting rooms, which offer tastings, tours, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Peoria

Peoria is home to a rich and diverse cultural scene, influenced by its Native American and Hispanic heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and should take the time to learn about the region’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Peoria

The best time to visit Peoria is in the winter and spring, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers in Peoria can be extremely hot, with temperatures averaging in the 90s and above.
