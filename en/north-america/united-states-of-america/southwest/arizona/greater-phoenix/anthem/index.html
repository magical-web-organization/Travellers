Introduction to Anthem

Anthem is a beautiful community located in the northernmost part of Phoenix, Arizona. It is often considered as a hidden gem of Arizona, with stunning natural landscapes, world-class amenities, and a thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends or family, you’re bound to have a great time in Anthem. Below is a comprehensive guide to everything you need to know about this lovely destination.

List of Places to Visit in Anthem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Anthem Veterans Memorial: This monument is a tribute to the service and sacrifices of the United States armed forces. The monument consists of five granite pillars representing each branch of the military, a plaza, and a large American flag.

  • Pioneer Living History Museum: This living history museum showcases Arizona’s pioneer history through exhibits and live demonstrations. Visitors can experience daily life in the late 1800s, explore historic buildings, and enjoy numerous events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Anthem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hiking and Biking Trails: Anthem boasts of numerous hiking, biking and horseback riding trails. Some of the popular hiking trails include Daisy Mountain, Anthem Trailhead, and Hieroglyphic Trail.

  • Lake Pleasant: This beautiful lake located just 15 miles from Anthem offers a range of outdoor activities including boating, fishing, kayaking, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anthem

Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Anthem:

  • Daisy Mountain Trail: This 3.7-mile hike offers some fantastic views of the surrounding landscape, including the Sonoran Desert, the Bradshaw Mountains, and Anthem itself.

  • Anthem Trailhead: This 3.5-mile hike offers a scenic trek through the desert landscape with moderate to low difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Anthem: Activities and Tours

  • Golfing: Anthem has two world-class golf courses, The Persimmon and The Ironwood, which are perfect for anyone who loves a good game of golf.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience Anthem’s stunning desert views and natural beauty from high up in the sky with a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Anthem

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sizzling Fajitas - A flavorful meal at Los Reyes De La Torta
  • Vietnamese Spring Rolls - The perfect appetizer at Street Cafe and Coffee Lounge
  • Burgers - The delicious burgers at Haymaker are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Sunrise Amber Ale - A refreshing beer at Helton Brewing Company
  • The Blitzkrieg Peppermint Schnapps - A specialty drink at The Horned Lizard

Where to Stay in Anthem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some great options for accommodation in Anthem:

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: A beautiful hotel with comfortable rooms, great amenities, and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Anthem RV Park: A perfect option for travelers on a camping trip looking for a place to stay.

What to Buy in Anthem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Arizona Native Jewelry: These unique pieces of jewelry make the perfect souvenir.

  • Succulent Plants: Take home a piece of Arizona with you by buying some succulent plants from local plant nurseries.

Local Culture and Customs of Anthem

Anthem has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and culture. A small tip or token of appreciation is always appreciated when visiting local shops and restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Anthem

The best time to visit Anthem is between October and April when the temperatures are milder, making it an ideal time for hiking and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anthem

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Visit Lake Pleasant for a fun day of water activities.
  • Go on a hot air balloon ride to experience the beauty of Anthem from the skies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.
  • Ignore the importance of sun protection.
  • Leave your pets in a parked car, as temperatures can be dangerously high.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anthem

Anthem is generally a very safe place for tourists. Visitors are advised to be careful when participating in outdoor activities, especially in extreme weather conditions.

Health in Anthem

Visitors are encouraged to stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun as temperatures in the area can be quite high.

Getting to Anthem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Anthem

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport: The largest airport near Anthem is located about 40 miles from Anthem and offers several transportation options, including rental cars and taxis.

Bus Stations Near Anthem

  • White Tank Mtns & Yankee E Valley View: This bus station offers transportation to and from Anthem and its surrounding areas.

Other Options

Rental cars are a popular way for visitors to get around Anthem and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Anthem

The best way to get around Anthem is by car, as the area is quite spread out. Visitors can also rent bikes or participate in guided tours if they prefer.

Where to Go Next from Anthem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Canyon National Park: One of the seven natural wonders of the world, Grand Canyon National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Arizona.

  • Sedona: A stunning destination just a few hours’ drive from Anthem, Sedona is known for its red rock formations and vibrant art scene.

Conclusion

Anthem is a hidden gem in Arizona with stunning natural landscapes, a thriving cultural scene, and world-class amenities. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or just a relaxing getaway, Anthem has something to offer. This comprehensive guide should help you plan the perfect trip to Anthem, Arizona. So pack your bags, grab your sunscreen, and get ready to experience all that Anthem has to offer!
