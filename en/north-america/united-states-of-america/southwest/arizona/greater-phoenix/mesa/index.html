Introduction to Mesa

Located in Greater Phoenix, Mesa is a city in Arizona, United States of America. It is known for its warm and sunny weather, stunning desert landscapes, and outdoor recreational activities. Mesa offers a perfect blend of history, culture, adventure, and relaxation to its visitors. With a population of more than 500,000 people, Mesa is the third-largest city in Arizona and a hub for arts, culture, and higher education.

List of Places to Visit in Mesa

There’s no shortage of things to do and see in Mesa. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Mesa has something for everyone. Here is a list of top attractions in Mesa.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arizona Museum of Natural History - This museum showcases the natural history and cultural heritage of Arizona and the Southwest. Visitors can explore dinosaur fossils, bones, and tracks; ancient civilizations; and Western history exhibits.
  • Mesa Arts Center – This is the largest performing and visual arts complex in Arizona and attracts visitors from all over the world. The center offers a diverse calendar of concerts, theatre performances, art exhibitions, classes, and workshops.
  • Mesa Historical Museum – This museum exhibits the history and heritage of Mesa. Visitors can see the display of ancient artifacts, interactive exhibits, and historic buildings.
  • Rose Garden at Mesa Commnity College - This flower garden boasts 9000 rose bushes with more than 1200 varieties. Visitors can explore this beautiful garden throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Mesa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Usery Mountain Regional Park - This park offers more than 29 miles of hiking and biking trails, camping grounds, archery range, picnic areas and scenic views of the mountain.
  • Tonto National Forest- Spanning 3 million acres, Tonto National Forest have an extensive trail system for hiking, cycling, and horse riding as well as boating, fishing, and camping grounds.
  • Salt River – This waterway offers various water activities including rafting, tubing, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Superstition Mountain – This mountain boasts scenic trails for hiking and an impressive view of the Sonoran Desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mesa

Mesa is known for its great trails and beautiful nature where pedestrians and biking enthusiasts can enjoy the scenery. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes of Mesa.

  • Salt River Trail - 11.8 miles of hiking on single and double-track trails, running alongside the Salt River.
  • Usery Pass Trail- A 3.2-mile trail with stunning views of the Sonoran Desert and the Superstition Mountains. It is perfect for both hikers and bikers.
  • Hawes Trail System - This system offers 19 miles of trails, with various routes for mountain bikers and hikers, including easy and challenging options.

Best Things to Do in Mesa: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor activities, there are many other things visitors can do in Mesa. These are some of the best things to do in Mesa:

  • Hot-air balloon ride – A hot air balloon ride is a must-try activity in Mesa to experience a stunning bird’s eye view of the city and the desert landscape.
  • Arizona food tour – Take a food tour to experience Mesa’s unique dining scene. Enjoy the local cuisines, arts, and culture.
  • Desert Botanical Gardens – This place offers a beautiful collection of desert plants, with guided tours led by expert botanists.
  • Wild West jeep tour – Take a ride through the desert of the Western Frontier on a jeep tour. Explore the flora, fauna, and rugged landscapes of Mesa.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mesa

If you’re planning to spend a week in Mesa, you can follow this week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip.

Day 1 - Visit the Arizona Museum of Natural History and spend the night in one of Mesa’s hotels.

Day 2 - Visit the Mesa Arts Center in the morning and then stroll around the Rose Garden at Mesa Community College in the evening.

Day 3 - Go for a hike and picnic at Usery Mountain Regional Park and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 4 - Explore the Tonto National Forest and experience kayaking and tubing on the Salt River.

Day 5 - Visit the Superstition Mountains and hike the challenging trails.

Day 6 - Enjoy a hot air balloon ride over Mesa and have a delicious cuisine of the town in the evening.

Day 7 - Take a guided tour of the Wild West in a jeep on your final day before leaving Mesa.

Food and Drink Scene of Mesa

Mesa is known for its delicious and diverse dining scene. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages in Mesa.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chimichangas - This deep-fried burrito is a Mesa specialty, filled with meat and vegetables.
  • Navajo Tacos – It is a fried dough with meat and vegetables, and is a staple of the Native American cuisine.
  • Green Chile Burritos - This dish is similar to Chimichanga but with a green chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Arizona wine - Mesa has a local wine scene with two wineries: Superstition Meadery and Carlson Creek Vineyard.
  • Craft beer – Mesa has many breweries, making it a hotspot for craft beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Mesa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mesa offers a range of options for accommodations, with both budget and luxury options. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mesa:

  • Sheraton Mesa Hotel at Wrigleyville West – A luxurious hotel with excellent amenities, including a rooftop pool.
  • Best Western Legacy Inn & Suites – A great option for families with an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Saguaro Lake Guest Ranch – A guesthouse for nature lovers, at Tonto National Park, with comfortable rooms, outdoor activities, and mountain views.

What to Buy in Mesa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mesa has a wide range of local products and souvenirs that visitors can bring home. Here are some of the best places to shop in Mesa:

  • Mesa Market Place Swap Meet – A market where visitors can find local handmade crafts, art, clothes, and pottery.
  • Superstition Farm – Visitors can grab some freshly made dairy products and learn about farm life and animals.

Local Culture and Customs of Mesa

Mesa has a unique cultural heritage and customs. The Native American Hohokam peoples originally inhabited the land, and now Mesa has a rich heritage of Hispanic and Western cultures. Visitors should be respectful of the culture while they enjoy their time there.

Best Time to Visit Mesa

The best time to visit Mesa is from March to May and September to November, when the temperatures are pleasant and the crowds are fewer than during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mesa

Do:

  • Respect the culture and local customs.
  • Follow the safety guidelines when hiking and exploring the outdoors.
  • Enjoy the local cuisines.

Don’t:

  • Litter and leave trash in the trails.
  • Ignore the warnings when it comes to outdoor safety.
  • Speak ill of the local customs and cultures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mesa

Mesa is a safe city, but it is essential to follow the safety measures and follow the guidelines when you are exploring the trails and outdoors. Beware of scorpions and rattlesnakes while hiking.

Health in Mesa

Mesa has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can find medical attention without any difficulty.

Getting to Mesa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Mesa by plane, train, or car. Here are some of the options and logistics:

Airports Near Mesa

The nearest airport is Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport, which is served by many airlines.

Bus Stations Near Mesa

Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Phoenix, which is accessible from Mesa.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Mesa by car via the major highways that run through the city.

How to Get Around in Mesa

Visitors can get around Mesa by renting a car, taking a taxi, using Uber or Lyft, or taking the bus.

Where to Go Next from Mesa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can add nearby destinations and excursions to their Mesa itinerary, like:

  • Phoenix – The capital of Arizona is a short drive from Mesa and offers unique dining, entertainment, and cultural experiences.
  • Sedona – It is located a two-hour drive from Mesa and is known for its beautiful landscapes, red mountains, and hiking trails.
  • Scottsdale – It’s located 20 minutes from Mesa and is known for its luxury spas, golf courses, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Mesa offers a perfect blend of history, culture, adventure, and relaxation to its visitors. With stunning desert landscapes, outdoor recreational activities, and a diverse dining scene, it is a must-visit destination for those who want to explore the Southwest.
