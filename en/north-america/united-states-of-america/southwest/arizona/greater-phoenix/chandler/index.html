Chandler, a suburb of Phoenix, is often overshadowed by the bigger tourist destinations in Arizona. However, this charming city has its own unique appeal, featuring a vibrant downtown, picturesque parks, and top-class museums. With a range of things to see and do, Chandler is perfect for a relaxing weekend getaway or a week-long vacation. Get ready to explore the best of Chandler with our ultimate travel guide.

Introduction to Chandler

Located in the Southwest region of the United States, around 20 miles southeast of Phoenix, Chandler is a city in Maricopa County, Arizona. Known for its tech industry and affluent lifestyle, Chandler has recently emerged as a popular tourist destination in the state. Travelers often flock to Chandler for its vibrant downtown, which is home to numerous restaurants, bars, and shops. Chandler also boasts of several parks and natural areas, providing visitors with opportunities for outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Chandler

Chandler is a city that is rich in culture, and it has a range of attractions to suit all interests. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or food, Chandler has something for you.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chandler has several museums and historical places that offer insight into the city’s rich heritage. The following are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Chandler:

  • Arizona Railway Museum: This museum showcases the history of Arizona’s railroads, including a range of old trains and locomotives.

  • Chandler Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the city. It features a range of exhibits, including artifacts and photographs from Chandler’s past.

  • Tumbleweed Ranch Museum: This living history museum reflects Chandler’s agricultural heritage and offers a glimpse into the lives of early settlers in the city.

Natural Attractions of Chandler: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chandler is blessed with scenic natural areas that offer opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. The following are some of the natural attractions you should check out:

  • San Tan Mountain Regional Park: This park is a popular destination for hiking and offers breathtaking views of the valley. It features several trails of varying difficulties.

  • Veterans Oasis Park: This park is notable for its wetlands and diverse range of wildlife, making it an ideal location for bird watching.

  • Paseo Vista Recreation Area: This park features a range of outdoor activities, including disc golf, hiking, and mountain biking, with a stunning view of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chandler

Chandler has several trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following are some of the popular spots:

  • The Chandler Bike Path: This bike path is a popular destination for both locals and visitors. It features several paved trails, and it’s perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a morning jog.

  • San Tan Mountain Regional Park: As mentioned earlier, this park features several trails, including a rock formation hike leading to a scenic 360-degree view of Phoenix.

  • Desert Breeze Park: This park features a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and jogging trails, fishing ponds, and playgrounds.

Best Things to Do in Chandler: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or as part of a family, Chandler has various activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of the most popular things to do in Chandler:

  • Koli Equestrian Center: At Koli Equestrian Center, you’ll have the opportunity to ride horses through the Sonoran Desert.

  • RAWhide Western Town and Event Center: This attraction offers an authentic western experience with stagecoach rides, gunfights, and a petting zoo.

  • Arizona Wine Tours: For wine enthusiasts, Arizona offers award-winning vineyards, and the city of Chandler is no exception. Arizona Wine Tours arrange tours for wine lovers to the vineyards around Chandler.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chandler

For visitors who have enough time, Chandler features enough attractions and activities to fill up an entire week. Here is a suggested week-long itinerary you may find helpful:

Day 1 Arrive in Chandler and explore downtown restaurants and shops
Day 2 Visit the Tumbleweed Ranch Museum, followed by a bike ride at San Tan Mountain Regional Park
Day 3 Take a Helicopter Tour of Chandler and surrounding areas
Day 4 Go for a hike at Paseo Vista Recreation Area, then experience the nightlife in downtown Chandler
Day 5 Take a day trip to Sedona, Arizona
Day 6 Wine tours and tasting around Chandler, and explore the art galleries in downtown Chandler
Day 7 Watch a performance at the Chandler Center for the Arts, then relax at Veterans Oasis Park or any other park near you

Food and Drink Scene of Chandler

Chandler has a diverse food scene, featuring local restaurants serving authentic dishes from around the world. Here are some dishes that you simply must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sonoran-Style Hot Dog: A bacon-wrapped hotdog paraded with beans, chopped tomatoes, onions, jalapeño salsa, and a drizzle of ketchup and mayo, usually served in a Mexican bread called bolillo.

  • Patio Beef Sandwich: This is a beef sandwich with Italian spices and pickled peppers, amongst other flavorful ingredients.

  • Fish Tacos: Made with fresh fish, these tacos come with different options such as chipotle, avocado, and lime cream.

Local Beverages

  • Four Peaks Brewing Company: This brewery offers a range of local craft beers, including the popular Kilt Lifter Scotch Ale.

  • San Tan Brewing Company: This brewery is the second-largest brewing facility in Arizona, and it offers a wide range of beers, including seasonal specialties.

  • Vintage 95 Wine Lounge: The lounge offers a range of wine from all over the world, along with charcuterie boards and fine cheeses.

Where to Stay in Chandler: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chandler has several accommodations options to suit different travel preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Chandler are:

  • Sheraton Grand at Wild Horse Pass: This luxury resort is set amid the tranquil surroundings of Gila River, offering luxurious rooms, a 36-hole golf course, and a full-service spa.

  • Hyatt Place Phoenix/Chandler-Fashion Center: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Chandler, with easy access to major attractions and shopping centers.

  • Crowne Plaza Phoenix-Chandler Golf Resort: The resort is famous for its four-star golf course, and it offers easy access to Chandler’s cultural attractions. It’s a perfect place to mix business with pleasure.

What to Buy in Chandler: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chandler boasts of several shopping centers, including malls, boutiques, and antique stores. If you’re looking to pick up souvenirs or local products, consider visiting the following places:

  • Historic Downtown Chandler: This area is home to several unique shops and boutiques, offering everything from vintage clothing to handcrafted jewelry.

  • Chandler Fashion Center: This mall offers a range of shopping options, including well-known brands and local stores.

  • Bashas’: Bashas is Chandler’s locally-owned grocery chain offering local delicacies such as jerky and jams at affordable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Chandler

Chandler is a friendly city, and visitors can expect a warm welcome from locals. It’s important to respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing conservatively and avoiding loud or disruptive behavior in public areas. Chandler is a relatively safe city, but it’s important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

Best Time to Visit Chandler

The best time to visit Chandler is during the spring (March - May) or the fall (September - November). These seasons offer mild temperatures, with fewer crowds and lower prices for accommodations and attractions. However, if you prefer warm weather, you can visit Chandler during the summer (June - August), although they will experience high temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chandler

Do:

  • Take a stroll along the downtown area and attend community events.
  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Be mindful of the weather and stay hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Litter or spitting in public places as it is considered a public annoyance and is considered punishable under local law.
  • Enter a private property without permission or trespassing public parks after dusk hours.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chandler

Chandler is considered a safe city, with low crime rates, but it’s essential to take precautions when visiting a new destination. Follow general safety tips like locking your doors and windows and not leaving valuable items in sight in your car.

Health in Chandler

Chandler is known for its high-quality healthcare facilities. In case of any health emergency during your stay, head out to Chandler Regional Medical Center.

Getting to Chandler: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chandler is conveniently located, with several transportation options available to visitors. Some of the transportation options include:

Airports Near Chandler

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport: This is the closest international airport to Chandler, located approximately 20 miles from Chandler.

  • Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport: This airport offers commercial flights from other airports located in western states, such as California and Nevada. The airport is located about 15 miles from the center of Chandler.

Bus Stations Near Chandler

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is situated at 1435 S. 19th Avenue, Phoenix, which is around 25 miles from Chandler.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Chandler has several car rental companies available at the airport and other locations around the city. You may consider renting a car to explore the surrounding areas.

  • Cab Services: Cab services like Uber and Lyft are also available in Chandler for comfortable and personalized rides.

How to Get Around in Chandler

Chandler is a well-connected city, with a range of transportation options available to visitors, including buses, taxis, and rideshare services like Uber and Lyft. If you’re looking to explore the city, consider renting a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from Chandler: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore beyond Chandler, some of the nearby destinations you may consider visiting include:

  • Scottsdale: Located approximately 25 miles from Chandler, Scottsdale offers a range of cultural attractions, including the Museum of the West and the acclaimed Taliesin West architecture studio complex.

  • Sedona: You can drive to Sedona in less than two hours west of Chandler. Sedona is in the heart of the Red Rock Country and is famous for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and spiritual centers.

  • Grand Canyon National Park: This is a must-visit destination when in Arizona. It is a four-hour drive from Chandler, which requires a separate stay to enjoy the full epicness of the Grand Canyon.

Conclusion

Chandler is a hidden gem that offers visitors a range of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to explore the museums, taste the local cuisine, or relax in the parks, Chandler has something to offer everyone. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has given you a glimpse of what Chandler has to offer and that you’re inspired to plan your next trip soon!
