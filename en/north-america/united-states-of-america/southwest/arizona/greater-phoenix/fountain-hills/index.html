Introduction to Fountain Hills

Fountain Hills is a charming town located in Greater Phoenix, Arizona, with a population of around 25,000 people. It’s a popular retirement community that attracts visitors with its diverse range of cultural attractions, beautiful natural scenery, and outdoor adventures. The town is most famous for its beautiful fountain, which is one of the tallest in the world, and shoots water up to 560 feet in the air. The town boasts an impressive food scene, with restaurants offering everything from Southwestern cuisine to international delicacies.

List of Places to Visit in Fountain Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fountain Hills Public Art (various locations) - a stunning collection of public art pieces located throughout the town, including sculptures, fountains, and murals.
  • River of Time Museum - a must-visit museum showcasing the history and heritage of Fountain Hills and the surrounding Verde River Valley.
  • Fountain Hills Theater - a community theater showcasing live productions of musicals, plays, and various other performances.

Natural Attractions of Fountain Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • McDowell Mountain Regional Park - a large park with hiking, camping, and mountain biking trails, and world-class bird-watching opportunities.
  • Fountain Park - where the famous fountain can be found, alongside beautiful walking and hiking trails.
  • Sonoran Preserve - a beautiful natural preserve with over 36,000 acres of scenic desert landscapes, hiking trails, biking trails, and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fountain Hills

Fountain Hills has no shortage of great hiking and biking trails, from easy, scenic walks to challenging treks through rugged terrain. Some of the best include:

  • Sunrise Trail - a popular hiking trail that takes you through the scenic McDowell Mountains, with stunning views of the surrounding desert landscapes.
  • Fountain Hills Loop Trail - a lovely walk taking you around the town’s iconic fountain, through lush greenspaces, and offering great views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Saguaro Trail - a challenging hike through rugged terrain, with beautiful views of the surrounding desert.

Best Things to Do in Fountain Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Take a hot air balloon ride to get a bird’s eye view of the stunning desert landscapes and the town’s famous fountain.
  • Explore the numerous art galleries and studios located throughout the town, including the Fountain Hills Artists’ Gallery.
  • Go wine tasting at the nearby Arizona Wine Collective, where you can sample local wines and discover the rich history of wine-making in Arizona.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fountain Hills

Day 1: Explore the town’s public art collection, starting with the iconic bronze statue of the famous Fountain Hills founder, Robert P. McCulloch.

Day 2: Take an early morning hike in the majestic McDowell Mountains, followed by lunch at one of the town’s many fantastic restaurants.

Day 3: Visit the River of Time Museum, participate in a guided tour of the town, or take a leisurely stroll around the beautiful Fountain Lake.

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride for a once-in-a-lifetime experience, or head out for a day trip to nearby Sedona for a scenic drive and some fantastic hiking trails.

Day 5: Explore the beautiful Sonoran Preserve, with its stunning desert landscapes and diverse wildlife.

Day 6: Go golfing at one of the beautiful golf courses located in and around Fountain Hills, or take a guided tour of the town’s wine country.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the town’s amazing spas, or hit the shops for some unique souvenirs to bring home from your trip.

Food and Drink Scene of Fountain Hills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chimichangas - a classic Southwestern dish featuring a deep-fried burrito filled with beans, cheese, and your choice of meat.
  • Tacos - another classic dish, with numerous variations on offer, including fish tacos and street-style tacos.
  • Green Chile Stew - a hearty and spicy stew made with roasted green chiles, pork, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Prickly Pear Margaritas - a unique twist on the classic margarita, made with fresh prickly pear juice and tequila.
  • Arizona Wines - Arizona is home to many excellent wineries, with numerous tasting rooms located throughout the region.
  • Craft Beer - Fountain Hills has a thriving craft beer scene, with many excellent breweries and taprooms to choose from.

Where to Stay in Fountain Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • CopperWynd Resort & Club - a luxury resort with stunning views, beautiful accommodations, and a range of amenities, including tennis courts, pools, and a spa.
  • Inn at Eagle Mountain - a boutique hotel located in the heart of Fountain Hills, with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and a range of amenities, including a fitness center and golf course.
  • Fountain Hills Vacation Properties - a range of rental properties available, from cozy apartments to luxurious villas, all with beautiful views and great amenities.

What to Buy in Fountain Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Art - Fountain Hills is located close to numerous Native American communities, and you’ll find a range of beautiful art and crafts at galleries and markets throughout the town.
  • Sonoran Desert Products - bring home a taste of the Southwest with local products made from the unique desert flora, including jams, jellies, and seasoning blends.
  • Handmade Jewelry - find unique and beautiful pieces made by local artisans, including turquoise, silver, and other precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Fountain Hills

Fountain Hills prides itself on its rich cultural heritage, with a strong focus on the arts, history, and connection to the natural environment. The town is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are always happy to share their knowledge and experiences with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Fountain Hills

The best time to visit Fountain Hills is in the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer months can be very hot, but there are still plenty of indoor activities and events to keep you busy. Winter months are also a great time to visit, with cooler temperatures and many outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fountain Hills

Do:

  • Take advantage of the numerous outdoor adventures and scenic vistas located throughout the town and the surrounding area.
  • Explore the diverse range of cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Southwestern specialties and locally crafted beers and wines.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or in restricted areas, such as archaeological sites or protected natural areas.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety warnings, particularly when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fountain Hills

Fountain Hills is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. As with any destination, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect your safety.

Health in Fountain Hills

Fountain Hills has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, with excellent medical care available. Visitors should ensure they have appropriate travel insurance and take any necessary precautions for their individual health needs.

Getting to Fountain Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fountain Hills

The closest airport to Fountain Hills is Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, located about 27 miles away. Mesa Gateway Airport is another option, located about 25 miles away, with flights from several major domestic carriers.

Bus Stations Near Fountain Hills

Several bus stations serve Fountain Hills, including the Mesa Greyhound Station and the Phoenix Bus Station. It’s also possible to take public transportation, including Valley Metro buses and light rail trains.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors, with several major car rental companies located in the area. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

How to Get Around in Fountain Hills

Fountain Hills is a small town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance. Alternatively, visitors can rent a car or take public transportation. Biking is also a popular option, with numerous bike rental shops in the area, and many bike trails and bike lanes available.

Where to Go Next from Fountain Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sedona - a stunning desert town located about two hours’ drive from Fountain Hills, famous for its striking red rock formations and spiritual energy.
  • Jerome - a historic mining town about two hours’ drive from Fountain Hills, perched on the side of a mountain and offering stunning views of the surrounding valley.
  • Phoenix - the largest city in Arizona, located about 30 minutes’ drive from Fountain Hills, with a range of cultural attractions, world-class shopping, and a thriving nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Fountain Hills is a charming and beautiful town with a lot to offer visitors, from rich cultural attractions and outdoor adventures, to fantastic food and drink and excellent accommodations. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adrenaline-filled adventure, Fountain Hills has something for everyone.
