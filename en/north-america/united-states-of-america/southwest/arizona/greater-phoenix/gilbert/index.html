Introduction to Gilbert

Gilbert is a town located in Greater Phoenix, Arizona, in the southwest region of the United States of America. Known for its beautiful natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Gilbert attracts thousands of tourists every year. The town also boasts numerous recreational opportunities, top-notch dining, and excellent shopping options. Whether you’re traveling alone, with friends, or family, Gilbert has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Gilbert

If you’re planning a trip to Gilbert, you’ll want to make sure to include these must-visit places on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gilbert Historical Museum: The museum features historic artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Gilbert’s development over the years.

  2. Hale Centre Theatre: This top-rated community theater showcases some of the best theatrical productions in the state.

Natural Attractions of Gilbert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Riparian Preserve at Water Ranch: Spanning over 110 acres, the preserve offers hiking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and a chance to learn about Gilbert’s natural history.

  2. San Tan Mountain Regional Park: With over 10,000 acres of rugged wilderness, hiking, horseback riding, and mountain biking opportunities are plentiful.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gilbert

The town of Gilbert offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the state. Some of the most popular trails include:

  1. Freestone Park Loop Trail: A beautiful 2.5-mile loop trail that offers excellent views of the surrounding mountains.

  2. Veterans Oasis Park Trail: A 5-mile loop trail that winds through pristine desert landscapes, offering wildlife sightings and scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Gilbert: Activities and Tours

Gilbert has plenty of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Gilbert:

  1. Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the Arizona desert from above, with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes.

  2. Escape Rooms: Challenge your puzzle-solving skills with a fun-filled escape room experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gilbert

Day 1: Visit the Gilbert Historical Museum and Hale Centre Theatre.

Day 2: Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning, followed by a leisurely hike in the afternoon.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring the Riparian Preserve at Water Ranch.

Day 4: Head out to San Tan Mountain Regional Park to hike or mountain bike.

Day 5: Rent a kayak or paddleboat and explore the beautiful waters of Gilbert’s lakes.

Day 6: Enjoy a game of golf at one of Gilbert’s many golf courses.

Day 7: Experience Gilbert’s dining scene, with a visit to one of its top-rated restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Gilbert

Gilbert is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse range of top-notch dining options. Make sure to try these must-try dishes when in Gilbert:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sonoran-style hot dogs: A local favorite, these hot dogs are topped with beans, onions, tomatoes, and other traditional Mexican flavors.

  2. Navajo tacos: A delicious twist on a classic American dish, featuring a frybread base topped with seasoned ground beef and flavorful toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Arizona wines: Gilbert is home to some of the best wineries in the state, featuring rich flavors and a unique character.

  2. Local craft beers: The town boasts numerous breweries that offer a range of unique and delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Gilbert: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gilbert offers numerous accommodations to fit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top-rated options:

  1. Doubletree by Hilton Phoenix - Gilbert: This hotel features luxurious suites, an outdoor pool, and a convenient location close to all of Gilbert’s top attractions.

  2. Hyatt Place Phoenix Gilbert: A high-end hotel that offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and complimentary Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Gilbert: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring back home, Gilbert offers a range of handmade crafts and local products. Some top options include Arizona-made wines and local jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Gilbert

Gilbert has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historic landmarks, museums, and community events. The town prides itself on its strong sense of community, and visitors are encouraged to participate in local events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Gilbert

The peak tourist season in Gilbert is from November to April, when the weather is mild and sunny. The summer months can be extremely hot, but offer fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gilbert

Do:

  1. Do explore the town’s natural landscapes, including its parks, lakes, and hiking trails.

  2. Do immerse yourself in the local culture, by visiting museums and attending community events.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t ignore safety precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

  2. Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and cafes, as it is customary in the U.S.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gilbert

Gilbert is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should exercise caution when hiking or participating in outdoor activities, and avoid isolated areas after dark.

Health in Gilbert

Gilbert has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer high-quality healthcare. It is recommended that visitors purchase travel insurance before their trip to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Gilbert: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gilbert

  1. Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport: The largest airport in the region, located about 20 miles from Gilbert.

  2. Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport: A smaller airport located about 8 miles from Gilbert, which serves several budget airlines.

Bus Stations Near Gilbert

  1. Greyhound: Provides bus services to and from Gilbert.

  2. Local buses: Connect Gilbert to other towns in the region, including Phoenix and Mesa.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are also available in Gilbert.

How to Get Around in Gilbert

The town is easily navigable by foot, bike or car. Renting a car is recommended for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Gilbert: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Phoenix: The state capital and a bustling metropolis, offering a range of cultural attractions, dining and shopping options.

  2. Sedona: A stunning desert town, known for its red rock formations and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Gilbert offers a unique blend of history, outdoor activities, culture, and excellent dining and shopping options. Plan your trip well, and make sure to include some of the must-visit places and activities, to enjoy a truly memorable vacation.
