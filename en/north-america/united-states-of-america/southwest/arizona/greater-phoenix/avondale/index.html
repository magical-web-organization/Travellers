Introduction to Avondale

Avondale is a rising star in the state of Arizona, situated in the Greater Phoenix area. Located in the southwest region of the United States, Avondale offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. This vibrant city has transformed from a farming community to one of the fastest-growing suburban locations in Arizona. With a booming economy and many cultural and natural attractions, Avondale has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Avondale

Avondale boasts a number of amazing attractions for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Avondale Historical Society Museum: This museum offers visitors a chance to explore the history of Avondale through photos, artifacts, and interactive exhibits.

  • Corral West Adventures: Explore the beauty of desert landscapes on a guided horseback ride through the Sonoran Desert. Corral West offers a variety of guided tours and experiences.

Natural Attractions of Avondale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Estrella Mountain Regional Park: This vast park boasts over 30 miles of trails through the Sonoran Desert. Activities include hiking, biking, camping, horseback riding, and wildlife viewing.

  • Phoenix International Raceway: If speed is your thing, head to Phoenix International Raceway for NASCAR races happening throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avondale

Avondale is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in the Phoenix area. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Estrella Mountain Regional Park Hikes: The park boasts a variety of hiking trails for all skill levels, including the Gadsden Trail, which winds through the rugged desert and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Tres Rios Wetlands Bike Path: This is a flat bike path with stunning views of the Estrella Mountains and wildlife. It’s a popular route among bikers and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Avondale: Activities and Tours

  • Wet ‘n’ Wild Phoenix: This 35-acre waterpark offers over 30 rides and attractions, including a wave pool, lazy river, and several adrenaline-inducing water slides.

  • Horseback Rides Near Phoenix: Take a guided horseback ride through the Sonoran Desert and explore the scenic trails of Estrella Mountain Regional Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avondale

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for your visit to Avondale:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Avondale Historical Society Museum, have lunch at the Haymaker Restaurant, and enjoy a guided horseback ride at Corral West Adventures.
Day 2 Explore Tres Rios Wetlands, a popular hiking and biking path, then head to Estrella Mountain Regional Park for a sunset hike on the popular Gadsden Trail.
Day 3 Take a day trip to Phoenix International Raceway to watch a NASCAR race.
Day 4 Enjoy a day at Wet ‘n’ Wild Phoenix water park.
Day 5 Head to the Phoenix Zoo and then explore the Desert Botanical Garden.
Day 6 Take a self-guided walking tour of downtown Avondale, followed by a visit to the popular Marketside at Verrado, where you can buy fresh, locally produced food products.
Day 7 Take a day trip to the Grand Canyon National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Avondale

Avondale’s food and drink scene is definitely worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sonoran Hot Dog: A grilled hot dog wrapped in bacon and served with beans, cheese, and a variety of condiments. A must-try if you’re visiting Greater Phoenix.

  • Chimichangas: This deeply-fried burrito is a classic Southwest cuisine and has been a staple in Arizona for years.

Local Beverages

  • Arizona Wine: Arizona may not be the first place you think of when it comes to wine-tasting, however, it produces some delicious varietals. Make sure to try some local wines during your visit.

  • Beer: Arizona’s craft beer scene has been booming in recent years. Some top breweries in Avondale include Cage Brewing, 8-Bit Aleworks and Complex City Brewing.

Where to Stay in Avondale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avondale has plenty of amazing accommodation options for visitors. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Renaissance Phoenix Glendale Hotel & Spa: This upscale hotel is located near the State Farm Stadium and features two on-site restaurants, a full-service spa, and plenty of amenities.

  • Comfort Suites Phoenix-Glendale: This mid-range hotel offers air-conditioned rooms with a free breakfast, outdoor pool, and fitness center.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Phoenix Avondale: This budget-friendly hotel features a pool, fitness center, and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Avondale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products, here are some things to consider buying during your visit:

  • Native American Crafts and Jewelry: Arizona is known for its rich Native American history, and you can buy a variety of handmade crafts and jewelry from local artisans.

  • Chiltepines: A type of chili pepper native to the Sonoran Desert, they’re often used to add flavor and spice to many Arizona dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Avondale

The Hispanic and Native American cultures have greatly influenced the customs and traditions of Avondale. Visitors are encouraged to respect the customs of the local population, including the use of appropriate language and clothing. The culture in Avondale is a blend of traditional and modern, and visitors should try to experience and appreciate both aspects of the city during their visit.

Best Time to Visit Avondale

The best time to visit Avondale is during the fall and winter season, which runs from late October to early April. During this time, the weather is milder, with less humidity and cooler temperatures. Summer months in Avondale can be quite hot, often reaching triple digits.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avondale

Do:

  • Respect the environment: Preserve the natural surroundings that make Avondale beautiful. Stick to designated trails, take your trash with you and be mindful of wildlife.

  • Try the local cuisine: Arizona’s food scene is unique, so take advantage of the diverse restaurants and food truck offerings.

Don’t:

  • Litter: It may seem simple, but littering is a serious offence in Avondale, and can result in fines and even jail time.

  • Hike without proper gear: The rugged landscape of the Sonoran Desert can be dangerous for inexperienced hikers. Make sure you have proper gear and bring plenty of water and snacks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avondale

Generally, Avondale is a safe destination. However, like all areas, visitors should take basic precautions, such as locking their car, not leaving valuables inside the car, and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Avondale

The extreme sunshine and heat during summer months can be dangerous, so it is important to stay hydrated and take breaks from outdoor activities. Visitors should also watch out for the local wildlife, such as snakes, scorpions, and black widows.

Getting to Avondale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can easily reach Avondale by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Avondale

Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport is the closest airport, located approximately 20 miles away from Avondale. Other nearby airports include Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport and Scottsdale Airport.

Bus Stations Near Avondale

The Greyhound bus station is located in downtown Phoenix, approximately 19 miles east of Avondale.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or use ride-sharing services from the airport or bus station to reach Avondale.

How to Get Around in Avondale

The best way to get around Avondale is by car or bike. Visitors can rent a car from the airport or use a ride-sharing service. Biking is also a popular way to explore Avondale, especially in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Avondale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting from Avondale. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Goodyear: This city is located just minutes away from Avondale and offers hiking trails, golf courses and local brewpubs.

  • Scottsdale: This affluent city is roughly an hour east of Avondale and offers luxury shopping, high-end restaurants, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Avondale is a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Avondale is a must-visit destination in Arizona. Explore the local food and drink scene, take a guided horseback ride through the Sonoran Desert, or simply embrace the local culture. Whatever you choose, a visit to Avondale is sure to be a memorable experience.
