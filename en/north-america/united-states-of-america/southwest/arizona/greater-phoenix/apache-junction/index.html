Introduction to Apache Junction

Located in the Greater Phoenix area, Apache Junction is a city in Arizona with a population of approximately 42,000 people. Known for its scenic beauty and outdoor adventures, the city is a popular tourist destination in the United States. Apache Junction is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, including the Superstition Mountains and the Tonto National Forest. It’s also home to a rich history dating back to the Apache Indians, who used to reside in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Apache Junction

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lost Dutchman State Park: This state park is a great place to enjoy hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. It’s also home to the famous Lost Dutchman’s Gold Mine, which is said to be hidden somewhere in the Superstition Mountains.

  • Superstition Mountain Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the area, including the Apache Indians and the early settlers. It also features exhibits on the local flora and fauna and the mining industry.

  • Goldfield Ghost Town: This reconstructed ghost town offers visitors a glimpse into life in an old-fashioned mining town. It features a saloon, a mine tour, a reptile exhibit, and a pottery shop.

  • Arizona Renaissance Festival: Held annually in February and March, this festival is a celebration of the Renaissance era. It features jousting tournaments, live performances, and artisan booths.

Natural Attractions of Apache Junction: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salt River: The Salt River is a popular spot for water sports, including tubing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. Visitors can also enjoy hiking and horseback riding in the surrounding Tonto National Forest.

  • Apache Trail Scenic Drive: This 40-mile drive offers breathtaking views of the Superstition Mountains and the Canyon Lake. Along the way, visitors can see historic sites and stop at overlooks for photo opportunities.

  • Dolly Steamboat: This steamboat tour offers visitors a chance to see the flora and fauna of the Canyon Lake. It’s a great way to enjoy a relaxing afternoon while taking in the scenic views.

  • Horseback Riding Tours: Visitors can choose from a variety of horseback riding tours, including a sunset ride and a guided trail ride through the Superstition Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apache Junction

  • Lost Dutchman State Park Trails: This park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to difficult. The Siphon Draw Trail leads to the top of the Superstition Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Peralta Trailhead: This trailhead offers several hiking trails, including the Peralta Trail, which leads to the top of a ridge with panoramic views of the Superstition Mountains.

  • Bulldog Canyon OHV Area: This area is open to off-road vehicles, but also has trails for hiking and mountain biking. It’s a great place to enjoy the rugged beauty of the desert landscape.

  • Usery Mountain Regional Park: This park offers over 29 miles of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to difficult. The Wind Cave Trail leads to a limestone cave with wind-carved tunnels and windows.

Best Things to Do in Apache Junction: Activities and Tours

  • ATV Tours: Visitors can book an ATV tour through the desert landscape of the Superstition Mountains.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: A hot air balloon ride over the Superstition Mountains offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Apache Junction Farmers Market: Open every Saturday, this farmers market offers locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and live music.

  • Salt River Tubing: This popular activity allows visitors to float down the Salt River in inner tubes.

Food and Drink Scene of Apache Junction

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mexican Food: Apache Junction has several restaurants that serve authentic Mexican food, including Los Gringos Locos and Elvira’s Mexican Restaurant.

  • Steak: The Hitching Post Steakhouse is a popular spot for juicy steaks and western-style ambiance.

  • Burgers: The Mining Camp Restaurant is known for its delicious burgers and homemade pies.

  • Craft Beer: Superstition Meadery and Goldwater Brewing Company both offer a variety of craft beers.

Local Beverages

  • Mead: Superstition Meadery is a great place to taste locally made mead.

  • Wine: The Queen Creek Olive Mill and Arizona Stronghold Vineyards both offer wine tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Apache Junction: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Apache Junction Inn: This hotel features comfortable rooms, a pool, and a fitness center.

  • Motel 6: This budget-friendly option is clean and basic, with a pool and coin-operated laundry facilities.

  • Lost Dutchman RV Resort: This RV resort offers a variety of lodging options, including tent sites, cabins, and RV hookups.

What to Buy in Apache Junction: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Superstition Mountain Souvenirs: This store offers a variety of souvenirs, including t-shirts, postcards, and locally made jewelry.

  • Queen Creek Olive Mill: Visitors can buy locally made olive oil, gourmet food products, and gifts.

  • Lost Dutchman State Park Gift Shop: This gift shop offers souvenirs and books on local history and natural wonders.

Local Culture and Customs of Apache Junction

  • Western Heritage: Apache Junction is proud of its western heritage and cowboy culture. Visitors can experience this firsthand by taking a horseback riding tour or visiting a western-themed restaurant.

Best Time to Visit Apache Junction

The best time to visit Apache Junction is in the fall or winter, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching over 100 degrees Fahrenheit. Spring is also a good time to visit, with blooming wildflowers and a variety of outdoor activities available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apache Junction

Do:

  • Do dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.

  • Do bring your own water and snacks on hikes and outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Don’t underestimate the intensity of the sun and heat.

  • Don’t approach or disturb wildlife in their natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apache Junction

  • Keep an eye on your surroundings and belongings while visiting tourist attractions.

  • Follow all safety guidelines and instructions when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Use caution when driving on rural roads and watch for wildlife crossing.

Health in Apache Junction

  • The Arizona heat can be intense, so be sure to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.

  • Check for ticks after hiking or spending time in the outdoors.

  • Use caution when consuming local food and drink to prevent illness.

Getting to Apache Junction: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apache Junction

  • Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport is approximately 34 miles from Apache Junction.

Bus Stations Near Apache Junction

  • Greyhound Bus lines has a station in Mesa, Arizona, which is approximately 15 miles from Apache Junction.

Other Options

  • Car rental companies, including Hertz and Enterprise, have locations in and around Apache Junction.

How to Get Around in Apache Junction

The best way to get around in Apache Junction is by car. However, there are also a variety of guided tours and transportation options available for tourists, including horseback riding tours, ATV tours, and hot air balloon rides.

Where to Go Next from Apache Junction: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Phoenix: The capital city of Arizona is approximately 35 miles from Apache Junction and offers museums, dining, shopping, and nightlife.

  • Saguaro Lake: This lake is approximately 20 miles from Apache Junction and offers water sports, hiking, and scenic views.

  • Tucson: This southern Arizona city is approximately 120 miles from Apache Junction and is known for its historic sites, museums, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Apache Junction is a great destination for travelers who love outdoor adventures, scenic beauty, and western culture. From hiking and horseback riding to hot air balloon rides and scenic drives, there’s something for everyone in this Arizona city. Its close proximity to nearby destinations like Phoenix and Saguaro Lake make it a great base for exploring the region.
