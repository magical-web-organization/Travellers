Introduction to Elko

Elko is a small city located in the northeastern corner of Nevada, United States. It’s surrounded by the beautiful Ruby Mountains and is known for its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and outdoor recreation opportunities. With a population of just over 20,000, it’s the largest city in the Elko County and a hub for tourists visiting Northeastern Nevada.

List of Places to Visit in Elko

Elko is home to several attractions and activities that cater to different interests. Here’s our list of must-visit places in Elko.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northeastern Nevada Museum

The Northeastern Nevada Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It showcases the history of Elko County, from prehistoric times to the present day. The museum exhibits include artifacts from Native American cultures, the Pony Express, and mining, ranching, and agriculture industries that shaped Elko.

California Trail Interpretive Center

Another important attraction is the California Trail Interpretive Center, which highlights the challenges pioneers faced as they traveled westward during the 1800s. The center offers interactive exhibits, multimedia presentations, and hands-on activities that provide insights into the rugged and dangerous terrain covered by the pioneers.

Western Folklife Center

The Western Folklife Center is a cultural hub of Elko, featuring exhibits, performances, and events showcasing the music, art, and folklore of the western region of the United States. Visitors can catch a concert, watch a film screening, participate in a workshop, or simply admire the beautiful artwork and literature on display.

Natural Attractions of Elko: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ruby Mountains

Elko is renowned for its proximity to the stunning Ruby Mountains, which provide a majestic backdrop for the city. The Ruby Mountains are home to over 300 high alpine lakes, snow-capped peaks, and varied landscapes that are perfect for hiking, camping, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

Lamoille Canyon

Lamoille Canyon is a beautiful scenic drive that provides access to the Ruby Mountains. The canyon features lush forests, colorful wildflowers, and cascading waterfalls, making it a popular spot for hiking and photography.

South Fork State Recreation Area

South Fork State Recreation Area is a great spot for fishing, boating, and wildlife viewing. The lake is stocked with rainbow trout, and visitors can rent boats and paddleboards from the park. The park also has picnic areas, campgrounds, and hiking trails with scenic views of the surrounding desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elko

Elko has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region. Some of the most popular hiking/biking trails include:

  • Ruby Crest National Recreation Trail
  • Secret Pass Trail
  • Lamoille Canyon Trail
  • Osino Trail

Best Things to Do in Elko: Activities and Tours

Whether you want to explore the city’s history or immerse yourself in outdoor activities, Elko has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Elko.

  • Tour the historic Sherman Station Visitor’s Center.
  • Visit the Western Folklife Center and catch a performance or workshop.
  • Take a scenic drive along Lamoille Canyon.
  • Explore the rich Native American history and culture at the South Fork Indian Reservation.
  • Go fishing or boating at South Fork State Recreation Area.
  • Attend the National Cowboy Poetry Gathering, held annually in Elko.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elko

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Elko, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your visit.

Day Activities
1 Visit the Northeastern Nevada Museum and the Sherman Station Visitor’s Center.
2 Take a scenic drive along Lamoille Canyon and visit the California Trail Interpretive Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Elko

Elko may be a small city, but it boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that’s worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample while in Elko.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Basque-style cuisine: Elko has a significant Basque population, and you’ll find many restaurants serving traditional Basque dishes, such as lamb chops, chorizo, and paella.
  • Steak and seafood: With its proximity to the Pacific Ocean and Snake River, Elko has access to some of the freshest seafood and beef in the region.
  • Sourdough bread: Elko’s sourdough bread is famous for its tangy flavor and dense texture.

Local Beverages

  • Great Basin Brewing Company: This local microbrewery offers a variety of craft beers, including the Ichthyosaur IPA and the Wild Horse Ale.
  • 10 Torr Distilling and Brewing: This local distillery/brewery serves up handcrafted spirits and craft beers.

Where to Stay in Elko: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elko offers several accommodations options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels.

  • Red Lion Hotel & Casino: This hotel-casino offers comfortable rooms, on-site dining, and gambling.
  • TownePlace Suites by Marriott Elko: This hotel offers spacious suites with kitchenettes, an indoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Thunderbird Motel: This budget-friendly motel features clean rooms, a central location, and easy access to downtown Elko.

What to Buy in Elko: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Elko offers plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for family and friends back home. Some popular items include:

  • Custom leather goods: Elko has a rich history of cattle ranching, and local artisans create beautiful leather goods such as wallets, belts, and hats.
  • Jewelry: Elko has several jewelry stores that sell authentic Native American jewelry made from silver and turquoise.
  • Whiskey: 10 Torr Distilling and Brewing offers handcrafted whiskeys that make great gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Elko

Elko has a rich history and a unique Basque culture that has been preserved over time. Elko hosts several Basque festivals throughout the year, where visitors can experience Basque food, music, and sports.

Best Time to Visit Elko

The best time to visit Elko is between May and September when outdoor activities are in full swing, and temperatures are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elko

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and leave no trace.
  • Try the Basque-style cuisine.
  • Tip your servers and bartenders.

Don’t:

  • Dispose of trash improperly.
  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Be disrespectful to the Native American community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elko

Elko is a safe city, but visitors should take precautions to avoid accidents and stay aware of their surroundings.

Health in Elko

Elko has several medical facilities, including Northeastern Nevada Regional Hospital, that offer emergency and urgent care services.

Getting to Elko: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elko

Elko Regional Airport is the closest airport to the city and offers flights from Salt Lake City, Utah, and Denver, Colorado.

Bus Stations Near Elko

Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to Elko from major cities in Nevada and Utah.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Elko is conveniently located on Interstate 80, which connects San Francisco, California, and Salt Lake City, Utah.

How to Get Around in Elko

The best way to get around Elko is by car. Car rentals are available at the Elko Regional Airport and in downtown Elko.

Where to Go Next from Elko: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jarbidge Wilderness Area: This remote wilderness area is located in the northern part of Elko County and offers hiking, fishing, and camping opportunities.
  • Great Basin National Park: This scenic park is located just south of Elko, and it features spectacular caves, mountain lakes, and hiking trails.
  • Salt Lake City, Utah: Salt Lake City is a major regional hub that offers world-class ski resorts, cultural attractions, and outdoor recreation opportunities.

Conclusion

Elko is a hidden gem in Nevada that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. From the stunning Ruby Mountains to the delicious Basque cuisine, Elko has something for everyone. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to Elko and discover all the beauty and excitement this city has to offer.
