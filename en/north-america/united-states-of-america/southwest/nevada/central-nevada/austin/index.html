Introduction to Austin

Welcome to Austin, a vibrant city located in the heart of Texas. Known for its laidback atmosphere, hip vibe, and lively music scene, Austin has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in culture, art, history, or nature, you’ll find plenty of things to see and do in this charming city.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Austin. From cultural and historical landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor adventures, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your ultimate trip to Austin.

List of Places to Visit in Austin

Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Austin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Texas State Capitol: A magnificent structure that was built in 1888, the Texas State Capitol is a must-visit landmark in Austin.

  • LBJ Presidential Library: Dedicated to President Lyndon Baines Johnson, this library showcases the life and history of the 36th US President.

  • Bullock Texas State History Museum: This museum tells the story of Texas through interactive exhibits and multimedia presentations.

  • Blanton Museum of Art: One of the largest university art museums in the country, the Blanton Museum of Art showcases an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art.

Natural Attractions of Austin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lady Bird Lake: A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Lady Bird Lake offers scenic views, opportunities for kayaking, canoeing, and hiking.

  • Zilker Metropolitan Park: Located in downtown Austin, Zilker Park offers a wide range of outdoor activities for families, including hiking trails, splash pads, and playgrounds.

  • Mount Bonnell: The highest point in Austin, Mount Bonnell offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding areas.

  • Barton Springs Pool: A natural spring-fed swimming pool located in Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool is a popular destination for locals and visitors alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Austin

Austin is a paradise for nature enthusiasts, with a plethora of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the most popular routes in Austin:

  • The Barton Creek Greenbelt: This six-mile trail offers stunning views of the limestone cliffs and cascading waterfalls.

  • McKinney Falls State Park: Located just southeast of downtown Austin, this state park offers several hiking trails that lead to beautiful waterfalls and rock formations.

  • The Violet Crown Trail: A 30-mile trail that stretches from Zilker Park to the Barton Creek Wilderness Park, the Violet Crown Trail offers stunning panoramic views of the city and the surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Austin: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Austin:

  • Visit the Texas Capitol Building and take a tour of its stunning interiors.

  • Take a stroll down South Congress Avenue and explore the local shops and restaurants.

  • Visit the Austin Zoo and meet some exotic animals up close.

  • Take a food tour of Austin and sample some authentic Texan cuisine.

  • Take a dip in the Barton Springs Pool and enjoy the natural beauty of Austin.

Week-Long Itinerary for Austin

If you’re planning to spend a week in Austin, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Texas Capitol Building and explore downtown Austin.
Day 2 Take a food tour of Austin and sample some of the city’s best cuisine.
Day 3 Spend the day hiking or biking at Barton Creek Greenbelt or McKinney Falls State Park.
Day 4 Visit the LBJ Presidential Library and the Bullock Texas State History Museum.
Day 5 Take a day trip to Fredericksburg, a charming German town known for its wineries and boutiques.
Day 6 Visit the Blanton Museum of Art and explore the University of Texas campus.
Day 7 Spend the day at Lady Bird Lake, enjoying kayaking, canoeing, and hiking.

Food and Drink Scene of Austin

Austin is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of cafes, bars, bakeries, and restaurants to choose from. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texas BBQ: Texas is famous for its slow-smoked meats, including brisket, pork ribs, and sausage.

  • Breakfast tacos: A local Austin favorite, breakfast tacos are a must-try dish that features scrambled eggs, bacon, and cheese wrapped in a tortilla.

  • Tex-Mex: A fusion of Texan and Mexican cuisine, Tex-Mex is a must-try cuisine in Austin.

  • Kolaches: A Czech pastry that’s popular in Texas, kolaches are a sweet or savory pastry stuffed with fillings like sausage, cheese, or fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Shiner Bock: A local beer made in Shiner, Texas, Shiner Bock is a popular drink in Austin.

  • Margaritas: A classic cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec, margaritas are a favorite drink in Austin.

Where to Stay in Austin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Austin has accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Driskill Hotel, Four Seasons Hotel Austin, and the W Austin Hotel are some of the best luxury hotels in Austin.

  • Mid-Range Hotels: The Aloft Austin Downtown, Hyatt Place Austin Downtown, and the Courtyard Austin Downtown are some of the best mid-range hotels in Austin.

  • Hostels and Guesthouses: The Firehouse Hostel, Drifter Jack’s Hostel, and the HI Austin Hostel are some of the best budget-friendly options for accommodation in Austin.

What to Buy in Austin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Austin is known for its unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  • Cowboy boots: A classic southern accessory, cowboy boots are a must-buy in Austin.

  • Hot Sauce: Known for its spicy cuisine, Austin has no shortage of hot sauces to choose from.

  • Texas-shaped souvenirs: Everything from t-shirts to keychains can be found in the shape of Texas, making them a fun souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Austin

Austin is known for its eclectic and laidback culture, with a passion for live music, outdoor adventures, and great food. Here are some tips to help you enjoy your stay in Austin like a local:

  • Embrace the live music scene: Austin is known as the “Live Music Capital of the World,” so be sure to catch a show while you’re in town.

  • Dress comfortably: Austin has a casual dress code, so don’t be afraid to dress down.

  • Respect the local environment: Austin locals are passionate about maintaining the city’s natural beauty, so be sure to dispose of trash properly and be mindful of your impact on the environment.

Best Time to Visit Austin

The best time to visit Austin is between September and November when temperatures are pleasant, and crowds are thinner. Spring is also a great time to visit when wildflowers are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Austin

Do:

  • Do try the Tex-Mex cuisine: Austin is known for its Tex-Mex, so be sure to try some of the local dishes.

  • Do visit the state capitol building: The Texas State Capitol is a beautiful structure that’s worth a visit.

  • Do check out the live music scene: Austin has a thriving music scene, so be sure to catch a show while you’re in town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Austin locals are passionate about maintaining the city’s natural beauty, so be sure to dispose of trash properly.

  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen: The Texas sun can be intense, so be sure to protect your skin.

  • Don’t drink and drive: Austin has strict laws against drinking and driving, so be sure to designate a driver or use a rideshare service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Austin

Austin is generally a safe city, but like any urban area, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to stay safe. Here are some safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

  • Keep valuables out of sight to avoid theft.

  • Use caution when walking or biking at night.

Health in Austin

Austin is known for its healthy and active lifestyle, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and healthy food options. Here are some tips to help you stay healthy during your visit:

  • Stay hydrated: The Texas heat can be intense, so be sure to drink plenty of water.

  • Practice sun safety: Austin has a high UV index, so be sure to wear sunscreen and a hat.

  • Try healthy food options: Austin has plenty of healthy food options, including vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free options.

Getting to Austin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Austin

  • Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (AUS): Located just 5 miles southeast of downtown Austin, this airport offers international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Austin

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in downtown Austin, Greyhound offers bus services to destinations across the US.

Other Options

  • Rideshare: Uber and Lyft are both available in Austin.

  • Car rental: Austin has several car rental options available.

How to Get Around Austin

Austin is a walkable city, with many attractions located within walking distance of each other. However, if you need to travel further, here are some transportation options:

  • Public transportation: The Capital Metro offers bus and rail services that connect Austin and its surrounding areas.

  • Rideshare: Uber and Lyft are both available in Austin.

  • Biking: Austin has a bike-share system called Austin B-cycle.

Where to Go Next from Austin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Texas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • San Antonio: Located just an hour and a half to the south, San Antonio is known for its rich history and vibrant culture.

  • Hill Country: The scenic region located west of Austin is known for its rolling hills, natural beauty, and charming small towns.

  • Fredericksburg: Located just an hour away from Austin, this German town is known for its wineries, boutiques, and rich history.

Conclusion

Austin is a vibrant city with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or just want to relax, this charming city has it all. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your ultimate trip to Austin and enjoy everything this beautiful city has to offer. Happy travels!
