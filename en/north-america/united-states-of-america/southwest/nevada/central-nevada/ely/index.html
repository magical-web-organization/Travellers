Introduction to Ely

Nestled in the central region of Nevada, Ely is a small and quaint city with some unexpected gems. The town is a unique blend of nature and history, with stunning outdoor locations and an exciting past. Set against the backdrop of the majestic Nevada mountains, Ely is a perfect getaway for all travelers who love exploring offbeat locations.

List of Places to Visit in Ely

There is so much to see and do in Ely, and we’ve curated a list of all the must-visit spots, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Take a step into Ely’s rich cultural heritage with a walk through its museums and historical places. Some of these incredible sites include:

  • Nevada Northern Railway Museum: Step back in time with a visit to the Nevada Northern Railway Museum, which showcases Ely’s mining history in the early 20th century.
  • White Pine Public Museum: Delve into the history of White Pine County, which hosts a vast collection of mining, ranching, and logging-related exhibitions.
  • Garnet Hill and Ward Charcoal Ovens State Historic Park: Take a drive to Garnet Hill, an open pit copper mine that was active from the early 1900s to the 1930s. Visit the nearby Ward Charcoal Ovens State Historic Park to see the four iconic stone ovens which were used in the 1870s to provide charcoal for nearby mines.

Natural Attractions of Ely: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ely is a haven for nature enthusiasts, and you can explore the following locations:

  • Great Basin National Park: Just an hour drive east of Ely, discover the wonders of the Great Basin National Park. Created in 1986, it boasts a plethora of attractions, including alpine lakes, limestone caves, and over 60 miles of hiking trails.
  • Cave Lake State Park: Enjoy fishing, camping, hiking, and a scenic drive along Cave Lake State Park in Ely. Home to a man-made lake, the park offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor fun.
  • Egan Crest Trail: The Egan Crest Trail is a beautiful trail that runs for miles along the peaks of the Egan mountain range, and is ideal for bikers and hikers alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ely

For adventure buffs, Ely offers exciting trails for a fun-filled experience. Here are a few of the recommended routes to traverse:

  • Ward Mountain: This challenging 9-mile hike is a favorite among hikers in the area. The trail gains around 2700 feet of elevation, but the views at the top of the mountain are worth the journey.
  • Comins Lake: The Comins Lake Trail is a 2.4-mile loop trail with stunning views of the lake. The trail is suitable for beginners and experts alike.

Best Things to Do in Ely: Activities and Tours

Apart from museums and natural attractions, Ely also offers numerous thrilling activities and tours, such as:

  • Ely Art Bank: If you’re an art connoisseur, visit the Ely Art Bank, which hosts a collection of local as well as international artwork.
  • Club 50 Casino: Try your luck at the Club 50 Casino - the casino offers exciting slot machines, table games, and bingo.
  • Ghost Tours: Go on a spooky adventure with the Ghost Tours of Ely. The tour takes you through haunted places in Ely, providing a thrilling experience for all.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ely

For travelers planning to spend an entire week in Ely, here’s a recommended itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Ely

Ely’s food and drink scene is a mix of classic American and exquisite local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Basque cuisine: Indulge in Basque fare like lamb chops, steak, and oxtail stew at unique local establishments such as the Hotel Nevada, the Jailhouse Casino and Hotel, and the old-school C.O.D. Casino.
  • Jerky: White Pine Trading Post on Aultman Street offers a selection of delicious jerky made with locally sourced meats.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Ely’s local brewery, the Cellar’s Brewery and Pub, offers a range of unique and quality craft beers.
  • Wine: At the Copper Kettle, sample a selection of Nevada-made wines.

Where to Stay in Ely: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For accommodation options, Ely has a range of choices available, such as:

  • Hotel Nevada: The iconic Hotel Nevada, located in the center of downtown Ely, offers modern rooms and an excellent dining scene.
  • Jailhouse Casino and Hotel: For a unique boutique stay, try the Jailhouse Casino and Hotel, which has converted the old jailhouse into luxury rooms.
  • All Aboard Cafe and Inn: For budget-friendly accommodations, All Aboard Cafe and Inn provides clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Ely: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ely offers some unique souvenirs and locally produced products to take back home, such as:

  • Outdoor gear: Economy Drug, located on Aultman Street, offers a selection of outdoor gear.
  • Local products: Stop by the Nevada Grown Store on Broad Street for unique local products, such as honey, jewelry, and home decor items.

Local Culture and Customs of Ely

Ely has a rich Basque culture with festivals and events throughout the year. The town also has a strong mining history, and many establishments have antique mining gear and tools on display. It is advised that visitors respect the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Ely

The best time to visit Ely is during the summer months from June to September. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and most outdoors activities are open to the public.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ely

Do:

  • Plan ahead and carry appropriate clothing and equipment while exploring the outdoors.
  • Respect the local culture and customs when visiting museums and historical places.

Don’t:

  • Do not venture off on unmarked trails; make sure to follow designated hiking and biking paths.
  • Do not litter at the natural attractions and maintain cleanliness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ely

Overall, Ely is a safe destination, but it is advised that travelers take necessary precautions such as:

  • Drinking water regularly, and always carrying extra water supplies, sunscreen, and hats.
  • Following the designated paths at all times and avoiding off-trail hikes.
  • Being aware of one’s surroundings and not venturing into unknown areas.

Health in Ely

Ely does not have a hospital, but it does have a medical center called William Bee Ririe Hospital and Clinic for urgent medical needs. Always carry necessary medications and insurance information.

Getting to Ely: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ely is easily accessible by road through Interstate 80 and 93. However, there are other transportation options available as well:

Airports Near Ely

  • Ely’s Yelland Field Airport (ELY) is a regional airport with scheduled flights to and from Salt Lake City.

Bus Stations Near Ely

  • Greyhound Lines offers bus service to Ely from a variety of destinations.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available from airports and downtown locations.

How to Get Around in Ely

The best way to explore Ely is by renting a car. City Taxi and Ride Share also offer cab services within the city.

Where to Go Next from Ely: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ely is a gateway to other exciting locations in Nevada, such as:

  • Tonopah: Explore the historic mining town of Tonopah and its numerous museums, antique shops, and restaurants.
  • Bob Scott Campground and Cave Lake State Park: Head to Bob Scott Campground and Cave Lake State Park for a camping and fishing trip and enjoy scenic views of the Ruby Mountains.

Conclusion

Ely is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a unique outdoors and historical experience. With numerous attractions, exciting activities, and rich cultural heritage, Ely is sure to leave you awestruck. Happy Travels!
