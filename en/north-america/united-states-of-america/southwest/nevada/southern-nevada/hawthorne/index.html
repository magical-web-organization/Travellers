Introduction to Hawthorne

Hawthorne is a small town in southern Nevada, nestled in the breathtakingly scenic desert landscape. It is located about 130 miles southeast of Reno and 100 miles southeast of Lake Tahoe. Hawthorne was originally founded as a mining town in the 19th century but has since evolved into a quiet, peaceful community that is perfect for those looking to explore the Great Basin Desert or simply relax and unwind in a tranquil setting. With a population of just over 3,000 people, it offers a unique, small-town charm that is sure to delight visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Hawthorne

If you’re planning a trip to Hawthorne, there are plenty of interesting attractions and places to see in and around town. Here are a few of the best:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hawthorne Ordnance Museum: A fascinating museum showcasing the history of the US Army ammunition depot in Hawthorne. The museum displays an impressive collection of military equipment and artifacts from World War II to the present day.
  • Mineral County Museum: A huge museum dedicated to the history and culture of Mineral County. It features numerous exhibits on local Native American and pioneer history, military history, and mining.
  • Hawthorne Army Depot: This installation serves as the largest ammunition depot in the world. The local area has some historical relevance too, like The Bombing Range, an area wherein various simulated bombing raids are conducted.

Natural Attractions of Hawthorne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Walker Lake: A gorgeous lake situated on the eastern side of Hawthorne, perfect for fishing, kayaking, and simply relaxing on its shoreline.
  • Mount Grant: A towering peak located nearby to Hawthorne that offer spectacular views of the surrounding desert landscape.
  • Hawthorne Greenwave Park: A picturesque park with picnic tables, playgrounds, and a walking trail. The park is well maintained and is great for an evening stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hawthorne

The natural beauty of the surrounding desert is reason alone to visit Hawthorne, but those looking to head out and explore it will find that there are plenty of great hikes and bike routes in the area. Here are just a few:

  • Mt. Grant Hike: A challenging 4-mile round trip hike that takes you up to the summit of Mount Grant and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountain range.
  • The Hawthorne Bike Trail: A 2-mile paved trail that begins at Hawthorne’s downtown and winds through the scenic countryside.
  • Babbitt Loop Trail: This intermediate hike takes you through the rugged terrain of nearby mountains, providing an up-close look at the natural landscape.

Best Things to Do in Hawthorne: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there are plenty of activities to enjoy in Hawthorne. Here are a few of the best:

  • Fishing: Walker Lake is a hot spot for fishing, with catches such as trout, catfish, and bass.
  • ATV tours and rentals: Explore the deserts of Hawthorne with off-road vehicles from local tour operators.
  • Military tours: Take a tour of the largest ammunition depot. It offers a historical exhibit on their decades of service.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hawthorne

If you’re planning to spend a week in Hawthorne, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your vacation:

Day 1: Arrive and settle into your hotel in Hawthorne.

Day 2:

  • Start your day with breakfast at the iconic El Capitan Cafe.
  • Visit the Hawthorne Ordnance Museum and learn about the history of ammunition depots.
  • Have lunch at the community-favourite Mexican restaurant, La Herradura.
  • Visit Walker Lake and enjoy an evening barbecue on its serene shore.

Day 3:

  • Drive to Mount Grant and hike to its summit for stunning panoramic views.
  • Grab lunch in nearby Smith Valley, a short 20-mile drive from Hawthorne. You can stop in for a slice of pie at Wolf Creek Eatery.
  • Explore the iconic. old-world charm of Reno, Nevada’s largest city, a 130-mile drive away.

Day 4:

  • Head out to tour the largest ammunition depot in the world, the Hawthorne Army Depot.
  • Take a scenic bike ride on Hawthorne Bike Trail.
  • Enjoy dinner at the family-friendly Pizza Factory.

Day 5:

  • Spend the day exploring the natural desert landscape on an ATV tour.
  • Have a late lunch at local eatery Courtyard Cafe.
  • Witness the gritty, off-road racing thrill of all kinds on the Fourth of July Hill.

Day 6:

  • Explore Mineral Country Museum and relive the history of the mining town.
  • Take a leisurely stroll through Hawthorne Greenwave Park.
  • Have dinner at the nearby town of Yerington at the Dini’s Lucky Club Casino.

Day 7: Depart Hawthorne for your onward adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Hawthorne

Hawthorne, despite being a small town, has some excellent food options that are a must-try. Take a look at some of the best places to eat and drink during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • El Capitan Cafe’s Chicken Fry: It’s the signature dish of El Capitan Cafe, you can’t go wrong with it - crispy and juicy chicken fried to perfection served with gravy and mashed potatoes.

  • Pizza Factory: The local hangout spot serves one of the best pizzas with its thin crust and wide array of toppings.

  • La Herradura’s Burrito Supreme: Everything about the burritos at La Herradura is amazing – the beans are perfect and the proportions of meat, cheese, and rice are always precise.

Local Beverages

  • Hawthorne’s very own Porter: Try a pint of Sawyer’s Porter at the Old Nevada Pizza co., loved by the locals for its balanced taste, nutty aroma, and smooth finish.

  • Roasted From the Ground: Coffee lovers must try a cup of freshly roasted coffee at this quaint, rustic coffee shop located in the heart of town.

Where to Stay in Hawthorne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hawthorne has a variety of accommodations to suit any budget and taste. Here are a few recommendations:

  • El Capitan Lodge and Casino: A comfortable stay with an impressive range of amenities like an indoor pool, fitness center, and casino.

  • Travelodge by Wyndham Hawthorne: Located in the heart of town, Travelodge provides classic motel comfort and is a convenient base for your explorations.

  • Desert Rose RV Park: A perfect accommodation for those travelling in an RV, it is a pet-friendly and spacious park with friendly staff and amenities like grilling stations and laundry.

What to Buy in Hawthorne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring home from your Hawthorne trip, here are a few ideas:

  • Military memorabilia: Hawthorne was largely affected and shaped by its rich military history. Visit the Hawthorne Ordnance Museum gift shop for T-shirts, patriotic decor, and other souvenirs.

  • Handmade gifts: Stop by the US Highway 95 Emporium, a popular shop with its vintage and handmade decor items.

Local Culture and Customs of Hawthorne

The locals of Hawthorne are warm, friendly and welcoming. A handshake, a wave, and a friendly smile will make your visit enjoyable and memorable. Dress codes are relaxed and casual, and tipping at restaurants and hotels is expected. Military service members and their families dominate the community, so show respect for the sacrifices they have made for their country. Remember basic manners and etiquette, and you’ll be welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Hawthorne

The best time to visit Hawthorne is during the spring, fall, or winter months when the weather is mild and pleasant. The temperatures during the summer months can soar, making outdoor activities quite uncomfortable. Peak tourist season is from June to August. To avoid the crowds and get the best experience, consider visiting in the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hawthorne

Do:

  • Carry plenty of water and snacks when out exploring.
  • Respect the military personnel and their families and show gratitude for their services.
  • Be mindful of desert wildlife, especially on hikes and bike trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the desert.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturbed wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hawthorne

Hawthorne is known for being a peaceful and safe community. However, visitors must be aware of the desert climate and take precautions when participating in outdoor activities. Always carry enough water, use sun protection, and wear proper attire when outdoors to avoid dehydration and sunburns. Crime rates are low, but it’s still best to stay aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure.

Health in Hawthorne

Your health and safety are of utmost importance during the pandemic and in general. If planning a trip to Hawthorne, it is best to stay updated regarding the current COVID-19 protocols mandates in Nevada.

Getting to Hawthorne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hawthorne can be accessed via highway and air. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Hawthorne

The nearest airports are:

  • Reno-Tahoe International Airport: Approximately 130 miles from Hawthorne, it serves multiple airlines and all major U.S airport hubs.

  • Bishop Airport: Located in Bishop, California, around 120 miles south of Hawthorne, and offers daily services to Los Angeles.

Bus Stations Near Hawthorne

The nearest bus station is:

  • Silver Stage Lines: Regular bus routes connect Hawthorne with other small towns in Nevada, and direct routes to Reno are also available.

Other Options

  • Drive: The most convenient way to reach Hawthorne is by car, with several highways connecting the town to nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Hawthorne

Since Hawthorne is a small town, the best way to explore its charm is by walking or cycling. For those who use vehicles:

  • Car rental services: There are quite some rental services available for the entire duration of your stay in Hawthorne.

  • Taxis/Cabs: Taxis and cabs are also at your disposal via phone calls to local businesses.

Where to Go Next from Hawthorne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few interesting destinations around Hawthorne worth exploring on your trip:

  • Tonopah, NV: Home to historic landmarks, like the Mizpah Hotel and the Tonopah Historic Mining Park, it is an hour away from Hawthorne.

  • Lake Tahoe, CA: Tahoe is the perfect mountain getaway, approximately two-and-a-half-hour drive from Hawthorne, and offers beautiful lakes, mountains, hiking trails, and a casino scene.

Conclusion

Hawthorne is a small desert town packed with fascinating cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor activities for everyone. Plan your trip well in advance to make the most of all the experiences and marvels it has to offer in abundance.
