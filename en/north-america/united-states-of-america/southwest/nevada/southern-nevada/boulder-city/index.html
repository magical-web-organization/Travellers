Introduction to Boulder City

Located in Southern Nevada and just 26 miles from Las Vegas, Boulder City is a charming town that offers a unique contrast from glitzy Vegas. It is known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a plethora of outdoor adventures. Boulder City offers tourists a relaxing and authentic desert experience. In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs, to help you plan the perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Boulder City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boulder City-Hoover Dam Museum: This museum showcases the history of the building of the Hoover Dam and the city that was created to house the thousands of workers who helped construct it.
  • The Nevada State Railroad Museum: It features an array of restored steam engines, railway cars, and other artifacts in a locomotive-themed park that the whole family will enjoy.
  • The Historic District: The Historic District is home to historic buildings dating back to the early 1900s that have been preserved so visitors can get a glimpse into what the town was like when it was first founded.

Natural Attractions of Boulder City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Mead National Recreation Area: The Lake Mead National Recreation Area offers a wide range of outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, hiking, and camping.
  • River Mountains Loop Trail: This 34-mile biking and hiking trail offers stunning views of Lake Mead, Boulder City, and the surrounding mountains.
  • Bootleg Canyon: Bootleg Canyon is a famous location for mountain biking enthusiasts and is home to some of the best trails in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boulder City

  • Bootleg Canyon trails: Bootleg Canyon has over 24 miles of interconnected trails for both hiking and mountain biking that are ideal for beginner and advanced mountain bikers alike.
  • River Mountains Loop Trail: The River Mountains Loop Trail provides a scenic and adventurous hiking and biking experience along the picturesque Lake Mead.

Best Things to Do in Boulder City: Activities and Tours

  • Hiking and Biking tours: There are several guided tours available in Boulder City and the surrounding areas for those who prefer having a professional guide alongside them.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: The calm waters of Lake Mead, the Colorado River, and the Black Canyon offer the perfect conditions for kayaking and paddleboarding.
  • Hoover Dam and Hemenway Park: The Hoover Dam is one of Boulder City’s main attractions, and a guided tour to the dam is an excellent experience. After that, the Hemenway Park just a short drive away offers an opportunity to view the Bighorn sheep up close and personal.

Food and Drink Scene of Boulder City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hemenway’s Shrimp Cocktail: This famous shrimp cocktail dish from Hemenway’s restaurant is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Southwest BBQ: The regional BBQ at Southwest BBQ is known to be one of the best in Nevada.
  • Prime Rib: Boulder City is famous for its expertly prepared Prime Rib, and you will find this dish available at many of its restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Boulder Dam Brewing Company: The Boulder Dam Brewing Company has a wide range of locally brewed beers on tap, and the tasting room is located in the heart of downtown Boulder City.
  • Brew Pub and Eatery: For those who enjoy a good cocktail, the Brew Pub and Eatery is the place to visit. They have an extensive cocktail menu that is updated frequently.

Where to Stay in Boulder City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Boulder Dam Hotel: This historic hotel was constructed in 1933 and has been meticulously renovated, preserving its unique Art Deco style and adding modern amenities for guests’ comfort.
  • El Rancho Boulder Motel: This vintage motel is located within walking distance of downtown Boulder City and has a 1950s-style decor theme.
  • Southwest Motel: This motel is conveniently located just a few minutes’ drive from the Hoover Dam and has a southwestern-inspired decor theme.

What to Buy in Boulder City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hoover Dam Memorabilia: Boulder City is the nearest town to the Hoover Dam, thereby making it a prime location to purchase Hoover Dam memorabilia.
  • Boulder City Antiques District: The Boulder City Antiques District is a must-visit for those interested in vintage and antique finds.
  • Local Handcrafted Goods: Boulder City has several shops offering locally handcrafted goods such as pottery, jewelry, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Boulder City

Boulder City takes pride in its town’s efforts to preserve the small-town charm that tourists from all over the world love. It is important to respect the locals’ privacy and properties while enjoying exploration.

Best Time to Visit Boulder City

The best time to visit Boulder City is between late fall and early spring (October to April). During this period, the average temperatures are mild to warm, allowing visitors to enjoy outdoor adventures and explore the city comfortably.

Do’s and Don’ts of Boulder City

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry plenty of water, sunscreen, and protective clothing while outdoors.
  • Take part in the outdoor adventures that Boulder City has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas, and respect all signs and barriers while exploring.
  • Handle or disturb local wildlife.
  • Take photos of people or their property without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boulder City

Boulder City is generally a safe town, but it is always important to stay vigilant while traveling. It is recommended that visitors check the areas they plan to visit before heading out and that they carry their personal belongings with them at all times.

Health in Boulder City

Boulder City has medical facilities that provide medical care to visitors who may require it. Visitors should also take precautions against excessive sun exposure and dehydration in the hot desert climate by hydrating regularly, wearing a hat, and using sunscreen.

Getting to Boulder City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Boulder City

The closest airport to Boulder City is the Boulder City Municipal Airport that provides charter flights from the nearby region. Additionally, McCarran International Airport in Las Vegas is about 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Boulder City

Boulder City has a local bus service that provides transport to the nearby destinations.

Other Options

Renting a vehicle is the best way to get around and explore all that Boulder City has to offer. Rental cars are available at Boulder City Municipal airport as well as at McCarran International Airport in Las Vegas.

How to Get Around in Boulder City

It is recommended that visitors rent a car when in Boulder City since it allows for more flexibility in exploring the town and its surroundings. However, there are local bus services available for those who prefer a more economical mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Boulder City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Las Vegas: Las Vegas is only a 30-minute drive from Boulder City, making it a convenient destination for sightseeing, shopping, and entertainment.
  • Red Rock Canyon: Just a 50-minute drive from Boulder City, visitors can explore Red Rock Canyon’s uniquely beautiful nature and its striking red rock formations.
  • Grand Canyon: The Grand Canyon’s South Rim is just a three-hour drive from Boulder City and offers an incredible excusion oppotunity viewing one of the world’s most beautiful natural wonders.

Conclusion

Boulder City is an ideal destination for those who enjoy outdoor adventures and want to experience the unique charm of a small town nestled among the gorgeous desert terrain. From historical landmarks to natural attractions, tourists will find that there are plenty of things to enjoy and appreciate. Use this guide to experience the best of Boulder City, Nevada.
