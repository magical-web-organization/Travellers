Introduction to North Las Vegas

Located in Southern Nevada, North Las Vegas is a city within a city and is home to over 245,000 people. It is situated in the southwest of the United States and is surrounded by mountain ranges, including the Sheep Range to its north.

North Las Vegas is known for its major attractions, including The Neon Museum, which is home to some of Sin City’s iconic neon signs, and the Las Vegas Motor Speedway. Along with this, it also has quite a few outdoor recreational activities for tourists and locals alike.

List of Places to Visit in North Las Vegas

If you’re planning to visit North Las Vegas, here are some must-visit places that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Neon Museum: This iconic museum is a testament to Las Vegas’s colorful past and is dedicated to preserving its iconic neon signs. It is a must-visit attraction for history buffs and art enthusiasts.

  • Old Las Vegas Mormon Fort State Historic Park: This state park highlights the cultural history of early settlers in Las Vegas. The park has a museum that houses artifacts and exhibits from the past and is a great way to dive deeper into Las Vegas’s rich history.

  • The Mob Museum: This incredibly engaging museum is dedicated to the history, rise, and fall of organized crime in Las Vegas and the United States. It offers a glimpse into the shady world of organized crime and the impact it had on the city.

Natural Attractions of North Las Vegas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Charleston: A quick trip to Mount Charleston offers a refreshing escape from the desert heat. The mountain offers a range of recreational activities, including hiking, skiing, and biking, and is a popular spot for nature enthusiasts.

  • Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area: This beautiful area is home to a range of wildlife and has a network of trails and roads offering diverse landscapes and fantastic views. One can hike, rock climb, or even horseback ride here.

  • Lake Mead National Recreation Area: This national recreation area is located close to North Las Vegas and is popular with locals and tourists for its beaches, water sports, and camping facilities. It is an excellent escape from the hustle and bustle of Las Vegas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Las Vegas

  • Craig Ranch Regional Park: This park offers stunning views of the nearby mountains and is a popular spot for hiking and biking. It also features a playground, splash pad and dog parks, making it a popular destination for families.

  • Tule Springs Fossil Beds National Monument: This destination has preserved pleistocene period fossil beds, a picnic area and multiple trails of varying difficulty to suit hikers of all experience levels.

  • Aliante Nature Discovery Park: This park offers quick and easy hikes, nature trails, and great views of the surrounding mountains. The park has shaded trails with water features, perfect for families.

Best Things to Do in North Las Vegas: Activities and Tours

  • Flightlinez Bootleg Canyon: Hold on tight as you zip across the canyon, offering a thrilling experience and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Las Vegas Motor Speedway: Home to NASCAR, the Speedway is a must-visit destination for motor racing enthusiasts. The speedway offers a range of experiences, including behind-the-scenes tours, driving experiences, and adrenaline-packed rides.

  • Cowabunga Bay Water Park: This water park is a great way of beating the Vegas heat. With over 20 water slides, a lazy river, and an inflatable water rollercoaster, it’s a great spot for a day of family fun.

Food and Drink Scene of North Las Vegas

Las Vegas is well known for its diverse food scene, and North Las Vegas is no exception. Here are some dishes and drinks you must try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chile Relleno: This dish is made up of stuffed green chile and cheese, then deep-fried and served with rice, beans, and guacamole.

  • Fish Tacos: A staple in Vegas, fish tacos comprise of battered and fried fish wrapped in a soft corn tortilla with lettuce, tomatoes, and a special sauce for flavor.

  • Squaw Bread: This locally made bread is a must-have. Squaw bread is a uniquely dense bread that tastes great on its own or with a side of butter.

Local Beverages

  • Das Biergarten: Located in North Las Vegas, this beer garden offers 30 craft beers on tap and hosts quiz nights, and other exciting events throughout the week.

  • Atomic’s Liquors: This swanky bar is famous for its ambiance and crowd. It has been operating since 1952 and is known for offering a range of drinks, including craft beer, wine, and cocktails.

Where to Stay in North Las Vegas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Las Vegas has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Aliante Casino + Hotel: This casino and resort offers comfortable rooms and great amenities, including a spa, a pool, and access to golf courses.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites: This modern hotel offers suites with separate sleeping areas and kitchenettes.

  • Cannery Casino and Hotel: A perfect choice for budget travelers, this hotel features classic rooms equipped with flat-screen TVs and has a gaming area and various restaurants.

What to Buy in North Las Vegas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back home as a memory of your visit:

  • Neon Signs: The Neon Museum has a gift shop where you can buy replicas of some of Las Vegas’s iconic neon signs.

  • Tumbleweed Pottery: A unique local product, tumbleweed pottery is made by rolling tumbleweeds and scraping the bark off to reveal a one-of-a-kind material that can be crafted into beautiful ceramics.

  • Vegas Kool Industries: A must-visit spot for functional and quirky souvenirs of Las Vegas, Vegas Kool Industries sells magnets, keychains, T-shirts, and other items.

Local Culture and Customs of North Las Vegas

Las Vegas, in general, is known for its unique culture, which is deeply rooted in entertainment, gambling, and glitz. North Las Vegas offers a chance to escape and immerse oneself in the city’s unique history and culture.

Best Time to Visit North Las Vegas

The best time to visit North Las Vegas depends on what you want to do. Summer months (June to August) can be scorching, with temperatures soaring to over 100°F. Spring and autumn are perfect for exploring the natural beauty of the region. Winter, while chilly, is the best time to visit if you want to experience snow and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Las Vegas

Do:

  • Do tip at least 15%: Tipping is crucial in the service industry, so don’t forget to tip at least 15% in restaurants and bars.

  • Do dress appropriately for the activity: Dress appropriately for the activity and venue you’re visiting. Be sure to pack comfortable shoes as there’s a lot of walking involved.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry cash: Cards are widely accepted, but it is always best to carry some cash with you.

  • Don’t get too carried away in casinos: Exercise restraint and gamble responsibly. Set a budget and stick to it.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Las Vegas

North Las Vegas is generally a safe place for tourists, but it is always best to exercise caution, particularly when venturing into unknown neighborhoods. Common sense precautions include staying alert, avoiding dark alleys, and not keeping large sums of cash with you.

Health in North Las Vegas

Las Vegas is situated in an arid region, and due to the heat, visitors should stay hydrated extensively. Apply sunscreen and wear a hat to prevent sunburn. Additionally, double-check the COVID-19 protocols that venues have in place, so you know what to expect.

Getting to North Las Vegas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Las Vegas

  • McCarran International Airport: This airport is located close to North Las Vegas and is the busiest airport in Nevada.

  • North Las Vegas Airport: This smaller regional airport offers services for private planes and helicopter tours.

Bus Stations Near North Las Vegas

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station offers intercity services connecting Las Vegas with other major cities in the US.

  • Regional Transportation Commission of Southern Nevada (RTC): RTC is the local transit company that operates buses, shuttles, and other transportation services in Las Vegas.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: North Las Vegas has a range of car rental companies that offer different vehicle types at various price points.

  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Both taxis and ride-sharing services are readily available in North Las Vegas.

How to Get Around in North Las Vegas

The best way to get around North Las Vegas is by car or bike. However, taxis and ride-sharing services are also available. It’s always a good idea to check with your local accommodation regarding transportation.

Where to Go Next from North Las Vegas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore places other than North Las Vegas, here are some nearby destinations that are worth checking out:

  • Las Vegas Strip: Las Vegas Strip is a must-visit, situated about 15 miles from North Las Vegas. The Strip is home to some of the best casinos, resorts, and restaurants in Las Vegas.

  • Hoover Dam: Hoover Dam is situated just a short drive from North Las Vegas and offers spectacular views of the Colorado River.

  • Valley of Fire State Park: This park features unique rock formations and stunning landscapes and is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.

Conclusion

North Las Vegas is a unique destination that offers a mix of culture, outdoor recreation, and entertainment. It caters to every budget, so you can have an enjoyable experience regardless of how much you have to spend. Book your trip, pack your bags, and get ready to experience North Las Vegas’s unique ambiance and attractions.
