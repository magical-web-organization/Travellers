Introduction to West of I-15

Located in Las Vegas, West of I-15 is an exciting destination for travelers who are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and scenic views. This region of Southern Nevada offers a unique combination of urban and natural experiences, making it a must-visit destination for all types of travelers.

This travel guide will give you an overview of the best things to do and see in West of I-15, including hiking and biking trails, museums, historical places, food and drink scene, popular activities and tours, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in West of I-15

Here is a list of the top attractions and things to see and do in West of I-15:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Neon Museum: This museum is a paradise for lovers of pop culture and neon lights, featuring displays of vintage neon signs from the 1930s to the present day.
  • Las Vegas Natural History Museum: Learn about the diverse wildlife and ecosystems of Nevada at this museum, which features exhibits on desert life, marine life, and prehistoric Nevada.
  • Old Las Vegas Mormon Fort State Historic Park: Take a step back in time to the early days of Las Vegas at this fascinating historic fort, which dates back to 1855.

Natural Attractions of West of I-15: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area: This stunning park is home to a network of hiking trails, climbing routes, and picturesque vistas. Visitors can explore the colorful canyons, see ancient petroglyphs, and spot wildlife like bighorn sheep and desert tortoises.
  • Spring Mountains National Recreation Area: Just a short drive outside of Las Vegas, this recreation area offers endless opportunities for outdoor fun, from hiking and biking to skiing and snowshoeing in the winter.
  • Valley of Fire State Park: Located about an hour away from Las Vegas, this park is home to towering red sandstone formations, petroglyphs, and stunning desert landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West of I-15

For outdoor enthusiasts, West of I-15 is a paradise of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes for exploring the natural beauty of the region:

  • Calico Hills Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is a moderate hike that offers stunning views of the red rock formations and canyons of Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area.
  • Ice Box Canyon Trail: This challenging 2.5-mile trail takes hikers through a narrow canyon filled with boulders and rock formations, eventually leading to a refreshing natural pool.
  • Bristlecone Trail: This 6-mile trail in Spring Mountains National Recreation Area is a lovely hike through dense forests of Ponderosa pine and bristlecone pine, with breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks and valleys.

Best Things to Do in West of I-15: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other exciting things to do in West of I-15, such as:

  • Off-Road Adventures: Take a tour of the region in a Jeep or ATV, getting an up-close look at the stunning landscapes and natural wonders of Southern Nevada.
  • Helicopter Tours: For a bird’s eye view of the region, consider taking a helicopter tour, which will take you high above the canyons, valleys, and forests of the area.
  • Zip Lining: For a thrilling adventure, try zip-lining through the canyons and forests of Bootleg Canyon in Boulder City, just outside of Las Vegas.

Week-Long Itinerary for West of I-15

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to West of I-15:

  • Day 1: Visit the Neon Museum and explore the historic downtown district of Las Vegas.
  • Day 2: Take a hike in Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area and enjoy an off-road Jeep tour of the area.
  • Day 3: Visit the Las Vegas Natural History Museum and explore the shops and restaurants in Summerlin.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Valley of Fire State Park and enjoy hiking and rock climbing in the stunning landscapes.
  • Day 5: Go skiing or snowshoeing in Spring Mountains National Recreation Area, followed by a relaxing afternoon at a local spa.
  • Day 6: Take a helicopter tour of the area and enjoy zip-lining in Bootleg Canyon.
  • Day 7: Explore the food and drink scene of West of I-15, trying local specialties like chile rellenos, cornbread, and craft beer.

Food and Drink Scene of West of I-15

No trip to West of I-15 is complete without sampling the delicious local food and drinks. Some must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chile Rellenos: This classic Mexican dish is made with roasted peppers, stuffed with cheese, and served with rice, beans, and tortillas.
  • Cornbread: Southern-style cornbread is a staple of many local restaurants in West of I-15, with variations ranging from sweet and cake-like to tangy and cornmeal-heavy.
  • Barbecue: Texas-style barbecue is a beloved cuisine in Southern Nevada, with many places offering fall-off-the-bone ribs, juicy brisket, and smoky pulled pork.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Las Vegas has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries producing unique and delicious beers with local flavors and ingredients.
  • Wine: Nevada is home to many small vineyards and wineries, producing delightful vintages that are perfect for pairing with local dishes.
  • Tequila: Tequila cocktails are a staple of many bars and restaurants in West of I-15, with many establishments offering flights of different tequilas for sampling.

There are many excellent lodging options in West of I-15, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • JW Marriott Las Vegas Resort & Spa: This stylish hotel features a world-class spa, championship golf course, and breathtaking views of the nearby mountains.
  • Hostel Cat: For budget travelers, this hostel in downtown Las Vegas offers comfortable dorms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge area.

Local Culture and Customs of West of I-15

Southern Nevada is a melting pot of different cultures and traditions, with a rich history of Native American, Spanish, Mexican, and American influences. Visitors to the area should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Dressing Appropriately: In many tourist areas, it’s acceptable to wear more casual attire, but if you’re visiting cultural attractions or religious sites, it’s important to dress modestly.
  • Tipping: In the United States, it’s customary to leave a gratuity of 15-20% at restaurants, bars, and other service establishments.
  • Being Aware of Drinking Laws: It’s legal to drink alcohol in public in most areas of West of I-15, but it’s important to be mindful of drinking and driving laws.
