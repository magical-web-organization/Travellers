Las Vegas is a city that needs no introduction. Its stunning skyline, vibrant nightlife, and world-renowned casinos and resorts have made it a top tourist destination in the United States. And at the heart of Las Vegas is The Strip, a four-mile stretch of road that’s filled with iconic landmarks, excellent restaurants, and world-class entertainment. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know about The Strip.

Introduction to The Strip

The Strip comprises of the most famous road in Las Vegas, also known as Las Vegas Boulevard. It stretches from the Stratosphere tower in the north to the Mandalay Bay resort in the south. Known for its mega-resorts and casinos, the Strip has numerous attractions, from towering architectural masterpieces to stunning performances by world-class musicians, acrobats, and magicians.

List of Places to Visit in The Strip

If you’re planning a trip to Las Vegas, The Strip should be at the top of your list of things to do. There are plenty of things to see and do here, so we’ve divided the top places into two categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mob Museum: A must-visit attraction for those interested in the mob, crime history and the law enforcement battle with organized crime.
  • The Neon Museum: Discover the history behind the neon lights that have become synonymous with Las Vegas.
  • The Titanic: The Artifact Exhibition: Learn about one of the most famous shipwrecks in history and see artifacts recovered from the Titanic’s wreckage.

Natural Attractions of The Strip: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The High Roller: This iconic attraction is the tallest observation wheel in the world. You’ll be taken on a scenic ride 550 feet above the Las Vegas skyline.
  • The Fountains of Bellagio: The Bellagio Fountains is a choreographed water show that takes place every day in front of the Bellagio Resort.
  • The Eiffel Tower Viewing Deck: At 460 feet tall, this Parisian landmark allows visitors to enjoy breathtaking views of the entire Las Vegas Strip and the surrounding desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Strip

If you prefer exploring the great outdoors, The Strip offers several hiking and biking routes, including:

  • The Lake Mead Trail: A scenic trail that takes you through the stunning Mojave Desert and offers views of Lake Mead.
  • The Red Rock Canyon: This route is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy challenging hikes or mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in The Strip: Activities and Tours

  • Cirque du Soleil: Cirque du Soleil is an immersive acrobatic performance that’s world-famous. Several of their shows are located on The Strip, such as O, Michael Jackson ONE, KÀ.
  • Rollercoaster at New York, New York: It’s one of the oldest and most famous roller coasters on The Strip. This rollercoaster takes you through a replica of Manhattan’s skyline.
  • Grand Canyon Helicopter Tours: Helicopter tours of one of the world’s natural wonders are offered by a number of tour operators on The Strip.

Week-Long Itinerary for The Strip

Here’s a one-week itinerary to help you get the most out of your visit to The Strip:

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at the hotel
Day 2 Visit the Mob Museum and the Neon Museum
Day 3 Take a trip to the Red Rock Canyon
Day 4 Explore the scenic drives that take you across the Mojave Desert.
Day 5 Visit the Eiffel Tower and watch the iconic Bellagio Fountains
Day 6 Take a Grand Canyon helicopter tour
Day 7 Check-out and departure

Food and Drink Scene of The Strip

No visit to Las Vegas would be complete without tasting its signature dishes and exclusive drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages on The Strip:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steakhouse: Las Vegas is known for its steakhouses. Top restaurants include Old Homestead Steakhouse at Caesar’s Palace and Prime Steakhouse at the Bellagio.
  • Buffets: A staple of the Vegas dining scene, the city boasts some of the best buffets in the world. The Buffet at the Wynn and Bayside Buffet at Mandalay Bay are highly recommended.
  • Sushi: With countless sushi spots on The Strip, you can never go wrong with the sake-sipping, sushi-rolling fun.

Local Beverages

  • Frozen Margaritas: Margaritas are a staple in Vegas. Try the famous YARD MARG at Virgil’s Real BBQ in the Linq Promenade.
  • Vegas Bomb: This fruity cocktail is a popular drink that’s well-known in the city.
  • Bloody Mary: Vegas-style Bloody Mary’s are not your average ones, they are signature drinks in most bars and restaurants on The Strip.

Where to Stay in The Strip: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Strip is home to many luxury hotels, including the Venetian Resort, Bellagio, the Cosmopolitan, and the Wynn. The Hard Rock Hotel & Casino and Mandalay Bay are also great options. If you’re looking for something more budget-friendly, an off-strip hotel could save you money.

What to Buy in The Strip: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, The Strip has plenty of options to choose from. These are some of the popular unique items to bring back home:

  • Playing Cards: Several shops along the Strip sell decks of playing cards, which are perfect for your next game night with family and friends.
  • Vegas Souvenir Gift box/ Basket: Local gift baskets include sweet treats, poker chips, shot glasses, and other items that are iconic to Vegas.
  • Stratosphere Tower Merchandise: The top of Stratosphere towers have a gift shop where you can grab some branded merchandise.

Local Culture and Customs of The Strip

It’s important to know that Las Vegas has a more relaxed dress code than many other tourist destinations, thanks to its desert climate. You can wear comfortable and trendy clothes. Especially during the night, the Strip lights up brightly, so dress up to match the scenery.

Best Time to Visit The Strip

The best time to visit The Strip is between March and May, when the temperatures are moderate and the crowd is bearable. September through November also brings lesser crowds, and mild temperatures. Avoid the summer months when temperatures can climb to over 100 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Strip

Do:

  • Bring comfortable shoes, as you’ll likely be doing a lot of walking.
  • Use the free parking spots available.
  • Enjoy MGM Resorts’ Loyalty Scheme and use the MLife rewards card to get some special discounts and perks during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Drink and Drive. Always have a designated driver or choose public transport options.
  • Ignore warning signs for safety reasons.
  • Overspend. Vegas can make you go over-budget, set yourself a realistic budget before you leave, and try and stick to it.

Safety and Health

Safety in The Strip

The Strip may be a bustling area filled with vibrant nightlife, but it’s still important to stay safe and aware. Follow these basic safety tips:

  • Always stick to well-lit areas.
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash everywhere.
  • Use the buddy system at all times.

Health in The Strip

Las Vegas weather can be hot, so be sure to stay hydrated, especially if you’re walking The Strip. Heat stroke is a real concern in the desert, make sure you have sunscreen, and wear a hat. Las Vegas is accommodating when it comes to serving food options catering to dietary requirements, allergies, and cultural preferences, be sure to ask your server about the options.

Getting to The Strip: Transportation Options and Logistics

Las Vegas offers several options for traveling to The Strip.

Airports Near The Strip

The McCarran International Airport is just a short ride away from The Strip. A number of rideshare services, rideshares, taxicabs, and shuttles are available to transport you from the airport to your hotel.

Bus Stations Near The Strip

Las Vegas has a public transportation network, including buses that can take you all along The Strip. The Deuce is the most popular among Las Vegas commuter buses, which operates 24-hours a day and seven days a week.

Other Options

The Strip has many parking options, with valet parking being the most popular option in Las Vegas. Travelers can also rent cars, and limousines services for their stay in Las Vegas, giving them flexibility to explore the city at their convenience.

How to Get Around in The Strip

The Strip is a pedestrian-friendly area with a variety of transportation options to try. These include walking, biking, or via taxi. Rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from The Strip: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Las Vegas is within driving distance of several popular outdoor destinations:

  • The Grand Canyon: A must-visit natural wonder of the world.
  • Hoover Dam: A quick and easy day trip from Las Vegas that offers stunning views of this engineering marvel.
  • Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area: This area features stunning geological formations that will appeal to hikers, rock climbers, and nature lovers.

Conclusion

The Strip is one of the world’s most popular tourist destinations, and with good reason. It’s a vibrant hotspot that offers visitors a chance to experience the best that Las Vegas has to offer. Whether you’re interested in gambling, dining, shopping, live entertainment, or outdoor adventures, The Strip has something for everyone. Stick to our advice, and you’re sure to have a safe, fun, and unforgettable visit to The Strip.
