Introduction to Henderson and East

Henderson and East, located in Southern Nevada near Las Vegas, offer a diverse range of attractions and activities for tourists seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and local cuisine. Whether you love hiking, biking, or scenic drives in natural parks and reserves, or you are interested in museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks, Henderson and East are the perfect destinations for you. In this travel guide, we will introduce you to the best things to do and see in Henderson and East, including a list of places to visit, cultural attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, local cuisine and drinks, and nearby excursions. We will also provide tips on how to get there, where to stay, and when to go. Let’s begin!

List of Places to Visit in Henderson and East

Here are some of the best places to visit in Henderson and East:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clark County Heritage Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the history and culture of Southern Nevada, from Native American artifacts to the days of the Wild West, and the development of Las Vegas.
  • Henderson Bird Viewing Preserve: A wonderful place for bird-watchers and nature lovers, with miles of trails and ponds that attract hundreds of bird species.
  • Ethel M Chocolate Factory and Botanical Cactus Garden: A unique destination that combines sweet treats with natural beauty, featuring a chocolate factory tour and a cactus garden with over 300 species.

Natural Attractions of Henderson and East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Mead National Recreation Area: The largest man-made lake in the US offers endless opportunities for fishing, boating, and water sports, as well as hiking and camping in the surrounding desert.
  • Valley of Fire State Park: A surreal landscape of red sandstone formations, ancient petroglyphs, and hiking trails that lead to hidden canyons and arches.
  • Sloan Canyon National Conservation Area: Home to thousands of petroglyphs and ancient dwellings of the Ancestral Puebloan people, and a popular spot for hiking and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Henderson and East

Henderson and East offer plenty of hiking and biking routes for all levels of fitness and experience. Here are some of the best:

  • River Mountain Loop Trail: A 34-mile paved trail that circles around Henderson and Boulder City, offering stunning views of Lake Mead, the mountains, and the city.
  • Bootleg Canyon Mountain Bike Park: A world-class mountain biking destination with over 24 miles of trails for all skill levels, including downhill and cross-country routes.
  • Black Mountain Trail: A challenging hike that rewards you with panoramic views of Henderson and the Las Vegas valley, with a summit elevation of 5,600 feet.

Best Things to Do in Henderson and East: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are many other things to do and see in Henderson and East:

  • Hoover Dam: A marvel of engineering and a symbol of US progress, the Hoover Dam is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in modern history and architecture.
  • Henderson Events Plaza and Amphitheatre: A vibrant hub of culture and entertainment, hosting concerts, festivals, and art shows throughout the year.
  • Lake Las Vegas: A luxurious destination for golfing, dining, and relaxation, featuring high-end resorts, spas, and waterfront activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Henderson and East

If you have a week to spend in Henderson and East, here’s a sample itinerary that will give you a taste of the best attractions and activities:

Food and Drink Scene of Henderson and East

Henderson and East offer a diverse range of local cuisine and beverages that reflect the region’s cultural heritage and modern sensibilities. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile Stew: A hearty soup made with New Mexico green chiles, pork, and potatoes, served with tortillas or bread.
  • Lobster Bisque: A creamy and rich soup made with lobster meat, cream, and spices, usually served as an appetizer or a side dish.
  • Basque-style Lamb Chops: A flavorful and tender dish that originated in the Basque Country of Spain, featuring grilled lamb chops with garlic, rosemary, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Henderson and East have many microbreweries and taprooms that serve a variety of craft beers, from pale ales to stouts and IPAs.
  • Wine: Nevada is home to many wineries and vineyards that produce unique and delicious wines, especially white and red varietals.
  • Margaritas: A classic cocktail that is popular in many Mexican restaurants and bars in Henderson and East, made with tequila, lime juice, and Cointreau or triple sec.

Getting to Henderson and East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Henderson and East are located about 16 miles south of Las Vegas, and they are easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Henderson and East

  • McCarran International Airport (LAS): The nearest and most convenient airport, located in Las Vegas, with direct flights from many US and international destinations.
  • Henderson Executive Airport (HND): A smaller and regional airport, mainly used for private and corporate flights.

Bus Stations Near Henderson and East

  • Greyhound Station Las Vegas: A bus station located in downtown Las Vegas, with daily connections to cities across the US.
  • Downtown Transportation Center: A bus station located near the Fremont Street Experience, with local and regional connections.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Henderson and East are best explored by car, and there are many rental companies to choose from at the McCarran International Airport and other locations.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Uber and Lyft are available in Henderson and East, as well as traditional taxi services.

How to Get Around in Henderson and East

The best way to get around Henderson and East is by car, as public transportation is limited and the distances between attractions can be large. However, if you don’t have a car, you can use the following options:

  • RTC Bus: The Regional Transportation Commission operates several bus routes that serve Henderson and East, including the Boulder Highway Express, the Henderson and Downtown Express, and the Strip and Downtown Express.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Uber and Lyft are available in Henderson and East, as well as traditional taxi services.
  • Bike Rentals: If you want to explore the city by bike, you can rent a bike from many local shops and providers, such as Vegas Bike Store and Bike Blast Las Vegas.
