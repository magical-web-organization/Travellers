Mesquite, Nevada is a hidden gem located in Southern Nevada in the Southwest region of the United States of America. Known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and outdoor adventures, this city is the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the American Southwest. In this comprehensive guide, we will introduce you to Mesquite and provide you with a list of must-see attractions, the best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Mesquite

Mesquite is a charming city situated in the Virgin River Valley in Southern Nevada. It is located on the border of Arizona and Nevada, making it a great starting point for exploring both states. The city is known for its rugged natural beauty, which includes the impressive Virgin Mountains and the beautiful Virgin River that runs through the city. Mesquite offers a unique blend of outdoor adventure, culture, and history that is perfect for travelers of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Mesquite

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mesquite has a rich history that is reflected in its many museums and historical places. Here are some of the must-visit attractions for history buffs:

  • Virgin Valley Heritage Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving and promoting the history of Mesquite and the surrounding Virgin Valley. It showcases exhibits on local flora and fauna, geology, and the history of the Paiute Native American tribes that once lived in the valley.
  • Mesquite Fine Arts Center: This cultural center and gallery is located in Mesquite’s historic downtown area. It features a rotating collection of contemporary art, including painting, sculpture, and photography.
  • Lost City Museum: Located in nearby Overton, the Lost City Museum showcases the history of the region’s ancient Anasazi people. The museum features artifacts, art, and exhibits on the everyday life of the Anasazi.

Natural Attractions of Mesquite: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mesquite is surrounded by stunning natural beauty that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some must-see attractions for nature lovers:

  • Virgin River Canyon Recreation Area: This expansive recreation area features miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and scenic views of the Virgin River Canyon. It’s also a popular spot for camping, picnicking, and wildlife viewing.
  • Red Cliffs National Conservation Area: Located just south of Mesquite, this stunning conservation area features colorful red rock formations, petroglyphs, and incredible hiking trails.
  • Mesquite Desert Preserve: This 400-acre preserve is located just minutes from downtown Mesquite. It features several hiking trails that wind through arid desert terrain, providing opportunities to see native desert wildlife like bighorn sheep and desert tortoises.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mesquite

Mesquite’s stunning natural scenery makes it ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails in the area:

  • Gold Butte National Monument: This expansive national monument features miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through stunning desert landscapes, slot canyons, and rock formations.
  • Desert Hill Trail: Located just outside Mesquite, this popular hiking trail offers panoramic views of the surrounding Virgin Mountains and Valley.
  • River Mountains Loop Trail: This scenic trail is perfect for bikers and hikers looking for a longer excursion. It offers stunning views of Lake Mead and the surrounding mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in Mesquite: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Mesquite offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some must-do activities:

  • Golf: Mesquite is known as a golfer’s paradise, with several world-class golf courses in the area.
  • ATV or Jeep Tours: Explore the rugged beauty of the region on an ATV or jeep tour.
  • Spa Day: After a day of outdoor adventure, nothing beats a relaxing spa day at one of Mesquite’s many luxurious spas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mesquite

For travelers planning a week-long trip to Mesquite, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Check out the Virgin Valley Heritage Museum and explore the historic downtown area.
  • Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking in the nearby Red Cliffs National Conservation Area.
  • Day 3: Take an ATV or jeep tour to explore the rugged natural beauty of the region.
  • Day 4: Tee off at one of Mesquite’s world-class golf courses.
  • Day 5: Relax with a spa day at one of Mesquite’s luxurious spas.
  • Day 6: Spend the day exploring nearby Lake Mead National Recreational Area.
  • Day 7: Take a scenic drive through the Virgin River Canyon Recreation Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Mesquite

Foodies will love the culinary scene in Mesquite, which offers a variety of delicious local eats and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mesquite Grilled Steak: Indulge in a juicy, perfectly grilled steak cooked over mesquite wood.
  • Navajo Tacos: A southwestern classic, these tacos feature fry bread piled high with hearty toppings like chili, cheese, and onions.
  • Fry Sauce: A local specialty, this dipping sauce is a savory blend of ketchup and mayonnaise.

Local Beverages

  • Virgin River IPA: Made by Mesquite’s own Nevada Beverage Company, this delicious IPA features a bold, hoppy flavor.
  • Bloody Mary: Mesquite is known for its delicious Bloody Marys, made with a blend of spicy tomato juice and your choice of vodka.

Where to Stay in Mesquite: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious resort or a budget-friendly hostel, Mesquite has a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some top picks:

  • CasaBlanca Resort: This luxurious resort is located in the heart of Mesquite and features a casino, spa, and golf course.
  • Virgin River Hotel & Casino: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable accommodations and a range of amenities, including a casino and bowling alley.
  • Best Western Mesquite Inn: This comfortable hotel is located near the historic downtown area and offers easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Mesquite: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mesquite offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Mesquite Wood Art: Mesquite wood is renowned for its beautiful grain patterns, and local artisans create stunning wooden sculptures and other pieces.
  • Native American Jewelry: Mesquite is located near several Native American reservations, and you can find beautiful handmade jewelry at local shops and galleries.
  • Nevada Wine: The nearby Pahrump Valley is home to several wineries that produce delicious Nevada wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Mesquite

Mesquite has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its museums, art galleries, and local festivals. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions and take the time to learn about the city’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Mesquite

The best time to visit Mesquite is in the fall and winter months when temperatures are milder and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Summers in Mesquite can be extremely hot, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mesquite

Do:

  • Take the time to explore Mesquite’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Get out and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the region.
  • Try some of Mesquite’s delicious local eats and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car.
  • Litter or damage natural attractions in the area.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mesquite

Mesquite is generally a safe destination, and visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other city. Visitors should lock their cars and keep valuables out of sight and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Mesquite

Mesquite has several medical facilities in case of emergency, and visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage during their stay.

Getting to Mesquite: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mesquite is located about an hour and a half northeast of Las Vegas. Here are the transportation options for getting to and around Mesquite:

Airports Near Mesquite

  • McCarran International Airport, Las Vegas (LAS): 90 miles from Mesquite.
  • St. George Regional Airport (SGU): 37 miles from Mesquite.

Bus Stations Near Mesquite

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in nearby St. George, UT.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car from Las Vegas or St. George to explore the region.
  • Shuttle Service: Several shuttle services offer transportation from Las Vegas to Mesquite.

How to Get Around in Mesquite

The best way to get around Mesquite is by car. Visitors can rent a car from the airport or take a shuttle service to Mesquite. Some hotels in Mesquite also offer shuttle services to local attractions.

Where to Go Next from Mesquite: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mesquite is located in the heart of the American Southwest, making it the perfect jumping-off point for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some top picks:

  • Grand Canyon: One of the world’s natural wonders is just a few hours’ drive from Mesquite.
  • Zion National Park: Located just a short drive from Mesquite, this stunning national park features towering sandstone cliffs, breathtaking hikes, and stunning vistas.
  • Las Vegas: The entertainment capital of the world is just an hour and a half drive from Mesquite and offers endless nightlife and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Mesquite is a true American gem that offers visitors a little bit of everything: scenic beauty, rich history, outdoor adventure, and cultural enrichment. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to explore Mesquite and all it has to offer. Happy traveling!
