Introduction to Ogden

Located in the beautiful Wasatch Range in Utah, Ogden is a hidden gem offering a plethora of cultural and natural attractions. Surrounded by stunning mountains, the city is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking, biking, or skiing. But it also boasts a diverse cultural scene with museums, theaters, and historical sites. The local food and drink scene is also worth exploring with a unique blend of international cuisines and local brews. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Ogden.

List of Places to Visit in Ogden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Union Station

A popular site for history buffs and train enthusiasts, Union Station was a major transfer point for the Union Pacific and Southern Pacific Railways. The station now houses several museums, including the Utah State Railroad Museum, Browning Firearms Museum, and the John M. Browning Auto Museum.

Eccles Community Art Center

The Eccles Community Art Center is a hub for visual and performing arts, featuring galleries, studios, and a theater. Visitors can participate in art classes, attend live theater performances, and explore the center’s exhibitions.

Hill Aerospace Museum

The Hill Aerospace Museum is a must-see for aviation enthusiasts and military history buffs. The museum features a vast collection of aircraft and exhibits that showcase the history of aviation and the contributions of the Air Force.

Natural Attractions of Ogden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Snowbasin Resort

Snowbasin Resort is a world-class ski resort with over 3,000 acres of ski slopes, nine lifts, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains. During the summer, the resort offers biking trails, concerts, and other outdoor activities.

Ogden River Parkway

The Ogden River Parkway is a 7.5-mile trail that winds along the Ogden River and through the city’s parks. The trail is perfect for walking, biking, or jogging, and offers visitors stunning views of the river and mountains.

Pineview Reservoir

Pineview Reservoir is a popular destination for water sports and offers activities such as swimming, boating, and fishing. The reservoir is surrounded by beautiful mountains and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ogden

Ogden is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The city boasts a vast network of hiking and biking trails, each offering stunning views of the mountains and surrounding landscapes. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Ogden include:

  • Waterfall Canyon Trail
  • Bonneville Shoreline Trail
  • Adams Canyon Trail
  • Skyline Trail
  • Ogden River Parkway Trail

Best Things to Do in Ogden: Activities and Tours

Treehouse Children’s Museum

The Treehouse Children’s Museum is a delightful and creative museum, perfect for children and families to explore and learn. The museum offers a range of exhibits and activities, including a bookstore, puppet theater, art activities, and more.

Guided Tours

Ogden offers a range of guided tours that take visitors through historical sites, breweries, and other local attractions. Some popular tours include:

  • Ogden River Brewing District Tour
  • Historic 25th Street Walking Tour
  • Historic Union Station Tour

Week-Long Itinerary for Ogden

Day 1: Arrive in Ogden, explore the historic 25th Street, and visit some of the museums at Union Station.

Day 2: Hike to Waterfall Canyon and enjoy the beautiful scenery and natural beauty.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Ogden River Brewing District and enjoy some local brews.

Day 4: Drive to Pineview Reservoir and spend the day swimming, boating, or fishing.

Day 5: Explore the Eccles Community Art Center and take a class or attend a performance.

Day 6: Ski or snowboard at Snowbasin Resort, followed by apres ski drinks at one of the resort’s bars.

Day 7: Take a scenic drive along the Wasatch Range and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Food and Drink Scene of Ogden

Must-Try Dishes

Fry Sauce

A Utah classic, fry sauce is a blend of ketchup and mayonnaise and is a popular condiment for french fries, burgers, and other fast foods.

J Dawgs

J Dawgs is a popular hot dog stand that serves up a unique blend of flavors, including secret sauce, sauerkraut, and cream cheese.

Funeral Potatoes

A classic dish at Mormon funerals, funeral potatoes are a cheesy, creamy casserole made with hash browns, sour cream, and cream of chicken soup.

Local Beverages

Roosters Brewing Company

Roosters Brewing Company is a local brewery that serves up a range of craft beers, including their popular Honey Wheat and Nut Brown Ales.

Ogden’s Own Distillery

Ogden’s Own Distillery is a local distillery that produces a range of liquors, including their flagship Five Wives Vodka.

Local Culture and Customs of Ogden

Ogden is a city with rich cultural heritage, particularly with the influence of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. Visitors should be respectful of their customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Ogden

The best time to visit Ogden is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities. But winter is also a popular time to visit, with its world-class ski resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ogden

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors with a hike or bike ride on one of Ogden’s many trails.
  • Try a local brew or dish at a restaurant or brewery.
  • Visit one of Ogden’s cultural attractions, such as the Eccles Community Art Center or Union Station.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Leave valuables in your car when parking in public areas.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ogden

Ogden is generally a safe city, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the outdoors or walking in urban areas at night. In case of emergency, dial 911.

Health in Ogden

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude of Ogden, which can lead to altitude sickness. Drink plenty of water and take it slow when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Ogden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ogden

The nearest airport to Ogden is Salt Lake City International Airport, located about 40 miles south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Ogden

Greyhound offers bus service to Ogden, with a station located in the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Ogden, as the city is located off Interstate 15. Renting a car is also available.

How to Get Around in Ogden

The best way to get around Ogden is by car, although the city does have a bus system, and bike rentals are available.

Where to Go Next from Ogden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salt Lake City

Salt Lake City is a vibrant city with a range of cultural attractions, including the Utah Museum of Fine Arts and Temple Square.

Antelope Island State Park

Antelope Island State Park is a unique destination with stunning natural beauty, including free-roaming bison, antelope, and birdlife.

Conclusion

Ogden is a hidden gem in Utah, offering a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local cuisine. With so much to see and do, visitors can easily spend a week exploring the city and surrounding areas.
