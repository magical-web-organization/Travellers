Introduction to Springville

Nestled in the heart of the Wasatch Range, Springville is a charming town in Utah known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and rich history. With its proximity to the Provo-Orem metropolitan area and various ski resorts, Springville makes an ideal base for exploring the attractions of the southwest.

List of Places to Visit in Springville

Whether you want to spend your days exploring museums and historical sites, hiking and biking scenic trails, or indulging in delicious local food and drink, Springville has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Springville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Springville has a rich history dating back to the 1800s, and visitors can learn about the town’s past by exploring its many museums and historical sites, including:

  • Springville Museum of Art: This premier art museum showcases works by local and national artists, including many from the famous Springville Art Museum Collection.
  • Historic Springville Carnegie Library: Built in 1905, this beautiful building is now home to the Springville Senior Center and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Springville Pioneer Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of Springville and the surrounding area, featuring exhibits on early settlers, Native Americans, and pioneers.

Natural Attractions of Springville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Springville is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, with the Wasatch Mountains rising up on one side and the Utah Valley stretching out on the other. Here are some of the best ways to explore the outdoors in Springville:

  • Hobble Creek Canyon: This scenic canyon offers ample opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and picnicking, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and wildlife.
  • Spanish Fork Peak: For experienced hikers, the challenging ascent of Spanish Fork Peak is rewarded with breathtaking views over the Utah Valley and surrounding mountains.
  • Sundance Resort: Just a short drive from Springville, the famous Sundance Resort offers skiing, snowboarding, hiking, mountain biking, and other outdoor activities year-round.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Springville

Springville’s stunning natural beauty makes it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Springville:

  • Provo River Parkway Trail: This paved trail runs along the Provo River for over 15 miles, offering scenic views of the river and surrounding countryside.
  • Mill Canyon Peak Trail: This moderate trail offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, with a total distance of about 5 miles.
  • Hobble Creek Canyon Trail: This trail offers beautiful views of the canyon and surrounding forests, with several options for different skill levels and distances.

Best Things to Do in Springville: Activities and Tours

Aside from outdoor adventures and cultural attractions, Springville also has a range of activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are a few ideas for how to spend your time in Springville:

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the stunning scenery of the Wasatch Mountains on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Hot Air Ballooning: Take in the breathtaking views of Springville and the surrounding mountains from above with a hot air balloon tour.
  • Ghost Tours: Learn about Springville’s paranormal past on a spooky ghost tour of the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Springville

If you’re planning a trip to Springville, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in this charming town:

Day 1: Arrive in Springville and explore the town, including the Springville Museum of Art and historic downtown.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking in the beautiful Hobble Creek Canyon and enjoying a picnic lunch in the great outdoors.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the famous Sundance Resort, where you can ski, snowboard, or enjoy other outdoor activities.

Day 4: Visit the Springville Pioneer Museum to learn about the town’s fascinating history, then head to the Spanish Fork Peak for a challenging hike with incredible views.

Day 5: Enjoy a leisurely morning exploring Springville’s local food and drink scene, then take a guided horseback ride through the Wasatch Mountains.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon tour of the area and admire the stunning landscape from above, then finish the day with a spooky ghost tour of Springville’s haunted spots.

Day 7: Depart Springville and head out to explore other nearby attractions and destinations, like the Great Salt Lake or Arches National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Springville

Springville is known for its delicious local food and drink scene, offering everything from classic American fare to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample while you’re in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry sauce: This tangy, creamy dipping sauce is a Utah specialty and is often served with french fries or onion rings.
  • Funeral potatoes: This cheesy, comforting casserole dish is a staple of Mormon culture and is often served at potlucks and family gatherings.
  • Navajo tacos: A delicious Southwestern dish made with frybread topped with seasoned ground beef, lettuce, tomato, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Utah craft beer: Utah’s craft beer scene is thriving, with breweries like Uinta Brewing, Wasatch Brewery, and Epic Brewing Company based in the state.
  • Fry sauce soda: This unique soda flavor is brewed by Utah-based soda company Lester’s Fixins and tastes just like the famous condiment.

Where to Stay in Springville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Springville offers a range of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s needs and budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Springville:

  • Hobble Creek Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast offers comfortable accommodations and delicious breakfasts in a scenic countryside setting.
  • Holiday Inn Express Springville-South Provo Area: This modern hotel offers clean, comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a heated indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Springville Inn: This affordable motel offers basic but comfortable rooms in a convenient downtown location.

What to Buy in Springville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique memento of your trip to Springville, here are some local souvenirs and products to consider:

  • Pioneer-themed merchandise: Springville has a rich pioneer history, and you can find a range of products inspired by this heritage, including clothing, home decor, and more.
  • Local artisanal foods: Utah is known for its delicious artisanal foods, such as local honey, jam, and cheese, which make great gifts and souvenirs.
  • Native American crafts: Utah is home to several Native American tribes, and you can find beautiful handcrafted jewelry, pottery, and textiles made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Springville

Springville is a welcoming and friendly town, and visitors can enjoy learning about the town’s unique customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Springville:

  • Respect religious traditions: The majority of the population in Springville is Mormon, and visitors should be respectful of local religious traditions and customs.
  • Be prepared for changes in weather: Springville experiences a range of weather conditions throughout the year, so be sure to pack accordingly and check the forecast before your trip.

Best Time to Visit Springville

Springville can be enjoyed year-round, with each season bringing its own unique attractions. Here’s a breakdown of the best time to visit Springville depending on your interests:

  • Summer: Summer is a great time to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as attending local fairs and festivals.
  • Fall: Fall is a beautiful time to visit Springville, with stunning fall foliage and mild weather perfect for outdoor activities.
  • Winter: Winter in Springville means skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports at nearby resorts like Sundance.
  • Spring: Spring is a lovely time to visit Springville, with flowers blooming and warmer weather perfect for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Springville

When visiting Springville, be sure to keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Take advantage of outdoor activities: Springville’s stunning natural beauty provides ample opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor adventures.
  • Explore the local food and drink scene: Springville offers delicious local specialties and unique beverages to sample.
  • Learn about local history: Springville’s rich history is reflected in its many museums and historical sites, which offer fascinating insight into the town’s past.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs: Springville has a unique culture and history, and visitors should be respectful of local traditions and customs.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment: Visitors should take care of the beautiful natural environment of Springville by following Leave No Trace principles and not littering.

Safety and Health

Springville is a safe and healthy destination for travelers, but visitors should be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions.

Safety in Springville

  • Weather conditions: Springville experiences a range of weather conditions throughout the year, including extreme heat in the summer and heavy snowfall in the winter. Visitors should be prepared for changes in weather and take necessary precautions.
  • Wildlife safety: Visitors to Springville’s outdoor areas should be aware of potential wildlife encounters and take necessary precautions, such as carrying bear spray and storing food securely.

Health in Springville

  • Altitude sickness: Springville is located at a high altitude, which can cause altitude sickness in some people. Visitors should be aware of the symptoms and take necessary precautions, such as drinking plenty of water and taking it easy for the first few days of their trip.

Getting to Springville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Springville is easily accessible from other parts of Utah and the surrounding areas. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind when planning your trip:

  • Airports Near Springville: The Salt Lake City International Airport is just a 45-minute drive from Springville and offers flights to destinations around the world.
  • Bus Stations Near Springville: The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in Provo, just a short drive from Springville.
  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car at the Salt Lake City International Airport or in nearby towns like Provo.

How to Get Around in Springville

Springville is a walkable town, but visitors may also want to rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding areas. Some options for getting around in Springville include:

  • Walking: Many of Springville’s attractions and restaurants are located within walking distance of each other.
  • Biking: Springville has several bike trails and paths, and visitors can rent bikes from local shops like ReRide Bikes.
  • Driving: Visitors may want to rent a car to explore the surrounding areas, as public transportation can be limited.

Where to Go Next from Springville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Springville is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and attractions. Here are a few ideas for where to go next from Springville:

  • Provo: This nearby college town offers a range of shopping, dining, and outdoor activities, as well as attractions like the Brigham Young University Museum of Art.
  • Arches National Park: A few hours’ drive from Springville, this stunning national park is home to some of the most iconic rock formations in the country.
  • Great Salt Lake: Located just over an hour’s drive from Springville, the Great Salt Lake is a unique natural wonder famous for its salt content and picturesque sunsets.

Conclusion

As you can see, Springville offers a wide range of attractions, activities, and experiences for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, hiking and biking scenic trails, or enjoying local food and drink, Springville has something for everyone.
