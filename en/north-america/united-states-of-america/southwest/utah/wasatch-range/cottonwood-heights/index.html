Introduction to Cottonwood Heights

Cottonwood Heights is a suburban city located in Salt Lake County, Utah. It is situated at the foothills of the Wasatch Range, just 20 minutes south of Salt Lake City. The town boasts breathtaking scenery, including stunning mountain views and beautiful canyons. The climate is mild, with plenty of sunshine all year-round, making it an ideal destination for outdoor activities. Cottonwood Heights is home to around 34,000 residents and is surrounded by other charming towns such as Sandy, Holladay, and Murray.

List of Places to Visit in Cottonwood Heights

There are several cultural and natural attractions in Cottonwood Heights that you shouldn’t miss during your visit. To help you plan your trip, we’ve compiled a list of places to visit in Cottonwood Heights.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Butler Gully Trailhead: This trailhead is located in the heart of Cottonwood Heights and is surrounded by stunning mountain views and beautiful forests. It is a popular spot for hiking and mountain biking.

  • Solitude Mountain Resort: This ski resort is located just 10 minutes from Cottonwood Heights and boasts some of the best skiing and snowboarding in Utah. It is also home to several restaurants and a spa.

  • Big Cottonwood Canyon: This beautiful canyon is located just outside of Cottonwood Heights and is home to stunning views, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

Natural Attractions of Cottonwood Heights: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Twin Peaks Trailhead: This trailhead is located in Big Cottonwood Canyon and is a perfect place for hiking and mountain biking. The scenery is breathtaking, and the trails are well-maintained.

  • Silver Lake Trailhead: This trailhead is located in the heart of Big Cottonwood Canyon and is a fantastic place for hiking and birdwatching. The trail loops around Silver Lake, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Mount Olympus Trailhead: This trailhead is located just outside of Cottonwood Heights and is a challenging hike that rewards you with jaw-dropping views of the Salt Lake Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cottonwood Heights

Hiking and biking are two of the most popular outdoor activities in Cottonwood Heights. There are plenty of trails to choose from, ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Cottonwood Heights include:

  • Mill D North Trail: This trail is an easy, 1.7-mile hike that offers stunning views of Big Cottonwood Canyon.

  • Lake Blanche Trail: This trail is a moderate, 6.9-mile hike that leads to a beautiful alpine lake surrounded by stunning mountain scenery.

  • Mount Raymond Trail: This trail is a challenging, 6.5-mile hike that takes you to the summit of Mount Raymond, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Cottonwood Heights: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are other exciting things to do in Cottonwood Heights. Some of the best activities include:

  • Skiing and snowboarding: Cottonwood Heights is home to several world-class ski resorts, including Snowbird and Solitude. Whether you’re a beginner or an expert, there’s a slope for everyone.

  • Try local cuisine: Cottonwood Heights boasts a fantastic food scene, with plenty of restaurants serving up local delicacies such as fry sauce, pastrami burgers, and green Jell-O.

  • Take a scenic drive: From Cottonwood Heights, you can take a scenic drive through Big Cottonwood Canyon, Little Cottonwood Canyon, or Guardsman Pass, offering stunning views of the surrounding scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Cottonwood Heights

Cottonwood Heights is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants, cafes, and bars to choose from. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastrami Burger: This local delicacy is a burger topped with thin slices of pastrami, melted Swiss cheese, and a special sauce.

  • Fry Sauce: This tangy dipping sauce is a mix of ketchup, mayonnaise, vinegar, and spices.

  • Green Jell-O: This dessert is a local favorite and is made with Jell-O, whipped cream, and mini marshmallows.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beer: Cottonwood Heights boasts several local breweries, including Proper Brewing, which serves up delicious craft beers.

  • Cider: You can try locally-made ciders at Red Rock Brewing.
