Introduction to Antelope Island

Antelope Island is a 42 square-mile island located in the Great Salt Lake, Utah. With stunning natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage, it is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs and families alike. The island is home to over 500 bison, as well as other wildlife including pronghorn antelope and bighorn sheep. We have created a comprehensive guide to help you plan your perfect trip to Antelope Island.

List of Places to Visit in Antelope Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Antelope Island State Park Visitor Center: The visitor center is an excellent place to start your journey. It offers a wealth of historical and natural information about the island through various exhibits, presentations and events.
  • Fielding Garr Ranch: The historic Fielding Garr Ranch, built in the mid-1800s by Mormon pioneers, offers visitors an insight into the life of the early settlers of the area.
  • White Rock Bay Petroglyphs: A fascinating look at the ancient Native American rock art located in the northeast corner of the island.

Natural Attractions of Antelope Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buffalo Point: One of the island’s most picturesque spots, Buffalo Point offers visitors a panoramic view of the Great Salt Lake and Wasatch Mountains.
  • Frary Peak Trail: Take the challenging hike to the summit of Frary Peak, the island’s highest peak. The stunning views make the climb worthwhile.
  • Antelope Island Shoreline Trail: A 15-mile trail that boasts scenic vistas of the lake and the islands surrounding it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Antelope Island

Antelope Island offers over 20 miles of biking and hiking trails. From easy, flat routes to challenging terrain, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Lakeside Trail: A relatively easy trail that offers stunning views of the Great Salt Lake shoreline.
  • Split Rock Bay Trail: A moderate hike that takes you through some of the island’s most picturesque landscapes.
  • Elephant Head Trail: A difficult trail that leads to a rocky outcropping that resembles an elephant’s head.

Best Things to Do in Antelope Island: Activities and Tours

  • Bison Roundup Experience: Participate in the island’s annual buffalo roundup where participants get to work alongside rangers to help move the bison to their winter feeding grounds.
  • Horseback Riding: Saddle up and ride through the island’s beautiful landscapes on horseback.
  • Kayaking or Paddle-boarding: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the lake up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Antelope Island

Day 1: Arrival in Salt Lake City, Utah. Drive to Antelope Island, check-in at your accommodation, explore the island’s visitor center and take an easy hike in the park.

Day 2: Start your day with a challenging hike to Frary Peak. After lunch, set off on a kayaking or paddle-boarding adventure around the island.

Day 3: Take a guided horseback ride through the island’s backcountry, followed by a picnic lunch overlooking the lake.

Day 4: Visit the historic Fielding Garr Ranch, located in the southern section of the island. In the afternoon, hike the Split Rock Bay Trail.

Day 5: Explore the White Rock Bay Petroglyphs, followed by a scenic drive around the island, stopping at Buffalo Point and other viewpoints.

Day 6: Take part in the annual Bison Roundup with the rangers. Afterward, enjoy a sunset picnic before returning to your accommodation.

Day 7: Departure from Antelope Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Antelope Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burgers: A must-try food item on the island, it is served at the island’s visitor center as well as the Island Buffalo Grill.
  • Game Meat Tasting: Try a variety of game meat such as elk, venison and bison at Island Buffalo Grill.
  • Fry Sauce: Locally popular condiment made with ketchup and mayo, it is found at almost every restaurant on the island.

Local Beverages

  • Solitude Brewery: Located in nearby Salt Lake City, Solitude Brewery offers a variety of locally crafted beers that are sold exclusively on Antelope Island.

Where to Stay in Antelope Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bridger Bay Campground: Located on the island, the campground offers both tent and RV camping options.
  • Island Buffalo Grill and Lodge: A rustic lodge located on the island that offers lodge rooms and cabins.
  • Salt Lake City: If you prefer, you can stay in Salt Lake City, which is just a short drive from the island.

What to Buy in Antelope Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bison Figurines: A popular souvenir, these figurines can be found at the visitor center as well as many of the island’s gift shops.
  • Local Honey: Made from the flowers that grow on the island, local honey is a delicious and unique product to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Antelope Island

The island played an important role in the traditional culture of the Northern Pauite and Shoshone Indian tribes who continue to visit the island for ceremonies and other cultural events. Visitors are requested to treat all cultural sites with respect.

Best Time to Visit Antelope Island

The best time to visit Antelope Island is between March and November when the weather is warmest. The summer months can be hot so it is advised to bring plenty of water and sunscreen. The annual Bison Roundup takes place in October, an event that attracts visitors from around the world.

Do’s and Don’ts of Antelope Island

Do:

  • Do respect all posted signs about staying within designated areas.
  • Do bring plenty of water and sunscreen.
  • Do dress in layers as the weather can change quickly and dramatically.

Don’t:

  • Don’t approach or feed the wildlife.
  • Don’t swim in the lake (due to high salt content).
  • Don’t disturb or remove any cultural artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Antelope Island

Antelope Island is generally considered a safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking vehicles and valuables. The island is remote, and visitors should be prepared for emergencies.

Health in Antelope Island

There are no medical facilities located on the island. Visitors should bring any necessary medications and be prepared for minor medical emergencies. In a serious emergency, visitors can be airlifted to nearby Salt Lake City.

Getting to Antelope Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Antelope Island is located approximately 50 miles north of Salt Lake City, Utah.

Airports Near Antelope Island

  • Salt Lake City International Airport: The closest commercial airport, approximately 60 minutes away from the island.

Bus Stations Near Antelope Island

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Salt Lake City, approximately 50 miles south of the island.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Many car rental agencies operate out of the Salt Lake City International Airport.

How to Get Around Antelope Island

The easiest way to get around the island is by car. Visitors can also explore the island by bike or hiking.

Where to Go Next from Antelope Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Great Salt Lake State Park: Located on the southeastern shore of the lake, it offers visitors a chance to swim, boat, or fish on the lake’s relatively calm waters.
  • Salt Lake City: Utah’s capital city is home to a number of attractions such as the Temple Square, the Utah State Capitol Building, and the Natural History Museum of Utah.

Conclusion

Antelope Island is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history and culture. From hiking and biking to experiencing the bison roundup, there is something for everyone on the island. Plan your perfect trip to Antelope Island using our comprehensive guide.
