Introduction to Lehi

Located in the scenic Wasatch Range of Utah, Lehi is a city with a rich blend of history and modernity. The city offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages, from outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and delicious local cuisine. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Lehi, Utah.

List of Places to Visit in Lehi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Thanksgiving Point - A complex that features several museums such as the Museum of Natural Curiosity, Museum of Ancient Life, Butterfly Biosphere, and Farm Country.
  • John Hutchings Museum of Natural History - A museum showcasing a large collection of insects, taxidermy animals, and fossils.
  • Lehi Heritage Museum - A museum dedicated to preserving the history of Lehi and its early Pioneer settlers
  • Peteetneet Museum and Cultural Arts Center - A historic mansion showcasing the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Lehi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • American Fork Canyon - A beautiful canyon with scenic drives and hiking trails.
  • Provo River Parkway Trail - A popular trail for biking, running, and hiking along the Provo River.
  • Alpine Loop Scenic Byway - A stunning drive through the Wasatch Mountains that offers breathtaking views of nature.
  • Timpanogos Cave National Monument - A network of caves that is accessible through a guided tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lehi

  • Dry Canyon Trail - A moderate hike featuring beautiful waterfalls and wildflowers.
  • Battle Creek Falls Trail - A family-friendly hike with a beautiful waterfall and mountain views.
  • Timpanogos Cave Trail - A guided tour through the stunning Timpanogos Cave National Monument.
  • Provo River Parkway Trail - A popular trail for biking, running, and hiking along the Provo River.

Best Things to Do in Lehi: Activities and Tours

  • Thanksgiving Point Gardens - A magnificent garden complex featuring over 50 acres of gardens.
  • Cornbelly’s Corn Maze - A fun-filled autumn-inspired attraction for families.
  • Outlets at Traverse Mountain - The biggest outlet mall in Utah with over 50 stores.
  • Skydive the Wasatch - A once-in-a-lifetime experience to skydive in the scenic Wasatch Mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lehi

Day Activity
1 Visit all of the museums located in Thanksgiving Point
2 Drive along the Alpine Loop Scenic Byway and go on a nature hike
3 Visit the John Hutchins Museum of Natural History and go mountain biking on the Provo River Parkway Trail
4 Visit Peteetneet Museum and Cultural Arts Center and go rock climbing at American Fork Canyon
5 Go on a guided tour of Timpanogos Cave and visit Lehi Heritage Museum
6 Spend the day exploring the Outlets at Traverse Mountain
7 Visit the Butterfly Biosphere and explore the various gardens at Thanksgiving Point

Food and Drink Scene of Lehi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Sauce - A delicious dipping sauce made of ketchup and mayonnaise that is commonly found in Utah.
  • Jell-O - Utah is famous for its love of Jell-O, and it can be found in all sorts of variations, from sweet salads to desserts.
  • Scones - A popular breakfast food in Utah, scones are deep fried and served with honey butter or cinnamon sugar.
  • Funeral Potatoes - A popular potato casserole dish that is typically served at funerals and potlucks.

Local Beverages

  • Utah Beer - Utah has a growing craft beer scene with breweries like Hoppers, Wasatch, and Squatters.
  • Apple Beer - A non-alcoholic drink that tastes like apple cider and root beer.
  • Fry Sauce - While it’s not technically a drink, Fry Sauce is a popular condiment that Utahns love to pair with their meals.

Where to Stay in Lehi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Springhill Suites by Marriott - A modern and comfortable hotel located near the Outlets at Traverse Mountain.
  • Tru by Hilton - A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Lehi, great for business or leisure travelers.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites - A convenient location close to American Fork Canyon, Timpanogos Cave National Monument, and Thanksgiving Point.

What to Buy in Lehi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey - Utah is famous for its honey production, and there are several local honey farms in the area.
  • Pioneer Artifacts - Lehi has a rich history of early Pioneer settlers, and you can find many artifacts and antiques to bring home as souvenirs.
  • Jell-O Mix - As mentioned before, Utah loves its Jell-O, and you can find many different varieties of Jell-O mix in local grocery stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Lehi

Utah has a unique culture that is deeply influenced by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and dress modestly when visiting religious landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Lehi

The best time to visit Lehi is during the summer months from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and most outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lehi

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious landmarks.
  • Embrace the local culture and cuisine.
  • Be courteous and polite when speaking with locals.

Don’t:

  • Use foul language or be disrespectful towards local customs.
  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Be disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lehi

Lehi is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take the usual precautions when traveling.

Health in Lehi

Visitors should be aware of the high altitude and dry climate of Utah and stay hydrated at all times. UV rays can be intense, so it is advised to wear sunscreen and protective clothing when outdoors.

Getting to Lehi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lehi

  • Salt Lake City International Airport - Located about 30 miles north of Lehi, this is the closest international airport.
  • Provo Municipal Airport - Located about 15 miles south of Lehi, this airport offers regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Lehi

  • FrontRunner Commuter Rail Station - Located in Lehi and providing easy access to Salt Lake City and other local cities.

Other Options

  • Rideshare Services - Services such as Uber and Lyft are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Lehi

The best way to get around Lehi is by car. Several companies offer rental cars, and taxi services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Lehi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Salt Lake City - The state capital and the largest city in Utah, known for its historic landmarks, shopping, and dining.
  • Park City - A resort town featuring world-class skiing and snowboarding during the winter months and hiking and mountain biking during the summer.
  • Moab - A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, featuring stunning red rock formations, hiking, and mountain biking trails.

Conclusion

Lehi is a vibrant and exciting city with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the natural wonders of the area, learning about the rich history and culture of Utah, or simply enjoying the local cuisine, Lehi is the perfect destination. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Lehi, Utah!
