Introduction to Spanish Fork

Nestled in the stunning Wasatch Range of Utah, Spanish Fork is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Known for its charming downtown area, beautiful natural scenery, and rich history, this vibrant city has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, ski, or simply relax and soak in the sights, Spanish Fork is the perfect destination for your next adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Spanish Fork

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Art

Located on the campus of Brigham Young University, the Museum of Art features an impressive collection of paintings, sculptures, and other works of art from around the world. Whether you’re an art aficionado or simply appreciate beauty and creativity, this museum is a must-visit.

Springville Museum of Art

Another art museum in the area worth checking out is the Springville Museum of Art. Known for its impressive collection of European and American art, this museum is a great place to spend an afternoon exploring.

Religious historical sites

Spanish Fork is also home to several historically significant religious sites, such as the Spanish Fork Presbyterian Church and the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints.

Natural Attractions of Spanish Fork: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spanish Fork Canyon

One of the most scenic drives in Utah is through Spanish Fork Canyon, which is part of the Wasatch Range. This winding road offers breathtaking views of the mountains and surrounding landscape, and is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and camping.

Diamond Fork Hot Springs

If you’re looking to relax and soak in some natural hot springs, Diamond Fork Hot Springs is the perfect destination. Located just a short drive from Spanish Fork, these hot springs offer crystal clear water and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Sundance Ski Resort

For the ski and snowboard enthusiasts, Sundance Ski Resort is just a short drive from Spanish Fork. With amazing runs, fantastic views, and plenty of amenities, Sundance is the perfect destination for a winter weekend getaway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spanish Fork

Spanish Fork offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Utah.

Bridal Veil Falls Trail

Located in the nearby town of Provo, Bridal Veil Falls Trail is a moderately difficult hike that offers fantastic views of the surrounding mountains and waterfalls.

Spanish Fork Peak Trail

For a more challenging hike, the Spanish Fork Peak Trail is a 10-mile round trip hike that reaches an elevation of over 10,000 feet. Along the way, you’ll pass through beautiful meadows, forests, and rocky peaks.

Best Things to Do in Spanish Fork: Activities and Tours

Spanish Fork Farmers Market

If you’re looking to sample some of the best local food and produce, the Spanish Fork Farmers Market is the perfect destination. Featuring vendors from around the region, this market is open every Saturday from June through October.

Spanish Fork Fair Days

For a fun-filled family event, Spanish Fork Fair Days is not to be missed. Featuring carnival rides, live music, food vendors, and much more, this annual event is a great way to experience the local culture and traditions.

Spanish Fork Water Park

If you’re looking to cool off and have some fun in the sun, the Spanish Fork Water Park is a great destination. Featuring water slides, a lazy river, and plenty of other water attractions, this park is perfect for families and enthusiasts alike.

Food and Drink Scene of Spanish Fork

Must-Try Dishes

Green Chile Burritos

A local favorite, green chile burritos are a must-try when visiting Spanish Fork. Made with spicy green chiles, beans, and cheese, these hearty burritos are a great way to start your day.

Rocky Mountain Oysters

For the more adventurous foodies out there, Rocky Mountain Oysters are a uniquely local delicacy. Made from bull testicles, these deep-fried morsels are said to be surprisingly delicious.

Fry Sauce

A Utah classic, fry sauce is a combination of ketchup and mayo that is great for dipping fries or any other fried foods.

Local Beverages

Uinta Brewing Company

One of the top craft breweries in Utah, Uinta Brewing Company offers a range of delicious beers, from IPAs to stouts.

Sodalicious

If you’re looking for a unique local beverage, Sodalicious is a must-try. This soda shop offers a range of creative and delicious soda combinations, such as Piña Colada and Root Beer Floats.

Where to Stay in Spanish Fork: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Western Mountain View Inn

Located just off I-15, the Best Western Mountain View Inn offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location. Guests can enjoy amenities such as a fitness center, an indoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast.

Holiday Inn Express & Suites

Another great option for travelers is the Holiday Inn Express & Suites. This hotel offers modern rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and a range of other amenities.

What to Buy in Spanish Fork: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Candles from Jefferson House

Located in downtown Spanish Fork, Jefferson House offers a range of beautifully scented candles made from high-quality materials.

Crafts from Spanish Fork Harvest Days

If you’re looking for unique and handmade crafts, Spanish Fork Harvest Days is the perfect destination. This annual event features local artists and craftspeople, offering everything from pottery to jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Spanish Fork

Utah is known for its strong religious culture, and Spanish Fork is no exception. Visitors should be aware of local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Spanish Fork

The best time to visit Spanish Fork is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spanish Fork

Do:

  • Do dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Do explore the stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the altitude; Spanish Fork is at a high elevation.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and plenty of water for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spanish Fork

Spanish Fork is a very safe city, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Make sure to stay on marked trails and bring plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Spanish Fork

Visitors to Spanish Fork should be aware of the high altitude and take precautions accordingly. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary medications and consult a healthcare professional before traveling.

Getting to Spanish Fork: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spanish Fork

The closest major airport to Spanish Fork is Salt Lake City International Airport, which is located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Spanish Fork

Visitors can take Greyhound buses to Spanish Fork, which has a bus station downtown.

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to drive can reach Spanish Fork via Interstate 15, which runs through the city.

How to Get Around in Spanish Fork

The best way to get around Spanish Fork is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the Salt Lake City International Airport or at rental car agencies in Spanish Fork.

Where to Go Next from Spanish Fork: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Provo

Located just a short drive from Spanish Fork, Provo is a charming college town known for its beautiful scenery, great food, and lively music scene.

Zion National Park

For nature lovers, Zion National Park is a must-visit. Located about three hours’ drive from Spanish Fork, this stunning national park features towering red cliffs, winding rivers, and a range of hiking trails.

Conclusion

Spanish Fork is a fantastic destination for adventurers, foodies, art enthusiasts, and anyone looking to explore beautiful natural scenery. With plenty of things to do, see, and try, this hidden gem of Utah is not to be missed.
