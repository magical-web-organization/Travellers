Introduction to Alta

Alta is a small town in the Wasatch Range of Utah known for its world-class skiing, stunning natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors come to Alta year-round to explore its pristine alpine trails, soak in its hot springs, and immerse themselves in the town’s unique history and local culture.

If you’re planning a trip to Alta, read on for our comprehensive guide to the town’s must-see attractions, best hiking and biking routes, most delicious local cuisine, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Alta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alta is a historical mining town, and the town’s rich history is on proud display at several local museums and historic sites.

  • Alta Museum: Located in the Albion Basin, the Alta Museum is dedicated to preserving the town’s mining history and the many cultures that have called Alta home. The museum features artifacts from the town’s early mining days, displays on local geology, and much more.

  • Alta Historic Town Hall: Constructed in 1895, the Alta Historic Town Hall is one of the town’s oldest buildings. The building has been beautifully restored and features a small museum on the second floor with exhibits on early mining life and the town’s unique ecology.

  • Our Lady of the Snows Chapel: This beautiful little chapel was built in 1939 and is the center of religious life in Alta. The chapel features stunning stained glass windows and an exquisite hand-carved alter and pulpit.

Natural Attractions of Alta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alta is one of the most beautiful places on earth, and there’s no shortage of natural attractions to explore here. From epic hiking trails to pristine lakes and waterfalls, Alta has it all.

  • Alta Ski Area: Alta is perhaps best known for its world-class ski resort. The resort is situated in a stunning alpine bowl and boasts some of the best skiing terrain in the country. In the summer months, the area is transformed into a stunning mountain playground with endless hiking and biking trails to explore.

  • Cecret Lake: This stunning alpine lake is situated just above Albion Basin and is a popular spot for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife watching. The lake is surrounded by wildflowers and stunning mountain views.

  • Sunset Peak Trail: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Sunset Peak, where you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of the surrounding mountain ranges. The trail is about 5 miles round-trip and gains over 2,000 feet of elevation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alta

Alta is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with hundreds of miles of trails to explore. Whether you’re looking for a leisurely stroll through an alpine meadow or a challenging hike to the top of a peak, Alta has it all.

  • High Rustler Trail: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Mt. Baldy, one of the highest peaks in the area. The trail is steep and rocky, but the views from the top are truly breathtaking.

  • Albion Meadows Trail: This easy, 2-mile trail takes you through one of the most beautiful alpine meadows in the area. The trail is known for its stunning wildflowers and colorful fall foliage.

  • Alta Lakes Trail: This intermediate-level hike takes you to a series of stunning alpine lakes nestled in the heart of the Wasatch Range. The trail is about 5 miles round-trip and gains about 1,500 feet of elevation.

Best Things to Do in Alta: Activities and Tours

Alta is a town that’s always buzzing with activity, and there are always new adventures to be had here. Whether you’re looking to hit the slopes for some world-class skiing, soak in a hot spring or indulge in some delicious local cuisine, Alta has it all.

  • Snowbird Ski Resort: This world-class ski resort is located right next door to Alta and offers an incredible variety of terrain for skiers and snowboarders of all levels.

  • Alta Hot Springs: These natural hot springs are located just a short drive from town and offer a relaxing, rejuvenating soak in the midst of stunning alpine scenery.

  • Alta Peruvian Lodge: This historic lodge is one of the most popular spots in town for dining and entertainment. The lodge hosts regular live music performances and is known for its delicious locally sourced cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Alta

Must-Try Dishes

Alta’s cuisine is a reflection of its unique history and local culture, with plenty of hearty mountain fare and locally sourced ingredients.

  • Bison Burgers: Made from locally sourced bison meat and served on a freshly baked bun, these burgers are a must-try for any meat lover.

  • Utah Trout: Freshly caught from local rivers and lakes, Utah trout is a local delicacy that’s best enjoyed grilled or fried.

  • Honeycomb Ice Cream: Made with local honey and cream, this rich and creamy ice cream is the perfect way to end any meal.

Local Beverages

Utah is known for its craft beer scene, and Alta is no exception. Here are a few local beverages to try during your visit.

  • Alta Ale: This locally brewed beer is a favorite among locals and visitors alike. Brewed with locally sourced ingredients, the beer has a bold, hoppy flavor and a smooth finish.

  • Utah Cider: Made with fresh, locally sourced apples, Utah cider is crisp, refreshing, and perfect for sipping on a warm summer afternoon.

Where to Stay in Alta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alta has a variety of lodging options to suit every budget and taste, from luxurious mountain lodges to cozy bed and breakfasts.

  • The Rustler Lodge: This luxurious mountain lodge is located right at the base of Alta Ski Resort and offers stunning mountain views, a full-service spa, and gourmet dining.

  • Alta Lodge: This historic lodge dates back to the early 1940s and is a beloved local institution. The lodge features cozy rooms with rustic decor, a communal hot tub, and a delicious full-service restaurant.

What to Buy in Alta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to take home from Alta, there are plenty of options to choose from.

  • Handmade Pottery: Local artisans create stunning pottery inspired by the natural beauty of the area. From mugs to plates to vases, there’s something to suit every taste.

  • Local Honey: Alta is home to several local beekeepers who produce some of the best honey in the state.

Local Culture and Customs of Alta

Alta has a rich cultural heritage, with a unique mix of Native American, European, and mining cultures.

  • Mining Heritage: The town’s mining history is still visible in many of its buildings and cultural attractions. Take some time to explore the town’s mining museums and historic sites to learn more about this fascinating part of Alta’s history.

  • Native American Culture: Alta is in the traditional homeland of the Ute tribe, and their rich cultural heritage is still celebrated in the area today. Look for local cultural events and exhibitions for a chance to learn more about this important local culture.

Best Time to Visit Alta

Alta is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests.

  • Winter: If you’re a skier or snowboarder, winter is the best time to visit. The ski season runs from November to April, and the skiing is world-class.

  • Summer: If you’re interested in hiking, biking, or exploring the town’s outdoor attractions, summer is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alta

Do:

  • Do Respect the Environment: Alta is a stunningly beautiful and fragile ecosystem. Please respect the area’s natural beauty and take care not to disturb local wildlife or damage fragile ecosystems.

  • Do Try Local Cuisine: Alta is home to some of the best mountain food in the country. Be sure to try local specialties like bison burgers, Utah trout, and honeycomb ice cream.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Litter or Pollute: Alta’s beauty is only possible because of its pristine environment. Please help keep the area clean and beautiful by not littering or polluting.

  • Don’t Trespass on Private Property: Alta is a small town with a tight-knit community. Please respect the privacy of local residents and avoid trespassing on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alta

Alta is a safe and welcoming town, but as with any outdoor adventure destination, it’s important to take some basic safety precautions.

  • Be Prepared for Weather: Alta’s weather can be unpredictable, even in the summer months. Be sure to bring appropriate clothing and gear for your outdoor activities, including sunscreen and insect repellent.

  • Stay on Marked Trails: Alta is home to some challenging and remote hiking trails. To avoid getting lost or injured, always stay on marked trails and carry a map and compass.

Health in Alta

Alta is a healthy and active community, but it’s important to take care of your health during your visit.

  • Stay Hydrated: Alta is at a high altitude, and it’s easy to become dehydrated. Be sure to drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol and caffeine, which can exacerbate dehydration.

  • Take Altitude Sickness Seriously: Altitude sickness is a real possibility in Alta, especially for visitors who are not used to the altitude. Be sure to rest as needed and seek medical attention if you experience symptoms like headache, dizziness, or shortness of breath.

Getting to Alta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Alta is easy thanks to several nearby airports and transportation options.

Airports Near Alta

  • Salt Lake City International Airport: Located just 45 minutes from Alta, Salt Lake City International Airport is the best option for visitors flying into the area.

Bus Stations Near Alta

  • Alta Ski Area Shuttle: This shuttle runs from Salt Lake City to Alta Ski Area, with stops at several nearby hotels and resorts.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: There are several rental car companies located at Salt Lake City International Airport, making it easy to get a car for your visit.

How to Get Around in Alta

Alta is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of one another. However, you may need a car or other transportation depending on your plans.

  • Free Local Shuttle: The Alta Ski Area Shuttle offers free transportation around the town and to nearby ski resorts.

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at Salt Lake City International Airport for those who prefer to drive themselves.

Where to Go Next from Alta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the area around Alta, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out.

  • Salt Lake City: Just a short drive from Alta, Salt Lake City is Utah’s largest city and is home to several world-class museums, cultural attractions, and restaurants.

  • Park City: Located just a short drive from Alta, Park City is a picturesque mountain town known for its skiing, shopping, and dining.

  • Timpanogos Cave National Monument: This stunning national park is located just a short drive from Alta and features beautiful caves, waterfalls, and mountain views.

Conclusion

Alta is a town that truly has it all - stunning natural beauty, rich cultural traditions, and a unique history that’s never far from sight. Whether you’re a skier, a hiker, a food lover, or a history buff, you’ll find plenty to enjoy in Alta. So come explore this mountain paradise for yourself and discover all that this delightful town has to offer.
