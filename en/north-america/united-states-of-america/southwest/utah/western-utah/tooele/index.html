Introduction to Tooele

Located in Western Utah, Tooele is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural attractions, rich culture, and adventurous activities. This picturesque city is surrounded by mountains, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

In this Tooele travel guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Tooele

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tooele has a rich history and culture, which is evident from its museums and historical attractions. Here are the top places to visit to experience the unique culture of Tooele:

  • Tooele Valley Museum & Historical Park: This museum showcases the history and culture of Tooele since its founding in the mid-1800s. Visitors can witness exhibits that cover various aspects of Tooele, including military history, mining, transportation, agriculture, and more.
  • Benson Grist Mill: This is a restored flour mill that is now a museum. Visitors can experience the process of flour milling, and learn about the history of wheat farming and irrigation in Tooele County.
  • Deseret Peak Complex: This is a recreational center that hosts various cultural events throughout the year, including rodeos, concerts, and festivals. It features an indoor arena, outdoor amphitheater, soccer fields, and more.

Natural Attractions of Tooele: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tooele is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, offering visitors the opportunity to enjoy a variety of outdoor activities. Here are the top natural attractions to visit in Tooele:

  • Stansbury Island: This is a large island located in the Great Salt Lake. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, bird watching, rock climbing, and more. It also offers some of the most stunning views of the sunset over the Great Salt Lake.
  • Oquirrh Mountains: These mountains offer some of the best hiking trails in Tooele. Visitors can hike to the top of the Oquirrh Mountains to witness the panoramic views of Tooele Valley.
  • Bonneville Salt Flats: This is a unique natural wonder and is one of the largest salt flats in the world. It stretches over 30,000 acres and is known for its mirror-like reflection of the sky.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tooele

Tooele provides an exhilarating outdoor experience to hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Tooele:

  • Deep Creek Trail: This is a 6.3-mile trail that offers breathtaking views of Deseret Peak and the surrounding mountains. Hikers can also witness the majestic Deep Creek waterfalls on this trail.
  • Skyline Trail: This is a 19.7-mile trail that connects Tooele Valley to the Wasatch Mountains. It offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Stansbury Front Trail: This is a 9-mile trail that runs along the Stansbury Mountains and provides breathtaking views of the Great Salt Lake. Bikers can also enjoy this trail.

Best Things to Do in Tooele: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Tooele offers visitors a range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best places to visit for fun and adventure:

  • Miller Motorsports Park: This is a world-class racetrack that hosts various racing and sports events throughout the year. Visitors can rent or drive their cars on the track.
  • Tooele Army Depot: This army depot has a fascinating history and plays a vital role in the defense of the United States. Visitors can tour and learn about the history and importance of this army depot.
  • Clark Historic Farm: This is a historic farm that showcases the lifestyles and culture of the early settlers of Tooele. Visitors can tour the farm and learn about the agriculture and lifestyles of the pioneers.

Food and Drink Scene of Tooele

Tooele has a diverse food scene, offering visitors a range of culinary delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Tooele:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Real Food: This is a locally-owned restaurant that serves home-style cooking with a hint of Southern flair. They are known for their buttermilk fried chicken, topped with country gravy.
  • El Charro Loco: This is a family-owned restaurant that serves authentic Mexican food. Their steak fajitas and chimichangas are a must-try.
  • Lorin Farr’s Candy Cupboard: This is a candy store that has been in business since 1934. They offer a variety of candies, including fudge, chocolate-covered nuts, and more.

Local Beverages

  • Tooele Brewing Company: This is a local brewery that offers a range of craft beers. Their most popular beer is the Lake Effect Gose, a sour wheat beer.
  • Spirits of the Rockies Distillery: This is a local distillery that produces vodka, gin, and whiskey. Their vodka is made with locally sourced wheat and potatoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Tooele

Tooele has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Tooele is a conservative community, and visitors should dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Tooele

Tooele is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. Here is a breakdown of the best time to visit Tooele:

  • Summer (June to August): This is the peak tourist season, and visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and water sports.
  • Fall (September to November): This is a great time to visit Tooele, as the weather is pleasant, and the fall colors are stunning.
  • Winter (December to February): Tooele experiences heavy snowfall during the winter, making it an ideal destination for winter sports enthusiasts.
  • Spring (March to May): This is a great time to visit Tooele if you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy the spring blooms.
