Introduction to Milford

Milford, located in Western Utah, is a hidden gem in the American Southwest. The small town is surrounded by a stunning landscape of mountains, valleys, and basins, making it a hotspot for outdoor enthusiasts. Beyond its natural beauty, Milford also has a rich cultural heritage, with plenty of museums and historical sites to explore. This comprehensive travel guide to Milford will help you plan your trip and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Milford

There are plenty of places to visit and things to do in Milford, from cultural attractions to natural wonders. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Milford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Milford Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the rich history and culture of Milford and the surrounding area. It features exhibits on the town’s pioneers, Native American tribes, and local industries.

  • Minersville Museum: Located in nearby Minersville, this museum offers a fascinating look into the history of mining in the region.

  • Fort Deseret State Park: This historic site was once a military outpost used to protect settlers from Native American attacks. Visitors can explore the restored adobe buildings and learn about the area’s history.

Natural Attractions of Milford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mineral Mountains: These stunning mountains are a popular destination for hikers, nature enthusiasts, and rock climbers. The rugged terrain offers a variety of trails and challenges for all skill levels.

  • Beaver Dam Wash National Conservation Area: This protected area features rugged canyons, natural arches, and stunning rock formations. It’s a popular destination for hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing.

  • Milford Flat: This vast expanse of salt flats is a unique and beautiful natural wonder. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the area and learn about its ecology and geology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milford

Milford has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Here are a few of the top routes to try:

  • Black Dragon Canyon Trail: This challenging 5-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the Mineral Mountains and takes hikers along a narrow canyon with towering rock formations.

  • Milford Flat Petroglyphs Trail: This 2-mile hike leads visitors to a site of ancient petroglyphs, which offer a glimpse into the culture and art of the Native American tribes that once lived in the area.

  • Oak Grove Trail: This 4-mile trail takes hikers through a scenic canyon with a stunning waterfall and lush vegetation. It’s a great spot for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Milford: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and exploring the natural beauty of Milford, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Milford:

  • ATV tours: Milford is surrounded by public lands with miles of designated ATV trails. Rent an ATV and explore the backcountry of the Mineral Mountains.

  • Stargazing: Milford is known for its clear skies and lack of light pollution, making it an ideal destination for stargazing. Book a tour with a local astronomy guide to learn about the constellations and galaxies visible in the night sky.

  • Rockhounding: The Mineral Mountains are a popular destination for rockhounds. Visitors can search for unique and rare minerals and fossils in the rugged terrain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milford

With so much to see and do in Milford, it’s easy to fill a week with activities and adventures. Here is a suggested itinerary for a seven-day trip to Milford:

1 Visit the Milford Museum and explore the town

6 Stargaze with a local astronomy guide

Food and Drink Scene of Milford

Milford may be a small town, but it still has plenty of options for food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Bread: This traditional Native American dish is a staple in Milford. It’s a fried dough that can be served sweet or savory, and is often topped with chili or honey.

  • Jell-O Salad: Jell-O salads are a classic comfort food in Utah. They are made with Jell-O, fruit, and sometimes marshmallows or whipped cream.

  • Green Jello with Carrots: This is a Utah classic that often makes it to the dinner table on special occasions. It’s a lime Jell-O salad with carrots, cream cheese, and sometimes canned pineapple.

Local Beverages

  • Brigham Tea: This tea made from the leaves of the Ephedra plant, which grows locally in Milford. It has a natural stimulant effect and is said to have medicinal properties.

  • Utah Microbrews: Milford is home to several microbreweries that produce locally crafted beers. Try a pint of the local brew at one of the town’s pubs or restaurants.

Where to Stay in Milford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Milford, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are a few suggestions:

  • The Milford Plaza: This budget-friendly motel is located in the heart of town and offers clean, comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

  • Whispering Pine Guesthouse: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a historic building in the center of town. It offers comfortable rooms and a homey atmosphere.

  • Mineral Mountains RV Park: This RV park is situated on the outskirts of town and offers full hookups and a variety of recreational amenities.

What to Buy in Milford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, Milford has several options. Here are some local products to consider:

  • Native American Crafts: Milford and the surrounding area is home to several Native American tribes, who produce traditional crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

  • Mineral Specimens: The Mineral Mountains are known for their unique and unusual minerals. Look for local shops that sell specimens and other mineral-related souvenirs.

  • Local Art: Milford has a thriving art community, with several galleries and shops selling locally produced paintings, sculptures, and other pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Milford

Milford has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its traditions, art, and crafts. The town is also known for its friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and engage with the community to learn more about their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Milford

The best time to visit Milford is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and the landscape is lush and green. Summers can be hot, but the evenings are cool and pleasant. Winter can be cold and snowy, and many of the outdoor attractions may be closed due to the weather. Check local weather forecasts and plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Milford

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Milford:

Do:

  • Engage with the local community and learn about their way of life.

  • Respect cultural heritage sites and artifacts.

  • Pack plenty of sunscreen and water for outdoor activities.

  • Plan ahead and book accommodations and tours in advance.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb wildlife.

  • Ignore safety warnings or signs on hiking trails and other outdoor areas.

  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Milford is generally a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should take precautions when exploring the outdoors. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Milford:

Safety in Milford

  • Always wear sunscreen and bring plenty of water when exploring the outdoors.

  • Stay on designated hiking trails and obey all safety warnings.

  • Keep a safe distance from wildlife, and never feed or approach animals.

Health in Milford

  • Altitude sickness can be a concern for visitors to Milford, which sits at an elevation of over 5,000 feet. Drink plenty of water and take rest breaks when hiking to acclimate to the altitude.

  • Milford has limited medical facilities, so visitors should pack any necessary medications and be prepared for emergencies.

Getting to Milford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Milford is located in a remote area of Utah, but it’s still accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options:

  • By car: Milford is located off of I-15, which runs north-south through Utah and connects to several major cities. Visitors can also take Highway 21, which leads directly into town.

  • By air: The closest airport is Cedar City Regional Airport, which is located about 85 miles south of Milford. Visitors can rent a car or book a shuttle to Milford.

Airports Near Milford

  • Cedar City Regional Airport: 85 miles

  • St. George Regional Airport: 120 miles

  • McCarran International Airport (Las Vegas): 220 miles

Bus Stations Near Milford

  • Greyhound Station (Cedar City): 85 miles

Other Options

  • Private shuttle services are available from nearby cities such as Cedar City and St. George.

  • Rental car services are available at the airports and at several locations in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Milford

Milford is a small town, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Visitors with a car can easily explore the surrounding area and access more remote attractions such as the Mineral Mountains.

Where to Go Next from Milford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Milford is located in a beautiful and diverse region of Utah, with plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Here are some suggestions:

  • Bryce Canyon National Park: This iconic park, known for its stunning red rock formations and hoodoos, is located about 100 miles east of Milford.

  • Zion National Park: This popular park, with its towering cliffs and emerald pools, is located about 125 miles southwest of Milford.

  • Cedar Breaks National Monument: This stunning natural wonder, with its vibrant colors and dramatic amphitheater, is located about 70 miles south of Milford.

Conclusion

Milford may be a small town, but it’s rich in natural beauty and cultural heritage. From hiking and rockhounding to exploring museums and historical sites, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem of the American Southwest.
