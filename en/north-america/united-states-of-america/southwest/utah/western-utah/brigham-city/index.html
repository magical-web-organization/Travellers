Introduction to Brigham City

Welcome to Brigham City, a charming city nestled in the heart of Western Utah, known for its rich history, scenic outdoor attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a nature lover seeking outdoor adventures or a cultural enthusiast wanting to explore the city’s vibrant arts and culture scene, Brigham City has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Brigham City’s top attractions that you must see, where to stay, eat, and shop, and the best time to explore this hidden gem. So buckle up and let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Brigham City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brigham City Museum of Art and History: Immerse yourself in the city’s rich history and culture at the Brigham City Museum of Art and History. The museum hosts a variety of exhibits, displays, and educational programs highlighting the city’s unique heritage.

  • Box Elder Tabernacle: A stunning example of early pioneer architecture, the Box Elder Tabernacle is a must-visit historical landmark in Brigham City. The building dates back to 1897 and houses an impressive pipe organ and beautiful stained-glass windows.

  • Golden Spike National Historic Site: A testament to the technological innovations and achievements of the 19th century, the Golden Spike National Historic Site is where the Transcontinental Railroad was completed in 1869. Today, visitors can explore the site’s exhibits, participate in guided tours, and even ride a replica of a historic locomotive.

Natural Attractions of Brigham City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge: Birdwatchers and nature lovers, rejoice! Brigham City is home to the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge, one of the largest birding destinations in the Western Hemisphere. With over 80,000 acres of wetlands, the refuge is a prime spot for observing migratory birds in their natural habitat.

  • Brigham City Canyon: A scenic drive through the breathtaking Brigham City Canyon is a must-do while visiting the city. The canyon boasts a variety of hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and stunning vistas.

  • Mantua Reservoir: Fishermen and water-sports enthusiasts will delight in the beauty of Mantua Reservoir, a popular spot for boating, kayaking, and fishing. The reservoir is surrounded by the stunning scenery of the Wasatch Mountains and the Wellsville Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brigham City

  • Brigham City Canyon Trail: This 3.4-mile trail is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels, offering scenic views of the canyon’s flora and fauna.

  • Steeplechase Trail: This 4.9-mile trail is popular among more experienced hikers, winding through the hills above Brigham City and offering stunning views of the valley below.

  • Perry Canyon Trail: This 8.3-mile trail takes hikers through a picturesque landscape of pine trees, wildflowers, and streams, with breathtaking views of the mountains in the distance.

Best Things to Do in Brigham City: Activities and Tours

  • Brigham City Farmers Market: Every Saturday from June to October, the Brigham City Farmers Market comes alive with the freshest produce, crafts, and artisanal goods from local vendors.

  • Beehive Cheese Factory: Cheese lovers won’t want to miss a visit to the Beehive Cheese Factory, where you can tour the facility and sample some of their delicious award-winning cheeses.

  • Segway Tour of Brigham City: Take an exciting and informative Segway tour of the city’s historical and cultural landmarks, led by knowledgeable local guides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brigham City

Day 1: Visit the Brigham City Museum of Art and History, followed by a scenic drive through Brigham City Canyon.

Day 2: Hike the Brigham City Canyon Trail, followed by a visit to the Box Elder Tabernacle.

Day 3: Explore the Golden Spike National Historic Site and take a ride on a replica locomotive.

Day 4: Spend the day at the Bear River Migratory Bird Refuge, followed by a visit to the Mantua Reservoir.

Day 5: Take a Segway tour of the city’s landmarks and visit local craft shops.

Day 6: Sample delicious cheeses at Beehive Cheese Factory, followed by a visit to the Perry Canyon Trail.

Day 7: Enjoy a day at the Brigham City Farmers Market, followed by a delicious meal at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Brigham City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Funeral Potatoes: A beloved local comfort food, this dish consists of diced potatoes, cheese, sour cream, and a crunchy cornflake topping.

  • Fry Sauce: A Utah staple, this tangy and creamy dipping sauce is the perfect accompaniment to French fries and other fried snacks.

  • Jell-O Salad: A quirky yet delicious side dish, Jell-O salad is a fruity gelatin dessert mixed with canned fruit and marshmallows.

Local Beverages

  • Fry Sauce Soda: This unique soda flavor pays homage to the famous Utah dipping sauce and is a must-try for adventurous taste buds.

  • Caffe Ibis Coffee Roasters: Brigham City is home to one of Utah’s most famous coffee shops, offering locally roasted beans and delicious baked goods.

  • Wasatch Brewery: Beer enthusiasts will enjoy a visit to the Wasatch Brewery, where you can taste a variety of locally brewed beers.

Where to Stay in Brigham City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Crystal Inn Hotel & Suites: This modern hotel offers comfortable and spacious rooms, a complimentary breakfast buffet, and a fitness center.

  • Days Inn by Wyndham Brigham City: Located in the heart of the city, this budget-friendly hotel offers clean and cozy rooms, a continental breakfast, and an outdoor pool.

  • Park Hotel of Brigham City: This historic hotel dates back to 1891 and has been restored to its former glory, with beautifully appointed rooms and charming décor.

What to Buy in Brigham City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Famous Fry Sauce: Bring home a bottle of the famous Utah dipping sauce, available at local supermarkets and souvenir shops.

  • Handmade Crafts: Brigham City is known for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, with a variety of handmade products available at local shops and markets.

  • Local Honey: Utah’s beekeeping industry is thriving, and local honey is a delicious and healthy souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Brigham City

Brigham City residents are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors should expect a warm reception when exploring the city. The city also has a rich pioneer heritage, with many cultural events and festivals celebrating the area’s unique history throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Brigham City

The best time to visit Brigham City is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller compared to the busy summer months. However, each season has its own unique attractions, including skiing and winter sports in the winter, and outdoor adventures and festivals in the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brigham City

Do:

  • Do visit the local farmer’s market and try local produce and crafts.

  • Do explore the city’s outdoor attractions, including the Brigham City Canyon and Mantua Reservoir.

  • Do respect the area’s pioneer heritage and participate in local cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the area’s outdoor attractions.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to local customs or traditions.

  • Don’t forget to try the local cuisine and support small businesses in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brigham City

Brigham City is a safe and friendly city, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should take common-sense precautions when exploring unfamiliar areas, such as staying aware of their surroundings and avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.

Health in Brigham City

Visitors to Brigham City should take care to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen, as the area’s dry and sunny climate can be harsh for those unaccustomed to it. Additionally, altitude sickness can be a concern for visitors coming from lower elevation areas, so it’s important to stay hydrated and take breaks as needed to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Brigham City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brigham City

The nearest airports to Brigham City are Salt Lake City International Airport, located approximately 65 miles south of the city, and Ogden-Hinckley Airport, located approximately 20 miles southwest of Brigham City.

Bus Stations Near Brigham City

Greyhound offers bus service to Brigham City, with a stop at the Brigham City Depot.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore the area, or take a private shuttle service from the airport to Brigham City.

How to Get Around in Brigham City

The most convenient way to get around Brigham City is by car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, as well as a small public bus system operated by the city.

Where to Go Next from Brigham City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Logan: Just a short drive from Brigham City, Logan is home to Utah State University and features a charming downtown area with local boutiques and restaurants.

  • Ogden: Located approximately 30 minutes from Brigham City, Ogden is a historic city with a thriving arts and culture scene, as well as beautiful outdoor attractions.

  • Salt Lake City: Utah’s capital city is located approximately 60 miles south of Brigham City and offers a variety of attractions, including museums, theaters, and outdoor recreational opportunities.

Conclusion

From outdoor adventures in the beautiful Wasatch Mountains to exploring the city’s rich pioneer heritage, Brigham City has something for everyone. Whether you’re planning a week-long vacation or a weekend getaway, make sure to add this hidden gem to your travel bucket list.
