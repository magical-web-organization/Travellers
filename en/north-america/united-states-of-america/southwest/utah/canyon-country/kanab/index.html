Introduction to Kanab

Kanab is a small, charming town in Southern Utah’s Canyon Country. Known as the “Gateway to the Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument,” Kanab is surrounded by breathtaking scenery and outdoor adventures. It’s an ideal destination for hikers, nature enthusiasts, and those seeking a relaxing retreat from the city’s hustle and bustle.

This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan a memorable trip to Kanab. From must-visit attractions, hiking routes, accommodations, and food to local customs, safety tips, and transportation options, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Kanab

In Kanab, you can explore cultural landmarks and natural wonders. Here are the must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kanab Heritage House Museum: Learn about Kanab’s pioneers, Western film history, and area’s geology through impressive exhibits.

  • Little Hollywood Museum: If you’re a movie buff, then this is a must-visit attraction. Get a glimpse of the sets and props used in famous Western movies, including Gunsmoke and The Lone Ranger.

  • Jacob Hamblin Home: Step back in time and tour the home of Jacob Hamblin, a Mormon pioneer, and important historical figure.

Natural Attractions of Kanab: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument: Kanab serves as a gateway to one of Utah’s most remarkable national monuments. The Grand Staircase boasts awe-inspiring landscapes, natural arches, rugged canyons, and colorful ridges.

  • Coral Pink Sand Dunes State Park: Take a peaceful hike or go sandboarding at one of Utah’s top picturesque spots.

  • The Wave: This geological wonder is located in the Vermilion Cliffs area and is a mesmerizing sandstone formation that will take your breath away. Do note that a permit is required to visit the Wave, so plan accordingly.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kanab

Kanab is a hiker and biker’s paradise. The following routes vary in difficulty, so make sure to pick one that fits your skill level. Don’t forget to pack plenty of water and sunscreen!

  • Wire Pass Trail: This beautiful trail runs through a narrow slot canyon and leads you to the famous Buckskin Gulch.

  • White Pocket Trail: A less crowded but equally beautiful alternative to the Wave. This route features stunning white sandstone formations, including scenic overlooks.

  • Angels Landing Trail: This challenging hike will test your limits, but the panoramic views of Zion National Park make it worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Kanab: Activities and Tours

Kanab offers a variety of activities and tours to maximize your vacation. Here are some recommended options:

  • ATV Tours: Take an ATV tour of the surrounding trails, and let the guide show you hidden gems and off the beaten path spots.

  • Stargazing Tours: Kanab is known for its dark skies, and what better way to admire it than with a guided stargazing tour.

  • Horseback Riding: Spend the day with a local guide on horseback and experience the rugged beauty of Utah’s backcountry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kanab

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long getaway in Kanab:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Kanab, check-in, and explore the town
2 Visit the Kanab Heritage House Museum. In the afternoon, take an ATV tour
3 Hike the Angels Landing Trail in Zion National Park
4 Go on a horseback riding tour in the morning, then visit the Coral Pink Sand Dunes State Park
5 Spend the day at the Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument
6 Take a tour of the Little Hollywood Museum in the morning. In the evening, go stargazing
7 End your trip with a visit to The Wave and a picnic at White Pocket.

Food and Drink Scene of Kanab

Kanab offers a unique food and drink scene. Make sure to sample the following:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kanabecue: A fusion of traditional barbeque and Kanab’s unique spices.

  • Fry Bread: A traditional Native American dish that is also a staple in Utah’s cuisine.

  • Navajo Tacos: Another dish that combines traditional Native American and Mexican flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Prickly Pear Margaritas: Made with prickly pear cactus juice, this margarita is famous in the Southwest.

  • Utah-made beer: Home to several breweries, Kanab boasts of original beers that are worth trying.

  • Local wines: Utah is not known for wine production, but its unique climate produces high-quality, fruity wines.

Where to Stay in Kanab: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kanab offers several accommodation options that cater to every budget and preference. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Canyons Lodge, a Canyons Collection Property: A top-rated hotel that boasts of luxurious amenities and a beautiful view of the surrounding scenery.

  • Parry Lodge: An iconic hotel that attracts history and Hollywood enthusiasts, thanks to its famous guests such as John Wayne and Frank Sinatra.

  • Kanab Creekside Cabins: A budget-friendly option that is still cozy and comfortable.

What to Buy in Kanab: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kanab offers several unique souvenirs that reflect its natural beauty and cultural heritage. Here’s what to add to your shopping list:

  • Sand Art: Artisans create sand paintings and sculptures of the surrounding landscapes that make for a unique and beautiful gift.

  • Handcrafted Pottery: Kanab boasts of local potters that create intricate pieces that reflect the region’s scenery.

  • Kanab Artisanal Soaps: Made with locally-sourced ingredients, Kanab’s soaps not only smell amazing but are also great for your skin.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanab

Kanab’s culture is deeply rooted in the area’s history and natural heritage. Respect for the environment and the community is key. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect the outdoors: When hiking or exploring the local scenery, make sure to take your trash with you and avoid damaging the flora and fauna.

  • Respect the Native American Heritage: The area has a rich Native American heritage that should be respected.

  • Local Etiquette: Kanab’s residents are warm and friendly, and it’s not uncommon to strike up a conversation with a stranger. However, it’s important to respect their privacy and boundaries.

Best Time to Visit Kanab

The best time to visit Kanab is between March and November when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. However, keep in mind that summers can be hot, and crowds might be heavy in peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanab

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Kanab:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and locals.

  • Take your time and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

  • Take advantage of the guided tours for a more authentic experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t rely solely on GPS when exploring the area. Keep a paper map handy as cell phone signals can be weak or nonexistent.

  • Don’t go off-trail when hiking.

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen, water, and appropriate clothing when exploring.

Safety and Health

Kanab is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, it’s essential to keep in mind the following:

Safety in Kanab

  • Avoid leaving valuables in your car, especially in remote trailheads.

  • Check the weather forecast when planning outdoor activities and avoid hiking or biking alone.

  • Know the safety protocols in case of an emergency.

Health in Kanab

  • Stay hydrated and avoid sunburn by applying sunscreen SPF 30 or higher.

  • Kanab’s high elevation can cause altitude sickness in some visitors, so make sure to acclimate slowly and drink plenty of water.

  • Keep in mind the area’s wildlife, including snakes, scorpions, and spiders. Take necessary precautions to avoid encounters.

Getting to Kanab: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kanab is accessible via several transportation options, including:

  • Airports Near Kanab: The nearest airports are St. George Regional Airport (SGU) and Cedar City Regional Airport (CDC).

  • Bus Stations Near Kanab: Greyhound Bus Lines and Southern Utah Stage Lines offer daily bus service to Kanab.

  • Other Options: Kanab is easily accessible via car or RV along US Route 89 or Utah Highway 89.

How to Get Around in Kanab

Kanab is a small town, and most of the attractions are accessible by foot or bike. However, a car or ATV is necessary to explore the surrounding trails. Rental cars, ATVs, and bikes are available in Kanab.

Where to Go Next from Kanab: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kanab serves as a gateway to several other exciting destinations. Here are some places to consider visiting next:

  • Bryce Canyon National Park: Located about 70 miles away from Kanab, Bryce Canyon is home to awe-inspiring natural wonders such as Hoodoos and the Queen’s Garden trail.

  • Lake Powell: About 76 miles from Kanab, Lake Powell offers stunning scenery and plenty of water activities, including fishing and boating.

  • Page, Arizona: Located just across the Utah border, three hours from Kanab, Page offers a variety of attractions, including the famous Antelope Canyon and Horseshoe Bend.

Conclusion

Kanab is a gem hidden in Southern Utah’s Canyon Country. From scenic views to cultural landmarks, hiking trails, and local delicacies, Kanab has something for everyone. Keep this comprehensive guide handy to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Kanab.
