Introduction to Torrey

Looking for an adventure in a small town? Look no further than Torrey, Utah. Nestled in the heart of Canyon Country, Torrey offers outdoor enthusiasts the chance to explore the beauty of the desert landscape. Known for its towering red rock formations, Torrey is a gateway to three National Parks: Bryce Canyon, Capitol Reef, and Zion; all easily accessible from Torrey.

This ultimate tour guide to Torrey will help you plan your trip to this hidden gem of Utah. From hiking and biking routes to local culture and customs, we have compiled everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to Torrey.

List of Places to Visit in Torrey

Torrey may be small, but it offers an abundance of attractions for its visitors. You won’t be bored while staying in Torrey.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Whether you’re a history buff or just interested in learning more about the local culture, Torrey has a range of museums and historical sites to explore.

  • The Ripple Rock Nature Center houses exhibits on the geology and ecology of the area; it is the perfect space to learn about the flora and fauna in the canyons surrounding Torrey.
  • Behunin Cabin gives a glimpse into how the pioneers used to live in the late 19th century.
  • Gifford Homestead is an interesting spot due to its history and, during the summer months, you can buy baked goods and other handcrafted items from the locals in a makeshift farmer’s market that is hosted outside the structure. It is a unique and culturally significant experience.

Natural Attractions of Torrey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Torrey is famous for its breathtaking scenery and outdoor activities. The following are the top must-visit outdoor attractions in Torrey:

  • Capitol Reef National Park provides many different hikes, amazing geological formations, and the opportunity to see Native American petroglyphs. It’s the best place to start with and grasp the beauty of this town.
  • Fish Lake National Forest, only 30 minutes away from Torrey, offers boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • Boulder Mountain is a favorite spot for both hiking and fishing. It is widely known for its scenic views; make a stop at the Hells Backbone Grill for lunch, it is worth enjoying.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Torrey

Torrey is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with a wide variety of trails and routes for all skill levels.

  • The Hickman Bridge Trail in Capitol Reef National Park offers an easy hike ideal for families. It has a striking view of the Capitol Reef, which makes it an excellent location for a photo-op.
  • The Windgate Trail has more of a moderate difficulty level and is perfect for those who like a bit of a challenge, with a distance of 2.4 miles.
  • The Cohab Canyon Trail is a longer hike with distance of 4.6 miles, and much more strenuous, with fantastic views over the park and some amazing canyon scenery along the way.
