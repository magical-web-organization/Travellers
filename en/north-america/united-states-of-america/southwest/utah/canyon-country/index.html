Introduction to Canyon Country

Canyon Country in Utah is located in the Southwest region of the United States. It is a landscape full of unique rock formations, vibrant red canyons, and natural arches. This destination offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, culturally important attractions, and mouthwatering local cuisine. In this guide, we’ll go over everything you need to know about visiting Canyon Country.

List of Places to Visit in Canyon Country

Whether you are a nature enthusiast or a history buff, Canyon Country has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places you should not miss on your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Edge of the Cedars State Park Museum: This museum features prehistoric artifacts from the ancient Puebloan culture that lived in the area.
  • Bluff Fort Historic Site: Learn about the pioneer history of Canyon Country at this restored fort.
  • John Wesley Powell River History Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Colorado Plateau and the way the river shaped the region.

Natural Attractions of Canyon Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arches National Park: Famous for its natural arches, this national park offers unforgettable hikes and scenic drives.
  • Canyonlands National Park: This park is home to breathtaking vistas and red rock formations that are sure to leave you in awe.
  • Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park: Take a guided jeep tour through the sandstone towers and buttes that tower over the desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canyon Country

Canyon Country is a hiker’s paradise. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Delicate Arch: This three-mile hike in Arches National Park leads to the iconic Delicate Arch, a must-see in Canyon Country.
  • Devil’s Garden Loop Trail: Explore several stunning rock formations, including Landscape Arch, on this seven-mile hike in Arches National Park.
  • Slickrock Bike Trail: If you’re looking for a challenging mountain biking adventure, this 10-mile trail in Moab is for you.

Best Things to Do in Canyon Country: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Soar high above the red rock formations at sunrise for an unforgettable experience.
  • River Rafting: Take a guided rafting trip down the Colorado River for an adventure you won’t forget.
  • Stargazing: Canyon Country is known for its clear, dark skies. Head out on a stargazing tour to see the Milky Way in all its glory.

Week-Long Itinerary for Canyon Country

Day 1: Arches National Park
Day 2: Canyonlands National Park
Day 3: Edge of the Cedars State Park Museum and Bluff Fort Historic Site
Day 4: San Juan River Rafting
Day 5: Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park
Day 6: Hot Air Balloon Ride and Stargazing Tour
Day 7: Slickrock Bike Trail in Moab

Food and Drink Scene of Canyon Country

Canyon Country has its own unique cuisine that you won’t find anywhere else. Here are some dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Navajo Taco: Fry bread topped with chili, beans, lettuce, and cheese.
  • Green Chili: Roasted green chilies are used in many dishes in Canyon Country, including stews and enchiladas.

Local Beverages

  • Sassafras Tea: This tea is made from the roots of the sassafras plant and has a unique, slightly sweet taste.
  • Prickly Pear Margarita: Made with the juice of the prickly pear cactus, this cocktail is a refreshing treat on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Canyon Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sorrel River Ranch: Located in Moab, this ranch-style resort offers luxurious accommodations and stunning views of the Colorado River.
  • Under Canvas Moab: For a unique glamping experience, stay in a safari tent at Under Canvas Moab, located just minutes from Arches National Park.
  • Canyonlands RV Resort & Campground: This campground in Moab offers both RV and tent camping options, as well as cabins for rent.

What to Buy in Canyon Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turquoise Jewelry: The Native American tribes in Canyon Country are known for their beautiful turquoise jewelry.
  • Navajo Rugs: These handwoven rugs are a stunning example of Native American artistry.

Local Culture and Customs of Canyon Country

Respect the local culture by asking permission to take photos of the Native American tribes you may encounter. It is also important to remember that the area is sacred to these tribes and should be treated with reverence.

Best Time to Visit Canyon Country

The best time to visit Canyon Country is from March to May and September to November. The temperatures are mild, and crowds are smaller during these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canyon Country

Do:

  • Do stay on designated trails to avoid damaging the fragile desert ecosystem.
  • Do drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore signs and warnings in the national parks and tribal lands.
  • Don’t attempt to climb on any of the rock formations in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canyon Country

  • Be aware of flash floods and always check weather forecasts before going on a hike or excursion.
  • Beware of rattlesnakes and other desert wildlife. Keep your distance and do not approach them.

Health in Canyon Country

  • The high altitude in some areas of Canyon Country can cause altitude sickness. Stay hydrated and take it easy until your body adjusts.
  • The sun is stronger at higher altitudes. Wear sunscreen and keep your skin protected.

Getting to Canyon Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Canyon Country

  • Grand Junction Regional Airport (GJT) is the nearest airport to Canyon Country, located about 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Canyon Country

  • Greyhound bus lines service Moab, about 12 miles from Arches National Park.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Canyon Country, as public transportation is limited.

How to Get Around in Canyon Country

  • A car is the best way to get around Canyon Country, as many attractions are spread out and public transportation is limited.

Where to Go Next from Canyon Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Canyon National Park: Located in Arizona, the Grand Canyon is a must-see destination for any nature lover.
  • Zion National Park: Just a few hours from Canyon Country, Zion National Park’s stunning cliffs and hiking trails will take your breath away.

Conclusion

Canyon Country offers a unique blend of outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well prepared for your trip and ready to experience all that Canyon Country has to offer.
