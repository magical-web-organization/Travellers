Introduction to Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

Glen Canyon National Recreation Area is a mesmerizing playground set amidst the timeless beauty of Utah. This recreation area covers 1.2 million acres of red-rock mesas, narrow canyons, and untouched wilderness. This canyon country destination is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, delighting them with plenty of opportunities for hiking, boating, fishing, and scenic drives. The Glen Canyon Dam, which created Lake Powell, along with the surrounding wilderness, make for a unique and unforgettable American adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

The Glen Canyon National Recreation Area is vast, with so much to offer to visitors. Here we have a list of the must-visit places that you can’t miss while exploring the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John Wesley Powell Museum - A historical museum dedicated to the legendary explorer who first explored the area and the Colorado River.
  • Navajo Bridge Interpretive Center - A beautiful bridge connecting the northern and southern rim of the Grand Canyon, learn about the history and significance of the ancient Navajo trading route.
  • Lee’s Ferry - Once a crossing for pioneers, homesteaders, and explorers, now a historic spot and museum for those interested in learning more about the early history of the Glen Canyon area.

Natural Attractions of Glen Canyon National Recreation Area: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Antelope Canyon - A stunning slot canyon famous for its uniquely angled shafts of sunlight that provide the perfect photo-op.
  • Rainbow Bridge National Monument - A natural arch sculpted over time that stretches 290ft across and 275ft high. The bridge is accessible only by hiking and boating.
  • Horseshoe Bend - A horseshoe-shaped bend in the Colorado River, making it a popular spot for sending Instagram-worthy postcards home.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

Glen Canyon National Recreation Area has over 1.2 million acres of land for exploring. With trails that range in difficulty, from novice to advanced, hikers, rock climbers, and bikers will have no shortage of adventures to embark upon. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Hanging Garden Trail - A 1.5-mile trek to Hanging Garden, a natural oasis of ferns and wildflowers, nestled in Navajo sandstone.
  • Wire Pass Trail - A 1.7-mile trail that leads through a winding slot canyon and features arches, red rock formations, and vibrant sandstone walls.
  • Lower Calf Creek Falls Trail - This 6-mile round-trip trail takes you to the 126ft Lower Calf Creek Falls.

Best Things to Do in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area: Activities and Tours

  • Boating - Rent a houseboat or pontoon to enjoy a day of fishing, water sports, and soaking up the sun on Lake Powell
  • Kayaking and Rafting - Tour the Colorado river via a guided kayaking or rafting tour.
  • 4x4 Jeep Tours - Discover the backcountry of Glen Canyon with the aid of a knowledgeable guide.
  • Stargazing - The area’s immense, open-sky makes it the perfect location to enjoy a stargazing adventure with low light pollution.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

Day 1 - Arrive at Lake Powell. Explore the lake’s impressive scope and scale by heading out on a houseboat trip or renting a pontoon for a day on the water.

Day 2 - Go hiking in Antelope Canyon, and walk in the shadows of towering rock formations while exploring the famous site’s slot canyon.

Day 3 - Take a guided kayak trip on the Colorado River to see towering sandstone cliffs, the Navajo Bridge, and the beautiful desert scenery.

Day 4 - Embark on a driving tour up Highway 89 to see Horseshoe Bend, with an iconic view of the Colorado River, and Lee’s Ferry historical site.

Day 5 - Take a peaceful day on the lake and visit Lone Rock Beach for some relaxation and sunbathing, surrounded by stunning landscapes.

Day 6 - Discover the Glen Canyon Dam and Power Plant on a 1.5 hour tour.

Day 7 - End the week with a guided horseback tour, observing beautiful vistas of the Utah mountains and valleys.

Food and Drink Scene of Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

Must-Try Dishes

The area’s food scene is famous for its Southwestern cuisine and traditional Navajo dishes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Fry Bread - A Navajo staple, including a puffy, fried dough that can be served in both sweet and savory versions.
  • Navajo Taco - A variation of the typical taco, served on Navajo fry bread instead of a shell.

Local Beverages

While exploring Glen Canyon, you’ll have plenty of options for local craft beers and local wine, which are readily available at most local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Glen Canyon National Recreation Area offers a wealth of accommodations for different budgets and travel styles.

  • Lake Powell Resort - The only resort located on Lake Powell, with comfortable rooms, suites, and villas featuring everything you need for an excellent stay.
  • Courtyard Page at Lake Powell - Located in Page, Arizona, conveniently located near Glen Canyon Dam and other local attractions.
  • Wahweap RV and Campground - A great option if you’re traveling with an RV or looking to enjoy a night under the stars.

What to Buy in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Navajo Pottery - Colorful pottery pieces handmade by local Navajo artists.
  • Indian Jewelry - Look for the stunning silver and turquoise jewelry on display at the visitor center gift shops.
  • Western Art and Photography - Pick up beautiful artwork or unique souvenirs that showcase the natural beauty of the Glen Canyon National Recreation Area.

Local Culture and Customs of Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

The Navajo tribe has a deep-rooted history in the Glen Canyon area. Respect their cultural beliefs and customs, and always remember to ask before taking photographs. Be sure to also adhere to the Leave No Trace principles, which include leaving the area as you found it and sticking to designated trails.

Best Time to Visit Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

The spring and fall months are the best time to visit Glen Canyon because of the mild temperatures and fewer crowds. The summertime is busiest, but also when you can take the best advantage of water activities and boat rentals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

Do:

  • Do bring plenty of water, sunscreen, and snacks on any outdoor excursion.
  • Do hike along designated trails and sign in/register before embarking.
  • Do respect wildlife in the area, while keeping an appropriate distance.

Don’t:

  • Don’t stray off designated trails, as this can cause damage to the fragile ecosystems of the area.
  • Don’t remove any natural artifacts or souvenirs from the area.
  • Don’t attempt any dangerous kayak or rafting activities without an experienced guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

  • Don’t underestimate the area’s heat. Ensure that you wear hats, sunscreen, and cover arms and legs appropriately when hiking and spending time outdoors.
  • Take all recommended safety precautions when operating a boat or watercraft.
  • Know the weather forecast for the area before heading out, because heavy rainstorms can create flash floods.

Health in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

Ensure that you drink plenty of water and stay hydrated when spending time outside. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should make sure they have appropriate medication and check the local weather forecast for high temperatures.

Getting to Glen Canyon National Recreation Area: Transportation Options and Logistics

Glen Canyon National Recreation Area is in the heart of Canyon Country between Page, Arizona, and the Utah border.

Airports Near Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

The closest airport to Glen Canyon National Recreation Area is Page Municipal Airport, located in Page, Arizona. The airport offers daily flights to Phoenix, Arizona.

Bus Stations Near Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

If you’re traveling by bus, the closest bus station is located in Page, Arizona. Shuttle services are available between Page and Flagstaff, Arizona.

Other Options

The area is also accessible via rental car, as most major rental providers operate in the region.

How to Get Around in Glen Canyon National Recreation Area

While you can drive around parts of Glen Canyon National Recreation Area, the majority of the park is accessible by boat or by foot. Some companies offer jeep tours, which provide you with the opportunity to explore the backcountry of the area.

Where to Go Next from Glen Canyon National Recreation Area: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Canyon National Park - Journey a few hours to the south to visit one of the most iconic natural wonders of the world.
  • Bryce Canyon - A three-hour drive from Glen Canyon, this park boasts stunning red-rock hoodoos that rise from the canyon floor.
  • Zion National Park - A 2.5-hour drive from Glen Canyon, Zion National Park is full of breathtaking hikes, beautiful views, and a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Glen Canyon National Recreation Area is an American gem that encompasses the vast beauty and magic of the Southwest. It is a stunning landscape that creates an irresistible feeling of awe in the hearts of explorers. The incredible views, outdoor adventures, and cultural offerings make it the ultimate destination for those looking to experience the stunning beauty and history of Canyon Country. Explore the possibilities of this magnificent National Recreation Area today!
