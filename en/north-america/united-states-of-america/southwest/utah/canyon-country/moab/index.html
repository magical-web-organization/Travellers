Moab is a picturesque city nestled in southeastern Utah’s Canyon Country that encompasses high-desert mesas, deep canyons, and the mighty Colorado River. People from all around the world travel to Moab to experience the unique landscape and to indulge in their love for outdoor adventures.

Introduction to Moab

Moab is a small town in Grand County, Utah, that is popular among tourists because of its proximity to the stunning Arches National Park and the Canyonlands National Park. It is a unique blend of rugged wilderness, stunning natural beauty, and small-town charm that offers a wide range of activities for tourists of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Moab

Visit the following must-see attractions in Moab:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Moab Museum - Learn about the history, geology, and archaeology of Moab and surrounding areas.
  • Dan O’Laurie Museum of Moab - Witness the exhibits that illustrate the life of the early settlers, ranchers, miners, and the Moabites themselves who helped shape the future of Moab.

Natural Attractions of Moab: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arches National Park - Take a hike or enjoy a scenic drive through this famous park that boasts more than 2,000 natural arches.
  • Canyonlands National Park - Explore the famous canyons, mesas, and buttes to satisfy your love for hiking, biking, and awe-inspiring views.
  • Dead Horse Point State Park - Enjoy the panoramic views of the Colorado River, the towering La Sal Mountains, and unforgettable sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moab

Hiking and biking are among the most popular activities in Moab because of its unique terrain. Here are some of the top suggestions for hiking and biking trails in Moab:

  • Slickrock Bike Trail - It is one of the most famous mountain bike trails in the world that offers a challenging ride on its slick rocks.
  • Delicate Arch - A 3-mile round-trip hike that leaves from Wolfe Ranch to the most famous arch in the park.
  • Devil’s Garden Trail - A 7.2-mile round trip that has a total of 8 premium arches to explore and appreciate.

Best Things to Do in Moab: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Moab offers many other activities and tours to keep you entertained for days. Choose from the list below:

  • Rafting - Enjoy a rafting trip on the famous Colorado River which has rapids ranging from Class I all the way up to Class V.
  • Jeep and ATV Tours - Take a guided tour and explore Moab’s rugged backcountry trails in the back of a jeep or ATV.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides - Experience an unforgettable adventure as you soar above Moab’s beautiful landscape in a hot air balloon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moab

Here is an itinerary that will help you see the most of Moab during your week-long vacation:

Day 1-2: Arrive in Moab, explore the town and its local attractions like Moab Museum and Dan O’Laurie Museum of Moab.

Day 3: Drive to Arches National Park, hike to the iconic Delicate Arch and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Visit Canyonlands National Park and take a scenic drive along the Island in the Sky Route.

Day 5: Have an epic outdoor adventure day with rafting, climbing, or mountain biking.

Day 6: Explore the backcountry in a jeep or ATV tour.

Day 7: Visit Dead Horse Point State Park and enjoy the breathtaking view of the Colorado River.

Food and Drink Scene of Moab

When it comes to food and drink, Moab has something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Navajo Taco - A dish that consists of fry bread topped with ground beef, beans, lettuce, tomatoes, and salsa.
  • Green Chile Cheeseburger - A juicy burger topped with Hatch green chiles and melted cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Moab Brewery - Try the local crafted beer at this popular brewery that offers a relaxed atmosphere and live music.
  • Canyonlands by Night and Day - Enjoy a dinner cruise on the Colorado River while sipping wine and experiencing live music.

Where to Stay in Moab: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Moab offers plenty of accommodation options that cater to tourists of different tastes and budgets. Here are some of the top places to stay in Moab:

  • Sorrel River Ranch & Resort - A luxurious resort designed with rustic charm and elegance situated along the Colorado River.
  • Moab Under Canvas - Stay in a luxurious tent with the surrounding red rock scenery and enjoy luxurious amenities.
  • Jailhouse Cafe & Hostel - An affordable option with a unique ambiance in a refurbished historic building, with a restaurant and hostel rooms.

What to Buy in Moab: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visiting Moab, it’s hard to resist buying some souvenirs to remember your trip. Among the many stores here, you may find something like:

  • Moab Rock Shop - Browse a unique collection of rocks, minerals, fossils, and jewelry, and take home a piece of Moab’s geological history.
  • Moab Made - Find homemade regional products and locally produced goods so that you can bring home a piece of Moab and support local businesses.

Local Culture and Customs of Moab

When in Moab, it is important to understand and respect the local culture. Talk to the locals to learn about their customs, be friendly and respectful, and always ask permission before taking photographs of people or places of significance.

Best Time to Visit Moab

Moab is most popular in the spring, summer, and fall months (April-October), when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. However, the off-season (November-March) offers its own unique charm and offers visitors a more peaceful and less crowded experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moab

Do:

  • Respect the environment and leave no trace of your visit.
  • Bring plenty of water with you on all of your outdoor activities.
  • Observe local wildlife from a safe distance.

Don’t:

  • Go off-trail and disturb the natural environment.
  • Camp in undesignated areas.
  • Litter or vandalize in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Moab

Moab is generally a safe place to visit, but a few precautions can help keep you safe. Stay alert, always keep valuables in a safe place, and take precautions to prevent sunburn and dehydration.

Health in Moab

Bring any necessary medication and protection against the sun, since the high altitude and dry climate can cause dehydration and other health concerns. Additionally, consider wearing sturdy walking shoes, sun hats, and sunglasses to protect against the bright, hot sun and irregular terrain.

Getting to Moab: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moab is located in the southeastern part of Utah, approximately 236 miles southeast of Salt Lake City. Here are some transportation options for your convenience:

Airports Near Moab

  • Grand Junction Regional Airport (GJT) - 113 miles from Moab, with daily flights from various cities in the United States.
  • Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC) - 234 miles from Moab, with daily flights from around the world.

Bus Stations Near Moab

  • Greyhound - The nearest bus station is located in Grand Junction, Colorado, approximately 2 hours from Moab.

Other Options

  • Drive your own vehicle - If you prefer to drive your own car, Moab is easily accessible from major highways in the surrounding areas.
  • Rent a car - Many rental car companies operate in Salt Lake City and Grand Junction airports, which can be a cost-effective option.

How to Get Around in Moab

The best way to get around Moab is by car or bike. There are rental car companies in Moab, and many hotels offer bike rentals that allow you to ride through town and the surrounding areas at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Moab: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve enjoyed your time in Moab, consider taking one of the following excursions:

  • Bryce Canyon National Park - Located in southern Utah, this stunning park is known for its unique geology and vibrant, towering hoodoos.
  • Zion National Park - Located in southwestern Utah, this park offers stunning views of red rock cliffs, canyons, and waterfalls, and features exceptional hikes as you explore the park.
  • Capitol Reef National Park - Located in south-central Utah, this park offers exciting recreational hiking, scenic drives, and cultural experiences to enjoy with your family and friends.

Conclusion

When it comes to a perfect blend of amazing landscapes, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and small-town charm, Moab stands out as one of the best destinations in the United States. With this guide, anyone can plan an unforgettable trip to Moab that is full of scenic beauty and adventure.
