Introduction to Goblin Valley State Park

Goblin Valley State Park is a mesmerizing natural wonder located in Canyon Country, Utah, in the Southwest region of the United States. It is famous for its unique geological formations, including thousands of hoodoos, mushrooms, and goblins that create a surreal landscape unlike any other.

The park encompasses 3,654 acres of red rock terrain and is home to a variety of flora and fauna, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Whether you’re hiking, biking, camping, or simply enjoying a leisurely stroll, Goblin Valley State Park provides an unforgettable experience for all.

List of Places to Visit in Goblin Valley State Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • John Wesley Powell Museum: This museum honors the life and accomplishments of John Wesley Powell, who explored the Colorado River and the Grand Canyon.

Natural Attractions of Goblin Valley State Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Goblins: The park’s signature feature, The Goblins, is a collection of mushroom-shaped rocks that create a surreal landscape.
  • Little Wild Horse Canyon: A popular hiking trail that winds through a slot canyon with stunning views of the landscape.
  • Observation Point: A scenic lookout that offers an unparalleled view of the park’s hoodoos and rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goblin Valley State Park

  • Goblin’s Lair Trail: A moderate hike that leads to a cavernous chamber, known as Goblin’s Lair.
  • Entrada Canyon Trail: A scenic hike that offers breathtaking views of the park’s red rocks and hoodoos.
  • Wild Horse Loop Trail: A challenging 8-mile hike that takes you through Little Wild Horse Canyon and Bell Canyon.

Best Things to Do in Goblin Valley State Park: Activities and Tours

  • Rock Climbing: The park’s unique rock formations offer a perfect playground for rock climbing enthusiasts.
  • Stargazing: The park’s remote location and lack of light pollution make it a perfect spot for stargazing
  • Horseback Riding: A scenic horseback ride through the park is a unique way to explore its natural wonders.

Food and Drink Scene of Goblin Valley State Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frybread: A traditional Native American dish made of deep-fried dough, commonly served with honey and jam.
  • Navajo Tacos: Frybread topped with chili, cheese, lettuce, and tomatoes, a filling and satisfying meal.

Local Beverages

  • Utah Craft Beer: Enjoy a cold pint of local craft beer from the region’s emerging breweries.
  • Huckleberry Lemonade: A refreshing summertime drink made from local huckleberries.

Where to Stay in Goblin Valley State Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Goblin Valley State Park Campground: Affordable camping, and RV sites conveniently located inside the park.
  • Hanksville Inn: A comfortable and modern motel just 20 minutes away from the park.

What to Buy in Goblin Valley State Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts: Handcrafted jewelry, pottery, and rugs that showcase the local Native American culture.
  • Local Honey and Jams: Made from locally sourced Utah fruit, a tasty and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Goblin Valley State Park

  • Native American Culture: The regional culture is heavily influenced by the Native American tribes that have a long and rich history in the area.
  • Respect for the Outdoors: Visitors are encouraged to respect the park’s natural wonders and to leave no trace of their visit.

Best Time to Visit Goblin Valley State Park

The best time to visit Goblin Valley State Park is between late spring and early fall, when the weather is mild and rain is minimal. The park can get busy during the peak season, so plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goblin Valley State Park

Do:

  • Pack plenty of water and snacks, as the park is in a remote location.
  • Take only photographs, leave only footprints.
  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Park in undesignated areas.
  • Disturb or damage the park’s geological formations.
  • Leave trash or litter behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goblin Valley State Park

  • The park’s remote location and rugged terrain can present hazards, so make sure to stay on designated trails and camp in designated areas.
  • Be aware of weather conditions, as flash floods and extreme heat can occur.

Health in Goblin Valley State Park

  • The park’s high altitude and dry climate can contribute to dehydration and altitude sickness, so make sure to drink plenty of water and take frequent breaks.
  • Wear sun protection, as the park’s intense sunlight can cause sunburn.

Getting to Goblin Valley State Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Goblin Valley State Park

  • Salt Lake City International Airport, 206 miles away
  • Grand Junction Regional Airport, 145 miles away

Bus Stations Near Goblin Valley State Park

  • Salt Lake City Bus Station, 175 miles away
  • Moab Greyhound Station, 75 miles away

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available in the nearby towns of Green River and Moab.
  • Private transportation services can be arranged.

How to Get Around in Goblin Valley State Park

The park is easily accessible by car, and several parking areas and viewpoints are available throughout the park. Biking and hiking are popular ways for visitors to explore the park’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Goblin Valley State Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Capitol Reef National Park: A scenic drive through the park’s rugged landscape, just a two-hour drive away.
  • Arches National Park: Another nearby national park, famous for its natural arches and sandstone formations.
