Introduction to Hanksville

Hanksville is a small town situated in the heart of Utah’s Canyon Country. Known for its scenic landscapes, geological formations, and outdoor activities, Hanksville is a popular destination among adventure seekers, hikers, and nature lovers. The town is located near several national parks, including Arches, Canyonlands, and Capitol Reef, making it an excellent base for exploring the region.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top places to visit, the best hiking and biking routes, the must-try dishes, and the most comfortable accommodations in Hanksville. We’ll also provide useful tips on getting around, the best time to visit, and the local customs and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Hanksville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Thornburgh Site: This historic site was once home to a US army post established in 1886. Visitors can explore the remains of the fort and learn about its history through informative displays and exhibits.

  • Hanksville-Burpee Dinosaur Quarry: One of the largest dinosaur quarries in the US, this site is home to a vast collection of fossils and bones. The quarry features a guided tour that takes visitors through the excavation pits and the preparation and processing labs.

  • The Mars Society Research Station: The Mars Desert Research Station is a habitat designed to simulate Mars-like conditions for scientific and engineering research. Visitors can take tours of the facility and learn about the technology behind space exploration.

Natural Attractions of Hanksville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Goblin Valley State Park: This beautiful state park is home to a unique collection of sandstone formations called hoodoos. Visitors can explore the park’s numerous hiking trails and vantage points to get a closer look at these bizarre rock formations.

  • Capitol Reef National Park: Located just to the east of Hanksville, the Capitol Reef National Park is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. The park features a range of geological formations, including canyons, arches, and monoliths, as well as a diverse collection of plants and animals.

  • Little Wild Horse Canyon: One of the best hiking trails in the area, Little Wild Horse Canyon takes visitors through narrow canyons and winding paths, revealing stunning geological formations along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanksville

Hanksville is an excellent base for exploring the nearby national parks and outdoor areas. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Cottonwood Wash: This 6-mile hiking trail takes you through a narrow canyon with towering walls on either side. The trail features numerous creek crossings and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Amasa Back Trail: This technical mountain biking trail takes you through slick rock and challenging terrain. The route features steep drops and narrow paths, making it ideal for advanced riders.

  • Maze Trail: Located in the Canyonlands National Park, the Maze Trail takes you through a fascinating labyrinth of canyons, rock formations, and natural arches. This hiking trail features a range of difficulty levels and is suitable for both beginner and advanced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Hanksville: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Jeep Tours: Visitors can explore the remote and scenic backroads of Utah’s Canyon Country on a guided Jeep tour. These tours take you through some of the most stunning landscapes in the area and offer a unique perspective on the local flora and fauna.

  • Off-Road Adventures: Adventurers can take a high-speed off-road adventure through the sand dunes and canyons surrounding Hanksville. There are several operators in the area that offer ATV rentals and guided tours.

  • Stargazing: The vast and pristine skies over Hanksville make it an excellent destination for stargazing. Visitors can set up camp at one of the several designated stargazing locations or take a guided tour to learn more about the constellations and the night sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hanksville

Day 1: Arrival in Hanksville and exploration of local landmarks such as the Fort Thornburgh site and the Hanksville-Burpee Dinosaur Quarry.

Day 2: Hiking in the Capitol Reef National Park.

Day 3: Exploring the scenic Little Wild Horse Canyon and Goblin Valley State Park.

Day 4: Jeep tour of the backroads and scenic vistas around Hanksville.

Day 5: Mountain biking on the Amasa Back Trail.

Day 6: ATV rentals and off-road adventures through the sand dunes and canyons surrounding Hanksville.

Day 7: Stargazing and campfire celebrations to cap off a week of outdoor adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Hanksville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Navajo Tacos: These delicious fried bread tacos are a staple of Navajo cuisine and are served at many local restaurants.

  • Green Chile Cheeseburgers: A signature dish in the Southwest, green chile cheeseburgers are an excellent option for those looking for a hearty and flavorful meal.

  • Fry Bread: This traditional Native American dish is a favorite in Hanksville and can be eaten plain or topped with a range of sweet or savory toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Utah Beer: There are several local breweries in the area that produce craft beers using locally sourced ingredients.

  • Agave Margaritas: These delicious cocktails feature agave nectar and fresh lime juice and are perfect for sipping on a warm Utah evening.

  • Juniper Berry Tea: This herbal tea is made using locally grown juniper berries and has a distinct and refreshing flavor.

Where to Stay in Hanksville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Red Sands Hotel: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, an outdoor heated pool, and easy access to some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area.

  • Whispering Sands Motel: This cozy motel features clean and comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a location close to restaurants and local attractions.

  • The Hanksville Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers basic amenities such as cable TV and free Wi-Fi and is located within walking distance of many of Hanksville’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Hanksville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Navajo Jewelry: Hanksville is home to several local artisans who produce beautiful and intricate pieces of Navajo jewelry.

  • Local Honey: There are several honey producers in the area who make honey using the nectar from the native flora and fauna.

  • Utah Salt: The salt flats near Hanksville produce high-quality salt that is sold in local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanksville

Hanksville is a small town with a rich cultural heritage. The Native American populations living in and around Hanksville have a long and storied history that dates back to prehistoric times. Visitors should respect the culture and customs of the local population and take care not to disturb or damage any sacred sites or historical landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Hanksville

The best time to visit Hanksville is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. The summer months can be scorching hot, making outdoor activities challenging. The winter months can bring snow and ice, which may make hiking and outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanksville

Do:

  • Respect the cultural heritage and customs of the local population.
  • Carry plenty of water when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Leave no trace, pack out all your trash, and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or damage any sacred sites or historical landmarks.
  • Go off-trail or hike in areas that are closed to the public.
  • Ignore the weather forecast or disregard any safety warnings from park rangers or local guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanksville

Hanksville is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. Always carry plenty of water and stay on marked trails. Cell phone service may be limited or non-existent in some areas, so it’s essential to inform someone of your planned route and expected return time.

Health in Hanksville

The high altitude and dry climate can be challenging for some visitors, so it’s essential to stay hydrated and take regular breaks when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. Visitors should also be aware of the risks associated with exposure to the strong UV rays and use sunblock and other protective measures to avoid sunburn and heat stroke.

Getting to Hanksville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hanksville

The nearest airport to Hanksville is the Canyonlands Field Airport in Moab, Utah, located about 72 miles from the town center. The Salt Lake City International Airport is another option, located about 228 miles from Hanksville.

Bus Stations Near Hanksville

There are no bus stations in Hanksville, but several operators in nearby towns provide bus services to the area. Greyhound offers daily bus services to the nearby town of Green River, and from there, visitors can take a taxi or a rental car to reach Hanksville.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Hanksville by car or motorcycle using the scenic roads and highways that run through Utah’s Canyon Country.

How to Get Around in Hanksville

The best way to get around Hanksville is by car or motorcycle. Visitors can rent vehicles from several operators in the area, and most of the local attractions and hiking trails are easily accessible by road. Visitors can also take guided tours or hire taxis to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Hanksville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Moab: Located about 72 miles north of Hanksville, Moab is a popular destination for outdoor activities such as hiking, rock climbing, and mountain biking.

  • Bryce Canyon National Park: Located about 220 miles southwest of Hanksville, the Bryce Canyon National Park is a scenic destination known for its unique geological formations and stunning vistas.

  • Grand Staircase Escalante National Monument: Located about 120 miles southwest of Hanksville, the Grand Staircase Escalante National Monument features rugged landscapes, slot canyons, and natural formations, making it a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.

Conclusion

Hanksville is a destination that offers something for everyone, from scenic hikes and off-road adventures to cultural attractions and local cuisine. Visitors can explore the beautiful national parks and geological formations surrounding the town, or take guided tours to discover the region’s rich cultural heritage and history. Whatever your tastes and interests, Hanksville is a destination that will provide unforgettable memories and experiences.
