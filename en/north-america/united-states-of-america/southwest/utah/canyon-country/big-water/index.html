Introduction to Big Water

Located in the heart of Canyon Country in the Southwest region of the United States of America, Big Water is a small town in the state of Utah. Surrounded by red rock cliffs, stunning canyons, and natural wonders, Big Water is a paradise for outdoor lovers and adventure seekers. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to Big Water with information on the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Big Water

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Big Water Visitor Center: Start your visit to Big Water at the Big Water Visitor Center, where you can get information on local attractions, hiking and biking routes, and natural wonders. The center also has exhibits on the history and geology of the area.
  • Glen Canyon Dam: Take a tour of the Glen Canyon Dam, which is located just 8 miles from Big Water. The dam provides hydroelectric power to the surrounding area and created Lake Powell, a popular recreational destination for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • John Wesley Powell Museum: Learn about the history of exploration and discovery in the Southwest region at the John Wesley Powell Museum in nearby Page, Arizona. The museum features exhibits on Powell’s expeditions along the Colorado River and the history of the area’s indigenous peoples.

Natural Attractions of Big Water: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paria Canyon-Vermilion Cliffs Wilderness Area: Experience the breathtaking beauty of the red rock canyons and cliffs in the Paria Canyon-Vermilion Cliffs Wilderness Area. This area is perfect for hiking, backpacking, and camping with trails that range from easy to challenging.
  • Coyote Buttes North and South: Explore the otherworldly landscape of Coyote Buttes North and South, which feature unique rock formations and sandstone arches. Permits are required for hiking in these areas and only a limited number of permits are issued each day.
  • Wire Pass Trailhead: Visit the Wire Pass Trailhead for a scenic hike through a narrow canyon that leads to the Wave, a popular sandstone rock formation that resembles the ocean’s waves. The Wave is a unique and unusual rock formation that attracts visitors from around the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Water

  • Buckskin Gulch Trail: This challenging 21-mile hike is located in the Paria Canyon-Vermilion Cliffs Wilderness Area and features stunning views of the red rock canyons and cliffs. The route follows the Buckskin Gulch, which is the longest and deepest slot canyon in the southwest.
  • Wire Pass Trail: This 3.7-mile trail leads through a narrow canyon and features stunning views of the surrounding cliffs and formations. The route leads to the Wave and is popular among hikers and photographers.
  • Lake Powell Shoreline Trail: The Lake Powell Shoreline Trail is a 2.5-mile hike that follows the shore of Lake Powell. The trail is easy and features scenic views of the lake and surrounding cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Big Water: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking or Paddleboarding on Lake Powell: Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the scenic waters of Lake Powell. The lake is perfect for water sports and offers stunning views of the surrounding cliffs and formations.
  • Rafting on the Colorado River: Take a guided tour down the Colorado River for an unforgettable experience. Tours range from half-day to multi-day trips and offer scenic views of the river and surrounding canyons.
  • Off-road Adventure: Explore the rugged and remote wilderness of the area on an off-road adventure. Tours are available in 4x4 vehicles, ATVs, and UTVs.

Food and Drink Scene of Big Water

Must-Try Dishes

  • Navajo Tacos: A traditional Southwest dish made with frybread, ground beef, lettuce, tomatoes, and cheese.
  • Green Chile Stew: A hearty soup made with roasted green chiles, potatoes, and pork.
  • Sopaipillas: A fried bread pastry that is typically served as a dessert with honey.

Local Beverages

  • Utah Craft Beers: Try a local craft beer from one of the many breweries in the area.
  • Agua Fresca: A refreshing drink made with fruits, water, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Big Water: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lake Powell Resort: This upscale resort is located on the shores of Lake Powell and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Best Western Plus at Lake Powell: This hotel offers affordable and comfortable rooms, a pool, and a fitness center.
  • Dreamkatchers Lake Powell Bed & Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast features comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Big Water: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Navajo Jewelry: The Navajo people are known for their beautiful silver and turquoise jewelry.
  • Southwest Artwork: Bring home a piece of the Southwest with artwork inspired by the local scenery and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Water

  • Respect for Nature: The Southwest region is known for its stunning natural beauty, and visitors are expected to follow the leave-no-trace principle and respect the environment.
  • Respect for Indigenous Culture: The Navajo and Hopi people have a rich and vibrant culture, and visitors are expected to respect their traditions and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Big Water

The best time to visit Big Water is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and busy, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Water

Do:

  • Do hike, bike, and explore the stunning natural wonders of the area.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t disrespect indigenous cultures or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Water

  • Be prepared for the weather and bring appropriate clothing and gear.
  • Stay hydrated and protected from the sun.

Health in Big Water

  • Altitude sickness can be a concern for visitors to the area, so be aware of the symptoms and consult with a healthcare professional if needed.

Getting to Big Water: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Big Water: The nearest airport is Page Municipal Airport, which is located in Page, Arizona, just a short drive from Big Water.
  • Bus Stations Near Big Water: The closest bus station is located in Page, Arizona, which is just a short drive from Big Water.
  • Other Options: Driving is the most convenient way to reach Big Water, as there are several highways that connect the town to surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Big Water

The best way to get around Big Water is by car or off-road vehicle. Many attractions and natural wonders are located outside of town, and a vehicle is necessary to reach them.

Where to Go Next from Big Water: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zion National Park: Located in southwestern Utah, Zion National Park is known for its stunning red rock cliffs, hiking trails, and scenic drives.
  • Bryce Canyon National Park: Located in southwestern Utah, Bryce Canyon National Park is known for its unique hoodoos, or rock spires, and hiking trails.
  • Grand Canyon National Park: Located in northern Arizona, the Grand Canyon is one of the most well-known natural wonders in the world and offers stunning views of the canyon and surrounding cliffs.
