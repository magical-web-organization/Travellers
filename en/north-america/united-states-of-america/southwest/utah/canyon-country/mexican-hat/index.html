Introduction to Mexican Hat

Mexican Hat is a small town in San Juan County, Utah, located in the heart of the Southwest. It is named after a curious rock formation that resembles a Mexican sombrero. Despite its small size, Mexican Hat is a hub for adventure seekers who come from all over the world to explore the surrounding canyons, mesas, and rivers. The town is surrounded by the stunning red-rock landscape that this region is known for, making it the perfect place to experience all that Canyon Country has to offer.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mexican Hat is steeped in history and offers plenty of opportunities to learn about the area’s rich Native American culture. Here are some of the top cultural attractions to explore:

  • The Edge of the Cedars State Park Museum: This museum features an impressive collection of artifacts from the ancestral Puebloan people, including pottery, baskets, and tools. Visitors can also step inside a reconstructed kiva, a traditional Puebloan gathering space.

Natural Attractions of Mexican Hat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mexican Hat is a nature lover’s paradise, with incredible terrain to explore and breathtaking scenery to admire. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions to check out:

  • Goosenecks State Park: Located just a short drive from Mexican Hat, Goosenecks State Park is a must-see destination for anyone visiting the area. The San Juan River has carved impressive meanders through the red rock cliffs, creating a stunning landscape that will take your breath away.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mexican Hat

Are you an outdoor enthusiast looking for an adventure? Mexican Hat has plenty of options for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

  • Muley Point Overlook: Located just outside of Mexican Hat, the Muley Point Overlook provides an incredible panoramic view of Monument Valley that you won’t want to miss. This overlook is accessible via a short trail that winds through the red rock cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Mexican Hat: Activities and Tours

Food and Drink Scene of Mexican Hat

Mexican Hat may be a small town, but it offers plenty of delicious dining options for you to enjoy after a day of outdoor adventure. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Getting to Mexican Hat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mexican Hat is located in a remote area of Utah, but there are still several transportation options to consider.

Airports Near Mexican Hat

The closest airport to Mexican Hat is the Cortez Municipal Airport, which is located approximately 90 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Mexican Hat

There are no bus stations located directly in Mexican Hat, but nearby towns like Kayenta and Bluff offer bus service to destinations throughout the Southwest.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for exploring the Southwest, as it offers flexibility and convenience.

How to Get Around in Mexican Hat
