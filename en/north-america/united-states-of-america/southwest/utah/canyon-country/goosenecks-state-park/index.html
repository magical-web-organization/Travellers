Introduction to Goosenecks State Park

Goosenecks State Park is located in Canyon Country, Utah, and is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore one of the most beautiful landscapes in the United States. The park is located in the southwest of the country and offers visitors the opportunity to experience a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and outdoor adventure. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Goosenecks State Park, including where to stay, what to do, and how to get there.

List of Places to Visit in Goosenecks State Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Goosenecks State Park is primarily known for its natural beauty, the park also has several culturally significant attractions worth visiting. These include:

  • Hovenweep National Monument: This monument is located on the border of Utah and Colorado and features a collection of abandoned Ancestral Puebloan villages. The monument offers a unique glimpse into the daily lives of the people who once inhabited the area.

  • Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park: This park is located on Navajo land and features towering sandstone formations that have been made famous in movies and TV shows over the years. Visitors can take tours of the park and learn about Navajo culture and history.

Natural Attractions of Goosenecks State Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural attractions of Goosenecks State Park are what draw most visitors to the area. These include:

  • Goosenecks Overlook: This is the main attraction in the park, and for good reason. Visitors can get a stunning view of the San Juan River as it winds its way through the deep canyon. The overlook is a great place to take photos and just soak in the natural beauty of the park.

  • Hiking and Biking Trails: The park has several trails that are perfect for hiking and biking. These include the Prospectors Trail and the Honaker Trail, both of which offer visitors stunning views of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goosenecks State Park

Goosenecks State Park has several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. These include:

  • Prospectors Trail: This is a 3.2-mile trail that starts at the Goosenecks Overlook and takes hikers down to the San Juan River. The trail is rated as moderate and offers stunning views of the canyon.

  • Honaker Trail: This is a 5.4-mile round-trip hike that takes visitors down to the San Juan River. The trail is rated as difficult and should only be attempted by experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Goosenecks State Park: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Goosenecks State Park, whether you are looking for outdoor adventure or cultural experiences. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Take a Scenic Drive: The park has a 2-mile scenic drive that takes visitors to all the major viewpoints in the area. The drive is perfect for visitors who want to experience the natural beauty of the park without having to hike.

  • Go Rafting on the San Juan River: Visitors can book rafting tours that take them down the San Juan River. This is a great way to see the park from a different perspective and experience some outdoor adventure at the same time.

Week-Long Itinerary for Goosenecks State Park

If you have a week to spend in Goosenecks State Park, you can experience everything the area has to offer. Here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in the area and take the scenic drive through the park to get your bearings.

  • Day 2: Hike the Prospectors Trail and take in the stunning views of the canyon.

  • Day 3: Take a rafting tour down the San Juan River.

  • Day 4: Visit Hovenweep National Monument and learn about the area’s cultural significance.

  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park.

  • Day 6: Drive to nearby Mesa Verde National Park and explore the abandoned cliff dwellings.

  • Day 7: Spend your last day in the park hiking the Honaker Trail and taking in the stunning views of the landscape.
