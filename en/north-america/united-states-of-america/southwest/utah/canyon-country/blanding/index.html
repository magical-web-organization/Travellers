Introduction to Blanding

Blanding is a small city located in San Juan County, Utah, and is known for its rich history and natural beauty. Situated in Canyon Country, the region is home to some of America’s most beautiful national parks, including the world-renowned Arches and Canyonlands, making it a must-see destination for any avid traveler.

Blanding offers a unique combination of outdoor adventures and cultural landmarks, making it an ideal vacation spot for families, couples, and solo travelers alike. With a plethora of museums, hiking and biking trails, and breathtaking scenic views, there’s no shortage of activities to keep visitors entertained during their stay.

List of Places to Visit in Blanding

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Blanding has a rich history that is well-preserved through the numerous museums and historical sites that are open to visitors throughout the year.

Edge of the Cedars State Park

Edge of the Cedars State Park is a historic site that features one of the most extensive archaeological collections in the United States. With over 5,000 artifacts from the Ancestral Puebloan people, including pottery, baskets, and tools, the park provides visitors with a glimpse into the lives and culture of this ancient civilization.

Dinosaur Museum

The Dinosaur Museum in Blanding is a must-visit for families with dinosaur enthusiasts. The museum houses an extensive collection of dinosaur bones and fossils, including a full-size skeleton of a Triceratops.

Bluff Fort Historic Site

Bluff Fort is a reconstructed historic site that offers a glimpse into the lives of the pioneers who settled in the area in the late 1800s. The site features restored buildings, including a blacksmith shop, a schoolhouse, and a general store, all adorned with historic artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Blanding: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Blanding is known for its natural beauty, with rugged landscapes and awe-inspiring vistas that are sure to take your breath away.

Bears Ears National Monument

Bears Ears National Monument is one of Utah’s newest national monuments, and it covers over 1.3 million acres of rugged terrain. The monument offers unparalleled opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking, rock climbing, and camping. Visitors can also marvel at the impressive cliff dwellings of the Ancestral Puebloan people, who lived in the area over a thousand years ago.

Natural Bridges National Monument

Natural Bridges National Monument boasts three naturally-formed bridges, making it a favorite destination for amateur and professional photographers. Visitors can hike down to the bridges and take in the beauty of the natural rock formations. During the summer months, the park also offers a nightly guided tour that takes visitors to some of the best stargazing locations in the park.

Hovenweep National Monument

Hovenweep National Monument is an ancient village that dates back over 800 years. The site features six distinct village groupings, with several beautifully preserved buildings that offer an insight into the lives of its former inhabitants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blanding

Blanding offers numerous trails for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. Here are a few of our favorite routes:

Mule Canyon Trail

The Mule Canyon Trail is a moderate three-mile hike that takes visitors to several well-preserved Ancestral Puebloan buildings, including the House on Fire ruins. The trailhead is located in Mule Canyon, just a few miles from Blanding.

Butler Wash Ruins Trail

The Butler Wash Ruins Trail is a three-mile hike that takes visitors to the impressive Butler Wash cliff dwelling, which is believed to have been occupied by the Ancestral Puebloan people over 800 years ago. The trail is rated as moderate and offers stunning views of the surrounding canyon.

White Canyon Trail

The White Canyon Trail is a challenging hike that takes visitors on a six-mile journey through the rugged desert terrain. The trail offers spectacular views of the natural rock formations in the area, including several arches and spires.

Best Things to Do in Blanding: Activities and Tours

Blanding offers a wealth of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained.

ATV Tours

ATV tours are a popular way to explore the rugged terrain around Blanding. Several companies offer guided tours that take visitors to some of the most remote areas of the surrounding countryside.

Rafting Tours

For a more adventurous experience, visitors can take a rafting tour down the San Juan River. The river offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mesas and canyons, as well as numerous opportunities for swimming and hiking.

Night Sky Tours

Blanding is located in one of the darkest regions of the United States, making it an ideal spot for stargazing. Several companies offer guided night sky tours, which take visitors to some of the best stargazing locations in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blanding

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Blanding:

  • Day 1: Explore Edge of the Cedars State Park and visit the Dinosaur Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a guided ATV tour through the surrounding countryside.
  • Day 3: Hike the Mule Canyon Trail and visit the House on Fire ruins.
  • Day 4: Take a rafting tour down the San Juan River.
  • Day 5: Visit the Butler Wash cliff dwelling and take a scenic drive through the area.
  • Day 6: Take a guided night sky tour and marvel at the beauty of the stars.
  • Day 7: Visit the Natural Bridges National Monument and hike to each of the three bridges.

Food and Drink Scene of Blanding

Must-Try Dishes

Blanding offers several unique dining experiences that are sure to delight even the most discerning palate.

Fry Bread

Fry bread is a Navajo staple and has become popular across the Southwest. It’s a flat, fried bread that is often served with a variety of toppings, including honey, jam, or powdered sugar.

Navajo Tacos

Navajo tacos are similar to traditional tacos, but are served on a thick piece of fry bread instead of a tortilla.

Hominy Stew

Hominy stew is a warm, comforting dish that typically consists of hominy, pork, and chili.

Local Beverages

Navajo Tea

Navajo tea is a traditional beverage made from the leaves of the greenthread plant. It has a slightly sweet, floral flavor and is often served hot.

Red Rock Brewery

Red Rock Brewery is located in nearby Salt Lake City and is known for its craft beers, which are available in many bars and restaurants throughout Blanding.

Where to Stay in Blanding: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blanding offers several accommodation options to suit any budget.

Stone Lizard Lodging

Stone Lizard Lodging is a popular option for families and couples. The hotel offers spacious rooms and suites, as well as a heated pool and hot tub.

Kokopelli Inn

Kokopelli Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The rooms are tastefully decorated and feature comfortable furnishings.

What to Buy in Blanding: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Navajo Jewelry

Navajo jewelry is a popular souvenir item, with several shops in Blanding offering unique, handcrafted pieces.

Traditional Pottery

Blanding is located in an area that was once home to the Ancestral Puebloan people, who were known for their intricate pottery designs. Visitors can find several pottery shops in the area that offer traditional handmade pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Blanding

Blanding is located near several Native American reservations, including the Navajo Nation. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and always ask permission before taking photographs or entering sacred areas.

Best Time to Visit Blanding

The best time to visit Blanding is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot, with temperatures often exceeding 90 degrees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blanding

Do:

  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do leave no trace when hiking or camping.
  • Do bring plenty of water when visiting outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of local residents without their permission.
  • Don’t enter sacred areas without first obtaining permission.
  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas, including plants and wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blanding

Blanding is generally a safe area, although visitors should take precautions when hiking or exploring remote areas. Always carry plenty of water and a fully charged cell phone, and let someone know your itinerary before heading out.

Health in Blanding

Blanding is located at a high elevation, which can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. It’s recommended that visitors drink plenty of water and avoid excessive alcohol consumption during their stay.

Getting to Blanding: Transportation Options and Logistics

Blanding is located in a remote area of Utah, and visitors should plan accordingly.

Airports Near Blanding

The nearest major airport is in Salt Lake City, which is approximately 320 miles from Blanding. Visitors can also fly into Moab, which is about 80 miles from Blanding.

Bus Stations Near Blanding

There are no bus stations in Blanding, but visitors can take a bus to nearby Bluff or Monticello.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a shuttle service from the nearest airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Blanding

Blanding is a small city, and visitors can easily explore the downtown area on foot. For those looking to explore the surrounding countryside, it’s recommended that visitors rent a car or take a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Blanding: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arches National Park

Arches National Park is located approximately 130 miles from Blanding and is home to over 2,000 natural sandstone arches, making it a favorite destination for photographers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Canyonlands National Park

Canyonlands National Park is located approximately 100 miles from Blanding and boasts stunning vistas of the surrounding mesas and canyons. Visitors can take a guided tour or explore the park on their own via hiking or biking.

Conclusion

Blanding, Utah, is a hidden gem in the Southwest that offers a unique combination of natural beauty and cultural landmarks. Visitors can explore the rugged terrain of the surrounding countryside, marvel at the natural rock formations, and immerse themselves in the rich history of the Ancestral Puebloan people. With numerous activities and tours, Blanding is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.
