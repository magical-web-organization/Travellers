Thompson Springs, located in Canyon Country in Utah, is a hidden gem of the Southwest. This small town is full of natural wonders and history that should not be missed. Thompson Springs is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best things to do, see, and experience in Thompson Springs.

Introduction to Thompson Springs

Thompson Springs is a small town in the Grand County, Utah, with a population of approximately 30 residents. The town has been the home of uranium mining in the past, with the railroad being an important factor in the economy of the town. It is a peaceful place where visitors can be close to nature and experience the authentic charm of Utah.

List of Places to Visit in Thompson Springs

Thompson Springs is an excellent combination of natural beauty and rich history. The town offers a plethora of attractions including museums, scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural and historical places, hiking/biking routes, and many more.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Railroad Museum: An interesting destination that features exhibits of rolling stock and mining equipment.
  • Sego Ghost Town: An abandoned mining town that gives visitors a glimpse into life in Utah during the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Thompson Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arches National Park: One of the most visited parks in Utah, just a short drive from Thompson Springs, with breathtaking landscapes, cliffs, and natural arches.
  • Dead Horse Point State Park: A stunning state park that offers magnificent views of the Colorado River and Canyonlands National Park.
  • La Sal Mountains: A range of lofty peaks that provide outdoor enthusiasts with an excellent spot for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thompson Springs

Thompson Springs is a hiker’s paradise, with many trails catering to varied skill levels. Among the most famous routes are:

  • Bar M Loops: A set of trails that offer a testing terrain, and an unforgettable experience of biking in the rust-colored landscape.
  • Corona Arch Trail: A hiking trail that is spectacular, not only for the Corona Arch but also for the Bowtie Arch and hiking via a ladder on narrow rock ledges of Bootlegger Canyon.

Best Things to Do in Thompson Springs: Activities and Tours

  • 4x4 Tours: The best way to explore the rocky terrain of Utah and take in the magnificent views.
  • Birdwatching: This region is home to a vast variety of birds, making it an excellent spot for birdwatching enthusiasts.
  • Stargazing: The town has minimal light pollution, making it an ideal spot for stargazing.
  • ATV Tours: An exciting way to explore the trails and canyons, and learn about the history of the area from knowledgeable guides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thompson Springs

Day 1-3: Arches National Park Day 4: Dead Horse Point State Park Day 5: La Sal Mountains Day 6: Sego Ghost Town and Railroad Museum Day 7: Bar M Loops

Food and Drink Scene of Thompson Springs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Sauce and Burgers: A combination of ketchup, mayo, and pickles is the hallmark of Utah burgers.
  • Utah Scones: A delicious, fluffy fried pastry dusted with sugar and cinnamon, served with honey butter.

Local Beverages

  • Pioneer Lager: A refreshing choice, brewed in-house in Thompson Springs.
  • Hard Cider: A tangy taste made with local fruits, a must-try for cider fans.

Where to Stay in Thompson Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Red Stone Inn: A comfortable, family-run inn located in the downtown area.
  • Pack Creek Ranch: A rustic retreat featuring natural pools, camping and cabins, and serene atmosphere.

What to Buy in Thompson Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Utah Honeys: Raw, organic honey made by local bees in hives placed throughout the region.
  • Arches and Canyonlands Socks: Perfect for keeping your feet cozy on your next outdoor adventure.

Local Culture and Customs of Thompson Springs

Thompson Springs is a small town with a humble culture. Its laid-back lifestyle and friendly residents make it a perfect destination for those seeking a break from their busy lives.

Best Time to Visit Thompson Springs

The best time to visit Thompson Springs is from May to October, mild temperatures, wildflowers, and a beautiful sunset, best for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thompson Springs

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the place.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the secret canyons, waterfalls, and vistas.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Forget to carry enough water and sunscreen.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thompson Springs

Thompson Springs, having a small population, is a peaceful place. Visitors can walk around safely. However, remember to follow the safety guidelines while hiking or biking.

Health in Thompson Springs

Sunscreen is a must due to the scorching sun in Utah. Visitors are recommended to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Thompson Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Thompson Springs is Moab Airport, which is approximately 33 miles away. Greyhound bus stations are available in nearby towns, including Green River and Moab. There is no public transportation in Thompson Springs, so renting a car is the most convenient option.

How to Get Around in Thompson Springs

Since Thompson Springs is a small town, walking is an excellent option for moving around. However, to explore the nearby natural attractions, renting a 4x4, bike, or ATV is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Thompson Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Moab, Utah: A town famous for its great cycling, hiking, and rock climbing.
  • Salt Lake City, Utah: A vibrant modern city known for its history, food, and art.
  • Telluride, Colorado: A charming town nestled in the lap of the mountains and surrounded by forests, known for winter sports.

Conclusion

Thompson Springs is a small town that packs a punch in terms of beauty, history, and attractions. From natural wonders and outdoor activities to rich history and local culture, this town has something for everyone. Plan your visit, pack your bags, and get ready to discover the picturesque beauty of Thompson Springs, Utah.
