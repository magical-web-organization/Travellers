Introduction to Fountain Green

Fountain Green is a small town located in Sanpete County in Central Utah. It was first settled in 1859 and has a rich history dating back to the early Mormon pioneers. The town is surrounded by scenic mountains, rolling hills, and lush green valleys - making it the perfect destination for nature lovers, hikers, and bikers. With a population of only around 1,100, the town has a friendly and welcoming vibe, and it’s easy to explore on foot.

List of Places to Visit in Fountain Green

Whether you’re interested in hiking in the mountains, exploring museums and historical sites, or soaking in the local culture, Fountain Green has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pioneer Relic Home Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the historical artifacts and stories of Fountain Green’s early settlers. It’s a great place to learn about the town’s Mormon pioneer and Native American history.
  • The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints: This iconic church serves as a stunning backdrop to Fountain Green’s beautiful Main Street, and it’s a great place to take photos.
  • The Heritage Pioneer Park: This park is a peaceful and pretty spot to relax and see some of the town’s historic landmarks. You’ll see an old jail, granary, and a replica of the town’s first home.

Natural Attractions of Fountain Green: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maple Canyon: This scenic canyon is a must-visit for rock climbing and hiking enthusiasts. It features hundreds of climbing routes that cater to all skill levels.
  • Manti-La Sal National Forest: This stunning national forest offers incredible scenic views, hiking, biking, and camping opportunities.
  • Skyline Drive: This scenic drive takes you through some of Utah’s most beautiful landscapes, including mountains, valleys, canyons, and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fountain Green

Fountain Green is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering numerous hiking and biking trails for all experience levels. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Blackhawk Trail: This moderate 6-mile hike takes you to the top of Blackhawk Mountain, offering incredible views of Utah’s mountains and valleys.
  • Rocky Ridge Trail: This 3-mile hike is perfect for families and beginners, as it offers a relaxed hike through scenic meadows and beautiful wildflowers.
  • Skyline Trail: This trail is a 84-mile long-distance hiking and biking trail that takes you through the scenic Wasatch Plateau.

Best Things to Do in Fountain Green: Activities and Tours

  • The Annual Fountain Green ATV Rodeo: Every July, Fountain Green hosts the ATV Rodeo, featuring exciting ATV competitions, a parade, music, and food.
  • Sleigh Rides: During winter months, visitors can enjoy a romantic and cozy horse-drawn sleigh ride through the snowy countryside.
  • Fourth of July Parade: Come to Fountain Green in July and enjoy the parade with live music, dancing, and other fun activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fountain Green

For a perfect week-long itinerary for Fountain Green, we suggest the following plan:

Day 1: Visit Main Street and the Pioneer Relic Home Museum. Day 2: Hike or bike one of the scenic trails in the area. Day 3: Take a drive to Manti- La Sal National Forest and enjoy the beautiful scenery. Day 4: Visit the nearby Maple Canyon and go rock climbing. Day 5: Take a scenic drive on Skyline Drive. Day 6: Enjoy some ATV fun in the annual ATV Rodeo. Day 7: Relax and unwind in the beautiful Heritage Pioneer Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Fountain Green

Fountain Green has a thriving food and drink scene, offering a variety of local and traditional dishes that will leave your taste buds wanting more.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Sauce: Originally from Utah, this dipping sauce made from ketchup and mayonnaise is a must-try.
  • Funeral Potatoes: This cheesy and creamy potato casserole is a staple of Utah cuisine and is often served at funerals.
  • Jell-O: Believe it or not, Utah is the world’s Jell-O capital, and it’s a must-try for visitors.

Local Beverages

  • Mormon Tea: A traditional drink made from the Ephedra plant and served hot or cold.
  • Sarsaparilla: A root beer-like beverage made from the Sarsaparilla root.

Local Culture and Customs of Fountain Green

Best Time to Visit Fountain Green
