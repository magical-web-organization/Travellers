Introduction to Gunnison

Nestled in Central Utah amidst stunning landscapes and endless outdoor adventures, Gunnison is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Visitors can explore the beauty of the area’s natural wonders, delve into its rich history, and enjoy a range of outdoor activities throughout the year. From scenic drives, biking and hiking trails, to local cuisine and historic landmarks, there’s something for everyone in Gunnison.

List of Places to Visit in Gunnison

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Gunnison can learn about the region’s fascinating history through the area’s many museums and historic landmarks. A few of the top cultural attractions in Gunnison are:

The Pioneer Museum

The Pioneer Museum showcases the history of Gunnison Valley, including displays of Native American artifacts, pioneer clothing, early settlers’ tools, and much more. Visitors can enjoy guided tours and interactive exhibits that bring the area’s rich history to life.

The Annabella Schoolhouse

As Utah’s last remaining, publically-used, one-room schoolhouse, the Annabella Schoolhouse offers a unique glimpse into the history of education in rural Utah. Built in 1873, the schoolhouse is a well-preserved piece of Utah’s past that visitors can explore through guided tours.

Cove Fort Historic Site

Cove Fort Historic Site was established in 1961 to commemorate the Mormon pioneer’s westward trek along the Old Spanish Trail. Visitors can explore the fort’s impressive architecture, learn about life on the trail, and enjoy live demonstrations of pioneer skills.

Natural Attractions of Gunnison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gunnison offers a wealth of natural attractions that visitors can explore throughout the year. Here are some of the best spots to visit:

Sanpete County Mountains

The Sanpete County Mountains are a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the many hiking trails, backpacking routes, and mountain biking paths that wind through the area’s stunning, mountainous terrain. Whether you’re looking for a challenging hike or a leisurely stroll through nature, the Sanpete County Mountains have something for everyone.

Fishlake National Forest

Fishlake National Forest is another must-visit natural attraction in Gunnison. Visitors can explore the forest’s diverse, well-marked hiking trails, fish in one of the many idyllic lakes and streams or enjoy breathtaking views on a scenic drive, making this an ideal spot for family-friendly outdoor adventures.

Factory Butte

Factory Butte is an iconic symbol of the southwestern United States. Its towering, multi-colored rock formations and sweeping vistas offer a one-of-a-kind adventure for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can hike or bike through the area’s rugged terrain, take pictures of stunning landscapes or enjoy a peaceful moment with nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gunnison

Gunnison is a hiker’s paradise, with a range of trails to suit all skill levels. From easy, family-friendly nature walks to challenging mountain treks, the area offers a diverse range of trails. A few of the best hiking and biking routes in Gunnison are:

  • Gooseberry Trail
  • South Summit Trail
  • Paradise Canyon Trail
  • Skyline Drive Trail

Best Things to Do in Gunnison: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Gunnison can enjoy a variety of indoor and outdoor activities beyond hiking and biking. A few popular options are:

  • ATVing
  • Hunting and Fishing
  • Snowshoeing and Skiing
  • Golfing

In addition, there are several unique tours offered in the area, including the legendary “Llama Trekking” experience, giving visitors a unique opportunity to explore the area’s natural beauty with the assistance of llamas and expert guides.

Food and Drink Scene of Gunnison

Must-Try Dishes

Gunnison has a vibrant local food scene with a range of must-try dishes, including:

  • Scones with Honey Butter: A traditional Utah snack, scones are served with sweet honey butter for a delectable treat.
  • Jello Salad: While it might sound odd, Jello salad is a Utah staple. It’s a unique blend of fruit, gelatin, and whipped cream.
  • Fry Sauce: A combination of ketchup and mayonnaise, fry sauce is a popular condiment for everything from fries to burgers.

Local Beverages

Gunnison may be known for Jello Salad, but did you know that it’s also home to a few beverage gems?

  • Brigham’s Brew Root Beer: Brigham’s Brew is a local soda maker famous for its unique blend of spices that creates the perfect root beer flavor.
  • High Bench Hard Cider: This locally-made hard cider is a refreshing treat on a sunny day, with a crisp and unique taste profile.

Where to Stay in Gunnison: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of lodging options available in Gunnison, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. A few of the top options include:

  • The Gunnison Inn: A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms
  • The Crystal Inn Hotel & Suites: A mid-range option with a pool, fitness center, and free breakfast
  • The Barrett House: A luxurious bed and breakfast in a historic mansion with elegant decor and gourmet breakfast options

What to Buy in Gunnison: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gunnison’s rich culture and heritage offer a range of unique, local products for visitors to purchase and take home. A few popular options include:

  • Handmade pottery from Grizzly Claw Pottery. Their unique designs and vibrant colors make for perfect souvenirs.
  • Local honey and beeswax products from Capital Bee Company. Their honey and lotions are a Utah favorite.
  • Goat milk soaps and lotions from the Rustic Roost. Made with locally sourced goat milk, their products are the perfect way to pamper yourself.

Local Culture and Customs of Gunnison

Gunnison is steeped in Mormon history and culture. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  • Utah law prohibits smoking in public buildings and enclosed areas.
  • Alcohol sales are regulated by the state of Utah and may not be available in all areas.
  • Sunday is considered a day of rest, with many stores and restaurants being closed.

Best Time to Visit Gunnison

The best time to visit Gunnison depends on your personal preference and the activities you plan to enjoy. Summers can be hot, with average temperatures of 90°F, making outdoor activities in the mountains ideal. Winters can be cold, with moderate to heavy snowfall, making it an excellent opportunity for a snowy retreat.

Do:

  • Do try the scones with honey butter
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Don’t smoke in public buildings or enclosed areas.
  • Don’t expect to purchase alcohol easily.
  • Don’t forget to plan for Sunday closures while in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gunnison

Gunnison is generally considered a safe place to visit. Be aware of your surroundings while out hiking, and stick to well-marked trails. While it’s generally safe to drink the local tap water, consider bringing a reusable bottle with you to fill up at designated locations.

Health in Gunnison

Visitors to Gunnison should ensure they’re up-to-date on routine vaccinations before visiting. Mosquitos can be prevalent in the summer months, and it’s recommended that visitors take proper precautions, such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Gunnison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gunnison

The closest major airport to Gunnison is Salt Lake City International Airport, which is about a 3.5-hour drive from the area.

Bus Stations Near Gunnison

Greyhound bus lines do not offer service to Gunnison.

Other Options

Visitors can take scenic drives or book a rental car from Salt Lake City to Gunnison. Be aware that public transportation is limited in the area and it’s recommended to rent a car to get around.

How to Get Around in Gunnison

The best way to get around Gunnison is by car. There are several rental car options available in Salt Lake City, and some lodging options offer free shuttle service. Public transportation options are limited, making car rentals the most practical option.

Where to Go Next from Gunnison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Gunnison is a gateway to several other exciting tourist destinations. A few popular options include:

  • Bryce Canyon National Park
  • Zion National Park
  • Moab

Conclusion

Gunnison is a hidden gem filled with history, natural beauty, and unique experiences. Whether you’re exploring the historic landmarks, hiking the stunning trails, or sampling the local cuisine, Gunnison is a must-visit destination in Utah.
