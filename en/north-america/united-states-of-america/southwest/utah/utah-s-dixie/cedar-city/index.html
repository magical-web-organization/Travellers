Introduction to Cedar City

Located in southwestern Utah, Cedar City is a charming city that offers visitors a wide range of cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, delicious local food, and comfortable accommodation options. With its easy access to Zion National Park, Bryce Canyon National Park, and other national parks and monuments, Cedar City is the perfect base camp for exploring the wonders of southern Utah.

List of Places to Visit in Cedar City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cedar City has a rich history and a vibrant culture that can be explored through its numerous museums and historical sites. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Frontier Homestead State Park Museum: This museum showcases the pioneer heritage and early iron industry of Cedar City and the region. Visitors can explore the museum’s exhibits, historic buildings, and outdoor heritage park.

  • Southern Utah Museum of Art (SUMA): This museum showcases contemporary and historical art from the region and beyond. Visitors can explore the museum’s exhibitions and attend various art events and workshops.

  • Adams Shakespearean Theatre: This iconic outdoor theatre is located on the campus of Southern Utah University and hosts the annual Utah Shakespeare Festival from June to October.

Natural Attractions of Cedar City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cedar City is surrounded by breathtaking natural wonders that offer endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Kolob Canyons: This part of Zion National Park is located just a short drive from Cedar City and offers stunning scenic drives, hiking trails, and camping options.

  • Brian Head Resort: This premier ski and snowboard destination is located just 30 minutes from Cedar City and offers world-class winter sports and summer activities such as mountain biking, hiking, and scenic chairlift rides.

  • Cedar Breaks National Monument: This natural amphitheater with its colorful rock formations and wildflower meadows is a must-visit destination for photographers, hikers, and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cedar City

Cedar City is home to some of the most scenic and challenging hiking and biking trails in Utah. Whether you’re looking for an easy family hike or an extreme adventure, you’ll find it all here. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Coal Creek Trail: This easy trail follows a historic wagon road through beautiful meadows and forests, offering stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Lava Flow Trail: This moderate trail takes you through the stark landscape of the volcanic lava flow, offering amazing views of the mountains, forests, and wildflowers.

  • Navajo Lake Trail: This challenging trail takes you through aspen groves, meadows, and past a scenic alpine lake, offering stunning views of the surrounding peaks and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Cedar City: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun activities and tours to do in Cedar City. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Utah Shakespeare Festival: This world-renowned festival is a must-see event for theater lovers. It features multiple plays and events each day from June to October.

  • Iron County Fair: This annual fair is the oldest in Utah and features live music, rodeos, carnival rides, and exhibits of local crafts, produce, and livestock.

  • Cedar City Historical Downtown Walking Tour: This self-guided tour takes you through the historic downtown area and showcases the city’s rich history and architecture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cedar City

If you’re planning to stay in Cedar City for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Cedar City

Cedar City offers a diverse and delicious food and drink scene that caters to every taste and budget. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastrami Burger: This mouth-watering burger is a local favorite and features a juicy beef patty, pastrami, cheese, and all the fixings.

  • Scone: This deep-fried dough pastry is a Utah classic and comes in both sweet and savory varieties.

  • Jalapeño Cream Cheese-Stuffed Pretzel: This warm and gooey pretzel is stuffed with jalapeño cream cheese and served with a side of cheese sauce for dipping.

Local Beverages

  • Polygamy Porter: This dark and rich beer is brewed by the Utah Brewers Cooperative and features a smooth and chocolaty flavor.

  • Aggie Juice: This locally-made energy drink features a fruity and refreshing flavor and is a favorite among students and athletes.

  • Fry Sauce: This tangy and creamy dipping sauce is a Utah classic and is served with fries and burgers at most local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Cedar City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cedar City offers a wide range of comfortable accommodation options that cater to every budget and preference. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Abbey Inn: This boutique hotel offers luxurious rooms, a heated indoor pool, and a daily continental breakfast.

  • El Rey Inn & Suites: This centrally-located budget hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast.

  • Bard’s Inn: This cozy guesthouse offers affordable and comfortable rooms, a communal kitchen, and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Cedar City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping, Cedar City offers a unique selection of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for family and friends. Here are some of the best places to shop for them:

  • Cedar City Farmer’s Market: This lively market takes place every Saturday during the summer and features local produce, arts and crafts, and live music.

  • Iron County Bookstore: This local bookstore offers a great selection of books about Cedar City and the region, as well as gifts and souvenirs.

  • LDS Cedar City Temple Visitor Center: This visitor center offers a wide selection of LDS-themed gifts and souvenirs, as well as information about the local LDS community.

Local Culture and Customs of Cedar City

Cedar City is a friendly and welcoming community that values its history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some tips to help you respect the local culture and customs:

  • Respect the locals: Cedar City is home to a diverse community that includes Mormons, Native Americans, and other ethnic groups. Be respectful of their beliefs, customs, and traditions.

  • Leave no trace: When hiking or camping, pack out all your trash and respect the natural environment.

  • Be mindful of the weather: Cedar City experiences extreme weather conditions, from hot summers to snowy winters. Dress appropriately and check the weather forecast before heading out.

Best Time to Visit Cedar City

The best time to visit Cedar City depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a quick guide:

  • Summer (June to August): This is the most popular time to visit Cedar City, with warm temperatures and a wide range of outdoor activities and events.

  • Fall (September to November): This is a great time to visit for hiking and sightseeing, with cooler temperatures and fall foliage.

  • Winter (December to February): This is the best time to visit for winter sports, with plenty of snow and affordable accommodations.

  • Spring (March to May): This is a great time to visit for outdoor adventures and cultural events, with milder temperatures and blooming wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cedar City

Do:

  • Visit the national parks and monuments.
  • Attend a play or event at Utah Shakespeare Festival.
  • Try the local food and drink scene.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the trails or in the parks.
  • Ignore the extreme weather conditions.
  • Disrespect the locals or their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cedar City

Cedar City is a safe and friendly city, but it’s always important to be mindful of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions:

  • Lock your car and accommodations.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Cedar City

Cedar City has several medical facilities, including a hospital, urgent care centers, and pharmacies. Make sure to bring any necessary medications and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Cedar City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cedar City is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Cedar City

  • Cedar City Regional Airport (CDC): This small airport offers daily flights to and from Salt Lake City and is located just 3 miles from downtown Cedar City.

  • McCarran International Airport (LAS): This large airport is located in Las Vegas, Nevada, and offers direct flights to and from Cedar City on select days.

Bus Stations Near Cedar City

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is located in St. George, Utah, about 50 miles from Cedar City, and offers daily bus service to and from major cities in the western United States.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental agencies located at the Cedar City Regional Airport and in downtown Cedar City.

  • Taxi and Uber: There are several taxi companies and Uber drivers available in Cedar City.

How to Get Around in Cedar City

Cedar City is a small and compact city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi companies and Uber drivers available. If you’re planning to explore the national parks or other attractions outside of the city, a rental car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Cedar City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cedar City is the perfect base camp for exploring southern Utah and its many wonders. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Zion National Park: This iconic national park is located just a short drive from Cedar City and offers stunning scenery, hiking trails, and camping options.

  • Bryce Canyon National Park: This otherworldly national park is located about two hours from Cedar City and features unique rock formations, hiking trails, and stargazing opportunities.

  • Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument: This vast and remote monument is located about three hours from Cedar City and offers breathtaking scenery, hiking trails, and camping options.

Conclusion

Cedar City is a gem of a city that offers visitors a perfect blend of history, culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week, you’re sure to fall in love with this charming and welcoming community in the heart of southern Utah.
