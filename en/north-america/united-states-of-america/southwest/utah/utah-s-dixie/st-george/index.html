St. George, located in Utah’s Dixie region, is a charming city with a rich history that is worth exploring. It’s surrounded by incredible natural landscapes, from stunning red rock formations to canyons, parks, and trails perfect for hiking, biking, and outdoor adventures. This guide details the best things to do in St. George, including cultural landmarks, natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and transportation options.

Introduction to St. George

St. George was founded in 1861 by Mormon pioneers, and its history has been shaped by various communities and cultures. Today, it’s a bustling city that is home to over 80,000 residents and attracts visitors from all over the world. It’s known for its sunny weather, mild winters, and incredible natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in St. George

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking to relax, St. George has plenty of attractions to offer. Here are some of the must-visit places in St. George:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brigham Young Winter Home and Office: This historic building was the winter home of the second president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, Brigham Young. It’s been beautifully preserved and now operates as a museum.

  • St. George Temple: Completed in 1877, this beautiful temple is a must-visit landmark in St. George. It’s the oldest operating temple of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

  • Daughters of Utah Pioneers Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Utah pioneers, and its collection includes artifacts, furniture, clothing, and documents.

Natural Attractions of St. George: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zion National Park: About an hour’s drive away from St. George, Zion National Park is a paradise for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. Its steep red rock cliffs, narrow canyons, and breathtaking vistas make it one of the most stunning national parks in the U.S.

  • Snow Canyon State Park: This stunning park is home to red sandstone formations, lava fields, and sweeping vistas. It’s a popular spot for hiking, biking, and camping.

  • Red Cliffs Recreation Area: Located in the Red Cliffs Desert Reserve, this area offers stunning landscapes, hiking trails, and a refreshing stream ideal for swimming and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. George

St. George is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its stunning desert landscapes and rock formations. Here are some of the best hiking/biking trails in St. George:

  • Red Mountain Trail: This moderate to difficult trail offers stunning views of Snow Canyon and the Red Mountain. It’s about five miles round trip.

  • Kanarra Creek Trail: This beautiful trail takes you through a slot canyon, past waterfalls and pools. It’s about 3.5 miles round trip.

  • Bear Claw Poppy Trail: This easy to moderate trail is perfect for families and offers beautiful views of the Virgin River and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in St. George: Activities and Tours

St. George offers a range of activities and tours, from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best things to do in St. George:

  • Tuacahn Amphitheatre: This outdoor theatre hosts musicals, concerts, and plays against a spectacular red rock backdrop.

  • Kayenta Art Village: This unique art village is home to galleries, studios, and shops featuring local artists.

  • St. George Dinosaur Discovery Site: This museum displays dinosaur tracks, fossils, and other prehistoric artifacts.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. George

If you’re planning a week-long trip to St. George, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Zion National Park. Day 2: Explore Snow Canyon State Park. Day 3: Visit the Red Cliffs Recreation Area. Day 4: Visit the Tuacahn Amphitheatre and Kayenta Art Village. Day 5: Go hiking in the Red Mountains or take a tour of St. George’s historical landmarks. Day 6: Discover local cuisine and drinks in St. George. Day 7: Depart for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of St. George

St. George’s food and drink scene is diverse and reflects the city’s cultural history. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jordanelle Trout: This locally sourced trout is grilled to perfection and served with a side of vegetables.
  • Navajo Taco: This dish features a frybread base topped with beef, beans, lettuce, and cheese.
  • Fry Sauce: This classic Utah sauce is a mix of mayonnaise and ketchup and is perfect for dipping fries.

Local Beverages

  • Polygamy Porter: This beer is brewed by the Wasatch Brewery and is named after Utah’s history of polygamy.
  • Utah Peach Beer: This light and fruity beer is a favorite among locals.
  • Caffe Ibis Coffee: This local coffee shop offers organic and fair-trade coffee beans.

Where to Stay in St. George: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. George offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in St. George:

  • The Inn at Entrada: This luxury hotel is located within a gated community and features a spa, golf course, and swimming pool.

  • Green Gate Village Inn: This historic inn offers cozy rooms and suites perfect for a romantic getaway.

  • Hostel Zion: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorm-style rooms and private rooms with shared bathrooms.

What to Buy in St. George: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. George offers plenty of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or keepsakes. Here are some of the best things to buy in St. George:

  • St. George T-Shirts and Hoodies: These classic souvenirs feature iconic St. George landmarks.

  • Local Honey: St. George is home to many beekeepers who produce delicious honey.

  • Cowboy Boots and Hats: St. George’s western heritage is reflected in its cowboy boots and “Stetson” hats.

Local Culture and Customs of St. George

St. George’s culture is heavily influenced by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, and its history of Mormon pioneers. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and avoid drinking alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit St. George

The best time to visit St. George is in the spring or fall when the temperatures are comfortable, and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be scorching, with temperatures exceeding 100 degrees Fahrenheit, while winters can be mild but chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. George

Do:

  • Hike or bike in St. George’s stunning natural landscapes.
  • Visit local museums and historical landmarks.
  • Try local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Litter or damage natural landmarks.
  • Disrespect local customs or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. George

St. George is a generally safe city, but visitors should be cautious when hiking in remote areas where there may not be cell phone reception. Visitors should also be careful when swimming in bodies of water as flash floods are possible during heavy rainfall.

Health in St. George

St. George has several medical facilities, including a hospital and urgent care clinics. Visitors should bring plenty of water and sunscreen when hiking in the desert, and be aware of the signs of heat exhaustion.

Getting to St. George: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. George

The closest airport to St. George is the St. George Regional Airport, located about 10 miles southeast of the city center. The airport offers direct flights to several cities, including Phoenix, Denver, and Los Angeles.

Bus Stations Near St. George

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in St. George and offers bus services to several destinations in Utah, Arizona, and Nevada.

Other Options

St. George is also accessible by car and is located on Interstate 15.

How to Get Around in St. George

St. George offers several transportation options, including taxis, rental cars, and a public bus system. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from St. George: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. George is a great base for exploring nearby destinations such as Las Vegas, Lake Powell, and Grand Canyon National Park. Visitors can also take day trips to nearby towns like Springdale, Hurricane, and Cedar City.

Conclusion

St. George is a hidden gem in Utah’s Dixie region. It offers a perfect combination of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, St. George has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next trip to St. George and discover its unique charm.
