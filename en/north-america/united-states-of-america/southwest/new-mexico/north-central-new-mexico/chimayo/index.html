Introduction to Chimayó

Nestled in the heart of North Central New Mexico, Chimayó is a small town with a big story. Once a thriving center of trade and agriculture, it is now a popular destination for tourists seeking to experience the rich history and natural beauty of the Southwest.

List of Places to Visit in Chimayó

Below is a comprehensive list of the top attractions in Chimayó:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santuario de Chimayó: One of the most important Catholic pilgrimage sites in North America, visited by thousands of pilgrims every year.
  • Chimayó Museum: Featuring exhibits on the history of Chimayó and the surrounding region.
  • Chimayó Trading Post: A historic trading post that has been in operation since the 1930s, offering a variety of local goods and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Chimayó: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rio Grande Gorge: A stunning natural wonder, the Rio Grande Gorge is a must-see attraction for visitors to Chimayó.
  • Trampas Lake: A serene oasis surrounded by stunning mountain views, ideal for hiking, fishing and picnicking.
  • Carson National Forest: Offering a wide range of outdoor recreational activities, including hiking, camping, and backpacking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chimayó

Chimayó offers some of the most scenic trails in the Southwest, here are some of our top picks:

  • El Potrero Trail: A challenging 4-mile hike that takes you through rugged high desert scenery.
  • Chimayó to Santa Fe Trail: A 14-mile bike trail that winds through the scenic landscape, connecting Chimayó with the historic city of Santa Fe.
  • Nambe Lake Trail: A beautiful 3-mile hike that takes you through stunning alpine terrain, culminating in a tranquil mountain lake.

Best Things to Do in Chimayó: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor recreation, Chimayó has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Chimayó:

  • Take a guided tour of Santuario de Chimayó to learn about the history and significance of this important pilgrimage site.
  • Go rock climbing or zip-lining at the nearby Santa Fe Climbing Center.
  • Go horseback riding through the stunning New Mexico landscape with a local outfitter.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chimayó

Day 1: Arrive in Chimayó and visit Santuario de Chimayó.

Day 2: Explore the Chimayó Museum and Chimayó Trading Post.

Day 3: Hike the El Potrero Trail or take a guided tour of Carson National Forest.

Day 4: Take a day trip to explore the historic city of Santa Fe.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of outdoor recreation at Trampas Lake.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of a local winery or brewery.

Day 7: Relax and soak in the natural hot springs at Ojo Caliente Mineral Springs Resort & Spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Chimayó

Chimayó is known for its unique blend of Native American and Spanish cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chimayó Red Chile: A spicy traditional sauce made from locally grown chili peppers.
  • Posole: A hearty stew made with hominy, pork, and red chili.

Local Beverages

  • Pinon Coffee: A local coffee blend with a nutty flavor that perfectly complements Chimayó’s spicy cuisine.
  • New Mexico Wine: The region’s high altitude and diverse soil types create the perfect conditions for producing excellent wines.

Where to Stay in Chimayó: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chimayó offers a wide variety of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Plaza Real: A traditional adobe-style hotel located in the heart of historic downtown Chimayó.
  • Camping at Carson National Forest: For the outdoor-minded, camping is available in the beautiful Carson National Forest.
  • Casa Escondida Bed & Breakfast: A charming bed and breakfast offering a unique blend of historic architecture and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Chimayó: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chimayó is known for its unique selection of handmade goods, including:

  • Traditional hand-woven textiles, such as rugs, blankets, and clothing.
  • Locally made pottery and ceramics, featuring intricate designs inspired by the nearby landscape.
  • Handmade jewelry, featuring natural stones and beads sourced from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Chimayó

Chimayó is deeply connected to its Spanish and Native American roots, resulting in a unique blend of cultures. Visitors are encouraged to respect local traditions and customs, such as attending a Feast Day celebration at Santuario de Chimayó and learning about the history and significance of Chimayó’s indigenous inhabitants.

Best Time to Visit Chimayó

The best time to visit Chimayó is during the fall and spring months when the weather is mild, and the landscape is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chimayó

Do:

  • Respect the area’s rich cultural history and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor recreational opportunities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural landscape in any way.
  • Disrespect the religious significance of Santuario de Chimayó.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chimayó

Chimayó is a generally safe destination, but visitors are advised to take the usual precautions such as staying aware and avoiding leaving valuables in cars.

Health in Chimayó

Visitors are advised to drink plenty of water and take precautions against the high altitude, such as gradually acclimating to the elevation and avoiding excessive physical activity in the first few days.

Getting to Chimayó: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chimayó is accessible by various means of transportation. Here are the main options:

Airports Near Chimayó

  • Albuquerque International Sunport Airport - 84 miles
  • Santa Fe Regional Airport - 31 miles

Bus Stations Near Chimayó

  • Santa Fe Greyhound Bus Station - 23 miles

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport to explore Chimayó and the surrounding area.
  • Private shuttle: Several companies offer private shuttle services from the airport to Chimayó.

How to Get Around in Chimayó

The best way to get around Chimayó is by car. Visitors can rent a car at the airport or take a taxi from Santa Fe.

Where to Go Next from Chimayó: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santa Fe: With its rich history and cultural attractions, Santa Fe is an excellent destination for art and architecture enthusiasts.
  • Taos: Just a short drive from Chimayó, Taos is known for its stunning natural beauty and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Los Alamos: A city with a rich scientific history, featuring the Los Alamos National Laboratory and the Bradbury Science Museum.

Conclusion

With its unique blend of culture and stunning natural scenery, Chimayó is a destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor recreation, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, Chimayó is sure to leave a lasting impression.
