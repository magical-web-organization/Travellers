Introduction to Santa Fe

Santa Fe, located in North Central New Mexico, is a city rich in history, culture, and art. It is the oldest capital city in the United States, with a unique blend of Native American, Spanish, and American cultures. From museums and galleries to outdoor adventures and delicious local cuisines, Santa Fe offers an unforgettable experience to every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Fe

Here is a list of must-visit places in Santa Fe divided into cultural and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palace of the Governors - A 400-year-old National Historic Landmark that houses the New Mexico History Museum.
  • Georgia O’Keeffe Museum - A museum dedicated to the works of Georgia O’Keeffe, one of the most important American artists of the 20th century.
  • Canyon Road - A historic arts district that features over 100 art galleries and studios.
  • Loretto Chapel - A famous architectural marvel, known for its helix-shaped spiral staircase.
  • Santa Fe Opera House - An open-air theater with a stunning backdrop of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains.

Natural Attractions of Santa Fe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Santa Fe National Forest - A vast national forest that offers various recreational activities such as hiking, camping, fishing, and skiing.
  • Bandelier National Monument - A prehistoric archaeological site that features ancient Puebloan cliff dwellings.
  • Kasha-Katuwe Tent Rocks National Monument - A unique geological formation that features towering cone-shaped tent rocks and canyons.
  • The Ski Basin - A popular ski resort with over 80 trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Fe

Santa Fe offers a variety of hiking and biking routes, ranging from beginner to advanced levels. Some of the famous trails include:

  • Atalaya Mountain Trail - A challenging 8.5-mile trail that offers stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  • Dale Ball Trails - A network of trails that offers a combination of easy, moderate, and difficult options.
  • La Tierra Trails - A beginner-friendly network of trails that offers easy access from the town.

Best Things to Do in Santa Fe: Activities and Tours

  • Santa Fe Margarita Trail - A fun way to explore the local bar scene and taste some of the best margaritas in the city.
  • Santa Fe Farmers Market - A seasonal event that features fresh local produce and food.
  • Ten Thousand Waves - A Japanese-style hot spring and spa that offers relaxation and rejuvenation.
  • Downtown Plaza - A historic landmark that offers a variety of dining, shopping, and entertainment options.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Fe

Day Activity
1 Explore historic landmarks such as Loretto Chapel and Palace of the Governors.
2 Visit art galleries and museums such as Georgia O’Keeffe Museum and Museum of International Folk Art.
3 Go on a hiking or biking adventure on La Tierra Trails or Atalaya Mountain Trail.
4 Explore the natural wonders of Santa Fe such as Bandelier National Monument and Kasha-Katuwe Tent Rocks National Monument.
5 Take a day trip to Taos Pueblo, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a historic Native American community.
6 Relax and rejuvenate at Ten Thousand Waves, a Japanese-style hot spring and spa.
7 Indulge in local cuisines and shop for souvenirs in Downtown Plaza and Canyon Road.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Fe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile Stew - A spicy stew made with New Mexico’s famous green chile.
  • Tamales - A traditional Mesoamerican dish made of masa and filled with meat, cheese or vegetables.
  • Horno Baked Bread - An oven-baked bread made with locally grown wheat.
  • Posole - A hearty soup made with pork, hominy, and red chile.

Local Beverages

  • Sangria - A refreshing alcoholic drink made with red wine, fruit, and brandy.
  • Cerveza - A light beer made by Santa Fe Brewing Company, the oldest brewery in the city.
  • Margarita - A classic cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec.

Where to Stay in Santa Fe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luxurious Options

  • Four Seasons Resort Rancho Encantado Santa Fe - A luxury resort with scenic views and outdoor activities.
  • Rosewood Inn of the Anasazi - A luxurious inn that offers a blend of modern amenities and traditional Southwestern elegance.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Santa Fe International Hostel - A cozy and affordable hostel that offers a variety of dorms and private rooms.
  • El Paradero Bed and Breakfast Inn - A budget-friendly bed and breakfast that offers a variety of accommodations and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Santa Fe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Handicrafts - Authentic handcrafted pottery, jewelry, textiles, and paintings made by Native American artisans.
  • Chimayo Chile - A spicy red chile that is locally grown in Chimayo, a small village near Santa Fe.
  • Santa Fe Style Jewelry - A blend of Native American, Spanish, and Western styles that are unique to Santa Fe.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Fe

  • Respect the Native American Culture - Santa Fe has a strong presence of Native American culture, so it’s important to respect their values and customs.
  • Appreciate the Art Scene - Santa Fe has a thriving art scene, so take some time to appreciate the works of local artists.
  • Embrace the Saddlebag Tradition - Santa Fe is famous for its use of saddlebags, a traditional Native American shoulder bag made of leather and wool, so consider purchasing one as a souvenir.

Best Time to Visit Santa Fe

The best time to visit Santa Fe is in the fall (September to November) and spring (March to May) when the weather is mild, and the city holds various festivals and events. The summer (June to August) is also popular, but it can get hot during the day.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Fe

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of Native Americans.
  • Take time to explore the local art scene.
  • Try the local cuisines and beverages that are unique to Santa Fe.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash and litter behind, especially in the outdoors.
  • Disrespect the historic landmarks and buildings.
  • Take photographs of Native Americans without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Fe

Santa Fe is generally a safe city, but it’s always important to take precautions and practice common sense. Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas and keep valuables secure.

Health in Santa Fe

Santa Fe is located at an elevation of 7,000 feet above sea level, so altitude sickness is a possibility, especially for those with respiratory problems. Stay hydrated and rest when needed, and consult a medical professional if symptoms persist.

Getting to Santa Fe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Fe

  • Santa Fe Regional Airport - The closest airport to the city is located 10 miles southwest.
  • Albuquerque International Sunport - A larger airport is located 65 miles south, which offers more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Santa Fe

  • Santa Fe Depot - A train station that doubles as a bus terminal is located downtown.

Other Options

  • Drive - Santa Fe is accessible by car, and it’s worth considering renting a car to explore the surrounding areas.
  • Train - The Amtrak Southwest Chief train runs daily between Los Angeles and Chicago, with stops in Santa Fe.

How to Get Around in Santa Fe

The best way to get around in Santa Fe is by car, especially if you want to explore the surrounding areas. However, if you’re staying in the downtown area, you can get around easily on foot, by bike, or by using the local bus system. Taxis and rideshare options are also available.

Where to Go Next from Santa Fe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taos Pueblo - A historic Native American community located 70 miles north of Santa Fe.
  • Jemez Mountains - A scenic mountain range located 40 miles west of Santa Fe.
  • Ghost Ranch - A scenic retreat and education center located 60 miles northwest of Santa Fe.

Conclusion

Santa Fe is a unique travel destination that offers an unrivaled blend of culture, art, history, and natural beauty. From the museums and galleries to the outdoor adventures and delicious local cuisines, there’s something for everyone in Santa Fe. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan the perfect trip and make unforgettable memories.
