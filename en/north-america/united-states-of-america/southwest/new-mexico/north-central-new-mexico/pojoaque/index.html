Introduction to Pojoaque

Pojoaque is located in North Central New Mexico, in the United States of America. This small town is known for its unique blend of cultures, beautiful natural landscapes, and rich history. Its Pueblo Indian origins can be traced back to the late 1500s, and a visit to its museums and historical places can give you insights into its past.

List of Places to Visit in Pojoaque

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Poeh Cultural Center: Learn about the history, customs, and arts of the Pueblo Indian people at this museum. It has a collection of art pieces, including paintings, pottery, and jewelry, as well as hands-on workshops for visitors.

  • Puye Cliff Dwellings: This archeological site is located outside Pojoaque and boasts of remnants of cliff dwellings built by ancestral Pueblo people between the 10th and 14th centuries. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the people who once lived here.

Natural Attractions of Pojoaque: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Towa Golf Club: This golf course is surrounded by stunning views of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains and the Jemez Mountains, making it unique among golf courses in the Southwest.

  • Bishop’s Lodge: This historic destination offers a chance to relax in the serene natural landscape of Pojoaque. Visitors can enjoy horseback riding, hiking, fishing, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pojoaque

  • Nambe Falls Hike Trail: This 4.3 mile trail starts at the Nambe Pueblo and leads to the stunning Nambe Falls. It is a moderate hike, and the views of the waterfall are worth the effort.

  • Las Dos: This 7-mile trail offers a mix of moderate to challenging terrain, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Pojoaque: Activities and Tours

  • Chimayo weavers tour: Visitors can take a tour of Chimayo weavers, a local group of artisans renowned for their hand-woven tapestries. You can learn about their techniques, view their work, and even purchase some local craftsmanship.

  • Balloon Fiesta: Attend Pojoaque’s annual hot air balloon fiesta, one of the most popular balloon festivals in the world. Witness the launch of hundreds of balloons, enjoy food, drink, and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pojoaque

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrival and check-in at one of Pojoaque’s comfortable and accommodating hotels.
Day 2 Explore Pojoaque’s Museums and Historical Sites such as Puye Cliff Dwellings and the Poeh Cultural Center.
Day 3 Go on a hike at Nambe Falls Hike Trail followed by lunch at the Towa Golf Club.
Day 4 Visit the Chimayo weavers and explore the colorful tapestries and art on display.
Day 5 Take a tour of the El Santuario de Chimayo and marvel at its beautiful architecture and surroundings.

Day 7 Attend the Balloon Fiesta for world class hot air balloon displays.

Food and Drink Scene of Pojoaque

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sopapillas: These fried pastry pillows are often served with honey and a salad.

  • Green Chile: An iconic ingredient in New Mexican cuisine, green chile is used in many dishes such as green chile stew and enchiladas.

Local Beverages

  • Blue Corn Brewery: This brewpub offers a rotating range of lovely beers and great ciders, as well as a full menu of tasty food.

  • Estrella Del Norte Winery: This family-owned winery produces a variety of red and white wines, as well as offers tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Pojoaque: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Santa Fe Opera: Known for its famous summer festival, this opera house offers comfortable and luxurious rooms for guests.

  • Hacienda De Los Santos: This boutique hotel offers unique and individually decorated rooms, each furnished with charming southwest-inspired decor.

What to Buy in Pojoaque: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery and Sculptures: The Pueblo Indian people of Pojoaque are known for their spectacular pottery and clay sculptures. Visit local shops and galleries to find some one-of-a-kind pieces to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Pojoaque

The Pueblo Indian people of Pojoaque are the primary residents of the area and are considered as one of the 19 Indian Pueblos of New Mexico. Some of their traditions include powwows, dances, and religious celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Pojoaque

The best time to plan a visit to Pojoaque is from late spring to early fall when the weather is pleasant, making it ideal to explore the natural wonders and outdoor spaces it has to offer. The Balloon Fiesta, which is held in October, is a great time to experience the town’s lively atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pojoaque

Do:

  • Respect the local arts, culture, and traditions of the Pueblo people.

  • Dress appropriately and avoid swimming in the natural pools and streams that the locals consider sacred.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the locals without asking for permission first.

  • Touch or remove any artifacts from the Puye Cliff Dwellings and other historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pojoaque

Pojoaque is generally safe, but like any town, it is best to be alert and aware of your surroundings, particularly when venturing into its natural spaces. Be cautious when hiking and biking, and be mindful of local wildlife.

Health in Pojoaque

Pojoaque has a small medical center, but for serious medical emergencies, it is best to visit the neighboring towns such as Santa Fe or Los Alamos.

Getting to Pojoaque: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pojoaque

The nearest airport to Pojoaque is Santa Fe Municipal Airport, which is around 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Pojoaque

The nearest major bus stations include Santa Fe Trailways and Greyhound.

Other Options

One of the best ways to experience Pojoaque’s natural beauty is by renting a car and driving from nearby major cities like Santa Fe or Albuquerque.

How to Get Around in Pojoaque

The best way to explore Pojoaque is by driving a rental car. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available but are not as readily available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Pojoaque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santa Fe: Considered one of the most important cultural and artistic centers of the United States, Santa Fe is an iconic destination famous for its museums, galleries, and restaurants.

  • Bandelier National Monument: A national monument with a variety of hiking trails that takes visitors through ancient ruins and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Pojoaque is a hidden gem tucked away in the heart of North Central New Mexico. It offers a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a unique blend of cultures that make for an unforgettable visit. Plan a trip to this town and make memories that will last a lifetime.
