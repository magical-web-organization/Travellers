Introduction to Española

Española is a small town located in North Central New Mexico, United States, and is known for its rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. This charming town has been inhabited by Native American tribes for centuries and was later colonized by the Spanish and Mexican settlers, making it a unique cultural destination. Visitors can dive into the city’s past by exploring its museums and historical sites or marvel at its scenic views and participate in outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Española

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bond House Museum

Bond House Museum is one of the oldest houses in Española, built-in 1915, and is now a museum showcasing the city’s colonial history. It is a great place to learn about the region’s culture and architecture.

Poeh Museum

Poeh Museum preserves the legacy of the Towa-speaking people of the Pueblo of Pojoaque, showcasing their arts, crafts, and traditions.

El Rancho de las Golondrinas

El Rancho de las Golondrinas, located in the outskirts of Española, is a restored Spanish colonial ranch that showcases rural life in the Southwest.

Natural Attractions of Española: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ojo Caliente Mineral Springs Resort and Spa

Ojo Caliente Mineral Springs Resort and Spa is a premier destination for relaxation and rejuvenation. The resort has a variety of hot springs and pools, and visitors can also indulge in spa treatments and yoga classes.

Santa Fe National Forest

Santa Fe National Forest stretches across 1.6 million acres, offers a great escape from the bustling city. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting throughout this natural wonderland.

Rio Grande Gorge

Rio Grande Gorge is a must-visit destination for those who enjoy scenic views. The gorge is located just north of Española, and visitors can marvel at the Rio Grande rushing through the canyon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Española

Española offers ample opportunities for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. Some of the trails that offer the most spectacular views include:

  • The Chamisa Trail
  • The Diablo Canyon Trail
  • The Las Conchas Trail
  • The Jeffrey Trail

Best Things to Do in Española: Activities and Tours

The following are a few must-do activities in Española:

Visit the Chimayó Trading Post

Chimayó Trading Post is one of the oldest trading posts in New Mexico, selling various handmade items like pottery, blankets, and jewelry.

Balloon Rides

Experience the stunning landscape of Española while being thousands of feet in the air with balloon rides.

Tour a Lavender Farm

Lavender in the Valley Farm is a beautiful lavender farm located near Española and is a unique experience with a great photo opportunity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Española

Day 1: Start with a visit to the Poeh Museum, learning about the local culture, including artisan demonstrations and shopping. Then head to the El Rancho de las Golondrinas to experience life in the Spanish colonial era.

Day 2: Start hiking along the Chamisa Trail and conclude by relaxation time at the Ojo Caliente Mineral Springs Resort and Spa.

Day3: Visit the Rio Grande Gorge and end the day off with a balloon ride during sunset.

Day4: Sample some of the best local food at Chimayó Trading Post. Stop by Shash Dine Eco-Retreat to take a tour and admire the eco-friendly accommodations.

Day5: Go shopping in the city center for souvenirs and try some local food for lunch. Afterward, head out to spend the evening strolling through the beautiful Lavender in the Valley Farm.

Day 6: Take a drive up to Santa Fe National Forest, spend the day fishing or picnicking in the beautiful surroundings.

Day 7: Relive history while exploring the Bond House Museum and its importance to 20th century Española.

Food and Drink Scene of Española

Must-Try Dishes

Española is known for a unique style of regional cuisine, where many restaurants specialize in a fusion of Mexican and Native American foods. Here are some must-try dishes:

Green Chile

Green Chile is a signature ingredient in Southwest cuisine and is a staple in local dishes.

Tamales

Tamales is a traditional Native American dish made from masa and steamed cornhusks, often filled with meats or cheese.

Sopaipilla

Sopaipillas is a fried pastry that resembles fluffy bread and is often served with honey or butter.

Local Beverages

Sangria

Sangria is a fermented red wine and fruit-based drink that is often served in New Mexico cafes.

Margaritas

The classic Margarita, a tequila-based cocktail is a popular beverage in many local restaurants.

Where to Stay in Española: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn at the Delta

The Inn at the Delta is a luxurious bed and breakfast lodge, located on the banks of the Rio Grande.

Days Inn by Wyndham Española

Days Inn is an affordable lodging option that offers guests comfortable rooms and is located right next to the Santa Fe National Forest.

The Santa Claran Hotel and Casino

The Santa Claran Hotel and Casino is located in the heart of the city and offers visitors a blend of comfort and entertainment in the form of slots and table games.

What to Buy in Española: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chimayó Chile Products

Chimayó chile is a unique type of chile that can only be found in New Mexico. You’ll find Chile powder, salsa, and rubs in local shops.

Native American Pottery

Visit the Poeh Museum’s gift shop or any of the local stores to purchase Native American pottery, which comes in various shapes, sizes, and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Española

Española has been shaped by the blending of Native American and Hispanic cultures and traditions, visible through its art and architecture. Locals have preserved the ancestral traditions’ vitality, which visitors can observe through festivals, ceremonies, and dances.

Best Time to Visit Española

The best time to visit Española is between March and May when the weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms are in full bloom. The summer can be hot, so it’s best to plan outdoor activities early in the morning or late in the evening.

Do’s and Don’ts of Española

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Visit the museums and learn about the city’s history.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride to see the city from above.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of the local Pueblos without permission.
  • Touch artifacts inside museums and historical sites.
  • Use drugs and alcohol on public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Española

Española is a safe place to visit, as the town has a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take safety precautions and always keep valuable items like wallets, purses, and electronic devices within reach.

Health in Española

Española is a high altitude location, and the strong sun can cause dehydration and heat stroke. Make sure to stay hydrated and use sunscreen.

Getting to Española: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Española

The Santa Fe Municipal Airport is the closest airport to Española. The Albuquerque International Sunport airport is just a couple of hours drive away and often is a much cheaper ticket option.

Bus Stations Near Española

The Rail Runner Express and Greyhound bus lines offer transportation to different cities across New Mexico.

Other Options

Car rentals are available downtown and allow visitors to enjoy the scenic drives throughout New Mexico and the Southwest.

How to Get Around in Española

The city has various transportation options that include rental cars, local shuttle services, and public transportation via the North Central Regional Transit District.

Where to Go Next from Española: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taos

The beautiful town of Taos offers a mix of art, history, and outdoor adventures. It is just a one-hour drive from Española.

Albuquerque

Albuquerque, known as the “Land of Enchantment,” is a fantastic city to explore and indulge in southwestern culture. The city is a two-hour drive from Española.

Conclusion

Española is an ideal travel destination as it offers a unique blend of culture and outdoor activities. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Española with ease and look forward to experiencing the city’s rich history, natural beauty, local food, and welcoming locals.
