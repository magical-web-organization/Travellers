Chama, New Mexico, located in the North Central region of the state, is a hidden gem just waiting to be discovered. With its beautifully rugged landscape and rich history, it’s no wonder that Chama has become a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and nature lovers alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect vacation to Chama.

Introduction to Chama

The small town of Chama, known for its charm and character, sits at an elevation of 7,871 feet in the San Juan Mountains. It’s surrounded by breathtaking landscapes and breathtaking views, making it an ideal destination for those seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Chama is the perfect place to relax, unwind, and get back to nature.

List of Places to Visit in Chama

Chama has a lot to offer visitors, from historically significant museums and landmarks to stunning natural beauty. Here are some of the top places to visit in Chama:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cumbres & Toltec Scenic Railroad: Hop on a historic train and take a ride through the mountains on the Cumbres & Toltec Scenic Railroad, where you’ll travel through tunnels, cross bridges, and see some of the most stunning views in the area.

  2. Chama Depot and Railroad Museum: Learn about the rich railway history of Chama at the Chama Depot and Railroad Museum, where you’ll see artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s history.

  3. Chama Cemetery: This cemetery offers a look into the history of Chama, where some of the oldest gravestones date back to the 1800s. The cemetery also offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Chama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Heron Lake State Park: With over 5,000 acres of land and water, this park offers a variety of water activities such as kayaking, fishing, and windsurfing. It’s also a great spot for hiking and camping.

  2. Brazos Cliffs: The stunning Brazos Cliffs are a popular spot for rock climbing and hiking. The 2,500-foot cliffs offer incredible views of the surrounding area.

  3. Edward Sargent Wildlife Area: This wildlife area offers a chance to see elk, deer, and other animals in their natural habitat. It’s also a great spot for bird watching and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chama

For those who love to hike or bike, Chama offers some of the best trails in the state. Here are some top routes to add to your itinerary:

  1. Continental Divide Trail: This 3,100-mile trail passes through Chama, offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  2. Cumbres Pass Trail: This 14-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks and valleys, with plenty of opportunities for wildlife spotting and bird watching.

  3. Upper Canyon Creek Trail: This 10-mile trail offers stunning views of the canyon and surrounding mountains, and is a great spot for bird watching and wildlife spotting.

Best Things to Do in Chama: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy during your stay in Chama. Here are some must-try activities:

  1. Horseback riding: There’s no better way to explore the area than by horseback. Many local ranches offer guided tours that take you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the area.

  2. ATV tours: Get your adrenaline pumping with an ATV tour through the mountains. You’ll see some of the most spectacular views in the area, and have a blast while doing it.

  3. Fly fishing: Chama is known for its excellent fly fishing opportunities, with the Chama River offering plenty of spots to cast a line.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chama

If you’re planning on spending a week in Chama, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Chama and check into your lodging.
2 Take a ride on the Cumbres & Toltec Scenic Railroad.
3 Explore the Chama Depot and Railroad Museum, and visit the Chama Cemetery for stunning views.
4 Spend the day at Heron Lake State Park, kayaking, fishing, and hiking.
5 Go rock climbing at the Brazos Cliffs.
6 Take a guided horseback or ATV tour through the mountains.
7 Spend the day fly fishing on the Chama River.

Food and Drink Scene of Chama

No vacation is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks in Chama:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicharron: Fried pork belly that’s crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

  2. Green Chile Stew: A hearty soup made with roasted green chiles, pork, and potatoes.

  3. Tacos: The tacos in Chama are some of the best you’ll ever have, with fillings like carne asada, grilled chicken, and pork.

Local Beverages

  1. Santa Fe Brewing Company: This brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their popular Santa Fe Gold Lager and Chicken Killer Barley Wine.

  2. Wine: The area around Chama is home to several wineries that offer tours and wine tastings, including the Vivac Winery and the Black Mesa Winery.

Where to Stay in Chama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for lodging in Chama, ranging from quaint bed and breakfasts to luxurious resorts. Here are some top options:

  1. The Shop: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Chama, and offers cozy rooms and personalized service.

  2. Branding Iron Motel: This motel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price, and is located just minutes from the Chama Depot.

  3. Chama River Bend Lodge: This lodge offers stunning views of the Chama River and surrounding mountains, as well as luxurious rooms and amenities.

What to Buy in Chama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring home, here are some ideas:

  1. Artwork: Chama is home to several artists who create stunning paintings, sculptures, and other works of art.

  2. Ceramics: The local ceramics scene in Chama is thriving, with several studios creating beautiful pottery, vases, and other decorative items.

  3. Jewelry: The area around Chama is known for its unique jewelry, including silver and turquoise rings, bracelets, and necklaces.

Local Culture and Customs of Chama

Chama has a rich cultural heritage, with several Native American tribes calling the area home for centuries. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and take time to learn about the history and culture of the area.

Best Time to Visit Chama

The best time to visit Chama is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chama

Here are some important things to keep in mind during your stay in Chama:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Take time to explore the area on foot or by horseback.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Leave garbage or litter in the area’s natural areas.

  • Ignore the signs and warnings when hiking or exploring.

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Chama is a safe and healthy place to visit, but visitors should take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Safety in Chama

  • Always hike and explore with a partner or in a group.

  • Follow the signs and warnings when hiking or exploring.

  • Be aware of the weather conditions and pack accordingly.

Health in Chama

  • Make sure to stay hydrated, especially when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and other skin damage.

  • Pack an emergency kit with first aid supplies and any necessary medication.

Getting to Chama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chama is located in a remote area of New Mexico, but there are still several transportation options to get you there.

Airports Near Chama

  • Albuquerque International Sunport: Located about 160 miles south of Chama.

Bus Stations Near Chama

  • The closest bus station to Chama is located in Espanola, about 55 miles away.

Other Options

  • Driving: Chama is located on State Highway 17, which connects to several major highways in the region.

  • Train: The Cumbres & Toltec Scenic Railroad offers a unique and historic way to travel to Chama.

How to Get Around in Chama

The best way to get around in Chama is by car, but many of the activities and attractions can also be explored on foot or by horseback. Local transportation services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Chama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Taos: This historic town is located about 75 miles northeast of Chama, and is known for its art galleries, shops, and restaurants.

  2. Santa Fe: Located about 120 miles south of Chama, Santa Fe is home to several museums, art galleries, and cultural events.

  3. Rio Grande Gorge: This stunning natural wonder is located about 70 miles southeast of Chama, and offers hiking, rafting, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Chama, New Mexico is a beautiful and fascinating destination for anyone who loves nature, history, and culture. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and unique cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Chama. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your perfect vacation to this hidden gem in North Central New Mexico.
