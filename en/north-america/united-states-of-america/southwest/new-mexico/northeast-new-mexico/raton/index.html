Introduction to Raton

Nestled in the mountains of Northeast New Mexico, Raton is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Founded in the 1800s, this city has served as a trading post, a stop on the Santa Fe Trail, and a strategic location during the Civil War. Today, Raton is a hub for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, foodies, and anyone looking for a unique Southwestern experience. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through all the must-see spots in Raton and provide excellent tips on how to spend your time in this one-of-a-kind city.

List of Places to Visit in Raton

Raton boasts a range of attractions that cater to different interests of tourists. Here’s a comprehensive list of places you shouldn’t leave Raton without visiting.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Raton Depot Museum: Located in the historic AT&SF Railway Depot, this museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the railroad in Raton and houses exhibits on the impact of the railroad on Raton’s development.
  • Raton Pass: Located on the Santa Fe Trail, this historic mountain pass was once an important gateway to the West and is now recognized as a National Historic Landmark.
  • Sugarite Canyon State Park: This former coal mining community is now a state park featuring hiking, fishing, and camping options. The park also offers guided tours of the historic mining town and its surface and underground relics.
  • The Capulin Volcano: Located just outside of Raton, this dormant volcano provides tourists with an opportunity to hike and enjoy the scenic views of the towering peak.

Natural Attractions of Raton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Maloya: This beautiful lake is known for its excellent trout fishing and various water sports activities like kayaking, paddleboarding, and swimming.
  • Santa Fe Trail Scenic Byway: This scenic drive stretches through southern Colorado and Northern New Mexico and offers breathtaking views of mountains, mesas, and scenic vistas.
  • Raton Pass Recreation Area: Located 7 miles southeast of Raton, this recreation area offers hiking trails and picnic areas as well as opportunities for rock climbing and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Raton

If you’re looking for hiking or biking trails in Raton, you would certainly be spoiled for choices. Here are a few of them.

  • The Front Range Trail: Located in the Purgatoire River Valley in Colorado, this scenic trail offers some of the most stunning views of the mountain ranges in the area.
  • The Lake Maloya Trail: Located in Sugarite Canyon State Park, this 4-mile loop trail offers gorgeous views of Lake Maloya and wildlife watching opportunities.
  • The Carry Canyon Trail: Located at the base of the Sweetgrass Hills, this 3-mile trail is ideal for bikers and hikers with stunning wildflowers fields and a panoramic view of the city.

Best Things to Do in Raton: Activities and Tours

  • Raton’s Rat Pack: This local company offers guided tours of historic sites and attractions in Raton, including the Raton Museum, the historic coal camp of Sugarite Canyon, and the Capulin Volcano.
  • Historic walking tours: Through walk along the town of Raton with a local guide to experience the rich history of Raton.
  • Raton Mainstreet: The downtown area of Raton offers an eclectic mix of local shops, cafes, and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Raton

When it comes to food and drink, Raton offers a mix of Western-style comfort food, exotic Mexican cuisine, and locally crafted beers. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages that are unique to Raton.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smothered Burritos: Originated in New Mexico, smothered burritos are a popular dish in the state. The dish typically consists of a flour tortilla stuffed with beans, cheese, and meat, topped with green chili sauce and melted cheese.
  • Raton’s Green Chili Burger: Raton’s green chili burger is a unique dish that combines a juicy beef patty with fire-roasted green chili and various toppings. It is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Rocky Mountain Oysters: This dish isn’t for everyone, but adventurous foodies might want to try it out. Rocky Mountain Oysters are bull testicles that are deep-fried and often served as an appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Trinidad Coffee Roasters: This local roastery offers a range of gourmet coffee blends that are organic, fair-trade, and locally roasted.
  • Raton Brewing Company: Established in 2016, the Raton Brewing Company is the first locally owned and operated brewery in Raton. The brewery offers a variety of beers that are crafted with local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Raton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or an affordable guesthouse, Raton offers many accommodation options. Here are a few recommendations.

  • El Portal Hotel: Located in the heart of downtown Raton, El Portal Hotel offers luxurious guestrooms in a historic building with a charming Southwestern decor.
  • Raton Pass Motor Inn: This 1950s-style motor inn offers a nostalgic experience with an outdoor pool, well-maintained rooms, and a convenient location on the highway.
  • Casa Lemus: This family-owned and operated hotel offers free Wi-Fi, comfortable beds, and a fantastic breakfast.

What to Buy in Raton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to purchase souvenirs or take home some locally made products, Raton won’t disappoint you. Here are some recommendations.

  • Raton Trading Company: This store offers a range of souvenirs specific to Raton, including T-shirts, hats, and mugs.
  • The Alley: This boutique shop offers clothing and gift items that are locally sourced and finely crafted.
  • Raton Farmers Market: The market offers fresh local produce, handmade jams, jellies, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Raton

Raton’s history and culture are deeply intertwined with its native peoples, Spanish, and European settlers. Visitors to Raton should respect the culture and customs of the local population. Some important things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting Catholic churches and other religious sites.
  • Respect the indigenous culture and use of language by learning some basic Spanish.
  • Raton is a small city, most of the parks and facilities have limited access and, the facilities are not open all year round.

Best Time to Visit Raton

The best time to visit Raton is from June to August when the weather is pleasant, and the area offers ample outdoor recreation opportunities. October is also a great time to visit Raton, as the autumn foliage colors the area’s majestic mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Raton

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Try the local cuisine and beers
  • Take part in outdoor recreational activities like hiking, fishing, and biking
  • Visit historic sites and scenic viewpoints

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property while hiking or exploring
  • Litter or damage natural or historic areas
  • Overlook the importance of sunscreen and plenty of water when engaging in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Raton is a relatively safe city, but it’s always a good idea to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Raton

  • Secure your valuables in your hotel room and don’t leave them unattended.
  • Park your vehicle in designated parking areas.
  • Be cautious while hiking and exploring remote areas, report any suspicious or threatening activities to the local authorities.

Health in Raton

  • Wear sunscreen and long-sleeved clothing when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, especially during summer.
  • Stay hydrated by carrying plenty of water and electrolyte drinks when taking part in outdoor recreational activities.
  • Altitude sickness is something that tourists should keep in mind when traveling to New Mexico. So, it’s better to stay hydrated and use proper sun protection.

Getting to Raton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Raton is accessible by car, bus or airplane, and the city offers several transportation options to travel around.

Airports Near Raton

  • Raton Municipal Airport: located just 9 miles away from the city, this airport operates chartered flights and general aviation.
  • Albuquerque International Sunport: Located 3 hours away from the city, this is the closest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Raton

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus station is located within the city limits and offers several transportation options to nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Private car rental services: Enterprise and Hertz are the two major car rental companies that operate in Raton.
  • Taxis are also available, with several reputable taxi companies working within the city.

How to Get Around in Raton

Raton is easy to navigate and is a walking-friendly city. The downtown area has wide sidewalks, and most attractions are within walking distance, but cycling and renting a car are also excellent options.

Where to Go Next from Raton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Raton’s location on the Colorado-New Mexico border makes it a great base for those looking to explore nearby areas.

  • Taos: Located around two and a half hours from Raton, Taos is known for its breathtaking landscapes, excellent skiing conditions, and thriving arts scene.
  • Santa Fe: This historic city is the cultural heart of the state, and its downtown area is a wonderland of galleries, shops, and Victorian architecture.

Conclusion

In all, a visit to Raton is a perfect opportunity to explore not only the history and culture of New Mexico but also the awe-inspiring landscapes and outdoor recreational activities that make this state so appealing to visitors. With a host of attractions, hiking trails, and historic landmarks, Raton will provide a unique and unforgettable experience for anyone looking to explore this scenic Southwestern city.
