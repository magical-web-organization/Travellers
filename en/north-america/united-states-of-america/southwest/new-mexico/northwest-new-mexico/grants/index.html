Introduction to Grants

Located in the heart of Northwest New Mexico, Grants is a small but bustling city known for its unique culture, stunning natural beauty, and rich history. Founded in the early 20th century as a mining town, Grants has since evolved into a hub for outdoor recreation, with its proximity to the stunning landscapes of the nearby Cibola National Forest and El Malpais National Monument.

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking to relax and unwind, Grants offers a plethora of activities and attractions to suit any traveler’s interests. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Grants.

List of Places to Visit in Grants

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

New Mexico Mining Museum

Initially used as a mining laboratory, the New Mexico Mining Museum has evolved into one of Grants’ most treasured cultural attractions. Here, visitors can delve deep into the history of Grants’ mining industry, exploring the intricate processes behind the extraction of uranium, coal, and other valuable minerals from the earth.

El Malpais National Monument

A testament to both the natural beauty and the cultural significance of the Grants region, the El Malpais National Monument is a must-visit for any traveler to the area. This vast expanse of public land is home to miles of hiking and biking trails, ancient lava tubes, and striking sandstone formations, as well as cave art and other artifacts bearing witness to the presence and influence of the native Pueblo peoples.

Natural Attractions of Grants: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mt. Taylor

A sacred site for the region’s native Navajo peoples, Mt. Taylor is a stunning peak that towers above the surrounding desert and is visible for miles around. Visitors can take part in a guided hike up the mountain or simply admire its striking contours from afar.

Bluewater Lake State Park

Just a short drive from Grants, Bluewater Lake State Park is a great place to relax and soak up the natural beauty of the Southwest. The park offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and swimming, as well as several hiking trails and picnic areas. Be sure to stick around for the sunset – the skies over Bluewater Lake are renowned for their vivid crimson and gold hues.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grants

Ice Cave and Bandera Volcano trail

This scenic hiking and biking trail take visitors through a region of New Mexico replete with natural wonders, including the Bandera Volcano, the Ice Cave, and the Continental Divide Trail.

El Malpais National Monument

The El Malpais National Monument offers myriad hiking and biking trails through its vast expanses of beautiful wilderness – among them the Continental Divide Trail, which offers stunning views of snow-capped peaks in the distance.

Best Things to Do in Grants: Activities and Tours

Route 66 Casino Hotel

Located just outside of Grants proper, the Route 66 Casino Hotel is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a getaway with a touch of luxury. The hotel features a full-service spa, a 24-hour fitness center, and an on-site casino, as well as a range of dining options.

Sandstone Bluffs Overlook

For a unique perspective on Grants’ stunning natural beauty, be sure to pay a visit to the Sandstone Bluffs Overlook. This lookout spot provides panoramic views of the El Malpais lava flows, the Zuni Mountains, and the surrounding high desert.

Food and Drink Scene of Grants

Must-Try Dishes

The Owl Cafe

The Owl Cafe is a Grants institution, serving up classic American diner fare with a southwestern twist. Be sure to try their famous green chile cheeseburgers – they’re a local favorite.

Mi Casita Cafe

Mi Casita Cafe is a hidden gem in the heart of Grants, serving up authentic Mexican cuisine with a home-cooked feel. Their tortas and tacos are particularly popular with locals.

Local Beverages

La Ventana Vineyards

New Mexico’s arid climate has long been a draw for winemakers, and La Ventana Vineyards is one of the region’s top producers. Visitors to Grants can take a tour of the winery and sample some of their award-winning wines.

Mining District Brewery

For craft beer lovers, the Mining District Brewery is a must-visit. This Grants microbrewery specializes in small-batch artisanal beers and often features live music and other special events.

Where to Stay in Grants: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Grants

This modern hotel in Grants offers travelers a comfortable and convenient home base from which to explore the area. The hotel features a fitness center, indoor pool and hot tub, as well as complimentary breakfast and WiFi.

Days Inn by Wyndham Grants

This budget-friendly option in the heart of Grants offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to the region’s top attractions, including the New Mexico Mining Museum and the El Malpais National Monument.

What to Buy in Grants: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Laguna Pueblo Pottery

The Laguna Pueblo people have been producing exquisite ceramic works for centuries, and visitors to Grants can take home a piece of this cultural legacy in the form of hand-painted pottery, figurines, and other ceramic pieces.

Uranium Glassware

Mining has been a part of the Grants region’s identity for over a century, and visitors can take home a piece of this history with uranium glassware. This distinctive type of glass glows bright green in the dark thanks to its uranium content – a unique and eye-catching souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Grants

Grants is a unique blend of cultures and traditions, with a rich history that is deeply intertwined with the region’s natural beauty. Visitors should respect the native Pueblo peoples and their cultural heritage, and observe local customs and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Grants

The best time to visit Grants is during the cooler and drier months of September through November, and March through May. The summer months can be hot and crowded, with temperatures reaching well above 100 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grants

Do:

  • Respect local culture and traditions
  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreation opportunities
  • Bring sunscreen and plenty of water
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property
  • Litter or damage natural areas
  • Take anything from archaeological or historical sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Grants

Grants is generally a safe destination, but travelers should exercise caution when hiking or exploring in remote areas.

Health in Grants

Travelers to Grants should take precautions against the intense Southwestern sun, including wearing protective clothing and using sunscreen.

Getting to Grants: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grants

The Albuquerque International Sunport is the nearest major airport, located roughly 75 miles southeast of Grants.

Bus Stations Near Grants

The Greyhound bus station in Gallup is the closest option for travelers arriving by public transportation.

Other Options

Many visitors to Grants choose to rent a car or drive their personal vehicle, as the area is best explored with access to private transportation.

How to Get Around in Grants

Travelers visiting Grants will find that they need a car to access many of the area’s top attractions and outdoor recreation opportunities. However, the city itself is small enough to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Grants: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chaco Culture National Historical Park

Just a few hours’ drive from Grants, Chaco Culture National Historical Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to an incredible collection of ancient astronomical observatories and architectural feats of the Ancestral Puebloan peoples.

Acoma Pueblo

Acoma Pueblo is a sovereign Native American community situated atop a mesa that looms over the surrounding desert. Visitors can take a guided tour of the pueblo, learn about its rich history and culture, and purchase unique local crafts and souvenirs.

Conclusion

Grants, New Mexico, offers travelers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor recreation opportunities that make it an ideal destination for adventurers of all types. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to explore all that this charming Southwestern city has to offer.
