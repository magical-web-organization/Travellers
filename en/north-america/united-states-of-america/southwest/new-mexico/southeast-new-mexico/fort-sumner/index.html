Introduction to Fort Sumner

Fort Sumner is a small town located in Southeast New Mexico, in the United States. The town was named after Fort Sumter in Charleston, South Carolina, where the first shots of the American Civil War were fired. Fort Sumner has a rich history and is home to several museums and historical sites, including the Bosque Redondo Memorial and the Fort Sumner Historic Site.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Sumner

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bosque Redondo Memorial

The Bosque Redondo Memorial is a must-visit for history buffs. The site is dedicated to the Navajo and Mescalero Apache people who were forced to make the 300-mile journey on foot to the Bosque Redondo Reservation in 1864. Visitors can explore the exhibits and learn about the lives of the people who were once held captive at the reservation.

Fort Sumner Historic Site

The Fort Sumner Historic Site is home to the remains of the military fort where Billy the Kid was incarcerated and eventually killed. Visitors can explore the grounds, view artifacts from the fort’s history, and learn about the life of this notorious outlaw.

Natural Attractions of Fort Sumner: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sumner Lake State Park

Sumner Lake State Park is a great spot for outdoor enthusiasts. The lake offers fishing opportunities, boating, and kayaking. Visitors can also hike, mountain bike, or enjoy a picnic in the park’s shaded areas.

Fort Sumner Lake

Fort Sumner Lake is a beautiful spot for bird watching and scenic walks. The area is home to a variety of bird species, including pelicans, eagles, and ducks. Fishing opportunities are also available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Sumner

Pecos River Nature Trail

The Pecos River Nature Trail is a scenic route ideal for hiking or mountain biking. The trail follows the Pecos River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Hikers can also spot a variety of bird species and wildlife along the way.

Fort Sumner High School Nature Trail

The Fort Sumner High School Nature Trail is a popular hiking spot among locals. The trail is relatively easy and offers scenic views of the nearby countryside. Visitors can spot a variety of bird species, including quails and hawks.

Best Things to Do in Fort Sumner: Activities and Tours

Visit the Billy the Kid Museum

Explore the life of one of America’s most infamous outlaws at the Billy the Kid Museum. The museum features exhibits showcasing Billy’s life, including memorabilia, firearms, and authentic artifacts.

Attend the Fort Sumner Annual Rodeo

Experience the excitement of a traditional rodeo at the Fort Sumner annual rodeo. The event takes place over four days and features bull riding, calf roping, barrel racing, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Sumner

Must-Try Dishes

Try the local specialty: green chile stew. Made with the region’s famous Hatch chiles, the hearty stew is a perfect meal on a chilly day. Also, don’t miss the New Mexico-style enchiladas, which are smothered in red or green chile sauce.

Local Beverages

New Mexico is famous for its local micro-breweries. Sample a few of Fort Sumner’s local beers, which are brewed with local ingredients and natural spring water.

What to Buy in Fort Sumner: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Handicrafts

Shop for local handicrafts, including Navajo pottery and hand-woven rugs. Also, check out the local boutiques and shops for unique souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Sumner

The people of Fort Sumner are friendly and welcoming. They take pride in their rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors should respect local customs, including the tradition of removing one’s hat when entering a building or meeting someone for the first time.

Best Time to Visit Fort Sumner

The best time to visit Fort Sumner is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s attractions during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Sumner

Fort Sumner is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, including locking their cars and securing valuables. Also, be aware of wildlife in outdoor areas.

Health in Fort Sumner

Fort Sumner has a small hospital and several clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance and should seek medical help in case of emergency.

Getting to Fort Sumner: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Sumner

The nearest major airport is Albuquerque International Sunport, located about 190 miles from Fort Sumner. Other nearby airports include Roswell International Air Center and Clovis Municipal Airport.

Bus Stations Near Fort Sumner

There is no bus station in Fort Sumner. The nearest Greyhound bus station is in Roswell, about 90 miles away.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to travel to Fort Sumner. Visitors can also book a taxi or private shuttle service from any of the nearby airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Fort Sumner

The best way to get around Fort Sumner is by car. There are no public transportation options in the town.

Where to Go Next from Fort Sumner: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Salinas Pueblo Missions National Monument

Salinas Pueblo Missions National Monument is a historic park located about 80 miles from Fort Sumner. The park features the ruins of three Native American missions and a museum.

Roswell

Roswell is a popular tourist destination famous for its alleged UFO sightings and alien-themed attractions. The town is located about 90 miles from Fort Sumner.

Conclusion

Fort Sumner is a small town with a rich history and unique cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the town’s museums, historical sites, and natural attractions, enjoy outdoor adventures, and experience the local cuisine and culture. Whether for a weekend getaway or a longer stay, Fort Sumner offers an enjoyable and memorable vacation experience.
