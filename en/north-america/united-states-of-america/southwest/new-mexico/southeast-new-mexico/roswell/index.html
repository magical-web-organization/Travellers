Introduction to Roswell

Roswell, New Mexico, located in the southeast part of the state, is best known for its connection to the alleged UFO crash in 1947, making it a popular destination for ufologists and sci-fi fans. However, there is much more to discover in Roswell beyond the extraterrestrial fascination. The city’s vibrant cultural scene, natural beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and delicious food and drink scene make it a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Roswell

Here is a comprehensive list of places to visit in Roswell.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • International UFO Museum and Research Center: Dedicated to the famous 1947 Roswell UFO incident, the museum traces the history and evolution of the UFO phenomenon through interactive exhibits, documentaries, and authentic artifacts.
  • Anderson Museum of Contemporary Art: Located in a beautifully restored 1927 adobe home, the museum features a diverse collection of contemporary artworks, from paintings and sculptures to photography and installations.
  • Historic District: Roswell’s downtown historic district is a showcase of the city’s rich heritage, with numerous historical buildings, shops, and restaurants.
  • Chaves County Courthouse: This 1912 Spanish Colonial Revival-style courthouse is a stunning architectural attraction, with its ornate façade and grand staircase.

Natural Attractions of Roswell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bottomless Lakes State Park: Just outside Roswell, this state park is a geological wonder, with its sinkholes filled with blue-green water surrounded by rocky cliffs. Visitors can swim, fish, hike, and camp in the park.
  • Bitter Lake National Wildlife Refuge: A sanctuary for migratory birds and endangered species, this wildlife refuge boasts diverse ecosystems, from desert shrubs to salt flats, and offers birdwatching, hiking, and photo opportunities.
  • Roswell Riverwalk Trail: A scenic 3.3-mile paved trail that follows the Pecos River, offering stunning views of the river, bridges, and surrounding landscapes. Ideal for jogging, biking, or walking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roswell

Roswell offers numerous hiking and biking opportunities, from easy urban trails to challenging wilderness routes. Here are some of the best:

  • Spring River Trail: A popular local trail that follows the Spring River through parks, woods, and urban neighborhoods.
  • Robert H. Goddard Nature Trail: A tribute to the father of modern rocketry, this trail winds through a natural area that highlights the region’s flora and fauna, with interpretive signs marking key points of interest.
  • Lincoln National Forest: For more challenging hikes and bike rides, head to this stunning forest, located about an hour’s drive from Roswell, which offers miles of trails through mountain ranges, valleys, and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Roswell: Activities and Tours

Beyond museums and nature, there are plenty of unique activities and tours to experience in Roswell. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Alien-themed Tour: Discover Roswell’s famous UFO history on a guided tour that takes you to the key sites related to the 1947 incident, including the crash site, the military base, and other relevant locations.
  • Cowboy Experience: Get a taste of the Wild West with a cowboy experience in the nearby ranches, where you can ride horses, watch cattle drives, learn how to lasso and shoot, and enjoy a delicious barbecue.
  • Roswell Symphony Orchestra: Don’t miss the opportunity to see a live performance by the Roswell Symphony Orchestra, which features a repertoire of classical and contemporary music.

Food and Drink Scene of Roswell

Roswell’s food and drink scene is a blend of traditional recipes and modern fusions, reflecting the city’s multicultural heritage and contemporary vibe. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile Cheeseburger: This New Mexican staple is a mouthwatering burger topped with green chiles, cheese, and other fixings, served with fries or chips.
  • Calabacitas: A vegetarian dish made with squash, corn, and other veggies, seasoned with traditional spices such as cumin and oregano.
  • Sopapilla: A sweet, fried pastry typically served with honey, perfect for dessert or breakfast.

Local Beverages

  • New Mexico Wine: The region produces excellent wines, especially red varietals such as Cabernet Sauvignon and Syrah.
  • Hatch Chile Beer: A unique beer made with Hatch chiles, a spicy local pepper, that adds a distinctive kick to the brew.
  • Pinyon Coffee: A rich, flavorful coffee made with roasted pinyon nuts, a local delicacy.

Where to Stay in Roswell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Roswell offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to stylish hotels and cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Roswell: A modern, upscale hotel with spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, a fitness center, and meeting facilities.
  • Roswell Inn: A budget-friendly hotel with clean rooms, free Wi-Fi, a pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Wayfarers Inn: A charming guesthouse with a vintage decor and personalized service, located in the heart of the historic district.

What to Buy in Roswell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Roswell is home to several unique stores where you can find souvenirs, gifts, and local products. Here are some ideas:

  • Alien Zone: A quirky store that sells alien-themed merchandise, from T-shirts and hats to collectibles and crystals.
  • Enchanted Lands: A gift shop that features handmade jewelry, pottery, and other artisanal items made by local artists.
  • Roswell Farmers and Growers Market: A market that offers fresh produce, honey, jams, and other locally sourced goods, every Saturday during the summer months.

Local Culture and Customs of Roswell

Roswell’s cultural heritage is a blend of Hispanic, Native American, and Anglo-American influences, making it a rich tapestry of traditions, customs, and celebrations. Respect for the environment and the city’s history is also an essential aspect of local culture, with various events and activities geared towards promoting sustainability and preservation.

Best Time to Visit Roswell

The best time to visit Roswell is between March and October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing. Summer months can be hot, but they also offer an opportunity to explore the state’s scenic highlights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roswell

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Roswell:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions;
  • Take advantage of the city’s cultural and natural attractions;
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages;
  • Be prepared for the high-altitude, dry climate, and bring sunscreen, water, and comfortable shoes.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the alleged UFO crash site;
  • Litter or damage the natural habitats;
  • Ignore warning signs and safety precautions;
  • Leave valuable items unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roswell

Roswell is a safe city, but visitors should take precautions, such as locking their cars and hotel rooms and avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night. Hiking and outdoor activities require a degree of caution, as the desert environment can be challenging and unpredictable. Stay on designated trails, carry plenty of water, and inform someone of your itinerary.

Health in Roswell

Roswell is a high-altitude city with a dry climate, which can cause dehydration and altitude sickness, especially for those who are not used to the environment. Drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and take it easy on your first days in the city. If you have any health concerns, consult a doctor before your trip.

Getting to Roswell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roswell

The closest airport to Roswell is the Roswell Regional Air Center, which operates flights to and from Dallas and Phoenix.

Bus Stations Near Roswell

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Roswell, which connects it to other cities in Arizona and New Mexico.

Other Options

Renting a car is perhaps the most convenient option for getting around Roswell and the surrounding areas. Taxi services and ride-hailing apps are also available in the city.

How to Get Around in Roswell

Driving is the most efficient way to get around Roswell, but the city also has a decent public transportation system, with buses operated by Pecos Trails Transit.

Where to Go Next from Roswell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roswell is an ideal starting point for exploring other destinations in the region:

  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park: A spectacular natural wonder, located about two hours’ drive from Roswell, featuring enormous underground caverns with stalactites and stalagmites.
  • White Sands National Park: An otherworldly landscape of pure white dunes, located about three hours’ drive from Roswell, that offer endless photo opportunities and recreational activities, such as sandboarding and picnicking.

Conclusion

Roswell, New Mexico, is a hidden gem in the Southwest, offering visitors a plethora of cultural, natural, and gastronomical attractions, unique experiences, and friendly hospitality. Whether you are interested in UFOs, art, hiking, or simply soaking up the local vibe, Roswell has something for everyone.
