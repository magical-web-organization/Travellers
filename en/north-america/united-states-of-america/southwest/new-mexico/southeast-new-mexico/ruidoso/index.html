Introduction to Ruidoso

Nestled in the heart of the scenic Lincoln National Forest, Ruidoso is a charming mountain village that attracts visitors throughout the year. Located in Southeast New Mexico, Ruidoso offers a delightful blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and vibrant nightlife. From hiking and biking to skiing and gambling, Ruidoso has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ruidoso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ruidoso is a town steeped in rich history, which is beautifully reflected in its museums and historical landmarks. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Ruidoso are:

  • Hubbard Museum of the American West: This museum showcases the fascinating history of the American West through its exceptional collection of art, artifacts, and memorabilia.

  • Smokey Bear Historical Park: This interesting museum is dedicated to one of the most iconic symbols of America’s national forests - Smokey Bear. Visitors can learn about the history and legacy of Smokey Bear and even take a selfie with the beloved bear.

Natural Attractions of Ruidoso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ruidoso is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with its stunning natural landscapes and exciting adventure activities. Some of the top natural attractions in Ruidoso are:

  • Ski Apache: Located on the majestic slopes of Sierra Blanca, Ski Apache is one of the best ski resorts in New Mexico, offering 55 runs, two terrain parks, and breathtaking views of the White Mountains.

  • Lincoln National Forest: This vast wilderness area covers over one million acres and offers endless possibilities for hiking, camping, fishing, hunting, and wildlife watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ruidoso

Ruidoso offers some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in the Southwest. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The River Trail: This easy trail follows the path of the Rio Ruidoso and passes through lush meadows, quaint towns, and historic landmarks.

  • Grindstone Lake Trail: This moderate 4.5-mile loop trail offers stunning views of Grindstone Lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ruidoso: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Ruidoso has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are some of the best experiences to have in Ruidoso:

  • Zip-lining: Experience the thrill of zip-lining through the scenic canyons and forests around Ruidoso.

  • Horseback riding: Explore the beauty of Lincoln National Forest on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ruidoso

Day 1: Visit Hubbard Museum of the American West and go hiking at Lincoln National Forest.
Day 2: Enjoy skiing at Ski Apache.
Day 3: Take a zip-lining tour of Ruidoso.
Day 4: Go horseback riding in Lincoln National Forest.
Day 5: Visit Smokey Bear Historical Park and enjoy a scenic drive around Ruidoso.
Day 6: Try your luck at one of the many casinos in Ruidoso.
Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate at one of the town’s many spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Ruidoso

Must-Try Dishes

Ruidoso’s thriving food scene offers a diverse range of cuisines and flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Ruidoso:

  • Green Chile Cheeseburger: Savor the flavors of New Mexico with this delicious burger that features green chile and melted cheese.

  • Smoked Brisket: Enjoy mouthwatering brisket that has been slow-cooked and smoked to perfection.

Local Beverages

No visit to Ruidoso is complete without trying some of the town’s local beverages. Here are some of the best drinks to sample in Ruidoso:

  • Wine: Ruidoso has some great wineries that produce delicious wines using locally grown grapes.

  • Craft beer: Ruidoso boasts several breweries that offer a wide variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Ruidoso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ruidoso has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Ruidoso are:

  • Inn of the Mountain Gods: This luxurious resort offers breathtaking views of Sierra Blanca and features a casino, a golf course, and an outdoor amphitheater.

  • Ruidoso River Resort & Inn: This cozy inn is located on the banks of the Rio Ruidoso and offers comfortable rooms, a hot tub, and a picnic area.

What to Buy in Ruidoso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ruidoso is known for its unique and locally made souvenirs and products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Ruidoso:

  • Native American Art: Ruidoso has several art galleries that specialize in Native American art, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

  • Local wine: Ruidoso’s wineries produce some of the best wines in New Mexico.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruidoso

Ruidoso is a friendly and welcoming town that values its cultural heritage and traditions. Here are some of the things to keep in mind while visiting Ruidoso:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially those related to the Native American culture.

  • Be mindful of your surroundings and avoid littering or damaging the natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Ruidoso

The best time to visit Ruidoso is between May and October, when the weather is pleasant and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruidoso

Do:

  • Enjoy the beauty and serenity of Lincoln National Forest.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.

  • Damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ruidoso

Ruidoso is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips to stay safe in Ruidoso:

  • Avoid hiking or camping alone.

  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.

Health in Ruidoso

Ruidoso’s high altitude can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. It is advisable to drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol and caffeine, and take it easy for the first few days of your visit.

Getting to Ruidoso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ruidoso

The nearest airport to Ruidoso is Roswell International Air Center, which is approximately 70 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ruidoso

Greyhound Lines operates a bus service to Ruidoso from several cities in New Mexico.

Other Options

Ruidoso is easily accessible by car, with highways connecting it to major cities in New Mexico and Texas.

How to Get Around in Ruidoso

The best way to get around Ruidoso is by car, as public transportation is not widely available. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Ruidoso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ruidoso is a great base for exploring other parts of New Mexico and Texas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park: This stunning national park is known for its incredible underground caves and formations.

  • White Sands National Monument: This ethereal landscape of white gypsum sand dunes is a must-see attraction in New Mexico.

Conclusion

Ruidoso is a hidden gem in New Mexico that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and vibrant nightlife. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation, Ruidoso has something for you. So, pack your bags and come explore the wonders of Ruidoso!
