Introduction to Lincoln National Forest

Lincoln National Forest is one of the most beautiful and diverse forests in New Mexico. Covering more than 1.1 million acres of land, the forest is home to scenic views, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local cuisine.

Located in Southeast New Mexico, Lincoln National Forest has a rich history dating back to the Apache tribes. The forest’s diverse landscape of pine forests, canyons, streams, and grasslands, make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

With this comprehensive guide, you’ll learn about the must-visit places, hiking/biking trails, local cuisine, accommodations, and nearby destinations. So, let’s explore the beauty of Lincoln National Forest in Southeast New Mexico.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln National Forest

Lincoln National Forest has a rich cultural heritage. The Mescalero Apache tribe, who have lived here for centuries, have a significant influence on the culture and customs of the region. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln National Forest

The best time to visit Lincoln National Forest is from May to September. The weather is pleasant, and the forest is open for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln National Forest

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife
  • Pack out what you pack in
  • Observe fire bans
  • Carry a map and a first-aid kit

Don’t:

  • Litter
  • Start campfires outside designated areas
  • Disturb wildlife
  • Leave your trash behind

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln National Forest

  • Stay on designated trails
  • Be aware of altitude sickness
  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions
  • Carry bear spray

Health in Lincoln National Forest
