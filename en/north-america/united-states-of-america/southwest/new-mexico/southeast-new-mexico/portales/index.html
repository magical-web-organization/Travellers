Introduction to Portales

Nestled in Southeast New Mexico, Portales is a vibrant city known for its rich history, diverse culture, and scenic beauty. With a population of more than 12,000 and a thriving business community, Portales is a popular destination for visitors seeking a unique and memorable travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Portales

Portales is home to a variety of must-see attractions, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Blackwater Draw Museum: Discover the history of the High Plains region, including the archaeological site of the Clovis culture.
  • Roosevelt County Courthouse: A historic landmark built in 1936, the courthouse is an excellent example of Art Deco architecture.
  • Eastern New Mexico University (ENMU): Take a campus tour of ENMU, founded in 1927 and home to over 6,000 students.

Natural Attractions of Portales: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • City Park: Located in downtown Portales, City Park is a perfect spot for picnics, strolls, or just taking in the views.
  • Blackwater Draw: Explore this fossil-filled silica sand canyon, formed over millions of years.
  • Roosevelt Lake: Go fishing, boating, or just soak up the stunning natural beauty of this pristine lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portales

Portales has several trails and routes perfect for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best spots:

  • Eastern New Mexico University Trails: Take a stroll or bike ride around the ENMU trail system, covering over two miles of beautiful views.
  • Roosevelt Lake Trail: Enjoy a scenic hike along the Roosevelt Lake Trail, a moderate-level trail along the banks of the lake.
  • Dora Canal Trail: This 1.5-mile trail winds through the beautiful countryside surrounding the tiny town of Dora, just a few miles outside Portales.

Best Things to Do in Portales: Activities and Tours

Portales offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best:

  • Portales Farmers Market: Shop for fresh local produce, handmade crafts, and more at this weekly farmers market in downtown Portales.
  • Rockin’ Ys’ Roadhouse Museum: Take a trip back in time to the Route 66 era at this unique museum, filled with memorabilia from the heyday of American road travel.
  • High Plains Film Festival: Catch a movie or two at this annual film festival, featuring independent cinema from all over the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Portales

Portales has a variety of dining options to please any palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chili Cheeseburgers: This spicy take on the classic cheeseburger is a New Mexico specialty.
  • Navajo Tacos: A variation on the traditional taco, these are served on fry bread for a unique and tasty twist.
  • Enchiladas: Whether smothered in red or green chili sauce, enchiladas are a New Mexican staple.

Local Beverages

  • Piñon Coffee: Made with locally sourced piñon nuts, this coffee is a delicious and distinctly New Mexican experience
  • Agua Fresca: A refreshing drink made with fresh fruit, water, and sugar.
  • New Mexico Wine: Try a bottle of wine from one of the many local wineries, known for their high-quality grapes and unique flavor profiles.

Where to Stay in Portales: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Portales offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from hotels and motels to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the best places to stay during your visit:

  • Best Western Portales Inn: A comfortable and affordable option located close to many of the city’s top attractions.
  • Roosevelt Inn and Suites: A boutique-style hotel featuring modern amenities and a convenient location.
  • The Historic Kiva Lodge: A charming bed and breakfast located in the quiet countryside just outside of Portales.

What to Buy in Portales: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Portales, be sure to pick up some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home with you. Some great items to look for include:

  • Local art and pottery: Visit one of the many art galleries or craft markets to find one-of-a-kind pieces from local artists.
  • Piñon nuts: These small, flavorful nuts are a New Mexico specialty, perfect for snacking or baking.
  • Handmade jewelry: From turquoise to silver, you can find stunning jewelry crafted by local artisans in Portales.

Local Culture and Customs of Portales

Portales has a unique culture influenced by its rich history and diverse population. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect the local religious traditions: Portales has a large population of devout Christians, so make sure to be respectful of local customs and practices.
  • Be polite and courteous: The people of Portales are friendly and welcoming, so be sure to reciprocate that courtesy during your visit.
  • Dress appropriately: While Portales is fairly casual, dressing modestly and respectfully is always appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Portales

The best time to visit Portales is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and dry, while winters can be chilly and windy.

Do’s and Don’tsof Portales

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Portales:

Do:

  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the region: From the stunning mesas to the wide-open spaces, don’t miss out on the incredible scenery that Portales has to offer.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions: Whether it’s observing quiet hours or dressing appropriately, always be respectful of the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Be reckless or disrespectful outdoors: Leave no trace, and avoid damaging the natural beauty of the area.
  • Be late or keep people waiting: Punctuality is important in Portales, so be sure to arrive on time for appointments and events.

Safety and Health

Portales is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe and healthy during their trip.

Safety in Portales

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any other city, Portales has its share of petty crime and other safety concerns. Always be aware of your surroundings, and take common-sense safety precautions.

Health in Portales

  • Stay hydrated: The climate in Portales is dry and arid, so it’s important to drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Be sure to wear sunscreen and a hat to protect your skin from the strong New Mexico sun.

Getting to Portales: Transportation Options and Logistics

Portales is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Portales

  • Clovis Municipal Airport: Located 20 miles from Portales, Clovis Municipal Airport offers daily flights to and from major cities in Texas and New Mexico.

Bus Stations Near Portales

  • Greyhound Bus Line: Located in Clovis, just 20 miles from Portales.

Other Options

  • Renting a car: Several rental car agencies are available in Clovis, allowing visitors to explore the area at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Portales

Portales is fairly small and easy to navigate by car, bike, or foot. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Portales: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve explored everything Portales has to offer, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out, including:

  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park: A stunning underground wonderland located just a few hours’ drive from Portales.
  • White Sands National Monument: Explore the otherworldly landscapes of this unique national park, just a few hours’ drive to the west of Portales.
  • Roswell: Just a short drive from Portales, Roswell is known for its UFO sightings and quirky alien-themed attractions.

Conclusion

From natural wonders to cultural attractions, Portales has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide provides everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming New Mexico city.
