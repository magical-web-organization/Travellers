If you’re looking for a destination off the beaten track with a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty, look no further than Clovis, New Mexico. Tucked away in Southeast New Mexico, Clovis boasts a wide range of attractions for any traveler. From the rich cultural history of Native American and cowboy traditions to the stunning natural landscapes surrounding the city, there is something for everyone in Clovis. Get ready to pack your bags and explore this hidden gem with our comprehensive tour guide.

Introduction to Clovis

Clovis is located in the southeastern part of New Mexico, United States. Founded in 1907, the city is rich in history and home to a diverse array of cultures and traditions. Clovis is the county seat of Curry County and has a population of just over 38,000 residents.

List of Places to Visit in Clovis

The following section lists the top destinations to visit in Clovis. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor activities, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clovis Depot Model Train Museum – This one-of-a-kind museum features a collection of model trains and railroad memorabilia. Visitors can learn about the history of the railroad in Clovis and how it impacted the city’s growth and development.
  • Norman and Vi Petty Rock and Roll Museum – This museum celebrates the life and legacy of Norman and Vi Petty, two of the most influential figures in rock and roll history. The museum houses a collection of rare artifacts and memorabilia from the early days of rock and roll.
  • Blackwater Draw Museum – This museum showcases the history of the area and the importance of the Blackwater Draw archaeological site. Visitors can see exhibits detailing the Clovis culture and the significance of the site for understanding early human history.
  • Lighthouse Mission and Museum – This museum and mission provides an inside look into homelessness and poverty and highlights the contributions made by the Lighthouse Mission in helping those in need within the community.

Natural Attractions of Clovis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Owl Creek Reservoir – This beautiful reservoir is a popular destination for fishing, hiking, and camping.
  • Grady Crater – This natural site is a unique geological feature and is considered a “must-see” for anyone in the area.
  • Muleshoe National Wildlife Refuge – This wildlife refuge is home to a wide variety of bird species, including endangered and threatened species. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the plants and animals of the refuge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clovis

  • North Plains Mall Trail – This trail offers a scenic and relaxing route for biking or walking around the mall area.
  • Riverwalk Trail – This trail follows the course of the Whitewater Creek and is a great option for those looking for a scenic route through the heart of the city.
  • Muleshoe Wildlife Refuge Trails – These trails are perfect for hikers and nature lovers, offering a chance to see wildlife up close and explore the natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Clovis: Activities and Tours

  • Clovis Music Festival – This annual festival celebrates the rich musical history of the area with live performances, dancing, and musical events.
  • Clovis Rodeo – This rodeo is a must-see for anyone interested in cowboy culture and tradition. Featuring bull riding, team roping, and other rodeo events, this is a highlight of the Clovis calendar.
  • North Plains Cinema 4 – This cinema offers a unique movie-going experience with multiple screens and a family-friendly atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clovis

Day 1: Visit Clovis Depot Model Train Museum and explore the Old Town District.
Day 2: Take a hike on the Riverwalk Trail in the morning and spend the afternoon at the Norman and Vi Petty Rock and Roll Museum.
Day 3: Explore the Blackwater Draw Museum and take a guided tour of the archaeological site.
Day 4: Explore the natural beauty of the Muleshoe National Wildlife Refuge and take a guided bird-watching tour.
Day 5: Go fishing and camping at Owl Creek Reservoir.
Day 6: Attend the Clovis Rodeo and experience cowboy culture and tradition.
Day 7: Spend the day at the North Plains Mall and catch a movie at the North Plains Cinema 4.

Food and Drink Scene of Clovis

Clovis has a diverse food and drink scene, with a range of options available for any craving. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile Cheeseburger – A New Mexico classic, this burger is topped with hot and spicy green chiles and gooey cheese.
  • Frito Pie – A unique dish that mixes traditional chili with crunchy Fritos, this dish is a local favorite.
  • Flan – A sweet and creamy dessert popular in Latino culture, this is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Mexican Beer – A range of Mexican beers are available throughout Clovis, with Corona and Dos Equis being popular options.
  • Red and Green Chile Margaritas – A twist on the classic margarita, these drinks are made with spicy chiles for a unique and unforgettable experience.

What to Buy in Clovis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cowboy Boots – A staple of Western culture, cowboy boots are available at several stores throughout Clovis.
  • Native American Art – Don’t miss the opportunity to buy unique Native American art, including jewelry, pottery, and textiles.
  • Green Chile – A staple of New Mexican cuisine, green chiles can be purchased fresh or canned and make a great souvenir or gift to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Clovis

Clovis has a rich cultural history shaped by a mix of Native American, cowboy, and Latino traditions. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, as well as the religious practices that are an integral part of the Clovis community.

Best Time to Visit Clovis

The best time to visit Clovis is during the spring and fall when temperatures are mild, and the weather is pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clovis

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the green chile dishes; they are a local specialty.
  • Visit the local museums to learn about the history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Ignore traffic laws; law enforcement is strict in the area.
  • Disrespect religious practices or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clovis

Overall, Clovis is a safe and welcoming community. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and use common sense when traveling around the area.

Health in Clovis

Clovis has several medical facilities available to visitors in case of emergency. Check with your insurance provider to make sure you are covered in the area.

Getting to Clovis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clovis is accessible by several means of transport, including air, bus, and car.

Airports Near Clovis

  • Clovis Municipal Airport (CVS) – This airport provides daily service to Denver, CO, and is located approximately 11 miles from downtown Clovis.

Bus Stations Near Clovis

  • Greyhound Bus Station – This bus station is located downtown and provides daily service to several destinations.

Other Options

Car rentals are available at the Clovis Municipal Airport and throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Clovis

Clovis is a small city, and most attractions are easily reachable on foot or by bike. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Clovis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Roswell – This nearby city is famous for UFO sightings and is home to several museums and attractions related to extraterrestrial phenomena.
  • White Sands National Monument – Located approximately two hours from Clovis, this natural wonder features stunning white sand dunes and opportunities for hiking and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Clovis, New Mexico, is a hidden gem with a rich history, beautiful natural landscapes, and a diverse range of cultures and traditions. With our comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip with ease and make the most out of your time in this unique and unforgettable destination.
