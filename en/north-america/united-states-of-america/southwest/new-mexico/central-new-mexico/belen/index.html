Introduction to Belen

Belen, a small town located in Central New Mexico, is often known as the “Hub City” as it sits at the intersection of two major interstate highways (I-25 and I-40). The town is home to about 7,000 residents and offers plenty of natural and cultural attractions that draw visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Belen

Whether you’re interested in exploring the cultural and natural attractions or seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views, Belen has something for everyone. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Belen:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Belen Harvey House Museum: Located in the historic building of the Harvey House, the museum showcases the history of the town’s involvement in the establishment of the Harvey House chain of restaurants.

  • Tierra Del Sol Country Club and Golf Course: This must-visit destination is a historic place that hosts a golf course and country club facilities that offer visitors a glimpse of the past through its decorations and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Belen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belen Marsh Wildlife Rehabilitation Area: This wetland, home to various species of birds and wildlife, is perfect for bird watching enthusiasts and nature lovers. Enjoy a scenic walk along the trails while listening to the soothing sounds of the nature.

  • Leasburg Dam State Park: Head over to the adjacent town of Radium Springs and visit this state park, which is known for its beautiful views of the Leasburg Dam and for offering activities such as fishing, boating, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belen

Belen is surrounded by various outdoor hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Belen River Trail
  • Tierra Del Sol Country Club and Golf Course Trail
  • Rio Grande Trail

Best Things to Do in Belen: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour through the historic Santa Fe Trail
  • Explore the various art galleries and studios located throughout the town
  • Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the stunning views from above
  • Visit the famous Route 66 and its antique shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belen

Day 1: Visit the Belen Harvey House Museum and learn about the town’s involvement in the establishment of the Harvey House chain of restaurants.

Day 2: Go on a hiking trip along Belen River Trail and explore the natural beauty of the area.

Day 3: Visit the famous Route 66 and enjoy the antique shops, restaurants, and cafes it has to offer.

Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy the stunning views from above.

Day 5: Go on a day trip to the Leasburg Dam State Park and enjoy activities such as fishing, boating, and hiking

Day 6: Visit the Tierra Del Sol Country Club and Golf Course and explore the historic country club facilities.

Day 7: Explore the various art galleries and studios located throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Belen

Belen has a rich cultural heritage and a strong arts community. The town hosts many festivals and events throughout the year, and visitors can experience the local culture and customs through these festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Belen

The best time to visit Belen is during the fall season (September to November) when the weather is milder and the fall foliage is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belen

Do:

  • Learn about the history and culture of the town
  • Respect the natural environment
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural environment
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Engage in illegal or harmful activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Belen

Belen is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take general precautions such as locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Belen

The nearest hospital is the Belen Regional Medical Center, located in the town.

Getting to Belen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belen

The Albuquerque International Sunport is the nearest major airport to Belen and is located approximately 30 miles north of the town.

Bus Stations Near Belen

The closest Greyhound bus station to Belen is the one located in Albuquerque, approximately 30 miles north of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider renting a car or taking a taxi from Albuquerque to Belen.

How to Get Around in Belen

Belen is a relatively small town, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot or by bike. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Belen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Albuquerque: The largest city in New Mexico, offering plenty of cultural and natural attractions, shopping and dining options, and opportunities for outdoor adventures.

  • Santa Fe: A popular tourist destination known for its rich cultural heritage, art galleries and museums, and scenic beauty of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains.

  • Sandia Peak Tramway: Located in Albuquerque, this is the world’s longest aerial tram ride, offering stunning views of the Sandia Mountains.

Conclusion

With its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine, Belen is a must-visit destination for all travelers. Plan your next trip to Belen and explore the many attractions and adventures that this charming town has to offer.
