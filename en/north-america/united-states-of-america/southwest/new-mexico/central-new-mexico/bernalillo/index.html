Introduction to Bernalillo

Located in Central New Mexico, Bernalillo is an enchanting town with a rich cultural heritage and scenic outdoors. Founded in 1695, Bernalillo is home to various museums, historic sites, outdoor adventures, and vibrant local culture. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, an adventurer, or a foodie, Bernalillo will surely leave an indelible mark on your heart. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your trip to Bernalillo with detailed information on the town’s top attractions, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, transportation options, nearby destinations, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Bernalillo

Bernalillo offers a plethora of attractions that cater to a wide range of interests. Here is a rundown of some of the must-visit places in Bernalillo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coronado Historic Site: This historic site is dedicated to the exploration and conquest of the Southwest by Francisco Vasquez de Coronado and his expedition in 1540. The site includes a museum, a visitor center, and the remains of a 500-year-old native Puebloan village.
  • Jemez State Monument: Located just outside of Bernalillo, Jemez State Monument is a protected historic site that preserves the remains of a 500 year old native Puebloan village. The site includes ruins of several religious structures, a visitor center and a museum, all nestled in a beautiful park like setting.
  • Sandoval County Historical Society Museum: The county’s only museum, located in the center of Bernalillo, showcases regional history through exhibits and educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Bernalillo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sandia Peak Tramway: Take a scenic ride up to Sandia Peak for unparalleled views of the Rio Grande Valley and the Sandia Mountains. Hiking trails, a mountain-top restaurant, and seasonal activities are also available.
  • Rio Grande Nature Center State Park: Explore the natural beauty of the Rio Grande River with hiking/biking trails, bird-watching, educational exhibits, and events.
  • Coronado State Monument: The monument includes a museum, a Kiva, prehistoric cave dwellings, picnic tables, grills, and a trailhead for a 5 mile out-and-back trail to the top of DeBaca Mesa.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bernalillo

Bernalillo is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes in Bernalillo:

  • Paseo del Bosque Trail: A scenic paved trail that runs along the Rio Grande River for 16 miles, perfect for a leisurely bike ride or an easy hike.
  • Montano Bridge Trailhead: This trailhead marks the beginning of the Bosque trail, and is also a starting point for a 7 mile hike along the Bosque.
  • Star Axis Trail: A challenging 8 mile trail that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Bernalillo: Activities and Tours

Bernalillo has no dearth of exciting activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Bernalillo:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Visit the various award-winning wineries in Bernalillo and enjoy a wine tasting tour.
  • Ghost Ranch: A scenic retreat located near Abiquiu, Ghost Ranch offers various outdoor activities such as horseback riding, hiking, and rock climbing.
  • Balloon Fiesta: Held annually in Albuquerque in early October, the Balloon Fiesta is the largest hot air balloon festival in the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Bernalillo

Bernalillo offers a variety of dining and drinking options that are as rich in taste as they are in culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Green Chile Stew: A hearty soup made with local Hatch green chiles, potatoes, pork, and spices served hot with fresh tortillas.
  • Posole: A traditional stew made with hominy, pork or chicken, garlic, onions, and red or green chiles.
  • Fry Bread: A Native American specialty, deep-fried dough that is crispy on the outside and pillowy soft on the inside served with honey or powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Margarita: A classic cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and triple sec, usually served in a salt rimmed glass.
  • Red Chile Beer: A local favorite, a beer brewed with red chiles giving it a spicy kick.
  • New Mexico Wine: Bernalillo has numerous award-winning wineries producing locally sourced wines.

Where to Stay in Bernalillo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bernalillo offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the top places to stay in Bernalillo:

  • Hyatt Regency Tamaya Resort and Spa: A luxury resort offering an array of amenities such as spa treatments, outdoor activities, and dining options.
  • Santa Ana Star Casino Hotel: A hotel with a casino, restaurants, and a spa located near the Balloon Fiesta Park.
  • Inn at Paradise: A guesthouse located within the historic district of Bernalillo and offering a unique local experience.

What to Buy in Bernalillo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bernalillo offers a wide range of locally sourced and handcrafted products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some ideas:

  • Red Chile Powder: Made from locally grown chiles, this spice is a staple in New Mexican cuisine.
  • Silver and Turquoise Jewelry: Handcrafted jewelry using traditional techniques by Native American artisans.
  • Pottery: Handcrafted clay pottery using traditional Southwest designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bernalillo

Bernalillo has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, architecture, and traditions. The town’s population is predominantly Hispanic with a significant Native American and Anglo population. The town’s culture and customs are centered around a strong sense of community, family, and faith.

Best Time to Visit Bernalillo

The best time to visit Bernalillo is from March to May or from September to November when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are smaller. Music and culture festivals, such as the New Mexico State Fair and Balloon Fiesta, take place during the fall months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bernalillo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the town with hiking and biking trails.
  • Visit the local wineries and try the local cuisine.
  • Attend the Balloon Fiesta in Albuquerque.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing while visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or disturb natural habitats.
  • Disrespect the sacred Native American sites and customs.
  • Overindulge in alcohol if you are taking part in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bernalillo

Bernalillo is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Bernalillo

Bernalillo has numerous medical facilities and pharmacies available to visitors. Visitors are advised to exercise caution while participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking or biking, and bring adequate supplies.

Getting to Bernalillo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bernalillo is easily accessible via various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Bernalillo

  • Albuquerque International Sunport: The nearest international airport to Bernalillo is Albuquerque International Sunport, which is located 21 miles south of Bernalillo.

Bus Stations Near Bernalillo

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Albuquerque, approximately 20 miles south of Bernalillo.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: For those looking to explore local sites at their own pace, car rentals are easily available at the Albuquerque International Sunport.

How to Get Around in Bernalillo

There are several ways to get around Bernalillo, depending on your preference and budget. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available, as well as rental cars, bicycles, and scooters.

Where to Go Next from Bernalillo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bernalillo is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations, such as:

  • Santa Fe: The state capital of New Mexico, known for its rich history, art galleries, and museums.
  • Taos: A picturesque town known for its historic adobe architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and outdoor adventures.
  • White Sands National Park: One of the world’s largest gypsum dune fields, which offers unique experiences such as hiking, sledding, and stargazing.

Conclusion

Bernalillo is an enchanting town with a rich cultural heritage and scenic outdoors that is worth a visit. With its various museums, historic sites, outdoor adventures, vibrant local culture, and excellent food and drink offerings, Bernalillo offers something for everyone. Plan your trip to Bernalillo with this comprehensive travel guide, and come make unforgettable memories in this charming New Mexican town.
