Introduction to Quad Cities

Located in the heartland of America, Quad Cities is a group of four cities situated on the Mississippi River. The cities include Davenport and Bettendorf in Iowa, and Rock Island and Moline in Illinois. The Quad Cities is a unique destination that offers visitors a treasure trove of cultural and recreational attractions. Whether it’s exploring historical sites, hiking or biking in beautiful parks, or enjoying the vibrant food and drink scene, Quad Cities has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Quad Cities

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Figge Art Museum

Located in Davenport, the Figge Art Museum houses an impressive collection of American and European art from the 17th century to the present. The museum’s building itself is an architectural marvel with glass walls overlooking the Mississippi River.

John Deere Pavilion

John Deere Pavilion is a museum dedicated to the agricultural company that bears its name, offering interactive exhibits and displays about their rich history.

Rock Island Arsenal Museum

The Rock Island Arsenal Museum was established in 1905, telling the story of the U.S. Army’s Rock Island Arsenal and offering visitors the chance to see exhibits focused on the history of weaponry and the Arsenal’s important contributions to the country’s defense.

Natural Attractions of Quad Cities: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Black Hawk State Historic Site

Located in Rock Island, the Black Hawk State Historic Site offers visitors the chance to explore the natural habitat of the region’s diverse flora and fauna. Journey through the park’s hiking trails and enjoy the lovely scenery and historical landmarks.

Niabi Zoo

Niabi Zoo is a beautiful and well-kept animal park that gives visitors the opportunity to see and interact with exotic animals from all over the world, from lions to zebras and everything in between.

Quad City Botanical Center

Located in Rock Island, the Quad City Botanical Center is a stunning indoor and outdoor garden filled with trees, plants, and flowers. This garden oasis is a great spot to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quad Cities

There’s no better way to explore the natural beauty of Quad Cities than on foot or on a bike. Some of the best trails include the Great River Trail, the Goose Creek Pathway, and the Hennepin Canal Parkway.

Best Things to Do in Quad Cities: Activities and Tours

Riverboat Cruises

Experience the Mississippi River by hopping onto one of Quad Cities’ riverboat cruises. Enjoy the scenic views and take in all the natural beauty of Quad Cities while on a relaxing, informative boat tour.

Art Hop

This annual art festival features live music, local artists, and fun activities for everyone. Visitors will have the chance to explore the different galleries around the neighborhoods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Quad Cities

Day 1: Visit the Figge Art Museum and take a stroll through Vander Veer Botanical Park.

Day 2: Take a riverboat tour and explore the Mississippi River up close.

Day 3: Hike the Great River Trail or the Hennepin Canal Parkway State Park State Trail

Day 4: Visit the John Deere Pavilion and explore the history of the agricultural leader.

Day 5: Take a tour through the German Heritage Center in Davenport and learn about the region’s German roots.

Day 6: Enjoy a golf round at TPC Deere Run.

Day 7: Shop, eat, and relax at the charming Village of East Davenport.

Food and Drink Scene of Quad Cities

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Quad Cities is complete without trying some of the region’s signature dishes:

Iowa Pork Tenderloin

This dish consists of a breaded and fried pork patty served on a bun. Tender, juicy, and popular everywhere in this part of the Hawkeye State.

Maid-Rites

Maid-Rites are a type of loose meat sandwich and are the specialty of Taylors Maid-Rite in Marshalltown, Iowa.

Local Beverages

Mississippi River Distillery

Mississippi River Distilling Company offers a delicious range of spirits, including vodka, gin, and whiskey. Stop by their tasting room and enjoy a drink, or sign up for a tour to learn how it’s made.

Bent River Brewing Company

Bent River Brewing Company offers a variety of handcrafted, award-winning beers that will wow any beer-lover.

Where to Stay in Quad Cities: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Blackhawk

Regarded as Quad Cities’ most elegant hotel, Hotel Blackhawk offers a beautiful blend of classic charm and modern amenities. The hotel is located in the heart of Downtown Davenport, offering excellent access to the city’s attractions.

The Current Iowa

The Current Iowa is a quirky boutique hotel set in a beautifully renovated historic building in the hip area of downtown Davenport. This hotel offers 24-hour room service and on-site dining options.

What to Buy in Quad Cities: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Modern Woodmen Park Gear

Modern Woodmen Park is home to the Quad Cities River Bandits, and you can find some great souvenirs of your trip when you shop at their store.

Beaded Hearts

Authentic beaded hearts are available for purchase from many local shops in Quad Cities. This craft represents the city’s hobbyists and artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Quad Cities

Quad Cities has a fascination with all things antique and vintage, highlighted by its many antique stores and vintage markets. Local customs involve appreciation for the region’s outdoor recreation, music, and craftsmanship in all forms.

Best Time to Visit Quad Cities

Quad Cities is an excellent destination all year-round. The best time to visit depends on activities that you prefer. Summer is great for outdoor activities, particularly in parks, while fall is good to feel the changes that come with the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quad Cities

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and take time to enjoy the region’s charm and history.
  • Take part in any cultural festivals, such as Art Hop, while in town.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and show disrespect for local waste disposal facilities.
  • Don’t go hiking or engage in outside activities without checking the weather forecast first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Quad Cities

Quad Cities is generally considered a safe destination with low crime rates. Visitors must still take reasonable precautions, such as locking their vehicles and hotel rooms.

Health in Quad Cities

Quad Cities offers a range of medical resources to ensure visitors stay safe and healthy. There are many clinics and hospitals, as well as certified medical practitioners to assist visitors in case of emergencies.

Getting to Quad Cities: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Quad Cities

Quad Cities International Airport (MLI) is the major airport serving the region. It’s the closest and most convenient airport to reach all four cities.

Bus Stations Near Quad Cities

The Greyhound Bus Station is conveniently located in downtown Davenport for travelers that prefer a bus ride across the Midwest.

Other Options

Visitors also have the option of traveling to Quad Cities by car or train via Amtrak.

How to Get Around in Quad Cities

The best way to get around Quad Cities is via car, which you can rent at the airport. Another option is to use Lyft or Uber to move around the cities.

Where to Go Next from Quad Cities: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby Galena, Illinois, and Lake Geneva, Wisconsin are great spots for a day trip out from Quad Cities, offering picturesque historic downtowns, beautiful parks, and recreational activities.

Conclusion

Quad Cities is a beautiful destination in the Midwest that offers something for almost every traveler. From natural beauty to cultural attractions, there’s never a shortage of things to do in this charming region. Plan a leisurely stay and leave your worries behind as you enjoy the beauty of Quad Cities.
