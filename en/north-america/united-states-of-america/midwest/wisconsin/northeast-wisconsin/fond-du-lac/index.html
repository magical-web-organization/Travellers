Introduction to Fond du Lac

Fond du Lac, located in Northeast Wisconsin, is a charming city that welcomes visitors with its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Located between Milwaukee and Green Bay, Fond du Lac offers a perfect balance of city amenities and small-town charm. The city boasts a wide range of attractions, including museums, scenic parks, and beautiful lakeside views.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a tour of Fond du Lac and help you plan your trip with information on the best things to do, see, eat, and stay.

Before we dive into the specifics of what to see and do in Fond du Lac, here is a quick list of places you must visit during your trip to this beautiful city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fond du Lac has a rich history, and its cultural attractions reflect that. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places you should include in your itinerary:

  • Galloway House and Village: A living history museum that offers visitors a chance to experience life in the 1800s. The village has 30 buildings that represent the architecture and lifestyle of that time.
  • Children’s Museum of Fond du Lac: A perfect place for families to spend an afternoon, the museum encourages hands-on exploration and learning. The museum has a variety of exhibits, including a fire truck, a music studio, and a water table.
  • Historic Downtown Fond du Lac: Take a stroll down Main Street and admire the beautiful architecture of the historic buildings. Some notable buildings include the Masonic Temple, the Retlaw Theater, and the Fond du Lac Public Library.

Natural Attractions of Fond du Lac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fond du Lac is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in the Midwest. Here are some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views you can explore during your visit:

  • Lakeside Park: One of the most popular attractions in Fond du Lac, Lakeside Park is home to a variety of activities. Take a ride on the miniature train, explore the petting zoo, or simply relax on the beach.
  • Horicon Marsh: Located just 20 miles north of Fond du Lac, Horicon Marsh is the largest freshwater cattail marsh in the United States. A bird watcher’s paradise, the marsh is home to over 300 different species of birds.
  • Kettle Moraine State Forest: This state forest offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and biking. The highlight of the forest is the Ice Age Trail, which winds through the forest and offers breathtaking views of the Kettle Moraine area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fond du Lac

For those interested in exploring Fond du Lac’s natural beauty, here are some great hiking and biking routes to consider:

  • Oven Island Nature Trail: A beautiful two-mile hiking trail located in the heart of the city. The trail winds through a wooded area and offers stunning views of Lake Winnebago.
  • Mascoutin Valley State Trail: A 13-mile trail that runs from Berlin to Ripon. The trail offers scenic views of the countryside, and there are several picnic areas along the way.
  • Wild Goose State Trail: A 34-mile trail that runs from Fond du Lac to Clyman Junction. The trail is perfect for both biking and hiking and offers beautiful views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Fond du Lac: Activities and Tours

Fond du Lac offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do during your trip:

  • Fondy Sports Park: Whether you’re a thrill-seeker or just looking for something fun to do with the family, Fondy Sports Park is the place to go. The park offers a wide range of activities, including go-karts, mini golf, and batting cages.
  • Escapade Cruises: Take a relaxing cruise on Lake Winnebago and enjoy stunning views of the city. The cruises are available for both public and private events.
  • Fond du Lac Ghosts and Legends Tour: Embark on a spooky tour of the city’s most haunted locations. The tour takes visitors through downtown Fond du Lac and shares tales of the city’s haunted past.

Food and Drink Scene of Fond du Lac

Fond du Lac is home to a vibrant food and drink scene that will leave visitors wanting more. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Cheese Curds: A Wisconsin staple, cheese curds are a must-try dish during your trip to Fond du Lac. Try them at any local restaurant, but we recommend Sebastian’s Steak House.
  • Kringle: A Danish pastry that is a popular breakfast item throughout Wisconsin. Order a kringle from La Sure’s Bakery, one of the best bakeries in the state.
  • Fish Fry: Friday nights in Wisconsin mean fish fry. Head to Schmitty’s Oar House for one of the best fish fry experiences in Fond du Lac.

Local Beverages

  • Spotted Cow: A popular beer from the New Glarus Brewing Company. It is only available in Wisconsin, so make sure to try it during your stay.
  • Bloody Mary: A popular drink in Wisconsin, the Bloody Mary is a meal in itself. Try the Bloody Mary at Salty’s Seafood & Spirits, which is known for its over-the-top presentation.

Where to Stay in Fond du Lac: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fond du Lac offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hotel Retlaw: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Fond du Lac. The hotel features luxurious rooms, a rooftop bar, and a full-service spa.
  • Holiday Inn Express: Located on the outskirts of Fond du Lac, the Holiday Inn Express offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Airbnb: There are plenty of Airbnb options in Fond du Lac, including guesthouses and apartments. This is a great option for those looking for a more unique accommodation experience.

What to Buy in Fond du Lac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Fond du Lac:

  • Wisconsin Cheese: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, so be sure to bring some local cheese home with you. Order from Widmer’s Cheese Cellars, a family-owned business that has been making cheese since 1922.
  • Kringle: As mentioned earlier, kringle is a popular pastry in Wisconsin and makes a great gift.
  • Wisconsin Beer: In addition to Spotted Cow, Wisconsin is home to several other popular breweries. Bring home a variety pack of local beer to enjoy with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Fond du Lac

Wisconsin is known for its friendly and welcoming culture, and Fond du Lac is no exception. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Use common courtesy: Wisconsin residents are known for their friendliness and politeness, so it’s important to reciprocate that during your visit.
  • Embrace the local culture: Fond du Lac has a rich history and culture. Take the time to visit museums and historical sites to learn more about the city’s heritage.
  • Appreciate the outdoors: Wisconsin is known for its natural beauty, so make sure to take advantage of the hiking trails and scenic views during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Fond du Lac

The best time to visit Fond du Lac is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, consider visiting in the fall (September-November) when the leaves change color and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fond du Lac

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Fond du Lac:

Do:

  • Try the local cheese and beer.
  • Explore the city’s natural beauty.
  • Visit museums and historical sites to learn about the city’s culture and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Wear Green and gold (Green bay Packer’s colors) in Wisconsin Badgers territory, or wear red in Ohio State territory
  • Litter in parks and other public spaces.
  • Disrespect local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Fond du Lac is a safe and healthy destination for visitors. Here are some tips on staying safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Fond du Lac

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Lock your car doors and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.
  • Observe traffic signals and laws.

Health in Fond du Lac

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Pack any necessary medications and a first aid kit.

Getting to Fond du Lac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fond du Lac is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Fond du Lac

  • Outagamie County Regional Airport: Located about an hour and a half from Fond du Lac.
  • General Mitchell International Airport: Located about an hour and forty-five minutes from Fond du Lac.

Bus Stations Near Fond du Lac

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in downtown Fond du Lac.
  • Lamers Bus Lines: Offers service to and from several nearby cities, including Milwaukee and Madison.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: A rental car is a great option for those who want to explore the surrounding areas and natural attractions.
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Taxis and rideshare services are available in Fond du Lac for those who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in Fond du Lac

Fond du Lac is a walkable city, but there are also several transportation options available:

  • Walking: Many of the city’s attractions are located within walking distance of each other.
  • Biking: Rent a bike from local shops, including Fat Tire Friends and Open Road Bicycles, and explore the city on two wheels.
  • Public Transportation: Fond du Lac Area Transit offers bus service throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Fond du Lac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Fond du Lac, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Green Bay: Located just over an hour away, Green Bay is home to Lambeau Field, the Green Bay Packers, and the National Railroad Museum.
  • Milwaukee: Located about an hour and a half away, Milwaukee offers a variety of cultural attractions, including the Milwaukee Art Museum and the Harley-Davidson Museum.
  • Door County: Located about two hours north of Fond du Lac, Door County is a popular destination for its beautiful scenery, wineries, and charming small towns.

Conclusion

Fond du Lac is a hidden gem in the Midwest and an ideal destination for those who want to explore the natural beauty and culture of Wisconsin. We hope this travel guide has given you a comprehensive understanding of the city and helps you plan a memorable trip to Fond du Lac.
