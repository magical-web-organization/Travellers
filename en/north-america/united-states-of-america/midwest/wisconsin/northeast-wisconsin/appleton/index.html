Introduction to Appleton

Appleton is a charming city located in Northeast Wisconsin, known for its rich history and vibrant culture. It is the largest city in Outagamie County and has a population of around 75,000 people.

Appleton has a lot to offer visitors, from outdoor adventures and scenic views to museums and historical places. Whether you’re interested in exploring local culture, experiencing the food and drink scene, or simply enjoying the natural beauty of the area, there’s something for everyone in Appleton.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will provide you with a detailed itinerary for a week-long trip to Appleton that includes all the must-visit attractions, as well as some local tips and recommendations for getting the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Appleton

Before we dive into the specifics of what to see and do in Appleton, here’s a quick list of some of the top attractions that you won’t want to miss:

  • Hearthstone Historic House Museum
  • The History Museum at the Castle
  • Gordon Bubolz Nature Preserve
  • 1000 Islands Environmental Center

  • Butterfly Gardens of Wisconsin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Appleton is steeped in history, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore. These are some of the top destinations for history buffs and culture enthusiasts:

Hearthstone Historic House Museum

Hearthstone Historic House Museum is a beautiful Victorian mansion with an interesting history. It was the first home in the world to be powered by a hydroelectric system, and you can still see the original equipment in the basement. Guided tours of the house are available, and you’ll learn about the fascinating life of the family who lived there.

The History Museum at the Castle

The History Museum at the Castle is another must-visit destination in Appleton. It showcases the history of the region, with exhibits ranging from the Native American tribes who first inhabited the area to the modern-day city that we know today. There are also interactive exhibits that kids will love, making this a great family-friendly destination.

Natural Attractions of Appleton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love spending time in nature, Appleton has plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views to offer. Here are some of the top natural attractions you won’t want to miss:

Gordon Bubolz Nature Preserve

Gordon Bubolz Nature Preserve is a 782-acre wildlife sanctuary with hiking trails, cross-country skiing, and snowshoeing. It’s a great place to explore the natural beauty of Appleton, and you may see some of the local wildlife, including deer, birds, and even beavers.

1000 Islands Environmental Center

The 1000 Islands Environmental Center is another great destination for nature lovers. It features a boardwalk through a wetland area, as well as hiking trails and educational programs. There is also an observation tower that provides stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Appleton

If you love hiking or biking, you’ll be happy to know that Appleton has several great routes to enjoy. Here are some of the top trails in the area:

  • Apple Creek Trail
  • Fox River Trail
  • Wiouwash State Trail
  • Newberry Trail

Whether you prefer a leisurely stroll or a more challenging hike, there’s a trail to suit your needs.

Best Things to Do in Appleton: Activities and Tours

In addition to its museums, natural attractions, and hiking trails, Appleton has plenty of other things to see and do. Here are some of the best activities and tours that you won’t want to miss:

Shopping at Fox River Mall

If you’re in the mood for some retail therapy, head to Fox River Mall. It’s the largest mall in the Fox River Valley, with over 180 stores and restaurants. You could easily spend a whole day shopping, dining, and enjoying the entertainment at this popular destination.

Lawrence University

Lawrence University is a small liberal arts college located in the heart of Appleton. It offers a range of cultural events, including music concerts, theater performances, and art exhibits. Check the schedule to see what’s happening during your visit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Appleton

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Appleton, here’s an itinerary that covers all the must-see destinations and activities:

Day 1: Visit Hearthstone Historic House Museum in the morning and then head to the Fox River Mall for an afternoon of shopping and dining.

Day 2: Explore The History Museum at the Castle in the morning and then take a leisurely hike at the Gordon Bubolz Nature Preserve in the afternoon.

Day 3: Spend the morning at the Butterfly Gardens of Wisconsin, followed by a visit to Lawrence University in the afternoon.

Day 4: Rent a bike and explore the Apple Creek Trail in the morning. Then, head to the 1000 Islands Environmental Center for some hiking and wildlife spotting in the afternoon.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Green Bay (just 30 minutes away) to visit Lambeau Field, home of the Green Bay Packers.

Day 6: Spend the day on the Fox River Trail, biking or hiking and enjoying the scenic views.

Day 7: Visit the Wiouwash State Trail in the morning and then spend the afternoon exploring some of the lesser-known attractions in Appleton, such as the Building for Kids Children’s Museum or the Paper Discovery Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Appleton

No trip to Appleton is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and craft beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Curds: These deep-fried bites of cheese are a Wisconsin staple and you can’t leave the state without trying them.

  • Bratwurst: Appleton has a strong German heritage, which means that bratwurst is a must-try dish. Try it with some sauerkraut and a local craft beer.

  • Friday Fish Fry: If you’re in Appleton on a Friday, make sure to try the traditional fish fry, which typically consists of fried perch, fries, and coleslaw.

Local Beverages

  • Wisconsin Beer: Wisconsin is known for its craft beer, so make sure to sample some of the local brews during your visit. Check out local breweries like Stone Arch Brewpub and Fox River Brewing Company.

  • Spotted Cow: This beer from New Glarus Brewing Company is a Wisconsin favorite.

  • Brandy Old Fashioned: This is a popular cocktail in Wisconsin, made with brandy, sugar, bitters, and soda water.

Local Culture and Customs of Appleton

Wisconsin is known for its friendliness and hospitality, and Appleton is no exception. Expect to be greeted with a smile and a warm welcome wherever you go.

Best Time to Visit Appleton

The best time to visit Appleton is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is pleasant and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, if you don’t mind the cold, Appleton is also a great destination for winter sports, such as skiing and snowshoeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Appleton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Appleton:

Do:

  • Do try some of the local cuisine, including cheese curds and bratwurst.

  • Do bring comfortable walking shoes for hiking and exploring.

  • Do visit the museums and historical places to learn about the area’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in any of the parks or outdoor areas. Appleton takes its conservation efforts seriously.

  • Don’t forget to tip your server. It’s customary to tip 15-20% in the United States.

  • Don’t ignore the local weather forecasts. Wisconsin weather can be unpredictable, so be prepared for all types of weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Appleton

Appleton is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking at night.

  • Use caution when carrying valuables, such as wallets and purses.

  • Always lock your car and hotel room doors.

Health in Appleton

Appleton has several hospitals and medical clinics, should you need medical attention during your visit. It’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Appleton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Appleton

The closest airport to Appleton is the Appleton International Airport (ATW), which is located just 3 miles west of downtown Appleton. The airport offers direct flights to many major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Appleton

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown Appleton and offers bus services to many cities in the United States.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Appleton, there are a number of major highways that pass through the city, including Interstate 41 and U.S. Highway 10.

How to Get Around in Appleton

The best way to get around Appleton is by car or bike. There are also several taxi and ridesharing options available, such as Uber and Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Appleton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time to explore Wisconsin, here are some nearby destinations that you may want to consider:

  • Door County: Located just 1.5 hours north of Appleton, Door County is a popular summer destination known for its stunning coastal scenery and charming small towns.
