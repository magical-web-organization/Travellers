Introduction to Ripon

Located in northeastern Wisconsin, Ripon is a charming city with a rich history and plenty to offer visitors. It is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, outdoor activities, museums, and quaint downtown area.

List of Places to Visit in Ripon

Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Ripon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Little White Schoolhouse: This one-room schoolhouse is the birthplace of the Republican Party, and now serves as a museum.
  • Ripon Historical Society: presides over seven historical buildings that showcase the city’s past in agriculture, architecture, and industry.
  • Vines & Rushes Winery: a family-owned winery with a tasting room, outdoor patio, and live music events.

Natural Attractions of Ripon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White River Marsh State Wildlife Area: over 12,000 acres of hiking trails and nature trails.
  • The 1,200-acre Green Lake Conference Center: a beautiful natural retreat center with bike trails, fishing, and other outdoor activities.
  • Town Square: situated in the downtown area, this green space has benches, a fountain, and a gazebo that hosts events and concerts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ripon

Ripon’s beautiful natural surroundings make it a great place to hike and bike. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Mascoutin Valley State Trail: a 12.1-mile trail between Berlin and Ripon, with scenic views of rolling hills and farmland.
  • White River State Trail: a 7.2-mile trail that goes through Ripon and the surrounding countryside.
  • Northwestern Trail: a 13.5-mile trail that connects Ripon to Oshkosh.

Best Things to Do in Ripon: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the weekly farmers market in downtown Ripon on Saturday mornings from May through October.
  • Take a paddleboat tour of Green Lake with Heidel House Resort.
  • Attend a show at the intimate and historic Ripon College Theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ripon

Day 1: Arrive in Ripon and check into your accommodations. Visit the Little White Schoolhouse and the Ripon Historical Society. Day 2: Hike the Mascoutin Valley State Trail in the morning, then explore Ripon’s downtown area, including the weekly farmers market and local shops. Day 3: Take a paddleboat tour of Green Lake with Heidel House Resort in the morning. Spend the afternoon at the Green Lake Conference Center, hiking and enjoying the outdoors. Day 4: Go on a biking tour of Northwestern Trail, visiting nearby towns and scenic areas. Day 5: Visit Vines & Rushes Winery in the morning for a wine tasting, then attend a show at the Ripon College Theatre in the evening. Day 6: Spend the day exploring White River Marsh State Wildlife Area, hiking and enjoying nature. Day 7: Depart from Ripon.

Food and Drink Scene of Ripon

Ripon has a variety of restaurants and cafes serving up delicious food and local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bratwurst: a classic Wisconsin dish that can be found at many local eateries.
  • Cheese curds: another Wisconsin favorite, served fried or fresh.
  • Kringle: a Danish pastry that is popular in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Ale Asylum Riverhouse Brewery and Pub: a local brewery with a variety of beers on tap.
  • Vines & Rushes Winery: a family-owned winery with a tasting room and live music events.
  • Square Roots Coffee: a cozy cafe serving coffee and pastries.

Where to Stay in Ripon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ripon has a variety of accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some options:

  • Heidel House Resort: a luxury lakeside resort with multiple dining options, a spa, and outdoor activities.
  • Ripon College Guest House: a historic building transformed into cozy guest rooms and suites.
  • Welcome Inn: a budget-friendly motel with all the essentials, including a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Ripon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Ripon, be sure to bring home some local products and souvenirs. Here are some ideas:

  • Cheese from local creameries, such as Widmer’s Cheese Cellars or Union Star Cheese.
  • Handcrafted jewelry and pottery from local artisans.
  • Ripon College gear from the campus bookstore.

Local Culture and Customs of Ripon

Ripon is a friendly and welcoming community, known for its small-town charm and hospitality. It’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, including honoring the history of the Republican Party, which was founded in Ripon in 1854.

Best Time to Visit Ripon

The best time to visit Ripon is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall foliage in the surrounding area is also a popular time to visit. However, Ripon is a lovely place to visit year-round, with plenty to see and do in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ripon

Do:

  • Visit the Little White Schoolhouse and learn about the birthplace of the Republican Party.
  • Take a paddleboat tour of Green Lake with Heidel House Resort.
  • Try some local favorites, like bratwurst and cheese curds.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Disrupt the natural surroundings of Ripon’s parks and wildlife areas.
  • Forget to bring your sense of adventure and exploration!

Safety and Health

Safety in Ripon

Ripon is a safe and welcoming city, but it’s always important to take precautions when exploring a new place. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Lock your car doors and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Use caution when walking or biking on roads, especially at night.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Ripon

Ripon has a hospital and several medical clinics in the area if needed. However, it’s always important to bring any necessary medications and take precautions to stay healthy while traveling.

Getting to Ripon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ripon

The closest major airport to Ripon is the Appleton International Airport, located about 50 miles to the northeast. Other airports in the area include the Milwaukee Mitchell International Airport and the O’Hare International Airport in Chicago, both of which are around a 2-hour drive from Ripon.

Bus Stations Near Ripon

There are several bus stations located in nearby cities, such as Fond du Lac and Oshkosh. Greyhound and other bus companies offer service to these stations.

Other Options

Ripon is easily accessible by car, with major highways nearby. There are also several rental car companies in the area if needed.

How to Get Around in Ripon

Ripon is a small city that is easy to navigate by foot or bike. There are also several taxi companies in the area for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Ripon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby cities and attractions worth exploring include:

  • Oshkosh: a vibrant university town with museums, parks, and events.
  • Green Bay: home to the NFL’s Green Bay Packers, with plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Door County: a vacation destination known for its beaches, lighthouses, and charming small towns.

Conclusion

Ripon is a hidden gem in northeastern Wisconsin, with plenty to offer visitors looking for a mix of outdoor adventures, culture, and history. Whether hiking a scenic trail, enjoying a local cheeseburger, or learning about the birthplace of the Republican Party, Ripon is sure to delight and inspire.
