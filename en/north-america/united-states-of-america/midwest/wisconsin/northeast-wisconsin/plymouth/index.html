Introduction to Plymouth

Plymouth, a serene city located in Northeast Wisconsin, welcomes visitors to explore its rich offerings of cultural and natural attractions, food scene, accommodations, and more. The city nestles along the Mullet River, which provides opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Whether you are seeking historical landmarks, outdoor activities, delicious dishes, or a relaxing gateaway, Plymouth offers something for everyone. Read on to discover the best things to do and places to visit in Plymouth, as well as travel tips, safety information, and more.

Best Things to Do in Plymouth: Activities and Tours

  • Try fishing, kayaking, or canoeing at Mullet River Park.
  • Check out local events and festivals held throughout the year, such as the Cheese Capital Jazz & Blues Crawl in June, Plymouth Dirt Track Racing in summer, and Harvest Fest in October.
  • Enjoy a guided tour of the Wade House Historic Site or the Cheese Counter and Dairy Heritage Center.
  • Take a scenic drive along the Kettle Moraine State Forest, especially during fall for vibrant foliage.
  • Have fun with the family at the Plymouth Aquatic Center or the Sheboygan County Fairgrounds.

Local Culture and Customs of Plymouth

Plymouth has a strong connection with cheese-making and dairy farming, and it is evident in its food scene and attractions. The city also hosts various events and festivals throughout the year, celebrating its local heritage and community spirit.

Best Time to Visit Plymouth

The best time to visit Plymouth is from May to October when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities. Fall is especially recommended for scenic drives through Kettle Moraine State Forest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plymouth

Do:

  • Try local cheese and dairy products
  • Enjoy outdoor adventures at Mullet River Park, Sheboygan County Marsh Park, or Kettle Moraine State Forest.
  • Attend local events and festivals, such as Cheese Capital Jazz & Blues Crawl and Harvest Fest.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural and cultural attractions
  • Disturb wildlife and their habitats
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Plymouth

Plymouth is a safe and welcoming city, and visitors must take the regular precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their cars and avoiding dark or deserted areas at night.

Health in Plymouth

Visitors must follow the standard health precautions, such as bringing necessary medication and maintaining personal hygiene.
