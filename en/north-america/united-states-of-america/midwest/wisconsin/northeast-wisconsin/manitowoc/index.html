Introduction to Manitowoc

Manitowoc is a city in Northeast Wisconsin, located on the shores of Lake Michigan. It is known for its maritime history, museums, outdoor activities, and scenic views. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Manitowoc has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Manitowoc

If you’re planning a trip to Manitowoc, make sure to add these must-visit places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wisconsin Maritime Museum: Learn about the maritime history of the Great Lakes, including the USS Cobia submarine.
  2. Rahr-West Art Museum: Discover an impressive collection of American and European art, including works by Picasso, Rembrandt, and Dali.
  3. Manitowoc County Historical Society: Explore the history of Manitowoc County and its early settlers through exhibits, buildings, and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Manitowoc: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Manitowoc Breakwater Lighthouse: Take a stroll on the pier and visit the iconic lighthouse that has been guiding ships into the harbor for over a century.
  2. Lincoln Park Zoo: Enjoy an afternoon with the family at this small zoo with a variety of animals including deer, otters, and monkeys.
  3. Mariners Trail: Walk, run, or bike along this scenic trail that follows the shores of Lake Michigan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manitowoc

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Manitowoc has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of Lake Michigan and the city skyline. Some popular routes include:

  1. Manitowoc-Two Rivers Bike Route: An 11-mile trail that runs along the coast, connecting the two cities.
  2. Ice Age Trail: A 1,000-mile trail that passes through Manitowoc and offers spectacular views of glacial landscapes.
  3. Point Beach State Forest: A 3,000-acre park with hiking trails, a sandy beach, and a lighthouse.

Best Things to Do in Manitowoc: Activities and Tours

Aside from the museums and outdoor activities, Manitowoc also offers a variety of tours and activities that cater to different interests:

  1. Submarine Tour: Experience what it’s like to live and work on board a World War II submarine with a guided tour of the USS Cobia.
  2. Wine Tasting Tour: Visit the local wineries and taste the unique wines produced in the region.
  3. Manitowoc County Farm Tour: Take a tour of local farms, and learn about the agricultural practices and products of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manitowoc

If you have a week to spare in Manitowoc, here’s an itinerary that covers the best places and activities:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Wisconsin Maritime Museum and USS Cobia
Day 2 Explore the Rahr-West Art Museum and walk along the Manitowoc-Two Rivers Bike Route
Day 3 Visit the Manitowoc County Historical Society and travel back in time

Food and Drink Scene of Manitowoc

Manitowoc has a diverse food and drink scene that gives travelers a taste of the local culture. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Wisconsin-style cheese curds: A local favorite - deep-fried cheese curds that are crispy on the outside and gooey on the inside.
  2. Bratwurst: Delicious grilled sausages made with pork, beef, or both.
  3. Fish fry: A Friday night tradition, where restaurants serve beer-battered fish and fries.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: Manitowoc has several microbreweries that produce a variety of beers, from traditional lagers to experimental IPAs.
  2. Wine: The region is known for its cool-climate wines, especially Rieslings and Gewurztraminers.

Where to Stay in Manitowoc: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manitowoc has several lodging options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular choices include:

  1. Holiday Inn Manitowoc: A modern hotel with an indoor pool and restaurant.
  2. Harbor Town Inn: A boutique hotel with nautical-themed decor and stunning lake views.
  3. Village Inn on the Lake: A family-owned motel with a private beach and outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Manitowoc: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Manitowoc offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts:

  1. Wisconsin-made cheese: Take home some of the local cheese, which is known for its rich, creamy flavor.
  2. Maritime-themed gifts: The Wisconsin Maritime Museum has a gift shop that sells nautical-themed items, from t-shirts to ship models.
  3. Local wines: Bring home a bottle (or two) of the region’s delicious wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Manitowoc

Manitowoc is proud of its maritime history and agricultural heritage. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and many community events and festivals celebrate the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Manitowoc

The best time to visit Manitowoc is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the shoulder seasons (spring and fall) also offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manitowoc

Do:

  1. Embrace the local culture and try the regional cuisine.
  2. Be respectful of the natural environment and leave no trace.
  3. Explore the lesser-known attractions and hidden gems.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  2. Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  3. Miss out on the museums and historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manitowoc

Manitowoc is generally a safe city, but like any other place, it’s important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Lock your car and hotel room, and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Manitowoc

Manitowoc has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer quality healthcare services. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and carry any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Manitowoc: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manitowoc is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Manitowoc

  • Austin Straubel International Airport in Green Bay (about an hour’s drive from Manitowoc)
  • General Mitchell International Airport in Milwaukee (about a two-hour drive from Manitowoc)

Bus Stations Near Manitowoc

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Green Bay (about an hour’s drive from Manitowoc)
  • Jefferson Lines Bus Station in Sheboygan (about a 30-minute drive from Manitowoc)

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental agencies have offices at the airports and in Manitowoc.
  • Train: Amtrak’s Empire Builder runs through nearby cities of Milwaukee and Chicago, connecting to major cities of the U.S.

How to Get Around in Manitowoc

Manitowoc is a small city that can easily be explored on foot, bike, or car. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available, as well as local public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Manitowoc: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Manitowoc is a great base for exploring other nearby cities and attractions, such as:

  1. Door County: A popular vacation spot known for its beaches, lighthouses, and cherry orchards.
  2. Sheboygan: A charming city on the shores of Lake Michigan, with several museums and parks.
  3. Green Bay: Home of the Green Bay Packers football team, with a variety of tourist attractions and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Manitowoc is a hidden gem in Northeast Wisconsin, offering a blend of natural beauty, rich history, and modern amenities. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Manitowoc is sure to delight and inspire you.
