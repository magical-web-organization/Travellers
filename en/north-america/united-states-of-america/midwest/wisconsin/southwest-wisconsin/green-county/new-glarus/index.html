Introduction to New Glarus

Nestled in the rolling hills of Green County, Southwest Wisconsin, New Glarus is a quaint village with rich Swiss heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant local arts and culture scene. Nicknamed as “America’s Little Switzerland,” New Glarus is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, foodies, history buffs, and art lovers. With its friendly locals, charming architecture, and year-round festivals and events, New Glarus is the perfect vacation destination for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in New Glarus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Swiss Historical Village and Museum: Take a trip back in time and explore the rich history and culture of Swiss immigrants in Wisconsin through beautifully restored buildings, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • Chalet of the Golden Fleece Museum: Visit the historic chalet-style building to learn about the lodge’s unique history, Swiss heritage, and the impact it had on the local community.
  • New Glarus Brewing Company: Get a behind-the-scenes tour, see the brewing process and sample some of the famous beers.

Natural Attractions of New Glarus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • New Glarus Woods State Park: Explore the park’s hiking trails and savor stunning views of the Sugar River and rolling hills.
  • Sugar River State Trail: Walk or bike on the scenic trail, which runs along the Sugar River and through beautiful scenic corridors.
  • Yellowstone Lake State Park: Escape into nature and indulge in a great variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, swimming, kayaking, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Glarus

New Glarus offers some of the best hiking and biking opportunities in Wisconsin. Here are some of the must-visit trails:

  • Sugar River State Trail: This 24-mile trail runs through the beautiful Driftless Area and has access points in various towns. Be sure to check out the New Glarus portion.
  • Military Ridge State Trail: This 40-mile trail runs from Fitchburg to Dodgeville and offers fantastic views and lush forest scenery.
  • New Glarus Woods State Park: This State Park offers over 4 miles of hiking trails, where you can enjoy the scenic beauty of flora and fauna including wildflowers, oak savanna woods, and Sugar River.

Best Things to Do in New Glarus: Activities and Tours

  • Swiss Historical Village Tours: Explore the history and culture of Swiss immigrants through guided tours of the historical buildings and artifacts.
  • Brewery Tours: Beers, Germans, and Swiss are an exquisite combination, visit the New Glarus Brewing Company, and learn about the brewing process, taste, and take some more with you.
  • Cheese Tasting at Maple Leaf Cheese & Chocolate Haus: Discover Wisconsin’s dairy traditions with a cheese tasting at Maple Leaf, followed by a sampling of quality chocolates.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Glarus

Day 1: Explore the Swiss Historical Village and Museum and tour the city welcoming the sculpture of “Cow Parade.”

Day 2: Enjoy an early hike in New Glarus Woods State Park or Sugar River State Trail, then visit the Chalet of the Golden Fleece Museum and learn about its history.

Day 3: Discover the art pieces at the Swiss United Church of Christ, followed by a brewery tour of the New Glarus Brewing Company.

Day 4: Enjoy outdoor adventure and scenic beauty in Sugar River State Trail or Yellowstone Lake State Park.

Day 5: Go shopping, sample local food and beverages, and stroll through the streets of downtown New Glarus.

Day 6: Discover the colorful cycling routes in Military Ridge State Trail accompanied by kilometers of joy, triumph and fun stories.

Day 7: Enjoy local history during a Cheese Tasting experience at Maple Leaf Cheese & Chocolate Haus, followed by a scenic drive through the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of New Glarus

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fondue: A classic Swiss dish, made with melted cheese, served with cubed bread and other bite-size snacks for dipping.
  • Raclette: Another Swiss dish, which features a type of cheese melted on a grill and served with various accompaniments, such as potatoes, cured meats, and vegetables.
  • Bratwurst: A popular German-style sausage, grilled and served with sauerkraut, mustard, and a hearty bun.

Local Beverages

  • New Glarus Brewing Company beers: Check out the local craft brewery, known for their Belgian Red fruit ale and Spotted Cow farmhouse ale.
  • Wine: Check the talents of Wollersheim Winery & Distillery and indulge in delicious wine, brandy, gin, and other spirits.

Where to Stay in New Glarus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chalet Landhaus Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast with a rustic ambiance, fireplace and beautiful views of the Sugar River Valley.
  • New Glarus Hotel: A historic landmark with comfortable rooms and modern amenities, located in the heart of downtown.
  • Airbnb: Many charming, picturesque homes are listed on Airbnb, where you can experience local living and updated amenities.

What to Buy in New Glarus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cow Parade: New Glarus has a Cow Parade, find cow statues painted as Swiss characters or cute, funny themes all over the city and take one home.
  • Swiss Cheese: Cheese curds and varieties of Swiss cheese can be found in shops and cideries throughout the region.
  • Swiss Watches: Discover the fine timepieces of the Swiss innovators, enjoy the luxury and craftsmanship associated with Swiss Watches

Local Culture and Customs of New Glarus

Swiss heritage and cultural traditions are evident across New Glarus, from the architecture to the food, and the city’s rich history. Swiss flag and cow statues are a common site around the city, and Swiss music is played during local festivals, such as the Wilhelm Tell Festival in August.

Best Time to Visit New Glarus

New Glarus has a pleasant climate and can be visited year-round, depending on your preferences. The summer months are the most popular for outdoor activities, but fall foliage makes autumn a great time to visit. Winter can be cold but provides opportunities for skiing, snowshoeing, and ice fishing. Spring brings in the cherry blooms, perfect for walks and scenic drives.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Glarus

Do:

  • Walk the streets of New Glarus, wear comfortable shoes and watch out for the cow parade sculptures
  • Explore the Swiss Historical Village and Museum, the Chalet of the Golden Fleece Museum, and the Swiss United Church of Christ to learn about the city’s rich history and Swiss heritage.
  • Sample the local cheese, wine, beer and attend local festivities and events during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the parking issues in the city, stop complete to check the places or activities available.
  • Miss the scenic drives and cycling trails for a scenic experience in Southwest Wisconsin
  • Rely on big commercial fast food chains, dine locally, and savor the Swiss and German inspired dishes.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Glarus

New Glarus is considered a safe city, however, visitors must take precautions while exploring the outdoor areas or enjoying local events. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables in secure places. Use the proper safety gear when indulging in outdoor recreational activities.

Health in New Glarus

New Glarus does not have any major health hazards, but precautions must be taken to avoid tick bite during outdoor activities in the parks. It is also recommended to follow the CDC health guidelines while traveling during a pandemic.

Getting to New Glarus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Glarus

  • Dane County Regional Airport (MSN): 38 miles
  • General Mitchell International Airport (MKE): 110 miles

Bus Stations Near New Glarus

  • Madison Greyhound Bus Station (MDB): 27 miles

Other Options

  • Car: Drive to New Glarus from nearby cities, it is roughly a two-hour drive from Chicago, IL, and a three-hour drive from Milwaukee, WI.
  • Bike: The Badger State Trail and Sugar River State Trail have connections to New Glarus’s Downtown
  • Train: The city does not have a train station, but the nearest train Station is located in Columbus, WI, roughly 30 miles away from the city

How to Get Around in New Glarus

The city is compact, and most of the attractions are within walking distance, and there is free public parking throughout the city. Busses, Taxis, and Uber or Lyft are not commonly found in the city, so renting a car or bike is the best option to travel to nearby attractions or explore the outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from New Glarus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Madison, WI: Located just 30-minutes away, Madison is home to the State Capitol building, museums, restaurants, and shops.
  • Milwaukee, WI: About three hours away, Milwaukee offers a wide variety of attractions, like the Harley-Davidson Museum, the Milwaukee Art Museum, and Lakefront Brewery.
  • Chicago, IL: A two-hour drive from New Glarus, Chicago is a cultural hub, overflowing with museums, theaters, restaurants, and scenic Lake Michigan.

Conclusion

A visit to New Glarus is a perfect escape from urban and metropolitan life. From Swiss heritage, scenic beauty, outdoor recreation, food, and drink scene, New Glarus has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to “America’s Little Switzerland.”
