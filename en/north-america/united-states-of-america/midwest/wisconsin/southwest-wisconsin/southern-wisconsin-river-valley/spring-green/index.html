Introduction to Spring Green

Spring Green is a charming village located in Southern Wisconsin River Valley in Southwest Wisconsin. It’s a popular destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. In this guide, we’ll cover all the best things to see and do in Spring Green, including natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, cultural hotspots, and delicious dining options.

List of Places to Visit in Spring Green

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Spring Green:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taliesin: This was the home of Frank Lloyd Wright, an iconic American architect. Visitors can take guided tours of the estate and see the great work of one of the greatest architects in America.
  • House on the Rock: If you’re looking for something quirky and unique, then you’ll love the House on the Rock. It has a collection of oddities, including a giant whale and a room full of automated music instruments.
  • The Old Feed Mill: The Old Feed Mill has been carefully preserved over the years to give visitors an authentic look at Wisconsin’s history. You can take a guided tour and learn about the village’s past.

Natural Attractions of Spring Green: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tower Hill State Park: If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, look no further than Tower Hill State Park. The park covers over 75 acres and offers hiking trails, fishing spots, and numerous picnic areas.
  • Spring Green Preserve: The Spring Green Preserve is home to an abundance of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and even rare bird species.
  • Knothole: A local emporium and outdoor sculpture garden: Wander through the outdoor gallery’s lush gardens and relax with the tranquility of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spring Green

Spring Green has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Here are some of the best:

  • Riverway Trail: This trail runs for 14 miles along the Wisconsin River, and it’s ideal for hiking, biking, or taking casual strolls.
  • Tower Hill Loop: This hike offers a beautiful view of the Wisconsin River and the surrounding countryside. The trail’s steep inclines can not be navigated on bike.
  • Military Ridge State Trail: This trail is an excellent destination for bikers, hikers, and rollerbladers, with a total length of 40 miles.

Best Things to Do in Spring Green: Activities and Tours

Spring Green has a variety of activities to offer, including:

  • Cruise on the Wisconsin River: Take a river boat ride and take in the beautiful scenery of the area.
  • Farmers Market: Visit the local farmers market and taste the delicious and freshly grown local produce.
  • Art Galleries and Workshops: Get inspired by local artists and visit the many art galleries and workshops around the village.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spring Green

Day 1: Exploring Taliesin and the House on the Rock

Day 2: Hike Tower Hill and enjoy a picnic lunch at the summit

Day 3: Visit the local art galleries and workshops

Day 4: Bike along Riverway Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch by the water

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Old Feed Mill

Day 6: Take a boat ride along the Wisconsin River with lunch provided.

Day 7: Spend the day browsing local shops and farmers’ markets to pick up souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Spring Green

Spring Green is home to several fantastic restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wisconsin Cheese and Wine Pairing: Try the local Wisconsin cheese, known for its unique taste and quality, paired with a glass of local wine.
  • Fish Fry: A traditional Wisconsin dish, the Fish Fry is a tasty favorite consisting of fried perch or cod and thin-cut French fries.
  • Bratwurst: Made from pork and served on a local pretzel bun, bratwurst is a must-try in Wisconsin.

Local Beverages

  • Wisconsin Craft Beer: There is nothing quite like a locally brewed ale. Check out local microbreweries around Spring Green for some unique beer experiences.
  • Wine Tasting: Enjoy a quiet evening on the vineyard accompanied by a glass or bottle of local Wisconsin wine.

Where to Stay in Spring Green: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several excellent options for accommodations in Spring Green. Here are some of the best:

  • The Usonian Inn: This jewel is a Frank Lloyd Wright-inspired design, located near Taliesin.
  • Round Barn Lodge: Rustic rooms with plenty of amenities such as indoor pool, hot tub and game room.
  • Franklin Victorian Bed & Breakfast: Located in the heart of historic downtown offers luxurious accommodations with delicious in-house breakfast and amazing garden views.

What to Buy in Spring Green: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spring Green is a great place to find unique and locally-made products. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Handmade Crafts from Arts and Crafts Fairs : Check out local art and craft fairs to shop some unique homemade souvenirs.
  • Local Cheeses: Bring Wisconsin home with you with some local cheese to delight your taste buds.
  • Wine Sauces: Take back some tasty condiments like wine-infused sauces and marinades.

Local Culture and Customs of Spring Green

Wisconsin locals are known for their hospitable and friendly nature. Make sure you bring your manners and say hello, hold the door for others, and make new friends.

Best Time to Visit Spring Green

The best time to visit Spring Green is in the spring and fall when the weather is pleasant, and nature is in full bloom. Summer is the prime season, but it can be crowded. Winter can get frosty, but there is plenty of indoor fun.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spring Green

Do:

  • Do visit Taliesin, House on the Rock, and Frank Lloyd Wright Visitor Center.
  • Take scenic drives and apply snow tires in winter.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or cut branches from local trees.
  • Don’t ignore or bypass locals who greet you along with a trail.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spring Green

Spring Green is generally a safe village. However, it’s always essential to take a few precautions, such as locking your car and not leaving valuable items in it.

Health in Spring Green

Spring Green has several clinics and primary care doctors. It’s always best to maintain good health practices to enjoy your stay.

Getting to Spring Green: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spring Green

The closest airports to Spring Green are Dane County Regional Airport in Madison and General Mitchell International Airport in Milwaukee. Both airports are about two hours away from Spring Green.

Bus Stations Near Spring Green

Spring Green is served by a small bus service that connects to the larger state-wide route.

Other Options

Spring Green is accessible by car via State Highways 14 & 23 that run through the center of the city. Car rentals are available at major nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Spring Green

The village is relatively compact and can be easily explored on foot, but rental bikes and cars provide convenience and access to other areas.

Where to Go Next from Spring Green: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several places to visit near Spring Green for day trips or extended stay:

  • Madison: The state capital is just an hour’s drive away and boasts stunning lake views, fantastic shopping, unique dining experiences, and museums.
  • Milwaukee: Two and a half hours away by car, Milwaukee has several breweries, museums, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Chicago: Just a drive away, Chicago is a city filled with amazing entertainment, dining, and shopping that will keep you thoroughly engaged.

Conclusion

Spring Green is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat and an abundance of outdoor activities and excellent dining options. We hope this guide has provided you with everything you need to plan a memorable trip to Spring Green. Happy traveling!
