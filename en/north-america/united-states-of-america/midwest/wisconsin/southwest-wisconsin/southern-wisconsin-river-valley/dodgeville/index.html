Introduction to Dodgeville

Dodgeville, located in the Southern Wisconsin River Valley, is a charming city in Southwest Wisconsin known for its rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and outdoor recreational activities. The city was founded in 1827 and is the county seat of Iowa County. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy a peaceful and relaxing vacation. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Dodgeville, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Dodgeville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Governor Dodge State Park: This 5,270-acre park is the perfect place to enjoy the great outdoors. It offers hiking and biking trails, camping, fishing, and swimming.
  • Iowa County Courthouse: Built in 1859, the courthouse is a historic landmark that’s worth visiting.
  • Cave of the Mounds: This popular tourist attraction is a natural limestone cave that was discovered in 1939 and is known for its stunning formations.

Natural Attractions of Dodgeville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Mound State Park: This park offers scenic views of the countryside, hiking trails, and a picnic area. It’s a popular spot for birdwatching.
  • Tower Hill State Park: Tower Hill State Park is home to a reconstructed 1830s lead mine shaft, museum, and visitor’s center. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, fishing, and picnicking in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dodgeville

  • Military Ridge State Trail: This 40-mile crushed limestone trail runs from Dodgeville to Madison and is perfect for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling.
  • Governor Dodge State Park Trails: The park has over 40 miles of hiking trails that vary in difficulty, ranging from easy to difficult.

Best Things to Do in Dodgeville: Activities and Tours

  • House on the Rock: This offbeat tourist attraction features a collection of oddities and collections, including the world’s largest indoor carousel.
  • The Dodge Mineralogical Society Museum: The museum features over 2,000 specimens of rocks, minerals, and gemstones from around the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Dodgeville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese curds: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and Dodgeville is no exception. Be sure to try the cheese curds while you’re in town.
  • Bratwurst: Another Wisconsin staple, bratwurst is a must-try during your visit.
  • Fish fry: Friday night fish fry is a Wisconsin tradition, and you’ll find plenty of restaurants in Dodgeville that serve up delicious fish fry.

Local Beverages

  • New Glarus Brewery: This brewery is one of the most popular microbreweries in the state, and its beer is available throughout Wisconsin.

Where to Stay in Dodgeville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Don Q Inn: This unique hotel features themed rooms, such as the “Cave Room” and the “Space Odyssey Room.”
  • Super 8 by Wyndham: This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of Dodgeville and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Dodgeville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and you’ll find plenty of cheese shops in Dodgeville where you can stock up on your favorites.
  • Maple syrup: Dodgeville is located near the Driftless Area of Wisconsin, which is known for its maple syrup production.
  • Local crafts: Dodgeville is home to many talented artists and craftspeople, and you’ll find plenty of unique handmade items in local shops and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Dodgeville

Dodgeville is a charming small town with a strong sense of community. It’s a place where people take pride in their history and traditions, and visitors are always welcomed with open arms. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Ice fishing: During the winter months, many locals participate in ice fishing on the area’s lakes and rivers.
  • Friday night fish fry: As mentioned earlier, fish fry is a Wisconsin tradition that’s taken seriously in Dodgeville.
  • Outdoor recreation: With so many great parks and trails in the area, outdoor recreation is a big part of life in Dodgeville.

Best Time to Visit Dodgeville

The best time to visit Dodgeville is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the colors of the trees are at their best. Summer is a popular time to visit, but it can be crowded, and temperatures can be hot and humid. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s a great time to enjoy winter sports like ice fishing and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dodgeville

Do:

  • Do explore the great outdoors. The parks and trails in the area are some of the best in Wisconsin.
  • Do try the local cheese. It’s some of the best in the world.
  • Do visit the House on the Rock. It’s a unique and fascinating attraction that’s not to be missed.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. The natural beauty of the area is one of its biggest draws, so it’s important to keep it clean and free of litter.
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs and traditions. Wisconsin is known for its friendly and welcoming people, so it’s important to be respectful of the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dodgeville

Dodgeville is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or walking in secluded areas.

Health in Dodgeville

There are no major health concerns in Dodgeville, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid common illnesses, such as the flu and colds. Be sure to wash your hands frequently and avoid close contact with sick people.

Getting to Dodgeville: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Dane County Regional Airport: Located in Madison, Wisconsin, this airport is the closest airport to Dodgeville.

Bus Stations Near Dodgeville

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Dodgeville and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Dodgeville

The best way to get around Dodgeville is by car. There are also several bike trails in the area, so biking is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Dodgeville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dodgeville is a unique and charming town that’s the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy the great outdoors. With its beautiful parks and trails, delicious food, and fascinating attractions, there’s something for everyone in this charming Wisconsin town.
