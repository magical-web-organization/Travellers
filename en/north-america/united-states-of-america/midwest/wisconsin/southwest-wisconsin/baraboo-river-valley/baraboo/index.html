Introduction to Baraboo

Located in the picturesque Baraboo River Valley of Southwest Wisconsin, Baraboo is a scenic city that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. The historic downtown area is home to numerous museums, galleries, and charming shops, while the surrounding region boasts some of the state’s most beautiful nature reserves, nature trails, lakes, and stunning views.

Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your visit to Baraboo.

List of Places to Visit in Baraboo

Baraboo is full of history, and visitors can explore the city’s roots at numerous museums and historical sites. Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy hiking and scenic drives around the city and explore nature around Lake Delton and Devil’s Lake State Park. Below is a list of the places visitors can enjoy:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Circus World Museum - The museum features live circus performances, exhibits of historic circus wagons, and numerous artifacts.
  • Al. Ringling Mansion - The former home of the co-founder of the Ringling Brothers circus, is now a museum that features exhibits on the Ringling family and their influence on the community.
  • Historic Downtown Baraboo - The downtown area is full of galleries and antique shops that display the city’s rich history.
  • International Crane Foundation - A nature reserve and research center that provides a home for rare and endangered cranes species, as well as a museum focused on conservation.

Natural Attractions of Baraboo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Devil’s Lake State Park - One of Wisconsin’s most noteworthy state parks, Devil’s Lake is famous for its rocky cliffs, beaches and scenic views.
  • Mirror Lake State Park - a 2,179-acre park centered around a calm lake with rolling hills and its own natural beauty.
  • Parfrey’s Glen State Natural Area - A glen that features unique caves and stunning waterfalls.
  • Baraboo Hills Scenic Drive - beautiful scenic drives that unveils the best of Baraboo’s nature and offer opportunities to view wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baraboo

Baraboo has some of Wisconsin’s most notable hiking and biking routes. Below are the top routes for nature enthusiasts:

  • Merrimac Ferry to Devil’s Lake State Park Trail - a 40-mile biking trail that runs through scenic views of Devil’s Lake.
  • Baraboo River Trail - A 27-mile bike trail that winds through the scenic Baraboo River Valley.
  • Ice Age Trail - a world-renowned nature trail that offers hiking and biking opportunities along scenic meadows and forests.

Best Things to Do in Baraboo: Activities and Tours

Here are the top activities and tours to try in Baraboo:

  • Zip Lining - Take an exhilarating zip-lining tour of the beautiful Baraboo Hills with Vertical Illusions.
  • Kayaking - see the Baraboo River valley from a unique angle while kayaking just through the Baraboo River.
  • Horseback Riding - Enjoy the scenic views of Devil’s Lake State Park while riding through the park.
  • Balloon Rides - ride in a hot-air balloon and experience the beauty of the Baraboo valley.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baraboo

Day 1: Visit Circus World Museum and explore downtown Baraboo.

Day 2: Experience Devil’s Lake State Park, from hiking to camping.

Day 3: Visit the International Crane Foundation for an eclectic mix of conservation research and nature preservation.

Day 4: Head over to the Baraboo Hills Scenic Drive for a breathtaking drive.

Day 5: Take a scenic hot-air balloon ride and enjoy views of the Baraboo River Valley.

Day 6: Head over to Mirror Lake State Park and enjoy a relaxing picnic with loved ones.

Day 7: Explore the area’s historical roots by visiting historical areas like the Al. Ringling Mansion.

Food and Drink Scene of Baraboo

Baraboo is known for its varied cuisine and vibrant food scene. Here are some places to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wisconsin Cheese Curds - the freshest and most delicious cheese curds you’ll ever taste, find these delicious bites in most restaurants.
  • Bratwurst - Enjoy Baraboo’s famous bratwurst served with local beers and wines.
  • Potato Salad - a Wisconsin side-dish staple that goes perfectly with bratwurst and other meats.

Local Beverages

  • Baraboo Brewing - A craft brewery that offers a variety of hoppy beers and tasty ciders.
  • Wollersheim Winery - a family-owned winery that offers an assortment of wines grown and produced on-site.
  • Con Amici Wine Bar - A cozy, romantic Italian-themed wine bar in the heart of downtown Baraboo.

Where to Stay in Baraboo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some top-rated places to consider for a comfortable stay:

  • Clarion Hotel and Convention Center - A cozy modern property that boasts a large pool and in-house restaurants.
  • Woodside Ranch Resort and Conference Center - a rustic, full-service ranch that caters to family vacations with large swimming pools and a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Thunderbird Motor Inn Baraboo/Wisconsin Dells - popular for its proximity to nearby attractions, this cozy property is versatile and great for families.

What to Buy in Baraboo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wisconsin Cheese and Dairy Products - find shops with an assortment of cheese and dairy products that make for delicious souvenirs.
  • Craft-Made Sauces and Rubs - from condiments to food toppings, many privately-owned businesses produce these using locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Mosaic Art Pieces - visiting the mosaic art pieces museums offer a great way to appreciate the beautiful pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Baraboo

Baraboo is known for its cheese, beer, and bratwurst. With Fun festivals or Farmers’ Markets held throughout the year, the area has a strong sense of community support and love for local businesses and locally-sourced ingredients.

Best Time to Visit Baraboo

The best time to visit Baraboo is during the summer season, from May to September, as the weather is mild and excellent for outdoor activities. Spring is also an excellent option for nature enthusiasts as it offers scenic drives and budding wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baraboo

Here are some helpful tips to note during your stay in Baraboo:

Do:

  • Explore the Outdoors - Baraboo has some of Wisconsin’s most excellent hiking and biking trails, and outdoor enthusiasts should certainly take advantage of this beautiful landscape.
  • Take a Hot-Air Balloon Ride - These rides offer one of a kind views of the landscape that are both serene and breathtaking.
  • Try Local Cuisine - Enjoy sausage and cheeses in a variety of ways and combinations during your stay in Baraboo.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate Wildlife - Always take cautions when exploring wildlife areas and stay aware of natural rules, restrictions, and safety guidelines.
  • Attempt to Touch Endangered Species - Local wildlife reserves and areas like the International Crane Foundation require respect for all-native species.

Safety and Health

Here are some safety guidelines to note during your stay:

Safety in Baraboo

Baraboo, like any other small city, has a low crime rate, but visitors should always take reasonable safety precautions. If hiking or exploring the outdoors, always remain on designated trails or in areas set aside for the public.

Health in Baraboo

Visitors should take necessary precautions for sunburns or suffocation when participating in outdoor activities, such as applying sunscreen. Mosquito repellent is also useful during the summer months.

Getting to Baraboo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baraboo

Madison’s Dane County Regional Airport (MSN) is the closest airport to Baraboo, just 40 miles away. The airport offers regular flights from major domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Baraboo

The Baraboo bus station, located at 111 8th Avenue, provides visitors with convenient access to the rest of the city.

Other Options

Visitors can choose to hire a rental car and drive from nearby cities like Madison or Milwaukee or travel to Baraboo by train to nearby Wisconsin Dells station.

How to Get Around in Baraboo

Public transportation is limited in Baraboo, making it best for visitors to either bring their vehicle or rent a car to move around the city. There are also several local taxi services to choose from and a limited area shuttle service.

Where to Go Next from Baraboo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baraboo is close to numerous attractions and locations that visitors should check out, including Wisconsin Dells, Madison, and La Crosse.

Conclusion

Baraboo, Wisconsin, offers visitors a blend of cultural and outdoor activities with plenty of options for history lovers, nature enthusiasts, families, couples, and solo travelers. From its vibrant community and locally-sourced ingredients to its scenic beauty and outdoor adventures, Baraboo is an experience worth cherishing.
