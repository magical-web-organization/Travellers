Introduction to Necedah

Necedah is a charming village located in the Baraboo River Valley of Southwest Wisconsin. It is surrounded by the beautiful landscapes and forests of the Midwest, making it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The village has a rich history and culture, with several museums and cultural attractions. Travelers looking for a peaceful getaway or an adventure-filled break will both find plenty to do in Necedah.

List of Places to Visit in Necedah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Necedah National Wildlife Refuge: This 44,000-acre refuge is home to over 200 species of birds and other wildlife. It is also home to the historic Necedah Crane Refuge, where visitors can see sandhill cranes.
  • Necedah State Wildlife Area: This 16,000-acre wildlife area is a popular destination for hunting, fishing, and wildlife viewing. It has several nature trails with beautiful scenic views.
  • Roche-A-Cri State Park: This beautiful state park features a 300-foot-high rock formation, with 303 steps leading up to a lookout point with a stunning 360-degree view of the area.
  • Necedah Area Historical Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and culture of the Necedah area, from Native American artifacts to the modern era.

Natural Attractions of Necedah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yellow River State Wildlife Area: This 14,000-acre wildlife area has several hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife viewing areas. It is also a popular destination for hunting and fishing.
  • Petenwell Lake: This beautiful lake is perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming. It has several campsites and picnic areas.
  • Necedah Bluff State Natural Area: This area has several hiking and biking trails, with breathtaking views of the Baraboo River Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Necedah

  • Necedah Wildlife Refuge Loop Trail: This 4.3-mile trail takes visitors through the Necedah National Wildlife Refuge, with stunning views of the sandhill crane refuge and wetlands.
  • Roche-A-Cri State Park Trail: This 2-mile trail takes visitors to the top of the Roche-A-Cri rock formation, with stunning views of the area.
  • Yellow River State Wildlife Area Trail: This 3-mile trail takes visitors through the beautiful landscapes and forests of the Yellow River State Wildlife Area.

Best Things to Do in Necedah: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing/Kayaking: There are several companies in Necedah that offer canoeing and kayaking tours of the Yellow River and other waterways in the area.
  • Fishing: Necedah is a popular destination for fishing, with several lakes and rivers in the area.
  • Birdwatching/Wildlife Viewing: The Necedah National Wildlife Refuge and other wildlife areas in the area are perfect for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

Food and Drink Scene of Necedah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wisconsin Cheese Curds: These deep-fried cheese curds are a Wisconsin staple and can be found at most restaurants in the area.
  • Bratwurst: This German sausage is a classic Wisconsin dish, and is served at many local restaurants.
  • Fish Fry: This Wisconsin tradition involves deep-fried fish (usually cod or perch) served with fries and coleslaw.

Local Beverages

  • New Glarus Beer: This Wisconsin brewery is famous for its Spotted Cow beer and other brews.
  • Wisconsin Wines: The Necedah area has several wineries, with wines made from locally grown grapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Necedah

Wisconsin is known for its friendly, welcoming culture, and Necedah is no exception. The area has a rich history and culture, with several Native American tribes calling the area home before European settlers arrived.

Best Time to Visit Necedah

The best time to visit Necedah is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, fall is also a popular time to visit, with the beautiful changing leaves and fall festivals.
