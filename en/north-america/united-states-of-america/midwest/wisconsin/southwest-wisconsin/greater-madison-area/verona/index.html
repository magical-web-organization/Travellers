Introduction to Verona

Located in the Greater Madison Area in Southwest Wisconsin, Verona is a charming city with a population of approximately 13,000. The city has plenty to offer, from its scenic views of rolling hills and historic architecture to its vibrant food and drink scene. Verona is also home to a number of cultural and outdoor attractions, perfect for travelers seeking a unique experience. This travel guide will provide a comprehensive resource for exploring Verona.

List of Places to Visit in Verona

Verona has a range of attractions for tourists to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Verona Area Historical Society and Museum - Explore the local history of Verona, including early settlers, agricultural heritage, and local businesses.
  • Sow’s Ear Winery - Take a tour of this local winery and enjoy a glass of wine with scenic views of the vineyard.
  • Epic Systems Campus - Visit the world-renowned healthcare software company’s campus and see its impressive artwork collection.

Natural Attractions of Verona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Military Ridge State Trail - This trail offers beautiful views of the rolling hills in Verona and can be enjoyed by hiking or biking.
  • Badger Prairie County Park - A 100-acre park that offers hiking trails, disc golf, and picnic areas.
  • Hawks Landing Golf Club - Golfers will enjoy playing on this 18-hole championship course with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Verona

Verona has several breathtaking hiking and biking routes that visitors can enjoy. Some of the most popular include:

  • Military Ridge State Trail - The trail offers a scenic, paved journey through beautiful hills and countryside.
  • Ice Age Trail - This trail offers a range of hiking options, including a scenic overlook and impressive glacial landscape.
  • Wildwood Park Trail - The trail is perfect for nature walks, with picturesque views of the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Verona: Activities and Tours

Verona offers a range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best:

  • Verona Food Tour - This tour takes visitors on a culinary adventure through Verona’s delicious food scene, with stops at local restaurants and cafes.
  • Hometown Junction Park - This park offers a range of family-friendly activities, including a disc golf course, playground, and a seasonal ice rink.
  • Verona Farmers Market - Visitors can explore the market and buy fresh, locally sourced produce and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Verona

If you’re looking to spend a week in Verona, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Verona’s local history at the Verona Area Historical Society and Museum.
Day 2 Hike or bike the Military Ridge State Trail.
Day 3 Enjoy an afternoon of golf at Hawks Landing Golf Club.
Day 4 Take a wine tour at the Sow’s Ear Winery.
Day 5 Learn about healthcare technology at the Epic Systems Campus.
Day 6 Experience the local food scene on a food tour.
Day 7 Spend the day at Hometown Junction Park, enjoying the many activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Verona

Verona offers a range of delicious food and drink options for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Curds - These bite-sized pieces of deep-fried Wisconsin cheese are a local favorite.
  • Bratwurst - A popular sausage dish that is commonly enjoyed at local festivals and events.
  • Fish Fry - On Fridays, many local restaurants serve a tradition Wisconsin fish fry.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Wisconsin is known for its breweries, and Verona has some of the best. Local favorites include Hop Haus Brewing Company, Capital Brewery, and The Grumpy Troll Brew Pub.
  • Wine - Sow’s Ear Winery is a popular destination for wine tasting and tours.
  • Coffee - Discover the local coffee shops that serve delicious espresso, latte, and other specialty coffee drinks.

Where to Stay in Verona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Verona offers a range of accommodations for visitors. Here are some options:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites - A modern hotel with excellent amenities, including a fitness center and indoor pool.
  • Verona Guest House - Located in the heart of Verona, this guesthouse offers cozy accommodations and a warm atmosphere.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Madison/Verona - This hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms with easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Verona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Verona has a range of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. Here are some ideas:

  • Wisconsin Cheese - Bring home a taste of Wisconsin with a variety of delicious cheeses, including cheddar, gouda, and havarti.
  • Craft Beer - Pick up a variety of local craft beers from Hop Haus Brewing Company, Capital Brewery, or The Grumpy Troll Brew Pub.
  • Pottery - Discover beautiful handcrafted pottery at the Verona Artisan Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Verona

Verona is a small town with a strong sense of community. Locals are welcoming and friendly, and visitors should feel comfortable exploring the city. Wisconsin is known for its dairy products, and visitors should be sure to try some of the local cheeses or dairy-based dishes.

Best Time to Visit Verona

The best time to visit Verona is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the leaves change color and the area becomes even more picturesque.

Do’s and Don’ts of Verona

Do:

  • Do try the local cheese and beer.
  • Do explore the beautiful hiking and biking trails.
  • Do attend a local festival or event.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip your server at restaurants.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for recommendations from locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Verona

Verona is a safe city, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions such as locking cars and securing valuables. Be cautious when hiking or biking on trails, always staying on designated paths and watching for any possible hazards.

Health in Verona

Verona has several medical centers and clinics that offer a range of healthcare services. For medical emergencies, dial 911.

Getting to Verona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Verona

  • Dane County Regional Airport - This major airport is located approximately 15 miles from Verona and offers connections to various domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Verona

  • Madison Greyhound Station - This bus station is located in downtown Madison, approximately 11 miles from Verona.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Verona from neighboring cities or take a taxi or rideshare service to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Verona

Verona is a small city that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Verona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Verona is located in the Greater Madison area, which has a range of attractions for visitors to explore. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Madison - The capital city of Wisconsin offers a range of attractions, including the University of Wisconsin-Madison, museums, and art galleries.
  • Mount Horeb - A nearby town known for its unique outdoor art sculptures, quaint shops, and excellent restaurants.
  • Blue Mound State Park - Located just outside of Verona, this park offers hiking, biking, skiing, and snowshoeing.

Conclusion

Verona is a charming city that offers a range of attractions for visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, the outdoors, food, or culture, Verona has something for everyone. Follow this guide to explore this wonderful city and have an unforgettable experience.
