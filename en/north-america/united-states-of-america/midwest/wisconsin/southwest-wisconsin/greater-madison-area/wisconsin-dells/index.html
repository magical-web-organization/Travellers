Introduction to Wisconsin Dells

Wisconsin Dells is a popular tourist destination in the Midwest of the United States. Located in the Greater Madison Area of Southwest Wisconsin, Wisconsin Dells is home to a number of natural wonders and adventure activities. Visitors can find excitement in its amusement parks, water parks, hiking trails, and many outdoor attractions.

In this guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know when planning your trip to Wisconsin Dells, including top things to do, places to visit, where to eat and stay, and tips for your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Wisconsin Dells

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Experience the rich history and culture of Wisconsin Dells by visiting the following museums and historical places:

  • H. H. Bennett Studio & Museum: Explore the works of H. H. Bennett, a famous photographer who captured the beauty of Wisconsin Dells in the late 1800s.
  • Museum of Historic Torture Devices: Learn about the medieval torture devices used throughout history.
  • Wisconsin Opry: See talented performers singing and playing live music at the Wisconsin Opry.

Natural Attractions of Wisconsin Dells: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Venture out into the great outdoors of Wisconsin Dells and explore its natural beauty:

  • Devil’s Lake State Park: Hike the trails, kayak/canoe on Devil’s Lake, or swim in its refreshing waters.
  • Witches Gulch: Take a guided tour through this narrow canyon in Wisconsin Dells’ Pine Island State Park.
  • Lost Canyon Tours: Explore the colorful rock formations, waterfalls, and wildlife of Lost Canyon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wisconsin Dells

Enjoy scenic views and stay active by hiking or biking these beautiful trails in Wisconsin Dells:

  • Merrimac Preserve: Hike or bike through this 150-acre preserve on the western shore of Lake Wisconsin, which includes prairies, wetlands, and ponds.
  • Great Sauk State Trail: Bike through the scenic Driftless Area, a breathtaking land of rolling hills and deep valleys.
  • Dells of the Wisconsin River State Natural Area: Hike to see the stunning rock formations and natural bridges that cover over 5 miles of the Wisconsin River.

Best Things to Do in Wisconsin Dells: Activities and Tours

For those looking for adventure and excitement, be sure to try these thrilling activities and tours in Wisconsin Dells:

  • Wisconsin Dells Go-Karts: Race on a multi-level track and compete with friends and family.
  • Jet Boat Adventures: Experience the thrill of a high-speed ride on the beautiful Wisconsin River.
  • Dells Boat Tours: Take a relaxing tour on the Upper Dells and see the stunning rock formations and scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Wisconsin Dells

Wisconsin Dells offers a diverse range of dining options that cater to every taste and budget. Be sure to try these must-try dishes and beverages that are unique to Wisconsin Dells:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Deep-fried Cheese Curds: Sample this cheesy snack that Wisconsin is famous for.
  • Bratwurst: Try Wisconsin’s famous locally-made bratwurst.
  • Fish Fry: Savor some delicious fried fish, typically served with coleslaw and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Wisconsin Craft Beer: Sample some of the local breweries’ produce by trying a variety of craft beers.
  • Wisconsin’s Famous Bloody Mary: Served with pickles, olives, cheese, and even bacon, Wisconsin’s Bloody Mary is famous for its elaborate garnishes.

Where to Stay in Wisconsin Dells: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodations in Wisconsin Dells, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Chula Vista Resort: A family-friendly resort with an indoor and outdoor water park, as well as an activity center and golf course.
  • Springhill Suites by Marriott: A modern hotel with indoor and outdoor pools, fitness center, and mini-golf course.
  • Thunderbird Motor Inn: A budget-friendly option with an outdoor pool, picnic area, and playground.

What to Buy in Wisconsin Dells: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Wisconsin Dells by purchasing these unique souvenirs:

  • Wooden Carvings: Take home a unique piece of Wisconsin Dells by purchasing a hand-carved wooden animal.
  • Wisconsin Cheese: Buy some delicious cheese from one of the local cheese shops to take home and share with family and friends.
  • Wisconsin Dells T-Shirts: Buy a t-shirt with Wisconsin Dells’ logo or a unique design to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Wisconsin Dells

Wisconsin Dells has a friendly and welcoming culture, with many locals proud to share their heritage and traditions with visitors. When visiting Wisconsin Dells, be sure to show respect for its cultural customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Wisconsin Dells

The best time to visit Wisconsin Dells is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring and fall are also great seasons to visit, where you can enjoy fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wisconsin Dells

Here are some do’s and don’ts to remember when visiting Wisconsin Dells:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.
  • Do plan ahead for popular attractions and tours.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the natural attractions.
  • Don’t ignore the cultural customs and traditions of Wisconsin Dells.
  • Don’t forget to bring insect repellent for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wisconsin Dells

Wisconsin Dells is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings in crowded areas.
  • Follow all safety protocols when engaging in adventure activities and tours.
  • Use caution when driving on rural roads, especially at night.

Health in Wisconsin Dells

Visitors to Wisconsin Dells should be aware of the hazards of sunburn, dehydration, and bug bites, especially while enjoying outdoor activities. Be sure to bring sunscreen, water bottles, and insect repellant to keep yourself safe and hydrated.

Getting to Wisconsin Dells: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wisconsin Dells can be accessed via different transportation options, depending on where you are coming from:

Airports Near Wisconsin Dells

  • Dane County Regional Airport (MSN): Located in Madison, Wisconsin, this airport is approximately 45 miles (72.4 km) from Wisconsin Dells.

Bus Stations Near Wisconsin Dells

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Madison, Wisconsin, this station is approximately 45 miles (72.4 km) from Wisconsin Dells.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Consider renting a car to easily get to and around Wisconsin Dells.
  • Private Airport Shuttle Service: There are various shuttle services that offer pick-up and drop-off options from the airport to Wisconsin Dells.

How to Get Around in Wisconsin Dells

It is recommended to rent a car to easily get around Wisconsin Dells. Taxis, buses, and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Wisconsin Dells: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Wisconsin Dells, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Baraboo: Explore the charming town of Baraboo, which is only 12 miles (19.3 km) away from Wisconsin Dells. Be sure to visit the International Crane Foundation and Circus World Museum.
  • Mineral Point: Visit the historic town of Mineral Point, which is approximately 88 miles (141.6 km) southwest of Wisconsin Dells. Mineral Point is famous for its charming, historic architecture and lead-mining heritage.
  • Madison: Explore the vibrant city of Madison, located approximately 46 miles (74 km) southeast of Wisconsin Dells. Madison is home to the University of Wisconsin-Madison, local breweries, and numerous cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Wisconsin Dells has something for everyone, whether you are looking for an adventure or a relaxing getaway. With its natural attractions, thriving dining scene, and friendly culture, Wisconsin Dells is a wonderful destination to explore in the Midwest of the United States. Be sure to follow our guide to make the most out of your trip and create unforgettable memories.
