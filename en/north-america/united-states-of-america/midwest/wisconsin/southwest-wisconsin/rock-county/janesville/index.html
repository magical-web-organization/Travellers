Introduction to Janesville

Janesville is a charming city located in Rock County, Wisconsin. Established in 1835, the city boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. It is an ideal spot for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

In this guide, we’ve rounded up the city’s top attractions, activities, and itinerary suggestions to help you plan your trip to Janesville with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Janesville

Janesville has something for everyone, from history buffs to nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lincoln-Tallman House: This beautifully preserved Victorian mansion provides visitors with a glimpse into the life of one of America’s most important presidents.

  2. Rock County Historical Society: Explore the history of Rock County at this museum featuring exhibits and artifacts that cover 1800s to present day.

  3. Helen Jeffris Wood Museum Center: This museum is home to a collection of Rock County’s art, history, and culture.

  4. Rotary Botanical Gardens: These beautiful 20-acres gardens feature over 24 themed garden areas and rotating exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Janesville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Palmer Park: This park offers a range of activities from picnicking to fishing, hiking to disc golf.

  2. Riverside Park: A beautiful and well-equipped park located along the Rock River, Riverside Park offers a range of amenities from picnic shelters to splash pads.

  3. Devils Staircase: A natural staircase in a wooded area that leads to beautiful views of the city.

  4. Ice Age Trail: The trail passes through the Janesville area, offering a scenic hike through diverse geological features of the Ice Age.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Janesville

Janesville has several scenic hiking and biking routes that offer a variety of nature and architecture views. Here are our top picks:

  1. Riverside Park Trail: Snaking downstream past lush forests and along impressive rock formations, this trail offers great views at any time of year.

  2. Eastwood Conservation Park: Trails at Eastwood offer hikers and bikers a chance to walk the river flats, explore the woods, and enjoy the expansive views South of Janesville.

  3. Rock County Bike Trail: This 23.6-mile bike trail offers a great opportunity to explore the area between Janesville and Beloit.

  4. Janesville’s Riverward Park Trail: A short trail that offers scenic views of both Upper and Lower Trestle Falls, as well as a chance to explore the many wildlife species that frequent the area.

Best Things to Do in Janesville: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of activities to enjoy in Janesville. Here are our top picks:

  1. Janesville Farmer’s Market: Held on Saturdays from May to October, the Janesville Farmer’s Market is a great place to grab some locally-grown produce and other goodies.

  2. Janesville Art Infusion: A community art center, featuring not only art exhibitions and galleries but also educational programs and other community events.

  3. Frank Lloyd Wright’s Historic Architecture Tour: A guided tour of famed architect Frank Lloyd Wright’s iconic creations around Janesville.

  4. Performing Arts Center: A major venue that puts on a variety of stage productions from plays to concerts to dance shows, with a range of beloved productions yearly.

Week-Long Itinerary for Janesville

Day 1: Visit the Rotary Botanical Gardens.

Day 2: Go to the Rock County Historical Society.

Day 3: Explore the downtown area and visit the Janesville Art Infusion.

Day 4: Head out for a hike on the Ice Age Trail.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of Frank Lloyd Wright’s historic architecture in Janesville.

Day 6: Go fishing or kayaking on the Rock River.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day in nature at the Palmer Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Janesville

There is no shortage of restaurants, cafes, and bars in Janesville. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Cheese Curds: No trip to Wisconsin is complete without trying some of their famous cheese curds.

  2. Bratwurst: A classic German-inspired food that is a staple of Wisconsin cuisine can be found in almost every diner.

  3. Fish Fry: This Friday night tradition is a delicious and popular meal that is served in most local diners.

  4. Craft Beer: With many local breweries and pubs, Wisconsin is serious about their beer.

Local Beverages

  1. Wine: Wisconsin is making a name for itself in the wine scene, and Janesville has plenty of spots to grab a glass or two of local wine.

Where to Stay in Janesville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast, Janesville has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Cobblestone Hotel and Suites: A great option for families with kids with an indoor pool, pet-friendly rooms, and continental breakfast.

  2. Holiday Inn Express Janesville-I-90 & US Hwy 14: The closest hotel to downtown Janesville, offering a shuttle service, hot breakfast buffet, and fitness center.

  3. Blackhawk Camping Resort: A family-friendly campground with a range of accommodations from tent sites to deluxe cabins.

  4. The Franklin Street Inn Bed and Breakfast: An ideal choice for those looking for a romantic getaway, offering beautifully decorated guest rooms and homemade breakfast served daily in the dining room.

What to Buy in Janesville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to take a piece of Janesville home with you? Here are some of the things you can buy in the city:

  1. Cheese: Janesville is in the heart of cheese country, offering cheesy souvenirs and the real deal.

  2. Antiques: Love history? Check out one of Janesville’s many antique stores to browse through unique finds.

  3. Artwork: Take home a piece of Janesville’s creative spirit with something from its many galleries and community art projects.

Local Culture and Customs of Janesville

Janesville is a friendly town with Midwestern values. Make sure to bring your politeness along with you, be friendly, and helpful with fellow visitors and locals.

Best Time to Visit Janesville

The spring and fall is the ideal time to visit Janesville, as the weather is mild, and the crowds are minimal. For those fond of outdoor activities, May and September are amongst the best months to visit, as most of the city’s parks and gardens are in bloom in warm weather, and the trees are in full fall colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Janesville

Do:

  • Do visit Janesville’s historical buildings and museums.

  • Do try local food during your stay.

  • Do explore the outdoors in one of Janesville’s many parks.

  • Do be polite and friendly to other tourists and locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural areas that you visit.

  • Don’t make noise in quiet residential areas.

  • Don’t trespass on private property or historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Janesville

Overall, Janesville is a safe place to visit, but it’s wise to take standard safety precautions. Lock your car doors when parked and avoid carrying large amounts of cash with you.

Health in Janesville

There are several hospitals located in the area if you need medical assistance during your stay. Make sure insurance and identification information—both your own and that of your dependents—is accessible to you and stored in a safe and secure place.

Getting to Janesville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Janesville

The nearest airport is General Mitchell International Airport located in Milwaukee (81 miles). From this point, you can either rent a car or travel by bus to Janesville.

Bus Stations Near Janesville

Janesville Transit System (JTS) operates various routes within the city, providing connection opportunities to regional bus lines operating out of Rockford, Illinois, Madison and Milwaukee, Wisconsin.

Other Options

Janesville’s airport has recently expanded and is conveniently situated in the southern part of the city, making it possible to fly directly into the city without having to make a long journey from one of the nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Janesville

Janesville is a compact city and as such, can be easily navigable on foot or by bike. Taxis and rideshare services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Janesville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Madison: The state capital of Wisconsin boasts a great food and drink scene, an interesting museum scene, scenic bike trails, and more.

  2. Chicago: One of America’s most famous cities, Chicago is just over two hours away from Janesville, offering a range of entertainment, cultural and sports experiences.

  3. Wisconsin Dells: A popular family-friendly vacation spot about an hour and a half to the north of Janesville, offering water parks, go-karts, and other fun activities for the whole family.

Conclusion

With its beautiful scenery, charming architecture, and rich history, Janesville is a hidden gem that is worth a visit. Plan your trip to Janesville with our comprehensive guide, and you’re sure to have a memorable experience.
