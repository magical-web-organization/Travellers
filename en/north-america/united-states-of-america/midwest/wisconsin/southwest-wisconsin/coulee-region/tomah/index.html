A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Tomah, Wisconsin

Tomah is a charming city located in the beautiful Coulee Region of Southwest Wisconsin. It attracts visitors with its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and an array of cultural attractions. Here is a comprehensive guide for anyone planning to visit Tomah.

Introduction to Tomah

Tomah is a small city with a population of around 9500 people. The city is located along the interstate between Madison, Wisconsin, and Minneapolis, Minnesota. The area is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and a rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Tomah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tomah Museum and Historical Society: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the local history of Tomah. It features exhibits on the Native American tribes who once called this area home, the arrival of the railroad, and the city’s growth throughout the years.

  • Monroe County Local History Room and Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Monroe County and houses a vast collection of artifacts and photographs.

Natural Attractions of Tomah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Butterfly Bluff Natural Area: Covering 82 acres and featuring a scenic overlook, Butterfly Bluff is a popular spot for hiking, birdwatching, and enjoying the outdoors.

  • The Omaha Trail: This trail is an 18-mile former railroad bed that offers a flat, easy hike for visitors and is perfect for jogging, cycling, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tomah

In addition to the Omaha Trail mentioned above, Tomah offers several other hiking and biking routes. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Mirror Lake County Park Trail: This trail features a 2.3-mile paved loop around a beautiful lake with picturesque views.

  • Lake Tomah Loop Trail: A 4.8-acre trail around Lake Tomah with many scenic views and fun things to do.

Best Things to Do in Tomah: Activities and Tours

  • Winery Tour: Tomah is known for its wineries and offers several winery tours to visitors.

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can enjoy horseback riding through the stunning natural beauty of Tomah.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tomah

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring Tomah:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Monroe County Local History Room and Museum
2 See the sights at the Tomah Museum and Historical Society
3 Hike through Butterfly Bluff
4 Bike the Omaha Trail
5 Visit a winery
6 Try horseback riding
7 Relax and enjoy the local scenery

Food and Drink Scene of Tomah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgers: Tomah has several amazing burger joints that are worth visiting.

  • Cheese Curds: Visitors should try the cheese curds to taste the local culture of Wisconsin.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Tomah is known for its unique wineries and visitors should try the local wine.

  • Craft Beer: Tomah also has a vibrant craft beer scene, and visitors can attend local breweries to have a glass of craft beer.

Where to Stay in Tomah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Best Western Tomah Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers several amenities.

  • Hampton Inn Tomah: A mid-range hotel with modern comforts and beautiful surroundings.

What to Buy in Tomah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Honey: Tomah is famous for honey, and visitors can bring home some local honey.

  • Crafts: Tomah has several local artisans and craft shops, and visitors should bring home some of their crafts to experience the local culture firsthand.

Local Culture and Customs of Tomah

Tomah has a unique culture that celebrates its history, natural beauty, and traditions. Visitors should respect this culture and try to immerse themselves in the local customs.

Best Time to Visit Tomah

The best time to visit Tomah is during the summer months, particularly from June to August, when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tomah

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the public property.

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tomah

Tomah is a relatively safe city. Visitors should take the usual precautions and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Tomah

Tomah has several medical facilities, hospitals, and pharmacies for visitors needing medical attention.

Getting to Tomah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tomah

  • La Crosse Regional Airport (LSE) is the nearest airport to Tomah, located 55 miles southeast of the city.

Bus Stations Near Tomah

  • Tomah Bus Station is located at 202 N Superior Ave.

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire a taxi or rental car to get to Tomah.

How to Get Around in Tomah

Tomah is a small city, and visitors could easily walk to most attractions. Visitors could also rent a bicycle from local shops or hire taxis.

Where to Go Next from Tomah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Madison: A two-hour drive from Tomah, the capital of Wisconsin, is home to several museums, parks, and entertainment venues.

  • Cedarburg: Located four hours away, this charming town is known for its historic architecture, unique shops, and galleries.

Conclusion

Tomah is a paradise for those looking to explore natural beauty and experience local culture. Visitors could explore the museums, hike or bike the trails, and enjoy the food and drinks scene. We hope that this guide has helped you plan your next visit to the beautiful city of Tomah.
