Introduction to La Crosse

Nestled in the Coulee Region of Southwest Wisconsin, the city of La Crosse is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Surrounded by lush green forests, rolling hills, and the Mississippi River, La Crosse offers unparalleled natural beauty and a unique blend of culture and history.

List of Places to Visit in La Crosse

Here are the top places to visit in La Crosse:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dahl Auto Museum: features vintage cars and automobile memorabilia
  • Hixon House: a beautifully preserved Victorian mansion
  • La Crosse Children’s Museum: an interactive and educational experience for kids
  • Riverside International Friendship Gardens: a unique garden featuring international sister cities of La Crosse

Natural Attractions of La Crosse: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grandad Bluff: the best viewpoint in La Crosse with stunning views of the city and surrounding areas
  • La Crosse River State Trail: a 21-mile trail for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing
  • Myrick Park: a beautiful park with hiking trails, picnic areas, and a bird sanctuary
  • Riverside Park: located on the Mississippi River, this park offers boat launches, fishing, and stunning views

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Crosse

La Crosse offers numerous hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the area. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Great River State Trail: a 24-mile trail that winds along the Mississippi River
  • Hixon Forest Trail System: a network of trails with different difficulty levels
  • LOWER MARSH TRAIL: an easy hike perfect for birdwatching and nature photography
  • Upper Hixon Trails: a challenging trail system with steep hills and rewarding views

Best Things to Do in La Crosse: Activities and Tours

Explore the Mississippi River

Take a riverboat cruise, rent a kayak, or paddle down the Mississippi River for an unforgettable experience.

Visit Local Breweries

La Crosse is home to several craft breweries, offering a wide selection of locally brewed beers. Check out Pearl Street Brewery, Turtle Stack Brewery, and The Root Note.

Take a Food Tour

Experience the diverse culinary scene of La Crosse with a food tour that takes you to some of the best local restaurants and eateries.

Attend Festivals and events

La Crosse hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the La Crosse Riverfest, Oktoberfest, and the Great River Folk Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Crosse

Day 1: Explore the downtown area, visit the Dahl Auto Museum and Riverside International Friendship Gardens.

Day 2: Hike the Upper Hixon Trails, enjoy the views at Grandad Bluff, and grab dinner downtown.

Day 3: Rent a kayak or take a riverboat cruise on the Mississippi River.

Day 4: Visit the Hixon House, stroll through Myrick Park, and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 5: Take a food tour and sample some of La Crosse’s best cuisine.

Day 6: Go on a bike ride along the Great River State Trail and visit local breweries.

Day 7: Attend a festival or event if there is one scheduled during your visit, otherwise, relax in Riverside Park and enjoy the river views.

Food and Drink Scene of La Crosse

Must-Try Dishes

  • Viroqua Breaded Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: a local favorite served at many restaurants in La Crosse
  • Knoephla Soup: a creamy German soup with dumplings
  • Beer Cheese Soup: a hearty soup made with beer, cheese, and vegetables
  • Cheese Curds: a quintessential Wisconsin snack

Local Beverages

  • Pearl Street Brewery: a local craft brewery with a diverse selection of beers
  • Wine: La Crosse is located in the heart of Wisconsin’s wine country, with several wineries in the area.
  • Coffee: Check out The Root Note, Bean Juice Coffee House, or Cabin Coffee Co. for locally roasted coffee.

Where to Stay in La Crosse: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Radisson Hotel: located in downtown La Crosse, with comfortable rooms and high-end amenities
  • Stoney Creek Hotel and Conference Center: a rustic log-cabin themed hotel with an indoor waterpark
  • The Charmant Hotel: a boutique hotel housed in a historic building in downtown La Crosse

What to Buy in La Crosse: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Craft Beer: pick up some locally brewed beer from Pearl Street Brewery, Turtle Stack Brewery, or The Root Note
  • Maple Syrup: Wisconsin is known for its maple syrup, and La Crosse has several producers in the area
  • Cheese: take home some of Wisconsin’s famous cheese from the local farmer’s market.

Local Culture and Customs of La Crosse

La Crosse has a rich history and culture that is celebrated through festivals, museums, and local traditions. The German heritage of the area is also evident in many of the town’s restaurants and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit La Crosse

The best time to visit La Crosse is during the fall when the leaves change color and the weather is mild. The weather is also pleasant during the spring and summer months, but these are peak tourist seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Crosse

Do:

  • Do visit the local breweries and wineries
  • Do take a riverboat cruise or rent a kayak to explore the Mississippi River
  • Do try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the parks
  • Don’t speed on the scenic drives - the winding roads require careful driving
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals

Safety and Health

Safety in La Crosse

La Crosse is a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions when traveling. Avoid carrying large sums of money and valuables, lock your car, and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in La Crosse

La Crosse has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, in case of emergency. Visitors should be cautious when hiking or participating in outdoor activities and wear appropriate clothing and gear.

Getting to La Crosse: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near La Crosse

The closest airport to La Crosse is La Crosse Regional Airport (LSE), which is located within 4 miles of the city center.

Bus Stations Near La Crosse

Greyhound and Megabus have bus stations in nearby cities, including Eau Claire and Winona, that offer connections to La Crosse.

Other Options

Driving is an excellent option for those who want to explore the scenic drives and byways of the area. Rental cars and taxis are available.

How to Get Around in La Crosse

La Crosse is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. The La Crosse Municipal Transit Utility (MTU) offers bus service throughout the city, with several routes that connect to nearby communities.

Where to Go Next from La Crosse: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Winona, MN: a picturesque river town with plenty of charming attractions
  • Trempealeau, WI: a quaint town nestled on the banks of the Mississippi River
  • Viroqua, WI: a charming small town with unique shops and restaurants

Conclusion

La Crosse is a hidden gem in Southwest Wisconsin that offers visitors an exceptional blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From its scenic drives and outdoor adventures to its local cuisine and festivals, there is something for everyone in La Crosse. By following this comprehensive guide, you are sure to have a memorable and enjoyable visit to this charming city.
