Rhinelander is a hidden gem in North Central Wisconsin that offers outdoor adventures, historic landmarks, and a friendly small-town charm. Known as the “Home of the Hodag” - a legendary beast - Rhinelander is a unique destination that will delight nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and curious travelers alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the top things to do and see in Rhinelander, from natural attractions and hiking trails to cultural landmarks and local cuisine.

Introduction to Rhinelander

Located in Oneida County, Wisconsin, Rhinelander is a small city that is surrounded by lakes, forests, and rolling hills. With a population of around 7,500 people, Rhinelander is a welcoming and laid-back place where visitors can experience the beauty of the Northwoods and learn about the region’s history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Rhinelander

To help you plan your trip to Rhinelander, here are some of the top attractions and landmarks to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rhinelander is a city with a rich history, and there are many museums and landmarks that offer a glimpse into the past. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Rhinelander:

1. Rhinelander Historical Society

Learn about the history of Rhinelander and the surrounding area at the Rhinelander Historical Society. Located in a historic school building, the museum features exhibits on logging, the railroad, and Native American culture. There is also a collection of historic photographs and artifacts that offer a fascinating look at the past.

2. Camp 10 Ski Museum

For ski enthusiasts, the Camp 10 Ski Museum is a must-visit attraction. The museum is dedicated to the history of skiing in Wisconsin, and features displays of vintage skis, snowshoes, and other winter sports equipment. There is also an exhibit on snowmobiles, which were invented in Wisconsin.

3. Historic Downtown Rhinelander

Take a stroll through historic downtown Rhinelander and admire the charming brick buildings and unique shops. You can also visit the Oneida County Courthouse, which was built in 1908 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Natural Attractions of Rhinelander: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rhinelander is located in an area of Wisconsin that is renowned for its natural beauty. From lakes and rivers to forests and wildlife, there are many outdoor adventures waiting to be discovered. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Rhinelander:

1. Lake Thompson

Lake Thompson is a large freshwater lake that is popular for boating, fishing, and swimming. The lake is surrounded by forests and has several public access points, including Hodag Park, where you can enjoy a picnic or take a scenic hike.

2. Bearskin State Trail

This scenic trail runs for 18 miles through the Northwoods and is perfect for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling. The trail follows the route of an old railroad line and offers beautiful views of forests, rivers, and wetlands.

3. Nicolet National Forest

Rhinelander is located near the Nicolet National Forest, which covers over 660,000 acres of forests, lakes, and streams. The forest is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including black bears, wolves, and bald eagles, and offers many opportunities for hiking, camping, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rhinelander

If you’re looking for a scenic hike or bike ride in Rhinelander, here are some of the top trails to explore:

1. Hiawatha Trail

This 6-mile trail follows the route of an old railroad line and offers scenic views of Lake George and the surrounding forests.

2. CAVOC Trails

These trails are located in the Rhinelander Hodag Park and offer over 6 miles of hiking and mountain biking trails. The trails wind through forests and wetlands and offer beautiful views of Lake Thompson.

3. Almon Park Trailhead

This trailhead is located in the Nicolet National Forest and offers access to several hiking and biking trails, including the Anvil Lake Trail and the Franklin Lake Trail.

Best Things to Do in Rhinelander: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fun activities and tours to try in Rhinelander, here are some of the top options:

1. Hodag Country Festival

This annual festival celebrates country music and attracts top performers from around the country. The festival takes place in July and features live music, food vendors, and a carnival.

2. Rhinelander Brewfest

This yearly event showcases local craft breweries and features live music, food trucks, and games. It’s a great way to sample some of the best beers in the region and enjoy a fun afternoon with friends.

3. Wilderness Tours

Explore the Northwoods wilderness with a guided tour of the Nicolet National Forest or the Bearskin State Trail. There are many tour companies in Rhinelander that offer hiking, biking, and snowmobiling tours through this beautiful region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rhinelander

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Rhinelander, here is a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Rhinelander and explore historic downtown, including the Oneida County Courthouse and the Rhinelander Historical Society.

Day 2: Rent a boat or go fishing on Lake Thompson, then enjoy a picnic lunch at Hodag Park.

Day 3: Hike the Hiawatha Trail or the CAVOC Trails in the morning, then take a guided tour of the Nicolet National Forest in the afternoon.

Day 4: Explore the Camp 10 Ski Museum and learn about the history of skiing in Wisconsin.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive through the Northwoods and enjoy the fall colors.

Day 6: Visit the Bearskin State Trail and enjoy a bike ride or hike through the beautiful forests.

Day 7: Attend a local festival, such as the Hodag Country Festival or the Rhinelander Brewfest.

Food and Drink Scene of Rhinelander

Rhinelander offers a range of delicious local cuisine and craft beer options. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Friday Night Fish Fry

A Wisconsin tradition, the Friday Night Fish Fry is a must-try dish when you’re in Rhinelander. You can find this dish at many local restaurants, including the Al-Gen Dinner Club and the Cross Country Bar & Grill.

2. Hodag Burger

This iconic burger is named after the legendary creature and is available at many local restaurants. It typically comes with bacon, cheese, and barbecue sauce.

3. Cheese Curds

Another Wisconsin classic, cheese curds are a must-try snack in Rhinelander. You can find them at many local restaurants, including Dairy Queen and Culver’s.

Local Beverages

1. Rhinelander Beer

This local craft beer is brewed in Rhinelander and is available at many local bars and restaurants. It’s a great way to sample some of the best beer in the region.

2. Wisconsin Wines

Wisconsin is home to many great wineries, including the Three Lakes Winery and the Rock Island Winery. You can sample these wines at local bars and restaurants, or visit the wineries themselves for a tasting.

3. Craft Cocktails

Many local bars and restaurants in Rhinelander offer craft cocktails made with local ingredients, such as cranberries, honey, and herbs.

What to Buy in Rhinelander: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home some local souvenirs and products from Rhinelander, here are some of the top options:

1. Hodag Souvenirs

You can find a range of Hodag-themed souvenirs at local gift shops, including t-shirts, hats, and stuffed animals.

2. Wisconsin Cheese

Wisconsin is famous for its cheese, and you can find a range of local cheeses at many local shops and markets.

3. Cranberry Products

Wisconsin is also a major producer of cranberries, and you can find a range of cranberry products, including jam, juice, and candy.

Local Culture and Customs of Rhinelander

Rhinelander has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its local festivals, traditions, and customs. Here are some of the most important cultural events and customs in Rhinelander:

1. Hodag Country Festival

This annual festival celebrates country music and attracts visitors from around the country. It’s a great way to experience the local culture and enjoy live music, food, and games.

2. Friday Night Fish Fry

As mentioned earlier, the Friday Night Fish Fry is a Wisconsin tradition that is deeply ingrained in the local culture. Many local restaurants offer this dish every Friday night.

3. Snowmobiling

Snowmobiling is a popular winter sport in Rhinelander, and many local residents take part in this pastime every year. It’s a great way to explore the Northwoods wilderness.

Best Time to Visit Rhinelander

The best time to visit Rhinelander depends on your preferences and interests. Here is a breakdown of the seasons and what they offer:

Spring (March-May)

Spring is a great time to visit Rhinelander if you enjoy birdwatching and hiking. The weather is mild, and there are many migratory birds that pass through the area.

Summer (June-August)

Summer is the peak season in Rhinelander, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy, including swimming, boating, and fishing. The weather is warm, and there are many festivals and events to attend.

Fall (September-November)

Fall is a great time to visit Rhinelander if you enjoy fall colors and hiking. The leaves change color in September and October, and the forests are ablaze with color.

Winter (December-February)

Winter is a great time to visit Rhinelander if you enjoy snowmobiling, skiing, and other winter sports. The snow is deep, and the Northwoods is a winter wonderland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rhinelander

When visiting Rhinelander, there are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do try some local cuisine, including the Friday Night Fish Fry and the Hodag Burger.
  • Do attend a local festival or event to experience the local culture.

  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions, as this can be seen as disrespectful.

Safety and Health

Rhinelander is generally a safe and healthy destination, but there are some important safety and health issues to keep in mind:

Safety in Rhinelander

  • Be cautious when hiking or biking on remote trails, and always carry a map and compass.
  • Use caution when swimming in lakes or rivers, as the water can be cold and deep.
  • Use common sense when interacting with wildlife, and always give animals plenty of space.

Health in Rhinelander

  • Protect yourself from tick bites when hiking or spending time outdoors.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Rhinelander: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rhinelander is located about three hours north of Madison, Wisconsin, and about four hours northwest of Milwaukee. Here are some of the transportation options for getting to Rhinelander:

Airports Near Rhinelander

The Rhinelander-Oneida County Airport (RHI) is a small regional airport that offers flights to and from Minneapolis-St. Paul and Detroit. The airport is located just a few miles from downtown Rhinelander.

Bus Stations Near Rhinelander

The closest bus station to Rhinelander is located in Wausau, about 50 miles to the south. From there, you can take a taxi or rental car to Rhinelander.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Rhinelander, the city is located off of Highway 8 and is easily accessible from other major highways in the region.

How to Get Around in Rhinelander

The best way to get around Rhinelander is by car or bike. There are several rental car companies in the area, as well as bike rental shops that offer a range of bicycles for rent.

Where to Go Next from Rhinelander: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other destinations in the region, here are some options:

1. Minocqua

Located about 25 miles south of Rhinelander, Minocqua is a charming small town that offers shopping, dining, and outdoor activities.

2. Eagle River

About 35 miles north of Rhinelander, Eagle River is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and skiing.

3. Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Located about four hours north of Rhinelander, this national park offers stunning views of Lake Superior and the surrounding islands.
