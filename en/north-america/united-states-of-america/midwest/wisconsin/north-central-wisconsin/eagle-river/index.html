Introduction to Eagle River

Nestled in the heart of Wisconsin, Eagle River is a picturesque town that has been a beloved vacation spot for generations. Known as the “Snowmobile Capital of the World,” Eagle River is a year-round destination that offers a wide range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local experiences.

Whether you are looking for a scenic spot to relax or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, Eagle River has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local customs to help you plan a memorable trip to Eagle River.

List of Places to Visit in Eagle River

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eagle River has a rich cultural history, and there are several museums and historical sites that showcase the town’s heritage. One of the must-visit attractions is the Eagle River Historical Society Museum, which displays a collection of artifacts and memorabilia from the town’s past. Other noteworthy sites include the Northwoods Children’s Museum, which offers hands-on exhibits and educational programs for kids, and the Almon Park Historical District, which is home to several historical buildings and landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Eagle River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eagle River is surrounded by pristine natural beauty, and visitors can fully experience it through a variety of outdoor activities. Lakes such as the Eagle River Chain of Lakes and Crystal Lake offer ample opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing. The Nicolet National Forest is also nearby, offering miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as prime hunting spots during the fall.

For the adventurous, the town provides numerous outdoor activities like ATV tours in Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest or zip-lining and kayaking Eagle River. Eagle River truly offers something for everyone.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eagle River

Some of the most memorable experiences in Eagle River involve immersing yourself in the serene landscapes through various hiking and biking trails. Biking along the Three Eagle Trail, a trail that offers a scenic route connecting Three Lakes and Eagle River, narrating stories of Native Americans and early European settlers. The Anvil Lake Trail, located in the southwestern part of the Nicolet National Forest, encompasses spectacular lake views and has challenging trails for mountain bike lovers.

Best Things to Do in Eagle River: Activities and Tours

Eagle River offers a plethora of experiences, from educational tours to adventurous activities, that will leave you with unforgettable memories and experiences. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Cranberry Marsh Tours: Discover the history behind Wisconsin’s cranberry industry and how it remains a vital part of the town’s culture.
  • Snowmobiling: When visiting in the winter, Eagle River should not miss snowmobiling atop frozen lakes and through Viletta Drive, claimed to be the “World Championship Snowmobile Derby” track. Locals would argue that it must be added to everyone’s bucket list.
  • Ice Fishing: When the lakes are frozen over, ice fishing is a must-do activity. Experience catching panfish, northern pike, and walleye through the ice.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eagle River

A potential itinerary for your week-long trip to Eagle River could look like this:

  • Day 1: Enjoy the beauty of the Eagle River Chain of Lakes by renting a boat, relaxing on a beach, or fishing.
  • Day 2: Take a bike ride along the Three Eagle Trail and spend the evening enjoying the theaters at Lake Forest Park.
  • Day 3: Enjoy kayaking on Eagle River, followed by an evening at the high ropes course drifting above the trees.
  • Day 4: Visit the Northwoods Children’s Museum in the morning, and then enjoy a leisurely day exploring the unique shops and restaurants of Eagle River.
  • Day 5: Take a cranberry marsh tour and exploring the country roads of Wisconsin’s country landscape
  • Day 6: Spend the day in Northwoods Zip Lines, navigating over six zipline bridges and experiencing the ‘Sky High’ tour.
  • Day 7: Visit the Almon Park Historical District and learn about Eagle River’s rich history before having a night out at the local entertainment spot.

Food and Drink Scene of Eagle River

Eagle River has a great culinary culture, and there are many local dishes and beverages that you must try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wisconsin Fish Fry: This classic Wisconsin dish is a must-try when visiting Eagle River. Savor fresh, flaky fish served with a side of traditional slaw and fries.
  • Cranberry Stuffed French Toast: A delicious breakfast dish that features french toast stuffed with cranberry sauce and served with a side of syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Eagle River’s craft brewery scene is taking the town by storm and offers delicious choices of Amber Ale, Hefeweizen, and Stout.
  • Cranberry Juice: As cranberry farming is prominent in the town, cranberry juice is a must-try when visiting the area.

Where to Stay in Eagle River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eagle River has plenty of options for accommodations, ranging from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels.

  • Best Western Derby Inn & Suites offers a convenient location and amenities like swimming pools, hot tubs, and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Eagle Waters Resort is perfect for travelers seeking more spacious options like cabins or condos, tucked into a quiet bay of Voyageur Lake.
  • If you’re looking for a romantic option, Wild Eagle Lodge Resort’s spa and wellness center offers a peaceful setting for guests to relax and unwind.

What to Buy in Eagle River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eagle River is a treasure trove of unique local products and souvenirs that make excellent mementos of your trip. Some great options include locally-made cranberry wine and merchandise from the Snowmobile Hall of Fame, including apparel and miniature snowmobiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Eagle River

Eagle River is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, and visitors should embrace and respect local customs. Wisconsin is famous for supper clubs, which are casual dining restaurants featuring hearty comfort food. Another Wisconsin tradition is the polka dance and other traditional dances that can be discovered when browsing the local events calendars.

Best Time to Visit Eagle River

Eagle River is a year-round destination, with each season bringing its own unique charm. Summer offers plenty of outdoor activities like boating, fishing, and hiking, while fall is a prime time for hunting and enjoying the fall foliage. Winter is perfect for snowmobiling, skijoring, and ice fishing, and Spring brings the blooming of cranberry plants where you will get to see spring blossoms and velvet leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eagle River

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the area; follow the Leave No Trace principles when exploring the outdoors.
  • Support local businesses by patronizing hotels, restaurants, and retail shops.
  • Dress appropriately for the season, as the weather can vary widely in Wisconsin.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in the natural areas;
  • Overuse natural trails and other activities, causing harm to wildlife and sensitive environments.
  • Ignore local customs and beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eagle River

Eagle River is generally a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should always practice caution while exploring the outdoors and follow posted safety warnings/trail markers. Snowmobiling can be particularly risky, and visitors should always wear helmets and operate their vehicles safely.

Health in Eagle River

Visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure that they remain healthy during their stay. Mosquitoes and ticks are common in the Wisconsin wilderness, and visitors should use repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid insect bites. In times of snowfall, dress in layers and cover exposed skin to prevent hypothermia.

Getting to Eagle River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eagle River

Travelers flying to Eagle River can use the Rhinelander-Oneida County Airport (RHI), which is located approximately 30 minutes south of Eagle River.

Bus Stations Near Eagle River

There are no direct bus routes from major cities leading to Eagle River.

Other Options

Renting a car or driving your vehicle to Eagle River is the most viable option, allowing you to explore the town and its surroundings at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Eagle River

Eagle River has several car and bike rental services, taxis, and limousine services. Some beautiful spots and theatres are easily walkable.

Where to Go Next from Eagle River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are extending your visit to the Midwest, there are several nearby destinations that you can include in your itinerary, such as Door County, Milwaukee, and Michigan. Wisconsin Dells, known as the waterpark capital of the world, offers a day trip full of amusement parks, water activities, and shopping options for the whole family.

Conclusion

Eagle River is a hidden gem in Wisconsin and has much to offer for travelers seeking natural beauty, outdoor adventure, local culture, and unforgettable experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide in hand, you can make the most out of your trip to Eagle River and explore all the fantastic experiences it has to offer.
