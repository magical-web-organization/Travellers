Introduction to Hurley

Welcome to Hurley, a charming small town located in North Central Wisconsin, United States. This historic mining town rich in culture and natural beauty offers myriad activities for travelers. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, a relaxing getaway, or a glimpse into local history, Hurley has you covered. In this travel guide, we provide a comprehensive itinerary to make your trip a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Hurley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Iron County Historical Museum: This museum showcases the region’s mining history with its extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits.

  2. Old Iron Bridge: The bridge was built in 1887 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

  3. Historic Courthouse: Built in the late 1800s, this iconic courthouse is a treasure trove for those interested in architecture and history.

  4. Northern Wisconsin Veterans Memorial Cemetery: A peaceful and solemn place to pay respects to the brave men and women who served the country.

Natural Attractions of Hurley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Copper Falls State Park: A favorite among hikers, this park offers a variety of trails that wind through forests, waterfalls, and streams.

  2. Black River Harbor: A scenic harbor that offers picturesque views of Lake Superior.

  3. Penokee Mountain Range: Known for its scenic beauty, this range offers a variety of outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, and hunting.

  4. Potato River Falls: A beautiful waterfall that’s perfect for taking pictures and enjoying nature’s beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hurley

  1. Iron Belle Trail: A great trail for hiking and biking that runs 1,273 miles from Detroit to the Upper Peninsula of Michigan.

  2. Powderhorn Trail: A scenic bike trail that runs through the Chequamegon National Forest.

  3. Houghton Falls Nature Preserve Trail: A beautiful waterfall and hiking trail that offers stunning views and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Hurley: Activities and Tours

  1. Hurley Historical Society Walking Tour: An excellent way to learn about the town’s mining history and architecture.

  2. Snowmobile Tours: Hurley is known for its excellent snowmobiling trails and tours.

  3. Wildlife Tours: A chance to see the native animals of the region, including black bears, wolves, and moose.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hurley

Day 1: Visit the Iron County Historical Museum and learn about the town’s mining history.
Day 2: Explore Copper Falls State Park and hike the trails.
Day 3: Take a scenic drive to Black River Harbor and enjoy the views.
Day 4: Visit the Northern Wisconsin Veterans Memorial Cemetery.
Day 5: Go snowmobiling in the winter or hiking or biking in the summer.
Day 6: Take a wildlife tour and experience the local fauna.
Day 7: End your trip with a relaxing day at one of Hurley’s local spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Hurley

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pasties: A traditional English dish consisting of meat and vegetables wrapped in pastry.

  2. Fried Cheese Curds: A regional favorite, these are cheese curds battered and fried for a crispy outside and gooey center.

Local Beverages

  1. Leinenkugel’s Beer: A Wisconsin-based beer brewed since 1867.

  2. Wisconsin Wines: The region is home to numerous wineries, including White Winter Winery, which specializes in mead.

Where to Stay in Hurley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Iron Nugget: A historic hotel that’s been recently renovated and offers a unique experience.

  2. Days Inn by Wyndham Hurley: Affordable and convenient hotel with all the modern amenities.

  3. Gogebic Lodge: A cozy and rustic lodge that’s perfect for those seeking a nature retreat.

What to Buy in Hurley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Copper Jewelry: Make your own jewelry or buy a pre-made piece from one of Hurley’s local jewelry shops.

  2. Maple Syrup: A staple of the region, Hurley’s maple syrup is produced locally and is a popular souvenir for visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Hurley

Hurley is a small town with a tight-knit community that takes pride in its mining history and natural beauty. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including taking off your shoes before entering someone’s home and always addressing people by their proper titles.

Best Time to Visit Hurley

The best time to visit Hurley is during the summer and fall months, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is stunning. However, winter in Hurley offers excellent snowmobiling and skiing opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hurley

Do:

  1. Learn about the town’s mining history at the Iron County Historical Museum.

  2. Take a wildlife tour and experience the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter and respect the natural beauty of the region.

  2. Don’t be disrespectful to the local community and their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hurley

Hurley is a safe town, but visitors should still take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Always carry a cell phone and inform someone of your plans before setting out.

Health in Hurley

Visitors should be aware of the potential for mosquito-borne illness and take precautions like wearing insect repellent.

Getting to Hurley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hurley

  1. Duluth International Airport - 130 miles away

  2. Rhinelander-Oneida County Airport - 109 miles away

Bus Stations Near Hurley

  1. Hurley Greyhound Station - 2 miles away

Other Options

  1. Car rental - Visitors can rent a car at the Duluth International Airport and drive to Hurley.

How to Get Around in Hurley

Visitors can get around Hurley on foot or by car. Biking is also a popular option for those looking to explore the surrounding trails.

Where to Go Next from Hurley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Apostle Islands - Explore the stunning sea caves and lighthouses of this group of 22 islands on Lake Superior.

  2. Porcupine Mountains - A wilderness area with hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning views.

Conclusion

We hope this guide has given you the information you need to plan your trip to Hurley, Wisconsin. From historic mining sites to scenic biking and hiking trails, Hurley offers something for everyone. Make sure to respect the local community and enjoy your time in this beautiful part of the country.
