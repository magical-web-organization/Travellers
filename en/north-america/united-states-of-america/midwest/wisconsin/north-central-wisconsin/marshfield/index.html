Introduction to Marshfield

Nestled in the heartland of Wisconsin, Marshfield is a charming small town known for its warm hospitality, scenic beauty, and a range of outdoor adventures. The town’s rich history, coupled with its thriving culture and dynamic local life, makes it a perfect vacation destination for people of all ages.

List of Places to Visit in Marshfield

From historical landmarks to natural attractions, Marshfield is home to a diverse range of places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marshfield boasts numerous cultural landmarks and historical places that offer a glimpse into its rich heritage. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • Jurustic Park: A unique outdoor museum featuring larger-than-life metal sculptures of prehistoric creatures.
  • World’s Largest Round Barn: A beautiful historic building and one of the largest round barns in America.
  • Wildwood Park and Zoo: A stunning park containing a vibrant zoo and botanical gardens that offer a range of fun activities and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Marshfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Marshfield is a haven for nature lovers, offering a host of activities that take advantage of the region’s natural beauty. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • McMillan Marsh Wildlife Area: An expansive wetland area offering excellent bird-watching opportunities and scenic views.
  • Ice Age Trail: A 31-mile trail through the beautiful Plover River State Fishery Area.
  • Braem Park: A beautiful park in the heart of Marshfield with walking trails, a playground, and a disc golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marshfield

Looking for an adventure in nature? Marshfield has a range of hiking and biking routes for all skill levels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Wildwood Park Trail: A beautiful trail that provides a 2.3-mile loop around Wildwood Park and Zoo.
  • McMillan Trail: A well-maintained trail that winds through the McMillan Wildlife Area.
  • Newingham Trail: A scenic trail that provides access to the Plover River State Fishery Area.

Best Things to Do in Marshfield: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing activity or an adrenaline-fueled adventure, Marshfield has something for everyone. Check out these must-try activities:

  • Visit the Jurustic Park, which offers a unique outdoor museum featuring larger-than-life metal sculptures of prehistoric creatures.
  • Take a walk on the Wildwood Trail to exploring wildlife habitat and native flowers in Wildwood Park.
  • Learn about the history of loggerheads and their contributions to American history at the Historical Upham Mansion and Garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Marshfield

Marshfield’s food and drink scene is a reflection of its vibrant culture and local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese curds: A Wisconsin staple, cheese curds are deep-fried nuggets of cheese.
  • Brats: A sausage made from pork and beef, often grilled or cooked in beer.
  • Kringle: A sweet Danish pastry filled with nuts, fruit, or almond paste.

Local Beverages

  • Wisconsin Beer: Known for its great hops, Wisconsin has a thriving craft beer scene.
  • Wisconsin Wine: Wisconsin offers a range of wine varieties produced from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Marshfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marshfield offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Hotel Marshfield: A boutique hotel with a modern yet cozy ambiance and exceptional amenities.
  • Holiday Inn & Suites: A comfortable hotel with a swimming pool, restaurant, and fitness center.
  • Mountain Bay Inn: A bed and breakfast located close to Marshfield’s downtown.

What to Buy in Marshfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home that encapsulate the spirit of Marshfield? Here are a few suggestions:

  • Cheese curds: Authentic Wisconsin cheese curds are a must-have souvenir to bring home with you.
  • Beer or wine: Pick up a local brew or vintage to savor the flavors of Marshfield at home.
  • Maple syrup: A delicious and iconic product given that Wisconsin is one of America’s leading maple-syrup producers.
  • Arts and crafts: Marshfield has a thriving local art scene featuring pottery, paintings, and other art.

Local Culture and Customs of Marshfield

Marshfield is proud of its rich cultural heritage, ranging from its iconic giant sculptures to its traditional fairs and festivals. Marshfield’s customs include being friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Marshfield

The summer months from late May through August are the best times to visit Marshfield when the weather is warm and there are various outdoor events happening throughout the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marshfield

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Learn about Marshfield’s rich heritage.
  • Enjoy the outdoor adventures and beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm wildlife in any way.
  • Neglect to follow safety protocols when exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marshfield

Marshfield is known for being a safe town. Still, it’s always a good practice to pay attention to your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Marshfield

Marshfield Clinic is a renowned healthcare facility located in the city, and there are numerous pharmacies and medical centers found throughout the town.

Getting to Marshfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marshfield is easily accessible by road, air, or bus.

Airports Near Marshfield

  • Central Wisconsin Airport (CWA): About 30 miles away from Marshfield
  • Chippewa Valley Regional Airport (EAU): About 80 miles west of Marshfield

Bus Stations Near Marshfield

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located at 517 N Central Avenue in Marshfield

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Car rentals are available at the Central Wisconsin Airport and throughout Marshfield.

How to Get Around in Marshfield

While it’s easy to get around Marshfield on foot, if you’re looking for other transportation options, there are several taxi or car rental companies available.

Where to Go Next from Marshfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marshfield is located in the heart of Wisconsin, making it an excellent starting point for several nearby destinations, including:

  • Green Bay: About two and a half hours by car from Marshfield, Green Bay is famous for its football culture and beautiful waterfront area.
  • Milwaukee: About two hours by car from Marshfield, Milwaukee offers a range of attractions, including an excellent food scene, breweries, museums, and art galleries.
  • Wisconsin Dells: About an hour and a half away by car from Marshfield, Wisconsin Dells is famous for its stunning natural beauty and range of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

We hope this travel guide has inspired you to visit Marshfield soon. Whether you’re after outdoor adventures, scenic drives, or delicious local cuisine, Marshfield offers something for every type of traveler. Don’t wait any longer—start planning your trip to this charming Wisconsin town today!
