Introduction to Wisconsin

Wisconsin, located in the Midwest region of the United States, is known for its natural beauty, diverse culture, and friendly people. It is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, foodies, and families. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an action-packed adventure, Wisconsin has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Wisconsin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wisconsin is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the state’s rich culture and heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Milwaukee Art Museum - This award-winning museum is known for its impressive collection of over 35,000 works of art, from ancient to contemporary.
  • Wisconsin State Capitol - Located in Madison, the Wisconsin State Capitol is a majestic building with a rich history. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the state’s government and architecture.
  • Harley-Davidson Museum - This museum in Milwaukee is a must-visit for motorcycle enthusiasts. It features a vast collection of Harley-Davidson motorcycles and artifacts.
  • Old World Wisconsin - This living history museum in Eagle lets visitors experience life in Wisconsin’s past. With more than 60 historic buildings and reenactors, it’s a great place to learn about Wisconsin’s pioneer days.

Natural Attractions of Wisconsin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wisconsin is home to many national parks, forests, lakes, and scenic areas, making it a paradise for nature lovers. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Wisconsin Dells - This popular vacation spot is known for its stunning natural beauty and thrilling waterparks. Take a boat tour through the towering cliffs, or hike through the lush forest trails.
  • Cave of the Mounds - Discovered in 1939, this natural limestone cave in Blue Mounds is a must-see for its stunning formations. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the cave’s unique features.
  • Devil’s Lake State Park - Located in Baraboo, this park offers breathtaking views of the 360-acre lake and surrounding hills. Visitors can hike the trails, swim, fish, or camp.
  • Apostle Islands National Lakeshore - Consisting of 21 islands and 12 miles of mainland, this national park in Bayfield is a popular destination for sailing, kayaking, hiking, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wisconsin

Wisconsin is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The state offers thousands of miles of scenic trails and routes, suitable for all skill levels. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Wisconsin include:

  • Ice Age Trail - This 1,200-mile trail winds through Wisconsin’s glacial landscape. It is a great destination for hikers and backpackers.
  • Elroy-Sparta State Trail - This 32-mile trail is one of the first “Rails-to-Trails” projects in the United States. Visitors can hike, bike, or snowmobile through tunnels and scenic routes.
  • Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest - This massive forest covers over a million acres and offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking amidst stunning scenery.
  • Great River State Trail - This 24-mile trail takes visitors along the Mississippi River, providing plenty of opportunities for scenic views and wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in Wisconsin: Activities and Tours

Wisconsin has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors. Some of the best things to do in Wisconsin include:

  • Wisconsin Door County Wine Trail - Explore the vineyards and wineries of Wisconsin’s Door County. Taste some of the state’s finest wines and take in the scenic views along the way.
  • Wisconsin Cheese Tour - Enjoy Wisconsin’s famous cheese by taking a tour of the state’s cheese factories. Visitors can see how cheese is made, taste different varieties, and even take some home.
  • Horse-drawn Carriage Tour - Explore the charming town of Cedarburg by taking a horse-drawn carriage tour. Learn about the town’s history, architecture, and culture while enjoying a peaceful ride.
  • Wisconsin Brew Tour - Visit some of Wisconsin’s top craft breweries and taste their signature beers. Learn about the brewing process, try some local snacks, and meet some of the state’s beer lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wisconsin

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Wisconsin, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Milwaukee, visit the Milwaukee Art Museum, Harley-Davidson Museum, and enjoy some local cuisine.
  • Day 3-4: Visit Door County, explore the vineyards, enjoy a cheese tour, and soak in the natural beauty of the area.
  • Day 5-6: Travel to Madison, explore the Wisconsin State Capitol, Olbrich Botanical Gardens, and the University of Wisconsin-Madison campus.
  • Day 7: Visit Wisconsin Dells and enjoy a day of hiking, swimming, and waterpark fun.

Food and Drink Scene of Wisconsin

Must-Try Dishes

Wisconsin is known for its delicious and hearty cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Bratwurst - This classic Wisconsin dish is a sausage made of a mixture of pork, beef, or veal. It is usually grilled or boiled and served with sauerkraut and mustard.
  • Cheese Curds - These deep-fried cheese bites are a popular snack in Wisconsin. They are made from fresh cheddar cheese and are best served hot and crispy.
  • Fish Fry - This classic dish is a must-try for seafood lovers. It features battered and deep-fried fish, usually served with fries and coleslaw.
  • Butter Burger - This juicy burger is made with a mixture of beef and butter, making it a Wisconsin delight. It is usually topped with American cheese and served on a buttered bun.

Local Beverages

Wisconsin is also known for its craft beer and cheese. Visitors can enjoy a variety of local beverages, including:

  • Wisconsin Beer - Wisconsin has a thriving craft beer scene, with over 170 breweries across the state. Some of the top breweries include New Glarus Brewing Company, Lakefront Brewery, and Central Waters Brewing Company.
  • Wisconsin Cheese - Wisconsin produces over 600 types of cheese, making it the top cheese-producing state in the country. Visitors can taste a variety of local cheeses by taking a cheese tour or visiting a local market.

Where to Stay in Wisconsin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wisconsin has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • The Iron Horse Hotel - This boutique hotel in Milwaukee blends modern luxury with industrial charm. It is located near several popular attractions and features a full-service spa, restaurant, and bar.
  • The Edgewater Hotel - Located on the shores of Lake Mendota in Madison, this historic hotel offers stunning views and luxurious amenities. It features a rooftop terrace, spa, and several dining options.
  • Hostelling International-Madison - This hostel in Madison is a great option for budget-conscious travelers. It offers private rooms and dormitories and is located near several popular attractions.
  • The Osthoff Resort - This family-friendly resort in Elkhart Lake offers a variety of activities, including golf, spa treatments, and water sports. It features luxurious suites, several dining options, and a kids’ club.

What to Buy in Wisconsin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wisconsin is known for its unique and delicious products that are perfect for souvenirs. Some of the top items to bring home include:

  • Cheese - Wisconsin’s famous cheese is a must-try, and it also makes a great souvenir. Visitors can bring home a variety of award-winning cheeses, including cheddar, gouda, and blue cheese.
  • Craft Beer - Wisconsin’s craft beer scene is thriving, and visitors can bring home a variety of local brews. Some of the popular breweries include New Glarus Brewing Company, Lakefront Brewery, and Central Waters Brewing Company.
  • Cranberries - Wisconsin is the top producer of cranberries in the country, and visitors can bring home a variety of cranberry products, such as cranberry sauce, juice, and dried cranberries.
  • Maple Syrup - Wisconsin produces high-quality maple syrup, and visitors can bring home a bottle as a souvenir. It is also a great gift for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Wisconsin

Wisconsin has a rich history and diverse culture. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Bratwurst Festivals - Wisconsin celebrates its German heritage by organizing several bratwurst festivals throughout the year. These festivals feature live music, food, and beer, and are a great way to experience Wisconsin’s culture.
  • Tailgating - Wisconsin is known for its love of football, and tailgating is a popular tradition. Fans gather in parking lots outside of the stadium before a game to grill, play games, and socialize.
  • Supper Clubs - Supper clubs are a unique Wisconsin tradition. These restaurants offer a cozy atmosphere where visitors can enjoy a leisurely meal and socialize with their fellow diners.

Best Time to Visit Wisconsin

The best time to visit Wisconsin depends on your interests and preferences. However, the peak tourist season is from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. Fall is also a popular time to visit Wisconsin, as the leaves change colors and the weather is pleasant. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it is a great time to enjoy winter sports and holiday events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wisconsin

Do:

  • Do try Wisconsin cheese and craft beer
  • Do visit the local farmers’ markets and festivals
  • Do explore Wisconsin’s natural beauty by hiking, biking, or boating
  • Do take a road trip to enjoy Wisconsin’s scenic routes

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment
  • Don’t disrespect the locals or their customs
  • Don’t miss out on Wisconsin’s famous dishes, such as bratwurst and fish fry

Safety and Health

Safety in Wisconsin

Wisconsin is a safe state for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions. Some safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas
  • Lock your valuables in a safe place when you’re not using them
  • Follow the posted speed limits and traffic laws
  • Stay safe while enjoying outdoor activities, such as hiking or boating

Health in Wisconsin

Wisconsin has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors are encouraged to purchase travel insurance before their trip. It’s also essential to take precautions to avoid illnesses, such as:

  • Practice good hygiene, such as washing your hands regularly and covering your mouth and nose when you cough or sneeze
  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot weather
  • Be cautious around wildlife, such as ticks, mosquitoes, and other insects

Getting to Wisconsin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wisconsin is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Some of the transportation options include:

Airports Near Wisconsin

  • General Mitchell International Airport, located in Milwaukee, is the largest airport in Wisconsin.
  • Dane County Regional Airport, located in Madison, is a smaller airport but offers several flights to major cities.
  • Austin Straubel International Airport, located in Green Bay, is another option for travelers to the northeastern part of the state.

Bus Stations Near Wisconsin

  • Greyhound, which operates bus services throughout the United States, has several stations in Wisconsin, including Milwaukee, Madison, and Green Bay.
  • Megabus also operates regular services to Wisconsin from several cities in the Midwest.

Other Options

Wisconsin is also connected to neighboring states by several highways and interstates. Amtrak also provides train service to several cities in Wisconsin, including Milwaukee and La Crosse.

How to Get Around in Wisconsin

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors who want to explore Wisconsin’s scenic routes and attractions. However, other options include:

  • Bus - Several cities in Wisconsin have bus systems, including Milwaukee and Madison.
  • Ridesharing - Services like Uber and Lyft operate in Wisconsin’s major cities.
  • Biking/Walking - Many cities in Wisconsin, including Madison, have well-maintained bike and pedestrian paths.

Where to Go Next from Wisconsin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, consider visiting some nearby destinations from Wisconsin, such as:

  • Chicago, Illinois - Just across the border, Chicago is a vibrant city with many attractions, including the Willis Tower and Navy Pier.
  • Minneapolis, Minnesota - This city is known for its vibrant arts scene, stunning lakes, and parks. It’s also home to the famous Mall of America.
  • Upper Peninsula, Michigan - This area is located just across Lake Michigan and offers stunning scenery, such as Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore and Tahquamenon Falls.

Conclusion

Wisconsin is a diverse and beautiful state that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, food, or culture, Wisconsin has it all. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your perfect trip to Wisconsin and experience the best it has to offer.
