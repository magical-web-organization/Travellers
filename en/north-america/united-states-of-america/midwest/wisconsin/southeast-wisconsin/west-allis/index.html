Introduction to West Allis

Located in Southeast Wisconsin, West Allis is a small yet vibrant city that combines natural beauty with a rich cultural heritage. Founded in the 19th century, this city is known for its friendly people, quaint neighborhoods, and a relaxed pace of life. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, West Allis has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in West Allis

Here are the top places to visit in West Allis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wisconsin State Fair Park: This sprawling park hosts a variety of events throughout the year, including the annual Wisconsin State Fair, one of the largest state fairs in the country.
  • West Allis Farmers Market: A local institution since 1919, this farmer’s market offers a wide range of fresh produce, flowers, and other goods from local vendors.
  • Milwaukee Mile Speedway: A historic racetrack that has been hosting races since 1903, Milwaukee Mile Speedway is a must-visit destination for motorsports fans.

Natural Attractions of West Allis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Root River Parkway: This picturesque parkway offers hiking, bird watching, and biking opportunities, as well as scenic views of the Root River.
  • Greenfield Park: With its lush greenery, lakes, and picnic areas, Greenfield Park is a favorite destination for families and nature lovers.
  • Konkel Park: Another great green space in West Allis, Konkel Park offers walking trails, a playground, and a splash pad for kids.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Allis

West Allis boasts a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Oak Leaf Trail: This 125-mile trail runs through some of the most scenic parts of Milwaukee County, including West Allis.
  • Hank Aaron State Trail: Named after the legendary baseball player, the Hank Aaron State Trail is a 14-mile trail that runs from Milwaukee’s lakefront to the western suburbs.
  • Root River Parkway Trail: This 13-mile trail winds through the Root River Parkway, offering scenic views and a chance to spot local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in West Allis: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in West Allis:

  • Explore the local food scene: West Allis is known for its diverse dining options, from classic American fare to ethnic cuisine. Some popular restaurants include West Allis Cheese & Sausage Shoppe, Sobelman’s Pub & Grill, and Thai-namite.
  • Take a brewery tour: Milwaukee is known as the beer capital of the world, and West Allis is no exception. Take a brewery tour at one of the local breweries, such as Raised Grain Brewing or Westallion Brewing Company.
  • Attend a festival: West Allis hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, including the Wisconsin State Fair, West Allis A La Carte, and the West Allis Downtown Farmers Market.

Food and Drink Scene of West Allis

Must-Try Dishes

When in West Allis, be sure to try these local specialties:

  • Wisconsin-style cheese curds: These deep-fried cheese curds are a classic Wisconsin snack, and West Allis Cheese & Sausage Shoppe is a great place to try them.
  • Bratwurst: Milwaukee and West Allis are known for their bratwurst, and Sobelman’s Pub & Grill serves up some of the best.
  • Kringles: A Danish pastry that is popular in Wisconsin, kringles can be found at O&H Bakery in nearby Racine.

Local Beverages

No trip to West Allis is complete without trying some of the local brews. Some of the best breweries in the area include:

  • Raised Grain Brewing: Known for its innovative beers and laid-back atmosphere, Raised Grain Brewing is a must-visit destination for beer lovers.
  • Westallion Brewing Company: This microbrewery specializes in traditional German-style beers, and offers tours and tastings.
  • Milwaukee Brewing Company: Located in nearby Milwaukee, Milwaukee Brewing Company offers a wide range of beers and a lively taproom.

Where to Stay in West Allis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several great places to stay in West Allis, depending on your budget and preferences. Some options include:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites West Allis: Located right off the highway and just a short drive from downtown Milwaukee, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • West Allis Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly option that offers basic rooms and free Wi-Fi, West Allis Inn & Suites is great for travelers on a tight budget.
  • The Clarke Hotel: Located in nearby Waukesha, The Clarke Hotel is a historic boutique hotel that offers luxurious rooms and amenities.

What to Buy in West Allis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in West Allis, be sure to browse these local stores for souvenirs and unique items:

  • West Allis Cheese & Sausage Shoppe: In addition to its delicious food offerings, West Allis Cheese & Sausage Shoppe also sells gourmet cheeses, meats, and other local products.
  • Stein’s Garden & Home: This local garden center offers a wide range of plants, flowers, and gardening supplies, as well as unique home decor items.
  • MKE Gift: This gift shop specializes in locally made items and Milwaukee-themed souvenirs, from T-shirts to handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of West Allis

West Allis is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and culture. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Respect for the environment: West Allis takes pride in its natural resources, and visitors are encouraged to treat the parks and trails with respect.
  • Friendliness: Locals are known for their friendly and welcoming personalities, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation.
  • Pride in the community: West Allis residents take pride in their city and its history, and visitors are encouraged to learn more about the local heritage.

Best Time to Visit West Allis

The best time to visit West Allis is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the city comes alive with festivals and outdoor events. However, the fall months are also a great time to visit for those who enjoy cooler temperatures and vibrant fall foliage.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Allis

West Allis is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates and friendly residents. However, visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in West Allis

West Allis has several medical facilities and pharmacies for visitors in need of health services. Visitors should also bring any necessary medications and travel insurance.

Getting to West Allis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Allis

The closest major airport to West Allis is General Mitchell International Airport in Milwaukee, which is a 20-minute drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near West Allis

West Allis is served by several bus stations, including the Milwaukee Intermodal Station and the Greyhound Bus Station in downtown Milwaukee.

Other Options

Visitors can also easily reach West Allis by car, with several major highways passing through the area. The city is located just west of downtown Milwaukee, making it easily accessible for those visiting the larger city.

How to Get Around in West Allis

West Allis is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car or use ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from West Allis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West Allis is located within easy driving distance of several other popular destinations in Southeast Wisconsin, including:

  • Milwaukee: Just a short drive east of West Allis, Milwaukee is known for its vibrant arts scene, historic architecture, and diverse dining options.
  • Lake Geneva: This scenic resort town is located about an hour southwest of West Allis and offers a variety of outdoor activities and luxury accommodations.
  • Wisconsin Dells: Located about two hours northwest of West Allis, Wisconsin Dells is a popular destination for families and outdoor enthusiasts, thanks to its many waterparks, amusement parks, and natural attractions.

Conclusion

West Allis may be a small city, but it has a lot to offer visitors looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious food. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, West Allis is well worth a visit.
