Introduction to Richfield

Located in Southeast Wisconsin, Richfield is a charming village renowned for its natural beauty, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. The area boasts several parks, hiking trails, and recreational opportunities, making it a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts. In this comprehensive travel guide to Richfield, you’ll discover the best things to do, places to visit, and where to eat, stay, and shop.

List of Places to Visit in Richfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • Shalom Wildlife Sanctuary – A non-profit organization dedicated to animal conservation and education. The sanctuary features over 400 animals and birds from around the world, including wolves, tigers, and bears.

  • Richfield Historical Society – A small museum showcasing the history of the village and its surrounding areas. Guests can learn about the Native Americans, early settlers, and local industry of Richfield.

  • St. Boniface Episcopal Church – A stunning medieval-style church built in 1892, known for its beautiful architecture and prominent gothic features.

Natural Attractions of Richfield: Outdoor Adventures, and Scenic Views

  • Richfield Nature/Historical Park – A 154-acre park offering a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and cross-country skiing. The park also features a museum, historic buildings, and a children’s playground.

  • Little Cedar Lake – A picturesque lake situated in the heart of Richfield. Visitors can go fishing, boating, or enjoy a picnic overlooking the lake.

  • Pike Lake State Park – A popular state park with over 500 acres of recreational opportunities. The park boasts several hiking trails, a sandy beach, and a boat launch for fishing and boating enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Richfield

  • Ice Age Trail – A 1,200-mile trail that passes along the edge of Richfield. The trail winds through hills, forests, and wetlands, offering stunning views of the surrounding wilderness.

  • Little Red Schoolhouse Nature Trail – A ¾ mile interpretive trail that showcases the native plants and wildlife of the area. The trail is ideal for families with young children or those seeking a leisurely hike.

  • Holy Hill Trail – A scenic trail that offers panoramic views of the surrounding region. The trail is popular among hikers and bikers for its challenging terrain and breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Richfield: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding – Explore the natural beauty of Richfield on horseback with Chasin’ Tail Trail rides.

  • Segway Tours – Discover the area’s unique history and natural beauty on an eco-friendly Segway tour with Glide Tours.

  • Fishing and Boating – Rent a boat or kayak, or bring your own gear and explore the numerous lakes and rivers in Richfield.

Week-Long Itinerary for Richfield

Day 1: Visit Shalom Wildlife Sanctuary and Richfield Historical Society Day 2: Hike the Ice Age Trail or Holy Hill Trail Day 3: Explore Pike Lake State Park Day 4: Go fishing or boating at Little Cedar Lake Day 5: Take a Segway tour of the area Day 6: Horseback ride at Chasin’ Tail Trail rides Day 7: Visit Richfield Nature/Historical Park and enjoy a picnic lunch

Food and Drink Scene of Richfield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Curds – A Wisconsin classic, these lightly battered and fried cheese curds are a must-try when visiting Richfield.

  • Bratwurst – A local favorite, this sausage is served grilled and topped with onions and sauerkraut.

  • Fish Fry – A Wisconsin tradition, guests can enjoy fresh and crispy fish fry at several local restaurants and taverns.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer – With a thriving craft beer scene, visitors can enjoy a cold local brew at the Silver Creek Brewery.

  • Wine Tasting – Wine lovers can visit The Little Swiss Clock Shop and enjoy a wine tasting experience.

Where to Stay in Richfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • West Bend Country Inn – A cozy bed and breakfast located in nearby West Bend, offering a relaxing and comfortable stay.

  • Quality Inn – A budget-friendly option in Germantown, just a short drive from Richfield.

  • Comfort Suites – A mid-range option in Jackson, featuring spacious rooms and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Richfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese – Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and visitors can sample and purchase a variety of cheeses at local markets and cheese shops.

  • Pottery and Crafts – Visitors can browse and purchase locally-made pottery, jewelry, and crafts at the Richfield Art Association studios.

Local Culture and Customs of Richfield

Wisconsinites are known for their love of beer, cheese, and all things outdoors. Visitors should be prepared for friendly locals, unpredictable weather, and a laid-back atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Richfield

Summer (June-August) is the best time to visit Richfield, with warm temperatures and numerous outdoor activities available. Fall (September-November) offers stunning foliage and fall festivals, while winter (December-February) provides opportunities for winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Richfield

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including cheese curds and bratwurst.

  • Do explore the natural beauty of the area, including hiking and boating.

  • Do embrace the friendly and laid back atmosphere of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t forget to bring suitable clothing for unpredictable weather conditions.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations or directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Richfield

Richfield is a relatively safe village, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as locking their vehicles and securing their belongings.

Health in Richfield

Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling to Richfield. The area boasts several medical facilities and pharmacies and is easily accessible from Milwaukee.

Getting to Richfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Richfield

  • General Mitchell International Airport – Located in Milwaukee, approximately 30 miles from Richfield.

  • Appleton International Airport – Located in Appleton, approximately 88 miles from Richfield.

Bus Stations Near Richfield

  • Greyhound Bus Station – Located in Milwaukee, approximately 30 miles from Richfield.

  • Badger Bus Station – Located in West Bend, approximately 7 miles from Richfield.

Other Options

  • Car rental – Visitors can rent a car from one of several rental agencies located at General Mitchell International Airport or in Milwaukee.

How to Get Around in Richfield

Due to the village’s rural location, visitors are advised to rent a car to explore the area. Alternatively, taxi services and ride-sharing apps are also available.

Where to Go Next from Richfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Milwaukee, Wisconsin – Located approximately 30 miles from Richfield, Milwaukee is a vibrant city known for its breweries, museums, and cultural attractions.

  • Lake Geneva, Wisconsin – A picturesque town located approximately 60 miles from Richfield, Lake Geneva is renowned for its scenic lakefront and vibrant downtown area.

  • Green Bay, Wisconsin – Home to the Green Bay Packers NFL team, Green Bay is a thriving city known for its rich history and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Whether you are seeking outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Richfield has something for everyone. From hiking trails and natural attractions to local cuisine and craft beer, visitors will leave Richfield with unforgettable memories.
