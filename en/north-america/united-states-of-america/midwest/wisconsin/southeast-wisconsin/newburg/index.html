Introduction to Newburg

Newburg is a charming small town located in Southeast Wisconsin, in the Midwest region of the United States. It is known for its scenic natural beauty, outdoor recreational activities, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-packed vacation, Newburg has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local attractions in Newburg.

List of Places to Visit in Newburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Schwengel Memorial Park

Schwengel Memorial Park is a must-visit attraction in Newburg, with a stunning scenic view of the Milwaukee River. It is named after a Newburg resident who was a World War II hero and features a memorial to honor his service to the country. The park also has a playground, picnic areas, and a boat launch.

2. Old Settlers Park

Old Settlers Park is a historical site located in the heart of Newburg. It features a restored log home, schoolhouse, and chapel dating back to the early 1800s. Visitors to the park can step back in time and experience what life was like for early settlers in the area.

Natural Attractions of Newburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Kettle Moraine State Forest

Kettle Moraine State Forest is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It covers over 22,000 acres and offers miles of hiking and biking trails, camping sites, and fishing opportunities. Visitors can also enjoy scenic drives along the forest’s winding roads and stop at the various scenic overlooks.

2. Riverside Park

Riverside Park is a scenic park located on the banks of the Milwaukee River. It offers picnic areas, fishing spots, and a playground for kids. Visitors can also rent kayaks and canoes to explore the river and enjoy the stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newburg

Newburg is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. The Kettle Moraine State Forest offers a network of trails of varying difficulties and lengths, including the Ice Age Trail, which offers stunning views of the glacial formations and valleys. The Ozaukee Interurban Trail is a 33-mile paved trail that allows visitors to bike or hike through various towns and villages in the area.

Best Things to Do in Newburg: Activities and Tours

1. Kayaking Tours

The Milwaukee River is an ideal spot for kayaking enthusiasts, with its scenic views and calm waters. Visitors can take a guided kayak tour and explore the river’s natural beauty.

2. Sunset Dinner Cruise

Experience the breathtaking sunsets while enjoying a delicious dinner aboard a dinner cruise on the Milwaukee River. The three-hour cruise offers stunning views of the Milwaukee skyline and the nearby bluffs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newburg

Day 1: Explore the history of the area by visiting the Old Settlers Park and Schwengel Memorial Park.

Day 2: Hike or bike the trails in the Kettle Moraine State Forest.

Day 3: Take a guided kayak tour of the Milwaukee River.

Day 4: Enjoy a sunset dinner cruise on the Milwaukee River.

Day 5: Visit the local farmer’s market and sample some of the local produce.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the nearby countryside and stop at some of the charming small towns in the area.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic in Riverside Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Newburg

Must-Try Dishes

1. Bratwurst

Newburg is famous for its delicious bratwurst, which is a type of sausage made with pork and beef. It is a local specialty and can be found at various restaurants and food stands in the town.

2. Cheese Curds

Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and visitors to Newburg shouldn’t miss the chance to try the local cheese curds. These delicious, bite-sized treats are available at various specialty stores and restaurants in the area.

Local Beverages

1. Local Craft Beer

Newburg is home to several local breweries that produce a variety of craft beers. Visitors can take a brewery tour and sample some of the local brews.

2. Wine Tasting

There are several wineries in the area that offer wine tasting and tours. Visitors can learn about the wine-making process and sample some of the local wines.

Where to Stay in Newburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newburg has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some popular options for visitors include hotels, motels, and guesthouses. There are also several campgrounds and RV parks in the area for those who prefer outdoor accommodations.

What to Buy in Newburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Newburg can bring home some unique souvenirs and local products to remember their trip. Some popular items to buy include local cheese, handmade crafts, and locally brewed beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Newburg

Newburg has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong German influence. The town celebrates its German heritage with several festivals throughout the year, including the German Fest and Oktoberfest.

Best Time to Visit Newburg

The best time to visit Newburg is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is warmest, and the outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and kayaking, are in full swing. Visitors can also experience the local festivals and events that take place during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newburg

Newburg is a safe and friendly town. Visitors should take the usual precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe, such as locking their cars and securing their valuable items.

Health in Newburg

Visitors to Newburg should be aware of the potential health risks associated with outdoor activities, such as hiking and camping. It is important to protect oneself from ticks and other insects that may carry diseases. Visitors should also bring adequate supplies of water and sunscreen to protect against dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Newburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Newburg

The nearest major airport to Newburg is General Mitchell International Airport in Milwaukee, approximately 40 miles south of Newburg. Visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle bus to reach Newburg from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Newburg

There are several Greyhound bus stations in the region, including one in Milwaukee, which is approximately 40 miles south of Newburg. Visitors can take a bus to Milwaukee and then rent a car or take a shuttle bus to reach Newburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Newburg by driving or by taking a train. Amtrak has a station in nearby Port Washington, which is approximately 20 miles east of Newburg.

How to Get Around in Newburg

Visitors can get around Newburg by car or by bike. There are several rental car companies in the area, and visitors can also rent bikes to explore the town and the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Newburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore from Newburg. Some popular options include:

  • Milwaukee: The largest city in Wisconsin, Milwaukee is located approximately 40 miles south of Newburg. It offers a range of cultural, historical, and recreational attractions.

  • Kohler-Andrae State Park: Located approximately 25 miles north of Newburg, Kohler-Andrae State Park offers beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and camping sites.

  • Sheboygan: Located approximately 30 miles north of Newburg, Sheboygan is a charming town that offers a range of outdoor recreational activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking.
