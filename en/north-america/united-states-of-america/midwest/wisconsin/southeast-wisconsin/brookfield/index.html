Introduction to Brookfield

Welcome to Brookfield, a charming city in Southeast Wisconsin that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cosmopolitan delights. Nestled in the Midwest and blessed with stunning lakes, rivers, and parks, Brookfield is a popular tourist destination for adventure seekers, history buffs, and foodies alike. Whether you’re looking to unwind in nature, explore cultural landmarks, or indulge in some retail therapy, Brookfield has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Brookfield

Are you ready to discover the best attractions in Brookfield? Here are some must-visit places that should be on every traveler’s itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pioneer Village: Take a trip back in time and explore the restored 19th-century buildings, including a schoolhouse, blacksmith shop, and church.
  • Elmbrook Historical Society: Learn about the history of Elmbrook, one of the earliest settlements in Brookfield.
  • Sharon Lynn Wilson Center for the Arts: Enjoy world-class performances, exhibitions, and workshops at this state-of-the-art arts center.

Natural Attractions of Brookfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lapham Peak: Hike or bike the trails of this breathtaking state park that offers stunning views of the Kettle Moraine region.
  • Fox Brook Park: Take a leisurely stroll or a paddleboat ride on the lake of this tranquil park that also features a swimming beach and a dog park.
  • Mitchell Park Horticultural Conservatory: Admire the exotic plant collections, including the Desert, Tropical, and Floral Show Domes, at this indoor garden oasis.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookfield

Brookfield is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer unforgettable experiences. Here are some popular routes to explore.

  • Capital City State Trail: Spanning over 17 miles, this trail connects Madison to the neighboring communities of Fitchburg and Verona.
  • New Berlin Trail: This 7-mile trail leads you through New Berlin’s scenic parks, forests, and wetlands.
  • Bugline Trail: Built on an abandoned railroad line, this 16-mile trail takes you through Menomonee Falls and Pewaukee, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Brookfield: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for an adrenaline rush or a laid-back adventure, Brookfield has something for everyone. Here are some top activities and tours to try.

  • Balloon rides: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy panoramic views of the Wisconsin landscape.
  • Escape rooms: Solve puzzles and clues to escape themed rooms with your team.
  • Wine-tasting tours: Explore the countryside and sample some of Wisconsin’s best wines on a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brookfield

If you’re wondering how to spend a week in Brookfield, here’s a sample itinerary that covers the best of the city.

Day 1: Visit the Sharon Lynn Wilson Center for the Arts and enjoy a performance. Day 2: Explore Lapham Peak and the nearby Kettle Moraine State Forest. Day 3: Discover the history of Elmbrook at the Elmbrook Historical Society. Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride followed by a wine-tasting tour. Day 5: Spend a day at Fox Brook Park and indulge in some outdoor activities. Day 6: Visit Pioneer Village and learn about the early settlements of Brookfield. Day 7: Unwind at the Mitchell Park Horticultural Conservatory.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookfield

Brookfield’s food and drink scene is a heaven for foodies and beer lovers with a range of restaurants and bars serving local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wisconsin cheese curds: Sample the state’s famous cheese curds, usually served deep-fried with dipping sauce.
  • Friday Fish Fry: A popular tradition in Wisconsin, this meal usually includes a fried fish dish with sides like coleslaw and fries.
  • Bratwurst: Experience the city’s German heritage with this juicy sausage that’s often served grilled with sauerkraut and mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Brookfield has a thriving craft beer scene with breweries like Biloba, Raised Grain and City Lights serving a variety of styles.
  • Old Fashioned: Try a classic Wisconsin cocktail, made with brandy, soda, bitters, and sugar, often topped with a cherry and an orange slice.
  • Kitsch Sippin Whiskey: This award-winning whiskey is made at Great Lakes Distillery and is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Brookfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brookfield has a range of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s budget and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some popular choices.

  • The Brookfield Suites Hotel and Convention Center: A full-service hotel with spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a convenient location near major attractions.
  • Mid-Range: Embassy Suites by Hilton Milwaukee Brookfield offers guests an outdoor pool, on-site restaurant, and fitness center.
  • Budget: Motel 6 Milwaukee West - Brookfield provides guests with a budget-friendly option in the heart of Brookfield.

What to Buy in Brookfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or locally-made products to bring back home, check out these popular options.

  • Wisconsin cheese: Pick up some artisanal Wisconsin cheeses from local cheese shops like Sendik’s Food Market.
  • Specialty foods: Visit Metcalfe’s Market to browse through a variety of locally-produced goods, including maple syrup and wild rice.
  • Crafts and Artwork: Stop by the Elmbrook Historical Society Museum Gift Shop to find unique crafts, artwork, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookfield

Brookfield has a mix of American and European cultural influences, with German heritage playing a significant role in the city’s traditions. Visitors are also encouraged to respect the locals’ customs and traditions, including being mindful of noise levels in residential areas and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Brookfield

The best time to visit Brookfield depends on your preferences and interests. Summer (June-August) is the peak tourist season, with warm temperatures and outdoor festivals. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit for autumn foliage viewing and harvest festivals. Winter (December-February) is cold but festive, offering opportunities for ice-skating, skiing, and snowshoeing. Spring (March-May) is mild and less crowded, with flowers blooming and outdoor activities starting to open up.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brookfield

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Brookfield

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Explore the outdoors and take advantage of the city’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize public places.
  • Ignore road safety rules and regulations.
  • Make noise in residential areas or after the designated quiet hours.

Safety and Health

Brookfield is a safe and healthy city, but visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their well-being.

Safety in Brookfield

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dark, isolated areas at night.
  • Always lock your doors and windows when staying in accommodations.
  • Keep your valuables out of sight and use a money belt to store your cash.

Health in Brookfield

  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Make sure to keep your vaccinations up to date.
  • In case of an emergency, dial 911 or visit the nearby hospitals such as Ascension SE Wisconsin Hospital or Aurora Medical Center.

Getting to Brookfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brookfield is easily accessible via multiple modes of transportation, including air, bus, and car.

Airports Near Brookfield

  • General Mitchell International Airport: Located in Milwaukee, about a 30-minute drive from Brookfield, this is the closest airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Brookfield

  • WIS 100 & Greenfield: This bus stop for Greyhound is located on South Moorland Road, about a 3-minute drive from Brookfield.

Other Options

  • Car: Brookfield is conveniently located near several major highways, including I-94 and I-43, and offers easy access to neighboring cities.
  • Train: The Amtrak Station in Milwaukee is located about 12 miles from Brookfield.
  • Rideshare: Services like Uber and Lyft are available in and around Brookfield.

How to Get Around in Brookfield

Brookfield is a small city that can be easily explored on foot, bike, or car. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s public transportation system, which includes buses and taxis.

Public Transportation

  • Buses: The Milwaukee County Transit System operates several bus routes that connect Brookfield to neighboring cities and attractions.
  • Taxis: Several taxi companies operate in Brookfield, including Brookfield Taxi and American United Taxi.

Where to Go Next from Brookfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve explored all that Brookfield has to offer, consider venturing out to nearby destinations for more adventure and excitement. Here are some top options.

  • Milwaukee: Located just 20 miles east of Brookfield, this vibrant city offers world-class museums, breweries, and restaurants.
  • Lake Geneva: A scenic drive from Brookfield, Lake Geneva is a charming resort town known for its picturesque lakefront, historic mansions, and scenic hiking trails.
  • Wisconsin Dells: A popular destination for families, Wisconsin Dells is about a two-hour drive from Brookfield and offers water parks, amusement rides, and boat tours.

Conclusion

Brookfield is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be explored. With a rich culture, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant food, and drink scene, there’s never a dull moment in this delightful city. Whether you’re here for a day or a week, make sure to check out these top attractions and experiences to make the most of your trip.
