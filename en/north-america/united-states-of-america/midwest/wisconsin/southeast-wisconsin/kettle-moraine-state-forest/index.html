Introduction to Kettle Moraine State Forest

Kettle Moraine State Forest is located in Southeast Wisconsin and spans across three counties - Fond du Lac, Walworth, and Washington. The forest consists of glacially-formed topography, including kettles, ridges, and winding rivers, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Kettle Moraine State Forest offers a wide range of activities and attractions, including hiking, biking, camping, wildlife viewing, and historical museums.

List of Places to Visit in Kettle Moraine State Forest

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old World Wisconsin: This museum showcases the state’s history and traditions with 60 historic buildings and immersive exhibits depicting the lives of early settlers and immigrants.

  • Wade House historic site: This site features an 1850s stagecoach inn, a blacksmith shop, and a carriage museum. Visitors can also participate in hands-on activities, such as hearth cooking and blacksmithing.

  • Kettle Moraine Forest Headquarters: This location offers hiking and snowshoeing trails, natural history exhibits, and educational programs about forest conservation.

Natural Attractions of Kettle Moraine State Forest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ice Age Trail: This 1,000-mile trail goes through the forest and showcases stunning views of glacial formations, lakes, and forests. It is a popular route for hiking and backpacking.

  • Lapham Peak Tower: This tower provides a panoramic view of the forest and is accessible through hiking and skiing trails.

  • Pike Lake Unit: This unit offers camping sites, biking and hiking trails, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kettle Moraine State Forest

  • John Muir Trails: A 3-mile loop that offers scenic views of the glacial topography and wildlife. It is considered a moderate hiking trail.

  • Zillmer Trails: 6.5-mile hiking trail that’s known for its varied terrain, including woods, prairies, and marshes.

  • Greenbush Trails: Offers 14 miles of hiking and biking trails that go through hilly terrain and hardwood forests.

Best Things to Do in Kettle Moraine State Forest: Activities and Tours

  • Kettle Moraine Scenic Drive: A scenic 115-mile drive through the forest, offering views of rolling hills, farmlands, and winding roads.

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: There are several waterways throughout the forest, including the Kettle Moraine Scenic River, the Milwaukee River, and several lakes.

  • Camping: There are several camping sites in the forest, offering both primitive and modern amenities.

Food and Drink Scene of Kettle Moraine State Forest

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese curds: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and cheese curds are a staple dish that visitors must try.

  • Bratwurst: A sausage made with beef and pork, served on a bun with mustard, onion, and sauerkraut.

  • Fish fry: A traditional Wisconsin dish that features breaded fish served with coleslaw and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Wisconsin is known for its craft beers, with several breweries located near the forest.

  • Wine: There are several vineyards and wineries in the area, offering wine tasting and tours.

Where to Stay in Kettle Moraine State Forest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kettle Moraine State Forest campgrounds: There are several campgrounds located throughout the forest, offering both primitive and modern amenities.

  • The Audubon Inn is a historic boutique hotel located in the nearby town of Mayville.

  • The Idlewile Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast located near the forest.

What to Buy in Kettle Moraine State Forest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and visitors can purchase a variety of cheeses made locally.

  • Maple Syrup: The forest is home to several maple syrup producers, and visitors can purchase locally-made maple syrup.

  • Crafts and Antiques: There are several antique shops and craft stores near the forest, offering unique items made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Kettle Moraine State Forest

Wisconsin is known for its friendly locals and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors should be respectful of the state’s cultural traditions and try to immerse themselves in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Kettle Moraine State Forest

The best time to visit Kettle Moraine State Forest is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities. However, visitors can also enjoy the forest’s beautiful fall foliage in September and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kettle Moraine State Forest

Do:

  • Plan ahead for camping and hiking permits.

  • Respect the forest’s wildlife and natural resources.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Ignore warning signs or advice from park rangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kettle Moraine State Forest

Visitors should be aware of potential hazards in the forest, including falling rocks, slippery terrain, and wildlife. It’s important to follow all park rules and regulations and carry appropriate safety gear, such as maps, first aid kits, and flashlights.

Health in Kettle Moraine State Forest

Visitors should be aware of potential health hazards in the forest, including insect bites and tick-borne illnesses. It’s important to wear appropriate clothing and use insect repellent to prevent bites.

Where to Go Next from Kettle Moraine State Forest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Madison: The state’s capital, Madison, is just an hour and a half’s drive away and offers several museums, historical sites, and outdoor activities.
