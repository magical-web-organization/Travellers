Introduction to Port Washington

Nestled on the shore of Lake Michigan, Port Washington is a charming city in the state of Wisconsin. It boasts a rich history, a vibrant culture, and scenic natural beauty that makes it an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, or a little bit of both. Whether you’re visiting for the first time or you’re a returning visitor, this guide will help you plan the perfect trip to Port Washington.

List of Places to Visit in Port Washington

There’s plenty to see and do in Port Washington, from exploring its cultural landmarks to immersing yourself in its natural beauty. Here’s a list of the city’s top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Port Washington has a long and fascinating history, and several museums and historical sites showcase its heritage. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Port Exploreum: Learn about the city’s maritime past and its role in the growth of the Great Lakes region at this interactive museum.
  • Possibility Playground: This inclusive playground has a nautical theme and is designed for children of all abilities.
  • Port Washington Historical Society: Discover the city’s rich history through displays of photographs, artifacts, and documents.

Natural Attractions of Port Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Port Washington’s natural beauty is a major draw for visitors. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the city:

  • Rotary Park: This lakefront park has scenic views of Lake Michigan, a beach, and a playground for children.
  • Tendick Nature Park: With hiking trails, fishing ponds, and picnic areas, this park is a perfect spot for a day trip.
  • Harrington Beach State Park: Explore the sand dunes, hike along the shoreline, or have a picnic at this scenic state park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Washington

Port Washington is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Ozaukee Interurban Trail: This 30-mile trail connects several communities throughout Ozaukee County, including Port Washington. It’s ideal for biking, walking, or running.
  • Lion’s Den Gorge Nature Preserve: This preserve has several hiking trails and scenic views of Lake Michigan.
  • Riveredge Trail: This trail follows the Milwaukee River and passes through forests, prairies, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Port Washington: Activities and Tours

From boat tours to golfing, Port Washington has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Port Washington Ghost Walks: These haunted walking tours of the city take you to historical places with ghostly legends.
  • Port Washington Marina: Rent a boat or take a harbor tour for a unique way to see the city.
  • Port Washington Golf Course: This scenic golf course has 18 holes and views of Lake Michigan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Washington

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Port Washington, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Port Exploreum, walk along the marina, and have dinner at Twisted Willow restaurant.
2 Hike the trails at Lion’s Den Gorge Nature Preserve, have lunch at Bernie’s Fine Meats, and go shopping on Franklin Street.
3 Take a boat tour or rent a kayak at the marina, relax at Rotary Park, and have dinner at Newport Shores restaurant.
4 Visit Possibility Playground, explore the Port Washington Historical Society, and have lunch at Schooner Pub.
5 Play a round of golf at Port Washington Golf Course, have lunch at The Patio, and enjoy the afternoon at Harrington Beach State Park.
6 Visit Tendick Nature Park, have dinner at Rusty Anchor Bar and Grill, and catch a movie at Marcus Cinema.
7 Take part in a Port Washington Ghost Walk, go shopping at the Farmers’ Market, and have lunch at Beanies Mexican restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Port Washington

Port Washington has a diverse food and drink scene that celebrates its local produce and culinary traditions. Here are some must-try dishes, as well as local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Fry: Wisconsin is known for its fish fry, and Port Washington is no exception. Try the fish fry at the Country Inn, Schooner Pub, or Newport Shores.
  • Bratwurst: Sausage is a popular dish in Wisconsin, and bratwurst is a must-try. Try the bratwurst at Bernie’s Fine Meats or The Patio.
  • Cheese Curds: These bite-sized pieces of cheese are a Wisconsin specialty. Try the cheese curds at Smith Bros. Coffee House or Schooner Pub.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Wisconsin is home to many breweries, and Port Washington has its fair share. Try the beer at Inventors Brewpub, Twisted Willow, or Dockside Deli.
  • Wine: Wisconsin’s wineries are gaining recognition for their high-quality wines. Try the wine at Vines to Cellar or Cedar Creek Winery.

Where to Stay in Port Washington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Port Washington, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Country Inn and Suites: This hotel is located near downtown Port Washington and offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and a free breakfast.
  • Port Washington Inn: This bed and breakfast serves delicious breakfasts and has cozy rooms with excellent views of Lake Michigan.
  • Holiday Inn Harborview: This hotel is located near the marina and has a pool, an on-site restaurant, and scenic views of the lake.

What to Buy in Port Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from Port Washington, here are some local products to consider:

  • Fish: Port Washington is known for its fresh fish, so consider bringing home some smoked salmon or trout from Ewig Brothers Fish Market.
  • Art: Franklin Street has several art galleries that showcase local artists, so consider buying a piece of artwork as a souvenir.
  • Soap: Lakefront Natural Soapworks makes handmade soaps with natural ingredients and unique scents, perfect for a small gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Washington

Port Washington is a friendly and welcoming city, but there are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect the nautical history of the city: Port Washington is known for its maritime past, so be sure to respect its cultural heritage.
  • Dress appropriately: Port Washington has a casual dress code, but it’s always a good idea to dress respectfully when visiting cultural landmarks or places of worship.
  • Be friendly: Residents of Port Washington are known for their friendliness, so don’t be shy to strike up a conversation.

Best Time to Visit Port Washington

Port Washington is a year-round destination, but there are some factors to consider when planning your trip. Here’s what to keep in mind:

  • Weather: Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy. Keep the weather in mind when planning outdoor activities.
  • Events: Port Washington has several events throughout the year, including Fish Day, a summer festival, and Christmas on the Corner.
  • Crowds: The summer months can be busy with tourists, while the offseason may have fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Washington

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Port Washington:

Do:

  • Take a boat tour to see the city from a different perspective.
  • Try the fish fry at one of the city’s restaurants.
  • Visit the Possibility Playground, an inclusive and unique attraction designed for children of all abilities.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the city’s maritime heritage and nautical culture.
  • Leave trash behind or damage natural areas.
  • Be overly loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Port Washington is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Port Washington

  • Be careful when swimming in Lake Michigan, as strong currents can sometimes be present.
  • Watch for traffic when crossing streets, especially in busy areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Port Washington

  • Bring sunscreen and wear protective clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot weather.
  • Bring insect repellent during mosquito season.

Getting to Port Washington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Port Washington is located about 30 miles north of Milwaukee and is accessible by several transportation options. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Port Washington

  • General Mitchell International Airport in Milwaukee is the nearest major airport, with flights to several destinations in the United States and Canada.

Bus Stations Near Port Washington

  • The Greyhound bus station in Milwaukee is about 30 miles from Port Washington and has several routes to destinations throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Driving: Port Washington is conveniently located near several major highways, including Interstate 43 and State Highway 32.
  • Train: The nearest Amtrak station is in Milwaukee, about 30 miles from Port Washington.

How to Get Around in Port Washington

Port Washington is a walkable city, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you need transportation, here are some options:

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in the Milwaukee area, and Port Washington has several options for parking.
  • Bicycle rental: Several businesses in Port Washington rent bicycles for a unique way to see the city.
  • Taxi or rideshare: Several cab companies and rideshare services are available in the Milwaukee area.

Where to Go Next from Port Washington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in the Milwaukee area, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Milwaukee: This bustling city has a vibrant arts scene, a rich history, and several attractions, including the Harley-Davidson Museum and the Milwaukee Public Museum.
  • Sheboygan: This beach town has several beaches, water sports options, and cultural attractions, making it a perfect day trip from Port Washington.

Conclusion

Port Washington is a hidden gem in the Midwest, with plenty to offer for tourists seeking adventure, relaxation, or a little bit of both. From exploring its cultural landmarks to immersing yourself in its natural beauty, this scenic city is worthy of a visit. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to Port Washington and experience all that this charming city has to offer.
