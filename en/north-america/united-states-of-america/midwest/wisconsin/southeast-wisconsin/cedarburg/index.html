Introduction to Cedarburg

Nestled in the heart of Southeast Wisconsin, Cedarburg is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. A charming small town with a vibrant downtown district, Cedarburg boasts a unique blend of historic architecture, beautiful natural scenery, and a rich cultural heritage. Visitors will be greeted with a warm welcome, friendly locals, and a host of exciting things to do.

List of Places to Visit in Cedarburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cedarburg is rich in history and culture, with a host of museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the town’s past. Some of the top places to visit in Cedarburg include:

  • Cedarburg History Museum: Located in the historic Grist Mill, the Cedarburg History Museum offers a fascinating look into the town’s past.
  • Wittenberg-Jochem Mansion: This well-preserved Victorian mansion is a testament to Cedarburg’s historic architecture and the lives of the town’s early residents.
  • Wisconsin Museum of Quilts & Fiber Arts: A unique museum that celebrates the art and craft of quilting and fiber arts.

Natural Attractions of Cedarburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, Cedarburg offers a host of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for a scenic hike, a leisurely bike ride, or a relaxing kayak trip, Cedarburg has something for everyone. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Cedarburg include:

  • Ozaukee Interurban Trail: This 30-mile trail is perfect for a scenic bike ride, connecting Cedarburg to nearby towns and offering beautiful views of the countryside along the way.
  • Cedar Creek Park: A beautiful park with scenic trails, picnic areas, and a playground, Cedar Creek Park is a great place to spend the day with family and friends.
  • Cedarburg Bog: A unique natural area with a diverse range of plant and animal species, Cedarburg Bog is a must-visit for nature lovers and birdwatchers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cedarburg

Cedarburg is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a host of hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the top routes to explore include:

  • Ice Age Trail: An iconic trail that winds through Wisconsin, the Ice Age Trail offers a challenging and scenic hike through Cedarburg’s rolling hills and beautiful forests.
  • Cedar Creek Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that follows the Cedar Creek, this scenic route is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a quick bike ride.
  • Riveredge Nature Center Trails: With over 10 miles of hiking trails, the Riveredge Nature Center is a great place to explore Cedarburg’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Cedarburg: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a fun-filled afternoon with the family or a romantic weekend getaway, Cedarburg has plenty of exciting things to do. Some of the top activities and tours to check out include:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Cedarburg is home to several wineries and breweries, making it a great destination for wine lovers. Take a tour of the Cedar Creek Winery or the Stone’s Throw Winery and sample some of the best wines in the region.
  • Cedar Creek Settlement: A historic shopping district that offers a unique shopping experience, Cedar Creek Settlement is home to over 20 specialty shops and galleries.
  • Cedarburg Cultural Center: A hub of arts and culture in Cedarburg, the Cedarburg Cultural Center hosts a variety of art exhibitions, concerts, and performances throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cedarburg

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Cedarburg, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Cedarburg History Museum and explore the Wittenberg-Jochem Mansion.
  • Day 2: Enjoy a day of outdoor activities at Cedar Creek Park and the Ozaukee Interurban Trail.
  • Day 3: Take a winery tour at Cedar Creek Winery or the Stone’s Throw Winery.
  • Day 4: Spend the day exploring the Cedar Creek Settlement and its specialty shops and galleries.
  • Day 5: Take a hike or bike ride on the Ice Age Trail, followed by a picnic lunch at Riveredge Nature Center.
  • Day 6: Visit the Wisconsin Museum of Quilts & Fiber Arts and the Cedarburg Cultural Center.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Cedarburg’s cozy bed and breakfasts or guesthouses.

Food and Drink Scene of Cedarburg

Must-Try Dishes

Cedarburg is home to a variety of restaurants and cafes that serve up delicious local cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Bratwurst: A Wisconsin staple, bratwurst is a must-try dish in Cedarburg. Head to the Anvil Pub and Grille for some of the best bratwurst and beer in town.
  • Fish Fry: Another Wisconsin classic, fish fry is a popular dish in Cedarburg. Check out the historic Stagecoach Inn and its famous fish fry.
  • Cheese Curds: No trip to Wisconsin is complete without trying cheese curds. Head to the Cedarburg Coffee Roastery or the Cream & Crepe Cafe for some of the best cheese curds in town.

Local Beverages

Cedarburg is home to several wineries and breweries, making it a great destination for beer and wine lovers. Some of the top local beverages to try include:

  • Cedar Creek Wine: Known for its award-winning wines, Cedar Creek Winery is a must-visit for wine lovers.
  • Stone’s Throw Winery: A family-owned winery that specializes in small-batch wines, Stone’s Throw Winery offers a unique wine-tasting experience.
  • Cedarburg Brewing Company: A local favorite, Cedarburg Brewing Company offers a variety of craft beers and a cozy taproom.

Where to Stay in Cedarburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cedarburg offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, from cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotels. Some of the top places to stay in Cedarburg include:

  • The Stagecoach Inn: A historic bed and breakfast that offers a unique glimpse into Cedarburg’s past.
  • The Washington House Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast with modern amenities and a prime location in the heart of downtown Cedarburg.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: A modern hotel with spacious rooms, heated indoor pool, and easy access to Cedar Creek Park and other attractions.

What to Buy in Cedarburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade crafts to delicious local products, Cedarburg offers a variety of souvenirs and gifts to bring home. Some of the top things to buy in Cedarburg include:

  • Cedar Creek Pottery: Handmade pottery and ceramics that showcase the work of local artists.
  • Cedarburg Coffee Roastery: Freshly roasted coffee beans and delicious pastries made in-house.
  • Amy’s Candy Kitchen: Gourmet chocolates and sweets that are perfect for a sweet treat or a unique gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Cedarburg

Cedarburg is proud of its rich cultural heritage, and visitors will find a friendly and welcoming community. Some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Polka Festivals: Cedarburg hosts several Polka festivals throughout the year, and visitors are welcome to join in the festivities.
  • Historic Architecture: Cedarburg is home to several well-preserved historic buildings, and visitors are encouraged to explore and appreciate the town’s architectural heritage.
  • Outdoor Activities: Cedarburg residents love spending time outdoors, whether it’s hiking, biking, or kayaking. Visitors are encouraged to take advantage of the beautiful natural scenery and enjoy some outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Cedarburg

Cedarburg is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique experience. The best time to visit Cedarburg depends on your interests and preferences.

  • Spring (March – May): Spring is a great time to visit Cedarburg for outdoor activities like hiking and biking, as well as to attend cultural events like the annual Polka Fest.
  • Summer (June – August): Summer is the busiest time to visit Cedarburg, with the peak tourist season coinciding with the warmer months. Visitors can enjoy a host of outdoor activities, as well as concerts and festivals.
  • Fall (September – November): Fall is a great time to visit Cedarburg for its beautiful fall foliage, as well as for wine-tasting tours and other seasonal events.
  • Winter (December – February): Winter is a quieter time in Cedarburg, but visitors can still enjoy outdoor activities like snowshoeing and cross-country skiing, as well as holiday events and markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cedarburg

Do:

  • Do explore the town on foot: Cedarburg’s historic downtown district is best explored on foot, so make sure to bring comfortable shoes.
  • Do try the local cuisine: Cedarburg is known for its delicious local cuisine, so make sure to try some of the must-try dishes.
  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities: From hiking and biking to kayaking and fishing, Cedarburg offers a wide variety of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring a jacket: Cedarburg can get chilly, even in the summer months, so make sure to bring warm clothing.
  • Don’t litter: Cedarburg is a beautiful town, so be respectful of the environment and avoid littering.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help: Cedarburg is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, so don’t hesitate to ask for recommendations or directions if you need them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cedarburg

Cedarburg is a safe town with a low crime rate, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety.

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any town, Cedarburg has its share of petty crime, so be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables close.
  • Stay on designated trails: When hiking or biking in Cedarburg’s natural areas, stay on designated trails and be mindful of any potential hazards.
  • Be cautious when enjoying a beverage: Whether it’s a glass of wine or a craft beer, be mindful of your alcohol consumption and always drink responsibly.

Health in Cedarburg

Cedarburg has several medical facilities that can provide quality care in case of emergencies or health issues.

  • Columbia St. Mary’s Hospital: A full-service hospital with emergency care services available 24/7.
  • Naturally Unbridled Wellness LLC: A holistic wellness center that offers a range of services, including acupuncture, massage therapy, and nutritional counseling.
  • Walgreens: A pharmacy with a variety of over-the-counter medications and healthcare products.

Getting to Cedarburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • General Mitchell International Airport (MKE): Milwaukee’s primary airport, which is located approximately 30 miles south of Cedarburg.
  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport (ORD): One of the largest airports in the Midwest, ORD is located approximately 100 miles south of Cedarburg.

Bus Stations Near Cedarburg

  • Greyhound: The nearest Greyhound station is located in Milwaukee, approximately 30 miles south of Cedarburg.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Cedarburg can be easily explored by car, and several car rental companies operate in the area.
  • Rideshare Services: Companies like Uber and Lyft operate in the area and can be a convenient way to get around.

How to Get Around in Cedarburg

Cedarburg is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, but visitors can also get around by car, bike, or public transportation.

  • Walking: Cedarburg’s historic downtown district is best explored on foot, and many of the town’s top attractions are within walking distance of each other.
  • Biking: Cedarburg’s well-maintained trails and scenic routes make it a great destination for cycling enthusiasts.
  • Driving: Visitors can also explore Cedarburg by car, and several car rental companies operate in the area.
  • Public Transportation: Ozaukee County Transit operates several bus routes in the area, providing affordable and convenient transportation for visitors and locals alike.

Where to Go Next from Cedarburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cedarburg is conveniently located near several other exciting destinations in Wisconsin, making it a great base for exploring the region. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Milwaukee: Just a short drive south, Milwaukee is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage and a wide variety of top attractions.
  • Lake Michigan: Known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters, Lake Michigan is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts and beachgoers.
  • Kohler-Andrae State Park: A beautiful state park that offers hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning views of the lake.

Conclusion

From its rich history and culture to its stunning natural scenery and exciting outdoor activities, Cedarburg has something for everyone. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Cedarburg is a destination that is not to be missed.
