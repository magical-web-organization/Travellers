Introduction to Eagle

Nestled in Southeast Wisconsin, Eagle is a small, friendly town that offers visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of big city life. The town is known for its beautiful scenery, friendly people, and abundance of outdoor activities. This comprehensive tour guide will help you explore the best things to do in Eagle, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, and must-see landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Eagle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eagle is steeped in history and culture, and visitors can explore numerous cultural attractions, including:

  • Old World Wisconsin: an open-air museum that showcases Wisconsin’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Kettle Moraine State Forest: an ideal destination for nature and outdoor enthusiasts. The state forest boasts of over 30,000 acres of pristine wilderness.
  • Eagle Diamond Center: a museum where visitors can learn about the history of diamond mining and see an extensive collection of diamonds and diamond jewelry.

Natural Attractions of Eagle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eagle is home to several natural attractions that provide visitors with the opportunity to get out and enjoy the great outdoors. Some of the must-visit natural wonders include:

  • The Ice Age Trail: A 1,200-mile trail that winds through Wisconsin’s most beautiful scenery, including forests, prairies, and wetlands.
  • Scuppernong Trails: A vast network of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to enjoy the gorgeous scenery of Wisconsin.
  • Lulu Lake: A popular spot for swimming, hiking, and picnicking, Lulu Lake is a must-visit destination when in Eagle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eagle

From rocky forest trails to meandering bike paths, Eagle has numerous hiking and biking routes that are sure to delight outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Glacial Drumlin State Trail: A popular multi-use trail that follows the path of an abandoned rail line.
  • The Kettle Moraine State Forest: With over 30,000 acres of pristine wilderness, the Kettle Moraine State Forest offers countless opportunities for hiking, fishing, and cycling.
  • The Ice Age Trail: A 1,200-mile trail that winds through Wisconsin’s most beautiful scenery, including forests, prairies, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Eagle: Activities and Tours

Eagle offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours, including:

  • Horseback Riding: Take a guided horseback riding tour through the beautiful Kettle Moraine State Forest.
  • Zip Lining: Fly through the trees of the Kettle Moraine State Forest on a thrilling zip line adventure.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the peaceful waters of the Scuppernong River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eagle

For visitors planning on staying in Eagle for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Old World Wisconsin

Day 2: Explore the Scuppernong Trails

Day 3: Go horseback riding in the Kettle Moraine State Forest

Day 4: Take a kayaking tour of the Scuppernong River

Day 5: Visit the Eagle Diamond Center

Day 6: Take a zip line tour of the Kettle Moraine State Forest

Day 7: Relax and unwind at Lulu Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Eagle

Must-Try Dishes

Eagle is a haven for foodies, with a vast range of local cuisine. Must-try dishes include:

  • Bratwurst: A Wisconsin staple for over a century, bratwurst is a must-try snack that visitors should not miss out on.
  • Cheese curds: Cheese curds are a local delicacy that every visitor needs to try. These small, crunchy pieces of cheese are often served with dipping sauce.
  • Fish Fry: A community tradition that goes back generations, fish fry is a Friday night ritual at local watering holes. Whether you prefer fried cod, perch, or bluegill, you’re sure to find your favorite version in Eagle.

Local Beverages

Eagle is home to several craft breweries and wineries that are worth a visit. Some popular local beverages include:

  • MKE Brewing: Known for its delicious craft beer, MKE Brewing is a must-visit destination for beer lovers.
  • Wollersheim Winery: Located in nearby Prairie du Sac, the Wollersheim Winery offers award-winning wines made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Eagle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotels, Eagle has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Eagle Springs Pub & Resort: A family-owned resort located on the shores of Eagle Spring Lake, this resort offers guesthouses, a lodge, and an RV park.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham: Located just minutes from the I-94 highway, this hotel offers comfortable guest rooms at an affordable price.
  • The Little Red Caboose: A charming bed and breakfast that offers guests cozy rooms in a beautifully restored train caboose.

What to Buy in Eagle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Eagle can bring home unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Local cheese: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and Eagle is no exception. Visitors can bring home a variety of cheese from local producers.
  • Handmade jewelry: The Eagle Diamond Center offers an extensive collection of diamond jewelry and other unique pieces handcrafted by local artisans.
  • Kettle Moraine Memorabilia: Visitors can find a range of memorabilia celebrating the natural beauty of the Kettle Moraine region.

Local Culture and Customs of Eagle

Eagle is known for its friendly, welcoming culture, and visitors should respect local customs and values. It’s important to be polite and courteous, and visitors should always be respectful of local residents and their belongings.

Best Time to Visit Eagle

Eagle is a year-round destination, and the best time to visit depends on your preferences. Visitors who enjoy outdoor activities will find the summer months (June to August) the most enjoyable, while those who prefer cooler weather should visit in the fall (September to November). Winter (December to February) is also a popular time to visit, particularly for winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eagle

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the Kettle Moraine Forest
  • Try the local cuisine, including bratwurst and cheese curds
  • Visit the Eagle Diamond Center to learn about the history of diamond mining

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and manners
  • Disrespect or damage natural areas or wildlife
  • Forget your sunscreen and insect repellent

Safety and Health

Safety in Eagle

Eagle is a safe destination, and visitors can enjoy their stay without worrying about crime or violence. It’s important to be aware of your surroundings and to take standard precautions, such as keeping your valuables in a safe place and sticking to well-lit areas at night.

Health in Eagle

Eagle has excellent healthcare facilities, including several hospitals and clinics. It’s important to bring any necessary medications with you and to keep them with you at all times.

How to Get Around in Eagle

Eagle is a small town, and walking is often the best way to get around. Visitors can also use a car or bike to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Eagle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eagle is located in Southeast Wisconsin, which is home to numerous other destinations worth exploring. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Madison: The capital city of Wisconsin, Madison offers visitors a range of cultural attractions and natural wonders.
  • Milwaukee: Wisconsin’s largest city, Milwaukee is known for its rich history and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Eagle, Wisconsin, is a charming destination that offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty and rich culture of Southeast Wisconsin. With a wide range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local delicacies, it’s no wonder that Eagle is a popular year-round destination for visitors from around the world.
