Introduction to Kenosha County

Kenosha County is a picturesque county located in Southeast Wisconsin in the Midwestern region of the United States. This county holds a special place in the history of Wisconsin and the United States. Kenosha natives were among the leading American forces in the Underground Railroad movement to abolish slavery during the Civil War era. The place draws many visitors from across the world who come to explore its natural beauty, multiculturalism, and heritage. This guide will introduce you to the fascinating places, festivals, food, and more, to make the most of your visit.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kenosha County

Here’s a week-long itinerary that lets you explore the highlights of Kenosha County:

Day 1: Visit Kenosha History Center, HarborPark Market, and Dinosaur Discovery Museum Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking at Pringle Nature Center Trails and have a picnic at Petrifying Springs Park Day 3: Take a tour of the Civil War Museum to learn about local history and visit the Kenosha Sand Dunes Day 4: Visit the Southport Lighthouse & Museum and head for a relaxing spa day at Apple Holler Day 5: Head to Hawthorn Hollow Nature Sanctuary & Arboretum for bird-watching and nature walks Day 6: Explore Richard Bong State Recreation Area to get your fill of outdoor adventure and take a factory tour of the famous Cheesehead Factory Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the North Shore Bike Trail and a sumptuous meal of locally produced food at The Baker House

Local Culture and Customs of Kenosha County

Kenosha County residents take pride in its diversity and inclusivity. They are welcoming and always happy to share insight into their customs and traditions. Visitors are expected to be courteous and respectful of local customs at all times.

Safety and Health

Kenosha County maintains a high level of safety for tourists and visitors. However, it is recommended to take basic safety precautions, especially when travelling in remote or unfamiliar areas.

Safety in Kenosha County

  • Avoid isolated areas at night
  • Always lock your car doors and secure your belongings
  • Be aware of road conditions, especially during inclement weather
  • Always carry identification

Health in Kenosha County
