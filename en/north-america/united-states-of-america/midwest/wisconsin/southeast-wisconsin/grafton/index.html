Introduction to Grafton

Nestled in Southeast Wisconsin, Grafton is a charming village that offers a blend of beautiful nature, rich history, and cultural experiences. Located near Lake Michigan, the Milwaukee River, and some of the best scenic routes in the region, Grafton serves as a perfect escape for those who are looking for outdoor adventures and a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. Aside from its natural attractions, Grafton has a vibrant cultural scene with museums, art galleries, and annual festivals that showcase the heritage of the village and its people.

Local Culture and Customs of Grafton

Grafton has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its architecture, festivals, and museums. The village’s German heritage is still prevalent today with various traditions celebrated throughout the year, including Oktoberfest and Christkindlmarkt.

Best Time to Visit Grafton

The best time to visit Grafton is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the fall is also a popular time to visit due to the beautiful colors of the changing leaves and various festivals celebrated during this time of year.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grafton

Grafton is a safe village with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

How to Get Around in Grafton

Grafton is a walkable village, and visitors can explore the downtown area, museums, and restaurants on foot. However, a car or bike is needed to get to the natural attractions and other places outside the village.
