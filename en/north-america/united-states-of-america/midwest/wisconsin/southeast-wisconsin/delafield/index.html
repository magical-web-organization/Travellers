Introduction to Delafield

Located in beautiful Southeast Wisconsin, Delafield is a charming small town that offers visitors a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and exciting activities. Home to over 7,000 people, Delafield has played an essential role in the state’s history since the 1800s. Today, it is widely recognized as a popular destination for tourists seeking a relaxing yet enriching experience.

From museums and historical sites to biking and hiking trails, Delafield is an ideal destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike. Explore the town and discover all of the exciting things to do, places to visit, and local culture that makes Delafield one of the Midwest’s most loved destinations. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help plan your trip to Delafield:

List of Places to Visit in Delafield

Here’s a list of places that you must visit while in Delafield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Delafield History Center - Visit the museum to experience Delafield’s history and heritage. The center features exhibits that showcase the city’s architecture, folklore, and cultural identity.

  • St. John’s Northwestern Military Academy - A historical prep school that hosts tours that highlight Delafield’s rich military history.

  • Cushing Memorial Park - Named after war hero Alonzo Cushing, this park is a great place to learn about the Civil War and see the historical marker erected to commemorate Cushing’s heroism.

Natural Attractions of Delafield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nashotah Park - Nashotah Park is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With over 444 acres of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails, this serene park offers visitors an opportunity to explore Wisconsin’s natural beauty.

  • Lapham Peak State Park - This park boasts some of the best views of the Kettle Moraine State Forest and has over 22 miles of hiking trails and beautiful nature, along with a crystal-clear lake.

  • Fowler Lake - This beautiful lake is nestled in the heart of Delafield and is an ideal spot for fishing, boating, and other water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delafield

  • Ice Age Trail - This 1,200-mile trail crosses Wisconsin, and the section of the trail found in Delafield offers breath-taking views of the surrounding countryside. This trail is open to hikers and bikers of all levels.

  • Glacial Drumlin State Trail - The Glacial Drumlin State Trail is a 52-mile trail that runs from Cottage Grove to Waukesha. Known for its flat, open terrain, this trail is a popular choice for bikers and hikers alike.

Best Things to Do in Delafield: Activities and Tours

  • Lake Country Bike Tour - Experience Delafield’s best bike trails with a guided bike tour. This tour showcases the scenic routes and will provide a fun and informative experience.

  • SUP Paddle Boarding - Take an exhilarating paddleboarding tour on Fowler Lake with Paddleboard Specialists - offering an experience that is both challenging and rewarding.

  • Brewery Tours - Take a tour of Delafield’s finest breweries and enjoy some of Wisconsin’s best local beer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Delafield

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Delafield that encapsulates all of the must-visit attractions and activities:

  • Day 1: Visit St. John’s Military Academy in the morning, followed by lunch at the iconic Delafield Hotel. Spend the afternoon hiking at Nashotah Park.

  • Day 2: Take a bike tour of Delafield’s scenic trails in the morning, followed by a brewery tour in the afternoon.

  • Day 3: Visit the Cushing Memorial Park in the morning, followed by an afternoon of fishing on Fowler Lake.

  • Day 4: Take a tour of the Delafield History Center to learn about Delafield’s rich cultural heritage followed by an outdoor picnic lunch at Lapham Peak State Park.

  • Day 5: Spend the day exploring the quaint downtown shops followed by a sunset paddleboarding session on Fowler Lake.

  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Milwaukee, visit the Harley-Davidson Museum in the morning and explore the city’s culinary scene in the afternoon.

  • Day 7: End your week-long trip with a leisurely day of activities such as an early morning yoga class at one of Delafield’s studios or a visit to a nearby winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Delafield

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Curds - A Wisconsin staple, these deep-fried delectables are a must-try when in Delafield.

  • Bratwurst - Another Wisconsin classic, bratwursts are a must-try dish when in Delafield. These savory sausages are typically served with plenty of condiments, including kraut or onions.

  • Fish Fry - Delafield’s pubs and bars serve up some of the best fish fries in the area. These Fridays-only dishes are a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  • Spotted Cow - This local beer is a Delafield favorite. This beer is only sold in Wisconsin, making it a must-try for visitors to the state.

  • Old Fashioned - Wisconsin is known for its Old Fashioneds, and Delafield’s bar scene does not disappoint. This classic cocktail is a must-try when in town.

Where to Stay in Delafield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Delafield Hotel - This iconic hotel is a local landmark and ideal accommodation in Delafield. At the heart of the town, the Delafield Hotel is centrally located and offers guests a top-tier experience.

  • Hawthorn Suites - This budget-friendly hotel located near Delafield’s main attractions is a great option for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Delafield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese - As a state known for its cheese, it’s a must to bring some of Wisconsin’s finest cheddar home as a gift or souvenir.

  • Beer - Wisconsin has some of the country’s best beer, so it’s a great idea to bring back the local favorite, Spotted Cow.

Local Culture and Customs of Delafield

Wisconsin is well-known for its dairy culture, and in Delafield, locals take pride in their locally produced cheese and beer. Wisconsin is also friendly, open, and laid-back, making visitors feel very comfortable.

Best Time to Visit Delafield

Delafield is enjoyable in any season. However, the best time to visit is in the spring and fall. These seasons showcase Delafield’s scenic beauty and bring milder temperatures perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delafield

Do:

  • Visit the local breweries and sample the famous Wisconsin beer
  • Explore Delafield’s culture and heritage at the local museums and parks
  • Try the local cheese and brats

Don’t:

  • Litter in the state’s serene parks and natural surroundings
  • Be impolite to locals - Wisconsin natives are friendly and welcoming

Safety and Health

Safety in Delafield

Delafield is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should be cautious when exploring the state’s natural surroundings, including the parks’ trails.

Health in Delafield

In case of a medical emergency, visitors can find healthcare at local clinics or hospitals in nearby cities such as Waukesha or Milwaukee.

Getting to Delafield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Delafield

  • General Mitchell International Airport in Milwaukee - nearest international airport. The drive from Milwaukee to Delafield takes approximately 30 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Delafield

  • Waukesha Transit Center - served by the Waukesha Metro Transit system, which operates daily bus services to Delafield.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars - Visitors can rent cars at the airport to explore Delafield and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Delafield

  • Rental Cars - Visitors can rent cars to explore the city and its surroundings.

  • Biking - Delafield is bike-friendly, with plenty of bike trails for visitors to enjoy.

Where to Go Next from Delafield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The charming town of Hartland is less than 10 minutes away from Delafield and is known for its downtown attractions and scenic beauty.

  • The city of Milwaukee, Wisconsin’s largest city, is only a 35-minute drive from Delafield. A vibrant city, Milwaukee boasts world-class attractions such as the Milwaukee Public Museum and the Harley-Davidson Museum.

  • The city of Madison is only an hour drive from Delafield and is home to the University of Wisconsin-Madison, the state capitol, and the beautiful Olbrich Botanical Gardens.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Delafield will keep you entertained and engaged. With its scenic outdoors, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Delafield is an ideal getaway destination for visitors seeking an authentic Midwest experience. Plan your trip using this comprehensive guide and enjoy an unforgettable experience in this beautiful small town in Wisconsin.
