Introduction to Neillsville

Nestled in the heart of Northwest Wisconsin, Neillsville is a charming small town that boasts a rich history, vibrant local culture, and breathtaking natural beauty. From exploring museums and historical sites to hiking challenging trails and indulging in delicious local cuisine, Neillsville is the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Midwest experience.

List of Places to Visit in Neillsville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clark County School Museum: Located in a restored 1915 school building, this museum showcases the history of education in Clark County, from the one-room schoolhouse era to modern times.
  • The Highground Veterans Memorial Park: A unique space that honors those who have served our country, The Highground consists of several memorials, sculptures, and gardens within a serene natural setting.
  • The Globe Schoolhouse: Built in the early 1900s, this one-room schoolhouse has been preserved to showcase the educational system of the past.
  • Press House Museum: Located in an early 1900s building, this museum showcases the history of the local newspaper industry and its impact on the community.

Natural Attractions of Neillsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bruce Mound Winter Sports Area: This recreation area boasts downhill skiing, snowboarding, tubing, and snowshoeing in the winter, and hiking, camping, and ATV trails during the summer months.
  • Silver Mound: This 10-acre nature reserve is home to unique geological formations and is a popular spot for hiking, bird watching, and nature photography.
  • Rock Dam County Park: This park features three lakes and offers boating, fishing, swimming, hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neillsville

  • The Levis/Trow Trails: A challenging trail system that features over 20 miles of hiking and mountain biking paths through scenic forests.
  • Wildcat Mound Trail: This trail is a 1.5-mile loop through the wildcat mound natural area, featuring steep inclines and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Neillsville: Activities and Tours

  • Lumberjack Bowl: Watch professional lumberjacks compete in traditional lumberjack events, such as log rolling, axe throwing, and chainsaw carving.
  • Neillsville Area Chamber of Commerce Tour: Take a guided tour of downtown Neillsville, visiting local shops, restaurants, and historic sites.
  • Farm to Table Experience: Learn about local agriculture and enjoy a farm-to-table dining experience at a nearby farm.

Food and Drink Scene of Neillsville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Curds: A Wisconsin classic, these deep-fried cheese curds are the perfect snack to enjoy with a cold beer.
  • Bratwurst: Another Wisconsin staple, bratwurst is a type of sausage made with pork and spices, often served with sauerkraut and mustard.
  • Fish Fry: Friday night fish fry is a tradition in many small towns in Wisconsin, and Neillsville is no exception. Enjoy fried perch, walleye, or cod, served with coleslaw and tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Wisconsin is known for its beer, and Neillsville has several microbreweries and wineries to explore.
  • Wine: Wisconsin is also becoming known for its wine, with several wineries producing award-winning varietals.

Where to Stay in Neillsville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Motel 95: A budget-friendly option, with clean rooms and a convenient location near downtown Neillsville.
  • The Highground Lodge: Located within The Highground Veterans Memorial Park, this cozy lodge offers peaceful and comfortable accommodations.
  • Lilac Resort and Campground: If you prefer camping or RV travel, this campground is a great option, with spacious sites and plenty of amenities.

What to Buy in Neillsville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese from Neillsville Dairy: As the self-proclaimed “cheese curd capital of Wisconsin,” Neillsville is known for its delicious cheese.
  • Handcrafted Wood Products: Local artisans in Neillsville create beautiful and unique handcrafted wood products, such as cutting boards and furniture.
  • Maple Syrup: Wisconsin produces some of the finest maple syrup in the country, and Neillsville has several farms that offer fresh, locally made syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of Neillsville

Neillsville is a small, tight-knit community where traditional Midwestern values are still alive and well. Residents are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their local heritage, which is reflected in the town’s many historic sites and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Neillsville

The best time to visit Neillsville is during the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, the winter months also offer plenty of opportunities for adventure, with skiing, snowboarding, and outdoor ice skating available at Bruce Mound Winter Sports Area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neillsville

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore Neillsville’s many historic sites and natural attractions.
  • Do try some local cuisine, including cheese curds, bratwurst, and fish fry.
  • Do be respectful of the town’s local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t ignore local traffic laws, such as speed limits and stop signs.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a friendly local!

Safety and Health

Safety in Neillsville

Neillsville is a safe and welcoming community, with low crime rates and friendly residents. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking car doors when leaving vehicles unattended.

Health in Neillsville

Neillsville has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should also be aware of ticks and other biting insects in wooded areas, and take appropriate precautions to avoid Lyme disease and other tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Neillsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neillsville

The closest airport to Neillsville is Central Wisconsin Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Neillsville

There are several Greyhound bus stations located within 50 miles of Neillsville, including stations in Eau Claire and Wausau.

Other Options

Renting a car or driving your own vehicle is the most convenient option for getting around Neillsville and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Neillsville

Walking, biking, and driving are the best ways to get around Neillsville and explore the town’s many attractions and outdoor recreation areas.

Where to Go Next from Neillsville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marshfield: A neighboring town that boasts several cultural attractions and beautiful natural areas, including the Jurustic Park outdoor sculpture garden.
  • La Crosse: Located on the Mississippi River, this vibrant river town is known for its historic architecture, local cuisine, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural enrichment, or just a peaceful Midwestern getaway, Neillsville offers something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience all that this charming small town has to offer!
