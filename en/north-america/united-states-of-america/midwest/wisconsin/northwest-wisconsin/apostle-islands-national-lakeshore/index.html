Introduction to Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is a 69,372-acre stretch of land and water comprising 21 islands and 12 miles of mainland on the northernmost tip of Wisconsin. It is one of America’s great outdoor playgrounds, drawing visitors from around the world to explore its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The diverse ecosystems of the area house unique flora and fauna, making it a prime location for activities such as kayaking, hiking, fishing, and camping.

List of Places to Visit in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit places in the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is the Madeline Island Museum, situated at the heart of the island. The museum is dedicated to the cultural heritage of the region, displaying a rich collection of artifacts from the Ojibwe, French, and American influences in the area.

Another unmissable stop is the Apostle Islands historic fishing village of Bayfield, which is just a few miles away from the park. Bayfield is home to several museums that showcase the region’s rich maritime history, including the Bayfield Maritime Museum, which offers a fascinating look at the lives and experiences of Great Lakes sailors over the past 200 years.

Natural Attractions of Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore offers a wealth of natural wonders to explore. The most popular attractions are the sea caves on the mainland shore, where visitors can kayak or take a boat tour to see the magnificent rock formations and waterfalls.

Another great attraction is the Raspberry Island Lighthouse, located on Raspberry Island and only accessible by boat. This beautiful lighthouse offers stunning views of the surrounding islands and a chance to see the Lake Superior shoreline from a unique perspective. Also, don’t forget to visit Big Bay State Park, which has 2,350 acres of undeveloped forest and sandstone bluffs, with several hiking trails that offer panoramic Lake Superior views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore has several hiking and biking trails. One of the most popular trails is the Lakeshore Trail, which is a 4.5-mile hike that takes you along the stunning shoreline of Lake Superior. Another popular option is the Brownstone Trail, a 1.5-mile trail that meanders through the dense forest, leading to the historic Brownstone Quarry.

If you want to explore the islands, the opportunities for hiking and biking are endless. Big Bay State Park offers over 8 miles of hiking trails, including the popular Houghton Point trail, which is a 3-mile round trip hike that provides stunning views of the Bayfield peninsula. Meanwhile, Stockton Island and Outer Island offer dozens of miles of trails through dense forests and rocky shoreline.

Best Things to Do in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Activities and Tours

From thrilling water sports to scenic bike rides, there are plenty of things to do in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore.

If you’re looking for adventure on the water, kayaking is a must-do activity. Once you get to the park, several kayaking outfitters in Bayfield offer guided tours around the islands. Visitors can also rent a personal watercraft and explore independently.

There are also several boat tours that provide a unique perspective of the islands. Visitors can take a guided lighthouse tour, where you get exclusive views of the historic lighthouses in the park. Another popular trip is the Apostle Islands Grand Tour, which provides passengers with a complete tour of all 21 islands in the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Here is a sample itinerary for a week in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore:

Day 1: Arrival and Exploration of Bayfield

Take your first day to explore the charming town of Bayfield, where you can soak in the local culture and indulge in the local cuisine.

Day 2: Sea Caves Tour

Set off on a full-day guided sea cave tour, one of the park’s most popular attractions. The tour is both exhilarating and awe-inspiring, providing you with unique views of the park’s enchanting landscape.

Day 3: Raspberry Island Lighthouse and Hiking

Take a boat tour of Raspberry Island Lighthouse, then spend the day hiking nearby trails with panoramic views of Lake Superior.

Day 4: Kayaking and Exploring the Sandstone Cliffs

Rent a kayak and explore the unique sandstone cliffs and hidden coves on the islands and take in the beautiful scenery.

Day 5: Island Exploration Day

Spend the day exploring both Stockton and Outer Islands, which provide you with some of the best views in the park.

Day 6: Relaxation Day

Take a break and unwind by the pristine beaches of Big Bay State Park.

Day 7: Departure

Take in some last-minute sights and perhaps do a little local shopping before catching your flight home.

Food and Drink Scene of Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Must-Try Dishes

A trip to the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is incomplete without trying some of the local cuisine. Start with the pie at the Bayfield Berry Farm, which offers blueberry, raspberry, and strawberry pies that are freshly baked.

For a heartier meal, head to the Wild Rice Restaurant, which serves traditional American cuisine with a local twist. Try some juicy steaks with local herbs and spices and locally sourced veggies.

Local Beverages

Wisconsin is known for its craft beer scene, and Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is home to several breweries, including the Dead River Coffeehouse & Tavern, which offers microbrews from around the state.

If beer isn’t your drink of choice, Red Cliff Band of Lake Superior Chippewa’s Food Sovereignty Initiative invites visitors to partake in their traditional tea ceremony experience, which gives visitors a chance to learn about the traditional tea-making process used in the area.

Where to Stay in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is a place where visitors can find accommodation to suit every budget. There are several options, including:

Hotels

One of the best places to stay while at Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is The Inn on Madeline Island, which features luxurious amenities and breathtaking views of the lake. The Bayfield Inn, situated in the heart of Bayfield, is another top-rated option.

Guesthouses and Bed & Breakfasts

If you would like to experience living like a local, choose a guesthouse or bed and breakfast. The Old Rittenhouse Inn is a lavish Victorian villa which sits atop a hill overlooking Lake Superior and offers a luxurious stay.

Camping

For nature lovers, the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore offers several campgrounds in the park.

What to Buy in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, don’t forget to take home some unique souvenirs. Bayfield Winery is a perfect place to buy a local wine to take home. Another good spot is Bayfield Apple Company, where you can get fresh jams, pies, and salsas using locally grown products.

For a more authentic experience, visit the Red Cliff Tribal Arts and Crafts Museum, where you can purchase locally crafted traditional art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore has a rich history that is preserved in the local culture. The area was home to the Ojibwe people, who lived in the region long before the arrival of Europeans. Visitors can learn more about their way of life and the traditions that have been passed down for generations at exhibitions and festivals such as the Red Cliff Traditional Powwow.

Best Time to Visit Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

The best time to visit the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is during the summer months when the climate is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Visitors can also enjoy seasonal events such as the Bayfield Apple Festival and the Apostle Islands Sled Dog Race.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Do:

  • Respect nature and leave no trace
  • Plan for all weather conditions
  • Obtain permits for fishing, camping, or other activities that require them
  • Hire a guide to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience
  • Ask permission before entering private property

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural resources
  • Feed or disturb wildlife
  • Trespass on private land
  • Neglect safety precautions
  • Build a fire except in designated areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Visitors should be aware of the risks that come with exploring a wild and remote area like Apostle Islands National Lakeshore. These risks include exposure to wildlife and inclement weather, as well as uneven terrain and rapidly changing water conditions. Visitors should hire expert guides and follow safety protocols, especially when participating in outdoor activities and water sports.

Health in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Visitors should take proper precautions to protect themselves from the sun, bugs, and other outdoor hazards. Since there are no medical facilities on most of the islands, it is also recommended that visitors carry a first aid kit.

Getting to Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

The closest airport to Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is the Duluth International Airport, which is approximately 2 hours away. Another option is the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport, which is approximately 4 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

Greyhound Bus Lines offer service to Washburn, Wisconsin, which is about a 15-minute drive from the park.

Other Options

Other options include driving, which is the most common way of getting to the park, and taking a train to the nearby towns of Hudson and Bayfield.

How to Get Around in Apostle Islands National Lakeshore

The best way to explore the islands is by kayak or boat, but visitors can also rent a bike, take a guided tour, or hike.

Where to Go Next from Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, make sure to visit other nearby destinations such as the charming towns of Bayfield, Washburn and Cornucopia. Hayward and other nearby towns offer other outdoor recreation areas, and the Wisconsin Senator Louis V. Hanson Wildlife Area is also located nearby and is home to a variety of wildlife, including tundra and trumpeter swans, Canada Geese, and Sandhill Cranes.

Conclusion

Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is a place of diverse natural beauty, where visitors can immerse themselves in a rich cultural heritage. With its stunning scenery, unique wildlife, and thrilling outdoor activities, this area offers something for everyone. Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventure-packed outdoor experience, Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is the perfect destination.
