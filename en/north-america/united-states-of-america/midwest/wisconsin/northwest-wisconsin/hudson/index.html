Introduction to Hudson

Located in Northwest Wisconsin in the Midwest region of the United States, Hudson is a charming town filled with natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Hudson is conveniently located near Minneapolis-St. Paul, making it easily accessible for travelers. With a rich history and a vibrant arts scene, there’s something for everyone in Hudson.

List of Places to Visit in Hudson

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Hudson has plenty of attractions to explore. Here are some top must-see destinations in Hudson:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Octagon House Museum: Built in 1855, this unique eight-sided house is a must-visit for history buffs. Take a guided tour to learn more about life in the mid-19th century.
  • Phipps Center for the Arts: This cultural center features rotating exhibitions, live theater, music performances, and more.
  • St. Croix County Historical Society: Dedicated to preserving the history of the area, this museum features exhibits on local industries, archaeology, and more.

Natural Attractions of Hudson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Willow River State Park: This beautiful state park offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and swimming spots. Don’t miss the iconic Willow Falls.
  • St. Croix River: Take a scenic boat tour or rent a kayak to explore this picturesque river.
  • Birkmose Park: A popular spot for fishing, this park also has hiking trails and a playground for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudson

Hudson is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some top routes to check out:

  • St. Croix County Hiking Trail: This 11.2-mile trail follows the St. Croix River and offers a variety of terrains and scenic views.
  • Hudson-Afton Trail: This 18-mile paved trail connects Hudson to Afton, Minnesota and offers beautiful river views.
  • Willow River State Park Trails: With over 13 miles of hiking trails, Willow River State Park is a must-visit for hikers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Hudson: Activities and Tours

Hudson has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some top things to do in Hudson:

  • Hop on a scenic boat tour of the St. Croix River
  • Rent a kayak or stand-up paddleboard for a leisurely paddle
  • Take a guided tour of the historic Octagon House Museum
  • Attend a live performance at the Phipps Center for the Arts
  • Go fishing or swimming in Willow River State Park
  • Explore the downtown shops and restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Hudson

Hudson is known for its food and drink scene, with plenty of local favorites to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese curds: A Wisconsin staple, fried cheese curds are a must-try when visiting Hudson.
  • Walleye: This freshwater fish is a local favorite and can be found on many restaurant menus.
  • Bratwurst: Served with sauerkraut and mustard, this German sausage is a classic Wisconsin dish.

Local Beverages

  • Leinenkugel’s Beer: This Wisconsin brewery is famous for its Shandy beer, but also offers a variety of other brews to try.
  • Knoke’s Chocolates and Nuts Hot Cocoa: This Hudson-based shop creates delicious hot cocoa mix with local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Hudson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hudson has plenty of lodging options to fit every traveler’s needs. From cozy bed and breakfasts to full-service hotels, here are some top choices:

  • Phipps Inn Bed and Breakfast: This charming Victorian-style inn offers a relaxing stay in the heart of Hudson.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham: Affordable and conveniently located, this hotel is great for budget travelers.
  • Hudson House Grand Hotel: If you’re looking for an upscale stay, this boutique hotel offers luxurious accommodations in a historic setting.

What to Buy in Hudson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to take home? Check out these local products in Hudson:

  • Knoke’s Chocolates and Nuts: This local shop creates handmade chocolates and candy with high-quality ingredients.
  • Caramel Crisp Cafe: This classic cafe serves up popcorn, root beer, and other goodies that make great gifts.
  • Willow River Olive Oil Co.: This specialty shop offers high-quality olive oils, vinegars, and other cooking ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudson

Wisconsin is known for its friendly locals and laidback culture, and Hudson is no exception. Visitors should expect a warm welcome and a relaxed atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Hudson

Hudson is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Summer is the busiest time and offers the warmest weather for outdoor activities. Fall is a great time to visit for the beautiful fall foliage, and winter is perfect for snowshoeing and cross-country skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudson

When visiting Hudson, keep these tips in mind:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking and kayaking.
  • Try the local cuisine, including Wisconsin cheese and bratwurst.
  • Attend a live performance at the Phipps Center for the Arts.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone eats meat or dairy. Hudson offers plenty of vegetarian and vegan options.
  • Leave without trying the local hot cocoa mix from Knoke’s Chocolates and Nuts.
  • Forget to pack appropriate clothing for the weather.

Safety and Health

Hudson is a safe destination with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions and be aware of their surroundings. As with any destination, it’s important to have proper travel insurance and vaccinations.

Safety in Hudson

While there are no major safety concerns in Hudson, it’s important to be aware of wildlife in the area. Bear and deer sightings are common, especially in the state parks.

Health in Hudson

Visitors should be aware of the risks of tick-borne illnesses, as ticks are common in the area. It’s important to check for ticks after spending time outdoors and to wear appropriate clothing and insect repellent.

Getting to Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hudson is conveniently located near Minneapolis-St. Paul, making it easily accessible by car or plane. Here are some transportation options for getting to Hudson:

Airports Near Hudson

  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport: This major airport is located approximately 30 miles from Hudson.

Bus Stations Near Hudson

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is located approximately 20 miles from Hudson in St. Paul, Minnesota.

Other Options

  • Car: Hudson is easily accessible by car, and there are several major highways that run through the area.
  • Train: While there is no train station in Hudson, Amtrak offers service to nearby St. Paul.

How to Get Around in Hudson

The best way to get around Hudson is by car, as many attractions and destinations are spread out. Taxis and Uber are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Midwest, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Minneapolis-St. Paul: This major metropolitan area is located just a short drive from Hudson and offers plenty of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.
  • Lake Pepin: This beautiful lake is located approximately 40 miles from Hudson and offers boating, fishing, and relaxing lakeside activities.
  • Apostle Islands: Located approximately 120 miles from Hudson, this group of islands on Lake Superior is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Hudson, Wisconsin is a charming destination with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, you’ll find plenty to explore in this beautiful town. From the outdoor adventures to the food and drink scene, Hudson is a must-visit destination in the Midwest region of the United States.
