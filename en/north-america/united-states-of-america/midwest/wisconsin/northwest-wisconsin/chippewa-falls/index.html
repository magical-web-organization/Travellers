Introduction to Chippewa Falls

Located in the Northwest region of Wisconsin, Chippewa Falls is a vibrant city characterized by its historic charm, scenic natural beauty, and thriving cultural scene. The city is known for its world-class breweries, which have earned it the nickname “The Brew City.” With its stunning landscape that includes forests, lakes, and rivers, Chippewa Falls offers a variety of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to fishing and boating. In addition to these fantastic experiences, Chippewa Falls is home to many cultural institutions and museums that showcase the city’s rich history and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Chippewa Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Chippewa County Historical Society

First on the list is the Chippewa County Historical Society, which features a museum filled with exhibits and artifacts that showcase the city’s rich history. The museum features displays about the city’s early inhabitants, industrial heritage, and agricultural traditions.

2. Heyde Center for the Arts

The Heyde Center for the Arts is another must-visit destination in Chippewa Falls. Its stunning architecture houses a theater, art exhibits, and workshops. The center features year-round performances and exhibitions, making it a perfect spot to experience the local arts scene.

Natural Attractions of Chippewa Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Irvine Park

Irvine Park is a beautiful outdoor space with over 300 acres of green space that also houses a zoo and many hiking trails. Visitors can also fish or paddleboat on its lake or have a picnic in the park’s abundant picnic areas.

2. Glen Loch Dam

The Glen Loch Dam is an impressive sight that also offers relaxing views for visitors looking to unwind. The Dam isn’t too big so visitors can hike around to the other side to enjoy the tranquil views of the Chippewa River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chippewa Falls

Chippewa Falls offers many opportunities for hikers and bikers looking to explore the great outdoors. One of the most scenic routes is the Old Abe State Trail, a 20-mile path that runs from Chippewa Falls to Cornell. The trail is suitable for hiking, biking, or even cross-country skiing in the winter months.

Best Things to Do in Chippewa Falls: Activities and Tours

1. Brewery Tours

In “The Brew City,” it’s no surprise that brewery tours are a top activity in Chippewa Falls. Leinenkugel’s Brewery is the oldest brewery in the city and also serves as our flagship. Visitors can discover how the famous “Leinie’s” beer is made and enjoy some samples.

2. Riverfest

Chippewa Falls is also known for unique events such as Riverfest. This is a festival attracting visitors in the summer, featuring an outdoor concert, carnival rides, pony rides, and much more. Riverfest is held at Coop’s Pizza every July.

Food and Drink Scene of Chippewa Falls

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Chippewa Falls would be complete without trying its local specialties. Dairy State Cheese is a must-go for anyone who loves cheese of any type. Uncle Mike’s Bakery makes the best doughnuts in the area, and Burnhearts Restaurant and Pub offers guests classic Wisconsin fare.

Local Beverages

As the “Brew City,” Chippewa Falls boasts many breweries and pubs. In addition to Leinenkugel’s, the local favorite Elk Creek Homebrewing Company, which offers visitors a chance to experience the city’s vibrant beer culture.

What to Buy in Chippewa Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chippewa Falls is home to many local shops and businesses that offer unique and handmade items. Visitors can find one-of-a-kind souvenirs at shops like the Tangled Up in Hue, the Brickhouse Goods, and Double Decker Antiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Chippewa Falls

Chippewa Falls is a welcoming and friendly city with a rich cultural heritage. Locals are proud of their tradition of exceptional beer-making and value the tight-knit community woven over the years. Politeness and friendliness are the norms when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Chippewa Falls

The best time to visit Chippewa Falls is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild, and a lot of outdoor activities are possible. Summers can be crowded, so aim for early or late in the season for fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chippewa Falls

Do:

  • Try the famous beer brewed in the city.
  • Visit the local museums and cultural institutions.
  • Experience the outdoor activities like hiking and fishing.
  • Shop at local places for one-of-a-kind finds.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Disrespect the natural environment while hiking or biking.
  • Arrive without a solid itinerary.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chippewa Falls

Chippewa Falls is known for its laid-back and welcoming vibe, with low crime rates. Visitors should generally take the same precautions they would in any large city, including being aware of their surroundings and avoiding high-risk areas.

Health in Chippewa Falls

Chippewa Falls has a modern medical system with quality hospitals and clinics. Visitors can be sure of excellent health care facilities should the need arise. It’s always good to carry your current medications and travel insurance with you.

Getting to Chippewa Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chippewa Falls

The city has its regional airport, Chippewa Valley Regional Airport, which is connected with other cities in the Midwest. The closest international airport is Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport, just less than two hours away.

Bus Stations Near Chippewa Falls

Greyhound and Indian Trails have several bus stations in the area, and visitors can take a bus ride from Minneapolis or Milwaukee.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Chippewa Falls is accessible from Hwy 29, which connects it to other cities in Wisconsin and the broader Midwest.

How to Get Around in Chippewa Falls

Chippewa Falls is relatively small and walkable, and most attractions are close to each other. Visitors can rent a bike or even take a guided tour to experience the city and see its beautiful surroundings.
