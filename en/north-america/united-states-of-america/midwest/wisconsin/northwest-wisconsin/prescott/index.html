Introduction to Prescott

Nestled in the scenic St. Croix River Valley in Northwest Wisconsin lies the charming town of Prescott. Known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Prescott offers a range of experiences for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or indulging in some delicious local cuisine, Prescott has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with everything you need to plan your perfect visit to Prescott.

List of Places to Visit in Prescott

Prescott is home to a plethora of diverse attractions guaranteed to pique everyone’s interest. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Prescott:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Great River Road Visitor & Learning Center: Located at the confluence of the St. Croix and Mississippi rivers, this museum offers exhibits and interactive displays about the history, culture, and ecology of the area.
  • Freedome Park: A park located in downtown Prescott honoring military veterans, past and present.
  • St. Croix County Historical Society: Offers visitors with an opportunity to explore the area’s rich history, including exhibits on farming, logging, and river transportation.
  • The Octagon House: An impressive estate house built-in 1856. The house features an eight-sided shape room that is unique in the Midwest. In summer, the leafy yard is an ideal place for picnicking or family gatherings.

Natural Attractions of Prescott: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. Croix National Scenic Riverway: A breathtaking area of winding freshwater streams, steep bluffs, and glimmering waterfalls, offering endless opportunities for outdoor recreation, hiking, and scenic drives.
  • Willow River State Park: Situated along the scenic St. Croix River, the park features nature trails, a beach, and fishing.
  • Kinnickinnic State Park: A birdwatcher’s haven that is home to bald eagles, great blue herons, and egrets. Kinnickinnic State Park is the perfect place to enjoy a hike, fishing, or canoe trip while taking in the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prescott

Prescott boasts some of the best trails in the region, offering visitors an unparalleled opportunity to explore the area’s pristine natural beauty. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Prescott:

  • Great River Road State Trail: A 24-mile paved trail that starts in Prescott and runs along the bluffs overlooking the Mississippi River, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Ice Age National Scenic Trail: A 1,200-mile hiking trail that meanders through Wisconsin, passing through some of the state’s most scenic natural areas.
  • The Prescott Circle Trail: A 7.1-mile loop trail that offers scenic views of the St. Croix River and surrounding bluffs.

Best Things to Do in Prescott: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some fun and excitement, Prescott has plenty of activities and tours to offer, ranging from guided riverboat tours to zip-lining adventures. Here are some of the best things to do in Prescott:

  • Take a Riverboat Tour on the Mississippi: Step aboard an authentic riverboat and enjoy a relaxing tour down the scenic Mississippi River.
  • Zip-Line Adventure: Zip through the towering trees on the high-flying Zipline Adventure Course.
  • Take a Sunset Kayak Tour: Paddle along the calm waters of the St. Croix River as the sun sets over the western horizon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prescott

A week-long itinerary will get you the most out of your visit to Prescott. Here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your week:

Food and Drink Scene of Prescott

Prescott’s cuisine has a little bit of everything, from classic American dishes to unique local flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese curds: A regional favorite, these fried, bite-sized pieces of cheese are a Wisconsin must-try.
  • Walleye: A freshwater fish that’s a staple on many Wisconsin menus.
  • Bratwurst: A hearty, flavorful sausage made from pork, beef, or veal and traditionally served on a fluffy bun.

Local Beverages

  • Leinenkugel’s Beer: A Wisconsin classic, Leinenkugel’s serves up a range of refreshing beers that are ideal for sipping on a hot summer day.
  • Wollersheim Winery & Distillery: Produces some of Wisconsin’s finest wines and spirits. Try the award-winning Prairie Fume, a semi-sweet white wine.

Where to Stay in Prescott: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Finding the best place to stay is crucial for an enjoyable and comfortable trip. Prescott offers a range of affordable lodging options, from comfortable B&Bs to high-end hotels. Here are our top picks:

  • St. Croix River Inn: A luxurious hotel located on the St. Croix River, offering stunning views of the water and surrounding bluffs.
  • Schooners Landing: A quaint B&B located in the heart of downtown Prescott, offering comfortable rooms and a homey feel.
  • Buckskin Bed and Breakfast: A sprawling bed and breakfast located just a few miles from downtown Prescott, offering comfortable rooms, a hot tub and relaxation area, and a delicious homemade breakfast to start your day.

What to Buy in Prescott: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without bringing home some souvenirs. If you’re looking for some unique local products, stop by these stores in Prescott:

  • Prescott Artisan Market: A marketplace for local artisans and vendors, offering handmade goods ranging from pottery to jewelry.
  • Wild Mountain Garden: A gift shop located in historic downtown Prescott, offering an impressive selection of locally-sourced artisanal goods, such as soaps, candles, and handbags.

Local Culture and Customs of Prescott

Prescott is a friendly and welcoming town, with locals who are always eager to show visitors around and share their knowledge of the area. Most shops and restaurants are closed on Mondays and Tuesdays, so keep this in mind when planning your visit.

Best Time to Visit Prescott

The best time to visit Prescott is in the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are at their best. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, with mild temperatures and stunning fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prescott

Do:

  • Do try some of the local cuisine, including cheese curds and bratwurst.
  • Do visit the St. Croix River for a relaxing paddle or hike.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray for your outdoor adventures.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prescott

Prescott is a relatively safe town, but it’s always important to take precautions when exploring any new area. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.
  • Stay in well-lit areas and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight and keep your personal belongings close by.

Health in Prescott

Prescott has no major health concerns and is a generally healthy environment, but travelers should take precautions to avoid tick and mosquito-borne illnesses by wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Prescott: Transportation Options and Logistics

Prescott is primarily accessible by car, with several major highways in the region. However, there are also several alternative transportation options for travelers.

Airports Near Prescott

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport: Located approximately one hour and 20 minutes from Prescott.

Bus Stations Near Prescott

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Hudson, WI, just a short drive from Prescott.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at major airports and in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Prescott

The best way to get around Prescott is by car. However, visitors can also use bikes or their own two feet to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Prescott: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider, including:

  • St. Paul, MN: Just a short drive from Prescott, St. Paul is a bustling city steeped in history and culture.
  • Minneapolis, MN: Less than an hour and a half away from Prescott, Minneapolis is a vibrant city with plenty of unique attractions, including the Mall of America and the Walker Art Center.
  • Taylors Falls, MN: Located just across the river from the St. Croix River, Taylors Falls is known for its stunning natural beauty and offers plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, canoeing, and apple-picking.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and welcoming locals, Prescott is the perfect travel destination for anyone looking for a mix of culture, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your perfect trip to Prescott today!
