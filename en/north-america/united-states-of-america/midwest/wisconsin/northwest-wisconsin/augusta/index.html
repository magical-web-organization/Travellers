Introduction to Augusta

Located in the northwest region of Wisconsin, Augusta is a scenic town that offers visitors a host of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and excellent dining options. Augusta is an ideal destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and foodies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Augusta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Augusta Area Historical Museum

The Augusta Area Historical Museum showcases the town’s rich history. Exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and documents which document the town’s development over the years. Visitors can learn about the town’s railroad history, local families, and important events.

Dells Mill Museum

The Dells Mill Museum is a fascinating destination that is a must-visit for those interested in the area’s history. The museum was built in 1864 and offers visitors a glimpse into the town’s past with a collection of historic artifacts, including antique farm equipment, historic buildings, and exhibits on logging.

Natural Attractions of Augusta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Augusta State Wildlife Area

The Augusta State Wildlife Area is an excellent destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The state wildlife area encompasses 1,244 acres and provides visitors with ample opportunities for wildlife watching, hiking, hunting, and fishing. The area features several ponds and a variety of bird species.

Lake Eau Claire County Park

Lake Eau Claire County Park is a peaceful destination that offers breathtaking views of Lake Eau Claire. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including swimming, boating, fishing, and walking trails. The park has over 800 acres to explore and features multiple campgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Augusta

Old Abe State Trail

The Old Abe State Trail is a 19.5-mile trail that runs through Augusta, connecting Chippewa Falls and Cornell. The trail is open year-round and provides visitors with ample opportunities for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling in the winter. The trail is named after the state’s Civil War eagle mascot, Old Abe.

Best Things to Do in Augusta: Activities and Tours

Eau Claire River Kayaking Tour

The Eau Claire River Kayaking tour is a scenic and exciting way to explore Augusta’s natural beauty. The tour includes a two-hour trip down the river, taking in the stunning views, and spotting local wildlife. The tour is suitable for all levels of experience.

Wine Tasting at Autumn Harvest Winery

Autumn Harvest Winery is a family-run winery located just a few miles outside of Augusta. Visitors can enjoy a tasting of the winery’s local wines, made from the area’s finest grapes. The winery also offers visitors a range of seasonal events, including live music and grape-stomping.

Food and Drink Scene of Augusta

Must-Try Dishes

Cheese Curds

Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and Augusta is no exception. Don’t miss the opportunity to try the town’s famous cheese curds. The deep-fried delights are the perfect midday snack or appetizer.

Chippewa Valley Burger

The Chippewa Valley Burger is a local favorite and a must-try for visitors. The burger features locally sourced beef, cheese, and a variety of toppings, served on a brioche bun.

Local Beverages

Leinie’s Beer

Leinie’s Beer is a Wisconsin favorite and a must-try for visitors. The brewery has been producing beer since 1867 and offers a range of delicious, locally brewed beers, including the classic Leinie’s Lager and Honey Weiss.

Autumn Harvest Winery

Autumn Harvest Winery is an excellent destination for wine lovers. Visitors can enjoy a variety of locally made wines, made from the area’s finest grapes.

Where to Stay in Augusta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Inn on Lake Wissota

The Inn on Lake Wissota offers visitors a luxurious stay and a stunning view of Lake Wissota. The inn features a range of amenities, including a lakeside pool, hot tub, and complimentary breakfast.

Teepee Motel

The Teepee Motel is a unique and affordable option for visitors. The motel features teepee-shaped rooms, modern amenities, and a location near local attractions.

What to Buy in Augusta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Augusta is known for its maple syrup, which is tapped from the area’s maple trees. Visitors can purchase locally made syrup from the Augusta Farmer’s Market or local shops.

Handmade Pottery

Augusta is home to several talented ceramic artists who create beautiful handmade pottery. Visitors can purchase unique, locally made pottery from local artisan shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Augusta

Augusta is a tight-knit community that values its heritage, traditions, and natural beauty. Visitors should respect the local customs and sensibilities and avoid littering or disturbing the town’s natural environment.

Best Time to Visit Augusta

Augusta offers visitors with a unique experience during each season. Spring and summer offer warm weather and ample opportunities for outdoor activities, including hiking and boating. Fall offers a stunning array of autumn colors and harvest festivals. Winter offers skiing, snowshoeing, and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Augusta

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment
  • Say hello to locals
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the town’s natural environment
  • Use offensive language or behavior
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Augusta

Augusta is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should still exercise common sense and take basic safety precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables while exploring the town.

Health in Augusta

Augusta is home to several medical clinics and hospitals. Visitors should also bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and other essentials when exploring the great outdoors.

Getting to Augusta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Augusta

The closest airport is Chippewa Valley Regional Airport, which is located approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Augusta

Greyhound has a bus station in Eau Claire, which is approximately 25 miles away.

Other Options

Renting a car is the easiest way to get to Augusta due to its remote location.

How to Get Around in Augusta

Most visitors to Augusta will want to have their own vehicle to explore the town and surrounding attractions. There are also bike rentals available for those interested in exploring the Old Abe State Trail.

Where to Go Next from Augusta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eau Claire

Eau Claire is a vibrant city located approximately 30 minutes from Augusta. The city’s highlights include access to the Chippewa River State Trail, the Children’s Museum of Eau Claire, and the Lowes Creek County Park.

Cadott

Cadott is a small town located approximately 20 minutes from Augusta. The town is known for its country music festival, Country Fest, held annually in June.

Conclusion

Augusta is a special destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. With its natural beauty, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, it’s no surprise why this town remains a favorite among visitors to the Midwest. Plan your perfect trip to Augusta today!
