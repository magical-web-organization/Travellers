Cornucopia, located in the northwest region of Wisconsin, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural history, Cornucopia is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of big city life. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to this charming village with ease.

Introduction to Cornucopia

Cornucopia is a small village situated on the shore of Lake Superior, the largest freshwater lake in the world. With a population of just over 100 people, Cornucopia is the epitome of a quaint, peaceful, and friendly community. The village is surrounded by lush forests, pristine beaches, and shimmering blue waters that stretch as far as the eye can see.

List of Places to Visit in Cornucopia

Cornucopia offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the must-visit places in Cornucopia.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bell Museum and Fish Hatchery: This museum showcases the rich history and cultural heritage of Cornucopia and the surrounding region. Here, you can explore exhibits on the area’s Native American history, early European settlement, logging, and fishing traditions.

Natural Attractions of Cornucopia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Meyers Beach: This beautiful sandy beach is the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, hiking, and taking in the stunning views of Lake Superior.

  • Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: This national park comprises 21 islands and a 12-mile stretch of mainland on Lake Superior. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, including camping, kayaking, hiking, and wildlife watching.

  • Siskiwit Falls: Located within the Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest, this waterfall is a popular spot for hiking, picnicking, and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cornucopia

For those who love outdoor adventure, Cornucopia has plenty of hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Lost Creek Trail: This 8-mile trail winds through the forest and along the lake, offering breathtaking views of the waterfalls, wildflowers, and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Cornucopia: Activities and Tours

Cornucopia offers a wide range of activities and tours that are perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Cornucopia:

  • Kayaking Tour: Explore the breathtaking coastline of Lake Superior on a guided kayaking tour.

  • Fishing Expedition: Try your luck at catching trout, salmon, and other freshwater fish in the clear waters of Lake Superior.

Food and Drink Scene of Cornucopia

Cornucopia has a small but vibrant food and drink scene that is sure to satisfy any palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Rice Soup: This hearty soup is made with local ingredients and is a staple of the region’s cuisine.

  • Lake Trout: This freshwater fish is a delicacy in Cornucopia, and you can find it served in many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • South Shore Brewery: This microbrewery produces a range of delicious, locally brewed beers that are perfect for relaxing after a day of exploring.

Local Culture and Customs of Cornucopia

The people of Cornucopia are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and traditions. One of the most important customs in the region is the tradition of wild rice harvesting, which is celebrated in many local festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Cornucopia

The best time to visit Cornucopia is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the water is perfect for swimming and other water-based activities.
