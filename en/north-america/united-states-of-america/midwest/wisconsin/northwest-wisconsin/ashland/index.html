Introduction to Ashland

Ashland is a charming city situated in Northern Wisconsin, a central location for outdoor adventure seekers. Scenic beauty, combined with rich history, makes it the perfect destination for anyone looking to experience authentic Wisconsin. Ashland is situated on the scenic Lake Superior, and its downtown offers visitors a range of attractions.

If you’re planning a trip to Ashland and looking for things to do, then you’ll want to read our comprehensive travel guide. Read on for the best places to visit in Ashland, top attractions, hiking/biking routes, hotel recommendations, food scene, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Ashland

Ashland offers a range of cultural and natural attractions. Here are the places that you must visit during your trip to Ashland:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ashland Historical Museum - Discover Ashland’s rich history, including the area’s maritime, railroad, and logging history.
  2. Chequamegon Bay Arts Council - A must-visit for art lovers, the Council features constantly changing exhibitions from local and national artists.
  3. Bay Theater - A historic venue that has been welcoming visitors since the 1920s. It hosts concerts, plays, movies, and more.

Natural Attractions of Ashland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Apostle Islands National Lakeshore - A must-visit for anyone visiting the area. The Apostle Islands are a group of 22 islands located off the coast of Lake Superior, known for their rugged beauty and scenic hiking trails.
  2. Copper Falls State Park - Located about 45 minutes from Ashland, this park features stunning waterfalls and beautiful rock formations.
  3. Northern Great Lakes Visitor Center - Learn about the history, ecology, and culture of Northern Great Lakes through interactive exhibits. A great place to start your trip to Ashland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashland

Ashland is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with several beautiful hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top routes for hikers and bikers:

  1. Morgan Falls Trail - A 2-mile trail leading to the stunning 70-foot Morgan Falls waterfall.
  2. Copper Falls Waterfall Trail - A hike through the Copper Falls State Park, offering scenic views of the park’s waterfalls.
  3. The Tri-County Corridor Trail - A 62-mile trail that runs from Ashland to Superior, open for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling.

Best Things to Do in Ashland: Activities and Tours

  1. Lake Superior Scenic Tour - A scenic tour that takes visitors through the best views of Lake Superior.
  2. Northwoods Adventures Paddle Boarding - An exciting water sport experience that is perfect for all ages.
  3. Blue Wave Adventures - A guided boat tour that takes visitors to explore Lake Superior, Apostle Islands, and other nearby attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashland

Here’s a week-long itinerary for your trip to Ashland:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Ashland Historical Museum
Day 2 Hike the Morgan Falls Trail and Copper Falls State Park
Day 3 Take a Lake Superior Scenic Tour and relax by the water
Day 4 Enjoy paddleboarding with Northwoods Adventures
Day 5 Spend the day exploring Apostle Islands National Lakeshore
Day 6 Take a guided boat tour with Blue Wave Adventures
Day 7 Indulge in food, drinks, and shopping in the downtown area

Food and Drink Scene of Ashland

Ashland has a burgeoning food and drink scene, with a range of locally sourced ingredients and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ashland:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pasties - A traditional British meat pie pastry stuffed with beef and potatoes.
  2. Fish Fry - A local favorite, the Fish Fry offers freshly caught fish and chips.
  3. Friday Night Seafood Buffet - A popular seafood buffet with a variety of dishes, located at the Bad River Lodge and Casino.

Local Beverages

  1. South Shore Brewery - Local craft brewery that offers a range of beers and tours.
  2. Deep Water Grille & Brewing Company - A family-owned brewery that brews several kinds of Madeline Island Brews.
  3. Northwoods Brew Pub - A local brewery known for its craft beer, burgers, and pizza.

Where to Stay in Ashland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Best Western The Hotel Chequamegon - A comfortable hotel with lake views and near historical attractions.
  2. AmericInn by Wyndham Ashland - A family-friendly hotel with an indoor pool and located near downtown.
  3. Super 8 by Wyndham Ashland - A budget-friendly hotel that offers guests free breakfast.

What to Buy in Ashland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ashland Baking Company - Shop for delicious baked goods like artisan bread, croissants, and muffin bread.
  2. Second Hand Rose - Browse a collection of antiques, collectibles, and vintage items.
  3. Ashland Area Farmers Market - Stock up on locally grown vegetables, fruits, honey, and flowers.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashland

Ashland has a rich cultural heritage, including its Ojibwe and European roots. Visitors should respect the local culture and customs during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Ashland

The best time to visit Ashland is from May to October when the weather is comfortable, and the natural attractions are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashland

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture.
  2. Visit Apostle Islands National Lakeshore.
  3. Take a scenic Lake Superior tour.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage any natural habitats.
  2. Ignore safety precautions while exploring the outdoors.
  3. Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashland

Ashland is generally a safe and peaceful place. However, visitors should use common sense and take basic security precautions.

Health in Ashland

Visitors to Ashland should carry any necessary prescription drugs, especially if the visit is during a more extended period, and carry health insurance.

Getting to Ashland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashland

The nearest airport to Ashland is the Duluth International Airport, located approximately 75 miles from Ashland.

Bus Stations Near Ashland

Greyhound Bus Lines serve Ashland three times per week, with routes from Minneapolis and Milwaukee.

Other Options

Some visitors arrive in Ashland by boat or personal aircraft but should confirm arrangements in advance.

How to Get Around in Ashland

Visitors can use rental cars or taxis to get around Ashland. Bicycle rentals are also available for visitors who want to explore the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Ashland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bayfield, Wisconsin - Just a 30-minute drive from Ashland, Bayfield is known for its apple orchards, fish bakes, and Apostle Islands Cruises, which operate from May to October.
  2. Duluth, Minnesota - Located just 2 hours from Ashland, visitors can explore Canal Park and the Great Lakes Aquarium.
  3. Madeline Island - A beautiful island located in Lake Superior, visitors can take a ferry ride to explore the island’s sandy beaches, historic sites, and forested trails.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Ashland is a hub of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or foodie, Ashland has something to offer for everyone. So what are you waiting for? Book your trip to Ashland today!
