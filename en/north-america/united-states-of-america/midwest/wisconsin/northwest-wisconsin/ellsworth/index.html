Introduction to Ellsworth

Ellsworth is a quaint village located in Northwestern Wisconsin. Its population is just over 3,000 people, and it’s known for its friendly and welcoming community. Ellsworth has a rich history and is home to several scenic views and natural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful vacation or an outdoor adventure, Ellsworth has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Ellsworth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pierce County Historical Association - Discover the history of Ellsworth and the surrounding area. The historical association hosts several exhibits and events throughout the year.
  • The Cheese Curd Capital of Wisconsin - Taste the famous cheese curds of Ellsworth, a local delicacy.

Natural Attractions of Ellsworth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Willow River State Park - Explore the trails of this beautiful state park and take in the scenic views. The park is home to several waterfalls, including the popular Willow Falls.
  • Ellsworth Community Orchard - Pick your own apples, berries, and other fruits at this beautiful orchard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ellsworth

  • The Great River State Trail - This 24-mile trail is perfect for hiking and biking. It’s surrounded by beautiful scenery and offers plenty of opportunities to spot wildlife.
  • The Ellsworth to River Falls Trail - This 7-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride. It runs along the scenic Kinnickinnic River.

Best Things to Do in Ellsworth: Activities and Tours

  • Ellsworth Cooperative Creamery - Take a tour of the famous creamery and learn how cheese curds, cheese, and ice cream are made.
  • Ellsworth Farmers Market - Visit the farmers market to buy local produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Ellsworth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese curds - Try the famous cheese curds of Ellsworth, a local favorite.
  • Walleye - This freshwater fish is popular in Wisconsin and is often served fried or grilled.
  • Bison burgers - Try a burger made from bison meat, a healthier alternative to beef.

Local Beverages

  • Leinenkugel’s Beer - This local brewery is popular for its classic and seasonal beers.
  • Wollersheim Winery - This Wisconsin winery produces award-winning wines and offers tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Ellsworth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Americas Best Value Inn - This hotel offers comfortable and affordable rooms and is located close to downtown Ellsworth.
  • The Ellsworth Motel - This cozy motel offers clean and spacious rooms and is a great option for families.
  • Airbnb - Explore the village’s hidden gems by staying at one of the many vacation rentals offered on Airbnb.

What to Buy in Ellsworth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese curds - Don’t leave Ellsworth without trying the famous cheese curds and bringing some home to share.
  • Fruit preserves and jams - Pick up some delicious preserves and jams made with locally grown fruits.
  • Handmade crafts - Ellsworth is home to several artisans who sell handmade jewelry, pottery, and art.

Local Culture and Customs of Ellsworth

Ellsworth is a friendly and welcoming community and values hard work, family, and good neighbors. It’s also known for its delicious cheese curds and local festivals such as the Cheese Curd Festival and Sweet Corn Festival.

Best Time to Visit Ellsworth

The best time to visit Ellsworth is from May to October when temperatures are mild, and outdoor activities are at their best. The summer months are particularly busy, so plan accordingly and book your accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ellsworth

Do:

  • Try the famous cheese curds
  • Explore the scenic views and natural attractions of the village
  • Attend local festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Overlook the quaint charm of downtown Ellsworth
  • Forget to book accommodation in advance during the busy summer months

Safety and Health

Safety in Ellsworth

Ellsworth is a safe village, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take precautions when exploring the outdoors and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Ellsworth

Medical facilities are located within the village, and emergency services are available. It’s important to bring any necessary medication or medical equipment with you on your trip.

Getting to Ellsworth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ellsworth

  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport - Located approximately 80 miles from Ellsworth, this airport provides convenient access to Northwest Wisconsin.

Bus Stations Near Ellsworth

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Hudson, WI, approximately 25 miles from Ellsworth.
  • Rochester Shuttle Service - Offers pick-up and drop-off services to and from the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport and surrounding areas.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Renting a car is a popular option for visitors who wish to explore the surrounding areas of Ellsworth.

How to Get Around in Ellsworth

  • Walking - Exploring the village on foot is a great way to discover its hidden gems.
  • Biking - Rent a bike or bring your own to explore the beautiful trails and scenic views of Ellsworth.
  • Driving - Renting a car or bringing your own will give you the freedom to explore the surrounding areas of Ellsworth.

Where to Go Next from Ellsworth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hudson - Explore the historic downtown of Hudson, located just 25 miles north of Ellsworth.
