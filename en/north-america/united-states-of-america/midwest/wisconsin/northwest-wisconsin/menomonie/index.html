Introduction to Menomonie

Menomonie, the county seat of Dunn County, is a charming city located in Northwest Wisconsin. With its rich history, stunning natural attractions, and lively downtown, Menomonie is a great place to visit for anyone who loves the great outdoors, cultural events, and small-town hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Menomonie

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Menomonie offers plenty of attractions and activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of the must-visit places in Menomonie:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wilson Place Mansion - A historic mansion built in 1859 that has been renovated and turned into a museum showcasing the history of Menomonie and its prominent citizens.
  • Russell J. Rassbach Heritage Museum - A local history museum featuring artifacts and exhibits related to the logging and lumbering industry that was once a major part of Menomonie’s economy.
  • Mabel Tainter Center for the Arts - A beautifully restored Victorian theater that hosts live performances and cultural events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Menomonie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hoffman Hills State Recreation Area - A 707-acre park that offers hiking and skiing trails, bird watching, and stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Red Cedar State Trail - A 14.5-mile trail that follows the scenic Red Cedar River, offering opportunities for biking, hiking, fishing, and picnicking.
  • Devil’s Punchbowl - A natural gorge and waterfall located within the Devil’s Punchbowl State Natural Area that can be reached by hiking through dense forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Menomonie

Menomonie is surrounded by beautiful natural areas, making it a haven for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes you can take:

  • Mound View Park Trail - A 1.5-mile hiking trail that loops around Mound View Park, offering scenic views of Lake Menomin and the surrounding prairies.
  • Irvington Loop - A 15-mile loop that follows a mix of paved and gravel roads through the rolling hills and farmlands of Dunn County.
  • Red Cedar State Trail - A 14.5-mile trail that follows the scenic Red Cedar River and offers opportunities for biking, hiking, fishing, and picnicking.

Best Things to Do in Menomonie: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Menomonie offers plenty of other activities and tours that cater to different interests. Some of the best things to do in Menomonie are:

  • Red Cedar Speedway - A dirt track racing venue featuring weekly races throughout the summer.
  • Off Broadway Banquet Center and Lounge - A restaurant and bar that offers live music, karaoke, and other entertainment on select nights.
  • Dunn County Fish and Game Association Shooting Range - A shooting range that offers a variety of shooting sports, including trap, skeet, and sporting clays.

If you’re planning to spend a week in Menomonie, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Menomonie

Menomonie may be a small town, but it offers a surprising variety of dining options, ranging from casual cafes to upscale restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burgers at Ted’s Pizza Palace - A family-owned pizza restaurant that serves delicious burgers made from locally sourced beef.
  • Fish Fry at Jake’s Supper Club - A classic Wisconsin dish that consists of battered and fried fish, served with tartar sauce and coleslaw.
  • Cheese Curds at Stout Ale House and Broadway Bowl - A classic Midwest snack that features deep-fried cheese curds, served with ranch dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Leinenkugel’s Beer - A Wisconsin-based beer company that produces a variety of ales and lagers, including the popular Summer Shandy.
  • Rush River Brewing Company Beer - A local brewery that specializes in small-batch craft beers, made with locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Menomonie

Menomonie has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its landmarks, museums, and festivals. Here are some of the important cultural and historical events in Menomonie:

  • UW-Stout Annual Pow Wow - A Native American cultural event that features dancing, music, and traditional food.
  • Memorial Day Parade - A parade that honors the sacrifices of military veterans and their families.
  • Taste of the Valley - A food festival that celebrates local cuisine, wine, and beer.

Best Time to Visit Menomonie

Menomonie has a temperate climate with warm summers and cold winters, making it an ideal destination for outdoor activities year-round. The best time to visit Menomonie depends on your preferences and interests:

  • Spring (March - May) - The weather in spring is mild and perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring Menomonie’s natural attractions.
  • Summer (June - August) - The peak tourist season when Menomonie hosts several festivals and outdoor events.
  • Fall (September - November) - The foliage in Menomonie is stunning in the fall, making it a great time for hiking and scenic drives.
  • Winter (December - February) - The snow-covered landscape offers opportunities for winter sports such as skiing and snowmobiling.

Safety in Menomonie
