Introduction to Northwest Wisconsin

Nestled in the Midwest region of the United States, Northwest Wisconsin is a gem for nature-lovers and adventure-seekers. The region boasts extensive forests, scenic rivers, and beautiful lakes that offer a plethora of outdoor activities. From kayaking to hiking to biking, there is no shortage of adventure in Northwest Wisconsin. This guide aims to acquaint tourists with all the beautiful and hidden gems of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest Wisconsin

This section highlights the best places to visit in Northwest Wisconsin. From culturally important attractions to natural wonders, this list is sure to have something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Chippewa Valley Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the lives of the people who settled in the Chippewa Valley area. Visitors can explore the history and culture of the region through interactive exhibits and guided tours.

  2. Wisconsin Historical Society: This organization manages several historical sites, including the Wisconsin Historical Museum in Madison. The museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the state’s history and culture.

  3. Spooner Railroad Park: Located in the town of Spooner, the Railroad Park gives visitors the opportunity to explore the history of the railroad in Wisconsin. The park has several exhibits, including a restored caboose and a collection of historic railroad artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Northwest Wisconsin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: The Apostle Islands National Lakeshore is a group of 21 islands that offer visitors a mix of scenic beauty and outdoor activities. The islands have several hiking and biking trails, sandy beaches, and crystal-clear waters that are ideal for kayaking and snorkeling.

  2. Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest: The Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest is a vast forest that spreads across more than a million acres. Visitors can explore the forest’s numerous hiking trails, fishing spots, and picnic areas. The forest is also home to several wildlife species, including black bears, elk, and wolves.

  3. Copper Falls State Park: The Copper Falls State Park is known for its spectacular waterfalls, including the aptly named Copper Falls. Visitors can hike the North Country Trail, bike, fish, and even camp in the park’s scenic campgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest Wisconsin

Northwest Wisconsin has plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer beautiful views and challenging terrain. Here are some of the best hikes and bike trails in the region:

  1. Ice Age National Scenic Trail: The Ice Age National Scenic Trail offers hikers a 1,200-mile journey through Wisconsin’s changing landscapes. The trail is known for its scenic views of glacial lakes, river valleys, and rolling hills.

  2. Wild Rivers State Trail: This 98-mile trail is ideal for biking and hiking. The trail runs through the Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest and passes by several beautiful lakes.

  3. Tuscobia State Trail: The Tuscobia State Trail stretches for 74 miles and is open to hiking, biking, and snowmobiling. The trail follows an old railroad bed and runs through the forests and wetlands of Northwest Wisconsin.

Best Things to Do in Northwest Wisconsin: Activities and Tours

Northwest Wisconsin offers a variety of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some of the best ones to try:

  1. Kayaking Tours: With its numerous calm rivers and crystal-clear lakes, Northwest Wisconsin is a kayaker’s paradise. Several tour operators offer guided kayaking tours, including some that take visitors to the Apostle Islands.

  2. Fly Fishing: Northwest Wisconsin is home to several scenic rivers that offer excellent fly fishing opportunities. Visitors can try their luck catching trout, bass, and pike.

  3. Boat Tours: Several boat tour operators offer sightseeing tours of the Apostle Islands and other scenic spots around the lakes and rivers of Northwest Wisconsin.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest Wisconsin

The food and drink scene in Northwest Wisconsin is a mix of traditional and contemporary fare. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish Fry: Freshwater fish is a staple in the Midwest, and Northwest Wisconsin is no exception. A Friday night fish fry is a tradition in the region.

  2. Cheese Curds: No trip to Wisconsin is complete without trying cheese curds. These deep-fried chunks of cheese are crispy on the outside and gooey on the inside.

  3. Bratwurst: Another Midwest favorite, the bratwurst is a type of sausage that is grilled and served on a bun with sauerkraut and mustard.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer: Wisconsin is known for its beer, and there are several breweries in Northwest Wisconsin worth trying. Notable breweries include Leinenkugel’s and Fat Tire.

  2. Cider: Apple cider is a refreshing drink that is popular in Northwest Wisconsin, especially during the fall season.

Where to Stay in Northwest Wisconsin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwest Wisconsin has several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that offer comfortable accommodations to travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in the region:

  1. The Cobblestone Inn & Suites: The Cobblestone Inn & Suites is a boutique hotel located in downtown Spooner. The hotel offers free breakfast, Wi-Fi, and parking.

  2. Black Bear Lodge: Located on the shores of Little St. Germain Lake, Black Bear Lodge is a cozy bed and breakfast that offers stunning views of the lake. The lodge has several cabins that are ideal for families or groups of friends.

  3. Eagle River Inn & Resort: The Eagle River Inn & Resort is a top-rated hotel located on the banks of the Eagle River. The hotel has an indoor pool, a fitness center, and a restaurant that serves traditional Wisconsin cuisine.

What to Buy in Northwest Wisconsin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northwest Wisconsin is known for its local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in the region:

  1. Cheese: Wisconsin is known for its cheese, and Northwest Wisconsin has several cheese factories that sell a variety of cheese products.

  2. Maple Syrup: Pure maple syrup is a popular product in Wisconsin, and visitors can buy it directly from the producers in the region.

  3. Handmade Crafts: Many local artisans sell handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and wooden items.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest Wisconsin

Northwest Wisconsin has a rich and vibrant culture that is influenced by its natural surroundings. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the laid-back way of life in the region.

Best Time to Visit Northwest Wisconsin

The best time to visit Northwest Wisconsin is from May to October when the weather is mild and conducive to outdoor activities. The fall season, in particular, is spectacular as the leaves change color, and the temperatures are cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest Wisconsin

Do:

  1. Embrace the local culture and customs.

  2. Take advantage of the natural surroundings and try outdoor activities.

  3. Try the local food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  1. Underestimate the weather; be prepared for sudden changes in temperature.

  2. Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  3. Be rude or disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Northwest Wisconsin is a safe region to visit, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their health and safety.

Safety in Northwest Wisconsin

  1. Avoid hiking or kayaking alone, particularly in remote areas.

  2. Be cautious of wildlife, particularly black bears.

  3. Check weather conditions regularly and be prepared for sudden changes in temperature.

Health in Northwest Wisconsin

  1. Be sure to pack any necessary medication, especially if you have allergies or other chronic conditions.

  2. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, particularly during outdoor activities.

  3. Practice social distancing and wear a mask as necessary to prevent the spread of COVID-19.

Getting to Northwest Wisconsin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northwest Wisconsin is accessible by several modes of transportation, including air, bus, and car.

Airports Near Northwest Wisconsin

  1. Chippewa Valley Regional Airport: Located in Eau Claire, Wisconsin, this airport serves several airlines, including Delta, United, and American.

  2. Rhinelander-Oneida County Airport: Located in Rhinelander, Wisconsin, this airport serves Delta and United airlines.

Bus Stations Near Northwest Wisconsin

Greyhound and Jefferson Lines both have bus stations in Eau Claire and Duluth.

Other Options

Northwest Wisconsin is also accessible by car via the major highways that run through the region, including Interstates 94 and 35.

How to Get Around in Northwest Wisconsin

Northwest Wisconsin is best explored by car, as it gives tourists the freedom to explore the many attractions in the region. Rental cars are available at most major airports in the area.

Where to Go Next from Northwest Wisconsin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northwest Wisconsin is conveniently located near several other tourist destinations, including:

  1. Madeline Island: This island is located in Lake Superior and is reachable by ferry from the town of Bayfield.

  2. Minneapolis, Minnesota: Minneapolis is located just a few hours south of Northwest Wisconsin and offers a mix of urban attractions and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Northwest Wisconsin is an adventurous and scenic destination that offers something for everyone. This guide has provided a comprehensive overview of the region, from its natural wonders to its cultural attractions and local cuisine. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, Northwest Wisconsin is sure to delight and inspire you.
