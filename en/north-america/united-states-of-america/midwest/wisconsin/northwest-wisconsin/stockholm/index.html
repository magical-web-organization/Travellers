Introduction to Stockholm

Stockholm is a charming city located in Northwest Wisconsin in the Midwest region of the United States of America. It is a favorite destination of many travelers owing to its scenic beauty, rich cultural heritage, and numerous recreational activities. Stockholm has something to offer for everyone, from museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and delectable cuisines. In this comprehensive tour guide, we bring you a list of must-visit places in Stockholm, along with a plethora of activities, food and drink options, and other insider tips. Read on to plan your perfect getaway to Stockholm!

List of Places to Visit in Stockholm

Here is a list of top attractions and landmarks in Stockholm that should definitely feature on your itinerary:

### Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Villa Bellezza Winery and Vineyards: Take a tour of this beautiful winery and vineyard, experience wine tasting sessions, and enjoy some panoramic views of the Lake Pepin.

  • Pepin Heights Orchard: If you’re visiting Stockholm during the fall season, make sure not to miss out on the delicious apples at the Pepin Heights Orchard.

  • Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum: For fans of Wilder’s Little House book series, this museum is the perfect opportunity to explore the world of Laura and her family.

  • Stockholm Pie Company: End your trip with some scrumptious pies, including the bestselling Apple Pie a la Mode and Blueberry Peach Pie.

### Natural Attractions of Stockholm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maiden Rock Bluff State Natural Area: Maiden Rock Bluff offers some of the most spectacular views of the Mississippi River in Stockholm. This state natural area features hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping sites, making it a perfect day trip for families and groups of friends.

  • Skoglund-Hayer Historic District: This historic district is a blend of natural beauty and architectural charm. Take a leisurely walk through the quaint town and admire the picturesque historic homes.

  • Great River Road Visitor and Learning Center: If you’re a history buff, make sure to visit this museum that gives you a history lesson of the Great River Road.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stockholm

  • Stockholm Village Park: Stockholm Village Park offers a beautiful, easy-going hike along a lush wooded trail. The trail follows the shoreline of Lake Pepin and offers fantastic views of the surrounding forest and water.

  • Governor Knowles State Forest: Explore Governor Knowles State Forest and discover serene forests, streams, and wildlife. The Pine Pines Trail covers a portion of the Ice Age Trail and offers some beautiful views of the forest.

Best Things to Do in Stockholm: Activities and Tours

  • Guide Service of Washington Island: Take an exclusive tour that lets you explore the beauty of the Washington Islands in an eco-friendly manner.

  • Burg Boat Tours: Take a Burg Boat Tour and gain an understanding of the rich history of the Mississippi River and the surrounding area.

  • Stockholm Village Art Walk: The Stockholm art walk features a number of galleries, studios, and artists that specialize in a wide variety of mediums.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stockholm

Day 1: Visit Pepin Heights Orchard and take a tour of the Villa Bellezza Winery and Vineyards.

Day 2: Visit the Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum and enjoy a hike at Maiden Rock Bluff State Natural Area.

Day 3: Take a Burg Boat Tour and visit the Great River Road Visitor and Learning Center.

Day 4: Explore the Governor Knowles State Forest and enjoy a leisurely bike ride in the Stockholm Village Park.

Day 5: Go on a guided tour of the Washington Island and experience the beauty of the environment.

Day 6: Take a walk through the Skoglund-Hayer Historic District and visit the Stockholm Village Art Walk.

Day 7: End your trip with some scrumptious pies at the Stockholm Pie Company.

Food and Drink Scene of Stockholm

(h2) Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak at the Harbor View Cafe: The Harbor View Cafe is a must-visit for meat lovers. It specializes in great cuts of steak and offers delicious seafood dishes.

  • Fish and Chips at The Pickle Factory: For an ultimate fish fry experience, head to the Pickle Factory. It serves succulent fish and crispy chips, and goes perfectly with a pint of beer.

### Local Beverages

  • Pepin Heights Apple Cider and Wine: The Pepin Heights Orchard is known for its delicious apple cider and wine that’s crafted using locally grown apples.

  • Craft beers at the Stockholm Brewing Company: Stockholm Brewing Company serves a variety of craft beers made on-site that you can enjoy on the outdoor terrace.

Where to Stay in Stockholm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wide Spot Organic Farm: If you’re a nature lover, Wide Spot Organic Farm is the perfect place to stay. It is a working farm that offers a peaceful environment and scenic beauty.

  • Stockholm Village Inn: The Stockholm Village Inn is affordable and well-maintained with excellent amenities, making it perfect for families on a budget.

What to Buy in Stockholm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Cheese: Visit the Ellsworth Cooperative Creamery, which is a cheese-lover’s paradise. They have a wide variety of locally made cheese, including the famous cheese curds.

  • Pottery: Stockholm is known for its local pottery artists. Visit the Gallery of Wisconsin Art to pick up some beautiful local pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Stockholm

Stockholm has a rich history and cultural heritage that is evident in its architecture, food habits, and festivals. The town is known for its Swedish rural heritage and its Norwegian influence. Stockholm honors this influence through its annual midsummer festival, which celebrates Nordic culture and pagan tradition. The community is known for its hospitality and friendliness, and visitors are always welcome to join in the local festivities and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Stockholm

The best time to visit Stockholm is from May to October, when the weather is mild and pleasant, making this time perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the natural beauty of Stockholm.

############ Do:’s and Don’ts of Stockholm

(h3) Do:

  • Respect nature: Stockholm is known for its peaceful environment; Respect it, and make sure to follow the rules and guidelines at all times.

  • Explore on foot: Stockholm is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. It’s the perfect opportunity to connect with nature and the local culture.

  • Visit local farms: Visit local farms and orchards to experience the true flavor of Stockholm.

############# Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Stockholm is known for its clean and green environment, so make sure not to litter the streets and walkways.

  • Don’t hike without proper gear: Stockholm has some great hiking and biking trails that might be challenging, so come fully equipped.

Safety and Health

### Safety in Stockholm

Stockholm is a peaceful town, and it’s generally a safe place for travelers. However, travelers are advised to take basic precautions such as watching their belongings and keeping valuables secure.

### Health in Stockholm

Stockholm has excellent medical facilities, and visitors can rest easy knowing that they have access to some of the best healthcare services in the state.

Getting to Stockholm: Transportation Options and Logistics

### Airports Near Stockholm

The nearest airport to Stockholm is the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport, located around 90 miles away.

### Bus Stations Near Stockholm

The nearest bus stations to Stockholm are the Amtrak Station, Greyhound Bus Station, and the Jefferson Lines Bus Stop.

### Other Options

Visitors can rent a car, hail a taxi, or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft to get around.

How to Get Around in Stockholm

Stockholm is a small town, and it can be easily explored on foot, bike, or car. Visitors can also use the local bus service, which is comfortable and reliable.

Where to Go Next from Stockholm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake City: Drive 8 miles south of Stockholm to explore the charming town of Lake City. Unwind with picturesque views of the lake.

  • Alma: Drive 8 miles north of Stockholm to visit Alma. Visit the Castlerock Museum and enjoy a stroll at the Alma Beach.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive guide to Stockholm helps you plan your perfect trip. With its blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly community, Stockholm is the perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. Happy traveling!
