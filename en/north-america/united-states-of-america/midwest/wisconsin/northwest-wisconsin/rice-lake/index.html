Introduction to Rice Lake

Nestled in Northwest Wisconsin, Rice Lake is a charming city that is home to a stunning lake by the same name. The city is situated about 100 miles northeast of Minneapolis, making it an ideal weekend getaway destination. With its natural beauty, rich history, and array of activities, Rice Lake is a perfect vacation spot for families, groups, and solo travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Rice Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Barron County Historical Society Located in the heart of Rice Lake, the Barron County Historical Society is a museum that showcases the history of the city and the surrounding area. The museum features exhibits that range from Native American artifacts to farm equipment to military memorabilia.

2. The Railroad Memories Museum This museum portrays the history of railroads in Wisconsin and the Midwest. It features various displays and exhibits that showcase the impact of railroads on the development of Rice Lake and the surrounding region.

Natural Attractions of Rice Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Rice Lake Rice Lake is the primary attraction in the area, with its clear water, stunning sunrises and sunsets, and opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming. The lake has 23 miles of shoreline, and visitors can rent canoes or kayaks for a leisurely paddle.

2. Cedar Side Walking Trail This trail follows the shoreline of Rice Lake for 3.5 miles, offering hikers scenic views of the area. The trail is perfect for walking, running, or biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rice Lake

1. Blue Hills Trail System Located about 17 miles east of Rice Lake, the Blue Hills Trail System offers 20 miles of hiking and biking trails that weave through stunning forests, hills, and valleys.

2. Tuscobia State Trail This 74-mile trail passes through Rice Lake and extends all the way to Park Falls in the north. Visitors can hike or bike the trail and enjoy the picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in Rice Lake: Activities and Tours

1. Fishing Rice Lake is popular among anglers, with its abundance of fish species such as northern pike, walleye, and bass. Visitors can fish from shore, rent a boat, or hire a fishing guide for a more rewarding fishing experience.

2. Golfing Rice Lake has several golf courses, including Turtleback Golf Course, that offer a challenging yet enjoyable golfing experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rice Lake

Day 1: Visit the Railroad Memories Museum and Cedar Side Walking Trail. Day 2: Take a guided fishing trip on Rice Lake. Day 3: Spend the day golfing at Turtleback Golf Course. Day 4: Hike the Blue Hills Trail System and enjoy the natural beauty of the area. Day 5: Visit the Barron County Historical Society. Day 6: Rent a boat and spend the day on Rice Lake. Day 7: Take a day trip to the Tuscobia State Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Rice Lake

Must-Try Dishes

1. Walleye Pike This freshwater fish is a popular local dish that visitors shouldn’t miss out on. Walleye pike can be found on many restaurant menus in Rice Lake.

2. Cheese Curds As a state recognized for its dairy products, it’s no surprise that Rice Lake has some of the best cheese curds in Wisconsin. Visitors can enjoy them at local restaurants or purchase them from one of the area’s dairy farms.

Local Beverages

1. Spotted Cow Beer A local brew from New Glarus Brewing Company, Spotted Cow is a famous ale in Wisconsin, and one that is worth sampling while in Rice Lake.

2. Chateau St. Croix Winery Located about 30 miles south of Rice Lake, Chateau St. Croix Winery offers guided tours and tastings of their award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Rice Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. AmeriVu Inn & Suites Located near Rice Lake’s downtown area, AmeriVu Inn & Suites offers 50 comfortable rooms and amenities, such as a heated indoor pool, hot tub, and fitness center.

2. Cobblestone Inn & Suites This hotel on South Main Street is perfect for families and business travelers, with its cozy, comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Rice Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Maple Syrup and Honey Rice Lake is home to various maple syrup and honey farms, where visitors can purchase delicious, locally-made products. They are perfect souvenirs to take home to friends and family.

2. Hand-Crafted Pottery Several local artisans create beautiful pottery that represents the area’s natural beauty. Visitors can explore galleries and shops to find unique, hand-crafted pieces to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Rice Lake

Rice Lake has a rich history, with Native American culture and European settlements coexisting for centuries. As a result, the city has a unique blend of cultural traditions, from Native American powwows to local festivals to traditional fish fries.

Best Time to Visit Rice Lake

The best time to visit Rice Lake is in the summer when the weather is warm, and the lake activities are in full swing. However, fall is also an excellent time to visit, with the stunning fall foliage and less crowded tourist sites.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rice Lake

Do:

  • Do respect the natural beauty and environment of Rice Lake.
  • Do attend local festivals and events.
  • Do try local foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rice Lake

Rice Lake is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions such as locking their cars, storing valuables safely, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Rice Lake

Visitors should be vigilant about Lyme disease, which is prevalent in Wisconsin. They should wear long pants and sleeves while hiking, use bug spray, and perform tick checks regularly.

Getting to Rice Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rice Lake

The closest airport to Rice Lake is the Duluth International Airport, which is about 110 miles away. The Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport is also an option, located approximately 120 miles southeast of Rice Lake.

Bus Stations Near Rice Lake

Rice Lake is served by the Jefferson Lines bus company, which runs between Minneapolis and Duluth, with stops in Rice Lake.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Rice Lake, with several highways leading to the city. Alternatively, they can cycle through the Tuscobia State Trail or rent a boat to travel to Rice Lake via the lake’s waterways.

How to Get Around in Rice Lake

Visitors can rent cars, bikes, or boats to get around Rice Lake. Alternatively, they can walk or take a taxi or ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Rice Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Duluth, MN Located about 111 miles northeast of Rice Lake, Duluth is a vibrant city that sits on the shores of Lake Superior. It features stunning views, fascinating museums, and abundant outdoor activities.

2. Wausau, WI Located about 90 miles south of Rice Lake, Wausau is home to the Granite Peak Ski Area, dozens of parks, hiking trails, and a thriving arts scene.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Rice Lake is a beautiful, welcoming destination for anyone looking for a relaxing vacation away from the hustle and bustle of big cities. With its gorgeous lake, natural beauty, and outdoor activities, Rice Lake offers an unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages.
