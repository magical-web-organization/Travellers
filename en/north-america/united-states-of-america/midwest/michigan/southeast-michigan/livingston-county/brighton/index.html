Brighton is a charming town located in Southeast Michigan, surrounded by scenic views and natural wonders. This hidden gem offers visitors a wide range of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious local dishes. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know about visiting Brighton, including the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Brighton

There is a plethora of things to do and see in Brighton. Here is a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brighton Area Historical Society
  • Murphy-Mill Historic District
  • Old Town Hall Museum

Natural Attractions of Brighton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Island Lake State Recreation Area
  • Mount Brighton
  • Brighton Recreation Area

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brighton

Brighton is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails for all levels of expertise. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Brighton State Recreation Area Hiking Trails
  • Island Lake Mountain Bike Trail
  • Lakeland Trail State Park

Best Things to Do in Brighton: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day enjoying local attractions or seeking adventure, Brighton has something to offer every kind of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Brighton:

  • Go skiing or snowboarding at Mount Brighton Ski Resort.
  • Take a hot balloon ride with Balloon Quest Inc.
  • Get a scenic view of Brighton area on a helicopter tour with Livingston Aviation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brighton

For an optimal experience in Brighton, consider following this suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Brighton Area Historical Society and Murphy-Mill Historic District
Day 2 Explore Lakeland Trail State Park by hiking or biking
Day 3 Enjoy skiing or snowboarding at Mount Brighton Ski Resort
Day 4 Take a scenic helicopter tour with Livingston Aviation
Day 5 Visit Island Lake State Recreation Area
Day 6 Take a hot air balloon ride with Balloon Quest Inc.
Day 7 Spend time at Brighton Recreation Area.

Food and Drink Scene of Brighton

Brighton offers a range of restaurants and bars, serving everything from classic American to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to satisfy your cravings:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Root Restaurant & Bar: Famous for their “Millionaire’s Bacon” and “Lobster Corn Dogs.”
  • Ciao Amici’s: Try their delicious wood-fired pizza.
  • Stillwater Grill: Known for their seafood dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Brewery Becker
  • Brighton Bar & Grill

Where to Stay in Brighton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brighton offers a variety of budget-friendly accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some highly recommended options:

  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Brighton
  • Courtyard by Marriott Detroit Brighton

What to Buy in Brighton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take a piece of Brighton with you, here are some local souvenirs and products to consider:

  • Brighton themed mugs, t-shirts, and other memorabilia from Brighton Chamber of Commerce.
  • Beverages from Brewery Becker and Brighton Bar & Grill.
  • Handmade candles and soaps from The Bees Knees Candle Co.

Local Culture and Customs of Brighton

Brighton’s culture and customs are heavily influenced by the town’s rich history and natural surroundings. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and take part in local festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Brighton

The best time to visit Brighton is during the summer months, but visitors can enjoy the town’s attractions and activities all year round. From snow sports in the winter to the vibrant fall foliage, there is always something to explore in Brighton.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brighton

To ensure a safe and respectful visit to Brighton, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy local cuisine and support small businesses.
  • Embrace outdoor activities and explore the natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter behind.
  • Be overly loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Ignore safety regulations and guidelines.

Safety and Health

Brighton is generally a safe place to visit with minimal risk of crime. Visitors are advised to take normal safety precautions, including locking doors and valuables in hotel rooms and being aware of surroundings at night.

Getting to Brighton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brighton is easily accessible by car or bus. The easiest way to reach Brighton is by driving, as it is only a 45-minute drive from Detroit. For visitors flying into the area, the closest airport is Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport.

Airports Near Brighton

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport
  • Oakland International Airport

Bus Stations Near Brighton

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Amtrak

How to Get Around in Brighton

The best way to get around in Brighton is by car or bike, but visitors can also use ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Brighton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brighton’s location makes it easy to explore nearby attractions in Michigan. Here are some nearby destinations to visit:

  • Ann Arbor, Michigan
  • Detroit, Michigan
  • Lake Michigan

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Brighton has something to offer. With its vibrant food scene, natural surroundings, and welcoming atmosphere, it is a destination worth visiting. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip to Brighton and explore everything this charming town has to offer.
