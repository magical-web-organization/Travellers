Monroe is a small but charming city located in Southeast Michigan. Its rich history, natural beauty, and unique cultural attractions make it a top travel destination in the Midwest. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Monroe has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll outline the top things to do, see, and experience in Monroe, Michigan.

Introduction to Monroe

Monroe is the largest city in Monroe County and is located along the western shore of Lake Erie. The city was founded back in 1817 and has a rich history dating back to the early 19th century. Monroe is home to several historic landmarks and museums, including the River Raisin National Battlefield Park and the Monroe County Historical Museum. The city’s downtown area is known for its charming architecture and is home to several boutique shops, art galleries, and restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in Monroe

Here are some must-visit attractions in Monroe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • River Raisin National Battlefield Park: Explore the rich history of Michigan in one of the most significant sites of the War of 1812.
  • Monroe County Historical Museum: Learn more about the growth and settlement of the city and county through captivating exhibits.
  • The Ellis Library and Reference Center: Visit the only library in the state of Michigan with an original Frank Lloyd Wright-designed building.
  • The Soldiers and Sailors Monument: A pivotal part of the city’s rich history, this monument commemorates the area’s role in the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Monroe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sterling State Park: Enjoy camping, fishing, and other outdoor activities at this beautiful state park.
  • River Raisin Heritage Trail: Stretching over 40 miles, this trail is perfect for biking or hiking along the river’s edge.
  • St. Julian Winery: Sample wines and explore the vineyards at one of the oldest and most prestigious wineries in the area.
  • Monroe Multi-Sports Complex: Enjoy indoor ice-skating, hockey and other sports in a state-of-the-art facility.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monroe

Monroe offers several great hiking and biking trails in various parts of the city and county. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • River Raisin Heritage Trail: As mentioned earlier, this 40-mile trail takes you through the city and county.
  • Oak Openings Preserve: This park is a great place to hike or bike with several designated trails and picturesque views.
  • The Munson Creek Trail: From the White Park parking lot, follow this trail for an easy walk to the Munson Creek waterfall viewing site.
  • Lake Erie Connector: A 13-mile trail in the city that provides cycling and walking access from northern Monroe to Sterling State Park.

Best Things to Do in Monroe: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Monroe, ranging from outdoor activities to guided tours and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best things to experience in the city:

  • Take a Ghost Tour: Explore Monroe’s haunted history while learning about eerie real-life incidents on a ghost tour.
  • Visit the Monroe Farmers Market: Check out local and seasonal produce from nearby farmers and artisans in the city weekly market.
  • Go Fishing: Michigan is a great place to go fishing, and generations of families have fished the River Raisin. If you don’t have a boat, stop by the Monroe Boat Club and Fish Hatchery.
  • Attend sporting events: Catch some sports action at the Monroe Multi-Sports Complex, or attend a college-level game at the nearby University of Toledo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monroe

Day 1: Check out the city’s historic landmarks, including the River Raisin National Battlefield and the Monroe County Historical Museum.

Day 2: Enjoy outdoor activities such as camping, hiking, or fishing at Sterling State Park.

Day 3: Take a winery tour and sample some local wines at St. Julian Winery.

Day 4: Explore the city’s downtown area, visit boutiques, and try culinary delights at the city’s many restaurants and bars.

Day 5: Rent a bike and cycle to Oak Openings Preserve for a day out in nature.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of the Ellis Library and Reference Center and learn about its unique architecture and history.

Day 7: Attend a concert or game at the Monroe Multi-Sports Complex.

Food and Drink Scene of Monroe

Monroe offers several excellent dining options, from local favorites to nationwide chains. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: You can’t leave Michigan without trying a Detroit-style Coney Dog, and you can find several delicious variations in Monroe.
  • Beef on Weck: A regional favorite in neighboring Buffalo, New York, where roast beef is served with horseradish on kummelweck bread (a variant of the Kaiser roll).
  • Mackinac Island Fudge: Choose from over ten themed varieties of Michigan-made fudge from the Original Murdick’s Fudge or the Doud’s Market.

Local Beverages

  • Michigan Microbrews: Try Thunder Bay, Monroe Brewing Company, and other Michigan microbreweries.
  • Wine: Michigan is also home to excellent wineries, including the St. Julian Winery, which should not be missed.

Where to Stay in Monroe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monroe offers a range of accommodations, ranging from hotels and chain motels to quaint bed-and-breakfast establishments. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Quality Inn and Suites: Enjoy modern amenities and room service at this affordable hotel.
  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: This lodging option offers a complementary breakfast and a pool area for guests.
  • The Sterling Inn: A boutique hotel ideal for couples and families offering a range of suites and packages.

What to Buy in Monroe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Monroe include:

  • Coney Sauce: You can purchase detroit-style Coney sauce from any of the finer hot dog joint that you will come across if you stay within Michigan.
  • Michigan-Made Fudge: Sample and take home of selection from the many fudge shops in Monroe.
  • Handmade Ceramics: From the 1,000 Degree Art Studio, select a beautiful and unique ceramic piece to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Monroe

The people of Monroe are warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to explore and participate in the local culture. Good manners and behavior are expected at all times. Michigan is known for being a very friendly state, so those who are outgoing and sincere will fit right into Monroe.

Best Time to Visit Monroe

The best time to visit Monroe is late spring to early autumn when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. Summer is undoubtedly the busiest season, while winters can be severe.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monroe

Do:

  • Embrace the city’s quaint and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Try local specialties, such as Michigan micro-brews and Detroit-style Coney Dogs.
  • Explore the city’s historic landmarks and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and behaviors.
  • Litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Be careless with your belongings or safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monroe

Monroe is a safe city, but visitors are still advised to exercise general safety precautions, such as staying in well-lit areas at night, avoiding unfamiliar parts of town, and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Monroe

Like any other travel location, visitors are advised to bring their own prescription medications and check the CDC’s website for any current health alerts or pandemics specific to the Midwest region.

Getting to Monroe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Monroe

The closest airports to Monroe are Detroit Metropolitan Airport (DTW) and Toledo Express Airport (TOL), both located a little over an hour’s drive away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Monroe

There are several bus stations near Monroe for those who prefer public transit, including the Greyhound Bus Station in Toledo, Ohio.

Other Options

Road travel is the most practical way to get around Monroe. Popular rental car companies include Enterprise, Hertz, and Budget.

How to Get Around in Monroe

Public transportation in Monroe is limited, and the best way to get around is by car or bike. Downtown Monroe is pedestrian-friendly, so exploring by foot is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Monroe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Several nearby destinations are worth visiting after Monroe, including:

  • Toledo: A vibrant city to visit, home to a world-class art museum, the Toledo Museum of Art.
  • Detroit: One hour away from Monroe, visit the US’s biggest Air and Space Museum or partake in Detroit’s world-renowned music and art scene.
  • Lake Erie Islands: Explore Put-In-Bay, South Bass Island, or Kelleys Island by ferryboat Island Hopper or Jet Express.

Conclusion

Monroe offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, exploring museums, or indulging in local cuisine, Monroe has something for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide has given you a glimpse of what to expect in this charming city. We hope that it has inspired you to plan your next trip to Monroe, Michigan.
