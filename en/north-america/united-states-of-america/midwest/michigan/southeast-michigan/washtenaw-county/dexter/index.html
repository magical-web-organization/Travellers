Introduction to Dexter

Nestled in the heart of Washtenaw County, Dexter is a quaint town located in Southeast Michigan, USA. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff or foodie, Dexter has something to offer every traveler. With its picturesque landscapes, historic landmarks and local restaurants, Dexter is fast becoming a top destination in the Midwest.

List of Places to Visit in Dexter

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dexter Area Historical Society Museum

The Dexter Area Historical Society Museum is a must-visit for history enthusiasts. Located in the heart of downtown Dexter, the museum chronicles the area’s history from the early settlers to the present-day. The museum features exhibits, artifacts and memorabilia detailing everything from the town’s agricultural roots to its involvement in the Underground Railroad.

Gordon Hall

Another key attraction for history lovers is Gordon Hall. This renowned historic landmark dates back to the 1840s and was once the home of a prominent local businessman. Today, the site is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and offers guided tours, giving visitors the chance to see how the wealthy residents of Dexter lived during the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Dexter: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hudson Mills Metropark

Hudson Mills Metropark is a must-visit for any outdoor enthusiast. This sprawling park offers miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing opportunities and picnic areas. Visitors can also tee off at an 18-hole golf course, or take part in seasonal activities such as cross-country skiing and sledding.

Mill Creek Park

Mill Creek Park is another natural gem located within the limits of Dexter. Tucked away amongst the winding roads and rolling hills, it provides visitors with serene views of the Mill Creek as it slowly meanders through beautiful tree-lined banks. Visitors can fish for trout and simply enjoy the peaceful tranquility of this hidden gem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dexter

Dexter-Huron Metropark Trail

The Dexter-Huron Metropark Trail is a picturesque, four-mile route that weaves through the woods and alongside the Huron River. The trail is perfect for hiking, running, or cycling. The natural beauty and serenity is awe-inspiring, making it a popular spot for daily exercise or a weekend jog.

Border-to-Border Trail

The Border-to-Border Trail is a 70-mile trail that connects several parks throughout southeastern Michigan, including Dexter. Offering amazing scenery along the route, this trail is perfect for hikers and bikers. The Border-to-Border Trail is especially famous for its fall foliage with orange, red and yellow canopy covering the sky above you.

Best Things to Do in Dexter: Activities and Tours

Dexter Cider Mill

Visitors who enjoy culinary adventures can’t leave Dexter without visiting the Dexter Cider Mill. Built in 1866, it’s the oldest continuously operating cider mill in Michigan, and one of the oldest in the US. Visitors can enjoy freshly-pressed cider and donuts made on-site, as well as a variety of locally made jams, jellies and honey.

Where the Wildflowers Are Tours

Where the Wildflowers Are Tours offers a fantastic guided Walk-n-Talk through the local woods and natural areas. Local experts provide fascinating insights into the area’s history and ecology while you stroll through beautiful scenery and take in the colorful wildflowers. The tours are on offer throughout the year, and each seasonal tour has its unique charm.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dexter

Day 1: Visit the Dexter Cider Mill and pick up some local jams, jellies and honey.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Gordon Hall Historical Landmark and learn about the local architecture and 19th-century lifestyle.

Day 3: Explore the Hudson Mills Metropark by bicycle, stopping at picnic areas for lunch and relaxing by the serene Mill Creek.

Day 4: Visit the Dexter Area Historical Society Museum and learn about the history of the town’s early settlers and involvement in the Underground Railroad.

Day 5: Hike the Dexter-Huron Metropark Trail, enjoying the scenery and gorgeous views.

Day 6: Take a Wildflower Tour and learn about the local flora and fauna with knowledgeable guides.

Day 7: Head out to explore the surrounding countryside and visit nearby towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Dexter

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Dexter is complete without trying some sweet, delicious locally produced cider and donuts from the Dexter Cider Mill. For more substantial meals, Red Brick Kitchen and Bar is a must-go-to destination. Visitors can gorge on their amazing burgers and heartwarming brunches served up in a cozy and rustic setting.

Local Beverages

In addition to its internationally known apple cider, Dexter’s Brewed Awakening Coffeehouse serves up high-quality gourmet coffee, top-notch teas, smoothies, and a variety of other amazing beverages.

Where to Stay in Dexter: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Dexter Inn

The Dexter Inn is the ideal place to stay in Dexter. This resort style motel with state-of-the-art amenities has an indoor pool, hot tub, bar and restaurant, fitness center, and a large outdoor hot tub. The spacious tastefully decorated rooms provide ultimate relaxation to the guests.

Airbnb and Vacation Rentals

For a more intimate experience, many visitors opt to stay in a local Airbnb vacation rental. These charming spaces range from small cottages to larger historic homes and some are even found in the rural countryside around Dexter.

What to Buy in Dexter: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local shops in Dexter offer a range of hand-woven baskets, pottery by local artisans, and other locally made gifts, making them the perfect souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Dexter

The friendly and hardworking locals in Dexter are proud of their town boasts about its history, eclectic food scene, and natural beauty. Visitors to Dexter will find a warm welcome from the people, and those who embrace the local customs and way of life will find their stay here all the more rewarding.

Best Time to Visit Dexter

July and August are the busiest times of the year for tourist activities and outdoor adventures. But for those who love fall foliage and tranquil walks along scenic trails, late September into early November is the perfect time to visit Dexter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dexter

Do:

  • Take time to enjoy the scenic beauty and natural surroundings.
  • Respect the history and heritage of this town.
  • Take a guided tour or explore the area on your own.

Don’t:

  • Litter and/or pollute the natural surroundings.
  • Intrude on personal spaces or properties for any reason.
  • Disrespect the monuments and historical landmarks in the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dexter

Dexter is a safe place to visit with a relatively low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions, such as locking car doors and keeping an eye on valuables when in public places.

Health in Dexter

Dexter offers high-quality medical care from its clinics providing basic or emergency care to its residents and visitors alike. This guarantees a safe and healthy environment for all who enter.

Getting to Dexter: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dexter

The nearest airport to Dexter is Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport (DTW), located about 25 miles away. Other airports include Willow Run Airport in Ypsilanti, MI, and Ann Arbor Municipal Airport.

Bus Stations Near Dexter

Greyhound buses pass through Ann Arbor, which is about 12 miles from Dexter. Upon arrival, visitors can take a taxi or ride-share service to get to Dexter.

Other Options

Car rental agencies are available at the airports, as well as in the cities of Ann Arbor, Ypsilanti, and Dexter. This would give you more flexibility to explore surroundings and visit other nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Dexter

There are rental cars, taxis, ride-share services, bicycles, etc. available for rent. One can also choose to walk around the town and enjoy the local sights.

Where to Go Next from Dexter: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Both Ann Arbor, Michigan, and the larger Detroit area are just a short drive away, giving visitors easy access to museums, parks, and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Dexter is an ideal travel destination, rich in culture, history, and scenic beauty. Whether you’re traveling solo or with friends or family, you’re sure to find something to enjoy in this charming Midwestern town. So, pack your bags, book your accommodations, and get ready for an adventure you’ll never forget.
