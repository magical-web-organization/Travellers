Introduction to Auburn Hills

Auburn Hills is a city located in Oakland County, in Metro Detroit, in Southeast Michigan, in Michigan, in the Midwest, in the United States of America, in North America. Home to Oakland University, this small city offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and entertainment.

List of Places to Visit in Auburn Hills

Auburn Hills has a range of attractions that cater to the tastes of all kinds of travelers. Below is a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Meadow Brook Hall: This iconic Tudor-revival mansion was built by the widow of auto pioneer John Dodge and is now a National Historic Landmark with guided tours available daily.

  • SEA LIFE Michigan Aquarium: This 35,000 square foot aquarium has more than 1,500 creatures in over 10 exhibits including a 120,000-gallon ocean tank tunnel.

  • Olympia Entertainment: This premier entertainment company situated in the heart of Auburn Hills has a range of venues, shows, and events that attract crowds from all over the country.

Natural Attractions of Auburn Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bald Mountain Recreation Area: This park contains over 4,000 acres of rugged terrain designed to cater to hikers, mountain bikers, anglers, and snowmobilers.

  • Clinton River Trail: This 16-mile scenic trail through several towns and cities is perfect for joggers, cyclists, and families who love to explore nature.

  • Innovation Hills Park: This park is perfect for picnics, family gatherings, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Auburn Hills

Auburn Hills has several walking and cycling paths that take you through some of the most scenic parts of the city. Here are a few popular trails:

  • Bald Mountain Recreation Area Trail: A 4.7-mile hike through Michigan hardwood forests.
  • Clinton River Trail: A 16-mile scenic trail that passes through several towns and cities.
  • Paint Creek Trail: This 8.9-mile compacted limestone trail takes you through historic landmarks and picturesque plains.

Best Things to Do in Auburn Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Meadow Brook Hall: Take a guided tour of this iconic mansion and learn about the history and architecture of the place.

  • Visit SEA LIFE Michigan Aquarium: Take a walk through one of the largest aquariums in the state and see over 1,500 creatures from all over the world.

  • Catch a show at the Olympia Entertainment venues: This entertainment company offers a range of events from sports games to concerts that attract crowds from all over the country.

Week-Long Itinerary for Auburn Hills

Day 1-2: Explore the cultural attractions of Auburn Hills such as Meadow Brook Hall and SEA LIFE Michigan Aquarium.

Day 3-4: Explore the natural attractions of Auburn Hills such as the Bald Mountain Recreation Area, Clinton River Trail, and Innovation Hills Park.

Day 5-6: Participate in some outdoor activities such as cycling, hiking, or fishing.

Day 7: Relax in one of Auburn Hills’ cafes or restaurants and reflect on your week-long journey.

Food and Drink Scene of Auburn Hills

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: A Michigan classic, this all-beef hot dog served in a bun topped with chili sauce, diced onions, and mustard is a must-try.

  • Pasty: A traditional Cornish pastry filled with beef, potatoes, and rutabaga, this hearty dish is a popular snack in Michigan.

  • Vernors Ginger Ale: This iconic ginger ale, created in Detroit in 1866, has since become a Michigan classic. This sweet and spicy drink is perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.

Local Beverages

  • Bell’s Brewery: This iconic brewery in Michigan has a range of beers and ales that cater to all kinds of tastes.

  • Wine Tasting: Michigan is known for its wine. So, be sure to participate in a wine tasting tour and sample some of the best wine Michigan has to offer.

Where to Stay in Auburn Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Auburn Hills has several accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guest houses. Here are a few options:

  • Hyatt Place Auburn Hills: This four-star hotel offers modern amenities and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • Hilton Auburn Hills Suites: This hotel offers affordable luxury and easy access to nearby attractions, including the Palace of Auburn Hills.

  • Sonesta Simply Suites Detroit Auburn Hills: This extended-stay hotel offers spacious suites with full kitchens, perfect for families or travelers who want to save on food costs.

What to Buy in Auburn Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vernors Ginger Ale: This iconic Michigan drink is a must-buy for anyone visiting Auburn Hills.

  • Frankenmuth Cheese Haus: This cheese shop is a great place to buy locally-made cheeses, sausages, and snacks.

  • Cherry Republic: This shop sells all things cherry, from cherry jams to cherry wine, making it a great place to buy souvenirs for loved ones back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Auburn Hills

  • Michigan’s Automotive Past: Michigan has a rich history in the automobile industry. Therefore, make sure to learn about the auto industry and visit the Henry Ford Museum if you get a chance.

  • Michigan’s Natural Beauty: Michigan is known as the Great Lakes state, and its natural beauty is widely celebrated. Therefore, be sure to enjoy the outdoors in Auburn Hills and beyond.

Best Time to Visit Auburn Hills

The best time to visit Auburn Hills is during the fall season when the weather is mild, and the leaves are changing colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Auburn Hills

Do:

  • Enjoy the scenic beauty of the city’s natural attractions
  • Visit the cultural attractions and learn about Michigan’s history
  • Try local dishes and beverages, like Vernors Ginger Ale and Bell’s Brewery beer
  • Shop for souvenirs and local products before leaving the city.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the rich history of the automobile industry in Michigan
  • Forget to visit Detroit, which is only a 30-minute drive from Auburn Hills.
  • Disrespect the environment by littering or damaging nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in Auburn Hills

Auburn Hills is a relatively safe city to visit, with low crime rates. However, it is always a good idea to remain vigilant and ensure your belongings are secured at all times.

Health in Auburn Hills

Auburn Hills has several hospitals and medical centers, ensuring that travelers can receive top-notch medical care, if needed.

Getting to Auburn Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Auburn Hills

The nearest airport to Auburn Hills is Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport, located approximately 45 minutes away by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Auburn Hills

Greyhound Bus Lines provides service to downtown Detroit and has a station located at 1001 Howard St, Detroit, MI 48226, approximately 30 minutes away by car or taxi.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Auburn Hills and nearby towns.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Auburn Hills and can be hailed from any street corner or arranged for online through Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Auburn Hills

Auburn Hills has several transportation options, including buses, taxis, and rental cars. However, the most convenient way to get around is by car.

Where to Go Next from Auburn Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Detroit: Just a 30-minute drive away, Detroit has a rich history and many cultural attractions, including the Detroit Institute of Arts and the Motown Museum.
  • Ann Arbor: Located about an hour drive away from Auburn Hills, Ann Arbor is a vibrant college town with many cultural attractions, including the University of Michigan Museum of Art and the Nichols Arboretum.

Conclusion

Auburn Hills offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and entertainment that caters to all kinds of travelers. Whether you’re interested in exploring the natural beauty of Michigan, learning about the automobile industry, or trying local dishes and beverages, this small city has something for everyone. So, be sure to include Auburn Hills in your travel plans and explore its hidden gems.
