Introduction to Clarkston

Clarkston, Michigan is a small town located in Oakland County, part of the Metro Detroit area. It is known for its charming downtown and outdoor recreational opportunities. Visitors can enjoy Michigan’s natural beauty with hiking, biking, and scenic drives, or explore the town’s history through museums and landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Clarkston

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clarkston Heritage Museum showcases Clarkston’s rich history with rotating exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  • Holcomb Farm Museum is a living history museum that preserves the area’s agricultural heritage with historic buildings, demonstrations, and hands-on activities.
  • Union Woodshop is a historic building turned into a restaurant and barbecue joint, famous for its smoked meats and craft beer selection.

Natural Attractions of Clarkston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Independence Oaks County Park is a 1,286-acre park that offers hiking, swimming, fishing, and boating opportunities. Visitors can also rent bikes, paddleboards, and kayaks.
  • Dixie Highway Drive is a scenic route that follows Michigan’s first paved road, offering views of charming downtowns, historic landmarks, and natural landscapes.
  • Clinton River Trail is a 16-mile paved trail that connects several towns, including Clarkston, and passes through parks, forests, and wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clarkston

Clarkston has several parks and trails that offer scenic hikes and biking routes. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Bald Mountain Recreation Area has over 20 miles of trails that wind through forests, fields, and lakes.
  • Heritage Park has a 4-mile loop trail that circles around a scenic lake, with fishing piers and picnic areas.
  • Pontiac Lake Recreation Area has over 11 miles of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for camping, kayaking, and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Clarkston: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides offer a unique perspective of the town and its surroundings, with panoramic views of the countryside and Lake Orion.
  • Clarkston Ghost Walks are guided tours that explore Clarkston’s haunted history, including tales of ghost sightings and paranormal activity.
  • Great Lakes Crossing Outlets is a premier shopping destination with over 185 stores, restaurants, and entertainment options, including a movie theater and a Legoland Discovery Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Clarkston

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pulled pork sandwich at Union Woodshop features tender, smoked pork with a tangy barbecue sauce, housemade pickles, and fresh buns.
  • Loch Ness Monster roll at Union Joints is a sushi-inspired roll filled with smoked salmon, cream cheese, and scallions, topped with sesame seeds and sweet soy sauce.
  • Biscuits and gravy at Union General is a classic southern breakfast dish, with fluffy biscuits and smooth gravy made from scratch.

Local Beverages

  • Michigan beer at Honcho features a rotating selection of local craft beers on tap, as well as bottle options and flights.
  • Wine and cider at Michigan by the Bottle showcases Michigan’s growing wine and cider industry, with tastings, flights, and bottle purchases.
  • Coffee at The Fed is a local favorite, with expertly brewed coffee drinks made from freshly roasted beans.

Where to Stay in Clarkston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Clarkston Table Rocker Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located in a historic mansion, with luxurious rooms, gourmet breakfasts, and stunning views of the town.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites offers comfortable accommodations, an indoor pool, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Oakland University Conference Center is a budget-friendly option with well-appointed rooms, conference facilities, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Clarkston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Clarkston Union Trading Company features a variety of locally sourced gifts, including clothing, candles, jewelry, and artisanal food items.
  • Independent Coffee Roasters offers fresh coffee beans, brewing equipment, and handmade coffee mugs.
  • Kunis Hometown Jewelry specializes in custom-made jewelry using Michigan stones and minerals, such as Petoskey stones and Lake Superior agates.

Local Culture and Customs of Clarkston

Clarkston is a small town with a tight-knit community, where people value friendliness, hospitality, and respect for nature. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture by exploring the town’s history and natural beauty, and connecting with the locals through food, drinks, and shopping.

Best Time to Visit Clarkston

The best time to visit Clarkston is in the summer and fall, from June to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant with plenty of outdoor activities and events. Winter and spring can be cold and snowy, with fewer attractions and limited outdoor opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clarkston

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s natural beauty by hiking, biking, and exploring the parks.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drink, especially craft beer, wine, and cider.
  • Do respect the locals and their traditions, and seek to learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the parks or streets.
  • Don’t trespass on private property or disturb wildlife.
  • Don’t disrespect the locals or their beliefs, and avoid controversial topics.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clarkston

Clarkston is a safe town with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors are advised to take common safety precautions, such as locking cars and valuables, being aware of surroundings, and using caution in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Clarkston

Clarkston has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors are recommended to have travel health insurance and to pack necessary medications and first-aid supplies.

Getting to Clarkston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clarkston

  • Detroit Metro Airport is the closest major airport, located about 45 minutes away from Clarkston. It offers domestic and international flights, as well as car rental options.
  • Bishop International Airport is another option, located about an hour away from Clarkston. It offers domestic flights and car rental options.

Bus Stations Near Clarkston

  • Greyhound Bus offers several daily routes to Flint and Detroit, with stops in surrounding towns like Pontiac and Rochester.
  • Amtrak Train offers daily service to several Michigan cities, including Ann Arbor, Detroit, and Grand Rapids, with stops in Royal Oak and Birmingham.

Other Options

  • Car rental is a popular option for visitors who want to explore the town and surrounding areas at their own pace. Several car rental companies have locations in nearby towns like Waterford and Rochester.
  • Taxi and ridesharing services are available in the area, including Uber and Lyft. Visitors can also call local taxi companies or use online platforms like RideGuru.

How to Get Around in Clarkston

Clarkston is a walkable town, with several attractions and services located within a short distance. Visitors can also use bicycles or cars to explore the surroundings, or take public transportation, such as buses and trains, for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Clarkston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ann Arbor is a vibrant college town located about an hour and a half away from Clarkston, with a lively arts and culture scene, delicious food options, and natural beauty.
  • Frankenmuth is a charming Bavarian-style village located about an hour and 45 minutes away from Clarkston, known for its historic architecture, specialty shops, and world-famous chicken dinners.
  • Mackinac Island is an iconic Michigan destination located about four hours away from Clarkston, featuring a car-free environment, historic landmarks, and breathtaking scenery.

Conclusion

Clarkston, Michigan is a gem of a town that offers something for everyone, whether it’s outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, or relaxation. With its friendly locals, scenic beauty, and rich history, it’s a perfect destination for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation. Plan your trip now to experience the best of Clarkston!
