Introduction to Farmington Hills

Nestled in the heart of Oakland County, Farmington Hills is a charming city with a rich history, diverse culture, and beautiful landscapes. Known for its scenic parks, lakes, and hiking trails, the city attracts nature lovers and adventure seekers from all over the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore Farmington Hills, its top attractions, local culture, food scene, safety tips, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Farmington Hills

Whether you are an adventure seeker, a history buff, or a foodie, Farmington Hills has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Farmington Hills:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Farmington Hills Historical Museum: Explore the history of Farmington Hills through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and special events.
  • Longacre House: A historical mansion built in 1869 that serves as an events venue and offers tours and educational programs.
  • Holocaust Memorial Center: This museum honors the victims and survivors of the Holocaust and educates visitors about the dangers of hatred and prejudice.

Natural Attractions of Farmington Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Heritage Park: Spread over 211 acres, this park offers hiking trails, wildlife viewing, playgrounds, splash pads, picnic areas, and more.
  • Woodland Hills Nature Preserve: This 72-acre nature preserve offers hiking trails, scenic views, and opportunities for wildlife watching.
  • Farmington Hills Nature Center: This nature center offers educational programs, exhibits, trails, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Farmington Hills

Farmington Hills offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the state. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Farmington Hills Trail System: A network of trails that covers over 160 miles, offering a variety of terrains and scenic views.
  • Maybury State Park: This 944-acre park offers hiking and biking trails, camping, fishing, and equestrian trails.
  • Lakeshore Park: This park offers hiking trails, fishing, picnic areas, and beautiful views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Farmington Hills: Activities and Tours

  • Farmington Farmers and Artisans Market: This market offers fresh produce, crafts, food, and live entertainment.
  • TreeRunner Adventure Park: This aerial adventure park offers zip lining, obstacle courses, and other aerial activities.
  • Farmington Hills Civic Theater: This theater offers a variety of shows, ranging from musicals to dramas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Farmington Hills

Day 1: Visit the Farmington Hills Historical Museum and Heritage Park Day 2: Explore Woodland Hills Nature Preserve and take a bike ride along Farmington Hills Trail System Day 3: Discover the Holocaust Memorial Center and Maybury State Park Day 4: Try the local cuisine at Farmington Farmers and Artisans Market and TreeRunner Adventure Park Day 5: Take a hike at the Farmington Hills Nature Center and visit Longacre House Day 6: Enjoy a show at Farmington Hills Civic Theater and relax at Lakeshore Park Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Ann Arbor or Detroit

Food and Drink Scene of Farmington Hills

Farmington Hills offers a diverse food scene, ranging from farm-to-table restaurants to ethnic cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogies: A Polish dish consisting of boiled dumplings, filled with cheese, potato, or other ingredients.
  • Chicken Shwarma: A Middle Eastern dish consisting of spiced chicken cooked on a spit and served with pita bread, hummus, and other toppings.
  • Barbecue Ribs: A classic American dish consisting of slow-cooked pork ribs, served with barbecue sauce and sides.

Local Beverages

  • Michigan Craft Beer: With over 400 breweries in Michigan, there is a beer for every taste and occasion.
  • Faygo: A carbonated soft drink, produced in Detroit since 1907.
  • Michigan Wine: Michigan is the fifth-largest wine producing state, with over 140 wineries.

What to Buy in Farmington Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Apple Cider: Michigan is known for its apples and cider, and Farmington Hills offers the best of both.
  • Pottery: Farmington Hills has a vibrant pottery scene, with many local artists offering unique pieces.
  • Cherry Products: Michigan is also known for its cherry orchards, and Farmington Hills offers cherry-based products like jams, pies, and chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Farmington Hills

Farmington Hills is a diverse city, with a rich history and culture. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect local customs: Be mindful of the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Be open-minded: Embrace the diversity of the city and engage in conversations with locals.
  • Tip etiquette: Tipping is expected in restaurants, hotels, and other service industries.

Best Time to Visit Farmington Hills

The best time to visit Farmington Hills is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. The city also offers several festivals and events during this time, like the Farmington Farmers and Artisans Market and the Founders Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Farmington Hills

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Take advantage of the city’s beautiful parks, hiking trails, and scenic routes.
  • Try the local cuisine: With its diverse food scene, Farmington Hills offers something for every taste bud.
  • Engage in local culture: Attend local events and festivals, and learn about the city’s rich history and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Maintain the cleanliness of the city by disposing of trash in the designated bins.
  • Use loud music or fireworks: Respect the peace and quiet of the residential areas by refraining from loud music and fireworks.
  • Ignore safety rules: Follow safety rules and guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities and adventure sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Farmington Hills

Overall, Farmington Hills is a safe city, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take general safety precautions, like keeping valuables locked away and not leaving items visible in vehicles. While hiking and biking, it’s important to stay on designated trails and carry enough water and food.

Health in Farmington Hills

Farmington Hills has several health clinics, hospitals, and pharmacies, offering medical assistance in case of emergencies. Visitors should carry adequate health insurance and vaccines according to the season.

Where to Go Next from Farmington Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
