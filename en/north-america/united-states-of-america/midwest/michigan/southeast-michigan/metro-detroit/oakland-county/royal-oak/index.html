Introduction to Royal Oak

Royal Oak is a vibrant city located in Oakland County, in Metro Detroit, Southeast Michigan. It is known for its lively downtown, rich cultural heritage, and beautiful natural attractions. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Royal Oak has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Royal Oak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to explore Royal Oak’s history and culture is by visiting its museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Royal Oak Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Royal Oak through various exhibits and artifacts.

  • Royal Oak Farmer’s Market: Established in 1925, this historic market is a hub for fresh produce, artisan goods, and live entertainment.

  • Stagecrafters Baldwin Theatre: This community theater presents a wide range of plays, musicals, and other performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Royal Oak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, Royal Oak won’t disappoint you. Take a hike, bike ride, or enjoy a picnic at the following natural attractions:

  • Red Run Stream Valley Trail: This trail spans over three miles and is perfect for hiking, biking, and walking your dog.

  • Royal Oak Nature Society: This nature reserve boasts over 10 acres of lush greenery, wildlife, and indigenous plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Royal Oak

Royal Oak is home to many biking and hiking trails. A few of the most popular trails include:

  • Ten Foot Loop Trail: This trail is a popular destination for walking or jogging with scenic views.

  • Dequindre Cut Greenway: A family-friendly trail through downtown, adjacent to Dequindre Cut.

Best Things to Do in Royal Oak: Activities and Tours

With a bustling art scene, friendly local people, and vibrant neighborhoods, Royal Oak has various activities and tours to enjoy. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Taste of Royal Oak Tour: Explore Royal Oak’s excellent local cuisine through this three-hour tour on wooden footpaths and cobblestone streets.

  • Mark Ridley’s Comedy Castle: This award-winning comedy club has been the destination for hilarious stand-up comedy acts since 1979.

Food and Drink Scene of Royal Oak

Must-Try Dishes

Royal Oak is home to a diverse food scene, with many restaurants specializing in local and international cuisines. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Coneys from Leo’s Coney Island: A Michigan staple, the coney dog, is a hot dog smeared with mustard sauce, onions, and chili.

  • TomoMaki Sushi: Royal Oak’s most popular sushi place, with a cool atmosphere and quality ingredients.

Local Beverages

Michigan’s microbrewery scene has been gaining speed and producing terrific beer. Some of the best places to taste a locally brewed beer in Royal Oak include:

  • Royal Oak Brewery: A traditional American brewpub that offers eight house brews on tape, alongside typical fare.

  • Lily’s Seafood Grille & Brewery: This place is famous for its signature beer, Cravings Ale, and Sunshine 4-Grain Ale.

Where to Stay in Royal Oak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, Royal Oak has a range of accommodation options. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hyatt Place Detroit Royal Oak: A popular choice for visitors to Royal Oak, boasting an outdoor pool, fitness center and well-appointed rooms.

  • The Royal Oak Inn: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean, comfortable rooms, a continental breakfast, and easy access to downtown.

What to Buy in Royal Oak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a souvenir of your trip, here are a few items we suggest:

  • Michigan-made gift baskets featuring local snacks, beer, and cider.

  • Detroit apparel featuring logos from your favorite Detroit sports teams.

  • Handmade arts and crafts from the local farmers market.

Local Culture and Customs of Royal Oak

Royal Oak is a friendly, thriving community with a unique local culture. Here are a few things you should know before visiting:

  • Respect people’s property and privacy.

  • Michigan’s weather can change quickly, so pack accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Royal Oak

The best time to visit Royal Oak is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Royal Oak

Do:

  • Explore the museums and historical sites.

  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

  • Respect local customs and property.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage community property.

  • Get involved in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Royal Oak

Royal Oak is a safe community. However, it is always wise to take precautions when visiting a new city. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Always lock your car doors and windows.

  • Keep valuables out of sight in your car.

  • Trust your intuition and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Royal Oak

Royal Oak has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. If you need medical attention, go to the emergency room at Beaumont Hospital or the Royal Oak Medical Center.

Getting to Royal Oak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Royal Oak

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport: Located in Romulis, about 23.9 miles from Royal Oak. It is the best choice for long-distance or international travelers.

  • Oakland County International Airport: Located in Waterford Township, about 22 miles from Royal Oak. It is best for local or short travels.

Bus Stations Near Royal Oak

Royal Oak has a regional hub for the SMART bus service. The bus network connects metro Detroit’s suburbs and downtown, making it easy to travel by bus.

Other Options

If you prefer private transportations such as taxi, limousine services, and car rental, you can always use them in Royal Oak.

How to Get Around in Royal Oak

Walking and biking are the most popular ways to get around downtown Royal Oak. You can also use local transportation services such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Royal Oak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore nearby places, here are some destinations we recommend:

  • Belle Isle: This beautiful island park is located in the Detroit River and features a golf course, conservatory, and playgrounds.

  • Lake St. Clair Metropark: This park offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, hiking trails, and picnic areas.

Conclusion

Royal Oak is an exciting destination that offers an ideal mix of culture, history, and natural scenery. Whether you enjoy the arts, history, or the outdoors, Royal Oak is the perfect getaway destination for you. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your stay and make it an unforgettable experience.
