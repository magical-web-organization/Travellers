West Side is a diverse and lively region of Detroit, Michigan, featuring cultural attractions, scenic outdoor adventures, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, West Side has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and places to stay in West Side, along with tips for a safe and healthy trip.

Introduction to West Side

West Side is a culturally rich and bustling region of Detroit, situated in Wayne County, in Metro Detroit, in Southeast Michigan, in Michigan, in Midwest, in United States of America, in North America. Its history is rooted in the automotive industry, with landmarks such as the Ford Rouge Factory and Greenfield Village providing a glimpse into Detroit’s industrial past. Today, West Side is home to thriving neighborhoods, cultural attractions, and outdoor recreation opportunities.

List of Places to Visit in West Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Henry Ford Museum of American Innovation: With over 26 million artifacts, the Henry Ford Museum of American Innovation is a must-visit for history buffs. Explore exhibits on American innovation, including exhibits on the Wright Brothers, Rosa Parks, and Thomas Edison.
  • Greenfield Village: Adjacent to the Henry Ford Museum is Greenfield Village, a living history museum featuring historical homes, workshops, and exhibits. Take a ride in a Model T Ford, watch demonstrations by artisans, and visit Thomas Edison’s lab.
  • Motown Museum: Visit the legendary “Hitsville U.S.A.” record label, where Motown Records was born. Learn about the history of Motown, see costumes and memorabilia from famous Motown artists, and stand in Studio A to see where hits were recorded.

Natural Attractions of West Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rouge Park: Rouge Park is the largest park in Detroit and offers several outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking and biking trails, fishing, and picnic areas.
  • Detroit Riverwalk: Stroll along the Detroit River and enjoy views of the iconic Detroit skyline. The Riverwalk features several parks, fountains, and monuments, as well as cafes and shops.
  • Belle Isle Park: Located in the Detroit River, Belle Isle Park offers scenic views of the river and city skyline. The park features a nature center, conservatory, museum, and a beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Side

  • Northwest Greenway Trail: The Northwest Greenway Trail is a 4.6-mile paved trail that connects several Detroit neighborhoods and parks, including Rouge Park, Eliza Howell Park, and Palmer Park.
  • Dequindre Cut: The Dequindre Cut is a 2-mile paved trail that runs from Eastern Market to the Detroit Riverfront. The trail features street art installations and connects to the Riverwalk.
  • Detroit Riverfront: The Detroit Riverfront offers several miles of scenic walking and biking paths, connecting several parks and attractions along the river.

Best Things to Do in West Side: Activities and Tours

  • Detroit Experience Factory: The Detroit Experience Factory offers walking and bus tours that showcase Detroit’s history, culture, and architecture.
  • Detroit Institute of Arts: The Detroit Institute of Arts is home to over 60,000 works of art, spanning several centuries and cultures. The museum offers guided tours, workshops, and special events.
  • Eastern Market: Eastern Market is a historic open-air market that operates year-round, featuring fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products. Take a free guided tour or stroll at your own leisure.

Food and Drink Scene of West Side

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dogs: Detroit-style Coney Dogs are a staple of the local food scene. Head to Lafayette or American Coney Island to try the classic version.
  • Square Pizza: Square or “Detroit-style” pizza is a local favorite. Check out Buddy’s Pizza or Jet’s Pizza for some of the best.
  • Soul Food: Detroit’s Soul Food scene is not to be missed. Visit Kuzzo’s Chicken & Waffles or Beans & Cornbread for a taste of Southern-inspired cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Faygo: This iconic Detroit soda company has been producing unique flavors since 1907. Try the classic Redpop or the unusual Cotton Candy.
  • Metro Detroit Brewery Scene: Detroit’s brewery scene is thriving, with several breweries and taprooms in the West Side area. Check out Atwater Brewery, Batch Brewing Company, or Founders Brewing Company.

Where to Stay in West Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at 97 Winder: This historic boutique hotel is located in a 19th-century mansion and offers 10 luxurious rooms and suites.
  • Trumbull & Porter: Trumbull & Porter is a stylish hotel located in the Corktown neighborhood, featuring 144 rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor courtyard.
  • Hosteling International-Detroit: This hostel is located in the historic Corktown neighborhood and offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms, as well as a shared kitchen and common areas.

What to Buy in West Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Detroit Made: Detroit Made is a gift shop located in the Fisher Building, featuring locally made products such as art, jewelry, apparel, and food.
  • Eastern Market: In addition to fresh produce and baked goods, Eastern Market features several stalls and shops selling local artisanal products.
  • City Bird: City Bird is a gift shop in Midtown featuring Detroit-themed gifts, including apparel, home decor, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of West Side

West Side is diverse and vibrant, reflecting the city’s history as a cultural melting pot. Visitors should respect the local communities and their customs, and be open to learning about different cultures and perspectives. Detroit is known for its artistic and musical scenes, so take the opportunity to explore local galleries, theaters, and music venues.

Best Time to Visit West Side

The best time to visit West Side is from May to October, when the weather is mild and several outdoor events and festivals take place. Summer can be hot and humid, but there are plenty of parks and green spaces to enjoy. Winter can be cold and snowy, but there are still plenty of indoor attractions and events to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Side

Do:

  • Respect the local communities and their customs.
  • Take advantage of the thriving cultural arts scene.
  • Enjoy the great outdoors, but be prepared for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car.
  • Wander around alone in unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.
  • Disrespect the history and diversity of Detroit.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Side

Like any big city, Detroit has some safety concerns, but West Side is generally considered safe for visitors. Be aware of your surroundings, keep valuables out of sight, and lock your car doors. Whenever possible, stick to well-lit and busy areas.

Health in West Side

Detroit has several hospitals and health clinics, but visitors should take precautions to avoid illness. Stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and wash your hands frequently. If you have any medical concerns, seek medical attention immediately.

Getting to West Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Side

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport: Located approximately 15 miles from West Side, this airport is the largest in Michigan and offers several domestic and international flights.
  • Bishop International Airport: Located approximately 60 miles from West Side in Flint, Michigan, this airport offers several domestic flights and some international flights.

Bus Stations Near West Side

  • Detroit Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Detroit, this station offers several bus routes to and from other major cities.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Amtrak offers several daily trains to and from Detroit.
  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies located at Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport and in downtown Detroit.

How to Get Around in West Side

West Side is best explored by car, but there are several public transportation options available, including buses and the Detroit People Mover. Walking and biking are also popular ways to explore the neighborhoods and parks.

Where to Go Next from West Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ann Arbor: Located approximately 45 minutes west of Detroit, Ann Arbor is a college town known for its cultural arts scene, restaurants, and shops.
  • Dearborn: Located approximately 25 minutes west of Detroit, Dearborn is home to the Arab American National Museum and the Henry Ford Estate.
  • Windsor, Ontario: Located across the Detroit River in Canada, Windsor offers stunning views of the Detroit skyline, as well as casinos, shopping, and restaurants.

Conclusion

West Side is a vibrant and diverse region of Detroit, offering cultural attractions, natural beauty, and a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or cuisine, West Side has something to offer every visitor. Follow our guide to plan your next trip to West Side and enjoy a safe and memorable experience.
