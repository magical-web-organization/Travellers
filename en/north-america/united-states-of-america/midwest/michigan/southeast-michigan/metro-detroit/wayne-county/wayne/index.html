Introduction to Wayne

Nestled in Wayne County, in Metro Detroit, Wayne is a small but bustling city in Southeast Michigan, a state that boasts the Great Lakes, countless forests and scenic drives and is known for its automotive industry — home to hundreds of assembly plants, suppliers and R&D centers. Wayne, filled with charming neighborhoods, welcoming locals and plenty of attractions, makes a great stop for a one-day or week-long trip in the area. This tourist-friendly city offers its residents and out-of-town visitors the perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture and history, vibrant shopping and dining destinations, and a dynamic nightlife.

List of Places to Visit in Wayne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Despite its small size, Wayne has a rich history and several cultural attractions that are worth a visit. Some of these must-visit sites for history buffs are:

  • Wayne Historical Museum: The Wayne Historical Museum is dedicated to preserving and sharing the history and heritage of Wayne. It has a range of exhibits and artifacts from the town’s past, including a reconstructed farmhouse, a schoolhouse, and the historical Blacksmith Shop.

  • Gleaners Community Food Bank: The Gleaners Community Food Bank is a nonprofit organization that collects food donations and distributes it to people in need. They offer tours of their facility, which is an informative and eye-opening experience for both adults and children.

  • Edsel and Eleanor Ford House: Located in Grosse Pointe Shores – just east of Wayne – the Ford House is a must-visit for history lovers. This beautiful 87-acre estate was the home of Edsel and Eleanor Ford, the son and daughter-in-law of Henry Ford, and boasts a priceless collection of antiques, paintings, and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Wayne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or just looking for a place to unwind, Wayne has some fantastic natural attractions that are sure to delight you. Some of the best ones are:

  • Hines Park: Hines Park is a popular outdoor destination covering over 17,000 acres along the Rouge River. The park has well-paved bike and hiking trails, picnic shelters, playgrounds, and plenty of opportunities to fish, canoe or kayak on the river.

  • Dairy Field Park: Dairy Field Park is a true hidden gem in Wayne, located close to Wayne State University campus and offering scenic views of woods and the city. The park has many nature trails that you can explore on foot or on a bike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wayne

For adventure lovers, Wayne offers some great hiking and biking routes, including the Riverwalk Trail, accessible right off the Detroit River Trail. This route winds around Wayne and offers scenic views of the Great Lakes and the Detroit skyline. Another great option is the Lower Rouge River Trail, a 7.6-mile hiking and biking loop. For more experienced hikers, check out the hiking trails in Hines Park.

Best Things to Do in Wayne: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor to Wayne, there’s no shortage of activities to keep you entertained. Some of the best things to do in Wayne are:

  • Wayne State University Campus Tour: Wayne State University is among the top-ranked universities in Michigan and has a rich history, beautiful architecture, and countless art pieces. A campus tour is a must-do activity for first-time visitors.

  • Stahls Automotive Museum Tour: If you’re an automotive enthusiast, head to Stahls Automotive Museum where you’ll find an impressive collection of classic cars and vintage memorabilia, many of which have been restored to their original glory.

  • Henry Ford Museum Tour: A visit to the Henry Ford Museum is a must-do activity for anyone visiting Wayne. The museum showcases some of the most significant and influential items of American history, including the Rosa Parks bus, Thomas Edison’s laboratory, and the Wright brothers’ bicycle shop.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wayne

If you’re planning to stay in Wayne for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Wayne Historical Museum and learn about the town’s past
2 Hike the Riverwalk Trail and enjoy scenic views of the Detroit skyline
3 Take the Wayne State University campus tour and visit its museums and the DIA
4 Visit the Edsel and Eleanor Ford House and take a picnic lunch to enjoy at their lakeshore patio
5 Go shopping at the upscale Twelve Oaks Mall, where you will find nearly 200 stores, boutiques and restaurants
6 Explore the natural beauty of Hines Park biking and hiking trails
7 Satisfy your inner foodie at some of Wayne’s excellent dining spots like The Avenue American Bistro or Travis Kitchen

Food and Drink Scene of Wayne

Wayne has a diverse food scene that combines many cuisines, flavors and cultures. Local favorites with unique flavors include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: A Michigan classic, a coney dog is a hot dog topped with chili, diced onions, and mustard. Head to a local coney island, like Leo’s Coney Island in Wayne to try one.

  • Paczki: Made popular by Detroit’s Polish community, paczki (pronounced “poonch-key”) are super-sized doughnuts filled with jelly or custard and dusted with powdered sugar. Head to Sister Pie in Detroit’s West Village to try one.

Local Beverages

  • Faygo: A Detroit classic, Faygo soda was established in 1907 and now offers over 50 flavors. Some popular ones include Redpop, Rock & Rye and Cotton Candy.

  • Beer from Atwater Brewery: Atwater Brewery features over 20 different beers on tap. Some of their best known include the Dirty Blonde and the Grand Circus IPA.

Where to Stay in Wayne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wayne has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly to luxury hotels. Whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a modern hotel, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the best ones include:

  • Bondy House Bed & Breakfast: This beautifully restored 1880s-era home offers comfortable rooms with antique furnishings and a fresh, homemade breakfast each morning.

  • Best Western Premier Executive Residency Detroit Southfield Hotel: Located in nearby Southfield, this modern hotel has contemporary rooms, on-site dining options, and a fitness center.

  • Marriott Detroit Livonia: This well-appointed hotel features spacious rooms, a restaurant and bar, and an indoor pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Wayne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Wayne can bring home some unique souvenirs to remember their trip. Some popular items include:

  • Vernors Ginger Ale: This Detroit favorite has been around since 1866 and is the oldest ginger ale brand in America.

  • Detroit-style Pizza: Detroit-style pizza is a unique style of pizza that has a crispy, almost burnt cheese crust and is thicker than a traditional pizza. Buddy’s Pizza in Detroit is famous for it.

Local Culture and Customs of Wayne

The people of Wayne are incredibly welcoming and friendly to visitors. They pride themselves on their community and cultural celebration of these excellent attributes. Wayne is a diverse community with a deep-seated pride in their schools, sports teams, and patriotic spirit.

Best Time to Visit Wayne

The best time to visit Wayne is during the summer months, between May and August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor attractions are at their best. Winter can be quite cold and snowy, so make sure to bundle up if you do visit in the colder months!

Do’s and Don’ts of Wayne

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Wayne by going for a bike or hike in Hines Park or along the Riverwalk Trail.
  • Try some of the unique and delicious food that Wayne has to offer, like a coney dog or paczki.
  • Visit the Detroit Institute of Arts or discover Greenfield Village at The Henry Ford Museum for more information on Michigan’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Take a day trip to nearby Belle Isle Park, a beautiful island park with scenic views of the Detroit River.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on trying some of the local craft beers and wines that Wayne is known for!
  • Don’t forget to show respect for the city’s cultural heritage and the environment when exploring the flora and fauna.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wayne

Wayne is a family-friendly community and an entirely safe place to visit. It’s always wise to stay vigilant and be aware of your surroundings in any city, especially when visiting unlit areas at night. It is best to lock your car and hotel room, park in a well-lit area, and avoid leaving personal belongings in plain sight in your car.

Health in Wayne

Wayne has an excellent medical system with several hospitals and clinics to choose from. In case of an emergency, you can contact the Wayne County Health Bureau or visit the Henry Ford Health System location nearby.

Getting to Wayne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Wayne is very easy, with flights, bus stations, and train stations located nearby.

Airports Near Wayne

  • Detroit Wayne County International Airport (DTW), located just 10 minutes from Wayne, served by all major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Wayne

  • Greyhound bus station in Detroit.

Other Options

  • Rent a car from Detroit Metro Airport or Wayne.
  • Take an Amtrak train to Dearborn Station, about 10 minutes from Wayne by taxi or car.

How to Get Around in Wayne

One of the best ways to get around in Wayne is by car, however, you also have other options:

  • Wayne State University Campus offers rental options such as Green Bike and Zipcar.
  • The buses in Wayne provide an excellent public transportation option and travel to most destinations across the city.

Where to Go Next from Wayne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for some nearby destinations to visit while in Wayne, we recommend:

  • Dearborn (10 minutes): A suburb just south of Detroit, with famous attractions such as The Henry Ford Museum and the Ford Rouge Factory Tour.
  • Detroit (30 minutes): A city full of cultural attractions, such as the Motown Museum, Detroit Institute of Arts, and the Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History.
  • Ann Arbor (45 minutes): Home to the University of Michigan and considered one of the most intellectual cities in the US with plenty of museums, galleries, and cafés.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a week-long adventure, Wayne has a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors. From its natural beauty and outdoor opportunities to its rich history and culture, it’s no wonder that Wayne is fast becoming one of the most popular tourist destinations in Southeast Michigan. So, what are you waiting for? Plan your trip to Wayne today!
