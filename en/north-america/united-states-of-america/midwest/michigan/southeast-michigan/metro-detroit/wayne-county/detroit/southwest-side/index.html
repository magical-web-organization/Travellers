Introduction to Southwest Side

Located in Wayne County, in the Metro Detroit area of Southeast Michigan, Southwest Side is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This multicultural neighborhood boasts a rich history, vibrant culture, and an array of attractions to satisfy any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Southwest Side

If you’re planning to visit Southwest Side, make sure to check out these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Motown Museum: Explore the birthplace of Motown music and discover the rich history of the genre.
  • Arab American National Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Arab Americans in the United States.
  • Michigan Science Center: Perfect for families, this museum has interactive exhibits on science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM).
  • Fort Wayne: Step back in time and explore the historic fort that played a significant role in American history.

Natural Attractions of Southwest Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rouge Park: The largest park in Detroit, Rouge Park offers hiking, biking, and bird-watching opportunities.
  • Riverwalk: Walk along the river and take in the stunning views of Detroit’s skyline.
  • Belle Isle: Escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy nature in this beautiful island park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southwest Side

If you love hiking and biking, Southwest Side won’t disappoint. Check out these routes:

  • Dequindre Cut: This former railway line has been transformed into a scenic pathway for walking and biking.
  • Southwest Detroit Greenlink: Explore the diversity of Southwest Side with this 10-mile trail network that connects important cultural sites, parks, and neighborhoods.
  • Detroit Riverwalk: Stretching for 5.5 miles, this paved path provides beautiful views of the Detroit River and downtown Detroit.

Best Things to Do in Southwest Side: Activities and Tours

  • Detroit Experience Factory Tours: Get an insider’s look at the city and explore the highlights of Southwest Side.
  • Detroit Institute of Arts: One of the largest art museums in the country, the DIA boasts stunning collections of art from around the world.
  • Z Lot: Get a unique view of the city from this rooftop parking lot that serves as an outdoor art gallery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southwest Side

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Southwest Side:

Day 1: Explore the Riverwalk and visit the Michigan Science Center. Day 2: Take a tour of the Motown Museum and visit Belle Isle. Day 3: Walk through Southwest Detroit Greenway and visit the Arab American National Museum. Day 4: Bike along the Dequindre Cut and visit the Z Lot. Day 5: Explore the Fort Wayne and visit the Detroit Institute of Arts. Day 6: Hike through Rouge Park and bike along the Detroit Riverwalk. Day 7: Spend the day exploring the local food scene and shopping for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Southwest Side

Southwest Side has a diverse food scene that mixes cultures and flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: A Detroit classic, a coney dog is a hot dog topped with chili, onions, and mustard.
  • Shawarma: This Middle-Eastern dish is a staple in Southwest Side and can be found in many restaurants.
  • Paczki: A Polish pastry that’s similar to a doughnut but with a sweeter, denser texture.

Local Beverages

  • Faygo: A Detroit-based soda brand that offers a variety of flavors, including the classic Rock & Rye.
  • Vernors: A ginger ale that has been made in Detroit since 1866.

Where to Stay in Southwest Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southwest Side has a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

  • Hotel St. Regis: A historical hotel that offers comfortable and elegant rooms.
  • Trumbull and Porter Hotel: A hip, boutique hotel that features locally-made furniture and artwork.
  • Hi Detroit Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to meet fellow backpackers and explore the city.

What to Buy in Southwest Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home? Check out these places:

  • Pure Detroit: A store that offers unique souvenirs and gifts, including clothing, bags, and prints.
  • ASHE Supply Co: A coffee shop and boutique that sells handmade products, including leather goods and grooming supplies.
  • Parker Street Market: A grocery store that offers locally-made products, including organic produce, beer, and wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Southwest Side

Southwest Side is a multicultural neighborhood that celebrates diversity and traditions. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect cultural and religious practices.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Learn some basic phrases in different languages.

Best Time to Visit Southwest Side

The best time to visit Southwest Side is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you want to experience the cultural festivals and events in the area, plan your visit accordingly:

  • Arab American Festival in June
  • Mexican Fiesta in August
  • African World Festival in August

Do’s and Don’ts of Southwest Side

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Use public transportation or ride-sharing services to get around.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the neighborhood on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your car.
  • Wander around unfamiliar neighborhoods alone at night.
  • Disrespect local residents and businesses.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Southwest Side, it’s important to keep safety and health in mind. Here are some tips:

Safety in Southwest Side

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Use common sense and take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.
  • Check travel advisories and consult with locals for updates on safety concerns.

Health in Southwest Side

  • Wear sunscreen and drink plenty of water during the hot summer months.
  • Bring bug spray if you plan to hike or camp.
  • Stay up-to-date on vaccinations and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Southwest Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southwest Side is accessible by car, bus, and train. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Southwest Side

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport (DTW)
  • Coleman A. Young International Airport (DET)

Bus Stations Near Southwest Side

  • Greyhound Bus Station
  • Megabus Station

Other Options

  • Amtrak train station: Detroit Station
  • Ride-sharing services: Uber, Lyft

How to Get Around in Southwest Side

Southwest Side is easily explored on foot or by bike, but there are other transportation options:

  • Detroit People Mover: A 2.9-mile elevated train that circles around downtown Detroit.
  • Bus: Detroit Department of Transportation (DDOT) and SMART bus services operate in Southwest Side.
  • Taxi/ride-sharing: Uber, Lyft, and taxis are available.

Where to Go Next from Southwest Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Southeast Michigan, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Ann Arbor: A college town with a thriving arts and cultural scene.
  • Frankenmuth: A quaint Bavarian-style village that’s home to Bronner’s Christmas Wonderland, the world’s largest Christmas store.
  • Mackinac Island: A car-free island in Lake Huron that’s known for its beautiful nature trails, historic sites, and fudge.

Conclusion

Southwest Side Detroit is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood that offers a wide range of attractions, activities, and experiences. With its rich culture, natural beauty, and friendly locals, Southwest Side is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
