Introduction to Midtown-New Center

Located in the heart of Detroit, Midtown-New Center is a neighborhood that is known for its diverse range of cultural attractions, world-renowned museums, and historical landmarks. The neighborhood is home to some of the most iconic landmarks such as the Detroit Institute of Art, the Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History, and the Fisher Building. Midtown-New Center is also known for its vibrant art scene, which can be seen in the form of public murals, street art, and galleries.

List of Places to Visit in Midtown-New Center

The following is a comprehensive list of the top places to visit in Midtown-New Center:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Detroit Institute of Art: The Detroit Institute of Art is one of the largest and most significant art museums in the United States. The museum is home to over 60,000 works of art from around the world, ranging from ancient to contemporary art.
  • The Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the rich and vibrant history of African Americans. The museum has a collection of more than 35,000 artifacts, artworks, and archival materials.
  • The Fisher Building: The Fisher Building is an iconic landmark in Midtown-New Center. It is known for its stunning architecture and is often referred to as one of the most beautiful buildings in Detroit.
  • The Detroit Historical Museum: This museum explores the rich history of Detroit with a collection of over 200,000 artifacts. The museum features exhibits on everything from the city’s automotive industry to the Motown music scene.

Natural Attractions of Midtown-New Center: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Dequindre Cut Greenway: The Dequindre Cut Greenway is a 2-mile-long paved path that runs through the heart of Detroit. The path is known for its beautiful murals and sculptures and is a popular destination for cyclists and joggers.
  • The Detroit Riverfront: The Detroit Riverfront is a scenic destination that offers stunning views of Canada and the Detroit skyline. The riverfront offers a variety of activities, including walking, cycling, and kayaking.
  • Belle Isle Park: Belle Isle Park is a 982-acre island in the Detroit River that is home to a variety of attractions, including a zoo, an aquarium, and a conservatory. It is also a popular destination for picnicking and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Midtown-New Center

Midtown-New Center is home to a variety of hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Some of the notable routes include:

  • The Dequindre Cut Greenway: The Dequindre Cut Greenway is a 2-mile-long paved path that runs through the heart of Detroit. The path is known for its beautiful murals and sculptures and is a popular destination for cyclists and joggers.
  • The RiverWalk: The RiverWalk is a 3.5-mile-long pathway that runs along the Detroit River. The path offers stunning views of the river and Canada and is a great destination for leisurely walks or runs.

Best Things to Do in Midtown-New Center: Activities and Tours

  • Street Art Tours: Midtown-New Center is known for its vibrant art scene, and visitors can take guided tours to see some of the best street art and murals in the city.
