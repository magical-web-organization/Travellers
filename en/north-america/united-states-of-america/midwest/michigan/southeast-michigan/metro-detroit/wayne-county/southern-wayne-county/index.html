Introduction to Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County is a charming destination located in Wayne County, Metro Detroit, Southeast Michigan, Midwest, United States of America, in North America. With its rich history, cultural diversity, and natural beauty, Southern Wayne County is an ideal spot for vacationers looking for an immersive experience.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, food and drinks scene, local culture, and more. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore the hidden gems of Southern Wayne County.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County boasts numerous attractions, including museums, historical sites, natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, and much more.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Southern Wayne County has plenty to offer, including:

  • Henry Ford Museum of American Innovation: This museum celebrates America’s ingenuity, creativity, and resourcefulness, showcasing exhibits on transportation, machinery, and inventions.

  • Greenfield Village: Step back in time and explore life in America’s past. This living history museum features 80 historic buildings, authentic artifacts, and rides around the 80-acre park on a real steam-powered locomotive, vintage model T, or horse-drawn carriage.

  • Motown Museum: A must-visit for music lovers, this museum is dedicated to the legacy of Motown and its founder, Berry Gordy. Explore the Hitsville U.S.A. recording studio and see where the magic of Motown was made.

Natural Attractions of Southern Wayne County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Wayne County is a nature lover’s paradise with a wide range of outdoor attractions, including:

  • Lake Erie Metropark: This park offers stunning views of Lake Erie, birdwatching, hiking trails, golf courses, picnic areas, and water sports such as swimming, boating, and fishing.

  • Sterling State Park: This park stretches 1,300 acres along the shores of Lake Erie, with sandy beaches, dunes, and trails for hiking, kiteboarding, and birdwatching.

  • Lower Huron Metropark: Located on the Huron River, this park offers pristine natural beauty and recreational opportunities like hiking, canoeing, fishing, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County offers a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness. Some of the best trails include:

  • Detroit Heritage River Trail: This 20-mile trail runs along the Detroit River, featuring stunning views of the riverfront, historical landmarks, parks, and attractions.

  • Belle Isle Park Loop: A scenic loop through the “jewel of Detroit” island park, featuring serene views of the Detroit River and skyline.

  • West River Parkway Trail: This 17-mile trail passes through woods, fields, and parks along the Huron River, offering views of wildlife and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Southern Wayne County: Activities and Tours

There is so much to see and do in Southern Wayne County, from food tours to riverboat cruises to hot air balloon rides. Here are some must-do activities and tours:

  • Detroit Riverboat Tours: Take a relaxing cruise on the Detroit River and explore the city from a unique perspective.

  • Masonry Tours Detroit: Discover the rich history and architectural beauty of Detroit through guided walking tours focusing on the city’s iconic Masonic Temple.

  • Paradise Valley Food Tour: Taste your way through Paradise Valley’s vibrant food scene with this walking tour, featuring local cuisine and cultural stories.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Wayne County

Day 1: Explore the Henry Ford Museum and Greenfield Village

Day 2: Hike the Detroit Heritage River Trail and visit Belle Isle Park

Day 3: Take a food tour of Paradise Valley and visit the Motown Museum

Day 4: Visit Sterling State Park and Lower Huron Metropark

Day 5: Bike the West River Parkway Trail and go hot air ballooning

Day 6: Take a Detroit Riverboat cruise and visit the Arab American National Museum

Day 7: Enjoy a day at the Lake Erie Metropark, featuring watersports, golf, and a nature center.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County is a melting pot of culinary traditions, with a wide array of eateries showcasing local and international cuisine. Here are some highlights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: A classic Detroit-style hot dog smothered in chili, mustard, and onions. A must-try when visiting Detroit.

  • Faygo Soda: A Michigan-made soda, known for its unique flavors like Red Pop, Rock & Rye, and Cotton Candy.

  • Paczki: A traditional Polish pastry, typically consumed on Fat Tuesday before the start of Lent. A sugary delight not to be missed.

Local Beverages

  • Detroit City Distillery: Visit this Detroit distillery and taste locally sourced spirits, including gin, vodka, and whiskey.

  • Batch Brewing Company: This Corktown brewery is known for its small-batch beers and experimental brews, featuring local ingredients and flavors.

  • Wright & Company: Enjoy a classic or creative cocktail at this cozy cocktail bar in the heart of downtown Detroit.

Where to Stay in Southern Wayne County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Wayne County offers a range of accommodations, from budget-friendly options to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Aloft Detroit at The David Whitney: A stylish boutique hotel located in a historic building near downtown Detroit.

  • Inn at 97 Winder: A luxurious guesthouse in a restored mansion in the iconic Brush Park neighborhood.

  • Hostel Detroit: A budget-friendly option in a historic building in the Corktown neighborhood, featuring dormitory-style accommodations and communal spaces.

What to Buy in Southern Wayne County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Southern Wayne County, don’t forget to pick up some local souvenirs and products, including:

  • Vernor’s Ginger Ale: A Detroit original, this sweet and spicy ginger ale has been a local favorite since 1866.

  • Detroit Tigers or Red Wings Gear: Show your support for the hometown teams with some official merchandise.

  • Motor City Blend Coffee: A locally roasted coffee blend featuring beans from around the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County is a multicultural destination, with a diverse mix of residents and visitors. It’s important to respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Politeness and Kindness: Southerners have a reputation for being hospitable and friendly, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation and be polite.

  • Religious Respect: Southern Wayne County has a diverse mix of religions, including Christianity, Islam, and Judaism, so it’s important to respect all faiths.

  • Cultural Festivals: Southern Wayne County hosts numerous cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Arab and Chaldean Festival, the annual Detroit Jazz Festival, and the Sweetest Heart of Mary Pierogi Festival.

Best Time to Visit Southern Wayne County

The best time to visit Southern Wayne County is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are numerous outdoor events and festivals taking place. Fall is also a great time to visit, with the changing colors of the leaves and Oktoberfest events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Wayne County

Do:

  • Be respectful: Respect the local culture and customs, including politeness, kindness, and religious differences.

  • Try the local cuisine: Southern Wayne County is home to some fantastic restaurants and eateries, so be sure to try the local cuisine.

  • Explore the outdoors: Southern Wayne County has many beautiful parks, gardens, and outdoor attractions, so be sure to explore them.

Don’t:

  • Be rude or judgmental: Remember that everyone is entitled to their own opinion, so don’t be rude or judgmental towards others.

  • Litter or damage property: Help keep Southern Wayne County beautiful by not littering or damaging property.

  • Ignore safety precautions: Pay attention to safety precautions and warnings when visiting attractions or participating in activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County is generally a safe destination, but it’s important to take common sense precautions, such as:

  • Watch your belongings: Keep your valuables with you at all times and never leave them unattended.

  • Stay on main roads and paths: Avoid walking alone at night or in areas that are not well-lit or populated.

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye out for suspicious activity and report anything unusual to the authorities.

Health in Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and urgent care centers. However, it’s always important to take precautions, such as:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water and avoid overexertion in hot weather.

  • Use sunscreen: Protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays by wearing sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher.

  • Wash your hands: Practice good hygiene by washing your hands frequently, especially before eating or touching your face.

Getting to Southern Wayne County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Wayne County

Southern Wayne County is served by several airports, including:

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport: Located in Romulus, Michigan, just minutes from downtown Detroit, this airport is the primary airport for the Detroit metropolitan area.

  • Oakland County International Airport: Located in Waterford Township, Michigan, this airport is convenient for those staying in the northern suburbs of Detroit.

Bus Stations Near Southern Wayne County

Greyhound operates several bus stations in the Detroit area, including:

  • Detroit Greyhound Station: Located downtown, this station is a major hub for bus travel throughout the region.

  • Dearborn Greyhound Station: Located in the western suburbs, this station is convenient for those staying in the Dearborn or Downriver areas.

Other Options

Southern Wayne County is easily accessible by car, with major highways like I-94, I-275, and M-14 running through the area.

How to Get Around in Southern Wayne County

The best way to get around Southern Wayne County is by car, either by renting a vehicle or using a ridesharing service like Uber or Lyft. However, there are also several public transportation options, including:

  • SMART Bus: The Suburban Mobility Authority for Regional Transportation (SMART) provides bus service throughout the Detroit metropolitan area.

  • QLine: Detroit’s streetcar system, the QLine, is a fun and convenient way to explore the downtown area.

  • People Mover: Detroit’s elevated rail system, the People Mover, is a quick and easy way to travel between downtown attractions.

Where to Go Next from Southern Wayne County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Wayne County is a great home base for exploring some of Michigan’s other attractions, including:

  • Ann Arbor: Located just 40 miles west of Detroit, Ann Arbor is a charming college town renowned for its food scene, cultural events, and natural beauty.

  • Dearborn: Located just south of Detroit, Dearborn is home to the Henry Ford Museum, Greenfield Village, and the Arab American National Museum.

  • Lake Michigan: Just a few hours west of Detroit, Lake Michigan offers stunning beaches, lighthouses, and charming beach towns like Saugatuck and Grand Haven.

Conclusion

Southern Wayne County is a hidden gem that offers an exciting blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With so much to see and do, we hope this tour guide has helped you plan your trip and experience all the best that Southern Wayne County has to offer. So, pack your bags, hit the road and get ready to make some unforgettable memories in Southern Wayne County!
