Introduction to East Side

Located in Wayne County, in the Metro Detroit area of southeast Michigan, East Side is a vibrant and diverse region with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor activities, or simply enjoying great food and drink, this region will not disappoint. Here’s what you need to know before planning your trip.

List of Places to Visit in East Side

When you visit East Side, you’ll find a wealth of attractions and experiences to enjoy. The following are some of the must-see destinations in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in the history and culture of the area, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to learn and explore:

  • Detroit Institute of Arts - A world-class museum featuring a vast collection of art from around the world, with a special focus on American art.
  • Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History - This museum is dedicated to celebrating the contributions of African Americans to the history and culture of Detroit and the United States.
  • Pewabic Pottery - A historic pottery studio and museum, Pewabic Pottery offers tours and workshops for visitors to learn about the art of pottery.

Natural Attractions of East Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, East Side is the perfect destination for you. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Belle Isle Park - Located on an island in the Detroit River, Belle Isle Park offers visitors a chance to enjoy hiking, biking, swimming, and other outdoor activities in a beautiful natural setting.
  • Lake St. Clair Metropark - This park features a large lake, nature trails, and a variety of outdoor recreation options, including fishing, boating, and swimming.
  • Edsel and Eleanor Ford House - A historic home and garden situated on Lake St. Clair, the Ford House offers guided tours and beautiful views of the lake and surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Side

If you’re looking for some great outdoor adventures and exercise, there are several hiking and biking routes to explore in East Side:

  • Dequindre Cut Greenway - This 1.35-mile greenway trail is perfect for walking, running, or cycling. It’s located below street level, offering a unique perspective on the built environment of the area.
  • The Detroit RiverWalk - This scenic trail winds along the Detroit River and offers stunning views of the downtown skyline. It’s a popular spot for walking, biking, and rollerblading.
  • Macomb Orchard Trail - This 23-mile paved trail is perfect for cycling or walking, with plenty of opportunities to take in the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in East Side: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with family or looking for fun activities to enjoy on your own, there’s no shortage of things to do in East Side:

  • Ford Rouge Factory Tour - This guided tour takes visitors to the Ford Rouge Factory, where they can see how Ford cars and trucks are made. It’s a fascinating look at the industrial history of Detroit.
  • Sea Life Michigan Aquarium - This aquarium features a variety of marine life, including sharks, sea turtles, and colorful fish. It’s a great place to visit with kids.
  • Detroit Zoo - Located in nearby Royal Oak, the Detroit Zoo features a wide variety of animals and exhibits, including the Arctic Ring of Life, which includes polar bears and arctic foxes.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Side

If you’re planning a trip to East Side and want to make the most of your time, consider this itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Detroit Institute of Arts and the Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History
2 Explore Belle Isle Park and the nearby Lake St. Clair Metropark
3 Take the Ford Rouge Factory Tour and check out Pewabic Pottery
4 Visit the Detroit Zoo or the Sea Life Michigan Aquarium
5 Enjoy hiking or biking along the Dequindre Cut Greenway or the Detroit RiverWalk
6 Check out the Macomb Orchard Trail and the Edsel and Eleanor Ford House
7 Spend a day shopping and trying local food and drink in the East Side area

Food and Drink Scene of East Side

East Side offers visitors a chance to sample some of the best food and drink in the Detroit area. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dogs - A Detroit classic, Coney Dogs are hot dogs topped with chili, mustard, and onions, served on a steamed bun.
  • Paczki - These Polish pastries are a Detroit favorite, with a variety of fillings, like strawberry, raspberry, and custard.
  • Detroit-Style Pizza - This square-shaped pizza is crispy on the bottom and has cheese all the way to the edge, with toppings like pepperoni, sausage, and green peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Faygo - A Detroit soda brand with a cult following, Faygo comes in a variety of flavors, like Redpop, Moon Mist, and Rock & Rye.
  • Vernors - Another Detroit soda brand, Vernors is a ginger ale with a unique flavor. Many locals swear by it as a cure for stomach ailments.
  • Craft Beer - The East Side area is home to several breweries, including Atwater Brewery and Detroit Beer Company. Stop in for a pint or a flight of local brews.

Where to Stay in East Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, East Side offers a variety of options to fit any budget:

  • The Westin Book Cadillac Detroit - This historic hotel is located in the heart of downtown Detroit, with easy access to the area’s top attractions.
  • Hostel Detroit - A budget-friendly option located in the historic Corktown neighborhood, Hostel Detroit offers dorm-style rooms and private rooms.
  • Two Hearted Queen - This stylish guesthouse is located in the West Village neighborhood and offers a unique and comfortable stay for travelers.

What to Buy in East Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a memento of your trip to East Side, there are several local products and souvenirs worth checking out:

  • Detroit Merchandise - Many local shops offer Detroit-themed t-shirts, hats, and other souvenirs to remember your trip.
  • Sanders Chocolate - Sanders is a Detroit chocolate company that offers a variety of sweets, including chocolate covered cherries, hot fudge, and sea salt caramels.
  • Detroit Made goods - Made in Detroit is a popular store featuring clothing, accessories, and home goods made by local artists and designers.

Local Culture and Customs of East Side

When visiting East Side, it’s important to be aware of the local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Respect the History - Detroit has a rich cultural history, and many residents take pride in their city’s contributions to music, industry, and arts. Take the time to learn about and appreciate the area’s history and culture.
  • Be Polite - Detroiters are known for being friendly and welcoming. Make sure to say “please” and “thank you,” and be respectful when interacting with locals.
  • Dress Appropriately - Depending on the time of year, the weather in Detroit can be unpredictable. Be prepared for both hot and cold temperatures, and dress appropriately for your activities.

Best Time to Visit East Side

The best time to visit East Side is between May and September, when temperatures are mild and there’s plenty of sunshine. However, if you’re interested in winter sports like ice fishing or snowmobiling, you may want to visit during the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Side

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting East Side:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the local food and drink scene. Detroit offers some of the best food and drink in the region.
  • Learn about the history and culture of the area. Detroit has a rich cultural history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites to explore.
  • Be respectful and polite to locals. Detroiters are known for being friendly and welcoming, so be sure to follow their lead.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car. As in any city, car break-ins can occur. Make sure to keep your valuables out of sight.
  • Ignore safety precautions. While Detroit has made great strides in recent years to improve safety, there are still some areas that can be unsafe at night. Be sure to take the necessary precautions to stay safe when out and about.

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, it’s important to be aware of safety and health concerns:

Safety in East Side

Detroit has a reputation for being a dangerous city, but it’s important to note that crime rates have been declining in recent years. Like any city, there are certain areas that can be unsafe, particularly at night. It’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take the necessary precautions to stay safe.

Health in East Side

East Side is a relatively safe and healthy area. It’s always a good idea to be up-to-date on your vaccinations and take precautions to avoid illnesses like the flu, but there are no specific health concerns to be aware of when visiting this region.

Getting to East Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Side is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near East Side

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport - Located about 30 minutes west of the city, Detroit Metro Airport is the largest airport in the region, with flights to destinations across the United States and around the world.

Bus Stations Near East Side

  • Detroit Greyhound Station - Located near downtown Detroit, the Greyhound station offers bus service to destinations across the United States and Canada.

Other Options

  • Amtrak - Amtrak offers train service to Detroit from several cities across the United States.
  • Car Rental - If you’re flying into Detroit, renting a car is a great option for getting around the city and exploring the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in East Side

When visiting East Side, there are several transportation options available:

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a great option for getting around the city and exploring the surrounding area.
  • Public Transportation - DDOT and SMART are the two bus systems in Detroit and the surrounding suburbs. Both offer affordable and convenient service throughout the area.
  • Biking - With several bike routes and trails available, biking is a great way to explore the city and surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from East Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels after visiting East Side, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring:

  • Ann Arbor - Located about an hour west of Detroit, Ann Arbor is a charming university town with lots of great cafes, shops, and cultural attractions.
  • Frankenmuth - Located about an hour north of Detroit, Frankenmuth is a Bavarian-themed village with lots of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions.
  • Niagara Falls - Located about four hours northeast of Detroit, Niagara Falls is one of the most breathtaking natural wonders in North America.

Conclusion

East Side is a vibrant and diverse region with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor activities, or simply enjoying great food and drink, this region will not disappoint. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and charm of East Side.
