Grosse Pointe is a charming city located in Wayne County, in Metro Detroit, in Southeast Michigan, in Michigan, in Midwest, in the United States of America, in North America. Its picturesque surroundings, unique cultural attractions, and scenic spots attract tourists from all over the world.

In this travel guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive overview of what to see and do in Grosse Pointe, where to eat and stay, and how to get around. Let’s dive in!

Introduction to Grosse Pointe

Grosse Pointe is a group of five waterfront communities: Grosse Pointe Farms, Grosse Pointe Park, Grosse Pointe City, Grosse Pointe Shores, and the Village of Grosse Pointe. Each community has its own character, but they all share the same stunning views of Lake Saint Clair and a rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Grosse Pointe

Grosse Pointe offers plenty of attractions and activities for all types of travelers. Here is a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Grosse Pointe has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its museums and historic sites. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • The Edsel and Eleanor Ford House: This National Historic Landmark was the family home of Edsel and Eleanor Ford, the son and daughter-in-law of Henry Ford. The estate includes a 60-room house, gardens, and a lakefront property.

  • The Grosse Pointe War Memorial: This beautiful building is a tribute to the brave men and women who served in World War II. It now serves as a community center, hosting events, lectures, and concerts.

  • The Lake Saint Clair Metropark: This sprawling park has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in camping, fishing, hiking, or birdwatching, you’ll find it here.

Natural Attractions of Grosse Pointe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grosse Pointe is known for its breathtaking scenery and outdoor activities. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • The Grosse Pointe Yacht Club: This private club is a must-visit spot for sailing enthusiasts. The club is home to several regattas throughout the year and has a beautiful facility with dining options, tennis courts, and a pool.

  • Windmill Pointe Park: This beautiful park is a great spot for a picnic or a leisurely walk. It offers stunning views of Lake Saint Clair and has a playground for kids.

  • Grosse Pointe Woods Nature Center: This nature preserve is perfect for a hike or a bike ride. It has over 70 acres of wooded trails and is home to a variety of wildlife, including birds, foxes, and deer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grosse Pointe

Grosse Pointe has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the lake and the city. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Grosse Pointe Loop: This 12-mile loop takes you through all five of Grosse Pointe’s waterfront communities. You’ll see beautiful mansions, parks, and beaches along the way.

  • The Detroit Riverfront Trail: This 5.5-mile trail takes you along the Detroit River and offers stunning views of the skyline. It’s a great option for both walkers and bikers.

  • The Lakeshore Park Trail: This 1.5-mile trail takes you through a beautiful wooded area and offers great views of the lake. It’s a popular spot for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Grosse Pointe: Activities and Tours

Grosse Pointe has a variety of activities and tours that are perfect for tourists. Some of the best things to do include:

  • The Michigan Pedaler: This unique tour takes you around the city on a pedal-powered trolley. You’ll stop at several of the top attractions and have a chance to sample some of the local beer.

  • The Detroit Princess Riverboat: This beautiful riverboat offers dinner cruises, sightseeing tours, and private events. It’s a great way to see the city from a different perspective.

  • The Grosse Pointe Art Center: This non-profit organization is dedicated to promoting the arts in Grosse Pointe. It hosts several exhibits throughout the year and offers classes for both children and adults.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grosse Pointe

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Grosse Pointe, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Edsel and Eleanor Ford House, followed by a picnic at Windmill Pointe Park.

  • Day 2: Take a bike ride along the Grosse Pointe Loop, stopping at some of the top attractions along the way.

  • Day 3: Spend the day at the Lake Saint Clair Metropark, fishing, hiking, or just relaxing on the beach.

  • Day 4: Take a tour of the city on the Michigan Pedaler, stopping at some of the top breweries and restaurants.

  • Day 5: Visit the Grosse Pointe War Memorial and attend a concert or event.

  • Day 6: Spend the day at the Detroit Princess Riverboat, enjoying a dinner cruise or a sightseeing tour.

  • Day 7: Visit the Grosse Pointe Art Center and take a class or buy a souvenir to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Grosse Pointe

Grosse Pointe has a thriving food and drink scene, influenced by its French and British heritage. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: This Detroit classic is a hot dog topped with chili, onions, and mustard.

  • Pasties: These savory pies are filled with meat and vegetables and are perfect for a quick lunch.

  • Detroit-Style Pizza: This rectangular pizza has a thick, crispy crust and is topped with sauce and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Faygo Pop: This local soda company has been around since 1907 and offers a variety of flavors, including Redpop and Rock & Rye.

  • Vernors Ginger Ale: This ginger ale was invented in Detroit in 1866 and is known for its distinct taste.

  • Local Breweries: Grosse Pointe has several local breweries, including the Atwater Brewery and Motor City Brewing Works.

Where to Stay in Grosse Pointe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grosse Pointe has a variety of lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few top options:

  • The Shorecrest Motor Inn: This cozy inn is located in Grosse Pointe Shores and offers comfortable rooms and a great location.

  • The Inn on Ferry Street: This historic hotel is located in Midtown Detroit and offers spacious rooms and modern amenities.

  • Airbnb: Grosse Pointe has several Airbnb options, including rooms in private homes and entire apartments.

What to Buy in Grosse Pointe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Grosse Pointe has several souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing home. Here are a few ideas:

  • Detroit-Style Pizza Cutter: This unique pizza cutter is shaped like a car and pays tribute to the city’s automotive heritage.

  • Motown Records Merchandise: This record label was founded in Detroit and had several hit songs in the 1960s and 1970s.

  • Coneys and Pasties: These local specialties can be bought frozen and brought home for a taste of Grosse Pointe.

Local Culture and Customs of Grosse Pointe

Grosse Pointe has a friendly, welcoming culture, influenced by its French and British heritage. The locals are proud of their city and love to share its history and attractions with visitors. A few local customs to keep in mind include:

  • Tipping: It’s customary to leave a 15-20% tip at restaurants.

  • Dressing Appropriately: Grosse Pointe is a relatively casual city, but it’s always a good idea to dress appropriately for the weather and the occasion.

Best Time to Visit Grosse Pointe

The best time to visit Grosse Pointe is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and most of the outdoor attractions are open. However, the fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit, with beautiful foliage and several events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grosse Pointe

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Grosse Pointe:

Do:

  • Take a bike ride along the Grosse Pointe Loop

  • Visit the Edsel and Eleanor Ford House

  • Try the local specialties, like Coneys and Pasties

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in plain sight in your car

  • Litter in the parks or on the beaches

  • Make noise after 11 PM

Safety and Health

Grosse Pointe is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, like locking your car and using common sense when walking alone at night.

Safety in Grosse Pointe

  • Make sure to lock your car and keep valuables out of plain sight

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas

  • Be aware of your surroundings and report any suspicious activity to the police.

Health in Grosse Pointe

  • Make sure to bring any necessary prescription medications with you

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent if you’re spending time outside

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated

Getting to Grosse Pointe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Grosse Pointe is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are a few transportation options:

Airports Near Grosse Pointe

  • Detroit Metropolitan Airport: This is the closest airport to Grosse Pointe, located about 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Grosse Pointe

  • Greyhound Bus Station: This bus station is located in Downtown Detroit, about 20 minutes away by car.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore Grosse Pointe and the surrounding area at your own pace.

  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also readily available in Grosse Pointe.

How to Get Around in Grosse Pointe

Grosse Pointe is a relatively small city and is easy to get around on foot or by bike. However, if you’re planning on exploring the surrounding areas, renting a car is a good option. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Grosse Pointe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grosse Pointe is located within easy driving distance of several other great destinations in Michigan. Here are a few ideas for day trips:

  • The Henry Ford Museum: This museum is located in Dearborn, about 30 minutes away by car. It showcases the history of the automotive industry and has several historic exhibits.

  • Mackinac Island: This beautiful island is located about four hours north of Grosse Pointe and is known for its scenic beauty and historic attractions.

  • Traverse City: This charming city is located about 3.5 hours north of Grosse Pointe and is known for its wineries and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Grosse Pointe is a beautiful city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, scenic views, outdoor adventures, or great food, you’ll find it here.
