Introduction to Downtown

Downtown Detroit is the beating heart of the city and the cultural, economic, and social hub of the region. The city is rich with history and tradition, and offers visitors a unique blend of classic architecture, museums, and world-class cultural attractions. Downtown Detroit is also home to some of the state’s most popular parks, outdoor adventures, and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Here’s our list of must-visit places in Downtown Detroit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Detroit Institute of Arts: One of the largest art museums in the country, the Detroit Institute of Arts is home to more than 60,000 works of art from around the world.
  • The Motown Museum: Located in the legendary Hitsville U.S.A., the Motown Museum takes visitors on a journey through the history of Motown music and its legendary performers.
  • The Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History: The largest museum of its kind in the world, the Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History tells the story of African Americans in Detroit and beyond, from the beginning of time to the present day.
  • The Michigan Science Center: With interactive exhibits, live demonstrations, and educational programs, the Michigan Science Center is the perfect place to learn about science and technology.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belle Isle: One of Michigan’s most popular parks, Belle Isle is a 982-acre island park with stunning views of the Detroit skyline.
  • The Dequindre Cut: A 1.35-mile urban trail, the Dequindre Cut offers visitors a unique view of the city, connecting the Detroit Riverfront with Eastern Market.
  • The Detroit Riverfront: A 3.5-mile stretch of waterfront, the Detroit Riverfront is the perfect place to enjoy a leisurely stroll or bike ride along the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

If you love the outdoors, Downtown Detroit has plenty of options for you. Here are our top picks for hiking/biking routes:

  • The Dequindre Cut Greenway: A 1.35-mile urban trail that runs between the riverfront and Eastern Market, the Dequindre Cut Greenway is perfect for bikers, walkers, and joggers.
  • Rouge River Gateway Trail: A 27-mile rail trail that runs from Rouge Park to the Detroit River, the Rouge River Gateway Trail is perfect for cyclists.
  • William G. Milliken State Park and Harbor Greenway: A 31-acre park located on the east side of Downtown Detroit, the William G. Milliken State Park and Harbor Greenway is the perfect place for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Downtown Detroit, there’s no shortage of options. Here are our top picks:

  • The Detroit Experience Factory: A non-profit organization that offers guided tours of the city, the Detroit Experience Factory is the perfect way to learn about the history and culture of Detroit.
  • The QLINE: A 3.3-mile circulating streetcar line that runs through Downtown Detroit, the QLINE is the perfect way to explore the city.
  • The Detroit Riverfront Conservancy: The Detroit Riverfront Conservancy offers a variety of events and activities throughout the year, from concerts and festivals to kids’ programs and fitness classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Downtown

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Downtown Detroit, here’s our suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Detroit Institute of Arts.
Day 2 Take a tour of the Motown Museum.
Day 3 Spend the day at Belle Isle.
Day 4 Explore the Dequindre Cut Greenway.
Day 5 Go on a guided tour of the city.
Day 6 Visit the Michigan Science Center.
Day 7 Relax on the Detroit Riverfront.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Downtown Detroit has plenty to offer. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Detroit-Style Pizza: A unique and delicious pizza style that originated in Detroit, Detroit-style pizza is square with a thick, crispy crust and plenty of sauce and toppings.
  • Coney Dog: A Detroit classic, the Coney dog is a hot dog topped with chili, mustard, and onions, served on a steamed bun.
  • Paczki: A sweet, fried dough pastry that is traditionally eaten on Fat Tuesday, Paczki is a must-try dessert in Detroit.

Local Beverages

Detroit is also known for its craft beer and cocktails. Here are some local beverages worth trying:

  • Faygo Soda: A Detroit original, Faygo Soda comes in a variety of flavors and is a must-try for anyone visiting the city.
  • City of Gold: A delicious cocktail made with Detroit City Distillery Homegrown Rye whiskey, honey, lemon, and ginger beer.
  • Two-Hearted Ale: A popular beer from Michigan’s Bell’s Brewery, Two-Hearted Ale is an American-style India Pale Ale with a citrusy aroma and strong hop finish.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Downtown Detroit offers a wide range of options for every budget. Here are some popular choices:

  • The Westin Book Cadillac Detroit: This historic hotel has been beautifully restored and offers luxurious accommodations in the heart of Downtown Detroit.
  • The Hostel Detroit: A budget-friendly option for travelers, this hostel offers shared and private rooms in a historic building in the Corktown neighborhood of Detroit.
  • The Inn on Ferry Street: Located in a group of restored Victorian mansions, The Inn on Ferry Street offers guests a unique and charming experience in the heart of Midtown Detroit.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or unique products to bring home from Downtown Detroit, here are some suggestions:

  • Detroit Made: This retail store in the Eastern Market offers a variety of products made by local artisans and entrepreneurs, from clothing and accessories to home decor and art.
  • City Bird: This boutique in Midtown Detroit offers a variety of products made by local artists and designers, from jewelry and clothing to stationery and home goods.
  • Detroit Mercantile Co.: This store in Corktown offers a variety of products made in Detroit, from clothing and accessories to home decor and art.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Detroit is a city with a rich history and culture, and there are many traditions and customs that are important to the people who call the city home. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect for the Automobile Industry: Detroit is known as the “Motor City” and has a deep connection to the automobile industry. It’s important to show respect for this industry and its history when visiting the city.
  • The Spirit of Detroit: The Spirit of Detroit is a bronze monument located in Downtown Detroit that represents the city’s spirit of progress and innovation. It’s a symbol of pride for the people of Detroit, and visitors are encouraged to take a photo with it.
  • Detroit Hustles Harder: This motto represents the city’s work ethic and determination, and is often used to describe the entrepreneurial spirit of Detroit. It’s important to recognize and appreciate this spirit when visiting the city.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown Detroit is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. The fall is also a great time to visit, with beautiful foliage and seasonal events like the Detroit Fall Beer Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Do try the local food and drink.
  • Do explore the city’s history and culture.
  • Do take a guided tour of the city.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the city’s history or industries.
  • Don’t litter or vandalize public spaces.
  • Don’t wander alone in unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Downtown Detroit is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to take normal precautions to stay safe. Here are some tips:

  • Stick to well-lit, populated areas.
  • Always be aware of your surroundings.
  • Keep your valuables secure.

Health in Downtown

Downtown Detroit offers visitors a variety of healthcare options, including hospitals, urgent care centers, and clinics. It’s important to have proper health insurance coverage before visiting the city.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Downtown Detroit is easy, thanks to its central location and variety of transportation options. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Downtown

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport: Located about 20 miles west of Downtown Detroit, Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport is the largest airport in Michigan.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

  • Detroit Greyhound Bus Station: Located in the heart of Downtown Detroit, the Detroit Greyhound Bus Station offers connections to cities around the country.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station: Located in Downtown Detroit, the Amtrak Train Station offers connections to cities around Michigan and the Midwest.
  • Car Rentals: Several car rental companies operate in Downtown Detroit, offering visitors the freedom and flexibility to explore the city at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Downtown Detroit is a walkable city, but there are also several transportation options available to visitors. Here are some options:

  • The QLINE: A streetcar line that runs through Downtown Detroit, the QLINE is a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.
  • MoGo Bike Share: With more than 40 bike stations throughout the city, MoGo Bike Share is the perfect way to explore Downtown Detroit on two wheels.
  • Ride Sharing: Services like Uber and Lyft are available in Downtown Detroit, offering visitors a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Michigan, there are plenty of options within a short drive of Downtown Detroit. Here are some nearby destinations worth considering:

  • Ann Arbor: Located about 45 minutes west of Downtown Detroit, Ann Arbor is a charming college town known for its art and culture.
  • Dearborn: Located about 15 minutes west of Downtown Detroit, Dearborn is home to the Henry Ford Museum and Greenfield Village.
  • Lake St. Clair: Located about 20 miles north of Downtown Detroit, Lake St. Clair is a beautiful freshwater lake with plenty of outdoor activities and water sports.

Conclusion

Downtown Detroit is a vibrant and dynamic city with plenty to offer visitors. From its rich history and culture to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Downtown Detroit. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s museums and attractions or simply soak up the local culture and cuisine, Detroit is the perfect destination for your next adventure.
