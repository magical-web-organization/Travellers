Introduction to Detroit

Detroit is a city located in Wayne County, in Metro Detroit, in Southeast Michigan, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Known for its rich history in the automotive industry, Motown music, sports teams, and iconic landmarks, Detroit is a city that continues to reinvent itself and attract visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Detroit

Detroit has plenty of attractions to suit every interest and taste. Here is a list of must-visit places in Detroit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Detroit Institute of Arts: Known for its impressive collection of art from around the world, the Detroit Institute of Arts is a must-visit destination for art enthusiasts and history buffs.
  • The Henry Ford Museum: Celebrating Detroit’s rich history in the automotive industry, The Henry Ford Museum is home to the largest collection of American artifacts, ranging from automobiles, machinery, and other inventions.
  • Motown Museum: Known as the birthplace of Motown music, the Motown Museum showcases the history of this genre of music and the contributions made by legends like Diana Ross, Smokey Robinson, and Stevie Wonder.
  • Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History: This museum offers a glimpse into the African American experience in Detroit and beyond, featuring exhibitions, artifacts, and interactive experiences.

Natural Attractions of Detroit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belle Isle Park: A 982-acre island park located in the Detroit River, Belle Isle Park offers visitors various activities, including fishing, boating, nature trails, playgrounds, and more.
  • Detroit RiverWalk: A scenic walkway that offers breathtaking views of the Detroit River and the city skyline, the Detroit RiverWalk stretches for five and a half miles and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.
  • The Dequindre Cut Greenway: A former rail line turned into a pedestrian walkway, the Dequindre Cut Greenway offers a unique experience for those who enjoy walking, biking, or jogging.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Detroit

Detroit has several hiking and biking trails that offer visitors the chance to explore the city and its surroundings. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Detroit Riverfront trail: A scenic route that passes by many of Detroit’s iconic landmarks, including the Renaissance Center and the Ambassador Bridge.
  • The Iron Belle Trail: A 2,000-mile trail that offers hikers and bikers access to Michigan’s natural beauty, including parks, forests, and lakes.
  • The Dequindre Cut Greenway: A 1.35-mile pedestrian walkway that offers stunning views of the city’s urban landscape.

Best Things to Do in Detroit: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions mentioned above, Detroit offers visitors a range of activities and tours that are worth considering. Here are a few:

  • Detroit Sporting Events: Detroit is home to some of the most passionate sports fans in the country, with teams like the Detroit Lions, Detroit Tigers, and Detroit Red Wings playing in world-class stadiums.
  • Detroit River Tour: Experience Detroit from a different perspective by taking a boat tour of the Detroit River, which highlights the history and importance of the river to the city’s growth.
  • Eastern Market: A bustling open-air market that offers fresh produce, flowers, and locally-made products, Eastern Market is a must-visit destination for foodies and those looking for unique souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Detroit

A week in Detroit allows visitors to immerse themselves in the city’s culture and history. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Detroit that includes some of the best attractions and experiences:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Detroit Institute of Arts
2 Tour the Motown Museum and attend a concert or show at the Fox Theatre
3 Explore Belle Isle Park and take a sunset boat tour on the Detroit River
4 Visit The Henry Ford Museum, the Ford Rouge Factory Tour, and head out to Dearborn for a visit to the Arab American National Museum
5 Take a bike ride on the Dequindre Cut Greenway and stop for lunch at Eastern Market
6 Catch a Detroit Tigers or Detroit Lions game at one of Detroit’s world-class stadiums
7 Visit the Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History and enjoy dinner at one of Detroit’s top-rated restaurants, such as Selden Standard or Wright & Co

Food and Drink Scene of Detroit

Detroit is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse and multicultural culinary scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try when in Detroit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Detroit-style Pizza: A deep-dish pizza that has crispy edges, thick crust, and is topped with tomato sauce and brick cheese.
  • Coney Dogs: A hot dog served on a steamed bun topped with chili, onions, and mustard.
  • Paczki: A Polish pastry that is traditionally eaten on Fat Tuesday, Paczki is known for its sweet filling and is a Detroit staple.

Local Beverages

  • Vernors Ginger Ale: A ginger-flavored soda that has been a Detroit favorite since 1866.
  • Faygo Soda: A family-owned company that has been producing soda since 1907.
  • Detroit Beer Co.: A brewery that offers a variety of beers, including the Detroit Dwarf, an ale made with Michigan-grown hops and malts.

Where to Stay in Detroit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Detroit has plenty of options for accommodation, including budget-friendly hostels, luxury hotels, and historic guesthouses. Here are some top picks:

  • The Westin Book Cadillac Detroit: A luxurious hotel located in downtown Detroit, the Westin Book Cadillac Detroit offers excellent amenities and easy access to many of the city’s top attractions.
  • The Detroit Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in Corktown that offers shared and private rooms, the Detroit Hostel is perfect for travelers on a budget.
  • The Inn on Ferry Street: A historic guesthouse located in Midtown Detroit that offers cozy rooms, excellent service, and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Detroit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Detroit has a variety of locally made products that make great souvenirs. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Pewabic Pottery: A pottery studio that produces handcrafted pieces inspired by Detroit’s architecture and history.
  • Detroit Merchandise: Several retailers in Detroit offer a variety of logo and slogan merchandise celebrating Detroit’s grit and determination.
  • American Coney Island Sauce: Have a taste of Detroit by bringing home a bottle of American Coney Island’s famous chili sauce, perfect for topping hot dogs or fries.

Local Culture and Customs of Detroit

Detroit is a diverse and multicultural city that celebrates its history, music, and art. Some cultural customs and traditions to be aware of when visiting Detroit include:

  • Detroit Music: Detroit is known as the birthplace of Motown and is a hub for music festivals, including Movement Electronic Music Festival and the Detroit Jazz Festival.
  • Detroit Pistons Dance Team: The Detroit Pistons basketball team is known for its popular dance team, the Detroit Pistons Dancers, who perform at home games.

Best Time to Visit Detroit

The best time to visit Detroit is during the summer months when the weather is warm, and plenty of outdoor activities and festivals are happening. Spring and fall are also great times to visit when the crowds are smaller, and the weather is mild. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s also an excellent time for indoor activities and winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Detroit

When visiting Detroit, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do visit some of Detroit’s must-see attractions, such as the Motown Museum, The Henry Ford Museum, and Belle Isle Park.
  • Do try some of Detroit’s famous foods, such as coney dogs and Detroit-style pizza.
  • Do take a stroll on the Detroit RiverWalk and enjoy the scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t stray too far from downtown Detroit, as certain areas can be unsafe.
  • Don’t underestimate the importance of proper attire, especially during the winter months.
  • Don’t forget to tip restaurant servers and other service workers, as it is customary in the U.S.

Safety and Health

When visiting Detroit, it’s important to keep safety and health in mind. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Detroit

Detroit has a reputation for being a dangerous city, so it’s crucial to exercise caution, especially if you are unfamiliar with the area. Stick to well-lit and populated areas, avoid walking alone at night, and be mindful of your belongings. It’s also a good idea to travel with a companion or stick to group tours.

Health in Detroit

Detroit has several world-class medical facilities, including the Henry Ford Hospital and the Detroit Medical Center. Remember to pack any necessary medications and check with your doctor before traveling if you have any pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Detroit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Detroit is easily accessible through several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Detroit

  • Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport: Located in nearby Romulus, Michigan, the Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport is the largest airport in Michigan and serves several major airlines, including Delta Air Lines and Spirit Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Detroit

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Detroit, the Greyhound bus station serves several major cities across the Midwest and beyond.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The Amtrak station provides train service to and from several major cities, including Chicago and New York City.

How to Get Around in Detroit

Detroit offers several transportation options, including:

  • Detroit People Mover: A one-way monorail that takes visitors around downtown Detroit.
  • QLine: A streetcar system that connects several downtown Detroit districts.
  • Suburban Mobility Authority for Regional Transportation (SMART): A bus system that serves the Detroit metropolitan area, including suburbs and neighboring cities.
  • Bike share programs such as MoGo and Zagster.

Where to Go Next from Detroit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Detroit is located near several exciting destinations that are worth exploring. Some nearby destinations to consider include:

  • Ann Arbor, Michigan: A charming college town located just 45 minutes west of Detroit, known for its vibrant arts and music scene.
  • Dearborn, Michigan: A suburb of Detroit that is home to several noteworthy attractions, including The Henry Ford Museum and the Arab American National Museum.
  • Windsor, Ontario, Canada: Located just across the Detroit River, Windsor, Ontario, Canada, offers visitors a chance to experience a different culture and take advantage of Canada’s attractions.

Conclusion

Detroit is a city that has something to offer to every type of traveler, from history buffs to foodies, to adventure seekers. With a rich cultural history, exciting attractions, and a vibrant music scene, Detroit is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. Follow this comprehensive tour guide to Detroit, and you’ll be sure to have a fantastic time in the Motor City.
