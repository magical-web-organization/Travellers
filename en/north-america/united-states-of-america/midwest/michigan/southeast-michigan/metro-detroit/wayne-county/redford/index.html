Introduction to Redford

Located in Wayne County, in Metro Detroit, in Southeast Michigan, in the Midwest region, in the United States of America, in North America, Redford is a charming town that boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant cultural scene. With its unique blend of small-town hospitality and big-city amenities, Redford offers something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs to foodies and culture vultures.

List of Places to Visit in Redford

Here’s a list of the top attractions and must-visit places in Redford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Redford Historical Society Museum: Explore the rich history of Redford and learn about the people who shaped this town.
  • Old St. Mary’s Cultural Center: Visit this historic church turned cultural center and enjoy the myriad of cultural events and activities.
  • Detroit Institute of Arts: Just a short drive away, this world-renowned art museum features an extensive collection of American, European, African, and Asian artworks.

Natural Attractions of Redford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hines Park: With its scenic trails, picnic areas, and sports facilities, this park is a great place to spend a day outdoors.
  • Maybury State Park: This picturesque park is ideal for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, with miles of trails winding through lush forests and open meadows.
  • Rouge River Gateway Trail: This 7.6-mile trail offers stunning views of the Rouge River and its surrounding wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redford

Redford offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Rouge River Gateway Trail
  • Hines Park Trail
  • Maybury State Park Trail

Each trail offers something unique, from scenic river views to lush forests and open meadows, so be sure to explore them all.

Best Things to Do in Redford: Activities and Tours

  • Pub Crawl: Take a tour of Redford’s best breweries and taprooms and sample some of the finest local brews.
  • Detroit Zoo: Just a short drive away, this world-renowned zoo features a wide variety of animals from all over the world.
  • Ford Rouge Factory Tour: Visit the Ford Rouge Complex and take a tour of the famed manufacturing facility.

Week-Long Itinerary for Redford

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel.

Day 2: Visit the Redford Historical Society Museum and Old St. Mary’s Cultural Center.

Day 3: Spend the day at Hines Park, enjoying the scenic trails and picnic areas.

Day 4: Take a tour of the Detroit Institute of Arts and explore the cultural scene of Detroit.

Day 5: Visit Maybury State Park and go hiking, biking, or horseback riding on the park’s miles of trails.

Day 6: Take a pub crawl tour and sample some of the finest local brews.

Day 7: Departure from Redford.

Food and Drink Scene of Redford

Redford is known for its diverse food and drink scene, with a wide variety of restaurants, cafes, and bars. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Coney Dog: This iconic Detroit dish features a hot dog topped with chili, mustard, and onions, served on a steamed bun.
  • Detroit-Style Pizza: This deep-dish pizza features a crispy crust, tangy tomato sauce, and plenty of cheese and toppings.
  • Paczki: This Polish pastry is a local favorite, especially during the annual Paczki Day celebration.

Local Beverages

  • Faygo Soda: This Detroit-based soda company has been producing its signature flavors since 1907.
  • Vernors Ginger Ale: This gingery, fizzy soda has been a hit in Michigan since the 1860s.
  • Bell’s Brewery Beer: This Kalamazoo-based brewery produces some of the finest craft beers in the region.

Where to Stay in Redford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Redford offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Redford:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Detroit/Canton: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary hot breakfast.
  • Quality Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a heated indoor pool.
  • Redford Motel: This basic motel offers affordable rooms and easy access to downtown Redford.

What to Buy in Redford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Redford? Here are some of the best options:

  • Motor City T-shirts: Show off your Detroit pride with a T-shirt featuring the iconic Detroit skyline.
  • Sanders Candy: This Michigan-based confectionery company produces some of the finest chocolates and sweets in the region.
  • Detroit Tigers Gear: Visit a local sports shop and pick up some gear featuring the Detroit Tigers, one of the city’s beloved sports teams.

Local Culture and Customs of Redford

Redford is a diverse town with a rich cultural heritage. While there are no specific customs unique to Redford, visitors should be respectful of the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Redford

The best time to visit Redford is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall, from September to November, is also a great time to visit, with the trees turning vibrant shades of orange and red.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redford

Do:

  • Explore the local parks and trails.
  • Try the local cuisine, including Coney Dogs and Detroit-style pizza.
  • Visit the cultural attractions and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions, especially when exploring the natural areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redford

Redford is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as avoiding secluded areas at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Redford

Redford has several medical facilities and pharmacies, so visitors should have no trouble finding healthcare services if needed.

Getting to Redford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redford

The nearest airport to Redford is the Detroit Metropolitan Airport, located about 15 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Redford

Greyhound offers bus services to Detroit, located about 15 miles away.

Other Options

Redford is easily accessible by car, with several major highways passing through the town, including I-96 and M-39.

How to Get Around in Redford

Redford is best explored by car, but there are also several taxi and ride-sharing services available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Redford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Redford is located close to many other interesting destinations in Michigan, including:

  • Detroit: This vibrant city is a must-visit destination, with its rich history, cultural attractions, and lively nightlife.
  • Ann Arbor: Just a short drive away, this college town is known for its beautiful architecture, vibrant arts scene, and world-class restaurants.
  • Belle Isle State Park: Located in the heart of Detroit, this beautiful park features a beach, a zoo, and plenty of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Redford may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of attractions for travelers of all kinds. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant cultural scene, Redford is a truly unique destination that should be on everyone’s travel list.
