Introduction to Harbor Country

Harbor Country is located in the southwest corner of Michigan, bordering the shores of Lake Michigan. It includes eight charming towns, each with its unique character, attractions, and natural beauty. The area is known for its picturesque wineries, miles of sandy beaches, world-class golf courses, and art galleries. With its blend of small-town charm and upscale amenities, Harbor Country attracts visitors throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Harbor Country

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or locally-made products, Harbor Country has something to offer. Here’s a sample of must-visit places in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • New Buffalo Railroad Museum: This museum showcases the history of railroading in Harbor Country and features vintage trains and memorabilia.
  • Three Oaks Township Public Library: This historic library is housed in an architecturally significant building and features a vast collection of books and resources.
  • Harbert Swedish Bakery: This bakery, located in a historic building, has been serving traditional Swedish baked goods since 1912.

Natural Attractions of Harbor Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Warren Dunes State Park: This 1,950-acre park is home to towering sand dunes, hiking trails, and panoramic views of Lake Michigan.
  • Galien River County Park: This park includes miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and bird-watching areas.
  • Fernwood Botanical Garden and Nature Preserve: This 105-acre garden and preserve features native plants, woodland trails, and a butterfly house.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harbor Country

Harbor Country offers endless opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the area’s natural beauty. Here are some popular hiking and biking trails.

  • Kal-Haven Trail: This 33-mile trail connects Kalamazoo to South Haven and passes through quaint towns and scenic rural landscapes.
  • Harbor Country Trails: These trails include the Red Arrow Highway Trail, the La Porte County Mountain Bike Trail, and the Galien River Trail.
  • Warren Woods State Park: This 311-acre park offers an immersive forest experience, with hiking trails that connect to the Galien River County Park.

Best Things to Do in Harbor Country: Activities and Tours

From wine tasting to art appreciation, Harbor Country offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some unique experiences to consider.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Harbor Country is home to more than 20 wineries, and several local tour operators offer guided wine tours.
  • Art Galleries and Studios: Harbor Country is home to many local artists, and visiting art galleries and studios is a great way to discover their work.
  • Harbor Country Trail Rides: This equestrian center offers horseback riding tours through the scenic countryside of Harbor Country.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harbor Country

Planning a week-long trip to Harbor Country? Here’s a suggested itinerary that covers the areas’ highlights.

Day 1: Arrive in New Buffalo, relax at the beach, and visit the New Buffalo Railroad Museum.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour through Warren Dunes State Park, and enjoy a picnic on the beach.

Day 3: Visit the Fernwood Botanical Garden and Nature Preserve, and explore Galien River County Park.

Day 4: Take a wine-tasting tour of the local wineries, and enjoy the scenic drives that connect them.

Day 5: Visit local art galleries and studios, and take a class or workshop.

Day 6: Go horseback riding at Harbor Country Trail Rides, and enjoy a sunset dinner at a winery.

Day 7: Depart from New Buffalo or explore nearby destinations, such as South Bend or Chicago.

Food and Drink Scene of Harbor Country

Harbor Country’s food and drink scene reflects the area’s abundant natural resources and creative spirit. From farm-to-table restaurants to artisanal breweries, here’s a taste of what’s on offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blueberry Pancakes: Made with fresh blueberries that grow abundantly in the area, this classic breakfast dish is a must-try.
  • Cherry BBQ Pork: Local pork is slow-roasted with a cherry-infused sauce, creating a sweet and savory flavor that’s unique to the area.
  • Lake Michigan Whitefish: This mild-flavored fish is a local favorite and can be enjoyed pan-fried, grilled, or smoked.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Harbor Country is known for its wineries, which produce a wide range of varietals, from dry reds to fruit-infused whites.
  • Beer: Several local breweries offer small-batch beers that are made with local ingredients, such as hops and fruit.
  • Cider: Local cideries produce crisp, refreshing ciders that showcase the area’s abundant apple orchards.

Where to Stay in Harbor Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harbor Country offers a range of lodging options to suit every budget and taste. From fully-equipped vacation homes to cozy B&Bs, here are some recommendations.

  • The Harbor Grand: This upscale hotel in New Buffalo offers luxurious amenities, harbor views, and proximity to attractions.
  • Garden Grove Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B in Union Pier has a garden setting, homemade breakfast, and an outdoor hot tub.
  • Stay Apart: This vacation rental company offers modern, fully-equipped homes in several Harbor Country communities.

What to Buy in Harbor Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Harbor Country without taking home a piece of its unique character. Whether you’re looking for locally-made products or traditional souvenirs, here are some ideas.

  • Cherry Products: Look for cherry preserves, chocolate-covered cherries, and cherry wine, all made with the area’s abundant cherry crops.
  • Artisanal Crafts: Harbor Country is home to many skilled artisans, who create everything from pottery to woodworking pieces.
  • Beach Souvenirs: From seashells to sand dollars, collect some of the natural treasures of Lake Michigan’s beaches.

Local Culture and Customs of Harbor Country

Harbor Country is a welcoming and inclusive community, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some tips to help you blend in.

  • Respect Private Property: Much of Harbor Country is private property, so be sure to ask for permission before entering or taking pictures.
  • Dress Appropriately: Although Harbor Country is a casual destination, some restaurants and cultural attractions may have dress codes.
  • Tip Generously: Tipping is customary in the service industry in the US, and it’s an important way to show appreciation for good service.

Best Time to Visit Harbor Country

Harbor Country’s charm and beauty can be enjoyed year-round, but some seasons may be more suitable for certain activities. Here’s an overview of the best times to visit.

  • Summer: From June to August, Harbor Country’s beaches, wineries, and outdoor attractions are bustling with visitors.
  • Fall: September and October are perfect for enjoying the autumn colors, apple picking, and harvest festivals.
  • Winter: Although it can be cold and snowy, winter is a great time to enjoy the area’s cozy cafes, holiday markets, and winter sports.
  • Spring: From April to May, Harbor Country’s gardens, farms, and vineyards come to life, offering opportunities for outdoor exploration.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harbor Country

To make the most of your Harbor Country experience, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind.

Do:

  • Explore the Area: Harbor Country is full of hidden gems and treasures, so take the time to explore back roads and side streets.
  • Try Local Cuisine: From farm-to-table restaurants to roadside stands, Harbor Country offers a range of culinary experiences that reflect the area’s unique character.
  • Engage with Locals: Harbor Country residents are friendly and welcoming, so strike up a conversation and learn more about the area’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Harbor Country’s natural beauty is one of its most precious assets, so be sure to properly dispose of trash and waste.
  • Ignore Traffic Laws: Although Harbor Country’s roads may be less crowded than big cities, traffic laws and signals still apply, and ignoring them can be dangerous.
  • Trespass on Private Property: Harbor Country residents value their privacy, so be sure to respect posted signs and ask for permission before entering private property.

Safety and Health

Harbor Country is generally a safe and healthy place to visit, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their well-being.

Safety in Harbor Country

  • Water Safety: Lake Michigan can be unpredictable and dangerous, so be sure to follow safety guidelines when swimming, boating, or kayaking.
  • Hiking and Biking Safety: Harbor Country’s trails can be steep or slippery, so wear proper footwear and be sure to carry a map and water.
  • Weather Safety: Harbor Country can experience severe weather, including thunderstorms, tornadoes, and snowstorms, so stay informed about changing weather conditions.

Health in Harbor Country

  • Sun Safety: Harbor Country’s beaches are beautiful but can be intense, so be sure to wear sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, and seek shade when possible.
  • Pollen and Allergens: Like many rural areas, Harbor Country has natural allergens, such as ragweed and pollen. Be sure to pack any allergy medications and ask for emergency services if needed.
  • COVID-19 Safety: Harbor Country is taking precautions to protect visitors and residents against COVID-19. Be sure to follow health guidelines regarding social distancing, face masks, and hand hygiene.

How to Get Around in Harbor Country

Harbor Country is a pedestrian- and bike-friendly area, and many attractions are within walking or biking distance. Public transportation is limited, so having a car or using ride-sharing services is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Harbor Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Harbor Country is an exciting destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or just a weekend getaway, Harbor Country’s blend of natural beauty, creativity, and hospitality will leave a lasting impression.
