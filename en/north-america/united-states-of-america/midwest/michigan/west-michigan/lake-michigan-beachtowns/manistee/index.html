Introduction to Manistee

Manistee, located in Lake Michigan Beachtowns, is a picturesque city in the northern part of Michigan’s lower peninsula. With a population of around 6,000, the city sits on the shores of Lake Michigan and offers visitors a perfect blend of stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. There are many exciting things to see and do here, from exploring museums and historical sites to hiking or biking through dense forests and scenic trails, and indulging in delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Manistee

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Manistee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manistee County Historical Museum - A fascinating museum that showcases the rich history and culture of the Manistee region.
  • Ramsdell Regional Center for the Arts - A historic theatre that hosts concerts, plays, and other cultural events throughout the year.
  • First Street Beach and Pier - A popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and sunset watching.
  • Manistee North Pierhead Lighthouse - A historic lighthouse on the Lake Michigan shoreline, which offers great views of the surrounding area.
  • Douglas Valley Vineyard and Winery - A lovely vineyard that produces some of Michigan’s best wines.

Natural Attractions of Manistee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manistee National Forest - A huge national forest that offers excellent opportunities for hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting.
  • Manistee River Trail - A popular hiking trail that follows the Manistee River, and offers stunning views of the river valley.
  • Lake Michigan Beaches - Some of Michigan’s finest beaches, perfect for swimming, sunbathing or enjoying water sports.
  • Pine River - A scenic river that’s ideal for kayaking and canoeing.
  • Arcadia Bluffs Golf Course - A world-class golf course on the shores of Lake Michigan, with breathtaking views of the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manistee

Manistee County is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with countless trails and routes for all skill levels. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking trails:

  • North Country Trail - A long-distance trail that stretches over 4,600 miles across seven states, including Michigan.
  • Manistee National Forest Trail System - A series of interconnected trails that offer a wide range of hiking and biking options.
  • Big M Trail - A popular mountain biking trail that covers over 30 miles of forest and hills.
  • Manistee River Loop Trail - This popular hiking trail follows the Manistee River and offers fantastic opportunities for camping and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Manistee: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities and tours in Manistee. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Riverwalk and Downtown Walking Tour - A guided walking tour of Manistee’s historic downtown area.
  • Fishing Charter - Take a fishing charter and head out onto Lake Michigan and try your luck catching salmon and trout.
  • Kayak or Canoe Rentals - Rent a kayak or a canoe and paddle down the Manistee River for a relaxing day on the water.
  • Speed Boat Tour of Lake Michigan - A thrilling boat tour that takes you to the most scenic spots on Lake Michigan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manistee

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most out of your visit to Manistee:

Day 1: Explore Downtown Manistee and visit the Manistee North Pierhead Lighthouse.

Day 2: Hike the Manistee River Trail and the Big M Trail.

Day 3: Take a fishing charter on Lake Michigan.

Day 4: Visit the Manistee County Historical Museum and the Ramsdell Regional Center for the Arts.

Day 5: Relax on the beach at First Street Beach and go for a swim.

Day 6: Go kayaking or canoeing down the Pine River.

Day 7: Play a round of golf at Arcadia Bluffs Golf Course.

Food and Drink Scene of Manistee

Manistee has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects the region’s rich agricultural heritage and love of craft beer and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties - A traditional Cornish pastry filled with meat, vegetables, and potatoes.
  • Lake Perch - Delicious fish that can be found at local restaurants and the farmer’s market.
  • Cherry Pie - A quintessential Michigan dish, made with sweet, tart cherries.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Manistee is home to several excellent craft breweries, including Ramsdell Brewing and North Channel Brewing Company.
  • Wine - Douglas Valley Vineyard and Winery produces some of the region’s best wines.

  • Little Riverside Motel and Marina
  • Manistee Hotel

What to Buy in Manistee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a souvenir or a gift to take home, here are some of the top products and souvenirs from Manistee:

  • Cherry Products - Manistee is known for its cherries, and you can find a wide range of cherry jams, jellies, and other products at the farmer’s market.
  • Handcrafted Goods - The region has a vibrant community of artisans who produce everything from pottery to jewelry.
  • Craft Beer and Wine - Take home a few bottles of craft beer or wine from a local brewery or winery.

Local Culture and Customs of Manistee

Manistee has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Native American, European, and African American influences. The residents of Manistee are welcoming and friendly, and the region’s festivals and events celebrate the area’s unique history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Manistee

The best time to visit Manistee is between May and October when the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are at their best. The fall season is particularly beautiful, with the changing colors of the leaves creating a stunning backdrop for outdoor adventures and scenic drives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manistee

Do:

  • Do respect the local community and its customs.
  • Do explore the local museums and learn about the history of the region.
  • Do be mindful of your surroundings if you’re hiking or biking in the forest.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of Lake Michigan’s waves and currents.
  • Don’t ignore the warnings of park rangers or forest service officials.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manistee

Manistee is generally a safe place to visit. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling, such as locking your car and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night. Always be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking in the forest or swimming in Lake Michigan.

Health in Manistee

Since Manistee is located in a rural area, it may be difficult to access medical facilities in case of an emergency. Before you travel, make sure you have adequate health insurance coverage and bring any necessary medications with you.

Getting to Manistee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manistee is well connected to other parts of Michigan and the neighboring states. Here are some transportation options to get to Manistee:

Airports Near Manistee

  • Manistee County Blacker Airport - The closest airport to Manistee, located just a few miles southeast of the city.

Bus Stations Near Manistee

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Traverse City, about 50 miles east of Manistee.

Other Options

  • Car - Manistee is easily accessible by car, with major highways passing through the city.
  • Train - Amtrak has a station in nearby Holland, Michigan, which is about 60 miles from Manistee.

How to Get Around in Manistee

While walking is a great way to explore downtown Manistee, a car is necessary for most other activities. Rental cars are available at the airport, and there are also several taxi and shuttle services in the area.

Where to Go Next from Manistee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Michigan or beyond, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore - A beautiful national park with towering sand dunes and breathtaking views.
  • Traverse City - A charming town on the southern end of Lake Michigan, known for its cherry orchards and wineries.
  • Mackinac Island - A popular resort island located between the Upper and Lower Peninsulas of Michigan, known for its historic downtown and stunning natural beauty.

Conclusion

Manistee is a hidden gem in Michigan that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, kayaking, or just relaxing on the beach, there is something for everyone in Manistee. Plan your trip today and discover all that this beautiful city has to offer!
