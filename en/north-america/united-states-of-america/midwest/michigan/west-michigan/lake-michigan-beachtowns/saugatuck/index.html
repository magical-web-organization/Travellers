Saugatuck Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Saugatuck

Welcome to Saugatuck, one of the charming towns of Lake Michigan Beachtowns, in West Michigan’s Midwest region - you’re in for a treat. Known for its stunning beaches, rich history, and vibrant art scene, Saugatuck attracts visitors from far and wide. With plenty of things to see and do, you won’t run out of options. In this travel guide, we’ll delve deeper into some of the most popular attractions and activities around Saugatuck. From the best places to visit and activities to do, to accommodations, transportations, and local culture, this comprehensive guide has everything you need to know before planning your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Saugatuck

Saugatuck is home to several must-visit destinations that cater to all interests. Here are some of the top places that you should consider when visiting Saugatuck.

Culturally Important Attractions - Museums and Historical Sites

  • Saugatuck-Douglas Historical Society: A must-visit for history buffs, the Saugatuck-Douglas Historical Society showcases the area’s unique history and culture.
  • Amazwi Contemporary Art Gallery: Featuring a mix of modern and traditional art, this gallery showcases some of the best contemporary African art from various artists.
  • Butler Street and Water Street Gallery: Considered Saugatuck’s art center, these two galleries feature an extensive collection of contemporary and traditional art from various artists.

Natural Attractions of Saugatuck - Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oval Beach: Rated one of the best beaches in the United States, Oval Beach is located on the shores of Lake Michigan and has plenty of amenities that will make your visit worth it.
  • Mount Baldhead Park: A popular spot for hiking and scenic views, Mount Baldhead Park offers panoramic views of Saugatuck and Lake Michigan.
  • Saugatuck Dunes State Park: A massive state park, Saugatuck Dunes State Park, boasts over 1,000 acres of land featuring beautiful trails and stunning beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saugatuck

If you’re a hiking or biking enthusiast, then you’ll love Saugatuck’s trail system. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced hiker, there’s always a trail for everyone. Check out some of the top hiking and biking trails listed below:

  • Saugatuck Harbor Natural Area: A great spot for nature lovers, the Saugatuck Harbor Natural Area offers stunning views of the lake and a mix of wetlands, forests, and dunes.
  • Kal-Haven Trail State Park: This 34-mile trail goes through beautiful farmland and wooded areas, perfect for biking or hiking.
  • Mount Baldhead Park: Experience a unique hiking trail that starts at the bottom of the dune and goes all the way to the top, providing panoramic views of the town and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Saugatuck - Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Saugatuck, there are plenty of options to choose from. Whether you’re traveling with your family, friends, or alone, you’ll find something that interests you. Below are some of the top things to do in Saugatuck:

  • Boat Tours: See Saugatuck’s beauty on multiple coast guards certified and regularly inspected vessels. Choose from Saugatuck River cruises, boat charters, sunset cruises, or even visit the neighboring town of Douglas by water taxi.
  • Art Galleries and Studio Tours: Saugatuck is a haven for artists, so don’t forget to explore the local galleries and studios. Participate in exhibitions, art walks, or just spend the day visiting the various galleries in town.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn about the local cuisine and how they are prepared. With top-rated chefs, take a cooking class and become the master of cooking these dishes. Or enjoy a hands-on cooking experience at any of the local restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saugatuck

Seven days in Saugatuck is more than enough to explore the town and its surroundings. Here’s an itinerary guide for your week-long stay in Saugatuck:

Day 1 & 2: Arrival, Beach Day, and Relaxation.

Day 3: Morning Hike in Mount Baldhead Park, afternoon visit the Amazwi Contemporary Art Gallery and delicious dinner at Southerner.

Day 4: Morning coffee at Uncommon Coffee Roasters, just visit the area around Butler Street or Water Street Gallery, and then embarks on a boat tour.

Day 5: Visit Saugatuck Dunes State Park for hiking and sightseeing. Continue your day out with a tour of some of the local wineries or breweries.

Day 6: Rent a bike and go for an afternoon ride on the Kal-Haven Trail State Park. End your evening with a cooking class or dinner at any of the local restaurants in town.

Day 7: Last day to enjoy the beach, visit the Saugatuck-Douglas Historical Society and complete your day with a visit to GROW, a 100% organic and sustainable café and juice bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Saugatuck

Saugatuck has a rich and diverse food culture. From local specialties to international cuisines, the food scene in Saugatuck has something that will appeal to everyone’s taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local drinks to savor in Saugatuck:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southerner Fried Chicken: Enjoy the crispy, tender fried chicken and biscuits that Southerner Fried Chicken serves with seasonally available farm-fresh sides.
  • Farmer’s Omelet: Start your day off with the Farmer’s Omelet, a classic omelet stuffed with fresh veggies and locally-sourced eggs.
  • Fresh Catch: With plenty of water bodies nearby, Saugatuck provides ample opportunities for seafood lovers. Try out any of the catches of the day at Coral Gables, a classic restaurant with outstanding seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wines: Visit the Fenn Valley Vineyards and experience the different types of wine they produce. Their tasting room has a relaxed atmosphere for a leisurely wine-tasting experience.
  • Local Breweries: Saugatuck Brewing Company, Guardian Brewing Company, and Mitten Brewing Company are some of the breweries you must visit. Try out their unique and creative beer flavors made from locally sourced and fresh ingredients.

Where to Stay in Saugatuck - Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saugatuck offers plenty of choices for accommodation. From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels, Saugatuck’s got it all. Here are some of the top places to stay in Saugatuck:

  • The Hotel Saugatuck: A picturesque 18-room luxury bed & breakfast overlooking the waterfront.
  • Maplewood Hotel: A charming bed and breakfast located on a quiet street near Saugatuck.
  • Starlite Resort: A family-friendly resort located just five minutes away from Oval beach.

What to Buy in Saugatuck - Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping is a must-do activity when visiting Saugatuck. With its vibrant culture and history, Saugatuck offers a unique shopping experience. From art pieces to locally sourced food items, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Below are some of the top places to shop in Saugatuck:

  • Simply Saugatuck: Visit Simply Saugatuck for some unique, hand-made clothing, jewelry, and art pieces created by local artists.
  • Saugatuck Antique Pavilion and Gift Shop: Offering a wide range of antique items such as furniture, décor, and more, this pavilion is the stop for those looking for unique finds that tell a story.

Local Culture and Customs of Saugatuck

Saugatuck is a vibrant, artsy, and welcoming town. It’s known for its vibrant art culture and beautiful beach scenes. Saugatuck has a relaxed atmosphere, and it’s a place where people of all backgrounds and cultures can come together and celebrate diversity. It’s common to see residents of Saugatuck engaging in conversations with visitors as they welcome them to their beloved town.

Best Time to Visit Saugatuck

Saugatuck’s peak tourist season is from Memorial Day to Labor Day, but the town is charming throughout the year. Spring and fall provide cooler temperatures and smaller crowds, making it an excellent time to visit. For warmer weather and a range of outdoor activities, summer is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saugatuck

Do:

  • Take a boat tour and explore the beauty of Saugatuck from a different perspective.
  • Enjoy a cup of coffee from the Uncommon Coffee Roasters and sample their locally-sourced fresh brews.
  • Rent a bike and explore the local trails and parks.
  • Visit Saugatuck’s famous attractions such as Oval Beach, the historical society, and the local art galleries.
  • Try out the local delicacies such as Farmer’s Omelet and Southerner Fried Chicken.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in any other public area.
  • Be disrespectful or insensitive to the local culture or customs.
  • Go beyond parking limits or break any traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saugatuck

Saugatuck is generally a safe town, but it’s always wise to exercise caution when traveling anywhere. The Saugatuck police department maintains a strong presence to ensure visitors’ safety, but it’s essential to take precautions and stay alert when out and about.

Health in Saugatuck

Saugatuck has a decent healthcare system and access to immediate medical assistance. There are pharmacies and clinics available in case of emergencies, and the Spectrum Health Services offer comprehensive medical services to residents and visitors.

Getting to Saugatuck - Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saugatuck

The closest airport to Saugatuck is Gerald R. Ford International Airport, which is located in nearby Grand Rapids, MI. The airport is a 45-minute drive from Saugatuck and has plenty of car rental options for visitors.

Bus Stations Near Saugatuck

Greyhound is the closest bus station to Saugatuck, located in nearby Holland, Michigan, just about a 10-minute drive from the town.

Other Options

The Amtrak Train Station is an alternative transportation option for visitors and is located approximately 20 minutes from Saugatuck in nearby Holland, Michigan. Amtrak offers several train lines to various destinations across the country.

How to Get Around in Saugatuck

Saugatuck is a small town with plenty of walkways, bike trails, and sidewalks that make walking and cycling convenient. Car rentals and taxis are also available for visitors who need to get around in comfort.

Where to Go Next from Saugatuck - Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Holland: Just a 20-minute drive from Saugatuck, Holland boasts beautiful beaches, excellent shopping, and a rich Dutch culture.
  • Grand Rapids: A 45-minute drive from Saugatuck, Grand Rapids offers a vibrant downtown food scene, cultural activities, and art festivals.
  • South Haven: Enjoy stunning sunsets, sand dunes, and great food in this town located approximately 22 miles north of Saugatuck.

Conclusion

Saugatuck is a quaint town with beautiful attractions and a peaceful atmosphere, making it a perfect getaway spot for anyone looking to escape the hustling cities and get in touch with nature. From beaches, hiking trails, local culture and cuisine, the town has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope to have given you an insight into the majestic town of Saugatuck, and we hope you have a fantastic time during your stay!
