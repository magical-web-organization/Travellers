Introduction to Holland

Located in the Lake Michigan Beachtowns in West Michigan, Holland is a charming city with a rich Dutch heritage, picturesque scenery, and plenty of outdoor recreation opportunities. Visitors will be awed by the city’s charming downtown, beautiful beaches, and well-preserved historic landmarks. With so much to see and do, Holland is the perfect destination for a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a solo adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Holland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Holland Museum: Explore the history of Holland and the Dutch culture through various exhibitions, including displays on the city’s Dutch roots, Michigan’s prehistoric past, and the life of early Dutch settlers.
  • Windmill Island Gardens: Admire the 252-year-old De Zwaan Windmill, explore the gardens, and learn about Dutch history and culture at the visitor center.
  • Cappon House Museum: Take a step back in time and explore what life was like for Holland’s early settlers at this beautifully restored house museum.
  • The Ticket Office: Learn about the history of rail travel in West Michigan and step on board the Pere Marquette 1223, a steam engine that was in service from 1941-1951.

Natural Attractions of Holland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Holland State Park: This beautiful park offers something for everyone, from hiking trails and picnic areas to beaches and a lighthouse.
  • Mt. Pisgah: Hike to the top of this 157-foot sand dune for breathtaking views of Lake Michigan and the surrounding area.
  • Tunnel Park: A family-friendly park with a playground, picnic areas, and a tunnel through the dune leading to Lake Michigan.
  • Saugatuck Dunes State Park: Take a scenic hike through the dunes or relax on the beach at this stunning natural area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Holland

  • Lakeshore Connector Path: This 20-mile trail connects the cities of Holland and Grand Haven and offers stunning views of Lake Michigan.
  • Lakeside Trail: A 6-mile trail that winds through parks, beaches, and historic sites in Holland.
  • Lighthouse Bike Trail: A 20-mile route that takes cyclists past several historic lighthouses and offers incredible views of Lake Michigan.

Best Things to Do in Holland: Activities and Tours

  • Tulip Time Festival: Held every May, this festival celebrates Holland’s Dutch heritage and features parades, concerts, traditional Dutch dance, and millions of tulips in bloom.
  • Holland Farmers Market: Visit the market on Wednesdays and Saturdays to taste local produce, enjoy live music, and shop local products.
  • Boat Tours: Take a guided tour of Lake Michigan, explore local waterways, or charter a fishing boat for a day on the water.
  • Downtown Holland: Stroll through the charming downtown area, visit local shops and restaurants, and enjoy the beautiful architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Holland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dutch Letters: A flaky pastry filled with almond paste and shaped like the letter “S”.
  • Stroopwafel: Two thin waffles with a layer of caramel syrup in the middle.
  • Oliebollen: A traditional Dutch donut made with raisins and dusted with powdered sugar.
  • Boerenkool: Dutch-style mashed potatoes with kale and sausage.

Local Beverages

  • Dutch Beer: Try a local brew from one of Holland’s breweries, such as New Holland Brewing Company or Big Lake Brewing.
  • Wine Tastings: Visit Fenn Valley Winery for wine tastings and tours of the vineyard.
  • Coffee Shops: Check out Lemonjello’s Coffee for a delicious cup of coffee or espresso.

Where to Stay in Holland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • CityFlatsHotel: A modern, eco-friendly hotel in the heart of downtown Holland.
  • Fairfield Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly hotel located near Holland State Park.
  • Centennial Inn: A charming bed and breakfast located in a restored 1874 mansion.

What to Buy in Holland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tulips and Bulbs: Visit Veldheer Tulip Gardens to purchase tulip bulbs, plants, and other flower-related gifts.
  • Dutch Keepsakes: Browse the shops in Downtown Holland for traditional Dutch souvenirs like wooden shoes, Delft pottery, and Dutch-style lace.
  • Local Products: Check out the Holland Farmers Market and local specialty shops for locally made food products, like Michigan apples, artisan cheeses, and organic honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Holland

Holland has a rich Dutch heritage, which is evident in the city’s architecture, food, and traditions. Visitors can experience Dutch culture through events like the Tulip Time Festival, dining at Dutch-style restaurants, and exploring historic sites and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Holland

The best time to visit Holland is in the spring when the tulips are in bloom and the city celebrates the Tulip Time Festival. Summer is also a great time to visit for outdoor activities and warm weather. The fall offers beautiful autumn foliage and fewer crowds. Winter can be cold and snowy, but the city celebrates with festive holiday events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Holland

Do:

  • Take part in the Tulip Time Festival.
  • Explore Dutch culture and history at Windmill Island Gardens and the Holland Museum.
  • Go hiking or biking on one of Holland’s scenic trails.
  • Try Dutch cuisine and local beer.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty of Holland’s beaches and parks.
  • Forget to respect Dutch customs and culture.
  • Miss out on local farmers’ markets and artisan shops.
  • Overlook the opportunity to rent a boat or take a boat tour.

Safety and Health

Safety in Holland

Holland is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Lock car doors, keep valuables out of sight, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Holland

Holland has excellent healthcare facilities and services. Visitors should have travel insurance and carry any necessary medical documents or prescriptions.

Getting to Holland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Holland

The nearest airports to Holland are the Gerald R. Ford International Airport in Grand Rapids (30 miles) and the Muskegon County Airport (40 miles).

Bus Stations Near Holland

The Holland Greyhound Bus Station is located downtown and is serviced by several bus lines.

Other Options

Holland is easily accessible by car, boat, or bike, making it a convenient stop on a road trip or cycling tour.

How to Get Around in Holland

Holland is a very walkable city, and downtown is easily accessible by foot. Biking is also a popular way to explore the city, and there are many bike trails and rental options available. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Holland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Haven: A charming lakeshore community with a boardwalk, lighthouse, and beautiful beaches.
  • Saugatuck: A historic town with picturesque streets, unique shops and restaurants, and a thriving arts scene.
  • Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore: A National Park on the northeast shore of Lake Michigan with pristine beaches, towering sand dunes, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Holland is a charming city with a rich Dutch heritage, beautiful natural scenery, and plenty of outdoor recreation opportunities. Whether you’re here for the Tulip Time Festival or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Holland has something for everyone. With this guide in hand, you’ll be ready to plan the perfect trip to this idyllic Midwest city.
