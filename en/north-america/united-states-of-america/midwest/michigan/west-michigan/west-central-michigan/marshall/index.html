Introduction to Marshall

Marshall is a charming city located in West Central Michigan, known for its historic architecture, vibrant culture, and picturesque natural scenery. With a population of around 7,000 residents, the city is small enough to explore by foot, yet big enough to offer a wide range of attractions and activities for travelers of all ages.

In this travel guide to Marshall, we will take you on a journey through the city’s must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay, and what to eat. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, foodie, or nature lover, there’s something for everyone in Marshall.

List of Places to Visit in Marshall

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marshall is home to an impressive collection of museums and historical landmarks that take you on a journey through time.

Honolulu House Museum

The Honolulu House Museum is a historic 19th-century mansion that showcases the city’s rich cultural heritage. The mansion features unique architecture and serves as a cultural center for the community.

American Museum of Magic

The American Museum of Magic is a must-visit attraction for magic lovers. It houses over 10,000 artifacts related to the history of magic, including posters, props, and costumes.

National Museum of the United States Air Force

The National Museum of the United States Air Force is a world-class museum that preserves the history of aviation, featuring over 300 aircraft and missiles.

Marshall Historical Society Museum

The Marshall Historical Society Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Marshall and the surrounding area, exhibiting artifacts ranging from Native American artifacts to 20th century tools and agricultural equipment.

Natural Attractions of Marshall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Marshall is surrounded by natural beauty, offering visitors a range of outdoor adventures and scenic vistas to explore.

Cornwell’s Turkeyville USA

Cornwell’s Turkeyville USA is a family-friendly attraction that features a restaurant, gift shop, and petting zoo, all surrounded by beautiful farmland.

Turkeyville Trail

The Turkeyville Trail is a 10-mile multi-use trail that starts in Marshall and winds through Turkeyville, connecting to other regional trails. It’s ideal for hiking, biking, or horseback riding.

Brooks Nature Area

Brooks Nature Area is a natural oasis that boasts over 140 acres of woodlands, wetlands, and meadows. It’s ideal for hiking, birdwatching, and enjoying the great outdoors.

Kalamazoo River

The Kalamazoo River is a popular spot for fishing and kayaking, with plenty of scenic spots for picnics and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marshall

Marshall is home to a network of hiking and biking trails that take you through the city’s beautiful natural surroundings. The Turkeyville Trail, as mentioned above, is a popular route for hiking, biking, or horseback riding.

The North Country Trail is another excellent hiking trail that stretches 4,600 miles from New York to North Dakota, and passes through Marshall, offering a unique opportunity to explore the city’s natural beauty on foot.

Best Things to Do in Marshall: Activities and Tours

In addition to the museums and natural attractions, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Marshall.

Dark Horse Brewing Company

Dark Horse Brewing Company is a local brewery that offers tours and tastings of their unique craft beers, made on-site.

Stagecoach Inn

The Stagecoach Inn is a historic landmark that dates back to 1835, and currently serves as a cozy bed and breakfast that boasts a rich history and charming atmosphere.

Marshall Riverwalk

The Marshall Riverwalk is a scenic walking path that runs alongside the Kalamazoo River. It’s ideal for strolling, jogging, or biking, offering stunning views of the river and surrounding landscape.

Turkeyville USA

As mentioned above, Cornwell’s Turkeyville USA is a family-friendly attraction that features a restaurant, gift shop, and petting zoo, all surrounded by beautiful farmland.

Food and Drink Scene of Marshall

Must-Try Dishes

Marshall is known for its classic American cuisine, featuring fresh local ingredients and a focus on comfort and flavor.

Corned Beef Hash

Corned beef hash is a signature dish in Marshall, with many local diners and restaurants offering their take on this classic breakfast favorite.

Pasties

Pasties are a traditional Michigan dish that originated in the Upper Peninsula, similar to a savory hand pie. Many local bakeries and cafes in Marshall offer their own version of pasties, featuring a variety of fillings like beef, vegetables, and cheese.

Blueberry Pancakes

Blueberry pancakes are a popular breakfast item in Marshall, featuring locally-sourced blueberries that add a sweet and juicy burst of flavor to the fluffy pancakes.

Local Beverages

Craft beer and cider are popular in Michigan, and Marshall features several local breweries and taprooms that offer a wide range of unique and delicious brews.

Dark Horse Brewing Company

Dark Horse Brewing Company is a popular local brewery that offers a variety of signature craft beers, including their famous Raspberry Ale.

Grand River Brewery

Grand River Brewery is a local craft brewery that features a taproom and serves a range of beer and pub fare, including their classic Red Eye Rye and Monkey Mouth IPA.

Where to Stay in Marshall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marshall offers a variety of options for accommodation, ranging from cozy bed and breakfasts to modern hotels and motels.

Hampton Inn Marshall

Hampton Inn Marshall is a popular choice for travelers looking for modern amenities and comfort. The hotel features a range of amenities, including a fitness center, indoor pool, and free breakfast.

The National House Inn

The National House Inn is a historic bed and breakfast that dates back to 1835, and features 16 unique and charming rooms, each with its own personality and style.

Stagecoach Inn

As mentioned above, the Stagecoach Inn is a historic landmark that currently serves as a cozy bed and breakfast, featuring classic décor and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Marshall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Na-Na’s Bakery Treats

Local Culture and Customs of Marshall

Marshall is steeped in history and culture, with a strong sense of community and pride in their heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, including the preservation of the historic landmarks and architecture.

Best Time to Visit Marshall

The best time to visit Marshall is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events happening around the city.
