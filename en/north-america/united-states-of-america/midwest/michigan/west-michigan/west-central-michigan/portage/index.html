Introduction to Portage

Nestled in West Central Michigan, Portage is a quaint city in Kalamazoo County. With its rich history and natural beauty, Portage has become a popular tourist destination in the Midwest region of the United States. Visitors to this scenic town can enjoy a diverse range of attractions, from hiking and biking trails to museums and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Portage

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Air Zoo: This unique museum features more than 60 rare and historic aircraft, including some of the world’s most significant military planes. In addition to its impressive collection of planes, the Air Zoo also boasts interactive exhibits and hands-on activities for kids.

Kalamazoo Institute of Arts: Established in 1924, the Kalamazoo Institute of Arts is one of Michigan’s oldest art museums. The museum features over 4,700 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs. With changing exhibits and programs, there’s always something new to see here.

Kalamazoo Valley Museum: This fascinating museum showcases the history of southwestern Michigan through interactive exhibits and engaging displays. From dinosaurs to early settlers, visitors can explore the area’s past in a fun and informative way.

Natural Attractions of Portage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Portage Creek Bicentennial Park: Located along Portage Creek, this 160-acre park offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area. With lush forests, rolling hills, and picturesque streams, the park is a favorite spot for nature enthusiasts and families alike.

Gourdneck State Game Area: Covering over 3,000 acres, Gourdneck State Game Area is a haven for hunters, anglers, and outdoor enthusiasts. With its diverse range of habitats, from forests and wetlands to grasslands, the area boasts a variety of wildlife, including deer, turkeys, and waterfowl.

Kalamazoo River Valley Trail: This scenic trail runs from Kalamazoo to Saugatuck and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. As you explore the trail, you’ll pass through historic towns, wind through forests and fields, and cross over picturesque bridges.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portage

Portage Creek Bicentennial Park: With over six miles of hiking trails and nearly three miles of bike trails, Portage Creek Bicentennial Park is a favorite spot for outdoor enthusiasts. Trail maps are available at the park’s entrance, and guided nature hikes are offered throughout the year.

Kalamazoo River Valley Trail: As one of the longest non-motorized trails in Michigan, the Kalamazoo River Valley Trail is perfect for hiking and biking. The trail features more than 28 miles of paved pathways and crosses through several parks and natural areas.

Best Things to Do in Portage: Activities and Tours

Air Adventures of Kalamazoo: Experience the thrill of flying high above Portage with Air Adventures of Kalamazoo. Offering both helicopter and airplane tours, this company provides breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Nature Connection: This outdoor education center offers a variety of fun and educational programs, including nature hikes, bird watching, and animal tracking. With knowledgeable guides and hands-on activities, Nature Connection is a great choice for families and nature lovers.

Food and Drink Scene of Portage

Must-Try Dishes

Barbecue: Portage is known for its flavorful barbecue, with popular spots including Hogzilla BBQ and Hog Wild BBQ.

Michigan Cherry Pie: Made with tart cherries that are grown locally, this classic Michigan dessert is a must-try when visiting Portage.

Local Beverages

Bell’s Brewery: This award-winning brewery has been a favorite of beer lovers since it opened in 1985. With a range of styles and flavors, there’s something for everyone at Bell’s.

St. Julian Winery: Founded in 1921, St. Julian Winery is Michigan’s oldest and largest winery. Visitors can take a tour of the facility and sample some of the winery’s delicious offerings.

Where to Stay in Portage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Homewood Suites by Hilton Kalamazoo-Portage: This all-suite hotel offers spacious rooms, free breakfast, and easy access to nearby attractions.

Courtyard Kalamazoo Portage: With its modern amenities and convenient location, the Courtyard Kalamazoo Portage is a great choice for business travelers and families alike.

What to Buy in Portage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kalamazoo Candle Company: This locally owned shop sells handmade soy candles in a variety of scents. With options like Fresh Linen and Lake Michigan Breeze, these candles make for a great gift or souvenir.

Wild Birds Unlimited: At this nature store, visitors can find a range of birdseed, birdhouses, and other birding supplies. It’s the perfect place to pick up a souvenir for bird watchers.

Local Culture and Customs of Portage

Portage is known for its friendly locals and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors are encouraged to explore the area’s rich history and enjoy its natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Portage

The best time to visit Portage is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and the area’s parks and natural areas are teeming with wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portage

Do:

  • Do try the barbecue - it’s some of the best in the Midwest.
  • Do take a guided tour of the Air Zoo for a unique and educational experience.
  • Do explore the area’s natural attractions, including parks and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the chance to try Michigan cherry pie.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for all the outdoor activities.
  • Don’t leave without taking a scenic drive through the countryside.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portage

Portage is a safe and welcoming community. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as locking car doors, staying aware of their surroundings, and following basic safety guidelines in outdoor areas.

Health in Portage

Portage has several medical facilities, including the Bronson Healthcare Group and Borgess Health. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication with them and to seek medical attention immediately in case of an emergency.

Getting to Portage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portage

The closest airports to Portage are Kalamazoo/Battle Creek International Airport (AZO) and Gerald R. Ford International Airport (GRR).

Bus Stations Near Portage

Greyhound buses stop in nearby Kalamazoo and Battle Creek, both of which are a short drive from Portage.

Other Options

Rental cars are available at both airports and in downtown Kalamazoo. Taxis and ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Portage

The best way to explore Portage and the surrounding area is by car. Rental cars are available at the airport and in downtown Kalamazoo.

Where to Go Next from Portage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kalamazoo: Just a short drive north of Portage, Kalamazoo is a vibrant college town with a charming downtown area.

Saugatuck: Located on the shores of Lake Michigan, Saugatuck is a picturesque beach town known for its beautiful beaches, local art scene, and thriving LGBT community.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Portage has something for everyone. With its natural beauty, friendly locals, and diverse range of attractions, Portage is a must-visit destination in the Midwest.
