Introduction to Ada

Nestled in the beautiful Grand Rapids Area of West Michigan, Ada is a charming village that packs a lot of punch. It is a popular destination for tourists who are looking to explore the great outdoors, enjoy outdoor adventures, and take in amazing scenic views.

Founded in the 1820s, Ada has a rich history and culture that dates back centuries. The village has a fascinating blend of old and new, with modern amenities and historic sites that give visitors a glimpse into the past.

Whether you’re looking to go on a hike, bike ride, tour local museums, or simply relax in a stunning natural setting, Ada has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Ada, Michigan.

List of Places to Visit in Ada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ada Historical Society - This non-profit organization aims to preserve and celebrate the rich history of Ada. The Ada Historical Society Museum is home to a collection of artifacts, exhibits, and photographs that document the village’s past.

  • Grand Rapids Public Museum - This museum is a must-visit for history buffs and families. The museum features exhibits on Michigan’s history, natural sciences, and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Ada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ada Park - This sprawling park offers a wide range of facilities for visitors, including playgrounds, picnic areas, trails, and more. The park is popular for hiking, biking, and bird watching.

  • Seidman Park - This beautiful park boasts over 400 acres of pristine forests, wetlands, and meadows. Visitors can go hiking or grab a bike to explore the 6.5 miles of trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ada

  • Ada Township Park - This park offers a network of over 10 miles of hiking and biking trails, with varying degrees of difficulty. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the Grand River and the natural beauty of West Michigan.

  • Egypt Valley Trail - This trail offers a challenging biking terrain that passes through scenic woodlands, rolling hills, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Ada: Activities and Tours

  • St. Julian Winery Tasting Room - Visitors can enjoy a unique wine-tasting experience at this establishment, which offers a variety of Michigan-made wines and a cozy atmosphere.

  • Ada Farmers Market - This farmers market is a great place to sample local produce and artisanal goods. Visitors can browse through a variety of vendors and enjoy live music and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ada

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to Ada Historical Society and museum, followed by a leisurely stroll through Ada Village.

Day 2: Explore Ada Park and try some bird watching.

Day 3: Take a bike ride through Egpyt Valley Trail, followed by a relaxing evening at St Julian Winery Tasting Room.

Day 4: Visit the Grand Rapids Public Museum and learn about the city’s rich cultural heritage.

Day 5: Take a hike through Seidman Park and take in some breathtaking views of the West Michigan landscape.

Day 6: Spend the day strolling through Ada Farmers Market and sampling local produce.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a trip to a nearby destination or excursion, such as Holland State Park or Saugatuck Dunes.

Food and Drink Scene of Ada

Must-Try Dishes

  • Michigan Cherry Salad - This salad features local cherries, crumbled feta cheese, mixed greens, and a balsamic dressing.

  • Fried Whitefish - This dish is a local favorite and features crispy fried whitefish served with tartar sauce and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Michigan Wine - Michigan is known for its thriving wine industry, and visitors can enjoy a variety of Michigan-made wines at local wineries and tasting rooms.

  • Craft Beer - West Michigan is home to many top craft breweries, such as Founders Brewing Company, Brewery Vivant, and Perrin Brewing Company. Visitors can sample a variety of locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Ada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Ada Covered Bridge Cottage - This cozy cottage features a historic covered bridge and is located adjacent to Ada Township Park.

  • Ada Boutique Hotel - This elegant hotel offers a luxurious stay in the heart of Ada village.

What to Buy in Ada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Michigan-made wine or beer - Visitors can purchase locally brewed wine or beer to bring home as a souvenir.

  • Local Artisanal Goods - Ada Farmers Market is a great place to browse and shop for handmade crafts and artisanal goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Ada

The people of Ada are known for their hospitality, and visitors can expect to receive a warm welcome. It is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, and travelers should dress appropriately for the weather and the occasion.

Best Time to Visit Ada

The best time to visit Ada is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and sunny. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful natural scenery and take part in various outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ada

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and the occasion.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural surroundings.

  • Be disrespectful to local residents or visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ada

Ada is generally a safe place to visit, but travelers should take basic precautions such as being aware of their surroundings, avoiding secluded areas at night, and locking their car doors.

Health in Ada

Travelers to Ada should have up-to-date routine vaccinations. It is also important to protect against tick bites when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Ada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ada

Gerald R. Ford International Airport is the closest airport to Ada, located about 13 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ada

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a bus station in Grand Rapids, located about 10 miles from Ada.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get to Ada.

How to Get Around in Ada

Ada is a small village, and visitors can easily explore its attractions on foot, by bike, or by car.

Where to Go Next from Ada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Ada can explore nearby destinations such as Holland State Park, Saugatuck Dunes, or head to Grand Rapids to experience the vibrant city life.

Conclusion

Ada, Michigan is a fantastic destination for tourists who are looking to experience natural beauty, history, and culture. From hiking and biking to visiting local museums and wineries, Ada has something to offer everyone. So come and explore this charming village and create fond memories that will last a lifetime.
