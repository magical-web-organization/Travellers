Grand Rapids is the second-largest city in Michigan and is known as the cultural hub of the Western part of the state. With a rich history, diverse outdoor activities, and a thriving food and drink scene, Grand Rapids has something to offer for everyone.

Introduction to Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is in the Grand Rapids Area, in West Michigan, in Midwest, in the United States of America, in North America. The city is located on the Grand River and is about 30 miles east of Lake Michigan. Grand Rapids is known for its world-famous art competition Artprize, the Grand Rapids Public Museum, the Gerald R. Ford Museum, and being the birthplace of the hottest new beer city in America.

List of Places to Visit in Grand Rapids

Here are some of the top attractions to visit when in Grand Rapids:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Frederik Meijer Gardens and Sculpture Park - This world-renowned sculpture park contains an incredible collection of sculptures and a vibrant floral garden.
  • Gerald R. Ford Presidential Museum - This museum showcases the life and presidency of the 38th President of the United States, Gerald Ford, and hosts several exhibits throughout the year.
  • Grand Rapids Public Museum - This museum has a little bit of everything, from interactive exhibits to interesting displays on history, science, and the arts.
  • Urban Institute for Contemporary Arts - Visit this contemporary art museum to experience and engage with local artists and their creations.

Natural Attractions of Grand Rapids: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Millennium Park - this park has a beach area, paved paths for biking and walking, and beautiful “sculpture on the prairie.”
  • Provin Trails Park - This nearly 5-mile loop trail is perfect for hiking and provides excellent views of the fall colors.
  • Reeds Lake - Rent a kayak or paddleboard to experience the serene beauty of this small lake.
  • John Ball Zoo - Located on the west side of the city, the John Ball Zoo is home to various animals from around the world, including lions, tigers, and bears.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking trails in and around the city. Here are some of the top hikes and bike routes:

Millennium Trail

This 8-mile trail is perfect for a bike ride or hike. The trail highlights Grand Rapids’ beautiful natural landscape and includes pit stops for resting and scenic views.

Ottawa County Patriot’s Trail

The Ottawa County Patriot’s Trail is a 22-mile multi-use trail located just a half-hour from downtown Grand Rapids. It’s perfect for a day trip and is open year-round for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing.

Best Things to Do in Grand Rapids: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Grand Rapids, whether you’re interested in the arts, culture, or outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best things to do in Grand Rapids:

  • Grand Rapids Art Museum - The GRAM is home to over 6,000 works of art and exhibits everything from contemporary pieces to Renaissance masterpieces and has an interactive sculpture garden.
  • The Great Escape Room - This interactive escape room is perfect for a fun evening with friends or family. Solve puzzles and mysteries in a variety of themed rooms.
  • Grand Rapids Symphony Orchestra - Check out the Grand Rapids Symphony Orchestra for some of the best classical music and entertaining performances in the state.

Food and Drink Scene of Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is known for its eclectic food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken - found in almost every restaurant in GR, the best comes from Daddy Pete’s BBQ.
  • Olive burgers - serve up some of the best olive burgers in town and compliments it with their secret sauce.
  • Beer cheese soup - found in the cozy atmosphere of Hopcat restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Founders Brewing Co. - try their All Day IPA, Porter, or KBS.
  • Brewery Vivant - try their Big Red Coq Ale, Farm Hand, or Paris.
  • Long Road Distillers - try their Aquavit or a cocktail using their world-class spirits.

Where to Stay in Grand Rapids: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grand Rapids has plenty of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best options:

  • Amway Grand Plaza Hotel - This luxurious hotel is located in downtown Grand Rapids and features a beautiful indoor pool and spa.
  • The Leonard at Logan House - This charming bed and breakfast is located in Grand Rapids’ Heritage Hill neighborhood and offers beautifully appointed rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Hostel Detroit - This affordable hostel offers dormitory-style accommodation and is located right in the heart of downtown Grand Rapids.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is a friendly and welcoming city, with a love for the arts and the outdoors. Visitors should expect to encounter plenty of walking and biking throughout the city and its peaceful neighborhoods.

Best Time to Visit Grand Rapids

The best time to visit Grand Rapids is from May to October when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Visit during Artprize in September or catch a Grand Rapids Griffins or Drive game in the fall!

Do’s and Don’ts of Grand Rapids

Do:

  • Explore the city with a bike.
  • Check out the art at ArtPrize in September.
  • Visit the Frederik Meijer Gardens and Sculpture Park.

Don’t:

  • Forget to dress in layers if visiting from fall through spring.
  • Overlook the wineries and breweries throughout the Grand Rapids area.
  • Jaywalk across busy streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is generally a safe city to visit, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid theft and keep belongings safe. Avoid walking alone at night and be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is home to world-class healthcare institutions like the Spectrum Health located in multiple locations throughout the city. Visitors should have no issues accessing quality medical care if needed.

Airports Near Grand Rapids

How to Get Around in Grand Rapids

The easiest way to get around Grand Rapids is using a bike, walk or public transit via The Rapid. The city is very bike-friendly and has plenty of bike lanes throughout the city.

Conclusion

Grand Rapids in Michigan is a fantastic destination for visitors of all ages and interests. With its rich history, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Plan your trip, explore the sights, and enjoy the food, drink and lively culture of this unique place!
