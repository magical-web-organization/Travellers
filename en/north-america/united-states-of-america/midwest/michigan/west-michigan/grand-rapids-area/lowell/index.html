Introduction to Lowell

Nestled in the Grand Rapids Area of West Michigan, Lowell is a charming city that attracts visitors with its scenic beauty, historical sites, and outdoor adventures. Located in the Midwest region of the United States of America, Lowell is a hidden gem that offers a perfect respite from the hustle and bustle of big cities. With its small-town charm and welcoming locals, Lowell is a great destination for tourists who want to experience the best of Michigan.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the top attractions and activities in Lowell, Michigan, along with useful information on where to stay, where to eat and drink, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Lowell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lowell has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit places for history buffs:

  • Lowell Area Historical Museum: This museum showcases the heritage and culture of Lowell and its surrounding areas. The exhibitions include photographs, artifacts, and interactive displays.

  • The Flat River Gallery and Framing: This art gallery features a wide selection of original artwork, prints, and framing services. It also hosts art exhibitions and workshops conducted by local artists.

  • Fallasburg Covered Bridge and Village: This historic bridge dates back to the Civil War era and is a charming location for taking photographs. The surrounding Fallasburg village features several historic buildings that provide insight into the past.

Natural Attractions of Lowell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lowell is surrounded by natural beauty, making it an ideal location for outdoor enthusiasts. These are the must-visit natural attractions of Lowell:

  • Lowell State Game Area: This natural area covers over 10,000 acres and offers hiking, biking, hunting, and fishing opportunities. It is also a great spot for birdwatching, with over 200 bird species spotted in the area.

  • Kent County Trail: This multi-use trail spans 42 miles across Kent County and passes through Lowell. It is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a scenic bicycle ride.

  • Creekside Park: This park features scenic views of Flat River and offers hiking trails and a playground for children.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lowell

Lowell is a great location for outdoor enthusiasts, with several scenic trails that showcase the beauty of the area. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Lowell:

  • North Country Trail: This trail spans over 4,600 miles across seven states, including Michigan. The Lowell section of the trail features beautiful scenery and challenging terrain.

  • Lowell Area Trails: The Lowell Area Trails cover over 50 miles of pathways and connect to several natural areas, including the Fred Meijer Flat River Valley Rail Trail.

  • Flat River Heritage Trail: This trail spans over 6.5 miles and features scenic views of Flat River. It is perfect for a relaxing stroll or a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Lowell: Activities and Tours

Lowell offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors. Whether you are interested in history, nature, or adventure, you will find something to suit your interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Lowell:

  • Lowell Showboat: The Lowell Showboat is an iconic attraction that hosts several live music performances and events throughout the year. It is a great spot for a picnic or a relaxing evening by the river.

  • LowellArts: This community arts center features exhibitions, concerts, and workshops conducted by local artists. It also hosts several arts and crafts markets throughout the year.

  • Canoeing/Kayaking: Lowell offers several opportunities for canoeing and kayaking, including Flat River and Murray Lake. Many local outfitters offer equipment rental and guided tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lowell

If you are planning to spend a week in Lowell, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Lowell

Lowell offers a variety of dining options that showcase local flavors and cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties: These are handmade meat pies that originated in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula. They are a staple of Michigan cuisine.

  • BBQ: Lowell is known for its delicious BBQ. Several local restaurants serve up mouth-watering ribs, pulled pork, and brisket.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Michigan is renowned for its craft beer scene, and Lowell has several microbreweries that offer a variety of brews.

  • Cider: Michigan is also known for its cider, and Lowell has several cider mills that offer freshly pressed cider.

Where to Stay in Lowell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lowell offers several lodging options that cater to various budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Lowell:

  • Red Arrow Inn & Suites: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and an indoor pool.

  • Candlestone Resort: This resort features several amenities, including an 18-hole golf course, on-site dining, and an indoor pool.

  • Airbnb: Several local hosts offer cozy guesthouses and apartments for rent on Airbnb, providing a more unique and personalized experience for visitors.

What to Buy in Lowell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some must-buy items:

  • Maple Syrup: Michigan is known for its maple syrup, and several local farms in Lowell offer freshly tapped syrup.

  • Handmade Crafts: Lowell prides itself on its art and craft scene. Several local shops offer handmade crafts and gifts made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Lowell

Lowell has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The locals are welcoming and friendly, and visitors are encouraged to explore the city’s unique culture. It is important to respect the local customs and traditions while visiting Lowell.

Best Time to Visit Lowell

The best time to visit Lowell is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and several outdoor festivals and events take place throughout the city. The fall season is also a great time to visit, with colorful foliage and seasonal activities such as apple picking and cider drinking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lowell

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Lowell by hiking, biking, or canoeing/kayaking.

  • Try the local cuisine, including BBQ and craft beer.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural areas or parks.

  • Ignore the historical and cultural landmarks.

  • Be disrespectful to the locals or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lowell

Lowell is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Lowell

Lowell has several medical facilities that cater to both urgent and non-urgent care needs. It is important to travel with any necessary medications and to have travel insurance.

Getting to Lowell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lowell

The nearest major airport to Lowell is Gerald R. Ford International Airport, located in Grand Rapids, Michigan. Several rental car companies and taxi services are available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Lowell

The Greyhound Bus Station in Grand Rapids, Michigan, is the nearest major bus station to Lowell. Local buses also connect Lowell to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Several rental car companies offer services in Lowell, making it a convenient location for road trips.

How to Get Around in Lowell

Lowell is a small city that can be easily navigated on foot or by bike. Rental cars and taxis are also available for visitors who wish to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Lowell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lowell is surrounded by several nearby destinations that are worth visiting. Some popular excursions from Lowell include:

  • Grand Rapids, Michigan: This vibrant city is located just 20 miles from Lowell and is home to several attractions, including the Gerald R. Ford Presidential Museum and the John Ball Zoo.

  • Lake Michigan: The beautiful beaches of Lake Michigan are just a short drive from Lowell.

  • Mackinac Island: This iconic island is located in northern Michigan and can be reached by ferry from several ports.

Conclusion

Lowell is a charming city that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
