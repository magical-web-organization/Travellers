Atlanta, also known as the cultural capital of the Southern United States, is a city that boasts an eclectic mix of history, culture, and the great outdoors. From stunning mountain ranges to lively urban nightlife, Atlanta is a destination that should be on everyone’s travel list. This comprehensive travel guide to Atlanta gives you a detailed account of all the essential information you need for your trip.

Introduction to Atlanta

Founded in 1837, Atlanta has a rich history, and that is evident in its architecture and landmarks. The city is a melting pot of different cultures, making it an ideal destination for those seeking an authentic Southern experience. Atlanta is home to the busiest airport in the world, making it a convenient destination to get to.

List of Places to Visit in Atlanta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Georgia Aquarium

The world’s largest aquarium, Georgia Aquarium has over 100,000 animals from 700 different species. Visitors can witness the magical marine life and enjoy exhibits like Cold Water Quest and Ocean Voyager.

Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site

Visit the birthplace and final resting place of the iconic civil rights leader at the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site. The site also includes the Ebenezer Baptist Church and the Freedom Hall Complex.

Atlanta History Center

The Atlanta History Center takes visitors on an educational journey through the city’s past. The highlight of the center is the Swan House, a 1920s historic mansion that offers a window into Atlanta’s rich past.

Natural Attractions of Atlanta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stone Mountain Park

The state’s most visited attraction, Stone Mountain Park, is home to the world’s largest, exposed granite mountain carving. The park offers a range of outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and fishing.

Piedmont Park

Piedmont Park is a beautiful green space in the heart of Atlanta, perfect for picnics, strolling, and relaxing. The park also hosts several music and cultural festivals throughout the year.

Atlanta Botanical Garden

One of Atlanta’s must-visit attractions is the beautiful Atlanta Botanical Garden. Visitors can explore its various sections, including the Children’s Garden, the Japanese Garden, and the Edible Garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Atlanta

Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area

The Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area boasts over 80 miles of hiking and biking trails. The park is the perfect getaway from the city and offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding forest.

Northside Trail

The Northside Trail is a popular trail that runs through the heart of Atlanta. Visitors can cycle or walk along the trail, which offers stunning views of the city and the Chattahoochee River.

Silver Comet Trail

Silver Comet Trail is a 61.5-mile trail that runs from Smyrna, Georgia, to the Georgia-Alabama state line. Visitors can cycle, walk, or run along the trail and enjoy the scenic views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Atlanta: Activities and Tours

Atlanta Movie Tours

Movie buffs will love Atlanta Movie Tours, which offers guided tours of famous movie locations throughout the city. The tours include trips to filming sites of popular movies like The Hunger Games and The Walking Dead.

Atlanta Food Walks

Explore Atlanta’s diverse food scene with a guided food tour from Atlanta Food Walks. The tours take visitors through different food neighborhoods and offer samples of classic Southern dishes.

Atlanta Carriage Rides

Explore Atlanta’s historic neighborhoods in style with an Atlanta Carriage Ride. The scenic rides take visitors through areas like Midtown and Grant Park while offering fascinating insight into the city’s history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Atlanta

Must-Try Dishes

Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a classic Southern dish, and Atlanta has some of the best in the country. Head to busy joints like Busy Bee Cafe and South City Kitchen for the best Fried Chicken experience.

Peach Cobbler

Peach Cobbler is a classic Georgia dessert, and Atlanta is the best place to try it. Visit Mary Mac’s Tea Room or Paschal’s for the perfect Peach Cobbler.

Boiled Peanuts

A Southern delicacy, boiled peanuts are a must-try in Atlanta. Head to the Farmers Market or any street vendor for the best boiled peanuts in town.

Local Beverages

Coca-Cola

Atlanta is the birthplace of the world-famous soda, Coca-Cola. Visit the World of Coca-Cola for a fascinating tour of the company’s history and to try different Coke variations.

Sweet Tea

Sweet tea is a classic Southern beverage, and Atlanta has some of the best sweet tea in the country. Visit local establishments like Home Grown and Busy Bee Cafe for an authentic sweet tea experience.

Craft Beer

Atlanta has a thriving craft beer scene, with breweries like Monday Night Brewing, Sweetwater Brewing Company, and Orpheus Brewing offering unique and creative locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Atlanta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luxury Hotels

The Ritz-Carlton Atlanta

Located in the city’s downtown core, The Ritz-Carlton Atlanta offers posh rooms, a heated indoor pool, and a rooftop bar with stunning views of the city.

Four Seasons Hotel Atlanta

The Four Seasons Hotel Atlanta is a luxurious high-rise that offers modern rooms, a high-end spa, and fine dining experiences.

Mid-Range Hotels

Hyatt Regency

The Hyatt Regency offers spacious, well-appointed rooms in the heart of downtown Atlanta. The hotel also offers an on-site fitness center and an outdoor pool.

Embassy Suites by Hilton Atlanta

Embassy Suites by Hilton Atlanta offers modern suites with separate living spaces and kitchenettes, making it perfect for families or extended stays.

Budget Hotels

The Ponce Hotel

The Ponce Hotel is an affordable, European-style boutique hotel located in the heart of Atlanta’s historic Poncey-Highland neighborhood.

HI Atlanta Hostel

The HI Atlanta Hostel is a budget-friendly option for travelers looking for a clean and comfortable place to stay. The hostel offers private and shared rooms in the heart of downtown.

What to Buy in Atlanta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peach Jam or Jelly

Bring home a taste of Georgia with peach jam or jelly. Visit the Peachtree Center Farmers Market or the Sweet Auburn Curb Market for the best local options.

Southern Hot Sauce

Add some Southern heat to your meals with a spicy hot sauce. Visit Ponce City Market for a range of locally made options.

Local Culture and Customs of Atlanta

Atlanta is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors should embrace the Southern hospitality. Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars, with 20% being the standard amount. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites, and smoking is prohibited in all public areas.

Best Time to Visit Atlanta

The best time to visit Atlanta is from March to May. The weather during this time is perfect for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions, and there are several cultural and music festivals during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Atlanta

Do:

  • Try the fried chicken and sweet tea
  • Visit the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site and learn about the civil rights movement.
  • Explore Atlanta’s famous street art in the Krog Street Tunnel.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Forget to factor in traffic when planning your itinerary.
  • Go off the designated hiking trails without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Atlanta

While Atlanta is generally a safe city, visitors should be aware of pickpockets and petty theft in tourist areas. It is also best to avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas, and visitors should always be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Atlanta

Visitors should bring sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the city’s outdoor attractions. Drinking tap water is safe in Atlanta, and visitors should avoid overexposure to the sun during peak hours.

Getting to Atlanta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Atlanta

Atlanta is home to the world’s busiest airport, Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport. The airport is located just 10 miles south of downtown Atlanta and offers several transportation options like taxi, Uber, and public transport.

Bus Stations Near Atlanta

The Greyhound Station is located in downtown Atlanta, making it a convenient option for visitors arriving via bus.

Other Options

Visitors can explore the city on foot, but it is best to use public transport or taxis for longer distances. MARTA, Atlanta’s public transport system, offers a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Atlanta

MARTA

MARTA, Atlanta’s public transport system, offers both bus and train services throughout the city. The system is easy to use, affordable, and offers a range of ticket options.

Taxis/Uber

Taxis and ridesharing options like Uber are readily available in Atlanta and are a convenient option for exploring the city.

Walking

Walking is the perfect way to explore Atlanta’s various neighborhoods and attractions. Visitors should take caution when walking in busy areas and always be aware of their surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Atlanta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savannah

Savannah is a charming city, located just a few hours’ drive from Atlanta. The city boasts beautiful architecture, stunning parks, and the perfect beach town vibe.

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

The Great Smoky Mountains National Park is located just five hours’ drive from Atlanta and is a stunning destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers several hiking trails, stunning scenic drives, and an excellent opportunity for wildlife spotting.

Conclusion

Atlanta offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures, making it a dream destination for travelers. Whether you are savoring the local cuisine, exploring historical sites, or enjoying outdoor activities, Atlanta is a city with something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect Atlanta getaway and experience the best the city has to offer.
