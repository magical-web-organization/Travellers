Introduction to Grayling

Grayling, Michigan is a charming city situated in the northern region of the state. Located in the Midwest of the United States, Grayling offers visitors a chance to experience natural beauty intermixed with rich history. With a population of just over 1,800 residents, Grayling is a small town that’s big on adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring the great outdoors or soaking up some local history, Grayling is the perfect place to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Grayling

There are many exciting attractions to explore in Grayling. From historical sites to natural wonders, here’s a list of must-visit places in Grayling:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Crawford County Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Crawford County through a variety of exhibits.
  • Michigan AuSable Valley Railroad: The Michigan AuSable Valley Railroad offers visitors a chance to ride and learn about the history of the railroad in northern Michigan.
  • Wellington Farm, USA: This living-history museum provides a glimpse into life on a Michigan farm in the 1930s and 1940s.

Natural Attractions of Grayling: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hartwick Pines State Park: Hartwick Pines State Park is a 9,672-acre park that’s home to old-growth pine forests, a logging museum, and a mile-long trail.
  • Mason Tract Pathway: The Mason Tract Pathway trail is a hiking and biking trail that winds through beautiful forests and past the scenic Mason Tract Bridge.
  • Grayling Fish Hatchery: The Grayling Fish Hatchery is a fun destination for nature lovers. Visitors can watch rainbow trout swim in the ponds and learn about the efforts to preserve the local fish population.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grayling

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Grayling, thanks to the town’s many scenic trails. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a novice biker, there’s a trail for you. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Grayling:

  • Hanson Hills Recreation Area: This park offers a variety of trails, from easy to challenging, that are ideal for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Aspen Park Trail: This easy-to-moderate 2.2-mile trail features beautiful views of the Au Sable River.
  • North Higgins Lake State Park: This park features several trails that offer stunning views of Higgins Lake, as well as the opportunity to spot wildlife like deer and birds.

Best Things to Do in Grayling: Activities and Tours

Grayling offers a variety of activities and tours to help visitors make the most of their trip. Some of the best things to do in Grayling include:

  • Canoeing or kayaking down the Au Sable River: The Au Sable River is a popular destination for canoeing and kayaking enthusiasts, thanks to its calm waters and beautiful scenery.
  • Horseback riding: Several stables in the area offer guided horseback rides through the forests and trails of northern Michigan.
  • Snowmobiling: In the winter months, visitors can rent snowmobiles and explore the scenic trails of Grayling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grayling

Are you planning a trip to Grayling for a week or longer? Here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Grayling Fish Hatchery and enjoy a scenic drive through the Huron-Manistee National Forest.

Day 2: Explore the trails at Hartwick Pines State Park and take a guided tour of the park’s logging museum.

Day 3: Take a day trip to nearby Mackinac Island, where you can explore the historic fort and enjoy fudge at one of the island’s many candy shops.

Day 4: Go horseback riding through the forests of northern Michigan.

Day 5: Rent a canoe or kayak and enjoy a leisurely float down the Au Sable River.

Day 6: Hit the slopes at Hanson Hills Recreation Area, a popular destination for downhill and cross-country skiing.

Day 7: Visit the Michigan AuSable Valley Railroad and take a ride through the stunning northern Michigan countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Grayling

Grayling offers plenty of dining options for visitors, from classic American fare to ethnic cuisine. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties: A traditional Upper Peninsula dish, pasties are a type of savory pastry filled with meat and vegetables.
  • Whitefish: Fresh-caught whitefish is a popular dish in the northern Michigan region.
  • Cherry pie: Michigan is known for its cherry production, and cherry pie is a staple dessert in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Michigan craft beer: The state boasts a thriving craft beer scene, and Grayling has several microbreweries worth visiting.
  • Cherry wine: Made from cherries grown in the area, cherry wine is a unique and refreshing local specialty.
  • Cider: Michigan is the third-largest producer of apples in the United States, and the region’s apple cider is a must-try beverage.

Where to Stay in Grayling: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Grayling has a variety of lodging options, from budget-friendly motels to upscale resorts. Here’s a list of some of the best places to stay in Grayling:

  • Ramada by Wyndham Grayling Hotel & Conference Center: This hotel is conveniently located near downtown Grayling and features an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • North Country Lodge: A cozy, family-owned motel located near Hartwick Pines State Park.
  • Woodland Motor Lodge: A budget-friendly motel that’s perfect for travelers on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Grayling: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a souvenir to remember your trip to Grayling? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Cherry products: From cherry jam to cherry salsa, there are plenty of cherry-based products to take home as a gift or a treat for yourself.
  • Outdoor gear: Grayling is home to several outdoor outfitters, so consider picking up some new hiking or fishing gear before you leave.
  • Local art and crafts: There are many talented artists and craftspeople in the Grayling area, making this a great place to find unique and one-of-a-kind souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Grayling

Northern Michigan is known for its friendly communities and laid-back atmosphere, and Grayling is no exception. Visitors should be aware that the area can get quite cold in the winter months, so be sure to pack warm clothing if you’re planning a trip during the off-season.

Best Time to Visit Grayling

The best time to visit Grayling depends on your interests. If you’re looking to enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking, the summer months are ideal. For winter sports like skiing and snowmobiling, plan your trip for the colder months. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, as the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grayling

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Grayling:

Do:

  • Dress for the weather, especially if you’re planning to spend time outdoors.
  • Take advantage of the town’s natural beauty by going for a hike or canoe trip.
  • Try some of the local cuisine, like pasties and cherry pie.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and etiquette.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Engage in activities that could harm the environment or wildlife.

Safety and Health

While Grayling is a safe destination for visitors, it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your stay in Grayling:

Safety in Grayling

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially if you’re hiking alone in the woods.
  • Follow all posted signs and warnings in parks and other outdoor areas.
  • Keep your valuables locked in your hotel room or car when you’re not using them.

Health in Grayling

  • Be sure to pack any necessary medications or medical supplies.
  • Drink plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Use sunscreen and bug spray to protect yourself from the sun and insect bites.

How to Get Around in Grayling

The best way to get around Grayling is by car. However, there are also several bike and hiking trails throughout the town that offer a scenic and eco-friendly way to explore.

Where to Go Next from Grayling: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grayling is located in the heart of northern Michigan, making it a great base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore: This national park is located about 100 miles from Grayling and is home to some of the most beautiful sand dunes in the United States.
