Introduction to Bear Lake

Located in the heart of Grand Traverse Bay Area in Northern Michigan, Bear Lake is a serene and picturesque destination that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town is surrounded by dense forests, pristine lakes, and miles of stunning coastline, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

With a rich history dating back to the early 19th century, Bear Lake has retained its small-town charm and boasts a unique cultural heritage that sets it apart from other destinations in Michigan. Visitors can explore the town’s museums and historic sites to learn about its intriguing past, or immerse themselves in the great outdoors by hiking, biking, fishing, or kayaking.

In this guide, we’ll take you through some of the top places to visit, things to do, and experiences to have in Bear Lake and help you plan your perfect getaway to this hidden gem in Northern Michigan.

List of Places to Visit in Bear Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bear Lake is home to several museums and historic sites that offer a fascinating insight into the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Bear Lake Historical Museum

One of the most popular attractions in Bear Lake is the Bear Lake Historical Museum, which is housed in a charming 19th-century building that was once a school. The museum features exhibits on the town’s history, including its logging industry, early settlers, and Native American heritage.

Portage Lake State Park

Portage Lake State Park is a beautiful natural reserve that offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and swimming. The park also includes several historic sites, such as the Point Oneida Historic District, which is home to several well-preserved 19th-century farmsteads and homesteads.

Natural Attractions of Bear Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bear Lake’s stunning natural landscapes make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love to hike, bike, fish, or simply soak up the beautiful scenery. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit in Bear Lake.

Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore

Located just a short drive from Bear Lake, the Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore is a must-see destination for nature lovers. The park covers over 35 miles of Lake Michigan’s eastern coastline and features some of the tallest dunes in the country, as well as miles of pristine beaches and hiking trails.

Arcadia Dunes

Arcadia Dunes is a beautiful natural reserve that offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and kayaking. The reserve covers over 3,000 acres of land and includes several well-marked trails that offer stunning views of Lake Michigan and the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bear Lake

Bear Lake offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Michigan, with miles of well-maintained trails that wind through its beautiful natural landscapes. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes to explore in Bear Lake.

The Betsie Valley Trail

The Betsie Valley Trail is a 22-mile long trail that stretches from Thompsonville to Frankfort and passes through several charming towns, including Beulah and Elberta. The trail is open to hikers and bikers and offers stunning views of Michigan’s countryside and Lake Michigan.

Hartwick Pines State Park

Hartwick Pines State Park is a beautiful natural reserve that offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and camping. The park features several well-marked trails of varying difficulty, including the Old Growth Forest Trail, which winds through a stunning old growth forest of towering pine trees.

Best Things to Do in Bear Lake: Activities and Tours

Bear Lake offers a range of activities and tours that are tailored to suit the interests and preferences of all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Bear Lake.

Go Fishing

Bear Lake is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts, with its pristine lakes and rivers teeming with trout, salmon, and other freshwater species. Visitors can hire a boat or head to one of the town’s many fishing piers to try their luck at catching a few fish.

Watch Wildlife

Bear Lake is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including black bears, coyotes, bald eagles, and deer. Visitors can take a guided tour or hike through one of the town’s many nature reserves to watch these animals in their natural habitats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bear Lake

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Bear Lake that includes some of the top attractions and activities in town.

Day 1: Visit the Bear Lake Historical Museum and learn about the town’s fascinating history.

Day 2: Take a hike through the Arcadia Dunes and enjoy the stunning views of Lake Michigan.

Day 3: Explore the Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore and enjoy a picnic on one of its beautiful beaches.

Day 4: Rent a boat and go fishing on Bear Lake or one of the other nearby lakes.

Day 5: Visit the Point Oneida Historic District at Portage Lake State Park and learn about the area’s early settlers.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the countryside and stop at some of the charming towns along the way.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Bear Lake’s many spas or wellness centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Bear Lake

Bear Lake is home to a range of restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer delicious local food and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in town.

Must-Try Dishes

Pasties

Pasties are a popular local dish that originated in Michigan’s upper peninsula. These hearty meat pies are filled with beef, potatoes, onions, and rutabaga and are perfect for a quick and filling meal.

Cherry Pie

Cherry pie is another must-try dish in Bear Lake, as the town is located in the heart of Michigan’s cherry country. Visitors can enjoy a slice of cherry pie at one of the town’s many bakeries or cafes.

Local Beverages

Michigan Craft Beer

Michigan is home to a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors to Bear Lake can enjoy a cold pint of locally-brewed beer at one of the town’s many bars and breweries.

Cherry Wine

Cherry wine is another popular local beverage in Bear Lake, made from the region’s famous cherries. Visitors can enjoy a glass of cherry wine at one of the town’s wineries or tasting rooms.

Where to Stay in Bear Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bear Lake offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury resorts to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some top options to consider.

Bella Vista Inn

Bella Vista Inn is a charming boutique hotel located in the heart of Bear Lake. The inn features spacious rooms with stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside, as well as a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.

Bear Lake Bed and Breakfast

Bear Lake Bed and Breakfast is a cozy and welcoming guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and a hearty breakfast each morning. The guesthouse is located within walking distance of the town’s many attractions and restaurants.

What to Buy in Bear Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bear Lake is home to several specialty shops and boutiques that offer unique souvenirs and locally-made products. Here are some top options to consider.

Cherry Products

Bear Lake is located in the heart of Michigan’s cherry country, and visitors can stock up on locally-made cherry products, such as jams, jellies, and cherry pie filling, at one of the town’s many specialty shops.

Homemade Crafts

Bear Lake is home to several talented artists and craftspeople who create beautiful handmade items, such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings. Visitors can support these local artisans by purchasing their unique creations at one of the town’s many art galleries or boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Bear Lake

Bear Lake has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and food. The town’s residents are known for their warm hospitality and love of the great outdoors, and visitors are encouraged to participate in community events and festivals. Bear Lake also has a strong Native American heritage, and visitors can learn about the town’s indigenous culture at several museums and cultural centers.

Best Time to Visit Bear Lake

The best time to visit Bear Lake is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and the town is abuzz with activity. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, fishing, and swimming, and take part in community events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bear Lake

Do:

  • Respect the town’s natural landscapes and wildlife. Dispose of trash properly and avoid disturbing plants and animals.
  • Take part in community events and festivals to learn about the town’s unique cultural heritage.
  • Be mindful of other visitors and residents and practice good manners and etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities. Always wear appropriate clothing and protective gear and follow safety procedures.
  • Leave the town’s natural landscapes and outdoor spaces littered or damaged.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions. Respect the town’s cultural heritage and participate in community events and festivals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bear Lake

Bear Lake is a safe and welcoming destination, with a low crime rate and a strong community spirit. However, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety and wellbeing, particularly when engaging in outdoor activities. Always wear appropriate clothing and protective gear and follow safety guidelines when hiking, biking, fishing, or swimming.

Health in Bear Lake

Bear Lake is a healthy destination, with clean air and water and plenty of opportunities for exercise and outdoor recreation. However, visitors should be mindful of common health concerns, such as sunburn and dehydration, and take precautions to protect themselves. Always wear sunscreen and drink plenty of water when engaging in outdoor activities.
