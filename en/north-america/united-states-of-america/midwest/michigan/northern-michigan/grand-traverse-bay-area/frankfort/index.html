Introduction to Frankfort

Frankfort is a cozy, picturesque town located in northern Michigan, in the Grand Traverse Bay Area. The town is situated on the shores of Lake Michigan and Betsie Bay, and its natural beauty attracts countless visitors from around the world. Frankfort boasts a rich history, a vibrant arts scene, excellent outdoor recreational opportunities, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation, Frankfort is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Frankfort

There are so many things to do and see in Frankfort, so we’ve put together a list of the top attractions to visit during your stay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Benzie Area Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Benzie County and is housed in the historic Dow Museum building.
  • Point Betsie Lighthouse: The Point Betsie Lighthouse was built in 1858 and is one of the oldest and most picturesque lighthouses in Michigan.
  • Frankfort Light Station Museum: This museum is located in the restored Coast Guard Station and features exhibits on the Great Lakes maritime history.
  • Eden Hill Museum: The Eden Hill Museum showcases the history of the area since the time of the Native Americans.

Natural Attractions of Frankfort: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elberta Beach: This beautiful beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxation.
  • Betsie Valley Trail: This trail is a 22-mile-long paved path that runs from Frankfort to Thompsonville, and is perfect for hiking, biking and running.
  • Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore: This national park is located just a short drive from Frankfort and offers stunning views of the lake and dunes.
  • Crystal Lake: This beautiful lake is perfect for fishing, boating, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frankfort

Frankfort is surrounded by beautiful nature and offers a range of hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore.

Betsie Valley Trail

This 22-mile trail follows the former Detroit and Milwaukee Railroad corridor, and offers stunning views of the surrounding nature and wildlife. The trail is open year-round and is perfect for hikers, bikers, and runners of all skill levels.

Frankfort-Elberta Area

The Frankfort-Elberta area is home to a number of different hiking trails, including the Betsie River Trail, Green Point Dunes Trail, and the Frankfort Beach Trail. Each offers beautiful views of the surrounding nature and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Frankfort: Activities and Tours

From water sports to art galleries, there’s something for everyone in Frankfort.

  • H2O Watersports: Rent a kayak, stand-up paddleboard, or paddleboat and explore the beautiful waters of Betsie Bay.
  • Point Betsie Lighthouse: Take a tour of this historic lighthouse to learn about its history and stunning architecture.
  • North Star Sailing Charters: Take a sunset or day sail on Lake Michigan and experience the beauty of Frankfort from the water.
  • Elizabeth Lane Oliver Center for the Arts: This art center offers a range of classes and exhibits for visitors of all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frankfort

If you’re planning to spend a week in Frankfort, we’ve put together a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time.

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Frankfort and settle into your accommodations. Spend the rest of the day exploring downtown and enjoying dinner at one of the local restaurants.
Day 2 Spend the day hiking or biking on the Betsie Valley Trail. Take lunch at a picnic spot along the way.
Day 3 Visit the Benzie Area Historical Society Museum and learn about the history of the region.
Day 4 Take a sunset sail on Lake Michigan with North Star Sailing Charters.
Day 5 Visit Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore and take a scenic drive or hike.
Day 6 Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore Betsie Bay.
Day 7 Spend the last day of your trip relaxing at Elberta Beach and taking in the beautiful views.

Food and Drink Scene of Frankfort

Frankfort is home to some delicious restaurants and drinks. Here are some must-tries:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: The local whitefish makes for a perfect fish and chips dish at The Fusion.
  • Cherry BBQ Pork: This sweet and savory dish is a favorite at Stormcloud Brewing Company.
  • Bennies: A pancake, stacked with poached eggs and bacon is a staple breakfast at Crescent Bakery.

Local Beverages

  • Microbrews: Frankfort’s local breweries including Stormcloud Brewing Company are must-visits for microbrew fans.
  • Cherry Wine: Cherry wine is a local favorite and can be found at St. Ambrose Cellars.

Where to Stay in Frankfort: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Frankfort has a range of accommodation options to suit all types of travelers and budgets.

  • The Hotel Frankfort: Located in the heart of downtown Frankfort, this historic hotel offers charming rooms and great amenities.
  • The Harbor Lights Resort: This beachfront resort offers stunning views of Lake Michigan and is perfect for those looking to relax and unwind.
  • Betsie Bay Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located just a few blocks from downtown Frankfort and features cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Frankfort: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are some great souvenir shops located in downtown Frankfort that sell unique products and gifts, including:

  • Betsie Bay Furniture: This store sells beautiful handcrafted furniture made from locally-sourced materials.
  • The Garden Theater: This historic theater sells a range of movie-themed products, including posters and collectibles.
  • Betsie Bay “Delights”: This store sells a wide range of delicious local products including cheese and fudge.

Local Culture and Customs of Frankfort

Frankfort is a friendly and welcoming community that values its natural beauty and small-town character.

Best Time to Visit Frankfort

The best time to visit Frankfort is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the fall and winter months also offer unique opportunities for outdoor recreation and relaxation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Frankfort

Do:

  • Do explore the surrounding nature: Frankfort is surrounded by some of the most beautiful nature in the region, and exploring the local beaches, hiking trails, and waterways is a must.
  • Do support local businesses: Frankfort is home to a range of local businesses, including shops, restaurants, and artists’ studios, so be sure to support them during your visit.
  • Do try the local cuisine: Whether it’s fresh whitefish, cherry wine, or Microbrews, Frankfort’s local cuisine is a must-try during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Frankfort is home to some beautiful natural areas, so be sure to dispose of your trash properly and keep the area clean.
  • Don’t be disrespectful: Frankfort is a friendly and welcoming community, so be sure to treat the locals and their customs with respect and kindness.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately: The weather in Frankfort can vary greatly depending on the season, so be sure to dress appropriately for the conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Frankfort

Frankfort is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take common-sense precautions, such as locking their car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Frankfort

Frankfort has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, and visitors can access healthcare services if needed.

Getting to Frankfort: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Frankfort

The closest international airport to Frankfort is Cherry Capital Airport, which is located in Traverse City, approximately 45 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Frankfort

There are no major bus stations near Frankfort, but visitors can access bus services from nearby towns in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Frankfort or use a ride-sharing service to get around the town and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Frankfort

Frankfort is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use a car or ride-sharing service if needed.

Where to Go Next from Frankfort: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other areas in the region, consider visiting:

  • Traverse City: Located about 45 miles south of Frankfort, Traverse City is a vibrant town with many attractions, including wineries, breweries, and scenic bike trails.
  • The Leelanau Peninsula: Located about 30 miles northeast of Frankfort, the Leelanau Peninsula is home to some of the best wineries and vineyards in the area, as well as stunning state parks and beaches.

Conclusion

Frankfort is a charming town with a rich history, vibrant arts scene, and beautiful surroundings. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventurous vacation, Frankfort has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide and ensure that you make the most out of your visit to this beautiful town in northern Michigan.
