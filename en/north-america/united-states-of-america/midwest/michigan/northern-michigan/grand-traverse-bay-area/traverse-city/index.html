Introduction to Traverse City

Traverse City is a charming city located in the heart of Grand Traverse Bay Area, in Northern Michigan. This city is known for its vast cherry orchards, crystal clear water, and sandy beaches. It has a perfect mix of outdoor recreation opportunities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. If you are looking for a peaceful and memorable vacation, Traverse City should be on your bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Traverse City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Music House Museum

The Music House Museum is a fascinating attraction for music lovers of all ages. It has a vast collection of musical instruments, including player pianos, antique phonographs, and music boxes. The museum also houses a concert hall where you can attend live performances of various musicians and artists.

The Dennos Museum Center

The Dennos Museum Center is an art and cultural center that celebrates the works of local and national artists. The museum has a permanent collection of over 2,500 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs. It also features traveling exhibitions throughout the year.

Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore

Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. This national park has towering sand dunes, pristine beaches, and crystal-clear lakes. The park offers several outdoor activities, such as hiking, swimming, and wildlife watching.

Natural Attractions of Traverse City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Old Mission Peninsula

Old Mission Peninsula is a narrow strip of land that juts out into Grand Traverse Bay Area. It offers a scenic drive with breathtaking views of the surrounding water and greenery. There are several vineyards, orchards, and farms on the peninsula where you can taste local wines, fresh fruits, and vegetables.

Traverse Area Recreation and Transportation Trails (TART Trail)

The Traverse Area Recreation and Transportation Trails (TART Trail) is a network of paved paths that connects Traverse City to nearby towns and villages. It is perfect for biking, jogging, and walking enthusiasts. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding forests, lakes, and hills.

Torch Lake

Torch Lake is a pristine lake with crystal clear blue water and sandy beaches. It is one of the most popular recreational destinations in the region. The lake offers several water sports activities, such as swimming, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Traverse City

Sleeping Bear Heritage Trail

The Sleeping Bear Heritage Trail is a scenic biking and hiking trail that runs through Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore. The trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests, sand dunes, and lakeshore. It is suitable for all skill levels.

Leelanau Trail

The Leelanau Trail is a paved biking and walking trail that runs for 17 miles through rural countryside and charming villages. The trail features several parks, rest areas, and scenic overlooks. It is perfect for casual bikers and families with children.

Boardman Lake Trail

The Boardman Lake Trail is a scenic trail that runs for 3.5 miles around Boardman Lake. It is suitable for biking, jogging, and walking enthusiasts. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and the lake.

Best Things to Do in Traverse City: Activities and Tours

Cherry Festival

The National Cherry Festival is an annual event held in Traverse City in July. It celebrates the cherry harvest season with music, food, and fun activities for everyone. The festival features cherry pit-spitting contests, parades, live music, and cherry- themed food.

Traverse Tall Ship Company

The Traverse Tall Ship Company offers a unique sailing experience on the Tall Ship Manitou. The ship sails on the crystal-clear waters of Grand Traverse Bay Area, offering stunning views of the shoreline and the sunset. It is perfect for romantic getaways and family outings.

Kayaking Tours

Kayaking tours are a popular activity in Traverse City. The tours offer an opportunity to explore the crystal-clear waters of the surrounding lakes and rivers. There are several kayak rental companies in the city that offer tours for beginners and experienced kayakers.

Food and Drink Scene of Traverse City

Must-Try Dishes

Cherry Pie

Cherry pie is a local specialty in Traverse City. The city is known as the Cherry Capital of the World and produces over 75% of the tart cherries in the United States. Several restaurants in the city serve delicious cherry pies.

Whitefish

Whitefish is a freshwater fish that is caught locally in the surrounding lakes. It is known for its mild flavor and delicate texture. Several restaurants in the city serve whitefish dishes.

Cherry Wine

Cherry Wine is a local specialty wine made from locally grown tart cherries. It has a unique flavor profile and pairs well with the local cuisine. There are several wineries in the city that offer cherry wine tasting.

Local Beverages

Breweries

Traverse City is home to several microbreweries that offer a range of craft beers. The breweries feature outdoor beer gardens and rooftop terraces with stunning views of the city and the bay.

Cherry Juice

Cherry juice is a refreshing drink made from locally grown tart cherries. It is rich in antioxidants and has several health benefits. Several juice bars in the city serve delicious cherry juice.

Where to Stay in Traverse City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Traverse City offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different travelers’ needs and budgets. From luxury hotels to budget hostels, the city has something for everyone.

Luxury Hotels

Park Place Hotel

The Park Place Hotel is a historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Traverse City. The hotel features luxurious rooms with stunning views of the city and the bay. It offers several amenities, such as a fitness center, a rooftop terrace, and a restaurant.

Grand Traverse Resort and Spa

The Grand Traverse Resort and Spa is a luxurious resort located in a scenic location near the Grand Traverse Bay Area. The resort features several amenities, such as a spa, a golf course, and several restaurants. It offers several accommodation options, including rooms and suites.

Budget Hostels and Guesthouses

Hostel on The Hill

Hostel on The Hill is a budget hostel located in a scenic location near Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore. The hostel features dormitory-style rooms and private rooms. It offers several amenities, such as a shared kitchen, a rooftop terrace, and a game room.

Lost Inn Paradise

Lost Inn Paradise is a charming guesthouse located in a peaceful location near the Boardman Lake Trail. The guesthouse features comfortable rooms with affordable rates. It offers several amenities, such as a shared kitchen, a garden, and a lounge.

What to Buy in Traverse City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cherry Products

Cherry products are the most popular souvenirs to bring back home from Traverse City. There are several shops in the city that sell cherry-themed products, such as cherry pie filling, cherry salsa, and cherry jelly.

Wines

Traverse City is known for its world-class wines made from locally grown grapes and fruit. There are several wineries in the city where you can taste and buy local wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Traverse City

Traverse City has a laid-back culture and is known for its friendly locals. The city has a rich history and is proud of its cherry farming heritage. The locals are passionate about outdoor recreation activities, such as hiking, biking, and watersports.

Best Time to Visit Traverse City

The best time to visit Traverse City is from May to September, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The peak tourist season is during July and August, when the National Cherry Festival is held.

Do’s and Don’ts of Traverse City

Do:

  • Do try the local cherry pies and cherry wines.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore and the local rivers and lakes.
  • Do attend live performances at the local art centers and theaters.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural areas and parks.
  • Don’t forget to wear sunscreen and bring a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Don’t miss the National Cherry Festival if you are visiting during July.

Safety and Health

Safety in Traverse City

Traverse City is a safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take precautions, such as not leaving valuables in their cars and locking their doors at night.

Health in Traverse City

Traverse City has several hospitals and medical clinics that provide excellent healthcare services. Visitors are advised to bring their prescription medications and medical documents with them.

Getting to Traverse City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Traverse City

The Cherry Capital Airport is the nearest airport to Traverse City. It is located 5 miles southeast of downtown Traverse City.

Bus Stations Near Traverse City

Greyhound Bus Lines and Indian Trails Bus Lines offer bus services to Traverse City from several nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Several car rental companies, such as Hertz, Enterprise, and Alamo, operate in Traverse City.

How to Get Around in Traverse City

Traverse City offers several transportation options, such as taxis, buses, and bikes, to get around the city. Biking and walking are popular modes of transportation in the city.

Where to Go Next from Traverse City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore

Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore is located near Traverse City and offers several hiking trails, beaches, and sand dunes.

Mackinac Island

Mackinac Island is a charming island located in Lake Huron. It is known for its horse-drawn carriages and bicycles as the only modes of transportation.

Conclusion

Traverse City is a charming, laid-back city that offers a mix of outdoor recreation, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. It is perfect for a peaceful and memorable vacation with family and friends.
