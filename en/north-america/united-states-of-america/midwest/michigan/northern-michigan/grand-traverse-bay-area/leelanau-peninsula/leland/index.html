Are you looking for a beautiful and peaceful getaway that can take you across time? If yes, then Leland, Michigan, is the perfect place for you. Located in Leelanau Peninsula, in Grand Traverse Bay Area, in Northern Michigan, Leland is a charming and historic village that will take you back in time with its beautiful, tree-lined streets and stunning views of Lake Michigan.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Leland, Michigan, you’ll discover must-visit tourist attractions, best things to do, where to eat and stay, useful travel tips, and more.

Introduction to Leland

As one of the oldest and most charming towns in Michigan, Leland is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. It is a veritable paradise for nature lovers, adventure-seekers, and history buffs. Whether you’re looking for a romantic getaway or a family vacation, Leland has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Leland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fishtown: Fishtown is a historic commercial fishing village with over a century of history. It features weathered shanties, shops, galleries, eateries, and docks. It is the perfect place to explore on foot, and you can also book a guided tour of the village.

  • Old Art Building: Originally built as a community center in 1892, this beautiful building now houses an art gallery, classrooms, and workshops. It hosts art exhibitions, theater performances, and various workshops throughout the year.

  • Leelanau Historical Society and Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and culture of the Leelanau region, including prehistoric times to the present. Exhibits explore the lumbering industry, marine history, agriculture, horticulture, fishing, and the cultural heritage of the Native Americans.

Natural Attractions of Leland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore: This 35-mile-long stretch along the northwest coast of Michigan is one of the most popular destinations in the state. Visitors can hike, swim, kayak, or just lounge on the beach. The area has an abundance of wildlife, including bald eagles, ospreys, and black bears.

  • Pierce Stocking Scenic Drive: This 7.4-mile scenic drive is a must-see for first-time visitors to the Sleeping Bear Dunes. It boasts sweeping views of Lake Michigan and the sand dunes, and it is easily accessible by car.

  • Empire Bluff Trail: This short and easy hike offers stunning views of the dunes, forests, and the picturesque town of Glen Arbor. The trail is perfect for families and beginners, and it is great for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leland

  • Leelanau Trail: This 15-mile trail starts in Suttons Bay and ends in Traverse City. It is perfect for walking, biking, and inline skating. The trail meanders through scenic forests, orchards, farmlands, and beaches.

  • Sleeping Bear Heritage Trail: This 27-mile trail runs through Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore and connects Glen Arbor to Empire. It is perfect for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing. The trail passes through forests, wetlands, and sand dunes.

Best Things to Do in Leland: Activities and Tours

  • Charter Fishing: Leland is known for its excellent fishing opportunities. You can hire a charter boat and enjoy a day out on the water with an experienced guide.

  • Wine Tasting: Leland and the surrounding area are known for their award-winning wineries. Take a tour of the vineyards, sample some of the best wines, and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

  • Canoeing and Kayaking: The Leelanau Peninsula has some of the most beautiful waterways in Michigan. You can rent a kayak or a canoe and explore the waterways at your own pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Leland

Must-Try Dishes

  • Whitefish: This local favorite is a freshwater fish that is served in several restaurants in Leland. It’s typically grilled or fried and served with a side of fries or vegetables.

  • Cherry Pie: Michigan is known for its cherry orchards, and Leland and nearby areas have several bakeries that serve delicious cherry pies.

Local Beverages

  • Cherry Wine: Leelanau and Traverse City wineries specialize in cherry wines, which are made with locally grown cherries.

  • Beer: Leland and the surrounding area have several microbreweries that serve locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Leland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Riverside Inn: This historic inn is located in the heart of Leland and offers a unique experience with its beautiful riverside views and charming rooms.

  • The Leland Lodge: This lodge is situated in a peaceful wooded area and is perfect for couples and families. It has comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.

  • The Whaleback Inn: This inn is known for its cozy accommodations and its proximity to the beach, vineyards, and hiking trails.

What to Buy in Leland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cherry Products: Leland and the nearby areas are known for their delicious cherry products, including jams, jellies, sauces, and dried cherries.

  • Antiques: Leland has antique shops that offer unique and rare items, including furniture, jewelry, and art.

Local Culture and Customs of Leland

Leland is a small town with a rich history and a deep appreciation for culture and tradition. Locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and the community takes pride in preserving the town’s historic and cultural landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Leland

The best time to visit Leland is between June to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town is crowded during the peak season, so it’s best to book accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leland

Do:

  • Respect the natural wildlife and environment

  • Explore the town on foot and take in the beautiful scenery

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter and pollute the beaches and forests

  • Ignore the local culture and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Leland

Leland is generally a safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution, especially when hiking or exploring the outdoors. Be aware of wildlife and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Leland

Leland has several medical facilities, including a community hospital and clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance and carry any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Leland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Leland

The nearest airport is Cherry Capital Airport in Traverse City, about 40 minutes away from Leland.

Bus Stations Near Leland

There are several bus stations in Traverse City, which is about a 30-minute drive from Leland.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to travel to Leland.

How to Get Around in Leland

The town is small enough to explore on foot, and there are several bike rental shops for those who want to cycle around town. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Leland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Traverse City: This vibrant town has plenty of attractions, including breweries, wineries, shopping, and outdoor adventures.

  • Torch Lake: This beautiful lake is known for its crystal-clear waters and sandy beaches. It’s perfect for swimming, boating, and paddleboarding.

  • Mackinac Island: This picturesque island is known for its historic sites, horse-drawn carriages, and fudge shops. It’s a perfect day trip destination from Leland.

Conclusion

Leland, Michigan, is a hidden gem that offers a delightful break from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. From natural attractions to cultural landmarks, scenic views to outdoor adventures, Leland has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and charm of this historic village.
