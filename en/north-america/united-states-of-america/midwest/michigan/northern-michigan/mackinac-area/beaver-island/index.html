Introduction to Beaver Island

Beaver Island is a remote paradise located in the northern part of Lake Michigan. This small island belongs to Charlevoix County and is part of Michigan’s mainland. The island has a rich cultural heritage, and natural beauty that has made it a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. Most of the island consists of forests and wetlands, which makes it perfect for hiking and biking. There are also plenty of lakes and beaches on the island, where you can enjoy watersports and sunbathing.

List of Places to Visit in Beaver Island

When you visit Beaver Island, there are many exciting places to see and activities to do. Here are some of the best attractions to check out:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Beaver Island Historical Society: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Beaver Island. It features exhibits on the island’s Native American heritage, the Mormon kingdom that ruled the island in the 19th century, and the Irish settlers who came to the island in the 20th century.
  • Holy Cross Church: This beautiful church was built by Irish settlers on the island in the late 1800s. It features stained-glass windows and a wooden altar, and is a must-see for visitors interested in the island’s history.
  • St. James Marine Heritage Trail: This scenic trail takes you around the harbor of St. James and past several historical landmarks, including the remains of a lighthouse and an old shipwreck.

Natural Attractions of Beaver Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaver Island State Park: This park is located on the south end of the island and features a stunning beach, hiking trails, and picnic areas. It is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Beaver Island Birding Trail: This trail takes you through some of the island’s most scenic natural areas, where you can spot a variety of bird species, including bald eagles and osprey.
  • Garden Island State Recreation Area: This nearby island is accessible by boat and features stunning rock formations and secluded beaches. It is a popular spot for kayaking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beaver Island

Beaver Island is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with miles of trails that wind through forests, wetlands, and along the shoreline. Some of the best trails to check out include:

  • Beaver Island Rural Roads: These lightly traveled roads are perfect for exploring the island by bike. You can choose from a variety of routes that range in difficulty from easy to challenging.
  • Font Lake Trail: This scenic trail takes you through an old growth forest and past several small lakes. It is a popular spot for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.
  • Mount Pisgah Trail: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Mount Pisgah, which offers stunning views of the island and Lake Michigan.

Best Things to Do in Beaver Island: Activities and Tours

When you visit Beaver Island, there is no shortage of exciting activities and tours to try out. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Fishing: Beaver Island is a popular spot for fishing, with several charter boats available for hire. You can catch a variety of fish species, including salmon, lake trout, and smallmouth bass.
  • Kayaking and Stand-Up Paddleboarding: There are several rental shops on the island where you can rent kayaks and paddleboards to explore the shoreline and nearby islands.
  • Touring Historic Sites: There are several guided tours available that take you through the island’s historical landmarks, including the Mormon king’s house and the Holy Cross Cemetery.

Food and Drink Scene of Beaver Island

Beaver Island has a small but thriving food and drink scene, with several restaurants and bars that specialize in local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lake Michigan Whitefish: This local specialty is a must-try for seafood lovers. It is usually served broiled or fried and comes with a side of fries or coleslaw.
  • Pasties: This savory pastry is a popular dish among the island’s Scottish and Irish communities. It is filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables and is perfect for a quick lunch on the go.
  • Cherry Pie: Michigan is famous for its cherries, and Beaver Island is no exception. Be sure to try a slice of cherry pie, which is a popular dessert on the island.

Local Beverages

  • Beaver Island Brewery Beer: This small brewery produces several craft beers that are only available on the island. Be sure to stop by their taproom for a tasting.
  • Cherry Wine: Beaver Island is also known for its cherry wine, which is made by several local wineries. It is a sweet dessert wine that pairs well with cherry pie.

Where to Stay in Beaver Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beaver Island has several options for lodging, including hotels, motels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices to consider:

  • Beaver Island Lodge: This upscale hotel is located on the waterfront and features stunning views of Lake Michigan. It offers spacious rooms, an on-site restaurant, and beach access.
  • Beaver Island Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers. It features private and shared rooms, a communal kitchen, and free Wi-Fi.
  • The Happy Pallet Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of St. James and features colorful decor and a cozy atmosphere. It offers several rooms, each with a private bathroom, and a shared kitchen/living area.

What to Buy in Beaver Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique gifts and souvenirs to bring back from Beaver Island, here are some of the best local products to consider:

  • Cherry Jam: Cherry is a popular fruit in Michigan, and you can find several varieties of cherry jam and preserves at local stores and farm stands.
  • Beaver Island T-Shirts: There are several souvenir shops on the island that sell T-shirts and other clothing items with the Beaver Island logo.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are several local artisan shops on the island that sell handmade crafts, such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Beaver Island

Beaver Island has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Native American, Irish, and Scottish communities. Visitors to the island should respect the island’s customs and traditions, including its religious practices and fishing regulations.

Best Time to Visit Beaver Island

The best time to visit Beaver Island is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny. However, this is also the busiest time of the year, so be sure to book your lodging and activities in advance. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beaver Island

When visiting Beaver Island, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural resources and fishing regulations.
  • Take care of the beaches and forests by disposing of trash properly.
  • Be courteous to other visitors and locals.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the island’s religious traditions and practices.
  • Damage historical landmarks or cultural sites.
  • Disturb the local wildlife or ecosystems.

Safety and Health

When visiting Beaver Island, it’s important to be aware of any safety or health risks. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Beaver Island

  • Be aware of the potential for strong currents and undertows when swimming or boating in Lake Michigan.
  • Avoid hiking or biking alone in remote areas of the island.
  • Be cautious when driving on rural roads, which may be narrow and winding.

Health in Beaver Island

  • Be sure to bring any necessary medications with you, as there are limited medical facilities on the island.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and bug bites.
  • Be aware of the risk of Lyme disease, which is carried by ticks in the island’s forests and brush.

Getting to Beaver Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beaver Island is a remote destination, and getting there can be a bit of a challenge. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Beaver Island

  • Beaver Island Airport: This small airport is located on the north end of the island and is served by several commercial airlines and private charters.

Bus Stations Near Beaver Island

  • There are no bus stations on Beaver Island.

Other Options

  • Ferry: The Beaver Island Boat Company operates a regular ferry service between Charlevoix and Beaver Island. The trip takes about two hours each way and offers stunning views of Lake Michigan.

How to Get Around in Beaver Island

Once you arrive on Beaver Island, there are several options for getting around the island, including:

  • Car Rental: There are several car rental agencies on the island, which offer cars, trucks, and other vehicles for rent.
  • Bicycle Rental: You can also rent bicycles from several vendors on the island, which is a popular way to explore the rural roads and trails.
  • Taxi Service: There are several taxi companies on the island, which offer affordable transportation to popular destinations.

Where to Go Next from Beaver Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other destinations near Beaver Island, here are some nearby attractions to consider:

  • Charlevoix: This picturesque town is located on the mainland and features several historic buildings, parks, and beaches.
  • Mackinac Island: This nearby island is a popular destination for its historic forts, stunning scenery, and horse-drawn carriages.
  • Petticoat Junction: This nearby park features several hiking trails and a scenic overlook that offers stunning views of the island and Lake Michigan.

Beaver Island is a hidden gem located in the northern part of Lake Michigan. It offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, historical landmarks, and cultural heritage that make it a must-visit destination for travelers. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, historic sites, or delicious local cuisine, there’s something for everyone on this remote paradise.
