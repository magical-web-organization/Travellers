Introduction to Cheboygan

Located in the Mackinac Area of Northern Michigan, Cheboygan is a quaint town that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich history and natural beauty of the region. Surrounded by lush forests and crystal-clear waters, Cheboygan is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the area’s biking and hiking trails, fishing spots, and water recreation activities. The town is also home to several cultural attractions, historic landmarks, and local restaurants that serve up delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit and things to do in Cheboygan, Michigan.

List of Places to Visit in Cheboygan

There are several places to visit in Cheboygan that offer unique experiences and showcase the town’s diverse cultural and natural offerings. Here’s our list of must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cheboygan County Museum: Housed in a former Carnegie Library, this museum showcases the history and culture of Cheboygan County through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Burt Lake State Park: Located just a few miles outside of Cheboygan, Burt Lake State Park offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the region. The park features hiking trails, picnicking areas, and a beach that’s perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Historic Downtown Cheboygan: Take a stroll through the streets of Historic Downtown Cheboygan and admire the town’s beautiful architecture, unique shops, and charming cafes.

Natural Attractions of Cheboygan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cheboygan State Park: Covering over 1000 acres of land, Cheboygan State Park is a beautiful natural area that offers visitors a chance to explore the region’s diverse natural landscapes. The park features hiking and biking trails, swimming beaches, and picnic areas.
  • Black Mountain Recreation Area: Featuring over 30 miles of hiking and biking trails, the Black Mountain Recreation Area is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the rugged beauty of the region.
  • Lake Huron: The crystal-clear waters of Lake Huron offer endless opportunities for water recreation activities, including swimming, boating, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cheboygan

Cheboygan is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to explore the region’s natural beauty and picturesque views. Some of the best routes include:

  • North Central State Trail: This 62-mile trail runs from Gaylord to Mackinaw City, passing through the heart of Cheboygan and offering stunning views of the region’s natural beauty.
  • Black Mountain Pathway: This 9-mile trail takes visitors through the rugged terrain of the Black Mountain Recreation Area and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Cheboygan River Trail: This scenic trail follows the banks of the Cheboygan River and is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Cheboygan: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours to enjoy in Cheboygan. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Fishing: With over 10,000 acres of inland lakes and 40 miles of shoreline along Lake Huron, Cheboygan is a paradise for fishing enthusiasts. You can fish for trout, salmon, pike, and more in the region’s crystal-clear waters.
  • Sailing and Boating: The waters of Lake Huron and the surrounding inland lakes offer endless opportunities for sailing and boating. Rent a sailboat or motorboat and explore the area’s waterways on your own or take a guided tour.
  • Horseback Riding: Several local companies offer horseback riding tours that take visitors through the area’s beautiful forests and countryside.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Paddle your way through the tranquil waters of the inland lakes or the Cheboygan River on a kayaking or canoeing tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cheboygan

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Cheboygan, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Food and Drink Scene of Cheboygan

No trip to Cheboygan is complete without trying out some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties: This traditional meat pie is a staple in Northern Michigan and a popular dish in Cheboygan. Try a pasty at one of the local bakeries or restaurants.
  • Smoked Fish: With access to fresh fish from the Great Lakes, it’s no surprise that smoked fish is a popular local dish. Try smoked whitefish or trout in one of Cheboygan’s seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Mackinac Island Fudge Coffee: This locally roasted coffee is infused with the flavors of Mackinac Island fudge and is a must-try for coffee lovers.
  • Cheboygan Brewery beers: This local brewery makes several award-winning beers including the Blood Orange Honey Ale and the Lighthouse Amber.

Where to Stay in Cheboygan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cheboygan offers a variety of options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Best Western River Terrace: Located in downtown Cheboygan, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • HI - Mackinaw City Hostel: Just a short drive away from Cheboygan, this hostel offers affordable accommodation in a charming Victorian-style building.
  • The Beach House: This elegant guesthouse is located on the shores of Lake Huron and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding nature.

What to Buy in Cheboygan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home from your trip to Cheboygan, here are some great ideas:

  • Mackinac Island Fudge: This delicious fudge is a must-buy souvenir from the region and is sold in several local shops and bakeries.
  • Handmade Pottery: Cheboygan is home to several artisan potters who create unique and beautiful pieces of pottery that make great gifts or souvenirs.
  • Local Honey: Cheboygan is surrounded by beautiful forests and countryside, which makes it the perfect place for beekeeping. Several local farms sell their own fresh honey, which makes a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Cheboygan

Cheboygan is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are proud of their town’s rich history and natural beauty. Visitors are encouraged to take part in local traditions such as attending the Cheboygan County Fair and Horse Shows, which take place every year in August.

Best Time to Visit Cheboygan

The best time to visit Cheboygan is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the lakes and rivers are perfect for swimming and water activities. However, fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit when the leaves change colors and the area is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cheboygan

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Cheboygan:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the natural beauty: Cheboygan is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, so be sure to spend time exploring the forests, waterways, and parks.
  • Try local cuisine: Cheboygan is famous for its pasties and smoked fish, so make sure to sample some of the local dishes during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Cheboygan is a pristine natural area, so visitors are encouraged to leave no trace and dispose of their waste properly.
  • Take natural resources: Visitors should not take any natural resources, such as rocks or plants, from the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cheboygan

Cheboygan is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any other small town, such as locking their doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Cheboygan

Cheboygan has several medical facilities that offer quality care to visitors. Visitors should also take note of any allergies or medical conditions and carry any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Cheboygan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cheboygan

The closest airports to Cheboygan are Pellston Regional Airport (PLN) and Cherry Capital Airport (TVC).

Bus Stations Near Cheboygan

The closest bus station to Cheboygan is the Indian Trails Bus station in St. Ignace, which is approximately 30 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Cheboygan by car or train.

How to Get Around in Cheboygan

The best way to get around Cheboygan is by car or bike. There are several rental car companies in town, and several bike rental shops as well.

Where to Go Next from Cheboygan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cheboygan is located close to several other interesting destinations in the region. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Mackinac Island: This charming island is known for its historic landmarks and natural beauty and is just a short ferry ride away from Cheboygan.
  • Mackinaw City: Located just a few miles from Cheboygan, Mackinaw City is a charming town with several cultural attractions and historic landmarks.
  • The Upper Peninsula: Cheboygan is a great base for exploring Michigan’s Upper Peninsula, which is known for its rugged beauty and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Cheboygan is a charming town that offers visitors a chance to explore the rich history and natural beauty of Northern Michigan. With several cultural attractions, natural landmarks, and local restaurants, Cheboygan is a top destination for outdoor enthusiasts and cultural travelers alike. Plan your trip today and discover all that this beautiful town has to offer!
