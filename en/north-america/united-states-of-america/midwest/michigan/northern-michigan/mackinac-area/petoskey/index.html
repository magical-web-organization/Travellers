Introduction to Petoskey

Petoskey is a charming city located in the Mackinac Area of Northern Michigan. Known for its scenic beauty, rich cultural history, and ample outdoor activities, Petoskey is a popular vacation spot for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Petoskey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Little Traverse Historical Society - This museum offers exhibits and artifacts that showcase the area’s rich history.
  • Crooked Tree Arts Center - The center features art exhibitions and a performing arts theater.
  • Ernest Hemingway’s Summer Cottage - Visitors can tour the famous writer’s cottage and learn about his time spent in Petoskey.
  • Birchwood Farms Golf & Country Club - This private club has a golf course, tennis courts, and a beautiful clubhouse.

Natural Attractions of Petoskey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Petoskey State Park - This park boasts a sandy beach, hiking trails, and breathtaking views of Lake Michigan.
  • Bear River Valley Recreation Area - The park offers kayaking, fishing, hiking, and scenic views of the Bear River Valley.
  • Tunnel of Trees Scenic Byway - Take a leisurely drive down this scenic byway and admire the beauty of the lush trees and Lake Michigan shoreline.
  • Biking on the Little Traverse Wheelway Trail - This award-winning trail provides stunning views of the bay and lake with convenient access points in downtown Petoskey to make for a memorable ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petoskey

There are several beautiful hiking and biking routes in Petoskey, including:

  • The North Country Trail - This 4,600-mile trail passes through Petoskey and offers views of Lake Michigan.
  • Petoskey State Park - In addition to its beach, the state park features several hiking trails with beautiful scenery.
  • Little Traverse Wheelway Trail - this path runs along the shoreline providing spectacular views of Lake Michigan.
  • Bear River Valley Recreation Area - The park has various trails that give excellent views of the Bear River and the surrounding wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Petoskey: Activities and Tours

  • Odawa Casino - Try your luck at the casino with its table games, slot machines, and entertainment.
  • Sunset Sailing in Little Traverse Bay - A scenic sunset sail is a perfect way to end your day in Petoskey.
  • Petoskey Community Snowshoe and Hiking Club - Join this community club to explore the local trails and meet other outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Petoskey Farms Vineyard and Winery - This winery offers wine tasting events and live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Petoskey

Day 1: Explore downtown Petoskey and visit the Little Traverse Historical Society and Crooked Tree Arts Center.

Day 2: Spend the day at Petoskey State Park, and enjoy the beach and hiking trails.

Day 3: Bike down Little Traverse Wheelway Trail and take in the scenic views.

Day 4: Visit historic sites like Ernest Hemingway’s Summer Cottage and the Bear River Valley Recreation Area.

Day 5: Take a winery tour and taste local wines at Petoskey Farms Vineyard and Winery.

Day 6: Go to Odawa Casino or enjoy a sunset sail in Little Traverse Bay.

Day 7: Plan a hike with Petoskey Community Snowshoe and Hiking Club to say goodbye to Petoskey in a unique way.

Food and Drink Scene of Petoskey

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasty - A local dish made by filling a pastry shell with beef and other vegetables.
  • Whitefish - A fish cooked in various ways, popularly served with chips, and a variety of sides.
  • Fudge - You can’t go to Northern Michigan without trying one of the hundreds of fudge varieties found there.

Local Beverages

  • Great Lakes Coffee Roasting Company - The coffee shop serves sustainably-sourced coffee, tea, and baked goods.
  • Petoskey Brewing Company - The brewery produces unique beer flavors and a fun atmosphere.
  • Leelanau Peninsula Wine Trail - Located nearby Petoskey, Leelanau Peninsula has a wine trail with some of the state’s most excellent wines.

Where to Stay in Petoskey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Stafford’s Bay View Inn - A historic hotel located in the heart of Petoskey.
  • Holiday Inn Express - A modern hotel with excellent amenities and comfortable accommodations.
  • Terrace Inn and 1911 Restaurant - A historic hotel located on the banks of the Little Traverse Bay and offering authentic local dining experience.

What to Buy in Petoskey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Petoskey stones - A unique fossilized rock that is found along the shore of Lake Michigan.
  • Fudge - Do not leave Northern Michigan without trying some fantastic fudge flavors.
  • Local artist crafts - Various artists sell their crafts around Petoskey that showcase the heritage and beauty of Northern Michigan.

Local Culture and Customs of Petoskey

Petoskey is known for its friendly community and hospitality. Most residents believe in preserving the local heritage, following ecological norms, and working together to support the tourism industry.

Best Time to Visit Petoskey

The best time to visit Petoskey is during summer when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities in Petoskey flourish.

Do’s and Don’ts of Petoskey

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do keep the surroundings clean and recycle if possible.
  • Do walk or bike around town for some social and environmentally-friendly fun.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter anywhere and dispose of your trash properly.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers if you eat out.
  • Don’t violate public health guidance like COVID-19 regulations and restrictions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Petoskey

Petoskey is considered a relatively safe city. However, it is always good to stay aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions like locking doors, especially if you are a solo traveler.

Health in Petoskey

Petoskey has several healthcare facilities nearby, including hospital and clinics.

Getting to Petoskey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Petoskey

The nearest airports to Petoskey are Pellston Regional and Cherry Capital International.

Bus Stations Near Petoskey

Greyhound Bus provides services to Petoskey, with the station in downtown Petoskey.

Other Options

Petoskey has train networks with Amtrak trains going through nearby cities, but there are no direct trains to the town.

How to Get Around in Petoskey

The best way to explore Petoskey is by walking or biking as there are plenty of trails and paths in the area. There are local taxi services, and renting a car from nearby towns is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Petoskey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mackinac Island - A picturesque island located to the north of Petoskey, offers stunning natural beauty, colonial charm, and a strong historic legacy to explore.
  • Harbor Springs - A nearby town known for its natural beauty and Victorian charm with plenty of golf and water sport opportunities.
  • Tahquamenon Falls - A scenic waterfall located within the serene plains of the Upper Peninsula, it is a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Conclusion

Petoskey is a charming city located in Northern Michigan that has plenty to offer its tourists. From outdoor adventures and scenic views to preserving local culture and cuisine, Petoskey has something for everyone.
