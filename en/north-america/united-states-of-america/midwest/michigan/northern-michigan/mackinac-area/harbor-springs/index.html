The Ultimate Tour Guide for Harbor Springs, Michigan

Located in Northern Michigan, Harbor Springs is a quaint town with a stunning natural landscape and a rich cultural heritage. With plenty of outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, and local culture, Harbor Springs is a perfect destination for travelers who love to explore. If you are planning to visit Harbor Springs, this comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip with ease.

Introduction to Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs is a town in Mackinac Area, in Northern Michigan. Known for its natural beauty, Harbor Springs is nestled between Lake Michigan and Little Traverse Bay. The town has a population of approximately 1,100 people, and it has been a popular vacation spot since the late 19th century. With its charming downtown district, scenic waterfront, and numerous recreational opportunities, Harbor Springs is a perfect destination for travelers who love to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Harbor Springs

Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or cultural experiences, Harbor Springs has something for everyone. Here is a list of places to visit in Harbor Springs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harbor Springs History Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the town’s history, including its Native American heritage, Victorian-era architecture, and the Great Lakes shipping industry.

  • Andrew J. Blackbird Museum: Dedicated to the life and legacy of Andrew J. Blackbird, a prominent Native American leader who lived in Harbor Springs during the 19th century.

  • Little Traverse Bay Historical Society: Explore the local history of Harbor Springs and the surrounding area, including its Native American heritage, logging industry, and Victorian-era architecture.

Natural Attractions of Harbor Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Petoskey State Park: A popular destination for hiking, camping, and swimming, Petoskey State Park offers stunning views of Lake Michigan and plenty of outdoor activities for visitors.

  • Harbor Springs Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Michigan, Harbor Springs Beach offers crystal-clear water, white sand, and stunning views of the bay.

  • The Tunnel of Trees Scenic Drive: A 20-mile drive through a stunning tree-lined highway that offers breathtaking views of Lake Michigan and the surrounding forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and pathways that offer stunning views of the natural landscape. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Harbor Springs:

  • Little Traverse Wheelway: A 26-mile paved path that runs from Charlevoix to Harbor Springs along the shoreline of Lake Michigan and Little Traverse Bay.

  • Boyne Highlands: A popular ski resort that offers more than 20 miles of hiking and biking trails during the summer months.

  • Bear River Valley Recreation Area: A nature preserve with more than 300 acres of forest and wetlands that offer several hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Harbor Springs: Activities and Tours

Harbor Springs offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Harbor Springs:

  • Sailing or kayaking in Little Traverse Bay: Take a sailing lesson or rent a kayak and explore the bay with a local guide.

  • Horseback riding: Enjoy a scenic horseback ride through the forest with a local guide.

  • Wine tasting: Visit the local vineyards and wineries to taste some of the best wines Michigan has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harbor Springs

If you are planning to stay in Harbor Springs for a week, here is a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Explore downtown Harbor Springs and visit the Harbor Springs History Museum.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive along the Tunnel of Trees and visit local wineries.

Day 3: Visit Petoskey State Park and enjoy hiking, swimming, and picnicking.

Day 4: Take a sailing lesson or a kayaking tour in Little Traverse Bay.

Day 5: Visit local farms and experience life on a Michigan farm.

Day 6: Visit Andrew J. Blackbird Museum and learn about Native American history in Harbor Springs.

Day 7: Enjoy a scenic horseback ride through the forest.

Food and Drink Scene of Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs has a vibrant food and drink scene that focuses on locally sourced ingredients and rustic, authentic dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cherry butter: Made with local cherries, this spread is perfect for toast, muffins, or bagels.

  • Pasties: A traditional Michigan dish made with meat, vegetables, and a flaky crust.

  • Whitefish: Grilled, broiled, or fried, this freshwater fish is a staple in Michigan cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Michigan craft beer: With more than 300 breweries, Michigan is a prime destination for beer lovers.

  • Cherry wine: Made with local cherries, this wine is a refreshing summer beverage.

  • Hard cider: Made with local apples, hard cider has become a popular drink in Michigan.

Where to Stay in Harbor Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harbor Springs has plenty of options for lodging, depending on your budget and preferences. Whether you want to stay in a luxurious hotel, a cozy guesthouse, or a rustic cabin, Harbor Springs has something for everyone. Some of the best options for lodging in Harbor Springs include:

  • The Inn at Bay Harbor: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of Lake Michigan and Little Traverse Bay.

  • Trout Creek Condominium Resort: A family-friendly resort that offers a pool, tennis courts, and plenty of outdoor activities.

  • Colonial Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast with antique furnishings and a charming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Harbor Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Harbor Springs has plenty of options. Here are some of the best things to buy in Harbor Springs:

  • Cherry products: From dried cherries to cherry preserves, there are plenty of products made with local cherries.

  • Michigan maple syrup: Made with the state’s iconic trees, Michigan maple syrup is a delicious and unique gift.

  • Handcrafted items: From pottery to jewelry to furniture, there are plenty of locally made handcrafted items to choose from.

Local Culture and Customs of Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs has a rich cultural heritage that celebrates its Native American roots, Victorian-era architecture, and love of nature. Visitors to Harbor Springs should be respectful of the local customs and traditions while enjoying the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Harbor Springs

The best time to visit Harbor Springs is during the spring and summer months, from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harbor Springs

Do:

  • Respect local nature and wildlife while hiking and exploring.

  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

  • Support local businesses and artisans by purchasing locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beach or on hiking trails.

  • Ignore safety warnings and precautions.

  • Be disrespectful of the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors should also take precautions to protect themselves from ticks and mosquitoes, which can carry diseases.

Getting to Harbor Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Harbor Springs is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Harbor Springs

  • Pellston Regional Airport: Located 20 minutes from Harbor Springs, Pellston Regional Airport offers daily flights to Detroit and Chicago.

  • Cherry Capital Airport: Located in Traverse City, Cherry Capital Airport offers daily flights to major hubs such as Chicago, Detroit, and Minneapolis.

Bus Stations Near Harbor Springs

  • Indian Trails: A bus service that operates between Harbor Springs and major cities like Detroit and Chicago.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several car rental agencies operate near Harbor Springs, including Alamo, Avis, and Enterprise.

How to Get Around Harbor Springs

Harbor Springs is a relatively small town, and many attractions are within walking or biking distance. Visitors can also take a taxi, bus, or shuttle service to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Harbor Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harbor Springs is a great starting point for exploring other attractions in Northern Michigan. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Mackinac Island: Located just a few hours from Harbor Springs, Mackinac Island offers a charming Victorian-era atmosphere, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of outdoor recreation.

  • Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore: A national park located on the shores of Lake Michigan that offers hiking, camping, and stunning views of the sand dunes and the lake.

Conclusion

Harbor Springs is a beautiful destination with plenty of attractions and activities for visitors. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or local culture, Harbor Springs has something to offer. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip to Harbor Springs and enjoy all that Northern Michigan has to offer.
