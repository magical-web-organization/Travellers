Introduction to Alpena

Alpena is a hidden gem tucked away in Northern Michigan, surrounded by lakes, rivers and an abundance of natural beauty. Located in Alpena County, the city is full of historical buildings, museums, plenty of outdoor activities, a lively food scene, and warm and welcoming locals. It is the perfect destination for a family vacation, solo trip, or a romantic getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Alpena

Here is a list of must-visit places in Alpena:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alpena is home to a number of museums and historical sites that highlight the city’s rich culture and heritage. Some of these include:

  • Thunder Bay National Marine Sanctuary and Underwater Preserve: This is the largest freshwater marine sanctuary in the United States. Visitors can explore the exhibits at the Great Lakes Maritime Heritage Center or take a boat tour through the Thunder Bay National Marine Sanctuary.
  • Besser Museum for Northeast Michigan: The museum features exhibits on history, art, and science, including dinosaur skeletons, Native American artifacts, and local artworks.
  • Jesse Besser Museum: This museum showcases the life and accomplishments of Jesse Besser, an industrialist and philanthropist who played a major role in the development of Alpena.
  • Great Lakes Lore Maritime Museum: This is a small, but charming maritime museum with exhibits on the history and culture of the Great Lakes.

Natural Attractions of Alpena: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alpena is surrounded by some of the most incredible natural wonders in the country. Here are a few of the must-see outdoor attractions:

  • Island Park and Wildlife Sanctuary: This a beautiful park located on an island in the middle of the Thunder Bay River. It is the perfect place to take a leisurely walk, have a picnic or birdwatch.
  • Thompson’s Harbor State Park: This park is known for its sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and the incredible rock formations that line the shoreline.
  • Huron-Manistee National Forest: The forest is home to over 900,000 acres of preserved land, making it the perfect spot for hiking, camping, hunting, fishing, and other recreational activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alpena

Alpena is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with a number of scenic routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Some of the best trails include:

  • Chippewa Hills Pathway: It is a 9-mile trail that winds through the scenic Chippewa Hills.
  • Alpena Bi-Path: This 10-mile trail takes you through some of the most beautiful parks and scenic spots in Alpena.
  • The Iron Belle Trail: This trail is part of a statewide trail system that stretches over 2,000 miles. This trail takes you through the beautiful forests and wetlands of Northern Michigan.

Best Things to Do in Alpena: Activities and Tours

Alpena is full of exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do while in Alpena, Michigan:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Alpena has plenty of scenic waterways to explore by kayak or canoe. Rent a kayak or canoe and paddle your way through the serene waterways of Alpena.
  • Fishing: Alpena is home to some of the best freshwater fishing spots in the Great Lakes. Charter a boat and spend a day fishing for salmon, trout, and other species.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit one of the local vineyards and wineries around Alpena and sample some of the best wines in Northern Michigan.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour and visit some of the area’s top attractions, including the Thunder Bay National Marine Sanctuary, lighthouses, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alpena

Here’s a 7-day itinerary of what to do in Alpena, Michigan:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Thunder Bay National Marine Sanctuary and Underwater Preserve.
Day 2 Take a bike ride along the Alpena bi-path and visit Island Park.
Day 3 Go kayaking or canoeing on the Thunder Bay River and visit the Besser Museum for Northeast Michigan.
Day 4 Visit the Huron-Manistee National Forest and hike the Chippewa Hills Pathway.
Day 5 Head to the beach at Thompson’s Harbor State Park and take in the incredible scenery.
Day 6 Go fishing on Lake Huron or charter a boat for a day trip.
Day 7 Take a day trip to the historic town of Mackinaw City and explore the Mackinac Island State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Alpena

Alpena has a thriving food and drink scene with a number of local restaurants, breweries, and wineries. Here are a few of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips at the Fresh Palate: This is a popular dish in Alpena, and the Fresh Palate is known for serving some of the best fish and chips in the area.
  • Burger at JJ’s Steak and Pizza House: This local restaurant has a huge selection of burgers, including some unique creations like the “Bacon Jam Burger”.
  • Whitefish at the Black Sheep Pub & Brewery: This dish is made with locally sourced whitefish and is served with a house-made tartar sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Thunder Bay Winery: This winery specializes in producing cold-climate wines that are crafted using locally grown grapes.
  • Austin Brothers Beer Company: This brewery produces a wide range of unique and flavorful craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and porters.
  • Alpena Coffee Company: This coffee shop serves some of the best coffee in the city, and also offers a variety of teas and baked goods.

Where to Stay in Alpena: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alpena has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are a few of the best places to stay in Alpena:

  • Ramada by Wyndham Alpena: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Creekside Inn Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a restored Victorian home and offers a full breakfast every morning.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Alpena - Downtown: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown Alpena and offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary hot breakfast.

What to Buy in Alpena: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or local product to bring home, check out these places:

  • Alpena Farmers Market: This market features over 30 vendors selling locally grown produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.
  • Antler Art Studio: This studio features handmade antler art, including lamps, chandeliers, and furniture.
  • Downtown Alpena: Downtown Alpena is full of local shops selling unique souvenirs, clothing, and other items.

Local Culture and Customs of Alpena

Alpena is known for its warm and welcoming locals. The community is proud of its rich heritage and culture, and visitors can see this reflected in the city’s architecture, food, and art. The people of Alpena are also known for their love of the outdoors, and many residents enjoy hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Alpena

The best time to visit Alpena is during the summer months, between June and August, when temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Fall is also a great time to visit Alpena when the leaves on the trees begin to change colors and the weather remains mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alpena

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Alpena:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including fresh fish and locally sourced produce.
  • Do respect the natural environment and pack out what you pack in while hiking or spending time outdoors.
  • Do explore the local museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Don’t disturb the wildlife or remove any natural or historical artifacts from public lands.
  • Don’t forget to check the weather forecast and be prepared for changing conditions if you plan to spend time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alpena

Alpena is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their belongings. Emergency services are easily accessible throughout the city.

Health in Alpena

Alpena has a hospital and several clinics that offer medical care. Visitors are advised to have adequate travel insurance coverage in case of a medical emergency.

Getting to Alpena: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alpena is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Alpena

  • Alpena County Regional Airport
  • Cherry Capital Airport (Traverse City)

Bus Stations Near Alpena

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Indian Trails

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car at the Alpena County Regional Airport or in downtown Alpena.

How to Get Around in Alpena

Alpena is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot, bike, or car. Rental cars are available at the airport and in downtown Alpena. There are also a few taxi services and ride-sharing options available.

Where to Go Next from Alpena: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Northern Michigan, there are plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Mackinaw City: This historic town is known for its charming shops, restaurants, and the Mackinac Island State Park.
  • Traverse City: Located on the shores of Lake Michigan, Traverse City is known for its wineries, beaches, and outdoor activities.
  • Sleeping Bear Dunes: This national park is home to towering sand dunes, stunning views of Lake Michigan, and a wide range of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Alpena is a hidden gem in Northern Michigan, with plenty of natural beauty, historical landmarks, and exciting activities to explore. Whether you’re looking to hike in the forest, kayak on the river, or relax on the beach, Alpena has something for everyone. Make sure to try the local cuisine, visit the museums, and take in the warm and welcoming culture of this unique destination.
