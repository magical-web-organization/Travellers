Lansing Travel Guide - Discover the Best Things to Do in Michigan

If you’re planning a trip to Michigan, be sure to include a visit to Lansing on your itinerary. The state capital, located in the heart of the Midwest, offers a wealth of cultural, historical, and natural attractions to explore. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, see, eat, and experience in Lansing.

List of Places to Visit in Lansing

Lansing is home to a variety of must-see attractions, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Michigan Historical Museum: Learn about the rich history of Michigan, from its Native American and French colonial roots to its rise as a manufacturing powerhouse. The museum’s exhibits include artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays.
  • R.E. Olds Transportation Museum: Car enthusiasts won’t want to miss this collection of vintage cars, trucks, and other vehicles. The museum focuses on the contributions of Ransom E. Olds, a pioneer of the American automobile industry.
  • Lansing Art Gallery & Education Center: This contemporary art gallery features rotating exhibitions showcasing the work of local and regional artists.
  • Turner-Dodge House & Heritage Center: Tour this historic mansion, which dates back to the 19th century and was once home to two prominent Lansing families. The house is now a museum that offers a glimpse into life in Michigan during the Victorian era.

Natural Attractions of Lansing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fenner Nature Center: This 134-acre nature preserve features walking trails, wildlife exhibits, and educational programming for visitors of all ages.
  • River Trail: Walk, bike, or rollerblade along this scenic trail that runs alongside the Grand River and connects several parks and green spaces in Greater Lansing.
  • Potter Park Zoo: Get up close and personal with over 500 animals from around the world at this family-friendly zoo. Highlights include the Amur tiger exhibit, the red panda habitat, and the indoor butterfly garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lansing

Lansing is a great spot for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the top routes to check out include:

  • Lansing River Trail: This 13-mile trail runs from Dietrich Park in the north to Jolly Road in the south, with plenty of parks and attractions along the way.
  • Hawk Island Park: This park boasts a 1.5-mile trail that circles the lake, as well as picnic areas, a splash pad, and a sandy beach for swimming.

Best Things to Do in Lansing: Activities and Tours

In addition to museums and outdoor adventures, Lansing has plenty of other activities to keep visitors entertained. Some of the best include:

  • Michigan State Capitol: Take a guided tour of the state capitol building and learn about the history and architecture of this iconic Michigan landmark.
  • Wharton Center for Performing Arts: Catch a Broadway show, ballet performance, or concert at this acclaimed venue, which hosts a wide range of cultural events throughout the year.
  • Lansing Lugnuts Baseball: Cheer on the local minor league team, which plays home games at Cooley Law School Stadium in downtown Lansing.
  • Michigan Princess Riverboat: Take a leisurely cruise along the Grand River on this authentic paddlewheel boat, which offers lunch, dinner, and special event cruises.

Food and Drink Scene of Lansing

Lansing offers a diverse food and drink scene featuring everything from classic American fare to global cuisine. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken & Waffles at The Creole: This Southern-inspired dish features crispy fried chicken and fluffy waffles drizzled with maple syrup.
  • Coney Dogs at Lafayette Coney Island: This iconic Detroit classic can be found at several locations in Lansing, but Lafayette Coney Island is a local favorite.
  • Pasties at Roma Bakery & Deli: These savory hand pies filled with beef, onions, and potatoes are a staple of Upper Peninsula cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Founder’s Brewing Co. Beers: This Grand Rapids-based brewery offers a wide variety of award-winning beers, including the popular All Day IPA and Breakfast Stout.
  • Faygo Pop: This Detroit-made soda comes in over 30 different flavors, many of which can be found in Lansing grocery stores and restaurants.
  • Michigan Wine: The state’s thriving wine industry produces a range of delicious varietals, from crisp Rieslings to full-bodied Cabernets.

Where to Stay in Lansing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lansing has plenty of accommodation options to fit any budget. Some top choices include:

  • Radisson Hotel Lansing at the Capitol: This downtown hotel offers stylish rooms and suites, as well as an indoor pool, fitness center, and onsite restaurant.
  • Red Roof Inn Lansing East: This budget-friendly option features clean, comfortable rooms at an affordable price, as well as complimentary breakfast and free Wi-Fi.
  • The Wild Goose Inn Bed and Breakfast: This charming guesthouse dates back to 1912 and offers six elegantly appointed rooms, a gourmet breakfast, and a beautiful garden setting.

What to Buy in Lansing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to remember your trip to Lansing? Here are some of our top picks:

  • Michigan-shaped Cutting Board: Show off your love for the Mitten State with a cutting board shaped like Michigan from local boutique shop, Uniquely Michigan.
  • Sparrow Health System Honey: This honey is sourced from the hives on top of Sparrow Hospital and benefits the hospital’s foundation.
  • Vintage Michigan Travel Posters: Find unique artwork and posters celebrating Michigan’s stunning natural beauty at local shops like Absolute Gallery and the Michigan History Center Gift Shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Lansing

Lansing is a friendly and welcoming city, but there are a few local customs to be aware of. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Be Polite: Michiganders take pride in their Midwestern politeness, so always remember to say “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals.
  • Respect the Outdoors: Michigan is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, so be sure to leave any hiking trails or parks you visit better than when you arrived. Pack out what you pack in and respect the environment.
  • Embrace the Seasons: Michigan experiences all four seasons, from snowy winters to sunny summers, so be prepared for whatever weather you might encounter during your visit!

Best Time to Visit Lansing

The best time to visit Lansing depends on your preferences. Summers tend to be warm and sunny, with plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy. Spring and fall offer milder temperatures and colorful foliage, while winters can be chilly and snowy, but perfect for winter sports or a cozy cabin getaway. No matter which time of year you choose, Lansing offers something for all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lansing

Here are some quick do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Lansing:

Do:

  • Do try the Coney Dogs: Whether you’re at the original Lafayette Coney Island or one of the other locations around town, be sure to try this local classic.
  • Do take advantage of the River Trail: Whether you prefer to walk, bike, or rollerblade, the River Trail offers beautiful views of Lansing and its surrounding landscapes.
  • Do attend a Michigan State University sporting event: Whether you’re a fan of football, basketball, or hockey, there’s nothing quite like a game day on the MSU campus.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget your sunscreen: If you’re planning on spending time outdoors, be sure to pack sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Don’t litter: Be sure to leave any hiking trails or parks you visit better than when you arrived. Pack out what you pack in and respect the environment.
  • Don’t skip the Michigan Historical Museum: This museum offers fascinating insight into the rich history and culture of Michigan, from its Native American roots to its rise as a major industrial powerhouse.

Safety and Health

Lansing is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should still take standard precautions to stay healthy and safe during their trip.

Safety in Lansing

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: As with any urban area, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take standard safety precautions.
  • Lock your car doors: Keep your vehicle secure by locking the doors and keeping valuables out of sight.
  • Avoid shady areas at night: Stick to well-lit, pedestrian-friendly areas at night for safety.

Health in Lansing

  • Stay hydrated: With its warm summers and winter sports, staying hydrated is important for visitors to Lansing.
  • Check for ticks: If you’re planning on spending time in wooded areas, be sure to check for ticks and wear insect repellent.

Getting to Lansing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lansing is conveniently located in the heart of Michigan, making it easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Lansing

  • Capital Region International Airport (LAN): Located just 3 miles from downtown Lansing, the Capital Region International Airport offers nonstop flights to several major U.S. cities.

Bus Stations Near Lansing

  • Greyhound Station: Located in downtown Lansing, the Greyhound Station offers bus service to several major U.S. cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Lansing is located at the intersection of several major highways, making it easily accessible by car from nearby cities like Detroit, Grand Rapids, and Ann Arbor.
  • Amtrak: The nearest Amtrak station is in East Lansing, just a few miles from downtown Lansing. The station offers daily service to and from Chicago.

How to Get Around Lansing

Lansing is a pedestrian-friendly city with plenty of options for getting around, including:

  • Walking: Many of Lansing’s top attractions are located within walking distance of downtown.
  • Biking: Lansing offers miles of bike-friendly trails, including the popular River Trail.
  • Public Transit: The Capital Area Transit Authority (CATA) offers bus service throughout Greater Lansing.
  • Ride-Sharing: Services like Uber and Lyft are widely available in Lansing for convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Lansing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore beyond Lansing, there are plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting, including:

  • Grand Rapids: This bustling city, located just 60 miles west of Lansing, boasts a thriving art scene, award-winning breweries, and world-class museums.
  • Ann Arbor: Home to the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor offers a lively downtown, beautiful parks and gardens, and plenty of cultural events and performances.
  • Mackinac Island: Located about 250 miles north of Lansing, Mackinac Island is a must-visit destination for its scenic beauty, historic architecture, and famous fudge.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or food, Lansing has something for every type of visitor. With its friendly locals, beautiful scenery, and array of activities and attractions, Michigan’s capital is a must-visit destination for any traveler to the Midwest. Plan your trip today and discover all that Lansing has to offer!
