Alma, a charming city in Central Michigan, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions, Alma offers a unique travel experience for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll walk you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Alma

Located in Gratiot County, Alma has a population of approximately 9,000 people. The city is home to Alma College, a private, liberal arts college, and a vibrant downtown district with locally-owned businesses, restaurants, and shops.

List of Places to Visit in Alma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alma Public Library – Established in 1901, the library houses a collection of over 70,000 books and resources and hosts various events and activities throughout the year.
  • Marquis Museum – This museum showcases the history and heritage of Alma and features exhibits on the military, agriculture, and local sports.
  • Wright Opera House – Built in 1884, the opera house is a historic landmark and has hosted numerous performances and events over the years.

Natural Attractions of Alma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pine River Nature Center – A 30-acre nature preserve with walking trails, wildlife viewing, and educational programming.
  • Wright Park – A beautiful park with a playground, picnic facilities, and sports fields.
  • Alma Dam – A picturesque spot to fish, hike, and enjoy the outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alma

  • Fred Meijer Heartland Trail – A 42-mile long trail that connects Alma with other nearby cities and is ideal for hiking and biking.
  • Pine River Trail – A scenic trail that follows the river and offers opportunities for hiking, biking, and kayaking.

Best Things to Do in Alma: Activities and Tours

  • Alma Highland Festival and Games - A Scottish heritage festival held in May with bagpipes, Highland dancing, and athletic competitions.
  • Alma Farmers Market – A weekly summer market with locally-grown produce, crafts, and entertainment.
  • Artwalk Central – A month-long festival showcasing local art and artists in various downtown venues.

Food and Drink Scene of Alma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties – A hearty pastry filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables, brought to the area by Cornish immigrants.
  • Michigan Cherry Pie – A sweet dessert made with locally-grown cherries.

Local Beverages

  • Alma Brewing Company – A microbrewery that brews a selection of craft beers and serves pub-style food.
  • Art and Vineyard – A winery that produces a range of wines using locally-sourced grapes.

Where to Stay in Alma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Saravilla Bed and Breakfast – A historic mansion converted into a bed and breakfast with comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Alma Motel – A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Alma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Alma College Gear – Support the local college and get some Alma spirit wear.
  • Charlie Brown’s Christmas Tree Farm – Get a freshly-cut Christmas tree during the holiday season.

Local Culture and Customs of Alma

Alma is a tight-knit community that values family, tradition, and volunteerism. The city hosts various festivals and events throughout the year to bring people together and celebrate their heritage.

Best Time to Visit Alma

The best time to visit Alma is during the summer, when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are plentiful. However, the fall season is also popular for its scenic beauty and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alma

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit the local farmers market for fresh produce and homemade goods.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Pine River and surrounding areas.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore the local speed limits while driving.
  • Be disrespectful to local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alma

Alma is a generally safe city, and visitors should exercise normal precautions such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Alma

Alma has a hospital and medical clinics for routine medical needs, and emergency services are available in case of urgent medical issues.

Getting to Alma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alma

The closest major airport to Alma is MBS International Airport in Saginaw, Michigan, approximately a one-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Alma

Greyhound bus station is located in nearby Ithaca, Michigan, approximately 15 miles away from Alma.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Alma by car. It is located about 30 miles east of Mount Pleasant on Route 127.

How to Get Around in Alma

Alma is a small city and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also car rentals available for visitors who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Alma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mt. Pleasant – A city located 30 miles west of Alma with Central Michigan University and the Soaring Eagle Casino.
  • Midland – A city located 30 miles east of Alma with the Dow Gardens and Midland Center for the Arts.

Conclusion

Alma, Michigan, offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the outdoors or experiencing local culture, Alma has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to this charming city in Central Michigan.
