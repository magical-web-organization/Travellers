Introduction to East Lansing

Located in Central Michigan, East Lansing is a quintessential college town that uniquely blends the charm of a small town with the energy of a big city. It is home to the prestigious Michigan State University, known for its academic excellence and sports programs. With an abundance of cultural and natural attractions, East Lansing offers visitors a diverse range of experiences. This travel guide will help you explore the best of East Lansing, from must-visit places to exciting outdoor adventures and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in East Lansing

East Lansing has something for everyone, from museums and historical landmarks to scenic parks and hiking trails. Here are the top places you must visit in East Lansing:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East Lansing has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its museums and historical sites. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Michigan State University Museum: Discover the cultural and natural history of Michigan through the museum’s collections of artifacts, fossils, and specimens.
  • Broad Art Museum: Explore contemporary art exhibitions and events that push the boundaries of artistic expression.
  • The Eli and Edythe Broad Art Museum at Michigan State University: Witness stunning artwork and exhibitions from diverse cultures, timelines, and perspectives and an architectural to boot.
  • Fenner Nature Center: This urban nature center is dedicated to teaching visitors about the natural world. Explore the hiking trails and wildlife exhibits.

Natural Attractions of East Lansing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Lansing is home to picturesque parks, lakes, and gardens that offer visitors plentiful opportunities for outdoor recreation. Here are the top natural attractions:

  • Hawk Island Park: Enjoy a relaxing picnic, fishing, swimming on the beach or gliding in a paddleboat or kayak in one of the beautiful lakes.
  • Fitzgerald Park: This gorgeous park is best known for its hiking and biking trails, riverfront park, sand volleyball court, and playgrounds.
  • Michigan State University Horticulture Gardens: These stunning gardens showcase unique plant species and landscape designs unique to Eastern Michigan.
  • The River Trail: The trail runs along the riverbanks of Red Cedar and Grand rivers, offering scenic views and recreational opportunities like hiking, biking, kayaking, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Lansing

East Lansing boasts several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • The East Lansing Northern Tier Trail: This 6.5-mile trail connects the cities of East Lansing and Lansing and offers views of the Grand River.
  • The Lansing River Trail: This trail connects Michigan State University to Old Town Lansing and offers scenic views of the Grand River, parks, and Downtown Lansing.
  • The Okemos-to-Mason Trail: This 13-mile trail runs from Central Park in Okemos to Mason and goes through several parks and natural areas.

Best Things to Do in East Lansing: Activities and Tours

East Lansing has plenty of exciting activities and tours to explore. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • See a game at Spartan Stadium or Breslin Center
  • Explore East Lansing Farmer’s Market for seasonal produce and baked goods.
  • Visit the Ice Arena, home to Spartans Hockey
  • Tour the Wine Tasting room or the Beer Cellar at Spartan Cellars

Week-Long Itinerary for East Lansing

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary to explore the best of East Lansing:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Michigan State University, including the Broad Art Museum and the Beal Botanical Garden
Day 2 Explore the cultural and historical sites downtown, including The Michigan History Center and Fenner Nature Center
Day 3 Spend a relaxing day at Hawk Island Park, paddleboating, kayaking, and swimming beach
Day 4 Hike the trails of Fitzgerald Park, Grand Woods Park or Sleepy Hollow
Day 5 Enjoy a scenic drive to nearby Williamston and visit the Red Cedar River State Park
Day 6 Get active and explore the Northern Tier Trail by biking or walking
Day 7 Visit the Meridian Historical Village and take a horse-drawn wagon ride at Wildwood Family Farm

Food and Drink Scene of East Lansing

East Lansing offers plenty of dining and drinking options that cater to a variety of taste buds. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • MSU Dairy Store: Try the famous homemade ice cream from Michigan State University Dairy Store, a fan favorite.
  • HopCat: This craft beer bar and restaurant offer classic pub fare with exciting twists.
  • The Peanut Barrel: One of the oldest taverns in the city features their famous burger.

Local Beverages

  • Thunderstruck Java: Innovative coffee shop that offers premium quality coffee drinks, teas, and smoothies.
  • Spartan Cellars: Visit the cellar to try the award-winning Wines of Michigan State University and fantastic cheese boards in an inviting setting.
  • Lansing Brewing Company: A local microbrewery that serves high-quality beer and seasonal menu offerings.

Where to Stay in East Lansing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Lansing has a range of accommodation options to cater to a variety of budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in East Lansing:

  • Hyatt Place Lansing: Located in the heart of Eastwood Towne Centre, the hotel offers modern amenities and easy access to shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • East Lansing Marriott at University Place: This contemporary hotel offers a AAA Four Diamond experience and is located in the heart of the city.
  • Wild Goose Inn Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located within walking distance of Michigan State University and offers a cozy and personal experience.

What to Buy in East Lansing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Lansing has many local shops that offer unique souvenirs and products. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • MSU Surplus Store: Browse a selection of unique and affordable products, including art supplies, furniture, and electronics.
  • State of Michigan gift shop: Find the perfect souvenir to commemorate your trip to East Lansing, this shop feature handcrafted products made by Michigan Artists.
  • Horrocks Farm Market: This unique market offers a variety of products, including fresh produce, artisan cheeses, and handcrafted gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of East Lansing

East Lansing has a vibrant and welcoming culture that values diversity and inclusivity. Visitors should be mindful of the campus and student population and note that large crowds are common during home football game weekends in the fall season.

Best Time to Visit East Lansing

East Lansing is enjoyable year-round, but the best time to visit is typically from May to October. The weather is warm, and the city is buzzing with fun events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Lansing

Do:

  • Visit Michigan State University and learn about its history, culture, and academic programs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, especially the famous ice cream from MSU Dairy Store.
  • Explore the natural beauty of East Lansing by hiking and biking on the trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter in parks or public spaces.
  • Park in the wrong areas or drive under the influence of alcohol or other drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Lansing

East Lansing is generally a safe place to visit. Visitors should take the same safety precautions as they would in any other city, including being aware of their surroundings and avoiding dark or isolated areas at night.

Health in East Lansing

East Lansing has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality health care services. Visitors should carry adequate health insurance and make sure they have received all necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to East Lansing: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Lansing is well-connected to other cities in Michigan and the United States. Here are the transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near East Lansing

  • Capital Region International Airport: Located 15 miles away from East Lansing, This commercial airport offers flights throughout the United States.

Bus Stations Near East Lansing

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The bus station is located at 1240 S Harrison Rd, East Lansing, MI 48823. The station offers services throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • By Car: East Lansing is located near I-496, a major highway that connects the city to other parts of Michigan.

How to Get Around in East Lansing

East Lansing offers several options to get around the city:

  • Bus: The Capital Area Transportation Authority offers reliable and affordable bus services throughout the city and neighboring areas.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car to explore the city at their convenience or use ride-share services like Uber or Lyft to travel around town.
  • Biking and hiking: The city has a dense network of bike lanes and hiking trails that make it easy for visitors to explore.

Where to Go Next from East Lansing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Lansing is located in close proximity to many other destinations in Michigan. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Lansing: The capital city of Michigan is located just a few miles from East Lansing and offers plenty of cultural, recreational, and entertainment options.
  • Grand Rapids: A two-hour drive westward takes you to this vibrant city known for its art scene, dining options, and outdoor recreation activities.
  • Detroit: This bustling metropolis is located two and a half hours southeast of East Lansing and offers visitors a range of cultural and entertainment options.

Conclusion

East Lansing is a delightful destination that offers something for everyone. Its unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty makes it a must-visit location in Michigan. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit to East Lansing and explore its diverse attractions, try its delicious cuisine, and experience its vibrant culture.
