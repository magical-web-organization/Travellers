Introduction to Ithaca

Ithaca is a charming small town in Central Michigan, located in the heart of Gratiot County. Despite its small size, it offers a variety of attractions and activities that make it a great destination for tourists and locals alike. Here, you can explore fascinating museums, hike or bike scenic trails, and enjoy delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Ithaca is definitely worth a visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ithaca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gratiot County Historical Museum: This museum houses a variety of exhibits and artifacts that showcase the history of Gratiot County, including Native American artifacts, antique farm equipment, and local memorabilia.

  • Ithaca Area Historical Society: This historic building serves as a museum and research center for the Ithaca area. Here, you can learn about the town’s past, including its early pioneers, Civil War veterans and much more.

  • Charles W. Axford House: A former private residence, the Charles W. Axford House is now a museum that showcases the Victorian-era architecture and lifestyle of the Axford family.

  • Davidson Mill: This restored historic mill offers visitors an intriguing glimpse into a bygone era. You can tour the mill and learn about the history of grain milling as it was done a century ago.

Natural Attractions of Ithaca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maple River State Game Area: At this expansive park, you can hike, bike, or walk scenic trails that wind through forests and meadows.

  • Emerson Park: This spacious park features a wide range of recreational activities, from swimming and fishing in the nearby pond, to hiking and exploring the trails.

  • The River Walk: This beautiful paved walkway follows the winding path of the Maple River. Take a leisurely stroll or bike ride to enjoy the colorful foliage and wildlife along the way.

  • Pere Marquette Rail Trail: This popular trail spans over 30 miles, leading visitors through picturesque countryside and offering stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ithaca

  • Emerson Park Trail: This trail is perfect for beginners, with its gentle incline and relatively smooth surface. You’ll get to enjoy scenic views of the park’s landscape while enjoying a leisurely hike or bike ride.

  • Maple River State Game Area Trail: This trail boasts some of the most breathtaking views of the Ithaca landscape. The Maple River State Game Area Trail has a well-defined path that winds through forests, meadows, and over streams.

  • Pere Marquette Rail Trail: Stretching over 30 miles long, this trail is ideal for hikers and bikers. You’ll have plenty of opportunity to enjoy stunning scenic views of Gratiot County’s countryside while you’re on bike or foot.

Best Things to Do in Ithaca: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Farmers’ Market: For a taste of local culture, visit the Ithaca Farmers’ Market and enjoy fresh produce and locally-made goods.

  • Experience Small-Town Festivals: There are several festivals that take place throughout the year in Ithaca, including a Christmas parade and a fall festival.

  • Enjoy the Local Cuisine: Ithaca is known for its delicious eateries. Try some local specialties like Fried Chicken, Doughnuts, and Steak while you’re in town.

Food and Drink Scene of Ithaca

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: A local specialty in Ithaca, you can find this comfort food at many of the local restaurants.

  • Doughnuts: One of the best things to try on your visit to Ithaca is the freshly-made doughnuts. They are a local favorite and sold at numerous bakeries throughout the town.

  • Steak: Ithaca is known for its exceptional steaks, which are served at many of the restaurants and cafes in town.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Michigan is known for its craft beer, and Ithaca is no exception. Check out the local brewery and try some of their unique creations.

  • Wine: Michigan is also known for its wine, and Ithaca is home to several wineries. Take a tour or enjoy a tasting session.

Where to Stay in Ithaca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Alma Motel Ithaca: This cozy family-owned motel is centrally located within easy reach of downtown and the various attractions in Ithaca.

  • Ithaca Motel: This is another great option for those looking for a budget-friendly stay with easy access to the town’s attractions.

  • Super 8 Ithaca: This popular chain hotel is known for its affordable rates and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Ithaca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Maple syrup is a staple in Michigan, and Ithaca makes some of the best in the state. Bring home a bottle or two to enjoy on pancakes or waffles.

  • Artisanal Crafts: Local artisans make everything from wooden spoons to macrame hangings. Support the local community and take home a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ithaca

The people of Ithaca are welcoming and friendly. It’s a small close-knit community that values family, community, and small-town culture.

Best Time to Visit Ithaca

The best time to visit Ithaca is in the fall season from September to November, as the leaves turn into vibrant shades of orange and yellow, and there is an array of local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ithaca

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Ithaca is known for its delicious food. From Fried Chicken to Doughnuts, and Steak, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

  • Explore the great outdoors: With so many scenics trails and parks to explore in and around the town, Ithaca is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local culture: Be respectful and engage with locals during your visit to get a true sense of the town.

  • Litter: Keep the town clean by not littering and disposing of waste in designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ithaca

Ithaca is a relatively safe town. However, it’s always best to take the necessary safety precautions like avoiding walking alone in unfamiliar areas, especially at night, and keeping your belongings safe.

Health in Ithaca

Ithaca has health care facilities, pharmacies, and hospitals. It’s always important to have travel insurance to cover all medical contingencies.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are also available in Ithaca.

How to Get Around in Ithaca

The best way to get around Ithaca is by car or bike. You can easily rent a car or bring your own vehicle to explore the town at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ithaca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mount Pleasant: A charming college town, located approximately 25 miles southeast of Ithaca, offers an abundance of unique attractions like the Ziibiwing Center.
