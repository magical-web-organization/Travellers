Introduction to Fenton

Located in Central Michigan, Fenton is a charming city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. The city boasts of beautiful lakes, lush parks, and vibrant communities making it an ideal destination for anyone looking to relax and unwind. From exploring fascinating museums to embarking on scenic hikes, Fenton offers an array of experiences for every type of traveler. In this travel guide, we will take an in-depth look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay while in Fenton.

List of Places to Visit in Fenton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fenton Historical Museum - This museum is the perfect place to learn more about Fenton’s past. The museum features artifacts that showcase Fenton’s history from the early Native American and pioneer days to the present.

  • Fenton Fire Hall Museum - The Fenton Fire Hall Museum houses the history of firefighting in Fenton. The museum features antique fire engines and firefighting equipment, including clothing and helmets from the past.

Natural Attractions of Fenton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Linden County Park - Located on the banks of Lobdell Lake, Linden County Park is the go-to destination for those who love the great outdoors. The park is perfect for activities such as hiking, biking, picnicking, and fishing.

  • Dauner-Martin Nature Sanctuary - Dauner-Martin Nature Sanctuary is a 122-acre wonderland that is home to some of the most spectacular flora and fauna in Michigan. With 3 miles of hiking trails, visitors can enjoy the beauty of this natural gem on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fenton

Fenton offers an array of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy strolls to more challenging hikes that take you through the countryside, lakes, and parks. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Torn Shirt Trail - This 4.5-mile trail is perfect for hikers and bikers who love a good challenge. The route takes you through woods and fields, offering stunning views of the countryside and wildlife.

  • Lakeshore Park Trail - This easy 2.8-mile trail circles around beautiful Lake Fenton offering breathtaking views of the lake. This trail is perfect for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Fenton: Activities and Tours

  • Paddle and Pour - Experience the beauty of Fenton while kayaking on Lake Fenton with Paddle and Pour. Starting at $25 per person, visitors can rent kayaks and take a self-guided tour of the lake.

  • Fenton Winery and Brewery - Fenton Winery and Brewery is a great place to unwind and sample some of the finest wines and beers in the region. Visitors can take a guided tour of the vineyard and brewery and enjoy tastings of some of their best wines and beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fenton

Day 2: Hike Torn Shirt Trail

Day 5: Visit Dauner-Martin Nature Sanctuary

Food and Drink Scene of Fenton

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Laundry - The Laundry is a farm-to-table restaurant that serves some of the best dishes in Fenton. Make sure to try their famous Mac n Cheese and signature cocktails.

  • Fenton Fire Hall - Fenton Fire Hall is known for its delicious burgers and sandwiches. Make sure to try their signature dish, the Firehouse Burger.

Local Beverages

  • Fenton Winery and Brewery - Offering a selection of wines and craft beers, Fenton Winery and Brewery is a great place to relax after a day of exploring Fenton.

Where to Stay in Fenton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fairfield Inn and Suites by Marriott - Fairfield Inn and Suites by Marriott is a great choice for visitors looking for a comfortable and affordable stay in Fenton. The hotel offers free Wi-Fi and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Fenton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crust Fenton - Crust Fenton is a bakery that offers a wide selection of baked goods, including their signature Artisanal Bread. Make sure to stop by and pick up a loaf to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Fenton

Fenton is known for its friendly locals, beautiful scenery, and rich history. Visitors should expect a warm welcome and be respectful of the city’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Fenton

The best time to visit Fenton is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy all the outdoor activities that Fenton has to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fenton

Do:

  • Do try the famous burgers at Fenton Fire Hall

  • Do take a guided tour of Fenton Historical Museum

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and natural reserves

  • Don’t miss out on the scenic views of Fenton’s hiking trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Fenton

Fenton is a relatively safe city, with no significant crime concerns. Visitors should exercise basic safety precautions such as keeping an eye on their valuables and not leaving them in plain sight.

Health in Fenton

Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance and take the necessary precautions to avoid tick bites, which are common in wooded areas and parks.

Getting to Fenton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fenton

The nearest airports are Bishop International Airport in Flint (19 miles away) and Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport (63 miles away).

Bus Stations Near Fenton

Fenton is serviced by Greyhound Bus Lines, with a bus station located in Flint, 18 miles away.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in Fenton.

How to Get Around in Fenton

The best way to get around Fenton is by rental car, but visitors can also take the Flint Mass Transportation Authority bus, which serves Fenton and neighboring areas.

Where to Go Next from Fenton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ann Arbor - Located 50 miles southwest of Fenton, Ann Arbor is a vibrant college town known for its arts and culture scenes.

  • Detroit - Detroit, located 58 miles south of Fenton, is a thriving metropolis with an array of attractions, including the Motown Museum, the Detroit Institute of Arts, and the Detroit Zoo.

Conclusion

Fenton is a hidden gem in Central Michigan, offering an array of experiences for every type of traveler. Whether you’re into outdoor adventures or prefer to explore the history and culture of a city, you’ll find plenty to love in Fenton. From scenic hikes to delicious food and drink, Fenton is a destination worth exploring.
