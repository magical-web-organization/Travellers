Introduction to St. Louis

Located in the heart of the Midwest, St. Louis is a vibrant city known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and beautiful outdoor spaces. Home to the iconic Gateway Arch and the St. Louis Cardinals baseball team, the city offers a wide range of activities and experiences for visitors of all ages.

Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, hiking and biking through scenic parks, or sampling the local cuisine and drinks, there’s plenty to see and do in St. Louis. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the city’s top attractions, best hiking and biking routes, must-try food and drinks, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in St. Louis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Louis has a rich history that dates back to its founding in 1764. Visitors can explore the city’s cultural heritage at one of its many museums and historical sites, including:

  • Gateway Arch National Park: The most iconic attraction in St. Louis, the Gateway Arch offers stunning views of the Mississippi River and the city skyline. Visitors can ride to the top of the arch for panoramic views, or explore the museum and exhibits at the base of the arch.

  • Missouri History Museum: Located in Forest Park, the Missouri History Museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the state’s history. Don’t miss the museum’s 1904 World’s Fair exhibit, which highlights St. Louis’s role in hosting the famous event.

  • Saint Louis Art Museum: One of the top art museums in the country, the Saint Louis Art Museum features an impressive collection of art from around the world. The museum also offers free admission to its permanent collection.

  • Anheuser-Busch Brewery: Founded in 1852, the Anheuser-Busch Brewery is a St. Louis institution. Visitors can take a guided tour of the brewery to learn about the brewing process and sample some of the company’s famous beers.

Natural Attractions of St. Louis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St. Louis is home to a number of beautiful parks and outdoor spaces that offer plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and scenic views. Some of the top natural attractions in the city include:

  • Forest Park: One of the largest urban parks in the United States, Forest Park is home to the Saint Louis Art Museum, the Missouri History Museum, and the Saint Louis Zoo. Visitors can also hike or bike through the park’s scenic trails.

  • Mississippi River Greenway Trail: This 15-mile trail follows the Mississippi River and offers stunning views of the water and the St. Louis skyline. The trail is popular with cyclists, walkers, and runners.

  • Tower Grove Park: Located south of downtown, Tower Grove Park is a beautifully landscaped park that features a rose garden, fountains, and a Victorian greenhouse. Visitors can stroll through the park’s many gardens or take a picnic by the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Louis

St. Louis offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the city’s beautiful parks and natural areas. Some of the top routes include:

  • Meramec River Greenway Trail: This 10-mile trail follows the Meramec River and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding forest. The trail is popular with cyclists, walkers, and runners.

  • Grant’s Trail: This 8-mile trail runs from Kirkwood to Crestwood and offers a scenic path through suburban St. Louis. Visitors can bike or walk the trail and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

  • Castlewood State Park Trails: This popular state park offers several hiking and biking trails that wind through the surrounding hills and forests. Hikers can enjoy stunning views of the Meramec River and the park’s many rock formations.

Best Things to Do in St. Louis: Activities and Tours

In addition to its museums, parks, and natural attractions, St. Louis offers a wide range of activities and tours that are perfect for visitors of all ages. Some of the top things to do include:

  • St. Louis Cardinals Game: Baseball fans won’t want to miss a game at Busch Stadium, home of the St. Louis Cardinals. Visitors can enjoy a classic American pastime while taking in the beautiful views of the city skyline.

  • City Tours: Visitors can explore St. Louis on foot, by bike, or by Segway with one of the city’s many guided tours. Popular tours include the Gateway Arch Riverfront Cruise and the Historic St. Louis Bike Tour.

  • City Museum: This unique museum offers a fun and interactive experience for visitors of all ages. Housed in a former shoe factory, the museum features mazes, slides, and other interactive exhibits that are perfect for families.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Louis

Day 1: Gateway Arch National Park and Museum

Start your trip to St. Louis with a visit to the Gateway Arch National Park. Ride to the top of the arch for stunning views of the city, then explore the museum and exhibits at the base.

Day 2: Forest Park

Spend the day exploring Forest Park, home to the Saint Louis Art Museum, the Missouri History Museum, and the Saint Louis Zoo. Hike or bike through the park’s scenic trails and enjoy a picnic lunch by the lake.

Day 3: Anheuser-Busch Brewery and City Museum

Take a guided tour of the Anheuser-Busch Brewery to learn about the brewing process and sample some of the company’s famous beers. In the afternoon, head to the City Museum to enjoy its interactive exhibits and activities.

Day 4: Tower Grove Park and St. Louis Science Center

Spend the morning exploring Tower Grove Park and its many gardens and attractions. In the afternoon, head to the St. Louis Science Center for an interactive and educational experience.

Day 5: Mississippi River Greenway Trail and Grant’s Farm

Bike or walk the Mississippi River Greenway Trail for stunning views of the river and the city skyline. In the afternoon, visit Grant’s Farm, a historic farm that features a petting zoo and tours of its beautiful grounds.

Day 6: City Tours and Shopping

Take a guided tour of the city to explore its many landmarks and attractions. Afterward, spend some time shopping in St. Louis’s many boutiques and shops.

Day 7: St. Louis Cardinals Game and Dining Out

End your trip to St. Louis with a classic American pastime – a St. Louis Cardinals game at Busch Stadium. Afterward, enjoy dinner at one of the city’s many world-class restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Louis

Must-Try Dishes

St. Louis has a unique food scene that blends traditional Midwestern fare with Cajun and Southern influences. Some of the must-try dishes in the city include:

  • Toasted Ravioli: A St. Louis classic, toasted ravioli features battered and deep-fried ravioli served with marinara sauce.

  • St. Louis-style Pizza: This distinctive pizza features a cracker-thin crust and is topped with Provel cheese, a blend of cheddar, Swiss, and provolone.

  • Gooey Butter Cake: A sweet and indulgent dessert, Gooey Butter Cake features a yellow cake crust topped with a gooey, buttery filling.

Local Beverages

St. Louis is also known for its local beers and beverages. Some of the top drinks to try in the city include:

  • Anheuser-Busch Beers: St. Louis is home to Anheuser-Busch Brewery, which produces several popular beer brands, including Budweiser and Busch.

  • Cocktails: St. Louis has a thriving cocktail scene, with many bars and restaurants offering unique and creative cocktail menus.

Where to Stay in St. Louis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Louis has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the top places to stay in the city include:

  • The Ritz-Carlton: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the Gateway Arch and features a spa, fitness center, and upscale dining options.

  • Hotel Saint Louis, Autograph Collection: A historic hotel located in downtown St. Louis, Hotel Saint Louis features elegant rooms and suites, a rooftop bar, and a fitness center.

  • St. Louis Union Station Hotel, Curio Collection by Hilton: Housed in a beautifully restored train station, this hotel offers unique and historic accommodations, as well as a pool and several dining options.

What to Buy in St. Louis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to St. Louis can bring home a wide range of souvenirs and local products that showcase the city’s history and culture. Some of the top items to buy include:

  • Baseball gear: Show your support for the St. Louis Cardinals with a team hat, shirt, or jersey.

  • Missouri wines: The state of Missouri is known for its excellent wines, and St. Louis has several wineries that offer tastings and tours.

  • Toasted ravioli kits: Bring home a taste of St. Louis with a toasted ravioli kit, featuring all the ingredients you need to make the city’s signature dish.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Louis

St. Louis has a friendly and welcoming culture that values family, community, and tradition. Visitors to the city should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as addressing elders with respect and avoiding public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit St. Louis

The best time to visit St. Louis is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Louis

Do:

  • Visit the Gateway Arch: No trip to St. Louis is complete without a visit to the iconic Gateway Arch.

  • Explore the parks and natural areas: St. Louis is home to many beautiful parks and natural areas that offer plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and scenic views.

  • Try the local dishes and beverages: From toasted ravioli to Gooey Butter Cake to local beers, St. Louis has a unique food and drink scene that’s a must-try for visitors.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: As with most places in the United States, it’s customary to tip at restaurants, bars, and other service establishments in St. Louis.

  • Be disrespectful of local customs: St. Louis has a friendly and welcoming culture, but visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Louis

Like any large city, St. Louis has its share of crime and safety concerns. Visitors should take precautions such as staying in well-lit areas, avoiding isolated areas at night, and not leaving valuables unattended in their vehicles.

Health in St. Louis

St. Louis has a good healthcare system and several hospitals and clinics. Visitors should be aware of any health concerns and take necessary precautions, such as bringing an adequate supply of medication.

Getting to St. Louis: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Louis is easily accessible by air, bus, and car. Some of the top transportation options include:

  • Lambert-St. Louis International Airport: Located just 14 miles from downtown, Lambert-St. Louis International Airport offers daily flights to and from many major cities.

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Greyhound Bus Station is located in downtown St. Louis and offers bus service to many nearby cities.

  • Car: St. Louis is easily accessible by car, with several major highways and interstates leading to the city.

How to Get Around in St. Louis

St. Louis has a number of transportation options for visitors, including:

  • Metrolink: St. Louis’s light rail system is a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.

  • Buses: The city’s bus system covers many areas of St. Louis and its suburbs.

  • Bikes and Scooters: Visitors can rent bikes and scooters through several companies, including Lime and Bird.

Where to Go Next from St. Louis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Louis is located in the heart of the Midwest and is within driving distance of many nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the top places to visit include:

  • Kansas City: Located just four hours west of St. Louis, Kansas City is known for its barbecue, jazz music, and historic landmarks.

  • Springfield, Illinois: The hometown of Abraham Lincoln, Springfield is located two hours northeast of St. Louis and offers many historic sites and museums.

  • Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Located three hours south of St. Louis, this beautiful park features stunning waterways and scenic views.

Conclusion

St. Louis is a dynamic and exciting city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring its rich history and culture, hiking and biking through its beautiful parks, or sampling its unique cuisine and drinks, there’s plenty to see and do in St. Louis. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this iconic city.
