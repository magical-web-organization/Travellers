Introduction to Upper Peninsula

Located in the northern region of Michigan, the Upper Peninsula (also known as UP) is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. It is home to stunning natural wonders, charming small towns, and a rich cultural heritage that makes it a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to the Midwest. The Upper Peninsula is also known for its friendly people, unique cuisine, and fascinating history.

List of Places to Visit in Upper Peninsula

The Upper Peninsula is divided into three main regions: Eastern UP, Central UP, and Western UP, each of which has its own attractions worth exploring. Here is a list of must-visit places in the Upper Peninsula.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mackinac Island - Known for its well-preserved Victorian architecture and horse-drawn carriages, the island is a living museum that takes visitors back in time.
  • Marquette Maritime Museum - This museum provides information about Marquette’s maritime history and a lighthouse tour.
  • Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore - The lakeshores contains an array of vivid color.
  • Historic Downtown Sault Ste. Marie - A historic downtown bustles with life, this is also the third oldest city in the US.
  • Tahquamenon Falls State Park - An opportunity to see one of the largest waterfall systems east of the Mississippi River and hike the surrounding trails.
  • The Great Lakes Shipwreck Museum - A museum dedicated to preserving the memory of the significant shipwrecks in the Great Lakes.

Natural Attractions of Upper Peninsula: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isle Royale National Park - This nature reserve is a paradise for hikers, wildlife enthusiasts, and kayakers.
  • Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park - The park is famous for its old-growth forests, scenic waterfalls, and challenging hiking trails.
  • Kitch-iti-kipi - This jade-colored natural pool is located inside the Palms Book State Park and provides visitors with a rare glimpse of underwater life.
  • Seney National Wildlife Refuge - This wildlife refuge is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including bald eagles, bears, and minks.
  • Sleeping Bear Dunes - The breathtaking dunes provide ample opportunity for hiking, biking, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upper Peninsula

The Upper Peninsula boasts an extensive network of trails that offers hikers and bikers the opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some notable trails:

  • North Country Trail - A network of 4,600 miles long trail, leading through eight states including a substantial section in the Upper Peninsula.
  • High Country Pathway - A 80-mile circular trail that includes scenic overlooks, quiet river landings, and rustic campsites along the way.
  • Iron Ore Heritage Trail - This 47-mile trail offers mountain bikers a chance to see the beautiful natural landscapes of the Upper Peninsula.

Best Things to Do in Upper Peninsula: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing - The Upper Peninsula is known for its pristine lakes and rivers and that attracts fishers from all over the world.
  • Snowmobiling - The Upper Peninsula is an excellent region for snowmobiling due to the heavy snowfall during the winters.
  • Kayaking - The lakeshores and rivers of the Upper Peninsula provide a spectacular view of the region from the water.
  • Northern Lights Tour - Climb to a secluded location where you have an excellent view of the night sky and light pollution is reduced.

Week-Long Itinerary for Upper Peninsula

Day 1-2: Mackinac Island Day 3-4: Sault Ste. Marie, Tahquamenon Falls State Park Day 5-6: Marquette, Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore Day 7: Sleeping Bear Dunes

Food and Drink Scene of Upper Peninsula

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasty - A savory tart with a flaky crust filled with potatoes, meats, and vegetables
  • Whitefish - Freshly caught from the Great Lakes, whitefish is a staple of Upper Peninsula cuisine.
  • Fudge - The region is famous for its creamy, high-quality fudge.

Local Beverages

  • Upper Peninsula Craft Beer - Beer aficionados will delight in the numerous breweries located across the Upper Peninsula.
  • Mackinac Island’s Lilac Wine - A unique wine made from the lilac flowers that bloom on Mackinac Island in the spring.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper Peninsula

The Upper Peninsula is known for its friendly, laid-back culture, where people place great value on loyalty to family, community, and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the customs of the region, such as the importance of hunting and fishing to the local economy.

Best Time to Visit Upper Peninsula

Summer and fall are the ideal time to visit the Upper Peninsula due to the mild weather and abundant outdoor activities. For winter sports enthusiasts, winter offers many snowmobiling and skiing opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper Peninsula

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Pack the appropriate outdoor gear for the season
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the natural resources of the Upper Peninsula
  • Ignore road signs and regulations
  • Trespass on privately owned lands without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper Peninsula

The Upper Peninsula is a relatively safe region, although it is essential to keep in mind precautions when exploring the wilderness. Visitors should carry appropriate gear and have a plan for emergencies.

Health in Upper Peninsula

The healthcare system in the Upper Peninsula is excellent, with hospitals and clinics located in urban centers.

How to Get Around in Upper Peninsula

The best way to explore Upper Peninsula is by car. There are also public transport options depending on the locations, rideshare apps are also available in more populated areas.

Where to Go Next from Upper Peninsula: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Upper Peninsula is located in the Midwest region of the United States, giving visitors the opportunity to explore nearby destinations.

  • Green Bay, Wisconsin - Known for its sports teams and cheese, Green Bay makes for an exciting day trip.
  • Traverse City, Michigan - Known as the Cherry Capital of the World, Traverse City is located about 3 hours south of Mackinaw Island.
  • Door County, Wisconsin - Located on the peninsula that separates Lake Michigan from Green Bay, Door County offers stunning wilderness areas, charming small towns, and a vibrant arts scene.

Conclusion

The Upper Peninsula is a hidden gem in the North American Midwest, with a wealth of natural and cultural attractions that are sure to keep visitors entertained. From stunning scenery to delicious food, friendly people to unique shopping opportunities, the Upper Peninsula truly has something for everyone. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or history buff, the Upper Peninsula is a must-see destination that should not be missed.
