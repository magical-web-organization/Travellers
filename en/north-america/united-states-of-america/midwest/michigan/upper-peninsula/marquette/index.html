Introduction to Marquette

Marquette is a charming city located in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, USA. Boasting a rich history and stunning natural beauty, it is a great destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and small-town charm. With a variety of things to see and do, Marquette offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Marquette

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marquette has a wealth of museums and historical sites that are worth a visit. Here are some of the most notable:

  • Marquette Maritime Museum: Located on the shores of Lake Superior, this museum showcases the region’s rich maritime history.
  • Marquette Regional History Center: This museum offers exhibits on the history of the Upper Peninsula, as well as local art and culture.
  • Father Marquette Park: This park is home to a statue of Father Jacques Marquette, the famous Jesuit missionary who explored the area in the 17th century.
  • Iron Ore Heritage Trail: This trail follows the route of the historic iron ore industry, which played a vital role in the local economy in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Marquette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Marquette is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Presque Isle Park: This park, located on a peninsula in Lake Superior, offers hiking trails, a beach, and stunning views of the lake.
  • Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore: Located about an hour’s drive from Marquette, this park boasts spectacular natural rock formations, towering sand dunes, and pristine beaches.
  • Sugarloaf Mountain: Hiking to the summit of Sugarloaf Mountain rewards visitors with panoramic views of Lake Superior and Marquette.
  • Lakenenland Sculpture Park: This unique outdoor park features quirky sculptures made from scrap metal, set amidst the backdrop of the Upper Peninsula’s forests and wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marquette

Marquette is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Noquemanon Trail Network: This extensive trail system covers more than 100 miles and includes everything from easy family-friendly routes to challenging backcountry adventures.
  • Dead River Falls: This trail offers stunning views of waterfalls and the Dead River, and is accessible year-round.
  • Iron Ore Heritage Trail: This 47-mile trail follows the historic route of the iron ore industry, offering scenic views along the way.
  • Harlow Lake Loop Trail: This moderate 8-mile loop trail takes hikers through scenic forests and around Harlow Lake.

Best Things to Do in Marquette: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Marquette. Here are some top picks:

  • Kayaking: Paddling on Lake Superior or one of the area’s streams is a popular activity, and a great way to experience the beauty of the landscape.
  • Fishing: Whether you’re hoping to catch trout, salmon, or other fish, Marquette offers plenty of opportunities for fishing enthusiasts.
  • City Tour: Take a guided tour of Marquette to learn more about its history and see the city’s top sights.
  • Winter Sports: In the winter months, Marquette becomes a playground for snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and sledding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marquette

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Marquette, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the city, visiting museums and historical sites like the Marquette Maritime Museum and the Iron Ore Heritage Trail.
  • Day 3-4: Get out into nature, hiking trails like Presque Isle Park and Sugarloaf Mountain.
  • Day 5: Take a kayaking or fishing trip on Lake Superior or one of the area’s streams.
  • Day 6-7: Enjoy winter activities, like snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, or sledding.

Food and Drink Scene of Marquette

Must-Try Dishes

Marquette has a vibrant food scene, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Pasty: This savory hand-held pie is a local favorite, and can be filled with a variety of meats and vegetables.
  • Whitefish: Caught fresh from Lake Superior, whitefish is a delicacy in Marquette.
  • Wild rice: A staple of Native American cuisine, wild rice is incorporated into a variety of dishes.
  • Smoked fish: Whether it’s trout or whitefish, Marquette’s smoked fish is renowned for its flavor.

Local Beverages

Marquette is home to a number of breweries and wineries, offering a variety of locally-crafted beverages. Here are some to check out:

  • Blackrocks Brewery: This brewery focuses on small-batch, creative beers with unique flavors.
  • Ore Dock Brewing Company: Offering a variety of American-style beers, this brewery has a spacious taproom with stunning lake views.
  • Marquette Harbor Brewery & Restaurant: This microbrewery offers a variety of beers along with a menu of pub fare.
  • Northern Sun Winery: This winery specializes in cold-hardy grape varieties, producing wines that reflect the unique climate of the Upper Peninsula.

Where to Stay in Marquette: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marquette offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget. Here are some options:

  • Landmark Inn: This historic hotel, located in the heart of downtown Marquette, offers elegant rooms and modern amenities.
  • StayHI Hostel: Budget travelers can opt for a bed in this clean and comfortable hostel, located in a renovated school building.
  • The Beach Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers basic amenities and lakefront views.
  • Roam Inn: This stylish boutique hotel is located in a historic building and offers amenities like a hot tub and fireplace suites.

What to Buy in Marquette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and unique products to bring home from your Marquette trip? Here are some ideas:

  • Map of the Upper Peninsula: Bring home a map of the area to serve as a reminder of your visit and help plan future trips.
  • Upper Peninsula-themed clothing: Show off your love for the Upper Peninsula with t-shirts or hats featuring the distinctive shape of the region.
  • Local art and crafts: Browse the galleries and studios in downtown Marquette to find unique pieces by local artists and craftspeople.
  • Pasties: Packaged pasties make a great snack or meal on the go, and are a staple of the local cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Marquette

Marquette has a distinct culture and customs influenced by its history, natural beauty, and remote location. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect for nature: Marquette residents take pride in their natural surroundings, and it’s important to show respect for the environment by following Leave No Trace principles.
  • Politeness and hospitality: Marquette residents are known for their friendly and welcoming demeanor, and visitors should strive to reciprocate this hospitality.
  • Winter sports: With long, snowy winters, winter sports are an important part of life in Marquette, and many locals participate in activities like skiing and snowshoeing.

Best Time to Visit Marquette

The best time to visit Marquette depends on what you’re interested in. Here’s a breakdown by season:

  • Summer (June-August): This is the most popular time to visit, with warm temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.
  • Fall (September-November): The fall colors in Marquette are spectacular, making this a great time for hiking and biking.
  • Winter (December-February): Winter sports enthusiasts will love the snowy conditions, and the city’s winter festivals offer plenty of fun activities.
  • Spring (March-May): While still chilly, the spring offers the opportunity to see maple syrup-making and other seasonal activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marquette

Do:

  • Do respect local customs and traditions, including a love for nature and the outdoors.
  • Do visit local breweries and wineries to try unique Upper Peninsula beverages.
  • Do take advantage of the many hiking and biking trails to explore the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind while hiking or camping.
  • Don’t assume that everything is open year-round;
  • Don’t underestimate the power of the lake, with changing weather conditions, be sure to exercise caution when swimming or boating.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marquette

Marquette is known for its low crime rate and friendly, safe atmosphere. However, there are still some precautions to take:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid venturing into isolated areas alone.
  • Listen to weather reports and be prepared for changes in weather conditions.
  • When hiking, stay on marked trails and bring water and first aid supplies.

Health in Marquette

Marquette has several medical facilities, including a hospital and urgent care clinics, in case of emergency. It’s also important to take precautions against tick-borne illnesses like Lyme disease, especially if spending time in wooded areas.

Getting to Marquette: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marquette

The nearest airport to Marquette is Sawyer International Airport, located about a 25-minute drive south of the city. It offers daily flights to/from major cities in the Midwest. Alternatively, Green Bay Austin Straubel International, and Minneapolis-St. Paul International airports offer additional flight connections for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Marquette

Greyhound offers service to Marquette, with a bus station located in the city center. Other transportation companies also provide bus services to and from the nearby towns.

Other Options

Those traveling from the nearby towns can rent a car, or drive to Marquette. The city is located at the intersection of US-41 and M-28, and is easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Marquette

Marquette is a compact city that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi services, as well as car and bike rentals available.

Where to Go Next from Marquette: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore: Located about an hour’s drive from Marquette, this park offers stunning natural beauty and hiking opportunities.
  • Mackinac Island: About a three-hour drive from Marquette, this historic island offers the chance to step back in time and enjoy the quintessential small-town charm of Michigan.
  • Sault Ste. Marie: Located on the border between Michigan and Canada, this historic town offers a glimpse into the region’s rich history.
  • Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park: Located about a two-hour drive from Marquette, these mountains offer a rugged wilderness experience with spectacular views.

Conclusion

Marquette is a destination that’s great for any traveler. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and outdoor recreation opportunities, it’s a must-visit spot in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan. Use our comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Marquette.
