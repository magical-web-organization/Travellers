Introduction to Isle Royale National Park

Isle Royale National Park is a remote island in Upper Peninsula, Michigan. It is the largest island in Lake Superior and the only national park in Michigan. The park attracts visitors who are seeking adventure and natural beauty. The island is not accessible by car and can only be reached by ferry or seaplane.

The park has a rich cultural and natural history and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including wolves and moose. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, kayaking, and many other outdoor activities. The island offers breathtaking views of the lake and is an ideal destination for those who want to explore the great outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Isle Royale National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rock Harbor Lighthouse: This lighthouse was built in 1855 and is located at the entrance of Rock Harbor on the eastern side of the island.
  • Edisen Fishery: This is the oldest fishery on the island and was established in the 1820s.
  • Windigo Ranger Station: This station is the starting and ending point for many hiking trails and is located on the west side of the island.

Natural Attractions of Isle Royale National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scoville Point: A scenic overlook that provides panoramic views of Lake Superior.
  • Mount Ojibway: The highest peak on the island that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Rock Harbor Trail: A 11-mile long trail that offers beautiful views of the lake and the forests.
  • Tobin Harbor: A quiet bay that is perfect for kayaking and fishing.
  • Greenstone Ridge Trail: A 40-mile long trail that crosses the island and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isle Royale National Park

Isle Royale National Park is known for its hiking and biking trails. Visitors can experience the rugged wilderness of the island by exploring the trails. The trails range from easy to strenuous and offer a range of experiences.

Hiking Trails

  • Minong Ridge Trail: A 28-mile long trail that crosses the island from east to west.
  • Feldtmann Lake Trail: A 15-mile long trail that leads to lakes and beaches.
  • Rock Harbor Trail: A 11-mile long trail along the coastline.

Biking Routes

  • Greenstone Ridge Trail: A 40-mile long trail that is suitable for experienced bikers.
  • Tobin Harbor Road: A 5-mile long road that provides easy cycling options.

Best Things to Do in Isle Royale National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: The island is well known for its fishing and visitors can enjoy fishing for trout, salmon, and bass.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the coastline and bays of the island.
  • Wildlife Watching: The island is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including wolves, moose, and foxes.
  • Ranger-led Tours: Park rangers offer tours that provide insight into the cultural and natural history of the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isle Royale National Park

Day 1: Arrive on the island and explore the surroundings. Day 2-3: Hike around Rock Harbor area and explore the lighthouse and Edisen Fishery. Day 4-5: Take the ferry to Windigo Ranger station and hike the Greenstone Ridge Trail. Day 6-7: Explore Tobin Harbor and experience kayaking and fishing.

Food and Drink Scene of Isle Royale National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Freshly caught fish: Trout, salmon, and bass are some of the popular fish caught around the island.
  • Wild berry pie: Wild berries like blueberries and raspberries can be found around the island and are used to make pies.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: The local brewer on the island offers craft beer that is made using local ingredients.
  • Coffee: Locally sourced coffee is available on the island.

Where to Stay in Isle Royale National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rock Harbor Lodge: A hotel located on the eastern part of the island that offers comfortable accommodations with lake views.
  • Windigo Camper Cabins: Rustic cabins that provide basic amenities and are located on the west side of the island.
  • Daisy Farm Campground: A campsite located on the Rock Harbor Trail that offers a backcountry experience.

What to Buy in Isle Royale National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Island-inspired artwork: Local artists sell artwork inspired by the island’s natural beauty.
  • Handcrafted jewelry: Unique jewelry that is made using local materials is available on the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Isle Royale National Park

The island has a rich cultural history and is home to Ojibwe people who have lived on the island for centuries. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Isle Royale National Park

The best time to visit Isle Royale National Park is between mid-June and mid-September when the weather is pleasant and the park is open. The park is closed during the winter months due to the harsh weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isle Royale National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Observe wildlife from a safe distance.
  • Pack and plan for emergencies.

Don’t:

  • Approach or feed wildlife.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Build fires outside of designated fire rings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isle Royale National Park

  • Be prepared for weather changes.
  • Carry adequate supplies and equipment.
  • Inform a park ranger of your planned itinerary.

Health in Isle Royale National Park

  • Bring all necessary medications and a first-aid kit.
  • Be aware of the risks associated with consuming untreated water.

Getting to Isle Royale National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Isle Royale National Park

  • Hancock County Airport, Houghton, MI.
  • Duluth International Airport, Duluth, MN.

Bus Stations Near Isle Royale National Park

  • Hancock: MTU Bus Station

Other Options

  • Car: Visitors can park their cars in the mainland parking lot in Copper Harbor.
  • Ferry: There are several ferry services from the mainland to the island.

How to Get Around in Isle Royale National Park

The island is not accessible by car and visitors can hike or bike to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Isle Royale National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Houghton: A historic city located on the Keweenaw Peninsula that offers various outdoor activities and cultural experiences.
  • Copper Harbor: A small town located on the northern tip of the Keweenaw Peninsula that is known for its historic lighthouse and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Isle Royale National Park offers visitors a unique experience and an opportunity to explore the natural beauty and cultural history of the island. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, kayaking, and many other outdoor activities. The island provides an unforgettable experience and is a must-visit destination for adventure-seekers and nature-lovers alike.
