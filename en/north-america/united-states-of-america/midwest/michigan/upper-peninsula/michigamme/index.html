Michigamme Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Michigamme

Located in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula in the Midwest region of the United States, Michigamme is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This picturesque village boasts a rich history and culture, breathtaking natural landscapes, and family-friendly activities that will make your trip to Michigamme an unforgettable experience.

Introduction to Michigamme

Michigamme is a small village with a population of around 300 residents. Named after the nearby lake, Michigamme is surrounded by forests and lakes that offer its visitors numerous outdoor activities all year round. The village has a friendly atmosphere and welcomes visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re here to explore the outdoors, learn about the local history, or just relax, Michigamme won’t disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Michigamme

Here are some of the must-visit places in Michigamme:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Michigamme Museum - The Michigamme Museum is a local treasure that showcases the history of the village and surrounding areas. The museum has a vast collection of artifacts, exhibits, and displays that offer visitors a glimpse into the life of the early settlers who lived here.

  2. The Craig Lake State Park - Offering visitors over 6,900 acres of wilderness, Craig Lake State Park is the perfect place to explore the great outdoors. Visitors can hike, fish, swim, or kayak on one of the many lakes in the park. The park also has several small cabins that offer visitors a place to stay while enjoying the beautiful surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Michigamme: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Michigamme Reservoir - The Michigamme Reservoir is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in water activities. The reservoir offers swimming, boating, and fishing in its crystal-clear waters. Visitors can also rent a boat or kayak to explore the many bays and inlets.

  2. The McCormick Wilderness Area - The McCormick Wilderness Area is a vast expanse of unspoiled wilderness that offers visitors a glimpse into the past. Visitors can explore the many trails, streams, and lakes that make up this wilderness area. The area is also home to a variety of wildlife, including wolves, bears, and cougars.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Michigamme

Michigamme offers visitors an abundance of hiking and biking trails that explore the beautiful natural surroundings. Here are some of the best ones to try:

  1. The Iron Ore Heritage Trail - This 47-mile trail connects several historic mining towns and offers visitors a chance to see the history and natural beauty of the area.

  2. The Craig Lake State Park Trail System - The Craig Lake State Park Trail System is a network of hiking trails that explore the beauty of the park’s wilderness. Visitors can choose from short or long hikes that offer stunning views of the park’s lakes and forests.

Best Things to Do in Michigamme: Activities and Tours

Michigamme offers a variety of activities and tours that are perfect for families, couples, and solo travelers. Here are some of the best ones to try:

  1. Michigamme River Canoeing and Kayaking Tours - These tours offer visitors a chance to explore the Michigamme River by canoe or kayak. Visitors can choose from half-day, full-day, or multi-day tours that offer breathtaking scenery and wildlife sightings.

  2. Wolf Mountain Nature Center - The Wolf Mountain Nature Center offers visitors a chance to interact with wolves and other wild animals in their natural habitat. Visitors can learn about the animals and conservation efforts in the area.

Week-Long Iternary for Michigamme

Here’s a week-long itinerary that offers visitors a chance to explore Michigamme and the surrounding area:

Day 1 - Visit the Michigamme Museum and learn about the history of the area.

Day 2 - Explore the Craig Lake State Park by hiking or fishing.

Day 3 - Take a canoeing or kayaking tour down the Michigamme River.

Day 4 - Visit the Wolf Mountain Nature Center and interact with the wolves and other animals.

Day 5 - Explore the McCormick Wilderness Area by hiking or mountain biking.

Day 6 - Rent a boat and spend the day on the Michigamme Reservoir.

Day 7 - Relax, explore the local shops and eateries, and enjoy the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Michigamme

Michigamme boasts a variety of restaurants and bars that offer traditional and modern cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pasties - A traditional Cornish dish that consists of meat, vegetables, and potatoes cooked inside a pastry shell.

  2. Freshwater Fish - Lake trout, whitefish, and salmon are commonly found on menus in the area.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer - The area is home to several craft breweries that specialize in brewing unique and flavorful beers.

  2. Cider - Michigamme is known for its apple orchards, and many local pubs and restaurants offer locally made apple cider.

Where to Stay in Michigamme: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Michigamme offers visitors several options for accommodations. Here are some of the best ones to choose from:

  1. Northwoods Lodge - The Northwoods Lodge offers visitors a charming and rustic stay in a log cabin-style lodge. The lodge is located near the Michigamme Reservoir and offers guests easy access to several hiking trails.

  2. The Pines Resort - The Pines Resort is located on the shore of Lake Michigamme and offers visitors a luxurious stay in a cozy and private cottage. The resort also has several amenities, including a hot tub and sauna.

What to Buy in Michigamme: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Michigamme will find several shops and boutiques that sell locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best ones to visit:

  1. The Michigamme Trading Post - The Michigamme Trading Post sells locally made crafts, clothing, and gifts.

  2. The Michigamme Moonshine Art Gallery - The Michigamme Moonshine Art Gallery offers visitors a chance to view and buy art made by local artists.
