Introduction to Escanaba

Escanaba is a hidden gem located in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula. The city, which is named after the nearby Escanaba River, has a rich history, beautiful scenery, and a variety of outdoor activities. Its location on Little Bay de Noc provides access to beaches, fishing spots, and boating opportunities. With a population of just over 12,000 residents, Escanaba offers a charming and laid-back atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Escanaba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Sand Point Lighthouse

Built in 1868, the Sand Point Lighthouse is a must-visit attraction in Escanaba. The lighthouse offers a glimpse into the city’s past and provides visitors with stunning views of Little Bay de Noc. The lighthouse is open to the public during the summer months and offers tours.

2. Delta County Historical Museum

The Delta County Historical Museum is a great place to learn about the history of the area. The museum features exhibits on local industries, such as logging and mining, as well as the Native American presence in the area.

Natural Attractions of Escanaba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Ludington Park

Ludington Park is a popular destination for both locals and visitors. The park, which is situated on the shores of Little Bay de Noc, features a beach, picnic areas, and a playground. The park also has a boardwalk that provides stunning views of the bay.

2. Pioneer Trail Park

Pioneer Trail Park is a great place for hiking and mountain biking. The park features over 5 miles of trails that wind through a forested area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Escanaba

Escanaba is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The city and surrounding areas offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Some of the best routes include:

  • Blueberry Ridge Pathway: This trail is a 6.2-mile loop that winds through a forested area. There are also opportunities for cross-country skiing and snowshoeing in the winter.
  • Rapid River Falls Trail: This trail is a 1.5-mile loop that leads to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Escanaba City Bike Trails: These trails wind through the city and offer a great way to explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Escanaba: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy in Escanaba, including:

  • Fishing Charters: With access to Little Bay de Noc, Escanaba is a great place to go fishing. Several companies offer fishing charters for visitors.
  • Delta County Fair: The Delta County Fair takes place every August and features carnival rides, food vendors, and livestock exhibits.
  • Weyerhaeuser Museum: The Weyerhaeuser Museum is located in nearby Gladstone and offers visitors a look at the lumber industry in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Escanaba

Day 1: Visit the Sand Point Lighthouse and Ludington Park. Day 2: Explore the Delta County Historical Museum and go for a hike on the Blueberry Ridge Pathway. Day 3: Go fishing on Little Bay de Noc with a charter company. Day 4: Visit the Pioneer Trail Park and take a picnic lunch. Day 5: Spend a day in nearby Gladstone and visit the Weyerhaeuser Museum. Day 6: Take a bike ride on the Escanaba City Bike Trails and have a picnic at Harbor Hideout. Day 7: Attend the Delta County Fair and enjoy the carnival rides and food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Escanaba

Must-Try Dishes

1. Pasties

Pasties are a traditional Cornish dish that has become popular in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula. The dish consists of meat, potatoes, and vegetables wrapped in a pastry dough.

2. Whitefish

Little Bay de Noc is known for its whitefish, so be sure to try this local delicacy when visiting Escanaba.

Local Beverages

1. Microbreweries

Escanaba has several microbreweries that offer a variety of locally brewed beers.

2. Wine

The nearby Upper Peninsula has several wineries that offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Escanaba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Escanaba offers several options for accommodations, including:

  • Terrace Bay Hotel: This waterfront hotel offers beautiful views of Little Bay de Noc.
  • Sunset Lodge: This cozy lodge is located in a forested area and offers a quiet retreat.
  • Hiawatha Motel: This budget-friendly option offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Escanaba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Yooperlites

Yooperlites are rocks that contain fluorescent minerals. They can only be found in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula.

2. Pasties

Bring home a taste of Escanaba by purchasing pasties to enjoy later.

Local Culture and Customs of Escanaba

Escanaba is known for its laid-back and friendly atmosphere. While in the city, be sure to greet others with a smile and strike up a conversation with locals to learn more about the area’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Escanaba

The best time to visit Escanaba is during the summer months when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing.
