Introduction to Garden

Tucked away in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, the charming town of Garden offers a perfect escape from hustle-bustle of the big city life with its unspoiled wilderness, diverse wildlife, pristine beaches, and rustic charm. This little-known gem in the Midwest region of the United States of America is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure buffs, and history enthusiasts alike. In this comprehensive guide, we have covered everything you need to know to plan your trip to Garden.

List of Places to Visit in Garden

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fayette Historic State Park

Step back in time and explore one of the best-preserved industrial-era communities in the country at the Fayette Historic State Park. This former bustling industrial iron-smelting town is now a fascinating living-history museum, featuring over 20 historic buildings, a visitor center, a gift shop, and a picnic area.

Natural Attractions of Garden: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hiawatha National Forest

Spanning over 800,000 acres, Hiawatha National Forest is a natural wonderland that offers a plethora of outdoor activities to visitors. From hiking, camping, and fishing to wildlife watching, kayaking, and canoeing – there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this forest reserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Garden

For the hikers and mountain bikers, Garden is a playground with its numerous trails. One of the most popular trails is the Garden Bay - Bumble Bee Trail which offers picturesque views of the bay and rugged terrain.

Best Things to Do in Garden: Activities and Tours

Visit the Garden Peninsula Lighthouse

The Garden Peninsula Lighthouse is a popular attraction that shouldn’t be missed. Explore the history behind it and the role it played in guiding ships through the waters.

Go Fishing and Kayaking

As a perfect spot for fishing & kayaking, there are more than enough locations to choose from in Garden to get the perfect catch or stroke.

Food and Drink Scene of Garden

Must-Try Dishes

Garden has many local foods which ranges from pastries, fishes, burgers, and much more. Try fish sandwiches from Finnibay to burgers from Bishop’s Bar & Restaurant. Don’t forget to complete your food trip with Upper Peninsula’s regional pie from Garden City Pie Shop.

Local Beverages

Garden enjoys multiple specialty drink options, indulge in some delicious cocktails at Young’s in Garden, or try D’s Bottle Shop to grab a quality beer or wine.

Where to Stay in Garden: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxurious stay or budget-friendly options, Garden has myriad options to choose from. Hotels, cottages, cabins and campgrounds all await you. There’s always something for your preference and budget.

What to Buy in Garden: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring some Garden memories to your home by purchasing unique souvenirs from Lake Antique Store to antique collectibles from garden.

Local Culture and Customs of Garden

Garden is a small town that takes pride in its close-knit community and local customs with deep rooted Norweigian and Finnish culture. One of the best ways to immerse yourself in Garden’s local culture is by exploring during festivals or fairs.

Best Time to Visit Garden

The best time to visit Garden is from late spring to early fall (May to October) when the weather is balmy and perfect for outdoor activities. Winter months offer snowsports activities and winter festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Garden

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the region.
  • Explore the town on foot or by bike.
  • Visit the local cultural heritage sites and sample the local cuisines.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any illegal or dangerous activities.
  • Litter or damage the natural or cultural sites.
  • Disrespect the local customs or people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Garden

Garden is a safe and friendly town that boasts a low crime rate. However, visitors should be cautious when exploring the outskirts of the town and the natural sites, and, must practice basic safety tips to avoid any unwanted situations.

Health in Garden

Travelers are encouraged to keep an eye on their well-being and take necessary precautions for any emergency.

Getting to Garden: Transportation Options and Logistics

Garden Peninsula is well-connected by major airports and bus stations, and by road, can be reached via US-2.

Airports Near Garden

The nearest airports to Garden are the Delta County Airport and the Sawyer International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Garden

The Island Resort and Casino bus station and the Escanaba Greyhound station are two of the most popular bus stations near Garden.

Other Options

For those who prefer driving, Garden can be conveniently reached by car via US -2 from Escanaba or Rapid River.

How to Get Around in Garden

Exploring the Garden town is best done on foot or by bike. It offers a dedicated network of trails and paths that are well-marked and easy to navigate. Visitors can rent a car or a bike to get around the town or to explore the natural sites.

Where to Go Next from Garden: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations include Escanaba, Marquette, and Menominee that offer exciting opportunities for outdoor activities, cultural exploration, and food.
