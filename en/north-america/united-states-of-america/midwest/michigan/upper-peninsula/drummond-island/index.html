Introduction to Drummond Island

Drummond Island is a beautiful gem in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, United States, known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, culture, history, and small-town charm. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat, exciting adventures, or a glimpse into the local culture, Drummond Island has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Drummond Island

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Drummond Island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Drummond Island Historical Museum - Learn about the island’s rich history and culture, from Native American settlements to the timber and fishing industries to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Drummond Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Maxton Plains - Explore the untouched 5,000 acres of natural beauty filled with meadows, streams, and wildlife that is owned and maintained by The Nature Conservancy!
  • The Marble Head - Hiking to the top of this limestone ridge offers stunning views of Lake Huron and the island.
  • The Fossil Ledges - Discover ancient coral reefs, marine fossils, and petrified wood while exploring the shores by foot or kayak.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Drummond Island

Drummond Island offers a variety of hiking and cycling routes, suitable for all levels of experience:

  1. Scout Island Loop - A 4.4-mile bike/hike trail circling around Scout Island offers breathtaking views of the surrounding woods and lake shoreline.
  2. East Tour trail - A 14 mile & tough single-track mountain bike trail from North Point through Marblehead and Ora-Smith road loop is something thrilling!

Best Things to Do in Drummond Island: Activities and Tours

Drummond Island has various activities and tours to make your trip unforgettable. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Kayak and Canoe Trips - Explore the island’s crystal-clear waterways and peaceful forests while canoeing or kayaking. Tiki-hut rental offers guided trips for the same!
  • ATV and Snowmobile Tours - Get on an ATV or snowmobile and explore over 100 miles of trails on Drummond Island while enjoying the wilderness.
  • Fishing Charters - Experience Great Lakes fishing with a local fishing charter and get a chance to catch different species of fish, including salmon and trout.

Food and Drink Scene of Drummond Island

Drummond Island offers various local dishes and drinks that you must try during your visit!

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Fry - Try the traditional fish fry, made with fresh Lake Huron whitefish, served at the local restaurants!
  • Burgers - The burgers are delicious at the Chuckwagon on Drummond Island.

Local Beverages

  • Island Root Beer - A non-alcoholic beverage that’s brewed locally, try the Island Root Beer to have a refreshing beverage during your trip!
  • Beers - Many of Michigan’s local beer treasures find a place in Drummond island. Northwoods Brewery is worth to try.

Where to Stay in Drummond Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Drummond Island offers a variety of places to stay, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels.

  • Drummond Island Resort & Conference Center - This legendary resort is best suited for a luxury stay with an outdoor pool, a restaurant, and an 18-hole golf course.
  • Driftwood Inn - This traditional inn overlooking the water offers breathtaking views & the cozy stay on the island.

What to Buy in Drummond Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Drummond Island without snagging some souvenirs! Here are some of the things you must buy:

  • Island-made maple syrup and jams - Grab some locally produced jams and maple syrup, made from surrounding forests and is sure to please your taste buds.
  • Handmade crafts and jewelry - Buy unique handmade items and jewelry made by local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Drummond Island

The locals of Drummond Island are known for their warm hospitality, hard work, and love of their culture and natural resources.

Best Time to Visit Drummond Island

The best time to visit Drummond Island is during the summer months, as the weather is ideal for outdoor activities and tourism is in full swing. However, visitors can also explore the island in early fall season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Drummond Island

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts of Drummond Island, to help you enjoy your trip:

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural resources and wildlife.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities, like hiking, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Discover the local culture and try the traditional dishes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the environment.
  • Don’t trespass on private property.
  • Don’t leave your trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Drummond Island

Drummond Island is a safe place to visit with a low crime rate, but visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions when exploring the outdoors.

Health in Drummond Island

Visitors are advised to bring insect repellent, sunscreen, and a first aid kit, as medical facilities are limited on the island.

Getting to Drummond Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Drummond Island by car, plane, or ferry.

Airports Near Drummond Island

  • Chippewa County International Airport - 61 miles away from the Island.

Bus Stations Near Drummond Island

  • There are many private bus services and charters but they are not recommended.

Other Options

  • Catch a ferry from DeTour Village, to the eastern end of the island.

How to Get Around in Drummond Island

Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Drummond Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Explore nearby destinations like Sault Ste. Marie, Mackinaw City, or take the car ferries to Mackinac Island.

Conclusion

Drummond Island is a perfect getaway for nature enthusiasts, adventure lovers, and anyone looking to explore small-town America. So pack up your essentials and get ready to unwind while exploring the natural beauty of Drummond Island.
