Introduction to Les Cheneaux Islands

Les Cheneaux Islands comprise 36 small islands located in the northern part of Lake Huron, in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula. It’s a popular destination for boating and fishing, with crystal clear waters and picturesque scenery. The area is also home to a vibrant arts and culture scene, featuring museums and galleries showcasing local talent.

List of Places to Visit in Les Cheneaux Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Les Cheneaux Islands Antique Wooden Boat Show and Festival: A must-visit for anyone who loves classic boats and history. The event showcases antique wooden boats and vintage motors, along with other fun activities.
  • Garlyn Zoological Park: Home to over 400 animals from around the world, including giraffes, tigers, and bears. The park also offers daily animal presentations, a petting zoo, and pony and camel rides.
  • E.J. Mertaugh Boat Works Museum: Experience the history of wooden boat building in this museum featuring antique wooden boats, tools, and photographs.
  • The Hessel School House: A charming one-room schoolhouse built in 1905 that has been transformed into a museum featuring exhibits on the area’s maritime history and early settlers.
  • The Les Cheneaux Culinary School: Learn how to cook like a Michigander with classes from local chefs, featuring ingredients grown and harvested in the region.

Natural Attractions of Les Cheneaux Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Les Cheneaux Islands Wildlife Area: With over 1,000 acres of marshland, forests, and waterways, the wildlife area is home to a variety of Michigan’s wildlife, including deer, waterfowl, and beavers.
  • Ferryboats: Take a ride on one of the local ferryboats to explore the scenic views of the islands from the water.
  • Marquette Island: Enjoy the panoramic vistas and charming community on Marquette Island, which is accessible via ferryboat.
  • Straits State Park: Located at the northern tip of the Lower Peninsula, this park offers camping sites, a swimming beach, picnic areas, hiking, and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Les Cheneaux Islands

  • The Drummond Island Trail System: This system is the perfect way to explore the island’s wilderness and stunning vistas on foot, bike, or ATV.
  • The Great Lakes Birding and Wildlife Trail: This trail offers different wildlife viewing spots, including along US Highway 2 which runs through Hiawatha National Park.
  • Les Cheneaux Islands Water Trail: The trail goes around the islands and the bay and offers excellent access to the water.

Best Things to Do in Les Cheneaux Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Charters: Les Cheneaux Islands offers world-class fishing, with salmon, trout or walleye the most common target species. Try a fishing charter for the best experience.
  • Kayaking and Paddleboarding: Rent gear from local providers and paddle around the islands.
  • Golfing: Tee off on of the island’s best golf courses, the Hessel Bay Golf Course.
  • Art Galleries Tour: Wander through the various art galleries around Les Cheneaux Islands. Meet local artists and discover more about their work.

Food and Drink Scene of Les Cheneaux Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties: A staple of Michigan’s Upper Peninsula, pasties are baked meat and vegetable-filled pastries that are a must-try in Les Cheneaux Islands.
  • Michigan-Style Pizza: A variety of pizza, made with savory tomato sauce and a slightly thicker, doughier crust than its New York-style counterpart.
  • Whitefish: The area’s freshwater whitefish is delicious and a popular local dish.

Local Beverages

  • Cherry Wine: The fertile soils of Michigan produce excellent cherries, and there are numerous wineries in the area producing delicious and unique cherry wines.
  • Milkshakes: There are a variety of ice cream shops serving up thick, creamy milkshakes, perfect after a day out on the water or exploring the islands.

Where to Stay in Les Cheneaux Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Island Resort and Casino: This casino-hotel offers luxury accommodations, onsite dining and a world-class gaming floor.
  • Les Cheneaux Landing and Lodge: A charming boutique hotel offering cozy rooms and spectacular views of the islands.
  • Driftwood Resort: This rustic and charming family resort boasts cabins and cottages with access to waterfront activities.

What to Buy in Les Cheneaux Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antiques and Collectibles: Several vintage and antique shops in the area that offer a range of unique and one-of-a-kind treasures.
  • Local Artwork: Les Cheneaux Islands is home to a group of talented artists and artisans, and you are bound to find something beautiful to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Les Cheneaux Islands

Les Cheneaux Islands have a rich history connected to Great Lakes life, where the local people are known for their friendly and easygoing attitude. The area is one where the community comes together to support one another, whether it’s for festivals, events or community projects.

Best Time to Visit Les Cheneaux Islands

The best time to visit Les Cheneaux Islands is during the summer months when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. With the region’s proximity to the water bodies, many of the locals take advantage of water sports activities such as kayaking or swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Les Cheneaux Islands

Do:

  • Experience the local history: Learn about maritime history and see antique wooden boats at the E.J. Mertaugh Boat Works or other local museums and cultural attractions.
  • Engage in water sports: Explore the crystal-clear waters, whether it’s fishing, swimming, kayaking, or simply taking a leisurely boat ride.
  • Pack plenty of sunscreen: The sun’s rays can be strong, so be sure to protect your skin.

Don’t:

  • Trash the islands: Do not leave your trash on the beaches or other natural areas. Respect the environment.
  • Trespass private property: Respect the local residents’ privacy and property.
  • Overlook the bugs: Mosquitoes and flies love the islands during the summer months, don’t forget the bug sprays.

Safety and Health

Safety in Les Cheneaux Islands

Les Cheneaux Islands are generally very safe for visitors. However, normal precautions such as locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight should be taken. Also, pay attention to weather patterns in the area to avoid getting into precarious situations.

Health in Les Cheneaux Islands

Health emergencies are rare in Les Cheneaux Islands, but in case you require any medical assistance, the towns across the Upper Peninsula maintained well-equipped medical centers offering 24-hour medical services.

Getting to Les Cheneaux Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Les Cheneaux Islands

  • Chippewa County International Airport: 30 miles away from Les Cheneaux Islands. Frequent daily flights to Chicago, Minneapolis, and Detroit.
  • Pellston Regional Airport: 41 miles away from Les Cheneaux Islands. Daily flights to Detroit.

Bus Stations Near Les Cheneaux Islands

  • Sault Ste. Marie Bus Terminal: Services linking Cheneaux Islands to other parts of the Upper Peninsula.

Other Options

  • Driving: Les Cheneaux Islands is 6-7 hours from Detroit or Chicago, and visitors can rent a car from either airport and drive to the island.
  • Ferry: Island residents and visitors use ferries to travel to nearby Mackinac Island or Drummond Island.

How to Get Around in Les Cheneaux Islands

The best way to get around the Les Cheneaux Islands is to rent a car and explore the expansive terrain and roads. Another option is cycling, with running trails around and stunning views. Renting kayaks or paddleboards is another alternative, and there are also several local taxi services available around the islands.

Where to Go Next from Les Cheneaux Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mackinac Island

Located about 80 miles southwest of the Les Cheneaux Islands, Mackinac Island is a must-see destination featuring island bike rides, horse-drawn carriages, and tours of historic buildings.

L’Anse, Michigan

L’Anse is situated on Lake Superior’s shores and is a small village named for the geographic stretch between Keweenaw Bay in the east and Huron Bay on the west.

Conclusion

Les Cheneaux Islands have so much to offer visitors to Michigan’s Upper Peninsula, from stunning vistas to engaging museums and outdoor adventures. The charming island community makes it an unforgettable and worthwhile destination for all types of travelers. We hope this travel guide will inspire you to plan your visit to Les Cheneaux Islands.
