Introduction to Sault Sainte Marie

Nestled in Upper Peninsula, Michigan, Sault Sainte Marie is a charming city that boasts a rich history and culture, breathtaking outdoor adventures, and delicious food and drink scene. This city sits on the banks of St. Marys River, which is the gateway to the Great Lakes. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Sault Sainte Marie has something to offer. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Sault Sainte Marie, Michigan.

List of Places to Visit in Sault Sainte Marie

Here are some of the top places to visit and things to do in Sault Sainte Marie:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Soo Locks: As the oldest operating lock system in North America, this historical landmark attracts thousands of visitors annually. It is fascinating to watch the gigantic freighters pass through the locks.
  • The Tower of History: This 210-foot-tall tower provides breathtaking views of the city and its surroundings. The Tower of History also has an exhibit showcasing the history of the area and its people.
  • River of History Museum: This museum takes a deep dive into Sault Sainte Marie’s rich and diverse history. Visitors here learn about the indigenous people who first settled in the area to how modern-day Sault Sainte Marie came to be.

Natural Attractions of Sault Sainte Marie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tahquamenon Falls State Park: A short drive from Sault Ste Marie, this park offers stunning views of one of Michigan’s largest waterfalls. Visitors can also enjoy activities such as hiking, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Whitefish Point: Located at the northernmost point of the Eastern Upper Peninsula, Whitefish Point is a wildlife sanctuary and birding area. Visitors can enjoy hiking on the beach, viewing the historic lighthouse, or visiting the Great Lakes Shipwreck Museum.
  • Soo Locks Park: The park near the Soo Locks provides stunning views of the freighters passing through. Visitors can also enjoy a stroll along the river’s edge and explore the beautiful flower beds and sculptures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sault Sainte Marie

Sault Ste Marie has numerous hiking and biking trails to choose from, depending on your experience level. The trails range from beginner-friendly to expert-level and offer scenic views and an opportunity to immerse yourself in nature. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Kincheloe Spur Rail Trail: The trail spans 8 miles and provides a scenic route through the woods, offering a chance to spot wildlife and take in the beautiful scenery.
  • The North Country Trail: As one of the longest trails in the country, this trail is popular among experienced hikers. The trail is dotted with stunning views of the Great Lakes and wildlife sightings.
  • Brimley State Park: This park sits on the shores of Lake Superior and has several trails perfect for hiking or biking. Trails range from easy to moderate and provide a glimpse of the stunning waterfalls and the lake.

Best Things to Do in Sault Sainte Marie: Activities and Tours

Sault Sainte Marie has various activities and tours guaranteed to make your visit memorable. Below are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Soo Locks Boat Tour: Experience the Soo Locks from a different perspective by taking a boat tour. Visitors on this tour get to see the locks’ inner workings and learn about the history of the area.
  • Island Tour: Take a ferry to the nearby islands for a chance to enjoy some of the best views of Lake Superior. The islands are home to beautiful beaches, forests, and lighthouses.
  • Fishing: Lake Superior is known for its abundance of fish, with lake trout, salmon, and walleye being some of the popular species. Visitors can take a fishing charter or head out on their own to catch their dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Sault Sainte Marie

Sault Sainte Marie has a diverse food and drink scene that caters to everyone’s tastes. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties: Originally introduced by the Cornish miners, pasties are flaky, savory pies filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Lake Whitefish: As the name suggests, this dish features fresh whitefish, caught daily in Lake Superior, and cooked with local herbs and spices.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A staple dish in the area, wild rice soup features wild rice, chicken or beef, vegetables, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Several breweries in the city craft local beers, including the Soo Brewing Company, 1668 Winery & Lockside Brewery, and Bird’s Eye Outfitters and Brewing Co.
  • Cider: Sample some of the best hard ciders at The Bearded Dogg Lounge, a local cider house and lounge.
  • Wine: Visit the 1668 Winery & Lockside Brewery to try some of the best wines made in the area.

Where to Stay in Sault Sainte Marie: Hotels, Guesthouses, and Hostels

Sault Sainte Marie has a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and tastes. Some of the best places to stay in the city include:

  • Ramada Plaza by Wyndham Sault Ste. Marie: This hotel is centrally located and provides comfortable and affordable accommodation.
  • Malcolm Motel: For budget travelers, this motel provides clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • The Inn on Ashmun Street: This boutique inn offers an intimate and comfortable stay with well-appointed rooms and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Sault Sainte Marie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sault Sainte Marie has a variety of souvenirs and local products you can buy. Be sure to check out:

  • Art Galleries: Sault Sainte Marie is home to several galleries showcasing the works of local artists.
  • Fudge Shops: Sault Sainte Marie is famous for fudge, and several shops sell freshly made fudge in several flavors.
  • Trout and Whitefish: Lake Superior is home to some of the best trout and whitefish, and several shops sell fresh and smoked varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Sault Sainte Marie

The culture of Sault Sainte Marie is deeply rooted in its history and natural surroundings. The area was traditionally the home of the Ojibwe people, and several cultural institutions celebrate their heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the indigenous culture and customs. Additionally, the people in the area are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, making visitors feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Sault Sainte Marie

The best time to visit Sault Sainte Marie is during the summer months when the temperatures are mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Late fall and early winter are also great times for visitors who enjoy winter sports like skiing and snowboarding. However, it is worth noting that Sault Sainte Marie can get quite cold in winter, so be sure to pack appropriately.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sault Sainte Marie

Do:

  • Be respectful of the indigenous culture and customs.
  • Spend time outdoors and explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Try the local food and drink scene.
  • Take a boat tour at the Soo Locks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety signs and guidelines when hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities.
  • Assume that the local customs are the same as your own culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sault Sainte Marie

Sault Sainte Marie is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Be sure to lock your car and room at night, avoid walking alone at night, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Sault Sainte Marie

The city has several medical facilities and pharmacies serving the community. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical issues, and bring a sufficient supply of any prescription medications.

Getting to Sault Sainte Marie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sault Sainte Marie

The nearest airport to Sault Sainte Marie is the Chippewa County International Airport, located about 20 minutes from the city center. Alternatively, visitors can fly into the Detroit Metropolitan Airport and take a connecting flight to Chippewa County.

Bus Stations Near Sault Sainte Marie

Greyhound operates bus services to Sault Sainte Marie from various cities in Michigan.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to the city via the I-75 highway or take the Amtrak train to the nearby town of Newberry, and then take a bus to Sault Sainte Marie.

How to Get Around in Sault Sainte Marie

Visitors can rent a car or bike to get around in Sault Sainte Marie. Taxis are also available for hire, and visitors can use the local bus services to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Sault Sainte Marie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sault Sainte Marie is a gateway to the Great Lakes, and several nearby towns and attractions are worth a visit. Here are some of the best nearby destinations:

  • Mackinac Island: A historic island town with beautiful architecture, beaches, and natural beauty.
  • Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore: A stunning national park with beautiful sandstone cliffs, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Lake Superior State Forest: A vast forested area with numerous hiking trails and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Sault Sainte Marie is a charming city that offers visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty of the area, immerse themselves in the rich history and culture, and sample the delicious food and drink scene. With several places to visit and things to do, there is never a dull moment in this city. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Sault Sainte Marie and experience the best that the city has to offer.
