Introduction to Cedar Falls

Nestled in the heart of the Midwest, Cedar Falls is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Located in Northeast Iowa, this charming city boasts both natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the outdoors with its many hiking trails, picturesque parks, and scenic views. The town is also home to several museums and historical sites, showcasing its past and present. Whether you’re seeking adventure, culture, or a mix of both, Cedar Falls has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cedar Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Hearst Center for the Arts

Located in a beautiful historic mansion, the Hearst Center for the Arts is a premier destination for traditional and contemporary art. It features a rotating collection of local and regional artists, as well as workshops, classes, and special events.

2. Cedar Falls Historical Society Museum

Get a glimpse of Cedar Falls’ past at the Cedar Falls Historical Society Museum. Exhibits include artifacts, maps, and photographs that showcase the town’s history, from its earliest days to the present.

3. Ice House Museum

Built in 1921, the Ice House Museum is the only remaining ice house in Iowa. Visitors can learn about the ice-harvesting process and its impact on the local economy and lifestyle.

Natural Attractions of Cedar Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Big Woods Lake Recreation Area

Spend a day in nature at Big Woods Lake Recreation Area, a beautiful park with hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and picnic areas. During the winter, the park offers ice skating and cross-country skiing.

2. Cedar Falls River Trail

For a scenic stroll or bike ride, head to Cedar Falls River Trail. This paved trail follows the Cedar River and offers stunning views of nature and wildlife.

3. Hartman Reserve Nature Center

Hartman Reserve Nature Center is a 340-acre wildlife sanctuary with trails, wetlands, and prairie grasses. Visitors can hike, birdwatch, and enjoy the natural beauty of Iowa.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cedar Falls

1. Panther Trail

This 10-mile trail is perfect for hiking or biking and takes you through picturesque woods and fields. Along the way, you’ll pass by the university’s Panther Village and a beautiful waterfall.

2. Prairie Lakes Trail

The Prairie Lakes Trail takes you through Cedar Falls and Waterloo, offering spectacular city views and nature scenery. This 18-mile trail is perfect for an extended bike ride or a leisurely hike.

Best Things to Do in Cedar Falls: Activities and Tours

1. Cedar Valley Food Tours

Get a taste of Cedar Falls with Cedar Valley Food Tours, which takes you on a culinary journey through the town’s best eateries. From pizza to tacos, you’ll sample a range of delicious food.

2. Lost Island Water Park

Cool off and have some fun at the Lost Island Water Park, a family-friendly destination with water slides, a lazy river, and a wave pool.

Food and Drink Scene of Cedar Falls

Must-Try Dishes

1. Maid-Rites

A local favorite, Maid-Rites are loose meat sandwiches that originated in Iowa. They’re made with seasoned beef and served with pickles and onions.

2. Cedar Falls Downtown Farmers’ Market

Every Saturday during the summer, the Cedar Falls Downtown Farmers’ Market offers a wide variety of fresh produce from local farmers, as well as artisanal baked goods and crafts.

Local Beverages

1. Millstream Brewing Company

Located in nearby Amana, Millstream Brewing Company is a must-visit destination for beer lovers. Its selection of crisp lagers and craft ales are made with locally sourced ingredients.

2. Cedar Ridge Winery and Distillery

For a taste of Iowa-inspired spirits, visit Cedar Ridge Winery and Distillery. Its award-winning bourbon, whiskey, and brandy are made from locally grown corn, wheat, and rye.

Where to Stay in Cedar Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Hilton Garden Inn Cedar Falls

This elegant hotel features spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center. It’s conveniently located near many of Cedar Falls’ top attractions.

2. Blackhawk Hotel

Built in 1853, the Blackhawk Hotel is a historic landmark that’s been beautifully restored and modernized. Its boutique-style rooms feature original artwork and vintage décor.

What to Buy in Cedar Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Vintage Finds

Cedar Falls’ downtown district is home to several antique and vintage shops, offering unique treasures and hidden gems.

Local Crafts

Visit the Cedar Falls Downtown Farmers’ Market for handmade crafts from local artists, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Cedar Falls

Cedar Falls is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community and local pride. Visitors are encouraged to participate in events and activities, and to explore its rich history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Cedar Falls

Spring and fall are the best times to visit Cedar Falls, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. During the summer, the town comes alive with festivals and events, while winter is perfect for winter sports and indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cedar Falls

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Support local businesses and artisans

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect nature
  • Disrupt local events or traditions
  • Make assumptions about local culture or people

Safety and Health

Safety in Cedar Falls

Cedar Falls is a safe and welcoming town, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take normal precautions to keep their belongings safe and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Cedar Falls

Cedar Falls has several medical facilities, including hospitals and urgent care centers. Visitors should also take precautions to protect themselves from sunburn and tick bites if hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Cedar Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cedar Falls

The closest airport to Cedar Falls is Waterloo Regional Airport, located just 15 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Cedar Falls

Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Jefferson Lines, offer service to Cedar Falls. The town’s bus station is located downtown.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a convenient option for getting around Cedar Falls and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Cedar Falls

Cedar Falls is easy to navigate by car, bike, or foot. The town’s downtown district is pedestrian-friendly, and many of its top attractions are within walking distance.

Where to Go Next from Cedar Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dubuque

Located along the Mississippi River, Dubuque is a charming town with historic architecture, museums, and outdoor adventures.

Iowa City

Home to the University of Iowa, Iowa City is a vibrant college town with a thriving arts and culture scene.

Conclusion

Cedar Falls is a hidden gem in the Midwest, offering a rich culture and natural beauty. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure or exploring local history and cuisine, Cedar Falls has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the heart of Iowa.
