Located in the northeast part of Iowa, Spillville is a quaint town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a peaceful getaway, Spillville has something for everyone. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to this hidden gem of Midwest.

Introduction to Spillville

Spillville is a small town in Winneshiek County, Iowa, with a population of fewer than 400 people. It was founded in the mid-19th century by Bohemian immigrants and has a rich history and cultural heritage. Spillville is located in the heart of Northeast Iowa’s scenic countryside, offering visitors an authentic and relaxing escape from busy city life.

List of Places to Visit in Spillville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bily Clocks Museum and Antonin Dvorak Exhibit: A must-visit museum that houses a fascinating collection of locally made clocks and watches, in addition to an exhibit dedicated to the time that famed composer Antonin Dvorak spent in Spillville.

  • St. Wenceslaus Church: A historic church with ornate interior decorations painted by the Czech artist Vojtech Preissig.

  • Inwood Ballroom: A historical dance hall that has hosted live music for nearly a century, including famous acts like Glenn Miller and Tommy Dorsey.

Natural Attractions of Spillville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seed Savers Exchange: A non-profit organization that preserves heirloom seeds and promotes sustainable agriculture practices. It offers guided tours of its gardens and farm.

  • Upper Iowa River: Offering opportunities for paddling, fishing, and hiking in scenic surroundings, the river is a great place to appreciate Iowa’s natural beauty.

  • Decorah Fish Hatchery: Take a tour of the trout hatchery, learn about the important role of fish, and enjoy a picnic in the park by the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spillville

  • Prairie Farmer Recreational Trail: A 4.5-mile trail built on a former railroad bed that is suitable for hiking, running, biking, and cross-country skiing.

  • Lower Quarry Loop Trail: A 1.6-mile trail that offers scenic views of the Upper Iowa River and passes by an old limestone quarry.

Best Things to Do in Spillville: Activities and Tours

  • Local Wineries and Breweries Tour: Spillville is home to several local wineries and breweries, offering visitors the opportunity to sample locally produced wines and beers.

  • Eagle Bluff Skills School: A unique outdoor education center located in the nearby town of Lanesboro, Minnesota, offering classes in outdoor skills, crafts, and nature.

  • Driftless Area Scenic Byway: A 100-mile scenic drive that passes through the rugged bluffs, rolling hills, and winding rivers of Iowa, Minnesota, and Wisconsin.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spillville

Day 1: Visit the Bily Clocks Museum and Antonin Dvorak Exhibit, followed by a tour of the St. Wenceslaus Church.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Seed Savers Exchange and hiking along the Upper Iowa River.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Decorah Fish Hatchery, followed by a picnic by the river.

Day 4: Rent bikes and explore the Prairie Farmer Recreational Trail, followed by a visit to the Inwood Ballroom.

Day 5: Take a local winery and brewery tour.

Day 6: Take a class at the Eagle Bluff Skills School.

Day 7: Enjoy a scenic drive along the Driftless Area Scenic Byway.

Food and Drink Scene of Spillville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kolaches: A traditional Czech pastry filled with fruit, cream cheese, or other sweet or savory fillings.

  • Trout: Freshly caught trout cooked with butter and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Local Wines: Spillville is home to several local wineries that produce delicious wines made with locally grown grapes.

  • Craft Beers: Iowa is known for its thriving craft beer scene, and Spillville has its share of local breweries.

Where to Stay in Spillville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Winneshiek: A historic hotel in downtown Decorah, just a short drive from Spillville, that offers elegant accommodation with modern amenities.

  • Top of the Hill: A charming guesthouse that offers lovely views of the Upper Iowa River and is just a short walk from the Lower Quarry Loop Trail.

  • Locally Owned Vacation Rentals: There are several locally owned vacation rentals in and around Spillville, ranging from cozy cabins to spacious farmhouses.

What to Buy in Spillville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wines and Beers: Spillville is known for its locally produced wines and craft beers, which make perfect souvenirs to bring back home.

  • Handmade Crafts: Spillville has a strong arts and crafts tradition, and visitors can purchase handmade quilts, pottery, and other unique items from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Spillville

Spillville has a strong Czech heritage, and visitors can experience the town’s rich cultural traditions by attending the annual Czech Days festival, eating traditional Czech cuisine, visiting local museums, and learning about the town’s history and folklore.

Best Time to Visit Spillville

The best time to visit Spillville is during the summer months, when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall is also a great time to visit, with colorful autumn foliage and harvest festivals in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spillville

Do:

  • Do visit local wineries and breweries to sample locally produced wines and beers.

  • Do take a class at the Eagle Bluff Skills School to learn more about outdoor skills, crafts, and nature.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the Bily Clocks Museum and Antonin Dvorak Exhibit, which offers a fascinating look at Spillville’s history and cultural heritage.

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothing for outdoor activities and hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spillville

Spillville is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Spillville

Spillville is a small town, and medical facilities are limited. Visitors with pre-existing conditions should plan accordingly and bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with them.

Getting to Spillville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spillville

The closest major airport to Spillville is Rochester International Airport in Minnesota, approximately 70 miles away. Eastern Iowa Airport in Cedar Rapids is also a viable option, located approximately 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Spillville

Greyhound and Jefferson Lines provide bus services in nearby cities such as Decorah, Cresco, and Waukon, which are relatively close to Spillville.

Other Options

Driving is the easiest and most convenient way to get to Spillville and explore the surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Spillville

The best way to get around in Spillville and the surrounding region is by car. Taxis and ridesharing services are not widely available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Spillville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Effigy Mounds National Monument: A scenic and historical site located just a short drive from Spillville, featuring preserved Native American burial mounds.

  • Decorah: A charming town just a few miles north of Spillville, offering unique shops, museums, and dining options.

  • Pikes Peak State Park: A popular state park located in McGregor, Iowa, offering scenic overlooks of the surrounding landscape.

Conclusion

Spillville is a hidden gem in Northeast Iowa, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a peaceful getaway, Spillville has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have a memorable trip to this quaint Midwestern town.
