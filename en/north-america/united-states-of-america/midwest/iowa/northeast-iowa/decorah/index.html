Introduction to Decorah

Decorah is a small town in Northeast Iowa, located in the heart of scenic bluff country near the Upper Iowa River. Its charming streets are lined with historic buildings, unique shops, and local eateries, making it a great place for a weekend getaway or a family vacation. Known for its natural beauty, Decorah offers an abundance of outdoor recreational activities, such as hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking. It is also home to numerous cultural and historical attractions, such as museums, art galleries, and festivals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Decorah

Day 1: Explore downtown Decorah, including Vesterheim Norwegian-American museum and Porter House museum.

Day 2: Visit Decorah Trout Hatchery and hike Trout Run Trail.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to local attractions such as Decorah Bald Eagles or Dunning’s Spring Park.

Day 4: Go canoeing or kayaking on Upper Iowa River.

Day 5: Attend local farmers market and visit Seed Savers Exchange Heritage Farm.

Day 6: Tour the Laura Ingalls Wilder Park and Museum and end the day with Nordic Fest.

Day 7: Relax with a day at Decorah Prairie or Ice Cave State Preserve.

Local Culture and Customs of Decorah

Decorah is known for its Norwegian heritage and its annual Nordic Fest. It is also known for its respect of the local land and conservation efforts.
