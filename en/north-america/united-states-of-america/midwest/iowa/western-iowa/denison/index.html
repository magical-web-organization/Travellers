Introduction to Denison

Located in Western Iowa, Denison is a small town with a population of approximately 8,000. Its unique location, nestled between the Boyer and East Boyer Rivers, makes it a prime destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, hiking, and biking trails, and birdwatching. Denison also boasts a rich history and vibrant cultural scene, making it an excellent destination for art and history lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Denison

Denison offers an array of attractions. Here are some must-see spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Crawford County Historical Museum: Located in a historic schoolhouse, this museum showcases the history of Crawford County, from prehistoric times to present day.
  • Yellow Smoke Park: This park offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, and also contains the Yellow Smoke Stone quarry. Visitors can hike the trails, fish in the pond, and explore the quarry.
  • Crawford County Speedway: Racing fans won’t want to miss a trip to the Crawford County Speedway, which has been hosting races for over 50 years.

Natural Attractions of Denison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Denison Aquatic Center: With slides, pools, and a lazy river, the Denison Aquatic Center is a great spot to cool off on a hot day.
  • Schleswig Wildlife Management Area: This area offers over 500 acres of nature, perfect for hiking and wildlife viewing.
  • Yellow Smoke Park: This park offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, and also contains the Yellow Smoke Stone quarry. Visitors can hike the trails, fish in the pond, and explore the quarry.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Denison

Denison is home to several excellent hiking and biking trails.

  • Boy Scout Trail: This 2-mile trail offers scenic views of the Boyer River and wildlife spotting opportunities.
  • Hickory Hills Park: With over 10 miles of hiking and biking trails, this park is the perfect spot for an outdoor adventure.

Best Things to Do in Denison: Activities and Tours

  • Denison Aquatic Center: With slides, pools, and a lazy river, this center is a great spot to cool off on a hot day.
  • Crawford County Speedway: Racing fans won’t want to miss a trip to the Crawford County Speedway, which has been hosting races for over 50 years.
  • Funday Monday: Held every Monday during the summer, this event offers fun activities, food, and entertainment downtown.

Week-Long Itinerary for Denison

Day 1: Arrive and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the town and enjoying a meal at one of the local restaurants.

Day 2: Visit the Crawford County Historical Museum and learn about the town’s past. In the afternoon, take a hike at Hickory Hills Park and enjoy the nature.

Day 3: Attend a race at the Crawford County Speedway. In the afternoon, grab lunch at a local restaurant and head to Yellow Smoke Park for hiking and exploring.

Day 4: Take a guided bike tour of the town and the surrounding countryside. In the evening, attend Funday Monday for food and entertainment.

Day 5: Enjoy a day trip to nearby Des Moines, Iowa’s capital city. Visit the Iowa State Capitol and the Pappajohn Sculpture Park.

Day 6: Spend the day relaxing at the Denison Aquatic Center and picnic lunch in the park.

Day 7: Depart Denison or attend a local event happening in town.

Food and Drink Scene of Denison

Denison offers a variety of dining options, from affordable fast food to upscale dining.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef and pork: Iowa is known for its high-quality meat, and Denison is no exception. Be sure to try a steak or pork dish while in town.
  • Tacos: Denison has a large Hispanic population, and as a result, fantastic taco spots can be found scattered around town.

Local Beverages

  • Kava Coffeehouse: Enjoy a freshly brewed cup of coffee or a creative latte at Kava Coffeehouse.
  • Don Jose’s Mexican Restaurant: Pair your meal with a margarita or other Mexican-inspired drink at Don Jose’s Mexican Restaurant.

  • Denison Farmers Market: Support local businesses and bring home fresh produce and handmade goods from the Denison Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Denison

Denison offers a rich cultural scene due to its diverse population. Iowa hospitality is a significant aspect of the local culture that is emphasized in restaurants, hotels, and shops.

Best Time to Visit Denison

The best time to visit Denison is during the summer months when the town’s outdoor attractions are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Denison

Do:

  • Do sample some of the high-quality meat dishes that Denison is so well-known for.
  • Do take advantage of the town’s outdoor adventures, from kayaking to hiking trails.
  • Do embrace the friendly, hospitable culture of Denison.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful of the town’s diverse population.
  • Don’t ignore the town’s rich history and cultural significance.
  • Don’t forget to bring your sunscreen, as the town can get hot and sunny during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Denison

Denison is a safe town, but visitors should use common sense and take precautions. Avoid leaving valuables in your car and always lock your doors.

Health in Denison

Denison has a hospital and several medical clinics. Bring your insurance card and any necessary medication with you, and stay hydrated during hot weather.

Getting to Denison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Denison

  • Omaha Eppley Airfield: Located 86 miles from Denison, Omaha Eppley Airfield is the closest major airport.
  • Sioux Gateway Airport: Located 92 miles away in Sioux City, Sioux Gateway Airport offers airlines such as Delta, United, and American.

Bus Stations Near Denison

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Sioux City, the Greyhound Bus Station offers bus service to and from Denison.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Denison is best explored by car, and several rental car services are available both in town and at nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Denison

While Denison is a small town, it is best explored by car. However, sidewalks and bike lanes are available throughout the town for those who prefer to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Denison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Des Moines: Located two hours away, Des Moines is Iowa’s capital city and offers a variety of entertainment options, from museums to shopping.
  • Omaha: Omaha, Nebraska, is located just a little over an hour away from Denison and offers excellent restaurants, museums, and sporting events.
  • Sioux City: Sioux City, Iowa, is located an hour and a half away from Denison and offers cultural and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Denison may be a small town, but it packs a punch with a variety of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local dining spots. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed vacation, Denison has something to offer every type of traveler.
