Introduction to Storm Lake

Located in the western side of Iowa, Storm Lake is a picturesque city that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for tourists. It lies in the region known as the Midwest, in the United States of America. The city’s scenic beauty, rich culture, and history attract visitors throughout the year.

In this guide, we will take you through the various tourist attractions, best things to do, local cuisine, places to stay, local customs, safety measures, and transportation options in Storm Lake. Whether you’re planning a day trip or an extended vacation, this guide will assist you in making the most of your visit to this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Storm Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buena Vista County Historical Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of Buena Vista County. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, exhibits, and photographs showcasing the region’s history from the early settlers to modern times.

  • The Harker House: This historic home is a stunning example of Queen Anne Victorian architecture. Built in 1884, the home has been restored to its original state and features antique furnishings and decorative arts.

  • Witter Gallery: This art museum features works from local and regional artists. It offers a range of exhibits, workshops, and classes throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Storm Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Storm Lake: The jewel of the city, Storm Lake is a 3,200-acre natural lake that offers visitors a range of water-based activities such as swimming, boating, fishing, and sailing. The lake boasts of crystal clear water and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Chautauqua Park: This park offers a range of amenities such as picnic areas, playgrounds, and walking trails. It also hosts various concerts and events throughout the year.

  • Sunrise Point: Located on the eastern shore of Storm Lake, this viewpoint offers stunning views of the sunrise. It is a popular spot for photography and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Storm Lake

Storm Lake offers ample opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Lakeshore Trail: This 12.5-mile trail offers stunning views of the lake. It is perfect for walkers, runners, and bikers.

  • The Little Storm Lake Trail: This trail is a 2.2-mile loop that takes visitors through a wetland area and offers views of local wildlife.

  • The Buena Vista Trail: This 10.7-mile trail takes visitors through the scenic countryside of Buena Vista County.

Best Things to Do in Storm Lake: Activities and Tours

  • Paddle boarding: Storm Lake is a perfect spot for paddle boarding. Visitors can rent paddleboards from various outlets and explore the lake at their leisure.

  • Fishing: Storm Lake offers excellent fishing opportunities for both professionals and amateurs. The lake is home to a wide range of fish species, including walleye, catfish, and largemouth bass.

  • Sailboat races: If you’re a sailing enthusiast, don’t miss the sailing races that take place on Storm Lake during the summer months.

Week-Long Itinerary for Storm Lake

Day 1: Explore the city’s historic landmarks such as The Harker House and Buena Vista County Historical Museum.

Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride along the Lakeshore Trail and enjoy the scenic views of Storm Lake.

Day 3: Indulge in water sports such as swimming, boating, or fishing in Storm Lake.

Day 4: Visit the Witter Gallery and explore its exhibits.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby attractions such as Albert City Tractor Museum or the Grotto of the Redemption.

Day 6: Enjoy local cuisine at The Boz Wellz or Lake Avenue Lounge.

Day 7: Relax at Chautauqua Park and watch the sunset over Storm Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Storm Lake

Must-Try Dishes

  • Corn-fed beef: Iowa is known for its high-quality beef, and Storm Lake has some excellent restaurants that serve delicious corn-fed beef.

  • Sweet corn: One of the seasonal produce that Iowa is known for, sweet corn is a must-try for food lovers visiting Storm Lake during the summer.

  • Pork tenderloin: A Midwest classic, the pork tenderloin sandwich is a must-try in Storm Lake.

Local Beverages

  • Storm Lake Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a range of craft beers that are brewed on-site.

  • Beaver Creek Vineyards: This winery offers a range of wines that are made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Storm Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, Storm Lake has something to offer for all types of travelers. Some recommendations include:

  • King’s Pointe Waterpark Resort: This family-friendly resort offers a range of amenities such as a water park, indoor and outdoor pools, and a spa.

  • AmericInn by Wyndham Storm Lake: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms with amenities such as a pool and fitness center.

  • The Inn at Lake Avenue: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Storm Lake and offers a homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Storm Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Corn souvenirs: Iowa’s cornfields are renowned, and visitors can buy souvenirs such as popcorn or corn-shaped memorabilia to take home.

  • Hand-crafted products: Storm Lake has several shops that offer hand-crafted products made by local artisans. Visitors can buy items such as pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Storm Lake

Storm Lake is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors are expected to show respect towards the local customs and traditions. Iowans have a strong work ethic and value honesty and integrity in their business dealings.

Best Time to Visit Storm Lake

The best time to visit Storm Lake is during the summer months from June to September. The weather is warm, and visitors can take part in various outdoor activities such as swimming, boating, and hiking. Visitors can also enjoy the sailing races that take place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Storm Lake

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy the scenic beauty and natural attractions of the city.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Observe the safety precautions while engaging in water-based activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Overindulge in alcohol or other substances.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Storm Lake

Storm Lake is a safe city, but visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Some tips include:

  • Always wear a personal floatation device while engaging in water sports.
  • Lock your vehicles and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Storm Lake

Storm Lake has several medical facilities that provide quality healthcare services to visitors.

How to Get Around in Storm Lake

Storm Lake is a small city, and visitors can explore it on foot or by using taxis. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the city and its surroundings on their own.

Where to Go Next from Storm Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Okoboji: Okoboji is a popular tourist destination and is located about an hour’s drive from Storm Lake. It offers a range of attractions such as amusement parks, lake-based activities, and scenic hiking trails.

  • Des Moines: The capital city of Iowa, Des Moines, is located about 2.5 hours from Storm Lake. It offers a range of cultural and historical attractions, including museums, art galleries, and botanical gardens.

Conclusion

Storm Lake is a hidden gem in the Midwest, and visitors will find plenty of activities and attractions to enjoy during their stay. From outdoor adventures to cultural exploration, Storm Lake has something to offer for all types of travelers. This guide should help visitors plan their trip to Storm Lake and make the most of their time in this beautiful city.
