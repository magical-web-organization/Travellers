Introduction to Spencer

Located in Western Iowa, Spencer is a charming small town that offers plenty of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. From cultural landmarks to stunning natural scenery, Spencer has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, hike and bike routes, things to do, where to stay and eat, local customs, safety and health concerns, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Spencer

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing weekend getaway, Spencer has plenty of attractions and landmarks to explore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Clay County Heritage Center is a must-visit for history lovers, showcasing the area’s rich cultural heritage. The Parker Historical Museum is another standout, featuring exhibits on local history, including the Great Lakes Naval Training Station. For those wanting to explore the local art scene, the Spencer Area Arts Council Gallery is a must-visit.

Natural Attractions of Spencer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Spencer offers plenty of options. The Prairie Heritage Center offers numerous trails to hike and explore, highlighting the area’s unique prairie landscape. The Gull Point State Park is also a great spot to explore, featuring scenic views of the West Okoboji Lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spencer

Spencer offers numerous hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular routes include the Little Sioux River Trail, the Clay County Recreation Trail, and the Dickinson County Trail System. These well-maintained paths are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Spencer: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling alone or with family, Spencer offers plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Explore the area’s unique culture through local festivals, including the Iowa Great Lakes Renaissance Festival or the Clay County Fair. For a more adrenaline-fueled adventure, go zip-lining at the Clay County Regional Events Center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spencer

Day 1: Visit the Clay County Heritage Center and explore the local art scene at the Spencer Area Arts Council Gallery. Day 2: Hike the Little Sioux River Trail and soak in scenic views. Day 3: Explore the Parker Historical Museum and the Dickinson County Trail System. Day 4: Bike the Clay County Recreation Trail and enjoy a local festival or event. Day 5: Visit the Prairie Heritage Center and learn about the unique prairie landscape of the area. Day 6: Go zip-lining at the Clay County Regional Events Center or go fishing at the West Okoboji Lake. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Spencer

When it comes to food and drink, Spencer won’t disappoint. With a variety of options ranging from comfort food to locally sourced cuisine, here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

The Waterfront is a local favorite, serving up classic American fare. For a taste of locally sourced cuisine, check out Cafe Munchies.

Local Beverages

A trip to Spencer wouldn’t be complete without sampling some of the area’s local beers. The West O Brewery and Okoboji Brewing Company are both great spots to sample the local brews.

Where to Stay in Spencer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spencer offers plenty of options when it comes to accommodations. From budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels, here are some of the top spots to stay in Spencer.

Budget-Friendly Accommodation

The Days Inn Spencer and Super 8 by Wyndham Spencer are both great options for travelers on a budget.

Mid-Range Accommodation

For mid-range accommodations, check out the AmericInn by Wyndham Spencer or the Best Western Plus Spencer Inn & Suites.

Luxury Accommodation

For those looking for a bit more luxury, the Cobblestone Hotel & Suites and the Diamond Jo Worth Casino are both great options.

What to Buy in Spencer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to take home? The Clay County Fairgrounds Marketplace offers an array of local products, from art to handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Spencer

Spencer is known for its friendly community and laid-back atmosphere. When visiting, be sure to embrace the local hospitality and take the time to explore the unique cultural offerings of the area.

Best Time to Visit Spencer

The best time to visit Spencer is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spencer

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the local history and art scene.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Do embrace the friendly community atmosphere.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate outdoor gear, as many activities are outdoors.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and culture.
  • Don’t forget to take the time to relax and enjoy the laid-back atmosphere.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spencer

Spencer is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always important to take standard safety precautions when exploring any new area.

Health in Spencer

The local healthcare system in Spencer is well-equipped to handle any health concerns travelers may have during their visit.

Getting to Spencer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re flying in or driving, Spencer is easily accessible from a variety of locations.

Airports Near Spencer

The closest airports to Spencer are the Sioux Gateway Airport and the Des Moines International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Spencer

Jefferson Lines offers bus service to Spencer from a variety of locations.

Other Options

For those driving, Spencer is easily accessible from I-90 and U.S. Highway 18.

How to Get Around in Spencer

While Spencer is a small town, having a car is recommended to fully explore all the area has to offer. However, biking and hiking are also great options to explore the local outdoor scenery.

Where to Go Next from Spencer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Midwest adventures, there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Sioux Falls, South Dakota and Omaha, Nebraska are both great options within a few hours’ drive.

Conclusion

Spencer, Iowa may be a small town, but it’s full of charm and character. Between the cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and friendly community atmosphere, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.
