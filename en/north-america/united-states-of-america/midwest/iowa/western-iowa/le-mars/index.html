Introduction to Le Mars

Nestled in the northwest corner of Iowa, Le Mars is a charming city known for its beautiful countryside, rich history, and friendly community. It’s famously known as the “Ice Cream Capital of the World” due to the large production of ice cream in the city, but there’s much more to see and do in this bustling town than just enjoy a cold treat.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the area’s natural beauty, delving into its history and culture, or seeking out some of the best food and drink options in the Midwest, Le Mars is a destination that won’t disappoint. Join us as we explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in this comprehensive travel guide for Le Mars, Iowa.

List of Places to Visit in Le Mars

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Le Mars has a rich cultural history that is best experienced through a visit to its museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit spots:

  1. Plymouth County Historical Museum: This museum is a treasure trove of artifacts and exhibits commemorating the history and heritage of Plymouth County, including displays on early settlers, Native Americans, agricultural history, and more.

  2. Olson Cultural Event Center: This center is home to a range of exhibits and activities that showcase the art, music, and cultural traditions of the region.

  3. Brunsville Historical Park: Take a step back in time at this historical park, which features a one-room schoolhouse, a pioneer cabin, and other relics from the region’s past.

Natural Attractions of Le Mars: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Le Mars is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  1. Floyd River: This picturesque river is a popular spot for canoeing, kayaking, fishing, and picnicking, as well as enjoying scenic views of the surrounding area.

  2. Plymouth County Fairgrounds: This sprawling fairgrounds host a range of events throughout the year, including rodeos, fairs, and concerts, as well as hiking and biking trails, playgrounds, and other outdoor recreation areas.

  3. Le Mars Bike Trail: This scenic bike trail winds through the heart of the city, providing a great way to explore the city’s architecture, parks, and urban landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Le Mars

Le Mars offers a range of scenic hiking and biking routes that are perfect for exploring the area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best:

  1. Le Mars Bike Trail: This scenic trail winds through the heart of the city, providing a great way to explore the downtown area and surrounding neighborhoods.

  2. Plymouth County Fairgrounds Trails: The fairgrounds are home to a variety of trails, from easy strolls to more challenging hikes, that offer great views of the area’s natural beauty.

  3. Floyd River Trail: This trail follows the meandering course of the Floyd River through some of the region’s most scenic landscapes, from woodlands to wetlands to prairies.

Best Things to Do in Le Mars: Activities and Tours

Le Mars is home to a wide variety of activities and tours that are sure to appeal to everyone, whether you’re looking for adventure, culture, or relaxation. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Ice Cream Capital of the World Visitor Center: This center is a must-visit for anyone who loves ice cream, offering tours of the local Blue Bunny ice cream factory and plenty of fun exhibits and hands-on activities.

  2. Le Mars Community Theatre: Catch a show at this bustling community theater, which hosts a variety of plays, musicals, and other performances throughout the year.

  3. Rock Rapids, Iowa Tours: Take a tour of the nearby town of Rock Rapids to learn more about the region’s history and culture, including visits to the Lyon County Historical Museum and the nearby Grand Falls Casino.

Week-Long Itinerary for Le Mars

If you’re planning a longer stay in Le Mars, here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 1: Explore the Plymouth County Historical Museum and the Olson Cultural Event Center to learn more about the region’s history and cultural heritage.

Day 2: Bike or hike along the city’s many scenic trails, including the Le Mars Bike Trail and Plymouth County Fairgrounds Trails.

Day 3: Enjoy a picnic or fishing excursion along the Floyd River, or take a leisurely stroll through one of the city’s many beautiful parks and gardens.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Rock Rapids, where you can explore the area’s cultural attractions and natural beauty.

Day 5: Sign up for a tour of the Blue Bunny ice cream factory, or visit the Ice Cream Capital of the World Visitor Center to learn more about the city’s sweet history.

Day 6: Catch a performance at the Le Mars Community Theatre, or attend one of the many concerts, festivals, and other events held throughout the year.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the city’s many top-rated hotels, hostels, or guesthouses, or hit up some of the area’s best restaurants and bars for a taste of the local cuisine and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Le Mars

No trip to Le Mars would be complete without sampling some of the city’s finest food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ice cream: Of course, no trip to the “Ice Cream Capital of the World” would be complete without sampling some of the local Blue Bunny ice cream.

  2. Pizza Ranch: This popular local pizza chain is a must-visit for anyone looking for delicious, family-style pizza and other hearty comfort foods.

  3. Molly’s Diner: This charming local diner is the perfect spot to enjoy classic American fare, from burgers and fries to milkshakes and apple pie.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft beers: Le Mars is home to several top-rated craft breweries, including Remedy Brewing Company and Keg Creek Brewing Company.

  2. Wineries: The region is also known for its excellent wines, with several wineries offering tours and tastings all year round.

  3. Coffee shops: For a cozy cup of coffee or tea, head to one of the city’s many coffee shops and cafes, such as Family Table Restaurant and Koffee Kup.

  2. Plymouth County Fairgrounds Camping: For a more rustic experience, you can camp at the fairgrounds, which offers RV and tent camping sites, as well as easy access to hiking and biking trails.

Local Culture and Customs of Le Mars

Le Mars is a friendly, welcoming community that celebrates its unique cultural heritage and traditions. Some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  1. The annual “Ice Cream Days” festival, held every summer and featuring parades, concerts, and plenty of delicious ice cream treats.

  2. The area’s rich agricultural heritage, which is celebrated through events like the Plymouth County Fair and the “Harvest Festival” held every fall.

  3. The importance of family and community in the local culture, with many events and festivals focusing on bringing people together for fun, food, and fellowship.

Best Time to Visit Le Mars

The best time to visit Le Mars largely depends on your interests and preferences. Here are some things to keep in mind when planning your trip:

  1. Summer: The summer months are a popular time to visit, due to the warm weather, outdoor festivals and events, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation.

  2. Fall: The fall is a beautiful time to visit Le Mars, with gorgeous autumn foliage and a range of fall-themed events and festivals.

  3. Winter: While the winter months can be cold and snowy, they are also a great time to enjoy winter sports like skiing, snowshoeing, and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Le Mars

When visiting Le Mars, here are some important things to keep in mind:

Do:

  2. Take advantage of the area’s abundant outdoor recreation opportunities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and more.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Le Mars, it’s important to take steps to stay safe and healthy. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Le Mars

  1. Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to stay safe, especially when out hiking, biking, or exploring more remote parts of the area.

  2. Keep your valuables secure, especially when traveling with large amounts of cash or expensive electronic devices.

  3. Use common sense when driving or navigating rural roads, especially in unpredictable weather conditions.

Health in Le Mars

  2. Stay hydrated, especially during hot summer months, and be sure to protect yourself from the sun with sunscreen and a hat or other protective clothing.

  2. Jefferson Lines Bus Station (FSD): This station is located in Sioux Falls, South Dakota, about 75 miles from Le Mars.

Other Options

  2. Private Transportation: There are several private transportation companies that offer shuttle services, limousine services, and other transportation options for visitors to the area.

How to Get Around in Le Mars

Le Mars is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, if you prefer to drive, rental cars are readily available, and there are plenty of parking options throughout the city. Public transportation options are limited, but there are several private transportation services that offer shuttle services and other options for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Le Mars: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Le Mars, there are plenty of great nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Here are some ideas:
