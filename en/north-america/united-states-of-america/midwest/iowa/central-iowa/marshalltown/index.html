Introduction to Marshalltown

Located in the heart of Central Iowa, Marshalltown is a charming city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Marshalltown has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Marshalltown’s best attractions, activities, and hidden gems.

List of Places to Visit in Marshalltown

Here’s a rundown of the top places to visit in Marshalltown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Marshalltown History Center - This museum showcases the history of Marshalltown dating back to the mid-1800s through various exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.

  2. Raymon drive landmark district - A popular spot among history enthusiasts, this district features a collection of well-preserved Victorian-era homes.

  3. Fisher Community Center Art Gallery - This art gallery features works of local artists and regularly hosts exhibitions and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Marshalltown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Linn Creek Trail - This 6-mile trail winds through scenic countryside and is popular among hikers and bikers alike.

  2. Green Castle Recreation Area - This 400-acre park offers a wide range of outdoor activities like boating, fishing, hiking, and camping.

  3. Marshalltown Aquatic Center - This facility boasts a massive outdoor swimming pool complex with water slides, splash pads, and a lazy river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marshalltown

Marshalltown has several excellent hiking and biking trails:

  1. High Trestle Trail - This 25-mile trail begins in Ankeny and passes through rural countryside, small towns, and woodlands before reaching Marshalltown.

  2. Linn Creek Trail - This scenic trail starts at Linn Creek Park and follows the course of Linn Creek, passing through wooded areas and open prairies.

Best Things to Do in Marshalltown: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Marshalltown:

  1. Take a trolley tour - See the best of Marshalltown’s historical landmarks and attractions in a trolley tour.

  2. Visit the Iowa River Brewing Company - A popular hangout spot for locals, this brewery boasts unique flavors of craft beer and a laid-back atmosphere.

  3. Attend the Central Iowa Fair - This annual event features a huge range of activities and attractions, including carnival rides, food vendors, live music and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marshalltown

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Marshalltown:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Marshalltown History Center and Raymon drive landmark district
Day 2 Hike the Linn Creek Trail and visit the Fisher Community Center Art Gallery
Day 3 Go boating or fishing at Green Castle Recreation Area

Food and Drink Scene of Marshalltown

Marshalltown offers a diverse range of food and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pork Tenderloin Sandwich - A staple of Iowa cuisine, this sandwich features a crispy breaded pork tenderloin served on a bun with toppings like lettuce, tomato, pickles, and mayo.

  2. Corn on the Cob - Sweet corn is a staple of Midwestern cuisine and is available at many local restaurants and farmer’s markets.

  3. Beef Steak - Iowa is known for its high-quality beef, and a juicy steak is a must-try when visiting Marshalltown.

Local Beverages

  1. Iowa Craft Beer - There are several breweries in Marshalltown and the surrounding areas, offering unique flavors and styles of craft beer.

  2. Sweet Corn Beer - A specialty of Iowa breweries, this beer is brewed with locally-sourced sweet corn for a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Marshalltown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marshalltown offers a wide range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  1. Baymont by Wyndham Marshalltown - A top-rated hotel with excellent facilities like an indoor pool, hot tub, fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.

  2. Super 8 by Wyndham Marshalltown - A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and Wi-Fi access.

  3. Red’s Retreat - A peaceful and cozy guesthouse located in a lush green garden setting.

What to Buy in Marshalltown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Marshalltown:

  1. Sweet corn - Iowa is known for its sweet corn, which is available for purchase at farmer’s markets and roadside stands.

  2. Handcrafted items - Marshalltown is home to several local artists and artisans who create unique items like pottery, paintings, and jewelry.

  3. Local cheese - Several local creameries in the area produce delicious artisanal cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Marshalltown

Marshalltown is a welcoming and friendly community that values family, hard work, and kindness. Residents take pride in their city and its history and are always happy to share their stories and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Marshalltown

The best time to visit Marshalltown is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is pleasant and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events. However, the city has something to offer all year round, with various winter sports and indoor attractions in the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marshalltown

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Marshalltown:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Pack comfortable clothes and shoes for outdoor activities.

  3. Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public spaces.

  2. Ignore the safety rules and guidelines.

  3. Disrespect the local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marshalltown

Marshalltown is generally a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should be mindful of their belongings and surroundings, especially in crowded areas or after dark.

Health in Marshalltown

Marshalltown has several healthcare facilities and hospitals, including the Central Iowa Healthcare Center, offering a wide range of medical services.

Getting to Marshalltown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marshalltown

The nearest airport to Marshalltown is the Des Moines International Airport, located approximately 60 miles south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Marshalltown

Greyhound operates from a station in Marshalltown, offering bus services to other parts of Iowa and neighboring states.

Other Options

Travelers can also consider renting a car or taking a taxi or rideshare service to reach Marshalltown.

How to Get Around in Marshalltown

Marshalltown has several options for getting around the city, including car rentals, taxis, and bike rentals. The city also offers a public transportation system (M-trans) with regular bus routes across the city.

Where to Go Next from Marshalltown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Marshalltown:

  1. Ames - A vibrant college town located about 30 miles east of Marshalltown, offering various outdoor activities and cultural events.

  2. Des Moines - The capital city of Iowa is located about 60 miles south of Marshalltown and offers a plethora of attractions and places to visit, including the Blank Park Zoo, the Pappajohn Sculpture Park, and the Iowa State Capitol.

Conclusion

Marshalltown may be a small city, but it offers a wealth of attractions, activities, and hidden gems for travelers of all interests. We hope this comprehensive tour guide helps you plan your trip to this charming town with ease and excitement.
