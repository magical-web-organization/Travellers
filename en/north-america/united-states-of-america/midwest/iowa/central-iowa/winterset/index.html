Introduction to Winterset

Nestled in Central Iowa’s Madison County, Winterset offers breathtaking views and rich history. Often known for being the birthplace of the famous actor John Wayne, Winterset is a gateway to the Midwest’s natural beauty. This small town, surrounded by hills, is perfect for those who seek adventures. Even better, the town is only a 30-minute drive away from Iowa’s state capital, Des Moines. So, let’s dive into the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Winterset.

List of Places to Visit in Winterset

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

John Wayne Birthplace Museum

At the John Wayne Birthplace Museum, you can dive into the history of the famous actor, his life, and achievements. The museum has over 40,000 artifacts from Wayne’s personal and professional life. Visitors can see the original birthplace home, including the furniture, clothing, and many more. Moreover, the gift shop is perfect for movie enthusiasts, and you can buy souvenirs of John Wayne to bring home.

Madison County Courthouse

The Madison County Courthouse, built-in 1876, is one of the most prominent architectural gems of the town. The courthouse has a local history museum that showcases Madison County’s rich history and achievements. The building is an excellent example of Second Empire architecture, and visitors can explore the courthouse, including the jail cells and a library on the second floor.

The Iowa Quilt Museum

The Iowa Quilt Museum is a must-visit for art enthusiasts. This tiny museum has a vast collection of Quilts. It is a permanent collection of 900+ quilts, including 500+ from Iowa and 350+ from other countries. Here, you can learn the art of quilting and admire complex designs.

Natural Attractions of Winterset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pammel State Park

Pammel State Park is where nature enthusiasts must visit. Located five minutes drive away from Winterset, the State Park is flanked by the Middle River and offers fantastic views. The Park is known for its catch-and-release trout fishing, and visitors can also spot deer and turkey while hiking or biking. You can also rent canoes and kayaks from the park office.

Madison County Covered Bridges

Madison County has six wooden covered bridges. These covered bridges are famous for their unique designs and history. The Hogback Covered Bridge is one of the oldest covered bridges in Iowa and is famous for its arched entrance. If you prefer a scenic view, then Cedar Bridge is the right choice as it is an ideal backdrop for photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Winterset

Cedar Lake Loop Trail

The Cedar Lake Loop Trail is a flat, 2-mile path across a wooden bridge and the Cedar Lake. It is perfect for beginners and families with kids. The trail also has a few resting benches, so visitors can sit back and enjoy the view.

Covered Bridges Trail

The Covered Bridges Trail is a 6-mile trail that connects all of the covered bridges of Madison County. The trail is perfect for biking or hiking, and visitors can have a picnic at the parks along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Winterset: Activities and Tours

Winterset Art Center

The Winterset Art Center is a nonprofit organization that focuses on creating awareness about art among the locals. The Art Center has permanent and temporary exhibitions. Moreover, visitors can participate in art classes and workshops too.

Madison County Scenic Drive

The Madison County Scenic Drive is a 76-mile route that covers Winterset, Earlham, and St. Charles. This route passes through covered bridges, quaint villages, and rolling hills. Visitors can stop at the shops and cafes, and spend a relaxing day at the charming countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Winterset

Must-Try Dishes

Northside Cafe

The Northside Cafe is famous for its classic American dishes, including mouthwatering burgers and sandwiches. It is an excellent place for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. They also have a bakery, so make sure to try their fresh pastries.

The Winterset Husky Deli

The Winterset Husky Deli provides Italian-American comfort food, including pastas, pizzas, and sandwiches. The Deli also uses local ingredients in their cuisine, so visitors can try local flavor here.

Local Beverages

Covered Bridges Winery

The Covered Bridges Winery offers an extensive wine list and a relaxed environment. Visitors can start with a wine tasting and buy their favorite wine bottles from the shop. The Winery also hosts events like live music and wine pairings.

Jefferson County Ciderworks

The Jefferson County Ciderworks offers hand-crafted cider, and visitors can see the cider-making process from the orchards to the bottle. The Ciderworks also has a tasting room, so visitors can try their hard cider.

Where to Stay in Winterset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cobblestone Inn & Suites Winterset

The Cobblestone Inn and Suites is famous for business and leisure trips. The hotel is near attractions like the Covered Bridges and Pammel State Park. The Inn offers free Wi-Fi, an indoor pool, and fitness center.

Windsor Manor Assisted Living

Windsor Manor Assisted Living is an excellent choice for seniors and families. It offers an elegant living space, and amenities like personal care services, housekeeping, and transportation.

What to Buy in Winterset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Iowa Quilt Museum Gift Shop

The Iowa Quilt Museum Gift Shop provides unique quilts, fabric, and exclusive quilt-themed merchandise. It is perfect for visitors who love art and handcrafted items.

John Wayne Birthplace Gift Shop

The John Wayne Birthplace Gift Shop provides exclusive John Wayne merchandise, including books, movies, DVDs, and posters. The shop has souvenirs for visitors of all ages.

Local Culture and Customs of Winterset

Winterset is a town with proud traditions and values. Visitors should respect the local customs and engaging with locals. The town celebrates many festivals and cultural events throughout the year, including the Madison County Festival, Covered Bridge Festival, and the Iowa Quilt Show.

Best Time to Visit Winterset

The best time to visit Winterset is from March to May or from September to November. The weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and events without any difficulty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winterset

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Shop from the local stores and farms to support the community.
  • Visit museums and historical places to learn about the town’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural or historical sites.
  • Don’t miss the scenic view of Madison County.
  • Don’t miss the chance to try the local food and drinks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Winterset

Winterset is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should, however, take precautions while exploring nature as they would in any other natural environment. Hiking and biking routes have signs, so visitors should follow them and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Winterset

Winterset has good healthcare facilities. Visitors can get medical help from Winterset Madison County Hospital or from clinics.

Getting to Winterset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Winterset

The nearest airport to Winterset is Des Moines International Airport (DSM) located 27 miles away. Visitors can hire a taxi, rental cars, or shuttle services from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Winterset

Greyhouse Bus Lines Inc. offers charter bus services to and from Winterset. Both one-way and round-trip bus services are available from the nearby towns of Des Moines and Ames.

Other Options

Visitors can drive to Winterset by taking Interstate 80’s exit 110 or Highway 92.

How to Get Around in Winterset

Winterset is a small town, and visitors can walk or ride a bike. Visitors can also use rental cars or hire taxis to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Winterset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Des Moines

Des Moines, Iowa capital, is only a 30-minute drive away from Winterset. Visitors can explore Des Moines’ historical and cultural sites, including the Iowa State Capitol, the Des Moines Art Center, and the Science Center of Iowa.

Pella

Pella, located 60 miles away from Winterset, is a charming Dutch town famous for its unique architecture and Tulip Time festival. Visitors can explore Pella’s historic sites, antique shops, and bakeries.

Conclusion

Winterset is a marvelous town that offers visitors a taste of the Midwest’s natural beauty and rich history. Visitors can explore the covered bridges, go for hiking, and enjoy local cuisine and drinks. Moreover, the small town is an excellent stop for a day or a weekend trip.
