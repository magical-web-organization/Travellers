Introduction to West Des Moines

Located in Central Iowa, West Des Moines is a rapidly growing city that boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a range of exciting opportunities for tourists. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, West Des Moines has something for everyone.

There are plenty of things to do in West Des Moines, including exploring its museums and historical landmarks, hiking or biking through its scenic trails, and trying out its delicious local cuisine. With its laid-back atmosphere and friendly locals, West Des Moines is a great place to unwind and relax.

So if you’re planning a trip to Iowa, make sure to add West Des Moines to your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in West Des Moines

West Des Moines is full of interesting places to explore. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jordan Creek Town Center - This shopping center is the perfect place to experience the best of West Des Moines’ retail scene. Featuring over 120 stores and restaurants, Jordan Creek Town Center is the largest mall in Iowa and a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

  • Valley Junction - Known for its unique shopping and dining offerings, Valley Junction is a historic district that boasts over 150 shops, art galleries, and restaurants.

  • Living History Farms - This interactive museum offers visitors a chance to experience life on a Midwestern farm as it was over 100 years ago. With hands-on activities, live demonstrations, and educational exhibits, Living History Farms is a great way to learn about Iowa’s agricultural heritage.

  • Jordan House Museum - Built in 1850, the Jordan House is a historic landmark that offers guided tours of the house and its surroundings. Visitors can learn about the role of the Jordan family in the Underground Railroad and Civil War history.

Natural Attractions of West Des Moines: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Raccoon River Park - This 630-acre park is home to some of Iowa’s most stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife. Visitors can hike, fish, bike, or boat on the lake and enjoy a variety of recreational activities.

  • Jester Park - Located on the western shore of Saylorville Lake, Jester Park is the perfect destination for camping, hiking, and fishing. The park offers a range of activities including boating, horseback riding, and picnicking.

  • Gray’s Lake Park - With its 167-acre lake, Gray’s Lake Park is a popular spot for kayaking, canoeing, and paddle boating. The park also features a walking trail, a playground, and a fountain that lights up at night.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Des Moines

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of West Des Moines is through its hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Clive Greenbelt Trail - This 11-mile trail spans from Water Works Park to the western edge of Clive. Featuring a mix of concrete and asphalt trails, it’s ideal for both hiking and biking.

  • Raccoon River Valley Trail - This 89-mile trail is one of the longest paved bike paths in Iowa and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Whether you’re an experienced cyclist or a casual rider, the Raccoon River Valley Trail is a must-see.

Best Things to Do in West Des Moines: Activities and Tours

With plenty of recreational and cultural activities in West Des Moines, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Wine Tasting at Summerset Winery - Located just a short drive from West Des Moines, Summerset Winery offers visitors a chance to sample locally produced wines in a beautiful setting.

  • Ghost Tours of Valley Junction - Explore the haunted history of West Des Moines with a ghost tour of Valley Junction. Learn about the area’s paranormal activity and the tales of the restless souls who still call it home.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Des Moines

Day 1: Explore Valley Junction

Day 2: Visit the Jordan House Museum and Living History Farms

Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Clive Greenbelt Trail

Day 4: Enjoy a day of outdoor activities at Raccoon River Park

Day 5: Visit Jester Park for a picnic, hiking, and fishing

Day 6: Tour Summerset Winery and sample their wines

Day 7: Take a ghost tour of Valley Junction

Food and Drink Scene of West Des Moines

West Des Moines is home to a thriving culinary scene and its own local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich - This famous Iowa dish is a breaded pork cutlet that’s bigger than the bun it’s served on. One of the best places to try this sandwich is at Nick’s Restaurant in nearby Des Moines.

  • Iowa Sweet Corn - During the summer months, sweet corn is a staple in Iowa’s cuisine. Look out for it on restaurant menus and at local farmers’ markets.

Local Beverages

  • Iowa Craft Beers - Iowa is home to a variety of top-notch craft breweries. Make sure to try some locally brewed beers at places like Fox Brewing or Firetrucker Brewery.

  • Maid-Rites - This classic Iowa loose meat sandwich is often paired with a refreshing Cherry Coke, a local tradition.

Where to Stay in West Des Moines: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Des Moines has plenty of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hyatt Place West Des Moines/Jordan Creek - Located near Jordan Creek Town Center, this hotel offers guests comfortable rooms, a 24/7 fitness center, and an indoor pool.

  • Extended Stay America West Des Moines - Ideal for budget travelers, this hotel offers apartment-style suites with kitchenettes and free Wi-Fi.

  • Breitbach’s Country Inn - A charming guesthouse with a cozy fireplace and a complimentary breakfast, located just a short drive outside of the city.

What to Buy in West Des Moines: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

West Des Moines is known for its unique stores and local products. Here are some souvenirs to bring home:

  • Local Artwork - Valley Junction is known for its art galleries featuring works by local artists.

  • Handmade Pottery - Visit the Iowa Ceramic Center and Glass Studio to buy beautiful, hand-crafted pottery pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of West Des Moines

West Des Moines is a friendly and welcoming city that values its cultural heritage. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Midwestern Courtesy - West Des Moines residents are known for their friendliness and politeness.

  • Farm Heritage - The area has a deep farming heritage, so visitors can expect to see plenty of rural landscapes and agricultural activities.

Best Time to Visit West Des Moines

The best time to visit West Des Moines is during the spring, summer, and fall months. The weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Keep in mind that Iowa can experience severe weather, with tornadoes and thunderstorms common during the spring and summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Des Moines

Do:

  • Take in the natural beauty - Whether it’s hiking or cycling, don’t miss out on the chance to explore West Des Moines’ stunning parks and trails.

  • Try local cuisine - From the famous pork tenderloin sandwich to craft beers, there’s plenty of delicious food and drink to sample.

Don’t:

  • Litter - West Des Moines is a beautiful city, so make sure to keep it that way by disposing of trash properly.

  • Disrespect the local culture - Be respectful of the area’s history and customs, and be mindful of the people who call it home.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Des Moines

West Des Moines is generally a safe city, but visitors should always take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Be aware of your surroundings, lock up your car and hotel room, and don’t leave valuables unattended.

Health in West Des Moines

West Des Moines has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Make sure to have health insurance and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to West Des Moines: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Des Moines

  • Des Moines International Airport - Located just 20 minutes from West Des Moines, this airport serves domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near West Des Moines

  • Jefferson Bus Lines - Located in Des Moines, this bus company services cities across the Midwest.

Other Options

  • Driving - West Des Moines is easily accessible by car, with major highways including I-80 and I-35.

How to Get Around in West Des Moines

West Des Moines is a car-dependent city, but there are some transportation options available. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi or Uber, or use the local bus service, DART.

Where to Go Next from West Des Moines: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Des Moines - Iowa’s capital city is just a short drive east of West Des Moines and offers museums, shopping, and nightlife.

  • Pella - Known for its Dutch heritage, this charming town is just an hour’s drive south of West Des Moines and features a historic square, Dutch bakeries, and windmills.

Conclusion

West Des Moines is a hidden gem in the heart of Iowa. With its unique mix of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, it’s the perfect destination for your next vacation. From exploring local museums to hiking through scenic trails, there’s something for everyone in West Des Moines.
