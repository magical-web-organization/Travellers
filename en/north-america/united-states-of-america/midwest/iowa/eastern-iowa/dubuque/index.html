Discovering Dubuque: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Located in Eastern Iowa, Dubuque is a charming city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Dubuque has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything Dubuque has to offer, including its top attractions, best hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Dubuque

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, Dubuque has a variety of attractions for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top places to visit in Dubuque:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dubuque’s rich history is on display throughout the city, with a variety of museums and historical sites to visit. Some of the must-see cultural attractions in Dubuque include:

  • National Mississippi River Museum & Aquarium: This museum showcases the history and ecology of the Mississippi River, including live animal exhibits and immersive experiences.
  • Dubuque Museum of Art: This museum features a collection of over 2,200 works of art, including pieces by Grant Wood and other regional artists.
  • The Mathias Ham Historic Site: This museum provides a glimpse into life in the 19th century, with tours of a restored Victorian mansion and a log cabin.
  • Dubuque Arboretum and Botanical Gardens: This 52-acre park features over 1,200 different plant species and a variety of beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Dubuque: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dubuque’s natural beauty is one of its biggest draws, with plenty of outdoor adventures to enjoy. Some of the top natural attractions in Dubuque include:

  • Eagle Point Park: This scenic park features panoramic views of the Mississippi River, as well as hiking trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds.
  • Mines of Spain Recreation Area: This 1,400-acre park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.
  • The Mississippi Riverwalk: This scenic walkway follows the Mississippi River, offering beautiful views of the water and the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dubuque

Dubuque is a great destination for hiking and biking, with a variety of trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Dubuque:

  • Heritage Trail: This scenic trail stretches for 26 miles through Dubuque’s countryside, offering beautiful views of the area’s rolling hills and farmland.
  • Dubuque Riverwalk: This trail follows the Mississippi River and offers beautiful views of the water and the city.
  • Swiss Valley Nature Preserve: This preserve features over 10 miles of hiking trails, including a variety of loops ranging in difficulty from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Dubuque: Activities and Tours

In addition to its top attractions and outdoor adventures, Dubuque has plenty of other exciting things to do. Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Dubuque:

  • Fenelon Place Elevator: This cable car ride offers beautiful views of the city and the Mississippi River.
  • Escape Room Dubuque: This escape room is a fun and challenging activity for groups of all sizes.
  • Dubuque River Rides: These scenic boat tours offer a unique perspective on the city and the surrounding area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dubuque

If you’re planning a trip to Dubuque and want to make the most of your time in the city, consider following this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the National Mississippi River Museum & Aquarium and take a walk along the Riverwalk.
  • Day 2: Explore the Dubuque Museum of Art and the Mathias Ham Historic Site.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a hike at Mines of Spain Recreation Area and have a picnic at Eagle Point Park.
  • Day 4: Take a cable car ride on the Fenelon Place Elevator and visit the Dubuque Arboretum and Botanical Gardens.
  • Day 5: Go on a scenic boat tour with Dubuque River Rides and have dinner at one of Dubuque’s many restaurants.
  • Day 6: Spend the day biking on the Heritage Trail and stop for lunch in one of the countryside towns.
  • Day 7: Buy souvenirs and local products to take home at the Farmers Market and head to one of Dubuque’s historic bars for a drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Dubuque

Dubuque has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants, bars, and breweries to explore. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Dubuque:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Catfish: Dubuque is known for its catfish, which is typically breaded and fried.
  • Maid-Rites: These loose-meat sandwiches are a popular local fast food, and can be found at the Maid-Rite chain restaurants throughout Dubuque.
  • Dubuque-style pizza: This regional specialty features a thin, crispy crust and plenty of toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Dubuque Star Brewery: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including a popular blonde ale.
  • Stone Cliff Winery: This winery offers a variety of award-winning wines, including several made from grapes grown in Iowa.

Where to Stay in Dubuque: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dubuque has a variety of lodging options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dubuque:

  • Hotel Julien Dubuque: This historic hotel offers luxurious accommodations and beautiful views of the Mississippi River.
  • The Redstone Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel is located near downtown Dubuque and offers comfortable rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  • The Richards House Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in a historic Victorian mansion and offers a cozy, home-like atmosphere.

What to Buy in Dubuque: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, Dubuque has plenty of options. Here are some of the best places to shop in Dubuque:

  • Dubuque Farmers Market: This outdoor market features a variety of locally grown produce, homemade baked goods, and handmade crafts.
  • River Lights Bookstore: This independent bookstore offers a wide selection of books and gifts, including many by regional authors.
  • The Smokestack: This shop offers a variety of retro and vintage items, including clothing, decor, and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Dubuque

Dubuque has a rich cultural heritage, rooted in its history as a river town and a hub for agriculture and industry. Visitors to Dubuque can experience this unique culture through its museums, historic sites, and local events.

Best Time to Visit Dubuque

The best time to visit Dubuque depends on your interests and preferences. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and events, summer and fall are the best times to visit, when the weather is mild and many festivals and events take place. Winter can be cold and snowy, but also offers opportunities for ice fishing and skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dubuque

When visiting Dubuque, it’s important to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Visit local museums and historical sites to learn about Dubuque’s rich history and culture.
  • Try local foods and beverages, including catfish, Maid-Rites, and craft beers.
  • Explore Dubuque’s natural beauty, including its parks and outdoor trails.

Don’t:

  • litter and respect the environment
  • act loud in the public places
  • be reckless in the outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Dubuque is a safe and healthy city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Dubuque:

Safety in Dubuque

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • During the summer, be aware of the risk of thunderstorms and flash flooding.
  • Follow any posted safety rules or warnings when enjoying Dubuque’s outdoor attractions.

Health in Dubuque

  • Like many areas in the Midwest, Dubuque is prone to cold and flu season during the winter months. Consider getting a flu shot before traveling.
  • Be aware of any allergies you may have, particularly if you’re planning to try new foods or outdoor activities.

Getting to Dubuque: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dubuque is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some of the best transportation options for getting to Dubuque:

Airports Near Dubuque

  • Dubuque Regional Airport (DBQ): Located just minutes from downtown Dubuque, Dubuque Regional Airport offers flights from Chicago and Minneapolis.

Bus Stations Near Dubuque

  • Jefferson Lines: This bus company offers service to Dubuque from several locations in the Midwest, including Chicago and Des Moines.
  • Greyhound: This national bus company also offers service to Dubuque.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The closest Amtrak station is in La Crosse, Wisconsin, approximately an hour and a half’s drive from Dubuque.

How to Get Around in Dubuque

Dubuque is easy to navigate by car, bike, or foot. There are also several taxi companies and ride-sharing services available in the city. Many of Dubuque’s top attractions, including its museums, parks, and outdoor trails, are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Dubuque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dubuque is a great starting point for exploring the Midwest. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Sinsinawa Mound Center: This retreat center is located just across the Wisconsin border and offers hiking trails, workshops, and spiritual retreats.
  • Galena, Illinois: This historic town is known for its charming downtown area, antique shops, and wineries.
  • Field of Dreams Movie Site: This movie site, located in Dyersville, Iowa, is a must-visit for fans of the classic film.

Conclusion

No matter what your interests or preferences, Dubuque has something to offer for everyone. With its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, this charming city is a must-visit destination in the Midwest. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Dubuque and provided you with plenty of ideas for things to see and do while you’re there. Happy travels!
