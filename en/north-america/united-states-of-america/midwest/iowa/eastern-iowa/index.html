Introduction to Eastern Iowa

Nestled in the Midwest region of the United States, Eastern Iowa is a destination that has something to offer every kind of traveler. The area includes the cities of Cedar Rapids, Iowa City, and Dubuque. From cultural events and museums to outdoor recreation and culinary delights, Eastern Iowa has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Iowa

Whether you’re a history buff or a nature lover, Eastern Iowa has something to offer. From cultural landmarks to state parks, we’ve rounded up some of the area’s top attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Herbert Hoover Presidential Library and Museum in West Branch, showcases the life and legacy of the 31st US president.
  • The National Czech & Slovak Museum & Library in Cedar Rapids, highlights the culture, history, and art of Czech and Slovak Americans.
  • The Amana Colonies, located in seven villages about 20 miles northwest of Iowa City, offers an unusual glimpse into 19th-century communal living.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Iowa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Great River Road National Scenic Byway in Dubuque, offers breathtaking views of the Mississippi River and the hills that line its banks.
  • The Maquoketa Caves State Park in Maquoketa, features a unique underground world of caverns and crevices beneath the surface.
  • The Cedar River Trail in Cedar Rapids, is a picturesque trail that stretches along the Cedar River, perfect for hiking, biking, and walking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Iowa

Eastern Iowa is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in the state. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Cedar Valley Nature Trail, located in Cedar Rapids, is a scenic route to bike, hike, or run.
  • The Wapsipinicon Trail in Anamosa, is a 5-mile multi-use trail that is great for cycling and hiking.
  • The Hawkeye Wildlife Area Trails, located in Iowa City, offers a peaceful and serene backdrop for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Iowa: Activities and Tours

Eastern Iowa has a lot to offer when it comes to activities and tours. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride above the rolling hills and countryside of Dubuque.
  • Enjoy a round of golf at the Riverside Casino and Golf Resort in Riverside.
  • Take a tour of the historic Brucemore Mansion in Cedar Rapids.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Iowa

If you’re a foodie, Eastern Iowa will not disappoint you. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maid-Rites, a loose ground beef sandwich served on a bun, is a staple of Iowa.
  • Iowa ham balls, is a mouth-watering dish made with ground ham and pork in a sweet and tangy sauce.
  • Sweet corn is a summer staple in Iowa and can be found everywhere.

Local Beverages

  • Cedar Ridge Winery and Distillery in Swisher, is one of Iowa’s top wineries and produces a variety of award-winning wines and spirits.
  • Kalona Brewing Company in Kalona, offers a variety of craft beers along with delicious food.
  • Cedar Ridge apple brandy is a must-try for any connoisseur.

Where to Stay in Eastern Iowa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly hostel or a luxurious hotel, Eastern Iowa has you covered. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Dubuque and Spa, Dubuque, is a luxurious hotel with stunning views of the Mississippi River.
  • The Blackhawk Hotel, Cedar Falls, is a budget-friendly historic hotel with plenty of old-world charm.
  • The Hillside Inn, Anamosa, is a cozy and comfortable B&B that is perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Eastern Iowa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Eastern Iowa, there are several local products and souvenirs that you can bring back home. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Amana furniture is renowned for its craftsmanship and quality.
  • Iowa honey is some of the best you’ll taste.
  • Sweet corn is a summer staple in Iowa and can be found everywhere.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Iowa

In Eastern Iowa, you will find a vibrant and unique culture that is a blend of Midwest American and Czech and Slovak traditions. The area has a rich history that is visible in the architecture and landmarks that dot the landscape. The people here are friendly and welcoming, and community is key.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Iowa

The best time to visit Eastern Iowa is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are plentiful. Winter is also a good time to visit if you enjoy winter sports, such as skiing, ice fishing, and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Iowa

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Attend a cultural event or festival
  • Explore the area’s natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the importance of bug spray
  • Assume all of Iowa is flat and boring, Eastern Iowa has hills and plenty to explore
  • Forget to take time and experience the small town charm

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Iowa

Eastern Iowa is a safe destination for travelers. However, like any destination, it is always best to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables in your car and locking your doors at night.

Health in Eastern Iowa

Eastern Iowa has a well-developed healthcare system with numerous hospitals and clinics throughout the region. It is always wise to travel with health insurance.

Getting to Eastern Iowa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastern Iowa is easily accessible by air, road or bus.

Airports Near Eastern Iowa

  • The Eastern Iowa Airport in Cedar Rapids (CID)
  • Dubuque Regional Airport in Dubuque (DBQ)
  • Quad City International Airport in Moline, IL (MLI)

Bus Stations Near Eastern Iowa

  • Greyhound in Cedar Rapids
  • Burlington Trailways in Iowa City

Other Options

  • Amtrak Station in Galesburg, IL (GLB) connects to Dubuque and Cedar Rapids by bus routes.
  • Rental Cars are available at all airports

How to Get Around in Eastern Iowa

Renting a car is the best option to get around the region. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Iowa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Field of Dreams movie site in Dyersville

Eastern Iowa is a destination that is full of surprises. From its rich history and culture to its natural beauty and unique cuisine, there is always something to explore and discover.
