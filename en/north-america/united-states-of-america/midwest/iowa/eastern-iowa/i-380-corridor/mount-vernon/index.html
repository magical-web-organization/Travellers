Introduction to Mount Vernon

Located in the I-380 Corridor, in Eastern Iowa, Mount Vernon is a charming town known for its picturesque Main Street, historic architecture, and scenic beauty. Founded in 1847, Mount Vernon is home to several cultural attractions, natural wonders, and fun activities that make it a sought-after destination for travelers looking to explore the Midwest. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Mount Vernon has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Vernon

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Mount Vernon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palisades-Kepler State Park – a beautiful state park with hiking trails, camping sites, and scenic views of the Cedar River.
  • First Street Community Center – a historic building that houses art galleries, a theater, a community space, and a farmer’s market.
  • Historic Cornell College – a beautiful college with a rich history and stunning architecture that’s worth a visit.
  • Herbert Hoover National Historic Site – a museum and historic site dedicated to the life and career of the 31st President of the USA, Herbert Hoover.
  • Uptown Theater – a charming historic theater that shows classic and independent films, live performances, and concerts.

Natural Attractions of Mount Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Vernon Trail System – a network of hiking and biking trails that take you through the beautiful scenery of Eastern Iowa.
  • Palisades-Dows Preserve – a beautiful preserve with hiking trails and scenic views of the Cedar River.
  • Kroul Farms – a family-owned farm with seasonal activities such as apple picking, pumpkin patches, and corn mazes.
  • Indian Creek Nature Center – a nature center with walking trails, gardens, wildlife exhibits, and educational programs.
  • Palisades-Kepler Observatory – a nonprofit observatory that offers stargazing experiences, educational programs, and astronomy events.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon Trail System is a network of over 20 miles of trails that take you through the scenic beauty of Mount Vernon and Eastern Iowa. The trails are open year-round and offer a variety of terrain for hikers and bikers of all levels. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Palisades-Kepler State Park Trail – a 1.4-mile trail that takes you through a beautiful wooded area and offers stunning views of the Cedar River.
  • Mount Vernon Riverfront Trail – a 1.6-mile trail that takes you along the Cedar River and through the heart of Mount Vernon.
  • Devonian Fossil Gorge Trail – a 1.4-mile trail that takes you through a prehistoric gorge with fossils dating back millions of years.
  • Wickiup Hill Trail System – a network of over 10 miles of hiking trails that take you through the beautiful Wickiup Hill Natural Area.

Best Things to Do in Mount Vernon: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Mount Vernon include:

  • Take a guided tour of Herbert Hoover National Historic Site and learn about the life and career of the 31st President of the USA.
  • Attend a play or concert at Uptown Theater, a historic theater that’s been in operation since 1912.
  • Go apple picking at Kroul Farms and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the surrounding countryside.
  • Take a stargazing tour at Palisades-Kepler Observatory and learn about the wonders of the universe.
  • Attend a music festival or cultural event at First Street Community Center and experience the local arts and culture scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon has a great food and drink scene with many local restaurants, cafes, and bars that offer delicious cuisine and refreshing drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maid-Rite Sandwich at Lincoln Cafe – a local favorite made of ground beef, onions, and a secret sauce.
  • Pizza at Palisades Cafe – delicious hand-tossed pizzas made with fresh local ingredients.
  • Breakfast Burrito at Fuel Coffee House – a hearty breakfast burrito made with local eggs and vegetables.
  • Gourmet Hot Dogs at MVP Cakes & Crafts – delicious hot dogs with creative toppings such as bacon jam and mac and cheese.
  • Pastries at Mount Vernon Confectionery – mouth-watering pastries, cakes, and cookies made from scratch.

Local Beverages

  • Beer at Big Grove Brewery – award-winning beers and delicious food in a beautiful setting.
  • Wine at Cedar Ridge Winery & Distillery – award-winning wines and spirits made from locally grown grapes and grains.
  • Coffee at Fuel Coffee House – expertly roasted coffee and espresso drinks made with locally roasted beans.

Where to Stay in Mount Vernon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mount Vernon has several accommodations options to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mount Vernon:

  • The Lincoln Hotel – a historic hotel with luxurious rooms and a great downtown location.
  • The Mount Vernon Inn – a charming bed and breakfast with comfortable rooms and delicious breakfasts.
  • Palisades-Kepler State Park Campground – a beautiful campground with RV and tent sites, hiking trails, and scenic views of the Cedar River.

What to Buy in Mount Vernon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mount Vernon has several shops and boutiques that offer unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best places to shop in Mount Vernon:

  • Chameleon’s Closet – a boutique that offers stylish clothes, jewelry, and accessories for women.
  • Skogman Realty’s Gift Shop – a gift shop that offers souvenirs, local products, and gift baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a friendly and welcoming town with a strong sense of community. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • The Mount Vernon Farmer’s Market – a weekly market that offers fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.
  • The Mount Vernon Lisbon Community Theatre – a local theater group that produces plays and musicals throughout the year.
  • The Mount Vernon-Lisbon Christmas Walk – an annual festival of lights and holiday cheer that takes place in December.

Best Time to Visit Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a great destination to visit year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, the summer and fall months are the best times to enjoy the hiking and biking trails, while winter offers the chance to go skiing and snowshoeing. The spring is a great time to see the flowers and trees in bloom, and the Christmas Walk in December is a must-see event for anyone who loves holiday festivities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a safe and peaceful town, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Lock your doors and windows when you leave your accommodations or vehicle.

Mount Vernon has a good healthcare system and several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. However, it’s always important to take care of your health when traveling. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Wash your hands frequently and carry hand sanitizer.
  • Be aware of any allergies or medical conditions and carry any necessary medications.

Getting to Mount Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mount Vernon is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Mount Vernon

The closest airport to Mount Vernon is The Eastern Iowa Airport (CID), which is located about 25 miles north. Another airport nearby is Quad Cities International Airport (MLI), which is located about 80 miles southeast.

Bus Stations Near Mount Vernon

Greyhound Bus Lines and Jefferson Lines both offer service to Iowa City, which is located about 20 miles northeast of Mount Vernon. From Iowa City, you can take a taxi or rental car to reach Mount Vernon.

Other Options

You can also reach Mount Vernon by rental car or by arranging a ride with a local transportation service.

How to Get Around in Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel further or prefer not to walk or bike, there are several transportation options available, including taxis, car rentals, and local transportation services.

Where to Go Next from Mount Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
