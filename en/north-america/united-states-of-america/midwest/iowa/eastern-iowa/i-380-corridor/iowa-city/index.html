Introduction to Iowa City

Iowa City is a charming and vibrant city located in the I-380 Corridor in Eastern Iowa. Known for being home to the University of Iowa, it offers a rich culture and history blended with modern amenities. From the culturally important attractions to the scenic views and outdoor adventures, Iowa City has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Iowa City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Old Capitol Museum, a National Historic Landmark that was once the seat of the Iowa government and later became the first building of the University of Iowa.
  • The Museum of Natural History, featuring interactive exhibits and displays of fossils, plants, and animals.
  • The Iowa City Public Library, an architectural gem that is considered one of the most beautiful in the country.
  • The Herbert Hoover Presidential Library and Museum, dedicated to the life and legacy of the 31st President of the United States.

Natural Attractions of Iowa City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Iowa River Trail, a 16-mile trail that runs along the Iowa River and offers stunning views of the city.
  • The Coralville Lake, a man-made lake surrounded by lush forests and walking trails.
  • The Hickory Hill Park, a 190-acre park that offers picnic areas, hiking trails, and breathtaking views of Iowa City.
  • The Kent Park, a 1,000-acre park that features a lake, a nature center, and a golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iowa City

  • The Terry Trueblood Recreation Area, a 200-acre park with multiple hiking and biking trails, a lake, and picnic areas.
  • The Sugar Bottom Recreation Area, offering over 12 miles of bike and hiking trails and scenic views of the Coralville Reservoir.
  • The Iowa River Trail, offering a flat, easy-to-navigate trail that follows the Iowa River for 16 miles.

Best Things to Do in Iowa City: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Kinnick Stadium, home of the Iowa Hawkeyes football team, and take a guided tour of the facility.
  • Take a walking tour of the University of Iowa campus and admire the stunning architecture of the buildings.
  • Attend a show at the Englert Theatre, a historic venue that hosts live performances and concerts.
  • Visit the farmer’s market in downtown Iowa City and try local produce and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iowa City

Day Activities
One Visit the Old Capitol Museum and take a walking tour of the University of Iowa campus.

Food and Drink Scene of Iowa City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iowa Pork Chop, a thick, juicy pork chop coated in a savory crust.
  • Sweet Corn, a local Iowa delicacy that is perfectly seasoned and grilled to perfection.
  • Maid-Rite Sandwich, a loose meat sandwich made with seasoned ground beef and served with pickles and mustard.
  • Amana Colonies Meat Platter, featuring smoked ham, bacon, and sausage from the Amana Colonies, a historic community just north of Iowa City.

Local Beverages

  • Iowa craft beer, with local breweries such as Big Grove Brewery and Iowa Brewing Company offering refreshing and unique brews.
  • Cedar Ridge Whiskey, distilled locally in Swisher, IA, and award-winning for its quality and flavor.
  • Wine from the local vineyards such as Cedar Ridge Winery or Fireside Winery.

Where to Stay in Iowa City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Graduate Iowa City, a trendy and luxurious hotel located in the heart of downtown.
  • The Iowa House Hotel, located on the University of Iowa campus and offering convenience and comfort.
  • The Brown Street Inn, a charming bed and breakfast located in a historic neighborhood.
  • The Hostel, offering affordable and cozy accommodations for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Iowa City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local handmade crafts and pottery from the Iowa Artisans Gallery.
  • Iowa Hawkeyes merchandise from the Herky’s Locker Room.
  • Sweet treats and candies from the Dutch-inspired bakery, Jaarsma Bakery.
  • Fresh and locally grown produce and meat from the farmer’s market in downtown Iowa City.

Local Culture and Customs of Iowa City

Iowa City prides itself on being a welcoming and inclusive community. It is important to respect the local customs and etiquette, including always saying “please” and “thank you” and tipping generously for good service. It is also important to dress appropriately for the weather and to be environmentally conscious, as Iowa City is known for its efforts in sustainability and conservation.

Best Time to Visit Iowa City

The best time to visit Iowa City is during the fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak colors. However, Iowa City offers something to do and see year-round, from winter sports and holiday markets to summer outdoor festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iowa City

Do:

  • Do explore the local hiking and biking trails.
  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do attend a Hawkeyes football game or another local sports event.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the local environment.
  • Don’t forget to tip appropriately for good service.
  • Don’t assume everyone is a student - Iowa City also has a large population of non-students.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iowa City

Iowa City is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. It is recommended to avoid walking alone at night and to keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Iowa City

Iowa City has a high level of healthcare services available, including the University of Iowa Hospitals and Clinics. It is important to bring sufficient medical documentation and medication if necessary.

Getting to Iowa City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iowa City

The Eastern Iowa Airport is located in Cedar Rapids, just 25 miles north of Iowa City. Transportation options from the airport include rental cars, shuttle services, and taxi services.

Bus Stations Near Iowa City

Iowa City is served by both Greyhound and Megabus, with stops located downtown at the Intermodal Transportation Center.

Other Options

Travelers can also explore Iowa City by car or by bike, with several car rental and bike rental options available.

How to Get Around in Iowa City

Iowa City is relatively small and easily navigable on foot or by bike. Public transportation options include the Iowa City Transit, which provides bus service throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Iowa City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cedar Rapids, located just 25 miles north of Iowa City and home to the Cedar Rapids Museum of Art and the National Czech and Slovak Museum and Library.
  • Amana Colonies, known for their German-inspired cuisine and historic buildings.
  • Des Moines, the capital of Iowa, offering a diverse culinary scene, art museums, and outdoor parks.

Conclusion

Iowa City offers a unique blend of rich history and modern amenities that make it a must-visit destination in the Midwest. From the cultural attractions to the outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in this eclectic and welcoming city.
