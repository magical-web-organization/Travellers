Introduction to Cedar Rapids

Cedar Rapids is a hidden gem in the Midwest, located in Eastern Iowa’s I-380 Corridor. This vibrant city has much to offer visitors, from cultural attractions to natural wonders, hiking and biking trails, and more. Whether you want to explore history, art, or the great outdoors, Cedar Rapids has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cedar Rapids

Here are some of the top attractions to put on your Cedar Rapids itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Czech & Slovak Museum & Library: This museum celebrates the history and culture of the Czech and Slovak people in America through exhibits, programs, and events. Visitors can explore galleries and collections, attend concerts and lectures, and even take part in a genealogy research program.

  • African American Museum of Iowa: This museum is a tribute to African American history and culture in the state. Through exhibits, archives, and educational programs, visitors can learn about the contributions that African Americans have made to Iowa’s history and culture.

  • Brucemore Mansion: This historic mansion was built in 1886 and is now a National Trust historic site. Visitors can take guided tours of the home and gardens, which are filled with antiques and artifacts from the original family who owned the property.

Natural Attractions of Cedar Rapids: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Indian Creek Nature Center: This nature center features over 290 acres of prairies, woods, and wetlands. Visitors can hike, bike, or snowshoe on the trails, visit the nature center and exhibits, or attend one of the many educational programs.

  • Cedar Rapids River Trail: This scenic trail runs along the Cedar River and is perfect for hiking, biking, or enjoying a leisurely stroll. Along the way, visitors can stop at parks, historical landmarks, and even spots for fishing and boating.

  • Squaw Creek Park: This beautiful park is a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can explore hiking trails, relax by the lake, or even go birdwatching in the wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cedar Rapids

Cedar Rapids is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are many hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Cedar Valley Nature Trail: This 52-mile trail runs from Evansdale to Hiawatha and is perfect for hiking, biking, or even horseback riding. Along the way, visitors can enjoy beautiful scenery, historical landmarks, and plenty of wildlife.

  • Squaw Creek Park Trails: These trails wind through the park’s woodlands and prairies and offer stunning views of the Cedar River. Visitors can choose from easy or challenging routes, perfect for families or experienced hikers.

  • Wickiup Hill Learning Center Trails: These trails feature over 5 miles of hiking and skiing trails through some of the most beautiful areas in the state. Visitors can explore wetlands, prairies, and woodlands, and even spot some wildlife along the way.

Best Things to Do in Cedar Rapids: Activities and Tours

In addition to the top attractions and hiking/biking trails, Cedar Rapids has plenty of other things to do. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Newbo City Market: This indoor market is a hub for local art, music, shopping, and dining. Visitors can browse through unique shops, get some delicious food from the many vendors, or even catch a concert or event.

  • Cedar Rapids Kernels Game: Catch a minor league baseball game at this beautiful stadium. Visitors can enjoy a hot dog and a beer while watching the game, and even meet some of the players after the game.

  • Breweries and Wineries: Cedar Rapids has a thriving craft beer and wine scene. Visitors can take a tour of a local brewery or winery, sample some of the best beers and wines in the state, and even attend events and tastings.

Food and Drink Scene of Cedar Rapids

No trip to Cedar Rapids is complete without trying some of the local food and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cedar Ridge Bourbon: This award-winning bourbon is made right in Cedar Rapids and is a must-try for any whiskey lover.

  • Sweetiepies Chicken and Fish Fry: This popular restaurant serves up some of the best fried chicken and fish in the state.

  • Sausage and Kraut Pizza from Tomaso’s Pizza: This unique pizza combines local ingredients and flavors for a truly unique taste.

Local Beverages

  • Lion Bridge Brewing Company: This local brewery serves up a variety of craft beers, including some unique and creative brews.

  • Prairie School Wines: This local winery produces some of the top wines in the state, using only the best grapes from the region.

  • Zoe’s Coffeehouse and Roastery: This coffee shop roasts its own beans in-house and offers a variety of unique blends and flavors.

Where to Stay in Cedar Rapids: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodations in Cedar Rapids, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Cedar Rapids Convention Complex: This 4-star hotel is located in the heart of downtown and offers stunning views of the city.

  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Cedar Rapids-North: This hotel is perfect for families or extended stays, with spacious suites and a variety of amenities.

  • Artisan Sanctuary Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic home and offers a cozy and unique experience for visitors.

What to Buy in Cedar Rapids: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Cedar Rapids, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to look for:

  • Cedar Rapids Market Basket: These baskets are made from natural fibers and are perfect for carrying groceries or using as a decorative piece at home.

  • Locally Made Crafts and Artwork: Cedar Rapids has a thriving arts and crafts scene, and visitors can find unique and beautiful handmade items at local markets and shops.

  • Local Foods and Wines: Don’t forget to bring home some of the delicious local food and wines, like the Cedar Ridge Bourbon or Prairie School Wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Cedar Rapids

Cedar Rapids is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors are sure to appreciate the community’s commitment to preserving history and culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Celebrating Czech and Slovak Heritage: The National Czech & Slovak Museum & Library is a testament to the community’s ties to these cultures, and visitors should take the time to learn about their unique traditions and history.

  • Respecting Native American Heritage: The Mesquaki Nation still has a significant presence in Iowa, and visitors should show respect for their culture and history when visiting Cedar Rapids.

Best Time to Visit Cedar Rapids

The best time to visit Cedar Rapids depends on what you want to do. For outdoor activities, the spring and fall offer cooler temperatures and beautiful scenery. For indoor activities, the winter months are perfect for museum visits and cultural events. Summer is a great time for festivals and outdoor concerts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cedar Rapids

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Cedar Rapids:

Do:

  • Explore the Local Breweries and Wineries: Cedar Rapids has a thriving craft beer and wine scene, and visitors should take advantage of the opportunity to try some unique and delicious drinks.

  • Visit the National Czech & Slovak Museum & Library: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about the history and culture of these two groups in America.

  • Take a Hike or Bike Ride: Cedar Rapids has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the state, so make sure to take advantage of this beautiful scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Miss the African American Museum of Iowa: This important museum is a tribute to the contributions that African Americans have made to Iowa’s history and culture.

  • Don’t Forget to Try the Local Foods and Drinks: Cedar Rapids has some delicious local cuisine and drinks that visitors shouldn’t miss.

Safety and Health

Cedar Rapids is a safe and healthy city to visit, but visitors should still take precautions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Cedar Rapids

  • Take precautions when hiking or biking: Visitors should be aware of the weather and trail conditions when hiking or biking in Cedar Rapids.

  • Be aware of surroundings when out late at night: Visitors should exercise caution when walking alone late at night and should stick to well-lit areas.

Health in Cedar Rapids

  • Check for allergies: Cedar Rapids has a high pollen count, so visitors with allergies should take precautions.

  • Protect yourself from the sun: Cedar Rapids has hot summers, so visitors should wear sunscreen and stay hydrated to avoid sunburn and heat stroke.

Getting to Cedar Rapids: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cedar Rapids is easily accessible by plane, bus, or car. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Cedar Rapids

  • Eastern Iowa Airport: This airport is located just 15 minutes from downtown Cedar Rapids and offers flights from major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Cedar Rapids

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers daily service to Cedar Rapids from major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore Cedar Rapids and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

  • Amtrak: The nearest Amtrak station is in Omaha, Nebraska, which is about 4 hours away by car.

How to Get Around in Cedar Rapids

Cedar Rapids is a pedestrian-friendly city, and many attractions and restaurants are within walking distance of downtown. For visitors who prefer to drive, there are plenty of parking options available in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Cedar Rapids: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Iowa, there are many other destinations and excursions worth considering from Cedar Rapids. Here are some ideas:

  • Amana Colonies: This charming community is just 30 minutes from Cedar Rapids and offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of the German immigrants who settled in the area in the 1800s.

  • Herbert Hoover Presidential Library and Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of the 31st President of the United States and is located just over an hour from Cedar Rapids.

  • Effigy Mounds National Monument: This national monument is located in northeast Iowa and features over 200 prehistoric mounds, including effigy mounds in the shapes of animals and birds.

Conclusion

Cedar Rapids is a wonderful destination for travelers interested in history, culture, and outdoor adventures. With plenty of attractions, activities, and beautiful scenery, there’s something for everyone in this gem of the Midwest.
