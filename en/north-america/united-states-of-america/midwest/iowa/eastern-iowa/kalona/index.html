Introduction to Kalona

Nestled in the heart of the Midwest, Kalona is an idyllic small town in Eastern Iowa that boasts a rich history and a unique blend of cultures. Known for its lush greenery, scenic countryside, and charming homesteads, Kalona is a perfect escape for those seeking an authentic rural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kalona

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kalona Historical Village: This living history museum offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of the Amish settlers who founded the town. Here, you can see a one-room schoolhouse, a blacksmith shop, a windmill, and more.
  • Kalona Mural Walk: A collection of 17 murals depicting the town’s history and culture.
  • Windmill Ridge Campground: A historic Dutch windmill and a working millwheel-grinding flour, along with campsites for overnight stays.

Natural Attractions of Kalona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Keomah State Park: Located just a few miles from Kalona, this park is a popular spot for fishing, boating, hiking, and camping. Its 366-acre lake is stocked with a variety of fish, including catfish, bass, and crappie.
  • Ginkgo Petrified Forest State Park: This park contains one of the largest single deposits of petrified wood in the world, with over 22,000 acres of fossilized trees and plants!
  • Kalona City Park: A perfect spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll. The park features a shelter house, playground, and walking trail, as well as tennis courts and a pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalona

Kalona is blessed with several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an opportunity to explore the town’s countryside.

  • Kalona Trail: This is a 13-mile trail that winds through the countryside surrounding Kalona and offers views of scenic farmland, woodlands, and streams. It is perfect for biking, hiking, and horseback riding.
  • English River Trail: A 16-mile trail that runs along the English River, offering scenic views of the water and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kalona: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of an Amish farm and learn about their way of life.
  • Enjoy a horse-drawn carriage ride through the town’s scenic countryside.
  • Explore the Kalona Historical Village and learn about the town’s history.
  • Visit the Kalona Mural Walk and admire the town’s public art.
  • Go camping, hiking, boating, or fishing at Lake Keomah or Ginkgo Petrified Forest State Park.
  • Treat yourself to some locally-made chocolates and baked goods at Kalona Chocolates.

Food and Drink Scene of Kalona

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kolaches: Czech pastry with various fruits, cream cheese, or meats, often enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack.
  • Fried Chicken: A staple in Midwest cuisine, crispy and juicy fried chicken is a must-try in Kalona.
  • Amish-style pies: Choose from a variety of homemade pies, including cherry, peach, and apple.

Local Beverages

  • Kalona Brewing Company: Offers hand-crafted brews, spirits, and an extensive wine list.
  • Coffee shops around town: Many local coffee shops serve locally roasted and brewed coffee.

Where to Stay in Kalona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kalona offers a variety of lodging options for travelers, ranging from cozy guesthouses to upscale hotels.

  • B&Bs: You can find several charming bed-and-breakfasts, many in historic Victorian homes.
  • Hotels: There are few independent hotels in city.
  • Vacation Rentals: Some visitors may prefer staying in a vacation rental while visiting Kalona.

What to Buy in Kalona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade quilts, blankets, and other textiles.
  • Handcrafted furniture in the Amish style.
  • Locally-grown produce, jams, and jellies.
  • Amish baked goods such as pies and breads.
  • Local art, crafts, and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalona

Kalona has a rich cultural heritage, shaped largely by its Amish and Mennonite communities. Visitors should be respectful of the locals’ customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kalona

The best time to visit Kalona depends on what you are looking for. Summers are great for outdoor activities, but fall is the perfect time for leaf-peeping with vibrant foliage. Winters bring freezing temperatures and snow, but with the chilly breeze, comes the holiday festivities, and when spring comes, the countryside bursts with flowers and greenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalona

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their way of life.
  • Take a horse-drawn carriage ride and taste Kalona’s famous bakery products.
  • Check out the seasonal festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of the Amish people.
  • Drive your car on the dirt roads that run through Amish farms.
  • Be disrespectful or make fun of their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kalona

Kalona is a safe and friendly town. However, visitors should take standard precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Kalona

There are several clinics and hospitals in the region, but emergency assistance may be limited and it’s best to carry your own medication for immediate requirements.

Getting to Kalona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kalona

  • The Eastern Iowa Airport (CID) is the closest airport, about one hour’s drive from Kalona.

Bus Stations Near Kalona

  • There are no commercial bus stations in Kalona. The nearest bus services are in Iowa City and Cedar Rapids.

Other Options

  • A rental car is needed to travel in and around Kalona.

How to Get Around in Kalona

The most convenient way of getting around is by car. A rental car is a good option, as public transportation is limited. Some local taxi services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Kalona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Iowa City: About twenty miles north of Kalona, it’s a college town and offers plenty of cultural and outdoor activities.
  • Cedar Rapids: Thirty miles northeast of Kalona, offers a variety of museums, galleries, and World’s largest popcorn ball.
  • Amana Colonies: Fifteen miles to the west of Kalona, it’s a group of seven historic German colonies that feature beautiful architecture, unique shopping, and local breweries and wineries.

Conclusion

Kalona is a place that offers a relaxing and unique experience, and a chance to slow down and enjoy the simple things in life. With an abundance of natural beauty, an enriching history, as well as friendly locals, Kalona is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.
