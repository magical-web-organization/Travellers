Introduction to Atlantic

Located in Southwest Iowa, Atlantic is a charming city with a population of approximately 6,900. It is known for its historic sites, beautiful parks, and outdoor recreational activities.

List of Places to Visit in Atlantic

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iowa Aviation Museum: This museum showcases the history of aviation in Iowa and features a collection of vintage aircraft, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • Heritage House Museum: Built in 1865, this beautifully restored Italianate-style mansion portrays life in the late 19th century with period furnishings, clothing, and historic memorabilia.
  • Rock Island Depot: This historic train station, built in 1899, now serves as a museum showcasing the history of the railroad and Atlantic’s role in the transportation industry.

Natural Attractions of Atlantic: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Anita State Park: Set on a 1,062-acre area, this park features a beautiful lake, hiking trails, fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Atlantic Golf and Country Club: This 18-hole golf course offers stunning views of the rolling hills and countryside of Southwest Iowa.
  • Schildberg Recreation Area: This park boasts four lakes ideal for fishing, boating, and swimming, as well as hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Atlantic

  • T-Bone Trail: This 22-mile paved trail runs from downtown Council Bluffs to the city of Shenandoah and passes through Atlantic, offering scenic views of the countryside.
  • Wabash Trace Nature Trail: This 63-mile trail from Council Bluffs to Blanchard includes a 10-mile section that runs through Southwest Iowa, offering biking and hiking opportunities through lush landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Atlantic: Activities and Tours

  • Farmers Market: Every Saturday morning from May through October, the farmers market offers fresh locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and baked goods.
  • AtlanticFest: This annual summer festival features a parade, live entertainment, carnival rides, and food vendors.
  • Cass County Fair: Held every July, this fair includes livestock exhibits, 4-H competitions, music, food, and family-friendly activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Atlantic

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maid-Rites: A popular Iowa loose meat sandwich served on a bun with mustard, pickles, and onions.
  • Iowa Sweet Corn: Known for its sweet flavor and tender kernels, this seasonal dish is a staple at Iowa picnics and barbecues.
  • Cherry Pie: Atlantic is known for its annual Cherry Festival and delicious cherry pie, made with locally grown tart cherries.

Local Beverages

  • Iowa Beer: Iowa boasts a thriving craft beer scene, with many breweries offering unique and flavorful varieties.
  • Sweetened Iced Tea: Known for its sweet and refreshing taste, this beverage is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Atlantic: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Boulders Inn & Suites: This modern hotel features comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and fitness center.
  • Chestnut Charm Bed & Breakfast: A historic home turned bed and breakfast with cozy rooms, a delicious breakfast, and charming decor.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham: A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Atlantic: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cherry Products: Atlantic is known for its annual Cherry Festival and various cherry products, including jams, jellies, and cherry salsa.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local artists and craftsmen offer unique and beautiful handmade pottery, jewelry, and home decor.
  • Maid-Rite Seasoning: Bring home the flavor of Iowa’s famous Maid-Rites with this unique seasoning blend.

Local Culture and Customs of Atlantic

Iowa is known for its Midwestern hospitality and friendly atmosphere. Visitors should expect warm and welcoming locals.

Best Time to Visit Atlantic

The best time to visit Atlantic is during the summer, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the city is in full swing with various outdoor festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Atlantic

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including the popular Maid-Rites and sweet corn.
  • Do visit the various natural attractions and parks, such as Lake Anita State Park and the Wabash Trace Nature Trail.
  • Do attend local festivals and events, such as AtlanticFest and the Cass County Fair.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t underestimate the power of Midwestern hospitality and friendliness.
  • Don’t miss out on the historical sites and museums, showcasing Atlantic’s rich history and heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Atlantic

Atlantic is a safe and friendly city, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take normal precautions while traveling, such as locking their cars and securing their valuables.

Health in Atlantic

There are several medical facilities in Atlantic, including the Cass County Memorial Hospital and numerous clinics and pharmacies.

Getting to Atlantic: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Atlantic

The closest major airport to Atlantic is Eppley Airfield in Omaha, Nebraska, approximately 55 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Atlantic

Greyhound bus lines offers service to nearby cities such as Council Bluffs, Des Moines, and Omaha.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option for visitors, as it provides the freedom to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Atlantic

Driving is the most common way to get around Atlantic and the surrounding area. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Atlantic: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Omaha, Nebraska: Located approximately 60 miles from Atlantic, Omaha offers various attractions such as the Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium, the Old Market district, and the famous Reuben sandwich.
  • Des Moines, Iowa: Iowa’s capital city, Des Moines, is located approximately 80 miles away from Atlantic and features various historical sites, museums, and shopping areas.
  • Kansas City, Missouri: Located approximately 200 miles from Atlantic, Kansas City offers various attractions such as the National World War I Museum, the Country Club Plaza shopping district, and the famous Kansas City-style barbecue.

Conclusion

Atlantic, Iowa, offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and Midwestern charm. Its various attractions and outdoor recreational opportunities make it a great destination for visitors of all ages. Plan your trip today and discover all that Atlantic has to offer.
