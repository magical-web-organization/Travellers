Introduction to Council Bluffs

Council Bluffs is a charming city located in Southwest Iowa, Midwest, United States of America. It is the county seat of Pottawattamie County and borders the state of Nebraska. The city has a rich history and culture, dating back to the 1800s and is home to iconic landmarks and museums, making it a must-visit destination for both history buffs and casual tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Council Bluffs

Council Bluffs is filled with exciting and fascinating attractions that cater to various interests. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Council Bluffs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Union Pacific Railroad Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Union Pacific Railroad and features interactive exhibits and artifacts. It is a great place to learn about the development of the American West.
  • RailsWest Railroad Museum: Dedicated to preserving the history of local railroad, this museum is set in a historic train station. Exhibits include antique railroad artifacts, photographs, and railroad equipment.
  • Western Historic Trails Center: This museum of the National Park Service depicts the story of the pioneer trails that helped to shape the American West specifically the Mormon Pioneer National Historic Trail. Also features exhibits of the Oregon, California, and Pony Express trails along with interactive displays and a film viewing area.
  • Harrah’s Casino: A popular entertainment center featuring a hotel, spa, live entertainment, and a wide selection of casino games.

Natural Attractions of Council Bluffs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Manawa State Park: Located at Lake Manawa, this park is ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. Activities include boating, camping, fishing, picnicking, hiking, and swimming.
  • Hitchcock Nature Center: Surrounded by forests, this beautiful nature center features hiking trails, bird-watching tours, and nature programs.
  • Kanesville Tabernacle: Historical church structure built 1846-1848. It was used by the Mormon pioneers for religious services before they left Winter Quarters for the Great Salt Lake Valley. It boasts stunning architecture, especially the hand-carved doors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Council Bluffs

Council Bluffs provides ample opportunities for hiking and cycling. The town boasts scenic trails with unique rock formations and beautiful water bodies. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in Council Bluffs:

  • Wabash Trace Nature Trail: A 62-mile limestone trail featuring beautiful landscapes and natural scenery, it follows an abandoned rail line and passes through valleys and forests.
  • Lake Manawa Trail: A gravel trail that is 7.5 miles long, and it circles around Lake Manawa providing excellent views of the lake.
  • Riverfront Trail: A stunning 16-mile trail that runs along the Missouri River, providing breathtaking views of Omaha skyline and river crossings via two pedestrian bridges.

Best Things to Do in Council Bluffs: Activities and Tours

Council Bluffs offers a wide range of activities and tours, perfect for families and adventure-seekers alike. Here are some of the best things to do in Council Bluffs:

  • Bass Pro Shops: A fishing enthusiast’s paradise that offers seminars, fishing demos, and much more for visitors of all levels.
  • Loess Hills Vineyard & Winery: A family-owned vineyard that is renowned for its wine and breathtaking views.
  • Lewis & Clark Monument & Scenic Overlook: A park that offers an excellent panoramic view of both Council Bluffs and Omaha cityscape. It has a historical significance, too, as it marks the place where Lewis and Clark met with native tribes to get information about the region’s geography and wildlife.

Local Culture and Customs of Council Bluffs

Iowans are known for their friendly and welcoming behavior. Visitors should expect to be treated kindly and with respect. Iowa is also known for its rich cultural heritage, with influences from its Native American, European, and African American populations.

Best Time to Visit Council Bluffs

The best time to visit Council Bluffs is during the summer months when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall are also great seasons for sightseeing because of the mild temperatures and scenic views.

Do’s and Don’ts of Council Bluffs

Do:

  • Visit the Council Bluffs Farmers Market: A great activity for family and kids to learn about local producers’ products and support small local businesses in the area.
  • Try Local Foods and Beverages: Council Bluffs has a unique food and beverage scene with plenty of fusion foods, unique flavors and craft breweries.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Iowa’s natural beauty is one of its major draws, so please respect the environment and dispose of trash responsibly.
  • Ignore Traffic Signs: Safety is crucial to everyone’s well-being, and following traffic rules and signs should be prioritized.

Safety and Health

Safety in Council Bluffs

Council Bluffs is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Council Bluffs

Council Bluffs has a good healthcare system and provides quality medical care for visitors. The city has a wide range of hospitals and clinics that offer medical services.

Council Bluffs is a walkable city, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot. Visitors can also opt for car rental, ride-share, or taxi services for convenience.

Where to Go Next from Council Bluffs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby destinations from Council Bluffs, such as:

  • Omaha: Omaha is just a short drive from Council Bluffs. Visit the Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium, explore the Old Market district, and stroll through the Joslyn Art Museum.
  • Lincoln: The capital city of Nebraska is another nearby destination worth exploring. Visit the Museum of American Speed, take a guided tour of the Nebraska State Capitol building, and stroll through the Sunken Gardens.

Conclusion

Council Bluffs is a hidden gem in the Midwest, filled with attractions and unique experiences that are waiting to be explored. Whether it’s hiking, exploring museums, or trying local cuisine, Council Bluffs has something for everyone. Use our comprehensive travel guide when planning your visit.
