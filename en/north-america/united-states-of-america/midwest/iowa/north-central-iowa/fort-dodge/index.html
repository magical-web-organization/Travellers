Introduction to Fort Dodge

Located in North Central Iowa, Fort Dodge is a hidden gem full of cultural and natural attractions that will leave visitors in awe. Known for its rich history, vibrant arts scene, and diverse outdoor recreation opportunities, Fort Dodge has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Dodge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Museum and Frontier Village: Take a trip back in time to the 1800s and experience life in a frontier town. This interactive museum features artifacts, exhibits, and buildings from the time period, as well as staff dressed in period clothing.
  • Blanden Art Museum: This museum showcases artwork from local, regional, and national artists, including paintings, sculptures, and modern art.
  • Webster County Historical Museum: Learn about the history of Fort Dodge and the surrounding area through exhibits and artifacts, including information about the Civil War, the railroad, and local businesses and industries.

Natural Attractions of Fort Dodge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gypsum City OHV Park: This 800-acre park features off-road trails for ATVs, dirt bikes, and other off-road vehicles.
  • Brushy Creek State Recreation Area: This popular recreation area offers hiking and biking trails, fishing, camping, and boating opportunities.
  • John F. Kennedy Memorial Park: This park features a 9-hole disc golf course, hiking trails, and scenic views of the Des Moines River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Dodge

  • Loomis Park Trail: This paved trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a stroll through nature, with scenic views of Dolliver Park and the Des Moines River.
  • Soldier Creek Nature Trail: This 7-mile trail is great for hiking and offers views of wildflowers and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Fort Dodge: Activities and Tours

  • River Hops Brewery Tour: Take a tour of this local craft brewery and learn about the brewing process while tasting their delicious beer.
  • Gypsum City Off-Highway Vehicle Park: Bring your own off-road vehicle or rent one on site and hit the trails at this popular park.
  • Blanden Memorial Art Museum Art Walk: Take a leisurely stroll through downtown Fort Dodge and view unique sculptures and murals created by local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fort Dodge

Day Activity
1 Visit the Fort Museum and Frontier Village in the morning and go hiking in John F. Kennedy Park in the afternoon.
2 Spend the day at Brushy Creek State Recreation Area and rent a boat or kayak to explore the lake.
3 Take a tour of River Hops Brewery and have a beer tasting in the afternoon.
4 Visit the Blanden Art Museum in the morning and go on a scenic drive to see the fall foliage in the afternoon.
5 Explore the Soldier Creek Nature Trail and have a picnic lunch along the way.
6 Take a day trip to the nearby city of Ames and visit the Reiman Gardens and Iowa State University campus.
7 Spend the day at Gypsum City Off-Highway Vehicle Park and ride the off-road trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Dodge

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taffy: Fort Dodge is known for its homemade saltwater taffy, which can be found at local candy shops.
  • Maid-Rite Sandwich: This loose meat sandwich is a regional specialty and can be found at various restaurants throughout Fort Dodge.
  • Sunday Brunch at Olde Boston’s Restaurant and Pub: This local favorite serves up a delicious brunch buffet on Sundays, featuring made-to-order omelettes and waffles.

Local Beverages

  • Beer from River Hops Brewery: This local craft brewery offers a variety of beers on tap, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Wine from Soldier Creek Winery: This nearby winery offers award-winning wines and a beautiful vineyard tour.

Where to Stay in Fort Dodge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quality Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as a fitness center and indoor pool.
  • Country Inn and Suites: This hotel features a complimentary breakfast and a whirlpool bathtub in each room.
  • Webster City RV Park and Campground: For those looking for a more rustic experience, this campground offers RV hookups and primitive tent sites.

What to Buy in Fort Dodge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saltwater Taffy: Pick up some of Fort Dodge’s famous saltwater taffy at one of the local candy shops.
  • Artwork by Local Artists: The Blanden Art Museum Gift Shop offers a variety of artwork and gifts created by local artists.
  • Honey from Ames Farm: This local farm produces honey with unique flavors such as lavender and wildflower.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Dodge

Fort Dodge is a friendly and welcoming community, with a strong emphasis on preserving its history and natural beauty. The locals take pride in their town and are happy to share their knowledge and recommendations with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Fort Dodge

The best time to visit Fort Dodge is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a great time to visit, when the leaves change color and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Dodge

Do:

  • Do visit the Fort Museum and Frontier Village and experience life in a frontier town.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Fort Dodge by hiking, biking, or boating.
  • Do try local specialties such as saltwater taffy and Maid-Rite sandwiches.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the Blanden Art Museum and view the amazing artwork.
  • Don’t forget to bring your off-road vehicle to Gypsum City Off-Highway Vehicle Park.
  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural habitats while exploring the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Dodge

Fort Dodge is a safe and friendly community, but visitors should always take precautions when exploring outdoors and in public areas. Lock car doors and secure valuables when leaving them in the car. Be aware of surroundings and follow safety guidelines for outdoor recreation activities.

Health in Fort Dodge

Health services are easily accessible in Fort Dodge. The UnityPoint Health Trinity Regional Medical Center is a full-service hospital with state-of-the-art facilities and excellent healthcare professionals.

Getting to Fort Dodge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fort Dodge

There are no major airports in Fort Dodge, but the closest airport is the Fort Dodge Regional Airport (FOD), located just three miles from downtown.

Bus Stations Near Fort Dodge

The Jefferson Lines Bus Station is located in Fort Dodge and offers daily routes to various cities in Iowa and surrounding states.

Other Options

Rental cars and taxis are available for those who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in Fort Dodge

Fort Dodge is a small town and is easily accessible by car or bike. Local taxis and ride-share services are also available

Where to Go Next from Fort Dodge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Des Moines, Iowa: The state capital is just 90 miles south of Fort Dodge and offers a variety of attractions such as the Science Center of Iowa and the Des Moines Art Center.
  • Arnolds Park, Iowa: Located 90 miles northwest of Fort Dodge, Arnolds Park is home to the historic Arnolds Park Amusement Park and the Okoboji Boat Works.
  • Pipestone National Monument, Minnesota: Located just over 100 miles north of Fort Dodge, Pipestone National Monument is a unique geological formation and cultural area, sacred to many Native American tribes.

Conclusion

Fort Dodge may be a small town, but it has a lot to offer visitors. From its rich history to its beautiful nature, Fort Dodge is a great destination for those looking for an authentic and diverse Midwestern experience. Plan your next trip to Fort Dodge and discover all that this hidden gem has to offer.
