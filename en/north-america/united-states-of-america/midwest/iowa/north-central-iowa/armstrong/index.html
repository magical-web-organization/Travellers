Introduction to Armstrong

Armstrong is a small town situated in North Central Iowa, known for its peaceful landscapes and friendly locals. The town offers a glimpse into the rural life of Iowa, with a mix of natural and cultural attractions that make it a great vacation spot. From outdoor adventures and scenic views to museums and historical places, Armstrong has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Armstrong

When planning a trip to Armstrong, here are the top places you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Armstrong Heritage Museum: The museum showcases the history of the town with exhibits featuring artifacts and photographs.

  • Palo Alto County Historical Museum: Located in nearby Emmetsburg, this museum offers exhibits that chronicle the history of Palo Alto County.

Natural Attractions of Armstrong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Emmitsburg Nature Center: This nature center is located just a short drive from Armstrong and offers trails for hiking and biking through the scenic Iowa landscape.

  • Iowa Great Lakes: Located about an hour’s drive from Armstrong, the Iowa Great Lakes offer beautiful beaches, boating opportunities, fishing, and a vibrant nightlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Armstrong

The Emmitsburg Nature Center offers several hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Lost Island Nature Trail: This trail is a 1.25-mile loop through a wetland area that provides a great opportunity for bird watching.

  • Knights of Columbus Trail: This 2.25-mile trail takes you through forested areas and along the shore of Five Island Lake.

Best Things to Do in Armstrong: Activities and Tours

  • Floyd’s Memorial Sculpture Garden: Located in nearby Rockwell City, this is a must-see attraction for art lovers. The garden is home to over 60 sculptures created by local artist Floyd Hammer.

  • Hawkeye Pointe: This is the highest point in Iowa, located about 20 miles from Armstrong. Visitors can enjoy scenic views from the observation deck and take photos at the site’s iconic sign.

Week-Long Itinerary for Armstrong

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Armstrong:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Armstrong Heritage Museum and take a walk through downtown Armstrong
2 Hike the Lost Island Nature Trail and explore nearby Emmetsburg
3 Visit the Palo Alto County Historical Museum and take a bike ride on the Knights of Columbus Trail
4 Spend the day at the Iowa Great Lakes and enjoy water activities
5 Take a tour of Floyd’s Memorial Sculpture Garden and visit the Russell Wildlife Area
6 Visit Hawkeye Pointe and take a bike ride on another trail at Emmetsburg Nature Center
7 Relax and enjoy the town of Armstrong, trying out local restaurants and shops

Food and Drink Scene of Armstrong

When visiting Armstrong, don’t forget to try out the local food and drink scene.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork tenderloin sandwich: A popular dish in Iowa, the pork tenderloin sandwich consists of a fried pork cutlet served on a bun with toppings of your choice.

  • Sweet corn: Iowa is known for its sweet corn, which is available from mid-June to September.

Local Beverages

  • Maid-Rite Tavern: This tavern is known for its greasy, loose meat sandwiches and cold beer.

  • The Brown Bear: This hometown bar offers a fun atmosphere and a variety of drinks.

Where to Stay in Armstrong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There aren’t many hotels or guesthouses in Armstrong, but nearby towns offer several options for visitors, including:

  • Wild Rose Casino & Resort: This resort, located in Emmetsburg, offers a casino and hotel with modern rooms and amenities.

  • Super 8 by Wyndham Algona: Located about 20 minutes from Armstrong, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Armstrong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Armstrong, consider picking up some of these local products as souvenirs:

  • Handmade crafts: Several local artists sell handmade crafts at the Armstrong Heritage Museum and other shops in the town.

  • Sweet corn: Bring home some of Iowa’s sweet corn for a taste of the local agriculture.

Local Culture and Customs of Armstrong

When visiting Armstrong, be sure to respect the local culture and customs. The town has a strong community feel and takes pride in its small-town charm.

Best Time to Visit Armstrong

The best time to visit Armstrong is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, if you’re looking for a quieter trip, consider visiting during the fall or winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Armstrong

Do:

  • Take time to explore downtown Armstrong and discover the many shops and restaurants in the area.

  • Visit the local museums and learn about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals or their way of life.

  • Litter or leave trash in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Armstrong

Armstrong is a small town with a low crime rate, making it a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions such as locking doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Armstrong

Armstrong has a medical clinic that is open during normal business hours, but visitors with serious health concerns should seek medical attention in a nearby town.

Getting to Armstrong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Armstrong

The nearest airport to Armstrong is the Fort Dodge Regional Airport, located about 50 miles west of the town. Another option is the Mason City Municipal Airport, located 70 miles east.

Bus Stations Near Armstrong

Jefferson Lines operates a bus station in nearby Estherville, which is about 20 miles north of Armstrong.

Other Options

Car rental is also an option for visitors who want to explore the area on their own. Several major car rental companies operate in nearby towns such as Algona, Spencer, and Emmetsburg.

How to Get Around in Armstrong

The best way to get around Armstrong is by car or bicycle. Visitors who don’t have their vehicle can rent a car at one of the rental agencies in nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Armstrong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Albert Lea, Minnesota: This Minnesota town, known as the “Land Between the Lakes,” offers water activities, shopping, and dining options.

  • Okoboji, Iowa: Located about an hour’s drive from Armstrong, the Iowa Great Lakes offer beautiful beaches, boating activities, fishing, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Armstrong may be a small town, but it has plenty of things to offer visitors. With its mix of natural and cultural attractions, there’s never a dull moment in this Iowa gem. So, plan your trip to Armstrong and don’t forget to include these top attractions in your itinerary!
