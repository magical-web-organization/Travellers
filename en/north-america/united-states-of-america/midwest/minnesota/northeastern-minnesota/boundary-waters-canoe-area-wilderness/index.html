A Comprehensive Guide to Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness (BWCAW) is a vast wilderness area in Minnesota that attracts nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and outdoor enthusiasts from all over the world. With over 1 million acres of pristine lakes, forests, and rivers, BWCAW is a paradise for canoeing, kayaking, fishing, hiking, camping, and wildlife watching.

Whether you’re planning a week-long canoe trip or a day hike, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your time in BWCAW.

Introduction to Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness is located in Northeastern Minnesota, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. It is a designated wilderness area with limited access and no roads, so visitors must travel by foot or canoe.

The area is characterized by its interconnected network of lakes, rivers, and forests. It is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including moose, black bears, wolves, and loons. The human history of the area dates back to at least 10,000 years ago, when Native American tribes lived and hunted here. In the 18th and 19th centuries, fur traders and loggers used the area for natural resources. Today, BWCAW is a protected natural area that offers visitors a chance to experience the wild beauty of Minnesota.

List of Places to Visit in Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chik-Wauk Museum and Nature Center: This museum showcases the human and natural history of the Gunflint Trail area.
  • Dorothy Molter Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of one of BWCAW’s most famous residents, “The Root Beer Lady.”
  • Grand Portage National Monument: This national monument tells the story of the fur trade and the role of Ojibwe people in the trade.

Natural Attractions of Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canoeing and kayaking: The interconnected waterways of BWCAW offer some of the best canoeing and kayaking opportunities in the world.
  • Fishing: The area is home to hundreds of lakes and streams that offer great fishing opportunities for walleye, northern pike, lake trout, and other species.
  • Hiking: There are many trails in BWCAW that offer scenic views of the lakes and forests, including the Border Route Trail and the Kekekabic Trail.
  • Wildlife watching: BWCAW is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including moose, black bears, wolves, and loons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

  • Border Route Trail: This 65-mile trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and passes through old-growth forests, waterfalls, and lakes.
  • Kekekabic Trail: This trail is a 41-mile trek through the heart of BWCAW and offers hikers a chance to experience the rugged wilderness of the area.

Best Things to Do in Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: Activities and Tours

  • Canoeing and kayaking: There are many tour companies that offer guided canoe trips through BWCAW, including outfitting companies like Gunflint Outfitters and Sawbill Canoe Outfitters.
  • Fishing: Whether you’re a seasoned angler or a beginner, there are many opportunities for great fishing in BWCAW.
  • Wildlife watching: The area is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including moose, black bears, wolves, and loons.

Food and Drink Scene of Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye: Considered to be the official state fish of Minnesota, walleye is a popular dish for fishermen in BWCAW.
  • Wild rice: A staple food of Ojibwe people, wild rice is grown in many of the lakes and streams of BWCAW and is a delicious addition to any meal.
  • Blueberries: BWCAW is home to a variety of wild berries, including blueberries, which are a nutritious and delicious snack.

Local Beverages

  • Root beer: Dorothy Molter, also known as “The Root Beer Lady,” was a famous resident of BWCAW who brewed her own root beer using ingredients from the area.
  • Craft beer: Several breweries in nearby towns, such as Voyageur Brewing Company, offer locally-made craft beer.

Where to Stay in Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lodges: There are several lodges in the BWCAW area, including Grand Marais Lodge and Hungry Jack Lodge.

What to Buy in Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade crafts: Many local artists create beautiful handmade crafts using natural materials found in the area, such as wood, bark, and fiber.

Local Culture and Customs of Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

  • Respect for nature: BWCAW is a protected natural area, and visitors are expected to respect the environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Respect for Native American culture: Many Native American tribes have long-standing relationships with the BWCAW area, and visitors are asked to respect their culture and land.

Best Time to Visit Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

The best time to visit BWCAW is from late spring through early fall, when the weather is mild and the waterways are open for canoeing and kayaking. Peak season is typically in July and August, so plan accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

  • Always wear a life jacket while boating.
  • Learn basic first aid and wilderness survival skills.
  • Bring a map and compass and be familiar with navigation.

Health in Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness

  • Bring a water filter or purifier to ensure safe drinking water.
  • Protect yourself from insect bites and sunburn.
  • Carry any necessary medications and first aid supplies with you.
