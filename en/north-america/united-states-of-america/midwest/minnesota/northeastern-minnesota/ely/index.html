Introduction to Ely

Located in the Northeastern part of Minnesota in the Midwest region of the United States, Ely is a charming city filled with natural beauty, unique culture, and plenty of outdoor adventures. Known as the gateway to the Boundary Waters Canoe Area (BWCA), Ely offers a plethora of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to buy, and essential tips to make your journey to Ely unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Ely

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Dorothy Molter Museum: With more than 25,000 visitors annually, this museum celebrates the iconic North Woods legacy of Dorothy Molter, the last legal resident of the BWCAW. Experience the history of the iconic Root Beer Lady and the peace of the surrounding forest.
  • Ely Arts and Heritage Center: Located in a beautifully restored building, this center features a historical museum, photo exhibits, and a summer farmers’ market.
  • International Wolf Center: A world-renowned organization dedicated to studying and preserving wolves and their habitats. Explore the center’s exhibits, engage with live ambassador wolves, and learn from knowledgeable interpreters.

Natural Attractions of Ely: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: A true wilderness, visitors to the BWCA experience unmatched opportunities for solitude and adventure in a preserved wilderness environment. The area offers unparalleled access to paddling opportunities, camping, fishing, wildlife watching, and stunning natural scenery.
  • Superior National Forest Scenic Byway: This 54-mile long drive will take you through the breathtaking wilderness and captivating landscapes of Northern Minnesota.
  • Soudan Underground Mine State Park: Explore the deepest underground iron ore mine in the world and witness the soul of Minnesota’s Iron Range.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ely

  • Bass Lake Trail: A 1.8-mile circular trail that follows the shoreline of Bass Lake and serves as an excellent early morning or evening walk for those camping at the Bass and Fall Lakes Campgrounds.
  • Kawishiwi Falls Trail: A one-mile hike that leads to an impressive 70-ft waterfall.
  • Hidden Valley Trail: A 7.8-mile hike that gives visitors the opportunity to see the stunning panorama of Blackstone Lake after 1.3 miles and leads to undammed Vera Lake after six miles.

Best Things to Do in Ely: Activities and Tours

  • Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness Tours: Join a guided canoe adventure to experience the beauty and tranquility of the BWCA.
  • Wilderness Dog Sled Rides: Experience the thrill of dog sledding during winter months and discover the picturesque landscape.
  • North American Bear Center Theater: Learn about these magnificent creatures, enjoy presentations by experts, and watch films about bears in the theater.

Food and Drink Scene of Ely

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye: A beloved species of fish in Minnesota, walleye is a must-try.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A hearty dish made with a regional specialty: wild rice, rich cream, and vegetables.
  • Beer Cheese Soup: A Wisconsin-inspired soup featuring local brews.

Local Beverages

  • Boundary Waters Brew: Ely’s first craft brewery, featuring a variety of craft beers, ales, and lagers.
  • Vermillion Falls Bottling Company: A family-owned business that fills growlers of carbonated refreshments like root beer, orange cream, and cream soda.

Where to Stay in Ely

  • Grand Ely Lodge Resort and Conference Center: Providing accommodations that range from standard rooms to lakefront villas, this luxurious lodge has all the amenities you could want.
  • Adventure Inn: A charming and comfortable bed and breakfast nestled into the woods, Adventure Inn has spacious private rooms designed with a rustic theme.
  • Moose Track Adventures: With beautiful lakeside cabins, this family-owned adventure company offer vacation rentals for visitors looking for a more intimate and adventurous lodging experience.

What to Buy in Ely: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pack and Paddle Shop: Offering everything you need for an adventure in the woods, like camping gear, canoe rentals, and fishing supplies, this shop is a one-stop-shop for all your outdoor needs.
  • Secret Sisters Trading Post: Browse through a vast selection of gifts, souvenirs, and unique home décor items, handcrafted by local artisans.
  • Ely Farmers’ Market: Held every Saturday during the summer months, this market offers a wide range of locally produced goods, including fresh fruits and vegetables, baked goods, artisan-made crafts, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Ely

Ely has a rich cultural heritage rooted in the history of the indigenous Ojibwe people and the European settlers who followed. Visitors can experience the unique customs of the area by attending traditional powwows, visiting local museums, and participating in local events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Ely

The best time to visit Ely is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and suitable for outdoor activities like hiking, canoeing, and fishing. Autumn can also be an excellent time to visit, with the changing fall colors providing a stunning backdrop.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ely

Do:

  • Respect the wilderness and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Visit local museums and cultural attractions to learn about the history of the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural environments and wildlife.
  • Approach or feed any animals, including bears and wolves.
  • Ignore safety procedures when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ely

  • Carry bear spray when entering bear country.
  • Wear bright clothing during hunting seasons.
  • Follow safety procedures when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Ely

  • Bring bug spray to prevent tick and mosquito bites.
  • Use sunscreen and wear protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Stay hydrated in hot weather to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Ely: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ely

  • Duluth International Airport (DLH): Located 120 miles away from Ely.
  • Range Regional Airport (HIB): Located 35 miles away from Ely.

Bus Stations Near Ely

  • Arrowhead Transit: Offers rides to and from the Ely area.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Available from major car rental companies at the Duluth International Airport.
  • Private Vehicles: Ely is located approximately 4 hours north of Minneapolis and 2 and a half hours south of the Canadian border.

How to Get Around in Ely

  • Rental Cars: Available at the Duluth International Airport.
  • Arrowhead Transit: Offers rides within the Ely area.
  • Walking/Biking: Due to the compact nature of Ely, walking and biking can be a great option for getting around.

Where to Go Next from Ely: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Voyageurs National Park: Located 3 hours north of Ely, this is one of Minnesota’s most stunning parks, known for its interconnected chains of lakes and waterways.
  • Duluth: The largest city in Northern Minnesota, Duluth is a great place to visit for its cultural attractions, lakefront parks, and delicious dining scene.
  • Apostle Islands National Lakeshore: Located about 5 hours away from Ely, this area features stunning caves, sandstone cliffs, and historic lighthouses.

Conclusion

Discovering the beauty of Ely, Minnesota can be a once-in-a-lifetime experience. This guide provided you with everything you need to plan your trip, from outdoor recreation, cultural experiences, to lodging options and more. Just remember to respect the natural environment, follow safety procedures, and immerse yourself in the charming local culture, and you are sure to have an unforgettable journey to Ely.
