Introduction to Iron Range

Iron Range is a breathtaking region located in northeastern Minnesota. Comprising a total of 11 cities, it is one of the most scenic areas in the midwest, with over 200 lakes, lush forests, and some of the largest iron deposits in the entire world. The region is a wonderful destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone who loves to explore charming small towns. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know to make the most of your travel experience in Iron Range.

List of Places to Visit in Iron Range

Iron Range is a treasure trove of stunning natural attractions and culturally significant places that you must add to your travel itinerary. Here is a list of the must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hull Rust Mine - The largest open-pit iron mine in operation since 1895, Hull Rust Mine offers a unique perspective on the Iron Range’s history and mining industry.
  • Minnesota Discovery Center - A museum that showcases the cultural, economic, and historical significance of Minnesota’s Iron Range.
  • Greyhound Bus Museum - A museum dedicated to the history of the Greyhound Bus company and its role in the development of transportation in the United States.
  • Iron Range Interpretive Center - A great place to learn about the culture of the Iron Range region, its history, and the people who have called it home.

Natural Attractions of Iron Range: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Giants Ridge Recreation Area - A premier outdoor recreation destination with two championship golf courses, miles of hiking and biking trails, a ski resort, and much more.
  • Bear Head Lake State Park - A beautiful park with more than 20,000 acres of pristine wilderness, perfect for hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Chisholm ATV Park - A rider’s paradise with over 36 miles of trails open to ATVs, dirt bikes, and other off-road vehicles.
  • Lake Vermilion - One of the most beautiful lakes in the region, Lake Vermilion is perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iron Range

Iron Range is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging. Here are a few popular routes you should check out:

  • Mesabi Trail - A 135-mile paved trail that spans across the Iron Range; perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a long-distance hike.
  • Lost Forty Trail - A 1.5-mile interpretive trail that takes hikers through an old-growth pine forest that was saved from logging.
  • Iron Range Off-Highway Vehicle State Recreation Area - A network of trails for off-road vehicles that covers over 1,200 acres and has varying levels of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Iron Range: Activities and Tours

Iron Range offers plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here are some top picks:

  • Mesabi Trail Tour - A yearly cycling event that attracts riders from all over the country; participants can choose from various routes of different lengths.
  • Soudan Underground Mine State Park - Take a guided tour of the deepest underground iron mine in the United States, complete with an elevator ride to the bottom.
  • Ironworld Discovery Center - A museum that showcases the history of Minnesota’s Iron Range; visitors can explore exhibits, watch demonstrations, and browse artisanal wares.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iron Range

For a comprehensive week-long itinerary, here is a sample schedule of what you could do each day:

Food and Drink Scene of Iron Range

Iron Range offers a unique culinary experience, with a strong emphasis on hearty dishes made with local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasties - A savory pastry filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables; a popular local dish that originated from the mining communities.
  • Wild rice soup - A creamy soup made with wild rice, mushrooms, and other ingredients, a quintessential Minnesota dish.
  • Lutefisk - A controversial but traditional dish made from dried cod soaked in lye, often served with potatoes and butter.
  • Walleye - A freshwater fish found in the lakes of Iron Range, and a popular dish in many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Iron Range Brewing - A local brewery in downtown Virginia that serves a wide variety of beer.
  • Maplewood Coffee and Tea - A coffee shop in Hibbing that serves high-quality coffee, tea, and baked goods.
  • Lake Superior Brewing Company - A brewery that opened in 1994 and offers a range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Iron Range: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Iron Range has plenty of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the best:

  • Ely Lodging - A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Ely, with easy access to outdoor activities.
  • Lakeshore Motor Inn - A family-owned motel on Lake Vermilion that offers stunning views of the water and a host of amenities.
  • Iron Range Cabins & Homes LLC - A rental management company that offers a variety of cabins, cottages, and homes in the area.

What to Buy in Iron Range: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or locally made products, Iron Range has plenty to offer. Here are some ideas:

  • Wild rice - A staple food in Minnesota Native American tribes; you can buy wild rice (and even wild rice soup mix) at a local farmers market or specialty store.
  • Iron ore and mining memorabilia - Mining is an important part of the region’s history, and you can find various mining-themed souvenirs, such as iron ore paperweights, at a local gift shop or museum.
  • Handcrafted items - The Iron Range is home to many talented artisans who create beautiful items such as pottery, jewelry, and wooden homeware.

Local Culture and Customs of Iron Range

Iron Range has a unique culture that is strongly influenced by the mining industry and the Finnish and Italian immigrant communities. People here are friendly, welcoming, and hardworking, with a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit Iron Range

Iron Range is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique attractions. Summers are warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing. Fall is the best time to enjoy the beautiful fall foliage and the harvest season. Winters are snowy, with plenty of opportunities for skiing, snowmobiling, and other winter sports. Spring is a time of renewal, with wildflowers in bloom and baby animals in the parks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iron Range

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Iron Range:

Do:

  • Do explore the many hiking and biking trails in the region, as they offer some of the best views and experiences.
  • Do try the local cuisine, as it is often hearty, delicious, and made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs and be friendly, courteous, and welcoming to everyone you meet.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, as keeping the area clean is important to both locals and visitors.
  • Don’t ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities, especially when it comes to hunting, fishing, or hiking in remote areas.
  • Don’t assume that everyone here is involved in the mining industry or that they fit into any particular stereotype or classification.

Safety and Health

Iron Range is a relatively safe place to visit. However, it’s important to be aware of potential risks and take steps to prevent accidents or injuries.

Safety in Iron Range

  • Be aware of wildlife, such as black bears and wolves, when hiking or camping in the woods.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and gear when participating in outdoor activities, such as hiking boots and a reflective vest when biking on trails.
  • Be mindful of the weather and potential changes, especially when engaging in water-based activities.

Health in Iron Range

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sun protection (hat, sunscreen, etc.) during outdoor activities to avoid sunburn.
  • Bring any necessary medication or prescription drugs, and make note of local medical centers and pharmacies in case of an emergency.

Getting to Iron Range: Transportation Options and Logistics

Iron Range is accessible by car, bus, and plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Driving - If you’re coming from Minneapolis or Duluth, Iron Range is a relatively easy drive on the I-35 and Highway 53.
  • Bus - Jefferson Lines and Greyhound offer bus service to several Iron Range towns, including Hibbibg and Virginia.
  • Flying - Hibbing/Chisholm Airport is the closest airport for those coming from outside of Minnesota. Additionally, Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport is about a 3-hour drive from the Iron Range.

Airports Near Iron Range

  • Hibbing/Chisholm Airport (HIB)
  • Duluth International Airport (DLH)
  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP)

Bus Stations Near Iron Range

  • Greyhound Hibbing Bus Station
  • Jefferson Lines Virginia Bus Stop

Other Options

  • Car rental is available at most airports, bus stations and downtown areas of the larger towns within Iron Range.

How to Get Around in Iron Range

The best way to get around Iron Range is by car, as it offers the most flexibility and access to all of the attractions. However, biking, walking, and buses are also viable options for exploring certain parts or towns of Iron Range.

Where to Go Next from Iron Range: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Iron Range is a great jumping-off point for exploring Minnesota’s many scenic destinations. Here are some nearby destinations you might want to include in your travel itinerary:

  • Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness - A pristine wilderness area that offers canoeing, camping, fishing, and hiking.
  • Lake Superior North Shore - A scenic drive that runs along the north shore of Lake Superior and offers stunning views and charming small towns.
  • Voyageurs National Park - A national park that boasts scenic lakes, forests, and wildlife, perfect for hiking, boating, and fishing.

Conclusion

Iron Range is an unmissable destination that offers something for everyone, from beautiful natural attractions to historic sites and cultural attractions. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit and have a memorable trip to Minnesota’s Iron Range region.
