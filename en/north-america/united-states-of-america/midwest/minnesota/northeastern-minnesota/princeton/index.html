Introduction to Princeton

Nestled in Northeastern Minnesota, Princeton is a beautiful town with a rich history and a vibrant outdoor scene. The town is small, but it packs a big punch when it comes to attractions and activities. From hiking and biking to museums and historical sites, Princeton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Princeton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mille Lacs Indian Museum and Trading Post

The Mille Lacs Indian Museum and Trading Post is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in Native American history and culture. The museum features exhibits on Ojibwe history, art, and traditions. Visitors can also purchase authentic Native American crafts and souvenirs at the trading post.

The Great Northern Depot

The Great Northern Depot is a historic train station that now houses a museum dedicated to the railroad history of Princeton and the surrounding area. The museum features exhibits on the role of the railroad industry in the development of the town and the people who worked on the trains.

Princeton Historical Society Museum

The Princeton Historical Society Museum showcases the town’s history, from its early settlement to the present day. The museum features exhibits on local businesses, education, and transportation, as well as artifacts and photographs of Princeton’s past.

Natural Attractions of Princeton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rum River State Forest

Rum River State Forest is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering miles of hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing trails. The forest is also home to several lakes and streams, making it an ideal spot for fishing, canoeing, and kayaking.

Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge

Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge is a vast expanse of protected wilderness that provides a home for a diverse array of plants and animals. Visitors can hike through the forests, marshes, and grasslands, or explore the refuge’s many lakes and ponds by boat.

Princeton Area Lakes

Princeton is surrounded by several beautiful lakes that offer a variety of recreational opportunities, including swimming, boating, and fishing. Green Lake, Elk Lake, and Blue Lake are all easily accessible from town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Princeton

Princeton has dozens of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging backcountry adventures. Some of the most popular routes include the Rum River State Trail, the Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge Hiking Trail, and the Elk River Trail.

Best Things to Do in Princeton: Activities and Tours

From kayaking down the Rum River to exploring the town’s rich history, there’s always something to do in Princeton. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • The Mille Lacs Indian Museum and Trading Post Tour
  • The Great Northern Depot Train Tour
  • Rum River State Forest Guided Hike
  • Princeton Historical Society Museum Tour
  • Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge Boat Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Princeton

Must-Try Dishes

Princeton is known for its hearty Midwestern cuisine, with plenty of comfort foods to try. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Juicy Lucy burgers
  • Tater Tot Hotdish
  • Walleye fish fry
  • Wild rice soup
  • Jucy Lucy beer cheese soup

Local Beverages

Princeton is home to several breweries and wineries, offering a range of locally-made beers, wines, and spirits. Some of the best places to grab a drink include Waconia Brewing Company and Next Chapter Winery.

Where to Stay in Princeton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Princeton has several options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, Princeton, MN
  • Princeton Inn
  • Blue Lake Resort

What to Buy in Princeton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Princeton is home to several unique shops and boutiques, offering a variety of local products and souvenirs. Some of the best places to shop include:

  • Carl’s Corner
  • Sunnyside Gift Co.

Local Culture and Customs of Princeton

Princeton is a Midwestern town with a friendly and welcoming culture. Visitors should expect small-town hospitality, and should be sure to say hello and strike up a conversation with the locals. The town is proud of its Native American heritage, and visitors should show respect when visiting cultural sites and museums.

Best Time to Visit Princeton

The best time to visit Princeton is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. The town hosts several festivals and events during this time, including the Rum River Festival and the Sherburne County Fair. Fall is also a great time to visit, with the changing leaves providing a beautiful backdrop for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Princeton

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the great outdoors
  • Take a tour of the town’s historical sites

Don’t:

  • Forget to show respect for the town’s Native American heritage
  • Overlook the local breweries and wineries
  • Underestimate the weather - be prepared for sudden changes in temperature and weather conditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Princeton

Princeton is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when exploring remote areas of the surrounding wilderness.

Health in Princeton

Princeton has one hospital, Fairview Northland Medical Center, and several clinics and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance and carry any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Princeton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Princeton

The closest airport to Princeton is Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP), which is approximately 60 miles southwest of the town.

Bus Stations Near Princeton

Greyhound operates a bus service to and from Minneapolis, with a stop in Princeton.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Princeton, with several major highways passing through the town.

How to Get Around in Princeton

Princeton is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or use a taxi service for longer trips or to explore the surrounding wilderness.

Where to Go Next from Princeton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Princeton is located in the heart of Northeastern Minnesota, surrounded by several other beautiful towns and attractions. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Duluth and the North Shore
  • Brainerd Lakes Area
  • St. Cloud Area
  • Voyageurs National Park

Conclusion

Princeton is a hidden gem in Northeastern Minnesota, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re exploring the town’s museums and historical sites or getting outside to hike, bike, or kayak, Princeton has something to offer every type of traveler.
