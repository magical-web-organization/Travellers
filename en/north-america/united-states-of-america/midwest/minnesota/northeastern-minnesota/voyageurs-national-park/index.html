Introduction to Voyageurs National Park

Voyageurs National Park is a natural wonderland spanning more than 218,000 acres in northeastern Minnesota. This park is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and nature lovers, with endless opportunities for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and scenic views of beautiful lakes, forests, and wildlife. Whether you want to hike, bike, paddle, or simply relax and enjoy the beauty of nature, Voyageurs National Park is the perfect destination for your next vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Voyageurs National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main draws of Voyageurs National Park is its rich history and cultural significance. The park is home to a number of museums and historical sites, including:

  • Kettle Falls Hotel: A historic hotel located on the shores of Rainy Lake, originally built in the 1910s to accommodate travelers on the Kettle Falls logging dam. Today, the hotel has been restored to its former glory, with cozy rooms, a restaurant, and a gift shop.

  • Ellsworth Rock Gardens: A beautiful and unique rock garden created by Jack Ellsworth in memory of his wife, Kate, who passed away in 1947. The garden features over 200 flowerbeds and a variety of sculptures, all made from local rocks and stones.

  • Bear Head Lake State Park: Located just a few miles outside the park, this state park is a great place to learn about the area’s cultural and natural history, with exhibits on mining, logging, and more.

Natural Attractions of Voyageurs National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Voyageurs National Park is home to several cultural landmarks, it’s perhaps best known for its stunning natural beauty and endless outdoor activities. Here are some of the park’s most popular natural attractions:

  • Rainy Lake: This massive lake stretches over 225,000 acres and offers endless opportunities for fishing, boating, and other water activities.

  • Kabetogama Lake: Another large lake within the park, Kabetogama is known for its crystal-clear waters and abundance of wildlife, including bald eagles, loons, and moose.

  • Hiking Trails: With over 27 miles of hiking trails, including the popular Echo Bay Trail and Locator Lake Trail, Voyageurs National Park is a hiker’s paradise. Whether you’re looking for a leisurely stroll or a challenging trek, you’re sure to find a trail that suits your needs.

  • Scenic Drives: For those who prefer to take in the sights from their car, Voyageurs National Park offers several scenic drives, including the Ash River Trail and the Rainy Lake Visitor Center Drive.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Voyageurs National Park

If you’re looking for a more active way to explore Voyageurs National Park, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the top options:

  • Echo Bay Trail: This 2.5-mile loop trail offers stunning views of Rainy Lake and is popular among hikers of all skill levels.

  • Locator Lake Trail: Another popular trail, the Locator Lake Trail is a 4.2-mile out-and-back route that takes hikers through beautiful forests and past several small lakes.

  • Kab-Ash Trail: This 28-mile trail connects Kabetogama Lake to Ash River and is a challenging but rewarding trek for experienced hikers. The trail is also open to mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Voyageurs National Park: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun and exciting things to do in Voyageurs National Park, no matter what your interests are. Here are some of the top activities and tours to consider:

  • Fishing: With over 500 lakes and streams in the park, Voyageurs is a haven for fishing enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking to catch walleye, northern pike, or bass, you’re sure to find a great spot to cast your line.

  • Canoeing/Kayaking: Paddling through the park’s pristine waters is one of the best ways to take in the scenery and spot wildlife. There are several outfitters in the park that offer rentals and guided tours.

  • Ranger-Led Programs: The park offers a variety of ranger-led programs and activities, including guided hikes, campfire programs, and wildlife viewing tours. Check with the park’s visitor center for a schedule of upcoming events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Voyageurs National Park

If you’re planning to spend a week in Voyageurs National Park, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive at the park and check into your lodging. Spend the afternoon exploring the Rainy Lake Visitor Center and taking a scenic drive along the Ash River Trail.
  • Day 2: Rent a canoe or kayak and paddle through the park’s beautiful waterways.
  • Day 3: Take a guided hike with a park ranger and learn more about the area’s history and wildlife.
  • Day 4: Spend the day fishing for walleye, northern pike, and other species in one of the park’s many lakes.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Bear Head Lake State Park for more hiking and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Day 6: Relax and enjoy the park’s scenery, or take a boat tour to see more of Rainy Lake.
  • Day 7: Depart the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Voyageurs National Park

While Voyageurs National Park is primarily known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities, there are a few great options for food and drink as well.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Rice Soup: This creamy soup is a Minnesota classic, with a rich, nutty flavor and plenty of vegetables and wild rice.

  • Blueberry Pie: With so many blueberry bushes in the park, it’s no surprise that blueberry pie is a local favorite. Be sure to try a slice while you’re in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Iron Range Brewing Company: This microbrewery in nearby Virginia, Minnesota, offers a variety of craft beers made with local ingredients.

  • Minnesota Wine Country: While Minnesota might not be the first place you think of when it comes to wine, there are actually several wineries in the state that produce delicious varietals.

Where to Stay in Voyageurs National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel, a cozy cabin, or a budget-friendly hostel, there are plenty of lodging options in and around Voyageurs National Park. Here are some of the best:

  • Kettle Falls Hotel: This historic hotel offers cozy rooms, a restaurant, and beautiful views of Rainy Lake.

  • Woodenfrog Campground: This popular campground offers both tent and RV sites, as well as showers and other amenities.

  • Voyageurs National Park Houseboats: For a truly unique and unforgettable experience, consider renting a houseboat for your stay in the park.

What to Buy in Voyageurs National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to remember your trip to Voyageurs National Park, here are a few great options:

  • Wild Rice: This local staple is delicious and nutritious, and makes for a great gift or addition to your own pantry.

  • Handmade Quilts and Crafts: There are several local artisans who sell beautiful handmade crafts and quilts in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Voyageurs National Park

Voyageurs National Park is home to a rich cultural heritage, with strong ties to the Ojibwe and French-Canadian communities. Visitors to the park should be respectful of the area’s cultural traditions and customs, and consider learning more about the park’s history and heritage during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Voyageurs National Park

The best time to visit Voyageurs National Park depends largely on your interests and priorities. If you’re looking for warm weather and opportunities for water activities, plan your trip for the summer months (June-August). If you’re interested in fall colors and cooler temperatures, visit in September or October. Winter can also be a great time to visit the park for snowmobiling, ice fishing, and other winter activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Voyageurs National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural resources.

  • Follow all park regulations and safety guidelines.

  • Carry out all of your trash and leave no trace behind.

Don’t:

  • Disturb or harass the park’s wildlife.

  • Damage or deface any natural or cultural resources.

  • Build fires outside of designated fire rings or areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Voyageurs National Park

Visitors to Voyageurs National Park should take precautions to ensure their safety in the wilderness. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and watch out for hazards like falling rocks and slippery trails.

  • Carry a map and compass, and be prepared for changes in weather or terrain.

  • Let someone know your itinerary and check in regularly while you’re in the park.

Health in Voyageurs National Park

As with any outdoor excursion, it’s important to take care of your health and wellbeing while visiting Voyageurs National Park. Here are some tips to help you stay healthy during your trip:

  • Stay hydrated and pack plenty of water.

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent.

  • Pack a first-aid kit and any necessary medications.

Getting to Voyageurs National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Voyageurs National Park is located in northeastern Minnesota, about 4 hours north of Minneapolis. Here are some transportation options for getting to the park:

  • By Car: The park is easily accessible by car, with several major highways leading to the area.

  • By Air: The closest airport to Voyageurs National Park is the Falls International Airport in International Falls, Minnesota.

  • By Bus: Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Jefferson Lines, provide service to nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Voyageurs National Park

The easiest way to get around Voyageurs National Park is by car, although there are also several hiking and biking trails within the park. If you don’t have your own car, consider renting one or taking advantage of one of the park’s guided tours or shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Voyageurs National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more places to explore after your visit to Voyageurs National Park, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: Located just to the north of Voyageurs National Park, this wilderness area is a paddler’s paradise with endless lakes and waterways to explore.

  • Duluth: This coastal city on the shores of Lake Superior offers plenty of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.

  • The North Shore: Further up the shore from Duluth, the North Shore is a picturesque destination with charming towns, waterfalls, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Voyageurs National Park is a true gem of the Midwest, with stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventure. Whether you’re into hiking, fishing, or simply enjoying the beauty of nature, this park has something for everyone.
