Introduction to Spring Grove

Welcome to Spring Grove, a charming town in Southern Minnesota. Known for its Norwegian heritage and picturesque landscapes, Spring Grove attracts visitors seeking outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see here.

List of Places to Visit in Spring Grove

Here’s a list of must-visit attractions in Spring Grove:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Giants of the Earth Heritage Center: Learn about the Norwegian-American experience through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and educational workshops.
  • Spring Grove Heritage Center: Explore local history and genealogy resources, and admire antique cars, tractors, and other memorabilia.
  • Heritage Grove: Stroll through a park filled with sculptures and monuments honoring Norwegian and American pioneers.

Natural Attractions of Spring Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Magelssen Bluff Park: Hike up to a 360-degree panoramic view of the surrounding valleys and bluffs.
  • King Apple Days Orchard: Pick your own apples, pumpkins, raspberries, and cherries in a family-owned farm.
  • Eagle Bluff Environmental Learning Center: Join guided hikes, birdwatching tours, and outdoor skill-building classes in a nature center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spring Grove

Spring Grove is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, thanks to its rolling hills, lush forests, and scenic trails. Here are some of the best routes for different skill levels:

  • Ironwood Trail: 3.5 miles of paved trail connecting Spring Grove to Mabel, with stunning views of the Root River.
  • Chester Woods Trail: 8 miles of rugged terrain winding through woods and valleys, with a picnic area and a playground.
  • Bluffview Trail: 1.5 miles of rugged terrain leading to the top of Magelssen Bluff, with a historic cemetery and a stone tower.
  • Root River Trail: 42 miles of paved trail following the Root River, with access to various parks and towns.

Best Things to Do in Spring Grove: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Spring Grove offers plenty of other fun activities and tours. Here are some recommendations:

  • Norwegian Ridge Birding and Nature Trail: Spot rare bird species and enjoy the flora and fauna along this self-guided trail.
  • Houston Nature Center: Discover the wildlife and ecosystems of Southeast Minnesota through educational exhibits and events.
  • S&S ATV Rentals: Explore the countryside atop an all-terrain vehicle, with guided tours and rentals available.
  • Pedal Pushers Cafe: Rent a tandem bike and grab a bite at this cozy cafe, which also serves homemade ice cream.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spring Grove

If you’re planning to spend a week in Spring Grove, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Check into a local hotel, such as Norwegian Ridge or Iron Horse Inn, and take a leisurely walk downtown to admire the architecture and murals. Have lunch at the Viking Cafe or Strutty’s Sports Bar & Grille, then visit the Giants of the Earth Heritage Center and the Spring Grove Heritage Center.

Day 2: Rent a bike or hike Ironwood Trail, stopping at Livingood’s Restaurant & Brewery for lunch and a pint. In the evening, attend a live music performance or a movie at the Fest Building.

Day 3: Head out to Magelssen Bluff Park for a picnic, and then join a guided tour of the historic Spring Grove Cemetery. Continue your outdoor adventure at King Apple Days Orchard, and end the day with a hearty dinner at Ginny’s Supper Club.

Day 4: Visit the nearby town of Lanesboro, known for its arts and culture scene, by taking the Root River Trail shuttle or driving. Explore the Commonweal Theatre, the Lanesboro Art Center, and the Root River State Bike Trail information center. Have lunch at the Parkway Market & Locker, and dinner at the Old Village Hall Restaurant & Pub.

Day 5: Treat yourself to a spa day at the Scandinavian Spa, which offers massages, facials, and body treatments inspired by Nordic traditions. Afterwards, shop for Scandinavian gifts and souvenirs at Lefse Records & Books or the Nordic Shop.

Day 6: Go on a fishing or kayaking expedition on the Root River, with help from a local outfitter like the Root River Outfitters. Have lunch at the Spring Grove Soda Pop & Candy Shop, and visit the Heritage Grove and the Historic Ag Schoolhouse.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a big breakfast at the Spring Grove Cafe, and a scenic drive to the National Eagle Center in Wabasha, where you can learn about bald eagles and watch them up close. Return to Spring Grove for lunch and last-minute shopping, and check out of your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Spring Grove

Spring Grove is proud of its culinary traditions, which combine Norwegian and American flavors in creative ways. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: A thin, flatbread-like pastry made of potato, flour, and cream, usually served with butter, cinnamon, or lingonberries.
  • Kringla: A soft, pretzel-shaped cookie made of almond paste, cardamom, and sugar, often served with coffee or tea.
  • Julekake: A sweet, braided bread made of raisins, candied fruit, and spices, traditionally eaten during Christmas.
  • Lutefisk: A distinctive fish dish made of dried whitefish that has been soaked in lye, which gives it a jelly-like texture and strong smell.

Local Beverages

  • Vikingsholm Brewery: A family-owned microbrewery that produces handcrafted beers with locally sourced ingredients, such as the Norwegian Blonde Ale and the Valley Girl IPA.
  • Whalan Standstill Parade: An annual community event that features a beer garden, live music, and a parade of stationary floats along the Root River Trail.
  • Spring Grove Soda Pop & Candy Shop: A vintage-style store that sells old-fashioned sodas, candies, and ice cream, such as the Norwegian Nuk, the Sour Worms, and the Blueberry Cheesecake.

Where to Stay in Spring Grove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spring Grove offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Here are some options:

  • Norwegian Ridge: A bed and breakfast that features Norwegian-inspired decor, top-notch amenities, and a home-cooked breakfast, located near downtown.
  • Iron Horse Inn: A historic hotel that offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and easy access to the Root River Trail, located next to the Fest Building.
  • Bluffscape Amish Tours & Lodging: A guesthouse that offers guided tours of Amish farms and crafts, cozy rooms, and homemade breakfast, located in nearby Lanesboro.

What to Buy in Spring Grove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Spring Grove without bringing back some unique gifts for yourself or your loved ones. Here are some suggestions:

  • Lefse and other Norwegian treats from Syttende Mai
  • Handmade pottery and artwork from Bluff Country Artists Gallery
  • Local honey and jams from Lynda’s Woods and Cliffs Honey Farm
  • Scandinavian gifts and books from Lefse Records & Books and the Nordic Shop

Local Culture and Customs of Spring Grove

Spring Grove has a strong Norwegian heritage that is celebrated throughout the year with festivals, parades, and cultural events. Norwegians were among the first settlers in the area in the mid-19th century, and their influence is visible in the local traditions, architecture, and cuisine. Spring Grove is also known for its tight-knit community, its volunteer spirit, and its commitment to sustainability.

Best Time to Visit Spring Grove

Spring and fall are the best seasons to visit Spring Grove, when the weather is mild and the foliage is colorful. Spring brings blooming wildflowers, migrating birds, and the Syttende Mai festival in May, which honors Norway’s independence. Fall brings apple picking, pumpkin patches, and the King Apple Days festival in September, which celebrates the orchard’s harvest. Winter can be chilly and snowy, but it offers opportunities for cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and cozy fireside gatherings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spring Grove

Here are some etiquette tips to keep in mind while visiting Spring Grove:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors and embrace the rural lifestyle.
  • Attend local festivals and events, and participate in community activities.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, including Norwegian heritage.
  • Support local businesses and artisans, and buy locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Be loud, rude, or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Litter, damage, or trespass on private property.
  • Ignore safety rules and warnings, especially when hiking or biking.
  • Disrespect wildlife, ecosystems, or natural resources.

Safety and Health

Spring Grove is generally a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should be aware of certain risks and precautions.

Safety in Spring Grove

  • Watch out for wildlife, such as bears, wolves, and coyotes, especially in wooded areas.
  • Use caution when hiking or biking on rugged terrain, and wear appropriate gear.
  • Beware of extreme weather conditions, such as thunderstorms, tornadoes, and blizzards.
  • Practice fire safety and respect local fire bans and rules.

Health in Spring Grove

  • Use sunscreen and bug spray to protect against sunburn and insect bites.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid heat exhaustion during hot weather.
  • Be aware of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease and Powassan virus, and use preventive measures, such as tick repellent and clothing.
  • Follow COVID-19 guidelines and restrictions, including face masks, social distancing, and vaccination.

Getting to Spring Grove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spring Grove is located about 150 miles southeast of Minneapolis and about 85 miles northwest of La Crosse. Here are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Spring Grove

  • Rochester International Airport: Located about 60 miles east of Spring Grove, with connections to major U.S. cities through Delta, American, and United airlines.
  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport: Located about 150 miles northwest of Spring Grove, with connections to domestic and international destinations through numerous airlines.

Bus Stations Near Spring Grove

  • Houston County Express: Provides daily bus service from La Crosse to Spring Grove, with connections to other towns in Southeast Minnesota, and affordable fares.
  • Jefferson Lines: Provides intercity bus service from Minneapolis to La Crosse, with connections to Spring Grove through Houston County Express, and comfortable amenities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Several major car rental companies, such as Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis, offer rental cars at Rochester and Minneapolis airports.
  • Private shuttle: Some hotels and resorts in the area offer private shuttle service to and from Spring Grove, such as Norwegian Ridge and Iron Horse Inn. Contact your accommodation for more information.

How to Get Around in Spring Grove

Spring Grove is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. If you need a car, there are several car rental options in nearby cities, such as Rochester and La Crosse. Taxis and ride-sharing services are not available in Spring Grove, but there are local shuttle services, such as the Houston County Express, that can take you to nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Spring Grove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Lanesboro: A charming town known for its arts and culture scene, with art galleries, theater productions, and the Root River Trail.
  • Decorah: A scenic town known for its Norwegian-American heritage, with the Vesterheim Norwegian-American Museum and the Seed Sowers, a folk dance group.
  • Winona: A historic town on the banks of the Mississippi River, with the Minnesota Marine Art Museum, the Garvin Heights Vineyards, and the Great River Shakespeare Festival.

Conclusion

Spring Grove offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural richness, and small-town charm that will delight any traveler. Whether you come for a day trip or a week-long vacation, you’ll find something to love here. Enjoy the outdoors, taste the local flavors, and immerse yourself in the local customs and traditions. Safe travels!
