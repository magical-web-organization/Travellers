Introduction to Redwood Falls

Nestled in Southern Minnesota, Redwood Falls is a charming city with a rich history. Known for its natural beauty, the city is home to several scenic attractions, including the Minneopa State Park and the Redwood River. If you’re looking for a relaxed and peaceful getaway, Redwood Falls is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Redwood Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Redwood County Historical Society Museum: The museum showcases the history of Redwood County, including its settlement by European immigrants, the Dakota Wars, and more.
  • Gilfillan Estate: A 20-room Victorian-style mansion listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the Gilfillan Estate offers a glimpse into the life of a wealthy family in the late 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Redwood Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Minneopa State Park: The park is home to a stunning 60-foot waterfall and offers miles of hiking trails and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Alexander Ramsey Park: The largest municipal park in Minnesota, Alexander Ramsey Park features natural beauty and recreational opportunities, including camping and fishing.
  • Redwood River: The river features stunning foliage, scenic overlooks, and fishing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Redwood Falls

  • Minneopa State Park Loop Trail: A 3.3-mile trail that winds through the park’s stunning natural scenery, including the waterfall and rocky bluffs.
  • Ramsey Falls Trail: This 1-mile trail takes hikers to the base of the Ramsey Falls, a smaller, but still impressive waterfall in Alexander Ramsey Park.
  • Redwood River Trail: This scenic trail follows the Redwood River and offers opportunities for fishing and wildlife viewing.

Best Things to Do in Redwood Falls: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: The Redwood River and nearby lakes provide excellent fishing opportunities for anglers of all levels.
  • Visit a Local Winery: The area is known for its beautiful vineyards and wineries, including Morgan Creek Vineyards and Round Lake Vineyards and Winery.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a trail ride through the beautiful Minnesota countryside with River Valley Horse Ranch.

Food and Drink Scene of Redwood Falls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: A traditional Norwegian flatbread made with potatoes and served with butter and brown sugar, lefse is a must-try when in Redwood Falls.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A regional specialty, wild rice soup features a creamy broth loaded with wild rice and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Sample craft beers from local breweries like Beaver Island Brewing and Mankato Brewery.
  • Wine: Local wineries offer tastings and tours, with Morgan Creek Vineyards and Round Lake Vineyards and Winery being top choices.

Where to Stay in Redwood Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Redwood Lodge: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and amenities like free Wi-Fi and continental breakfast.
  • Smart Choice Inn and Suites: A highly-rated hotel that offers clean rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Anderson House Hotel: A beautiful, historic hotel with luxurious rooms and top-notch amenities.

What to Buy in Redwood Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wild Rice: A Minnesota specialty, wild rice is a great souvenir to bring home and use in your own recipes.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: The Redwood Falls area is home to several talented potters who create beautiful handcrafted pieces.
  • Local Wine: Pick up a bottle of wine from one of the area’s beautiful wineries to bring home and enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Redwood Falls

  • Norwegian Heritage: Many of the area’s residents are of Norwegian descent, and the city celebrates its heritage with festivals and events throughout the year.
  • Natural Beauty: Redwood Falls residents are proud of the stunning natural beauty of the area and work hard to preserve it.

Best Time to Visit Redwood Falls

The best time to visit Redwood Falls is in the summer months, between June and August, when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities can be enjoyed to the fullest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Redwood Falls

Do:

  • Do take in the stunning natural beauty of the area.
  • Do visit the local wineries and try some of the area’s excellent wines.
  • Do participate in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment in any way.
  • Don’t disrespect the area’s cultural heritage.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Redwood Falls

Redwood Falls is a safe and welcoming city, but it’s important to take normal precautions when traveling.

Health in Redwood Falls

Redwood Falls has excellent healthcare facilities, including the Redwood Area Hospital.

Getting to Redwood Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Redwood Falls

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP): Located about two hours from Redwood Falls, MSP is one of the largest airports in the region and offers flights to destinations around the world.
  • Hector International Airport (FAR): Located about two hours from Redwood Falls, FAR offers flights to several major U.S. cities.

Bus Stations Near Redwood Falls

  • Jefferson Lines: Jefferson Lines operates a bus stop in nearby Marshall, Minnesota, which is about 30 minutes from Redwood Falls.

Other Options

Redwood Falls is easily accessible via car or bike, with several major highways running through the area.

How to Get Around in Redwood Falls

The best way to get around Redwood Falls is by car or bike. There are several places to rent bikes in the area, and local car rental companies offer affordable rental options.

Where to Go Next from Redwood Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New Ulm, Minnesota: A charming German-American town known for its beautiful Bavarian architecture and Oktoberfest celebrations.
  • Mankato, Minnesota: A bustling city with plenty of shopping, dining, and recreational opportunities.
  • Sioux Falls, South Dakota: Located just a few hours from Redwood Falls, Sioux Falls offers shopping, dining, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Redwood Falls is a hidden gem in Minnesota, with plenty of natural beauty and outdoor activities to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply want a peaceful getaway, Redwood Falls is the perfect destination.
