Introduction to Lanesboro

Nestled in the heart of Southern Minnesota, Lanesboro is a charming town that is perfect for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation. Known for its breathtaking scenery, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural offerings, Lanesboro has something for everyone.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, you’ll find plenty to love in this quaint Midwestern town. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the top things to do and see in Lanesboro, along with tips for getting there, getting around, and staying safe.

List of Places to Visit in Lanesboro

If you’re planning a trip to Lanesboro, you won’t want to miss these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, be sure to add these attractions to your itinerary:

  • Lanesboro Historical Museum: This museum provides a fascinating look at the town’s rich history, including its early pioneer days and its role in the lumber industry.
  • Commonweal Theatre: This professional theater company offers a wide range of productions, from classic plays to contemporary works.
  • Lanesboro Arts Center: This thriving arts center features exhibitions, classes, and events that showcase the talents of local artists.

Natural Attractions of Lanesboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lanesboro is an outdoor lover’s paradise, with endless opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors. Don’t miss these top natural attractions:

  • Root River State Trail: This 60-mile trail passes through Lanesboro and offers stunning views of the Root River Valley. Rent a bike or lace up your hiking boots and explore this beautiful trail.
  • Lanesboro Dam: Located on the edge of town, this picturesque dam offers a great spot for fishing, picnicking, or simply enjoying the view.
  • Eagle Bluff Environmental Learning Center: This center offers a wide range of educational programs and outdoor activities, including kayaking, rock climbing, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lanesboro

If you love hiking and biking, you’ll be in heaven in Lanesboro. Some of the top trails to explore include:

  • Root River State Trail: With 60 miles of scenic trail to explore, you’ll never get bored on the Root River State Trail. This trail is perfect for biking, hiking, or even cross-country skiing in the winter.
  • Lanesboro Bluff Country Loop: This 25-mile loop takes you through some of the most beautiful countryside in the area, with plenty of opportunities to stop and soak up the scenery.
  • Dakota Rail Regional Trail: Located just a short drive from Lanesboro, this trail offers 26 miles of paved trail along the shores of Lake Minnetonka.

Best Things to Do in Lanesboro: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun and unique activities to enjoy during your stay in Lanesboro? Check out these top things to do:

  • Old Barn Resort Zip Line Tour: Soar through the treetops on this adrenaline-boosting zip line tour. With six different lines to explore, this is a must-do for adventure seekers.
  • Grandpa’s Amish Tours: This tour takes you into the heart of Amish country, where you can experience the simple and peaceful way of life of this fascinating community.
  • Lanesboro Farmers Market: Taking place every Saturday from April to October, this vibrant farmers market offers plenty of opportunities to sample some of the best local foods and products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lanesboro

If you’re planning a longer stay in Lanesboro, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Lanesboro, including the Lanesboro Historical Museum and the Lanesboro Arts Center. In the evening, catch a show at the Commonweal Theatre.
  • Day 2: Hit the trails! Spend the day hiking or biking along the Root River State Trail, taking in the stunning scenery along the way.
  • Day 3: Get an up-close look at the local Amish community with a tour of the area’s farms and markets.
  • Day 4: Explore the great outdoors at Eagle Bluff Environmental Learning Center. Try your hand at kayaking, rock climbing, or archery.
  • Day 5: Discover the local cuisine at the Lanesboro Farmers Market, and then head out on a zip line tour at Old Barn Resort.
  • Day 6: Drive out to the Dakota Rail Regional Trail for a day of biking, hiking, or cross-country skiing.
  • Day 7: Relax and recharge with a spa day at one of the area’s top wellness centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Lanesboro

No trip to Lanesboro is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: This traditional Norwegian flatbread is a local specialty in Lanesboro. Enjoy it with butter, jam, or cheese.
  • Bison burger: Try a juicy bison burger at one of the many local restaurants in town.
  • Scones: These freshly baked pastries are a breakfast favorite in Lanesboro.

Local Beverages

  • Brewed Awakenings: This local coffee shop offers artisanal coffee and tea drinks, as well as tasty pastries and light fare.
  • Elmaro Vineyard: Sample award-winning wines at this family-owned vineyard, located just a short drive from Lanesboro.
  • Root River Brewing Co.: This popular brewery offers a range of craft beers and tasty pub fare.

Where to Stay in Lanesboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lanesboro has plenty of options for accommodations, whether you’re looking for a cozy bed and breakfast or a modern hotel.

  • Stone Mill Hotel & Suites: This historic hotel offers beautifully restored guest rooms and suites, as well as an on-site spa and restaurant.
  • Lanesboro Barn Bed & Breakfast: Stay in a charming converted barn at this cozy B&B.
  • Habberstad House Bed & Breakfast: This beautiful Victorian home offers comfortable accommodations and a delicious breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Lanesboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to remember your trip to Lanesboro? Here are some of the top local products to bring home:

  • Lefse-making supplies: Pick up some lefse-making supplies so you can recreate this local delicacy at home.
  • Local honey: Try some of the delicious honey produced by local beekeepers.
  • Handmade jewelry: Browse the collections of local artisans, who craft beautiful jewelry using materials like silver and gemstones.

Local Culture and Customs of Lanesboro

Lanesboro is a friendly and welcoming town, with a rich history and a strong sense of community. Here are some things to keep in mind during your stay:

  • Respect the Amish: If you take a tour of Amish country, be sure to dress modestly and avoid taking photos of the Amish people or their property.
  • Be friendly and courteous: Lanesboro residents are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature, so be sure to return the favor with a smile and a kind word.

Best Time to Visit Lanesboro

Lanesboro is beautiful year-round, but there are some times of the year that are particularly special. Here are the best times to visit:

  • Spring: Visit in April or May to enjoy the blooming wildflowers and the opening of local farmers markets.
  • Summer: Summer is peak season in Lanesboro, with plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.
  • Fall: For spectacular fall foliage, plan a trip to Lanesboro in September or October.
  • Winter: While it can be chilly, winter is a great time to visit Lanesboro for cross-country skiing or cozy nights by the fire.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lanesboro

When visiting Lanesboro, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do bring comfortable shoes: With so many great hiking and biking trails, you’ll want to be prepared.
  • Do try the local cuisine: Lanesboro has some amazing local dishes to try, so be sure to sample the local fare.
  • Do take a tour of Amish country: This is a unique and fascinating experience that shouldn’t be missed.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Help keep Lanesboro beautiful by disposing of your trash properly.
  • Don’t take photos of the Amish people or their property: Be respectful of this community’s privacy and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to tip: Like most places in the United States, it’s customary to tip at restaurants, bars, and other service establishments.

Safety and Health

As with any destination, it’s important to stay safe and healthy during your visit to Lanesboro.

Safety in Lanesboro

Lanesboro is a safe and welcoming town, but it’s still important to take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Lock your car and hotel room: This is a simple way to protect your belongings from theft.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye out for any suspicious behavior, and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Lanesboro

Lanesboro has several medical facilities in case of emergency, including the Lanesboro Community Medical Center and the Gundersen Health System in nearby La Crosse, Wisconsin. Remember to pack any necessary medications and enjoy your trip with peace of mind.

Getting to Lanesboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lanesboro is easily accessible by car, and is located just off of US Route 52. If you’re flying in, the closest airport is the Rochester International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from Lanesboro. Alternatively, you could fly into Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport and rent a car for the 2-hour drive to Lanesboro.

Airports Near Lanesboro

  • Rochester International Airport (RST): This airport is located about 45 miles from Lanesboro.
  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport (MSP): This airport is located about 125 miles from Lanesboro.

Bus Stations Near Lanesboro

Lanesboro is not served by any public transit, but there are several bus stations within driving distance:

  • Rochester Bus Terminal: Located in Rochester, about 45 miles from Lanesboro.
  • Winona Greyhound Terminal: Located in Winona, about 30 miles from Lanesboro.

Other Options

Alternatively, you could rent a car or take a taxi from a nearby airport or bus station. This will give you more flexibility and the ability to explore the area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Lanesboro

Lanesboro is a small town that is easy to explore on foot. However, if you’d like to venture outside of the town center, you may need a car or bike. Bikes can be rented in town, and there are several car rental options in nearby cities like Rochester and Winona.

Where to Go Next from Lanesboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’d like to extend your trip and explore more of the Midwest, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Minneapolis/St. Paul: Just a 2-hour drive from Lanesboro, the Twin Cities offer a wide range of cultural offerings and attractions.
  • Wisconsin Dells: Located about 2.5 hours from Lanesboro, this popular tourist destination is known for its waterparks and family-friendly activities.
  • Great River Road National Scenic Byway: Take a scenic drive along the Mississippi River, exploring charming river towns and stunning natural beauty along the way.

Conclusion

Lanesboro is a hidden gem in the heart of the Midwest, offering breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural offerings, and a warm and welcoming community. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Lanesboro. Use this travel guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this charming town.
