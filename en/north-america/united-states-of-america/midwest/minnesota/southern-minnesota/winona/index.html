Introduction to Winona

Winona, a picturesque city in Southern Minnesota, is known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Situated in the heart of Mississippi River Valley, Winona offers endless opportunities to explore the outdoors, from hiking and biking trails to waterfalls and scenic river views.

Whether you are a history buff or a nature lover, Winona is a destination that is sure to impress. With stunning architecture, world-class museums, and a lively food and drink scene, Winona has a little something for everyone. Let’s explore the best things to do in Winona and discover this hidden gem of Southern Minnesota.

List of Places to Visit in Winona

Here are the top places to visit in Winona:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Polish Cultural Institute and Museum

The Polish Cultural Institute and Museum houses an eclectic collection of Polish artifacts and exhibits which are testament to the rich Polish heritage and ancestry. The museum also holds regular talks and events to promote Polish culture and traditions.

2. Winona County Historical Society and Museum

The Winona County Historical Society and Museum exhibits the history, culture, and evolution of Winona County. The museum is home to thousands of artifacts, photographs, and documents that reflect the history of the county. Visitors can also take tours of the nearby buildings and learn about the region’s architectural evolution.

3. Watkins Heritage Museum and Store

Watkins Heritage Museum and Store is a tribute to the iconic Winona-based Watkins Company. The company is famous for its spices, extracts and other consumer goods. At the museum, visitors can see the company’s original headquarters, learn about its history, and purchase Watkins products.

Natural Attractions of Winona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Garvin Heights City Park

Garvin Heights City Park is one of the most popular hiking trails in the city. The park offers a panoramic view of the city and the Mississippi River. Visitors can hike the trail, enjoy a picnic, or just sit back and take in the scenic views.

2. Great River Bluffs State Park

Great River Bluffs State Park is located on the bluffs overlooking the Mississippi River. It offers miles of hiking and biking trails, picnic area and a campground. Visitors can enjoy the spectacular views of the river and the valley.

3. Sugar Loaf

Sugar Loaf is a 500-foot bluff that offers one of the best views of the city. Visitors can take a short hike to the top of the bluff and enjoy the stunning sunset views of the valley, river, and city.

Best Things to Do in Winona: Activities and Tours

Winona offers various activities and tours that suit every type of traveler. Here are the top things to do in Winona:

  • Explore the historical places to learn about the region’s past.
  • Go hiking or biking trails to enjoy the beautiful scenic views.
  • Visit the winery or brewery tours to savour the local flavours.
  • Attend an art exhibition or a theatre show to experience the cultural beauty of the city.
  • Participate in local outdoor events like canoeing, kayaking, or fishing to experience the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Winona

Here’s a perfect week-long itinerary to experience the best of Winona:

  • Day 1: Visit the Winona County Historical Society and Museum.
  • Day 2: Explore the Garvin Heights City Park.
  • Day 3: Drive to Lake City and enjoy a day at the beach.
  • Day 4: Visit the Great River Bluffs State Park.
  • Day 5: Hit the Sugar Loaf and combine it with a visit to nearby local wineries.
  • Day 6: Take a bike ride on the Great River State Trail and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 7: Attend an event like the Great River Shakespeare Festival or the Boats and Bluegrass Festival to end the trip on a high note.

Local Culture and Customs of Winona

Winona has a rich cultural history. The city is known for several traditions, including the Boats and Bluegrass Festival, the Great River Shakespeare, and various outdoor recreational activities. Visitors should respect the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Winona

Winona can be visited throughout the year, but the best time is from June to September when the weather is pleasant, river activities are popular, and the festivals are happening. Moreover, a visit during Fall will give you a breathtaking view of the foliage colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Winona

Here are a few things to follow and avoid during your visit to Winona:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Follow the safety guidelines while indulging in outdoor activities
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in the natural areas.
  • Ignore the safety guidelines while indulging in outdoor activities.
  • Be rude to locals or take any objects or artifacts from the historical places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Winona

Winona is a safe city, and visitors need not worry about general safety. However, visitors should follow usual safety measures like locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Winona

The city has medical facilities, emergency services, and walk-in clinics for minor health issues. Visitors should carry essential medications and travel insurance when traveling to any new place.

Getting to Winona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Winona

The nearest airport to Winona is Rochester International Airport (RST) located in Rochester, Minnesota, which is a one-hour drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Winona

Winona has its own bus station. Visitors can also take buses from nearby cities like Rochester, La Crosse, and Minneapolis.

Other Options

Winona is accessible by car via highways like Interstate 90 and U.S. Highways 14, 61 and 43.

How to Get Around in Winona

Winona is a walkable city and also has multiple bike routes and rental options. Visitors can use taxi services, or rent a car to explore the city comfortably.
