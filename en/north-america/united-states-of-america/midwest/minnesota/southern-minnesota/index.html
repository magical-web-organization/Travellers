Introduction to Southern Minnesota

Located in the Midwest region of the United States, Southern Minnesota boasts an abundance of natural beauty, rich culture, engaging activities, and mouthwatering cuisine. From picturesque landscapes and historical landmarks to exciting outdoor adventures and delicious food, Southern Minnesota has something for everyone. Whether you’re a solo adventurer, a family looking for a fun getaway, or simply looking for a place to unwind and recharge, you’ll find everything you need in Southern Minnesota.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Minnesota

Southern Minnesota is full of must-see destinations. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southern Minnesota is steeped in history and culture. Here are some of the best museums and historical places to visit:

  • Minnesota Marine Art Museum: Located in Winona, this museum is home to an impressive collection of marine art.
  • Hermann Monument: This monument in New Ulm honors the memory of German freedom fighter Hermann and provides stunning views from the top.
  • Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum: Located near Walnut Grove, this museum celebrates the life and legacy of the famous author of the “Little House on the Prairie” books.
  • Owatonna Arts Center: This art center houses galleries, classrooms, and studios for artists of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Southern Minnesota: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking and biking trails to breathtaking views of lakes and waterfalls, Southern Minnesota’s natural beauty is irresistible. Here are some of the natural attractions worth visiting:

  • Minneopa State Park: Located just outside of Mankato, this stunning park offers hiking trails, waterfalls, and opportunities to see bison up close.
  • Niagara Cave: This popular tourist destination in Harmony offers a 1-mile guided tour through the cave’s chambers and contains waterfalls, fossils, and stalactites.
  • Lake Pepin: Located along the Mississippi River, Lake Pepin is a popular spot for swimming, boating, and relaxing on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Minnesota

Southern Minnesota offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Root River Bike Trail: This 42-mile trail from Lanesboro to Houston offers stunning views of the river and small towns along the way.
  • North Shore Trail: This 6.5-mile trail in Owatonna meanders along the scenic Straight River and is popular for walking, running, and biking.
  • Red Jacket Trail: This 13-mile trail in Mankato is great for beginners, offering beautiful views of the Minnesota River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Southern Minnesota: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in Southern Minnesota. Here are some of the most exciting activities and tours:

  • Farm-to-Table Tours: Southern Minnesota is known for its farming culture and fresh produce. Take a tour of a local farm and enjoy a farm-to-table meal.
  • Zipline Tour: Take in the beauty of Southern Minnesota from above! Refreshing Mountain Retreat offers a thrilling zipline tour in Lanesboro.
  • Civil War Reenactments: If you’re a history buff, check out the annual Civil War reenactment in Owatonna.
  • La Crosse Queen Paddlewheel: This historic steamboat offers a scenic ride on the Mississippi River with its paddle wheel and Victorian design.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Minnesota

If you have a week to spend in Southern Minnesota, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Minnesota Marine Art Museum and stroll Winona’s downtown
Day 2-3 Hike in Minneopa State Park and Lake Pepin
Day 4 Take a farm-to-table tour and enjoy a meal made from local produce
Day 5 Go ziplining in Lanesboro and take a scenic drive through the countryside
Day 6 Take a paddlewheel boat ride on the Mississippi river in La Crosse
Day 7 Visit the Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum and explore New Ulm

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Minnesota

Southern Minnesota is known for its fresh produce, locally sourced meat, and unique flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hot Beef Commercial: A popular sandwich in Winona made with roast beef, mashed potatoes, and gravy served on bread.
  • Meat Raffle: A weekly tradition at many local bars, where patrons can win meat packages while enjoying a night out.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A classic Midwestern recipe made with wild rice, chicken, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Schell’s Beer: The second-oldest family-owned brewery in America located in New Ulm. Schell’s offers a wide range of German-inspired beers.
  • Mighty Fine Cider: This award-winning cider company in Grand Meadow offers refreshing flavors like Peach Ginger and Jumping Juniper.

Where to Stay in Southern Minnesota: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Minnesota offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget. Whether you prefer a cozy guesthouse or a luxury hotel, you’ll find a place to rest your head. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • The Kahler Grand Hotel: Located in Rochester, this historic hotel features elegant decor and modern amenities.
  • Hemker Park & Zoo Cabins: This unique accommodation option offers cabins situated alongside the zoo’s animals.
  • Simpson House Bed & Breakfast: Located in Sleepy Eye, this charming B&B features beautiful Victorian architecture and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Southern Minnesota: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some of the best options:

  • Wild Rice: A staple food of Minnesota, wild rice is available in many local shops.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Many artisans sell handcrafted pottery made from local clay.
  • Minnesota Grown Products: From honey to maple syrup to vegetables, you can find many locally grown products at farm stands and markets around Southern Minnesota.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Minnesota

Southern Minnesota has a rich cultural heritage shaped by its history and natural surroundings. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Minnesota Nice: “Minnesota Nice” is a term used to describe the friendly and welcoming nature of the people of Minnesota.
  • German Influence: Many cities in Southern Minnesota were founded by German immigrants, and their influence is still felt today in the form of Oktoberfest celebrations and German food traditions.
  • Farming Culture: Agriculture is an important part of Southern Minnesota’s economy and culture, and many traditions and celebrations revolve around farming activities.

Best Time to Visit Southern Minnesota

The best time to visit Southern Minnesota is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is mild and sunny. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with stunning fall foliage and harvest festivals. However, Southern Minnesota can be visited year-round, with winter offering a chance to enjoy winter sports such as ice fishing and cross-country skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Minnesota

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable visit to Southern Minnesota, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Embrace Minnesota Nice and strike up a conversation with locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the farmers’ markets.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather. Southern Minnesota can get very cold in the winter and hot in the summer. Be prepared with proper clothing.
  • Disrespect the natural surroundings. Southern Minnesota has many protected natural areas, so be sure to follow Leave No Trace principles and respect wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Minnesota is generally not a concern, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Southern Minnesota

  • Lock your car doors and don’t leave valuables in plain sight.
  • If hiking or biking, be sure to stay on designated trails and carry a map.
  • Take weather reports seriously and follow any warnings or advisories.

Health in Southern Minnesota

  • Tap water is safe to drink in Southern Minnesota.
  • Make sure to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • If you have allergies or asthma, be sure to pack any necessary medications.

Getting to Southern Minnesota: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southern Minnesota is easily accessible by car, plane, or bus. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Southern Minnesota

  • Rochester International Airport
  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport
  • La Crosse Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Southern Minnesota

  • Greyhound Bus Stations in Rochester and Winona
  • Jefferson Bus Lines in Mankato and Rochester

Other Options

  • Amtrak’s Empire Builder route stops in Winona and Red Wing.
  • Road tripping to Southern Minnesota is also a popular option, with Interstate 90 running east-west through the region.

How to Get Around in Southern Minnesota

Southern Minnesota is easy to navigate by car, but other transportation options include:

  • Biking: Many cities, like Rochester and Winona, have bike-sharing programs and designated bike lanes.
  • Public Transit: Rochester and other cities offer public transit with routes that cover the entire city.
  • Tours: Many tour companies offer guided tours of Southern Minnesota and its various attractions.

Where to Go Next from Southern Minnesota: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning a longer trip, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Twin Cities: Just over an hour north of Southern Minnesota, the Twin Cities offer a lively urban experience with museums, nightlife, and shopping.
  • Mississippi River Valley: Take a riverboat tour or drive on the Great River Road to explore charming river towns and unique attractions.
  • Iowa Great Lakes Region: Located just south of Southern Minnesota, the Iowa Great Lakes Region is known for its boating, fishing, and beaches.

Conclusion

Southern Minnesota is a hidden gem with plenty to offer travelers of all types. From its natural beauty and outdoor activities to its rich history and cultural attractions, there’s always something to discover and enjoy. By taking advantage of this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the ultimate itinerary and truly experience all that Southern Minnesota has to offer.
