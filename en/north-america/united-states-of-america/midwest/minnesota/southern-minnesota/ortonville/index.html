Ortonville, Minnesota, is a charming city located in Southern Minnesota. It is one of the hidden gems in the Midwest, blessed with natural beauty, a rich cultural heritage, and a vibrant food scene. Our comprehensive travel guide includes everything you need to know to explore the city like a local, from the must-visit tourist spots to the best biking/hiking trails.

Introduction to Ortonville

Ortonville is a small, friendly and welcoming town. It sits on the border of the Big Stone Lake and the Minnesota-South Dakota state line, boasting some of the most beautiful natural scenery in the state. It offers visitors a unique blend of both natural and cultural attractions, making it an ideal destination for those interested in history, outdoor adventure, and local food.

List of Places to Visit in Ortonville

Here are some of the highlights of what you can see and experience in Ortonville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Big Stone County Museum: A museum that showcases the history and culture of Big Stone County.
  • Ortonville Municipal Airport: A historical airport dating back to the 1930s.
  • Minnesota’s Unusual Natural Wonders: A museum that displays some of the most unique natural wonders in Minnesota.

Natural Attractions of Ortonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Stone Lake and State Park: A beautiful lake, perfect for swimming, fishing, and birdwatching.
  • West Otter Tail County Trail: A scenic bike trail that takes you through beautiful natural scenery.
  • Big Stone National Wildlife Refuge: A pristine wilderness area that is home to a variety of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ortonville

Ortonville offers a range of hiking and biking trails, each providing a unique experience to help you revel in the magnificent landscapes that surround the town. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Big Stone Lake Trail: A 7.5-mile trail that winds around the glacial lake.
  • Sisseton/Wahpeton Dakota Wokiksuye Makoce Trail: A 2.1-mile trail that goes through stunning natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Ortonville: Activities and Tours

If you are interested in exploring the city, Ortonville offers some fantastic things to do, from tours to outdoor activities, including:

  • Ortonville Municipal Golf Course: A popular golf course that offers exceptional greens and stunning views.
  • Big Stone County Art Council: An art museum featuring exhibitions of local and regional artists.
  • Camping: Explore the serenity of the city with your to-go camping gear as you enjoy the stillness offered by beautiful landscapes around Ortonville.

Check out our week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Ortonville:

Day 1: Visit the Big Stone Lake Trail Day 2: Explore the Big Stone County Museum Day 3: Go biking along the West Otter Tail County Trail Day 4: Visit the Ortonville Municipal Airport, Minnesota’s Unusual Natural Wonders and the Big Stone National Wildlife Refuge Day 5: Head to the Ortonville Municipal Golf Course Day 6: Visit the Big Stone County Art Council Day 7: Grab your camping gear and enjoy the serenity of Ortonville’s natural surroundings

Food and Drink Scene of Ortonville

Ortonville offers a range of dining options, from small-town diners to upscale restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to experience in Ortonville:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye: A staple dish of Minnesota, cooked to perfection in Ortonville.
  • Fried Chicken: Tender and juicy fried chicken, typically served with mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Bootlegger’s Supper Club: A traditional supper club in Ortonville, known for its unique selection of beer.

Local Culture and Customs of Ortonville

Ortonville has a rich cultural heritage. It is home to the Big Stone County Museum, which showcases the history and culture of the area. The city is known for its long-standing traditions and customs that attract visitors from all across the Midwest.

Best Time to Visit Ortonville

The best time to visit Ortonville is during the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures are warm, and the days are long. However, the fall season, from September to November, offers a beautiful scenic view of the changing colors of the leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ortonville

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ortonville:

Do:

  • Respect the nature and leave only footprints.
  • Carry your necessary medical kit in case of an emergency in the wildness.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the city’s natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to take care of your food, as uncooked food and drinks might attract the animals in the bushes.

Safety and Health

Ortonville is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates. Visitors should take the necessary precautions while exploring the city’s natural surroundings.

Safety in Ortonville

  • Stick to authorized paths and traiks, and try to avoid going alone without a friend or a partner.
  • Keep an ear out for any alerts, notifications by the park or tourist services about any presents of animals outside of their habitat.

Health in Ortonville

  • Visitors should carry their necessary medical kit with them while traveling in Ortonville.
  • Make sure to apply insect repellent if you’re planning on exploring the forests or natural parks in Ortonville.
