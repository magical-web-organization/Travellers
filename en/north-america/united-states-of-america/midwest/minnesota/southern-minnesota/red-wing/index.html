Introduction to Red Wing

Red Wing is a charming city located in the southern part of Minnesota. With a population of just over 16,000 people, it is a small but bustling city that offers a range of experiences for travelers. Perched on the banks of the Mississippi River, Red Wing is known for its stunning natural beauty, lush green hills, and sparkling waterways. The city has a rich history, and visitors can immerse themselves in the local art and culture scene while enjoying the warm hospitality of its residents.

List of Places to Visit in Red Wing

With its abundance of natural and cultural attractions, Red Wing is a popular destination for travelers. Here are some of the top places to visit in Red Wing.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Red Wing Shoe Company Museum: Discover the history of Red Wing’s famous shoe company and see antique footwear dating back to the 1800s.
  • Goodhue County Historical Society: Take a trip back in time at this museum that showcases the history of Goodhue County, featuring exhibits about Native American culture, early pioneers, and the region’s development over time.
  • Anderson Center at Tower View: Explore this historic estate, which was home to Alexander Pierce Anderson, a well-known book publisher, editor, and writer. The Center now serves as a hub for the arts, hosting artist residencies, exhibitions, and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Red Wing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Barn Bluff: Hike to the top of this bluff to enjoy stunning views of the Mississippi River and the surrounding valleys.
  • Frontenac State Park: This park is known for its scenic beauty and a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and camping.
  • Cannon Valley Trail: This 20-mile trail is perfect for biking and hiking, boasting some of the most beautiful countryside landscapes in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Red Wing

Red Wing has an abundance of hiking and biking trails that showcase the region’s natural beauty. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Barn Bluff Trail: A moderately difficult hike that offers beautiful views of the Mississippi River and the town.
  • Hay Creek Trail: A peaceful trail that passes through Hay Creek Valley, connecting to the Cannon Valley Trail and Hay Creek Recreation Area.
  • Memorial Park Trail: An easy trail that loops around Memorial Park, offering beautiful views of the treetops and the Mississippi River.

Best Things to Do in Red Wing: Activities and Tours

Red Wing offers a range of enjoyable activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Red Wing:

  • Red Wing Trolley Tours: Take a tour of Red Wing’s historic downtown and learn about its rich history.
  • Red Wing Stoneware Factory: Learn how Red Wing Stoneware is made and make your own piece to take home with you.
  • Pottery Place Plus: Shop for unique pottery, art, and other local handmade craft items.

Week-Long Itinerary for Red Wing

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Red Wing, here’s a sample itinerary to get the most out of your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Red Wing and explore the historic downtown area, including shops, restaurants, and museums. Day 2: Visit the Red Wing Shoe Company Museum and Anderson Center at Tower View. Day 3: Enjoy a day at Frontenac State Park, hiking, fishing, and exploring the area’s natural beauty. Day 4: Rent a bike and ride along the Cannon Valley Trail. Stop at the Hay Creek Recreation Area for a picnic lunch. Day 5: Take a trolley tour of Red Wing and visit Pottery Place Plus. Day 6: Spend the day at the Barn Bluff Trail, taking in the stunning views of the Mississippi River and the town. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products, and depart from Red Wing.

Food and Drink Scene of Red Wing

Red Wing has a vibrant food and drink scene, featuring a range of cuisines and establishments. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye: A freshwater fish found in the region, often served in sandwiches or battered and fried.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A creamy soup made with local wild rice and vegetables.
  • Red Wing Bread: A sourdough bread famous in the area, made with locally grown wheat.

Local Beverages

  • Red Wing Brewery: This brewery offers a range of handcrafted beers, brewed using traditional methods and locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Falconer Vineyards and Winery: This winery produces wines made from grapes grown on the estate, offering beautiful scenic views of the vineyards and surrounding hills.

Where to Stay in Red Wing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Red Wing offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Red Wing:

  • St. James Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown, featuring charming rooms and elegant dining options.
  • Best Western Rivertown Hotel and Suites: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and a heated indoor pool.
  • Pratt-Taber Inn: This Victorian-style bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a warm welcome to guests.

What to Buy in Red Wing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Red Wing is known for its local products and handcrafted items. Here are some souvenirs to take home with you:

  • Red Wing Stoneware: Handmade pottery in beautiful designs, made and sold in the area.
  • Red Wing Bread: Take home a loaf or two of this delicious sourdough bread.
  • Wild Rice: A local delicacy that makes a great gift or side dish.

Local Culture and Customs of Red Wing

Red Wing has a warm and welcoming community, with deep roots in local traditions and practices. Visitors may notice the strong sense of community and pride in the area’s history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Red Wing

The best time to visit Red Wing is from late spring through early fall, from May to October. The weather is mild and pleasant during this time, and the area is at its most vibrant and colorful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Red Wing

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Red Wing:

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and historical places to learn about the area’s rich history.
  • Take advantage of the stunning natural beauty by hiking, biking, or fishing.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages, and shop for handcrafted souvenirs and products.

Don’t:

  • Forget to respect local customs and traditions, and be mindful of the environment.
  • Leave valuables in your vehicle while parked.
  • Ignore safety measures while engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Red Wing

Red Wing is generally a safe destination, but it’s always important to take common-sense precautions to avoid theft or other incidents. Ensure you lock your car doors, and don’t leave valuables where they can be seen from the outside.

Health in Red Wing

Red Wing has quality healthcare facilities and emergency services, and visitors will have access to medical care if needed. Still, it’s essential to take precautions to avoid illness or injury while traveling, including staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen, and taking necessary medications.

Getting to Red Wing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Red Wing is located about an hour from the Twin Cities of Minneapolis and St. Paul. Here are some transportation options to get you there.

Airports Near Red Wing

  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport: The closest major airport, located about 60 miles northwest of Red Wing.

Bus Stations Near Red Wing

  • Red Wing Bus Station: Serves local and regional transit routes.
  • Jefferson Lines: Provides daily bus services to and from Red Wing.

Other Options

  • Car: Red Wing is easily accessible by car, with major highways connecting to the city.

How to Get Around in Red Wing

Red Wing is a walkable city and most attractions are within walking distance from each other. However, travelers can also rent bikes, use the local trolley services, or drive their own car.

Where to Go Next from Red Wing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in the area and want to explore more of the Midwest, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Minneapolis/St. Paul: Visit the Twin Cities for excellent dining, shopping, and cultural experiences.
  • Lake Pepin: Take a scenic drive along the lake, and enjoy water activities like boating, kayaking, or windsurfing.
  • Wisconsin Dells: Known as the “Waterpark Capital of the World,” this entertainment destination offers a range of activities, including waterparks, zip-lining, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Red Wing is a beautiful destination, offering something for everyone- from outdoor enthusiasts to history buffs. With a range of activities, attractions, and experiences, it is a perfect place for a day trip or a longer vacation. Plan your visit to Red Wing today and enjoy the charm and magic of this gem of Minnesota!
