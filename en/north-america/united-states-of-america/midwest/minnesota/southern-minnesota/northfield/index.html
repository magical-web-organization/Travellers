Introduction to Northfield

Located in Southern Minnesota, Northfield is a beautiful town that offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and adventure. Surrounded by the rolling hills of the Cannon River Valley, this charming town is home to a thriving arts community and a rich history. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or foodie, Northfield has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Northfield

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northfield is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s rich history and culture. Here are a few must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Northfield Historical Society Museum: Located in downtown Northfield, this museum offers visitors an insight into the town’s history and its famous bank raid of 1876.
  • St. Olaf College: This beautiful campus is home to several museums, including the Flaten Art Museum, which displays works of art from around the world.
  • Carleton College: Founded in 1866, this beautiful campus is home to several museums, including the Gould Library, which houses rare and unique collections.

Natural Attractions of Northfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northfield boasts of several natural attractions, including parks, lakes, and hiking trails. Here are some top natural attractions you wouldn’t want to miss:

  • Downtown Riverwalk: A scenic riverfront path that provides splendid views of the Cannon River.
  • Carleton Arboretum: This 880-acre nature reserve located on the edge of campus features several trails, waterfalls, and beautiful gardens.
  • Nerstrand Big Woods State Park: Located just outside of town, this beautiful park is home to a dense forest of hardwood trees called the Big Woods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northfield

Northfield has several well-maintained trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Cannon Valley Trail: A 19.7-mile trail that provides stunning views of the Cannon River and its surrounding bluffs.
  • Mill Towns Trail: An 18-mile trail that connects Northfield to Faribault and takes riders on a scenic route through the towns’ historic mill districts.
  • River Bend Nature Center Trails: Located just outside of town, this beautiful nature center features several trails for hiking and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Northfield: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a fun day trip or an adventurous night out, Northfield has plenty of things to do for all ages. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Northfield Farmers Market: Held every Saturday from May to October, this market features locally grown produce, baked goods, and handcrafted gifts.
  • Theater and Performing Arts: Both Carleton and St. Olaf host numerous plays, concerts, and performances throughout the year.
  • Wineries and Breweries Tours: Northfield is home to several wineries and breweries that offer tours and tastings, including Loon Juice, Chapel Brewing, and Imminent Brewing.

Food and Drink Scene of Northfield

Northfield has a vibrant food and drink scene, offering everything from farm-to-table restaurants to craft breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Reuben Sandwich at Hogan Brothers: This classic sandwich is made with freshly baked rye bread, tender corned beef, melted Swiss cheese, sauerkraut, and Thousand Island dressing.
  • The Hot Dish at Ole Store Restaurant: A Minnesota staple, this casserole features tater tots, ground beef, cream of mushroom soup, and shredded cheese, topped with a crispy layer of tater tots.
  • The Malt at Jesse James Lanes & Lounge: This classic diner treat is made with vanilla ice cream, malt powder, and milk, blended to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Loon Juice Cider: Made with Minnesota-grown apples and other natural ingredients, this sweet and tangy cider is perfect for any occasion.
  • Imminent Brewing Beer: This small brewery offers a rotating selection of craft beers, with everything from pale ales to stouts.
  • The Contented Cow: A traditional English pub located in downtown Northfield, offering a wide selection of beers and spirits.

Where to Stay in Northfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northfield offers several options for accommodation, ranging from hotels to hostels to guesthouses. Here are some top picks:

  • St. Olaf College Guesthouses: These cozy bed and breakfast-style guesthouses are located on the beautiful St. Olaf campus and offer clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Archer House River Inn: Located in downtown Northfield, this historic hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • Northfield Motel: This family-owned motel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price, just minutes away from downtown Northfield.

What to Buy in Northfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and gifts to bring back from your trip to Northfield? Here are some local products that you wouldn’t want to miss:

  • Northfield Candles: Hand-poured soy candles inspired by the scents of Northfield.
  • Ruthie’s Weaving Studio: Hand-woven items like shawls, scarves, and blankets made with wool and other natural fibers.
  • Tiny Planet Chocolate: Handmade artisan chocolates made with high-quality ingredients, including cocoa beans roasted on-site.

Local Culture and Customs of Northfield

Northfield is a friendly and welcoming town, and its residents take pride in their community. Here are a few local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect the Environment: Northfield’s residents care deeply about the environment, and they expect visitors to do the same. Please use the designated trash cans and recycling bins, and avoid littering.
  • Be Polite and Courteous: Northfield residents are known for their friendliness and politeness. Be sure to say “hello” and “thank you” to the locals you meet during your visit.
  • Support Local Businesses: Northfield’s small businesses are an integral part of the town’s charm and character. When you visit, be sure to support the local shops and restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Northfield

Northfield is a beautiful destination year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here’s a breakdown:

  • Summer (June - August): Ideal for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and water sports. The town also hosts several events and festivals during this time.
  • Fall (September - November): The fall colors add a beautiful hue to the town’s surroundings, making it perfect for nature walks and sightseeing.
  • Winter (December - February): Winter sports enthusiasts will enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing in the nearby parks and resorts.
  • Spring (March - May): The weather starts warming up, making it perfect for outdoor activities like hiking and exploring the town’s historical sites.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northfield

Do:

  • Do your research: Before you arrive, read up on the town’s history, attractions, and culture to make the most of your visit.
  • Do Follow Local Customs: Be respectful of the town’s environment, and be polite and courteous to the locals.
  • Do Try Local Products: From local wine and beer to handmade chocolates and baked goods, Northfield’s food scene is a must-try!

Don’t:

  • Don’t Litter: Northfield’s residents take pride in their town’s beauty. Be respectful of the environment by using the designated trash cans and recycling bins.
  • Don’t Be Late: Punctuality is appreciated in Northfield, so be sure to arrive on time for appointments and meetings.
  • Don’t Be Disruptive: Northfield is a quiet town, so please avoid being loud and disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northfield

Northfield is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions, such as locking their doors and windows, not leaving valuables in their cars, and using common sense when walking alone at night.

Health in Northfield

Northfield has an excellent healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located in the surrounding areas. Visitors should have health insurance and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Northfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northfield

The nearest major airport to Northfield is the Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP), located about 45 miles north of the town. From there, visitors can rent a car, take a shuttle or taxi to Northfield.

Bus Stations Near Northfield

Greyhound operates several bus routes to the nearby town of Faribault, located about 10 miles south of Northfield. Visitors can then take a taxi or shuttle to reach Northfield.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Northfield by car, bike, or on foot, depending on their location.

How to Get Around in Northfield

Northfield is a walkable town, with most attractions located within a short distance of each other. Visitors can also bike, drive, or take a taxi to explore the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Northfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby destinations and excursions from Northfield, including:

  • Minneapolis/St. Paul: Located about 45 miles north of Northfield, the famous twin cities offer visitors a bustling urban experience, with shopping, restaurants, museums, and nightlife.
  • Lake Pepin: Located about 45 miles southeast of Northfield, this stunning lake boasts of picturesque scenery and offers several watersports and outdoor activities.
  • Welch Village Ski Resort: Located about 25 miles east of Northfield, this ski resort offers visitors a chance to hit the slopes during the winter months.

Conclusion

Northfield is a hidden gem in Southern Minnesota, offering visitors a perfect blend of history, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re a foodie, nature lover, or history enthusiast, Northfield has something to offer. Be sure to follow our guide to make the most of your visit!
