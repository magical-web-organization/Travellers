Introduction to Austin

Located in Southern Minnesota, Austin is a charming city that boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a thriving food and drink scene. Visitors can explore a range of attractions, from world-class museums and historic landmarks to scenic hiking trails and outdoor adventures. With its small-town feel and big-city amenities, Austin is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique and memorable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Austin

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, Austin has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • SPAM Museum: This museum is dedicated to the beloved canned meat product and features interactive exhibits, vintage ads, and a gift shop.
  • Mower County Historical Society: History buffs can explore the society’s collection of artifacts and exhibits focusing on the local area.
  • Weyerhaeuser Museum: Visitors can learn about the history of the area and its natural resources, including logging and agriculture.

Natural Attractions of Austin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hormel Nature Center: This nature preserve offers over 500 acres of scenic trails, wildlife viewing, and educational programs.
  • Mill Pond: A peaceful and scenic area on the Cedar River, ideal for fishing or picnicking.
  • Jay C. Hormel Nature Trail: Perfect for hiking and biking, this 17-mile route takes visitors through picturesque scenery including woods and wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Austin

Austin boasts plenty of hiking and biking trails for visitors in search of outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Hormel Nature Center Trail: With over 10 miles of trail, the center offers easy to moderate hiking trails that wind through a variety of ecosystems, including forest, prairie, and wetland.
  • Cedar River Trail: A scenic trail that runs along the Cedar River, featuring bridges, scenic views, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Turtle Creek Parkway Trail: Ideal for bikers, this trail runs along Turtle Creek and offers a flat, smooth surface that is perfect for leisurely rides.

Best Things to Do in Austin: Activities and Tours

From culinary adventures to cultural explorations, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy in Austin. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Austin ArtWorks Center: Visitors can explore various art exhibits, classes, and workshops.
  • Self-Guided Brewery Tours: Austin has a burgeoning local brewery scene, with five craft breweries in town- book a tour and sample a few local beers.
  • Austin Symphony Orchestra: Enjoy an evening of classical music performed by the talented musicians at Austin Symphony Orchestra.

Week-Long Itinerary for Austin

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Austin, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the SPAM museum, explore downtown Austin.
Day 2 Take a hike at the Hormel Nature Center Trail, picnic at Mill Pond
Day 3 Go on a brewery tour, enjoy a pint of local ale or lager.
Day 4 Learn about local history at the Mower County Historical Society. Afterward, stretch your legs out walking on the Cedar River Trail.
Day 5 Experience the local arts scene at the Austin ArtWorks Center.
Day 6 Listen to some classical music at Austin Symphony Orchestra.
Day 7 Do some shopping and buy souvenirs at the local stores and boutiques. Try some local cuisine before you head back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Austin

Austin is renowned for its culinary delights, and visitors can indulge in a range of delicious local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • SPAM Burger: A hamburger topped with a slice of SPAM, a staple of the area.
  • Cornish Pasty: A hearty meat-and-vegetable pie that is a favorite among locals.
  • Lefse: A traditional Norwegian flatbread often served with butter and cinnamon-sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beer: Over the past few years, Austin has developed an exciting local craft beer scene. Check out one of the five local breweries in town.
  • Kombucha: A refreshing and healthy fermented drink that’s popular in Minnesota.
  • Lemonade Days: A popular lemonade festival in Austin, held annually over a three-day summer weekend.

Where to Stay in Austin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Austin boasts a range of affordable and comfortable accommodations, from charming guesthouses to modern hotels. Here are some options to consider:

  • Holiday Inn Austin: This downtown hotel offers comfortable rooms and is conveniently close to many attractions.
  • Comfort Inn: This affordable hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and an indoor pool.
  • Red Cedar Lodge: A charming bed-and-breakfast with cozy rooms, gourmet breakfasts, and scenic views.

What to Buy in Austin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home as a reminder of your trip to Austin? Here are some ideas:

  • SPAM Merchandise: Pick up some SPAM-themed souvenirs at the SPAM Museum gift shop.
  • Local Crafts: Austin ArtWorks Center features a wide selection of locally-made arts and crafts.
  • Local Beers: Bring home a bottle (or a six-pack) of your favorite local brew to savor after your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Austin

Minnesota is known for its friendly and hospitable locals, and Austin is no exception. Visitors can expect warm hospitality and a welcoming local culture. A few customs to keep in mind include:

  • Greetings: A friendly smile or hello is always appreciated.
  • Tipping: It is customary to tip around 15-20% for good service in restaurants.
  • Weather Awareness: The weather in Minnesota can be unpredictable, so take an extra layer of clothing or a raincoat just in case.

Best Time to Visit Austin

The best time to visit Austin is from late spring through autumn, when the weather is pleasant and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Late spring in May and June offers a chance to see the beautiful Lilac Blooms in the area. Winter in Minnesota can be harsh, so it is not recommended to visit Austin during that season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Austin

It’s important to respect the local culture and customs when visiting Austin. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try Local Foods and Beverages: Austin’s food and drink scene is a highlight of the city, take the chance to try some new and delicious food options.
  • Explore the Outdoors: With scenic nature trails and natural attractions, Austin is an ideal place for outdoor adventures.
  • Experience the Local Culture: Check out local arts and cultural events to learn more about the area’s history and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Help keep the area beautiful by disposing of trash properly.
  • Ignore Safety Rules: Follow safety guidelines like wearing a helmet when riding a bike or staying on marked trails when hiking.
  • Disrespect the Environment: Be mindful of the local wildlife and their habitats, and try not to disturb the natural surroundings during your stay.

Safety and Health

Austin is a relatively safe city, but visitors should be aware of some safety and health concerns when planning their trip.

Safety in Austin

  • Walking at Night: It is always recommended to avoid walking alone at night, pedestrian traffic is scarce and not well-lit.
  • Wildlife Encounters: Be observant during nature hikes, especially with snakes and other small animals, don’t confront wild animals if you encounter them.
  • Traffic Safety: Stay alert when driving, there are not many stoplights in Austin, which some visitors may find different than the more densely populated areas.

Health in Austin

  • Ticks and Mosquitoes: Insect repellent is recommended for outdoor activities; ticks and mosquitoes can cause Lyme disease and West Nile virus, respectively.
  • Extreme Temperatures: Minnesota can endure harsh winters and heatwaves during the summer months, take precautions regarding clothing and hydration.

Getting to Austin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Austin is easily accessible from the surrounding areas via car, bus, and air travel. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Air Travel: The nearest airport is Rochester International Airport, which is around 30 miles away from Austin. Visitors can drive or take a bus to Austin.
  • Bus Travel: Greyhound Bus Lines provides access to Austin from nearby cities, with a stop in town.
  • Car Travel: National and regional highways connect with Austin. You can rent a car from Rochester Airport or nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Austin

While having a car is convenient, it is still possible to explore Austin without one. Public transportation options are limited, so some visitors may want to consider renting a car or bike to get around the city.

  • Car Rental: Well-known car rental companies are available in and around Austin, including Enterprise and Hertz.
  • Biking: Austin has several biking trails, and renters can choose from various bike rental places.
  • Taxi: Taxis or ride-hailing services such as Uber and Lyft are available in Austin.

Where to Go Next from Austin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Austin is a great destination on its own, it’s also an excellent jumping-off point for exploring nearby towns and attractions. Here are some options to consider:

  • Rochester, Minnesota: Just half-hour drive from Austin, the city is known for its world-class medical facility and historic architecture, including the famous Mayo Clinic Hospital.
  • Lanesboro, Minnesota: A charming small town that features art galleries, live theater, and outdoor activities such as fishing, canoeing, and kayaking.
  • Mississippi River: About an hour and a half drive, exploring the headwaters of the mighty Mississippi River is an adventure in itself.

Conclusion

From its rich cultural history and scenic natural beauty to its exciting food and drink scene and charming small-town feel, Austin, Minnesota, offers something for everyone. Visitors can explore a range of attractions, indulge in local cuisine and beverages, and enjoy outdoor adventures. So, pack your bags and prepare yourself for an unforgettable adventure in Austin!
