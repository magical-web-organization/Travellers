Introduction to Rochester

Located in Southern Minnesota, Rochester is a charming city with a population of approximately 119,000. Known as the “Med City,” Rochester is famous for its highly renowned healthcare facilities that attract numerous patients from around the globe. However, there’s a lot more to this city than just medical tourism. Rochester has a rich history, scenic natural beauty, a fantastic food and drink scene, and plenty of outdoor adventures that make it an ideal vacation spot.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve curated a list of the top things to do in Rochester, including the best places to visit, the food and drink scene, and cultural and safety tips. Whether you’re here for a medical visit, a business trip, or a leisurely vacation, our guide will help you plan your trip effortlessly.

List of Places to Visit in Rochester

Rochester has numerous attractions that cater to different interests and age groups. Here is a list of the best places to visit in the “Med City”:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rochester has several historical and cultural spots that attract tourists and residents alike. Here are a few places that you shouldn’t miss:

Mayowood Mansion

Built-in 1911, the Mayowood Mansion was home to the famous Mayo family and is now a museum. The mansion reflects the old-world charm of Rochester and is a must-visit for history buffs. The mansion has beautiful gardens and a carriage house that has now been converted into a gift shop.

History Center of Olmsted County

The History Center of Olmsted County showcases the region’s history through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays. Here you’ll find information on the region’s Native American history, Olmsted County’s community history, and Rochester’s innovations in medicine.

Assisi Heights Spirituality Center

The Assisi Heights Spirituality Center is a stunning architectural marvel that reflects the style of Italy’s Assisi region. The center offers visitors various programs and retreats focused on experiencing spiritual and religious traditions.

Natural Attractions of Rochester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rochester’s natural charm will surprise you. There are plenty of opportunities to explore outdoor activities or capture scenic views. Here are some must-visit natural attractions.

Silver Lake Park

Silver Lake Park is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. This park offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and hiking. The park features a picnic area, a playground, a beach, and plenty of opportunities to relax and enjoy nature.

Quarry Hill Nature Center

Quarry Hill Nature Center is an excellent place for nature lovers of all ages. The center has hiking, biking, and skiing trails that allow visitors to explore the park and enjoy stunning views. The park has a limestone quarry, caves, a pond, and a visitors’ center with educational exhibits and kids’ programs.

Archie and Gwendolyn Wai Mizner Park

This park has a beautiful Japanese garden with koi ponds, a nature trail, a bamboo garden, a tea garden, and a waterfall. It’s an excellent place to relax, meditate, and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rochester

Rochester has numerous hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the city and its surroundings while staying fit. Here are some popular routes.

Douglas State Trail

This trail extends through the scenic rolling hills of southeastern Minnesota. This former rail line provides fascinating views of farmlands, meadows, and forests.

Root River State Trail

The Root River State Trail covers 42 miles of the scenic landscape, including limestone bluffs, scenic valleys, and picturesque streams. It’s an excellent option for cycling enthusiasts and is suitable for all skill levels.

Zumbro River Trail

The Zumbro River Trail is a beautiful trail that spans across 27.5 miles of the picturesque landscape. Visitors can explore the natural splendor of the area, including the Zumbro River and its wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Rochester: Activities and Tours

Rochester offers several activities and tours that cater to different interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Rochester:

Rochester Trolley & Tour Company

The Rochester Trolley & Tour Company offers a narrated tour of the city and its notable attractions. The trolley is air-conditioned, and the tour lasts for approximately 90 minutes.

Rochester Art Center

The Rochester Art Center is an impressive art gallery that showcases a wide range of contemporary art exhibitions and educational programming for all ages.

Grand Rounds Scenic Byway

The Grand Rounds Scenic Byway is a scenic drive that takes visitors through Rochester’s most beautiful parks. The route is approximately 27 miles long and includes several parks, including Cascade Lake Park, Silver Lake Park, and Quarry Hill Nature Center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rochester

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Rochester, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your stay:

Day 1: Visit Mayo Clinic Campus and learn about the facility’s history.

Day 2: Explore the Silver Lake Park and the History Center of Olmsted County.

Day 3: Relax at the Assisi Heights Spirituality Center and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Day 4: Visit the Mayowood Mansion for a glimpse into Rochester’s history.

Day 5: Spend the day exploring the Quarry Hill Nature Center.

Day 6: Take a trolley tour of the city and visit the Rochester Art Center.

Day 7: Drive the Grand Rounds Scenic Byway and soak in the stunning natural beauty of Rochester.

Food and Drink Scene of Rochester

Rochester’s food culture is a blend of American and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that will tantalize your taste buds:

Must-Try Dishes

Juicy Lucys at The Loop

The Juicy Lucys at The Loop is a must-try dish in Rochester. This burger is stuffed with molten cheese inside the patty and is grilled to perfection. It’s served with French fries and coleslaw.

Jambalaya at Terza Ristorante

Terza Ristorante serves one of the best jambalayas in Rochester. The dish features an exciting blend of chicken, sausage, shrimp, and vegetables in spicy tomato sauce.

Scandinavian Meatballs at Canadian Honker

The Scandinavian Meatballs at Canadian Honker are a popular dish with visitors. The meatballs are made from pork and beef, served with mashed potatoes, lingonberry sauce, and cucumber salad.

Local Beverages

Beer at Forager Brewing Company

Forager Brewing Company is a popular brewery that serves craft beer made from local ingredients. It’s an excellent place to relax and sample some of the best beer in Rochester.

Wine at Post Town Winery

Post Town Winery is a family-owned winery that serves several varieties of wine made from locally and regionally grown grapes. It’s an excellent place to relax and enjoy some excellent wine.

Where to Stay in Rochester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rochester has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and needs. Here are some top picks:

Hilton Rochester Mayo Clinic Area

The Hilton Rochester Mayo Clinic Area is a comfortable hotel that’s located within walking distance of Mayo Clinic. The hotel is modern, well-maintained, and offers excellent service.

Kahler Inn & Suites

Kahler Inn & Suites is a budget hotel located in downtown Rochester. The hotel rooms are clean and comfortable, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

The Firehouse Hostel

The Firehouse Hostel is an excellent option for budget travelers. The hostel offers comfortable and affordable accommodation, and the location is convenient for exploring the city.

What to Buy in Rochester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rochester has several unique products that make excellent souvenirs. Here are some popular options:

Mayo Clinic Merchandise

The Mayo Clinic has its merchandise store, where visitors can purchase apparel, accessories, books, and more.

Olmsted County Fair

The Olmsted County Fair is an annual event that takes place in July. Here visitors can purchase locally made crafts, food items, and souvenirs.

Rochester Farmers’ Market

The Rochester Farmers’ Market is held on Saturdays from May until October. Here visitors can purchase fresh produce, baked goods, and local artisanal products.

Local Culture and Customs of Rochester

Rochester is a friendly and welcoming community. Here are a few cultural customs that you should keep in mind:

  • Visitors must follow the laws about smoking cigarettes, using e-cigarettes, and consuming alcohol in public spaces.
  • Rochester has a significant proportion of Muslim residents, so visitors should be mindful of their cultural and religious customs.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants, cafes, and bars in Rochester.

Best Time to Visit Rochester

The best time to visit Rochester is during the summers, between June and August. The temperature is warm and sunny, with an average temperature of 78°F. The summer months are also an excellent time to attend the Olmsted County Fair and explore the Grand Rounds Scenic Byway.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rochester

Here are some essential cultural and safety tips that visitors should keep in mind while exploring Rochester:

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the natural beauty and outdoor adventures that Rochester has to offer.
  • Do adhere to the local laws and customs.
  • Do ask for help if you need it.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in public spaces.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and insect repellent while exploring the natural sites.

Safety and Health

Rochester is generally a safe city. However, visitors should be aware of the following:

Safety in Rochester

  • Take precautions while walking or biking on the streets at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of pickpocketing.
  • Be mindful of traffic rules while driving.

Health in Rochester

  • Visitors are encouraged to have health insurance while visiting Rochester.
  • Always carry a mask, sanitizers, and follow the recommended Covid-19 safety guidelines.

Getting to Rochester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rochester

Rochester International Airport (RST) is the primary airport that serves Rochester. It’s located approximately four miles south of the city. The airport is served by several airlines and has daily direct flights from several destinations in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Rochester

Rochester Transit Authority and Jefferson Lines are the two major bus service providers in Rochester. They offer daily bus services to other major cities in Minnesota and other neighboring states.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi service to explore the city and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Rochester

Rochester is relatively easy to navigate, and there are several transportation options for visitors, including taxis, public transportation, and car rentals.

Public Transportation

Rochester Transit Authority is the primary public transportation service provider in the city. They operate a fleet of buses that run on several routes throughout the city.

Taxis

Several taxi companies operate in Rochester and offer 24/7 services to visitors and residents.

Car Rental

Visitors can also rent a car from several car rental companies in Rochester.
