Introduction to Faribault

Located in southern Minnesota, Faribault is a charming city known for its beautiful parks, historic buildings, and rich cultural heritage. With a population of just over 23,000, it’s big enough to have plenty of amenities yet small enough to feel like a close-knit community. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s many museums and historical sites or simply enjoying the great outdoors, Faribault offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Faribault

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Faribault Heritage Center and Museum: Celebrate Faribault’s rich cultural history at this informative museum that features exhibits on everything from local architecture to Civil War history.
  • The Paradise Center for the Arts: This historic arts center hosts a wide variety of concerts, art exhibitions, and theater productions throughout the year.
  • The Rice County Historical Society Museum: Located in the heart of Faribault’s historic district, this fascinating museum showcases the city’s past through interactive exhibits, educational programs, and more.

Natural Attractions of Faribault: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • River Bend Nature Center: This expansive nature center features miles of scenic hiking trails, a butterfly garden, and educational exhibits on Minnesota’s native wildlife.
  • Nerstrand-Big Woods State Park: Hike through towering hardwood trees, fish in the clear streams, and experience the beauty of the Minnesota prairie at this stunning state park.
  • Shattuck-St. Mary’s Campus: The beautiful campus of this renowned private school is open to the public and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and enjoying the outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Faribault

  • Legacy Loop Trail: This 10-mile trail winds through the heart of Faribault and offers spectacular views of the city’s historic architecture and natural beauty.
  • Straight River Trail: Follow the banks of the picturesque Straight River on this easy-to-navigate trail that stretches for more than 12 miles.
  • Cannon River Wilderness Area Trail: Take a leisurely stroll through some of Minnesota’s most pristine wilderness on this stunning trail that offers breathtaking views of the Cannon River.

Best Things to Do in Faribault: Activities and Tours

  • Faribault Farmers Market: Shop for fresh produce, artisanal crafts, and more at this bustling weekly market that takes place in Faribault’s downtown area.
  • Segway Tours of Faribault: Explore the city’s historic sites and scenic countryside on this fun and informative tour that’s perfect for all ages.
  • F-Town Brewing Company: Sample craft beers made with locally sourced ingredients at this beloved Faribault brewery that’s been producing some of Minnesota’s most delicious beers since 2017.

Food and Drink Scene of Faribault

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ Chicken Pizza from Dave’s Downtown: This local favorite features a crispy, chewy crust topped with tangy BBQ sauce, juicy chicken, and plenty of melty cheese.
  • Wild Rice Soup from The Cheese Cave: Perfect for a chilly day, this rich and creamy soup is made with locally grown wild rice and plenty of herbs and spices.
  • Key Lime Bars from Bluebird Cakery: These tangy bars are the perfect balance of creamy and zippy, and are the perfect dessert after any Faribault meal.

Local Beverages

  • F-Town Brewing Company: This beloved brewery produces a wide variety of delicious beers, from hoppy IPAs to smooth and creamy stouts.
  • 10,000 Drops Craft Distillers: Sample locally made spirits, including whiskeys, gins, and vodkas, at this family-owned and operated distillery.
  • Bluebird Cakery: In addition to their delicious desserts, this eclectic bakery serves up an array of coffee drinks, teas, and other beverages perfect for a quick pick-me-up.

Where to Stay in Faribault: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Shattuck-St. Mary’s: Located on the historic Shattuck campus, this elegant inn offers comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities, including an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • AmericInn by Wyndham Faribault: Conveniently located just off the highway, this affordable hotel features spacious rooms and plenty of modern amenities.
  • The Vintage Inn: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a peaceful atmosphere, making it the perfect place to relax and unwind after a day of sightseeing.

What to Buy in Faribault: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Faribault Woolen Mill: Pick up a beautifully crafted wool blanket, scarf, or other item from this historic mill that’s been producing high-quality textiles since 1865.
  • The Cheese Cave: Take home some artisanal cheeses made with locally sourced ingredients, including the famous Cave-Aged cheddar.
  • The Paradise Center for the Arts Gift Shop: Browse the selection of unique jewelry, pottery, and other handmade items produced by local artists at this charming gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Faribault

Faribault is known for its rich cultural heritage, from its historic downtown buildings to its many museums and galleries. The city is steeped in history, and locals take great pride in preserving its unique character and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Faribault

The best time to visit Faribault is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities are plentiful. However, the city is a great destination year-round, with plenty of indoor attractions and cozy cafes to enjoy during the colder months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Faribault

Do:

  • Explore the city’s many historic sites and museums.
  • Spend time enjoying the great outdoors in one of Faribault’s many scenic parks or nature reserves.
  • Try some of the local craft beers or spirits produced by Faribault’s many local breweries and distilleries.

Don’t:

  • Ignore Faribault’s rich cultural heritage – take the time to learn about the city’s fascinating history and traditions.
  • Forget to pack appropriate gear for outdoor activities, including sunscreen, bug spray, and sturdy footwear.
  • Leave without sampling some of the delicious local cuisine, including wild rice soup, BBQ chicken pizza, and key lime bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Faribault

Faribault is a safe city with relatively low crime rates. However, visitors should still take basic precautions such as locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Faribault

There are several hospitals and medical clinics in and around Faribault, including the local hospital, which offers a wide variety of medical services.

Getting to Faribault: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Faribault

  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport: Located just over an hour’s drive from Faribault, this major international airport offers flights to cities all over the world.
  • Rochester International Airport: Located about an hour and a half away, the Rochester airport offers flights to several major Midwest destinations.

Bus Stations Near Faribault

  • Jefferson Lines: This bus company operates a stop in Faribault, providing connections to several cities throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: There are several rental car companies located in Faribault, making it easy to get around the city and explore the surrounding area on your own.
  • Taxi Services: Several taxi companies operate in Faribault and can provide reliable transportation for local trips.

How to Get Around in Faribault

The best way to get around Faribault is by car, as many of the city’s main attractions are spread out and public transportation is limited. However, there are several bike paths and pedestrian-friendly areas in the downtown area that are perfect for exploring on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Faribault: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Minneapolis/St. Paul: Located just over an hour’s drive from Faribault, the Twin Cities offer endless opportunities for shopping, dining, and entertainment.
  • Rochester: This vibrant city is home to the world-renowned Mayo Clinic, as well as several museums, galleries, and cultural attractions.
  • Northfield: Just 20 minutes from Faribault, Northfield is a charming college town with beautiful parks, historic buildings, and plenty of local charm.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring Faribault’s rich cultural heritage, experiencing the great outdoors, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, this charming city has something for everyone. From its scenic hiking trails and picturesque parks to its many museums, galleries, and historical sites, Faribault is a destination that’s sure to leave a lasting impression.
