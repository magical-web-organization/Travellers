Introduction to Owatonna

Nestled in Southern Minnesota, Owatonna boasts a unique blend of rich history and modern culture. Owatonna is a charming small town that offers an authentic Minnesota experience. From its stunning natural beauty to its vibrant arts scene, there is something for everyone in Owatonna. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, or looking for a relaxing weekend getaway, Owatonna will not disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Owatonna

Are you planning a trip to Owatonna and wondering where to go? Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Owatonna:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Downtown Owatonna offers an array of shops and restaurants in a picturesque setting.
  • Owatonna State Public School Museum showcases the history of the former state-run school for orphans, juvenile delinquents, and children with disabilities.
  • Minnesota State School Orphanage Museum provides a glimpse into the life of orphans in the late 1800s to the mid-1900s.
  • National Farmer’s Bank is an architectural marvel, designed by Louis Sullivan, that showcases a rich history from the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Owatonna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kaplan’s Woods Parkway offers over 6 miles of trails, perfect for hiking, biking, and snowshoeing.
  • Morehouse Park is home to a serene lake, playgrounds, and a public swimming pool.
  • Rice Lake State Park is a beautiful park that offers camping, hiking, and fishing opportunities.
  • River Springs Water Park is a family-friendly attraction with a large pool, water slides, and other features.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Owatonna

Owatonna offers beautiful hiking and biking routes. Kaplan’s Woods Parkway has several trails that take you on an adventure through the woods. You can bike on the paved trail, which is over 4 miles long and is perfect for cyclists of all skill levels. If you are looking for a challenge, you can hike on the unpaved trail, which offers a more rugged and natural route.

Best Things to Do in Owatonna: Activities and Tours

Owatonna is home to several unique and exciting activities for all ages. You can take a guided tour of the Owatonna State Public School Museum or the Minnesota State School Orphanage Museum to learn about the rich history of Owatonna. Kids will love the River Springs Water Park, which features water slides, a lazy river, and other fun water features. For an unforgettable experience, you can go on a hot air balloon ride with Soaring Sports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Owatonna

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Owatonna, here is an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Owatonna State Public School Museum and explore the shops and restaurants in Historic Downtown Owatonna.

Day 2: Spend the day at River Springs Water Park.

Day 3: Take a hike or bike ride in Kaplan’s Woods Parkway and then enjoy a relaxing picnic lunch at Morehouse Park.

Day 4: Visit the Minnesota State School Orphanage Museum and then take a hot air balloon ride with Soaring Sports.

Day 5: Go fishing or camping at Rice Lake State Park.

Day 6: Visit the National Farmer’s Bank and then explore the local arts scene at the Owatonna Arts Center.

Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Owatonna

Minnesota is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, and Owatonna has some excellent options for foodies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juicy Lucy – A cheese-filled burger patty that is grilled to perfection.
  • Tater Tot Hotdish – A classic Minnesota casserole made with tater tots, ground beef, and cream of mushroom soup.
  • Walleye – A freshwater fish that is a delicacy in Minnesota.

Local Beverages

  • Minnesota craft beer – Minnesota has a thriving craft beer scene, and Owatonna has several breweries worth checking out, such as Man Cave Brewery and Mineral Springs Brewery.
  • Fruit Wines – Minnesota is also known for its fruit wines, and Owatonna is home to a few vineyards that offer wine tastings.

Where to Stay in Owatonna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Owatonna has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Some popular options include:

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, Owatonna, MN – A cozy and comfortable hotel that offers a complimentary breakfast.
  • Village of Yesteryear – A unique experience where you can stay in a historic home that has been restored to its original beauty.
  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Owatonna – A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Owatonna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some excellent options:

  • Gabriel Kjelshus Honey – Owatonna is known for its honey, and Gabriel Kjelshus Honey is a local favorite.
  • Minnesota-shaped Cutting Board – A unique and practical souvenir that is sure to remind you of your trip to Minnesota.
  • Owatonna Gift Cards – Owatonna has a range of gift shops and restaurants that offer gift cards perfect for gift-giving.

Local Culture and Customs of Owatonna

Minnesotans are known for their friendliness and “Minnesota Nice” attitude. Owatonna is no different, and visitors are sure to feel welcomed and appreciated. In Owatonna, it is customary to say “You betcha” or “Don’tcha know” at the end of sentences.

Best Time to Visit Owatonna

Owatonna is a great place to visit all year round. The summer months (June-August) are perfect for outdoor activities such as swimming, hiking, and biking. Fall (September-November) is ideal for those seeking picturesque autumn foliage. Winter (December-February) offers opportunities for ice-skating, snowshoeing, and skiing. Spring (March-May) is a great time to witness the blooming natural beauty of Owatonna.

Do’s and Don’ts of Owatonna

Do:

  • Experience the local culture and try Minnesota cuisine
  • Visit the historical sites of Owatonna
  • Visit local breweries and vineyards

Don’t:

  • Ignore Owatonna’s rich history
  • Leave without exploring the natural beauty of Owatonna

Safety and Health

Owatonna is generally considered safe, however, as with any travel destination, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. For health concerns, there is a hospital in Owatonna that can provide medical assistance if needed.

Getting to Owatonna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Owatonna is easily accessible by car or public transit. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Owatonna

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) – This is the nearest major airport and is located about 60 miles north of Owatonna.

Bus Stations Near Owatonna

  • Greyhound – Greyhound has a bus stop in Owatonna.
  • Jefferson Lines – Jefferson Lines has a bus stop in Owatonna.

Other Options

  • Driving – Owatonna is easily accessible by car and is located near Interstates 35 and 90.
  • Rideshare Services – Rideshare companies such as Uber and Lyft are available in Owatonna.

How to Get Around in Owatonna

Owatonna is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. If you prefer to bike, the city offers several bike-friendly routes and trails. Owatonna also has a public transit system that provides bus service throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Owatonna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Owatonna is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Some popular options include:

  • Rochester, MN – Home to the world-renowned Mayo Clinic and other attractions such as the Plummer Building and Silver Lake Park.
  • Minneapolis, MN – Minnesota’s largest city, Minneapolis is known for its vibrant arts scene, world-class museums, and delicious cuisine.
  • Winona, MN – A picturesque town that offers stunning views of the Mississippi River and some unique attractions such as the Minnesota Marine Art Museum.

Conclusion

Owatonna is a hidden gem in Minnesota that has something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or relaxing downtime, Owatonna is sure to deliver. With its rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and friendly locals, Owatonna is a must-visit destination in Minnesota.
