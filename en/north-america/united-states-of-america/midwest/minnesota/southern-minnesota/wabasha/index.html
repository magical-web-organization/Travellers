Introduction to Wabasha

Located in Southern Minnesota, Wabasha is a charming city known for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Home to the famous National Eagle Center, Wabasha attracts bird watchers and nature enthusiasts throughout the year. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an action-packed adventure, Wabasha has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Wabasha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Eagle Center: This popular attraction offers visitors a chance to get up close and personal with eagles, learn about their habitat, and see live eagle displays.
  • Wabasha County Historical Society Museum: Explore the history of Wabasha County through exhibits on farming, logging, and more.
  • LARK Toys: Find an impressive collection of toys for all ages, handmade wooden toys, and mini golf.

Natural Attractions of Wabasha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Great River Bluffs State Park: Hike the scenic trails through hardwood forests and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Mississippi River Valley.
  • Zumbro River: Visitors can paddle down the Zumbro River and enjoy the beautiful views of the riverbanks.
  • Coffee Mill Ski Area: Enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and tubing in the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wabasha

Wabasha offers several options for hiking and biking to take in the natural beauty of the area. Here are some popular routes:

  • Chippewa River State Trail: A beautiful trail extending 30 miles from Durand to Eau Claire, this trail is perfect for hiking and biking.
  • Great River Ridge State Trail: This 13-mile trail goes from the Mississippi River to the St. Croix River and offers stunning views.
  • Root River State Trail: This 60-mile trail runs through several charming towns and offers scenic views of the Root River.

Best Things to Do in Wabasha: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re with friends, family, or solo, Wabasha offers a variety of activities and tours that are sure to make your trip memorable.

  • Eagle Watching Cruises: Get up close and personal with the national symbol by taking an eagle-watching tour.
  • Wabasha-Kellogg Fishing Contest: Enjoy a relaxing day by the river that includes fishing games, casting contests, and big prizes.
  • Wabasha Street Dance: Dance the night away with the locals during the popular Wabasha Street Dance.

Food and Drink Scene of Wabasha

Must-Try Dishes

If you visit Wabasha, you can’t miss out on these local dishes:

  • Walleye: A fish found mostly in the state of Minnesota and a must-try.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A creamy soup made with wild rice that is a local favorite.
  • Pasties: A pastry filled with meat or vegetables, this dish is popular around the copper mining regions of Michigan.

Local Beverages

  • Wabasha Brewing Company: Enjoy some homegrown craft beer at the Wabasha Brewing Company.
  • Old Main Village Winery: Sip on a wide range of wines made from grapes grown in nearby vineyards.

Where to Stay in Wabasha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wabasha offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to both budget and luxury travelers. Here are some top picks:

  • AmericInn by Wyndham Wabasha: A clean and cozy hotel with comfortable rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a Jacuzzi.
  • Turning Waters Bed, Breakfast and Brewery: A unique bed and breakfast with a brewery, homemade breakfast, and well-maintained guestrooms.

What to Buy in Wabasha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Wooden Toys: Purchase high-quality and handmade wooden toys from LARK Toys.
  • Eagle-themed Merchandise: Take home some eagle-themed souvenirs such as t-shirts, hats, and mugs from the National Eagle Center.
  • Craft Beer: Bring home some local craft beer from Wabasha Brewing Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Wabasha

Wabasha is known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and eagles. It is also famous for its walleye fishing. Respect the local customs and laws while enjoying all that Wabasha has to offer.

Best Time to Visit Wabasha

The best time to visit Wabasha is during the spring, summer, and fall when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The winter months offer skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wabasha

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty and wildlife of Wabasha.
  • Take some time to explore the local culture and history.
  • Try out the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or natural areas.
  • Disturb the eagles or their habitat.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when playing extreme sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wabasha

Wabasha is a safe town, but it is essential to take reasonable precautions while exploring the area. Always keep your valuable belongings close and secure.

Health in Wabasha

Wabasha has good medical facilities and hospitals. In case of an emergency, call 911.

Getting to Wabasha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wabasha

The closest airport to Wabasha is Rochester International Airport (43.7 miles away). From there, you can rent a car or hire a taxi to get to your destination.

Bus Stations Near Wabasha

Greyhound Bus Station and Jefferson Lines Bus Station both operate in nearby cities and connect to Wabasha.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient option to reach Wabasha. You can also opt for a rental car or use a ride-sharing service.

How to Get Around in Wabasha

Most of the attractions and activities in Wabasha are in close proximity, so walking is a convenient option. You can also rent a bike to explore the area or drive your car.

Where to Go Next from Wabasha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Winona: Known for its beautiful views of the Mississippi River bluffs and home to the Minnesota Marine Art Museum.
  • Lake City: A popular destination for its water activities like boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Red Wing: Famous for its pottery, hiking trails, and skiing opportunities.

Conclusion

Wabasha is a beautiful city with natural landscapes, cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor activities. Plan your trip to this charming destination and create unforgettable memories. Just remember to respect the local culture and customs and follow safety guidelines while exploring the area.
