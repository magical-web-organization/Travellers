Introduction to Bemidji

Bemidji is a charming city located in Northwestern Minnesota and is known for its natural beauty, popular outdoor recreational activities, and rich cultural heritage. With its picturesque lakes, historic landmarks, and vibrant arts scene, it is no surprise that Bemidji has become the go-to destination for adventurers, history buffs, and culture enthusiasts alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Bemidji, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, the top things to do, and the local culture of this delightful city in the Midwest.

List of Places to Visit in Bemidji

Bemidji has an array of natural and cultural attractions that hold endless opportunities for exploration. Below is a list of the most popular places to visit in Bemidji.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Headwaters Science Center: This interactive museum is perfect for families with young children. The exhibits provide hands-on learning experiences on various sciences, engineering, and technology.

  • Beltrami County Historical Society: Exhibits at the Historical Society include antiques, photographs, artifacts, documents, and other collectibles that show the local history of Northern Minnesota.

  • Lake Bemidji State Park: A popular State Park destination, this park has a vast campground, great trails to hike and bike, and stunning lakeside views.

  • Paul Bunyan and Babe the Blue Ox Statue: A must-visit attraction, featuring the famous lumberjack and his ox.

Natural Attractions of Bemidji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chippewa National Forest: A vast forested paradise of 1.6 million acres that spans across the entire country.

  • Itasca State Park: The park is known for its northern red oak, eastern white pine, and big-tooth aspen, and of course, for the Mississippi River source that starts here.

  • Bemidji Sculpture Walk: This famous sculpture walk features 20 rocks, and gardens to wander, and various unique sculptures located throughout the city representing various famous personas.

  • Lake Bemidji: The lake is famous for water activities like boating, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bemidji

Bemidji offers an extensive network of bike trails, bike-friendly roads, and hiking through a large national forest. Here are a few of the best hiking/biking routes in Bemidji:

  • Paul Bunyan Trail: A 118-mile trail stretching across several counties that provides an opportunity to pedal through some of the majestic woods.

  • Lake Bemidji Loop Trail: An eight-mile paved trail that surrounds Lake Bemidji, providing scenic views and access to beaches, picnic areas, and camping grounds.

Best Things to Do in Bemidji: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Bemidji is known for its water sports facilities such as boating, kayaking, and swimming, which can be done at various natural bodies like the scenic Lake Bemidji. Bemidji State Park miles of gorgeous sand beaches perfect for swimming or sunbathing.

  • Winter Sports: Bemidji boasts of a wide range of winter sports, such as snowshoeing, snowmobiling, ice fishing, and cross-country skiing.

  • Live Performance “Common Grounds”: Bemidji’s famous stage for live performances and music events.

  • Public Art/ Sculpture Tour: Bemidji has a unique collection of Public Arts to explore throughout the city. Take one of the art galleries or studios tours to learn more about their work.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bemidji

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Bemidji:

  • Day 1-2: Explore downtown Bemidji for its Public Arts and the Paul Bunyan statue. Head out for a walk on Lake Bemidji Loop Trail, or take a scenic drive through Chippewa National Forest.
  • Day 3: Visit Headwaters Science Museum and head to the Bemidji Woolen Mills afterward to shop.
  • Day 4: Visit Itasca State Park for hiking and explore the Mississippi River’s source.
  • Day 5: Take an adventure in the winter and snowmobiling in the snow, or off-roading ATV in the forests.
  • Day 6-7: Visit a casino or just relax at the lake enjoying water sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Bemidji

Bemidji, despite its small size, has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages for food enthusiasts:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild Rice Soup: a soup made up of Wild Rice, which is a staple of the northern Minnesota’s cuisine.
  • Walleye: a fish that is a staple of Central Minnesota cuisine and a staple of Bemidji culture.
  • Fry Bread: A traditional Native American dish, made with flour and water, this is a treat to your taste buds.

Local Beverages

  • Bemidji Brewing: The Brewery features unique handmade beers brewed with malted barley, hops, and wheat. They even have seasonal brews according to the season.

  • Lake Bemidji Brewing: The Brewery is a specialist in crafting high-quality and creative craft beers. Taste one of their high-end beer flavors and satisfy your taste buds.

Where to Stay in Bemidji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn & Suites BemidjiA 3-star hotel with a friendly atmosphere, children’s activities, and scenic surroundings.

  • Candlewood Suites Bemidji: An affordable and pet-friendly hotel with an indoor pool and hot tub facility.

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: A three-star hotel located next to Lake Bemidji, boasting of a relaxed and comfortable atmosphere.

What to Buy in Bemidji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bemidji is famous for its unique takeaways, including handcrafted souvenirs, outdoor gear, and local specialties like wild rice, huckleberries, handmade rugs, and pottery. Locally made crafts and souvenirs are perfect for bringing home to remind you of your time in Bemidji.

Local Culture and Customs of Bemidji

Bemidji’s local culture is largely influenced by its Native American heritage, Scandinavian roots, and lumberjack traditions. Locals take pride in their heritage and pay homage to it through various celebrations and festivals. Occasionally, musical events and cultural performances occur in the city to showcase the city’s rich arts scene.

Best Time to Visit Bemidji

The best time to visit Bemidji is during the summer season from June to August when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery of the city can be explored. Winter also offers a unique experience around the snow sports and other winter activities. However, before planning your trip, be sure to check the weather forecast for the dates you wish to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bemidji

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take part in community events and festivals, such as the Folk Festival and Dragon Boat Festival.

  • Take up the opportunity to get to know the locals and seek out their stories.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to ask for permission before taking anyone’s photo or capturing a video.

  • Don’t litter or pollute the natural surroundings and landscapes.

  • Don’t disregard wildlife and natural habitats.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bemidji

Overall, Bemidji is considered a safe destination, and most visits are trouble-free. However, like anywhere else, visitors should always take necessary precautions like knowing the area around you at night, avoid wandering into improper areas.

Health in Bemidji

Make sure that you are updated with your vaccinations before visiting Bemidji, particularly if you plan on indulging in water activities. It’s always best to pack a first aid kit and bring all your essential medicines with you for an enjoyable trip.

Where to Go Next from Bemidji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Bemidji is a charming city with many attractions and activities to offer. From outdoor recreational opportunities to historical landmarks and cultural institutions, visitors to Bemidji are sure to find something to suit their interests.
