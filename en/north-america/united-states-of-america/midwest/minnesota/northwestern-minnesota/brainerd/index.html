Brainerd is a charming getaway destination tucked away in the northwestern corner of Minnesota. Surrounded by breathtaking scenery, this small town is a hub of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and local cuisine that you’ll love. Whether you’re seeking a solo adventure or a family vacation, Brainerd has something for every traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll show you the top places to go, things to do, and local tips for making unforgettable memories in Brainerd.

Introduction to Brainerd

Brainerd is a small but vibrant city in Crow Wing County, Minnesota. It’s known for its picturesque lakes, lush vegetation, and the Paul Bunyan Trail, which is great for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The town is named after Lawrence Brainerd, a businessman who played a significant role in establishing railroads in the area. Today, Brainerd is popular with outdoor adventurers, families, and history buffs who relish the chance to explore the town’s rich past.

List of Places to Visit in Brainerd

Brainerd is full of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and hidden gems. Here is a complete list of places you should visit during your stay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brainerd is rich in history, and several landmarks pay homage to its heritage. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • The Crow Wing County Historical Society and Museum: This museum is an excellent source of information about the local people, their way of life, and their struggles. The outdoor exhibits and original artifacts will take you on a journey back in time.

  • The Northern Pacific Center: Originally constructed in the early 1900s, the Northern Pacific Center played an essential role in the region’s railroad and industrial activities. It’s now home to many businesses and is open to the public for a feel of the local industry.

  • The Paul Bunyan Statue: A visit to Brainerd is never complete without seeing the world-famous statue of Paul Bunyan and Babe, his Blue Ox. The statue is located in a picturesque park, which is perfect for a picnic.

Natural Attractions of Brainerd: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brainerd is an excellent place for adventure seekers. Here are the top attractions:

  • The Paul Bunyan Trail: This scenic trail is ideal for biking, hiking, or a leisurely stroll. It stretches over 100 miles, from Brainerd to Bemidji, and offers plenty of beautiful views of the countryside.

  • The Gull Lake Recreation Area: This lake is perfect for water sports like fishing, swimming, and boating. You can rent a boat and indulge in water sports or relax on the beach and enjoy the stunning view.

  • The Northland Arboretum: Spread over 500 acres, this nature reserve offers lush gardens, hiking trails, and a bird sanctuary. You can spot a diverse range of bird species, including eagles and hawks, and enjoy the colorful flora of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brainerd

Brainerd is a heaven for biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top trails:

  • The Cuyuna Lakes State Trail: This trail spans over 7 miles through the picturesque Cuyuna Range. It offers a stunning view of the surrounding countryside and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride.

  • The Dutchman’s Bluff Trail: This trail gives a panoramic view of Upper East Gull Lake’s stunning views. The hike is uphill but is worth the effort as the beautiful scenery rewards with the effort.

Best Things to Do in Brainerd: Activities and Tours

From thrill-seekers to relaxing family fun, Brainerd has plenty of options for everyone. Here are the top things to do in Brainerd:

  • Ziplining: Brainerd Zipline Tour offers thrilling zipline experiences through the treetops. The views from above are unbeatable, and the adrenaline rush after the zip is hard to beat.

  • Visit Pirates Cove Adventure Golf: This is the perfect spot for a family outing and a fun round of miniature golf in a pirate-themed atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brainerd

Whether you have a week or just a few days, you can experience the best of Brainerd with this itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and check-in at your hotel. Explore downtown Brainerd on foot and check out some local restaurants.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the nearby lakes or taking a hiking or biking trip on one of the trails.

Day 3: Take a cultural tour of Brainerd and visit the museums and historical places.

Day 4: Get your adrenaline pumping with a zipline adventure or a round of miniature golf.

Day 5: Spend the day relaxing and enjoying the town’s stunning views and scenery.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive around Brainerd and explore some of the nearby towns and attractions.

Day 7: Take a tour of the local breweries or wineries and sample local products before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Brainerd

Brainerd is an excellent destination for foodies and beer lovers. Here are some of the top spots to check out during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye Sandwich: A local delicacy made with fresh walleye and served on a toasted bun. The sandwich is a must-try for seafood lovers.

  • Wild Rice Soup: The soup is a local favorite, with a creamy texture and a unique taste, thanks to the presence of wild rice.

Local Beverages

  • Spiked Lemonade: A refreshing drink that’s perfect on a hot day. It’s made with freshly squeezed lemon juice, vodka, and a touch of sweetness.

  • Local craft beer: Brainerd is home to several breweries, and you can sample a range of local craft beers at any of them.

Where to Stay in Brainerd: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer five-star hotels or budget-friendly accommodations, Brainerd has plenty of options for every taste and price point. Here are some of the top places to stay in Brainerd:

  • The Grand View Lodge: This luxury resort is perfect for a romantic getaway or a family vacation. It’s located near the lake, and there are plenty of activities to keep everyone entertained.

  • Arrowwood Lodge at Brainerd Lakes: This lodge offers comfortable rooms and some fantastic amenities like indoor and outdoor pools and a water park.

What to Buy in Brainerd: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Brainerd, there are several souvenirs and local products you can take home as a memento of your trip. Here are the top things to buy:

  • Wild rice: A staple food in the region.

  • Birch bark items: Birch bark baskets, trays, and other crafts are popular in the region and visitors’ access.

Local Culture and Customs of Brainerd

The people of Brainerd are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their community and heritage. Saying ‘Hello’ and ‘Thank You’ goes a long way, and it’s polite to remove hats indoors and make eye contact while shaking hands. The traditional attire includes boots, jackets, and hats due to the winter temperatures.

Best Time to Visit Brainerd

The best time to visit Brainerd is from May through September. Summers are warm and sunny, and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities. The fall season offers a colorful and vibrant landscape, with cooler temperatures, ideal for a relaxing day. Winters can be cold and harsh but perfect for snow activities if you are adequately bundled up.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brainerd

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.

  • Dress sensibly and in layers during the winter season.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.

  • Overlook the importance of local stores and businesses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brainerd

Brainerd is generally a safe town. However, it’s essential to be cautious at night and avoid dark and remote areas. It’s advisable to carry a phone or some safety alarm or whistle with you and be mindful of your surroundings.

Health in Brainerd

Healthcare amenities this city has include various clinics and hospitals that offer a full range of services, regardless of insurance coverage.

Getting to Brainerd: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brainerd can be easily accessed via plane, bus, or car. Here are some of the travel options:

Airports Near Brainerd

  • Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport is the closest airport, situated about two hours away.

Bus Stations Near Brainerd

  • The Jefferson Lines bus service connects Brainerd to nearby towns like Duluth and Minneapolis.

Other Options

  • Car rentals and taxi services: Brainerd has several car rental and taxi services that can take you anywhere you need to go within the city.

How to Get Around in Brainerd

Brainerd has easy access to public transportation, or visitors can rent bikes, cars, or taxis. Here are some of the options for getting around:

  • Buses and taxis: Public transportation is readily available, and taxis are located throughout the city.

  • Car rentals: Several car rental companies operate in Brainerd, giving visitors free access to their transport.

Where to Go Next from Brainerd: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have the time, there are plenty of nearby attractions and towns to visit. Here are some of the best destinations:

  • Duluth: An exciting town situated nearby, it offers museums, aquariums and plenty of outdoor activities like rock climbing and kayaking.

  • The Paul Bunyan Trail: Known to be the longest rail-trail in Minnesota, it stretches almost 128 miles and offers some stunning views while trekking.

Conclusion

Brainerd is a hidden gem in Minnesota that is worth exploring. This small town offers an incredible mix of natural beauty, cultural significance, and adventure opportunities. Plan your trip today, and immerse yourself in the region’s luxuries!
