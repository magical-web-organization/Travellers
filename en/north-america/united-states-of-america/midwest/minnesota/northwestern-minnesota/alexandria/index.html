Introduction to Alexandria

Located in the heart of Northwestern Minnesota, Alexandria is a charming small town that boasts natural beauty, history, and culture. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historical sites, Alexandria has something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Alexandria

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Runestone Museum: Explore the history of Vikings in Minnesota and discover the story behind the Kensington Runestone.
  • Legacy of the Lakes Museum: View classic wooden boats and learn about the history of fishing and boating in the area.
  • Big Ole Viking Statue: Snap a picture with the iconic 28-foot Viking statue located in the heart of Alexandria.
  • Douglas County Historical Society: Get a deeper insight into the local history of Alexandria.

Natural Attractions of Alexandria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Carlos State Park: Enjoy hiking, fishing, swimming, and camping in this beautiful state park.
  • Central Lakes Trail: Walk, bike, or rollerblade along one of the most scenic trails in Minnesota.
  • Andes Tower Hills: Go skiing, snowboarding, or tubing in this winter sports paradise.
  • Alexandria Chain of Lakes: Take a boat tour or paddle your own canoe or kayak through the interconnected lakes of Alexandria.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alexandria

  • Lake Carlos State Park Trail: A 2.5-mile hiking trail that circles around Lake Carlos and offers beautiful views.
  • Central Lakes Trail: A 55-mile paved trail that connects several towns and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Bug-A-Boo Bay Trail: A 1.5-mile trail that offers scenic views of Lake L’Homme Dieu.

Best Things to Do in Alexandria: Activities and Tours

  • Carlos Creek Winery: Sample award-winning wines and enjoy live music at this popular winery.
  • Casey’s Amusement Park: Take a ride on the Ferris wheel or play a game of mini-golf at this family-friendly amusement park.
  • Viking Speedway: Catch a race or participate in a go-kart race at this adrenaline-packed speedway.
  • Alexandria Lakes Area Golf Courses: Play a round of golf at one of the many golf courses in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Alexandria

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye Sandwich: A popular local sandwich made with fresh walleye fish.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A delicious soup made with wild rice and chicken.
  • Lefse: A traditional Norwegian flatbread served with butter and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Carlos Creek Winery Wine: Sample award-winning wines made right in Alexandria.
  • Zorbaz Margaritas: Enjoy a delicious margarita while taking in views of the lake at Zorbaz.

Where to Stay in Alexandria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Arrowwood Resort & Conference Center: A luxurious resort with a golf course, spa, and waterpark.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: A comfortable hotel with a pool and hot tub.
  • HI Alexandria Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers looking for a hostel experience.
  • Lake Carlos Villas: A cozy guesthouse located on Lake Carlos.

What to Buy in Alexandria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kensington Runestone Replica: Bring home a replica of the famous Kensington Runestone.
  • Carlos Creek Wine: Purchase a bottle (or two) of delicious wine made in Alexandria.
  • Wild Rice: Purchase locally harvested wild rice to bring home and enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Alexandria

  • Hunting and fishing are popular activities in Alexandria, and many locals enjoy spending time in nature.
  • Scandinavian heritage is celebrated in Alexandria, and the town is home to several museums and monuments dedicated to Viking history.

Best Time to Visit Alexandria

The best time to visit Alexandria is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is warm and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy. Winter also offers plenty of activities, such as skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alexandria

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities available in Alexandria.
  • Try the local cuisine, including wild rice soup and walleye sandwiches.
  • Support local businesses and buy souvenirs made in Alexandria.

Don’t:

  • Venture off designated hiking and biking trails.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be disrespectful of Scandinavian heritage and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alexandria

Alexandria is generally a safe place for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. When hiking or biking, be sure to stay on designated trails and let someone know your route and expected return time.

Health in Alexandria

There are several medical facilities in Alexandria, including Alexandria Clinic and Douglas County Hospital. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications and inform your travel companions of any allergies or medical conditions.

Getting to Alexandria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alexandria

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport: Located approximately two hours from Alexandria, this airport offers connections to major cities.
  • Hector International Airport: Located in Fargo, North Dakota, this airport is approximately two hours from Alexandria.

Bus Stations Near Alexandria

  • Alexandria Trailways Bus Stop: Located in downtown Alexandria with connections to several cities in Minnesota.

Other Options

  • Driving: With easy access from Interstate 94, driving is a popular option for travelers to Alexandria.
  • Amtrak: The Empire Builder line stops in nearby Staples, Minnesota, with connections to several major cities.

How to Get Around in Alexandria

Renting a car is the best option for getting around in Alexandria. However, many attractions are located in the downtown area and are easily accessible by foot or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Alexandria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fargo, North Dakota: A vibrant college town with plenty of cultural attractions and shopping.
  • Minneapolis, Minnesota: A bustling city with a vibrant art and music scene.
  • Itasca State Park: Home to the headwaters of the Mississippi River and beautiful hiking trails.

Conclusion

With its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture, Alexandria is a must-visit destination in Northwestern Minnesota. From hiking and biking trails to museums and wineries, this charming town offers something for every type of traveler.
