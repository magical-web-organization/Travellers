Introduction to Cold Spring

Located in Northwestern Minnesota, Cold Spring is a charming city known for its picturesque landscapes, outdoor adventures, and rich history. This small town is a perfect getaway for those who seek scenic beauty and tranquility away from the hustle and bustle of big cities. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Cold Spring has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover all the things you need to know to plan your unforgettable trip to Cold Spring.

List of Places to Visit in Cold Spring

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Stearns History Museum

Located just a few miles from Cold Spring, the Stearns History Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. The museum displays the history of Stearns County and its surrounding areas, including exhibits on Native American culture, pioneer life, and the agricultural history of the region.

2. Cold Spring Brewery Historic District

The Cold Spring Brewery Historic District is home to the iconic Cold Spring Brewing Company, one of the oldest and largest breweries in Minnesota. The district also features several beautiful historic buildings and public artwork, making it a must-visit attraction for those interested in history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Cold Spring: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

3. Quarry Park and Nature Preserve

One of the most popular attractions in Cold Spring is the Quarry Park and Nature Preserve. This park spans over 600 acres and features several beautiful lakes and hiking trails. Visitors can enjoy swimming, picnicking, and fishing in the summer and cross-country skiing and snowshoeing in the winter.

4. Horseshoe Lake Trail

If you’re looking for a scenic hiking trail, the Horseshoe Lake Trail won’t disappoint. This 2.2-mile trail surrounds the picturesque Horseshoe Lake and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cold Spring

1. Rocori Trail

The Rocori Trail is a popular biking and hiking trail that spans over 7 miles and connects Cold Spring with nearby cities of Richmond and Rockville. This trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and lakes.

2. Lake Wobegon Trail

The Lake Wobegon Trail is a 62-mile trail that stretches from St. Joseph to Osakis and is perfect for biking and hiking enthusiasts. This trail passes through several picturesque towns and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Cold Spring: Activities and Tours

1. Cold Spring Brewing Company Tour

A tour of the iconic Cold Spring Brewing Company is a must-do activity for beer lovers. Visitors can learn about the history of the brewery and its beer-making process and sample some of the finest brews produced here.

2. Cold Spring Sculpture Walk

The Cold Spring Sculpture Walk is an annual event that features several beautiful public artworks in downtown Cold Spring. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk while admiring the beautiful sculptures.

Food and Drink Scene of Cold Spring

Must-Try Dishes

1. Cheese Curds

Cheese curds are a specialty of Midwest cuisine and a must-try dish while in Cold Spring. These appetizers are made from deep-fried cheese curds that are served hot and crispy.

2. Juicy Lucy

Juicy Lucy, a classic Minnesota burger, is a must-try dish for meat lovers. This burger is made by stuffing cheese inside the patty, resulting in a burst of molten cheese with every bite.

Local Beverages

1. Beer from Cold Spring Brewing Company

Cold Spring Brewing Company is one of the largest breweries in Minnesota and offers several varieties of beer that are not available anywhere else. Visitors can tour the brewery and sample some of its finest brews.

2. Wine from Millner Heritage Vineyard and Winery

Millner Heritage Vineyard and Winery is a family-owned business that produces some of the finest wines in Minnesota. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyard and sample their wines in a beautiful outdoor setting.

Where to Stay in Cold Spring: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. River Inn Hotel

Located in the heart of downtown Cold Spring, the River Inn Hotel offers comfortable and affordable accommodations. The hotel features a beautiful courtyard and easy access to several nearby attractions.

2. Mill Creek Inn

The Mill Creek Inn is a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful and secluded vacation. This inn is located in a beautiful setting surrounded by lush forests and offers several outdoor activities, such as hiking and fishing.

What to Buy in Cold Spring: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home several souvenirs and local products from Cold Spring. Some popular items include:

  • Cheese Curds
  • Juicy Lucy seasonings and sauces
  • Cold Spring Brewing Company merchandise
  • Handicrafts and artworks from local artists

Local Culture and Customs of Cold Spring

Cold Spring is a small town with strong community values and traditions. Visitors are expected to show respect to local customs and become a part of the general culture.

Best Time to Visit Cold Spring

The best time to visit Cold Spring is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is pleasant and warm, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall and spring are also good times to visit as the foliage changes color and the temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cold Spring

Do:

  • Take a walk on the Horseshoe Lake Trail
  • Visit the Stearns History Museum
  • Enjoy a beer at Cold Spring Brewing Company
  • Try the local delicacies, such as cheese curds and Juicy Lucy burgers.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas
  • Disturb the local wildlife
  • Damage public property

Safety and Health

Safety in Cold Spring

Cold Spring is a safe town, but visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to stay safe, such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking cars and bikes.

Health in Cold Spring

Visitors should take care of their health by carrying sunscreen, insect repellent, and appropriate clothing according to the season. They should also stay hydrated and avoid consuming tap water from lakes or rivers.

Getting to Cold Spring: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cold Spring

The nearest airport is St. Cloud Regional Airport located approximately 20 miles from Cold Spring. Other nearby airports include Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport and Rochester International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Cold Spring

Greyhound Bus Lines and Jefferson Lines offer bus services to Cold Spring. The nearest bus station is located in St. Cloud, approximately 20 miles from Cold Spring.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Cold Spring using their private vehicles or hire a taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Cold Spring

Visitors can hire a car, bike or scooter to get around in Cold Spring. Several car rental agencies and bike rental shops are available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Cold Spring: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore several nearby destinations from Cold Spring, including:

  • Saint Cloud, Minnesota
  • Minneapolis, Minnesota
  • Alexandria, Minnesota

Conclusion

Cold Spring is a perfect vacation destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and adventure seekers. With several natural attractions, historical places, delicious local delicacies, and comfortable accommodations, Cold Spring is sure to make your trip unforgettable. Plan your trip today and experience the charm of this beautiful city.
