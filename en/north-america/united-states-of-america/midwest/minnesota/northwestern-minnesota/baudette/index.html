Baudette is a small city situated in Northwestern Minnesota, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Often referred to as the “Walleye Capital of the World”, Baudette is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts. However, it’s not just fishing that makes Baudette worth visiting. This charming city has a lot of natural beauty and cultural attractions to offer, making it a perfect vacation spot for all kinds of travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will tell you about the best things to do, places to stay, food and drink scene, and local culture of Baudette, Minnesota.

Introduction to Baudette

Baudette is located in Lake of the Woods County, which is home to a number of waterbodies, wildlife, and scenic views. The city has a small-town feel, but it is full of friendly locals who are always ready to make you feel at home. In addition to fishing, Baudette offers a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and bird-watching.

List of Places to Visit in Baudette

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Baudette has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical sites that you can visit to learn more about its past. One of the most popular attractions is the Lake of the Woods County Historical Museum. Here, you can explore exhibits about the region’s history and culture, including Native American artifacts, logging equipment, and fishing memorabilia. Another must-visit place is the Baudette Depot Museum, which is housed in the old train station and tells the story of the Great Northern Railway.

Natural Attractions of Baudette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Baudette is known for its fishing, there are many other outdoor activities to enjoy as well. One of the most popular is bird-watching. Baudette is home to a variety of birds, including Bald Eagles, Ospreys, and Golden Eyes. You can also go hiking or biking on one of the many trails in the area. Zippel Bay State Park and Beltrami Island State Forest are just a couple of the parks where you can enjoy the beauty of nature. Don’t miss out on the stunning sunset views from the Baudette Overlook.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baudette

Baudette boasts a number of scenic trails that will take you through stunning natural landscapes and give you an opportunity to spot local wildlife. One of the most popular trails is the North Country Trail, which weaves through the woods and along the shoreline. Another popular trail is the Pine to Prairie Birding Trail, where you can observe numerous bird species.

Best Things to Do in Baudette: Activities and Tours

Baudette has a range of activities and tours available for visitors. As mentioned earlier, fishing is a popular activity. You can hire a guide or rent a boat from one of the many marinas around Baudette. Alternatively, take a scenic float plane tour over Lake of the Woods. For winter activities, ice fishing and snowmobiling are popular. Be sure to check out the annual Walleye fishing tournament that takes place in the summer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baudette

If you plan on spending a week in Baudette, here’s what we recommend:

  • Day 1-2: Enjoy fishing and water activities on Lake of the Woods.
  • Day 3: Visit the Lake of the Woods County Historical Museum and Baudette Depot Museum.
  • Day 4-5: Explore the great outdoors by hiking or biking on one of the trails in the area.
  • Day 6: Take a scenic float plane tour of Lake of the Woods.
  • Day 7: Enjoy some ice fishing or snowmobiling in the winter months, or take a relaxing drive through the scenic countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Baudette

Must-Try Dishes

Baudette has a diverse food scene, but you can’t leave without trying a few local dishes. One of the most popular is the walleye fish fry, a battered and deep-fried local favorite. You can also enjoy homemade sausages, wild rice, and blueberry pie.

Local Beverages

Craft beer has been growing in popularity in Minnesota, and Baudette is no exception. There are a few local breweries that you can visit to try some unique and delicious brews. Additionally, don’t forget to check out the local coffee shops and enjoy a cup of freshly roasted coffee.

Where to Stay in Baudette: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baudette has several accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From cozy B&Bs and vacation rentals to motels and hotels, there’s something for everyone.

What to Buy in Baudette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take back home, Baudette has a range of local products to choose from. Some popular items include wild rice, maple syrup, and locally made crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Baudette

Baudette is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are always appreciated. The people of Baudette are proud of their heritage, and it reflects in the way they interact with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Baudette

The best time to visit Baudette is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and the days are long. Fall is also a good time to visit, with beautiful foliage and cooler temperatures. Winter activities can be enjoyed from December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baudette

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Try some new outdoor activities like fishing, hiking, or bird-watching.
  • Enjoy some local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or disturb the natural habitat.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.
  • Ignore the weather conditions during winter activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baudette

Baudette is a safe destination, but it’s always best to take precautions. Follow safety guidelines when you’re engaging in outdoor activities, and avoid traveling alone if possible.

Health in Baudette

Baudette has a medical clinic that provides emergency and urgent care services. Make sure to bring any necessary medications with you and follow any guidelines provided by your doctor.

Getting to Baudette: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baudette

The nearest major airport is Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport, located approximately 150 miles north of Baudette. There are also smaller regional airports serving the area.

Bus Stations Near Baudette

Greyhound Bus Lines has a terminal in International Falls, located approximately 50 miles east of Baudette.

Other Options

Baudette is best explored with your own transportation like a rental car, but there are other options available like cabs, or ride-sharing services.

How to Get Around in Baudette

The best way to get around Baudette is by car. There are several car rental companies operating in the area. Biking or walking is also an option for exploring the downtown area.
