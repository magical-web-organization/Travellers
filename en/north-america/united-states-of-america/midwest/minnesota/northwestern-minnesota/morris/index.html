If you’re looking for a unique and charming Midwestern city to explore, look no further than Morris, Minnesota. Situated in the northwestern part of the state, Morris offers a blend of cultural and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers of all ages and interests. In this travel guide, we’ll offer a comprehensive overview of the best things to do and see, eat and drink, and discover in Morris.

Introduction to Morris

Morris is a small city with a population of approximately 5,000 people. It is the county seat of Stevens County and is known for its picturesque landscapes, lively cultural scene, and friendly people. Morris is home to the University of Minnesota-Morris, a public liberal arts college, which contributes to the city’s youthful vibe and diversity.

List of Places to Visit in Morris

From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Morris has plenty to offer to its visitors. Here’s a list of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stevens County Historical Society and Museum: Discover the rich history and culture of Morris and its surrounding area. The museum has a collection of over 10,000 artifacts and documents, including Native American artifacts, antique machines, and household items.
  • Pomme de Terre Park: This park is home to the Morris Wetland Management District, which features excellent wildlife and bird-watching opportunities. Visitors can also learn about the importance of wetland ecosystems through interactive exhibits.
  • WCROC Horticulture Display Garden: A beautiful garden with over 1,000 species of plants and flowers, including native Minnesota plants, vegetables, and fruit trees.

Natural Attractions of Morris: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glenwood State Park: Located just a short drive from Morris, this park offers breathtaking views of the rolling hills and forests, including the scenic Lake Minnewaska. Activities include hiking, fishing, boating, camping, and more.
  • Pomme de Terre River: A tranquil river perfect for kayaking, canoeing, fishing, or just enjoying the serene atmosphere. The river is an important part of Morris’s history and culture and has significance to the Dakota and Ojibwe peoples.
  • Crystal Lida Resort: A popular resort with over 600 acres of scenic land, including lakes and woods. Visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, hiking, and other outdoor activities, as well as stay in cozy cabins.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morris

Morris has a great network of hiking and biking trails that offer a fun way to explore the city’s natural beauty. Here are some popular routes:

  • Western Segment Trail: A 2.5-mile paved trail that connects the University of Minnesota-Morris campus to the town center.
  • Pomme de Terre State Trail: A 27-mile multi-use trail that follows the Pomme de Terre River, passing through scenic forests, wetlands, and prairies.
  • Stevens County Lake Association Trail: A 4.5-mile trail that takes you around Lake Pomme de Terre, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Morris: Activities and Tours

Morris has something for every type of traveler, whether you’re interested in art and culture, outdoor adventures, or food and drink. Here are some of the best things to do in Morris:

  • Catch a show at the historic Morris Theatre: A beautifully restored theater that offers a variety of events, from movies to live performances.
  • Take a brewery tour at the Back Shed Brewery: A local brewery that produces their own craft beer. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample their creations.
  • Attend the Prairie Pioneer Days: A yearly festival that celebrates Morris’s heritage and culture, featuring live music, food, and entertainment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Morris

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary to make the most of your time in Morris:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Stevens County Historical Society and Museum & Pomme de Terre Park
2 Explore the WCROC Horticulture Display Garden, and go kayaking or canoeing on the Pomme de Terre River
3 Take a day trip to Glenwood State Park and go hiking or fishing, then relax in a cabin at Crystal Lida Resort
4 Bike the Western Segment Trail, have lunch at a local restaurant, and catch a show at the Morris Theatre
5 Take a brewery tour at Back Shed Brewery, have dinner at a local restaurant, and enjoy the live music scene
6 Attend Prairie Pioneer Days festival, featuring live music, food, and entertainment
7 Spend the day shopping for souvenirs and local products before departing Morris

Food and Drink Scene of Morris

Morris offers a diverse array of dining options, from classic American cuisine to unique farm-to-table eateries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Morris American Legion’s Walleye Dinner: A classic Minnesota dish that features fresh-caught walleye fish served with potatoes and onions.
  • Vegetable Lasagna at Twilight Cafe & Bakery: A vegetarian lasagna made with locally sourced vegetables and served with a side salad.
  • Ice Cream at Lee’s Riverside Cafe: Homemade ice cream made with fresh dairy, available in a variety of flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Back Shed Brewery’s Craft Beer: A locally brewed beer that features unique flavors, such as apricot wheat and milkshake stout.
  • Cider from Klein’s Cider Mill: A locally produced cider made from fresh, hand-picked apples.
  • Coffee from Common Cup Coffeehouse: A cozy coffee shop that serves locally roasted beans, alongside delicious pastries.

Where to Stay in Morris: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Morris offers a variety of lodging options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some options to consider:

  • Super 8 by Wyndham Morris: A budget-friendly motel that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Prairie Inn Motel: A family-owned motel that offers clean rooms and free breakfast.
  • GrandStay Hotel & Suites: A modern and luxurious hotel that offers spacious rooms and premium amenities.

What to Buy in Morris: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Morris is known for its rich agricultural industry, so it’s no surprise that some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring back home are farm-fresh goods! Here are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs and local products:

  • Morris Farmers Market: A weekly farmers market that offers a variety of fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products from local farmers and vendors.
  • Stevens County Historical Society Gift Shop: A gift shop that offers a variety of books, souvenirs, and apparel inspired by Morris’s history and culture.
  • Local Boutiques: Morris has a variety of boutique stores that offer unique, locally made products, such as jewelry, pottery, and home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Morris

Morris is home to a vibrant and diverse community that has a rich history and culture. The city has a deep connection to the Dakota and Ojibwe people and is known for its agricultural heritage. Morris has a strong commitment to sustainability and is home to a variety of eco-friendly initiatives.

Best Time to Visit Morris

The best time to visit Morris is during the summer months, from June to August. During these months, the weather is warm and sunny, and the city comes alive with festivals and events. However, Morris is a beautiful destination year-round, with each season offering its own charm and unique activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morris

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while in Morris:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and history, including the Dakota and Ojibwe peoples.
  • Use eco-friendly practices and support local sustainability initiatives.
  • Go hiking or biking on the many trails around the city.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect local customs or lifestyles.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Morris is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s important to take precautions while traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Morris

  • Always lock your belongings and car doors when leaving them unattended.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Be cautious while engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or fishing.

Health in Morris

  • Morris has a variety of medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics, offering comprehensive medical care.
  • It’s important to stay hydrated and wear proper clothing while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Take precautions to avoid tick bites during the summer months.

Getting to Morris: Transportation Options and Logistics

Morris is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Morris

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport: Morris is approximately 3 hours away from this airport.
  • Hector International Airport in Fargo, ND: Morris is approximately 1.5 hours away from this airport.

Bus Stations Near Morris

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Morris: Located in the town center, the station offers daily bus services to and from Morris.

Other Options

  • Driving: Morris is located between two major highways, making it an easy drive from nearby cities, such as Minneapolis and Fargo.
  • Bicycle: Morris is a bike-friendly city, and visitors can easily bike around the town and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Morris

While Morris is a small city, there are several transportation options available, including:

  • Walking: Morris is a very walkable city, and many of the attractions are within walking distance.
  • Biking: Morris has a network of bike lanes and trails that make biking a convenient option.
  • Driving: Morris has plenty of parking, and renting a car is a good option for exploring the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Morris: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Northwestern Minnesota, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Alexandria: A charming city that offers a mix of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and family-friendly fun.
  • Fergus Falls: A quaint town with a historic downtown, art galleries, and scenic lakes.
  • Lake Itasca State Park: A stunning state park that offers hiking, fishing, and boating, as well as the headwaters of the mighty Mississippi River.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the rich history and culture of Morris, or you want to experience the natural beauty and outdoor adventures that the city has to offer, Morris is a destination that won’t disappoint. With this comprehensive travel guide in hand, you’ll be sure to make the most of your time in Northwestern Minnesota.
