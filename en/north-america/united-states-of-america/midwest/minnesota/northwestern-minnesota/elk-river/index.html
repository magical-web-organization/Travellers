A Complete Guide to Elk River, Minnesota

Elk River, located in northwestern Minnesota, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With a range of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions, Elk River offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Elk River.

List of Places to Visit in Elk River

There’s no shortage of things to see in Elk River. From historically significant museums to natural wonders, there’s plenty to explore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oliver H. Kelley Farm: Dive into the rich history of agriculture and farming in America at the Oliver H. Kelley Farm. The exhibit features three generations of the Kelley family and their contribution to Minnesota’s agricultural industry.
  • Jimbob’s Chuckwagon: This unique museum offers a glimpse into the Wild West era, showcasing an impressive collection of cowboy memorabilia and chuckwagon gear.
  • Sherburne History Museum: Located in downtown Elk River, the Sherburne History Museum will take you on a journey through the area’s rich history, from the Ice Age to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Elk River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elk River Municipal Park: This picturesque park offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and fishing. It’s also a popular spot for birdwatching, with over 55 bird species calling the area home.
  • Lake Orono: For those seeking a relaxing day out, head to Lake Orono for swimming, boating, or just lounging on the beach.
  • Elk River State Wildlife Management Area: Nature lovers will enjoy a trip to the Elk River State Wildlife Management Area. With over 2,200 acres of wetlands, forests, and grasslands, it’s the perfect place to spot wildlife such as deer, turkey, and pheasant.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elk River

Elk River’s stunning natural landscapes make it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few of the top trails to check out:

  • Elk River Trail: This multi-use 10-mile trail is perfect for biking, hiking, or even cross-country skiing in the winter months.
  • Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge: With over 30 miles of hiking trails, the Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge is a must-visit for nature lovers. Be sure to keep an eye out for the wide variety of birds and wildlife that call the refuge home.
  • Lake Orono Trail: This 2-mile paved trail is great for an afternoon stroll or bike ride. Enjoy scenic views of the lake and surrounding nature as you explore.

Best Things to Do in Elk River: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with family and friends, Elk River has plenty of fun activities to keep you entertained.

  • Escape Room: Put your problem-solving skills to the test with a visit to the Elk River Escape Room. With challenging puzzles and exciting themes, it’s a great way to spend an afternoon.
  • Summit Brewing Company Tour: Take a tour of the popular Summit Brewing Company for a behind-the-scenes look at the beer-making process. Tastings are included, so be sure to come thirsty!
  • Elk River Art District: Art lovers will enjoy a trip to the Elk River Art District, home to several galleries and studios featuring the works of local artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elk River

Looking to plan a longer trip to Elk River? Here’s a sample one-week itinerary to help get you started:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Oliver H. Kelley Farm and learn about the history of agriculture in America.
2 Take on the Elk River Trail for a day of biking or hiking.
3 Spend the day relaxing at Lake Orono, complete with swimming and water activities.
4 Tour the Jimbob’s Chuckwagon museum for a unique look into the Wild West era.
5 Head to the Sherburne National Wildlife Refuge for a day of birdwatching and nature hiking.
6 Check out the Elk River Art District for a day of exploring local art galleries.
7 Wrap up your trip with a tour of the Summit Brewing Company and tasting of some of their signature brews.

Food and Drink Scene of Elk River

Whether you’re looking for a quick bite or a sit-down meal, Elk River has plenty of eateries to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wild rice soup: A Minnesota staple, this savory soup is made with wild rice, chicken, and vegetables.
  • Juicy Lucy: A burger with cheese cooked inside the patty, the Juicy Lucy is a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Walleye: Known as the state fish of Minnesota, walleye is a popular choice for seafood fans.

Local Beverages

  • Summit Brewing Company: This popular brewery offers a range of beers, from hoppy IPAs to light-bodied lagers.
  • Wisco Pop!: Head to the nearby Wisco Pop! cidery for a refreshing handcrafted soda made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Elk River: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a budget-friendly hostel or a luxurious hotel, Elk River has plenty of options for accommodations.

  • Holiday Inn Elk River: Located near many of Elk River’s top attractions, the Holiday Inn Elk River offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.
  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: Just a short drive from downtown Elk River, this hotel features spacious rooms and a complimentary breakfast buffet.
  • Keller Motel: For budget-conscious travelers, the Keller Motel offers affordable rooms with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Elk River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from your trip to Elk River? Check out these local products and shops:

  • Wild rice: This Minnesota staple can be found at several local grocery stores and farmers’ markets.
  • Local art and crafts: The Elk River Art District features several galleries and studios showcasing the works of local artists.
  • Maple syrup: A popular sweet treat, Minnesota-made maple syrup can be found at several local specialty food shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Elk River

Minnesota is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, and Elk River is no exception. When visiting, be sure to take the time to get to know the residents and learn about their unique way of life.

Best Time to Visit Elk River

The best time to visit Elk River is during the summer months, when temperatures are warm and the outdoor attractions are in full swing. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with stunning foliage and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elk River

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the natural beauty of the area, including hiking and biking trails.
  • Try local dishes such as wild rice soup and walleye for a taste of Minnesota flavor.
  • Check out the Elk River Art District for a glimpse into the local art scene.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack appropriate outdoor attire for hiking and biking.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Leave without taking a tour of at least one of Elk River’s popular attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Elk River

Elk River is a safe destination for travelers. However, as with any small town, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as locking your car and securing your valuables.

Health in Elk River

There are no major health concerns to be aware of when visiting Elk River. As with any trip, be sure to pack any necessary medications and practice good hygiene to avoid illness.

Getting to Elk River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Elk River

The closest airport to Elk River is Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport, located approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Elk River

The nearest bus station to Elk River is the Metro Transit Northstar Commuter Rail Station, located in nearby Big Lake, Minnesota. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rideshare service to Elk River.

Other Options

For those driving to Elk River, it’s located just off of U.S. Highway 10.

How to Get Around in Elk River

While having a car will make it easier to explore the area, there are also several taxi and rideshare services available in Elk River. Some accommodations may also offer shuttle services to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Elk River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Minnesota adventure, here are a few nearby destinations worthwhile to visit:

  • Duluth, Minnesota: Located approximately 2 hours north of Elk River, Duluth is home to stunning lake views and a range of outdoor activities.
  • Mall of America: Just a 45-minute drive from Elk River, the Mall of America is one of the largest shopping complexes in the world, featuring over 520 stores and attractions.
  • Minneapolis, Minnesota: Located approximately 30 minutes southeast of Elk River, Minneapolis offers world-class dining, shopping, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Elk River, Minnesota is a charming town with a lot to offer visitors.
