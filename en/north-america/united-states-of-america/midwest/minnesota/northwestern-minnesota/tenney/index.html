Introduction to Tenney

Tenney is a small town located in Northwestern Minnesota, in the Midwest region of the United States. This charming town is known for its scenic views and outdoor recreation opportunities. If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination, Tenney is the perfect place to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Tenney

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tenney Area Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Tenney and its surrounding areas. Exhibits include antique farm equipment, home furnishings, and photographs.
  • Wilkin County Courthouse: Built in 1907, the Wilkin County Courthouse is an excellent example of neoclassical architecture. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the history of the courthouse and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Tenney: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Tenney: This pristine lake offers excellent fishing and boating opportunities. Visitors can rent a boat or bring their own to enjoy a day on the water.
  • Camp Wilderness: This 660-acre camp offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Visitors can also stay overnight in one of the camp’s cabins or campsites.
  • Buffalo River State Park: Located just a short drive from Tenney, Buffalo River State Park is an excellent destination for hiking, wildlife viewing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tenney

  • Lake Tenney Trail: This easy 2-mile trail loops around the scenic Lake Tenney, offering beautiful views of the water and surrounding forest.
  • Buffalo River State Park Trails: With over 12 miles of hiking trails, Buffalo River State Park has something for hikers of all skill levels. Visitors can choose from a variety of loop trails and take in the park’s unique prairie and woodland landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Tenney: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Lake Tenney is known for its excellent bass and panfish populations, making it a popular destination for anglers.
  • Kayaking: The calm waters of Lake Tenney make it an excellent place for kayaking. Visitors can rent a kayak from the nearby Camp Wilderness.
  • Guided Tours: The Tenney Area Historical Society offers guided tours of the town’s historic sites and landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Tenney

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye: A popular fish in the Midwest, walleye can be found on many local menus.
  • Lefse: This Scandinavian flatbread is a regional specialty and can be found at many local bakeries.
  • Wild Rice Hotdish: A hearty casserole made with wild rice, cream of mushroom soup, and ground beef. This dish is a classic in the Midwest.

Local Beverages

  • Surly Brewing Company: Based in nearby Minneapolis, Surly Brewing Company is known for its excellent craft beers.
  • Wild Prairie Winery: Located just a short drive from Tenney, Wild Prairie Winery offers a variety of locally-made wines.

Where to Stay in Tenney: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tenney Motel: A basic motel with comfortable rooms and affordable rates.
  • Camp Wilderness Cabins: Visitors can stay overnight in one of Camp Wilderness’s rustic cabins.
  • Airbnb: Tenney has a variety of Airbnb options, ranging from cozy guesthouses to private cabins.

What to Buy in Tenney: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lefse: This regional specialty is the perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Wild Rice: A staple in the Midwest, wild rice makes for a great cooking ingredient.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Local artisans in Tenney and the surrounding area create unique and beautiful pieces of jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Tenney

Tenney has a strong Scandinavian heritage, reflected in its food and culture. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Tenney

The best time to visit Tenney is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the outdoor recreation opportunities are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tenney

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and culture.
  • Take advantage of the area’s outdoor recreational opportunities.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tenney

Tenney is a safe town with low crime rates.

Health in Tenney

Visitors should be aware of ticks, which can carry Lyme disease. It’s recommended that visitors wear long sleeves and pants when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Tenney: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tenney

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP): Located about 250 miles from Tenney, MSP is the closest major airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Tenney

  • Greyhound: Greyhound has a bus station in nearby Fergus Falls, about 20 miles from Tenney.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Tenney. The town is located just off of Highway 9, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Tenney

While Tenney is small enough to walk around, visitors may wish to rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Tenney: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fergus Falls: This nearby town offers a variety of cultural attractions, including the Fergus Falls Center for the Arts and the Otter Tail County Historical Society Museum.
  • Detroit Lakes: Known for its beautiful lakes and outdoor recreation opportunities, Detroit Lakes is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • Minneapolis-St. Paul: Located about 250 miles from Tenney, the Twin Cities offer endless opportunities for entertainment, culture, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a peaceful, off-the-beaten-path destination, Tenney is the perfect place to visit. With its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and unique culture, Tenney has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the charms of this hidden gem in Northwestern Minnesota.
