Introduction to Northwestern Minnesota

Located in the Midwest region of the United States, Northwestern Minnesota is a collection of charming small towns, rolling hills, and pristine lakes. The region offers a wealth of outdoor recreational activities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Its rich history and unique traditions are evident at its museums and events. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Northwestern Minnesota and make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Northwestern Minnesota

Whether you have a penchant for culture, outdoor adventures, or both, Northwestern Minnesota has countless attractions to keep you busy.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Minnesota History Center Museum - Explore Minnesota’s rich history at this museum in Saint Paul, featuring exhibits about the state’s native cultures, early explorers, and significant events.

  • Headwaters Science Center - Introduce your kids to science and technology at this museum in Bemidji. Enjoy interactive exhibits, workshops, and events throughout the year.

  • Douglas Lodge - This historic lodge in Itasca State Park was built in 1905 and is listed in the National Register of Historic Places. Take a tour to learn about its history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Northwestern Minnesota: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Itasca State Park - Itasca State Park is the oldest state park in Minnesota and home to Lake Itasca, which is the source of the Mississippi River. The park offers miles of hiking trails, campgrounds, and scenic drives.

  • Tamarac National Wildlife Refuge - This refuge in Rochert is home to over 250 species of birds and plenty of hiking and wildlife-watching opportunities. Discover the seasonal beauty of the North Woods.

  • Gooseberry Falls State Park - Located in Two Harbors, this state park is renowned for its spectacular waterfalls and lake views. Enjoy hiking, camping, and picnicking amidst the natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwestern Minnesota

Northwestern Minnesota offers countless opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few of the most popular routes:

  • Paul Bunyan State Trail - This 120-mile trail runs from Brainerd to Bemidji and is Minnesota’s longest continuously paved trail. Enjoy hiking, biking, and in-line skating amidst stunning natural beauty.

  • Heartland State Trail - This 49-mile trail runs from Park Rapids to Cass Lake and winds through pristine lakes and forests. Discover local history and abundant wildlife along the trail.

  • Superior Hiking Trail - Located along the rocky ridgeline of Lake Superior’s North Shore, this 310-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the lake and forests.

Best Things to Do in Northwestern Minnesota: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or solo, there is no shortage of things to do in Northwestern Minnesota. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

  • North Shore Scenic Railroad - This historic railroad in Duluth offers an unforgettable scenic ride along Lake Superior’s shores. Choose from classic passenger cars or a first-class dining car experience.

  • North Country Trail Association Events - Join the North Country Trail Association for a variety of events including group hikes, trail building, and community cleanup projects.

  • Lakewood Cemetery Tour - Take a guided tour of this historic cemetery in Minneapolis and learn about the lives of some of Minnesota’s most famous residents, including Hubert Humphrey and Roger Maris.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northwestern Minnesota

If you’re planning to spend a week exploring Northwestern Minnesota, consider this itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Itasca State Park, including the Mississippi Headwaters
2 Visit Bemidji’s Headwaters Science Center and take a hike on the Paul Bunyan Trail
3 Day trip to Duluth, including the North Shore Scenic Railroad and Canal Park
4 Enjoy the natural beauty of Tamarac National Wildlife Refuge
5 Discover the history of the region by visiting Fort Snelling and the Minnesota History Center Museum
6 Relax along the rocky shores of Lake Superior in Two Harbors
7 Visit Minneapolis and take a tour of the city, including the Lakewood Cemetery

Food and Drink Scene of Northwestern Minnesota

Northwestern Minnesota offers a variety of delicious local cuisines and beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye - This fish is a Minnesota staple, often prepared with a crispy coating and served with a side of wild rice.

  • Lefse - This Scandinavian flatbread is a popular treat in the region, often served with butter and sugar.

  • Wild Rice Soup - A creamy soup with wild rice, chicken, and vegetables is a tried and true comfort food enjoyed in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Summit Brewing Company - Take a tour of this award-winning brewery in Saint Paul and enjoy a pint of one of their craft beers.

  • Flat Earth Brewing Company - Enjoy a pint of one of their variety of craft beers in their taproom in Saint Paul.

  • Castle Danger Brewery - Located in Two Harbors, this brewery offers a variety of beers inspired by Lake Superior’s rugged coast.

Where to Stay in Northwestern Minnesota: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwestern Minnesota offers a range of lodging options to suit your budget and preferences. Here are a few of our top recommendations:

  • Sheraton Minneapolis Midtown - This hotel in Minneapolis offers modern amenities, a convenient location, and comfortable accommodations.

  • TownePlace Suites Bemidji - This hotel in Bemidji offers spacious rooms and suites and a complimentary breakfast.

  • Northern Pine Lodge - This family-owned lodge in Park Rapids offers a tranquil setting and cozy cabins along the edge of Hart Lake.

What to Buy in Northwestern Minnesota: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without buying a souvenir or two. Here are a few local products to bring home as a reminder of your Northwestern Minnesota experience:

  • Fudge from Lark Toys - Located in Kellogg, Lark Toys offers handmade fudge and classic toy store items.

  • Wild Rice - Purchase locally harvested wild rice from one of the many area farms.

  • Ojibwe Crafts - Find an authentic Ojibwe craft such as intricate beadwork or quillwork at one of the many Native American shops in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwestern Minnesota

Northwestern Minnesota’s culture is heavily influenced by its lakes, forests, and Scandinavian heritage. Folk music, traditional crafts, and local foods are all hallmarks of the region’s unique customs. If you’re interested in learning about the Ojibwe culture of the area, consider visiting the Mille Lacs Indian Museum near Onamia.

Best Time to Visit Northwestern Minnesota

Northwestern Minnesota can be enjoyed any time of year, but the best time to visit depends on the activities you have planned. Summer offers outdoor activities galore, including hiking, biking, and water recreation. Winter offers cross-country skiing, snowmobiling, and ice fishing. Spring and fall offer mild temperatures and scenic views of the changing seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwestern Minnesota

Do:

  • Take advantage of the lakes and outdoor recreation.

  • Explore the region’s history and cultural attractions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the area’s natural beauty by littering or damaging hiking and biking trails.

  • Ignore the region’s cold, snowy winters without packing warm clothes.

  • Underestimate the scenic drives and diverse landscapes of Northwestern Minnesota.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwestern Minnesota

Northwestern Minnesota is generally safe, but always exercise common sense and take precautions, especially when hiking or boating. Always check weather conditions before any outdoor activities and let someone know where you’re going and when you’ll be back.

Health in Northwestern Minnesota

The region is largely rural, so ensure that you have any necessary medication and seek medical help if required. The region also experiences cold, harsh winters, so take appropriate precautions in terms of exposure and frostbite.

Getting to Northwestern Minnesota: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northwestern Minnesota is accessible by car, plane, or bus. Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) is the largest airport in the region, and several bus companies service the area.

Airports Near Northwestern Minnesota

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP)
  • Hector International Airport (FAR)

Bus Stations Near Northwestern Minnesota

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Bemidji
  • Jefferson Lines: Bemidji, Duluth, Fergus Falls, Park Rapids

Other Options

If you are driving, Northwestern Minnesota can be accessed via major highways such as I-94, I-35, and U.S. Highway 2.

How to Get Around in Northwestern Minnesota

A car or bike is the most convenient way to get around Northwestern Minnesota, but several shuttle and tour bus services are also available for various outdoor attractions.

Where to Go Next from Northwestern Minnesota: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If your time in Northwestern Minnesota is over, you can continue your travels with these nearby destinations:

  • Duluth - Located on Lake Superior, Duluth is known for its waterfront attractions, historical landmarks, and outdoor recreational opportunities.

  • Minneapolis - Discover this vibrant arts and culture scene in the largest city in Minnesota, featuring amazing museums, restaurants, and nightlife.

  • Boundary Waters Wilderness Area - Located to the north of Northwestern Minnesota, this wilderness area is known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor adventures, including hiking, paddling, and fishing.

Conclusion

Northwestern Minnesota is a hidden gem with a rich culture, natural beauty, and a wide variety of activities to suit any traveler. Whether you’re looking for relaxation or adventure, you won’t be disappointed by this unique and welcoming region. Plan your trip today and discover all that Northwestern Minnesota has to offer.
