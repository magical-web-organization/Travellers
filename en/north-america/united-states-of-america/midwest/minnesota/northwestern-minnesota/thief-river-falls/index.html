Introduction to Thief River Falls

Thief River Falls is a charming city located in northwestern Minnesota, also known as the “Land of 10,000 Lakes”. The Thief River, which flows through the city, is the source of the city’s name and offers numerous outdoor activities ranging from fishing to boating to kayaking. The city is rich in history and offers a variety of attractions to explore. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Thief River Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pennington County Historical Society and Museum

The Pennington County Historical Society and Museum offers visitors a glimpse into the history of the area. The museum showcases a variety of exhibits including Native American history, pioneer life, and military history. The museum is also home to the Genealogy Library which is dedicated to preserving family history.

Old Soo Depot Transportation Museum

Located in the historic Soo Line depot, the Old Soo Depot Transportation Museum features exhibits related to the history of transportation in the area. The museum features a variety of exhibits including vintage cars, snowmobiles, airplanes, and trains.

Natural Attractions of Thief River Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Seven Clans Casino Water Park

The Seven Clans Casino Water Park is a great place to spend a hot summer day in Thief River Falls. The water park features numerous water slides, a lazy river, and a wave pool.

Agassiz National Wildlife Refuge

The Agassiz National Wildlife Refuge is a great place to explore nature in the area. Visitors can hike through the trails, observe wildlife, and take in stunning views of the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thief River Falls

Northland Trail

The Northland Trail is a 17-mile paved trail that takes visitors through scenic views of the Thief River. The trail is perfect for biking, hiking, and cross-country skiing during the winter months.

Red Lake River State Water Trail

The Red Lake River State Water Trail is a 44-mile canoe trail that takes visitors through the scenic views of Thief River Falls. The trail has several access points where visitors can launch their canoes.

Best Things to Do in Thief River Falls: Activities and Tours

Golf at the Thief River Golf Club

Golf enthusiasts should visit the Thief River Golf Club, which offers an 18-hole course with a beautiful view of the river. The course is challenging for beginners and experienced golfers alike.

Warroad Tours

Take a tour of the nearby city of Warroad, known for its hockey tradition and manufacturing industry. Visitors can visit the Warroad Heritage Center and the Marvin Windows and Doors plant.

Food and Drink Scene of Thief River Falls

Must-Try Dishes

Wild Rice Soup

Wild Rice Soup is a dish made with wild rice, vegetables, and cream or chicken broth. It is a local staple in Thief River Falls

Swedish Meatballs

Swedish Meatballs are a popular dish in Minnesota and can be found in many local restaurants in Thief River Falls.

Local Beverages

Surly Brewing Company

Surly Brewing Company is a well-known craft brewery in Minnesota that offers a variety of beers. They have a taproom in Minneapolis, but their beer can be found in many local restaurants in Thief River Falls.

Schell’s Brewery

Schell’s Brewery is the second-oldest family-owned brewery in the United States, located in New Ulm, Minnesota. Visitors can take a tour and sample their beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Thief River Falls

Thief River Falls has a rich Native American history, so it is important to respect their culture and customs. The city is also known for its ice hockey tradition, so be prepared to see a lot of locals wearing ice hockey gear.

Best Time to Visit Thief River Falls

The best time to visit Thief River Falls is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall foliage season (September-October) also offers beautiful views of the colorful leaves.

Getting to Thief River Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thief River Falls

The Thief River Falls Regional Airport (TVF) is the closest airport to the city, with Delta Connection flights to Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport (MSP).

Bus Stations Near Thief River Falls

Thief River Falls does not have a bus station. The nearest Greyhound station is located in Grand Forks, North Dakota, about an hour away.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive or rent a car to get to Thief River Falls.

How to Get Around in Thief River Falls

Thief River Falls is a small city and can easily be navigated on foot. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Thief River Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Grand Forks is a nearby city that is home to several attractions, such as the Columbia Mall, the North Dakota Museum of Art, and the Ralph Engelstad Arena.

Voyageurs National Park

Located about 140 miles from Thief River Falls, Voyageurs National Park is a great place to explore nature and offers several outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and boating.
