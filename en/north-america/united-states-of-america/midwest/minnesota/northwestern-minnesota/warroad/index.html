Warroad, Minnesota is a charming town situated in Northwestern Minnesota, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. In this comprehensive travel guide to Warroad, we will explore everything this small town has to offer, including the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, and more.

Introduction to Warroad

Warroad, Minnesota, is a small town with a population of just over 1,700 people. The town was founded in 1896 and has a rich history that visitors can explore through its museums and historical attractions. Warroad has a vibrant culture and is known for its hockey legacy. In fact, Warroad is known as the “Hockeytown USA” due to the number of professional hockey players who have come from this small town.

List of Places to Visit in Warroad

Warroad has plenty of places to visit, from museums and historical attractions to scenic outdoor destinations. Here are some of the top places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marvin Windows Visitor Center: The Marvin Windows Visitor Center is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in architecture and design. The center showcases Marvin’s award-winning products and features interactive exhibits that highlight the company’s history and craftsmanship.

  • The Shed at Warroad: The Shed at Warroad is an innovative museum that celebrates Warroad’s hockey legacy. The museum is built from a converted hockey arena and features interactive exhibits, displays, and memorabilia.

  • Lake of the Woods County Historical Society Museum: The Lake of the Woods County Historical Society Museum is housed in a former Carnegie Library and offers visitors a glimpse into the history of Warroad and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Warroad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zippel Bay State Park: Zippel Bay State Park is located just a short drive from Warroad and offers visitors breathtaking views of Lake of the Woods. The park boasts a variety of recreational activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating.

  • Roseau River Wildlife Management Area: The Roseau River Wildlife Management Area is a 65,000-acre tract of land located just a few miles east of Warroad. The area is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, moose, and black bears.

  • Hayes Lake State Park: Hayes Lake State Park is located just a short drive from Warroad and offers visitors a variety of recreational activities, including hiking, fishing, and boating. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including black bears, wolves, and bald eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Warroad

Warroad is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Roseau River Trail: The Roseau River Trail is a scenic hiking trail that winds through the Roseau River Wildlife Management Area.

  • The Warroad 5K Trail: The Warroad 5K Trail is a scenic trail that winds its way around the town of Warroad. The trail is perfect for walkers, joggers, and bikers.

  • The Pine to Prairie Trail: The Pine to Prairie Trail is a multi-use trail that stretches 28 miles from Warroad to Thief River Falls. The trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and snowmobiling.
