Introduction to Little Falls

Located in northwestern Minnesota in the United States, Little Falls is a charming small town that offers a perfect combination of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Known for its scenic views, outdoor recreational activities, and fascinating museums, Little Falls is a hidden gem that is worth exploring. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will share everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Little Falls.

List of Places to Visit in Little Falls

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Charles A. Lindbergh Historic Site: This museum celebrates the life and accomplishments of the famous aviator Charles A. Lindbergh. The site includes his childhood home, a visitor center with interactive exhibits, and a museum store.

  • Minnesota Military Museum: This museum honors Minnesota’s military history, from the Civil War to the present day. Exhibits include artifacts, photos, and memorabilia.

  • Morrison County Historical Society: This museum features exhibits on the history of Morrison County, including Native American artifacts, pioneer life, and industrial development.

Natural Attractions of Little Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belle Prairie Park: This park features picnic areas, trails for hiking and cross-country skiing, and a playground. It also has a beach and swimming area on the Mississippi River.

  • Pine Grove Zoo: This zoo is home to over 50 different species of animals, including tigers, wolves, and monkeys. It also offers a petting zoo and pony rides.

  • Blanchard Dam Overlook: This scenic overlook offers stunning views of the Mississippi River and the Blanchard Dam, which was built in the early 1900s.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Falls

  • Soo Line Trail: This trail is perfect for biking or hiking and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and wetlands.
  • Pine Grove Pathway: This hiking trail winds through wooded areas and offers beautiful views of the Mississippi River.

Best Things to Do in Little Falls: Activities and Tours

  • Linden Hill Historic Estate: This historic mansion and estate offers tours of the home and gardens, as well as special events like concerts and paranormal investigations.

  • Little Falls Dam Festival: This annual festival celebrates the construction of the Little Falls Dam and features live music, food vendors, and a parade.

  • Mississippi River Cruises: Take a relaxing cruise down the mighty Mississippi River and enjoy the scenic views and wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Falls

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jumbalaya Stuffed Peppers: This unique dish is a blend of Cajun and Midwestern flavors and features green bell peppers stuffed with a spicy rice and sausage mixture.

  • Wild Rice Soup: A local favorite, this creamy soup is made with wild rice and features chunks of vegetables and chicken.

  • Blueberry Muffin French Toast: This decadent breakfast dish features thick slices of French toast stuffed with blueberry muffins and topped with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Third Street Brewhouse: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

  • Great River Arts: This arts center hosts regular wine tastings and provides a perfect setting for a relaxed night out.

Where to Stay in Little Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • AmericInn by Wyndham Little Falls: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like a pool and fitness center.

  • Waller House Inn: This historic bed and breakfast features beautifully appointed rooms and a gourmet breakfast.

  • Little Falls Hostel: This affordable hostel is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Little Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wild Rice: A staple of Minnesota cuisine, wild rice is readily available in Little Falls and makes for a great souvenir or gift.

  • Hand-Crafted Pottery: Several local artists and galleries offer handmade pottery in unique designs and styles.

  • Hunting and Fishing Gear: Little Falls is a popular destination for hunting and fishing, so shops like R&J’s Tackle and Hunting Supply offer a wide range of equipment and gear.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Falls

Little Falls is a friendly and welcoming community, where people value honesty and integrity. Visitors are expected to respect the local traditions and customs, including the importance of nature and the conservation of natural resources.

Best Time to Visit Little Falls

The best time to visit Little Falls is from May to September, when the weather is mild and many outdoor recreational activities are available. There are also several festivals and events during this time, like the Little Falls Dam Festival and the Lemonade Concert and Art Fair.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Falls

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area, including the Mississippi River and local parks and trails.

  • Visit the historic sites and museums that celebrate the town’s rich history.

  • Attend the local festivals and events to experience the unique culture and traditions of Little Falls.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitats of local wildlife.

  • Disrespect local customs, traditions, or beliefs.

  • Leave without trying some of the local cuisine and beverages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Falls

Little Falls is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should still take common sense precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Little Falls

Little Falls has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Take care to protect against ticks and other outdoor hazards.

Getting to Little Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Little Falls

  • St. Cloud Regional Airport (STC): 30 miles away, with regional airline services to Minneapolis and Chicago.

Bus Stations Near Little Falls

  • Jefferson Lines: This bus line serves Little Falls and offers service to several nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Driving: Little Falls is easily accessible by car, with major highways like Interstate 94 and U.S. Route 10 passing through the town.

  • Train: The Amtrak station in St. Cloud is 30 miles from Little Falls and offers train service to several cities, including Chicago and Seattle.

How to Get Around in Little Falls

A car is the easiest way to get around Little Falls, but visitors can also use taxis or ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Little Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Brainerd: This nearby town is known for its outdoor recreational activities like fishing, boating, and golfing.

  • St. Cloud: This city offers cultural attractions like the Paramount Theatre and the St. Cloud Art Crawl.

  • Minneapolis: This major city is just a two-hour drive from Little Falls and offers endless attractions like the Mall of America and the Minneapolis Institute of Art.

Conclusion

Little Falls may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Little Falls, Minnesota.
