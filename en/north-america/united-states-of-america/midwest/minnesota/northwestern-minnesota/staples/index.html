Staples, located in Northwestern Minnesota, is a charming small town brimming with beautiful natural landscapes and welcoming locals. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or food aficionado, you’ll find plenty of things to see and do in this hidden gem of Minnesota. In this guide, we will take you on a tour of the best places to visit and things to do in Staples.

Introduction to Staples

Staples is a beautiful town where visitors can experience the local culture and enjoy the scenic beauty of Northern Minnesota. It was named after a civil engineer, Samuel P. Staples, who helped build the Northern Pacific Railroad. The town is located in the Heartland Lakes region and is surrounded by several lakes, forests, and parks. The locals are friendly, and the town exudes a laid-back vibe, making it an ideal destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

Best Things to Do in Staples: Activities and Tours

Staples offers a diverse range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a scenic drive on the Edge of the Wilderness Scenic Byway
  • Go paddling on the Crow Wing River
  • Visit the Staples World’s largest statue of Paul Bunyan
  • Attend the Staples Hostfest, one of the largest Nordic festivals in the United States

Week-Long Itinerary for Staples

Here is a sample itinerary that covers the best things to see and do in Staples in a week:

Day 1: Arrive in Staples, explore downtown Staples, visit The Staples Historical Society Museum.

Day 2: Visit the Rice Lake Wildlife Refuge, go hiking on the Lake Wadena Loop Trail, and have dinner at The Great Northern Cafe.

Day 3: Take scenic drive on the Edge of the Wilderness Scenic Byway, go paddling on the Crow Wing River, and have lunch at The Iron Wok.

Day 4: Visit the Staples World’s largest statue of Paul Bunyan, go biking on the Paul Bunyan State Trail, and have dinner at The Rusty Nail.

Day 5: Visit the Crow Wing River State Park, go hiking on the Nordic Trail, and have lunch at Stompin’ Grounds coffee shop.

Day 6: Attend the Staples Hostfest and enjoy the Nordic music, dance, and food.

Day 7: Take a leisurely stroll through Crow Wing Antique Mall.

Local Culture and Customs of Staples

Staples is a welcoming town where visitors can experience the local culture and customs. The locals are friendly, and the town is known for its Nordic heritage, which is reflected in its festivals, food, and music.

Best Time to Visit Staples

The best time to visit Staples is during the summer months (June-August) when there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy and festivals to attend. Fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit, with the beautiful foliage in the area.
