Introduction to Crookston

Crookston is a small town in Northwestern Minnesota. It is located in Polk County and has a rich history and culture. The town is famous for its outdoor adventures, scenic views, museums, and historical places. It is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a peaceful getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Crookston

Here is a list of places to visit in Crookston:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Polk County Museum: The museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the rich history of Crookston and Polk County.
  • Historic Holmes Theatre: The theatre is a cultural hub of Crookston and hosts numerous shows and concerts throughout the year.
  • Carnegie Library: The library has a vast collection of books and archives related to the history of Crookston.

Natural Attractions of Crookston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crookston Sports Center: The sports center offers a variety of activities, including ice skating, hockey, and curling.
  • River Walk and Nature Trail: The river walk is a scenic route that runs along the Red Lake River. Visitors can enjoy hiking and biking, and take in the stunning views.
  • Spruce Hill Park: Spruce Hill Park is a beautiful park with a playground, picnic area, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crookston

Crookston offers several hiking and biking routes, including the River Walk and Nature Trail, University of Minnesota Crookston Trail System, and Red River State Recreation Area Trail. These trails provide visitors with opportunities to experience the natural beauty of the town.

Best Things to Do in Crookston: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Crookston:

  • Take a tour of the Polk County Museum and learn about the town’s heritage.
  • Attend a concert or show at the Historic Holmes Theatre.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the town on the River Walk and Nature Trail.
  • Go fishing or golfing at one of the local clubs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Crookston

Day 1: Visit the Polk County Museum. Day 2: Explore the River Walk and Nature Trail. Day 3: Go fishing or golfing. Day 4: Visit the Historic Holmes Theatre. Day 5: Take a bike tour of the town. Day 6: Try local cuisine. Day 7: Spend the day at the Crookston Sports Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Crookston

Crookston offers a diverse selection of dining options, ranging from casual to fine dining. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: A Norwegian flatbread, served with butter and sugar or jam.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A creamy soup, made with wild rice, chicken, and vegetables.
  • Walleye: A freshwater fish, served pan-fried or grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Loon Juice: A hard cider made with honeycrisp apples.
  • Schell’s Beer: A regional beer from New Ulm, Minnesota.

Where to Stay in Crookston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crookston has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • AmericInn by Wyndham: A cozy hotel with indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Cobblestone Hotel and Suites: A modern hotel that offers a fitness center and free breakfast.
  • Crookston Inn and Convention Center: A luxurious hotel that features an indoor pool, restaurant, and bar.

What to Buy in Crookston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Crookston has several shops and markets that sell unique souvenirs and local products. Some popular items include:

  • Lefse: Norwegian flatbread, available at Widman’s Candy Shop.
  • Wild Rice: A local staple, available at Crookston Farmer’s Market.
  • Handmade Crafts: Unique crafts made by local artists, available at North Country Store.

Local Culture and Customs of Crookston

Crookston has a rich history and culture, with a significant influence of Scandinavian heritage. The locals are friendly, hospitable, and take pride in their town’s heritage and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, such as removing shoes before entering homes and temples.

Best Time to Visit Crookston

The best time to visit Crookston is during the summer season, from June to September. The weather is ideal for outdoor activities, and several festivals and events take place during this time. The fall season, from September to November, is also a good time to visit, with beautiful fall foliage and mild weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crookston

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Crookston:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect religious or cultural practices.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Crookston

Crookston is a safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as locking their cars and keeping valuables out of sight. Outdoor enthusiasts should be aware of potential hazards, such as sudden weather changes and wildlife.

Health in Crookston

Crookston has several healthcare facilities, including clinics and hospitals. Visitors should carry necessary medications and first-aid kits, especially if they plan to go on outdoor adventures.

Crookston is easily accessible by car, bus, and air. Here are some transportation options:

  • Hector International Airport

Bus Stations Near Crookston

How to Get Around in Crookston

Crookston is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles from local rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Crookston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Crookston is situated in a scenic location, and there are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore. Some options include:

  • Grand Forks: A city just across the border in North Dakota, with a variety of entertainment options, including shopping, dining, and nightlife.
  • Lake Bronson State Park: A scenic park with a lake, hiking trails, and camping facilities.
  • Itasca State Park: Minnesota’s oldest park, famous for its beautiful landscape and the source of the Mississippi River.

Conclusion

Crookston is a wonderful vacation destination, with its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage. Visitors can enjoy a mix of indoor and outdoor activities, try local cuisine and beverages, and take in the town’s unique customs and culture. A visit to Crookston is a perfect way to experience the beauty and tranquility of Northern Minnesota.
