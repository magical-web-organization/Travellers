Introduction to Fergus Falls

Located in Northwestern Minnesota, Fergus Falls is a small city that offers an abundance of things to do and see. Set on the banks of Otter Tail River, Fergus Falls is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With a history rooted in logging and agriculture, the city is now a center for art and culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, you will discover all that Fergus Falls has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Fergus Falls

When it comes to sightseeing, Fergus Falls has something for everyone. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or the great outdoors, this list has got you covered.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Otter Tail County Historical Society Museum - Explores the history of Otter Tail County through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Fergus Falls State Hospital - A former state hospital that now houses a museum and art center.
  • Kaddatz Galleries - A vibrant art space that showcases local and national artists.
  • The River Walk of Fergus Falls - A scenic walkway along the Otter Tail River that offers beautiful views of the city and surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Fergus Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phelps Mill County Park - A picturesque park with a historic mill, waterfall, and hiking trails.
  • Minnesota Central Trail - A bike trail that spans over 100 miles across West Central Minnesota.
  • Maplewood State Park - A stunning park with over 25 miles of hiking trails, sparkling lakes, and lush forests.
  • Prairie Wetlands Learning Center - A nature center that offers educational programs and exhibits on the prairie ecosystem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fergus Falls

Fergus Falls is a paradise for hikers and bikers. With a network of trails that take you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Minnesota, there is no shortage of options.

  • Central Lakes Trail - A 55-mile trail that runs from Fergus Falls to Osakis.
  • Glendalough State Park - Offers a variety of hiking trails through pristine prairies, forests, and lakes.
  • North Country National Scenic Trail - 4,600 miles of trail that crosses seven states, including Minnesota.

Best Things to Do in Fergus Falls: Activities and Tours

From adventurous outdoor activities to cultural experiences, Fergus Falls has plenty to offer.

  • Red Horse Ranch Arena - Offers horseback riding lessons and trail rides.
  • Fergus Falls YMCA - Offers a variety of fitness classes and facilities for all ages.
  • Fergus Falls Center for the Arts - A vibrant arts center that offers live performances and art exhibits.
  • Fergus Falls Municipal Sports Complex - Offers ice skating, hockey, and curling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fergus Falls

Day 1: Visit the Otter Tail County Historical Society Museum and River Walk
Day 2: Take a hike along Maplewood State Park trails or bike the Central Lakes Trail
Day 3: Explore Phelps Mill County Park and the historic mill
Day 4: Learn about the prairie ecosystem at the Prairie Wetlands Learning Center
Day 5: Take a horseback ride at Red Horse Ranch Arena
Day 6: Relax at Fergus Falls Municipal Sports Complex
Day 7: Visit the Fergus Falls Municipal Sports Complex

Food and Drink Scene of Fergus Falls

Fergus Falls offers a wide range of dining options that showcase the flavors of the region.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye - This freshwater fish is popular in the region and can be found on most menus.
  • Wild rice - This nutty grain is traditionally harvested in Minnesota and is a staple of many local dishes.
  • Lefse - This traditional Norwegian flatbread is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Schell’s Brewery - Located in nearby New Ulm, this brewery offers a variety of craft beers.
  • Jack Pine Brewery - Brewery located in nearby Baxter, offering unique craft beers.
  • Malt-E-Fez Coffee - Offers locally roasted coffee and delicious pastries.

Where to Stay in Fergus Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fergus Falls offers a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hotels to charming guesthouses.

  • The Otter Lodge - A cozy hotel located near downtown Fergus Falls.
  • AmericInn by Wyndham Fergus Falls - A comfortable hotel with an indoor pool.
  • Riverview Place - A charming guesthouse located on the banks of the Otter Tail River.

What to Buy in Fergus Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to remember your visit to Fergus Falls? Check out these local shops and markets.

  • Uncle Tom’s Antiques and Collectibles - Offers a variety of vintage and antique items.
  • Red Brick Boutique - A trendy boutique featuring handmade and unique gifts.
  • Fergus Falls Farmers Market - Offers fresh produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal products.

Local Culture and Customs of Fergus Falls

Fergus Falls is a small, friendly community with a strong sense of community and local pride. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and enjoy the relaxed pace of life.

Best Time to Visit Fergus Falls

The best time to visit Fergus Falls is in the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season is also a beautiful time to visit when the leaves are changing and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fergus Falls

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Try some of the local cuisine, including walleye and wild rice dishes.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local traffic laws.
  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fergus Falls

Fergus Falls is a safe community with a low crime rate. Visitors are encouraged to take normal safety precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Fergus Falls

Fergus Falls has a variety of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors are advised to bring their own medications and consult a medical professional before traveling.

Getting to Fergus Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fergus Falls

  • Hector International Airport – Fargo, ND
  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport – Minneapolis, MN

Bus Stations Near Fergus Falls

  • Greyhound – Fargo, ND
  • Jefferson Lines - Alexandria, MN

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available at all major airports and cities.
  • Amtrak train service is available in nearby Staples, MN.

How to Get Around in Fergus Falls

Fergus Falls is a small city and most attractions are within walking or biking distance. Car rentals and taxis are also available for those who prefer to travel by vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Fergus Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Itasca State Park - Home to the headwaters of the Mississippi River and beautiful hiking trails.
  • Lake Itasca - A sprawling lake with plenty of opportunities for fishing and boating.
  • Fargo, ND - Only an hour drive away, Fargo offers a variety of museums, restaurants, and shopping opportunities.

Conclusion

Fergus Falls is a hidden gem in Northwestern Minnesota, with endless opportunities for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and relaxation. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan the perfect trip to this charming city.
