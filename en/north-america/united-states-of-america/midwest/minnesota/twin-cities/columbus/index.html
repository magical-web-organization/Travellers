Introduction to Columbus

Columbus, located in Twin Cities, Minnesota, is a vibrant and bustling city offering a wealth of cultural and natural attractions for visitors to explore. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, you’ll find plenty to keep you busy and entertained here. In this comprehensive travel guide to Columbus, we’ll take a closer look at the top tourist spots, local culture, and must-try dishes, as well as provide tips for getting around and planning your itinerary.  

List of Places to Visit in Columbus

Here is a quick list of the top tourist attractions to visit in Columbus:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Minnesota State Capitol - This majestic building built in 1905 showcases the architectural skills of Cass Gilbert, an award-winning architect. The Capitol complex covers 27 acres and features more than 200 different materials from around Minnesota, the United States, and the world. Don’t forget to take the guided tour of this historic building.
  • Minnesota History Center - This museum in St. Paul is home to an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits exploring the history of Minnesota. From the fur trade to modern technology, you’ll find plenty to learn here.
  • Mall of America - Located just 15 minutes from Columbus, the Mall of America is one of the world’s largest malls, featuring over 500 stores, an indoor amusement park, and an aquarium.  

Natural Attractions of Columbus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Minnehaha Regional Park - This 193-acre park features a beautiful 53-foot waterfall, limestone bluffs, and a scenic overlook. Enjoy hiking trails, picnic areas, and a wading pool in the summer.
  • Chain of Lakes - Comprised of five lakes and over 15 miles of scenic trails, the Chain of Lakes is a popular spot for biking, jogging, and boating.
  • Como Park Zoo and Conservatory - Spread over 384 acres, Como Park Zoo and Conservatory is home to hundreds of animal species and an impressive collection of exotic plants and flowers. It’s a perfect destination for families with children.  

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbus

Columbus boasts a variety of scenic hiking and biking trails for nature lovers. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Midtown Greenway - This 5.5-mile-long former railway corridor is now a popular biking and walking trail that runs through the heart of Minneapolis.
  • Minneapolis Chain of Lakes Regional Park - This seven-mile loop around five of the city’s most beautiful lakes provides breathtaking views with plenty of pit stops for picnics and swimming.
  • Gateway State Trail - Located in St. Paul, this 18-mile crushed limestone trail runs from the Capitol building to Pine Point and is perfect for both hiking and biking.  

Best Things to Do in Columbus: Activities and Tours

Columbus offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the highlights:

  • Segway Tours - Experience Columbus’s beauty while gliding on an eco-friendly Segway. Enjoy an organized citywide Segway tour that covers the city’s top landmarks, including parks, sculptures, and museums.
  • Escape Rooms - Adventure seekers shouldn’t miss visiting one of the city’s escape rooms, where you can test your critical thinking skills and unwind with friends and family.
  • Cycling Tours - Book a guided cycling tour adventure and explore the magnificent nature that Columbus offers while creating unforgettable memories.  

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbus

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Columbus, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1-2: Visit the major cultural landmarks, such as the Minnesota State Capitol and the Minnesota History Center.
  • Day 3-4: Explore the natural attractions, including the Minnehaha Regional Park and Chain of Lakes.
  • Day 5: Try out one or two of the outdoor adventures or tours, such as an escape room or Segway tour.
  • Day 6-7: Experience the local food and drink scene, including must-try dishes and local beverages.  

Food and Drink Scene of Columbus

Minnesota is home to some tasty dishes and delightful beverages, so be sure to sample some of the local cuisine while you’re in Columbus.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juicy Lucy - This cheese-stuffed burger originated in Minneapolis and is a must-try for any serious burger lover.
  • Wild Rice Soup - This soup made from native Minnesota wild rice is a creamy and filling bowl of warmth.
  • Walleye - This freshwater fish is an iconic dish in Minnesota and can be found at many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • **Surly Brewing ** - This Minneapolis-based brewery offers a range of tasty craft beers to sample.
  • Vikre Distillery - This Duluth based distillery is known for its artisanal gin, whiskey and aquavit
  • Ward 6 - A popular Saint Paul bar that offers cocktails and beer, and a great environment for a drink after a long day.  

Where to Stay in Columbus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Columbus has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top picks in the city:

  • The Saint Paul Hotel - An elegant and historic hotel located in downtown Saint Paul.
  • LeMeridien Chambers Minneapolis - This stylish and chic hotel is located in downtown Minneapolis and offers an art-filled stay.
  • Minneapolis International Hostel - A budget-friendly accommodation option located in the trendy Uptown neighborhood.  

What to Buy in Columbus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring home, here are some options:

  • Minnesota Wild Rice - A unique and tasty ingredient to bring home and use in your own kitchen.
  • Handmade Jewelry - You can find local artists’ jewelry from shops and boutiques around the city.
  • Candlelight – Soy candle makers such as Minneapolis-based company Illume make beautifully fragranced candles that make for a perfect gift.  

Local Culture and Customs of Columbus

Columbus locals are friendly and welcoming, always ready to strike up a conversation on any topic, share archeological and cultural details. Visitors should be prepared to dress in layers, as temperatures can range from chilly in the mornings to hot in the afternoons, carry an umbrella to avoid sudden rains, motorists should watch for bike lanes, and non-motorized vehicles while driving.

Best Time to Visit Columbus

Columbus experiences all four seasons, with warm summers, cool autumns, snow-filled winters, and mild springs. The best time to visit Columbus is from June to September when outdoor attractions and activities are in full swing. During summer, visitors can experience outdoor community events, including art fairs, music festivals, and family-friendly events. Columbus is usually cold and snowy from December to February.  

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbus

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Columbus:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors - Columbus has a lot to offer, including numerous parks and lakes with outdoor recreational activities.
  • Try the local cuisine - Minnesota is known for its unique food dishes, which you should definitely try while you’re in the area.
  • Connect with locals - You can learn a lot about the city from the people who live there, so don’t hesitate to ask for restaurant recommendations or opinions on attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect nature - Be sure to leave nature in good condition, including the trails and parks.
  • Overlook Minneapolis - Take a day or two to explore the Twin Cities of Minneapolis and Saint Paul. There are so many attractions, landmarks, and local neighborhoods to see and explore.
  • Forget to tip - Standard practice is to tip 15-20% for services like food and drinks, so be sure to budget accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbus

Columbus is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take basic precautions, such as locking car doors and valuables secure while out and about, watch out for bike lanes while driving or walking, and stay aware of your surroundings in areas that are less popular among tourists.

Health in Columbus

Be sure to pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and medication as necessary if you’re allergic to something specific to the area. If you have medical concerns and are traveling, it’s always best to check with your doctor before leaving.  

Getting to Columbus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Columbus has several transportation options for visitors:

Airports Near Columbus

  • Minneapolis/Saint Paul International Airport - This is the main airport serving the Twin Cities and offers direct flights to numerous domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Columbus

  • Minneapolis Bus Station - Located in downtown Minneapolis, the Minneapolis Bus Station connects Columbus to other destinations across the United States via bus routes.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a great way to explore the city and surrounding areas at your leisure.
  • Uber or Lyft - These ride-sharing services operate in the Twin Cities and offer a convenient way to get around for visitors who don’t want to rent a car.

How to Get Around in Columbus

Columbus has a variety of transportation options available, including public transportation, taxis, and car rentals. The metro area is bicycle-friendly, so you can consider renting bikes or using local bike-share options to explore the city while being eco-friendly.

Where to Go Next from Columbus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Columbus is located in the heart of the Midwest region of the United States, making it an excellent location for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Duluth - This small city in northern Minnesota offers a range of outdoor activities, stunning natural scenery, and a historic canal park that makes it a perfect day trip from Columbus.
  • Madeline Island - Located in Lake Superior, this island is known for its pristine natural beauty and charming fishing villages.
  • Wisconsin Dells - Located about 150 miles from Columbus, Wisconsin Dells is a popular tourist destination known for its water parks, amusement parks, and other family-friendly attractions.  

Conclusion

Columbus is an exciting city with a wealth of cultural and natural attractions to explore. Whether you’re interested in learning about local history and culture, experiencing the natural beauty of the region, or sampling the unique cuisine, you’re sure to have a memorable experience in this Midwest gem. So, plan your trip, pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in Columbus!
