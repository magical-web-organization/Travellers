Introduction to Rogers

Rogers is a charming city located in the heart of the Midwest, Minnesota. With a population of just over 12,000 people, Rogers offers a relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of big city life. It is also an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a unique cultural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Rogers

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rogers has a rich history, which is apparent in its many museums and historical attractions. Here are some must-see places:

  • The Ellingson Car Museum: This museum features a collection of vintage and rare cars, including some that date back to the early 1900s.
  • The North Star Museum of Boy Scouting and Girl Scouting: This museum offers a fascinating look at the history of scouting in Minnesota and how it has evolved over time.
  • The Oliver H. Kelley Farm: This historic farm was once home to the father of the American agricultural movement and now serves as a living history museum that transports visitors back in time.

Natural Attractions of Rogers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rogers is surrounded by natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some places to explore:

  • The Crow-Hassan Park Reserve: This park features over 2,600 acres of prairie and forest land, making it perfect for hiking, biking, and picnicking.
  • The Riverwood National Golf Course: With 18 holes of beautifully landscaped greens and fairways, this golf course offers a great way to experience the natural beauty of Rogers.
  • The Lake Sarah Regional Park: This park offers visitors a chance to fish, boat, and swim in the crystal-clear waters of Lake Sarah.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rogers

If you love exploring the great outdoors, Rogers has plenty of hiking and biking trails to keep you busy. Here are some popular routes to check out:

  • The Elm Creek Park Reserve: This park features over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through forests, prairies, and wetlands.
  • The Rogers-Lucia Trail: This 2.2-mile trail runs along the Crow River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Lake Orono Trail: This 5.2-mile trail takes you around the beautiful Lake Orono and is perfect for hiking, biking, or jogging.

Best Things to Do in Rogers: Activities and Tours

Rogers offers a wide variety of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some popular options:

  • The Minnesota Zoo: Located just a short drive from Rogers, the Minnesota Zoo features over 4,000 animals from all around the world.
  • The Four Daughters Vineyard and Winery: This scenic winery is located in nearby Spring Valley and offers tours and tastings of their award-winning wines.
  • The Anoka-Hennepin Ghost Tours: These spooky tours take you through some of the most haunted locations in the Twin Cities, including Anoka and St. Paul.

Food and Drink Scene of Rogers

Must-Try Dishes

Rogers may be a small town, but it still has a thriving food scene. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Minnesota Wild Rice Soup: This hearty soup is made with wild rice, chicken, and vegetables and is a staple of Minnesota cuisine.
  • Juicy Lucy: This iconic burger is stuffed with cheese and is a must-try for anyone visiting the Twin Cities area.
  • Hotdish: This casserole dish is a Minnesota classic, made with ground beef, vegetables, and tater tots.

Local Beverages

Rogers is also known for its craft beer scene. Here are some local breweries to check out:

  • Unmapped Brewing Co.: This brewery offers a rotating selection of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and blondes.
  • The Copperwing Distillery: This distillery specializes in small-batch, handcrafted spirits, including gin and vodka.

Where to Stay in Rogers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rogers has a variety of lodging options to choose from, no matter your budget. Here are some options:

  • The Hampton Inn & Suites Rogers: This modern hotel offers free breakfast and an indoor pool, making it perfect for families.
  • The Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, Albertville: Located just a short drive from Rogers, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the nearby outlet mall.

What to Buy in Rogers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to remember your trip to Rogers? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Wild Rice: Minnesota is famous for its wild rice, which makes for a delicious souvenir or gift.
  • Vikre Distillery Spirits: These handcrafted spirits from nearby Duluth make for a great gift for the whiskey or gin lover.
  • Minnetonka Moccasins: These comfortable and stylish moccasins are made right here in Minnesota.

Local Culture and Customs of Rogers

Minnesota is known for its friendly people and laid-back culture. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Friendly Greetings: Minnesotans are known for their friendly personality, so don’t be surprised if someone strikes up a conversation with you.
  • Tipping: Tipping is a common practice in Minnesota, so be sure to tip your servers, bartenders, and hotel staff accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Rogers

The best time to visit Rogers is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and the parks and attractions are open. However, if you’re looking to save money, consider visiting in the off-season (September-May), when lodging and airfare prices tend to be lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rogers

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the Wild Rice Soup and Juicy Lucy burger.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Rogers, including the parks and hiking trails.
  • Do tip your service staff accordingly.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in the parks or on the hiking trails.
  • Don’t forget to dress warmly if you’re visiting during the winter months.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rogers

Rogers is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Rogers

Minnesota has a high-quality healthcare system, so visitors should have no problem finding medical care if needed.

Getting to Rogers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rogers

The closest airport to Rogers is the Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP), which is approximately a 30-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Rogers

Greyhound offers service to nearby cities like Maple Grove, MN and Minneapolis, MN.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option for getting around in Rogers, as public transportation is limited.

How to Get Around in Rogers

Renting a car or bringing your own car is the best way to get around in Rogers. Some hotels offer shuttle service to nearby attractions as well.

Where to Go Next from Rogers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Minnesota, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • The Twin Cities: The vibrant cities of Minneapolis and St. Paul are just a short drive away and offer a variety of attractions and activities.
  • Lake Minnetonka: Located just 30 minutes from Rogers, Lake Minnetonka is a popular destination for boating, swimming, and fishing.
  • Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness: This beautiful wilderness area is located in northeastern Minnesota and is a great destination for canoeing, hiking, and camping.

Conclusion

Rogers, Minnesota is the perfect destination for anyone looking for a relaxing getaway or an outdoor adventure. With its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly people, you’re sure to have a memorable experience. Just be sure to try some Wild Rice Soup and Juicy Lucy before you leave!
