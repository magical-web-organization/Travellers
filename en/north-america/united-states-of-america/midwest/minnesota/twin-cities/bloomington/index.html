Introduction to Bloomington

Bloomington is a charming city in Minnesota, located in the heart of Midwest, United States of America. Known as the home of the famous Mall of America, Bloomington is a vibrant destination for travelers looking for an adventure. This city boasts a rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty and modern amenities, making it an ideal destination for anyone looking for an interesting vacation spot. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the top attractions, outdoor adventures, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more, to help you plan your trip to Bloomington.

List of Places to Visit in Bloomington

Bloomington is home to many exciting places to visit, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Works Museum: A hands-on museum for children that encourages STEM learning.
  • The Museum of Russian Art: Explore the rich traditions and art of Russia.
  • The Hennepin History Museum: Tells the story of Hennepin County and the people who lived here.
  • Pavek Museum of Broadcasting: Experience the history of broadcasting.
  • Fort Snelling State Park: A historical fort that offers a scenic and educational tour.

Natural Attractions of Bloomington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Minnesota Valley National Wildlife Refuge: A beautiful place to take a walk, hike or bike ride.
  • Hyland Lake Park Reserve: A popular spot for hiking and cross-country skiing, with beautiful natural scenery.
  • Normandale Japanese Garden: A serene traditional Japanese garden.
  • Lake Nokomis: A popular lake for kayaking, fishing, and swimming.
  • Mall of America: A shopper’s paradise filled with entertainment, attractions, dining, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bloomington

Bloomington has many hiking/biking trails that are waiting to be explored. Here are a few of the most popular ones:

  • Minnesota Valley National Wildlife Refuge Trail: A 7.7-mile trail that runs through the forest and along the Minnesota River.
  • Hyland Lake Park Reserve Trail: A 9.3-mile trail that takes you through beautiful woods and past crystal-clear lakes.
  • Normandale Lake Trail: A 2.5-mile loop around Normandale Lake that provides a scenic view.
  • Cedar Lake Trail: A 4.7-mile trail that provides incredible views of Cedar Lake.

Best Things to Do in Bloomington: Activities and Tours

Bloomington has many fun activities to offer visitors, including:

  • Nickelodeon Universe: A massive indoor theme park filled with exciting rides and attractions.
  • Sea Life Minnesota Aquarium: Home to thousands of aquatic creatures, including sharks and stingrays.
  • Escape MSP: A popular escape room experience.
  • FlyOver America: Experience a virtual flight across different scenic locations in America.
  • Segway Tours: Explore Bloomington with these fun tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bloomington

Day 1: Visit the Mall of America, Nickelodeon Universe, and the Minnesota Sea Life Aquarium. Day 2: Explore the Hyland Lake Park Reserve and go on a Segway tour of the park. Day 3: Visit the Normandale Japanese Garden, the Museum of Russian Art, and The Works Museum. Day 4: Take a hike at the Minnesota Valley National Wildlife Refuge and visit the Pavek Museum of Broadcasting. Day 5-6: Enjoy the scenic beauty of the Cedar Lake Trail, Lake Nokomis and Fort Snelling State Park. Day 7: Enjoy some shopping and local cuisine before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Bloomington

Bloomington has a vibrant food and drink scene, with many delicious options to choose from. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Minnesota’s famous “Juicy Lucy” burger, served at Matt’s Bar & Grill.
  • A Jucy Lucy from 5-8 Club or Blue Door Pub is also worth trying.
  • A classic Scandinavian smorgasbord from Ingebretsen’s.
  • The steak au poivre and prime rib at Kincaid’s.

    Local Beverages

  • Minnesota is home to many breweries, including Surly Brewing Company, Summit Brewing Company, and Fulton Beer.
  • For a refreshing drink, try the Honeycrisp Apple Cider from Minnetonka Orchards.
  • Grab a cup of coffee from one of the many local shops, including Jinx Tea and Grandma’s Bakery.

Where to Stay in Bloomington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Bloomington, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are a few popular options:

  • Great Wolf Lodge: A family-friendly resort that offers an indoor water park and suites.
  • Radisson Blu Mall of America: A modern hotel located right next to the Mall of America.
  • Sheraton Bloomington Hotel: A comfortable and convenient hotel located near the airport.
  • Embassy Suites by Hilton Minneapolis Airport: A hotel offering spacious and comfortable suites with a free shuttle to the airport.

What to Buy in Bloomington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bloomington offers many unique souvenirs and locally-made products to buy as a memory of your trip. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Minnesota-themed clothing and accessories, including t-shirts, sweatshirts, and hats.
  • Scandinavian gifts and household items from Ingebretsen’s in Minneapolis.
  • Handmade artisan goods from the Minnesota Center for Book Arts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bloomington

Minnesota is known for its friendly and hospitable people. Visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs and observe the following etiquette:

  • Use common courtesy and polite language at all times.
  • Remove your shoes when visiting someone’s home.
  • Do not be afraid to ask questions if you are unsure about something.
  • Respect all religious and cultural beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Bloomington

The best time to visit Bloomington is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm, and many outdoor activities are available. However, spring and fall are also great seasons to visit, with mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bloomington

Do:

  • Tip your service providers, including waitstaff, bartenders, and taxi drivers.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.
  • Be respectful of local customs and laws.
  • Try local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Walk alone in desolate areas at night.
  • Be loud or disruptive in quiet areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bloomington

Bloomington is a safe city to visit, with a low crime rate. However, visitors need to take normal precautions.

  • Keep valuables like passports and money in a safe place.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings while out at night.
  • Be aware of traffic when walking on streets.

Health in Bloomington

Visitors to Bloomington should ensure they are up-to-date on their routine vaccinations. It is recommended to consult with a travel medicine specialist before traveling. Bloomington has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in the city.

Getting to Bloomington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bloomington is conveniently located near many transportation options, making it easy to reach from anywhere within the United States.

Airports Near Bloomington

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport (MSP) is the closest airport and is conveniently located near Bloomington.
  • St Cloud Regional Airport (STC) is about 70 miles away, and Rochester International Airport (RST) is about 90 miles away.

    Bus Stations Near Bloomington

  • Greyhound Bus Lines offers regular bus services to Minneapolis, where you can take a local bus or taxi to Bloomington.

    Other Options

  • Metro Transit: The city of Bloomington is well-served by the Metro Transit system, which includes buses and the light rail network.

How to Get Around in Bloomington

Bloomington is easy to navigate, with many transportation options available. The best ones include:

  • Rental Cars: There are several rental car companies that operate in and around Bloomington.
  • Metro Transit: The Metro Transit system offers bus and light rail services to many parts of the city.
  • Taxis and Ridesharing Services: Taxis, Uber, and Lyft services are also available in Bloomington.

Where to Go Next from Bloomington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bloomington is surrounded by several nearby destinations and excursions to explore, including:

  • Minneapolis: A vibrant city located about 12 miles from Bloomington, offering many cultural and sporting events, parks, museums, and more.
  • St. Paul: The state capital, located about 20 miles from Bloomington, has many historical and cultural attractions and is also great for shopping.
  • Mankato: A charming college town, located about 90 miles from Bloomington, with many outdoor adventures and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Bloomington is an exciting destination for travelers, filled with beautiful nature, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and more. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Bloomington, ensuring that you make the most of your visit and explore everything this vibrant city has to offer.
