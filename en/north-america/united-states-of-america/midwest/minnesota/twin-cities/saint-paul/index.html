Introduction to Saint Paul

Saint Paul is a charming city located in the state of Minnesota, in the Midwest region of the United States. Home to an abundance of museums, cultural attractions, and outdoor recreational activities, Saint Paul attracts tourists from all over the world. The city is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and diverse culinary scene. If you’re planning a trip to Saint Paul, this travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know before your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Paul

Saint Paul is full of fascinating places to visit, each with its own unique charm and character. Here are some of the top attractions that you definitely shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Minnesota History Center is a museum that showcases the history, culture, and arts of the state. The museum features permanent exhibits, as well as rotating exhibits, that tell the story of Minnesota and its people.

  • The Science Museum of Minnesota is a great destination for families with kids. The museum features interactive exhibits, including a dinosaur exhibit, an alligator exhibit, and a hands-on science lab.

  • The Cathedral of Saint Paul is a stunning architectural masterpiece that boasts a rich history and serene beauty. Visitors can take a guided tour of the cathedral and admire its impressive stained-glass windows, marble columns, and intricate mosaics.

  • The James J Hill House is a historic mansion that once belonged to a prominent businessman in the early 20th century. The mansion is now a museum that offers guided tours of the opulent interiors and manicured gardens.

Natural Attractions of Saint Paul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Como Park Zoo and Conservatory is a popular destination for families with kids. The zoo features a wide variety of animals, including gorillas, giraffes, and polar bears, while the conservatory is home to a stunning collection of exotic plants.

  • The Mississippi National River and Recreation Area is a beautiful park that offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and boating. Visitors can also take a riverboat tour to admire the stunning landscapes and wildlife along the river.

  • Summit Avenue is a picturesque street that runs through the heart of Saint Paul’s historic district. This street is famous for its stunning Victorian mansions, charming gardens, and tree-lined sidewalks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Paul

Saint Paul is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the city:

  • The Gateway State Trail is a 18-mile trail that runs from St. Paul to Pine Point Regional Park. This trail is ideal for biking, with scenic views of the city and the Mississippi River.

  • The Mississippi River Trail is a 32-mile trail that runs along the banks of the Mississippi River. This trail is perfect for hiking and biking, with beautiful scenery and plenty of wildlife to observe.

  • The Bruce Vento Regional Trail is a 6-mile trail that runs through the city’s downtown area. This trail offers stunning views of the city’s landmarks and is perfect for a quick morning run or an afternoon bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Saint Paul: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for cultural activities, outdoor adventures, or unique experiences, Saint Paul has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Take a brewery tour of one of the city’s many craft breweries, such as Surly Brewing Co. or Summit Brewing Company.

  • Visit the Saint Paul Farmers’ Market, which is held every weekend and offers fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and homemade jams and jellies.

  • Catch a movie at the historic Fitzgerald Theater, which features classic films, live music performances, and other cultural events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint Paul

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Saint Paul, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in the city:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Minnesota History Center and the Science Museum of Minnesota
Day 2 Take a walking tour of the historic district and visit the Cathedral of Saint Paul
Day 3 Explore the Mississippi National River and Recreation Area
Day 4 Take a brewery tour and visit the Saint Paul Farmers’ Market

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Paul

Saint Paul has a diverse culinary scene, with plenty of restaurants, cafes, and bars to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juicy Lucy: This famous Minnesota classic is a cheeseburger with the cheese inside the patty. The 5-8 Club and Matt’s Bar are two iconic destinations for this local delicacy.

  • Wild Rice Soup: This delicious soup is a staple of many Minnesota restaurants, including The St. Paul Grill, which is known for its version of the soup.

  • Seafood Boil: The seafood boil at the Cajun Twist restaurant is a mouth-watering feast of corn, potatoes, and fresh seafood, drenched in spicy Cajun sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Summit EPA: This locally brewed beer is a fan favorite in Saint Paul. It has a light, refreshing taste and pairs well with most dishes.

  • Minnesota Mule: This local cocktail features vodka, ginger beer, and cranberry juice. It’s a refreshing drink that’s perfect for a summer day.

  • Spiked Cider: Saint Paul has several bars that serve up hot ciders, infused with your choice of whiskey, brandy, or rum.

Where to Stay in Saint Paul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint Paul has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some top choices:

  • The Saint Paul Hotel: This historic hotel is situated in the heart of downtown Saint Paul and offers elegant rooms, a fine-dining restaurant, and a fitness center.

  • The Covington Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located on a renovated paddleboat, moored on the Mississippi River. Each room has a private balcony with stunning views of the river.

  • The Hyatt Place Saint Paul/Downtown: This modern hotel is located in the city’s entertainment district and offers spacious rooms, free breakfast, and a rooftop bar.

What to Buy in Saint Paul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Saint Paul, here are some top options:

  • Wild Rice: This staple of Minnesota cuisine is a great option to bring back home. Look for it at the Saint Paul Farmers’ Market or at local grocery stores.

  • Scandinavian Crafts: Saint Paul has a strong Scandinavian heritage, and you can find plenty of traditional crafts and gifts at stores like Ingebretsen’s.

  • Local Art: Saint Paul has a vibrant arts scene, and you can find plenty of local artwork, pottery, and jewelry at galleries like The Grand Hand and Dow Art Galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Paul

Saint Paul is known for its friendly and welcoming culture. Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your visit:

  • Say “hello” or “hi” to locals you meet on the street or in shops. Minnesotans are known for their friendliness.

  • Don’t be afraid to try new foods, and don’t be surprised if you’re offered a “hotdish” (casserole).

  • Dress in layers, as the weather can change quickly. It’s not uncommon to experience all four seasons in one day.

Best Time to Visit Saint Paul

The best time to visit Saint Paul is between May and October when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events. The city is particularly lively during the summer months, with outdoor concerts, street festivals, and farmers’ markets to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint Paul

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Saint Paul:

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

  • Take advantage of the city’s outdoor activities, especially during the summer.

  • Try the local cuisine and beer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the city’s beautiful parks or public spaces.

  • Be rude or dismissive to locals or other tourists.

  • Visit during the winter unless you’re prepared for cold and snowy weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint Paul

Saint Paul is generally a safe city to visit, but it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take safety precautions, such as:

  • Keep your valuables close and out of sight.

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone.

  • Be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Saint Paul

Saint Paul has quality health care facilities and hospitals, including Regions Hospital and United Hospital. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance, as medical expenses can be costly in the United States.

Getting to Saint Paul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint Paul

The closest airport to Saint Paul is Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport (MSP), which is located about 10 miles from downtown Saint Paul. From the airport, you can take a taxi, Uber, or rental car to reach your destination in the city.

Bus Stations Near Saint Paul

If you’re traveling by bus, there are several stations located in or near Saint Paul, including the Metro Transit Union Depot and the Jefferson Lines Station.

Other Options

If you’re traveling by car, Saint Paul is easily accessible via the major highways and interstates in the area, including Interstate 35E and Interstate 94. You can also take a train to the Union Depot station in Saint Paul.

How to Get Around in Saint Paul

Saint Paul has a variety of transportation options, including public transit, taxis, and bike rentals. The easiest way to get around the city is by using the Metro Transit bus and light rail system, which covers most of the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Saint Paul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time during your visit to Saint Paul, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out:

  • Minneapolis: Just a short drive or train ride away from Saint Paul, Minneapolis offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining options.

  • Stillwater: This charming town on the banks of the St. Croix River is known for its historic downtown area, antique shops, and scenic river views.

  • Afton State Park: This beautiful state park is located just a few miles outside of Saint Paul and offers plenty of hiking trails, fishing, and camping opportunities.

Conclusion

Saint Paul is a wonderful destination that offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Saint Paul has something for everyone. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide, and have a wonderful time exploring all that this beautiful city has to offer.
