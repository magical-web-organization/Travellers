Introduction to Arden Hills

Located in the Twin Cities area of Minnesota, Arden Hills is one of Midwest’s hidden gems waiting to be explored. This serene suburban city offers plenty of attractions for all kinds of tourists, from nature enthusiasts to history buffs to foodies. With its abundance of parks, lakes, and trails, Arden Hills is an outdoor lover’s paradise. It is also home to several respected higher education institutions and boasts a culturally diverse community.

List of Places to Visit in Arden Hills

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Arden Hills that will give you a glimpse into the city’s history, culture, and natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hamline University – Established in 1854, Hamline is one of the oldest and most esteemed private universities in the region. The campus features impressive Gothic architecture and is home to several museums and art galleries.

  • Gideon S. Ives House – Built in 1854, this historic home is a beautiful example of Greek Revival architecture and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

  • The Museum of Russian Art – Displays a beautiful collection of Russian art dating back from the 17th century to contemporary times. The museum offers guided tours, concerts, and events year-round.

Natural Attractions of Arden Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Knox Jewelers Lake Johanna Beach – Features a beach and picnic area, biking and hiking trails, and a boat launch for non-motorized watercraft. The lake is great for fishing, swimming, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding.

  • Tony Schmidt Regional Park – A beautiful park featuring a playground, picnic shelters, volleyball, basketball, and tennis courts, and miles of hiking trails perfect for bird-watching and nature observation.

  • TCAAP Wildlife Management Area – A massive natural area encompassing over 2,000 acres of prairies, wetlands, and woodlands, the TCAAP WMA is an excellent place for hiking, birding, hunting, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arden Hills

Arden Hills offers plenty of trails and routes to explore the city’s sprawling natural beauty. Here are a few top routes to try out:

  • Rice Creek North Regional Trail – A beautiful 4.1-mile long trail that passes through wooded areas, marshes, and prairies and connects the cities of Shoreview and New Brighton.

  • Bennett Lake Trail – A scenic 2.6-mile route that takes you around Bennett Lake and through a wooded area filled with wildlife sightings.

  • TCAAP Trailhead – A 9.5-mile long trail that wanders through the beautiful TCAAP WMA and passes by several ponds, trails, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Arden Hills: Activities and Tours

Besides exploring the city’s natural beauty, Arden Hills offers plenty of activities that cater to all types of travelers.

  • Catch a show at the Mounds Theater – This historic theater features a range of performances, including live music, comedy shows, and plays.

  • Visit the Harvest Grill and Cellar – A popular local restaurant that serves delicious farm-to-table dishes made with locally sourced produce and freshly harvested herbs.

  • Go shopping at Rosedale Center – A sprawling mall filled with big-name brands, independent shops, and dining options.

Food and Drink Scene of Arden Hills

Arden Hills has a thriving food and drink scene that offers something for every taste bud.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juicy Lucy – This iconic Midwestern burger stuffed with cheese is a must-try.

  • Lefse – Made with potatoes, butter, and flour, Lefse is a traditional Norwegian flatbread often served with butter or jam.

  • Wild Rice Soup – A hearty soup made with local walleye fish and wild rice, which is native to the region.

Local Beverages

  • Surly Brewing Company – A popular brewery that offers a range of award-winning beers and ales like Furious and Bender.

  • Summit Brewing Company – The second largest brewery in Minnesota, Summit features classic and seasonal brews with accolades such as the Great American Beer Festival.

  • Two Rivers Vineyard & Winery – A local winery that offers a range of delicious wines made from grapes grown on their estate.

Where to Stay in Arden Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arden Hills has several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences.

  • Country Inn & Suites – A cozy and budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and amenities

  • Residence Inn – A stylish and upscale hotel that offers spacious suites and free breakfast.

  • Hilton Garden Inn – A modern and upscale hotel that offers well-appointed rooms, on-site dining, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Arden Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arden Hills is known for its unique local products and souvenirs that make for great gifts and mementos.

  • Wild rice – A staple of the region, wild rice is a nutrient-dense grain that has been harvested from Minnesota’s lakes and wetlands for hundreds of years.

  • Handcrafted pottery – Arden Hills is home to several skilled potters who make beautiful and intricate pottery that reflects the region’s natural beauty.

  • Maple syrup – Maple syrup lovers will want to sample the local syrup, crafted fresh each spring in the surrounding forests.

Local Culture and Customs of Arden Hills

Arden Hills is known for its friendly and laid-back culture. Locals are proud of their outdoorsy lifestyle and appreciate visitors who share their love of nature and outdoor activities.

Best Time to Visit Arden Hills

The best time to visit Arden Hills is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months offer long days and plenty of sunshine, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arden Hills

Do:

  • Respect the local wildlife and natural habitats.

  • Embrace the outdoorsy lifestyle and enjoy the city’s abundant parks and trails.

  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter! The locals in Arden Hills take pride in their clean and green community and expect visitors to do the same.

  • Don’t ignore the weather, especially when planning outdoor activities. Summers can get hot, while winters can be extremely cold.

  • Don’t forget to tip! In the USA, it’s customary to tip at least 15% of the total bill at restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arden Hills

Arden Hills is generally a safe place, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take basic precautions like locking their cars, not leaving valuables in plain view, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Arden Hills

Arden Hills has several hospitals and clinics that provide excellent healthcare services, so visitors need not worry about their health during their stay.

Getting to Arden Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arden Hills

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport – The primary airport in the region, located approximately 19 miles from Arden Hills.

  • St. Cloud Regional Airport – Located approximately 58 miles from Arden Hills, St. Cloud Regional Airport is a smaller airport that serves the region.

Bus Stations Near Arden Hills

  • Metro Transit – Provides bus service to and from the entire region, including Arden Hills.

Other Options

  • Driving – Arden Hills is easily accessible by car from major interstate highways in the area.

How to Get Around in Arden Hills

Arden Hills is a walkable and very bike-friendly city, with miles of well-maintained trails and bike lanes. It is also easy to get around by car or public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Arden Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Arden Hills might enjoy:

  • Minneapolis – Located just a short drive away, Minneapolis features a range of attractions, dining, and shopping options.

  • Saint Paul – A historic and charming city known for its beautiful architecture, museums, and parks.

  • Mall of America – One of the largest malls in the world, located 20 miles south of Arden Hills. Featuring hundreds of shops, restaurants, and entertainment options, the mall is a great place to spend a day.

Conclusion

Arden Hills is a hidden gem in the Midwest waiting to be discovered. The city offers something for everyone, from nature lovers to culture enthusiasts to foodies. With its well-maintained trails, beautiful parks, and historic landmarks, Arden Hills is a great place to spend a weekend or an entire week.
