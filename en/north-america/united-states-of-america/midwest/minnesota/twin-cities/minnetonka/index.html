Introduction to Minnetonka

Minnetonka is a city in Hennepin County, Minnesota, United States, located just southwest of Minneapolis and northwest of Saint Paul. It is a suburban city with a population of approximately 55,000 people. The city has a rich history, and it was named after the Dakota Sioux Princess “Tawasentha,” which means “prairie stretching out.” Here is a comprehensive tour guide that highlights the best places to visit, things to do, dining and accommodation options, local customs, and practical travel information you need to plan your trip to Minnetonka.

List of Places to Visit in Minnetonka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Minnetonka Center for the Arts

The Minnetonka Center for the Arts is an art gallery that showcases fine art exhibitions and provides art classes to people of all ages and skill levels. The center also hosts art events, workshops, and lectures.

Burwell House

Burwell House is a historical landmark and house museum located in the heart of Minnetonka. It was built-in 1862 and was home to the Burwell family for 112 years. Burwell House is open for guided tours and educational programs that explore the history of the Burwell family and early Minnetonka settlers.

The Hennepin Overland Railway Historical Society Museum

The Hennepin Overland Railway Historical Society Museum is a nonprofit museum that offers educational programs and exhibits about the history of railroading in Minnesota. The museum features an impressive collection of model trains, including the largest G-scale model train layout in the state.

Natural Attractions of Minnetonka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Minnetonka

Lake Minnetonka is the city’s biggest attraction, with more than 14,000 acres of water spreading across the western suburbs of Minneapolis and Minnetonka. The lake offers scenic boat tours, fishing, swimming, and various water sports activities.

Minnehaha Creek

Minnehaha Creek is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and picnicking. The creek is surrounded by beautiful scenery and runs 22 miles through the western suburbs of Minneapolis.

Noerenberg Memorial Gardens

Noerenberg Memorial Gardens is a beautiful botanical garden on the north shore of Lake Minnetonka. The garden features a collection of unique plants, gorgeous landscapes, and fountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Minnetonka

Minnetonka is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in the Midwest region. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

  • Luce Line Trail
  • Cedar Lake Regional Trail
  • Minnesota River Bottoms Trail
  • Lake Minnetonka Regional Trail

Best Things to Do in Minnetonka: Activities and Tours

Segway Tour

Explore the city and its surroundings in a unique way with a Segway Tour. There are different tour options available that take you through the city’s parks and beautiful neighborhoods.

Escape Rooms

Escape rooms have become a popular activity worldwide in recent years, and Minnetonka has some of the best ones in the state. Grab a group of friends or family and try to escape a puzzle room full of clues and riddles under time pressure.

Shopping

Minnetonka offers a wide range of shopping options. Browse the local boutiques, vintage stores, and shopping malls to find unique souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Minnetonka

Must-Try Dishes

Minnetonka is a melting pot of diverse cuisines. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Juicy Lucy Cheeseburger: A burger stuffed with cheese, a Minnesota classic.
  • Wild Rice Soup: A creamy soup made with Minnesota’s native wild rice.
  • Tater Tot Hotdish: A casserole dish made with tater tots, vegetables, and ground beef.

Local Beverages

Minnesota is known for its craft beer and delicious cocktails. Here are some of the local beverages you should try:

  • Grain Belt Premium: A classic beer brewed in Minneapolis and enjoyed throughout the state.
  • Surly Furious: An IPA from Surly Brewing Company located in Minneapolis.
  • Bootleg: A small-batch whiskey produced by Wander North, a craft distillery located in northeast Minneapolis.

Where to Stay in Minnetonka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Minnetonka offers a wide range of accommodation options, from affordable budget hotels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Sheraton Minneapolis West Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near Lake Minnetonka with a heated indoor pool and an on-site restaurant.
  • Holiday Inn Express and Suites: A modern hotel with complimentary breakfast located in the heart of Minnetonka.
  • Airbnb: A popular option for travelers who want to experience the city like a local. The platform offers a variety of options, from private rooms to entire houses.

What to Buy in Minnetonka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Minnetonka offers a variety of local souvenirs and products that are perfect to bring back home. Here are some of the best shops to visit:

  • Tonka Bay Marina: A store that offers Minnetonka-themed clothing, accessories, and home decor.
  • General Store of Minnetonka: A gift shop that specializes in unique souvenirs and locally made products such as artisanal food, home decor, jewelry, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Minnetonka

Minnetonka residents value a laid-back and friendly lifestyle, with a strong sense of community. The people are welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs. Respect the environment, stay active, and embrace Minnesota’s outdoors is essential in Minnetonka’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Minnetonka

The best time to visit Minnetonka is between May and September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The summer months are peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds and higher hotel rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Minnetonka

Do:

  • Respect the environment and keep the city clean.
  • Embrace the local culture and customs.
  • Say hello and smile at locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the city’s flora & fauna.
  • Assume that everyone knows each other.
  • Ignore mandatory safety measures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Minnetonka

Minnetonka is considered a safe city. However, it is essential to remain vigilant, especially in high-traffic tourist areas.

Health in Minnetonka

Minnetonka is home to several quality health care facilities and hospitals. It is recommended to have travel insurance before visiting Minnetonka.

Getting to Minnetonka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Minnetonka

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport: The main airport serving the city, located approximately 20 minutes from Minnetonka.

Bus Stations Near Minnetonka

  • Minnesota Valley Transit Authority (MVTA): A bus service that connects the suburbs to Minneapolis and St. Paul.

Other Options

  • Driving: Minnetonka is easily accessible by car, with several highways leading to and from the city.
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft are available in Minnetonka.

How to Get Around in Minnetonka

The best way to get around Minnetonka is by car, either by renting one or using taxi services. However, public transportation and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available.

Where to Go Next from Minnetonka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Minneapolis: The largest city in the state, full of vibrant cultural experiences and attractions.
  • Saint Paul: The capital city of Minnesota, known for its history, museums, and shopping.
  • Afton State Park: A scenic park featuring hiking, camping, and cross-country skiing trails.

Conclusion

Minnetonka is a beautiful and welcoming city that offers plenty of things to do for visitors of all ages. Its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and friendly community make it an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Minnesota. Plan your trip using this comprehensive tour guide and have a fantastic time exploring Minnetonka.
