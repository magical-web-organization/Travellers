Introduction to Hopkins

Nestled in the heart of Twin Cities, Hopkins is a charming city with a rich history and vibrant culture. Set against the backdrop of rolling hills, Hopkins boasts a mix of natural beauty and urban amenities that make it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic Minnesota experience. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, Hopkins has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hopkins

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hopkins has a rich history that is reflected in its numerous cultural attractions, such as museums and historical sites. Visitors can tour the Hopkins Historical Society Museum, which showcases the city’s history from its founding in the 19th century to the present day. Other must-see cultural attractions include the Art Center, which features rotating exhibitions of contemporary art, and the Stages Theatre, which hosts live performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Hopkins: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those looking to experience the great outdoors, Hopkins offers a variety of natural attractions. The city is home to several parks, including the expansive Shady Oak Beach Park, which has hiking trails, beach access, and picnic areas. Visitors can also explore the nearby Minnehaha Creek Watershed, which features scenic views and nature trails, or take a short drive to the scenic Lake Minnetonka for fishing, boating and other water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hopkins

Hopkins is a walker’s and cyclist’s paradise, with miles of scenic routes throughout the city. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Hopkins include the Lake Minnetonka LRT Regional Trail, which connects Hopkins to the surrounding area, and the Hopkins Mainstreet Greenway, which features scenic views of the city center.

Best Things to Do in Hopkins: Activities and Tours

In addition to its cultural and natural attractions, Hopkins offers a variety of fun and engaging activities for visitors of all ages. Some of the best things to do in Hopkins include taking a brewery tour at the renowned LTD Brewing Company, catching a flick at the historic Mann Hopkins Cinema 6, or shopping for unique gifts and souvenirs at the bustling Mainstreet Marketplace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hopkins

Day 1: Tour the Hopkins Historical Society Museum and the Art Center.

Day 2: Enjoy a day of hiking and exploring the Minnehaha Creek Watershed or visit the Shady Oak Beach Park.

Day 3: Take a brewery tour at the LTD Brewing Company and sample some of the city’s best local beers.

Day 4: Check out the local foodie scene with a visit to some of Hopkins’ top restaurants, including the popular Thai Table restaurant.

Day 5: Go on a shopping spree at the Mainstreet Marketplace or explore the city’s boutiques and artisanal stores.

Day 6: Attend a live performance at the Stages Theatre or catch a flick at the Mann Hopkins Cinema 6.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the city’s spas or wellness centers, such as the nearby Spa of the Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Hopkins

Must-Try Dishes

Hopkins is home to a diverse culinary scene, with a variety of restaurants and cafes offering everything from classic American fare to international cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes in Hopkins include the Rasta Stew at the popular Jamaican eatery Pimento Kitchen, the award-winning pizzas at Punch Pizza, and the delicious Pad Thai at Thai Table.

Local Beverages

Hopkins is also known for its thriving craft beer scene, with several local breweries offering a variety of delicious beers and ales. Visitors can sample some of the city’s best craft beers at LTD Brewing Company, Sisyphus Brewing, or the Hopkins-based Nordic Brew Works. For non-alcoholic beverages, be sure to try the signature Pomegranate Hibiscus Lemonade at the popular cafe, Muddsuckers Coffee.

Where to Stay in Hopkins: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hopkins offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top-rated hotels in the city include the Residence Inn Minneapolis Eden Prairie, the Springhill Suites Minneapolis West/St. Louis Park, and the Best Western Eden Prairie Inn. For a more budget-friendly option, consider staying at the Hopkins Inn or the nearby AirBnbs.

What to Buy in Hopkins: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Hopkins has a wide range of unique local products to choose from. Some of the best options include handmade jewelry from the Mainstreet Marketplace, artisanal cheese from the nearby Tonkadale Market, or a bottle of locally-brewed craft beer to take home as a memento.

Local Culture and Customs of Hopkins

Hopkins is a friendly and welcoming community that celebrates its diverse cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to explore the city’s unique customs and traditions, such as the annual Hopkins Raspberry Festival, which takes place every July and features a parade, live music, and other fun activities.

Best Time to Visit Hopkins

The best time to visit Hopkins is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city is buzzing with street festivals, outdoor concerts, and other fun events. However, visitors can also enjoy Hopkins’ natural beauty and cultural attractions throughout the year, with fall and spring offering colorful foliage and mild weather, and winter providing plenty of opportunities for snow sports and holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hopkins

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the city’s many cultural and natural attractions.
  • Do try the delicious local cuisine, including the city’s famous craft beer offerings.
  • Do follow local customs and traditions, such as the annual Hopkins Raspberry Festival.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the city’s vibrant arts and entertainment scene, including live music and theater performances.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring the city’s many trails and parks.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations and advice on where to eat, shop, and explore in Hopkins.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hopkins

Hopkins is a safe and friendly city, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors should still take precautions, however, such as securing valuables and being aware of their surroundings when exploring the city after dark.

Health in Hopkins

Hopkins is home to several excellent healthcare facilities, including the nearby Park Nicollet Methodist Hospital and the PrairieCare Medical Group. Visitors should also take standard health precautions, such as staying hydrated, using sunscreen, and practicing good hand hygiene.

Getting to Hopkins: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hopkins

Hopkins is conveniently located near several major airports, including the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport, which is just a 20-minute drive from the city. Other nearby airports include the Flying Cloud Airport and the Crystal Airport.

Bus Stations Near Hopkins

Hopkins is well-served by public transportation, with several bus routes serving the city center and surrounding areas. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s convenient bike-sharing program, which makes it easy to explore the city’s many trails and bike paths.

How to Get Around in Hopkins

Hopkins is a walkable and bike-friendly city, with miles of scenic trails and bike paths throughout the city center and surrounding areas. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s public transportation options, such as bus and light rail lines, or rent a car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Hopkins: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hopkins is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in Minnesota, making it the perfect starting point for a road trip or off-the-beaten-path adventure. Some of the best nearby excursions include exploring the nearby Lake Minnetonka area, shopping and dining in nearby Minneapolis, or heading north to visit Duluth and the scenic North Shore of Lake Superior.

Conclusion

Hopkins is a hidden gem in the heart of Minnesota, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and urban amenities. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s rich history, sample its delicious cuisine, or simply soak up its friendly small-town charm, Hopkins is the perfect destination for your next Midwestern adventure.
