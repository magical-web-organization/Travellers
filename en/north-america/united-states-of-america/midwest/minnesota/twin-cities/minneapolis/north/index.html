Introduction to North

Welcome to North, the vibrant neighborhood in the heart of Minneapolis! This area is a culturally-rich, historic district where you can explore a plethora of museums, architectural landmarks, and natural wonders. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, North has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in North

Here’s a list of the top attractions to visit during your trip to North:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North is home to a myriad of cultural attractions that will enrich your understanding of the city’s history. Here are some must-visit museums and historical places in North:

  • Minnesota African American Heritage Museum and Gallery: This museum displays the contributions of the African American community to the state of Minnesota. The exhibits showcase the histories of civil rights pioneers, musicians, athletes, and other prominent figures.
  • Hennepin History Museum: This museum is located in the former mansion of George Christian, a local businessman. You can learn about the social and cultural history of Hennepin County from the early 19th century to the present.
  • Elmer L. Andersen Library: This library and gallery display an extensive collection of rare books, maps, photographs, and other archival materials.
  • Minneapolis Farmers Market: This year-round market features fresh produce, flowers, artisanal products, and local souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North has plenty of picturesque parks and recreation centers where you can soak up nature. Here are some of the top natural attractions in North:

  • Theodore Wirth Regional Park: This vast park covers over 750 acres and includes a golf course, hiking trails, a beach, and picnic areas.
  • North Mississippi Regional Park: This park has miles of walking trails, a boat launch, a fishing pier, and a swimming pool.
  • Webber Park: This park features a community center, a golf course, an archery range, and a spray pool for kids.
  • Plymouth Avenue Bridge: This historical bridge offers scenic views of the Mississippi River and connects North Minneapolis to downtown.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

North has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the Twin Cities area. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Grand Rounds Scenic Byway: This 50-mile route takes you through seven of Minneapolis’s major park areas, including Theodore Wirth Regional Park and Minnehaha Park.
  • Luce Line State Trail: This 63-mile trail runs through the western suburbs of Minneapolis and offers picturesque views of rolling hills, prairies, and woods.
  • Riverfront Trail: This trail runs along the Mississippi River and connects downtown Minneapolis to North Mississippi Regional Park.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in arts and culture or outdoor adventures, North has plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in North:

  • Northside Art Walk: This walking tour showcases the vibrant art scene in North and allows you to meet local artists and gallery owners.
  • Northside Summer Jam: This annual festival features live music, food and drink vendors, and a car show.
  • Fireworks at Webber Park: This community event features a fireworks display, live music, and family-friendly activities.
  • RiverWalk Yoga: This outdoor yoga class takes place on the banks of the Mississippi River and offers stunning views of the water and surrounding parks.

Food and Drink Scene of North

North offers a diverse range of culinary delights, from soul food to fusion cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jucy Lucy: This iconic Minneapolis burger features a juicy, cheese-filled patty and is a cult favorite among locals.
  • Fried Chicken: Head to Breaking Bread Cafe for some of the best fried chicken in town.
  • Walleye: This freshwater fish is a Minnesota delicacy and can be found on many local menus.
  • Wild Rice Soup: This creamy soup is a staple of Midwestern cuisine and is a must-try for first-time visitors.

Local Beverages

  • Surly Brewing Company: This local brewery is a popular spot for beer enthusiasts, with a wide variety of craft beers on tap.
  • Indeed Brewing Company: This brewery offers a laid-back atmosphere and a diverse selection of beers, including seasonal and limited releases.
  • Caffetto Coffee Shop: This cozy cafe serves up a mean cup of coffee and offers live music and art shows on occasion.
  • Tattersall Distilling: This craft distillery specializes in unique spirits, such as aquavit and gin, and offers tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top places to stay in North:

  • The Freehouse: This modern gastropub has a rooftop patio and stylish guest rooms with upscale amenities.
  • Hyatt Regency Minneapolis: This luxury hotel boasts spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Loews Minneapolis Hotel: This sleek and stylish hotel offers stunning skyline views, a fitness center, and a coffee shop in the lobby.
  • Minneapolis International Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorm-style rooms and private rooms, and is located in a historic building in North Loop.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring back a unique souvenir from North, check out these local products:

  • Wild Rice: This indigenous grain is a Minnesota specialty and can be found in many local markets.
  • Local Artwork: North has a vibrant arts scene, with many galleries and studios selling unique artwork and crafts.
  • Bikes: Minneapolis has a strong bike culture, and North Loop has several bike shops where you can buy or rent a bike to explore the city.

Local Culture and Customs of North

Minneapolitans are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you. The city has a laid-back atmosphere and a thriving arts and culture scene, with many residents passionate about the local food and music scenes.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the parks and museums are in full swing. However, fall and spring can also be pleasant times to visit, with colorful foliage and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine at the Minneapolis Farmers Market and sample a Jucy Lucy burger at one of the city’s many bars and restaurants.
  • Do explore North Mississippi Regional Park and the other parks and recreation centers in the area.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t overlook the city’s thriving arts scene, with many galleries and music venues featuring local talent.
  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and other outdoor essentials.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals, who are known for their friendly and welcoming nature.

Safety and Health

Safety in North

As with any urban area, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, stay in well-lit areas, and keep valuables out of sight. Call 911 in case of emergency.

Health in North

North has several clinics and hospitals within easy reach, including Hennepin County Medical Center and North Memorial Health Hospital. Bring a first-aid kit and any prescription medications you may need during your trip.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport is the closest airport to North, with regular flights from domestic and international destinations. The airport is located about 20 minutes south of the city center and is easily accessible by public transportation or taxi.

Bus Stations Near North

The main bus stations serving Minneapolis are the Greyhound Station and the Megabus Station, both located in downtown. From there, you can take a local bus or taxi to reach North.

Other Options

If you’re driving, North is easily accessible from major highways, including I-94 and I-35W. Parking is generally available on the street, or you can use one of the many parking ramps in the area.

How to Get Around in North

North is a walkable neighborhood with plenty of bike lanes and trails. You can also take a local bus or use a ride-sharing service like Uber or Lyft. Renting a bike is another popular way to explore the area, with many bike rental shops located in the city.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Minneapolis, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Chain of Lakes: This series of interconnecting lakes in Minneapolis is a popular destination for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Mall of America: This enormous shopping and entertainment complex is located about 15 minutes south of Minneapolis and offers over 500 stores, an amusement park, and an aquarium.
  • St. Paul: This nearby city is home to the Minnesota State Capitol, the Science Museum of Minnesota, and the Cathedral of Saint Paul.
  • Mississippi River Cruises: Several companies offer scenic boat tours of the Mississippi River, with departures from many points along the riverfront in Minneapolis.

Conclusion

North is a delightful neighborhood in the heart of Minneapolis, offering a mix of history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking to explore museums and galleries, hike through scenic parks, or sample the local cuisine, North has plenty to offer. Plan your trip today and discover this vibrant destination for yourself.
