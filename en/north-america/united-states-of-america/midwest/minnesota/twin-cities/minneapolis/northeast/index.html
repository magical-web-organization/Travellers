Introduction to Northeast

Located in the Twin Cities region in Minnesota, Northeast is a neighborhood that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities. This bustling area is known for its breweries, art galleries, and trendy restaurants, but it also has plenty of green spaces and historical landmarks worth visiting. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, places to stay, food scene, and local culture and customs of Northeast.

List of Places to Visit in Northeast

Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical sites, taking in scenic views, or trying out outdoor adventures, Northeast has something for everyone. Here are the top places to visit in Northeast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Soap Factory With its innovative contemporary art exhibitions and immersive installations, the Soap Factory is a must-visit for art enthusiasts. The Soap Factory also regularly hosts events and workshops for artists and the community.

  • The Museum of Russian Art One of the only museums in North America dedicated to Russian art and culture, the Museum of Russian Art has an extensive collection of paintings, sculptures, and textiles, as well as regular events and educational programs.

  • The Hennepin History Museum This local history museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the heritage and stories of Hennepin County and the surrounding region. From pop culture memorabilia to vintage clothing and textiles, the Hennepin History Museum offers a unique window into Twin Cities history.

Natural Attractions of Northeast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mississippi Riverfront The Mississippi Riverfront offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city with trails for biking and walking, scenic lookout points, and riverboat tours.

  • Minneapolis Sculpture Garden Home to more than 40 sculptures by world-renowned artists, the Minneapolis Sculpture Garden is a must-see for art and nature lovers alike. The garden is open year-round and admission is always free.

  • Quarry Park and Nature Preserve Quarry Park and Nature Preserve is a scenic destination for hiking, rock-climbing, and swimming. This park also features unique geological formations and trails that offer scenic views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northeast

Northeast has plenty of trails for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Midtown Greenway The Midtown Greenway is a 5.5-mile trail that runs along an old railroad corridor. This trail is popular with commuters and walkers alike and is known for its scenic views and easy access to local restaurants, shops, and attractions.

  • Mississippi River Trail Running parallel to the Mississippi River, the Mississippi River Trail offers stunning views of the river and its surrounding landscape. This trail is over 3,000 miles long and passes through several states, making it a great option for longer bike trips.

  • Theodore Wirth Parkway This parkway features over 13 miles of trails that are perfect for a leisurely bike ride or hike. The landscape offers a mix of woodlands, wetlands, and meadows, making it a diverse and scenic destination.

Best Things to Do in Northeast: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in beer tasting, art tours, or outdoor adventures, Northeast has plenty of activities to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Northeast:

  • Northeast Minneapolis Art District This vibrant district is home to numerous art galleries, studios, and public art installations. Take a self-guided tour of the district or join a guided tour to learn more about the local art scene.

  • Brewery Tours Northeast is home to several breweries, including Surly Brewing Co., Dangerous Man Brewing Co., and Indeed Brewing Co. Take a brewery tour to learn about the brewing process and sample some of the locally-made beers.

  • Escape Rooms Looking for a fun and interactive challenge? Try an escape room adventure at Riddle Room or Escape Plan.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Northeast, here’s a sample itinerary to get you started:

Food and Drink Scene of Northeast

Northeast is known for its vibrant and diverse food scene, with plenty of options for foodies of all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juicy Lucy A local favorite, the Juicy Lucy is a cheeseburger with a twist. Encased in the patty, it’s an explosion of melted cheese goodness that’s sure to satisfy your cravings.

  • Hot Dish A staple of Midwest cuisine, Hot Dish is a one-dish meal that typically includes meat, vegetables, and a starch like potatoes or pasta, all topped with a layer of crispy tater tots.

  • Smoked Fish With a thriving fishing community nearby, smoked fish is a must-try local specialty. Try smoked salmon or trout at one of the local markets or restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer Northeast is home to several breweries that offer a wide variety of craft beers. Try a Surly Furious or an Indeed Day Tripper for some local flavor.

  • Vikre Distillery Spirits Based in nearby Duluth, Vikre Distillery produces a range of artisanal spirits, including gin, whiskey, and aquavit. Sample some of their locally-made drinks at a local bar or liquor store.

Where to Stay in Northeast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From upscale hotels to cozy guesthouses, Northeast has plenty of lodging options to fit any budget and preference. Here are some top recommendations:

  • The Hewing Hotel This chic and stylish hotel is located in the heart of the historic North Loop neighborhood and features luxurious amenities, a rooftop bar, and stunning skyline views.

  • The Aster Bed and Breakfast This charming bed and breakfast is located in a historic 1888 mansion and offers cozy rooms, homemade breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.

  • Minneapolis International Hostel Perfect for budget travelers, this hostel offers affordable dorm rooms and private rooms, as well as free breakfast and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Northeast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or locally-made product to bring home? Here are some of the top options:

  • Local Art Visit the Northeast Minneapolis Art District to find unique paintings, prints, sculptures, and other artworks by local artists.

  • Midwest-Made Goods From artisanal jams and cheeses to handmade crafts and jewelry, you’ll find plenty of locally-made products and souvenirs at the numerous markets and boutiques throughout Northeast.

Local Culture and Customs of Northeast

Northeast has a laid-back and friendly culture that values community, creativity, and innovation. Here are some key customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Be Polite Minnesotans are famously polite and friendly. Say “please” and “thank you,” hold doors open for others, and greet people with a smile.

  • Respect for Nature With its many natural attractions and outdoor activities, Northeast values conservation and environmentalism. Be mindful of your waste, follow park rules, and leave no trace.

Best Time to Visit Northeast

The best time to visit Northeast depends on your interests and preferences. Summers are warm and sunny, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities, festivals, and events. Winters can be cold and snowy, but also offer unique winter sports and activities. Spring and fall offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds, making it a great time to enjoy the changing seasons and fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northeast

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Northeast:

Do:

  • Be Respectful Respect local customs and traditions, as well as the privacy of locals and other visitors.

  • Try Local Cuisine Northeast is known for its delicious and unique food scene. Don’t be afraid to try new dishes and flavors.

Don’t:

  • Litter Keep Northeast tidy and beautiful by throwing away trash properly and not littering.

  • Be Loud and Disruptive Northeast values peace and community, so be mindful of your noise level and avoid disruptive behavior.

Safety and Health

Here are some important safety and health considerations to keep in mind during your visit to Northeast:

Safety in Northeast

  • Stay Alert As with any city, it’s important to stay vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

  • Use Public Transportation Public transportation in Northeast is safe and affordable, making it a great option for getting around.

Health in Northeast

  • Stay Hydrated Minnesota summers can be hot and humid, so it’s important to stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

  • Protect Yourself from Mosquitoes In the summer months, mosquitoes can be a nuisance and carry diseases. Bring mosquito repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to protect yourself.

Getting to Northeast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northeast is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Northeast:

  • Airports Near Northeast The Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport is the closest and most convenient airport to Northeast, with plenty of transportation options available to get you to your destination.

  • Bus Stations Near Northeast Greyhound Bus Lines and MegaBus offer bus service to Minneapolis, with several stops in or near Northeast.

  • Other Options Amtrak does not have a station in Minneapolis, but there are several nearby stations in other cities. You can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service to get to Northeast.

How to Get Around in Northeast

Northeast is a walkable and bike-friendly neighborhood, with plenty of bike lanes and trails available. It’s also easy to get around by public transportation, with several bus routes and light rail options available.

Where to Go Next from Northeast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for nearby destinations and excursions, here are some top recommendations:

  • Downtown Minneapolis Located just across the river, downtown Minneapolis offers plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, as well as several cultural attractions like the Walker Art Center and the Guthrie Theater.

  • Chain of Lakes Just a short drive or bike ride from Northeast, the Chain of Lakes offer beautiful scenery and plenty of outdoor activities like swimming, boating, and fishing.

  • St. Paul Minnesota’s other Twin City, St. Paul, is just a short drive or light rail ride away. St. Paul offers a unique blend of Midwestern charm and vibrant culture, with plenty of historic landmarks, museums, and events.

Conclusion

Northeast is an exciting and vibrant neighborhood that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and plenty of food and drink. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking trails, or trying locally-made beers, Northeast has something for every traveler.
