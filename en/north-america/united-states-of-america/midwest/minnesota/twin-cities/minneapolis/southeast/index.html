Introduction to Southeast

Southeast is a vibrant neighborhood in Minneapolis, located in the Twin Cities area of Minnesota. It is a cultural hub with a diverse population, offering a variety of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. Southeast is home to the University of Minnesota, making it an energetic and lively place. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, and more in Southeast Minneapolis.

List of Places to Visit in Southeast

Southeast is a beautiful neighborhood with plenty of things to see and do. Here is a list of some of the most popular attractions in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Weisman Art Museum – located on the University of Minnesota campus, this museum boasts over 24,000 works of art dating back to ancient times.
  • Mill City Museum – the museum showcases the city’s history, including the flour milling industry that powered the region.
  • Guthrie Theater – a world-renowned theater known for its productions of Shakespeare and contemporary plays.

Natural Attractions of Southeast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mississippi National River and Recreation Area – visitors can enjoy hiking and biking trails, fishing, and stunning views of the Mississippi River.
  • Minnehaha Regional Park – this park features a stunning waterfall, hiking trails, and picnic areas for visitors to enjoy.
  • Stone Arch Bridge – an iconic pedestrian and bicycle bridge over the Mississippi, offering stunning views of the river and city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southeast

Southeast Minneapolis is known for its scenic routes for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Grand Rounds Scenic Byway – a 50-mile scenic route that encircles the city and includes beautiful parks and lakes.
  • Mississippi River Trail – a 3,000-mile trail that follows the Mississippi River from its headwaters in Minnesota to the Gulf of Mexico.
  • Midtown Greenway – a 5.5-mile paved trail that runs parallel to Lake Street and connects several neighborhoods in Minneapolis.

Best Things to Do in Southeast: Activities and Tours

Southeast has a plethora of activities to offer visitors. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Minnesota Streetcar Museum – a historic streetcar museum where visitors can ride vintage streetcars through the neighborhood.
  • Best Brewery Tours – Southeast Minneapolis has a plethora of breweries, including Surly Brewing Company, which offers brewery tours and tastings.
  • Food Tours – Southeast is known for its diverse food scene. Some popular tours include Taste Twin Cities and Secret Food Tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southeast

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help visitors make the most of their trip to Southeast:

Day Activities
Day 1 Check out the Weisman Art Museum and walk around the University of Minnesota campus
Day 2 Visit the Mill City Museum in the morning and take a stroll through St. Anthony Main for lunch and drinks
Day 3 Spend the morning hiking at Minnehaha Regional Park and the Stone Arch Bridge. In the evening, check out a show at the Guthrie Theater.
Day 4 Rent bikes and take a ride through Grand Rounds Scenic Byway
Day 5 Enjoy a brewery tour at Surly Brewing Company
Day 6 Explore the Mississippi National River and Recreation Area
Day 7 Take a food tour to experience the diverse local cuisine of Southeast

Food and Drink Scene of Southeast

Southeast Minneapolis offers a variety of dining options, ranging from trendy gastropubs to ethnic eateries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jucy Lucy – a Minneapolis classic, this cheese-stuffed burger can be found on menus throughout the city.
  • Hotdish – a classic Minnesota casserole often made with tater tots and ground beef.
  • Walleye – a freshwater fish commonly found in Minnesota’s lakes, and is a must-try if you love seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Surly Furious IPA – a bold and hoppy brew from the always popular Surly Brewing Company.
  • Grape Ape – a cocktail made from grape soda and vodka – a childhood favorite meets adult beverages.
  • Indeed Brewing Mexican Honey Light – a refreshing beer with a hint of honey that is perfect during the summer.

Where to Stay in Southeast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southeast offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some popular places to stay:

  • Graduate Minneapolis – a stylish hotel on the University of Minnesota campus.
  • Aloft Minneapolis – located near the Guthrie Theater, this trendy hotel offers an on-site bar and lounge.
  • University Inn – affordable and comfortable accommodation located just a few blocks from the University of Minnesota campus.

What to Buy in Southeast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southeast Minneapolis offers a variety of unique and locally-made products that make for great souvenirs. Here are some things to consider buying:

  • Minnesota-made Hotdish kit – contains all the essentials for making your own hotdish at home.
  • Minneapolis Skyline print – celebrate your trip to Southeast with a piece of artwork featuring the iconic Minneapolis skyline.
  • Surly Brewing Company merch – a great way to take home a slice of the Twin Cities’ famous local craft beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Southeast

Minneapolis is known for its unique local culture, including a love for art, food, and outdoor activities. Minnesotans are known to be friendly and welcoming to visitors. It’s also important to know that this region has on average 3 months of the coldest weather of any major American city - so bundle up in the winter months!

Best Time to Visit Southeast

The best time to visit Southeast Minneapolis is in the summer (June-August) when the weather is warm and the city is alive with outdoor activities. Fall (September-November) also offers beautiful foliage and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southeast

Do:

  • Do explore the neighborhood by foot or bike.
  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do visit the various museums and cultural landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to dress in layers during the winter months.
  • Don’t forget to try the Jucy Lucy – a Minneapolis classic.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waitstaff – tipping is customary in Minneapolis.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southeast

Southeast Minneapolis is generally a safe neighborhood to visit. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions such as not walking alone at night.

Health in Southeast

Visitors to Southeast should also be aware of the risk of Lyme disease, which can be contracted through tick bites while hiking. Be sure to use insect repellent and check yourself for ticks after spending time outdoors.

Getting to Southeast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southeast

Minneapolis−Saint Paul International Airport is the closest airport to Southeast Minneapolis. It is located just a 20-minute drive from the neighborhood.

Bus Stations Near Southeast

The Megabus station is located in downtown Minneapolis, about 2 miles from Southeast. Greyhound buses also run regularly to and from the Minneapolis station, located just a few miles from Southeast.

Other Options

Southeast Minneapolis is also accessible via light rail through the Green Line, which runs through the neighborhood.

How to Get Around in Southeast

The best way to get around Southeast Minneapolis is by foot, bike, or public transportation. The city is bike-friendly with several bike rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Southeast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to spare, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Saint Paul – Minnesota’s capital city is just a 20-minute drive from Minneapolis.
  • Minneapolis Chain of Lakes – a beautiful chain of lakes in the heart of the city.
  • Mall of America – just a 15-minute drive from Southeast, it is the largest mall in the United States with over 500 stores.

Conclusion

Southeast Minneapolis is a vibrant and exciting neighborhood full of culture, history, and outdoor adventure. With this comprehensive tour guide in hand, visitors will be well-equipped to make the most of their trip to Southeast. So pack your bags, and we’ll see you in Minneapolis!
