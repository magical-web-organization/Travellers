Introduction to South Minneapolis

Ranked as one of America’s most attractive cities, Minneapolis has a lot to offer to its visitors. Twin Cities is a metropolitan area comprising Minneapolis and the neighboring Saint Paul. South Minneapolis, located in Twin Cities, Minnesota, is a diverse and welcoming community with a significant number of cultural and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis has something to offer to everyone, from culturally important attractions to natural scenic locations.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Minneapolis has a vast array of museums and historical places that provide an insight into the city’s rich history and culture.

  • Minnehaha Park: One of the oldest and most famous parks in Minnesota, Minnehaha Park, is located at the confluence of the Mississippi River and Minnehaha Creek. Visitors can witness the famous 53-foot waterfall, which inspired Longfellow’s epic poem, “Song of Hiawatha.”
  • The Museum of Russian Art: The Museum of Russian Art is dedicated to Russian art, iconography, history, and culture. It features a collection of over 16,000 objects and is the only museum dedicated to Russian art outside of Russia.
  • Paisley Park: Paisley Park is the former home and recording studio of Prince, a Minnesota native, and musical legend. Visitors can take a guided tour of the facility and explore the artist’s costume collection, recording studios, and personal effects.
  • Wells Fargo History Museum: Located in the downtown area, the Wells Fargo History Museum is a journey through the company’s history, its stagecoach heritage, and Minnesota’s early frontier days.

Natural Attractions of South Minneapolis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love outdoor activities and picturesque scenery, South Minneapolis will leave you speechless.

  • Lake Harriet: One of the 10,000 lakes of Minnesota, Lake Harriet is a scenic location where visitors can enjoy biking, sailing, and fishing. The lake offers a serene environment with beautiful sunsets.
  • Fort Snelling State Park: Located at the confluence of the Mississippi and Minnesota Rivers, Fort Snelling State Park is a mix of history and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the fort’s history and enjoy hiking, birdwatching, picnicking, and fishing in the park.
  • Minnehaha Falls Regional Park: Another famous park in Minneapolis, Minnehaha Falls Regional Park, is a magnificent 167-acre park. This park features a stunning 53-foot waterfall, limestone bluffs, and limestone caves.
  • Chain of Lakes Regional Park: Chain of Lakes Regional Park comprises five lakes in Minneapolis, Lake of the Isles, Cedar Lake, Lake Calhoun, Brownie Lake, and Lake Harriet. Visitors can indulge in outdoor activities like swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Stone Arch Bridge: The Stone Arch Bridge offers a scenic view of the Mississippi River with an enchanting view of Saint Anthony Falls and the Downtown Minneapolis skyline. Visitors can walk or bike on the bridge and witness the beautiful natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes in South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis is also a perfect location for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Here are some hiking/biking routes to explore in the area.

  • Minnehaha Parkway Trail: Spanning over 51 miles, the Minnehaha Parkway Trail is a picturesque trail that starts from the Mississippi River, flows through Minnehaha Regional Park, and ends at the Chain of Lakes. The trail offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cityscape.
  • Midtown Greenway: Midtown Greenway is a 5.5-mile biking and walking trail built on an abandoned railroad corridor. This trail links the Chain of Lakes to the Mississippi River, and it is dedicated to pedestrian and bicycle activities.
  • Luce Line State Trail: Luce Line State Trail is a 59-mile trail that runs from Plymouth to Cosmo Park. This trail is a mixed-use trail for bikers, horse riders, and hikers. The Luce Line State Trail allows visitors to witness beautiful natural scenery, including the Cedar Lake, grasslands, and oak savannas.

Best Things to Do in South Minneapolis: Activities and Tours

Apart from the culturally rich museums and natural scenic places, South Minneapolis offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some must-try activities while visiting:

  • Minnesota Brewery Tours: Minnesota Brewery Tours offer a comprehensive beer-tasting experience of Minneapolis’s finest breweries. They offer guided tours on a bus along with samples of locally brewed beer.
  • Segway Tours: If you want to explore the city in a fun and eco-friendly way, then a segway tour is a perfect choice. Various Segway companies offer city tours of Minneapolis.
  • Guthrie Theater: Guthrie Theater is a classic theater in Downtown with great architecture and performances. Visitors can watch classic movies, plays, and musicals in the three different stages there.
  • Eat Street Food Tour: Eat Street is the modest and less touristy street with fantastic food options. The Eat Street Food Tour offers visitors a chance to eat some of the best indigenous food.
  • Escape Rooms: The majority of the escape rooms in Minneapolis offer team challenges, Crack the Code, and hints to escape a specific scenario in a fun and interactive manner.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Minneapolis

To get the best out of South Minneapolis, here is a week-long itinerary you can follow:

  • Day 1: Begin your trip by visiting Minnehaha Regional Park and its famous 53-foot waterfall. Afterward, visit the Museum of Russian Art to explore the rich culture of Russia.
  • Day 2: Visit the Stone Arch Bridge and the Guthrie Theater to experience the beautiful landscape and theater culture of Minneapolis. End the day by enjoying a performance or film at the theater.
  • Day 3: Take the Midtown Greenway bike trail to explore several parts of Minneapolis, including the Chain of Lakes and the famous Lake Harriet.
  • Day 4: Visit Paisley Park, the legendary home and recording studio of Prince, and witness the artist’s legacy. Afterward, head to the Wheaton Wells Museum to learn about Minnesota’s early history.
  • Day 5: Take a brewery tour of Minneapolis and taste the finest beer the city has to offer.
  • Day 6: Enjoy some food on the Eat Street and end the day at one of the popular escape rooms to solve fun puzzles and challenges.
  • Day 7: Finish your trip with a peaceful visit to the Fort Snelling State Park, where you can walk around the fort, picnic, or birdwatch.

Food and Drink Scene of South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis has a diverse and thriving food and drinks scene. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Juicy Lucy: Juicy Lucy is a classic Minneapolis dish that is a cheeseburger with cheese stuffed into the middle of the burger patty.
  • Tater Tot Hotdish: Tater Tot Hotdish is a classic Midwestern casserole that includes tater tots, cream of mushroom soup, beef, and vegetables.
  • Fudge: A trip to South Minneapolis is incomplete without trying creamy and chocolatey fudge from one of the many local shops.

Local Beverages

  • Surly Brewing Co. Beer: Surly Brewing Co. is a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers like Furious, Coffee Bender, and Hell.
  • Distillery Tours: Distilleries like Tattersall Distilling offer tours that allow visitors to witness the process of handcrafted spirits.

Where to Stay in South Minneapolis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Minneapolis offers a range of options for accommodation, from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best options are:

  • Loews Minneapolis Hotel: Located in the heart of Minneapolis, Loews Minneapolis Hotel offers luxury accommodation with stunning views of the city.
  • Moxy Minneapolis Uptown: Moxy Minneapolis Uptown is a trendy and affordable hotel that offers modern design and unique amenities like a rooftop bar and lounge.
  • Minnehaha Lake Bed and Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse that provides a tranquil and comfortable experience with views of Lake Minnehaha.
  • The Minneapolis Hostel: If you’re looking for budget accommodation, The Minneapolis Hostel offers shared and private rooms in the heart of Minneapolis.

What to Buy in South Minneapolis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Minneapolis has several options for souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring back home. Some of the popular options are:

  • Minnesota Sota Clothing: Minnesota Sota Clothing offers hoodies, t-shirts, and other clothing items that feature Minnesota’s name and state outline.
  • Finn Style: Finn style is a store that sells contemporary Finnish design goods like mugs, dishware, and home decor items.
  • Midtown Global Market: Midtown Global Market is an indoor market that features international cuisine and locally made products like chocolates, spices, teas, and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis celebrates its culture and customs through various events and activities throughout the year.

  • Art Fairs and Festivals: South Minneapolis celebrates the arts and hosts several art fairs and festivals like the Uptown Art Fair, Powderhorn Art Fair, and the May Day Parade and Festival.
  • Minnesota State Fair: The Minnesota State Fair is an iconic event that takes place annually in Saint Paul. It attracts visitors from all over the state and features live music, food vendors, and various attractions.
  • Hmong Village: The Hmong village is a unique market that features cultural goods, food, and live music. It is a great way for visitors to experience the culture and customs of the area.

Best Time to Visit South Minneapolis

The best time to visit South Minneapolis is from May to October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and lake activities are possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Minneapolis

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts of South Minneapolis:

Do:

  • Try the Juicy Lucy: Juicy Lucy is a classic dish of Minneapolis, and every visitor should taste it.
  • Visit the Museums: Visit museums to learn about the history and culture of Minneapolis. There are several museums to choose from, so you’re sure to find one that interests you.
  • Visit the Parks: Minneapolis has several beautiful parks, and they are a great way to relax and enjoy nature.
  • Be friendly and polite: The people of South Minneapolis are welcoming and friendly, so treat them with respect and kindness.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Keep the city clean and don’t litter. Use trash cans and recycling bins.
  • Don’t speed: Observe the traffic laws while driving or cycling and avoid accidents.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs: Respect the local customs and traditions, and don’t offend locals intentionally.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis is a safe and welcoming community. However, visitors should take precautions in the city, such as staying in well-lit areas, and keeping their valuables safe. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis is a healthy and safe community. However, like any other city, visitors should carry a precautionary health kit, sunscreen, water bottles, etc. in case of emergencies.

Getting to South Minneapolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Minneapolis is well-connected to several major transportation options.

Airports Near South Minneapolis

The Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport is the primary airport in the area, located about 10 miles from South Minneapolis. Visitors can take a taxi, shuttle bus, or light rail to get to South Minneapolis.

Bus Stations Near South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis has several bus stations, including the Greyhound Bus Terminal in Downtown Minneapolis and the Megabus station in the Uptown area. Both services offer multiple options to cities across the United States.

Other Options

South Minneapolis is easily accessible by car from nearby cities like Saint Paul, Rochester, and Duluth. There are several car rentals and ride-hailing options in the city for convenient travel.

How to Get Around in South Minneapolis

South Minneapolis is easy to navigate, and there are several options to get around the city.

  • Biking: Minneapolis is one of the most bike-friendly cities in America, and there are several bike rental options.
  • Walking: South Minneapolis is a walkable area, and there are several sidewalks and pedestrian-friendly areas.
  • Public Transport: Minneapolis has several public transportation options like buses and light rails to travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from South Minneapolis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Minneapolis is an excellent location to explore other parts of Minnesota. Some nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Saint Paul, Minnesota: Saint Paul is the twin city of Minneapolis and is located just across the Mississippi River. The city has a rich history and culture with several museums and historic sites.
  • Rochester, Minnesota: Rochester is a city located about 90 miles southeast of Minneapolis. It is famous for the Mayo Clinic but also offers historic landmarks and outdoor activities.
  • Duluth, Minnesota: Duluth is a city located about 2.5 hours north of Minneapolis and offers a fantastic view of the north shore of Lake Superior. There are several outdoor activities like hiking, skiing, and water activities.

Conclusion

South Minneapolis is an excellent location for travelers looking for a culturally rich and naturally scenic destination. With its vast array of options for people with different interests, there is something for everyone. So, plan your trip to South Minneapolis today and let the natural and historical beauty of the city inspire you.
