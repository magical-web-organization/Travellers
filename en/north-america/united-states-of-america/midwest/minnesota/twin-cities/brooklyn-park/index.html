Introduction to Brooklyn Park

Brooklyn Park is a vibrant city located in Twin Cities, Minnesota. As one of the largest cities in the state, Brooklyn Park boasts lively culture, natural beauty, and excellent opportunities for outdoor recreation. With its proximity to major highways and public transportation, Brooklyn Park is easily accessible from other Midwest destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Brooklyn Park

Whether you’re a history buff, active adventurer, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Brooklyn Park. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Historic Eidem Farm: Immerse yourself in the early 20th century by visiting the Historic Eidem Farm. Learn about life on the farm through guided tours, hands-on demonstrations, and special events.
  • Brooklyn Historical Society Museum: If you’re interested in the history of Brooklyn Park and the Twin Cities, check out the Brooklyn Historical Society Museum. The museum features exhibits on the city’s past, including local industries and notable residents.

Natural Attractions of Brooklyn Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mississippi Gateway Regional Park: Located on the banks of the Mississippi River, this regional park offers hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the river.
  • Coon Rapids Dam Regional Park: This park features trails for hiking and biking, fishing, and boats rentals to explore the scenic Mississippi River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brooklyn Park

  • North Hennepin Regional Trail: This 42-mile trail runs through Brooklyn Park, offering a scenic route for hiking, biking, and rollerblading.
  • Rush Creek Regional Trail: This 9-mile paved trail runs through Brooklyn Park, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Brooklyn Park: Activities and Tours

  • Go Ape Treetop Adventure Course: For an adrenaline rush, try the Go Ape Treetop Adventure Course in Elm Creek Park Reserve. This course has zip lines, suspended obstacles, and high ropes challenges to put your physical abilities to the test.
  • American Crown Circus: For family fun, check out the American Crown Circus, which features talented performers from around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brooklyn Park

  • Day 1: Explore the Historic Eidem Farm and Brooklyn Historical Society Museum.
  • Day 2: Bike along the North Hennepin Regional Trail and picnic at Mississippi Gateway Regional Park.
  • Day 3: Take the Go Ape Treetop Adventure Course in Elm Creek Park Reserve.
  • Day 4: Visit the Coon Rapids Dam and rent a boat for a scenic river tour.
  • Day 5: Experience the American Crown Circus.
  • Day 6: Visit local shops and markets for souvenirs and local products.
  • Day 7: Relax at a local hotel or spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Brooklyn Park

Brooklyn Park offers a diverse culinary scene, with plenty of options for local and international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jucy Lucy: A famous dish from Minnesota, the Jucy Lucy is a cheeseburger with the cheese stuffed inside the burger patty.
  • Tater Tot Hotdish: A classic comfort food, Tater Tot Hotdish is a casserole dish made with ground beef, vegetables, and, of course, tater tots.

Local Beverages

  • Surly Brewing Co.: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their famous Furious IPA.
  • Summit Brewing Company: Another favorite local brewery, Summit offers a variety of year-round and seasonal beers.

Where to Stay in Brooklyn Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brooklyn Park offers a variety of lodging options to suit any budget. Here are some top choices:

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton Minneapolis North: Located near the Mississippi River, this hotel features a fitness center, indoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Minneapolis NW Brooklyn Park: This budget-friendly hotel offers an on-site fitness center and free breakfast.
  • Airbnb: For a more unique stay, Brooklyn Park offers many affordable options on Airbnb.

What to Buy in Brooklyn Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Be sure to pick up some local souvenirs and products to remember your visit to Brooklyn Park. Here are some great options:

  • Coffee from Brooklyn Coffee & Deli: This cafe features locally roasted coffee beans, with a variety of blends and roasts available for purchase.
  • Minnesota-themed souvenirs: Minnesota-themed souvenirs, such as t-shirts, mugs, and keychains, are available at a variety of local shops and gift stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Brooklyn Park

Brooklyn Park is known for its friendly locals and welcoming atmosphere. Be sure to take the time to explore the city’s cultural offerings and learn more about its history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Brooklyn Park

The best time to visit Brooklyn Park is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brooklyn Park

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including the Jucy Lucy and Tater Tot Hotdish.
  • Do explore the city’s outdoor attractions, including hiking trails and parks.
  • Do visit the local museums and historical sites to learn more about Brooklyn Park’s past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas, trails, or parks.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions, such as not touching someone’s head or offering a firm handshake when greeting someone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brooklyn Park

Brooklyn Park is generally a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking car doors and not carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Brooklyn Park

Brooklyn Park has several medical facilities and clinics, including North Memorial Health Hospital and HealthPartners Clinic.

Getting to Brooklyn Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brooklyn Park is conveniently located and easily accessible from other Midwest destinations.

Airports Near Brooklyn Park

The closest airport to Brooklyn Park is Minneapolis−Saint Paul International Airport, which is approximately 25 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Brooklyn Park

Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to the Twin Cities area, with the closest station located in Minneapolis, about 20 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Brooklyn Park is easily accessible by car and public transportation, including Metro Transit buses and light rail trains.

How to Get Around in Brooklyn Park

Brooklyn Park is easy to navigate by car, bike, or foot. Metro Transit also offers bus routes that stop at several locations throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Brooklyn Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brooklyn Park is located near several other popular destinations in the Midwest. Some nearby destinations include:

  • Minneapolis: Just a short drive from Brooklyn Park, Minneapolis offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, dining, and nightlife.
  • Mall of America: One of the largest malls in the United States, the Mall of America is located in Bloomington, Minnesota, about 30 minutes away from Brooklyn Park.
  • St. Cloud: Located about an hour northwest of Brooklyn Park, St. Cloud offers scenic parks, historic architecture, and a variety of cultural and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Brooklyn Park is a hidden gem in the Midwest, offering a diverse range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s natural beauty, learn about its history and culture, or simply enjoy some local cuisine, Brooklyn Park has something to offer. Plan your visit today and experience all that this vibrant city has to offer!
