Introduction to North Branch

North Branch is a small town located in Chisago County, Minnesota, in the Midwest region of the United States of America. The town is situated just 45 miles north of the Twin Cities and is known for its natural beauty, charming atmosphere, and rich history. Whether you’re looking to explore the outdoors, discover the local culture, or simply relax and unwind, North Branch has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in North Branch

North Branch offers a variety of exciting attractions for visitors of all ages. Below is a list of top places to visit in North Branch:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. North Branch Area Historical Society - Learn about the town’s rich history through various displays and exhibits at this small museum.
  2. Folsom House Museum - Explore the elegant architecture and furnishings of this historic home dating back to 1855.
  3. Pioneer Park - Visit this recreated pioneer village to experience life as it was in the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of North Branch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wild River State Park - Located just 20 miles east of North Branch, this park offers scenic hiking and biking trails, camping, and river access for kayaking and tubing.
  2. Interstate State Park - Located just 30 miles northeast of North Branch, this park is home to several hiking trails, stunning river views, and unique geological formations.
  3. Richardson Nature Center - Located just 10 miles south of North Branch, this center offers an array of outdoor activities, including hiking, bird watching, and nature programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Branch

North Branch offers several scenic trails for hiking and biking. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Soo Line Trail - This 77-mile trail runs between Superior, Wisconsin, and Moose Lake, Minnesota, passing through North Branch. The trail offers scenic views of forests, lakes, and wetlands.
  • Chisago Lakes Trail - This 13-mile paved trail runs through North Branch and the surrounding communities, offering scenic views of lakes, forests, and wildlife.
  • Wild River State Park Trails - This park offers over 30 miles of hiking trails, ranging from easy to challenging, with scenic views of the St. Croix River.

Best Things to Do in North Branch: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting things to do in North Branch. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Canoeing/Kayaking - Rent a canoe or kayak and explore the St. Croix River or other nearby lakes and rivers.
  • Golfing - North Branch offers several golf courses, including the beautiful Purple Hawk Country Club.
  • Winery Tours - Take a tour of the award-winning wine vineyards located in nearby Taylors Falls.

Food and Drink Scene of North Branch

North Branch offers a variety of dining options, ranging from casual to fine dining. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye Sandwich - A local favorite, this dish features a fresh Walleye fillet served on a toasted bun.
  • Wild Rice Soup - This hearty soup features locally-sourced wild rice, chicken, and vegetables.
  • Poutine - A Canadian classic, this dish features French fries topped with cheese curds and smothered in gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Vineyards - The nearby Taylors Falls region is home to several award-winning wineries, with tastings and tours available.
  • Breweries - Nearby cities such as Forest Lake and Blaine offer a variety of craft breweries.

Where to Stay in North Branch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Branch offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference. Some popular choices include:

  • AmericInn by Wyndham North Branch - A modern hotel with a pool and spa, located just off I-35.
  • Sunset Acres Bed & Breakfast - A charming guesthouse in nearby Rush City, featuring cozy rooms and home-cooked breakfast.
  • Camping - Wild River State Park and other nearby campgrounds offer a variety of camping options for outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in North Branch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Branch offers several unique souvenirs and local products to take home, including:

  • Wild Rice - A local staple, this natural grain can be found at various farmer’s markets and gift shops.
  • Handmade Crafts - Browse local artisan shops for unique handmade jewelry, pottery, and other crafts.
  • Maple Syrup - Local maple syrup is a sweet and delicious gift to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of North Branch

North Branch has a rich culture and customs that are worth exploring during your visit. Some notable traditions include:

  • St. Patrick’s Day Parade - North Branch hosts an annual St. Patrick’s Day Parade, featuring floats, music, and plenty of green.
  • Fall Harvest Festival - A beloved event, this festival celebrates the town’s agricultural heritage with music, games, and food.
  • Ice Fishing - In the winter months, residents enjoy ice fishing on local lakes and rivers.

Best Time to Visit North Branch

North Branch is a year-round destination, each season with its own unique charm. The best time to visit depends on your preferred activities and weather preferences. Summer is perfect for outdoor activities and festivals, while winter offers snowmobiling, ice fishing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Branch

Do:

  • Do explore the outdoors - North Branch offers a variety of outdoor activities, from hiking to kayaking.
  • Do try the local cuisine - North Branch has a unique food and drink scene, featuring locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Do engage with the locals - North Branch residents are friendly and welcoming, and love to share their town with visitors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter - Respect the natural beauty of North Branch by properly disposing of waste.
  • Don’t drive under the influence - DUI laws are strictly enforced in Minnesota.
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs - North Branch has a rich cultural heritage that should be respected and celebrated.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Branch

North Branch is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate. As with any destination, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking car doors and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in North Branch

North Branch has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors are advised to have adequate health insurance coverage and bring any necessary medications.

Getting to North Branch: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Branch is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are the most common options:

Airports Near North Branch

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport - Located just 50 miles south of North Branch, this airport is served by several major airlines and offers rental cars and shuttles.

Bus Stations Near North Branch

  • Greyhound Bus Line - The nearest Greyhound station is located in Minneapolis, about 50 miles south of North Branch.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Several car rental companies operate near North Branch, including Hertz and Enterprise.
  • Amtrak Train - The nearest Amtrak station is located in St. Paul, about 50 miles south of North Branch.

How to Get Around in North Branch

Visitors can easily get around North Branch by car, bike, or foot. Public transportation is limited, so having access to a vehicle is recommended.

Where to Go Next from North Branch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Branch is located in a beautiful area of Minnesota, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring. Some popular options include:

  • St. Croix River Valley - Visit charming river towns such as Taylors Falls and Stillwater, just a short drive from North Branch.
  • Minneapolis - Explore the vibrant city of Minneapolis, just a 45-minute drive south of North Branch, featuring world-class dining, shopping, and entertainment options.
  • Lake Superior North Shore - A scenic 3-hour drive north of North Branch, this area is home to stunning waterfalls, rugged coastline, and charming small towns.
