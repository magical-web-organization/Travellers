Introduction to Stillwater

Located in the beautiful state of Minnesota, Stillwater is a charming town in the Midwest that offers a perfect blend of history, nature, and adventure. Known as the “birthplace of Minnesota,” the town boasts a rich heritage that dates back to the mid-19th century. Today, visitors can experience the town’s fascinating past through its historic landmarks, museums, and cultural festivals.

But Stillwater has more to offer than just history. Surrounded by the stunning St. Croix River and rolling hills, the town is also a hub for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking, biking, kayaking, and fishing. Moreover, Stillwater has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a range of restaurants, cafes, and breweries offering delicious dishes and drinks that capture the flavors of the region.

To help you plan your trip to Stillwater, we have put together a comprehensive guide to the town’s top attractions, activities, accommodations, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Stillwater

If you want to make the most of your time in Stillwater, you should visit the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Warden’s House Museum: The Warden’s House Museum represents the life of Stillwater’s early pioneers and offers a glimpse into the town’s rich history. The museum was once the residence of the first Minnesota Territorial Prison warden, Henry Hastings Sibley. Today, it showcases a range of exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of Stillwater’s past.

  • Washington County Historic Courthouse: Built-in 1870, the Washington County Historic Courthouse is a stunning example of Victorian architecture. The courthouse is open to the public for guided tours, and visitors can explore its grand courtroom, jail cells, and sheriff’s residence.

  • St. Croix Boom Site: The St. Croix Boom Site is a historic landmark that played a critical role in Stillwater’s early logging industry. The site was used to sort and transport millions of logs that floated down the river, making Stillwater a prosperous logging town. Visitors can learn about the site’s history and see the boom chain that controlled the logs’ flow.

Natural Attractions of Stillwater: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • St. Croix River: The St. Croix River is the centerpiece of Stillwater, and visitors can enjoy a range of activities, such as kayaking, canoeing, fishing, and boating. The river is also an excellent spot for hiking and biking, with several trails that provide stunning views of the valley.

  • Stillwater Overlook : The Stillwater Overlook is a scenic spot that offers breathtaking views of the town and the St. Croix River. Visitors can access the overlook via a short hiking trail that takes them through a beautiful deciduous forest.

  • Brown’s Creek State Trail: The Brown’s Creek State Trail is a 5.9-mile trail that connects Stillwater to the Gateway State Trail. The trail passes through rolling hills, wetlands, and meadows, offering an excellent opportunity for hiking, biking, and wildlife viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stillwater

Stillwater is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several trails that offer scenic views of the St. Croix River and the town. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Gateway State Trail: The Gateway State Trail is a 18.3-mile trail that connects St. Paul to Pine Point Regional Park. The trail passes through scenic landscapes, including wetlands, woodlands, and prairies, offering an immersive nature experience. The trail is suitable for hiking, biking, and rollerblading.

  • Brown’s Creek State Trail: As mentioned earlier, the Brown’s Creek State Trail is a 5.9-mile trail that connects Stillwater to the Gateway State Trail. The trail’s highlights include the scenic Brown’s Creek Bridge and the charming downtown of Stillwater.

  • St. Croix Crossing Loop Trail: The St. Croix Crossing Loop Trail is a 4.7-mile hiking and biking trail that crosses the iconic St. Croix River Bridge and offers stunning views of the river and the valley. The trail is suitable for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Stillwater: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s landmarks, visitors can enjoy several other activities in Stillwater. Here are a few options to consider:

  • St. Croix River Boat Tours: The St. Croix River Boat Tours offer a unique perspective of Stillwater and the St. Croix River. Visitors can choose from a range of tours, including scenic lunch and dinner cruises, wine and cheese tours, and even murder mystery cruises.

  • Shopping in Downtown Stillwater: Downtown Stillwater is a charming area filled with boutiques, antique stores, and specialty shops. Visitors can spend hours strolling through the streets and browsing the unique offerings of the town.

  • Stillwater Trolley Tours: The Stillwater Trolley Tours take visitors on a fun and informative journey through the town’s historic district. The trolley stops at several landmarks, including the Warden’s House Museum, the Historic Courthouse, and the Old Athletic Field.

Food and Drink Scene of Stillwater

Stillwater is a culinary destination that offers a range of dining and drinking options. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to explore:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Minnesota Wild Rice Soup: Minnesota Wild Rice Soup is a hearty soup made with wild rice, chicken broth, and cream and is a staple of Minnesota cuisine.

  • Juicy Lucy: A Juicy Lucy is a cheeseburger with the cheese inside the patty, creating a delicious, gooey center.

  • Walleye: Walleye is a freshwater fish that is popular in Minnesota and is often served deep-fried or baked.

Local Beverages

  • Summit Brewery: Summit Brewery is a local brewery located in St. Paul that produces a range of beers, including their flagship Summit Extra Pale Ale.

  • Winehaven Winery and Vineyard: Winehaven Winery and Vineyard is a local winery that produces a range of award-winning wines, including their signature Firehouse Red.

  • Lift Bridge Brewery: Lift Bridge Brewery is located in Stillwater and produces a range of craft beers, including their popular Farm Girl Saison.

Local Culture and Customs of Stillwater

Stillwater is a friendly and welcoming town that values community and history. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s culture and customs, such as:

  • Be Respectful: Stillwater is a relatively quiet community, so visitors should keep noise levels down and be respectful of their neighbors.

  • Follow the Rules: Visitors should always follow the town’s rules and regulations, such as parking rules, noise ordinances, and littering policies.

  • Support Local Businesses: Stillwater is a thriving and vibrant community, and visitors are encouraged to support local businesses by shopping, dining, and staying in local establishments.

Best Time to Visit Stillwater

The best time to visit Stillwater is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s beauty in the fall, when the leaves begin to change colors, providing a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities.

Getting to Stillwater: Transportation Options and Logistics

Stillwater is conveniently located near several major airports and bus stations, making it easily accessible from anywhere in the world. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Stillwater

  • Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport: The Minneapolis-Saint Paul International Airport is the closest major airport to Stillwater, located about 30 miles away from the town. Several airlines provide flights to and from the airport, with connections to destinations across the world.

Bus Stations Near Stillwater

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Greyhound Bus Station is located in St. Paul, about 20 miles away from Stillwater. Several regional and national bus services provide connections to and from the station.

  • Megabus Station: The Megabus Station is located in Minneapolis, about 30 miles away from Stillwater. Megabus provides low-cost bus services to several destinations across the Midwest.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: Visitors can also arrange private shuttle or cab services to and from Stillwater.

How to Get Around in Stillwater

Stillwater is a compact town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors who prefer to drive can also rent a car or hire a cab service to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Stillwater: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore some of the nearby destinations and attractions, here are some options to consider:
