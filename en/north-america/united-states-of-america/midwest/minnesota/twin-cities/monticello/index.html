Introduction to Monticello

Nestled in Twin Cities metropolitan area, Monticello is a charming city surrounded by beautiful natural scenery and rich history. This charming town in the Midwest region of the United States offers a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor recreational activities, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Monticello has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Monticello

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Monticello will impress you with its many historical sites and museums. Here are a few places you should visit:

  • Historic Downtown Monticello: Take a stroll through the charming downtown area and discover beautifully preserved buildings dating back to the late 19th century.
  • Wright County Historical Society Museum: Learn about the history of Wright County and the city of Monticello at this fascinating museum, which features a collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  • The Depot: This historic building dates back to 1880 and houses the Monticello Chamber of Commerce and Tourism Bureau.

Natural Attractions of Monticello: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Monticello is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, making it a perfect destination for anyone looking for an outdoor adventure. Here are a few must-visit natural attractions:

  • Pinewood Golf Course: This stunning 18-hole golf course is set in the midst of breathtaking natural scenery and offers a challenging yet rewarding golf experience.
  • Montissippi Park: This picturesque park offers beautiful views of the Mississippi River, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Kaleidoscope Indoor Playground: If you’re traveling with kids, this indoor playground is the perfect place for them to burn off some energy while you take a break.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monticello

If you love hiking and biking, Monticello offers a variety of scenic trails.

  • Monticello River Trail: A beautiful trail that takes you along the Mississippi River.
  • Lake Maria State Park: This beautiful park offers hiking and biking trails, as well as fishing and camping opportunities.
  • Millennium Trail: This paved trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or a stroll through the beautiful natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Monticello: Activities and Tours

From exciting outdoor adventures to fascinating historical tours, Monticello has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Monticello Riverfest: This annual festival celebrates the city’s rich history and culture with live music, food vendors, and a variety of family-friendly activities.
  • Becker Furniture World: Shop for unique home decor and furniture at this local favorite.
  • Allure Private Jet Tours: If you’re looking for a once-in-a-lifetime experience, book a private jet tour and see the beauty of Monticello from the sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monticello

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Monticello:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Wright County Historical Society Museum and explore Historic Downtown Monticello
Day 2 Play a round of golf at Pinewood Golf Course
Day 3 Spend the day at Lake Maria State Park hiking and fishing
Day 4 Take a tour of Becker Furniture World
Day 5 Enjoy a private jet tour with Allure Private Jet Tours
Day 6 Spend the day at Montissippi Park and have a picnic
Day 7 Attend the Monticello Riverfest and immerse yourself in the city’s culture and history

Food and Drink Scene of Monticello

Must-Try Dishes

Monticello offers a variety of delicious local dishes that you won’t want to miss. Here are some of the best:

  • The Staghead: Try the mouth-watering burgers and craft beers at this local favorite.
  • Pancho Villa Mexican Restaurant: Savor authentic Mexican cuisine at this popular establishment.
  • Cornerstone Cafe: This cozy cafe serves delicious breakfast and lunch dishes made with fresh, local ingredients.

Local Beverages

Monticello has a thriving craft beer scene that you won’t want to miss. Here are a few places to check out:

  • Buffalo Rock Winery: This family-owned winery produces a variety of delicious wines made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Excelsior Brewing Company: This popular brewery offers a variety of craft beers and live music on weekends.
  • Surly Brewing Company: This award-winning brewery offers a variety of beers and delicious food at its taproom and beer garden.

Where to Stay in Monticello: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monticello offers a range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs. Here are a few options:

  • Riverwood Inn & Conference Center: This comfortable hotel offers spacious rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Monticello: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Monticello Motel: This affordable motel is a great option for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Monticello: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Monticello with you by purchasing some of the city’s locally made products. Here are a few ideas:

  • Handmade Soap and Lotion: Buy locally made soap and lotion from Rustic Birch Soap and give your skin the TLC it deserves.
  • Craft Beer: Pick up a few bottles of locally brewed craft beer from Excelsior Brewing Company or Surly Brewing Company.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Buy unique handmade jewelry from Tillypickles and add some local charm to your wardrobe.

Local Culture and Customs of Monticello

Monticello is a friendly and welcoming city, and its residents take pride in their community. Here are a few tips to help you navigate the city’s customs and culture:

  • Small-Town Friendliness: Monticello’s residents are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you.
  • Respect the Outdoors: Monticello is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, and locals take great pride in preserving it. Respect the environment by leaving no trace and following park rules.
  • Experience Local Events: Monticello hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year, from the Monticello Riverfest to the Wright County Fair. Don’t miss the opportunity to experience local culture at these events.

Best Time to Visit Monticello

Monticello’s weather is mild and enjoyable year-round, making it a pleasant destination for all seasons. However, the best time to visit is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warm and the city’s outdoor attractions are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monticello

Do:

  • Explore the city’s charming downtown area.
  • Visit the Wright County Historical Society Museum.
  • Try the locally brewed craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t disrupt local wildlife.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable walking shoes for hiking and sightseeing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monticello

Monticello is a safe city, but as with any destination, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are a few tips:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.
  • Secure your belongings and keep them out of sight in your car or hotel room.
  • Follow park rules when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Monticello

Monticello has excellent healthcare facilities, and emergency services are readily available if needed. However, it’s essential to take precautions to stay healthy during your trip. Here are a few tips:

  • Use sunscreen and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Bring insect repellent and use it when exploring the outdoors.
  • Be mindful of your diet and opt for locally sourced, fresh ingredients when possible.

Getting to Monticello: Transportation Options and Logistics

Monticello is easily accessible by air, car, and bus.

Airports Near Monticello

  • Minneapolis Saint Paul International Airport: This major airport is located approximately one hour from Monticello.

Bus Stations Near Monticello

  • Metro Transit Northstar Commuter Rail: This commuter rail line serves the northern metropolitan area and stops at the Monticello Station in downtown Monticello.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Rent a car at the airport or in downtown Monticello to explore the city and the surrounding area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Monticello

Monticello is a walkable city with a charming downtown area perfect for strolling. Additionally, there are a variety of transportation options available, including taxis, ride-sharing services, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Monticello: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Monticello’s central location in the Midwest makes it an excellent base for exploring the surrounding region. Here are a few nearby destinations worth checking out:

  • Minneapolis: Visit the famous Mall of America, explore the city’s thriving food and arts scene, and enjoy the beautiful natural scenery at the Chain of Lakes.
  • St. Cloud: Visit the Stearns History Museum, experience the local food scene, and explore Quarry Park and Nature Reserve.
  • Duluth: Enjoy the city’s stunning harbor views, visit the Great Lakes Aquarium, and explore the scenic North Shore.

Conclusion

Monticello is a charming city with so much to offer, from rich history and cultural attractions to beautiful natural scenery and a vibrant food and drink scene. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan the perfect trip and make the most out of your visit to this wonderful destination.
