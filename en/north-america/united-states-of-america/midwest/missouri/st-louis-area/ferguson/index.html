Introduction to Ferguson

Ferguson is a small city located in the St. Louis area of Missouri in the Midwest region of the United States. It is widely known for its rich cultural heritage, exciting outdoor adventures, and vibrant food and drink scene. Despite its small size, Ferguson boasts several interesting attractions for avid travelers and nature enthusiasts. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and accommodations to book in Ferguson, Missouri.

List of Places to Visit in Ferguson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ferguson has a rich history, making it a great spot for history buffs. The following are some of the most popular museums and historical places to visit in Ferguson:

  1. The Ferguson History Museum: This museum showcases the history of Ferguson, with exhibits that detail the city’s founding, growth, and people.
  2. The Emerson Theater: Built in 1921, this historic theater has been restored and plays host to a variety of live productions and events.
  3. Combs House: This historic mansion was built in 1890 and now houses the Ferguson City Hall.
  4. St. Peter’s Catholic Church: Built-in 1917, this church is an architectural gem with beautiful stained-glass windows and a serene atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Ferguson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ferguson is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in the Midwest. Here are some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views to visit:

  1. Forestwood Park: This park offers fantastic views of the Missouri River and boasts a playground, picnic area, and walking trails for visitors.
  2. January-Wabash Memorial Park: This park is a tranquil spot with beautiful views of the Wabash River and offers several walking trails and a pier for fishing enthusiasts.
  3. Ferguson Farmers’ Market: This outdoor market is an excellent spot for foodies, with vendors selling locally sourced produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ferguson

Ferguson offers several hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular:

  1. Quercus Grove Trail: This trail is a 0.7-mile loop located in Forestwood Park. It’s great for a quick hike or bike ride and offers beautiful views of the Missouri River.
  2. Little Creek Trail: This trail stretches for 1.5 miles and connects Forestwood Park and January-Wabash Memorial Park. It’s a popular spot for hikers, bikers, and joggers in Ferguson.
  3. North Hanley Trail: This 3.2-mile trail is perfect for experienced hikers and bikers. It starts at Ferguson Avenue and runs south through some of the most beautiful scenery in Ferguson.

Best Things to Do in Ferguson: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to check out in Ferguson. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Locust Street Festival: This festival is held annually in June and features live music, food vendors, and a car show.
  2. Hayride Tours: Take a hayride tour of Ferguson and take in the beautiful scenery and natural attractions.
  3. Nashville Nights: If you’re a music enthusiast, head to the Emerson Theater on Fridays for Nashville Nights, featuring live country music and dancing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ferguson

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ferguson, here’s a sample itinerary for you:

Day 1: Visit the Ferguson History Museum and Combs House. Day 2: Explore Forestwood Park and take a bike ride on the Quercus Grove Trail. Day 3: Take a tour of St. Peter’s Catholic Church and enjoy the Locust Street Festival. Day 4: Head to January-Wabash Memorial Park and walk the Little Creek Trail. Day 5: Try the local cuisine at Ferguson Farmers’ Market and spend the evening at Nashville Nights. Day 6: Take a hayride tour of Ferguson and enjoy the beautiful scenery. Day 7: Relax and unwind at your accommodation or explore some of the other nearby attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Ferguson

When it comes to food and drink, Ferguson has something for everyone.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the must-try dishes in Ferguson:

  1. St. Louis Style BBQ Ribs: Ferguson is known for its delicious BBQ ribs, which are typically slow-cooked over a wood fire and coated in a sweet, tangy sauce.
  2. Toasted Ravioli: This unique St. Louis dish consists of meat or cheese-filled ravioli that is breaded and deep-fried to perfection.
  3. Gooey Butter Cake: This delectable dessert is a St. Louis favorite, made with yellow cake mix and cream cheese.

Local Beverages

Ferguson is a great spot to sample some local beverages, including:

  1. Budweiser Beer: Budweiser beer is brewed in St. Louis and is a popular local choice.
  2. Ferguson Brewing Company: This award-winning craft brewery offers a variety of beers, including IPAs and stouts.
  3. St. Louis Lemonade: This refreshing drink is a local favorite, made with bourbon, lemon juice, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Ferguson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Ferguson has several options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Ferguson include:

  1. Holiday Inn Express & Suites Ferguson: This modern hotel is located close to several attractions and offers clean, comfortable rooms and free breakfast.
  2. Comfort Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel is a great choice for families and offers several amenities, including a pool and fitness center.
  3. Ferguson Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse offers a home-away-from-home experience and is perfect for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Ferguson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or local product to take home from Ferguson, consider the following:

  1. Ferguson Farmers’ Market: The market is a great spot to buy locally sourced produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.
  2. Combs House Gift Shop: The gift shop at Combs House offers a variety of souvenirs and gifts that highlight the history of Ferguson.

Local Culture and Customs of Ferguson

Ferguson is known for its warm and friendly locals, who are always ready to welcome visitors with open arms. Visitors to Ferguson should respect local customs and traditions, including the emphasis on community and family.

Best Time to Visit Ferguson

The best time to visit Ferguson is during the fall when the weather is mild, and the leaves change colors. The spring is also a great time to visit when the flowers are in bloom, and the weather is pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ferguson

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the St. Louis-style BBQ and local beer.
  3. Take a hike or bike ride on one of the scenic trails.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage natural attractions.
  2. Engage in disrespectful behavior towards locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ferguson

Ferguson is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still exercise caution when visiting. Avoid walking alone at night and keep your belongings safe and secure.

Health in Ferguson

Ferguson has several hospitals and medical facilities for visitors who require medical attention. Visitors should also drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Ferguson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ferguson is well-connected by air, road, and rail, making it easy to get to. Here are some transportation options for getting to Ferguson:

Airports Near Ferguson

  1. Lambert-St. Louis International Airport (STL): Located just 7 miles southwest of Ferguson.

Bus Stations Near Ferguson

  1. Greyhound Bus Station: Located in St. Louis, 11 miles southwest of Ferguson.

Other Options

  1. MetroLink: A light rail system that connects Ferguson to St. Louis and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Ferguson

Ferguson is a small city that is easy to navigate by foot, bike, or car. Visitors can also use the MetroLink system to get around Ferguson and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Ferguson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have enough time, consider checking out the following nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Gateway Arch: Located in St. Louis, this massive arch is a symbol of the city and offers fantastic views of the Mississippi River.
  2. Laumeier Sculpture Park: This outdoor sculpture park features contemporary works of art set among rolling hills and wooded areas.
  3. Six Flags St. Louis: Located just 18 miles southwest of Ferguson, this amusement park offers thrilling rides and attractions.

Conclusion

Ferguson, Missouri, is a hidden gem in the Midwest, offering beautiful natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Ferguson. Plan your trip today and experience all that this charming city has to offer!
