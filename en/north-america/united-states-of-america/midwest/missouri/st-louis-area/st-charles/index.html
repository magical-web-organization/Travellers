Introduction to St. Charles

The charming city of St. Charles is nestled in the heart of the St. Louis area, Missouri in the Midwest region of the United States of America. With its historic architecture and cobblestone streets, St. Charles is a place that has preserved the culture and history of the early 19th century.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Charles

St. Charles celebrates its rich history and culture through multiple events like the Annual Festival of the Little Hills. People here value old-fashioned traditions and a strong sense of community.

Best Time to Visit St. Charles

The best time to visit St. Charles is during the fall season when you can experience the vibrant foliage providing a picturesque view.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Charles

Do:

  1. Wear comfortable shoes to explore the cobblestone streets and brick buildings
  2. Bring cash to support local businesses that only accept cash payments
  3. Enjoy the small town charm of historic St. Charles

Don’t:

  1. Litter or smoke in public areas as it can lead to fines
  2. Visit during summer as it can get very hot and humid
  3. Hesitate to ask locals for recommendations or directions

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Charles

St. Charles is a reasonably safe town, but it’s still a good idea to be vigilant and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in St. Charles

St. Charles has several medical facilities that provide quality healthcare services, including SSM Health St. Joseph Hospital West and St. Charles County Ambulance District.
