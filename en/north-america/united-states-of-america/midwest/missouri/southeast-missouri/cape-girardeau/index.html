Introduction to Cape Girardeau

Nestled in Southeast Missouri, Cape Girardeau is a charming city known for its rich history, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. Founded in 1793 by French fur trader Louis Lorimier, Cape Girardeau played a significant role during the Civil War and is home to many museums and historical landmarks.

Whether you’re looking to explore the natural surroundings, learn about the region’s past, or indulge in local cuisine, Cape Girardeau has something to offer every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Cape Girardeau

Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Cape Girardeau, categorized by their type:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bollinger Mill State Historic Site: A 19th-century mill that showcases the history of Missouri’s agricultural and industrial roots. Visitors can take a tour of the mill and learn about its operations and the people who worked there.

  • Cape Girardeau Conservation Nature Center: A nature center that features interactive exhibits highlighting the natural history of Missouri. Visitors can participate in various programs, including birdwatching, hiking, and fishing.

  • Crisp Museum: A museum that showcases the history of Southeast Missouri through artifacts, photographs, and interactive exhibits. Visitors can learn about early settlers, the Civil War, and local industry.

  • Fort D Historic Site: A Civil War fort that was occupied by Union troops during the war. Visitors can explore the grounds and learn about the fort’s history.

Natural Attractions of Cape Girardeau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Trail of Tears State Park: A 3,415-acre park that commemorates the forced relocation of Native American tribes during the 1830s. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and camping in the area.

  • La Cruz Vineyards: A family-owned vineyard that produces award-winning wines. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyard and sample some of their wines.

  • Mississippi River: The Mississippi River runs along the eastern border of Cape Girardeau, providing stunning views and opportunities for boating and fishing.

  • Tower Rock: A limestone formation in the Mississippi River that was once used as a navigational landmark for riverboats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cape Girardeau

Cape Girardeau offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Cape LaCroix Trail: A 9-mile trail that offers scenic views of the Mississippi River and ends at the Missouri Delta Medical Center.

  • Trail of Tears State Park Trail: A 3.5-mile trail that winds through the park’s forests and along the lake.

  • Himmelberger Loop Trail: A 6.7-mile trail that features rolling hills and scenic views of Cape Girardeau.

Best Things to Do in Cape Girardeau: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in Cape Girardeau:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the Mississippi River and enjoy scenic views of the city skyline and surrounding nature.

  • Wine Tasting: Visit one of the local wineries, such as La Cruz Vineyards or Steele Crest Winery, for a wine tasting and tour of the vineyard.

  • Ghost Tours: Explore Cape Girardeau’s haunted history on a ghost tour of the city’s most haunted locations.

  • Cape Riverfront Market: Check out the local farmers’ market, which takes place on Saturday mornings from April to October, for fresh produce, artisanal goods, and live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cape Girardeau

To help you plan your trip to Cape Girardeau, here is a week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Crisp Museum in the morning, followed by lunch at Katy O’Ferrell’s Irish Pub and Restaurant. In the afternoon, explore the Cape Girardeau Conservation Nature Center.
Day 2 Take a day trip to the nearby city of Perryville, known for its Civil War history and outdoor adventures.
Day 3 Spend the day exploring Trail of Tears State Park and hiking the park’s trails. Enjoy dinner at the Park Place Restaurant overlooking the lake.
Day 4 Take a boat tour of the Mississippi River in the morning. In the afternoon, visit the Bollinger Mill State Historic Site.
Day 5 Enjoy a relaxing day at one of the local wineries, such as La Cruz Vineyards or Steele Crest Winery.
Day 6 Take a ghost tour of the city’s haunted locations in the evening.
Day 7 End your trip with a visit to Tower Rock and a scenic drive along the Mississippi River.

Food and Drink Scene of Cape Girardeau

No trip to Cape Girardeau is complete without indulging in the local food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: Known for its mouth-watering barbecue, Cape Girardeau is home to various BBQ restaurants, such as Dexter Bar-B-Que and Smokin’ Brothers BBQ.

  • Fried Chicken: The region is known for its Southern-style fried chicken, and one of the best places to try it is at Port Cape Girardeau.

  • Locally Sourced Foods: For a taste of local ingredients, visit Farm-to-Table Cafe or Celebrations Restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Craft beer enthusiasts should visit the Buckner Brewing Company, which offers a variety of locally brewed beers.

  • Wine: As mentioned earlier, La Cruz Vineyards and Steele Crest Winery are two great options for wine tasting.

  • Soda: Visit the Tower Rock Soda Company to try their range of unique soda flavors, such as sarsaparilla and root beer.

Where to Stay in Cape Girardeau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cape Girardeau offers various accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some options to consider:

  • Drury Plaza Hotel: A historic hotel located in downtown Cape Girardeau that offers luxurious rooms and stunning views of the Mississippi River.

  • Isle Casino: A casino hotel that offers comfortable rooms and on-site amenities such as a casino, restaurants, and a pool.

  • Airbnb: For a more personal and authentic experience, consider staying in an Airbnb rental in one of Cape Girardeau’s charming neighborhoods.

What to Buy in Cape Girardeau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to remember your trip to Cape Girardeau? Consider these local products:

  • Gourmet Popcorn: Visit Poppin’ Around for a wide variety of gourmet popcorn flavors.

  • Locally Made Soaps and Candles: Check out Whirlwind of the Heartland for handmade soaps, candles, and other bath and body products.

  • Craft Beer: Bring home some of the local brews from Buckner Brewing Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Cape Girardeau

Cape Girardeau is a friendly and welcoming community that values its history and natural surroundings. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions and take the time to learn about the city’s past and present.

Best Time to Visit Cape Girardeau

The best time to visit Cape Girardeau is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. However, visitors can enjoy the city’s attractions all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape Girardeau

Do:

  • Do take a boat tour of the Mississippi River for stunning views of the city and surrounding nature.

  • Do visit the local wineries for a taste of the region’s award-winning wines.

  • Do check out the farmers’ market for fresh produce and artisanal goods.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings in any way.

  • Don’t forget to tip your restaurant servers and tour guides.

  • Don’t ignore the city’s rich history and cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape Girardeau

Cape Girardeau is generally a safe and welcoming city. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Cape Girardeau

Visitors should stay hydrated during the hot summer months and wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors. There are several hospitals and urgent care centers throughout the city in case of any medical emergencies.

Cape Girardeau is a walkable city, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use local taxi services or ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft to get around.

Where to Go Next from Cape Girardeau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to continue your Missouri adventure? Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • St. Louis: Located two hours north of Cape Girardeau, St. Louis is known for its iconic Gateway Arch, museums, and world-class dining.

  • Branson: Located three hours south of Cape Girardeau, Branson is a popular destination for its live shows, theme parks, and family-friendly attractions.

  • Lake of the Ozarks: Located two hours west of Cape Girardeau, Lake of the Ozarks is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and water sports.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, Cape Girardeau has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the charm and beauty of Southeast Missouri.
