Introduction to Poplar Bluff

Poplar Bluff is a charming city located in the southeastern region of Missouri, in the Midwest region of the United States. With its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and vibrant activities, Poplar Bluff is a must-visit destination for all travelers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, family-friendly attractions, or just a peaceful getaway, this city has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Poplar Bluff

Here are the top attractions in Poplar Bluff that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Butler County Historical Society Museum: This museum features artifacts and exhibits highlighting the history of the region, including early settlers, Native Americans, and the Civil War era.
  • Margaret Harwell Art Museum: The museum features a wide range of art, including works by local artists, as well as rotating exhibitions.
  • Poplar Bluff Railroad Museum: This museum houses a collection of railroad artifacts, including a restored caboose and locomotive.

Natural Attractions of Poplar Bluff: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Wappapello: A popular destination for fishing, boating and swimming. This lake is surrounded by scenic wooded hills, providing a beautiful backdrop for outdoor recreation.
  • Mark Twain National Forest: With over 1.5 million acres of forest, including hiking trails, rivers, and lakes, there is no shortage of outdoor activities here.
  • Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Enjoy a relaxing float down the crystal-clear Current River or take a hike through the beautiful Ozark hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poplar Bluff

  • Mingo National Wildlife Refuge Trails: With five different hiking trails, you can explore the natural beauty of the area and see wildlife up close.
  • Poplar Bluff Conservation Area: This area has three hiking trails and offers beautiful views of the Ozark hills.
  • Lake Wappapello State Park: A great option for mountain biking with over 12 miles of trails available.

Best Things to Do in Poplar Bluff: Activities and Tours

  • Bluff Falls Water Park: A fun, family-friendly water park with waterslides, a lazy river, and a wave pool.
  • Poplar Bluff Speedway: Enjoy a night of fast-paced racing at the Poplar Bluff Speedway.
  • Drive-in Movie Theatre: The 67 Drive-In Theatre is an experience to remember. Enjoy a movie in the comfort of your own car.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poplar Bluff

If you’re looking to stay for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Mark Twain National Forest and hike one of the scenic trails.
  • Day 2: Explore the history of Poplar Bluff at the Butler County Historical Society Museum.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Lake Wappapello boating, fishing or swimming.
  • Day 4: Take a relaxing float down the Current River in Ozark National Scenic Riverways.
  • Day 5: Visit the Poplar Bluff Railroad Museum and then cool off at Bluff Falls Water Park.
  • Day 6: Try your luck fishing at Mingo National Wildlife Refuge.
  • Day 7: Take a leisurely bike ride through Poplar Bluff Conservation Area and enjoy a drive-in movie at night.

Food and Drink Scene of Poplar Bluff

Poplar Bluff offers a wide range of dining options for all tastes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbecue: You can’t leave Missouri without trying the barbecue, and Poplar Bluff has some of the best around. Be sure to try Dexter Bar-B-Que or Lil Boo’s.

  • Fried Chicken: Hay’s Grill has some of the best fried chicken in town, served with all your favorite Southern sides.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Poplar Bluff is home to Ozark Beer Company, a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers.
  • Soda Pop: Fitz’s Root Beer is a local favorite, with its unique blend of flavors and handmade process.

Where to Stay in Poplar Bluff: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Poplar Bluff. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Comfort Inn Poplar Bluff: A great hotel with comfortable rooms and a pool.
  • Royal Inn and Suites: An affordable option with clean rooms and friendly service.
  • The 1894 Lodge: A unique bed and breakfast housed in a historic mansion.

What to Buy in Poplar Bluff: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cottonwood Station: This gift shop features a variety of souvenirs, including apparel, jewelry, and local crafts.
  • Poplar Bluff Farmers Market: Stock up on fresh produce and locally made products like jams, cheese, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Poplar Bluff

  • Southern Hospitality: Poplar Bluff residents are known to be warm, friendly, and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Poplar Bluff

Spring (March-April) and fall (September-October) are the best times to visit Poplar Bluff. The weather is mild and pleasant, and there are several festivals and events during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poplar Bluff

Here are some helpful tips for your visit:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the barbecue and fried chicken.
  • Do be respectful to the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the natural areas. Keep our environment clean.
  • Don’t underestimate the beauty of the city and its outdoor offerings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Poplar Bluff

Poplar Bluff is generally considered a safe city to visit. However, it is always recommended to take general precautions like being aware of your surroundings, securing your valuables, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Poplar Bluff

Poplar Bluff has several options for medical care, including Poplar Bluff Regional Medical Center and several clinics around the city.

Getting to Poplar Bluff: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Poplar Bluff

The Lambert-St.Louis International Airport (STL) is the closest major airport, located approximately 150 miles north of Poplar Bluff.

Bus Stations Near Poplar Bluff

The Poplar Bluff Bus Station is located in downtown Poplar Bluff, where Greyhound and Trailways buses make stops.

Other Options

If you want to drive, Poplar Bluff is conveniently located near major highways including US Highways 60 and 67.

How to Get Around in Poplar Bluff

The best way to get around Poplar Bluff is by car. However, taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber are also available for shorter trips around town.

Where to Go Next from Poplar Bluff: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Branson, Missouri: Home to live music shows and amusement parks, Branson is just a couple of hours’ drive from Poplar Bluff.
  • Eureka Springs, Arkansas: With its Victorian architecture and vibrant arts scene, Eureka Springs is a charming small town located just over an hour’s drive away from Poplar Bluff.

Conclusion

Poplar Bluff is a hidden gem in the Midwest, with a rich history, thriving food scene, and beautiful natural scenery. Plan your trip now and experience all that this wonderful city has to offer.
