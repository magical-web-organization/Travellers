Introduction to Van Buren

Welcome to Van Buren – a small town nestled in the Ozark Foothills of Southeast Missouri. With its rolling hills, rivers, and parks, Van Buren offers a perfect escape from everyday life. This historic town has a lot to offer, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Van Buren

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Current River State Park

Current River State Park is situated on the banks of the Current River. It offers multiple outdoor activities, such as swimming, fishing, hiking, and camping. It also houses the Ozark Cultural Resource Center, which exhibits the regional history of the Ozarks.

Missouri Mines State Historic Site

This site provides a glimpse of the mining industry in Missouri from its earliest days to the present. Visitors can go on a guided tour that takes them through the mines and learn about the history of mining in the state.

Natural Attractions of Van Buren: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ozark National Scenic Riverways

Ozark National Scenic Riverways is a national park that comprises two of Missouri’s most pristine rivers – the Current and the Jack’s Fork. Visitors can enjoy swimming, boating, and fishing in crystal-clear streams or go for a hike in the forests that cover the hills.

Mark Twain National Forest

If you’re looking for an adventure, Mark Twain National Forest is your destination. This forest covers 1.5 million acres and offers plenty of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and horse riding. Visitors may also camp, swim, or canoe in its ponds or streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Van Buren

Van Buren is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Ozark Trail: It covers hundreds of miles through Mark Twain National Forest. Hikers can enjoy views of the Meramec River and deep rock canyons.
  • The Bell Mountain Loop: This trail rewards hikers with a spectacular view of the St. Francois Mountains.
  • The Current River Trail: This 1.25-mile hike takes visitors along the Current River, providing ample opportunities for fishing and swimming.

Best Things to Do in Van Buren: Activities and Tours

  • Take a float trip on the Current or Jack’s Fork River.
  • Explore the outdoors on horseback.
  • Visit the historic Mingo Wildlife Refuge.

Where to Stay in Van Buren: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rocky River Resort: This resort is located on the banks of the Current River and offers cabins and lodges that are perfect for families.
  • Twin Pines Conservation Education Center: This center offers dormitory-style housing and is perfect for large groups.
  • The Landing at Van Buren: This inn is located in the heart of Van Buren and offers cozy rooms for couples.

Local Culture and Customs of Van Buren

Van Buren is a relatively conservative town. Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Van Buren

The best time to visit Van Buren is from May to October. The weather is warm and pleasant during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Van Buren

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take part in outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Litter or damage natural environments.
  • Ignore warning signs or swim in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Van Buren

Van Buren is a safe town. However, visitors should use common sense and take precautions against theft and other crimes.

Health in Van Buren

Visitors should take precautions to prevent tick and mosquito bites, which could cause vector-borne diseases such as Lyme disease and West Nile virus.

Getting to Van Buren: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Van Buren

The closest airport is Lambert-St. Louis International Airport, which is approximately 155 miles away from Van Buren.

Bus Stations Near Van Buren

Greyhound has a station in Poplar Bluff, which is approximately 70 miles away from Van Buren.

Other Options

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get to Van Buren from any nearby city.

How to Get Around in Van Buren

Van Buren is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. However, visitors may also rent a car or bike to see attractions that are outside the town limits.

Where to Go Next from Van Buren: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Springfield, Missouri

Springfield is a bustling city that offers a range of activities for visitors. It is approximately 150 miles away from Van Buren.

Eureka Springs, Arkansas

Eureka Springs is a charming town that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. It is approximately 70 miles away from Van Buren.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural history and stunning natural landscapes, Van Buren is the perfect destination for those seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. With this guide, visitors can discover the best that Van Buren has to offer.
