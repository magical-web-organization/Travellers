Introduction to Ozark National Scenic Riverways

Ozark National Scenic Riverways is a national park in Southeast Missouri and one of the first NPS units to protect a river system, encompassing 134 miles of the Current and Jacks Fork Rivers. The park provides an opportunity to explore the Ozarks’ natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture. Here, visitors can hike through the wilderness, enjoy floating, fishing, and kayaking.

List of Places to Visit in Ozark National Scenic Riverways

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Alley Spring Grist Mill and spring: A historic mill and spring located in a scenic valley.
  • Big Spring: One of the largest springs in the world, releasing 278 million gallons of water daily.
  • Round Spring Cave: A guided tour to the underground world of karst formations and crystal-clear streams.
  • The Ozark National Scenic Riverways Visitor Center: A great starting point to your trip.

Natural Attractions of Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pulltite Springs: A large spring with an impressive flow of emerald water.
  • Rocky Falls: A unique rock formation where the cool waters of the Rocky Creek tumble.
  • Devils Well: A stunning blue pool where the Devil went for a swim.
  • Buck Hollow: A great place to enjoy streams and waterfalls and explore hidden caves.
  • The Ozark Trail: A scenic trail through the Ozarks with breathtaking views of forests, rivers, and vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ozark National Scenic Riverways

The Ozark National Scenic Riverways boasts 130 miles of hiking trails and 50 miles of designated mountain bike trails. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Ozark Trail: A 19-mile section from the Current River to Rocky Falls.
  • Trail to the Stars: A 7-mile round trip hike for stargazing with a breathtaking view.
  • Powder Mill Trail: A 2.5-mile trail to the rock formations and limestone shut-ins.
  • Falling Spring Trail: A strenuous 3-mile round trip to the cascading waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Activities and Tours

  • Floating on the rivers: A fantastic way to see the park.
  • Fishing: There are plenty of game fish in the riverways.
  • Scenic Drives: The park offers scenic drives with stunning views of the Ozarks.
  • Guided Cave Tours: Explore the underground wonderland of karst caves and springs.
  • Wildlife Watching: Ozark National Scenic Riverways is home to a diverse range of wildlife.
  • Horseback Riding: There are designated horse trails within the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ozark National Scenic Riverways

Day 1: Arrive at the park, check-in, and explore the Ozark National Scenic Riverways Visitor Center.

Day 2: Go on a hiking trip to Round Spring. Afterward, make a short drive to the Big Spring and enjoy a picnic.

Day 3: Go on a guided cave tour at Round Spring Cave. Afterward, head to Buck Hollow for a relaxing hike.

Day 4: Get on the water and enjoy a full day of floating or fishing.

Day 5: Rent a mountain bike and explore the parks designated mountain bike trails.

Day 6: Back to water activities. Enjoy an early morning paddle followed by a picnic at Alley Spring.

Day 7: Make the drive to Rocky Falls for a short hike and to Buck Hollow for the final exploration.

Food and Drink Scene of Ozark National Scenic Riverways

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ: Missouri is famous for its BBQ, and Ozark National Scenic Riverways has many BBQ joints to try.
  • Fried Catfish: A local delicacy, that you can’t miss.
  • Ozark cuisine: Try some of the local delicacies from one of the bakeries or diners.

Local Beverages

  • Local Microbrew: Missouri has many microbreweries, with some local favorites being 4 Hands Brewery, Schlafly Beer, and Urban Chestnut Brewing Company.
  • Missouri Wine: Missouri is the second-largest wine-producing state in America.

Where to Stay in Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Big Spring Lodge: A beautiful lodge with an excellent view of the Big Spring.
  • Campsites: The park has ten campgrounds, providing an opportunity to experience the Ozarks’ natural beauty.
  • Cabins: The park’s cabin rentals are the perfect way to escape to nature.

What to Buy in Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally made crafts: Unique locally made crafts such as quilts and woodworking crafts.
  • Fishing gear: Ozark National Scenic Riverways is a fantastic place to indulge in fishing activities, so make sure to buy the necessary equipment.

Local Culture and Customs of Ozark National Scenic Riverways

The Ozarks is known for its country charm, deep-rooted music, and community spirit. Visitors can explore the local culture by visiting the local towns and farmers markets or attending local performances or festivals.

Best Time to Visit Ozark National Scenic Riverways

The best time to visit Ozark National Scenic Riverways is from May to October when temperatures are warmer. However, spring and fall offer pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ozark National Scenic Riverways

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and leave the natural areas as you found them.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Bring water and snacks for your hikes.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Disturb the local flora and fauna.
  • Hike in the park after dark.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ozark National Scenic Riverways

  • Know the current river conditions and weather before embarking on a hike or float trip.
  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear.
  • Carry a first aid kit and familiarize yourself with basic first aid.

Health in Ozark National Scenic Riverways

  • Watch out for ticks and other insects.
  • Bring enough water and drink plenty of fluids.
