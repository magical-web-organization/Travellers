Introduction to Independence

Nestled in the Kansas City area of Missouri, Independence offers a perfect blend of history, outdoor recreation, and culture. As the hometown of President Harry S. Truman, this city boasts rich heritage and historical treasures that will enthrall history buffs. From hiking and biking to museums and a vibrant food scene, Independence offers something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Independence

Whether you’re traveling solo, as a couple, or with family, Independence has no shortage of places to visit. Here are some recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harry S. Truman Presidential Library and Museum: Get an insight into the life and legacy of the United States’ 33rd president. The museum features exhibits, photos, and interactive displays that highlight his life and presidency.

  • 1859 Jail, Marshal’s Home & Museum: Explore the cramped and dingy confines of the jail and learn about the law and order history of Independence. The museum houses several artifacts related to the town’s history since its settlement in 1827.

  • National Frontier Trails Museum: Discover the legacy and story of America’s pioneers who traveled westward in search of land and wealth. The museum lets visitors experience the journey of the pioneers and the harsh conditions they faced.

Natural Attractions of Independence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clinton Lake State Park: Spend a day hiking, fishing, and boating around the lake while enjoying the scenic views.

  • George Owens Nature Park: Explore the park’s vast network of nature trails that showcase the local wildlife’s diversity.

  • Adventure Oasis Water Park: Cool off in the relaxing pools and exhilarating water slides at the only water park in Independence.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Independence

Independence offers several trails where visitors can immerse themselves in nature and explore places. Here are a few options:

  • Truman Rail Trail: This trail covers 6.7 miles and connects Independence to Kansas City. Ideal for walking, running, and cycling.

  • Little Blue Trace Trail: Stretching across 15 miles, this trail offers a perfect place for jogging and biking.

  • Burr Oak Woods Conservation Area: Situated north of Blue Springs, this park features several trails that offer hiking, picnicking, bird-watching, and more.

Best Things to Do in Independence: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to do in Independence. Here are our recommendations:

  • Harry S. Truman Walking Tour: Take a stroll around Independence’s most historic landmarks and discover significant historical events from Truman’s life.

  • Escape Room: Solve the puzzles at the Escape Room in Independence and create unforgettable memories with your friends and family.

  • Vaile Mansion Tour: Visit the 31-room Queen Anne-style Victorian mansion and marvel at the exceptional craftsmanship and the well-preserved interior design details.

Week-Long Itinerary for Independence

If you plan to stay in Independence for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Independence

The food scene in Independence is diverse, and visitors can enjoy several dishes and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas City Barbecue: Enjoy delicious slow-cooked meats that have made Kansas City famous.

  • Grilled Chicken Salad: Made with fresh lettuce, tomatoes, onions, and grilled chicken breast, this salad is a local staple.

  • Freckled Lemonade: This refreshing and yummy drink contains vanilla and lemonade and tiny pieces of strawberry.

Local Beverages

  • Boulevard Beer: Discover Independence’s local flavor with an array of Boulevard craft beers that are famous for their distinct taste and aroma.

  • Free State Brewing Co.: Grab a pint of locally brewed beer and then take a tour of the brewery itself.

Where to Stay in Independence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to lavish hotels, Independence has a wide range of accommodations to cater to any traveler. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Stoney Creek Hotel and Conference Center: This lodge features spacious, comfortable rooms for guests traveling with family and offers many conveniences such as a fitness center and indoor pool and is ideal for groups.

  • Best Western Truman Inn: This hotel is suitable for guests on a budget and features low-priced rooms with great amenities such as a fully equipped gym and parking.

  • Silver Heart Inn Bed & Breakfast: This bed and breakfast offer an ideal combination of comfort, luxury, and charming historical details, making it best for couples.

What to Buy in Independence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping enthusiasts will love exploring the countless retail shops in Independence. Here are some of the best places to find local products and souvenirs:

  • Missouri Town: Find locally-made pottery, candles, and much more at this marketplace.

  • Independence Square: This quaint shopping district features several stores that sell locally-made garments, accessories, and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Independence

Independence takes pride in its folk traditions, music, and regional cuisine. Visitors can experience regional festivals such as Applefest and take part in local customs by attending events such as Irish Fling and Independence Annual Uptown Chili Cook-Off.

Best Time to Visit Independence

Autumn is the ideal time to travel to Independence. The mild temperatures, fall foliage, and festival season make the city delightful and charming. Spring and Summer are also popular among tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Independence

Do:

  • Learn the history and heritage of Independence by visiting museums and historical places.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

  • Attend the city’s festivals and invest time in the local cultural scene.

Don’t:

  • Litter or violate basic environmental norms.

  • Engage in loud and inappropriate behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Independence

Independence is a safe city to visit, but visitors should be cautious and protect themselves from petty theft. Be careful in public spaces, pay attention to your surroundings, and avoid walking in low-light areas at night.

Health in Independence

Before traveling to Independence, consult with your healthcare provider to get the necessary vaccinations. Be cautious about food hygiene and drink enough water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Independence: Transportation Options and Logistics

Independence is easily accessible via multiple transportation routes. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Independence

Kansas City International Airport is the closest airport with multiple airlines servicing it daily.

Bus Stations Near Independence

Greyhound buses serve Independence, and the bus terminal is located downtown.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing apps such as Uber and Lyft are widely available in Independence.

How to Get Around in Independence

Visitors can rent a car or use the public transportation network consisting of buses.

Where to Go Next from Independence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Exploring nearby areas is a great way to extend your trip. Here are some ideas:

  • Kansas City: Visit museums like the Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art and the National World War I Museum.

  • Blue Springs: Explore the vast conservation area.

Conclusion

Independence is an excellent combination of culture, history, and outdoor recreation. Whether you’re a solo traveler or traveling with family, plan your trip to Independence and experience what the city has to offer.
