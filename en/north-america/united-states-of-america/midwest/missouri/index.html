Introduction to Missouri

Missouri, located in the Midwest region of the United States, is a state that offers a diverse range of activities and experiences. The state is known for its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or delicious local cuisine, Missouri has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will explore the top things to do in Missouri and help you plan your perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Missouri

Missouri has a variety of attractions to explore, including museums, historic sites, natural wonders, and more. Here are some of the top places to visit in Missouri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gateway Arch, St. Louis: One of the most iconic landmarks in the United States, the Gateway Arch is a 630-foot-tall stainless-steel monument that symbolizes the country’s westward expansion. Visitors can take a tram ride to the top for stunning views of the city.

  • Mark Twain Boyhood Home and Museum, Hannibal: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of one of America’s most beloved writers, Mark Twain. Visitors can tour the author’s childhood home and learn about his life and work.

  • Harry S. Truman Presidential Library and Museum, Independence: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of the 33rd President of the United States. Visitors can see exhibits on the Cold War, Truman’s presidency, and more.

Natural Attractions of Missouri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake of the Ozarks: This massive man-made lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can also hike in the surrounding Ozark Mountains or explore nearby caves.

  • Missouri Botanical Garden, St. Louis: The Missouri Botanical Garden is one of the oldest botanical gardens in the United States, with over 79 acres of beautiful displays and gardens. Visitors can stroll through the Japanese Garden, explore the Climatron conservatory, and more.

  • Ha Ha Tonka State Park, Camdenton: This beautiful state park features rugged bluffs, forests, and caves. Visitors can hike the trails, explore the castle ruins, or enjoy a picnic by the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Missouri

Missouri has numerous trails for hiking and biking, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the state’s natural beauty up close. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Missouri:

  • Katy Trail State Park: This 240-mile long trail runs along the Missouri River and is one of the longest rail-trails in the United States. Visitors can bike or hike the trail and pass through charming small towns along the way.

  • Ozark Trail: The Ozark Trail is a 500-mile trail that winds through the scenic Ozarks. The trail offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, backpacking, and camping, with stunning views of waterfalls, rock formations, and more.

Best Things to Do in Missouri: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions mentioned above, Missouri has countless other activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Missouri:

  • Visit the Anheuser-Busch Brewery, St. Louis: Beer lovers should visit the Anheuser-Busch Brewery in St. Louis, where they can take a tour of the facility and learn about the brewing process. And of course, sample some of the famous Budweiser beer.

  • Visit the Silver Dollar City Theme Park, Branson: Silver Dollar City is a 1880s-themed amusement park with roller coasters, attractions, and live shows. Visitors can also learn about traditional crafts like blacksmithing and glassblowing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Missouri

If you have a week to spend in Missouri, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Missouri

Missouri is known for its delicious barbecue, but the state has much more to offer in terms of cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • St. Louis-style BBQ: St. Louis-style barbecue is known for its dry rub and sweet tomato-based sauce, and is often served with slaw and baked beans.

  • Toasted Ravioli: Toasted ravioli is a St. Louis specialty – deep-fried ravioli served with marinara sauce.

  • Gooey Butter Cake: This St. Louis dessert is a rich, buttery cake with a gooey filling.

Local Beverages

  • Anheuser-Busch Beer: Anheuser-Busch, based in St. Louis, is one of the largest brewing companies in the world. Visitors to the area can take a tour of the brewery and sample some of their famous beers.

  • Missouri Wine: Missouri is the oldest wine region in the United States, with over 125 wineries in the state producing a variety of wines.

Where to Stay in Missouri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Missouri has numerous options for accommodation, from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some top choices in the state:

  • The Ritz-Carlton, St. Louis: This luxury hotel in downtown St. Louis offers stunning views of the city skyline, a rooftop bar, and a spa.

  • Drury Plaza Hotel, Columbia: This centrally-located hotel in Columbia offers upscale amenities at an affordable price, including a hot breakfast, evening drinks, and a rooftop pool.

  • Cliff Manor Bed and Breakfast, Hannibal: This charming bed and breakfast in Hannibal offers views of the Mississippi River and a farm-to-table breakfast.

What to Buy in Missouri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Missouri, there are a number of local products and souvenirs to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are a few ideas:

  • Budweiser Merchandise: If you are a fan of Budweiser beer, you can purchase merchandise at the Anheuser-Busch Brewery.

  • Missouri Wine: Missouri wines are unique and delicious, making them a great souvenir to bring home.

  • Handmade Crafts: Missouri has a number of artisanal craft shops where you can buy handmade pottery, jewelry, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Missouri

Missouri is known for its friendly Midwestern culture, and visitors should expect warm hospitality and a relaxed pace of life. Visitors should also expect to encounter a variety of local traditions and customs, such as tailgating at sporting events, barbecuing, and celebrating Mardi Gras in St. Louis.

Best Time to Visit Missouri

The best time to visit Missouri depends on the activities you have planned. Spring and fall offer mild temperatures and beautiful foliage, making them great for outdoor activities. Summer is a popular time for festivals and events, but it can also be hot and humid. Winter is often cold and snowy, but visitors can enjoy holiday events and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Missouri

Do:

  • Try the barbecue – it’s a Missouri specialty.

  • Visit both Kansas City and St. Louis – both cities have their own unique charm.

  • Take advantage of the state’s natural beauty with hiking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Discount the charms of smaller towns and cities – Missouri has a number of hidden gems.

  • Forget to try the local beer and wine.

  • Be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals – Missourians are friendly and welcoming.

Safety and Health

Safety in Missouri

Missouri is generally a safe state, but visitors should take normal safety precautions such as locking car doors, being aware of surroundings, and avoiding areas that appear unsafe. Visitors should also take appropriate precautions when hiking or camping in the state’s parks and natural areas.

Health in Missouri

Visitors to Missouri should take normal health precautions, including drinking bottled water or treated water, wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, and being aware of ticks and other outdoor hazards.

Getting to Missouri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Missouri is easily accessible by car or plane. The state has several airports, including Lambert-St. Louis International Airport, Kansas City International Airport, and Springfield-Branson National Airport. Visitors can also take a Greyhound bus or Amtrak train to major cities in the state.

Airports Near Missouri

  • Lambert-St. Louis International Airport: Located in St. Louis, this is the largest airport in the state.

  • Kansas City International Airport: Located in Kansas City, this airport offers non-stop flights to many U.S. cities.

  • Springfield-Branson National Airport: This airport serves Springfield and Branson, and offers flights on several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Missouri

  • Greyhound: Greyhound offers bus service to several stations in Missouri, including St. Louis, Kansas City, and Springfield.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Amtrak trains serve several cities in Missouri, including St. Louis, Kansas City, and Jefferson City.

How to Get Around in Missouri

Missouri is best explored by car, but visitors can also take advantage of public transportation options in larger cities like St. Louis and Kansas City. The state’s rural areas and natural attractions are best visited by car.

Where to Go Next from Missouri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Missouri is centrally located, making it easy to explore other Midwestern destinations. Nearby options include:

  • Chicago, Illinois: Just a few hours’ drive from St. Louis, Chicago is a world-class city with attractions like the Magnificent Mile, the Field Museum, and Navy Pier.

  • Oklahoma City, Oklahoma: Oklahoma City is less than a day’s drive from many Missouri cities, and offers attractions like the National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum and the Oklahoma City National Memorial.

  • Kansas City, Kansas: Just a few hours’ drive from St. Louis, Kansas City is known for its barbecue, jazz scene, and historic sites like the National World War I Museum.

Conclusion

Missouri is a diverse and dynamic state with an abundance of activities, attractions, and experiences to offer visitors. From exploring natural wonders to learning about the state’s rich history and culture, Missouri has something for everyone. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Missouri. Happy travels!
