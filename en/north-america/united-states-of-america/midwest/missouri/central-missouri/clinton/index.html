Introduction to Clinton

Nestled in the heart of the Midwest, in the state of Missouri, lies the charming town of Clinton. Known for its friendly locals, historical sites, and breathtaking scenery, Clinton is an ideal destination for both nature lovers and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Clinton

Clinton has an array of attractions to suit every taste and interest. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, here are the top things to see and do in Clinton.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Henry County Museum and Cultural Arts Center: Learn about the history of Henry County through a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  • Truman Lake Historical Museum: Discover the history of Truman Lake and its impact on the community, as well as the local history of Clinton and Henry County.
  • Golden Valley Memorial Hospital: Take a guided tour of this modern hospital and learn about its history and importance to the community.

Natural Attractions of Clinton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Truman Lake: With over 55,000 acres, Truman Lake is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Camping, fishing, boating, and hiking are all popular activities in the area.
  • Shawnee Bend Golf Course: Enjoy a round of golf in the midst of stunning natural beauty.
  • Clinton Community Garden: Stroll through the community garden and admire the beautiful flowers and produce.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clinton

Clinton has something for everyone when it comes to hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes for exploring the great outdoors in and around the town:

  • Truman Lake Mountain Bike Park: With over 8 miles of trail, this park is perfect for both beginner and advanced mountain bikers.
  • Katy Trail: This 240-mile trail is Missouri’s longest rails-to-trails project and passes through Clinton.
  • Harry S. Truman State Park: This park offers several trails, from easy to challenging, with stunning lake views on the Onondaga Trail.

Best Things to Do in Clinton: Activities and Tours

  • Clinton Aquatic Center: Cool off and have fun with the whole family at the Clinton Aquatic Center, featuring multiple pools and water slides.
  • Truman Dam Visitor Center: Learn about the Truman Dam and its impact on the community, as well as the importance of water conservation.
  • Clinton Fireplace Center: Experience the rich history and craftsmanship of fireplace designs and wood stoves at this unique center.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clinton

Day 1: Explore the quaint downtown area and shop for unique gifts and locally made items.

Day 2: Visit the Henry County Museum and Cultural Arts Center to learn about the history and culture of the area.

Day 3: Take a bike ride on the Katy Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the Missouri countryside.

Day 4: Hike the Onondaga Trail in Harry S. Truman State Park and enjoy the stunning views of Truman Lake.

Day 5: Visit the Truman Dam Visitor Center and learn about the dam’s historical significance and role in the community.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of Golden Valley Memorial Hospital and learn about the modern healthcare services provided in the area.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings of Clinton by visiting one of the local parks or taking a leisurely walk around town.

Food and Drink Scene of Clinton

Clinton is home to a variety of dining options, from casual cafes to high-end restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Clinton is famous for its delicious fried chicken, and you can find it at many local restaurants.
  • BBQ: Missouri is known for its BBQ, and Clinton is no exception. Try the delicious smoked meat at one of the local BBQ joints.
  • Apple Pie: Nothing says “American” like a slice of homemade apple pie. Try one at one of the local bakeries.

Local Beverages

  • Missouri Wine: Missouri is the second-largest wine-producing state in the U.S., and you can taste the local wines at several wineries and vineyards in the area.
  • Golden Valley Brewery Beer: Taste the locally brewed beer at the Golden Valley Brewery and experience the unique flavors of Clinton.

Where to Stay in Clinton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clinton offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Clinton:

  • Holiday Inn Express: This mid-range hotel offers clean, comfortable rooms and a complimentary hot breakfast.
  • Super 8: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • The Guesthouse at Dreamer’s Paradise: This charming guesthouse offers a cozy, home-like atmosphere and a peaceful rural setting.

What to Buy in Clinton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Clinton has several unique shops and boutiques that offer locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some ideas for what to bring home:

  • Clinton Antique Mall: Shop for vintage and antique items, including furniture, jewelry, and artwork.
  • Missouri Made: This boutique features locally made products, including handmade soaps, candles, and pottery.
  • Clinton Farmers Market: Pick up locally grown produce, fresh eggs, and other farm-to-table items at this popular market.

Local Culture and Customs of Clinton

Clinton is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and way of life. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Friendliness: The locals in Clinton are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation or offer a helping hand.
  • Rural Lifestyle: Clinton is a rural community, so visitors should be prepared for a slower pace of life and a focus on outdoor activities and nature.
  • Respect: As with any community, visitors should show respect for local customs, traditions, and beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Clinton

The best time to visit Clinton depends on your interests and preferences. If you enjoy outdoor activities and mild weather, spring and fall are the best times to visit. Summers can be hot and humid, but they are also the peak season for boating and water sports on Truman Lake. Winters can be cold, but visitors can still enjoy cozy indoor activities like exploring museums and shopping for unique gifts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clinton

Do:

  • Take advantage of outdoor activities: Clinton has plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and boating, so be sure to explore the great outdoors.
  • Engage with the locals: The locals in Clinton are friendly and welcoming, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation or ask for recommendations.
  • Embrace the local culture: Experience the unique culture and way of life in Clinton by trying the local food, shopping for locally made products, and learning about the history and traditions of the area.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather: Clinton experiences hot, humid summers and cold, snowy winters, so be sure to pack appropriately for the season.
  • Disrespect the natural environment: As with any outdoor destination, visitors should respect the natural environment and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Expect a bustling city: Clinton is a rural community, so visitors should expect a slower pace of life and fewer amenities than in a larger city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clinton

Clinton is a safe community, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables. In case of emergencies, dial 911 for assistance.

Health in Clinton

Clinton has several medical facilities, including Golden Valley Memorial Hospital, Clinics of South West Missouri - Clinton, and Truman Lake Dental. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving in Clinton.

Getting to Clinton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clinton is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting to and around Clinton:

Airports Near Clinton

  • Kansas City International Airport (MCI): Located approximately 90 miles northeast of Clinton, MCI is the closest major airport.
  • Springfield-Branson National Airport (SGF): Located approximately 100 miles south of Clinton, SGF is another major airport option.

Bus Stations Near Clinton

  • Jefferson Bus Lines: This bus line offers service to several cities in Missouri and surrounding states, with stops in Clinton.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Several rental car companies, including Enterprise and Hertz, have locations in or near Clinton.
  • Taxi: Several taxi companies operate in Clinton and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Clinton

Clinton is a small town, and many attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car or use a taxi service. Biking is also a popular option for getting around in the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Clinton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clinton is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding areas of Missouri. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Kansas City: Located approximately 90 miles northeast of Clinton, Kansas City is known for its BBQ, jazz music, and museums.
  • Lake of the Ozarks: Located approximately 80 miles southeast of Clinton, Lake of the Ozarks offers several outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking.
  • St. Louis: Located approximately 190 miles east of Clinton, St. Louis is home to the Gateway Arch, the St. Louis Zoo, and the Missouri Botanical Garden.

Conclusion

Clinton may be a small town, but it offers plenty of things to see and do for visitors of all ages and interests. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Clintin is a destination worth exploring. Be sure to sample the local cuisine, meet the friendly locals, and embrace the unique culture of this charming Missouri town.
