Introduction to Columbia

Columbia is a hidden gem in Central Missouri, a small but vibrant city that offers something for everyone. With a charming downtown, numerous outdoor attractions, and a rich history and culture, Columbia is an ideal destination for families, outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies alike. In this guide, we will lead you through some of the best attractions, restaurants, outdoor activities, and cultural hotspots that Columbia has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Columbia

No trip to Columbia would be complete without exploring some of the city’s unique and exciting attractions. Here are a few must-visit places in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Art and Archaeology: Nestled on the University of Missouri campus, this museum boasts world-class art collections and archeological treasures from all around the world.
  • Boone County History and Culture Center: A fascinating museum that showcases the history and culture of the Boone County region, including exhibits on local architecture, agriculture, and the Civil War.
  • Mark Twain Boyhood Home and Museum: Located in nearby Hannibal, Missouri, this museum celebrates the famous American author Mark Twain and showcases his boyhood home and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Columbia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rock Bridge Memorial State Park: This park is a nature lover’s paradise, with hiking trails, caves, sinkholes, and natural rock bridges.
  • Finger Lakes State Park: Known for its incredible off-road motorcycle trails, this state park is an adrenaline-filled adventure waiting to happen.
  • Capen Park: For a breathtaking view of the Missouri River, check out this park’s lookout point.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbia

Columbia is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with some fantastic hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of the best routes:

  • Katy Trail State Park: This 237-mile trail is one of the most beautiful bike routes in the United States, with stunning views of the Missouri River and breathtaking vistas.
  • Grindstone Nature Area: With over 200 acres of rolling hills and dense forests, this nature reserve offers some of the best hiking trails in the area.
  • Three Creeks Conservation Area: Located on the outskirts of Columbia, this conservation area is perfect for a day-long hiking adventure, with trails that lead through fields, woods, and along the creek.

Best Things to Do in Columbia: Activities and Tours

Here are a few of the best things to do in Columbia:

  • Columbia Art Walk: Held on the first Friday of every month, this art walk is a great way to experience local galleries, shops, and eateries.
  • Shelter Gardens: This botanical garden is free to the public and features gorgeous flowers, waterfalls, and fountains.
  • Columbia Farmers Market: A vibrant market that showcases local produce, crafts, and more, the farmers market is a must-visit spot for foodies.

Food and Drink Scene of Columbia

Columbia has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties to enjoy. Here are a few of the best things to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shakespeare’s Pizza: A legendary local joint that serves up fantastic pizza and calzones.
  • Harold’s Donuts: A local favorite that has been serving up sweet treats since 1956.
  • Booches Billiard Hall: A dive bar with fantastic burgers and a unique, old-school atmosphere.

Local Beverages

  • Bur Oak Brewing Company: This local brewery is known for its delicious beers and relaxed atmosphere.
  • Sparky’s Homemade Ice Cream: This ice cream parlor serves up unique flavors made from fresh, local ingredients.
  • Lakota Coffee Company: A popular local coffee shop that roasts its beans on-site.

Where to Stay in Columbia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of excellent options for lodging in Columbia, no matter your budget or travel style. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • The Tiger Hotel: A luxury hotel in the heart of downtown Columbia, this spot boasts elegant rooms and plenty of amenities.
  • The Gathering Place Bed and Breakfast: A charming, cozy B&B with comfortable rooms and a beautifully landscaped yard.
  • Hostelling International Columbia: A budget-friendly option for travelers, this hostel offers clean dorm rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Columbia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of unique local products to bring home from Columbia. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • The District: Columbia’s downtown shopping district is home to a variety of fashionable boutiques, vintage stores, and gift shops.
  • True/False Film Festival merchandise: If you’re lucky enough to visit Columbia during the annual True/False Film Festival, be sure to pick up some merchandise or take advantage of festival discounts.
  • Missouri wines: Missouri is home to some fantastic wineries, so be sure to pick up a bottle or two to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Columbia

Columbia is a welcoming, laid-back city with a diverse population and a strong sense of community. Visitors should be prepared for a friendly, down-to-earth atmosphere and plenty of opportunities to connect with locals.

Best Time to Visit Columbia

Columbia experiences all four seasons, with hot summers, cold winters, and pleasant spring and fall weather. The best time to visit depends on your interests and activities. For outdoor adventurers, spring and fall are ideal for hiking and exploring; for foodies, the summer months are the best time to enjoy local produce and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbia

Do:

  • Do visit the beautiful parks and hiking trails around Columbia.
  • Do try local cuisine and support local businesses.
  • Do visit during one of the many local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the natural attractions of Columbia.
  • Don’t overindulge in alcohol or disturb the peace in public places.
  • Don’t forget to learn about the local history and culture of Columbia.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbia

Columbia is a relatively safe city, but as with any destination, visitors should exercise common sense and take normal precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings in public places, and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Columbia

Columbia has a few hospitals and several clinics, so medical care is easily accessible. Visitors should consider purchasing travel insurance before visiting to ensure they are financially prepared for any unexpected health issues.

Getting to Columbia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Columbia is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Columbia

  • Columbia Regional Airport (COU)
  • Lambert-St. Louis International Airport (STL)
  • Kansas City International Airport (MCI)

Bus Stations Near Columbia

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located at 1315 Wilkes Blvd, Columbia.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Station: Jefferson City, MO. From Jefferson City, visitors can rent a car or transfer to a bus for the final leg of their journey.

How to Get Around in Columbia

The city is relatively compact, so walking and biking are both excellent options for getting around. There are also several taxi services available, as well as a reliable public transportation system.

Where to Go Next from Columbia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Columbia is ideally located for exploring the natural attractions and quaint towns of the Midwest. Here are a few nearby destinations worth considering:

  • Lake of the Ozarks: A popular vacation spot with plenty of outdoor activities and a beautiful lake.
  • Hermann: A charming, historic town in Missouri’s wine country.
  • St. Louis: A major city with plenty of cultural attractions and impressive architecture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural hotspots, delicious food, or a laid-back atmosphere, Columbia has something to offer. We hope this guide has provided you with some inspiration for your next trip to this charming Missouri city.
