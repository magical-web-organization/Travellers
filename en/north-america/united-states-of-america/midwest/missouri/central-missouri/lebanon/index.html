Introduction to Lebanon

Located in central Missouri, Lebanon is a small city with a vibrant history and numerous attractions. The city is home to several culturally significant museums and historical sites, as well as a variety of natural attractions, including parks with hiking and biking trails, streams, and lakes. Lebanon is also known for its delicious cuisine, local culture, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a longer stay, there’s plenty to see and experience in Lebanon.

List of Places to Visit in Lebanon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Lebanon-Laclede County Library

Located in the heart of downtown Lebanon, the Lebanon-Laclede County Library houses a variety of media, including books, DVDs, audiobooks, and e-books. The library also offers meeting rooms, public access computers, and free Wi-Fi.

2. Route 66 Museum

The Route 66 Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the iconic highway that ran through Lebanon. The museum features artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia detailing the highway’s history, as well as exhibits on the city’s automotive industry.

3. Munger-Moss Motel

The Munger-Moss Motel is a historic motel on Route 66 that retains much of its original design and furnishings. A popular destination for travelers on the Mother Road, the motel has hosted numerous celebrities and is often used as a backdrop for film and television productions.

Natural Attractions of Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Bennett Spring State Park

Bennett Spring State Park is a sprawling park with plenty of activities for nature lovers. From fishing and boating to hiking and camping, there’s something for everyone here. The park also features a variety of trails, including the Natural Tunnel Trail and the Savanna Ridge Trail, which offer scenic views of the park’s streams and forests.

2. Boswell Park

Boswell Park is a popular destination for picnicking, hiking, and birdwatching. The park features several trails, including the Frisco Highline Trail, which runs through the heart of the city and offers views of the nearby Ozark Mountains.

3. Bennett Spring Hatchery

Owned and operated by the Missouri Department of Conservation, the Bennett Spring Hatchery is a great place to learn about fish and their habitats. Visitors can take a tour of the hatchery, watch fish being raised, and even participate in fishing clinics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lebanon

1. Frisco Highline Trail

The Frisco Highline Trail is a 35-mile trail that runs from Bolivar to Springfield. The trail passes through several towns, including Lebanon, and offers scenic views of the surrounding wilderness.

2. Bennett Spring State Park Trails

Bennett Spring State Park features several walking trails, including the Natural Tunnel Trail and the Savanna Ridge Trail. These trails offer views of the park’s streams and forests and are suitable for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Lebanon: Activities and Tours

1. Bennett Spring Fishing

One of the most popular activities in Lebanon is fishing at Bennett Spring State Park. The park is home to several species of fish, including rainbow trout, and offers a variety of fishing opportunities, including fly fishing and catch-and-release fishing.

2. Ozark Mountain Ziplines

For adrenaline junkies looking for a thrill, Ozark Mountain Ziplines offers a variety of zipline tours through the Ozark Mountains. Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness while flying through the trees.

Food and Drink Scene of Lebanon

Must-Try Dishes

1. Barbecue

Lebanon is known for its delicious barbecue, and there are several restaurants in the city that specialize in this regional cuisine. From pulled pork sandwiches to ribs, there’s something for every barbecue lover in Lebanon.

2. Fried Chicken

Another local favorite is fried chicken. Many restaurants in the area offer this southern classic, and it’s a great option for a quick lunch or dinner.

Local Beverages

1. Route 66 Root Beer

Route 66 Root Beer is a locally produced soft drink that’s a favorite among residents and visitors alike. Brewed with real cane sugar, this root beer has a unique flavor that’s not to be missed.

2. Missouri Wine

Missouri is known for its wine, and Lebanon is no exception. There are several wineries in the area that offer tastings and tours, and it’s a great way to experience the local culture.

Where to Stay in Lebanon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Dowd’s Catfish & BBQ Bed and Breakfast

Dowd’s Catfish & BBQ Bed and Breakfast is a unique lodging option that offers guests the chance to stay in a cozy cabin while enjoying the best of southern cuisine. The B&B is located just a few minutes from Bennett Spring State Park.

2. Super 8 by Wyndham Lebanon

For those looking for a more traditional hotel experience, the Super 8 by Wyndham Lebanon offers comfortable rooms and a variety of amenities, including free Wi-Fi and a complimentary continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Lebanon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Route 66 Memorabilia

Lebanon is located on the historic Route 66, and there are plenty of gift shops and antique stores in the area that sell Route 66 memorabilia. From T-shirts to coffee mugs, there’s something for every type of souvenir collector.

2. Amish Goods

Lebanon is located near several Amish communities, and there are several shops in the area that sell handmade quilts, furniture, and other goods. These items make great souvenirs or gifts for loved ones back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lebanon

Lebanon is a friendly city with a strong sense of community. Visitors will often find locals greeting them with a smile and offering assistance if needed. The city is also proud of its history and heritage, and there are several events throughout the year, such as the Lebanon Route 66 Festival, that celebrate this legacy.

Best Time to Visit Lebanon

The best time to visit Lebanon is during the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Visitors can enjoy the many outdoor activities and events that take place during these seasons without having to contend with summer crowds or winter weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lebanon

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the barbecue and fried chicken.
  • Do visit the Route 66 Museum and take a tour of the Munger-Moss Motel.
  • Do take a fishing trip at Bennett Spring State Park or go on a zipline tour at Ozark Mountain Ziplines.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions, and be mindful of local residents by obeying traffic laws and keeping noise levels down.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural landmarks or attractions.
  • Don’t ignore safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or fishing.
  • Don’t be intrusive when taking photographs or filming in public areas.
  • Don’t leave without trying Route 66 Root Beer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lebanon

Lebanon is a safe city, but visitors should still take the same precautions they would in any unfamiliar place. It’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night. It’s also a good idea to keep valuables locked in a safe or secure location.

Health in Lebanon

Health services are widely available in the city, and there are several clinics and hospitals in the area. It’s always a good idea to carry a first aid kit and any necessary medications with you when traveling.

Getting to Lebanon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lebanon

The closest airport to Lebanon is Springfield-Branson National Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away. Other nearby airports include Kansas City International Airport and Lambert-St. Louis International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lebanon

Greyhound offers service to Lebanon from several nearby cities, including Springfield and St. Louis.

Other Options

Lebanon is easily accessible by car, as it is located on major highways including Route 66 and I-44. Rental cars are available at the nearby airports and in the city.

How to Get Around in Lebanon

The easiest way to get around in Lebanon is by car. Several rental car companies operate in the area, and there are also several taxi and shuttle services available. Biking and walking are also options for those who prefer to explore the city on foot.

Where to Go Next from Lebanon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Springfield

Located approximately 45 minutes north of Lebanon, Springfield is a vibrant city with a variety of attractions, including museums, parks, and a popular downtown area with numerous shops and restaurants.

2. Lake of the Ozarks

Located approximately an hour east of Lebanon, the Lake of the Ozarks is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and water sports. The lake is also home to numerous resorts and vacation rentals.

Conclusion

Lebanon is a charming city with a rich history, a variety of attractions, and a warm and welcoming community. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, exploring local culture, or simply relaxing and enjoying some delicious food, there’s plenty to see and experience in Lebanon.
