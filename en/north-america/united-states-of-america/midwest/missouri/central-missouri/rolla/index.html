Introduction to Rolla

Rolla, located in Central Missouri, is a picturesque city that is the quintessential snapshot of small-town America. From its historic landmarks to natural beauty, there is something for everyone. Known for its world-class research-intensive university, Missouri University of Science and Technology, Rolla is a hub of academic excellence and innovation.

List of Places to Visit in Rolla

Whether you are interested in culture, nature, or history, Rolla has an array of attractions to offer.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rolla is home to a number of museums that showcase the city’s cultural heritage and history. The Missouri S&T Minerals Museum is a must-visit attraction for people of all ages. It houses an impressive collection of rocks and minerals, including the largest collection of fluorescent minerals in the world. The Memoryville USA Car Museum showcases classic cars, while the Old Stagecoach Stop Museum provides a glimpse into the past of the Ozarks. The Allgood-Bailey Stadium is a landmark for sports enthusiasts and has a rich history.

Natural Attractions of Rolla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Visitors to Rolla can explore the iconic attractions of the Ozarks, such as the Mark Twain National Forest, which provides ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and fishing. The Rolla Lions Club Park is another ideal spot for a picnic and enjoying nature, while the Spring Creek Park & Trail is perfect for a tranquil walk. Scenic views such as the Forum Waterfall and the Devil’s Elbow are breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rolla

The Ozark Trail, a scenic hiking trail system, stretches over 350 miles of Missouri wilderness and is a world-famous destination for hikers and nature lovers. The Katy Trail State Park and Green Rock Trail offer good alternatives to explore the peaceful countryside and scenic beauty of Rolla. Bicycle enthusiasts have the Little Prairie Trail, a 7-mile paved trail that runs through the city’s outskirts and connects to the Route 66 trail system.

Best Things to Do in Rolla: Activities and Tours

For the curious traveler, there are a variety of activities and tours to choose from. Visitors can explore the history of Rolla with the Haunted Walk of Rolla or the Rolla Downtown Walking Tour. For those interested in science and technology, a tour of the Missouri S&T campus is a must. The Phelps County Winery offers wine tasting and a chance to enjoy live music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rolla

A week-long trip to Rolla is a perfect way to experience the city’s beauty and charm. Spend the first day exploring Rolla’s downtown area and historic landmarks such as the Old Stagecoach Stop Museum. Day two can be spent hiking and exploring the picturesque beauty of the Ozarks. Day three can be reserved for a trip to the Missouri S&T campus and enjoying some of the cultural attractions of the city. Day four can be an adventurous day spent biking on the Little Prairie Trail or enjoying a wine-tasting tour at the Phelps County Winery. Day five can be spent exploring the scenic views of Rolla. Day six can be dedicated to rest and relaxation at local cafes and parks, and day seven can be used to explore the surrounding area and neighboring towns such as St. James and Steelville.

Food and Drink Scene of Rolla

The food and drink scene of Rolla is as rich and diverse as its cultural heritage. Visitors can indulge in some of the best local food and beer offerings.

Must-Try Dishes

For a taste of the Ozarks, try the local specialty Fried Chicken or the Smoked Beef Brisket. The Greenbean House offers mouth-watering sandwiches, while the Alex’s Pizza Palace satisfies all your pizza cravings.

Local Beverages

Rolla has a number of breweries and wineries that showcase the unique flavors of the region. The Public House Brewing Company is a must-visit, and the St. James Winery Tasting Room is perfect for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Rolla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rolla has a wide range of accommodations to fit every budget and preference. There are numerous hotels and motels in the city center, such as the Best Western Coachlight and Hampton Inn Rolla, that offer quality services and affordable rates. For more of a local experience, try staying at one of the cozy Airbnbs or guesthouses.

What to Buy in Rolla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rolla’s local markets and gift shops are perfect for buying souvenirs that capture the city’s essence. The Westside Marketplace and Ozark Hills Soap Company offer quality and unique products such as homemade soaps, ceramics, and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Rolla

Rolla is a friendly and welcoming city where hospitality is one of its most defining characteristics. The city prides itself on its heritage and values.

Best Time to Visit Rolla

Rolla is a year-round destination with each season offering unique experiences. Summer is the busiest and is ideal for outdoor activities, while spring and fall provide mild weather and picturesque foliage. Winter is perfect for indoor activities such as museum tours and exploring local cafes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rolla

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Explore the scenic beauty of Ozarks.
  • Visit the Missouri S&T campus.

Don’t:

  • Litter. It’s a punishable offense.
  • Take photographs without permission of local residents.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rolla

Rolla is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to practice basic safety measures such as securing valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Rolla

Rolla has a good healthcare system with several hospitals and clinics. It is recommended that visitors purchase appropriate travel insurance.

Getting to Rolla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rolla is accessible by various transportation modes and has a good public transportation system.

Airports Near Rolla

The closest airport is Waynesville-St. Robert Regional Airport, located about 17 miles from Rolla. Lambert–St. Louis International Airport is about 100 miles from Rolla.

Bus Stations Near Rolla

The Greyhound Bus Station in Rolla is a major hub for intercity bus services.

Other Options

Rolla is easily accessible via car, and several car rental companies are available at the airport and in the city center.

How to Get Around in Rolla

Rolla is a small city with good public transportation services such as buses and taxis. Visitors also have the option to hire a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Rolla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Rolla, visitors can explore the neighboring cities such as St. James, Steelville and Cuba. A short drive takes visitors to the Famous Route 66, with significant landmarks related to the highway’s history.

Conclusion

Rolla is the perfect destination for those who want to explore the beautiful countryside and experience small-town America. With its rich history, cultural heritage, and scenic beauty, Rolla is a must-visit destination for travelers.
