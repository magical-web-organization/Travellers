Introduction to Lake Ozark

Located in Central Missouri, Lake Ozark is a charming town that sits by the Lake of the Ozarks. Known as one of Missouri’s most popular tourist destinations, Lake Ozark offers a range of adventures, outdoor activities, scenic views, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a family, or a group of adventurers, Lake Ozark has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Ozark

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bagnell Dam: A historic landmark stretching over a mile in length, the Bagnell Dam was built in the 1930s and generates hydropower for the area. Visitors can explore the dam’s history at the nearby visitor center.

  • Lake of the Ozarks State Park: The largest state park in Missouri, this park covers more than 17,000 acres of land and water. It offers hiking trails, caves, beaches, campsites, and picnic areas.

  • Ozark Distillery and Brewery: This local distillery and brewery is a popular spot for tastings and tours. Visitors can learn about the processes of making spirits, beer, and wine while enjoying the scenic views of the lake.

Natural Attractions of Lake Ozark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ha Ha Tonka State Park: Just a few miles from Lake Ozark, Ha Ha Tonka State Park is home to stunning natural attractions such as cave formations, natural bridges, and beautiful hiking trails.

  • Lake of the Ozarks Waterfalls: This stunning waterfall can only be seen by boat but it is worth the trip. It is a must visit if you are searching for scenic views and relaxing natural scenes.

  • Bridal Cave: This magnificent natural cave, which is more than 350 million years old and contains a beautiful underground lake, is a great place to explore. Visitors can join a guided tour or explore on their own.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Ozark

  • The State Park Trail: This trail stretches for 14.0 miles through the Ozark hills and is suitable for hiking and biking. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful views of the lake, forests, and cliffs.

  • Horseshoe Bend Trail: A 5.0 mile trail that winds through dense woods, rocky areas, and beautiful views. It is an excellent trail for hiking or mountain biking.

  • Bearskin Trail: The 3.0-mile trail, located near the village of Four Seasons, is perfect for hiking, with beautiful vistas of the lake and the Ozarks.

Best Things to Do in Lake Ozark: Activities and Tours

  • Boating: Lake Ozark is a water-based destination; hence one of the most popular activities is boating. Visitors can rent a boat or take a chartered tour to explore the lake’s main channel, hidden coves, and calm waterways.

  • Fishing: Fishing enthusiasts may enjoy trying their luck in the lake’s warm waters, which are home to a variety of fish species like largemouth bass and catfish. Some local companies offer guided fishing tours.

  • Golfing: For golf enthusiasts, the Lake of the Ozarks has many beautiful golf courses, including the Ridge Golf Course located just south of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Ozark

Must-Try Dishes

  • Barbeque: Missouri-style barbeque is a must-try cuisine in Lake Ozark. Try the mouth-watering ribs, pulled pork, beef brisket cooked to perfection.

  • Fish Frys: A midwestern specialty made with fresh caught fish and pay homage to the fishing culture surrounding the lake.

  • Homestyle Cooking: Enjoy meals made from scratch at Lake Ozark’s quaint diners and restaurants like Black Bear Lodge in Horseshoe Bend and York Shack in Camdenton.

Local Beverages

  • Wines: Missouri’s wine country surrounds the lake, and the area is known for many delicious wines. Local wine spots to check out include Shawnee Bluff Winery.

  • Breweries: Lake of the Ozarks has a growing craft beer scene, such as H. Toad’s Bar and Brewery, which is situated near the water.

Where to Stay in Lake Ozark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Lodge at Port Arrowhead: Overlooking the lake, the Lodge at Port Arrowhead is a great option for visitors who want to stay in modern accommodations with all the best amenities.

  • Town & Country Motel: This is a budget-friendly option for visitors who want to stay near the lake. It offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and easy access to local dining and shopping.

  • Camden on the Lake Resort: Located at the west end of the lake, this luxury resort has something for everyone, including private balconies, hot tubs, and restaurants.

What to Buy in Lake Ozark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ozarks Pottery: There are many local studios in the area selling handmade Ozarks pottery.

  • Missouri Wine and other Sauces: Take home a bottle of your favorite local wine or try tasty Missouri sauces.

  • Lake Themed Merchandise: Find personalized gifts for friends and family at local shops, like Lake of the Ozarks tee-shirts, coffee mugs, and other memorabilia.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Ozark

  • Fishing Culture: Residents and visitors alike appreciate the rich fishing culture in Lake Ozark.

  • Water-Based Activities: Lake Ozark’s water is the heart and soul of the area, which means that there are many water-based activities to try out.

  • Missouri Hospitality: Missouri’s hospitality is famous across the United States because of locals who greet everyone with kindness and a smile.

Best Time to Visit Lake Ozark

Lake Ozark’s peak season is from May through September; the weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and many attractions are open. Lake Ozark also has a vibrant fall season, with changing colors and relaxing weather. The winter months are usually quieter, but it is still possible to enjoy winter sports, like ice-fishing or hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Ozark

Do:

  • Spend time on the water.

  • Visit one of the many local wineries or breweries.

  • Embrace the outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.

  • Forget about Missouri’s best barbecue.

  • Leave without experiencing the Ozark’s hospitality.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Ozark

Lake Ozark is a relatively safe destination. Visitors should be aware of water safety-related issues, such as the danger of drowning and boating risks.

Health in Lake Ozark

Lake Ozark has local clinics and hospitals available for visitors who need medical attention. Hiking trails and outdoor activities may pose various risks, so visitors should take appropriate safety measures.

Getting to Lake Ozark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Ozark

  • Columbia Regional Airport: This airport is situated around 75 miles from the Lake Of The Ozarks, and it is home to non-stop flights to and from several cities in the United States.

  • Springfield-Branson National Airport: This airport is located around 95 miles from Lake Ozark, and it serves several major airlines, including American, Delta, and United.

Bus Stations Near Lake Ozark

Greyhound Lines offering direct service to the Lake of the Ozarks only from Jefferson City.

Other Options

Several car rental agencies operate from Columbia Regional Airport and from Springfield-Branson National Airport. There is also the option for visitors to hire a private driver or taxi to take you to Lake Ozark from either airport.

How to Get Around in Lake Ozark

Visitors can hire vehicles from one of the many local car rental businesses, or utilize taxi services. Another excellent option is to take a guided tour that will save time and ease of access for visitors.

Where to Go Next from Lake Ozark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. Louis: A vibrant city located about 150 miles from Lake Ozark, which has plenty of activities, animal habitats, and botanical gardens to offer.

  • Kansas City: Another vibrant city located at around two hours from Lake Ozark, offering a stellar selection of food, history, and culture.

  • Branson: A unique travel destination about three hours from Lake Ozark, perfect for those who love music and entertainment.

Conclusion

With an abundance of natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and cultural highlights, Lake Ozark is a fantastic travel destination for visitors. Whether it’s enjoying fishing culture or exploring the scenic beauty, this town offers much to see and do. Visitors will appreciate the area’s hospitable and friendly citizens and enjoy a comfortable stay at one of the many accommodations available in the area.
