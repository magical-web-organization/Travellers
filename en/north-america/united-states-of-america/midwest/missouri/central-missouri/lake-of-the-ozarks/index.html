Introduction to Lake of the Ozarks

Lake of the Ozarks is a pristine vacation destination located in Central Missouri. With over 1,150 miles of shoreline, this massive artificial lake has become one of the most popular tourist spots for those wanting to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. Offering an abundance of outdoor activities, scenic landscapes, delicious food, and local culture, Lake of the Ozarks is a gem waiting to be discovered.

List of Places to Visit in Lake of the Ozarks

There are plenty of places to explore in Lake of the Ozarks, ranging from culturally important attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bagnell Dam: This historical dam offers stunning views of the lake and features a museum and visitor center.
  • Lake of the Ozarks State Park: The largest state park in Missouri offers a variety of trails, campsites, and natural attractions.
  • Casa de Loco Winery: Enjoy a glass of wine while overlooking the beautiful Ozark hills at this picturesque winery.
  • Paradise Parasail: Take a thrilling parasailing ride and see the lake’s beauty from the sky.
  • Timber Falls Indoor Waterpark: Family-friendly indoor waterpark perfect for a fun day out.

Natural Attractions of Lake of the Ozarks: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ha Ha Tonka State Park: A natural wonderland featuring a castle-like ruins and miles of hiking trails offering spectacular views.
  • Big Surf Water Park: The biggest water park in Lake of the Ozarks, offering over 15 rides and attractions perfect for families.
  • Bridal Cave: A mesmerizing cave tour with stalactites, stalagmites, and underground lakes.
  • Ozark Distillery and Brewery: Enjoy local beer, spirits, and whiskey while soaking in the beautiful Ozark scenery.
  • Golf Courses: Lake of the Ozarks is home to many championship-level golf courses surrounded by scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake of the Ozarks

There are plenty of hiking and biking trails scattered all around Lake of the Ozarks. Some of the best routes that offer beautiful views of the lake include:

  • Lake of the Ozarks State Park Trails: A network of 10 trails that vary in length and difficulty.
  • Ha Ha Tonka State Park Trails: Mileage of trails that offer stunning vistas at every turn.
  • Gravois Creek Conservation Area Trail: A moderate-level 6-mile trail that offers scenic views of Gravois Creek.

Best Things to Do in Lake of the Ozarks: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the natural beauty of Lake of the Ozarks, here are some exciting things to do:

  • Boat Tours & Rentals: Hire a boat and explore the Lake of the Ozarks, or take a guided boat tour with local experts.
  • Fishing: Lake of the Ozarks is a renowned fishing destination, offering a variety of fish species.
  • Golfing: Play a round of golf at some of the finest golf courses in Missouri surrounding Lake of the Ozarks.
  • Zipline Adventure: Experience the thrill of a zipline tour through the Ozark forest and lake views.
  • Helicopter Tours: Take an aerial tour and witness the beauty of Lake of the Ozarks from the sky.
  • Water Parks: Cool off from the summer heat with Big Surf and Timber Falls waterparks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake of the Ozarks

Here’s a suggested itinerary of how to spend a perfect week in Lake of the Ozarks:

Food and Drink Scene of Lake of Ozarks

There’s a variety of delicious and locally sourced food and drink that visitors should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ ribs, pulled pork, and brisket: Missouri is known for serving mouth-watering BBQ, and Lake of the Ozarks is no exception.
  • Fried chicken and catfish: Satisfy the southern craving with a crispy fried chicken or catfish plate.
  • Ozark fried potatoes: A classic for any meal, try the Ozarks twist on regular fried potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Backwater Jacks Brewery is a lakefront brewery that offers locally brewed craft beers, and delicious food that pairs perfectly with a cold beer.
  • Casa de Loco Winery: Features award-winning wines and beautiful Ozarks landscapes.
  • J. Bruner’s Restaurant: A top-notch restaurant featuring local cuisine and a wine list hand-selected by the restaurant’s owners.

Where to Stay in Lake of the Ozarks: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options for visitors to Lake of the Ozarks, ranging from hotels to vacation rentals:

  • The Lodge of Four Seasons: A luxury hotel that features its own golf course and spa, offering breathtaking Lake of the Ozarks views.
  • Tan-Tar-A Resorts: A family-friendly hotel that features on-site water parks and access to a private beach.
  • The Ozark Harbor Resort: If you’re looking for a more authentic lake experience, this resort offers lakeside cabins and boats for rent.
  • Airbnb & Vrbo: Plenty of lakefront and forest cabins are available for rent for smaller group get-togethers.

What to Buy in Lake of the Ozarks: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors should take home some local products and souvenirs of their Lake of the Ozarks experience. Some popular items include:

  • Ozark paddle: A finely crafted wooden paddle that can be used at the lake or as a beautiful decoration.
  • Fried Pie Mix: A mix made with all the flavors of a classic fried pie, a specialty of Missouri.
  • Local Wines and Craft Beers: Take home some of the region’s finest spirits and brews.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake of the Ozarks

The region has its unique customs, and it’s essential to respect them, including:

  • Tipping: A common custom when you receive good service in restaurants and bars in the USA.
  • Respectful dress: It’s generally acceptable to dress casually in most places, but dressing appropriately to some sites like museums.
  • Kindness and hospitality: Missourians are proud of their kindness and transfer a spirit of hospitality to their guests.

Best Time to Visit Lake of the Ozarks

Lake of the Ozarks is fantastic year-round, but the summer season is the most popular time to visit. Summer kicks off with the Lake of the Ozarks Shootout boat race and is full of great events, including Fourth of July fireworks and plenty of live music concerts. The fall season is the perfect time to take in the natural beauty of the changing leaves, while winter is for those who want to hit the slopes or snuggle by the fire with a glass of wine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake of the Ozarks

Here are some things to keep in mind when planning your trip to Lake of the Ozarks:

Do:

  • Enjoy outdoor activities: With plenty of scenic trails and plenty of water sports available, outdoor activities are a must-do in Lake of the Ozarks.
  • Respect the lake: Help keep the lake pristine by adhering to trash rules and boating etiquette.
  • Plan ahead: Lake of the Ozarks can get quite busy during peak season, so plan ahead to avoid the crowds.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the sun: Missouri summers can be brutal, so pack a hat, sunscreen, and light clothing.
  • Disrespect the locals or local customs: Be respectful to locals, who are friendly and welcoming.
  • Break the law: It’s crucial to understand that Missouri is a strict law-enforcing state, so obey all traffic rules and laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake of the Ozarks

Lake of the Ozarks is a safe place to visit, but visitors should always be cautious and aware of safety rules, including:

  • Water Safety: Always use a life jacket when doing water sports and obey boating safety rules.
  • Bicycle Safety: Wear a helmet while biking around the lake to prevent head injuries.
  • Personal Safety: Be aware of your surroundings at night and avoid unlit or isolated roads.

Health in Lake of the Ozarks

Visitors should take precautions to stay healthy while visiting Lake of the Ozarks, including:

  • Hydration and Sun Protection: Drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.
  • Insect Protection: Apply insect repellent to prevent getting bitten by mosquitoes that carry diseases.
  • COVID-19 Safety: Follow COVID-19 safety protocols like wearing a mask and social distancing when necessary.

How to Get Around in Lake of the Ozarks

Public transportation options are limited in Lake of the Ozarks, so renting a car or a motorcycle is an ideal option to get around. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Lake of the Ozarks: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of exciting destinations near Lake of the Ozarks. Some of these nearby destinations include:

  • Jefferson City: Missouri’s capital, offering historical landmarks, museums, and parks.
  • Truman Lake: A picturesque lake area that’s a great destination for boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • Mark Twain National Forest: A natural paradise with over 1.5 million acres of land spread in south-central Missouri
