Introduction to Boonville

Located in the heart of Central Missouri, Boonville is a charming and quaint town that seamlessly blends history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Founded in 1810 and settled by the French in 1765, Boonville is rich in history and heritage. From its unique architecture to its well-preserved downtown area, the town is a delight for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.

Boonville also offers a plethora of outdoor activities, with its scenic parks, rivers and trails providing opportunities for fishing, hiking, biking and boating. Additionally, the town has an array of local shops and restaurants that offer an immersive cultural experience. Whether you’re here for a day or a week, Boonville has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Boonville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Isle of Capri Casino Hotel

The Isle of Capri Casino Hotel is a popular destination for those who enjoy gambling. The casino features a wide range of slot machines, table games, and poker rooms. The hotel also offers luxurious accommodations with top-class amenities such as a spa and fine dining.

Katy Trail State Park

Covering over 200 miles, the Katy Trail State Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The trail is open for hiking, cycling, and running and has several points of entry along its route. The trail also provides scenic views of the Missouri River.

Boonville Visitors Center

Located in the historic former train depot, the Boonville Visitors Center is an excellent starting point for visitors who want to explore the town. It has a wealth of information about the town’s history and establishments. The center also has a gift shop with a variety of souvenirs and local products.

Mitchell Antique Motorcar Museum

The Mitchell Antique Motorcar Museum is a must-visit museum for car enthusiasts. The museum features an impressive collection of vintage cars and motorcycles that dates back to the early 1900s. Visitors can take a self-guided tour of the museum, accompanied by the helpful staff.

Natural Attractions of Boonville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Boonville Riverwalk

The Boonville Riverwalk is a scenic trail along the Missouri River, offering stunning views of the water and surrounding landmarks. The trail is perfect for biking, walking or jogging and is suitable for visitors of all ages. The trail is also pet-friendly and allows dogs on leashes.

Harley Park

Harley Park is one of the most popular outdoor destinations in Boonville. The park covers a large area and offers a wide range of recreational activities, including fishing, boating and picnicking. The park also has a playground for children and well-maintained trails for hikers and bikers.

Boonville Quarry Conservation Area

The Boonville Quarry Conservation Area is a nature preserve that features an abundance of wildlife and natural habitats. The area has several trails that lead visitors through beautiful woodlands, streams and wetlands. The preserve is also home to native bird species such as bald eagles and red-tailed hawks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Boonville

Katy Trail State Park

The Katy Trail is Missouri’s longest hiking and biking trail. The trail runs more than 200 miles through the heart of the state and has an entry point in Boonville. Visitors can rent bikes or bring their own and explore the trail’s scenic views and natural habitats.

Boonville Riverwalk

The Boonville Riverwalk is a great spot for a leisurely walk or bike ride. The trail provides stunning views of the Missouri River. Visitors can also stop at several restaurants and cafes along the way for a bite to eat.

Best Things to Do in Boonville: Activities and Tours

Boonville Walking Tours

The Boonville Walking Tours offer a fun and educational way to explore the town’s rich history and architectural heritage. These tours are led by knowledgeable guides who provide insights into the town’s past and present.

Boonville Depot Museum

The Boonville Depot Museum is a restored train depot that now serves as a museum, showcasing Boonville’s railroad history. The museum also has an impressive collection of photographs, artifacts and memorabilia from bygone days.

Week-Long Itinerary for Boonville

Day 1: Boonville Depot Museum & Riverwalk

Spend the first day exploring the Boonville Depot Museum and Riverwalk. Learn about Boonville’s railroad history and take a walk along the beautiful Missouri River.

Day 2: Katy Trail State Park

Rent a bike and spend the day exploring the Katy Trail State Park. Take in the scenic views of Missouri’s countryside and enjoy some fresh air and exercise.

Day 3: Harley Park

Visit Harley Park and indulge in outdoor activities such as fishing and picnicking. Explore the trails and take in the beauty of the park’s natural surroundings.

Day 4: Mitchell Antique Motorcar Museum

Spend the day at the Mitchell Antique Motorcar Museum and admire the vast collection of vintage automobiles and motorcycles.

Day 5: Shopping and Dining in Downtown Boonville

Spend the day exploring the unique shops and restaurants in downtown Boonville. Sample some local cuisine and take home some souvenirs.

Day 6: Boonville Visitors Center and Boonville Quarry Conservation Area

Visit the Boonville Visitors Center to learn more about the town’s history and attractions. Spend the afternoon hiking or biking at the Boonville Quarry Conservation Area.

Day 7: Isle of Capri Casino Hotel

End the trip with some fun and excitement at the Isle of Capri Casino Hotel. Enjoy some gambling, fine dining, and relaxation at the hotel’s spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Boonville

Must-Try Dishes

BBQ brisket

Boonville is known for its delectable BBQ brisket. This dish is prepared with slow-cooked beef that is tender and juicy with a smoky flavor. You can find this dish in many local restaurants and BBQ joints in town.

Missouri River walleye

The Missouri River walleye is a local specialty. This freshwater fish is caught locally and is known for its delicate yet flavorful taste. You can find this dish in many local restaurants that serve fresh and locally sourced seafood.

Local Beverages

Les Bourgeois Vineyards

Les Bourgeois Vineyards is a popular destination for wine lovers in Boonville. The vineyard offers an array of wines that are made from locally grown grapes. Visitors can enjoy a tasting or purchase a bottle to take home.

Warm Springs Ranch

Warm Springs Ranch is a destination for beer enthusiasts. The ranch produces beer with locally grown hops and is served exclusively in local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Boonville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isle of Capri Casino Hotel

The Isle of Capri Casino Hotel is the most luxurious option in Boonville. The hotel offers top-class amenities such as a spa, fine dining, and a casino.

Hotel Frederick

Hotel Frederick is a historic boutique hotel that offers a unique blend of the past and present. The hotel features elegant rooms with antique furnishings and modern amenities.

Cooper’s Landing

Cooper’s Landing is a quaint and cozy guesthouse with charming rooms and warm hospitality. The guesthouse offers stunning views of the Missouri River and is a favorite among couples and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Boonville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Boonville T-shirts

Boonville T-shirts are a popular souvenir item that can be found in many local shops and gift stores in town. You can find T-shirts with unique Boonville designs and logos.

Missouri River memorabilia

Missouri River memorabilia such as postcards, prints, and photographs can be found in many local shops in Boonville. These items make excellent souvenirs and gifts for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Boonville

Best Time to Visit Boonville

The best time to visit Boonville is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town also has several festivals and events during these seasons, which provide exciting entertainment options for visitors.

Getting to Boonville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Boonville

The nearest airport to Boonville is the Columbia Regional Airport, which is approximately 30 miles away. Other nearby airports include the Kansas City International Airport and St. Louis Lambert International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Boonville

Boonville does not have a bus station. The nearest bus station is located in Columbia, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Boonville. The town is easily accessible from major highways and routes.

How to Get Around in Boonville

Boonville is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent cars or hire taxis to get around the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Boonville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Columbia

Columbia is a vibrant city located approximately 20 miles from Boonville. The city has several attractions such as the Columbia Art League and the Rock Bridge Memorial State Park.

Jefferson City

Jefferson City is the capital of Missouri and is located approximately 25 miles from Boonville. The city has several landmarks and attractions such as the Missouri State Capitol Building and the Missouri State Penitentiary Museum.
