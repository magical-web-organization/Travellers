Sedalia, a city in Central Missouri, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. From historical attractions to beautiful outdoor scenery, Sedalia offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, hike/bike routes, activities and tours, food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Sedalia

Sedalia is located in the heart of Missouri and is known for its easygoing culture and friendly locals. The city is rich in history and is famous for hosting the Missouri State Fair. Visitors can explore its charming downtown area and historic architecture, or venture out into the scenic countryside. Sedalia has something for everyone and is a perfect destination for a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Sedalia

Sedalia has a variety of attractions, both natural and cultural. Here are some of the must-visit places in Sedalia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Katy Depot Historic Site: This historic railroad depot now serves as a museum and features exhibits on the history of the railroad in Missouri.
  • Bothwell Lodge State Historic Site: A castle-like lodge built-in 1890, now serves as a museum offering tours of the property.
  • Liberty Center Association for the Arts: This center hosts performances and exhibitions throughout the year, and showcases works from local artists.

Natural Attractions of Sedalia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Katy Trail State Park: Located just a few miles outside of Sedalia, this park is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts.
  • Bothwell Lodge State Historic Site: The lodge is located on a bluff and offers excellent views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Daum Museum of Contemporary Art: This museum showcases contemporary art from both national and international artists in a beautiful building surrounded by nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sedalia

Sedalia is home to many scenic bike and hiking trails. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Katy Trail State Park: This trail runs for 237 miles with Sedalia one of the stops.
  • The Bobcat Walk Trail: A 3-mile loop trail that takes you around Liberty Park and features beautiful lake views.
  • Katy Trail State Park Trailheads: There are many trailheads throughout the park offering different routes with varying difficulty levels.

Best Things to Do in Sedalia: Activities and Tours

  • Missouri State Fair: Hosted in August each year, the Missouri State Fair is a fun-filled event for all ages featuring carnival rides, live music, and more.
  • Daum Museum of Contemporary Art: Take a tour of the museum and enjoy the artwork of contemporary artists.
  • Liberty Center Association for the Arts: Catch a play or musical performance at this local theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sedalia

Here is our suggested itinerary for Sedalia:

Food and Drink Scene of Sedalia

Sedalia may be small, but it is big on flavor. From traditional Missouri fare to international cuisine, Sedalia has a diverse food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Missouri Pork Steak: A staple of Missouri cuisine, this dish features a pork shoulder steak, usually grilled.
  • Toasted Ravioli: This Italian-inspired dish features breaded and deep-fried ravioli and is popular in Missouri.
  • Gooey Butter Cake: This iconic St. Louis dessert features a dense, buttery cake topped with powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Missouri Wine: Missouri is home to many vineyards, producing wines ranging from dry whites to sweet reds.
  • Boulevard Beer: This Kansas City brewery produces a wide range of beers, including IPAs, stouts, and ales.

Where to Stay in Sedalia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sedalia has many accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Bothwell: Located in the heart of downtown Sedalia, this hotel features elegant rooms and suites.
  • Comfort Inn Sedalia Station: A budget-friendly option located near the Katy Trail State Park.
  • Hotel Bothwell Annex: A stylish, modern guesthouse located in downtown Sedalia.

What to Buy in Sedalia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sedalia has several unique shops and boutiques where you can find local products and souvenirs. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Katy Depot Gift Shop: Located inside Katy Depot Historical Site, this gift shop sells unique train-themed gifts and souvenirs.
  • Blondee’s Bazaar: This boutique features handmade jewelry and gifts made by local artists.
  • Third Street Social: This restaurant and bar features an extensive selection of Missouri-made products, including beer, wine, and spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Sedalia

Sedalia is a friendly, welcoming place with a rich history and culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore the city and immerse themselves in local customs.

Best Time to Visit Sedalia

The best time to visit Sedalia is in the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and the Missouri State Fair takes place. Spring and fall are also good times to visit as the weather is mild, and the parks and trails offer beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sedalia

Do:

  • Explore the beautiful countryside and scenic parks.
  • Attend the Missouri State Fair for a true Sedalia experience.
  • Try Missouri cuisine, including pork steak and toasted ravioli.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Do not disturb the wildlife you encounter in the parks.
  • Do not disrespect local traditions or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sedalia

Sedalia is a generally safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions when exploring the area. Always keep an eye on personal belongings and be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

Health in Sedalia

Sedalia has several medical facilities, including Bothwell Regional Health Center, which offers emergency services and general medical care.

Getting to Sedalia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sedalia

The closest airport to Sedalia is Kansas City International Airport, located approximately 100 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Sedalia

Jefferson City, MO is the closest major bus station to Sedalia, located approximately 50 miles away.

Other Options

Sedalia is easily accessible by car or by train. Amtrak offers daily service from Sedalia to Kansas City and St. Louis.

How to Get Around in Sedalia

Many of the main attractions in Sedalia are within walking distance of each other, but there are also several transportation options available. Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi, or use ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Sedalia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sedalia is located in the heart of Missouri, making it an ideal base for exploring nearby destinations. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Ha Ha Tonka State Park: Located just over an hour’s drive from Sedalia, this park features caves, hiking trails, and a castle ruin.
  • Jefferson City: The capital of Missouri is located just 50 miles from Sedalia and is home to many historical and cultural attractions.
  • Lake Ozark: Located approximately an hour and a half away from Sedalia, this resort town offers boating, fishing, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Sedalia offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Whether you’re exploring the city’s cultural attractions, hiking in the beautiful countryside, or enjoying the local cuisine, Sedalia has something for every traveler. We hope this guide has provided you with the information you need to plan the perfect Sedalia getaway.
