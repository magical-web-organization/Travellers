Introduction to Bourbon

Nestled in the heart of Central Missouri, Bourbon is a charming town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. The town takes its name after Bourbon County in Kentucky, and has a deep-seated connection to the whiskey that has become synonymous with the region.

Whether you’re looking to delve into the town’s past, explore the great outdoors, or simply unwind with a glass of bourbon, there’s something for everyone in Bourbon. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through the top attractions, activities, and must-visit destinations in the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bourbon is home to several museums and historical places that showcase its rich heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Crawford County Historical Museum: This museum is a treasure trove of artifacts and exhibits that offer a glimpse into the history of the region. From Native American artifacts to early settler tools, the museum has something for everyone.
  • Old Bourbon County Museum: Located in the heart of town, the Old Bourbon County Museum is housed in a historic building that dates back to the early 1900s. The museum tells the story of the town’s past, and offers a fascinating look at its growth and development over the years.

Natural Attractions of Bourbon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bourbon is blessed with natural beauty, and there are several outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Meramec River: The Meramec River is a popular destination for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing. The river is also home to several sandy beaches where you can relax and soak up the sun.
  • Bourbon State Park: The Bourbon State Park offers a range of recreational activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and camping. The park is also home to a beautiful lake where you can go fishing or boating.
  • Ozark National Scenic Riverways: Located just a short drive from Bourbon, the Ozark National Scenic Riverways offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, and canoeing in this stunning wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bourbon

Whether you’re an avid cyclist or a casual hiker, Bourbon has plenty of trails to explore. Some of the best routes include:

  • Ozark Trail: The Ozark Trail is a 350-mile long hiking and biking trail that winds its way through some of the most beautiful landscapes in Missouri. The trail offers a range of difficulty levels, from easy to challenging, and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Meramec River Trail: The Meramec River Trail is a picturesque hiking trail that follows the banks of the Meramec River. The trail offers stunning views of the river and is a great spot for birdwatching.

Best Things to Do in Bourbon: Activities and Tours

From adventure sports to cultural experiences, there’s plenty to do in Bourbon. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Bourbon Distillery Tours: Bourbon is famous for its whiskey, and visitors can take a tour of the local distilleries to learn more about this famous beverage.
  • Meramec Caverns: Located just a short drive from Bourbon, the Meramec Caverns offer a fascinating glimpse into the underground world of caves. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the unique rock formations and learn about the history of the caverns.
  • Float Trips: The Meramec River is a popular destination for float trips, and visitors can rent canoes, kayaks, or rafts to explore the river at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bourbon

Day 1: Explore the Crawford County Historical Museum and learn about the town’s rich history.

Day 2: Take a hike in the Ozark National Scenic Riverways and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Day 3: Go on a Bourbon Distillery Tour and learn about the town’s famous whiskey.

Day 4: Spend the day fishing at Bourbon State Park.

Day 5: Take a float trip down the Meramec River and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Day 6: Visit the Meramec Caverns and explore the fascinating underground world of caves.

Day 7: Relax at one of the sandy beaches along the Meramec River.

Food and Drink Scene of Bourbon

No trip to Bourbon is complete without sampling the local cuisine and drinks. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Bourbon is famous for its fried chicken, and several restaurants in town offer this classic dish.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: This hearty breakfast dish is a local favorite and can be found at several diners and cafes in Bourbon.

Local Beverages

  • Bourbon Whiskey: Made with the local corn, wheat, and rye, Bourbon whiskey is the town’s most famous beverage.
  • Local Craft Beer: Several microbreweries in Bourbon offer a range of craft beers that are sure to please any beer enthusiast.

Where to Stay in Bourbon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bourbon offers a range of lodging options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Baymont Inn & Suites Bourbon: This conveniently located hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • The Bourbon Hotel: Housed in a historic building, the Bourbon Hotel offers charming rooms and personalized service.
  • Airbnb Rentals: You can rent a room or an entire house through Airbnb, and enjoy an authentic local experience.

What to Buy in Bourbon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you want to bring home a piece of local history or a unique piece of art, there are plenty of souvenirs and local products to choose from in Bourbon. Some of the top items include:

  • Bourbon Whiskey: Bring home a bottle of the town’s famous whiskey.
  • Handmade Crafts: Several local artisans sell handmade ceramics, jewelry, and other crafts.
  • Regional Foods: Stock up on local jams, jellies, and other food products to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Bourbon

Bourbon is known for its friendly locals and welcoming culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s traditions and customs, including its love for whiskey.

Best Time to Visit Bourbon

The best time to visit Bourbon is during the spring or fall, when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are thinner. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bourbon

Do:

  • Respect the town’s heritage and traditions.
  • Try the local whiskey and other regional products.
  • Explore the great outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive.
  • Disrespect the locals or their culture.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Bourbon is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take normal precautions when traveling. In terms of health, visitors should ensure that they have the necessary vaccinations and are prepared for the local climate.

Getting to Bourbon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bourbon is located in Central Missouri, and can be reached by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Bourbon

  • Lambert-St. Louis International Airport: Located approximately 90 miles east of Bourbon, Lambert-St. Louis International Airport is the closest major airport to town.

Bus Stations Near Bourbon

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located approximately 25 miles south of Bourbon, the Greyhound Bus Station in Rolla offers bus service to several major cities in Missouri and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in nearby towns to explore Bourbon and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Bourbon

The best way to get around Bourbon is by car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Bourbon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whether you want to explore more of Missouri or venture further afield, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Some of the top options include:

  • Jefferson City: Missouri’s capital city is located approximately 70 miles north of Bourbon, and offers plenty of urban attractions and cultural experiences.
  • Lake of the Ozarks: Located approximately 60 miles southwest of Bourbon, the Lake of the Ozarks offers a range of recreational activities and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Bourbon is a charming town that offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s past, venturing out into the wilderness, or simply relaxing with a glass of whiskey, there’s something for everyone in Bourbon.
