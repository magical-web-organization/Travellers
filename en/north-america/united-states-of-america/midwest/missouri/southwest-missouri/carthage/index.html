Introduction to Carthage

Carthage is a charming city in Missouri’s Southwest region, located in the heart of the Ozarks. The city boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. It’s a perfect destination for those looking to connect with nature, history, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Carthage

There are plenty of places to explore in Carthage, from historical landmarks to scenic hiking trails.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visit these museums and historical landmarks to learn more about Carthage’s past and present:

  • Battle of Carthage State Historic Site: This site commemorates the Battle of Carthage, which took place in 1861 during the American Civil War. The park features a walking trail and a museum with exhibits on the history of the battle.

  • Carthage Civil War Museum: This museum showcases artifacts and stories related to the Civil War, with a special focus on the Battle of Carthage.

  • Powers Museum: This museum showcases the history of Carthage and Jasper County through exhibits and artifacts.

  • Precious Moments Chapel: This unique attraction features beautiful artwork and sculptures created by Samuel J. Butcher. It’s a must-visit for art lovers.

Natural Attractions of Carthage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Carthage offers plenty of opportunities to connect with nature:

  • McCune Brooks Regional Hospital Trail: This scenic hiking and biking trail offers views of rolling hills and lush trees. It’s perfect for those looking for a peaceful escape into nature.

  • Kellogg Lake Nature Center: This nature center offers a variety of activities, including fishing, hiking, and birdwatching. It’s a great place to spend a relaxing afternoon.

  • Red Oak II: This outdoor museum features a collection of vintage buildings and automobiles. Take a stroll through the streets and enjoy the unique ambiance of this historical site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carthage

Carthage offers plenty of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes to explore:

  • McCune Brooks Regional Hospital Trail: This 3.1-mile trail winds through scenic countryside, offering beautiful views of the Ozarks. Whether you’re hiking or biking, this trail provides a peaceful escape into nature.

  • Kellogg Lake Nature Center Trail: This 1.4-mile trail takes you around the beautiful Kellogg Lake, offering scenic views of the water and surrounding forest.

Best Things to Do in Carthage: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Carthage:

  • ArtCentral Gallery: This gallery showcases the work of local artists. It’s a wonderful place to explore the region’s art scene.

  • Route 66 Drive-In Theatre: Take a trip back in time at this classic drive-in theater. Enjoy a movie under the stars and experience a slice of Americana.

  • Precious Moments Chapel and Gardens Tour: This tour takes you through the beautiful Precious Moments Chapel and Gardens, showcasing Samuel J. Butcher’s stunning artwork.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carthage

If you’re planning to spend a week in Carthage, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Visit the Precious Moments Chapel and take a stroll through the Red Oak II outdoor museum.

  • Day 2: Explore the McCune Brooks Regional Hospital Trail for some outdoor adventure.

  • Day 3: Visit the Battle of Carthage State Historic Site and Carthage Civil War Museum to learn more about the city’s role in the Civil War.

  • Day 4: Spend the day at Kellogg Lake Nature Center, fishing, hiking, and enjoying the scenery.

  • Day 5: Check out the Powers Museum and learn more about the history of Carthage and Jasper County.

  • Day 6: Take a tour of the Route 66 Drive-In Theatre and enjoy a classic movie experience.

  • Day 7: Explore the local art scene at ArtCentral Gallery.

Food and Drink Scene of Carthage

Carthage offers a variety of local cuisine and beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried chicken at Chick-Inn Drive In: This classic drive-in restaurant serves up delicious fried chicken, burgers, and more.

  • Mexican cuisine at El Charro: This family-owned restaurant serves up authentic Mexican dishes, including tacos, burritos, and enchiladas.

  • Pizza at Manny’s Pizza and More: This local pizzeria offers delicious pizza and sandwiches in a friendly atmosphere.

Local Beverages

  • Hubble Bubble Kombucha: This locally brewed kombucha is available at many restaurants and cafes in Carthage.

  • Piney River Brewing Company: This brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and ales.

Where to Stay in Carthage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carthage offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Boots Court Motel: This historic motel was built in 1939 and has been recently refurbished. It offers a unique experience for those looking for a touch of nostalgia.

  • Precious Moments Hotel: This hotel is located right next to the Precious Moments Chapel and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.

  • Best Western Precious Moments Hotel: This hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Carthage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some places to check out:

  • Carthage Farmers Market: This farmers market offers local produce, baked goods, and crafts.

  • Carthage Candle Company: This shop sells handmade candles in a variety of scents and sizes.

Local Culture and Customs of Carthage

Carthage has a friendly and welcoming local culture. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, including saying “thank you” and “please.”

Best Time to Visit Carthage

The best time to visit Carthage is during the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carthage

Do:

  • Do explore the local history and culture.

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Do appreciate the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city and its natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carthage

Carthage is a safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking their cars and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Carthage

Visitors should bring any necessary medications and take standard precautions against sunburn and tick bites during outdoor activities.

Getting to Carthage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carthage

The nearest airports to Carthage are:

  • Joplin Regional Airport (JLN): This airport is about 14 miles east of Carthage. It offers flights from major airlines.

  • Springfield-Branson National Airport (SGF): This airport is about 85 miles northeast of Carthage. It offers flights from major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Carthage

The nearest bus station to Carthage is:

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Joplin: This station is about 12 miles east of Carthage and offers frequent bus service to major cities.

Other Options

Car rental services are available at both Joplin Regional Airport and Springfield-Branson National Airport.

How to Get Around in Carthage

The best way to get around Carthage is by car. However, there are also several bike rental services available in town.

Where to Go Next from Carthage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to check out:

  • Springfield: Located about 85 miles northeast of Carthage, Springfield offers a variety of cultural and dining options.

  • Roaring River State Park: This beautiful state park is located about 30 miles south of Carthage and offers hiking trails, fishing, and camping.

  • Tulsa, Oklahoma: Located about 115 miles southwest of Carthage, Tulsa offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions.

Conclusion

Carthage is a beautiful city with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, you’re sure to find something to enjoy in this charming Missouri town.
