Introduction to Buffalo

Located in Southwest Missouri, Buffalo is a hidden gem with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in the outdoors, learning about history and culture, or simply soaking up some local flavor, Buffalo has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions and activities that this charming town has to offer.

Best Things to Do in Buffalo: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Dallas County Courthouse Museum and learn about the area’s history
  • Take a scenic drive down Historic Route 66
  • Go fishing or boating at Stockton Lake
  • Explore the outdoors with a hike in Mark Twain National Forest
  • Attend the Cow Days Festival, held annually in September

Local Culture and Customs of Buffalo

Buffalo is known for its friendly and welcoming community, so visitors are sure to feel right at home. Be sure to greet locals with a smile and strike up a conversation to get to know them and learn more about the area. Additionally, it’s important to respect nature and leave no trace while exploring the great outdoors.

Best Time to Visit Buffalo

The best time to visit Buffalo is during the fall or spring, when the weather is mild and comfortable. If you’re interested in attending the Cow Days Festival, plan your trip for September to experience this annual event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buffalo

Do:

  • Explore the great outdoors and take in the scenic natural beauty
  • Greet locals with a smile and strike up a conversation
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or damage local attractions while exploring the outdoors

Safety and Health

Safety in Buffalo

Buffalo is a safe and welcoming community, but it’s always important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings while traveling.

Health in Buffalo

Visitors to Buffalo should be aware of tick-borne illnesses, as ticks are prevalent in the area. Be sure to wear insect repellent and check for ticks frequently while outdoors.

Getting to Buffalo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buffalo is easily accessible by car, with Interstate 44 running through the area. The nearest airports are Springfield-Branson National Airport, located about an hour away, and Kansas City International Airport, located about three hours away. Additionally, bus services are available through Greyhound and Jefferson Lines.

Airports Near Buffalo

  • Springfield-Branson National Airport
  • Kansas City International Airport

Bus Stations Near Buffalo

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Springfield)
  • Jefferson Lines Terminal (Springfield)

Other Options

  • Rental cars or rideshare services such as Uber or Lyft are available

How to Get Around in Buffalo

Buffalo is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by car. Bike rentals are also available for those interested in exploring the great outdoors on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Buffalo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Springfield, MO (1 hour away)
  • Lake of the Ozarks (2 hours away)
  • Branson, MO (2 hours away)
  • Kansas City, MO (3 hours away)

Conclusion

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or simply in search of charming small-town ambiance, Buffalo has plenty to offer. Plan your trip today and discover all that this hidden gem has to offer.
