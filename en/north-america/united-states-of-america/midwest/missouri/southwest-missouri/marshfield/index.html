Introduction to Marshfield

Marshfield is a quaint town that offers a quiet retreat from the hustle and bustle of big cities. It is located in Webster County in the southwest region of Missouri. Marshfield is situated along the famous U.S. 66 highway, known as the “Mother Road,” and features beautiful countryside and greenery that are perfect for exploring.

Marshfield had its beginnings as a small trading post, with the arrival of early pioneers who settled in the area. Today, it’s a haven for families, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone looking to get away from it all. With plenty of places to visit, outdoor activities, and excellent dining options, Marshfield is the perfect place to recharge and unwind. So, pack your bags and join us as we explore this beautiful town.

List of Places to Visit in Marshfield

Marshfield has many exciting places to visit, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions that showcase the town’s rich heritage. Here are some of the top attractions to check out.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marshfield’s rich history is displayed in numerous museums and historical landmarks throughout the town. Here are some of the top places to visit.

Laura Ingalls Wilder Home and Museum

The Laura Ingalls Wilder Home and Museum is a must-visit attraction for literature lovers. It is the childhood home of Laura Ingalls Wilder, the author of the “Little House on the Prairie” book series. The museum features artifacts from Wilder’s life, including manuscripts, pictures, and family heirlooms.

Webster County Historical Museum

Explore Marshfield’s history through the exhibits at the Webster County Historical Museum. The museum features artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia from the area’s earliest settlers to present day.

Trail of Tears

Visit the Trail of Tears National Historic Trail, commemorating the thousands of Cherokee people forcibly removed from their home in the southeastern U.S. and relocated to Indian Territory in Oklahoma in the 1830s. The Marshfield section of the trail is particularly noteworthy because many of the Cherokee passed through on their way west.

Natural Attractions of Marshfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Marshfield offers a wealth of natural scenery, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and biking trails to fishing and camping, there’s no shortage of activities to enjoy in Marshfield.

Busiek State Forest

Busiek State Forest is a 2,505-acre park that offers miles of hiking and biking trails through stunning natural scenery. The park also features picnic areas, fishing spots, and campsites that are perfect for a weekend getaway.

Sac River

Sac River is a beautiful and serene spot for fishing and relaxing. It’s perfect for a family picnic, or for simply taking in the scenery while enjoying some quiet time in nature.

Nathanael Greene/Close Memorial Park

Nathanael Greene/Close Memorial Park is a scenic spot that features a lake, walking trails, and plenty of places to enjoy a picnic. It’s a great place to get outside and enjoy the fresh air while taking in the beautiful views of Marshfield.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marshfield

The rolling hills and beautiful countryside surrounding Marshfield offer exceptional opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore.

Frisco Highline Trail

Frisco Highline Trail is a 36-mile paved trail that spans from Springfield to Bolivar, Missouri. The trail passes through Marshfield and offers scenic views of the countryside, making it a perfect spot for hiking, biking, and taking in the fresh air.

Marshfield Park and Trail

Marshfield Park and Trail is a short, easy trail that covers about 1.6 miles and circles around the town’s park. It’s a great option for walkers or joggers who want to get some exercise while enjoying the town’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Marshfield: Activities and Tours

Marshfield offers plenty of leisure activities and tours to help you explore the town’s picturesque landscapes and rich history. Here are some of the best things to do in Marshfield.

Horseback Riding at Riverside Riding Stables

Enjoy a relaxing day of horseback riding with a tour of the beautiful Missouri countryside at Riverside Riding Stables. The experienced guides will take you on an unforgettable ride through the scenic trails near Marshfield.

Brewery Tour

A brewery tour at the Wages Brewing Company is a must-do for any beer enthusiast. You’ll learn about the brewing process and, of course, get to taste some of the best beers in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Marshfield

Marshfield offers a variety of dining options for foodies, with many locally grown and locally sourced ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Ozark Fried Chicken

Try the regional specialty, Ozark fried chicken, at Vernelle’s restaurant, where the chicken is cooked perfectly crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Missouri Barbeque

Don’t miss out on Missouri’s famous barbeque style, which you can find at Back Roads BBQ.

Local Beverages

Wages Brewing Company

Sample some of Marshfield’s finest locally brewed beers at the Wages Brewing Company, which also offers tours of the brewery along with tastings.

Local Culture and Customs of Marshfield

Marshfield is a small town with a friendly community, so it’s important to arrive with a respectful attitude and polite demeanor. Marshfield is known for its appreciation of country music and Ozark culture.

Best Time to Visit Marshfield

Marshfield is beautiful year-round, but the town is particularly lively in the summer months, when locals and visitors alike come out to enjoy the warm weather and festivities. If you prefer cooler temperatures and fall foliage, then visit Marshfield in October.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marshfield

In general, Marshfield is a safe town. However, it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions, especially if you’re traveling alone. Always lock your car and keep valuables out of plain sight.

Health in Marshfield

There are several pharmacies and medical facilities available in Marshfield. It’s always a good idea to carry your medications with you, and to have your health insurance information readily available.

Getting to Marshfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marshfield is easily accessible by car, and there are several airports and bus stations within driving distance of the town.

Airports Near Marshfield

The closest airport to Marshfield is Springfield-Branson National Airport, which is about 40 miles away. Another option is the Branson Airport, located approximately 90 miles from Marshfield.

Bus Stations Near Marshfield

Greyhound and Trailways both have bus stops in Springfield, Missouri, which is about 35 miles from Marshfield.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive to Marshfield, there are several options available. You can rent a car in Springfield or St. Louis, or take a taxi or ride-sharing service from the airport to Marshfield.

How to Get Around in Marshfield

The best way to get around Marshfield is by car. However, if you’re interested in exploring the town on foot, there are several walking trails available, such as the Marshfield Park and Trail.

Where to Go Next from Marshfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marshfield is located near several other towns and attractions in Missouri. Here are some of the nearby destinations that are worth exploring.

Springfield, Missouri

Springfield, Missouri, is just a short drive from Marshfield and offers plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options, as well as several museums and cultural attractions.

Branson, Missouri

Branson, Missouri, is a popular vacation destination that offers a range of outdoor activities, entertainment shows, and dining options. It’s a great place to visit for a day trip from Marshfield.
