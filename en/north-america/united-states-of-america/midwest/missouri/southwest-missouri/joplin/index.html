Introduction to Joplin

Joplin, located in the southwestern corner of Missouri, is a charming city that attracts visitors with its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. Founded in the 19th century as a mining town, Joplin has transformed into a bustling city that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary delights.

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Joplin has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will take you on a tour of the top places to visit in Joplin, hiking and biking routes, the food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Joplin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Joplin History & Mineral Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Joplin and its mining industry. Explore exhibits on indigenous history, the Civil War, and Route 66.
  • George Washington Carver National Monument: This historic site honors the life and work of George Washington Carver, a renowned botanist and African-American educator.
  • Evergreen Cemetery: One of the largest cemeteries in the United States, the Evergreen Cemetery features the resting place of many notable Joplin figures including Bonnie and Clyde’s family.

Natural Attractions of Joplin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grand Falls: This beautiful waterfall is a must-visit attraction in Joplin. Located on Shoal Creek, Grand Falls is the largest continuously flowing natural waterfall in Missouri.
  • Wildcat Glades Conservation & Audubon Center: A nature lover’s paradise, Wildcat Glades Conservation & Audubon Center features multiple trails and educational displays showcasing Joplin’s diverse ecosystems.
  • Cave Springs Park: Known for its natural spring and stunning scenic views, Cave Spring Park offers 36 acres of hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Joplin

  • Frisco Greenway Trail: Experience the beauty of Joplin while walking, biking, or rollerblading on the Frisco Greenway Trail. This paved trail covers 7.8 miles and takes visitors through some of the city’s most scenic areas including parks and waterways.
  • Murphy Boulevard Loop: A perfect route for a leisurely bike ride, the Murphy Boulevard Loop covers 3 miles of beautiful scenery including parks, residential neighborhoods, and shopping districts.
  • Twin Bridges Bike Trail: This 6.5-mile long crushed limestone trail offers bike enthusiasts a scenic route along Shoal Creek that features beautiful trees, rock formations, and various wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in Joplin: Activities and Tours

  • Axe Laying: If you are looking for an adventure to remember, chop up your stress by participating in an axe-throwing game at Got Axes.
  • Painting with a Twist: Creative types will enjoy the chance to create their own keepsake, while sipping on wine and enjoying a light-hearted atmosphere.
  • Mosaics: Let your imagination run wild as you create your own mosaic masterpiece with help from the folks at Bits ‘n Pieces Studio.

Week-Long Itinerary for Joplin

Day 1 Visit Joplin History & Mineral Museum, grab lunch at Joplin’s oldest restaurant, the famous Granny Shaffer’s, followed by an afternoon strolling through the Northpark Mall.
Day 2 Spend the day at Waters Edge Mini-Golf, Wildcat Glades, and Reddings Mill, and grab dinner at Instant Karma.
Day 3 Check out George Washington Carver National Monument in the morning, then continue to Grand Falls and Cave Spring Park in the afternoon. End the day with dinner and drinks at Hackett Hot Wings.
Day 4 Art-lovers will enjoy an afternoon and evening on Main Street where they can see public art pieces, take in a show at Spiva Center for the Arts, and dine at the newly opened Blackthorn Pizza & Pub.
Day 5 Bike along the Frisco Trail or hike Shoal Creek, and grab an artisanal sandwich from Stork’s Nest before heading to Landreth Park to grill out and spend the afternoon with friends.
Day 6 Take the day to rest and relax, then enjoy a live show at the Downtown Joplin Alliance’s Third Thursday event.
Day 7 Pick up some coffee at Bearded Lady Roasters and spend the morning exploring old Route 66. End your trip with dinner and a show at Joplin Little Theatre or a late-night slice of pizza at Hero Deli.

Food and Drink Scene of Joplin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buttermilk Fried Chicken: Served with homemade mashed potatoes and gravy, this dish is a staple of the Midwest.
  • Burgers: Joplin has a multitude of options to get a juicy, handmade burger served with house-seasoned fries.
  • BBQ: From mouthwatering slow-cooked meats to flavorful sauces, Joplin’s BBQ scene serves up some of the best in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Local Roasted Coffee: Joplin offers some of the best roasted coffee, with Bearded Lady Roasters serving up some of the finest blends.
  • Joplin Ale Trail: Beer enthusiasts can hop along the Joplin Ale Trail, visiting microbreweries and pubs, all serving up delicious craft beers.

Where to Stay in Joplin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Broadway Inn: Conveniently located near the airport, the Broadway Inn features spacious rooms and suites, a fitness center, and free continental breakfast.
  • The Connor Hotel: A charming boutique hotel located in the heart of downtown Joplin, the Connor Hotel offers elegant rooms and suites, a fitness center, and complimentary continental breakfast.
  • TownePlace Suites Joplin: Perfect for an extended stay, the TownePlace Suites Joplin offers fully equipped kitchens, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Joplin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Joplin T-shirt: Show your love for Joplin with a retro t-shirt featuring the city’s iconic landmarks and attractions.
  • Route 66 Memorabilia: As part of the original Route 66 highway, Joplin is filled with nostalgic memorabilia – including magnets, postcards, and bumper stickers – that make for great souvenirs.
  • Local Artwork: Joplin has a thriving arts scene, and visitors can purchase unique, one-of-a-kind pieces from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Joplin

Joplin residents are known for their friendly attitude and down-to-earth nature. The city’s diverse history has led to a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in local events, art exhibitions, and festivals. Visitors are advised to respect local customs and to show their appreciation by shopping at local stores and dining at locally owned restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Joplin

Spring and fall are the best times to visit Joplin when the weather is mild and the trees are in full color. Summer, while hotter and more humid, is also a great time to visit Joplin with many outdoor activities, concerts, and festivals in full swing. Winter can be cold and blustery, but many of Joplin’s indoor attractions are open year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Joplin

Do:

  • Do try out the local food scene and explore the city’s diverse culinary offerings
  • Do make new friends and join in on local events
  • Do get out and explore the natural beauty of the area

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage public property
  • Don’t talk with your mouth full
  • Don’t miss out on visiting one of Joplin’s historic sites or museums

Safety and Health

Safety in Joplin

Joplin is a safe city with low crime rates. It is always wise to take the usual precautions such as locking your car doors and being aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Joplin

Joplin has a variety of medical facilities, including the Freeman Health System and Mercy Hospital, which can accommodate any of your medical needs.

Getting to Joplin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Joplin

Joplin Regional Airport (JLN) is the main airport serving the city, with daily flights from major hubs such as Dallas and Chicago.

Bus Stations Near Joplin

Greyhound has a station in downtown Joplin with regular service to major cities throughout the region.

Other Options

If driving, Joplin is located on I-44, between Springfield, Missouri, and Tulsa, Oklahoma. Amtrak stations are in Springfield and St. Louis, with Joplin accessible via a connecting bus from Springfield.

How to Get Around in Joplin

Joplin is a relatively small city, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis, and rideshare services, and car rentals are also available. Joplinglobal Taxi, Yellow Cab Joplin, and Uber are examples of options for taxi and rideshare services.

Where to Go Next from Joplin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Branson, Missouri: Known as the live entertainment capital of the world, Branson has a plethora of shows, attractions, and museums to explore.
  • Tulsa, Oklahoma: Tulsa is a full of art, history, and beautiful architecture. The Philbrook Museum of Art and Gathering Place are beautiful attractions to visit.
  • Fayetteville, Arkansas: Home of the University of Arkansas, Fayetteville is a college town full of famous architecture, museums, shops, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Joplin, with its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture will leave you with unforgettable memories. From hiking and biking to exploring delicious cuisine and local artwork, this charming town has something for everyone. Plan your trip to Joplin today and prepare to be charmed by the heart of Missouri’s Southwest.
