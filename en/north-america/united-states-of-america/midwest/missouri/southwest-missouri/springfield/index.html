Springfield, Missouri, is a hidden gem in the Midwest. Its charming small-town vibe, rich cultural heritage, and natural beauty make it a must-visit destination for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, family-friendly attractions, or a taste of local culture, Springfield has something for everyone.

Introduction to Springfield

Springfield is located in Southwest Missouri, in the heart of the Ozarks. It’s the third-largest city in Missouri, with a population of over 150,000. Nicknamed “The Queen City of the Ozarks,” Springfield is known for its quaint downtown, vibrant arts scene, and easy access to the great outdoors.

List of Places to Visit in Springfield

Here’s a list of must-visit attractions in Springfield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wonders of Wildlife National Museum and Aquarium: This world-class museum showcases the diversity of life on earth, with immersive exhibits and engaging activities for all ages.
  • Wilson’s Creek National Battlefield: History buffs will love this site, where the first major battle of the Civil War took place. Explore the preserved battlefield, watch living history demonstrations, and visit the museum to learn more about the war.
  • Springfield Art Museum: This art museum features over 10,000 works of art, including American, European, and Asian pieces. Admission is free, and there are often rotating exhibitions featuring local artists.
  • Route 66 Car Museum: Car enthusiasts will love this museum, which features over 70 vintage cars and motorcycles, all restored to their original glory.

Natural Attractions of Springfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Table Rock Lake: This scenic lake is located just south of Springfield and is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and swimming. There are several marinas and campgrounds around the lake, as well as hiking trails and picnic areas.
  • Springfield Conservation Nature Center: This nature center features over 80 acres of forests, meadows, and creeks, as well as educational exhibits and programs.
  • Fantastic Caverns: This underground cave system is one of the few in the world that can be explored by car. Take a guided tour of the caverns and learn about the unique geology and history of the area.
  • Nathanael Greene/Close Memorial Park: This 113-acre park features walking trails, a lake, a butterfly house, and a Japanese garden. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Springfield

Springfield is home to many great hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best:

  • Galloway Creek Greenway: This 4.5-mile paved trail runs through southeast Springfield and connects to the James River Greenway.
  • Frisco Highline Trail: This 36-mile trail runs from Springfield to Bolivar and follows the old Frisco railroad line. It’s a popular spot for biking, hiking, and horseback riding.
  • Wilson’s Creek Greenway: This 5.5-mile trail runs from Nathanael Greene/Close Memorial Park to the James River. It features scenic views and offers opportunities for fishing and kayaking.

Best Things to Do in Springfield: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Silver Dollar City: This amusement park, located in nearby Branson, offers thrilling rides, shows, and craft demonstrations. It’s a great spot for families and thrill-seekers alike.
  • Take a ghost tour: Springfield has a rich history and several haunted sites, including Pythian Castle and the Gillioz Theatre. Join a ghost tour and learn about the spookier side of the city.
  • Explore the downtown area: Downtown Springfield is filled with art galleries, boutique shops, and delicious restaurants. Take a stroll and soak in the small-town charm.

Week-Long Itinerary for Springfield

Day 1: Visit Fantastic Caverns and explore the nearby wineries. Day 2: Take a tour of Wonders of Wildlife National Museum and Aquarium and spend the afternoon hiking at Wilson’s Creek National Battlefield. Day 3: Go fishing or swimming at Table Rock Lake. Day 4: Visit the Springfield Art Museum and take a ghost tour of the city. Day 5: Explore the downtown area and enjoy some local cuisine. Day 6: Take a bike ride on the Frisco Highline Trail and have a picnic at Nathanael Greene/Close Memorial Park. Day 7: Visit Silver Dollar City and enjoy the rides and shows.

Food and Drink Scene of Springfield

Springfield has a diverse food scene, with something to please every palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cashew chicken: This dish, invented in Springfield, features breaded and fried chicken served with a sweet and savory sauce and topped with cashews. Try it at Leong’s Asian Diner.
  • Toasted ravioli: This appetizer, popular in the Midwest, features deep-fried ravioli served with marinara sauce. Try it at the Springfield Brewing Company.
  • BBQ: Springfield has several great BBQ spots, including Whole Hog Cafe and Crosstown BBQ.

Local Beverages

  • Springfield-style cashew ale: This beer, created by Mother’s Brewery, features notes of cashew and spicy hops.
  • Mint julep: This classic Southern cocktail, made with bourbon and mint, is a refreshing drink on a hot day. Try it at Civil Kitchen.

Where to Stay in Springfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Springfield has several great accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are a few suggestions:

  • University Plaza Hotel and Convention Center: This hotel is located in the heart of downtown and features a pool, restaurant, and bar on-site.
  • Carpenter Street Hotel: This cozy boutique hotel has 30 rooms and is located in the historic district.
  • Route 66 Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located on historic Route 66 and offers both private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in Springfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cashew chicken sauce: Bring home a bottle of the famous cashew chicken sauce to recreate the dish at home.
  • Ozark Mountains t-shirt: Show off your love for Springfield and the Ozarks with a t-shirt or other merchandise featuring the local landscape.
  • Local art: Springfield has a thriving arts scene, so consider purchasing a piece of art to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Springfield

Springfield has a relaxed and friendly culture, with a strong emphasis on community and family. Midwest hospitality is alive and well here, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you.

Best Time to Visit Springfield

The best time to visit Springfield is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the area is showcased. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Springfield

Do:

  • Visit the local breweries and wineries.
  • Take a scenic drive on historic Route 66.
  • Say “hello” to strangers and strike up a conversation.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip your server at restaurants.
  • Intentionally litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Be afraid to try the local cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Springfield: Springfield is a safe city overall, but it’s always important to take precautions like locking your car and being aware of your surroundings in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Springfield: Springfield has several hospitals and medical centers, including CoxHealth and Mercy Hospital, which provide quality healthcare services to residents and visitors alike.

Getting to Springfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Springfield: Springfield-Branson National Airport is located just 5 miles northwest of downtown and is served by several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Springfield: Greyhound and Ozark Regional Transit provide bus service to Springfield from other parts of Missouri and nearby states.

Other Options: Amtrak provides train service to nearby cities, including St. Louis and Kansas City.

How to Get Around in Springfield

Springfield has a public transportation system, including buses and trolleys, but having a car is recommended to fully explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Springfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Branson: This nearby resort town is known for its live entertainment shows, theme parks, and natural beauty.
  • Lake of the Ozarks: This large lake is located in central Missouri and is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • St. Louis: This nearby city is home to several cultural attractions, including the Gateway Arch and the Missouri Botanical Garden.

Conclusion

Springfield, Missouri, is a destination that’s sure to surprise and delight visitors. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or tasty cuisine, this vibrant city has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip now and discover the charm of Springfield for yourself.
