Rockaway Beach, Missouri is a hidden gem located in the Southwest region of the state. Known for its quaint charm and picturesque scenery, this charming town offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. Whether you’re a solo traveler or exploring with friends and family, Rockaway Beach has something for everyone.

Introduction to Rockaway Beach

Rockaway Beach is a small town located in the Ozark Mountains, which are known for their dense forests, scenic riverways, and stunning landscape. This area is a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts to hike, fish, and kayak. Rockaway Beach is no exception, with its breathtaking views and serene atmosphere. While the town sprawls only over a few miles, it boasts many activities for visitors to indulge.

List of Places to Visit in Rockaway Beach

No matter what your interests are, Rockaway Beach has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ozark Folk Center State Park - This park features live music, artisan demonstrations, and craft workshops by local artisans. It also showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Ozarks.
  • Mark Twain Boyhood Home and Museum - Home to famous writer Mark Twain, this museum offers a glimpse into his life and his most celebrated works.

Natural Attractions of Rockaway Beach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Taneycomo - This spot is a haven for fishing enthusiasts to catch trout and explore the stunning views of the lake.
  • Bull Shoals Lake - A perfect spot for water sports, including boating, water skiing, and fishing. It is a great place for adventurers to try out their skills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rockaway Beach

Rockaway Beach has some of the best outdoor trails suited for hikers and bikers. It is a perfect chance to indulge in physical activity and take in the stunning natural beauty.

  • White River Valley Trail System - A 27-mile trail system, it is perfect for hiking, biking, running, and horseback riding.
  • Lakeside Wilderness Trails - A series of four loops offering a chance to witness scenic beauty on foot or bike.

Best Things to Do in Rockaway Beach: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling with family, friends or solo, Rockaway Beach has something for everyone. Here are some must-try activities and tours:

  • Branson Scenic Railway - a 40-mile round trip through the Ozarks showcasing stunning scenery and the rich history of the area.
  • Dogwood Canyon Nature Park - A nature reserve offering a range of activities such as hiking, horseback riding, and wildlife tours of the Ozarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rockaway Beach

If you want to explore Rockaway Beach in more detail, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Mark Twain Boyhood Home and Museum and immerse yourself in his work
Day 2 Go kayaking along the Lake Taneycomo and hike along the trail system
Day 3 Visit the Ozark Folk Center State Park and take part in an artisan workshop or watch a live musical performance
Day 4 Explore the Dogwood Canyon Nature Park and go wildlife spotting
Day 5 Take a scenic train ride through the Ozarks with the Branson Scenic Railway and visit Bull Shoals Lake
Day 6 Take part in outdoor activities like horseback riding and fishing in one of the nature reserves
Day 7 Go shopping for souvenirs at the local shops in downtown Rockaway Beach and end the day with delicious cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Rockaway Beach

This quaint town does not disappoint in terms of food and drink, either. Here are some must-try cuisine and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ozark-style biscuits and gravy at Jen’s Getaway Café
  • Farm-fresh grits at Rocking Chair Resort

Local Beverages

  • Ozark beer - Locally brewed beer using water from Taneycomo Lake and malted barley

Where to Stay in Rockaway Beach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rockaway Beach has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and traveler preference. Here are some of the best places to stay at:

  • Rockaway Beach Resort - A lakeside resort perfect for family vacations and romantic getaways.
  • River of Life Farm - A peaceful retreat overlooking the river with stunning cabins and lodges
  • Ozark Mountain cabins - Cabins tucked away in the natural settings of the Ozarks.

What to Buy in Rockaway Beach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Rockaway Beach home by purchasing these local products:

  • Ozark-style artisanal crafts at the Ozark Folk Center State Park
  • Locally-made jams and maple syrups at Koinonia Bible College cafeteria.

Local Culture and Customs of Rockaway Beach

Rockaway Beach values local customs and traditions that are still visible in the town’s culture. Here are some examples:

  • The Ozark Folk Center State Park hosts regular live music events, artisanal workshops, and cultural festivals to educate visitors about the local culture of the Ozarks.

Best Time to Visit Rockaway Beach

The best time to visit Rockaway Beach is during the summer months (June to August) to enjoy the outdoor activities and festivals. However, Rockaway Beach is just as wonderful during the other seasons, as well.
