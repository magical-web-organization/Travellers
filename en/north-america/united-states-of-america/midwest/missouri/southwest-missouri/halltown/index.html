Introduction to Halltown

Located in the Southwest region of Missouri, Halltown is a small town with a population of around 200. It’s a peaceful and calm place where you can enjoy outdoor activities, explore local culture and traditions, and get away from the hustle and bustle of the city. This travel guide will provide a comprehensive list of things to do and places to visit in Halltown, from hiking and biking routes to museums and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Halltown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Halltown Church of Christ - Built-in 1876, the Halltown Church of Christ is a historical landmark and an excellent example of Gothic Revival architecture.
  • Halltown Cemetery - Established in 1872, the Halltown Cemetery is the final resting place of many of Halltown’s early settlers.
  • Route 66 Iconic Sign - Halltown is located on the famous Route 66, and you can see the iconic Route 66 sign on the highway.

Natural Attractions of Halltown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pioneer Memorial Forest - A 3600-acre forest dedicated to protecting the ecosystem and wildlife. The forest features several trails and picnic areas.
  • Boone Creek Conservation Area - A perfect spot for fishing, birdwatching, and hunting. Boone Creek is home to several species of fish and migratory birds.
  • Turkey Creek Conservation Area - A conservation area that includes hiking trails, a fishing lake, and several picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Halltown

  • Halltown-Joplin Trail - a 36-mile multi-use trail that stretches from Halltown to Joplin and offers scenic views of the countryside.
  • Boone Creek Trail - A 1-mile trail through the Boone Creek Conservation Area that takes you to scenic overlooks of the creek.
  • Pioneer Memorial Forest Trail - A 6-mile loop trail that takes you through the forest and offers views of several species of wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Halltown: Activities and Tours

  • Halltown Summer Fest - Held annually, the Halltown Summer Fest celebrates the town’s history and culture with music, food, and games.
  • Route 66 Drive-In Theater - Enjoy a movie under the stars at this classic drive-in theater on Route 66.
  • Mercantile Halltown - A unique shopping experience featuring local artisans and antiques.

Food and Drink Scene of Halltown

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken - A Southern classic, Halltown’s fried chicken is a must-try.
  • Biscuits and Gravy - A hearty breakfast favorite made with buttermilk biscuits and gravy.
  • Cobbler - A delicious dessert made with fresh fruit and a warm, flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  • Route 66 Root Beer - A locally-made root beer that’s perfect on a hot summer day.
  • Missouri Wines - Missouri is home to several wineries that offer tastings and tours.
