Introduction to Licking

Licking, a small town in Texas County, Missouri, is one of the most charming and friendly places you can visit in the Midwest. The town has a rich history and a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions that make it a perfect destination for various interests. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Licking has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Licking

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Boiling Spring Campground

Covering an area of 90 acres within the Ozark National Scenic Riverways, Boiling Spring Campground offers visitors an opportunity to explore through hiking, swimming, fishing, and many other outdoor activities.

Mahaffie Stagecoach Stop & Farm Historic Site

A stop at Mahaffie Stagecoach Stop & Farm Historic Site gives a glimpse of life on the Missouri frontier in the mid-1800s. Visitors can experience the same things that travelers on the Santa Fe and Oregon-California trails explored as they rested overnight here.

Texas County Museum

Established in 1976, the Texas County Museum is dedicated to preserving and promoting the history and culture of the region. The museum houses a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents related to the county’s history.

Natural Attractions of Licking: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montauk State Park

Montauk State Park is a great escape from city life and is one of the best fishing spots in Missouri. The park is a well-known destination for rainbow and brown trout fishing, and visitors can also enjoy hiking, biking, camping, and picnicking.

Devils Well

Located in the Mark Twain National Forest, Devil’s Well is a natural swimming pool filled with crystal-clear water. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, and enjoy a picnic in this serene location.

Paddy Creek Wilderness

For hikers and backpackers, Paddy Creek Wilderness offers miles of trails through beautiful forests. The trails provide stunning views of rocky bluffs, deep valleys, and peaceful streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Licking

Montauk State Park Trails

Montauk State Park has hiking trails that are perfect for hikers of all levels. The trails offer scenic views of the park, as well as fishing opportunities along the way.

Current River Trail

The Current River Trail is a 19-mile trail that extends along the Current River. The trail is perfect for hikers and bikers, and visitors can enjoy scenic views of the river and the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Licking: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding

Horseback riding is a popular activity in Licking, and several local businesses offer guided tours through the Ozark Mountains.

Ozark National Scenic Riverways Tours

The Ozark National Scenic Riverways offers guided tours of the region’s crystal-clear springs and rivers. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, canoeing, and fishing opportunities on these tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Licking

Must-Try Dishes

Missouri BBQ

Missouri BBQ is a must-try dish in Licking. Several local restaurants offer mouthwatering BBQ options, such as pulled pork sandwiches and BBQ ribs.

Fried Catfish

Fried Catfish is another popular dish in Licking. Local restaurants serve crispy, golden-fried catfish, served hot and fresh.

Local Beverages

Ozark Born and Bred Wine

Ozark Born and Bred Wine is a locally made wine that has gained popularity in recent years. Visitors can enjoy a glass of wine at one of the many wine tasting tours in the region.

Piney River Brewing Company Beer

Piney River Brewing Company Beer is a local favorite famous for its unique blend of flavors. The brewery offers tours and tastings, where visitors can try its authentic, locally made beer.

Where to Stay in Licking: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Boiling Spring Campground

Boiling Spring Campground offers both RV and tent campsites, as well as cabins for overnight stays. The campground provides visitors with easy access to the surrounding nature of the Ozark National Scenic Riverways.

Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites

The Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites offers travelers comfortable and modern lodging options. The hotel is conveniently located near several attractions, including Texas County Museum.

What to Buy in Licking: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Leathercrafts

Licking is known for its Leathercrafts, and several local shops offer handmade leather products, including belts, wallets, and purses.

Woodcrafts

Local artisans create beautiful, handcrafted wood products, such as bowls, platters, and furniture, making them a perfect souvenir or gift to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Licking

Licking is a community-oriented town, and residents are known for their welcoming and friendly nature. Visitors are encouraged to participate in local events, such as the Annual Easter Egg Hunt and the Texas County Fair.

Best Time to Visit Licking

The best time to visit Licking is in the early fall, when the weather is pleasant and the fall foliage adds an extra touch of beauty to the region. Spring and summer months are also great for outdoor activities, although visitors can expect higher temperatures and humidity during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Licking

Do:

  • Visit the Texas County Museum to learn about the region’s history and culture.
  • Explore the Ozark National Scenic Riverways and take part in outdoor activities.
  • Try the local BBQ and fried catfish dishes.
  • Experience local crafts and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Litter in natural areas.
  • Leave valuables in plain sight in a vehicle.
  • Ignore posted safety guidelines at outdoor locations.
  • Interrupt local events or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Licking

Visitors should be aware of potential hazards, such as sharp rocks and steep drop-offs, when hiking and exploring the natural areas of Licking. It is also recommended to follow basic safety precautions, such as locking up valuables in your vehicle and being aware of surroundings.

Health in Licking

Visitors should ensure they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations and take measures to protect against tick-borne illnesses while exploring the great outdoors in Licking. It is also wise to carry an adequate supply of any necessary prescription medications.

Getting to Licking: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Licking

The nearest airport to Licking is Springfield-Branson National Airport. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Licking, which is about a two-and-a-half-hour drive from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Licking

The nearest bus stations to Licking are in Rolla and West Plains, both approximately one hour’s drive away. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Licking from these stations.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Licking from nearby cities and towns such as St. Louis, Springfield, and Branson by following scenic routes through the Ozark Mountains.

How to Get Around in Licking

Getting around Licking is easy, as the town is small enough to explore on foot. However, visitors may opt for car rentals to explore the surrounding areas such as the Ozark National Scenic Riverways.

Where to Go Next from Licking: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Johnson’s Shut-Ins State Park

A day trip to Johnson’s Shut-Ins State Park offers visitors an opportunity to explore the beautiful landscape of the region. The park is known for its small waterfalls, hiking trails, and unique natural formations.

Springfield

Springfield, a vibrant city located just two hours away from Licking, offers visitors plenty of fun and exciting activities, including the Fantastic Caverns, Wonders of Wildlife National Museum and Aquarium, and the Springfield Art Museum.

Conclusion

Licking, Missouri, is a hidden gem in the Ozarks, home to unique cultural and natural attractions that are perfect for travelers seeking outdoor adventures or a relaxing getaway. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the best of what Licking has to offer and plan the perfect trip to this charming Midwest town.
