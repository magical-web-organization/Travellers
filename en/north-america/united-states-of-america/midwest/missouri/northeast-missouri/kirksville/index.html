Introduction to Kirksville

Kirksville, located in Northeast Missouri, is a charming small town known for its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. This vibrant city of 17,500 residents is home to Truman State University and boasts a thriving arts and entertainment scene.

List of Places to Visit in Kirksville

Kirksville offers an array of attractions for visitors looking to explore the city’s history and natural beauty. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kirksville Arts Association Gallery: Showcasing a variety of regional and national artists, this contemporary art gallery in downtown Kirksville is a must-visit for art lovers.
  • Kirksville Downtown Historic District: Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, this district is home to a variety of architectural styles from the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
  • Ruth W. Towne Museum and Welcome Center: Located on Truman State University’s campus, this museum showcases the history of Kirksville and Northeast Missouri, and provides a wide range of visitor information.

Natural Attractions of Kirksville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thousand Hills State Park: Covering over 3,000 acres, this park offers a variety of outdoor recreational activities, including boating, fishing, hiking, and camping in Missouri.
  • Forest-Llewellyn Cemetery: Peaceful, quiet and scenic views with great some great hiking opportunities.
  • Missouri Conservation Center: Learn about the forests, wetlands, and prairies of Northeast Missouri at this outdoor educational center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirksville

For avid hikers and bikers, Kirksville offers a variety of trails and routes to explore. Some popular options include:

  • Kirksville Trail System: With over 10 miles of trails, this system connects numerous parks and green spaces throughout the city.
  • Missouri Conservation Center: Their trail is a great escape taking you through the forested area and different habitats.

Best Things to Do in Kirksville: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Campus Tour: Truman State University offers guided tours of its beautiful campus, including historical buildings and cultural landmarks.
  • Attend a Festival: Kirksville hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, including the Red Barn Arts and Crafts Festival and the Kirksville Fall Festival.
  • Take a Ghost Tour: Learn about the haunted history of Kirksville on a guided ghost tour downtown!

Food and Drink Scene of Kirksville

Kirksville offers a variety of dining options, ranging from classic diners to contemporary bistros. Here are some must-try dishes to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sweet Potato Fries: Crisp and perfectly sweet, this dish is a staple at many Kirksville restaurants.
  • Pulled Pork Sandwich: This savory sandwich is a Missouri favorite, typically made with smoky and sweet barbecue sauce and served with traditional sides like mac and cheese.
  • Fried Chicken: Served crispy and juicy, this southern classic is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Wine Ranch Winery: This family-owned winery produces a variety of red, white, and blush wines made from Missouri-grown grapes.
  • Jackson Street BrewCo: This Kirksville favorite is known for its delicious craft beer and laid-back atmosphere.
  • Fork and Cork: This locally owned restaurant specializes in seasonal food and unique cocktails made with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Kirksville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kirksville offers a variety of lodging options to suit any budget or preference. Some popular choices include:

  • Holiday Inn Express: With comfortable rooms and a convenient location near Truman State University, this hotel is a great choice for families and business travelers.
  • Budget Host Village Inn: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms and easy access to downtown Kirksville.
  • The Depot Inn and Suites: This award-winning hotel is housed in a beautifully restored train depot and offers luxury accommodations, with incredible food and lots of activities.

What to Buy in Kirksville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for unique souvenirs and local products to take home? Check out these shops:

  • Heartland Embroidery: This custom embroidery shop offers unique Kirksville-themed apparel and gifts.
  • Kirksville Farmer’s Market: Open seasonally, this market offers a variety of locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and artisanal products.
  • Traditions Mercantile: This locally owned gallery features art, jewelry, and handmade gifts from regional artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Kirksville

Kirksville has a strong local culture and history. Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Midwest Friendliness: Kirksville residents are known for their hospitality and friendly nature. Don’t be shy about asking for directions or recommendations!
  • Seasonal Weather: The weather in Kirksville can vary greatly depending on the season, so be sure to pack accordingly.
  • Respectful Conduct: As in any small town, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and conduct yourself with politeness and good manners.

Best Time to Visit Kirksville

Getting to Kirksville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kirksville is conveniently located near several major highways, making it easily accessible by car. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Kirksville

  • Kirksville Regional Airport: Served by Cape Air and located just four miles south of downtown Kirksville.

Bus Stations Near Kirksville

  • Jefferson Lines: Offers service to several nearby cities from Kirksville.
  • Greyhound: Connections available to Kirksville from various cities.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Renting a car is the best option for exploring the surrounding areas. Several major car rental companies have offices in Kirksville.

How to Get Around in Kirksville

Kirksville is a walkable and bikeable town, with a variety of trails and bike lanes available. If you prefer to drive, there are several car rental options available or taxi services. The city also features a bus service for travel around town or to nearby communities.

Where to Go Next from Kirksville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Looking to expand your trip beyond Kirksville? Here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Mark Twain Boyhood Home and Museum: Located in nearby Hannibal, this historic site celebrates the life and work of legendary author Mark Twain.
  • Bennett Spring State Park: Known for its world-class trout fishing, this park is located near Lebanon, Missouri.
  • Salt River Equestrian Trail: Enjoy horseback riding through a scenic river valley near Mexico, Missouri.

Conclusion

From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, Kirksville offers something for everyone. So pack your bags and come explore this charming small town in the heart of Northeast Missouri.
