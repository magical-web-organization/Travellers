Mexico is a beautiful country located in the Northeast region of Missouri in the Midwest of the United States of America. Its vibrant culture, rich history, and stunning natural scenery make it a popular destination for travelers from all around the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the must-visit places, exciting activities, delicious food and drinks, and everything else you need to know to plan your trip to Mexico.

Introduction to Mexico

Mexico is a country that is rich in history and culture. It is a country that is known for its beautiful beaches, lush rainforests, and colorful architecture. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Mexico has something for everyone. Its capital, Mexico City, is a vibrant city that is home to some of the best museums, restaurants, and nightlife in the country.

List of Places to Visit in Mexico

Mexico has countless places to visit, here are just a few:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chichen Itza: The ancient Mayan ruins of Chichen Itza are located in the Yucatan Peninsula and are some of the most significant archaeological sites in Mexico.

  • Palenque: Located in the southern state of Chiapas, Palenque is another significant Mayan ruin site that dates back to the 7th century.

  • Frida Kahlo Museum: Located in Mexico City, this museum is dedicated to the life and work of the famous Mexican artist Frida Kahlo.

Natural Attractions of Mexico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Copper Canyon: Located in the northern state of Chihuahua, the Copper Canyon is a series of stunning canyons that are significantly larger than the Grand Canyon in the United States.

  • Cancun Beaches: Cancun is famous for its stunning beaches with crystal-clear waters and white sand.

  • Cozumel: Cozumel is located off the coast of the Yucatan Peninsula and is a popular destination for snorkeling and scuba diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mexico

Mexico also has plenty of hiking and biking routes that adventurous travelers can explore:

  • Sierra Norte Mountains: Located near Oaxaca, the Sierra Norte Mountains offer some of the most breathtaking hiking trails in Mexico.

  • Baja California: Baja California is a peninsula that offers some of the best off-road biking trails in the country.

  • Copper Canyon: In addition to its stunning views, Copper Canyon also has several hiking trails that travelers can explore.

Best Things to Do in Mexico: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Mexico include:

  • Attend a Lucha Libre Match: Lucha Libre is a type of Mexican wrestling that is famous all over the world.

  • Visit the Teotihuacan Pyramids: Located near Mexico City, the Teotihuacan Pyramids are some of the oldest and most significant pyramids in the world.

  • Go Whale Watching: Baja California is home to several species of whales, and travelers can go on whale-watching tours to see them up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mexico

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring Mexico:

Day 1-3: Mexico City

Day 4-5: Oaxaca

Day 6-7: Cancun

Food and Drink Scene of Mexico

Mexican cuisine is famous all over the world and is a must-try for anyone visiting the country. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tacos al pastor: A popular street food consisting of small corn tortillas filled with marinated pork.

  • Chiles en nogada: A Puebla specialty that consists of roasted chiles stuffed with meat and topped with a walnut sauce.

  • Guacamole: Made with fresh avocados, tomato, onion, and lime juice, guacamole is a staple in Mexican cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Tequila: Mexico’s national drink, made from the blue agave plant and is commonly drunk with salt and lime.

  • Horchata: A sweet rice milk beverage that is commonly consumed in Mexico.

  • Michelada: A popular beer cocktail made with beer, lime juice, tomato juice, and hot sauce.

Where to Stay in Mexico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mexico offers a wide range of accommodations that cater to all budgets and travel styles. Here are some options:

  • Hotels: Mexico has plenty of luxury hotels, including the Four Seasons, Rosewood, and St. Regis.

  • Hostels: Mexico also has a thriving hostel scene, with options like Hostel One and Selina.

  • Guesthouses: For a more authentic experience, travelers can stay at guesthouses like Casa Mermejita in Oaxaca.

What to Buy in Mexico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mexico is famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and silver jewelry. Here are some of the best things to buy in Mexico:

  • Talavera Pottery: Hand-painted pottery that is a specialty of the Puebla region.

  • Huipil: A traditional blouse that is often brightly colored and embroidered with intricate patterns.

  • Silver Jewelry: Taxco is a town in Mexico that is famous for its silver jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Mexico

Mexican culture is a colorful mix of pre-Columbian traditions and Spanish influences. It is a culture that values family, food, and music. Mexicans are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature.

Best Time to Visit Mexico

Mexico is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on the region you plan to explore. Generally, the best time to visit is from December to April when the weather is dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mexico

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Mexico:

Do:

  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases.

  • Explore the local culture and try new foods.

  • Carry cash with you as some places may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, always drink bottled water.

  • Don’t hail taxis off the street; use an authorized taxi service.

  • Don’t wear flashy jewelry or carry around expensive gadgets.

Safety and Health

Mexico is a safe destination for travelers, but like anywhere else in the world, it’s important to take precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Mexico

  • Avoid walking around alone at night.

  • Take an authorized taxi service, especially at night.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Mexico

  • Drink only bottled water.

  • Use mosquito nets or repellents to protect yourself from mosquito-borne diseases.

  • Be cautious of what you eat and drink to avoid contracting traveler’s diarrhea.

Getting to Mexico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mexico can be easily accessed by air, bus, or car. Here are some of the best transportation options:

Airports Near Mexico

  • Mexico City International Airport
  • Cancun International Airport
  • Los Cabos International Airport

Bus Stations Near Mexico

  • Mexico City Central de Autobuses
  • Cancun Bus Station
  • Guadalajara Central de Autobuses

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Renting a car is a great way to explore the country at your own pace.
  • Trains: Mexico has a limited train system, but there are a few scenic routes, like the Copper Canyon train.

How to Get Around in Mexico

  • Taxis: Taxis are a popular mode of transportation in Mexico. Always use an authorized taxi service.

  • Buses: Mexico has a vast network of buses that connect most towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Mexico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Guatemala: Explore the ancient Mayan ruins of Tikal.

  • Belize: Visit the stunning coral reefs and lush rainforests.

  • Cuba: Experience the vibrant culture and stunning architecture of Cuba.

Conclusion

Mexico is a beautiful country that has something to offer everyone. Whether you are looking for adventure, relaxation, or a cultural experience, Mexico has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and explore all that Mexico has to offer.
