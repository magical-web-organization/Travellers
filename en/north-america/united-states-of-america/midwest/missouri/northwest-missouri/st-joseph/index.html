Introduction to St. Joseph

St. Joseph is a charming and historic city located in Northwest Missouri, known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural landmarks. Originally founded as a trading post in the early 19th century, today, it has become a thriving city with a diverse community and numerous attractions that cater to visitors of all interests and ages.

From exploring its fascinating museums and historical landmarks to hiking through its scenic parks and enjoying the local food and drink scene, St. Joseph offers something for everyone. So, whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, St. Joseph should be on your travel bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in St. Joseph

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the primary attractions of St. Joseph is its rich history, and there are numerous museums and historical landmarks that cater to history enthusiasts.

The Pony Express National Museum

The Pony Express National Museum offers a fascinating insight into the history and legacy of the Pony Express, which was a mail delivery service that ran from 1860 to 1861.

The Jesse James Home Museum

Another museum worth visiting is the Jesse James Home Museum, which was the house where the infamous outlaw, Jesse James, was shot and killed in 1882. Today, it has been turned into a museum that showcases the life and times of Jesse James and his gang.

The Glore Psychiatric Museum

If you’re interested in a more macabre history, the Glore Psychiatric Museum provides an eye-opening look at the mental health treatments used in the past.

Natural Attractions of St. Joseph: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St. Joseph offers stunning natural beauty, with parks, rivers, and lakes that are perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, fishing, and boating.

Krug Park

Krug Park is a popular park that spans 163 acres and offers numerous amenities, including an outdoor amphitheater, a rose garden, a lagoon, and a stunning view of the Missouri River.

The St. Joseph Parkway System

The St. Joseph Parkway System is a network of trails that connect the city’s parks and open spaces, providing an excellent opportunity for hikers and bikers to explore the area’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Joseph

St. Joseph is home to numerous hiking and biking trails, offering breathtaking scenery and opportunities to get outdoors.

The Weston Bend State Park

The Weston Bend State Park is a beautiful park located on the banks of the Missouri River, with diverse trails that cater to both beginner and advanced hikers.

The Pony Express National Historic Trail

The Pony Express National Historic Trail is a 1,800-mile trail that runs from St. Joseph, MO, to Sacramento, CA, and follows the route taken by Pony Express riders in the 1860s. The trail offers hikers and bikers a chance to explore the history and natural beauty of the American West.

Best Things to Do in St. Joseph: Activities and Tours

St. Joseph offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences.

Patee House Museum and the Jesse James Home

One of the popular activities in St. Joseph is the Patee House Museum and Jesse James Home tour, which takes visitors on a two-hour tour that covers the city’s history and major landmarks.

Riverboat Cruise

Another activity worth experiencing is the riverboat cruise, which offers a unique perspective of the Missouri River and its stunning vista.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Joseph

If you’re planning a week-long trip to St. Joseph, here is one suggested itinerary that includes some of the city’s top attractions:

Day 1: Explore the history and culture of the city with visits to the Pony Express National Museum, Patee House Museum, and the Jesse James Home.

Day 2: Get outdoors by hiking or biking the St. Joseph Parkway System and enjoying the scenery at Krug Park.

Day 3: Visit the Glore Psychiatric Museum for an eerie and eye-opening look at the history of mental health treatments.

Day 4: Explore the Weston Bend State Park and its diverse trails

Day 5: Indulge in the local food and drink scene of St. Joseph by sampling the best dishes and beverages the city has to offer.

Day 6: Take a riverboat cruise and savor the stunning views of the Missouri river.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Kansas City or explore the local shops and buy souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Joseph

St. Joseph is well-known for its delicious food and drink, with numerous restaurants, bars, and cafes that cater to different tastes and preferences.

Must-Try Dishes

The Famous Steak Soup

The Famous Steak Soup is a signature dish of St. Joseph, made with beef, potatoes, carrots, celery, onions, and tomatoes.

The Pork Tenderloin Sandwich

Another iconic St. Joseph dish is the Pork Tenderloin Sandwich, which features a breaded pork tenderloin served on a Kaiser bun with toppings such as lettuce, tomato, and onion.

Local Beverages

The Root Beer

One of the most popular drinks in St. Joseph is the root beer from Fritz’s Railroad Restaurant, which is a classic root beer served in frosted mugs.

The Grape Nehi

Another iconic St. Joseph drink is the Grape Nehi, which is a grape-flavored soda that was first produced in the city.

Where to Stay in St. Joseph: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Joseph offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses that cater to different budgets and preferences.

Hotel Frederick

Hotel Frederick is a historic hotel located in the heart of downtown St. Joseph, with luxurious rooms and amenities.

Stoney Creek Hotel

For a more affordable accommodation option, Stoney Creek Hotel is a great choice with spacious rooms and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in St. Joseph: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of St. Joseph, consider these local products:

Vintage Jesse James wanted posters

Authentic vintage Jesse James wanted posters are a popular souvenir to bring home to commemorate the infamous outlaw.

Marie Antoinette Caramels

Marie Antoinette Caramels is a local confectionery that makes delicious caramels, perfect for a sweet treat or a gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Joseph

St. Joseph is a melting pot of various cultures and traditions. Still, one aspect that stands out is the city’s love for history and preservation of historical landmarks and stories.

Best Time to Visit St. Joseph

The best time to visit St. Joseph is during the fall season, from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant and the fall foliage offers stunning scenery for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Joseph

Do:

  • Explore the city’s rich history and visit its museums and historical sites
  • Get outdoors and explore the scenic parks and trails
  • Indulge in the local food and drink scene
  • Experience the local culture and customs
  • Buy souvenirs and local products to bring home

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the city’s historical landmarks or natural beauty
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Joseph

St. Joseph is a relatively safe city for travelers, but it is advisable to practice usual safety protocols such as keeping your belongings secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in St. Joseph

St. Joseph has several reputable healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, in case of any medical emergencies during your visit.

Getting to St. Joseph: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Joseph

Kansas City International Airport is the nearest international airport and is approximately 35 miles south of St. Joseph.

Bus Stations Near St. Joseph

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a terminal in downtown St. Joseph, connecting it with other U.S. cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in St. Joseph by train or drive in through its well-connected highway system.

How to Get Around in St. Joseph

St. Joseph offers several transportation options such as taxis, public buses, and car rentals to help travelers get around.

Where to Go Next from St. Joseph: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore the area, here are some destinations near St. Joseph worth visiting:

Kansas City

Kansas City is a vibrant city with numerous attractions such as the National World War I Museum and Memorial, the Country Club Plaza, and the Power & Light District.

Weston

Weston is a nearby charming, small town known for its antiques, cozy bed and breakfasts, wineries, and coffee shops.

Conclusion

St. Joseph is an authentic and cultural city with something for everyone, from its fascinating history and natural beauty to its food, drink, and local products. Plan your itinerary today, explore the city’s rich culture and make memories that will last a lifetime.
