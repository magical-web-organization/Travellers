Introduction to Weston

Located in Northwest Missouri, Weston is a quaint and picturesque town that offers a perfect mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a shopaholic, Weston has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Weston

Before we dive into the details, here is a quick list of must-visit places in Weston:

  • Weston Bend State Park
  • Weston Historical Museum
  • Pirtle Winery
  • Green Dirt Farm
  • Holladay Distillery
  • O’Malley’s Pub

Now let’s take a closer look at these places.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Weston is home to several museums and historical places that will take you back in time and offer a glimpse into the town’s rich heritage. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Weston Historical Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Weston and its surrounding areas. It features exhibits on the town’s founding, development, and notable events and people.
  • Weston State Hospital Museum: Located in the historic Weston State Hospital building, this museum offers an eerie and fascinating look into the history of mental health treatment in the United States.
  • Weston Tobacco Barns: These old tobacco barns scattered throughout Weston are a testament to the town’s tobacco farming history. Take a self-guided tour to explore them at your own pace.

Natural Attractions of Weston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Weston’s natural beauty is awe-inspiring and offers visitors a chance to explore the great outdoors. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Weston Bend State Park: This beautiful park offers stunning views of the Missouri River Valley and features miles of hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and campsites.
  • Weston Bluffs Trail: This easy hike offers panoramic views of the Missouri River and the surrounding valleys.
  • Weston Orchard and Vineyard: Located on the banks of the Missouri River, this working farm offers visitors the chance to pick their own fruit and enjoy wine-tasting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Weston

Weston Bend State Park boasts several hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of outdoor enthusiasts. The scenic Weston Bluffs Trail is a must-do for avid hikers, while the easy-going paved trail provides a leisurely bike ride through the picturesque park.

Best Things to Do in Weston: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the town’s museums and historical sites, Weston offers plenty of other activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Weston:

  • Pirtle Winery: Wine lovers should check out Pirtle Winery, which offers tastings of locally grown and produced wines.
  • Green Dirt Farm: Visit this local cheese farm and try some of their award-winning cheeses, made from sustainably raised sheep and cows.
  • Holladay Distillery: Take a tour of this historic distillery and learn about the art of making fine whiskey while enjoying tastings.
  • O’Malley’s Pub: This charming pub is a local hot-spot, offering visitors the chance to try local beer and enjoy live music or trivia nights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Weston

Here is a week-long itinerary that takes in all the must-visit attractions in Weston:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Weston Historical Museum and enjoy lunch at O’Malley’s Pub.
Day 2 Hike the Weston Bluffs Trail and visit Green Dirt Farm.
Day 3 Go on a wine-tasting tour at Pirtle Winery.
Day 4 Take a tour of the Holladay Distillery and enjoy lunch in downtown Weston.
Day 5 Relax and take a picnic in the Weston Bend State Park.
Day 6 Visit the Weston State Hospital Museum and take a self-guided tour of the Weston Tobacco Barns.
Day 7 Explore downtown Weston and try some local food, beers and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Weston

Weston has a thriving food and drink scene, with something to offer everyone. Be sure to try some of their local dishes and drinks!

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Chicken: Head to O’Malley’s Pub for their delicious fried chicken, which is crispy and juicy.
  • Missouri River Trout: Try this local favorite fish at Green Dirt Farm.
  • Loaded Taters: A dish that is a must-try at Weston’s Fork & Grin Grill. This unique version of loaded tater tots is a locals favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Missouri Wine: Pirtle Winery offers tastings of locally grown and produced wines. Do try the Norton.
  • Local Beer: Weston Brewing Company is a locally famous brewery. Try their O’Malley’s Irish Cream Ale.

Where to Stay in Weston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Weston has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly to luxury. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • The Inn at Weston Landing: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Weston and offers cozy rooms with antique furnishings and modern amenities such as WIFI. They also serve breakfast.
  • Weston Red Barn Farm: This lodging option is perfect for families or groups who want more room to spread out. The Farmhouse has multiple bedrooms, a fully equipped kitchen, and a large outdoor patio with a grill.
  • Cedar Crest Lodge: This luxurious lodge is located just a few miles from Weston and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Every room has a private outdoor hot tub.

What to Buy in Weston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, Weston is the perfect place for you. Here are some suggestions:

  • Weston Tobacco Company: Buy some of their locally grown tobacco, handcrafted cigars, and other smoking accessories. The shop also a great historical section.
  • Hammerpress: Get some unique and locally made stationery, prints, and other paper goods from this charming shop located in downtown Weston.

Local Culture and Customs of Weston

Weston is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who are always happy to share the town’s history and traditions. Don’t be surprised if you get a friendly wave or smile from a passerby on the street and do wave back.

Best Time to Visit Weston

The best time to visit Weston is during the fall and spring when the weather is mild and the leaves are changing or blooming, respectively. The holiday season is also a peak time to enjoy the festivities and lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Weston

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts of visiting Weston:

Do:

  • Visit the town’s museums and historical places to learn about the town’s fascinating history.
  • Try the local wine, beer, and food.
  • Take a hike or bike ride through Weston Bend State Park to enjoy the natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be afraid to ask local, they are always willing to help visitors.
  • Don’t speed through town and miss out on Weston’s charming slow pace and history.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the town.

Safety and Health

Weston is a generally safe town, but visitors should still take necessary precautions and practice common sense. Here are some tips on safety and staying healthy in Weston:

Safety in Weston

  • Always lock your car and keep valuables out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when hiking or walking alone.
  • Make sure to check weather conditions before going out on any outdoor adventure activities.

Health in Weston

  • Use insect repellent since ticks are common in the natural areas around Weston.
  • Stay hydrated while exploring and ensure to wear appropriate clothing for the weather.

Getting to Weston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Weston is easily accessible by car or bike. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Weston

  • Kansas City International Airport: Located about 20 miles south of Weston, offering flights to various domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Weston

  • Greyhound Bus Station located in Kansas City and St. Joseph, Missouri, offers interstate bus service to and from Weston.

Other Options

  • Weston is just off of Interstate 29, which connects to the greater highway system of the Midwest.
  • Uber and Lyft are available in Weston.

How to Get Around in Weston

The town of Weston is rather small, and most attractions and restaurants are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer to drive or bike, there is plenty of parking available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Weston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Weston is a great jumping-off point for several nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the places that are worth a visit:

  • St. Joseph, Missouri: A larger city with plenty of restaurants and shops, located just a twenty-minute drive from Weston.
  • Kansas City, Missouri: A vibrant and cosmopolitan city with lots of cultural attractions, located about a forty-five-minute drive from Weston.
  • Wildwood Outdoor Education Center: A unique and fun outdoor adventure center located just a few miles north of Weston, featuring ziplines, ropes courses, and team building activities.

Conclusion

Weston is a charming and unique town that offers something for everyone. Whether you are interested in history, culture, nature, or simply want to relax and enjoy the slower pace of life, Weston will not disappoint.
