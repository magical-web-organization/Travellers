Introduction to Lincoln Park-Old Town

Lincoln Park-Old Town is a stunning neighborhood located in the heart of Chicago, Illinois. Boasting both old-world charm and contemporary attractions, this area is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. This guidebook will explore the best things to see and do in Lincoln Park-Old Town, including culturally important attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, the food and drink scene, local customs, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Lincoln Park-Old Town

Here is a list of the must-visit places in Lincoln Park-Old Town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Chicago History Museum: Learn about the rich history of Chicago and the Midwest at this interactive museum.

  2. The Peggy Notebaert Nature Museum: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the city’s natural landscape, featuring interactive exhibits and live animals.

  3. The Second City: Founded in 1959, this iconic improv comedy club has launched the careers of many famous comedians, including Tina Fey and Stephen Colbert.

Natural Attractions of Lincoln Park-Old Town: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lincoln Park Zoo: This free zoo is home to over 1,000 animals and is a great place to spend an afternoon with the family.

  2. The Lincoln Park Conservatory: This glass-enclosed conservatory houses a beautiful variety of flowers, plants, and gardens.

  3. North Avenue Beach: Enjoy stunning views of Lake Michigan and the Chicago skyline while lounging on this popular beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lincoln Park-Old Town

Lincoln Park-Old Town is known for its beautiful trails and scenic views. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  1. The 606 Trail: This elevated trail is perfect for bikers and walkers, offering stunning views of the city and several art installations along the way.

  2. Lincoln Park Nature Boardwalk: This wooden boardwalk offers a serene stroll through Lincoln Park’s natural habitats and winding lagoons.

  3. Lakefront Trail: This scenic trail runs for over 18 miles alongside Lake Michigan and is perfect for biking, jogging, or walking.

Best Things to Do in Lincoln Park-Old Town: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Lincoln Park-Old Town:

  1. Chicago Helicopter Experience: See the city from a bird’s eye view on this exhilarating helicopter tour.

  2. Chicago Architecture River Cruise: Learn about the city’s stunning architecture on this 90-minute river cruise.

  3. The Second City Walking Tour: Explore the city’s rich history of comedy with this informative walking tour of Old Town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lincoln Park-Old Town

Day 1: Visit the Chicago History Museum, followed by a walk through Lincoln Park and the Lincoln Park Zoo.

Day 2: Tour the city’s stunning architecture with the Chicago Architecture River Cruise, followed by a visit to the Lincoln Park Conservatory.

Day 3: Take a stroll through Old Town and visit the famous Second City comedy club, and end the night with a delicious dinner in one of the neighborhood’s top restaurants.

Day 4: Explore the stunning views of the Lakefront Trail with a bike rental, followed by an afternoon at North Avenue Beach.

Day 5: Take a helicopter tour of the city with Chicago Helicopter Experience, followed by a casual dinner at one of the many charming cafes in Lincoln Park.

Day 6: Visit the Peggy Notebaert Nature Museum and take a stroll through the nearby Lincoln Park Nature Boardwalk.

Day 7: End your trip with a leisurely day at the beach or exploring the many local shops and boutiques in the neighborhood.

Food and Drink Scene of Lincoln Park-Old Town

No visit to Lincoln Park-Old Town would be complete without indulging in the amazing food and drink offerings. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Deep-Dish Pizza from Lou Malnati’s Pizzeria: This iconic Chicago-style pizza is a must-try when visiting the city.

  2. BBQ Ribs from R.J. Grunts: This casual eatery serves up mouth-watering ribs, fries, and other classic American fare.

  3. Hot Dogs from The Weiner’s Circle: This famous hot dog stand is known for its juicy and delicious hot dogs.

Local Beverages

  1. Chicago’s Own Goose Island Beer Company: Sample some of the city’s best craft beer at this popular brewery.

  2. The Chicago Old Fashioned: This classic cocktail is a must-try in Chicago, with several local bars and restaurants putting their own spin on the recipe.

Where to Stay in Lincoln Park-Old Town: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great options for accommodation in the Lincoln Park-Old Town area, including boutique hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Here are some top recommendations:

  1. Hotel Lincoln: This charming boutique hotel boasts stunning views of Lincoln Park and Lake Michigan.

  2. The Inn at Lincoln Park: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a central location within walking distance of several attractions.

  3. Chicago Getaway Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and plenty of organized activities and events.

What to Buy in Lincoln Park-Old Town: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lincoln Park-Old Town is home to many charming boutiques and shops, perfect for picking up unique souvenirs and locally-made products. Here are some top recommendations:

  1. The Fudge Pot: This iconic candy store has been a staple in the neighborhood since 1963, and sells everything from homemade fudge to saltwater taffy.

  2. The Spice House: This specialty spice shop sells fresh, high-quality spices for cooking and baking, as well as unique spice blends and gift sets.

Local Culture and Customs of Lincoln Park-Old Town

Lincoln Park-Old Town is a welcoming and diverse neighborhood, with a rich history of arts, culture, and community. Visitors should be sure to respect local customs and etiquette, and take time to learn about the city’s unique history and character.

Best Time to Visit Lincoln Park-Old Town

The best time to visit Lincoln Park-Old Town is from late spring through early fall, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities and attractions to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lincoln Park-Old Town

Do:

  • Take time to explore the neighborhood on foot, as Lincoln Park-Old Town has many hidden gems and charming streets to discover.

  • Try the local food and drink offerings, and make sure to indulge in some deep-dish pizza and craft beer.

  • Respect local customs and etiquette, including being courteous to other pedestrians and following the rules of the road while biking.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public, as this is illegal in Chicago and can result in a fine or citation.

  • litter or leave trash on the streets or in the parks, as this is considered disrespectful and can damage the environment.

  • Leave valuables unattended or unsecured, as theft can be a problem in busy tourist areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lincoln Park-Old Town

Overall, Lincoln Park-Old Town is a safe and welcoming neighborhood, with low crime rates and friendly locals. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Lincoln Park-Old Town

There are several hospitals and medical facilities located near Lincoln Park-Old Town, including Advocate Illinois Masonic Medical Center and Presence Saint Joseph Hospital. Visitors should also take standard health precautions, such as staying hydrated and using sunscreen during the summer months.

Getting to Lincoln Park-Old Town: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lincoln Park-Old Town

Chicago is served by two major airports: O’Hare International Airport and Midway International Airport. Both offer convenient transportation options to Lincoln Park-Old Town, including taxis, shuttle buses, and public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Lincoln Park-Old Town

The Greyhound bus station in Chicago is located in the West Loop neighborhood, about 3 miles south of Lincoln Park-Old Town. Visitors can easily take public transportation or a taxi to reach the neighborhood.

Other Options

Chicago is also accessible by train, with several Amtrak routes serving the city’s Union Station. Additionally, several major highways run through the city, making it easy to arrive by car.

How to Get Around in Lincoln Park-Old Town

Lincoln Park-Old Town is a highly walkable neighborhood, with several attractions and amenities located within a short distance of each other. Visitors can also take advantage of public transportation options, including the Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) buses and “L” trains, as well as bike rentals and taxi services.

Where to Go Next from Lincoln Park-Old Town: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many great destinations within easy reach of Lincoln Park-Old Town, including:

  1. The Magnificent Mile: Located just a few miles south of Lincoln Park-Old Town, this famous shopping district features more than 460 stores, restaurants, and hotels.

  2. The Art Institute of Chicago: This iconic art museum is located just south of Millennium Park and is home to more than 300,000 works of art.

  3. Navy Pier: Located on the shores of Lake Michigan, Navy Pier is a family-friendly entertainment destination with attractions, restaurants, and special events year-round.

Conclusion

Lincoln Park-Old Town is a beautiful and vibrant neighborhood, with plenty to offer visitors of all ages and interests. From the stunning natural attractions to the world-renowned food and drink scene, this area is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Chicago. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan an unforgettable trip to Lincoln Park-Old Town.
