Introduction to Loop

Loop is the commercial, cultural, and financial center of Chicago, located in the heart of the city’s downtown area. It is named after the elevated train tracks that form a loop around the district. This neighborhood is home to some of the most iconic landmarks in the city, such as Millennium Park, the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, and the Art Institute of Chicago. Whether you’re into culture, history, or just want to explore the city, Loop has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Loop

Here are some of the top places to visit in Loop:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Art Institute of Chicago: One of the oldest and largest museums in the United States, the Art Institute of Chicago features an extensive collection of art and artifacts from around the world.
  • The Chicago Cultural Center: Originally built as a public library, the Chicago Cultural Center now serves as a cultural hub for the city, hosting a wide range of performances, exhibitions, and programs.
  • The Harold Washington Library Center: The main library of the Chicago Public Library system, the Harold Washington Library Center is known for its impressive architecture and design.
  • The Chicago History Museum: Learn about the history of Chicago, from its founding to the present day, through a collection of exhibits and artifacts at this fascinating museum.

Natural Attractions of Loop: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Millennium Park: This iconic park features a wide variety of outdoor art installations, including the famous “Bean,” as well as a state-of-the-art music pavilion.
  • Grant Park: One of the city’s largest parks, Grant Park offers scenic views of Lake Michigan, as well as a variety of recreational activities and events.
  • Chicago Riverwalk: Take a stroll along the Chicago River and enjoy beautiful views of the city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Loop

Looking for a way to explore Loop on foot or by bike? Here are some great route options:

  • Lakefront Trail: This 18-mile trail follows the shoreline of Lake Michigan and offers stunning views of the water and the city skyline.
  • Chicago Riverwalk: Follow the Chicago Riverwalk for a unique perspective on the city, with plenty of sights to see along the way.
  • The 606 Trail: This former rail line has been converted into a trail and park, providing a unique urban green space and a great way to explore the city.

Best Things to Do in Loop: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top things to do in Loop:

  • Architecture Boat Tour: Learn about Chicago’s iconic buildings and skyline on a relaxing boat tour along the Chicago River.
  • Chicago Symphony Orchestra: Experience world-class music at the Symphony Center, home to the renowned Chicago Symphony Orchestra.
  • Navy Pier: This popular tourist destination offers a wide range of activities and attractions, including restaurants, shops, and a Ferris wheel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Loop

Looking to spend a week exploring Loop? Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Millennium Park, including the “Bean” and the Jay Pritzker Pavilion.

Day 2: Visit the Art Institute of Chicago and the Chicago Cultural Center.

Day 3: Check out Grant Park and take a scenic walk along the Chicago Riverwalk.

Day 4: Take an architecture boat tour and visit the Chicago Symphony Orchestra.

Day 5: Explore Navy Pier and take in the views of Lake Michigan.

Day 6: Visit the Chicago History Museum and the Harold Washington Library Center.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products to bring home in the Loop’s many stores and boutiques.

Food and Drink Scene of Loop

Chicago is known for its delicious food, and Loop is no exception.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style deep-dish pizza: This iconic pizza is known for its thick crust, hearty toppings, and generous cheese.
  • Italian beef sandwich: A Chicago classic, this sandwich features thinly sliced roast beef that is slow-cooked with Italian spices and served on a crusty roll.
  • Hot dog: Chicago-style hot dogs are all-beef, served on a poppy-seed bun, and topped with mustard, onions, relish, and other condiments.

Local Beverages

  • Chicago-style beer: Chicago is home to a wide range of craft breweries, offering a variety of delicious beers.
  • Chicago-style cocktails: Many Chicago bars and restaurants offer unique and creative cocktails, often inspired by the city’s history and culture.

Where to Stay in Loop: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Loop offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget and taste.

  • The Palmer House: This historic hotel is located in the heart of the Loop and features luxurious accommodations, fine dining, and easy access to all the city’s attractions.
  • Hostelling International Chicago: For budget travelers, this hostel offers affordable and comfortable accommodations in the heart of the city.
  • The Alise Chicago: This boutique hotel is known for its stylish decor and personalized service, making it a great choice for travelers looking for a unique and upscale experience.

What to Buy in Loop: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring home from your trip to Loop? Here are some great options:

  • Chicago-style popcorn: This sweet and savory popcorn is a local favorite and a great snack to bring back home.
  • Chicago-themed souvenirs: From t-shirts to mugs to keychains, there are plenty of souvenirs available featuring iconic Chicago landmarks and symbols.
  • Local art and crafts: The city is home to many talented artists and artisans, offering a wide range of unique and beautiful handmade products.

Local Culture and Customs of Loop

Chicagoans are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. When visiting Loop, be sure to greet locals with a smile and a friendly hello. Chicago is also known for its robust arts and culture scene, with a wide variety of music, theater, and dance performances happening all year round.

Best Time to Visit Loop

Chicago has a distinct four-season climate, with cold winters and hot summers. The best time to visit Loop is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but be prepared for crowds and high temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Loop

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Loop:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including deep-dish pizza, hot dogs, and Italian beef sandwiches.
  • Do take in a performance by the Chicago Symphony Orchestra.
  • Do explore the city’s cultural and historical attractions, such as the Art Institute of Chicago and the Chicago Cultural Center.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to dress for the weather, especially during the winter months.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on where to eat, shop, and explore.
  • Don’t forget to tip your server at a restaurant or bartender at a bar.

Safety and Health

Chicago is generally a safe city, but as with any urban area, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Loop

  • Stick to well-lit, busy areas, especially at night.
  • Keep your belongings close to you and be mindful of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.

Health in Loop

  • Chicago has excellent healthcare facilities, should you need medical attention.
  • Be sure to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun during the summer months.
  • Chicago is known for its delicious food, but be sure to also eat plenty of fruits and vegetables to stay healthy.

Getting to Loop: Transportation Options and Logistics

Loop is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options.

Airports Near Loop

  • O’Hare International Airport (ORD): Located approximately 17 miles northwest of the Loop.
  • Midway International Airport (MDW): Located approximately 12 miles southwest of the Loop.

Bus Stations Near Loop

  • Greyhound Station: Located at 630 W Harrison St, just west of the Loop.
  • Megabus Stop: Located at Canal St and Jackson Blvd, within walking distance of the Loop.

Other Options

  • Chicago Union Station: Amtrak train station located in the West Loop.
  • Chicago Transit Authority (CTA): Chicago’s public transportation system, including buses and trains.

How to Get Around in Loop

Loop is a walkable neighborhood, making it easy to explore on foot. Additionally, the CTA’s “L” train system provides easy access to other areas of the city.

Where to Go Next from Loop: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While Loop has plenty to offer on its own, there are also many nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, such as:

  • The Museum of Science and Industry: Located on the south side of the city, this museum is dedicated to science and innovation.
  • The Lincoln Park Zoo: Located in the Lincoln Park neighborhood, this free zoo is home to a variety of animals from around the world.
  • The Magnificent Mile: Just north of the Loop, this shopping district is home to luxury boutiques, restaurants, and hotels.

Conclusion

Loop is a vibrant and dynamic neighborhood that offers something for everyone. From cultural attractions to outdoor adventures to delicious food and drink, there’s always something new to discover in this iconic district.
