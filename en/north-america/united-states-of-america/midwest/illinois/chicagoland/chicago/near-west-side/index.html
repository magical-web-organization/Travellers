Introduction to Near West Side

The Near West Side is a charming neighborhood located just minutes away from Chicago’s downtown, comprising some of the city’s most vibrant and culturally diverse areas. With plenty to offer in terms of food, drink, entertainment, and history, the Near West Side is a perfect destination for those looking to experience the real Chicago. In this guide, we’ll take a detailed look at all the must-see attractions, local culture, and much more, so you can make the most of your time in this exciting part of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Near West Side

The Near West Side is truly a melting pot of different cultures and interests, with plenty of attractions to suit all tastes. Here are some of the top places to visit while you’re in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jane Addams Hull House Museum: A historic landmark that celebrates the life and work of Jane Addams, a pioneer in social work and feminism.
  • National Hellenic Museum: Dedicated to showcasing the Greek-American experience.
  • Mexican Fine Arts Center Museum: A space where Mexican art and culture come to life.
  • Chicago History Museum: Boasting a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s rich history.
  • Glessner House: A beautiful and historically important mansion that dates back to 1886.

Natural Attractions of Near West Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Union Park: A beautiful space with picnic areas, sports facilities, and a lagoon.
  • Eisenhower Park: A large, well-equipped park offering plenty of recreational opportunities.
  • Chicago Riverwalk: A scenic pedestrian walkway located along the Chicago River, with stunning views of the city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Near West Side

For those who love hiking and biking, the Near West Side is a great area to explore on foot. Here are a few of the best routes to take:

  • The 606 Trail: A 2.7-mile trail that runs along an old railway line, offering stunning views of the city skyline.
  • The Prairie Path: A 61-mile trail that runs through some of the most picturesque areas of the Near West Side.
  • Greenway Trail: A 25-mile trail that runs along the Des Plaines River, offering beautiful views and plenty of opportunities to spot wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Near West Side: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something a little more exciting, there are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in the Near West Side:

  • Kayaking the Chicago River: Paddle your way through the city’s waterways with kayaking tours that offer fantastic views of the skyline.
  • Chicago Architecture Foundation River Cruise: This is a must-do for any architecture buffs, taking you on a journey through the city’s most unique and stunning structures.
  • Second City Comedy Club: One of the city’s most famous comedy clubs, featuring hilarious shows that will leave you in stitches.
  • Chicago Athletic Association: A historic building that now houses a hotel and private club, it also has some of the best views of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Near West Side

Chicago is famous for its food scene, with the Near West Side being no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style pizza: A deep-dish pizza layered with cheese and toppings that is a Chicago classic.
  • Italian beef sandwich: A sandwich made with juicy, thinly sliced beef, served on a toasted French roll with peppers and cheese.
  • Chicago-style hot dog: A hot dog topped with mustard, onions, relish, and celery salt, served on a poppy seed bun.

Local Beverages

  • Chicago-style cocktail: A classic cocktail made with gin, bitters, and orange liqueur, with a twist of lemon.
  • Goose Island beer: An award-winning Chicago brewery that offers a range of craft beers, including its famous IPA.
  • Malort: A bitter liqueur that is a Chicago tradition, often consumed as a shot.

Local Culture and Customs of Near West Side

The Near West Side is a melting pot of cultures, and it’s essential to respect the diversity and customs of the area. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be respectful of religious and cultural customs: There are many houses of worship in the area, so be mindful of customs and dress appropriately.
  • Be mindful of noise levels: The Near West Side is a residential area, so keep noise levels down, especially at night.
  • Be friendly and polite: The locals are friendly and welcoming, so be sure to reciprocate that warmth.

Getting to Near West Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Near West Side is easily accessible from Chicago’s downtown and surrounding areas. Here are a few transportation options:

  • Chicago Transit Authority (CTA): The CTA offers bus and train services that run through the Near West Side and surrounding areas.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services: There are plenty of taxis and ride-sharing services available that will take you to and from the Near West Side.
  • Car rental: If you prefer to drive, there are plenty of car rental options available in Chicago.

Airports Near Near West Side

  • O’Hare International Airport: Located approximately 20 miles away from the Near West Side, O’Hare is Chicago’s largest airport and offers plenty of domestic and international flights.
  • Midway International Airport: Located approximately 10 miles away from the Near West Side, Midway offers domestic flights from various airlines.

Bus Stations Near Near West Side

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located just a few miles north of the Near West Side in the West Loop, the Greyhound Bus Station offers a variety of domestic and international bus routes.

Other Options
