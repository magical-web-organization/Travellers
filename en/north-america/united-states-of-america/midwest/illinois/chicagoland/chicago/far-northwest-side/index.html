Introduction to Far Northwest Side

Far Northwest Side is a hidden gem in Chicago, a thriving and diverse community that offers visitors a wide range of exciting activities and attractions. From exploring culturally important landmarks to hiking and biking in beautiful natural areas, Far Northwest Side has something for everyone. This neighborhood is located in Chicagoland, Illinois, which is in the Midwest region of the United States of America, in North America.

List of Places to Visit in Far Northwest Side

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Far Northwest Side.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Edison Park is a charming and historic neighborhood in Far Northwest Side that is famous for its stunning architecture, vintage boutiques, and buzzing nightlife.
  • Niles Historical Society and Museum is a fascinating museum that gives visitors an insight into the history and culture of the area with exhibits ranging from American Indian artifacts to classic toys.
  • Norwood Park is a historic neighborhood adorned with unique architecture, quaint streets, antiques shops, parks, and numerous sports fields.

Natural Attractions of Far Northwest Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Forest Preserve Trail is a 15-mile nature trail that meanders through beautiful greenery, wildflowers, and abundant animal life.
  • LaBagh Woods is a 260-acre forest preserve with plenty of hiking trails, bike paths, and opportunities to observe wildlife.
  • Norwood Park Prairie is a small and tranquil nature preserve that features splendid tallgrass prairie and native oak savanna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far Northwest Side

Far Northwest Side provides several fantastic trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts, including the Caldwell Woods Trail, North Branch Trail, and Des Plaines Bike Trail.

Best Things to Do in Far Northwest Side: Activities and Tours

Far Northwest Side offers plenty of opportunities for adventure and sightseeing. Some of the best things to do in the area include:

  • Take a Culinary Tour of Best Restaurants - Have a quick break with the freshly made pastries or have a hearty breakfast or lunch with local cuisine at the cafés and restaurants of Edison Park.
  • Explore the History of Far Northwest Side with a Walking Tour – Discover the historic neighborhoods, landmarks, and buildings in Far Northwest Side.
  • Enjoy Indoor Kart Racing - Experience the thrills of go-kart racing at Niles’ K1 Speed.

Food and Drink Scene of Far Northwest Side

Far Northwest Side prides itself on its diverse cuisines, including some regional specialties. A few dishes not to miss from Far Northwest Side:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vienna Beef Hot Dogs - Grab the famous local hot dog, typically topped with tomatoes, pickles, onions, relish, and mustard at Superdawg Drive-in.
  • Chocolate Cake Shake - Treat yourself with the decadent Chocolate Cake Shake at Portillo’s.
  • Italian Beef Sandwich - Savor the famous Italian beef sandwiches dipped in au jus at Buona.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer - A Chicago delicacy that is some of the best beer you can get in the United States.
  • Chicago Cocktail - A classic drink composed of gin, lime juice, and green Chartreuse, that was invented during Chicago’s 1893 World’s Fair.

Where to Stay in Far Northwest Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Far Northwest Side has a variety of accommodation options for visitors:

  • The Hilton Chicago/O’Hare Airport - Offers guests with proximity to the airport and excellent amenities like an indoor pool and a fitness center.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Chicago North Shore – Niles - Offers guests with classic Midwestern charm, free Wi-Fi, and a hot breakfast buffet every morning.

What to Buy in Far Northwest Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Far Northwest Side has plenty of opportunities to shop for unique souvenirs and keepsakes to take home with you. Some ideas include:

  • Designer Clothes and Accessories - Shop designer wear and the latest trends at Harlem Irving Plaza Shopping Center’s 140 stores.
  • Produce and Groceries - Visit Chicago Market, a store that is packed with local produce, meats, and dairy products, together with prepared meals and other gourmet goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Far Northwest Side

Far Northwest Side is a culturally diverse and friendly neighborhood that welcomes visitors with open arms. It is customary to greet people with a smile and small talk, and tipping 15-20% is customary in most restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Far Northwest Side

Far Northwest Side is an all-year-round destination. Spring and autumn months bring beautiful colors, while summer is the most favorable season, with longer days and warmer temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far Northwest Side

Do:

  • Clean-up After Yourself - Keep the region clean, and discard garbage in designated locations.
  • Respect People Around You - Far Northwest Side is a culturally diverse community, and accommodating to its uniqueness is a must.
  • Embrace the Surrounding Environment - There is plenty of beautiful scenery to see and parts of the natural world around Far Northwest Side that make for a peaceful experience for everyone.

Don’t:

  • Litter or Vandalize - It is strictly forbidden, and the city imposes heavy fines for those caught littering or leaving behind big messes.
  • Be Disrespectful to Local Culture or Customs - Always keep an open, accepting mindset when experiencing new cultures and customs.
  • Leave Valuables Unattended - Remember to keep your valuable belongings close to you in crowded and popular tourist areas.

Safety and Health

The Far Northwest Side is considered safe, but tourists should take standard safety measures such as locking car doors and not leaving personal belongings visible. Travelers should make note of their surroundings and avoid suspicious looking individuals. In case of any medical emergency that may arise, it is advised to contact your embassy or call the 911 emergency hotline.

Getting to Far Northwest Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to reach Far Northwest Side is by flying into Chicago O’Hare International Airport, which is 10 minutes away from O’Hare International terminal station, from where you can take the train into the city. Multiple taxi and rideshare options are available at airports and train stations as well.

Airports Near Far Northwest Side

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport - The airport is located 5 miles from Far Northwest Side.

Bus Stations Near Far Northwest Side

  • Jefferson Park Transit Center – Buses that go in and around the region stop at this station.

Other Options

  • Rideshare Services - Lyft and Uber are popular modes of transportation in Far Northwest Side and rides to the city from the airport can range from $25 to $60.
  • Subway and Rail Travel - The CTA Blue line subway train offers affordable public transport from Chicago Downtown and O’Hare airport to the Far Northwest Side.

How to Get Around in Far Northwest Side

Far Northwest Side is best explored by car. It’s easier to reach every destination without wasting time in public transport. However, CTA is the most common mode of transport for short journeys in the region.

Where to Go Next from Far Northwest Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re interested in exploring nearby areas, check out:

  • Downtown Chicago - Visit the world-famous landmarks such as the Navy Pier and Millennium Park.
  • Lincoln Park Zoo - See indoor and outdoor exhibits showcasing all kinds of animals.
  • Evanston - Enjoy the lakefront beaches or have a stroll in the downtown area.

Conclusion

Far Northwest Side is the perfect destination for travelers looking for adventure, natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. With all the activities and attractions mentioned in this guide, we hope it will help you plan the perfect trip to Far Northwest Side.
