Introduction to Near South

Near South is a vibrant neighborhood located in the heart of the Windy City, Chicago. Once a commercial and industrial area, it has transformed into a cultural and recreational hub. Situated on the south side of downtown Chicago, this diverse neighborhood is rich in history and culture, providing tourists with a unique experience during their visit to the city.

Near South is a part of the Chicagoland region, located in Illinois, a state in the Midwest region of the United States of America, in North America. With its convenient location and varied attractions, Near South is a perfect destination for those who want to explore Chicago beyond its traditional tourist sites.

List of Places to Visit in Near South

There is an abundance of things to see and do in Near South. Here’s a list of places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Field Museum of Natural History

Located at 1400 S Lake Shore Dr, the Field Museum of Natural History is one of the largest natural history museums in the world. The museum boasts an impressive collection of artifacts and specimens, including Sue, the largest and most complete T. Rex dinosaur fossil, as well as mesmerizing exhibitions on ancient Egypt and the Pacific Islands.

Adler Planetarium

Located at 1300 S Lake Shore Dr, the Adler Planetarium is a popular scientific and cultural attraction for all ages. Its stunning sky shows, virtual reality experiences, and interactive exhibits will take you on an unforgettable journey through the universe and provide you with a deeper understanding of space and our place in it.

Chinatown

Located on the Near South Side of Chicago, Chinatown offers a unique cultural experience with its rich Chinese heritage, vibrant architecture, and delicious cuisine. Take a stroll through the ChiNatown Square and explore the area’s local shops and eateries.

Buckingham Fountain

Located at 500 S Columbus Dr, the Buckingham Fountain is an iconic landmark in the city, famous for its stunning architecture and water displays that attract both locals and tourists alike.

Natural Attractions of Near South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Grant Park

Located between Michigan Avenue and Lake Michigan, Grant Park is the city’s largest park, boasting 319 acres of green space, beautiful gardens, stunning artwork, and stunning views of Lake Michigan. Take a stroll or rent a bike to explore this urban oasis.

Northerly Island

Northerly Island is a man-made peninsula located on the shores of Lake Michigan. It features beautiful nature trails, bird watching, and stunning views of the city skyline. It’s also home to the Adler Planetarium and the former site of the Meigs Field airport.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Near South

Lakefront Trail

The Lakefront Trail offers an excellent opportunity to enjoy the beautiful scenery of Lake Michigan while getting some exercise. The trail spans 18 miles, stretching from Ardmore Street on the north side to 71st Street on the south side.

Burnham Greenway Trail

The Burnham Greenway Trail spans over 15 miles from Burnham to Lynwood. As you explore the trail, you will come across beautiful nature reserves and parks.

Best Things to Do in Near South: Activities and Tours

Segway Tours

Explore Chicago on a Segway and experience the best that Near South has to offer. Segway tours take you past many significant attractions in the area such as Soldier Field, the Chicago Harbor and along the Lakefront.

Architecture Boat Tours

Take a relaxing boat tour of the Chicago River and enjoy the stunning skyline views, while learning about the city’s rich architectural history.

Art Institute of Chicago

Visit one of the oldest and largest art museums in the United States, located at 111 S Michigan Ave. The Art Institute of Chicago is home to thousands of works of art from around the world, making it an excellent destination for art lovers of all ages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Near South

Day 1: Grant Park, the Buckingham Fountain, and Art Institute of Chicago. Day 2: Field Museum of Natural History and Segway Tours. Day 3: Chinatown and nearby sushi bars and dim sum restaurants. Day 4: Northerly Island and the Adler Planetarium Day 5: Lakefront Trail and Burnham Greenway Trail Day 6: Shopping at the Southgate Market and nearby shops. Day 7: Chicago Harbor and nightlife in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Near South

Must-Try Dishes

Chicago-style Pizza

Chicago-style pizza is a true classic and is a must-try for anyone visiting the city. The famous deep-dish pizza served in Malnati’s and Giordano’s restaurants can’t be missed.

Italian Beef Sandwich

Another Chicago classic, the Italian beef sandwich is a staple in Near South and can be found at Al’s Beef.

Chicago-style Hot Dog

You can’t come to Chicago without trying the famous Chicago-style hot dog, which consists of an all-beef hot dog, mustard, relish, onions, tomato slices, and a pickle spear served on a steamed poppy seed bun.

Local Beverages

Goose Island Beer

Goose Island Beer Company is a craft brewery that offers a variety of beers brewed on-site, including some unique brewery-specific creations.

Malort - A Chicago staple

Malort, a distinctly bitter and unusual spirit, is a Chicago staple that is not for the faint of heart.

Where to Stay in Near South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hilton Chicago

Located at 720 S. Michigan Avenue, the Hilton Chicago is a luxurious hotel with beautiful views of Grant Park and Lake Michigan.

The Wheeler Mansion

Located at 2020 S. Calumet Avenue, the Wheeler Mansion is a historic boutique hotel that offers comfortable and chic accommodations.

Chicago Getaway Hostel

Located at 616 West Arlington Place, Chicago Getaway Hostel is a cozy hostel that offers budget-friendly accommodation.

What to Buy in Near South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sports Gear

Near South is home to the iconic Soldier Field, and you can find all kinds of sports gear shops in the area.

Socks

Chicago’s own Stance Socks have a wide range of designs sold in shops around the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Near South

The Near South community is diverse, welcoming, and embracing of various cultures. Whether you’re exploring the area’s historical landmarks or trying out local cuisines, the Near South community invites you to join them and experience the city’s unique character.

Best Time to Visit Near South

The best time to visit Near South is during the spring and autumn months (March to May and September to November). During these times, temperatures are mild, and crowds are smaller than during peak tourist season in the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Near South

Do:

  • Do visit the Field Museum of Natural History, Adler Planetarium, and the Art Institute of Chicago.
  • Do try the Italian beef sandwich, pizza, and Malort.
  • Do rent a bike and explore the Lakefront Trail.
  • Do embrace the local culture and learn about the city’s history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drive in the city if possible – public transportation or walking will save time and money.
  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable walking shoes.
  • Don’t miss visiting Chinatown for an authentic cultural experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Near South

Near South is generally safe, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions like keeping your belongings close and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Near South

Visitors should take precautions to avoid the spread of COVID-19.

Getting to Near South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Near South

Midway International Airport is the closest airport to Near South, located just 10 miles away. Chicago O’Hare International Airport is located 17 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Near South

Greyhound Bus Station, Megabus Terminal, and Union Station are all located within a few miles of Near South.

Other Options

There are many car rental options, bike rentals, and taxi services in Near South.

How to Get Around in Near South

Public transportation in Near South is the best and most efficient way to travel around the area. The Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) provides bus and train services throughout the city.

Conclusion

Near South has something to offer for everyone: history, culture, beautiful scenery, and an abundance of activities and delicious food to enjoy. Explore the area’s diverse neighborhoods, take a stroll through a park, and immerse yourself in the local culture. With its fantastic location, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Chicago.
