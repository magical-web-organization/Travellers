Introduction to North Lincoln

North Lincoln is a charming neighborhood located in the North Side of Chicago, Illinois, offering a diverse range of attractions and activities for all types of travelers. Whether you are an adventurer, a foodie, a history buff, a culture seeker, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, North Lincoln has everything you need to make your trip unforgettable. From historical landmarks and museums to scenic hikes and outdoor adventures, here’s everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to North Lincoln.

List of Places to Visit in North Lincoln

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lincoln Park Zoo: One of the oldest zoos in the US, Lincoln Park Zoo is home to over 200 species of animals and offers free admission for visitors.
  • Peggy Notebaert Nature Museum: A natural history museum featuring exhibits on the ecology and wildlife of the Great Lakes and the Midwest region.
  • Edgar Miller Murals: A collection of colorful murals depicting Chicago history and culture, located in the Charnley-Persky House.
  • Chicago History Museum: A museum showcasing the history and culture of Chicago, including exhibits on the Great Chicago Fire, the city’s architecture, and more.

Natural Attractions of North Lincoln: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • North Branch Trail: A 20-mile trail perfect for hiking, biking, and birdwatching, offering scenic views of the Chicago River and the surrounding forest.
  • Lincoln Park Conservatory: A lush greenhouse featuring tropical and subtropical plants, and a popular spot for a leisurely stroll.
  • North Avenue Beach: A popular sandy beach on the shore of Lake Michigan, offering stunning views of the Chicago skyline and ample space for sunbathing, swimming, and other water activities.
  • Alfred Caldwell Lily Pool: A hidden gem located in the heart of Lincoln Park, this tranquil sanctuary features a pond, waterfalls, and a variety of aquatic plants and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Lincoln

North Lincoln offers several scenic trails and routes for hikers and bikers alike. Among them are:

  • North Branch Trail: A 20-mile trail stretching from the Chicago Botanic Garden in the north to the Chicago River in the south.
  • Green Bay Trail: A 9-mile trail connecting several North Shore suburbs, including Kenilworth, Winnetka, Glencoe, and Highland Park.
  • Lakefront Trail: A 18-mile scenic trail running parallel to Lake Michigan, offering stunning views of the lake and the Chicago skyline.

Best Things to Do in North Lincoln: Activities and Tours

North Lincoln offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests, including:

  • Chicago Architecture Foundation River Cruise: A leisurely boat tour along the Chicago River, showcasing the city’s iconic architecture and landmarks.
  • Chicago Food Planet Tours: A walking food tour exploring the city’s diverse culinary scene, including deep-dish pizza, hot dogs, and other local specialties.
  • Chicago Helicopter Experience: A thrilling helicopter tour of the Chicago skyline, offering bird’s-eye views of the city’s iconic landmarks and skyscrapers.
  • Second City Comedy Club: A world-famous improvisational comedy club, where many of the greatest comedians of all time got their start.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Lincoln

Food and Drink Scene of North Lincoln

Must-Try Dishes

Chicago is famous for its deep-dish pizza, hot dogs, and Italian beef sandwiches, and North Lincoln has plenty of options for visitors looking to try these local specialties. Among the best places to get a taste of Chicago-style pizza are Lou Malnati’s, Pequod’s Pizza, and Gino’s East, while Portillo’s, Superdawg, and the Wiener’s Circle are popular spots for hot dogs and Italian beef sandwiches.

Local Beverages

Chicago is also known for its craft beer scene, and North Lincoln has several breweries and taprooms worth a visit. Among them are Half Acre Beer Company, Metropolitan Brewing, and Empirical Brewery. For wine lovers, Vin Chicago and Enolo Wine Café offer an excellent selection of local and international wines, while coffee aficionados should not miss a visit to Intelligentsia Coffee and Tea, one of the most famous coffee roasters in the US.

Where to Stay in North Lincoln: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Lincoln offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Among the best places to stay in North Lincoln are:

  • The Willows Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Lincoln Park, offering stylish rooms and upscale amenities.
  • Chicago Getaway Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located near DePaul University, offering dormitory-style rooms as well as private rooms and suites.
  • Alden Lincoln Park: A senior living community with furnished apartments available for short-term stays, featuring a rooftop terrace and fitness center.

What to Buy in North Lincoln: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Lincoln has several stores and shops selling unique and locally-made products, perfect for souvenirs or gifts. Among the best places to shop in North Lincoln are:

  • Lincoln Park Farmers Market: A weekly outdoor market selling fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, baked goods, and other locally-made products.
  • Uncle Dan’s: A camping and outdoor gear store selling high-quality equipment and gear for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Chicago Architecture Foundation Shop: A gift shop selling books, home decor, and other items related to Chicago architecture and design.

Local Culture and Customs of North Lincoln

Chicagoans are generally friendly and open-minded towards visitors, and appreciate respect for local customs and sensitivity towards issues that may arise during travel. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Tipping is expected in most service industries, including restaurants, cafes, and bars.
  • Casual attire is widely accepted in most places, although dress codes may apply in some more upscale establishments.
  • Smoking is prohibited in most public places, including restaurants, bars, and public transit.

Best Time to Visit North Lincoln

The best time to visit North Lincoln is during the spring (April-June) and fall (September-November) months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid, with larger crowds and higher prices. Winter (December-February) can be cold and snowy, although some visitors enjoy the city’s winter festivities, such as ice skating and holiday markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Lincoln

Do:

  • Do explore the city’s diverse neighborhoods and cultural scenes, such as Pilsen, Hyde Park, and Wicker Park.
  • Do take advantage of the city’s public transportation system, which includes buses, trains, and bike-sharing programs.
  • Do try the local food and drink specialties, such as deep-dish pizza, hot dogs, and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wander too far off the beaten path, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Don’t underestimate the traffic and parking challenges in downtown Chicago, consider taking public transportation or ridesharing options.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather, especially during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Lincoln

North Lincoln is a relatively safe neighborhood, although visitors should take certain precautions while traveling. These include:

  • Avoiding walking alone at night or in secluded areas.
  • Keeping valuables out of sight and securing them in a safe place, such as a locker or hotel room.
  • Staying aware of your surroundings and avoiding distracted behavior, such as texting while walking.

Health in North Lincoln

North Lincoln has several hospitals and medical facilities in the vicinity, including Advocate Illinois Masonic Medical Center, Presence Saint Joseph Hospital, and Swedish Hospital.

Getting to North Lincoln: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Lincoln is easily accessible by several transportation options, including:

Airports Near North Lincoln

  • O’Hare International Airport (ORD): Located approximately 18 miles from North Lincoln, O’Hare is one of the busiest airports in the world, serving over 83 million passengers per year.
  • Midway International Airport (MDW): Located approximately 12 miles from North Lincoln, Midway is a smaller airport with a focus on low-cost carriers and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near North Lincoln

  • Union Station: A major transportation hub located in downtown Chicago, offering bus, train, and subway service.
  • Greyhound Station: A bus station located in the South Loop neighborhood, offering bus service to various destinations in the US and Canada.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: A nationwide rail service with several stations in Chicago, including Union Station and the Ogilvie Transportation Center.
  • Chicago Transit Authority (CTA): The city’s public transportation system, consisting of buses and trains, including the famous “L” trains that run above ground and through downtown Chicago.

How to Get Around in North Lincoln

North Lincoln is a highly walkable and bike-friendly neighborhood, with several bike-sharing programs and rental shops available for visitors. The city’s public transportation system is also an easy and affordable way to get around, with buses and trains running frequently to various parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from North Lincoln: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Lincoln is a great starting point to explore other areas and destinations in the Chicago area, such as:

  • Downtown Chicago: Just a few miles south of North Lincoln, downtown Chicago offers a wealth of attractions, including the Magnificent Mile shopping district, the Willis Tower, and the Art Institute of Chicago.
  • Wrigleyville: Located just west of North Lincoln, Wrigleyville is home to Wrigley Field, the historic ballpark of the Chicago Cubs, as well as several bars, restaurants, and shops.
  • Evanston: Located approximately 10 miles north of North Lincoln, Evanston is a charming suburb known for its beaches, parks, and historic downtown district.

Conclusion

North Lincoln is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Chicago. With its rich history, vibrant cultural scene, natural beauty, and plethora of attractions and activities, North Lincoln has something for everyone. From hiking and biking along the scenic trails to sampling the city’s famous deep-dish pizza, visitors to North Lincoln are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this charming neighborhood.
