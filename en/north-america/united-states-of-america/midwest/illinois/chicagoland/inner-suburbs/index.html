Hiking/Biking Routes of Inner Suburbs

Inner Suburbs offers a variety of hiking and biking routes, including the Illinois Prairie Path and the Fox River Trail.

Illinois Prairie Path

The Illinois Prairie Path is a 61-mile hiking and biking trail that stretches from Wheaton to Aurora. The trail passes through scenic landscapes, historic towns, and natural preserves. It offers multiple access points and is suitable for all skill levels.

Fox River Trail

The Fox River Trail is a 43-mile trail that runs along the Fox River from Algonquin to Aurora. The trail passes through forests, parks, and historic downtown areas. It is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Inner Suburbs: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of the historic homes in Oak Park
  • Attend a show or concert at the Arcada Theatre in St. Charles
  • Visit the Naper Settlement in Naperville to learn about local history
  • Take a tasting tour of the local breweries and wineries
  • Go shopping at Oakbrook Center in Oak Brook or Yorktown Center in Lombard

Week-Long Itinerary for Inner Suburbs

Day 1: Explore the Frank Lloyd Wright Home and Studio and Ernest Hemingway Birthplace Museum in Oak Park.

Day 2: Take a trip to the Brookfield Zoo and then head to Elmhurst for dinner at one of the local restaurants.

Day 3: Go hiking or biking on the Illinois Prairie Path, followed by a visit to the DuPage County Historical Museum in Wheaton.

Day 4: Visit the Graue Mill and Museum in Oak Brook before heading to Cantigny Park and First Division Museum in Wheaton.

Day 5: Spend the day at the Morton Arboretum in Lisle.

Day 6: Take a tasting tour of the local breweries and wineries.

Day 7: Go shopping at Oakbrook Center in Oak Brook or Yorktown Center in Lombard.
