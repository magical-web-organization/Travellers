Introduction to Wilmette

Wilmette is a charming village located in the North Shore of Chicago, Illinois. With a population of just over 27,000, it’s a small town with a big personality. Wilmette is a perfect place for those who want to experience a blend of natural beauty and culture. This guide will take you through the must-see places and things to do in Wilmette, along with where to stay and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Wilmette

Discover the best sightseeing spots and popular landmarks in Wilmette. Here are the top destinations for visitors to Wilmette.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wilmette is a rich melting pot of culture and history. Here are some important cultural and historical places worth exploring:

  • Baha’i House of Worship: This architectural marvel is a must-visit attraction in Wilmette. It’s the oldest of the eight Baha’i Houses of Worship in the world and a place for prayer and meditation.
  • Gillson Beach: This beach is a popular spot for visitors to enjoy the sun, sand and water.Other activities like paddling and sailing are also available.
  • Wilmette Historical Museum: The Wilmette Historical Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Wilmette through various stories and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Wilmette: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wilmette is known for the beautiful natural attractions it offers. Here are some of the most-loved natural spots for visitors.

  • Gillson Park: Gillson Park is a vast parkland with a beach, swimming pool, playgrounds, picnic areas, and sports facilities. The park is a perfect spot to spend the day with family and friends.
  • Wilmette Harbor: The harbor is a picturesque location for visitors to enjoy fishing, take a boat tour or simply relax by the water.
  • Baha’i Temple Grounds: The gardens surrounding the Baha’i Temple are peaceful and filled with blooming flowers and tranquil fountains. The temple grounds are open year-round to visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilmette

Wilmette offers some great hiking and biking routes in its beautiful, natural landscapes. Here are some of the popular routes available:

  • Green Bay Trail: The Green Bay Trail is a 9-mile long hiking and biking trail that runs from Wilmette to Highland Park. The trail runs along the Green Bay Road and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Wilmette Harbor path: The harbor’s paved walking path is a great spot to bike along as it takes you through beautiful views of Lake Michigan and past some historic homes.
  • **West Park Loop Trail **: A 1.2-mile loop trail provides a scenic walk around West Park.

Best Things to Do in Wilmette: Activities and Tours

Wilmette is filled with numerous activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the most exciting things to do in Wilmette:

  • The Wilmette Theatre: The Wilmette Theatre is an intimate venue that often hosts local plays, films, and live performances. It’s a perfect spot for entertainment.
  • Home Tours: A walk through the historic homes in the area is a beautiful way to appreciate the architecture and charm of Wilmette.
  • Kayak and Canoe Rentals: Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes to paddle around Gillson Beach and explore the surrounding area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilmette

Wilmette has plenty of attractions to see and experience. Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your visit to Wilmette:

  • Day 1: Visit the Baha’i Temple and its beautiful gardens.
  • Day 2: Explore the Wilmette Historical Museum and learn about the village’s history.
  • Day 3: Take a bike ride on the Green Bay Trail and visit Ravinia Festival.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at Gillson Beach and enjoy the sun and water.
  • Day 5: Take a scenic walk by Wilmette Harbor and check out the historic homes.
  • Day 6: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the waters of Gillson Beach.
  • Day 7: Enjoy some live entertainment at the Wilmette Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of Wilmette

Wilmette has a diverse culinary scene that will excite foodies of all types. Here is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages to sip on.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bowls of Pho: China Chef is a great place to try some delicious pho with a bowl of flavorful broth accompanied by meat, noodles, and vegetables.
  • Chicago-style Pizza: Lou Malnati’s on North Shore serves some of the most delicious Chicago-style deep-dish pizzas in Wilmette.
  • Ribs: For mouth-watering BBQ ribs, look no further than Homer’s American Bar and Grill.

Local Beverages

  • Draft beer: Find top-rated beers at Wilmette’s Village Inn and Pizzeria.
  • Fine wine: The Wilmette Wine Cellar has a wide variety of fine wines from all regions.

Where to Stay in Wilmette: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wilmette has a variety of lodging options to suit different tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Wilmette:

  • Hampton Inn and Suites Chicago-North Shore: This hotel has an excellent location and provides guests with breakfast, free parking, and Wi-Fi.
  • HI Chicago Hostel: Situated at Rogers Park, provides budget-friendly rates with comfortable rooms and great hospitality.
  • The Wilmette Inn: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Wilmette village. The Inn holds seven guestrooms that give guests a home-away-from-home experience.

What to Buy in Wilmette: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take back some souvenirs that represent the charm and character of Wilmette. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy in Wilmette:

  • Illustrated Wilmette Map: Get a beautiful map of Wilmette that features landmarks and top destinations.
  • Baha’i Temple Posters: Take back a beautiful poster that showcases the Baha’I Temple.
  • Handmade Crafts: The Wilmette Artisans Market features handmade arts and crafts from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilmette

Wilmette has a diverse and welcoming culture, which is shaped by its architecture, art, music, and food. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. The cultural venues often have dress codes that must be adhered to.

Best Time to Visit Wilmette

The best time to visit Wilmette is between June and August when the climate is mild, and there is plenty of sunshine for outdoor activities. May and September also offer beautiful, mild weather, making them great times to explore the outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilmette

Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit to Wilmette:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the cultural attractions and landmarks in Wilmette.
  • Take advantage of the beautiful natural areas.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beach or any other public areas.
  • Park in No parking zones along Green Bay Road
  • Ignore the dress code for cultural attractions and events.

Safety and Health

Wilmette is known to be a safe destination for visitors. Here are a few things to keep in mind to ensure your safety and health during your trip.

Safety in Wilmette

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings during your visit.
  • Always keep your valuables on you and never leave them unattended in public areas.

Health in Wilmette

  • If you have any health concerns, visit the nearby hospitals like North Shore University Health System (10 miles from Wilmette) and Evanston Hospital (5 miles from Wilmette).
  • Stay hydrated and use sunblock when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Wilmette: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wilmette is well-connected to neighboring cities and towns. Here are the different options available to reach Wilmette:

  • Airports Near Wilmette
    • O’Hare International Airport (14 miles away).
    • Chicago Midway International Airport (27 miles away)
  • Bus Stations Near Wilmette
    • Wilmette is well connected by bus routes such as Pace buses and Metra commuter rail
  • Other Options
    • Visitors can also take cabs, book private cars or rental cars.

How to Get Around in Wilmette

To get around in Wilmette, visitors can use public transportation such as the Pace bus system, CTA and trains. Taxis and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Wilmette: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wilmette is surrounded by several other exciting destinations to travel to. Here are some places to consider visiting after exploring Wilmette.

  • Evanston: Evanston (5 miles from Wilmette) offers a vibrant downtown with a mix of restaurants, coffee shops, and boutique shopping.
  • Chicago: Chicago (14 miles from Wilmette) is a must-visit city known for its iconic architecture, museums, and entertainment venues.
  • Glenview: Glenview (6 miles from Wilmette) is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with hiking trails and parks.

Conclusion

Wilmette is a hidden gem that’s worth exploring, with its beautiful natural attractions, fascinating cultural landmarks, and attractive food scene. This guide is designed to help visitors make the most of their time in Wilmette, with information on where to stay, what to eat, and unique experiences to have in the village.
