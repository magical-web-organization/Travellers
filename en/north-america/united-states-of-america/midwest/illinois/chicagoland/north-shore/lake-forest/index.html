Introduction to Lake Forest

Nestled in the North Shore area of Chicagoland, in Illinois, Lake Forest is a beautiful and historic community that is known for its sprawling parks, lush landscapes, and stately mansions and gardens. The city sits on the shores of Lake Michigan and is located just 30 miles north of downtown Chicago. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, Lake Forest offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Forest

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history, there are a number of places in Lake Forest that offer insights into the city’s past. The Ragdale Foundation is a historic artists’ residency that has housed artists, writers, and composers for over 40 years. Lake Forest College is a popular destination for those interested in architecture, as it features a Gothic Revival-style campus designed by famed Chicago architects Holabird & Roche. Other must-see attractions include the Lake Forest-Lake Bluff Historical Society and the Gorton Community Center.

Natural Attractions of Lake Forest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty of things to do in Lake Forest. The Lake Forest Open Lands Association offers over 800 acres of natural areas that are perfect for hiking, birdwatching, and picnicking. The Deerpath Golf Course provides an 18-hole course with stunning views of the lake, while the Lake Forest Beach is a great place to relax and enjoy the waves. Don’t miss out on the Lake Forest Market Square, which is the heart of the city and offers a number of unique shopping and dining options.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Forest

Biking and hiking are two of the most popular outdoor activities in Lake Forest. The Middlefork Savanna is a great place to go for a hike, as it features a four-mile loop that winds through a rare oak savanna ecosystem. The North Shore Bike Path runs through Lake Forest and connects to other trails in the area. If you’re looking for a more challenging hike, check out the Skokie River Nature Preserve Trail, which features 10 miles of hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Lake Forest: Activities and Tours

While there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in Lake Forest, there are also a number of activities and tours that are perfect for visitors of all ages. Take a tour of the Elawa Farm, which is a working farm that dates back to the early 1900s. If you’re interested in art, take a class at The Art Center Highland Park or visit the Brushwood Center at Ryerson Woods, which is a nature-inspired art exhibit that features works from local and regional artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Forest

Day 1: Visit the Lake Forest Beach and take a walk around the Market Square.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Lake Forest Open Lands Association and the Middlefork Savanna.

Day 3: Take a tour of the historic Ragdale Foundation and spend the afternoon browsing the shops and galleries of the Gorton Community Center.

Day 4: Play a round of golf at the scenic Deerpath Golf Course.

Day 5: Take a class or visit the galleries at The Art Center Highland Park.

Day 6: Hike the Skokie River Nature Preserve Trail and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 7: Spend the day at the Elawa Farm and learn about local agriculture.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Forest

Must-Try Dishes

Lake Forest boasts a diverse culinary scene that is sure to delight any foodie. For breakfast, try the famous Peach Cobbler Waffle at Egg Harbor Cafe or enjoy fresh bagels and lox at NY Bagel & Bialy. Lunch options include mouth-watering burgers at Five Guys or fresh sushi at Hatsuhana. For dinner, head to Tommy’s Red Hots for a classic Chicago-style hot dog or try the Italian beef sandwich at Portillo’s.

Local Beverages

While in Lake Forest, be sure to sample some of the local beers and wines. Lake Bluff Brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are brewed on-site, while The Lantern, Lake Forest’s oldest bar, serves up classic cocktails and a wide selection of local wines.

Where to Stay in Lake Forest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a number of great places to stay while visiting Lake Forest. The Deer Path Inn is a historic hotel that features stunning architecture and classic decor. For a more contemporary feel, check out The Westin Chicago North Shore, which offers modern amenities and a convenient location. The Central Hotel and Suites is another popular choice, as it provides spacious rooms and suites at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Lake Forest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some of Lake Forest’s local products as souvenirs. Visit LF Juice for refreshing cold-pressed juices made with locally sourced ingredients. The Olive Tap offers gourmet olive oils and vinegars that are perfect for cooking. Market House on the Square is a unique specialty store that sells handmade soaps, local honey, and other artisanal goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Forest

Lake Forest is a friendly, welcoming community that values its traditions and natural beauty. Be sure to show respect for the local culture by dressing appropriately and observing local customs.

Best Time to Visit Lake Forest

The best time to visit Lake Forest is during the summer months, from June through August. The weather is mild and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Fall is another great time to visit, as the leaves change into a riot of colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Forest

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors and natural beauty of Lake Forest
  • Support local businesses and try the local food and drink
  • Participate in community events and festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or natural areas
  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Be afraid to ask for directions or recommendations

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Forest

Lake Forest is a relatively safe community, with low crime rates and a strong police presence. Still, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and windows and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lake Forest

Lake Forest has a number of high-quality medical facilities, including the Northwestern Lake Forest Hospital and the Lake Forest Health and Fitness Center. If you need medical attention while visiting the area, you can rest assured that you will receive top-notch care.

Getting to Lake Forest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Forest

Lake Forest is located just 30 miles north of Chicago, which means that visitors can fly into either Chicago O’Hare International Airport or Chicago Midway International Airport. Both airports offer a number of transportation options for getting to Lake Forest, including taxi services, rental cars, and public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Lake Forest

The Lake Forest Metra Station is a popular destination for those arriving by bus, as it connects to the city’s major train stations and bus terminals. Visitors can take a taxi from the station to their desired destination, or they can rent a car from one of the local rental agencies.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Lake Forest, there are a number of major highways that pass through the area, including Interstate 94 and Route 41. Visitors can also take advantage of the city’s public transportation options, such as the Pace Bus Service and the Metra Train System.

How to Get Around in Lake Forest

Visitors can easily get around Lake Forest via car, bike, or public transportation. The city offers a number of bike paths and designated lanes that are perfect for cyclists, while those who prefer to drive can take advantage of the city’s numerous parking options. Public transportation is also available through the Pace Bus Service and the Metra Train System.

Where to Go Next from Lake Forest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding areas, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. The city of Highland Park is just a short drive away and offers a number of cultural attractions and outdoor activities. The Chicago Botanic Garden is a great option for those interested in gardens and horticulture, while the city of Chicago is just a quick train ride away.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, Lake Forest offers a wealth of opportunities for visitors. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and art galleries, there is something for everyone in this beautiful and historic community.
