Introduction to Waukegan

Located in the Far North Suburbs of Chicagoland, Waukegan is a picturesque city in Illinois that offers a perfect blend of nature and urbanization. It is known for its beautiful beaches, stunning parks and trails, and unique culture that’s deeply rooted in local traditions. Waukegan has something for everyone, whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for a fun-filled vacation. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will highlight the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Waukegan.

List of Places to Visit in Waukegan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Waukegan History Museum - This museum brings Waukegan’s rich history to life and provides a fascinating insight into the city’s past.
  2. Jack Benny Center for the Arts - The museum showcases the life and legacy of Jack Benny, a legendary comedian from Waukegan.
  3. Genesee Theatre - Built in 1927, this theatre is a historic landmark and hosts a variety of shows and performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Waukegan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Waukegan Beach - Enjoy the sun, sand, and sea at Waukegan Beach, one of the most beautiful beaches in Illinois.
  2. Bowen Park - Explore the beautiful gardens, hiking trails, and a variety of recreational activities available at Bowen Park.
  3. Lake County Forest Preserves - With over 200 miles of hiking and biking trails, this preserve is an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.
  4. Independence Grove Forest Preserve - This popular destination has something for everyone, featuring a swimming beach, fishing, hiking, and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Waukegan

Waukegan has an abundance of parks and trails that are perfect for hiking and biking, some of which include:

  1. Robert McClory Bike Path - This 30-mile trail runs through Waukegan and other nearby communities and offers stunning views of Lake Michigan.
  2. Greenbelt Forest Preserve Trail - A three-mile loop that’s perfect for hiking and features a boardwalk that goes through a marsh.
  3. Bevier Park Loop - A gorgeous 1.2-mile trail that loops around the park and lake.

Best Things to Do in Waukegan: Activities and Tours

  1. Tour of Historic Homes - Take a guided walking tour of some of Waukegan’s historic homes.
  2. Sunset Cruise on Lake Michigan - Enjoy a romantic sunset cruise on Lake Michigan and take in the beautiful views.
  3. Dinner and Movie at the Genesee Theatre - Watch a movie or live event at the historic Genesee Theatre and then grab dinner at one of the nearby restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Waukegan

Food and Drink Scene of Waukegan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicago-style pizza - Chicago is famous for its deep-dish pizza, and Waukegan has some of the best places to try this regional delicacy.
  2. Italian beef sandwich - A local sandwich consisting of thinly sliced beef, bread, and giardiniera.
  3. Portillo’s hotdog - A Chicago classic, Portillo’s is a must-visit for their delicious hot dogs.

Local Beverages

  1. Goose Island Beer Company - This Chicago-based brewery offers a variety of craft beer and is a popular destination for beer lovers.
  2. Chicago Distilling Company - This local distillery produces high-quality whiskey, gin, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Waukegan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. SpringHill Suites by Marriott Waukegan - A modern and comfortable hotel located near many of Waukegan’s top attractions.
  2. Candlewood Suites Chicago-Waukegan - Ideal for families, this hotel features spacious suites with kitchens and a fitness center.
  3. The Waukegan House - A charming guesthouse located in downtown Waukegan with easy access to many restaurants and shops.

What to Buy in Waukegan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Artwork from the Jack Benny Center for the Arts - Browse the collection of local artwork and bring home a unique piece of Waukegan.
  2. Waukegan-made candles - A local candle maker, Waxman Candles, produces high-quality candles with unique scents.

Local Culture and Customs of Waukegan

Waukegan has a rich history and is home to several local customs and traditions. A few to keep in mind include:

  1. Polish Festival - This festival is held annually and celebrates Waukegan’s Polish heritage.
  2. Waukegan Symphony Orchestra - Enjoy some classical music and support the local orchestra, which has been a fixture in the city since 1974.

Best Time to Visit Waukegan

The best time to visit Waukegan is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm, and there are many outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Waukegan

Do:

  1. Do explore the beautiful parks and trails in Waukegan.
  2. Do try the local cuisine, especially Chicago-style pizza.
  3. Do attend a show or performance at the Genesee Theatre.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t leave valuables in your car, especially in unguarded parking areas.
  2. Don’t litter or damage the natural landscape or historical buildings.
  3. Don’t forget to carry sunscreen, especially during summer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Waukegan

Waukegan is generally a safe city. However, it’s always essential to be cautious when walking or driving, especially at night. You should also avoid leaving valuables in plain sight in your vehicle, as car break-ins can occasionally occur.

Health in Waukegan

Waukegan has access to excellent healthcare facilities, including Vista Health System and the Northshore University Health System.

Getting to Waukegan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Waukegan

  1. Chicago O’Hare International Airport - About 35 miles away from Waukegan, this airport is the closest major airport to the city.
  2. Chicago Midway International Airport - Located about 51 miles from Waukegan, this airport is another option for those flying to the region.

Bus Stations Near Waukegan

  1. Waukegan Greyhound Station - This station is located in downtown Waukegan.
  2. Gurnee Mills Bus Station - This station is located about five miles away from Waukegan.

Other Options

  1. Metra Train - Waukegan has a station for the Metra train, which provides excellent connectivity to nearby areas such as Chicago.
  2. Rental Car - Visitors who wish to explore the region on their own can rent a car at Chicago O’Hare International Airport or in downtown Waukegan.

How to Get Around in Waukegan

The best way to get around Waukegan is by car. Rental cars are available, or you can use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft. Waukegan also has a public transportation system, Pace Bus, which provides services to nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Waukegan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Waukegan’s surrounding areas offer plenty of exciting destinations to explore on a day trip. A few options include:

  1. Six Flags Great America - Located in nearby Gurnee, this amusement park offers thrilling rides, waterpark fun, and entertainment for the whole family.
  2. Chicago - A bustling metropolis with world-class museums, entertainment options, and culinary experiences.
  3. Milwaukee - Located on the shores of Lake Michigan, this city offers beautiful architecture, breweries, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Waukegan has a lot to offer, from its beautiful beaches and parks to its rich history and unique culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip with ease and experience all the best that Waukegan has to offer.
