Introduction to North Chicago

Located in the northern suburbs of Chicago, North Chicago offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and scenic beauty. From its local museums and historical landmarks to its picturesque hiking and biking trails, North Chicago has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in North Chicago.

List of Places to Visit in North Chicago

North Chicago offers visitors a variety of attractions to explore, from its culturally-rich museums and historic landmarks to its scenic parks and trails. Here are some must-visit places in North Chicago:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Great Lakes Naval Museum: Located in the Naval Station Great Lakes, the museum showcases the history of the U.S. Navy’s training center and its impact on the Great Lakes region.

  • Baha’i House of Worship: This impressive temple serves as the center of Baha’i faith in North America. The unique architecture and beautiful gardens make it worth a visit.

  • Waukegan History Museum: Housed in a historic courthouse, this museum showcases the history of the town of Waukegan, its people, and industries that helped shape it.

Natural Attractions of North Chicago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Illinois Beach State Park: Comprising the only remaining beach ridge shoreline in the state, this park offers visitors picturesque views of Lake Michigan and miles of sandy beaches to explore.

  • Greenbelt Forest Preserve: With over 2,000 acres of forest and wetlands, the preserve offers visitors a variety of outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, biking, and canoeing.

  • Ryerson Woods: As one of the largest remaining natural areas in Lake County, this forest preserve is home to a diverse range of plants and animals and offers visitors miles of scenic hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Chicago

North Chicago boasts a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer visitors a chance to explore its scenic beauty. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Des Plaines River Trail: This 31-mile trail follows the Des Plaines River, taking riders through forest preserves, wetlands, and open plains.

  • Illinois Beach State Park Trail: This 5.8-mile trail takes visitors through the park’s picturesque sand dunes and offers scenic views of Lake Michigan.

  • Green Bay Trail: This 9-mile trail runs through Lake Forest and Highland Park, offering scenic views of parks, gardens, and residential areas.

Best Things to Do in North Chicago: Activities and Tours

North Chicago offers visitors a variety of activities to enjoy, from its beautiful parks and museums to its unique tours and events. Here are some must-do activities in North Chicago:

  • Attend a Show at the Genesee Theatre: As a historic theater dating back to 1927, the Genesee offers visitors a chance to enjoy world-class performances and events.

  • Visit the North Shore Distillery: Tour the distillery and sample some of its award-winning spirits, all made on-site.

  • Explore the Local Farmers’ Markets: With several local farmers’ markets, visitors can sample the region’s freshest produce, meats, and cheese.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Chicago

For visitors looking to spend a week exploring North Chicago and its surroundings, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Great Lakes Naval Museum and explore the Naval Station Great Lakes.
2 Hike the Des Plaines River Trail and spend the afternoon at the Illinois Beach State Park.
3 Visit the Baha’i House of Worship and tour the Waukegan History Museum.
4 Rent a bike and explore the Green Bay Trail.
5 Spend the morning at the Greenbelt Forest Preserve and the afternoon at the North Shore Distillery.
6 Relax on the beach at Illinois Beach State Park and take a sunset cruise on Lake Michigan.
7 Explore local farmers’ markets and enjoy a show at the Genesee Theatre.

Food and Drink Scene of North Chicago

North Chicago offers visitors a variety of dining experiences, from local pub food and craft beer to fine dining and wine tasting. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style hot dogs: A local classic, this hot dog features yellow mustard, neon green relish, onion, tomato, peppers, pickles, and celery salt.

  • Deep-dish pizza: Another Chicago staple, this thick-crusted pizza is loaded with cheese and toppings and often baked in a cast-iron skillet.

  • Italian beef sandwiches: Made with thinly sliced roast beef and served on a long roll with peppers, onions, and giardiniera.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: With several local breweries, visitors can sample a variety of craft beers, from IPAs and stouts to lagers and ales.

  • Chicago-style cocktails: From the classic Old Fashioned to the modern craft cocktail, North Chicago’s bars offer a variety of unique drinks to try.

  • Wine tasting: With several wineries located in the surrounding area, visitors can enjoy wine tastings and tours of local vineyards.

Where to Stay in North Chicago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Chicago offers visitors a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some top options:

  • Hotel Indigo: Offering a modern and stylish atmosphere, the Hotel Indigo is located in the heart of downtown Waukegan and offers easy access to local attractions and restaurants.

  • Country Inn and Suites: With comfortable rooms and a convenient location near local highways, the Country Inn and Suites is a great option for travelers looking for affordability and convenience.

  • The Inn at Market Square: This boutique hotel is located in the historic downtown of Zion, and offers guests a quaint and charming atmosphere, along with a variety of amenities and services.

What to Buy in North Chicago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to North Chicago can bring home a variety of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Chicago-style pizza and hot dog seasonings: Bring home the flavor of Chicago with a variety of seasonings and spices used in the city’s classic dishes.

  • Handcrafted jewelry: Located in downtown Waukegan, the Arts Warehouse features a variety of unique and locally made jewelry pieces.

  • Craft beer: With several local breweries, visitors can bring home a variety of craft beers to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of North Chicago

North Chicago has a diverse culture and history, with influences from Native American tribes, European settlers, and African American communities. Visitors can experience this rich heritage through the city’s local museums and historical landmarks.

Best Time to Visit North Chicago

North Chicago’s climate is humid continental, with warm summers and cold winters. The best time to visit is during the summer months (June-August), when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Chicago

Do:

  • Take advantage of the city’s parks and trails: North Chicago is home to several beautiful parks and trails, perfect for hiking, biking, and outdoor activities.

  • Sample some of the city’s local cuisine: From classic dishes like hot dogs and pizza to craft beer and cocktails, North Chicago has a unique and diverse food and drink scene worth exploring.

  • Visit the local museums and landmarks: North Chicago offers visitors a chance to learn about the city’s rich history and cultural heritage through its local museums and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables in your car: Like any major city, North Chicago has its share of crime. Visitors should be cautious and avoid leaving valuables in their cars.

  • Litter in public areas: North Chicago takes pride in its clean and beautiful parks and trails. Visitors should make an effort to keep these areas clean and litter-free.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Chicago

North Chicago is generally safe, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling in any major city. Avoid walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas, and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in North Chicago

North Chicago has several hospitals and medical facilities, including the Naval Health Clinic Great Lakes. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and any necessary vaccinations before traveling to the city.

Getting to North Chicago: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Chicago is located approximately 40 miles north of Chicago and is easily accessible by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near North Chicago

The closest airport to North Chicago is O’Hare International Airport (ORD), located approximately 24 miles away. Midway International Airport (MDW) is located approximately 46 miles away.

Bus Stations Near North Chicago

The closest bus station to North Chicago is the Waukegan Metra Station, located approximately 3 miles away. The station offers service to and from downtown Chicago and other local areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach North Chicago by car via several major highways, including I-94 and US-41. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in North Chicago

North Chicago is easily navigable by car or bike. Visitors can also travel via public transportation, such as the Metra rail system, which offers service to and from downtown Chicago and other local areas.

Where to Go Next from North Chicago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors looking to explore more of the surrounding area, nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Chicago: Just a short drive or train ride away, visitors can experience all that Chicago has to offer, from its world-class museums and attractions to its vibrant culture and nightlife.

  • Lake Geneva: Located approximately 40 miles northwest of North Chicago, Lake Geneva offers visitors a chance to enjoy outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, and hiking, along with its charming downtown area and historic landmarks.

  • Milwaukee: Located approximately 60 miles north of North Chicago, Milwaukee offers visitors a chance to explore its rich history and culture, along with its scenic Riverwalk and museums.

Conclusion

North Chicago offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its scenic parks and trails to its local museums and landmarks, North Chicago has something to offer everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s unique food and drink scene or embark on outdoor adventures, North Chicago is a destination worth adding to your travel list.
