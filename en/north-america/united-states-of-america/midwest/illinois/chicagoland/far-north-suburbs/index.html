Introduction to Far North Suburbs

Located in Chicagoland, Illinois, Far North Suburbs is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by tourists. With its proximity to Lake Michigan and abundant natural beauty, visitors can expect a mix of outdoor adventures and urban attractions. Far North Suburbs offers a unique blend of modern amenities and historical landmarks that cater to all ages. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, museums, or delicious food, this tourist destination has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Far North Suburbs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Evanston History Center - this museum features the city’s local history from the 18th century up to the present.
  • Mary and Leigh Block Museum of Art - located in Northwestern University, this museum features art exhibitions and educational programs that cater to all ages.
  • Grosse Point Lighthouse - built in 1874, this historic landmark offers a glimpse of what life was like for lighthouse keepers in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Far North Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Northwestern University Lakefill - this beautiful park provides visitors with panoramic views of Lake Michigan, walking trails, and a beach area.
  • Chicago Botanic Garden - a perfect spot for nature lovers, this garden features a variety of outdoor exhibits and activities such as a butterfly exhibit, walking trails, and a curated collection of plant species.
  • Ryerson Conservation Area - a vast nature reserve that is perfect for hiking, birdwatching and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Far North Suburbs

  • Green Bay Trail - this 9.5-mile trail is perfect for those who enjoy running or biking. It follows the old Chicago North Shore railroad.
  • North Shore Channel Trail - a 7.9-mile stretch of paved trail that follows the North Branch of the Chicago River.
  • Des Plaines River Trail - a perfect spot for experienced hikers, this trail offers challenging terrains and a mix of scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Far North Suburbs: Activities and Tours

  • Chicago Food Planet Food Tours - visitors can indulge in the local cuisine while learning about the city’s food culture and history.
  • Evanston Century Theatre - this iconic theater is perfect for those who love the performing arts.
  • Pace Bus Tours - visitors can take a guided tour around Far North Suburbs on a bus.

Food and Drink Scene of Far North Suburbs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style hot dogs - a classic Chicago dish, this hot dog features a beef sausage on a poppy-seed bun topped with onions, tomatoes, pickles, relish, mustard, and celery salt.
  • Deep-dish pizza - this thick, hearty pizza is a beast of a dish that was invented in Chicago. There are many places in Far North Suburbs that serve it, such as Giordano’s and Lou Malnati’s.
  • Italian beef sandwich - a Chicago favorite, this sandwich features thinly sliced beef topped with gravy and peppers served on a soaked torpedo roll.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer Company - a craft brewery that offers a variety of beers for visitors to taste and enjoy.
  • Fountainhead - a whiskey bar and restaurant that offers a curated collection of beers and spirits.

Where to Stay in Far North Suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hilton Orrington/Evanston - located in downtown Evanston, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and top-notch amenities.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites - conveniently located near Chicago O’Hare International Airport, this hotel offers an airport shuttle service and comfortable rooms.
  • The Homestead - this bed and breakfast is a cozy and charming option for those who want a more intimate lodging experience.

What to Buy in Far North Suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Garrett Popcorn Shops - a Chicago classic, Garrett Popcorn is a staple of the city’s cuisine. Visitors can bring back a can of caramel, cheese, or mixed popcorn for their loved ones at home.
  • Evanston Farmers Market - visitors can buy fresh produce and local goods such as baked goods, artisan bread, and handmade crafts.
  • Kilwins - a sweet-tooth must-visit, Kilwins offers a variety of delicious chocolates and candies.

Local Culture and Customs of Far North Suburbs

The people of Far North Suburbs have a mix of customs derived from the city of Chicago and their own local history. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local culture by attending festivals and events such as the Evanston Ethnic Arts Festival.

Best Time to Visit Far North Suburbs

The best time to visit Far North Suburbs is during the summer months, from June through August. This is when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Far North Suburbs

  • Do visit the local attractions and try the local cuisine.
  • Do dress appropriately for the weather when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Do be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t trespass or vandalize historical landmarks.
  • Don’t engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Far North Suburbs

Far North Suburbs is a relatively safe area to visit. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions like locking their doors at night and avoiding walking alone in deserted areas.

Health in Far North Suburbs

Visitors to Far North Suburbs should ensure that they are up-to-date with their immunizations and consult their doctor before traveling. Tap water is safe to drink, and there are plenty of medical facilities in the area in case of emergencies.

Getting to Far North Suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Far North Suburbs

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport - located approximately 20 miles from Far North Suburbs, this airport is the primary gateway for international flights.
  • Chicago Midway International Airport - located 30 miles from Far North Suburbs, this airport is convenient for domestic travelers.

Bus Stations Near Far North Suburbs

  • Evanston Davis Street Metra Station - a commuter rail station located in downtown Evanston that offers convenient service to downtown Chicago.
  • Pace Northwest Transportation Center - located in Schaumburg, this station services various bus routes throughout suburban Cook County.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Far North Suburbs.
  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are also readily available.

How to Get Around in Far North Suburbs

  • The metra train system is a convenient way to get around Far North Suburbs and Chicago.
  • Pace buses also serve the area.
  • Renting a car is also a viable option.

Where to Go Next from Far North Suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Downtown Chicago - located approximately 20 miles from Far North Suburbs, downtown Chicago offers visitors a vibrant atmosphere with endless entertainment options.
  • Illinois Beach State Park - a beautiful beach park located in Zion, Illinois, just 20 miles north of Far North Suburbs.

Conclusion

Far North Suburbs is a lesser-known tourist destination that offers a unique mix of outdoor adventures, museums, and historical landmarks. Visitors can explore the local culture and customs while indulging in delicious local cuisine. With its proximity to Lake Michigan and other nearby tourist destinations, Far North Suburbs is a perfect spot for those looking to escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
