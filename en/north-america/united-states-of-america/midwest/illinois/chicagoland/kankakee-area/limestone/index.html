Introduction to Limestone

Located in the Kankakee area of Chicagoland in Illinois, Limestone is a small town with a big personality. The town is located in the Midwest region of the United States and is known for its natural beauty, outdoor recreation opportunities, and rich history. With its stunning limestone cliffs, scenic biking and hiking routes, and fascinating museums, Limestone has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Limestone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Limestone is home to several museums and historical attractions that offer a glimpse into the town’s rich past. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Kankakee County Museum: This museum provides a fascinating look at the history of the Kankakee area. Exhibits include vintage vehicles, local art, and displays on local industries.

  • Kankakee County Soldier’s War Monument: This monument pays tribute to the men and women from Kankakee County who served in the armed forces.

  • Limestone Building: This limestone building was built in the 1800s and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It has been restored and now houses several businesses.

Natural Attractions of Limestone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Limestone is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, from towering limestone cliffs to peaceful riverside parks. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Limestone Park: This park features several hiking trails, picnic areas, and a playground.

  • Kankakee River State Park: This park offers miles of hiking trails, fishing, and boating on the Kankakee River.

  • Limestone Bluffs: These towering limestone cliffs provide a stunning backdrop for hiking, biking, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Limestone

Hikers and bikers will love the scenic routes available in Limestone. The Limestone Bluffs Trail is a popular hiking trail that provides stunning views of the limestone cliffs. The Kankakee River Trail is a 31-mile trail that runs through the heart of Kankakee County and is a popular spot for biking.

Best Things to Do in Limestone: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Limestone offers several other exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Limestone:

  • Kankakee Riverboat Tour: This tour takes visitors on a scenic boat ride down the Kankakee River. Along the way, you’ll learn about the history and ecology of the river.

  • Limestone Fall Fest: This annual festival features live music, food vendors, and a car show.

  • Geo-caching: Limestone has several geo-caching sites, perfect for an outdoor adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Limestone

Day 1: Visit the Kankakee County Museum and learn about the history of the area.

Day 2: Take a scenic boat tour down the Kankakee River and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 3: Explore the stunning Limestone Bluffs on foot or by bike.

Day 4: Spend the day at Limestone Park, hiking, picnicking, and relaxing.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Chicago, just over an hour’s drive away.

Day 6: Visit the Kankakee County Soldier’s War Monument and pay tribute to those who served.

Day 7: Spend the day shopping for souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Food and Drink Scene of Limestone

Must-Try Dishes

Limestone is home to several restaurants that serve up delicious local cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: This classic Midwest sandwich is a must-try in Limestone.

  • Ribs: Limestone is known for its delicious BBQ, and the ribs are a must-try.

  • Pumpkin Pie Shake: This unique dessert is a local favorite and can be found at several ice cream shops in town.

Local Beverages

There are several local breweries and wineries in the area that offer tastings and tours. Some top spots include:

  • BrickStone Brewery: This popular brewery features a variety of craft beers and a lively atmosphere.

  • Vineyard at Hershey’s: This local winery offers tastings, tours, and beautiful vineyard views.

Where to Stay in Limestone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Limestone, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Country Inn & Suites: This hotel is located just 10 minutes from Limestone and offers comfortable rooms and an indoor pool.

  • Hilton Garden Inn Kankakee: This hotel features a fitness center, on-site restaurant, and modern rooms.

  • Home2 Suites by Hilton Bourbonnais: This all-suite hotel is located just a short drive from Limestone and offers easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Limestone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Limestone can bring home a variety of locally made products and souvenirs. Some top picks include:

  • Limestone Rocks: These unique rocks can be found at several gift shops around town.

  • Local Art: Several galleries in town feature the work of local artists.

  • Wine: Several local wineries offer bottled wine that make for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Limestone

Limestone is a friendly and welcoming town with a strong sense of community. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as always saying hello to people you meet on the street.

Best Time to Visit Limestone

The best time to visit Limestone is during the summer months when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Limestone

Do:

  • Do explore the stunning Limestone Bluffs.

  • Do try the local cuisine, including BBQ and pork tenderloin sandwiches.

  • Do take a scenic boat tour down the Kankakee River.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces.

  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals.

  • Don’t forget to say hello to people you meet on the street.

Safety and Health

Safety in Limestone

Limestone is a generally safe town, but visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables visible in a car.

Health in Limestone

There are several hospitals and medical centers located in the nearby town of Kankakee. Visitors should also bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Limestone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Limestone

The nearest airport to Limestone is the Chicago Midway International Airport, located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Limestone

There are several bus stations located near Limestone, including the Kankakee Amtrak station and the Greyhound station in Kankakee.

Other Options

Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Limestone and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Limestone

Limestone is a small town, and it is easy to get around on foot or by bike. For those traveling further afield, renting a car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Limestone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Limestone is located in a beautiful and historic area, with several nearby destinations worth visiting. Some top picks include:

  • Route 66: This famous road is just a short drive from Limestone and offers plenty of opportunities for sightseeing and exploration.

  • Kankakee River State Park: This nearby park offers miles of hiking trails and stunning river views.

  • Chicago: The Windy City is just over an hour’s drive from Limestone and is a must-visit destination for its world-class museums, restaurants, and attractions.

Conclusion

Limestone may be a small town, but its natural beauty, rich history, and friendly community make it a must-visit destination for travelers. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply relax and take in the scenery, Limestone has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip today and experience all that this charming town has to offer.
