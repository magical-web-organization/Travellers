A Comprehensive Guide to Bourbonnais, Illinois

Located in the Kankakee area, Bourbonnais is a charming small town in the Chicagoland region of Illinois. Famous for being home to Olivet Nazarene University, it is a popular destination for students and travelers looking for a unique Midwest experience. From museums and historical landmarks to scenic trails and outdoor adventures, Bourbonnais has something for everyone, making it a great getaway destination.

List of Places to Visit in Bourbonnais

Here are some of the top places to visit in Bourbonnais:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bourbonnais Grove Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Bourbonnais Grove area, including farming, industry, and the development of Olivet Nazarene University.

  • Kankakee County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Kankakee County, including Native American culture, industry, and the Civil War.

  • Kankakee Railroad Museum: Located in a historic train depot, this museum displays antique locomotives, uniforms, and artifacts, and tells the story of the railroad’s impact on the community.

Natural Attractions of Bourbonnais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Perry Farm Park: A popular destination for families, Perry Farm Park offers beautiful trails, playgrounds, picnic areas, and a petting zoo.

  • Kankakee River State Park: With over 4,000 acres of natural preserves, this park offers hiking, camping, fishing, boating, and picnicking.

  • Diamond Point Park: This scenic park features a beautiful lake perfect for fishing and canoeing, and a walking trail, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bourbonnais

  • Bonner Heritage Farm Trail: A scenic trail located in the north of Kankakee. With 19 miles of trails, this is the perfect spot for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • Kankakee River Trail: This trail follows the Kankakee River and takes adventurers through forests and wetlands. The 31-mile trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and paddling.

  • Perry Farm Hiking Trail: This trail is located in Perry Farm Park. With a 2.5-mile loop, this is an easy hike suitable for all ages and skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Bourbonnais: Activities and Tours

  • Olivet Nazarene University campus tour: Take a tour of this beautiful university, including the Centennial Chapel, the Tiger Athletic Complex, and the Ozinga Chapel.

  • Bourbonnais Friendship Festival: This annual festival is held in the summer and features live music, carnival rides, and fun games and activities for all ages.

  • Frosty Mug: This popular ice cream shop offers delicious soft-serve ice creams, milkshakes, sundaes, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bourbonnais

Day 1: Visit the Bourbonnais Grove Historical Society Museum and the Kankakee County Historical Society Museum, and explore the downtown area.

Day 2: Spend the day at Perry Farm Park, go on a hike, have a picnic, and enjoy the Petting Zoo.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Olivet Nazarene University campus and explore the surrounding area.

Day 4: Visit Kankakee River State Park and spend the day fishing, hiking, and picnicking.

Day 5: Go on a tour of the Kankakee Railroad Museum and explore the train depot’s history.

Day 6: Rent a canoe or kayak and paddle down the Kankakee River Trail.

Day 7: Relax at Diamond Point Park, enjoy the scenic beauty, and top off your trip with a visit to Frosty Mug.

Food and Drink Scene of Bourbonnais

Must-Try Dishes

  • Italian Beef Sandwich: A regional specialty and a must-try in the Chicagoland area.

  • Deep Dish Pizza: A Chicago staple, this thick crust pizza is loaded with delicious cheese, sauce, and toppings.

  • Chicago Hot Dog: A delicious hot dog on a bun topped with mustard, onion, pickle relish, sport peppers, and celery salt.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: For beer enthusiasts, make sure to try the local brews at Brickstone Brewery or Bourbonnais’ own Rocco’s Pub.

  • Wine: Head down to the nearby Kankakee area and sample some local wines at the Kankakee Valley Winery.

  • Coffee: Stop by Common Grounds Coffeehouse for a delicious latte or cappuccino.

Where to Stay in Bourbonnais: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express: This budget-friendly hotel is located close to shopping centers, restaurants, and attractions, making it the perfect spot to stay while exploring Bourbonnais.

  • Magnuson Hotel Bourbonnais: Magnason is a budget-friendly option in Bourbonnais. With comfortable rooms and friendly staff, it is a great option for families or travelers.

  • Hamenstay Suites: Hamenstay is a great option for travelers looking for something special. These luxurious suites come equipped with a full kitchen, living area, and bedroom.

What to Buy in Bourbonnais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olivet Nazarene University Apparel: Grab a shirt or hat from the Olivet Nazarene University store to proudly show your support

  • Brickstone Brewery Beer: Treat yourself (or your beer-loving friends) to a bottle of Brickstone’s beer, brewed locally in Bourbonnais.

  • Harvest Market: A large farmers’ market, it offers a variety of fresh produce and artisanal products.

Local Culture and Customs of Bourbonnais

Bourbonnais has a unique mix of Midwest charm, Illinois pride, and college-town flair. The locals are friendly, warm, and welcoming and take great pride in their community, history, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bourbonnais

Summers in Bourbonnais are warm and pleasant, making them the best time to visit. The Bourbonnais Friendship Festival takes place in the summer and is one of the most significant events of the year. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, with comfortable temperatures and beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bourbonnais

Do:

  • Do explore the local food scene, including trying famous Chicago-style pizza.

  • Do attend the Bourbonnais Friendship Festival (If you are visiting in summer)

  • Do take a campus tour of Olivet Nazarene University.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss visiting one of the local parks for an outdoor adventure.

  • Don’t forget to bring an extra layer of clothing, as the weather can change quickly.

  • Don’t forget to tip your servers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bourbonnais

Bourbonnais is a safe place to explore, but you should still take reasonable precautions. Keep your car locked, be aware of your surroundings, and keep your valuables out of sight.

Health in Bourbonnais

If you feel sick on your trip to Bourbonnais, there are several clinics and hospitals available, including the Riverside Medical Center and St. Mary’s Hospital.

Getting to Bourbonnais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bourbonnais

  • Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW): Located around 60 miles from Bourbonnais.

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport (ORD): Located around 80 miles from Bourbonnais.

Bus Stations Near Bourbonnais

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Kankakee): Located about 5 miles from Bourbonnais.

  • Peoria Charter Coach (Champaign): Located about 50 miles from Bourbonnais.

Other Options

  • Driving: The most convenient way to get to Bourbonnais is by car. It is located just off Interstate 57.

  • Train: The closest train station is located in Kankakee, about 5 miles from Bourbonnais.

How to Get Around in Bourbonnais

Car rental is the best way to get around Bourbonnais. Uber and Lyft are also available, but they might not be readily available in this area.

Where to Go Next from Bourbonnais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kankakee: Located just five miles away from Bourbonnais, Kankakee offers plenty of outdoor activities, historic sites, and vibrant communities.

  • Chicago: The Windy City is less than two hours away and offers a wide variety of shopping, culture, and entertainment options.

  • Starved Rock State Park: This scenic park offers hiking, rock climbing, and stunning views, just an hour’s drive from Bourbonnais.

Conclusion

Bourbonnais is a charming, welcoming town with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, outdoor adventures, or just good food and drinks, Bourbonnais is a great place to visit. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip and have an unforgettable experience in this delightful Midwest town.
