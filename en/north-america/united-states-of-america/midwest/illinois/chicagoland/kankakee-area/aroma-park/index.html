Introduction to Aroma Park

Located in Kankakee Area, Chicagoland, Aroma Park is a quaint village in Illinois that offers a mix of natural beauty and cultural attractions. From hiking and biking to museums and historic sites, Aroma Park has something for everyone.

In this guide, we’ll take you through the top things to do in Aroma Park, including where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Aroma Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aroma Park is home to several museums and historic sites that provide insight into the region’s rich history. Here are some must-visit sites:

  • Kankakee County Museum: Exhibits feature Native American artifacts, regional agriculture, and the history of the Kankakee River.
  • Kankakee Railroad Museum: Learn about the importance of rail transportation in the Midwest through displays of historic trains, railroading tools, and memorabilia.
  • B. Harley Bradley House: Designed by famed architect Frank Lloyd Wright, this is Illinois’ only Prairie-style house open for public touring.
  • Kankakee County Courthouse: A historic landmark in the area, this courthouse opened in 1912 and features a beautiful dome and Renaissance-inspired design.

Natural Attractions of Aroma Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aroma Park is blessed with stunning natural beauty, offering numerous opportunities for outdoor recreation. Here are some natural attractions not to miss:

  • Aroma Park Boat Launch: Rent a kayak or bring your own to explore the Kankakee River, a naturally flowing and biologically diverse river.
  • Kankakee River State Park: Enjoy hiking and biking on trails that cross forests, prairies, and wetlands. The park has multiple campgrounds and fishing spots.
  • Bird Park: This park is home to a variety of birds, including waterfowl and songbirds. Enjoy the beautiful landscaping, playground, and picnic areas.
  • Kankakee Sands: This unique ecological habitat offers some rare and endangered species of plants and wildlife in its prairies. Plan your visit for spring or summer when the flowers bloom, and keep an eye out for grazing bison.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aroma Park

Aroma Park boasts of several nature trails and leisure bike routes that wind through forests, rivers, and prairies. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • River Trail: This trail is a 6-mile out-and-back stretch through woods along the Kankakee River. It’s a great spot for wildlife viewing.
  • Bourbonnais Grove Trail: With 5.3 miles of paths, this trail presents you with scenic views of the Kankakee River, and you can hike or bike its full length with ease.
  • Prairie Trail: Spanning 8.8 miles, this trail provides views of Kankakee Sands’ prairies and wetlands while featuring several interpretive signs that showcase the rare species in the habitats.

Best Things to Do in Aroma Park: Activities and Tours

No trip to Aroma Park is complete without enjoying some of its activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Kankakee River Water Trail: Rent a kayak or canoe and spend a day on the Kankakee River. You can bring your own equipment, or you can rent from a local facility.
  • Kankakee River Tour: A guided boat tour along the Kankakee River offers some excellent historical and scenic perspectives, accompanied by the narrator sharing the relevance of the landmarks.
  • Harvest Tyme Pumpkin Patch: This working farm, established in 1995, offers a memorable trip to the pumpkin patch to find the perfect one of the season. This farm offers sensory and carnival-type experiences from early September till late October.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aroma Park

To maximize your time in Aroma Park, here’s a week-long itinerary for your Aroma Park visit.

  • Monday: Head to the Kankakee County Museum to learn about the area’s indigenous inhabitants as well as its history, ranging from early settlements and the arrival of the railroads to World War II.
  • Tuesday: Spend the day hiking in the Kankakee River State Park, covering the trails surrounded by prairies, forests, and wetlands.
  • Wednesday: Visit the Harly Bradley House and learn about the Prairie-style architecture or explore the B. Harley Bradley House, one of the few Wright styles located in the US.
  • Thursday: Take a guided boat tour of the Kankakee River, soaking some fresh airs amid the beauty of nature and history of the place.
  • Friday: Spend the day at Kankakee Sands - trails, wildlife, and other activities to participate in.
  • Saturday: The morning calls for the Aroma Park Farmer’s market, followed by the Harvest Tyme Pumpkin Patch.
  • Sunday: A relaxing day filled with bird-watching at the Bird Park, and cultural exploration at the Kankakee County Courthouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Aroma Park

Aroma Park is home to several eateries that serve up delicious food and refreshing beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style pizza: Visit Angelo’s Pizza to indulge in a slice of Chicago’s famous deep dish pizza, made with a thick crust and lots of cheese and sauce.
  • Hot dogs: Head over to the Depot Restaurant, a historic spot known for its hot dogs and burgers, which has been open since 1893 and is still serving games and events to date.
  • Fresh Seafood: Head over to The Boathouse for some of the freshest seafood in the town.

Local Beverages

  • Apple cider: Grab this classic fall drink at Harvest Tyme Pumpkin Patch or Puckerville Farms.
  • Craft beer: Aroma Park has several local breweries, including Brickstone Brewery and Kankakee Valley Brewery.

Where to Stay in Aroma Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to lodging, Aroma Park offers plenty of options. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Holiday Inn Express: This hotel offers a modern stay and an optional breakfast bar to start a new day.
  • Kankakee River Motel: This motel prides itself on affordability, easy access to the river and hiking trails, as well as a riverside picnic area and barbecue grills.
  • Birch Bark Motel: A charming stay with several amenities such as refrigerators, microwaves, ironing boards, and more.

What to Buy in Aroma Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Aroma Park home with you with souvenirs and local products such as:

  • Kankakee County Historical Society Swag: Visit the Kankakee County Museum gift shop to find books on local history, ornaments, and clothing that represent the region.
  • Local crafts: Make a stop at the Farmers Market to find local crafts such as knitted clothing, paintings, and homemade jams as souvenirs.
  • Pumpkins and Gourds: Late September and early October, you will find local markets where they sell pumpkins, gourds, and scarecrows to decorate your porch for the season.

Local Culture and Customs of Aroma Park

Aroma Park enforces cultural norms and customs such as dining etiquettes, social formalities, and courteous communication. The town values community well-being, and visitors are always welcome to enjoy their leisure time in respectful ways.

Best Time to Visit Aroma Park

The early fall season is the best time of the year to visit Aroma Park. Late September to early October is when the Aroma Park Farmers Market and the Harvest Tyme Pumpkin Patch kick-off, bringing a festive vibe and live music. The fall colors are also at their peak during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aroma Park

Do:

  • Respect other visitors, residents, and wildlife around the river, trails, nature preserves, and public spaces.

  • Come prepared with water, wear appropriate footwear, observe signs, take part in camping activities near the river, and leave no trace behind.

  • Observe the seasonal guidelines given by the local authorities, be aware of weather conditions, and forest-fire season.

Don’t:

  • Litter any public space or park.

  • Feed the wildlife or approach them, as it can provoke wild animals’ behavior.

  • Smoke, litter, or campfires on the Kankakee River.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aroma Park

Overall, Aroma Park is a safe and secure destination for travelers and visitors. It has a low crime rate, and the residents are welcoming and helpful. Always ensure you secure your valuables and observe the local safety rules and regulations.

Health in Aroma Park

In case of emergencies or immediate medical assistance, call 911 or visit the emergency room or urgent care facility. It’s recommended to carry masks and sanitizer as Aroma Park follows mask guidelines. Ensure you are up to date with your vaccinations if you plan to go camping or hiking in the area.

Getting to Aroma Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aroma Park is easily accessible via several modes of transportation. Here are some of the ways to reach the village:

Airports Near Aroma Park

The nearest airports to Aroma Park are Chicago-O’Hare International Airport (ORD) and Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW). Both are 60 to 70 miles away from Aroma Park, with rental cars and shuttle services available.

Bus Stations Near Aroma Park

The nearest bus station to Aroma Park is Kankakee Terminal, providing transport for Greyhound and Cardinal Buses as major carriers.

Other Options

Aroma Park is easily accessible by car, located near the following main highways: I-57, I-80, and Route 45/52.

How to Get Around in Aroma Park

The easiest way to get around Aroma Park is by car. You can rent one at any of the nearby airports or take a driving service like Uber or Lyft. Bikes are also an ideal way to enjoy the scenic routes and the city, and there are plenty of rentals available on short notice.

Where to Go Next from Aroma Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area around Aroma Park, here are some nearby destinations worth checking out:

  • Starved Rock State Park: About an hour’s drive from Aroma Park, this park is known for its unique rock formations and waterfalls, with ample trails for hiking.

  • Kankakee River National Wildlife Refuge: Located in nearby Wilmington, this wildlife refuge offers beautiful river views, birding, and paddling opportunities, and seasonal hunting.

  • Chicago: The world-famous Windy City is a little over an hour’s drive from Aroma Park, offering spectacular architecture, museums, cuisine, and shopping experiences.

Conclusion

Aroma Park offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Whether you want to hike, paddle, or explore historic sites, this picturesque village has something for everyone. Come discover this hidden gem in the Midwest and start planning your trip today!
