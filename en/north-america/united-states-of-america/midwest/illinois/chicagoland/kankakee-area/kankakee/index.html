Introduction to Kankakee

Nestled in the heart of the Midwest, Kankakee is a charming city in Illinois that offers visitors a blend of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures. Situated near the Kankakee River and surrounded by beautiful parks, the city is a paradise for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers.

But Kankakee isn’t just about the great outdoors–the city also boasts several museums and historical attractions that showcase its rich heritage. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Kankakee has something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions in Kankakee, as well as the best things to do, eat, and see while you’re here. We’ll also provide practical tips for planning your trip, including transportation options and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in Kankakee

Here’s a quick rundown of the top attractions in Kankakee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kankakee County Museum: This fascinating museum showcases the history and culture of Kankakee County through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Kankakee Railroad Museum: Train enthusiasts won’t want to miss this museum, which features vintage locomotives, cabooses, and other train memorabilia.
  • Kankakee Valley Theatre: Catch a play, musical, or concert at this intimate theater, which showcases the best of local talent.
  • Riverview Historic District: Take a stroll through this charming neighborhood and admire the beautiful Victorian and Queen Anne-style homes.

Natural Attractions of Kankakee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kankakee River State Park: Home to 4,000 acres of rolling hills, forests, and wetlands, this state park is a paradise for hikers, campers, and anglers.
  • Perry Farm Park: This sprawling park features walking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful gardens, as well as a popular farmers’ market on Saturdays.
  • Kankakee Sands: This nature preserve is home to over 700 species of plants and animals, including rare birds and butterflies.
  • Riverfront Trail: Take a scenic bike ride or stroll along this paved trail, which runs along the Kankakee River and offers beautiful views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kankakee

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll love the hiking and biking opportunities in Kankakee. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • Kankakee River State Park Trail: This challenging 10-mile trail offers beautiful views of the Kankakee River and is a favorite among hikers and bikers.
  • Riverfront Trail: This 3-mile paved trail runs along the Kankakee River and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk.
  • Kankakee Sands Trail: This easy 1-mile trail winds through the Kankakee Sands nature preserve and offers scenic views of the prairie landscape.

Best Things to Do in Kankakee: Activities and Tours

Looking for some fun things to do in Kankakee? Check out these activities and tours:

  • Kayak or canoe the Kankakee River: Rent a kayak or canoe and paddle your way down the scenic Kankakee River.
  • Guided tours of historic homes: Take a guided tour of some of Kankakee’s historic homes, including the gorgeous B. Harley Bradley House.
  • Wine and brew tours: Sample some of the best local wines and brews on a guided tour of Kankakee’s wineries and breweries.
  • Skydiving: Take an exhilarating jump with Skydive Midwest and get a bird’s-eye view of Kankakee from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kankakee

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kankakee, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Explore downtown Kankakee and check out the local shops and restaurants.

Day 2: Visit the Kankakee County Museum and the Kankakee Railroad Museum.

Day 3: Hike or bike the Kankakee River State Park Trail.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of some of Kankakee’s historic homes.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of wine or brew tours.

Day 6: Go skydiving or take a scenic flight over Kankakee.

Day 7: Spend your last day exploring the beautiful natural attractions of Kankakee, like Perry Farm Park and Kankakee Sands.

Food and Drink Scene of Kankakee

No trip to Kankakee is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Deep dish pizza: Try a slice of the iconic Chicago-style pizza at the famous Little Caesars.
  • Italian beef sandwich: Sink your teeth into this classic sandwich, which features tender slices of beef and giardiniera.
  • Corned beef sandwich: Another local favorite, this sandwich is piled high with tender, juicy corned beef.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Sample some of the best local brews at places like BrickStone Brewery and Tangled Roots Brewing Company.
  • Wine: Sip on some delicious local wines at Kite Hill Vineyards and Winery or Hidden Cove Orchard.

Where to Stay in Kankakee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Looking for a place to stay in Kankakee? Here are some options to consider:

  • Hilton Garden Inn: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a convenient location near downtown Kankakee.
  • Riverview Guest House: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of the Riverview Historic District and offers cozy rooms and great hospitality.
  • Kankakee River Valley Inn: This historic inn features beautifully restored rooms and a lovely outdoor seating area overlooking the Kankakee River.

What to Buy in Kankakee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for some unique souvenirs or gifts to bring home from Kankakee? Here are some local products to look for:

  • Homemade soap: Snag a bar of all-natural soap from The Soap Shoppe, a local store that specializes in handmade bath and body products.
  • Local honey: Get a taste of Kankakee with some locally produced honey from Kankakee Farmer’s Market.
  • Artisanal pottery: Check out the beautiful handmade pottery at Kankakee Valley Pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Kankakee

Kankakee has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historic buildings, museums, and local traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions and take the time to learn about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Kankakee

The best time to visit Kankakee is during the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kankakee

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kankakee:

Do:

  • Explore downtown Kankakee and check out the local shops and restaurants.
  • Take a hike or bike ride in one of Kankakee’s beautiful parks.
  • Visit the Kankakee County Museum and learn about the area’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment while hiking or biking.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers at restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Kankakee is generally a safe and healthy place to visit, but visitors should always take precautions to stay safe and healthy while traveling.

Safety in Kankakee

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Use caution when driving in snowy or icy conditions during the winter months.
  • Lock your doors and windows when staying in accommodations.

Health in Kankakee

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during hot weather.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid tick bites, which can transmit Lyme disease.
  • Pack any necessary medications and check with your doctor about any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Kankakee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kankakee is located about 60 miles south of Chicago and is easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Kankakee

The closest airport to Kankakee is Chicago Midway International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Kankakee

Greyhound operates a bus station in Kankakee, providing service to Chicago and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the South Shore Line, a commuter train that runs from downtown Chicago to South Bend, Indiana, with stops in Kankakee.

How to Get Around in Kankakee

The best way to get around Kankakee is by car, but visitors can also use a taxi or rideshare service to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Kankakee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Midwest, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Chicago, Illinois: Just an hour’s drive north, Chicago is a must-visit for its world-class museums, stunning architecture, and vibrant food scene.
  • Starved Rock State Park, Illinois: This beautiful state park is located about an hour’s drive southwest of Kankakee and features stunning canyons, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Lake Geneva, Wisconsin: This charming town is known for its beautiful lakefront, quaint shops, and elegant mansions.

Conclusion

Kankakee may be a small Midwestern city, but it packs a big punch when it comes to outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local cuisine. Whether you’re seeking natural beauty or rich history, Kankakee is a destination that is sure to delight.
