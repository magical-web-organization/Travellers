Introduction to Manteno

Located in the Kankakee Area of Chicagoland, Manteno is a charming village in Illinois known for its rich history, outdoor attractions, and local culture. Whether you are an adventure seeker, history buff, or foodie, Manteno has something to offer you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the best things to see and do in Manteno.

List of Places to Visit in Manteno

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Manteno Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Manteno, featuring exhibits on local industries, pioneer life, and military history.

  • Riverside Medical Center: Visit the first hospital in Kankakee County, established in 1876.

  • The Outpost: Known as the “Best Little Country Store in Illinois,” the Outpost is famous for its unique collection of antiques and country-themed gifts.

Natural Attractions of Manteno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manteno Sportsmen’s Club: This beautiful 80-acre nature preserve offers hiking and biking trails, fishing, bird-watching, and hunting.

  • Heritage Park: This park is perfect for a leisurely walk, a quick picnic, or watching a game of baseball.

  • Manteno Golf Club: Enjoy a round of golf on this well-manicured 18-hole championship course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manteno

  • Bourbonais Grove Trail: This 6-mile trail winds through the beautiful natural areas surrounding the Kankakee River, offering stunning views of the water and wildlife.

  • Manteno Trail: This 4-mile trail runs through the heart of Manteno, providing a convenient and scenic route for biking or walking.

  • Rock Creek Trail: This 27-mile trail is perfect for a serious hike or bike ride, offering breathtaking views of the rolling landscape surrounding Manteno.

Best Things to Do in Manteno: Activities and Tours

  • Ghost Tour of Manteno: Take a spooky guided tour through the haunted and historic sites in Manteno.

  • Wine Tasting: Visit some of the local wineries in Manteno and try some of the best wines produced in the region.

  • Kayaking on the Kankakee River: Experience the beauty of Manteno by kayaking along the Kankakee River.

Food and Drink Scene of Manteno

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style pizza: Enjoy a classic Chicago-style pizza at Rocca’s Pizza, famous for its thick crust and generous toppings.

  • BBQ ribs: Head to the Manteno Sportsmen’s Club to try some of the best ribs in the region, cooked to perfection on an open flame.

  • Corn on the cob: Visit the local farmers market and try some delicious freshly picked corn on the cob.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Enjoy a locally brewed pint of craft beer at the Brickstone Brewery or Evil Horse Brewing Company.

  • Wine: Head to one of the local wineries and indulge in a tasting of the best wines that the region has to offer.

Where to Stay in Manteno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a complimentary breakfast, and a heated indoor pool.

  • Comfort Inn: This hotel offers cozy rooms, free WiFi, and a fitness center.

  • Manteno Manor: This guesthouse is perfect for those who want to experience the local culture and enjoy the hospitality of the locals.

What to Buy in Manteno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Antiques: Check out the unique collection of antiques at the Outpost, or visit one of the many local antique shops.

  • Local wine: Bring home a bottle of wine from one of the local wineries, a perfect souvenir and a delicious reminder of your trip to Manteno.

Local Culture and Customs of Manteno

  • Midwestern hospitality: Manteno locals are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness, and visitors are always welcomed with open arms.

  • Love of the outdoors: The people of Manteno have a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of their region, and many of the local attractions focus on outdoor activities or events.

Best Time to Visit Manteno

The best time to visit Manteno is in the fall when the leaves are changing, and the weather is cooler. Visit in October for the Manteno Oktoberfest, or in November for the Manteno Christmas Market.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manteno

Do:

  • Do try the local wine: Manteno is known for its excellent local wineries, and no trip is complete without a tasting.

  • Do explore the outdoors: With so many beautiful natural areas to explore, spending time outside is a must-do in Manteno.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget your sunscreen: Whether you are hiking or kayaking, be sure to protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays.

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for directions: The locals are friendly and always willing to help visitors navigate the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manteno

Manteno is a safe village, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should be aware of traffic on the roads and avoid hiking or biking alone in remote areas.

Health in Manteno

Visitors should be aware of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease, while spending time outdoors in Manteno. Always check for ticks after spending time outside and seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Manteno: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manteno

The closest airport to Manteno is Chicago Midway International Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Manteno

The closest bus station to Manteno is the Kankakee River Metropolitan Agency (KRMA), located in Kankakee, approximately 7 miles away.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby cities or airports.

How to Get Around in Manteno

The best way to get around Manteno is by car or bike. There are several rental car options available near Manteno, and the local biking trails are perfect for exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from Manteno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kankakee: Just a short drive away from Manteno, Kankakee has several attractions, including the Kankakee County Museum and Kankakee River State Park.

  • Chicago: With its world-class museums, theaters, and restaurants, Chicago is a must-see destination for visitors to the region.

Conclusion

Manteno is a hidden gem in Illinois, with something for everyone, from outdoor adventures and history to local culture and delicious cuisine. Plan your trip to Manteno today and discover all that this charming village has to offer.
