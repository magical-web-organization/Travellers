Introduction to Roselle

Roselle is a charming village located in the northwest suburbs of Chicago, Illinois. It is a peaceful community surrounded by nature, making it an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts and those who seek a break from the hustle and bustle of the city. Despite being small, Roselle has a lot to offer to its visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Roselle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roselle Historical Foundation Museum

The Roselle Historical Foundation Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. It showcases the history of Roselle through interactive exhibits and displays. Visitors can learn about the village’s founding, the growth of the community over the years, and the impact of the railroad on the area’s development. The museum also offers special events, including guided tours and historical reenactments.

Medinah Shriners Temple

The Medinah Shriners Temple is a stunning landmark that has been standing in Roselle since 1929. The building has a unique architecture that resembles a Moorish temple and is a great place to take some photos. Visitors can also attend cultural events hosted by the temple, such as dance performances and music concerts.

Natural Attractions of Roselle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roselle Park District

Roselle Park District is an ideal place for outdoor activities. It features over 20 different parks and recreational facilities, including the beautiful Turner Pond. Visitors can enjoy various sports and games, from basketball and volleyball to skateboarding and disc golf. The park district also offers several walking and biking trails, such as the 11-mile-long Schaumburg Bikeway.

Mallard Lake Forest Preserve

Mallard Lake Forest Preserve is a serene oasis that offers hiking, fishing, and kayaking opportunities. The preserve includes a scenic lake surrounded by lush greenery and some impressive wildlife - keep your eyes peeled for deer, beavers, and coyotes. Visitors can also enjoy some picnic areas and playgrounds in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roselle

Salt Creek Trail

The Salt Creek Trail is a 27-mile-long route that connects many villages and cities in the suburbs of Chicago, including Roselle. The trail runs along the Salt Creek and offers stunning views of the natural landscape. It is an excellent option for hiking, biking, and even horseback riding. The trail also features many rest stops and picnic areas if you need a break.

Best Things to Do in Roselle: Activities and Tours

Lynfred Winery

Lynfred Winery is a must-visit if you are a wine lover. It is the oldest and largest continuously operating winery in Illinois, featuring over 80 different types of wine. Visitors can take a guided tour of the winery, learn about the wine-making process, and taste some of the best wines in the area. The winery also hosts various events, including live music and wine pairing dinners.

Chicago Helicopter Experience

For a unique perspective of the city’s skyline, take a tour with Chicago Helicopter Experience. The company offers different packages that take you through some of the best sights in and around Chicago. The helicopter tours also offer narration from a highly knowledgeable and entertaining guide, sharing fascinating tidbits about the city’s history and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Roselle

Must-Try Dishes

Italian Beef Sandwich

Italian Beef Sandwich is a Chicago classic and a must-try when in Roselle. It’s a sandwich stuffed with thin slices of roast beef, spicy giardiniera, and a crispy roll. You can find this delicious dish at Portillo’s, a local favorite.

Deep Dish Pizza

Deep dish pizza is another local specialty that you must try. Visit Lou Malnati’s, a chain that serves some of the best deep-dish pizzas in the area. The crust is buttery and flaky, with a heaping portion of sauce, cheese, and toppings.

Local Beverages

Beer

Roselle has many breweries that produce some excellent beers. The Village Vintner Winery & Brewery is a great option to try some local brews. They offer a wide range of beers on tap, from light lagers to bold stouts.

Coffee

Coffee lovers should head to Conscious Cup Coffee Roasters, a modern and cozy coffee shop that serves some of the best coffee in the region. They roast their beans in-house, and the aroma of fresh coffee fills the air as you sip your latte.

Where to Stay in Roselle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Holiday Inn Express Roselle

Holiday Inn Express is a comfortable and convenient place to stay in Roselle. The hotel features cozy and spacious rooms, a complimentary breakfast buffet, and a fitness center. It’s also close to some of the best attractions in the area.

Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, Schaumburg, IL

Country Inn & Suites offers a cozy and homey experience, with welcoming guest rooms, complimentary breakfast, and a fitness center. The hotel also features a charming lobby and a heated indoor pool.

What to Buy in Roselle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chicago Cubs Apparel

Chicago Cubs are one of the most popular sports teams in the city, and you can find some great apparel and merchandise at Wrigleyville Sports. The store offers a wide range of clothing, hats, and accessories for fans of all ages.

Roselle Honey

Roselle Honey is a local favorite that is harvested by a family-owned business. You can find this delicious honey at the Roselle Farmers Market, which takes place on Fridays from June through September.

Local Culture and Customs of Roselle

Roselle has a rich culture that revolves around family and community. The village hosts several events and festivals throughout the year that celebrate their heritage and traditions. Visit the annual Roselle Cruise Night, which showcases classic cars and hot rods, or the Taste of Roselle, where you can try some of the best food and drinks from local vendors.

Best Time to Visit Roselle

The best time to visit Roselle is from May to September when the weather is mild and comfortable. During these months, you can enjoy outdoor activities and events, such as concerts, festivals, and farmers markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roselle

Do:

  • Do try the Italian Beef Sandwich and Deep-Dish Pizza.
  • Do explore the many parks and outdoor recreational areas.
  • Do take a guided tour of Lynfred Winery.
  • Do attend one of Roselle’s many community events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothes for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t miss the shopping opportunities at the Roselle Farmers Market.
  • Don’t forget to try some of the excellent local beers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roselle

Roselle is generally a safe village to visit, and crime rates are low. However, it’s wise to take normal precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Roselle

Roselle has several hospitals and medical centers nearby, including Alexian Brothers Medical Center and St. Alexius Medical Center. It’s also a good idea to bring insect repellent during the summer months to prevent tick bites.

Getting to Roselle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roselle

  • O’Hare International Airport is the nearest airport to Roselle, located 16 miles away.
  • Midway International Airport is another option, located 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Roselle

  • Greyhound station in Schaumburg is the closest bus stop to Roselle.
  • Roselle also has a Metra train station that connects the village to Chicago and other suburbs.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a convenient option for traveling around Roselle and other suburbs of Chicago.
  • Taxis and ridesharing services such as Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Roselle

Roselle is a small village, and it’s relatively easy to get around on foot, especially if you are staying close to the central area. Biking is also an excellent option, and there are many bike trails around the village. Taxis and ridesharing services are readily available, and some hotels and inns offer shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Roselle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Schaumburg

Schaumburg is a nearby suburb of Chicago that offers plenty of shopping and entertainment opportunities. Visit Woodfield Mall, one of the largest shopping centers in the region, or explore the Legoland Discovery Center for some family fun.

Chicago

Chicago is a world-class city that offers a wide range of experiences. Visit famous attractions such as Navy Pier, the Willis Tower, and the Art Institute of Chicago. Take a stroll in the historic Millennium Park or enjoy some deep-dish pizza in one of the many pizzerias around the city.

Conclusion

Roselle is a hidden gem in the suburbs of Chicago that offers a true taste of Midwestern charm and hospitality. From outdoor activities to cultural attractions, this village has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the best of Roselle.
