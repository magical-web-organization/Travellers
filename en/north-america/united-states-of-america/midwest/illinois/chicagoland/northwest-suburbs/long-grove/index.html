Introduction to Long Grove

Tucked away in the rolling hills of the Northwest Suburbs of Chicago lies Long Grove, a picturesque village with a small-town feel and big-city amenities. Rich in history, natural beauty, and quaint charm, this idyllic destination is a must-visit for travelers seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

With its tree-lined streets, beautifully preserved historic buildings, and charming shops and restaurants, Long Grove offers a glimpse into a bygone era, while providing modern-day visitors with a host of activities and attractions to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Long Grove has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Long Grove

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Long Grove is home to several institutions dedicated to preserving the area’s rich history and cultural heritage. These include:

  • The Long Grove Historical Society: Located in the heart of the village, this museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the history and development of Long Grove over the centuries. Visitors can learn about the village’s early settlers, its agricultural heritage, and its role in the development of the region as a whole.

  • The Elawa Farm Foundation: This historic farmstead, located just outside the village limits, offers a glimpse into the area’s rural past. Visitors can explore the original farmhouse and barn, attend cooking and gardening classes, and enjoy the scenic grounds, which include an orchard, vegetable gardens, and a pond.

Natural Attractions of Long Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Long Grove is surrounded by some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the Midwest, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top attractions for nature lovers include:

  • Buffalo Creek Forest Preserve: This 1,200-acre preserve features 6.5 miles of hiking trails through pristine woodlands and wetlands. Visitors can also take advantage of fishing and boating opportunities on the creek and explore the nature center, which offers educational exhibits and programs.

  • Reed-Turner Woodland Nature Preserve: Located just a few minutes from downtown Long Grove, this 35-acre preserve provides a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of daily life. Visitors can stroll through the woods, observe native wildlife, and take part in guided nature walks and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Grove

Long Grove and its surrounding areas boast a variety of hiking and biking trails that provide stunning views of the natural landscape. Some of the top routes include:

  • The Des Plaines River Trail: This 31.4-mile trail travels through Lake and Cook counties, passing through the heart of Long Grove. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the river, as well as the surrounding woodlands and prairies.

  • The Deer Grove Trail: This 4.3-mile trail winds through the woods and hills of Deer Grove Forest Preserve, a short distance from Long Grove. It’s a popular destination for hikers, bikers, and equestrians, offering views of the lake, marshes, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Long Grove: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Long Grove has plenty of activities and tours to keep you engaged and entertained. Some top options include:

  • Wine Tasting: Long Grove is home to several wineries, including the renowned Long Grove Winery, where visitors can sample a wide variety of locally produced wines.

  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can enjoy guided trail rides through the hills and forests surrounding Long Grove, taking in the stunning natural scenery along the way.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take to the skies and explore Long Grove’s beautiful countryside from a bird’s-eye view with a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Grove

Day 1: Explore the Village: Start your trip with a walking tour of Long Grove’s charming downtown, including stops at the local shops, museums, and restaurants.

Day 2: Outdoor Adventures: Spend a day exploring the area’s natural beauty, with hiking, biking, and fishing opportunities at Buffalo Creek Forest Preserve.

Day 3: Wine Tasting: Discover the region’s award-winning wineries, with tastings and tours at Long Grove Winery and other local vineyards.

Day 4: Culinary Delights: Enjoy a day of gastronomic indulgence, with a food and drink tour of Long Grove’s best restaurants and cafes.

Day 5: Elawa Farm: Take a break from the hustle and bustle of daily life with a visit to this historic farmstead, where you can attend cooking and gardening classes and enjoy the scenic grounds.

Day 6: Horseback Riding: Get up close and personal with the area’s natural beauty by taking a guided trail ride through the hills and forests surrounding Long Grove.

Day 7: Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take to the skies and explore Long Grove’s beautiful countryside from a bird’s-eye view with a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Grove

Must-Try Dishes

Long Grove is home to a diverse range of restaurants, cafes, and eateries, offering up a variety of delicious dishes to suit all tastes. Some local favorites include:

  • Long Grove Turkey Sandwich: This hearty sandwich features smoked turkey, bacon, avocado, tomato, and peperoncini, served on freshly baked sourdough bread.

  • Lobster Bisque: This rich and creamy soup, infused with the flavors of fresh lobster and sherry, is a Long Grove classic.

Local Beverages

Long Grove is renowned for its wine and craft beer scenes, with a variety of locally produced libations available at restaurants, bars, and tasting rooms throughout the village. Some top options include:

  • Long Grove Winery: This award-winning winery produces a range of locally sourced, handcrafted wines, including reds, whites, and rosés.

  • Buffalo Creek Brewing: This family-owned microbrewery offers a selection of craft beers, including stouts, IPAs, and seasonal specialties.

Where to Stay in Long Grove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Grove offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels and resorts. Some top options include:

  • The Country Inn & Suites: This charming hotel offers comfortable rooms, a complimentary breakfast buffet, and easy access to downtown Long Grove.

  • The Strawberry Field Bed & Breakfast: This historic farmhouse provides a cozy and comfortable base for exploring the village, with a complimentary breakfast and other amenities.

What to Buy in Long Grove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Long Grove is a shopper’s paradise, with a variety of unique boutiques, specialty shops, and gift stores offering a wide range of high-quality goods and handmade crafts. Some top options include:

  • Long Grove Confectionery: This chocolate shop offers a variety of delicious treats, including handmade truffles, fudge, and chocolate-dipped fruits.

  • The Olive Tap: This gourmet store specializes in high-quality olive oils, vinegars, and other specialty foods, sourced from around the world.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Grove

Long Grove has a rich cultural heritage, with strong ties to its agricultural roots and a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of the region. Visitors are encouraged to explore the area’s history and traditions, and to engage with locals to learn more about the vibrant community that calls Long Grove home.

Best Time to Visit Long Grove

Long Grove is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique attractions and activities. Summer is the busiest time of year, with warm weather, outdoor festivals, and a buzzing downtown scene. Fall is a popular time to visit as well, thanks to the area’s stunning fall foliage and harvest festivals. Winter is also a great time to explore Long Grove, with cozy shops and cafes, holiday events, and a magical winter wonderland downtown.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Grove

Do:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic downtown area.

  • Explore the area’s natural beauty with hiking and biking trails.

  • Indulge in some of the local cuisine, including lobster bisque and locally produced wine and beer.

Don’t:

  • Miss the opportunity to explore the nearby Buffalo Creek Forest Preserve.

  • Forget to pick up some locally made crafts and artisanal products to take home with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Grove

Long Grove is generally a safe and welcoming destination, with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions, such as locking their vehicles and securing their belongings in public places.

Health in Long Grove

Long Grove is home to several medical facilities, including Northwestern Medicine Lake Forest Hospital, which offers a full range of medical services to residents and visitors alike.

Getting to Long Grove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Grove

Long Grove is located approximately 25 miles northwest of Chicago O’Hare International Airport, one of the busiest airports in the world. Visitors can easily hire a taxi or ride-sharing service to reach Long Grove from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Long Grove

Several bus companies offer service to Long Grove, including Greyhound, which operates a station in nearby Palatine.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Long Grove via car or train, with several major highways and train lines running through the area.

How to Get Around Long Grove

Long Grove is a small village, easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also drive between the various attractions and activities, or take advantage of public transportation options, such as taxis and buses.

Where to Go Next from Long Grove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Long Grove is located in the heart of the Midwest, within easy reach of several other charming destinations and attractions. Some nearby options include:

  • Chicago: Just 35 miles away, the bustling city of Chicago offers world-class attractions, dining, and entertainment.

  • Milwaukee: Located about two hours north of Long Grove, Milwaukee is a historic city with a rich cultural scene and abundant natural beauty.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking to escape the stresses of daily life, Long Grove is the perfect destination for a relaxing and rejuvenating getaway. Explore the area’s cultural attractions, sample the local cuisine, and take in the stunning natural beauty of this charming village, and discover for yourself why Long Grove is a hidden gem of the Midwest.
