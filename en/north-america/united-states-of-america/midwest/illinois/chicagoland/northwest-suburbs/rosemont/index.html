Introduction to Rosemont

Rosemont is a charming village situated in the Northwest Suburbs, in Chicagoland, Illinois. It is located less than five miles from the O’Hare International Airport and approximately 17 miles northwest of the downtown area of Chicago. Rosemont is known for its extensive entertainment and hospitality venues. It’s a popular destination for business travelers, concert-goers, and families.

List of Places to Visit in Rosemont

Rosemont offers a wide range of attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural escapes. Here are the top places you must visit when in Rosemont.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • THE HOLOCAUST MUSEUM: The museum features several exhibitions showcasing historical artifacts, videos, and oral histories. It’s an excellent place to learn more about the Holocaust.
  • AMERICAN ACADEMY OF DERMATOLOGY HEADQUARTERS: This stunning building is renowned for its architecture and incorporates state-of-the-art dermatology facilities.
  • LEGOLAND DISCOVERY CENTER: For families with young children, Legoland Discovery Center is a must-visit attraction, with interactive LEGO exhibits, play structures, and more.

Natural Attractions of Rosemont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • ROSEMONT PARK DISTRICT: The Rosemont Park District is an excellent option for outdoor enthusiasts, offering several sports fields, a mini-golf course, and an ice-skating rink.
  • MB FINANCIAL PARK: This outdoor entertainment and dining district is a perfect place to enjoy a game of outdoor bowling, flying in the zipline, savor delicious cuisine, and witness outdoor concerts and movies on the weekends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rosemont

Rosemont provides ample opportunity for hiking and biking, with a variety of trails and paths to choose from. Some of the notable routes are:

  • NORTHEASTERN ILLINOIS UNIVERSITY TRAIL: This trail is approximately 7.6 miles long, starting from East River Road and extending to Dempster Street.
  • THE DES PLAINES RIVER TRAIL: Beginning at Cook County Forest Preserve, this trail is approximately 57 miles long, stretching north to the Wisconsin border.

Best Things to Do in Rosemont: Activities and Tours

Rosemont is brimming with engaging activities and tours to experience. Here are the best activities to do in Rosemont:

  • WATCH A SHOW AT THE ROSEMONT THEATRE: Located in the heart of the village, this theater invites people to witness comedy shows, musical concerts, circus, and more.
  • GO SHOPPING AT FASHION OUTLETS OF CHICAGO: For a shopping day, visit Fashion Outlets of Chicago, hosting a wide range of high-end retail stores.
  • TAKE A HELICOPTER TOUR OF CHICAGO: Plan a tour of the city and witness the panoramic view of the Windy City from a bird’s view.

Food and Drink Scene of Rosemont

You can find a wide range of dining options in Rosemont, from fast food to high-end restaurants. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • CHICAGO-STYLE HOT DOG: A hot dog with all the toppings like diced onions, dill pickle spears, pepperoncini, and a generous drizzle of mustard, relish, and celery salt.
  • ITALIAN BEEF SANDWICHES: Pieces of seasoned roast beef soaked in gravy.

Local Beverages

  • CHICAGO-STYLE MICROBREWERIES: Chicago-style microbreweries are exceptionally famous globally.

Where to Stay in Rosemont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rosemont offers plenty of options when it comes to accommodations. There are many hotels and guesthouses to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly to luxury accommodations. Here are the top choices:

  • HYATT REGENCY O’HARE: Conveniently located across the street from the Donald E. Stephens Convention Center and one block away from the Rosemont Theatre.
  • HILTON ROSEMONT/CHICAGO O’HARE: Just a two-minute walk from the Rosemont Theatre.

What to Buy in Rosemont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Rosemont, you may want to bring home some souvenirs and local products. Here are the top things you should consider:

  • CHICAGO MARKET: Located inside the Fashion Outlets of Chicago, the Chicago Market offers local delicacies such as cowboy jerky and Garrett Popcorn.
  • CHICAGO SPORTS MERCHANDISE: Bring home some Chicago sports merchandise, such as Chicago Cubs or Chicago Bulls jerseys, hats, and shirts.

Local Culture and Customs of Rosemont

Rosemont has a distinct culture and customs worth knowing before visiting. The village is a melting pot of cultures, and diversity is praised.

Best Time to Visit Rosemont

The best time to visit Rosemont is from May to September when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed. Spring and autumn can also be considered, although it may be chilly, but the colors of the trees are stunning, especially in autumn. December is also an excellent time to visit Rosemont when the village is all dressed up for Christmas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rosemont

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Rosemont:

Do:

  • Take part in celebrations: Rosemont takes pride in its festivities, so make sure you immerse yourself in the community spirit.
  • Engage with locals: Rosemontians are friendly, so feel free to strike up a conversation.

Don’t:

  • Litter: The Rosemont region is immaculately clean, and residents take tremendous pride in keeping it this way.
  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping your waitstaff and other service providers is customary in the U.S.

Safety and Health in Rosemont

Rosemont is a safe place for tourists, with low crime rates. Here are some safety and health tips to consider during your visit:

Safety in Rosemont

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: Always be mindful of your belongings and surroundings and avoid any streets that appear unsafe.
  • Use reputable transportation services: Stick to well-established transportation services to avoid any scams.

Health in Rosemont

  • Know where the hospitals are: Although the hospitals in Rosemont are top-notch, it’s always good to know where the nearest medical center is before your arrival.
  • Stay hydrated: During the summer months, temperatures can soar, so it’s essential to keep yourself hydrated.

Getting to Rosemont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Rosemont offers an array of transportation options to help travelers get to and from the village.

Airports Near Rosemont

  • O’HARE INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT: One of the largest airports globally and located within five miles of Rosemont.

Bus Stations Near Rosemont

  • ROSEMONT BLUE LINE STATION: It is connected to the Chicago Transit Authority’s Blue line, running directly between downtown Chicago and O’Hare airport.

Other Options

  • TAXI/UBER: Available 24/7 in Rosemont

How to Get Around in Rosemont

Rosemont is a walkable destination, and the trains and buses run regularly. Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft and the local taxi service is also available.

Where to Go Next from Rosemont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you find you have extra time, there are plenty of nearby areas and excursions available for a day trip.

  • CHICAGO: Experience the bustling streets, food scene, and cultural attractions of Chicago.
  • DES PLAINS RIVER TRAIL: Take a beautiful 57-mile ride to see the Des Plaines River Trail ending in the picturesque small town of Libertyville.

Conclusion

Rosemont is a great destination for all types of visitors ranging from solo travelers to families to business travelers. The range of attractions and amenities provides visitors with plenty of options to help plan their visit. This travel guide has provided all the necessary information and recommendations needed to help plan a memorable trip to Rosemont, Illinois.
