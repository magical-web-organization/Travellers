Nestled in the Northwest Suburbs of Chicagoland, Des Plaines is a charming city located in the heart of Midwest United States. Its strategic location makes it a popular attraction among travelers visiting the area. From cultural museums to natural attractions, there is a lot that the city offers to its visitors. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll help you discover the best things to do and see in Des Plaines, Illinois.

Introduction to Des Plaines

Des Plaines is a picturesque city, with a rich history that dates back to the early 1800s. It was established as a trading center, and the Des Plaines River played a significant role in the town’s development. Today, the city is a vibrant hub with a diverse population and a thriving economy. Des Plaines is also known for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, and architectural landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Des Plaines

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Des Plaines is a city rich in history, and its cultural heritage reflects in its collection of museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit places in this category include:

  • Des Plaines History Center: The center is dedicated to preserving the history of Des Plaines and showcasing the town’s cultural heritage.
  • Kiddieland: This amusement park is a perfect place to visit for a fun-filled day with family and friends.
  • Mount Prospect Historical Society: This museum is dedicated to the preservation and exhibition of the history of Mount Prospect city, which is just a few miles away from Des Plaines.

Natural Attractions of Des Plaines: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Des Plaines boasts many parks and natural attractions that bring tourists close to nature. Here are some of the must-see outdoor destinations in Des Plaines:

  • Lake Park: This park is an excellent destination for a day out with family and friends. It features scenic trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds.
  • Lake Opeka: This picturesque lake is an ideal location for fishing, boating, and picnics.
  • Montrose Point Bird Sanctuary: This bird sanctuary is an excellent spot for birdwatching, photography, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Des Plaines

Des Plaines has some of the most scenic hiking/biking routes in Illinois. Here are some of the top trails worth checking out:

  • North Shore Channel Trail: This meandering trail is ideal for mountain biking or a leisurely stroll.
  • Des Plaines River Trail: This 56-mile-long trail is perfect for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers.
  • Lakefront Trail: This 18-mile trail offers stunning views of Lake Michigan while providing ample opportunities for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Des Plaines: Activities and Tours

Des Plaines has many fun-filled activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Des Plaines Summer Fling: Enjoy music, food, and games during Des Plaines’ annual city festival, which takes place every July.
  • Des Plaines Farmer’s Market: Visit this seasonal market to indulge in fresh organic produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts.
  • Des Plaines Depot Museum: This museum offers tours of an 1892 railroad depot that was once a bustling transportation hub.

Week-Long Itinerary for Des Plaines

Here’s a list of activities to include in a week-long itinerary for Des Plaines:

Day Activity
1 Des Plaines History Center, followed by a hike or bike ride on the Des Plaines River Trail.
2 Spend the day at Lake Opeka, enjoying fishing and boating.
3 Explore the Lake Park or Des Plaines Municipal Pool.
4 Visit the Mount Prospect Historical Society museum and take a stroll in Busse Woods.
5 Head to the downtown area and visit the Des Plaines Farmer’s Market and the Depot Museum.
6 Take a trip to Montrose Point Bird Sanctuary and enjoy hiking and birdwatching.
7 Spend the day at Kiddieland with family and friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Des Plaines

Des Plaines is known for its diverse culinary scene with plenty of options to indulge in. Here are some local specialties worth trying:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style hotdogs: A classic in the Midwest, these dogs are a must-try.
  • Deep-dish pizza: A Chicago staple and a must-try for pizza lovers.
  • Italian beef sandwiches: Savory with a hint of spice, these sandwiches are a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer: A locally brewed beer with a smooth, refreshing taste.
  • Chicago-style cocktails: A range of cocktails that pays homage to the Chicago scene.

Where to Stay in Des Plaines: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Des Plaines has plenty of accommodation options for tourists and travelers. Some of the city’s top-rated hotels include:

  • Hilton Garden Inn: A luxury hotel located near the downtown area.
  • Best Western Des Plaines Inn: A budget-friendly hotel with excellent amenities.
  • Extended Stay America: Ideal for travelers looking for an extended stay in the city.

What to Buy in Des Plaines: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the top souvenirs you should consider buying in Des Plaines:

  • Chicago-themed merchandise: Look for t-shirts, baseball hats, mugs, and keychains.
  • Food specialties: Bring home some local goodies like Chicago-style popcorn and chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Des Plaines

Des Plaines is a highly diverse city that welcomes people from all ethnicities and cultures. It’s essential to respect the local community’s customs and traditions when visiting the area.

Best Time to Visit Des Plaines

The best time to visit Des Plaines is during the autumn season between September and November when the weather is mild, and crowds are less. The city also organizes a lot of festivals during this time, providing ample opportunities to immerse in its cultural vibe.

Do’s and Don’ts of Des Plaines

Safety and Health

Safety in Des Plaines

Des Plaines is a relatively safe city, but it’s essential to take standard safety precautions when traveling to any destination. It’s always best to keep an eye on your belongings and avoid strolling alone at night.

Health in Des Plaines

The city houses several hospitals and medical centers that offer top-notch healthcare services to the residents and tourists.

  • Public Transportation: Des Plaines is well-connected via bus routes and trains.

Where to Go Next from Des Plaines: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
