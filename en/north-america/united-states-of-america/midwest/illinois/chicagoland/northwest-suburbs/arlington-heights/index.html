Introduction to Arlington Heights

Arlington Heights is a city located in the northwest suburbs of Chicago, in Illinois, USA. This city is known for its tranquil and vibrant ambiance. Arlington Heights is a melting pot of culture, with a rich history and a thriving art scene. Be prepared to discover some real gems in this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Arlington Heights

If you are traveling to Arlington Heights, don’t miss out on visiting these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arlington Heights Historical Museum
  2. Metropolis Performing Arts Centre
  3. Arlington Park Racetrack

The Arlington Heights Historical Museum is a wonderful place to learn about the history of this city. The museum has three buildings that showcase the history of Arlington Heights from its inception to the present day. The Metropolis Performing Arts Centre is a great place to see exhibitions, performances, and creative workshops. The Arlington Park Racetrack is a famous horse racing track that has a rich history and tradition.

Natural Attractions of Arlington Heights: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Arlington
  2. Heritage Park
  3. Campbell Street

Lake Arlington is a breathtaking man-made lake that offers visitors a range of outdoor activities such as fishing, boating, and picnicking. Heritage Park is a beautiful park located in the heart of Arlington Heights and features a playground, picnic facilities, walking paths, and a concert pavilion. Campbell Street is another popular destination with a mix of retail stores and restaurants, a farmers’ market, and live music performances.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arlington Heights

If you are a nature lover and want to explore the outdoors in Arlington Heights, there are several great hiking and biking routes to explore, such as:

  1. Northwest Community Hospital Trails
  2. St. Viator Trail
  3. Deer Grove Trail

The Northwest Community Hospital Trails offer visitors a scenic and serene hike, with a wide variety of flora and fauna. The St. Viator Trail is a bike route that takes you through residential neighborhoods and beautiful parks. The Deer Grove Trail is a multi-use trail that offers a mix of natural and urban environments.

Best Things to Do in Arlington Heights: Activities and Tours

  1. Arlington Heights Farmer’s Market
  2. Legoland Discovery Center
  3. Chicago Loop Tour

The Arlington Heights Farmer’s Market is a great place to find fresh produce, baked goods, and handmade crafts. Legoland Discovery Center is a fun and educational destination for families with children. The Chicago Loop Tour is a great way to experience the city of Chicago, with its famous landmarks, museums, and architectural wonders.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arlington Heights

To make the most of your time in Arlington Heights, we recommend this exciting week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Arlington Heights Historical Museum, followed by lunch at Mago Grill & Cantina
Day 2 Spend the day at Lake Arlington, try fishing and boating
Day 3 Explore the St. Viator Trail in the morning, and have lunch at Daruma Japanese Restaurant
Day 4 Tour the Chicago Loop, including the Willis Tower and Millennium Park
Day 5 Spend the day exploring the amazing Legoland Discovery Center
Day 6 Visit Metropolis Performing Arts Centre, catch a play or an exhibition
Day 7 Finish your trip at Arlington Park Racetrack, place a bet and enjoy some horse racing

Food and Drink Scene of Arlington Heights

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Italian Beef Sandwich
  2. Chicago-style pizza
  3. Polish Kielbasa Sausage

Italian Beef Sandwich is a classic Chicago dish, made with thinly sliced roast beef, sweet peppers, and au jus sauce. Chicago-style pizza is a must-try, with its deep-dish crust, chunky tomato sauce, and plenty of cheese. Polish Kielbasa Sausage is a traditional dish that’s spicy, flavorful and great for lunch or dinner.

Local Beverages

  1. Goose Island Beer
  2. Old Style Beer
  3. Malort Liquor

Goose Island Beer is brewed in Chicago and is a great beer to try. Old Style Beer is a classic Chicago beer, known for its light taste. Malort Liquor is an acquired taste, but it’s a classic Chicago spirit.

Where to Stay in Arlington Heights: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation in Arlington Heights, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are our top picks:

  1. Hilton Garden Inn
  2. Comfort Inn & Suites
  3. Red Roof Inn

The Hilton Garden Inn is centrally located, with easy access to many attractions. Comfort Inn & Suites is a budget-friendly option that still offers comfort and convenience. Red Roof Inn is a great choice for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Arlington Heights: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Chicago Cubs Merchandise
  2. Colectivo Coffee
  3. Garrett Popcorn

Chicago Cubs Merchandise is a great souvenir to bring home, especially if you love baseball. Colectivo Coffee is a great local coffee brand that’s perfect for coffee lovers. Garrett Popcorn is a must-try snack, especially if you love popcorn.

Local Culture and Customs of Arlington Heights

Arlington Heights is a culturally diverse city, with a rich history and unique customs. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Be respectful of local customs, including tipping, dress code, and local social norms.

Best Time to Visit Arlington Heights

The best time to visit Arlington Heights is during the summer, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Arlington Heights has plenty of indoor activities, so it’s a great destination to visit year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arlington Heights

Do:

  1. Respect local laws and customs
  2. Try the local cuisine and drinks
  3. Visit the Arlington Heights Historical Museum

Don’t:

  1. Litter or pollute the environment
  2. Be disrespectful or rude to locals
  3. Trespass or damage public or private property

Safety and Health

Safety in Arlington Heights

Arlington Heights is a safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it’s still important to use common sense and take precautions when walking around at night, and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Arlington Heights

There are several hospitals and healthcare facilities located in Arlington Heights, should you need medical assistance during your trip.

Getting to Arlington Heights: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arlington Heights

  1. O’Hare International Airport
  2. Midway International Airport

O’Hare International Airport and Midway International Airport are both located within an hour’s drive of Arlington Heights.

Bus Stations Near Arlington Heights

  1. Arlington Heights Transit Center
  2. Northwest Transportation Center

The Arlington Heights Transit Center and Northwest Transportation Center are both located within the city of Arlington Heights and are easily accessible.

Other Options

  1. Metra Train
  2. Uber and Lyft

The Metra Train is a convenient and affordable way to travel to Arlington Heights. Uber and Lyft are also great options for getting around the city.

How to Get Around in Arlington Heights

Getting around Arlington Heights is easy, thanks to its reliable public transportation system, including buses and trains. You can also rent a bike or a car to explore the city. Walking is also a great way to explore the city, especially in the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Arlington Heights: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Chicago
  2. Milwaukee
  3. Lake Geneva

Arlington Heights is located within easy driving distance of Chicago, Milwaukee, and Lake Geneva. All three of these destinations are fantastic for a day trip from Arlington Heights.

Conclusion

Arlington Heights is a fantastic destination for travelers who want to experience the best of the Midwest. With its rich history, diverse culture, and natural beauty, this city has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Arlington Heights and make the most of your time in this amazing city.
