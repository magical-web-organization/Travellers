Introduction to Lisle

Lisle, a village in the Western Suburbs of Chicagoland in Illinois, is an up-and-coming tourist destination. While the community has its roots in farming, today it is a modern and dynamic hub of culture, history, and natural beauty. Lisle is home to vibrant nature trails, fascinating museums, and exciting gastronomic options. This guide will take you through the best things to do and places to visit in Lisle, and offer valuable tips on how to make the most of your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Lisle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lisle boasts several museums and historical sites that offer unique insights into its rich heritage. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Morton Arboretum: The Morton Arboretum is a 1,700-acre outdoor museum featuring a stunning collection of trees and other plants. Visitors can explore 16 miles of trails or take a guided tram tour.
  • The Museums at Lisle Station Park: A historic landmark, Lisle Station Park features museums dedicated to preserving the village’s history, including a railroad museum and an original 19th century train station.
  • Naper Settlement: A living history museum in nearby Naperville, Naper Settlement offers visitors a glimpse into life in 19th century Illinois through its beautifully restored buildings, gardens, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Lisle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Lisle has an abundance of natural attractions to explore. Here are a few of the best:

  • Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve: A picturesque forest preserve with 11 miles of hiking trails, including a waterfall hike.
  • Danada Forest Preserve: Danada’s natural features include wetlands, woodlands, and prairies. This nature preserve’s trails are open for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Hidden Lakes Park: This park is home to birds like herons and ducks that nest among the hidden lakes. The park also offers picnicking, fishing, and a nature trail around the lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lisle

Lisle offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some trails that you should explore during your visit:

  • Illinois Prairie Path: A 61-mile bike trail that connects several western Chicago suburbs, the Illinois Prairie Path cuts through the heart of Lisle.
  • Morton Arboretum: The Arboretum’s trails are open to hikers and bikers (who can rent bikes on site). Highlights include the Meadow Lake Trail and the East Side Main Route.
  • Danada Forest Preserve: Danada offers miles of horse trails plus hiking and biking trails through its natural habitats of woods and prairie.

Best Things to Do in Lisle: Activities and Tours

Lisle offers several activities to make your visit enjoyable and memorable. Here are some that you should try:

  • Zip Lining at the Morton Arboretum: Experience the thrill of gliding over the trees at the Morton Arboretum’s zip lining course.
  • Barrel-aged Beer Tasting at the Afterthought Brewery: Afterthought Brewery in Lisle offers a unique barrel-aged brewing style with a variety of tastings to choose from.
  • Beer Gardens at the Bavarian Lodge: The Bavarian Lodge in Lisle is home to a beautiful outdoor beer garden and a vast selection of German beers to try.

Food and Drink Scene of Lisle

Lisle offers a delicious and diverse culinary scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wood-fired pizza from Forged Pizza: Get piping hot, creative pies made with local, seasonal ingredients at Forged Pizza.
  • Burger and fries from Mullen’s Bar and Grill: Enjoy classic American pub fare in a lively atmosphere at Mullen’s.
  • Fish taco from Delta Taco: Tacos made with fresh ingredients and homemade corn tortillas are Delta Taco’s specialty.

Local Beverages

  • German beer at the Bavarian Lodge: Enjoy a variety of German beers in the Bavarian Lodge’s beautiful outdoor beer garden.
  • Wine from Lynfred Winery: Located in nearby Roselle, Lynfred Winery offers a selection of regional wines.
  • Barrel-aged beer from Afterthought Brewery: Afterthought Brewery is known for its unique barrel-aged brewing style, making their beer a must-try.

Where to Stay in Lisle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lisle has a range of lodging options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options in each category:

  • Hyatt Regency Lisle near Naperville: Located in nearby Naperville, the Hyatt Regency Lisle offers plush accommodations and great amenities.
  • Extended Stay America: More affordable and designed for longer stays, Extended Stay America provides spacious suites with kitchenettes.
  • Hostelling International: A great budget option, Hostelling International offers clean and comfortable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Lisle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lisle isn’t widely known for its shopping scene, but there are a few places where you can find unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some places to check out:

  • The Museums at Lisle Station Park gift shop: Browse the gift shop at The Museums at Lisle Station Park for souvenirs and books on Lisle’s history.
  • DuPage Coin & Jewelery, Inc.: This shop offers a variety of unique vintage and antique items such as coins, jewelry, and sports memorabilia.
  • Nature’s Best Health Foods: Browsing this shop for natural products and supplements to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lisle

Lisle residents are friendly and welcoming to visitors. Before you go, here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Be respectful of nature: Lisle takes great pride in its nature reserves and historic sites, so make sure to leave nature trails and historical landmarks just as you found them.
  • Enjoy the outdoors: Lisle’s natural surroundings are some of the area’s greatest assets, so don’t hesitate to spend time outdoors, hiking, biking, or picnicking.
  • Experience the local lifestyle: Talk to locals, attend community events, and check out local eateries and businesses to get a feel for Lisle’s unique lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Lisle

The best time to visit Lisle is in the spring and fall, when the weather is cool and mild, making it perfect for outdoors activities. However, Lisle is a year-round destination, with plenty of indoor activities to enjoy regardless of the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lisle

Do:

  • Do take advantage of Lisle’s natural beauty and explore its many nature trails.
  • Do experience the local culture by trying the food and drinks and attending community events.
  • Do respect nature and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment or the historical sites.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the season when enjoying the outdoors.
  • Don’t hesitate to ask for help or directions from Lisle residents if needed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lisle

Lisle is a safe destination overall, with a low crime rate; however, visitors should take the same precautions as they would when visiting any other destination.

Health in Lisle

Lisle’s hospitals offer top-notch medical care. The nearest ones are:

  • Edward Hospital, located in nearby Naperville.
  • Advocate Good Samaritan Hospital, located in nearby Downers Grove.

Getting to Lisle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lisle is easily accessible from Chicago and other surrounding suburbs via several transportation options.

Airports Near Lisle

  • O’Hare International Airport: 22 miles from Lisle, about 30-45 minutes by car.
  • Midway International Airport: 27 miles from Lisle, about 35-50 minutes by car.

Bus Stations Near Lisle

  • Greyhound Bus Station, located in nearby Bolingbrook, about 8 miles from Lisle.
  • Megabus Station, located in downtown Chicago, about 25 miles from Lisle.

Other Options

  • Metra Train: Visitors can take the train to the Lisle station, located in the heart of the village.
  • Driving: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Lisle. From Chicago, Lisle is about 25 miles west via I-290 and I-88.

How to Get Around in Lisle

Lisle is a small, walkable community, but visitors may want to use a car or other transportation option to explore the surrounding areas. Taxis and ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Lisle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lisle is just one of many interesting destinations in the Chicagoland area. Here are some other places to explore:

  • Chicago: Lisle is just a short drive or train ride from Chicago, one of the country’s most dynamic and vibrant cities.
  • Naperville: Naperville, a nearby suburb, offers fantastic dining and shopping, as well as cultural landmarks like Naper Settlement.
  • Elmhurst: Head to Elmhurst for a charming downtown area and the Elmhurst Art Museum featuring modern and contemporary art.

Conclusion

Lisle is an underrated destination that offers something for everyone, from outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks, fantastic dining, and unique shopping. Plan your trip to Lisle today and discover the hidden gems of this charming suburban village.
