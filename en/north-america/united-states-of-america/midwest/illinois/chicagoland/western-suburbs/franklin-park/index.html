Introduction to Franklin Park

As you explore Franklin Park, you’ll discover a charming western suburb of Chicago, located in the heart of Chicagoland. Offering ample opportunities for outdoor recreation, cultural exploration, and delicious cuisine, this destination is perfect for solo travelers, couples, and families alike. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Franklin Park, Illinois.

List of Places to Visit in Franklin Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Franklin Park Historical Society Museum - showcases the history of the area’s notable residents, community heritage, and artifacts from the late 1800s to present-day.

  • The Leyden Township Historical Society Museum and Research Center - a unique museum that focuses on the history of the region from prehistoric times to the present-day, with exhibits ranging from antique cars to early farm equipment.

Natural Attractions of Franklin Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Salt Creek Forest Preserve - situated in the western part of Franklin Park, this forest preserve offering hiking trails, multi-use trails, fishing, and picnicking, making it a perfect place to unwind and connect with nature.

  • Franklin Park Prairie Nature Preserve - a beautifully preserved natural area with over 80 acres of land and native flora and fauna, offers hiking trails for visitors to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklin Park

Explore the beautiful nature of Franklin Park by partaking on one of its many biking and hiking routes. Below are some recommended routes:

  • Salt Creek Trail - one of the most popular routes in Franklin Park. Spanning almost 16 miles and connecting multiple forest preserve, this trail offers breathtaking views of nature and wildlife.

  • The Des Plaines River Trail - spans 30 miles in total, is perfect for cyclists and hikers, passes through multiple towns and cities; and connects two state parks.

Best Things to Do in Franklin Park: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Frank Lloyd Wright Home and Studio Tour – Enjoy a walking tour through the iconic Frank Lloyd Wright-designed home, studio, and gardens. Be sure to stop by the onsite museum dedicated to Wright’s design philosophy and artwork.

  • Attend a Concert or Event in Franklin Park – Enjoy one of the outdoor concerts held in the summer months along with other family-friendly events in Franklin Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Franklin Park

Day 1: Visit the Franklin Park Historical Society Museum and the Leyden Township Historical Society Museum and Research Center

Day 2: Explore the Salt Creek Preserve

Day 3: Visit Franklin Park Prairie Nature Preserve

Day 4: Take a Frank Lloyd Wright Home and Studio Tour

Day 5: Attend a Concert or Event in Franklin Park

Day 6: Hike along Salt Creek Trail

Day 7: Experience the Des Plaines River Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Franklin Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Italian Beef – a Chicago classic, this sandwich is made with roast beef, dipped in au jus, and stuffed into a hoagie roll with hot gardinera peppers and served to perfection.

  • Chicago-style pizza – Indulge in the deep-dish, layered pizza with a focus on the cheese, making it a deliciously indulgent pizza.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer Company – drink like a local with an award-winning beer from the popular Goose Island Brewery.

  • Intelligentsia Coffee – sip on it iconic Chicagoan coffee to get you started on your day of exploration.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklin Park

  • Be sure to respect the local residents and their customs.

  • Chicago’s music scene is iconic, so try to catch a show where possible.

Best Time to Visit Franklin Park

The best time to visit Franklin Park is during the summer from June to August when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklin Park

Do:

  • Visit the local historical museums to learn about the unique history of Franklin Park.

  • Indulge in the local cuisine – especially Italian Beef and Chicago-style pizza.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural preserves.

  • Attempt to feed the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklin Park

Franklin Park is generally safe, but visitors should take standard precautions such as not leaving valuables in cars, being aware of their surroundings, and keeping a watchful eye on their belongings.

Health in Franklin Park

The medical facilities in Franklin Park are adequate. In the event of a health emergency, call 911 or visit one of the local hospitals.

Getting to Franklin Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Franklin Park

  • O’Hare International Airport (ORD), 5 miles from Franklin Park.

  • Midway International Airport (MDW), 16.4 miles from Franklin Park.

Bus Stations Near Franklin Park

  • Greyhound Bus Station – Chicago

  • Jefferson Park Transit Center

Other Options

  • Ride-Sharing: Ride-sharing is an easily accessible and affordable option in Franklin Park.

How to Get Around in Franklin Park

Getting around in Franklin Park is made easy with ride-sharing, rental cars, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Franklin Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chicago - Explore the Windy City via public transit or ride-sharing, and soak up the diverse food, culture, architecture, and art scenes.

  • The Morton Arboretum - Spend a day in Lisle and explore the beautiful, 1,700-acre Morton Arboretum.

Conclusion

Franklin Park, Illinois, is a great destination for those looking to explore nature, culture, and exquisite cuisine. With many activities, historical sites, and beautiful natural preserves; Franklin Park guarantees visitors an unforgettable travel experience.
