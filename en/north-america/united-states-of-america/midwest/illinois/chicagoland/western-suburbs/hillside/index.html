Introduction to Hillside

Located in the Western Suburbs of Chicagoland, Hillside is a beautiful village in Illinois. Its close proximity to Chicago makes it an ideal destination for those who want to explore the city, but prefer a quieter, more suburban atmosphere to return to at night.

List of Places to Visit in Hillside

If you’re planning a trip to Hillside, here are some of the must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Loyola University Museum of Art: Located at Loyola University Chicago in the suburb of Maywood, this museum features a diverse collection of art from around the world.

  • Ernest Hemingway Birthplace and Museum: Visit the home where Ernest Hemingway was born and spent his early years in Oak Park, just a short drive from Hillside.

  • Frank Lloyd Wright Home and Studio: Also located in Oak Park, this building was the personal residence and workplace of American architect Frank Lloyd Wright.

Natural Attractions of Hillside: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Schiappacasse Park: This peaceful park offers walking paths, a playground, and a picnic area, making it the perfect place for a family outing.

  • Salt Creek Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking or biking, offering picturesque views of the Salt Creek and surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hillside

If you’re a fan of outdoor activities, Hillside has plenty of options. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • Salt Creek Trail: The Salt Creek Trail is a 27-mile nature trail that stretches through several parks in the Chicagoland area, including Hillside.

  • Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve: Located in Darien, just a short drive from Hillside, this preserve features 9.5 miles of trails for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Hillside: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Hillside! Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Brookfield Zoo: Just a 10-minute drive from Hillside, Brookfield Zoo is a family-friendly attraction with over 2,000 animals and countless exhibits to explore.

  • Chicago Architecture Boat Tour: Take a boat tour of the Chicago River to learn about the city’s stunning architecture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Hillside

No trip is complete without trying the local cuisine! Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages of Hillside:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style deep-dish pizza: A staple of the Windy City, this thick, cheesy pizza pie is a must-try when in Hillside.

  • Italian beef sandwich: A popular sandwich in the Chicago area, this dish features thin slices of beef piled high on a roll and topped with peppers, onions, and other toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer Company: This Chicago-based brewery offers a range of delicious craft beers, including their award-winning IPA.

  • Malort: A traditional Chicago liquor, Malort is known for its incredibly strong and bitter taste. It’s definitely an acquired taste, but worth trying if you’re feeling adventurous!

Where to Stay in Hillside: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re looking for a place to stay in Hillside, here are some great options:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Chicago West - O’Hare: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast, as well as easy access to the Chicago O’Hare International Airport.

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Chicago-Westchester: This hotel is just a few miles from Hillside and offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Hillside: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home from your trip to Hillside, here are some great options:

  • Chicago Cubs or White Sox memorabilia: Sports fans will love picking up a souvenir from one of the city’s baseball teams.

  • Frango Mints: A chocolatey treat from the famous Marshall Field’s department store, these mints are a classic Chicago souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Hillside

When visiting Hillside, it’s important to be aware of the local customs and culture. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Tipping: Tipping is expected in restaurants and bars in the United States. A good rule of thumb is to leave 15-20% of the total bill as a tip.

  • Public Transportation: In Chicago, it’s common to use public transportation to get around. The CTA “L” train and bus system is extensive and can take you almost anywhere in the city.

Best Time to Visit Hillside

The best time to visit Hillside is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, keep in mind that this is peak tourist season, so prices may be higher and attractions may be more crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hillside

To make the most of your trip to Hillside, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Visit Chicago: Hillside is just a short drive from Chicago, so take advantage of the proximity and explore the city!

  • Try the local cuisine: Chicago is famous for its delicious food, so make sure to try some of the local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip: Tipping is an important part of American culture, so make sure to leave a tip when dining out.

  • J-walk: Crossing the street outside of designated crosswalks can result in a ticket or fine, so make sure to only cross at intersections.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Hillside, it’s important to keep safety and health in mind:

Safety in Hillside

Hillside is a relatively safe area, but it’s always important to practice common sense safety precautions. Make sure to keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Hillside

If you need medical attention while in Hillside, there are several hospitals and urgent care facilities in the area. Make sure to have adequate travel insurance and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Hillside: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you’re traveling to Hillside, there are several transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Hillside

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: Located just 11 miles from Hillside, O’Hare is one of the busiest airports in the world and offers flights to and from destinations all over the globe.

Bus Stations Near Hillside

  • Greyhound: Greyhound operates several bus stations in the Chicago area, including one in downtown Chicago.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore the Chicago area and get to Hillside.

How to Get Around in Hillside

Getting around Hillside is easy with several transportation options available, including:

  • Metra train: The Metra UP-W line runs through Hillside and connects to downtown Chicago.

  • Bus: The PACE bus system operates several routes throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Hillside: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time while in the area, consider checking out these nearby destinations:

  • Oak Park: Known for its stunning architecture, Oak Park is just a short drive from Hillside and offers plenty of things to do and see.

  • Naperville: Located just outside of Chicago, Naperville is a charming town with plenty of parks, shops, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Hillside, Illinois is a great destination for those looking to explore the Chicago area. With plenty of things to do, see, and eat, this charming village is sure to delight visitors of all ages and interests.
