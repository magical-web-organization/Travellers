Introduction to Wheaton

Nestled in the Western suburbs of Chicago, Wheaton is a charming and culturally rich city in Illinois. Known for its numerous parks and lush greenery, this small city offers an ideal escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Wheaton is also home to a diverse range of restaurants, shops, museums, and galleries, making it a destination worth exploring. Below is a comprehensive travel guide to Wheaton, Illinois, covering all the must-visit attractions, food scene, accommodations, safety, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Wheaton

Whether you’re looking for natural wonders, culturally rich museums, or outdoor adventure, Wheaton has something for everyone. Below is a list of top places to visit in Wheaton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cantigny Park: This 500-acre outdoor park provides visitors with an opportunity to explore the beauty of nature, gardens, picnic grounds, trails, and museums dedicated to military history.
  • DuPage County Historical Museum: Located in Wheaton’s Wheaton Park District, the museum is home to many exhibits and artifacts detailing the rich history of DuPage County.
  • Billy Graham Center Museum: This museum on the campus of Wheaton College visually tells the story of the Rev. Billy Graham and the history of evangelism in America.
  • Wheaton Park District Memorial Park: Visit beautiful Memorial Park in Wheaton for baseball diamonds, football, and soccer fields, tennis and basketball courts, playground equipment, and a sledding hill for winter fun.

Natural Attractions of Wheaton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Morton Arboretum: Discover the colors of four seasons at Morton Arboretum. This vast garden, which spans 1700 acres, features trees, shrubs, and plants from around the world that provide visitors with autumn color, winter wonderland, spring blooms, and summer tranquility. Be sure to check out the Children’s Garden, a child-centric area with various interactive exhibits.
  • Pratt’s Wayne Woods: Pratt’s Wayne Woods is the perfect spot for hikers and bikers who love off-the-grid winding trails and beautiful, quiet woods. With over 3,400 acres of woods and wetlands, this forest preserve is perfect for nature lovers and bird watchers.
  • Cantigny Golf Course: Featuring spectacular views of the surrounding gardens and lakes, the Cantigny Golf Course is a golf destination unlike any other. It’s located on the grounds of Cantigny Park.
  • Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve: For the nature lover in you, visit the 2,500-acre Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve with more than 11 miles of paved trails winding through woods, fields, and wetlands. The scenic preserve is best enjoyed during the fall season.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wheaton

  • Great Western Trail: Formerly a railroad track, the paved Great Western Trail now serves as a favorite route for bikers, hikers, and joggers. Stretching 17 miles from Sycamore, IL to Saint Charles, IL, the Great Western Trail is a great way to explore some of the charming towns, rolling landscape, nature preserves, and parks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Wheaton: Activities and Tours

  • Downtown Wheaton: The downtown Wheaton area offers plenty of dining, shopping, and entertainment options for visitors to explore. You’ll find a wide variety of specialty shops, art galleries, bookstores, and cafes all housed in beautifully restored historic buildings. Downtown Wheaton holds several events each year that are both fun and informative.
  • Cosley Zoo: Cosley Zoo is a beloved family-friendly attraction in Wheaton. This zoo is home to over 90 animals, most of which are native to Illinois. Visitors can see red foxes, ducks, pigs, chickens, sheep, goats, cows, and more. The zoo also offers educational programs for both kids and adults.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wheaton

For visitors planning a week-long trip to Wheaton, here is a suggested 7-day itinerary:

Day Activity
1. Start your trip at Cantigny Park and explore the museums and gardens.
2. Visit the DuPage County Historical Museum and learn about the region’s rich history.
3. Spend a day at Morton Arboretum, walking or driving through the vast gardens.
4. Check out downtown Wheaton for shopping, dining, and entertainment.
5. Take the Great Western Trail for a scenic bike ride or hike.
6. Visit Cosley Zoo for a family-friendly animal experience.
7. Relax at Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve, enjoying hiking or biking trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Wheaton

From delicious farm-to-table restaurants to cozy cafes, Wheaton’s food scene has something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste in Wheaton:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hotdog at Woofaa: This unique Asian-fusion hot dog is topped with crispy wontons and signature sauces, offering a flavorful spin on a Chicago classic.
  • Sushi at Kome Japanese Restaurant: Kome Restaurant is known for its fresh sushi, but they also serve delicious tempura dishes, udon noodles, and ramen.
  • Bakery Items at Suzettes Creperie: Suzette Creperie is a perfect spot for breakfast, lunch, and dessert. Their menu features sweet and savory crepes and fresh baked goods.
  • Farm-to-Table Meal at Ivy Restaurant: Ivy serves New American cuisine using locally sourced ingredients. The ambience is elegant, making it a perfect date spot.

Local Beverages

  • Beer at Two Brothers Tap House: Two Brothers Tap House offers a great selection of their brewery’s signature beers brewed onsite.
  • Coffee at Arrowhead Restaurant and Bar: Arrowhead may be known for their food, but they also serve impressive coffee. Enjoy happy hour with coworkers at this relaxed spot.

Where to Stay in Wheaton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wheaton has plenty of options when it comes to places to stay. From luxury hotels to budget guesthouses, here are some of the best options:

  • Fairfield Inn and Suites: With its central location and comfortable accommodations, Fairfield Inn and Suites is a great choice for travelers seeking a convenient and affordable hotel.
  • Chicago Marriott Naperville: This upscale hotel is located in nearby Naperville and offers spacious rooms, a gym, an indoor pool, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Hostel International: A great option for budget travelers who want to meet new people, Hostel International offers affordable rooms with shared facilities.
  • Downtown Wheaton Inn: Located in Wheaton’s downtown, the Downtown Wheaton Inn is housed in a historic building and offers guests a cozy and charming experience.

What to Buy in Wheaton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wheaton is known for its variety of charming boutique shops, specialty stores, and antiques. Here are three uniquely local items you must take home:

  • Antique furniture and trinkets: Visit the antique shops in downtown Wheaton to find unique pieces of furniture, trinkets, and items that add a personal touch to your home.
  • Handmade Gifts at The Sipping Muse: The Sipping Muse is a local business that offers painting classes while enjoying wine and beer. You can even take home your finished art piece.
  • Artisanal Beer at Two Brothers Tap House: Bring home a bottle of Two Brothers artisans’ beers only created in Wheaton. This beer makes for a great last-minute gift for the beer lover in your life.

Local Culture and Customs of Wheaton

Wheaton is a friendly and welcoming city that values its community’s traditions and history. Residents often participate in community events, festivals, and performances. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and culture, which include:

  • Be ready to adapt to changing weather, especially during the summer and the winter season.
  • Be respectful of the area’s diverse cultures and beliefs.
  • Always ask for permission and respect any private property while on hiking/biking trails.

Best Time to Visit Wheaton

Wheaton experiences four distinct seasons, with hot summers and cold winters. The best time to visit Wheaton is during the fall from September to November when you can enjoy the picturesque parks and trails with fiery autumn colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wheaton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to remember when visiting Wheaton:

Do:

  • Do explore the vast parks, gardens, and outdoor trails of Wheaton.
  • Do try the unique Asian-fusion hotdog at Woofaa.
  • Do shop for antiques and locally made gifts in downtown Wheaton.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to wear comfortable shoes for hiking/biking and exploring the various trails and forests.
  • Don’t litter in waterways, trails, or parks, as it can harm wildlife and disrupt the ecological balance.
  • Don’t forget to respect other visitors’ privacy and personal space while exploring the city.

Safety and Health

Wheaton is generally safe, but it’s always good to take precautions. Below is a list of safety and health guidelines for visitors to Wheaton:

Safety in Wheaton

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep valuables like cell phones, wallets, and purses out of sight.
  • Always leave your vehicle locked and do not leave any valuables visible inside.

Health in Wheaton

  • Always carry sunscreen and drink plenty of water when exploring the outdoors.
  • Wheaton is a smoke-free city, smoking is therefore prohibited on public streets, in parks, and at public events.

Getting to Wheaton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wheaton is easily accessible by car or train, making it a convenient destination for visitors from nearby areas or further afield.

Airports Near Wheaton

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: Located about 45 minutes from Wheaton, Chicago O’Hare International Airport is one of the largest and busiest airports in the United States.
  • Chicago Midway International Airport: Located about 30 minutes from Wheaton, Chicago Midway International Airport is a gateway to the city’s Southwest Side and surrounding suburbs.

Bus Stations Near Wheaton

  • Downers Grove Metra Station (4 miles)
  • Lombard Metra Station (4 miles)
  • Glen Ellyn Metra Station (3 miles)

Other Options

  • Car: Wheaton is easily reached by car via the Interstate 88 or 290 highways.
  • Train: Wheaton can be reached via the Chicago Metra Union Pacific West Line. The station is located in downtown Wheaton.

How to Get Around in Wheaton

Wheaton is a relatively small city and is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. You can also rent a car or take a taxi service.

Public Transportation in Wheaton

  • Metra Rail: Metra Rail provides a convenient means of transportation for visitors looking to travel outside Wheaton.
  • Pace Bus: Pace Bus is a local bus service that connects Wheaton to other parts of DuPage County.

Where to Go Next from Wheaton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other nearby destinations and plan excursions, you can check out these nearby cities:

  • Chicago: The Windy City is just a short drive or train ride away from Wheaton, making it the perfect day trip destination.
  • Naperville: This charming and picturesque city is located just a few miles away from Wheaton, and it’s known for its beautiful downtown and peaceful Riverwalk.
  • Oak Brook: Oak Brook is an upscale village known for its high-end shopping malls, golf courses, and fine dining restaurants.

Conclusion

Wheaton is an excellent getaway and vacation spot for travelers looking to experience the best of what the Midwest has to offer. The city’s natural landscapes, charming downtown, museums, and vibrant food scene provide visitors with a truly unique travel experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors to Wheaton can quickly plan their trip, knowing all the best things to see and do, local customs, safety tips, and transportation options.
