Introduction to West Chicago

West Chicago is a charming town located in DuPage County, Illinois, in the Western Suburbs of the Chicagoland area. With its rich history, diverse culture, and breathtaking scenery, this town is a popular destination for travelers looking for an adventure away from the bustling city.

In this travel guide, we will explore everything that West Chicago has to offer. From top attractions and natural wonders to local culture, food, and safety tips, this guide covers it all.

List of Places to Visit in West Chicago

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or adventure seeker, West Chicago has something for everyone. Below is a list of top places to visit in West Chicago:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Chicago City Museum: Located in the heart of historic downtown, the museum offers a glimpse into the town’s history, culture, and people.

  • Kline Creek Farm: This living history farm takes visitors back in time to the 1890s and provides insight into farming life during that era.

  • First Division Museum: Located on the grounds of the Cantigny Park, this museum features exhibits that honor the history of the U.S. Army’s First Infantry Division.

Natural Attractions of West Chicago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Morton Arboretum: With more than 1,700 acres of gardens, nature trails, and woodlands, Morton Arboretum is a paradise for nature lovers and hikers.

  • Hawk’s Hollow Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is a perfect spot for a quick hike or a scenic picnic.

  • West Chicago Prairie: This nature preserve is home to diverse flora and fauna and is an excellent spot for bird watching and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Chicago

West Chicago boasts some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. Here are some top routes to explore:

  • Illinois Prairie Path: This 61-mile trail passes through West Chicago and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  • Great Western Trail: This 17-mile trail is perfect for biking, hiking, and running and features stunning views of the local scenery.

  • DuPage River Trail: This 30-mile trail follows the DuPage River and offers a fantastic opportunity to enjoy the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in West Chicago: Activities and Tours

From wine tasting and hot air balloon rides to arts and crafts festivals, West Chicago offers a wide range of activities and tours for travelers of all ages. Here are some top-rated things to do in West Chicago:

  • Fox Valley Winery: Take a tour of the vineyards, sample local wines, and enjoy a relaxing afternoon at the Fox Valley Winery.

  • Heritage Festival: This annual festival celebrates West Chicago’s history, culture, and community and features live music, food, and carnival games.

  • Balloon rides: Explore the scenery from above with a hot air balloon ride over beautiful West Chicago.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Chicago

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to West Chicago:

Day Activities
1 Visit the West Chicago City Museum, explore the historic downtown area, and do some shopping at local boutiques.
2 Hike the Illinois Prairie Path and visit the Kline Creek Farm.
3 Enjoy a wine tasting tour at the Fox Valley Winery, followed by a relaxing picnic at the local park.
4 Take a hot air balloon ride over the town and visit the First Division Museum at Cantigny Park.
5 Explore the Morton Arboretum and enjoy a scenic picnic at the Hawk’s Hollow Nature Trail.
6 Visit the West Chicago Prairie and attend a local arts and crafts festival.
7 Do some souvenir shopping and enjoy a farewell dinner at a local restaurant before departing West Chicago.

Food and Drink Scene of West Chicago

West Chicago has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers something for everyone. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style pizza: No visit to Chicago is complete without tasting the famous deep-dish pizza.

  • Hot dog: Try the classic Chicago-style hot dog with all the fixings, including yellow mustard, green relish, onions, tomatoes, pickles, and peppers.

  • Italian beef sandwich: This iconic sandwich is made with thinly sliced beef, soaked in savory juices, and served on a fluffy roll.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: West Chicago is home to several craft breweries, including Nevin’s Brewing Company and Two Brothers Brewing Company, offering a wide variety of locally brewed beers.

  • Malört: This bitter liqueur is a Chicago specialty and definitely an acquired taste.

Where to Stay in West Chicago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, West Chicago has plenty of options to choose from:

  • Hotel Baker: This iconic hotel is located in the heart of downtown and features luxurious rooms and suites, an on-site restaurant, and stunning river views.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and an indoor pool and hot tub.

  • Pheasant Run Resort: This 250-acre resort features a spa, restaurants, golf courses, and a live theater.

What to Buy in West Chicago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenir shopping, West Chicago has something for everyone. Here are some top picks:

  • Local wine: Bring home a bottle of locally produced wine from the Fox Valley Winery.

  • Craft beer: Visit Nevin’s Brewing Company or Two Brothers Brewing Company and bring back some bottles of your favorite beer.

  • Handcrafted items: Check out local artisan markets and bring home a handcrafted item such as pottery, jewelry, or art.

Local Culture and Customs of West Chicago

West Chicago is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, with a strong emphasis on family, community, and outdoor activities. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and be mindful of the natural environment and local wildlife.

Best Time to Visit West Chicago

The best time to visit West Chicago is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Chicago

Do:

  • Do explore the local history and culture of West Chicago by visiting museums, historical sites, and cultural festivals.

  • Do be respectful of the natural environment and local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural habitat of the local wildlife.

  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing for the weather and outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Chicago

West Chicago is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still take precautions such as practicing common sense safety measures and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in West Chicago

West Chicago has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, providing necessary services to residents and visitors.

Getting to West Chicago: Transportation Options and Logistics

West Chicago is easily accessible by air, bus, and car. Below are some transportation options:

Airports Near West Chicago

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: Located about 20 miles east of West Chicago, O’Hare is one of the busiest airports in the region.

  • Midway International Airport: Located about 40 miles northeast of West Chicago, Midway is a smaller airport but still provides domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near West Chicago

  • Metra Rail: Metra provides commuter rail service between West Chicago and downtown Chicago.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore the town and nearby attractions.

  • Taxi service: Several taxi services operate in and around West Chicago, providing reliable and affordable transportation.

How to Get Around in West Chicago

West Chicago is a walkable town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use public transportation, ride-sharing services, or rent bikes to explore the town and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from West Chicago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

West Chicago is an excellent base for exploring the Chicagoland area and nearby attractions. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Chicago: The vibrant city of Chicago is just a short drive or train ride away and offers a wide variety of attractions, including museums, shopping, and dining.

  • Naperville: This charming town is home to several museums, shops, and festivals, and is located just a few miles south of West Chicago.

  • Starved Rock State Park: Located about 90 miles southwest of West Chicago, this state park is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers and boasts breathtaking views of canyons and waterfalls.

Conclusion

West Chicago is a hidden gem in the Chicagoland area, with its rich history, stunning scenery, and vibrant culture. This comprehensive travel guide provides everything you need to know to explore this beautiful town and nearby attractions. So, pack your bags and get ready to discover the best of West Chicago!
