Brookfield, Illinois, nestled in the Western Suburbs of Chicago, is a charming village with plenty to offer for travelers. From museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Brookfield has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide to Brookfield, we’ll cover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Brookfield

Brookfield is a village located in Cook County in the state of Illinois. It has a population of around 18,000 people and is known for its beautiful parks, wildlife, and historical landmarks. The village was officially incorporated in 1893 and has a rich history that is visible throughout the town.

List of Places to Visit in Brookfield

There are plenty of places to visit in Brookfield for both history and nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brookfield Zoo: One of the top attractions in Brookfield is the Brookfield Zoo. Spanning across 216 acres, it’s home to over 2,000 animals representing 400 different species. It’s an excellent place to visit for families with kids and animal lovers.
  • Zion Lutheran Church: Built-in 1926, the Zion Lutheran Church is a historic church that features beautiful Art Deco architecture. It’s an excellent place to visit for those interested in religious history and architecture.
  • Riverside Water Tower: It’s a Historic Water Tower that was added to the U.S. National Register of Historic Places in 1975. The tower stands 100 feet tall and has beautiful architecture with intricate designs.

Natural Attractions of Brookfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Salt Creek Woods: If you’re looking for a hiking spot, Salt Creek Woods is a great option. It’s known for its quiet trails and beautiful scenery, and the area is full of wildlife.
  • Brookfield Woods: Brookfield Woods is another great spot for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers. The trails are well-marked, and there are plenty of shaded areas to rest.
  • Kiwanis Park: Kiwanis Park is a beautiful park that features a pond and lots of greenery. It’s a great place for a family picnic, fishing, or a relaxing walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookfield

Brookfield has plenty of options for those who enjoy nature walks and bike rides. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes include the Des Plaines River Trail, the Salt Creek Trail, and the Chicago Lakefront Trail.

Best Things to Do in Brookfield: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Brookfield beyond hiking and exploring nature, there are plenty of options as well.

  • Brookfield Farmers Market: If you’re in Brookfield on a Saturday, make sure to visit the farmers’ market. It’s a great place to buy fresh produce and locally made goods.
  • Konow’s Corn Maze: This is a great family-friendly attraction in the fall months. The corn maze is a popular destination for families.
  • Haunted Woods: If you’re visiting Brookfield around Halloween, try the Haunted Woods attraction. It’s a spooky outdoor adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brookfield

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Brookfield, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Brookfield Zoo in the morning and Salt Creek Woods in the afternoon.
  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Riverside Water Tower and visit the Frederick Law Olmsted Historic Site.
  • Day 3: Visit Kiwanis Park in the morning and go biking on the Des Plaines River Trail in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: Explore the Brookfield Farmers Market in the morning and take a trip to the Konow’s Corn Maze in the afternoon.
  • Day 5: Visit the LaGrange Art Museum and check out some of the art galleries in the area.
  • Day 6: Go on a guided nature hike in the Brookfield Woods in the morning and enjoy some shopping in downtown Brookfield in the afternoon.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at Haunted Woods and experience the spooky outdoor adventure in the evening.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookfield

Brookfield offers a unique food and drink scene, with plenty of options for all types of travelers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Brookfield:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style deep dish pizza: No trip to Chicago is complete without trying the famous deep dish pizza.
  • Italian Beef Sandwich: This sandwich, made with roast beef, peppers, and cheese, is a Chicago specialty.
  • Chicago-style hot dog: A classic American hot dog loaded with toppings like mustard, onions, and relish is a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Chicago craft beer: Chicago features a thriving craft beer scene, and many breweries offer tours and tastings.
  • Chicago-style cocktail: Bartenders in Chicago are known for their creative and unique cocktails, like the old-fashioned.

Where to Stay in Brookfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brookfield offers plenty of options for travelers when it comes to accommodation. Some of the top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include the Best Western Plus Chicagoland-Countryside, the Hampton Inn & Suites, and The Flats at Loyola Station.

What to Buy in Brookfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, make sure to check out some of the boutiques and specialty stores in downtown Brookfield. Some of the popular items to buy include handmade crafts, jewelry, and locally made food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookfield

Brookfield features a diverse community, with plenty of cultural events and festivals throughout the year. Some of the popular cultural events in Brookfield include the Brookfield Jazz Society Concert Series and the Brookfield Fine Arts Festival.

Best Time to Visit Brookfield

The best time to visit Brookfield is from May to September when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, Brookfield can be visited throughout the year for its festivals and indoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brookfield

Do:

  • Take a guided tour of the historical landmarks in the area.
  • Try the local cuisine, including deep dish pizza and Italian beef sandwiches.
  • Visit the farmer’s market for fresh produce and locally made goods.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities available in the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural attractions and parks in the area.
  • Ignore the safety precautions when hiking in the parks.
  • Eat at chain restaurants when there are plenty of local options to try.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brookfield

Brookfield is a safe village, but visitors should exercise caution when hiking in the parks.

Health in Brookfield

Visitors should take basic health precautions, such as washing their hands frequently, staying hydrated, and using sunscreen.

Getting to Brookfield: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brookfield is easily accessible by car, train, or plane.

Airports Near Brookfield

  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: About 30 minutes by car from Brookfield.
  • Chicago Midway International Airport: About 20 minutes by car from Brookfield.

Bus Stations Near Brookfield

  • Greyhound: Located about 20 minutes from Brookfield.

Other Options

  • Train: The Metra commuter train connects Brookfield to downtown Chicago and other surrounding suburbs.

How to Get Around in Brookfield

The best way to get around Brookfield is by car. There are plenty of rental car agencies in the area.

Where to Go Next from Brookfield: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chicago: No trip to Illinois is complete without visiting the bustling city of Chicago.
  • LaGrange: LaGrange is another charming western suburb of Chicago that is worth a visit.
  • Forest Park: Forest Park offers many outdoor activities, including biking, hiking, and golfing.

Conclusion

Brookfield, Illinois, is a charming village that offers a range of activities for visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, food, or culture, Brookfield has something to offer.
