Introduction to Lemont

Lemont is a charming village located in the Western Suburbs of Chicago, Illinois. It is known for its historic downtown, scenic landscape, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adventure enthusiast, Lemont has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll find everything you need to plan your perfect trip to Lemont.

List of Places to Visit in Lemont

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lemont Area Historical Society Museum: Located in a restored 19th-century church building, this museum explores the history of Lemont and its surrounding areas through exhibits, artifacts, and photos.
  • The Forge: Lemont Quarries: A newly developed adventure park that features zip-lining, kayaking, rock-climbing, and mountain biking.
  • Old Stone Church: A historic church built in 1861 that features stained-glass windows and a cemetery with graves dating back to the Civil War era.
  • Argonne National Laboratory: A world-renowned scientific research center open for guided tours.

Natural Attractions of Lemont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve: A 2,492-acre forest preserve with 11 miles of trails, including a 9.5-mile loop that takes hikers past a stunning 100-foot waterfall.
  • Heritage Quarries Recreation Area: A 400-acre park with scenic views, fishing and kayaking opportunities, and over 5 miles of trails for hiking and biking.
  • Illinois and Michigan Canal State Trail: A 61-mile trail that follows the historic I&M Canal, offering scenic views of the canal, forests, and the Des Plaines River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lemont

Lemont offers an abundance of hiking and biking trails, with something suitable for all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Centennial Trail: A 10.6-mile trail that runs through the Cook County Forest Preserve and offers scenic views of the Des Plaines River.
  • I&M Canal State Trail: As mentioned above, this 61-mile trail offers a flat and easy ride suitable for all skill levels.
  • Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve: With over 11 miles of trails, this preserve has something for everyone, including a 9.5-mile trail that takes hikers past a stunning 100-foot waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Lemont: Activities and Tours

Besides the natural and historical attractions, Lemont offers a variety of activities and tours that will interest visitors of all ages. Some popular ones include:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Take a guided tour or rent a kayak or canoe from Heritage Quarries Recreation Area or Sagawau Environmental Learning Center and explore the Des Plaines River or the nearby quarries.
  • Lemont Farmers Market: Open from June to October, this farmers market features fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products from local vendors.
  • Brewery Tours: Lemont is home to a few craft breweries, including Pollyanna Brewing Company and The Forge Brewhouse, both offering tours and tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lemont

Day 1: Visit the Lemont Area Historical Society Museum and learn about the village’s history.

Day 2: Spend a day at The Forge: Lemont Quarries and enjoy outdoor adventures such as zip-lining, kayaking, rock-climbing, and mountain biking.

Day 3: Take a hike at Waterfall Glen Forest Preserve and enjoy the stunning 100-foot waterfall.

Day 4: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the Des Plaines River or the nearby quarries.

Day 5: Visit the Old Stone Church and explore its cemetery with graves dating back to the Civil War era.

Day 6: Take a tour of the Argonne National Laboratory, a world-renowned scientific research center.

Day 7: Spend a day at Heritage Quarries Recreation Area and enjoy fishing, kayaking, hiking, and biking.

Food and Drink Scene of Lemont

Must-Try Dishes

  • Polish Sausage: A Chicago classic, polish sausage is a must-try when visiting Lemont. Head over to Lemont Legends to try their famous Leo’s Polish Sausage.
  • Fish Fry: Another popular dish in Lemont is the fish fry, which you can find at many local establishments, including The Cottage on Dixie and Brown’s Chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Pollyanna Brewing Company: A craft brewery located in the heart of Lemont, offering a variety of local brews and seasonal options.
  • Cork and Kerry: A neighborhood bar with a wide selection of craft beers and cocktails.

Where to Stay in Lemont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Hampton Inn Chicago/Burr Ridge: Located just a few miles from Lemont, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and complimentary breakfast.
  • Lemont Airbnb: For a more budget-friendly option, consider booking an Airbnb in Lemont, where you can experience the local life and culture.

What to Buy in Lemont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Polish Pottery: Pick up some beautiful hand-painted pottery from the Lemont-based store, What a Jewel.
  • Lemont Legends Merchandise: Celebrate the village’s history and love of sports by picking up some merchandise from Lemont Legends.

Local Culture and Customs of Lemont

Lemont is a friendly and welcoming village, where people take pride in their history and traditions. Some local customs and events to be aware of include:

  • St. Patrick’s Day Parade: Lemont hosts an annual St. Patrick’s Day parade, which is a highlight of the village’s calendar.
  • Lemont Heritage Fest: A three-day festival celebrating the village’s history, culture, and community, held annually in June.

Best Time to Visit Lemont

The best time to visit Lemont is from March to October when the weather is pleasant and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re looking to experience the village’s local culture and traditions, consider planning your visit around the St. Patrick’s Day Parade or the Lemont Heritage Fest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lemont

Do:

  • Do take some time to explore the village’s historic downtown, where you’ll find many charming shops and restaurants.
  • Do try the local food and craft beer scene, which is often overlooked by visitors.
  • Do embrace the outdoor activities that Lemont has to offer, including hiking, biking, and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate outdoor clothing and gear, especially if you’re planning to hike or kayak.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural areas during your outdoor adventures.
  • Don’t expect a bustling nightlife or big-city attractions–Lemont is a small village with a relaxed lifestyle.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lemont

Lemont is a safe and friendly village, with low crime rates. However, as with any outdoor activities, it’s important to take appropriate safety precautions, especially when hiking or kayaking.

Health in Lemont

Lemont has a few medical facilities and pharmacies, including Advocate Medical Group and Walgreens, which are easily accessible from downtown.

Getting to Lemont: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lemont

The closest airports to Lemont are:

  • Chicago Midway International Airport: (MDW) 20 miles away
  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport: (ORD) 27 miles away

Bus Stations Near Lemont

Lemont is easily accessible by bus from Chicago, with the following bus stations nearby:

  • Burr Ridge Park-n-Ride: Located just a few miles from Lemont, this station offers bus service to downtown Chicago.
  • Bolingbrook Park-n-Ride: Another nearby station that offers bus service to downtown Chicago.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Lemont, you can take I-55 or I-294 and exit at Lemont Road. There is ample parking in downtown Lemont, with free and paid options available.

How to Get Around in Lemont

Lemont is a small village, and most of the attractions can be easily visited on foot or by bike. However, if you need transportation, there are a few options:

  • Taxi and Rideshare Services: Uber and Lyft are available in the area, as well as local taxi services.
  • Rental Cars: If you’re arriving by plane, you can rent a car at the airport and drive to Lemont.

Where to Go Next from Lemont: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Starved Rock State Park: A stunning natural area located about an hour’s drive from Lemont, featuring canyons, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Brookfield Zoo: A world-renowned zoo located in Brookfield, Illinois, about 17 miles from Lemont.
  • Chicago: Explore the Windy City, located just 28 miles from Lemont, home to world-class museums, top-rated restaurants, and iconic attractions such as Willis Tower and Navy Pier.

Conclusion

Lemont is a hidden gem in the Western Suburbs of Chicago, offering a perfect blend of history, nature, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Lemont has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you have all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to this charming and welcoming village.
