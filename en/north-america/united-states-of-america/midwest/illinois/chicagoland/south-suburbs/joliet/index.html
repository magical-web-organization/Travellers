Introduction to Joliet

Located in the South Suburbs of Chicagoland in Illinois, Joliet is a charming city boasting a mix of rich history, natural beauty, and modern city attractions. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, or culinary scene, Joliet is an ideal pick for your next vacation destination.

Week-Long Itinerary for Joliet

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for your trip to Joliet:

Day 1: Visit the Joliet Area Historical Museum, followed by dinner at Al’s Steakhouse, a Joliet institution serving hearty Italian-American cuisine.

Day 2: Head to the Rialto Square Theatre and catch a show, visit the Route 66 Visitor Center, and then have lunch at the Iron Horse Bar & Grill.

Day 3: Take a scenic hike along the I&M Canal Trail, have a picnic lunch at Rock Run Preserve, and then visit the Pilcher Park Nature Center.

Day 4: Spend the day at Harrah’s Joliet Casino and try your luck at the table games and slot machines.

Day 5: Visit the picturesque Bird Haven Greenhouse and Conservatory, followed by a relaxing afternoon at Hammel Woods Trail.

Day 6: Check out the haunts at Statesville Haunted Prison and then have dinner at Truth Italian Restaurant, serving authentic Italian cuisine.

Day 7: Head to the Chicagoland Speedway and catch a race, then have dinner at Joliet’s oldest restaurant, The Public Landing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Joliet

Joliet has a crime rate higher than the national average, so always remain aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Joliet

Joliet has plenty of medical clinics and hospitals to choose from if necessary. For those with preexisting health conditions, consult your healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Joliet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Whether you’re traveling domestically or internationally, there are several transportation options to get to Joliet.

Airports Near Joliet

The nearest international airport to Joliet is Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW). Other regional airports include Chicago O’Hare International Airport (ORD) and Chicago Rockford International Airport (RFD).

Bus Stations Near Joliet

Greyhound has a bus station located at 143 E Cass St in Joliet.

Other Options

There are several options for traveling to Joliet, including taking the train on Amtrak’s Illinois Service or the METRA BNSF Railway.

How to Get Around in Joliet

To get around Joliet, the easiest option is renting a car. Rental companies such as Avis, Enterprise, and Budget have outlets in Joliet. Alternatively, there are cab services and public transportation options like buses.

Where to Go Next from Joliet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for a change of scenery, nearby cities like Aurora and Naperville offer plenty of activities to explore.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or enjoy outdoor adventures, Joliet has something to offer. With its unique cultural heritage and beautiful natural scenery, it’s a destination you won’t want to miss.
