Introduction to South Suburbs

Located in the Chicagoland area in Illinois, South Suburbs is a desirable location for travelers seeking a perfect blend of urban and suburban living. The region is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and diverse dining options. This comprehensive travel guide features all the information you need to plan your trip to South Suburbs, including the must-visit places, things to do, where to stay, and local customs.

List of Places to Visit in South Suburbs

Whether you’re exploring the natural beauty of South Suburbs or learning about the region’s cultural history, there’s plenty to see and do in this region. Here’s a list of must-visit places in South Suburbs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pullman National Monument Museum and Historic Landmark: This museum showcases the story of the town of Pullman, which was founded as a model industrial and residential community in 1880.
  • Harold Washington Cultural Center: This center features African American cultural and performing arts events and exhibits.
  • Bronzeville Children’s Museum: This museum celebrates African American history and culture while offering a fun and educational experience for children.

Natural Attractions of South Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Midewin National Tallgrass Prairie: This national park offers picturesque hiking trails, bike paths, and spectacular views of the Illinois prairie landscape.
  • Lake Katherine Nature Center and Botanic Gardens: This beautiful park features a variety of gardens, hiking trails, and a lake where visitors can fish, boat or picnic.
  • Little Red Schoolhouse Nature Center: This nature center has indoor and outdoor exhibits, live animals, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Suburbs

If you’re looking to get active while exploring South Suburbs, there are plenty of scenic hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some popular routes:

  • Cal-Sag Trail: This 26-mile trail runs along the Calumet Sag Channel, offering views of wetlands, woodlands, and the historic Pullman District.
  • I&M Canal State Trail: This 61-mile trail passes through Lockport, Channahon, Morris, and Ottawa, highlighting the historic Illinois and Michigan Canal.
  • Centennial Trail: This trail spans over 18 miles, running through several parks and forest preserves, including Grissom Woods, Oak Forest Heritage Preserve, and Midlothian Meadows.

Best Things to Do in South Suburbs: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking trails, there are plenty of other fun and exciting things to do in South Suburbs. Here are some recommendations:

  • Take a brewery tour: South Suburbs has a thriving brewery scene, so why not take a tour and sample some local beers? Some recommended breweries include Hailstorm Brewing Co., One Trick Pony Brewery, and Flossmoor Station Restaurant & Brewery.
  • Enjoy a live performance: Whether you’re into theater, music, or dance, South Suburbs has many performing arts venues worth visiting. Some popular options include The Center for Performing Arts at Governors State University, Freedom Hall Nathan Manilow Theatre, and The Drama Group.
  • Shop local: South Suburbs has many unique shops and boutiques offering one-of-a-kind items. Some must-visit shopping destinations include Frankfort Country Market, Homewood Farmers’ Market, and Bucktown Holiday Art Show & Sale.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Suburbs

If you have a week to explore South Suburbs, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Pullman National Monument and explore the historic Pullman District.
  • Day 2: Hike or bike the Centennial Trail, stopping at Grissom Woods, Oak Forest Heritage Preserve, and Midlothian Meadows.
  • Day 3: Visit the Bronzeville Children’s Museum and take a tour of the Harold Washington Cultural Center in Chicago.
  • Day 4: Take a brewery tour, visiting Hailstorm Brewing Co., One Trick Pony Brewery, and Flossmoor Station Restaurant & Brewery.
  • Day 5: Explore the Lake Katherine Nature Center and Botanic Gardens, taking a stroll around the lake and exploring the gardens.
  • Day 6: Go shopping at Frankfort Country Market and visit the Homewood Farmers’ Market.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to Chicago to visit its top attractions, such as the Art Institute of Chicago, Millennium Park, and Navy Pier.

Food and Drink Scene of South Suburbs

South Suburbs has a diverse dining scene, offering something for every taste. From traditional American fare to international cuisine, you’ll find it all here.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicago-style pizza: A deep-dish pizza covered with chunky tomato sauce and large amounts of melted cheese.
  • Italian beef sandwich: A sandwich made with thin slices of seasoned beef, cooked in its own juices, and served on a warm Italian roll with sweet peppers or giardiniera.
  • Jibarito: A Puerto Rican sandwich made with garlic, oil, and green plantains, served with meat, lettuce, tomato, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer Co.: This award-winning brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their signature IPA and Honkers Ale.
  • Malort: A bitter liqueur with a distinct taste, popular among locals.

Where to Stay in South Suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Suburbs has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly guesthouse, you’ll find it here. Some popular options include:

  • Doubletree by Hilton Hotel Chicago-Alsip: This hotel features luxurious guest rooms, an indoor pool, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Sleep Inn Tinley Park: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and free breakfast, located just a few miles from Hollywood Casino Amphitheatre.
  • Harvey Guesthouse: This budget-friendly guesthouse features cozy rooms and a shared kitchen, centrally located near public transportation.

Local Culture and Customs of South Suburbs

South Suburbs is a diverse region that celebrates its cultural heritage through food, music, and festivals. Some local customs and practices to keep in mind include:

  • Being punctual: Arriving on time is important to many locals, so it’s best to avoid running late.
  • Respecting cultural norms: South Suburbs has a diverse population, so it’s important to be respectful of different customs and religious practices.

Getting to South Suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Suburbs

The main airports serving South Suburbs are:

  • Chicago Midway International Airport
  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport

Both airports offer public transportation options to get to and from South Suburbs.

Bus Stations Near South Suburbs

  • Greyhound Chicago Terminal: This Greyhound station is located in Chicago’s River North neighborhood, offering bus service to South Suburbs and other destinations.
  • Jefferson Lines: Jefferson Lines offers bus service to the region’s various bus terminals.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: The Amtrak train station in Homewood provides service to Chicago and other destinations.
  • Metra: Metra is a commuter rail system that offers service to many areas of South Suburbs.

How to Get Around in South Suburbs

Public transportation is available in South Suburbs via Metra trains and the CTA bus system. Car rentals are also available at the airports and in various locations throughout the region. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.
