Introduction to Chicago Heights

Welcome to one of the most underrated destinations in Chicagoland - Chicago Heights! Known for its culturally diverse neighborhoods, scenic beauty, and outdoor adventures, Chicago Heights offers something exciting for everyone. Located in the South Suburbs of Illinois, this hidden gem is perfect for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Chicago Heights

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Prairie State College Art Gallery - Explore the impressive collection of contemporary art by emerging and established artists.
  • Flossmoor Station Restaurant and Brewery - Located in an old train station, this restaurant and brewery offers a unique experience with delicious food, beer, and ambience.
  • Illinois Central Railroad Historical Society Museum - Discover the rich history of the railroad industry and the role of Illinois Central Railroad in the development of Chicago Heights.

Natural Attractions of Chicago Heights: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Deer Creek Nature Center - Get lost in the beauty of nature with miles of hiking trails, picturesque lakes, and impressive flora and fauna.
  • Thorn Creek Nature Preserve - Hike through the lush forest, observe the colorful birds and butterflies, and take a break at the peaceful picnic areas.
  • West Branch Gallery and Sculpture Park - Admire the unique and impressive outdoor sculptures by world-famous artists amidst a beautiful landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chicago Heights

  • Old Plank Road Trail - This 22-mile trail offers excellent opportunities for hiking, biking, and even horseback riding.
  • Chicago Heights Park District Bike Trails - The park district offers 6 miles of well-maintained bike trails, perfect for a scenic ride.
  • Forest Preserve Trails - The Cook County Forest Preserve District offers miles of well-marked trails, ranging from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Chicago Heights: Activities and Tours

  • Skydive Midwest - Thrill-seekers can enjoy an unforgettable experience of skydiving and scenic views over Lake Michigan.
  • Balmoral Park Racetrack - Enjoy a fun time with family and friends at the racetrack, filled with horse races, food, and excitement.
  • SATC Ghost Tours - Take a spooky adventure with the Supernatural and True Haunted Chicago Tour, exploring the haunted places of Chicago Heights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chicago Heights

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Prairie State College Art Gallery, enjoy lunch at Flossmoor Station Restaurant and Brewery, and take a brewery tour.
Day 2 Explore the Illinois Central Railroad Historical Society Museum and the Thorn Creek Nature Preserve.
Day 3 Hike the Old Plank Road Trail and spend the afternoon at the West Branch Gallery and Sculpture Park.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Chicago and explore the iconic sights and neighborhoods.
Day 5 Visit the Deer Creek Nature Center and enjoy a picnic, fishing, or birdwatching.
Day 6 Attend an event or a horse race at the Balmoral Park Racetrack.
Day 7 Take the SATC Ghost Tour and end the trip on a spooky note.

Food and Drink Scene of Chicago Heights

Must-Try Dishes

  • Italian Beef Sandwiches - Thinly sliced roast beef, simmered in a savory broth, served on a toasted Italian roll with peppers and cheese.
  • Chicago-style Pizza - Deep-dish pizza with a buttery crust, piled high with cheese and toppings.
  • Pierogis - A traditional Polish dish made with dough and filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, and meats.

Local Beverages

  • Chicago-style Hot Chocolate - This creamy, frothy, and rich hot chocolate is a must-try during the winter months.
  • Goose Island Beer Company - This local brewery offers a selection of craft beers, including the famous 312 Urban Wheat Ale.

Where to Stay in Chicago Heights: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Banque Hotel - Located in a historic bank building, this chic hotel offers luxury amenities and stunning views of downtown Chicago.
  • Motel 6 Chicago Heights - A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Chicago-Lincolnshire - This family-friendly hotel offers spacious suites with a living area, bedroom, kitchenette, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Chicago Heights: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Brookfield Zoo and Lincoln Park Zoo Gift Shop - Shop for unique gifts, souvenirs, and apparel, supporting the charitable work of these famous zoos.
  • Chicago Heights Farmer’s Market - Pick up fresh produce, cheeses, artisanal meats, and baked goods from local vendors.
  • Eisenhower Tower Shopping Center - This shopping center offers a range of shops, including clothing stores, gift shops, and jewelry stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Chicago Heights

Chicago Heights is a culturally diverse city, where diverse communities of Asian, Hispanic, Polish, and African American add to the vibrant tapestry of the city’s culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions and embrace the opportunity to learn about and appreciate the unique heritage and history of the city.

Best Time to Visit Chicago Heights

The best time to visit Chicago Heights is during the spring and fall months, from March to May and September to November. The weather is pleasant and comfortable, and the city is less crowded than during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chicago Heights

Do:

  • Explore the city’s cultural attractions, museums, and galleries.
  • Hike and bike the scenic trails and parks.
  • Try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in unsafe or illegal activities.
  • Disrespect the local communities and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural resources of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chicago Heights

Chicago Heights is a relatively safe city, but visitors should exercise caution, especially in the downtown areas and at night. Keep an eye on your belongings, stay in well-lit and populated areas, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Chicago Heights

Chicago Heights offers excellent medical facilities and hospitals for residents and visitors alike. It is advisable to carry travel insurance and consult with your physician regarding any health concerns before traveling.

Getting to Chicago Heights: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chicago Heights

  • Chicago Midway International Airport - Located 20 miles from Chicago Heights, it serves several airlines and destinations.
  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport - Located 30 miles from Chicago Heights, it is the largest airport serving Chicago and offers excellent connectivity.

Bus Stations Near Chicago Heights

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in nearby Homewood, it serves several buses connecting to major US cities.

Other Options

  • Metra Electric Line - This commuter rail line connects Chicago Heights to downtown Chicago and beyond, with several stops in between.
  • Amtrak - Amtrak trains stop at the Homewood station, connecting Chicago to major US cities like New York, Boston, and Los Angeles.

How to Get Around in Chicago Heights

The best way to explore Chicago Heights is by car. Several car rental companies are available at the airports and in the city. Taxis, rideshare services, and public transportation are also available.

Where to Go Next from Chicago Heights: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Indiana Dunes National Park - Only an hour’s drive away, this scenic park offers gorgeous beaches, sand dunes, and hiking trails.
  • Starved Rock State Park - A two-hour drive from Chicago Heights, this park offers breathtaking waterfalls, canyons, and stunning views of the Illinois River Valley.
  • Milwaukee, Wisconsin - A two-hour drive from Chicago Heights, Milwaukee offers a range of attractions, including the Milwaukee Art Museum, the Harley Davidson Museum, and the Historic Third Ward.

Conclusion

Chicago Heights is a fascinating destination, offering a unique blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re a history buff, a thrill-seeker, a nature lover, or a foodie, Chicago Heights has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and uncover the city’s hidden gems!
