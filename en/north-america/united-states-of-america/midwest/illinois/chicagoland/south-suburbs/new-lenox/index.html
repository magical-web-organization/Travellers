Introduction to New Lenox

New Lenox is a scenic and vibrant suburb situated in the South Suburbs of Chicagoland. This charming village is a perfect getaway for a refreshing break from the city’s hustle and bustle. The town has something for everyone, from historic sites and museums to natural attractions, local cuisine, and more.

New Lenox is conveniently located, which makes it a great base for exploring the surrounding areas and even taking day trips. The town is easily accessible by car, train, or bus, and there are plenty of accommodations to choose from, whether you are looking for a hotel, hostel or guesthouse.

List of Places to Visit in New Lenox

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

New Lenox is rich in history, and there are plenty of historic sites and museums you can visit. The following are some popular destinations:

  • Old Plank Road Trail: This trail is the perfect option for those who love outdoor activities. It is a 21-mile-long trail built on an abandoned railroad that spans through 4 counties. This trail offers amazing views and is ideal for hiking, biking, and walking.

  • Forest Preserve District of Will County: The district offers more than 20 different nature preserves for people to explore and enjoy. Visitors can find trails, woods, meadows, prairies, wetlands, and rivers for fishing.

  • Jacob Henry Mansion Estate: The mansion is a historical and architectural marvel constructed in the 1800s. Visitors can tour the mansion and enjoy the gardens and museum located on the grounds.

  • Lincoln-Way Area Historical Society Museum: The museum displays artifacts and exhibits related to the Lincoln-Way area’s past, including the New Lenox Township.

These places offer a glimpse into the town’s rich history and provide an educational and fun experience.

Natural Attractions of New Lenox: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

New Lenox’s peaceful and serene surroundings offer abundant opportunities for outdoor adventures. Some popular outdoor attractions include:

  • Hickory Creek Preserve: The preserve is the perfect option for nature lovers, bird watchers, hikers, and photographers. It features a stream, woodland, and grassland areas that provide habitats for various wildlife species.

  • Lake Renwick Preserve: The preserve is an excellent spot for bird watching. Visitors can watch over 20,000 heron nests, along with other bird species.

  • Iron Bridge Trailhead: The trailhead is located along the Old Plank Road Trail. The trail features woodland, prairie, and wetland areas and offers stunning views and an adventurous journey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Lenox

New Lenox has a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels and ages. The following are some popular options:

  • Old Plank Road Trail: This trail spans 21 miles and is ideal for hikers, bikers, and walkers of all skill levels. It is a scenic route full of natural and cultural attractions.

  • Iron Bridge Trailhead: The trailhead features a 3.5-mile trail that is perfect for biking and hiking. It has stunning views of forests and waterways.

Best Things to Do in New Lenox: Activities and Tours

Visitors to New Lenox have plenty of fun activities to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • iFLY Indoor Skydiving: This indoor skydiving facility offers an unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages. Guests can simulate a skydive in a high-tech wind tunnel.

  • Wine and Paint Nights: Escape your daily routine and get creative at one of New Lenox’s paint and sip events. Wine and paint nights are a perfect way to unwind and tap into your creative side.

  • Chicago Helicopter Experience: See New Lenox and its surroundings from a new perspective by taking a scenic helicopter tour. This is a great way to experience breathtaking views of the city and its outskirts.

Local Culture and Customs of New Lenox

New Lenox town is known for its friendly and welcoming locals. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. The village residents are generally proud of their community and the passion they have in keeping it as a historically rich and influential place.

Best Time to Visit New Lenox

The best time to visit New Lenox is during the spring, summer, or fall. Throughout mid-June to mid-August, the weather is hot and humid. The city does not experience much rainfall, with occasional showers throughout the season. The fall season from September to November features cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, making it an ideal time to visit the town.
