Bartlett, located in Fox River Valley in Chicagoland, Illinois, is a picturesque village that offers visitors a wide range of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or foodie, this guide has everything you need to plan your trip to Bartlett.

Introduction to Bartlett

Bartlett is a small suburb that is rich in history and culture. The area was first settled in 1832 and named in honor of Luther Bartlett, a Chicago and Aurora Railroad director. Today, Bartlett is a bustling village with a population of around 41,000 people.

List of Places to Visit in Bartlett

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bartlett History Museum: This museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the history of Bartlett and its role in the development of the region.
  • Fox River Trolley Museum: Here, visitors can take a ride on an antique trolley and learn about the history of the electric trolley car in Illinois.
  • Stearns History Museum: This museum is located in nearby St. Charles and features exhibits on the history of the Fox River Valley.

Natural Attractions of Bartlett: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bartlett Hills Golf Club: This 18-hole golf course is known for its beautiful scenery and challenging layout.
  • Apple Orchard Golf Course: This scenic golf course is located on the outskirts of Bartlett and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Bartlett Park: This 41-acre park is perfect for a picnic, bike ride, or nature walk. It features a playground, sports fields, and a bike trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bartlett

  • Bartlett Park Trail: This 2.1-mile trail runs through Bartlett Park and is ideal for hiking and biking. It features beautiful views of the forest preserve and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Prairie Path: This 61-mile trail runs from Cook County to DuPage County and is perfect for biking and hiking. It passes through Bartlett and offers scenic views of the Fox River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Bartlett: Activities and Tours

  • Escape Quest: This escape room attraction offers challenging puzzles and mysteries to solve. It’s perfect for families and groups of friends.
  • Bartlett Nature Center: This nature center offers classes, workshops, and events for all ages. Visitors can learn about the natural history of the area and enjoy hands-on activities.
  • Chicago Helicopter Experience: This helicopter tour offers an amazing bird’s eye view of the Chicago skyline. The tour takes off from nearby Schaumburg Airport.

Food and Drink Scene of Bartlett

Must-Try Dishes

  • Italian beef sandwich: This sandwich is a Chicago classic. It’s made with thin slices of beef, giardiniera, and sweet peppers.
  • Deep dish pizza: This thick pizza is loaded with cheese and toppings. It’s a must-try when visiting Chicago.
  • Chicago-style hot dog: This hot dog is served on a poppy seed bun and topped with mustard, relish, onions, tomatoes, pickles, and sport peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Goose Island Beer Co.: This Chicago-based brewery offers a wide selection of craft beers, including the popular Goose IPA.
  • Malört: This local liqueur is an acquired taste but is beloved by many Chicagoans. It’s made from wormwood and has a bitter flavor.

Where to Stay in Bartlett: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

What to Buy in Bartlett: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Craft beer: Chicago is known for its craft beer scene, so be sure to pick up a few bottles of local brews to take home.
  • Chicago-style hot dog kit: Bring home the flavors of Chicago with a kit that includes everything you need to make your own Chicago-style hot dogs at home.
  • Chicago memorabilia: Bring home a piece of Chicago’s history with a souvenir from the gift shop at the Bartlett History Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of Bartlett

Bartlett is a friendly and welcoming community that prides itself on its diversity and inclusivity. Visitors should be respectful to all members of the community and be open to experiencing new cultures and customs.

Best Time to Visit Bartlett

The best time to visit Bartlett is between May and October, when the weather is mild and the outdoor attractions are at their best.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bartlett

Bartlett is a safe community, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, lock your car doors, and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Bartlett

Visitors to Bartlett should take standard health precautions, including washing hands frequently and wearing sunscreen when outdoors.

Getting to Bartlett: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bartlett

  • O’Hare International Airport: Approximately 18 miles away from Bartlett
  • Chicago Midway International Airport: Approximately 33 miles away from Bartlett

Bus Stations Near Bartlett

  • Greyhound: Closest station is in nearby Elgin, approximately 8 miles away from Bartlett

Other Options

  • Metra: The Bartlett Metra station connects to downtown Chicago and other suburbs in the region.

How to Get Around in Bartlett

The best way to get around Bartlett is by car or bike. There are also several taxi and ride-sharing options available in the area.
