A Comprehensive Guide to East St. Louis: Explore The Hidden Gems of Midwest

Located in St. Louis Metro East, Illinois, East St. Louis is a gem of the Midwest that is yet to be fully discovered. With a cultural significance and natural beauty, it offers a wide variety of attractions that cater to both cultural and adventure-seeking visitors. If you’re planning a trip to East St. Louis, then follow our comprehensive guide to make the most of your journey.

Introduction to East St. Louis

With a population of over 26,000 people, East St. Louis is a city that traces its history back to the early 18th century. Known for the heavy industry and railroads that drove its economy, today, the city is shifting its focus towards attracting tourists. To our benefit, this means that the city is fairly affordable with plenty of fun things to do.

List of Places to Visit in East St. Louis

Below is a list of the must-visit places in East St. Louis that you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site: Explore the impressive 100-foot-high Mississippian-era earthen mounds, which date back over 1000 years.
  • Lewis and Clark State Historic Site: Discover the historical significance of the Lewis and Clark expedition, which started its westward journey from the Confluence of Missouri and Mississippi Rivers.
  • National Shrine of Our Lady of the Snows: See the 200-acre sanctuary, frequented by millions of pilgrims and catholic faithful every year.

Natural Attractions of East St. Louis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malcolm W. Martin Memorial Park: Take in the 360-degree panoramic views of the St. Louis skyline from the 150-feet tall observation tower.
  • Gateway Geyser: Witness the sight of the world’s second-tallest fountain that stretches up to 630 feet in the air, just next to the famous Gateway Arch.
  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site Trails: Capture the breathtaking beauty of the site’s many hiking trails that run through the preserved historical ruins and dense forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East St. Louis

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, East St. Louis offers several trails that cater to various skill levels:

  • Sam Vadalabene Bike Trail: An 18-mile long trail alongside the Mississippi river that passes through Alton, IL, and takes you on the classic midwest landscape experience.
  • Cahokia Mounds Hiking Trails: Trails that traverse through the historic site’s prehistoric mounds, forests, and old native settlements.
  • Pere Marquette State Park: A 2.5-mile trail within the state park that offers scenic views of the Illinois River, towering bluffs, and wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in East St. Louis: Activities and Tours

  • Attend a Baseball Game: Catch a game of the Frontier League affiliated baseball team of the Gateway Grizzlies at GCS Ballpark.
  • Explore Downtown St. Louis: Cross the river into downtown St. Louis and enjoy the classic St. Louis style BBQ food joints and craft breweries while exploring the historic architecture of the city.
  • Visit A Local Zoo: Have a fun family day out at the nearby St. Louis Zoo, which has been ranked as one of the country’s best zoos.

Week-Long Itinerary for East St. Louis

Here is a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip efficiently:

Day Activities
1 Visit Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site, St. Louis Gateway Arch and the Gateway Geyser
2 Hike Pere Marquette State Park’s trail and explore the Lewis and Clark State Historic Site
3 Take an afternoon off and check out a Grizzlies’ game at GCS Ballpark
4 Explore Downtown St. Louis
5 Go birding and take your camera out at Teal Lake and check out the tennis courts at Arthur Park
6 Take a day trip to visit the National Shrine of Our Lady of the Snows
7 Spend your last day at Malcolm W. Martin Memorial Park and Gateway Geyser.

Food and Drink Scene of East St. Louis

East St. Louis has a diverse culinary scene with several must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork steaks: One of the most beloved dishes of the midwest, the pork steak is a juicy and tender piece of pork. Most of the restaurants in East St. Louis serve pork steaks with a side of barbecued sauce.
  • Gooey Butter Cake: This sponge cake with a custard-like texture and gooey top is a delicious and traditional dessert of St.Louis and must-try when in East St. Louis.

Local Beverages

  • Anheuser Busch Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the historic Anheuser Busch brewery that dates back to 1852, now owned by the Belgian company InBev, and try their signature beers during the tour.
  • Grafton Winery and Brewhaus: Take a scenic drive to the famous winery and brewery, located in Grafton, IL, overlooking the Mississippi river.

Where to Stay in East St. Louis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East St. Louis has many options for accommodation that cater to any budget, including:

  • Hotel Indigo: A hip and trendy hotel located in the heart of East St. Louis downtown that provides a luxurious stay experience.
  • Legacy Motel: A budget-friendly motel located near Gateway Arch, offering clean, safe, and simple accommodations.
  • Comfort Inn: A reliable hotel chain that offers comfortable rooms with all basic amenities and more affordable price than Hotel Indigo.

What to Buy in East St. Louis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home, these local products are worth trying:

  • Pork Steaks: Bring home the flavors of Midwest with a pack of pork steaks from local butchers.
  • St. Louis-Style BBQ Sauce: Take home a taste of St.Louis’s famously flavorful BBQ sauce home.

Local Culture and Customs of East St. Louis

East St. Louis is culturally rich and diverse, with a high population of African American and Muslim residents. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and respectfully while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit East St. Louis

The best time to visit East St. Louis is from April to May and September to October, when the weather is pleasant. Traveling in these shoulder seasons also helps you avoid the high-season crowds and prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of East St. Louis

Do:

  • Dress in layers as the temperatures may vary throughout the day
  • Be respectful of the historic sites and landmarks
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables in an unattended car
  • Visit remote areas alone after dark
  • Disrespect the religious and cultural traditions of the region

Safety and Health

Safety in East St. Louis

While East St. Louis has made positive strides in recent years to reduce crime, the city still can be dangerous for unaware visitors. It’s important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions as you would in any major urban center.

Health in East St. Louis

East St. Louis has multiple clinics and hospitals that provide quality care for visitors. However, it’s always recommended to have health insurance while traveling.

Getting to East St. Louis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East St. Louis

  • St. Louis Lambert International Airport: Located just 21 miles from downtown East St. Louis, the airport offers connections to many major cities in the US and abroad.

Bus Stations Near East St. Louis

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located a short ten-minute taxi ride from East St. Louis, this station provides regular connections to all major cities in the US.

Other Options

  • Via Car: East St. Louis is well connected by interstate highway across the state and across the country.
  • Rail Transport: Amtrak station from St.Louis Gateway Station is almost 8 miles away, and it can be reached via taxi, bus, or ride-sharing apps.

How to Get Around in East St. Louis

The best way to get around East St. Louis and the St. Louis metropolitan area is by car, either renting one at the airport or hiring a taxi. Some hotels offer free or low-cost shuttle services to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from East St. Louis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Louis, Missouri, is just across the Mississippi River and has many attractions to offer, such as:

  • Gateway Arch National Park: Visit the iconic landmark to experience the expanded museum, exhibits and cruises on the river.
  • City Museum: Explore the playful and artistic children’s museum that caters to all ages, famous for its tall slides and caves.
  • Missouri Botanical Garden: Stroll through the lush beauty of the botanical gardens, which include 79 acres of horticultural display.

Conclusion

East St. Louis is a hidden gem that offers a one-of-a-kind experience waiting for you to discover. From hiking trails to the historical site, the rich culinary scene to cultural diversity, and easy access to nearby destinations; it is an ideal destination for your next Midwest holiday plan.
