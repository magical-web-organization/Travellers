Introduction to Collinsville

Located in St. Louis Metro East, Illinois, Collinsville is a hidden gem in the Midwest region of the United States of America. Known for its rich cultural history, beautiful natural attractions, and friendly locals, Collinsville is a must-visit destination for those seeking a unique and authentic travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Collinsville

Collinsville offers a diverse range of attractions that cater to all interests. Whether you are interested in learning about the city’s history, exploring the outdoors, or indulging in local food and drink, here are the top places to visit in Collinsville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site: Step back in time and explore one of the largest pre-Columbian settlements in North America.
  • Collinsville Historical Museum: Discover the city’s fascinating past through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • World’s Largest Catsup Bottle: Take a photo with the iconic water tower, which boasts its love for Heinz ketchup.

Natural Attractions of Collinsville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horseshoe Lake State Park: Go boating, fishing, or camping at this picturesque state park.
  • Kidd Lake: Enjoy a hike or bike ride around this serene lake.
  • Catsup Tower Park: Take a leisurely stroll and admire the scenic views of the city from the top of the hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Collinsville

Collinsville offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are the most popular ones:

  • MCT Nickel Plate Trail: This 14-mile long trail offers scenic views of the countryside and connects to other trails in the area.
  • Cahokia Creek Trail: Follow the creek and enjoy the lush scenery on this 5.7-mile long trail.
  • Horseshoe Lake Trail: This 2.8-mile long trail surrounds the lake and offers stunning views of the water and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Collinsville: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for unique experiences in Collinsville, here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Haunted History Tour: Learn about the spookiest stories of Collinsville on this ghost tour.
  • Beer Tasting Tour: Indulge in local craft beer and learn about the brewing process at the Kräftig Haus in Breese.
  • Troy Town Ghost Tour: Explore the history and hauntings of the nearby town of Troy on this one-of-a-kind tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Collinsville

Collinsville is home to many unique and delicious restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Horseshoe Sandwich: This local specialty is a open-faced sandwich that usually includes meat, fries, and cheese sauce.
  • Pork Steak: A Midwest favorite, this tender and juicy cut of pork is a must-try in Collinsville.
  • Fried Chicken: Get your hands on some crispy and flavorful fried chicken at one of the many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Kräftig Lager: This local brew is a popular choice among beer enthusiasts in Collinsville.
  • Egyptian Chai: Sip on a warm cup of Egyptian Chai, a sweet and spicy tea that blends traditional chai spices with locally grown herbs.

Local Culture and Customs of Collinsville

Collinsville is a friendly and welcoming community that values its rich history and unique culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including:

  • Being polite and courteous to locals.
  • Respecting historical and cultural landmarks, such as the Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site.
  • Sampling local cuisine and beverages.

Best Time to Visit Collinsville

The best time to visit Collinsville is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Getting to Collinsville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Collinsville is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are the most convenient options:

Airports Near Collinsville

Bus Stations Near Collinsville

Other Options
