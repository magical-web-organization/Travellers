Introduction to Lebanon

Lebanon is a hidden gem located in the heart of the St. Louis Metro East region of Illinois, surrounded by natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Whether you are interested in learning about the rich cultural history of the region, want to explore the great outdoors, or just take a relaxing vacation, Lebanon has something to offer for everyone.

This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the necessary information to plan your next trip to Lebanon.

List of Places to Visit in Lebanon

Lebanon offers a plethora of places to visit, each with its unique charm and attractions. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lebanon is a culturally and historically rich region with numerous museums and historical sites that give travelers an insight into the past.

  • McKendree University Archives
  • Mermaid House Hotel Museum
  • Lebanon Historical Society Museum and Archives

Natural Attractions of Lebanon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, or just enjoying the beautiful scenery, then Lebanon won’t disappoint you.

  • Mckendree Metro Rec Plex
  • Horner Park
  • Emerald Mound

Lebanon offers diverse landscapes to explore on foot or bike. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the region:

Best Things to Do in Lebanon: Activities and Tours

Lebanon offers a variety of activities and tours to make your trip even more memorable. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Brewery Tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Lebanon

If you’re planning to spend a week in Lebanon, then consider following this itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Lebanon Historical Society Museum and Archives, and enjoy a guided city tour.

Day 2: Explore Mckendree Metro Rec Plex Trail, and end your day with dinner at one of Lebanon’s famous restaurants.

Day 3: Discover the Mermaid House Hotel Museum, and enjoy the scenic view at Horner Park.

Day 4: Enjoy a winery tour at Villa Marie Winery, and have lunch at one of the local cafes.

Day 5: Visit Mckendree University Archives and Emerald Mound. Later, take a brewery tour at Old Bakery Beer Company.

Day 6: Experience Route 66 Trail, and end your day by indulging in some local delicacies.

Day 7: Spend your last day enjoying the beauty of Lebanon, and take back some souvenirs for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Lebanon

Lebanon has a diverse culture influenced by various religions and ethnicities. It is essential to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Lebanon

The best time to visit Lebanon is during late spring or early fall when pleasant weather prevails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lebanon

Do:

  • Respect local culture and traditions.
  • Carry cash, as few places accept cards.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lebanon

Lebanon is generally a safe place. It has a low crime rate, but it is advisable to take precautions.

Health in Lebanon

Lebanon has modern healthcare facilities, and medical treatment is available at any time.
