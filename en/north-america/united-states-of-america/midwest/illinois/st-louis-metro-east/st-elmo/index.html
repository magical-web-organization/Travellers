A Comprehensive Travel Guide to St. Elmo, Illinois

Located in St. Louis Metro East, in Illinois, St. Elmo is a charming small town that offers visitors a taste of rural Midwest life. With plenty of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities, St. Elmo is a great destination for travelers looking for an authentic, unique experience. In this guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in St. Elmo, including hiking/biking routes, cultural landmarks, food and drink, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in St. Elmo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Elmo has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its many museums and landmarks. Some of the most popular attractions include:

  • The Fayette County Museum: This museum is housed in a historic courthouse and contains exhibits and artifacts that showcase the history and culture of Fayette County.
  • The St. Elmo Public Library: Founded in 1892, this beautiful library is a historic landmark in its own right, with impressive architecture and a vast collection of books.
  • The Hotrod Institute: This automotive school specializes in hotrods and offers various courses on building and modifying cars.

Natural Attractions of St. Elmo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love nature, St. Elmo has plenty to offer. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • The Kaskaskia River: This beautiful river runs through St. Elmo and offers excellent opportunities for fishing, boating, and kayaking.
  • Brownstown Park: This peaceful park is perfect for a picnic or a relaxing walk. With natural beauty and a serene atmosphere, it’s a wonderful place to unwind.
  • Okaw Valley Orchard: Located just outside of town, this orchard boasts beautiful scenery and plenty of opportunities for apple-picking, pumpkin patches, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Elmo

St. Elmo is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • The Kaskaskia River Trail: This 13-mile trail follows the Kaskaskia River and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and even horseback riding.
  • Brownstown Nature Trail: This scenic trail is just under a mile long and is perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • Ramsey Lake State Park: While not technically in St. Elmo, this nearby state park is a great place to hike, fish, and enjoy the great outdoors.

Best Things to Do in St. Elmo: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours to keep you entertained in St. Elmo. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Wine Tasting at Tuscan Hills Winery: This local winery features a beautiful outdoor tasting area and delicious wines.
  • Checking out the local farmers market: Open from May through October, this market offers fresh produce and handmade crafts from local vendors.
  • Taking a horseback riding lesson at Little Creek Ranch: This ranch offers beginner and advanced lessons and is a fun way to get up close and personal with some beautiful horses.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Elmo

If you’re planning a longer visit to St. Elmo, here’s a sample itinerary to help you get started:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town and visit the Fayette County Museum and the St. Elmo Public Library
Day 2 Hike the Kaskaskia River Trail and enjoy a picnic at Brownstown Park

Day 4 Visit the Hotrod Institute and take a wine tasting tour at Tuscan Hills Winery

Food and Drink Scene of St. Elmo

No trip to St. Elmo is complete without sampling some of its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: This classic sandwich features a breaded and fried pork tenderloin on a bun, served with all the fixings.
  • Fried Chicken: Southern-style fried chicken is a local favorite and can be found at many restaurants around town.
  • Apple Pie: With a nearby orchard, apple pie is a must-try dessert in St. Elmo.

Local Beverages

  • Tuscan Hills Winery: This local winery produces a variety of delicious wines, including reds, whites, and fruit wines.
  • Local Microbreweries: There are several microbreweries in the area that offer unique, locally brewed beers.

Where to Stay in St. Elmo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While there are not many lodging options in St. Elmo itself, there are plenty of nearby options in larger towns. Some of the top picks include:

  • AmericInn by Wyndham Vandalia: This hotel is just a short drive from St. Elmo and offers comfortable rooms and a free continental breakfast.
  • The McKnight Hotel: This historic hotel is located about 30 minutes from St. Elmo and offers elegant rooms and a charming ambiance.
  • Airbnb: There are many Airbnb options in the area, including cozy cabins and charming guesthouses.

What to Buy in St. Elmo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a unique souvenir or gift from St. Elmo, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the most popular local products include:

  • Local Crafts: The local farmers market is a great place to find handmade crafts and goods.
  • Apple Products: With a nearby orchard, there are plenty of apple products to choose from, including apple butter and apple cider.
  • Hotrod Parts and Accessories: The Hotrod Institute offers parts and accessories for car enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Elmo

St. Elmo is a friendly, welcoming community that values hard work, family, and tradition. Visitors are encouraged to respect these values and enjoy the area’s unique charms.

Best Time to Visit St. Elmo

The best time to visit St. Elmo is during the spring and fall, when temperatures are mild and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Elmo

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and appreciate the area’s unique charms.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area through hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the area’s cultural traditions or values.
  • Leave litter or damage natural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Elmo

St. Elmo is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as locking their cars and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in St. Elmo

Visitors should be aware of basics health and hygiene practices, including washing hands regularly and getting vaccinated as needed.

Getting to St. Elmo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Elmo

The nearest airport is Lambert International Airport in St. Louis, Missouri, which is about 90 minutes from St. Elmo by car.

Bus Stations Near St. Elmo

Greyhound offers bus service to Vandalia, which is just a short drive from St. Elmo.

Other Options

Driving is the most popular way to get to St. Elmo, as the town is located just off Interstate 70.

How to Get Around in St. Elmo

The best way to get around in St. Elmo is by car.

Where to Go Next from St. Elmo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Midwest, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • St. Louis, Missouri: This vibrant city is just a short drive away and offers plenty of cultural landmarks, restaurants, and bars.
  • Springfield, Illinois: This charming city is the birthplace of Abraham Lincoln and offers many historic landmarks and attractions.
  • Brown County, Indiana: Known for its scenic beauty and outdoor activities, Brown County is a great destination for nature lovers.

Conclusion

St. Elmo is a hidden gem in the heart of the Midwest, offering visitors a taste of small-town charm and natural beauty. From hiking and outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this quaint community. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, St. Elmo is a destination worth exploring.
