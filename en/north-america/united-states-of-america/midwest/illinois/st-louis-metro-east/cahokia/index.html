Introduction to Cahokia

Cahokia is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the rich cultural history of the St. Louis Metro East. Located in Illinois, Midwest, United States of America, North America, Cahokia is an ancient Native American city that flourished between 600 and 1400 AD. In 1982, it was named a UNESCO World Heritage Site and has since become a popular attraction for tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Cahokia

Here is a list of the best places to visit in Cahokia that will give you a glimpse of its rich history and natural beauty:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site: The largest pre-Columbian settlement north of Mexico and the heart of Cahokia culture.
  • Cahokia Courthouse State Historic Site: Built in the early 1800s, it is the oldest continuously operating courthouse in Illinois.
  • Cahokia Canal Interpretive Center: tells the story of the people, history, and culture of the St. Louis Canal.

Natural Attractions of Cahokia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gateway Arch National Park: a beautiful park that offers breathtaking views of the Mississippi River and St. Louis skyline.
  • Horseshoe Lake State Park: A natural oasis with hiking trails, fishing, boating, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cahokia

Cahokia offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the Midwest. Here are a few excellent routes that you can try:

  • Cahokia Mounds Interpretive Trail: A 2.2-mile trail that takes you through the history and natural beauty of the site.
  • McKinley Bridge Bikeway Trail: A 1.7-mile trail that takes you across the Mississippi River to a beautiful park on the other side.

Best Things to Do in Cahokia: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Cahokia:

  • Take a guided tour of Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site: A knowledgeable guide will take you through the site’s history and culture.
  • Visit the Gateway Arch: Take a tram ride to the top for stunning views of St. Louis and the Mississippi River.

Food and Drink Scene of Cahokia

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re visiting Cahokia, you must try some of these local dishes:

  • St. Louis-style pizza: A thin crispy crust pizza with provel cheese, a blend of provolone, Swiss, and white cheddar.
  • Toasted ravioli: A St. Louis original dish consisting of deep-fried, breaded ravioli filled with meat or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Anheuser-Busch Brewery: A brewery that offers tours for visitors to see the making of Budweiser Beer, one of St. Louis’s popular brands.

Where to Stay in Cahokia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodation options in Cahokia:

  • Cahokia Guesthouse: A comfortable and affordable guesthouse that offers all the amenities that you need.
  • Holiday Inn St. Louis Downtown-Convention Center: A luxury hotel that offers great amenities and is located within walking distance of the Gateway Arch.

What to Buy in Cahokia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products that you can take home from Cahokia:

  • Native American Artifacts: You can buy authentic Native American artifacts and artwork from the Cahokia Mounds gift shop.
  • St. Louis Blues Hockey Jerseys: If you’re a hockey fan, you can buy Blues jerseys from local stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Cahokia

The people of Cahokia are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. Native American culture is deeply rooted in the area and is celebrated through events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Cahokia

The best time to visit Cahokia is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and temperate. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cahokia

Do:

  • Respect the native culture and traditions of Cahokia.
  • Be mindful of the environment and do not litter.

Don’t:

  • Do not touch or remove any artifacts from the Cahokia Mounds site.
  • Do not trespass or disturb any wildlife in Horseshoe Lake State Park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cahokia

Cahokia is a safe place to travel, but it’s always best to take precautions while exploring. Keep your valuables safe and secure and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Cahokia

Cahokia generally has good healthcare services. In an emergency, call 911 for immediate assistance.

Getting to Cahokia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the best transportation options to get to Cahokia:

Airports Near Cahokia

  • Lambert-St. Louis International Airport: 17 miles away

Bus Stations Near Cahokia

  • Greyhound Bus Station: located in St. Louis

Other Options

  • Car Rental: A car rental is a convenient way to explore the area.
  • Taxi: Taxis are easily accessible around Cahokia.

How to Get Around in Cahokia

The best way to get around Cahokia is by car or bike. Several rental car and bike rental options are available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Cahokia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Cahokia, here are some nearby destinations that you can visit:

  • St. Louis: Just across the river, St. Louis offers plenty of attractions to explore.
  • Alton: A historic city located on the Mississippi River that offers scenic river views.

Conclusion

Cahokia is a unique and fascinating destination that offers a glimpse into the ancient Native American culture. With plenty of museums, historical attractions, hiking trails, and natural beauty, Cahokia is the perfect destination for history buffs and nature lovers.
