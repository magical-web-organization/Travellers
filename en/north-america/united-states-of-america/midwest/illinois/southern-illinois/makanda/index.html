Introduction to Makanda

Nestled in the heart of Southern Illinois, Makanda is a charming hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This small village, situated in the scenic Shawnee Hills region, is home to breathtaking natural wonders, quaint shops and galleries, and friendly locals eager to share their laid-back lifestyle. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat in nature or an adventure-packed trip, Makanda offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the must-see attractions, best hiking and biking routes, top places to stay and eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Makanda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Makanda has a rich history that is well-preserved through its museums and landmarks. Here are some must-visit attractions for history buffs:

  • Makanda Museum & Visitors Center: Learn about the village’s heritage, arts, and crafts at this museum, which features exhibits on local geology, Native American history, and the famous Eclipse Crossroads of 2017.
  • Little Grand Canyon: This awe-inspiring natural wonder features stunning rock formations and hiking trails.
  • Giant City State Park: Explore the beauty of Giant City State Park, which has an extensive network of hiking, biking, and equestrian trails, rock climbing sites, and picturesque picnic areas.

Natural Attractions of Makanda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Makanda boasts some of the most stunning natural scenery in the Midwest. From lush forests to clear streams, here are the top natural attractions in Makanda:

  • Shawnee National Forest: This national forest is home to some of Illinois’ most beautiful landscapes, featuring over a hundred miles of hiking trails, scenic drives, and horseback riding opportunities.
  • Ferne Clyffe State Park: This scenic park is famous for its towering rock formations, waterfalls, and diverse wildlife.
  • Pomona Natural Bridge: A magnificent bridge-shaped rock formation that spans a 90-foot-wide creek bed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Makanda

Makanda has ample opportunities to explore the great outdoors on foot or on wheels. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes around the area:

  • Little Grand Canyon Trail: a three-mile out-and-back trail that offers captivating views of the Little Grand Canyon and unique rock formations.
  • Cedar Lake Trail: a scenic three-mile hike around Cedar Lake, which features a variety of forest landscapes and clear water views.
  • Shawnee Hills Wine Trail: This 25-mile scenic route features eight wineries, charming small towns, and stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Makanda: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun and exciting activities to do in Makanda, whether you want to go on a wine tour, take a pottery class, or enjoy a scenic horseback ride. Here are some top things to do in Makanda:

  • Cedar Lake Pottery: Take a pottery class or shop for unique handmade ceramics at Cedar Lake Pottery, a family-owned business that has been in operation for over 40 years.
  • Shawnee Bluffs Canopy Tour: Zip line through the scenic Shawnee Hills region on this exciting tour that features eight zip lines, three aerial suspension bridges, and beautiful treetop views.
  • Ankiewicz Wildlife Center: Observe majestic birds of prey and learn about conservation efforts at this wildlife rehabilitation center, which is home to several species of owls, hawks, eagles, and falcons.

Week-Long Itinerary for Makanda

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Makanda, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Explore the town of Makanda, visit the Makanda Museum & Visitors Center, and grab a bite to eat at The Wineaux.
  • Day 2: Hike the Little Grand Canyon Trail and visit the Pomona Natural Bridge.
  • Day 3: Take a scenic drive on the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail and try some local wines at Blue Sky Vineyard and Owl Creek Vineyard.
  • Day 4: Go on a horseback ride at Giant City Stables and enjoy a picnic at Giant City State Park.
  • Day 5: Visit the Fern Clyffe State Park, hike the Big Rocky Hollow Trail, and cool off at the Ferne Clyffe waterfall.
  • Day 6: Take a pottery class at Cedar Lake Pottery and visit Rustle Hill Winery for some more wine tasting.
  • Day 7: Go on the Shawnee Bluffs Canopy Tour and end your trip with a memorable dinner at Alto Vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Makanda

Makanda has a vibrant food and drink scene that celebrates local ingredients and flavors. Whether you’re in the mood for Southern-style cooking or international cuisine, there’s something to satisfy every palate.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken and waffles: Get the best of both worlds with this classic Southern comfort dish, served at The Wineaux in Makanda
  • Catfish: Try this crispy and flavorful fish at The Giant City Lodge Restaurant.
  • Southern barbecue: Satisfy your meat cravings at the 17th Street BBQ restaurant.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Makanda is part of the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail, which features eight unique wineries, each with their own specialty wines.
  • Beer: Sample locally crafted beers at Scratch Brewing Company, which is known for its inventive and experimental brews.
  • Moonshine: Take a tour of the StarView Vineyards Distillery and try their signature “white lightning”.

Where to Stay in Makanda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Makanda offers a range of accommodations to suit any budget and style, from cozy bed and breakfast inns to rustic cabins. Here are some popular options:

  • Giant City Lodge: This charming lodge is situated in the heart of Giant City State Park and features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a gift shop.
  • Makanda Inn & Cottages: These luxurious cottages feature rustic decor, modern amenities, and stunning views of the surrounding forests.
  • Shawnee Forest Cabins: These cozy cabins offer a peaceful retreat in nature, with scenic trails, fishing ponds, and outdoor hot tubs.

What to Buy in Makanda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Makanda is brimming with locally crafted treasures that make for perfect souvenirs or gifts. Here are some unique products to look out for:

  • Pottery: Take home a one-of-a-kind ceramic piece from Cedar Lake Pottery or Blue Sky Vineyard Pottery.
  • Artwork: Browse the galleries and shops around town for handcrafted jewelry, paintings, and sculptures.
  • Wine: Stock up on bottles of your favorite local wines from the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail.

Local Culture and Customs of Makanda

Makanda is a laid-back and welcoming community with a rich cultural heritage. Here are some customs and cultural events you might encounter during your visit:

  • Southern hospitality: Expect friendly and welcoming locals who are happy to share their town’s hidden gems with you.
  • Eclipse Crossroads: In August 2017, Makanda was in the path of totality for a total solar eclipse, a rare phenomenon that drew visitors from around the world.
  • Spring Fest: Every May, the town hosts a Spring Fest event, which features live music, food vendors, arts and crafts exhibits, and more.

Best Time to Visit Makanda

Makanda is gorgeous year-round, but some seasons offer special benefits. Spring and fall are ideal for mild temperatures and vibrant foliage, while summer offers endless outdoor activities and events. Winter can be a quieter time to enjoy the area’s scenic beauty and cozy cabins.

Do’s and Don’ts of Makanda

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Makanda is home to some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the region, so make sure to explore them on foot or on wheels.
  • Try the local wine: The Shawnee Hills Wine Trail is a must-visit for wine enthusiasts, featuring unique wineries and stunning views.
  • Meet the locals: The locals in Makanda are friendly and welcoming, so strike up a conversation and learn more about the town’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Respect the area’s natural beauty by disposing of your trash responsibly and following the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Trespass: Many of the area’s natural attractions are protected or privately owned, so make sure to stay within designated areas and respect local regulations.
  • Overpack: The weather in Makanda can be unpredictable, but it’s best to pack light and be prepared for different conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Makanda

Makanda is a relatively safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions when exploring unfamiliar areas. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay on designated paths: Some of the area’s natural attractions can be treacherous, so make sure to follow marked trails and stay within safe areas.
  • Watch for wildlife: Makanda is home to a variety of wildlife, including venomous snakes and ticks, so make sure to take appropriate precautions and seek medical attention if necessary.

Health in Makanda

Makanda has a few medical centers and hospitals, including the Carbondale Memorial Hospital, which is around 20 miles away. However, it’s always best to take preventative measures and make sure you have a first-aid kit, insect repellent, and plenty of water during outdoor activities.

Getting to Makanda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Makanda is easily accessible by car, bus, or airplane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Makanda

  • Southern Illinois Airport: This regional airport is located around 15 miles from Makanda and offers daily flights to and from Chicago and St. Louis.

Bus Stations Near Makanda

  • Amtrak Station Carbondale: This station is around 20 miles from Makanda and offers bus and train services to other destinations in the Midwest.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Makanda, the major highways that pass through the area are I-57 and I-24. The closest major cities are St. Louis (around 120 miles away) and Chicago (around 350 miles away).

How to Get Around in Makanda

Makanda is a small town, so walking or biking is the best way to get around town. For more distant attractions, a car or taxi may be necessary.

Where to Go Next from Makanda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Makanda is a great base for exploring the Shawnee Hills region and other nearby destinations. Consider these options for a side trip:

  • Carbondale: This college town is home to Southern Illinois University Carbondale, as well as a thriving arts and culture scene.
  • St. Louis: Just a couple of hours away by car, St. Louis offers a wealth of attractions, including the iconic Gateway Arch, the St. Louis Zoo, and the City Museum.
  • Paducah, Kentucky: This charming riverfront town boasts a vibrant arts district, historic landmarks, and delicious Southern cuisine.

Conclusion

Makanda is a unique and beautiful destination that offers an authentic taste of Southern Illinois culture and nature. From hiking and biking to wine tasting and pottery classes, there is something for every visitor to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or an adventure-packed trip, Makanda is sure to captivate you, and leave you wanting to come back for more.
