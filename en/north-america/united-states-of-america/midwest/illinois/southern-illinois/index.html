Introduction to Southern Illinois

Southern Illinois, also known as “Little Egypt,” is a gem in the Midwest region of the United States of America. It is known for its rolling hills, lush forests, and the mighty Mississippi River that runs alongside it. Southern Illinois boasts of its culturally rich history, natural beauty, and abundance of outdoor activities that attract visitors from all around the world.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Southern Illinois. From culturally important attractions to the best hiking/biking routes and local cuisine, we have you covered!

List of Places to Visit in Southern Illinois

Here is a quick list of must-visit places in Southern Illinois:

  • Giant City State Park
  • Shawnee National Forest
  • Little Grand Canyon
  • Cache River State Natural Area
  • Garden of the Gods
  • Wine Trail in Shawnee Hills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southern Illinois has a rich cultural history, and you can explore it by visiting some of the museums and historical places in the region. Here are some places worth a visit:

  • Lincoln-Herndon Law Offices in Springfield
  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site
  • The General John A. Logan Museum in Murphysboro
  • The National Quilt Museum in Paducah
  • The Delta Interpretive Center in Cairo

Natural Attractions of Southern Illinois: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Illinois is famous for its natural beauty and abundance of outdoor activities. Here are some natural attractions that are a must-visit:

  • Garden of Gods Recreation Area
  • Little Grand Canyon Trail
  • Indian Kitchen Trail
  • Pomona Natural Bridge
  • Giant City Nature Trail

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Illinois

Southern Illinois has some of the best hiking and biking trails in the Midwest region. Here are some notable trails:

  • Garden of the Gods Trail

Best Things to Do in Southern Illinois: Activities and Tours

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Illinois

Southern Illinois is known for its friendly, welcoming, and inclusive culture. It’s good to keep in mind the following customs:

  • Be respectful of Southern Illinois locals and communities.

Best Time to Visit Southern Illinois

The best time to visit Southern Illinois is in the fall when the region is ablaze with the colors of autumn trees and the temperature is still warm enough to have outdoor fun.

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Illinois

Southern Illinois is generally a safe place to visit. However, It is always good to follow basic safety practices while traveling such as locking your car and keeping valuables safe, especially when hiking.

Health in Southern Illinois

Tick-borne illnesses are a concern in Southern Illinois, especially in wooded areas of Southern Illinois. Make sure to take the necessary precautions like using a tick repellent spray and checking yourself after hikes.
