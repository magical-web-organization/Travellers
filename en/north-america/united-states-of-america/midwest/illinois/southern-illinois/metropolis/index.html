Introduction to Metropolis

Metropolis is a small city in Southern Illinois, located along the Ohio River and just a stone’s throw away from Kentucky. Despite its size, this city is known for being the “Hometown of Superman” and has a lot to offer for tourists and locals alike.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Metropolis. From must-see attractions to the best hiking and biking routes to the top local restaurants, this guide will cover it all.

List of Places to Visit in Metropolis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Superman Statue and Square: Get your photo taken with the iconic Superman statue, which stands tall in the heart of Metropolis. While you’re there, explore the surrounding square, which is lined with small shops and restaurants.
  • Super Museum: Learn about the history of Superman and immerse yourself in the superhero’s world at this unique museum. With an extensive collection of Superman memorabilia and interactive exhibits, this is a must-visit attraction for fans.
  • Fort Massac State Park: Step back in time and explore the remains of Fort Massac, a French and Indian War-era fort. The park also has a visitor center with exhibits about the fort’s history and the surrounding area.
  • The Center for American History: This small museum showcases the local history of Metropolis and Southern Illinois. With exhibits on everything from Native American artifacts to local industry, this is a great place to learn about the area’s culture and heritage.

Natural Attractions of Metropolis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Shawnee National Forest: Located just a short drive from Metropolis, Shawnee National Forest is a nature lover’s paradise. The park features miles of hiking and biking trails, beautiful waterfalls, and stunning views of the valley.
  • Trail of Tears State Forest: This forest is home to the southernmost portion of the Trail of Tears, a historic route used by Native Americans during their forced removal. The forest has several hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and picnic areas.
  • Ohio River Scenic Byway: Take a drive along this scenic route to see some of the best views of the Ohio River. The byway runs for over 300 miles and passes through several charming towns and villages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Metropolis

  • River to River Trail: This 160-mile trail runs from the Mississippi River to the Ohio River and passes through Metropolis. The trail is open to hikers, bikers, and horseback riders and offers stunning views of Southern Illinois.
  • Riverfront Trail: This trail runs along the Ohio River and is perfect for a scenic walk or bike ride. The trail is flat and paved, making it accessible for all fitness levels.
  • Shawnee National Forest Trails: With over 400 miles of trails, the Shawnee National Forest offers hiking options for all skill levels. Some of the most popular trails in the area include the Garden of the Gods Trail, the Rim Rock Trail, and the Tunnel Hill State Trail.

Best Things to Do in Metropolis: Activities and Tours

  • Superman Celebration: Held annually in June, this festival celebrates all things Superman. With live music, costume contests, and celebrity appearances, this festival is a must-attend event for fans of the Man of Steel.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Take a leisurely drive through the scenic Shawnee Hills Wine Trail and visit some of the region’s top wineries. With a variety of wines to taste and beautiful views to take in, this is a great way to spend a relaxing day.
  • Riverboat Tour: See Metropolis from a different perspective with a riverboat tour. Learn about the history of the Ohio River and enjoy the scenic views of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Metropolis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Metropolis-style Pizza: This regional specialty is a thin, crispy, and slightly sweet pizza topped with provolone cheese. Check out local pizzerias like Fat Edd’s Roadhouse and Pizza by the Pound for a taste of this unique pizza.
  • Moonshine Burger: This burger is made with a moonshine-infused patty and topped with bacon, cheddar cheese, and a moonshine glaze. Try it at Big John’s Shake Shack, a popular local spot.
  • Fried Fish Platter: You can’t visit Southern Illinois without trying the fried fish. Check out Hook Line and Sinker or The Blue Boar for a classic fish and chips platter.

Local Beverages

  • Shawnee Hills Wine: The Shawnee Hills Wine Trail is known for its high-quality wines, including local varietals like chardonel and vignoles. Stop by the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail Tasting Room to sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Ginger Ale: Metropolis is home to a ginger ale bottling plant that has been producing the soda since 1909. Try a cold bottle of Ski or Double Cola ginger ale for a taste of local history.

Where to Stay in Metropolis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Harrah’s Metropolis: This hotel and casino offers luxurious accommodations in the heart of Metropolis. With a variety of dining options and entertainment, this is a great choice for those looking for a bit of excitement.
  • Metropolis Inn: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location on the edge of town.
  • Timber Ridge Outpost and Cabins: For a unique experience, stay in one of the cozy cabins at Timber Ridge Outpost. The property is located in the Shawnee National Forest and offers easy access to hiking and biking trails.

What to Buy in Metropolis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Superman Memorabilia: Take home a piece of Metropolis’s Superman history with a souvenir from the Super Museum or one of the local gift shops.
  • Shawnee Hills Wine: Bring home a bottle or two of your favorite local wine from the Shawnee Hills Wine Trail Tasting Room.
  • Handmade Crafts: Check out the local artisan shops and flea markets for handmade crafts and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Metropolis

Metropolis is a friendly city with a strong sense of community. Locals are proud of their Superman heritage and are happy to share it with visitors. The area is also known for its love of BBQ and country music.

Best Time to Visit Metropolis

The best time to visit Metropolis is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The Superman Celebration festival in June is also a popular time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Metropolis

Do:

  • Do visit the Superman Statue and Square and take a photo with the iconic superhero.
  • Do try the Metropolis-style pizza and other regional specialties.
  • Do hike the Garden of the Gods Trail and explore the Shawnee National Forest.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the chance to visit the Super Museum and learn about the history of Superman.
  • Don’t forget to try the local ginger ale and other regional beverages.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings while enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Metropolis

Metropolis is a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions like avoiding dark and isolated areas at night.

Health in Metropolis

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in the area, including the Massac Memorial Hospital and the Metropolis Healthcare Clinic.

Getting to Metropolis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Metropolis is located just off Interstate 24, making it easy to access by car. There are also several airports and bus stations in the area for those traveling from further away.

Airports Near Metropolis

  • Barkley Regional Airport (28 miles)
  • Cape Girardeau Regional Airport (50 miles)
  • Evansville Regional Airport (64 miles)

Bus Stations Near Metropolis

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Paducah, KY (11 miles)

Other Options

  • Amtrak - Carbondale, IL (69 miles)

How to Get Around in Metropolis

The best way to get around Metropolis is by car. There are several car rental options in the area. The Riverfront Trolley runs seasonally and provides transportation to some of the top attractions in town.

Where to Go Next from Metropolis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Paducah, KY: Just a short drive across the Ohio River, Paducah is a charming town known for its arts and culture scene.
  • Shawnee National Forest: Explore more of the beautiful scenery and outdoor activities in the area.
  • Land Between The Lakes National Recreation Area: This outdoor recreation area offers camping, fishing, hiking, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Metropolis may be a small city, but it has a lot to offer visitors. From the Superman statue to the Shawnee National Forest, there’s something for everyone in this charming Southern Illinois town. Plan your trip using this comprehensive guide and get ready to discover the hidden gem of the Midwest.
