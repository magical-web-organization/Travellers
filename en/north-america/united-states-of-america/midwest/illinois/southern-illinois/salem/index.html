Introduction to Salem

Nestled in the heart of Southern Illinois, Salem is a charming city that boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and a vibrant culture. Historically known as the “Gateway to Little Egypt,” Salem is home to many unique attractions, including museums, historical sites, hiking trails, and much more. This hidden gem is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Southern Illinois.

List of Places to Visit in Salem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salem is steeped in history and culture, making it an ideal destination for history buffs and culture vultures. Here are some must-visit attractions in Salem:

  • Salem Courthouse Square: The Salem Courthouse Square is a historic district that has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It features several historic sites, including the Marion County Courthouse, which was built in 1907.
  • William Jennings Bryan Boyhood Home: The William Jennings Bryan Boyhood Home is a historical landmark that commemorates the life and legacy of the famed politician and orator. The home has been restored to its original condition and now serves as a museum.
  • Salem History Museum: The Salem History Museum is a small, but impressive museum that tells the story of Salem’s rich history. Exhibits range from the city’s earliest days to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Salem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salem is also known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Here are some must-visit attractions for nature lovers:

  • Stephen A. Forbes State Park: Stephen A. Forbes State Park is a nature lover’s paradise. The park features several hiking trails, fishing spots, and picnic areas.
  • Kaskaskia River: The Kaskaskia River is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and kayaking.
  • Carlyle Lake: Carlyle Lake is a massive man-made lake that offers a variety of recreational activities, including fishing, boating, swimming, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salem

Salem is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Gitchie Manitou Trail: The Gitchie Manitou Trail is a 3.5-mile hiking trail that winds through the forests of Stephen A. Forbes State Park.
  • Salem Bike Trail: The Salem Bike Trail is a 10-mile paved trail that takes riders through the heart of the city and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Salem: Activities and Tours

Salem offers plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Salem:

  • Take a walking tour of the historic Salem Courthouse Square.
  • Explore the William Jennings Bryan Boyhood Home and Museum.
  • Take a guided tour of the picturesque countryside surrounding Salem.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salem

Day 1: Explore Salem Courthouse Square and the Salem History Museum.

Day 2: Hike the Gitchie Manitou Trail in Stephen A. Forbes State Park.

Day 3: Visit the William Jennings Bryan Boyhood Home and Museum.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the countryside surrounding Salem.

Day 5: Spend a day at Carlyle Lake, swimming, boating, and fishing.

Day 6: Explore the Kaskaskia River by kayak or canoe.

Day 7: Cycle the scenic Salem Bike Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Salem

Must-Try Dishes

Salem has a thriving culinary scene, with many restaurants and eateries offering local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: A Southern Illinois classic, the pork tenderloin sandwich is a breaded and fried pork cutlet served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and pickle.
  • Chicken and Dumplings: Chicken and dumplings is a hearty, comforting dish made with tender chunks of chicken and fluffy dumplings in a rich, creamy sauce.
  • Skillet Fried Chicken: Another Southern Illinois classic, skillet fried chicken is crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside, served with mashed potatoes and gravy.

Local Beverages

Salem is home to several craft breweries and wineries, offering a variety of locally produced beer and wine. Some of the best places to sample these drinks include:

  • Starview Vineyards: Starview Vineyards is a family-owned winery that produces a variety of award-winning wines, including Chardonnay, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot.
  • Reformation Brewery: Reformation Brewery is a craft brewery that produces a variety of beer styles, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Hidden Lake Winery & Banquet Center: Hidden Lake Winery & Banquet Center is a beautiful vineyard that produces a variety of wines, including Chardonnay, Riesling, and Sangria.

Where to Stay in Salem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salem offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and tastes. Here are some of the best places to stay in Salem:

  • Salem Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as free Wi-Fi and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Mariner Motel: A cozy and charming motel that offers affordable rates and easy access to all of Salem’s attractions.

What to Buy in Salem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Salem-made Wine: Many of Salem’s wineries offer locally produced wine that makes a perfect souvenir or gift for wine lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Salem

Salem has a rich cultural history, with many traditions and customs that are unique to the region. Some of the most prominent customs include:

  • Southern Hospitality: Visitors to Salem can expect to experience the warm and friendly hospitality that is characteristic of Southern Illinois.
  • Festivals and Events: Salem hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, including the Marion County Fair, the Salem Art and Craft Fair, and the Salem Balloon Fest.

Best Time to Visit Salem

The best time to visit Salem is in the fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. However, Salem is a great destination year-round, with plenty of activities and events happening throughout the year.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salem

Salem is a safe city to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly and welcoming community. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking doors and securing valuables and belongings.

Health in Salem

Salem has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that provide quality healthcare to residents and visitors alike. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before visiting Salem.

Getting to Salem: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salem

The nearest airport to Salem is Lambert-St. Louis International Airport, located approximately 85 miles northwest of the city. Other regional airports include Quincy Regional Airport and Williamson County Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Salem

Bus services to Salem are provided by Greyhound, with the nearest station located in Centralia, approximately 10 miles from Salem.

Other Options

Visitors to Salem can also reach the city by car, with several major highways and interstates passing through the area.

How to Get Around in Salem

Salem is a small city that is easily navigable by car or bicycle. Visitors can also explore the city on foot, taking in the historic sights and landmarks at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Salem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
