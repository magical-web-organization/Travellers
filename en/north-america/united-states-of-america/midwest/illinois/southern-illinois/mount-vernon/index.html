Introduction to Mount Vernon

Located in southern Illinois, Mount Vernon is a small city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and excellent outdoor adventures. The town is situated in the heart of Jefferson County and has been a prominent city since the 1800s. From quaint antique shops to outdoor recreational areas, the city offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Vernon

Here are the top tourist attractions and things to do in Mount Vernon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cedarhurst Center for the Arts: This museum is one of the most popular places to visit in Mount Vernon. It features an extensive collection of modern and contemporary artwork, pottery, and sculptures. It also has various indoor galleries and outdoor exhibitions.

  • Jefferson County Historical Village: This historic village features a variety of preserved buildings that represent the lifestyle and architecture of the mid-19th century. The village includes a one-room schoolhouse, candle shop, general store, and church.

  • Dr. Richard E. Sauder Cultural Center & Museum: This museum features an extensive collection of artifacts from Native American, African American, and European settlers. It also shows the contributions of Dr. Sauder in the medical field in central Illinois.

Natural Attractions of Mount Vernon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rend Lake: This beautiful lake is one of the top attractions in the Mount Vernon area. It offers various outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, camping, and hiking.

  • Jefferson County Conservation Area: This nature preserve covers more than 1,000 acres and offers miles of hiking trails, birdwatching, and scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Wayne Fitzgerrell State Park: This state park is located on the Rend Lake shoreline and offers boating, fishing, hunting, hiking, and camping. The park also has a championship golf course and an archery range.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Vernon

  • Horseshoe Lake Trail: This beautiful trail in Wayne Fitzgerrell State Park is perfect for hiking, biking, or walking. It offers scenic views of the lake and the surrounding woodlands.

  • Governors Trail: This 10-mile trail runs through the south suburbs of Mount Vernon and offers an excellent opportunity for an outdoor workout.

  • River to River Trail: This 160-mile trail passes from the Ohio River to the Mississippi River, crossing through the Shawnee National Forest. It provides a fantastic hiking experience, with scenic views and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Mount Vernon: Activities and Tours

  • Mount Vernon Ghost Tour: This popular guided tour takes visitors on a walk through Mount Vernon’s haunted history. The tour highlights some of the most haunted locations in the city, including the Old Masonic Temple and C.E.Brehm Memorial Public Library.

  • Jefferson County Farmers’ Market: This weekly event is held throughout the summer months. It offers visitors fresh produce, baked goods, flowers, and handmade crafts.

  • Lincoln Heritage House: This historic house tour offers insight into the life of Abraham Lincoln before he became president. Located on the campus of the University of Illinois, the house features authentic furnishings, artwork, and historical artifacts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Vernon

Day 1: Visit Cedarhurst Center for the Arts, enjoy a picnic at the nearby Indian Hills Lake.

Day 2: Explore the Jefferson County Historical Village and learn about the area’s rich history.

Day 3: Go biking on the Horseshoe Lake Trail and explore Wayne Fitzgerrell State Park

Day 4: Visit the Dr. Richard E. Sauder Cultural Center & Museum and learn about the contributions of Dr. Sauder in the local medical field.

Day 5: Take a tour of the historic Lincoln Heritage House, located on the campus of the University of Illinois.

Day 6: Explore the Rend Lake area and enjoy various water-based activities such as boating, fishing, and swimming.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic at the beautiful Veterans Memorial Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Vernon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Tenderloin Sandwich: This classic Midwest dish is a must-try when visiting Mount Vernon. The sandwich is made with a breaded pork tenderloin and served with various toppings such as lettuce, pickles, and onions.

  • Fried Chicken: You can never go wrong with a classic Southern-style fried chicken.

  • Pie: Mount Vernon is known for its homemade pies, such as cherry, apple, and pecan. You can’t leave without trying at least one slice.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The region is home to various vineyards, such as the Blue Sky Vineyard and Von Jakob Vineyard, where you can taste delicious wines.

  • Craft Beers: There are a few local breweries in the area, including The St. Nicholas Brewing Company and Scratch Brewing Company, which offer craft beers made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Mount Vernon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Drury Inn & Suites: This hotel is located near the Rend Lake area and offers amenities such as an indoor pool, fitness center, and hot breakfast.

  • Best Western Plus: This hotel is located in the heart of Mount Vernon and offers comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and free breakfast.

  • Super 8 by Wyndham: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean rooms and excellent customer service.

What to Buy in Mount Vernon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally made crafts: Mount Vernon is home to various artists and craftsmen. Consider bringing home pottery, candles, or handmade jewelry.

  • Gourmet foods: From locally made jams, jellies, and sauces, to jars of fresh honey or maple syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Vernon

The locals in Mount Vernon are known for their friendly and welcoming personalities. The area is steeped in history and culture, with a rich heritage that includes Native American, African American, and European settlers.

Best Time to Visit Mount Vernon

The best time to visit Mount Vernon is during the spring and fall seasons when the temperatures are mild, and the outdoor adventures are at their best. The summer season is also a great time to visit for water activities and outdoor events. However, the winters can be harsh, with occasional snowstorms and sub-zero temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Vernon

Do:

  • Visit the local museums and learn about the area’s rich history.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor recreational opportunities, such as hiking and boating.

  • Try the local cuisine, including pork tenderloin sandwiches and homemade pies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural and historic sites.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites or conservative communities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Vernon

Mount Vernon is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always recommended to take standard precautions when traveling, such as locking your car and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Mount Vernon

Make sure to carry insect repellents when traveling to outdoor recreational areas to avoid bug bites and stings. It is also recommended to bring sunscreen to avoid skin irritation from the sun.

Getting to Mount Vernon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Vernon

The nearest airport is the MidAmerica St. Louis Airport, which is 70 miles away from Mount Vernon.

Bus Stations Near Mount Vernon

Greyhound bus lines service the area with routes to major cities in Illinois and surrounding states.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient way of getting around Mount Vernon, but you can also take the local taxi service, Uber, or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Mount Vernon

The best way to get around the city is by car. Alternatively, you can rent a bike or take a walk around the downtown area.

Where to Go Next from Mount Vernon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shawnee National Forest: Located 80 miles south of Mount Vernon, this forest offers great hiking, camping, and sightseeing opportunities.

  • St. Louis, Missouri: Located approximately 85 miles northwest of Mount Vernon, St. Louis offers various attractions, including the Gateway Arch, the St. Louis Zoo, and the Missouri Botanical Garden.

  • Nashville, Tennessee: Located approximately 191 miles southeast of Mount Vernon, this music city is known for its live music venues, historical sites, and excellent cuisine.

Conclusion

Mount Vernon offers a unique combination of historical landmarks, outdoor recreational opportunities, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing weekend or an active outdoor adventure, Mount Vernon has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and explore all that this charming city has to offer.
