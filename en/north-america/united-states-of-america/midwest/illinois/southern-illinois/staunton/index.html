Introduction to Staunton

Staunton is a historic city nestled in Southern Illinois, USA. With a population of approximately 5,000, the city is known for its charming small-town atmosphere, historic sites, and natural beauty. Founded in 1853, Staunton has a rich history dating back to the early days of America. Whether you’re interested in local history, outdoor adventures, or simply a relaxing getaway, Staunton has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Staunton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Staunton is home to several museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into its rich past. One such site is the Henry’s Rabbit Ranch, a Route 66 classic offering visitors a chance to see the famous VW Rabbit cars. Visitors can tour the museum, pet bunnies, and enjoy a burger at the on-site diner. The Coal Country Miniature Golf, which features replicas of historic coal mines and equipment, is another popular attraction.

For history buffs, the Macoupin County Historical Society Museum is a must-visit destination. Housed in an 1830s mansion, the museum highlights the history of the area with exhibits on local life and industry. The Staunton Heritage Museum, located in a historic building in downtown Staunton, features a variety of exhibits on local history, from early Native American inhabitants to the town’s role in the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Staunton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Staunton offers plenty of outdoor activities for those seeking adventure. The Staunton City Lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and hiking. The lake is surrounded by trails, and visitors can also enjoy picnicking and camping in the area. The adjacent Staunton State Park offers visitors additional opportunities for hiking, camping, and bird watching.

The city is also a great place for a bike ride. The Staunton Bike Trail is a 20-mile paved trail running from Staunton to Staunton City Park, providing gorgeous vistas of the surrounding countryside along the way. The Horseshoe Lake State Park is another popular destination for nature lovers, offering opportunities for boating, swimming, hiking, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Staunton

Staunton has several excellent hiking and biking trails that provide stunning views of the local landscape. The Staunton Bike Trail offers cyclists a scenic route through fields and farm country, and the Chesterfield Valley Trail is a 12-mile trail that winds through the picturesque hills of Madison County. Hikers can explore the beautiful trails in the Horseshoe Lake State Park, which features over 10 miles of well-maintained trails.

Best Things to Do in Staunton: Activities and Tours

Staunton offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit all interests. One popular attraction is the Sunset Drive-In Theater, a classic drive-in movie theater that offers a unique viewing experience. At the Prairie Vista Golf Course, visitors can enjoy a leisurely round of golf while taking in the breathtaking views of the countryside.

For a taste of the local cuisine, visitors can join a local food tour to sample some of the area’s best dishes, including the famous Staunton Burger – a hearty sandwich made with freshly ground beef and served on a locally-baked bun. Other popular activities include visiting local wineries and breweries, and taking guided tours of the city’s historic landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Staunton

Must-Try Dishes

Staunton is known for its hearty Midwestern cuisine, including the famous Staunton Burger and other local specialties. Visitors can also enjoy delicious barbeque, homemade pizza, and fresh-baked pastries at local restaurants and cafes.

Local Beverages

Staunton is home to several local wineries and microbreweries that offer visitors a taste of the region’s best libations. Popular wineries include Piasa Winery and Fergedaboudit Vineyard and Winery, while local breweries include Macoupin Brewing Company and Old Bakery Beer Company.

Where to Stay in Staunton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Staunton can choose from a range of accommodation options, including traditional hotels, quaint bed-and-breakfasts, and cozy guesthouses. Popular options include the Magnuson Grand Hotel, Best Western Plus, and the Carlin Villa Motel.

What to Buy in Staunton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Staunton can find a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, such as handmade crafts, locally-grown produce, and artisanal cheeses. The Staunton Farmers’ Market is a great place to explore for locally-grown food, while the city’s local shops and boutiques offer a range of handmade crafts and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Staunton

Staunton has a strong cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions. Residents of Staunton are known for their friendly nature and hospitality, and visitors should be prepared for casual conversation and a warm welcome.

Best Time to Visit Staunton

The best time to visit Staunton is in the spring and fall when the weather is comfortable, and the foliage is at its peak. Summer is also a great time to visit, but temperatures can be hot, and crowds can be at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Staunton

  • Do taste the famous Staunton Burger and other local specialties.

Safety and Health

Safety in Staunton

Staunton is a relatively safe city, but visitors should heed standard safety precautions such as locking doors and windows and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Staunton

There are several medical facilities in Staunton, including the Staunton Community Health Center and the Staunton Memorial Hospital.

Getting to Staunton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Staunton

The nearest major airport is the Lambert St. Louis International Airport, located approximately 40 miles from Staunton. Visitors can also fly into the MidAmerica St. Louis Airport, located approximately 40 miles from the city.

Bus Stations Near Staunton

Greyhound Bus Lines offers service to the nearby city of Carlinville, approximately 10 miles from Staunton.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Staunton by car via the historic Route 66 or the nearby Interstate 55.

How to Get Around in Staunton

Visitors can explore the downtown area by foot or bike, while a car is necessary for those looking to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from Staunton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Staunton can enjoy a range of nearby destinations and excursions, including the Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site, the Gateway Arch in St. Louis, and the Shawnee National Forest.

Conclusion

Staunton, Illinois, is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With its charming downtown area, fascinating historical sites, and abundance of outdoor adventures, Staunton is the perfect destination for a relaxing getaway.
